- Proposes a two-stream convolutional architecture separating spatial (RGB, ImageNet-pretrained) and temporal (stacked dense optical flow) information for video action recognition.
- Studies several temporal-input design choices: flow stacking vs. trajectory stacking, uni- vs. bi-directional flow, and mean flow subtraction.
- Fuses spatial and temporal streams by late fusion (average or linear SVM on softmax scores).
- Introduces multi-task learning jointly training on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate data scarcity and improve generalization.
- Implements a CNN-M-style architecture with aggressive data augmentation, multi-GPU training, and precomputation/compression of optical flow for practicality and reproducibility.
- Claims substantial improvement over previous deep stacked-RGB baselines and competitiveness with hand-crafted trajectory-based state-of-the-art.
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with informative ablation studies on temporal-design choices.
- Strength: Novel and compelling two-stream formulation; temporal optical-flow ConvNet gives strong performance and complements the spatial stream.
- Strength: Multi-task learning is practical and effective without manual class curation.
- Strength: Thorough implementation/training details support reproducibility.
- Strength: Strong empirical results that outperform prior deep methods and compete with hand-crafted pipelines.
- Weakness: Contributions feel split across multiple threads, diluting narrative focus.
- Weakness: Abstract/introduction should be tightened to emphasize the central two-stream/optical-flow contribution.
- Weakness: Missing related-work citations (Le et al. CVPR 2011; Wang et al. BMVC 2009/2010) that should be discussed.
- Quality: Technically sound with appropriate baselines and careful experimental analysis.
- Clarity: Well organized and reproducible, but framing could be more focused.
- Originality: Novel combination of two-stream ConvNet with optical-flow temporal stream and cross-dataset multi-task training.
- Significance: Important results likely to influence practice, showing optical-flow ConvNet outperforms stacked-RGB and complements spatial networks.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical gains, well-motivated architecture, careful ablations, and practical contributions.
- Cons for acceptance: Narrative needs tightening and related-work citations are missing.
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014) conditional on tightening the abstract/introduction and adding the suggested related work.
