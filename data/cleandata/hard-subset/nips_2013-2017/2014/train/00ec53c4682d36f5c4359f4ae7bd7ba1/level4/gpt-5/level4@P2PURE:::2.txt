This paper introduces a neural architecture for classifying human actions in videos. It fuses predictions from two convolutional networks: one trained on individual video frames (spatial stream) and another trained on short stacks of dense optical flow (temporal stream). The approach significantly outperforms prior ConvNet-based methods for action recognition and matches the current state of the art on two standard video classification datasets.

Although the method largely repurposes the conventional ConvNet image-classification pipeline—applying it to dense optical flow and combining it with a single-frame ConvNet—the experimental study is thorough and the results are compelling. The exploration of several training strategies for the optical-flow ConvNet, alongside clear comparisons, convincingly demonstrates the benefits of using optical flow as input.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why the spatial stream performs substantially better than the models in [13], given the similar architectures.
- A comparison of different frame-sampling schemes for the inputs to the spatial stream would be informative.

Overall, this is a strong empirical paper that will interest researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
