This paper proposes a novel two-stream neural network for human action recognition in video. The architecture fuses the outputs of two separate convolutional neural networks: a spatial stream that processes individual video frames, and a temporal stream trained on sequences of dense optical flow. The proposed method demonstrates superior performance over other convolutional network approaches and achieves state-of-the-art results on two widely used video classification benchmarks.

Although the core idea involves adapting a standard CNN image classification framework to a different input modality (optical flow) and combining it with a frame-based CNN, the empirical evaluation is rigorous and the performance gains are remarkable. A notable strength is the comparison of multiple training methods for the optical flow network, which effectively validates the contribution of using optical flow as an input signal.

My comments are minor:
- The authors could provide more clarification on why the spatial stream significantly outperforms the architecturally similar model from reference [13].
- The paper could be further strengthened by an investigation into the impact of different frame sampling strategies on the spatial network's performance.

Overall, this is a strong, empirically-driven contribution that will be highly relevant to researchers working on video analysis with deep learning.
