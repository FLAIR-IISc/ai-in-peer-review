This paper presents a novel neural network architecture for classifying videos of human actions. The model combines the predictions of two convolutional neural networks: one trained on single video frames and the other trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. The proposed architecture achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets, significantly outperforming other ConvNet-based approaches to action recognition.

While the paper essentially adapts the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and combines this with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough and the results are impressive. It is noteworthy to see several different methods of training a ConvNet on optical flow compared, with the results clearly demonstrating the benefits of using optical flow as input.

I have only a few minor comments:
- The significant performance gap between the spatial network and the models from [13] is not entirely clear, given the similarities in their architectures.
- A comparison of different sampling schemes for the frames fed into the spatial stream would be an interesting addition. This is a well-executed empirical paper that will be of interest to anyone working on video classification with ConvNets.
