- Proposes a dual-convolutional-network video model: one network for appearance frames and one for stacked dense optical flow, combined by a weighted average of independent classifiers.  
- Evaluated on two datasets, achieving results that match or nearly match state-of-the-art methods using heavy hand-crafted features.  
- Strength: simple yet effective application of convolutional networks yielding strong performance.  
- Strength: demonstrates optical flow fields are a valuable input representation for action recognition.  
- Weakness: processes only a fixed window of 10 consecutive frames, missing long-range temporal dynamics and full-video context.  
- Experimental design is sound; would benefit from an error-mode analysis identifying specific classes or video types of failure.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.  
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features.  
- Significance: simple, strong-performing approach with potential to advance video-based vision research.
