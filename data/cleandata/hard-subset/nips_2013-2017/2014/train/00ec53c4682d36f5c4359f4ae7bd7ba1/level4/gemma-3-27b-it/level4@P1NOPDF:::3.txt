The paper proposes several new methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly. The paper is clear, well-written, and convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods through strong experimental results.

The approaches presented are novel. While the paper builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream network is particularly interesting, demonstrating a significant improvement through the incorporation of optical flow. This finding will be of considerable interest to many researchers in the field. The presentation is clear, though the paper feels somewhat dispersed across multiple contributions rather than focused on a single, unifying theme. Ultimately, the results show impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with the current state-of-the-art.

Here are a few suggestions to improve the paper:

1. The primary significance appears to lie in the two-stream network and the incorporation of optical flow; the abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused presentation of this key contribution.
2. The following citations are missing and should be included:
   * Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
   * Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC, 2010.

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions with interesting ideas that complement current research in deep learning. I recommend accepting the paper to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
