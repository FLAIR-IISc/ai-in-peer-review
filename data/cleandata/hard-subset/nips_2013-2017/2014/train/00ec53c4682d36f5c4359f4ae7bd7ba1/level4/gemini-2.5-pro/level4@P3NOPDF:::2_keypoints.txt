- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet architecture that processes spatial and temporal information separately and fuses their predictions for action recognition.  
- Spatial network operates on individual video frames to learn static scene and object appearance.  
- Temporal network is trained on stacked dense optical flow fields to explicitly learn motion patterns.  
- Fusion of the two streams yields final classification, leveraging complementary appearance and motion cues.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, significantly outperforming prior ConvNet-based methods.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, and effective approach for incorporating motion into deep learning for video.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation with detailed ablation studies on temporal stream training choices.  
- Ablation results show clear benefits of using stacked optical flow fields as ConvNet input.  
- Request clarification on why the spatial network outperforms similar single-frame models (e.g., Karpathy et al.), asking about pre-training, augmentation, or optimization differences.  
- Suggests discussing the frame sampling strategy for the spatial stream and analyzing performance sensitivity to different temporal sampling schemes.  
- Conclusion: strong empirical contribution likely to become a new baseline for action recognition; recommends acceptance.
