This paper proposes a novel two-stream convolutional neural network (ConvNet) architecture for human action recognition in videos. The model fuses the predictions from two separate networks: a spatial stream ConvNet trained on individual video frames and a temporal stream ConvNet trained on stacks of dense optical flow fields. On two standard video classification benchmarks, the proposed model significantly outperforms previous ConvNet-based methods and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

While the core idea—applying a standard ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow and fusing it with a single-frame network—is conceptually straightforward, the paper is distinguished by its thorough experimental validation and impressive results. A particular strength is the ablation study on the temporal stream, where the authors compare several methods for training a ConvNet on optical flow. The results clearly demonstrate the significant benefits of using optical flow as an input modality for capturing motion.

I have a few minor comments:
- The authors should clarify why the spatial network performs significantly better than the models from [13], given the architectural similarities.
- The analysis could be further strengthened by a comparison of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.

Overall, this is a strong empirical paper that will be of great interest to researchers working on video classification with convolutional neural networks.
