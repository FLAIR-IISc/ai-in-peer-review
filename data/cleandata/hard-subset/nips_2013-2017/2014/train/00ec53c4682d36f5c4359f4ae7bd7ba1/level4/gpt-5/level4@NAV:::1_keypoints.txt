- Proposes a two-stream convnet: one stream for an appearance frame, one for stacked optical-flow; streams trained separately and averaged for final video prediction; evaluated on two datasets with near state-of-the-art results.
- Strength: simple convolutional-network approach that yields strong performance.
- Strength: shows optical-flow fields are an effective input representation for action recognition, countering prior trends that avoided hand-crafted features.
- Weakness: does not capture long-range temporal structure, operating on fixed-length windows of adjacent frames (L=10).
- Quality: experiments are well executed but lack analysis of failure modes, per-class weaknesses, and types of videos where the method performs poorly.
- Clarity: manuscript is clearly written and the model is well explained.
- Originality: novel use of optical-flow features as convnet input.
- Significance: a straightforward, promising approach that could advance research on video and motion-based vision tasks.
