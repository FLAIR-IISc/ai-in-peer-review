

The paper introduces several novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described comprehensively. The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and presents a compelling case for the proposed approaches through strong empirical results.  

The proposed methods represent genuine novelty. While the work incorporates modifications to the framework in [13], the two-stream approach is particularly compelling, demonstrating that the optical flow component substantially enhances performance—a finding of significant interest to the research community. The paper is well-presented, though it appears fragmented across multiple contributions rather than cohesively unified under a single theme. The results achieve notable improvements over [13] and are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  

Suggestions for improvement:  
1.  Most significance centers on the two-stream architecture and optical flow integration; the abstract and introduction would benefit from a more focused thematic emphasis.  
2.  Missing citations in deep learning:  
    *   Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.  
    *   Missing citations in computer vision:  
    *   Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC 2010.  

The paper offers substantial practical contributions, with ideas that effectively complement core deep learning concepts. I recommend acceptance at NIPS 2014, conditional on the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
