The paper introduces new methods for video classification with deep learning, specifically a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning approach. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly. The manuscript is clear, well written, and the results make a convincing case for the proposed methods.

The contributions are novel. While the work extends the framework of [13], the two-stream design is particularly compelling because incorporating optical flow yields substantial improvements—an insight that will interest many researchers. Although the presentation is clear, the paper feels distributed across multiple contributions rather than organized around a single unifying theme. The results show impressive gains over [13] and are competitive with the state of the art.

Suggestions for improvement:
1. Because the primary impact lies in the two-stream architecture and the use of optical flow, the abstract and introduction should adopt a more focused central theme around this contribution.
2. Missing citations:
   - Deep learning: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis,” CVPR 2011.
   - Computer vision: “Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition,” BMVC 2010.

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions and introduces interesting ideas that complement mainstream deep learning approaches. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, conditional on addressing the suggested revisions.
