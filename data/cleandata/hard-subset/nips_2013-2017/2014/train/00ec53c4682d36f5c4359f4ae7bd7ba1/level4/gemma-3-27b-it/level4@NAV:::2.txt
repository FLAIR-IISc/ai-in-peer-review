This work introduces a novel neural network architecture designed for human action classification in videos. The proposed model leverages a fusion strategy, integrating predictions from two distinct convolutional neural networks: one operating on individual video frames and another trained on short, dense optical flow sequences. The presented results demonstrate substantial improvements over existing ConvNet-based methods for action recognition, achieving performance competitive with the current state-of-the-art on two commonly used video classification benchmarks.

Although the approach fundamentally adapts established ConvNet image classification techniques to the domain of dense optical flow data, and subsequently combines this with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the findings are compelling. The systematic comparison of various ConvNet training strategies applied to optical flow is particularly valuable and clearly illustrates the advantages of utilizing optical flow as input. 

I have a few minor points for consideration:
- The significant performance gain of the spatial network compared to the architectures described in [13], despite their similarities, warrants further discussion.
- Exploring the impact of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream would be a worthwhile extension of this work. 

Overall, this is a strong empirical contribution that will be of considerable interest to researchers in the field of video classification using convolutional neural networks.
