- Proposes a two-stream convolutional network for action recognition: a spatial net on single frames and a temporal net on stacked optical flow, trained separately and fused late.  
- Argues decoupling spatial and temporal streams better captures motion than prior deep models that use stacked frames.  
- Paper is well-organized and the model is clearly explained.  
- Thorough evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 shows competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.  
- Strength: simple yet effective application of convnets to video, achieving strong results.  
- Strength: novel use of optical flow as convnet input, showing hand-crafted features can aid deep models.  
- Strength: highlights the importance of motion information and benefits of separate spatial/temporal streams.  
- Weakness: does not model long-range or whole-video action sequences, focusing on fixed-length adjacent frames.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error-mode analysis (which videos/classes fail and why).  
- Weakness: not evaluated on larger datasets (e.g., Sports-1M), so scalability and robustness are unclear.  
- Quality: experiments are well-designed and use multiple metrics (accuracy and mean accuracy).  
- Clarity: writing and background exposition are clear and accessible.  
- Originality: applying convnets to optical flow and the two-stream architecture are novel contributions.  
- Significance: approach could strongly influence video/motion research and serves as a promising baseline.  
- For acceptance: novel, effective method with unique optical-flow/two-stream design and competitive results.  
- Against acceptance: limited temporal modeling, insufficient error analysis, and untested scalability raise concerns.
