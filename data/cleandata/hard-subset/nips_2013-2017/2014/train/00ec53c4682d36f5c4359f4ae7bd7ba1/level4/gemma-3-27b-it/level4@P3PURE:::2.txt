This paper presents a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos. The model leverages the complementary strengths of two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames (spatial stream) and the other trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images (temporal stream). The proposed approach significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods for action recognition and achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets.

While the work largely applies the established ConvNet image classification pipeline to dense optical flow frames and integrates this with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough and the results are compelling. The systematic comparison of different training strategies for ConvNets applied to optical flow is particularly valuable, clearly demonstrating the benefits of utilizing optical flow as input. 

I have a few minor suggestions for improvement:
- The substantial performance advantage of the spatial stream compared to models reported in [13], despite their architectural similarities, warrants further discussion.
- It would be interesting to see an evaluation of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream to assess their impact on performance. 

Overall, this is a strong empirical paper that will be of considerable interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
