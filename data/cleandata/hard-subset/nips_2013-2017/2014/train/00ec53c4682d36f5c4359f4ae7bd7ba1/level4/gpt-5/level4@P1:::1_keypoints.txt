- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: a spatial stream on RGB frames and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, trained independently and fused by late averaging.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving impressive, near state-of-the-art performance.  
- Clean, straightforward ConvNet application that yields strong results on standard benchmarks.  
- Demonstrates optical flow as an effective input representation for ConvNets, outperforming prior attempts to learn motion from stacked RGB frames.  
- Two-stream decomposition (appearance vs. motion) effectively leverages complementary information.  
- Limitation: processes only fixed-length windows (e.g., L=10), so it cannot capture long-range temporal structure or action ordering across full videos.  
- Experiments convincingly show benefits of the temporal stream and complementarity, but lack analysis of error modes.  
- Missing per-class accuracies, confusion matrices, and qualitative failure cases to identify systematic failures (e.g., heavy camera motion, small/fast motion, clutter).  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, with architecture and temporal input construction explained clearly.  
- Novel contribution: applying a ConvNet directly to optical flow for temporal modeling is new and contrasts with prior stacked-RGB approaches.  
- Likely significant impact on video understanding: a practical, reproducible approach others can build upon.
