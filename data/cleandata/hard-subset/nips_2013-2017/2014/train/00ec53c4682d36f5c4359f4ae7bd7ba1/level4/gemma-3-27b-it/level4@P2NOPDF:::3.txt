This paper introduces novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream network architecture and a multi-task learning approach. The methodology is detailed and clearly explained, and the implementations and experimental procedures are thoroughly described. The writing is clear and concise, and the authors convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed methods through strong experimental results.

The contributions of this work are significant. While building upon the framework presented in [13], the two-stream approach is particularly noteworthy. The demonstrated improvement gained by incorporating optical flow as a separate stream provides valuable insight for the research community, and is a result likely to be of broad interest. However, the paper feels somewhat fragmented, presenting multiple contributions without a strong unifying theme. Despite this, the results achieve substantial improvements over [13] and are competitive with current state-of-the-art performance. 

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following:

1. **Focus the presentation:** The primary significance of the work appears to lie in the two-stream network and the incorporation of optical flow. The abstract and introduction would benefit from a more focused narrative reflecting this core contribution.

2. **Address missing citations:** The authors should acknowledge relevant prior work in the fields of deep learning and computer vision. Specifically, I recommend citing:
    *  "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis." *CVPR*, 2011.
    *  "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition." *BMVC*, 2010. 

Overall, this paper offers substantial practical contributions and presents interesting ideas that complement existing deep learning techniques. I recommend accepting this paper to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements regarding focus and citations.
