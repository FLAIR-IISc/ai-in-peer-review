- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: one network on individual frames (spatial) and one on short sequences of dense optical flow (temporal).
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets, outperforming other ConvNet-based methods.
- Primarily adapts the conventional image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow and fuses it with a single-frame ConvNet.
- Experiments are comprehensive and yield impressive empirical results.
- Thoroughly investigates methods for training a ConvNet on optical flow and clearly shows the benefits of optical flow input.
- Minor revision: clarify why the spatial network outperforms the similar models in [13].
- Minor revision: include a comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream.
- Overall, a well-crafted empirical paper of significant interest to video classification researchers.
