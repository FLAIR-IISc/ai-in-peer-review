This paper presents a novel neural network architecture for classifying videos of human actions, combining the predictions of two convolutional neural networks (ConvNets): one trained on single video frames and the other trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. The proposed model significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches to action recognition and achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets.

While the paper essentially applies the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and combines this with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough, and the results are impressive. It was beneficial to see several different methods of training a ConvNet on optical flow compared, with the results clearly demonstrating the advantages of using optical flow as input.

I have a few minor comments:

- The paper does not provide a clear explanation for why the spatial network performs substantially better than the models from [13], despite their similar architectures.
- A comparison of different sampling schemes for the frames fed into the spatial stream would be an interesting addition, as it could provide further insight into the model's performance.

Overall, this is a good empirical paper that will be of interest to anyone working on video classification with ConvNets.
