This paper proposes deep learning methods for video classification, notably a two-stream architecture that separates appearance and motion (via optical flow) and a multi-task learning setup. The methodology and implementation details are clearly described, and the experimental procedures are sound. Overall, the paper is well written and presents a compelling case for the proposed approaches with strong empirical results.

Novelty and significance:
- The two-stream formulation is the most impactful contribution. The explicit incorporation of optical flow leads to substantial performance gains, a result that will be of broad interest to the community and that advances the understanding of how motion cues complement appearance in deep video models.
- While the work builds on [13], the improvements are meaningful, and the reported results show clear gains over that baseline and competitiveness with the state of the art at the time.
- The multi-task learning component is a useful addition, though its contribution feels secondary to the two-stream insight.

Quality and clarity:
- The paper’s technical presentation is clear, and the methods are described in sufficient detail to enable reproduction.
- Empirical results are well organized and support the central claim that motion (optical flow) substantially improves recognition performance.
- One limitation is that the narrative feels somewhat split across multiple contributions. A tighter thematic focus would strengthen the overall message.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Focus the abstract and introduction around the main takeaway: a two-stream architecture with an optical flow stream delivers significant gains for video classification. Clearly distinguish primary contributions (two-stream with optical flow) from secondary ones (multi-task learning), and describe how they interact.
- Expand the related work to include missing references:
  - Deep learning for spatio-temporal features: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis,” CVPR 2011.
  - Classical spatio-temporal features: “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition,” BMVC 2010.
  These works provide important context on learned and hand-crafted spatio-temporal representations and would position your contributions more precisely.
- If space permits, briefly quantify the relative contributions of each component (appearance stream vs. optical flow stream vs. multi-task learning) to the final performance, to reinforce the paper’s central insights.
- Ensure that comparisons to [13] are matched in terms of training data and evaluation protocol, and clarify any differences in implementation choices that might affect performance.

Overall assessment:
This paper offers significant practical and conceptual contributions to deep video classification. The two-stream design and the demonstration of optical flow’s value are timely and influential ideas, and the results convincingly improve upon [13] while remaining competitive with the state of the art. The paper would be further strengthened by a tighter focus in the abstract/introduction and a more complete related-work section.

Recommendation: Accept (conditional on addressing the suggested improvements).
