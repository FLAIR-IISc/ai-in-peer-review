This paper presents a new neural network architecture for classifying video of human activities. The model combines predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on single video frames and the other trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. This model performs significantly better than other ConvNet-based approaches for action recognition and matches current state-of-the-art technology on two standard video classification datasets.

Although this paper essentially applies the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and combines it with a single frame ConvNet, the experiments are intensive and the results are impressive. It was good to see several different ways to train ConvNet on optical flow, compared to results that clearly demonstrated the benefits of using optical flow as input.

I have a few minor comments:

- It is not clear why it is so much better than models with spatial networks [13], even though the architectures are similar.
- It would be interesting to see comparisons of different sampling schemes for frames fed into the spatial stream. This is a good empirical paper that would be of interest to anyone working on video classification with ConvNets.
