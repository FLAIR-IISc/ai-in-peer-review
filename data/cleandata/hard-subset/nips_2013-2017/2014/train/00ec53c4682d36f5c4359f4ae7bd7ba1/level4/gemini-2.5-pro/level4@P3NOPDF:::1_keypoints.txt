- Two-stream model: spatial ConvNet on frames and temporal ConvNet on stacked optical flow, trained separately and fused for final prediction.  
- Strong empirical performance: competitive or superior results on two standard action recognition benchmarks.  
- Strength — simplicity: elegant, effective architecture that combines appearance and motion cues.  
- Strength — hybrid approach: effectively integrates pre-computed optical flow into a deep learning framework, challenging end-to-end-only assumptions.  
- Weakness — limited temporal modeling: temporal stream uses a short fixed-length stack (L=10) and clip-wise averaging ignores long-range dependencies and sequence order.  
- Experiments are thorough and validate the method, but the paper could benefit from detailed failure-case analysis (per-class errors, cases with subtle motion, camera movement, long actions).  
- Clarity: well-written and reproducible; architecture and experimental setup are clearly explained.  
- Originality: novel application of ConvNets to dense optical flow as a distinct temporal stream for action recognition.  
- Significance: provides a strong, simple baseline likely to influence future video-understanding research and promote hybrid learned/engineered representations.
