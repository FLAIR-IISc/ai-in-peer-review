- Introduces a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture fusing a spatial stream (RGB frames) and a temporal stream (dense optical flow) for video action recognition.
- Uses standard image-classification ConvNet pipelines for both streams and combines their predictions to improve performance.
- Achieves significant gains and state-of-the-art results on two major video classification benchmarks.
- Well-motivated contribution: the effective fusion of streams and the detailed study of training strategies for the temporal (optical flow) stream.
- Experiments are comprehensive and convincingly show the benefit of incorporating optical flow; systematic comparison of ConvNet training for optical flow is insightful.
- Reviewer asks for deeper analysis of why the spatial stream outperforms prior similar models (e.g., differences in data, regularization, optimization) for reproducibility.
- Suggests evaluating sensitivity to frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream (uniform, random, shot-boundary-based) to optimize performance and cost.
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper, clear results and contributions, recommendation for acceptance.
