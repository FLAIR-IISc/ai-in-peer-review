- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that decouples spatial and temporal feature learning for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream processes individual RGB frames to capture appearance cues.  
- Temporal stream processes stacked dense optical flow fields to learn motion patterns.  
- Final classification is obtained by fusing predictions from the two streams.  
- Evaluated thoroughly on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks, showing complementary strengths of the two streams.  
- Combined model is competitive with state-of-the-art and significantly outperforms prior deep learning approaches.  
- Key insight: a ConvNet trained on optical flow alone can outperform one trained on RGB frames.  
- Architecture is effective and elegant, providing a significant conceptual advance over stacked-frame approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including ablations on input length, stacking methods, bi-directional flow, and mean subtraction.  
- Results set a new standard for deep learning in video understanding and influenced subsequent research.  
- Weakness: unclear why the spatial network outperforms Karpathy et al.; claim about data augmentation needs direct experimental support.  
- Weakness: spatial stream input evaluation is limited; more analysis of temporal sampling strategies (number of frames, spacing) would be useful.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-motivated empirical work with minor issues; strong recommendation for acceptance.
