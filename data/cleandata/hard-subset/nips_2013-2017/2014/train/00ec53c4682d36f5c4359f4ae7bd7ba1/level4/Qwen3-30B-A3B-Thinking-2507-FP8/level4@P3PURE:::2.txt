

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture for classifying human actions in videos. The model integrates predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames and another trained on short sequences of dense optical flow. It significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based action recognition methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on two standard video classification benchmarks.

While the approach adapts the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to dense optical flow frames and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is thorough and the results are compelling. The authors' exploration of multiple training strategies for ConvNets on optical flow, with results clearly demonstrating its benefits, is particularly valuable.

The following minor points should be addressed:  
- The spatial network's substantial performance advantage over the models in [13] is not explained, despite architectural similarities.  
- A comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream would provide useful insights.  

This strong empirical contribution will be of significant interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
