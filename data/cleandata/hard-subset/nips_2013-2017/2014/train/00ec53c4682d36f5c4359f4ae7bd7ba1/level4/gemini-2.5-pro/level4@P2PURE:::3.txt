This paper proposes novel deep learning methods for video classification, most notably a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning approach. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described in sufficient detail. The paper is well-written and makes a compelling case for the proposed methods through strong experimental results.

The proposed methods are novel. While the work builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream architecture is a particularly interesting contribution. The demonstration that the optical flow stream yields significant improvements is an important finding that will be of broad interest to the community. A minor criticism is that the paper presents several distinct contributions, which slightly dilutes its central message. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with the state-of-the-art.

Suggestions to improve the paper:
1.  Given that the primary significance lies in the two-stream approach and the use of optical flow, the abstract and introduction could be revised to better reflect this as the central theme.
2.  The authors have omitted some relevant citations:
    *   Deep Learning: Le, Q. V., et al. "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis." CVPR 2011.
    *   Computer Vision: Wang, H., et al. "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition." BMVC 2010.

Overall, this paper offers significant practical contributions and interesting ideas that complement existing deep learning approaches for video analysis. I recommend that the paper be accepted to NIPS 2014, conditional on the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
