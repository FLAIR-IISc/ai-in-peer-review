- Proposes a two-stream convolutional model: one network on appearance frames and one on stacked dense optical flow, trained independently and fused by a weighted average for final video prediction.  
- Evaluated on two datasets, achieving results that match or approach state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.  
- Strength: simple, straightforward application of convnets that yields strong performance.  
- Strength: demonstrates that optical flow fields are a valuable input representation for CNNs in action recognition.  
- Weakness: only processes fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames and does not model full-video temporal sequence.  
- Experiments are solid but lack error-mode analysis—no per-class or failure-case characterization.  
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well-described.  
- Originality: applying CNNs to optical flow is an innovative contribution.  
- Significance: simple, effective approach with potential to influence video/motion research.
