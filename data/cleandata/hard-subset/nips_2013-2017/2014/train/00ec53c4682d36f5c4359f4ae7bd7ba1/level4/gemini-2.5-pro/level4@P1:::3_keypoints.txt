- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture separating spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing.  
- Spatial stream: ConvNet on individual frames to learn appearance features.  
- Temporal stream: ConvNet trained on dense optical flow fields to learn motion features.  
- Explicit modeling of motion via optical flow yields strong performance, even with relatively small datasets.  
- Introduces a multi-task learning framework to effectively combine datasets (UCF-101 and HMDB-51) for training.  
- Methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described in thorough detail.  
- Evaluated on standard benchmarks, showing significant improvements over prior deep learning methods and competitive state-of-the-art results.  
- Contributions (two-stream architecture and ConvNets on optical flow) are novel, well-motivated, and of high interest to the community.  
- Key finding: pre-computed optical flow as input to a temporal ConvNet substantially outperforms learning motion from raw stacked frames.  
- Experimental evaluation includes detailed ablation studies that validate design choices for the temporal stream.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with thoughtful discussion relating temporal ConvNet to hand-crafted features (HOF, MBH).  
- Presentation is slightly diffused by multiple distinct contributions; abstract/introduction should be tightened to focus on separating spatial and temporal modeling.  
- Literature review is incomplete; should cite and discuss Le et al. (CVPR 2011) and Wang et al. (BMVC 2010).  
- Strengths summarized: novel architecture, strong empirical results, clear insight on motion representation, and thorough evaluation.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: sharpen focus in presentation and add missing relevant citations.  
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014), conditional on addressing minor focus and related-work concerns.
