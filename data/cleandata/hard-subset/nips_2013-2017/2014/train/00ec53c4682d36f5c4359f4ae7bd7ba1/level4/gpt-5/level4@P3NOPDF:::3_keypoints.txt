- Proposes deep video classification methods: a two-stream architecture (appearance + optical flow) and a multi-task learning setup.
- Methodology and implementation are clearly described, with sound experimental procedures.
- Two-stream formulation is the most impactful contribution; incorporating optical flow yields substantial performance gains.
- Results advance understanding of how motion cues complement appearance in deep video models and are of broad interest.
- Builds on [13] but provides meaningful improvements and competitive state-of-the-art results for the time.
- Multi-task learning is a useful but secondary contribution compared to the two-stream insight.
- Technical presentation is clear and sufficiently detailed to enable reproduction.
- Empirical results are well organized and support the central claim that optical flow improves recognition.
- Narrative feels split across multiple contributions; a tighter thematic focus would strengthen the message.
- Suggest focusing abstract/introduction on the two-stream/optical flow main takeaway and clearly distinguishing primary vs. secondary contributions.
- Suggest expanding related work to include missing spatio-temporal references (CVPR 2011; BMVC 2010) for proper context.
- Recommend quantifying the relative contributions of appearance, optical flow, and multi-task components to final performance.
- Ensure comparisons to [13] match training data and evaluation protocol and clarify any implementation differences.
- Overall: significant practical and conceptual contributions, timely and influential; recommendation to accept conditional on addressing suggested improvements.
