Paper introduces a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining spatial (single frames) and temporal (dense optical flow sequences) networks for action recognition.  
Model significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on two standard video datasets.  
Provides a comprehensive literature overview, clearly highlighting challenges and limitations of prior video-action approaches.  
Two-stream design captures complementary appearance and motion information, effectively generalizing top hand-crafted features within a data-driven framework.  
Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are impressive; multi-task learning is used to augment training data and boost performance.  
Minor unclear point: why the spatial network outperforms a similar architecture reported in [14].  
Suggested additional analysis: compare different frame sampling schemes and selection strategies for the spatial stream.  
Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical results, multi-task learning contribution, and solid literature framing.  
Arguments against acceptance: contributions may be incremental, ConvNet comparisons could be broader, and a few clarifications (e.g., comparison with [14]) are needed.
