- Introduces a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos using a fusion of two ConvNets.  
- Fusion integrates a spatial ConvNet on individual frames with a temporal ConvNet on short, dense optical flow sequences.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvements over existing ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on two video benchmarks.  
- Adapts established image ConvNet techniques to dense optical flow data and combines them with a single-frame ConvNet.  
- Provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation with compelling findings.  
- Systematically compares various ConvNet training strategies for optical flow, highlighting their benefits.  
- Clearly illustrates the advantages of using optical flow as input for action recognition.  
- Minor concern: the large performance gain of the spatial network over similar architectures in [13] needs further discussion.  
- Suggestion: explore the impact of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: a strong empirical contribution of high interest to video classification researchers.
