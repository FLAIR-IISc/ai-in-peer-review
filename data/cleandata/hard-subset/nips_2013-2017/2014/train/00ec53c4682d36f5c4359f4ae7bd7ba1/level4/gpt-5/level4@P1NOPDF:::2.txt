This paper proposes a new neural network architecture for classifying videos of human actions. The model fuses the predictions of two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames and another trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. It significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods for action recognition and matches the current state of the art on two standard video classification datasets.

Although the approach primarily adapts the standard ConvNet image-classification pipeline to a different modality (dense optical flow) and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough and the results are compelling. I appreciated the systematic comparison of several training strategies for the optical-flow ConvNet; the results clearly demonstrate the value of using optical flow as input.

I have only a few minor comments:
- It is not clear why the spatial network outperforms the models in [13], given that the architectures are similar.
- It would be informative to compare different frame-sampling schemes for the inputs to the spatial stream.

Overall, this is a strong empirical paper that will be of interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
