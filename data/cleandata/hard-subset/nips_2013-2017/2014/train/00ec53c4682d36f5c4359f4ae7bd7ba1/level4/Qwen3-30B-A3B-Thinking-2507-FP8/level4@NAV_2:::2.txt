

This paper introduces a two-stream convolutional neural network architecture for action recognition in videos, combining a spatial stream trained on single frames with a temporal stream trained on dense optical flow. The authors demonstrate that their approach significantly outperforms prior ConvNet-based methods for video classification and achieves competitive results with the state of the art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks. While the core approach adapts standard ConvNet image classification techniques to a novel data type (optical flow), the thorough experimental evaluation clearly establishes the benefits of using motion information explicitly represented through optical flow. The paper provides a comprehensive comparison of different optical flow input configurations, revealing that stacking multiple frames of optical flow (L=10) and mean flow subtraction substantially improve performance. The authors also effectively leverage multi-task learning to combine datasets, demonstrating significant gains on smaller datasets like HMDB-51. I found the experimental design and results to be particularly strong, with clear evidence supporting the complementary nature of the spatial and temporal streams. I have two minor suggestions for improvement: 1) The paper would benefit from a more detailed explanation of why the spatial stream (trained on ImageNet) outperforms the results reported in [14] for similar architectures, as this is a key comparative point; 2) It would be valuable to include an analysis of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream, as this could provide additional insights into how to optimally leverage frame information.
