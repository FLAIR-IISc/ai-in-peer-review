- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture that decouples spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream uses multi-frame dense optical flow as ConvNet input, effectively modeling explicit motion.  
- Demonstrates that discriminatively trained ConvNets on optical flow outperform previous attempts using stacked raw frames.  
- Shows multi-task learning across datasets improves performance in data-limited settings.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive results and surpassing prior deep-learning video classifiers.  
- Experimental setup is thorough with ablation studies (input configurations, multi-task learning) that strengthen analysis.  
- Detailed implementation notes and GPU training procedures are provided and commendable.  
- Paper is clearly presented and technically sound, making a valuable contribution to video action recognition.  
- Lacks a focused, unifying narrative; the three contributions feel somewhat fragmented.  
- Related work is comprehensive but missing a few relevant citations (Le et al. CVPR 2011; Wang et al. BMVC 2010).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel architecture, significant performance gains, competitive with hand-crafted features, and solid experiments.  
- Rejection arguments: fragmented presentation and incomplete related work coverage.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to improve narrative focus and add missing citations.
