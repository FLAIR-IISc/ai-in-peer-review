- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: a spatial RGB stream and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused at score level; temporal stream improved via multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies on flow stacking length, trajectory vs. grid sampling, bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction; achieves accuracy competitive with hand-crafted pipelines and better than prior deep RGB-frame models.  
- Positions method relative to dense-trajectory/MBH/HOF pipelines and prior deep or HMAX-style two-stream work; explicitly separating appearance and motion is principled and effective.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with detailed implementation, training protocol, data augmentation, and multi-GPU optimization for reproducibility.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence—extensive ablations, consistent gains from multi-task learning, and complementary improvements from fusing streams.  
- Strength: Conceptual clarity—the two-stream design directly addresses motion vs. appearance and aligns with vision literature.  
- Strength: Practical impact—explicit optical flow input outperforms stacked RGB under limited data, and pre-training/multi-tasking act as effective regularizers.  
- Weakness: Paper feels like multiple contributions (architecture, flow stacking, multi-tasking, fusion) rather than a single unified message; abstract/introduction should be sharpened to foreground the two-stream optical-flow idea.  
- Weakness: Related work coverage should be strengthened, with clearer discussion/distinction from key prior spatio-temporal learning papers (e.g., ISA CVPR 2011, BMVC 2010).  
- Weakness: Limitations are under-discussed—reliance on precomputed optical flow, compute and storage overhead, sensitivity to flow quality, and scalability to web-scale data need explicit treatment.  
- Quality (NIPS criteria): Methodologically sound with careful ablations and standard benchmarks; would benefit from a brief error analysis and failure-mode discussion.  
- Clarity (NIPS criteria): Generally clear and reproducible, but the narrative could be more cohesive by emphasizing the central contribution earlier.  
- Originality (NIPS criteria): Meaningful advance—optical-flow-based temporal stream plus multi-task learning extends prior deep models that relied on stacked RGB.  
- Significance (NIPS criteria): Results are competitive and likely to influence the community; design choices (flow stacking, mean subtraction, multi-tasking) are impactful.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical gains, compelling architectural idea, solid ablations and training insights, and high reproducibility/impact potential.  
- Cons against acceptance: Diffuse contribution narrative, incomplete related work discussion, and insufficient discussion of optical-flow computational trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on tightening the abstract/introduction to emphasize the two-stream optical-flow contribution, strengthening related-work distinctions, and adding a brief discussion of limitations and computational trade-offs.
