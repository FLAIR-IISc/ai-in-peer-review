This paper proposes a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos, leveraging the strengths of two convolutional neural networks. One network is trained on individual video frames, while the other is trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images, resulting in a model that outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches and achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets.

Although the paper primarily adapts the conventional ConvNet image classification pipeline to accommodate dense optical flow frames and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are comprehensive and yield impressive results. The thorough investigation of various methods for training a ConvNet on optical flow, along with the clear demonstration of the benefits of using optical flow as input, is a notable strength of the paper.

A few minor suggestions for improvement are:

- Further clarification is needed to explain why the spatial network outperforms the models from [13], given the similarities in their architectures.
- A comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream would provide additional insight. Overall, this is a well-crafted empirical paper that will be of significant interest to researchers working on video classification using ConvNets.
