This paper proposes novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically introducing a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning approach. The methodology, implementations, and procedures are described in good detail, making the paper clear and well-written. The authors present a compelling case for their proposed methods by demonstrating good results.

The methods presented in this paper are novel and build upon the framework established in [13]. The two-stream framework is particularly noteworthy, as it highlights the significant improvements gained by incorporating the optical flow component. This finding is likely to be of great interest to many researchers in the field. While the paper is well-presented, it appears to be divided across multiple contributions rather than focusing on a single theme. Nevertheless, the results show impressive improvements upon [13] and are competitive with the state-of-the-art.

To further enhance the paper, the authors may consider the following suggestions:

1. The abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused theme, as the majority of the paper's significance is concentrated on the two-stream approach and the incorporation of optical flow.

2. The authors may want to include additional citations in the deep learning and computer vision literature, specifically:
- Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis (CVPR 2011)
- Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition (BMVC, 2010)

This paper offers significant practical contributions and presents interesting ideas that complement the main concepts in deep learning. Based on its merits, I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
