- Proposes a novel deep-learning approach to video classification using a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning.  
- Two-stream framework, especially incorporating optical flow, significantly improves performance and is an important contribution.  
- Methodology, implementation, and procedures are clearly described and well-written.  
- Builds on framework of [13] but offers distinct and novel contributions.  
- Experimental results show impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Paper feels split across multiple contributions rather than focused on a single theme.  
- Suggest refining the abstract and introduction to emphasize the two-stream formulation and optical flow.  
- Missing important citations in deep learning and computer vision (e.g., CVPR 2011 ISA paper; BMVC 2010 spatio-temporal features paper).  
- Technically sound; claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for expert reproduction of results.  
- Originality is strong: novel combination of techniques and clear differentiation from prior work.  
- Significance is high: advances the state of the art and likely to be adopted or built upon.  
- Recommendation: accept at NIPS 2014 conditional on addressing focus and citation concerns.
