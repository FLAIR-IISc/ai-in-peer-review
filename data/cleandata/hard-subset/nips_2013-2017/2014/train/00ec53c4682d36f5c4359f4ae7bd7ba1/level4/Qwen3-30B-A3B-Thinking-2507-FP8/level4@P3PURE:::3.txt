

The paper introduces novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning framework. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described with sufficient clarity and precision. The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and presents a compelling case for the proposed approaches through strong empirical results.  

The contributions are novel. While the work builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream formulation is notably insightful, demonstrating that incorporating optical flow yields significant performance gains—a result of broad interest to the research community. The paper achieves impressive improvements over [13] and demonstrates competitive results against state-of-the-art methods. However, the presentation occasionally appears fragmented, as it integrates multiple contributions without a unified central theme.  

To strengthen the paper, the following revisions are recommended:  
1. The abstract and introduction should focus more cohesively on the core contribution (the two-stream formulation with optical flow), as this represents the primary source of significance.  
2. Key prior work is omitted:  
   - *Deep learning*: "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011).  
   - *Computer vision*: "Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition" (BMVC 2010).  

Despite these minor issues, the paper offers meaningful practical contributions and complementary insights to deep learning research. I recommend acceptance at NIPS 2014, subject to the authors addressing the suggested revisions.
