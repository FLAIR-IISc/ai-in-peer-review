

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture for classifying human actions in videos. The model integrates predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames and another trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. It significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based action recognition methods and achieves the current state-of-the-art results on two standard video classification datasets.  

While the approach essentially adapts the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to dense optical flow frames and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the results are compelling. The paper effectively demonstrates multiple training strategies for ConvNets on optical flow, with clear empirical evidence supporting the benefits of optical flow as input.  

I have only two minor comments:  
- The reason for the spatial network's superior performance compared to the models in [13] is not clearly explained, despite similar architectures.  
- A comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream would be valuable.  

This is a strong empirical contribution that will be of significant interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
