- Proposes a two-stream convolutional model: one network for single-frame appearance, another for stacked optical flow, combined by averaging or SVM.
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive, near state-of-the-art results without heavy hand-crafted features.
- Strength: simple, straightforward CNN application that nonetheless yields strong performance.
- Strength: demonstrates optical flow as a valuable input representation for action recognition.
- Strength: multi-task learning across datasets improves performance and generalization.
- Weakness: uses a fixed-length window (L=10) and does not model long-term temporal dependencies across full videos.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of failure cases and types of actions the model misclassifies.
- Quality: experiments are well-designed and thorough but would benefit from error-mode analysis.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and well-organized; model explanation is accessible.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow and a two-stream spatial/temporal decomposition.
- Significance: approach is simple yet promising, with potential broad impact on video understanding and motion-based vision.
- Argument for acceptance: novel and effective deep-learning application to action recognition with strong results.
- Argument for acceptance: clear presentation and relevance to an important computer vision problem.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient error analysis and limited temporal modeling capability.
