This paper introduces novel deep learning methods for video classification, specifically employing a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning approach. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly and the writing is clear and well-organized, effectively demonstrating the efficacy of the proposed techniques.

The presented work offers a valuable contribution, building upon the framework of [13] while uniquely highlighting the substantial benefits of incorporating an optical flow component within the two-stream architecture – a finding likely to be of considerable interest to the research community. While the paper is well-presented, the multiple contributions feel somewhat disjointed and could benefit from a more unified central theme. The results demonstrate significant improvements over [13] and achieve performance competitive with current state-of-the-art methods. 

To further strengthen the paper, I recommend the following:

1. Given the strong impact of the two-stream approach and the utilization of optical flow, consider refining the abstract and introduction to emphasize this primary contribution and provide a more focused narrative.
2. Please include citations to relevant prior work, specifically: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis” (CVPR 2011) in the deep learning context, and “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition” (BMVC, 2010) within computer vision.

Overall, this paper presents significant practical contributions and introduces compelling ideas that complement existing deep learning approaches. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
