- Introduces a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture for video action recognition combining spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked dense optical flow) streams.  
- Spatial stream learns appearance-based features from individual video frames.  
- Temporal stream models motion by training on stacked dense optical flow fields.  
- Fusion of the two streams significantly outperforms prior ConvNet-based methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on two benchmark datasets.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrating that ConvNets trained on dense optical flow effectively capture motion and complement spatial ConvNets for powerful video classification.  
- Strong experimental support, including a thorough ablation study showing the benefits of optical flow as an input representation.  
- Minor comment: authors should provide insight into why the spatial stream outperforms architecturally similar models from reference [13] (e.g., pre-training, augmentation, implementation differences).  
- Minor suggestion: include discussion or analysis of alternative frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream to justify design choices.  
- Overall verdict: excellent empirical paper with compelling results and high relevance to video classification and deep learning communities.
