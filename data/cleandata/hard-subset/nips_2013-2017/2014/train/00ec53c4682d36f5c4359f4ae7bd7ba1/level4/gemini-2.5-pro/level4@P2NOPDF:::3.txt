This paper introduces a novel deep learning framework for video classification, centered around two primary contributions: a two-stream convolutional network architecture and a multi-task learning approach. The two-stream model ingeniously processes spatial and temporal information separately, with one network trained on static video frames for appearance and another on pre-computed optical flow fields for motion. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described with sufficient clarity to ensure reproducibility. The paper is well-written and presents a compelling case for the proposed methods, substantiated by strong empirical results.

**Strengths**

1.  **Novelty of the Two-Stream Architecture:** The core contribution, the two-stream framework, is both novel and insightful. The explicit modeling of temporal information through a dedicated optical flow stream is a compelling idea that effectively addresses a key challenge in video understanding. The paper convincingly demonstrates that this temporal component provides a substantial performance boost over models that rely solely on spatial appearance. This finding is of significant interest to the broader computer vision and deep learning communities.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results are impressive. The proposed methods achieve marked improvements over the baseline framework of [13] and deliver performance that is highly competitive with other state-of-the-art techniques on standard action recognition benchmarks. This thorough empirical validation effectively demonstrates the efficacy of the proposed architecture.

3.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is clearly structured and well-written. The authors provide a detailed description of their methodology and implementation, which should facilitate the replication of their work by other researchers.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

1.  **Clarity of Focus:** While the paper presents several interesting ideas, its central narrative could be strengthened. The primary and most impactful contribution appears to be the two-stream architecture incorporating optical flow. However, the abstract and introduction give considerable weight to the multi-task learning aspect, which can make the paper feel somewhat divided between two distinct themes. It is suggested that the authors revise the abstract and introduction to more clearly frame the two-stream network as the main contribution, with multi-task learning presented as a complementary technique for further improvement. This would create a more cohesive and impactful narrative.

2.  **Missing Citations:** The literature review is generally thorough, but could be improved by including the following relevant works, which explore related concepts in spatio-temporal feature learning and evaluation:
    *   Le, Q. V., et al. "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis." CVPR 2011.
    *   Wang, H., et al. "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition." BMVC 2010.

**Recommendation**

Overall, this paper makes a significant practical and conceptual contribution to the field of video understanding. The ideas presented are innovative, the empirical validation is thorough, and the findings are likely to be influential.

I recommend that this paper be accepted at NIPS 2014. My recommendation is conditional on the authors addressing the suggestions for improvement outlined above, particularly regarding the narrative focus and the inclusion of the missing citations. These minor revisions will further strengthen the manuscript and enhance its impact.
