- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture combining spatial and temporal networks for video action recognition.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with a thorough related-work review.  
- Technical content is sound with a clear explanation of the architecture and components.  
- Thorough evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 datasets.  
- Demonstrates strong results that outperform state-of-the-art hand-crafted and other deep learning methods.  
- Effectively generalizes hand-crafted features (HOG, HOF) using ConvNets.  
- Provides analysis of input configurations (optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking) for the temporal ConvNet.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to increase effective training data and improve temporal ConvNet performance.  
- Minor issue: unclear description of multi-GPU training implementation.  
- Suggestion: would benefit from more detailed analysis of input-configuration impacts.  
- Paper is original and significant with practical applications (surveillance, HCI, video analysis).  
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS with minor revisions.
