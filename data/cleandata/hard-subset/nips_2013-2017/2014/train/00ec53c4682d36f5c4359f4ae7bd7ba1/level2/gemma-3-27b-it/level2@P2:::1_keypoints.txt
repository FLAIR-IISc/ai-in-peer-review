- Proposes a two-stream CNN separating spatial and temporal processing for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream uses multi-frame dense optical flow and multi-task learning to improve performance.  
- Demonstrates competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep-learning video methods.  
- Builds on resurgence of deep learning for video and addresses limitations of applying CNNs to raw frame stacks.  
- Briefly links architecture to ventral/dorsal stream neurobiological hypothesis.

Strengths:
- Clear, well-motivated decoupling of spatial and temporal cues allowing specialization and use of ImageNet pretraining.  
- Convincing evidence that ConvNets trained on multi-frame dense optical flow perform well with limited data.  
- Multi-task learning effectively increases training data and boosts performance.  
- Strong empirical results with detailed ablation studies on temporal inputs and fusion methods.  
- Comprehensive implementation details enhance reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Core CNN architecture (based on CNN-M-2048) is not novel; main contribution is the two-stream application.  
- Optical flow computation is computationally expensive and may limit scalability or real-time use.  
- Limited exploration of richer temporal modeling (e.g., RNNs or 3D convolutions) beyond stacking frames.  
- Dorsal/ventral stream analogy is mentioned but not deeply explored or leveraged architecturally.  
- Heavy dependence on dense optical flow risks failure in challenging scenarios (fast motion, occlusions).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Sound technical approach with clear motivation and strong empirical validation.  
- Two-stream architecture significantly improves over prior direct-frame CNN attempts.  
- Competitive state-of-the-art results validate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Well-written, organized, and reproducible.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited novelty in the CNN backbone architecture.  
- Reliance on dense optical flow could be a practical limitation.  
- Insufficient exploration of alternative temporal modeling techniques.

Overall assessment:
- Strong contribution to video action recognition; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Expand discussion of the dorsal/ventral stream analogy and neurobiological inspirations.  
- Discuss limitations of relying on dense optical flow and possible alternatives.  
- Address computational cost of optical flow and impact on scalability.  
- Suggest exploring more sophisticated temporal models (RNNs, 3D convs) in future work.
