- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition: a spatial stream for appearance and a temporal stream trained on stacked dense optical flow.  
- Spatial stream is ImageNet-pretrained; temporal stream is the main novelty and captures motion from optical flow stacks.  
- Final predictions are obtained by late fusion (score averaging or SVM) of the two streams.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited video data.  
- High technical quality with comprehensive experiments on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 that substantiate the claims.  
- Thorough ablation studies validate design choices (pre-training benefits, flow stacking, mean subtraction, etc.).  
- Multi-task learning shows clear benefit over simpler data augmentation strategies.  
- Exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, with detailed implementation info enabling reproducibility.  
- Good contextualization: relates temporal ConvNet to hand-crafted features (HOF, MBH) and prior work.  
- Highly original: first effective discriminative deep ConvNet separation of appearance and motion using stacked optical flow.  
- Novel multi-task setup for combining action datasets is practical and useful.  
- Significant impact: narrows gap between deep models and hand-crafted-feature methods, providing an influential baseline.  
- Strength: simple yet powerful two-stream design that effectively separates appearance and motion.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.  
- Strength: extensive evaluations and ablations that convincingly support contributions.  
- Weakness: requires pre-computing dense optical flow—computationally expensive and storage-intensive, not end-to-end.  
- Weakness: simple late fusion limits learning of joint spatio-temporal features and may overfit if fused earlier.  
- Weakness: performance depends on quality of off-the-shelf optical flow; paper lacks analysis of this dependency.  
- Suggestion: discuss why fine-tuning the full spatial network gives only marginal gains over last-layer tuning.  
- Suggestion: provide discussion on computational trade-offs of optical flow pre-computation versus training a larger end-to-end network.  
- Suggestion: visualize deeper temporal-stream filters to give insight into learned motion patterns.
