- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: spatial stream (ImageNet-pretrained frames) and temporal stream (dense optical flow) with late fusion.  
- Demonstrates optical flow–based temporal stream significantly outperforms stacked RGB approaches.  
- Introduces multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate small dataset limitations.  
- Shows the two streams are complementary and together achieve state-of-the-art results on benchmarks (e.g., 81.5% on UCF-101).  
- Situates the approach in prior work and neuroscience (two-streams hypothesis), contrasting with stacked-frame and shallow hand-crafted methods.  
- Temporal stream design generalizes hand-crafted features (HOF, MBH, trajectories) and visualizes first-layer filters to support this.  
- Experiments are thorough with clear ablation studies (flow stacking length, mean flow subtraction, multi-task learning).  
- Implementation details (flow compression, multi-GPU training) demonstrate practical rigor.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with helpful figures and precise input configurations.  
- The two-stream design is novel and significant, shifting paradigm from stacked-frame learning to explicit motion encoding.  
- Multi-task dataset training is a practical, impactful solution to limited labeled video data.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis explaining why optical flow outperforms stacked RGB beyond stating it describes motion.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on why the model competes with shallow methods despite smaller datasets and on scalability.  
- Weakness: trajectory stacking is evaluated but not deeply justified despite underperforming optical flow stacking.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful work with robust experimental evidence and a recommendation to accept.
