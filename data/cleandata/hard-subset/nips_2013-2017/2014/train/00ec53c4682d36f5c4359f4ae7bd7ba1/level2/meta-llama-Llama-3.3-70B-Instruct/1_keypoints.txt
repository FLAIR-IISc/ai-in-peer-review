- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet combining spatial and temporal networks for video action recognition.
- Shows a ConvNet trained on multi-frame dense optical flow effectively captures motion and performs well despite limited data.
- Uses multi-task learning to increase training data and improve performance across datasets.
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks and is competitive with the state of the art.
- Strength: two-stream architecture is novel and effectively captures complementary appearance and motion information.
- Strength: multi-frame dense optical flow input outperforms other motion representations tested.
- Strength: thorough evaluation on two benchmark datasets with clear, well-written presentation.
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of computational cost, raising concerns for real-time use.
- Weakness: does not explore alternative input representations (e.g., 3D CNNs) that might capture more video information.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to contextualize performance.
- For acceptance: novel, effective approach with solid empirical evaluation and clear exposition.
- Against acceptance: missing computational-cost analysis, limited input representation exploration, and insufficient SOTA comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but revise to address computational cost, include more comparisons, and consider other input modalities.
