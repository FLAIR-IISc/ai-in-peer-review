- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet separating appearance (RGB spatial stream) and motion (temporal stream on stacked optical flow), with mean flow subtraction and options like trajectory vs optical-flow stacking and bi-directional flow.  
- Fuses streams by averaging softmax scores or training a linear SVM; uses multitask training to share ConvNet and use dataset-specific heads for UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Positions work relative to dense trajectory+Fisher-vector methods, earlier raw-frame deep models, and two-stream HMAX-like models, arguing the temporal ConvNet generalizes HOF/MBH.  
- Methodology is sound with thorough ablations (stack length, mean subtraction, stacking variants, bi-directional flow) and detailed training/test protocols; not fully end-to-end due to precomputed flow.  
- Paper is well organized, choices well motivated, and implementation/testing details are sufficient for reproduction; relation to prior shallow features is insightful.  
- Originality: explicit optical-flow ConvNet two-stream decomposition and simple effective temporal input encodings are novel and impactful in the 2014 context; multitask learning is sensible and useful.  
- Significance: effectively addresses motion modeling, closes much of gap to trajectory methods, outperforms prior deep baselines, and provides important insights favoring explicit flow over raw frames.  
- Pro: Clear, simple two-stream design with large empirical gains on UCF-101/HMDB-51.  
- Pro: Thorough ablations demonstrate value of stacked optical flow and mean subtraction and compare well to multi-frame RGB models.  
- Pro: Multitask learning leverages multiple datasets to improve generalization under limited data.  
- Pro: Comprehensive training details (architecture, schedules, augmentations, crops) aid reproducibility.  
- Pro: Convincing conceptual link between temporal ConvNet features and classical motion descriptors.  
- Con: Reliance on precomputed optical flow prevents end-to-end training and adds computational/storage overhead; sensitivity to flow choice is unevaluated.  
- Con: Fusion is limited to late score fusion; attempts at joint fusion overfit and no stronger regularization strategies are explored.  
- Con: Camera motion compensation is rudimentary (mean flow subtraction); stronger stabilization methods are not tested.  
- Con: Limited analysis of temporal scale—fixed L=10 and dense sampling with little exploration of longer-range or multi-scale temporal modeling.  
- Con: Multitask training details lack clarity on handling overlapping classes and potential test leakage when combining splits.  
- Q/S: How sensitive is performance to the optical flow algorithm and its parameters; have alternatives (e.g., TV-L1) or faster/coarser flows been tried?  
- Q/S: What is the effect of flow quantization (0–255 JPEG-like encoding) on accuracy?  
- Q/S: Would stronger global camera motion compensation (e.g., homography) improve results?  
- Q/S: For SVM late fusion, how is overfitting avoided when training scores on data used for stream training—please clarify validation protocol.  
- Q/S: Could stronger regularization (dropout, batch norm, weight decay) enable a small joint fusion MLP without overfitting?  
- Q/S: Please provide per-class gains from temporal vs spatial streams and an error analysis to reveal failure modes.  
- Q/S: In multitask learning, how are batches balanced between datasets and how are overlapping classes handled to prevent leakage?  
- Overall: Strong, timely contribution with clear ideas, careful experiments, and likely impact; recommend acceptance despite limitations from precomputed flow.
