- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: spatial stream for frames, temporal stream for multi-frame dense optical flow.
- Shows ConvNets trained on optical flow perform strongly despite limited training data.
- Demonstrates multitask learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 improves performance.
- Thorough experimental evaluation on standard benchmarks with clear comparisons to prior work.
- Two-stream approach significantly outperforms prior deep learning attempts (~20% over [14]) and is competitive with hand-crafted methods.
- Temporal stream’s optical flow input effectively generalizes traditional motion descriptors like HOF and MBH.
- Multitask learning successfully leverages limited video data to boost accuracy.
- Implementation and training details (optical flow computation, protocols) are sufficiently comprehensive for reproducibility.
- Strengths: well-motivated architecture, strong empirical results, and clear demonstration of complementary spatial/temporal information.
- Acknowledged limitation: misses certain aspects of state-of-the-art shallow representations such as trajectory-based pooling.
- Weakness: limited exploration of the temporal stream’s relationship to the full trajectory-based pipeline.
- Weakness: brief analysis of why optical flow input outperforms stacked-frame approaches; more detailed investigation would help.
