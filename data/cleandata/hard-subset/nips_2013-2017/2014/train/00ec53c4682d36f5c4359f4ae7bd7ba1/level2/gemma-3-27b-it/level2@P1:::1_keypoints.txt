- Proposes a two-stream CNN that decouples spatial and temporal information for video action recognition.  
- Trains a CNN on multi-frame dense optical flow to capture motion, and uses multi-task learning to improve generalization.  
- Achieves strong, competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep learning approaches.  
- Novel architecture effectively leverages CNN strengths for still images and extends them to video via optical flow.  
- Demonstrates that training on dense optical flow is a powerful method for motion representation.  
- Multi-task learning is used practically to mitigate limited labeled video data.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and positions the work well within related literature.  
- Technical details and experimental setup are concise, thorough, and well-analyzed.  
- Major strength: substantial empirical performance gains and clear validation on benchmarks.  
- Major weakness: computational cost and potential errors from optical flow estimation are not deeply analyzed.  
- Limited ablation study leaves some design choices (fusion methods, layer contributions) insufficiently validated.  
- Sensitivity to camera/global motion is a concern; mean subtraction may be insufficient for compensation.  
- Lacks qualitative visualizations of learned features and examples of successes/failures to provide deeper insight.  
- Paper is somewhat dated relative to newer methods (3D CNNs, Transformers) and could briefly acknowledge subsequent advances.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with recommendation for acceptance despite some minor limitations.
