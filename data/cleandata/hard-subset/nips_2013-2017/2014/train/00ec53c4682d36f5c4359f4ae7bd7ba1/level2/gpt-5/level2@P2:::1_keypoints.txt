- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video: spatial stream on RGB (ImageNet-pretrained) and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused by late score combination.  
- Introduces multi-task learning with separate softmax heads for UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited video data, improving both datasets.  
- Evaluated design choices for temporal input: flow stacking vs. trajectory stacking, uni- vs. bi-directional flow, and mean flow subtraction.  
- Shows large gains over prior deep stacked-RGB models and is competitive with hand-crafted dense trajectory + Fisher vector pipelines.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated architecture aligning with appearance vs. motion separation and two-stream cognitive hypothesis.  
- Strength: convincing ablations demonstrating benefits of multiple stacked flows, mean flow subtraction, and optical flow stacking.  
- Strength: sensible spatial pretraining and an effective multi-task formulation that increases supervision for the temporal stream.  
- Strength: thorough training/testing details and fair evaluation on standard splits.  
- Strength: practical engineering (multi-GPU training, compressed flow storage) making the method feasible.  
- Weakness: reliance on externally computed optical flow prevents end-to-end learning and requires heavy precomputation and storage.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to flow algorithm/parameters and lacking runtime comparisons to IDT.  
- Weakness: claim that temporal ConvNet subsumes hand-crafted motion descriptors is only qualitatively supported (visualizations) without quantitative validation.  
- Weakness: fusion limited to late score fusion; deeper fusion overfits but no quantitative exploration of regularized alternatives is provided.  
- Weakness: camera motion compensation is simplistic (mean flow subtraction) compared to homography-based stabilization used in IDT.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of temporal modeling granularity, longer contexts, sampling strategies, and robustness to temporal misalignment.  
- Weakness: multi-task learning implementation details (sampling ratios, loss weighting, class overlap handling) are sparse and sensitivity is unclear.  
- Quality: technically sound with experiments supporting main claims that explicit motion helps and streams are complementary; main limitation is external flow dependence and shallow fusion.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with sufficient implementation details and ablation numbers, but lacks runtime/throughput measurements.  
- Originality: notable and impactful integration of two-stream idea with learned temporal ConvNet and multi-task training, original relative to 2014 deep video models.  
- Significance: high — provides a practical, high-performing alternative to hand-crafted pipelines and a strong baseline for future work.  
- Suggestions: report computational trade-offs (time, memory, flow cost) and compare to IDT; evaluate lighter/slower flow methods and different flow algorithms.  
- Suggestions: quantitatively probe whether conv features approximate HOF/MBH, include per-class analyses and failure cases.  
- Suggestions: explore regularized joint fusion (bottlenecks, normalization, compact bilinear pooling) to model inter-stream interactions without overfitting.  
- Suggestions: test explicit camera stabilization (homography), MBH-like flow gradients, and robustness to different temporal sampling (L, stride, non-uniform sampling, longer context).  
- Acceptance arguments: strong empirical improvements, clear evidence explicit motion is crucial, and practical multi-task strategy.  
- Rejection arguments: heavy reliance on external flow with limited computational analysis, shallow fusion, and some unvalidated claims.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (strong).  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
