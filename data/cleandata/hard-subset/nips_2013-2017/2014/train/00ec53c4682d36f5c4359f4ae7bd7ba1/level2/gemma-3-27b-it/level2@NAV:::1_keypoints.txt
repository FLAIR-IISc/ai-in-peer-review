- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that separately models spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) information for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream processes multi-frame dense optical flow with a ConvNet, showing strong performance even with limited training data.  
- Multi-task learning across datasets improves temporal stream performance.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive results relative to prior state-of-the-art.  
- Key strength: clear and effective decoupling of spatial and temporal information, overcoming limitations of frame-stacking approaches.  
- Uses ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream and explores multi-task learning as beneficial contributions.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and clearly motivates and explains the method and results.  
- Major limitation: reliance on dense optical flow, which is computationally expensive and not extensively addressed.  
- Architectures are relatively simple compared to more recent state-of-the-art models.  
- Limited exploration of alternative motion representations, fusion strategies, and network configurations.  
- Discussion of biological plausibility (ventral/dorsal analogy) is underdeveloped.  
- Recommendation: solid and influential contribution; accept but encourage future work on more efficient motion representations and more sophisticated network designs.
