- Summary: Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial + temporal via dense optical flow) for video action recognition, outperforming prior shallow and deep methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Claim 1: Two-stream ConvNet effectively captures spatial and temporal information for action recognition.
- Claim 2: The temporal stream trained on multi-frame dense optical flow performs well despite limited training data.
- Claim 3: Multitask learning can increase training data and improve performance across datasets.
- Support: Experiments show two-stream outperforms single-stream and previous deep architectures on UCF-101.
- Support: Temporal stream evaluation on UCF-101 demonstrates effectiveness with limited data.
- Support: Multitask learning experiments on both datasets yield performance improvements by leveraging extra data.
- Usefulness: Architecture has practical applications in surveillance, robotics, healthcare, automatic video annotation, HCI, and sports analysis.
- Field knowledge: Authors cite relevant literature and compare to state-of-the-art methods, showing good domain understanding.
- Novelty: Two-stream design and use of multitask learning to leverage more data are novel contributions.
- Completeness: Paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility (architecture, training, evaluation protocols).
- Limitations: Requires large training data, risks overfitting, and may miss elements of state-of-the-art shallow representations (e.g., local pooling over spatio-temporal tubes).
- Constructive criticism: Could better handle camera motion (explicit compensation or advanced optical flow) and explore alternative architectures like 3D ConvNets.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective architecture with thorough experimental evaluation and practical relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper analysis of limitations/biases and more insight into learned representations/features.
- Recommendation: Overall recommend acceptance due to significant contribution and solid evaluation.
