- Proposes a two-stream deep ConvNet that decouples appearance (spatial stream) and motion (temporal stream) for video action recognition.
- Spatial stream is a standard ConvNet operating on single RGB frames, benefiting from ImageNet pre-training.
- Temporal stream is a ConvNet trained on stacked dense optical flow fields to explicitly learn motion patterns.
- Final predictions are combined via late fusion (averaging or SVM) to produce the video-level classification.
- Using pre-computed optical flow addresses prior deep models' failure to learn motion directly from raw pixels and guides learning toward motion representations.
- Temporal ConvNet serves as a learned, data-driven generalization of hand-crafted motion features like HOF and MBH.
- Technical quality is high: well-motivated design, clear description, and thorough experiments with ablation studies on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Experimental evaluation is rigorous and shows the two streams are complementary, achieving competitive state-of-the-art performance.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and reproducible, with logical presentation, helpful diagrams, and sufficient implementation details.
- Original contribution: first effective implementation of a two-stream idea within a modern discriminatively trained deep ConvNet framework; uses multi-task learning to combine datasets.
- High significance: matches engineered methods (improved dense trajectories), became an influential baseline and spurred follow-up research.
- Suggested improvements: discuss the computational/storage cost of pre-computing optical flow for scaling and real-time use.
- Suggested improvements: consider and discuss alternative fusion strategies (e.g., intermediate feature fusion) beyond late fusion.
- Suggested improvements: acknowledge limited temporal modeling (local L-frame window) and propose longer-range aggregation (e.g., recurrent architectures) as future work.
