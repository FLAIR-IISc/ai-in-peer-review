- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture that separates spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream processes single frames and benefits from ImageNet pre-training.  
- Temporal stream operates on stacked dense optical flow fields (L=10) with mean subtraction to model motion.  
- Final predictions are combined via late fusion (averaging or SVM).  
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train temporal stream on small video datasets (UCF-101, HMDB-51) to reduce overfitting.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation and ablation studies validate key design choices (pre-training, stacked flow, multi-task learning, fusion).  
- Clear, well-written paper with helpful figures and reproducible experimental details.  
- Original insight: successfully training 2D ConvNets on dense optical flow as image-like inputs, inspired by the two-streams hypothesis.  
- Significant impact: competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods and became a foundational baseline for subsequent video research.  
- Main weakness: reliance on pre-computed optical flow means the system is not fully end-to-end and adds computational cost.  
- Secondary weakness: simple late fusion prevents deeper joint learning or interaction between streams.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, original, and influential work; strongly recommended for acceptance.
