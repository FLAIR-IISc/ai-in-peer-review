- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture separating spatial (frames) and temporal (optical flow) networks.  
- Spatial network processes individual frames; temporal network is trained on multi-frame dense optical flow.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep-learning video approaches.  
- Demonstrates optical flow input is more effective than raw stacked frames for temporal modeling.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to increase effective training set size for the temporal network.  
- Novel integration of spatial and temporal streams for action recognition.  
- Use of optical-flow-based temporal stream and multi-task framework are original contributions.  
- Advances the field by improving performance and proposing a new spatial–temporal fusion approach.  
- Practical relevance for applications like HCI, surveillance, and robotics.  
- Limited available training data may lead to overfitting.  
- Computing optical flow is computationally expensive, despite proposed mitigations.  
- Evaluation only on two datasets limits demonstrated generalization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical performance, novelty, and contribution to the field.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited data, computational cost, and restricted generalization.
