- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture combining separate spatial (still frames) and temporal (motion) recognition streams.  
- Spatial stream is pre-trained on ImageNet and fine-tuned on target video datasets.  
- Temporal stream is trained on optical flow displacement fields, yielding an effective motion representation.  
- Two streams are complementary and their fusion significantly boosts recognition performance.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-written with a thorough related-work review and clear explanation of the architecture.  
- Provides detailed experimental results and analysis of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Strength: decoupling spatial and temporal processing enables effective pretraining and specialized motion modeling.  
- Weakness: limited training dataset sizes; proposed multi-task learning may not scale to very large datasets.  
- Weakness: camera motion handled by mean displacement subtraction, which may be insufficient for complex camera motion.  
- Originality: novel two-stream design and the use of a temporal net on optical flow are new contributions.  
- Significance: approach is competitive with shallow representations and could impact applications like surveillance and HCI.  
- Reviewer recommendation: arguments for acceptance include novelty and strong results; against include dataset and camera-motion concerns.  
- Overall rating 8/10, confidence 9/10.
