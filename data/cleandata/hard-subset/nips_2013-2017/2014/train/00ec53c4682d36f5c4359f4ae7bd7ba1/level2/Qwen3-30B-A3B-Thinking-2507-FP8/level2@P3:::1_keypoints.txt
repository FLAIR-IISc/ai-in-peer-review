- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition separating spatial appearance and temporal motion processing.  
- Spatial stream uses ImageNet-pretrained ConvNet on frames; temporal stream uses multi-frame dense optical flow to model motion.  
- Decoupled approach significantly outperforms prior deep attempts (e.g., stacked-frame method of Karpathy et al.), with ~20% higher accuracy on UCF-101 vs that baseline.  
- Proposes multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited data, improving shared representation and performance.  
- Fusion of streams (SVM on softmax scores) yields competitive results: 70.7% on UCF-101 and 45.6% on HMDB-51.  
- Architecture effectively generalizes hand-crafted features (HOF, MBH) into a discriminatively trained, learned framework without manual feature engineering.  
- Thorough ablation studies show optical flow stacking (L=10) and mean subtraction are critical; trajectory stacking underperforms.  
- Multi-task learning implementation is principled and boosts HMDB-51 accuracy by 3.1% over single-dataset training.  
- Provides sufficient implementation details (optical flow pre-computation, multi-GPU training) for reproducibility.  
- Results are technically sound with clear comparisons to prior work and evaluation on standard benchmarks.  
- Minor weakness: conceptual relation to Kuehne et al.’s HMAX two-stream work should be acknowledged, though this paper advances deep ConvNets.  
- Analysis gap: needs more explanation why trajectory stacking underperforms optical flow stacking.  
- Omission: brief discussion of emerging 3D ConvNet approaches (e.g., Ji et al., 2013) would strengthen related work context.  
- Limitation: does not fully capture local spatio-temporal pooling over trajectories used by top hand-crafted methods—clear future work direction.  
- Overall significance: shows explicit optical-flow encoding enables deep learning to handle video recognition, influencing later architectures; recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
