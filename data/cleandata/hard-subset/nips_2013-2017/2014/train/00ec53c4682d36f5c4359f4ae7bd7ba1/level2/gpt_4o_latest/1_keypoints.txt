- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet combining spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) information for video action recognition.  
- Main contributions: two-stream architecture, training temporal stream on multi-frame dense optical flow, and multitask learning across datasets.  
- Validated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive state-of-the-art results.  
- Strength: effectively decouples spatial and temporal recognition, improving over single-stream approaches.  
- Strength: dense optical flow input outperforms raw stacked-frame models; multitask learning mitigates limited-data issues.  
- Strength: extensive experiments and ablation studies on flow configurations, multitask learning, and fusion strategies.  
- Strength: situates work within existing literature with comprehensive and relevant references.  
- Weakness: temporal stream relies on existing optical flow techniques rather than a novel motion representation.  
- Weakness: pre-computing optical flow is computationally expensive, hindering scalability and real-time applicability.  
- Weakness: camera motion handling is limited—mean displacement subtraction may be insufficient compared to trajectory-based compensation.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns due to reliance on pre-trained models and multi-GPU hardware.  
- Suggestion: explore end-to-end learning of motion features and integrating optical flow computation within the network.  
- Suggestion: investigate advanced camera-motion compensation methods (e.g., trajectory-based pooling).  
- Suggestion: address scalability by optimizing optical flow computation or using lightweight motion representations.  
- Suggestion: provide more hyperparameter and training-schedule details to improve reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability concerns and add implementation details.  
- Pros: novel and effective architecture, thorough validation, and practical multitask learning for small datasets.  
- Cons: computational bottlenecks in flow pre-computation and limited novelty in motion representation/camera-motion handling.
