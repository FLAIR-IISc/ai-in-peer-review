- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream (appearance, ImageNet-pretrained) and temporal stream (motion, stacked optical flow), fused at score level.  
- Claim 1: Multi-frame optical-flow input enables strong ConvNet performance despite limited video data.  
- Claim 2: Spatial and temporal streams are complementary; late fusion improves accuracy.  
- Claim 3: Multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 increases effective training data and boosts performance.  
- Well positioned vs prior work (stacked RGB deep models and hand-crafted pipelines); temporal stream viewed as a learnable generalization of HOF/MBH.  
- Empirical support strong on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with ablations (flow stacking length, trajectory vs spatial stacking, mean subtraction, bi-directional flow).  
- Appropriate comparisons to slow-fusion RGB models and single-frame baselines; implementation details (augmentation, schedules, multi-GPU) adequate for reproduction.  
- Missing: statistical significance tests and runtime/throughput comparisons.  
- Major limitation: reliance on pre-computed optical flow prevents end-to-end training and adds compute/storage overhead.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written, well organized, with adequate architectural and training detail and insightful links to traditional motion features.  
- Originality: clean, novel combination of separated spatial/temporal pathways with explicit optical-flow inputs and multi-task learning.  
- Significance: closes gap between deep and hand-crafted methods on benchmarks and likely to influence future video architectures.  
- Suggestion: report end-to-end computational costs (flow time, training/inference throughput) and storage; test sensitivity to flow algorithm.  
- Suggestion: provide per-class analyses and statistical tests across splits.  
- Suggestion: explore better camera-motion compensation and trajectory-aware pooling within the network.  
- Suggestion: investigate longer-range temporal modeling (temporal convolutions/LSTMs) and integrate motion estimation for end-to-end training.  
- Suggestion: evaluate scalability on larger datasets (e.g., Sports-1M).  
- Acceptance arguments: strong empirical gains over prior deep baselines, competitive with state of the art, clear architecture, thorough ablations, and practical multi-task strategy.  
- Rejection arguments: heavy dependence on pre-computed optical flow and limited analysis of computational efficiency and statistical significance.
