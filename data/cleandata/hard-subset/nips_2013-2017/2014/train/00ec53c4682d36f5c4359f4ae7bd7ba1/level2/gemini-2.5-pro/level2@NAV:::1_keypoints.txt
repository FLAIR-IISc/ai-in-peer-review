- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that decouples appearance and motion learning for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream ConvNet processes individual frames to learn static appearance features.  
- Temporal stream is trained on stacked dense optical flow to explicitly model motion patterns.  
- Predictions from the two streams are combined via late fusion of softmax scores.  
- Addresses prior models' inability to learn motion from raw pixels and generalizes hand-crafted motion features (MBH/HOF) in a learnable way.  
- Simple, intuitive, and effective idea that significantly advances deep-learning-based action recognition and influenced later work.  
- Strong technical validation with extensive experiments and ablation studies on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Benefits from ImageNet pretraining for the spatial stream and uses multi-task learning to reduce overfitting on small datasets.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation and implementation details.  
- Relies on pre-computed optical flow, preventing end-to-end training and creating computational/storage bottlenecks.  
- Late fusion may miss richer spatial-temporal correlations that earlier fusion could capture.  
- Overall assessment: strengths far outweigh weaknesses; a foundational, rigorous, and significant contribution.
