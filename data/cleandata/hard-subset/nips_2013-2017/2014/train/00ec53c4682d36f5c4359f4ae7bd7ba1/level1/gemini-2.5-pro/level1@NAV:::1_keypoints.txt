- Proposes a novel two-stream CNN for video action recognition separating appearance (spatial) and motion (temporal) processing.
- Spatial stream operates on RGB frames and is pre-trained on ImageNet to capture scene and object information.
- Temporal stream is trained from scratch on stacked dense optical flow to learn motion patterns.
- Final prediction is produced by late fusion of the two streams' softmax scores.
- Effectively addresses prior deep models' inability to learn motion from raw pixels and rivals hand-crafted methods like Improved Dense Trajectories on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Original and significant: explicit decoupling of spatial and temporal cues with pre-computed optical flow is a simple, powerful idea that sets a strong baseline.
- High quality: technically sound with comprehensive experiments and ablation studies showing complementary streams, benefit of ImageNet pre-training, superiority of optical flow over stacked RGB, and gains from multi-task learning.
- Clear and reproducible presentation with well-explained architecture and experimental setup.
- Weakness: relies on pre-computed optical flow, preventing end-to-end learning and making performance dependent on external flow quality.
- Weakness: fusion is simple (averaging/SVM); more sophisticated joint or fusion techniques are not explored and could improve results.
