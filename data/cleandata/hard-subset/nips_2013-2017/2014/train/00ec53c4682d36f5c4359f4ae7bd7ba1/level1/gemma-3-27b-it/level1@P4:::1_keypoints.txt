- Proposal: two-stream CNN that decouples spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) streams and fuses their outputs.  
- Key findings: temporal stream trained on optical flow is especially effective; multi-task learning provides additional benefits.  
- Positioning: builds on trajectory-based hand-crafted methods and prior deep-video work; inspired by the two-stream neuroscience hypothesis but not deeply explored.  

Strengths:
- Clear contributions: novel two-stream architecture, successful optical-flow ConvNet training, and multi-task learning.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep-learning approaches.  
- Thorough experimentation: evaluates temporal input variants, mean flow subtraction, and multi-task learning effects.  
- Reproducibility: provides detailed network, training, and optical flow implementation details.  
- Literature coverage: related work is comprehensive and well-connected to the proposed approach.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: separate spatial/temporal streams are not entirely new (e.g., prior work [16]).  
- Fusion analysis lacking: no detailed ablation explaining why SVM-based fusion performs better.  
- Optical-flow bottleneck: computational cost and scalability concerns (e.g., for Sports-1M) are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Superficial biological link: mentions two-stream hypothesis but does not explore deeper connections or insights.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Technically sound, well-executed approach with strong empirical gains and reproducible details.  

Arguments for rejection:
- Core idea not radically novel; would benefit from deeper fusion analysis and discussion of optical-flow costs.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong, well-documented paper that advances video action recognition; recommend acceptance.
