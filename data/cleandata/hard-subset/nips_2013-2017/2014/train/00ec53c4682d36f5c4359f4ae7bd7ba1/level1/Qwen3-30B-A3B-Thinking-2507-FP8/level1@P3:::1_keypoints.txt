- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream (ImageNet-pretrained frames) + temporal stream (multi-frame dense optical flow).  
- Demonstrates optical flow stacking outperforms stacked RGB frames for modeling temporal information.  
- Shows spatial and temporal streams are complementary and their fusion improves recognition accuracy.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51, alleviating data scarcity for temporal training.  
- Builds on deep image-classification successes and addresses limitations of prior deep video methods that used stacked frames.  
- Temporal stream generalizes hand-crafted motion features (HOF, MBH, trajectories) by learning hierarchies directly from optical flow.  
- Architecture is inspired by ventral/dorsal two-stream hypothesis and is well positioned against related work.  
- Thorough experiments compare temporal inputs (flow vs. trajectory stacking, bidirectional flow, mean subtraction), finding multi-frame flow + mean subtraction best.  
- Reliance on pre-computed optical flow is a limitation; the impact of different flow methods is not explored.  
- Paper is technically sound, reproducible, and follows standard evaluation protocols on UCF-101/HMDB-51.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization with helpful visualizations and ablation studies.  
- Original: first discriminatively trained deep ConvNets applied to both spatial and temporal streams, advancing prior two-stream models.  
- Results significantly outperform previous deep learning approaches (~20%+ over [14]) and are competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, comprehensive temporal evaluations, effective multi-task learning, strong empirical gains, excellent presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of different optical-flow methods, missing detailed failure cases, computational cost of precomputed flow not addressed, and limited explanation why it outperforms hand-crafted features.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-validated contribution to video action recognition; recommended for acceptance.
