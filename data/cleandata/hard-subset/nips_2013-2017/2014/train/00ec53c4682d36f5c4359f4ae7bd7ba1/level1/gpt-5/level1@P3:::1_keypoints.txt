- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition that decouples appearance (RGB) and motion (stacked optical flow) and fuses scores.  
- Spatial stream: ImageNet-pretrained ConvNet fine-tuned on video frames.  
- Temporal stream: ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow fields with mean subtraction and trajectory vs fixed-location variants.  
- Fusion performed at score level (average or linear SVM); multi-task learning uses two softmax heads for UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Motivation tied to neuroscience (ventral/dorsal pathways) and limitations of prior video deep models; temporal stream injects motion explicitly.  
- Relates temporal ConvNet to classical motion features (HOF/MBH, trajectory stacking) and earlier two-stream HMAX work.  
- Empirically, temporal ConvNets on optical flow outperform single/multi-frame RGB ConvNets on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Late fusion of spatial and temporal streams yields accuracy competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted pipelines and improves over prior deep baselines.  
- Strength: clear, principled architectural decomposition with careful exploration of temporal stream design choices.  
- Strength: thorough empirical ablations on stacked flow length, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction, and fusion strategies.  
- Strength: simple, effective multi-task learning that increases effective data and is reproducible.  
- Strength: careful engineering and training protocol (ImageNet pretraining, multi-GPU, multi-crop/frame testing).  
- Strength: meaningful connection and empirical support linking learned filters to hand-crafted motion features.  
- Weakness: reliance on precomputed optical flow breaks end-to-end learning, adds overhead, and sensitivity to flow algorithm/parameters is untested.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of computational efficiency, storage needs, and runtime comparisons with iDT and frame-stack ConvNets.  
- Weakness: fusion scope limited to late score fusion; feature-level or cross-stream fusion attempts overfit and are underexplored.  
- Weakness: only mean flow subtraction used for camera motion compensation; more principled methods (homography/RANSAC) not evaluated.  
- Weakness: no evaluation of combining two-stream features with iDT or broader comparisons to 3D ConvNets under matched data regimes.  
- Weakness: potential dataset bias from spatial stream using object/scene priors; per-class and failure-mode analysis is missing.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-executed, but missing analyses (flow sensitivity, efficiency, motion compensation) limit completeness.  
- Clarity: well written and reproducible; minor additions needed (runtime/memory, flow normalization details, SVM fusion specifics).  
- Originality: two-stream with optical-flow input is a clear, impactful contribution; multi-task training is practical and useful.  
- Significance: high — clean, scalable design with strong accuracy likely to influence video understanding research.  
- Suggestions: evaluate sensitivity to different flow algorithms and frame rates, report runtime/throughput, explore lightweight cross-stream fusion with stronger regularization, implement better camera motion compensation, provide class-wise analysis, and test combination with iDT.  
- Acceptance arguments: strong simple idea with compelling ablations, competitive with SOTA hand-crafted pipelines, and practical multi-task strategy.  
- Rejection arguments: heavy dependence on precomputed flow, limited fusion and combination analyses, and missing generalization/efficiency studies.  
- Final recommendation: Accept.
