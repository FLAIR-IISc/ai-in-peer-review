- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial: frames, temporal: dense optical flow) for video action recognition.
- Shows ConvNet trained on multi-frame dense optical flow achieves strong performance despite limited training data.
- Uses multi-task learning to augment training data and improve performance across datasets.
- Novel architecture that is effective on two challenging datasets.
- Multi-task learning is a useful idea that boosts results.
- Thorough evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art and analysis of input/configuration effects.
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational cost, raising real-time applicability concerns.
- Fails to discuss limitations and assumptions of using optical flow (e.g., constant brightness, flow estimation errors).
- No detailed comparison to other deep learning–based action recognition approaches.
- Technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments.
- Paper is well-written and clear.
- Original contribution in combining two-stream architecture with multi-task learning.
- Significant results demonstrating the importance of motion information for action recognition.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, comprehensive evaluation, and significant empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing computational cost analysis and insufficient discussion of optical-flow limitations.
