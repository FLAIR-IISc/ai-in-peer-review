- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet combining a spatial stream (single frames) and a temporal stream (multi-frame dense optical flow) for action recognition.
- Introduces multi-task learning to augment training data and improve performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Paper is well-written with a clear related-work overview and a well-motivated, novel architecture.
- Demonstrates competitive, state-of-the-art performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Strength: two-stream design effectively fuses appearance and motion information.
- Strength: multi-task learning meaningfully increases training data and boosts results.
- Strength: thorough evaluation on two challenging datasets.
- Weakness: relies on pre-computed optical flow, limiting scalability to large-scale video datasets.
- Weakness: does not explicitly handle camera motion, which may degrade temporal-stream performance.
- Weakness: limited analysis of learned features and convolutional filters in the temporal network.
- Pro-acceptance: novel, effective architecture with strong experimental validation and useful multi-task contribution.
- Con-acceptance: applicability limited by optical-flow dependence, missing camera-motion compensation, and insufficient feature analysis.
- Overall: positive recommendation—novel and effective but would benefit from deeper analysis and handling of camera motion.
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
