- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet separating spatial (RGB, ImageNet-pretrained) and temporal (stacked dense optical flow) inputs for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream inputs are stacked multi-frame optical flow fields; streams fused by score averaging or linear SVM.  
- Introduces multi-task learning by sharing the ConvNet trunk and using separate softmax heads for UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to increase effective temporal training data.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with detailed ablations showing strong empirical gains.  
- Optical flow–based temporal ConvNets substantially outperform RGB-only models trained from scratch on these datasets.  
- Stacking multiple flow fields, bi-directional flow, and mean flow subtraction improve accuracy.  
- Fusion of spatial and temporal streams yields competitive performance versus state-of-the-art trajectory-based pipelines.  
- Clear and principled decomposition that is easy to implement and reason about.  
- Strong empirical results with thorough ablations (flow stacking, trajectory vs fixed stacking, mean subtraction).  
- Demonstrates that explicit motion input outperforms implicit motion learning from raw RGB stacks given limited data.  
- Multi-task learning setup is simple, well-defined, and provides measurable gains for smaller datasets.  
- Training/testing protocols and implementation details are well documented for reproducibility.  
- Learned ConvNet filters relate to classic motion descriptors (HOF/MBH), suggesting the model subsumes them.  
- Reliance on precomputed optical flow incurs significant computational/storage overhead and breaks end-to-end learning.  
- No sensitivity analysis to flow quality, different flow algorithms, noise, or compression artifacts.  
- Temporal modeling is local (e.g., L=10 frames) with no explicit long-term aggregation beyond clip averaging.  
- Camera motion compensation is only approximated via mean flow subtraction; stronger methods are not compared.  
- Limited analysis of error modes, per-class improvements/degradations, and robustness to frame rate or sampling stride changes.  
- Learnable/deeper fusion reportedly overfits; no explored regularization strategies to enable joint fusion learning.  
- Comparisons to top trajectory-based pipelines and runtime/throughput trade-offs are limited or missing.  
- Technically sound approach with carefully defined temporal inputs and correctly implemented multi-task objective.  
- Experiments support claims of stream complementarity and multi-task effectiveness across three dataset splits.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and precise in mathematical/implementation descriptions.  
- Novelty lies in integrating two-stream architecture with learned temporal ConvNet on stacked optical flow and simple multi-task learning.  
- Significant contribution advancing deep approaches toward matching hand-crafted trajectory methods, while highlighting practical limitations and future research directions.  
- Suggested improvements: report compute/time/storage, test sensitivity to different/degraded flow, explore stronger camera compensation or learnable flow, investigate long-range temporal aggregation and regularized learned fusion, and provide per-class/failure analyses and cross-dataset generalization.
