- Proposes a two-stream convolutional network separating spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) information for video action recognition.  
- Key idea: train deep CNNs for both appearance and a temporal stream based on stacked optical flow.  
- Builds on the two-stream hypothesis from neuroscience and addresses limitations of frame-stacking approaches.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to leverage multiple datasets and mitigate limited labeled video data.  
- Technically sound with thorough experiments supporting claims.  
- Strong ablation studies (e.g., optical flow stacking, mean subtraction) justify design choices.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvement over prior deep-learning baseline [14] and competitive performance with hand-crafted features.  
- Training and testing procedures are described in enough detail for reproducibility; notation is consistent.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear architecture illustrations.  
- Some sections (e.g., Sec. 3.2) could be more concise.  
- Novelty is in effectively applying deep CNNs to both streams and training temporal stream on optical flow—not a radical theory advance but a clever, well-executed application.  
- Results are significant: show deep learning’s potential for video and likely influenced subsequent work using temporal streams and optical flow.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, well-motivated architecture, thorough ablations, effective multi-task learning, high relevance to NIPS audience.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical grounding of the neuroscience link and experimental scope could be expanded to more recent baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly accept — significant, well-presented contribution with clear experimental support.
