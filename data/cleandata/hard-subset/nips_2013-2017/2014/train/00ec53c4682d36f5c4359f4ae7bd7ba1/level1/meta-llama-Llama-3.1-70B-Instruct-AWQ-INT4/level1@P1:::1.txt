This paper proposes a two-stream Convolutional Network (ConvNet) architecture for action recognition in videos, which incorporates spatial and temporal networks. The spatial stream performs action recognition from still video frames, while the temporal stream is trained to recognize action from motion in the form of dense optical flow. Both streams are implemented as ConvNets. The authors demonstrate that a ConvNet trained on multi-frame dense optical flow can achieve good performance despite limited training data. They also show that multi-task learning can be used to increase the amount of training data and improve performance on both UCF-101 and HMDB-51 datasets.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear overview of the related work and the proposed architecture. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture for action recognition in videos.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of using multi-frame dense optical flow as input to the temporal stream.
* The use of multi-task learning to increase the amount of training data and improve performance is a good idea.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

Weaknesses:

* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed architecture.
* The authors do not compare their results with other deep learning-based approaches for action recognition in videos.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the learned features and the optical flow inputs.
