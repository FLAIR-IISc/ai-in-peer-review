- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet (spatial + temporal) for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream classifies from single video frames; temporal stream uses multi-frame dense optical flow.  
- Shows ConvNet trained on multi-frame optical flow performs well despite limited data.  
- Uses multi-task learning to increase training data and improve performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear related work and architecture description.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are clearly presented.  
- Strength: novel two-stream architecture for action recognition.  
- Strength: effective use of multi-frame dense optical flow as temporal input.  
- Strength: multi-task learning to boost training data and performance.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational cost.  
- Weakness: no comparison with other deep learning-based action recognition methods.  
- Weakness: needs more visualizations of learned features and optical flow inputs.  
- Quality: technically sound; claims supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem, achieves state-of-the-art on two datasets, and is likely to impact the field.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, effective architecture with strong experimental support.
