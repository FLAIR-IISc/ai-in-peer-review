This paper proposes a two-stream Convolutional Network (ConvNet) architecture for action recognition in videos. The architecture incorporates spatial and temporal networks, where the spatial network recognizes actions from still video frames and the temporal network recognizes actions from motion in the form of dense optical flow. The authors demonstrate that a ConvNet trained on multi-frame dense optical flow achieves good performance despite limited training data. They also show that multi-task learning can be used to increase the amount of training data and improve performance on both datasets.

Strengths of the paper:

* The proposed two-stream architecture is a novel approach to action recognition in videos, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on two challenging datasets.
* The use of multi-task learning to increase the amount of training data is a good idea, and the authors show that it can improve performance on both datasets.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and an analysis of the effects of different input configurations and training settings.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of their approach, which could be a limitation for real-time applications.
* The authors do not discuss the potential limitations of using optical flow as input to the temporal network, such as the assumption of constant brightness and the potential for errors in the optical flow estimation.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison to other deep learning-based approaches to action recognition, which could be useful for understanding the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and the results are well-presented.
