- Proposes a two-stream deep architecture: spatial ConvNet on RGB (ImageNet-pretrained) and temporal ConvNet on stacked optical flow, fused at score level.  
- Temporal net systematically explores optical-flow input designs: L-frame stacking, trajectory stacking, forward vs. bidirectional flow, and mean flow subtraction.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with separate softmax heads to mitigate limited video supervision.  
- Positions work relative to handcrafted pipelines (IDT/FV/MBH) and raw-frame deep methods, arguing explicit flow input improves over frame-stacking approaches.  
- Strength: clear architectural decomposition leveraging ImageNet pretraining for appearance and optical-flow for motion.  
- Strength: thorough ablation studies on temporal input design and spatial pretraining/fine-tuning.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, surpassing prior deep baselines and approaching handcrafted-feature methods.  
- Strength: practical and effective multi-task training protocol that increases effective data and regularizes training.  
- Strength: solid implementation and reproducibility details (multi-GPU training, efficient precomputed flow storage).  
- Weakness: reliance on precomputed optical flow breaks end-to-end learning, adds computation/storage, and is not real-time.  
- Weakness: camera motion handled only by mean flow subtraction; lacks comparison to more principled global motion compensation (e.g., homography).  
- Weakness: fusion limited to softmax-level because deeper fusion overfits, indicating insufficient exploration of regularized joint/mid-level fusion.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of statistical significance, per-class error modes, and robustness to flow quality/errors.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to combining deep features with IDT/MBH, a common strong complementary baseline.  
- Methodologically sound; ablations convincingly support main claims: flow-based nets outperform frame-stacking, longer temporal context helps, mean subtraction reduces camera motion confounds, and multi-task training aids small datasets.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized with precise temporal input definitions and implementation details; could add exact flow parameters and runtime/memory table.  
- Originality: two-stream idea has precedents, but contributions are novel and substantive: discriminative temporal ConvNet on stacked flow, systematic input exploration, and cross-dataset multi-task training.  
- Significance: high — provides a practical, general recipe for deep video action recognition that narrows the gap with handcrafted pipelines.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical gains, careful ablations, simple effective architecture, and reproducibility.  
- Cons for acceptance: not end-to-end, limited camera-motion handling, lack of IDT/MBH fusion experiments, and overfitting limits deeper fusion exploration.  
- Suggestions: report runtime/memory estimates including flow computation; compare mean subtraction to explicit global motion compensation; explore mid-level fusion with stronger regularization; test robustness to flow quality; provide per-class error analysis; evaluate fusion with IDT/MBH.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — technically solid, clear, and impactful despite some limitations.
