- Summary: Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial on frames, temporal on multi-frame dense optical flow) that outperforms prior deep models and is competitive with top shallow methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.

- Related work: Thorough survey highlighting limitations of frame-stacking deep models and motivating decoupled spatial/temporal streams.

- Methodology: Two separate ConvNets (spatial and temporal) with temporal input variants (optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow) and a multi-task learning scheme to combine datasets.

- Strength: Novel two-stream architecture that meaningfully advances video action recognition.

- Strength: Demonstrates state-of-the-art or competitive performance on challenging benchmarks.

- Strength: Extensive evaluation including ablations and comparisons to existing methods.

- Weakness: Limited training data for video actions, raising overfitting concerns.

- Weakness: High computational cost due to optical flow computation (authors propose partial mitigation).

- Weakness: Insufficient evidence of generalization to other datasets or tasks.

- Originality: Two-stream design and multi-task dataset combination are significant, novel contributions.

- Significance: Offers a new paradigm for action recognition and shows deep models can reach top performance.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel architecture, strong empirical performance, and thorough evaluation.

- Arguments for rejection: Data scarcity/overfitting risk, computational expense, and limited generalization evidence.

- Recommendations: Provide more cross-dataset/generalization experiments.

- Recommendations: Explore additional applications (varying lighting, camera motion) to validate robustness.

- Recommendations: Improve computational efficiency (faster optical flow, parallelization, etc.).
