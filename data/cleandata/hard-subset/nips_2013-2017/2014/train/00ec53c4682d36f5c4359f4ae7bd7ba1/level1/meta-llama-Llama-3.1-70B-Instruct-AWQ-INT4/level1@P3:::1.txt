**Summary**

The paper proposes a two-stream Convolutional Network (ConvNet) architecture for action recognition in videos. The architecture consists of two separate recognition streams: a spatial stream that operates on individual video frames and a temporal stream that operates on multiple-frame dense optical flow. The authors demonstrate that the proposed architecture outperforms existing deep architectures and is competitive with state-of-the-art shallow representations on two challenging datasets: UCF-101 and HMDB-51.

**Related Work**

The paper provides a thorough review of existing work on action recognition in videos, including shallow and deep architectures. The authors highlight the limitations of existing deep architectures, which often rely on stacking consecutive video frames as input, and propose a new architecture that decouples spatial and temporal recognition streams.

**Methodology**

The proposed architecture consists of two ConvNets: a spatial ConvNet that operates on individual video frames and a temporal ConvNet that operates on multiple-frame dense optical flow. The authors describe different input configurations for the temporal ConvNet, including optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, and bi-directional optical flow. They also propose a multi-task learning framework to combine training data from multiple datasets.
