- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition that decouples appearance (RGB spatial stream pretrained on ImageNet) and motion (temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow).  
- Temporal and spatial streams are fused by averaging softmax scores or using an SVM over scores.  
- Introduces simple multi-task training to jointly learn on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, reducing data scarcity for the temporal net.  
- Extensive ablations show stacking multiple flow fields, mean displacement subtraction, and multi-task training substantially boost accuracy.  
- Outperforms prior deep models that learn from raw frames and is competitive with trajectory-based hand-crafted pipelines.  
- Connects to the two-stream hypothesis in human vision and shows ConvNets can generalize traditional motion descriptors.  
- Methodology is technically sound with careful experimental design, varied ablations, and thorough training details.  
- Major limitation is reliance on precomputed optical flow (computational and storage overhead; not end-to-end).  
- Evaluation limited to two benchmarks and lacks variance/significance testing and failure analysis.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized; optical-flow formulations are precise.  
- Suggested clarifications: impact of JPEG compression on flow, SVM fusion hyperparameters, and runtime/memory for the full pipeline.  
- Originality lies in the explicit optical-flow temporal ConvNet combined with a pretrained spatial stream and multi-task scheme.  
- Significant contribution as a strong, reproducible baseline likely to influence future video recognition architectures.  
- Pros: principled architecture, strong empirical gains, bridges deep learning with classical motion cues, practical multi-task learning.  
- Cons: heavy dependence on external flow, limited datasets/statistical/runtime reporting, and simple softmax fusion with deeper fusion hindered by overfitting.
