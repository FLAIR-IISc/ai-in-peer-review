- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial stream on RGB frames and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused at score level.  
- Temporal stream variants include trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction; multi-task learning shares trunk but uses dataset-specific softmax heads.  
- Key insight: explicitly providing motion via optical flow to ConvNets outperforms learning motion implicitly from raw RGB.  
- Well positioned relative to prior work: closes gap between deep models and hand-crafted pipelines (e.g., dense trajectories + Fisher vectors).  
- Experiments are thorough with ablations on temporal input design, pre-training, RGB multi-frame baselines, multi-task training, and fusion strategies.  
- Results significantly outperform previous deep approaches and are competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods, showing complementarity between streams.  
- Methodology is straightforward, well-motivated, and evaluated with relevant ablations (e.g., varying L, mean subtraction, bidirectional flow).  
- Empirical claims are well supported by experiments on standard splits and metrics; optical-flow-based temporal ConvNets convincingly beat stacked-RGB ConvNets.  
- Main limitation: reliance on precomputed optical flow (Brox), which is computationally expensive and not learned end-to-end.  
- Paper lacks analysis of sensitivity to choice/quality of optical flow algorithm and effects of lossy flow compression (JPEG).  
- Runtime, memory, and computation trade-offs are only briefly discussed and need quantification for practical comparison.  
- Paper is clearly written with sufficient implementation/training details for reproduction.  
- Clarifications requested: temporal sampling stride τ and its train/test selection, temporal data augmentation specifics, JPEG impact on flow accuracy, and class-imbalance handling in multi-task training.  
- Originality: feeding multi-frame dense optical flow directly into a discriminatively trained ConvNet and the two-stream deep design are novel contributions.  
- Significance: provides a simple, effective recipe likely to influence practice and narrows the gap between deep and hand-crafted video recognition.  
- Pros: simple architecture, comprehensive ablations, clear evidence for optical flow input, demonstrated complementarity of streams, and practical multi-task training gains.  
- Cons: heavy dependence on external flow computation, limited fusion exploration beyond score-level, no combination with top hand-crafted baselines, limited per-class/failure analysis.  
- Suggestions: report preprocessing/inference runtimes, test robustness to different flow algorithms and JPEG compression, explore earlier fusion with regularization, provide per-class analyses, and evaluate combination with improved dense trajectories.  
- Clarify multi-task details: dataset sampling ratios, loss weighting, and measures to prevent split leakage when combining datasets.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — well-motivated, novel, practical, and empirically solid work that would benefit from additional analysis on flow robustness and computational profiling.
