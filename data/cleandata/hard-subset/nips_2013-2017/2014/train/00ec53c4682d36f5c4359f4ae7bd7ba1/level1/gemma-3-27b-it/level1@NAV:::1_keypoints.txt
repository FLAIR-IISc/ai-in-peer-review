- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet that separates spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) information for video action recognition.  
- Shows that a ConvNet trained on multi-frame dense optical flow performs strongly; multi-task learning across datasets improves generalization.  
- Builds on prior work addressing temporal dynamics limitations in deep video methods and leverages ImageNet pretraining for the spatial stream.  
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition.  
- Strength: effective and well-motivated two-stream architecture.  
- Strength: competitive empirical results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, nearing hand-crafted feature performance.  
- Strength: multi-task learning helps mitigate limited training data.  
- Strength: detailed implementation information enhances reproducibility.  
- Weakness: reliance on dense optical flow increases computational cost and lacks exploration of efficient alternatives.  
- Weakness: limited ablation study—insufficient analysis of network depths and fusion strategies.  
- Weakness: comparisons focus on accuracy without reporting computational complexity or memory usage.  
- Weakness: missed opportunity to explore biological plausibility beyond citing the two-stream hypothesis.  
- For acceptance: novel and effective architecture, strong results, clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: computationally expensive optical flow dependence and relatively limited ablation.  
- Overall: strong paper making a valuable contribution; recommendation to accept.
