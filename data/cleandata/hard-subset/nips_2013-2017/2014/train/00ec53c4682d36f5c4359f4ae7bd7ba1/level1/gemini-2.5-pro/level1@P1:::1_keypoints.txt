- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet separating spatial (single-frame) and temporal (stacked optical flow) pathways, combined via late fusion.
- Addresses prior ConvNet failures on video by feeding explicit motion (optical flow) to a dedicated temporal network instead of learning motion from raw pixels.
- Spatial stream leverages ImageNet pretraining; temporal stream uses stacked flows (L=10) with mean flow subtraction.
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, alleviating data scarcity for the temporal stream.
- Strong empirical gains: ~20% improvement over previous deep methods on UCF-101 and competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.
- Thorough ablation studies validate choices like flow stacking length, mean subtraction, and pretraining strategies.
- Architecture is simple, elegant, effective, and the paper is clear and reproducible.
- Main limitation: reliance on pre-computed optical flow makes the approach not fully end-to-end and computationally/storage intensive.
- Fusion is simple late fusion of softmax scores, which may miss richer spatial-temporal interactions available with earlier learned fusion.
- Limited exploration of longer-term temporal structure — the temporal stream uses a relatively short temporal window.
- Overall high technical quality, originality, and significant impact on video action recognition research.
- Conclusion/recommendation: strong accept despite minor limitations.
