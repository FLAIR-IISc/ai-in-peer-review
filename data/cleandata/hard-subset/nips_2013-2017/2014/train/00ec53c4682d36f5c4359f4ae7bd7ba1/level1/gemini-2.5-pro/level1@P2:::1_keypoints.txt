- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet separating appearance (spatial stream) and motion (temporal stream) for video action recognition.
- Spatial stream: standard ConvNet on single frames, benefits from ImageNet pre-training.
- Temporal stream: ConvNet trained on stacked dense optical flow to explicitly capture motion.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks.
- Thorough ablation studies on temporal input configurations (stacked flow, trajectory vs flow stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction).
- Uses multi-task learning to combine datasets and mitigate overfitting on small video datasets.
- Positions work as addressing prior deep-learning failures on video (e.g., Karpathy et al.) by providing explicit motion input.
- Frames temporal ConvNet as a learnable generalization of hand-crafted motion descriptors (HOF, MBH).
- High technical quality with comprehensive experiments and reproducible implementation details.
- Exceptionally clear writing, precise architecture description, and helpful diagrams.
- Original contribution: first successful modern deep implementation of two-stream idea with optical flow input.
- High significance and impact: competitive with best hand-crafted methods and likely to spur substantial follow-up work.
- Argument for acceptance: novel and effective architecture, strong deep learning results, excellent exposition, and high potential impact.
- Argument against acceptance: not trained end-to-end due to pre-computed optical flow and uses simple late fusion that may be suboptimal.
- Overall conclusion: strong, timely contribution with minor limitations; recommendation for acceptance.
