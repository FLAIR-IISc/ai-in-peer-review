- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that separately captures appearance (still frames) and motion (between frames) for video action recognition.  
- Seeks to generalize top-performing hand-crafted features within a data-driven deep learning framework.  
- Links the design to the two-streams hypothesis (ventral/dorsal pathways) but does not further investigate the neuroscience connection.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Gives detailed descriptions of spatial and temporal ConvNet configurations and a multi-task learning framework.  
- Evaluated on large-scale datasets (UCF-101, HMDB-51) and shows competitive performance with state-of-the-art hand-crafted models.  
- Strength: Novel architecture that effectively captures complementary spatial and temporal information.  
- Strength: Comprehensive related work review and clear architectural explanation.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation on large annotated video datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational cost and efficiency.  
- Weakness: Does not study the effect of different hyperparameters on performance.  
- Weakness: Fails to explain how the architecture generalizes to tasks beyond action recognition.  
- Arguments for acceptance reiterate novelty, clarity, and strong evaluation on large datasets.  
- Arguments against acceptance emphasize missing computational analysis, hyperparameter studies, and limited discussion of broader applicability.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
