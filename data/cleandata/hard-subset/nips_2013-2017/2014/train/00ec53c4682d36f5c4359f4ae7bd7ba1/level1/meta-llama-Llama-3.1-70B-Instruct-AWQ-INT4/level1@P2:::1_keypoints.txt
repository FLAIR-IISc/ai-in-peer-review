- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) networks for video action recognition.
- Paper is well-written with a clear overview of related work and a detailed explanation of the architecture and multi-task learning.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art shallow representations.
- Provides extensive evaluation of architectural and input variations (stacked flows, trajectory stacking, mean displacement subtraction, bi-directional flow).
- Strength: novel and effective deep ConvNet approach for video action recognition.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across multiple architectures and input configurations.
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of methods and related work.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and implications for real-time use.
- Weakness: limited discussion of the architecture's limitations and directions for future work.
- Weakness: some figures and tables need improved clarity and readability.
- Meets review criteria: technically sound (quality), clear (clarity), original (originality), and significant (significance).
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novelty, thorough evaluation, competitive results, and clear exposition.
- Con-acceptance arguments: missing computational complexity analysis, limited limitations/future work discussion, and presentation issues.
