- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture decoupling spatial (frames) and temporal (optical flow) information and combining their outputs.  
- Trains a spatial ConvNet on still frames and a temporal ConvNet on multi-frame dense optical flow; also explores multi-task learning across datasets.  
- Builds on prior trajectory-based hand-crafted features and earlier CNN video work, directly addressing the failure of simple frame-stacking.  
- Achieves strong results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior CNN baseline and matching state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.  
- Conducts thorough ablation studies testing temporal input variants (stacking vs. trajectory, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction) and fusion strategies.  
- Multi-task learning effectively increases training data and yields performance improvements.  
- Provides detailed implementation and optical flow computation details that support reproducibility.  
- Individual components (ConvNets, optical flow) lack novelty; main contribution is the effective integration and empirical validation.  
- Optical flow computation is a significant computational bottleneck, limiting scalability despite pre-computation and compression mitigations.  
- Missing comparisons to more recent deep learning video architectures beyond the cited baseline [14].  
- Limited analysis/visualization of what the temporal ConvNet learns and how it compares to hand-crafted features.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, strong empirical evidence, thorough ablations, and important contribution to video action recognition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of components, optical flow cost, and lack of comparison with newer deep methods.  
- Overall: Strong paper recommended for acceptance; integration and multi-task learning yield state-of-the-art results, but discussion of recent deep models would strengthen the work.
