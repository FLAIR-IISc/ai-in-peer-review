- Proposes a two-stream convolutional architecture that decouples spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) information for video action recognition.
- Leverages pre-trained image models for the spatial stream and trains a separate ConvNet on optical flow for motion modeling.
- Utilizes multi-task learning to combine datasets and improve generalization.
- Builds on the two-stream hypothesis from visual neuroscience and addresses limitations of applying ConvNets directly to video.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on standard benchmarks (UCF-101, HMDB-51).
- Provides a clear, well-structured paper with logical architectural explanations.
- Includes comprehensive experimental evaluation and ablation studies (stacking, mean subtraction, bi-directional flow).
- Compares favorably to both earlier deep learning approaches and state-of-the-art hand-crafted features.
- Computing and storing dense optical flow is computationally expensive and may limit scalability and real-time use.
- Limited analysis of what the temporal ConvNet learns from optical flow; more visualizations of learned filters would help.
- Lacks comparison to emerging 3D CNN approaches (C3D), weakening assessment against contemporaneous methods.
- Over-reliance on dense optical flow may reduce robustness in sparse motion or challenging lighting scenarios.
- Fine-tuning strategy (training only last layer in some experiments) appears arbitrary and underexplored.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and well-evaluated work that merits acceptance (accept for NIPS 2014).
