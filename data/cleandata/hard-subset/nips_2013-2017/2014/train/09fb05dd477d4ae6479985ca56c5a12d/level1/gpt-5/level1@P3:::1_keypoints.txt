- Proposes LSDA: learn a category-invariant classifier→detector transformation on categories with boxes (B) and transfer it to categories without boxes (A).  
- Adaptation comprises: learn background class + fine-tune shared features, fine-tune category-specific detection weights for B, and transfer fc8 weight deltas to A via nearest neighbors.  
- Implemented with an AlexNet-like CNN and R-CNN pipeline; evaluated on ILSVRC-2013 with 100/100 split (all classes have images, only 100 have boxes).  
- LSDA raises mAP on held-out categories from 10.31% to 16.15% (≈50% relative), mainly from feature-level adaptation and background modeling; category-specific delta transfer gives modest extra gain.  
- Demonstrates scalability with a 7,604-category detector and a faster SPP-style variant; code/models released.  
- Strength: tackles practical problem of scaling detection to many categories with few box annotations.  
- Strength: simple, well-motivated approach with ablations isolating component contributions.  
- Strength: shows nontrivial gains on unseen categories and reduced localization/background errors.  
- Strength: releases models/code and demonstrates practical scalability.  
- Weakness: absolute performance on held-out classes remains low and category-specific transfer yields small improvement.  
- Weakness: nearest-neighbor heuristic in fc8 space is ad hoc and not well-justified versus semantic or visual alternatives.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single 100/100 split; no analysis of effect of number or composition of detection-annotated classes (m).  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to strong weakly supervised (MIL) baselines and OverFeat-style transfer; R-CNN upper bound not fully matched (no SVMs/bbox regression).  
- Weakness: 7.6K detector only qualitatively evaluated; needs a sampled quantitative audit.  
- Weakness: some training details underspecified (background sampling, class/region balancing, learning rates/schedules, score calibration).  
- Quality: methodology and ablations are reasonable and support the main claim that feature-level adaptation + background modeling transfers to unseen categories.  
- Clarity: generally clear but Section 3 should formalize fine-tuning objectives and detail background sampling, k choice, distance metric, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Originality: moderate—builds on R-CNN/SPP ideas but framing transfer to unseen categories and fc8-delta sharing is novel for 2014.  
- Significance: high practical impact by enabling detectors for thousands of categories with zero box labels; code release increases community value.  
- Suggested comparisons: tuned OverFeat cls→det baseline, MIL-based weakly supervised detector on A, and category-invariant bbox regression from B applied to A.  
- Suggested analyses: performance vs. m and different B compositions; alternative similarity metrics (WordNet/attributes/visual features) and sensitivity to k.  
- Suggested validation: small quantitative evaluation for a subset of the 7.6K classes (50–100) via crowdsourcing.  
- Suggested calibration: investigate whether score_i − score_bg corresponds to log-odds and if temperature/Platt scaling improves cross-category ranking.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, simple effective method, demonstrated gains, released resources, insightful error analysis.  
- Cons for acceptance: low absolute mAP, modest category-transfer gains, limited baselines/evaluation scope, heuristic choices lacking justification.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — sound, useful work with practical impact despite missing comparisons and limited absolute performance.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
