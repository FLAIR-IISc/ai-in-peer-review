- Proposes LSDA: convert image classifiers into detectors by learning a category-invariant classifier→detector adaptation from categories with boxes (B) and transferring it to held-out categories (A).
- Adaptation components: model background class, fine-tune shared CNN layers (1–7) for detector behavior, and transfer category-specific fc8 “delta” via nearest neighbors in l2-normalized fc8 space.
- Experiments on ILSVRC-2013 simulate 100 categories with boxes and 100 without; start from AlexNet-like pretraining and fine-tune on detection data for set B.
- Held-out 100 categories mAP improves from 10.31% (baseline) to 15.85% with category-invariant adaptation and to 16.15% with k=10 nearest-neighbor transfer (+50% relative).
- Error analysis shows marked reductions in background confusion and localization errors.
- Scales to a 7.6K-category detector (adapting 7,404 leaf categories without boxes) and offers a faster SPP/proposals variant achieving ~2 fps; models/code released.
- Builds directly on R-CNN fine-tuning idea but novelly learns a transferable adaptation to categories lacking box annotations.
- Related to weakly supervised detection, domain adaptation, and concurrent CNN detectors, but differs by explicitly learning and transferring the classifier→detector transformation.
- Strength: addresses an important scalability problem with a clear, principled decomposition and strong empirical ablation and error analysis.
- Strength: demonstrated scalability and public release increase practical impact and reproducibility.
- Weakness: per-class transfer via nearest neighbors in fc8 is heuristic with limited justification and sensitivity analysis for k.
- Weakness: score_i − score_bg scoring is ad hoc; lacks calibration or probabilistic treatment comparisons.
- Weakness: evaluation protocol reuses val1 for training (as in R-CNN) rather than test-server split, which may limit generalization claims.
- Weakness: no direct comparison to weakly supervised MIL methods on ImageNet detection to contextualize performance.
- Clarity issues: missing fine-tuning hyperparameters, schedules, data balancing, and detailed sensitivity results (k variation, alternate neighbor defs).
- Originality: incremental over R-CNN but novel in learning transferable per-class deltas and explicit background modeling for held-out categories.
- Significance: high — practical method to leverage classification data to bootstrap many detectors; impactful 50% relative gain on held-out categories.
- Suggestions: evaluate alternative neighbor metrics (WordNet/semantic, intermediate visual features, learned embeddings) and provide sensitivity plots for k and layer adaptation strategies.
- Suggestions: include lightweight calibration baselines, per-class/semantic-family analyses, long-tail behavior in 7.6K setting, and discuss/mitigate negative transfer.
- Acceptance arguments: tackles an important bottleneck with a practical, scalable approach, solid ablations, and released resources.
- Rejection arguments: some heuristic choices are insufficiently justified, limited comparisons to weakly supervised methods, and absolute performance lags fully supervised detectors.
- Recommendation: accept after minor clarifications and expanded analyses as suggested.
