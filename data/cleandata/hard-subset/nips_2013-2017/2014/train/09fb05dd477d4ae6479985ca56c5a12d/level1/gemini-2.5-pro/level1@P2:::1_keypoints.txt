- Introduces LSDA: convert image classifiers into object detectors by treating the shift from classification to detection as a transferable domain-adaptation problem, using source categories with bounding boxes to adapt target categories without them.  
- Builds on R-CNN and proposes a three-stage pipeline: pre-training on ImageNet, category-invariant fine-tuning of layers 1–7 on detection data, and category-specific adaptation of fc8 by applying averaged weight deltas from nearest supervised classes.  
- Empirically strong: thorough evaluation on ILSVRC2013 with ablations showing category-invariant adaptation provides the majority of gains.  
- Ablation/error analysis demonstrates LSDA reduces localization errors and background confusion, explaining its effectiveness.  
- Presents a complete pipeline and large-scale demonstration (7.6K detectors) with public release of models and code for reproducibility.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized; figures and experimental protocol effectively communicate the method.  
- Original and novel: reframes classifier→detector as a learnable, transferable adaptation and introduces a practical scheme to adapt unseen categories.  
- Significant impact: provides a scalable path for large-scale detection and is likely to influence weakly-supervised and transfer-learning research.  
- Category-specific adaptation is heuristic (k-NN averaged weight deltas) and could be improved with a learned mapping (e.g., an MLP).  
- Lacks comparison to contemporary MIL-based methods on large-scale benchmarks, limiting perspective (though such methods weren’t evaluated on ILSVRC then).  
- Performance gap remains: held-out categories (16.15% mAP) trail fully supervised categories (24.38% mAP), so further work is needed to close the gap.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, novel, significant, and well-presented, with minor heuristic limitations noted as future directions.
