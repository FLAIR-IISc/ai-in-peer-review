- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers to object detectors using bounding boxes for only a subset of categories.  
- Frames classification-to-detection transfer as a domain adaptation problem (classification as source, detection as target).  
- Three-stage approach: pre-train CNN on classification, adapt network with subset of detection data for category-invariant features, use nearest neighbors in feature space for category-specific adaptation.  
- Novelty: first to generalize adaptation to held-out categories with no detection data, distinct from prior detection domain-adaptation work.  
- Builds on R-CNN and domain-adaptation literature while addressing large-scale detection scaling.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear methodology and thorough experiments on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection.  
- Ablation study convincingly shows component importance and ~50% relative mAP gain over classification-only baseline.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations (gap to oracle performance) and suggest future MIL work.  
- Experiments are well-designed, but computational cost of adaptation and broader comparisons to domain-adaptation methods are underexplored.  
- Clarity: well-organized, clearly written, with effective figures and reproducible methodology details.  
- Nearest-neighbor category-specific adaptation needs stronger justification.  
- Originality: novel contribution in adapting to categories without detection labels; nearest-neighbor adaptation is a distinguishing idea.  
- Significance: addresses a highly important problem, demonstrating scalability to 7,604 categories and a 2fps fast version.  
- Strengths: meaningful problem, novel framing, thorough validation, scalability to thousands of categories, code/models provided.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to state-of-the-art domain-adaptation methods, weak justification for Euclidean fc8 NN, minimal discussion of adaptation cost, reliance on weak image-level labels.  
- Pro acceptance: solves a practical, significant problem with novel, well-validated approach and shared resources.  
- Con acceptance: insufficient comparisons to related methods, some methodological details need justification, unclear comparison to top MIL approaches on large-scale detection.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution meeting NIPS criteria; recommend acceptance.
