- Introduces LSDA: learn a transferable classifier-to-detector transformation to create detectors for classes with only image-level labels.
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation: learn transformation on "source" classes with boxes and apply to "target" classes without boxes.
- Multi-stage pipeline: pretrain CNN on classification, category-invariant fine-tuning on detection (shared layers + background), then category-specific adaptation of final layer.
- Category-specific adaptation estimates weight deltas for unlabeled classes by averaging k-nearest-neighbor deltas in classifier weight space.
- Builds on CNN/R-CNN successes but removes the need for bounding boxes for every class.
- Distinct from MIL-based weakly supervised methods by transferring from fully supervised subset rather than solving MIL directly.
- Novel application of domain adaptation across tasks (classification → detection) rather than across visual domains for the same task.
- High impact: addresses scaling bottleneck of collecting bounding boxes and demonstrates a 7,604-category detector.
- Methodologically original and intuitive: decomposition into category-invariant and category-specific adaptations is well-motivated.
- Strong empirical support: ablation studies, 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories, and detailed error analysis.
- Shows LSDA reduces localization and background confusion errors, indicating it learns key detection behaviors.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and authors release code/models, aiding reproducibility and impact.
- Weakness: category-specific k-NN delta averaging is heuristic and yields only marginal mAP gain.
- Weakness: no experimental comparison to MIL-based weakly supervised approaches; conceptual trade-offs not fully explored.
- Weakness: evaluation uses a simulated setting (holding out 100 of 200 ILSVRC classes), so source/target similarity may overestimate real-world transfer.
- Pro acceptance: significant problem, elegant transferable solution, solid experiments, and high reproducibility.
- Con acceptance: ad-hoc category-specific step with small benefit and possibly limited generalization to diverse target sets.
- Overall conclusion: strong, original contribution with minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
