- Proposes LSDA to adapt image classifiers into object detectors via domain adaptation to address bounding-box scarcity.  
- Leverages abundant image-level labels plus limited detection data to enable detection for thousands of categories without bounding boxes.  
- Implements the adaptation as a CNN-based transformation from classification to detection.  
- Demonstrates a 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories and scales to a 7.6K-category detector on ImageNet.  
- Releases models, code, and implementation details to support reproducibility.  
- Addresses a significant real-world scalability problem in object detection.  
- Presents a novel, scalable approach distinct from prior methods.  
- Solid technical quality with ablation studies and informative error analysis.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful visual and quantitative results.  
- Evaluation is limited to ImageNet, reducing evidence of generalizability.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparison to related methods (e.g., MIL-based or other domain-adaptation techniques).  
- There remains a performance gap versus fully supervised oracle detectors, with limited discussion on closing it.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful, technically sound, reproducible, and improves baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow evaluation scope and insufficient comparison to alternative weakly supervised approaches.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
