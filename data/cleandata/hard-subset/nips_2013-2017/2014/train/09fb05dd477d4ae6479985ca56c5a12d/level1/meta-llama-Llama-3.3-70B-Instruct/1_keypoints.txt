- Proposes LSDA to transform image classifiers into object detectors without bounding boxes for all categories.  
- Tackles scaling object detection to tens of thousands of categories by using image-level labels and adapting CNNs.  
- Builds on domain adaptation, multiple instance learning, and deep CNNs with a novel classifier-to-detector adaptation.  
- Demonstrates on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection, achieving a 50% relative mAP improvement over a classification-only baseline.  
- Problem statement is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Method is novel and empirically effective.  
- Strong experimental results on a large-scale detection challenge.  
- Provides thorough error analysis that highlights mistake types and improvement areas.  
- Relies on large amounts of classification data, which may not always be available.  
- Requires a small amount of detection data for fine-tuning, limiting applicability to categories with no detection labels.  
- Potential for overfitting to adaptation data, which may reduce generalizability to new categories.  
- Overall: significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses, but revisions needed to address limitations.
