This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), for adapting image classifiers into object detectors. The main idea is to learn the difference between the two tasks and transfer this knowledge to classifiers for categories without bounding box annotated data. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their approach on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge and show significant improvement over a baseline of just using raw classifier weights on object proposal regions.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its advantages. The related work section is comprehensive, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the differences between their approach and previous work.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel algorithm for adapting image classifiers into object detectors, which has the potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories.
* The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed algorithm on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, and the results show significant improvement over a baseline.
* The authors provide a detailed analysis of the types of errors that their system makes on the held-out object categories, which provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the approach.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes that the classification data is available for all categories, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art object detection algorithms, which makes it difficult to evaluate the performance of the proposed algorithm in the context of the current state of the art.
