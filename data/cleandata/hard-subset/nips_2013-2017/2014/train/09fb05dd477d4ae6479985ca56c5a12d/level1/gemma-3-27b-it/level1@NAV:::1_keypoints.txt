- Proposes LSDA: adapt image-level classifiers into detectors by learning a transformation from categories with bounding boxes to those without.  
- Tackles the expensive bounding-box annotation bottleneck by leveraging plentiful image-level labels.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation between classification and detection tasks.  
- Builds on CNN-based detection (e.g., R-CNN) and transfers knowledge from annotated to unannotated categories.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation, method, and experimental setup.  
- Shows substantial improvement over a baseline on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge.  
- Ablation studies convincingly isolate the contribution of each LSDA component.  
- Provides error analysis and demonstrates scaling to over 7,000 categories plus a fast detection option.  
- Code and models are publicly released, improving reproducibility.  
- Positioning relative to weakly-supervised, contrastive, and self-supervised domain adaptation work is limited.  
- Experiments focus mainly on ImageNet, leaving generalization to other datasets unclear.  
- Discussion of limitations, failure cases, and biases from noisy image-level labels is brief.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with recommendation to accept.
