- Problem: Addresses scarcity of bounding-box annotations for large-scale object detection.  
- Method: Proposes LSDA to adapt image classifiers into object detectors via category-invariant and category-specific adaptation.  
- Category-invariant step: Fine-tunes a CNN on a subset of detection-labeled categories including a background class.  
- Category-specific step: Approximates adaptation for held-out classes by transferring averaged fc8 parameter deltas from nearest neighbors.  
- Results: On ILSVRC13, held-out mAP rises from 10.3% to 16.2% (+50% relative); scales to a 7.6K-class detector; offers a faster SPP/proposal variant.  
- Related work: Builds on R-CNN and AlexNet; relates to MIL weakly supervised detection and domain adaptation literature.  
- Quality: Methodology is sound; ablation isolates gains (background modeling, feature adaptation, fc8 deltas) with significance testing.  
- Main weakness: Category-specific transfer is heuristic (nearest-neighbor averaging in fc8) and under-explored.  
- Missing comparisons: Lacks head-to-head evaluation versus strong weakly supervised baselines and alternative adaptation/regression schemes.  
- Implementation trade-offs: Foregoes SVMs and bbox regression for simplicity but leaves accuracy trade-offs unquantified.  
- Clarity: Clearly written with training protocol, data splits, and implementation details; some hyperparameters (k, distance normalization) need more detail.  
- Originality: Novel framing of classification-to-detection as task/domain adaptation; pragmatic fc8-delta transfer is an interesting contribution.  
- Significance: Scaling detection to thousands of classes is important; releasing a 7.6K detector and code increases impact despite remaining gap to full supervision.  
- Pros: Practical adaptation framework with substantial gains, strong empirical analysis and diagnostics, scalable and faster variants, code/models released.  
- Cons: Heuristic category transfer, missing strong baseline comparisons, no bbox regression/SVM analysis in partial-supervision setting.  
- Overall recommendation: Solid, impactful contribution; recommend acceptance.
