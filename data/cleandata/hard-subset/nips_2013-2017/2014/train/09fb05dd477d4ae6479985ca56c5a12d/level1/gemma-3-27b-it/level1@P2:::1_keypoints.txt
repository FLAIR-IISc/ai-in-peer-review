- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from image-level classifiers to object detectors using a subset of categories with boxes, then apply to held-out categories.  
- Frames the problem as simultaneous domain and task adaptation, using fine-tuning and nearest-neighbor transfer.  
- Well-positioned in literature, citing R-CNN, MIL, domain adaptation, and modern CNN advances.  
- Clearly identifies the scalability bottleneck caused by expensive bounding-box annotations.  
- Addresses a significant practical problem: scaling detection with limited bounding-box data.  
- Novel combination of domain adaptation, transfer learning, and nearest-neighbor adaptation for held-out categories.  
- Strong empirical gains: ~50% relative mAP improvement over classifier-weight baseline on ImageNet detection.  
- Demonstrates scalability: builds a 7.6K detector and reports reasonable speed (~2 fps).  
- Public code and models released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Provides detailed analysis and error breakdown showing reduced localization and background confusion.  
- Relies heavily on quality of the pre-trained classifier; sensitivity to different pre-training datasets is not explored.  
- Nearest-neighbor selection process appears ad-hoc; choice of similarity metric and k not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Limited direct comparison or deeper discussion of MIL-based methods and potential synergies.  
- Figure 2 (training pipeline) is somewhat complex and could be clarified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a critical problem, novel and effective method, strong results, code release, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on pre-trained classifier and need for refinement/analysis of nearest-neighbor adaptation.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommended as Accept (Strong Accept).
