- Proposes LSDA: adapt CNN image classifiers into object detectors to scale detection to tens of thousands of categories using image-level labels plus a small set with bounding boxes.  
- Thorough related-work review covering R-CNN, MIL, and domain adaptation.  
- Clearly explains key insights: recognizing background, transferring category-invariant information, and adapting category-specific parameters.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Provides sufficient methodological detail on CNN architecture, training, and adaptation techniques.  
- Strong experimental results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection with a 50% relative mAP boost over the baseline.  
- Includes detailed analysis: ablation study and error analysis.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach addressing large-scale detection challenges.  
- Strength: clear explanation of techniques and convincing empirical gains.  
- Weakness: depends on image-level labels from search engines, which may be noisy or unreliable.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational cost for large-scale deployment.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to other state-of-the-art detectors such as Faster R-CNN and YOLO.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address noted weaknesses.  
- Requested revisions: add computational-cost analysis, stronger SOTA comparisons, and discussion of limitations and future work.
