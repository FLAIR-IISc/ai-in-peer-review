- Proposes LSDA: adapting classifiers into detectors using abundant image-level labels and learning a transformation from classification to detection with few boxed categories.  
- Targets scaling object detection to tens of thousands of categories.  
- Well-written paper with clear explanation of approach and technical details.  
- Experiments on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection show significant improvement over using raw classifier weights on proposals.  
- Provides thorough analysis, including ablation study and error analysis.  
- Strength: novel approach addressing large-scale detection scalability.  
- Strength: clear exposition of key insights and implementation.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness on a major benchmark.  
- Weakness: assumes classification data is available for all categories, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art object detection methods.  
- Weakness: does not discuss computational complexity or scalability costs of the method.  
- Review scores: high quality, clear, original, and significant.  
- Pro-accept: novel scalable approach, strong experimental validation, and thorough analysis.  
- Con-accept: questionable data availability assumption, missing SOTA comparisons, and absent complexity discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to include more comparisons and a computational complexity discussion.
