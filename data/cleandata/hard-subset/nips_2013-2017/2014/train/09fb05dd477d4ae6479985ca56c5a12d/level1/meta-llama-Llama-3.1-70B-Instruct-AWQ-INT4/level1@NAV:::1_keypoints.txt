- Proposes LSDA, a novel algorithm to adapt image classifiers into object detectors by learning and transferring the classifier→detector difference.  
- Method enables detection for categories lacking bounding-box annotations by transferring adaptation knowledge.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge and shows significant improvement over using raw classifier weights on proposals.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a clear explanation of the approach and advantages.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and contrasts the proposed method with prior work.  
- Strength: Novel algorithm with potential to scale detection to tens of thousands of categories.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on ImageNet and significant baseline improvements.  
- Strength: Detailed error analysis on held-out categories, offering insights into system weaknesses.  
- Weakness: Assumes classification data is available for all categories, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art object detection algorithms.  
- Acceptance argument: Novel, scalable approach with solid empirical gains and informative error analysis.  
- Rejection argument: Unrealistic data availability assumption and insufficient SOTA comparisons; needs more evaluation and discussion.  
- Overall: Promising and well-presented work that would benefit from more comparisons and addressing the classification-data assumption.
