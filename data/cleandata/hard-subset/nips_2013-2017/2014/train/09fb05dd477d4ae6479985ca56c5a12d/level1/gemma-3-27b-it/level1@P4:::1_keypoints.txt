- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors using categories with bounding boxes, then apply it to adapt classifiers for categories without detection labels.
- Addresses a relevant problem: scales detection by leveraging abundant image-level labels to reduce bounding-box annotation needs.
- Clear, well-defined approach with three insights: model background, transfer category-invariant info, and handle category-specific differences.
- Strong experimental results: large mAP improvement (+50% relative) over classifier-weight baseline and convincing ablation study.
- Large-scale demonstration: builds a 7.6K detector and releases models/software for reproducibility.
- Provides qualitative error analysis showing improved localization.
- Relies heavily on ImageNet pre-training, raising questions about generalization to other datasets or domains.
- Nearest-neighbor based adaptation (k=10) in fc8 space is somewhat arbitrary and not well-justified.
- Lacks direct quantitative comparison to Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) methods on ImageNet detection.
- Initial implementation appears computationally expensive; accessibility and resource requirements are not fully discussed.
- Unclear how the weakly annotated nature of classification data (single image-level labels with multiple objects) affects performance.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a critical scaling challenge, is technically sound, shows convincing results, and provides code/models.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalizability due to pre-training reliance, ad-hoc nearest-neighbor choice, and missing MIL comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective approach with significant contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance.
