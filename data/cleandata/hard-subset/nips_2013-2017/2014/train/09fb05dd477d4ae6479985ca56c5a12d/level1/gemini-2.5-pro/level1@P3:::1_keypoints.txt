- Tackles scaling object detection by transforming image classifiers into detectors to avoid costly bounding-box annotation for every class.  
- Proposes LSDA: frame classifier-to-detector conversion as domain adaptation using shared CNN feature fine-tuning and per-class weight adaptation.  
- Learns category-invariant adaptation by fine-tuning convolutional layers on detection data and adding an explicit background class.  
- Uses a heuristic category-specific adaptation: approximate detector weight deltas for unseen classes by averaging k-nearest neighbor deltas in classifier weight space.  
- Demonstrates experimental setup by holding out 100 of 200 ILSVRC2013 classes to test adaptation to classes with no bounding-box data.  
- Strong empirical results: ablation shows both feature and weight adaptations help, yielding ~50% relative mAP improvement over classification baseline.  
- Error analysis shows LSDA reduces localization and background confusion errors typical of classifiers applied to detection.  
- Produces a large-scale release (7.6K category detector) and is clearly written with effective figures and organization.  
- Main weakness: the category-specific k-NN averaging is heuristic and lacks theoretical justification or exploration of principled alternatives.  
- Limited comparison/discussion with Multiple Instance Learning and other weakly-supervised detection approaches.  
- Omission of SVM training and bounding-box regression (from R-CNN) is justified for speed but the resulting performance upper-bound is a “softmax-only” R-CNN and should be clarified/quantified.  
- Significant gap remains between LSDA on held-out classes (16.15% mAP) and full-supervision oracle (22.56% mAP), needing deeper analysis of causes and remedies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, introduces a novel adaptation-based solution, strong validated experiments, high impact potential, and released resources.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic per-class adaptation, limited related-method comparisons, and unclear performance trade-offs relative to full R-CNN pipeline.  
- Conclusion: overall excellent and impactful work with minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
