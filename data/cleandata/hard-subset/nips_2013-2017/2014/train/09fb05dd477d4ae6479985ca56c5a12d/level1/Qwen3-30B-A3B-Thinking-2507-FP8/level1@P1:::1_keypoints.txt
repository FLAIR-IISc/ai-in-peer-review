Summary: LSDA learns a transformation from classifiers to detectors using boxed-annotated categories and applies it to unannotated categories to enable large-scale detection from classification data.

Quality: Technically sound with rigorous experiments, clear ablation study, error analysis showing improved localization/background rejection, and released code/models.

Originality: Novel domain-adaptation approach to transfer detection ability across categories and a clever nearest-neighbor transfer of adaptation parameters.

Significance: Enables a 7,600-category detector using mostly classification data, addressing annotation bottlenecks for large-scale detection.

Clarity: Well-written, well-organized, with clear figures and reproducible implementation details.

Limited comparisons: Does not sufficiently compare against other state-of-the-art detectors (e.g., Fast/Faster R-CNN).

Nearest-neighbor justification: Uses k=10 without thorough justification or sensitivity analysis on k's effect.

Scalability concerns: May not generalize to categories very dissimilar to those with bounding boxes; limitations and remedies are not fully discussed.

Speedup details lacking: The 2 fps claim for the 7.6K detector is not sufficiently explained (region proposals, SPP specifics).

Pro-acceptance: Significant, novel, and well-validated contribution with 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories.

Con-acceptance: Needs more comprehensive baselines, deeper analysis of k, and discussion of scalability for dissimilar categories.

Final recommendation: Recommend acceptance—minor weaknesses do not undermine the paper's significant, well-supported contribution.
