- Problem: Scale object detection when only a subset of classes has bounding-boxes and many classes have only image-level labels.  
- Method: LSDA learns category-invariant detection adaptation (background class + shared-layer fine-tuning) on classes B and transfers category-specific output-layer deltas to classes A via nearest neighbors in fc8.  
- Scoring: At test time per-region scores for K classes plus background are computed and combined as score_i − score_background.  
- Experimental setup: ILSVRC-2013 with 200 classes, using detection labels for first 100 classes and only image labels for the remaining 100 to simulate partial supervision.  
- Main result: LSDA raises held-out-class mAP from 10.31% to 16.15% (+50% relative), with most gain from category-invariant adaptation and a smaller but significant gain from category-specific transfer (p=0.017).  
- Error analysis: LSDA reduces localization and background confusions compared to classification-only baseline.  
- Large-scale demo: Built a 7.6K-class detector by combining 200 detection-supervised and 7.4K classification-only classes, plus a faster SPP-based variant (~0.5s/image).  
- Novelty: Introduces task adaptation (classification→detection) learned on a subset of classes and transferred to unlabeled classes, distinct from prior domain adaptation and MIL work.  
- Strength — experimental design: Careful, standard-benchmark evaluation with realistic partial-supervision setup and clear ablations.  
- Strength — ablations & stats: Ablations isolate background modeling, shared-layer fine-tuning, and output-layer transfer; category-specific effect is statistically significant.  
- Strength — diagnostics & release: Useful error breakdowns and public release of code/models increase practical impact.  
- Weakness — baselines missing: Lacks comparison to a category-agnostic bounding-box regressor or stronger shared-layer fine-tuning baselines/calibration.  
- Weakness — ad hoc transfer: Nearest-neighbor delta transfer in fc8 is heuristic with modest absolute gain; sensitivity to k and distance metric not analyzed.  
- Weakness — scoring clarity: Unclear whether score combination uses logits or softmax and whether it corresponds to log-odds; needs clarification for reproducibility.  
- Weakness — scalability analysis: No experiments showing how performance scales with number m of detection-supervised classes (m→mAP curve missing).  
- Weakness — 7.6K evaluation: Qualitative large-scale results lack quantitative or human-verified evaluation on a held-out subset.  
- Clarity suggestions: Explicitly state logits vs softmax for scoring, consolidate ablation descriptions for Table 1, and fix minor typographical issues.  
- Suggested analyses: Sensitivity of k and neighbor metrics, comparison to WordNet/semantic neighbors, m-varying experiments (e.g., 25/50/100 supervised classes), and testing a shared category-agnostic regressor or calibration layer.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, original, practically useful, with convincing empirical evidence despite reasonable limitations.
