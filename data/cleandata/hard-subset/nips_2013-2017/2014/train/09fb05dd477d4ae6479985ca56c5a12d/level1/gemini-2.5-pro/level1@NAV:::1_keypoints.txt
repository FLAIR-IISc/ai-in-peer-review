- Tackles scaling object detection to many categories where bounding-box labels are too costly.  
- Proposes LSDA: learn a general classifier-to-detector transformation using categories with both label types.  
- Applies the learned transformation to adapt classifiers for new categories without bounding-box annotations.  
- Builds on R-CNN fine-tuning but extends it to a domain-adaptation setting with held-out target categories.  
- Significant practical impact: enables creation of a 7,604-category detector and addresses annotation bottleneck.  
- Novel idea: transferable, category-invariant adaptation from classification to detection.  
- Thorough experiments on ILSVRC2013 with clear ablation studies isolating each component’s contribution.  
- Achieves a substantial 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories.  
- Error analysis shows reduced localization errors and less background confusion, validating the approach.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient experimental detail.  
- Weakness: absolute mAP on held-out categories remains low (16.15%), far below fully supervised performance.  
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation via averaging k-nearest neighbor weight-deltas is heuristic and lacks theoretical grounding.  
- Argument for acceptance: elegant, novel, significant contribution with strong experimental validation.  
- Argument against acceptance: modest absolute performance on held-out classes highlights limitations of the approach.
