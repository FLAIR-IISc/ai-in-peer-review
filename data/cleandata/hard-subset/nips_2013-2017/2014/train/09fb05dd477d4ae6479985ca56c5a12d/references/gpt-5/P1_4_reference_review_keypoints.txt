- Paper proposes LSDA: convert classifiers to detectors by learning category-invariant (shared-layer fine-tuning + background class) and category-specific (transfer fc8 deltas from nearest base categories) adaptations.
- Evaluated on ILSVRC13 by using detection labels for 100/200 classes; shows ~50% relative mAP gain over naive classification-on-proposals and a 7.6K-class demo.
- Builds on R-CNN and deep feature transfer; differs from MIL-based weakly supervised detectors by leveraging a subset of detection labels to learn a transferable transform.
- Strength: addresses an important practical bottleneck of obtaining boxes for many categories.
- Strength: method is conceptually simple and practical to implement on standard CNNs.
- Strength: empirical ablations clearly show most gains come from shared-layer fine-tuning and background modeling; output-layer transfer adds a smaller but significant boost.
- Strength: demonstrates scalability (7.6K detector) and releases code/models.
- Strength: provides insightful error analysis showing reductions in background and localization errors.
- Weakness: absolute performance on held-out classes is modest (~16% mAP) and far from fully supervised upper bound.
- Weakness: abandoning SVMs and bbox regression to keep compatibility with unseen classes may reduce baseline performance; unclear how much this choice contributes to the gap.
- Weakness: comparison set lacks strong weakly supervised baselines and a simple baseline that freezes shared layers and retrains fc8 for held-out classes.
- Weakness: category-specific transfer is heuristic (Euclidean distance on L2-normalized fc8 weights); other similarity metrics or learned mappings are not explored.
- Weakness: limited analysis of how performance scales with m (number of detection-labeled categories).
- Weakness: some implementation/training details are sparse (sampling strategy, learning schedules, proposal/NMS settings) and minor typos/inconsistencies reduce reproducibility.
- Quality: technically sound and claims supported by ablations, but evaluation could be stronger with additional baselines and scaling analyses.
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but needs more training hyperparameters, precise dataset splits, and typographical fixes.
- Originality: novel idea to learn a classifier-to-detector transform on a subset of classes and transfer it to unseen classes; nearest-neighbor delta transfer is simple but original.
- Significance: moderate-to-high practical impact; relative gains without boxes are meaningful and the large-scale demo increases potential adoption.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to k and similarity metric; performance when adding SVMs/bbox reg only for base classes; mAP vs m curve; effect of adding background class alone; more training/proposal details.
- Suggestions: add stronger/weakly-supervised baselines, explore learned category-specific mappings, provide scaling curves and robustness studies, analyze calibration across classes, and improve notation and training detail clarity.
- Recommendation: weak accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses but paper would benefit from more baselines and analyses.
- Pros summary: practical regime, simple method with released code, solid ablations, scales to thousands of categories.
- Cons summary: modest absolute mAP, heuristic transfer with limited sensitivity analysis, missing implementation details.
