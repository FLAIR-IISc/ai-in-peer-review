- Paper proposes LSDA: adapting image classifiers into object detectors to scale detection to many categories using limited bounding box data.  
- Frames the task as domain adaptation from classification to detection and adapts both visual domain and task.  
- Builds on R-CNN but transfers detector knowledge to held-out categories without detection data.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on ImageNet with ~50% relative mAP improvement over a strong baseline.  
- Ablation study shows contributions of each component and analysis highlights reduced localization and background confusion errors.  
- Method is practical, leveraging pre-trained CNNs and requiring far fewer bounding boxes than standard approaches.  
- Authors release models and code, aiding reproducibility and future work.  
- Main weakness: reliance on nearest-neighbor selection to transfer category-specific parameters, which may be sensitive to distance metric and k.  
- Limited analysis of failure cases—needs deeper qualitative examination of why specific errors occur.  
- Comparisons to state-of-the-art, especially MIL-based approaches, are limited and should be expanded or discussed.  
- Computational cost (training/inference time and memory for 7604-category detector) is underreported and needs more detail.  
- Suggestion to explore more principled weighting (e.g., similarity-weighted averaging) instead of simple averaging of nearest neighbors.  
- Request for additional examples of diverse false positives and a breakdown of LSDA vs. Oracle performance for categories with bounding boxes.  
- Questions about memory footprint and how performance scales as category count increases.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to study nearest-neighbor sensitivity, discuss limitations and MIL synergies, analyze failure cases and computational costs, and expand related-work comparisons.
