- Proposed LSDA converts classifiers to detectors with partial box supervision via category-invariant feature fine-tuning and category-specific fc8 weight transfer (δ) from nearest base neighbors, scoring regions by score_i − score_background.
- On ILSVRC13 with 100/200 detection labels, LSDA raises held-out class mAP from 10.31% to 16.15% (+50% relative) and demonstrates a scalable 7.6K-class detector with released code/models.
- Positioned relative to R-CNN, MIL weakly supervised detection, and domain adaptation work; novelty is adapting classification→detection for novel categories without boxes using feature shift + parameter transfer.
- Strength: clear problem focus addressing annotation bottleneck and a simple effective method leveraging existing CNNs and proposals with strong empirical gains and useful error analysis.
- Strength: ablations show background modeling and feature fine-tuning are primary drivers; category-specific transfer gives smaller additional gain.
- Strength: practical impact amplified by scalable demonstration and released resources.
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation is heuristic—nearest neighbors in fc8 may not reflect detection similarity and alternatives aren’t evaluated.
- Weakness: sensitivity to number of neighbors k and to choice/composition of base set B is underexplored.
- Weakness: category split (first 100 alphabetically) may introduce semantic skew; no results averaged over random/stratified splits.
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other baselines (e.g., R-CNN base-only inference, MIL on held-out classes, SVMs on deep features with proposal background).
- Weakness: omission of bbox regression and SVM post-processing leaves unclear how much gap to fully supervised methods is due to these choices.
- Weakness: incomplete implementation details (fine-tuning iterations, learning rates, negative sampling, proposal settings) hurt reproducibility.
- Weakness: statistical uncertainty is limited to a single test; broader confidence estimates (e.g., bootstrap CIs) are missing.
- Suggestion: learn a mapping from classification weights/features to δ (ridge regression or MLP) instead of nearest-neighbor transfer.
- Suggestion: evaluate alternative similarity metrics/metric learning, sensitivity to k and B size/composition, and average results over multiple random splits.
- Suggestion: add baselines—MIL on held-out classes, R-CNN base-only inference, and SVMs on deep features with proposal-derived background.
- Suggestion: test bbox regression trained on base classes to assess category-invariant localization benefits and quantify gap to supervised upper bound.
- Suggestion: clarify scoring rule (subtracting background logit vs softmax probabilities) and report calibration across categories.
- Suggestion: provide more ablations on which layers to fine-tune and how freezing early layers affects transfer.
- Quality: technically sound with experiments supporting core claims, though some category-specific choices are heuristic and need broader analyses.
- Clarity: generally clear and organized but needs consistent A/B definitions, formatting fixes, and full hyperparameter/data usage details.
- Originality: novel decomposition of category-invariant feature adaptation plus category-specific parameter transfer for detection without box labels.
- Significance: high practical significance for scaling detectors and reducing annotation burden; released resources increase impact.
- Recommendation: Accept — clear contribution with strong empirical evidence and high utility, pending additional sensitivity analyses and stronger baselines.
- Confidence: High.
