- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers to detectors when only a subset of categories has bounding-boxes by learning shared-layer adaptation and transferring category-specific fc8 deltas via nearest neighbors.
- Demonstrates gains on ILSVRC13: held-out classes mAP improves from 10.31% to 16.15% and builds a 7.6K-category detector; code/models released.
- Builds on R-CNN but addresses scalability by avoiding per-class bounding-box annotation; complementary to MIL-based weakly supervised approaches.
- Frames classification→detection as domain/task adaptation and fine-tunes feature extractor, unlike many shallow adaptation methods.
- Claimed novelty: first to adapt to held-out categories without any bounding boxes by transferring learned detector changes.
- Strength: tackles an important scalability problem with a simple, efficient decomposition (category-invariant + category-specific).
- Strength: solid empirical validation with ablations and error analysis showing reduced localization and background confusions.
- Strength: reproducible work with released code/models and an efficient SPP-based variant.
- Weakness: category-specific transfer via averaging fc8 deltas from nearest neighbors is ad hoc and under-justified.
- Weakness: sensitivity analysis for neighbor metric, layer choice (fc7 vs fc8), and k selection is brief and insufficient.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to relevant baselines (MIL methods, learned regression mapping, semantic transfers, bbox regression).
- Weakness: absolute performance on held-out classes remains low (~16% mAP) and the gap to supervised upper bound is not deeply analyzed.
- Weakness: scoring rule (score_i − score_background) is ad hoc; lacks probabilistic calibration analysis for large-scale detection.
- Weakness: data-split/annotation sourcing is unclear and may suggest potential leakage; clarify which images/categories are used for classification vs detection fine-tuning.
- Weakness: limited exploration of scalability variables (varying number of annotated categories m, domain similarity, proposal methods).
- Quality: technically sound with careful ablations, but relies on heuristic components and misses important baselines.
- Clarity: generally well written and organized; needs clearer details on data splits, k selection, neighbor metrics, and SPP variant performance.
- Originality: moderately novel—combines known components in a new, useful way to enable zero-bbox category adaptation.
- Significance: high practical impact for scaling detection to thousands of classes; influential for low-supervision detection research.
- Suggested improvements: learn a mapping from Wc→δ (ridge/MLP), analyze sensitivity to k/metric, add bbox regression and MIL baselines, calibrate scores, and study performance vs number of detection-labeled categories.
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, simple scalable method, empirical gains, released resources, compelling large-scale demo.
- Cons for acceptance: heuristic category transfer, missing baselines, modest absolute results, and clarity issues in experiments.
- Recommendation: weak accept—practical and influential contribution but would benefit from stronger baselines and clearer experimental details.
