- Proposes LSDA: convert image classifiers into object detectors by treating classification→detection as domain adaptation and transferring learned transformations from base (boxed) to held-out (unboxed) categories.  
- Method components: category-invariant adaptation via shared-layer fine-tuning and explicit background class; category-specific adaptation by averaging fc8 deltas from k nearest base classes.  
- Builds on CNN classification (Krizhevsky) and region-based detection (R-CNN) but removes the need for detection labels for all classes; complementary to MIL weakly supervised methods.  
- Clear motivation and practical impact: reduces bounding-box annotation burden for large-scale detection.  
- Strong empirical evidence: ~50% relative mAP gain on held-out classes (10.31% → 16.15% on ILSVRC13 val2) with ablation studies.  
- Key insight: learning a background class and category-invariant feature adaptation are crucial to reduce background confusion and localization errors.  
- Large-scale demonstration: qualitative 7.6K-class detector and a faster SPP-based variant show feasibility at scale.  
- Reproducibility: uses Caffe and promises to release models/code.  
- Lacks comparisons to alternative transfer baselines under the same supervision budget (e.g., invariant-only network, class-agnostic bbox regression, alternative category-specific mappings).  
- Category-specific adaptation (k-NN delta averaging) is intuitive but ad hoc; choice of metric and k lacks deep justification and alternatives (WordNet, feature similarity, learned mappings) are not explored.  
- Calibration of final score (scorei − scorebackground) is unclear and potentially unprincipled if using softmax probabilities; logits vs probabilities and alternative calibrations should be clarified/evaluated.  
- 7.6K-class result is only qualitative; quantitative proxies or subset evaluations would strengthen the large-scale claim.  
- Sensitivity to number of supervised detection classes m is not reported; performance vs m (e.g., 25/50/100/150) is needed.  
- Architecture choices avoid SVMs and bbox regression for simplicity, but claimed 1–2 mAP gap to R-CNN components is not quantified on the same split; class-agnostic bbox regression could help held-out classes.  
- Empirical analysis is solid with ablations and error breakdowns, but category-specific adaptation remains heuristic without theoretical backing.  
- Paper is well written and organized; training pipeline and A/B notation are clear.  
- Requests clarity on whether background subtraction uses logits or probabilities and more explicit numbers for each adaptation component’s effect.  
- Original framing (classifier→detector as domain adaptation with background modeling) is novel, and output-layer delta averaging is a simple new instantiation.  
- High practical significance: enables scaling detection with limited bounding-box supervision and likely to inspire follow-ups.  
- Suggested experiments/questions for authors: sensitivity to k and neighbor metric, mAP vs m, exact background subtraction formulation and alternative calibration, class-agnostic bbox regressor effect, quantitative validation for subset of 7.6K classes, comparison to objectness+classification baseline.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, simple scalable method, convincing benchmark gains, code/models release likely.  
- Cons against acceptance: heuristic category-specific adaptation, missing baselines and sensitivity analyses, large-scale results qualitative only.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept; solid practical contribution that would benefit from strengthened evaluations. Confidence: high.
