- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation to convert image classifiers into object detectors to address scarce bounding-box labels.  
- Fine-tunes a classifier on categories with boxes and transfers that adaptation to categories without box annotations.  
- Demonstrates substantial gains — ~50% relative mAP improvement over a raw-classifier baseline on ImageNet detection.  
- Positions work relative to R-CNN (builds on classifier-to-detector ideas) and contrasts with MIL and other domain-adaptation methods.  
- Claims novelty as first to adapt classifiers to detect held-out categories with no detection labels.  
- Tackles an important practical problem: scaling detection despite limited bounding-box annotations.  
- Innovative idea of learning a generalizable transformation from classification to detection networks.  
- Strong empirical results with ablation studies supporting component contributions.  
- Provides detailed error analysis and localization visualizations for deeper insights.  
- Releases code, model definitions, and weights to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Well-written and clearly structured paper.  
- Lacks direct comparison to recent competitive weakly-supervised (especially MIL-based) detection methods on ImageNet.  
- Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, notably the category-specific k parameter.  
- Needs deeper discussion of adaptation complexity, time/performance trade-offs, and training-data-size effects.  
- Over-reliance on the slow R-CNN pipeline; transferable applicability to faster detectors (YOLO/SSD) is not demonstrated.  
- Baseline (raw classifier on proposals) appears weak; stronger baselines should be evaluated.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, effective approach with weaknesses that are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, including comparisons to MIL methods, detailed k analysis, discussion of portability to modern detectors, and stronger baselines.
