- Overall: LSDA adapts classifiers into detectors to scale object detection and is recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Problem: Tackles the annotation bottleneck by leveraging abundant image-level labels to create detectors for many categories.
- Approach: Learns a generic transformation from classification to detection and applies it to categories lacking bounding boxes.
- Related work: Properly situates LSDA relative to R-CNN, MIL, and domain adaptation, highlighting a gap in large-scale zero-shot detection.
- Empirical results: Shows ~50% relative mAP improvement over baseline with clear ablation studies.
- Method clarity: Well-defined algorithm with a logical split into category-invariant and category-specific adaptation.
- Practicality: Authors released code/models and provide a faster spatial pyramid pooling implementation.
- Analysis: Thorough error analysis and compelling localization visualizations.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to stronger detection frameworks (e.g., Faster R-CNN, YOLO).
- Weakness: Category-specific adaptation uses an ad-hoc nearest-neighbor heuristic; more principled mapping (and analysis of k) is advised.
- Weakness: Assumes ImageNet classification labels are reliable; needs discussion of label noise impact and mitigation strategies.
- Weakness: Evaluation uses a single CNN architecture (Krizhevsky et al.); robustness across architectures should be explored.
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of failure cases, particularly for fine-grained categories.
- Originality: Combination of existing techniques into large-scale, zero-shot detection is a significant and impactful contribution.
- Recommendation details: Accept with minor revisions—compare to stronger baseline, justify or replace NN heuristic, address label noise, and discuss architectural limitations.
