- Proposes LSDA: transforms image classifiers into object detectors without bounding boxes for all categories by treating the problem as domain adaptation.
- Key insights: background recognition is crucial, category-invariant feature transfer is possible, and category-specific classifier→detector differences exist.
- Methodology is technically sound with well-designed experiments and a convincing ablation showing fine-tuning layers 1–7 yields most improvements.
- Reports statistical significance for the main result (p = 0.017).
- Implementation details support reproducibility; uses Caffe, releases weights, and provides dataset splits.
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; clear motivation and logical exposition, with only minor missing CNN architecture specifics.
- Novel contribution: first method to adapt to held-out categories with no detection data; nearest-neighbor category-specific adaptation is a practical idea.
- Significant impact: reduces annotation burden, enabling large-scale detection (demonstrated on 7604 categories) and 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out classes.
- Demonstrates practical scalability and faster detection times with improved region proposals.
- Strengths: solves an important problem, strong experimental validation, large-scale demonstration, clear writing, and public code/models.
- Weakness: nearest-neighbor category-specific adaptation is heuristic and lacks justification for using Euclidean distance in fc8.
- Weakness: does not study how performance scales with the size of the annotated subset B.
- Weakness: comparisons are limited to R-CNN; lacks evaluation against newer detectors like Fast/Faster R-CNN.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically valuable work that meets NIPS criteria; recommendation: Accept.
