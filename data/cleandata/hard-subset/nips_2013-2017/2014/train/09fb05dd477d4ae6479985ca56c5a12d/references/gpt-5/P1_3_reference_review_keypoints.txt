- Proposes LSDA: convert image classifiers to detectors by category-invariant fine-tuning and category-specific weight transfer, enabling detection with few bounding-box labels.  
- Method: learn background class and fine-tune shared layers using detection data for subset B; transfer fc8 deltas from k nearest neighbors to categories A; use background-adjusted scores on region proposals.  
- Results: On ILSVRC13 with 100 held-out classes, LSDA raises mAP on held-out classes from 10.31% to 16.15% (+50% relative).  
- Ablations: Most gains come from learning a background class and fine-tuning conv/fc layers (1–7); fc8 delta transfer adds a smaller but significant boost.  
- Large-scale demo: Adapts 7,404 classifiers to build a 7,604-category detector and provides a faster SPP/proposals variant (qualitative results only).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN’s fine-tuning insight but uniquely adapts to categories with no detection labels, distinct from MIL and standard domain adaptation.  
- Technical soundness: Approach is straightforward, well-motivated, and aligns with known effects of detection fine-tuning; decomposition into invariant and specific adaptation is reasonable.  
- Empirical support: Ablation study and error analysis support claims; localization and background confusion errors are reduced.  
- Heuristic element: Category-specific transfer via nearest neighbors in fc8 weight space is simple but heuristic.  
- Missing sensitivity analyses: Lacks exploration of k, neighbor distance metric (cosine vs Euclidean), and alternatives like k=1 or full average.  
- Missing scalability analysis: No study of performance vs. number of detection-labeled categories m (e.g., m=25/50/100).  
- Calibration and scoring: Background-adjusted softmax subtraction used; alternatives (sigmoid per-class, Platt scaling) are not explored.  
- 7.6k model evaluation: No quantitative metrics for the large-scale detector; only qualitative demonstration provided.  
- Comparison baseline: No controlled comparison to stronger R-CNN variants that add SVMs and bbox regression for B, or transferring bbox regression to A.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with helpful figures, but could tighten Section 3, clarify losses/ROI sampling, and fix minor typos.  
- Originality: Novel idea to transfer classifier→detector via shared transformation and weight-space similarity; not present in prior literature then.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem for scaling detection with limited box labels; shows substantial relative improvements and practical impact.  
- Pros: Practical path to large-scale detection, clear decomposition, strong empirical gains, simple method with released models and demo.  
- Cons: Heuristic category transfer, limited sensitivity studies, no quantitative 7.6k evaluation, unexamined bbox-regression transfer and calibration/NMS choices.  
- Suggestions: Provide sensitivity analyses for k/metric and semantic neighbors, learn Wc→Wd mapping, vary m, transfer bbox regression, calibrate scores, analyze cross-category NMS, and add quantitative proxies for 7.6k model.  
- Recommendation: Accept (7/10) — clear, novel, practically useful approach with solid evidence despite some missing analyses.
