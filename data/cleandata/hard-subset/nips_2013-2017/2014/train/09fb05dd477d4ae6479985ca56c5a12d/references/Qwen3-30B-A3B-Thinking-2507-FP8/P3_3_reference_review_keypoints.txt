- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers into detectors using domain adaptation learned from a small set of bounding-box-annotated categories.
- Builds on R-CNN fine-tuning ideas and frames the problem as source (classification) to target (detection) domain adaptation, leveraging background modeling, category-invariant features, and category-specific adaptation.
- Strong empirical result: ~50% relative mAP improvement over classification-only baseline on held-out categories, supported by thorough ablation studies.
- Practical impact: scalable approach that produced a 7,604-category detector using mostly image-level labels, implemented by simple fine-tuning of existing CNN architectures.
- Well-designed experiments with appropriate baselines, error-type analysis, and demonstrated reduction in localization errors after adaptation.
- Weakness: lacks direct quantitative comparison to MIL-based and other weakly supervised detection methods.
- Weakness: relies on a nearest-neighbor assumption for category-specific adaptation without thorough evaluation when semantic neighbors are dissimilar.
- Weakness: computational efficiency claims (e.g., training time reduction) lack hardware details and direct comparison to standard R-CNN training.
- Weakness: scalability beyond 7,604 categories is not analyzed; nearest-neighbor adaptation may degrade as category count and semantic complexity increase.
- Overall: novel, technically sound, and impactful contribution with strong experimental support.
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested revisions to address comparisons, nearest-neighbor limitations, efficiency details, and scalability analysis.
