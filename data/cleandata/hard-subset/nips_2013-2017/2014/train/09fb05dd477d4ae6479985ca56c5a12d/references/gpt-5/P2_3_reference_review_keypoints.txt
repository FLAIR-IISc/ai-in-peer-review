- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers to object detectors when only some categories have bounding boxes by treating it as domain adaptation with two components — category-invariant fine-tuning (layers 1–7 + background) and category-specific transfer via nearest-neighbor δ averaging in output weights.  
- Reports ILSVRC13 results: held-out categories mAP rises from 10.31% (classification-only) to 15.85% with invariant adaptation and to 16.15% with category-specific transfer; includes 7.6K-category demo and faster SPP implementation.  
- Builds directly on R-CNN; novelty is learning a transferable “classification-to-detection” transformation to apply to unseen categories, distinct from MIL-based weakly supervised methods and prior adaptation work requiring boxes for all classes.  
- Strength: tackles high-impact scaling problem with a simple, interpretable method that leverages standard CNN fine-tuning and adds a background class and lightweight output-layer transfer.  
- Strength: solid ablation studies and error analysis showing background and localization error reduction; statistical test supports output-layer transfer effect.  
- Strength: practical impact via released code/models, large-scale demo, and runtime discussion.  
- Weakness: limited algorithmic novelty — most gains come from standard fine-tuning and background modeling; the output-layer transfer is heuristic and gives marginal mAP gains.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to strong weakly supervised baselines and broader sensitivity studies (dependence on number of detection categories m and neighbor count k).  
- Weakness: nearest-neighbor metric (L2 in fc8 weights) is ad hoc; alternatives (visual, semantic, learned metrics) are not evaluated.  
- Weakness: absolute performance still far below oracle detectors; limited discussion on combining with MIL or other approaches to close the gap.  
- Weakness: some evaluation/training details unclear (train/val splits, class-wise image overlap, calibration, NMS choices for 7.6K demo).  
- Quality: technically sound, reproducible (Caffe models released), ablations justify main claims; statistical testing is a plus.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized; could benefit from pseudocode and more explicit training hyperparameters.  
- Originality: moderate — useful framing and empirical validation but mechanisms are simple and close to existing practice.  
- Significance: high practical significance for scaling detection; solid but not groundbreaking scientific contribution.  
- Key questions for authors: how performance scales with m; sensitivity to k and neighbor metric; effect of MIL-based mining initialized from LSDA; whether training sets are disjoint to avoid leakage; quantitative evaluation for the 7.6K detector.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, clear empirical gains, ablations, public release, and scalability demonstration.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, missing baselines/sensitivity analyses, and low absolute accuracy in comparison to full-supervision oracle.  
- Recommendation: lean accept — valuable, practical contribution that would benefit from added sensitivity analyses and stronger baselines.
