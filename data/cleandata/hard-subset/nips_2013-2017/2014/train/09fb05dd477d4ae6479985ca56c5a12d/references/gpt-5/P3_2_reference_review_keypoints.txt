- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from image classifiers to detectors using detection data for a subset, then apply to held-out classes with only image-level labels.  
- Method has three parts: learn background and fine-tune layers 1–7 (category-invariant), apply held-out fc8 weights on adapted features, and add a category-specific delta averaged from nearest detection classes.  
- Reports ~50% relative improvement on held-out ILSVRC classes (10.3% → 15.9% mAP) and qualitative results for a 7.6K-class detector.  
- Positions work between MIL-based weakly supervised detection and per-class supervised detector adaptation; novel for adapting across task (classification→detection) and to held-out categories at scale.  
- Strength: clear, practically important problem framing that reduces per-class box annotation needs.  
- Strength: simple, coherent, and principled methodology with dominant contribution from background modeling and feature adaptation.  
- Strength: solid empirical study with held-out split, ablations disentangling components, and error analysis showing gains from background rejection and localization.  
- Strength: code/models released; discussion of speed-accuracy tradeoffs and faster SPP variant.  
- Weakness: absolute mAP on held-out classes remains low (~16%), raising questions about practical utility without further methods.  
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation (nearest-neighbor δ averaging) is heuristic, with limited justification for distance metric, k, and stability; negative transfer risk under-analyzed.  
- Weakness: limited baselines—need stronger comparisons like background-only adaptation, naive calibration, and MIL on same data for context.  
- Weakness: evaluation scope is narrow—quantitative results only on 100 held-out classes and only qualitative results for 7.6K detector.  
- Weakness: no analysis of how performance scales with number of detection-labeled classes m or whether gains saturate.  
- Weakness: architectural choices (avoiding SVMs/bbox regression) could confound whether limitations are from model capacity vs. supervision scarcity.  
- Quality: technically sound, careful experiments and statistical testing for δA, but some design choices remain heuristic.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clear; some implementation hyperparameters and sensitivity details could be expanded, though code release mitigates reproducibility concerns.  
- Originality: novel contribution in adapting classifiers to detectors for held-out categories and learning category-invariant transformations across tasks.  
- Significance: opens a practical path toward large-scale detection with limited box labels and is likely to be extended in follow-up work.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to m; robustness of NN δ to feature space/metric and negative transfer; whether learned regression from Wc→Wd was tried; impact of proposal method quality.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze alternative mappings (ridge regression, metric learning), vary feature spaces and k, add scaling study vs m, include hybrid detector baselines, and quantify a subset of 7.6K results via crowdsourcing or cross-dataset tests.  
- Pros summary: addresses annotation bottleneck, demonstrates importance of background and feature adaptation, provides error analysis and public resources.  
- Cons summary: low absolute mAP, heuristic and under-analyzed category transfer, limited evaluation beyond val splits.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — well-motivated, technically solid, and original with promising results despite modest absolute performance and heuristic components.
