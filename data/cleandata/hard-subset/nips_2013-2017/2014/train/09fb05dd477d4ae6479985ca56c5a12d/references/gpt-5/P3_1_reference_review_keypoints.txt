- Proposes LSDA: convert an ImageNet classifier to a detector by fine-tuning shared layers + adding a background class, and transfer category-specific final-layer deltas via nearest neighbors in fc8.  
- On ILSVRC13 with 100 classes having boxes and 100 held-out, LSDA raises held-out mAP from ~10.3% to ~16.1% (+50% relative).  
- Most gains stem from modeling background and fine-tuning category-invariant shared features; the category-specific delta transfer yields a smaller but significant boost.  
- Releases a practical 7.6K-class detector, code, and a faster SPP/proposals variant.  
- Novelty: learning a classifier→detector transformation on a subset of classes and transferring it to held-out classes is new relative to R-CNN and standard domain adaptation.  
- Strength: clear decomposition into category-invariant vs. category-specific adaptation and strong empirical/ablation evidence.  
- Strength: sensible, scalable ILSVRC13 evaluation with error diagnosis and realistic train/test split.  
- Strength: method is simple, architectural, and can be plugged into any ImageNet-trained classifier, increasing practical impact.  
- Weakness: category-specific transfer is heuristic (fc8 nearest neighbors) and under-motivated; alternatives (WordNet, embeddings, feature-space) not explored.  
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis—no study of varying m (number of classes with boxes) or k (neighbors) and no quantification of negative transfer risk.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to WSOD/MIL baselines (even on smaller benchmarks like PASCAL), limiting positioning against weakly supervised methods.  
- Weakness: oracle/upper-bound gap (boxes for all classes) not fully quantified or separated from architectural choices like SVMs/bbox regression.  
- Weakness: notation/exposition around output layer (fcA, fcB, δB, fc−BG) is cluttered and sometimes confusing.  
- Quality: technically sound; ablations support claims about reduced localization/background errors; category-specific transfer gives modest but significant improvement.  
- Suggested experiments: vary m and k, compare neighbor definitions (fc8, WordNet, embeddings, fc7 prototypes), test random/full averages, learn Wc→Wd mapping, and check class-agnostic bbox regressor.  
- Suggested analysis: quantify negative transfer for semantically dissimilar neighbors and report numeric gap to oracle detectors (with boxes for all classes).  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized; recommend streamlining notation and providing absolute numbers/confidence intervals where small deltas are claimed.  
- Originality: setting and decomposition are novel and practically relevant; the delta-transfer itself is straightforward/heuristic.  
- Significance: high practical importance for scaling detection with limited box annotations; clarifies key factors in classifier→detector gap.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important problem with a simple, effective approach, careful analysis, and released large-scale models/code.  
- Cons against acceptance: heuristic and underexplored category-specific adaptation, missing WSOD baselines, and some expositional/quantitative tightening needed.  
- Recommendation: Accept (leaning).  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
