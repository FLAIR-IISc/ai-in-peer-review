- Proposes LSDA to adapt classifiers into detectors when only a subset of categories (B) has bounding boxes, by learning category-invariant fine-tuning and category-specific fc8 delta transfer to held-out categories (A).
- Frames the classification→detection shift as a domain adaptation problem across categories with missing target labels.
- Learns background modeling and shared feature adaptation from B; transfers average fc8 weight deltas from nearest neighbors in B to A; subtracts background score at test time.
- Uses R-CNN pipeline and selective search; implementation reproducible (Caffe, architecture/fine-tuning details provided).
- On ILSVRC13 (100 B / 100 A) mAP on held-out A improves from 10.31% to 16.15% (+50% relative).
- Ablation study shows most gain comes from background modeling and fine-tuning shared layers; category-specific fc8 transfer gives a smaller but statistically significant improvement.
- Error analysis indicates LSDA reduces localization and background confusions compared to classification-only on proposals.
- Demonstrates a qualitative 7.6K-category detector built with LSDA, suggesting scalability.
- Method is simple, well-motivated, and experimentally sound; paired t-test supports significance of fc8 transfer.
- Novel framing and practical importance: transferable background model and shared feature shift is an original, useful insight.
- Limitations: single alphabetical B/A split (no random-split variance), limited sensitivity analysis for fc8 transfer and similarity metrics.
- Category-specific transfer is heuristic (nearest-neighbor delta); alternatives or learned transfers not explored.
- Large-scale 7.6K evaluation is qualitative; lacks quantitative probe (e.g., precision on curated subset).
- No direct comparison to weakly supervised MIL-style detectors on a shared subset, which would be informative despite differing settings.
- Minor clarity issues: neighbor selection details (L2 vs cosine) and exact k usage could be clearer; some reference formatting.
- Suggested improvements: report results over multiple random splits, explore semantic/learned transfer methods, add quantitative 7.6K evaluation, test bbox-regression transfer, include runtime/memory analysis.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — technically sound, clear, novel and practically significant with convincing empirical support, despite addressable weaknesses.
