- Overall assessment: Practical, well-written approach to scaling object detection via adapting classifiers to detectors; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Problem addressed: Training detectors for many categories where bounding-box labels are scarce; framed as domain adaptation from classification to detection.
- Method: LSDA learns category-invariant and category-specific transformations to convert image classifiers into detectors, leveraging detection data for a subset of categories.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN and domain adaptation literature; distinct in adapting to held-out categories with no detection labels.
- Strength — practical significance: Tackles a high-impact, real-world bottleneck in scaling detection to many categories.
- Strength — methodology: Clear decomposition into category-invariant and category-specific adaptation enables effective transfer learning.
- Strength — empirical results: Shows ~50% relative mAP improvement over baseline and informative ablation studies.
- Strength — analysis: Error analysis and false-positive visualizations illuminate reductions in localization and background confusion.
- Strength — reproducibility: Code and models publicly released.
- Strength — clarity: Well-written paper with informative figures.
- Weakness — theory: Lacks rigorous theoretical justification for the adaptation techniques.
- Weakness — reliance on pretraining: Performance depends heavily on the quality of the pre-trained ImageNet classifier; sensitivity to other pre-training choices is not explored.
- Weakness — hyperparameter choice: Limited justification and exploration for k=10 nearest neighbors in category-specific adaptation.
- Weakness — baselines: Needs comparison to stronger zero-shot or few-shot detection baselines.
- Qualitative — quality: Claims are well-supported and methodology is complete with limitations acknowledged.
- Qualitative — clarity: Explanations and organization are generally clear.
- Qualitative — originality: Novel application of domain adaptation to transfer detection ability to held-out categories, though components are not individually novel.
- Qualitative — significance: Provides a practical path to scale object detection to many categories with broad application potential.
- For acceptance: Addresses critical scaling problem, effective solution with strong results, clear writing, and public code.
- Against acceptance: Limited theory, dependence on strong pretraining, and insufficient baseline comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: Add brief theoretical justification, sensitivity analysis to different pre-trained classifiers, systematic study of k in category-specific adaptation, and discussion of recent zero/few-shot detection work.
