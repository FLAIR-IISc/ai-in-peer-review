- Overall: Compelling, practical method for scaling object detection by adapting classifiers to detectors; merits acceptance.
- Core idea: Frame transforming an image classifier into an object detector as a domain adaptation problem from classification to detection.
- Novelty/significance: Focus on producing detectors for held-out categories without detection labels is a valuable step toward large-scale detection.
- Strength: Directly addresses the bounding-box annotation bottleneck for scaling detection.
- Strength: Strong empirical results — ~50% relative mAP improvement over using raw classifier weights; held-out category results convincing.
- Strength: Well-motivated methodology built on three insights: background modeling, category-invariant transfer, and category-specific differences.
- Strength: Clear writing and helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 2); code and models released for reproducibility.
- Strength: Useful error analysis that breaks down false positives into localization, background, and other errors.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) methods; claim that MIL hasn’t been evaluated on ImageNet is inaccurate.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on the quality of the pre-trained classification network; results may depend on pretraining dataset/architecture.
- Weakness: Heuristic nearest-neighbor approach for approximating category-specific fine-tuning (choice of k is arbitrary).
- Weakness: Simplified test-time processing (skipping SVM and bbox regression) trades off speed for performance without full analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing MIL comparisons, correcting claims/citations, and justifying or improving the k-NN heuristic.
