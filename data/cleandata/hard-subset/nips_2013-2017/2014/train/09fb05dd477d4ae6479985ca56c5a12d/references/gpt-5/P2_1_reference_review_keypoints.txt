- Proposes LSDA: convert image classifiers to detectors with few bounding-box classes via domain adaptation—category-invariant fine-tuning (shared conv+fc, add background) and category-specific fc8 delta transfer using k-NN.

- ILSVRC2013 experiments: with 100 detection classes (B) and 100 held-out (A), category-invariant adaptation raises held-out mAP from 10.31% to 15.85%; adding k-NN deltas yields 16.15% (p=0.017).

- Error analysis shows reductions in background confusion and localization errors; demonstrates a 7,604-class detector (200 with boxes, 7,404 without) and an SPP-based faster variant.

- Builds on R-CNN and complements weakly supervised/MIL work; novel contribution is adapting classification→detection for held-out categories without detection labels.

- Technical soundness: methodology is reasonable and empirically supported; main benefits come from background modeling and shared representation adaptation; k-NN delta is a simple, justifiable heuristic.

- Experimental design follows R-CNN protocols and includes sensible baselines and an upper bound, but lacks direct comparisons to weakly supervised detectors and alternative category-specific mappings or metrics.

- Completeness: complete empirical study with useful error analysis and large-scale demo, though some training details (learning rates, iterations, chosen k, score calibration) are under-specified.

- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized; should explicitly state k, fine-tuning hyperparameters, and justify score_i − score_background versus softmax/calibrated scores.

- Originality: moderately high—transferring detection adaptation to held-out classes and learning a transferable background-aware delta is a novel, practical idea.

- Significance: high practical impact for scaling detectors with limited box annotations; insight that category-invariant adaptation and background modeling provide most gains is valuable.

- Strengths: clear two-component formulation, strong ablation with statistical significance, insightful error analysis, scalable demonstration and released code, and efficient training without SVMs/bbox regression.

- Weaknesses: category-specific transfer is heuristic and under-explored, missing MIL/weakly-supervised comparisons, limited sensitivity analysis to k and neighbor selection, and some reproducibility gaps.

- Suggested experiments/questions: analyze sensitivity to k and neighbor metric (cosine vs L2), try learned linear mapping (e.g., ridge regression) from W^c→W^d, report per-class gains and similarity correlations, test score calibration, and add a MIL baseline on PASCAL.

- Decision: Weak accept—important problem solved with a simple, validated approach and practical impact, despite missing comparisons and deeper analyses.
