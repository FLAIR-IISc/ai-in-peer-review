- Proposes LSDA: adapt a CNN trained for classification into a detector for K categories using detection data for m categories and image labels for all.  
- Two adaptation components: category-invariant fine-tuning (layers 1–7 + background) and category-specific adaptation via k-NN averaging of fc8 deltas.  
- At test time, region proposals scored by K+1 softmax and per-class scores adjusted by subtracting background score.  
- On ILSVRC13 with 100 held-out categories, LSDA raises mAP from 10.31% (classification-only) to 15.85% (category-invariant) and 16.15% (with class-specific transfer).  
- Main gains come from shared feature adaptation and background modeling; class-specific transfer gives a smaller but significant extra boost (p=0.017).  
- Error analysis shows reductions in localization and background confusions.  
- Demonstrates qualitative results for a 7.6K-category detector and mentions a faster SPP-based implementation.  
- Method is technically straightforward, builds on R-CNN fine-tuning, and is well justified for detection needs.  
- Empirical evaluation uses a solid held-out protocol, established benchmark, ablations, and statistical testing.  
- Missing baselines: no comparison to learned regression mappings from W^c→W^d, weakly supervised MIL on the same setup, or a “background-augmented classifier” baseline.  
- Some reproducibility/details lacking: learning rates/schedules, broader k sensitivity, precise proposal settings, per-class APs, and only qualitative 7.6K results.  
- Paper is well organized and readable; insights and pipeline are clear, but minor formatting/notation issues and missing ablation numbers should be fixed.  
- Novelty is moderate: category-invariant idea follows R-CNN but transferring classification→detection across classes is a novel, useful contribution; k-NN class transfer is a simple new twist.  
- Significance is high due to practical importance of scaling detection with limited box annotations and released code/models.  
- Absolute held-out mAP remains modest and the large-scale demo lacks quantitative validation, leaving real-world scaling uncertain.  
- Pros: important problem, simple effective method, clear ablations, error analysis, practical contributions (code/fast variant).  
- Cons/suggestions: limited novelty, heuristic class-specific transfer—compare to learned mappings, add weakly supervised and background-augmented baselines, evaluate sensitivity to m and k, and report per-class APs and quantitative SPP/7.6K results.  
- Overall assessment: good quality and clarity, moderate originality, high significance; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
