- Proposes LSDA, a method to adapt image classifiers into object detectors by learning a transformation from classification to detection using a subset of annotated categories.  
- Demonstrates a substantial empirical gain: ~50% relative mAP improvement over using classifier weights directly for detection.  
- Tackles a critical scalability problem by reducing the need for expensive bounding-box annotations for many categories.  
- Novel framing of detection as a domain adaptation problem and adapting classifiers to detectors rather than training detectors from scratch.  
- Strong empirical validation with a detailed ablation study isolating component contributions.  
- Clear presentation with helpful figures explaining workflow and adaptation effects.  
- Practical contribution: releases code and a 7.6K-category detector, and provides a faster SPP-based implementation.  
- Limited comparison to related work (MIL, domain adaptation) and insufficient discussion of trade-offs and complementary approaches.  
- Heavy reliance on pre-trained ImageNet features; lacks analysis of sensitivity to pre-training dataset or feature quality.  
- Error analysis focuses on localization and background confusion but lacks deeper investigation into misclassification causes.  
- Method appears complex with multiple fine-tuning stages and nearest-neighbor steps; computational cost and scalability discussion is limited.  
- Paper is technically sound and experiments are well-designed, but dependence on pre-trained features slightly lowers novelty/quality assessment.  
- Well-written and easy to follow; notation and figures are clear.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept, with minor revisions requested to expand related-work comparison and analyze sensitivity to pre-trained features.
