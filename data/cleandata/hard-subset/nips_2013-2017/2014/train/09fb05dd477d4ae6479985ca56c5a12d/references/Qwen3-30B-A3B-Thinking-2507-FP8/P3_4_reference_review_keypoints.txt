- Summary: Proposes LSDA to convert image classifiers into object detectors by learning a transformation from classification to detection using bounding boxes for a small subset and applying it to held-out categories; uses category-invariant fine-tuning (layers 1–7) and category-specific nearest-neighbor adaptation, achieving ~50% relative mAP gain and scaling to 7,604 categories.

- Strength: Novel problem formulation framing classifier-to-detector conversion as domain adaptation, addressing bounding-box scarcity.

- Strength: Strong experimental validation and ablation study showing the contribution of each component, especially category-invariant adaptation.

- Strength: Practical impact demonstrated by a large-scale 7,604-category detector and released code/models for reproducibility.

- Strength: Technical rigor with clear connections to R-CNN and domain adaptation literature and sufficient implementation detail.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent weakly supervised detection methods (e.g., MIL, class activation map approaches).

- Weakness: Insufficient justification or theoretical/empirical support for the nearest-neighbor category-specific adaptation assumption.

- Weakness: Does not fully address implications of ImageNet's single image-level labels as weak supervision, which can be noisy for multi-object images.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison or discussion of compatibility with newer detection frameworks (e.g., Faster R-CNN, Mask R-CNN).

- Overall assessment: Technically sound, practically relevant contribution that meaningfully advances large-scale detection despite gaps in related-work comparison and adaptation justification.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Revision suggestion: Add comparisons with recent weakly supervised detection methods (e.g., Song et al. ICML 2015).

- Revision suggestion: Provide stronger justification or ablation studies for the nearest-neighbor category-specific adaptation and different similarity metrics.

- Revision suggestion: Discuss compatibility with modern detection pipelines (Faster/Mask R-CNN) to broaden applicability.

- Revision suggestion: Analyze the effects of ImageNet's weak labels, e.g., how often the single label corresponds to the primary object and how label noise impacts adaptation.
