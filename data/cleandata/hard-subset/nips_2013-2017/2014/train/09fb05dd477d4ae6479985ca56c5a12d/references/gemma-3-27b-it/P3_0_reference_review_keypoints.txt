- Proposes LSDA: a domain adaptation method that transforms image classifiers into object detectors using abundant image-level labels and limited bounding boxes.  
- Leverages pre-trained CNN classifiers and fine-tunes to detection, improving performance on categories without bounding-box labels.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains on ImageNet detection, notably a ~50% relative mAP improvement over baseline.  
- Correctly situates work relative to R-CNN, MIL, domain adaptation literature, and CNN transferability results.  
- Addresses a critical bottleneck in scaling detection by reducing dependence on per-category bounding box annotations.  
- Clear methodology centered on three insights: background modeling, category-invariant transfer, and category-specific adaptation.  
- Fine-tuning layers 1–7 and adapting output layers provides a straightforward adaptation pipeline.  
- Ablation study shows feature representation adaptation is the most important component.  
- Error analysis breaks down false positives and visualizes localization improvements, offering useful insights.  
- Releases a 7.6K model and code, aiding reproducibility and community use.  
- Comparison to stronger weakly-supervised detection baselines is limited and should be expanded.  
- Nearest-neighbor averaging for output-layer adaptation is somewhat ad-hoc and lacks rigorous exploration (distance metrics, weighting).  
- Reliance on the slower R-CNN pipeline limits practical applicability; discussion of integration with modern detectors is brief.  
- Error analysis notes types of mistakes but lacks deeper investigation into why certain categories fail consistently.  
- Choice of k=10 for neighbor selection is insufficiently justified and appears arbitrary.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, offers a novel adaptation approach, shows strong results, and provides resources.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete baseline comparisons, some ad-hoc methodological choices, and reliance on an older detection pipeline.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
