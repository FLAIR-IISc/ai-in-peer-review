- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classification to detection feature space using categories with bounding boxes and apply it to categories without boxes.  
- Demonstrates ~50% relative mAP improvement on ImageNet detection compared to using raw classifier weights.  
- Positions work relative to R-CNN, MIL, and domain adaptation, emphasizing adaptation of both domain and task and enabling detection for held-out categories.  
- Addresses the critical bottleneck of scarce bounding-box annotations by leveraging abundant image-level labels.  
- Clear, intuitive approach built on three insights: background recognition, category-invariant transfer, and category-specific adaptation.  
- Strong empirical results with an ablation study showing each component’s contribution.  
- Helpful qualitative analysis and visualizations illustrating improved localization and error modes.  
- Public release of code and models enhances reproducibility and impact.  
- Method depends heavily on a strong pre-trained classifier, risking degraded performance with weaker bases.  
- Category-specific adaptation via nearest neighbors (Euclidean on fc8) is heuristic and lacks strong justification.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison to other weakly supervised methods (e.g., MIL) on ImageNet.  
- Computational cost (training time and ~4s/image inference) may hinder adoption despite SPP speedups.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and experiments are well-defined; Equation 1 needs clearer explanation.  
- Approach is original and addresses a significant problem with potential high impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; requested fixes: analyze sensitivity to pre-trained classifier, justify or explore alternatives to Euclidean NN selection, compare to state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods on ImageNet, and clarify computational complexity/optimizations.
