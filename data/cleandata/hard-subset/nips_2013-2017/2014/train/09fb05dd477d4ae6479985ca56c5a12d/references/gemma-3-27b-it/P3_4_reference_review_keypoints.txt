- Summary: Proposes LSDA, a method that adapts ImageNet classifiers into detectors via CNN fine-tuning and category-specific adjustments, yielding a 50% relative mAP improvement and a 7604-category detector.

- Relation to prior work: Positions LSDA relative to R-CNN, MIL, and domain adaptation, highlighting its unique goal of adapting to held-out categories without detection labels.

- Novelty: Introduces a new approach for transforming classifiers into detectors for unseen categories, addressing a major scaling bottleneck.

- Empirical strength: Demonstrates substantial gains on ImageNet detection (50% relative mAP) and scalability to thousands of categories.

- Scalability potential: Shows feasibility of building very large detectors (7604 categories) using primarily image-level labels.

- Presentation and analysis: Well-written with helpful diagrams, an informative ablation study, and detailed error analysis identifying localization and background confusions.

- Dependence on pre-training: Results heavily rely on the quality of the ImageNet-trained classifier, with insufficient sensitivity analysis to pre-training or architecture choices.

- Nearest-neighbor heuristic: Using Euclidean distance on fc8 weights for neighbor selection is arbitrary; metric choice and k-sensitivity need more justification and exploration.

- Limited MIL comparison: Lacks a direct quantitative comparison to related MIL methods, which would strengthen claims given the conceptual overlap.

- Pipeline simplifications: Omitting SVMs and bounding box regression seems driven by speed rather than principled design; the speed/performance trade-off is under-discussed.

- Qualitative results: Provides only limited visual examples for the 7604-category detector; more comprehensive successes and failure cases are needed.

- Recommendation: Overall a strong, impactful paper; accept with minor revisions to address sensitivity analysis, neighbor-selection justification, MIL comparison, and more qualitative results.
