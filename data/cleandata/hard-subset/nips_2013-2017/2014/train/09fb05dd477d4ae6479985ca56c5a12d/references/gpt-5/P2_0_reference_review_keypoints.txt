Summary — Proposes LSDA to adapt image classifiers into detectors by fine-tuning shared CNN layers for detection and transferring category-specific output-layer deltas from nearest annotated neighbors, improving held-out ILSVRC detection mAP and releasing code/models.

Relation to prior work — Builds on R-CNN and domain adaptation but uniquely adapts both representation and task with limited-box supervision and complements weakly supervised MIL approaches.

Strength — Clear motivation: scaling detection without exhaustive bounding-box annotation.

Strength — Simple, principled decomposition: category-invariant fine-tuning + lightweight category-specific transfer.

Strength — Empirical ablation shows background modeling and shared-layer fine-tuning drive most gains; category-specific transfer adds a smaller significant boost.

Strength — Sensible evaluation on ILSVRC13 with held-out categories and informative error analysis.

Strength — Practical impact: reduced training complexity (no SVMs/bbox regression) and released large-scale model/code.

Weakness — Absolute performance is modest (~16% mAP on held-out classes) and far from fully supervised upper bounds.

Weakness — Category-specific adaptation is heuristic (nearest neighbors via L2 on normalized fc8); alternatives and sensitivity beyond k are not explored.

Weakness — Baseline comparisons are incomplete; missing alternatives (e.g., MIL baseline, R-CNN SVM/bbox reuse, variants without background subtraction) limit assessment.

Weakness — Score computation and calibration choices (logits vs probabilities, normalization) are not discussed.

Weakness — 7.6K-category demonstration is qualitative only, making large-scale generality unclear.

Weakness — Alphabetical split of categories may bias neighbor selection; stratified/semantic splits would be more robust.

Quality — Technically sound with well-justified shared-layer/background adaptation and informative ablations and statistical testing.

Clarity — Well written and organized with reproducible training details and helpful figures; minor typos only.

Originality — Novel framing of classification-to-detection as mainly category-invariant transfer plus small class-specific corrections and emphasis on background class.

Significance — Addresses a key bottleneck in large-scale detection; simple approach with meaningful relative gain likely to spur follow-up work.

Questions for authors — How sensitive are results to distance metric/normalization for neighbor selection and did you try semantic metrics?

Questions for authors — Does using logits vs softmax probabilities for score_i − score_background change performance?

Questions for authors — Can you provide baselines that fine-tune on B but omit background class/subtractive scoring?

Questions for authors — Any quantitative results using SPP/fast proposals on the held-out split?

Suggestions — Strengthen baselines and perform sensitivity analysis on neighbor selection and k.

Suggestions — Explore pseudo-box mining (MIL) for held-out classes to complement LSDA.

Suggestions — Calibrate scores across classes (e.g., temperature scaling) to reduce Oth-type errors.

Suggestions — Use stratified category splits to better evaluate robustness of transfer.

Arguments for acceptance — Clear, impactful problem, practical method with code release, solid empirical gains, and a novel adaptable decomposition.

Arguments against acceptance — Limited absolute performance, heuristic category transfer with limited exploration, and only qualitative large-scale results.

Overall recommendation — Lean accept: timely, practical contribution with strong evidence and potential impact despite missing baselines and heuristic components.
