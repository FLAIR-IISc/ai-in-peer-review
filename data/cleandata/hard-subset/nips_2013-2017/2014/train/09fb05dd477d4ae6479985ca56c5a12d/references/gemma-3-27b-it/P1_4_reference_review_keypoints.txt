- Summary: LSDA learns a transformation to convert image classifiers into object detectors via domain adaptation to scale detection to categories lacking bounding boxes, yielding a ~50% relative mAP improvement on ImageNet.
- Related work: Positions work relative to MIL and domain adaptation, emphasizing adaptation of both visual domain and task and comprehensive referencing.
- Novelty: Introduces a general transformation to adapt classifiers into detectors, removing the requirement for bounding boxes for all categories.
- Empirical results: Strong quantitative gains (50% relative mAP) and ablation studies that isolate component contributions.
- Practical impact: Enables detecting many categories with minimal annotation, advancing scalability toward human-level recognition.
- Presentation: Well-written, well-organized, clear figures, and code/model release for reproducibility.
- Error analysis: Detailed breakdown of false positives (localization, background, other) providing insight into failure modes.
- Dependence on pre-training: Performance tied to quality of the pre-trained ImageNet classifier; sensitivity to other pre-training choices not explored.
- Category-specific adaptation: Nearest-neighbor approach for categories without boxes is ad-hoc and needs more principled justification or alternatives.
- Comparison to MIL: Lacks direct empirical comparison to MIL methods on ImageNet, which would strengthen validation.
- Computational cost: Runtime claims lack a clear breakdown across pre-training, fine-tuning, and detection stages; trade-offs need more detail.
- Quality: Technically sound with solid experimental support and a thorough ablation study.
- Clarity: Algorithm and presentation are easy to follow.
- Originality: Novel approach representing a significant advancement in large-scale detection.
- Significance: Results are important and likely to have substantial impact on the field.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective, well-presented work with strong results and released code/models.
- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on pre-trained classifier, ad-hoc nearest-neighbor adaptation, and missing MIL comparison.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
