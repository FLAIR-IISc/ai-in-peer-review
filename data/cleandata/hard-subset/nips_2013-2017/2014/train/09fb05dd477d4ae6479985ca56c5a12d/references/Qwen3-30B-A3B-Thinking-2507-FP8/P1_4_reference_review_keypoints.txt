- Introduces LSDA: transforms image classifiers into object detectors using few bounding-box labels and many image-level labels.
- Key insight: treat classification-to-detection as a domain adaptation problem and learn a generic feature transformation.
- Method has two parts: category-invariant feature adaptation and category-specific parameter transfer via nearest neighbors.
- Demonstrates ~50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories vs. classification-only baseline.
- Scales to thousands of categories (demonstrated up to 7,600).
- Technically sound with strong experimental validation and clear implementation details for reproducibility.
- Ablation studies and error analysis effectively show each component’s contribution.
- Lacks comparison to MIL-based weakly supervised detection methods that address similar problems.
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, well-organized, with helpful figures; minor detail missing on nearest-neighbor selection.
- Original contribution: novel adaptation from classification to detection for held-out categories, distinct from prior domain-adaptation work.
- High significance: addresses annotation bottleneck and enables large-scale detection with practical impact.
- Strengths: solves important real-world problem, substantial empirical gains, novel approach, comprehensive evaluation, strong scalability.
- Weaknesses: limited MIL comparisons, insufficient analysis of nearest-neighbor failures for dissimilar categories, potential noise from weak image-level labels, remaining gap to oracle detection.
- Recommendation: strong accept—technically sound, impactful, well-executed; would be stronger with MIL comparisons and more failure analysis.
