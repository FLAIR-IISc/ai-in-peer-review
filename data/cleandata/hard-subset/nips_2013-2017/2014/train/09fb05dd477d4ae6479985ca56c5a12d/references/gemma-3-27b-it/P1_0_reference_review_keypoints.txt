- Summary: LSDA learns a transformation from image classifiers to object detectors using image-level labels plus limited bounding boxes, with category-invariant and category-specific adaptations, improving detection (especially for held-out categories) and releasing a 7604-category detector.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN, MIL, deep feature learning, and domain adaptation; focuses on adapting to held-out categories but could discuss adversarial domain adaptation more.

- Strength: Addresses bounding-box annotation scarcity with a novel classifier→detector transformation and disentangled adaptations.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains (≈50% relative mAP improvement) and impressive results on held-out categories.

- Strength: Large-scale demonstration and resource release (7604-category detector), clear writing, and informative ablation study.

- Weakness: Comparisons are limited to a weak baseline; lacks evaluation against stronger/ MIL-based baselines.

- Weakness: Nearest-neighbor strategy for category-specific adaptation feels ad-hoc and needs stronger justification or exploration.

- Weakness: Insufficient details on computational cost for training and inference at large scale.

- Weakness: Error analysis and discussion of failure cases are shallow; needs more quantitative and diagnostic analysis.

- Qualitative scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.

- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles a critical limitation, offers a novel effective approach, shows strong results, and provides a valuable large-scale detector.

- Arguments against acceptance: Needs stronger baselines, better justification of NN selection, and more detail on compute and failure modes.

- Recommendation: Strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add stronger comparisons, justify NN selection, report computational costs, and expand error analysis.
