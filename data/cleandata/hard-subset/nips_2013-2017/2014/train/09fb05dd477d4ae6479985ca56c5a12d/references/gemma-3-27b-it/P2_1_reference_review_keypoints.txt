- Proposes LSDA to adapt image classifiers into object detectors, reducing bounding-box annotation needs and yielding ~50% relative mAP improvement on ImageNet.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation, distinguishing from R-CNN reliance on boxes and from MIL approaches by adapting classifiers rather than training detectors from weak labels.  
- Tackles a critical bottleneck (bounding-box annotation) with a clear three-stage adaptation method and nearest-neighbor scheme for category-specific adaptation.  
- Strong empirical results and convincing ablation study demonstrating contribution of each component.  
- Detailed error analysis shows reduced localization and background confusion after adaptation.  
- Provides reproducibility by releasing model definitions and weights.  
- Demonstrates scalability with a 7604-category detector.  
- Heavily relies on the quality of the pre-trained classifier; sensitivity to that choice is not explored.  
- Multi-stage algorithm and hyperparameters (e.g., k for nearest neighbors) add implementation complexity.  
- Lacks thorough direct comparison to recent MIL methods on common benchmarks.  
- Nearest-neighbor adaptation risks negative transfer if neighbors are not representative.  
- Limited analysis of non-max suppression and score combination impact.  
- Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses major bottleneck, substantial performance gains, well-motivated algorithm, reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on pre-trained classifier, algorithmic complexity, limited MIL comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant, and well-executed paper; recommendation: Strong Accept.
