- Proposes LSDA: learn a category-invariant classification→detection feature transform on classes with boxes and transfer it to classes without boxes.  
- Method: pretrain classification CNN on K classes, fine-tune shared layers for detection on subset B (plus background), and adapt fc8 for held-out A by averaging deltas from nearest neighbors.  
- Tested on ILSVRC13 with 200 classes (100 with boxes, 100 without); held-out classes mAP improves from 10.31% to 15.85% (invariant) and 16.15% (with k-NN adaptation).  
- Reports large relative gains (~50%) on held-out categories and demonstrates a 7,604-class detector with faster SPP/fast-proposal runtime (~0.5s/image).  
- Strength: addresses scalable detection beyond boxed categories with a simple, well-motivated decomposition (shared vs. class-specific adaptation).  
- Strength: sound experimental protocol with thorough ablations showing background modeling and feature fine-tuning are dominant factors.  
- Strength: shows error-mode shifts (fewer localization/background errors), is computationally efficient, and releases code/models.  
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation is heuristic (k-NN averaging in fc8 space) with small additional gains and limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to weakly supervised MIL baselines on ILSVRC or a smaller shared subset.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses val splits; no test-set or full state-of-the-art comparisons (including SVMs/bbox regression); absolute mAP remains low.  
- Weakness: some design choices (background subtraction, calibration, cross-category NMS) need better justification and quantification.  
- Quality: technically sound and compelling ablation isolating key components, but needs more sensitivity studies (vary |B|, k, similarity metrics, oracle bounds).  
- Clarity: generally clear and reproducible; suggest clarifying detection loss, rationale for score subtraction, and reporting results vs. k and other metrics.  
- Originality: novel framing of transferring a generic classification→detection transform and simple new idea for class-specific delta transfer.  
- Significance: high practical impact—enables thousands of detectors without box labels and shows most of the gap is closed by background and shared feature adaptation.  
- Suggestions: analyze sensitivity to number of labeled classes, try learned delta regressors/hypernetworks, compare to MIL baselines, report calibration experiments, and explore transferring bbox regression.  
- Pros summary: important problem, simple scalable solution, strong ablations, open-source release.  
- Cons summary: limited baseline comparisons, heuristic class-specific step, and lower absolute accuracy vs fully supervised detectors.  
- Recommendation: Accept — clear contribution with practical impact and solid empirical evidence.
