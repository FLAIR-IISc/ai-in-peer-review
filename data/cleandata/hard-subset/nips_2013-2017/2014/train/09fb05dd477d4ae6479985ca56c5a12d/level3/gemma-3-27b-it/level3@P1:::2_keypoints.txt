- Proposes LSDA to transfer image classification networks into object detectors by learning a transformation from classifiers to detectors for categories without bounding-boxes.  
- Tackles the relevant problem of scaling detection to many categories using abundant image-level labels instead of costly bounding boxes.  
- Builds on CNN and R-CNN successes and situates work relative to MIL and domain adaptation, focusing on held-out categories.  
- Method is clearly presented, simple architecture, and authors provide code/models for reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, including a 50% relative mAP improvement and a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Achieves speed gains using spatial pyramid pooling, a practical contribution.  
- Core adaptation method lacks principled theoretical justification and feels somewhat ad hoc.  
- Equation and k-NN averaging for category-specific adaptation are insufficiently explained; the offset parameter’s meaning is unclear.  
- Choice of adapting layers 1–7 and implementation details need stronger theoretical grounding or rationale.  
- Experimental issues: part of the validation set was used for training, limiting generalizability.  
- Comparisons to R-CNN on val2 are weak due to small dataset size and limited statistical significance.  
- Ablation shows small gains from category-specific adaptation, raising questions about its impact.  
- Error analysis is informative but the examples shown do not provide a comprehensive view of error distribution.  
- Overall: valuable, well-presented empirical contribution with code; currently rated weak accept pending clearer theoretical justification and clearer experimental details.
