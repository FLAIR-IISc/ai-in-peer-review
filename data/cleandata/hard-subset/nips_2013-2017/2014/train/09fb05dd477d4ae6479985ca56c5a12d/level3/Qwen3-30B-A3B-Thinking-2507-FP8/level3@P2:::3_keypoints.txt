- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers into object detectors using few bounding-box annotations by framing it as domain adaptation.
- Fine-tunes a pre-trained CNN on a subset of bbox-annotated categories to learn a generic transformation applied to held-out categories.
- Builds on R-CNN, domain adaptation, and deep CNN advances; novelty lies in adapting to categories with no detection data.
- Addresses the important problem of scaling object detection to thousands of categories.
- Method is conceptually simple, logically constructed, and accessible to the vision community.
- Reports compelling results: ~50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories (10.31% → 16.15%).
- Demonstrates scalability by producing a 7,604-category detector using ImageNet classification data.
- Implementation details are well-described and code/models are publicly released.
- Lacks critical baselines: full-image positives with region negatives, MIL-based region selection, and center/mean-box classifiers.
- Without those baselines, improvement may stem from strong CNN features rather than the adaptation technique (attribution concern).
- Experiments and Table 1 need clearer explanations linking methods to specific equations/layers.
- Technically sound and well-evaluated on ImageNet, but missing baselines weakens the strength of the claims.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, though experimental details could be more transparent.
- Originality is primarily in the application (adapting to held-out categories) rather than introducing new core techniques.
- Significant practical impact potential by enabling large-scale detection using abundant classification data.
- Recommendation: strong candidate but should add the suggested baselines and clarify attribution before publication.
