- Proposal: LSDA learns a classifier-to-detector transformation from categories with bounding boxes and transfers it to held-out categories with only image-level labels.  
- Implementation: fine-tune a classification CNN on detection for m categories to learn background modeling and a category-invariant shift; approximate category-specific deltas for held-out classes via nearest-neighbor averaging in classifier weight space.  
- Experimental protocol: train with boxes+labels for 100 ImageNet detection categories and evaluate on 100 held-out categories with labels only.  
- Positioning: combines ideas from R-CNN fine-tuning, background modeling, and simple parameter transfer to reduce bounding-box supervision.  
- Ablation findings: background modeling and feature-level adaptation are the main performance drivers; category-specific delta gives smaller gains.  
- Reporting concern: headline “78%/50% relative” improvements are misleading; main result should be reported as fraction of oracle gap closed (≈35%); request absolute mAP, gap-closed metrics, and confidence intervals.  
- Comparison clarity: clarify in Figure 3a which competing methods use category labels for detection to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Writing: paper is clearly written; minor issues include inconsistent method name (“DNN” vs “DDA”) and a typo on line 431.  
- Suggested revision: add a dedicated table/subsection itemizing differences from R-CNN (background class, removal of SVM/BBR, adaptation steps, training time) and consider removing Figure 6 for space.  
- Originality: a novel combination of existing adaptation ideas rather than a fundamentally new paradigm; formulation is crisp and interesting.  
- Significance: practical and likely to influence follow-up work but may be surpassed by stronger weak/semi/self-supervised methods; long-term importance depends on annotation-cost trends.  
- Request for analysis: provide a curve varying m and annotation cost to clarify the trade-off between fewer boxes and mAP.  
- Pros: simple, well-executed idea; clear experimental setup; closes ≈35% of oracle gap; code/models released.  
- Cons: incremental originality; ambiguous headline numbers; unclear cost–performance trade-off; uncertain long-term impact.
