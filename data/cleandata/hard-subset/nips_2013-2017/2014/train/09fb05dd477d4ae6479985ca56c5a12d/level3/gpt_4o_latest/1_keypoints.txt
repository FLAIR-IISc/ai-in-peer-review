- Addresses scarcity of bounding-box annotations for large-scale object detection and proposes scaling detection with image-level labels.
- Introduces LSDA (Large Scale Detection through Adaptation) to adapt classifiers into detectors using limited bounding-box categories.
- Trains on a subset of annotated categories and transfers learned transformations to unannotated categories.
- Demonstrates a 50% relative mAP improvement over a classifier baseline on held-out categories.
- Claims LSDA closes 35% of the performance gap between an oracle detector and the baseline.
- Method is simple, effective, and relevant for enabling detection across thousands of categories.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.
- Situates the work relative to prior methods (e.g., R-CNN) and domain adaptation literature, but needs clearer contrast.
- Requests clarification and better presentation of Figure 3a and Figure 3b; suggests a dedicated comparison table vs. R-CNN.
- Notes minor issues: inconsistent naming ("DNN" vs. "DDA"), a typo on line 431, and suggests removing Figure 6 to keep focus.
- Originality stems from a novel combination of domain adaptation techniques to convert classifiers to detectors.
- Expresses uncertainty about long-term significance given potential future annotation advances and lack of rigorous trade-off evaluation.
- Strengths: practical problem focus, simple/effective algorithm, solid writing and references.
- Weaknesses: unclear long-term impact, insufficient evaluation of performance vs. annotation trade-offs, and some unclear figures/comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution and worthy of acceptance despite reservations about long-term impact.
