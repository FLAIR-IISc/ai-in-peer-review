- Paper proposes LSDA: transform a pre-trained image classifier into an object detector via domain adaptation, fine-tuning on annotated categories and transferring category-specific changes to unannotated categories using nearest neighbors; evaluated on ILSVRC2013 with significant gains.

- Strength: addresses a significant, practical problem—scaling detection to thousands of categories—and demonstrates a 7.6K detector.

- Strength: method is simple, elegant, and well-motivated, separating category-invariant and category-specific adaptation.

- Strength: strong empirical results — ~50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories and reduced localization/background errors; qualitative shift from part to whole-object localization.

- Weakness: missing crucial baselines (weakly supervised full-image positives vs region negatives, MIL-based region selection, center/mean-box heuristic), leaving uncertainty about true contribution.

- Weakness: experimental section lacks clarity linking table rows to specific layers and training steps, hurting reproducibility and understanding.

- Weakness: limited originality—builds heavily on R-CNN and standard fine-tuning; novelty mainly in the adaptation transfer for unannotated classes.

- For acceptance: important problem, simple/elegant solution, significant results, and high potential impact.

- Against acceptance: absent baseline comparisons undermine the claim that LSDA is an effective training method from weak labels; results may largely reflect strong CNN features rather than the adaptation technique.
