- Proposes LSDA: learn classification→detection transformation on categories with boxes and transfer it to held-out categories using category-invariant fine-tuning and averaged fc8 deltas.  
- Pipeline: pretrain CNN on K classes, fine-tune layers 1–7 + background on m boxed categories (B), transfer category-specific deltas to held-out set A via nearest-neighbor averaging.  
- Builds on R-CNN and model/domain adaptation ideas but removes per-category box requirement at scale.  
- Evaluation: ImageNet Detection protocol with 100 categories trained with boxes and 100 held-out with image labels only; shows clear gains over classification-only baseline.  
- Method is technically straightforward, well-justified, and ablations isolate contributions (background, shared-layer fine-tuning, fc8 deltas).  
- Main gains come from background modeling and shared layers (1–7); category-specific fc8 deltas are less critical.  
- Experimental setup and “oracle” upper bound are appropriate and reproducible (Caffe, standard proposals).  
- Central claim is supported by held-out mAP, but some reported relative percentages are misleading; focus on gap-based result (LSDA closes ≈35% of baseline→oracle gap).  
- Additional analyses needed: sensitivity to size of B, k in nearest-neighbor averaging, and choice of nearest-neighbor metric.  
- Paper is clearly written and figures informative, but clarify which competing methods in Figure 3a use detection labels and how.  
- Recommend adding a concise table/subsection contrasting LSDA vs R-CNN (data per category, background use, SVMs/bbox regressor, training time, joint training).  
- Originality: novel combination of existing ideas applied to transferring detection without boxes; useful but not deeply conceptually novel.  
- Practical significance: offers a pragmatic path to scale detection with fewer boxes; empirical gains on held-out categories are nontrivial.  
- Longevity uncertain: likely to be superseded by weaker/self-supervised methods; paper should quantify annotation savings vs mAP.  
- Main quantitative result: LSDA improves held-out mAP from ~10.3% to ~16.1% (oracle ~26%), bridging ≈35% of the gap.  
- Suggest reporting class-wise improvements and PR curves, and analyzing error types (Loc, BG, Oth) further.  
- Key questions: clarify baselines’ use of labels/background, sensitivity to k and B composition, effect of class-agnostic bbox regressor, and influence of proposal method.  
- Suggestions: reframe abstract/conclusion to emphasize gap closed, add cost–benefit mAP vs number of boxed categories, and consider moving large qualitative figure to supplement.  
- Pros: addresses relevant bottleneck, clear ablations, practical/reproducible, useful detector release.  
- Cons: moderate conceptual novelty, potentially misleading headline numbers, unclear baseline comparability, and unquantified annotation trade-offs.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent naming (DNN vs DDA vs LSDA), typo “classi?ers,” and unclear figure captions about which settings use boxes.  
- Overall: high-quality, clearly written paper with a useful, well-executed idea; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying comparisons and reframing quantitative claims.
