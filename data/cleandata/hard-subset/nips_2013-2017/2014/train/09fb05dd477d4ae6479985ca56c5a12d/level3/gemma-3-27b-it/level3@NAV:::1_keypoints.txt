- Tackles scaling object detection by adapting classifiers to detectors to avoid costly bounding-box annotations.
- Core idea (LSDA) learns a transformation from classification to detection to enable detection for categories without box annotations.
- Focuses on zero-shot transfer to entirely unseen categories and builds on prior R-CNN adaptation work.
- Algorithm is straightforward, elegant, and clearly presented.
- Experimental protocol (training on 100 box-annotated categories, testing on 100 label-only categories) is strong and appropriate.
- Results show substantial improvement, filling ~35% of the gap between oracle and raw classifier baselines.
- Paper is very well written, organized, and the authors provide released code and models for reproducibility.
- Novelty is incremental—combines established adaptation ideas rather than introducing fundamentally new techniques.
- Significance is unclear because the trade-off between reduced annotation cost and decreased mAP is not fully analyzed.
- The long-term importance of the problem may be limited if annotation resources increase.
- Clarifications needed: explicitly state in Figure 3a which methods use category labels for detection, and emphasize the ~35% gap-filling result in Figure 3b over the 78%/50% claims.
- Recommend adding a clear table or section comparing LSDA and R-CNN (possibly replacing Figure 6) and fixing minor issues (inconsistent “DNN” vs “DDA” terminology, typo on line 431).
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing the points above.
