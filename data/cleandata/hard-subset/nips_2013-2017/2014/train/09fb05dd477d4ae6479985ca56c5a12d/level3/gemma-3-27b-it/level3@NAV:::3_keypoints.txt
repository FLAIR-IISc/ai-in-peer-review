- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors and apply it to categories without bounding-box data to scale detection.  
- Targets a key bottleneck (bounding-box annotation cost) by leveraging large-scale classification datasets.  
- Method is simple, conceptually clear, and builds logically on CNN classification/detection work.  
- Results show substantial gains over a baseline that uses classifier weights, enabling a 7.6K-category detector from limited bbox data.  
- Code release and clear writing improve reproducibility and accessibility.  
- Major weakness: missing crucial baseline comparisons — (1) full-image positives with region negatives, (2) MIL-based region selection, and (3) mean-box classifier.  
- Experiments need clearer reporting (e.g., Table 1 should state which equations/layers apply to each variant for the ablation study).  
- Originality is adequate but not radical; approach is somewhat specific to Krizhevsky-style CNNs and is an application of existing principles.  
- Concern that observed gains may primarily come from strong CNN features rather than the proposed adaptation procedure.  
- Recommendation: accept with the request that authors include the suggested baselines and clarify experimental details.
