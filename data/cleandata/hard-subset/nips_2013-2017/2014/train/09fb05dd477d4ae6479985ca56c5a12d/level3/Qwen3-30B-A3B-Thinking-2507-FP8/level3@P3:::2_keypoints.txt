- Proposes LSDA: adapt classification-trained CNNs into object detectors without bounding-box annotations for all categories.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation from classification to detection, learning a transform from a subset of bbox-annotated categories.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, ranks 4th in detection challenge and enables a 7,600-category detector using mostly classification data.  
- Demonstrates technical soundness and strong results: ~50% relative mAP improvement over classification-only baseline.  
- Ablation study (Sec. 4.2) provides useful insight into the contribution of adaptation components.  
- Lacks principled/theoretical justification for key methodological choices (e.g., weight adaptation formula Wj^c + W_avg).  
- No physical interpretation or justification provided for the “offset” parameter in the weight adaptation.  
- Unclear why the kNN variant outperforms other variants; explanation and analysis are insufficient.  
- Evaluation protocol is problematic: uses part of validation set (val2) instead of the official test set for comparisons.  
- Validation subset (val2) is small (~20k images, half used), so claims about RCNN performance lack statistical justification.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, reproducible, with effective figures and an easy-to-implement CNN architecture.  
- Novel contribution in scaling classifier-to-detector adaptation to categories without detection data, but conceptually a straightforward application of domain adaptation.  
- Insufficient positioning relative to prior domain adaptation/detection literature (e.g., Aytar et al.).  
- High potential significance for large-scale detection, but impact is reduced by unfair comparisons and limited analysis of limitations.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, practical/simple approach, validated on large benchmark, meaningful empirical gains.  
- Weaknesses: missing principled justification, unclear motivations for formulas/variants, unfair evaluation, statistically weak claims, limited failure-mode discussion.  
- Recommendation: interesting and promising but requires significant revisions (method justification and fair evaluation) before acceptance.  
- Suggested improvements: provide theoretical justification for weight adaptation, use official test set, add statistical analysis of results, explain kNN superiority, discuss limitations/failure cases, and better relate to domain adaptation literature.
