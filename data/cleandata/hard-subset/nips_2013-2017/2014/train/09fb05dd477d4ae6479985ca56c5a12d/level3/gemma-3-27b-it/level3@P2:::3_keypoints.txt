- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classification CNNs into object detectors to handle scarce bounding-box annotations.  
- Leverages abundant image-level labels to scale detection to many categories.  
- Positions work relative to recent CNN/detection papers and distinguishes from MIL and domain adaptation.  
- Addresses a significant bottleneck in scaling object detection.  
- Intuitive three-stage adaptation (category-invariant, category-specific, background modeling).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains (+50% relative mAP on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 baseline).  
- Shows large-scale feasibility by creating a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Publicly releases code and models for reproducibility.  
- Lacks critical baseline comparisons (full-image positives with region negatives, MIL region selection, mean-box classifier).  
- Unclear if LSDA outperforms simple or MIL baselines in practice.  
- Experimental descriptions (Table 1 and adaptation-technique mappings) need more clarity for reproducibility.  
- Hard to separate gains from LSDA adaptation vs. the power of the underlying Krizhevsky CNN features.  
- Builds on existing CNN/fine-tuning techniques, so novelty is incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Paper is technically sound with supportive experiments and useful ablation studies.  
- Generally well-written and organized, though experiment section needs more detail.  
- Results are significant and have potential impact by enabling detection for many categories.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, shows substantial improvements, is scalable, and shares code/models.  
- Against acceptance: missing baselines, unclear attribution of gains, and limited novelty.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include suggested baselines and clarify experiments.
