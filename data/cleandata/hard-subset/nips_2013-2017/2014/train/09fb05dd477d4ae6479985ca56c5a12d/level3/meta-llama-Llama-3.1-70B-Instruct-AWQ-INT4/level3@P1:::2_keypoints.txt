- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation to adapt image-classification CNNs into object-detection CNNs using a subset of categories with bounding-box annotations.  
- Uses a multi-stage algorithm leveraging paired classification and detection data to learn the change from a classification CNN to a detection CNN.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection; achieves comparable/state-of-the-art detection performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and the CNN architecture is simple to implement.  
- Main adaptation method lacks principled justification and detailed motivation.  
- No clear rationale for computing adapted detection weights as classification weights plus the average of nearest-neighbor detection weights.  
- The physical meaning of the "offset" parameter is not clearly explained.  
- No explanation for why the kNN variant outperforms other variants.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair because authors used part of the validation set instead of the test set.  
- The claim that R-CNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified due to val2’s small size.  
- Significant contribution: enables scaling object detection to many categories where bounding-box labels are scarce.  
- Overall high-quality work but needs stronger explanation/justification of the adaptation method to be more convincing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical results, valuable for large-scale detection.  
- Arguments for rejection: weak theoretical grounding and questionable experimental choices.
