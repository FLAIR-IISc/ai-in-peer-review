- Problem & goal: Scale object detection to many categories by transforming classifiers into detectors to avoid costly bounding-box annotations.  
- Method: Proposes LSDA, treating classifier→detector conversion as domain adaptation with category-invariant lower-layer and category-specific final-layer mappings.  
- Data setup: Trained on 100 fully supervised ILSVRC categories and evaluated on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN but removes the requirement for bounding boxes for every category.  
- Strength — significance & originality: Tackles an important practical bottleneck with a novel, general "classifier-to-detector" transformation.  
- Strength — quality & clarity: Well-written, intuitive method with sound experiments, ablations, and public code/model release.  
- Strength — experimental evidence: LSDA substantially improves over raw classifier baseline, mainly reducing localization and background confusion errors.  
- Weakness — result framing: Paper emphasizes a "50% relative mAP boost" but should frame results as closing ~1/3 of the gap to fully supervised oracle (10.31→16.15 vs ~27 mAP).  
- Weakness — practical trade-off: Lacks analysis of annotation-vs-performance cost (how much mAP is sacrificed to avoid annotating N boxes).  
- Weakness — comparison to R-CNN: Differences to R-CNN are scattered; a dedicated subsection/table is needed (and Figure 6 could be removed).  
- Weakness — experimental clarity: Comparisons (e.g., Overfeat) must clarify whether competing methods faced the same lack-of-boxes constraint.  
- Acceptance summary (pro): Novel, timely, technically sound work with solid experiments and useful public release.  
- Acceptance summary (con): Practical impact unclear without annotation-cost analysis and performance still substantially below fully supervised detection.
