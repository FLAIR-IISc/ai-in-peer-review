- Proposes LSDA: adapting image-classification-trained CNNs into detectors to scale detection to many categories using image-level labels.  
- Learns a transformation from classification to detection using a subset of categories with bounding-box annotations and applies it to unannotated categories.  
- Differs from R-CNN by not requiring bounding-box data for all categories and enabling adaptation for categories without any detection labels.  
- Connects to domain adaptation work addressing dataset bias between classification and detection domains.  
- Well-written paper with a simple, practical CNN architecture that’s easy to implement.  
- Strong empirical results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, showing a ~50% relative mAP boost over raw classifier weights on proposal regions.  
- Main adaptation method (Wj^c + W_avg) lacks principled justification and clear motivation.  
- The physical meaning of the learned "offset" parameter is unclear and under-explained.  
- No clear explanation for why the kNN variant outperforms other variants.  
- Comparisons are potentially unfair because part of the validation set was used instead of the test set.  
- Claim that R-CNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified due to val2’s small size.  
- Technically sound with well-supported claims, but would benefit from deeper analysis of adaptation components and why the method works.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to justify the adaptation design, clarify Section 3.2, and justify dataset/comparison choices.
