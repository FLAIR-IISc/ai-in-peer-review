- Proposes LSDA: learn classification-to-detection transformation on categories with boxes, transfer to categories without boxes.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation from classification data (source) to detection data (target).  
- Builds on R-CNN but avoids needing bounding boxes for all categories.  
- Method is practical and scalable, demonstrated by a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and reproducible; CNN architecture is simple to implement.  
- Ablation study shows category-invariant adaptation is most valuable, yielding ~50% relative mAP improvement over classification-only baseline.  
- Main adaptation technique lacks principled theoretical justification and explanation of the offset term.  
- Use of k-nearest neighbors for weight adaptation seems arbitrary and unexplained.  
- Evaluation protocol is flawed: training used part of validation (val1) rather than standard test, making comparisons unfair.  
- Claims about comparable R-CNN performance on val2 are not statistically supported given val2’s small size and absence of significance testing.  
- Quality: technically sound and complete but undermined by the flawed evaluation methodology.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear exposition and effective figures.  
- Originality: addresses an important, novel scaling problem and offers a meaningful practical contribution, but adaptation idea is more engineering than theoretical novelty and not well distinguished from prior domain-adaptation/MIL work.  
- Significance: potentially high (large-scale detection), but reported gains vs. state-of-the-art are modest and evaluation issues weaken the claim.  
- Recommendation: lean reject unless authors provide principled justification for adaptation and re-evaluate using standard test protocols; could be acceptable with those fixes.
