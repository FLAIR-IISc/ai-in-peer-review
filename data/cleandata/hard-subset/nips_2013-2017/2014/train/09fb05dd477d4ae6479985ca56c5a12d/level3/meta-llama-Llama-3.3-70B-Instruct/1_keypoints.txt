- Proposes a novel policy-search algorithm combining trajectory optimization with guided policy search to learn arbitrary parameterized policies under unknown dynamics.
- Iteratively fits local linear dynamics and employs a background dynamics distribution as a prior to reduce sample complexity.
- Introduces a KL-divergence constraint in trajectory optimization, enabling handling of complex and discontinuous dynamics.
- Demonstrates improved performance over prior model-free and model-based methods on simulated robotic tasks (peg insertion, octopus arm, swimming, walking).
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithm and its components.
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, thorough evaluation, and effective use of a background dynamics prior to lower sample complexity.
- Weaknesses: limited to linear-Gaussian controllers which may restrict applicability; evaluation lacks broader comparison to state-of-the-art and deeper analysis of limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution with solid empirical support despite some limitations.
