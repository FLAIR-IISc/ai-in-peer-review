- Proposes LSDA to convert image classifiers into detectors for categories without bounding-boxes by treating it as domain adaptation.
- Three-stage method: pre-train CNN for classification, fine-tune feature layers for detection with a background class on boxed categories, adapt classifier weights for unboxed classes using average weight changes of nearest neighbors.
- Demonstrates large-scale detection (7.6K classes) and significant relative mAP gains on held-out ILSVRC2013 classes.
- Strength: Addresses the practical problem of scaling detection when bounding-box annotation is infeasible.
- Strength: Method is simple, well-motivated, and technically sound, building logically on R-CNN style approaches.
- Strength: Paper is generally clear and well organized.
- Main weakness: Lacks comparisons to important baselines, making it hard to isolate the contribution of the adaptation technique.
- Missing baseline 1: Direct weakly-supervised fine-tuning using full-image positives and proposal negatives for held-out classes.
- Missing baseline 2: Multiple Instance Learning (MIL)-based methods that pick promising proposal positives within weakly labeled images.
- Missing baseline 3: Simple localization sanity checks (e.g., center-box or mean-box) to quantify trivial localization performance.
- Attribution concern: Results may rely heavily on the pre-trained Krizhevsky CNN; authors do not fully disentangle adaptation benefit from general fine-tuning effects.
- Experimental clarity issue: Table 1’s variants (e.g., LSDA-BG, LSDA-fc7) are not explicitly mapped to the exact training procedures and layer changes.
- Suggested clarifications/questions: justify nearest-neighbor weight averaging versus direct weakly-supervised training, explore alternative similarity metrics (e.g., WordNet), and explicitly link each table row to its training details.
- Final assessment: Novel and significant idea worthy of acceptance, technically solid, but experimental evaluation must add key baselines and clearer explanations to strengthen claims.
