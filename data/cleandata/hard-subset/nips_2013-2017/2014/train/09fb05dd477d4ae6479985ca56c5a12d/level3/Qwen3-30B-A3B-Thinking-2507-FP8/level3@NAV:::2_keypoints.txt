- Presents LSDA: a framework adapting classification CNNs into detectors using detection data for only a subset of categories.  
- Demonstrates strong practical results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, enabling detection for 7,604 categories and placing 4th in the challenge.  
- Clear, well-structured writing and an easy-to-implement CNN architecture.  
- Shows large empirical gains (≈50% relative mAP) over classification-only baselines.  
- Effectively positions LSDA relative to prior work (e.g., R-CNN) and domain adaptation literature.  
- Key adaptation step (Wj^c + W_avg) lacks principled explanation or theoretical justification.  
- No explanation for why the kNN adaptation variant outperforms others.  
- Comparison to R-CNN is misleading—evaluations use a small val2 subset rather than the official test set.  
- Statistical claims about R-CNN parity on val2 are unsound due to small sample size.  
- Experimental setup issues undermine the validity of comparative claims.  
- Overall contribution is incremental; methodological/theoretical weaknesses limit scientific significance despite clear scalability potential.
