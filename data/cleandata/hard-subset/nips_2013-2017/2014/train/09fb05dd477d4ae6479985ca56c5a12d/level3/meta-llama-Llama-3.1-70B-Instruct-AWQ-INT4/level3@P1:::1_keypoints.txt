- Proposes LSDA: adapts image classifiers into object detectors to avoid needing bounding-box annotations for many categories.  
- Motivates work by arguing large-scale bounding-box collection is impractical.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains motivation, approach, and experiments.  
- Provides thorough analysis including ablation study and error analysis.  
- Algorithm is simple and straightforward, easing implementation and understanding.  
- Demonstrates filling ~35% of the gap between oracle and baseline on held-out categories.  
- Originality stems from a novel combination of existing adaptation ideas, though not groundbreaking.  
- Contribution is practically significant but long-term impact and how it will be surpassed are unclear.  
- Trade-off between using fewer bounding boxes and reduced mAP is not well established.  
- Raises question whether bounding-box scarcity will remain important given improving annotation availability/tools.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent method naming (“DNN” vs “DDA”) and a typo on line 431.  
- Figure 3a needs clarification about which methods use category labels for detection for fair comparison.  
- Main result should emphasize DNN performance in Figure 3b showing ~1/3 gap closed; 78% and 50% claims are misleading and should be removed.  
- Recommend adding a dedicated table/subsection detailing differences between LSDA and R-CNN; Figure 6 may be unnecessary.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel practical approach, simple algorithm, thorough analysis, promising empirical gains (~35% gap closed).  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited originality, unclear long-term significance, unclear trade-offs, and minor presentation issues.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10 with confidence 8/10.
