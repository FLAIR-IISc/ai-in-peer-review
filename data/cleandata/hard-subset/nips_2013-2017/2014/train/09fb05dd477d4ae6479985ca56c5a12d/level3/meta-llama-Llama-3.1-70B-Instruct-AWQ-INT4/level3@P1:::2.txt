This paper, "LSDA: Large Scale Detection through Adaptation", proposes a novel approach to adapt image classification-trained deep neural networks for object detection. The authors address the challenge of scaling object detection to a large number of categories, where obtaining labeled images with bounding boxes is difficult and expensive.

The main idea of the paper is to learn a transformation from image classification to object detection using a subset of categories with bounding box annotations and then apply this transformation to adapt classifiers for categories without bounding box annotations. The authors achieve this through a multi-stage algorithm that uses corresponding classification and detection data to learn the change from a classification CNN network to a detection CNN network.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed framework and its evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge. The CNN architecture is interesting and simple to implement, and the overall quality of the paper is good.

However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement. The main contribution of the paper, the adaptation method, lacks principled justification and detailed motivation. The authors do not provide a clear explanation for why the adapted detection weights are computed as the sum of the classification weights and the average of the detection weights for the nearest neighbors. Additionally, the physical meaning of the "offset" parameter is not clearly explained.

Furthermore, the authors do not provide a clear explanation for why the kNN variant outperforms other variants. The comparisons in the paper are also unfair because the authors used part of the validation set instead of the test set. The claim that R-CNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified due to val2's small size.

Despite these limitations, the method achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art object detection methods, and the paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of computer vision. However, the adaptation method needs better explanation and justification to make the paper more convincing.
