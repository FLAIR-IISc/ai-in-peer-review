- Proposes a novel hybrid policy-search method combining model-based and model-free strengths.  
- Fits local, time-varying linear dynamics to optimize trajectory distributions.  
- Uses guided policy search to train arbitrary parameterized (neural network) policies.  
- Introduces a background dynamics distribution to reduce sample complexity.  
- Demonstrates success on complex, partially observed tasks like robotic manipulation and locomotion.  
- Claims ability to handle discontinuous dynamics and high-dimensional state spaces.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough background and related work.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with comparisons to prior methods and diverse tasks.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, significance, and potential wide applicability.  
- Weakness: needs more comparisons to other baselines, especially model-based RL methods.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of limitations and potential extensions (e.g., hybrid discrete-continuous tasks).  
- Weakness: may be overly focused on robotic applications; broader applicability could be further explored.  
- Overall recommendation: suitable for acceptance at NIPS due to its novel and significant contribution.
