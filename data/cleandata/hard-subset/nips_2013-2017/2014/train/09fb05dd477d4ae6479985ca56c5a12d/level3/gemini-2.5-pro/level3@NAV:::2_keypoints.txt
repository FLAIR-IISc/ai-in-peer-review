- Introduces LSDA: adapting image-classification networks to object detection to create detectors for categories without bounding-box labels.
- Key idea frames the problem as domain adaptation, learning a transformation from classification to detection using categories with both label types.
- Leverages existing approaches (e.g., R-CNN fine-tuning) but extends to categories lacking detection data.
- Strength: clear presentation and straightforward, implementable method.
- Strength: strong empirical results (ranked 4th in ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection), demonstrating practical viability.
- Strength: addresses an important, scalable goal—extending detection to tens of thousands of categories.
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation (adding averaged offset Wj^c + W_avg) lacks principled theoretical justification.
- Weakness: no clear interpretation of the offset parameter or why the additive formulation is appropriate.
- Weakness: kNN averaging variant outperforms others without analysis, making the approach seem heuristic.
- Weakness: experimental protocol problematic—evaluation on val2 instead of standard test set hinders fair comparisons.
- Weakness: claims of parity with R-CNN are not statistically robust given small val2 set; paper needs standard-benchmark evaluation and clearer justification.
