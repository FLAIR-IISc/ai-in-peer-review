- Problem: adapt a classification CNN to large-scale detection when bounding boxes exist only for a subset of categories via training K-way classifier, fine-tuning on boxed subset with background, and transferring fc8 deltas by nearest neighbors.

- Framing: cast as domain adaptation from classification (source) to detection (target).

- Relation to prior work: builds on R-CNN and ImageNet pretraining, differing by not needing boxes for all categories and using a background-aware softmax detector.

- Strength: simple, logical approach leveraging strong CNN features and an explicit background class.

- Strength: clear split between category-invariant (layers 1–7) and category-specific (fc8) adaptations; ablations show most gains from invariant layers.

- Strength: compelling large-scale demos (7.6K-category detector) and faster SPP/fast-proposal variant.

- Strength: practical impact—reduces annotation burden; models and code released.

- Strength: solid error analysis showing reduced localization and background confusions post-adaptation.

- Missing baselines: absent three important baselines—(1) full-image positives + region negatives, (2) MIL-based region selection, (3) center/mean-box calibration baseline.

- Attribution concern: improvements may mainly come from CNN features and background modeling rather than the proposed fc8 nearest-neighbor δ transfer.

- Experimental clarity issues: Table 1 lacks explicit mappings (which layers adapted, background inclusion, loss used, k selection), and score calibration details are unclear.

- Quality: method is technically sound and validated, but missing baselines and limited significance testing weaken the claims.

- Clarity: paper is generally well written, but experiments need precise descriptions of variants, scoring, and neighbor selection.

- Originality: useful and sufficiently novel (classifier-to-detector transfer), but incremental over R-CNN and tied to softmax + background approach.

- Significance: high practical significance for scaling detection to thousands of categories; valuable released resources.

- Requested experiments: include the three baselines; attribution controls (freeze conv1–fc7, compare nearest/random/zero δ, vary k and neighbor metrics); report per-class AP deltas and runtime vs. accuracy for SPP variant.

- Pros: simple, scalable, effective on held-out classes; good error analysis and released artifacts.

- Cons: missing strong baselines, possible misattribution of gains, and imprecise experimental reporting.

- Overall assessment: solid, timely contribution with practical value; likely acceptable if authors address missing baselines and clarify experiments.
