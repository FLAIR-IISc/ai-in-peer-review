- Proposes LSDA to adapt object classifiers into detectors for categories without bounding-box annotations.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation using category-invariant and category-specific transformations, building on R-CNN and domain-adaptation ideas.  
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 held-out categories with labels only.  
- Main empirical result: method closes roughly 35% of the gap between the oracle (full boxes) and the baseline (raw classifier weights) on held-out categories.  
- Claims of "78% relative improvement" and "50% relative mAP" are misleading; actual performance is about one-third of the gap (see Figure 3b).  
- Strengths: well-written, technically sound, clear exposition; simple, scalable method demonstrated by a practical 7.6K detector.  
- Related work is adequately discussed but the paper should clarify which methods in Figure 3a use category labels for detection for fair comparison.  
- Weaknesses: unclear trade-off between fewer bounding boxes and reduced detection mAP; long-term importance is questionable if bounding-box scarcity is solved.  
- Significance is modest: novel combination of existing ideas but unlikely to remain state-of-the-art.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of "DNN" vs "DDA" and a typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: replace Figure 6 with a clear table showing differences from R-CNN; rephrase main result as "one-third of the performance gap" instead of the 50% claim.
