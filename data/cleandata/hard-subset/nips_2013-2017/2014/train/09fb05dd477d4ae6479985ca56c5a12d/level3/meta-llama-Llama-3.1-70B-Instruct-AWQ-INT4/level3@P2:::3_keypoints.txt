- Proposes LSDA: transform image classifiers into object detectors via domain adaptation from classifier (source) to detector (target).  
- Leverages abundant image-level labels and requires bounding-box data for only a small subset of categories.  
- Extends R-CNN approach by learning a general transformation from classification to detection networks.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection (+50% relative mAP vs. raw classifier-on-regions baseline).  
- Shows a fast variant capable of ~2 FPS for a 7.6K-category detector; code/models released.  
- Strengths: clear, simple, logically motivated multi-stage method and good presentation.  
- Significance: enables scaling detection to tens of thousands of categories lacking box annotations.  
- Concern: approach is not highly novel and improvements may stem from strong CNN features rather than the adaptation method.  
- Major missing comparisons: full-image positives with region negatives, MIL-based region selection, and center/mean-box classifier baselines.  
- Experiments need clearer explanations (e.g., what each method in Table 1 corresponds to in terms of equations/layers).  
- Implementation details lacking: specific CNN architecture choices and hyperparameter settings.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10); add baseline comparisons and clearer experimental/implementation details.
