- Proposes LSDA: convert image classifiers to detectors using image-level labels for most categories and bbox data for a small subset via pretraining, fine-tuning (layers 1–7 + background), and nearest-neighbor category weight transfer.  
- Builds on R-CNN and targets the scalability problem of obtaining bounding boxes for thousands of categories.  
- Empirical results: ~50% relative mAP improvement over classification-only baselines on held-out 100/200 categories and a 7,604-category detector demo.  
- Technical execution is sound with thorough ablation studies showing category-invariant adaptation is the most impactful component.  
- Missing key baseline: no “full-image positives with region negatives” weakly-supervised comparison (simple strong baseline).  
- Missing key baseline: no comparison to MIL-based weakly-supervised methods, which are the most relevant competitors.  
- Missing key baseline: no mean-box (center-box) baseline to establish a minimal performance floor.  
- Attribution concern: reported gains may reflect the strong CNN backbone rather than LSDA’s adaptation mechanism without the missing baselines.  
- Clarity issue: Table 1 lacks explicit mapping of rows to specific adaptation techniques/equations and omits justification for k=10 in nearest-neighbor adaptation.  
- Originality: Novel in adapting to held-out categories without detection data, combining domain/task adaptation and nearest-neighbor transfer—logical but non-radical.  
- Significance: Addresses an important scalability problem; 7.6K demo and code release increase practical impact, though gap to oracle detectors remains.  
- Recommendation: Conditional accept — accept if authors add the missing baselines and clarify experimental details; otherwise contribution is overstated.
