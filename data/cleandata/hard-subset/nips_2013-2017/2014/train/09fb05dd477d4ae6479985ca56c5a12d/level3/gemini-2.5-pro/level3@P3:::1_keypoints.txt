- Tackles an important practical problem: scaling detection to many categories lacking bounding-box annotations by converting classifiers into detectors via domain adaptation.
- Proposes LSDA: learn a category-invariant feature transformation from fully supervised classes and transfer category-specific classifier-to-detector weight changes to held-out classes.
- Uses fine-tuning of shared CNN layers to learn detection features and a generic "background" model; transfers final-layer adaptations via nearest-neighbor weight changes.
- Well-written, clear, and logically presented with a clean experimental setup (100 supervised vs 100 held-out categories).
- Simple, intuitive, and elegant decomposition into category-invariant and category-specific adaptation with a multi-stage training procedure.
- Strong empirical gains: substantially outperforms unmodified classifiers and closes ~35% of the gap to a fully supervised oracle on held-out categories.
- Novelty is incremental: combines known ideas (R-CNN fine-tuning, domain adaptation) with the new twist of transferring transformations to unseen classes.
- Long-term significance unclear: data-collection advances (crowdsourcing, active learning, synthetic data) could reduce the problem’s importance; paper should quantify annotation savings vs mAP cost.
- Presentation of results can mislead: relative “50% mAP boost” over a low baseline is less informative than absolute gap-closing (e.g., ~10% → ~16% mAP); recommend emphasizing absolute improvements.
- Comparison clarity lacking: unclear which baselines used image-level labels for held-out categories—needs explicit statement to ensure fair comparisons.
- Relationship to R-CNN should be distilled into a dedicated table/subsection listing concrete differences (softmax vs SVMs, no bbox regression, adaptation mechanism) for clarity.
- Suggest removing less informative qualitative Figure 6 to make room for a direct quantitative/system-architecture comparison with R-CNN.
- Inconsistent terminology across text and figures (LSDA vs DNN/DDA); standardize naming throughout.
- Minor typo: line 431 “classi?ers” should be “classifiers.”
