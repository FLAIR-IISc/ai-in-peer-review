- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors using categories with bounding boxes, then apply it to classifier-only categories to scale detection.  
- Technically sound with compelling experimental gains over a raw-classifier baseline.  
- Ablation study (Fig. 3) supports component contributions and provides useful analysis.  
- Major weakness: missing comparisons to three crucial baselines (full-image positives, MIL-based region selection, mean-box classifier).  
- Concern that the proposed method’s gains may largely come from pretrained Krizhevsky CNN features rather than the adaptation itself.  
- Experiments (Table 1) lack detail about which methods correspond to which equations/layers and hyperparameter choices.  
- Motivation for certain design choices (e.g., k-NN for category-specific adaptation) needs clearer explanation.  
- Approach is novel enough for acceptance, combining domain adaptation and weak supervision for large-scale detection.  
- Method is somewhat specific to certain CNN architectures and not a major architectural innovation.  
- Addresses a significant problem: scaling object detection with limited bounding-box annotations.  
- Demonstrates practical impact (7.6K detector) and releases models/code, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Strengths: clear writing, strong improvements over a simple baseline, publicly available resources, and thorough ablation.  
- Weaknesses: lack of key baselines, unclear attribution of improvements, and insufficient experimental reproducibility details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on adding missing baseline comparisons and expanding experimental detail.
