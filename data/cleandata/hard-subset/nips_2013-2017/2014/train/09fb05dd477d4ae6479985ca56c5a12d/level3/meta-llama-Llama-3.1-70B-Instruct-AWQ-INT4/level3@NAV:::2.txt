This paper proposes a novel framework, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), to adapt image-classification-trained deep nets for object detection. The authors address the challenge of scaling object detection to tens of thousands of categories, where collecting bounding box annotations is impractical. By leveraging image-level annotations, which are easier to obtain, the proposed method learns to transform a classification network into a detection network.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. The CNN architecture is interesting and simple to implement. The evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge demonstrates the efficacy of the approach, with the method ranking 4th in the detection challenge.

However, the main contribution of the paper, the adaptation method, lacks principled justification and detailed motivation. The authors do not provide a clear explanation for why the adapted detection weights are computed as Wj^c + W_avg in Section 3.2, and the physical meaning of the "offset" parameter is unclear. Additionally, the authors do not provide a clear explanation for why the kNN variant outperforms other variants.

The comparisons in the paper are also unfair, as the authors used part of the validation set instead of the test set. Furthermore, the claim that RCNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified due to val2's small size.

Despite these limitations, the method achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art approaches, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the types of errors made by the system. The paper also presents a large-scale detection experiment, demonstrating the capabilities of the technique.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

1. The paper proposes a novel framework for adapting image-classification-trained deep nets for object detection.
2. The method achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art approaches on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge.
3. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the types of errors made by the system.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

1. The main contribution of the paper, the adaptation method, lacks principled justification and detailed motivation.
2. The comparisons in the paper are unfair, and the claim that RCNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified.
3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation for why the adapted detection weights are computed as Wj^c + W_avg in Section 3.2, and the physical meaning of the "offset" parameter is unclear.

**Suggestions for improvement:**

1. Provide a more detailed motivation and justification for the adaptation method.
2. Use a more fair comparison setup, such as using the test set instead of the validation set.
3. Provide a clear explanation for the computation of the adapted detection weights and the physical meaning of the "offset" parameter.
4. Consider providing more analysis on the types of errors made by the system and how they can be improved.
