- Proposes LSDA to adapt image-classification networks into detectors by learning a transformation between tasks.  
- Achieves competitive performance (4th on ImageNet LSVRC-2013) and creates a large-scale 7.6K-category detector.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses a standard CNN (Krizhevsky et al.) aiding reproducibility.  
- Models and software are publicly available, strengthening practical impact.  
- Experimental results appear convincing and technical quality is good.  
- Method lacks a principled theoretical justification for the adaptation approach.  
- The category-specific adaptation term (Wj^c + W_avg) is not well motivated; the meaning of the "offset" and sufficiency of averaging are unclear.  
- The superior performance of the kNN variant (k=10) is unexplained and not analyzed for implications on the feature space.  
- Experimental setup is flawed: part of the validation set (val1) was used for training, making comparisons to other methods unfair.  
- The comparison to R-CNN on small val2 is statistically questionable; paired t-test results lack context given small sample size.  
- Scaling object detection to many categories is a significant and valuable contribution.  
- Validity of state-of-the-art comparisons is undermined by the non-standard train/validation split.  
- Pros for acceptance: competitive benchmark results, addresses an important scaling problem, clear presentation, and available code/models.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient methodological justification, unclear implementation choices, unfair training/validation split, and questionable statistical claims.  
- Overall: promising and impactful direction but current methodological and experimental flaws warrant rejection until re-evaluated with proper splits and stronger justification.
