- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers into object detectors via category-invariant fine-tuning (layers 1–7 + explicit background) and category-specific weight transfer using averaged δ from nearest neighbors in fc8 space.  
- At test time, scores are class score minus background score; avoids SVM and bbox regression for efficiency; demonstrates large-scale (7.6K) and faster SPP-based variant.  
- Builds on R-CNN and relates to domain adaptation and weakly supervised MIL, but focuses on classifier-to-detector transformation for held-out categories without detection labels.  
- Technically sound: simple, well-motivated approach; fine-tuning and background modeling address classification→detection shift; δ transfer is reasonable and ablated.  
- Empirically strong: LSDA substantially outperforms applying a classifier to proposals on held-out categories; ablations show shared-layer fine-tuning and background modeling provide most gains.  
- Completeness: includes oracle upper-bound, strong baseline, and ablations, but some reporting choices and full R-CNN comparisons need clarification.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and organized with easy-to-follow pipeline and informative figures, though some labels and notation need fixing.  
- Originality: combines established components into a coherent adaptation framework; novel empirical demonstration at scale but not a fundamental algorithmic breakthrough.  
- Significance: addresses practical annotation bottleneck and is likely influential, though long-term impact depends on future annotation-cost reductions and trade-offs remain unclear.  
- Pro: Clear problem setup and simple, effective algorithm with solid ablation and error analysis.  
- Pro: Practical large-scale demonstration (7.6K) and a faster inference variant.  
- Pro: Careful framing as task/domain adaptation with standard-benchmark results.  
- Con: Reporting of relative improvements (e.g., “50% relative”, “78%”) is misleading; recommend standardizing on absolute mAP and clearly defining “gap closed.”  
- Con: Figure 3a comparisons need clear markings for which methods use detection labels, proposals, or background modeling to ensure fair comparison.  
- Con: Need concise R-CNN vs LSDA comparison (training data, adaptation steps, background handling, SVM/bbox usage, runtime, mAP); consider replacing Figure 6.  
- Con: Category-specific transfer uses Euclidean fc8 distance—ask for experiments with other similarity measures (WordNet, confusion, earlier-layer features) and sensitivity to k.  
- Con: Evaluation scope limited—report results for seen categories after adaptation, and investigate combining LSDA with SVM/bbox regression to measure final mAP.  
- Con: Positioning relative to weakly supervised/MIL baselines is weak—suggest a small-scale comparison or discussion.  
- Minor: Ensure consistent naming (LSDA), fix typos (e.g., “classi?ers”), and clarify hardware/settings for “fast detector” results.  
- Minor: Explicitly state whether background subtraction is applied to logits or softmax scores and justify the choice.  
- Overall: High-quality, well-validated paper with moderate novelty; with clearer reporting and comparisons it would be stronger.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
