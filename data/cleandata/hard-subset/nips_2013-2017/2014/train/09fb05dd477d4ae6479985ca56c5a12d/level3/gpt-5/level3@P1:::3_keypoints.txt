- Proposes LSDA to convert image classifiers into detectors by fine-tuning layers 1–7 and adding a background class for m detection-labeled categories, plus transferring category-specific weight deltas to K−m unlabeled categories.
- Category-specific transfer uses nearest-neighbor averaging in fc8 space: W^d_j = W^c_j + (1/k) Σ_i δ_B(N_B(j,i)).
- Differs from R-CNN by framing classifier→detector as task/domain adaptation and omitting per-class SVMs and bbox regression for simplicity and speed.
- Empirical setup: simulate 200-class ILSVRC detection with boxes for 100 classes and evaluate on the other 100.
- Results: applying classification to proposals yields ~10.3% mAP; fine-tuning layers+background raises mAP to ~15.85%; neighbor-based category-specific transfer adds a small gain to ~16.15% (p=0.017).
- Error analysis shows substantial reductions in localization and background confusion after adaptation.
- Demonstrates scalability with a released 7.6K-category detector and a faster SPP/fast proposal variant (~2 fps).
- Strengths: simple, principled decomposition (generic fine-tuning + light category-specific transfer) and addresses bounding-box scarcity; solid ablations and public models.
- Main weakness: critical baselines are missing, so gains may be due to strong CNN features and fine-tuning rather than the proposed transfer.
- Missing baseline 1: full-image positives with region negatives to test a simple supervision proxy under identical training.
- Missing baseline 2: MIL-based region selection over proposals using image labels, then fine-tune the detector.
- Missing baseline 3: center/mean-box classifier baseline to gauge dataset bias toward centered objects.
- Attribution concern: most improvement comes from category-invariant fine-tuning/background class; the category-specific nearest-neighbor transfer contributes little.
- Other weaknesses: architecture specificity (AlexNet-like), under-specified experimental details (k sensitivity, effect of m, Table 1 mapping), and limited evaluation on B categories post-adaptation.
- Quality: technically sound and carefully implemented, but empirical support incomplete without the missing baselines.
- Clarity: method is clear, but experiments need clearer mapping of model variants and more hyperparameter reporting (k-sweep, proposal/negative sampling).
- Originality: moderate—incremental over R-CNN, though the classifier→detector delta transfer is a neat twist.
- Significance: high practical impact for scaling detection to thousands of classes, but scientific impact would improve with missing comparisons and broader architecture validation.
- Requested experiments: add the three baselines, analyze sensitivity to k and m, compare visual vs semantic neighbor selection, report performance on B and per-class gains on A, clarify Table 1, and test SVMs/bbox regression variants.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on adding the missing baselines and clarifying experimental variants; Confidence: Medium.
