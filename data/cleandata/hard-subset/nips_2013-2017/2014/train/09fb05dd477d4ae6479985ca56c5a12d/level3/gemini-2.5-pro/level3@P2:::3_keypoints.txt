- Problem: Scale object detection to many categories when bounding-box annotations are too expensive; propose LSDA to turn classifiers into detectors.  
- Method: Start with ImageNet-pretrained CNN, fine-tune on image-level labels, then fine-tune on region proposals for a subset with boxes to learn a category-invariant transform and background model.  
- Adaptation detail: For categories without boxes, use adapted lower-layer features and adapt the final layer by averaging adaptations of nearest annotated neighbors.  
- Empirical result: 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories over the initial classifier baseline.  
- Error analysis: Method mainly reduces localization errors and confusion with background.  
- Strength — significance: Tackles an important, timely bottleneck in large-scale vision systems.  
- Strength — novelty & simplicity: Framing classifier-to-detector conversion as domain adaptation is elegant; the three-step procedure is simple and logical.  
- Strength — empirical: Strong quantitative gains and supportive error analysis.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Weakness — missing baselines: Lacks comparisons to obvious weakly-supervised baselines (e.g., full-image positives + region negatives; MIL-based region selection; simple center/average-box baseline).  
- Weakness — attribution: Unclear how much improvement comes from the adaptation vs. the powerful pretrained CNN and R-CNN pipeline.  
- Weakness — experiment clarity: Table 1 and some experimental details need clearer mapping to architecture/training steps for reproducibility.  
- For acceptance: Significant problem, novel and useful idea, strong results — merits publication.  
- Against acceptance: Incomplete experimental validation; missing baselines leave central claim insufficiently supported.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept but require authors to add stronger baselines and clarify experimental descriptions (esp. Table 1).
