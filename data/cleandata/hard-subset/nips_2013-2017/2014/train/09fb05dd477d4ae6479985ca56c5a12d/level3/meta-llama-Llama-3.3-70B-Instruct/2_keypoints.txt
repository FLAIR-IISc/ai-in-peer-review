- Proposes a hybrid policy-search method combining model-based and model-free strengths using local time-varying linear dynamics and guided policy search.  
- Introduces a KL-divergence constraint to limit per-iteration changes in trajectory distributions, enabling handling of complex/discontinuous dynamics.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with a clear introduction and methodology.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering simulated robotic manipulation and locomotion tasks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness in learning complex, high-dimensional policies.  
- Strength: combines sample efficiency of model-based methods with versatility of model-free techniques.  
- Strength: KL constraint provides a principled way to stabilize updates under discontinuous dynamics.  
- Strength: experiments are thorough and well-designed.  
- Weakness: relies on assumptions (e.g., time-varying linear-Gaussian controllers) that may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: computational cost and scalability are not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Weakness: comparisons to other methods may be unfair due to differing sample/iteration settings.  
- Overall: significant contribution with broad potential, but requires further evaluation of limitations and practical applicability.
