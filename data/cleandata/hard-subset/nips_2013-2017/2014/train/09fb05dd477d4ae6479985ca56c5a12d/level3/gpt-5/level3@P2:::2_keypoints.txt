- Proposes LSDA: convert classification CNN into detectors for categories without bounding-boxes using background learning, shared-layer fine-tuning, and kNN-based category-specific weight deltas.
- Detection score computed as class score minus background score; demonstrated 50% relative mAP gain on held-out categories and a 7.6K-category detector demo.

Strengths:
- Solid empirical gains on held-out categories with clear ablations isolating components.
- Error analysis shows reduced localization and background confusions, supporting the approach’s motivation.
- Demonstrates large-scale feasibility and releases code/models.

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- The additive category-specific adaptation Wd = Wc + δ lacks principled justification; why additive and why only at the final layer?
- kNN selection and averaging using L2 on normalized fc8 weights is heuristic; no analysis of other similarity metrics, different k, or robustness.
- Comparisons are not strictly apples-to-apples (train/val split differences); leaderboard claims are hard to substantiate without matching protocols.
- Omitting SVMs and bbox regression complicates fair comparison against methods that include them.

Clarity issues:
- Section 3.2 needs clearer derivation/motivation for the additive δ and its “physical meaning.”
- Rationale for kNN averaging and choice of k lacks explanation and sensitivity analysis.
- Leaderboard and “similar performance” claims should explicitly state training splits used by competing methods.

Originality:
- Category-invariant adaptation is close to prior R-CNN work; kNN δ-averaging to synthesize detectors for unseen categories is a simple but novel pragmatic idea.

Significance:
- Scaling detection to thousands of categories without bounding boxes is important and potentially impactful, but evaluation and justification weaknesses temper the claims.

Detailed suggestions:
- Provide a principled justification for Wd = Wc + δ (e.g., Taylor approximation or multi-task regularizer derivation).
- Analyze sensitivity to k, alternative similarity metrics (taxonomy, semantic embeddings, confusion-based), and neighbor selection strategies.
- Report test server results or retrain baselines under the same protocol; provide confidence intervals for val2 results.
- Include ablations adding bbox regression and/or SVMs to LSDA to assess compatibility with stronger heads.
- Clarify calibration of background subtraction (scorei − scorebg) and consider scaling methods to address label imbalance.
- Discuss failure modes (e.g., near-synonym confusions) and potential fixes like taxonomy-aware rescoring.

Pros (acceptance arguments):
- Addresses an important problem, is simple and scalable, shows meaningful gains, and provides code/models.

Cons (rejection arguments):
- Core adaptation is heuristic and under-justified; limited analysis of kNN choice; comparisons lack rigorous fairness.

Recommendation:
- Borderline (lean weak reject): interesting and promising but needs stronger theoretical justification and more rigorous, fairer evaluation.
