- Problem: Scaling object detection to many categories with limited bounding-box annotations.
- Contribution: LSDA method to transform image classifiers into object detectors using CNN fine-tuning.
- Key steps: add a background class, transfer category-invariant features, approximate category-specific transformations for unlabeled classes.
- Implementation: Builds on Krizhevsky et al.'s CNN, pre-trained on classification data and fine-tuned with a subset of detection-labeled categories.
- Evaluation: Tested on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, showing significant improvement over baselines.
- Strength — Relevance: Addresses an important, practical problem of reducing expensive bounding-box annotation needs.
- Strength — Technical: Frames classification→detection as domain adaptation and reports a 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories.
- Strength — Scalability: Demonstrates a 7.6K-category detector using mostly classification data.
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written paper with thorough experiments, ablation studies, and error analysis.
- Weakness — Baselines: Lacks comparisons to key baselines (full-image training, MIL-based iterative methods, single-box classifiers).
- Weakness — Experimental clarity: Table 1 and method configurations need clearer presentation for reproducibility.
- Weakness — Feature dependence: Gains may largely come from improved CNN features rather than the adaptation technique itself.
- Weakness — Novelty: Builds heavily on R-CNN and existing domain adaptation ideas; methodological novelty is limited.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to improve experimental clarity and add baseline comparisons in a future revision.
- Pro-accept arguments: Practical, scalable solution with strong benchmark results and clear exposition.
- Anti-accept arguments: Missing critical baselines and limited methodological novelty reduce impact.
