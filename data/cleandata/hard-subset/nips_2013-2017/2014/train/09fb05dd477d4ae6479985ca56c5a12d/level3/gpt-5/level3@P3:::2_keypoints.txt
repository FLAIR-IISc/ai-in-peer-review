- Proposes LSDA to convert image classifiers into detectors when bounding boxes exist only for a subset of categories, framing it as domain adaptation from classification to detection.
- Learns a category-invariant transformation by fine-tuning layers 1–7 and adding a background class, plus a category-specific final-layer adaptation W^d = W^c + δ.
- For unseen classes, predicts δ by averaging δ of k nearest neighbor seen classes in fc8 weight space; test scores are adjusted by a background score.
- Closely related to R-CNN (pretrain on classification, fine-tune on detection) and domain adaptation literature, but transfers to categories with no detection data.
- Reports substantial empirical gains on ILSVRC-2013 (mAP on held-out categories from 10.3%→16.2%) and a 7.6K-class detector with a faster variant.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with reproducible CNN architecture and training protocol; code/models released.
- Addresses a practical, important problem: scaling detection to thousands of categories with limited boxes.
- Strong empirical improvements over classification-only baseline and informative error analysis showing reduced background/localization errors.
- Clean, modular idea likely useful to practitioners; background class and category-invariant fine-tuning are effective.
- Main category-specific adaptation (additive δ and averaging in fc8 space) is heuristic and lacks principled justification or theoretical explanation.
- No exploration of alternative adaptation forms (e.g., multiplicative scaling, learned mapper, feature-space adaptation).
- Choice of kNN, neighbor metric, and sensitivity to k are underexplained and only briefly analyzed.
- Some comparisons use non-standard data splits (val1/val2), complicating fair comparison to R-CNN and challenge entries; statistical significance is not fully reported.
- Omitting SVM heads and bbox regression simplifies training but reduces comparability with contemporaneous state-of-the-art detectors.
- Experimentation and ablation isolate main gains but do not justify the additivity assumption, fc8 neighbor space, or kNN averaging.
- Section 3.2 needs clearer derivation and interpretation of δ, including magnitude/direction and calibration after adding background head.
- The approach is a novel practical synthesis but has limited theoretical originality—combines known components in a useful way.
- Significant practical impact (large-scale detector, 2 fps variant) but claims are weakened by heuristic adaptation and evaluation caveats.
- Suggested improvements: provide principled rationale for additivity, analyze δ, compare neighbor spaces/metrics, report sensitivity to k, and explore learned mappers.
- Suggested improvements: strengthen evaluation fairness (standard train+val splits, CI/bootstrap tests), calibrate background scores, and analyze failure modes and taxonomy-based neighbor selection.
- Pros for acceptance: timely problem, practical method with public release, clear paper, solid ablations, large-scale demonstration, competitive ILSVRC results.
- Cons against acceptance: adaptation step lacks principled grounding, insufficient analysis of kNN, fairness/statistical rigor issues in comparisons, limited exploration of alternatives.
- Overall: useful, clearly written paper with promising results; recommend acceptance if authors better justify category-specific adaptation and strengthen comparative evaluation, otherwise a strong borderline.
