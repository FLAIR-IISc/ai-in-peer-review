- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation to adapt image classifiers into object detectors to leverage abundant image-level labels and scarce bounding boxes.  
- Implements a CNN-based three-stage adaptation: category-invariant adaptation, category-specific adaptation, then detection.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, showing a reported ~50% relative mAP improvement and a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Practical contribution: released models and code (lsda.berkeleyvision.org) and offers a fast detector variant (~2 fps).  
- Strength: directly addresses the bounding-box data scarcity limiting large-scale detection.  
- Strength: strong experimental results and demonstration at large scale (7.6K classes).  
- Strength: conceptually clear, simple, and well-motivated approach that is reproducible.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to key baselines (full-image positives with region negatives, MIL region selection, mean-box classifier).  
- Weakness: experimental details (especially Table 1 configurations) are insufficiently clear about which equations/layers were used.  
- Weakness: originality is incremental—builds heavily on existing CNN and adaptation techniques rather than introducing a radical new idea.  
- Weakness: potential bias due to reliance on Krizhevsky et al. CNN features; unclear how much gain comes from adaptation versus features.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: important problem, meaningful improvements, practical and reproducible work.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing baseline comparisons, unclear experiments, and strong dependence on pre-trained features weaken claims.  
- Overall assessment: solid but borderline paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add baselines, clarify experiments, and discuss pre-trained feature influence.
