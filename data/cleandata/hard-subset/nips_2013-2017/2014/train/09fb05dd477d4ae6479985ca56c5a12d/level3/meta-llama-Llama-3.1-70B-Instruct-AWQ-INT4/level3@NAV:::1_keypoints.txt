- Proposes LSDA algorithm to adapt object classifiers into detectors without requiring bounding boxes at scale.  
- Learns and transfers the difference between classification and detection tasks to categories lacking box annotations.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, approach, and experiments.  
- Provides thorough analysis including ablation study and detailed error analysis.  
- Algorithm is simple, straightforward, and clearly explained.  
- Experimental setup and evaluation protocol are well-designed with a strong baseline comparison.  
- LSDA yields substantial gains: ~50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories.  
- Demonstrates scalability by producing a 7,604-category detector.  
- Original contribution: combines two existing adaptation ideas in a novel way and differentiates from prior work.  
- Technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments; authors are careful and honest about strengths and weaknesses.  
- Significance somewhat limited since the approach may be surpassed by future work.  
- Trade-off between needing fewer boxes and achieved mAP is unclear and hard to assess.  
- Long-term importance of bounding-box scarcity may decrease with increased resources.  
- Recommended minor revisions: clarify which methods in Figure 3a use category labels for detection.  
- Suggested addendum: provide a dedicated table/subsection explicitly contrasting LSDA with R-CNN (consider removing Figure 6).  
- Minor fixes needed: consistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and correct typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
