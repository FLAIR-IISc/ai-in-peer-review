- Proposes LSDA: train CNN on image-level labels, fine-tune shared layers+background on detection data for subset B, and transfer fc8 deltas from nearest neighbors to held-out classes A for detection on proposals.  
- Builds on R-CNN and domain adaptation; differs by learning background, transferring classification→detection transform, and nearest-neighbor fc8 delta transfer.  
- Strength: tackles scalable detection without per-class boxes with clear motivation.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains (~50% relative mAP over classification-on-proposals) and convincing error analysis reducing background/localization errors.  
- Strength: Ablations show shared-layer adaptation and background class are dominant contributors; fc8 delta adds smaller but significant improvement.  
- Strength: Demonstrates scalability (7.6K detector, SPP variant) and releases models/code.  
- Weakness: Omits three important baselines—(1) full-image positives with region negatives, (2) MIL-based region selection with same CNN, and (3) center/mean-box classifier—that could change conclusions.  
- Weakness: Attribution concern that gains may primarily come from strong CNN features and background modeling rather than the neighbor-based fc8 transfer.  
- Weakness: Experimental clarity issues—Table 1 lacks precise mapping of rows to layer updates, background use, fc8 deltas, k values, and equations.  
- Weakness: Missing comparison to R-CNN-style SVM/regressor training on B to contextualize absolute performance and cost of skipping SVM/regression.  
- Clarity request: Provide explicit line-by-line descriptions of each Table 1 variant, data split details, and nearest-neighbor selection sensitivity.  
- Clarity request: Include runtime/memory summary for the 7.6K detector variants.  
- Originality: Novel framing of learning a generic classification→detection transform on subset classes and transferring it, though components are familiar.  
- Significance: Important problem with practical impact; released large-scale detectors are valuable to the community.  
- Pros for acceptance: Timely problem, simple effective method, empirical gains, error analysis, and released code/models.  
- Cons against acceptance: Missing strong baselines, reproducibility ambiguities, and possibility that main gains stem from generic fine-tuning/background rather than LSDA transfer.  
- Questions: How does LSDA compare to the full-image-positives baseline and a MIL baseline; sensitivity to neighbor metric, feature layer, and k; which layers matter most to fine-tune; runtime/memory and proposal counts?  
- Suggestions: Add the three missing baselines, perform k-sweep and neighbor-distance variants, clarify Table 1 and experimental details, and report per-class error breakdowns and failure modes.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—promising and useful but needs stronger baselines and clearer experiments.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
