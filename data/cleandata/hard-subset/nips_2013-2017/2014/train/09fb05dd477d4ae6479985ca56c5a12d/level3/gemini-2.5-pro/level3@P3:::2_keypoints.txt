- Proposal: LSDA adapts image-classification networks into object detectors to overcome scarcity of bounding-box annotations.  
- Motivation: Leverage abundant image-level labels to scale detection to many more categories.  
- Method (category-invariant): Fine-tune shared conv1–fc7 layers on detection data and learn a generic background class.  
- Method (category-specific): Adjust final-layer weights by applying additive offsets learned from k-nearest neighbor categories with detection data.  
- Novelty: Introduces a transferable classifier→detector mapping enabling detection for categories without bounding-box supervision.  
- Clarity: Paper is very well written, organized, and supported by clear figures and ablation studies.  
- Empirical results: Reports ~+50% relative mAP on held-out categories, shows both adaptation stages help, and releases a 7604-category detector.  
- Weakness: Category-specific adaptation is heuristic—the additive-offset model and k-NN averaging lack principled justification or deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: Uses a non-standard evaluation split (val1/val2), making fair comparison to prior work difficult.  
- Weakness: Some statistical claims (significance, closeness to fully-supervised R-CNN) are not fully justified given small val2 and differing protocols.  
- Overall: Simple, effective, and significant contribution, but would be stronger with a principled adaptation rationale and standard evaluation reporting.
