- Proposes LSDA: learn a general transformation from classifiers to detectors using categories with bounding boxes and apply it to categories with only image labels (domain adaptation).
- Addresses a practical, important problem—scaling object detection to many categories—via a simple, intuitive three-stage adaptation process.
- Shows compelling results with significant mAP improvement over a classifier baseline and is well-written and clear.
- Major experimental weakness: missing critical baseline comparisons—(1) model trained on full-image positives + region-proposal negatives, (2) standard MIL for weakly-supervised region selection, (3) center/mean-box baseline.
- Concern that improvements may largely stem from strong Krizhevsky CNN features rather than the adaptation method itself.
- Experiments need clearer reporting—Table 1 should explicitly link each result to the specific model components (equations, adapted layers).
- Overall: novel and significant enough for acceptance, but claims would be stronger if the authors add the missing baselines.
