- Problem: Scaling object detection is bottlenecked by bounding-box annotations; paper leverages image-level labels to train detectors for categories without box annotations.  
- Core idea: Learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors on annotated categories and apply it to classifier-only categories.  
- Method novelty: Adapting classifiers to detectors for categories lacking bounding boxes is a novel and important contribution.  
- Empirical strength: Competitive results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 with ~50% relative mAP improvement over raw classifier baseline.  
- Scalability: Demonstrates potential to scale detection to thousands of categories (e.g., 7604) and provides a fast detector implementation.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, figures are helpful, and code/models are publicly available for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical weakness: Central adaptation method lacks strong theoretical justification and detailed motivation.  
- Parameter choice unclear: Using average offset W_avg for category-specific adaptation is not well motivated or interpreted; alternatives not explored.  
- Evaluation concerns: Using part of validation set for training raises fairness and rigor issues; test-set comparisons and statistical support are lacking.  
- Ablation analysis limited: Ablation study is presented but the discussion is superficial and lacks deeper analysis of why components work.  
- Comparison fairness: Computational resources, training time, and hyperparameter tuning are not detailed, making comparisons to prior work potentially unfair.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a key detection challenge, shows strong empirical and scalability results, and provides public code/models.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks theoretical grounding, has potential evaluation bias, and needs deeper experimental analysis and parameter clarity.  
- Overall assessment: Good, promising paper with compelling empirical results but requires stronger justification and more rigorous evaluation to be fully convincing.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (recommend minor revisions to address clarity and justification).
