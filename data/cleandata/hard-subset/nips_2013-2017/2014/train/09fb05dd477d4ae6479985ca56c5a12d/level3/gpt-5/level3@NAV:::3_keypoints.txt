- Proposal: LSDA adapts an ImageNet-trained AlexNet classifier into a detector when only some classes have bounding boxes by fine-tuning shared layers on annotated classes and transferring category-specific deltas to unannotated classes via nearest neighbors.
- Pipeline details: (1) train K-way classifier, (2) fine-tune on region proposals for m annotated classes adding a background class, (3) approximate unannotated class detector weights by averaging fc8 shifts from nearest annotated neighbors.
- Detection scoring: score region proposals and subtract the background logit for final detection scores.
- Relation to prior work: akin to R-CNN-style fine-tuning and domain adaptation; complementary to MIL-based weakly supervised detection.
- Quality: technically sound with compelling results — ~50% relative mAP gain on held-out classes and a released 7.6K-category detector.
- Ablations: analyses highlight the importance of background modeling and feature adaptation.
- Missing baselines weaken claims: no “full-image positives + region negatives” baseline using same AlexNet features is provided.
- Missing baselines: no MIL-based region selection baseline trained with weak labels to compare against weak-supervision methods.
- Missing baseline: no trivial center/mean-box classifier to calibrate dataset localization difficulty.
- Attribution concern: without (1) and (2), unclear if gains come from LSDA or from AlexNet/background modeling; baseline (3) would quantify trivial localization effects.
- Clarity issue: experiments/table need explicit mapping of each row to exact training configuration (layers updated, background presence, neighbor k, scoring formula) for reproducibility.
- Originality: the domain-adaptation + nearest-neighbor delta transfer is novel enough, but the contribution depends on architectures with separable shared layers and linear heads (e.g., AlexNet).
- Significance: high — scaling detection with minimal box supervision addresses an important problem and released models/resources increase impact.
- Pros for acceptance: simple logical method, strong empirical gains and large-scale deployment, clear error analysis, practical relevance with released code/models.
- Cons against acceptance: three critical baselines missing, attribution ambiguity (may primarily reflect AlexNet/background effects), and some experimental details need clearer exposition.
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance conditional on adding the missing baselines or providing strong justification for their absence.
