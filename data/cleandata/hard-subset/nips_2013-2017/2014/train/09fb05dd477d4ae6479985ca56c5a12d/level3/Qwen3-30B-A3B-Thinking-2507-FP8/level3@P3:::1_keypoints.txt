- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors using bounding-box annotations for a subset of categories to adapt classifiers for categories without boxes.
- Frames the task as domain adaptation from classification (source) to detection (target).
- Key insight: the classification→detection transformation learned on some categories generalizes to other categories lacking bounding boxes.
- Builds on R-CNN and MIL ideas; differs from R-CNN by requiring boxes for only a subset of categories.
- Experiments train on 100 box-annotated categories and test on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Main quantitative result: LSDA closes ~35% of the gap between classifier baseline and oracle detector (Figure 3b).
- Claims like "50% relative mAP improvement" and "78%" are misleading or refer to different metrics and need clarification.
- Strengths: clear writing, simple implementable algorithm, meaningful improvement over baseline, scalable to thousands of categories, thorough experiments.
- Weaknesses: main result presentation unclear; significance of bounding-box scarcity as a long-term problem is not fully argued.
- Requested clarifications: specify which methods in Figure 3a use category labels for detection, and add a table contrasting LSDA vs R-CNN.
- Presentation fixes: remove/replace Figure 6 to make space, fix inconsistent terminology ("DNN" vs "DDA") and typos.
- Originality: moderate—novel combination of existing ideas rather than a groundbreaking new concept.
- Significance: limited by unclear trade-off between reduced box annotation and mAP loss, and the possibility that annotation scarcity may be solved by resources.
- Recommendation: solid NIPS paper worth acceptance after minor revisions to clarify results, comparisons, and presentation.
