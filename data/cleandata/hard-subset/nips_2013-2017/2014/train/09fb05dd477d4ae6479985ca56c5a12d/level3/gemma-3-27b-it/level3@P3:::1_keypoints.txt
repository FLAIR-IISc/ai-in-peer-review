- Addresses scaling object detection by adapting classifiers trained on image-level labels into detectors to overcome bounding-box scarcity.
- Proposes LSDA: a straightforward domain-adaptation algorithm using fine-tuning and nearest-neighbor weighting to transfer classification knowledge to detection.
- Experimental setup trains on 100 categories with boxes and tests on 100 held-out categories with labels only to isolate adaptation capability.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clear; Figures 1–2 effectively illustrate the adaptation process.
- Technical quality is strong; rigorous experiments and an ablation study (Figure 3) support component effectiveness.
- Originality is in the specific combination of existing techniques (domain adaptation + nearest-neighbor weighting), not a revolutionary new method.
- Significance: demonstrates a viable path to large-scale detection but only fills ~35% of the gap to oracle performance, leaving substantial mAP shortfall.
- Concern: clarify which methods in Figure 3a (besides LSDA and R-CNN) use image-level labels or MIL to ensure fair comparison.
- Suggestion: emphasize the DNN result in Figure 3b (~35% gap filling) as the main result and avoid misleading relative-improvement claims.
- Suggestion: add a dedicated comparison to R-CNN (training data needs, computational cost, fine-tuning, output handling, SVMs/bbox regression); consider removing Figure 6 to make space.
- Minor edits: fix typo on line 431 ("classi?ers") and ensure consistent method naming ("DNN" vs. "LSDA").
- Pros: tackles a critical problem, presents a simple effective algorithm, shows measurable improvement on held-out categories, and is clearly presented.
- Cons: incremental advance, limited by mAP trade-off, long-term relevance may decline as annotation resources grow, and performance claims can be overstated.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
