- Introduces LSDA: converts pre-trained image classifiers into object detectors for categories without bounding-box annotations by framing it as domain adaptation.
- Learns a generic classifier-to-detector transformation from categories with both image-level labels and bounding boxes, then applies it to held-out categories.
- Builds on R-CNN’s fine-tuning strengths while addressing R-CNN’s limitation of requiring bounding boxes for every new category.
- Paper is high quality, clearly written, and presents a simple, well-motivated algorithm with a sound experimental setup using held-out categories.
- Original contribution is the novel combination of fine-tuning for detection and domain adaptation to transfer knowledge across categories.
- Significance is debatable: method closes ~35% of the gap to a fully supervised oracle, raising questions about practical utility versus mAP drop.
- Long-term relevance is uncertain given possible future large-scale bounding-box data collection that may reduce annotation scarcity.
- Abstract’s "50% relative mAP boost" claim is misleading; results are better described as closing one-third of the gap to the oracle (Figure 3b).
- Revision 1: Clarify in Figure 3a which compared methods use category labels during training for fair comparison.
- Revision 2: Add a dedicated table/subsection explicitly comparing LSDA and R-CNN architectures and training differences; consider removing Figure 6 to free space.
