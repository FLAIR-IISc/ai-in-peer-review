- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors to train detectors for categories lacking bounding boxes, showing improved performance on ImageNet LSVRC-2013.

- Addresses scalable detection problem by leveraging abundant image-level labels to reduce reliance on costly bounding box annotations.

- Builds on R-CNN and domain adaptation ideas but extends them to held-out categories without box annotations.

- Strength: method is technically sound with a clear, well-motivated training procedure and convincing ablation study.

- Strength: paper is very well written, organized, and supported by helpful figures and tables.

- Strength: original combination of existing ideas to tackle large-scale detection for held-out categories.

- Strength: significant potential impact by enabling detection for many categories using only image-level labels.

- Strength: release of models and code enhances reproducibility and community value.

- Weakness: contribution is somewhat incremental—combines existing techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new algorithms.

- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the trade-off between annotation cost savings and reduced detection accuracy.

- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the long-term relevance if large-scale bounding box annotation efforts increase.

- Weakness: presentation of claimed improvements (e.g., “78%” and “50% relative mAP”) is potentially misleading and needs clearer framing around the held-out-category baseline.

- Weakness: needs a more explicit, focused comparison to R-CNN to clarify differences.

- For acceptance: tackles an important problem with a sound method, clear presentation, convincing experiments, and released code—recommend accept.

- Against acceptance: incremental nature, insufficient trade-off analysis, and uncertain long-term relevance could argue against acceptance.

- Specific comment: clarify which methods use category labels in Figure 3a for fair comparison.

- Specific comment: center discussion on DNN performance in Figure 3b and revise the “78%”/“50%” claims accordingly.

- Specific comment: add a dedicated section/table comparing LSDA to R-CNN (consider removing Figure 6).

- Specific comment: fix typo on line 431 (“classi?ers”) and resolve naming inconsistencies (e.g., “DNN” vs. “DDA”).

- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper with minor presentation and comparison issues; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
