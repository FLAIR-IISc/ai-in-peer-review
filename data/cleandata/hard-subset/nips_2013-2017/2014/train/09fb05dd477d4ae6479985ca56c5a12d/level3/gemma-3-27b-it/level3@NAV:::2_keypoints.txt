- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classification to detection features using categories with bounding boxes, then apply it to categories without boxes; strong ImageNet LSVRC-2013 results (4th) and a 7.6K detector.  
- Paper is very clear and well-written; CNN choice (Krizhevsky et al.) is sensible and practical to implement.  
- Empirical results are promising, showing potential for leveraging abundant classification data to scale detection.  
- The adaptation mechanism lacks principled justification: the update rule (Wj^c + W_avg / offsets) is ad-hoc and the “offset” parameter’s meaning is unclear.  
- The kNN variant’s superior performance is unexplained—why averaging nearest-neighbor offsets works better needs analysis.  
- Evaluation methodology is problematic: training on val1 and testing on val2 deviates from standard practice and may inflate results, making comparisons unfair.  
- The claim that R-CNN performs similarly on val2 is not statistically significant due to the small test set.  
- Overall: compelling empirical approach but needs stronger theoretical/intuition for adaptation and corrected, fair evaluation.
