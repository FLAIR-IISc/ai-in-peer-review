- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers into detectors via weakly-supervised learning using Krizhevsky CNN architecture.  
- Method: train classifier on all classes, fine-tune on region proposals with bounding boxes for a subset, add background class, and apply category-specific adaptation for unlabeled classes.  
- Empirical result: significant improvement on ImageNet detection—approximately 50% relative mAP gain over classification-only baseline.  
- Scalability: method scales to 7,600 categories using classification data for most categories.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation addressing large-scale detection and a simple, logical approach likely valuable to the community.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on a challenging dataset indicate practical impact.  
- Main weakness: lacks comparisons to three obvious baselines—(1) full-image positives with region negatives, (2) MIL-based region selection, and (3) a mean-box classifier baseline.  
- Attribution concern: improvements may be due to strong CNN features rather than the proposed adaptation technique, undermining the claimed contribution.  
- Experimental clarity issue: Table 1 and experiment descriptions lack clear explanations of what each method corresponds to and implementation choices.  
- Novelty: sufficiently novel for acceptance but not highly innovative.  
- Recommendation: authors should add the missing baseline comparisons (especially mean-box) and clarify experimental details before publication.  
- Overall: solid and significant contribution, but missing critical baselines prevents a compelling case for method superiority.
