- Proposes LSDA, a framework to adapt image-classification-trained deep nets into object detectors to scale detection to tens of thousands of categories.  
- Leverages readily available image-level annotations to avoid costly bounding-box collection.  
- Paper is well-written and presents a simple, implementable CNN architecture.  
- Method ranks 4th on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, showing competitive performance.  
- Demonstrates a large-scale detection experiment and provides thorough error-type analysis.  
- Main adaptation method lacks principled justification and detailed motivation.  
- No clear explanation for the adapted detection weights formula Wj^c + W_avg or the physical meaning of the "offset" parameter.  
- Unclear why the kNN variant outperforms other variants; explanation is missing.  
- Comparisons are potentially unfair because part of the validation set was used instead of the test set.  
- Claim that RCNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified given val2’s small size.  
- Despite limitations, method achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Suggested improvements: better theoretical motivation, use proper test-set comparisons, clarify weight/offset computation, and add more analysis on error reduction strategies.
