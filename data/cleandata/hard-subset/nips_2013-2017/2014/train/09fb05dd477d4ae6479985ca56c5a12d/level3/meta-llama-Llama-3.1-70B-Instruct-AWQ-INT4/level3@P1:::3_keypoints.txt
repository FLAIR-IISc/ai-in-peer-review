- Proposes LSDA: transform classifiers into detectors without bounding-box annotations for all categories.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, achieving ~50% relative mAP boost over using raw classifier weights on proposals.
- Method is a clear, simple extension of the Krizhevsky CNN and is well-written and logical.
- Experiments are thorough and results are compelling, though largely leveraging strong CNN features rather than a radical new idea.
- Reviewer requests comparisons to three missing baselines: full-image positives with region negatives, MIL-based region selection, and center/mean-box classifier.
- Unclear whether LSDA outperforms the first two baselines; these comparisons are necessary to support claims.
- Experimental descriptions lack clarity—Table 1 methods should be explicitly tied to specific equations/layers.
- Include the simple mean-box baseline to contextualize dataset/task difficulty.
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance but the approach is somewhat specific to certain CNN architectures and not highly innovative.
- Concern that improvements may stem from strong CNN features rather than the weakly-supervised detector training; additional analysis is needed.
- Recommendations: add the three baseline comparisons, clarify experimental methods in Table 1, analyze feature vs. method contribution, and include mean-box baseline.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution and likely worthy of acceptance despite baseline/clarity concerns.
