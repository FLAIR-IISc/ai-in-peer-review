- Problem: scaling object detection to many categories where bounding-box labels are expensive.
- Core idea: treat classifier→detector conversion as a domain adaptation problem (LSDA).
- Method: learn a generic transformation on categories with detection data, apply it to classifiers for categories with only image-level labels.
- Implementation: builds on R-CNN but generates detectors for categories without bounding-box supervision.
- Two adaptation steps: category-invariant fine-tuning of shared CNN layers (1–7) on detection data.
- Two adaptation steps (cont.): category-specific adaptation of final layer by averaging weight changes from nearest-neighbor source detectors.
- Evaluation: ILSVRC2013 with 100 held-out detection categories (out of 200).
- Results: significant improvement over raw classifier baseline; scales to produce a 7.6K-category detector.
- Quantitative performance: held-out mAP 16.15% vs baseline 10.31% and fully supervised oracle 26.89%.
- Ablation & analysis: strong ablation study and error analysis showing reduced localization and background confusion.
- Clarity & quality: clearly written, well-organized, convincingly motivated and experimentally validated.
- Originality: novel problem formulation of transferable classifier-to-detector mapping; combines known techniques in a new way.
- Significance caveat: improvement closes only ~35% of gap to fully supervised model; practical utility of trade-off unclear.
- Long-term concern: significance depends on continued scarcity of bounding-box data; large annotation efforts may reduce need.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses practical problem, simple and intuitive method, sound experiments, novel idea, impressive scalability.
- Arguments against acceptance: held-out performance still far from supervised upper bound; contribution is a novel combination rather than a fundamentally new technique; long-term impact debatable.
- Suggestion — framing: report gap closed to oracle (~35%) rather than just 50% relative boost for clearer context.
- Suggestion — comparison: add explicit table/subsection contrasting differences from R-CNN (fine-tuning data, SVMs, bbox regression).
- Minor: final proofreading to remove typos.
