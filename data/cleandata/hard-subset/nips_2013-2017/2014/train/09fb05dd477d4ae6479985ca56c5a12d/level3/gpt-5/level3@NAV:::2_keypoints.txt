- Proposal: LSDA adapts CNN image classifiers into detectors when bounding boxes exist for only some classes, combining category-invariant and category-specific adaptation.
- Category-invariant adaptation: fine-tune shared detection layers and learn a background class to transfer classification features to detection.
- Category-specific adaptation: add an “offset” to fc8 classification weights for unlabeled classes by averaging fine-tuning deltas of k nearest labeled neighbors.
- Relation to prior work: builds on R-CNN’s finding that detection fine-tuning matters and relates to domain adaptation and weakly supervised detection/MIL.
- Empirical results: competitive on ILSVRC-2013 (4th) and demonstrates a scalable 7.6K-category detector with a fast variant.
- Methodological strength: overall sound approach with clear empirical gains over classification-only baselines.
- Main theoretical weakness: the adaptation rule Wj^d = Wj^c + average(δ) lacks a principled derivation or interpretation.
- Missing analysis: the physical/statistical meaning of δ (e.g., gradient/Hessian or first-order update view) is unexplained.
- Unclear kNN rationale: no justification for why fc8 Euclidean proximity predicts transferability or why kNN averaging outperforms alternatives.
- Evaluation concerns: some training uses val portions while comparisons use different protocols; claims vs. R-CNN on val2 lack proper statistical support due to small size.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and reproducible; architecture and training are straightforward to implement.
- Suggested clarifications: Section 3.2 needs explicit motivation/derivation for the offset and discussion of calibration effects from the background softmax.
- Originality: novel idea to learn a generic classifier-to-detector transform and transfer category-specific adjustments without per-class boxes.
- Significance: important potential to scale detection to thousands of categories, though mixed evaluation settings weaken evidential strength.
- Pros: practical, simple to implement with released code/models; meaningful gains and a demonstrative large-scale 7.6K result.
- Cons: insufficient principled justification for the offset, unexplained kNN choice, and fairness/statistical issues in comparisons.
- Recommendations: derive Wj^d (e.g., first-order Taylor/meta-learning), analyze δ semantics and calibration, compare neighbor metrics (semantic/visual), report k/class-split sensitivity, and include official test-server or matched-val results with significance tests.
