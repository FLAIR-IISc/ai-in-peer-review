- Proposes LSDA, an algorithm to adapt image classifiers into object detectors without bounding-box annotations by learning a transformation from a small set of annotated categories.  
- Method framed as domain adaptation using CNNs with three components: category-invariant adaptation, category-specific adaptation, and detection.  
- Relevant to the community for addressing scalability of object detection to many categories.  
- Clear, well-organized, and well-written paper with sufficient details for expert reproducibility.  
- Experimental results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 show significant improvement over baseline classifier-weights-on-proposals.  
- Demonstrates LSDA can convert any ImageNet-trained classifier into a detector even without detection labels for that class.  
- Novel contribution in scaling detection without bounding boxes, with a simple and straightforward algorithm.  
- Originality limited: combines two existing adaptation ideas rather than introducing a fundamentally new concept.  
- Significance unclear: trade-off between using fewer bounding boxes and mAP improvement is hard to evaluate.  
- Questionable long-term importance: bounding-box scarcity might be solved by future resource investment.  
- Minor clarity issues: inconsistent method naming ("DNN" vs "DDA"), a typo ("classi?ers"), and unclear Figure 3a; need to state which methods use category labels for fair comparison.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; reviewer rating 7/10.
