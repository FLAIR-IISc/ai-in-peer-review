- Proposes LSDA: a three-stage method to adapt a Krizhevsky CNN from classification to detection (classification training, region-proposal training with background, add background + adapt weights).  
- Demonstrates strong results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection, achieving ~50% relative mAP improvement over using raw classifier weights on proposals.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough analysis including ablation studies and detailed error analysis on held-out categories.  
- Strength: simplicity and logical use of strong CNN features to enable detection for many classes without bounding-box annotations.  
- Limitation: lacks comparisons to obvious baselines (full-image positives with region negatives, MIL-based region selection, center/mean-box classifier).  
- Attribution concern: unclear how much improvement comes from the adaptation method versus the pre-existing strength of Krizhevsky CNN features.  
- Originality: sufficiently novel for acceptance but relatively specific to certain CNN architectures and not highly innovative.  
- Overall: promising and potentially impactful contribution, but results need stronger baseline comparisons and deeper attribution analysis.  
- Recommendation: compare to missing baselines to better validate claims.  
- Recommendation: provide further analysis to isolate the contribution of adaptation versus CNN features.  
- Recommendation: evaluate on additional datasets/tasks to demonstrate generalizability and robustness.
