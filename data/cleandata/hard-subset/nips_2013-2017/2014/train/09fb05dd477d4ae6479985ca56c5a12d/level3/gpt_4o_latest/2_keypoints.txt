- Proposes LSDA framework to adapt CNNs from image classification to object detection, addressing label vs. bounding-box scarcity.  
- Demonstrates practical impact with ~50% relative mAP improvement over baselines and 4th place in ImageNet LSVRC-2013.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to varied audiences.  
- CNN structure and adaptation method are simple and easy to implement, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Provides detailed experimental setup and ablation study to analyze component contributions.  
- Releases models and software (lsda.berkeleyvision.org), promoting transparency and follow-up work.  
- Novel contribution: adapts classifiers into detectors without per-category bounding boxes using category-invariant and category-specific transformations.  
- Shows scalability by building a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Lacks clear theoretical justification for the adaptation method and certain design choices (e.g., kNN offset).  
- Superiority of the kNN variant is not convincingly demonstrated due to weak analysis.  
- Evaluation methodology is questionable: comparisons use validation rather than test set, risking bias.  
- Reported performance gains on the small validation set are not statistically significant, reducing result reliability.  
- Recommendation: promising work but needs stronger theoretical explanation and fairer, more rigorous evaluation.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, scalable, strong empirical results, reproducible methodology.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient theory, unfair evaluation, and lack of statistically significant gains.
