- Proposes LSDA: learn transformation from classification to detection on a subset of categories and transfer it to others.  
- Demonstrates ~50% relative mAP improvement over a classification-only baseline on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and includes a thorough ablation study.  
- Ablation shows adapting the feature representation is the most important component; adapting category-specific parameters has smaller effect.  
- Method scales to produce high-quality detectors for tens of thousands of categories, addressing labeled-data scarcity.  
- Main adaptation step (computing adapted detection weights as Wj^c + W_avg) lacks principled justification and clear motivation.  
- The physical meaning of the "offset" parameter is unclear.  
- No clear explanation for why the kNN variant outperforms other variants.  
- Some comparisons are unfair: authors use part of the validation set instead of the test set.  
- Claim that R-CNN has similar performance on val2 is not statistically justified due to small val2 size.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques (builds on R-CNN) but not a major departure in architecture or adaptation method.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and shows results that could impact applications with limited labeled data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, significant empirical improvement, tackles large-scale detection with limited labels.  
- Arguments against acceptance: adaptation lacks theoretical justification, unfair comparisons, limited novelty in method.  
- Recommendations: explain adaptation formula and "offset" meaning, use test set for comparisons, provide detailed error breakdown and limitations, and add experiments across architectures/datasets.
