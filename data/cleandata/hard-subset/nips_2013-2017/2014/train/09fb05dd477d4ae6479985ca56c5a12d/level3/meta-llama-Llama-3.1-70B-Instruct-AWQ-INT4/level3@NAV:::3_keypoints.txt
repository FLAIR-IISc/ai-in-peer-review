- Proposes LSDA: adapt a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to detection by learning and transferring classification→detection differences to categories without bounding-box data.  
- Demonstrates efficacy on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, achieving ~50% relative mAP boost over using raw classifier weights on object proposals.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains the algorithm and components, and is organized for reproducibility.  
- Provides thorough analysis including an ablation study and useful error analysis.  
- Results are high-quality, compelling, and significant—addresses a difficult detection problem and is the first to adapt to held-out categories with no detection data.  
- Lacks comparisons to obvious baselines (full-image positives with region negatives, MIL-based region selection, center/mean-box classifier), which could challenge claims.  
- Approach appears relatively specific to certain CNN architectures; unclear how to apply to other architectures or domains.  
- Concern about attribution: improvements may stem from strong CNN features rather than the proposed weakly-supervised detector training.  
- Recommends acceptance but requests revisions to address the noted concerns.  
- Suggested revisions: add comparisons to the missing baselines, explain applicability to other architectures/domains, and provide additional analysis to address attribution.
