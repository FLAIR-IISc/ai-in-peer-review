- Proposes LSDA: adapting object classifiers into detectors without requiring bounding boxes at scale.  
- Uses a simple, multi-stage algorithm that learns the classifier→detector transformation and applies it to categories lacking detection data.  
- Shows ~50% relative improvement over a baseline that applies raw classifier weights to object proposals.  
- Demonstrates scalability by producing a 7604-category detector.  
- Experiments on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 include ablation study and error analysis; results are reproducible from provided details.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with well-annotated figures and tables.  
- Originality: novel combination of two existing adaptation ideas, but not a ground-breaking advance.  
- Significance: high — addresses a difficult problem and is likely to influence future work.  
- Concern: unclear trade-off between using fewer boxes and the reported mAP improvements.  
- Concern: the long-term importance of bounding-box scarcity may diminish if resources are invested to collect boxes.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").  
- Clarification requested: in Figure 3a, indicate which methods (besides the authors’ method and R-CNN) use category labels for detection.  
- Reporting suggestion: focus main result on the DNN performance in Figure 3b; current 78% and 50% claims are potentially misleading.  
- Suggestion: add a dedicated table/subsection clearly specifying differences from R-CNN and consider removing Figure 6 to make space.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality paper with strong technical soundness and evaluation (Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scalable method, simplicity, strong empirical gains and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: likely to be surpassed by future work, unclear trade-offs, and possible reduced long-term problem importance.
