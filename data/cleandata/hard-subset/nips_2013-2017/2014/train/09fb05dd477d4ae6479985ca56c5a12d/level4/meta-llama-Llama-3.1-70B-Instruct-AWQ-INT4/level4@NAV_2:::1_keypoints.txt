- Tackles object detection by adapting object classifiers to detection to avoid needing bounding-box-scale annotations.  
- Proposes a straightforward algorithm combining adapting classifiers for detection and adapting across category sets.  
- Paper is well-written with a clearly explained approach and well-described experimental protocol.  
- Approach is novel but not groundbreaking, building on existing adaptation ideas.  
- Significance is uncertain; results may be overtaken by more sophisticated future methods.  
- Main result trade-off: using fewer bounding boxes reduces mAP and there’s no objective way shown to judge if this trade-off is worthwhile.  
- Questionable long-term importance: unclear if bounding-box annotation cost is a critical barrier or solvable by allocating more resources.  
- Figure 3a needs clarification on which methods (besides theirs and R-CNN) use category labels for detection for fair comparison.  
- Figure 3b should highlight blue bars showing the method is roughly one-third between baseline and oracle, rather than the stated 78%.  
- Add a table or subsection clearly delineating differences between the proposed method and R-CNN.  
- Inconsistent naming: method referred to as both “DNN” and “DDA,” which can confuse readers.  
- Minor typo: line 431 ("classi?ers").  
- Overall: a reasonably good paper that merits acceptance to NIPS despite limitations.  
- Pros: novel combination of ideas, simple and understandable algorithm, clear experiments and results.  
- Cons: not highly groundbreaking, performance sacrifice vs fewer boxes, and may not address a critical long-term challenge.
