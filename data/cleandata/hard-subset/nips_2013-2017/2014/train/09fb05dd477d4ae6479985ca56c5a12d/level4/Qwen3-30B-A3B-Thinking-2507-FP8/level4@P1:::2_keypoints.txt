- Proposes LSDA to adapt image classifiers into object detectors via domain adaptation, enabling detection for categories without bounding-box annotations.  
- Addresses scalability gap: large classification datasets vs. limited detection datasets.  
- Method: fine-tune a pre-trained CNN on a small set of detection-labeled categories (B) and transfer adaptation to unlabeled categories (A).  
- Key innovations: explicit background modeling, transfer of category-invariant feature representations, and kNN-based category-specific adaptation.  
- Evaluated on ILSVRC-2013: 50% relative mAP improvement over classification-only baselines on 100 held-out categories.  
- Demonstrates scalability with a 7,604-category detector using mostly classification data.  
- Strength — Quality: technically sound, well-validated, and avoids weakly-supervised pitfalls; ablation isolates contributions.  
- Strength — Originality: novel adaptation to held-out categories without detection labels, extending R-CNN fine-tuning across categories.  
- Strength — Significance: tackles a key bottleneck for large-scale detection and releases models/code for impact.  
- Strength — Clarity: well-organized paper with clear figures and reproducible implementation details.  
- Weakness — kNN rationale: Section 3.2 needs stronger justification for using fc8 Euclidean distance to select kNN for category-specific adaptation.  
- Weakness — Validation protocol: the review’s criticism was incorrect — using val2 follows R-CNN precedent and is a reasonable choice.  
- Overall recommendation: technically rigorous, significant, and clear work; minor clarification on kNN would help — recommend acceptance.
