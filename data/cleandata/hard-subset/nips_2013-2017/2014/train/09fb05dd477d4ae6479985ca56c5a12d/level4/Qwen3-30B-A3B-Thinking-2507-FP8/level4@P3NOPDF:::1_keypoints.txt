- Problem addressed: adapting object classifiers for detection using category labels without bounding boxes.
- Method: conceptually simple approach that adapts classifiers directly for detection, avoiding box annotations.
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes and labels, test on 100 held-out categories with labels only.
- Main result: method closes about 35% of the performance gap between an oracle (full boxes) and a no-box baseline on held-out categories.
- Quality and clarity: well-written, technically sound, and reproducible.
- Originality: novel combination of classifier-to-detector adaptation and category-level domain adaptation; not a breakthrough but a meaningful contribution.
- Significance: modest—results are incremental, trade-offs in mAP with fewer boxes are unclear, and long-term impact is uncertain given evolving data-collection methods.
- Issue 1 (Figure 3a): unclear which methods use category labels in the comparison—needs explicit annotation for apples-to-apples comparison.
- Issue 2 (metrics): primary emphasis should be the ~35% gap closure; the 78% and 50% figures are misleading or lack proper baseline/context and should be corrected.
- Issue 3 (R-CNN comparison): differences with R-CNN are scattered—add a dedicated table/subsection comparing supervision, adaptation strategy, and cost; consider removing anecdotal Fig.6.
- Minor issues: inconsistent method naming ("DNN" vs "DDA"), a typo ("classi?ers" → "classifiers"), and small editorial fixes needed.
- Overall recommendation: solid, accept (NIPS) as a useful, incremental contribution pending minor clarifications and corrections.
