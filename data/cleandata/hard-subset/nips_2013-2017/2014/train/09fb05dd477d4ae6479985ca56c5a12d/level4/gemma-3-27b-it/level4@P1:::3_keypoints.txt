- Paper adapts a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to perform detection using few bounding-box-annotated classes by training region-based CNN components and combining them at test time.
- Method is logical, straightforward, and shows promising experimental results built on strong CNN features rather than a radical new architecture.
- Missing crucial baseline comparisons: (1) fine-tuning all CNN layers using full images as positives and other-class regions as negatives.
- Missing crucial baseline comparisons: (2) a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach that iteratively selects highest-scoring positive regions per image.
- Missing crucial baseline comparisons: (3) running the classifier at test time to predict a single centered bounding box (e.g., mean ImageNet box) to establish dataset difficulty.
- Unclear whether the proposed method outperforms these baselines—baseline 1 may be competitive and baseline 2 is a related learning paradigm.
- Experimental section is generally clear but needs more detail explaining methods in Table 1 and whether Equations 1/2 apply to specific CNN layer subsets.
- Sufficiently novel for consideration but approach is relatively simple and architecture-specific.
- Problem addressed is significant—scaling detection to many categories with limited boxes—and results (+50% relative mAP over raw classifier weights) are compelling.
- Public release of models and software increases practical value and reproducibility.
- Concerns that improvements may stem from CNN feature power rather than a fundamentally better weakly supervised detection method.
- Presentation issues and missing baselines hinder assessment of true contribution.
- Recommendation: borderline accept contingent on authors adding the suggested baseline comparisons and clarifying experimental details.
