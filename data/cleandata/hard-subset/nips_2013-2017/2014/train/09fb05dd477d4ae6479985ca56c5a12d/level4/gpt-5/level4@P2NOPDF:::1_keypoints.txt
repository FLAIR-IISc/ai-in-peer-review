- Tackles detection with scarce bounding boxes by adapting object classifiers to detection.
- Approach is deliberately simple, aiding clarity and reproducibility.
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, evaluate on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Method closes about 35% of the gap between a baseline and an oracle on held-out categories.
- Paper is clearly written and technically solid.
- Originality: novel combination of classifier-to-detector adaptation and cross-category transfer, but not transformative.
- Significance: a useful first-step method—novel but unlikely to be long-term state-of-the-art; trade-off between fewer boxes and lower mAP is real but not decisively evaluated.
- Side note: the problem’s long-term importance is uncertain as annotation costs may change with resources.
- Figure 3a: explicitly state which competing methods use category labels for detection to ensure fair comparisons.
- Figure 3b: emphasize the blue-bar result (≈1/3 gap closed) instead of the misleading 78% figure; report performance relative to both baseline and oracle and focus on held-out categories.
- Claims of a 50% improvement (line 425) are similarly misleading and should be clarified.
- Comparison to R-CNN: consolidate and emphasize differences in a dedicated subsection or table; consider removing anecdotal Figure 6.
- Minor fixes: clarify the method name (“DNN” vs “DDA”) and fix the typo on line 431 (“classi?ers”).
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance.
