- Proposes adapting object classifiers for detection to avoid collecting bounding boxes at scale.
- Algorithm is straightforward and simple, which is acceptable.
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes and labels, test on 100 categories with only labels.
- Results: method closes roughly 35% of the gap between baseline and oracle on left-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and of good quality.
- Originality: novel combination of adapting classifiers for detection and adapting across categories.
- Significance is unclear — approach may be surpassed and there’s an inherent mAP trade-off from using fewer bounding boxes.
- Questionable problem importance: label-collection constraints might be addressed by investing more resources.
- Request to clarify in Figure 3a which methods use category labels for fair comparison.
- Metric/reporting issue: highlight the ~1/3 gap closure as key and correct misleading 78% and 50% claims to account for left-out categories and baseline.
- Ask for a dedicated table/subsection clearly contrasting the proposed method with R-CNN; consider replacing anecdotal Figure 6.
- Minor issues: clarify method name (DNN or DDA) and fix typo on line 431. 
- Overall recommendation: a reasonable, novel paper that merits acceptance and presentation.
