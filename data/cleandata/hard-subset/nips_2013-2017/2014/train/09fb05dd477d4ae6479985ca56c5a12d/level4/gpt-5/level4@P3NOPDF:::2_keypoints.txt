- Summary: Proposes a simple adaptation mechanism to convert classification CNNs to detectors, achieving competitive ILSVRC2013 performance (4th); paper is clear and practical.

- Strength: Clear presentation and easy-to-follow methodology.

- Strength: Simple, implementable CNN adaptation that performs competitively.

- Strength: Strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark suggesting practical value.

- Main concern 1: No principled motivation for additive decomposition of detection weights (W_j^c + W_avg); needs statistical/optimization justification (e.g., MAP, multitask view).

- Main concern 1 (cont.): Offset parameter lacks interpretation tied to detection-specific factors (background separation, localization, bbox invariances).

- Main concern 1 (kNN): kNN variant is unexplained—unclear what neighbors represent, how similarity is measured, and why averaging helps; requires sensitivity analysis (k, metric, neighbor pool) and ablation studies.

- Main concern 1 (ablations): Request explicit ablations comparing W_j^c only, W_avg only, fixed sum, learned convex combination, other priors, and alternative parameter-sharing schemes (low-rank, L2 toward classification weights, multitask loss).

- Main concern 2: Evaluation protocol fairness—uses a small val subset instead of official test set, making cross-method comparisons potentially unfair and statistically weak.

- Main concern 2 (remedy): Ask for either official test-server results for all methods or matched evaluations on the same validation split, with confidence intervals or multiple seeds to show robustness.

- Main concern 2 (data): Clarify what data each method used for training/tuning and whether hyperparameters were picked on val2 to assess overfitting risk.

- Additional: Provide training details—which layers fine-tuned/frozen, learning schedule, regularization, data augmentation, bounding-box regression, and runtime/memory costs.

- Additional: Provide per-class APs, qualitative detections, error analysis (localization vs classification), and study across object sizes.

- Additional: Show generalization on another dataset (e.g., PASCAL VOC) or transfer setting to demonstrate broader applicability.

- Additional: Clarify how W_avg and kNN are computed (parameter, feature, or semantic space), impact on rare classes, and behavior for visually vs semantically related categories.

- Additional: Better position the work relative to transfer learning, multitask learning, and contemporary detection pipelines (e.g., R-CNN), clarifying what is novel.

- Overall assessment: Practical and competitive method but requires stronger principled justification, deeper empirical analysis, and fair/statistically sound evaluation to be convincing.

- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifying evaluation protocol and providing principled motivation plus extensive empirical/ablation analysis (including kNN variant).
