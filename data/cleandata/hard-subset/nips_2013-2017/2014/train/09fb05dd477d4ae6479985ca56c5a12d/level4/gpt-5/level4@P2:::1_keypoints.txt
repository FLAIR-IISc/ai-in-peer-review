- Paper proposes LSDA: learn a category-invariant CNN adaptation from classification→detection on m categories with boxes, then transfer to K−m categories using nearest-neighbor averaged weight deltas.  
- Implementation: fine-tune classification CNN into a detection CNN with a background class, adapt shared layers, and adjust category-specific output weights via neighbor averaging.  
- Evaluation: ILSVRC2013 with 100 categories with boxes and 100 held-out without; LSDA improves over classification-on-region-proposals and closes ~1/3 of the gap to a fully supervised oracle on held-out classes.  
- Scales to a 7.6K-category detector and includes a faster variant; authors release code/models.  
- Relation to prior work: closely related to R-CNN but differs by transferring adaptation from a subset of boxed categories to unseen categories; also relates to MIL and domain adaptation but differs in adapting task and label availability.  
- Technical soundness: method is simple, well-motivated, and ablations show adding a background class and adapting shared layers are important; category-specific averaging gives smaller gains.  
- Empirical support: experimental setup is reasonable and shows meaningful gains, but reporting needs clearer definition of improvement measures.  
- Completeness: paper should offer a more consolidated comparison to R-CNN and clearer statements of the supervision used by competing methods.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized with helpful figures and ablations; some claims and captions need to be more explicit for apples-to-apples comparisons.  
- Originality: combination of known techniques in a novel configuration for large-scale detection is interesting though conceptually incremental.  
- Significance: practical contribution enabling detectors for many categories without boxes; usefulness depends on application cost–benefit trade-offs and future methods.  
- Detailed issue — supervision clarity: clarify in Figure 3a which methods use bounding boxes for evaluated categories and whether image-level labels are used during detection training.  
- Detailed issue — reporting improvements: explicitly state that LSDA closes ~1/3 of the baseline→oracle gap on held-out classes and define denominators for percentage improvements (e.g., “X% improvement” relative to baseline mAP).  
- Detailed issue — R-CNN comparison: add a concise table/subsection enumerating differences from R-CNN (supervision, training time, background class, SVMs/bbox regression, performance) and consider moving qualitative Figure 6 to supplement.  
- Minor issues: ensure consistent method naming (LSDA vs DNN/DDA) and fix typos (e.g., “classi?ers”).  
- Pros: simple, well-motivated method with clear practical value; strong gains on held-out categories; scales to thousands of categories; code/models released.  
- Cons: accuracy lags fully supervised detectors; some claims and comparisons need more precise reporting; differences from R-CNN should be summarized more prominently.  
- Recommendation: Accept — practical, useful contribution with solid experiments and analysis, likely to be built upon by the community.
