- Paper proposes adapting a classification CNN (trained without boxes) to detection using three steps: global classification, region-based training for classes with boxes (plus background), then integrating background and adapting weights; test-time uses region proposals.
- Quality: technically sound with promising results, but evaluation is weakened by missing comparisons to key baselines.
- Missing baseline 1: fine-tune the entire CNN using full images as positives and regions from other classes as negatives.
- Missing baseline 2: Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) on images without boxes by iteratively selecting highest-scoring regions as positives.
- Missing baseline 3: train only for classification and at test time predict a centered bounding box (e.g., using mean ImageNet box) to measure dataset/task difficulty.
- It is unclear whether the proposed method outperforms these baselines; the approach may not clearly beat baseline 1 and MIL might be competitive or better.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but experiments (especially Table 1) lack detail about which methods/equations apply to which layers, hurting reproducibility.
- Originality: contribution is novel enough for acceptance but conceptually straightforward and potentially specific to certain CNN architectures.
- Significance: addresses an important problem (detection without many boxes) and shows meaningful results, but the absence of key baselines clouds the attribution of gains.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a scaling challenge for detection, shows promising ImageNet results, introduces a practical LSDA adaptation method, and releases models/software.
- Arguments against acceptance: critical baseline comparisons are missing, reducing confidence in the claimed contribution and leaving open the possibility gains are due to CNN feature power rather than the adaptation.
- Recommendation: accept conditionally—authors should include the suggested baselines and clarify experimental details to strengthen and validate the paper's claims.
