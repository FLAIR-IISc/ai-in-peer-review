The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks pre-trained on image classification tasks to object detection. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset demonstrates that the proposed method achieved 4th place in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN structure is both interesting and simple to implement. Overall, the paper is of good quality. However, there are two areas where the authors could strengthen their work.

First, the adaptation method presented – positioned as the main contribution – requires more robust justification. The underlying motivations are not clearly articulated in a principled manner. For example, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), the rationale for weighting the adapted detection task with a summation of W<sub>j</sub><sup>c</sup> and W<sub>avg</sub> is unclear. The meaning of this ‘offset parameter’ is not explained, and consequently, the reasons for the improved performance of the kNN variant remain obscure.

Second, the comparison with other methods is potentially flawed due to the use of a portion of the validation set rather than the held-out test set. While the authors acknowledge in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN exhibits similar performance on the validation set (val2), they also concede that the performance gain observed is not statistically significant due to the small size of this set.

In summary, the authors propose a framework to adapt deep neural networks from image classification to object detection, achieving competitive performance. However, the comparisons require clarification regarding the evaluation setup, and the proposed adaptation method would benefit from a more thorough explanation of its underlying principles.
