The paper presents a framework that adapts deep neural networks trained for image classification to object detection. On the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 benchmark, the method reportedly attains 4th place in the detection challenge. The presentation is clear and generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN architecture is both interesting and straightforward to implement. Overall, the paper is of good quality, but there are two main concerns.

First, the central adaptation method requires stronger justification. The underlying motivation is not articulated in a principled manner. For example, in Section 3.2 (p. 5, line 231), it is unclear why the adapted detection weights are expressed as a sum of W_j^c and an average offset W_avg. The meaning of this “offset parameter” is not explained, which also makes it unclear why the kNN variant should yield better results.

Second, the comparisons to other methods rely on a portion of the validation set rather than the test set, which undermines fairness. The authors note in a footnote (line 322) that R-CNN achieves similar performance on val2, but given the small size of this split, the reported gains are not statistically well supported.

In summary, the authors propose a framework to convert classification networks into detectors. The approach achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art models, but the evaluations are conducted under different settings, and the adaptation mechanism itself requires clearer explanation.
