- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky et al. classification CNN (trained without bounding boxes) to object detection using a limited set of classes with bounding box annotations.  
- Training pipeline: (1) train CNN for classification on all classes; (2) train a region-based CNN on bbox-annotated classes including a background class; (3) integrate the background class into the full CNN and adapt selected weights.  
- At test time the model scores multiple image regions per image for detection.  
- Approach is simple, logical, and yields reasonable detection results but largely leverages the strength of the pretrained Krizhevsky model rather than a fundamentally novel method.  
- Paper lacks comparison to baseline 1: fine-tuning all CNN layers using full images as positives and other-class regions/images as negatives.  
- Paper lacks comparison to baseline 2: same as baseline 1 but using Multiple Instance Learning (iteratively selecting the highest-scoring positive region per image).  
- Paper lacks comparison to baseline 3: train classification CNN and at test time predict a single bounding box (e.g., mean bounding box) to contextualize dataset difficulty.  
- Superiority of the proposed method over these baselines is unclear; it seems less justified than baseline 1 and likely worse than baseline 2.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well written, but the experiments section (Table 1) needs clarification about what each method entails and which layers Eq.1/Eq.2 are applied to.  
- Originality: sufficiently novel for acceptance but simple and tied to specific CNN architectures.  
- Significance: addressing large-scale detection without bbox labels is relevant and compelling, but results may mainly reflect improved CNN features rather than a superior weakly supervised detector training strategy due to missing critical baselines.
