The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method, ranking 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is well-presented and generally easy to follow, with an interesting and simple-to-implement CNN structure. Overall, the paper quality is good.

However, there are two concerns that the authors could address to improve the paper. Firstly, the adaptation method, which is the main contribution of this work, requires more rigorous justification. The underlying motivations and details are not presented in a principled manner. For instance, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), the reasoning behind using the summation of Wj^c and W{avg} as the weights for the adapted detection task is unclear. Furthermore, the physical meaning of the 'offset parameter' is not explained, making it unclear why the kNN variant is superior.

Secondly, the comparison with other methods is not entirely fair, as the authors used part of the validation set, but not the test set. The authors acknowledge in the footnote (Line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but the small size of this set makes the performance gain statistically insignificant.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should provide a more detailed explanation of their adaptation method and ensure a fair comparison with other methods by using the test set. The proposed method has comparable performance to the state-of-the-art model, but the comparisons are made under different settings, and the adaptation method requires better explanation.
