- Proposes LSDA: adapting object classifiers into detectors to scale detection to many categories using image-level labels.  
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with bounding boxes, test on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach and protocol.  
- Originality: combines two existing adaptation ideas (classifier→detector and cross-category adaptation); novel but not groundbreaking.  
- Significance: offers a new direction for large-scale detection, though long-term impact is uncertain.  
- Results trade off performance (mAP) due to using fewer bounding boxes, making the practical worth of the trade-off unclear.  
- Main quantitative result: LSDA closes ~35% of the gap between baseline and oracle on held-out categories (blue bars in Fig.3b).  
- Needs clearer, prominent comparison to R-CNN—recommend adding a table or subsection outlining differences.  
- Minor issues: typo on line 431 ("classi?ers") and inconsistent naming of the method as "DNN" or "DDA."  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear exposition, and promising mAP improvements on held-out categories.  
- Arguments against acceptance: apparent performance sacrifice, limited novelty, and insufficient comparison to closest competing method.  
- Recommendation: accept, pending minor fixes and a clearer R-CNN comparison.
