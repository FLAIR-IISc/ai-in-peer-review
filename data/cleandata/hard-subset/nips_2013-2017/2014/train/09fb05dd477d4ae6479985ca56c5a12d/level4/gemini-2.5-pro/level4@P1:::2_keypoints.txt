- Proposes LSDA: a framework to adapt image classifiers into object detectors to address scarcity of bounding-box annotations.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation, learning a generic classifier-to-detector transformation.  
- Learns the transformation on a subset of fully annotated categories and applies it to novel categories with only image-level labels.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection; shows large improvement over unadapted classifiers.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and easy to follow.  
- Method is significant and original, enabling scaling detection to thousands of categories without exhaustive bounding-box annotation.  
- Strong empirical result: ~+50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories.  
- Weakness 1: Adaptation model lacks principled justification — the additive offset model and k-NN averaging for new classes are not well motivated.  
- Weakness 2: Comparisons to state-of-the-art (e.g., R-CNN) are indirect and incomplete; lacks fair implementation of baselines and statistical significance testing on val2.  
- Summary: Valuable and promising approach with strong results, but needs better theoretical grounding for the adaptation and more rigorous, direct baseline comparisons.
