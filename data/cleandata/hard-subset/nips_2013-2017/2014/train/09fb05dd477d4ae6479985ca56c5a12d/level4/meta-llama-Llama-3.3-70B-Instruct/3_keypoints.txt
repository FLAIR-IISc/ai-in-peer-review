- Proposes a three-stage method to adapt a pre-trained Krizhevsky CNN for detection using few bounding-box-annotated classes: (1) full-image classification, (2) region-based training with a background class, (3) integrate background with partial weight adaptation; testing uses multiple regions per image.  
- Results are reasonable and promising, building on Krizhevsky et al.'s work, but the approach is not highly novel.  
- Method is straightforward, logical, and potentially useful to CV/ML researchers, especially for architectures like Krizhevsky’s.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparison to obvious baselines, leaving unclear whether gains come from the method or stronger CNN features.  
- Missing baseline 1: train/fine-tune on full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives, fine-tuning all CNN layers.  
- Missing baseline 2: a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach that iteratively selects the highest-scoring positive region per image.  
- Missing baseline 3: train/fine-tune for classification and during testing predict a single bounding box at the image center to gauge dataset/task difficulty.  
- Superiority over baseline 1 (and others) is not clearly demonstrated.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, but the experiments section needs more detail—especially explanations of Table 1 and how equations map to specific CNN layers.  
- Despite simplicity, the work has enough originality and significance to merit acceptance, though the absent baselines weaken the causal interpretation of the improvements.
