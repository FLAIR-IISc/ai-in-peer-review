This paper presents a method for using a Krizewski CNN network (without a bonding box layer) for object detection. This network has been trained for classification. It contains a bonded box layer for a small number of classes. During training, the method is as follows: 1) training a CNN for classification on images from all classes, 2) training a CNN on the region of images from classes marked with the bonded box layer, where a negative background region class is added, and 3) adding the background class to the complete CNN model while changing some CNN weights. During testing, a CNN is run on multiple image regions in a test image.

Quality: This is an interesting paper, and it is quite strong, providing reasonable results. These results are largely based on the success of Krizewski et al.'s model, which is nothing new; however, it will be valuable for researchers in computer vision and machine learning. The general method is simple, but reasonable. The tests are promising; however, comparisons with other baselines are missing:
1) Use the complete image as a positive example and use images and regions from other classes as negative examples. Train and fine-tune all layers of the CNN.
2) Do the same as above, but use an MIL method, where you repeatedly select the highest scoring positive region for each image from the set A (instead of the whole image).
3) Train and fine-tune the CNN for classification. During testing, run the classifier and predict a single bonded box in the center of the image (e.g., the average bonded box for ImageNet).

I am not sure whether the proposed method will give better results than these baselines. It is not clear to me whether the proposed method is more meaningful than baseline 1, and I think it is probably less meaningful than baseline 2. It would be important to include a third baseline to show the reader how easy/difficult the problem and dataset are.

Clarity: The paper is clear and well-written. The clarity of the test section could be improved, especially the explanation of each method in Table 1 should be detailed (does this mean applying equations 1 or 2 to a subset of CNN layers?).

Novelty: The paper is new enough to be accepted, although the basic method is simple and specific to a certain type of CNN network.

Significance: The problems and results are significant and valuable.

The paper has found good detection results in a large-scale dataset without a relevant problem and bonding box label, which is very important. At the same time, some clear baselines are missing in the paper, and it is very possible that the results are not due to the introduction of a good way to train the object detector from weak supervision, but rather based on improved CNN features.
