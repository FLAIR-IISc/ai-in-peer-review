- Paper tackles object detection by adapting object classifiers for detection to avoid needing bounding boxes.  
- Proposed approach is novel but builds on two existing ideas: classifier-to-detector adaptation and category-set adaptation.  
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes and labels, test on 100 left-out categories with only labels.  
- Results: method closes about 35% of the gap between an oracle and a baseline on left-out categories.  
- Paper is well-written and overall quality is good.  
- Contribution is interesting but not groundbreaking; results may be surpassed by future methods.  
- Main drawback: method sacrifices mAP by using fewer bounding boxes, with no objective way to judge the trade-off.  
- Unclear if the addressed problem is a critical long-term challenge since additional resources might solve it.  
- Reviewer requests clearer labeling in Figure 3a to show which methods use category labels for detection.  
- Authors should emphasize the main numeric result (≈1/3 gap closed) rather than relative improvement percentages (78%/50%).  
- Recommend adding a table or subsection explicitly contrasting the method with R-CNN as the closest alternative.  
- Minor fixes needed: typo on line 431 and consistent naming of the method ("DNN" vs "DDA").  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with revisions to address the issues above.
