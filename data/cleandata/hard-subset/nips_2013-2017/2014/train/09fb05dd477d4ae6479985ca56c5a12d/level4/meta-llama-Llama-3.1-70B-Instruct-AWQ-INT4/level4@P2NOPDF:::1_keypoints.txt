- Addresses object detection where bounding boxes are scarce by adapting object classifiers for detection, avoiding large-scale bounding-box annotation.
- Proposes a simple, straightforward adaptation algorithm (not complex, which is not a drawback).
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with labels+boxes, test on 100 held-out categories with labels only.
- Results: method bridges about 35% of the gap between an oracle and a baseline on the 100 left-out categories.
- Paper is well written and of good quality.
- Originality: combines two existing adaptation ideas into a novel and interesting approach, though not groundbreaking.
- Significance uncertain: promising but likely to be surpassed; trade-off in mAP from using fewer bounding boxes lacks an objective justification.
- Questionable importance of the challenge: collecting more bounding boxes might be a viable alternative given historical scalability of labeling efforts.
- Figure 3a needs to clearly state which methods use category labels for detection to enable fair comparisons.
- Figure 3b/results presentation: authors should acknowledge DNN is roughly one-third of the way between baseline and oracle and correct misleading 78% and 50% claims by properly accounting for baseline and left-out categories.
- Provide a dedicated table/subsection comparing the proposed approach to R-CNN (closest alternative); consider replacing anecdotal Figure 6.
- Minor issues: inconsistent naming ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").
