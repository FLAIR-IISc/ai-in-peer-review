This paper proposes a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The authors evaluate their method on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset and achieve a ranking of 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, with an interesting and simple-to-implement CNN structure. Overall, the quality of the paper is good.

However, there are two concerns that the authors could address to improve the paper. Firstly, the adaptation method proposed in this paper requires better justification. The underlying motivations and details are not presented in a principled fashion. For instance, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are a summation of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' is not explained, making it unclear why the kNN variant is superior.

Secondly, the comparison with other methods is not entirely fair, as the authors use part of the validation set instead of the test set. Although they mention in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, the small size of this set means that the performance gain is not statistically justified.

The authors propose a framework to adapt deep neural networks from image classification tasks to object detection tasks, achieving comparable performance to state-of-the-art models. However, the comparisons are made under different settings, and the proposed adaptation method requires better explanation to fully understand its contributions.
