- Presents a method to adapt a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to object detection with few bounding-box-annotated classes using three training steps and region-based testing.  
- Training pipeline: (1) train CNN for classification on all classes, (2) train CNN on image regions with an added background class, (3) add background class to full CNN and adapt some weights.  
- At test time, the CNN is applied to multiple image regions per test image.  
- Approach is simple, logical, and builds effectively on Krizhevsky et al.’s model, yielding reasonable results.  
- Not radically novel but valuable to computer vision and machine learning researchers.  
- Experiments are promising but lack comparisons to key baselines.  
- Suggested baseline 1: use full images as positives and images/regions from other classes as negatives, then fine-tune all CNN layers.  
- Suggested baseline 2: adopt a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach selecting the highest-scoring positive region per image iteratively.  
- Suggested baseline 3: train/fine-tune CNN for classification, then at test time predict a single center/mean bounding box (crucial and simple baseline).  
- Including these baselines, especially baseline 3, is crucial to convey dataset difficulty and method benefit.  
- It’s unclear whether the proposed method outperforms these baselines; it may be worse than MIL.  
- Paper is well-written overall, but the experiments section (Table 1) needs clearer explanations about which equations apply to which CNN layers.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance though the approach is simple and specific to certain CNNs.  
- Significance is high since decent large-scale detection was achieved without bbox labels, but gains may stem from improved CNN features rather than a superior weakly-supervised training method.
