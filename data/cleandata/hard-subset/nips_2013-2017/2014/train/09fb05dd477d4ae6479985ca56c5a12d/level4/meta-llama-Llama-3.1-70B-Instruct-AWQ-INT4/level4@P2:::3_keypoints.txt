- Proposes LSDA: adapting a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to perform detection using few classes with bounding boxes.  
- Training pipeline: (1) train CNN for classification on all classes, (2) fine-tune CNN on region proposals for bbox-trained classes with added background class, (3) add background class to full model and adapt weights.  
- At test time, run the adapted CNN on multiple image regions (object proposals) per image.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough background and explanation of the method.  
- Experimental results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection are promising, showing significant improvement over using raw classifier weights on proposals.  
- Concern that improvements may stem from strong CNN features rather than a novel weakly-supervised detector training method.  
- Paper lacks comparisons to several obvious baselines, especially other methods that leverage CNN features for detection.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance but approach is simple and specific to certain CNN architectures.  
- Significance is high: enables large-scale detection for many categories without bounding box labels.  
- Suggested improvements: add more baselines (including CNN-feature-based methods) and expand experimental details (clarify methods in Table 1).  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, promising empirical gains, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: possible reliance on existing CNN feature gains, missing baselines, and insufficient experimental detail.
