- Problem and proposal: Scales object detection to many categories with few bounding boxes by learning a transformation (LSDA) from classification to detection and transferring it to classes without box data.  
- Method details: Fine-tune an AlexNet pretrained on classification, update layers 1–7 and a background class with detection data for m categories, and adapt fc8 for remaining classes via nearest-neighbor delta averaging.  
- Test-time: Score region proposals per category and subtract background score.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN, relates to domain adaptation and weakly supervised detection but does not perform MIL on held-out classes.  
- Evaluation: Tested on ILSVRC2013 with 100/100 simulation and scaled to a 7.6K-class detector; reports a 50% relative mAP gain over using the classification network directly.  
- Main technical finding: Category-invariant feature adaptation (layers 1–7 + background) is the primary source of improvement; fc8 nearest-neighbor adaptation gives smaller gains.  
- Error analysis: Adaptation mainly reduces localization and background confusions.  
- Strengths: Technically sound, simple, logical pipeline; practical reduced training time versus full R-CNN; useful ablations and error analysis; public models/code and large-scale demo.  
- Missing baselines: No full-image supervision baseline that uses full-image positives and comprehensive negatives.  
- Missing baselines: No weakly supervised/MIL baseline for held-out classes to compare category-specific adaptation.  
- Missing baselines: No center-box baseline to quantify how much recognition vs localization contributes on this dataset.  
- Concern: Without these baselines, gains may be attributable to strong CNN features or preprocessing rather than the proposed adaptation.  
- Clarity issues: Ablation table (Table 1) lacks explicit definitions of which layers are fine-tuned, background inclusion, and fc8 adaptation settings (including k).  
- Clarifyments requested: Explicitly map “category-invariant” to layers 1–7 + background and “category-specific” to fc8 nearest-neighbor deltas; specify nearest-neighbor computation and sensitivity to k.  
- Originality: Straightforward but novel application of adapting classification-to-detection for classes without boxes; nearest-neighbor fc8 transfer is pragmatic.  
- Significance: Important and realistic problem with promising results; potentially impactful if it outperforms strong weakly supervised baselines.  
- Suggestions: Add the three missing baselines, report mAP vs k for fc8 adaptation, show per-class results and confusion matrices, analyze failure cases where transfer hurts, and discuss SVMs/bbox regression on adapted features.  
- Overall judgment: Solid, timely contribution with promising impact that would be substantially strengthened by adding the suggested baselines and clearer experimental descriptions.
