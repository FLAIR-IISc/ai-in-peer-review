- Tackles object detection by adapting classifiers to detectors, using image-level labels to reduce reliance on bounding-box annotations.  
- Training setup: 100 categories with labels and boxes; evaluation on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels.  
- Algorithm is straightforward and yields a reasonable improvement, closing ~35% of the gap between baseline and oracle on held-out categories.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and experiments are clearly defined.  
- Novelty lies in combining classifier-to-detector adaptation and category-level knowledge transfer; a useful contribution though not revolutionary.  
- Significance: promising approach for scaling detection to many categories, but practical value depends on the mAP vs annotation-cost trade-off and limits of image-level supervision.  
- Clarify Figure 3a: explicitly state which methods use category labels during detection beyond the proposed method and R-CNN.  
- Emphasize the DNN result that closes roughly 1/3 of the gap (Figure 3b) and avoid overemphasizing the 78% figure and the reported 50% improvement without clear baseline/oracle context.  
- Add a focused comparison (table/section) contrasting the proposed method with R-CNN, consolidating scattered distinctions; consider removing anecdotal examples (Figure 6) to make space.  
- Minor: ensure consistent method name usage (“DNN” vs “LSDA”) and fix typo on line 431 (“classi?ers”).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance for presentation at NIPS.
