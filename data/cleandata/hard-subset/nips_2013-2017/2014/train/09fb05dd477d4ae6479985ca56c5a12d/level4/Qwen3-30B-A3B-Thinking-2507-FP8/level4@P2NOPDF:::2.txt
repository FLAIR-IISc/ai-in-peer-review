

This paper proposes a framework for adapting pre-trained image classification CNNs to object detection tasks, demonstrating strong results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 (ranking 4th in the detection challenge). The paper is clearly written and well-structured, with a simple yet effective CNN adaptation approach that is straightforward to implement. Overall, the technical quality is good, though two key issues require clarification before publication.  

First, the core adaptation mechanism lacks sufficient theoretical justification. The paper states that detection task weights are derived as the sum of classification weights (W_j^c) and an average weight matrix (W_avg), but provides no principled motivation for this formulation. As noted in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), the physical interpretation of this "offset parameter" is unclear, making it difficult to understand why this specific combination improves performance. Furthermore, the rationale for why the kNN variant outperforms other configurations is not adequately explained, weakening the contribution’s novelty.  

Second, the comparison with state-of-the-art methods is compromised by the use of the validation set instead of the test set for evaluation. The authors acknowledge in Footnote 1 (Line 322) that RCNN achieves similar performance on the validation set, but due to its small size, this comparison lacks statistical significance. Using the validation set for performance benchmarking creates an unfair advantage for the proposed method, as the test set should be reserved exclusively for final evaluation to ensure objective comparison. The paper claims "comparable performance" to SOTA models, but these comparisons are made under different evaluation protocols (e.g., different test sets or configurations), undermining the validity of the claims.  

While the adaptation framework is promising and the results are competitive, the absence of rigorous justification for the methodological choices and the questionable evaluation protocol significantly weaken the paper’s impact. The authors should: (1) provide a principled derivation of the weight adaptation mechanism, including the physical meaning of the offset parameter and the kNN variant’s advantage; and (2) repeat all comparisons using the official test set to ensure a fair benchmark. Addressing these points would substantially strengthen the paper’s contribution and reproducibility.  

In summary, the work presents a practical adaptation strategy with strong empirical results, but the lack of methodological clarity and evaluation fairness prevents it from making a definitive contribution to the field. With the suggested revisions, this could be a valuable addition to object detection literature.
