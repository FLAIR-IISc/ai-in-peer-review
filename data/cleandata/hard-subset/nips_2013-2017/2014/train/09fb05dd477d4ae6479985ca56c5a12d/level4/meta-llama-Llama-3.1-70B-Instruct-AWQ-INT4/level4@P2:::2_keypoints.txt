- Proposes LSDA, an algorithm to transform image classifiers into object detectors.  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, showing ~50% relative mAP improvement over baseline classifier weights on proposals.  
- Paper is well-written; CNN structure is simple and easy to implement.  
- Clear explanation of LSDA components: category-invariant adaptation, category-specific adaptation, and detection pipeline.  
- Experiments are thorough and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Main concern: adaptation method lacks principled justification—unclear why adapted weights are W_j^c + W_avg and what the offset represents.  
- Unclear why the kNN variant outperforms alternatives; motivation and rationale are not explained.  
- Comparison issues: authors used part of validation (not test), making comparisons potentially unfair and statistically weak given small val2.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive but needs more detailed contrast with other adaptation methods (e.g., MIL).  
- Overall quality is good and the approach could enable detection for many categories without bounding-box labels.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address justification, comparison fairness, and related-work distinctions.  
- Pros: novel algorithm, thorough experiments, strong empirical gains.  
- Cons: weak theoretical justification for adaptation, fairness concerns in comparisons, and limited discussion of differences from existing adaptation methods.
