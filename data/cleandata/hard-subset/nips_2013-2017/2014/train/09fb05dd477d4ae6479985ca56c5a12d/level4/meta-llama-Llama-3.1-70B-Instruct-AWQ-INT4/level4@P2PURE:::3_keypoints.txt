- Proposes adapting a pre-trained Krizhevsky CNN for detection with few classes that have bounding-box annotations using a three-stage training pipeline (image-level classification, region-level training with background class, then integrating background and adapting weights).  
- At test time, detection is done by applying the CNN to multiple image regions.  
- Quality: approach is straightforward, logical, and achieves reasonable detection results building on Krizhevsky et al.'s model.  
- Experiments are promising but lack comparisons to important baselines, so it's unclear if gains come from the method or stronger CNN features.  
- Missing baseline 1: train/fine-tune all CNN layers using full images as positives and images/regions from other classes as negatives.  
- Missing baseline 2: a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach that iteratively selects the highest-scoring positive region per image.  
- Missing baseline 3: train/fine-tune CNN for classification and predict a single center bounding box at test time to measure dataset difficulty.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written overall but the experiments section (Table 1) needs clearer explanations, e.g., which equations apply to which subset of layers.  
- Originality: novel enough for acceptance despite being a simple, specific adaptation.  
- Significance: problem and results are relevant and valuable, but omission of obvious baselines weakens claims that the method (not just better features) improves weakly supervised detection.
