- Proposes LSDA to adapt a CNN trained for classification into an object detector by learning a transformation from a few fully-annotated categories to many weakly-labeled categories.  
- Training involves three stages: fine-tune CNN for classification, fine-tune on region proposals for bbox-annotated categories (adding a background class), then apply learned category-invariant and category-specific adaptations to remaining classes.  
- At test time the adapted network scores region proposals to produce detections.  
- Quality: paper is interesting, well-executed, and experimentally promising; contribution valuable for large-scale detection with limited annotation.  
- Missing baselines weaken claims: no comparison to a simple weakly-supervised baseline using full images as positives and other-class regions as negatives.  
- Missing baseline: no multiple-instance learning (MIL) comparison that iteratively selects highest-scoring region proposals for weakly-labeled classes.  
- Missing baseline: no trivial baseline that predicts a canonical/average bounding box for positive classifications to contextualize task difficulty.  
- Clarity: experiments section and ablation descriptions (e.g., "All Layers + BG", "k=10") are too brief and need clearer explanations of which components are included/excluded.  
- Originality: sufficiently novel—applying transfer/fine-tuning to transfer detection knowledge from fully- to weakly-supervised categories within one model.  
- Significance: addresses scaling detection to thousands of categories with scarce bboxes, but lack of baseline comparisons leaves open whether gains come from LSDA or simply strong CNN features.
