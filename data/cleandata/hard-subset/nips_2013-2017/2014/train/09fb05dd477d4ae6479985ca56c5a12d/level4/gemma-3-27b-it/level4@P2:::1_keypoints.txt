- Proposes LSDA, an algorithm to transfer image classifiers into object detectors to enable detection for categories without bounding-box annotations.
- Core idea: learn a transformation between classification and detection tasks and apply it to classifiers lacking box annotations.
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013: trained on 100 categories with boxes and tested on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels.
- Shows significant improvement over a classifier-weight baseline, recovering roughly one-third of the gap to an “oracle” detector trained with full annotations.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and explains methodology and experiments clearly.
- Novelty lies in applying transfer/domain adaptation ideas specifically to adapt classifiers into detectors at large scale.
- Related work (Multiple Instance Learning, domain adaptation) is appropriately discussed.
- Contribution is practically significant for scaling detection under low-annotation regimes, contingent on mAP vs annotation-cost tradeoffs.
- Results are promising but the long-term importance of reducing annotation cost remains an open question.
- Request to clarify Figure 3a about which methods use category labels during detection versus bounding-box annotations.
- Recommend reporting absolute mAP values for baseline, DNN, and oracle and clarifying the “50% improvement” claim and baselines used.
- Suggest adding a dedicated section/table comparing LSDA and R-CNN, highlighting LSDA’s ability to generalize without bounding boxes.
- Ask to disambiguate terminology where “DNN” and “LSDA” are used interchangeably.
- Minor typo to fix: line 431 ("classi?ers").
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, is novel and well-motivated, well-implemented, shows significant baseline improvement, and acknowledges limitations.
- Arguments against acceptance: improvements are not dramatic, long-term impact uncertain, and the performance vs annotation-cost trade-off is not fully explored.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; recommend acceptance to NIPS 2017.
