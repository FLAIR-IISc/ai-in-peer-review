- Problem: Addresses object detection when bounding boxes are scarce by adapting object classifiers to detectors.  
- Proposal: Introduces a simple, novel adaptation approach combining classifier-to-detector and cross-category adaptation.  
- Experimental setup: Train on 100 categories with labels+boxes, test on 100 left-out categories with only labels.  
- Results: Method bridges roughly 35% of the gap between an oracle and baseline on left-out categories; DNN is about one-third of the way from baseline to oracle.  
- Quality/Clarity: Paper is well-written and ideas are presented clearly.  
- Originality: Combines two existing adaptation ideas in a novel, interesting (but not groundbreaking) way.  
- Significance: Contribution is novel but long-term impact is uncertain; using fewer boxes reduces mAP and the trade-off’s value is debatable.  
- Issue 1: Clarify in Figure 3a which methods (besides the authors’ and R-CNN) use category labels to aid detection.  
- Issue 2: Correct performance claims (78% and 50% are misleading); metrics should be computed only on left-out categories and relative to baseline and oracle.  
- Issue 3: Provide a clear table/subsection comparing the proposed approach and R-CNN (replace or supplement Figure 6 anecdotal examples).  
- Minor issues: Inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: Paper is solid and should be accepted for presentation.
