- Proposes LSDA: learn a general transformation to convert image classifiers into object detectors to address scarcity of bounding-box labels.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation, learning the transformation from categories with both classification and detection data and applying it to categories without boxes.  
- Two adaptation steps: category-invariant fine-tuning of shared CNN features for detection, and category-specific adjustment of the final layer.  
- For held-out categories, category-specific adaptation is approximated by averaging weight deltas from k-nearest neighbors in classifier weight space.  
- Builds on R-CNN but differs by not requiring bounding boxes for every target category; contribution is learning/transferring the adaptation process.  
- Strong empirical validation on ILSVRC 2013: ~50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories.  
- Well-executed ablation study and error analysis show reduced localization and background confusion errors.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and reproducible with sufficient experimental detail.  
- Public release of 7.6K detector models and code is a valuable community contribution.  
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation is heuristic (averaging deltas) and lacks principled theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses a validation split, making direct comparison to published test-set results (e.g., R-CNN) less straightforward.  
- Suggestion: quantify the trade-off from omitting SVM and bbox regression and report R-CNN under the same val2 protocol for a fairer comparison.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant scaling problem, offers a novel effective framework, strong results, and will likely spur follow-up work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic transfer mechanism needs stronger grounding and experimental comparisons could be more directly quantified.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, impactful paper and recommended for acceptance.
