- Paper proposes adapting an AlexNet-style classifier to detection with bounding boxes for only a subset of classes via a three-stage training pipeline (classification CNN, region-level CNN with background class, then background incorporation and partial weight adaptation).  
- At test time the adapted CNN is applied to multiple regions per image for detection.  
- Approach is logical, straightforward, yields reasonable results, and is potentially useful to vision/ML communities.  
- Reported gains likely depend heavily on the strong Krizhevsky et al. CNN rather than a fundamentally new architecture.  
- Missing key baselines: Baseline 1—treat whole labeled images as positives and other images/regions as negatives, fine-tune all layers end-to-end.  
- Missing Baseline 2—same as Baseline 1 but with multiple-instance learning (iteratively select highest-scoring region per positive image).  
- Missing Baseline 3—train/fine-tune for classification only and at test time predict a single fixed bounding box (e.g., dataset mean box) to calibrate difficulty.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced the method outperforms Baseline 1 and suspects it may be weaker than the MIL-based Baseline 2; inclusion of these baselines is needed.  
- Paper is generally clear, but experimental section lacks precise descriptions of which CNN layers are adapted and which equations (Eq.1 vs Eq.2) apply to which layers/variants.  
- Contribution is novel enough for acceptance but simple and architecture-specific; significance is high but weakened by absence of the obvious baselines to show the method’s advantage.
