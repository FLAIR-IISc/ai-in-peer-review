The authors present a framework for adapting deep neural networks pre-trained for image classification to the task of object detection, achieving a strong 4th place ranking on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 object detection challenge. The paper is well-written and generally accessible, and the proposed CNN architecture is both compelling and straightforward to implement. Overall, this is a solid contribution. However, two areas would benefit from further development.

First, the core adaptation method requires stronger justification. The underlying rationale for key design choices isn’t fully articulated in a principled manner. Specifically, the summation of weights *Wj^c* and *W{avg}* in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231) lacks a clear explanation of its purpose – the physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' remains unclear. Consequently, the advantage of the kNN variant isn’t readily apparent.

Second, the comparative evaluation is potentially flawed. The authors utilized a portion of the validation set for comparison, rather than the standard test set, introducing a potential bias. While the authors acknowledge similar performance of RCNN on the *val2* set (Footnote, Line 322), the small size of this set limits the statistical significance of any observed gains. 

In summary, the authors demonstrate a promising framework with performance competitive with state-of-the-art approaches. However, a more thorough explanation of the adaptation method and a fairer comparative evaluation using the standard test set would significantly strengthen the paper.
