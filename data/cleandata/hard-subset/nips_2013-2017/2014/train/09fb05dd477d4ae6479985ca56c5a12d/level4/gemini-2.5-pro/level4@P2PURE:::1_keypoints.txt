- Problem: Scaling object detection when bounding-box annotations are costly; method adapts object classifiers to detection using image-level labels.  
- Method: Simple, straightforward algorithm to adapt classifiers for detection across categories.  
- Experimental setup: Train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 distinct categories with only image-level labels.  
- Main result: Promising performance — closes roughly 35% of the gap between baseline and oracle on novel categories.  
- Quality and clarity: Paper is well-written and high quality.  
- Originality: Novel combination of two existing adaptation ideas (classifier→detector and known→new-category adaptation); interesting but not groundbreaking.  
- Significance concerns: Long-term impact unclear—method likely to be outperformed by future approaches; trade-off due to fewer boxes reduces mAP and no objective metric is given to evaluate that trade-off.  
- Broader concern: Questionable whether expensive bounding-box annotation is a fundamental long-term problem given possible large-scale data collection investments.  
- Fig. 3a issue: Unclear which competing methods (besides R-CNN and proposed) use category labels — comparisons need clarification.  
- Results emphasis: Authors should highlight Fig. 3b (blue bars) showing ~1/3 gap closure; the 78% and 50% figures are misleading without baseline/oracle context and focus on novel categories.  
- R-CNN comparison: R-CNN is the closest alternative — paper should include a dedicated table/subsection explicitly detailing differences (replace Fig. 6 anecdotal examples with this).  
- Minor issues: Inconsistent use of "DNN" vs "DDA" for the method; typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").  
- Recommendation: Solid, novel paper deserving acceptance and interest from the NIPS community, though long-term impact remains uncertain.
