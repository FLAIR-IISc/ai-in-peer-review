- Problem: tackles large-scale object detection where bounding-box annotations are scarce compared to abundant image-level labels.  
- Method: adapts image classifiers into detectors with a deliberately simple and direct algorithm.  
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+image labels and evaluate on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels.  
- Main result: method closes roughly 35% of the gap between a naïve baseline and an oracle on the held-out categories.  
- Quality/clarity: paper is solid and clearly written.  
- Originality: novel combination of classifier-to-detector adaptation and cross-category transfer; interesting but not a major breakthrough.  
- Significance: modest contribution; results likely to be surpassed and long-term impact unclear due to possible mitigation of annotation bottlenecks.  
- Key concern: no straightforward metric to decide if the accuracy–annotation trade-off (mAP loss vs fewer boxes) is justified.  
- Figure 3a: need explicit statement of which methods (besides the proposed approach and R-CNN) use category labels to aid detection for apples-to-apples comparison.  
- Figure 3b / metrics: emphasize the blue-bar takeaway (proposed method is ~1/3 of the way from baseline to oracle); recompute/report the 78% and claimed 50% figures correctly (only on held-out categories and relative to baseline).  
- R-CNN comparison: add a dedicated table or subsection clearly enumerating differences from R-CNN; consider removing Figure 6 to make room.  
- Minor issues: clarify whether the method is called “DNN” or “DDA” and fix the typo on line 431 (“classi?ers”).  
- Recommendation: accept so the community can see this promising first step.
