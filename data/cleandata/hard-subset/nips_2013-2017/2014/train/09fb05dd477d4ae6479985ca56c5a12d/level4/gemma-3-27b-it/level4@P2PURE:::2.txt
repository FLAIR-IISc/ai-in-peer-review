The authors present a framework for adapting deep neural networks pre-trained on image classification to the task of object detection. Their method achieves strong performance, ranking 4th on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 object detection challenge. The paper is clearly written and easy to understand, and the proposed CNN structure is both interesting and straightforward to implement. Overall, this is a solid contribution. However, I have two primary concerns.

First, the adaptation method, presented as the main contribution, lacks sufficient justification. The underlying motivations are not clearly articulated in a principled manner. For instance, Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231) does not explain why the weights for the adapted detection task are calculated as a summation of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical interpretation of this ‘offset parameter’ remains unclear, and consequently, the rationale for the superior performance of the kNN variant is also unclear.

Second, the comparison with other methods is potentially flawed, as it utilizes a portion of the validation set rather than the standard test set, creating an uneven comparison. While the authors acknowledge in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN exhibits similar performance on the val2 set, they also concede that the small size of this set prevents statistically significant conclusions regarding performance gains. 

In summary, the authors propose a promising framework with competitive performance, but the proposed adaptation method requires more thorough explanation and the comparative evaluations should be revisited to ensure fairness.
