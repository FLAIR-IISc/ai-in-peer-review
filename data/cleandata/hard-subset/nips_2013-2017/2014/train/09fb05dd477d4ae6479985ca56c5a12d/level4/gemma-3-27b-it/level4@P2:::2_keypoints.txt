- Proposes LSDA to adapt image-classification CNNs for object detection by leveraging image-level labels.
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge and ranks 4th overall.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents a relatively simple, adoptable CNN-based method.
- Main concern: core adaptation method lacks strong justification and explanation of design choices.
- Specific unexplained choice: the summation of weights W_c^j and W_avg and the meaning of the "offset parameter."
- Specific unexplained choice: rationale for using a k-nearest neighbors approach to determine W_avg.
- Main concern: evaluation uses only part of the validation set (val2) rather than the standard test set, weakening comparisons.
- Small size of val2 undermines statistical significance of observed gains and comparisons to other methods.
- Recommendation to provide a more principled discussion of the adaptation design choices to strengthen the contribution.
- Recommendation to evaluate on the standard test set (or larger validation) for a more robust, fair comparison.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, addresses scaling detection with limited bounding boxes, simple method, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation/explanation of method, limited evaluation setup, and unclear statistical significance.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution rated as weak accept; addressing justification and evaluation concerns could make it a strong accept.
