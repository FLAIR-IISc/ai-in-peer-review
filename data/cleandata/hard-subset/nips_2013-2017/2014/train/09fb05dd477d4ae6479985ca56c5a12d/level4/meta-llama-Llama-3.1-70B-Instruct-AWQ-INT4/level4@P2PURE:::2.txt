The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection tasks. Their evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset yields a 4th-place ranking in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, with an interesting and straightforward CNN structure. Overall, the paper's quality is good. However, there are two areas that could be improved.

Firstly, the adaptation method, which is the main contribution of this work, requires more rigorous justification. The underlying motivations are not presented in a systematic manner. For instance, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are a summation of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' is not explained, making it unclear why the kNN variant is superior.

Secondly, when comparing with other methods, the authors use part of the validation set, rather than the test set, which makes the comparison unfair. Although the authors mention in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, the small size of this set means that the performance gain is not statistically significant.

The authors' proposed framework has comparable performance to the state-of-the-art model, but the comparisons are made under different settings. Furthermore, the proposed adaptation method requires more thorough explanation to fully understand its effectiveness.
