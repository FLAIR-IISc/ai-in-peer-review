This paper proposes a novel approach to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The authors introduce Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), an algorithm that learns to transform an image classifier into an object detector. The proposed method is evaluated on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, where it achieves a 50% relative improvement in mean average precision (mAP) over a baseline of using raw classifier weights on object proposal regions.

The paper is well-written, and the proposed CNN structure is interesting and simple to implement. The authors provide a clear explanation of the LSDA algorithm and its components, including category invariant adaptation, category specific adaptation, and detection with LSDA. The experiments are thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are a few concerns that the authors could address to improve the paper. Firstly, the adaptation method proposed in this paper needs better justification. The details of the underlying motivations are not presented in a principled fashion. For example, in Section 3.2, it is not clear why the weights for the adapted detection task are a summation of Wj^c and the W{avg}. The physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' is not explained. As a consequence, it is also not clear why the kNN variant is better.

Secondly, in comparing with other methods, the authors used part of the validation set, but not the test set, making the comparison not entirely fair. The authors mention in footnote 1 that R-CNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but due to the small size of this set, the performance gain is not statistically justified.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing methods for object detection and domain adaptation. However, it would be beneficial to discuss the differences between the proposed LSDA algorithm and other adaptation methods, such as Multiple Instance Learning (MIL), in more detail.

Overall, the quality of the paper is good, and the proposed method has the potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories that lack bounding box annotations. However, addressing the concerns mentioned above would strengthen the paper and provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The proposed LSDA algorithm is novel and has the potential to enable detection for a large number of categories.
* The paper is well-written, and the experiments are thorough.
* The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The adaptation method proposed in this paper needs better justification.
* The comparison with other methods is not entirely fair.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of related work and the differences between the proposed LSDA algorithm and other adaptation methods.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the concerns mentioned above to strengthen the paper and provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.
