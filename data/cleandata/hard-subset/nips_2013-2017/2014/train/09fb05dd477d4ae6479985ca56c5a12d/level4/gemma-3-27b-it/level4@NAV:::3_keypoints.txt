- Proposes adapting a pre-trained Krizhevsky CNN for object detection with limited bounding-box classes using a three-step training procedure and multi-region testing.  
- Approach is interesting and yields promising results but largely builds on the strong Krizhevsky model rather than introducing a fundamentally novel idea.  
- Methodology is straightforward and logically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparison to a baseline that fine-tunes the full CNN using full images as positives and other-class regions as negatives.  
- Evaluation should include a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) baseline that iteratively selects the highest-scoring positive region per image.  
- Evaluation should include a naive baseline that trains only for classification and predicts a single centered bounding box (e.g., mean ImageNet box) to assess dataset difficulty.  
- The advantage over baseline 1 is unclear and the paper’s motivation appears weaker than for baseline 2; baseline 3 is crucial to contextualize results.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, but the experiments section (Table 1) needs clearer explanations, especially which CNN layers equations 1 or 2 apply to.  
- The work shows sufficient novelty for acceptance despite simplicity and some architecture-specific applicability.  
- Results are significant and valuable—achieving reasonable detection on a large dataset with limited bounding boxes is compelling—but missing baselines leave open whether gains stem from features rather than the proposed training method.
