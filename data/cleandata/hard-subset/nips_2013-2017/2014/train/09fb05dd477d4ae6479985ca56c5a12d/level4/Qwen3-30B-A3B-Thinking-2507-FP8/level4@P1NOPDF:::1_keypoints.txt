- Addresses object detection by adapting object classifiers for detection to avoid needing bounding boxes at scale.  
- Experiments: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 held-out categories with labels only.  
- Results: method bridges ~35% of the gap between baseline and oracle on held-out categories.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Novelty: combines classifier-to-detector and cross-category adaptation; interesting but not groundbreaking.  
- Limitation: method trades off mAP by using fewer bounding boxes and lacks an objective metric to justify this trade-off.  
- Long-term significance is uncertain because dataset scaling could diminish the core problem.  
- Major concern: Figure 3a does not indicate which methods use category labels for detection—needs explicit clarification.  
- Major concern: Figure 3b and claims (e.g., 78%, 50%) are misleading; should report gap-bridging on held-out categories relative to baseline and oracle.  
- Major concern: comparison to R-CNN is scattered; a dedicated table/subsection is needed and Figure 6 could be removed.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent method naming ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo ("classi?ers").  
- Overall verdict: strong, elegant paper that merits acceptance despite limited/uncertain long-term impact.
