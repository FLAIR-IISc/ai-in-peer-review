- Tackles object detection by adapting object classifiers for detection to scale bounding-box generation to label availability.
- Proposes a straightforward adaptation algorithm (DNN/DDA) to convert classifiers into detectors.
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes and labels, test on 100 categories with labels only.
- Results: method bridges ~35% of the gap between baseline and ideal on the 100 test categories; reported 78% figure is misleading.
- Paper is well written and of good quality.
- Originality: novel combination of adapting classifiers for detection and cross-category adaptation, but not revolutionary.
- Significance: approach is interesting, but long-term impact is uncertain as more sophisticated methods may outperform it.
- Major concern: trade-off in mAP from using fewer boxes lacks an objective measure to justify the compromise.
- Questionable long-term importance: bounding-box scarcity might be solved by allocating more resources, reducing the problem’s urgency.
- Clarification needed: Figure 3a should indicate which methods use category labels to aid detection for fair comparison.
- Clarification needed: authors should emphasize that the blue bars (~one-third gap) are the key result and correct the misleading 78% claim (should consider left-out categories and baseline-adjusted metric).
- Suggestion: add a dedicated table/subsection explicitly outlining differences between the proposed method and R-CNN (replace anecdotal Figure 6).
- Minor issues: inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").
- Overall recommendation: paper is reasonably novel and deserves acceptance and presentation to the NIPS community.
