

The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge demonstrates that their method achieves 4th place. The paper is clearly presented and generally accessible, with an interesting and straightforward CNN adaptation structure. Overall, the quality is good. However, two concerns require attention.  

First, the adaptation method constitutes the primary contribution but lacks sufficient justification. The underlying motivations are not presented in a principled manner. Specifically, in Section 3.2 (p.5, line 231), it is unclear why the adapted detection weights are defined as the sum of $W_j^c$ and $W_{\text{avg}}$, nor is the physical meaning of this "offset parameter" explained. Consequently, the rationale for why the k-NN variant outperforms other approaches remains unconvincing.  

Second, the comparison with baseline methods uses part of the validation set rather than the test set, compromising the fairness of the evaluation. While the authors note in footnote 3 (line 322) that RCNN achieves similar performance on the validation set, the small size of this subset prevents statistically significant conclusions about performance gains.  

The proposed adaptation method achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art models, but the comparisons are conducted under non-identical settings. The adaptation mechanism itself requires more rigorous theoretical explanation to support its design choices.
