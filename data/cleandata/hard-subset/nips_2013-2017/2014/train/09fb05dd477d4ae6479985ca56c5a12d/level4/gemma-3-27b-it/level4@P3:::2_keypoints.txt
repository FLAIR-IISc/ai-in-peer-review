- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from classifiers to detectors to use image-level labels for large-scale detection.  
- Applies the learned classifier→detector transform to categories without bounding-box annotations.  
- Demonstrated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013, achieving 4th place and detectors for ~7,600 categories.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and appropriate placement in prior work (domain adaptation, weak supervision).  

Strengths:
- Novel approach: classifier-to-detector transformation is a new contribution.  
- Scalable: can produce detectors for thousands of categories using image-level labels.  
- Strong empirical performance: 4th place and ~50% relative mAP improvement over raw classifier-weight baseline.  
- Reproducibility: authors release models and code.  
- Clear presentation and helpful figures.  

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical justification for the adaptation method (weight summation and δ_B) is weak and unclear.  
- Rationale for using k-NN averaging to adapt categories without bboxes is not well motivated.  
- Evaluation compares on a validation split rather than the standard test set, weakening baseline comparisons.  
- Ablation study lacks depth (e.g., impact of different CNN backbones not explored).  
- Error analysis is not specific enough; frequencies of error types (Loc, BG, Oth) are not quantified.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses an important scalability problem, is novel, shows promising results, and shares code/models.  

Arguments for rejection:
- Insufficient theoretical grounding, non-standard evaluation split, and relatively shallow ablation raise robustness concerns.  

Overall recommendation:
- Solid contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions.  

Requested revisions:
- Provide deeper explanation/theoretical motivation for the weight summation in Section 3.2.  
- If possible, report results on the standard test set for firmer comparisons.  
- Expand the ablation study with more granular component analyses.
