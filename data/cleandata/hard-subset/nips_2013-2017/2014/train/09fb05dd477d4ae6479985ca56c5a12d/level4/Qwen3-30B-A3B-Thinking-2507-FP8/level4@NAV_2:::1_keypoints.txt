- Paper proposes LSDA to adapt image classifiers into object detectors using bounding boxes for only a subset of categories, enabling detection for many categories; evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 with a reported 50% relative mAP improvement over a classification-only baseline on held-out categories.

- Quality/clarity: high-quality, well-written, logically organized, with sufficient experimental detail for reproducibility.

- Originality: novel combination of classifier-to-detector adaptation and cross-category transfer; builds on R-CNN and domain adaptation but the specific large-scale application is new.

- Significance: practical approach that reduces the annotation bottleneck and could enable detection for tens of thousands of categories, though performance lags full detection training and long-term impact depends on practical utility.

- Issue: Figure 3a needs clarification on which methods (besides LSDA and R-CNN) use category labels for detection; comparison of methods’ use of classification vs detection data is unclear.

- Issue: Main result framing is misleading — the gap reduction between baseline and oracle on held-out categories is 35%, not 78%; the 50% relative improvement claim should be recalibrated to reflect baseline performance on held-out categories.

- Issue: Provide a dedicated comparison table or subsection contrasting LSDA and R-CNN to clarify novelty and advantages; consider removing anecdotal Figure 6 to focus on quantitative results.

- Minor: Terminology confusingly uses “DNN” for the classification-only baseline despite the core method also using deep nets; clarify naming.

- Minor: Typo at line 431 ("classi?ers" should be "classifiers").
