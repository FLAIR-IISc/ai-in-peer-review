- Paper proposes adapting object classifiers to detection to avoid needing bounding boxes at scale.  
- Approach is simple and straightforward; simplicity is not a drawback.  
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, evaluate on 100 held-out categories with only labels.  
- Results: method closes about 35% of the gap between the baseline and an oracle on the held-out categories.  
- Paper quality and clarity: solid and clearly written.  
- Originality: novel combination of two established adaptation ideas (classifier→detector and cross-category adaptation), but not ground-breaking.  
- Significance: contribution is the method itself; long-term impact uncertain since more sophisticated methods will likely surpass it.  
- Limitation: using fewer bounding boxes reduces mAP and there is no objective way to judge whether this trade-off is worthwhile.  
- Broader concern: unclear whether reducing box annotation is a long-term priority given potential increases in labeling resources.  
- Issue: Figure 3a needs clarification on which methods (besides the proposed method and R-CNN) use category labels to aid detection for fair comparisons.  
- Issue: Figure 3b’s blue-bar result (about one-third of the way from baseline to oracle) is a key finding and should be emphasized instead of the 78% figure; evaluation should focus on held-out categories and performance relative to the baseline.  
- Issue: The claimed 50% improvement is similarly misleading and should be re-evaluated.  
- Request: add a table or subsection clearly delineating differences from R-CNN (suggest removing Figure 6 to make space).  
- Minor issues: inconsistent naming of the method (“DNN” vs “DDA”) and a typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: paper merits acceptance and will interest the NIPS community.
