- Proposal: adapt a Krizhevsky-style CNN from image-level classification to detection via (1) full-image training, (2) region training with a background class, and (3) adding background and adapting a subset of weights; test by applying the CNN to multiple image regions.

- Quality: interesting and reasonably convincing results, but gains largely leverage the strong preexisting CNN rather than introducing radically new methods; approach is simple and logical.

- Missing baseline 1: use full images as positives and images/regions from other classes as negatives, fine-tune all CNN layers.

- Missing baseline 2: MIL approach that iteratively selects the highest-scoring positive region per image and fine-tunes.

- Missing baseline 3: train/fine-tune the CNN for classification and at test-time predict a single bounding box (e.g., image-center or mean bbox) to calibrate dataset difficulty.

- Concern: unclear if the proposed approach outperforms these baselines; likely inferior to MIL and possibly not better than baseline 1.

- Clarity: paper is generally clear but the experiments section should better explain what each method in Table 1 entails (which equations/layers are applied).

- Originality: sufficiently novel for acceptance but straightforward and tailored to specific CNN architectures.

- Significance: results are valuable—showing decent detection without bounding-box labels on a large dataset—but omission of obvious baselines leaves open that improvements may stem from stronger CNN features rather than a superior weakly-supervised detection method.
