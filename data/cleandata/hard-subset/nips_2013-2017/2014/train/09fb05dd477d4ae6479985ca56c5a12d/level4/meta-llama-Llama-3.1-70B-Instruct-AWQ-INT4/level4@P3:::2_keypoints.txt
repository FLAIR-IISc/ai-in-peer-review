- Proposes LSDA framework to adapt image-classification CNNs into object detectors.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 with ~50% relative mAP improvement over baseline.
- Paper is well-written and presents the proposed CNN structure clearly.
- Evaluation is thorough with detailed analysis of each algorithm component.
- Includes a large-scale detection experiment showing scalability to many categories.
- Adaptation method lacks clear justification; underlying motivations and principles are not well explained.
- Equation 1 and the physical meaning of the 'offset parameter' are not clearly motivated.
- Choice of k‑NN variant is insufficiently justified and alternatives are not compared.
- Comparison with other methods is potentially unfair because training/evaluation used parts of the validation set rather than the test set.
- Small val2 set makes reported performance gains statistically questionable.
- Should compare against state-of-the-art models on the test set for a more convincing evaluation.
- Originality: novel application of domain adaptation to transfer classifiers into detectors.
- Significance: potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories and achieve strong performance on a challenging dataset.
- Needs a more detailed discussion of related work in domain adaptation and object detection.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing explanations of the method and providing fairer comparisons to SOTA.
