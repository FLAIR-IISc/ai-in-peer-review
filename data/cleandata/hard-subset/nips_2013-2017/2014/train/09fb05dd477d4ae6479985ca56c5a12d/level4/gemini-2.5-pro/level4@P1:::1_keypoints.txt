- Addresses large-scale detection annotation gap by adapting pre-trained classifiers into detectors (LSDA) using a transformation learned from categories with bounding boxes.  
- LSDA: learn classifier→detector transformation on 100 fully supervised categories and apply to 100 held-out categories with only image labels.  
- Experimental result: method closes ≈36% of the performance gap between a classification-only baseline and an oracle with full bounding-box supervision.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound; experimental setup appropriately tests the hypothesis.  
- Idea is a novel combination of existing concepts but is incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Significance: useful proof-of-concept showing a trade-off between mAP and reduced annotation, though long-term impact is uncertain.  
- Raises question whether bounding-box scarcity is a lasting problem given potential large-scale data collection.  
- Major issue 1: clarify which methods in Figure 3a use image-level labels to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Major issue 2: reframe results to report fraction of gap closed (≈36%) rather than percent relative gain over a low baseline.  
- Major issue 3: explicitly compare LSDA to R-CNN (dedicated subsection/table on data needs, training stages, and trade-offs); consider moving anecdotal examples to appendix.  
- Minor issues: ensure consistent naming of LSDA throughout and fix typos (e.g., “classiﬁers” on line 431).  
- Recommendation: solid contribution and recommend acceptance as a valuable early exploration of classifier-to-detector adaptation.
