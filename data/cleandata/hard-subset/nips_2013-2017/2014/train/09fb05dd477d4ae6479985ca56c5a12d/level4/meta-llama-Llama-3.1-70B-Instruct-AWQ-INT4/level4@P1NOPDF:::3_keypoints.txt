- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky CNN (trained for classification) to detection using three training steps (classification on all classes; region-based training with bbox-labeled classes + background; incorporate background and adapt weights) and testing by applying the CNN to multiple image regions.  
- Results are compelling and build effectively on Krizhevsky et al.'s model, though the approach is not radically new.  
- Experiments are promising but lack comparisons to several obvious baselines, weakening the empirical claims.  
- Suggested baselines: (1) full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives with full-layer fine-tuning; (2) same but using Multiple Instance Learning to pick highest-scoring regions; (3) classifier run at test time to predict a single central/mean bounding box for context.  
- Experiments section needs clearer explanations (e.g., Table 1) about which equations are applied to which subset of CNN layers.  
- Originality: approach is simple and specific to certain CNNs but sufficiently novel for acceptance.  
- Significance: addressing detection with limited bbox labels on a large-scale dataset is valuable and the positive results are compelling.  
- Concern: omitting obvious baselines leaves open that improvements may stem from better CNN features rather than the proposed weakly-supervised detection training.
