- Problem: Adapt object classifiers for detection to avoid needing bounding boxes at the scale of category labels.  
- Method: Proposes a relatively simple algorithm that adapts classifiers into detectors.  
- Experimental setup: Train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 left-out categories with labels only.  
- Main result: Method closes ~35% of the gap between an oracle and a baseline on the left-out categories.  
- Misleading claims: Reported 78% (and 50%) figures are incorrect—evaluation should focus on left-out categories and account for the baseline.  
- Quality: Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Originality: Combines known ideas (classifier→detector adaptation and category transfer); novel and interesting but not groundbreaking.  
- Significance: Potentially useful but long-term impact unclear; using fewer boxes reduces mAP and there’s no objective way to judge the trade-off.  
- Fig. 3a issue: Clarify which methods (besides the authors’ method and R-CNN) use category labels to aid detection for fair comparison.  
- Fig. 3b issue: Emphasize the blue-bar result (DNN ≈ one-third between baseline and oracle) as the main metric rather than the 78% number.  
- Comparison to R-CNN: Add a dedicated table/subsection clearly outlining differences to R-CNN; consider removing anecdotal Figure 6.  
- Minor issues: Clarify whether the method is named "DNN" or "DDA" and fix the typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer supports acceptance.
