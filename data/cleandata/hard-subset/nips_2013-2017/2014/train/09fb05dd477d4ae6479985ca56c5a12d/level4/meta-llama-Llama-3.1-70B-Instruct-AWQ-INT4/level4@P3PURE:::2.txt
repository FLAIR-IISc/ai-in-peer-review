The authors propose a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection tasks. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset demonstrates that the proposed method ranks 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, with the proposed CNN structure being interesting and simple to implement. Overall, the quality of the paper is good.

However, there are two concerns that the authors could improve upon. Firstly, as the main contribution of this work, the adaptation method proposed in this paper requires better justification. The underlying motivations are not presented in a principled fashion. For instance, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are a summation of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' is not explained, which also makes it unclear why the kNN variant is better.

Secondly, in comparing with other methods, the authors used part of the validation set, rather than the test set, making the comparison unfair. Although the authors mention in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, the performance gain is not statistically justified due to the small size of this set.

In conclusion, the authors propose a framework that has comparable performance to the state-of-the-art model, but the comparisons are made under different settings. Furthermore, the proposed adaptation method needs a better explanation to fully understand its contribution.
