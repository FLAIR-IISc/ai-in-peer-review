- Problem: tackles object detection when bounding-box annotations are scarce compared to category labels.  
- Method: adapts object classifiers for detection with a simple transfer algorithm.  
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 held-out categories with only labels.  
- Results: method bridges ≈35% of the gap between an oracle and baseline on held-out categories.  
- Quality/Clarity: paper is well-written and technically sound.  
- Originality: combines known ideas (classifier-to-detector adaptation, category transfer) in a novel, useful way.  
- Significance: primary value is the approach; long-term impact uncertain as stronger methods may follow.  
- Practicality concern: reducing bounding-box annotation reliance is worthwhile, but annotation costs may fall over time.  
- Fig. 3a issue: clarify which methods besides the authors’ method and R-CNN use category labels for detection.  
- Fig. 3b / evaluation issue: explicitly highlight the blue-bar improvement on held-out categories and avoid emphasizing the misleading 78% (and 50%) figures.  
- Evaluation recommendation: focus comparisons solely on held-out categories and measure improvements relative to the baseline, not just as a percent of oracle.  
- Comparison to R-CNN: add a dedicated table or subsection contrasting the proposed method with R-CNN and streamline anecdotal Figure 6.  
- Minor fixes: use consistent method name (DNN vs. LSDA) and correct typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: accept for presentation at NIPS — simple, promising approach deserving dissemination.
