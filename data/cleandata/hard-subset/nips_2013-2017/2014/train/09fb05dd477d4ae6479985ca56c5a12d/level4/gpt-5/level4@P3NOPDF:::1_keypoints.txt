- Paper tackles object detection with limited bounding-box supervision by adapting classifiers to detectors and transferring that adaptation across categories.
- Experimental setup: train on 100 fully supervised categories, adapt and evaluate on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels.
- Method closes roughly 35% of the gap between a baseline and a fully supervised oracle on held-out categories.
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and technically sound; experiments are well motivated and carefully executed.
- Novelty lies in combining classifier-to-detector adaptation with cross-category transfer; interesting though not revolutionary.
- Contribution: a simple adaptation framework that reduces dependence on bounding boxes with solid empirical gains.
- Long-term impact is uncertain since annotation scale can change, which may reduce bounding-box scarcity as a bottleneck.
- Clarify Figure 3a: specify which methods use image-level labels for detection to ensure fair comparisons.
- Main quantitative takeaway should emphasize the ~1/3 gap closure on left-out categories rather than the potentially misleading “78%” or “50%” figures; recompute and reframe relative to baseline and oracle.
- Consolidate differences to R-CNN in a dedicated subsection or table and consider moving Figure 6 to make room for clearer comparisons.
- Add an ablation to separate benefits from classifier-to-detector adaptation versus cross-category transfer.
- Provide at least a coarse estimate of annotation effort saved versus mAP lost to make the cost–performance trade-off concrete.
- Minor fixes: use consistent name (“DNN” vs “DDA”), correct typo on line 431, and clarify in captions/text whether reported numbers are over all categories or only left-out set.
- Overall: solid, timely, convincing work within scope; recommend acceptance with requested clarifications and claim calibration.
