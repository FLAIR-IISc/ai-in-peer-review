- Problem: Adapts object classifiers trained with bounding boxes on source categories to detect novel target categories with only image-level labels, closing ~35% of the gap between a baseline detector and an oracle.
- Strength — clarity: Well-written, clearly motivated, simple and reproducible algorithm.
- Strength — novelty: Novel combination of classifier-to-detector adaptation and cross-category adaptation; new formulation for reducing annotation dependence.
- Strength — significance: Addresses a practical, important challenge of reducing costly bounding-box annotations for large-scale detection.
- Weakness — quantitative framing: Emphasize relative improvement on held-out categories (≈1/3 of the gap) instead of reporting percentages of oracle mAP (e.g., “78% of oracle”) or vague “50% improvement”.
- Weakness — experimental clarity: Explicitly state the level of supervision each baseline/competing method uses (e.g., in Figure 3a) for fair comparisons.
- Weakness — R-CNN comparison: Provide a dedicated subsection or table contrasting the proposed method and R-CNN (methodology, assumptions, problem setting); consider replacing anecdotal Figure 6.
- Weakness — annotation trade-off: Discuss whether the performance gains justify reduced annotation effort and the long-term implications as annotation costs evolve.
- Conclusion: Solid, sensible contribution that opens a new direction; recommendation for acceptance.
- Minor issues: Inconsistent naming ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431.
