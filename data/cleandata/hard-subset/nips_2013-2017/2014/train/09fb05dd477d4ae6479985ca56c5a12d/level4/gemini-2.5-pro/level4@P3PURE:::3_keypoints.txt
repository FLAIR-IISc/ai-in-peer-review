- Presents a three-stage method to adapt an image-level CNN for detection when bounding boxes exist for only a subset of classes; at test time the network is applied to multiple image regions.  
- Results are compelling but rely heavily on the strength of the Krizhevsky et al. model rather than a novel architecture.  
- The method is simple, logical, and potentially valuable to vision/ML researchers.  
- Missing baseline 1: fine-tune all CNN layers using full images as positives and other-class images/regions as negatives.  
- Missing baseline 2: a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) baseline that iteratively selects the highest-scoring region per positive image for training.  
- Missing baseline 3: a trivial baseline that predicts a single fixed center bounding box (e.g., mean training bbox) at test time.  
- It is unclear whether the proposed approach outperforms these baselines; justification over baseline 1 is weak and MIL (baseline 2) may be more principled.  
- Experimental descriptions (e.g., entries in Table 1) are ambiguous—need clarification on which equations/methods apply to which CNN layers.  
- Originality is sufficient for publication despite being straightforward and tailored to a specific CNN.  
- Significance is high because it addresses an important weakly-supervised detection problem, but missing baselines leave open that gains may be due mainly to strong CNN features.
