- Proposes a framework to adapt pre-trained image-classification DNNs for object detection; strong empirical result (4th place on ImageNet LSVRC-2013).  
- Paper is clearly written and the architecture is straightforward and easy to implement; overall a solid contribution.  
- Major concern: the adaptation method lacks principled justification for its design choices.  
- Unclear why detection weights are modeled as the sum of a class-specific term and a class-agnostic average ("offset parameter").  
- Without that foundation, the improved performance of the kNN variant is hard to interpret.  
- Experimental concern: evaluation on a validation subset instead of the standard test set prevents fair comparison to competing methods.  
- The small validation subset raises doubts about the statistical significance of reported gains.  
- Suggested fixes: provide deeper theoretical/intuition for the modeling choices and benchmark against state-of-the-art on the standard test set.
