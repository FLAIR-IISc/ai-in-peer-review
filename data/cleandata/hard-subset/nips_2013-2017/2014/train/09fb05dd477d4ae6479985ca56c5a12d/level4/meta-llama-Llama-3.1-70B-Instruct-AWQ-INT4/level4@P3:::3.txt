This paper presents a novel method for adapting a Krizhevsky CNN network, trained for classification without bounding box annotations, to work for object detection, given a small number of classes with annotated bounding boxes. The approach is intuitive and leverages the success of Krizhevsky et al.'s model. 

The method is well-motivated by the challenge of scaling object detection to a large number of categories, where obtaining labeled images with bounding box annotations is difficult. The authors propose Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), an algorithm that learns the difference between classification and detection tasks and transfers this knowledge to classifiers for categories without bounding box annotated data. 

The paper is clear and well-written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The experiments section is comprehensive, and the results are promising, demonstrating a significant improvement over a baseline of just using raw classifier weights on object proposal regions.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors carefully evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of their approach, discussing the importance of each component of their algorithm through an ablation study. However, the paper would benefit from additional comparisons to other baselines, such as using full images as positive examples, training and fine-tuning all layers of the CNN, or using a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach. Including these baselines would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach's effectiveness.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written. However, some sections, such as the experiments, could be improved with more detailed explanations. For example, the explanation of what each method in Table 1 refers to could be elaborated upon.

Originality: The paper presents a novel approach to adapting classifiers to detectors, which is sufficiently original to be accepted. While the basic idea is simple, the authors provide a thorough analysis of their method and demonstrate its effectiveness on a large-scale dataset.

Significance: The problem addressed in the paper is relevant, and the results are significant and valuable. The fact that decent detection results were obtained on a large-scale dataset without bounding box labels is compelling. However, it is unclear whether the results are due to the proposed approach or the improved performance of CNN features.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to adapting classifiers to detectors.
* The method is well-motivated by the challenge of scaling object detection to a large number of categories.
* The experiments are comprehensive, and the results are promising.
* The paper is clear and well-written.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper is missing some obvious baselines, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach's effectiveness.
* It is unclear whether the results are due to the proposed approach or the improved performance of CNN features.
* The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations in some sections, such as the experiments.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel approach to adapting classifiers to detectors. While it is missing some obvious baselines, the results are promising, and the paper is significant and valuable. With some revisions to address the limitations, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
