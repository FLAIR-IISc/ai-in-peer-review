This paper introduces LSDA, a framework for adapting deep convolutional neural networks trained for image classification to the task of object detection. The approach addresses the challenge of limited labeled bounding box data by leveraging the abundance of image-level labels. Evaluated on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, the method achieves strong performance, ranking 4th overall. The paper is well-written and generally clear, presenting an interesting and relatively straightforward CNN architecture. Overall, the work demonstrates good quality. However, two primary areas could benefit from further development.

First, the core adaptation method requires stronger justification and explanation. While the approach demonstrably improves performance, the underlying motivations and design choices are not fully articulated. For example, Section 3.2 (page 5, line 231) introduces a summation of weights *W<sub>c</sub><sup>j</sup>* and *W<sub>avg</sub>* for the adapted detection task without a clear explanation of its rationale. The meaning of this "offset parameter" and why a k-nearest neighbors (kNN) approach for determining *W<sub>avg</sub>* is effective are not sufficiently explained. A more principled discussion of these choices would strengthen the contribution.

Second, the comparison to other methods is slightly weakened by using only a portion of the validation set and not the dedicated test set. The authors acknowledge this limitation in a footnote (line 322), noting that R-CNN exhibits similar performance on the val2 set. However, due to the small size of val2, any observed gains are not statistically significant. A comparison on the standard test set would provide a more robust and fair evaluation.

The authors successfully demonstrate a method for transferring knowledge from classification to detection, achieving competitive results. However, enhancing the justification for the adaptation method and providing a more comprehensive comparison to existing approaches would significantly strengthen the paper. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark (ImageNet LSVRC-2013).
*   Addresses a practically important problem: scaling object detection to a large number of categories with limited bounding box annotations.
*   The proposed method is relatively simple and could be easily adopted by other researchers.
*   The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The underlying motivations for the adaptation method are not fully explained.
*   The comparison to other methods is somewhat limited by the evaluation setup.
*   The statistical significance of the improvements is not convincingly demonstrated, particularly in light of the limited size of the validation set.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field. While the concerns raised above are valid, they do not fundamentally detract from the quality of the work. I would rate this as a **weak accept**. Addressing the concerns regarding justification and evaluation would elevate this paper to a strong accept.
