The authors proposed a framework to adapt trained deep neural networks for image classification tasks to object detection tasks. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSRC-2013 dataset shows that the proposed method ranks fourth in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally easy to understand, and the proposed CNN structure is interesting and simple to implement. Overall, the quality of the paper is good. However, there are two concerns that the authors could improve.

First, as a major contribution to this work, the optimization method proposed in this paper needs better justification. The details of the underlying motivations are not presented in a theoretical manner. For example, in section 3.2 (line 231, page 5), it is not clear why the sum of the weights Wj^c and W{avg} is for the optimized detection task. The physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' is not clarified. Consequently, it is also not clear why the kNN version is better.

Second, when comparing with other methods, users used a subset of the validation set, but not the test set, which made the comparison less meaningful. The authors mention in the footnote (line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but due to the small size of this set, the performance gain is not statistically significant.

The authors proposed a framework to adapt trained deep neural networks for image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The performance of the proposed method is similar to that of state-of-the-art models, but the comparison was made on different settings, and the proposed optimization method needs better clarification.
