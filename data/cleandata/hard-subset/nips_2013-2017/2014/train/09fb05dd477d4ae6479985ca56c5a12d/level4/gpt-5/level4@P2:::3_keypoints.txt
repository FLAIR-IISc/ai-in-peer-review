- Proposes LSDA: adapt a CNN trained for classification into a detector using bounding-box labels for only a subset of classes via three-step fine-tuning and delta transfer.  
- Method: pre-train on classification, fine-tune layers 1–7 for detection on subset B with a background class, transfer category-specific output-layer deltas from B to held-out A via nearest-neighbor averaging.  
- At test time LSDA scores region proposals for K classes plus background and uses score_i − score_background for final detections.  
- Empirical claim: 50% relative mAP gain over classification-only baseline on held-out ILSVRC13 categories and scaling to a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Builds on R-CNN and relates to domain adaptation and weakly-supervised detection, but targets categories with no detection labels.  
- Strength: clear, practical problem and a simple, logical algorithm.  
- Strength: effective use of background modeling and category-invariant adaptation to bridge classification and detection.  
- Strength: large-scale demonstration (7.6K classes) and public code/models increase impact.  
- Strength: quantitative gains and error analysis show reduced localization and background confusions.  
- Major missing baselines: weakly supervised full-image positives baseline (fine-tune using whole images as positives, evaluate on proposals).  
- Major missing baselines: MIL baseline that iteratively selects top-scoring regions per weakly labeled image and refines detectors.  
- Major missing baseline: default-box sanity check that predicts a single default bounding box per image to quantify localization benefit.  
- Concern: unclear whether LSDA outperforms strong weakly supervised alternatives (baselines could change conclusions).  
- Technical soundness: category-invariant fine-tuning and background class are convincing; category-specific delta transfer is reasonable but heuristic.  
- Empirical support: ablations indicate most gains from shared representation and background class, smaller gains from category-specific deltas.  
- Completeness: absence of suggested baselines leaves open alternative explanations for gains.  
- Clarity issues: need explicit mapping from Table 1 to training variants (which layers adapted, background learned, where Eq.1 applied).  
- Clarity issues: need details on nearest-neighbor selection (feature space, normalization, k sensitivity) and whether semantic neighbors were tried.  
- Clarity request: quantify performance loss from omitting SVMs/box regression on held-out categories and whether box regression on B transfers.  
- Originality: moderate—novel setting and delta-transfer mechanism though built on existing R-CNN fine-tuning ideas.  
- Significance: high practical value for large-scale detection with limited box supervision; resources released boost impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, simple general method, strong empirical gains over classification baseline, scales to thousands, resources released.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing strong weakly supervised baselines, heuristic category-specific transfer with limited sensitivity analysis, and some experimental ambiguities.  
- Overall: solid, impactful paper; leaning toward acceptance if authors add the proposed baselines or justify their omission and clarify experimental/transfer details.
