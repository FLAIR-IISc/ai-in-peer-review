- Tackles adapting object classifiers to detection to avoid costly bounding box annotations.  
- Proposes a simple, novel method that adapts classifiers for detection and transfers knowledge across category sets.  
- Experiments: trained on 100 categories with boxes, evaluated on 100 held-out categories with only labels.  
- Results: method recovers ~35% of the gap between oracle and baseline on unseen categories (encouraging).  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented with easy-to-follow experimental details.  
- Originality: novel combination of existing adaptation ideas, not a radical departure but an interesting contribution.  
- Significance: provides a useful baseline and direction, though long-term impact depends on annotation/resource trade-offs and justification for mAP vs. annotation savings.  
- Clarification needed for Figure 3a: specify which methods use category labels for detection for fair comparison.  
- Results should emphasize the blue bars in Figure 3b (~one-third improvement) and contextualize claimed percentages (78% and 50%) against baseline and left-out categories.  
- Add a clear, focused comparison to R-CNN (table/subsection) instead of scattering distinctions; consider removing Figure 6.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance for presentation at NIPS as a valuable baseline and new research direction.
