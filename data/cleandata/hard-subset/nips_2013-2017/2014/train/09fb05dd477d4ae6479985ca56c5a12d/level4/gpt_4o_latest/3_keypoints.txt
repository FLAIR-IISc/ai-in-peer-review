- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to object detection for a few classes with bounding boxes.  
- Training pipeline: (1) train CNN for classification on all classes, (2) train CNN on image regions with bounding-box classes plus a background class, (3) integrate background class and adapt certain CNN weights.  
- At test time the adapted CNN is applied to multiple image regions per test image.  
- Results are promising and the approach is straightforward and logical.  
- Strong reliance on the Krizhevsky et al. model; novelty is limited and gains may stem from the base CNN rather than the method.  
- Key missing baselines: (1) train on full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives with full fine-tuning, (2) same but with MIL picking highest-scoring positive region per image, (3) use classifier at test time to predict a single central/mean bounding box.  
- It’s unclear whether the proposed method outperforms those baselines; baseline 1 may be as good and baseline 2 is arguably more intuitive.  
- Paper is generally well-written but the experiments section (e.g., methods in Table 1 and application of Eq.1/2 to layers) needs clearer explanation.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance but relatively simple and architecture-specific.  
- Significance is high (detection without bounding boxes is valuable), but the absence of key baselines weakens the claim that the method itself is superior.
