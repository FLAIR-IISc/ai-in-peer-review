- Proposes LSDA: learn a category-invariant transformation to convert image classifiers into object detectors using a small set of categories with bounding boxes; built on R-CNN and tested on ILSVRC, producing a 7,604-category detector with ~50% relative mAP gain on held-out classes.

- Tackles a significant, practical problem: scaling detection to many categories despite scarce bounding-box annotations.

- Novel, elegant formulation: frames classifier-to-detector conversion as domain adaptation and learns a transferable transformation plus a background model.

- Strong empirical results: substantial mAP improvement on held-out categories, informative ablation study, qualitative results, and error analysis showing reduced localization/background confusion.

- Demonstrated scalability and community value: large-scale detector release (7.6K+ classes) and code/models provided.

- Main weakness: missing key baseline comparisons, leaving unclear whether gains stem from the adaptation or from strong CNN features.

- Suggested baseline omissions: simple weakly-supervised fine-tuning, Multiple Instance Learning (MIL), and a simple center/mean-box “sanity check” baseline.

- Quality: technically sound with well-conducted experiments, but evaluation incomplete without the aforementioned baselines.

- Clarity: generally well-written with effective figures; experimental details (e.g., which layers are fine-tuned in Table 1) could be clearer.

- Originality: novel idea of learning a generic classifier→detector transformation applicable to new classes without boxes.

- Significance: high potential impact for large-scale detection research and applications.

- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, presents a novel framework, shows strong quantitative gains and error analysis, and demonstrates impressive scalability.

- Argument against acceptance: insufficient experimental comparisons to critical weakly-supervised/MIL baselines.

- Recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance but strongly encourage adding discussion and experiments for missing baselines in the final version.
