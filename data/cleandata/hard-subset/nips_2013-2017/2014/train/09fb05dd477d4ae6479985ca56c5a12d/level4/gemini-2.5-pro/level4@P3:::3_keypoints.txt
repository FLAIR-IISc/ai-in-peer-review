- Problem: Scale object detection to many categories without bounding-box labels by converting image classifiers into detectors via domain adaptation (LSDA).
- Method: Train a K-way classifier, fine-tune shared conv/fc layers on categories with boxes for category-invariant adaptation, and transfer category-specific parameter changes to held-out classes.
- Result: LSDA yields substantial gains (+50% relative mAP) on held-out ILSVRC2013 categories versus using classifiers directly.
- Strength: Addresses a significant, practical bottleneck and demonstrates scalability (detector for >7,600 categories).
- Strength: Novel framing—learning a transferable classifier-to-detector transformation with clear category-invariant vs. category-specific components.
- Strength: Strong empirical and ablation results showing adaptation reduces localization/background errors; feature adaptation is most critical.
- Main weakness: Experimental evaluation omits key baselines, making it unclear if gains are due to LSDA or strong pre-trained CNN features.
- Missing baseline 1: Weakly-supervised learning (fine-tuning on full images as positives and random patches as negatives) for held-out categories.
- Missing baseline 2: Multiple Instance Learning baseline (iteratively selecting top-scoring proposals per positive image).
- Missing baseline 3: Simple localization baseline (e.g., canonical centered bounding box) to quantify localization difficulty.
- Minor clarity issue: Table/section labels for adaptation strategies should explicitly state which layers were frozen vs. fine-tuned for reproducibility.
- Conclusion: Promising and elegant contribution likely of high interest, but acceptance should be conditioned on adding the omitted baselines to strengthen validation.
