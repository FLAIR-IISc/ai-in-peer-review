- Proposes a framework to adapt classification-trained CNNs for object detection, evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013.  
- Achieves 4th place in the ImageNet object detection challenge.  
- Paper is clearly written and generally easy to follow.  
- Proposed CNN architecture is interesting and straightforward to implement.  
- Main concern: the adaptation method lacks principled justification—design choices and motivations are not well explained.  
- Specific uncertainty about the formulation of detection weights as W_j^c + W_avg and the intuition behind the offset parameter.  
- Unclear why the kNN variant of the model should be expected to perform better.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair: evaluation on a validation subset instead of the official test set and the small subset size limits statistical significance.  
- Recommendation: strengthen the paper by providing a principled explanation of the adaptation technique and comparing against competitors on a standard benchmark.
