This paper proposes a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification to object detection. On the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset, the method ranks 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented, generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN architecture is interesting and simple to implement. Overall, the quality is good.

However, I have two main concerns:

1) Justification of the adaptation method. As the central contribution, the adaptation approach needs stronger, more principled motivation. The underlying rationale is not presented in a sufficiently rigorous way. For example, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the detection weights are defined as the sum of Wj^c and Wavg. The physical meaning of this “offset parameter” is not explained, and as a result it is also unclear why the kNN variant should perform better.

2) Fairness of the empirical comparison. In comparing with other methods, the authors use part of the validation set rather than the test set, which makes the comparison not directly fair. The footnote (Line 322) notes that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but given the small size of this set, the reported performance gains are not statistically justified.

In summary, the framework adapts classification-trained CNNs to detection and achieves performance comparable to the state of the art, but the comparisons are conducted under different settings, and the proposed adaptation method requires clearer, more principled explanation.
