- Paper proposes LSDA: learn a classifier→detector transformation via fine-tuning on a subset with boxes and transfer it to classes with only image labels.  
- Category-invariant adaptation is learned by fine-tuning layers 1–7 including a background class.  
- Category-specific deltas for fc8 are learned on labeled classes and transferred to held-out classes via nearest-neighbor averaging.  
- Detection scores are produced on region proposals with K class scores plus a background score; per-class SVMs and bbox regression are omitted for simplicity/speed.  
- Evaluated on ILSVRC-2013 by training on 100 box-labeled classes and testing on 100 held-out classes; LSDA outperforms raw classification on proposals and closes part of the gap to an oracle detector.  
- Demonstrated a 7.6K-category detector by adapting ImageNet leaf-node classifiers and offered a faster variant using SPP/fast proposals.  
- Method builds on R-CNN, MIL, and domain adaptation, but is novel in transferring classifier→detector adaptation to completely held-out categories within a deep network.  
- Technical approach is straightforward, well-motivated, and technically sound; background modeling and feature adaptation are key contributors.  
- Empirical protocol and ablations are clear and informative; category-specific deltas provide a smaller but significant improvement.  
- Error analysis (localization, background, other) aligns with the adaptation goals and is useful.  
- Request clearer figure legends to show which baselines use image-level labels, background modeling, and matching proposal methods for fair comparison.  
- Recommend reporting improvements on held-out classes as fraction of the baseline–oracle gap closed, not just percent of oracle.  
- Ask for sensitivity analysis of nearest-neighbor transfer: choice of k and similarity metric (fc8 distance vs semantic/WordNet/confusion-based).  
- Suggest reporting impact and compute trade-offs of adding back per-class SVMs and bbox regression for classes with boxes.  
- Large-scale 7.6K results are only qualitative; request quantitative proxies (subset with boxes or cross-dataset tests) to validate practical impact.  
- Clarity is good overall; suggest a dedicated subsection/table contrasting LSDA vs R-CNN, unify naming to “LSDA,” and fix minor typos.  
- Originality: novel combination of learning a generic classifier→detector transformation and transferring it to classes without boxes; simple but insightful.  
- Significance: addresses annotation bottleneck, shows promising gains and scalability, but long-term impact depends on closing remaining performance gap to supervised detectors.  
- Pros: clear/simple method, strong ablations, first large-scale adaptation demo, error analysis, code/models released, faster variant provided.  
- Cons: held-out mAP remains far below fully supervised oracle; category-specific transfer is heuristic with limited sensitivity analysis; large-scale results lack quantitative evaluation; comparisons to R-CNN need clearer baselining.  
- Specific suggestions: clarify baselines, reframe headline metrics as gap-closure, add LSDA vs R-CNN table, analyze neighbor choice and definitions, provide small-scale quantitative check for 7.6K model, and unify naming/fix typos.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — timely, well-executed approach with novel scaling insight, solid empirical study, and high practical potential.
