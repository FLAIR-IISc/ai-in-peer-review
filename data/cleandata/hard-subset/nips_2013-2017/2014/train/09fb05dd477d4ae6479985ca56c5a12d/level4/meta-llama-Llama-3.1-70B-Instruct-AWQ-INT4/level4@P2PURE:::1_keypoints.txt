- Tackles detection with few bounding boxes by adapting object classifiers to detectors; algorithm is simple and straightforward.
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 left-out categories with only labels; method closes ~35% of gap between oracle and baseline on left-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and clear.
- Novel combination of existing ideas (classifier→detector adaptation and cross-category adaptation); interesting but not groundbreaking.
- Significance uncertain: approach sacrifices mAP by using fewer boxes and it’s unclear if this trade-off is worthwhile or long-term important given possible resource scaling.
- Metric/claim issues: authors should emphasize the blue-bar result (DNN ≈ one-third between baseline and oracle) rather than the 78% number, and evaluate percentages only on left-out categories while accounting for the baseline.
- Clarify Figure 3a by stating which methods do/do not use category labels to aid detection for fair comparisons (including versus R-CNN).
- Add a dedicated table/subsection clearly outlining differences between the proposed method and R-CNN instead of scattered statements; replace anecdotal Figure 6.
- Minor: clarify method name (DNN vs DDA) and fix typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").
- Overall recommendation: accept — reasonably novel and simple approach, though long-term impact is uncertain.
