- Presents a framework to adapt pretrained image-classification nets to detection with competitive ImageNet LSVRC-2013 results (ranked 4th).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and the adaptation mechanism is simple and implementable.  
- Technical quality is strong and the contribution has potential community value.  
- Core adaptation method (W_j^d = W_j^c + W_avg) lacks theoretical justification; the linear combination and "offset parameter" are unexplained.  
- No principled derivation or task-alignment argument is provided to justify the weight formulation; the kNN variant’s superiority is unsupported.  
- Authors should provide a principled rationale or empirical ablation studies to substantiate design choices.  
- Comparison methodology is flawed: experiments use val2 instead of the official test set, compromising fairness and replicability.  
- Val2 is too small for reliable significance; reported gains may reflect overfitting to the validation partition.  
- Experimental settings are inconsistent across methods (different backbones/training protocols), obscuring whether gains come from the adaptation method.  
- Recommend re-running comparisons on the test set and/or matching baselines’ training protocols to validate claims.
