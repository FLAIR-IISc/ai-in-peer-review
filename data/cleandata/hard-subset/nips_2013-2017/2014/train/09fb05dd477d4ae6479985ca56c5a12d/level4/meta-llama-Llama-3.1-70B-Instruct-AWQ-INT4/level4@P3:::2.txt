This paper proposes a novel framework, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset, achieving a 50% relative improvement in mean Average Precision (mAP) over a baseline method. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the proposed CNN structure.

The quality of the paper is good, with a thorough evaluation of the proposed method on a challenging dataset. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the importance of each component of their algorithm and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in adapting classifiers to detectors. The paper also presents a large-scale detection experiment, showcasing the capabilities of the proposed technique.

However, there are some concerns that the authors could address to improve the paper. Firstly, the adaptation method proposed in this paper needs better justification. The underlying motivations and principles behind the method are not clearly explained, making it difficult to understand the reasoning behind certain design choices. For example, the calculation of the final output detection weights for categories in set A (Equation 1) is not clearly motivated, and the physical meaning of the 'offset parameter' is not explained. Additionally, the choice of kNN variant is not well-justified, and it is not clear why this approach is better than other possible variants.

Secondly, the comparison with other methods is not entirely fair. The authors use part of the validation set for training and evaluation, but not the test set. While the authors mention that the R-CNN model has similar performance on the val2 set, the small size of this set makes the performance gain not statistically justified. It would be more convincing to compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art models on the test set.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to adapting classifiers to detectors, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of a domain adaptation framework to transfer knowledge from classification to detection tasks is a new and interesting idea. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work in domain adaptation and object detection, to better situate the proposed method in the context of existing research.

In terms of significance, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of object detection, with a novel approach that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging dataset. The proposed method has the potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories, which is a significant advancement in the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of object detection. However, the authors could improve the paper by providing a clearer explanation of the underlying principles and motivations behind the proposed method, and by presenting a more fair comparison with state-of-the-art models.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of object detection.
* The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging dataset.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The adaptation method proposed in this paper needs better justification and explanation.
* The comparison with other methods is not entirely fair.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work in domain adaptation and object detection.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but require the authors to address the concerns mentioned above, particularly the need for a clearer explanation of the underlying principles and motivations behind the proposed method, and a more fair comparison with state-of-the-art models.
