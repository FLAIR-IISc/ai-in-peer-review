- Proposal: three-stage method to adapt an AlexNet classification CNN to detection with limited bounding-box supervision (train classifier, region-level fine-tune with background, integrate background and adapt layers; score regions at test time).

- Strength: simple, logical pipeline that leverages strong CNN features to obtain useful detectors with weak supervision plus limited boxes.

- Main weakness: evaluation omits key baselines, making it hard to isolate the benefit of the proposed adaptation versus the underlying CNN features.

- Baseline 1 missing: fine-tune the CNN on full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives to test whether region-level fine-tuning is necessary.

- Baseline 2 missing: a multiple-instance learning (MIL) weakly supervised detection baseline that iteratively selects highest-scoring regions.

- Baseline 3 missing: pure classification model with a canonical bounding box at test time as a sanity check for localization vs. classification effects.

- Clarity issues: need clear definitions for method variants (which layers adapted), explicit mapping of equations to layers, and clearer Table 1 descriptions.

- Data/protocol details missing: describe background class construction and sampling, region proposal method and counts, NMS/calibration, learning rates, schedules, iterations, freezing vs. fine-tuning, and data splits.

- Evaluation improvements requested: per-class AP and variance, ablations isolating each training stage, and sensitivity to number/quantity of box annotations.

- Originality: incremental but meaningful — straightforward adaptation that is valuable for large-scale settings without full box labels.

- Significance: important problem and promising results, but gains may largely reflect strong CNN features rather than the specific adaptation; stronger baselines needed.

- Suggested experiments: add the three baselines; ablate which layers are adapted and effect of background class; freeze vs. fine-tune subsets; leave-out-stage experiments; vary box-annotation amounts.

- Additional comparisons/analyses: train detector from scratch or using only boxed classes, error-mode analysis (localization vs. classification), qualitative success/failure examples, and runtime/memory/run-time throughput reporting.

- Generalization/clarification: state if method extends beyond AlexNet and note any architecture-specific assumptions.

- Minor notes: use consistent “adapt/fine-tune” terminology, explicitly map equations to layers, and describe handling of class imbalance when adding the background class.

- Overall assessment: relevant and timely with promising results; leans toward acceptance conditional on adding missing comparisons, clearer experimental detail, and requested ablations.
