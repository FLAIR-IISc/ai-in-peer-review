The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection tasks. Their approach achieves impressive results, ranking 4th in the object detection challenge on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset. The paper is well-presented and easy to follow, with an interesting and simple-to-implement CNN structure. Overall, the paper's quality is good. However, there are two areas that could be improved.

First, the adaptation method, which is the main contribution of this work, requires more rigorous justification. The underlying motivations and principles are not clearly presented. For instance, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are a summation of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical meaning of the 'offset parameter' is also not explained, making it unclear why the kNN variant is superior. Providing a clearer explanation of these components would strengthen the paper's core contribution.

Second, the comparison with other methods is not entirely fair, as the authors use part of the validation set, rather than the test set. Although the authors mention in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN achieves similar performance on the val2 set, the small size of this set means that the performance gain is not statistically significant. To provide a more accurate comparison, the authors should use the test set or provide a more detailed analysis of the validation set results.

In summary, the authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks to object detection tasks, achieving comparable performance to state-of-the-art models. However, the comparisons are made under different settings, and the proposed adaptation method requires more detailed explanation and justification to fully convince the reader of its merits.
