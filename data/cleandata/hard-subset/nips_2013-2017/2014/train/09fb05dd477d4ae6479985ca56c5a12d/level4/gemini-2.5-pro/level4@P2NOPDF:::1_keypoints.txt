- Tackles object detection with scarce bounding boxes by adapting object classifiers for detection using a simple, straightforward algorithm.  
- Trained on 100 categories with boxes+labels and tested on 100 novel categories with only labels; closes ~35% of the gap between a baseline and an oracle on held-out categories.  
- High-quality, well-written paper with a clear presentation and experimental setup.  
- Originality: novel combination of adapting classifiers for detection and adapting across category sets—an interesting incremental contribution.  
- Significance: proposes a novel formulation and adaptation approach, but long-term impact is uncertain and the method trades detection performance for reduced annotation cost without an objective evaluation framework for that trade-off.  
- Raises the point that bounding-box scarcity might be resolved by large-scale data collection, limiting long-term necessity of this approach.  
- Figure 3a: unclear which competing methods also use category-level labels—needs explicit clarification for fair comparison.  
- Recommend emphasizing held-out category performance (blue bars in Fig 3b) and reporting improvements relative to the baseline instead of percentages of the oracle.  
- R-CNN comparison is scattered; consolidate differences into a dedicated subsection or comparative table and consider replacing anecdotal Fig 6.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent naming of the method ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431.  
- Overall: solid, novel, and deserving of acceptance despite modest long-term impact; reviewer recommends acceptance.
