- Paper proposes LSDA: learn a category-invariant transformation to convert image classifiers into object detectors using bounding-box supervision on a subset of categories and transferring it to others.  
- Method: fine-tune CNN for classification, adapt feature layers for detection with some categories, learn category-specific output adjustments and approximate them for unlabeled categories via nearest neighbors.  
- Experiments on ILSVRC-2013 simulate 100 adapted / 100 held-out categories; LSDA improves detection over classifier baseline and scales to ~7,600 categories.  
- Strength: tackles an important, practical problem—reducing reliance on expensive bounding-box annotations to scale detection.  
- Strength: novel and elegant formulation of classifier-to-detector adaptation, combining ideas from domain adaptation and transfer learning in a clear way.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and thorough ablation/error analysis showing reduced localization and background confusion.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, logically organized, and experiments support claims.  
- Weakness: headline “50% relative mAP boost” is potentially misleading; suggested reframing as percent of gap closed between baseline and oracle (~34.5% gap closed).  
- Weakness: need clearer, consolidated comparison with R-CNN (training data needs, pre-training vs adaptation, SVMs/BBox regression, training time).  
- Weakness: motivation could better acknowledge that large-scale bounding-box annotation might be addressable long-term (e.g., crowdsourcing) while emphasizing current practicality.  
- Weakness: clarify supervision used for every baseline/method in figures/tables so comparisons are unambiguous.  
- Minor: typo in conclusion (“classi?ers” → “classifiers”).  
- Recommendation: high-quality, influential contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
