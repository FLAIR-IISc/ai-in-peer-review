- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style classification CNN to detection via three stages: train classification CNN on full images, train region-based CNN on labeled classes (including background), integrate background class and fine-tune; test uses multiple image regions.  
- Method is logically structured, uses established CNN architectures, and shows that weakly supervised detection can work at large scale.  
- Experimental evaluation omits critical baselines, leaving the contribution’s advantage unclear.  
- Missing baseline 1 (Full-image baseline): train on full images as positives and regions from other classes as negatives, fine-tuning all layers.  
- Missing baseline 2 (MIL-based): iteratively select top-scoring regions per image for training.  
- Missing baseline 3 (Simple heuristic): use a precomputed mean bounding box (e.g., class center) with the classification CNN.  
- Without these baselines, improvements may simply reflect strong CNN features rather than a novel detection strategy; Baseline 1 is especially important.  
- Clarity issue: Table 1 cites Eq.1/2 but does not specify whether those equations apply to specific layers or the entire network—needs clarification.  
- Originality is modest: a practical but not radical extension of existing ideas.  
- Significance is high in principle (reducing annotation cost), but its impact is weakened by missing baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending substantial revisions to address experimental gaps.  
- Required revisions: add the three baselines, explicitly clarify implementation details (e.g., layer-wise application of equations), and compare/discuss results against these baselines in the main text.
