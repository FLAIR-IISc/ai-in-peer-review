The authors introduce a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The proposed approach achieves a 4th place ranking in the object detection challenge on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset. The paper is well-presented, easy to follow, and proposes an interesting and implementable CNN structure. Overall, the paper's quality is good, but there are two areas for improvement.

Firstly, the adaptation method, which is the primary contribution of this work, requires more robust justification. The underlying motivations and principles are not clearly articulated, such as in Section 3.2 (Page 5 Line 231), where the summation of Wj^c and W{avg} for adapted detection task weights lacks clear explanation. The physical significance of the 'offset parameter' is also not provided, making it unclear why the kNN variant is superior.

Secondly, the comparison with other methods is compromised by the use of only a subset of the validation set, rather than the test set, resulting in an unfair comparison. Although the authors note in the footnote (Line 322) that RCNN exhibits similar performance on the val2 set, the small size of this set renders the performance gain statistically insignificant.

The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks from image classification to object detection tasks, demonstrating comparable performance to state-of-the-art models. However, the comparisons are made under different settings, and the adaptation method warrants a more detailed explanation.
