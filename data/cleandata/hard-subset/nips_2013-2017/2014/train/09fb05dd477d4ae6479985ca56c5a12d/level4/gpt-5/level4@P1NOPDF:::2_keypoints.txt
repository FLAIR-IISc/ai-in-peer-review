- Proposes a framework to adapt image-classification CNNs to object detection, ranking fourth on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection.
- Paper is clearly written, easy to follow, with an interesting and straightforward CNN architecture; overall quality is good.
- Main concern 1: adaptation method lacks principled justification and motivation.
- Unclear why adapted detection weights are defined as W_j^d = W_j^c + W_avg and what the “offset parameter” represents.
- No intuition or analysis explaining why this decomposition is effective or why the kNN variant would outperform others.
- Main concern 2: comparisons use a small validation subset rather than the standard test set, undermining fairness.
- Reported gains are not statistically convincing given the small subset; need test-set results or proper statistical analysis.
- Summary: practical, competitive approach but requires stronger theoretical justification for the adaptation mechanism and fairer, more robust evaluation.
