- Proposes adapting a classification CNN to detection using a three-stage procedure: train on image labels, train region-based CNN with a background class for classes with boxes, integrate background and fine-tune subset, then detect via multi-region scoring.  
- Results are promising but largely rely on powerful pre-trained Krizhevsky-style features rather than a fundamentally new model.  
- Experimental evaluation is weakened by the absence of key baselines, hindering proper performance contextualization.  
- Suggested baseline 1: fine-tune all CNN layers using full images of target classes as positives and other images/regions as negatives.  
- Suggested baseline 2: incorporate Multiple Instance Learning (MIL), selecting the highest-scoring region per positive training image.  
- Suggested baseline 3: simple fixed-center-box classification baseline using the mean ImageNet bounding box at test time.  
- Without these comparisons, it’s unclear if the proposed method outperforms simpler or more principled alternatives; justification over baseline 1 and MIL is not obvious.  
- Clarity issue: experiments/Table 1 need clearer descriptions (which equations are used, which CNN layers are fine-tuned for each method).  
- Originality: novel enough for acceptance—straightforward and practical but tailored to a specific CNN family.  
- Significance: tackles an important problem (detection with limited box labels) and shows compelling large-scale potential, but missing baselines weaken the claimed contribution.
