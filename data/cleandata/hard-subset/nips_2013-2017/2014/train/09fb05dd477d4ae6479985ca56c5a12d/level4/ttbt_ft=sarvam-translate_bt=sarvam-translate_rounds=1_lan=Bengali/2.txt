The authors propose a framework for adapting trained deep neural networks from image classification tasks to object detection tasks. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset shows that the proposed method ranks fourth in the object detection challenge. This paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN framework is interesting and easy to implement. Overall, the paper's quality is good. However, the authors could improve on two points.

Firstly, as a major contribution to this work, the paper needs a better rationale for the proposed adaptation method. The underlying objectives were not detailed in principle. For example, in section 3.2 (line 231, page 5), it is not clear why the weights for the adapted detection task are a sum of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical meaning of this 'offset parameter' is not explained. Consequently, it is also not clear why the kNN variant is better.

Secondly, when comparing with other methods, users used a subset of the validation set, but not the test set, which does not justify the comparison. The authors note in the footnote (line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but due to the small size of this set, the performance improvement is not statistically justified.

The authors propose a framework for adapting trained deep neural networks from image classification tasks to object detection tasks. The performance of the proposed method is comparable to state-of-the-art models, but the comparisons were made in different settings, and the proposed adaptation method needs further explanation.
