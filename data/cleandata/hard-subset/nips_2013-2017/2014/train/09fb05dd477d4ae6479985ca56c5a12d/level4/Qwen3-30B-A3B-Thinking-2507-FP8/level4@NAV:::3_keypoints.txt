- Proposal: adapt a Krizhevsky classification CNN to detection using three training phases (full-image classification, region-based CNN with annotated classes + background, and selective weight adaptation to integrate background).  
- Test-time: apply the CNN to multiple image regions per test image for detection.  
- Strength: interesting idea with reasonable detection results and straightforward, logical methodology.  
- Novelty: builds substantially on Krizhevsky et al.’s model—sufficiently novel for acceptance but not radically new and somewhat specific to certain CNN architectures.  
- Missing baselines: fails to compare against key alternatives—(1) full-image classifier fine-tuned with positive full images and negatives, (2) MIL-style region selection with full-layer fine-tuning, and (3) predicting a single center-bounded box from a classification CNN.  
- Comparative ambiguity: unclear advantage over Baseline 1 and likely inferior to Baseline 2; Baseline 3 is needed to contextualize dataset difficulty.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written overall but the experiments lack detail (e.g., which methods in Table 1 use Equation 1 vs. Equation 2 and which layers are fine-tuned).  
- Significance: addresses a relevant problem (detection without bounding-box labels) with promising results, but improvements may reflect better CNN features rather than a superior weakly supervised training method due to omitted baselines.
