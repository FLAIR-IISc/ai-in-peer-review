- Proposes adapting a classification CNN to detection via a three-stage training pipeline using limited bounding-box classes and a background class.  
- Practical and promising contribution but largely leverages Krizhevsky et al.’s CNN rather than offering deep methodological novelty.  
- Method is conceptually straightforward and logically sound.  
- Evaluation is weak: missing key baselines that could challenge the reported improvements.  
- Baseline 1: fine-tune the entire CNN using full images as positives and other-class regions as negatives — this is a strong, necessary comparison.  
- Baseline 2: use a Multiple Instance Learning approach (select highest-scoring region per positive image) which may be more effective.  
- Baseline 3: train/fine-tune CNN for classification only and predict a centered bounding box at test time to contextualize dataset difficulty.  
- It’s unclear whether improvements come from the proposed training scheme or simply from better CNN feature representations; baselines are needed to disambiguate.  
- Paper is well-written, but experimental presentation lacks detail (Table 1 and whether Eq.1/2 apply to all or only some layers is ambiguous).  
- Novelty and significance are sufficient for acceptance, but addressing the suggested baselines would strengthen confidence in the results.
