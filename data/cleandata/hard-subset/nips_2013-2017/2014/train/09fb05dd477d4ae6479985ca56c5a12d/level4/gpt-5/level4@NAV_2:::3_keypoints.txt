- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style CNN trained for classification into an object detector using bounding boxes for only a subset of categories, with a three-step training procedure and region-proposal testing.  
- Quality: interesting and reasonably convincing results, leveraging existing CNN architecture rather than a novel paradigm; straightforward and sensible strategy.  
- Missing key baselines: (1) train/fine-tune on whole images as positives and other-class images/regions as negatives, updating all CNN layers.  
- Missing key baselines: (2) same as above but using MIL to iteratively select the highest-scoring positive region per image.  
- Missing key baselines: (3) classification-only training with a single center/mean-ImageNet bounding box output at test time to calibrate dataset/task difficulty.  
- Reviewer unclear that the proposed method outperforms these baselines—may be no better than baseline 1 and likely worse than baseline 2.  
- Clarity: paper is well written, but experimental section/Table 1 needs clearer explanation of what each variant does and which equations/layers are applied.  
- Originality: novel enough for acceptance but conceptually simple and specific to certain CNN architectures.  
- Significance: important problem and valuable results for large-scale detection without full box labels, but lack of baselines leaves open whether gains are from training method or stronger CNN features.
