- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky et al. classification CNN to detection with limited bounding-box classes via three training steps and region-wise testing.  
- Results are interesting and promising but largely leverage the pre-trained Krizhevsky model rather than a radically novel technique.  
- Missing critical baseline: train a CNN using full images as positives and images/regions from other classes as negatives with full-layer fine-tuning.  
- Missing critical baseline: same as above but using a MIL approach (iteratively selecting highest-scoring positive region per image).  
- Missing critical baseline: predict a single bounding box per image at test time (e.g., mean/center box) to contextualize dataset difficulty.  
- Unclear whether the proposed method outperforms these baselines; baseline 1 may be simpler and potentially superior.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the experiments/Table 1 need explicit clarification about which equations are used and which CNN layers are adapted.  
- Originality is adequate for acceptance but the approach is straightforward and specific to certain CNN architectures.  
- Significance is relevant—large-scale detection without bounding-box labels is compelling—but tempered because improvements may reflect better CNN features rather than a superior weakly supervised strategy due to missing comparisons.
