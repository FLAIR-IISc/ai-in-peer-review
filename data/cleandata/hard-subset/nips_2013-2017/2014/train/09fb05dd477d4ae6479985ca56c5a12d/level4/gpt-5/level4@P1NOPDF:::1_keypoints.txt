- Tackles object detection by adapting object classifiers to detection to avoid large-scale bounding-box annotation.
- Trains on 100 categories with boxes and tests on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Method is simple and straightforward; simplicity is not a drawback.
- Results close ~35% of the gap between baseline and oracle on held-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and of good quality.
- Combines adaptation of classifiers for detection and adaptation across category sets; novel but not ground-breaking.
- Main contribution is the novel approach; long-term significance uncertain and likely to be surpassed by more sophisticated methods.
- Trade-off: reducing bounding-box needs sacrifices mAP, and no objective criterion is provided to judge if the trade-off is worthwhile.
- Reviewer questions whether the bounding-box labeling bottleneck is a critical long-term problem.
- Request clarification in Figure 3a about which methods use category labels for detection to ensure apples-to-apples comparison.
- Suggest highlighting the blue-bar result in Figure 3b (DNN closes ~1/3 of the gap) instead of the 78%/50% metrics, and report gap-closed on held-out categories.
- Ask for a dedicated table/subsection clearly articulating differences from R-CNN and recommend replacing Figure 6 if needed.
- Minor: standardize method name (“DNN” vs “DDA”) and fix typo on line 431. 
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance; contribution is worthwhile and timely despite uncertain long-term impact.
