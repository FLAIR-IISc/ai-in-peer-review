- Proposes a framework to adapt pre-trained image-classification CNNs to object detection, achieving strong empirical results (4th on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and the adaptation approach is simple and easy to implement.  
- Overall technical quality is good but two major issues need clarification before publication.  
- Core adaptation mechanism lacks theoretical/principled justification: detection weights are formed as W_j^c + W_avg without motivation.  
- Physical interpretation of the offset parameter (W_avg) is unclear, making it hard to understand why the combination helps.  
- The advantage and rationale for the kNN variant are not adequately explained, weakening the novelty.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art is compromised by using the validation set instead of the official test set for evaluation.  
- The validation set is small and comparisons on it lack statistical significance, giving a potential unfair advantage.  
- Claims of “comparable performance” to SOTA are undermined by differing evaluation protocols and test sets.  
- Authors should provide a principled derivation/interpretation of the weight adaptation and explain the kNN benefit.  
- Authors should repeat all comparisons on the official test set to ensure fair benchmarking and reproducibility.  
- With those revisions (methodological justification and fair evaluation), the work could be a valuable contribution despite current weaknesses.
