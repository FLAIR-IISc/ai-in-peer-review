

The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks pretrained on image classification tasks to object detection. Their method achieves 4th place in the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 object detection challenge, and the paper is well-structured and accessible, with a straightforward CNN architecture that is easy to implement. Overall, the paper is of high quality. However, two concerns require clarification before acceptance.

First, the core contribution—the adaptation method—lacks sufficient theoretical justification. The underlying motivations are not presented with sufficient rigor. For instance, Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231) states that the adapted detection weights are a summation of $W_j^c$ and $W_{\text{avg}}$, but the rationale for this formulation is unclear. The physical interpretation of this "offset parameter" is not explained, making it difficult to assess its validity. Consequently, the justification for why the kNN variant outperforms other approaches remains opaque.

Second, the comparison with competing methods is unfair because the authors evaluate on a portion of the validation set rather than the official test set. While the authors note in Footnote 1 (Line 322) that RCNN achieves similar performance on the val2 subset, the small size of this subset renders any reported performance gains statistically insignificant. This undermines the claim that the proposed adaptation method achieves comparable results to state-of-the-art approaches, as the comparisons are conducted under different evaluation protocols. The paper should explicitly address this discrepancy and provide results on the full test set to ensure a fair assessment.
