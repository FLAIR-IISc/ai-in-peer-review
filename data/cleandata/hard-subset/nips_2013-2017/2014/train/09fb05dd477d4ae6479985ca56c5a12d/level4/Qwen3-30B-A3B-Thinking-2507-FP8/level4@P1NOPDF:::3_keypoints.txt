- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style classification CNN to detection using a three-step training: full-image classification, region-based training with a background class, then integrating background weights; test uses multiple image regions.  
- Interesting and sensible approach but largely builds on existing Krizhevsky CNN rather than offering radical novelty.  
- Missing comparison to a full-image baseline (fine-tune all CNN layers using full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives).  
- Missing a MIL-based baseline (iteratively select highest-scoring positive region per image from set A).  
- Missing a simple center-bounding-box baseline (train classifier, predict detection with a center/mean box).  
- Unclear if the method outperforms these baselines—especially baseline 1—and results may mainly reflect stronger CNN features rather than a novel weakly supervised strategy.  
- Paper is well-written and clear overall, but the experiments need more precision (e.g., which methods in Table 1 use Equation 1 vs. Equation 2 and which layers are adapted).  
- Approach is novel enough for acceptance but is simple and specific to certain CNN architectures, limiting generality.  
- The problem is significant and results compelling for large-scale weakly supervised detection, but the absent baselines weaken the claimed impact.
