- Proposes LSDA to convert image classifiers into detectors using detection labels for only a subset of categories via domain-adaptation: fine-tune layers 1–7, add a background class, transfer feature-level changes, and add category-specific deltas from nearest neighbors in fc8; test with K+1 softmax and score_i − score_background.

- Differs from R-CNN by requiring detection boxes for only some classes, learning a background class, and transferring adaptations to held-out classes; contrasts with MIL-based weak supervision and standard domain adaptation.

- Evaluated on ILSVRC2013 with a 100/200 split, reports ~50% relative mAP gain on 100 held-out classes, and demonstrates scalability with a released 7.6K-category detector and an SPP-based faster variant.

- Methodology is coherent and well-motivated; category-invariant fine-tuning (with background) is the main source of gains, while category-specific nearest-neighbor deltas give smaller but significant improvements.

- Ablations and error analysis (localization vs. background vs. other errors) are informative and consistent with the classification→detection shift.

- Experimental setup is reasonable, but key baselines are missing, leaving uncertainty about whether gains stem from adaptation versus stronger CNN features or background modeling.

- Recommended missing baselines: (1) weakly supervised fine-tuning treating full images as positives with a background class, (2) MIL-based weak supervision for held-out classes, (3) simple localization heuristic using canonical/mean boxes.

- Additional analysis suggested: sensitivity to nearest-neighbor k and distance metric (e.g., cosine), per-class gains vs. frequency/object size, results with/without bbox regression and SVMs, and clarification of hard negative mining and proposal sampling during fine-tuning.

- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, but needs clearer labels in Table 1 (which layers adapted, background inclusion, k value), a concise error taxonomy definition, and clearer distinction between LSDA and the full-box upper bound.

- Originality: novel and practical framing of transferring detection adaptations to categories without boxes, despite prior work on classifier→detector transfer (R-CNN).

- Significance: addresses an important scalability bottleneck in detection; practical impact amplified by code/models and large-scale detector release.

- Pros: timely problem, simple and efficient method, solid empirical gains with informative analyses, and public release of large models and faster variants.

- Cons: missing critical baselines, modest gains from category-specific adaptation with heuristic nearest-neighbor averaging in fc8 space, and reliance on AlexNet-era architecture with limited discussion of broader generalization.

- Suggestions for improvement: add proposed baselines, expand Table 1 details, provide sensitivity analyses for k and distance metrics, compare with bbox regression/SVMs, and include per-class diagnostics by size/similarity.

- Overall recommendation: solid, practical contribution worth acceptance, but would be strengthened by the missing baseline comparisons and additional analyses.
