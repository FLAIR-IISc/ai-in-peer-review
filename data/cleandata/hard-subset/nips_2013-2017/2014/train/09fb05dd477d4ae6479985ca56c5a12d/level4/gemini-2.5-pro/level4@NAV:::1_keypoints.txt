- Tackles object detection when bounding-box annotations are scarce by adapting object classifiers to detection.  
- Proposed algorithm is elegant and simple.  
- Experimental setup: train with full supervision on 100 categories, test on 100 held-out categories with only image-level labels during training.  
- Method closes about 35% of the gap between a baseline and an oracle on held-out classes (promising performance).  
- Paper is high quality and clearly written.  
- Novelty: combines two known ideas (classifier-to-detector adaptation and category transfer); the combination is novel but not revolutionary.  
- Significance: useful problem formulation, but long-term impact unclear and future methods may outperform it; tradeoff between mAP and reduced annotations lacks an objective evaluation framework.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state which compared methods in Figure 3a use category-level labels to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Suggestion: emphasize the ~35% gap closure on held-out classes rather than the 78% figure or relative-to-oracle percentages; measure gains relative to the baseline.  
- Suggestion: provide a consolidated, direct comparison to R-CNN (e.g., a dedicated subsection or table) instead of scattered distinctions.  
- Minor issues: method name is inconsistent ("DNN" vs "DDA") and there is a typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, merits acceptance and will interest the community, though long-term relevance is uncertain.
