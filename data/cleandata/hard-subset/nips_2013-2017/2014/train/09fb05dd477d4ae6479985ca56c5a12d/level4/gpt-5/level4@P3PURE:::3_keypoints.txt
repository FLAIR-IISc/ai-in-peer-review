- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style CNN trained for image-level classification to object detection when only a subset of classes has bounding-box annotations using a three-step training procedure (classification, region-based training with background class, then integrating background and adapting subset of weights).

- Empirical results are promising and the approach is simple and logical, but performance largely builds on the strong pretrained CNN rather than a radically new idea.

- Key baselines are missing, making it unclear if the method truly outperforms simpler alternatives.

- Baseline 1: Train/fine-tune the CNN using entire images as positives and images/regions from other classes as negatives, fine-tuning all layers.

- Baseline 2: Same as Baseline 1 but use a MIL scheme that iteratively selects the highest-scoring positive region per image.

- Baseline 3: Train/fine-tune purely for classification and at test time predict a single bounding box (e.g., image-center or mean bbox) to gauge dataset difficulty.

- Experimental section lacks clarity—Table 1 should explicitly map each method to the paper’s equations and specify which CNN layers are adapted.

- Originality: sufficiently novel for acceptance but conceptually simple and tailored to specific CNN architectures.

- Significance: addresses an important problem and shows compelling large-scale detection without full bounding-box supervision.

- Overall: relevant and timely work, but without the suggested baselines and clearer experimental details, it’s hard to assess whether gains stem from the method or just strong CNN features.
