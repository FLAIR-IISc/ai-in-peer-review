- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to detection using three training steps (classification on all classes; region-based training with bounding-box classes plus background; incorporate background and adapt CNN weights) and multi-region testing.

- Method builds logically on Krizhevsky et al.’s model and produces reasonable detection results, especially notable given limited bounding-box annotations.

- Approach is simple and not radically new, raising questions about its novelty despite being useful to the community.

- Experiments are promising but lack comparisons to obvious baselines, weakening claims about superiority.

- Suggested baseline comparisons missing: using full images as positives, using images/regions from other classes as negatives, MIL selecting highest-scoring positive region, and predicting a central bounding box.

- Clarity is generally good, but the experiments section needs more detail—specifically whether methods in Table 1 apply Eq.1 or Eq.2 to subsets of CNN layers.

- Originality is moderate: specific to certain CNN architectures but sufficiently novel for consideration.

- Significance is high—the ability to get decent detection on large-scale data without bounding boxes is compelling—but results may be due to stronger CNN features rather than the proposed weakly supervised detection training.
