- Proposes a three-stage method to adapt a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to object detection with limited bounding-box labels, testing by applying the CNN to multiple image regions.  
- Method is straightforward, logical, builds on Krizhevsky et al.’s model, and achieves reasonable, promising results.  
- Lacks comparison to key baselines: (1) training/fine-tuning with full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives.  
- Lacks comparison to a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) baseline that iteratively selects highest-scoring positive regions.  
- Lacks comparison to a simple baseline of running a classifier at test time to predict a center bounding box.  
- Unclear whether the proposed approach outperforms these baselines; it may not beat baseline 1 and likely is weaker than baseline 2.  
- Paper is well-written overall, but the experiments section (especially explanations of methods in Table 1) needs clearer detail about which equation applies to which subset of CNN layers.  
- Novel enough for acceptance despite simplicity and network-specific focus.  
- Problem and results are significant and valuable, but omission of obvious baselines and possible reliance on stronger CNN features rather than a better weakly supervised detector training method are important limitations.
