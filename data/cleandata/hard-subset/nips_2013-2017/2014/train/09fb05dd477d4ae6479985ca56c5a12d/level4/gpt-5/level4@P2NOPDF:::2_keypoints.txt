- Proposes a practical framework to adapt classification CNNs for object detection; ranks 4th on ImageNet LSVRC-2013; clear writing and easy-to-implement architecture.

- Major concern: adaptation mechanism lacks principled justification—unclear motivation/derivation for modeling detection weights as W_j^c + W_avg and ambiguous meaning of the “offset” parameter.

- Suggest providing a principled interpretation (e.g., transfer learning, regularization, metric-learning), ablation studies isolating the offset’s effect, and analyses of k and neighbor-selection for the kNN variant.

- Major concern: experimental comparisons are not strictly fair—results reported on a small validation subset rather than the official test set or identical splits used by baselines, reducing statistical power.

- Recommend reporting results on the official test set or exactly the same splits as baselines and including confidence intervals or statistical tests to support claimed improvements.
