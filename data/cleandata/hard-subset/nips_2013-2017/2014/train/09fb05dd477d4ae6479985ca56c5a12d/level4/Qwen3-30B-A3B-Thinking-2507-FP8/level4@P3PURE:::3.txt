

This paper presents a method for adapting a Krizhevsky et al. CNN trained for classification (without bounding box annotations) to object detection, using a small number of classes with annotated bounding boxes. The approach involves three key steps at training time: (1) training a CNN for classification on all classes; (2) training a CNN on image regions for classes with bounding boxes (including a negative background class); and (3) integrating the background class into the full CNN model while adapting selected weights. At test time, the CNN processes multiple image regions within a test image.  

**Quality:** The paper is interesting and compelling in demonstrating reasonable detection results. However, these results largely leverage the success of Krizhevsky et al.'s model rather than introducing a radically novel technique; they remain valuable to computer vision and machine learning researchers. The general approach is simple yet logical, and the experiments are promising. The work, however, omits critical comparisons to baseline methods:  
1) Training a CNN using full images as positive examples and images/regions from other classes as negative examples, with full-layer fine-tuning.  
2) Applying the same setup as baseline 1 but using a MIL approach (iteratively selecting the highest-scoring positive region per image from set A instead of the full image).  
3) Training a classification CNN, then at test time predicting a single bounding box at the image center (e.g., the mean bounding box for ImageNet).  
It remains unclear whether the proposed method outperforms these baselines. Baseline 1 appears conceptually simpler and potentially superior, while baseline 2 may offer further advantages. Including baseline 3 is essential to contextualize the difficulty of the problem and dataset.  

**Clarity:** The paper is generally clear and well-written. The experiments section could be improved by explicitly clarifying what each method in Table 1 entails (e.g., whether Eq. 1 or Eq. 2 is applied to a subset of CNN layers).  

**Originality:** The work demonstrates sufficient novelty for acceptance, though the core approach is straightforward and specific to certain CNN architectures.  

**Significance:** The problem addressed is relevant, and obtaining decent detection results on a large-scale dataset without bounding box labels is compelling. However, the results may primarily reflect improved CNN feature representations rather than a superior weakly supervised training strategy, as the missing baselines suggest. The significance is tempered by the omission of these comparisons.
