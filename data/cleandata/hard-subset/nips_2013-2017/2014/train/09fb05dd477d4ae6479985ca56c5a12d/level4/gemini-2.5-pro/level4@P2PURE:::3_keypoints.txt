- Paper proposes adapting a classification-trained CNN to object detection using a small set of classes with bounding boxes.  
- Training stage 1: Train a CNN for classification on all image-level labels.  
- Training stage 2: Train a separate CNN on region proposals from classes with bounding boxes, adding a background class.  
- Training stage 3: Integrate the background class into the full CNN and fine-tune selected weights.  
- At test time, the final network is applied to multiple image regions to produce detections.  
- Strength: Results are compelling and the approach is simple, logical, and builds on a strong pretrained Krizhevsky-style CNN.  
- Major weakness: Experimental evaluation omits key baselines, leaving unclear whether gains are due to the method or pretrained features.  
- Missing baseline 1 (Full-Image): Fine-tune all CNN layers using full images as positives and other-class images/regions as negatives.  
- Missing baseline 2 (MIL): Use Multiple Instance Learning to iteratively select high-scoring regions within positive images for training.  
- Missing baseline 3 (Fixed-Box): At test time, predict a single fixed mean bounding box per image to establish a performance floor.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written but experiments section needs clearer mapping of equations and CNN layer subsets to results in Table 1.  
- Originality: Sufficiently novel and practical despite being straightforward and tied to a specific CNN architecture.  
- Significance: Addresses weakly supervised detection on large-scale data, but the lack of baselines weakens claims about methodological superiority vs. pretrained feature effects.
