- Summary: Proposes LSDA to convert image-classification CNNs into detectors when bounding boxes exist for only some categories, using background modeling, category-invariant fine-tuning, and kNN-based additive offsets for unlabeled classes; shows strong gains on ILSVRC-2013 and a 7.6K-category detector.

- Strength: Practical, scalable approach that reduces dependence on bounding-box annotations and can scale to large vocabularies.

- Strength: Clear training pipeline and ablations showing importance of background modeling and feature-level fine-tuning.

- Strength: Empirical improvements on held-out categories, with released code/models improving reproducibility.

- Strength: Integrates cleanly with existing CNN detection pipelines (e.g., R-CNN style).

- Weakness: Category-specific adaptation W^d = W^c + δ is not theoretically justified; rationale for additive model, meaning of δ, and choice of nearest neighbors is unclear.

- Weakness: Sensitivity and choice of k for kNN and why classification-weight neighbors predict detection shifts are not well analyzed.

- Weakness: Comparisons rely mainly on a validation split (val2) rather than the official test set, weakening cross-paper fairness and statistical confidence.

- Quality: Technically sound as an empirical method with supportive ablations, but lacks deeper analysis or alternatives (e.g., learned regressors or nonlinear mappings).

- Clarity: Paper is well organized, but Section 3.2 needs clearer notation, intuition for δ, neighbor distance choice, and what the additive offset captures.

- Originality: Novel in adapting classifiers to detectors for held-out categories; combining background learning, category-invariant fine-tuning, and kNN transfer is a useful contribution.

- Significance: High practical significance for reducing annotation cost and scaling to thousands of categories, demonstrated by a large-scale detector and speed-up variant.

- Suggestion: Provide principled justification or empirical probes for the additive model (compare to learned linear/nonlinear mappings, visualize δ, analyze neighbor metrics and k).

- Suggestion: Strengthen evaluation on the official test set or use multiple random splits with confidence intervals and significance tests versus strong baselines.

- Suggestion: Include category-level analyses showing where kNN transfer helps or fails and quantify error-type reductions.

- Suggestion: Discuss trade-offs of omitted components (SVMs, bbox regression) and whether lightweight regression could be integrated.

- Arguments for acceptance: Practical, scalable, simple, reproducible method with clear empirical gains and large-scale demonstration.

- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient justification for category-specific rule, reliance on validation splits instead of official test comparisons, and several ad hoc design choices (distance metric, k, additive assumption).
