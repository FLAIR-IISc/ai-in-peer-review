- Presents a three-stage method to adapt a pre-trained CNN for detection when only a subset of classes has bounding-box annotations.  
- Training: (1) full-image classification, (2) cropped-region CNN with an added background class, (3) integrate background weights and fine-tune selected layers; inference applies the network to multiple image regions.  
- Results are promising, but performance may mainly reflect the strength of the Krizhevsky et al. pre-trained features rather than a novel detection paradigm.  
- Evaluation is weakened by missing baseline comparisons, making it hard to quantify the method's true contribution.  
- Missing baseline 1: direct fine-tuning using full images of target classes (positives) and other images (negatives) to evaluate localization performance.  
- Missing baseline 2: a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach that iteratively selects highest-scoring regions within positive images.  
- Missing baseline 3: a simple localization baseline that predicts a single fixed bounding box (e.g., mean training-box) per test image.  
- Without these baselines, it’s unclear if the multi-stage approach outperforms simpler or more natural alternatives.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-written, but Table 1’s experimental descriptions are ambiguous and need precise configuration details (which equations, which layers fine-tuned).  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance: the approach is straightforward but valuable for weakly supervised detection.  
- Significance: tackles an important problem and shows compelling results, but missing comparisons leave open whether gains come from the method or from pre-trained features.
