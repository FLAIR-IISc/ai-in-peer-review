- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style classification CNN to detection using limited bounding-box labels via three training stages (classification, region training with background class, background integration/adaptation).

- Tests by applying the trained CNN to multiple image regions at inference time.

- Quality: Results are compelling and useful but largely benefit from the pretrained CNN rather than a radical methodological advance.

- Approach is logical, straightforward, and promising, but experiments omit key baseline comparisons.

- Missing baseline 1: fine-tuning all CNN layers using full images as positives and other-class regions as negatives.

- Missing baseline 2: same as baseline 1 but using Multiple Instance Learning (iteratively selecting highest-scoring region per image).

- Missing baseline 3: classification-trained CNN predicting a single center/mean bounding box at test time to gauge dataset/task difficulty.

- Reviewer doubts the method outperforms baseline 1 and suspects it may perform worse than MIL (baseline 2); baseline 3 is necessary to contextualize results.

- Clarity: paper is well-written, but Experimental section/Table 1 needs clarification on whether Eq.1 or Eq.2 is applied to which CNN layers.

- Originality: method shows sufficient novelty for acceptance but is simple and tailored to certain CNN architectures.

- Significance: addresses an important problem and achieves notable detection performance with limited box labels, but lack of baselines raises concern that gains stem from feature quality rather than the proposed training strategy.
