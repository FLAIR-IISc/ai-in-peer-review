- Addresses object detection by adapting object classifiers to perform detection to reduce bounding-box annotation needs.
- Proposed algorithm is simple and straightforward, which is a positive attribute.
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with bounding boxes and labels, test on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Results: method bridges ~35% of the gap between an oracle and baseline on held-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and clear.
- Originality: novel combination of adapting classifiers for detection and transferring knowledge between category sets, but not groundbreaking.
- Significance: contribution is novel but long-term impact is uncertain; method trades off mAP for using fewer bounding boxes and it’s unclear if this trade-off is worthwhile.
- Practicality concern: bounding-box annotation costs may be mitigated by increased annotation resources over time.
- Clarify Figure 3a: state which methods besides the authors’ method and R-CNN use category labels for detection.
- Recommend emphasizing that the DNN method fills about one-third of the gap (blue bars in Fig 3b) rather than the 78% figure, and correct evaluation metrics/denominators for claimed improvements.
- Add an explicit table/subsection comparing the proposed method to R-CNN and remove Figure 6 (anecdotal) to make space.
- Minor issues: inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431.
