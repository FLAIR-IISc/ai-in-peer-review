- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style classification CNN to detection using a three-stage training procedure and region-based inference.  
- Leverages a small set of classes with bounding boxes plus a learned background class to convert classification features to detectors.  
- Method is straightforward, sound, and produces reasonable detection performance on a large-scale dataset.  
- Strong results may largely reflect the power of the underlying CNN rather than a fundamentally new detection method.  
- Major experimental weakness: missing comparisons to three key baselines—(1) full fine-tuning on whole-image positives/negatives, (2) a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) region-selection approach, and (3) a naive canonical bounding-box baseline.  
- It is unclear whether the proposed approach outperforms those alternatives; justification against full fine-tuning is unconvincing and MIL may be more principled.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the experimental section lacks detail—Table 1 is ambiguous about which CNN layers are modified per Equations 1 and 2.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance but the idea is simple and may be limited to specific CNN architectures.  
- Significance: tackles an important weakly supervised detection problem and demonstrates the effectiveness of deep features, but omission of baselines leaves open whether the contribution is methodological or primarily empirical (feature-driven).
