- Presents a three-stage method to adapt a Krizhevsky CNN trained on image-level labels to object detection using limited bounding-box classes and a background class.  
- Method is conceptually simple, logically motivated, and yields promising empirical results.  
- Main weakness: missing crucial baselines, which weakens the evaluation.  
- Baseline 1: Fine-tune all CNN layers for classification using full images as positives and other images/regions as negatives.  
- Baseline 2: Implement a Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approach that iteratively selects highest-scoring positive regions for training.  
- Baseline 3: Train/fine-tune CNN for classification only and at test time apply the classifier to the whole image with a simple bounding-box prior (e.g., mean ImageNet box).  
- Unclear whether proposed method consistently outperforms these alternatives—especially baseline 1—so inclusion is crucial.  
- Experiments section needs clearer explanation (notably what applying Eq.1/2 to subsets of layers means and the methods in Table 1).  
- Novel enough for acceptance but relatively simple and likely tailored to classification-pretrained CNNs; significance is high but may largely reflect pretrained feature quality rather than the training procedure.  
- Recommendation: address baseline comparisons to convincingly demonstrate the method’s added value.
