- Addresses object detection by adapting object classifiers to detection; trains on 100 categories with boxes and tests on 100 held-out categories with only labels, closing ~35% of the gap between an oracle and a baseline on held-out categories.
- Well-written, high-quality paper.
- Combines two established adaptation concepts (classifier-to-detector and across-category adaptation); the combination is novel and interesting but not groundbreaking.
- Main contribution is the novel but incremental approach; long-term significance is uncertain since gains may be surpassed by more sophisticated methods.
- Inherent trade-off: fewer bounding boxes reduce mAP, and there is no objective metric provided to assess whether this trade-off is justified.
- Unclear whether the addressed problem is a critical long-term challenge, since increased annotation resources could mitigate it.
- Figure 3a needs clarification on which methods (besides the proposed method and R-CNN) use category labels for detection to ensure fair comparison.
- Figure 3b: emphasize that the DNN method closes roughly one-third of the gap on held-out categories relative to the baseline; the 78% figure is misleading and should be re-evaluated as relative to the baseline on held-out categories (same critique for the 50% claim).
- R-CNN comparison is scattered and insufficiently prominent; consolidate differences into a dedicated table or subsection and consider removing anecdotal Figure 6.
- Minor issues: inconsistent method naming ("DNN" vs "DDA")—standardize the name.
- Minor typo: "classi?ers" on line 431.
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, simple approach deserving acceptance and visibility at NIPS, though long-term impact remains to be determined.
