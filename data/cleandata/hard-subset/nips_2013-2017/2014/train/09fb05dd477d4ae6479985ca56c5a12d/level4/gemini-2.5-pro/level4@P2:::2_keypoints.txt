- Proposes LSDA: transform image-level classifiers into object detectors via domain adaptation to scale detection without bounding-box annotations.  
- Learns category-invariant feature-level adaptation (conv1–fc7) and category-specific classifier weight adaptation (fc8) using categories with detection data.  
- Applies learned transformation to novel categories with only image-level labels to enable detection without bounding boxes.  
- Evaluated on ILSVRC2013; shows significant improvement over classification-only baseline, including ~50% relative mAP gain on held-out categories.  
- Strong empirical validation with a thorough ablation study isolating feature adaptation, background modeling, and category-specific weight adaptation.  
- Error analysis demonstrates adaptation reduces localization and background confusion, explaining why the method works.  
- Novel and significant contribution addressing the practical gap between large-scale classification and scarce detection data.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures that clarify architecture and data flow.  
- Release of a 7,600+ category detector and code increases the work’s practical impact.  
- Category-specific adaptation rule (additive offset averaged over k-NN in weight space) is presented as a heuristic with limited theoretical justification.  
- Lacks a principled explanation for why Euclidean-distance-based averaging in classifier weight space is optimal for adaptation.  
- Experimental comparison to state-of-the-art (e.g., R-CNN) is indirect; omission of SVM training and bounding-box regression prevents apples-to-apples benchmarking.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, original, impactful work meriting acceptance despite heuristic adaptation and comparative-evaluation limitations.
