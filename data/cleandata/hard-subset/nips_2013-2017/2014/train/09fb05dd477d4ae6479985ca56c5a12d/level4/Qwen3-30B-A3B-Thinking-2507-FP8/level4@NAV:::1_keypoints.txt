- Tackles object detection by adapting object classifiers for detection to avoid per-category bounding box annotation.
- Proposed method is straightforward, well-motivated, and evaluated by training on 100 categories with boxes and testing on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Results claim the method closes 35% of the gap between a baseline and an oracle on held-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and high quality.
- Novelty comes from combining classifier-to-detector and cross-category adaptation; interesting but not revolutionary.
- Significance is limited: approach trades off mAP for fewer bounding boxes and lacks an objective metric to justify this trade-off.
- Long-term importance is uncertain since large-scale bounding-box annotation may remain feasible and outperform this approach.
- Required fix: clarify in Figure 3a which methods use category labels for detection for fair comparisons.
- Required fix: correct emphasis and wording around the main result—report gap closure specifically on held-out categories and fix misleading 78% and 50% claims.
- Required fix: provide a dedicated table/subsection comparing the proposed method to R-CNN and replace anecdotal Figure 6 with this comparison.
- Minor issues: inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431.
