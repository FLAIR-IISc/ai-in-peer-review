- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers into detectors using bounding boxes for only a subset of categories.  
- Frames classifier-to-detector conversion as domain adaptation from classification (image labels) to detection (boxes+labels).  
- Learns a category-invariant transformation by fine-tuning on few boxed categories and applies it to categories without boxes.  
- Demonstrates large-scale detection (7,604 categories) and a 50% relative mAP improvement over classification-only baselines on held-out categories.  
- Delivers a practical, fast detector (2 fps for the 7.6K model) with a released model for immediate use.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, clearly written, and provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Ablation study and error analysis convincingly show the value of each adaptation component (category-invariant adaptation > category-specific).  
- Novelty: combines domain adaptation and cross-category transfer to enable detection without bounding boxes for target categories.  
- Distinct from prior work (R-CNN, MIL) by requiring boxes for only m categories instead of all K and scaling to thousands of classes.  
- Significant practical impact by addressing the annotation bottleneck and enabling detection using abundant classification labels.  
- Absolute mAP remains below oracle, but the relative improvement and recovered portion of the performance gap are substantial.  
- Strengths: scalability, practical impact, rigorous experiments, clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: limited evaluation scope (small 100/100 simulation), needs clearer consolidation of R-CNN comparison.  
- Minor presentation issues: ambiguous Figure 3a caption and less informative qualitative examples in Figure 6.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, well-executed, and significant contribution with fixable weaknesses.
