

The authors propose a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification to object detection tasks. Their method, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), demonstrates strong performance on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, achieving a ranking of 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a simple and practical CNN architecture that is straightforward to implement. Overall, the paper presents a high-quality contribution to the field.

However, two concerns require attention. First, the adaptation methodology, which constitutes the main contribution of the paper, lacks sufficient theoretical justification. The underlying motivations for the approach are not presented in a principled manner. Specifically, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), the formulation where the detection weights are expressed as the sum of Wj^c and W{avg} is not adequately explained. The physical meaning of this "offset parameter" remains unclear, and consequently, it is not evident why the kNN variant would yield better results.

Second, the comparison with other methods is not entirely fair as the authors used part of the validation set rather than the test set for evaluation. The authors mention in a footnote (Line 322) that R-CNN achieves similar performance on the val2 set, but due to the limited size of this validation subset, any performance gains cannot be statistically validated. The comparisons between LSDA and other methods are conducted under different experimental settings, which complicates direct performance assessment.

The proposed adaptation method shows promising results that are comparable to state-of-the-art models, but the paper would benefit from a more rigorous theoretical foundation for the adaptation approach and fairer experimental comparisons that use the same evaluation protocol as other methods.
