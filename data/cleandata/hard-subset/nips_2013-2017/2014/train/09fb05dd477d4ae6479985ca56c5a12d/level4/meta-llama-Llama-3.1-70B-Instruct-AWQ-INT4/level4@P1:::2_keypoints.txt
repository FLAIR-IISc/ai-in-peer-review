- Proposed LSDA framework to adapt image-classification CNNs into object detectors using a three-stage process (category-invariant adaptation, category-specific adaptation, detection).  
- Evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 and shows significant improvement over a baseline.  
- Achieves ~50% relative mAP boost for categories without bounding-box annotations.  
- Method is simple to implement and computationally inexpensive; CNN structure is interesting for large-scale detection.  
- Paper is clearly presented and overall of good quality.  
- Main concern: adaptation method lacks principled justification and clear motivations.  
- Unclear why detection weights are modeled as original weights plus a learned offset and what the offset represents.  
- Unclear why the kNN variant performs better; needs explanation of underlying assumptions.  
- Comparison fairness concern: authors used part of the validation set rather than the test set for comparisons.  
- Small val2 set limits statistical significance of claimed gains and comparisons to R-CNN.  
- Recommend including test-set comparisons against state-of-the-art to strengthen evaluation.  
- Related work coverage is thorough but should clarify differences from MIL and other weakly supervised detector approaches.  
- Conclusion: promising and practical method with comparable performance to SOTA under different settings, but needs better methodological justification and more comprehensive evaluation.
