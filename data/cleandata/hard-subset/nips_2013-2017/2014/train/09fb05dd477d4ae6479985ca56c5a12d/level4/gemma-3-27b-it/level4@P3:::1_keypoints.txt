- Paper proposes LSDA: learn a generic transformation to turn image classifiers into object detectors to scale detection to many categories with few bounding boxes.  
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels and evaluate on 100 held-out categories with labels only.  
- Reported results: substantial improvement over raw classifier baseline, closing ~35% of the gap to an oracle detector on held-out categories.  
- Quality/Clarity: well-written, technically sound, clearly motivated, and easy to follow with minor clarifications needed.  
- Originality: novel combination of existing ideas—learning a generalized classification-to-detection transform—positioned well against prior work.  
- Significance: promising step toward large-scale detection with limited boxes, but trade-off between mAP and annotation cost requires further study.  
- Strength: novel and effective approach leveraging classification data for detection.  
- Strength: clear presentation and logical structure.  
- Strength: promising empirical gains on held-out categories.  
- Strength: practical, simple, and efficient algorithm.  
- Strength: models and code are publicly released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the performance vs. annotation-cost trade-off and comparison with training detectors on fewer boxes.  
- Weakness: comparison to R-CNN is acknowledged but not detailed enough; needs clearer differences/advantages.  
- Weakness: performance claims (percentages) should be clarified by reporting absolute mAP and relative gains versus baseline.  
- Weakness: inconsistent terminology (e.g., "DNN" vs. "LSDA") throughout the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, promising results, clear writing, and available resources for reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: gains may not justify annotation trade-offs; comparison to related work (R-CNN) is incomplete.  
- Specific suggestion: clarify Figure 3a about which methods use category labels during detection versus only bounding boxes.  
- Specific suggestion: refocus results discussion on absolute mAP improvements and relative gains from the baseline.  
- Specific suggestion: add a dedicated R-CNN comparison subsection/table, even if it means removing another figure.  
- Specific suggestion: use consistent terminology (e.g., consistently use "LSDA").  
- Overall recommendation: accept — valuable, practical contribution that would benefit from the suggested clarifications.
