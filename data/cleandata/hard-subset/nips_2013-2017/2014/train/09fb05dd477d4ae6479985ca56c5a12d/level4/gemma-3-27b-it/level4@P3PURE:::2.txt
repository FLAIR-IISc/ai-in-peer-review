The authors propose a framework to adapt deep neural networks pre-trained for image classification to the task of object detection. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset demonstrates that the proposed method achieves 4th place in the object detection challenge. This paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN structure is both interesting and simple to implement. Overall, the paper is of good quality. However, there are two primary concerns that the authors should address.

First, the adaptation method presented as the main contribution of this work requires stronger justification. The underlying motivations are not presented in a principled manner. For example, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), the rationale for weighting the adapted detection task with a summation of W<sub>j</sub><sup>c</sup> and W<sub>avg</sub> is unclear. The physical meaning of this ‘offset parameter’ is not explained. Consequently, the reason for the improved performance of the kNN variant remains unclear. 

Second, the comparison to other methods utilizes a portion of the validation set rather than the standard test set, leading to an unfair comparison. The authors acknowledge in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN exhibits similar performance on the val2 set, but note that the small size of this set prevents statistically significant justification of any observed gain. 

In summary, the authors propose a framework with competitive performance for adapting networks from classification to detection. However, the comparisons are made under differing settings, and the proposed adaptation method itself requires further explanation and justification.
