The authors present a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification to object detection. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset shows that the method ranks 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly written and generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN architecture is interesting and straightforward to implement. Overall, the paper’s quality is good. Nevertheless, there are two issues the authors should address.

First, as the central contribution of this work, the proposed adaptation approach needs stronger justification. The underlying motivation is not laid out in a principled manner. For example, in Section 3.2 (Page 5 Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are defined as the sum of Wj^c and W{avg}. The physical meaning of this “offset parameter” is not explained. Consequently, it is also unclear why the kNN variant performs better.

Second, in the comparison with other methods, the authors used part of the validation set rather than the test set, making the comparison less fair. The authors note in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but given the small size of this set, the reported performance gain is not statistically justified.

In summary, the paper proposes a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification to object detection. The method achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art models, but the comparisons are made under different settings, and the proposed adaptation method requires clearer explanation.
