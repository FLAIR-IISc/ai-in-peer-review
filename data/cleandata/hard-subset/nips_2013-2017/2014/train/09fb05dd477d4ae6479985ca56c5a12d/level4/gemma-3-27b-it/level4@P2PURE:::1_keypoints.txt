- Addresses detection label scarcity by adapting object classifiers for detection; simple, straightforward method.
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, evaluate on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Results: method closes roughly 35% of the gap between an oracle detector and a baseline on held-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and clear.
- Novelty: combines classifier-to-detector adaptation with cross-category transfer; interesting but not groundbreaking.
- Significance concern: unclear if mAP trade-off versus fewer bounding boxes is justified and long-term importance is debatable.
- Clarify Fig. 3a: explicitly state which methods (besides the authors’ method and R-CNN) use category labels for detection for fair comparison.
- Clarify key result in Fig. 3b (blue bars ≈ one-third gap); correct/avoid reporting the 78% figure and the 50% claim—these metrics are misleading or improperly computed.
- Provide a dedicated table/subsection clearly contrasting the proposed method with R-CNN; remove Figure 6 (anecdotal examples) as recommended.
- Minor issues: inconsistent method naming ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431. 
- Overall recommendation: solid, novel contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance though long-term impact is uncertain.
