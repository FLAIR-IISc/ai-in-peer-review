- Presents a three-stage method to adapt a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to detection using limited bounding-box-annotated classes (train on full images, train on region proposals with background class, incorporate background and adapt weights; apply to test regions).  
- Results are interesting and fairly compelling, showing reasonable detection performance at large scale without full bounding-box labels.  
- Performance appears to build heavily on the strength of the Krizhevsky pretrained model rather than introducing a radically new concept.  
- The approach is simple, logical, and likely valuable to computer vision and ML researchers despite limited novelty.  
- Experiments are promising but incomplete due to missing comparisons to important baselines.  
- Suggested baselines missing: (1) train/fine-tune on full images as positives and other-class images/regions as negatives, updating all CNN layers.  
- Suggested baseline (2): a Multiple Instance Learning scheme that iteratively selects the highest-scoring positive region per image.  
- Suggested baseline (3): train/fine-tune for classification and at test time predict a single bounding box (e.g., mean ImageNet box) to characterize dataset difficulty.  
- Unclear whether the proposed method outperforms these baselines or whether gains come from CNN features rather than a better weakly-supervised training approach.  
- Paper is clear and well-written overall, but the experiments section (especially explanations for methods in Table 1 and which equations/layers are applied) needs clarification.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance but the approach is simple and tailored to certain CNN types.  
- Significance is high because addressing detection with limited annotations is important, but omission of obvious baselines weakens the claim that the method itself is superior.
