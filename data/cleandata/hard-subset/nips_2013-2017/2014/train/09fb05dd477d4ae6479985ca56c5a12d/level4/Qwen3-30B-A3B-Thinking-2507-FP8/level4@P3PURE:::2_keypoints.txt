- Paper proposes a framework to adapt image-classification CNNs to object detection.  
- Evaluation on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection yields 4th place.  
- Presentation is clear and accessible; adaptation structure is interesting and straightforward.  
- Overall paper quality judged as good.  
- Primary contribution (adaptation method) lacks principled justification.  
- Unclear why adapted detection weights are defined as W_j^c + W_avg in Section 3.2.  
- Physical meaning of the "offset parameter" is not explained.  
- Rationale for why the k-NN variant outperforms other approaches is unconvincing.  
- Baseline comparisons use part of the validation set rather than the test set, compromising fairness.  
- Small validation subset prevents statistically significant conclusions about performance gains.  
- Comparisons are conducted under non-identical settings versus state-of-the-art methods.  
- The adaptation mechanism requires more rigorous theoretical explanation to support design choices.
