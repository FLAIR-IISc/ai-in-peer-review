- Problem and approach: Adapts object classifiers to detection to reduce annotation effort; trains on 100 fully-annotated categories and evaluates on 100 novel categories with only labels, achieving ~35% of the gap between oracle and baseline on unseen categories.  
- Quality/clarity: Well-written with a clear, sound methodology.  
- Originality: Novel combination of established adaptation and transfer techniques; not a fundamentally new direction but a worthwhile exploration.  
- Contribution/significance: Provides a simple, valuable baseline; unlikely to remain state-of-the-art but has potential long-term interest.  
- Key limitation: Unjustified trade-off between detection accuracy (mAP) and number of predicted bounding boxes.  
- Practical relevance concern: The need to solve annotation-cost disparity may be debatable given possible resource allocation alternatives.  
- Figure 3a issue: Clarify which methods use category labels for detection beyond the proposed method and R-CNN.  
- Result reporting issue: Emphasize the central result (≈1/3 of the gap for left-out categories) and correct misleading statistics (the 78% and 50% figures are misrepresented).  
- Comparison to R-CNN: Add a dedicated table/subsection detailing differences from R-CNN; consider removing Figure 6 (anecdotal) to make room.  
- Minor issues: Inconsistent naming (“DNN” vs “DDA”); typo on line 431.  
- Recommendation: Accept for presentation at NIPS.
