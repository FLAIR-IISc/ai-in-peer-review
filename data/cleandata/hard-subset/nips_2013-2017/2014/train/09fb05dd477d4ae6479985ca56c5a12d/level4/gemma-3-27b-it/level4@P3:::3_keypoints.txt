- Paper proposes LSDA: fine-tuning a classification CNN to become a detector for categories without bounding boxes by learning category-invariant and category-specific adaptations.  
- Leverages pre-trained Krizhevsky et al. network and demonstrates detection for 7,600+ categories on ImageNet LSVRC-2013.  

Strengths:
- Tackles the important problem of scarce bounding-box annotations for large-scale detection.  
- Practical, straightforward method building on existing CNN architectures and training techniques.  
- Shows strong empirical gains (+50% relative mAP) over a raw classifier baseline.  
- Demonstrates scalability by producing detectors for thousands of categories.  
- Releases code and models, aiding reproducibility.  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks comparisons to key baselines: full CNN fine-tuning with negatives, MIL region-selection, and naive center/mean-box detector.  
- Ablation study (Table 1) and Equations 1–2 are unclear about which layers/methods were applied.  
- Improvements may partly stem from CNN feature power rather than the proposed adaptation; missing baselines prevent disentangling.  
- Error analysis is superficial and does not deeply explain causes of failures.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses a significant, relevant problem with practical impact.  
- Method produces clear improvements and is scalable.  
- Builds on established techniques and provides reproducible resources.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Insufficient baseline comparisons weaken the evaluation of novelty and effectiveness.  
- Potential CNN feature bias is not adequately controlled or discussed.  
- Ablation/clarity issues reduce interpretability of contributions.  

Overall assessment:
- Solid, practically valuable work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add baselines, clarify ablations, and discuss feature bias.  

Rating: Accept (with minor revisions).
