- Paper addresses adapting object classifiers for detection to scale bounding-box availability; trains on 100 box-labeled categories and evaluates on 100 label-only categories.  
- Method is simple, well-executed, and closes ~35% of the performance gap between baseline detector and oracle.  
- Manuscript is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Combines established ideas (classifier→detector adaptation and cross-category adaptation) into a novel though not groundbreaking approach.  
- Significance is promising as a first exploration, but long-term impact is uncertain due to inherent mAP limits from reduced bounding-box usage.  
- Paper lacks a principled evaluation/discussion of the trade-off between annotation effort and detection performance, and assumes annotation costs cannot be mitigated by future large-scale labeling.  
- Clarify Figure 3a: explicitly state which methods use category labels to aid detection for fair comparisons.  
- Correct performance reporting: emphasize the gap-closure metric (~35%) and fix misrepresented claims of 78% (line 341) and 50% (line 425).  
- Consolidate differences between DDA and R-CNN into a dedicated table/subsection; remove anecdotal Figure 6 to improve clarity.  
- Minor fixes: method name is DDA (not DNN); correct typo "classi?ers" (line 431).  
- Recommendation: accept—novel, simple, actionable first step toward scalable detection; minor issues are easily fixed.
