Paper proposes adapting a Krizhevsky classification CNN to object detection with limited bounding-box classes via region training, adding a background class, and region-based testing.

Well-motivated: tackles scaling object detection to many categories.

Builds on strong CNN features (Krizhevsky); approach is simple and logical but not radically novel.

Experiments are promising and suggest effectiveness, but largely leverage existing CNN strengths.

Missing important baselines that weaken evaluation.

Baseline 1 missing: use full images as positives and other-class images/regions as negatives, fine-tune all CNN layers.

Baseline 2 missing: MIL-style training that iteratively selects highest-scoring positive regions per image.

Baseline 3 missing: train classifier and at test time predict a single center/mean bounding box.

Clarity: paper is generally clear but experiments section needs clearer explanation of methods in Table 1 and more implementation/hyperparameter/resource details.

Originality: sufficiently novel for acceptance but specific to certain CNN architectures.

Significance: results on large-scale data without box labels are compelling, but gains may be due to improved CNN features rather than the proposed weakly-supervised method.

Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, simple/logical approach, promising results, clear background and exposition.

Arguments against acceptance: lacks critical baseline comparisons, experimental clarity issues, and potential confound from general CNN feature quality.
