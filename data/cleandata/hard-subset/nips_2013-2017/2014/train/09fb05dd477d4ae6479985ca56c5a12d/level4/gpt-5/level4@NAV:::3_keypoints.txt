- Proposes repurposing a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to perform detection with bounding-box labels for only a subset of classes, by adding a background class and adapting a subset of weights; test-time uses region proposals.
- Interesting and reasonably convincing empirical performance, but gains largely leverage the strength of the Krizhevsky model rather than a fundamentally new idea.
- Overall training strategy is simple, sensible, and likely useful to vision and ML researchers.
- Missing key baseline 1: treat full images as positives, use images/regions from other classes as negatives, and fine-tune all CNN layers.
- Missing key baseline 2: same as above but use MIL to iteratively select the highest-scoring positive region per image.
- Missing key baseline 3: train/fine-tune CNN for classification and at test time output a single bounding box at the image center (e.g., mean ImageNet bbox).
- Paper is clear and well written, but the experimental section (especially Table 1) needs clarification on which equations/layer subsets were applied.
- Original enough for acceptance but conceptually straightforward and tailored to specific CNN architectures.
- Problem and results are significant, but omission of obvious baselines leaves open whether improvements come from stronger CNN features rather than the proposed weakly-supervised detection method.
