- Tackles scaling object detection to many categories by avoiding expensive bounding-box annotations.
- Proposes LSDA: frame classifier-to-detector conversion as a domain adaptation problem.
- Learns a generic transformation from classifiers to detectors using source categories with boxes.
- Applies the learned transformation to adapt classifiers for target categories that lack bounding boxes.
- Builds on CNN and R-CNN advances but generalizes adaptation to novel classes without per-class fine-tuning.
- Significance/originality: practical, novel solution addressing annotation bottleneck for large-scale detection.
- Technical quality: method is sound, ablation studies thorough, and shows substantial mAP improvement (+50%) over a strong baseline.
- Error analysis: adaptation mainly reduces localization errors and background confusion—key detection challenges.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, figures illustrate the pipeline effectively, and authors plan to release code/models.
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation lacks strong justification—averaging nearest-neighbor delta vectors is plausible but not well motivated or theoretically grounded.
- Weakness: experimental scope limits comparisons—evaluation on an ILSVRC2013 validation split and omission of SVM/bbox-regression make direct SOTA comparisons difficult.
- Suggestion: provide more principled explanation of the "offset" vector and position LSDA relative to other weakly-supervised/transfer detection methods (e.g., MIL).
