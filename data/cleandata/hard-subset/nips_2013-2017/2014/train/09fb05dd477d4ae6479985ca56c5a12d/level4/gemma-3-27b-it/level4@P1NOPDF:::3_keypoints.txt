- Paper adapts a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to perform object detection for a subset of classes with bounding boxes using a three-stage training pipeline.
- Three training stages: (1) CNN trained for classification on all classes, (2) CNN trained on regions for classes with bounding boxes with a background class, (3) integrate background class into full CNN and adapt some weights.
- At test time the CNN is applied to multiple image regions to produce detections.
- Results are solid and convincing but largely build on the Krizhevsky model rather than a radically new method.
- Approach is simple, logical, and likely valuable to vision and ML researchers.
- Experiments are promising but miss comparisons to several important baselines.
- Missing baseline 1: treat full images as positives and other-class images/regions as negatives, fine-tune all CNN layers.
- Missing baseline 2: Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) that iteratively selects highest-scoring positive regions per image.
- Missing baseline 3: train CNN for classification and at test time predict a centered/mean bounding box to gauge dataset difficulty.
- Reviewer is uncertain the method outperforms baseline 1 and suspects it may underperform baseline 2.
- Clarity is generally good, but the experiments section should better explain methods in Table 1 and whether Equations 1/2 apply to subset of layers.
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance though the approach is simple and architecture-specific.
- Significance: addressing detection with limited bounding-box labels on large-scale data is important, but gains may reflect stronger CNN features rather than a superior weakly-supervised training method.
