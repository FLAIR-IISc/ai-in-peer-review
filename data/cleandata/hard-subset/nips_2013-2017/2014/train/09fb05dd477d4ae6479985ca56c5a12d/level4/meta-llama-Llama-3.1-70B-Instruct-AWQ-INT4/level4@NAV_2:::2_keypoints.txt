- Proposes a framework to adapt image classifiers into object detectors using deep neural networks.  
- Demonstrates a 50% relative mAP improvement over a baseline that uses raw classifier weights on proposal regions (ILSVRC-2013).  
- Paper is clearly presented, easy to follow, and the proposed CNN structure is simple to implement.  
- Main contribution: formulates the problem as domain adaptation from classifier (source) to detector (target) and learns a general transformation.  
- Strength: can adapt classifiers to detectors without requiring bounding-box annotations for every category.  
- Strength: produces detection networks for all categories whether or not bounding boxes are available at training time.  
- Experimental results on ILSVRC2013 show significant performance gains, supporting practical usefulness.  
- Concern: adaptation method lacks principled justification—motivation and interpretation of the offset Wj^c + Wavg are unclear.  
- Concern: unclear why the kNN variant outperforms other variants due to missing explanation.  
- Concern: experimental comparison may be unfair because authors used part of the validation set instead of the test set, risking statistical insignificance.  
- Quality: technically sound, well-supported claims, clear writing, but limited originality since it builds on existing domain adaptation and deep learning work.  
- Significance: results are important and likely to be adopted or extended by practitioners and researchers.
