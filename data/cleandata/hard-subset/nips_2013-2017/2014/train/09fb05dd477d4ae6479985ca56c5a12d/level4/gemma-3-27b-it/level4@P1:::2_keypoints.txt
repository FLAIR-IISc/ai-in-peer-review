- Proposes a framework to adapt image-classification DNNs for object detection and reports competitive results on ImageNet LSVRC-2013.
- Paper is clearly written; proposed CNN structure is interesting and straightforward to implement; overall quality is good.
- Main concern: the adaptation method lacks a fully principled justification and its motivations are not clearly presented.
- Specific ambiguity: unclear why detection weights are W_c^j + δ_avg and what the offset parameter physically means; k-NN variant rationale is not explained.
- Second concern: comparisons use only part of the validation set instead of the dedicated test set, which may lead to unfair comparisons.
- Authors note R-CNN has similar validation performance but argue limited set size makes gains not statistically significant.
- Suggested improvements: clarify the adaptation strategy (theoretical/intuition) and perform more rigorous comparisons on a common test set.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important scaling problem, achieves strong detection performance, is well-written, and provides public code/models.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical justification for adaptation, potentially unfair evaluation protocol, and an ablation study that could be expanded.
