- Problem & approach: Proposes LSDA to convert ImageNet classifiers into detectors using limited bounding-box labels via domain adaptation (category-invariant fine-tuning + category-specific residual transfer) and explicit background modeling.
- Score computation: Detection scores computed by subtracting learned background score from per-class scores.
- Novelty & relation: Builds on R-CNN and domain-adaptation ideas; novel contribution is transferring a learned additive residual to held-out categories via nearest neighbors.
- Scalability & impact: Demonstrates a 7.6K-category detector and releases models/code, addressing scaling of detection to thousands of classes.
- Simplicity & efficiency: Method is straightforward to implement in standard CNN toolchains and is computationally efficient (fine-tuning in hours; faster test-time variant provided).
- Empirical support: Ablations show benefits of background modeling and shared-layer fine-tuning; ~50% relative mAP gain on held-out categories and useful error analysis reducing localization/background confusions.
- Strength of transfer idea: Category-specific residual transfer to unlabeled classes appears practically useful and novel relative to prior work.
- Lack of principled justification: No clear rationale for the additive residual model at the output layer or for why residuals transfer across semantically/visually similar classes.
- Missing δ analysis: Paper lacks analysis of δ statistics (sparsity, magnitude, alignment) and correlation with class similarity measures.
- Neighbor choice concerns: Unjustified use of L2 in fc8 for nearest neighbors; needs comparison to alternative similarity metrics and embeddings.
- Sensitivity to k: Paper does not report sensitivity to number of neighbors k (including extremes like k=1 or k=|B|).
- Alternative transforms unexplored: No experiments with other transforms (affine per-class, low-rank/shared factors, meta-learned regressors) to predict δ.
- Evaluation limitations: Main comparisons use val2 rather than ILSVRC test server and a small split; claims of parity with R-CNN need stronger evidence.
- Baseline mismatches: Unclear whether baselines include SVMs and bbox regression; need matched R-CNN variants for fair comparison.
- Bounding-box regression missing for held-out classes: Suggests training a category-agnostic bbox regressor on labeled categories and applying it to held-out classes to reduce localization errors.
- Sensitivity to labeled set B: No study on how performance varies with different selections or sizes of labeled categories B (m and composition).
- Clarity issues: Section 3.2 needs clearer notation, and a schematic contrasting classification vs detection logits and the role of background subtraction would help.
- Score calibration justification: Needs brief justification for using background subtraction instead of softmax and report any calibration effects on mAP.
- Suggested additional experiments: Provide δ motivation/empirical analysis, sensitivity to k and similarity metrics, multi-split or test-server evaluation with confidence intervals, matched baselines, and category-agnostic bbox regression results.
- Overall assessment: Well-written, important, simple and effective method with meaningful gains and high practical impact; leans toward acceptance conditional on stronger justification of residual transfer and more robust evaluation.
