- Paper proposes adapting object classifiers for detection to avoid extensive bounding-box annotations, with a simple and straightforward approach.
- Experimental protocol: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 disjoint categories with labels only.
- Main quantitative result: method closes ~35% of the gap between oracle (full box supervision) and baseline (no adaptation) on test categories.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Originality: combines known ideas (classifier→detector adaptation and cross-category adaptation) in a novel way not previously explored.
- Significance: promising but likely to be surpassed; approach sacrifices mAP by using fewer bounding boxes and lacks an objective metric to justify this trade-off.
- Questioned whether bounding-box scarcity is a critical long-term bottleneck, since resources could scale label collection.
- Figure 3a must explicitly indicate which methods (besides the authors’ and R-CNN) use category labels for detection for fair comparisons.
- Key-result presentation is misleading: emphasize 35% gap closure (on left-out 100 categories) rather than the 78% figure or the "50% improvement" claim.
- Comparison to R-CNN is scattered; provide a dedicated table/subsection clearly explaining differences instead of anecdotal Figure 6.
- Inconsistent method naming: labeled both "DNN" and "DDA" (lines 127, 431).
- Typo: "classi?ers" should be "classifiers" (line 431).
- Overall recommendation: reasonable, novel approach deserving acceptance for the NIPS community despite uncertain long-term significance.
