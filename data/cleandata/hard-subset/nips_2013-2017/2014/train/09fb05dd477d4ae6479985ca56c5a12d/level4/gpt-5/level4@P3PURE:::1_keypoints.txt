- Addresses object detection with few bounding boxes by adapting classifiers into detectors; trained on 100 categories with boxes and evaluated on 100 held-out categories with labels only, closing ~35% of the gap to an oracle on held-out categories.  
- Paper is well-written and of good quality.  
- Combines two existing adaptation ideas; the combination is novel and interesting but not groundbreaking.  
- Significance is uncertain: approach loses mAP by using fewer bounding boxes and no objective assessment is provided to judge that trade-off; long-term importance of the problem is unclear.  
- Clarify Figure 3a: specify which methods do or do not use category labels to assist detection for fair comparison.  
- Clarify Figure 3b: the DNN result appears ~one-third of the way from baseline to oracle on left-out categories — this should be highlighted rather than the 78% oracle claim; use improvement-over-baseline on left-out categories as the metric.  
- Add a dedicated table/subsection clearly articulating differences from R-CNN (suggest replacing Figure 6 with this comparison).  
- Minor fixes: clarify whether the method is called “DNN” or “DDA” and fix the typo on line 431 (“classi?ers”).  
- Overall recommendation: accept — simple and timely work likely to be of interest to the NIPS community, though it may be soon surpassed.
