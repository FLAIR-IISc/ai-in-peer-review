- Paper proposes adapting object classifiers to detection to reduce need for bounding-box annotations, with a simple algorithm.
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels and evaluate on 100 held-out categories with only labels.
- Main quantitative result: method closes roughly 35% of the gap between an oracle detector and a baseline on held-out categories.
- Paper is well-written and clear.
- Combines existing ideas (classifier-to-detector adaptation and transfer across categories) in a novel but not revolutionary way.
- Provides a valuable baseline for limited-bbox detection, though long-term significance is uncertain as annotation methods evolve.
- Unclear whether the trade-off between mAP performance and reduced bounding-box need is advantageous.
- Clarify Figure 3a by explicitly stating which methods use category labels for detection beyond the proposed method and R-CNN.
- Emphasize the blue-bar result (~one-third gap closure) more than the 78% figure, which is misleading because it’s not solely on held-out categories and ignores baseline performance.
- Similar concern about the 50% improvement claim—needs clearer context.
- Provide a dedicated table or subsection comparing to R-CNN; current comparisons are scattered and lack prominence.
- Consider removing Figure 6 (anecdotal examples) to make room for a more substantial R-CNN comparison.
- Minor issues: inconsistent terminology (DNN vs DDA) and a typo on line 431 (“classi?ers”).
- Overall recommendation: solid, promising work; recommend acceptance for presentation at NIPS.
