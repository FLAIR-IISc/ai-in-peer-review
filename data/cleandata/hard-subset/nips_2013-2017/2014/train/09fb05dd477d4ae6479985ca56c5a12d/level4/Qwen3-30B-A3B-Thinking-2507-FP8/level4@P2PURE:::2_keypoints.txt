- Proposes a framework to adapt image-classification DNNs to object detection; evaluated on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 and ranked 4th in the detection challenge.  
- Paper is clearly presented, easy to follow, with an interesting and straightforward CNN structure; overall good quality.  
- Main contribution (the adaptation method) lacks principled justification and clear underlying motivations.  
- It is unclear why adapted detection weights are defined as the sum W_j^d = W_j^c + W_avg (Section 3.2); the physical meaning of this "offset parameter" is not explained.  
- No explanation for why the kNN variant yields better performance than the baseline.  
- Comparisons to other methods use part of the validation set rather than the test set, making the comparisons unfair.  
- Small validation set size undermines statistical justification for reported gains; the footnote about RCNN's similar val performance is insufficient.  
- Overall, performance claims may be misleading due to differing evaluation settings, and the adaptation method requires clearer explanation.
