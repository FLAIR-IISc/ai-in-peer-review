- Paper proposes LSDA: convert a CNN classifier into an object detector via domain-adaptation-style transformation learned from categories with bounding-boxes.  
- Method learns a generic mapping (feature adaptation + background model) on a subset with detection data and transfers it to many categories with only image-level labels.  
- At test time the adapted network runs on region proposals to perform detection.  
- Quality: method is technically sound and results show reasonable large-scale detection, building logically on R-CNN and Krizhevsky et al.  
- Major weakness: missing comparisons to key baselines, making it hard to assess the true contribution.  
- Suggested baseline comparisons: (1) weakly-supervised full-image positives with CNN fine-tuning, (2) MIL variant selecting top region per positive image, (3) trivial baseline predicting canonical bounding box from a fine-tuned classifier.  
- Without those baselines, reported gains may simply reflect strong CNN features rather than the adaptation strategy.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, but ablation descriptions (Table 1) need more detail on exactly which layers/methods are adapted.  
- Originality: sufficiently novel—framing classifier-to-detector conversion as a transferable learned transformation is a new and useful combination of ideas.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem (scaling detection to thousands of categories) and demonstrates a viable direction, but impact is limited by the lack of stronger experimental validation.
