This paper presents a method for adapting a Krizhevsky CNN network that is trained for classification (without bounding box annotations) to work for object detection, given a small number of classes that have annotated bounding boxes. At train time, the approach 1) trains a CNN for classification on images of all classes, 2) trains a CNN on image regions on classes labeled with bounding boxes, while adding a negative background region class, and 3) adds the background class to the full CNN model while adapting some of the CNN weights. At test time, the CNN is run on multiple image regions in a test image.

The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to follow the authors' argument. The authors provide a thorough background on the problem of object detection and the challenges of scaling it to large numbers of categories. They also clearly explain their proposed method, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), and how it addresses these challenges.

The quality of the paper is good, with promising experimental results on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge. The authors demonstrate a significant improvement in detection performance over a baseline of using raw classifier weights on object proposal regions. However, the paper could benefit from additional comparisons to other baselines to further establish the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

One potential concern is that the paper may be capitalizing on the improved performance of CNN features rather than introducing a fundamentally new way of training object detectors from weak supervision. To address this, the authors could consider adding more baselines that compare their approach to other methods that use CNN features, such as training a CNN for classification and then using the features for detection.

In terms of originality, the paper is sufficiently novel to be accepted, although the basic approach is simple and specific to certain types of CNN networks. The authors' contribution lies in applying this approach to the problem of large-scale object detection and demonstrating its effectiveness.

The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses a relevant problem in computer vision and provides a promising solution. The fact that decent detection results were obtained on a large-scale dataset without bounding box labels is compelling, and the authors' approach has the potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories that lack bounding box annotations.

To improve the paper, the authors could consider adding more details to the experiments section, such as elaborating on the explanation of what each method in Table 1 refers to. Additionally, they could consider adding more baselines to compare their approach to other methods, including those that use CNN features.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses a relevant problem in computer vision and provides a promising solution.
* The experimental results are promising, with a significant improvement in detection performance over a baseline.
* The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to follow the authors' argument.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may be capitalizing on the improved performance of CNN features rather than introducing a fundamentally new way of training object detectors from weak supervision.
* The paper is missing some obvious baselines, which could further establish the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* The authors could consider adding more details to the experiments section to improve the clarity of the paper.
