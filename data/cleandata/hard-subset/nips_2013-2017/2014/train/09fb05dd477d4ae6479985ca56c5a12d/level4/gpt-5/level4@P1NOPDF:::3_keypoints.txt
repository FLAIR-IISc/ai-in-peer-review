- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky-style image-classification CNN to object detection using a small subset of bounding-box annotations via a three-step training that adds a background class and adapts weights.  
- Interesting and compelling results that largely leverage the strength of the Krizhevsky model rather than a radically new algorithm; approach is simple and logical.  
- Experiments are promising but omit several important baselines that could challenge the claimed gains.  
- Missing baseline 1: train/fine-tune CNN on full images as positives and images/regions from other classes as negatives, fine-tuning all layers.  
- Missing baseline 2: same as (1) but use MIL to iteratively select the highest-scoring positive region per image instead of using the full image.  
- Missing baseline 3: train/fine-tune CNN for classification only and at test time predict a single bounding box (e.g., image-center or mean ImageNet box) to gauge dataset/task difficulty.  
- Reviewer unsure the proposed method outperforms these baselines—may not beat baseline 1 and likely worse than baseline 2.  
- Paper is generally clear and well written, but the experimental section (especially Table 1) needs clearer definitions of methods and which equations/layers are applied.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance but the idea is simple and somewhat architecture-specific.  
- Significance: solving detection without bounding boxes on a large dataset is valuable, but omitted baselines raise the possibility that improvements stem from stronger CNN features rather than a better weakly-supervised detection approach.
