- Tackles adapting object classifiers into detectors to obtain bounding boxes at scale comparable to category labels.  
- Proposes a simple, straightforward algorithm to adapt classifiers for detection.  
- Trains on 100 categories with boxes and tests on 100 held-out categories with only labels.  
- Results bridge ~35% of the gap between a baseline and an oracle on the held-out 100 categories.  
- Paper is well written and of good quality.  
- Idea is novel in combining classifier-to-detector and cross-category adaptation, though not revolutionary.  
- Contribution is useful but likely to be surpassed; trade-off in mAP due to fewer boxes is a key limitation with no objective metric to justify it.  
- Questionable long-term importance: bounding-box scarcity may be solved by resource allocation.  
- Clarify which methods in Figure 3a use category labels for detection to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Emphasize the blue-bar DNN result (one-third gap reduction on held-out categories) rather than the misleading 78%/50% figures; evaluation should focus on held-out categories and account for baseline.  
- Include a dedicated table/subsection explicitly contrasting the method with R-CNN and consider removing Figure 6 to make room.  
- Minor: clarify method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and fix typo on line 431.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, recommend acceptance; likely of interest though long-term impact uncertain.
