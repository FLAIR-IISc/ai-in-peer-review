- Proposes adapting a Krizhevsky classification CNN (trained without bounding boxes) into an object detector using a small set of classes with bounding-box annotations.

- Builds on Krizhevsky et al.'s model; approach is simple, logical, and achieves reasonable results on large-scale detection.

- Technical quality is sound and claims are supported by experiments; strengths and weaknesses are evaluated.

- Important baselines are missing, e.g., using full images as positives and other-class regions as negatives.

- Should compare to training/fine-tuning all CNN layers rather than only a subset.

- Should compare to Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) approaches and to predicting a single center bounding box at test time.

- Clarity is generally good, but Table 1’s methods need clearer explanation about whether Equation 1 or 2 is applied to which CNN layers.

- Originality: novel application of classification-to-detection adaptation at large scale.

- Significance: addresses a relevant problem and shows compelling results without bounding-box labels.

- Caveat: gains may be due to improved CNN features rather than a fundamentally better weakly-supervised detector training method.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, significant results, tackles an important problem.

- Arguments against acceptance: missing baselines, possible attribution of gains to CNN features, and insufficient comparisons to alternative methods.
