- Proposes adapting Krizhevsky et al.’s classification CNN for detection using three training steps (classification on all classes, region-based CNN on bbox-labeled classes with a background class, then integrating/fine-tuning) and tests on multiple image regions.  
- Addresses a relevant weakly supervised detection problem and shows promising results on a large-scale dataset leveraging a strong CNN backbone.  
- Method is conceptually straightforward and builds on established CNN success rather than introducing radical new techniques, but offers practical value.  
- Experimental evaluation critically lacks three key baselines: Full-image baseline (fine-tune on whole-image positives/negatives), MIL-based baseline (iterative top-region selection), and Center-bounding-box baseline (predict centered mean bbox at test time).  
- Without those baselines, it’s unclear if gains come from the proposed adaptation or merely from the strong pre-trained CNN features.  
- Paper is well-written and structured, but experimental descriptions (e.g., Table 1 labels like “Region-based” or “Full-image”) need precise specification of which equations/layers are applied for reproducibility.  
- Originality is sufficient for acceptance: adapting a pre-trained classifier via region-based training with a background class is novel in this context.  
- Significance is high due to real-world relevance of minimizing bbox annotations, but is partially undermined by missing baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on adding the three missing baselines (especially the simple Center-bbox baseline); without them the work risks being seen as incremental.
