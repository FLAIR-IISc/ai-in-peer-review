

The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification tasks to object detection tasks. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset shows the proposed method achieves 4th place in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally easy to follow, with an interesting and straightforward CNN structure. Overall, the paper's quality is good. However, two concerns require addressing.  

First, as the main contribution, the adaptation method lacks sufficient justification. The underlying motivations are not presented in a principled manner. For instance, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are defined as the sum of $W_j^c$ and $W_{\text{avg}}$. The physical meaning of this "offset parameter" is not explained, making it unclear why the kNN variant performs better.  

Second, comparisons with other methods use part of the validation set rather than the test set, rendering the comparison unfair. The authors note in a footnote (Line 322) that RCNN achieves similar performance on the validation set, but due to the small size of this set, any performance gain lacks statistical justification. While the proposed method achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art models, the comparisons are conducted under different settings, and the adaptation method requires better explanation.
