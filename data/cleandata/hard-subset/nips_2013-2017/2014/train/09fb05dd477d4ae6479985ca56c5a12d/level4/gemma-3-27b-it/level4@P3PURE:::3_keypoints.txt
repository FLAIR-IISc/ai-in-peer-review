- Paper proposes adapting a Krizhevsky CNN (three-stage training: classification, region-based with background, then full-model adaptation) to detection using limited bounding-box labels and region proposals at test time.
- Results are promising and useful to the community, but largely build on the pre-trained Krizhevsky model rather than a radically new technique.
- Approach is conceptually simple and logical, with reasonably compelling experimental results.
- Crucial baseline comparisons are missing; the reviewer recommends adding three specific baselines (full-image fine-tuning, MIL over proposed regions, and classification with center-box prediction at test time).
- Reviewer doubts the proposed method’s advantage over baseline 1 and expects baseline 2 (MIL) may outperform it; baseline 3 would help gauge dataset difficulty.
- Experiments section needs clearer explanation, especially what applying Eq.1/2 to a subset of CNN layers means and the methods in Table 1.
- Novelty is sufficient for acceptance but the method is relatively simple and tied to certain CNN architectures.
- Significance is high—tackles an important weakly supervised detection problem—but absent baselines raise concerns that gains stem from CNN feature power rather than the proposed training method.
