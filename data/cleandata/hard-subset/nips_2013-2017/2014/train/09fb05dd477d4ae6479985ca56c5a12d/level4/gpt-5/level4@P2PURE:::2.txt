The authors propose a framework to adapt deep neural networks trained for image classification to object detection. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset shows that the method ranks 4th in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly written and generally easy to follow, and the proposed CNN architecture is interesting and straightforward to implement. Overall, the paper is of good quality. However, I have two concerns.

First, as the main contribution of the work, the adaptation method requires stronger justification. The underlying motivation is not presented in a principled manner. For example, in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231), it is unclear why the weights for the adapted detection task are the sum of Wj^c and Wavg. The physical meaning of this “offset parameter” is not explained. Consequently, it is also unclear why the k-NN variant performs better.

Second, in the comparisons with other methods, the authors evaluate on part of the validation set rather than the test set, making the comparison unfair. The authors note in a footnote (Line 322) that R-CNN has similar performance on the val2 set, but given the small size of this set, the reported performance gain is not statistically justified.

In summary, the paper presents a framework to adapt deep neural networks from image classification to object detection. The method achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art models, but the comparisons are made under different settings, and the proposed adaptation method requires clearer explanation.
