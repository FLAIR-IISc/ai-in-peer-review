The authors present a framework for adapting deep neural networks pre-trained for image classification to the task of object detection, achieving a strong 4th-place ranking on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 object detection challenge. The paper is well-written and generally accessible, with an interesting and straightforward CNN architecture. Overall, this is a solid contribution. However, two areas would benefit from further development.

First, the core adaptation method requires stronger justification. The underlying rationale for design choices isn’t thoroughly explained. Specifically, the summation of weights *Wj^c* and *W{avg}* in Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231) lacks a clear explanation of its purpose or physical interpretation as an “offset parameter.” Consequently, the advantage of the kNN variant remains unclear. 

Second, the comparison to existing methods is potentially flawed due to the use of a subset of the validation set rather than the standard test set. While the authors acknowledge similar performance of RCNN on the *val2* set (Footnote, Line 322), the small size of this set limits the statistical significance of any observed gains.

In summary, the authors demonstrate a promising framework with competitive performance; however, a more detailed explanation of the adaptation method and a fairer comparative evaluation would strengthen the work.
