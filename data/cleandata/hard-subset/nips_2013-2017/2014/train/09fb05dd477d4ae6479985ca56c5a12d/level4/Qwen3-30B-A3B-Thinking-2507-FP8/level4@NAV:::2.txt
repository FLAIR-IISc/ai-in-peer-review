

The authors propose a framework for adapting deep neural networks trained on image classification to object detection tasks. Evaluation on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 dataset shows the method achieves 4th place in the object detection challenge. The paper is clearly presented and generally accessible, with an interesting and straightforward-to-implement CNN structure. Overall, the paper quality is good. However, two concerns require addressing.  

First, the core adaptation method lacks sufficient theoretical justification. The underlying motivations are not rigorously presented. For instance, Section 3.2 (Page 5, Line 231) does not explain why the adapted detection weights use a summation of $W_j^c$ and $W_{\text{avg}}$, nor does it clarify the physical meaning of this "offset parameter." Consequently, the rationale for the superior performance of the kNN variant remains unclear.  

Second, the comparison with other methods uses only a portion of the validation set (not the test set), resulting in an unfair comparison. While the authors note in footnote (Line 322) that RCNN shows similar performance on the val2 set, the limited size of this subset prevents statistically significant validation of the performance gain.  

Although the proposed adaptation method achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art models, the comparisons are conducted under inconsistent settings, and the adaptation mechanism requires more thorough explanation.
