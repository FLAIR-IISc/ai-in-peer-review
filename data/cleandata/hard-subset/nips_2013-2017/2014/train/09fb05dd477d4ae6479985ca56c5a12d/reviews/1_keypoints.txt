- Paper proposes adapting object classifiers for detection to avoid needing bounding boxes at scale.
- Algorithm is simple and straightforward.
- Experimental setup: train on 100 categories with boxes+labels, test on 100 held-out categories with labels only.
- Results: fills ~35% of the gap between oracle and baseline on the left-out categories.
- Paper is high quality and clearly written.
- Originality: combines two existing adaptation ideas; novel and interesting but not ground-breaking.
- Significance: contribution is novel but likely to be surpassed; trade-off between fewer boxes and mAP is unclear and hard to judge.
- Question raised about long-term importance of the problem (bounding-box scarcity may be solved by resource investment).
- Clarify in Figure 3a which methods (besides the authors’ method and R-CNN) use category labels for detection for fair comparison.
- Main result should be the DNN performance in Figure 3b (about one-third between baseline and oracle); the 78% and 50% claims are misleading for reasons noted.
- Provide a dedicated table/subsection clearly specifying differences from R-CNN (suggest removing Figure 6 to make space).
- Minor issues: inconsistent method name ("DNN" vs "DDA") and a typo on line 431 ("classi?ers").
