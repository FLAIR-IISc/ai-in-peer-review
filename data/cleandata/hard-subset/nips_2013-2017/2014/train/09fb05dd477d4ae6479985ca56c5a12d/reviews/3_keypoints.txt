- Method: Adapts a Krizhevsky CNN trained for classification to detection by (1) classification training on all classes, (2) training on region proposals for annotated classes with a background class, and (3) adding the background class and adapting some CNN weights; test by running CNN on multiple image regions.
- Quality: Interesting and compelling results, largely building on the strength of the Krizhevsky model rather than a radical new idea.
- Simplicity: Approach is simple and logical, likely valuable to vision and ML researchers.
- Missing baselines: Paper lacks comparisons to three obvious baselines that could challenge its claims (full-image positives with region negatives, MIL-based region selection, and center/mean-box classifier baseline).
- Baseline concerns: Reviewer is unsure the proposed method outperforms baseline 1 and likely baseline 2, so comparisons are needed.
- Dataset difficulty: Including the simple mean-box baseline (baseline 3) is important to show how easy/hard the dataset/problem is.
- Clarity: Paper is well written, but the experiments section needs clearer explanations—especially what each method in Table 1 corresponds to (which equations/layers applied).
- Originality: Sufficiently novel for acceptance but relatively specific to certain CNN architectures and not highly innovative.
- Significance: Problem and results are significant and valuable for the community.
- Attribution concern: Results might be due to strong CNN features rather than a superior weakly-supervised detector training method.
