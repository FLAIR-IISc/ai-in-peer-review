- Proposes LSDA: adapt image classifiers into detectors using bounding boxes for only a small subset of categories via category-invariant feature transformation.  
- Treats classifier-to-detector conversion as domain adaptation, applying learned transformation to many categories lacking bounding boxes.  
- Demonstrates significant relative improvement (≈50% rel. mAP) on ImageNet held-out categories and builds a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem addressing scalability of detection to many categories.  
- Strength: technically sound with ablation studies showing importance of category-invariant (feature) adaptation over category-specific steps.  
- Strength: impressive 7.6K detector result, practical scalability, and released implementation/code.  
- Major weakness: lacks direct comparison to MIL-based weakly-supervised detection methods on the same ImageNet setup, undermining novelty claims.  
- Major weakness: category-specific adaptation via nearest neighbors (k=10) is unjustified theoretically and lacks sensitivity analysis or safer similarity metrics.  
- Major weakness: limited significance—50% relative gain is on held-out categories with low absolute mAP (16.15%), and few/no examples shown for the 7.4K unannotated categories.  
- Minor issue: "fast detector" (2 fps) claim lacks comparison to other fast detectors (e.g., Fast R-CNN, SSD).  
- Minor issue: background adaptation rationale could be clearer about why background class is critical for detection vs classification.  
- Minor issue: error analysis reduces background errors but lacks quantitative PR curves or measures for held-out categories.  
- Recommendation: compare LSDA to MIL methods under the same weak supervision to establish novelty.  
- Recommendation: justify and analyze choice of k in Eq.1 or use a principled similarity metric (e.g., WordNet distance).  
- Recommendation: report absolute mAPs for baselines and provide more detection examples for the 7.4K held-out categories.  
- Recommendation: discuss computational cost of adaptation vs training detectors from scratch.  
- Overall: promising and technically plausible but the lack of MIL comparison and unvalidated category-specific adaptation weaken the claimed novelty and impact.  
- Recommendation outcome: Weak Accept pending stronger empirical comparisons and justification of adaptation strategy.
