- Proposes LSDA: adapt image-level classifiers into object detectors using bounding boxes for only a small subset of categories.  
- Key insight: learn a generic classification→detection transformation from categories with boxes, then apply it to categories without boxes.  
- Addresses a major scaling bottleneck by reducing need for bounding-box annotations across many categories.  
- Builds on R-CNN but removes the requirement for detection data for every category.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains (~50% relative mAP) over a classification-only baseline on ImageNet LSVRC-2013.  
- Shows a compelling 7.6K-category detector as a practical scalability demonstration.  
- Strong experimental evaluation and ablation study; category-invariant adaptation (background + conv fine-tuning) gives the largest boost.  
- Error analysis indicates LSDA reduces background confusion and localization errors compared to classification-only methods.  
- Well-motivated as a domain-adaptation problem with appropriate related-work citations.  
- Clear, well-organized writing and sufficiently explained algorithm; some implementation specifics are missing.  
- Original contribution: novel application of domain adaptation to the classification→detection shift not handled by prior work.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior domain-adaptation and MIL methods, noting those require detection data for all categories or lack ImageNet evaluation.  
- High significance: enables detection for tens of thousands of categories with only image-level labels, lowering annotation cost.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why background adaptation is so crucial despite its large impact.  
- Weakness: category-specific adaptation gives only marginal gains; choice of nearest-neighbor similarity needs justification and analysis.  
- Weakness: "fast detector" implementation (fast proposals, spatial pyramid pooling) lacks technical detail and timing breakdowns.  
- Suggestion: compare more directly with recent detection-focused domain-adaptation methods and provide theoretical guarantees or deeper analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical, and novel contribution suitable for NIPS, with minor clarifications and additional comparisons recommended.
