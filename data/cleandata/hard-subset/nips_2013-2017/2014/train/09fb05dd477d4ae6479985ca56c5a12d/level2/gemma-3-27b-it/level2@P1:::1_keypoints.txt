- Proposes LSDA, a method to transform image classifiers into object detectors to address scarce bounding-box annotations.  
- Core idea: learn a mapping from classification to detection feature space using categories with both types of data, then apply it to classifiers lacking detection data.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains, including a 50% relative mAP improvement over raw classifier weights.  
- Shows scalability by building a 7.6K-category detector using ImageNet leaf nodes.  
- Builds on R-CNN insights but introduces a task-specific domain adaptation rather than only fine-tuning.  
- Correctly situates work relative to MIL and domain adaptation literature, noting complementarity and novelty in adapting both domain and task.  
- References are comprehensive and the paper is well written with available code/models for reproducibility.  
- Good error analysis: adaptation reduces localization/background errors and shifts errors toward finer classification mistakes.  
- Lack of comparison to stronger, more recent detection frameworks (e.g., Faster R-CNN, YOLO) and advanced domain adaptation methods.  
- No direct comparison to MIL-based methods at scale; such evaluation would strengthen claims.  
- The choice of k for category-specific adaptation (k=10) appears arbitrary and needs more justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Method seems to struggle on fine-grained category distinctions (e.g., lemon vs. orange), indicating limits for subtle classification tasks.  
- Test-time simplifications (no SVMs or bbox regression) are noted but the performance trade-offs are not fully quantified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant scaling problem, introduces a novel effective method, is reproducible, and demonstrates scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons to state of the art, under-justified implementation choices, and potential weaknesses on fine-grained categories.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to compare against recent detectors and justify the k-selection.
