- Proposes LSDA, a method to transform image classifiers into object detectors without bounding-box annotations for all categories.  
- Claims LSDA enables large-scale detection, potentially for tens of thousands of categories.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvement: ~50% relative mAP gain over a baseline on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise presentation and well-explained technical details.  
- Provides thorough experiments including ablation studies and error analysis to elucidate strengths and weaknesses.  
- Novel approach addressing the key challenge of scarce bounding-box annotations.  
- Results convincingly show potential for scaling detection to many categories.  
- Method requires substantial classification data, which may not exist for all categories.  
- Lacks direct comparison to other state-of-the-art object detection methods, limiting assessment of relative performance.  
- Paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and future work directions.  
- Overall judgment: significant contribution with promising results, but needs more discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, impressive experimental results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on classification data, missing SOTA comparisons, and insufficient limitations/future-work discussion.
