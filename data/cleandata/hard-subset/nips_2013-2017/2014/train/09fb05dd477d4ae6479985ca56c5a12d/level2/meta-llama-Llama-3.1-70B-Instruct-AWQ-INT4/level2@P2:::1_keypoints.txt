- Proposes LSDA, a method to convert image classifiers into object detectors to scale detection to many categories with limited bounding-box labels.  
- Uses domain adaptation with category-invariant and category-specific adaptation steps to bridge classification→detection gaps.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough related work.  
- Technical contributions are significant and novel for domain adaptation in detection.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, includes ablation analysis, and outperforms a classification-only baseline.  
- Demonstrates practical value by addressing large-scale detection with limited bounding-box annotations.  
- Main limitation: assumes classification data is available for all categories, which may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and runtime costs.  
- Overall strong: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid experiments, tackles an important real-world problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic data availability assumption and missing complexity analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze computational complexity, handle missing classification data, and add more experiments across datasets/scenarios.
