- Proposes LSDA, a novel algorithm that adapts image classifiers into object detectors to enable large-scale detection.  
- Casts detection at scale as a domain adaptation problem and uses a multi-stage transformation from classification to detection networks.  
- Targets scalability to tens of thousands of categories where bounding-box annotations are impractical.  
- Well-written paper with clear explanation of LSDA components and key insights.  
- Experiments on ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection show significant improvement over using raw classifier weights on object proposals.  
- Provides a detailed error analysis that clarifies the method’s strengths and limitations.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant and practical challenge in object detection scalability.  
- Strength: Demonstrates effectiveness empirically and explains the approach clearly.  
- Strength: Useful error analysis aids understanding and future improvements.  
- Weakness: Relies heavily on the availability of large-scale classification data, which may not exist for all categories.  
- Weakness: Still requires a subset of categories with bounding-box annotations, limiting applicability in some domains.  
- Weakness: Lacks a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art object detection methods.  
- Originality: Novel use of domain adaptation to transform classification networks into detection networks.  
- Significance: Potential to enable detection across many more categories, impacting vision, robotics, and autonomous systems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective approach with strong experimental validation and insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Data requirements and limited annotated subset remain constraints; comparative evaluation is incomplete.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but authors should address limitations and provide more detailed comparisons in future work.
