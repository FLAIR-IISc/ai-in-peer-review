The paper "LSDA: Large Scale Detection through Adaptation" proposes a novel algorithm for adapting image classifiers into object detectors, leveraging the availability of large-scale classification data and limited detection data. The authors address the challenge of scaling object detection to tens of thousands of categories, where collecting bounding box annotations is impractical. By casting the task as a domain adaptation problem, they develop a multi-stage algorithm that learns to transform a classification network into a detection network.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the key insights and components of the LSDA algorithm. The experiments on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge demonstrate the efficacy of the approach, showing a significant improvement over a baseline of using raw classifier weights on object proposal regions. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the types of errors made by their system, which is helpful in understanding the strengths and limitations of the approach.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in object detection, and the proposed algorithm has the potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories.
* The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments.
* The analysis of errors made by the system is helpful in understanding the strengths and limitations of the approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper relies heavily on the availability of large-scale classification data, which may not always be available for all categories.
* The algorithm requires a small subset of categories to have bounding box annotations, which may still be a limitation for some applications.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art object detection algorithms.

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm for adapting image classifiers into object detectors, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The use of domain adaptation techniques to transform a classification network into a detection network is a new and interesting approach.

Significance:

* The paper has the potential to enable object detection for tens of thousands of categories, which could have significant impacts on various applications, including computer vision, robotics, and autonomous vehicles.
* The proposed algorithm could also be useful for other tasks that require adapting models to new domains or tasks.
