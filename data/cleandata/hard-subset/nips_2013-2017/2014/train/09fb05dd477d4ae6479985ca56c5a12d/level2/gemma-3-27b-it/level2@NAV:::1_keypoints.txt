- Addresses scaling object detection by leveraging abundant image-level labels and scarce bounding-box annotations.  
- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation to convert image classification networks into object detectors.  
- Builds on prior work (feature transfer from classification to detection) and targets the annotation cost bottleneck.  
- Algorithm clearly decomposed into category-invariant and category-specific adaptation steps.  
- Experimental results on ImageNet detection show a significant improvement (~50% relative mAP) over classification-only baseline.  
- Ablation study effectively isolates contributions of different components.  
- Demonstrates scalability by producing a 7.6K-category detector.  
- Public release of models and code is a valuable community contribution.  
- Error analysis is present but limited; deeper investigation into failure causes is needed.  
- Lacks quantitative comparison with other weakly-supervised detection methods (e.g., MIL approaches).  
- Reliance on ImageNet hierarchy may limit applicability to datasets with different taxonomies.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, urging more thorough comparisons to related weakly-supervised work and expanded error analysis.
