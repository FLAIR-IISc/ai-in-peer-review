- Proposal: LSDA adapts image classifiers into object detectors when bounding boxes exist for only a subset of categories.  
- Method: Two components — category-invariant adaptation via fine-tuning shared CNN layers + explicit background class, and category-specific adaptation by transferring the average fine-tuning delta from nearest-neighbor categories in fc8 space.  
- Evaluation/results: On ILSVRC13 (100 trained / 100 held-out) LSDA gives ~50% relative mAP gain over using classifier weights, demonstrates a 7.6K-category detector and an SPP-based faster variant.  
- Relation to prior work: Positioned relative to R-CNN (requires boxes for all classes), domain adaptation (feature/model adaptation), and weakly supervised/MIL methods.  

- Methodological quality: Technically sound with ablation studies isolating background modeling, shared-layer fine-tuning, and output-layer transfer, plus paired t-test significance testing.  
- Error analysis: Informative Loc/BG/Oth breakdown consistent with the classification→detection task shift hypothesis.  
- Limitations in evaluation: Quantitative validation for the 7.6K model is missing and no large-scale comparison to strong weakly supervised (MIL) baselines.  

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear, well-organized, with implementation details and released code.  
- Minor clarity issues: Need to clarify sets A/B indexing, report sensitivity to k in nearest-neighbor transfer, and specify fine-tuning schedules/hyperparameters for reproducibility.  

- Originality: Novel to adapt unseen categories to detection via shared-layer transfer plus learned background; nearest-neighbor delta transfer is a simple plausible heuristic.  
- Significance: Addresses box-annotation bottleneck and is practically useful for scaling detectors despite modest absolute mAP on held-out classes.  

- Acceptance arguments: Clear, effective formulation with strong ablations; practical impact with large-scale detector, faster variant, and released code/models; solid error analysis reducing localization/background confusions.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited quantitative validation for the 7.6K detector and no MIL comparison; category-specific transfer yields small gains and nearest-neighbor choice is not deeply justified; reliance on region proposals without bbox regression limits performance.  

- Suggestions: Provide quantitative evaluation of the 7.6K model on a measurable subset and compare with MIL-based training for held-out categories.  
- Suggestions: Explore learned meta-mappings (regression from classifier to detector weights) using fc8 or semantic/WordNet features instead of nearest neighbors.  
- Suggestions: Reintroduce bbox regression or apply lightweight distillation to recover performance without losing adaptability.  
- Suggestions: Evaluate generalization to other datasets or domains.
