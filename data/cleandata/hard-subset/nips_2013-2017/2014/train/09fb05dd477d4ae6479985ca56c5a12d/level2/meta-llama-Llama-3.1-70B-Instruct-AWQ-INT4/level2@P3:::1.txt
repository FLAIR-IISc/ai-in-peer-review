**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel approach, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), to adapt image classifiers into object detectors. The key idea is to learn a transformation from a classification network to a detection network using a small subset of categories with bounding box annotations, and then apply this transformation to adapt classifiers for categories without bounding box annotations. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the ImageNet LSVRC-2013 detection challenge, achieving a 50% relative improvement in mean Average Precision (mAP) over a baseline of using raw classifier weights on object proposal regions.

The paper builds upon previous work on deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for object classification and detection. The authors acknowledge the work of Girshick et al.
