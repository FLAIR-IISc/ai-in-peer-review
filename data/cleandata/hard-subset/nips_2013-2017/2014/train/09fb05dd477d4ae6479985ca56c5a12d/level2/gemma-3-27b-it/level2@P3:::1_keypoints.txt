- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from image-level classifiers to detectors to enable detection for categories without bounding-box annotations.  
- Builds on R-CNN and reduces dependence on bounding boxes, scaling to a 7.6K-category detector with improvements on ImageNet.  
- Novel framing of classifier-to-detector conversion as a domain-adaptation learning problem.  
- Experimental results are convincing; demonstrates clear benefits on held-out categories and includes an effective ablation study.  
- Provides error-type analysis (localization, background confusion, other) and explores a faster 2fps detector variant.  
- Releases code and models to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Choice of network architecture (Krizhevsky et al.) is not sufficiently motivated or contrasted with alternatives.  
- Nearest-neighbor selection for category-specific adaptation (Eq.1) appears ad-hoc and lacks exploration of other similarity metrics or strategies.  
- Statistical-significance claims (paired t-test) are made without reporting test statistics or p-values.  
- Reports a "50% relative mAP boost" without specifying the baseline mAP, obscuring the absolute improvement.  
- Statement about requiring SVMs to achieve R-CNN performance is somewhat misleading; comparisons are not precisely framed.  
- Acknowledges an oracle performance gap but does not quantify it numerically.  
- Error-analysis figures (Fig.5) would be more informative with numerical percentages in addition to qualitative examples.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important scaling problem, proposes a well-motivated novel approach, shows strong empirical gains, provides ablation and reproducibility assets.  
- Arguments against acceptance: some method components lack detailed justification/analysis, statistical reporting is incomplete, and performance comparisons could be more precise.  
- Overall: strong paper with relatively minor issues; revision addressing these points is recommended.
