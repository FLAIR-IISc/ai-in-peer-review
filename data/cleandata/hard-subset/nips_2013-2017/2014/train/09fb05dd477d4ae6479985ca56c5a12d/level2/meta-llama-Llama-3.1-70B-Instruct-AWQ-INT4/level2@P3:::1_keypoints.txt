- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from a classification network to a detection network using a small set of box-annotated categories to adapt classifiers without bounding boxes.
- Achieves ~50% relative improvement in mAP over a baseline using raw classifier weights on object proposals (ImageNet LSVRC-2013).
- Builds on prior CNN detection/classification work (e.g., R-CNN, Krizhevsky) and connects to domain adaptation, multiple-instance learning, and detection literature.
- Novel method for converting classifiers into detectors; significant contribution to computer vision.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on a challenging dataset with substantial quantitative gains.
- Scalable potential to enable detection for tens of thousands of categories.
- Well-written paper with clear explanations and intuition.
- Evaluation limited to a single dataset, leaving generality and robustness uncertain.
- Assumes availability of classification data for all categories, which may not always be true.
- Requires significant computational resources, which could limit applicability.
- Original approach: use a small annotated subset to learn a transferable transformation applied to many classes.
- Significant implications for object detection and further evidence of deep CNNs’ effectiveness.
- Pro acceptance: novel, effective, and potentially scalable approach.
- Con acceptance: limited evaluation, data availability assumptions, and computational cost concerns.
- Recommend evaluating on multiple datasets to demonstrate robustness.
- Recommend exploring alternative architectures or training methods for adaptation.
- Recommend investigating ways to reduce computational cost (smaller networks, more efficient training).
