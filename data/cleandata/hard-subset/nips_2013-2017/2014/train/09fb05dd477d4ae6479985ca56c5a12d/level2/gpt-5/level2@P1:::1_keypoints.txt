- Addresses scaling object detection to thousands of categories using plentiful image-level labels and scarce bounding boxes via LSDA.  
- LSDA learns a transformation from classification to detection using a deep CNN, with category-invariant and category-specific adaptations.  
- Category-invariant adaptation: fine-tune shared layers for detection and learn an explicit background class.  
- Category-specific adaptation: estimate per-class weight deltas from detected categories and transfer them to held-out classes via k-NN averaging in classification weight space.  
- Experiments on ILSVRC2013 show ~50% relative mAP gain on 100 held-out categories versus using classification weights on region proposals.  
- Demonstrates scalability with a 7.6K-class detector using only 200 box-annotated categories and a faster SPP-based variant.  
- Builds on CNN classification and R-CNN detection but differs by not requiring detection labels for all categories.  
- Contrasts with MIL weakly supervised detection and domain adaptation methods; adaptation of deep features for unseen categories is novel.  
- Methodology is sound: clear decomposition into background modeling, shared feature adaptation (layers 1–7), and simple per-class delta transfer.  
- Nearest-neighbor transfer in fc8 is computationally light and compatible with large K.  
- Ablation shows most gain comes from feature adaptation plus background class; category-specific transfer adds a smaller but significant boost.  
- Error analysis supports that LSDA reduces localization and background confusion errors.  
- Missing comparisons: weakly supervised MIL baseline on held-out classes, R-CNN-style SVMs and bbox regression on LSDA features, and alternative δ transfer schemes (WordNet/embeddings/sparse coding).  
- Experimental protocol needs clarification: hyperparameter selection (k), no tuning on val2, and precise train/val splits.  
- Training time claim (5.5 hours vs ~3 days) is compelling but requires hardware and per-stage timing details for reproducibility.  
- Clarity is generally good but δ layer descriptions could be streamlined with an algorithm box or schematic.  
- Request specification: how k was selected, whether weighted/similarity-based averaging tried, and exact fine-tuning hyperparameters.  
- Need details on test-time scoring/calibration (softmax vs logits, per-class thresholds) and cross-category NMS for large-scale detection.  
- Originality is moderate-to-high: category-invariant transformation + per-class delta transfer for unseen categories is novel.  
- High practical significance for large-vocabulary detection; code/models release is valuable.  
- Pros: addresses scalability, simple and scalable approach, solid ablations/error analysis, large-scale demo and promised code/models.  
- Cons: limited alternative-transfer and weakly supervised comparisons, simplistic category-specific transfer with modest gains, and missing protocol/runtime/per-class analyses.  
- Suggestions: evaluate semantic/visual similarity-based δ transfer, add MIL and class-agnostic bbox regression baselines, report sensitivity to k and similarity metrics, and show per-class correlation with similarity to B.  
- Ask authors to clarify calibration across 7.6K classes, potential negative transfer, and provide exact hardware/timings and throughput (proposals per image, FPS).  
- Recommendation: weak accept to accept — important problem, simple effective approach, but stronger baselines and more protocol detail would strengthen the paper.
