- Proposes LSDA: learn a transformation from image-level classifiers to object detectors using abundant image labels and few bounding boxes.  
- Frames the problem as domain adaptation and applies the learned transform to categories without box annotations.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on ImageNet, with significant mAP gains on held-out categories.  
- Shows a large-scale 7.6K-category detector and provides a fast 2 fps detector variant for practicality.  
- Method builds naturally on R-CNN and recent deep learning/domain adaptation advances.  
- Experimental setup includes ablation studies that isolate component contributions.  
- Code release enhances reproducibility and is a notable strength.  
- Well-motivated solution to the bottleneck of scarce bounding-box labels for large-scale detection.  
- Related work section is somewhat superficial; stronger connections to semi-supervised, transductive, GAN-based, and self-training transfer methods are needed.  
- The nearest-neighbor adaptation (Eq. 1) lacks sufficient justification; Euclidean distance in fc8 may not be the best similarity metric.  
- Would benefit from exploring alternative similarity measures or neighbor selection methods for transfer.  
- Ablation study shows feature adaptation is most important but lacks deeper analysis explaining why.  
- Error analysis is useful but missing quantitative breakdowns of false positives and misclassification rates.  
- Visualizations are helpful but could be improved by showing misclassifications alongside correct examples.  
- Some sections (e.g., Section 3.2) could be streamlined for clarity and readability.  
- Training details for the 7.6K detector—how labeled and unlabeled models were combined—are insufficiently explicit.  
- Overall: novel, effective, and impactful approach with convincing results; weaknesses are addressable and merit minor revisions.
