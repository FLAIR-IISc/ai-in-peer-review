## Review of LSDA: Large Scale Detection through Adaptation

This paper addresses the crucial challenge of scaling object detection to a large number of categories, leveraging the abundance of image-level labels compared to the scarcity of bounding box annotations. The core idea, Large Scale Detection through Adaptation (LSDA), is to learn a transformation from image classification networks to object detectors, transferring knowledge from categories with bounding box data to those without. This is a compelling approach, and builds upon the earlier work of Girshick et al. demonstrating the effectiveness of transferring features from classification to detection. The authors correctly identify the bottleneck of annotation cost and propose a practical solution using readily available classification data.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the LSDA algorithm, breaking it down into category-invariant and category-specific adaptation steps. The experimental evaluation on the ImageNet detection challenge is convincing, demonstrating a significant (50% relative mAP) improvement over a baseline relying solely on classification weights.  The ablation study effectively highlights the contribution of each component of the algorithm. Furthermore, the ability to scale to a 7.6K detector is a notable achievement, showcasing the potential of this approach.  The release of models and software (lsda.berkeleyvision.org) is a valuable contribution to the community.

However, the paper has a few weaknesses. The analysis of error types, while insightful, is somewhat limited. A more thorough investigation of *why* the model fails on certain categories, beyond just stating confusion with similar categories, would be beneficial. While the authors acknowledge the performance gap compared to models trained with full bounding box supervision, a more quantitative comparison against other weakly-supervised detection methods (like MIL approaches mentioned in the Related Work) would strengthen the evaluation. The reliance on the ImageNet tree structure for scaling might limit the generalizability of the approach to datasets with different hierarchical structures.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant problem in scaling object detection.
*   Presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm (LSDA).
*   Demonstrates strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark.
*   Provides publicly available models and code.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison with other weakly-supervised detection methods.
*   Analysis of error types could be more detailed.
*   Reliance on ImageNet hierarchy might limit generalizability. 

**Overall:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, this is a strong paper with a clear contribution. The proposed LSDA algorithm offers a practical and effective way to leverage large-scale classification data for object detection, and the results are compelling. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically encouraging a more thorough comparison to related work in weakly-supervised detection and a more detailed error analysis.
