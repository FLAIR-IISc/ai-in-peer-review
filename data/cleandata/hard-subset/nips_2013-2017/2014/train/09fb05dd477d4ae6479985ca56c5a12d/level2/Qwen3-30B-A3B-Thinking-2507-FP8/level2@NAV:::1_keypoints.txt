- Introduces LSDA: a method to convert image classifiers into object detectors so detection can scale to thousands of categories using classification data without bounding boxes for all categories.
- Frames the classifier-to-detector transformation as a domain adaptation problem.
- Key methodological insights: modeling background is crucial; category-invariant information can be transferred; category-specific differences between classifiers and detectors must be handled.
- Experimental result: 50% relative mAP improvement over a classifier-only baseline on held-out categories, statistically significant (p=0.017).
- Demonstrates scalability with a 7.6K-category detector implementation.
- High technical quality and clarity; methodology well-explained and experiments comprehensive.
- Implementation details are sufficient for reproducibility.
- Authors acknowledge limitations: still requires bounding boxes for a subset of categories and does not reach oracle performance with full annotations.
- Original and meaningful advancement over prior work (e.g., R-CNN) by enabling detector creation for held-out categories.
- Significant practical impact by leveraging abundant classification data to alleviate detection annotation bottlenecks.
- Main weaknesses: limited discussion of failure cases and insufficient comparison to MIL-based approaches.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution; recommendation for acceptance.
