The paper "LSDA: Large Scale Detection through Adaptation" presents an innovative approach to object detection that addresses the challenge of scaling to large numbers of categories with limited bounding box annotations. The authors propose an algorithm that learns to transform an image classifier into an object detector by adapting to the differences between classification and detection tasks.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The introduction effectively sets up the problem and provides context for the proposed solution. The related work section is thorough and highlights the key differences between the proposed approach and existing methods.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors present a novel approach to domain adaptation that enables the transformation of a classification network into a detection network. The use of a category invariant adaptation step and a category specific adaptation step is a key insight that allows the algorithm to effectively adapt to the differences between classification and detection tasks.

The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the importance of each component of their algorithm and show that their approach significantly outperforms a baseline that uses only classification data.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to address a significant challenge in object detection. The authors provide a solution that can be applied to a large number of categories with limited bounding box annotations, which is a common problem in many real-world applications.

The paper also has some limitations. One of the main limitations is the assumption that the classification data is available for all categories. While this is a common assumption in many object detection approaches, it may not always be the case in practice. Additionally, the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach, which may be an important consideration for many applications.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the technical contributions are significant. The authors provide a novel approach to domain adaptation that enables the transformation of a classification network into a detection network. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the paper addresses a significant challenge in object detection.
