- Addresses scaling object detection to many categories by converting classifiers into detectors without bounding-box labels (LSDA domain-adaptation approach).  
- Proposes learning a transferable transformation from classification to detection on fully supervised categories and applying it to others.  
- Novel and significant idea with a compelling 7.6K-category detector demonstration.  
- Strong empirical support on ILSVRC2013, with ablation showing category-invariant fine-tuning (layers 1–7) gives the largest boost (+50% relative mAP).  
- Error analysis shows gains mainly from reduced localization and background-confusion errors.  
- Paper is very clear and well-presented.  
- Category-specific adaptation (averaging δ from nearest neighbors using L2 on classifier weights) is somewhat heuristic and could be better motivated or learned.  
- Absolute performance on held-out categories (16.15% mAP) remains noticeably below the fully supervised upper bound (22.56% mAP).  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
