- Proposal: LSDA transforms image classifiers into object detectors via domain adaptation to scale detection without extensive bounding-box annotations.
- Results: Achieves a 50% relative mAP improvement on held-out categories and enables a 7.6K-category detector.
- Efficiency: Introduces a faster detector variant, improving practicality for large-scale applications.
- Novelty/significance: Tackles bounding-box annotation scarcity and enables large-scale detection using classification data.
- Technical soundness: Method grounded in domain adaptation with category-invariant and category-specific transformations; robust experimental support.
- Practical impact: Models and code released; method applicable to large datasets (e.g., video) and practical deployments.
- Evaluation: Comprehensive experiments including ablation study, error analysis, and ILSVRC2013 quantitative results; analyzes false positives and localization errors.
- Weakness — comparisons: Lacks direct experimental comparison to state-of-the-art alternatives like MIL-based weakly supervised methods.
- Weakness — reproducibility: Some implementation details (e.g., nearest-neighbor choices for category-specific adaptation) need more elaboration.
- Weakness — scalability: Computational efficiency and applicability to much larger label sets (20K+) or domains with limited classification data are not fully addressed.
- Pro arguments: Addresses a critical problem with a novel, practical, and experimentally supported solution; code/model release increases impact.
- Con arguments: Limited comparative baselines, incomplete implementation details, and unclear limits on scalability.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novel and impactful contributions despite areas for improvement.
