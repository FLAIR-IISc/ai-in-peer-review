**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel control-theoretic framework for building attractor networks that satisfy physiological constraints, including Dale's law, sparse and recurrent synaptic connections, and graded neuronal activity. The authors directly optimize network parameters to force sets of arbitrary analog patterns to become stable fixed points of the dynamics. The resulting networks operate in the balanced regime, are robust to corruptions of the memory cue and ongoing noise, and explain the reduction of trial-to-trial variability following stimulus onset.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Physiological constraints**: The paper addresses the limitations of previous models by incorporating key physiological constraints, such as Dale's law and sparse synaptic connections, into the network architecture.
2. **Analog memory patterns**: The authors demonstrate the ability to store and retrieve analog memory patterns, which is a significant improvement over previous models that were limited to binary patterns.
3. **Robustness to noise**: The network shows robustness to corruptions of the memory cue and ongoing noise, which is a critical aspect of biological systems.
4. **Balanced regime**: The network operates in the balanced regime, where excitation and inhibition are tightly balanced, which is consistent with experimental observations.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Computational complexity**: The optimization algorithm used to train the network may be computationally expensive, which could limit its applicability to larger networks.
2. **Simplifications**: The authors use a rate-based formulation of the circuit dynamics, which may not capture the full complexity of spiking neural networks.
3. **Lack of biological plausibility**: The optimization algorithm used to train the network may not be biologically plausible, as it requires a global optimization of network parameters.

**Originality and Significance**

1. **Novel framework**: The paper proposes a novel control-theoretic framework for building attractor networks, which is a significant contribution to the field of theoretical neuroscience.
2. **Advances in understanding memory**: The paper provides new insights into the neural substrate of memory, including the role of analog memory patterns and the importance of balanced regime operation.
3. **Implications for neural networks**: The paper has implications for the development of more realistic neural networks, which could be used to model a wide range of cognitive and neural processes.
