- Novel framework for biologically plausible attractor networks that store and recall analog (graded) memory patterns via a dual optimization ensuring target fixed points and their stability.  
- Core innovation: applying Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) minimization from robust control to directly and efficiently optimize dynamical stability.  
- Addresses prior limitations (binary patterns, non-biological connectivity, neuronal saturation) while enforcing Dale’s law, E/I balance, and low firing rates without strong structural priors.  
- Flexible approach that does not impose strong connectivity or pattern structure assumptions.  
- Strong empirical support: comprehensive simulations show robust autoassociative recall, noise resilience, emergent sparse connectivity, and stimulus-induced reduction in trial-to-trial variability.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation that makes complex methodology accessible.  
- Main weakness: learning uses a global, centralized gradient-based optimizer (BFGS), lacking a demonstrated biologically plausible, local synaptic learning rule.  
- Scalability concern: proof-of-concept limited to a 150-neuron network; discussion on computational challenges and memory-capacity scaling to larger networks is needed.  
- Overall: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
