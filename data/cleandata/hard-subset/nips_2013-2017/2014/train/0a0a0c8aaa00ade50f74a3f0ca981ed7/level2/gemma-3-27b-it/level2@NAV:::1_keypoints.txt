- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework (Smoothed Spectral Abscissa minimization) to construct biologically plausible balanced E–I memory networks.  
- Aims to overcome prior model limitations by enforcing Dale’s Law, avoiding binary representations, and preventing saturating activity.  
- Frames memory storage as an optimization problem to produce stable fixed points encoding analog memory patterns.  
- Methodologically sound, leveraging established robust control techniques.  
- Demonstrates successful storage and recall of analog patterns and robustness to noise.  
- Network exhibits dynamic excitation–inhibition balance and reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset, matching cortical observations.  
- Includes sparsity in synaptic weights and analyzes its role.  
- Parameter choices in the optimization (ηs, ηF, ε) appear arbitrary and need stronger justification or ablation studies.  
- Connection to spiking neural network models is underdeveloped; translation from rate to spiking dynamics needs deeper discussion.  
- Limited discussion of scalability to larger, more complex networks.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worth acceptance contingent on minor revisions addressing parameter justification, spiking links, and scalability.
