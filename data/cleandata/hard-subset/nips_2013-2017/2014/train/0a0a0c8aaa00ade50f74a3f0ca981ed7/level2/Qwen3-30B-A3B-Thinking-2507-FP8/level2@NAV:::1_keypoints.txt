- Introduces a control-theoretic framework that optimizes E-I networks to stabilize arbitrary analog memory patterns while respecting Dale’s law and graded activity.
- Leverages the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for robust stability optimization of recurrent E-I networks.
- Demonstrates storage of 30 analog patterns (capacity ≈ 0.2) with noise-robust recall.
- Produces sparse connectivity and a balanced excitation/inhibition regime that explains reduced trial-to-trial variability in sensory cortices.
- Experiments show successful noisy-cue recall (Fig. 3), close performance to an ideal observer (Fig. 3b), and quantified synaptic sparseness (Fig. 4).
- Shows precise neuron-level E/I balancing across the network (Fig. 5).
- Uses log-normal firing rate distributions consistent with in vivo observations (Fig. 1a).
- Optimization procedure is clearly articulated (Eq. 5) and parameter details are provided (Table 1).
- Thoughtful discussion of limitations: rate-based model applicability to spiking networks and need for local learning rules (e.g., inhibitory plasticity).
- Minor concern: non-zero ||dv/dt|| at fixed points (Fig. 2) needs more discussion of practical stability margins.
- Minor concern: modest storage capacity (0.2) should be contextualized against theoretical limits for analog patterns.
- Overall highly original, reproducible, and practically useful; merits acceptance at the conference.
