- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework for attractor networks that enforces Dale's law, sparse recurrent connectivity, and graded neuronal activity.  
- Optimizes network parameters so arbitrary analog patterns become stable fixed points of the dynamics.  
- Resulting networks operate in the balanced regime and are robust to corrupted recall cues and ongoing noise.  
- Main claims are supported by theoretical analysis and numerical simulations.  
- Demonstrates embedding of multiple analog memories that remain stable under noise and cue corruption.  
- Provides detailed analysis of synaptic weight evolution and excitation–inhibition balance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and contains a thorough literature review.  
- Limitation: uses a rate-based model, so applicability to spiking memory networks is uncertain.  
- Limitation: relies on global optimization for parameter tuning, which may be biologically implausible.  
- Unclear how local synaptic learning rules could achieve the same stabilization objective.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address limitations and future research directions.  
- Pros: novel, physiologically constrained approach with significant implications for understanding memory; Cons: simplified neuronal model and lack of plausible learning mechanisms.
