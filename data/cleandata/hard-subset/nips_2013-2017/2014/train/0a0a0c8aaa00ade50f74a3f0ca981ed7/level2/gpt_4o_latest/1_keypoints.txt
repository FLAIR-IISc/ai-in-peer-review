- Novel control-theoretic framework to construct attractor networks that embed analog memories while enforcing Dale’s law, graded activity, and balanced E–I dynamics.
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to optimize synaptic connectivity and ensure stable memory fixed points.
- Networks demonstrate robust memory recall under noise and show biologically plausible features like sparse weights and variability reduction after stimulus onset.
- Strength: Incorporates key physiological constraints, improving biological plausibility over prior models.
- Strength: Shows impressive robustness to noise in cues and ongoing dynamics.
- Strength: Innovative and computationally efficient use of SSA for stability optimization.
- Strength: Provides biological insight by reproducing experimentally observed trial-to-trial variability reduction.
- Strength: Clear, comprehensive experimental results validating performance, weight distributions, and E–I balance.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to heuristic optimization parameters (SSA smoothness, regularization) not fully explained.
- Weakness: Model is limited to rate-based dynamics; applicability to spiking networks is untested.
- Weakness: Does not propose biologically plausible learning rules for how connectivity could be learned.
- Weakness: Demonstrated storage capacity (~0.2) is relatively low and scalability needs further discussion.
- Pro: Integrates multiple physiological constraints, addressing a long-standing theoretical-neuroscience challenge.
- Con: Lack of learning mechanisms and untested generalization to spiking networks limit practical applicability.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify parameter tuning and discuss scalability and generalization.
