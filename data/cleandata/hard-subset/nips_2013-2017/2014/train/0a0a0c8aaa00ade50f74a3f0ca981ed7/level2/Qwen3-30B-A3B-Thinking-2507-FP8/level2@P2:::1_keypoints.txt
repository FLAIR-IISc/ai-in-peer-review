- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to build balanced rate-based E/I networks that store and retrieve analog memory patterns while respecting Dale’s law.  
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to directly optimize network parameters for stabilizing desired analog activity patterns.  
- Resulting networks operate in a balanced regime, show robust memory recall under noise, and reproduce reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.  
- Strength: addresses multiple physiological constraints simultaneously that prior models failed to satisfy.  
- Strength: novel and technically sound application of SSA for stability optimization in this context.  
- Strength: experiments demonstrate storage of arbitrary analog patterns, stable recall under noise, balanced E/I inputs, and variability reduction.  
- Weakness: claim of “directly optimizes” to create stable fixed points is overstated—dv/dt remains nonzero, so targets are slow points rather than exact fixed points.  
- Weakness: authors do not adequately discuss implications of slow-point behavior for their theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: insufficient distinction and explicit comparison to related works, especially spike-timing graded memory models [12] and Roudi & Latham [13].  
- Weakness: robustness evaluation is limited—only Gaussian white noise tested and more biologically realistic noise sources are not explored.  
- Weakness: claim of robustness to cue corruption needs clearer explanation; high-noise performance drop (σ=0.75) is not well accounted for.  
- Weakness: mechanistic explanation for reduced trial-to-trial variability is tenuous beyond noting E/I balance; clearer causal account is lacking.  
- Minor suggestion: provide more detail on the optimization procedure and how SSA minimization enforces stability.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify the role and choice of the baseline state among target patterns.  
- Overall verdict: strong, significant contribution with solid experiments and clarity; recommend acceptance after minor revisions addressing stability claims, robustness tests, and comparisons to related work.
