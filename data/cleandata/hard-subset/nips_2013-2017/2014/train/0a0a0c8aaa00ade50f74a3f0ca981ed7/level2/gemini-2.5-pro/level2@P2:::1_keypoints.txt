- Presents a novel method to construct E-I recurrent networks that store and retrieve multiple graded, analog memory patterns by directly optimizing synaptic weights so target patterns are stable fixed points.  
- Frames memory storage as a control-theoretic optimization enforcing zero time-derivative at targets and local stability.  
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to provide a differentiable upper bound on spectral abscissa, enabling gradient-based stability optimization.  
- Respects biological constraint Dale’s Law and stores analog patterns at low firing rates, unlike many prior models.  
- Stability emerges as dynamic E-I balance from optimization rather than being hand-designed.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and convincing: robust autoassociative recall comparable to an ideal observer and multiple biological-like emergent properties.  
- Emergent properties include sparse, log-normal-like synaptic weight distributions, tight E-I current balance, and stimulus-induced variability reduction.  
- Paper is high technical quality, clearly written, with well-motivated formulation, clear methods, and informative figures.  
- Highly original application of robust control theory to sculpt biologically constrained recurrent dynamics.  
- Significant implications for computational neuroscience (proof-of-concept for realistic graded memories) and machine learning (designing RNNs with specified stable fixed points).  
- Main weakness: learning uses global offline optimization (L-BFGS), lacking biological plausibility; authors should discuss prospects for local/online gradient approximations.  
- Secondary weakness: experiments on 150 neurons/30 patterns—need discussion of scalability, optimization difficulty, capacity scaling (m/n), and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Tertiary weakness: inhibitory activities treated as free per-memory parameters — clarify biological interpretation of inhibitory neurons encoding part of memory traces.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor/clarifying revisions; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
