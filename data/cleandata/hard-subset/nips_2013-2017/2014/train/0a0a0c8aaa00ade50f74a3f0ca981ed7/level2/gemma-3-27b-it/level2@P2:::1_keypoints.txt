- Proposes a control-theoretic framework that uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to directly optimize network parameters and stabilize arbitrary analog patterns as fixed points.  
- Integrates physiological constraints (Dale’s law, graded activity, low firing rates, E/I balance) into attractor network design.  
- Demonstrates robust memory recall and resilience to noisy cues in the optimized networks.  
- Provides a mechanistic explanation for stimulus-induced reduction in trial-to-trial variability.  
- Builds on prior attractor work while addressing common limitations (relaxing Dale’s law, binary representations, saturating dynamics).  
- Framing memory storage as a control problem and applying SSA is a novel and well-motivated methodological contribution.  
- The approach overcomes difficulties of direct stability analysis in high-dimensional parameter spaces.  
- Detailed analyses include synaptic weight distributions, E/I balance, and effects of synaptic pruning.  
- Writing and organization are clear, facilitating understanding of complex methods.  
- Optimization appears computationally intensive, raising concerns about scalability to larger networks.  
- Key hyperparameters (ηs, ηF, ε) are chosen heuristically; sensitivity analysis is lacking.  
- The rationale for the threshold-quadratic gain function needs expansion and comparison to alternatives.  
- Translation from rate-based to spiking networks is acknowledged but not sufficiently developed (e.g., spike timing/correlations).  
- Limited direct comparisons to recent related models reduces clarity about unique contributions.  
- Paper presents a technically sound, novel approach that addresses an important open problem in computational neuroscience.  
- Results connect model behavior to empirical observations, strengthening biological relevance.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: significant contribution, clear writing, and innovative use of robust control theory.  
- Concerns against acceptance: computational cost and heuristic parameter tuning warrant further attention.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper meriting acceptance with minor revisions to address parameter sensitivity and links to spiking dynamics.
