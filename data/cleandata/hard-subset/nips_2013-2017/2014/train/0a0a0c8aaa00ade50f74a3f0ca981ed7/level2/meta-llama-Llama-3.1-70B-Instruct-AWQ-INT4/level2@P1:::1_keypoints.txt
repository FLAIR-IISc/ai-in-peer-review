- Presents a novel control-theoretic framework for attractor networks that respects physiological constraints (E/I separation, sparse recurrence, graded activity).
- Optimizes network parameters so desired activity states are fixed points and dynamics are stable around them.
- Introduces Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) as a differentiable tool for robust stability optimization.
- Demonstrates storage of multiple analog memories as stable fixed points robust to noise and corrupted recall cues.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and informative figures.
- Provides a thorough discussion of implications and potential applications.
- Assumes substantial background in control theory and neuroscience, reducing accessibility for non-experts.
- Lacks a detailed comparison with previous models of memory circuits.
- Does not address how local synaptic learning rules could achieve the presented stabilization objectives.
- Use of SSA in this context is an original contribution.
- Work has significant potential to advance understanding of neural memory substrates and realistic memory models.
- High overall quality and clear organization.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with clear results and strong presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: limited accessibility, missing comparative analysis, and omission of learning-rule mechanisms.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to provide detailed comparisons and address implementation via local synaptic learning rules.
