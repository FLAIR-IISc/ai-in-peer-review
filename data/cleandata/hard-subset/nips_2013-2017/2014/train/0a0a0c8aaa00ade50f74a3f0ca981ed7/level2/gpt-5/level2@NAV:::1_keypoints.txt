- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to build rate-based E/I attractor networks that store/retrieve analog memories while respecting Dale’s law, low firing rates, and E/I balance.
- Jointly optimizes synaptic weights and memory-specific inhibitory steady states so prescribed excitatory patterns are fixed points and locally stable via Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) minimization.
- Uses SSA, a differentiable upper bound on spectral abscissa, to enforce local stability in a numerically tractable way.
- Simulations show robust autoassociative recall comparable to an ideal observer under cue noise, operation in a dynamically balanced regime, emergent sparsity in effective synapses, and stimulus-induced variability quenching.
- Integrates ideas from Hopfield-style associative memory, graded-response analyses, balanced network theory, variability quenching, and prior SSA applications into a unique Dale-constrained analog-memory design.
- Strength: methodologically careful with explicit fixed-point/stability criteria and multiple validation axes (recall vs noise, ideal-observer comparison, ongoing noise robustness, E/I balance, variability quenching).
- Strength: original application of robust stability optimization to embed analog attractors in E/I networks that obey Dale’s law, moving beyond binary/global-inhibition models.
- Strength: significant as a physiologically grounded template for associative memory with explanatory links to variability quenching.
- Strength: generally clear presentation, equations, and coherent cost function.
- Weakness: modest demonstrated capacity (~0.2 for n=150) with no systematic scaling laws, basin-size quantification, or theoretical comparisons.
- Weakness: learning relies on nonlocal gradient-based optimization over many parameters and per-memory inhibitory setpoints, raising biological/plausibility concerns.
- Weakness: reproducibility details lacking (hyperparameter sensitivity like SSA ε, convergence behavior, computational cost) and code availability not stated.
- Weakness: no spiking-network validation of learned connectivity and robustness to parameter perturbations or structural sparsity constraints is unexplored.
- Suggestion: provide capacity and basin-size scaling with n, m, sparsity and compare to graded Hopfield-like Dale-obeying baselines.
- Suggestion: validate transfer of learned connectivity to LIF spiking networks and quantify recall/variability quenching there.
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to ε, initialization, regularization, and report computation/time and convergence behavior.
- Suggestion: add structural sparsity constraints or L1 penalties and compare to emergent sparsity; explore local/plausible learning rules approximating the SSA objective.
- Suggestion: release code and include a reproducible protocol.
- Overall: principled, physiology-respecting, and novel; promising contribution that would be stronger with added scaling/robustness analyses, spiking validation, and reproducibility materials.
