- Proposes a control-theoretic framework that optimizes synaptic weights and inhibitory firing rates to stabilize analog patterns as attractors under physiological constraints (Dale’s law, graded activity, balanced regime).  
- Novel use of the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to guarantee robust stability during optimization.  
- Comprehensive experiments show successful memory storage/recall, robustness to noise, and biologically plausible emergent properties (balanced E/I, reduced trial-to-trial variability).  
- Strong motivation: addresses a long-standing gap by unifying multiple physiological constraints in attractor-network models.  
- Thorough validation, including comparisons to an “ideal observer,” strengthens the empirical claims.  
- Particularly compelling link between model dynamics and empirical cortical observations of variability reduction after stimulus onset.  
- Major omission: computational cost and scalability of the optimization (optimizing O(n^2 + nIm) parameters) are not discussed.  
- Suggest adding discussion or methods to improve efficiency (e.g., sparsity regularization, faster SSA computation).  
- Choice of threshold-quadratic gain function is somewhat arbitrary; alternatives and their impact should be explored.  
- Cost-function hyperparameters (ηs and ηF) are set heuristically; sensitivity analysis or principled selection is needed.  
- Claim that sparsity “must be a genuine consequence” is assertive; further analysis is needed on how SSA/optimization produce sparsity.  
- Recommend showing performance as sparsification is increased beyond the naturally occurring level.  
- Limited architecture: results derived for fully connected E/I populations; generalization to sparse or structured biological topologies is unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled approach, strong experimental support, and relevance to neuroscience.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear computational cost, heuristic parameter choices, limited exploration of gain functions/topologies, and speculative sparsity explanation.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommend minor revisions to address computational, sensitivity, and generalizability concerns.
