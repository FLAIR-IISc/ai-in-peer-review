- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks under physiological constraints.  
- Addresses limitations of prior models by enforcing Dale’s law and allowing analog (non-binary) memory representations.  
- Framework optimizes networks of excitatory and inhibitory neurons to make arbitrary analog patterns stable fixed points.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and effective use of figures and tables.  
- Methods are thoroughly described, including the optimization algorithm and stability cost function.  
- Results are supported by theoretical analysis and experimental demonstrations.  
- Paper is original and provides a new perspective on memory storage and retrieval in neural networks.  
- High clarity and appropriate use of technical terms, accessible to non-experts.  
- Significant implications for understanding neural substrates of memory and applications in neuroscience/AI.  
- Concerns: relies on rate-based dynamics which may not generalize to all neural models.  
- Concerns: potential computational expense limiting scalability and lack of direct comparison to other memory models.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
