- Introduces a control-theoretic framework for recurrent networks that store and recall analog (graded) memory patterns while enforcing biological constraints like Dale’s law and low firing rates.  
- Formulates memory storage as an optimization problem requiring zero time-derivative (fixed-point) and local stability for each target pattern.  
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) minimization from robust control to enforce dynamical stability in a differentiable way.  
- Demonstrates embedding of dozens of analog patterns in a 150-neuron E-I network via numerical optimization.  
- Shows robust autoassociative recall comparable to an ideal observer, even with substantially corrupted cues.  
- Finds optimized synaptic weights are sparse with a long-tailed distribution, matching cortical data.  
- Networks operate in a dynamically balanced E/I regime enabling precise, stable graded firing rates.  
- Reproduces stimulus-induced quenching of trial-to-trial variability observed experimentally.  
- High technical quality: SSA application is appropriate, claims supported by thorough simulations and analyses.  
- Suggests improvement: analyze “slow points” (dv/dt small but nonzero) to assess local stability and dynamics near them.  
- Suggests improvement: discuss sensitivity of results to heuristic hyperparameters (e.g., ηs, ε) to aid reproducibility.  
- Manuscript clarity is excellent, with strong motivation, transparent methods, and effective figures.  
- Highly original: novel use of control-theoretic tools to store analog patterns under biological constraints, distinct from Hebbian/statistical approaches.  
- Highly significant for computational neuroscience: provides a normative model linking realistic structure/dynamics to robust analog memory and testable predictions.  
- Main limitations: optimization algorithm is not a biologically plausible learning rule and the model is rate-based rather than spiking.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, integrates multiple critical constraints, produces biologically realistic emergent properties, technically sound, and impactful.  
- Rejection arguments: lacks biologically plausible learning mechanism and untested applicability to spiking networks.
