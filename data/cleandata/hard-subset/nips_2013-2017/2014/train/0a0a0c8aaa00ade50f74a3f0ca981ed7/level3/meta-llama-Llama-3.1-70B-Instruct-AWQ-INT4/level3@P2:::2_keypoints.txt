- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks that meet physiological constraints (e.g., Dale’s law, non-saturated activity).  
- Aims to store multiple stable analog activity patterns in an excitatory–inhibitory rate network by directly optimizing network parameters.  
- Builds on Hopfield-style attractor models and prior work but addresses their simultaneous failure to satisfy multiple biological constraints.  
- Introduces smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) for robust stability optimization as a new methodological contribution.  
- Shows learned synaptic weight distributions matching experimental data and excitation–inhibition balance in attractor states.  
- Demonstrates graded, non-saturated attractors encoded in synaptic weights and inhibitory activity.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and detailed methods and results.  
- Strengths: combines biological constraints to produce biologically plausible features and advances understanding of memory substrates.  
- Weakness: generalization to more complex/realistic tasks is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: reliance on gradient-descent optimization may limit scalability to larger networks.  
- Suggestion: paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and future research directions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
