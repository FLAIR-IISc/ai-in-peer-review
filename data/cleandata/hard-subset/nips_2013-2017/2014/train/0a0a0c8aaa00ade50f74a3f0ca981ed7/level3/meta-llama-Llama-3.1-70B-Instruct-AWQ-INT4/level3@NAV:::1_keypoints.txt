- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework for rate-based auto-associative memory networks that respect physiological constraints.  
- Memories encoded via gradient-descent synaptic updates combined with a method enforcing stability of linearized dynamics around fixed points.  
- Trained networks exhibit biologically consistent features: synaptic weight distribution centered at zero with long tails and per-neuron E/I balance.  
- Networks show robustness to perturbations and stochastic input noise, yielding reliable auto-associative recall.  
- Clear, well-written manuscript with effective figures that clarify methods and results.  
- Technically sound work with complete methods, results, and discussion; reproducible for expert readers.  
- Novel combination of rate-based memory model and recent stability-enforcing technique; original contribution to theoretical neuroscience.  
- Results have significant implications for understanding neural memory storage/retrieval and for ML robustness.  
- Weakness: training procedure lacks detailed justification and comparison to alternatives.  
- Weakness: authors do not assess whether alternative training methods could achieve similar outcomes or explain mechanistically why theirs succeeds.  
- Weakness: no comparison of memory capacity (memories vs. network size) to previous models.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel, physiologically constrained attractor network approach with robust results.  
- Con-acceptance: needs minor revisions to better justify and compare the chosen training procedure.
