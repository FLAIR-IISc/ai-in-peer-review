- Introduces a rate-based auto-associative network that enforces Dale’s Law and supports non-saturating graded activity.  
- Trains synapses and inhibitory dynamics via gradient descent combined with Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) minimization to ensure stable fixed-point attractors for analog memories.  
- Generates biologically consistent features: synaptic weight distributions centered near zero with long tails, per-neuron excitatory/inhibitory balance, and robust recall under noise and perturbations.  
- Stability validated both via SSA minimization (theoretical) and empirical network dynamics (Figs. 2–3).  
- Novel integration of control-theoretic stability optimization (SSA) into neural memory modeling, addressing prior violations of biological constraints.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear with thorough literature context, well-justified architecture, and compelling figures (e.g., Fig. 1c, 5b, 6).  
- Significant contribution to understanding how graded memories can be stored and stabilized in cortex, with implications for variability reduction in sensory/motor systems.  
- Weakness: underdeveloped justification for choosing SSA-based training over alternative stability metrics or learning rules.  
- Weakness: lacks a brief mechanistic explanation of why SSA uniquely enables graded, non-saturating memories.  
- Weakness: capacity comparisons to prior models (e.g., refs. [5,6]) are missing and would help contextualize significance.  
- Recommendation: Accept; minor revision suggested to address the training-procedure justification.
