**Paper Review: Analog Memories in a Balanced Rate-Based Network of E-I Neurons**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel control-theoretic framework for building functioning attractor networks that satisfy key physiological constraints. The authors introduce a rate-based auto-associative memory model, constrained by biological plausibility, where memories are encoded via synaptic updates from gradient descent combined with a recent method that enforces stability of the linearized dynamics around fixed points. The trained networks display biologically consistent features, such as synaptic weight distribution centered at zero with long tails and approximate excitatory/inhibitory balance per neuron. The authors demonstrate that the trained networks are robust to perturbations and stochastic input noise, exhibiting reliable auto-associative recall.

**Strengths**

* The paper presents a novel approach to building attractor networks that satisfy key physiological constraints, making it a valuable contribution to the field of theoretical neuroscience.
* The authors provide a clear and well-written manuscript, making it easy to follow their reasoning and methodology.
* The results are well-illustrated with effective figures that help to clarify the authors' findings.
* The paper demonstrates the robustness of the trained networks to perturbations and stochastic input noise, which is an important aspect of any neural network model.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the chosen training procedure and why it was selected over other methods.
* The authors could provide more insight into whether alternative training procedures could achieve similar results or explain mechanistically why this method succeeds.
* A comparison of the trained network performance to previous models in terms of capacity (number of memories vs network size) would be a valuable addition to the paper.

**Originality**

* The paper presents a novel approach to building attractor networks that satisfy key physiological constraints, making it a valuable contribution to the field of theoretical neuroscience.
* The authors' use of a rate-based auto-associative memory model, combined with a recent method that enforces stability of the linearized dynamics around fixed points, is a new and innovative approach.

**Significance**

* The paper's results have significant implications for our understanding of the neural substrate of memory and how memories are stored and retrieved in the brain.
* The authors' demonstration of the robustness of the trained networks to perturbations and stochastic input noise makes it a valuable contribution to the field of neural networks and machine learning.

**Quality**

* The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a clear methodology.
* The authors provide a complete piece of work, with a clear introduction, methods, results, and discussion.
* The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read.

**Clarity**

* The paper is clearly written, with a well-organized structure and effective figures that help to clarify the authors' findings.
* The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce their results.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

* Pro: The paper presents a novel approach to building attractor networks that satisfy key physiological constraints, making it a valuable contribution to the field of theoretical neuroscience.
* Con: The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the chosen training procedure and why it was selected over other methods.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of this paper, with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
