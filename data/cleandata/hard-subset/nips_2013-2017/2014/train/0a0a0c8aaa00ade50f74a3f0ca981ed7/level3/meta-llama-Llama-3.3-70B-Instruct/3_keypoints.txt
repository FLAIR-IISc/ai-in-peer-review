- Introduces a novel control-theoretic perspective on associative memory that incorporates biological constraints.  
- Proposes a three-term cost function to address binary representations, non-sparse coding, and Dale’s law.  
- Method naturally balances excitation and inhibition; saturation emerges from optimization rather than being imposed.  
- Validated with numerical simulations showing noise robustness.  
- Explains experimental findings on trial-to-trial variability.  
- Tackles a classic neural computation problem with clear and important contributions.  
- Major limitation: lacks a biologically plausible learning mechanism.  
- Minor issue: sensitivity to parameter choice.  
- Minor issue: unclear motivation for assuming a log-normal distribution.  
- Minor issue: potential violation of the fixed-point nature of memories.  
- Minor issue: no discussion of capacity limits or computational benefits of Dale’s law.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, original, and significant with thorough analysis.
