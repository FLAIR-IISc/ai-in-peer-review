- Trains a rate-based recurrent E-I network to store multiple stable, graded activity patterns while enforcing biological constraints (Dale’s law via log-exp reparameterization).  
- Frames memory storage as a constrained control problem minimizing activity drift at targets, a smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) cost for local stability, and an L2 weight penalty.  
- Uses SSA as a differentiable proxy for local stability during training.  
- Learned networks perform autoassociative recall without firing-rate saturation and show E/I balance in attractor states.  
- Emergent synaptic weight distributions are sparse and heavy-tailed, matching cortical measurements.  
- Model robustly recalls from noisy cues, resists ongoing noise, and reproduces stimulus-onset variability quenching.  
- Unifies multiple physiological constraints and control-theoretic stability, complementing prior balanced-memory and transient-control work.  
- Methodology is technically careful with validation via recall success and perturbation robustness; analyses of weight statistics and E/I balance are strong.  
- Limitation: some fixed points are “slow points” and basin sizes are not quantified.  
- Limitation: capacity and scaling with network size, pattern number, and sparsity are not systematically compared to graded-capacity theory.  
- Limitation: sensitivity to hyperparameters (ε, ηs, ηF) and initialization is not explored.  
- Limitation: networks are dense during training; structural sparsity constraints are not explicitly tested.  
- Limitation: spiking instantiations are discussed but not demonstrated.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well written and figures clearly support claims; additional ablations (hyperparameter sensitivity, computational complexity, scaling curves) would improve reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel combination of SSA-based stability optimization with Dale-constrained analog attractors and inhibitory co-optimization.  
- Significance: advances biologically plausible attractor network design and links recall to variability quenching, though impact depends on scalability and translation to local/plausible learning rules.  
- Pros: unified biologically grounded formulation, emergent E/I balance and realistic weight statistics, robust analog attractors, clear writing.  
- Cons: limited capacity/scaling analysis, no spiking validation, light hyperparameter and basin-of-attraction analyses.  
- Overall: strong and timely contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
