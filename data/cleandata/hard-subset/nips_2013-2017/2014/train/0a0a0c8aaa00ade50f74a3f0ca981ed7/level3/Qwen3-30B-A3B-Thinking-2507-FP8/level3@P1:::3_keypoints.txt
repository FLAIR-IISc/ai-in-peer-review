- Contribution: introduces a novel control-theoretic framework that stabilizes arbitrary analog memory patterns while enforcing balanced excitation–inhibition and Dale’s law.  
- Core innovation: optimizes network parameters via Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) minimization to produce robust fixed-point memories without saturating neuron models.  
- Dale’s law: enforced through a reparameterized weight matrix (Eq. 2), addressing a major biophysical constraint missing from many prior models.  
- Graded activity: preserves continuous firing rates (no artificial binary/saturating states) as shown in Fig. 1c.  
- Sparse connectivity: sparse weights emerge naturally from optimization and match cortical-like sparsity (Fig. 4b).  
- Cost function: Eq. 5 elegantly balances fixed-point stability (min |dv/dt|), robust stability (SSA), and sparsity (L2 regularization).  
- Explains data: model accounts for reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset by convergence to attractors (Fig. 6b).  
- Numerical validation: thorough demonstrations of recall robustness to noise (Fig. 3), balanced E/I inputs (Fig. 5), and cortex-like sparsity.  
- Writing and presentation: clear exposition, well-justified modeling choices, and effective figures.  
- Major weakness: no biologically plausible local learning rule is provided—only a vague suggestion about inhibitory plasticity; this is the most critical gap.  
- Parameter justification missing: the gain parameter γ=0.04 (Eq. 1) lacks empirical citation or motivation.  
- Pattern distribution: using a log-normal memory distribution (Fig. 1a) is unmotivated; alternative choices (e.g., Gaussian) are not discussed.  
- Fixed-point accuracy: |dv/dt| remains >0 after optimization (Fig. 2) but is not quantified—need average |dv/dt| across patterns and discussion of its stability impact.  
- Capacity claim ambiguous: “0.2” in Fig. 2 must be defined (memories per neuron) and compared to theoretical limits (e.g., Treves, 1990).  
- Minor issues: unclear phrasing around lines 254–256 and insufficient discussion of whether Dale’s law is merely biophysical or computationally enabling the reported robustness.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, original work that merits acceptance with minor revisions addressing the learning-rule gap, parameter justifications, fixed-point quantification, and capacity clarification.
