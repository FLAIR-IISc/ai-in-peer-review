- Proposes a novel rate-based attractor network trained with a control-theoretic framework that enforces biological constraints (Dale’s law, graded activity, E/I balance) and avoids activity saturation.
- Training minimizes activity changes, includes weight regularization, and optimizes attractor stability via the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA).
- Trained networks show robust memory recall, experimentally consistent synaptic weight distributions, and dynamic excitation-inhibition balance.
- Strength: Incorporates realistic biological constraints, improving plausibility over prior attractor models.
- Strength: Technically rigorous use of SSA with clear mathematical formulation and extensive simulations.
- Strength: Model outputs align with experimental data on weights and network dynamics, enhancing credibility.
- Strength: Manuscript is well-written, logically organized, and figures effectively illustrate findings.
- Strength: Addresses the long-standing issue of graded, non-saturated memory states, offering a significant conceptual advance.
- Weakness: Generality is unclear—extension to spiking networks and more complex biology is speculative and not demonstrated.
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—storage capacity (~0.2; 30 attractors/150 neurons) is relatively low and raises questions about larger networks.
- Weakness: Learning rule is algorithmic and not biologically plausible; inhibitory plasticity is mentioned but not explored.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with a request to expand discussion on extensions to spiking dynamics and biologically plausible learning rules.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, biologically realistic approach; strong experimental alignment; clear presentation; potential to inspire future memory-modeling work.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited exploration of scalability/generalization to spiking networks and lack of biologically plausible learning mechanisms.
- Overall: Meaningful advancement in biologically constrained attractor networks and suitable for conference presentation.
