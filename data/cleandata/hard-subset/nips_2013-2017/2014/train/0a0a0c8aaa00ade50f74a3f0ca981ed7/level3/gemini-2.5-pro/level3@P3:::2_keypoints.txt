- Introduces a novel framework for biologically plausible attractor networks that store multiple graded (analog) memory patterns.  
- Uses a rate-based network with distinct excitatory and inhibitory populations that respects Dale's law.  
- Employs gradient-descent optimization (control-theoretic) to directly learn synaptic weights and inhibitory steady states rather than a predefined local learning rule.  
- Defines a cost combining three objectives: fixed-point condition, local stability via Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA), and Frobenius-norm weight regularization.  
- Demonstrates successful storage and robust recall of analog memories, with resilience to noisy cues.  
- Trained networks show emergent E-I balance in memory states consistent with experiments.  
- Learned synaptic weights are sparse with a heavy-tailed distribution and a few strong connections.  
- Networks reproduce stimulus-induced reduction of trial-to-trial variability (variance quenching).  
- Technical contribution: novel application of SSA from robust control to enforce stability in neural network optimization.  
- Quality: technically sound, thorough simulations, and convincing analyses (recall, weight distributions, E-I balance, noise robustness).  
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written, organized, and methods are described precisely and reproducibly.  
- Originality: unifies multiple biological constraints in a single optimization framework—novel and conceptually significant.  
- Significance: provides a normative model linking function (stable graded memory) to biological structure (E-I balance, sparse connectivity), relevant to neuroscience and ML.  
- Weakness: learning procedure (global gradient descent on a cost) is biologically implausible and needs clearer framing as a normative proof-of-concept.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—unclear how SSA computation and optimization scale to much larger, biologically realistic networks.  
- Weakness: key hyperparameters (ηs, ηF, ε) chosen heuristically; sensitivity analysis is lacking.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly discuss biological implausibility in the main text and frame the work as normative rather than mechanistic.  
- Suggested improvements: add discussion on computational complexity and potential scalability strategies.  
- Suggested improvements: include a brief sensitivity analysis to show robustness to hyperparameter choices.  
- Acceptance pros: novel, coherent framework that integrates Dale’s law, graded activity, and E-I dynamics; explains emergent cortical features and is technically solid and clear.  
- Acceptance cons: non-biological global learning rule and unaddressed scalability to larger networks.
