- Introduces a rate-based auto-associative memory network enforcing Dale’s Law, explicit E/I populations, and non-saturating graded units while optimizing fixed-point stability.  
- Formulates storage as joint minimization of drift at target states and the smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) of the Jacobian for gradient-based stabilization.  
- SSA-based training produces locally stable learned fixed points with slow near-attractor dynamics.  
- Demonstrates robust auto-associative recall under large cue corruption and ongoing noise.  
- Emergent cortical-like phenomena: E/I balance, heavy-tailed weight distributions centered near zero, and stimulus-onset variability quenching.  
- Methodologically sound and carefully executed; SSA training is appropriate and evaluated across multiple metrics.  
- Some empirical claims (e.g., “near fixed points”) need more systematic quantification of basin sizes and scaling.  
- Manuscript is clearly written with informative literature review, transparent objectives, and effective figures.  
- Novel combination of graded, non-saturating units with strict Dale constraint and differentiable stability objective.  
- Significant potential impact for computational neuroscience and control-oriented ML in training biologically constrained RNNs.  
- Request clarification of why SSA was chosen over alternatives (Lyapunov shaping, contraction metrics, spectral norm constraints, FORCE-like schemes).  
- Ask for a mechanistic account explaining success (eigenvalue spectra/modes, role of inhibition, link to stabilized supralinear network theory).  
- Request capacity analysis: scaling with network size, comparisons to prior graded-memory capacities (e.g., Treves-Rolls), basin size, and interference analyses.  
- Ask for ablation studies: sensitivity to ε, regularization, initializations, clipping weak synapses, and origins of sparsity.  
- Ask to discuss computational cost and scalability to larger networks.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong biological plausibility, robust noise-tolerant recall, clear control-theoretic training, emergent E/I balance and realistic synaptic stats, high clarity and novelty.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited capacity/scale benchmarks, lack of theoretical guarantees on basins, missing comparative analysis with alternative stabilization methods, and unquantified scalability/computational cost.
