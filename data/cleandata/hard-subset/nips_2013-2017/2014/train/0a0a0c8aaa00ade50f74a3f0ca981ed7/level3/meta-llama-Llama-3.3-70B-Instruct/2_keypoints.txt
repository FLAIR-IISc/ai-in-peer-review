- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks satisfying physiological constraints.  
- Designs a rate-based neuronal network that optimizes parameters so arbitrary analog patterns become stable fixed points.  
- Uses the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for robust stability optimization and provides a clear explanation of the procedure.  
- Network operates in a balanced excitation–inhibition regime, enabling low firing rates during recall.  
- Demonstrates storage and recall of multiple analog memories with high robustness to noise and corrupted cues.  
- Shows robustness to ongoing noise and cue corruption in recall performance.  
- Explains reduction of trial-to-trial variability following stimulus onset, matching experimental observations.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and easy to follow with clear organization and justification of claims.  
- Significance lies in providing a realistic, robust framework for attractor networks with potential to explain experimental phenomena.  
- Strength: overcomes limitations of previous models by combining biological constraints with control-theoretic design.  
- Strength: results are well-supported and demonstrate desirable dynamical and robustness properties.  
- Weakness: model is rate-based and may not directly translate to spiking network implementations.  
- Weakness: requires further work to show how local synaptic learning rules could implement the stabilization objective.  
- Weakness: novel framework may complicate direct comparisons with existing models in the literature.
