- Introduces a control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks that satisfy Dale’s law, support analog memories, and produce realistic firing rates.  
- Directly optimizes network parameters, including synaptic connectivity, to create stable fixed points corresponding to target memory patterns.  
- Uses the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for stability optimization and enforces Dale’s law via a specific weight parameterization.  
- Resulting networks show robust memory recall, reduced sensitivity to noise, and may explain stimulus-driven reductions in trial-to-trial variability.  
- Situates the work within longstanding associative memory literature (e.g., Hopfield) and clearly identifies prior shortcomings.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: mathematically rigorous framework, novel use of SSA, and effective Dale’s-law parameterization.  
- Strength — Clarity: exceptionally well-written, with informative figures and consistent notation.  
- Strength — Originality: novel combination of control theory, physiological constraints, analog memory representation, and optimization of inhibitory activity.  
- Strength — Significance: broad implications for understanding biologically plausible memory networks and links to cortical phenomena.  
- Weakness — Lacks a biologically plausible learning rule; current approach is a design/optimization rather than a mechanistic learning process.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: choice of γ = 0.04 is not analyzed for sensitivity or its effects on stability/capacity.  
- Weakness — Memory distribution: insufficient justification for using a log-normal distribution and unclear sensitivity to alternative distributions.  
- Weakness — Fixed-point approximation: cost function may not enforce exact fixed points; needs quantitative assessment of deviations and impacts.  
- Weakness — SSA vs SA: statements linking SSA to SA need clarification on how much SSA relaxes stability and the implications.  
- Weakness — Memory capacity: lacks analysis of capacity limits and scaling of stored memories with network size.  
- Weakness — Dale’s law: no discussion on whether enforcing Dale’s law confers computational advantages beyond biological plausibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, significant results, excellent presentation, and honest discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments for rejection: major limitation is absence of a plausible learning rule; insufficient capacity analysis and need for stronger justification/analysis of some methodological choices.  
- Overall assessment: strong, potentially high-impact paper recommended for acceptance provided authors address learning-rule, sensitivity, and fixed-point clarification concerns.
