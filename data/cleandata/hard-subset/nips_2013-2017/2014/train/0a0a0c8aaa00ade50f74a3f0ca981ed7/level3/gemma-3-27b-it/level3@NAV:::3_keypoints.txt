- Novel approach that addresses Dale’s Law, non-binary representations, and avoids neuronal saturation in associative memory models.
- Introduces a unified control-theoretic framework using spectral bounding to optimize network parameters for stable analog memory storage.
- Resulting networks show balanced excitation/inhibition dynamics, robustness to noise, and reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.
- Holistic optimization simultaneously targets fixed points, stability, and weight minimization, unlike prior models that handle constraints separately.
- Dale’s principle enforced via weight parametrization is elegant and yields natural E/I balance.
- Single-neuron saturation emerges from optimization rather than being explicitly imposed, increasing biological plausibility.
- Numerical simulations validate the approach and support the explanation for reduced variability.
- No concrete proposal for a biologically plausible learning rule is provided—a significant omission.
- Sensitivity of results to the specific gamma = 0.04 parameter (Eq. 3) requires further analysis.
- Motivation for choosing a log-normal distribution for memory states needs stronger justification.
- The paper should quantify how closely the cost function (Eq. 4) enforces fixed-point constraints.
- Clarification is needed for the statements on lines 254–256.
- Lacks any analysis or discussion of memory capacity—crucial omission.
- Should investigate whether benefits of enforcing Dale’s Law are purely computational or reflect evolutionary/biophysical constraints.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, original, clear contribution with high potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing learning rule and missing capacity analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing learning rule, gamma sensitivity, distribution justification, cost-function deviation, line-254–256 clarification, and a preliminary capacity discussion.
