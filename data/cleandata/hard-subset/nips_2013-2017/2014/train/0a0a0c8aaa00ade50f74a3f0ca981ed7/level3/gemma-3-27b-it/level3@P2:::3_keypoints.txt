- Proposes a novel, biologically grounded attractor-network framework that enforces Dale’s law, graded neuronal responses, and E/I balance within a control-theoretic approach.  
- Formalizes memory storage as optimization for fixed points and their stability, using the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for robust stability optimization.  
- Resulting networks show robustness to noise, avoidance of neuronal saturation, and can explain reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, making complex methods accessible.  
- Technically sound use of control theory and SSA represents a creative application to network neuroscience.  
- Comprehensive numerical validation demonstrates memory recall performance, robustness, balanced activity, synaptic weight distribution, and effects of pruning.  
- Major weakness: no biologically plausible learning rule is provided to implement the proposed parameter optimization.  
- Parameter choice concerns: γ = 0.04 appears arbitrary and sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Memory distribution motivation: log-normal choice needs stronger justification and comparison to other plausible distributions.  
- Fixed-point approximation: cost function only approaches fixed points—need quantitative analysis of constraint violations and their impact.  
- Capacity analysis is limited: only ~30 memories shown, lacking discussion of maximum capacity and scaling with network size.  
- Dale’s law discussion should examine possible computational advantages, not just biological realism.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem, offers significant technical contributions, produces compelling results, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of a biologically plausible learning rule and limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and capacity.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution likely to stimulate research; recommend accept with minor revisions to address noted concerns.  
- Requested revisions: address parameter sensitivity, memory-distribution rationale, fixed-point approximation impact, capacity/scaling, functional role of Dale’s law, and clarify lines 254–256.
