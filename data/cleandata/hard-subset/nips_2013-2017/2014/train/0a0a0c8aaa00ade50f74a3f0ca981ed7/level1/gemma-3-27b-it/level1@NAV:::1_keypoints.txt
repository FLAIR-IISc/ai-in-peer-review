- Addresses building biologically plausible attractor networks for analog memory storage and recall.
- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework that optimizes E-I networks to stabilize arbitrary analog patterns as fixed points.
- Leverages the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for robust stability optimization — a significant methodological contribution.
- Explicitly enforces physiological constraints (Dale’s law, graded activity, E/I balance) improving biological realism.
- Demonstrates successful storage and recall of analog memories and robustness to noise in a balanced E-I network.
- Links model behavior to observed reductions in trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.
- Presentation is clear and figures are helpful, though Methods’ mathematical details need more elaboration for broader accessibility.
- Network size (n=150) is relatively small, limiting generalizability and requiring scaling studies.
- Hyperparameter choices (ηs, ηF, ε) appear arbitrary and need more systematic exploration.
- Limited discussion of how the rate-based approach would transition to spiking networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, strong theoretical grounding, successful demonstrations, and neuroscientific insight.
- Arguments against acceptance: small network scale, arbitrary hyperparameters, and limited spiking translation; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
