- Proposes a control-theoretic framework that directly optimizes excitatory–inhibitory networks to make arbitrary analog patterns stable fixed points.  
- Uses a rate-based circuit model and a smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) for robust stability optimization.  
- Optimized networks operate in the balanced regime and are robust to corrupted cues and ongoing noise.  
- Model explains the commonly observed reduction of trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.  
- Provides a thorough review of prior memory network models and their limitations.  
- Simulations show storage and recall of multiple analog memories with high accuracy under noise.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes informative, well-designed figures.  
- Methodology and simulation details are clearly and concisely presented.  
- Novel use of the smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) for robustness optimization.  
- Significant contribution to neural network and memory modeling with potential applications (prosthetics, BMIs, AI).  
- Main limitation: relies on a rate-based formulation, limiting direct applicability to spiking networks.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale simulations.  
- Does not clearly explain practical deployment or use of optimized networks in applications like neural prosthetics or BMIs.  
- Recommendation: add computational complexity analysis, clarify practical uses, and consider extending the approach to spiking models.
