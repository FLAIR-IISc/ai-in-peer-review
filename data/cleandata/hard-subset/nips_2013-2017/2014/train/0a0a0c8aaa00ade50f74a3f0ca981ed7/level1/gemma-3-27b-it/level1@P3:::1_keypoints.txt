- Novel control-theoretic framework that builds attractor networks satisfying multiple physiological constraints simultaneously (Dale’s law, graded activity, reasonable firing rates, E/I balance).  
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to optimize stability, avoiding difficulties of directly optimizing spectral abscissa.  
- Demonstrates robustness to noise in recall cues and ongoing dynamics, improving biological plausibility.  
- Provides a mechanistic explanation for trial-to-trial variability reduction observed in cortex.  
- Enforces biological realism (Dale’s law, non-saturating dynamics, graded patterns) improving over prior attractor models.  
- Thorough analysis of dynamics: synaptic weight distributions, E/I balance, role of weak synapses, and an informative ablation study.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful figures despite mathematical complexity.  
- Optimization procedure is computationally intensive with unclear scalability to larger networks or complex tasks.  
- Key optimization details (iterations, convergence criteria) and computational cost are underreported.  
- Parameter choices (ηs, ηF, ε) seem heuristic; sensitivity analysis and theoretical justification (especially ε scaling) are lacking.  
- Rate-based model omits temporal dynamics and spike-timing plasticity, limiting biological validity and learning interpretation.  
- Memory capacity exploration is limited (m=30, n=150); scaling with network size/noise and comparison to theoretical limits are needed.  
- Choice of log-normal distribution for memory patterns is plausible but not rigorously justified or compared to alternatives.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, biologically realistic, robust results, and links to experimental observations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, heuristic parameter choices, and limitations of rate-based simplification.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Revision request 1: provide detailed optimization procedure (iterations, convergence criteria, computational cost).  
- Revision request 2: conduct sensitivity analysis of key parameters (ηs, ηF, ε).  
- Revision request 3: discuss limitations of rate-based modeling and pathways to extend to spiking networks.  
- Revision request 4: explore how memory capacity scales with network size and noise levels (and relate to theoretical capacity limits).
