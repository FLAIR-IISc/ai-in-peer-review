- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks that respect Dale’s law, sparsity, and graded activity by directly optimizing network parameters.  
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa and robust-control ideas to enforce stability and create stable fixed-point memories.  
- Extends prior memory-network work (e.g., Hopfield models) by allowing non-binary patterns and satisfying physiological constraints.  
- Overcomes previous models’ violations of Dale’s law and limitations to binary memories.  
- Successfully incorporates key physiological constraints: Dale’s law, sparse connectivity, and graded activity.  
- Demonstrates robustness to corrupted memory cues and ongoing noise.  
- Produces networks operating in the balanced excitation–inhibition regime consistent with experiments.  
- Novel combination of control-theoretic and robust-control techniques for neural memory networks.  
- Optimization procedure may be computationally intensive, limiting scalability to large networks.  
- Relies on simplifying assumptions about single-neuron models and gain functions that may reduce biological realism.  
- Generalizability may be limited; approach might not apply directly to other neural network types or systems.  
- Pro-acceptance: significant advance in understanding memory substrates and produces robust, stable, biologically plausible networks.  
- Pro-acceptance: methodological novelty by blending control theory with neural network design.  
- Con-acceptance: computational cost could hinder practical applicability to large-scale systems.  
- Con-acceptance: unrealistic neuron/gain assumptions and limited applicability to other architectures.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear organization and well-supported claims via theory and experiments.  
- Writing and figures are clear and accessible, though some technical details may challenge non-experts.  
- Approach is original and potentially impactful for neuroscience and machine learning.  
- Could have more discussion on limitations and future work.
