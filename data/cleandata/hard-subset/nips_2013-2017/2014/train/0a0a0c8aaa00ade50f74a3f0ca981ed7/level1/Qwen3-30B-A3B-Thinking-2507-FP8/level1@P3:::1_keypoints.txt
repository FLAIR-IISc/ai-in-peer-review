- Paper proposes a novel control-theoretic framework using Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to construct stable analog memory networks that respect Dale’s law, balanced E-I dynamics, and non-saturating firing rates.
- Formalizes the problem via fixed-point conditions (dv/dt=0) and stability (spectral abscissa α(J)<0) and uses SSA as a differentiable proxy for optimization.
- Experimental validation shows robustness to noise, sparse connectivity feasibility, maintained E-I balance, and reduced trial-to-trial variability.
- Storage capacity reported is modest (~0.2 patterns per neuron) but reasonable given physiological constraints.
- Technical soundness is strong, with clear methodology and principled optimization, though SSA–stability relationship needs more rigorous analysis for larger networks.
- Claim that “small weights are not necessary” requires stronger statistical validation beyond single-instance evidence.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with effective figures and mathematical exposition.
- Transition from rate-based to spiking networks is only briefly treated and needs more detailed discussion for biological plausibility.
- Relation between the rectified quadratic gain used and real cortical neuron I/O curves should be elaborated.
- Use of SSA optimization in this context is novel and addresses a key gap between theoretical memory models and physiological constraints.
- Could better distinguish the approach from recent balanced network models that achieve low firing rates via different mechanisms.
- The model’s explanation of reduced trial-to-trial variability links well to experimental observations and extends prior binary-pattern work to graded patterns.
- Practical neuroscience impact would be stronger with more direct connections to experimental datasets or electrophysiological predictions.
- Pro-accept: first demonstration of stable analog memory with physiological constraints, rigorous SSA framework, broad experimental validation, and clear writing.
- Con-accept: modest storage capacity, limited spiking translation, insufficient statistical validation for some claims, and minimal comparison to alternative balanced dynamics approaches.
- Recommendation: accept at NIPS as a significant and well-executed advance in theoretical neuroscience.
