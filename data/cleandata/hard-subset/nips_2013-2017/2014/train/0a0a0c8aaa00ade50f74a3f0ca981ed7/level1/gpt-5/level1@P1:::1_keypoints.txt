- Proposes a control-theoretic method to construct E/I rate RNNs that store and retrieve multiple analog memory patterns as stable fixed points while satisfying Dale’s law, low firing rates, non-saturating nonlinearities, and dynamic E/I balance.  
- Formulates storage per memory with two objectives: (i) minimize dv/dt at target to create (approximate) fixed points and (ii) minimize Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to ensure local stability.  
- Enforces Dale’s law via a softplus reparameterization, adds L2 weight regularization, and jointly optimizes synapses while treating inhibitory steady-state activities per memory as free variables.  
- Demonstrates robust recall from noisy cues, retention under ongoing noise, memory-specific E/I current cancellation, sparsity-like weight distributions, and trial-to-trial variability quenching.  
- Technical formulation is clear and principled; simulations show SSA<0 for targeted patterns and recall performance near a nearest-neighbor ideal-observer bound.  
- Balanced regime convincingly characterized with E/I cancellation plots across memories.  
- Caveat: optimizing inhibitory steady-state activities per memory introduces n_I×m extra degrees of freedom, raising concerns of over-parameterization and whether stability depends on unconstrained memory-specific inhibitory states.  
- Caveat: capacity and scaling not systematically analyzed; reported capacity ~0.2 patterns/neuron for n=150 with unclear scaling in n, m, and SSA tolerance ε.  
- Caveat: fixed-point conditions are only approximately satisfied (dv/dt small but nonzero); basin sizes and distances to true fixed points are not quantified.  
- Caveat: uses a rectified-quadratic gain without saturation; sensitivity to gain exponent and soft saturation is not tested.  
- Caveat: limited empirical comparisons to alternative E/I attractor constructions and trained RNN approaches (e.g., clustered balanced networks, FORCE).  
- Manuscript clarity is generally good, with clear SSA rationale and illustrative figures, but computational complexity/scaling and biological interpretation of memory-specific inhibitory states need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: novel combination of explicit SSA-based stability optimization, Dale’s-law E/I separation, analog low-rate pattern embedding, and demonstration of balanced dynamics and variability quenching.  
- Significance: provides a constructive, biologically constrained recipe linking robust control and memory networks, but impact is limited by current network scale, modest capacity, and use of per-memory inhibitory auxiliaries.  
- Strength: principled tying of fixed-point and stability conditions into optimization that yields biologically plausible E/I balanced memories.  
- Strength: robust recall comparable to ideal-observer bound and resilience to ongoing noise.  
- Strength: emergent sparsity-like weight distributions and variability quenching consistent with cortical observations.  
- Suggestion: constrain or regularize per-memory inhibitory steady-states, or train with fixed inhibitory parameters to test robustness and parsimony.  
- Suggestion: provide systematic capacity and scaling analysis (capacity vs n and m), report computational costs (SSA eigendecomposition scaling) and how ε should scale with network size.  
- Suggestion: quantify fixed-point accuracy and basin sizes (distance to true fixed points, attraction basins).  
- Suggestion: perform sensitivity analysis to gain function shape (power-law exponent, soft saturation).  
- Suggestion: include comparative baselines (clustered balanced architectures, trained RNNs like FORCE) to contextualize trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: demonstrate a path to spiking networks or a proof-of-concept translation using learned weights and matched f–I curves to strengthen biological relevance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled method integrating robust control with biologically constrained memory networks and clear demonstrations of balanced dynamics and variability quenching.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability and capacity unestablished, reliance on per-memory inhibitory auxiliary variables, and limited comparisons to alternative approaches.
