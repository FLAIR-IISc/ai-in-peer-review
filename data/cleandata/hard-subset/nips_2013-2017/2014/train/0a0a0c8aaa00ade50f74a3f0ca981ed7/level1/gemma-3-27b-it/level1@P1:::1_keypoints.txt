- Proposes a control-theoretic framework that directly optimizes network parameters to produce stable fixed points for arbitrary analog patterns.  
- Enforces physiological constraints including Dale’s law, graded activity, realistic firing rates, and balanced excitation–inhibition.  
- Uses the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) and robust control tools to optimize stability rather than relying on architecture-specific properties.  
- Demonstrates robustness to noise and explains observed reductions in trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.  
- Extends associative memory work by addressing binary representations, capacity limits, and reliance on saturating dynamics.  
- Novelty lies in unified treatment of multiple biological constraints and direct optimization for fixed-point stability.  
- Clear presentation and thorough explanation of methods and results.  
- Computational cost and scalability are unclear; optimization (even with BFGS) may be expensive for large networks.  
- Key hyperparameters (ηs, ηF, ε) appear chosen heuristically; systematic parameter-space analysis is missing.  
- Reliance on a rate-based model limits direct applicability to spiking neural dynamics; bridging this gap is under-discussed.  
- Biological validation is limited beyond trial-to-trial variability; more comparisons (e.g., synaptic distributions, plasticity rules) would strengthen claims.  
- Pre-conditioning the network with a stable baseline state may simplify optimization and limit generality; impact of not pre-setting baseline is not explored.  
- Observed sparsity may not be fully explained by the optimization objective; initialization, structure, and gain choices likely contribute.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advancement, well-motivated control-theoretic framework, demonstrated robustness, and biological relevance.  
- Arguments for rejection: practical limitations from computational cost, parameter sensitivity, rate-model gap, and heuristic choices.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions addressing parameter sensitivity, scalability, and strategies to relate rate-based results to spiking dynamics.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
