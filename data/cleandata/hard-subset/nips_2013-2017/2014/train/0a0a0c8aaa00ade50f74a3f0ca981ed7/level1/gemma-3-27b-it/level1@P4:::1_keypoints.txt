- Proposes a control-theoretic framework for attractor networks that enforces Dale’s law and graded neuronal activity.  
- Directly optimizes synaptic weights and inhibitory firing rates to stabilize analog memory patterns in a balanced regime.  
- Builds on classical memory-network theory by addressing binary representations, saturation, and lack of explicit inhibitory neurons.  
- Uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) from robust control theory to ensure network stability.  
- Technically sound with a clear mathematical formulation.  
- Clever and significant application of SSA to neural-network optimization.  
- Simulation results convincingly show robust storage and recall of analog memories.  
- Inclusion of ongoing noise and observed post-stimulus variability reduction strengthens biological relevance.  
- Reliance on heuristic parameter settings (ηs, ηF, ε) undermines reproducibility and generalizability.  
- Fixed-point condition is not exactly met but is close; authors acknowledge this limitation.  
- The work presents a complete, functional model rather than a position paper.  
- Generally well-written and organized; introduction and methods are clear for expert readers.  
- Mathematical notation could be improved; some variables (e.g., vµexc, vµinh) need explicit definitions.  
- The link between Eq. (5) and biological intuition should be explained more clearly.  
- Novel combination of control theory, SSA, and biologically realistic constraints; SSA application is a key original contribution.  
- Adequately differentiates from prior work and provides comprehensive related-work references.  
- Potentially significant for understanding neural memory substrates and inspiring artificial memory algorithms; shows sparsity–performance link.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical rigor, robust recall results, alignment with experiments, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic parameter dependence, notation/readability issues, model simplifications, and possibly overstated novelty.
