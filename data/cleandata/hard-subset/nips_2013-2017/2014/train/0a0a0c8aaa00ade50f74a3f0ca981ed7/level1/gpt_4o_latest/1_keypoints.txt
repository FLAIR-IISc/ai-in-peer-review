- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks that embed graded memories while enforcing Dale's law, sparse connectivity, and E–I balance.  
- Optimizes synaptic weights using the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to ensure robust fixed-point stability.  
- Resulting networks show robust memory recall, low firing rates, and reduced trial-to-trial variability, matching cortical observations.  
- Addresses prior model limitations such as binary memories, unrealistic inhibitory feedback, and saturating activity.  
- Strength: Physiological plausibility via biologically constrained connectivity and dynamics.  
- Strength: Robustness to noise and corrupted cues beyond many earlier models.  
- Strength: Novel use of SSA for stability optimization.  
- Strength: Experimental relevance—reproduces variability reduction seen in sensory/motor cortices.  
- Strength: Clear presentation with detailed analyses, figures, and metrics.  
- Weakness: Rate-based model limits direct applicability to spiking neural dynamics.  
- Weakness: Optimization is global/algorithmic, raising questions about biologically plausible local learning rules.  
- Weakness: Demonstrated storage capacity (~0.2) is low and scalability to larger networks is unclear.  
- Weakness: Computationally intensive optimization (SSA) may hinder practical scaling and simulations.  
- For acceptance: Significant contribution to realism and robustness in memory models using innovative control-theoretic methods.  
- Against acceptance: Reliance on rate dynamics and global optimization, plus unresolved scalability and computational feasibility concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address scalability and applicability to spiking models.
