This paper addresses the important problem of building biologically plausible memory networks that can store and recall graded, analog patterns. The authors propose a control-theoretic framework based on optimizing network parameters to create stable fixed points corresponding to desired memory states, using the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for robust stability analysis. They explicitly incorporate Dale’s law, realistic firing rate dynamics, and a balance of excitation and inhibition. This work builds on previous attempts to create more realistic memory models (e.g., [7, 8, 12]) by addressing limitations in stability, firing rate control, and biological realism.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Biologically Plausible Constraints:** The model explicitly incorporates Dale’s Law and attempts to generate balanced excitation and inhibition, making it more realistic than many previous memory models.
* **Novel Optimization Framework:** The use of robust control theory and the SSA for stability optimization is a novel and promising approach. It addresses the challenges of achieving stability in recurrent networks with graded activity.
* **Demonstrated Performance:** The results show successful storage and recall of analog memories, with performance comparable to an "ideal observer." The network exhibits robustness to noise in both the recall cue and ongoing dynamics.
* **Sparsity & Efficiency:** The resulting networks exhibit sparse connectivity, a feature observed in real cortical circuits, and demonstrate that a significant proportion of these weak connections are not essential for recall performance.
* **Variability Reduction:** The network demonstrates a reduction in neural variability following stimulus onset, a feature observed in biological systems [21].
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methods, and results.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** The optimization process is computationally expensive. The paper doesn't thoroughly discuss scalability to larger networks or more complex memory sets.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The method relies on several heuristic parameters (ηs, ηF, ε). The sensitivity of the results to these parameters isn't adequately explored. The choice of ε, especially, seems somewhat ad-hoc and the scaling rule for larger networks feels arbitrary.
* **Limited Exploration of Dynamics:** While the paper demonstrates stable fixed points, it doesn't deeply investigate the full dynamical landscape of the network. Understanding the basins of attraction and the potential for spurious attractors would be valuable.
* **Simplification of Neuron Model:** The use of a threshold-quadratic gain function (Eq. 1), while simplifying, doesn't capture the full complexity of neuronal dynamics, like adaptation or spike timing. While acknowledged, the impact of this simplification is not thoroughly discussed.
* **Lack of Comparison to Alternative Methods:** The results are compared to an ideal observer, but not to other state-of-the-art memory models, particularly those incorporating similar biological constraints.
* **Justification of Log-Normal Distribution:** The choice of a log-normal distribution for generating memory patterns is motivated by observed firing rate distributions, but a more rigorous justification or sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.


**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and potentially significant approach to building biologically plausible memory networks.
* The combination of control theory, robust stability analysis, and biological constraints is innovative.
* The results demonstrate promising performance and robustness.
* The work addresses a core problem in computational neuroscience and has the potential to advance our understanding of memory mechanisms.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost and parameter sensitivity raise concerns about the practical applicability and generalizability of the method.
* The lack of detailed analysis of the dynamical landscape and comparison to alternative models limits the impact of the work.
* The simplified neuron model may limit the biological relevance of the findings.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an important problem with a novel and promising approach. The authors demonstrate compelling results that showcase the potential of their method. However, the limitations related to computational cost, parameter sensitivity, and the depth of dynamical analysis prevent me from recommending unconditional acceptance. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly:

1. **Scalability:** Discuss the computational limitations and potential strategies for scaling the method to larger networks.
2. **Parameter Sensitivity:** Perform a more thorough sensitivity analysis of the key parameters (ηs, ηF, ε) and provide guidance on choosing appropriate values.
3. **Dynamical Landscape:** Investigate the basins of attraction and the presence of spurious attractors.
4. **Comparison to Alternatives:** Compare the performance of the proposed method to other state-of-the-art memory models with similar biological constraints.
5. **Justification of Pattern Generation:** Provide a more rigorous justification for the choice of the log-normal distribution.
6. **Clarify ε scaling:** Provide a stronger theoretical or empirical justification for the heuristic scaling of ε based on network size.



Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential impact on the field.
