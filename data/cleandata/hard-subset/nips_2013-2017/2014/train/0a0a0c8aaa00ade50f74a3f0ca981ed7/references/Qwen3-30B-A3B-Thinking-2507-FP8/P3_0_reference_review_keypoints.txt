- Summary: Proposes a biologically plausible rate-based E-I network that stores and recalls analog memory patterns by optimizing stability via the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa, respecting Dale's law and balanced sparse connectivity.

- Strength 1: Novel use of the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa for stability optimization enabling non-saturating graded memories.

- Strength 2: Integrates multiple biological constraints (Dale's law, explicit inhibitory neurons, low firing rates, E-I balance, sparse connectivity).

- Strength 3: Rigorous evaluation including noise robustness, synaptic sparsity, E-I balance, reduced variability, and an ideal-observer comparison.

- Strength 4: Clear theoretical framework defining fixed-point storage conditions and a principled optimization approach.

- Strength 5: Strong experimental results demonstrating convergence, robust recall, and balanced dynamics.

- Weakness 1: Scalability is unclear—tested only at 30 memories/150 neurons with declining performance as memories increase.

- Weakness 2: Connection to spiking networks is speculative with no empirical verification of the rate-to-spike gain function.

- Weakness 3: Limited analysis of memory interference and performance with cues near multiple stored patterns.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient direct quantitative comparisons to other biologically plausible memory models cited in the paper.

- Weakness 5: Optimization procedure details and computational complexity (large number of parameters) are under-specified for larger networks.

- Overall assessment: High-quality, significant contribution that meaningfully advances biological plausibility of analog memory models, but needs more on scalability and spiking connections.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Suggestion 1: Expand discussion and analysis of how storage capacity scales with network size.

- Suggestion 2: Provide empirical evidence or preliminary spiking-network results to justify the rate-to-spike mapping.

- Suggestion 3: Analyze memory interference when cues are similar to multiple stored patterns.

- Suggestion 4: Add a comparison table quantitatively contrasting this approach with key prior models.

- Suggestion 5: Detail the optimization algorithm's computational cost and practical limits for larger networks.
