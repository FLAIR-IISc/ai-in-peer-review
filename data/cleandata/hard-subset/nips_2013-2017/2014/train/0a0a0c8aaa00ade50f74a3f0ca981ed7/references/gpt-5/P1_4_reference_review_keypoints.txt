- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to build Dale-constrained recurrent E/I networks storing multiple graded (analog) attractors using softplus weight reparameterization and rectified-quadratic gains.  
- Core technical idea: jointly optimize fixed-point residuals (minimize ‖dv/dt‖^2) and Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for local stability over weights and per-memory inhibitory steady-states.  
- Demonstrates robust autoassociative recall from noisy cues, resilience to ongoing noise, dynamic E/I balance, low firing rates, and heavy-tailed weight distributions; reports capacity ≈0.2 (30 attractors in N=150).  
- Novelty: applies SSA-based robust stability optimization to Dale-constrained, balanced, graded attractor networks, enabling graded attractors without saturating single-neuron nonlinearities.  
- Related work: connects to Hopfield networks, graded-response capacity analyses, E/I balance/stability literature, and SSA methods; should more explicitly contrast with FORCE and Lyapunov-based stabilization.  
- Soundness: objective formulation and optimization (L-BFGS) are reasonable; softplus for Dale’s law is appropriate; empirical results are coherent.  
- Caveat: learned states are "slow points" with nonzero fixed-point residuals; existence of nearby true fixed points is plausible but unproven and should be checked numerically.  
- Caveat: optimizing per-memory inhibitory steady-states (vinhµ) as free auxiliary variables increases degrees of freedom and may inflate apparent capacity/stability, raising biological-interpretability concerns.  
- Caveat: capacity results are reported for a single network size only; no scaling analysis with N, E/I ratio, pattern statistics, or SSA smoothing ε is provided.  
- Caveat: trained weights are heavy-tailed but structural sparsity is not enforced; clipping leaves a dense graph, so claims about sparsity should be moderated or enforced explicitly.  
- Caveat: SSA smoothing parameter ε is heuristically chosen (ε = 0.01·150/n) and unvalidated; sensitivity analyses are needed.  
- Clarity: overall clear and organized, but needs more detail on SSA computation/gradients, exact ε, optimization settings, and full simulation parameters.  
- Clarity: clarify the role and regularization of vinhµ during training versus recall, define external inputs h and τi, and justify success thresholds and robustness to their choice.  
- Originality: the methodological combination is original and timely, but broader comparisons to alternative recurrent stabilization/training methods are warranted.  
- Significance: framework could be impactful if shown to scale; current experiments are a promising proof-of-concept but limited in scope.  
- Pros: respects biological constraints (Dale, low rates, E/I balance), uses principled SSA stability objective, achieves robust recall and variability quenching consistent with cortical data.  
- Cons: reliance on per-memory inhibitory variables, lack of scaling/capacity analysis, no formal guarantee of true fixed-point existence, structural sparsity not enforced, and limited method comparisons.  
- Suggested experiments: provide N/m scaling and ε sensitivity, ablate or regularize vinhµ, numerically find exact fixed points near slow points, add explicit sparsity (L1/hard masks), compare to alternatives (e.g., FORCE), and release code/pseudocode.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept — technically sound and novel but needs ablations and scaling/sparsity analyses to strengthen biological plausibility and impact.
