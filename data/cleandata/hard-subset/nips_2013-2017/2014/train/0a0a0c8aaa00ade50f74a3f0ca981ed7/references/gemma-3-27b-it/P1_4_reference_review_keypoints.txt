- Summary: Proposes a biologically constrained rate-based E/I memory network, frames memory storage as a control problem, and uses smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) to create stable analog fixed-point memories with robust recall and E/I balance.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Hopfield and later models, addresses their biological shortcomings, and leverages robust control/SSA while comparing to spatially connected and spike-timing models.
- Strength — biological realism: Enforces Dale’s law, sparse connectivity, low firing rates, and E/I balance absent in many prior memory models.
- Strength — control-theoretic approach: Novel formulation using SSA to jointly optimize storage and stability of fixed-point attractors.
- Strength — graded activity and robustness: Supports graded analog patterns and robust recall under noisy inputs, overcoming binary-state limitations.
- Strength — analysis & presentation: Thorough analysis of weights, E/I balance, pruning effects; clear writing and informative figures; reproducibility aided by NLopt usage.
- Weakness — rate-based simplification: Rate model omits spike-timing dynamics; translation to spiking networks and associated challenges need more discussion.
- Weakness — parameter tuning: Key hyperparameters (ηs, ηF, ε) chosen heuristically; sensitivity and justification (e.g., ε scaling) are underexplored.
- Weakness — limited capacity: Reported storage capacity (~0.2) is low; scaling with network size and comparison to theoretical limits are missing.
- Weakness — optimization issues: Potential local optima and convergence challenges of SSA-based optimization are glossed over and need elaboration.
- Weakness — baseline attractor bias: Strong bias toward baseline attractor from initial conditions may be problematic; unclear if this is generic or an initialization artifact.
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles a fundamental neuroscience problem with a novel, theoretically grounded method and demonstrates strong stability, robustness, and biological plausibility.
- Arguments against acceptance: Concerns about generalizability due to rate model, low capacity, heuristic tuning, and insufficient treatment of optimization pitfalls.
- Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution with limitations that are addressable; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested minor revisions: Expand discussion on spiking-network translation, provide parameter sensitivity analysis, discuss capacity scaling, detail optimization procedure (iterations, convergence, local-optima strategies), and clarify the role/sensitivity of the baseline attractor.
