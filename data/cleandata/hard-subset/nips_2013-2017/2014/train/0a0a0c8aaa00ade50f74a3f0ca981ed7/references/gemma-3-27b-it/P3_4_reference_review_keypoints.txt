- Presents a rigorous method for designing recurrent networks that store analog (graded) memories while respecting biological constraints (Dale’s Law, E/I balance).  
- Frames memory storage as stabilizing desired activity patterns as fixed points and applies Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) optimization to enforce robust stability.  
- Addresses key limitations of prior models: violations of Dale’s Law, reliance on saturating dynamics, lack of explicit inhibition, and sensitivity to noise.  
- Situates work within Hopfield-style literature and credits prior SSA/control-theory methods appropriately.  
- Novel contribution: applying robust control theory/SSA to memory network design to achieve stable fixed points and robust dynamics.  
- Biological realism: enforces Dale’s Law, excitation-inhibition balance, and graded activity for more plausible neural dynamics.  
- Demonstrates strong robustness to noisy cues, achieving performance comparable to an “ideal observer.”  
- Reports encouraging storage capacity (~0.2), suggesting scalability potential.  
- Finds emergence of sparse connectivity and analyzes its role, noting sparsity is not essential.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across recall performance, noise robustness, and synaptic weight roles.  
- Generally clear writing, but complex methods require careful reading.  
- Methodological complexity: SSA calculation and gradient-based optimization are computationally expensive and hard to reproduce without expertise.  
- Parameter choices (ηs, ηF, ε) are heuristic; more systematic tuning and justification are needed.  
- SSA sensitivity to the smoothness parameter ε is acknowledged but not fully explored.  
- Biological scope limited by use of rate-based neurons; extension to spiking models remains future work.  
- Lacks discussion or proposals for biologically plausible learning rules to implement optimized connectivity.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: report computational resources used, systematically justify key parameters, discuss spiking and learning-rule extensions, and add a supplementary figure illustrating SSA calculation.
