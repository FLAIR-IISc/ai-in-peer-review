- Overall assessment: Technically sophisticated, biologically plausible approach to analog memory networks; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: Control-theoretic framework optimizing synapses and inhibitory activity to store graded memories while enforcing Dale’s law and stability.  
- Key innovation: Use of the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for gradient-based robust stability optimization.  
- Biological realism: Explicit enforcement of Dale’s law and E/I balance improves plausibility over prior models.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates stable storage and recall of analog memories with robustness to noise and comparison to an ideal observer.  
- Presentation: Clear, thorough writing with helpful equations and figures despite methodological complexity.  
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental computational neuroscience problem with a novel, powerful methodology.  
- Strength: Robustness to noisy initial conditions and ongoing noise is convincingly shown.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation and useful analysis of dynamics and weight distributions.  
- Weakness: Optimization appears computationally intensive and scalability to larger networks is unclear.  
- Weakness: Reliance on heuristically chosen parameters (ηs, ηF, ε) without systematic exploration or justification.  
- Weakness: Limited treatment of how results would generalize to spiking neuron models; only discussed briefly.  
- Weakness: Mechanism behind observed synaptic sparsity (claimed to arise from stability) is not fully explained.  
- Suggestion: Cite Ref [9] more specifically for dynamic feedback inhibition claims.  
- Suggestion: Clarify units in Equation 3 and the noise model’s statistical properties (mean, variance).  
- Suggestion: Add schematic of E/I population interactions and report optimization stopping criteria and runtimes.  
- Suggestion: Justify ε=0.01 and test sensitivity; provide confidence intervals or SDs for recall performance.  
- Suggestion: Explain why L2 regularization doesn’t produce sparsity and elaborate on stability-driven sparsity mechanisms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, significant, biologically plausible, technically sound, robust results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Potential computational complexity/scalability, heuristic parameter tuning, and limited spiking-model exploration.
