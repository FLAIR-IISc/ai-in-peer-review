- Proposes an optimization-based framework to embed multiple graded attractor memories in Dale-compliant EI recurrent rate networks using a threshold-quadratic f–I curve and smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) for stability.  
- Allows per-memory inhibitory steady-state potentials v_inh(µ) as free variables and optimizes all synapses to match fixed-point conditions and local stability.  
- Demonstrates recall from noisy cues, robustness to ongoing noise, balanced E/I at recall, emergence of many near-zero synapses, and variability quenching.  
- Embeds m=30 memories in a 150-neuron network (capacity ~0.2 per neuron) with recall comparable to an ideal nearest-neighbor observer.  
- Connects classic associative memory models and balanced EI dynamics with robust control tools; however, ML training methods (FORCE, low-rank RNNs) are not discussed.  
- Conceptual novelty: principled control-theoretic formulation to embed graded attractors under Dale’s law.  
- Biological relevance: stabilizes low-rate regimes without f–I saturation, enforces Dale’s law, and produces heavy-tailed firing distributions.  
- Empirical demonstrations align with cortical phenomenology and are convincing for the reported effects.  
- Interesting emergence of many near-zero “silent” synapses without explicit sparsity regularization; weak-weight pruning preserves most memories.  
- Fixed-point condition is only minimized (‖dv/dt‖ small) not enforced exactly; SSA evaluated at non-fixed points is problematic and true fixed points should be demonstrated.  
- Capacity and scaling are underexplored: only one network size reported, no systematic analysis vs N, E/I ratio, pattern statistics, or basins of attraction.  
- Treating v_inh(µ) as free per-memory variables may artificially inflate capacity and stability; biological plausibility needs testing without per-memory inhibitory freedoms.  
- SSA provides only local linear stability; global stability, runaway dynamics, and unintended high-rate attractors are not sufficiently assessed.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to alternative training methods (FORCE/full-FORCE, low-rank constructions, spectral/Lyapunov regularization).  
- Connectivity starts dense and sparsity may be an artifact of L2 shrinkage or parameterization; biological degree distributions and heterogeneity robustness not analyzed.  
- Reproducibility issues: SSA definition, gradients, computational cost, optimization settings, and many hyperparameters are insufficiently specified; some thresholds look arbitrary.  
- Overall technically competent but key claims rely on approximate fixed-point satisfaction and single-scale experiments; more rigorous validation needed.  
- Suggested improvements: enforce dv/dt=0 or find true nearby fixed points and report distances/spectra; perform systematic capacity/scaling and basin analyses; remove/regularize per-memory inhibitory targets; compare to baselines; provide full methodological details and code.  
- Pros: principled and biologically motivated approach with compelling qualitative phenomena and robust recall demonstrations.  
- Cons: approximate fixed-point satisfaction, limited capacity/scaling analysis, per-memory inhibitory freedoms, and lack of comparisons.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject (leans weak accept if authors address fixed-point exactness, provide basic capacity/scaling analysis, and ablate per-memory inhibitory freedoms).
