- Paper proposes a control-theoretic optimization to embed multiple graded attractor memories in EI-balanced rate networks while respecting Dale’s law and low firing rates.  
- Method jointly minimizes dv/dt at target patterns and the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) of the Jacobian to enforce local stability.  
- Dale’s law enforced via softplus reparameterization; inhibitory steady-state activities per memory treated as auxiliary optimization variables.  
- Demonstrations show recall robustness to noisy cues and ongoing noise, emergence of E/I balance, and that a few strong synapses are functionally critical.  
- Capacity demonstrated empirically is modest (~30 memories in 150 neurons, capacity ~0.2).  
- Technique differs from classic Hopfield and LIF-balanced approaches by not relying on saturating nonlinearities or global inhibition for stability.  
- Optimization objective is coherent: driving dv/dt toward zero and minimizing SSA enforces approximate fixed points and local asymptotic stability.  
- Dale constraint implementation via log(1+exp) is clean and differentiable.  
- Results show SSA and spectral abscissa decrease during training and recall basins comparable to an ideal observer.  
- Fixed points are not guaranteed exactly; dv/dt remains small but nonzero and exact nearby fixed points are unverified.  
- Learning is biologically implausible (global LBFGS, free inhibitory variables per memory), limiting mechanistic claims.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments are modest scale (n=150), SSA computation may not scale well and runtime/memory are not reported.  
- Comparisons are limited: no benchmarks against sign-constrained graded Hopfield, FORCE/RSNN, Lyapunov-based, or modern Hopfield methods.  
- Sparsity finding is qualitative: many weights are small but not structurally zero, clipping is heuristic and can eliminate attractors.  
- Clarity is generally good, but some hyperparameters/details are relegated to supplement and exact residuals/SA at convergence and runtime complexity are missing.  
- Originality: novel use of SSA for embedding analog attractors in Dale-constrained EI-balanced networks and treating inhibitory steady-states as optimization variables.  
- Significance: constructive and interesting for linking robust control and neuroscience, but limited by scale, lack of theoretical capacity/basin analysis, and biological implausibility of learning.  
- Requested clarifications: locate exact fixed points post-training and report SA there, provide scaling/runtime experiments, compare to alternative methods, explore structural sparsity, and discuss local inhibitory plasticity approximations.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept for NeurIPS CNS track — technically sound and novel but limited in scale, comparisons, and biological plausibility; reviewer confidence medium.
