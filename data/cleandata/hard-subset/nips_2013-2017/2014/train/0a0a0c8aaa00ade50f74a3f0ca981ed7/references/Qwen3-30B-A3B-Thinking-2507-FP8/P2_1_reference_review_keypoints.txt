- Introduces a novel framework for storing analog (graded) memories as stable fixed points in a biologically plausible excitatory–inhibitory (E–I) network.  
- Model respects Dale’s law, supports graded activity, maintains low firing rates, and achieves dynamic E–I balance.  
- Optimizes synaptic weights and inhibitory activity using a control-theoretic approach minimizing the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) to ensure stability.  
- Demonstrates robust memory recall from noisy cues, sparse connectivity, and balanced E–I inputs across memory patterns.  
- Provides a clear mathematical formulation and a differentiable relaxation (SSA) to handle stability optimization.  
- Experimental validation is thorough with appropriate metrics (e.g., normalized distance) and comparisons to an ideal observer.  
- Acknowledges that stored patterns are “slow points” (small non-zero dv/dt) rather than exact fixed points and discusses implications.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility, with effective figures.  
- Explanation of inhibitory neurons as “auxiliary” variables could be more detailed in Methods.  
- Novelty: combines multiple previously separate desiderata (Dale’s law, graded patterns, low rates, E–I balance) in one model.  
- Novel application of SSA/control theory to neural network stability and creative use of inhibitory neurons as optimization variables.  
- Comparison to the most similar recent work (e.g., [12]) is somewhat superficial and needs more detailed benchmarking.  
- Biological plausibility would be strengthened by a more thorough translation from rate-based to spiking networks.  
- Significant contribution: advances theoretical understanding of memory in biologically plausible circuits and opens directions for inhibitory plasticity and local learning rules.  
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality, technically sound, and a meaningful advance for NeurIPS.  
- Specific suggestions: expand discussion on rate-to-spiking mapping and gain functions, provide detailed comparison to related work, discuss possible local synaptic learning implementations, and analyze storage capacity scaling.
