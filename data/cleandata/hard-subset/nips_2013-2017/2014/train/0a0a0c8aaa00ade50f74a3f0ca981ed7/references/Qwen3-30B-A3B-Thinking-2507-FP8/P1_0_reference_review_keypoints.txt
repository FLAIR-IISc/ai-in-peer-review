- Addresses a major limitation in memory models by enforcing Dale's law, graded low-rate activity, and dynamic E-I balance using an SSA-based optimization to make analog patterns stable fixed points.
- Builds on and extends prior models (Hopfield, rate, spiking, balanced networks) and explicitly targets analog memory patterns rather than binary or spatial bumps.
- Technically sound and mathematically rigorous; SSA optimization is well-motivated and experiments validate capacity, recall robustness, sparsity, and E-I balance.
- Appropriately acknowledges limitations, notably absence of a biologically plausible learning rule and scalability concerns.
- Clearly written and well-organized with detailed methods and excellent figures; could use a brief justification for preferring SSA over SA.
- Novel contribution by bridging control theory and computational neuroscience—applying SSA to enable biologically plausible analog memory storage under Dale’s law.
- Significant impact: shows analog memory is feasible without saturation, demonstrates robust recall and E-I balance, and opens avenues for control-theoretic approaches in neuroscience and ML.
- Specific concern: discussion of mapping optimized rate connectivity to spiking networks is superficial and needs more detail on synaptic mechanisms.
- Specific concern: scalability of the optimization with network size and number of memories is not addressed; potential computational bottlenecks should be discussed.
- Specific concern: insufficient comparison to some recent memory models (e.g., Lengyel et al.); connections to inhibitory plasticity work (Vogels et al.) could be deeper.
- Specific concern: the SSA smoothing parameter ε is chosen heuristically without sensitivity analysis; robustness to this choice should be shown.
- Pros: fills a literature gap, novel SSA application, robust analog storage with realistic E-I balance, biologically plausible network properties, comprehensive validation.
- Cons: limited discussion of scaling and spiking implementations, unclear application to real neural data, lacks some comparisons to recent models.
- Recommendation: Accept.
