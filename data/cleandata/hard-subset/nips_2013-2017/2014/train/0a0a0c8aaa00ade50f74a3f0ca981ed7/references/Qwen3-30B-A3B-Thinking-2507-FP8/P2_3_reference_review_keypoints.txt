- Addresses building biologically plausible networks that store and recall analog patterns while respecting Dale's law, sparse connectivity, low firing rates, and E-I balance.  
- Proposes a control-theoretic framework that optimizes connectivity to embed multiple analog memory patterns as stable fixed points.  
- Introduces the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) as a smooth surrogate for the spectral abscissa to enable gradient-based stability optimization.  
- Produces networks with sparse connectivity, robust recall from noisy cues, and tight E-I balance maintaining low firing rates.  
- Technically sound, well-executed, and reproducible with comprehensive experiments and ablation studies.  
- Novel integration of multiple biological constraints; SSA-based stability optimization is a key original contribution.  
- Clear writing and organization; methods and figures effectively communicate results.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: discuss how optimization scales with network size and memory count.  
- Limitations: rate-based model (not spiking), computational cost of optimization underexplored, and no local-plasticity learning rule provided.  
- Significant implications for computational neuroscience and machine learning, with potential extensions to spiking networks.  
- Strong case for acceptance: addresses a major gap, combines constraints in one framework, and demonstrates robust, biologically plausible memory storage.
