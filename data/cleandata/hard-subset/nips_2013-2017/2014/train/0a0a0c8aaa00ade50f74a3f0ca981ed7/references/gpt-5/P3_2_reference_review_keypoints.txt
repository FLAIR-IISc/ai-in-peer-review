- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to embed multiple graded memory patterns as stable attractors in a Dale-compliant E/I rate network using SSA for robust local stability.
- Parametrizes excitatory/inhibitory weights via softplus, uses rectified-quadratic neurons, and jointly optimizes weights and per-memory inhibitory steady-states by minimizing fixed-point residuals, SSA, and L2 weight penalty.
- Demonstrates recall robustness to cue noise and ongoing noise, emergent sparse/heavy-tailed weights, tight dynamic E/I balance, and reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.
- Compares recall performance to an ideal-observer nearest-neighbor baseline and finds similar performance at tested noise levels (n=150, m=30).
- Novelty: applies smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA) from robust control to associative memory embedding in E/I networks, extending prior work on Hopfield-like and balanced networks.
- Strength: elegant joint fixed-point and robust-stability optimization framing with strong biological constraints (Dale’s law, low rates, sparsity, E/I balance).
- Strength: empirical validation covers robustness to noise, emergent sparsity, and biologically plausible network phenomena.
- Weakness: limited scalability and capacity analysis—only one small network shown (capacity ~0.2) with no systematic scaling vs n, m/n, or ε.
- Weakness: treating inhibitory steady-states v_inh^μ as free per-memory variables reduces biological plausibility and may act as slack variables rather than true fixed points.
- Weakness: SSA is tuned heuristically and does not guarantee SA<0; need histograms of SA/SSA and sensitivity analyses for ε after training.
- Weakness: no analysis of basins of attraction or spurious attractors—need retrieval radii, convergence rates, and spurious attractor counts.
- Weakness: missing comparisons/ablations to graded autoassociative networks, global inhibition models, and alternatives (e.g., saturation-based stabilization); need ablation of L2 and Dale’s law and fuller pruning curves.
- Weakness: computational cost likely high (optimizing O(n^2 + n_I m) parameters with Jacobian/eigen computations; possibly O(m n^3)); runtime/scaling discussion needed.
- Weakness: clarity and reproducibility issues—missing supplementary details, incomplete references, and no code or pseudo-code provided.
- Weakness: biological interpretation limited because learning is global/nonlocal; discuss whether local inhibitory plasticity could approximate SSA and whether inhibitory patterns are predictable from E activity.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, well-motivated control-theoretic approach enforcing biological constraints and reproducing cortical phenomena.
- Cons for acceptance: limited scale/capacity analysis, per-memory inhibitory setpoint plausibility concerns, and insufficient comparisons/quantification of pruning and stability guarantees.
- Overall evaluation: good conceptual/methodological contribution with promising empirical support, but requires stronger stability guarantees, scaling/capacity analyses, and additional ablations to justify acceptance.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on addressing SA after training, capacity/scaling and pruning analyses, and clarifying the role and plausibility of optimized inhibitory steady-states.
