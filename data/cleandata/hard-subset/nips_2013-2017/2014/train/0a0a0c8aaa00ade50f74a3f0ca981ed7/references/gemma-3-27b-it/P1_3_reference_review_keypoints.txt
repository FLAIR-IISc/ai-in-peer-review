- Novel framework for recurrent networks that store and recall analog memories in a biologically plausible E–I, sparse, low-rate setting.
- Memory storage formulated as a control problem optimizing synaptic weights and inhibitory firing rates to create stable fixed points.
- Uses robust control theory (Smoothed Spectral Abscissa minimization) to ensure stability and counteract positive feedback.
- Demonstrates successful storage of multiple graded memories, robust recall under noise, and dynamic E/I balance.
- Clearly situates work relative to Hopfield models, LIF/spiking approaches, and recent SSA-based neural network research.
- Extends SSA/minimization techniques to biologically realistic E–I network architectures.
- Strength: adheres to biological constraints (Dale’s Law, firing rates, E/I balance), increasing biological plausibility.
- Strength: demonstrated robustness to noise in cues and ongoing dynamics.
- Strength: novel control-theoretic framing and clear presentation with helpful figures.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation of capacity, recall, sparsity, and E/I dynamics; well-referenced.
- Weakness: rate-based model limits direct biological realism and applicability to spiking networks.
- Weakness: parameter choices (ηs, ηF, ε) appear heuristic and underexplored.
- Weakness: reported capacity is low (≈0.2, e.g., 30 memories in 150 neurons).
- Weakness: experiments use a fixed network size (n=150) with no scaling analysis.
- Weakness: choice of log-normal memory distribution lacks rigorous justification or comparison.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, robust results, methodological advance, biologically relevant.
- Arguments against acceptance: rate-based limitation, limited capacity, ad-hoc parameter tuning, unclear scalability.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution worth publishing despite limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to (1) discuss translation to spiking networks, (2) justify or analyze parameter choices, and (3) address scalability and capacity.
