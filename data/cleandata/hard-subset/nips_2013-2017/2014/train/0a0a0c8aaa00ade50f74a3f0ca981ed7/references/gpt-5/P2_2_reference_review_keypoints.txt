- Presents a control-theoretic framework to embed multiple graded attractors in a Dale-constrained recurrent rate network using a rectified quadratic gain and SSA-based stability optimization.
- Optimization minimizes composite objective: small state velocity at targets, negative Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for local stability, and L2 weight regularization, with inhibitory steady-states treated as auxiliary variables.
- Method is technically coherent; using SSA as a differentiable upper bound for spectral abscissa is appropriate for gradient-based stability enforcement.
- Empirical results show robust recall from noisy cues, resilience to ongoing noise, E/I balance across attractors, sparse-like weight distributions, and variability quenching consistent with cortical data.
- Performance approaches an “ideal observer” that picks the nearest stored pattern in cue space.
- Novelty: combines graded attractor embedding, Dale’s law, non-saturating gains, and SSA-based robust stability in a single optimization framework.
- Conceptual contribution: shows E/I balance can stabilize graded attractors without single-neuron saturation.
- Major caveat: fixed points are only approximated (small dv/dt) and stability is certified at these approximate points, leaving no formal guarantee that true fixed points exist or are stable.
- Recommend numerically finding exact post-training fixed points (e.g., Newton solve) and verifying α(J) < 0 and closeness to targets.
- Capacity analysis is limited (example n=150, m=30, capacity ~0.2) with no systematic scaling vs. n, pattern statistics, or regularization.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (ε smoothing, ηs, ηF) is not explored and could materially affect stability, recall, and sparsity.
- Treating inhibitory steady-state activities v_inh^μ as free variables adds nI·m degrees of freedom and may ease storage; need ablations to quantify connectivity vs per-memory inhibitory contributions.
- Biological plausibility is limited: training is global and offline, with no constructive local learning rules beyond cursory discussion.
- Evaluation thresholds and clipping choices feel ad hoc; recommend reporting basins of attraction (fractional σ leading to correct recall) and confusion matrices per attractor.
- Clarity issues: need more detail on gradient computation for SSA and dv/dt, step sizes/termination, initialization distributions, noise amplitudes, and consistent references.
- Suggested experiments: find exact fixed points and report their spectra, capacity vs n and m/n, ablations for ε, ηs, ηF, constrain or validate v_inh^μ, compare to alternative baselines (pseudo-inverse, Lyapunov approaches), and test transfer to spiking networks.
- Strengths: principled differentiable stability objective respecting Dale’s law, robust analog recall without saturation, emergent pruning-friendly sparsity, and links to experimental phenomena.
- Weaknesses: no scaling/capacity study, no explicit verification of exact fixed points, unclear necessity of auxiliary inhibitory variables, hyperparameter/initialization sensitivity untested, and global offline learning rule.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — compelling and novel framework with thoughtful experiments, but needs additional verification and scalability analyses to strengthen claims.
