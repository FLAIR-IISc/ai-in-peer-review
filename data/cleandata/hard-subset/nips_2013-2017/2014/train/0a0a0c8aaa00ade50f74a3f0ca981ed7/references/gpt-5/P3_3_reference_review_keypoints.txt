- Proposes an SSA-based control-theoretic optimization to build Dale’s-law E–I rate networks that store multiple graded activity patterns as locally stable fixed points.  
- Uses rectified-quadratic f–I functions and jointly optimizes synaptic weights and per-memory steady-state inhibitory activities v_inh^μ to minimize fixed-point drift and a smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA).  
- Demonstrates autoassociative recall with probabilistic success vs cue noise, robustness to dynamic noise, E/I balance across memories, emergent heavy-tailed weights, and variability quenching.  
- SSA as a differentiable proxy for local stability is appropriate and technically sound; Dale reparameterization is standard and simulations are coherent.  
- Major concern: treating v_inh^μ as free per-memory parameters inflates learned degrees of freedom, potentially overfitting stability and confounding true capacity claims.  
- Unclear whether v_inh^μ are uniquely determined by dv/dt=0 for a given W; authors must clarify or constrain them to avoid degeneracy.  
- Fixed-point term is not driven to zero; no constructive proof or tolerance analysis that a true attractor exists nearby.  
- Need post-hoc verification of actual fixed points (e.g., Newton solver) and reporting of distances from targets.  
- Capacity and scaling are uncharacterized: only m/n≈0.2 at n=150 shown, with no systematic scaling vs n, m, nI or pattern statistics.  
- Computational complexity and scalability are unclear given SSA requires eigen/schur computations; per-μ costs likely scale superlinearly with n.  
- Sparsity claim is weak: L2 regularization yields many near-zero weights but not true sparsity; 0.01 threshold is arbitrary.  
- Suggest comparing L1/elastic-net, reporting exact zeros, and structured pruning analyses to substantiate sparsity.  
- Experiments lack sensitivity analyses to hyperparameters (ε, η_s/η_F, gain exponent) and random seeds; results are mostly single-setting.  
- Comparisons are indirect; need baselines like graded Hopfield with Dale projection, global-inhibition models, and spiking/LIF implementations.  
- Clarity/reproducibility issues: specify whether v_inh^μ are determined by dynamics, SSA definition used, gradient computation, per-iteration complexity, stopping criteria, noise units, integration details, and runtime.  
- Report failures/spurious attractors and inter-run variability across seeds to assess robustness.  
- Novelty: applying SSA to shape local stability across multiple graded attractors under Dale’s law is a valuable and seemingly new contribution.  
- Significance contingent on scalability and resolving inhibitory-parameter ambiguity; with additional experiments this could be impactful.  
- Pros: principled SSA optimization, respects Dale’s law, achieves graded memories, shows E/I balance and variability quenching, and robustness to pruning.  
- Cons: per-memory inhibitory optimization inflates DOFs, no scaling or runtime analysis, fixed-point errors unverified, sparse claims rely on L2/arbitrary thresholds, limited ablations and baselines.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on addressing inhibitory-parameter concerns and scaling/verification issues; otherwise borderline.
