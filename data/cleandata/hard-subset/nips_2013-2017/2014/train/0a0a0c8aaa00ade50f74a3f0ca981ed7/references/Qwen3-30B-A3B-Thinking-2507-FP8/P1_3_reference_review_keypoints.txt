- Summary: Proposes a control-theoretic framework using the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa to embed arbitrary analog memory patterns as stable fixed points in Dale-compliant E/I rate networks.  
- Summary: Produces networks with sparse connectivity, dynamic E/I balance, low firing rates, and robust graded memory recall matching an ideal observer.

- Strength: Technically rigorous—clear fixed-point and stability conditions with SSA as a differentiable stability objective and reproducible optimization details.  
- Strength: Biologically plausible—simultaneously enforces Dale's law, graded activity, sparse connectivity, and balanced E/I consistent with cortical data.  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation—demonstrates noise robustness, graded pattern recall, sparse emergent connectivity, and comparison to an ideal observer.  
- Strength: Clearly differentiated from prior work—addresses limitations of Hopfield/binary models and avoids spatial/spiking assumptions of other approaches.

- Weakness: Spiking translation is superficial—the paper lacks evidence that the rate-model gain matches spiking behavior in the relevant regime.  
- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity unexplored—key hyperparameters (e.g., ε, learning rates) are chosen heuristically without robustness analysis.  
- Weakness: Limited capacity analysis—reports 0.2 capacity but does not study scaling with network size or practical capacity limits.  
- Weakness: Learning-rule gap—offers an optimal algorithmic solution but provides only brief, untested discussion of how biologically plausible local learning could achieve this.

- Overall Quality: Technically sound with comprehensive experiments; minor clarifications (SSA vs SA, parameter details) would help.  
- Overall Clarity: Well-written and well-illustrated.  
- Overall Originality: Novel combination of SSA-based stability optimization with multiple biological constraints.  
- Overall Significance: Advances biologically plausible memory modeling and implications for cortical circuit organization.

- Recommendation: Strong accept—contribution is significant and weaknesses do not undermine core results.

- Pro: Solves a long-standing neural memory modeling problem while respecting multiple biological constraints and offering rigorous validation.  
- Con: Insufficient connection to spiking implementations, limited parameter/capacity exploration, and brief treatment of biologically plausible learning mechanisms.
