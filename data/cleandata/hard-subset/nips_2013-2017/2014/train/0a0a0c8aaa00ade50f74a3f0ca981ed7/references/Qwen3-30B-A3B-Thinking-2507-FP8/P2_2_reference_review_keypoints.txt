- Summary: Proposes a control-theoretic framework that uses the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa to construct balanced E-I rate networks that store multiple analog memory patterns as stable fixed points while respecting Dale’s law, graded activity, low-rate firing, and E-I balance.

- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous mathematical formulation, clever use of SSA for gradient optimization, and thorough experimental validation across convergence, recall, sparsity, balance, and robustness.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, methods are reproducible, and figures effectively illustrate key results.

- Originality: Novel application of SSA/control theory to neural memory models, combining features (Dale’s law, graded analog patterns, non-saturating low-rate activity, E-I balance) not previously unified.

- Significance: High impact for neuroscience and ML—provides a plausible biological mechanism for analog memory storage and suggests implications for robust, compressible network design.

- Weakness 1: Rate-based model vs spiking networks needs deeper analysis or justification linking the rate gain to spiking neurons.

- Weakness 2: Storage capacity (e.g., 30 patterns in 150 neurons) seems modest and should be compared to theoretical capacity limits for analog patterns.

- Weakness 3: Limited exploration of trade-offs between number of memories and network size; behavior beyond 30 memories is not shown.

- Weakness 4: Use of a log-normal distribution to generate memories is plausible but not rigorously justified or motivated biologically.

- Recommendation: Strong accept — meets high standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.

- Pros (summary): Solves multiple prior limitations simultaneously, novel SSA-based stability optimization, comprehensive validation, ablation studies show practical utility, and strong biological connections.

- Cons (summary): Rate-vs-spiking gap, capacity/contextualization issues, insufficient scaling/trade-off analysis, and limited justification for memory distribution choice.
