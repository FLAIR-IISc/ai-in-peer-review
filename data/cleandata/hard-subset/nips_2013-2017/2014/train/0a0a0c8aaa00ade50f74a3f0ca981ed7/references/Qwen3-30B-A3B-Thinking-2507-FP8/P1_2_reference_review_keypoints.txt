- Paper introduces a control-theoretic framework to store and recall analog (graded) activity patterns in biologically constrained E-I networks.
- Formulates memory storage as finding stable fixed points that satisfy dv/dt = 0 and negative spectral abscissa for arbitrary analog patterns.
- Contribution: avoids symmetric connectivity and respects Dale’s law via weight reparameterization.
- Contribution: uses Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) as a differentiable relaxation for stability optimization.
- Contribution: demonstrates robust recall of graded activity patterns in rate-based E-I networks.
- Contribution: analyzes and reports sparse connectivity properties emerging from the optimization.
- Contribution: provides evidence of dynamic E-I balance in the optimized networks.
- Strength: technically rigorous framework with well-motivated optimization and correct SSA implementation.
- Strength: addresses multiple biological constraints (Dale’s law, sparsity, graded activity, low firing rates, E-I balance).
- Strength: novel application of control theory (SSA) to neural network stability for analog memories.
- Strength: comprehensive validation across metrics (capacity, noise robustness, sparsity, E-I balance, variability).
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and effective figures supporting reproducibility.
- Weakness: computationally intensive optimization (BFGS) with unclear scalability to much larger networks.
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparisons to alternative analog memory methods, e.g., synaptic plasticity approaches.
- Weakness: limited analysis of interference between multiple stored patterns and its effect on capacity.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why stability optimization produces synaptic sparsity (beyond L2 regularization).
- Weakness: limited discussion of how the rate-based model maps to spiking network implementations.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, biologically grounded contribution to computational neuroscience.
- Argument for acceptance: fills a gap for analog memory networks with biological realism.
- Argument for acceptance: presents a principled control-theoretic framework using SSA optimization.
- Argument for acceptance: demonstrates robust performance with multiple validation benchmarks.
- Argument for acceptance: respects key biological constraints (Dale’s law, E-I balance, graded activity).
- Argument for acceptance: well-written, technically sound, and extensible to future work on learning rules/spiking networks.
- Argument against acceptance: pattern interference analysis is limited.
- Argument against acceptance: scalability and computational complexity for larger networks not fully addressed.
- Argument against acceptance: mechanism of emergent sparsity is not sufficiently explained.
- Argument against acceptance: limited benchmarking against other analog memory approaches.
- Additional comment: authors should discuss extensions to spiking dynamics and links to known plasticity rules.
- Additional comment: emphasize and further explore the finding that small synaptic weights are not necessary for recall.
- Additional comment: the focus on graded (not binary) activity patterns is an important advance toward biological realism.
