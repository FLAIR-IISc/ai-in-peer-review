- Unifies constraints (Dale’s law, graded patterns, biologically realistic sparse connectivity) in a control-theoretic framework for associative memory.
- Frames memory storage as requiring target patterns to be fixed points and locally stable, optimized via a three-term objective (fixed-point term, SSA-based stability term, weight regularization).
- Enforces Dale’s law with a log-exp weight reparameterization and treats inhibitory neurons’ steady-states as optimization variables while memories reside in excitatory neurons.
- Learned networks store analog (graded) memories, exhibit E/I balance, robust autoassociative recall with corrupted cues and ongoing noise, and maintain realistic firing rates without explicit saturation.
- Reproduces stimulus-induced reduction in trial-to-trial variability as convergence toward attractors from diverse initial conditions.
- Technical approach is sound: using Smoothed Spectral Abscissa for robust stability optimization is appropriate and experiments convincingly validate recall, robustness, sparsity, and E/I balance.
- Important methodological concern: quantify residual drift when fixed-point term does not reach zero (distance to true fixed points and time-to-drift under noise-free dynamics).
- Manuscript is generally clear and well organized, but lines 254–256 need clarification.
- Motivate the choice of log-normal target firing-rate distributions and report sensitivity to this choice.
- Provide sensitivity analysis for γ = 0.04 in Eq. (3) and for other key hyperparameters (e.g., SSA smoothing).
- Novelty: unified control-theoretic synthesis embedding analog attractors with Dale’s law and balanced dynamics; optimizing inhibitory steady-states is an original contribution.
- Significance: offers a principled, biologically plausible route to attractor networks with robust recall, variability quenching explanation, and potential for spiking implementations.
- Pros: clear unified solution to multiple biological constraints, technically solid SSA use, strong empirical validation, and conceptual clarity on inhibitory role.
- Cons/requests: quantify fixed-point accuracy, analyze parameter sensitivity, provide preliminary capacity/scaling estimates, discuss computational impact of enforcing Dale’s law, clarify lines 254–256, and test dependence on log-normal patterns.
- Minor fixes: correct “attractor closets” → “attractor closest” and briefly relate emergent sparsity to the L2 weight regularization despite no explicit L1 penalty.
- Overall assessment: a strong, impactful paper that meaningfully advances biologically plausible associative memory models, though lacking a biologically plausible learning rule and requiring the above clarifications.
