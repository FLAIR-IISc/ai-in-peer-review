- Authors design a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns ("memories").
- Paper targets shortcomings of prior attractor models, specifically violating Dale’s law and producing saturated activity states.
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost that minimizes activity changes in desired attractors, penalizes the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and promotes attractor stability.
- Trained networks produce a weight distribution that closely matches experimental observations.
- Attractor states exhibit balanced excitation and inhibition at the network level.
- Manuscript is clearly written and well organized, making the model, training procedure, and results easy to follow.
- Biological constraints on synapses and neurons yield graded, non-saturated attractors and biologically plausible inhibitory activity.
