This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network trained to store multiple stable activity patterns, or memories. The goal is to overcome key shortcomings of previous attractor models, namely the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of neuronal activities in memory states. The authors employ gradient descent on a cost function designed to minimize activity changes in the desired attractors, minimize the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and maximize the stability of the attractors. The primary results demonstrate that the trained network exhibits a weight distribution consistent with experimental findings and achieves a balance of excitation and inhibition in its attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written and clear. I found the model, the training procedure, and the presentation of the results easy to understand. The combination of incorporating biological constraints into the model and analyzing the resulting emergent features makes this paper an interesting contribution to the field of attractor neural networks. By imposing plausible constraints on synapses and neurons, the authors successfully train a network to develop graded, non-saturated attractors, leading to a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition that enhances the model's biological relevance.
