- Presents a rate-based neuronal network that stores multiple stable activity patterns (memories) while respecting biological constraints.
- Addresses limitations of prior attractor models, notably violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation in memory states.
- Uses gradient-descent training on a cost function that reduces activity changes in attractors, penalizes weight norm, and enhances attractor stability.
- Trained networks produce weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.
- Attractor states exhibit a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level.
- Model maintains graded, non-saturated attractors in neural activity and synaptic weights.
- Incorporates biologically plausible constraints on synapses and neurons in the training procedure.
- Manuscript is clearly written and methods/results are easily comprehensible.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing a novel, biologically plausible, and robust contribution to attractor neural networks.
