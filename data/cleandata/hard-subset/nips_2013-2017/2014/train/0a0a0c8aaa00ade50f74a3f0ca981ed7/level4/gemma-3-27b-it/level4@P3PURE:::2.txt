In this manuscript, the authors present a rate-based neuronal network designed to store a number of stable activity patterns, or memories. The work addresses several limitations of prior attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation within memory states. The network is trained using gradient descent on a cost function that simultaneously minimizes activity changes within desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing attractor stability. The primary results demonstrate a weight distribution consistent with experimental observations and a balanced excitation-inhibition dynamic in the attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written and clear. I found the model and training procedure easy to follow, as was the presentation of the results. The combination of biologically-motivated constraints incorporated into the model and the resulting network characteristics make this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. Specifically, the authors successfully train a rate-based network to support graded, non-saturated attractors through careful consideration of synaptic weights and inhibitory activity. Imposing constraints for biological plausibility leads to a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level.
