- Proposes a unified framework that enforces multiple biological constraints (Dale’s law, non-binary memories, sparse activity) simultaneously.  
- Uses a novel control-theoretic, spectral-bounding construction and optimization to build the network.  
- Optimization balances three objectives: making memory patterns fixed points, ensuring stability, and regularizing synaptic weights.  
- Enforces Dale’s law in the weight parameterization, producing natural excitation–inhibition balance.  
- Encodes memories in excitatory neurons while treating inhibitory neurons as optimizable resources for stability.  
- Neuronal firing-rate saturation emerges naturally from the optimization without explicit constraints.  
- Validated by simulations showing robustness to noise and explaining reduced trial-to-trial variability via convergence to attractors.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and makes a significant computational-neuroscience contribution.  
- Does not provide a biologically plausible learning rule for weights, though points to recent work that could lead to one.
