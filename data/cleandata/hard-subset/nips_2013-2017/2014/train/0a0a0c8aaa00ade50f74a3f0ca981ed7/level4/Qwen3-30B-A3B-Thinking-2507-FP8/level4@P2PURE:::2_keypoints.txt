- Proposes a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Specifically addresses prior attractor-model limitations: violations of Dale’s law and saturation of memory-state activity.
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost combining: minimal activity change within target attractors, Frobenius-norm weight penalty, and maximization of attractor stability.
- Learned weight distribution matches experimental observations.
- Attractor states show balanced excitation–inhibition rather than saturated activity.
- Manuscript is well-written with clear presentation of model, training, and results.
- Integration of biological constraints produces non-saturated, biologically plausible attractors and is a compelling contribution to attractor network understanding.
