- Paper is clearly written, novel, and will interest the community.  
- Authors provide a strong review of prior work on training networks for auto-associative tasks, highlighting main contributions and shortcomings.  
- Network architecture and training procedure are clearly presented, with effective, illustrative figures.  
- Experiments show network performance degrades with added noise, underscoring the robustness of the learned solution.  
- Recommend clarifying the relationship between the chosen training procedure and the paper’s stated objectives.  
- Note that other methods have enforced Dale’s Law or achieved graded memories; authors should justify why their training method is particularly suitable or whether alternatives would yield similar results.  
- Suggest adding a comparison of network capacity (number of memories vs. network size) against previous models.  
- Contribution: introduces a new auto-associative memory model using a recent method for stable fixed points that respects biological constraints (non-saturating units, Dale-like structure).
