- Rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Addresses limitations of prior attractor models, specifically violation of Dale's law and activity saturation.
- Uses gradient descent on a cost that minimizes activity changes in desired attractors, minimizes Frobenius norm of weights, and maximizes attractor stability.
- Learned weight distribution matches experimental findings.
- Network shows balanced excitation and inhibition in attractor states.
- Produces graded, non-saturated attractors with biologically plausible synapses and inhibitory activities.
- Manuscript is well-written and clearly presents the model and training procedure.
- Integrating biological constraints is a valuable contribution to understanding attractor neural networks.
