- Introduces a biologically plausible associative memory framework addressing Dale’s law, binary representations, and non-sparse encodings.  
- Presents a unified control-theoretic approach using spectral bounding techniques.  
- Core innovation: a cost function balancing fixed-point stability for target memories, enhanced stability, and small synaptic weights.  
- Enforces Dale’s law via weight parameterization, yielding natural excitation–inhibition balance without explicit constraints.  
- Single-neuron saturation is not enforced but emerges from optimization.  
- Treats inhibitory neurons as optimization variables and uses excitatory neurons for memory storage, aligning with biology.  
- Numerical results show robust noise tolerance and explain reduced trial-to-trial variability as attractor convergence.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and bridges classical neural computation with modern control theory.  
- Lacks a biologically plausible learning rule at this stage, though promising directions are noted.

- Minor concern: log-normal distribution assumption for memory states requires biological or computational justification.  
- Minor concern: quantify deviations from exact fixed points produced by the cost function and their impact on retrieval.  
- Minor concern: clarify statements in Lines 254–256 to link mathematical findings to biological/computational implications.
