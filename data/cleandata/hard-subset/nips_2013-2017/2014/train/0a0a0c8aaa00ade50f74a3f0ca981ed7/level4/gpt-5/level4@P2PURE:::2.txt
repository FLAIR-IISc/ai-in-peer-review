In this manuscript, the authors present a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories). The aim is to address key limitations of prior attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and saturation of activity in memory states. Training is performed via gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes activity changes within the target attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing attractor stability. The principal outcomes are a synaptic weight distribution that aligns with experimental observations and balanced excitation and inhibition within attractor states.

The manuscript is clearly written. The model, training procedure, and presentation of results are straightforward to follow. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the emergent properties after training make this a valuable contribution to the study of attractor neural networks. The authors train a rate-based network that supports graded, non-saturated attractor states, reflected in the synaptic weights and inhibitory activity. Biologically motivated constraints on synapses and neurons yield a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
