- Presents a rate-based neural network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Targets prior attractor-model shortcomings: violations of Dale’s law and saturation of neuronal activities.  
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost combining: minimizing activity changes at desired attractors, penalizing the Frobenius norm of weights, and maximizing attractor stability.  
- Resulting synaptic weight distribution closely matches experimental observations.  
- Trained networks exhibit excitation–inhibition balance in attractor states.  
- Paper is well written, with a clear presentation of model, training procedure, and results.  
- Combining biologically motivated constraints yields interesting emergent features and contributes valuably to attractor network studies.  
- The network supports graded, non-saturated attractors via learned synaptic weights and inhibitory activity.
