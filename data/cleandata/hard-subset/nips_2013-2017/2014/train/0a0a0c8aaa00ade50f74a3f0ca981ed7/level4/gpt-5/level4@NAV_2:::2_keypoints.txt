- Presents a rate-based neural network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Targets limitations of prior attractor models, notably violations of Dale’s law and firing-rate saturation during recall.
- Uses gradient-based optimization with a stationarity-penalty cost, L2 (Frobenius) weight regularization, and explicit enhancement of local attractor stability.
- Learns synaptic weight distributions that resemble experimental observations.
- Achieves balanced excitation and inhibition within attractor states.
- Demonstrates storage of graded, non-saturating attractors via synaptic weights and inhibitory steady-state activity.
- Biologically motivated neuron and synapse constraints produce network-level E/I balance.
- Paper is clearly written, with a straightforward model/training procedure and well-presented results.
- Integration of biological constraints with learned dynamics is a valuable contribution to attractor network studies.
