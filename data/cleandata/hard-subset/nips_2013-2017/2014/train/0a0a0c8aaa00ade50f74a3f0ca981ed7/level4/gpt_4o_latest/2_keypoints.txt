- Proposes a rate-based neuronal network to store multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Targets key limitations of prior attractor models, notably violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation.
- Trains the network with gradient descent on a cost that minimizes changes in target attractors, penalizes Frobenius norm, and promotes attractor stability.
- Produces synaptic weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.
- Demonstrates emergent balance of excitation and inhibition in attractor states.
- Manuscript is well-written with a clear presentation of model, training procedure, and results.
- Shows that biologically inspired synaptic and neuronal constraints lead to graded, non-saturated attractors and network-level E/I balance, making a valuable contribution.
