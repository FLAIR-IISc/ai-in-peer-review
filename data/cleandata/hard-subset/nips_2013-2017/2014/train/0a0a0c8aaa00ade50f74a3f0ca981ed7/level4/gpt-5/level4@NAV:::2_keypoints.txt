- Proposes a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Aims to address limitations of prior attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation.
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost that minimizes activity changes in target attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix while maximizing attractor stability.
- Resulting synaptic weight distribution matches experimental observations.
- Trained attractor states exhibit a balance between excitation and inhibition.
- Biological constraints on synapses and neurons produce network-level E/I balance.
- Network stores graded, non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities.
- Manuscript is well written; model, training procedure, and presentation are clear and easy to follow.
- Overall, the work is a valuable contribution to understanding attractor neural networks.
