- Presents a rate-based auto-associative memory model that enforces Dale’s law and supports graded, non-saturating neural responses.  
- Memories are encoded by gradient descent on synaptic weights with constraints enforcing stability of the linearization around fixed points.  
- Trained attractors are stable and exhibit slow dynamics near target states (close to, but not exactly, fixed points).  
- Learned synaptic weight distribution is centered near zero with heavy tails.  
- Networks show approximate excitatory/inhibitory balance at the level of individual units.  
- E/I balance and weight statistics appear crucial for robust recall under perturbations and stochastic input noise.  
- Clear, original, well-written submission with effective figures and a helpful overview of prior auto-associative models.  
- Analysis of network performance under noise is particularly useful and informative.  
- Reviewer asks for clearer rationale/mechanistic explanation for why the chosen training method produces graded attractors and whether alternatives could achieve similar results.  
- Reviewer recommends comparing memory capacity (e.g., number of storable memories vs. network size) to prior models or providing scaling arguments.
