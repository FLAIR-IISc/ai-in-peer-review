- Core contribution: introduces a control-theoretic framework that directly optimizes recurrent network parameters to embed analog activity patterns as stable attractors.

- Optimization objective: cost balances (1) minimizing dv/dt at target patterns, (2) minimizing Smoothed Spectral Abscissa for stability, and (3) weight regularization.

- Biological constraints: enforces Dale's law with distinct E/I populations; excitatory activities define memories while inhibitory activities are optimized per pattern.

- Key results: networks store and recall graded memories, are noise-robust, operate in a dynamically balanced low-firing E/I regime, and reproduce stimulus-induced variability reduction.

- Strength — originality: novel application of SSA-based control-theoretic optimization to construct attractor networks.

- Strength — biological plausibility: integrates multiple constraints (Dale’s law, E/I balance, low graded rates, sparse connectivity) in one model.

- Strength — significance: demonstrates stable embedding of complex analog patterns and pattern-specific E/I balance as a stability mechanism.

- Strength — presentation: well-written, technically sound, and supported by convincing simulations.

- Weakness — learning mechanism: framework is normative using global optimization; lacks biologically plausible local learning rules or a detailed bridge to synaptic plasticity.

- Weakness — storage capacity: no systematic analysis of how capacity scales with network size, load, or memory statistics.

- Weakness — fixed-point approximation: optimized states are “slow points” not exact fixed points; the timescale and impact of residual drift are not quantified.

- Weakness — parameter choices: some choices (e.g., log-normal pattern distribution, gain γ) need clearer justification and sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness — role of Dale’s law: discussion could expand on whether E/I separation confers computational advantages beyond being a biological constraint.

- Overall recommendation: technically impressive, high-impact work with minor clarifications needed; recommends acceptance.
