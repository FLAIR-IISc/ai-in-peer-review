In this manuscript, the authors design a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain a number of stable activity patterns, referred to as memories. The primary goal is to address several shortcomings of previous attractor models, specifically the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. To achieve this, the authors employ a gradient descent approach on a cost function that minimizes activity changes in the desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing the stability of attractors.

The main results of this work include a weight distribution that closely resembles experimental findings and a balance of excitation and inhibition in the attractor states. The writing in the manuscript is clear and well-organized, making the model and training procedure easy to comprehend, as well as the presentation of the results. The combination of biological constraints incorporated into the model and the resulting features that emerge after training render this paper an interesting contribution to our understanding of attractor neural networks.

Notably, the authors successfully train a rate-based neuronal network to hold graded and non-saturated attractors in the synaptic weights and inhibitory activities. The constraints imposed on synapses and neurons to ensure biological plausibility lead to a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level.
