- Proposes a novel rate-based neuronal network that stores and maintains multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Addresses prior attractor-model limitations, notably violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation in memory states.
- Trains the network via gradient descent minimizing attractor activity changes and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix while maximizing attractor stability.
- Manuscript is well written and presents the model and training procedure clearly.
- Integrates biological constraints into the model, producing biologically plausible features after training.
- Trained network supports graded, non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities.
- Biologically motivated constraints produce a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
- Resulting weight distribution closely resembles experimental observations, supporting biological relevance.
- Overall, a compelling contribution with clear presentation that enhances understanding of attractor neural networks.
