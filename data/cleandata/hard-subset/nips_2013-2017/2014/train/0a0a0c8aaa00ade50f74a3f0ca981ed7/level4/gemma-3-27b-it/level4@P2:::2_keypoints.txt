- Proposes a novel gradient-descent method to design rate-based networks that store multiple stable, graded (non-saturating) attractor states.  
- Optimizes a cost function that minimizes attractor deviation, regularizes weights (Frobenius norm), and maximizes attractor stability.  
- Incorporates biologically plausible constraints (Dale’s law, realistic firing rates) during training.  
- Uses a control-theoretic tool (Smoothed Spectral Abscissa) to robustly optimize network stability.  
- Learned weight distributions resemble experimentally observed patterns.  
- Trained networks exhibit balanced excitation–inhibition dynamics within attractor states.  
- Demonstrates robustness to noisy recall cues and ongoing noise, increasing biological plausibility.  
- Shows reduction in trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset, consistent with experimental findings.  
- Manuscript is well-written and clearly explains model, methods, and results.  
- Limitation: rate-based model abstracts away spiking dynamics; translation to spiking networks is not explored.  
- Limitation: heuristic parameter choices (ηs, ηF, ε) lack systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Limitation: potential high computational cost for optimizing large networks.  
- Limitation: focus on supervised weight learning; biologically plausible unsupervised learning rules are not investigated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, technically sound approach addressing key attractor-network limitations with strong results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: simplifications (rate model), heuristic tuning, and limited task/benchmark comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss spiking translation and parameter-sensitivity analysis.
