- Paper introduces a rate-based neuronal network to store stable activity patterns (memories) while addressing Dale’s law violations and activity saturation in attractor models.
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost that minimizes activity changes within desired attractors, penalizes the Frobenius norm of weights, and maximizes attractor stability.
- Model yields graded, non-saturated attractors through synaptic and neuronal constraints promoting biological plausibility.
- Resulting weight distribution matches experimental observations.
- Attractor states exhibit a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
- Manuscript is well-written, clear, and the model and training procedure are easy to follow.
- Combination of biologically-motivated constraints and outcomes represents a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks.
