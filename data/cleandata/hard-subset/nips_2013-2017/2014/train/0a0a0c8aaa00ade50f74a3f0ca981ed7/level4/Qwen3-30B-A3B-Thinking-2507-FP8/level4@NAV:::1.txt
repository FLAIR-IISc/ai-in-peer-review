

This is a clearly-written, novel submission that will be of great interest to the community. The authors effectively review the history of training neural networks for auto-associative tasks, highlighting key contributions and limitations of prior work. The network architecture, training procedure, and supporting figures are presented clearly and illustrate the main results effectively. The authors demonstrate the network's performance limitations under noise, underscoring the robustness achieved by their training procedure.

One recommendation is to clarify the rationale for the chosen training procedure relative to the stated objectives. While the authors note that previous methods have been used to enforce Dale's Law or achieve graded memories (with the latter condition notably harder to enforce), the manuscript does not explicitly justify why this specific method was selected as particularly well-suited for achieving non-saturating units. It would be valuable to address whether alternative training procedures could achieve the same outcome and why this method is effective.

Additionally, given the long-standing literature on auto-associative networks, a brief comparison of network capacity (number of memories versus network size) against previous models would be valuable to the community, though this may extend beyond the current scope.
