In this manuscript, the authors present a rate-based neural network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories). The aim is to address key shortcomings of prior attractor models—namely, violations of Dale’s law and saturation of neuronal activities in memory states. Training is performed via gradient descent on a cost function that (i) minimizes activity changes at the desired attractors, (ii) penalizes the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and (iii) maximizes attractor stability. The main outcomes are a synaptic weight distribution closely matching experimental observations and a balance of excitation and inhibition in the attractor states.

The paper is well written: the model, training procedure, and results are clearly presented and easy to follow. The combination of biologically motivated constraints in the model and the emergent features after training make this an interesting and valuable contribution to the study of attractor neural networks. The trained rate-based network supports graded, non-saturated attractors through its synaptic weights and inhibitory activities, and the biologically plausible constraints imposed on synapses and neurons produce a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
