- Presents a rate-based neural network trained to store multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Targets key limitations of prior attractor networks: violation of Dale’s law and saturation of neural activity in memory states.
- Training via gradient descent on a cost that enforces stability (minimize activity change within attractors), regularizes weights (Frobenius norm), and explicitly maximizes attractor stability.
- Trained network develops a synaptic weight distribution consistent with experimental observations.
- Network achieves a balance between excitation and inhibition within attractor states.
- Produces emergent graded, non-saturated attractor states.
- Main contribution is the integration of biological constraints that lead to emergent network features.
- Manuscript is well-written with clear presentation of model, training procedure, and results.
- Overall, an interesting contribution to understanding attractor neural networks.
