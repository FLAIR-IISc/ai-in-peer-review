- Introduces a biologically plausible attractor network that stores and retrieves analog memory patterns while respecting key physiological constraints.  
- Addresses prior limitations: Dale’s law violations, binary memory representations, and firing rate saturation in earlier models.  
- Proposes a control-theoretic optimization framework that stabilizes arbitrary analog patterns as fixed points while preserving balanced E–I dynamics.  
- Optimization jointly minimizes the time derivative of neural activity (to enforce fixed points) and the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for stability.  
- Trained networks operate in the balanced regime and produce graded activity patterns rather than binary outputs.  
- Demonstrates robustness of recall to noisy cues and ongoing dynamics, matching near-ideal observer performance.  
- Learned synaptic weights are realistic: sparse connectivity, biologically plausible weight distributions, and E–I balance consistent with experiments.  
- Incidentally explains the empirically observed reduction in trial-to-trial variability following stimulus onset.  
- Technical approach is rigorous, well-motivated, and validated across multiple metrics (recall, noise robustness, weight distributions).  
- Writing and presentation are clear, figures are effective, and supplementary materials support reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: first to simultaneously satisfy Dale’s law, graded activity, low firing rates, and balanced E–I while achieving stable memory retrieval.  
- Significant impact: advances theoretical understanding of memory in cortical circuits and offers potential applications in neuroscience and neural engineering.  
- Strength: novel control-theoretic framework that meets multiple physiological constraints simultaneously.  
- Strength: rigorous SSA-based optimization and realistic emergent network properties.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of how rate-based results translate to spiking networks.  
- Weakness: could compare the optimization to alternative stability metrics in greater detail.  
- Weakness: does not fully explore generalization to more complex or spatially structured memory patterns.  
- Weakness: empirical connection could be strengthened with more specific in vivo comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong, technically sound contribution with clear biological relevance.
