This manuscript presents a rate-based neural network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories). The objective is to address limitations of prior attractor models, particularly violations of Dale’s law and saturation of firing rates in recalled states. The authors employ gradient-based optimization of a cost that penalizes deviations from stationarity at target states, includes an L2 (Frobenius) regularizer on the weight matrix, and explicitly enhances the local stability of the desired attractors. Key outcomes include a synaptic weight distribution resembling experimental observations and balanced excitation and inhibition within attractor states.

The paper is clearly written; both the model and training procedure are straightforward to follow, and the results are well presented. Integrating biologically grounded constraints with the resulting learned dynamics makes this a valuable contribution to the study of attractor networks. The authors demonstrate storage of graded, non-saturating attractors via synaptic weights and inhibitory steady-state activities. The biologically motivated constraints on neurons and synapses yield network-level E/I balance.
