- Proposes a unified framework for biologically plausible associative memories that jointly enforces Dale’s law, non-binary rate-based representations, and sparse memory codes.  
- Leverages control-theoretic spectral bounding to formulate an optimization balancing (1) fixed-point embedding of memories, (2) stability of fixed points, and (3) synaptic weight regularization to avoid neuronal saturation.  
- Enforces Dale’s principle directly in weight parameterization, yielding excitation–inhibition balance; neuronal saturation emerges implicitly from optimization.  
- Models memories as primarily stored by excitatory neurons while treating inhibitory neurons as optimizable internal variables for stability and dynamics shaping.  
- Validates the model with simulations showing effective associative memory retrieval, noise robustness, and reproduction/explanation of reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset via attractor convergence.  
- Paper is clearly written and makes a valuable contribution; framing inhibitory neurons as optimizable resources is particularly insightful.  
- Does not provide a biologically plausible learning rule, but references recent work that could point toward such mechanisms.
