- Proposes a rate-based neural network trained to sustain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Targets two common attractor-model issues: violations of Dale’s law and saturation of neuronal activity in memory states.  
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost penalizing activity deviations at target attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while explicitly maximizing attractor stability.  
- Resulting synaptic weight distribution matches experimental observations.  
- Trained networks maintain graded, non-saturated attractors through appropriate synaptic weights and inhibitory activity.  
- Imposed synaptic and neuronal constraints produce a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition in attractor states.  
- Manuscript is well written; model, training procedure, and results are clearly presented and easy to understand, making it a valuable contribution.
