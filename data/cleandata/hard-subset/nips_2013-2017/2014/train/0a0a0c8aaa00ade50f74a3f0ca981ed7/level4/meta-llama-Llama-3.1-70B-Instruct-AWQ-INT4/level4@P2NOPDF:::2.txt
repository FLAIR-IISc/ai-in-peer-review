This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network designed to store multiple stable activity patterns, or memories, addressing several limitations of previous attractor models, including the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. The authors employ a gradient descent-based training approach, minimizing a cost function that reduces activity changes in desired attractors, the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and maximizes attractor stability. The main findings include a weight distribution consistent with experimental observations and a balance of excitation and inhibition in attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written, making the model and training procedure easily comprehensible, along with the presentation of the results. The integration of biological constraints in the model and the resulting features after training render this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. By training a rate-based neuronal network to maintain graded and non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities, the authors demonstrate the feasibility of their approach. The incorporation of biologically plausible constraints on synapses and neurons leads to a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level, highlighting the model's potential in capturing essential aspects of neuronal networks.

I recommend acceptance of this manuscript, as it provides a novel and well-motivated contribution to the field of attractor neural networks, offering a more biologically plausible and robust alternative to existing models.
