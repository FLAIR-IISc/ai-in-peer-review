1. Proposal of an auto-associative memory model in a rate-based neural network that enforces biological constraints (Dale’s Law, non-saturating/graduated units).  
2. Memories encoded via synaptic modifications using gradient descent, constrained by a recent method to ensure linearized-fixed-point stability.  
3. Trained fixed points are “slow” (nearly fixed but not exact) and stable under the applied stability constraints.  
4. Network reproduces two experimental cortical features: synaptic weights centered around zero with long tails, and approximate excitatory–inhibitory input balance per unit.  
5. Those synaptic and E/I balance features are identified as critical for robust memory performance under perturbation and stochastic noise.  
6. Manuscript is clearly written, novel, and of significant interest; prior work is well reviewed and figures effectively illustrate results.  
7. Training procedure and network architecture are well-presented and shown to confer robustness to perturbations and noise.  
8. Key suggestion: clarify the connection between the chosen gradient-descent training procedure and the objective of achieving non-saturating units, including why this method was selected over alternatives.  
9. Additional suggestion: include (even briefly) a comparison of memory capacity (number of memories vs. network size) to prior models.
