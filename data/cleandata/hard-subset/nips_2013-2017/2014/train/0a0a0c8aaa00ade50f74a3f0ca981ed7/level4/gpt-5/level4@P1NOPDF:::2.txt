In this manuscript, the authors design a rate-based neural network trained to sustain multiple stable activity patterns (memories). The work aims to overcome two common shortcomings of previous attractor models: violations of Dale’s law and saturation of neuronal activity in memory states. Training is performed via gradient descent on a cost function that penalizes activity changes at the target attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while also explicitly maximizing attractor stability. The main outcomes are a synaptic weight distribution consistent with experimental observations and a balance of excitation and inhibition within attractor states.

The manuscript is well written. The model, training procedure, and results are clearly presented and easy to understand. The integration of biologically motivated constraints into the model, together with the properties that emerge after training, makes this paper an interesting and valuable contribution to the study of attractor neural networks. The authors successfully train a rate-based network to maintain graded, non-saturated attractors through appropriate synaptic weights and inhibitory activities. The synaptic and neuronal constraints imposed for biological plausibility yield a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
