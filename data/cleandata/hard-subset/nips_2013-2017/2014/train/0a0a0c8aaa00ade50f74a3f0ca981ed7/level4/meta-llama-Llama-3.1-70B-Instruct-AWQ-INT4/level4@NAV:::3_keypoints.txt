- Revisits associative memory with a fresh, insightful perspective on a classic problem.  
- Points out prior work often ignored biological constraints (Dale’s law), binary rate models, and non-sparse memory representations.  
- Develops a unified approach that simultaneously addresses those shortcomings.  
- Leverages a recent control-theoretic/spectral-bounding framework to construct networks.  
- Proposes a three-term cost function balancing fixed-point formation, stability, and weight regularization.  
- Enforces Dale’s principle in weight parameterization, producing naturally balanced excitation/inhibition.  
- Single-neuron firing saturation is not forced but emerges from optimization.  
- Implements memories via excitatory neurons while treating inhibitory neurons as optimized variables.  
- Validated with simulations showing noise robustness and accounts for reduced trial-to-trial variability via attractor convergence.  
- Paper is clearly written and makes an important contribution to neural computation.
