- Presents a rate-based neuronal network that stores multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Targets limitations of existing attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation in memory states.  
- Trains the network with gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes activity changes within desired attractors, penalizes the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and maximizes attractor stability.  
- Demonstrates weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.  
- Shows emergent balanced excitation–inhibition dynamics within attractor states.  
- Successfully produces graded, non-saturating attractors through synaptic and neuronal constraints.  
- Manuscript is well written and clearly presented; model and training procedure are readily understandable.  
- Integration of biological constraints yields interesting emergent network properties and constitutes a meaningful contribution to attractor neural network literature.
