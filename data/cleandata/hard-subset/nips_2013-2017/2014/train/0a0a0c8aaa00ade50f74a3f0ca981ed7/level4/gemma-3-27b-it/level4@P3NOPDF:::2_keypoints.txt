- Presents a rate-based network model that stores and maintains multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Directly addresses limitations of prior attractor models, namely violations of Dale’s law and saturation of activity.  
- Uses gradient descent to train the network with a cost function balancing attractor stability, minimal activity changes, and weight regularization.  
- Cost function includes Frobenius norm regularization, encouraging sparse, biologically plausible weight matrices.  
- Trained networks exhibit weight distributions closely matching experimental observations.  
- Emergent global excitation–inhibition balance within attractor states is demonstrated.  
- Model supports graded, non-saturated attractors through biologically motivated synaptic and neuronal constraints.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and the methods/results are easy to follow.  
- Experimental validation convincingly compares learned weight distributions to empirical data and shows biologically relevant E–I balance.  
- Suggested further analyses: robustness to perturbations/noisy inputs and network capacity (number of reliably stored attractors).  
- Recommend testing pattern completion with incomplete/noisy cues to demonstrate utility beyond stable-state storage.  
- Request more discussion on computational cost and scalability of the training method to larger or more complex networks.  
- Suggest acknowledging limitations of rate-based models versus spiking models and trade-offs in biological fidelity.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
