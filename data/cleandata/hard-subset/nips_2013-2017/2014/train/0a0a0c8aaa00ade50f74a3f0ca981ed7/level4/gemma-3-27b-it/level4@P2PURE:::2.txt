This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network designed to store multiple stable activity patterns, or memories. The authors aim to address limitations of existing attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation within memory states. They train the network using gradient descent on a cost function that simultaneously minimizes activity changes within desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing attractor stability. The key findings demonstrate a weight distribution consistent with experimental observations and a balanced excitation-inhibition dynamic within the attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written and clearly presented. I found both the model and the training procedure readily understandable, as were the results. The integration of biological constraints into the model, and the resulting emergent network properties, constitute an interesting contribution to the field of attractor neural networks. Specifically, the authors successfully train a rate-based network to support graded, non-saturating attractors, achieved through synaptic and neuronal constraints that promote excitation-inhibition balance at the network level.
