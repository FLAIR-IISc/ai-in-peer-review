- Proposes a novel control-theoretic framework to build attractor networks that satisfy physiological constraints.  
- Directly optimizes excitatory/inhibitory networks to make arbitrary analog patterns stable fixed points.  
- Enforces biological constraints (Dale’s law, recurrent and sparse synapses) in the learned networks.  
- Resulting networks operate in the balanced regime, matching a key feature of cortical circuits.  
- Networks are robust to corrupted memory cues and ongoing noise.  
- Trained networks naturally reproduce the observed reduction in trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains prior limitations, and presents methods/results accessibly.  
- Combination of biological constraints and post-training features makes the work an interesting contribution.  
- Weakness: model is rate-based, so applicability to spiking networks is unclear.  
- Weakness: training via gradient descent on a cost function may lack biological plausibility.  
- Weakness: paper does not clearly explain how the approach could be implemented biologically.  
- Arguments for acceptance rest on the novel framework, biological constraints satisfaction, balanced dynamics, and robustness results.  
- Arguments against acceptance focus on the rate-based assumption and non-biological learning rule.  
- Overall recommendation positive; scores—Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
