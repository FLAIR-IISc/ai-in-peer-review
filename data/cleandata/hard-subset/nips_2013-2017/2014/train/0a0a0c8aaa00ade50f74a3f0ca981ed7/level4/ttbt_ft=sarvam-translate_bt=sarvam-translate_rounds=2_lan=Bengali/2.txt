In this manuscript, the authors have designed a rate-based neuronal network trained to capture several stable patterns of activity, such as memory. Its goal is to overcome several shortcomings of previous attractive models, such as violations of Del's law and the saturation of memory state activity. For training, the authors use gradient descent on an expense function that reduces the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix and reduces changes in activity in the desired attractions, maximizing the stability of the attractions. The main results are a weight distribution close to the experimental results and a balance between excitation and inhibition in the attractive state.

The writing of the manuscript is good. I found the models and training methods, as well as the presentation of the results, to be easy to understand. The combination of the model's biological constraints and the characteristics obtained after training makes this paper an interesting contribution to understanding attractive neural networks. The authors train a rate-based neuronal network to capture graded and non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities. The constraints imposed on synapses and neurons for biological plausibility create a balance between excitation and inhibition at the network level.
