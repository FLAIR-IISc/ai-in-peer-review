- Introduces a rate-based network that stores multiple stable activity patterns as memories.
- Targets limitations of prior attractor models, specifically Dale’s law violations and saturated neuronal activity.
- Trains the network via gradient descent on a cost that enforces attractor stability by minimizing activity changes.
- Includes regularization of synapses using the Frobenius norm and an explicit term to maximize overall attractor stability.
- Produces synaptic weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.
- Achieves dynamic balance of excitation and inhibition within attractor states.
- Manuscript is well-written and clear; model, training, and results are easy to follow.
- Main contribution: showing that imposing biological constraints (graded, non-saturated attractors and plausible synaptic properties) yields emergent realistic network features, making the work a valuable contribution to attractor network studies.
