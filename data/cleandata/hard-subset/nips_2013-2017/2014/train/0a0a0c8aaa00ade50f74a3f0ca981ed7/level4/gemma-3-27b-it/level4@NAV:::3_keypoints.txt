- Fresh, insightful contribution to associative memory addressing overlooked biological constraints (Dale’s law, binary representations in rate-based models, non-sparse memories).  
- Presents a unified framework that simultaneously enforces these biological principles.  
- Leverages a recent control-theoretic framework and spectral bounding techniques to construct biologically plausible networks.  
- Core method uses a cost function balanced across three terms: memory formation, fixed-point stability, and weight regularization.  
- Directly incorporates Dale’s principle into weight parameterization, producing excitation–inhibition balance naturally.  
- Neuronal saturation emerges from optimization rather than being imposed at single-neuron level.  
- Uses excitatory neurons to implement memories while treating inhibitory neurons as optimization variables.  
- Demonstrates efficacy with robust numerical simulations showing resilience to noise.  
- Explains reduced trial-to-trial variability on stimulus onset via convergence to nearest attractor.  
- Paper is clearly presented, well written, and offers a concise, significant contribution.  
- Biological learning rule remains an open question; authors acknowledge and reference related ongoing work.  
- Creative and noteworthy use of inhibitory neurons as optimizable resources.
