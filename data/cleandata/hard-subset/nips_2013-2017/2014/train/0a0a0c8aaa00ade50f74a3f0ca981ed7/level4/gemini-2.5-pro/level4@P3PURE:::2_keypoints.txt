- Presents a rate-based neuronal network trained to store multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Aims to fix prior attractor-model issues: violation of Dale’s law and saturation of neuronal activities.  
- Uses gradient descent on a cost combining minimization of activity changes in target attractors, Frobenius norm of weights, and attractor stability.  
- Shows trained networks produce weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.  
- Demonstrates attractor states exhibit a balance of excitation and inhibition.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clear, and easy to follow regarding model, training, and results.  
- Incorporating biological constraints yields graded, non-saturated attractors and enhances the model’s biological relevance.
