The authors of this manuscript design a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns, or memories, aiming to address several limitations of previous attractor models, specifically the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. To achieve this, they employ gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes activity changes in desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing attractor stability. The primary results include a weight distribution comparable to experimental findings and a balance of excitation and inhibition in attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written, making the model and training procedure easily comprehensible, as well as the presentation of results. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the ensuing features after training render this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. By training a rate-based neuronal network to sustain graded and non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities, the authors demonstrate the efficacy of imposing biological constraints on synapses and neurons, yielding a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level.
