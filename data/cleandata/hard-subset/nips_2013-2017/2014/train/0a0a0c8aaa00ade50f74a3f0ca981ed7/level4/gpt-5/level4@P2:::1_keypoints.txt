- Proposes a control-theoretic framework to construct autoassociative E/I rate networks that obey Dale’s law and store graded (analog) memories as stable fixed points.  
- Enforces fixed-point conditions by minimizing state derivatives at target patterns and enforces stability by minimizing the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for gradient-based optimization.  
- Learned networks operate in a dynamically balanced E/I regime, avoid firing-rate saturation, and recall memories robustly from noisy cues and ongoing noise.  
- Networks exhibit realistic cortical phenomenology: across-trial variability reduction after stimulus onset and heavy-tailed synaptic weights with many small values.  
- Many weak synapses can be pruned with little loss of recall, indicating functional sparsity and dependence on a subset of stronger synapses.  
- Paper is clearly written, well structured, and supported by helpful figures and simulations.  
- Integrates multiple physiological constraints (Dale’s law, E/I balance, graded responses) within a single unified framework, addressing limits of classic models.  
- Technically solid: fixed-point enforcement combined with SSA-based stability provides a principled route to stable attractors and is implemented carefully.  
- Robust empirical performance under significant input noise and ongoing fluctuations strengthens biological relevance.  
- Lacks clear justification for choosing SSA over alternative stability methods (e.g., convex relaxations, Lyapunov approaches) and would benefit from ablation or comparison.  
- No direct comparison of storage capacity to prior models; empirical capacity curves or qualitative comparisons are missing.  
- Target states are “slow points” before convergence and basin widths are not reported; empirical basin-size measures (vs. cue noise) are needed to contextualize robustness.  
- Clarity could improve on linking objectives to method and explaining trade-offs introduced by the SSA smoothing parameter ε.  
- Overall, novel and significant integration of control-theoretic stability optimization with biologically constrained analog attractors, but needs more comparative and ablation analyses to strengthen claims.
