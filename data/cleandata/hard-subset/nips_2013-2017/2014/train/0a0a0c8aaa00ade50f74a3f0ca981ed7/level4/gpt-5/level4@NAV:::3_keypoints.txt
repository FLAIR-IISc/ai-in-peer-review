- Imposes Dale’s principle through weight parameterization, producing balanced excitation–inhibition naturally.

- Single-neuron saturation emerges from optimization even though it is not explicitly enforced.

- Accounts for reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset by convergence to the nearest attractor.
