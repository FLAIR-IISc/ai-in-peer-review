- Proposes a rate-based auto-associative memory network that enforces Dale’s Law and encodes memories with graded, non-saturating responses.  
- Learning modifies synapses via gradient descent combined with a recent method that constrains learning to ensure stable fixed points.  
- Trained networks show stable, slow dynamics around target memory states in simulations.  
- Emergent heavy-tailed synaptic weight distribution centered near zero.  
- Emergent approximate excitatory/inhibitory (E/I) balance at the neuron level.  
- E/I balance and weight distribution are identified as key contributors to robust memory retrieval under perturbations and stochastic noise.  
- Paper is clearly written, novel, and provides an effective historical review and clear presentation of architecture and training.  
- Figures are well-chosen and the authors demonstrate performance limitations under noise to highlight robustness.  
- Reviewer requests clearer explanation of why this specific training procedure was chosen and what makes it especially effective at producing graded responses.  
- Reviewer asks whether alternate training methods could achieve similar results and requests more insight into method uniqueness/advantages.  
- Reviewer suggests adding comparisons to previous models, particularly memory capacity as a function of network size.
