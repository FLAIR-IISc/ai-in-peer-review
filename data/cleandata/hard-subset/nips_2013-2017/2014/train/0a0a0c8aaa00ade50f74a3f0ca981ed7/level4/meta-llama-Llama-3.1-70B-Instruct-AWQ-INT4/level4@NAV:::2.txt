This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network designed to maintain multiple stable activity patterns, or memories, with the aim of addressing the limitations of previous attractor models, specifically the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. The authors employ a gradient descent approach on a cost function that minimizes activity changes in desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing attractor stability. The key findings include a weight distribution that aligns with experimental observations and a balance of excitation and inhibition in attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written, making the model and training procedure straightforward to comprehend, as are the results. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the resulting features after training render this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. By training a rate-based neuronal network to sustain graded and non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities, the authors demonstrate that biologically plausible constraints imposed on synapses and neurons yield a balanced excitation and inhibition at the network level.
