- Presents a rate-based neuronal network that maintains multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Targets limitations of prior attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and saturation of activity in memory states.  
- Uses gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes changes in desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix while maximizing attractor stability.  
- Finds a learned weight distribution consistent with experimental observations.  
- Demonstrates balanced excitation and inhibition in attractor states.  
- Shows training yields graded, non-saturated attractors with biologically plausible synaptic and neuronal constraints.  
- Manuscript is well-written and the model/training procedure are easy to understand.  
- Argues that integrating biological constraints produces biologically plausible network-level E/I balance and contributes to understanding attractor neural networks.
