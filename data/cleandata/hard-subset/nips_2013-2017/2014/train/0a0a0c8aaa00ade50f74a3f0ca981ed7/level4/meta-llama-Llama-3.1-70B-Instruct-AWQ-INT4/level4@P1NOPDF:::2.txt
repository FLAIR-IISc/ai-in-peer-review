In this manuscript, the authors design a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain a number of stable activity patterns, or memories, thereby addressing several shortcomings of previous attractor models. Specifically, they aim to overcome the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. To achieve this, the authors employ a gradient descent method to minimize a cost function that reduces activity changes in desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing the stability of attractors.

The manuscript is well-written, making the model and training procedure easy to comprehend, as well as the presentation of the results. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the resulting features after training render this paper an interesting contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. The authors successfully train a rate-based neuronal network to hold graded and non-saturated attractors in the synaptic weights and inhibitory activities. By imposing constraints on synapses and neurons for biological plausibility, the authors achieve a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level.
