- Introduces a rate-based neural network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Addresses key limitations of prior attractor models, notably violations of Dale’s law and saturation of neural activity.
- Employs gradient descent on a cost function with three terms: minimize activity changes at target attractors, penalize large synaptic weights (Frobenius norm), and enhance attractor stability.
- Trained networks produce synaptic weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.
- Networks achieve a balanced state of excitation and inhibition within attractors.
- Integration of biological constraints leads to emergent graded, non-saturated attractors and network-level E–I balance.
- Constraining synapses and neuronal types according to neurobiological principles yields stable, robust attractor dynamics.
- Paper is clearly written; the model and training procedure are easy to follow and results are transparently presented.
- Overall, a solid and interesting contribution demonstrating biologically grounded rate-based networks can implement memory states without violating Dale’s law or causing activity saturation.
