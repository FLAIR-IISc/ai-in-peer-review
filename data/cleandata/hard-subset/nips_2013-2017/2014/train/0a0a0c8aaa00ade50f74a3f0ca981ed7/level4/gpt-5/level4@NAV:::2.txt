In this manuscript, the authors propose a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns, i.e., memories. The goal is to address several limitations of earlier attractor models, namely violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation in memory states. For training, they apply gradient descent to a cost function that minimizes activity changes in the target attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing attractor stability. The main outcomes are a weight distribution in line with experimental observations and a balance between excitation and inhibition in the attractor states.

The manuscript is well written. I found the model, the training procedure, and the presentation of the results clear and easy to follow. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the resulting properties after training make this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. The authors train a rate-based neuronal network to store graded, non-saturated attractors in the synaptic weights and inhibitory activities, and biologically motivated constraints on synapses and neurons yield a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
