In this manuscript, the authors have designed a rate-based neuronal network trained to retain several stable activity patterns, i.e., memories. This aims to overcome several shortcomings of previous attraction models, namely the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. For training, the authors use gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes activity changes in desired attractions and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, maximizing the stability of the attractions. The main result is a weight distribution close to experimental findings and a balance of excitation and inhibition in attraction states.

The writing of the manuscript is good. I found the model and the training process easy to understand, as well as the presentation of the results. The combination of biological constraints going into the model and the characteristics obtained after training makes this paper an interesting contribution to the understanding of attraction neural networks. The authors train a rate-based neuronal network to retain graded and non-saturated attractions in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities. Constraints imposed on synapses and neurons for biological probability create a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level.
