In this manuscript, the authors present a rate-based neuronal network designed to store a set of stable activity patterns, or memories. The work aims to address limitations of existing attractor models, specifically violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation within memory states. To train the network, the authors employ gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes activity changes within desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while simultaneously maximizing attractor stability. The key findings demonstrate a weight distribution consistent with experimental observations and a balance of excitation and inhibition in the attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written and clear. I found the model and training procedure easy to follow, as was the presentation of the results. The combination of biologically-motivated constraints incorporated into the model and the resulting network properties make this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. Specifically, the authors successfully train a rate-based network to support graded, non-saturated attractors, achieved through careful consideration of synaptic and neuronal constraints promoting biological plausibility. This ultimately leads to a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
