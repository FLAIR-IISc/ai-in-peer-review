- Proposes a novel rate-based network architecture trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Explicitly addresses prior attractor-model limitations, notably violation of Dale’s law and activity saturation in memory states.  
- Training uses gradient descent on a cost that minimizes deviations from desired attractors while penalizing weight magnitude (Frobenius norm) and maximizing stability.  
- Resulting synaptic weight distribution aligns with experimental observations.  
- Trained networks exhibit balanced excitation and inhibition within attractor states.  
- Produces graded, non-saturated attractors in both synaptic weights and inhibitory activities.  
- Integration of biological constraints into the model yields emergent, biologically plausible network features.  
- Manuscript is well-written with clear explanations of model, training, and results.  
- Overall, a significant contribution to understanding biologically plausible attractor neural networks.
