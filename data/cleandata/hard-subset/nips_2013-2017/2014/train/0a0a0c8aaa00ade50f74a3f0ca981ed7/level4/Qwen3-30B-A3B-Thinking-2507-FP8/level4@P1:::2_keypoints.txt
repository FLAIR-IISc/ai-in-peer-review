- Presents a control-theoretic method to build biologically plausible attractor networks for analog memory storage.  
- Directly optimizes synaptic weights and inhibitory dynamics while enforcing Dale’s law.  
- Stabilizes arbitrary graded activity patterns as fixed points without activity saturation.  
- Strictly enforces E/I separation and naturally achieves E/I balance in trained networks.  
- Uses the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) for stability optimization — a novel application in neural modeling.  
- Resulting networks explain reduced trial-to-trial variability observed in cortical recordings.  
- Produces sparse synaptic weight distributions matching cortical data, with many silent excitatory synapses not required for recall.  
- Demonstrates robust recall against cue and ongoing noise, with performance near an ideal observer.  
- Model stabilizes a realistic baseline firing rate (5 Hz) across trials.  
- Presentation is clear and reproducible; cost function, parameters, and optimization are well-specified.  
- Limitation: rate-based model only — no demonstration of transfer to spiking (LIF) networks.  
- Limitation: modest storage capacity (≈0.2 for 30 patterns in 150 neurons); scalability discussion is limited.  
- Limitation: optimization is algorithmic and not biologically plausible; local learning rules are not provided.  
- Originality/significance: unifies Dale’s law, graded activity, and E/I balance in a single robust framework.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
