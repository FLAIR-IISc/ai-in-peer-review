In this manuscript, the authors have designed a rate-based neuronal network that is trained to capture several stable patterns of activity, i.e., memory. Its goal is to overcome several shortcomings of previous attractive models, such as violations of Dale's law and the saturation of activity in memory states. For training, the authors use gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix and changes in activity in the desired attractors, maximizing the stability of the attractors. The main results are a weight distribution close to the experimental results and a balance between excitation and inhibition in the attractors state.

The writing of the manuscript is good. I found the model and training methods, as well as the presentation of the results, to be easy to understand. The combination of biological constraints in the model and the characteristics obtained after training makes this paper an interesting contribution to understanding attractive neural networks. The authors train a rate-based neuronal network to capture graded and non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities. The constraints imposed on synapses and neurons for biological plausibility create a balance between excitation and inhibition at the network level.
