- Tackles constructing biologically plausible E–I networks that store/retrieve analog memory patterns while enforcing Dale’s law, graded activity, low firing rates, and dynamic balance.  
- Introduces a control-theoretic framework using Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) optimization and weight reparameterization to enforce Dale’s law and stabilize arbitrary analog patterns as fixed points.  
- Achieves stable, low-rate dynamics without explicit neuronal saturation, naturally balanced excitation and inhibition, and accounts for post-stimulus reduction in trial-to-trial variability.  
- Fixed-point error is small (‖dv/dt‖² ≈ 0.05 Hz²) and shown to have negligible impact on recall; network is robust to noise and sparse connectivity.  
- Capacity reported ≈ 0.2 memories per neuron; sparsity levels align with cortical data and method can extend toward spiking networks via gain matching.  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous SSA-based stability optimization, quantified performance, and thorough experiments (Figs. 1–6).  
- Clarity is excellent: concepts (SSA, Dale’s enforcement) precisely defined and figures effectively convey results; connection to experimental data is compelling.  
- Originality: first unified approach satisfying all listed physiological constraints; frames inhibitory neurons as novel "auxiliary variables."  
- Significance: provides a testable framework for cortical analog memory and explains observed variability reduction.  
- Weakness 1: Target patterns are not exact fixed points (residual ‖dv/dt‖²); practical impact needs quantification (e.g., drift over behaviorally relevant timescales).  
- Weakness 2: Choice of log-normal pattern distribution lacks biological citation/motivation; cite in vivo firing-rate distributions to justify heavy-tailed assumption.  
- Weakness 3: Capacity scaling with network size is not discussed; add note/analysis on how SSA-based stability limits storage capacity.  
- Weakness 4: Paper doesn’t address whether Dale’s law confers computational advantages vs. being a biophysical constraint; clarify intent and cite evolutionary/functional perspectives.  
- Pro: Unifies critical physiological constraints with a novel, rigorous method and compelling empirical validation.  
- Con: Minor gaps in fixed-point quantification, distributional motivation, and capacity discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-executed contribution with minor, addressable revisions.
