This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network designed to store multiple stable activity patterns, or memories, and addresses limitations of existing attractor models – specifically, violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation within memory states. The authors employ a gradient descent approach to train the network, optimizing a cost function that minimizes activity perturbations within desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while simultaneously maximizing attractor stability. Key findings include a weight distribution consistent with experimental data and a balanced excitation-inhibition dynamic observed in the attractor states. 

The manuscript is well-written and clear; both the model and training methodology are readily comprehensible, as is the presentation of results. The integration of biological constraints into the model, and the resulting network characteristics, represent a valuable contribution to the field of attractor neural networks. Notably, the authors successfully train a rate-based network to support graded, non-saturating attractors, achieved through biologically-informed constraints on synaptic weights and neuronal activity, ultimately leading to excitation-inhibition balance at the network level.
