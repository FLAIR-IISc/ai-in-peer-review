- Proposes a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Targets and overcomes key shortcomings of prior attractor models: violation of Dale’s law and activity saturation.  
- Training via gradient descent minimizes activity changes in desired attractors, penalizes the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and maximizes attractor stability.  
- Manuscript is well-written; model, training procedure, and results are clearly presented.  
- Integrates biological constraints on synapses and neurons, increasing model plausibility.  
- Successfully trains networks to hold graded, non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities.  
- Imposed constraints lead to a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition.
