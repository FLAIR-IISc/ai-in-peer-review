- Revisits associative memory, providing new insights and a comprehensive strategy addressing long-standing issues.
- Highlights neglect of biological constraints in prior work: Dale's law, rate models with binary memories, and non-sparse memory representations.
- Integrates these challenges simultaneously using a control-theoretic spectral-bounding approach.
- Introduces a three-term cost function (fixed-point enforcement, stability, and weight regularization) optimized via gradient descent.
- Enforces Dale’s principle through weight parameterization, producing naturally balanced excitation and inhibition.
- Uses excitatory neurons to implement memories while treating inhibitory neurons as optimized resources.
- Single-neuron saturation emerges naturally from the optimization rather than being explicitly enforced.
- Validates results with numerical simulations showing robustness to noise and good associative-memory performance.
- Offers an explanation for reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset via convergence to the nearest attractor.
- Does not propose a biologically plausible learning mechanism yet, though relevant recent proposals are mentioned.
