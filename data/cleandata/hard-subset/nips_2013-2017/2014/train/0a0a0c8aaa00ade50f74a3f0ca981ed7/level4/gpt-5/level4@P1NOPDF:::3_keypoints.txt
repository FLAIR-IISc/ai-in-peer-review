- Revisits associative memory with a fresh perspective and adds substantial insight to a classic topic.  
- Highlights that prior work often ignored biological constraints: Dale’s law, non-binary rate-based representations, and sparsity.  
- Proposes a unified strategy that addresses all those biological constraints simultaneously.  
- Employs a recent control-theoretic framework using spectral-bounding techniques to construct networks.  
- Optimizes a cost function with three terms enforcing target fixed points, stability, and weight-penalty to avoid saturation.  
- Enforces Dale’s principle via weight parameterization, producing a natural excitation–inhibition balance.  
- Single-neuron firing saturation emerges from optimization even though it is not explicitly enforced.  
- Uses only excitatory neurons to encode memories while treating inhibitory neurons as optimizable resources.  
- Validates the approach with numerical simulations and demonstrates robustness to noise.  
- Explains post-stimulus reduction in trial-to-trial variability as convergence to the nearest attractor.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and an important contribution; using inhibitory neurons as resources is noted as interesting and advantageous.  
- Does not provide a biologically plausible learning mechanism but cites recent proposals that might lead to one.

- Minor concern: motivate the use of a log-normal distribution for memory states.  
- Minor concern: quantify how much the cost function (4) allows violations of exact fixed-point memory conditions.
