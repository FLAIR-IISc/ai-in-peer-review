- Presents a rate-based neuronal network trained to store multiple stable activity patterns (memories).  
- Objective: build an attractor network that respects Dale's law and avoids neuronal activity saturation.  
- Training via gradient descent on a cost combining: minimize activity changes in attractors, Frobenius-norm regularization of weights, and explicit attractor stability maximization.  
- Main results: learned synaptic weight distribution closely matches experimental observations.  
- Main results: recall of attractor states exhibits dynamic excitation–inhibition balance.  
- Manuscript is well-written with clear presentation of model, training, and results.  
- Key strength: effective integration of biological constraints (Dale’s law, graded firing rates) into model design.  
- Contribution: shows that imposing plausible synaptic/neural constraints can produce emergent network-level features and offers new insights into attractor network principles.
