This manuscript presents a novel rate-based neuronal network designed to store and retrieve analog memories, addressing several limitations of previous attractor models. The authors propose a control-theoretic framework that satisfies key physiological constraints, including Dale's law and the absence of activity saturation in memory states. The network is trained using a gradient descent algorithm to minimize a cost function that combines fixed point and stability conditions, while incorporating biological constraints.

The manuscript is well-written, and the model and training procedure are clearly explained. The results are presented in a logical and easy-to-follow manner, highlighting the network's ability to store and retrieve graded, non-saturated attractors. The authors demonstrate that the trained network exhibits a balance of excitation and inhibition, which is a desirable feature of biological neural networks.

The paper's strengths include its ability to overcome previous limitations in attractor models, its incorporation of biological constraints, and its demonstration of a balanced excitation-inhibition regime. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of the results, highlighting the implications of their work for understanding the neural substrate of memory.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide a direct comparison with other existing models or methods. While the authors mention previous work in the field, they do not explicitly compare their results to those obtained using alternative approaches. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the network's robustness to different types of noise or perturbations.

Overall, the manuscript presents a significant contribution to the field of attractor neural networks, and its findings have important implications for understanding the neural mechanisms of memory storage and retrieval. The paper is well-written, and the results are clearly presented and well-supported by the data.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The manuscript presents a novel and well-designed rate-based neuronal network that addresses several limitations of previous attractor models.
* The authors incorporate biological constraints into the model, leading to a more realistic and plausible representation of neural networks.
* The results demonstrate a balance of excitation and inhibition in the trained network, which is a desirable feature of biological neural networks.
* The paper provides a thorough discussion of the results and their implications for understanding the neural substrate of memory.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the network's robustness to different types of noise or perturbations.
* The authors do not provide a direct comparison with other existing models or methods, which would help to contextualize their results.
* Some readers may find the mathematical derivations and technical details of the model and training procedure to be challenging to follow.

Recommendations for improvement:

* Consider adding a direct comparison with other existing models or methods to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the results.
* Provide a more detailed analysis of the network's robustness to different types of noise or perturbations.
* Consider adding more technical details or appendices to help readers who may find the mathematical derivations challenging to follow.
