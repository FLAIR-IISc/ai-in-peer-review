

This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories) while addressing key limitations of prior attractor models, specifically violations of Dale's law and activity saturation in memory states. The authors employ gradient descent optimization on a cost function that minimizes activity fluctuations within target attractors, reduces the Frobenius norm of the synaptic weight matrix, and enhances attractor stability. The primary outcomes demonstrate synaptic weight distributions aligning with experimental observations and a network-level balance of excitation and inhibition within the attractor states. The manuscript is clearly written, with the model, training methodology, and results presentation being readily comprehensible. The integration of biological constraints into the model, yielding biologically plausible features post-training, constitutes a significant and interesting contribution to the field of attractor neural network research. The authors successfully train a rate-based network to sustain graded, non-saturated attractor states, where biological plausibility constraints on synapses and neurons naturally produce balanced excitation and inhibition across the network.
