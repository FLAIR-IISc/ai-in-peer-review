- Presents a novel rate-based auto-associative memory model that enforces several biological constraints.  
- Reviews prior work on training networks for auto-associative tasks, clearly highlighting contributions and shortcomings.  
- Introduces a gradient-descent training procedure constrained to keep the linearized dynamics around fixed points stable using a recent stability method.  
- Demonstrates via simulations that the trained network achieves robust auto-associative recall under stochastic input noise.  
- Trained networks exhibit biologically consistent features: synaptic weight distribution centered at zero with long tails and balanced excitatory/inhibitory inputs per unit.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, effective figures that illustrate main results.  
- Methods are described clearly and in detail, facilitating reproducibility and understanding.  
- Reviewer asks for clarification of why the chosen training procedure was selected and whether other procedures could achieve similar results.  
- Requests explanation of what makes this particular method effective at producing non-saturating graded memories.  
- Suggests adding a comparison of network capacity (number of memories vs. units) to prior models to better characterize strengths and weaknesses.  
- Notes the authors discuss limitations but recommends expanding empirical comparisons to the literature.  
- Overall positive assessment with scores: Originality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Significance 8.5/10, Overall 8.5/10.
