- Proposes a novel control-theoretic spectral-bounding method to construct biologically constrained networks.
- Defines a cost function with three terms—fixed-point enforcement, stability, and weight regularization—optimized by gradient descent.
- Incorporates Dale’s principle into weight parameterization, producing natural excitation–inhibition balance and emergent neuronal saturation.
- Uses excitatory neurons for memory storage while treating inhibitory neurons as auxiliary, optimizable resources.
- Validates the model with simulations showing effective associative memory performance and robustness to noise.
- Explains trial-to-trial variability quenching after stimulus onset as convergence to the nearest attractor.
- Does not provide a biologically plausible learning rule, though points to recent work as a possible path.
