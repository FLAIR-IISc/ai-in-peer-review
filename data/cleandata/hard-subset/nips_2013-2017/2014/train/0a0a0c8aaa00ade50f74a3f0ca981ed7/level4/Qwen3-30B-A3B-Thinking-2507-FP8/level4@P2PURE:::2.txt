

The authors propose a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories), addressing two key limitations in prior attractor models: violations of Dale's law and saturation of activity in memory states. Training employs gradient descent on a cost function that minimizes activity changes within target attractors, minimizes the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and maximizes attractor stability. The primary results demonstrate a weight distribution aligning with experimental observations and a balanced excitation-inhibition profile in the attractor states. The manuscript is well-written, with the model, training procedure, and results clearly presented. The integration of biological constraints into the model, yielding non-saturated attractor states with balanced excitation and inhibition, constitutes a compelling contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks.
