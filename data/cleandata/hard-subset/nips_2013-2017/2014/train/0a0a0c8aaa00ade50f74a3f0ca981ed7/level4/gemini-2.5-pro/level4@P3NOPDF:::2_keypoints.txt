- Proposes a method to train a rate-based recurrent neural network to act as an attractor network storing multiple stable activity patterns.  
- Targets key shortcomings of classical attractor models: violation of Dale’s law and saturated memory-state activity.  
- Uses gradient descent on a custom objective that enforces fixed-point memories, maximizes attractor stability, and regularizes synaptic weights via Frobenius-norm minimization.  
- Training produces emergent synaptic weight distributions that align with experimental neurobiological findings.  
- Training also yields dynamic excitatory–inhibitory balance during memory recall.  
- Manuscript is well-written and clear; model, training method, and results are easy to follow.  
- Major strength: successful integration of biological constraints (Dale’s law, non-saturating responses) into a functional learning framework.  
- Demonstrates that optimizing for memory under realistic constraints causes self-organization of brain-like features, linking normative objectives to observed cortical phenomena.
