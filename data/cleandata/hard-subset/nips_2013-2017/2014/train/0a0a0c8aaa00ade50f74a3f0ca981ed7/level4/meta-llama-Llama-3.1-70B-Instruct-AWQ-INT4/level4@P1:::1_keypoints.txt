- Presents a biologically plausible rate-based auto-associative memory model obeying Dale’s Law and using graded (non-saturating) unit responses.
- Encodes memories via synaptic modifications from gradient-descent training with a stability constraint on linearized fixed-point dynamics.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides a useful historical review of prior approaches.
- Network architecture and training procedure are presented clearly, with effective figures illustrating results.
- Demonstrates robustness and limitations of the trained network under added network noise.
- Unclear why the chosen training procedure was selected or whether it is uniquely suited to enforce Dale’s Law and graded memories.
- Questions remain whether alternative training methods could achieve similar non-saturating memory solutions.
- Lacks quantitative comparison of memory capacity (number of memories vs. network size) against previous models.
- Strengths: clarity, biological plausibility, novelty, and demonstration of noise robustness.
- Weaknesses: insufficient explanation of method-objective relationship and limited capacity comparisons.
- Recommendations: clarify rationale for the training method, discuss alternatives/mechanisms for its effectiveness, and add capacity comparisons to prior work.
