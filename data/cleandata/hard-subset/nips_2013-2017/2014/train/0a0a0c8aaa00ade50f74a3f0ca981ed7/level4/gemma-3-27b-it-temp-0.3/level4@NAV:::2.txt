This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network designed to store multiple stable activity patterns, or memories, and addresses limitations found in existing attractor models – specifically, violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation within memory states. The authors employ a gradient descent approach to train the network, optimizing a cost function that simultaneously minimizes activity fluctuations within desired attractors and the magnitude of synaptic weights (via the Frobenius norm), while maximizing attractor stability. Key findings demonstrate a weight distribution consistent with experimental data and a balanced excitation/inhibition profile within the attractor states. 

The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized. The model, training methodology, and results are readily comprehensible. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the subsequent emergence of biologically relevant network features represent a valuable contribution to the field of attractor neural networks. Specifically, the authors successfully train a network to support graded, non-saturating attractors through constrained synaptic weights and neuronal properties, ultimately leading to excitation-inhibition balance at the network level.
