- Clearly written, novel submission of significant interest to the neural computation community.
- Provides a well-contextualized review of prior work, highlighting contributions and limitations of existing approaches.
- Network architecture and training procedure are presented with clarity; figures effectively illustrate key results.
- Demonstrates the network’s robustness by showing performance limitations under noisy conditions.
- Introduces a rate-based auto-associative memory model that enforces biological constraints, notably Dale’s Law.
- Enables encoding of memories without relying on saturating or binary unit responses.
- Memories are learned via gradient descent constrained by a recent method that stabilizes linearized dynamics around fixed points.
- Simulations show the trained fixed points are stable but exhibit slow dynamics.
- Trained network reproduces cortical-like features: synaptic weight distribution centered near zero with heavy tails and balanced excitatory/inhibitory inputs.
- Reviewer requests clearer justification for choosing this specific training procedure over alternatives and whether other methods would yield similar results.
- Suggests that comparing network capacity (number of memories vs. network size) to existing models would be valuable.
- Overall, a significant contribution that would be strengthened by clarifying the rationale for the training approach and benchmarking against established models.
