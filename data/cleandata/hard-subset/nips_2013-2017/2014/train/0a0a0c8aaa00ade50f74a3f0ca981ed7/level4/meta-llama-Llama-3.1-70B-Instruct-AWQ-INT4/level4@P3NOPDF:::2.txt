This manuscript presents a novel rate-based neuronal network designed to store and maintain a number of stable activity patterns, or memories. The authors' primary goal is to address several limitations of previous attractor models, specifically the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states. To achieve this, they employ a gradient descent training method that minimizes activity changes in desired attractors and the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, while maximizing the stability of attractors.

The manuscript is well-written, making the model and training procedure easy to comprehend. The presentation of the results is also clear and concise. The integration of biological constraints into the model and the resulting features after training render this paper a valuable contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks. By training a rate-based neuronal network to hold graded and non-saturated attractors in synaptic weights and inhibitory activities, the authors demonstrate the potential of their approach.

Notably, the constraints imposed on synapses and neurons for biological plausibility lead to a balance of excitation and inhibition at the network level. This balance is a crucial aspect of neural networks and is often overlooked in simplified models. The authors' attention to this detail adds credibility to their findings and enhances the overall impact of the paper.

One of the main results of this study is the weight distribution of the trained network, which closely resembles experimental findings. This suggests that the proposed model can capture essential features of real neuronal networks. Furthermore, the balance of excitation and inhibition in the attractor states is a significant achievement, as it is a fundamental property of biological neural networks.

Overall, this manuscript presents a compelling contribution to the field of attractor neural networks. The authors' approach to incorporating biological constraints into the model and the resulting features after training make this paper an interesting and valuable read. The clarity of the writing and the presentation of the results further enhance the paper's impact.
