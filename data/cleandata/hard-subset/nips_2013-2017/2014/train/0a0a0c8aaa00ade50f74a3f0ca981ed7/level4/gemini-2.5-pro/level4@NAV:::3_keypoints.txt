- Unified approach tackling multiple prior limitations (Dale's law, non-binary rates, non-sparse patterns) simultaneously.  
- Novel use of control-theoretic framework and spectral bounding to construct associative memory networks.  
- Optimization via gradient descent balances three objectives: promote memories as fixed points, ensure fixed-point stability, and penalize large synaptic weights.  
- Direct enforcement of Dale's principle in weight parameterization yields natural excitation–inhibition balance.  
- Memory representations are implemented exclusively in excitatory neurons; inhibitory neurons are optimizable shaping dynamics.  
- Single-neuron firing-rate saturation emerges naturally from optimization rather than being explicitly enforced.  
- Numerical simulations validate associative memory function and demonstrate robustness to noise.  
- Model accounts for reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset via convergence to nearest attractor.
