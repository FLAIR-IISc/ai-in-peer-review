This manuscript presents a novel rate-based neuronal network model designed to store and retrieve analog memories while adhering to key physiological constraints. The authors successfully overcome several limitations of previous attractor models, including the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of activities in memory states.

The model is based on a network of excitatory and inhibitory neurons, where the activity of each neuron is represented by a scalar potential. The authors use a control-theoretic framework to optimize the network parameters, including synaptic weights and inhibitory neuron firing rates, to store a set of arbitrary analog patterns as stable fixed points of the dynamics. The optimization procedure involves minimizing a cost function that includes terms for the stability of the attractors, the smoothness of the spectral abscissa, and the regularization of the synaptic weights.

The authors demonstrate that their model can store and retrieve graded, non-saturated activity patterns, and that the resulting network exhibits a balance of excitation and inhibition. The weight distribution of the trained network is sparse and similar to that observed in experimental findings. The authors also show that the network is robust to noise and can perform successful recall even when the initial cue is corrupted.

The manuscript is well-written, and the model and training procedure are easy to follow. The presentation of the results is clear, and the authors provide a good discussion of the implications of their findings. The combination of biological constraints and the resulting features of the trained network make this paper an interesting contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks.

Weaknesses:

* The model is based on a rate-based formulation of the circuit dynamics, which may not be directly applicable to spiking neural networks.

* The manuscript could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the model and experimental findings.
