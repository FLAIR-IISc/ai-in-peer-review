- Proposes a rate-based network that stores multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Explicitly addresses prior attractor-model limitations, including Dale’s law violations and activity saturation.
- Trains the network with gradient descent on a cost combining stability of desired attractors, minimization of activity change, and Frobenius-norm weight regularization.
- Produces weight distributions consistent with experimental observations.
- Demonstrates balanced excitation–inhibition dynamics within attractor states.
- Manuscript is well-written and the model/training procedure are clearly presented.
- Shows that biologically-motivated constraints yield graded, non-saturated attractors and network-level E/I balance.
