- Develops a rate-based neuronal network that stores stable, graded activity patterns while addressing violations of Dale’s law and activity saturation.  
- Trains the network by gradient descent on a cost combining deviation from desired attractors, Frobenius norm of weights, and attractor stability.  
- Uses the Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA) from robust control theory to enforce and optimize attractor stability.  
- Results show weight distributions consistent with experiments and attractor states with balanced excitation and inhibition.  
- Successfully produces graded, non-saturating attractors by optimizing synaptic weights and inhibitory neuron activities.  
- Incorporates biologically plausible constraints (Dale’s law, threshold-quadratic gain), increasing biological relevance.  
- Emergent network properties include sparse connectivity, E/I balance, robustness to noise, and reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clearly presented, and supported by informative figures.  
- Weakness: reliance on a rate-based model abstracts away important spiking dynamics and limits biological realism.  
- Weakness: optimization hyperparameters (ηs, ηF, ε) appear chosen heuristically with limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: limited, non-systematic exploration of network capacity and its dependence on size and connectivity.  
- Weakness: the chosen scaling rule for the SSA smoothness parameter ε lacks rigorous justification and may affect results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem, overcomes key prior limitations, uses principled stability methods, and links to experimental phenomena.  
- Arguments against acceptance: rate-model limitations and heuristic tuning/limited capacity analysis raise concerns about generalizability and robustness.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-supported contribution that outweighs weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
