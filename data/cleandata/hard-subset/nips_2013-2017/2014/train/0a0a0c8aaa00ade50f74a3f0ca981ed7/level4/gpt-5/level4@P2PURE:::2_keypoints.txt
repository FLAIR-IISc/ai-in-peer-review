- Proposes a rate-based network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories).
- Aims to fix prior attractor-model limitations: violations of Dale’s law and saturation of activity in memory states.
- Uses gradient descent on a cost combining attractor stability, minimal activity changes in target states, and Frobenius-norm weight regularization.
- Produces synaptic weight distributions that align with experimental observations.
- Trained networks show balanced excitation and inhibition within attractor states.
- Supports graded, non-saturated attractor states, reflected in synaptic weights and inhibitory activity.
- Incorporates biologically motivated synaptic and neuronal constraints that yield emergent network-level E/I balance.
- Manuscript is clearly written; model, training procedure, and results are straightforward to follow.
- Considered a valuable contribution to the study of attractor neural networks.
