- Revisits associative memory with new insight by enforcing biological constraints (Dale’s law, non-binary, sparse codes).
- Unifies previously separate considerations into a single control-theoretic framework based on spectral bounding.
- Optimizes a three-term cost (fixed-point enforcement, stability promotion, weight-penalty) via gradient descent.
- Encodes Dale’s law directly in weight parameterization, producing naturally balanced excitation and inhibition.
- Single-neuron saturation emerges from optimization despite not being explicitly enforced.
- Memory is implemented by excitatory neurons while inhibitory neurons are treated as optimized resources.
- Numerical simulations validate the approach and show robustness to noise.
- Model accounts for experimentally observed reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset via attractor convergence.
- Paper is clearly written and presents an important contribution to neural computation.
- Does not yet propose a biologically plausible learning rule, though potential directions are noted.
