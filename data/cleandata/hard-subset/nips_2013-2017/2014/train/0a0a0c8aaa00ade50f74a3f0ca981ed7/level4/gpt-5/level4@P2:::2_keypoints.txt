- Proposes a rate-based recurrent excitatory–inhibitory (E–I) network trained to embed multiple stable analog activity patterns while respecting biological constraints.  
- Addresses prior attractor-model shortcomings (violates Dale’s law, binary patterns, high-rate saturation) by directly optimizing synaptic weights and inhibitory steady-state activities.  
- Cost function penalizes drift at target states, includes Frobenius-norm weight regularization, and enforces stability via robust-control smoothed spectral abscissa (SSA).  
- Learned networks show realistic, sparse-like weight distributions without explicit sparsity penalties.  
- Networks operate in a dynamically balanced regime with excitation and inhibition closely matched per attractor.  
- Manuscript is clearly written, well organized, and makes modeling assumptions and training protocol easy to follow.  
- Strength: clear objective and rigorous optimization framework enforcing fixed-point and stability conditions.  
- Strength: respects neural constraints (separate E/I populations, graded low-rate responses) and yields balanced dynamics.  
- Strength: emergent realistic connectivity and demonstrated robustness of recall to noisy cues and ongoing noise.  
- Weakness: relies on global optimization with access to full network states and SSA gradients; limited discussion on how local plasticity could approximate this.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of basin sizes and capacity—results mainly shown for a single n=150, m=30 example.  
- Weakness: only rate-based model shown; transfer of learned connectivity to spiking networks is not demonstrated.  
- Weakness: some hyperparameter choices (SSA smoothing ε, regularization weights) appear heuristic and sensitivity analyses are lacking.
