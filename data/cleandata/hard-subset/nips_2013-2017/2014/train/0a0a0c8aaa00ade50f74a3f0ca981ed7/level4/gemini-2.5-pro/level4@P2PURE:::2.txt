This manuscript presents a rate-based neural network trained to store multiple stable activity patterns, or memories. The model aims to address key limitations of previous attractor networks, namely the violation of Dale's law and the saturation of neural activity in memory states. The network is trained using gradient descent on a cost function designed to enforce stability by minimizing activity changes within desired attractors, regularize the connection strengths by minimizing the Frobenius norm of the weight matrix, and explicitly maximize the stability of these attractors. The primary results show that the trained network develops a synaptic weight distribution consistent with experimental findings and achieves a balance between excitation and inhibition within the attractor states.

The manuscript is well-written, and the model, training procedure, and results are presented with clarity. The paper's main contribution lies in its integration of biological constraints, which after training, leads to emergent network properties such as graded, non-saturated attractors and a dynamic balance of excitation and inhibition. This approach, which combines biologically plausible constraints with an analysis of the resulting network features, makes the paper an interesting contribution to the understanding of attractor neural networks.
