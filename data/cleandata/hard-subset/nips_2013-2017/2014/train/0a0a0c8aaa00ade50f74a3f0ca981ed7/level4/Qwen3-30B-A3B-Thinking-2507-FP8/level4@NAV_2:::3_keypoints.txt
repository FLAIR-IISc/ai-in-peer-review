- Tackles building biologically plausible neural networks that store/retrieve analog memories while respecting Dale’s law, graded activity, low firing rates, and dynamic E/I balance.  
- Introduces a control-theoretic framework formulating memory storage as dual constraints (fixed-point and local stability).  
- Optimizes network parameters via a cost combining residual dynamics (‖dv/dt‖²), Smoothed Spectral Abscissa (SSA), and synaptic weight magnitude.  
- Enforces Dale’s law by reparameterization and treats inhibitory neurons as auxiliary optimized variables.  
- Shows balanced E/I dynamics emerge naturally without explicit saturation mechanisms.  
- Demonstrates robust retrieval from noisy cues, reduced trial-to-trial variability after stimulus onset, sparse connectivity, and noise resilience.  
- Generalizes to graded (log-normal) memory patterns, unlike prior binary-only models.  
- Strength: rigorous, theoretically grounded method (SSA) with thorough numerical validation achieving stable, non-saturating recall.  
- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and provides reproducible details and effective figures.  
- Strength: original synthesis of previously separate constraints and novel application of SSA to rate-based E–I networks.  
- Strength: significant contribution linking model behavior to empirical variability reduction and resolving physiological inconsistencies.  
- Weakness: no analysis of storage capacity scaling with network size or comparison to theoretical limits.  
- Weakness: choice of log-normal patterns needs stronger empirical justification tied to specific cortical firing statistics.  
- Weakness: cost yields approximate fixed points (‖dv/dt‖²>0); paper should quantify residuals and their effect on recall stability.  
- Weakness: misses discussion on whether Dale’s law provides computational benefits beyond biological plausibility.  
- Weakness: ambiguous wording about inhibitory dynamics (lines 254–256); needs clarification of transient vs. steady-state behavior.  
- Recommendation: accept after minor refinements; core methodology and results are compelling and align with conference goals.
