

This manuscript presents a rate-based neuronal network trained to maintain multiple stable activity patterns (memories) while addressing key limitations of prior attractor models: violation of Dale's law and saturation of activity in memory states. The authors employ a gradient descent optimization on a cost function that minimizes activity fluctuations within target attractor states, penalizes large weight matrix magnitudes via the Frobenius norm, and enhances attractor stability. The results demonstrate a synaptic weight distribution aligning with experimental observations and balanced excitation-inhibition dynamics within attractor states.  

The manuscript is well-written and clearly presents the model, training procedure, and results. The integration of biological constraints—specifically enforcing Dale's law and preventing activity saturation—into the network architecture yields a biologically plausible model that successfully generates non-saturated, stable activity patterns. This approach provides a significant contribution to understanding how attractor neural networks can simultaneously satisfy biological plausibility and exhibit desired computational properties. The model's ability to produce weight distributions matching empirical data and achieving balanced excitation-inhibition dynamics through these constraints is particularly compelling.
