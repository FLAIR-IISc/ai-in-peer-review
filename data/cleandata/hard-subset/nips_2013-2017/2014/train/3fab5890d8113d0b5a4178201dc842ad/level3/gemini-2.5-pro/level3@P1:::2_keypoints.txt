- Derives the EPPF for the BNBP via an ECPF-for-random-group-sizes then Bayes’ rule — a significant theoretical advance.
- The technique (ECPF → condition on observed sizes → EPPF) is elegant and potentially generalizable.
- Enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP models, with closed-form updates and a clear prediction rule.
- The algorithm appears straightforward to implement and immediately useful to practitioners.
- The paper is well-written, well-motivated, and clearly distinguishes from prior BNBP and HDP work.
- Major weakness: experiments are insufficient and mismatched — comparisons are against HDP-LDA, not other BNBP samplers.
- Because the comparison mixes different models and samplers, claims about faster convergence, better mixing, and efficiency are unproven.
- The authors should directly compare to existing BNBP inference methods (finite truncation, slice sampling) to validate sampler benefits.
- Quality: strong theoretical work but overall diminished by weak empirical validation.
- Clarity: high — mathematical developments and exposition are easy to follow.
- Originality: high — novel EPPF derivation and collapsed sampler for BNBP.
- Significance: potentially high if empirical advantages are demonstrated; currently undermined by inadequate experiments.
- Summary: elegant and important theoretical contribution, but acceptance requires direct experimental comparison to other BNBP inference schemes.
