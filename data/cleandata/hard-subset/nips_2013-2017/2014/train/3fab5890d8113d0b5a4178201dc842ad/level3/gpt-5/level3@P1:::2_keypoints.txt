- Proposes an EPPF and prediction rule for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership settings, enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the beta process, topic parameters, and group weights.
- Uses a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation to derive an ECPF and a group-size-dependent EPPF by conditioning on observed group sizes, yielding a truncation-free, collapsed inference rule.
- Instantiated as a topic model, the sampler shows fast convergence, good mixing, and often matches or outperforms HDP-LDA perplexity with more compact topic sets on three corpora.
- Extends classic EPPF and NRMI ideas to integer-valued, mixed-membership processes, filling a missing marginal-law analogue to the CRP for the BNBP.
- Prior BNBP work relied on truncation or slice/adaptive truncation; this paper provides the marginal law and collapsed sampler previously absent.
- Conceptual advance: gives a general recipe to obtain mixed-membership EPPFs from integer-valued processes by randomizing group sizes and conditioning.
- Novel derivations: Lemma 1 (beta–gamma–Poisson equivalence), closed-form ECPF/EPPF, and a simple prediction rule are technically elegant and useful.
- Characterizes a column-exchangeable random count matrix (Poisson number of columns, digamma column sizes, Dirichlet-multinomial row splits).
- Inference contribution: a truly fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with simple updates and no truncation or slice variables; multiplicative shrinkage differs from HDP’s additive mechanism.
- Empirical indications: faster mixing than HDP direct-assignment sampler and compact, competitive predictive performance.
- Main empirical weakness: experiments compare primarily to HDP-LDA and omit direct comparisons to existing BNBP samplers (truncated/blocked and slice/adaptive).
- Lacks diagnostics: missing time-to-target plots, ESS, autocorrelation, and wall-clock comparisons to substantiate claims of faster convergence and efficiency.
- Limited sensitivity analyses: little exploration of priors (rj, c, γ0), corpus size effects, or ablations on collapsing choices.
- Clarity issues: the nonstandard “digamma distribution” needs intuition, notation is dense, and the paper would benefit from main-text proof sketches and pseudo-code for reproducibility.
- Quality: mathematical development appears sound and well-motivated, but empirical methodology is weakened by absence of direct BNBP baseline comparisons.
- Clarity summary: generally well-written but heavy; recommend defining the digamma PMF, adding an algorithm box, and unifying notation.
- Originality: high — first mixed-membership EPPF for BNBP and the first fully collapsed BNBP Gibbs sampler.
- Significance: important for Bayesian nonparametrics and count-based mixed-membership models; practical impact depends on demonstrating clear gains over existing BNBP samplers.
- Suggested experiments: add comparisons to truncated/blocked and slice/adaptive BNBP samplers, report wall-clock to fixed perplexity, ESS/sec, active topics, and memory.
- Suggested analyses: include mixing diagnostics, sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters, and an ablation showing effects of collapsing B and Θ separately.
- Additional recommendations: compare to CRF-collapsed HDP samplers, include pseudo-code and proof sketches, and discuss implications of sample-size-dependent EPPFs.
- Arguments for acceptance: new general EPPF construction, first fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP, and empirical evidence of faster convergence and compact representations.
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical evaluation doesn’t validate superiority over prior BNBP samplers, lacks quantitative efficiency diagnostics and sensitivity studies, and has presentation gaps.
- Overall judgment: strong theoretical and algorithmic contribution whose empirical section needs better alignment with claims; borderline but leaning positive pending suggested experiments and diagnostics.
