- Summary: Proposes a group-size–dependent EPPF/ECPF and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP mixed-membership models, claiming faster convergence and competitive perplexity with fewer topics.  
- Novelty: Lemma 1 and derivation of group-size–dependent EPPF/ECPF and prediction rule are technically novel and useful.  
- Method: Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler marginalizes beta process, group gammas, topic distributions, and weights with simple update rules.  
- Conceptual contribution: General method to derive mixed-membership EPPFs via integer-valued processes, potentially reusable beyond BNBP.  
- Technical soundness: Augmentation and lemmas are coherent and carefully handle group-size dependence.  
- Empirical weakness: Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to other BNBP samplers (truncated, slice/adaptive, block samplers), so claimed advantages are not convincingly isolated.  
- Baseline weakness: HDP-LDA baseline uses a weaker direct-assignment sampler instead of stronger collapsed CRF samplers, biasing comparisons.  
- Diagnostics: Limited MCMC metrics shown (only trace plots and perplexities); lacks autocorrelation, ESS, wall-clock-to-target, and ablation studies.  
- Clarity/organization: Dense EPPF normalization and notation could be streamlined; add brief definition of “digamma distribution” and a pseudocode algorithm for reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: Add head-to-head comparisons with other BNBP samplers, include HDP collapsed baseline, run ablations (partial vs full collapse), synthetic validation, and report complexity/memory.  
- Pros: Theoretical novelty, fully collapsed simple updates, and likely better mixing than partially collapsed alternatives.  
- Cons: Missing direct comparisons to existing BNBP inference methods, weak HDP baseline, and limited diagnostic/ablation evidence.  
- Significance: Potentially significant if empirically validated against other BNBP samplers; currently suggestive but not definitive.  
- Overall assessment: Technically strong and original but empirically under-supported; borderline accept with recommendation to strengthen experimental validation.
