- Proposes a novel, principled framework for constructing structured-variable priors using maximum entropy and information projection onto constraint sets.  
- Focuses on sparse structures and shows approximate inference via convex subsets is equivalent to maximizing a submodular function under cardinality constraints.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly explains the proposed approach.  
- Experiments on simulated and high-dimensional neuroimaging data show superior support recovery and predictive accuracy versus other sparsity-inducing models.  
- Strength: novel and principled methodology for structured priors.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation of technical material.  
- Strength: effective empirical validation demonstrating method superiority.  
- Weakness: limited comparison with other related work — needs more detailed literature comparison.  
- Weakness: experimental section lacks detailed dataset descriptions and experimental setup.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with Bayesian modeling and information theory, reducing accessibility for non-experts.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, clarity, and strong experimental results support publication.  
- Rejection concerns: need for more comparisons, clearer experiments, and additional background for broader accessibility.  
- Rating: 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons, experimental detail, and accessibility.
