- Novel contribution: Derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).
- Algorithmic contribution: Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the BNBP that is simple to implement and mixes well.
- Application: Applies the BNBP and CG sampler to topic modeling, demonstrating practical potential.
- Clarity and motivation: Main ideas are well-motivated, clearly related to prior work, and the paper is well-written.
- Technical soundness: The EPPF derivation and CG sampler technical development are sound and well-explained.
- Strength: Novel EPPF derivation is an important advance for nonparametric Bayesian mixture modeling.
- Strength: Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler offers implementation simplicity and good mixing properties.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is insufficient, limited to comparison with HDP-LDA only.
- Weakness: Lacks thorough analysis of the BNBP's statistical properties (e.g., convergence, asymptotics).
- Weakness: Limitations of the proposed method are not thoroughly discussed.
- Suggested improvement: Provide a more comprehensive experimental evaluation including other state-of-the-art baselines.
- Suggested improvement: Analyze statistical properties (convergence rate, asymptotic behavior) and discuss limitations and future work.
