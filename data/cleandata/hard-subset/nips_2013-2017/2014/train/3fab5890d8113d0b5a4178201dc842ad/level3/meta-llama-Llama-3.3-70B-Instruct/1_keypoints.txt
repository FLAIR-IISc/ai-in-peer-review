- Introduces a novel maximum-entropy framework for constructing priors over structured variables.  
- Proposes a method to design priors that incorporate domain constraints, focused on sparse structured variables.  
- Develops a new approximate inference approach for structured subdomains and applies it to sparse support estimation.  
- Provides theoretical analysis supporting the technical contributions.  
- Presents thorough experimental validation and comparison with existing methods.  
- Compares against Bayesian hierarchical and non-probabilistic sparse predictive models.  
- Unifies several existing priors (e.g., spike-and-slab, horseshoe) as special cases within a more principled framework.  
- Shows empirical improvements in support recovery and predictive accuracy over Lasso and ARD.  
- Demonstrates scalability and biologically plausible results on high-dimensional fMRI data.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Limitation: inference algorithm complexity may hinder applicability to very large datasets.  
- Limitation: method performs best when the base density is well-understood, which may not always hold.  
- Future work needed for more efficient, robust inference algorithms and broader problem applications.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with potential for wide impact.
