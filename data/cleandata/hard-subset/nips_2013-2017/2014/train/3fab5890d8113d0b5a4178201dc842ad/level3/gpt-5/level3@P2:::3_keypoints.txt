- Develops an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership settings via a group-size–dependent ECPF and Bayes’ rule, plus a prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling without truncation or slice sampling.
- Instantiates a BNBP topic model and compares it to HDP-LDA, showing a multiplicative shrinkage mechanism that favors more compact topic representations and fast mixing.
- Extends EPPF/CRP-style marginal characterizations from Dirichlet processes to mixed-membership count models, filling a gap where marginal partition laws were previously hard to derive.
- Technical derivations are careful and appear correct; the augmented beta–gamma–Poisson → ECPF → EPPF path and prediction rule are well motivated, with proofs in the appendix.
- Empirically, the fully collapsed BNBP sampler mixes quickly and attains comparable or slightly better perplexity than HDP-LDA while using fewer topics.
- Empirical improvements in predictive performance versus HDP-LDA are modest; main practical advantages are compactness and convergence speed.
- Evaluation is incomplete: missing direct comparisons to slice-sampling–based BNBP inference and truncated/block Gibbs BNBP samplers to quantify mixing and wall-clock tradeoffs.
- Manuscript is well written and organized; roles of ECPF, EPPF, and prediction rule are clear, though notation is heavy.
- The “digamma distribution” section needs more intuition and a reference; adding sampler pseudo-code or a schematic would improve accessibility.
- Novelty: first derivation of an EPPF for BNBP in the mixed-membership case and a general recipe (randomize group sizes → ECPF → condition → EPPF) likely extensible to other integer-valued processes.
- Methodological contribution: a fully collapsed sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process, distinct from existing HDP samplers.
- Significance: useful for practitioners wanting truncation-free, fast-mixing inference in count-based mixed-membership models; compactness and speed have practical impact.
- Pros: clear/principled EPPF and prediction rule derivation; fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with good mixing; insightful contrast with HDP-LDA; solid experiments and available code.
- Cons: limited predictive gains over HDP-LDA; baselines limited to HDP-LDA; insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (c, γ0) and posterior behavior of dispersion rj; implications of size-dependent EPPF (e.g., projectivity) under-discussed.
- Suggested improvements: add comparisons to slice and truncated BNBP samplers with convergence and wall-clock metrics; discuss partially/uncollapsed variants for parallelization and mixing vs throughput tradeoffs.
- Further suggestions: provide sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, and rj with visualizations; report topic coherence and sparsity metrics; clarify digamma terminology and include collapsed-sampler pseudo-code.
- Overall recommendation: strong, original, and technically sound paper; recommend acceptance after addressing minor experimental clarifications and additional comparisons.
