This paper introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive the EPPF and use it to develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model. The paper is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.

The primary contribution of the paper is the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, which is interesting and broadly useful. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the properties of the BNBP and its application to topic modeling. The BNBP topic model is shown to have distinct characteristics compared to the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) and latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) models.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the BNBP topic model in achieving state-of-the-art predictive performance with a compact representation of the corpus. The authors also provide a comparison with the HDP-LDA model, showing that the BNBP topic model has a multiplicative control mechanism to shrink the number of topics, whereas the HDP has a softer additive shrinkage mechanism.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model, which is efficient and effective.
* The thorough analysis of the properties of the BNBP and its application to topic modeling.
* The experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the BNBP topic model.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in nonparametric Bayesian methods and topic modeling, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The experimental results are limited to a few datasets and may not be representative of all possible applications.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art topic models.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian methods and topic modeling.
* The derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling is a novel and important result.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the BNBP topic model in achieving state-of-the-art predictive performance.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may be too specialized for a general audience.
* The experimental results are limited and may not be representative of all possible applications.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art topic models.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, but with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
