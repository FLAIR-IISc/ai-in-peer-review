- Derives the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling mixed-membership modeling and filling a notable literature gap.
- The EPPF derivation is mathematically rigorous and novel, extending techniques to integer-valued stochastic processes.
- Provides a unified framework for understanding BNBP clustering behavior and enables fully collapsed inference.
- Proposes a collapsed Gibbs sampler that is simple to implement, converges quickly, mixes well, and avoids truncation.
- The BNBP-based topic model yields strong predictive performance and a compact representation of the corpus.
- Comparison with HDP-LDA highlights distinct behavior: more compactness and faster convergence, though not consistently better perplexity.
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations and detailed proofs in the appendix, aiding reproducibility.
- Experiments are limited in scope, focusing mainly on HDP-LDA and lacking comparisons with other inference methods (e.g., sliced/truncated samplers).
- The paper does not analyze parallelization and scalability trade-offs between collapsed and uncollapsed representations for large datasets.
- Perplexity improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal under standard settings; clearer use-case scenarios for BNBP would strengthen the paper.
- Recommendation: Accept — technical rigor, originality, and practical utility outweigh the experimental limitations.
- Suggested future work: expand experimental comparisons to alternative inference methods and discuss parallelization/scalability implications.
