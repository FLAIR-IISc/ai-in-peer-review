- Derives a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP, a significant and non-trivial theoretical contribution.  
- Uses that EPPF to develop the first fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP mixed-membership models, analytically marginalizing the infinite beta process.  
- Presents a simple-to-implement sampler that highlights a multiplicative global/local cluster popularity mechanism (contrast to HDP’s additive mechanism).  
- Clear, well-written exposition connecting beta process, negative binomial process, and augmented beta-gamma-Poisson representation.  
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is insufficient to support the paper’s central claims about the sampler.  
- Experiments compare BNBP topic model to HDP-LDA, confounding model differences with sampler performance.  
- Missing critical baselines: no direct comparison to other BNBP inference methods (truncated/block Gibbs, slice sampler).  
- Authors’ claims that the collapsed sampler outperforms blocked Gibbs in convergence, complexity, and memory are unsubstantiated.  
- Trace plots only compare mixing to an HDP sampler, not to other BNBP samplers.  
- Recommendation: run direct empirical comparisons of the collapsed sampler against other BNBP samplers on the same model and datasets.  
- Suggested evaluation metrics: convergence speed (held-out log-likelihood/perplexity vs time/iterations), mixing (autocorrelation plots), and final predictive performance.  
- Keep comparison to HDP-LDA as a secondary analysis to investigate modeling differences after validating sampler efficiency.  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution but inadequate empirical validation; paper would be much stronger with revised experiments directly testing sampler claims.
