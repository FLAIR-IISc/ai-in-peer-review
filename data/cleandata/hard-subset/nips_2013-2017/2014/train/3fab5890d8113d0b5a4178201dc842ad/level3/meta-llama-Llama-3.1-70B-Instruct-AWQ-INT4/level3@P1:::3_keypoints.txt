- Derivation of the BNBP's EPPF enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership/count-mixture modeling.  
- Manuscript presents the derivation and methods clearly and with technical rigor.  
- Proposes a novel use of the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling.  
- Develops a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that is efficient and scalable for the BNBP topic model.  
- Empirical comparison to HDP-LDA shows distinct behavior but only marginal performance improvements.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset and needs broader validation.  
- No comparison against other samplers (e.g., sliced or truncated samplers), limiting benchmarking.  
- Does not explore uncollapsed model representations that could enable parallelization.  
- The EPPF derivation for the BNBP is an original and significant theoretical contribution.  
- The approach has potential to impact nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership modeling.  
- The paper is well-organized, readable, and technically sound.  
- Overall quality is high despite empirical and benchmarking limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication based on novelty and technical contribution.  
- Limitations (marginal gains, limited experiments, missing comparisons) noted but not fatal.
