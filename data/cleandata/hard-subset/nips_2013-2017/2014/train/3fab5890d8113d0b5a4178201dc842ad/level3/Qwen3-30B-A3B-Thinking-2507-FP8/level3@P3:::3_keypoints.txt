- Main contribution: Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling fully collapsed inference for mixed-membership models.  
- Theoretical method: Develops an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for group-size dependent models and uses Bayes' rule to derive the BNBP's EPPF for a column-exchangeable random count matrix.  
- Prediction rule: Derives a simple prediction rule to simulate exchangeable random partitions under the BNBP EPPF.  
- Practical application: Proposes a novel BNBP topic model and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process.  
- Implementation benefits: Collapsed model yields simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance.  
- Theoretical quality: Paper is technically sound, well-written, and connects clearly to prior EPPF and mixed-membership work.  
- Literature gap: Correctly identifies and fills the lack of marginal distributions for integer-valued stochastic processes in mixed-membership modeling.  
- Generality: Contribution is broadly useful and potentially generalizable to other integer-valued stochastic processes.  
- Comparison to HDP-LDA: Provides an alternative with different shrinkage behavior (multiplicative vs. additive) and typically uses fewer topics.  
- Empirical results: Performance gains over HDP-LDA are marginal; sometimes higher perplexity at small Dirichlet smoothing (η).  
- Missing experiments: Should compare the collapsed sampler to sliced and truncated samplers for BNBP inference and evaluate uncollapsed representations for parallelization and mixing tradeoffs.  
- Main strength: Significant theoretical advance (first EPPF for BNBP) plus a clear practical demonstration via the topic model.  
- Main weakness: Empirical advantages are limited and the paper does not sufficiently clarify when BNBP is preferable beyond compact representation.  
- Recommendation: Meets NIPS publication threshold—minor experimental suggestions only; overall a valuable, accept-worthy contribution.
