- Main contribution: Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).
- Main contribution: Develops a prediction rule for the BNBP enabling simulation of exchangeable random partitions for grouped data.
- Main contribution: Constructs a BNBP-based nonparametric topic model for document corpora.
- Strength: Provides significant theoretical advances by linking BNBP and EPPF analysis.
- Strength: Proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model distinct from existing approaches.
- Strength: Demonstrates empirical performance on three corpora, achieving strong predictive results with compact representations.
- Weakness: Inference algorithm is more complex than the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP) in theory and implementation.
- Weakness: Paper lacks an intuitive explanation for why BNBP should outperform the HDP in practice.
- Weakness: Empirical improvements over the HDP appear insufficient to justify the added complexity.
- Overall leaning: Reviewer leans toward acceptance but requests clarification on practical performance gains and intuition.
- Pro-acceptance argument: Significant theoretical and methodological contributions (EPPF derivation, prediction rule, new model).
- Pro-acceptance argument: Empirical results show state-of-the-art predictive performance with compact topic representations.
- Con-acceptance argument: Increased complexity may hinder accessibility and adoption.
- Con-acceptance argument: Lack of intuitive rationale and marginal empirical gains weaken the case for replacing HDP.
