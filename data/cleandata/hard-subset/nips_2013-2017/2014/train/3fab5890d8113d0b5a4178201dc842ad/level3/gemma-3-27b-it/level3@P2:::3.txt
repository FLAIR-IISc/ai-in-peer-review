This paper presents a significant contribution to nonparametric Bayesian modeling by deriving the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors leverage this EPPF to develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model, offering a novel approach to topic modeling and addressing a gap in the literature regarding tractable inference for BNBP-based models.

The manuscript is well-written and technically sound. The authors clearly articulate the motivation, background, and contributions of their work. The derivation of the EPPF is presented with sufficient detail, and the subsequent development of the Gibbs sampler appears logically sound. The paper effectively connects the BNBP to existing work in nonparametric Bayesian statistics, particularly the Dirichlet process, hierarchical Dirichlet process, and related exchangeable partition models. The comparison to existing methods, like LDA and HDP-LDA, is thorough and helps contextualize the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

The primary contribution – deriving the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership/count-mixture modeling – is genuinely interesting and broadly useful. While previous work has utilized the BNBP, inference often relied on truncation methods or slice sampling, which can be computationally expensive or require careful tuning. This work provides a more efficient and theoretically grounded approach through the development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler. The derived prediction rule is also a valuable addition, facilitating simulation from the BNBP model.

The application of the BNBP to topic modeling serves as a concrete illustration of the EPPF and prediction rule. The empirical results on the JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS corpora are well-presented, and the comparison to HDP-LDA is insightful. However, the performance gains over HDP-LDA appear to be marginal, and the authors rightly acknowledge that neither model consistently outperforms the other. The key takeaway seems to be that the BNBP and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics, particularly in terms of model complexity and the trade-off between compactness of representation and predictive performance.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** Derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF is a significant theoretical advancement.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler offers a computationally efficient inference method.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The authors effectively situate their work within the broader context of nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation is thorough and provides valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Marginal Performance Gains:** The empirical results do not demonstrate a substantial performance advantage over HDP-LDA.
* **Limited Discussion of Computational Tradeoffs:** While the collapsed Gibbs sampler is efficient, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of its computational complexity and potential limitations, especially concerning scalability.
* **Missing Comparisons:** The reviewer notes a pertinent point - the paper doesn’t compare the collapsed sampler to other BNBP inference methods, specifically the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8]. This would help establish the relative advantages of the proposed approach.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature regarding tractable inference for BNBP-based models.
* The derivation of the EPPF is a valuable theoretical contribution.
* The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler provides a computationally efficient inference method.
* The paper is well-written, technically sound, and thoroughly evaluated.
* The work meets the quality standards expected of a NIPS publication.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical results do not demonstrate a dramatic performance improvement over existing methods.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of computational tradeoffs and scalability.




**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend accepting this paper. While the empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are not overwhelming, the theoretical contribution of deriving the BNBP’s EPPF and the development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler are significant. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and addresses an important problem in nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The reviewer’s suggestion to compare with sliced and truncated samplers is a valid one, but does not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. Additionally, further exploration of uncollapsed representations for potential parallelization advantages, as suggested, represents a valuable direction for future research, but is not essential for acceptance. The paper’s contribution is substantial enough to warrant publication in NIPS.
