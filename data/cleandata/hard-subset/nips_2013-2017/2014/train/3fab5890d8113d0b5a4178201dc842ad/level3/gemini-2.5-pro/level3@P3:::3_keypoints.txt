- Derives the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models, enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.  
- Novel and significant: fills a gap versus previous approximations (truncation, slice sampling) and is broadly useful beyond topic models.  
- Manuscript is well-written and technically sound; derivation proceeds cleanly from beta-gamma-Poisson to ECPF to EPPF with an intuitive prediction rule (Eq. 10).  
- Introduces a BNBP topic model as a proof-of-concept and highlights the BNBP’s distinct multiplicative shrinkage compared to LDA/HDP (Eq. 12).  
- Empirical gains are marginal: predictive perplexity similar to a well-tuned HDP-LDA, though BNBP yields more compact representations and faster convergence.  
- Major experimental omission: no direct comparisons to other inference methods for the same BNBP (truncated or slice samplers) to validate the collapsed sampler’s practical advantages.  
- Scalability/parallelization concerns: paper should better discuss or test uncollapsed/blocked samplers and trade-offs between mixing and parallelizability for large datasets.  
- Overall assessment: a strong, novel theoretical contribution that is well-executed and publishable, but experiments should be strengthened to demonstrate practical benefits.
