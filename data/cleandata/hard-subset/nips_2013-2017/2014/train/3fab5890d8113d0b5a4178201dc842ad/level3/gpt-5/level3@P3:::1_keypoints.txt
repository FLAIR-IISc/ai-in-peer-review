- Main contribution: Derives a group-size–dependent EPPF for the BNBP via a randomize-then-condition ECPF→EPPF recipe and gives a corresponding prediction rule.
- Technical soundness: The derivation and augmentations are elegant and consistent with prior BNBP literature; induced count-matrix prior and sampler updates appear correct.
- Topic-model implementation: Presents a fully collapsed, truncation-free Gibbs sampler marginalizing the beta and gamma processes and topics.
- Empirics: Shows faster convergence and better local mixing than HDP direct-assignment on three corpora, but perplexity improvements are modest and dataset/η-dependent.
- Practical evaluation gap: Lacks stronger, systematic evidence (held-out likelihood vs topics/time, sensitivity to c, γ0, priors on rj, and ablation studies).
- Clarity: Well organized but technically dense; readers would benefit from a toy example, intuition on multiplicative vs additive shrinkage, and hyperparameter guidance.
- Originality: High — first derivation of a mixed-membership, group-size–dependent EPPF for an integer-valued process and a novel fully collapsed BNBP sampler.
- Significance: Substantial theoretical advance with potential to generalize to other count-valued processes; practical impact less decisive until stronger empirical gains are shown.
- Strengths: Principled ECPF→EPPF construction, first prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership, collapsed sampler with good mixing, and conceptual unification of EPPF and BNP mixed-membership models.
- Weaknesses: Higher theoretical/implementation complexity than HDP samplers, modest empirical gains, limited intuition on when BNBP is preferable, and limited sensitivity analyses.
- Questions for authors: Provide analytical/empirical regimes favoring multiplicative shrinkage, practical hyperparameter guidance, held-out perplexity vs runtime/topic curves, discuss exchangeability implications of conditioning on m, and reconcile “simple implementation” claim with code availability.
- Pros for acceptance: Novel theoretical contribution, useful general recipe, and a well-mixing collapsed sampler likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Cons for acceptance: Empirical advantages over HDP not consistently compelling and added complexity may limit practical adoption.
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance for theoretical/methodological contributions, contingent on stronger empirical validation and clearer practitioner guidance.
