- Derives the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership models.  
- Presents a BNBP topic model as an illustration of the derived EPPF and collapsed sampler.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, logically structured, and the EPPF derivation appears correct.  
- The EPPF derivation is novel, significant, and provides important theoretical understanding and practical tools for count/mixture modeling.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: performance gains over a well-tuned HDP-LDA are marginal and claims of “distinct characteristics” lack strong empirical support.  
- Missing comparisons of the proposed collapsed sampler to other inference schemes for the same BNBP (e.g., slice sampler [10], truncated sampler [7]) to evaluate mixing, convergence, and efficiency.  
- Lacks discussion/experimentation on scalability and trade-offs between collapsed mixing and uncollapsed parallelizable representations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution despite need for stronger empirical/sampler comparisons.
