- Derives an EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership/count-mixture settings via an ECPF for random group sizes, marginalization, and Bayes’ rule.  
- Presents a simple prediction rule enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the infinite beta process, topic weights, and topics.  
- Extends EPPF-based marginal samplers to mixed-membership models, complementing HDP’s CRF by providing the first explicit BNBP EPPF/prediction rule.  
- Clarifies connections to gamma–Poisson negative-binomial constructions and NB-IBP style models, and notes BNBP is outside σ-stable Poisson–Kingman class.  
- Shows a multiplicative shrinkage mechanism for topic popularity, contrasting with HDP-LDA’s additive mechanism.  
- Technical derivations (ECPF, EPPF, prediction rule) are clear, well-motivated, and consistent with the augmented beta–gamma–Poisson view.  
- Main contribution is novel and broadly useful: a truncation-free, EPPF-based route to collapsed inference for BNBP mixed-membership models.  
- The collapsed Gibbs sampler is simple to implement, mixes quickly in practice, and provides instructive contrasts to HDP-LDA.  
- Empirical study uses three corpora, perplexity evaluation, convergence diagnostics, and sensitivity analysis, showing comparable predictive performance and more compact representations.  
- Empirical gains over HDP-LDA are marginal; paper should better characterize when BNBP’s multiplicative shrinkage is advantageous (e.g., document length dispersion, burstiness).  
- Missing baseline comparisons to other BNBP inference methods (sliced sampler, truncated/block sampler); recommend reporting ESS/autocorrelation, wall-clock to target perplexity, memory, final perplexity.  
- Raises scalability concerns: evaluate uncollapsed/partially collapsed representations that admit data-parallel updates and discuss mixing vs parallelization tradeoffs.  
- Requests additional diagnostics: effective sample sizes for topic counts/number of topics, acceptance rates for new-topic proposals, and sensitivity to c, γ0, and inferred rj.  
- Suggests broader evaluation beyond perplexity: topic coherence, intruder tasks, qualitative topics, robustness to splits and smaller η, and runtime per iteration vs KJ.  
- Clarity nits: restate implications of EPPF’s sample-size dependence (lack of addition rule), tighten discussion of connections to NB-IBP/“ice-cream” processes and feature-allocation vs group-partition differences.  
- Paper is technically sound with coherent derivations; claims about mixing/speed are supported by traces but would benefit from added BNBP baselines and rigorous MCMC diagnostics.  
- Writing and organization are strong; ECPF→EPPF flow and sampler presentation are clear, with minor suggested improvements.  
- Originality: high — the group-size dependent EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership modeling is new and transferable to other integer-valued processes.  
- Significance: moderate-to-high — offers a useful collapsed sampler that removes truncation/slicing overhead, though clearer guidance on when to prefer BNBP would increase impact.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to priors on rj, c, γ0 and multimodality; wall-clock comparison vs HDP-LDA CRF-style collapsed sampler; whether the prediction rule admits sparse sampling tricks.  
- Pros: novel EPPF/prediction rule theory, practical collapsed sampler with good mixing, clear exposition, and available code.  
- Cons: limited BNBP baseline comparisons, marginal empirical advantages vs HDP-LDA, and no parallelization study.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on adding sliced/truncated comparisons and more thorough MCMC diagnostics.
