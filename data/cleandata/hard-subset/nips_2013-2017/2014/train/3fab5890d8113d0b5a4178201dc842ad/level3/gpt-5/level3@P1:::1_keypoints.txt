- Main contribution: derive an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) via an ECPF construction that randomizes group sizes, marginalizes the beta process, then conditions on observed sizes.  
- Methodological payoff: use the EPPF to build a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic models that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process.  
- Key modeling insight: sampler induces multiplicative shrinkage depending on global cluster popularity and group-specific dispersion rj, contrasting with HDP’s additive structure.  
- Empirical claim: faster convergence and better mixing than HDP direct-assignment sampler, with comparable or lower perplexity using fewer topics on three corpora.  
- Strength: clear theoretical advance—first usable EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership models.  
- Strength: derivation clarifies BNBP prior over column-exchangeable random count matrices and yields closed-form collapsed updates.  
- Strength: practical sampler is simple to implement (no CRF bookkeeping) and yields compact topic representations.  
- Strength: useful conceptual contrast between multiplicative (BNBP) and additive (HDP) shrinkage and the role of rj in overdispersion.  
- Weakness: heavy and dense theoretical development; some conditioning steps and the “digamma” count distribution are hard for non-specialists to follow.  
- Weakness: empirical gains over strong HDP baselines are modest and not consistently large across metrics/datasets.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to a collapsed CRF HDP sampler and competitive Poisson factorization baselines.  
- Weakness: limited intuition and explanation for when multiplicative shrinkage outperforms HDP and for the learning dynamics of rj.  
- Weakness: potential modeling–inference mismatch risk from randomizing group sizes; needs clearer discussion of conditioning implications.  
- Quality: derivations appear technically sound and experiments are competently executed, supporting mixing and compactness claims but only partially supporting predictive superiority.  
- Clarity: generally well written but notation-heavy and dense; would benefit from more intuition, a high-level roadmap, pseudocode, and a generative-structure figure.  
- Originality: high—novel ECPF→EPPF methodology and first EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership with usable prediction rule and collapsed sampler.  
- Significance: high for the Bayesian nonparametrics community; enables truncation-free inference in mixed-membership count models, though practical impact depends on dissemination and guidance.  
- Key questions for authors: provide intuition/variance–mean analysis for the multiplicative term, sensitivity to priors (rj, c, γ0), comparisons to collapsed CRF HDP and gamma–Poisson factorization, topic coherence/human interpretability metrics, and extensibility to GNBP/gamma–Poisson.  
- Suggested improvements: add intuition and a derivation sketch for the digamma count distribution, ablation/sensitivity studies for rj and hyperparameters, comparisons to stronger baselines, wall-clock/per-iteration cost reporting, pseudocode, and code release.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong novel theory with clear methodological payoff, good empirical behavior, and broad potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy theory limiting accessibility, modest practical gains vs strongest baselines, missing key comparisons and qualitative analysis.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept.
