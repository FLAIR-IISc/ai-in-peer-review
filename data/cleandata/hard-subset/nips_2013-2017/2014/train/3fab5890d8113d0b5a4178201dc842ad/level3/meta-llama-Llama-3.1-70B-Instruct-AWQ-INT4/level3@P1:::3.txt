**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper introduces a novel approach to mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, which enables the development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model. The manuscript provides a clear and technically sound presentation of the primary contribution, which is the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership/count-mixture modeling. The BNBP topic model is reasonably illustrated, and its performance is compared to the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP)-LDA, demonstrating distinct characteristics but not clear superiority.

**Strengths**

1. **Derivation of the BNBP's EPPF**: The primary contribution of the paper is the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP, which is a significant achievement in the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
2. **Clear and technically sound presentation**: The manuscript is well-written, and the technical details are presented in a clear and concise manner.
3. **Novel approach to mixed-membership modeling**: The use of the BNBP for mixed-membership modeling is a new and interesting approach that has the potential to provide new insights in this area.
4. **Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler**: The development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model is a significant contribution, as it provides a efficient and scalable algorithm for inference.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Marginal performance gains**: The performance gains of the BNBP topic model over the HDP-LDA are only marginal, which may limit the practical impact of the paper.

3. **Comparison to other samplers**: The paper does not compare the collapsed sampler to other samplers, such as the sliced sampler [10] or the truncated sampler [8], which would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
