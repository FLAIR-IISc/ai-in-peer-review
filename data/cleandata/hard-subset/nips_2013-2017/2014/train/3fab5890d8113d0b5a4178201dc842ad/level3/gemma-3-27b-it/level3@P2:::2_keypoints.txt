- Proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) via a derived EPPF.  
- Derives the BNBP exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) and a prediction rule (Lemmas 1, 2, 4), a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Demonstrates the sampler within a topic-modeling framework, showing potential for efficient nonparametric Bayesian inference.  
- Well-motivated and grounded in prior work on Dirichlet processes, NRMIs, and the Chinese Restaurant Process.  
- Clear distinction from existing methods (e.g., HDP-LDA) and proper positioning of the BNBP sampler as complementary for mixed-membership models.  
- Strong focus on algorithm development: simple implementation, reportedly fast convergence and good mixing.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and the paper is generally well-organized.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: compares to HDP-LDA (a different model) rather than other BNBP samplers, obscuring sampler-specific benefits.  
- Lacks direct comparisons with existing BNBP inference methods (finite truncation, slice sampling) on speed, mixing, and memory.  
- Empirical claims about sampler advantages (speed, mixing, memory) are not adequately demonstrated.  
- Some sections need more intuitive explanations, particularly linking the EPPF to the sampling process; notation and equation density can overwhelm.  
- Originality is strong in inference methodology, though not a new statistical model.  
- Significance is moderate: potential for scalable BNBP use, but limited by insufficient empirical validation and scant parallelization exploration.  
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision and resubmission after comprehensive empirical comparisons to other BNBP samplers.
