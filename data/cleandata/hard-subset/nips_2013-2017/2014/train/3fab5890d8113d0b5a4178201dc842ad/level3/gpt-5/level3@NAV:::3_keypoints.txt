- Primary contribution: Derives an EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership/count-mixture settings and provides a simple prediction rule and fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- Derivation approach: Introduces a group-size–dependent ECPF, marginalizes the beta process, and applies Bayes’ rule to obtain the EPPF.
- Prediction rule: Intuitive form—global popularity times group-specific affinity.
- Implementation: Instantiates results in a topic model and builds an easy-to-implement, fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.
- Gap filled: Addresses prior reliance on truncation/slice samplers for BNBP by enabling truncation-free, collapsed inference.
- Technical quality: Sound and careful derivation; sampler is correct and converges/mixes well.
- Empirical findings: Clear convergence/mixing improvements over HDP-LDA, but only marginal predictive (perplexity) gains; BNBP and HDP-LDA exhibit multiplicative vs. additive shrinkage respectively.
- Clarity: Well organized and readable; good connections to EPPF literature and count-factorization views.
- Minor clarity suggestions: Emphasize earlier that the EPPF is sample-size dependent and give intuition for the digamma prior on cluster sizes.
- Originality: High—first mixed-membership EPPF for BNBP with prediction rule and collapsed sampler.
- Significance: Enables truncation-free, collapsed inference for count-mixture models and is likely to be reused despite modest perplexity improvements.
- Suggested experiments: Compare against sliced and truncated/block BNBP samplers with wall-clock, perplexity, and effective sample size metrics.
- Suggested extensions: Explore uncollapsed/partially collapsed representations for parallelization and discuss mixing trade-offs versus the collapsed sampler.
- Additional analyses requested: Provide sensitivity analyses for rj and η, report topic coherence as well as perplexity, and discuss scalability with sparse updates.
- Pros: Novel EPPF and prediction rule; clean fully collapsed sampler with fast mixing; clear exposition and solid theory.
- Cons: Empirical advantages over HDP-LDA are mostly marginal; missing direct comparisons to sliced/truncated BNBP inference and parallelizable alternatives.
- Overall recommendation: Interesting, technically solid work; recommend acceptance with minor experimental clarifications.
