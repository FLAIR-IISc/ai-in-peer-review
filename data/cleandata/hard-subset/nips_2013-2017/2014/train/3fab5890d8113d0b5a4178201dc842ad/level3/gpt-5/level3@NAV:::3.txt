This paper derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in the mixed-membership/count-mixture setting and uses it to build a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model. The derivation proceeds by introducing a group-size–dependent exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF), marginalizing the beta process, and then applying Bayes’ rule to obtain the EPPF and a simple prediction rule. The topic model instantiates these results and is contrasted empirically with HDP-LDA. Relative to prior work on DP/NRMI EPPFs, the CRP/CRF, and integer-valued processes (e.g., gamma–Poisson, BNBP, GNBP), this fills a notable gap: previous BNBP inference relied on truncation or slice samplers, without a mixed-membership EPPF enabling fully collapsed inference.

Quality: The paper is technically sound. The ECPF→EPPF derivation is careful, and the prediction rule is intuitive (global popularity times group-specific affinity). The sampler is correct and easy to implement. Empirically, convergence/mixing improvements over HDP-LDA are clear, though predictive gains are generally marginal; the main qualitative conclusion is that BNBP and HDP-LDA shrink in distinct ways (multiplicative vs. additive).

Clarity: The manuscript is well organized and readable. The connection to related EPPF literature and to count-factorization views is helpful. Minor suggestions: emphasize earlier that the EPPF is sample-size dependent; provide a brief intuition for the digamma prior on cluster sizes.

Originality: High. The primary contribution—an EPPF for BNBP in mixed-membership models with an accompanying prediction rule and collapsed sampler—is new and broadly useful.

Significance: The result enables truncation-free, collapsed inference for a class of count-mixture models used in topics and beyond. While perplexity gains over HDP-LDA are modest, the methodological advance is valuable and likely to be reused.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Compare the collapsed sampler against a sliced sampler [10] and a truncated/block sampler [8] for BNBP, reporting wall-clock vs. perplexity and effective sample size.
- Explore uncollapsed/partially collapsed representations for parallelization and discuss mixing trade-offs versus the collapsed sampler.
- Provide sensitivity analyses for rj and η; include topic coherence in addition to perplexity; discuss scalability with sparse updates.

Pros:
- Novel EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership modeling.
- Clean fully collapsed sampler; fast mixing.
- Clear exposition; solid theory.

Cons:
- Empirical advantages over HDP-LDA are mostly marginal.
- Missing comparisons to sliced/truncated BNBP inference and parallelizable variants.

Overall: Interesting, technically solid contribution that meets the publication bar; I recommend acceptance with minor experimental clarifications.
