- Proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) and derives a group-size dependent EPPF for mixed-membership modeling.
- Emphasizes sampler development rather than new statistical modeling techniques.
- Claims practical advantages: simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance.
- Main contributions: derivation of the BNBP EPPF and development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- Provides comparisons with Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP) and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA).
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically sound.
- Technical details are well-explained and results are reproducible for expert readers.
- Experimental results are clearly presented and illustrate the proposed model’s performance.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is insufficiently comprehensive.
- Lacks comparisons to other existing BNBP sampling algorithms.
- Comparison with HDP-LDA is not comprehensive enough to fully support claims.
- Originality and significance: the sampler and EPPF represent a meaningful contribution to nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novelty, clarity, solid theoretical backing, and promising empirical results.
- Con-acceptance arguments: inadequate experimental comparisons, especially against other BNBP samplers.
