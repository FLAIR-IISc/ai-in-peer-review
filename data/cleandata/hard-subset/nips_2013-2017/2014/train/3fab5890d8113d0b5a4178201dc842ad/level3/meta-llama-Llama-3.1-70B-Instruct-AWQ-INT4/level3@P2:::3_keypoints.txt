- Introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and a related nonparametric Bayesian topic model.  
- Provides a thorough, rigorous derivation of the BNBP EPPF using a group-size-dependent model that yields a closed-form expression.  
- Presents the BNBP topic model with a clear description of the model and its inference algorithm.  
- Experimental results demonstrate the model’s effectiveness and include a thoughtful comparison with HDP topic modeling.  
- Manuscript is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.  
- The EPPF derivation is original and significant, enabling new nonparametric Bayesian models for complex data.  
- Performance gains over HDP-LDA are only marginal; no clear overall superiority of BNBP over HDP-LDA.  
- Main empirical conclusion: BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics rather than one being clearly better.  
- Reviewer recommends comparing the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.  
- Reviewer suggests evaluating uncollapsed representations to assess parallelization advantages and mixing tradeoffs versus the collapsed sampler.  
- Some technical details may be difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, addressing experimental-section comments and additional comparisons.
