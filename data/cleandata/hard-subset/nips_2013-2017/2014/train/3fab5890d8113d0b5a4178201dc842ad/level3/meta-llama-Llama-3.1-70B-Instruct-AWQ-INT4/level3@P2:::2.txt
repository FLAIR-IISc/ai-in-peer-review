The paper "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling" proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), a nonparametric Bayesian prior for mixed-membership modeling. The authors derive the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP and develop a CG sampler for the BNBP topic model, which is distinct from existing ones and intuitive to interpret.

The main idea of the paper is to develop a CG sampler for the BNBP, which is a nonparametric Bayesian prior for mixed-membership modeling. The authors start by deriving the EPPF for the BNBP, which describes the distribution of exchangeable random partitions of grouped data. They then develop a CG sampler for the BNBP topic model, which is a special case of mixed-membership modeling.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the BNBP and its applications. The derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP is novel and interesting, and the proposed CG sampler is simple to implement and has fast convergence and good mixing properties.

However, the experimental evaluation of the proposed sampler is insufficient. The authors compare the proposed sampler to the HDP-LDA, which is a different model, rather than comparing it to other BNBP samplers. This makes it difficult to assess the performance of the proposed sampler in relation to other BNBP samplers.

The authors' motivation for developing the proposed sampler centers on its advantages, including simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance. However, the work focuses on sampling algorithm development, rather than on new statistical modeling. Therefore, experiments should compare the proposed sampler to other BNBP sampling algorithms, rather than to HDP-LDA.

The paper provides the CG sampler but lacks experimental results comparing it with other BNBP samplers. This is a major concern, as it is unclear how the proposed sampler performs in relation to other BNBP samplers.
