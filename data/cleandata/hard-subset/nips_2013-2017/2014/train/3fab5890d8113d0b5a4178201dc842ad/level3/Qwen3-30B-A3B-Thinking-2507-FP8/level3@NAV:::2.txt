

This paper introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), enabling mixed-membership modeling with exchangeable random partitions across groups. The authors derive a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the BNBP, which they claim offers simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance. The work is theoretically grounded in extending EPPF models to mixed-membership settings, which has been a gap in the literature since previous work focused on single-group modeling.

The paper's main strengths lie in its theoretical contributions. Lemma 1 and the derived sampler represent a significant advance in understanding BNBP's exchangeable partition structure, filling an important gap in the literature. The derivation of the EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via a group-size dependent approach is novel and potentially generalizable to other integer-valued stochastic processes. The BNBP topic model is well-motivated and clearly distinct from existing approaches like HDP-LDA.

However, the paper suffers from a critical weakness in experimental evaluation. The authors' primary claim centers on sampler advantages, yet they compare their method only to HDP-LDA rather than to other BNBP sampling algorithms as required by their own stated motivation. This is a major concern as the work focuses on sampling algorithm development, not new statistical modeling. The experiments do not demonstrate the claimed advantages (fast convergence, good mixing) by comparing against alternative BNBP samplers. The paper lacks necessary comparisons to other BNBP inference methods, making it impossible to assess the true performance benefits of their proposed sampler.

The paper is technically sound with clear theoretical development (Quality), well-written with logical structure (Clarity), and makes a novel contribution to EPPF theory for mixed-membership modeling (Originality). The significance is limited by the insufficient experimental validation, though the theoretical framework could be valuable for future work.

Pro: Strong theoretical contribution to understanding BNBP's exchangeable partition structure; novel derivation of EPPF; well-motivated topic model application.

Con: Insufficient experimental evaluation; inappropriate comparison to HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP samplers; fails to demonstrate claimed sampler advantages through proper benchmarks.

While the theoretical work is valuable, the lack of appropriate experimental validation prevents me from recommending acceptance at this stage. The authors should provide comparative experiments with other BNBP inference methods before the paper can be considered for publication.
