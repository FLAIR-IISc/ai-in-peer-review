## Review of “Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling”

This paper presents a significant contribution to nonparametric Bayesian modeling by deriving the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). This allows for fully collapsed Gibbs sampling for a BNBP topic model, a feat previously hindered by the lack of a closed-form marginal distribution. The work builds upon the established framework of EPPFs, extending it to the challenging context of mixed-membership modeling with integer-valued stochastic processes. The paper is well-written and technically sound, clearly articulating the derivation of the EPPF and its application to topic modeling. 

The core originality lies in bridging the gap between the BNBP and the EPPF framework. While the BNBP has been applied to mixed-membership modeling before (e.g., [7, 10, 11]), those approaches relied on truncation or slice sampling. This work provides a fully collapsed sampler, offering computational advantages and a more principled Bayesian approach. The derived prediction rule is also a valuable contribution, facilitating efficient sampling. The authors appropriately position their work within the broader context of nonparametric Bayesian methods, referencing key papers like those by Ferguson [1], Lijoi et al. [2], and Teh et al. [6]. 

However, the empirical results demonstrate only marginal performance gains over the HDP-LDA. The authors honestly acknowledge this, concluding that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics rather than clear superiority. This is a strength of the paper – a careful and nuanced assessment of the results. The analysis of the impact of the dispersion parameter *rj* is insightful, highlighting the BNBP’s sensitivity to this parameter.

To further strengthen the work, I concur with the supervisor’s suggestions. A direct comparison of the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference would be valuable. Investigating uncollapsed representations for potential parallelization benefits, and analyzing the trade-offs between mixing and computational cost compared to the collapsed sampler, would also be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF is a novel and significant theoretical contribution.
*   The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler provides a computationally efficient and principled inference method.
*   The paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly explains the methodology.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Empirical performance gains over HDP-LDA are marginal.
*   Limited exploration of alternative inference methods (e.g., sliced sampling).



**Overall:** This paper meets the publication threshold. It presents a valuable theoretical contribution with a clear and well-executed implementation. While the empirical results are not groundbreaking, the work is significant and likely to be of interest to researchers in nonparametric Bayesian modeling and topic modeling. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the suggestions outlined above.
