- Proposes a new Bayesian nonparametric mixed‑membership model based on the Beta‑Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).  
- Derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP and corresponding prediction rules.  
- Introduces a BNBP topic model and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for inference.  
- Main technical idea: randomize group sizes (instead of fixing them) to obtain a group‑size‑dependent EPPF via the integer‑valued BNBP.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a new perspective linking BNBP and EPPF analysis, enabling marginal distributions and prediction rules.  
- Paper is well‑organized with rigorous derivations and thorough analysis of model properties.  
- Some sections are dense and require careful reading, so clarity could be improved in parts.  
- Practical advantage over existing methods (e.g., HDP) is unclear; no intuitive explanation why BNBP should outperform alternatives.  
- Inference algorithm is more complex than HDP in theory and implementation, raising concerns about usability.  
- Unclear whether added complexity is justified by significant empirical gains; reviewer requests empirical clarification.  
- Originality: significant theoretical novelty, though using BNBP for mixed‑membership is not entirely new and builds on prior work.  
- Potential significance: could impact BNP research by unifying EPPF analysis, but practical implications need stronger justification.  
- Recommendations: reviewer leans toward acceptance but asks authors to clarify practical performance gains and justify added complexity.
