- Main contribution: Derives the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic models.
- Novelty: Provides a tractable, theoretically grounded inference method for BNBP-based models where prior work relied on truncation or slice sampling.
- Prediction rule: Derives a prediction rule enabling simulation from the BNBP model.
- Theoretical grounding: Clearly connects the BNBP results to existing nonparametric Bayesian frameworks (DP, HDP, exchangeable partitions).
- Empirical evaluation: Tests on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS corpora with comparisons to HDP-LDA, showing thorough experimental work.
- Performance: Empirical gains over HDP-LDA are marginal and neither model consistently outperforms the other.
- Model characteristics: Highlights a trade-off between compactness of representation and predictive performance differing between BNBP and HDP-LDA.
- Strength — theory: The EPPF derivation is a significant theoretical advancement.
- Strength — computation: The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler offers improved computational efficiency versus some existing approaches.
- Strength — presentation: Manuscript is well-written, technically sound, and easy to follow.
- Strength — context: Effectively situates the work within broader nonparametric Bayesian literature.
- Weakness — empirical impact: Limited demonstrable performance improvement over existing methods.
- Weakness — computational analysis: Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, limitations, and scalability.
- Weakness — missing baselines: Does not compare the collapsed sampler to other BNBP inference methods (sliced sampler [10], truncated sampler [8]).
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses a gap in tractable BNBP inference, offers a valuable theoretical result and efficient sampler, and meets NIPS quality standards.
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient empirical advantage and inadequate discussion of computational tradeoffs could weaken the paper’s impact.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with suggested additions of comparisons to sliced/truncated samplers and exploration of uncollapsed/parallelizable representations as future work.
