- Main contribution: Derives the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models, filling a key theoretical gap.
- Methodological novelty: Derives an ECPF for random group sizes and uses Bayes' rule to obtain a group-size-dependent EPPF.
- Inference advance: Enables the first fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP models.
- Prediction rule: Produces a simple, intuitive multiplicative prediction rule (Eq. 10) linking global and local topic popularity.
- Comparison to HDP: Contrasts the BNBP's multiplicative shrinkage with the HDP's additive behavior and formulates a BNBP topic model.
- Technical quality: The derivations and collapsed sampler appear technically sound and straightforward.
- Clarity: Manuscript is well-written, well-organized, and effectively situates the work in prior literature.
- Experimental limitation 1: Experiments compare only to HDP-LDA, conflating model and sampler performance.
- Experimental limitation 2: Lacks direct comparison to other BNBP inference methods (e.g., truncated block Gibbs, slice sampler) to isolate sampler benefits.
- Empirical gains: BNBP yields more compact topic representations but shows marginal, hyperparameter-sensitive improvements over HDP-LDA.
- Practical trade-off: Collapsed sampler converges faster per iteration but is inherently sequential; uncollapsed, parallelizable samplers may win in wall-clock time.
- Pros summary: High novelty, a new inference method, technical clarity, and broader significance for integer-valued process models.
- Cons summary: Limited experimental comparisons and only marginal empirical superiority in predictive performance.
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance subject to strengthening experimental comparisons.
