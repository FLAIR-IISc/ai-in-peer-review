- Introduces a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP via a derived EPPF enabling truncation-free collapsed sampling.  
- Correctly places the work within nonparametric Bayesian literature, referencing related EPPF and random measure papers.  
- Derivation of Lemma 1 and resulting sampler are technically sound and interesting.  
- Claims practical benefits: simple implementation, potentially faster convergence, and good mixing.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and misdirected by comparing to HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP samplers.  
- Lacks comparisons to existing BNBP inference methods (e.g., slice sampling, truncated Gibbs), limiting assessment of the sampler’s contribution.  
- Results discussion focuses on advantages relative to HDP-LDA instead of within-BNBP performance.  
- Needs analysis of computational complexity, runtime comparisons, and more detail on parallelization potential.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel EPPF derivation, clear theoretical advantages, and solid writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate experimental validation and inappropriate experimental setup.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after focused empirical comparison to other BNBP samplers.
