- Introduces an Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).
- Uses the EPPF to derive a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model, removing the need for truncation.
- Extends nonparametric Bayesian methods (DP/HDP) to mixed-membership modeling with integer-valued data.
- Provides a unified, principled marginalization framework where prior BNBP work relied on ad-hoc truncation.
- Manuscript is well-written, logically structured, and mathematically clear.
- Technical derivations appear sound with careful treatment of the EPPF and Gibbs sampler development.
- Principal novelty is the EPPF derivation for BNBP in mixed-membership contexts.
- Significant theoretical contribution with applicability beyond topic modeling.
- Resulting collapsed sampler is a practical contribution enabling efficient inference in BNBP models.
- Paper clarifies differences between BNBP and HDP in controlling model complexity.
- Empirical results show BNBP topic model is competitive with HDP-LDA, achieving comparable perplexities with a more compact representation.
- Performance gains over HDP-LDA are marginal; no clear superiority demonstrated.
- Empirical section lacks deeper analysis of why BNBP performs similarly; an ablation study on the dispersion parameter rj is recommended.
- Missing comparisons to other BNBP inference methods (sliced and truncated samplers) limits the empirical assessment.
- Potential parallelization benefits of uncollapsed representations are mentioned but not investigated; exploring this tradeoff is suggested.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, including runtime comparisons to alternative samplers and a brief discussion of parallelization challenges/solutions.
