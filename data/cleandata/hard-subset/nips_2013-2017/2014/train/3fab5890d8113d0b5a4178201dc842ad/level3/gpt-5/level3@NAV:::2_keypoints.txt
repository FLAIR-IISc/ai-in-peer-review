- Develops an EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and derives a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership models (topic models).
- Proceeds via an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) and a group-size–dependent EPPF to obtain a simple prediction rule that marginalizes beta process, topic distributions, and topic weights.
- Lemma 1 establishes equivalence between the group-size–dependent construction and the BNBP; the resulting sampler appears novel and interesting.
- Theoretical development is careful and technically sound, with well-motivated marginalizations and closed-form sampler updates.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient to support claims of simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance.
- Comparisons are made to HDP-LDA rather than competing BNBP samplers (e.g., truncated blocked Gibbs, slice sampling), so “state-of-the-art” predictive claims are unsubstantiated.
- Paper is notation-heavy and would benefit from an algorithm box, per-sweep complexity analysis, and a discussion of sample-size–dependent EPPFs versus classical addition rules.
- Originality is high on the inference side: providing a BNBP EPPF for grouped data and a fully collapsed sampler is new.
- Significance is potentially high if the sampler demonstrably outperforms existing BNBP inference methods.
- Pros: novel EPPF/ECPF derivations and elegant prediction rule (Lemmas 1–4); fully collapsed sampler likely improves mixing and efficiency; clear relation to and differentiation from HDP-LDA.
- Cons: lacks direct comparisons to existing BNBP samplers; limited diagnostics (ESS, autocorrelation, wall-clock per effective sample) and ablation studies (role of r_j, sensitivity to c, γ0).
- Missing experiments: no synthetic recovery tests or scalability experiments.
- Recommendation: Weak reject unless camera-ready includes rigorous comparisons against BNBP samplers and stronger empirical diagnostics.
