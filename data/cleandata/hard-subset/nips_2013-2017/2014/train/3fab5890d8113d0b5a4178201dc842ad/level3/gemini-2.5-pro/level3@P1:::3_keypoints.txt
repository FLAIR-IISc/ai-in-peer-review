- Main contribution: Derives the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling fully collapsed inference for mixed-membership models.  
- Method: Uses a sample-size-dependent model to obtain an ECPF and applies Bayes' rule to derive the group-size-dependent EPPF; implements a BNBP topic model with a collapsed Gibbs sampler.  

Strengths:
- Originality/significance: Novel, general theoretical result filling a gap for integer-valued stochastic processes and likely useful beyond topic modeling.  
- Technical quality: Derivations (ECPF, EPPF, predictive rule) and Gibbs updates are sound and give intuitive multiplicative popularity interactions.  
- Clarity: Well-written, well-motivated, and clearly compares BNBP update rules to LDA/HDP-LDA.  
- Empirical insights: Collapsed sampler shows faster convergence/mixing and yields more compact topic representations for comparable perplexity.

Weaknesses:
- Limited experimental comparison: Lacks direct baselines against other BNBP inference methods (truncated Gibbs, slice sampler) to quantify benefits of the collapsed sampler.  
- Overstated performance claims: “State-of-the-art predictive performance” is too strong; results show trade-offs (compactness vs. perplexity) and HDP-LDA can achieve lower perplexity in some settings.  
- Scalability discussion lacking: Does not adequately address that the collapsed sampler is inherently sequential while blocked/uncollapsed samplers are easier to parallelize for large-scale data.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, novel theoretical contribution and solid paper; experimental evaluation should be strengthened but merits publication.
