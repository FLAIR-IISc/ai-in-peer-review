- Introduces a novel fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and fills a literature gap.
- Derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling, enabling explicit description of BNBP-induced partitions.
- Lemma 1 and the EPPF derivation are innovative and technically sound, showing deep understanding of underlying processes.
- The CG sampler construction is technically solid and nontrivial.
- Empirically, the fully collapsed sampler yields faster convergence, better mixing, and strong predictive performance.
- Demonstrates practical utility via application to topic modeling, producing compact corpus representations.
- Main weakness: lacks comparisons to other BNBP sampling algorithms (e.g., finite truncation or slice sampling).
- Comparison only to HDP-LDA is insufficient and may not validate claimed advantages.
- Paper focuses heavily on the sampling algorithm and insufficiently addresses broader statistical modeling considerations.
- Some derivations (notably the EPPF) are mathematically dense and may be hard for nonexperts; more intuition or visuals would help.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty of CG sampler, Lemma 1/EPPF contributions, and strong empirical utility.
- Rejection arguments: missing key experimental baselines, limited modeling discussion, and readability concerns; addressing these would strengthen impact.
