- Derives a group-size-dependent EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), enabling fully collapsed inference for mixed-membership models.  
- Key technique: randomize group sizes to obtain an ECPF, then condition on observed sizes via Bayes’ rule to get the EPPF.  
- Produces a collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the infinite beta process and compares it to HDP-LDA.  
- Theoretical contribution is non-trivial and sound; proofs, prediction rule, and sampler derivations are correct.  
- Empirically shows faster convergence and better mixing than HDP-LDA but does not consistently improve predictive perplexity.  
- BNBP tends to achieve comparable perplexity with a more compact topic representation, rather than clear predictive superiority.  
- Paper is well-written and structured; the comparison of update equations (multiplicative vs additive sharing) is clear.  
- Lacks intuitive explanation for why the BNBP’s multiplicative topic sharing is desirable or when it is preferable to HDP’s additive sharing.  
- Highly original methodological idea; randomizing group sizes may generalize to other integer-valued processes.  
- Theoretical significance is high; practical significance is more limited given modest empirical gains relative to added complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novel theory, innovative technique, improved sampler mixing, and advances toward EPPF-based BNP analysis.  
- Arguments for rejection: weak empirical predictive gains, missing intuition for modeling choice, and possibly unjustified added complexity.  
- Questions for authors: provide intuition/empirical scenarios favoring multiplicative sharing (e.g., burstiness); clarify “state-of-the-art” claim to reflect perplexity vs compactness; note other practical benefits beyond faster convergence.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept—publish for its theoretical value—subject to tempering predictive claims and adding intuitive motivation.
