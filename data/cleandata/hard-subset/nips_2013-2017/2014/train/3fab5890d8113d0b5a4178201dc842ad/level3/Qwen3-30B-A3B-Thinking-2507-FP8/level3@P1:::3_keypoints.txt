- Introduces an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling nonparametric mixed-membership modeling with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.  
- Core contribution: derives the BNBP’s EPPF, filling a gap where prior methods relied on indirect representations (e.g., Chinese restaurant franchise).  
- Generalizes the EPPF framework to grouped data with observed sizes using a group-size-dependent model and Bayes’ rule, producing a prediction rule for simulation.  
- Proposes a BNBP topic model that analytically marginalizes the infinite-dimensional beta process, yielding closed-form updates.  
- Provides a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler (Equation 12) with closed-form parameter updates and rigorous derivations (Lemmas 2–4).  
- Empirical results on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 show faster convergence and better mixing than HDP-LDA, with comparable perplexity using fewer topics.  
- Technical soundness is strong, with careful handling of marginalization and connections to Poisson factor models.  
- Clarity is excellent: paper is well-organized, with accessible prediction rule and clear intuition for dispersion parameters.  
- Originality is high: the EPPF derivation is novel and generalizable (e.g., to gamma-Poisson); the topic model extends prior BNBP work.  
- Significance is high: provides a template for other integer-valued processes, practical benefits (no tuning, compact topic representations), and avenues for parallelization research.  
- Key weaknesses: lacks direct empirical comparison to sliced/truncated BNBP samplers [8,10]; “state-of-the-art” predictive claim is overstated given marginal perplexity gains; discussion of parallelization is underdeveloped.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — technically rigorous, original, clear, and impactful, with minor addressable weaknesses.
