- Main contribution: Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership modeling.  
- Novel extension: Generalizes the EPPF framework to grouped data with fixed group sizes, beyond prior single-set mixture models.  
- Theoretical foundation: Provides rigorous derivation of the BNBP's EPPF and prediction rule, explaining how the integer-valued process governs exchangeable partitions across groups.  
- Practical model: Constructs a BNBP topic model that is straightforward to implement and shows faster convergence and better mixing than HDP-LDA in experiments.  
- Technical quality and clarity: Manuscript is rigorous, well-organized, with careful proofs and clear explanations of novel concepts.  
- Relation to prior work: Effectively connects the BNBP to related processes (e.g., HDP, negative binomial IBP) demonstrating strong domain knowledge.  
- Originality: The group-size-dependent EPPF for mixed-membership modeling is a novel theoretical framework for integer-valued stochastic processes.  
- Empirical limitations: Performance gains over HDP-LDA are marginal and not clearly established under standard predictive metrics.  
- Missing comparisons: Should compare the collapsed sampler to alternative inference methods (sliced sampler, truncated sampler) to better assess efficiency.  
- Suggested extensions: Explore uncollapsed representations for potential parallelization benefits to strengthen empirical evaluation.  
- Significance and recommendation: High theoretical significance; paper meets publication threshold with minor experimental additions recommended — reviewer recommends acceptance.
