Paper derives a novel, group-size dependent EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) enabling a new fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.

First fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for BNBP presented as an alternative to finite truncation and slice sampling.

Work is well-situated in prior literature (e.g., Zhou et al. 2012; Broderick et al. 2013) and connects BNBP to EPPF and HDP frameworks.

Primary strength is theoretical originality and high-quality derivations (Lemma 1–4).

The CG sampler is expected to yield better mixing and faster convergence; derivations allow reproducible implementation.

Paper is clearly written and accessible to experts.

Main weakness: experimental evaluation fails to validate the claimed algorithmic advantages.

Experiments incorrectly compare BNBP-CG to an HDP-LDA sampler, conflating model differences with inference performance.

Authors should compare against existing BNBP inference methods (e.g., truncated blocked Gibbs, slice sampler); without this, claims about improved convergence/mixing are unsupported.

Practical significance is weakened because the empirical evidence does not demonstrate the asserted benefits of the new sampler.
