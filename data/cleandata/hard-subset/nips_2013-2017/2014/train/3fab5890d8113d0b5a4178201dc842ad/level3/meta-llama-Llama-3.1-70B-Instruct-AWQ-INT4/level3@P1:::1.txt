This paper proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model based on the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The authors derive an Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the BNBP, which is a crucial step towards unifying EPPF analysis and Bayesian nonparametric (BNP) research. The paper also presents a prediction rule for the BNBP, enabling the simulation of exchangeable random partitions of grouped data.

The paper makes significant theoretical contributions by introducing a new method for deriving the EPPF of the BNBP. The authors randomize group sizes, which allows them to derive marginal distributions. This approach is distinct from existing methods, which typically treat group sizes as fixed.

The authors also develop a BNBP topic model, which is a special case of mixed-membership modeling. They derive a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the model, which is more complex than the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP) in both theory and implementation.

The paper presents empirical results on three corpora, demonstrating the performance of the BNBP topic model compared to the HDP. While the BNBP topic model achieves comparable performance to the HDP, the reviewer notes that the empirical improvements are not sufficient to justify the added complexity of the model.

One of the limitations of the paper is the lack of an intuitive explanation for why the proposed method should outperform the HDP in practice. The reviewer requests clarification from the authors on practical performance gains.

Despite this limitation, the reviewer leans towards acceptance, acknowledging the significant theoretical contributions of the paper.

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method. However, the reviewer notes that the paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of why the BNBP topic model should outperform the HDP in practice.

**Originality**: The paper proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model based on the BNBP, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Significance**: The paper makes significant theoretical contributions to the field of Bayesian nonparametrics, and the proposed method has the potential to be applied to a wide range of problems.

**Arguments for acceptance**:

* Significant theoretical contributions to the field of Bayesian nonparametrics
* Novel Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model based on the BNBP
* Thorough derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP
* Comparable performance to the HDP on three corpora

**Arguments against acceptance**:

* Lack of an intuitive explanation for why the proposed method should outperform the HDP in practice
* Added complexity of the model compared to the HDP
* Empirical improvements over the HDP are not sufficient to justify the added complexity

Overall, the reviewer recommends acceptance of the paper, acknowledging its significant theoretical contributions and potential impact on the field of Bayesian nonparametrics.
