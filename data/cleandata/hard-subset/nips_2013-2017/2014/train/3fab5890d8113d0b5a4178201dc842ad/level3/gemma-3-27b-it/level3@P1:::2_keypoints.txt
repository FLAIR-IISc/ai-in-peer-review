- Introduces an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process.  
- Fills a gap where prior BNBP inference relied on truncation due to lack of a marginal distribution for exchangeable partitions.  
- Core contribution: derivation of the EPPF (Lemmas 1 & 2, Equations 6 and 9) and leveraging it to build the CG sampler.  
- Situates the work within nonparametric Bayesian models, relating to DP/CRP, HDP, and other integer-valued stochastic processes, with appropriate citations.  
- Theoretical strength: significant, novel derivation with clear mathematical exposition and closed-form update equations aiding reproducibility.  
- Practical strength: development of a straightforward-to-implement fully collapsed Gibbs sampler claimed to improve convergence speed and mixing.  
- Experimental weakness: evaluation focuses on model comparison (BNBP vs. HDP-LDA via perplexity) rather than directly testing the sampler’s advantages.  
- Missing direct comparisons to other BNBP inference methods (truncated approaches and slice sampling) for runtime, mixing diagnostics, and convergence rates.  
- Claim of “state-of-the-art” predictive performance is unsupported without focused sampler comparisons.  
- Discussion of parallelization is superficial and needs more concrete exploration.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requiring added experiments comparing CG sampler runtime, convergence, and mixing to existing BNBP samplers.
