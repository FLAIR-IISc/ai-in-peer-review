- First EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed‑membership setting via randomizing group sizes → ECPF → conditioning to get sample‑size dependent EPPF and a simple prediction rule; yields a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler and multiplicative (vs HDP additive) assignment probabilities.
- Connects to DP/CRP, HDP/CRF, Poisson factorization, and recent BNBP/GNBP literature; clarifies BNBP lies outside σ‑stable Poisson–Kingman family.
- Conceptual advance: reusable ECPF→EPPF route to obtain sample‑size dependent EPPFs for integer‑valued mixed‑membership processes.
- Clean prediction rule and compact, truncation‑free collapsed sampler with no auxiliary table bookkeeping; publicly released code.
- Clear articulation of multiplicative vs additive shrinkage distinction compared to HDP‑LDA.
- Empirically mixes quickly and produces compact topic representations (fewer topics at comparable perplexity) on three corpora.
- Practical gains over HDP‑LDA are modest and inconsistent; HDP can outperform for small η and BNBP is not clearly superior when topic counts are matched.
- Sample‑size dependence of the EPPF is acknowledged but not fully explored regarding classical addition rules and predictive exchangeability as m changes.
- The randomize‑then‑condition path is elegant but lacks intuitive explanation for why it improves modeling/practical performance over HDP.
- Increased theoretical/implementation complexity (new distributions, special functions, hyperparameters c, γ0, rj) not yet justified by empirical results.
- Evaluation gaps: missing comparisons to HDP collapsed CRF, other Poisson factorization/GNBP variants, runtime/memory profiling, and ablations on learning vs fixing rj.
- Derivations appear sound and self‑consistent; collapsed sampler follows standard marginalization, but normalization and exchangeability/consistency conditions need more detail.
- Presentation is mathematically dense; would benefit from a high‑level diagram, pseudocode/implementation guidance, intuitive narrative on multiplicative shrinkage and rj, and clearer notation.
- Originality is high: first BNBP EPPF in mixed‑membership and a novel ECPF→EPPF technique that may generalize.
- Significance: strong theoretical contribution to Bayesian nonparametrics; moderate practical impact due to limited predictive gains.
- Requests for clarification/experiments: (1) precise implications of sample‑size dependence; (2) intuition/toy example for multiplicative shrinkage benefits; (3) sensitivity/posterior summaries for c, γ0, rj; (4) ablation for fixed vs learned rj; (5) add HDP CRF and GNBP/Poisson baselines; (6) report per‑iteration time, memory, and total runtime to target perplexity.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, technically solid theory and a working, well‑mixing, truncation‑free collapsed sampler with potential generality.
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical advantage over strong baselines, higher complexity, and insufficient discussion of sample‑size dependent EPPF consequences.
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
