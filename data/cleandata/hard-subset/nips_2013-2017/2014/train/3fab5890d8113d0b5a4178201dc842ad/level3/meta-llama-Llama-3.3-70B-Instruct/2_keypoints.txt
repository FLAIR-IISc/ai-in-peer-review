- Proposes a novel maximum-entropy framework for designing priors by projecting a base distribution onto constraint sets for structured variables.  
- Applies the framework to sparse structured variables and demonstrates practical relevance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and thorough methodological exposition.  
- Offers rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the proposed approach.  
- Combines familiar techniques in a novel way to enforce structure in Bayesian models.  
- Claims improved handling of a difficult problem compared to prior work.  
- Experiments show strong support recovery and predictive accuracy, notably on high-dimensional functional neuroimaging data.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited—only compared against HDP-LDA and not other BNBP sampling algorithms.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity of the proposed approach.  
- Needs a more thorough discussion of limitations and potential applications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, as strengths (originality, soundness, significance, empirical results) outweigh weaknesses.
