This paper introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a nonparametric Bayesian topic model. The authors provide a thorough derivation of the EPPF and demonstrate its application in topic modeling. The manuscript is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.

The primary contribution of the paper is the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership/count-mixture modeling. This is an interesting and broadly useful result, as it enables the development of new nonparametric Bayesian models for complex data. The authors' approach is based on a group size dependent model, which allows them to derive a closed-form expression for the EPPF.

The BNBP topic model is a natural application of the EPPF, and the authors provide a clear and concise description of the model and its inference algorithm. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the model in topic modeling tasks, and the authors provide a thoughtful comparison with the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) topic model.

The reviewer recommends comparing the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method and help to identify its strengths and weaknesses. Additionally, the reviewer suggests evaluating uncollapsed representations for parallelization advantages and examining mixing tradeoffs versus the collapsed sampler.

Overall, the paper is interesting and meets the publication threshold. The primary contribution is significant, and the experimental results are convincing. However, the reviewer notes that the performance gains over HDP-LDA are only marginal, and the main empirical conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics, but not clear superiority.
