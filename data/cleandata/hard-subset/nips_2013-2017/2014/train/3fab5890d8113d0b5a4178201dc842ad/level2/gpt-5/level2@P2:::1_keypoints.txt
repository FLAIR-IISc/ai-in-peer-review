- Summary: Derives an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) for grouped data by constructing an ECPF via randomized group sizes and conditioning on observed sizes, yielding a simple prediction rule and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for topic modeling.
- Relation to prior work: Well-situated among DP/NRMI/EPPF, HDP, and BNBP/GNBP literature, clarifying distinctions from recent predictive structures and noting BNBP lies outside some Poisson–Kingman classes.
- Theory quality: Lemmas for the ECPF, matrix prior, EPPF, and prediction rule form a coherent, sound theoretical contribution despite algebraic complexity of the EPPF.
- Inference quality: The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler appears correct and leverages a novel multiplicative global–local shrinkage different from HDP’s additive form.
- Empirics quality: Experiments on three corpora show faster mixing/convergence and competitive or better held-out perplexity, using standard predictive perplexity evaluation.
- Clarity: Paper is dense but organized; needs a notation table and more intuitive explanation of the “digamma distribution”; contains minor typos and incomplete refs.
- Originality: Novel derivation of a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP and a fully collapsed sampler integrating out the beta process and topic weights.
- Significance: Advances theoretical understanding of count-based nonparametrics and provides a simple, efficient sampler with practical impact for topic modeling.
- Pro: Fills a gap by deriving a principled BNBP EPPF for grouped data without truncation or slicing.
- Pro: Provides a simple, usable prediction rule enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.
- Pro: Introduces interpretable multiplicative global–local shrinkage for topics.
- Pro: Empirical evidence shows faster mixing and competitive perplexity with more compact topic representations.
- Pro: Methodology (ECPF → EPPF) may generalize to other integer-valued processes.
- Con: Lacks comparison to the standard collapsed CRF HDP sampler, which would be a fairer baseline for mixing and speed.
- Con: Hyperparameter η is swept rather than inferred; c and γ0 sensitivity is underexplored.
- Con: The role and interpretation of the “digamma distribution” are unclear and unusual terminology.
- Con: Some clarity issues, typos, and incomplete bibliography entries remain.
- Suggestion: Compare against an HDP collapsed CRF sampler or provide runtime/overhead comparisons to better benchmark mixing and speed.
- Suggestion: Infer η and c (e.g., with gamma priors) and report posterior summaries and sensitivity analyses for γ0 and c.
- Suggestion: Provide more intuition for key equations (e.g., Eq. 10) including limiting behaviors and contrasts with HDP.
- Suggestion: Report runtime normalized per token/word and per topic to make speed comparisons robust.
- Suggestion: Expand discussion contrasting this EPPF with combinatorial structures in related IBP/BNBP predictive frameworks and clarify overlaps/differences.
- Suggestion: Fix typos, clarify notation (symbols-at-a-glance), and complete references.
- Overall evaluation: Strong theoretical and methodological contribution with practical impact and recommend acceptance despite some missing baselines and minor clarity issues.
