- Introduces an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), filling a theoretical gap for integer-valued stochastic processes in mixed-membership modeling.
- Derives the BNBP EPPF, prediction rule, and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler using a group size dependent model.
- Theoretical contribution is novel and technically sound, providing a foundation for exchangeable partitions with BNBP.
- The BNBP topic model is intuitive and empirically superior: faster convergence, better mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance versus HDP-LDA.
- Experiments on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS corpora convincingly demonstrate the model’s advantages and more compact corpus representation.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition, though the EPPF derivation could use a more intuitive explanation.
- Insightful comparison with HDP-LDA, highlighting BNBP’s multiplicative control of topic number versus HDP’s additive mechanism.
- Sufficiently connects to prior work on EPPFs and integer-valued processes, justifying the methodological choices.
- Quality: technically sound derivations and comprehensive, well-designed experiments.
- Clarity: generally clear organization, but some sections (EPPF derivation) should be more accessible to non-specialists.
- Originality: genuine novelty in deriving the BNBP EPPF and using group size dependent models for mixed-membership EPPFs.
- Significance: addresses an important literature gap and offers practical advantages; approach could extend to other integer-valued processes.
- Minor suggestion: better explain why BNBP’s EPPF depends on group sizes, a departure from standard EPPFs.
- Minor suggestion: include comparisons with other recent nonparametric topic models (e.g., discrete infinite logistic normal).
- Minor suggestion: provide quantitative comparisons of the number of topics inferred by BNBP versus HDP-LDA across datasets.
- Limitation: focus is narrow on topic modeling, though framework likely generalizes to other applications.
- Recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
