Summary and relation to prior work: The paper develops an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling induced by the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors show how to obtain a group-size–dependent EPPF by first introducing a sample-size–randomized model (ECPF) and then applying Bayes’ rule, yielding a prior over column-exchangeable random count matrices whose row sums are the observed group sizes. They derive a simple prediction rule and construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process. Applied to topic modeling, the method is contrasted theoretically and empirically with HDP-LDA. The work builds on the tradition of EPPFs for mixture models (Pitman), hierarchical modeling via the HDP (Teh et al.), and count-based processes (BNBP, gamma–Poisson, IBP), and complements recent Poisson–Kingman and negative-binomial process results.

Quality: The technical development appears sound. The ECPF/EPPF derivations are carefully motivated, with closed-form sampling updates and a clear prediction rule. Empirically, the evaluation across three corpora with multiple η settings uses standard perplexity metrics and multiple splits; mixing/convergence diagnostics are informative. Some proofs and implementation details are deferred to the appendix/code, but overall the work is reproducible.

Clarity: The paper is well organized but notation is dense. The dependence of the EPPF on group sizes (and its implications vis-à-vis classic addition rules) could be emphasized earlier. Minor expository improvements would help (e.g., a small running example of the count-matrix prior and the digamma distribution).

Originality: High. To my knowledge, this is the first explicit EPPF for mixed-membership modeling with an integer-valued process (BNBP), enabling truncation-free, fully collapsed inference. The multiplicative shrinkage mechanism is a novel contrast to HDP’s additive scheme.

Significance and usefulness: Significant. The method provides a practical, faster-mixing alternative to HDP-LDA with competitive or better perplexity at comparable topic counts, and a compact representation. The general blueprint (ECPF→EPPF via size randomization) may transfer to other count processes.

Limitations and suggestions:
- Scope: Experiments focus on topic models; adding a non-text grouped dataset would strengthen claims of generality.
- Comparisons: Include NGGP/Pitman–Yor-based topic models and Poisson factorization baselines beyond HDP-LDA.
- Sensitivity: Provide ablations for rj, c, γ0 priors and a robustness analysis; discuss when very small η favors HDP.
- Theory: Clarify conditions under which the group-size–dependent EPPF approximates size-free behavior; add a sanity check that recovers known special cases.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel and technically solid EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership
- Fully collapsed, truncation-free sampler with strong empirical performance
- Clear impact for Bayesian nonparametrics and topic modeling

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited empirical breadth and baselines
- Heavy notation; some proofs deferred and minor clarity issues
- Group-size–dependent EPPF may raise questions about general exchangeability assumptions
