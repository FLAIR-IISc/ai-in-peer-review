- Paper derives ECPF, group-size dependent EPPF, and a prediction rule for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership modeling.
- Shows random count matrix has a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns with digamma-distributed sizes and Dirichlet–multinomial within-column allocations.
- Constructs a fully collapsed, truncation-free Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model and compares it to HDP-LDA.
- Fills a gap: provides marginal EPPF representation for integer-valued processes (BNBP) analogous to DP/CRP results.
- Clarifies BNBP assignment rule combines global and local popularity multiplicatively, unlike additive HDP behavior.
- Theoretical derivations (beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, Lemmas 1–2) are plausible and align with CRM conjugacy, though proofs are deferred.
- Count-matrix factorization into digamma + Dirichlet–multinomial is elegant and offers intuition for the model.
- Recommend authors state assumptions ensuring absolute convergence and measurability when interchanging infinite sums/integrals.
- Fully collapsed sampler marginalizes topics, weights, and beta process, yielding simple updates and fast mixing.
- Empirically the sampler reaches target topic counts faster and mixes better than the direct-assignment HDP-LDA baseline.
- Experiments across three corpora evaluate convergence, mixing, perplexity, and runtime for multiple η values.
- Concern about fairness: HDP baseline is partially collapsed; comparison to a fully collapsed CRF sampler is needed.
- Sensitivity to hyperpriors for γ0, c, and rj is not thoroughly explored; posterior summaries for rj are suggested.
- Paper is dense but well-organized; separation of ECPF vs EPPF and the prediction rule aid clarity.
- Some notation (e.g., digamma distribution, “.” subscripts) needs clearer intuition and brief property descriptions.
- Originality is high: novel group-size dependent EPPF and collapsed sampler that integrates out the beta process.
- Significance is high: enables truncation-free collapsed inference for integer-valued processes and has practical impact for topic models.
- Strengths: principled derivations, collapsed sampler with strong mixing, insightful HDP comparison, reproducible code, and empirical validation.
- Weaknesses: missing fully collapsed HDP baseline, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and lacking complexity/memory scalability details.
- Theoretical suggestions: clarify consistency conditions as group counts/sizes grow and asymptotics related to ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c).
- Generality claim should be supported by sketching requirements or an example beyond BNBP.
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to priors, comparison to CRF-based collapsed HDP, numerical stability for ψ evaluations, projective-limit properties, and posterior KJ/n·k summaries.
- Reasons to accept: novel theoretical contribution enabling practical collapsed inference, strong empirical support, and broad relevance to Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Reasons to reject: empirical comparison fairness and some deferred technical details need addressing before acceptance.
