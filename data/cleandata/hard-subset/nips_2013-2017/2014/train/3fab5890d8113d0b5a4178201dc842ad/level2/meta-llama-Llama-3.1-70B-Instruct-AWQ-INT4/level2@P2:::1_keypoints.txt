- Introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).
- Derives a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, producing a distinct nonparametric Bayesian topic model.
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of technical derivations.
- Shows BNBP can model exchangeable random partitions of grouped data and provides a prediction rule for simulating these partitions.
- Proposes a BNBP-based topic model that achieves state-of-the-art predictive performance in evaluations.
- Provides a thorough empirical comparison with other methods.
- Main strength: novel application of BNBP to mixed-membership modeling with clear technical development.
- Secondary strength: comprehensive evaluation and strong empirical results.
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with BNBP and EPPF, making the paper potentially hard for non-experts.
- Weakness: lacks clear intuitive explanation of why the proposed method is a good solution.
- Suggestion: improve exposition of intuition and reduce prerequisite assumptions for readers.
- Suggestion: include more details on computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity and scalability concerns.
