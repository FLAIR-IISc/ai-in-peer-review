- Introduces an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), removing the need for truncation in BNBP inference.  
- Derives a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model showing fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance.  
- Extends exchangeable random partition theory to mixed-membership modeling with count data, correctly situating within prior nonparametric Bayesian work.  
- Properly references related models (Dirichlet processes, NRMIs, Chinese Restaurant Franchise) and recent integer-valued stochastic process approaches.  
- Novelty: deriving the BNBP EPPF is a significant, long-standing theoretical contribution enabling marginal inference.  
- Technical soundness: derivations are rigorous, supported by lemmas and an augmented beta-gamma-Poisson representation.  
- Practicality: Gibbs sampler is computationally efficient, reproducible (detailed updates in appendix), and outperforms HDP-LDA on benchmarks.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized despite mathematical complexity.  
- Evaluation: comprehensive experiments, convergence diagnostics, and parameter-effect analyses demonstrate advantages of the method.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters c, a0, b0, e0, f0 beyond η.  
- Weakness: explicit EPPF form is complex (Eq. 9), potentially limiting direct applicability and requiring more computational-strategy discussion.  
- Weakness: evaluation restricted to standard benchmarks; lacks demonstration on complex real-world applications.  
- Weakness (minor): notation is dense; a notation table would improve readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, practically useful, well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and likely to advance the field.  
- Arguments against acceptance: EPPF complexity, limited hyperparameter sensitivity study, and lack of real-world application may hinder adoption.  
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to analyze hyperparameter sensitivity and discuss ways to simplify or handle EPPF computational complexity.
