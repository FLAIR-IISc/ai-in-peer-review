This paper introduces an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model, which is distinct from existing ones, with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that converges fast, mixes well, and has state-of-the-art predictive performance. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of the methodology and the experimental results.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP, which is a challenging problem. The authors provide a general method to arrive at an EPPF for mixed-membership modeling using an integer-valued stochastic process, which is an important contribution. The paper also provides a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model, which is straightforward to implement and has state-of-the-art predictive performance.

The experimental results are thorough and well-presented. The authors compare the performance of the BNBP topic model with the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) and latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) on three different corpora. The results show that the BNBP topic model has better predictive performance than the HDP and LDA when the number of topics is comparable.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the methodology and the experimental results. The writing style is clear and concise, making it easy to follow. The paper is well-suited for the NIPS conference, as it presents a novel methodology and experimental results that are relevant to the field of machine learning.
