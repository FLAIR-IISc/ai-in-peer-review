- Introduces the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) and derives an Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for grouped mixed-membership modeling.  
- Derivation of the BNBP's EPPF is a significant theoretical advance that removes the need to truncate the number of atoms.  
- Develops a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a new nonparametric Bayesian topic model, eliminating tuning of K and Dirichlet α.  
- Empirical results claim faster convergence, better mixing, and a more compact corpus representation compared to HDP-LDA.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with effective figures (notably Figures 1 and 2) and solid positioning in related work.  
- Claim of “state-of-the-art predictive performance” is overstated—BNBP often has higher perplexity than HDP-LDA for moderate η and results are sensitive to η.  
- Conclusion that BNBP outperforms HDP-LDA when inferred topic counts are comparable requires stronger evidence.  
- Insufficient comparison and unclear distinction from related models like the negative binomial IBP and the “ice cream” buffet process.  
- The novelty of the EPPF derivation relative to existing EPPF derivation methods is not clearly explained.  
- Statement that the method removes need to tune parameters is misleading—hyperparameters (c, r, γ0) still require tuning.  
- Lacks analysis of model performance on highly imbalanced group sizes, an important practical consideration.  
- Experimental evaluation could be strengthened with more datasets and comparisons to additional recent nonparametric topic models.  
- The claim that the group-size-dependent EPPF derivation is unique needs a more detailed justification.  
- Overall: technically strong and valuable contribution; recommend acceptance if claims about predictive performance and distinctions from related work are clarified.
