- Introduces the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and derives its exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling.  
- EPPF enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, applied to topic modeling (BNBP topic model).  
- Extends exchangeable partition theory from single-set mixture models to grouped mixed-membership data, filling a theoretical gap.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous derivations, establishing the ECPF and EPPF and providing detailed proofs.  
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler analytically marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process, yielding closed-form updates.  
- Empirical results on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 show faster convergence, better mixing, and comparable or improved predictive performance versus HDP-LDA.  
- BNBP achieves a more compact corpus representation while maintaining predictive accuracy.  
- Novel derivation addresses prior need for truncation in BNBP inference by providing the marginal distribution for mixed-membership.  
- General method could be extended to other integer-valued processes (e.g., gamma-Poisson, gamma-negative binomial).  
- BNBP’s multiplicative mechanism for controlling topic count contrasts with HDP-LDA’s additive mechanism, offering a new modeling perspective.  
- Claim of state-of-the-art predictive performance is supported but would benefit from comparisons beyond HDP-LDA.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and experimentally reproducible details.  
- Lacks thorough discussion of computational complexity and scalability to very large datasets; sparse computation adaptation is only briefly mentioned.  
- Limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis beyond Dirichlet smoothing parameter η; needs systematic study of c, r_j, and γ0.  
- Novelty is clear relative to related BNBP work (Zhou et al. 2012; Heaukulani & Roy 2014) since prior work lacked EPPF and fully collapsed inference.  
- Practical advantages: simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and competitive predictive performance—valuable addition to nonparametric Bayesian topic modeling.  
- Main recommendations: broader experimental comparisons with recent topic models and a more detailed analysis of computational requirements and scalability.
