- Develops an EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership models via a group-size dependent model, deriving an ECPF then conditioning to obtain an EPPF.
- Key technical device: marginalize the beta process using a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation to get a fully factorized likelihood and ECPF.
- Uses Bayes’ rule on the ECPF to produce an EPPF and a simple prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling without truncation or slice variables.
- Instantiates the framework in topic modeling, producing a nonparametric topic model with multiplicative coupling between global and local popularity.
- Collapsed sampler is simple, cleanly derived (Eq. 12), and yields competitive predictive performance with relatively few topics.
- Theoretical derivations (Lemmas 2–4) are careful and appear sound, with clear augmentation and marginalization steps.
- Presents a useful random count matrix characterization: Poisson-distributed number of clusters, digamma-distributed column sums, and Dirichlet–multinomial row allocations.
- Prediction rule is intuitive and leads directly to the collapsed sampler; empirical claims match experimental results.
- Experiments compare to HDP-LDA (direct assignment) on three corpora and analyze convergence, mixing, and perplexity vs. topic count and η.
- Paper is generally clear but dense, with heavy and occasionally inconsistent notation and some minor typos.
- Some derivations are deferred to the appendix; more intuition in the main text (esp. on the digamma role and multiplicative shrinkage) would help readability.
- Several references are missing publication years.
- The explicit EPPF and truncation-free collapsed inference for BNBP mixed-membership models appears novel and potentially generalizable.
- The multiplicative shrinkage mechanism meaningfully differs from HDP’s additive mechanism.
- Contribution fills a notable gap—prior BNBP mixed-membership work lacked an EPPF and required truncation/slice sampling.
- Structural insights and the truncation-free sampler are practically valuable and may stimulate further work on count-based nonparametrics.
- Pros: EPPF derivation, fully collapsed Gibbs sampler, clear HDP distinction, useful count-matrix result, code and sensible experiments.
- Cons: baselines limited to HDP-LDA (no collapsed CRF or other nonparametric topic baselines), limited sensitivity analysis for c, γ0, and rj priors.
- Cons (cont.): scalability discussion is brief with no large-scale or parallelization experiments; presentation issues (notation, typos, missing years).
- Essential suggestions: add a collapsed HDP-LDA (CRF) baseline or discuss differences and include a small empirical check; add sensitivity study for c, γ0, and Gamma priors on rj.
- Recommended optional improvements: provide pseudocode and per-iteration complexity, expand intuition for multiplicative shrinkage, report standard errors and topic coherence, clarify implications of sample-size dependent EPPFs, and fix exposition/typos and reference years.
- Overall: recommend strong accept if a collapsed HDP-LDA baseline is added or convincingly discussed; otherwise, accept based on theoretical and practical contributions.
