Summary
The paper develops an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in the mixed-membership setting, where groups consist of exchangeable observations and share a potentially unbounded set of clusters. The key technical device is to first consider a group-size dependent model that treats per-group sample sizes as random, derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) by marginalizing the beta process, and then use Bayes’ rule to obtain an EPPF conditional on observed group sizes. This yields a prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling without truncation or slice variables. Instantiating the framework in topic modeling leads to a nonparametric Bayesian topic model with a multiplicative coupling between global and local popularity, a simple, collapsed sampler, and competitive predictive performance using comparatively few topics. The paper relates the results to the Chinese restaurant/EPPF literature [5], HDP and the Chinese restaurant franchise [6], and recent BNBP/IBP developments [10,11,16,17].

Quality
The theoretical development is careful and appears sound. The derivation of the ECPF (Lemma 2) and its use to obtain the EPPF (Lemma 3) are well motivated, with a clear augmentation via a beta–gamma–Poisson representation and fully factorized likelihood enabling marginalization. The random count matrix characterization with Poisson-distributed number of clusters, digamma-distributed column sums, and Dirichlet–multinomial row allocations (Eq. 8) is a useful structural result. The prediction rule (Lemma 4) is simple and intuitive, and the collapsed sampler for the topic model follows cleanly (Eq. 12). Experiments compare to HDP-LDA with direct assignment on three corpora and examine convergence, mixing, and perplexity vs. number of topics and η. Empirical claims are commensurate with the results.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear but dense. The notation is heavy and occasionally inconsistent (e.g., minor typos like “disjoint disjoint”). The notion of a sample-size dependent EPPF is explicitly acknowledged and placed in the context of [12], which is helpful. Some derivations are deferred to the appendix, which is reasonable, though additional intuition in the main text (especially around the digamma distribution role and the multiplicative shrinkage mechanism) would aid readability. References are missing years in several cases.

Originality
Providing an explicit EPPF and prediction rule for the BNBP in mixed-membership modeling, enabling fully collapsed inference without truncation, appears novel. The construction route—via a group-size dependent model to an ECPF, then conditioning to obtain the EPPF—offers a potentially general recipe for other integer-valued processes. The multiplicative shrinkage mechanism in the topic model sampler differs meaningfully from the HDP’s additive mechanism.

Significance
The contribution fills a notable gap: prior BNBP-based mixed-membership models required truncation or slice sampling and lacked an EPPF. A truncation-free, fully collapsed sampler is practically valuable, often improving mixing and simplifying implementation. The structural insights (Poisson number of clusters; digamma/Dirichlet–multinomial factorization) may catalyze further work on count-based nonparametrics and mixed-membership models beyond topic modeling.

Pros
- Derivation of an EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership modeling.
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler without truncation; simple update rule; good empirical convergence/mixing.
- Clear conceptual distinction from HDP-LDA (multiplicative vs. additive coupling), with empirical support.
- Useful random count matrix characterization (digamma + DirMult).
- Code availability and sensible experimental protocol across multiple datasets.

Cons
- Empirical baselines limited to HDP-LDA with direct assignment; no comparison to fully collapsed CRF samplers or alternative nonparametric topic models (e.g., DP-CRF implementations).
- Sensitivity analysis for c, γ0, and priors on rj is limited; the role of c in practice is not thoroughly explored.
- Scalability discussion is brief; no experiments on truly large-scale corpora or parallelization tests.
- Some presentation issues (heavy notation; minor typos; missing reference years) and limited intuitive explanations in the main text.

Suggestions for improvement
Essential:
- Include a comparison to a collapsed HDP-LDA sampler based on the Chinese restaurant franchise, or discuss expected differences and provide at least a small-scale empirical check to ensure the advantage is not an artifact of using a partially collapsed baseline.
- Add a short sensitivity study for c, γ0, and the Gamma priors on rj, reporting effects on topic counts, perplexity, and mixing.

Recommended (optional but beneficial):
- Provide pseudocode and per-iteration complexity in terms of tokens and active topics; clarify how sparsity is exploited.
- Expand the intuitive explanation of the multiplicative shrinkage factor n·k/(c+n·k+r·)·(n·jk+rj), including its impact on variance/mean of topic-usage counts across documents.
- Report standard errors for perplexity and include qualitative topics or coherence metrics to complement perplexity.
- Clarify implications of using sample-size dependent EPPFs: discuss the addition rule, exchangeability conditions, and when such dependence is desirable.
- Tighten exposition and fix minor typos; add missing years to references.

Overall rating: Strong accept if a baseline with a collapsed HDP-LDA is added or convincingly discussed; otherwise, accept. The theoretical advance and the practical sampler are both valuable, and the empirical evidence supports the claims about mixing and compactness with competitive predictive performance.
