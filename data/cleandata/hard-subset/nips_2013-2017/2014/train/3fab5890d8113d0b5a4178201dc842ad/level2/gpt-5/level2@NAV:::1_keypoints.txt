- Proposes an EPPF for mixed-membership models induced by the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) via an ECPF→EPPF size-randomization approach.
- Derives a group-size–dependent EPPF, a simple prediction rule, and a fully collapsed, truncation-free Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, weights, and the beta process.
- Applies the method to topic modeling and contrasts it theoretically and empirically with HDP-LDA.
- Builds on EPPF/mixture model literature (Pitman), HDP (Teh et al.), and count-process work (BNBP, gamma–Poisson, IBP), complementing Poisson–Kingman and negative-binomial process results.
- Technical development appears sound with carefully motivated derivations and closed-form sampling updates.
- Empirical evaluation across three corpora, multiple η settings, and convergence diagnostics is informative and reproducible (some proofs/impl. details in appendix/code).
- Notation is dense; the dependence of the EPPF on group sizes and implications for classic addition rules should be emphasized earlier.
- Minor expository fixes recommended (e.g., a running example of the count-matrix prior and the digamma distribution).
- Originality is high: first explicit integer-valued BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership enabling truncation-free, fully collapsed inference and a novel multiplicative shrinkage contrast to HDP’s additive scheme.
- Significant and useful: offers a faster-mixing alternative to HDP-LDA with competitive or better perplexity and a compact representation; ECPF→EPPF blueprint may generalize to other count processes.
- Experiments are limited to topic models; adding non-text grouped datasets would strengthen generality claims.
- Missing comparisons to NGGP/Pitman–Yor-based topic models and Poisson factorization baselines beyond HDP-LDA.
- Needs sensitivity analyses/ablations for priors (rj, c, γ0) and discussion of regimes (e.g., very small η) favoring HDP.
- Theoretical clarification requested on when the group-size–dependent EPPF approximates size-free behavior and sanity checks that recover known special cases.
- Acceptance arguments: novel and solid EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership, fully collapsed/truncation-free sampler with strong empirical performance, clear BNPM and topic-modeling impact.
- Rejection concerns: limited empirical breadth and baselines, heavy notation and deferred proofs, and potential issues/questions arising from group-size–dependent exchangeability.
