- Derives the previously unknown marginal distribution (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling fully collapsed inference in mixed-membership models.  
- Uses an auxiliary model with random group sizes to derive an ECPF, then conditions on observed sizes to obtain the EPPF and a simple predictive rule.  
- Produces a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out the infinite beta process, avoiding truncation approximations.  
- Novel and significant theoretical advance analogous to the Chinese restaurant process for the Dirichlet process.  
- Practical and efficient inference: sampler converges faster and mixes better than HDP-LDA direct assignment samplers.  
- Clear, well-written derivations with insightful comparison: BNBP’s multiplicative global×local popularity vs. HDP’s additive effect, explaining stronger shrinkage.  
- Strong empirical validation: state-of-the-art predictive performance on three corpora and more compact topic representations (fewer topics for given perplexity).  
- Weakness: implications of the EPPF’s group-size dependence are under-discussed, particularly for datasets with heterogeneous group sizes.  
- Weakness: final EPPF expression is complex with an intractable normalizing constant; more commentary on its intuition would help.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the beta process concentration parameter c is not addressed—unclear if it was fixed and how it was chosen.  
- Overall summary: excellent theoretical and practical contribution, well-supported experiments, minor discussion gaps; strong accept recommendation.
