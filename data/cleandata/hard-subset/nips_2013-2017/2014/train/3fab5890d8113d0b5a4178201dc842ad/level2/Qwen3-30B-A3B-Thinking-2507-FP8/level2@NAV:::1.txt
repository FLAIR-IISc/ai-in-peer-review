

This paper introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), addressing a significant gap in mixed-membership modeling. The authors derive the BNBP's EPPF by first randomizing group sizes and then applying Bayes' rule, which enables fully collapsed inference without truncating the infinite-dimensional beta process. The key contributions include: (1) a theoretically sound EPPF for BNBP that governs exchangeable random partitions of grouped data; (2) a prediction rule for simulating these partitions; and (3) a novel BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that outperforms HDP-LDA in convergence speed and mixing.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with rigorous theoretical derivations (Lemmas 1-4) and comprehensive experimental validation across three standard topic modeling corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12). The authors clearly establish how the BNBP topic model differs from existing approaches, particularly in its multiplicative control mechanism for topic shrinkage versus HDP-LDA's additive approach. The empirical results show the BNBP topic model converges in under 100 iterations (compared to HDP-LDA's 300+ iterations) and achieves comparable or better predictive performance with a more compact topic representation.

The paper excels in clarity with well-structured derivations and helpful visualizations (Figure 1). The significance is evident in the practical advantages: the BNBP model requires fewer hyperparameters, has simpler implementation, and provides state-of-the-art predictive performance. The authors appropriately acknowledge limitations, noting the BNBP's sampling is less straightforward to parallelize than finite-truncation methods.

While the EPPF derivation is complex, the authors make it accessible through clear intermediate steps. The related work is well-cited and contextualized against recent advances in nonparametric Bayesian modeling, including the HDP and other integer-valued stochastic processes. The paper's methodological contribution—using group-size randomization to derive EPPFs—is particularly valuable for future work on mixed-membership modeling.

The main weakness is the lack of comparison with other nonparametric topic models beyond HDP-LDA (e.g., IBP-based approaches), though this might be justified by the paper's specific focus. Overall, this is a high-quality contribution that advances the state of the art in nonparametric Bayesian modeling with clear theoretical and practical significance. The paper should be accepted for presentation at NIPS.
