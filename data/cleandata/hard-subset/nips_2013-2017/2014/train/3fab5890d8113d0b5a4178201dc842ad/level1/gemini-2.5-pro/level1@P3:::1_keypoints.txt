- Derives the EPPF for the BNBP, giving the marginal distribution of data partitions after integrating out the beta process.
- Uses an ECPF for random group sizes and Bayes' rule to condition on observed group sizes to obtain the EPPF.
- Yields a simple predictive distribution and enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP models.
- Constructs a BNBP topic model as a nonparametric alternative to LDA/HDP and contrasts update rules.
- Identifies a key modeling difference: BNBP combines global/local topic popularity multiplicatively, HDP does so additively.
- Experiments on three corpora show faster convergence, better mixing, competitive perplexity, and more compact topic representations.
- Novel theoretical contribution: places BNBP on par with DPs by enabling collapsed inference and suggests generalizable techniques.
- Practical inference advance: simple prediction rule and straightforward fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with good mixing and easy implementation.
- Clear exposition and insightful comparison with HDP-LDA that explains differing shrinkage behaviors.
- Strong empirical validation with trace plots and perplexity results supporting the method's benefits.
- EPPF complexity: normalization constant over partitions makes the prior hard to reason about; more intuition or discussion is needed.
- Empirical baseline choice: comparison to the HDP direct assignment sampler (partially collapsed) rather than the fully collapsed CRF is a limitation.
- Limited exploration of BNBP hyperparameters (notably concentration c and dispersion rj); sensitivity analysis is missing.
- Overall: high-quality, original, and significant paper with minor weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
