This paper develops a group-size–dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and leverages it to design a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership models, with a focus on topic modeling. Building on the beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, the authors first derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) by marginalizing the beta process, show a random count–matrix representation with Poisson-distributed columns whose sizes follow a digamma distribution and within-column allocations follow a Dirichlet–multinomial, and then obtain the EPPF via Bayes’ rule. A simple prediction rule enables collapsed sampling without truncation. The resulting BNBP topic model marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process, and is contrasted empirically with LDA and HDP-LDA. The work relates to CRP/EPPFs (Pitman), HDP and the Chinese restaurant franchise (Teh et al.), IBP-style constructions, and recent BNBP/GNBP work that relied on truncation or slice sampling (e.g., Zhou et al.; Heaukulani & Roy). The σ-stable Poisson–Kingman marginal samplers (Lomelí et al.) do not include the BNBP, underscoring novelty.

Quality: The technical development is careful and substantial. The augmentation, marginalization steps, and prediction rule are coherent, and the topic model’s collapsed sampler is derived cleanly. Experiments on three corpora show faster convergence and good mixing vs. HDP’s direct-assignment sampler, and competitive or better perplexity at comparable topic counts. However, the empirical comparison omits the fully collapsed HDP (CRF) sampler and other strong count models (e.g., Poisson factorization), which limits conclusions about state-of-the-art status. Sensitivity to γ0, c and the role of rj is argued qualitatively; a systematic ablation would strengthen claims.

Clarity: The paper is generally well organized, but notation is dense. The dependence of the EPPF on group sizes (and implications for addition rules) deserves a clearer discussion, and the “digamma” distribution could be more intuitively motivated. Minor formatting glitches appear.

Originality: High. Providing an EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership is new, and the general pathway from ECPF to EPPF via group-size randomization is potentially reusable for other integer-valued processes.

Significance: High. A truncation-free, fully collapsed sampler for BNBP mixed-membership models is practically valuable and conceptually advances count-based nonparametrics.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel EPPF/prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership
- Fully collapsed, simple sampler with fast mixing
- Clear relationship to and distinction from HDP/CRF and IBP lines
- Strong potential for extension to other processes

Cons against acceptance:
- Missing comparison to collapsed HDP (CRF) and Poisson factorization baselines
- Limited sensitivity analyses (γ0, c, rj) and scalability discussion
- Dense exposition; some definitions could be clarified further
