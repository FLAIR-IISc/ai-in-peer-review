- Derives marginal laws (ECPF and EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership settings via group-size randomization and Poisson–gamma augmentation.
- Introduces a group-size dependent construction, derives an ECPF jointly modeling group sizes and partitions (Eq.6), and conditions to obtain an EPPF for fixed group sizes (Eq.9).
- Provides a simple prediction rule (Eq.10) that facilitates simulation and fully collapsed Gibbs sampling.
- Shows the latent count matrix has i.i.d. columns with column counts from a “digamma” distribution and Dirichlet–multinomial group allocations (Eq.8); yields a Poisson law for KJ.
- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a nonparametric BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process with a concise update (Eq.12).
- Empirical comparisons to LDA and HDP-LDA (direct-assignment) show faster mixing/convergence and competitive or better perplexity on three corpora.
- Theoretical derivations use standard beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and appear correct and insightful; main proofs are deferred to the appendix.
- The EPPF is sample-size dependent (acknowledged) — this should be emphasized and connected explicitly to Pitman (2006) and Zhou & Walker (2014).
- Inference advantage: fully collapsed sampler is a practical improvement over HDP direct-assignment samplers that do not marginalize key parameters.
- Experiments use standard perplexity evaluation with multiple η settings and splits, supporting claims about convergence and compactness.
- Minor methodological concern: sensitivity to hyperparameters (notably c) is not explored; γ0 is inferred but c appears fixed.
- Baselines are limited: no comparison to fully collapsed HDP-CRF or Pitman–Yor variants; evaluation is confined to text/topic modeling.
- Clarity is generally good; the core idea (randomize group sizes → ECPF → condition → EPPF) is clear and useful.
- Suggested clarifications: explicitly state sample-size dependence of EPPF early, define the “digamma distribution” carefully, and provide intuition for Eq.8.
- Reproducibility suggestion: include concise pseudocode for the collapsed sampler and fix minor typos/notation inconsistencies (e.g., “perdition” → “prediction”, KJ vs K).
- Originality: novel and nontrivial derivation of ECPF/EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership models; the conditioning recipe is broadly applicable.
- Practical significance: simple, fast, and well-mixing sampler that yields compact topic representations and is likely extensible to other integer-valued processes/modalities.
- Limitations: narrow empirical scope, missing baselines, and some accessibility issues due to deferred proofs and notation choices.
- Specific reviewer requests: discuss/hyperprior c and γ0/rj, consider sampling c and η, compare runtime/mixing to collapsed HDP-CRF, and empirically validate the Poisson law for KJ.
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with a practical sampler and solid empirical evidence; reviewer is highly confident.
