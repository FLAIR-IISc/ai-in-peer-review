- Presents a novel mixed-membership modeling approach using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Main contribution: derivation of the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP.  
- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance.  
- Demonstrates unified and flexible treatment of mixed-membership modeling, applied to topic modeling as a case study.  
- Empirical results show state-of-the-art predictive performance with a compact corpus representation.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides clear notation and mathematical derivations.  
- Thorough literature review and clear discussion of the EPPF’s relationship to the BNBP.  
- High overall quality and clarity; theoretical framework and methodology are well-explained.  
- Limited experimental scope: only three corpora, raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with other nonparametric Bayesian methods (e.g., gamma–Poisson process).  
- Possible downside: may be too specialized for a general audience.  
- Significance: potential broad impact across topic modeling, image analysis, genomics, and other problems.  
- Originality: the EPPF derivation for BNBP is a significant and novel contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept—contributions outweigh limitations despite experimental scope concerns.
