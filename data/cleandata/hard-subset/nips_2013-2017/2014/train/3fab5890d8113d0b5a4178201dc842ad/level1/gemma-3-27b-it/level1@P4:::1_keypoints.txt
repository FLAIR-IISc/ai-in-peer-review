- Introduces an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling for a BNBP topic model.  
- Builds on nonparametric Bayesian mixture models (DP, HDP) and addresses prior BNBP inference limitations (truncation/complex methods).  
- Core contribution is a non-trivial, apparently rigorous derivation of the BNBP EPPF.  
- Provides a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form update equations, a significant practical contribution.  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior perplexity to HDP-LDA with more compact topic representations.  
- Analysis indicates faster convergence and better mixing for the BNBP sampler versus HDP-LDA.  
- Discussion of related work is comprehensive and Matlab code is provided for reproducibility.  
- Presentation is dense and may be hard to follow for readers not familiar with the mathematical machinery.  
- The intuition behind BNBP’s multiplicative control versus HDP-LDA’s additive control needs clearer explanation.  
- Performance gains over HDP-LDA are modest; the paper lacks detailed analysis of when BNBP substantially outperforms HDP-LDA.  
- The "state-of-the-art" predictive performance claim requires stronger justification and broader empirical comparison.  
- Evaluation scope is limited—comparisons are primarily to HDP-LDA rather than a wider set of topic models.  
- Parallelization is mentioned but not developed or demonstrated, so practical scalability remains unexplored.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand empirical comparisons.
