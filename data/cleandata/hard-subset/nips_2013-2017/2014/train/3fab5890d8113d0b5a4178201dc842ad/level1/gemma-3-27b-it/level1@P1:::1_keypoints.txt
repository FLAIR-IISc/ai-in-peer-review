- Introduces an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling for a new nonparametric Bayesian topic model.
- Addresses a gap in marginal distributions for integer-valued processes like the BNBP, which hindered mixed-membership modeling for grouped data.
- Derives the EPPF, provides a prediction rule, and demonstrates the resulting topic model empirically.
- Builds on solid nonparametric-Bayesian literature (Dirichlet processes, EPPFs, HDP) and acknowledges related work (negative binomial IBP, “ice cream” buffet).
- Novelty: the BNBP EPPF derivation is a significant contribution and potentially generalizable to other integer-valued processes.
- Technical soundness: rigorous mathematical development, complete derivations, and well-defined Gibbs sampler with supporting lemmas.
- Completeness: problem definition, theoretical framework, closed-form updates, convergence analysis, and empirical validation are all provided.
- Experimental evaluation: competitive performance on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS datasets with favorable perplexity and convergence versus HDP-LDA.
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized, though some sections are dense with notation.
- Mathematical density: heavy notation and formalism may impede accessibility; more intuitive explanations would help.
- Hyperparameter discussion: choices for (a0, b0, e0, f0) lack rationale and there is no thorough sensitivity analysis; η’s effect is underexplored.
- Computational cost: claims of comparable complexity to HDP-LDA lack detailed analysis or runtime scaling comparisons with corpus/vocabulary size.
- Experimental scope: evaluation is limited to topic modeling; additional mixed-membership tasks would strengthen applicability claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, substantial contribution (EPPF derivation), and strong experimental results.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to density, limited experimental scope, and insufficient hyperparameter/computational analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, add hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and broaden or justify experimental scope.
