- Introduces the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) EPPF for mixed-membership grouped data, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Derives the EPPF by first obtaining an ECPF for random group sizes and conditioning on observed sizes via Bayes’ rule.  
- Enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the infinite beta process, offering a simple implementation.  
- Proposes a BNBP topic model where topic assignment uses a multiplicative global×local popularity interaction, unlike the HDP’s additive form.  
- Empirical results on three corpora show faster convergence, better mixing, state-of-the-art held-out perplexity, and more compact topic representations than HDP-LDA.  
- Theoretical derivations are dense but appear sound and yield a clear predictive probability rule (Equation 10).  
- Experimental methodology is standard and thorough, including evaluation across hyperparameter ranges.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized, with clear comparisons to LDA and HDP via conditional equations.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: include more intuition in the main text for the ECPF (Lemma 2) to explain the Gamma-function terms.  
- Highly original: novel derivation of the BNBP EPPF and a creative ECPF-based technique that could generalize to other processes.  
- Significant theoretically: places BNBP on firm combinatorial footing comparable to the Dirichlet process/CRP.  
- Significant practically: provides an efficient, high-performing alternative to HDP-LDA likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical result, elegant and practical sampler, strong empirical advantages, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments are limited to topic modeling/perplexity and do not explore other grouped-data tasks or metrics like topic coherence.  
- Additional concern: sensitivity to beta-process hyperparameters (e.g., concentration c) is not fully investigated in experiments.
