- Introduces a novel BNBP-based mixed-membership model and derives a well-defined EPPF for exchangeable random partitions.  
- Derivation of the EPPF is achieved via a group-size dependent model, filling a gap in prior BNBP work.  
- The EPPF enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form update equations.  
- The proposed sampler shows fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.  
- Connects BNBP to existing nonparametric Bayesian frameworks (e.g., CRP, HDP, integer-valued processes).  
- Provides a derived prediction rule and practical algorithms as valuable contributions.  
- Thorough theoretical development and clear presentation of derivations and updates.  
- Empirical evaluation on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 shows competitive perplexity and advantages over HDP-LDA.  
- Demonstrates a more compact representation and faster convergence than HDP-LDA (Figures 2 & 3).  
- Matlab code availability enhances reproducibility.  
- Paper clarity suffers from dense mathematical notation; more intuitive explanations would help readers.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of scenarios where BNBP outperforms HDP-LDA and discussion of computational costs beyond iteration time (e.g., memory).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept — strengths and rigor outweigh minor clarity concerns.
