- Introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) to enable mixed-membership modeling.  
- Develops a group-size dependent EPPF that explicitly characterizes how BNBP clusters data across groups.  
- Novel methodological step: randomizes group sizes before deriving the EPPF to generalize EPPF to grouped data.  
- Builds on Pitman’s EPPF framework and prior BNBP work (Zhou et al.) but extends them to grouped/multi-set settings.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical contributions: derivation of the EPPF, prediction rule, and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.  
- Empirically, the BNBP topic model converges faster, mixes better, and achieves state-of-the-art predictive performance with more compact topic representations than HDP-LDA.  
- Presentation and clarity are excellent, with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Originality is strong: the group-size dependent EPPF derivation and the BNBP topic model offer a distinct approach from existing nonparametric topic models.  
- Significance is high: fills a theoretical gap in mixed-membership modeling and has practical benefits for applications like topic modeling.  
- Minor weaknesses: limited discussion of computational complexity comparisons and insufficient contextualization of BNBP’s multiplicative shrinkage relative to other nonparametric priors.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, novel, and significant—strong recommendation for acceptance to NIPS.
