## Review of "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"

This paper introduces a novel approach to mixed-membership modeling using the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), addressing a key limitation of existing BNBP implementations – the lack of a defined marginal distribution for exchangeable random partitions of grouped data. The authors derive an Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the BNBP, enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling and leading to a new nonparametric Bayesian topic model.

The work builds upon the established foundation of nonparametric Bayesian methods like the Dirichlet Process and Normalized Random Measures with Independent Increments (NRMIs), referencing key papers like Ferguson (1973) and Lijoi et al. (2008). It correctly identifies the gap in existing BNBP implementations (Zhou et al., 2011; Broderick et al., 2013) which relied on truncation or slice sampling, and positions the EPPF as a step towards a unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling analogous to the EPPF model for single datasets (Pitman, 2006). The derivation of the EPPF, through randomization of group sizes, is a significant contribution, and the resulting Gibbs sampler appears well-defined.

**Strengths:** The paper is technically sound, presenting a clear derivation of the EPPF and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler. The novelty lies in providing a principled way to handle grouped data with the BNBP, overcoming limitations of previous approaches. The experimental results on standard corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS) demonstrate competitive performance, with the BNBP topic model achieving comparable or better perplexity than HDP-LDA (Teh et al., 2006) with a more compact representation. The authors also provide a thorough comparison with related work, highlighting the differences in shrinkage mechanisms between the BNBP and HDP-LDA.

**Weaknesses:** While the derivations are detailed, the paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the EPPF and its implications. The notation is dense, and readers unfamiliar with the BNBP and related concepts might find it challenging to follow. The experimental section, while comprehensive, lacks a discussion of the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter settings beyond η. A more detailed ablation study exploring the impact of the dispersion parameter 'r' would also be valuable.  The claim of "state-of-the-art" performance should be tempered, as the improvements over HDP-LDA are modest and depend on the choice of η.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound derivation of the BNBP EPPF.
*   Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler enabling efficient inference.
*   Competitive performance on standard topic modeling benchmarks.
*   Addresses a significant limitation of existing BNBP implementations.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Dense notation and potentially challenging for readers unfamiliar with the field.
*   Experimental analysis could be more comprehensive, particularly regarding hyperparameter sensitivity.
*   "State-of-the-art" claim is somewhat overstated.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The technical soundness, novelty, and empirical validation make it a worthwhile addition to NIPS. While improvements in clarity and experimental analysis would strengthen the paper, the core contribution is significant and addresses an important open problem. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental analysis.
