- Main contribution: Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling exchangeable random partitions for mixed-membership modeling.  
- Novel method: Uses a group size-dependent model plus Bayes’ rule to obtain the EPPF — a new, generalizable approach for integer-valued stochastic processes.  
- Theoretical results: Presents Lemma 2 (ECPF for BNBP), Lemma 3 (EPPF, Eq. 9), and Lemma 4 (prediction rule, Eq. 10).  
- Practical application: Develops a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes beta process, topics, and topic weights.  
- Empirical performance: Shows faster convergence, better mixing, and competitive or improved predictive performance on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 corpora.  
- Model behavior: BNBP’s multiplicative shrinkage yields fewer, more compact topics compared to HDP-LDA.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous derivations with proofs; technically sound and complete theoretical development.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear exposition and intuitive interpretation of results.  
- Originality: Novel theoretical advance — prior work on BNBP did not derive an EPPF for mixed-membership modeling.  
- Significance: Fills an important gap in nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership modeling and provides a practical alternative to HDP-LDA.  
- Strengths: Strong theoretical contribution, clear connection to applications, convincing empirical evidence, and easy-to-implement inference.  
- Weaknesses: Empirical comparisons are limited (primarily vs. HDP-LDA); lacks direct comparison to more recent NIPS 2013–2014 topic models and discussion of when to prefer BNBP over alternatives (e.g., hierarchical gamma process).  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance based on substantial theoretical advance and practical benefits.
