- Develops a group-size-dependent EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership settings via beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, deriving an ECPF→EPPF pathway and a compact prediction rule enabling fully collapsed, truncation-free Gibbs sampling.

- Applies the method to a nonparametric topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process; compares primarily to LDA and HDP-LDA (direct assignment).

- Positions the work relative to classic EPPFs, HDP, BNBP/GNBP literature and related beta-process marginalization efforts, emphasizing a focus on token partitions within groups rather than feature allocations.

- Technical strength: principled derivation of a group-size-dependent EPPF with finite-dimensional distributions and a simple prediction rule, filling a gap in BNBP mixed-membership modeling.

- Inference strength: fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that integrates out the beta process and topic variables, with a multiplicative popularity term that contrasts with HDP’s additive shrinkage.

- Empirical strength: experiments on three corpora show faster convergence and better mixing than HDP-LDA direct assignment, and competitive or better perplexity when matched on effective topic count.

- Clarity strength: the augmentation and connection between Poisson factorization and mixture modeling are well explained and insightful.

- Major concern: the EPPF’s explicit sample-size dependence requires more discussion on projectivity, predictive coherence across different group sizes/subsets, and practical implications for exchangeability.

- Limited empirical comparisons: lacks collapsed HDP (CRF) baseline, slice/adaptive truncation BNBP/GNBP baselines, and Poisson factorization baselines to substantiate mixing and computational claims.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity missing: concentration c appears fixed and not inferred; need sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, and η and systematic reporting of inferred K alongside perplexity.

- Perplexity computation unclear: need explicit clarification of normalization (document-wise / per MCMC sample) to ensure fair comparisons with HDP-LDA.

- Reproducibility issues: request pseudocode, full update equations beyond the assignment rule, and quantitative tables (means/variances for perplexity, K, runtimes) rather than figures alone.

- Theoretical clarity: more intuition required about the digamma-derived column-size distribution and when multiplicative shrinkage should outperform HDP’s additive mechanism.

- Derivations and methodology are sound and connect to known augmentations, but “state-of-the-art” claims should be tempered pending broader empirical validation.

- Readability: paper is dense; a notation table and concise algorithm box would improve accessibility, and the role/inference of c and predictive normalization should be clarified.

- Originality: high — novel group-size-dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership and a fully collapsed beta-process-marginalized sampler.

- Significance: high — provides a new tool beyond HDP with potential impact on topic modeling and grouped-count applications; the ECPF→EPPF recipe may generalize to other integer-valued processes.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid EPPF development; truncation-free collapsed inference with practical benefits; empirical evidence of improved mixing and compact representations; generalizable methodology.

- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient baseline comparisons, missing sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters, and unclear exposition of sample-size dependence and predictive normalization.

- Suggested revisions: add collapsed HDP-LDA (CRF) and Poisson factorization baselines (and slice/blocked BNBP/GNBP where possible); report quantitative summaries with variances; perform sensitivity analyses for c and γ0; provide pseudocode, update equations, and algorithmic complexity; expand discussion on exchangeability/projectivity.
