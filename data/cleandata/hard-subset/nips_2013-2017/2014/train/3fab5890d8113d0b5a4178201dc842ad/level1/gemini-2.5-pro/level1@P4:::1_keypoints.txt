- Main contribution: derives the EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership modeling, enabling fully collapsed, truncation-free inference.
- Approach: introduce a sample-size-dependent model, derive its ECPF, then use Bayes’ rule to obtain the EPPF for fixed observed group sizes.
- Practical outcome: construct a BNBP topic model and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that does not require truncation.
- Predictive rule: although the EPPF is complex, the authors extract a simple, implementable predictive rule from it.
- Theoretical soundness: derivations are correct and well-motivated, using a beta-gamma-Poisson augmentation and linking to known distributions.
- Empirical performance: sampler shows faster convergence and better mixing than HDP-LDA direct assignment; perplexity is competitive and more compact when topic counts are comparable.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and logically organized, with particularly effective comparison of update equations to LDA/HDP-LDA.
- Minor clarity issue: the formidable EPPF (Eq.9) would benefit from a brief intuitive explanation of its terms.
- Originality: first derivation of the BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership models, solving a known open problem with a potentially generalizable technique.
- Novel modeling mechanism: introduces multiplicative shrinkage for sharing strength across groups, contrasting with HDP’s additive mechanism.
- Theoretical significance: provides a combinatorial treatment for BNBP analogous to the CRP for the DP, advancing understanding of integer-valued processes.
- Practical significance: offers an easy-to-implement, efficient sampler and a new tool for parsimonious topic modeling with interpretability benefits.
- Future work: method could be applied to other processes (e.g., gamma-Poisson) and to further study multiplicative shrinkage properties.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical result, practical algorithm, clear empirical advantages, technically sound and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: “state-of-the-art” predictive claim may be overstated given HDP sometimes attains lower perplexity; experiments compare to only one HDP sampler variant.
