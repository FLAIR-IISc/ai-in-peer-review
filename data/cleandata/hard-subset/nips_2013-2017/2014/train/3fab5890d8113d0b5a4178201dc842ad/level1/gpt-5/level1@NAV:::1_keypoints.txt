- Develops a group-size–dependent EPPF for the BNBP and uses it to design a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership/topic models.
- Derives an ECPF by marginalizing the beta process via beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation; shows a random count-matrix with Poisson columns, column sizes following a digamma distribution, and within-column allocations Dirichlet–multinomial; obtains the EPPF by Bayes’ rule.
- Proposes a simple prediction rule that enables collapsed sampling without truncation.
- Constructs a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process, and contrasts it empirically with LDA and HDP-LDA.
- Technical development is careful and substantial; augmentation, marginalization, prediction rule, and sampler derivation are coherent and clean.
- Empirical results on three corpora show faster convergence and better mixing than HDP’s direct-assignment sampler, with competitive or better perplexity at comparable topic counts.
- Lacks comparison to the fully collapsed HDP (CRF) sampler and to strong count-model baselines (e.g., Poisson factorization), limiting claims about state-of-the-art performance.
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (γ0, c) and the role of rj are argued qualitatively; a systematic ablation and scalability analysis are missing.
- Notation is dense; the dependence of the EPPF on group sizes, implications for addition rules, and intuitive motivation for the “digamma” distribution need clearer discussion.
- Minor formatting glitches present.
- Originality is high: first EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership and a reusable pathway from ECPF to EPPF via group-size randomization.
- Significance is high: provides a practical, truncation-free, fully collapsed sampler that advances count-based nonparametrics.
- Pros for acceptance: novel EPPF/prediction rule, simple fully collapsed sampler with fast mixing, clear relation/distinction to HDP/CRF and IBP, strong potential for extensions.
- Cons against acceptance: missing key empirical baselines (collapsed HDP, Poisson factorization), limited sensitivity/scalability analysis, and dense exposition needing clearer definitions.
