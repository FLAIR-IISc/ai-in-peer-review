## Review of "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"

This paper introduces a novel approach to mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), addressing a key limitation of existing BNBP implementations – the lack of a well-defined marginal distribution for exchangeable random partitions of grouped data. The authors derive an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling for a new nonparametric Bayesian topic model. This results in a sampler with fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.

The work builds upon the well-established framework of exchangeable random partitions (e.g., the Chinese Restaurant Process [4, 5]) and extends it to the BNBP context, filling a gap identified in previous work [7, 10, 11]. The derivation of the EPPF through a group-size dependent model is a significant contribution, offering a principled way to handle the complexities of mixed-membership data.  The paper effectively connects the BNBP to existing literature on nonparametric Bayesian methods, including the hierarchical Dirichlet process [6] and related integer-valued stochastic processes [8, 9].  The derived prediction rule and the fully collapsed Gibbs sampler are valuable practical contributions.

A key strength is the thoroughness of the theoretical development, clearly presenting the derivation of the EPPF and the closed-form update equations. The empirical evaluation on three standard corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12) demonstrates competitive performance, particularly in terms of perplexity. The authors convincingly show the BNBP topic model’s benefits over HDP-LDA, notably its compact representation and faster convergence, as illustrated in Figures 2 & 3. The provided Matlab code availability further enhances reproducibility. 

However, the paper's clarity could be improved in certain sections. The mathematical notation, while generally sound, can be dense, making it challenging for readers unfamiliar with the intricacies of nonparametric Bayesian methods to follow the derivations.  More intuitive explanations alongside the equations would be beneficial. While the comparison to HDP-LDA is helpful, further analysis exploring the specific scenarios where the BNBP model shines would strengthen the conclusions. A brief discussion of computational cost beyond iteration time (e.g. memory usage) would also be useful.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF.
*   Practical contribution of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with favorable computational properties.
*   Competitive performance on standard benchmark datasets.
*   Clear connection to existing literature and identified gap in previous work.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Mathematical notation can be dense and challenging to follow.
*   Further analysis of the conditions under which the BNBP outperforms HDP-LDA would be valuable.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance. The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling, offering a valuable new tool for mixed-membership analysis.  The strengths of the work outweigh the minor clarity concerns, and the thoroughness and rigor demonstrated throughout the paper make it a strong addition to the NIPS conference.
