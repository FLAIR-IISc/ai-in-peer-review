- Derives an ECPF and group-size dependent EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling collapsed mixed-membership inference for grouped count data.  
- Integrates out the beta process to obtain a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix with a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns.  
- Shows column sizes follow a “digamma” distribution and within-column allocations follow a Dirichlet–multinomial.  
- Uses Bayes’ rule to produce a simple, interpretable prediction rule for allocations.  
- Develops a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process without truncation.  
- Empirically reports faster mixing and convergence than direct-assignment HDP-LDA and competitive/better perplexity on three corpora, often with fewer topics.  
- Technical novelty: first derivation of an EPPF/prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership and a generalizable ECPF→EPPF technique.  
- Practical inference contribution: collapsed, truncation-free sampler and simple prediction rule valuable for practitioners.  
- Conceptual clarity: neatly links the beta–gamma–Poisson representation to mixed-membership and highlights multiplicative global/local popularity vs HDP’s additive mechanism.  
- Potential impact: approach likely extensible to other integer-valued processes and could stimulate further theory/methods.  
- Limited experimental baselines: lacks comparison to a fully collapsed CRF HDP sampler and to related count-process models (e.g., gamma–Poisson, GNBP).  
- Narrow evaluation metrics: relies solely on perplexity; missing qualitative topic analysis, wall-clock-to-convergence, and memory/scalability results.  
- Sensitivity concerns: results depend strongly on η; limited discussion and guidance on sensitivity to c, γ0, and priors on rj.  
- Theoretical caveat: EPPF is sample-size dependent, deviating from classic EPPFs; implications for exchangeability and prediction across group sizes need fuller treatment.  
- Clarity and notation: manuscript is dense; “digamma distribution” is nonstandard and needs intuition/properties; a running toy example would help.  
- Quality assessment: technical development appears sound and coherent, with plausible proofs in the appendix; more baselines and ablations would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: add an algorithm box for the collapsed sampler, a synthetic example illustrating the prediction rule and rj effects, and clarify sample-size dependence implications.  
- Originality: high — novel derivation and useful collapsed sampler that marginalizes the beta process.  
- Significance: high — enables truncation-free, collapsed inference for a prominent count-based nonparametric prior with practical modeling distinctions.  
- Requests for authors: include collapsed HDP-LDA (CRF) results, wall-clock and ESS diagnostics, sensitivity analyses for c/γ0/rj, details on the digamma distribution, and discussion of extensibility to gamma–Poisson/GNBP.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantive theoretical and algorithmic contributions, practical benefits, and encouraging empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental baselines/metrics, dense notation and nonstandard distributions limiting accessibility, and unresolved conceptual issues around sample-size dependent EPPF.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept, contingent on stronger experimental comparisons and clearer exposition.
