- Paper derives a group-size dependent EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models via a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and Poissonization of group sizes.  
- The authors obtain an ECPF for joint law of group sizes and partitions, then condition on observed sizes to get the EPPF and a simple prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling.  
- Theory: Augmentation and factorization are carefully done; Lemma 1 and the ECPF/EPPF derivations are plausible and consistent with resulting digamma and Dirichlet–multinomial structure.  
- The prediction rule intuitively combines global popularity (n·k) and local popularity (njk) with dispersion rj, plus a new-cluster term involving γ0 and c, though some constants are unclear.  
- Algorithm: The fully collapsed sampler marginalizes topics, document weights, and the beta process, producing a simple, easy-to-implement update; closed-form parameter updates are in the appendix.  
- Experiments: Compared to HDP-LDA, BNBP shows faster convergence/mixing, competitive or better held-out perplexity, and produces more compact topic representations; code is provided.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense but generally clear to experts; several presentation issues exist (hard-to-read equations, typos, missing reference years, footnotes breaking flow).  
- Ambiguity: The exact normalization/constants in the new-cluster probability and the treatment/update of c (fixed vs inferred) need explicit clarification.  
- Originality: Main novelty is the “randomize-then-condition” derivation of a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP and the resulting fully collapsed sampler, which prior BNBP work did not provide.  
- Interpretation: The multiplicative shrinkage mechanism versus HDP’s additive mechanism is a useful and novel perspective.  
- Significance: Provides a principled collapsed inference algorithm for a common nonparametric count prior with potential to extend to other integer-valued processes.  
- Weakness — baselines: Comparison lacks a fully collapsed CRF HDP-LDA baseline and other relevant baselines like Pitman–Yor or other NB-based models.  
- Weakness — sensitivity: Hyperparameter sensitivity (γ0, c) and whether c is inferred are not fully explored; posterior/updates for c should be provided or sensitivity analyzed.  
- Weakness — empirical scope: Only perplexity reported; missing qualitative topic coherence, sparsity analyses, and broader scalability/runtime experiments.  
- Notational/detail issues: Lévy measure notation for the beta process and the uncommon "digamma distribution" need clearer conventions and brief intuition/moments.  
- Pros summary: Strong theoretical advance (ECPF/EPPF), simple collapsed sampler, intuitive shrinkage interpretation, evidence of faster mixing and compact topics, and available code.  
- Cons summary: Missing comparisons to fully collapsed HDP-LDA and broader baselines, ambiguity in prediction-rule constants and c/γ0 inference, minor presentation errors, and limited empirical analyses.  
- Key questions for authors: Is c inferred and if so how; provide exact new-cluster probability including ψ terms; can recipe extend to GNBP/gamma–Poisson; behavior on very long/overdispersed documents; comparison to collapsed CRF HDP-LDA.  
- Minor fixes requested: Correct typos, add missing reference years, and clarify the role/intution of the “log-beta” random variable Q(A).  
- Overall recommendation and confidence: Accept (score 7) with reviewer confidence 0.8.
