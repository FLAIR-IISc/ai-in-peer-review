- Derives the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) for grouped data, enabling collapsed inference.  
- Uses randomization of group sizes to obtain the BNBP EPPF and generalizes existing EPPF derivation techniques.  
- Implements a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model with closed-form updates.  
- Empirical comparisons to HDP-LDA on standard corpora show faster convergence and comparable or better predictive performance with a more compact representation.  
- Positions the work within the literature on normalized random measures and EPPFs, extending prior BNBP and related nonparametric model work.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant, unlocking efficient inference beyond truncation-based or approximate methods.  
- Methodological novelty is strong, with a clever probabilistic approach to derive the EPPF.  
- Paper is generally well-written, with consistent notation and clear algorithm/parameter descriptions.  
- EPPF derivations are dense and sometimes hard to follow; more intermediate steps and explanations are needed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; needs sensitivity analyses (e.g., beta process c), more datasets, and comparisons to other nonparametric topic models.  
- Missing detailed computational cost analysis and wall-clock time comparisons to HDP-LDA.  
- The impact of the EPPF’s dependency on group sizes, especially with highly variable group sizes, is not thoroughly explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: major theoretical advance, practical sampler, positive empirical results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: derivation complexity may limit accessibility, experiments could be broader, and group-size dependency requires more discussion.  
- Overall: strong paper with valuable contribution; minor clarifications and expanded experiments would improve impact — recommendation: Strong Accept.
