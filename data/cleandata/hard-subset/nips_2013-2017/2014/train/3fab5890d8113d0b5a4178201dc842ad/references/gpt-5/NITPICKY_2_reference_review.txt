Summary
The paper studies the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data. Since directly deriving an EPPF for the BNBP is difficult, the authors propose a two-step approach: (i) introduce a sample-size dependent construction by randomizing group sizes to derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for the joint distribution of group sizes and partitions; (ii) marginalize the group sizes to obtain a group-size dependent EPPF via Bayes’ rule. They then derive a prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling. As an application, they present a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process, and compare it empirically against HDP-LDA on three corpora, claiming faster convergence, good mixing, and competitive perplexity with fewer topics.

Strengths
- Addresses an important gap: a principled EPPF-like treatment for mixed-membership models driven by integer-valued processes, specifically for the BNBP.
- The ECPF→EPPF route is conceptually neat and generalizable; the resulting prediction rule is simple and practically useful.
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model is of real practical interest; empirical evidence suggests faster mixing than the HDP direct-assignment sampler.
- Clear interpretation of how rj control dispersion and how BNBP combines global/local popularity multiplicatively vs the HDP’s additive form.

Weaknesses and issues (many are small but numerous)
Technical correctness/derivations:
- Levy measure of the beta process (Eq. (1)) appears to be missing a factor c: standard BP(c,B0) often has ν(dpdω) = c p^{-1}(1−p)^{c−1} dp B0(dω). If absorbed into B0, please state; otherwise subsequent expectations (e.g., Poisson mean γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)]) may be off by a factor.
- Posterior for topics in Sec. 4: “(φk|−) ∼ Dir(η + n1·k, …, η + nJ·k)” is dimensionally wrong. It should be over V terms, Dir(η + n1·k, …, η + nV·k). This is a serious error if not a typo.
- Parameterization inconsistencies for Gamma: earlier θjk ∼ Gamma[rj, pk/(1−pk)] (shape–rate?), later (θjk|−) ∼ Gamma(njk + rj, pk). Clarify whether pk is rate or scale and be consistent; currently the posterior expression does not match the stated prior parameterization.
- Prediction rule Eq. (10): the line immediately below contains a stray “ψ(c+∑j rj)−ψ(c)” and figure caption fragments; the new-cluster weight looks like γ0 rj/(c + r·), but the normalizer is unclear. Please provide the exact normalized probabilities and fix the formatting glitch.
- EPPF in Eq. (9): denominator is a sum over K′ and all partitions; not computationally usable but acceptable if used only for conceptual grounding. However, the constraints and sums (n·k′ ≥ 1) should be stated clearly; as written, it is hard to parse and verify.
- “Digamma distribution” (Eq. (8)): provide a clear derivation/reference for the PMF and support n ≥ 1; the 1/[ψ(c+r)−ψ(c)] normalization and the 1/n factor deserve more exposition. Cite Sibuya (1960) precisely.
- Exchangeability: since your “EPPF” explicitly depends on group sizes, call it “group-size dependent EPPF” consistently; note explicitly where the usual addition rule fails.
- Measure-theoretic details are deferred to the appendix; some crucial steps (e.g., Poissonization argument, Fubini/Tonelli justifications for swapping sums/integrals) should be briefly sketched to assure rigor.

Empirical evaluation:
- Perplexity formula: the expression shown is dimensionally inconsistent. Predictive probability for a test token should be averaged across samples as (1/S)∑s ∑k φ(s)vk θ(s)jk normalized per document; your denominator “∑s ∑v ∑k …” is not standard and seems incorrect. Please correct and re-run if needed.
- Baselines are limited: only HDP-LDA (direct assignment). Missing comparisons against other collapsed samplers for HDP (CRF-based), Poisson factorization variants (e.g., PFA, NB/PFA), and closely related BNBP topic models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2012/2014; Heaukulani & Roy).
- No uncertainty quantification (e.g., std across splits) on perplexities or number of topics. Claims of “state-of-the-art” are too strong given the limited baselines and lack of statistical testing.
- Hyperparameters c, γ0, rj: you state Gamma priors but do not show full posteriors/updates in the main text; sensitivity analysis is missing. How sensitive are results to e0, f0, a0, b0 and to η? Extreme η values are explored, but consequences like overfitting are not quantified.
- Runtime: wall-clock comparisons are anecdotal; report per-iteration cost vs K and total time to a given perplexity, with identical code optimizations (sparse updates) for fairness.

Clarity and presentation:
- Many typos/formatting issues: “perdition rule,” duplicated words (“disjoint disjoint”), broken sentence around Eq. (10), inconsistent bold/dot notation, undefined symbols in situ (e.g., DJ in Sec. 3.1), and incomplete figure references (no figures in submission).
- Several references lack years/venues; please provide complete bibliographic info. Distinguish clearly how your results differ from Heaukulani & Roy (2014) and from Zhou et al. (2014) on random count matrices and the negative binomial IBP.
- Using “Lemma” for central results downplays their importance; consider “Theorem” with precise statements and conditions.

Originality and relation to prior work:
- The ECPF→EPPF route for BNBP and the explicit prediction rule are valuable. However, the novelty relative to Heaukulani & Roy (combinatorial structure of BNBP), and to the recent work on random count matrices (Zhou et al. 2014) needs clearer positioning. Please delineate exactly what is new (e.g., the group-size dependent EPPF and fully collapsed sampler) vs what is already implicit in prior combinatorial analyses.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually interesting and potentially general method (via Poissonization) to obtain EPPFs for mixed-membership models based on integer-valued processes.
- Practical, fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with a simple prediction rule; promising convergence behavior.
- Insightful comparison of multiplicative vs additive shrinkage mechanisms (BNBP vs HDP).

Cons for acceptance
- Multiple technical inconsistencies/typos that affect core formulas (topic posterior dimensions, Gamma parameterization, perplexity computation).
- Levy measure constant ambiguity; could propagate to key quantities (Poisson mean for KJ).
- Evaluation limited in baselines and lacks statistical rigor; “state-of-the-art” claim not substantiated.
- Clarity issues and incomplete references; key proofs relegated to appendix with minimal sketch in main text.

Recommendation
Reject (borderline). The core idea is interesting and, with corrections, could be impactful. However, the accumulation of technical and presentation issues (some potentially affecting results) makes the current version too fragile. I encourage the authors to correct the mathematical inconsistencies (especially the Dirichlet posterior, Gamma parameterization, Levy measure, and perplexity computation), expand/clarify comparisons to closely related work, and tighten empirical evaluation.
