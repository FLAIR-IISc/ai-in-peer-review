- Paper derives the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) in a mixed-membership (grouped data) setting.
- Authors develop a prediction rule based on the BNBP EPPF and build a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form updates for a BNBP topic model.
- Empirical results on standard topic datasets show competitive perplexity and faster convergence compared to HDP-LDA, with a more compact model representation.
- Work connects to prior exchangeable partition models (CRP/EPPF) and to recent marginalizations of the beta process from the negative binomial process, filling a gap by providing an EPPF for grouped data.
- Theoretical contribution: deriving the BNBP EPPF enables efficient, fully collapsed inference without truncation.
- Methodological contribution: provides a general recipe for deriving EPPFs from integer-valued stochastic processes, potentially extendable to Gamma-Poisson and Gamma-Negative Binomial processes.
- Practical contribution: delivers a ready-to-use collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form updates for topic modeling.
- Presentation is generally clear and logical in its mathematical derivations.
- Main weakness: derivations are dense and may be hard to follow without substantial background in Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Experiments lack thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (notably a0, b0, e0, f0 and Dirichlet base η).
- Computational comparison to HDP-LDA is not fully quantified—runtime and memory tradeoffs and sparse LDA techniques are not rigorously compared.
- Evaluation is limited to topic modeling; broader mixed-membership applications (e.g., genetics, networks) are not explored.
- Notation is sometimes heavy and can be hard to track through dense derivations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel EPPF derivation, efficient collapsed inference, competitive empirical performance, and a general method useful for future work.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to mathematical complexity and incomplete empirical analyses (hyperparameter sensitivity and broader applications).
- Recommended revisions: add more intuitive explanations/ intermediate steps for EPPF and prediction rule, expand hyperparameter sensitivity experiments, and demonstrate at least one additional mixed-membership application.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and practical contribution to Bayesian nonparametrics with minor concerns about clarity and empirical scope.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on improving clarity and expanding empirical evaluation.
