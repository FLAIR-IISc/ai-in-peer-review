Summary
The paper develops a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). Because deriving the BNBP’s mixed-membership EPPF directly is difficult, the authors first construct a group-size dependent model (augmenting the BNBP with a beta–gamma–Poisson representation), derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) describing the joint law of random group sizes and their partitions (Eq. 6), marginalize to obtain a prior on a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix (Eq. 7–8), and then use Bayes’ rule to obtain the desired EPPF (Eq. 9). They further derive a simple prediction rule (Eq. 10) to simulate partitions governed by this EPPF.

Building on these results, the paper proposes a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that integrates out topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process (Eq. 12). Empirically, on three corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS), the sampler converges quickly and tends to achieve comparable or lower perplexity than HDP-LDA when matched for effective number of topics, while often using a more compact set of topics. The paper relates to DP/NRMI-based EPPFs (Pitman), HDP and its Chinese restaurant franchise, and prior work on the BNBP/negative binomial IBP and random count matrices. Unlike prior BNBP work that focused on infinite random count matrices, this paper targets exchangeable partitions of grouped data with fixed group sizes.

Quality
- Theory: The augmentation and marginalization steps are sound and consistent with known BNBP/gamma–Poisson constructions. The ECPF, random count matrix prior with Poisson number of columns and digamma–Dirichlet-multinomial factorization, and the group-size dependent EPPF are plausible and useful. While proofs are in the appendix, the main steps are clearly motivated.
- Prediction rule: The resulting rule has an intuitive multiplicative structure combining global popularity and group-specific usage via rj, distinct from the HDP’s additive form. This is a valuable insight for algorithm design.
- Inference: The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler is a practical contribution; integrating out the beta process and weights is nontrivial and yields a simple update.
- Experiments: The evaluation on three corpora is standard (heldout perplexity with word hold-out) and shows faster convergence and strong perplexity when controlling for topic count. However, details on HDP hyperparameter learning (e.g., α inference) and comparisons to stronger HDP baselines (e.g., CRF collapsed sampler or modern sparse implementations) are limited. Reporting variance/error bars and wall-clock comparisons would strengthen claims about convergence and mixing.

Clarity
- The paper is dense but generally well-organized. The augmentation-to-ECPF-to-EPPF pipeline is clear.
- Some notation is heavy; a compact notation table would help. The “digamma distribution” is defined, but a brief intuition and support clarification (n ≥ 1) would aid readability.
- Minor typos and wording issues (e.g., “perdition rule”, repeated words).
- The relationship to closely related BNBP combinatorial structures (e.g., Heaukulani & Roy 2014; Madrid-Padilla & Scott 2014) is acknowledged; a crisper contrast to those works (infinite count matrices vs fixed row sums and mixed-membership EPPF) would help readers.

Originality
- Deriving a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership settings is new and important. The construction via ECPF and Bayes’ rule appears to be a general recipe that could apply to other integer-valued processes.
- The collapsed BNBP topic sampler that marginalizes the beta process, topics, and weights is novel and practically useful.
- The observation about multiplicative vs additive shrinkage (BNBP vs HDP) offers fresh perspective.

Significance
- Theoretical: Provides a missing marginal description (EPPF) for the BNBP in mixed-membership modeling, filling a gap relative to DP/HDP. The Poisson–digamma–DirMult column model is useful beyond topic modeling.
- Practical: The collapsed sampler is simple to implement, converges quickly, and often uses fewer topics for comparable perplexity, which is attractive for compact modeling.
- Broader impact: The proposed method suggests a pathway to derive EPPFs for other integer-valued processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP), potentially catalyzing further work in nonparametric mixed-membership modeling.

Strengths
- Clear, principled derivation of an EPPF for BNBP with fixed group sizes.
- Intuitive prediction rule; insights into the role of dispersion parameters rj in controlling overdispersion.
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form updates; practical and fast.
- Solid empirical evidence on multiple corpora; good mixing and convergence.
- Potentially general methodology applicable to other processes.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Baselines: Comparison is primarily to HDP-LDA with a direct assignment sampler. Please add a CRF-collapsed HDP baseline and/or modern sparse collapsed baselines to better contextualize performance and mixing claims.
- Hyperparameters: Specify how α and other HDP hyperparameters are inferred; clarify whether c is learned for BNBP. Provide ablations on γ0, c, and priors on rj; include posterior summaries for rj to validate the overdispersion interpretation.
- Evaluation: Report standard deviations over splits, wall-clock per iteration and to convergence, and topic counts at the point perplexity is computed. Consider document completion setups used in recent topic modeling work.
- Clarity: Add a brief “roadmap” figure for the derivation; include a notation table. Expand intuition for the digamma cluster-size distribution and its tail behavior. Fix typos.

Questions for the authors
1) Is c learned or fixed? How sensitive is performance to c, γ0, and the Gamma prior on rj?
2) Can your ECPF→EPPF recipe be instantiated for the gamma–Poisson or GNBP, and what technical obstacles arise?
3) How does the sampler scale with very large KJ, and can sparse-LDA tricks be fully adapted?
4) Could you provide a collapsed split–merge move to further improve mixing on topic counts?

Minor issues
- Typos: “perdition rule” → “prediction rule”; “disjoint disjoint”; a few missing years in references.
- Reference updates and formatting would help (years, venues).

Recommendation
Accept. The paper provides a novel and technically solid EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership settings, an intuitive prediction rule, and a fully collapsed topic model with compelling empirical behavior. With stronger baselines and minor clarifications, this will be a valuable contribution to the NeurIPS community in Bayesian nonparametrics and topic modeling.
