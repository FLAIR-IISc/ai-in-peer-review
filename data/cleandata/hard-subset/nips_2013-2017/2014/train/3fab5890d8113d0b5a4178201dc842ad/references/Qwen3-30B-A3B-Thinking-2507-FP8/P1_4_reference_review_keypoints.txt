- Summary: Derives the EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) via randomizing group sizes and Bayes' rule, and presents a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler showing faster convergence and competitive predictive performance vs. HDP-LDA.

- Strength — Theoretical Contribution: Novel derivation of the BNBP EPPF fills a major gap and can generalize to other integer-valued stochastic processes.

- Strength — Practical Impact: Fully collapsed BNBP topic model yields faster convergence, better mixing, and more compact corpus representation than HDP-LDA.

- Strength — Clarity and Organization: Paper is well-written with clear, reproducible mathematical derivations and strong linkage between theory and application.

- Strength — Experimental Validation: Thorough experiments on three corpora demonstrate advantages in convergence, mixing, and predictive performance against HDP-LDA.

- Weakness — Limited Related Work Comparison: Insufficient detailed comparison with other recent mixed-membership models (e.g., GNBP, beta-Poisson).

- Weakness — Computational Complexity: Claims of sampler speed lack formal time-complexity analysis relative to alternatives.

- Weakness — Parameter Sensitivity: Hyperparameter/dispersion effects are shown but not explored systematically.

- Quality: Technically sound with correct derivations and comprehensive experiments; not merely a position paper.

- Clarity: Notation and presentation are consistent and accessible to experts; reproducible details provided.

- Originality: Novel application of EPPF derivation to BNBP for mixed-membership modeling and the randomization method are new contributions.

- Significance: Advances nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership modeling with practical and theoretical benefits; useful to the community.

- Arguments for Acceptance: Fills a literature gap on BNBP EPPF, introduces a novel derivation method, shows clear practical advantages over HDP-LDA, provides an easy-to-implement collapsed sampler, and is theoretically sound.

- Arguments Against Acceptance: Lacks broader model comparisons, missing formal complexity analysis, and needs deeper hyperparameter sensitivity study.

- Conclusion/Recommendation: Strong paper warranting acceptance (score 8/10) as a significant, well-executed contribution to nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership modeling.
