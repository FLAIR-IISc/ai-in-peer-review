- Derives an ECPF and a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP via Poissonization and conditioning, plus a simple prediction rule for grouped partitions.  
- Shows columns are i.i.d., with column sizes following a “digamma” distribution and row allocations following Dirichlet-multinomial.  
- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic models that marginalizes topics, weights, and the beta process.  
- The sampler’s update combines global and local popularity multiplicatively, unlike HDP-LDA’s additive direct-assignment update.  
- Empirical results on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 show faster convergence/mixing and competitive or better perplexity with typically fewer topics.  
- Conceptual advance: fills a known gap by providing a collapsed/marginal representation for an integer-valued process analogous to CRP/CRF for DP/HDP.  
- Probabilistic development is solid: the beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, factorization, and DirMult×Digam characterization are insightful and reusable.  
- Practical impact: yields simple predictive updates and an efficient collapsed inference algorithm.  
- Originality: first derivation of a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership models and first fully collapsed BNBP topic sampler.  
- Significance: likely to influence inference for other integer-valued processes (e.g., GNBP) and enable more efficient BNBP-based models.  
- Weakness: the EPPF’s dependence on group sizes (violating Pitman’s addition rule) needs more discussion on exchangeability, sufficiency, and asymptotics (cluster growth with m).  
- Weakness: notation-heavy and occasionally terse; the nonstandard “digamma distribution” must be defined (support, normalization) and summation notation clarified.  
- Weakness: comparison set is limited—missing Pitman–Yor/PY-topic model baselines and prior BNBP truncation/slice-sampling methods for direct empirical comparison.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter treatment incomplete—γ0, c, and rj prior settings largely fixed; sensitivity analysis or posterior summaries are needed and c’s inference should be clarified.  
- Weakness: experimental protocol choices (50% per-document holdout) are unusual; recommend standard alternatives (left-to-right, document completion) and reporting ESS/wall-clock time.  
- Weakness: claims of generality would be stronger with a sketch or demonstration of extension to GNBP/gamma–Poisson and discussion of obstacles.  
- Quality: derivations and augmentation/marginalizations appear correct; require explicit statements of normalization and parameter conditions (e.g., c>0, rj>0).  
- Clarity suggestions: include a small concrete example of the ECPF→EPPF conditioning, pseudocode for the collapsed sampler, and update formulas for γ0, c, rj.  
- Clarity suggestion: define the “digamma distribution” once with intuition (origin, mean/variance) to avoid confusion with the digamma function.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel theoretical contribution, practical collapsed sampler, intuitive multiplicative shrinkage interpretation, and empirical evidence of efficiency and compactness.  
- Cons for acceptance: sample-size-dependent EPPF needs more analysis, limited baseline and hyperparameter studies, dense presentation, and narrow experimental focus.  
- Questions for authors: provide asymptotic behavior of E[KJ | m] as m increases and its dependence on r· and c when conditioning on m.  
- Questions for authors: is c inferred—if so, give conditional updates; if not, report sensitivity to c.  
- Questions for authors: how does the recipe extend to GNBP or gamma–Poisson—are there obstacles to obtaining a similarly simple prediction rule?  
- Questions for authors: quantify variance-to-mean control of topic usage counts under BNBP versus HDP.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—substantive theoretical and practical contribution, but needs clearer exposition, broader baselines, and additional sensitivity/asymptotic analysis.
