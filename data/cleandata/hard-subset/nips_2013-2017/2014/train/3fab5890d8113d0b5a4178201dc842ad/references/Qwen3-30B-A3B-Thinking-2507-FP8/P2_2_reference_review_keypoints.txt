- Contribution: Develops the first EPPF for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), filling a gap in nonparametric Bayesian theory.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides clear, rigorous proofs for ECPF, EPPF, and the BNBP prediction rule (lemmas 1–4).  
- Practical inference: Derives a usable prediction rule (eq. 10) and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process.  
- Novel method: Presents a general approach to derive EPPFs for mixed-membership models from integer-valued stochastic processes, extensible beyond BNBP.  
- Comparison to HDP: Shows BNBP’s multiplicative topic popularity control contrasts with HDP’s additive mechanism, with theoretical and empirical implications.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates faster convergence, better mixing, and more compact topic representations than HDP-LDA on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 using perplexity evaluations.  
- Trade-off noted: BNBP can have higher perplexity than HDP-LDA for very small Dirichlet smoothing (η) due to stronger topic shrinkage, which favors compactness.  
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition, consistent notation, helpful figures (e.g., Fig.1), and comprehensive appendix proofs.  
- Significance: Substantial impact potential for mixed-membership modeling across domains and for future nonparametric Bayesian research.  
- Strengths summary: Novel EPPF, rigorous theory, fully collapsed sampler, strong empirical advantages, and insightful theoretical comparisons.  
- Weaknesses summary: Complex EPPF expression (though prediction rule alleviates this), limited demonstration beyond topic modeling, and limited discussion of scalability to very large datasets.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound, original, clearly written, and significant for theory and practice.
