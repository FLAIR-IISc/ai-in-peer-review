Summary
The paper studies the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling and derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) that governs the exchangeable random partitions of grouped data with observed group sizes. Because directly obtaining an EPPF for the BNBP is difficult, the authors introduce a group-size dependent model: they first treat group sizes as random, derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for the joint distribution of group sizes and partitions by marginalizing the beta process, then marginalize the group sizes to arrive (via Bayes’ rule) at the desired EPPF for partitions conditioned on fixed group sizes. They also derive a simple prediction rule enabling simulation of partitions, show that the implied random count matrix has i.i.d. columns whose total counts follow a digamma distribution and whose across-group allocations follow a Dirichlet-multinomial, and present a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process. Experiments on three corpora compare against HDP-LDA, showing faster mixing and comparable or better held-out perplexity at similar numbers of topics, with a more compact representation.

Relation to prior work: The paper situates itself against the HDP and CRF (Teh et al. 2006) for mixed-membership, and recent work on integer-valued processes (gamma–Poisson, BNBP, GNBP) used for counts and mixed membership (e.g., Zhou & Carin, 2015; Zhou et al., AISTATS 2012). Prior works have provided predictive structures such as negative-binomial IBP or “ice cream” buffet processes for infinite count matrices, but not EPPFs for grouped data with fixed group sizes. The general marginal samplers for σ-stable Poisson–Kingman mixtures (Lomelí et al., 2014) do not cover the BNBP. The paper builds on sample-size dependent EPPFs (Zhou & Walker, 2014) and extends them to mixed-membership models.

Strengths
- Technical contribution: Derivation of an ECPF and group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP, including a clear augmentation via the beta–gamma–Poisson representation and closed-form marginalization leading to (i) an explicit random count matrix prior with i.i.d. columns following a digamma–DirMult mixture; (ii) a simple prediction rule for collapsed sampling.
- Modeling utility: A fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic models that analytically integrates the beta process and topic parameters; clean update equations.
- Empirical behavior: Demonstrated fast mixing and convergence relative to HDP-LDA, and competitive or better perplexity at comparable topic counts on multiple corpora. The method yields more compact models when moderate smoothing is used.
- Generality: The methodology—randomizing group sizes to obtain an ECPF and then conditioning to get the EPPF—appears portable to other integer-valued processes.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Scope of evaluation: Experiments focus solely on topic modeling and perplexity; comparisons to other strong nonparametric baselines (e.g., Pitman–Yor HDP-LDA, fully collapsed CRF sampler for HDP, or state-of-the-art Poisson factorization methods) are not reported.
- Baseline fairness: The HDP baseline uses the direct-assignment sampler (partially collapsed). A comparison to the standard CRF-based collapsed sampler could better isolate the gains from the proposed collapsing strategy.
- Dependence on sample-size dependent EPPF: While justified and referenced, the implications of violating the addition rule (dependence on group sizes) are only briefly discussed. Additional interpretation of when this dependence is desirable or potentially problematic would help.
- Clarity of some derivations: Given the density of notation, readers would benefit from more intuition around the digamma distribution’s role and the normalization factors in the ECPF/EPPF. Some definitions (e.g., support restrictions n·k ≥ 1 for non-empty columns) are implicit.
- Limited sensitivity analysis: The empirical section varies η extensively, but the effects of c, γ0, and the inferred rj on performance and model compactness are shown qualitatively (simulations) rather than thoroughly quantified on real data.

Quality
- The probabilistic construction and augmentations are standard and sound (Gamma–Poisson mixture, beta process marginalization). The ECPF, random matrix prior, and prediction rule are consistent with known Poisson random measure calculus and with prior BNBP combinatorial results. While full proofs are in the appendix, the main steps are plausible and coherent.
- The sampler is fully collapsed and the update rules are given; complexity claims are reasonable. Empirical protocols (train/test split, averaging across splits) are standard for topic models.
- Some derivational details (normalization constants and exact conditions for the digamma PMF) could be elaborated, but no obvious contradictions were detected.

Clarity
- Generally clear but mathematically dense. The incremental path (BNBP → augmented model → ECPF → random count matrix distribution → EPPF → prediction rule) is logical.
- Suggestions: define all distributions at first use with support; provide a brief derivation sketch of the digamma distribution normalization; add pseudocode for the collapsed sampler; clarify the handling of empty columns and indexing.

Originality
- The group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP and the associated prediction rule for mixed membership are, to the best of my knowledge, new. Prior BNBP work did not provide an EPPF for grouped data with fixed sizes nor a fully collapsed inference scheme that marginalizes the beta process.

Significance
- The contribution advances the theoretical understanding of BNBP in mixed-membership settings and yields a practical, efficient sampler for topic models. Given the centrality of HDP-LDA, a compelling alternative with different shrinkage behavior and faster mixing is useful. The method for deriving EPPFs may stimulate analogous developments for other count processes.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel and technically solid derivation of an EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed membership.
- Fully collapsed sampler with clear practical benefits (speed, mixing, compactness).
- Empirical results on multiple datasets consistent with claimed advantages.
- Potentially general methodology beyond BNBP.

Cons for acceptance
- Baseline comparisons could be stronger (omits fully collapsed HDP CRF and PYP variants).
- Empirical focus is narrow (topic modeling, perplexity).
- Limited ablations on key hyperparameters c and γ0; sensitivity and robustness could be better documented.
- Heavy mathematical presentation may limit accessibility without more intuition.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Provide results with a fully collapsed CRF sampler for HDP-LDA (and/or PYP-LDA) to strengthen comparative claims.
- Include sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, and priors on rj; report how these affect topic counts, perplexity, and runtime.
- Elaborate on the practical implications of sample-size dependent EPPFs: when is dependence desirable, and could it bias comparisons against size-independent priors?
- Add pseudocode and complexity analysis per iteration as a function of nonzero counts and topics; discuss applicability of sparse-LDA speedups.
- Clarify test-time prediction for new documents (how rj and other parameters are inferred).

Overall, the paper presents a meaningful theoretical and algorithmic advance for BNBP mixed-membership modeling with promising empirical properties.
