- Paper develops a group-size dependent EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership settings by randomizing group sizes to derive an ECPF and applying Bayes’ rule to get an EPPF, plus a simple prediction rule and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler instantiated in a BNBP topic model.  
- Empirical claim: faster convergence/mixing and comparable or better perplexity with a more compact topic representation versus HDP-LDA (direct assignment sampler).  
- Positions work relative to prior literature on EPPFs/CRP, NRMIs, beta process/IBP variants, and integer-valued mixed-membership processes; previous marginalizations did not give a mixed-membership EPPF for fixed group sizes.  
- Theoretical soundness: augmentation BNBP ≡ beta–gamma–Poisson is standard; Lemma 1 and factorization are correct; ECPF, induced random count matrix prior, EPPF, and prediction rule appear consistent and carefully derived (proofs in appendix).  
- Algorithmic contribution: a clean fully collapsed Gibbs sampler combining word likelihood, global topic popularity, and document-specific dispersion, with a distinctive multiplicative shrinkage mechanism (vs HDP’s additive).  
- Experiments: solid setup (three corpora, multiple η, convergence diagnostics, code released); comparisons mainly to HDP direct assignment; results support mixing and compactness claims but perplexity advantages are nuanced.  
- Missing baseline: lack of comparison to the standard collapsed CRF sampler for HDP (marginalizing G̃) weakens claims about mixing and perplexity.  
- Sensitivity analysis lacking: limited exploration of hyperparameters c, γ0 and the role of inferred rj; posterior summaries and ablations (e.g., fixed rj) are needed.  
- Complexity and reproducibility: per-iteration time/space complexity in terms of nonzeros/active topics is not clearly provided; an algorithm box/pseudocode would help.  
- Evaluation protocol: perplexity could be complemented by document-completion or left-to-right evaluation; calibrating HDP’s α to match topic counts would make comparisons fairer.  
- Clarity: paper is dense but mostly clear; heavy notation, late explanation of the “digamma” distribution, and insufficient emphasis on the EPPF’s sample-size dependence reduce accessibility; more figures/pseudocode suggested.  
- Originality: high — first derivation of a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership models and a neat, potentially extensible construction (randomize sizes → ECPF → EPPF).  
- Significance: fills a methodological gap by providing a truncation-free, fully collapsed inference mechanism for BNBP; practical gains moderate but the methodological advance is substantial.  
- Pros summary: clear theoretical contribution, practical fully collapsed sampler, faster mixing/compact topics, open-source code, extensibility to other count processes.  
- Cons summary: omitted collapsed HDP baseline, limited sensitivity/ablation studies, dense notation and accessibility issues.  
- Suggestions for authors: include collapsed CRF HDP comparisons and tuned α, clarify whether c is inferred and provide sensitivity/gamma0/rj posterior summaries, add algorithm box and complexity discussion, and give more intuition/synthetic examples for key distributions and the sample-size dependent EPPF.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong, original, and significant — recommend acceptance (leaning accept).
