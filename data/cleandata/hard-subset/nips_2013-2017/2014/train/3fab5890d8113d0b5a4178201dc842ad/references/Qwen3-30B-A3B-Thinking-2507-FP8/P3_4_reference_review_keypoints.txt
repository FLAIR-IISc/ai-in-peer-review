- Develops a group size dependent method to derive an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Derives the BNBP's EPPF by randomizing group sizes, obtaining the joint distribution of group sizes and partitions, then applying Bayes' rule.  
- Constructs a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process.  
- Provides a clear prediction rule (Eq. 10) showing topic assignment is controlled by a multiplicative combination of cluster popularity and group-specific dispersion.  
- Empirically compares BNBP topic model to HDP-LDA on three corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12) under various η settings.  
- Shows BNBP converges faster (<100 iterations vs. >300 for HDP-LDA), mixes better, and attains comparable or better predictive performance with fewer topics.  
- Demonstrates that BNBP’s multiplicative shrinkage better prevents overfitting than HDP’s additive mechanism, especially for small η.  
- Claims novelty in providing the first EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via BNBP and suggests the derivation method can generalize to other integer-valued processes.  
- Lacks explicit discussion of limitations and unclear distinction or comparative theoretical properties relative to related processes (e.g., GNBP).  
- Offers no deep theoretical analysis explaining why multiplicative shrinkage outperforms HDP’s additive mechanism or their asymptotic properties.  
- Does not provide a detailed computational complexity analysis of the BNBP sampler or comparisons to optimized LDA implementations using sparse techniques.  
- Technical derivations are sound, paper is well-written and organized, with clear figures and intuitive explanations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant theoretical contribution with convincing empirical evidence; recommended for acceptance.
