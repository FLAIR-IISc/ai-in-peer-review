- Paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) and presents a prediction rule for grouped mixed-membership modeling.  
- Introduces a novel BNBP-based topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler and demonstrates competitive empirical performance on standard corpora.  
- Positions the work relative to DP, NRMIs, HDP, NB-IBP and related stochastic-process literature, clarifying where BNBP differs from existing EPPF frameworks.  
- Theoretical contribution: first principled derivation of the BNBP EPPF for grouped data, filling a gap in nonparametric Bayesian theory.  
- Analytical tractability: provides an analytically marginalized, fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that simplifies inference and enables efficient sampling.  
- Practical utility: application to topic modeling shows competitive perplexity and reasonable convergence/mixing behavior compared to HDP-LDA.  
- Clear presentation: well-written manuscript with consistent notation and transparent mathematical derivations.  
- Unified framework: advances a unified EPPF-style understanding for mixed-membership models akin to single-dataset settings.  
- Complexity concern: the derived EPPF (Eq. 9) is algebraically complex and may be difficult to apply broadly without simplification.  
- Hyperparameter study limited: experiments explore a narrow range of η and do not fully assess its impact on topic number and performance.  
- Computational scalability underexplored: lacks detailed profiling/benchmarking of runtime and scaling with number of groups (J) and data size.  
- Visualization and interpretability limited: missing visualizations of partitions/topic assignments and topic coherence metrics.  
- Assumption sensitivity not addressed: little discussion of how alternative base distributions for the beta process would affect results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical advance, sound derivations, useful collapsed sampler, competitive empirical results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability hindered by EPPF complexity, limited hyperparameter exploration, and insufficient computational/visual analyses.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; weaknesses are minor and addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required minor revisions: acknowledge EPPF complexity and avenues for simplification; provide more detailed computational/scalability analysis; add visualizations of partitions/topics; discuss sensitivity to base distribution choices.
