- Proposes a sample-size dependent EPPF for mixed-membership models induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) via an ECPF→EPPF construction.  
- Key technical steps: randomize group sizes, marginalize the beta process using a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, derive a Poisson-number-of-columns count-matrix prior, then condition on observed group sizes to get the BNBP EPPF and prediction rule.  
- Develops a fully collapsed BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process, with a closed-form Gibbs sampler.  
- Empirical results on three corpora show faster convergence/mixing and competitive or better perplexity with more compact topic usage compared to HDP-LDA (direct-assignment).  
- Situates work with classical EPPF/CRP literature and mixed-membership models (HDP/CRF), noting prior BNBP work lacked grouped-partition EPPFs and fully collapsed samplers.  
- Mathematical derivations appear sound and internally consistent (ECPF, count-matrix prior, EPPF, prediction rule) and align with known BNBP characterizations.  
- Some technical details are deferred to the appendix; main text sketches are clear but constants in the new-cluster probability (Eq. 10) may be missing a normalizing factor and need checking.  
- The topic-model sampler is intuitive: existing-topic probability decomposes into global popularity and group-specific terms; new-topic probability scales with γ0, rj, and c.  
- Experiments are reasonable (multiple η settings, trace plots, perplexity curves), but lack comparison to a fully collapsed HDP sampler (CRF) and stronger modern baselines; wall-clock convergence times are not reported.  
- Paper is dense with heavy notation; a small synthetic example illustrating the prediction rule would improve intuition.  
- Equation (10) needs careful proofreading to remove ambiguity in the new-cluster term; minor typos and formatting glitches should be fixed.  
- Contribution is novel: first group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership and a truncation-free fully collapsed sampler marginalizing the beta process.  
- Methodological construction (ECPF→EPPF) may generalize to related integer-valued processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP).  
- Conceptually significant—parallels CRP/CRF for DP/HDP and can influence collapsed inference design for count-based nonparametric models.  
- Practically relevant—simple to implement, mixes faster than HDP-LDA direct assignment, and yields compact topic representations.  
- Suggested improvements: include collapsed HDP (CRF) and stronger baselines, report wall-clock times, clarify Eq. (10) normalization, discuss exchangeability/predictive implications, perform sensitivity analyses for γ0, c, rj, and add topic coherence/qualitative examples.  
- Pros: novel theoretical contribution, principled augmentation, practical collapsed algorithm, improved mixing, generalizable approach.  
- Cons: missing comparisons to fully collapsed HDP and stronger baselines, some ambiguity/typos in key equations, heavy notation, empirical focus mainly on perplexity.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, original, and timely; with minor clarifications and stronger baselines the paper is a valuable NeurIPS submission—reviewer leans toward acceptance.
