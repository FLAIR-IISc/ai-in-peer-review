- Main contribution: first derivation of the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership modeling.  
- Novel technique: derive the BNBP EPPF by randomizing group sizes and using Bayes' rule via a group size–dependent model.  
- Practical model: propose a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the infinite beta process.  
- Theoretical rigor: detailed, correct mathematical derivations and proofs (ECPF, EPPF, lemmas) building on prior work.  
- Experimental validation: thorough experiments on three standard corpora using perplexity and comparisons to HDP-LDA and LDA.  
- Empirical results: faster convergence, better mixing, more compact topic representations, and comparable or better predictive performance than HDP-LDA.  
- Topic control mechanism: BNBP controls topic counts multiplicatively (distinct from HDP-LDA’s additive control).  
- Clarity: well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and helpful figures; some equations (e.g., EPPF Eq. 9) are complex.  
- Presentation weakness: augmented representation (Section 2.1) is dense and could use more intuitive explanation; earlier link to topic model could be clearer.  
- Originality: novel, significant gap-filling work—first to derive BNBP’s EPPF and introduce a distinct BNBP topic model.  
- Generalizability: derivation method could potentially extend to other integer-valued stochastic processes (e.g., GNBP), but this is not explored in depth.  
- Practical limitation: EPPF complexity may hinder immediate adoption and implementation.  
- Hyperparameter issue: limited discussion on selecting or inferring dispersion parameters r_j (authors recommend inference but give few practical details).  
- Recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance due to theoretical significance and practical advantages.
