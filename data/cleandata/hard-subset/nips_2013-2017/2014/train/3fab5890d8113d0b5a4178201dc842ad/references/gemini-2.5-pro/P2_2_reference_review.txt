**Summary of the Paper**

This paper addresses a significant gap in the literature on nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership models. While Exchangeable Partition Probability Functions (EPPFs) provide a unified framework for standard mixture models by enabling fully collapsed inference, a similar unifying framework for mixed-membership models has been lacking. The paper focuses on the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), a powerful prior for count data, which previously lacked a fully collapsed inference scheme for mixed-membership settings. The core contribution is a novel method to derive the EPPF for the BNBP. Instead of a direct derivation, the authors introduce a clever auxiliary model where group sizes are random. They first derive the joint distribution of these random group sizes and their partitions (termed an Exchangeable Cluster Probability Function, or ECPF). By marginalizing out the group sizes and applying Bayes' rule, they successfully derive the desired EPPF, which describes the partition probabilities for fixed, observed group sizes.

Building on this theoretical result, the paper derives a simple and intuitive prediction rule for Gibbs sampling. This rule is then used to construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model. The authors provide a clear comparison to the samplers for Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP), highlighting the BNBP's distinct multiplicative mechanism for combining global and local topic popularity. Finally, they present compelling empirical results on several standard text corpora, demonstrating that their BNBP topic model converges significantly faster and often achieves better predictive performance with a more compact representation (fewer topics) than the widely-used HDP-LDA.

**Review**

This is an excellent paper that makes a strong contribution to both the theory and practice of nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The work is technically sound, clearly presented, and the results are significant.

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The derivations, particularly the central idea of using a random group size model to derive the EPPF, are rigorous and well-motivated. Lemma 1 correctly establishes the link between the proposed auxiliary model and the standard BNBP. The characterization of the BNBP count matrix prior (Eq. 8) as a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns with Digamma-distributed sizes and Dirichlet-Multinomial partitioning is an elegant result that provides deep intuition. The resulting collapsed Gibbs sampler (Eq. 12) follows directly from this theoretical work. The claims are well-supported by extensive experiments on three real-world datasets, and the authors are careful in their comparison to the HDP-LDA, the appropriate state-of-the-art baseline. The work is a complete and self-contained contribution, progressing from foundational theory to a practical, high-performing algorithm.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem in the introduction, clearly situating their work relative to the HDP and the general theory of EPPFs. The step-by-step derivation in Section 2, moving from the BNBP definition to the ECPF and finally to the EPPF and prediction rule, is logical and easy to follow. The direct comparison of the prediction rules for LDA, HDP-LDA, and the new BNBP topic model in Section 3.1 is particularly effective at highlighting the core differences between these models. The experimental setup and results are also presented clearly. The paper provides enough detail for an expert to understand and likely reproduce the results.

**Originality:** The work is highly original. To my knowledge, this is the first paper to derive the EPPF for the BNBP in a mixed-membership context and, consequently, the first to develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for it. The method of using a size-dependent model (ECPF) as an intermediate step to arrive at the EPPF is a novel and powerful technique that may be applicable to other integer-valued stochastic processes, as the authors suggest. The resulting BNBP topic model is a new addition to the literature, offering a distinct and compelling alternative to the HDP, with a different shrinkage mechanism (multiplicative vs. additive) that appears to have significant practical benefits. The related work is adequately cited and distinguished.

**Significance:** The results presented in this paper are highly significant for several reasons:
1.  **Theoretical Advance:** It solves a long-standing problem for a key nonparametric process, providing a deeper understanding of the combinatorial structure of the BNBP and bringing it into the unifying framework of EPPFs for mixed-membership modeling. The derivation technique itself is an important contribution.
2.  **Practical Algorithm:** It provides a new, practical, and high-performing nonparametric topic model. The empirical results showing dramatically faster convergence and mixing compared to the HDP-LDA are a major practical advantage. The ability to achieve state-of-the-art predictive perplexity with a more compact model is very desirable for real-world applications where interpretability is key.
3.  **Future Research:** This work opens up new avenues for research. Researchers are likely to build on these ideas, potentially applying the derivation technique to other processes (e.g., gamma-negative binomial process) or developing other models based on the BNBP's newly derived EPPF.

**Strengths:**
*   **Novel theoretical contribution:** Derivation of the BNBP-EPPF for mixed-membership modeling.
*   **Elegant derivation technique:** The use of the ECPF as an intermediate step is a powerful and potentially generalizable idea.
*   **Practical impact:** The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler is computationally efficient, converges much faster than the HDP-LDA baseline, and produces excellent results.
*   **High clarity:** The paper is extremely well-written, motivated, and easy to follow.
*   **Intuitive results:** The multiplicative prediction rule and the generative view of the count matrix prior provide clear intuition about the model's behavior.

**Weaknesses / Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   The empirical evaluation could be slightly strengthened by including topic coherence metrics in addition to perplexity. This would provide a more complete picture of the quality of the topics discovered.
*   The paper rightly highlights the benefits of its stronger, multiplicative shrinkage. A brief discussion of potential scenarios where the HDP's softer, additive shrinkage might be preferable would add nuance and further strengthen the paper's discussion.

In conclusion, this is a standout paper that presents a significant advance in nonparametric Bayesian methods. It combines deep theoretical insight with a practical, high-performing algorithm and compelling experimental validation. I strongly recommend acceptance.
