

# Review of "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"

## Summary
This paper proposes a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership modeling. The authors derive a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP, develop a prediction rule for simulating exchangeable random partitions, and construct a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler. The paper positions itself as filling a gap in the unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling, which currently lacks a general framework like the EPPF approach for single-set mixture modeling.

## Strengths
The paper makes a technically sound contribution by developing a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP, which addresses an important gap in the literature. The derivation of the exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) and subsequent EPPF is mathematically rigorous. The BNBP topic model is well-motivated and provides a new perspective on topic modeling through a count-based approach. The experimental results on three corpora demonstrate the BNBP topic model's faster convergence and better mixing properties compared to HDP-LDA.

## Weaknesses

### Technical Inconsistencies
1. **Notation Errors**: The paper contains multiple inconsistent notations that undermine technical clarity:
   - In Lemma 2, "γ KJ 0" should be "γ_0^{K_J}" (not "γ KJ 0")
   - "K −ji J" appears repeatedly (e.g., "K −ji J" in Lemma 4), which should be "K_{-ji}"
   - "r·" should be "r_·" or "r_{\cdot}"
   - "n·k" should be "n_{\cdot k}"
   These inconsistent notations make the mathematical derivations difficult to follow.

2. **Mathematical Errors**: 
   - Lemma 1's expression "zji ∼ ∑∞ k=1 θjk Θj(Ω) δk" is incorrect; it should be "zji ∼ ∑∞ k=1 (θjk/Θj(Ω)) δk"
   - In Lemma 4, the prediction rule's normalization is missing the denominator term "c + n−ji·k + r·" in the first proportionality term
   - The expression for the EPPF in Lemma 3 appears to be missing a normalization constant

3. **Experimental Methodology Issues**:
   - The paper states "we randomly choose 50% of the words in each document as training," which is unconventional and would likely lead to poor performance, contradicting their claim of "state-of-the-art predictive performance"
   - The hyperparameter settings (a0 = b0 = e0 = f0 = 0.01) are arbitrary and not justified, which is problematic for a paper claiming to provide a principled framework
   - The claim that "the BNBP topic model produces state-of-the-art predictive performance" contradicts Figure 3, which shows higher perplexity for BNBP compared to HDP-LDA for the same η

### Conceptual and Presentation Issues
4. **Misleading Claims**: The paper claims "the BNBP topic model is a nonparametric Bayesian algorithm that removes the need to tune these parameters," but it still requires tuning hyperparameters (c, r, γ0, etc.), which is not addressed in the paper.

5. **Figure 1 Interpretation**: The caption states "the jth row of each matrix, which sums to 50," but the paper assumes fixed group sizes (mj), so all rows should sum to the same observed value (not "random number of exchangeable clusters" as stated).

6. **Inconsistent Results**: The paper states "the BNBP topic model's perplexity tends to be lower than that of the HDP-LDA if their inferred number of topics are comparable," but Figure 3's middle column shows the opposite (BNBP has higher perplexity for the same η).

7. **Formatting Errors**: The reference list contains incomplete citations (e.g., "A Bayesian analysis of some nonparametric problems, T.S. Ferguson, Ann. Statist., ,"). The paper also lacks proper spacing in mathematical expressions (e.g., "γ KJ 0" instead of "γ_0^{K_J}").

### Technical Justification Gaps
8. **Unjustified Assumptions**: The paper assumes the BNBP's EPPF can be derived via a group-size dependent model, but doesn't fully justify why this approach is necessary when other methods (like the Chinese restaurant franchise for HDP) exist for similar problems.

9. **Comparative Analysis**: The comparison with HDP-LDA in Section 3.1 is incomplete. The paper doesn't provide a fair comparison of computational complexity or scalability, which is critical for practical adoption.

## Recommendation
While the paper addresses a meaningful gap in mixed-membership modeling and presents technically sound derivations, the numerous technical inconsistencies, misleading claims, and experimental issues significantly undermine the paper's contribution. The mathematical errors in key lemmas (Lemma 1, 2, 4) and the contradiction between claims and results (Figure 3 vs. text) are particularly concerning. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to correct these issues before being considered for publication.

The paper's technical contribution is interesting but is overshadowed by the numerous flaws that prevent the reader from fully understanding or trusting the results. Given NIPS' standards for technical rigor and clarity, I recommend rejection. The authors should address these issues thoroughly before resubmitting to a venue that values precision in mathematical presentation and experimental reporting.
