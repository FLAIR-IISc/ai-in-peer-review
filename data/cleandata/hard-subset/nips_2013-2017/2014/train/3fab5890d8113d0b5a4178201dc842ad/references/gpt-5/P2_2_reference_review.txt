Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies mixed-membership modeling via the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). Its main theoretical contribution is to derive an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for grouped data under the BNBP. Because deriving the EPPF directly is difficult, the authors first introduce a group-size dependent model (randomizing group sizes), derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for the joint distribution of group sizes and partitions, and then condition on observed group sizes to obtain the EPPF. They further show that, marginalizing the beta process, the prior over columns (clusters) yields a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns whose sizes follow a “digamma” distribution and whose group allocations follow a Dirichlet-multinomial. From this they derive a simple prediction rule for Gibbs sampling. As an application, they build a fully collapsed BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process, and provide a collapsed Gibbs sampler. Empirically, they compare against HDP-LDA on three corpora, reporting faster convergence/mixing and competitive or better perplexity for comparable numbers of topics, often with a more compact representation.

This positions the work with respect to DP/NRMIs and EPPFs (Pitman), mixed-membership via HDP (Teh et al.), and BNBP/GNBP/gamma–Poisson processes (Zhou et al., Titsias). It is also related to recent analyses of the combinatorial structure of BNBP and negative-binomial IBP variants (Heaukulani & Roy; Broderick et al.; Zhou et al.), but focuses on deriving a sample-size dependent EPPF for grouped data and a fully collapsed sampler—both missing in prior BNBP work.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The augmentation (beta–gamma–Poisson) and the construction of the ECPF are standard and carefully used; Lemma 1’s equivalence and factorization are correct and give tractable marginalization. The finite-dimensional distribution (Lemma 2) and the induced Poisson i.i.d. column representation (eq. 8) are plausible and align with known CRM thinning/superposition results. The EPPF (Lemma 3) being sample-size dependent is consistent with Zhou & Walker (species models with sample-size dependent EPPFs). The prediction rule (Lemma 4) is clean and intuitive, balancing global and local popularity via a multiplicative term. The topic model sampler is derived by standard conjugacy and Bayes’ rule, yielding a truly collapsed scheme (unlike HDP direct assignment).
- Proofs are deferred to the appendix; from what is shown, the steps are reasonable. A minor technical request: clearly state conditions ensuring the normalizing constant of the “digamma” distribution and verify the Poisson mean γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)] is finite for the assumed parameter ranges. Also clarify exchangeability properties (within-group exchangeability and column-exchangeability) under the sample-size dependent EPPF.
- Empirics: The comparisons focus on perplexity and trace plots of K. Results support the claims on faster convergence/mixing and compactness. It would strengthen the case to report effective sample sizes/autocorrelation, wall-clock to reach a target perplexity, and uncertainty (error bars) on perplexity across splits/seeds.

Clarity
- The paper is dense but generally well-organized: motivation → ECPF → EPPF → prediction rule → topic model → experiments. Notation is heavy; a small table summarizing symbols (rj, γ0, c, njk, n·k, etc.) would help. A few typos (“perdition rule”) and minor grammatical issues exist.
- The notion of sample-size dependent EPPF is central; it would help to give a short intuition box contrasting classical (size-free) EPPFs and the present setting, and why mixed-membership naturally leads to group-size dependence.
- The “digamma distribution” is not widely known; give a brief derivation, normalization, and moments in the main text (currently only a citation).
- For the topic model, a short pseudocode of the collapsed sampler would improve reproducibility.

Originality
- Deriving an EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via the “randomize group sizes → ECPF → condition” route is novel and useful. Prior BNBP work did not provide a mixed-membership EPPF or a fully collapsed sampler. The prediction rule is new in this context and contrasts clearly with the HDP’s additive structure.
- The identification of the BNBP’s induced column prior (Poisson number of DirMult-partitioned clusters with digamma-distributed sizes) is insightful and not, to my knowledge, present in earlier BNBP topic modeling papers.

Significance
- Theoretical significance: Provides a unifying, generalizable recipe to obtain EPPFs for grouped data from integer-valued CRMs, potentially extensible to GNBP/gamma–Poisson processes.
- Practical significance: The fully collapsed sampler is simple, fast, and mixes well; the multiplicative shrinkage can yield compact models with competitive perplexity. Given the prevalence of HDP-LDA, a competitive alternative with different shrinkage behavior is valuable.
- Broader impact: The framework may catalyze new mixed-membership priors with tractable prediction rules and collapsed inference.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Empirical scope: Only text corpora and only HDP-LDA baseline. Consider adding GNBP-based topic modeling or Pitman–Yor process baselines, and a non-text grouped dataset to demonstrate generality.
- Sensitivity: Provide sensitivity analyses for priors on c, γ0, and rj, not just η. The results suggest rj has strong effects; show posterior behavior or hyperprior choices’ impact.
- Complexity: Report per-iteration complexity and memory vs HDP direct assignment across different K, and wall-clock to reach target perplexity.
- Relation to concurrent work: More explicit comparison to Heaukulani & Roy (2014) and Zhou et al. (2014) on combinatorial structure and random count matrices to delineate what is strictly new (EPPF for grouped data; fully collapsed sampler; prediction rule).

Pros (arguments for acceptance)
- Clear, nontrivial theoretical contribution (ECPF/EPPF/prediction rule) for BNBP mixed-membership.
- Fully collapsed, simple Gibbs sampler with demonstrated fast mixing.
- Insightful comparison to HDP showing distinct multiplicative shrinkage and compactness.
- Potentially generalizable methodology to other integer-valued processes.

Cons (arguments against acceptance)
- Empirical evaluation is somewhat narrow; lacks broader baselines, ablations, and more comprehensive diagnostics.
- Heavy notation and some clarity issues; key definitions (digamma distribution) could be better exposed.
- Proof details are deferred; more main-text intuition would help verification.

Overall recommendation: Accept. The paper makes a solid theoretical advance with practical impact, presenting a principled EPPF and a fully collapsed sampler for BNBP-based mixed-membership modeling. With minor improvements in clarity and a slightly broader empirical evaluation, it would be a strong NeurIPS contribution. Confidence: high.
