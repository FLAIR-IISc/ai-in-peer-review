Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling and provides a principled derivation of the marginal combinatorial laws governing exchangeable random partitions of grouped data. The key contributions are: (i) a group-size dependent exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) obtained by marginalizing the beta process in a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, (ii) from the ECPF, a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) via Bayes’ rule, (iii) a simple prediction rule enabling simulation and fully collapsed inference, and (iv) a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process, with empirical comparisons against LDA and HDP-LDA.

This work sits at the intersection of nonparametric Bayesian partition models (CRP/EPPF, Pitman-Yor; NRMIs) and count-based processes (gamma–Poisson, BNBP/GNBP). While HDP-based mixed-membership models have a well-understood marginal partition representation (CRF), comparable marginal laws for BNBP-based models have been missing. Related recent works addressed infinite random feature/count matrices and predictive structures for BNBP (e.g., negative binomial IBP, multi-scoop IBP), and Poisson–Kingman samplers for σ-stable processes. The present paper focuses specifically on grouped data with fixed group sizes and derives group-size dependent EPPFs and a tractable prediction rule for BNBP, which appears novel and complementary to prior work (Heaukulani & Roy; Zhou et al.) that emphasized feature allocation rather than grouped partitions.

Technical quality
- The augmentation Xj|Θj ~ PP(Θj), Θj|B ~ ΓP[rj, B/(1−B)], B ~ BP(c,B0) is standard and clearly connects counts and mixtures. Lemma 1 shows equivalence to the BNBP via collapsing Θj, yielding independent NB(rj, pk) counts per atom; the factorization (Eq. 4–5) is sound.
- The ECPF (Eq. 6) that integrates out B is nontrivial and seems carefully derived; the Poisson distribution on the number of occupied clusters with rate γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)] and the i.i.d. column construction (Eq. 7–8, with Digam and DirMult distributions) provide intuitive structure.
- The group-size dependent EPPF (Eq. 9) is formal and, while unwieldy, underpins the simple and interpretable prediction rule (Eq. 10). The rule shows a multiplicative combination of global popularity n·k and local popularity (njk + rj) with a new-cluster term scaling as γ0 rj/(c + r·).
- The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the topic model (Eq. 12) is derived by collapsing Φ, Θ, and B. The comparison with LDA (Eq. 13) and HDP-LDA direct assignment (Eq. 14) is technically insightful: BNBP’s multiplicative shrinkage vs HDP’s additive mechanism.

Empirical evaluation
- Experiments on three corpora compare convergence/mixing, number of topics, and held-out perplexity across η. The BNBP sampler reaches stationarity quickly and mixes well; BNBP tends to use fewer topics for comparable perplexity, indicating a more compact representation. Runtime per iteration is comparable to HDP direct assignment at similar K.
- The evaluation setup is reasonable (word holdout, multiple η), but comparisons omit the collapsed CRF sampler for HDP (which is standard and often mixes substantially better than direct assignment) and more recent scalable/collapsed HDP variants. Including a collapsed HDP baseline would provide a fairer assessment of mixing and predictive performance.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well organized, with clear motivation and a logical path: augmentation → ECPF → count-matrix prior → EPPF → prediction rule → topic model and sampler.
- However, there are several distracting typesetting/notation glitches (e.g., stray line after Eq. 10, minor duplication “disjoint disjoint”, and some equation spacing). The “digamma distribution” is unusual terminology but is defined and cited.
- It would help to summarize the role and practical range of c and γ0 and clarify whether c is inferred (no prior is given in Eq. 11). Sensitivity to c is not discussed.

Originality
- Deriving a group-size dependent EPPF and associated prediction rule for the BNBP in the mixed-membership setting appears new and important. The route through an ECPF and Bayes’ rule is elegant and may generalize to other count processes.
- The fully collapsed sampler for BNBP topic modeling that also collapses the beta process is a noteworthy algorithmic contribution.

Significance
- The results fill a conceptual gap analogous to CRF for HDP, enabling collapsed inference for BNBP-based mixed-membership models. This should stimulate broader use of BNBP/GNBP in text and other grouped count data, and the methodology (ECPF→EPPF) likely transfers to other integer-valued processes.
- Empirically, the sampler’s convergence and compactness advantages are valuable, although more comprehensive baselines would strengthen claims about predictive superiority.

Main strengths
- Novel derivation of group-size dependent EPPF and simple prediction rule for BNBP.
- Fully collapsed, conjugate Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic modeling.
- Clear interpretability of the multiplicative popularity mechanism and dispersion parameters rj.
- Solid empirical evidence for faster mixing and compactness.

Main weaknesses/concerns
- Missing comparison to collapsed HDP (CRF) samplers; conclusions about mixing may be overstated relative to HDP direct assignment baselines.
- Limited discussion/experiments on the role of c and γ0; is c fixed or inferred? Sensitivity?
- Some notation/typo issues that impede readability.
- Anonymity concern: the paper includes an explicit code link revealing author identity, violating double-blind policy.

Questions for authors
- Please clarify whether c is fixed or inferred; if inferred, provide the prior and update; if fixed, discuss sensitivity.
- Could you add a comparison to a collapsed HDP (CRF) sampler in the final version?
- Are there diagnostics on posterior KJ vs γ0 and c that support the Poisson rate interpretation in practice?
- Any insights on extending the ECPF→EPPF derivation to GNBP or gamma–Poisson processes?

Minor suggestions
- Fix the typesetting after Eq. (10) and minor typos; standardize “Digam” distribution notation.
- Add topic coherence or sparsity metrics to complement perplexity.
- Briefly discuss computational complexity per token and memory vs K.

Recommendation: Weak accept
The paper makes a substantive theoretical and algorithmic contribution by providing the BNBP analogue of CRF-style marginal laws and a fully collapsed sampler, with promising empirical behavior. Addressing the anonymity issue and strengthening baselines would make it a clear accept.
