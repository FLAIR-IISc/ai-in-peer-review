Summary
This paper develops a group-size-dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). The key idea is to circumvent the difficulty of directly marginalizing the beta process in the mixed-membership setting by first randomizing group sizes to derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF), then conditioning on observed group sizes via Bayes’ rule to obtain the EPPF. This yields: (i) a finite-dimensional joint law over group sizes and partitions (ECPF), (ii) a group-size-dependent EPPF for a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix, (iii) a simple prediction rule to simulate partitions, and (iv) a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process. Empirically, the proposed sampler mixes quickly, converges faster than HDP-LDA’s direct assignment sampler, and achieves competitive or better held-out perplexity at comparable topic counts, yielding more compact representations.

Relation to prior work: While classical EPPFs/CRPs address single-set mixtures, and HDP uses the Chinese restaurant franchise for group structure, prior BNBP/GNBP work did not provide the marginal partition laws needed for fully collapsed mixed-membership inference. Recent “negative binomial IBP/ice-cream buffet” provide predictive structures for infinite random count matrices but do not condition on fixed group sizes. This paper fills that gap for BNBP and outlines a general recipe potentially applicable to other integer-valued processes.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A clear and general method to derive an EPPF for mixed-membership from an integer-valued process via a randomized group-size model and Bayes conditioning. This is a meaningful step toward a more unified treatment of mixed-membership priors beyond HDP.
- Technical contributions: Closed-form ECPF (Eq. 6), random count matrix prior with Poisson number of columns and Digamma/DirMult structure (Eq. 8), EPPF (Eq. 9), and a simple prediction rule (Eq. 10) that enables truncation-free, fully collapsed inference.
- Practical impact: A straightforward collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model (Eq. 12) that marginalizes all nonparametric components, with clear advantages in mixing and convergence compared to HDP-LDA’s direct assignment sampler; available code.
- Insightful comparison: Multiplicative shrinkage (global×local) in BNBP vs additive in HDP-LDA; empirical evidence that BNBP often achieves similar perplexity with fewer topics.

Weaknesses
- Empirical scope: Baselines are limited to HDP-LDA; no comparisons to other count-based nonparametric topic models (e.g., Poisson factorization variants, GNBP) or strong parametric LDA baselines with sparse sampling/alias methods when K is large.
- Sensitivity/ablation: While rj’s role is illustrated in simulations, less is said about sensitivity to c and γ0, or practical guidance for these hyperpriors; no ablations on fixing rj or hierarchical structures for rj beyond simple Gamma priors.
- Clarity/notation: The paper is dense and sometimes hard to parse (e.g., transitions between ECPF/EPPF, the sum in Eq. 9, and the role of the Digamma distribution). Some typographical/formatting issues and heavy notation could be streamlined.
- Theory beyond construction: No asymptotic properties or deeper characterization of exchangeability properties beyond group-size dependence; no complexity analysis beyond empirical runtime.

Quality
The derivations appear technically sound and consistent with known augmentations (beta–gamma–Poisson mixture) and previously reported count-matrix priors. The logic from ECPF to EPPF via Bayes rule is convincing, and the prediction rule aligns with the derived random count matrix prior. Proofs are in the appendix; the main text provides enough sketch for expert readers. Experiments are well-aligned with claims (mixing speed and compactness), though broader baselines would strengthen the empirical case.

Clarity
Overall well-organized but mathematically dense. The introduction clearly motivates the gap (lack of EPPFs for mixed-membership outside HDP). It would help to:
- Add a small didactic example that walks through ECPF → EPPF normalization on a toy J=2, small m.
- Clarify constants in Eq. 10 and ensure formatting carries the dependence on ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c) consistently.
- Provide practitioner guidance for setting/inferring c and γ0.

Originality
High. To my knowledge, this is the first derivation of a group-size-dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership along with a tractable prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs without truncation. The “recipe” to get EPPFs for mixed-membership via integer-valued processes is novel and likely to inspire follow-ons (e.g., gamma–Poisson and GNBP).

Significance
Significant for Bayesian nonparametrics and topic modeling. Having a fully collapsed, truncation-free sampler that mixes well and yields compact models is practically valuable. The general methodology may catalyze further development of mixed-membership priors with clear marginal laws and efficient inference.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Could you report wall-clock time to target perplexity vs HDP-LDA, and topic counts at that point?
- Provide sensitivity analyses for c and γ0 and discuss default priors; consider learning c.
- Compare against a Poisson factorization baseline with gamma priors to situate performance beyond HDP-LDA.
- Discuss whether the recipe extends cleanly to GNBP or gamma–Poisson; any obstacles?
- Minor: fix typos/formatting, and clarify Eq. 10’s constants.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong conceptual novelty; closes a notable gap.
- Solid technical development with clear predictive rule.
- Practical, fully collapsed sampler with good mixing and compactness.
- Potentially generalizable methodology.

Cons against acceptance
- Limited empirical baselines and sensitivity analysis.
- Dense presentation may hinder accessibility.
- No theoretical exploration of asymptotics or deeper properties.

Recommendation
Accept. Despite some empirical and expository limitations, the core contribution—deriving an EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership and delivering an effective fully collapsed sampler—is novel, bold, and likely to influence subsequent work.
