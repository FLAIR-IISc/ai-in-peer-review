Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data. The main technical advance is to characterize the marginal law (in terms of partitions) induced by a BNBP on grouped count data. Because directly deriving an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) is difficult, the authors first construct a sample-size dependent model that treats group sizes as random, derive the joint law of group sizes and partitions—an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF)—and then use Bayes’ rule to obtain a group-size dependent EPPF over column-exchangeable random count matrices whose row sums equal the observed group sizes. They further derive a simple prediction rule enabling simulation and collapsed inference, and instantiate a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process. Experiments on three corpora compare against HDP-LDA, showing faster mixing and competitive or improved perplexity for comparable topic counts, typically using a more compact number of topics.

This work builds on and extends: (i) EPPFs and CRP/NRMIs (Blackwell & MacQueen; Pitman; Regazzini et al.), (ii) mixed-membership models including HDP (Teh et al.), (iii) integer-valued CRMs and BNBP/GNBP/gamma–Poisson connections (Lo; Titsias; Zhou et al.), and (iv) recent BNBP-related combinatorial structures over random count matrices (Heaukulani & Roy; Zhou et al. 2014 arXiv) and sample-size dependent species models (Zhou & Walker). The negative binomial IBP and “ice cream” buffet (related to count-feature models) are appropriately cited and contrasted: those target infinite random count matrices with random row sums, whereas this paper targets partitions conditional on fixed group sizes for mixed-membership.

Quality (technical soundness)
- The ECPF (Eq. 6) and the induced count-matrix prior (Eq. 7) appear consistent with a Poisson number of occupied columns K with mean γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)], per-column size following a “digamma” distribution, and per-column allocation across groups via a Dirichlet-multinomial. This aligns with known gamma–Poisson and beta–negative binomial conjugacy structures.
- The route ECPF → EPPF via Bayes’ conditioning on observed group sizes is technically sound and well motivated by prior size-dependent partition theory.
- The prediction rule (Eq. 10) is simple and implementable; it factorizes into a global popularity term and a group-specific term, consistent with the underlying probability calculus.
- The topic model sampler (Eq. 12) is fully collapsed and leverages conjugacy with Dirichlet–multinomial terms, which is standard and appropriate.
- Proofs are deferred to the appendix; the main text gives sufficient high-level structure, but several normalization constants are terse. In particular, clarifying constants in Eq. (10) (dependence on ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c) vs c+r·) would improve confidence in exactness.

Clarity
- The paper is dense but largely clear. The distinction between ECPF and group-size dependent EPPF is explicit. The use of “digamma distribution” (Sibuya 1979) is defined.
- Notation is heavy. Suggestions:
  - Collect all symbols (γ0, c, rj, r·, KJ, n·k, etc.) in a small notation table.
  - Explicitly state whether c is fixed or inferred; update steps for c are not in the main text.
  - In Sec. 3, the held-out perplexity equation omits indices (e.g., normalization over ∑k φvk θjk should be per document j); please clarify the exact estimator used and ensure per-document normalization.
  - Brief pseudocode for the collapsed sampler would aid reproducibility.

Originality
- Novel derivation of a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership modeling, with an ECPF-to-EPPF construction, appears original relative to prior work that handled BNBP via infinite random count matrices or truncations without a partition law for fixed group sizes.
- The prediction rule and fully collapsed sampler for a BNBP topic model are new and practically useful.
- The work complements and goes beyond Heaukulani & Roy (combinatorial structure) and Zhou et al. (priors for random count matrices) by delivering a mixed-membership EPPF and sampler conditioned on observed group sizes.

Significance
- The results provide a principled, truncation-free, collapsed inference scheme for BNBP mixed-membership models, which is of interest to Bayesian nonparametrics and topic modeling.
- The multiplicative global–local popularity interaction in the prediction rule offers a different shrinkage mechanism compared to HDP-LDA’s additive structure.
- Experiments suggest competitive or improved perplexity for comparable topic counts, with faster convergence and compact topic usage—practically relevant traits.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Empirical comparison: The HDP baseline is the direct assignment sampler (partially collapsed). For fairness, comparisons to a fully collapsed HDP sampler via the Chinese Restaurant Franchise (Teh et al. 2006) would be appropriate, or at least a discussion of expected differences. Including Pitman–Yor topic models or modern LDA baselines (e.g., SparseLDA) would strengthen the empirical case.
- Perplexity evaluation needs clarification. The shown formula seems to omit document indices in normalizers; please provide the exact per-sample, per-document computation.
- Hyperparameters: c appears fixed; given its influence on K, inference for c (e.g., via auxiliary-variable updates) should be described or justified if fixed. Similarly, report posteriors or diagnostics for rj to support the claims on dispersion control.
- Theoretical scope: Because the EPPF is group-size dependent, discuss consistency under adding/removing groups and whether projectivity holds across subsets of documents.
- Minor: Some references are incomplete (years/pages). A short intuitive explanation of the “digamma distribution” and its relation to the logarithmic/Yule–Simon family could help readers.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear theoretical contribution: a general ECPF→EPPF method for BNBP mixed-membership, with explicit formulas and a prediction rule.
- Fully collapsed sampler with demonstrated fast mixing and competitive predictive performance at comparable topic counts.
- Good positioning within the literature; connections to CRMs and partition structures are well made.
- Code availability enhances reproducibility.

Cons for acceptance
- Empirical comparisons do not include a fully collapsed HDP baseline; conclusions about relative performance may be confounded.
- Some derivations are only sketched; key constants in predictive rules and normalization could be clearer in the main text.
- Evaluation protocol (perplexity computation) needs clarification to avoid ambiguity.
- Limited ablations on the role of c and rj in real datasets.

Overall assessment
This is a solid contribution to Bayesian nonparametrics and mixed-membership modeling, providing both new theory (group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP) and an effective collapsed inference algorithm with promising empirical behavior. Addressing the evaluation clarity and strengthening baselines would further solidify the case.
