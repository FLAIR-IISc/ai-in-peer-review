- Proposes a fully collapsed, principled treatment of the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed‑membership models via Poissonizing group sizes.
- Derives an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for grouped data and conditions it to obtain a group‑size dependent EPPF.
- Provides a prior on a latent column‑exchangeable random count matrix with prescribed row sums.
- Gives a simple, intuitive prediction rule combining global and local popularity modulated by dispersion parameters.
- Introduces a nonparametric BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process.
- Theoretical construction is sound and consistent with completely random measure and Poissonization literature.
- Augmentation via the gamma–Poisson representation and derivation of ECPF/EPPF are technically well‑motivated.
- Fully collapsed sampler is an important algorithmic contribution, reducing variance and avoiding truncation/slice methods.
- Empirically, BNBP mixes substantially faster than an HDP-LDA direct assignment sampler and achieves comparable or better perplexity with fewer topics on three corpora.
- Code availability is promised, supporting reproducibility.
- Empirical comparison is limited: lacks a collapsed Chinese restaurant franchise (CRF) HDP baseline and other modern baselines (e.g., Poisson factor models, GNBP).
- Only perplexity is reported; topic coherence and additional evaluation metrics are missing.
- Central conceptual novelty is the group‑size dependent EPPF, but implications for projectivity and exchangeability need more discussion.
- Paper is densely written but largely clear; decomposition from ECPF to EPPF and the count‑matrix view aid intuition.
- Notation is heavy and could be streamlined; the “Digam” distribution should be clarified to avoid confusion with the digamma function.
- Proofs are deferred to the appendix; brief proof sketches in the main text would help readability.
- Recommend reporting computational complexity per iteration and wall‑clock times to a target perplexity for fair speed comparisons.
- Clarify hyperparameter treatment (is c inferred?) and provide sensitivity analyses and posterior behavior for rj to substantiate dispersion claims.
- Suggest adding baselines (collapsed CRF HDP, Poisson factor analysis, GNBP), topic coherence metrics, and scalability/memory discussions.
- Pros: principled ECPF/EPPF derivation, simple prediction rule, fully collapsed sampler with fast mixing, strong theoretical connections, compact models with competitive perplexity.
- Cons: limited experimental comparisons, incomplete discussion of exchangeability/projectivity, dense notation and appendix reliance, hyperparameter sensitivity unexplored.
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on strengthening experiments and clarifying theoretical/presentation points.
