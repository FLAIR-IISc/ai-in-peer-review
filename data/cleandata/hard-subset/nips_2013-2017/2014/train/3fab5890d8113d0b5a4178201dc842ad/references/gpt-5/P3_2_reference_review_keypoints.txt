- Introduces a group-size dependent EPPF for mixed-membership models induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Proposes a sample-size dependent construction treating group sizes as random via a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation.  
- Derives an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) by marginalizing the beta process and obtains the EPPF for fixed observed group sizes via Bayes’ rule.  
- Presents a multiplicative prediction rule enabling fully collapsed, truncation-free Gibbs sampling.  
- Applies the method to a collapsed BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process.  
- Empirically shows fast mixing and strong perplexity with fewer topics than HDP-LDA on three corpora.  
- Connects to Pitman’s combinatorial stochastic processes and extends sample-size-dependent species models (cf. Zhou & Walker).  
- Builds on BNBP/gamma–Poisson and related count-process literature while noting BNBP lies outside Poisson–Kingman class.  
- Contrasts with HDP/CRF by offering an alternative fully collapsed sampler and a new multiplicative prediction rule.  
- Conceptual strength: provides a general recipe (ECPF → Bayes → EPPF) linking integer-valued processes to EPPFs for grouped data.  
- Technical strength: nontrivial marginalization yields closed-form ECPF with interpretable normalization involving digamma functions.  
- Inference strength: simple fully collapsed Gibbs sampler avoiding truncation and CRF bookkeeping; separates global popularity and local dispersion.  
- Empirical strength: convincing convergence diagnostics and competitive perplexity/compact topic representations.  
- Concern: sample-size dependence raises questions about projectivity, limiting behavior (e.g., J=1), and comparison to size-independent EPPFs.  
- Concern: normalization term exp{-γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)]} and digamma distribution need clearer intuition in main text for reproducibility.  
- Concern: presentation of new-cluster probability and constants (ψ terms) is confusing; Eq.(10)/Sec 2.2 formatting obscures constants.  
- Concern: empirical baselines are limited—missing collapsed HDP-CRF, Pitman–Yor topic models, and other count-based nonparametric models.  
- Concern: insufficient detail on HDP hyperparameter treatment (fixed/sampled/optimized), which affects fairness of comparisons.  
- Concern: evaluation could use left-to-right or document-completion perplexity, and report wall-clock time and memory to quantify practical gains.  
- Concern: role and identifiability of r_j need posterior summaries, sensitivity analysis, and discussion of trade-offs with γ0 and c.  
- Quality: derivations are technically sound though some proofs deferred; minor constants/formatting issues reduce clarity.  
- Clarity: well-organized but dense notation and minor typos; a roadmap figure and concise algorithm box would help.  
- Originality: high—first explicit EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership with a fully collapsed sampler.  
- Significance: significant—fills a gap analogous to CRP/CRF for DP/HDP and offers practically useful collapsed inference.  
- Suggestions: clarify constants and Lemmas 2–4, add comparisons to collapsed HDP-CRF and PDP models, report hyperparameter treatment, provide r_j posterior summaries, and move key intuition from appendix to main text.  
- Pros: novel principled derivation, fully collapsed truncation-free sampler, clear conceptual link between count processes and partitions, compact empirical representations.  
- Cons: sample-size dependent EPPF may challenge projectivity, limited baseline/evaluation coverage, and minor clarity/formatting issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (weak accept to accept); technically solid, original, and significant with high reviewer confidence.
