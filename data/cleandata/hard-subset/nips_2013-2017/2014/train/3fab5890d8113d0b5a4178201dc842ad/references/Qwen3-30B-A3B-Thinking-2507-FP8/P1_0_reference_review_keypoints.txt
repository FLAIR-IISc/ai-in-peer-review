- Paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), filling a theoretical gap for mixed-membership modeling.  
- Novel method: randomize group sizes, derive joint distribution, then use Bayes' rule to obtain the BNBP EPPF.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and potentially generalizable to other integer-valued stochastic processes.  
- Derivations are mathematically rigorous and correctly situate the work relative to prior EPPF, beta process, and BNBP literature.  
- Practical impact demonstrated via a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that converges faster, mixes better, and uses fewer topics than competitors.  
- Comprehensive experiments on multiple corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS) with sensitivity analysis and baseline comparisons.  
- EPPF expression is quite complex (Eq. 9), which may hinder practical adoption and scalability.  
- Limited theoretical analysis comparing the statistical effects of BNBP’s multiplicative topic popularity control vs. HDP’s additive approach.  
- Insufficient computational complexity analysis and sparse implementation details regarding scaling with document/vocabulary count.  
- Dispersion parameters {r_j} are important but the paper lacks practical guidance on setting or estimating them.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a well-known theoretical gap, produces a usable prediction rule (Eq. 10), yields strong empirical results, and the derivation approach could inspire further work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: EPPF complexity and unresolved computational scalability may limit practical utility; broader theoretical implications beyond topic modeling are underexplored.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical contribution with strong practical results despite complexity concerns.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss approximate/efficient EPPF computation for large-scale use, provide detailed complexity comparison to HDP-LDA, and describe methods to estimate dispersion parameters {r_j}.
