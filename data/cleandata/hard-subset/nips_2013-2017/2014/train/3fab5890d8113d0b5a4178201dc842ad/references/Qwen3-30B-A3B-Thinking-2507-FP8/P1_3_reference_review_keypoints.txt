- Paper derives a group-size dependent EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) applied to mixed-membership modeling.  
- Novel derivation uses randomizing group sizes to obtain the BNBP's EPPF.  
- Constructs a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.  
- Empirical results on three corpora show BNBP converges faster and mixes better than HDP-LDA.  
- BNBP achieves comparable or better predictive performance with fewer (more compact) topics than HDP-LDA.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with rigorous proofs for key lemmas and theorems.  
- Experiments are well-designed and compare to a strong baseline (HDP-LDA).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation; appendices provide supporting technical details.  
- Figures effectively illustrate BNBP behavior and convergence properties.  
- Original contributions include the first mixed-membership EPPF for BNBP and a novel derivation approach that may generalize to other processes.  
- Highlights multiplicative topic-shrinkage in BNBP versus additive shrinkage in HDP, explaining compact representations.  
- Limitation: comparison is mainly against HDP-LDA, lacking broader comparison to other recent nonparametric topic models.  
- Minor clarity issue: could better explain necessity of the group-size dependent approach versus direct EPPF derivation.  
- Suggestion: more explicit discussion of computational complexity relative to other methods.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically strong, original, significant, and practically relevant for the NIPS community.
