Summary
This paper develops a sample-size (group-size) dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership models induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). The key idea is to (i) randomize group sizes and derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) by marginalizing out the beta process in a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation; (ii) obtain a prior on a latent column-exchangeable count matrix with a Poisson number of columns, each column’s size following a digamma distribution and split across groups via a Dirichlet-multinomial; and (iii) use Bayes’ rule to condition on observed group sizes, yielding the BNBP EPPF and an associated prediction rule (a CRP/CRF-like seating rule for grouped data). Building on this, the authors propose a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process. Experiments on three corpora compare against HDP-LDA, showing faster convergence/mixing and competitive or better perplexity for a comparable number of topics, with more compact topic usage.

Relation to prior work
- Dirichlet processes and CRP/EPPFs are classical (Ferguson; Blackwell & MacQueen; Pitman). Normalized random measures and σ-stable Poisson–Kingman priors provide broad EPPFs for mixture modeling but not directly for mixed-membership grouped data.
- For mixed-membership, HDP and the Chinese restaurant franchise are standard; collapsed inference for HDP requires additional table bookkeeping and is only partially collapsed in direct-assignment form.
- Recent BNBP work (e.g., Zhou et al.; Broderick et al.; Heaukulani & Roy) established modeling and predictive structures (negative-binomial/“multi-scoop” IBP) and inference via truncation or slice sampling, but did not provide an EPPF for grouped partitions or a fully collapsed sampler.
- The current paper fills this gap for BNBP, with an ECPF→EPPF route inspired by sample-size dependent EPPFs and negative binomial process count-matrix priors.

Quality
- The modeling and augmentation are sound and standard (BNBP as a gamma–Poisson mixture; Poissonization of counts; DirMult and digamma constructions). The ECPF (Eq. 6), count-matrix prior (Eq. 7–8), EPPF (Eq. 9), and prediction rule (Eq. 10) appear internally consistent and align with known BNBP count-matrix characterizations.
- The derivations are relegated to the appendix; within the main text, technical steps are sketched clearly enough to follow the logic. It would be helpful to check constants in the new-cluster probability in Eq. (10), as the typesetting suggests a missing normalizing factor involving ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c).
- The topic model sampler (Eq. 12) is clean and intuitive: existing-topic probability factors into global popularity times a group-specific term; new-topic probability scales with γ0, rj, and c. The comparison to HDP-LDA clarifies the multiplicative vs. additive shrinkage mechanisms.
- Experiments are sensible: three corpora, multiple η settings, trace plots showing convergence/mixing, and perplexity curves as a function of η and number of topics. However, comparisons to a fully collapsed HDP sampler via CRF (with table variables) are missing; only the direct-assignment baseline is used.

Clarity
- The paper is dense but generally well organized. The ECPF→EPPF methodology is clearly motivated and related to prior EPPF work. The introduction and Section 2 effectively set up the problem and contributions.
- Notation is heavy; a small synthetic example illustrating the prediction rule and how counts are updated across groups would aid intuition. Equation (10) needs careful proofreading to avoid ambiguity in the new-cluster term. Some minor typos and formatting glitches (e.g., repeated words, missing punctuation, dangling references to figures) should be cleaned.

Originality
- The core contribution—deriving a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership and using it to build a fully collapsed sampler that marginalizes the beta process—is novel and timely. It extends the utility of integer-valued processes in grouped mixture models beyond existing truncation/slice-based inference.
- The ECPF→EPPF construction is of independent methodological interest and may carry over to related processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP), as the authors suggest.

Significance
- Conceptually significant: offers a unifying EPPF viewpoint for mixed-membership with BNBP, paralleling the CRP/CRF role in DP/HDP. This can influence how practitioners design collapsed inference for count-based nonparametric models.
- Practically relevant: the fully collapsed sampler is simple to implement, mixes faster than HDP-LDA direct assignment, and achieves strong perplexity with fewer topics (compactness), which is attractive for interpretability and efficiency.

Strengths
- Clear, principled derivation of ECPF and EPPF for BNBP grouped partitions.
- Simple prediction rule enabling truncation-free, fully collapsed Gibbs sampling.
- Well-motivated topic model, fully collapsed updates, and closed-form parameter updates.
- Empirical evidence of faster convergence/mixing and competitive/better perplexity for similar topic counts.
- Connections to prior BNBP/IBP literature and to sample-size dependent EPPFs.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Fairness of baselines: include a collapsed HDP sampler via CRF (with table variables) or strong modern baselines (e.g., sparse/alias LDA/HDP samplers), and report wall-clock time to convergence, not only per-iteration speed.
- Clarify and rigorously state the new-cluster probability constant in Eq. (10) and the dependence on ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c). Provide a short derivation or pointer.
- Expand discussion on when sample-size dependent EPPFs are preferable and the implications for exchangeability and predictive consistency.
- Sensitivity analyses for γ0, c, and rj priors; ablate the effect of learning rj (which the figures suggest is important).
- Include topic coherence or qualitative topic examples to complement perplexity.
- Minor notation/typo cleanup; ensure all referenced figures/equations are correct and self-contained.

Pros
- Novel EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership; first fully collapsed sampler marginalizing the beta process.
- Strong theoretical grounding with clear augmentation strategy.
- Practical, easy-to-implement algorithm with improved mixing and compact representations.
- Potentially generalizable methodology to other integer-valued processes.

Cons
- Lack of comparison to fully collapsed HDP (CRF) and stronger baselines; limited runtime analysis.
- Some ambiguity/typos in key predictive equation; heavy notation can impede readability.
- Empirical evaluation centered on perplexity; limited analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters beyond η.

Overall assessment
This is a technically strong and original contribution that advances the theory and practice of mixed-membership modeling with BNBP. With clearer presentation of the prediction rule constants, additional baseline comparisons, and minor clarifications, it would make a valuable addition to the NeurIPS program. I lean toward acceptance.
