- Summary: Derives an EPPF for grouped data under the BNBP by randomizing group sizes to get an ECPF, conditioning on observed sizes, and derives a Poisson i.i.d. column representation (digamma-distributed sizes + Dirichlet-multinomial allocations), a prediction rule, and a fully collapsed BNBP topic model with a collapsed Gibbs sampler showing faster mixing and competitive perplexity vs HDP-LDA.

- Technical soundness: Augmentation and ECPF construction are standard and correctly used; lemmas and Poisson column representation are plausible and consistent with CRM results; sample-size-dependent EPPF and prediction rule are coherent.

- Proofs and technical clarifications: Proofs are deferred to the appendix; need explicit conditions ensuring normalization of the digamma distribution and finiteness of the Poisson mean, and clarification of exchangeability properties under the sample-size-dependent EPPF.

- Empirics: Results support faster convergence/mixing and compactness, but should additionally report effective sample sizes/autocorrelations, wall-clock times to target perplexity, and uncertainty/error bars across splits/seeds.

- Clarity and presentation: Paper is dense with heavy notation—add a symbol table, fix typos, include an intuition box contrasting size-free vs sample-size-dependent EPPFs, give a brief derivation/normalization/moments for the digamma distribution, and provide pseudocode for the collapsed sampler.

- Originality: Novel route to obtain a mixed-membership EPPF via randomizing group sizes → ECPF → conditioning, a new prediction rule for BNBP, and the identification of the induced column prior structure not present in prior BNBP topic papers.

- Theoretical significance: Provides a generalizable recipe for obtaining EPPFs from integer-valued CRMs, potentially extensible to GNBP/gamma–Poisson processes.

- Practical significance: Fully collapsed sampler is simple, fast, mixes well, and induces multiplicative shrinkage that yields compact models with competitive perplexity.

- Weaknesses and suggestions: Broaden empirical baselines (e.g., GNBP, Pitman–Yor), include non-text grouped datasets, provide sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, rj, report per-iteration complexity/memory and wall-clock comparisons, and more explicitly contrast with concurrent related work.

- Pros (acceptance arguments): Nontrivial theoretical contribution (ECPF/EPPF/prediction rule), practical fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with fast mixing, and useful contrast to HDP’s additive structure.

- Cons (rejection arguments): Narrow empirical evaluation, heavy notation and clarity issues, and deferred proof details that need more main-text intuition.

- Overall recommendation: Accept (high confidence).
