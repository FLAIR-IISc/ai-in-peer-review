Summary
The paper develops a group-size–dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling under the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). Because directly deriving an EPPF for BNBP is hard, the authors first construct a model where group sizes are random, derive the joint law of group sizes and partitions (an exchangeable cluster probability function, ECPF), and then condition on observed group sizes via Bayes’ rule to obtain the desired EPPF. They also show an equivalent beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, derive a tractable prediction rule to simulate partitions, and build a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process. They compare against HDP-LDA on three corpora, reporting faster mixing and comparable or better perplexity at similar numbers of topics, and argue that BNBP achieves a more compact representation.

Relation to prior work is carefully positioned: beyond the DP/NRMI and Pitman’s EPPFs, the work connects to HDP and recent BNBP/IBP/gamma-Poisson literature. The key claim is that previous works provided predictive structures for random count matrices or partially collapsed samplers, but not an EPPF governing exchangeable random partitions of grouped data with fixed observed group sizes nor a fully collapsed BNBP sampler.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The derivations are principled. The ECPF (Lemma 2) follows from a clean Poissonization argument: by introducing Poisson total counts, the joint likelihood factorizes (Eq. 4), enabling conjugate marginalization of the gamma–Poisson layers and, subsequently, of the beta process. The resulting count-matrix prior (Eq. 7) with a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns and the Digam–DirMult factorization (Eq. 8) appears consistent and insightful. The EPPF (Eq. 9) is admittedly complex but aligns with the construction. The prediction rule (Eq. 10) has a clear interpretation: a multiplicative combination of global popularity and local usage, with new-cluster probability controlled by γ0, c, r·, and rj.
- Correctness concerns are minor but worth clarification:
  - Normalization constants in the prediction rule: The new-cluster term depends on γ0 and rj, and c, r·, but the appearance of c+r· (versus the digamma normalization ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c) introduced for the Digam distribution) may confuse readers; a short derivation or remark on the exact constant would improve confidence.
  - Notational consistency and small typos (e.g., “disjoint disjoint”, missing years in references) should be fixed.
- Experiments: The topic modeling evaluation is standard (held-out perplexity), uses three corpora, sweeps η, and reports mixing behavior and per-iteration runtime. The qualitative claims (faster convergence, more compact models, competitive perplexity) are supported. However, details about HDP-LDA hyperparameter learning are sparse (e.g., whether α was inferred or fixed; whether the global DP stick-breaking weights r̃ were integrated out or sampled), which affects fairness. Including results with a fully collapsed HDP sampler (CRF) or at least inferring α would strengthen the comparison. Additional metrics (topic coherence) would give a more complete picture beyond perplexity.

Clarity
- The paper is mathematically dense but mostly well organized. The flow from ECPF to EPPF is logical, and the augmentation gives good intuition. The prediction rule is clearly interpretable and practically useful.
- Suggestions:
  - Provide a compact, intuitive derivation sketch for Lemmas 2–4 in the main text (with full proofs in appendix), especially clarifying the normalization in Eq. 10.
  - Include a simple pseudocode box for the collapsed Gibbs sampler, listing all updates (currently referenced as being in the appendix).
  - Improve notation hygiene (consistent use of dots, sums, and index ranges) and complete reference details (years).
  - Briefly discuss the implications of using sample-size–dependent EPPFs (what properties are gained/lost relative to classical addition rules).

Originality
- The main theoretical contribution—deriving a group-size–dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership modeling and the associated prediction rule—is, to my knowledge, new and not covered by prior BNBP/IBP or σ-stable Poisson–Kingman EPPF work. Prior BNBP works addressed random count matrices, truncation-based inference, or slice sampling; here, the authors provide a fully collapsed route grounded in an EPPF over partitions of grouped data with fixed sizes.
- The count-matrix prior characterization via Poisson number of columns with Digam and DirMult factors is elegant and likely to be reusable.
- The fully collapsed BNBP topic model contrasts meaningfully with HDP-LDA, offering a distinct multiplicative shrinkage mechanism.

Significance
- The EPPF/ECPF construction for mixed-membership modeling closes a gap between mixture-model EPPFs and grouped-data models. This is a substantial conceptual advance in Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Practically, a fully collapsed sampler that avoids truncation or slice variables and mixes faster is valuable for topic modeling and potentially other grouped-count applications.
- The “general method” of randomizing group sizes to obtain an ECPF and then conditioning to get an EPPF seems portable to other integer-valued processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP), opening a line of follow-on research.

Strengths
- Clear, principled probabilistic construction; nontrivial theory.
- Useful prediction rule; intuitive multiplicative popularity term.
- Fully collapsed sampler with good mixing and competitive perplexity.
- Compact model representations at comparable predictive performance.

Weaknesses
- Experimental comparison could be fairer and more comprehensive (HDP hyperparameter inference, CRF baseline, topic coherence).
- Some normalization/notation details under-explained in the main text.
- References incomplete; minor typos.
- Limited ablations (sensitivity to c, γ0; effect of rj prior) and limited discussion of computational scaling beyond per-iteration time.

Questions for the authors
- Please clarify whether α in HDP-LDA was inferred; if not, why not, and how was it set?
- Can you comment on the constant in the new-cluster probability in Eq. 10 versus the digamma normalization?
- How sensitive are results to priors on c, γ0, and rj? Any guidance for practitioners?
- Could the proposed approach be directly adapted to the GNBP or gamma–Poisson processes? Any obstacles?

Overall evaluation
This is a strong paper: technically solid, original, and significant for Bayesian nonparametrics and mixed-membership modeling. With minor clarifications and slightly stronger baselines, it would be an easy accept. I recommend acceptance.
