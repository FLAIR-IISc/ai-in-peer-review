- Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership modeling, filling a key theoretical gap.  
- Uses a sample size–dependent modeling approach to obtain the EPPF and constructs a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler; approach could generalize to other integer-valued processes.  
- BNBP topic model converges faster and mixes better than HDP-LDA, achieving comparable or better predictive performance with fewer topics.  
- Paper is well organized with clear notation; prediction rule and figures provide intuitive understanding of model behavior.  
- Insufficient differentiation from closely related recent work (notably [17] on negative binomial processes).  
- Lacks discussion of limitations: behavior on extremely sparse data, highly variable group sizes, and sensitivity to hyperparameters (γ₀, c, rⱼ).  
- Theoretical contribution could be strengthened by more explicitly situating the BNBP EPPF within the broader class of exchangeable partition models.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel theoretical contribution with meaningful practical benefits and strong empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pro arguments: first EPPF derivation for BNBP; generalizable theoretical framework; practical and computational advantages over HDP-LDA; rigorous empirical evaluation.  
- Con arguments: needs clearer distinction from [17]; more thorough limitations/hyperparameter sensitivity analysis; stronger connections to broader exchangeable partition literature.
