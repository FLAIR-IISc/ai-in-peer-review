- Novel theoretical contribution: first derivation of an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), with sample-size dependence.  
- Methodological approach: derive the EPPF by randomizing group sizes, obtaining the joint distribution, then applying Bayes' rule.  
- Model and inference: propose a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics and the infinite beta process, giving closed-form updates.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates faster convergence, better mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance with fewer topics compared to HDP-LDA.  
- Theoretical strength: fills an important gap in nonparametric Bayesian statistics and distinguishes BNBP’s sample-size-dependent EPPF from single-set EPPFs.  
- Practical strength: sampler is straightforward to implement and yields compact corpus representations useful in applications.  
- Rigor: thorough mathematical proofs and comprehensive experiments on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 with appropriate metrics.  
- Limited comparisons: lacks evaluation against more recent topic models (e.g., discrete infinite logistic normal and other modern nonparametric methods).  
- Scalability concerns: limited analysis and no concrete evidence for applying sparse computation techniques or scaling to very large corpora.  
- Accessibility issue: the EPPF derivation is mathematically dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant both theoretically and practically.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
