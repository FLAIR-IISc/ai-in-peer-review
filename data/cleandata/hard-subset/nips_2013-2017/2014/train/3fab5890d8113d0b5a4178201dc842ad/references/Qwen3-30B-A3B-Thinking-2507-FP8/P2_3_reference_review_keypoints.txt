- Develops a group-size-dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Derives the BNBP EPPF by randomizing group sizes, obtaining the joint distribution of group sizes and partitions, then applying Bayes’ rule.  
- Constructs a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process.  
- Empirical results on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS show faster convergence, better mixing, and more compact corpus representation compared to HDP-LDA.  
- Technically rigorous contribution with thorough mathematical derivations and proofs (appendix provided).  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with consistent notation, illustrative figures, and sufficient implementation details for reproduction.  
- Novel method filling a gap in mixed-membership modeling by providing the first unified EPPF treatment and a multiplicative topic-shrinkage mechanism.  
- Theoretical significance: provides a framework potentially generalizable to other integer-valued stochastic processes (e.g., gamma–Poisson).  
- Practical significance: offers faster, more compact topic modeling with potential real-world benefits over HDP’s additive shrinkage.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to other nonparametric methods (e.g., gamma–Poisson, gamma–negative binomial).  
- Weakness: experimental validation is confined to topic modeling, so generalizability to other mixed-membership tasks is unclear.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and discussion of computational trade-offs versus HDP-LDA.  
- Overall assessment: a strong, original, and well-executed theoretical contribution with compelling experiments.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
