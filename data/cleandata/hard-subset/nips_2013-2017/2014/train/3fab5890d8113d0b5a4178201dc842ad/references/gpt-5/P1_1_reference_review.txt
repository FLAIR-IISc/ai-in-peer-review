Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies mixed-membership modeling for grouped data via the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). While nonparametric mixture priors like the Dirichlet process (DP) admit fully collapsed inference through marginal EPPFs (e.g., CRP/CRF), analogous EPPFs for integer-valued processes used in mixed-membership models (gamma–Poisson, BNBP, GNBP) have been lacking. The authors’ main contributions are: (1) a derivation of the BNBP’s exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for random group sizes by marginalizing the beta process; (2) from this, a group-size dependent EPPF for exchangeable random partitions of grouped data via Bayes’ rule; (3) a simple prediction rule enabling simulation of partitions; and (4) a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite beta process, with closed-form updates. Experiments on three corpora compare against HDP-LDA, finding faster convergence, better mixing, and comparable/lower perplexity at comparable numbers of topics, often with more compact representations.

The work connects to: DP/NRMIs and their EPPFs (Ferguson, Pitman), HDP and the Chinese restaurant franchise (Teh et al.), negative-binomial processes and Poisson factorization (Zhou et al.), and recent predictive structures for BNBP (negative-binomial IBP, “ice-cream” buffet). Distinct from feature models on infinite random count matrices, the focus here is a prior on partitions of grouped data with fixed row sums, a gap not addressed by prior BNBP/GNBP work.

Quality
- Theory: The augmentation Xj|Θj ~ PP(Θj), Θj|B ~ ΓP[rj, B/(1−B)], B ~ BP(c,B0), and Poissonization yield a factorized likelihood, leading to an ECPF after marginalizing Θ and B. The random count matrix view gives a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns with a digamma-distributed column size and Dirichlet-multinomial allocation across groups. The EPPF is then obtained via Bayes and a normalization over all compatible count matrices. The prediction rule is intuitive (global popularity × group-specific popularity). The measure-theoretic conditions (γ0 finite, c>0, rj>0) ensure finiteness of KJ’s Poisson rate γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)].
- Inference: The collapsed sampler for the topic model is clean and fully marginalizes the beta process; update equations (including rj, γ0) are said to be closed-form. The multiplicative shrinkage term differs from HDP’s additive mechanism and is well explained.
- Experiments: Convergence/mixing diagnostics via trace plots are informative; perplexity comparisons across η values and matched topic counts suggest the BNBP achieves similar predictive power with fewer topics and mixes faster. Runtime claims are reasonable for unoptimized Matlab.

Caveats:
- Proofs are deferred to the appendix; a brief proof sketch of key steps (ECPF, especially normalization in the EPPF) would strengthen the main text.
- Hyperparameters: It is unclear whether c is inferred (γ0 and rj are, but c appears fixed). Sensitivity to c and to priors on rj is not reported.
- Experimental fairness: HDP-LDA is compared using the direct assignment sampler; stronger baselines include the CRF collapsed sampler and inference of α. No comparisons to related Poisson factorization/NB-process topic models (e.g., PFA, GNBP) are provided.
- Scalability: While per-iteration times are given, there is no large-scale stress test, parallelization, or sparse sampling evaluation (though discussed).
- The EPPF is sample-size dependent (as acknowledged), which departs from classical EPPFs; more discussion of implications (e.g., projective properties, consistency under addition) would be useful.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized and technically careful, with good motivation and connections to prior work. The prediction rule provides welcome intuition. Some notation is dense; small editorial issues remain (“perdition rule,” repeated words, minor typos). A high-level algorithm box for the sampler and a short proof sketch in the main text would improve readability.

Originality
The derivation of an EPPF for mixed-membership using an integer-valued process, and the explicit ECPF→EPPF route for BNBP, appear novel. The fully collapsed sampler that also marginalizes the beta process, and the simple prediction rule, are valuable additions. The work differs from prior BNBP/IBP-style results that target feature matrices rather than grouped partitions with fixed row sums.

Significance
Conceptually, the paper fills a gap: EPPF-style treatments for mixed-membership with integer-valued processes. Practically, the collapsed sampler is simple, fast, and competitive in perplexity while being compact in topic usage, which may attract users of HDP-LDA. The methodology could extend to other count processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP), potentially broadening impact.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Do you infer c? If not, please clarify and discuss sensitivity; if yes, provide the update and empirical behavior.
- Provide an explicit expression or derivation sketch for p(m|r,γ0,c) used in Bayes’ step to obtain the EPPF.
- Compare to the HDP CRF collapsed sampler (and/or infer α) for a fair baseline; optionally include a Poisson factorization baseline.
- Discuss projectivity/consistency properties of the sample-size dependent EPPF and any implications for exchangeability across varying m.
- Add sensitivity analyses for rj priors and c, and a brief complexity discussion per-token per-iteration.
- Minor typos: “perdition rule”→“prediction rule”; “disjoint disjoint”; a few formatting glitches.

Pros
- Novel ECPF/EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP in mixed-membership.
- Fully collapsed, simple Gibbs sampler; good mixing and speed.
- Clear probabilistic story linking count and partition views.
- Competitive or better perplexity at similar topic counts; compact models.
- Code available.

Cons
- Proofs/normalization details mostly in appendix; limited intuition for EPPF.
- Comparison limited to HDP direct assignment; no CRF or other baselines.
- Unclear treatment/sensitivity of c; limited hyperparameter analysis.
- Modest scale experiments; no strong scalability study.

Recommendation: Weak accept. Solid theoretical contribution with a practical sampler and encouraging results; strengthen empirical comparisons and clarify hyperparameter handling. Confidence: High.
