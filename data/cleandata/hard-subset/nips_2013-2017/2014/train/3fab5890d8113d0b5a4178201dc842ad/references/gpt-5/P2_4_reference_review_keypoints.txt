- Paper constructs a group-size–dependent EPPF for BNBP by introducing random group sizes to derive an ECPF, then conditioning on observed sizes to obtain the EPPF, plus a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and prediction rule, and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for topic models.
- Derivations are principled: ECPF follows from a Poissonization argument enabling conjugate marginalization and yielding a consistent count-matrix prior with Digam–DirMult factorization.
- The EPPF is complex but consistent with the construction; the prediction rule cleanly combines global popularity and local usage with new-cluster probability controlled by γ0, c, and r·.
- Minor correctness/clarity issues: normalization constants in the prediction rule (appearance of c+r· vs. digamma normalization) need clarification and a short derivation would help.
- Notational inconsistencies and small typos (e.g., duplicated words, missing reference years) should be fixed.
- Experiments show faster mixing and competitive or better perplexity versus HDP-LDA and more compact models, but fairness concerns remain about HDP hyperparameter handling.
- Comparison would be strengthened by using a fully collapsed HDP baseline (CRF) or inferring α, and by adding topic-coherence and additional ablation metrics (sensitivity to c, γ0, rj).
- Clarity suggestions: include compact derivation sketches for Lemmas 2–4 in the main text, a pseudocode box for the collapsed Gibbs sampler, and improve notation hygiene.
- Originality: deriving a group-size–dependent EPPF for BNBP and the associated prediction rule appears novel relative to prior BNBP/IBP and Poisson–Kingman work.
- The count-matrix prior characterized as a Poisson number of columns with Digam and DirMult factors is elegant and likely reusable.
- Significance: closes a gap between mixture-model EPPFs and grouped-data models, and offers a practically useful fully collapsed sampler that avoids truncation or slice variables.
- Strengths: principled probabilistic construction, intuitive prediction rule, fully collapsed sampler with good mixing, and compact model representations.
- Weaknesses: experimental comparisons could be fairer and more comprehensive, some normalization/notation details under-explained, incomplete references, and limited ablations and scaling discussion.
- Questions for authors requested: whether α in HDP-LDA was inferred; clarification on the new-cluster constant vs. digamma normalization; sensitivity to priors on c, γ0, rj; and adaptability to GNBP or gamma–Poisson processes.
- Overall evaluation: strong, technically solid, original, and significant work that would be an easy accept after minor clarifications and stronger baselines.
