

# Peer Review: Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling

## Summary and Context
This paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by developing a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive the EPPF through a novel approach of first randomizing group sizes to obtain an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF), then applying Bayes' rule to arrive at the desired EPPF. They further develop a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process. The paper demonstrates the BNBP's advantages over HDP-LDA in convergence speed, mixing properties, and compact topic representation.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Contribution**: The derivation of the BNBP's EPPF via the group-size dependent model is a significant technical advance. Previous work on mixed-membership modeling (e.g., HDP [6], BNBP [10, 11]) lacked a unified EPPF framework, and this paper fills that gap with rigorous mathematical development.

2. **Practical Impact**: The BNBP topic model demonstrates clear advantages in practice. The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler converges faster (often <100 iterations vs. >300 for HDP-LDA), mixes better, and achieves comparable or better predictive performance with fewer topics, making it valuable for applications requiring compact representations.

3. **Methodological Novelty**: The prediction rule (10) and the multiplicative mechanism for topic popularity (n−ji·k/(c+n−ji·k+r·)·(n−jijk+rj)) provides a distinct theoretical perspective on topic modeling compared to HDP-LDA's additive mechanism.

4. **Experimental Rigor**: The evaluation on three standard corpora with careful parameter tuning and multiple η values provides strong empirical validation of the theoretical claims.

## Weaknesses and Concerns

1. **Clarification of Related Work**: The paper claims "fully collapsed inference is available for neither the BNBP nor the GNBP" while citing [16, 17] which explicitly marginalize out the beta process. This contradiction needs clarification. [17] presents the "ice cream" buffet process, which is directly related to the BNBP, and the paper should better explain how their approach differs.

2. **EPPF Derivation Complexity**: While the derivation of the EPPF (9) is mathematically sound, it's extremely complex, making it difficult to grasp the practical implications. The paper could better highlight which aspects of the EPPF are most useful for practitioners.

3. **Perplexity Claim**: The paper claims "state-of-the-art predictive performance" but shows BNBP often has higher perplexity than HDP-LDA for the same η. The claim should be refined to state that BNBP achieves comparable performance with fewer topics, which is the key advantage.

4. **Comparison with [17]**: The relationship to [17] needs clearer elaboration. [17] also derives a process for random count matrices, but the paper doesn't explain how their group-size dependent approach differs from [17]'s framework.

5. **Computational Details**: The paper states the BNBP sampler is "straightforward to implement" but doesn't provide clear implementation details or discuss how the method scales with very large datasets (beyond the NIPS12 corpus).

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong technical contribution with clear practical significance for Bayesian nonparametric modeling. The paper makes a valuable advance in understanding the theoretical foundations of mixed-membership modeling and provides a practically superior topic modeling algorithm.

The quality is excellent - the mathematical derivations are rigorous, the experiments are thorough, and the claims are well-supported. The clarity is generally good but could be improved with better explanations of the EPPF's practical implications. The originality is high, as the paper addresses a fundamental gap in the literature with a novel methodological approach. The significance is substantial, offering both theoretical insights and practical benefits for a wide range of applications.

## Recommendations

**Accept**. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian nonparametrics with clear theoretical advances and practical benefits. While some clarifications on related work are needed, the core contributions are strong and well-supported.

**Pro**: 
- First unified treatment of EPPF for mixed-membership modeling
- Novel derivation method for EPPF via group-size dependent model
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with faster convergence and better mixing
- Demonstrated practical advantages over HDP-LDA in compact topic representation

**Con**:
- Some contradictions in related work claims need clarification
- EPPF complexity could be better contextualized for practitioners
- Perplexity claim needs refinement to emphasize topic count efficiency

The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it advances the theoretical foundations of Bayesian nonparametric methods while demonstrating practical utility in a widely studied application (topic modeling). The work is sufficiently distinct from recent NIPS papers (e.g., [10, 11, 17]) to warrant publication.

This paper will be valuable to the NIPS community as it provides both theoretical insights and a practical algorithm that addresses a longstanding gap in mixed-membership modeling. The methods could be extended to other integer-valued stochastic processes, opening new research directions in Bayesian nonparametrics.
