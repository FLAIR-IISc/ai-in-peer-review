Summary
This paper develops a group-size–dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). The key idea is to obtain the EPPF not by direct marginalization (which is intractable), but by: (i) introducing a sample-size–dependent model that randomizes group sizes, (ii) deriving an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for the joint law over random group sizes and partitions, and (iii) using Bayes’ rule to condition on observed group sizes and obtain the desired EPPF. The authors further show that the resulting latent count matrix prior can be seen as a Poisson number of i.i.d. columns with sizes following a “digamma” distribution and group-wise allocation following Dirichlet-multinomial. From the EPPF, they derive a simple prediction rule for simulation/inference. Leveraging these results, they propose a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process. Experiments on three corpora compare against HDP-LDA (direct-assignment sampler), demonstrating faster mixing and competitive or better perplexities for a comparable number of topics, often with a more compact representation.

Relation to prior work
- Extends the EPPF view (Pitman; σ-stable Poisson–Kingman family) beyond Dirichlet/NRMIs to an integer-valued process (BNBP), where previous work offered collapsed inference for single-group mixture models but not mixed-membership.
- Connects to BNBP-based count modeling (Zhou et al.; Broderick et al.) and IBP variants for count matrices (negative binomial/multi-scoop/ice-cream buffet), but addresses a distinct target: the EPPF for grouped-data partitions with fixed group sizes and an accompanying prediction rule.
- Provides a unifying construction pathway (randomize group sizes → ECPF → condition to get EPPF) that appears generalizable to other integer-valued processes (e.g., gamma–Poisson, gamma–negative binomial).

Strengths
- Novel and bold contribution: a principled route to EPPFs for mixed-membership from integer-valued processes. To my knowledge, this closes a notable gap relative to HDP’s CRF-based marginalization.
- Technically substantive: nontrivial marginalizations (to ECPF), a clear count-matrix prior with Poisson number of columns, the digamma column-size distribution, and a simple prediction rule.
- Practical impact: a fully collapsed sampler that integrates out the infinite-dimensional beta process plus local/global parameters. This is algorithmically appealing and rare for nonparametric mixed-membership models outside HDP/CRF.
- Empirically compelling: faster convergence/mixing than HDP-LDA DA sampler; comparable or lower perplexity with fewer topics for moderate η, suggesting more compact models.

Weaknesses and questions
- Baseline choice: comparisons are to the HDP-LDA direct-assignment sampler (only partially collapsed). The fairest baseline would include the CRF collapsed sampler or at least cite why it’s impractical here. This matters since the main empirical claim is faster mixing and competitive perplexity.
- Scope of baselines: no comparisons to other nonparametric topic models (e.g., Pitman–Yor–based HDP variants) or to NB-process based Poisson factorization with strong inference schemes. The “state-of-the-art” claim should be toned down or supported with richer baselines.
- Clarity/notation: the paper is dense. The derivation from Eq. (7) to the i.i.d. column construction (digamma + DirMult) is important and could use a concise proof sketch in the main text. The nonstandard “digamma distribution” should be contextualized with intuition (e.g., tail behavior, moments) and clearer parameterization. Some typos (e.g., “Kigman”) and formatting issues remain.
- Assumptions and exchangeability: the EPPF is group-size dependent and does not satisfy the addition rule. While this is stated and justified, a brief discussion of consequences for modeling (e.g., predictive consistency under sub/super-sampling within groups) would help readers calibrate when this is desirable.
- Sensitivity: the role of c, γ0, and the Gamma priors for rj is central (Figure 1 shows strong effects). More ablation/sensitivity analyses would increase confidence in robustness, especially across corpora with different document-length distributions.

Quality
Overall technically sound, with substantial derivations backed by appendices. The prediction rule is elegant and consistent with the constructed EPPF. The empirical evaluation is honest about trade-offs (BNBP’s stronger shrinkage vs HDP’s additive mechanism). The main caveat is the baseline choice.

Clarity
Well-organized but heavy. Adding a running example, a short intuitive derivation of the column process, and a boxed summary of the final sampler steps would greatly aid readability. A brief comparison table of prediction rules (LDA, HDP-DA, HDP-CRF, BNBP) would also help.

Originality
High. The route to an EPPF for mixed-membership from BNBP via ECPF and conditioning appears new and important. The fully collapsed sampler that marginalizes the beta process is also a notable advance.

Significance
High potential. This work can spur a new line of collapsed inference for integer-valued processes in mixed-membership models beyond BNBP, with practical algorithmic benefits. The topic modeling application is compelling and likely to be adopted.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add comparisons to a collapsed HDP (CRF) sampler or justify omission; include runtime/mixing plots for that baseline if feasible.
- Provide an intuition/proof sketch for the Poisson i.i.d. column representation and properties of the digamma distribution.
- Include sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, and priors on rj; report posterior over number of topics vs these hyperparameters.
- Tone down “state-of-the-art” claims or broaden baselines.
- Minor: fix typos, and standardize notation.

Pros (arguments for acceptance)
- Conceptually novel pathway to EPPFs for mixed-membership from integer-valued processes.
- Fully collapsed sampler that is simple, fast-mixing, and practically useful.
- Empirical evidence of compact, competitive models and improved mixing.
- Likely to influence subsequent work on gamma/GNBP EPPFs and inference.

Cons (arguments against acceptance)
- Missing comparison to the strongest HDP baseline (CRF).
- Limited sensitivity analyses; heavy notation may limit accessibility.
- Claims of state-of-the-art not fully substantiated across baselines.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper introduces a significant new concept and direction with strong theoretical and algorithmic contributions. While the experimental section could be broadened, the novelty and potential impact justify acceptance.

Reviewer confidence
High on technical assessment of Bayesian nonparametrics and mixed-membership models; medium on exhaustive empirical benchmarking.
