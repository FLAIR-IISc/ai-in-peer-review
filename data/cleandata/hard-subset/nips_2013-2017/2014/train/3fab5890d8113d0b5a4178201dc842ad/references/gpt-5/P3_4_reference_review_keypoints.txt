- Derives a sample-size dependent EPPF for mixed-membership under the BNBP by Poissonizing group sizes, marginalizing via a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, then conditioning back on observed sizes.  
- Derives a prediction rule and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process.  
- Empirically, the BNBP sampler mixes faster than an HDP-LDA direct-assignment sampler, often attains lower perplexity for comparable K, and yields more compact topic representations via multiplicative shrinkage.  
- Situates work relative to CRMs/NRMIs (Pitman; Lijoi–Prünster) and notes BNBP lies outside the sigma-stable Poisson–Kingman family.  
- Builds on prior BNBP/PFA and combinatorial BNBP/IBP literature but uniquely focuses on grouped partitions with fixed row sums rather than binary features or infinite count matrices.  
- Contrasts with HDP/Chinese restaurant franchise: full marginalization here avoids CRF bookkeeping.  
- The Poissonization augmentation (Xj|Θj ~ PP(Θj), Θj|B ~ ΓP[rj, B/(1−B)], B ~ BP(c,B0)) is standard and clarifies the mixed-membership link.  
- The ECPF (Eq.6) includes exp{−γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)]} from unobserved atoms and implies a Poisson number of columns, digamma (Sibuya)-distributed cluster sizes, and Dirichlet–multinomial row allocations.  
- The sample-size dependent EPPF (Eq.9) is algebraically complex but conceptually sound and underpins the predictive rule for the marginal Gibbs sampler.  
- The prediction rule factorizes into an “across-group popularity” term and a “within-group” term (n−jijk + rj), which is interpretable.  
- There is an apparent inconsistency in the new-cluster probability constants: sometimes expressed with ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c), elsewhere as γ0 rj/(c+r·); the reviewer requests reconciliation and a short derivation/pointer.  
- The topic-model sampler (Eq.12) is a clean fully collapsed rule, but the V·γ0/(c+r·) rj new-topic term needs clarification on where digamma normalization goes after conditioning on m and integrating φ.  
- Strength: provides a general recipe to derive EPPFs for mixed-membership models built from integer-valued CRMs by randomizing and conditioning group sizes.  
- Strength: gives a clear probabilistic characterization of BNBP count matrices and a simple, fast-mixing fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.  
- Strength: offers insightful comparison with HDP-LDA on multiplicative versus additive shrinkage and effects on compactness and perplexity.  
- Weakness: must resolve inconsistencies/missing constants in prediction rules (normalization of new-cluster term).  
- Weakness: experimental fairness—HDP baseline is direct-assignment (partially collapsed); add or compare to the CRF fully collapsed HDP sampler.  
- Weakness: missing sensitivity/ablation on inferring rj—request experiments fixing rj, show impact on partitions/perplexity, and report posterior summaries (e.g., histograms vs document length).  
- Weakness: broader baselines absent—include Poisson factorization/NBFA or Gamma–Poisson topic models for perplexity comparison.  
- Weakness: theoretical note—add brief discussion of projectivity/exchangeability properties (behavior under merging/splitting groups or adding observations).  
- Weakness: clarity/notation dense and repetitive; suggest a worked example (e.g., generating NJ via Eq.8), fix typos, and include sampler pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Quality: assessed as high with solid probabilistic derivations and a practically useful sampler, conditional on resolving normalization and experimental issues.  
- Originality: strong—novel group-size dependent EPPF for mixed-membership and a fully collapsed marginalization of the beta process.  
- Significance: high—recipe likely to influence future integer-valued CRM mixed-membership models and the sampler is practically valuable.  
- Recommendation: broadly positive; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying predictive constants and strengthening experimental baselines and ablations.
