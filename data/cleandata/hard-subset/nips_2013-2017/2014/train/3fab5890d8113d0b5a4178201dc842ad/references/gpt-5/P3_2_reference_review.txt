Summary
The paper develops a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling induced by the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). The key methodological contribution is to (i) introduce a sample-size dependent construction that treats group sizes as random via a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, (ii) derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) by marginalizing the beta process, and (iii) obtain the desired EPPF for fixed observed group sizes via Bayes’ rule. The authors further derive a simple prediction rule that enables Gibbs sampling without truncation and apply this to a fully collapsed BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process. Empirically, the sampler mixes quickly and yields strong perplexity with fewer topics than HDP-LDA on three corpora.

Relation to prior work
- Connects to Pitman’s combinatorial stochastic processes and EPPFs for mixture models, but extends to mixed-membership with group-size dependence (cf. Zhou & Walker on sample-size-dependent species models).
- Builds on BNBP/gamma–Poisson process literature showing count-process views of mixed-membership (Zhou et al., Broderick et al., Heaukulani & Roy; negative-binomial IBP variants), and on Poisson–Kingman marginal samplers (Lomelí et al.), while noting BNBP is outside that class.
- Contrasts with HDP’s Chinese Restaurant Franchise (CRF), providing an alternative, fully collapsed sampler for BNBP topic modeling with a new multiplicative prediction rule.

Strengths
- Conceptual: Clear bridge from integer-valued processes to EPPFs for grouped data via ECPF + Bayes rule. This is a useful general recipe that could apply to other count processes (e.g., GNBP, gamma–Poisson).
- Technical: Nontrivial marginalization over the beta process yields a closed-form ECPF with an interpretable normalization involving digamma functions. The induced random count matrix prior (Poisson number of i.i.d. columns with digamma size and Dirichlet–multinomial allocation) is elegant and insightful.
- Inference: Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that avoids truncation and the CRF’s bookkeeping. The prediction rule clearly separates global cluster popularity and group-local dispersion in a multiplicative way.
- Empirical: Good convergence speed and mixing; persuasive diagnostics. At matched topic counts and moderate η, BNBP achieves competitive or better perplexity than HDP-LDA while using fewer topics.

Weaknesses and questions
- Sample-size dependence: The EPPF depends on group sizes; while acknowledged and justified by [Zhou & Walker], the implications for projectivity and comparison to size-independent EPPFs could be discussed more. For J=1, does the model recover any known special cases, and can the limiting behavior be characterized?
- Normalization and intuition: The normalization term exp{-γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)]} and the digamma distribution arise from integrating over the beta process Lévy measure. A brief intuitive derivation in the main text (not only the appendix) would aid reproducibility.
- Prediction rule constants: The presentation around the new-cluster probability vs. the appearance of ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c) is slightly confusing; a clean, self-contained statement with all constants (and a check against Lemma 2/8) would help. There are minor formatting glitches around Eq. (10)/Section 2.2 that obscure the constants.
- Empirical baselines: The comparison is limited to HDP-LDA with a direct assignment sampler. It omits (i) a collapsed CRF sampler for HDP, (ii) Pitman–Yor (PDP)-based topic models, and (iii) other count-based nonparametric topic models (e.g., negative-binomial topic models, GNBP). Since the claim is “state-of-the-art predictive performance,” broader baselines would strengthen the case.
- Fairness/details of HDP: The handling of HDP hyperparameters (e.g., α, γ0) is not fully specified; were they fixed, sampled, or optimized? This can materially affect perplexity and mixing. Include priors/sampling steps or tuned values.
- Evaluation: Perplexity computation is standard but a left-to-right evaluation or document completion variant would be more comparable to prior topic-model evaluations. Also, reporting wall-clock per token and memory would quantify the practical gains of “fully collapsed.”
- Role/identifiability of rj: rj critically control overdispersion and shrinkage. Some posterior summaries, priors’ sensitivity, and a brief discussion of identifiability (e.g., trade-offs with γ0 and c) would be helpful.

Quality
Overall technically sound. The derivations are consistent with the beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and known Lévy–Khintchine calculus; the random count matrix characterization is particularly compelling. Some proofs are deferred and a few constants/formatting issues reduce clarity, but the core is credible. Experiments are adequate but could be broader and more tightly controlled.

Clarity
Well-organized but dense. Notation is heavy; minor typos (“disjoint disjoint,” formatting around ψ lines, footnote links in text) detract. Adding a high-level roadmap figure (augment → ECPF → EPPF → prediction rule) and a concise algorithm box would improve readability.

Originality
High. To my knowledge, this is the first explicit EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership partitions with a fully collapsed sampler that marginalizes the beta process. The construction is novel and likely reusable.

Significance
Significant. Unifying ECPF→EPPF for count processes fills a gap analogous to CRP/CRF for DP/HDP, and the practical collapsed sampler for BNBP topic modeling is likely to be adopted. The insights on multiplicative shrinkage vs. HDP’s additive mechanism are also valuable.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify constants and provide a compact, self-contained statement of Lemmas 2–4 with proof sketches.
- Add comparisons to a collapsed HDP-CRF sampler and a PDP-based topic model; report hyperparameter treatment.
- Provide posterior summaries of rj and sensitivity to c, γ0.
- Clean up typos/formatting; move essential intuition from appendix to main text.

Pros
- Novel and principled derivation of EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership.
- Fully collapsed, truncation-free Gibbs sampler with strong mixing.
- Clear conceptual link between count processes and exchangeable partitions.
- Empirical evidence of compact representations with competitive perplexity.

Cons
- Sample-size dependent EPPF may raise questions about projectivity; limited discussion.
- Baseline coverage and evaluation protocol could be stronger.
- Minor clarity/formatting issues; some constants obscured.

Overall recommendation: Accept (weak accept to accept). This is a technically solid, original contribution with clear significance to Bayesian nonparametrics and mixed-membership modeling. My confidence is high.
