Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling. The main technical contribution is to derive a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP by first constructing an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) under a model that randomizes group sizes, and then conditioning on the observed sizes via Bayes’ rule. This yields a prediction rule for partition simulation and enables a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that integrates out topics, topic weights, and the beta process. The work connects to and extends prior lines on Dirichlet process EPPFs/CRP [Blackwell & MacQueen; Pitman], NRMIs, and integer-valued processes for counts (gamma–Poisson, GNBP, BNBP), as well as BNBP/IBP-style constructions for random count matrices [Heaukulani & Roy; Zhou et al.]. Empirically, the paper compares the proposed sampler to HDP-LDA on three corpora, reporting faster mixing and competitive or better perplexity when the number of topics is matched, with fewer topics overall.

Strengths
- Nontrivial theoretical development: a sample-size dependent BNBP EPPF for grouped data, with a coherent path from ECPF to EPPF and a simple prediction rule.
- Clear algorithmic payoff: a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic modeling without truncation or slice sampling, with closed-form updates.
- Empirical evidence suggests faster convergence/mixing than a common HDP-LDA sampler and good perplexity with more compact topic representations.
- The paper situates the result within the literature on exchangeable partitions and count processes, and the connection to Dirichlet-multinomial/digamma structure is insightful.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Sample-size dependent EPPF: The construction explicitly relaxes the addition rule and yields group-size dependent EPPFs. While [Zhou & Walker] justifies such models for species sampling, this is a nonstandard choice that weakens the usual EPPF invariance properties. The implications for posterior coherence/Kolmogorov consistency and sensitivity to the conditioning set (e.g., different group-size patterns) are not discussed.
- Generality claims: The introduction suggests a general method to obtain EPPFs for mixed-membership using integer-valued processes; however, only the BNBP is worked out. No demonstrations for gamma–Poisson or GNBP are provided, so the generality remains a claim rather than a result.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and possibly unbalanced:
  - The HDP baseline uses the direct assignment sampler (partially collapsed). Stronger baselines exist: fully collapsed CRF samplers (albeit with bookkeeping) and modern inference variants. Without these, the mixing-speed comparisons are not conclusive.
  - There is no comparison to other nonparametric topic priors that encourage power-law behavior (e.g., PYP-HDP), nor to prior BNBP inference methods (finite truncation/slice) to isolate the benefit of the new collapsed sampler.
  - Metrics focus on perplexity; no topic quality/coherence or robustness studies. Hyperparameter sensitivity (c, γ0, priors on rj) and stability across random seeds are not reported.
  - Runtime is reported per-iteration in unoptimized Matlab; fair wall-clock comparisons normalized per token and per effective sample are missing.
- Clarity/notation issues:
  - The “digamma distribution” is not standard; although cited, more intuition and properties (moments, tail behavior) would aid readers.
  - Some notational slips (e.g., Dirichlet posterior for φk appears to index by documents rather than vocabulary) and heavy reliance on an appendix for key proofs. The main text should include at least proof sketches for Lemmas 2–4 to aid verification.
- Theoretical guarantees: Beyond exchangeability statements, there is no discussion of identifiability or posterior consistency under the group-size dependent EPPF, nor of asymptotic behavior of KJ as data grow.

Quality
Technically solid in parts, but several steps are deferred to the appendix and the central reliance on sample-size dependent EPPFs is insufficiently interrogated. The algorithmic derivations are plausible and consistent with standard conjugacies. Empirical support is suggestive but not comprehensive, and baseline selection favors the proposed method.

Clarity
Generally well-written with useful intuition for the prediction rule. However, the exposition is dense, symbols are overloaded, and some definitions (e.g., the digamma distribution) deserve more space. Proof sketches in the main text would improve readability and verifiability.

Originality
Moderate. Deriving an EPPF for the BNBP in the grouped setting and using it to obtain a fully collapsed sampler appears novel and goes beyond prior BNBP inference based on truncation/slicing. However, the use of sample-size dependent EPPFs follows prior work, and the application to topic modeling is incremental relative to extensive HDP/BNBP literature.

Significance
Potentially useful for researchers working on nonparametric count/mixed-membership models who want collapsed samplers without truncation. That said, the practical advantage over established HDP/PYP methods needs stronger, fairer evidence, and the generality claims should be substantiated.

Detailed suggestions
- Include proof sketches for Lemmas 2–4; clarify assumptions needed for the ECPF→EPPF conditioning step and discuss implications of sample-size dependence.
- Add comparisons to (i) CRF-based HDP samplers, (ii) PYP-HDP, and (iii) prior BNBP inference (truncation/slice), with wall-clock and effective sample size analyses.
- Report topic coherence and sensitivity to η, c, γ0, and priors on rj. Provide posterior summaries for rj to illustrate their role.
- Clarify the definition/properties of the digamma distribution; provide intuition for its role in cluster sizes.
- Fix notation issues (e.g., Dirichlet posterior over φk should be V-dimensional).
- Temper claims of generality or include at least one additional process (e.g., gamma–Poisson) worked out.

Pros
- Novel EPPF/prediction rule for BNBP in mixed-membership.
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form updates.
- Empirical indication of faster mixing and compact representations.

Cons
- Nonstandard sample-size dependent EPPF with limited discussion of implications.
- Incomplete and potentially unbalanced empirical comparisons.
- Generality not demonstrated beyond BNBP.
- Clarity and verification hindered by heavy appendix reliance.

Recommendation
Leaning toward reject in current form. The theoretical contribution is interesting, but the reliance on sample-size dependent EPPFs needs deeper justification, and the empirical evaluation should be strengthened and made fairer against established baselines. With these addressed, the work could be impactful.
