- Paper derives a group-size dependent EPPF for mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), obtained by randomizing group sizes to get an ECPF then applying Bayes' rule.  
- Develops a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out the infinite-dimensional beta process.  
- Theoretically novel: provides a technically sophisticated, generalizable method to derive EPPFs for integer-valued stochastic processes, filling a gap for BNBP/GNBP.  
- Methodological clarity: presents a clear prediction rule showing BNBP combines global and local topic popularity multiplicatively, explaining stronger topic shrinkage versus HDP.  
- Empirical rigor: experiments on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS show faster convergence (<100 vs >300 iterations), better mixing, and competitive perplexity with fewer topics.  
- Practical impact: closed-form Gibbs updates, straightforward implementation, and reported efficiency (~2.5s/iteration on NIPS with ~180 topics).  
- Conceptual clarity issue: distinction between ECPF and EPPF is obscured by dense notation and needs more intuitive explanation for group-size dependence.  
- Comparative scope limited: omits comparisons to other nonparametric topic models (e.g., discrete infinite logistic normal, infinite gamma-Poisson feature model).  
- Theoretical limitations: paper notes but does not explore implications of EPPF dependence on group sizes, which may matter with highly variable group sizes.  
- Computational analysis incomplete: suggests sparse computation could help but lacks a detailed complexity comparison against HDP-LDA.  
- Overall assessment: significant, rigorous contribution that offers a compelling alternative to HDP-LDA and a general framework for deriving EPPFs.  
- Recommendation: Accept — pros include novel EPPF derivation, faster fully collapsed sampler, and strong empirical results; cons are mainly expositional and comparative gaps.
