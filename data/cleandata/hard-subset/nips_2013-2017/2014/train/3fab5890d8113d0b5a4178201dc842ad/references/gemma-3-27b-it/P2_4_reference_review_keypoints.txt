- Derives the previously open Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), filling a theoretical gap.
- Develops a prediction rule and provides a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based mixed-membership models.
- Demonstrates practical utility with a BNBP topic model showing competitive performance and often lower perplexity than HDP-LDA on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12.
- Novel and original contribution that generalizes prior work and connects BNBP to existing EPPF frameworks.
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and supported by proofs in the Appendix.
- Provides useful practical implementation details and convergence/mixing analysis showing advantages over HDP-LDA.
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with standalone lemmas improving readability despite dense math.
- The core derivations are mathematically complex and may be hard to follow for non-experts; Equation 9 needs clearer exposition or simplification.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to text corpora; broader domain tests (e.g., image segmentation, single-cell RNA-seq) would strengthen claims.
- Results are sensitive to hyperparameter η; more thorough sensitivity analysis and guidance on choosing hyperparameters are needed.
- Computational cost (~2.5s per iteration on a large corpus) is noted but lacks detailed discussion of scalability and optimization strategies.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, practical inference method, and strong empirical results addressing a long-standing problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility due to complexity, narrow experimental scope, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to Bayesian nonparametrics and recommended for acceptance, with suggestions to add intuitive explanation of the EPPF, broader experiments, and scalability discussion.
- Rating and recommendation: Strong Accept (8/10); Accept.
