- Paper derives a group-size–dependent EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) via a group-size–randomized ECPF and Bayes conditioning.
- Constructs a collapsed BNBP topic model by marginalizing topics, topic weights, and the beta process, yielding a simple Gibbs sampler with closed-form updates.
- The ECPF yields an intuitive random count-matrix prior: Poisson number of columns, digamma-distributed column sums, and Dirichlet–multinomial row allocations.
- The resulting EPPF (complex but consistent) and a simple multiplicative prediction rule enable fully collapsed inference and practical sampling.
- Theoretical derivations use standard beta–gamma–Poisson conjugacies and appear technically sound; proofs are in the appendix.
- Empirical results on three corpora show faster convergence/mixing than HDP-LDA’s direct assignment sampler and competitive or better perplexity with fewer topics.
- Code is provided and diagnostics (trace plots) support the claimed mixing advantages.
- Comparison baseline is limited: HDP-LDA used a direct assignment (partially collapsed) sampler rather than a fully collapsed CRF sampler, which may unfairly favor the proposed method.
- Unclear whether HDP hyperparameters (α, γ0, global weights r̃) were inferred or fixed; this affects comparison validity.
- The EPPF’s sample-size dependence is nonstandard; the paper should discuss implications for exchangeability across groups.
- Hyperparameter treatment is unclear (notably c); sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, and rj priors are needed due to their effect on cluster proliferation.
- Empirical baselines could be strengthened by comparisons to PYP-based topic models and NB-process models with advanced sampling schemes.
- Evaluation should include held-out likelihood under identical inferential budgets and runtime vs. topic-count trade-offs to support practical claims.
- Paper is dense but generally well organized; key constructions and intuition are explained, though more on the digamma role and a simulate–condition schematic would help.
- The ECPF→EPPF conditioning trick is a potentially general recipe applicable to other integer-valued processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP).
- Theoretical contribution fills a gap by providing marginal laws enabling fully collapsed inference for count processes in mixed-membership settings.
- Practically significant: the model offers a straightforward collapsed sampler with good mixing and compact representations appealing to practitioners.
- Pros: principled ECPF/EPPF derivation, simple collapsed sampler, strong mixing, code release, and conceptual generality.
- Cons: limited HDP baseline, insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, nonstandard sample-size-dependent EPPF with limited discussion, and narrower empirical evaluation.
- Questions for authors include whether HDP hyperparameters were sampled, how c is handled, requests for sensitivity analyses, comparisons to CRF-based HDP samplers, and preliminary extensions to gamma–Poisson or GNBP.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with novel theoretical contribution and practical sampler; lean toward acceptance pending minor clarifications and improved baseline comparisons.
