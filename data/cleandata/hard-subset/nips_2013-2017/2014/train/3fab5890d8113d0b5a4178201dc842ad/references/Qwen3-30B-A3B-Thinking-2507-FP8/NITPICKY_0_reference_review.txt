

# Review of "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"

This paper proposes a novel approach to mixed-membership modeling using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), focusing on deriving an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP. The authors develop a method to derive the EPPF by first randomizing group sizes and then applying Bayes' rule to arrive at the marginal distribution of group sizes. They further construct a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process.

The main strengths of the paper include a theoretically sound derivation of the BNBP's EPPF, which fills a gap in the literature for mixed-membership modeling. The paper's method to derive EPPFs for integer-valued stochastic processes is novel and could be generalized to other processes. The BNBP topic model demonstrates fast convergence and competitive performance compared to HDP-LDA, with the added benefit of not requiring tuning of topic count or smoothing parameters.

However, the paper contains numerous technical errors and formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility and make it difficult to assess the validity of the claims. These issues are substantial enough to warrant rejection, despite the paper's potentially significant contributions.

**Major Technical Errors:**

1. In Lemma 1's proof, the expression for f(zj, mj |Θj) contains a critical error: it states "e^{-θjk}" instead of the correct e^{-Θj(Ω)}. This mistake propagates through the derivation.

2. The paper contains multiple formatting errors in equations: "γ KJ 0" should be γ_0^{K_J}, "1KJ !" should be 1/K_J!, and "∑m· K′=1" should be ∑_{K'=1}^{m·}.

3. In equation (10), the prediction rule has a critical missing division: "γ0 c+r· rj" should be "γ0/(c+r·) * rj".

4. The text contains a typo: "perdition rule" should be "prediction rule" (appears in section 1.1).

5. In section 2.1, the phrase "disjoint disjoint Borel sets" is clearly a typo.

**Minor but Significant Issues:**

1. The paper claims to be the first to derive the BNBP's EPPF, but [16, 17] already describe similar structures (negative binomial Indian buffet process and "ice cream" buffet process). The paper fails to adequately distinguish its approach from these prior works.

2. The experimental section lacks clarity on how the 50% training split was implemented (per document or overall), and the topic modeling details are ambiguous (e.g., "draw the topics (φk|−) ∼ Dir(η + n1·k, . . . , η + nJ·k)" should specify the correct notation for the Dirichlet parameters).

3. Figure 1 caption states "the jth row of each matrix, which sums to 50," but the paper never explicitly states that mj = 50 for all groups, creating confusion.

4. The paper claims the BNBP topic model "has state-of-the-art predictive performance," but the results in Figure 3 suggest that for common η values, HDP-LDA often has lower perplexity, contradicting this claim.

5. In the comparison with HDP-LDA (equation 14), the paper incorrectly describes the HDP-LDA's direct assignment sampler as "partially collapsed" when the Chinese restaurant franchise actually provides a fully collapsed representation.

**Overall Assessment:**

While the paper presents a theoretically interesting approach to deriving EPPFs for mixed-membership modeling, the numerous technical errors and inconsistencies significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The errors are not merely typographical but represent substantive mistakes in the mathematical derivations that would require substantial reworking to correct. The paper's claims about the BNBP's advantages over HDP-LDA are not fully supported by the experimental results, and the relationship to prior work is not clearly articulated.

The paper's potential contribution is overshadowed by these technical issues. A paper with such fundamental errors in its mathematical derivations cannot be accepted for publication, as it would mislead readers and potentially harm the field. The authors should thoroughly revise the paper, correcting all mathematical errors and clarifying the relationship to prior work, before resubmitting.

**Recommendation:** Reject

**Pro Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Novel method for deriving EPPFs for mixed-membership modeling
- Theoretical contribution to understanding BNBP's properties
- Practical topic model with fast convergence

**Con Arguments for Rejection:**
- Critical mathematical errors in key derivations
- Numerous formatting and typographical errors
- Inadequate distinction from prior work on negative binomial processes
- Experimental results contradict some of the paper's claims
- Unclear notation and ambiguous descriptions in key sections
