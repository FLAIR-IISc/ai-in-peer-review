- Derives the EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) in the grouped-data mixed-membership setting, filling a theoretical gap.  
- Provides a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler enabling efficient, scalable inference without truncation.  
- Introduces a BNBP-based topic model to demonstrate practical utility and applies the derived EPPF.  
- Empirical results on standard corpora show competitive performance with HDP-LDA and good convergence/mixing.  
- Positions work within nonparametric Bayesian literature, generalizing prior BNBP and EPPF-related results.  
- Comparison to HDP-LDA offers insights on shrinkage mechanisms and computational complexity advantages.  
- Mathematical derivation is rigorous and well-presented, with clear organization and detailed proofs.  
- The EPPF expression (Eq. 9) is complex and may be hard for readers to intuitively understand.  
- Limited sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters beyond η; parameters like c, a0, b0 need more exploration.  
- Topic modeling application lacks novel algorithmic innovation beyond the BNBP framework.  
- Vocabulary restriction to terms in ≥5 documents is standard but its impact is not discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, practical sampler, strong experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to mathematical complexity, limited novelty in application, incomplete hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper with minor weaknesses that do not undermine its contributions.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
