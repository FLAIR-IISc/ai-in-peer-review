Summary
The paper develops a principled, fully collapsed treatment of the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling. The key methodological contribution is to derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for grouped data by “Poissonizing” group sizes, marginalizing the beta process, and then conditioning back on observed group sizes to obtain a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF). This yields: (i) a prior on a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix with prescribed row sums, (ii) a simple prediction rule for sampling exchangeable random partitions across groups, and (iii) a nonparametric BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that analytically marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process. Empirically, the BNBP topic model mixes faster than an HDP-LDA direct assignment sampler and achieves comparable or better perplexity using fewer topics on three corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12). The work is clearly positioned relative to the CRP/EPPF literature, HDP/Chinese restaurant franchise, and recent count-process constructions (gamma–Poisson, BNBP/GNBP, negative-binomial IBP, Poisson factor analysis).

Quality
- Theory: The construction is technically sound and well-motivated. The augmentation via the gamma–Poisson representation, the derivation of the ECPF (Eq. 6), and the random count matrix prior with i.i.d. columns (Eq. 8) are consistent with the literature on completely random measures and Poissonization. The prediction rule (Eq. 10) is simple and intuitive, emphasizing a multiplicative combination of global and local popularity modulated by dispersion parameters.
- Inference: The fully collapsed sampler that also marginalizes the beta process is a notable algorithmic contribution, reducing variance and avoiding truncation/slice approximations used previously for BNBP.
- Experiments: Perplexity, convergence speed, and mixing are evaluated on three datasets. Results are sensible: BNBP tends to achieve similar/better predictive performance with more compact topic sets and mixes substantially faster than the HDP direct assignment sampler. Code is promised.
- Caveats: (i) The empirical comparison is limited to HDP-LDA with a direct assignment sampler, not the fully collapsed Chinese restaurant franchise sampler; this may bias mixing comparisons. (ii) Only perplexity is reported; qualitative topic coherence or additional baselines (e.g., Poisson factor models, GNBP, Pitman–Yor HDP) would strengthen claims of “state-of-the-art.” (iii) The group-size dependent EPPF is the central conceptual step; while the paper cites sample-size dependent EPPFs (Zhou & Walker, 2014), more discussion of the implications for projectivity and exchangeability would be valuable.

Clarity
- The paper is densely written but largely clear. The decomposition from ECPF to EPPF and the count-matrix perspective are helpful for intuition. The prediction rule is especially clear.
- Notation could be streamlined (e.g., indices and “dot” sums) and the introduction of the “digamma distribution” (Sibuya) could be clarified to avoid confusion with the digamma function. A compact summary of assumptions and the exact exchangeability properties (row vs column) would aid readability.
- Proofs are deferred to the appendix; the main text might benefit from brief proof sketches for Lemmas 2–4. A short discussion of computational complexity per iteration as a function of nonzero counts and KJ would be useful.

Originality
- Novelty is high. To my knowledge, a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership settings, with a usable prediction rule and a fully collapsed sampler that marginalizes the beta process, has not appeared. Prior BNBP works largely used truncation or slice sampling, or addressed infinite random count matrices without conditioning on fixed row sums. This paper fills a gap parallel to the CRF for the HDP but in the count-process/BNBP domain.
- The unification via ECPF→EPPF and the column-exchangeable count-matrix view are conceptually elegant and potentially generalize to other integer-valued CRMs.

Significance
- Theoretically, the work provides tools (EPPF, prediction rule) that can catalyze new mixed-membership models beyond the DP/HDP family, and clarifies the role of dispersion parameters in shaping cross-group partition structure.
- Practically, the sampler is simple to implement, fast, and yields compact models, which matters for real-world topic modeling. The approach may transfer to other grouped count modeling tasks (e.g., multivariate event data).
- Impact would be stronger with broader empirical validation and comparisons to additional modern baselines.

Specific comments and suggestions
- Fairness of comparison: Include results for a collapsed CRF sampler for HDP-LDA (at least on one dataset), or explicitly discuss trade-offs; consider also a Poisson factor analysis baseline or GNBP.
- Hyperparameters: You infer γ0 and rj but appear to fix c. Please clarify whether c is inferred; if fixed, provide sensitivity analysis or motivation. Report posterior behavior of rj to support the claims in Fig. 1 about dispersion control.
- Theory: Elaborate on exchangeability and projectivity: what properties are preserved under the group-size dependent EPPF? Provide the expected number of clusters as a function of m and r in the conditioned model, if available.
- Clarity: Define the Digam distribution clearly, provide intuition for ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c), and summarize the main computational steps of the collapsed sampler. A small worked example would help.
- Metrics: Add topic coherence or heldout likelihood calibration; perplexity alone can be misleading. Report wall-clock to a target perplexity for a fair speed comparison.
- Scalability: Discuss memory/runtime scaling with vocabulary size V and sparsity; demonstrate the suggested sparse-LDA optimizations in practice if possible.

Pros
- Clear, principled derivation of ECPF and group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP
- Simple prediction rule enabling collapsed Gibbs sampling without truncation
- Fully collapsed topic-model sampler that marginalizes the beta process and mixes fast
- Solid theoretical connections; potential to generalize to other integer-valued CRMs
- Empirical evidence of compact models with competitive perplexity; code availability

Cons
- Experimental comparison limited (no CRF-collapsed HDP, few baselines, single metric)
- Some theoretical aspects (exchangeability/projectivity under conditioning) not fully discussed
- Clarity could be improved; heavy notation and reliance on appendix
- Sensitivity to c and other hyperparameters not explored

Recommendation
Leaning accept. The paper makes a substantial theoretical and algorithmic contribution by bringing EPPF-style, fully collapsed inference to BNBP mixed-membership models, with promising empirical behavior. Strengthening the experimental section and clarifying a few theoretical and presentation points would make it even more compelling.
