Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies mixed-membership modeling (grouped exchangeable data) under the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). While EPPFs provide a unifying view and fully collapsed inference for mixture models (e.g., CRP/Dirichlet process), analogous sample-size-dependent EPPFs for mixed-membership settings are lacking. The authors (i) derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) by randomizing group sizes via a beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation; (ii) marginalize the beta process to obtain a prior on a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix with fixed row sums; (iii) use Bayes’ rule to obtain a group-size-dependent EPPF for the BNBP and a simple prediction rule; and (iv) construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that integrates out topics, topic weights, and the beta process. Empirically, on three corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12), the sampler mixes quickly, infers a compact number of topics, and achieves competitive or lower perplexity than HDP-LDA when matched on topic count.

This connects to a substantial literature: CRP/EPPFs (Pitman), HDP and the Chinese restaurant franchise (Teh et al.), NRMIs, and count-based BNP processes (gamma-Poisson, BNBP, GNBP). It relates to recent work on marginalizing the BNBP for infinite random count matrices (Heaukulani & Roy; Zhou et al.), and to σ-stable Poisson–Kingman samplers (Lomelí et al.), while claiming novelty in deriving an EPPF that conditions on fixed group sizes for mixed-membership tasks, a prediction rule, and a fully collapsed sampler.

Quality (soundness and evaluation)
- Theory: The augmentation to beta–gamma–Poisson is standard; the ECPF derivation (Eq. 6) and count-matrix prior (Eq. 7) look consistent with known BNBP marginalizations, including the Poisson number of columns KJ with rate γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)] and column sizes following a digamma distribution. The prediction rule (Eq. 10) has an intuitive multiplicative form combining global popularity n·k and local terms (n−jijk + rj), with the new-cluster weight proportional to γ0, rj, and a function of c and r·. While full proofs are deferred, the steps and references are credible.
- Algorithm: The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model is a clear contribution, integrating out both B and Θ, yielding simple updates (Eq. 12). The comparison to LDA and HDP-LDA samplers is fair in form, and the distinction between multiplicative vs additive shrinkage is insightful.
- Experiments: Held-out perplexity on three corpora, multiple η settings, five train/test splits, and trace plots demonstrate faster convergence and good mixing relative to HDP-LDA (direct assignment). Runtime and topic-count behavior are reported. However, empirical scope is limited to perplexity and a single HDP baseline; no comparisons to alternative nonparametric topic models (e.g., PYP/HDP variants) or to fully collapsed HDP samplers (CRF-based) are included.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written given the technical depth. The organization—ECPF, EPPF, prediction rule, then topic model—is logical and accompanied by intuition. However:
- There are typographical errors and minor inconsistencies (e.g., “perdition rule”, duplicated words, inconsistent dataset naming NIPS12 vs NIPS2012).
- Some normalizing constants in Eq. (10) are glossed over; a brief remark clarifying which constants can be dropped under “∝” and how they depend on j would help.
- The role and inference of c is under-specified relative to γ0 and rj.
- The “digamma distribution” is unfamiliar to many readers; more intuition or a short appendix recap would help.

Originality
The main novelty is a general methodology to derive an EPPF for mixed-membership modeling from an integer-valued process by randomizing group sizes, marginalizing, and conditioning back on observed sizes. For the BNBP, this yields, to my knowledge, the first explicit group-size-dependent EPPF and prediction rule enabling a fully collapsed sampler. Prior works on BNBP focused on infinite random count matrices or truncation/slice-based inference. This is a meaningful step beyond HDP’s CRF construction and complements recent marginal samplers for other random measures.

Significance
The contribution is significant both theoretically and practically:
- Theoretical: An EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership models provide a new tool, with potential to extend to other count processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP).
- Practical: A fully collapsed BNBP topic model that mixes faster and yields compact topic representations is of interest, especially when one wants strong shrinkage on the number of topics.
That said, broader experimental validation would strengthen claims of “state-of-the-art” predictive performance.

Pros
- Clear and principled derivation of ECPF, EPPF, and prediction rule for BNBP in mixed-membership settings.
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that integrates out B, Θ, and Φ with simple, interpretable updates.
- Empirical evidence of faster convergence and competitive perplexity with fewer topics than HDP-LDA.
- Useful intuition contrasting multiplicative vs additive shrinkage mechanisms.

Cons
- Experimental comparisons limited to HDP-LDA (direct assignment). No results versus CRF-based collapsed HDP, Pitman–Yor topic models, or other BNP count models.
- Limited sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (c, γ0 priors) and dispersion parameters rj; unclear how robust performance is across settings.
- Some notation and typographical issues; references are incomplete in places.
- The EPPF is sample-size dependent; more discussion of implications and limits (e.g., coherence across subsampling) would help.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add baselines: include CRF-based collapsed HDP-LDA and a PYP/HDP variant; optionally compare to gamma–Poisson/GNBP topic models under similar collapsed schemes if feasible.
- Provide sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, rj priors and discuss practical defaults; clarify whether c is inferred and how.
- Report ablations showing the impact of the multiplicative term (e.g., fixing rj vs inferring; varying c) on topic count and perplexity.
- Expand runtime and scalability analysis (per-token cost, behavior as K grows, use of sparse LDA tricks).
- Improve clarity: fix typos, unify notation (n·k vs n:k), and give a brief intuitive explanation of the digamma column-size distribution.
- Discuss theoretical properties of the sample-size-dependent EPPF (e.g., projectivity or lack thereof, implications for exchangeability).

Recommendation: Weak accept
This is a solid and timely contribution that advances the theory and practice of BNP mixed-membership modeling beyond the HDP. The derivations are sound and the collapsed sampler is practically valuable. Strengthening the empirical comparisons and clarifying some presentation details would elevate the paper further.
