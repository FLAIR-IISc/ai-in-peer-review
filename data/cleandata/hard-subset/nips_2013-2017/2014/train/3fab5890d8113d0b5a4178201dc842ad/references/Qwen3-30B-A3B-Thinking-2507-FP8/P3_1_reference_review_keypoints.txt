- Paper derives a group-size dependent EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) via randomizing group sizes to obtain an ECPF and applying Bayes’ rule to get the EPPF.

- Presents a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, weights, and the beta process.

- Empirically shows faster convergence (<100 vs >300 iterations), better mixing, and comparable or better predictive performance with fewer topics than HDP-LDA.

- Key technical contribution: unified EPPF framework for mixed-membership modeling, filling a gap in prior work.

- Practical impact: compact topic representations and improved sampler efficiency useful for real applications.

- Methodological novelty: introduces a distinct prediction rule and a multiplicative mechanism for topic popularity differing from HDP-LDA’s additive mechanism.

- Experimental rigor: validated on three standard corpora with careful tuning and multiple η values.

- Related-work clarification needed: paper contradicts citations ([16,17]) that marginalize the beta process and should explain differences with prior fully collapsed approaches.

- EPPF derivation is mathematically sound but extremely complex; paper should better highlight practitioner-relevant aspects.

- Perplexity claim is overstated—BNBP often has higher perplexity than HDP-LDA for the same η; emphasize achieving comparable performance with fewer topics instead.

- Relationship to [17] is unclear; the paper must explain how its group-size dependent approach differs from [17]’s random count matrix process.

- Computational/implementation details lacking: claims that the sampler is "straightforward" need backing and scalability beyond NIPS12 should be discussed.

- Overall assessment: strong, rigorous technical contribution with high originality and substantial significance for Bayesian nonparametrics.

- Recommendation: Accept — the work advances theory and provides a practically superior topic modeling algorithm.

- Pros summarized: first unified EPPF treatment for mixed-membership, novel derivation, fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with faster convergence, practical advantages over HDP-LDA.

- Cons summarized: contradictions in related work, complex EPPF needing better contextualization, and overstated perplexity claims relative to topic-count efficiency.
