

# Review of "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"

This paper introduces a novel approach to deriving an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership modeling. The authors develop a group-size dependent EPPF by first deriving an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) and then using Bayes' rule to obtain the EPPF. They apply this to construct a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out infinite-dimensional beta processes. The paper claims to fill a gap in the literature by providing a unified treatment for mixed-membership modeling, which has previously been limited to methods like the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP).

## Strengths
The paper makes a technically interesting contribution to the literature on nonparametric Bayesian methods for mixed-membership modeling. The derivation of the EPPF via a group-size dependent model is novel, and the BNBP topic model provides an alternative to existing nonparametric topic models like HDP-LDA. The experimental results demonstrate that the BNBP topic model converges faster than HDP-LDA and achieves comparable or better predictive performance with fewer topics. The paper provides clear theoretical derivations for the ECPF and EPPF, which is valuable for understanding the underlying probabilistic structure.

## Weaknesses

1. **Technical Errors**: The paper contains several critical technical errors that undermine the theoretical foundation. In equation (1), the Lévy measure of the beta process is incorrectly defined as ν(dpdω) = p^{-1}(1-p)^{c-1}dpB0(dω). The standard definition should have (1-p)^{-c-1} instead of (1-p)^{c-1} for c > 0, which would make the measure infinite for c > 0. This error propagates through subsequent derivations and makes the entire theoretical framework questionable.

2. **Notational Inconsistencies**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout, particularly with subscripts (e.g., "γ KJ 0" instead of "γ_0^{K_J}", "n−jivji·k" instead of "n_{-ji,v_{ji}\cdot k}"). In Lemma 2, the expression "γ KJ 0" is ambiguous and should be clearly written as γ_0^{K_J}.

3. **Formatting and Missing Elements**: The paper references Figure 1 (a)-(c) but does not include the figure, making it impossible to verify the claims about the random partitions. The abstract contains a strange character "￼" in "Mixed-Membership Modeling" that appears to be a formatting error. The reference list has incomplete entries (e.g., "Ann. Statist., ," with missing page numbers).

4. **Theoretical Justification**: The paper claims to have "a unique method to derive the EPPF" but fails to clearly explain how it differs from the methods in [12] and [17], which also derive EPPFs for mixed-membership models. The justification for why the group-size dependent approach is necessary is not sufficiently developed.

5. **Experimental Claims**: The paper states that "the BNBP topic model's perplexity tends to be lower than that of the HDP-LDA if their inferred number of topics are comparable," but the experimental results in Figure 3 do not clearly support this claim. The middle column shows higher perplexity for BNBP, while the right column claims lower perplexity under comparable topic counts, creating a contradiction in the narrative.

6. **Missing Details**: The paper introduces Q(A) = -∑_{k:ωk∈A} ln(1-pk) as a "log-beta random variable" but provides no justification for why this is necessary or how it aids the analysis. The paper also fails to explain why the BNBP's EPPF is dependent on group sizes while other EPPFs are not.

## Recommendation

While the paper presents an interesting approach to mixed-membership modeling and contains some novel theoretical insights, the technical errors and notational inconsistencies significantly undermine the validity of the theoretical framework. The incorrect definition of the beta process Lévy measure (equation 1) is particularly concerning as it invalidates the entire derivation of the ECPF and EPPF. The paper's claims about convergence speed and predictive performance are also not fully supported by the experimental results presented.

Given the paper's fundamental technical errors, I recommend rejection. These are not minor issues but rather core to the paper's theoretical contribution. A paper that contains such errors in the definition of a fundamental stochastic process cannot be considered technically sound.

The authors should address these issues before considering resubmission. Specifically, they need to correct the Lévy measure definition, clarify the theoretical justification for their approach relative to existing work, and provide complete experimental results that support their claims. The paper has potential but requires significant revision to address these critical technical flaws.
