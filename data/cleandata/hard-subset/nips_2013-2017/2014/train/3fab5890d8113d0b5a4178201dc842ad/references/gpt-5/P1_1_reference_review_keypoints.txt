- Paper derives the BNBP’s ECPF for random group sizes by marginalizing the beta process.  
- From the ECPF the authors obtain a group-size dependent EPPF via Bayes’ rule.  
- They propose a simple prediction rule for partition simulation (global popularity × group-specific popularity).  
- They present a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process with closed-form updates.  
- Theoretical development uses Poissonization and a factorized likelihood to obtain the ECPF and a digamma-distributed column-size view.  
- Measure-theoretic conditions (γ0 finite, c>0, rj>0) guarantee finiteness of the number of clusters.  
- Collapsed sampler is clean, marginalizes the beta process, and has closed-form updates including for rj and γ0.  
- The multiplicative shrinkage mechanism differs from HDP’s additive shrinkage and is well explained.  
- Experiments on three corpora show faster convergence, better mixing, and comparable or lower perplexity with often fewer topics than HDP-LDA.  
- Runtime claims are plausible for unoptimized Matlab, but no large-scale benchmarking is provided.  
- Key proofs (ECPF normalization and some derivations) are deferred to the appendix; a main-text sketch would help.  
- It is unclear whether hyperparameter c is inferred; sensitivity to c and priors on rj is not reported.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited: HDP direct-assignment sampler only, no CRF collapsed sampler or Poisson factorization/NB-process baselines.  
- No strong scalability tests, parallelization, or sparse-sampling evaluations are provided despite brief discussion.  
- The derived EPPF is sample-size dependent, raising questions about projectivity and consistency across different m.  
- Paper is generally well organized and technically careful, though notation is dense and there are minor editorial issues.  
- A high-level algorithm box and a short proof sketch in the main text would improve readability.  
- The ECPF→EPPF derivation and the fully collapsed sampler that marginalizes the beta process are novel and original.  
- The method fills a conceptual gap for EPPF-style treatments of mixed-membership models with integer-valued processes.  
- Practically, the sampler is simple, fast, and often produces compact topic representations, suggesting potential adoption by HDP-LDA users.  
- Suggested author actions: clarify whether c is inferred and provide update/sensitivity; give p(m|r,γ0,c) derivation sketch; compare to CRF collapsed HDP and Poisson factorization baselines; discuss projectivity and provide per-token complexity.  
- Pros: novel ECPF/EPPF and prediction rule, fully collapsed Gibbs sampler, clear count–partition linkage, competitive perplexity, code available.  
- Cons: main proofs in appendix, limited baseline comparisons, unclear hyperparameter handling, modest-scale experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid theoretical contribution with practical sampler but needs stronger empirical comparisons and hyperparameter clarification.
