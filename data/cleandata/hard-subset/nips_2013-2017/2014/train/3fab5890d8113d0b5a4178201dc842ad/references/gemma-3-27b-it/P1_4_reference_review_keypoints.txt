- Paper derives the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) applied to grouped mixed-membership modeling.  
- Authors model group sizes as random, derive the joint distribution, and obtain a prediction rule and EPPF for the BNBP.  
- Presents a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler and provides code.  
- Builds on Dirichlet processes, NRMIs, and prior integer-valued processes (BNBP, GNBP) while filling the gap of missing EPPFs.  
- Contrasts with Chinese Restaurant Franchise, negative binomial IBP, and “ice cream” buffet process to highlight novelty.  
- Theoretical contribution: significant advance by deriving BNBP’s EPPF enabling principled inference and fully collapsed sampling.  
- Extends the EPPF framework from single-dataset mixtures to grouped-data mixed-membership models.  
- Practical implementation: demonstrates a working topic model and sampler, showing real applicability.  
- Empirical results: competitive perplexity on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS12 with compact representations; ablation on η included.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear notation and exposition.  
- Derived EPPF (Eq. 9) is complex and hard to interpret intuitively.  
- Experiments are limited mainly to perplexity; lacks other evaluations like topic coherence or qualitative analysis.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is only partially explored (η); more analysis needed for a, b0, c.  
- Computational cost: 2.5s/iteration on NIPS12 noted as substantial; scalability and runtime comparisons to HDP-LDA are limited.  
- Minor clarifications needed: link between Lemma 1 and Eq. (2) and better integration/definition of `n:k` in Lemma 4.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a theoretical gap, enables collapsed sampling, shows empirical promise, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: EPPF complexity may limit usability, evaluation could be broader, and computational costs raise concerns.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important theoretical and practical contributions, weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add intuitive explanations/visualizations for the EPPF, broaden empirical metrics, perform deeper hyperparameter sensitivity checks, discuss scalability/runtime, and clarify Lemma 1 vs. Eq. (2).
