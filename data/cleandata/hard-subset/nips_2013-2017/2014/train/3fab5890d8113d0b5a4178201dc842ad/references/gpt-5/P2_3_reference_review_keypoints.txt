- Paper derives a group-size–dependent EPPF/ECPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models via beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and Poissonization.
- Authors condition on observed group sizes to obtain an EPPF for column-exchangeable random count matrices and derive a simple prediction rule.
- They construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process.
- Empirically, the sampler mixes faster than HDP-LDA’s direct-assignment sampler and achieves comparable or better perplexity, often using fewer topics.
- The work fills a gap by extending EPPF theory to grouped mixed-membership settings, where prior BNBP work relied on truncation or slice sampling.
- Derivations use standard, appropriate tools and yield coherent, intricate ECPF/EPPF formulas with an intuitive prediction rule.
- The column-wise generative construction (Poisson columns, digamma-distributed sizes, Dirichlet-multinomial allocation) provides a clear check on formulas.
- Theoretical claims are supported by explicit formulas and appendix proofs; algorithmic and empirical claims are consistent with reported results.
- Experiments are limited to three corpora and compare only to HDP-LDA’s direct-assignment sampler, with evaluation focused on perplexity.
- Paper is dense but largely clear for experts; logical organization from ECPF → matrix prior → EPPF → prediction rule aids comprehension.
- Improvements suggested: define the nonstandard “digamma distribution” clearly, add sampler pseudo-code, summarize key appendix steps, and clarify projectivity implications.
- Main contribution is novel: a group-size–dependent EPPF for BNBP and a corresponding collapsed topic model with multiplicative shrinkage contrasted with HDP’s additive shrinkage.
- The framework is significant for Bayesian nonparametrics and practical mixed-membership modeling of count data, likely reusable for related processes.
- Pros: clear theoretical advance, easy-to-implement collapsed sampler, sound derivations, and interpretable global/local popularity terms.
- Cons: limited empirical baselines, heavy reliance on appendix for proofs, unclear notation/distributions, and untested generality to other processes.
- Suggestions for authors: clarify digamma normalization/tails, discuss projectivity across dataset sizes, provide sampler complexity and pseudo-code, add CRF/Poisson-factorization baselines, and analyze prior sensitivity.
- Reproducibility: code is available and, with appendix details, results should be reproducible by experts.
- Overall: strong, novel paper with practical algorithmic benefits; recommend acceptance pending clarity fixes and, if possible, stronger empirical comparisons.
