Summary
The paper studies mixed-membership modeling with the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). The main technical contribution is a derivation of an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for grouped data under the BNBP by randomizing group sizes, then obtaining the group-size–dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) via Bayes’ rule. From this, the authors derive a simple prediction rule for simulating partitions, and construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that analytically marginalizes the random measure B, the group-specific gamma processes, and topic parameters. Empirically, the sampler mixes quickly and achieves competitive or better perplexity than HDP-LDA at comparable topic counts, often using fewer topics.

Relation to prior work
- The work builds on the beta process and negative binomial process literature, including BNBP-based Poisson factor models and feature allocations (e.g., IBP variants, Heaukulani & Roy; Zhou et al.). Unlike prior work that focused on infinite random count matrices or used truncation/slice samplers, this paper targets the marginal distribution on exchangeable partitions of grouped data with fixed group sizes, deriving an explicit EPPF and a collapsed sampler.
- It complements Pitman’s EPPF framework and the sample-size–dependent EPPFs of Zhou & Walker. It is outside the Poisson–Kingman class studied by Lomelí, Favaro, and Teh, and thus provides a novel example of an EPPF for mixed membership driven by an integer-valued process.
- The empirical comparison to HDP-LDA (Teh et al.) highlights a distinct multiplicative shrinkage mechanism (global popularity times local usage + dispersion) vs. the additive HDP mechanism.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A clear and general recipe—introduce random group sizes to obtain an ECPF, marginalize and condition back on observed sizes—to arrive at an EPPF and prediction rule for an integer-valued CRM-based model. This is likely portable to other count-process priors (GNBP, gamma–Poisson).
- Technical depth: Derivations of the joint law after marginalizing B via the Lévy–Khintchine machinery yield interpretable building blocks (Poisson number of columns; digamma distribution for column sizes; Dirichlet–multinomial across groups). The prediction rule is remarkably simple given the complexity of the EPPF.
- Algorithmic contribution: A truly fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes all random measures and topic parameters. Inference equations are closed-form.
- Empirical evidence: On three corpora, the sampler mixes faster than HDP’s direct assignment sampler, converges in fewer iterations, and at comparable topic counts achieves equal or lower perplexity with a more compact representation.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Clarity and correctness of some formulas: Several equations suffer from formatting and normalization ambiguities. In the prediction rule (10), the new-cluster probability appears to miss a factor involving ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c) or c + r·; the text directly below suggests such a factor but is unclear. Similarly, the Lévy measure for the beta process is presented without the usual multiplicative c; if this is a deliberate reparameterization (absorbing c into B0), please make explicit how γ0, c, and B0 interact. The “digamma distribution” PMF and support should be stated cleanly with normalizing constants.
- Exchangeability and size dependence: The resulting EPPF is group-size dependent. This is acceptable for many applications but violates the addition rule for EPPFs. The practical and theoretical implications (projectivity, consistency under subsampling, exchangeability across varying group sizes) deserve a brief discussion beyond a citation to sample-size–dependent EPPFs.
- Scope of empirical comparison: Only HDP-LDA is considered. Since the paper’s framing is mixed membership with count-based CRMs, comparisons to Pitman–Yor LDA, GNBP-based topic models, or σ-stable/PK mixtures (even if via marginal samplers) would strengthen claims. At minimum, a sensitivity study for c, γ0, and rj priors and their effect on the inferred number of topics and perplexity is warranted.
- Hyperparameter treatment: The experiments fix a0 = b0 = e0 = f0 = 0.01 and vary only η. It is unclear whether c is fixed or inferred, and how α, r̃ in HDP-LDA are handled (priors, sampling of α). For fairness, matching topic counts is shown post hoc, but differences in priors can affect mixing and final perplexities.
- Reproducibility: Many proofs and all update equations are deferred to the appendix; please ensure these contain full derivations, including the Laplace functional leading to exp(−γ0[ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c)]) and the construction of the “digamma” distribution.

Quality
- The mathematical program is sound and in line with the theory of CRMs and augmentation via gamma–Poisson. The ECPF expression and factorization steps are plausible and consistent with known results. However, the paper would benefit from fixing typos, clarifying normalizations, and making the prediction rule and constants unambiguous.

Clarity
- Generally well organized, but readability suffers from notational overload and several typographical issues (duplicate words, inconsistent symbols, missing fractions). Provide a concise notation table (r·, n·k, n:k, KJ, m·, γ0, c) and restate all distributions with explicit supports and normalizing constants. Explain the choice of the beta process Lévy measure and parameterization.

Originality
- High. To my knowledge, this is the first explicit EPPF and predictive rule for BNBP mixed membership with a fully collapsed sampler, and the ECPF→EPPF route is a useful pattern.

Significance
- Significant for Bayesian nonparametrics and practical topic modeling. The derivation completes an important gap for count-process mixed-membership models and can influence future work on CRMs beyond the DP/HDP.

Questions for the authors
- Please clarify the exact new-cluster probability in (10), including all constants. Does it include γ0 rj divided by ψ(c + r·) − ψ(c), or by c + r·, or both?
- How is c treated in experiments (fixed vs inferred)? Provide the update equation and posterior.
- For HDP-LDA, do you sample α and the base weights r̃? Which priors?
- Can you report wall-clock time vs effective sample size, and complexity scaling with KJ and V?
- Any results with Pitman–Yor or GNBP baselines?

Pros
- Novel EPPF/ECPF derivation for BNBP mixed membership
- Simple predictive rule enabling a fully collapsed sampler
- Strong mixing and competitive perplexity with fewer topics
- Potentially generalizable methodology

Cons
- Some formula/notation ambiguities and typos
- Limited baseline comparisons and sensitivity analysis
- Group-size dependence and its implications not fully discussed

Recommendation
Accept with minor revisions. The theoretical contribution and practical sampler are impactful; addressing clarity and experimental details will make the paper strong.

Reviewer confidence
High.
