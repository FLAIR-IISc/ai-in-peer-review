Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies mixed-membership modeling via the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). While nonparametric priors like the DP/HDP and NRMIs admit elegant marginal (collapsed) representations via EPPFs (e.g., CRP/CRF), comparable marginal laws for integer-valued processes used in mixed-membership settings (gamma–Poisson, BNBP, GNBP) have been missing. The authors’ key contribution is to derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) and a group-size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, together with a simple prediction rule for simulating exchangeable random partitions of grouped data. The derivation proceeds by “Poissonizing” group sizes (introducing mj as Poisson with mean Θj(Ω)), marginalizing the beta process, and then conditioning back on observed group sizes to obtain an EPPF over a latent column-exchangeable count matrix whose row sums are the fixed group sizes. They show the implied prior over columns is i.i.d. with column size distributed as a “digamma” distribution and row allocations as Dirichlet-multinomial.

Building on this foundation, they propose a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the beta process. The resulting update combines global and local popularity in a multiplicative form, in contrast to the additive form in HDP-LDA’s direct assignment sampler. Experiments on three corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12) compare against HDP-LDA, showing faster convergence/mixing and competitive or better perplexity for a comparable number of topics, typically with a more compact representation.

This work connects to: collapsed representations for DP/HDP (Teh et al.), EPPFs (Pitman), beta processes (Hjort; Thibaux & Jordan), and recent BNBP/negative-binomial-based models (Zhou et al.; Heaukulani & Roy). The paper extends prior BNBP work by providing the missing marginal partition law and a practical collapsed sampler.

Strengths:
- Conceptual advance: A clear path from an integer-valued stochastic process (BNBP) to an ECPF/EPPF for grouped data via Poissonization and Bayes conditioning. This addresses a known gap relative to CRP/CRF for DP/HDP.
- Practical impact: A fully collapsed sampler for BNBP topic modeling with simple predictive updates, good mixing, and competitive perplexity with fewer topics.
- Solid probabilistic development: The augmentation to beta–gamma–Poisson, factorization in Eq. (4), and column-wise i.i.d. characterization (DirMult × Digam) are insightful and reusable.
- Empirical evidence: Convergence diagnostics and perplexity comparisons across multiple corpora, with systematic variation of η.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification:
- Scope of the EPPF: The EPPF is group-size dependent (deviates from Pitman’s addition rule). This is acknowledged and the connection to sample-size dependent EPPFs is cited, but the implications for exchangeability/sufficiency and asymptotic behavior (e.g., growth of the number of clusters with m) deserve more discussion. For instance, expected KJ depends on r· and c via ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c); how does conditioning on m change cluster growth?
- Clarity and notation: The paper is notation-heavy and occasionally terse. The “digamma distribution” is nonstandard; please define it clearly once (support, normalization), and explain its name to avoid confusion with the digamma function. Summation notation like ∑∑K k=1 n:k=m is hard to parse; a brief example would help. Minor typos and layout issues (e.g., dangling text near Eq. 10).
- Comparison set: HDP-LDA is a reasonable baseline, but HPYP/HPDP and Pitman–Yor topic models are common strong baselines with collapsed samplers. Including at least one PY-based baseline (or discussing expected differences) would strengthen claims. Also, prior BNBP topic/factor models with truncation/slice sampling (e.g., Zhou et al. AISTATS; adaptive truncation) could be compared empirically to highlight the benefit of full collapsing.
- Hyperparameter treatment: η is varied extensively, but γ0, c, and the rj prior hyperparameters (a0, b0) are mostly fixed. Since these govern shrinkage and the expected topic count (via ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)), a short sensitivity study or posterior summaries would be valuable. It would also help to state whether c is inferred and how.
- Experimental protocol: Perplexity computation is standard, but using 50% per-document holdout is relatively extreme; including document completion or left-to-right perplexity would connect to broader practice. Reporting the inferred number of topics jointly with perplexity is good; adding wall-clock time per effective sample (ESS) would quantify the claimed mixing advantage more rigorously.
- Generality: The method is presented as a general recipe for inferring EPPFs for other integer-valued processes (e.g., GNBP). A short sketch of how the steps extend (or where they fail) for GNBP or gamma–Poisson would increase impact.

Quality:
Overall technically sound. The augmentation and marginalizations (Eqs. 4–8) appear correct and consistent with related work. The prediction rule (Eq. 10) is simple and intuitive (global popularity term times local reinforcement). The empirical results support the claims on convergence and compactness; predictive comparisons are fair within the chosen baselines. Some proofs are deferred; ensuring key normalizations and conditions on parameters (e.g., c>0, rj>0) are explicit would improve rigor.

Clarity:
Good high-level structure but dense. Suggestions:
- Provide a small, concrete example illustrating the ECPF→EPPF transformation by conditioning on fixed m and the induced random count matrix.
- Include pseudocode for the collapsed sampler and the parameter updates (especially for γ0, c, rj).
- Define the “digamma distribution” once with intuition (e.g., arises from compound Poisson with beta mixing) and give its mean/variance.

Originality:
High. To my knowledge, this is the first derivation of a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership models and the first fully collapsed BNBP topic sampler. Related contemporary work (Heaukulani & Roy; Zhou et al.) does not provide this particular EPPF/prediction-rule for grouped partitions conditioned on m.

Significance:
High. Providing a collapsed representation unlocks efficient inference for BNBP-based mixed-membership models and is likely to influence further work on integer-valued processes (e.g., extending to GNBP). The practical sampler and demonstrated performance on standard corpora increase utility.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and useful theoretical contribution (ECPF/EPPF + prediction rule) for BNBP in grouped settings.
- Fully collapsed sampler that is simple, fast, and effective.
- Clear, intuitive interpretation of multiplicative shrinkage vs HDP’s additive form.
- Empirical evidence on multiple datasets supporting better mixing and compact models.

Cons for acceptance:
- EPPF is sample-size dependent; implications and asymptotics could be elaborated.
- Limited baseline set (no PY-based topic models) and limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
- Dense presentation; clarity can be improved in definitions and notation.
- Experiments focus on perplexity; no qualitative topic inspection or non-text domains.

Questions for authors:
1) Can you provide asymptotic behavior of E[KJ|m] as m increases, and how it depends on r· and c under conditioning on m?
2) Is c inferred? If so, provide the conditional updates; if not, how sensitive are results to c?
3) How would your recipe extend to GNBP or gamma–Poisson; are there obstacles in obtaining a similarly simple prediction rule?
4) Could you comment on the variance-to-mean control of topic usage counts under BNBP vs HDP quantitatively?

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper offers a substantive theoretical advance with practical impact and solid empirical support; addressing clarity and comparative baselines would further strengthen it.
