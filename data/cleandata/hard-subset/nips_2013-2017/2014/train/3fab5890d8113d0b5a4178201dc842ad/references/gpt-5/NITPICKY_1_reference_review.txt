Summary
The paper studies the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data. The main theoretical contribution is a derivation of a group-size-dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, obtained by (i) introducing a group-size-randomized construction to derive an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF), (ii) marginalizing out group sizes to characterize the joint law of counts and partitions, and (iii) using Bayes’ rule to obtain the EPPF. The authors also derive a simple prediction rule for simulation and inference, show that the induced column-exchangeable random count matrix has a Poisson number of columns with digamma-distributed column sizes and Dirichlet-multinomial splitting across groups, and develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model. Empirically, they compare against HDP-LDA on three corpora, claiming faster mixing and competitive or better perplexity at comparable topic counts with more compact representations.

Quality
Strengths:
- The augmentation (BNBP as beta–gamma–Poisson; equation (3)) is clear and useful for connecting count and partition views.
- The ECPF (Lemma 2) and its implications for a column-exchangeable random count matrix (equation (7), (8)) are interesting and potentially broadly useful.
- The prediction rule (Lemma 4) is simple and could enable efficient, truncation-free inference.
- The collapsed BNBP topic sampler and the discussion of multiplicative vs additive shrinkage relative to HDP-LDA are insightful.

Weaknesses / technical concerns:
- Important derivations are deferred to the Appendix (not provided here). The EPPF (equation (9)) contains a complicated normalizing denominator (sum over all partitions of m), and the paper relies on the prediction rule; without the appendix, it is hard to verify correctness and normalization. Please provide full proofs and a sanity check for limiting cases (e.g., J=1).
- In Section 3, there is a likely error: the sampling step “draw the topics (φk|−) ∼ Dir(η + n1·k, …, η + nJ·k)” uses document-indexed counts (J-dimensional); φk is V-dimensional and should be Dir(η + n1k·, …, η + nVk·). This is a serious correctness/replicability issue.
- The new-topic probability in the collapsed assignments appears to have an extra factor V (equation (12) for BNBP; equation (14) for HDP-LDA). In standard collapsed LDA/HDP, the integrated likelihood for a new topic is η/(Vη)=1/V; your formulas seem to multiply by V rather than include 1/V. If you are scaling all terms by a constant, clarify that the factor is common to both existing and new-topic cases; otherwise this is incorrect and materially affects results.
- Parameterization ambiguity: Gamma[r, β] vs shape/scale vs shape/rate is inconsistent (e.g., θjk ∼ Gamma[rj, pk/(1 − pk)]; later θjk|− ∼ Gamma(njk + rj, pk)). Please fix parameterization and units. Also, pk is not available after marginalizing B; how are θjk sampled for perplexity? The held-out evaluation section does not specify how pk or B are handled post-collapse.
- The “digamma distribution” is used (equation (8))—please include a checkable derivation and better intuition; verify support starting at n=1 and justify its appearance from the Lévy measure.
- The prediction rule (equation (10)) contains a garbled line with ψ(·) likely from a figure caption. The new-cluster mass should be clearly stated (and match the Poisson mean γ0[ψ(c+r·)−ψ(c)]).
- Fairness of comparison: the baseline HDP-LDA uses a direct-assignment sampler (only partially collapsed) rather than the fully collapsed CRF sampler; this likely disadvantages HDP in mixing. Please add CRF-based HDP or a stronger baseline, and clarify priors/updates for α and the base DP. Also, equating topic counts by tuning η undermines the nonparametric setting; consider plotting perplexity against inferred K across samplers with matched priors.
- The experimental protocol for computing perplexity uses posterior draws of θjk and φk; details are insufficient to reproduce (e.g., priors for α, c; updates for rj, γ0, c; initialization; handling of hyperparameters; convergence diagnostics beyond traces).

Clarity
- Many typos/formatting inconsistencies hinder readability:
  - “perdition rule” → prediction rule (multiple occurrences).
  - “disjoint disjoint” (Section 2).
  - “Poisson-Kigman” → Kingman; “Lévy” accent inconsistent.
  - Equation (10) line-break corruptions around ψ(·).
  - Inconsistent notation for sums (n·k, n:k). Define all clearly and stick to a convention.
  - Footnotes/URLs intrude in the text (e.g., dangling URLs in Section 4).
  - References are missing years, venues, and are sometimes incomplete.
- The paper would benefit from a small running example to illustrate the ECPF→EPPF derivation.

Originality
- Deriving a group-size-dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership modeling and the associated prediction rule appears novel and fills a gap relative to prior BNBP/IBP work that focused on feature allocations or random count matrices (e.g., Heaukulani & Roy 2014; Zhou et al. 2014).
- The collapsed BNBP topic sampler is a nontrivial algorithmic contribution distinct from HDP’s CRF.

Significance
- If correct and clearly presented, the EPPF/ECPF framework is of interest beyond topic models and could impact nonparametric mixed-membership modeling with other count processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP).
- The empirical advantage (faster mixing, compact representation) is promising but needs stronger baselines and clearer methodology to be convincing.

Pros
- Conceptually clean route from BNBP to EPPF via ECPF and Bayes’ rule.
- Practical prediction rule enabling truncation-free collapsed inference.
- Interesting multiplicative shrinkage mechanism vs HDP’s additive.
- Empirical signs of good mixing and compact topic usage.

Cons
- Multiple technical ambiguities and likely errors (Dirichlet dimension mismatch; V factor in new-topic term; parameterization; missing details for post-collapse sampling).
- Proofs relegated to appendix; core normalization/completeness not verifiable.
- Comparisons may be unfair; baselines insufficient.
- Numerous typos, formatting problems, incomplete references.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The paper contains interesting and potentially significant contributions, but the accumulation of technical ambiguities, likely errors, missing methodological details, and presentation/formatting issues undermines confidence and reproducibility. I encourage the authors to:
- Fix the φk Dirichlet update (J vs V indices) and the new-topic probability scaling.
- Provide full, checkable derivations and consistent parameterizations.
- Clarify held-out evaluation sampling under full collapse.
- Strengthen and fairly configure HDP baselines.
- Thoroughly edit for clarity and complete the references.
