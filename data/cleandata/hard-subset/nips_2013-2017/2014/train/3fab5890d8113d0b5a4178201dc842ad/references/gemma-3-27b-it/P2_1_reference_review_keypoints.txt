- Paper derives the missing EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) and applies it to mixed-membership modeling.  
- Develops a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model and compares it to HDP-LDA.  
- Builds on Dirichlet Processes and NRMIs, addressing the lack of EPPF for grouped-data mixed-membership models.  
- Distinguishes from related work (negative binomial IBP, “ice cream” buffet) that focuses on random count matrices rather than group-size dependent EPPFs.  
- Theoretical contribution: deriving the BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership is significant and novel.  
- Practical algorithm: fully collapsed sampler simplifies inference and removes need for certain parameter tuning.  
- Empirical validation: competitive perplexity and more compact topic representations; faster convergence shown in experiments.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Novelty: combination of new EPPF derivation and practical topic modeling application is original.  
- Weakness: the derived EPPF (Equation 9) is complex and hard to interpret intuitively.  
- Weakness: experiments focus mainly on perplexity; other metrics like topic coherence or downstream tasks are missing.  
- Weakness: model performance is sensitive to hyperparameter η and its impact is not thoroughly explored.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational cost and runtime comparisons to HDP-LDA.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, practical competitive model, clear presentation, and useful collapsed sampler.  
- Arguments against acceptance: EPPF complexity may hinder usability, empirical evaluation is incomplete, and computational costs are under-discussed.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important contributions; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested minor revisions: provide intuitive explanation of Equation 9, add evaluation metrics or downstream tasks, and expand discussion of computational cost.  
- Meta ratings: Quality — Excellent; Clarity — Very Good; Originality — Excellent; Significance — Very Good.
