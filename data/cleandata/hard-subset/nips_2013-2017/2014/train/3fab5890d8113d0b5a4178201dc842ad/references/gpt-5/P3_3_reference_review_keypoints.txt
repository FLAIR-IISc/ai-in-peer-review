- Derives an ECPF and group-size–dependent EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) by randomizing group sizes, then obtains a simple predictive rule and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.  
- Constructs a collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the random measure B, group-specific gamma processes, and topic parameters with closed-form updates.  
- Empirically the sampler mixes faster than HDP’s direct assignment sampler, converges in fewer iterations, and often achieves equal or lower perplexity with fewer topics on three corpora.  
- Builds on beta/negative-binomial process literature, differing from prior work that focused on infinite count matrices or used truncation/slice samplers by targeting marginal partitions with fixed group sizes.  
- Complements Pitman’s EPPF framework and sample-size–dependent EPPFs; provides a novel integer-valued CRM example outside the Poisson–Kingman class.  
- Highlights a distinct multiplicative shrinkage mechanism (global popularity × local usage + dispersion) versus the additive HDP mechanism.  
- Conceptual novelty: introduces a general recipe (randomize group sizes → marginalize → condition back) likely portable to other count-process priors.  
- Technical depth: uses Lévy–Khintchine marginalization to yield interpretable components (Poisson number of columns, digamma distribution for column sizes, Dirichlet–multinomial across groups).  
- Algorithmic contribution: first truly fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic models with analytic marginalization and simple prediction rule.  
- Empirical evidence: faster mixing and competitive or better perplexity with more compact topic representations.  
- Formula and presentation issues: several equations have formatting/normalization ambiguities, and the prediction-rule constant appears unclear or possibly missing factors.  
- Lévy measure and parameterization concerns: the beta process Lévy measure is presented without the usual multiplicative c, requiring explicit reparameterization clarification.  
- Digamma distribution: PMF, support, and normalizing constants should be clearly stated.  
- Exchangeability/size dependence: the EPPF depends on group sizes, violating addition rule/projectivity; practical and theoretical implications need discussion.  
- Empirical scope limited: comparisons only to HDP-LDA; missing baselines like Pitman–Yor LDA, GNBP models, or σ-stable/PK mixtures and sensitivity analyses for c, γ0, rj.  
- Hyperparameter treatment unclear: fixed priors a0=b0=e0=f0=0.01, unclear whether c is fixed or inferred and how HDP hyperparameters are handled—this can affect fairness and results.  
- Reproducibility concerns: many derivations and update equations are deferred to the appendix; request full derivations including Laplace functional steps.  
- Presentation issues: notational overload, typographical errors, inconsistent symbols; request a concise notation table and explicit distribution statements.  
- Originality: high—likely the first explicit EPPF and predictive rule for BNBP mixed membership with a fully collapsed sampler.  
- Significance: important for Bayesian nonparametrics and practical topic modeling; fills a gap for count-process mixed-membership models.  
- Questions for authors: clarify the exact new-cluster probability constant in (10); how c is treated and updated; HDP-LDA hyperprior/sampling details; report wall-clock time vs effective sample size and scaling; any results with Pitman–Yor or GNBP baselines?  
- Pros summary: novel EPPF/ECPF derivation, simple predictive rule enabling a fully collapsed sampler, strong mixing and competitive perplexity, generalizable methodology.  
- Cons summary: formula/notation ambiguities and typos, limited baseline comparisons and sensitivity analysis, and insufficient discussion of group-size dependence implications.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
