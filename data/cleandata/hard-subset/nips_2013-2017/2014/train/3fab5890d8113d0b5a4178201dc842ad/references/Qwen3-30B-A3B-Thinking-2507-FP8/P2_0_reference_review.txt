

# Review: "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"

This paper addresses a significant gap in nonparametric Bayesian modeling: the lack of a unified treatment for mixed-membership modeling using exchangeable partition probability functions (EPPFs). While the Dirichlet process and hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) have been extensively used for mixture modeling, their extension to mixed-membership problems (where multiple groups share a common set of topics/clusters) has been limited. The authors introduce a novel EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), which governs exchangeable random partitions of grouped data, and demonstrate its application to topic modeling.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical derivations. The authors successfully derive the exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) and then the EPPF using Bayes' rule, which is a significant contribution to the field. The proofs for Lemmas 1-4 (provided in the appendix) are thorough and well-justified. The experimental results on topic modeling are compelling: the BNBP topic model converges faster (in less than 100 iterations versus 300+ for HDP-LDA), mixes better, and achieves comparable or better predictive performance with a more compact representation of the corpus. The paper acknowledges limitations (e.g., the BNBP's multiplicative shrinkage mechanism versus HDP's additive mechanism) and provides a balanced comparison with relevant baselines.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with logical progression from problem statement to theoretical contributions to experimental validation. The mathematical derivations are presented carefully, though the EPPF (equation 9) is complex. Figure 1 effectively illustrates how dispersion parameters affect random partitions, and the experimental setup is well-described with clear metrics (perplexity). The appendix provides sufficient detail for reproducibility. The only clarity issue is that the EPPF formula is quite dense, which might make it challenging for some readers to grasp the full implications immediately.

## Originality
This work makes a substantial original contribution by addressing a critical gap in the literature. The method to derive the EPPF via a group-size dependent model is novel and could be generalized to other integer-valued stochastic processes. The BNBP topic model is distinct from existing approaches with its multiplicative shrinkage mechanism, which differs from the additive mechanism in HDP. The paper properly cites and differentiates from related work, including Zhou et al.'s recent papers on BNBP [10, 11, 16, 17] and the HDP [6]. The authors also clearly articulate how their work extends beyond the "Chinese restaurant franchise" approach used for HDP.

## Significance
This work advances the state of the art in nonparametric Bayesian modeling for mixed-membership problems. The BNBP topic model demonstrates practical advantages over HDP-LDA in terms of convergence speed, mixing properties, and compact representation of the corpus. The theoretical contribution provides a foundation for future work on mixed-membership modeling. While the paper focuses on topic modeling, the methodology could be applied to other domains where mixed-membership modeling is relevant, such as collaborative filtering or multi-view learning.

## Weaknesses
The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other nonparametric topic models beyond HDP-LDA. While the BNBP topic model outperforms HDP-LDA in convergence and compactness, it would be valuable to see how it compares to more recent nonparametric topic models like the discrete infinite logistic normal distribution [26] or other feature models. The computational complexity analysis of the BNBP sampler is somewhat limited, though the paper notes it runs in about 2.5 seconds per iteration on the NIPS12 corpus.

## Conclusion
This is a strong, high-quality paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The theoretical work is rigorous, the experimental results are compelling, and the significance of the contribution is clear. The paper addresses an important gap in the literature and provides a novel method for deriving EPPFs that could be generalized to other processes.

**Pro Acceptance Arguments:**
- Fills a critical gap in mixed-membership modeling with a unified EPPF framework
- Novel method for deriving EPPFs via group-size dependent models
- BNBP topic model demonstrates clear practical advantages over HDP-LDA
- Rigorous theoretical derivations with careful experimental validation
- Provides a foundation for future work on mixed-membership modeling

**Con Acceptance Arguments:**
- EPPF formula is complex, which might limit immediate adoption
- Could benefit from more comprehensive comparison with other nonparametric topic models
- Computational complexity analysis of BNBP sampler is somewhat limited

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field and meets all the criteria for high-quality NIPS papers. The theoretical work is sound, the experimental results are compelling, and the significance of the contribution is clear. I recommend acceptance.
