- Paper derives group-size dependent ECPF and EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and provides a prediction rule and fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP topic models.  
- Key contributions: (i) ECPF via beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, (ii) EPPF from ECPF by Bayes’ rule, (iii) simple prediction rule for simulation/collapsed inference, (iv) fully collapsed Gibbs sampler marginalizing topics, weights, and the beta process.  
- Positions work between nonparametric partition models (CRP/Pitman–Yor) and count processes (gamma–Poisson/BNBP), filling a gap in marginal laws for BNBP-based mixed-membership models.  
- Technical derivations are sound: standard augmentation, Lemma 1 collapsing to independent NB counts, and factorization (Eqs. 4–5) are correct.  
- The ECPF integrating out B is nontrivial and carefully derived, yielding a Poisson-distributed number of occupied clusters and an intuitive i.i.d. column construction.  
- The group-size dependent EPPF (Eq. 9) supports an interpretable prediction rule where cluster assignment combines global popularity and local popularity with a specific new-cluster term.  
- Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler (Eq. 12) is derived; comparison shows BNBP’s multiplicative shrinkage mechanism contrasts with HDP’s additive mechanism.  
- Empirically, the BNBP sampler mixes quickly, reaches stationarity fast, and tends to use fewer topics for comparable perplexity, with runtime per iteration similar to HDP direct assignment.  
- Evaluation is reasonable (word holdout, multiple η) but omits the collapsed CRF HDP sampler and recent scalable/collapsed HDP variants, weakening comparisons on mixing and predictive performance.  
- Paper is well organized and motivated, following a clear logical progression from augmentation to sampler.  
- Some typesetting/notation glitches and unusual terminology (“digamma distribution”) impede readability; definitions are provided but standardization is needed.  
- Unclear treatment of hyperparameter c (fixed or inferred) and lack of sensitivity analysis for c and γ0.  
- Originality: novel derivation of group-size dependent EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP, with an elegant ECPF→EPPF route that may generalize.  
- Significance: fills a conceptual gap analogous to CRF for HDP, enabling collapsed inference for BNBP and likely broader adoption for grouped count data.  
- Main strengths: novel theoretical derivation, fully collapsed conjugate Gibbs sampler, clear interpretability of multiplicative popularity and dispersion, and solid empirical evidence for faster mixing/compactness.  
- Main weaknesses/concerns: missing collapsed HDP baseline, limited discussion/experiments on c and γ0 sensitivity, notation/typo issues, and a double-blind violation via code link.  
- Questions for authors: clarify if c is fixed or inferred (and provide prior/update if inferred); add collapsed HDP (CRF) comparison; provide diagnostics on posterior KJ vs γ0 and c; comment on extending ECPF→EPPF to GNBP/gamma–Poisson.  
- Minor suggestions: fix typesetting/typos (after Eq. 10), standardize “Digam” notation, add topic coherence/sparsity metrics, and discuss per-token computational complexity and memory vs K.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — substantive theoretical and algorithmic contribution, promising empirical behavior; address anonymity and strengthen baselines to make a clear accept.
