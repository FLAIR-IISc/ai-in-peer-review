

**Review of "Beta-Negative Binomial Process and Exchangeable Random Partitions for Mixed-Membership Modeling"**

This paper develops an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) to address mixed-membership modeling, a significant gap in the literature as previous work (e.g., HDP) lacked a unified treatment. The authors derive a prediction rule for simulating exchangeable random partitions, construct a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler, and demonstrate competitive performance against HDP-LDA.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper identifies and addresses a meaningful gap in mixed-membership modeling literature by providing the first unified treatment via BNBP's EPPF.
2. The derivation of the BNBP's EPPF generalizes techniques from [12, 17] and offers a novel method to derive EPPFs for mixed-membership modeling.
3. The BNBP topic model's implementation is straightforward, and the experimental results suggest promising performance with compact topic representations.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Technical Inconsistencies:** Equation (1) contains a typo: "ν(dpdω) = p−1(1− p)c−1dpB0(dω)" should be "ν(dp dω) = p^{-1}(1-p)^{c-1} dp B_0(dω)". Equation (12) has unclear notation: "V · γ0 c+r· · rj" should be "V · γ0/(c+r·) · rj" to indicate proper division.
   
2. **Incomplete Derivations:** Lemma 1 claims equivalence between BNBP (2) and the group size dependent model (3), but the transition from (4) to (5) skips critical steps. The marginalization of θjk is not fully justified, as the derivation assumes the sum of Poisson terms equals the marginalization without explicit demonstration.

3. **Ambiguous Notation:** The notation NJ is introduced in equation (7) without prior definition. The paper should explicitly define NJ when first mentioning the random count matrix to avoid confusion.

4. **Lack of Quantitative Support:** The paper claims "BNBP topic model's perplexity tends to be lower than HDP-LDA" but provides no concrete numerical evidence. Figure 3's middle column shows trends but lacks specific perplexity values for different η, making the claim unsubstantiated.

5. **Overstated Claims:** The paper claims "state-of-the-art predictive performance" without comparing to recent nonparametric topic models beyond HDP-LDA (e.g., [26, 27]). A more comprehensive comparison would strengthen the contribution.

6. **Formatting Issues:** The title contains an invisible character "￼" in "Exchangeable ￼Random Partitions" that should be removed. The paper inconsistently uses "EPPF" and "EPPF" in sections 1.1 and 1.2, requiring standardization.

7. **Lack of Intuition for Key Formulas:** Lemma 3 presents a complex EPPF formula without an intuitive explanation or simplified example. A basic case (e.g., J=2 groups, rj=1) would help readers grasp the structure.

**Overall Assessment:**
While the paper addresses a significant gap in mixed-membership modeling, the technical inconsistencies, incomplete derivations, and lack of quantitative support for key claims undermine its scientific rigor. The paper's novelty is overshadowed by these flaws, which are particularly concerning given NIPS' emphasis on technical precision. The authors' methodological approach is promising, but the execution contains avoidable errors that prevent the paper from meeting NIPS' standards.

**Pro and Con Acceptance:**
- **Pro:** Novel EPPF derivation for mixed-membership modeling, well-motivated topic model, clear connection to existing literature.
- **Con:** Technical errors, incomplete derivations, unsubstantiated claims about performance, ambiguous notation, formatting inconsistencies.

**Recommendation: Reject**  
The paper's technical flaws and lack of rigorous support for its claims prevent it from being considered for publication at NIPS. While the core idea is valuable, the current presentation requires substantial revision to address the identified issues.
