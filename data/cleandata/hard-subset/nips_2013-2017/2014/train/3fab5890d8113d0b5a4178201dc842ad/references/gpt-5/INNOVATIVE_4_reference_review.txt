Summary
This paper develops a unified, collapsed, partition-based view of mixed-membership modeling under the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP). The core contribution is a general recipe to obtain an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for grouped data from an integer-valued stochastic process by (i) randomizing group sizes, (ii) deriving an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF) for the joint law of group sizes and partitions, and (iii) applying Bayes’ rule to obtain a group-size–dependent EPPF. Specializing to the BNBP, the authors derive closed-form expressions for the ECPF and EPPF, a simple prediction rule enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling, and a BNBP topic model that analytically marginalizes the beta process, topics, and topic weights. They show that the resulting sampler mixes quickly and yields competitive or improved perplexity with fewer topics compared to HDP-LDA, highlighting a multiplicative global-local shrinkage mechanism distinct from the HDP’s additive one. The paper situates the results with respect to the Chinese restaurant franchise, IBP variants (negative-binomial/“ice cream” buffet), Poisson–Kingman mixtures, and sample-size–dependent EPPFs.

Relation to prior work
Prior work has strong collapsed semantics for mixture models (CRP/EPPF) but not for mixed-membership models, where HDP often relies on the Chinese restaurant franchise. For BNBP/GNBP, existing works used truncations or slice sampling and provided predictive structures for infinite random count matrices, but not an EPPF for grouped data with fixed group sizes. This paper fills that gap by deriving an EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership modeling and by delivering a fully collapsed sampler. The connection to Dirichlet–multinomial and the “digamma distribution” for column sizes is well drawn, and the paper clarifies why BNBP lies outside σ-stable Poisson–Kingman.

Strengths
- Novel theory: A clear, reusable recipe to obtain EPPFs for mixed-membership from integer-valued processes; concrete ECPF/EPPF for BNBP and a neat prediction rule. This is a genuine conceptual advance beyond HDP-style franchise constructions.
- Fully collapsed inference: The sampler marginalizes the beta process and all local/global weights, leading to fast mixing and simple updates.
- Insightful comparison: The multiplicative combination of global and local popularity in BNBP vs. additive in HDP provides intuition for topic shrinkage and compactness.
- Empirical evidence: On three corpora, the BNBP sampler mixes faster and, for a comparable number of topics, often achieves lower perplexity with fewer topics.
- Practicality: Implementation appears straightforward; the method likely generalizes to other integer-valued processes.

Weaknesses
- Scope of experiments: Baselines are limited to HDP-LDA. Given the framing, comparisons to other BNP count/factor models (e.g., Poisson factor analysis, gamma–Poisson/GNBP, or NBP-based models with truncation/slice) would strengthen claims.
- Sensitivity and ablations: Limited exploration of the role of c, γ0, and rj inference; no ablations showing the benefit of learning rj versus fixing them.
- Sample-size dependent EPPF: While discussed and justified, some readers may worry about coherence under changes in group sizes; more guidance on when such dependence is desirable and its implications for posterior consistency would help.
- Clarity/notation: The paper is densely notated; the definition and usage of the “digamma distribution” could be made more self-contained; a few typos and minor formatting glitches are distracting.

Quality
The derivations are plausible and grounded in known constructions (beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, Dirichlet–multinomial compound, Lévy calculus). The prediction rule and collapsed updates are consistent with the presented EPPF/ECPF. Proofs are deferred to the appendix, which is acceptable, but the main text could more explicitly state key assumptions ensuring exchangeability and validity of the group-size dependent EPPF. Empirical methodology is standard and fair, though adding more baselines and sensitivity analyses would bolster the empirical case.

Clarity
Overall well-organized, but heavy notation makes it challenging on first read. Suggestions:
- Provide an intuitive summary box for the ECPF/EPPF and the prediction rule, with brief interpretation of each term.
- Define the “digamma distribution” in-line with its support and normalizer.
- Clean minor typos (e.g., “perdition” → “prediction”, duplicate words, NIPS12 vs NIPS2012) and clarify the brief extraneous ψ line near Eq. (10).
- Briefly recap exchangeability and the implications of sample-size dependent EPPFs up front.

Originality
High. The mixed-membership EPPF for BNBP and the general recipe to go from integer-valued processes to group-size dependent EPPFs/prediction rules are new and important. The fully collapsed BNBP topic model, marginalizing the beta process, is also a notable advance.

Significance
High potential. The methodology should impact BNP mixed-membership modeling beyond topics, offering collapsed samplers where only truncation/slice approaches existed. The multiplicative shrinkage mechanism is practically valuable for compact models. With more empirical breadth, the work could become a reference point.

Questions for authors
- Can your recipe be applied verbatim to gamma–Poisson and GNBP to yield analogous EPPFs and prediction rules? Any obstacles?
- Practical guidance on setting/inferring c and γ0? How sensitive is performance to these hyperparameters?
- Does the group-size dependence create any issues for posterior consistency or for adding/removing groups at test time?
- Could you report wall-clock comparisons and memory profiles vs HDP-LDA on larger corpora, and results with sparse LDA-style speedups?
- Ablation on fixing vs learning rj to quantify its effect on partitions and perplexity.

Minor comments
- Standardize notation for counts and indices; fix small typos and formatting glitches.
- Add a small synthetic study where the true partition structure is known to validate recovery.

Recommendation
Accept. This paper introduces a significant new conceptual and algorithmic direction—an EPPF-based collapsed framework for mixed-membership modeling under BNBP—with clear theoretical contributions and promising practical benefits. The novelty and potential impact outweigh the relatively modest experimental breadth.

Confidence
Medium. I am familiar with BNP mixture/membership models and exchangeable partitions, though I did not verify all appendix proofs.
