

This paper develops a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership modeling, addressing a significant gap in the literature. Previous work on EPPFs has primarily focused on single-set data, while mixed-membership modeling (e.g., HDP) has lacked a unified theoretical framework for deriving marginal distributions. The authors derive the BNBP's EPPF by first randomizing group sizes to obtain an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF), then applying Bayes' rule to arrive at the EPPF governing the prior distribution of latent column-exchangeable random count matrices. This approach generalizes the techniques of Zhou et al. (2014) and [12] to mixed-membership settings. The authors then construct a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that analytically marginalizes out both topic weights and the infinite-dimensional beta process, providing closed-form update equations.

The paper makes several significant contributions: (1) a general method to derive EPPFs for mixed-membership modeling using integer-valued stochastic processes; (2) a prediction rule for simulating exchangeable random partitions governed by the BNBP's EPPF; and (3) a BNBP topic model that converges faster, mixes better, and achieves state-of-the-art predictive performance with a more compact representation compared to HDP-LDA.

Strengths:
- The theoretical contribution is substantial and addresses a clear gap in the literature. The derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling is novel and well-supported by rigorous mathematical proofs provided in the appendix.
- The BNBP topic model is well-motivated and demonstrates clear practical advantages over HDP-LDA, including faster convergence (reaching target distributions in <100 iterations vs. >300 for HDP-LDA), better mixing properties, and competitive predictive performance with fewer topics.
- The paper provides a thorough comparison of the BNBP's multiplicative topic control mechanism with HDP-LDA's additive mechanism, highlighting key theoretical differences.
- The experimental evaluation on three document corpora (JACM, PsyReview, NIPS12) is comprehensive and well-designed, using standard heldout perplexity metrics and comparing against a relevant baseline (HDP-LDA).

Weaknesses:
- The derived EPPF (equation 9) is quite complex, which may limit its immediate practical adoption without further simplification or approximation techniques.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to topic modeling; the paper doesn't demonstrate the BNBP framework on other mixed-membership problems (e.g., image clustering, recommendation systems) to show broader applicability.
- While the paper compares with HDP-LDA, it doesn't directly compare with more recent nonparametric topic models like the discrete infinite logistic normal distribution (Paisley et al., 2012) or other BNBP variants.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical development and well-executed experiments. The claims are well-supported by both theoretical derivations and empirical results. The authors carefully evaluate both strengths (faster convergence, compact representation) and weaknesses (higher perplexity for small η values) of their approach.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and logically organized, with clear mathematical notation and sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results. The connection between the theoretical development and practical application (topic modeling) is effectively established.

Originality: This work makes a clear contribution to the field by deriving the first EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership modeling. It extends the EPPF framework beyond single-set data to grouped data, which is a significant advance. The paper properly situates itself within the literature, citing relevant prior work on EPPFs, BNBP, and topic modeling.

Significance: The results are important for advancing mixed-membership modeling, as they provide a unified theoretical framework and a practical algorithm with demonstrable advantages. The BNBP topic model's ability to achieve comparable predictive performance with fewer topics represents a meaningful advance in compact representation of document corpora. The method to derive EPPFs for mixed-membership modeling could be generalized to other integer-valued stochastic processes, suggesting broader applicability.

Pro Acceptance:
- Fills a significant gap in the literature by deriving the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling
- Provides a theoretically sound and practically useful topic model with clear advantages over HDP-LDA
- Well-supported by rigorous mathematical derivations and thorough experimental evaluation
- Offers a general method applicable to other integer-valued stochastic processes

Con Acceptance:
- The complexity of the EPPF may limit immediate practical adoption
- Experimental evaluation limited to topic modeling without broader demonstration of applicability
- Doesn't compare with several recent nonparametric topic models beyond HDP-LDA

The paper makes a substantial theoretical contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling, providing a novel EPPF for mixed-membership modeling that enables fully collapsed inference. The BNBP topic model demonstrates clear practical advantages in terms of convergence speed, mixing properties, and compact representation. While the complexity of the EPPF is a limitation, the theoretical framework and practical benefits of the BNBP topic model represent a significant advance in the field. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a valuable contribution to NIPS.
