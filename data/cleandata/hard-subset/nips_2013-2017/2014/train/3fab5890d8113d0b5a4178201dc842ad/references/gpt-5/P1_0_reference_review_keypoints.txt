- Summary: Develops a group-size dependent EPPF for the BNBP via beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation, deriving an ECPF, marginal count-matrix prior, EPPF, and a prediction rule; proposes a collapsed BNBP topic model with Gibbs sampler and compares to HDP-LDA.

- Theory: Augmentation and marginalization steps are sound; ECPF, Poisson-number-of-columns prior, and group-size dependent EPPF are plausible and well-motivated.

- Prediction rule: The derived rule has an intuitive multiplicative structure combining global popularity and group-specific usage (rj), distinct from HDP’s additive form.

- Inference: Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the beta process, topic weights, and topics is nontrivial and yields simple closed-form updates.

- Experiments: Evaluated on three corpora showing fast convergence and competitive or better perplexity than HDP-LDA when matched for effective topic count; often uses fewer topics.

- Experimental limitations: Comparisons to stronger HDP baselines (CRF collapsed sampler, sparse implementations), hyperparameter learning details, variance/error bars, and wall-clock timings are lacking.

- Clarity: Paper is dense but generally well-organized; the augmentation→ECPF→EPPF pipeline is clear.

- Notation/readability: Notation is heavy—recommend a compact notation table; digamma distribution needs more intuition and support clarification.

- Minor clarity issues: Typos and wording mistakes (e.g., “perdition rule”) should be fixed.

- Related work: Acknowledges related BNBP combinatorial structures but needs a crisper contrast to prior infinite-count-matrix work.

- Originality: Deriving a group-size dependent EPPF for BNBP in mixed-membership settings is novel and the ECPF→EPPF recipe could generalize to other integer-valued processes.

- Sampler novelty: The fully collapsed BNBP topic sampler that marginalizes beta process, topics, and weights is new and practically useful.

- Insight: The multiplicative vs additive shrinkage observation (BNBP vs HDP) provides a fresh algorithmic perspective.

- Theoretical significance: Fills a gap by providing a marginal EPPF description for BNBP and introduces a useful Poisson–digamma–DirMult column model.

- Practical significance: Collapsed sampler is simple to implement, converges quickly, and yields compact topic representations for comparable perplexity.

- Broader impact: Method suggests a pathway to derive EPPFs for other integer-valued processes (gamma–Poisson, GNBP), enabling further BNP work.

- Strength: Clear principled derivation of BNBP EPPF for fixed group sizes.

- Strength: Intuitive prediction rule and role of dispersion parameters rj in overdispersion control.

- Strength: Practical fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with closed-form updates and good empirical mixing.

- Strength: Solid empirical evidence on multiple corpora supporting convergence and compactness.

- Weakness: Baselines—need CRF-collapsed HDP and modern sparse collapsed baselines for fairer comparisons.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter handling—specify HDP α inference, whether c is learned for BNBP, and provide ablations/posterior summaries for γ0, c, and rj.

- Weakness: Evaluation—report standard deviations, wall-clock per iteration and to convergence, and topic counts at evaluation; consider document completion setups.

- Weakness: Clarity additions—add roadmap figure, notation table, and expanded intuition for digamma cluster-size tail behavior.

- Questions for authors: Is c learned or fixed, and how sensitive is performance to c, γ0, and the Gamma prior on rj?

- Questions for authors: Can the ECPF→EPPF recipe be instantiated for gamma–Poisson or GNBP, and what technical obstacles arise?

- Questions for authors: How does the sampler scale with very large KJ, and can sparse-LDA tricks be fully adapted?

- Questions for authors: Could a collapsed split–merge move be provided to improve mixing on topic counts?

- Minor issues: Fix typos (“perdition rule” → “prediction rule”, repeated words), missing years in references, and update reference formatting.

- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on stronger baselines and minor clarifications to strengthen empirical and presentation claims.
