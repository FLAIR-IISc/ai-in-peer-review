The paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model. The manuscript is well written, reasonably clear, and technically sound. Its main contribution—the derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via count-mixture constructions—is interesting in its own right and may provide useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Additional comments:
While the BNBP topic model is a reasonable demonstration of the utility of the developed EPPF and its corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal, and the most defensible conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics. A more informative experiment would contrast the collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations have natural advantages for parallelization, and it would be useful to examine mixing trade-offs relative to the collapsed sampler developed here. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. I have only a few minor comments on the experimental section (see above).
