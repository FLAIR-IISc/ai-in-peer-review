This paper presents a new Bayesian non-parametric model for mixed-membership modeling of aggregated data. The model is based on a beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). While this stochastic process has been used by previous works, as mentioned in the paper, there are several aspects that distinguish this work.

Firstly, this work obtains an EPFP (Exchangeable Partition Probability Function) and a prediction rule based on it. The relationship between Bayesian non-parametrics and EPFP has long been studied in statistics, following the seminal work of J. Pittman. However, this concept has not been widely used in the practice of BNP research. The relationship between BNP and EPFP established in this paper represents, for me, a significant step towards integrating EPFP analysis and BNP research, which could lead to more work in this direction.

Secondly, the idea of randomizing the group size (which is considered a constant in most other works) is interesting. It turns out that this idea makes it possible to obtain certain marginal distributions.

I think this paper has a significant theoretical contribution. However, the practical applicability of this work is still questionable. The estimation algorithm is clearly more complex than the HDP (both in theory and in the algorithm). The improvements demonstrated in the results are not sufficient to convince me that this increased complexity is meaningful in practice. Furthermore, the paper does not provide any intuitive explanation (or justification) as to why the proposed framework would work better than the HDP in practice.

I think this paper has significant theoretical value. However, practical applicability is still unclear. I wish the authors could clarify how this method can improve practical performance against the HDP and why. Overall, I still lean towards acceptance.
