- Introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) that removes the need for truncation via a derived EPPF.  
- Key technical insight: derive the EPPF through a group size dependent model (Lemma 1).  
- Resulting sampler promises simplicity, faster inference, and improved mixing.  
- Appropriately situates work within DP, NRMIs, CRP, EPPF, and HDP literature.  
- Mathematical derivations and lemmas appear technically sound and well-formulated.  
- Novel contribution: first fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP addressing a practical inference limitation.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, with reasonable notation.  
- Potential significance: could substantially advance BNBP use in topic and mixed-membership modeling.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation compares mainly to HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP samplers (truncation- or slice-based).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implementation and scaling (complexity, parallelization, tuning).  
- Weakness: claims of “state-of-the-art predictive performance” are overstated given limited comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid contribution with promising empirical behavior.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacking BNBP-specific empirical comparisons and practical implementation details; some overstated claims.  
- Recommendation: weak accept with minor revisions — add comparisons to other BNBP samplers, temper performance claims, and expand practical/computational discussion.
