- Introduces the EPPF for the BNBP and uses it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based topic modeling.
- Paper is clearly written and technically sound.
- Main theoretical contribution: derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via count-mixture models; theoretically interesting and broadly useful for related models.
- BNBP topic model illustrates the EPPF's utility and prediction rule, but shows only marginal improvements over HDP-LDA.
- Empirical conclusion: BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct modeling properties rather than one being clearly superior.
- Suggests more compelling experiments: directly compare the collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8].
- Notes that uncollapsed representations offer advantages for parallelization and recommends analyzing mixing tradeoffs between uncollapsed and the proposed collapsed sampler.
- Overall assessment: interesting paper that meets the publication threshold; only minor experimental suggestions.
