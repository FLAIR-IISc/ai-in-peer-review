- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).
- The derivation is novel and interesting, relying on Lemma 1.
- Primary concern: the experimental evaluation is inadequate.
- Authors frame the contribution as a sampling-method improvement (simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, strong predictive performance), not a modeling advance.
- For this focus, the algorithm should be compared against other sampling algorithms for the BNBP.
- Current comparison to HDP-LDA is insufficient because it evaluates model performance rather than sampler advantages.
- A direct experimental comparison with other BNBP inference methods is required to substantiate the claims.
