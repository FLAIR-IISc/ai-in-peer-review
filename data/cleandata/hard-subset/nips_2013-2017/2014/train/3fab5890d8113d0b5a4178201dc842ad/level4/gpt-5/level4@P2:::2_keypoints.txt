- Paper derives an EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership models via an ECPF and Bayes’ rule, and uses it to obtain a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler and prediction rule, applied to topic modeling.
- Empirical comparison mainly against HDP-LDA shows fast mixing and competitive or better perplexity when inferred topic counts are similar.
- Novel contribution: first derivation of an EPPF and explicit prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership modeling; construction is elegant.
- Algorithmic contribution: simple, truncation-free, fully collapsed sampler that appears to mix rapidly and is easy to implement.
- Modeling clarity: beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation cleanly links count and mixed-membership interpretations.
- Practical impact: collapsed BNBP topic model is likely useful for practitioners seeking nonparametric, compact representations.
- Major experimental weakness: comparisons are limited to HDP-LDA; must compare to other BNBP inference methods (finite-truncation/blocked Gibbs, slice samplers) and report wall-clock time to target perplexity, ESS, autocorrelation, and memory.
- Ablation gap: need experiments on fixed vs inferred rj, sensitivity to priors for rj, γ0, and c, and clarity on whether c is inferred.
- Metrics/fairness issue: evaluate beyond perplexity (held-out log-likelihood, document completion, predictive likelihood at fixed compute budgets) and equalize topic counts or compute time when comparing methods.
- Complexity: paper should give per-iteration complexity in terms of nnz and KJ, wall-clock runtimes for all methods, and include pseudocode for the collapsed sampler.
- Clarity improvements requested: more intuition for group-size dependent EPPF, move prediction rule earlier with a small worked example, and summarize ECPF→EPPF derivation in main text.
- Notation: unify and define notation early (e.g., n·k, n:k, m, KJ) and reduce repetition; add a roadmap and notation table.
- Claims: “state-of-the-art predictive performance” is overstrong without broader baselines and should be softened or supported by more experiments.
- Technical quality: derivations and augmentation appear correct; add pointers to key lemmas, assumptions, and conditions under which the EPPF holds.
- Presentation issues: generally organized but dense and heavy on notation; minor typographical errors present.
- Originality: high — the EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP mixed-membership and a fully collapsed sampler are novel.
- Significance: potentially high if empirical superiority over other BNBP samplers is demonstrated.
- Pros for acceptance: novel theory (EPPF/prediction rule), simple collapsed sampler, clear modeling link between counts and mixed-membership.
- Cons for acceptance: lacks direct comparisons to existing BNBP samplers, limited evaluation diagnostics and ablations, some clarity issues and overclaiming.
- Recommendation: borderline/weak reject in current form; strengthen experiments (head-to-head BNBP baselines, runtime/ESS diagnostics) and clarify exposition to be acceptable.
