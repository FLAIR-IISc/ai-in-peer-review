This paper presents an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a compact Gibbs sampler for the BNBP subject model.

It is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The primary result of this paper – the derivation of the EPPF for BNBP for mixed-membership modeling ("count-mixture" model) – is interesting in itself and provides a potentially useful tool for obtaining EPPFs for related models.

Additional notes:
While the EPPF and related prediction rule developed for the BNBP subject model are a fair example of its usefulness, the improvements on HDP-LDA are only minor, and the only reassuring conclusion anyone can draw is that the BNBP subject model and HDP-LDA have different characteristics. A more interesting experiment would be to contrast the compact sampler against [10]'s sliced sampler and [8]'s truncated sampler for estimation in the BNBP subject model. There are natural benefits to un-truncated representations when it comes to parallelization, and it would be useful to examine the mixing tradeoff in relation to the compact sampler developed here. Overall, this is an interesting paper and more than the scope of the publication. I only have a few minor comments on the experimental section (see above).
