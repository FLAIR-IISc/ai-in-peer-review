- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP.  
- Develops a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model with simple implementation.  
- Reports fast convergence, good mixing properties, and strong predictive performance.  
- Derivation of Lemma 1 and the associated sampling algorithm is a significant, novel technical contribution.  
- Main concern: paper focuses on the sampling algorithm rather than modeling innovations.  
- Experimental critique: comparisons should evaluate the proposed CG sampler against other BNBP sampling methods, not HDP-LDA.  
- Claims of state-of-the-art predictive performance are unsubstantiated without comparisons to existing BNBP samplers (e.g., finite truncation [10], slice sampling [11], and methods in [16,17]).  
- Recommendation: provide experiments directly comparing the fully collapsed Gibbs sampler to prior BNBP sampling approaches to validate claimed advantages.
