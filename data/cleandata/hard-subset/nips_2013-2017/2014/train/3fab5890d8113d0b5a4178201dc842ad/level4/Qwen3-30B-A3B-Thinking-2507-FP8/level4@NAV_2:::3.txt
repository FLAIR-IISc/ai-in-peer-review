

The paper introduces an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and employs it to develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model. This is a well-written, clearly articulated, and technically rigorous contribution. The paper's main achievement—deriving the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling through a count-mixture framework—is both theoretically interesting and provides valuable methodological tools for developing EPPFs in related contexts.

Additional Comments:
While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable demonstration of the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the performance improvements over HDP-LDA are modest, and the most definitive conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics. A more compelling experiment would compare the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler from [10] and the truncated sampler from [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. The uncollapsed representations naturally offer advantages for parallelization, and it would be beneficial to examine the tradeoffs in mixing performance compared to the collapsed sampler presented here. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication standards for this conference. I have only a few minor suggestions regarding the experimental section (see above).
