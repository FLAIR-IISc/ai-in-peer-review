This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and applies it to topic modeling. The authors derive an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, which is a novel contribution. The proposed BNBP topic model is distinct from existing ones and has a straightforward implementation.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the BNBP and its application to topic modeling. The experimental results show that the proposed BNBP topic model has state-of-the-art predictive performance and is competitive with the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) topic model.

However, the main contribution of this paper is the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP and the proposed fully collapsed Gibbs sampler. The experimental results should focus on comparing the proposed sampler with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP, rather than comparing it with the HDP-LDA. The authors should provide experimental results that demonstrate the advantages of the proposed sampler in terms of convergence speed, mixing, and computational complexity.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should:

* Compare the proposed fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP, such as the blocked Gibbs sampler.
* Provide experimental results that demonstrate the advantages of the proposed sampler in terms of convergence speed, mixing, and computational complexity.
* Discuss the limitations of the proposed sampler and potential future work.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed BNBP topic model is a valuable contribution to the field of topic modeling. However, the experimental results should be more focused on demonstrating the advantages of the proposed sampler.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP.
* The proposed BNBP topic model is distinct from existing ones and has a straightforward implementation.
* The experimental results show that the proposed BNBP topic model has state-of-the-art predictive performance.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The experimental results do not focus on comparing the proposed sampler with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP.
* The paper does not provide a thorough comparison with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP.
* The limitations of the proposed sampler are not thoroughly discussed.
