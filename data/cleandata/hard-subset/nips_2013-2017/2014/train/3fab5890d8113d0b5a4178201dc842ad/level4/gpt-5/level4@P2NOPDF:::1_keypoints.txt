- Proposes a new Bayesian nonparametric mixed‑membership model for grouped data based on the Beta–Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).  
- Derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) and associated predictive rules for the model.  
- Establishing the BNBP–EPPF link is an important step toward unifying EPPF analysis and BNP practice.  
- Introduces randomization of group sizes (instead of treating them as fixed) to enable derivation of otherwise inaccessible marginal distributions.  
- Theoretical contributions are significant and valuable.  
- Practical utility is unclear because inference appears more complex than for the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP).  
- Empirical improvements shown do not convincingly justify the added complexity.  
- Paper lacks an intuitive explanation for why the proposed framework should outperform the HDP in real applications.  
- Reviewer asks authors to clarify when and why the method provides practical benefits and to provide intuition/theoretical insight for observed performance gains.  
- Despite practical concerns, reviewer leans toward acceptance because of the theoretical advances.
