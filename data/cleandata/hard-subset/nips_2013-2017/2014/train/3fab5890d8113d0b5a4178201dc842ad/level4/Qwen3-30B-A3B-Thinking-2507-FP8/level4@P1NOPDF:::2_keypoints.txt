- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Bayesian nonparametric nested Chinese restaurant process (BNBP).  
- Lemma 1 and the derived sampling procedure are novel and well-motivated.  
- Paper’s primary contribution is algorithmic (the sampler) rather than a new statistical model.  
- Evaluation must therefore compare the proposed sampler to other existing BNBP sampling algorithms.  
- Current experiments incorrectly compare to HDP-LDA, which is a different model, not a BNBP sampler.  
- Comparing to HDP-LDA does not validate the claimed advantages (simplicity, convergence, mixing, predictive performance) for BNBP inference.  
- Required experiments: direct comparisons to established BNBP samplers on standard benchmarks with metrics like predictive perplexity, effective sample size, and runtime.  
- Without those comparative results, the experimental section fails to support the paper’s central claims and weakens the contribution.
