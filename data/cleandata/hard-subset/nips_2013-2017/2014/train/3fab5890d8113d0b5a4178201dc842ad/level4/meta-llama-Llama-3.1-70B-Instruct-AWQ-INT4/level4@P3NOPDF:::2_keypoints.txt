- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) with a novel lemma and clear theoretical contribution.  
- Sampler has potential value due to its theoretical novelty.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is inadequate.  
- Authors claim benefits (simplicity, fast convergence, good mixing, SOTA predictive performance) that require algorithm-focused comparisons.  
- Current experiments compare to HDP-LDA, which is not an appropriate baseline for a BNBP sampling algorithm.  
- Paper should compare the proposed sampler to other BNBP-specific sampling algorithms.  
- Including experiments on convergence, mixing, and predictive performance against other BNBP samplers would significantly strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: promising theory but experimental results fall short; revise experiments to provide comprehensive BNBP algorithm comparisons.
