- Presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Uses the BNBP EPPF to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and demonstrates strong subject-matter understanding.  
- Provides a thorough, technically sound derivation of the BNBP EPPF.  
- The BNBP EPPF derivation is novel and interesting, with potential utility for related models.  
- Experimental comparisons to HDP-LDA are provided and well-organized.  
- Empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal.  
- Would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with additional baseline models.  
- Some technical details may be difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Overall significance: offers a useful tool for deriving EPPFs and advancing mixed-membership modeling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of EPPF derivation, collapsed sampler contribution, and broader applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical gains versus HDP-LDA and accessibility concerns for non-experts.
