- Lemma 1 and the derived fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP are novel and interesting.  
- Main concern: the experimental evaluation is insufficient.  
- Stated motivation: develop a CG sampler for BNBP for simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.  
- The contribution is primarily algorithmic, not a new statistical model.  
- Experiments should compare the proposed CG sampler directly against other BNBP sampling algorithms rather than HDP-LDA.  
- Suggested baselines: uncollapsed or partially collapsed Gibbs, other MCMC variants, and existing BNBP samplers from the literature.  
- Empirical evaluation should report convergence speed, mixing behavior, and predictive performance using wall-clock time, iterations to convergence, and standard mixing diagnostics.  
- Summary: theoretical contribution is compelling, but the paper needs benchmarking against other BNBP samplers to support claimed advantages.
