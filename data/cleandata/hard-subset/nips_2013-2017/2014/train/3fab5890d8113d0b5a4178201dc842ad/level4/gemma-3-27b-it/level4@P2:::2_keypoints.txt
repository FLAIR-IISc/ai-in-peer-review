- Introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) to enable efficient inference.  
- Derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, which is key to the CG sampler (Lemma 1).  
- Addresses the core challenge that BNBP lacks a readily available marginal distribution, forcing truncation or complex samplers.  
- The fully marginalized sampler offers a compelling analytical solution and is a novel contribution.  
- Builds appropriately on prior nonparametric Bayesian work (Dirichlet processes, NRMIs, EPPFs).  
- Acknowledges related models (negative binomial IBP, “ice cream” buffet) while emphasizing the unique EPPF-based approach.  
- Mathematical derivations appear rigorous, well-presented, and technically sound.  
- Inclusion of closed-form parameter update equations is a significant practical advantage.  
- Major weakness: experimental comparison is to HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP sampling methods, which is misaligned with the paper’s primary contribution.  
- Missing benchmarks against truncated Gibbs or slice sampling for BNBP weakens claims about speed, mixing, and computational efficiency.  
- The “state-of-the-art predictive performance” claim is unsubstantiated without direct algorithmic comparisons within the BNBP framework.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add focused experiments comparing the proposed CG sampler to existing BNBP samplers to strengthen the evaluation.
