This manuscript proposes a new Bayesian nonparametric approach to mixed-membership modeling for grouped data, built on the Beta–Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Although the BNBP has been explored previously, the paper introduces several distinguishing elements. 
First, it derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP and provides the corresponding prediction rule. The connection between BNP models and EPPFs—originating in Pitman’s work—has been well studied in statistics but has not been widely utilized in practical BNP modeling. Establishing a concrete EPPF for the BNBP represents, in my view, a meaningful step toward unifying EPPF analysis with BNP methodology and may stimulate additional work in this direction.
Second, the choice to randomize group sizes (typically treated as fixed in much of the literature) is intriguing and enables derivations of marginal distributions that would otherwise be difficult.

I view the theoretical contributions as significant. However, I am less convinced about the practical benefits. The proposed inference appears more involved than HDP—both conceptually and algorithmically—and the empirical improvements shown do not yet clearly warrant the additional complexity. In addition, the paper does not provide an intuitive rationale for why, in practice, this framework should outperform the HDP.

In summary, the theoretical value is high, but the practical utility remains uncertain. I encourage the authors to clarify how and why their method improves practical performance relative to the HDP, and under what conditions. Overall, I remain slightly in favor of acceptance.
