This paper proposes a new Bayesian nonparametric model for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data, built on the Beta–Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Although the BNBP has appeared in prior work, this paper introduces two notable advances.

First, it derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the model and the associated predictive rules. While the relationship between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPFs has been well studied in statistics since Pitman’s seminal work, it has been underutilized in practical BNP modeling. Establishing a concrete link between the BNBP and an EPPF is, in my view, an important step toward unifying EPPF-based analysis with BNP practice and could catalyze further research.

Second, the paper proposes randomizing group sizes, which are typically treated as fixed. This choice is interesting and enables the derivation of certain marginal distributions that would otherwise be inaccessible.

Overall, the theoretical contributions are significant. However, the practical utility is less clear. The inference procedure appears more complex than that of the HDP—both conceptually and algorithmically—and the empirical improvements shown do not convincingly justify this added complexity. Moreover, the paper lacks an intuitive explanation for why the proposed framework should outperform the HDP in real applications.

I encourage the authors to clarify when and why their method provides practical benefits over the HDP, and to offer intuition or theoretical insight that explains the observed (or expected) performance gains relative to the increased complexity.

In sum, I value the paper’s theoretical contributions and the EPPF connection, which I see as an important development. Despite my concerns about practical impact, I lean toward acceptance.
