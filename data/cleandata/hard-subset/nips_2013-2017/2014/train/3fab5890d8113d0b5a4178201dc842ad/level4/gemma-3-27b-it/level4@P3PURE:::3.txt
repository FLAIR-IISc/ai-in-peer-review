The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. 

This is a well-written and reasonably clear, technically sound paper. The primary result – a derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, achieved via “count-mixture” models – is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Additional Comments: 

While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration of the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal, and the only firm conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics. A more compelling experiment would be to contrast the proposed collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations offer natural advantages for parallelization, and examining the mixing tradeoffs relative to the collapsed sampler developed here would be valuable. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold; my comments pertain primarily to the experimental section, as detailed above.
