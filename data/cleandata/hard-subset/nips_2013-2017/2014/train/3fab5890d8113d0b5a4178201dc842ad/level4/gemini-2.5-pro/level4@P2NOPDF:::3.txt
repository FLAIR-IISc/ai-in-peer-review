This paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and leverages this result to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.

The paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented. Its primary contribution—the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP in the context of mixed-membership modeling—is significant. This result, obtained through a "count-mixture" model formulation, is not only novel but also offers a valuable methodology that could be adapted for deriving EPPFs for related stochastic processes.

The BNBP topic model serves as a suitable application to demonstrate the utility of the derived EPPF and its associated predictive rule. However, the experimental comparison with the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP) Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) model is not entirely convincing. The reported performance improvements are marginal, and the primary conclusion drawn is that the BNBP and HDP models have distinct characteristics, rather than demonstrating a clear advantage for the proposed model.

A more insightful experiment would be to compare the proposed collapsed Gibbs sampler against existing uncollapsed inference methods for the BNBP topic model, such as the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8]. Such a comparison would be particularly illuminating because uncollapsed representations offer inherent advantages for parallelization. An analysis of the trade-offs between the mixing efficiency of the proposed collapsed sampler and the scalability of uncollapsed alternatives would constitute a much stronger empirical contribution and provide valuable guidance to practitioners.

In summary, this is a strong paper with a valuable theoretical contribution. While the core results are publication-worthy, the experimental section could be significantly strengthened. Despite this reservation regarding the experiments, the paper meets the threshold for publication and I recommend acceptance.
