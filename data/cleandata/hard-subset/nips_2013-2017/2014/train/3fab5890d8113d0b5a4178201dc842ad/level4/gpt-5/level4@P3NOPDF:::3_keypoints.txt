- Derives the EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) via a count-mixture construction and develops a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and technically careful; derivations appear correct and easy to follow.
- Main contribution: the BNBP EPPF is a valuable foundation for collapsed inference and a template for deriving EPPFs for related processes.
- The count-mixture perspective provides an insightful unification of BNP viewpoints that may aid future methodological work.
- Empirical results: BNBP topic model is a reasonable illustration but shows only marginal improvements over HDP-LDA, reflecting different inductive biases rather than clear superiority.
- Recommend explicitly discussing use-cases where BNBP should excel (e.g., modeling over-dispersion or burstiness) and providing targeted evidence.
- Recommend directly comparing the proposed collapsed Gibbs sampler to sliced and truncated samplers for the same BNBP model to clarify practical trade-offs.
- Comparison should evaluate mixing/sample efficiency, convergence speed and wall-clock time, per-iteration complexity and scalability, and parallelization behavior.
- Ask for additional experimental details for reproducibility: per-iteration runtime, total runtime to fixed accuracy, and convergence diagnostics.
- Request full dataset/preprocessing/hyperparameter details and clarification on whether priors and initialization were matched across methods.
- Suggest including sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters.
- Overall assessment: interesting and methodologically meaningful contribution; recommend acceptance contingent on strengthening empirical comparisons and discussing mixing/scalability trade-offs.
