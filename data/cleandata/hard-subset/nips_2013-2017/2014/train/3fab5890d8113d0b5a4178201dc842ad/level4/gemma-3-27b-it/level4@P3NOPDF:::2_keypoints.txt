- Novel contribution: derivation of Lemma 1 and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP is interesting and novel.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is misaligned with the paper’s sampling-methodology focus.  
- Wrong baseline: experiments compare to HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP sampling algorithms.  
- Missing comparisons: no direct empirical comparison to existing BNBP samplers using sampling-relevant metrics (runtime, convergence diagnostics, mixing rates).  
- Need to demonstrate advantages: show computational cost and statistical efficiency trade-offs and scenarios where the sampler outperforms alternatives.  
- Lemma 1 clarity: statement and proof need clearer presentation and an intuitive explanation of implications.  
- Scalability: discuss performance and limitations on high-dimensional or large datasets.  
- Implementation details: provide optimizations and implementation specifics to improve reproducibility and fair comparison.  
- Convergence analysis: include more rigorous convergence diagnostics beyond visual trace plots.  
- Recommendation: theoretically sound but experiments insufficient; recommend rejection now but willingness to reconsider after a revised submission addressing these issues.
