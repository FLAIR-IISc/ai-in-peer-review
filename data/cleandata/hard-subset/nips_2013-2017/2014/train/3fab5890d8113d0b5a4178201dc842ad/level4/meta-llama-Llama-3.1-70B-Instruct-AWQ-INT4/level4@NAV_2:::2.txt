This paper proposes a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the integer-valued beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, leading to a nonparametric Bayesian topic model that is distinct from existing ones, with simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.

The main ideas of the paper are related to previous work on nonparametric Bayesian mixture modeling, such as the Dirichlet process and the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP). The authors build upon this work by introducing an EPPF for the BNBP, which is a unique contribution. The paper also discusses the strengths and weaknesses of the BNBP compared to other models, such as the HDP.

The strengths of the paper include the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP, which is a significant contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The authors also provide a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, which is a novel and interesting result. The experimental results show that the BNBP topic model has state-of-the-art predictive performance and is able to achieve the same predictive power as the HDP-LDA with a more compact representation of the corpus.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The experimental results are limited to a comparison with the HDP-LDA, and it would be beneficial to compare the proposed algorithm with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the BNBP and other nonparametric Bayesian models.

In terms of the criteria for evaluation, the paper scores high on originality, as the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP is a unique contribution. The paper also scores high on significance, as the results have the potential to impact the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The paper scores well on quality, as the derivation of the EPPF and the Gibbs sampler are technically sound. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the BNBP and other nonparametric Bayesian models, which would improve the clarity of the paper.
