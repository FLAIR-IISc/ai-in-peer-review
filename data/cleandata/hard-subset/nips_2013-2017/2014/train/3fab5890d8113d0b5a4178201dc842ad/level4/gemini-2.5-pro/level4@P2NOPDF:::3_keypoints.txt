- Paper derives the EPPF for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) using a "count-mixture" model formulation.  
- The EPPF derivation is novel and significant, and the technique could be adapted to derive EPPFs for related stochastic processes.  
- Constructs a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model leveraging the derived EPPF and predictive rule.  
- Manuscript is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented.  
- BNBP topic model is an appropriate application to demonstrate the EPPF's utility.  
- Experimental comparison to HDP-LDA is unconvincing: performance gains are marginal and the main takeaway is differing model characteristics rather than clear superiority.  
- Recommends comparing the proposed collapsed sampler to existing uncollapsed BNBP inference methods (e.g., sliced sampler [10], truncated sampler [8]).  
- Suggests evaluating trade-offs between mixing efficiency of the collapsed sampler and scalability/parallelization advantages of uncollapsed samplers.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution, experiments need strengthening, but paper meets publication threshold and reviewer recommends acceptance.
