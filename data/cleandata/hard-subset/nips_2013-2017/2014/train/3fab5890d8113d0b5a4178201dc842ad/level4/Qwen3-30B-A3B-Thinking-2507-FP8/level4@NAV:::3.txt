

The paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and employs it to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based topic modeling.  
This is a clearly written, technically rigorous contribution. The paper's central result—a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling (obtained through "count-mixture" models)—is both theoretically compelling and offers valuable methodological tools for deriving EPPFs in related processes.  
Additional Comments:  
While the BNBP topic model effectively demonstrates the utility of the derived EPPF and prediction rule, its performance gains over HDP-LDA are marginal, and the only definitive conclusion is that BNBP and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct properties. A more compelling experiment would compare the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler in [10] and the truncated sampler in [8] for BNBP topic modeling inference. The uncollapsed representations inherently support parallelization, making it valuable to evaluate their mixing behavior relative to the collapsed sampler presented here. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold; minor suggestions regarding the experimental section are provided above.
