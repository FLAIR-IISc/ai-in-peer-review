The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

This is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The primary result – a derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, achieved through “count-mixture” models – is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models. 

Additional Comments:

While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal. The only firm conclusion that can be drawn is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics. A more compelling experiment would be to compare the proposed collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations offer inherent advantages in terms of parallelization, and it would be beneficial to examine the mixing tradeoffs relative to the collapsed sampler developed here. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. My only substantive comments pertain to the experimental section, as outlined above.
