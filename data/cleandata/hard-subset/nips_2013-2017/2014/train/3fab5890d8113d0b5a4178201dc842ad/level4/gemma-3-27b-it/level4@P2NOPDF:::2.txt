This paper introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler specifically designed for the Bayesian Nonparametric Mixture Model (BNBP). The proposed lemma and the resulting sampler represent a novel and interesting contribution to the field.

My primary concern lies with the experimental evaluation. The authors motivate the development of this CG sampler by highlighting its potential for simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing properties, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. This framing clearly positions the work as a contribution to sampling methodology, rather than to statistical modeling itself. Consequently, the evaluation should focus on comparing the performance of *different sampling algorithms for the BNBP*, and not against HDP-LDA. 

Currently, the paper lacks a critical comparison to existing BNBP sampling methods. While comparisons to HDP-LDA may be informative in a broader context, they do not directly address the core claim of improved sampling efficiency for the BNBP. To adequately demonstrate the benefits of the proposed CG sampler, the authors should include results from other established BNBP sampling techniques. Providing such a comparative analysis is crucial to substantiating the claimed advantages of simplicity, convergence speed, and mixing quality.

In summary, the theoretical contribution is valuable. However, the current experimental setup does not fully support the stated goals of the work and a more focused evaluation against alternative BNBP samplers is necessary.
