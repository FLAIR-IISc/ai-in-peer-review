- Derives an EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed‑membership settings by forming an ECPF for randomized group sizes and applying Bayes’ rule.  
- Provides a simple, informative prediction rule that highlights the role of dispersion parameters.  
- Presents a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the beta process, topic parameters, and topic weights (truncation‑free).  
- Shows the BNBP topic model differs from HDP‑LDA via multiplicative coupling of global and document‑specific popularity.  
- Empirically, the collapsed sampler mixes rapidly and achieves competitive held‑out perplexities with a relatively compact set of topics.  
- Situates the work within nonparametric mixture and mixed‑membership literature (DP/EPPF, NRMIs, gamma–Poisson, BNBP, GNBP).  
- Novelty: first EPPF for BNBP in mixed‑membership context and associated collapsed inference; methodology (ECPF→EPPF) is potentially generalizable.  
- Technical quality: derivations and augmentation strategies are sound, well motivated, and correctly implemented.  
- Clarity: generally well written; ECPF→EPPF exposition and digamma distribution role are clear, though notation could be streamlined for non‑specialists.  
- Significance: moderate–high — enables truncation‑free, collapsed inference for an important count‑based prior and suggests extensions to other integer‑valued processes.  
- Main empirical weakness: gains over HDP‑LDA are modest; differences are primarily in shrinkage behavior and model compactness.  
- Missing comparisons: should include uncollapsed BNBP samplers (slice‑based and truncated variants) to quantify mixing, computational cost, and accuracy trade‑offs.  
- Scalability concern: limited discussion of parallelization and wall‑clock performance; uncollapsed samplers may parallelize more naturally.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: additional analyses recommended for η, dispersion rj, c, γ0 due to observed dependence.  
- Pros summarized: new EPPF and prediction rule, simple collapsed sampler with fast mixing, clear relation to HDP‑LDA, competitive performance with fewer topics.  
- Cons summarized: limited empirical comparison to alternative BNBP inference, modest average improvement over HDP‑LDA, and sparse discussion of parallel/large‑scale deployment.  
- Recommendation: accept — solid theoretical and practical contribution; advise adding comparisons to slice/truncated samplers and discussion of parallelization/mixing trade‑offs.
