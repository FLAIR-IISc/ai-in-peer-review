- Paper derives the EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and constructs a collapsed Gibbs sampler; manuscript is well written, clear, and technically sound.
- Main contribution is the EPPF for BNBP in mixed-membership settings via a count-mixture construction, which is interesting and provides useful tools and a practical predictive rule.
- BNBP topic model reasonably demonstrates the EPPF’s utility, but empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal.
- More accurate conclusion is that BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics rather than one being clearly superior.
- Experiments should compare the proposed collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.
- Because uncollapsed representations are naturally parallelizable, the authors should examine their mixing and computational trade-offs relative to the collapsed sampler.
- Overall assessment: interesting paper that meets the publication threshold, with only minor comments on the experimental section.
