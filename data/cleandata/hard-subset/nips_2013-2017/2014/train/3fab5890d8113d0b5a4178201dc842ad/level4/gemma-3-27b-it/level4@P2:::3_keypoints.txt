The paper introduces an EPPF for the BNBP and uses it to build a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.  
Well-written, technically sound, and generally clear.  
Primary contribution: novel derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF via “count-mixture” models, useful for related models.  
Extends the EPPF framework to mixed-membership settings and resolves the prior difficulty of defining a BNBP marginal.  

Strength: Rigorous mathematical derivations enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling.  
Strength: Novel approach (group size dependent model) filling a gap in the literature.  
Strength: Clear exposition with sufficient detail for expert readers.  
Strength: Potential impact — foundation for more efficient inference and new integer-valued process models.  

Weakness: Empirical gains over HDP-LDA are marginal and need stronger demonstration of benefits.  
Weakness: Lacks comparison to other BNBP inference methods (e.g., slice sampling, truncated sampling).  
Weakness: Limited discussion of scalability and computational trade-offs between collapsed and uncollapsed inference.  
Weakness: Experimental section is narrow; needs more datasets and hyperparameter exploration.  

Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, enables efficient inference, clear writing, and potential to inspire further work.  
Arguments against acceptance: modest empirical improvements, insufficient comparisons to other BNBP techniques, and limited scalability analysis.  

Overall: Solid and interesting paper with valuable theoretical contributions despite limited empirical gains.  
Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experimental comparisons and discuss scalability/computational complexity.
