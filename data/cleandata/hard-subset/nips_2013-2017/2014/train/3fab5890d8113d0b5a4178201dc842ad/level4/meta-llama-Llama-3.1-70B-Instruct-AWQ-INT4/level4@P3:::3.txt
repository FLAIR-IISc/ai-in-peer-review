This paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. The primary result of the paper - a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling (arrived at via "count-mixture" models) - is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

The paper is well-written and reasonably clear. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the background and related work, which helps to contextualize their contributions. The technical presentation is sound, and the authors provide sufficient detail to enable the reader to follow their derivations.

The BNBP topic model is a reasonable illustration of the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule. However, the improvements over the HDP-LDA are only marginal, and the only confident conclusion one can draw is that the BNBP topic model and the HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics. A more interesting experiment would be to contrast the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. The uncollapsed representations have natural advantages when it comes to parallelization, and it would be useful to examine mixing tradeoffs wrt to the collapsed sampler developed here.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method. However, the evaluation could be strengthened by including more comparisons to other methods and exploring the robustness of the results to different parameter settings.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, but some sections could be improved for better readability. For example, the introduction could be more concise, and the technical sections could be broken up with more headings and subheadings.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, which is a significant contribution to the field.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to impact the field of topic modeling and nonparametric Bayesian methods. The BNBP topic model and the corresponding collapsed Gibbs sampler provide a new tool for researchers and practitioners in these areas.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling.
* The BNBP topic model and the corresponding collapsed Gibbs sampler provide a new tool for researchers and practitioners in the field of topic modeling and nonparametric Bayesian methods.
* The paper is well-written and reasonably clear.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The improvements over the HDP-LDA are only marginal.
* The evaluation could be strengthened by including more comparisons to other methods and exploring the robustness of the results to different parameter settings.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that passes the publication threshold. I only have a few minor comments on the experimental section, and I believe that the paper would benefit from addressing these points.
