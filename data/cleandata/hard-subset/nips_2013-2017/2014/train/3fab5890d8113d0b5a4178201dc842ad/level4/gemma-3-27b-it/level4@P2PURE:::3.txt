The paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and demonstrates its application in a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. 

This is a well-written and technically sound paper with a reasonably clear presentation. The core contribution – a derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF within the context of mixed-membership modeling, achieved through “count-mixture” models – is both theoretically interesting and offers potentially valuable tools for deriving EPPFs for related models. 

Additional Comments: 
Although the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable demonstration of the EPPF’s utility and associated prediction rule, the performance improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal. The results suggest that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics, but a stronger evaluation would involve a comparison of the collapsed sampler presented here against the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] for inference within the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations offer inherent advantages regarding parallelization, and exploring the mixing tradeoffs between these approaches and the proposed collapsed sampler would be beneficial. Overall, this is an interesting and publishable paper; my comments are limited to minor points concerning the experimental section (see above).
