- Introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and applies it in a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound.  
- Core contribution is a novel derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF via a “count-mixture” framework, offering a foundation for deriving EPPFs for similar models.  
- The BNBP topic model reasonably demonstrates the EPPF’s utility and prediction rule.  
- Empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are modest; results show differing behaviors rather than a clear performance advantage.  
- Recommend comparing the collapsed Gibbs sampler to alternative BNBP inference methods (sliced sampler and truncated sampler).  
- Suggest investigating mixing properties, parallelization benefits, and computational tradeoffs of uncollapsed vs. collapsed representations, especially on large datasets.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, worthwhile contribution that meets publication standards; experimental section could be strengthened by the suggested comparisons (minor revisions).
