This paper presents an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to create a reduced Gibbs sampler for the BNBP subject model.

It is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The main result of this paper – a derivation of the EPPF for BNBP for mixed-membership modeling (obtained through a "mixture-count" model) – is itself interesting and provides potentially useful tools for creating EPPFs for related models.

Additional comments:
Although the BNBP subject model is a reasonable example of the utility of the improved EPPF and associated prediction rules, the improvements are minor compared to the HDP-LDA, and the only confident conclusion that can be drawn is that the BNBP subject model and the HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics. A more interesting test would be to compare the reduced sampler with [10]'s sliced sampler and [8]'s truncated sampler, which can be used for BNBP subject model estimation. The unreduced representations have the usual advantage of parallelism and the advantages of mixing with the reduced sampler created here need to be tested. Overall, it is an interesting paper and worthy of publication. I have a few small comments about the experimental section (see above).
