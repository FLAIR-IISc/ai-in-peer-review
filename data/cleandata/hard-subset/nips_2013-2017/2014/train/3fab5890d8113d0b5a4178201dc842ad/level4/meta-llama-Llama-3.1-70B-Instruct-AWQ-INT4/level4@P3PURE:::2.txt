This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Bayesian Nonparametric Beta Process (BNBP), presenting Lemma 1 and the derived sampler as novel and interesting contributions. However, my primary concern lies in the experimental evaluation.

The motivation behind developing the CG sampler for the BNBP is its potential for simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. Given this focus on the sampling algorithm rather than statistical modeling, a comparison with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP is necessary to assess the proposed method's effectiveness.

Instead, the experiments compare the proposed sampler to HDP-LDA, which is not directly relevant to the stated goals of this work. To provide a comprehensive evaluation, experimental results comparing the proposed fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP to other existing sampling algorithms for the BNBP are needed.
