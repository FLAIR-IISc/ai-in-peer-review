- Presents the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and develops a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that avoids truncation.
- Derivation via a “count-mixture” approach is a significant theoretical contribution, enabling systematic EPPF derivations for related models.
- The collapsed Gibbs sampler is a practical advance that improves inference efficiency and accuracy over truncation-based methods.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and connects appropriately to nonparametric Bayesian literature (DP, CRP, HDP).
- Novel approach of group-size dependent modeling broadens the toolkit for mixed-membership and grouped-data models.
- Empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal, so practical benefits are not strongly demonstrated.
- Experimental comparisons are limited—missing direct evaluation against other BNBP inference methods (e.g., sliced and truncated samplers) for speed and mixing.
- Discussion of parallelization is brief; potential for parallelizing the collapsed sampler or its challenges should be addressed.
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (especially η) is noted but requires deeper analysis and tuning strategies.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, addresses key limitations, clear presentation, and competitive empirical viability.
- Arguments against acceptance: modest empirical gains, limited baselines, and insufficient parallelization discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong and valuable contribution that likely meets publication standards despite limited empirical dominance.
- Minor comments: better motivate unified mixed-membership treatment in the introduction, clarify BNBP vs HDP-LDA shrinkage differences, and fix inconsistent reference formatting.
