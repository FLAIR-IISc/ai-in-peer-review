- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP); Lemma 1 and the sampler are novel and interesting.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is inadequate.  
- Paper’s motivation claims CG sampler gives simpler implementation, faster convergence, better mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance; contribution is the inference algorithm rather than a new model.  
- Experiments should directly compare the proposed CG sampler to alternative BNBP inference methods instead of comparing to HDP-LDA.  
- Need comparisons to other BNBP algorithms (uncollapsed/partially collapsed Gibbs, existing MCMC, variational baselines) and metrics for convergence speed, mixing quality, and predictive performance.  
- Current empirical results do not evaluate against other BNBP inference schemes; such comparisons are necessary to validate the claimed advantages.
