

This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP and develop a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model with simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing properties, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. The derivation of Lemma 1 and the associated sampling algorithm represent a significant technical contribution that is novel and interesting.

However, I have concerns regarding the experimental evaluation. The paper's primary motivation centers on developing a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP to achieve the claimed advantages of simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and superior predictive performance. This work is fundamentally focused on the sampling algorithm rather than on statistical modeling innovations. Consequently, the experimental comparison should directly evaluate the proposed CG sampler against alternative sampling approaches for the BNBP itself, rather than comparing against HDP-LDA, which is a different model entirely.

The paper claims the BNBP topic model achieves state-of-the-art predictive performance, but without comparative results on other sampling algorithms specifically designed for the BNBP (such as the finite truncation approach in [10], slice sampling in [11], or the approaches in [16, 17]), these claims remain unsubstantiated. To properly validate their sampling algorithm's advantages, the authors need to provide experimental results comparing their fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with existing sampling methods for the BNBP. Without these comparisons, the experimental section does not adequately support the paper's central claims about the sampler's performance benefits.
