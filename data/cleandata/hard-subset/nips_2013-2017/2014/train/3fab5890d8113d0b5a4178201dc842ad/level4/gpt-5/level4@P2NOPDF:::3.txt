The paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. The manuscript is well written, clear, and technically sound. The main contribution—the EPPF for BNBP in mixed-membership settings, obtained via a count-mixture construction—is interesting in its own right and offers useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models, as well as a practical predictive rule for inference.

While the BNBP topic model is a reasonable demonstration of the utility of the EPPF and prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal. The most defensible conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics rather than a clear superiority of one over the other. A more informative experiment would compare the proposed collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Because uncollapsed representations are naturally amenable to parallelization, it would be valuable to examine their mixing and computational trade-offs relative to the collapsed sampler developed here. 

Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. I have only minor comments on the experimental section (as noted above).
