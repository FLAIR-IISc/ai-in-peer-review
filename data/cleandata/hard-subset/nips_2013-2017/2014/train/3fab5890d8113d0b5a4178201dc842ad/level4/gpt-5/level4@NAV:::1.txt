This paper introduces a new Bayesian nonparametric model for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data, built on the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). While prior work has leveraged this stochastic process, as noted in the paper, several elements differentiate this study.

First, the paper derives an EPPF (exchangeable partition probability function) and the associated prediction rules. The relationship between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPFs has been explored in statistics since the seminal work of J. Pitman, yet this perspective has not been widely utilized in practical BNP research. The linkage established here between the BNBP and an EPPF represents, in my view, a significant step toward unifying EPPF-based analysis with BNP methodology, which could spur further developments.

Second, the choice to treat group sizes as random (where most prior work takes them as fixed constants) is intriguing. This modeling decision enables the derivation of certain marginal distributions.

I find the theoretical contributions substantial. However, the practical utility remains uncertain. The inference scheme is clearly more complex than HDP, both conceptually and algorithmically. The empirical improvements reported are not, to me, sufficient to justify the increased complexity in practical settings. Additionally, the paper does not provide an intuitive rationale for why the proposed framework should outperform HDP in practice.

In summary, the paper offers strong theoretical value, but its practical benefits are unclear. I encourage the authors to clarify how and why this method could improve real-world performance relative to HDP. Overall, I still lean toward acceptance.
