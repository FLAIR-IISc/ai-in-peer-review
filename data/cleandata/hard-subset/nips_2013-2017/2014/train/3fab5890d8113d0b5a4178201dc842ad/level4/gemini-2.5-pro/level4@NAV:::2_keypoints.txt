- Introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).  
- Theoretical contribution (Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler) is novel and compelling.  
- Primary concern: the experimental evaluation is weak.  
- Authors claim benefits in implementation, convergence speed, mixing, and predictive performance, framing the work as an algorithmic contribution.  
- Experiments must compare the proposed sampler against other inference algorithms for the BNBP to validate those claims.  
- Current comparison to HDP-LDA is insufficient because it conflates sampler performance with differences in underlying models.  
- Recommendation: add direct benchmarks versus alternative BNBP samplers to strengthen the paper.
