This paper introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Bayesian nonparametric Bayesian prior (BNBP), accompanied by a novel lemma and its corresponding sampler. The theoretical contribution of this work is noteworthy and engaging.

However, the experimental design raises some concerns. The primary motivation behind developing the CG sampler for the BNBP is its simplicity, rapid convergence, good mixing properties, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. As this work focuses on the sampling algorithm rather than statistical modeling, a more suitable comparison would be with other existing sampling algorithms for the BNBP.

A fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP is indeed presented, but to further establish its efficacy, the experimental results should be complemented with comparisons to other sampling algorithms designed specifically for the BNBP, rather than the hierarchical Dirichlet process latent Dirichlet allocation (HDP-LDA) model.
