- Introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP process with a novel Lemma 1 and derived sampling procedure.  
- Motivation (simplicity, fast convergence, efficient mixing, strong predictive performance) is appropriate for advancing BNBP sampling methodology.  
- Main experimental flaw: inappropriate baseline comparison—only compared against HDP-LDA rather than BNBP samplers.  
- HDP-LDA and BNBP are fundamentally different models, so the comparison is irrelevant to sampler evaluation.  
- Claims of "fast convergence" and "good mixing" cannot be validated without direct comparisons to other BNBP sampling algorithms.  
- Lack of baseline BNBP samplers (e.g., standard Gibbs, variational methods, alternative collapsed schemes) renders results inconclusive.  
- No reported standard metrics (perplexity, clustering accuracy, convergence speed, effective sample size, autocorrelation) to support algorithmic claims.  
- The observed performance may reflect model differences rather than sampler improvements.  
- Required fixes: replace HDP-LDA with relevant BNBP baselines and run side-by-side experiments.  
- Required fixes: report convergence/mixing diagnostics and predictive metrics under identical conditions.  
- Conclusion: theoretical contribution is valuable, but experimental shortcomings significantly weaken the paper; recommend rejection unless critical experiments are added.
