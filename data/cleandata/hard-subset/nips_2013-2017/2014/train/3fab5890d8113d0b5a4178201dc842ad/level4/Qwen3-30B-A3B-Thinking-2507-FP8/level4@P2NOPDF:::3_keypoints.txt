- Introduces the EPPF for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and a collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based topic models.  
- Paper is well-written, technically solid, and presents a novel EPPF derivation via count-mixture modeling.  
- EPPF derivation is the primary technical contribution and enables mixed-membership modeling under the BNBP.  
- The derivation provides a reusable framework for deriving EPPFs in related exchangeable processes.  
- BNBP topic model is a reasonable application, but empirical gains over HDP-LDA are only marginal.  
- Results mainly show that BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct modeling properties rather than clear superiority.  
- Recommend stronger experimental comparisons against alternative BNBP inference methods (sliced and truncated samplers).  
- Suggest evaluating mixing efficiency and scalability tradeoffs, since uncollapsed samplers may parallelize better.  
- Minor experimental fixes: report runtimes, clarify hyperparameter selection, and discuss BNBP’s handling of varying document lengths.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful theoretical contribution, technically sound, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
