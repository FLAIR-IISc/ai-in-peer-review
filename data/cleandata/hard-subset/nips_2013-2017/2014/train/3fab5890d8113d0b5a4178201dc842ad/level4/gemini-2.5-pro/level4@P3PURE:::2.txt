This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The derivation, which relies on Lemma 1, is both novel and interesting.

My primary concern, however, lies with the experimental evaluation. The authors' stated motivation is to develop a sampler for the BNBP that offers simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. This frames the contribution as an improvement in sampling methodology, rather than in statistical modeling.

Given this focus, the proposed algorithm should be compared against other sampling algorithms for the BNBP. The current comparison to HDP-LDA is insufficient, as it evaluates the performance of the underlying model, not the specific advantages of the proposed sampler. To properly substantiate the paper's claims, a direct experimental comparison with other inference methods for the BNBP is required.
