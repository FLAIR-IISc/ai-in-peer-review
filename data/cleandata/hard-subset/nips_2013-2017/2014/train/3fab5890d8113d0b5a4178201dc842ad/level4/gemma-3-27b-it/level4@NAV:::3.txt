This paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and leverages this result to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler specifically for the BNBP topic model.

The work is presented clearly and is technically solid. The core contribution – a derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF within the context of mixed-membership models, achieved through a “count-mixture” framework – is both a valuable result and offers promising avenues for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Further Comments: 
The BNBP topic model serves as a sensible demonstration of the EPPF’s applicability and associated predictive distribution, however, observed performance gains over the hierarchical Dirichlet process latent Dirichlet allocation (HDP-LDA) model are limited. The analysis suggests that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit differing characteristics rather than a clear performance advantage for either. A compelling extension would be a comparative analysis of the proposed collapsed sampler against existing inference methods for the BNBP topic model, specifically the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8].  Considering the parallelization benefits of uncollapsed representations, investigating the mixing properties of the developed collapsed sampler in relation to these alternative approaches would be insightful.  In summary, this is a worthwhile contribution that meets the criteria for publication; my feedback primarily concerns potential refinements to the experimental evaluation (as outlined above).
