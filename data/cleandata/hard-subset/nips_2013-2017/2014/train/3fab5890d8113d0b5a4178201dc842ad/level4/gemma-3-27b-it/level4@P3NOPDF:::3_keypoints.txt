- Paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and applies it to a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- Manuscript is well-written, technically sound, and presents a clear exposition of a challenging topic.
- Core contribution is the BNBP EPPF derivation via a “count-mixture” framework, offering theoretical interest and a practical toolkit for related models.
- Authors demonstrate the EPPF’s utility through application and a prediction rule for the BNBP topic model.
- Empirical results show only marginal improvements over HDP-LDA; main takeaway is the models’ differing characteristics rather than a clear performance win for BNBP.
- Reviewer recommends targeted comparisons of the collapsed Gibbs sampler against alternative BNBP inference methods, specifically the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8].
- Rationale: uncollapsed samplers may offer parallelization advantages; comparing mixing properties and computational tradeoffs is important to assess practical utility.
- Experimental section is solid but should include deeper analysis of why BNBP and HDP-LDA differ, e.g., examining learned topic characteristics.
- Overall assessment: interesting, valuable contribution that exceeds publication threshold; EPPF derivation is significant and comparisons to other inference methods would strengthen impact.
