- Summary: Derives an EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process in the mixed-membership setting via a count-mixture construction, gives a prediction rule and fully collapsed Gibbs sampler, and applies them to topic modeling.

- Strength: Clear and technically solid derivation of the BNBP EPPF for grouped data.

- Strength: Conceptual novelty in using count-mixture modeling to obtain an EPPF, potentially useful for other integer-valued processes.

- Strength: Practical inference contribution—a straightforward, truncation-free fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with good convergence and mixing.

- Strength: Empirical comparison to HDP-LDA highlights different shrinkage mechanisms (multiplicative for BNBP vs additive for HDP) and comparable predictive performance with a more compact representation.

- Weakness: Empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal; main takeaway is different inductive biases rather than clear superiority.

- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other BNBP inference schemes (slice sampler and truncated sampler), which would clarify mixing and runtime trade-offs.

- Weakness: Does not explore parallelization trade-offs; uncollapsed representations may be easier to parallelize than the collapsed sampler.

- Weakness: Experimental section could be more comprehensive by including alternative BNBP inference strategies.

- Quality: Technically sound and internally consistent, with well-supported derivations and samplers.

- Clarity: Well written and organized, with clear distinctions from HDP-LDA.

- Originality: Novel derivation of the BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership models and a meaningful collapsed inference scheme.

- Significance: Useful methodological contribution to nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership models, expanding tools for count-based processes.

- Overall assessment: Interesting, well-executed paper that merits acceptance, though broader empirical comparisons (e.g., slice and truncated samplers) would strengthen the work.
