- Paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) based on a novel marginalization (Lemma 1), with elegant derivation and claimed gains in convergence, mixing, and predictive performance.

- Strength: Lemma 1 is an interesting, seemingly correct technical contribution that enables a clean, broadly useful collapsed construction for BNBP models.

- Strength: The sampler could have high practical impact if the claimed mixing and runtime improvements hold, given growing use of BNBP for count data and topic modeling.

- Strength: The derivation and intuition around marginalization are clearly presented.

- Primary concern: Experimental evaluation is insufficient and does not isolate sampler quality, since comparisons are mainly to HDP-LDA (a different model family).

- Needed baseline comparisons: include within-BNBP baselines—uncollapsed/standard Gibbs, partially collapsed/blocked Gibbs, existing slice or other MCMC variants, and where feasible variational baselines.

- Needed metrics: report wall-clock time to reach target held-out likelihood/perplexity, effective sample size per second or integrated autocorrelation time, R-hat across chains, and provide trace plots/summaries.

- Needed experimental controls: hold model, preprocessing, hyperparameters, initialization, iterations/burn-in/thinning, datasets, and compute hardware constant across methods; use same train/validation/test splits.

- Ablations: directly quantify the benefit of full collapsing by comparing to semi-collapsed and uncollapsed variants.

- Scalability and robustness: show results across small to large corpora, sensitivity to hyperparameters and rare words, and provide per-iteration computational/memory complexity and empirical scaling.

- Additional comments: if claiming state-of-the-art predictive performance, compare to best BNBP inference methods (HDP-LDA may remain as secondary reference).

- Additional comments: make assumptions of Lemma 1 explicit (conjugacy/exchangeability), state applicable likelihood families, and discuss extensions to non-conjugate cases.

- Reproducibility requests: provide pseudocode, complexity analysis, and ideally release code/scripts used for experiments.

- Minor clarity fixes: ensure consistent notation across text and appendix, define symbols on first use, and specify how predictive likelihood is estimated (e.g., left-to-right vs document completion).

- Overall evaluation: strong technical novelty and high potential impact, but experimental support is currently insufficient; recommend acceptance conditional on adding within-BNBP baselines, mixing/efficiency metrics, and ablations, otherwise borderline.
