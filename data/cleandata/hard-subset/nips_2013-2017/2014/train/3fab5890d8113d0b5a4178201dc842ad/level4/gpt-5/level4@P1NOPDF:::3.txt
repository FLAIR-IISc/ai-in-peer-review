The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. It is well written, reasonably clear, and technically sound. The primary contribution—the derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling (obtained via the “count-mixture” construction)—is interesting in its own right and offers potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Additional comments:
While the BNBP topic model is a reasonable illustration of the utility of the derived EPPF and the associated prediction rule, the empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal. The most confident conclusion one can draw from the experiments is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics. A more informative experiment would compare the proposed collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations often have natural advantages for parallelization, and it would be useful to examine mixing trade-offs with respect to the collapsed sampler developed here.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. I have only a few minor comments on the experimental section (as noted above).
