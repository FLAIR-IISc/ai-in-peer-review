- Introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to build a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and technically robust.
- Main contribution: deriving the EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership/count-mixture modeling, providing insights useful for deriving EPPFs for related models.
- Empirical gains over HDP-LDA are modest, indicating the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct properties rather than clear superiority.
- Recommends a stronger experimental comparison between the developed collapsed sampler and the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.
- Notes that uncollapsed representations offer parallelization advantages, so assessing mixing tradeoffs versus the collapsed sampler would be valuable.
- Overall assessment: engaging work that meets publication criteria, with only minor suggested improvements to the experimental section.
