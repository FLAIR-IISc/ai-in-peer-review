- Presents the EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and develops a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- Paper is well written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.
- Primary contribution—the BNBP’s EPPF via the count-mixture construction—is interesting and potentially useful for related models.
- BNBP topic model is a reasonable illustration, but empirical improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal.
- Experiments mainly show the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics rather than clear superiority.
- Recommends comparing the proposed collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.
- Notes uncollapsed representations may offer parallelization advantages; suggests examining mixing trade-offs between collapsed and uncollapsed samplers.
- Overall assessment: interesting paper that meets the publication threshold, with only minor experimental comments.
