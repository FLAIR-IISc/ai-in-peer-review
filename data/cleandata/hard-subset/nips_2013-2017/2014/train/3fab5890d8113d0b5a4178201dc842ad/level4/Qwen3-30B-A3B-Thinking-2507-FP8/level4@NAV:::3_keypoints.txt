- Introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and constructs a collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based topic modeling.  
- The paper is clearly written and technically rigorous.  
- Central result: derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via "count-mixture" models.  
- The derivation is theoretically compelling and offers methodological tools for deriving EPPFs in related processes.  
- Empirical finding: BNBP topic model shows utility but only marginal performance gains over HDP-LDA; main clear conclusion is that BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct properties.  
- Suggestion: compare the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.  
- Note that uncollapsed representations support parallelization—worth evaluating their mixing behavior relative to the collapsed sampler.  
- Overall judgment: interesting paper that meets the publication threshold, with only minor experimental suggestions.
