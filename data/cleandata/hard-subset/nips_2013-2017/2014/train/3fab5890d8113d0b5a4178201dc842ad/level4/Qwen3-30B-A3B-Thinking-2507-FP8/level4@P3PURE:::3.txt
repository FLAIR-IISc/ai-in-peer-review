

The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and develops a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model using this EPPF. The paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound. The primary contribution—a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, derived via "count-mixture" models—is both theoretically interesting and provides valuable tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.  

While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration of the EPPF's utility and the corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal. The most confident conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics. A more compelling comparison would involve contrasting the collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations inherently support parallelization, and it would be valuable to examine the mixing properties relative to the collapsed sampler presented here.  

Overall, this is an interesting contribution that meets the publication threshold. Minor comments on the experimental section are provided above.
