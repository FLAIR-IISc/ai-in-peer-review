- Paper introduces a novel fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Bayesian Nonparametric Mixture Model (BNBP).
- The proposed lemma and resulting sampler are an interesting and novel methodological contribution.
- Authors frame the work as a contribution to sampling methodology (simplicity, fast convergence, good mixing, strong predictive performance), not to statistical modeling.
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation does not align with the methodological framing.
- Evaluation focuses on comparisons to HDP-LDA, which is not the appropriate baseline for BNBP sampling claims.
- Paper lacks critical comparisons to existing BNBP sampling methods.
- Comparisons to HDP-LDA do not directly address the claimed improvements in sampling efficiency for BNBP.
- To substantiate claims, the authors should include results from established BNBP samplers and compare convergence/mixing/simplicity directly.
- The theoretical contribution is valuable, but the current experimental setup fails to fully support the stated goals; a more focused BNBP sampler evaluation is necessary.
