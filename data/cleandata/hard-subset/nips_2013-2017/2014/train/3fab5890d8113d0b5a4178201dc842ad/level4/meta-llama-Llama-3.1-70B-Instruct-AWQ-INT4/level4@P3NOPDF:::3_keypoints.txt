- Presents the EPPF for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and develops a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.  
- The paper is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.  
- The main contribution is the derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF via count-mixture models, which is interesting and broadly useful.  
- The theoretical development is clear and constitutes a significant contribution to the field.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited but provides a useful illustration of the model’s performance.  
- Improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal; results mainly show the BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics.  
- Practical implications and concrete advantages of the proposed model over existing approaches remain unclear.  
- Recommends comparing the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.  
- Notes that uncollapsed representations offer natural parallelization advantages; suggests examining mixing tradeoffs with the collapsed sampler.  
- Such comparisons would clarify the proposed method’s strengths, weaknesses, and position in the topic-modeling landscape.  
- Overall assessment: interesting paper that meets publication threshold with minor revisions to experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept (confidence 4).
