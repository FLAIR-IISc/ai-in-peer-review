- Paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), filling a gap in mixed-membership nonparametric Bayesian modeling.  
- Novel derivation technique: randomize group sizes to obtain an exchangeable cluster probability function (ECPF), then condition on observed group sizes via Bayes’ rule to get the EPPF.  
- Presents a practical BNBP topic model and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler enabled by the EPPF.  
- Experiments on three text corpora compare the BNBP model (with collapsed sampler) against HDP-LDA.  
- Empirical findings: the BNBP sampler converges faster, mixes better, and yields more compact topic representations, with competitive or sometimes superior predictive performance.  
- Theoretical derivations (Lemmas 2–4) are clear, technically sound, and complete, convincingly solving the marginal partition distribution problem for BNBP.  
- Writing and organization are strong; the paper clearly motivates the work and explains differences between BNBP and HDP (multiplicative vs. additive topic popularity).  
- Contribution is original and significant: first EPPF for BNBP in mixed-membership setting and a potentially generalizable derivation technique for other processes.  
- The collapsed sampler is practical, elegant, and easier to implement, offering a compelling alternative to HDP samplers.  
- Main experimental weakness: lack of direct comparison between the proposed collapsed sampler and other inference methods for the same BNBP (truncated and slice samplers).  
- Performance claims should be nuanced: HDP-LDA can achieve lower perplexity by using many more topics, so BNBP’s advantage is clearer when compact representations are desired.  
- Paper should discuss parallelization trade-offs (uncollapsed samplers can be easier to parallelize) in the main text to give a balanced practical perspective.  
- Pros summary: strong theoretical contribution, novel technique, useful practical algorithm, clear presentation.  
- Con: limited experimental comparisons to other BNBP inference schemes.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, original work that materially advances Bayesian nonparametrics; recommendation for acceptance.
