- Introduces a Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model using the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and derives its EPPF.
- Fills a literature gap by developing the marginal distribution for BNBP that governs exchangeable partitions of grouped data.
- Novel technique: derives EPPFs for integer-valued stochastic processes via randomization of group sizes.
- Theoretical contributions include an EPPF for BNBP, a prediction rule for exchangeable partitions, and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler.
- Strong technical quality with rigorous mathematical derivations and clear links to prior work (Pitman, HDP, NRMIs).
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, connecting mathematical developments to topic modeling applications.
- High originality with potential to generalize the randomization method to other processes.
- Empirically, the BNBP topic model shows faster convergence, better mixing, and more compact corpus representation than HDP-LDA.
- Practical utility is unclear: the inference algorithm is more complex both theoretically and computationally.
- Improvement in predictive performance (perplexity) over HDP-LDA is marginal when topic counts are comparable.
- Lacks an intuitive explanation for why BNBP should outperform HDP and how multiplicative shrinkage yields practical advantages.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a significant theoretical gap, introduces a novel EPPF derivation method, and provides an efficient collapsed sampler with strong empirical behavior.
- Arguments against acceptance: added algorithmic complexity without compelling practical benefits, marginal gains, and insufficient justification of computational overhead.
- Overall recommendation: substantial and technically sound theoretical contribution that merits acceptance, though more demonstration of practical benefits is needed.
