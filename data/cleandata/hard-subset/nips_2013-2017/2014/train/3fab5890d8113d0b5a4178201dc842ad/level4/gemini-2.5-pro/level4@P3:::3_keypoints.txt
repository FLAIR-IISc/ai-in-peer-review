- Derives the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models by introducing a group-size-dependent ECPF and conditioning to obtain the EPPF.  
- Novel methodological trick (ECPF → EPPF) that could generalize to other integer-valued stochastic processes (e.g., gamma-Poisson).  
- Presents a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out the infinite beta process, topics, and topic weights.  
- Technical derivations are rigorous and well-motivated; the resulting prediction rule is simple and intuitive (multiplicative global × local popularity).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and positions itself effectively relative to prior work.  
- Empirical results on three corpora show faster convergence, better mixing, and competitive predictive performance versus HDP-LDA, often with more compact topic representations.  
- Experimental comparison scope is limited: lacks direct comparisons to other inference schemes for the same BNBP (e.g., truncated Gibbs, slice sampler) to isolate collapsed-sampler benefits and trade-offs like parallelizability.  
- The claim of "state-of-the-art" predictive performance is overstated; results are competitive but not uniformly superior to HDP-LDA and should be toned down.  
- Overall verdict: significant and elegant theoretical contribution with clear practical impact; recommend acceptance.
