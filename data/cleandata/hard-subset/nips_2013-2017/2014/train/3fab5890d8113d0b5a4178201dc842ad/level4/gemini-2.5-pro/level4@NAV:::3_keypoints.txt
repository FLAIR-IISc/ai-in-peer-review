- Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to formulate a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.  
- Manuscript is clear, technically correct, and methodologically rigorous.  
- Core contribution: BNBP EPPF derivation for mixed-membership via a "count-mixture" framework—valuable and potentially a template for other processes.  
- BNBP topic model is a suitable demonstration but experiments show only modest gains over HDP-LDA and mainly illustrate differing model behaviors rather than clear superiority.  
- Suggests more informative experiments comparing the proposed collapsed sampler to alternative BNBP inference methods (sliced sampler [10], truncated sampler [8]).  
- Recommends empirical analysis of trade-offs between mixing efficiency and parallelization for uncollapsed versus collapsed samplers.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper that meets publication threshold; minor revisions should focus on strengthening experimental evaluation.
