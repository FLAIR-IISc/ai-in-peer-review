- Derivation of the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) fills a key gap in mixed-membership modeling.  
- Novel derivation strategy: randomize group sizes to obtain an Exchangeable Cluster Probability Function (ECPF), then apply Bayes' rule to get the group-size-dependent EPPF.  
- From the theoretical result the authors derive a simple predictive rule and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based nonparametric topic model.  
- The collapsed sampler’s update equations are simple and intuitive, combining global and local topic popularities multiplicatively (contrasting HDP’s additive updates).  
- Experiments compare the BNBP model to HDP-LDA, showing faster convergence and better mixing for BNBP but only marginal improvements in predictive perplexity.  
- Main experimental takeaway is that BNBP and HDP exhibit different shrinkage behaviors rather than one model being clearly superior.  
- Paper lacks direct experimental comparison to other BNBP inference schemes (truncated samplers, slice samplers), which would better quantify practical benefits of the collapsed sampler.  
- Discussion of trade-offs (e.g., parallelization advantages of uncollapsed representations) is missing and would strengthen practical claims.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and places contributions clearly in context of prior work.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution (EPPF derivation) merits acceptance.
