- Proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).
- Lemma 1 and the derived sampler are interesting and appear to be significant contributions.
- Primary concern: the experimental evaluation is insufficient.
- Authors claim algorithmic advantages: simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.
- The paper's main contribution is algorithmic (inference), not a new statistical model.
- To validate claimed advantages, direct comparisons to existing BNBP sampling algorithms are required.
- Current experiments compare to HDP-LDA, which conflates sampler performance with model differences and is therefore insufficient.
- Experimental results against other BNBP inference methods are necessary to support the paper's claims.
