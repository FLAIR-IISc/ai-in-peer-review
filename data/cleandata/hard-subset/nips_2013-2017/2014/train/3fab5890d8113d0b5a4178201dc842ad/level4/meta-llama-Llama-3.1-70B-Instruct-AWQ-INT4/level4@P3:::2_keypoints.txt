- Proposes a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the integer-valued beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).
- Derives a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP and builds a nonparametric Bayesian topic model.
- Claims simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.
- Provides clear theoretical background and detailed derivation of the EPPF and sampler.
- Demonstrates the BNBP topic model on several corpora and compares results to the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) topic model.
- Main concern: evaluation emphasizes modeling comparison to HDP rather than assessing the new sampling algorithm itself.
- Reviewer requests comparisons to other BNBP sampling algorithms (e.g., blocked Gibbs, slice sampler) to validate sampler advantages.
- Requests more experimental details (hyperparameter choices, number of iterations) for reproducibility.
- Strength: novel EPPF derivation is an interesting theoretical contribution.
- Strength: fully collapsed Gibbs sampler has practical potential and could be widely used.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is not comprehensive enough to substantiate algorithmic claims.
- Overall: paper is a valuable contribution and potentially NIPS-worthy but requires revisions addressing experimental comparisons and setup details.
