The manuscript derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model. This is a well-written, fairly clear, and technically solid paper. The main contribution—the derivation of the BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via a count-mixture construction—is interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful techniques for obtaining EPPFs for related models.

Additional Comments:
While the BNBP topic model suitably demonstrates the utility of the proposed EPPF and associated prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are modest; the most robust conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have different characteristics. A more informative experiment would compare the collapsed sampler against the slice-based sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for BNBP topic-model inference. Because uncollapsed formulations are naturally advantageous for parallelization, it would be helpful to examine mixing trade-offs relative to the collapsed sampler presented here. Overall, this is an interesting submission that meets the acceptance threshold. I have only minor comments regarding the experimental section (see above).
