

The paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and leverages it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based topic modeling. This is a clearly written, technically sound contribution. The primary result—the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, achieved through count-mixture models—is both theoretically interesting and provides valuable tools for deriving EPPFs in related models.

The BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration of the EPPF's utility and its associated prediction rule. However, the improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal, and the only clear conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct modeling properties. A more compelling experiment would directly compare the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler from [10] and the truncated sampler from [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations offer inherent advantages for parallelization, making it worthwhile to analyze the mixing tradeoffs between these approaches and the collapsed sampler presented here.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. I have only minor suggestions for the experimental section (detailed above).
