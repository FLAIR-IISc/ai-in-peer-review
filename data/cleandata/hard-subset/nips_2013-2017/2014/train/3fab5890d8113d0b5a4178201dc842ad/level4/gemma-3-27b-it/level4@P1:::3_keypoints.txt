- Paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to build a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- The manuscript is well-written, clear, and technically sound.
- The primary theoretical contribution (BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership/count-mixture models) is novel, interesting, and fills a gap in marginal distributions for integer-valued stochastic processes.
- The derived EPPF/prediction rule could be useful for deriving EPPFs for related models.
- The BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration but shows only marginal improvements over HDP-LDA.
- The authors should compare the collapsed sampler to existing BNBP inference methods (slice sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8]) to assess mixing and parallelization trade-offs.
- Uncollapsed representations may offer parallelization advantages; examining mixing trade-offs between collapsed and uncollapsed samplers would be insightful.
- Experiments show faster convergence and better mixing of the BNBP sampler versus HDP-LDA, but predictive gains are sensitive to the Dirichlet smoothing parameter η.
- The evaluation would be strengthened by guidance on tuning η in practice or a more robust parameter-selection comparison with HDP-LDA.
- Overall assessment: interesting paper that meets the publication threshold, with only minor experimental-section comments.
