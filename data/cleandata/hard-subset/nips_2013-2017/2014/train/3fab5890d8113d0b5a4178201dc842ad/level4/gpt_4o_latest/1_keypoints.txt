1. Introduces a novel Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model for grouped data built on the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).  
2. Derives an EPPF and prediction rules, connecting BNBP to exchangeable partition probability functions.  
3. Establishes a significant bridge between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPF theory, a relatively underused connection in BNP research.  
4. Proposes randomizing group sizes (rather than treating them as fixed), enabling derivation of new marginal distributions.  
5. Makes meaningful theoretical contributions overall.  
6. Practical utility is unclear: the inference algorithm is more complex than HDP in formulation and implementation.  
7. Empirical results do not convincingly show substantial practical benefits over HDP.  
8. Paper lacks an intuitive explanation or justification for expected real-world improvement over HDP.  
9. Reviewer recommends adding a clearer rationale for practical performance gains compared to HDP.  
10. Overall recommendation: inclined to accept.
