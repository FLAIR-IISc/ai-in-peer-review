This paper proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The introduction of Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler is an interesting theoretical contribution.

However, my primary concern lies with the experimental evaluation. The authors' stated motivation for this work is to develop a sampler for the BNBP that offers simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. This framing establishes that the paper's core contribution is algorithmic—a new inference method for an existing model—rather than the development of a new statistical model.

Given this focus, the experimental validation should directly support these algorithmic claims. Consequently, the most appropriate and necessary comparison would be against other existing sampling algorithms for the BNBP. Instead, the authors have chosen to compare their method with HDP-LDA. While this may provide some context, it does not allow for a direct assessment of the new sampler's performance relative to the state-of-the-art for the BNBP model. To substantiate claims of faster convergence, better mixing, or superior performance, a direct benchmark against alternative BNBP inference schemes is required.

In summary, while the paper provides a novel sampler, the experimental results are insufficient. The work would be significantly strengthened by including a comparative analysis with other sampling algorithms specifically designed for the Beta Negative Binomial Process.
