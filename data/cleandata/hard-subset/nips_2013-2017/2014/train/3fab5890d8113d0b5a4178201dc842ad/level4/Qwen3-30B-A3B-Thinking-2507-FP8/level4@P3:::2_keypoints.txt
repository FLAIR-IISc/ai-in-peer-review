- Paper derives the ECPF and EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP), enabling inference without truncating the number of atoms.  
- The authors develop a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based mixed-membership models that is simple to implement.  
- Lemma 4 (prediction rule) is a useful practical contribution for implementing the model.  
- Theoretical justification highlights BNBP's multiplicative topic-shrinkage mechanism vs. HDP-LDA's additive mechanism.  
- Novel extension: generalizes Zhou et al. (2014) and extends prior EPPF work to mixed-membership modeling.  
- Empirical claims (fast convergence, good mixing, state-of-the-art predictive performance) are insufficiently validated.  
- Major experimental flaw: comparisons are made to HDP-LDA instead of alternative BNBP inference methods (e.g., finite truncation [10], slice sampling [11]).  
- Because experiments compare different models rather than inference methods, it's unclear whether gains come from the BNBP model or the new sampler.  
- Figure 3 showing comparable perplexity with fewer topics does not address superiority of the proposed inference algorithm.  
- Paper’s significance is weakened by lack of fair comparison against other BNBP inference approaches.  
- Clarity and exposition are good, with clear mathematical derivations and structured presentation.  
- Paper would benefit from more intuition on why BNBP is especially suited for mixed-membership vs. other integer-valued processes (e.g., gamma-Poisson).  
- Need stronger discussion connecting to related work (negative binomial IBP [16], “ice cream” buffet [17]).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution but recommend rejection due to fundamental experimental evaluation flaw; revise to include direct comparisons to other BNBP inference methods.
