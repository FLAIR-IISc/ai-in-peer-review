This paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and demonstrates its application in a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

The paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound. Its core contribution – a derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF within the context of mixed-membership modeling, achieved through a “count-mixture” framework – is both novel and potentially valuable. This result offers a useful foundation for deriving EPPFs for similar models, extending beyond the specific application presented. 

The BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable demonstration of the EPPF’s utility and the associated prediction rule. However, the observed improvements over existing hierarchical Dirichlet process latent Dirichlet allocation (HDP-LDA) models are modest. Consequently, the primary conclusion appears to be that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct behaviors rather than a clear performance advantage for either. 

To further strengthen the analysis, a comparison of the proposed collapsed Gibbs sampler against alternative inference methods for the BNBP topic model would be beneficial. Specifically, contrasting its performance with the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] would provide valuable insights. Furthermore, given the advantages of uncollapsed representations regarding parallelization, investigating the mixing properties and computational tradeoffs relative to these alternative approaches would be a worthwhile extension. A discussion of these tradeoffs in the context of large datasets could be particularly impactful. 

Overall, this is an interesting and worthwhile contribution that meets the standard for publication. The experimental section could be strengthened by the aforementioned comparisons, but these suggestions represent minor points for improvement.
