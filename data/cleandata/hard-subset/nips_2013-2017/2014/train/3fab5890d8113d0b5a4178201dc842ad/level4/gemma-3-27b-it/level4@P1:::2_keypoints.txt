- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) with a novel EPPF derivation.  
- Derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF and resulting sampler are novel and significant for nonparametric Bayesian methods.  
- Authors demonstrate strong familiarity with related literature (Dirichlet processes, NRMI, mixed-membership models).  
- Paper provides a unified treatment for mixed-membership modeling analogous to EPPF models for single datasets.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and well-connected to exchangeable partition theory and NRMIs.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized; derivations are relatively easy to follow despite complexity.  
- A fully collapsed Gibbs sampler has potential for implementation simplicity, faster convergence, better mixing, and efficiency gains.  
- Primary experimental concern: comparisons are made to HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP-specific samplers.  
- Lacks baseline comparisons to existing BNBP inference methods (e.g., finite truncation methods, slice sampling).  
- Evaluation relies solely on perplexity and should include additional metrics (e.g., topic coherence, qualitative analyses).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound EPPF derivation and a promising fully collapsed sampler.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental validation, missing comparisons to alternative BNBP samplers, and limited evaluation metrics.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but experimental evaluation must be strengthened; recommend borderline accept contingent on adding comparisons with existing BNBP samplers.
