This paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and applies this result to formulate a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.

The manuscript is commendable for its clarity, technical correctness, and rigorous methodology. The paper's core contribution—the derivation of the BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership modeling through "count-mixture" frameworks—is a valuable finding in its own right. Furthermore, the approach taken may provide a useful template for deriving EPPFs for similar stochastic processes.

Additional Comments:
The BNBP topic model serves as a suitable application to demonstrate the utility of the derived EPPF and its corresponding predictive distribution. However, the experimental comparison reveals only modest performance gains over HDP-LDA. The current results primarily highlight the differing behaviors of the two models rather than establishing a clear superiority of the BNBP topic model.

A more insightful experimental analysis would contrast the performance of the proposed collapsed sampler with alternative inference techniques for the BNBP topic model, namely the sliced sampler presented in [10] and the truncated sampler from [8]. Given that uncollapsed samplers often offer benefits for parallel implementation, an empirical investigation into the trade-offs in mixing efficiency versus the collapsed sampler developed here would be highly valuable.

Overall, this is a strong paper that meets the threshold for publication. My recommendations are minor and focus on strengthening the experimental evaluation as noted above.
