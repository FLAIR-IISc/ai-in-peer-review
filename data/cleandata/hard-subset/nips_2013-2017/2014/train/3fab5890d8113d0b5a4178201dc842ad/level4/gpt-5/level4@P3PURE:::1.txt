This paper introduces a new Bayesian nonparametric model for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data, built on the Beta–Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Although prior work has employed the BNBP, this paper makes several notable advances.

First, it derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the model and develops the associated prediction rules. The connection between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPFs has been studied extensively in statistics since Pitman’s seminal work, but has not been widely leveraged in applied BNP research. The explicit link this paper establishes between the BNBP and an EPPF is, in my view, an important step toward unifying EPPF-based analysis with BNP modeling and may encourage further work along this direction.

Second, the paper proposes randomizing the group sizes, which are typically treated as fixed constants in most prior work. This modeling choice is interesting and, importantly, enables the derivation of certain marginal distributions.

Overall, I view the theoretical contributions as significant. However, the practical utility remains uncertain. The inference algorithm appears more complex than that of the HDP, both conceptually and algorithmically. The empirical improvements reported are not sufficient to convince me that the added complexity is justified in practice. In addition, the paper lacks an intuitive explanation or justification for why the proposed framework should outperform the HDP in real applications.

I encourage the authors to clarify how and why this approach could deliver practical gains over the HDP—beyond the theoretical appeal—and to provide intuition for the mechanisms that yield improved performance.

Recommendation: Leaning toward acceptance for its theoretical contributions, with reservations about practical impact.
