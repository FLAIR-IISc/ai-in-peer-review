This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Bayesian Nonparametric Mixture Model with Indian Process Prior (BNBP). Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler are novel and interesting contributions.

However, my primary concern lies with the experimental evaluation. The motivation for developing this CG sampler for the BNBP centers on its simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing properties, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. This framing clearly positions the work as focused on the *sampling algorithm* itself, rather than on statistical modeling advancements. Consequently, a compelling evaluation requires comparison against other existing sampling algorithms specifically designed for the BNBP – not against the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process Latent Dirichlet Allocation (HDP-LDA) model. 

A fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP has been presented, but the experimental results of alternative sampling algorithms applied to the BNBP are crucial for establishing the merits of the proposed method. Providing such a comparative analysis would significantly strengthen the paper’s evaluation and demonstrate the advantages claimed for the CG sampler.
