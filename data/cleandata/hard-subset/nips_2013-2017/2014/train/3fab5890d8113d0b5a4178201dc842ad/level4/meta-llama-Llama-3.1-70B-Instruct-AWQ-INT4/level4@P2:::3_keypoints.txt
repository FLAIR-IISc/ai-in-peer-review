- Derivation of the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) is the paper's primary, novel contribution.  
- Authors use the BNBP EPPF to develop a prediction rule for simulating exchangeable random partitions of grouped data.  
- A fully collapsed Gibbs sampler is derived that marginalizes topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process.  
- The paper proposes a new BNBP-based nonparametric Bayesian topic model distinct from existing models.  
- The proposed sampler is straightforward to implement, converges quickly, mixes well, and yields state-of-the-art predictive performance.  
- Experiments on JACM, PsyReview, and NIPS123 show the BNBP model matches HDP-LDA predictive power with a more compact corpus representation.  
- Theoretical analysis thoroughly investigates the BNBP law governing exchangeable random partitions for grouped data.  
- The paper is technically sound, well written, and clearly explains background and technical details.  
- The derivation of the BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership modeling is original and significant for nonparametric Bayesian modeling.  
- Results have important implications for deriving EPPFs and building related nonparametric Bayesian models.  
- Improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal, so the main confident conclusion is that BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics.  
- Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive; missing comparisons to other samplers (e.g., sliced and truncated samplers) and exploration of parallelization/mixing tradeoffs.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, publishable work that meets the publication threshold with only minor experimental concerns.
