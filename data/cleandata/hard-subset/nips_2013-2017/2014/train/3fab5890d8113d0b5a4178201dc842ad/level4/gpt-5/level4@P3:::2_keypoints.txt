- Paper derives an explicit ECPF and a group-size–dependent EPPF for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models via beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and Poissonization.  
- Two-stage approach: marginalize beta process with random group sizes to get ECPF, then condition on fixed sizes to obtain the EPPF and a simple prediction rule.  
- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the beta process, topic parameters, and group-specific weights, enabling a nonparametric topic model distinct from HDP-LDA.  
- Empirical comparison to HDP-LDA on several corpora shows faster convergence, good mixing, and competitive or better perplexity at comparable topic counts.  
- Novelty: first explicit, tractable EPPF and prediction rule for BNBP in mixed-membership settings, allowing a truncation-free collapsed sampler.  
- Strength — technical: clean derivation of ECPF/EPPF and intuitive prediction rule separating global vs. local popularity.  
- Strength — inference: collapsed sampler yields fast mixing and reduced bookkeeping compared to HDP’s Chinese restaurant franchise.  
- Strength — conceptual: clarifies how integer-valued processes induce exchangeable partitions, linking count modeling and mixed-membership clustering.  
- Strength — practical: topic-model instantiation is well-motivated, computationally efficient, and appears easy to implement.  
- Main empirical weakness: lacks direct comparisons to other BNBP inference schemes (truncated/adaptive truncation, slice samplers); should report wall-clock, iterations to convergence, ESS, and perplexity vs. time.  
- Needs ablation/sensitivity studies to isolate the effect of full collapse vs. partial collapse and to assess sensitivity to c, γ0, and priors on rj (impact on overdispersion and predictive performance).  
- Evaluation should be broadened beyond perplexity (e.g., topic coherence, sparsity/compactness trade-offs, calibration of held-out likelihoods) and include perplexity vs. topic count and runtime plots.  
- Presentation issues: explain practical implications of sample-size–dependent EPPF, define/justify the “digamma distribution” intuitively, and streamline notation with supportive appendix.  
- Quality: mathematical development appears sound and the prediction rule supports the collapsed sampler, but empirical validation doesn’t fully substantiate algorithmic claims over other BNBP methods.  
- Originality: high for theory/inference; first explicit BNBP EPPF and collapsed sampler for mixed-membership models.  
- Significance: fills a gap by providing a principled, truncation-free collapsed sampler for BNBP and suggests extensions to other count processes.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, technically solid EPPF derivation; simple, useful collapsed sampler; clear link between count processes and exchangeable partitions with promising empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing comparisons to alternative BNBP inference algorithms, limited ablations/sensitivity analyses, and dense exposition needing clarity improvements.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and algorithmic contribution likely of interest to the community, but acceptance should be contingent on added empirical comparisons to other BNBP inference methods and modest clarity revisions.
