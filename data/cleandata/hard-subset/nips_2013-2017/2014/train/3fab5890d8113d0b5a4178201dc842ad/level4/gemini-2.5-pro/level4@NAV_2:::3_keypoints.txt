- Main contribution: Derives the EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models by introducing an auxiliary random-group-size model to get an ECPF, then applying Bayes’ rule for fixed group sizes.  
- Application: Uses the EPPF to build a BNBP topic model and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out the infinite beta process.  
- Originality/significance: Fills a notable gap in nonparametric Bayesian literature; the ECPF trick is clever and potentially generalizable to other integer-valued processes.  
- Technical quality: Mathematical derivations (Lemma 2–4) and the resulting predictive rule are sound and well-presented.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is well-written, motivated, and situates the work within prior EPPF/HDP literature.  
- Insight: Compares sampler update equations to LDA and HDP-LDA, highlighting distinct multiplicative vs. additive shrinkage behaviors.  
- Experimental weakness: Empirical comparisons are limited—shows comparable perplexity and more compact representation but not clear superiority of the model.  
- Missing experiments: Lacks direct comparison of the new fully collapsed sampler to existing BNBP inference methods (truncated Gibbs or slice samplers) to demonstrate mixing, convergence, or efficiency gains.  
- Practical trade-offs: Mentions parallelization benefits of uncollapsed samplers but provides no quantitative analysis of mixing vs. computational trade-offs.  
- Overall verdict: Strong theoretical contribution and solid technical work; acceptance recommended but experiments should better quantify practical benefits of the new inference scheme.
