- Proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model using the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) with derived EPPF and prediction rules, unifying EPPF analysis and BNP research.  
- Introduces randomizing group sizes (rather than treating them as constants), enabling derivation of marginal distributions.  
- Provides substantial theoretical insights into BNBP and its application to mixed-membership modeling.  
- Inference algorithm is more complex than the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP) in both theory and implementation.  
- Empirical improvements over HDP are modest and may not justify the increased complexity.  
- Lacks an intuitive explanation for why the proposed framework should outperform HDP in practice.  
- Recommends clearer justification of practical benefits and more detailed empirical comparisons with HDP and related models.  
- Paper is technically sound with thorough derivations of EPPF and prediction rules.  
- Main concerns are inference complexity and absence of intuitive/empirical justification for practical gains.  
- Paper is well-organized and clear overall, though technical derivation sections may be challenging for non-experts.  
- Original contributions include the novel BNBP–EPPF connection and the idea of randomizing group sizes.  
- Significance is high theoretically, but practical impact remains uncertain.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and significant theoretical contribution, valuable EPPF connection, thorough derivations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: uncertain practical utility, higher complexity than HDP, and lack of intuitive rationale.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with encouragement to address practical justification and comparative evaluation.
