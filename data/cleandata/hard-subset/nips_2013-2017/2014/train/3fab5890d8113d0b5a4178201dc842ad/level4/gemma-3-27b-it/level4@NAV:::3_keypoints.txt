- Introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to build a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.  
- Presentation is clear and technically sound; main contribution is the EPPF derivation via a “count-mixture” framework.  
- The EPPF result is valuable and could enable deriving EPPFs for related models.  
- BNBP topic model sensibly demonstrates the EPPF and predictive distribution, but shows limited performance gains over HDP-LDA; differences are characteristic rather than a clear superiority.  
- Recommends comparative experiments of the collapsed sampler against existing BNBP inference methods (sliced sampler [10], truncated sampler [8]).  
- Suggests studying mixing properties and parallelization trade-offs between the collapsed sampler and uncollapsed representations.  
- Overall assessment: worthwhile contribution appropriate for publication; main concern is improving the experimental evaluation.
