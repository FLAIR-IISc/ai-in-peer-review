- Introduces an EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) for grouped (mixed-membership) data, filling a significant theoretical gap.
- Derives the BNBP's EPPF via a group-size dependent approach, enabling fully collapsed Gibbs sampling for BNBP topic models.
- Provides rigorous, well-supported mathematical derivations and prediction rules with detailed proofs.
- Establishes closed-form Gibbs update equations that marginalize out topics and the infinite-dimensional beta process.
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and contrasts the approach effectively with prior methods like HDP and NB-IBP.
- Demonstrates strong originality by deriving marginal distributions for exchangeable partitions of grouped data and a novel multiplicative shrinkage mechanism.
- Shows practical significance with state-of-the-art predictive performance and a compact corpus representation using fewer topics on standard corpora.
- Empirically the sampler converges faster and mixes better than HDP-LDA, simplifying implementation and improving convergence speed.
- Main experimental weakness: marginal perplexity improvements and insufficient comparisons to other BNBP inference methods (sliced and truncated samplers).
- Needs more exploration of tradeoffs between the collapsed sampler and uncollapsed representations, particularly regarding parallelization.
- Overall, a valuable theoretical and practical contribution to nonparametric Bayesian mixed-membership modeling and a strong candidate for publication.
