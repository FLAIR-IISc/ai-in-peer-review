

The paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and employs it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.  
This is a well-written, clear, and technically sound paper. The primary contribution—a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling, derived using "count-mixture" models—is both interesting in its own right and provides useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.  
Additional Comments:  
While the BNBP topic model reasonably demonstrates the utility of the developed EPPF and prediction rule, improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal. The most defensible conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics. A more compelling experiment would compare the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Uncollapsed representations offer natural advantages for parallelization, and it would be valuable to examine mixing trade-offs relative to the collapsed sampler presented here. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. Minor comments on the experimental section are noted above.
