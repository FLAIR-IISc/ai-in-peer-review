- Introduces a Bayesian nonparametric mixed-membership model for grouped data based on the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP).  
- Main contribution 1: derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP and corresponding prediction rules.  
- Main contribution 2: randomizing group sizes to obtain marginal distributions that were previously inaccessible.  
- Theoretical significance: connects BNBP to EPPF analysis, potentially unifying EPPF-based theory and practical BNP modeling.  
- Analytical benefit: randomizing group sizes is a clever device with clear analytical advantages.  
- Practical concern: proposed inference algorithm is conceptually and computationally more complex than HDP inference.  
- Empirical concern: reported empirical improvements are not compelling enough to justify added complexity.  
- Missing intuition: paper lacks an intuitive explanation for when and why the BNBP framework should outperform the HDP in real applications.  
- Request for clarification: explain at a high level why the BNBP’s EPPF and prediction rule should yield better practical performance than the HDP.  
- Request for clarification: clarify computational trade-offs relative to standard HDP inference, both theoretically and in wall-clock time, and relate to empirical gains.  
- Overall judgment: clear theoretical value but uncertain practical benefits.  
- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward acceptance despite reservations about practical utility.
