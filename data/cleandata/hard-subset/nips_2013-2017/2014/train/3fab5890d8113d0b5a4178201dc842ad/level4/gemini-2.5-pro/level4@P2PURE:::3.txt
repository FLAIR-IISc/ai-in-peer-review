This paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses this result to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

The paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound. Its primary contribution—a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via "count-mixture" models—is significant in its own right. Furthermore, the methodology presented offers a potentially useful framework for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Additional Comments:

The BNBP topic model is a reasonable application to demonstrate the utility of the derived EPPF and its corresponding prediction rule. However, the experimental comparison with HDP-LDA shows only marginal improvements. The only confident conclusion from this experiment is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics, rather than a clear advantage for one over the other.

A more compelling evaluation would contrast the proposed collapsed sampler against existing uncollapsed inference methods for the BNBP topic model, such as the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8]. Since uncollapsed representations have inherent advantages for parallelization, it would be valuable to analyze the trade-offs in mixing efficiency between those approaches and the collapsed sampler developed here.

Overall, this is an interesting and valuable paper that meets the threshold for publication. My comments are minor and pertain only to the experimental section as noted above.
