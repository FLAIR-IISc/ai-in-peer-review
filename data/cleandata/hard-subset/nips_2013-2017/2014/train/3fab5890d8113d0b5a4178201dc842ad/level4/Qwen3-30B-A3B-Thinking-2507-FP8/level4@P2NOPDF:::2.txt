

The paper introduces a novel fully collapsed Gibbs (FCG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), with Lemma 1 and the derived algorithm presenting theoretically interesting contributions. However, the experimental evaluation significantly undermines the paper’s core claims.  

The authors position the FCG sampler as superior due to its simplicity, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance within BNBP applications. Crucially, the work centers on *algorithmic efficiency* for the BNBP, not statistical modeling innovations. Consequently, the evaluation must compare the proposed FCG sampler against *other BNBP-specific sampling methods* (e.g., standard Gibbs, partially collapsed variants, or existing BNBP samplers). Instead, the paper compares against HDP-LDA—a distinct model framework—which is methodologically invalid for assessing the BNBP sampling algorithm’s merits.  

This comparison fails to address the paper’s stated objectives. HDP-LDA involves a different hierarchical structure and inference problem, making it irrelevant for evaluating the FCG sampler’s performance *as a BNBP-specific algorithm*. Without results from competing BNBP samplers (e.g., those in [cite key BNBP papers]), the claims about "fast convergence," "good mixing," and "state-of-the-art predictive performance" remain unsubstantiated. The authors must include these comparisons to validate their algorithmic claims.  

Additionally, the experimental setup lacks critical details: convergence diagnostics (e.g., effective sample sizes, autocorrelation plots), runtime comparisons, and sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters. Without these, it is impossible to assess the claimed "fast convergence" or "good mixing." The results section should explicitly benchmark the FCG sampler against established BNBP inference methods using identical datasets and evaluation protocols.  

In summary, while the theoretical contribution (Lemma 1, FCG sampler) is promising, the experimental section is deficient. The authors must:  
1) Replace HDP-LDA comparisons with direct benchmarks against other BNBP samplers,  
2) Provide convergence diagnostics and runtime analysis,  
3) Clarify the predictive performance claims within the BNBP context.  

Without these revisions, the empirical support for the paper’s primary contributions is inadequate. The current evaluation misaligns with the work’s focus, rendering the results inconclusive for the intended audience.
