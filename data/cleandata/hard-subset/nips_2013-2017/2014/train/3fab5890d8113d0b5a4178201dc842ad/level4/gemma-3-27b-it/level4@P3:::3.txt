This paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. This work addresses a key limitation of existing BNBP inference methods, which often rely on truncation, by providing a fully collapsed sampler grounded in a novel derivation of the EPPF.

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The core contribution – the derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via a “count-mixture” approach – is both interesting and potentially valuable. This provides a systematic method for deriving EPPFs for related models and contributes to a more unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling, bridging a gap between work on single datasets and grouped data. The authors appropriately connect their work to existing nonparametric Bayesian literature, including the Dirichlet process, Chinese restaurant process, and hierarchical Dirichlet process. 

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The derivation of the BNBP's EPPF is a significant theoretical advance, offering a foundation for more efficient and accurate inference.
* **Collapsed Gibbs Sampler:** The development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler is a practical and valuable contribution, avoiding the computational limitations of truncation-based methods.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally clearly written, with a logical flow of ideas and adequate mathematical detail.
* **Novelty:** The approach of deriving the EPPF through a group-size dependent model is innovative and expands the toolkit for nonparametric Bayesian modeling.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Empirical Gains:** While the BNBP topic model demonstrates the utility of the EPPF, the reported improvements over the HDP-LDA are marginal. The authors rightly acknowledge this and suggest the models have distinct characteristics, but a more substantial empirical demonstration of benefit would strengthen the paper.
* **Limited Comparison:** The experimental evaluation primarily focuses on comparison to HDP-LDA. A comparison to other BNBP inference methods, such as the sliced sampler [10] and truncated samplers [7], would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the proposed method’s advantages and disadvantages.  Specifically, examining the speed and mixing properties relative to these alternative BNBP inference approaches would be insightful.
* **Parallelization:** The authors mention the benefits of uncollapsed representations for parallelization. Discussing the potential for parallelizing the proposed collapsed sampler, or outlining the challenges involved, would be valuable.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The sensitivity of the results to the choice of hyperparameters (η in the topic model) is acknowledged, but a more in-depth analysis of hyperparameter tuning strategies could be included.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
* The derivation of the BNBP EPPF and the resulting collapsed Gibbs sampler address a significant limitation of existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
* The empirical results, while not overwhelmingly superior, demonstrate the viability and competitiveness of the proposed approach.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical improvements over existing methods, particularly HDP-LDA, are relatively modest.
* The comparison to alternative BNBP inference methods is limited.
* The discussion of parallelization is brief and could be expanded.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the literature on nonparametric Bayesian modeling. While the empirical gains may not be dramatic, the theoretical novelty and practical utility of the proposed method are significant. The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and addresses an important problem in the field.  I believe this paper exceeds the publication threshold for NIPS.



**Minor Comments:**

* In the introduction, consider briefly motivating *why* a unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling is desirable beyond simply stating that it's a gap.
* In Section 3.1, expanding on the specific differences in shrinkage mechanisms between the BNBP and HDP-LDA would be insightful.
* The reference formatting seems slightly inconsistent (e.g., some have full details, others don't). Please ensure consistency.
