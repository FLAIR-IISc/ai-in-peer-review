The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. This is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The primary result of the paper - a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling (arrived at via "count-mixture" models) - is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

The paper starts by providing a clear summary of the main ideas and their relationship to previous work. The authors then discuss the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, addressing the criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The technical arguments and criticisms are well-presented, and the core meaning is preserved throughout the paper.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling using the BNBP. The authors derive an exchangeable partition probability function to describe how the BNBP clusters data points into exchangeable partitions, which are shared across all groups. This is a significant contribution, as it provides a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model that is distinct from existing ones.

The paper also presents a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model, which is a significant improvement over previous methods. The sampler is shown to converge fast, mix well, and produce state-of-the-art predictive performance with a compact representation of the corpus.

The experimental section is well-presented, and the results are clearly discussed. However, it would be more interesting to contrast the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. The uncollapsed representations have natural advantages when it comes to parallelization, and it would be useful to examine mixing tradeoffs with respect to the collapsed sampler developed here.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that passes the publication threshold. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling, and their work is likely to be of interest to researchers in the field.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling.
* The authors provide a unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling using the BNBP.
* The paper presents a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model, which is a significant improvement over previous methods.
* The experimental section is well-presented, and the results are clearly discussed.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The improvements over the HDP-LDA are only marginal.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other samplers, such as the sliced sampler and the truncated sampler.
* The experimental section could be improved by including more detailed results and analysis.
