This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The authors derive a novel lemma, which they use to develop an interesting sampler. The theoretical contribution of this work is clear, and the proposed sampler has the potential to be a valuable tool in the field.

However, my primary concern lies in the experimental evaluation. The authors motivate their work by highlighting the benefits of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, including its simplicity, fast convergence, good mixing properties, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. Given this focus on the sampling algorithm itself, rather than statistical modeling, I believe it is essential to compare the proposed algorithm to other sampling algorithms for the BNBP.

The current experimental setup, which compares the proposed sampler to HDP-LDA, does not adequately address the authors' stated goals. To demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed sampler, the authors should compare its performance to other sampling algorithms specifically designed for the BNBP. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed sampler's strengths and weaknesses.

Furthermore, including experimental results for other sampling algorithms for the BNBP would significantly strengthen the paper. This would enable a more nuanced evaluation of the proposed sampler's convergence, mixing properties, and predictive performance, and would provide valuable insights for practitioners working with the BNBP.

Overall, while the theoretical contribution of this work is promising, the experimental evaluation falls short of adequately demonstrating the proposed sampler's benefits. I encourage the authors to revisit their experimental setup and provide a more comprehensive comparison to other BNBP sampling algorithms.
