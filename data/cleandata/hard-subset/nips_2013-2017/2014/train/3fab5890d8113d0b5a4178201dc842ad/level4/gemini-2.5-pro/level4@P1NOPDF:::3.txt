This paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.

This is a well-written, clear, and technically sound paper. The primary contribution—the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via a "count-mixture" framework—is both interesting in its own right and provides a valuable tool for deriving EPPFs for related models.

**Additional Comments:**

While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable application to demonstrate the utility of the derived EPPF and prediction rule, the reported improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal. The experiments effectively show that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics, but they do not establish a clear advantage for the new model.

A more insightful experiment would have been to contrast the proposed collapsed sampler with existing uncollapsed inference techniques for the BNBP topic model, such as the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8]. Given that uncollapsed representations offer natural advantages for parallelization, it would be particularly useful to examine the trade-offs in mixing speed and efficiency with respect to the collapsed sampler developed here.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the threshold for publication. My only suggestions for improvement relate to the experimental section as noted above.
