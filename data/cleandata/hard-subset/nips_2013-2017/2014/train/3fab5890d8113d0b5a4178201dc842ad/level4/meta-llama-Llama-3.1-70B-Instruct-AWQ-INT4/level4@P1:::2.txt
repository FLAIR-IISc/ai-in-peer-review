The main idea of this paper is to develop a group size dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the integer-valued beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, leading to a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model that is distinct from existing ones. The proposed model has simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.

The paper starts by introducing the BNBP and its application to mixed-membership modeling. The authors then derive the EPPF for the BNBP, which describes the prior distribution of a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix. They also develop a prediction rule for the BNBP to simulate exchangeable random partitions of grouped data governed by its EPPF.

The authors apply the BNBP to topic modeling of a document corpus, a special case of mixture modeling of grouped data. They construct a nonparametric Bayesian fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model and compare its performance with that of the HDP-LDA.

The paper makes several contributions, including the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP, the development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model, and the application of the BNBP to topic modeling.

The strengths of the paper include its novel contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling, its simple and intuitive model formulation, and its state-of-the-art predictive performance. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the technical details.

However, my concern is the experiments. The paper's motivation is to construct a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP because of its simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. That is, this work focuses on the sampling algorithm, not statistical modeling. Therefore, the authors should compare other sampling algorithms for the BNBP with the proposed algorithm, not HDP-LDA.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper makes a novel contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
* The proposed model has simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the technical details.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The experiments do not compare the proposed algorithm with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP.
* The comparison with HDP-LDA is not relevant to the paper's motivation and contribution.

To improve the paper, the authors should provide experimental results comparing the proposed algorithm with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP. This would strengthen the paper's contribution and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm.
