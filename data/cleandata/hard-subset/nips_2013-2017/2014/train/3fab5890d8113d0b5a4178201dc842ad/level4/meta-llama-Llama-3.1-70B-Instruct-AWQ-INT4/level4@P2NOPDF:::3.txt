This paper presents a derivation of the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model. The paper is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.

The primary result of the paper, a derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via "count-mixture" models, is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Additional Comments:
While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration of the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over the HDP-LDA are only marginal. This makes it difficult to draw any confident conclusions beyond the fact that the BNBP topic model and the HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics. A more interesting experiment would be to contrast the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. This comparison would be valuable, as the uncollapsed representations have natural advantages when it comes to parallelization, and it would be useful to examine the tradeoffs in mixing with respect to the collapsed sampler developed here.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that passes the publication threshold. I only have a few minor comments on the experimental section, which are noted above.
