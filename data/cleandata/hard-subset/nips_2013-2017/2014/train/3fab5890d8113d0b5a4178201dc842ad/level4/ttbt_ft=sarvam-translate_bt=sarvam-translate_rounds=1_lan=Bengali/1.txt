This paper presents a new Bayesian nonparametric model for modeling mixed-membership data in a group setting. This model is based on a Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Although this stochastic process has been used in previous research, as mentioned in this paper, it has several aspects that distinguish this work.

Firstly, this work achieves an EPPF (Exchangeable Partition Probability Function) and creates a prediction rule based on it. The relationship between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPF in statistics has been studied for some time, following the seminal work of J. Pitman. However, this concept has not been widely used in BNP research practice. The connection established between BNBP and EPPF in this paper, in my opinion, is an important step towards integrating EPPF analysis and BNP research, which will likely help further work on this subject.

Secondly, the concept of randomization of the group size (which is considered a given constant in most other works) is interesting. It appears that this concept makes it possible to create some marginal distributions.

I believe this paper has important theoretical contributions. However, the practical applicability of this work is questionable. The algorithm for the estimate is clearly more complex than HDP (both in theory and in the algorithm). The improvement shown in the results is not enough to convince me that this increased complexity is actually worthwhile. Also, the paper does not provide any intuitive explanation (or justification) as to why the proposed framework would actually work better than HDP.

I believe this paper has important theoretical value. However, practical applicability is still unclear. I hope the authors can clarify how this method can improve practical performance compared to HDP and why. Overall, I am still leaning towards adopting it.
