The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and utilizes it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

This is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The primary result – the derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling (achieved via “count-mixture” models) – is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models. The paper successfully addresses a gap in the literature regarding the marginal distributions of integer-valued stochastic processes like the BNBP, which have previously lacked a clear framework for collapsed inference.

Additional Comments:

While the BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable illustration of the utility of the developed EPPF and prediction rule, the improvements over the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP-LDA) appear marginal. The primary conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and the HDP-LDA possess distinct characteristics. A potentially more compelling experiment would involve comparing the collapsed sampler presented here against the slice sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference within the BNBP topic model. The uncollapsed representations offer inherent advantages regarding parallelization, and examining the mixing trade-offs relative to the developed collapsed sampler would be insightful. Overall, this is an interesting paper and meets the publication threshold. I have only a few minor comments regarding the experimental section. 

Specifically, the experimental results demonstrate faster convergence and better mixing of the BNBP sampler compared to the HDP-LDA sampler. However, the gains in predictive performance (perplexity) appear sensitive to the choice of the Dirichlet smoothing parameter η. While the authors acknowledge this, further discussion of how to appropriately tune η in practice, or a more robust comparison with parameter selection methods for the HDP-LDA, would strengthen the evaluation.
