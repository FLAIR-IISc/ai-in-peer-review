This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the BNBP. Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler are novel and interesting.

My main concern is the experimental evaluation. Your stated motivation is to develop a CG sampler for the BNBP due to its simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. In other words, the contribution is primarily algorithmic rather than a new statistical model.

Given this focus, the experiments should directly compare the proposed sampler against other sampling algorithms for the BNBP, rather than against HDP-LDA, which is a different model. To substantiate the claimed advantages, please include empirical results comparing your fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with alternative BNBP inference methods (e.g., uncollapsed or partially collapsed Gibbs, other MCMC variants, or existing BNBP samplers in the literature), evaluating convergence speed, mixing behavior, and predictive performance. Wall-clock time, iteration counts to convergence, and standard mixing diagnostics would help quantify the benefits you claim.

In short, while the theoretical contribution (Lemma 1) and the derived sampler are compelling, the paper needs experiments that benchmark the proposed CG sampler against other BNBP sampling algorithms to support the stated advantages.
