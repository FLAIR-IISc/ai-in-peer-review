The paper presents the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

This is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The primary result—the derivation of the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling (arrived at via “count-mixture” models)—is both interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for deriving EPPFs for related models.

Additional comments:
- While the BNBP topic model is a reasonable illustration of the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal. The only confident conclusion one can draw is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics.
- A more informative experiment would be to contrast the collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. The uncollapsed representations have natural advantages for parallelization, and it would be useful to examine mixing trade-offs with respect to the collapsed sampler developed here.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold. I have only a few minor comments on the experimental section (see above).
