This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The core contribution lies in deriving an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP, enabling the development of this CG sampler, which addresses a limitation of previous BNBP inference methods that relied on truncation or slice sampling. The authors demonstrate the utility of their approach through a topic modeling application.

The paper clearly articulates the motivation for developing a fully collapsed sampler – to provide a simple, efficient, and scalable inference method for the BNBP. Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler are indeed novel and represent a valuable addition to the toolkit for nonparametric Bayesian modeling. The derivation of the EPPF is a significant theoretical step, generalizing techniques from related models and providing a foundational element for the sampler.  The authors appropriately situate their work within the broader context of nonparametric Bayesian mixture modeling, referencing key papers on Dirichlet processes, normalized random measures with independent increments, and related stochastic processes like the HDP and gamma-Poisson process. The connection to the Chinese restaurant process and exchangeable partition probability functions is well-explained.

However, the experimental evaluation raises some concerns. While the comparisons to HDP-LDA are insightful and demonstrate competitive performance, the primary claim of the paper centers around the *sampling algorithm* itself – its simplicity, speed, and mixing properties. Consequently, a more direct evaluation against existing sampling methods specifically designed for the BNBP (e.g., the slice sampling approach from [11], or finite truncation methods [10, 7]) would be significantly more compelling.  The motivation explicitly highlights “fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance,” but the empirical focus is heavily weighted towards comparison with a different model (HDP-LDA) rather than benchmarking the new sampler’s efficiency *relative to other BNBP samplers*. Demonstrating a clear advantage in computational time, mixing diagnostics, or convergence speed over alternative BNBP sampling techniques would substantially strengthen the paper's contribution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP and the resulting fully collapsed Gibbs sampler are novel contributions.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear rigorous and well-motivated, building upon existing literature in nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the underlying concepts and methodology.
*   **Competitive Performance:** The BNBP topic model achieves competitive predictive performance compared to HDP-LDA.
*   **Practical Significance:** A fully collapsed sampler offers potential benefits in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Benchmarking:** The primary weakness is the lack of a direct comparison of the proposed sampler against other existing sampling algorithms specifically designed for the BNBP.
*   **Overemphasis on HDP-LDA Comparison:** While valuable, the comparison to HDP-LDA doesn’t directly address the central claim regarding the advantages of the new BNBP sampler.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** While acknowledged, a more in-depth discussion of the impact of the hyperparameters (a0, b0, e0, f0) and the potential for sensitivity analysis could be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling.
*   The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler offers a potentially significant improvement in computational efficiency for BNBP inference.
*   The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on standard topic modeling benchmarks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of a direct comparison to other BNBP sampling algorithms weakens the empirical validation of the proposed sampler's advantages.
*   The emphasis on HDP-LDA comparison detracts from the central focus on the sampling algorithm's performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a valuable contribution to the field. However, strengthening the empirical evaluation by including a direct comparison to alternative BNBP sampling methods would significantly enhance its impact. I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors addressing the benchmarking concerns raised above. I would rate this paper as a **weak accept** at present, with the potential to be upgraded to a strong accept with the suggested revisions.
