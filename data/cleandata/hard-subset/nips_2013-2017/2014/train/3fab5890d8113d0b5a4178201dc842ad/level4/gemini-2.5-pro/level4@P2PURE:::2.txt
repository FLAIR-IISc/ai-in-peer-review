This paper proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The presented Lemma 1 and the derived sampler are interesting and appear to be significant contributions.

However, my primary concern is the experimental evaluation. The authors state that their motivation is to develop a superior sampling algorithm for the BNBP, citing benefits such as simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. This positions the work's main contribution as algorithmic, rather than as a new statistical model.

Therefore, to validate the claimed advantages of the proposed sampler, a rigorous evaluation requires a direct comparison against other existing sampling algorithms for the BNBP. The current experiments, which compare the proposed method to HDP-LDA, are insufficient. This comparison conflates the performance of the sampling algorithm with the performance of the underlying statistical model, making it impossible to assess the specific merits of the proposed sampler. To properly support the paper's claims, experimental results comparing it against other inference methods for the BNBP are necessary.
