This paper presents the derivation of the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The authors then utilize this EPPF to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

The paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound. Its primary contribution—the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via a "count-mixture" representation—is interesting in its own right. Furthermore, this approach provides a potentially useful framework for deriving EPPFs for related models.

The BNBP topic model serves as a reasonable application to illustrate the utility of the derived EPPF and its corresponding prediction rule. However, the experimental comparison to HDP-LDA shows only marginal improvements, leading to the modest conclusion that the two models simply have distinct characteristics. A more insightful experiment would have been a direct comparison of inference methods for the BNBP topic model itself. Specifically, contrasting the proposed collapsed Gibbs sampler with existing uncollapsed methods, such as the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8], would provide a much clearer picture of its practical advantages. Given that uncollapsed representations offer natural advantages for parallelization, it would be valuable to examine the trade-offs in mixing efficiency between those methods and the collapsed sampler developed in this work.

Overall, this is an interesting and valuable contribution that meets the threshold for publication. My suggestions are minor and pertain primarily to strengthening the experimental evaluation.
