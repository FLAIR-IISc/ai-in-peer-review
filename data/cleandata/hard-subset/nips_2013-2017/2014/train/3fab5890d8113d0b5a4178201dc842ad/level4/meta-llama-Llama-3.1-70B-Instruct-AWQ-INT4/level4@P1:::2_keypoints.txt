- Develops a group-size-dependent exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the integer-valued beta-negative binomial process (BNBP).
- Derives a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, producing a novel nonparametric Bayesian topic model distinct from existing models.
- Derives the EPPF that describes the prior of a latent column-exchangeable random count matrix and provides a prediction rule for simulating exchangeable partitions.
- Applies the BNBP to topic modeling and constructs a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler, with empirical comparison to HDP-LDA.
- Main contributions: EPPF derivation for BNBP, development of a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler, and application to topic modeling.
- Strengths: novel nonparametric-Bayesian contribution, simple/intuitive formulation, easy implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and strong predictive performance; paper is well written.
- Primary concern: experiments compare against HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP sampling algorithms, which mismatches the paper’s stated focus on sampling methodology.
- Pro-acceptance points: novelty, algorithmic benefits (implementation, convergence, mixing), and clarity of exposition.
- Con-acceptance points: inadequate experimental comparison to alternative BNBP samplers.
- Suggested improvement: provide experimental comparisons between the proposed sampler and other BNBP sampling algorithms to better support the paper’s claims.
