- Paper introduces a BNBP-based mixed-membership model and derives its EPPF, enabling new theoretical and algorithmic developments.  
- Derivation of the EPPF allows a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that analytically marginalizes the infinite-dimensional beta process.  
- Randomizing group sizes is a clever technique that enables the analytic marginalization and a simple prediction rule.  
- The collapsed sampler avoids truncation or slice sampling and empirically shows fast convergence and good mixing.  
- Empirical gains over the HDP are modest; the practical advantages versus HDP-LDA are not clearly convincing given added complexity.  
- The paper lacks an intuitive explanation for why the BNBP’s multiplicative global/local popularity model should outperform the HDP’s additive structure.  
- Overall, the work is a significant theoretical contribution with promising methodology, but practical significance and motivation need clearer justification; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
