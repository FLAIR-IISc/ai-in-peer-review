This paper presents a new Bayesian non-parametric model for mixed-membership modeling of aggregated data. This model is based on a beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). While this stochastic process has been used by previous works, as mentioned in the paper, there are several aspects that distinguish this work.

Firstly, this work obtains an EPपीएफ (Exchangeable Partition Probability Function) and, based on that, a prediction rule. The relationship between Bayesian non-parametrics and EPपीएफ in statistics has been studied for a long time, following the seminal work of J. Pittman. Nevertheless, this concept has not been widely exploited in the practice of BNP research. The relationship between the BNP and EPपीएफ created in this paper represents, for me, an important step towards integrating EPपीएफ analysis and BNP research, potentially leading to more work in this direction.

Secondly, the idea of randomizing group sizes (which are considered constants in most other works) is interesting. It turns out that this idea makes it possible to obtain certain marginal distributions.

I think this paper has a significant theoretical contribution. However, the practical applicability of this work is still questionable. The estimation algorithm is clearly more complex than HDP (both in theory and in the algorithm). The improvements demonstrated in the results are not sufficient to convince me that this increased complexity is meaningful in practice. Furthermore, the paper does not provide any intuitive explanation (or justification) as to why the proposed framework would work better than HDP in practice.

I think this paper has significant theoretical value. However, practical applicability remains unclear. I wish the authors could clarify how this method can improve practical performance against HDP and why. Overall, I still lean towards acceptance.
