- Proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) enabled by deriving an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF).
- Core novelty: Lemma 1 and the EPPF derivation that enable a novel collapsed sampler for the BNBP.
- Motivation: provide a simple, efficient, and scalable inference method avoiding truncation or slice sampling.
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous derivations that generalize techniques from related nonparametric Bayesian models.
- Clear exposition linking to Chinese restaurant process, EPPFs, and related literature (DP, NRMI, HDP, gamma-Poisson).
- Demonstrated utility via a topic modeling application with competitive predictive performance against HDP-LDA.
- Practical significance: fully collapsed sampler potentially improves computational efficiency and scalability for BNBP inference.
- Major empirical concern: lack of direct benchmarking against existing BNBP samplers (e.g., slice sampling or finite truncation methods).
- Overreliance on HDP-LDA comparisons that do not directly validate the sampler’s claimed speed, mixing, or convergence benefits.
- Need for empirical evidence of computational time, mixing diagnostics, and convergence speed versus alternative BNBP methods.
- Parameter sensitivity: paper insufficiently explores impact of hyperparameters (a0, b0, e0, f0) and lacks sensitivity analysis.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound contribution with potential practical benefits and competitive empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical validation specific to BNBP sampler performance limits the strength of claims.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on strengthening empirical benchmarking against other BNBP samplers; current stance = weak accept.
