- Introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and applies it in a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.

- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented.

- Core contribution: derivation of the BNBP EPPF in a mixed-membership setting via “count-mixture” models, offering theoretical interest and tools for related models.

- Demonstration with a BNBP topic model shows utility of the EPPF and prediction rule but yields only marginal performance gains over HDP-LDA.

- Evaluation should be strengthened by comparing the proposed collapsed sampler to existing BNBP inference methods (sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8]).

- Notes that uncollapsed representations have parallelization advantages; recommends studying mixing tradeoffs between uncollapsed and the proposed collapsed sampler.

- Overall assessment: interesting and publishable work; concerns are minor and mainly pertain to the experimental section.
