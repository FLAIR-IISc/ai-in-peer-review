- Derived the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) in mixed-membership models via a count-mixture framework — a significant theoretical result.  
- Proposed an efficient collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model using the derived EPPF.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and technically sound.  
- The count-mixture technique could serve as a useful template for deriving EPPFs for related models.  
- Experiments use the BNBP topic model as a proof of concept to demonstrate the predictive rule.  
- Comparison to HDP-LDA is not fully convincing: only marginal improvements and mainly shows differing characteristics rather than clear superiority.  
- Suggests stronger experimental evaluation by comparing the proposed collapsed sampler to uncollapsed methods (e.g., sliced and truncated samplers) to assess mixing speed vs. wall-clock time and parallelization trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance.
