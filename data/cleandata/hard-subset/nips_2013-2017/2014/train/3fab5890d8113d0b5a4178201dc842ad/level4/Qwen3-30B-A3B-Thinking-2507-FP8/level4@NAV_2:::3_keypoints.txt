- Introduces an EPPF for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to build a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.
- Paper is well-written, clearly articulated, and technically rigorous.
- Deriving the BNBP's EPPF via a count-mixture framework is theoretically interesting and provides useful methodological tools for related EPPF development.
- The BNBP topic model demonstrates the EPPF’s utility but yields only modest performance improvements over HDP-LDA.
- Main empirical conclusion is that BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics rather than large performance gains.
- Recommend additional experiments comparing the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler ([10]) and the truncated sampler ([8]) for BNBP inference.
- Note that uncollapsed representations naturally support parallelization; suggest evaluating tradeoffs in mixing performance versus the presented collapsed sampler.
- Overall assessment: interesting contribution that meets the conference’s publication standards, with only minor experimental suggestions.
