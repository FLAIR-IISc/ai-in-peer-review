- Paper proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs (FCG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) with a theoretically interesting Lemma 1.  
- The experimental evaluation significantly undermines the paper’s empirical claims.  
- Authors claim the FCG sampler is simple, fast-converging, mixes well, and achieves state-of-the-art predictive performance for BNBP.  
- Evaluation is methodologically invalid because it compares against HDP-LDA rather than other BNBP-specific samplers.  
- HDP-LDA has a different hierarchical structure and inference problem, making it irrelevant for assessing a BNBP sampler.  
- Without direct comparisons to established BNBP samplers, claims about convergence, mixing, and predictive performance are unsubstantiated.  
- Experimental setup omits crucial details: convergence diagnostics (e.g., effective sample size, autocorrelation), runtime comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.  
- Results should explicitly benchmark the FCG sampler against other BNBP inference methods on identical datasets and protocols.  
- Required revisions: replace HDP-LDA comparisons with BNBP sampler benchmarks, provide convergence and runtime analyses, and clarify predictive-performance claims within the BNBP context.  
- As is, empirical support is inadequate, the evaluation is misaligned with the paper’s focus, and the results are inconclusive.
