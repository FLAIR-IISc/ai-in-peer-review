- Paper proposes a novel fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the Beta Negative Binomial Process (BNBP).
- Introduction of Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler is an interesting theoretical contribution.
- Authors claim the sampler offers simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.
- Core contribution is algorithmic (new inference method for an existing model), so experiments should directly support algorithmic claims.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient because it does not compare against other existing BNBP sampling algorithms.
- Comparison only to HDP-LDA is inappropriate for assessing BNBP sampler performance.
- To substantiate claims of faster convergence, better mixing, and superior performance, a direct benchmark against alternative BNBP inference schemes is required.
