- Derived the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta–negative binomial process (BNBP) and constructed a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.
- Manuscript is well written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.
- Main contribution—the EPPF derivation for mixed-membership modeling via count-mixture constructions—is interesting and potentially useful for related models.
- Empirical gains over HDP-LDA are only marginal; the main defensible conclusion is that BNBP and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics.
- Experimental evaluation should more informatively compare the collapsed sampler to the BNBP sliced sampler and the truncated sampler.
- Uncollapsed representations have natural advantages for parallelization; mixing and parallelization trade-offs versus the collapsed sampler should be examined.
- Overall the paper is interesting, meets the publication threshold, and only requires minor experimental-section comments.
