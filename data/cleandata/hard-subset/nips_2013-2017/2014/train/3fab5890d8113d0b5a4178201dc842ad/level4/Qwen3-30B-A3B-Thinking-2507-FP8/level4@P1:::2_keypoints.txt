- Proposes a novel EPPF for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for BNBP-based mixed-membership models.  
- Derives the EPPF by randomizing group sizes and using Bayes' rule to obtain the marginal distribution enabling fully collapsed inference.  
- Presents a nonparametric Bayesian topic model claimed to be simple to implement, fast to converge, mixing well, and achieving state-of-the-art predictive performance.  
- Provides a rigorous derivation of the BNBP EPPF, filling an important theoretical gap for integer-valued stochastic processes in mixed-membership modeling.  
- Derives a prediction rule for simulating exchangeable random partitions under the BNBP EPPF.  
- Constructs a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes out topics, topic weights, and the infinite-dimensional beta process, yielding closed-form updates.  
- Technical contributions are sound; Lemma 1 (equivalence with group-size dependent model), Lemma 3 (EPPF derivation), and Lemma 4 (prediction rule) are novel and well-executed.  
- Thoroughly compares BNBP’s multiplicative topic shrinkage mechanism with HDP-LDA’s additive approach, highlighting conceptual differences.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is insufficiently focused on validating the claimed sampler advantages (convergence speed, mixing) because it only compares to HDP-LDA, a different model.  
- Missing empirical comparisons with other BNBP inference methods (e.g., finite truncation, slice sampling, blocked Gibbs sampling referenced in [10,11,7]).  
- Reported results show comparable perplexity to HDP-LDA at moderate topic counts but do not demonstrate the collapsed sampler’s superior convergence or mixing.  
- Recommendation: add experiments directly comparing the proposed collapsed Gibbs sampler to other BNBP samplers to substantiate claims about speed, mixing, and predictive performance.  
- Overall: well-written with strong theoretical contributions, but experimental validation must be strengthened to fully support the paper’s main algorithmic claims.
