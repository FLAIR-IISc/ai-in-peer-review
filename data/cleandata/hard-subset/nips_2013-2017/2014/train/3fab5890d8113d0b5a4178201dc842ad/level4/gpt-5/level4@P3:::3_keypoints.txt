- Derives an EPPF for the BNBP in mixed-membership settings by introducing an ECPF via Poissonizing group sizes and marginalizing the beta process.  
- Recovers the EPPF with Bayes’ rule and provides a simple prediction rule for collapsed Gibbs sampling.  
- Instantiates a fully collapsed BNBP topic model (marginalizing topics, topic weights, and the beta process) and compares to HDP-LDA.  
- Connects to classical EPPF/CRP literature and recent count-process work; method appears portable to other integer-valued processes.  
- Technical soundness: beta–gamma–Poisson augmentation and factorization are standard and correctly applied, enabling analytic marginalization.  
- Paper delivers a complete pipeline (ECPF→EPPF, prediction rule, collapsed sampler) with proofs motivated though deferred.  
- Experiments: perplexity on three corpora and mixing diagnostics show fast convergence and good local mixing for the collapsed sampler.  
- Empirics show modest improvements over HDP-LDA; BNBP can achieve similar/better perplexity with fewer topics for moderate η.  
- Empirical scope limited: lacks direct comparisons to other BNBP inference schemes (slice/truncation) and broader baselines.  
- Clarity: generally well written with clear probabilistic constructions and helpful intuition for the prediction rule.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: explain the digamma distribution, reduce notational overload, provide a symbol table, add pseudo-code and complexity per iteration, and proofread.  
- Originality: novel derivation of a group-size-dependent EPPF for BNBP mixed-membership; prediction rule and collapsed model are practically valuable.  
- Significance: fills a gap enabling truncation-free collapsed inference; practical algorithm is simple, fast, and of interest to BN practitioners.  
- Strengths: principled EPPF derivation, simple prediction rule, multiplicative shrinkage mechanism, empirical mixing and compact topic representations, transferable methodology.  
- Weaknesses: narrow empirical comparisons, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and missing runtime/scaling and parallelizability evaluations.  
- Suggested experiments: compare to BNBP slice/truncated samplers, ablate hyperparameters (c, γ0, rj), add more baselines (e.g., gamma–Poisson), report runtime and topic coherence.  
- Theory suggestion: discuss asymptotic behavior of the sample-size-dependent EPPF and when sample-size dependence is beneficial.  
- Minor comments: clarify the addition rule implication, unify notation (KJ, superscripts −ji), and fix typos (e.g., “perdition”→“prediction”).  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and practical paper recommended for acceptance, with suggested expansions to the empirical section and minor clarifications.
