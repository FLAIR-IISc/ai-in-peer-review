- Novel theoretical contribution: Lemma 1 is new and provides the foundation for a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP.
- Technical soundness: The sampler derivation appears correct and is a non-trivial technical advance for BNBP inference.
- Practical potential: A fully collapsed sampler could materially benefit practitioners using BNBP models.
- Misframed evaluation: The paper frames the contribution as algorithmic (sampling methodology) rather than a new statistical model.
- Inadequate experiments: Current empirical validation does not adequately support claims about implementation simplicity, fast convergence, good mixing, or state-of-the-art predictive performance.
- Problematic baseline: Comparing the proposed BNBP sampler to HDP-LDA conflates model differences with algorithmic performance and is therefore inappropriate.
- Needed baselines: The authors should compare against other inference methods for the same BNBP model (e.g., uncollapsed Gibbs, variational inference, other MCMC).
- Suggested metrics: Evaluate convergence, mixing, and efficiency directly (e.g., perplexity or held-out likelihood over iterations/time).
- Unsupported claims: Without direct BNBP algorithm comparisons, the paper cannot demonstrate the sampler’s superiority.
- Required revision: A major revision with direct experimental comparisons to other BNBP inference techniques is necessary.
