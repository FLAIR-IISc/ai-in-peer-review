- Paper introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), centered on Lemma 1 that enables integrating out random measures.  
- Lemma 1 provides the conditional distributions needed to implement the collapsed sampling scheme.  
- The theoretical contribution is novel, interesting, and appears technically sound.  
- A collapsed sampler is well-motivated due to potential gains in convergence speed and mixing over uncollapsed methods.  
- The paper frames the contribution as primarily algorithmic (inference method) rather than proposing a new statistical model.  
- Primary concern: experimental validation does not adequately support the algorithmic claims.  
- Convincing evaluation should compare the CG sampler against other BNBP inference methods (e.g., uncollapsed Gibbs, slice samplers, variational inference).  
- Relevant empirical metrics include wall-clock convergence to target perplexity, mixing efficiency (trace plots, autocorrelations), and final predictive accuracy.  
- Current experiments compare BNBP (with proposed sampler) against HDP-LDA, which conflates model performance with inference performance.  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution but insufficient empirical evidence; authors should add direct comparisons to other BNBP inference algorithms to substantiate practical advantages.
