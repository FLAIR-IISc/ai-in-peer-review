This paper proposes a new Bayesian nonparametric model for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data, built on the Beta–Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Although prior work has used this stochastic process, the paper introduces several distinct contributions.

First, it derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the model and the associated prediction rules. The relationship between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPFs has been studied in statistics since Pitman’s seminal work, but it has not been widely leveraged in practical BNP research. The explicit connection established here between the BNBP and an EPPF is, in my view, an important step toward unifying EPPF-based analysis with BNP modeling and may catalyze further developments.

Second, the paper treats group sizes as random rather than fixed (as is common), an idea that enables the derivation of certain marginal distributions.

I consider these to be significant theoretical contributions. However, the practical utility remains uncertain. The inference algorithm appears substantially more complex than the HDP—both conceptually and algorithmically—and the empirical improvements reported do not, to me, justify the added complexity. In addition, the paper does not provide an intuitive explanation or justification for why, in practice, the proposed framework should outperform the HDP.

In sum, the paper has strong theoretical value, but its practical benefits are unclear. I encourage the authors to clarify when and why their method should yield better real-world performance than the HDP. Overall, I still lean toward acceptance.
