- Derives the EPPF for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), enabling a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for mixed-membership models.  
- Uses a group-size dependent model to derive an ECPF, then conditions to obtain the EPPF and a simple prediction rule.  
- Implements a BNBP topic model with a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler that marginalizes the beta process, topic proportions, and topics.  
- Claims faster convergence, better mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance compared to HDP based on experiments.  
- Strength: Novel, significant theoretical contribution filling a key gap—first EPPF for BNBP in mixed-membership setting.  
- Strength: Mathematical derivations (Lemmas 1–4) appear sound and the approach may generalize to other integer-valued processes.  
- Strength: Clear writing and a simple, intuitive prediction rule that makes the sampler easy to implement.  
- Strength: Empirically shows BNBP’s multiplicative global-local shrinkage yields more compact topic representations while remaining competitive.  
- Weakness: Experiments conflate model and inference effects—comparison is BNBP+new sampler vs HDP+its sampler, not sampler vs sampler.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct benchmarking of the proposed sampler against other BNBP inference methods (truncated Gibbs, slice samplers) on sampler-specific metrics.  
- Weakness: “State-of-the-art” claim is overstated; HDP can achieve lower perplexity with more topics—BNBP’s strength is compactness for similar perplexity.  
- Overall: Theoretical and methodological contributions are strong and warrant acceptance, but experimental validation of the sampler should be strengthened.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
