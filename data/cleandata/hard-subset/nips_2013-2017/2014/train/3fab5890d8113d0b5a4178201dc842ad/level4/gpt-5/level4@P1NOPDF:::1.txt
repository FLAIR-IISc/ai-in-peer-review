This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for mixed-membership modeling of grouped data, built on the Beta–Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Although the BNBP has been used previously, this work differs in several important ways.

First, it derives an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the BNBP and the corresponding prediction rules. While the connection between Bayesian nonparametrics and EPPFs has been studied extensively in statistics since Pitman’s seminal work, it has not been widely leveraged in practical BNP research. The explicit link established here between the BNBP and an EPPF is, in my view, an important step toward unifying EPPF-based analysis with BNP methodology, and it may inspire further developments along this line.

Second, the paper introduces randomization of group sizes, which are typically treated as fixed constants. This is an interesting idea and appears to make certain marginal distributions analytically tractable.

I consider the theoretical contributions significant. However, the practical utility remains uncertain. The proposed inference algorithm is clearly more complex than that of the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP), both conceptually and computationally. The empirical improvements shown are not, in my opinion, sufficient to justify this added complexity in practice. Moreover, the paper does not provide a clear intuitive explanation or justification for why the proposed framework should outperform the HDP in real applications.

In summary, this work has strong theoretical value, but its practical benefits are not yet convincing. I encourage the authors to clarify how and why their method may improve practical performance compared with the HDP, and to articulate the trade-offs involved. Overall, I still lean toward acceptance.
