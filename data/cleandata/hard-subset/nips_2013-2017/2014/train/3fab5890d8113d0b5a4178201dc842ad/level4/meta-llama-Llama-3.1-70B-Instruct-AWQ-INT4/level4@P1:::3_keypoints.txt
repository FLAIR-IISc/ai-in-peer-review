- Novel derivation of the BNBP's exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling.  
- Provides a unified treatment of mixed-membership modeling using the BNBP, yielding a distinct nonparametric Bayesian topic model.  
- Develops a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model with fast convergence, good mixing, compact corpus representation, and strong predictive performance.  
- Paper is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound; technical arguments are well-presented.  
- Experimental section is well-presented and results are clearly discussed.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison to alternative inference methods (sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8]).  
- Uncollapsed representations have natural parallelization advantages; the paper should examine mixing vs. parallelization tradeoffs relative to the collapsed sampler.  
- Improvements over HDP-LDA appear only marginal.  
- Experimental results could be strengthened with more detailed analyses and additional metrics.  
- Overall the work is a significant contribution to nonparametric Bayesian modeling and merits publication.
