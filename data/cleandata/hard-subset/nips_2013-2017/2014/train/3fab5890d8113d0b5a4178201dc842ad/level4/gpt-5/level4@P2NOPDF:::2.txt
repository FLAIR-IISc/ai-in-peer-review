This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler are novel and interesting.

My main concern is the experimental evaluation. The stated motivation is to develop a CG sampler for the BNBP because it offers simple implementation, faster convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. Thus, the contribution centers on the sampling algorithm rather than a new statistical model.

Given this focus, the experiments should directly compare the proposed CG sampler with alternative inference methods for the BNBP, rather than with HDP-LDA. To substantiate the claimed advantages, please include comparisons against other BNBP sampling algorithms (e.g., uncollapsed or partially collapsed Gibbs, existing MCMC or variational baselines) and report metrics reflecting convergence speed, mixing quality, and predictive performance.

As it stands, while a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP is presented, the empirical results do not evaluate it against other BNBP inference schemes. Such comparisons are necessary to validate the motivation and demonstrate clear benefits.
