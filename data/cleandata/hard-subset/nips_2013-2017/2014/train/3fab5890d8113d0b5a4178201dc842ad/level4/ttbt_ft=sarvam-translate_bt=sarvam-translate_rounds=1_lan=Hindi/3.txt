This paper presents an exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta-negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a compact Gibbs sampler for the BNBP subject model.

It is a well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound paper. The primary result of this paper – the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP for mixed-membership modeling ("count-mixture" model) – is interesting in itself and provides a potentially useful tool for obtaining the EPPF for related models.

Additional Notes:
While the BNBP subject model developed EPPF and related prediction rule is a fair example of its usefulness, the improvements on HDP-LDA are only minor, and the only confident conclusion one can draw is that the BNBP subject model and HDP-LDA have different characteristics. A more interesting experiment would be to contrast the compact sampler against [10]'s sliced sampler and [8]'s truncated sampler for estimation in the BNBP subject model. There are natural advantages to un-truncated representations when it comes to parallelization, and it would be useful to examine the mixing tradeoff in relation to the compact sampler developed here. Overall, this is an interesting paper and exceeds the scope of the publication. I have only a few minor comments on the experimental section (see above).
