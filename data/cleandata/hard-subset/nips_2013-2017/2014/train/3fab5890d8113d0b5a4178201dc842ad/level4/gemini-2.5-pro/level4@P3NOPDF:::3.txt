This paper derives the Exchangeable Partition Probability Function (EPPF) for the Beta Negative Binomial Process (BNBP) and applies this result to develop an efficient collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP-based topic model.

The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and technically sound. Its primary contribution—the derivation of the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership models via a "count-mixture" framework—is a significant theoretical result. This contribution is valuable not only on its own merits but also because the underlying technique may provide a useful template for deriving EPPFs for related models in the future.

The experimental section uses the BNBP topic model to illustrate the utility of the derived EPPF and its corresponding predictive rule. While this application serves as a reasonable proof of concept, the comparison against the HDP-LDA topic model is not fully convincing. The results show only marginal improvements in performance, and the primary conclusion that can be drawn is that the BNBP and HDP-LDA models exhibit different characteristics, rather than one being demonstrably superior.

A more insightful and compelling experimental evaluation would involve comparing different inference strategies for the BNBP topic model itself. Specifically, a comparison of the proposed collapsed Gibbs sampler against existing uncollapsed methods, such as the sliced sampler [10] or the truncated sampler [8], would be highly valuable. Such a comparison would directly assess the practical trade-offs of the collapsed sampler. While uncollapsed samplers often have advantages for parallelization, collapsed samplers can exhibit faster mixing. An empirical analysis of these trade-offs (e.g., mixing speed vs. wall-clock time) would provide crucial insights for practitioners seeking to apply the BNBP model and would more directly highlight the benefits of the authors' proposed inference scheme.

In summary, this is a strong paper with a valuable theoretical contribution. The derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP is a noteworthy achievement. While the experimental section could be strengthened by including a direct comparison of different inference algorithms for the BNBP model, this does not detract from the core contribution. The paper is a solid addition to the literature, and I recommend its acceptance.
