This paper introduces a novel exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for mixed-membership modeling using the integer-valued beta-negative binomial process (BNBP). The authors derive a fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP, leading to a nonparametric Bayesian topic model with simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance.

The main contributions of the paper are the derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP and the construction of the fully collapsed Gibbs sampler. The authors also demonstrate the use of the BNBP topic model on several document corpora and compare its performance with the hierarchical Dirichlet process (HDP) topic model.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the theoretical background and the derivation of the EPPF and the Gibbs sampler. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the comparison with the HDP topic model is informative.

However, I have some concerns regarding the experimental evaluation. The authors motivate the construction of the fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP because of its simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. This suggests that the focus of the work is on the sampling algorithm, rather than statistical modeling. Therefore, I believe that the authors should compare other sampling algorithms for the BNBP with the proposed algorithm, rather than comparing it with the HDP-LDA.

Specifically, I would like to see a comparison of the proposed fully collapsed Gibbs sampler with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP, such as the blocked Gibbs sampler or the slice sampler. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm and help to establish its advantages and limitations.

Additionally, I would like to see more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the number of iterations used for the Gibbs sampler. This would help to ensure that the results are reliable and reproducible.

Overall, I think that the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling, and I believe that it has the potential to be published in NIPS. However, I would like to see some revisions to address my concerns regarding the experimental evaluation.

Strengths:

* Novel derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP
* Fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP
* Simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing, and state-of-the-art predictive performance
* Clear explanation of the theoretical background and the derivation of the EPPF and the Gibbs sampler

Weaknesses:

* Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive
* Comparison with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP is needed
* More details on the experimental setup would be helpful

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of nonparametric Bayesian modeling
* The derivation of the EPPF for the BNBP is novel and interesting
* The fully collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP has the potential to be widely used
