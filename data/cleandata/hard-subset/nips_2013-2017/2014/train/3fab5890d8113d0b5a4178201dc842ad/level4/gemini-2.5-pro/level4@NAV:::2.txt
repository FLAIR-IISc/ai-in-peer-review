This paper introduces a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP). The theoretical contribution, particularly Lemma 1 and the resulting sampler, is novel and compelling.

My primary concern, however, lies with the experimental evaluation. The authors motivate the work by claiming the proposed CG sampler offers benefits in implementation, convergence speed, mixing, and predictive performance. This positions the paper's main contribution as algorithmic rather than one of statistical modeling.

Therefore, to validate these claims, the experimental setup must compare the proposed algorithm against other inference algorithms for the BNBP. The current comparison against HDP-LDA is insufficient, as it conflates the performance of the sampler with the performance of the underlying model. To strengthen the paper, a direct benchmark against alternative samplers for the BNBP is required.
