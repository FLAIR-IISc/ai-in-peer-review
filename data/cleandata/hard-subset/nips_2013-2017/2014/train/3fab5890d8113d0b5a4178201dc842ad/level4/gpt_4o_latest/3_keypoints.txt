- Derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to design a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.  
- Paper is well-written, fairly clear, and technically robust.  
- Main contribution—BNBP EPPF for mixed-membership ("count-mixture") models—is novel and potentially useful for related EPPF derivations.  
- BNBP topic model is a reasonable demonstration, but performance gains over HDP-LDA are modest.  
- Primary empirical takeaway is that BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics rather than large performance differences.  
- Recommends additional experiments comparing the collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler [10] and truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.  
- Notes that uncollapsed representations are more amenable to parallelization; suggests exploring mixing vs. parallelization tradeoffs relative to the collapsed sampler.  
- Overall assessment: engaging paper that meets publication criteria, with only minor experimental-section suggestions.
