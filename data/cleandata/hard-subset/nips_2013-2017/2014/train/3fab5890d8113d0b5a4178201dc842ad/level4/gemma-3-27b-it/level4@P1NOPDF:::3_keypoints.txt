- Paper derives the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and builds a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.  
- The manuscript is well-written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.  
- The main contribution—deriving the BNBP’s EPPF via “count-mixture” models—is interesting and useful for related models.  
- The BNBP topic model is a reasonable illustration but shows only marginal improvements over HDP-LDA.  
- The clear takeaway is that BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics.  
- The experimental section should compare the proposed collapsed sampler against the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8].  
- Uncollapsed representations have advantages for parallelization; experiments should examine mixing tradeoffs relative to the collapsed sampler.  
- Overall assessment: an interesting paper that meets the publication threshold, with substantive comments mainly about experiments.
