The paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and leverages it to construct a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.
This manuscript is well written, reasonably clear, and technically sound. The main contribution—deriving the BNBP’s EPPF for mixed-membership modeling via “count-mixture” models—is interesting in its own right and provides potentially useful tools for obtaining EPPFs for related models.
Additional Comments:
While the BNBP topic model is a reasonable demonstration of the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are only marginal, and the most defensible conclusion is that the BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA exhibit distinct characteristics. A more informative experiment would contrast the collapsed sampler with the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Since uncollapsed representations have natural advantages for parallelization, it would be valuable to examine mixing trade-offs relative to the collapsed sampler presented here. Overall, this is an interesting paper and clears the publication threshold. I have only a few minor comments on the experimental section (see above).
