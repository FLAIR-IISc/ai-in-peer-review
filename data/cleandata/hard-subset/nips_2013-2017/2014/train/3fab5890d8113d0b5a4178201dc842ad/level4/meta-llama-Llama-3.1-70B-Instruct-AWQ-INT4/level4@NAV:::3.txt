This paper introduces the exchangeable partition probability function (EPPF) for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and leverages it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for the BNBP topic model.

The paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound. The primary contribution - deriving the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership modeling through "count-mixture" models - is intriguing and offers valuable tools for deriving EPPFs in related models.

Additional Comments:
Although the BNBP topic model adequately demonstrates the utility of the developed EPPF and corresponding prediction rule, the improvements over HDP-LDA are marginal, and the key takeaway is that both models exhibit distinct characteristics. A more compelling experiment would involve comparing the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler of [10] and the truncated sampler of [8] for inference in the BNBP topic model. Investigating mixing tradeoffs with respect to the collapsed sampler, particularly considering the natural advantages of uncollapsed representations in parallelization, would be beneficial. Overall, this is an interesting paper that meets the publication criteria. Minor suggestions for improvement are primarily related to the experimental section (noted above).
