This paper proposes a fully collapsed Gibbs (CG) sampler for the Beta-Negative Binomial Process (BNBP), a novel and interesting contribution, as demonstrated by Lemma 1 and the derived sampler. However, my primary concern lies in the experimental evaluation.

The motivation behind developing the CG sampler for the BNBP is its potential for simple implementation, fast convergence, good mixing properties, and state-of-the-art predictive performance. Given this focus on the sampling algorithm rather than statistical modeling, a more relevant comparison would be with other sampling algorithms for the BNBP, rather than the Hierarchical Dirichlet Process Latent Dirichlet Allocation (HDP-LDA) model.

To comprehensively evaluate the proposed CG sampler, experimental results comparing its performance to other BNBP sampling algorithms are necessary. The inclusion of such comparisons would strengthen the paper's contributions and provide a more thorough understanding of the proposed sampler's efficacy.
