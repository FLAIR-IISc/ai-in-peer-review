- Paper derives the EPPF for the beta negative binomial process (BNBP) and uses it to develop a collapsed Gibbs sampler for a BNBP topic model.
- The manuscript is well written, reasonably clear, and technically sound.
- The primary contribution — deriving the BNBP's EPPF for mixed-membership/count-mixture modeling — is interesting and broadly useful.
- The BNBP topic model reasonably illustrates the EPPF and prediction rule, but performance gains over HDP-LDA are only marginal.
- The main empirical conclusion is that BNBP topic model and HDP-LDA have distinct characteristics, not clear superiority.
- Reviewer recommends comparing the collapsed sampler to the sliced sampler [10] and the truncated sampler [8] for BNBP inference.
- Suggests evaluating uncollapsed representations for parallelization advantages and examining mixing tradeoffs versus the collapsed sampler.
- Overall the paper is interesting, meets publication threshold, with only minor experimental-section comments.
