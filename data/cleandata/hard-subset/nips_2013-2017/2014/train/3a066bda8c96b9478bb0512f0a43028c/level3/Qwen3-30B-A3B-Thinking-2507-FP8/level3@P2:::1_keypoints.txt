- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous positiveness scores by treating labels as Bernoulli random variables with those probabilities.  
- Extends e-SVM to a latent formulation for weak supervision, enabling learning from bounding-box annotations instead of pixel labels.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Differentiates from MIL and weighted SVMs by weighting labels (positiveness) rather than instances, handling partial positives (e.g., IoU scores).  
- Strength — Quality: sound technical development, principled extension of SVMs, and thorough experiments with new metrics (TAPC, NDCG).  
- Strength — Clarity: exceptionally well-written, clear problem formulation, model description, and integration details.  
- Strength — Originality: novel focus on continuous positiveness and a practical latent-variable approach for weak supervision.  
- Strength — Significance: addresses costly pixel-level annotation problem and works with readily available bounding boxes, yielding practical impact.  
- Weakness — Limited comparison to recent deep-learning based weakly supervised segmentation methods beyond MIL.  
- Weakness — Lacks discussion of computational complexity and potential overhead of iterative latent e-SVM optimization.  
- Weakness — Theoretical choices (Bernoulli model, regularization strength) are pragmatically motivated but lack deeper theoretical justification.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically valuable contribution with strong experimental support.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem, novel method, solid experiments, clear writing, and SOTA results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete empirical comparisons, missing complexity analysis, and limited theoretical backing.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS given contribution and results despite the noted weaknesses.
