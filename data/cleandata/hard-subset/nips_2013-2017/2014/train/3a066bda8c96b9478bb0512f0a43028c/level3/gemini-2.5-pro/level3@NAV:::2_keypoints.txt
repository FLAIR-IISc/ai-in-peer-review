- Proposes e-SVM: a hinge-loss SVM weighted per-example by a continuous "positiveness" score, plus a latent e-SVM variant for weak supervision.  
- Positions work within weakly supervised learning and instance-weighted SVM literature.  
- Strong presentation and thorough empirical evaluation on segmentation and detection, outperforming SVC/SVR and achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Major originality concern: approach is functionally equivalent to longstanding example-dependent cost SVMs (e.g., SVMlight) and fails to adequately cite/contrast prior work.  
- Latent-weight extension leads to a non-convex optimization with no theoretical convergence guarantees provided.  
- Heavy regularization on latent weights raises doubt whether weights are truly learned versus remaining near initialization; no analysis offered.  
- Incorrect claim that regression on [0,1] is "non-standard," overlooking common models like logistic regression.  
- Overall: well-executed empirical study but limited novelty and missing theoretical analysis are significant shortcomings.
