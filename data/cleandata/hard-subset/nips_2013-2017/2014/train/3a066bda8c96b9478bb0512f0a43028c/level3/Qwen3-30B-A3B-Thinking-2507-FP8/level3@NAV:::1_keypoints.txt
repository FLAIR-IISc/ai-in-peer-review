- Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for training with continuous "positiveness" values in [0,1] via soft weighting.
- Extends traditional SVM and multiple instance learning by replacing binary positives with soft weights and handling observed and latent positiveness.
- Applied to weakly supervised visual segmentation using bounding-box annotations.
- Technically sound with well-executed experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Demonstrates clear improvements over SVC and SVR baselines.
- Achieves state-of-the-art semantic segmentation on PASCAL VOC 2011.
- Improves RCNN object detection by 1.5% AP on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Experimental design is robust with appropriate baselines and evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG).
- Clarity is excellent with a well-structured and well-motivated presentation.
- Originality is significant though the extension from SVM is conceptually straightforward and may seem incremental.
- High significance for weakly supervised learning and large-scale computer vision applications.
- Overall recommendation: strong NIPS contribution; recommend acceptance.
