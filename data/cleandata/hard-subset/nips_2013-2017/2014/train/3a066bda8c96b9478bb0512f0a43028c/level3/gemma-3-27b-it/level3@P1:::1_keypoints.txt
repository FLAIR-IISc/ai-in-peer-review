- Introduces e-SVM: an SVM variant that uses continuous “positiveness” weights instead of binary labels to handle weakly supervised data.  
- Addresses weak supervision in computer vision, enabling learning from bounding-box or other soft annotations instead of pixel-level labels.  
- Treats positiveness as weights on labels (not samples), allowing compatibility with standard SVM solvers and soft assignment of positives.  
- Distinguishes from MIL and traditional weighted SVMs by explicitly handling continuous confidence measures without ad-hoc binarization.  
- Novelty: provides a principled way to incorporate continuous confidence into SVM learning.  
- Practicality: targets common real-world scenarios with abundant weak labels and scarce pixel annotations.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive performance on semantic segmentation and state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Theoretical soundness: well-motivated loss formulation and clear extension to latent-variable settings.  
- Clarity and presentation: well-written, organized, and easy to follow with thorough experimental descriptions.  
- Generality: method can work with different segment proposals, features, and base detectors, increasing applicability.  
- Weakness — regularization: limited justification and analysis of the KL-divergence regularizer; alternative strategies not explored.  
- Weakness — dependence on proposals: performance tied to segment proposal quality; sensitivity to different proposal methods not deeply investigated.  
- Weakness — computational cost: latent-variable extension increases complexity; more detailed large-scale cost analysis is needed.  
- Weakness — comparison to recent work: lacks deeper comparison to newer weakly supervised methods, especially deep-learning–based approaches.  
- Recommendation: overall strong contribution with compelling results; reviewer recommends acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
