- Paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for [0,1] “positiveness” targets (IoU), optimizing expected hinge loss and a latent variant regularized toward box-IoU via a KL term with alternating convex optimization.  
- Method is applied to segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and as class-specific spatial confidence features that improve RCNN on VOC07.  
- Conceptually similar to instance-weighted SVMs; latent e-SVM resembles weighted/transductive SVMs with per-example soft labels.  
- Missing key prior-work citations (weighted SVM/LS-SVM, TSVM, SVM+/weighted SVM); position e-SVM formally against these is required.  
- Strength: clear, practical formulation that reduces to standard SVM solvers and admits a straightforward latent extension.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains, notably improving RCNN on VOC07 using conventional components.  
- Strength: sensible IoU-regression and spatial pooling design with ablations showing improvement over SVC/SVR for segment scoring.  
- Weakness: limited originality—latent e-SVM not clearly differentiated from weighted TSVM/soft-label SVMs; need to justify sum-to-one per-example weighting as novel.  
- Weakness: missing standard probabilistic baselines for [0,1] targets (cross-entropy logistic regression, isotonic/beta regression) and tests of unconstrained vs sum-constrained weights.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter details missing—how λW and λR are chosen, search ranges, validation protocol, sensitivity analyses, and convergence/initialization for the alternating scheme.  
- Weakness: clarity and framing issues—title/abstract misleading about information content of unit-interval targets and exposition downplays important engineering choices (features, proposals, confidence-map design).  
- Weakness: prior-work section conflates weak supervision with MIL; needs clearer distinction and citations of soft-label/transductive formulations.  
- Minor: fix typos/notation, ensure symbol consistency, and add citations for TAPC/NDCG usage.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on thorough rewrite, proper positioning relative to WSVM/TSVM, and addressing methodological and clarity concerns; empirical results justify publication.
