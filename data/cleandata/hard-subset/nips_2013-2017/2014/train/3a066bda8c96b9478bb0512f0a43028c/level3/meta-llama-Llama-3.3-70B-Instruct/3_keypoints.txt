- Proposes a novel hybrid policy-search method combining model-based and model-free benefits via guided policy search.
- Fits local, time-varying linear dynamics models to optimize trajectory distributions under unknown dynamics.
- Uses trajectory optimization results to train general parameterized policies (e.g., neural networks).
- Introduces a background dynamics distribution as a prior to reduce sample complexity when refitting local models.
- Algorithm is iterative: refit local linear models and optimize trajectory distributions repeatedly.
- Main contribution: enabling trajectory optimization and policy learning when true dynamics are unknown.
- Experiments are thorough and show superior performance on simulated robotic manipulation and locomotion tasks.
- Demonstrates learning of complex neural policies that perform well in partially observed environments.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, balancing technical detail with high-level intuition.
- Weakness: insufficiently situates the work within the broader RL and robotics literature.
- Weakness: some notation and terminology assume strong control/RL background; a glossary or appendix would help.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with convincing empirical results and likely impact.
