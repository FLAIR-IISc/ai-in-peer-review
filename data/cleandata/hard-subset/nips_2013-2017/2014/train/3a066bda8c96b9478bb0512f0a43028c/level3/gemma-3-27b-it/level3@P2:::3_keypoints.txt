- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous “positiveness” labels by framing it as a weighted SVM.  
- Extends e-SVM to a latent-variable setting for weakly supervised learning when positiveness is unobserved.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Tackles a relevant problem—learning from weak supervision—which is important for computer vision due to annotation costs.  
- Method is versatile, handling both strongly and weakly supervised scenarios.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; e-SVM is closely related to existing weighted and transductive SVM methods.  
- Key prior work is missing from the literature review (e.g., Suykens et al. 2002, Yang et al. 2004, Vapnik 1998, Lapin et al. 2010).  
- Latent e-SVM resembles a weighted transductive SVM, a connection not adequately discussed or differentiated.  
- The sum constraint on weights might be novel but its benefits are not fully investigated or demonstrated.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparison to standard strong baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for continuous labels).  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection (λW, λR) and no sensitivity analysis reported.  
- Paper exposition is unclear: title and abstract misrepresent contributions and suggest misleading claims about unit-interval targets.  
- Excessive focus on minor algorithmic variants while omitting crucial details about feature engineering for segmentation and detection.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors and some awkward wording throughout the manuscript.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong experimental results (VOC2007 SOTA), relevance of problem, and methodological versatility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, missing critical prior work, lack of strong baselines, insufficient hyperparameter/feature detail, and clarity problems.  
- Overall assessment: potentially useful but overshadowed by omission of related work and insufficient justification of novelty; needs substantial revision.  
- Recommendation: Reject for NIPS with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing prior-work relations, baselines, and methodological justification; may be more suitable for a vision-focused venue after revision.
