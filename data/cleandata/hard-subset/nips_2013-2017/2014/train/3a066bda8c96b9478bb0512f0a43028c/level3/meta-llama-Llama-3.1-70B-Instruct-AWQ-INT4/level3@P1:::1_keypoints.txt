- Proposes e-SVM, a novel algorithm that uses continuous "positiveness" instead of binary labels and extends to weak supervision where positiveness is unobserved.  
- Models segment labels as Bernoulli random variables; expected loss reduces to weighting samples by a function of their positiveness.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.  
- Reports e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR under both strong and weak supervision and can be integrated with object detectors via appearance cues.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated extension of multiple-instance learning that handles continuous positive attributes via soft weighting.  
- Strength: validated on multiple tasks with promising results; experiments are interesting and well-executed.  
- Strength: clear and well-motivated presentation.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art (e.g., deep learning) methods.  
- Weakness: regularization in the latent e-SVM is not well justified and its effect is under-explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Overall: promising and original work but needs more SOTA comparisons and complexity/regularization analysis.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
