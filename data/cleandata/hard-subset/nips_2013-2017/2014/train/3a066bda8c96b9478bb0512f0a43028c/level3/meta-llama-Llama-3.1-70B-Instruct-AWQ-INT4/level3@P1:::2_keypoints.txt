- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from weakly supervised data with continuous labels in [0,1].
- Targets segment proposal classification by treating segment positiveness as a continuous value between 0 and 1.
- Uses a linear prediction function and an expected-loss objective that is convex in model parameters, solvable with standard SVM solvers.
- Introduces a latent extension that models unobserved segment positiveness as latent variables with regularization to avoid poor local minima.
- Demonstrates improved performance over SVC and SVR on segment proposal classification using TAPC and NDCG metrics.
- Shows effectiveness on semantic segmentation and achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Strength: novel algorithm for weak supervision with clear description and empirical benefits over equal-weight training.
- Strength: latent e-SVM is an interesting extension enabling learning with unobserved positiveness.
- Weakness: claimed novelty is unclear because the method resembles example-dependent cost approaches (e.g., SVMlight).
- Weakness: optimization for the latent extension is non-convex and lacks theoretical analysis.
- Weakness: relies on heavy regularization, which may constrain the model’s ability to learn from data.
- Weakness: contains minor errors (e.g., incorrect claim that positivity in [0,1] is non-standard regression).
- Originality: proposes a novel weakly supervised learning approach and a latent extension.
- Significance: validated on major vision tasks and potentially applicable to other weakly supervised problems.
- Quality: paper is well-written, experiments are thorough, and code availability aids reproducibility.
- Clarity: well-organized presentation with clear notation and well-explained equations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical results, and useful latent extension.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty, non-convex latent optimization without theory, and heavy regularization concerns.
