- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous “positiveness” values in weak supervision, applied to visual segmentation and detection.  
- Extends SVM by weighting labels with continuous values and supports both observed and latent positiveness settings.  
- Introduces a latent-variable extension and a defined optimization algorithm for weakly supervised learning.  
- Introduces TAPC and NDCG as evaluation metrics for segment proposal classification.  
- Empirically validated on semantic segmentation and object detection, reporting promising results and state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Addresses an important problem—reducing reliance on expensive pixel-level annotations—with clear motivation.  
- Technically sound and theoretically justified; presentation and organization are clear.  
- Experiments are extensive and generally demonstrate improvements over baselines.  
- Improvements over strong baselines (e.g., SVR) are sometimes modest.  
- Ablation studies are insufficient; contributions of components (e.g., latent e-SVM regularization) need deeper analysis.  
- Related work discussion is limited and should cover more recent advances in weakly-supervised learning.  
- Evaluation focuses heavily on CPMC segment proposals; testing alternative proposal methods would broaden impact.  
- Overall assessment: solid, relevant contribution with minor shortcomings; recommendation to accept.
