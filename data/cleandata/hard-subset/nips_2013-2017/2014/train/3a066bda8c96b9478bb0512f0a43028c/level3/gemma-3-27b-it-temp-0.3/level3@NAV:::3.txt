## Review of “Learning From Weakly Supervised Data by The Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm”

This paper proposes a novel Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm for semantic labeling and object detection using weakly supervised data, specifically bounding box annotations. The core idea is to treat the “positiveness” of segment proposals (IoU with ground truth) as a continuous value and learn a classifier that predicts this value, rather than relying on a binary threshold. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.

The approach builds upon Support Vector Machines (SVMs) and extends them to handle continuous labels and latent variables when only bounding box supervision is available. The authors present both a basic e-SVM formulation for observed positiveness and a latent e-SVM for unobserved positiveness, utilizing a regularization term to stabilize the latent variable estimation. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in the object detection task where it outperforms existing approaches.

However, the paper suffers from significant shortcomings regarding originality and related work. While the idea of using a continuous “positiveness” value is reasonable, the algorithmic novelty is limited. The proposed latent e-SVM is essentially a weighted transductive SVM, a well-established technique in machine learning. Critically, the paper fails to cite foundational work on weighted SVMs (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Vapnik, Lapin et al.) and transductive SVMs, representing a major oversight. The imposed sum-constraint on weights, while potentially novel, wasn’t thoroughly investigated by the reviewer. 

Furthermore, the paper lacks sufficient justification for the chosen methodology. The authors should report standard baselines, such as regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression, to demonstrate the benefits of using a sum-constrained weighted SVM. The selection of regularization coefficients (λW and λR) also lacks detail. The title and abstract are misleading, claiming unit-interval targets are less informative than binary labels, which is not a central contribution. The paper spends excessive space on minor algorithmic variations while neglecting crucial details about feature engineering.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Achieving state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 is significant and of high interest to the community.
*   The approach of directly modeling the “positiveness” value is a reasonable and potentially useful extension to existing methods.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited algorithmic novelty and significant omissions in related work.
*   Lack of justification for the chosen methodology and insufficient baseline comparisons.
*   Misleading title and abstract.
*   Uneven focus and exposition.



**Overall Recommendation:**

Reject with request for major revisions. The paper has potential, but the significant deficiencies in related work and methodological justification must be addressed. A substantial rewrite, focusing on clearly differentiating the contribution from existing literature and providing a more thorough experimental evaluation, is required. This work may be more suitable for a computer vision venue. The quality score is contingent on the authors adequately addressing the prior-work relations.
