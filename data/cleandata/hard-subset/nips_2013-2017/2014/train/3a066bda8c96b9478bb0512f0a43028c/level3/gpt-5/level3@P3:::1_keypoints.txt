- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models positiveness in [0,1] and minimizes expected hinge loss, reducible to standard SVMs by duplicating weighted pseudo-instances.  
- Latent e-SVM treats per-instance positiveness u as latent with a convex KL regularizer toward bounding-box IoU estimate u_bb, optimized by alternating minimization.  
- Applied to segment proposal scoring, semantic segmentation (ranking/selecting CPMC segments), and detection (class-specific spatial confidence maps combined with RCNN features).  
- Introduces two segment-level evaluation metrics: TAPC (thresholded AP averaged over IoU thresholds) and NDCG.  
- Empirical: outperforms SVC/SVR on segment scoring, yields competitive segmentation, and improves RCNN mAP by 1.2–1.5 points (state-of-the-art on VOC07 at the time).  
- Conceptually between strong supervision and MIL: allows fractional positiveness and does not assume pure positive proposals exist.  
- Distinct from weighted SVMs by weighting labels (positive and negative hinge terms) rather than samples.  
- Latent variant similar to latent SVM but avoids degeneracy by keeping u continuous and regularized.  
- Technical soundness: expected hinge loss is a coherent surrogate; reduction to off-the-shelf SVM solvers is practical.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough across tasks and metrics; reported improvements are consistent and meaningful.  
- Key limitations: choice g(u)=u is ad hoc with no calibration study; KL prior assumption (u ≈ IoU with bbox) may be dataset/category dependent.  
- Missing analyses: sensitivity to λ_R, initialization u_bb, number of alternations, convergence behavior, and computational/runtime cost.  
- Lacks comparison to prior “probabilistic-label SVM” or soft-label/label-distribution learning methods.  
- Clarity: well-written with clear diagrams and derivations; needs cleaner notation and an implementation recipe for reproducibility; report memory/runtime for pixel-level operations.  
- Originality: moderate — clean expected-margin SVM for soft labels and tailored latent KL prior are useful, but related prior work exists.  
- Significance: high for weakly supervised visual learning; practical, general, and easily integrated into existing pipelines.  
- Strengths: principled soft-label SVM, convex latent regularizer, careful evaluation including new metrics, demonstrated downstream gains, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: justify or learn g(·), analyze λ_R and u_bb sensitivity (annealing/early stopping), compare to related soft-label methods, discuss computation, and explore richer priors on u.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to g(u) and nonlinear mappings, convergence criteria and typical alternations, performance with other proposal generators/features, and exact reduction to standard SVM solvers.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses important weak-supervision problem, solid empirical gains, clear/reproducible method, and likely broad adoption.  
- Cons for acceptance: modest theoretical novelty, heuristic design choices (g, KL prior), and limited analysis of optimization/compute costs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
