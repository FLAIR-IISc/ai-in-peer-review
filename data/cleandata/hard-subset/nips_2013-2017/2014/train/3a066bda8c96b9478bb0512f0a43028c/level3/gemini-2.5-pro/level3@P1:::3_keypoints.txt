- Paper proposes e-SVM: treat labels as continuous positiveness scores (IoU in [0,1]) leading to a weighted SVM where each sample contributes to positive and negative slack with weights u_i and 1-u_i.  
- Introduces latent e-SVM for unknown positiveness scores (e.g., from bounding boxes), optimized iteratively akin to TSVMs.  
- Applies method to semantic segmentation and object detection; builds a spatial confidence map feature that, when combined with R-CNN, yields state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical improvement over R-CNN on VOC07, demonstrating practical impact.  
- Addresses important middle ground of supervision strength (bounding boxes vs. pixel labels), a relevant real-world setting.  
- Effective system design: spatial confidence map successfully integrates segment scores into window-based detection.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited algorithmic originality and missing citations: fails to adequately position against prior weighted SVM, label-proportion, and TSVM work (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Vapnik; Lapin et al.).  
- Insufficient justification for e-SVM over standard alternatives; lacks comparisons to natural [0,1] regression baselines (e.g., logistic or beta regression).  
- Clarity/exposition issues: imprecise title/abstract, too little detail on feature engineering (second-order pooling, spatial confidence map), and missing hyperparameter selection procedure.  

Overall recommendation:
- Valuable engineering and strong results but novelty is unclear due to literature gaps; recommend acceptance only with a major revision to add related work, stronger baselines, and clearer exposition.  

Minor comments:
- Notation inconsistencies (wTxi + b simplification) and nonstandard regularizer notation; weak claim about bounded outputs not excluding regression; provide details on additive kernel approximation.
