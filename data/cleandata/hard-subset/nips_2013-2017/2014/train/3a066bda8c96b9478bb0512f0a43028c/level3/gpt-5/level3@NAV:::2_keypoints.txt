- Proposal: Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) replacing hinge loss with its expectation under per-example positiveness µi, yielding u·ℓ+ + (1−u)·ℓ−.  
- Latent variant: Treats u as latent and alternates SVM fitting with u updates using a convex KL regularizer anchored to IoU-based initialization.  
- Empirical results: Evaluated on segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection, showing consistent gains over SVC/SVR and modest (1–1.5 AP) improvements on PASCAL.  
- Relation to prior work: Core formulation is equivalent to instance-weighted/ cost-sensitive SVMs and training on soft labels—needs explicit citation and alignment with WSVM literature.  
- Latent method novelty: Latent alternating scheme is straightforward and incremental relative to existing approaches.  
- Optimization concerns: Latent model is non-convex with no convergence analysis or excess-risk guarantees provided.  
- Regularization concern: Heavy KL regularization likely keeps u close to initialization; paper should report distance moved by u (KL, L2, or histogram of |u−u_bb|) and sensitivity to λR.  
- Incorrect claim: Statement that bounded targets make regression “non-standard” is wrong; logistic/softmax regression on bounded probabilities is standard and should be compared.  
- Missing comparisons: Should compare to logistic/softmax with calibrated probabilities and to existing WSVM/instance-weighted SVM implementations.  
- Clarity and presentation: Generally clear and organized, but fix abstract typo (“continues”→“continuous”), notation glitches (Eq.1), and temper solver-novelty claims.  
- Implementation detail: Explicitly state how per-example weights are implemented in LibLinear.  
- Originality: Limited — methodological contribution largely overlaps with example-dependent cost SVMs and soft-label training.  
- Significance: Practically useful for CV pipelines with meaningful AP gains, but methodological impact is modest without clearer novelty or theoretical analysis.  
- Acceptance pros: Simple, easy to implement, broad positive empirical results, and useful integration for detection.  
- Acceptance cons: Unclear novelty vs WSVM, no theoretical analysis of latent optimization, heavy regularization limits learning of u, and limited ablation/comparisons.
