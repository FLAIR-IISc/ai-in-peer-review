- Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous training labels (positiveness) by incorporating per-example weights into the loss.
- Proposes a latent-variable extension of e-SVM for cases where positiveness values are unobserved.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Introduces new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment classification.
- Algorithm is flexible and generalizable across segment proposal detectors, image features, and classifiers.
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the non-convex optimization and convergence guarantees for the latent-variable extension.
- Novelty is unclear relative to existing instance-weighted SVMs (e.g., SVMlight); distinctions are insufficiently clarified.
- Strong regularization to constrain latent variables raises concerns about robustness and generalizability; sensitivity to regularization is not explored.
- Empirical gains over SVC and SVR baselines are demonstrated (e.g., ~1.5% AP improvement).
- Minor errors: incorrect claim about logistic regression being unbounded and a typo ("continues" vs. "continuous") in the abstract.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on theoretical analysis and clearer differentiation from prior work.
- Pros: addresses a relevant weakly supervised learning problem, shows clear empirical improvements, and offers a broadly useful, adaptable method.
- Cons: theoretical gaps, insufficiently established novelty, and potential robustness issues due to strong regularization.
