- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) minimizing expected hinge loss under soft labels u (g(u)=u) and a latent e-SVM alternating w and u with a convex KL regularizer; applied to segment scoring, semantic labeling, and object detection with +1.5 mAP on VOC07.  
- Positioning: problem sits between fully supervised segmentation and weak/MIL; basic e-SVM relates to instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs and probability-target regression; latent e-SVM resembles transductive/semi-supervised SVMs.  
- Missing related work: must cite/discuss Vapnik transductive inference, Joachims (1999) TSVM, Bennett & Demiriz (1999) S3VM, Sindhwani & Keerthi (2006), Collobert et al. (2006), and broader weighted SVM literature; clarify relation to SVM+.  
- Strength: addresses IoU-valued and box-supervision settings with a simple, efficient SVM formulation solvable by off-the-shelf solvers.  
- Strength: clear, useful instantiation for semantic labeling and detection via a spatial confidence map feature that complements CNN features.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains over SVC/SVR for segment scoring and competitive/state-of-the-art VOC07 detection when combined with RCNN.  
- Strength: latent formulation is blockwise convex and stabilized with a principled convex KL regularizer.  
- Weakness: limited originality — essentially reweighting hinge losses by soft labels; latent version is close to weighted transductive/S3VM with entropy/KL regularization.  
- Weakness: misleading claim that unit-interval targets are “less informative” than binary labels; needs rephrasing and clarification of supervision signal.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to standard probabilistic/regression baselines for [0,1] targets (logistic/Beta regression, isotonic/ordinal regression, calibrated SVMs) to justify the objective.  
- Weakness: insufficient transparency on hyperparameters and optimization (selection of λW, λR, convergence criteria, number of alternations, u-update details) and no sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: exposition omits critical engineering choices and ablations (segment features, pooling, proposal generation, spatial-map design), hindering reproducibility.  
- Quality: technically sound and experimentally relevant, but omission of key related work and baselines weakens novelty and necessity claims.  
- Clarity: overall readable but title/abstract should better reflect the core idea (hinge-loss with soft labels and latent transductive extension) and avoid misleading statements; add implementation details for reproduction.  
- Originality: moderate — possible novelty if a sum-constraint on weights is enforced (needs highlighting/evaluation); spatial confidence map is useful but incremental.  
- Significance: high practical interest due to complementary gains with CNN detectors and generality of the drop-in SVM formulation.  
- Requested experiments: add logistic/Beta/isotonic/ordinal regression baselines, calibrated SVMs, TSVM/S3VM comparisons, ablations for grid size/pooling/encoding/CPMC vs superpixels, effect of λR=0, and hyperparameter sensitivity plots.  
- Requested details: report how λW and λR were chosen, provide u-update/optimization specifics, initialization, convergence thresholds, and number of alternations.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos, clarify notation (Eqns, Bern(ui)), define TAPC, state whether g(u)=u is fixed or learned, and proofread/complete references.  
- Recommendation: Accept after revision conditional on adding missing prior-work discussion, clarity fixes, and the requested baselines and hyperparameter/implementation details.  
- Pros: simple, general, empirically strong, practical impact, and efficient optimization with standard solvers.  
- Cons: limited algorithmic novelty and missing baselines/experimental and implementation details.
