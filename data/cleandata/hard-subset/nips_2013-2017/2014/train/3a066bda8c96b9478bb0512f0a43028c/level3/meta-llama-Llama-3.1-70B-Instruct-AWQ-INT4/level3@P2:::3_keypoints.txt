- Proposes e-SVM to learn from weak supervision where labels are continuous "positiveness" scores, applied to segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.  
- Builds on weakly supervised learning, multiple instance learning, and weighted SVMs but omits key prior works (Suykens et al., Yang et al., Lapin et al.); latent e-SVM resembles a weighted transductive SVM.  
- Strength: novel practical approach for weak supervision with demonstrated effectiveness on segmentation and detection tasks.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Weakness: limited algorithmic novelty overall and several pertinent prior works are not cited.  
- Weakness: the imposed sum constraint on weights may be new but is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: paper devotes excessive space to minor algorithmic variations and insufficient detail to critical feature-engineering.  
- Weakness: title and abstract mislead about unit-interval targets being less informative than binary labels.  
- Quality: well-written with thorough experiments, but lacks crucial references and relies on substantial engineering for detection.  
- Quality: choice of regularization coefficients (λW and λR) is not adequately explained.  
- Clarity: generally organized, but title/abstract and feature-engineering descriptions need improvement.  
- Originality: limited in algorithmic novelty despite proposing a useful weak-supervision approach.  
- Significance: notable community-relevant result due to SOTA detection performance.  
- Recommendation: accept after revision to address prior-work relations and potentially better targeted to a computer-vision venue.
