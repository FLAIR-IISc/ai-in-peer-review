- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle weakly supervised data with continuous labels in [0,1].
- Demonstrates effectiveness on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.
- Core idea: weight each example in the loss by its positiveness (continuous 0–1), with an extension to latent/unobserved per-example weights.
- Empirically outperforms SVC and SVR on segment proposal classification.
- Paper is well-written and experiments are thorough and convincing.
- Concern: weighting examples in the loss is not novel (e.g., example-dependent costs in SVMlight); authors must clarify differences from prior work.
- Latent-weight extension leads to non-convex optimization, but no theoretical analysis or discussion of optimization implications is provided.
- Experiments use heavy regularization, suggesting learned weights stay near initial values; authors should analyze regularization effects.
- Incorrect claim: labeling in [0,1] is not inherently a non-standard regression problem (e.g., logistic regression is bounded to [0,1]).
- Overall contribution to computer vision is significant, but novelty and theory are weak points.
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: handles weak supervision, strong empirical results on two major CV tasks, clear writing and thorough experiments.
- Arguments for rejection: unclear novelty versus prior work, lack of theoretical analysis for non-convex optimization, and an incorrect claim about bounded regression.
