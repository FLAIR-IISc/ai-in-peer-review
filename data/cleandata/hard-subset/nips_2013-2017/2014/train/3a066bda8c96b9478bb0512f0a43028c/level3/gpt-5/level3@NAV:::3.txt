Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for training with “positiveness” targets in [0,1], instantiated as IoU of candidate segments with ground-truth. The loss treats the label as Bernoulli with mean ui and optimizes the expected hinge loss; with only bounding boxes, ui becomes latent and is regularized toward IoU with the box via a KL term, yielding an alternating convex optimization. The method is used for segment confidence prediction, semantic segmentation, and as class-specific spatial confidence features that, when concatenated with RCNN features, yield state-of-the-art VOC07 detection. Conceptually, the basic model is close to instance-weighted SVMs, and the latent variant is essentially a weighted transductive SVM (TSVM) with per-example soft labels and a regularizer. However, core WSVM/TSVM literature is not cited (e.g., Suykens et al. on weighted LS-SVM, Yang et al. on WSVM, Vapnik/Joachims on TSVM, Lapin et al. on SVM+/weighted SVM).

Strengths:
- Clear and practical formulation that reduces to standard SVM solvers; straightforward latent extension.
- Strong empirical results; improving RCNN on VOC07 with conventional machinery is noteworthy and of broad interest.
- Sensible use of IoU-regression and spatial pooling, with ablations showing gains over SVC/SVR on segment scoring.

Weaknesses:
- Originality is limited; the latent e-SVM is not sufficiently differentiated from weighted TSVM and related soft-label SVMs. The sum-to-one weighting over labels per example may be the only novelty; please articulate and justify this (the paper currently does not).
- Missing crucial references (WSVM, TSVM, SVM+). Please position e-SVM against these formally.
- Baselines/methodology: include standard probabilistic baselines for unit-interval targets, e.g., regularized logistic regression with cross-entropy on [0,1] targets, isotonic or beta-regression, and report if the sum-constrained weighting is necessary versus unconstrained weights.
- Hyperparameters: specify how λW and λR are chosen (search ranges, validation protocol), sensitivity analyses, and convergence criteria/initialization for the alternating scheme.
- Clarity: The title/abstract do not convey the core contribution and suggest that unit-interval targets are “less informative” than binary labels—this is misleading; please revise. The exposition over-emphasizes small algorithmic variations while eliding important engineering choices (features, proposal mechanisms, confidence-map design).
- Prior-work section conflates weak supervision with MIL; please sharpen the distinction and cite relevant soft-label and transductive formulations.

Minor comments: fix typos/notation (e.g., lines 19, 94, 98, 350); ensure consistent symbols; add citations for TAPC/NDCG usage.

Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on a thorough rewrite and proper positioning relative to WSVM/TSVM. The empirical significance justifies publication; the paper may be even better received at a computer-vision venue. Pros: strong results, practicality, relevance. Cons: limited novelty, missing references, clarity.
