- Proposal: Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning when only "positiveness" scores, not binary labels, are available, applied to semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Motivation: Problem is important and well-motivated with a clear, concise introduction.  
- Related work gap: Missing crucial citations, notably literature on weighted SVM and transductive SVM.  
- Novelty concern: Algorithmic novelty is limited; latent e-SVM is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and not sufficiently differentiated from prior art.  
- Sum-constraint: The sum-constraint on weights might be novel, but the reviewer did not verify this thoroughly.  
- Misleading claim: Claim that unit-interval targets are less informative than binary labels is misleading; title and abstract do not clearly convey the core contribution.  
- Strong empirical results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on VOC 2007 and notably beats recent CNN results with a conventional approach.  
- Engineering effort: Object detection system involves substantial engineering, which is within norms and of community interest.  
- Missing baselines: Fails to report standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression) to justify the chosen approach.  
- Hyperparameter details: Lacks detail on how regularization coefficients (λW and λR) were selected.  
- Emphasis imbalance: Too much space devoted to minor algorithmic variations and too little to critical feature-engineering details.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper has unclear writing in places, confusing notation, typographical errors, and missing key references.  
- Revision requested: Authors should rewrite to clarify core contribution, better situate the work in prior literature, and detail algorithmic novelty.  
- Experimental fixes: Authors should report standard baselines and provide details on regularization and other implementation choices.  
- Recommendation: Recommend publication after rewriting to address concerns; paper may be better suited to a computer-vision venue.
