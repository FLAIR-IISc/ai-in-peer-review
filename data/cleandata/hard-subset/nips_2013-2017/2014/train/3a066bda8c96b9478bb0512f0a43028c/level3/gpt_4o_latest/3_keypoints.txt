- Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for training with continuous bounded labels, applied to segment proposal classification.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR baselines in segment proposal classification.  
- Combining e-SVM features with RCNN yields notable improvements in object detection.  
- Focus on weakly supervised learning is timely and reduces reliance on pixel-level annotations.  
- Introduces new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) that add value to the field.  
- Limited originality: primarily an extension of existing SVM frameworks rather than a fundamentally new concept.  
- Missing citations to key related work on weighted and transductive SVMs, weakening contextual positioning.  
- Clarity issues: title and abstract do not clearly convey main contributions, and minor algorithmic variations are overemphasized.  
- Feature engineering is underexplained, hindering reproducibility.  
- Baseline comparisons and parameter selection strategies are inadequately discussed, reducing experimental rigor.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding missing references, improving clarity (title/abstract/features), detailing baselines/parameter choices, and fixing typos and unclear abbreviations; better suited to a vision conference than NIPS.
