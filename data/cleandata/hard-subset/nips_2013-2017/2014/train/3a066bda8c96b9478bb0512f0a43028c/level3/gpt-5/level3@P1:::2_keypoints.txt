- Proposal: Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), an expected hinge-loss SVM for examples with soft “positiveness” scores u ∈ [0,1] using weights g(u) (set to u in experiments).  
- Objective: Each example contributes both +1 and −1 hinge terms weighted by g(u) and 1−g(u), solvable with standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Weak supervision: Extends to latent u with a convex proximity regularizer to an initial estimate and optimized by alternating minimization.  
- Application: Instantiated for segment proposal scoring and used to build spatial confidence map features concatenated with RCNN features for object detection.  
- Metrics: Proposes two task-agnostic segment-scoring metrics, TAPC and NDCG, to decouple evaluation from downstream tasks.  
- Empirical results: e-SVM outperforms SVC with thresholding, slightly improves over SVR for segment scoring, comparable for semantic segmentation, and adds +1.2–1.5 mAP to RCNN on VOC07.  
- Relation to prior work: Method is closely related to soft-label/fuzzy SVMs, weighted/instance-dependent-cost SVMs, and can be implemented by duplicating examples with weights; latent extension resembles latent SVM with added convex regularizer.  
- Novelty concern: Core idea is incremental — expected hinge loss under soft labels overlaps substantially with existing soft-label and weighted SVM formulations.  
- Missing positioning: Paper should more clearly position and explain differences from WSVM, soft-label SVMs, and probabilistic-label SVM variants.  
- Nonconvexity: Latent optimization is non-convex; no analysis of convergence properties, conditions, or sensitivity provided.  
- Regularizer strength: Regularizer on u may be strong; unclear whether learned u significantly deviates from initialization without ablation.  
- Baseline omissions: Lacks comparison to natural baselines like cross-entropy/logistic regression with soft labels and example-weighted SVM variants.  
- Ablation needs: Missing experiments fixing u, varying λR, testing alternative g(u), and sensitivity to initialization and proposal quality.  
- Clarity issues: Minor typos, inconsistent notation in objective, and incomplete references; loss presentation could be streamlined.  
- Quality assessment: Experiments are broadly sound and practical gains are shown, but important ablations and baselines are missing.  
- Originality assessment: Moderate — practical engineering contributions (spatial confidence map) but limited theoretical novelty.  
- Significance assessment: Moderate — easy-to-adopt recipe with measurable detection gains but limited theoretical impact.  
- Suggestions: Clarify equivalences to existing methods, add logistic and weighted-SVM baselines, include λR/init ablations, analyze convergence/runtime, and fix writing/notation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Simple, general technique with practical benefits, empirical gains in detection, and useful evaluation metrics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited novelty, missing key baselines/ablations, some inaccurate claims, and insufficient theoretical/algorithmic analysis.
