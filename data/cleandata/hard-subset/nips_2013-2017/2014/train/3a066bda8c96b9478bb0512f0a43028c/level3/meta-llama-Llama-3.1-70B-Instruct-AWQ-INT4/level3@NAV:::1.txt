**Paper Review: Learning From Weakly Supervised Data by The Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm**

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle continuous attributes of positive instances via soft weighting. The e-SVM algorithm is applied to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, where only bounding box annotations are available. The authors validate their approach on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, achieving promising results.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision, which is a common challenge in computer vision tasks. The authors draw connections to multiple instance learning (MIL) and weighted SVMs, highlighting the differences between their approach and existing methods.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective algorithm for learning from weakly supervised data.
* The authors provide a clear and well-motivated presentation of their approach.
* The experiments are interesting and well-executed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the e-SVM algorithm on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.

Weaknesses:

* The technical content is an extension of multiple instance learning by replacing binary positives with soft weights, which may not be entirely novel.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations and potential pitfalls of the e-SVM algorithm.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The authors provide sufficient details for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel combination of familiar techniques, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from previous contributions and provide adequate references to related work.

**Significance**

The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The e-SVM algorithm has the potential to advance the state of the art in visual learning under weak supervision.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective algorithm for learning from weakly supervised data.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the e-SVM algorithm on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.

Con:

* The technical content may not be entirely novel, as it builds upon existing multiple instance learning approaches.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations and potential pitfalls of the e-SVM algorithm.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication at NIPS, as it makes a significant contribution to the field of visual learning under weak supervision. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated presentation of their approach, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the e-SVM algorithm on challenging tasks.
