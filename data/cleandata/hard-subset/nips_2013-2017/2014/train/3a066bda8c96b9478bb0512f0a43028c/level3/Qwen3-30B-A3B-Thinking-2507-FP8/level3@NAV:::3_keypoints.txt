- Proposes an "expectation loss SVM" (e-SVM) for continuous [0,1] labels and a latent e-SVM for weak supervision; reports SOTA on PASCAL VOC 2007 (60.0 mAP).  
- Strength: conceptually simple, effective approach addressing a practically important problem of bounded-interval labels.  
- Strength: well-motivated applications to semantic segmentation and object detection with strong empirical results.  
- Major weakness: novelty is limited—e-SVM is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and not clearly differentiated from prior work (Suykens, Vapnik, Lapin et al.).  
- Missing foundational citations on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs, undermining the novelty claim.  
- The claimed novelty of the sum-constraint on weights is not thoroughly justified or compared to existing methods.  
- Quality issue: title/abstract claim that unit-interval targets are "less informative than binary labels" is unsupported by evidence.  
- Methodological details are lacking, notably hyperparameter selection (λW, λR) and critical feature engineering steps.  
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants while omitting important baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression).  
- Although results are compelling, the paper needs substantial revision to situate the work in literature, clarify contributions, and add methodological details.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance if missing references and methodological clarifications are addressed; likely better suited for a computer vision venue than a general AI conference.
