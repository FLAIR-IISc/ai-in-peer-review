- Proposes e-SVM to learn from continuous "positiveness" labels (e.g., IoU) instead of binarizing them.  
- Motivated by reducing pixel-level annotation costs using segment proposals and bounding boxes.  
- Distinguishes problem setting from MIL and weighted SVMs; most proposals are mixed foreground/background so MIL assumptions fail.  
- Models labels as Bernoulli random variables with success probability from observed positiveness.  
- Defines an expectation loss (expected label loss) that can be optimized with standard SVM solvers.  
- Introduces a latent-variable extension for weak supervision with iterative optimization.  
- Uses a KL-divergence-based regularizer to prevent label collapse to 0 or 1 (heuristic design acknowledged).  
- Regularization term (Eq.3) lacks strong theoretical motivation and alternatives are not explored.  
- Scalability to very large datasets or highly cluttered scenes with many overlapping objects is not fully addressed.  
- Object detection results are strong (1.5% AP gain over RCNN on PASCAL VOC 2007) but per-category analysis is missing.  
- Experimental validation is thorough: improves over SVC/SVR on TAPC, NDCG and standard segmentation metrics.  
- Provides sufficient implementation detail and pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Technical formulation is elegant and represents a novel extension of SVM theory for continuous labels.  
- Limited theoretical analysis of optimization convergence properties.  
- Overall significance is high for weakly supervised computer vision; reviewer recommends acceptance.
