- Proposes e-SVM for continuous "positiveness" labels in [0,1], with latent extension for unobserved positiveness.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art PASCAL VOC 2007 detection (60.0% AP, +1.5% over R-CNN) and applicability to segmentation and weak supervision.  
- Strength: compelling empirical improvements, especially on object detection over RCNN.  
- Strength: latent e-SVM is well-motivated for weakly supervised pixel/box-labeled vision tasks.  
- Strength: clear framework for applying the method to segmentation and detection.  
- Weakness (originality): limited algorithmic novelty—essentially a weighted SVM with g(u)=u and a weighted transductive SVM latent extension.  
- Weakness (related work): fails to cite key weighted SVM and transductive SVM literature (e.g., Suykens, Lapin, Yang, Vapnik).  
- Weakness (misleading claim): abstract claim that unit-interval targets are less informative than binary labels is misleading given results showing continuous labels help.  
- Weakness (methodology): lacks justification why sum-constrained weighted SVM is superior to standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression); no baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness (hyperparameters): hyperparameters (λW, λR) are not explained and selection procedure is unclear.  
- Weakness (clarity): title/abstract do not clearly convey core contribution and paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants while omitting feature/implementation details.  
- Weakness (technical accuracy): insufficient differentiation from prior transductive methods and unclear explanation of why MIL methods don’t apply.  
- Weakness (presentation): missing foundational references, plus several typographical and notation errors.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance—requires addressing citations, justifying sum-constraint with baselines, clarifying hyperparameter selection, relating latent e-SVM to transductive learning, and fixing misleading abstract claims.
