- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous "positiveness" labels (e.g., IoU) by weighting SVM losses with positiveness scores.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision where positiveness values are unobserved, resembling transductive SVM.  
- Applied to semantic segmentation and object detection, reporting state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 when augmenting a strong CNN detector.  
- Major empirical contribution: strong results using an SVM-based framework that outperform contemporary deep methods.  
- Problem formulation for unit-interval/IoU supervision is well-motivated for weakly supervised vision tasks.  
- Critical originality concern: e-SVM and latent e-SVM are essentially weighted SVM and transductive SVM reformulations.  
- Fails to cite and discuss substantial prior work (e.g., Suykens et al., Vapnik, Lapin et al.), undermining novelty claims.  
- Experimental methodology incomplete: missing standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression) to justify the SVM choice.  
- Reproducibility harmed by missing crucial details on regularization hyperparameter selection.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract do not clearly present core contribution and make questionable claims about informativeness of unit-interval targets.  
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants and underreports feature engineering details that likely drive success.  
- Recommendation: not acceptable in current form; major revision required to contextualize prior work, improve clarity, add baselines, and provide experimental details; possibly better suited for a CV venue.
