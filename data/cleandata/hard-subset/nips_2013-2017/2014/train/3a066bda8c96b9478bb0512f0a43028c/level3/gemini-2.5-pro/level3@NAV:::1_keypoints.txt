- Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous-valued "positiveness" labels instead of binary labels.  
- Core idea: treat hinge loss as an expectation over a latent binary label weighted by observed positiveness (e.g., IoU).  
- Conceptually extends weighted SVMs and Multiple Instance Learning by using soft, continuous affiliation instead of binary bag membership.  
- Applied to weakly supervised visual learning: trains segment classifiers from bounding-box annotations using positiveness scores as latent variables.  
- Significance: addresses reducing reliance on expensive pixel-level annotations, a practical problem in computer vision.  
- Technical quality: method is sound with a clear formulation.  
- Experimental quality: comprehensive evaluations on semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Empirical result: outperforms baselines like SVC and SVR.  
- Practical impact: augmenting R-CNN with e-SVM features yields a non-trivial boost on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally clear, well-motivated, and logically organized.  
- Originality: a novel, well-motivated extension of existing techniques rather than a paradigm shift.  
- Weaknesses: no major flaws noted; contribution is incremental but complete, impactful, and generalizable.
