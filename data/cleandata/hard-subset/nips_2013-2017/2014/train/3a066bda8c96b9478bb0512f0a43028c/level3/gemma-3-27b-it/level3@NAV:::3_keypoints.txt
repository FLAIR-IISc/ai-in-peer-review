- Paper proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for weakly supervised semantic labeling and object detection using weighted SVM loss based on continuous “positiveness” (e.g., IoU).
- Extends weighting idea to a latent-variable setting when positiveness is unobserved and reports state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Builds on standard SVMs and segment proposals, attempting to bridge fully supervised and weakly supervised learning by modeling proposal confidence.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited — weighting samples by correctness is intuitive and not clearly new.
- Crucial related work on weighted SVMs and transductive/instance-weighting (e.g., Suykens, Yang, Lapin, Vapnik) is missing and must be cited.
- The sum-constraint on weights is introduced but not well justified or thoroughly investigated.
- The latent e-SVM appears largely equivalent to weighted transductive SVMs, lacking clear differentiation from prior methods.
- Strong empirical contribution: achieves competitive/state-of-the-art object detection and segmentation without deep learning, which is practically significant.
- Authors should compare to standard regularized logistic regression for unit-interval targets as a baseline.
- More detail is needed on how regularization coefficients (λW and λR) were chosen and their impact.
- Paper suffers from clarity issues: title/abstract overclaim contributions and mischaracterize unit-interval targets versus binary labels.
- Exposition is uneven, focusing on minor algorithmic variants while omitting critical feature-engineering and implementation details; several typos present.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results and potential impact in weakly supervised learning.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, incomplete related work, clarity problems, and insufficient methodological justification.
- Recommendation: Reject with request for major revisions — must address missing literature, clarify novelty, improve justification and clarity; possibly better suited for a computer-vision venue.
