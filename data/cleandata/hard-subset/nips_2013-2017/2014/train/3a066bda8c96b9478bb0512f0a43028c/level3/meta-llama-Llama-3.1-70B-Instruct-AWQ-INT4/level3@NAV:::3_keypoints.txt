- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for weakly supervised data with continuous bounded targets.  
- Designed for computer vision tasks like semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.  
- e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR in accuracy and robustness according to experiments.  
- Strength: handles weak supervision well and is clearly explained with well-presented experimental results.  
- Limitation: limited algorithmic novelty and lack of citation/engagement with pertinent prior work.  
- Needs a more comprehensive related-work review and clearer differentiation from existing methods.  
- Experimental details lacking: hyperparameter settings and regularization coefficient choices need more detail.  
- Clarity issues: title and abstract do not clearly convey the core contribution and a claim about unit-interval targets is misleading.  
- Paper focuses too much on minor algorithmic variations and too little on critical feature-engineering details.  
- Recommendation: publish after revision to address related work, clarity, and experimental detail shortcomings.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
