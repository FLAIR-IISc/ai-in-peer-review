- Proposes e-SVM to learn from continuous "positiveness" values and a latent variant for unobserved positiveness (e.g., bounding boxes).
- Reports state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 (60.0 AP) using conventional features, and useful semantic segmentation results.
- Experimental design and implementation (spatial confidence map) are well-conceived and show consistent improvements over SVC/SVR baselines.
- Core idea—weighting SVM loss with continuous labels—is not sufficiently novel and overlaps with weighted SVM and transductive learning literature.
- Fails to cite or clearly differentiate from key prior work (Suykens et al. 2002; Yang et al. 2008; Lapin et al. 2014).
- The "sum constraint" on weights is presented as novel but appears to be a specific instantiation of existing weighted SVM approaches.
- Title and abstract contain misleading claims (asserting unit-interval targets are less informative when results show the opposite).
- Paper emphasizes minor algorithmic variations over important feature-engineering details and contains notational inconsistencies.
- Lacks comparisons to standard unit-interval regression baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression) and other relevant methods.
- Hyperparameter selection (λW, λR) is inadequately justified and needs stronger motivation.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical value, practical framework for weak supervision, clear experiments, and a clever spatial feature.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, missing citations/differentiation, misleading claims, weak methodological justification, and unfocused exposition.
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending substantial revision to contextualize within weighted SVM literature and strengthen methodological comparisons.
- Required revisions: add comprehensive citations, clarify relationship to existing weighted/transductive SVMs, justify hyperparameters, include unit-interval regression baselines, and correct misleading title/abstract.
