- Introduces e-SVM: an expected-hinge loss using per-instance positiveness µi∈[0,1] and a latent e-SVM variant that jointly optimizes unobserved ui with a cross-entropy prior; applied to segmentation proposal ranking and RCNN detection (+1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC2007).  
- Formulation closely related to instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs, transductive/latent SVMs, and learning with soft/probabilistic targets; paper underdevelops positioning and missing relevant references.  
- The expected hinge is convex in w for fixed µ, the latent extension is bi-convex with a sensible cross-entropy regularizer, but convergence behavior, sensitivity to initialization ubb, and effect of λR are not analyzed.  
- Empirically shows consistent gains in proposal ranking and practically strong detection improvements, but lacks baselines isolating source of gains (e.g., RCNN+SVR/SVC confidence maps).  
- Key missing baselines/ablations: logistic regression with µ as soft targets, Brier/MSE/beta regression, comparisons using SVR/SVC-derived confidence maps, and hyperparameter/architecture ablations (λw, λR, g(u), spatial binning/pooling).  
- Reproducibility is weak: missing engineering/training details (feature specifics for confidence maps, hyperparameter search, class balancing, solver settings) and explicit mapping to standard SVM solvers (e.g., example duplication with weights).  
- Readable but overclaims novelty and sometimes implies soft [0,1] targets are less informative than binary labels; title/abstract should be rephrased to emphasize soft-label contributions.  
- Organization overemphasizes minor variations and underemphasizes critical implementation/feature details for the detection pipeline; notation inconsistent (u vs µ; g(u) fixed to identity).  
- Several typos and bibliography issues; unify terminology (e-SVM vs eSVM) and clarify “positiveness” → “soft label/IoU”.  
- Originality is moderate: the sum-constrained per-instance weighting (µ and 1−µ) is a neat practical twist but algorithmically incremental relative to weighted/latent SVM literature.  
- Practical significance is high due to notable VOC’07 mAP gains; methodological significance is modest without stronger baselines and clearer positioning.  
- Pros: simple convex formulation for soft labels; principled latent extension; strong empirical detection results; modular pipeline usable with different proposals and detectors.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs prior weighted/latent SVMs; missing key baselines and ablations; insufficient hyperparameter/feature detail; some overclaiming and clarity issues.  
- Requests for clarification: how λw and λR are selected (per-class or global, validation protocol) and sensitivity; full details to reproduce spatial confidence map (m, pooling, normalization, kernel params, sampling); compare to logistic with µ targets and RCNN+SVR maps; report convergence behavior and initialization sensitivity for latent e-SVM; justify g(u)=u and whether calibrations were tried.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos and bibliographic fields, explicitly state duplication/weighting implementation of expectation loss, and unify notation/terminology.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — incremental method but sound and practically impactful; requires stronger related work, baselines, ablations, and clearer exposition for full confidence.
