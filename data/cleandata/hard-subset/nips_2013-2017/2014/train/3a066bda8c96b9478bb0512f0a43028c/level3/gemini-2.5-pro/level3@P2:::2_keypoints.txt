- Summary: Proposes e-SVM that treats positiveness scores in [0,1] as expectations over latent binary labels, extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision, and shows empirical gains on segmentation and detection (including +1.5% mAP with R-CNN).

- Strength: Addresses important weakly supervised learning problem relevant to large-scale annotation costs.

- Strength: Strong empirical results and well-motivated evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG); outperforms SVC/SVR and improves detection performance.

- Strength: Method is simple, clearly presented, and implementable via standard SVM solvers with instance weights.

- Weakness: Novelty concern — e-SVM is mathematically equivalent to weighted SVM on a duplicated dataset; connection to long-standing WSVM implementations is insufficiently acknowledged.

- Weakness: Latent e-SVM optimization is treated superficially; no discussion of non-convexity, convergence guarantees, or quality of local minima.

- Weakness: Heavy KL regularization on latent u raises doubts whether latent variables are truly learned; sensitivity to regularization and changes in u are not analyzed.

- Weakness: Flawed motivation against SVR — bounded targets do not invalidate regression approaches (e.g., logistic regression) and this weakens justification for e-SVM.

- For acceptance: Tackles a significant problem, delivers strong practical results, and offers a simple, usable method.

- For rejection: Core technical contribution may be a re-formulation of instance-weighted SVM lacking novelty.

- For rejection: Analysis of latent model and optimization is inadequate.

- Minor comment: Typo in abstract ("continues" -> "continuous").

- Conclusion: Empirically strong but major concerns about novelty and shallow analysis of latent optimization; acceptance contingent on clearer differentiation from WSVM and deeper analysis.
