This paper proposes the expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), which replaces binary labels with “positiveness” scores in [0,1] and optimizes the expectation of the hinge loss under a Bernoulli label model. With observed positiveness (e.g., IoU to pixel masks), e-SVM reduces to a convex surrogate that can be solved with standard linear SVM solvers by soft-weighting the positive and negative hinge terms. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), the positiveness is treated as latent and learned by alternating minimization of the classifier and the soft labels, regularized by a KL term that keeps the latent scores near the box-IoU initialization. The method is applied to segment proposal confidence prediction and then used to build spatial confidence map features that improve semantic segmentation and RCNN-based object detection on PASCAL VOC, yielding consistent gains over SVC/SVR and a reported +1.5 mAP on VOC07 detection.

Relation to prior work: The paper sits between MIL-style weak supervision and fully supervised segmentation. It is related to instance-weighted SVMs and “fuzzy/probabilistic-label” SVM variants that incorporate label uncertainty or soft labels; the authors argue their loss weights labels rather than examples and retains compatibility with off-the-shelf solvers. The application to segmentation/detection leverages standard proposal methods (CPMC, selective search) and deep features (RCNN), echoing trends in recent NIPS/CVPR work.

Quality: The formulation is sound and simple, the alternating scheme is convex in blocks, and experiments are well executed with nontrivial improvements. However, theory is limited (no generalization analysis or convergence guarantees beyond block-convexity), and ablations on the choice of g(u), λR, and the KL regularizer are sparse.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized. Some details (hyperparameters, stopping criteria, runtime, sensitivity to proposal quality) could be expanded. References need polishing.

Originality: The idea of expected hinge with soft labels is natural and close to prior fuzzy/uncertain-label SVMs; the latent extension with KL regularization is a practical twist. The contribution is incremental but well targeted.

Significance: Weak supervision for visual learning is important. The reported gains over strong baselines (SVR, RCNN) and the generality of the approach make this a useful contribution.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, simple method; compatible with standard SVM solvers
- Addresses an important weakly supervised setting between MIL and full supervision
- Solid empirical results on segmentation and detection (+1.5 mAP on VOC07)
- Practical latent formulation with effective regularization

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited novelty relative to fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVM literature
- Missing comparisons to alternative soft-label baselines (e.g., logistic with soft targets, calibrated margins)
- Sparse sensitivity analysis and theoretical guarantees
- Dependence on proposal quality not systematically evaluated
