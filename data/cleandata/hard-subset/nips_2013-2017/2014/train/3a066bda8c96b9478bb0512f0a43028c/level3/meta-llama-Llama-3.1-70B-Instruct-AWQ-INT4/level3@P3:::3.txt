This paper proposes a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that is devoted to the problems where only the "positiveness" instead of a binary label of each training sample is available. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm on two fundamental computer vision tasks: semantic segmentation and object detection.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem it addresses is important. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and the related work section is adequate. However, the reviewer is concerned that the algorithmic novelty of the paper is limited, and many pertinent prior works are not cited. Specifically, the literature on weighted SVM and transductive SVM is not referenced.

The proposed latent e-SVM is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and not sufficiently differentiated from prior art. The sum-constraint on weights may be novel, but the reviewer did not investigate thoroughly. The paper's claim that unit-interval targets are less informative than binary labels is misleading, and the title and abstract do not clearly convey the core contribution.

The paper's strengths include its strong results, which achieve state-of-the-art performance on VOC 2007. The authors' approach to beating recent CNN results with a conventional approach is notable and of high community interest. The object detection system involves substantial engineering, but it is within norms.

However, the paper has several weaknesses. The authors do not report standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression) to justify using sum-constrained weighted SVM. The paper lacks detail on how regularization coefficients (λW and λR) were chosen. The authors spend excessive space on small algorithmic variations and too little on critical feature-engineering details.

In terms of quality, the paper is missing crucial references, and the writing is not clear in some places. The notation is sometimes confusing, and there are several typographical errors.

To address the reviewer's concerns, the authors should rewrite the paper to clearly convey the core contribution and provide more detail on the algorithmic novelty. They should also provide more context and references to prior work, particularly in the area of weighted SVM and transductive SVM. Additionally, the authors should report standard baselines and provide more detail on the choice of regularization coefficients.

Overall, the reviewer recommends publishing the paper after rewriting, but it may fit better at a computer-vision venue.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC 2007
* Novel approach to beating recent CNN results with a conventional approach
* Object detection system involves substantial engineering

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited algorithmic novelty
* Missing crucial references, particularly in the area of weighted SVM and transductive SVM
* Lack of detail on the choice of regularization coefficients
* Excessive space spent on small algorithmic variations
* Typographical errors and confusing notation

**Recommendation:**

Publish after rewriting to address the reviewer's concerns. The paper may fit better at a computer-vision venue.
