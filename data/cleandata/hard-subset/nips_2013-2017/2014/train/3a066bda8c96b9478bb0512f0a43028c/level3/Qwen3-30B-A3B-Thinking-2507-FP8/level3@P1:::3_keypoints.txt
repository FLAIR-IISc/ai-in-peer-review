- Proposes an Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous “positiveness” values and a latent e-SVM when positiveness is unobserved.  
- Defines a loss that takes expectation over a random label, effectively weighting examples by their positiveness in [0,1].  
- Applies the method to segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, reporting state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC (RCNN+spatial features: 60.0% AP, +1.5%).  
- Empirical results are strong and show consistent improvements across metrics (TAPC, NDCG) and tasks.  
- Framework is general and potentially applicable beyond computer vision weakly supervised problems.  
- Major omission of key prior work: fails to cite established weighted SVM and transductive SVM literature (e.g., Suykens et al. 2004, Yang et al. 2007, Lapin et al. 2014).  
- Latent e-SVM closely resembles weighted/transductive SVM, so claimed novelty is questionable without proper differentiation.  
- Title and abstract are misleading by suggesting unit-interval targets are less informative while the method requires continuous targets.  
- Insufficient baseline comparisons, notably lacking direct interval-regression or regularized logistic regression baselines.  
- Crucial methodological details missing (selection of regularization coefficients λW and λR), harming reproducibility.  
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants and underreports critical feature-engineering details.  
- Detection gains come from conventional feature concatenation with RCNN rather than a novel detection architecture, limiting methodological significance.  
- Clarity and contextualization are weak; the paper should better explain how it differs from and improves upon transductive/weighted SVM frameworks.  
- Recommendation: substantial revision required to add missing references, clarify novelty, include stronger baselines and methodological details; current version not ready for acceptance.
