- Paper proposes e-SVM to regress continuous "positiveness" (IoU) values and a latent e-SVM for weakly supervised settings, applied to segmentation and object detection and integrated with R-CNN, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Strong empirical result: improves a deep R-CNN detector, showing classical SVM-based methods can complement deep features.
- Problem formulation (learning from [0,1] labels and weak supervision) is well-motivated and practically relevant.
- Major originality concern: e-SVM is essentially an instance-weighted SVM and related literature on weighted SVMs is not cited.
- Latent e-SVM closely resembles weighted transductive SVM (TSVM); foundational transductive and related works are omitted.
- Claim of novelty for weighting labels vs. data points is unconvincing; the purported sum-constraint novelty is not clearly argued or analyzed.
- Title and abstract are misleading about novelty and about the informativeness of [0,1] targets.
- Presentation overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants and underdescribes critical components like the spatial confidence map feature.
- Experiments lack important baselines (e.g., logistic regression, beta regression) for [0,1] targets.
- Hyperparameter selection (λW, λR) and other reproducibility details are omitted.
- Significant omission of prior references reduces scholarly quality.
- Requested fixes: thoroughly review/cite weighted SVM and TSVM literature and reframe contributions in that context.
- Requested fixes: revise title/abstract, expand feature and R-CNN integration details, and add standard regression baselines.
- Requested fixes: report how hyperparameters were chosen (e.g., cross-validation) and correct notation/terminology issues.
- Recommendation: promising empirical work but requires major revision to address novelty, prior work, clarity, and reproducibility before acceptance; may be better suited for a vision venue.
