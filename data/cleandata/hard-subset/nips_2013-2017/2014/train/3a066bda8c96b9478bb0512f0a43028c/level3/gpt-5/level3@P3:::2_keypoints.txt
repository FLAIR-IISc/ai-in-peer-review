- Proposes e-SVM: a hinge-loss variant where each example contributes positive and negative hinge terms weighted by per-example positiveness ui (g(ui)=ui used).
- Latent extension treats ui as unobserved, initialized from bounding-box IoU (ubb) and regularized with a convex KL term to stay near ubb.
- Optimization uses alternating minimization: linear SVM update for w and convex updates for u.
- Evaluated on segment proposal scoring, semantic segmentation (VOC2011), and detection (VOC2007) by concatenating spatial confidence maps with RCNN features.
- Empirical gains: outperforms SVC/SVR on proposal scoring and yields +1.2–1.5 mAP when combined with RCNN.
- Method closely relates to instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs, fuzzy SVMs, and soft-label/probabilistic-label SVMs.
- Latent formulation is similar to EM-like alternating optimization used in latent SVMs and MIL variants, with a strong entropy-style regularizer.
- Strength: clear motivation and well-described, easy-to-implement loss with off-the-shelf linear SVM solvers.
- Strength: thorough CV-focused empirical evaluation and a practical spatial confidence map integration for detectors.
- Strength: consistent improvements on segment scoring and meaningful detection gains when used as an extra cue.
- Strength: latent formulation is convex in each block and the KL regularizer is pragmatic.
- Weakness: novelty unclear—supervised e-SVM is effectively an example-dependent cost/weighted SVM and mathematically equivalent in standard implementations.
- Weakness: latent alternating optimization is non-convex with no theoretical analysis (monotonicity, convergence, identifiability).
- Weakness: strong KL regularizer may prevent u from meaningfully deviating from ubb, questioning the value of latent estimation.
- Weakness: missing critical baselines/ablations (fixed u=ubb, instance-weighted/fuzzy SVM with same u, logistic regression on soft labels).
- Weakness: incorrect claim that regression is “not standard” for [0,1] targets—logistic regression and SVR can handle bounded targets.
- Weakness: optimization details for u (closed-form vs gradient), runtime, and fairness of detection comparisons (splits, augmentation, kernel mapping) are not provided.
- Weakness: writing/typos and incomplete references (e.g., “continues”→“continuous”).
- Quality: promising and generally sound empirical results but weakened by lack of direct related baselines and latent-analysis.
- Clarity: mostly clear, but needs explicit equivalence/distinction to weighted SVM, u-update details, and sensitivity plots for λR and u deviation.
- Originality: limited—the core supervised objective is a special case of known formulations; latent KL-regularized block descent is straightforward.
- Significance: practical recipe useful for practitioners and shows detection impact, but scientific contribution is modest without deeper analysis.
- Requested revisions: position among weighted/fuzzy/soft-label SVMs, add missing baselines and ablations, report λR sensitivity and u movement, detail u optimization and convergence, and isolate detection contributions.
- Pros for acceptance: practical, easy to adopt, solid empirical gains, and relevant weak-supervision framing.
- Cons for acceptance: unclear novelty, latent extension lacks evidence of meaningful learning, and missing key baselines/ablations.
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject; could be acceptable if related-work positioning, missing baselines, and latent-weight analyses are addressed.
