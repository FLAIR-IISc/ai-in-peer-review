- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from weak supervision where only sample "positiveness" is available.
- e-SVM extends classical SVC and can be applied to learn segment classifiers under weak supervision.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explaining motivation, formulation, and experiments.
- Thorough related-work discussion that highlights differences from existing methods.
- Experimental results show effectiveness on tasks including semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Novelty is questionable because weighting examples in loss is not new and distinction of weighting labels vs. data points is unclear.
- The latent extension to handle weak supervision appears straightforward and offers limited theoretical or practical advantage.
- Lacks theoretical analysis, guarantees, or bounds on algorithm performance.
- Optimization for latent e-SVM is non-convex and the paper provides no convergence analysis.
- Minor typos and grammatical errors present.
- Overall promising with effective experiments but needs revisions to address novelty and theoretical/optimization concerns.
- Reviewer rating: 7/10; confidence: 8/10.
