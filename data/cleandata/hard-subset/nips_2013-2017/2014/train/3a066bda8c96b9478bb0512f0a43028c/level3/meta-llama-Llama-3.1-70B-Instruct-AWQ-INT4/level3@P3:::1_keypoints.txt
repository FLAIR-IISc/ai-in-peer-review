- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from weakly supervised data with continuous-valued labels by taking expectation of the loss over an unobserved label distribution.  
- Introduces latent e-SVM to handle completely unobserved continuous labels, using a regularization term to avoid local minima.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on visual segmentation/classification tasks and achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Extends classical SVM framework to continuous labels via latent-variable modeling — a novel contribution.  
- Presentation is clear with a thorough review and clear distinctions from prior weakly supervised and weighted SVM methods.  
- Technical content and algorithmic description are sound and well-detailed.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, well-executed, and evaluate robustness across metrics and datasets.  
- Strength: addresses an important, common real-world weak supervision problem in visual learning.  
- Strength: proposed approach is flexible and applicable to various segmentation/classification tasks.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation supports effectiveness and robustness.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis or discussion of computational complexity and resource requirements.  
- Weakness: could provide a more detailed discussion of method limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance (rating 9/10, confidence 8/10).
