- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous "positiveness" instead of binary labels.  
- Treats true binary label as latent Bernoulli variable with positive probability µ derived from positiveness.  
- Modifies SVM loss to incorporate example-specific positiveness and extends to bounding-box annotations via latent weights.  
- Evaluated on PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation, compared to SVC and SVR.  
- Introduces two evaluation metrics for segment-wise prediction: TAPC (Threshold Average Precision Curve) and NDCG.  
- e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR on the segmentation tasks.  
- Applied to object detection, improving RCNN performance using a new spatial confidence map feature.  
- Strength: novel handling of positiveness-only supervision.  
- Strength: clearly described method with empirical benefits over equal-weight training.  
- Strength: validated on two major computer vision tasks (segmentation and detection).  
- Weakness: approach may be equivalent to existing example-dependent cost methods (e.g., SVMlight), making novelty unclear.  
- Weakness: latent-weight extension is straightforward, optimization is non-convex, and lacks theoretical analysis.  
- Weakness: heavy regularization used, suggesting learned weights remain near initialization—authors should discuss this.  
- Weakness: claim that positiveness ∈ [0,1] is non-standard regression is incorrect (e.g., logistic regression).  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with experimentally supported claims.  
- Clarity assessment: well-written and clear overall, but some notation/terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts and needs more explanation.  
- Originality assessment: proposes a novel formulation, but differentiation from prior work is insufficiently discussed.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on clearer discussion of novelty relative to prior work, explanation of regularization effects, and improved explanations for non-experts.
