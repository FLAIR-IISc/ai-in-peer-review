- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for weakly supervised learning with continuous “positiveness” labels and a latent-variable extension.  
- Formulates the problem as a weighted SVM (weights from positiveness); latent variant effectively a weighted transductive SVM.  
- Achieves strong empirical results, including state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited, largely building on existing weighted/transductive SVM ideas.  
- Fails to cite key prior work on weighted and transductive SVMs (e.g., Suykens, Yang, Vapnik, Lapin et al.).  
- Does not sufficiently acknowledge or differentiate the latent e-SVM from weighted transductive SVMs.  
- Title and abstract are misleading—claim that continuous targets are less informative than binary labels is unjustified.  
- Core contribution is a learning method for continuous targets, not a claim about their informativeness.  
- Exposition is uneven: excessive detail on minor algorithmic variants but insufficient coverage of feature engineering.  
- Feature extraction details are glossed over, hurting reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Selection and impact of regularization hyperparameters (λW, λR) are not explained.  
- The imposed sum-constraint on weights is noted but not thoroughly investigated.  
- Methodology omits standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression).  
- Contains minor typographical and notation errors that need correction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical performance, relevance to weak supervision, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited originality, missing citations, clarity problems, and methodological gaps.  
- Overall: technically solid with promising results but requires major revision to address prior-work, clarity, and evaluation issues.  
- Recommendation: publish after major revision; may be better suited to a computer-vision venue.
