- Proposes Expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that minimizes expected hinge loss for [0,1] “positiveness” scores, yielding per-example weighted positive/negative hinge terms.
- Introduces latent e-SVM for weak supervision that alternates between optimizing model weights and latent positiveness u, regularized by a convex KL term toward box-IoU initialization.
- Demonstrates applications to segment proposal scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection, adding a spatial confidence map to RCNN and improving VOC2007 mAP by 1.2–1.5 points.
- Method is implementable with standard linear SVM solvers using per-example weights and is practically useful in pipelines with segment proposals.
- The convex KL regularizer makes the weakly supervised extension easy to integrate and optimize within existing frameworks.
- Proposes new evaluation measures for segment scoring (TAPC and NDCG) tailored to the task.
- Novelty is limited: expected hinge loss with probabilistic/soft labels and instance-weighting are known, and the approach is equivalent to weighted/duplicated-example formulations.
- Latent extension is straightforward alternating minimization with no theoretical analysis on convergence beyond convex subproblems or identifiability/generalization guarantees.
- Heavy dependence on KL regularizer raises questions about how much latent u actually changes; lacks ablation on λR and sensitivity to initialization.
- Incorrect claim that [0,1]-valued targets are “not standard regression”; omits comparisons to logistic regression, calibrated probabilistic baselines, and soft-label SVMs.
- Reproducibility is weak: missing hyperparameters (λw, λR), stopping criteria, solver settings, and no statistical significance or variance reporting for detection gains.
- Empirical evaluation is broad and shows consistent gains, but baselines could be stronger and additional ablations (g(u), initialization, fixed-u vs latent) are missing.
- Clarity is generally good, with straightforward exposition, but relationship to WSVM/soft-label SVM should be explicitly clarified and minor typos/broken references fixed.
- Originality is modest; the spatial confidence map is practical but incremental relative to prior work.
- Significance is moderate: useful practical improvements but limited conceptual novelty and incomplete comparisons reduce broader impact.
- Recommendations: clarify relations to instance-weighted/soft-label/latent SVMs, include logistic and calibrated baselines, analyze KL regularizer effect and sensitivity, explore g(u) choices, and provide full training details.
- Minor issues: typos (e.g., “continues” → “continuous”), broken/duplicated citations, Algorithm 1 formatting, and unclear stopping criterion.
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, practical method with consistent empirical gains, easy integration, and new segment scoring metrics.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, missing stronger baselines and theoretical grounding, unclear latent contribution, and reproducibility/statistical rigor concerns.
- Overall: solid engineering contribution with practical value but insufficient novelty, analysis, and baselines to meet top-conference standards; needs stronger positioning and ablations.
