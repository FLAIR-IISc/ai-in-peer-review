- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from "positiveness" values by treating them as expectations and using a weighted SVM loss.  
- Extends e-SVM to a latent-variable setting for weak supervision (bounding-box annotations) and applies it to proposal classification, segmentation, and detection.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Motivated by the need to leverage weakly supervised/imperfect labels rather than binarizing or regressing positiveness values.  
- Claims weighting the loss by positiveness is better than standard SVC or SVR for this problem.  
- Strengths: practical application, empirical results, versatility across tasks, and clear experimental setups with comparisons to SVC and SVR.  
- Major omission: inadequate citation and discussion of prior work on weighted SVMs, transductive SVMs, and related privileged-information weighting schemes.  
- The latent e-SVM closely resembles weighted transductive SVM formulations, reducing claimed novelty.  
- The "sum constraint" on weights needs more comparison to existing constrained SVM optimization literature.  
- Limited algorithmic novelty: the method is mainly a specific instantiation/combination of existing techniques rather than a fundamental advance.  
- Significance: valuable for weakly supervised learning and notable for outperforming recent CNN-based methods with a conventional approach.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-described algorithmically, but lacks thorough engagement with prior work and more implementation detail.  
- Needs additional experiments: compare to natural baselines like regularized logistic regression and justify regularization choices (λW, λR).  
- Clarity issues: misleading title/abstract, insufficient detail on key features (e.g., spatial confidence map), typographical errors, and notation inconsistencies.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions — must address omitted related work, rewrite intro/related work, provide baseline comparisons and implementation/regularization justification; possibly better suited to a vision venue.
