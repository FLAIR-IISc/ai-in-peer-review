- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for examples with continuous positiveness in [0,1] and a latent-weight extension for unobserved positiveness.  
- Applied to semantic segmentation and object detection, showing practical gains (e.g., +1.5% AP on PASCAL VOC 2007).  
- Paper is well-written and provides sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Core idea (weighting examples by positiveness) is equivalent to existing example-dependent costs in SVM literature (e.g., SVMlight), so novelty claim is problematic.  
- Latent-weight extension is useful but appears straightforward and lacks theoretical justification for the resulting non-convex optimization.  
- Heavy regularization keeps learned weights close to initial IoU-based estimates and needs explicit discussion.  
- Abstract contains errors (typo “continues” → “continuous”) and incorrectly claims [0,1]-bounded targets are “non-standard regression” (ignores logistic regression).  
- Related work section inadequately addresses existing weighted SVM approaches, making it hard to assess contribution.  
- While technically sound with good empirical results, unclear novelty and theoretical gaps undermine the contribution.  
- Significance is mainly limited to specific computer vision tasks without broader ML implications.  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient novelty and lack of theoretical grounding.
