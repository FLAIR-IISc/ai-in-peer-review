- Proposes e-SVM: models continuous "positiveness" in [0,1] as Bernoulli probabilities and minimizes expected hinge loss, yielding a convex weighted SVM objective solvable by standard solvers.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision via alternating minimization, alternating between training SVM and updating positiveness weights.  
- Applies method to semantic segmentation and object detection using IoU as positiveness; reports improvements over SVC/SVR and boosts R-CNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Technical formulation and experiments are sound and well-executed, with strong empirical results on challenging vision tasks.  
- Introduces useful segment-wise evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG) for the prediction task.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the non-convex latent optimization: no convergence or local-optima quality guarantees.  
- Presentation is generally clear and organized, though the distinction from weighted SVMs is not well articulated; minor typo in abstract.  
- Core e-SVM appears equivalent to existing instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs available in SVMlight/LibSVM, raising originality concerns.  
- The claimed distinction of “weighting labels instead of data points” is unclear and seems semantic rather than functional.  
- Latent e-SVM is more novel but follows established latent-variable + alternating minimization patterns (e.g., latent SVM).  
- KL regularizer in latent model may keep u close to initialization; paper should analyze how much u_i actually change during optimization.  
- Incorrect claim that SVR is unsuitable for bounded [0,1] targets; standard regressions (logistic, Beta regression) can handle bounded outputs.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, is simple and practical, and shows strong empirical gains including over R-CNN.  
- Against acceptance: major originality concerns relative to instance-weighted SVMs, incremental latent contribution, and missing theoretical analysis of optimization.
