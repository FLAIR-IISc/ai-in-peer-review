- Proposes e-SVM for weakly supervised learning using sample "positiveness" instead of binary labels and applies it to segmentation and detection, achieving state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Fails to adequately situate the method within prior work on weighted SVM and transductive SVM; needs discussion of Suykens, Yang, Vapnik, Lapin, etc.
- Latent e-SVM appears essentially a weighted transductive SVM and must be clearly differentiated from prior art.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited, though the application to semantic segmentation and object detection is novel and valuable.
- Paper is generally well-written but the title/abstract misstate contributions and the claim about unit-interval targets being less informative than binary labels is misleading.
- Excessive focus on small algorithmic variations and insufficient detail on crucial feature-engineering choices.
- Missing standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression) to justify the chosen approach.
- Insufficient detail on how regularization coefficients (λW and λR) were selected.
- Results are significant: conventional approach outperforms recent CNNs on VOC2007, making the work of high community interest.
- Minor notation and typography issues reduce clarity and should be fixed.
- Recommendation: accept after revision and rewriting to address literature review, clarity, methodological details; suggests a computer-vision venue may be more appropriate.
- For acceptance: strong empirical results, novel application, and clear algorithm explanation; Against: weak relation to prior work, limited novelty, and misplaced emphasis in the paper.
