- Proposal: Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that weights labels by continuous “positiveness” values for weakly supervised learning.  
- Novelty: Weighting labels rather than instances is a subtle but impactful new idea relative to MIL and weighted SVMs.  
- Positioning: Properly situates work within MIL and weighted-SVM literature and highlights differences (doesn't assume MIL’s at-least-one-positive).  
- Theoretical soundness: Loss formulation and latent-variable extension are clearly presented and appear theoretically well-founded.  
- Empirical results: Strong validation across segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, including state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Generality: Method is compatible with various proposal detectors, features, and base detectors.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with helpful figures and clear explanation of methodology and experiments.  
- Limited ablation: Lacks detailed ablation studies (e.g., alternative regularizers, sensitivity to proposal algorithms).  
- Comparison depth: Needs more thorough empirical comparison to recent weakly supervised state-of-the-art methods beyond literature citations.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection and sensitivity analysis.  
- Failure cases: Missing discussion of limitations and examples of failure modes.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, theoretically sound, well-validated, clearly written, and of interest to the community.  
- Rejection arguments: Shortcomings in ablation, comparative depth, and discussion of limitations could warrant revision.  
- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add more ablation, deeper comparisons, and discussion of limitations.
