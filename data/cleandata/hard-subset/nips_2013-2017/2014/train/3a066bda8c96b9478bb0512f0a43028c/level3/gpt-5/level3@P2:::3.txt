Summary and contributions
This paper introduces the expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a soft-label variant of the SVM hinge loss that takes, for each training instance, a “positiveness” µi ∈ [0,1] (in practice set to IoU with ground-truth) and optimizes the expected hinge loss: µi max(0,1 − f(xi)) + (1−µi) max(0,1 + f(xi)). The authors also propose a latent extension for weak supervision where positiveness values ui are unobserved and are optimized jointly with the classifier using an alternating (bi-convex) procedure, regularized by the cross-entropy to an initialization ubb (IoU w.r.t. bounding boxes). Applications are shown to segment proposal ranking for semantic segmentation and to object detection, where a “spatial confidence map” is pooled over candidate windows and concatenated with RCNN features, yielding +1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 over RCNN.

Relation to prior work
The formulation ties closely to:
- Instance/label-weighted SVMs and cost-sensitive SVMs (e.g., Suykens et al.; Yang et al.; Lapin et al.), where example-specific costs weight hinge losses. Here, each instance contributes to both positive and negative hinge losses with weights that sum to 1; this “sum-constrained” soft labeling is a useful twist but closely related to known formulations and can be implemented by duplicating each xi with labels ±1 and weights µi and 1−µi.
- Transductive/latent SVMs (Vapnik; Joachims; Felzenszwalb et al. for latent SVM), in which unknown labels/latents are optimized jointly with the classifier via alternating minimization. The proposed latent e-SVM resembles a weighted TSVM/latent-SVM, with a strong prior on the latent ui via KL/cross-entropy to ubb.
- Learning with soft/probabilistic targets and regression-to-[0,1] (logistic/Platt, proper scoring rules, SVR on bounded intervals) are also relevant baselines.

Overall, the positioning relative to the above is underdeveloped; several pertinent references are missing or not clearly contrasted.

Quality
- Soundness: The expected hinge loss is convex in w given fixed µi, and the latent extension is bi-convex with reasonable alternating optimization. The cross-entropy regularizer is convex and well-motivated to discourage degenerate 0/1 u. However, the paper lacks analysis of convergence behavior, sensitivity to initialization ubb, and the effect of λR.
- Empirics: The segment proposal ranking results (TAPC/NDCG) show consistent gains over SVC and small but consistent gains over SVR in both strong and weak annotation settings. The detection results are practically strong and of clear interest: a simple concatenation with RCNN yields SOTA on VOC’07 at the time.
- Missing baselines/ablations: To justify the learning choice, please include:
  - Logistic regression with cross-entropy using µi as soft targets; Brier score/MSE with calibration; beta regression or bounded regression baselines.
  - A comparison in detection to RCNN + spatial confidence map features computed from SVR or SVC (to isolate whether gains come from e-SVM vs. using any segment-derived confidence map).
  - Ablations on λw, λR selection, g(u) choices (identity vs. calibrated/logit mappings), and on the spatial binning (m, pooling type).
- Reproducibility: Important engineering decisions (feature details for confidence maps, training protocols, hyperparameter search ranges/criteria, class balancing, sample weighting, solver settings) are not sufficiently described. The mapping from the expectation loss to a standard SVM solver (e.g., via example duplication and per-example weights) should be made explicit.

Clarity
- The paper is readable, but the title/abstract overstate novelty and suggest that [0,1] targets are somehow less informative than binary labels (they are more informative). Please rephrase to emphasize soft labels and latent soft labels.
- Organization spends too much space on small variations and too little on critical implementation/feature details for the detection pipeline.
- Notation is occasionally inconsistent (u vs. µ; g(u) fixed to identity in experiments). Provide a clear derivation of the loss and its implementation.
- Several typos and minor issues (examples): lines 19, 94, 98, 350; unify capitalization (e-SVM vs eSVM); fix missing years/pages in references; clarify “weighted SVMs” vs “weights on labels”.

Originality
- Moderate. The expected hinge with soft labels and the latent extension are close to known weighted/TSVM/latent-SVM ideas. The “sum-constrained” per-instance weighting (µi and 1−µi) is a neat and practically relevant choice, but algorithmically incremental. The main novelty is in the particular combination and application to segment confidence learning and the confidence-map integration with RCNN.

Significance
- High practical significance: beating RCNN by ~1.5 mAP on VOC’07 with a simple, modular addition is noteworthy and will interest the community.
- Methodological significance is modest; clearer positioning and stronger baselines would increase confidence that the gains derive from the proposed loss rather than from generic segment-derived cues.

Pros
- Simple, convex (in w) formulation for soft labels; easy to implement with standard SVM solvers.
- Principled and effective latent extension with a well-motivated regularizer.
- Strong empirical results on VOC’07 detection; consistent improvements in segment ranking.
- General, modular pipeline usable with various proposal methods and base detectors.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to weighted/latent/transductive SVM literature; missing or thin discussion of related work (e.g., Vapnik; Joachims; cost-sensitive/weighted SVMs; soft-target logistic regression).
- Missing key baselines (logistic/soft labels; SVR-based detection features) and ablations.
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection and feature-engineering.
- Some overclaiming/misleading phrasing in abstract/title; clarity issues and typos.

Requests for clarification
- How are λw and λR selected (per-class? global? validation protocol)? Report sensitivity curves.
- Provide details to reproduce the spatial confidence map features (exact m, pooling, normalization, kernel map parameters) and training data sampling.
- Compare against logistic regression with µi as soft targets and RCNN + SVR-based confidence maps.
- Discuss convergence (number of alternations, stopping criterion) and initialization sensitivity for latent e-SVM.
- Justify the choice g(u)=u; did you try calibration or monotone transforms?

Minor comments
- Fix typos and missing bibliographic fields. Clarify that the expectation loss can be implemented by duplicating each xi with weights µi and 1−µi. Unify notation and terminology (“positiveness” → “soft label/IoU”).

Recommendation
Weak accept. The methodological contribution is incremental but sound; the empirical impact on a high-profile benchmark is meaningful. With a stronger related-work discussion, additional baselines, and clearer exposition, this would be a solid paper. Even if better aligned with a vision venue, the learning content and practical value fit NIPS.
