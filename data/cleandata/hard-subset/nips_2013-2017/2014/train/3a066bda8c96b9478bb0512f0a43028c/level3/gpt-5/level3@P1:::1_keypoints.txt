- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models soft labels as Bernoulli(g(u)) and minimizes expected hinge loss, yielding a standard SVM with instance-dependent label weights.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM when u is unobserved by regularizing u toward a bounding-box-derived proxy ubb with a convex KL penalty and alternating minimization (biconvex).  
- Contrasts with SVC (thresholding), SVR (regressing bounded IoU), WSVMs (weighting examples), and MIL/latent SVMs (different weak-supervision regime).  
- Applied to segment confidence prediction and as a spatial confidence-map feature for RCNN; improves TAPC/NDCG, yields competitive segmentation, and adds +1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC07.  
- Formulation is sound: convex in observed case, each latent subproblem convex, and alternating scheme reasonable though only local optimality guaranteed.  
- Experimental protocol is standard and results persuasive; introduction of TAPC and NDCG is a useful contribution.  
- Missing analyses: sensitivity to choice of g(·), λR, initialization; convergence behavior; and runtime/memory comparisons to SVR/SVC.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and clearly distinguishes label-weighting vs instance-weighting; some references need correction and related work on probabilistic/soft-label SVMs should be tightened.  
- Originality is moderate: expected-hinge-loss SVM is natural and underexplored in this exact form, related to prior soft-label large-margin work; latent KL prior and spatial map integration are practical, distinct twists.  
- Significance: important for scaling vision systems from soft/weak supervision; detection gains are strong evidence of impact though segmentation gains are modest.  
- Strengths: simple, convex, easy to implement with standard solvers; clean latent-variable extension; solid empirical validation; task-agnostic metrics; reproducible practical details.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: learn/calibrate g(·); provide hyperparameter and initialization ablations and convergence plots; cite and compare to soft-label/uncertain-label SVM literature; explore alternative convex priors to KL; report per-class detection gains and error analysis; provide training time/memory overhead and LibLinear implementation details.  
- Open questions for authors: sensitivity to g and λR and whether non-linear or learned g was tried; how often latent u departs from ubb and in what scenarios; typical number of alternating iterations and any oscillations; effect of using other proposal methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, simple method for soft/weak supervision with clear empirical gains and easy integration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental conceptual novelty relative to existing soft-label SVM ideas and limited theoretical/ablation analysis.  
- Overall: solid, practically useful contribution recommended for acceptance after minor revisions to strengthen positioning and analysis.
