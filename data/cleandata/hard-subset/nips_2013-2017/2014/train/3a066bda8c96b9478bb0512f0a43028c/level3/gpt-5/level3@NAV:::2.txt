This paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), which replaces the binary hinge loss with its expectation under a per-example “positiveness” µi ∈ [0,1], yielding a weighted combination of positive and negative hinge losses u·ℓ+ + (1−u)·ℓ−. A latent version treats u as unobserved and alternates between standard SVM fitting and updating u with a convex KL regularizer that anchors it to an initialization from bounding-box IoU. The method is evaluated on segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection (as features fused with R-CNN), showing consistent gains over SVC/SVR and small but nontrivial AP improvements on PASCAL.

Relation to prior work: The basic formulation appears equivalent to example-dependent cost-weighted SVM (instance-weighted SVMs, e.g., SVMlight’s per-example costs and numerous WSVM variants). The loss can also be viewed as training on “soft labels” or label confidences, a well-studied setting. The latent extension is a straightforward alternating scheme with a convex regularizer.

Quality: The empirical section is solid and reasonably thorough across tasks. The optimization of the latent model is non-convex; no convergence analysis or excess-risk guarantees are provided. The heavy regularization on u suggests minimal deviation from the initialization; the paper should report how far learned u moves (e.g., KL, L2, or histogram of |u−u_bb|) and sensitivity to λR. The claim that bounded targets make regression “non-standard” is incorrect; logistic regression is a standard bounded model. Comparisons to logistic/softmax regression with calibrated probabilities would be informative.

Clarity: Generally clear and well organized. Please fix the abstract typo (“continues” → “continuous”) and temper statements about solver novelty—if this reduces to instance-weighted SVM, cite and align terminology. Explicitly state how weights are implemented in LibLinear. Minor notational glitches in Eq. (1) could be cleaned up.

Originality: Limited. The core contribution matches existing example-dependent cost/soft-label SVMs; the latent extension is incremental. The paper should clearly articulate novelty beyond WSVM and probabilistic labels, with citations and empirical comparisons to those baselines.

Significance: Practically useful for CV pipelines; the 1–1.5 AP gains are meaningful. Methodological impact is modest without clearer novelty or analysis.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, general, and easy to implement with standard solvers
- Broad and positive empirical results across tasks
- Clear presentation and useful feature integration for detection

Cons for acceptance:
- Novelty over example-dependent cost SVM/soft labels is unclear
- No theoretical insight for the latent non-convex optimization
- Heavy regularization undermines the “learning” of u; limited ablations
- Missing comparisons to logistic/regression baselines and WSVM implementations
