This paper proposes a novel learning algorithm called Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle weakly supervised data where only the "positiveness" of each training sample is available. The algorithm is designed to work with data that has continuous values in a bounded interval, which is a common problem in computer vision tasks such as semantic segmentation and object detection.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm on several tasks, including segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection. The results show that the e-SVM algorithm outperforms other methods, including Support Vector Classification (SVC) and Support Vector Regression (SVR), in terms of accuracy and robustness.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to handle weakly supervised data, which is a common problem in many computer vision tasks. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their algorithm and its advantages over other methods. The experimental results are also well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The algorithmic novelty is limited, and many pertinent prior works are not cited. The authors should provide a more comprehensive review of related work and clearly differentiate their algorithm from existing methods. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more detail on the hyperparameter settings and the choice of regularization coefficients.

In terms of clarity, the title and abstract do not clearly convey the core contribution of the paper, and the claim about unit-interval targets being less informative than binary labels is misleading. The paper spends too much space on small algorithmic variations and not enough on critical feature-engineering details.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel algorithm that can handle weakly supervised data. However, it could benefit from more comprehensive related work, clearer explanations, and more detail on experimental settings.
