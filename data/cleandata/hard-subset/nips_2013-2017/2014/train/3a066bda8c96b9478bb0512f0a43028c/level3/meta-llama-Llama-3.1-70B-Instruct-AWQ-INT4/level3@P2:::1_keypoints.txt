- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous "positiveness" values instead of binary labels.  
- Core idea: treat each segment label as an unobserved random variable and use its expectation in the loss function.  
- Extends the method to a latent setting where positiveness values are unobserved.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on segment proposal classification under strong and weak supervision.  
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks with positive results.  
- Method is positioned as an extension of multiple instance learning and relates clearly to prior work.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, with clear notation and good figures.  
- Experiments are interesting, well-executed, and show the algorithm’s effectiveness.  
- Strengths: novel approach to continuous positiveness, applicability in observed and latent settings, thorough analysis.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and actual running-time measurements on large datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art algorithms on segmentation and detection.  
- Suggests need for more discussion of limitations and potential future work.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, broadly applicable to weak supervision, strong experimental support and analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: missing complexity/runtime analysis and comprehensive SOTA comparisons; limited discussion of limitations.
