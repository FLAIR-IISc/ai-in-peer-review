- Presents a novel hybrid policy-search method combining model-based and model-free techniques.  
- Fits local, time-varying linear dynamics to optimize trajectory distributions and uses guided policy search for arbitrary parameterized policies.  
- Key innovation: KL-divergence constraint to limit per-iteration trajectory distribution change, enabling handling of complex/discontinuous dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise explanation of methodology and principles.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, covering robotic manipulation and locomotion tasks.  
- Results show the method outperforms prior model-free and model-based approaches and generalizes to new situations.  
- Strength: novel combination of model-based and model-free strengths.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation across multiple tasks.  
- Strength: clear exposition of methodology and underlying principles.  
- Weakness: requires significant computational resources and large amounts of data to train policies.  
- Weakness: may not scale well to high-dimensional state and action spaces due to sample complexity.  
- Weakness: relies on approximating dynamics with time-varying linear-Gaussian models, which may be inappropriate for some systems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novel contribution, strong experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high compute/data requirements, scalability concerns, restrictive dynamics assumptions.  
- Overall rating: 8/10, with strong contributions tempered by practical limitations.
