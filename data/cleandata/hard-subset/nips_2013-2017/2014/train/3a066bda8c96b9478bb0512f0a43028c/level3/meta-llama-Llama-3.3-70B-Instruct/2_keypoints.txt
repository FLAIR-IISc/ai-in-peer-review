- Proposes a hybrid policy-search method combining model-based local linear dynamics with model-free guided policy search.  
- Fits local, time-varying linear dynamics to optimize trajectory distributions and then trains general parameterized policies.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness for learning complex neural-network policies in partially observed robotic manipulation and locomotion tasks.  
- Shows superior sample efficiency and policy quality compared to prior model-free and model-based methods.  
- Handles complex, discontinuous dynamics (e.g., contacts) better than many prior approaches.  
- Scales to learning high-dimensional policies with thousands of parameters.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains technical details thoroughly.  
- Provides extensive experiments and a thorough analysis of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Main limitation: relies on a strong local linearity assumption of dynamics that may not hold universally.  
- Practical drawback: significant computation and memory required to store and update local linear models, challenging for large-scale problems.  
- Quality score: 8/10.  
- Clarity score: 9/10.  
- Originality score: 8/10.  
- Significance score: 9/10.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, effective hybrid approach that advances guided policy search and solves challenging tasks.  
- Argument for rejection: dependence on local linearity and heavy computational/memory demands may limit applicability.
