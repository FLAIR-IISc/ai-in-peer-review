- Proposes Expectation-Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle weak supervision with continuous “positiveness” values.  
- Core idea: weight SVM loss by sample positiveness so partially correct proposals contribute proportionally.  
- Introduces a latent e-SVM variant to handle unobserved positiveness values.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Builds on MIL and weighted SVMs but distinguishes itself by weighting labels (positiveness) rather than data points, yielding a novel loss.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of standard SVC and SVR for bounded continuous labels in this setting.  
- Appropriately cites related work (e.g., Carreira on region proposals, Girshick R-CNN).  
- Strength: tackles an important practical problem (reducing annotation cost in vision).  
- Strength: novel and effective formulation that extends standard SVMs.  
- Strength: clear writing and concise mathematical presentation.  
- Strength: strong, comprehensive experimental validation across tasks and supervision levels.  
- Strength: thorough comparison to relevant baselines (SVC, SVR).  
- Strength: practical and versatile across detectors, features, and classifiers.  
- Weakness: KL-divergence regularizer in latent e-SVM needs stronger justification and exploration of alternatives.  
- Weakness: spatial confidence map description lacks analysis of bin-size (m=5) sensitivity; an ablation would help.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of failure cases and scenarios where e-SVM may struggle.  
- Weakness: could include more nuanced comparison to regression methods for bounded outputs (e.g., sigmoid-based regression).  
- Recommendation: technically sound, significant, well-evaluated paper — reviewer recommends acceptance.
