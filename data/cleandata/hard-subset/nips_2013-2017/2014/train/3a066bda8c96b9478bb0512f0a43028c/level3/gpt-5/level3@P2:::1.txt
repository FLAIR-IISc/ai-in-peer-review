Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a simple, convex modification of the SVM hinge loss that allows training with “soft” labels indicating the degree of positiveness ui ∈ [0,1]. For a linear classifier w, the per-example loss is the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label with success probability µi = g(ui), instantiated as l+ i g(ui) + l− i (1−g(ui)), where l+ i = max(0, 1 − w⊤xi) and l− i = max(0, 1 + w⊤xi). This is equivalent to weighting the positive and negative hinge losses by the soft label. The method extends naturally to a latent e-SVM when soft labels are unobserved (e.g., only bounding boxes are given). In this case, ui is treated as a latent variable and learned by alternating minimization over w and u with a convex regularizer R(u) (KL divergence to an initialization ubb based on IoU with the bounding box). The approach is validated on segment proposal scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection (by building a spatial confidence map from segment scores and concatenating it with RCNN features). The authors report small but consistent gains over SVC and SVR for segment scoring (using TAPC and NDCG metrics), and an average +1.5 AP improvement over RCNN+BB-reg on PASCAL VOC 2007.

The work sits between MIL/weakly supervised learning (e.g., latent SVM for detection) and regression on IoU used in segmentation pipelines (e.g., O2P/CPMC). Unlike MIL assumptions (“at least one fully positive instance per bag”), e-SVM handles partial positiveness by soft labels; unlike SVR on IoU, it respects the bounded nature of “positiveness” and remains a margin-based classifier. There are also connections to instance-weighted SVMs, SVMs with probabilistic/fuzzy labels, and learning from label distributions/label proportions, which the paper discusses partly (WSVM) but could further relate to.

Strengths
- Clear, well-motivated problem: learning from bounded soft labels and weak supervision (bounding boxes) is important and practical.
- Simple, convex formulation for the observed-soft-label case; easy to implement with off-the-shelf linear SVM solvers.
- Latent extension with a principled convex regularizer (KL) to stabilize alternating optimization.
- Empirical validation on three levels: direct segment scoring metrics (TAPC, NDCG), semantic segmentation accuracy, and object detection AP on VOC 2007. Gains on detection (+1.5 mAP) are non-trivial in the RCNN era.
- The spatial confidence map feature provides a straightforward, class-specific mechanism to inject segmentation cues into detection.

Weaknesses and questions
- Novelty is modest: the expected hinge loss with probabilistic/soft labels has close ties to “fuzzy SVMs” and SVMs with uncertain labels; mathematically, the loss equals training two weighted hinge terms per sample (positive and negative) and is equivalent to an instance-weighted SVM with duplicated examples. The paper should explicitly discuss this equivalence and contrast to prior “probabilistic label SVMs” and learning from label distributions/proportions.
- The mapping g(u) is set to identity with no calibration or justification. Since ui (IoU or box overlap proxy) is not a calibrated class probability, exploring learned/calibrated g (e.g., Platt/isotonic mapping) or sensitivity analysis would strengthen the claims.
- The latent e-SVM relies on a strong KL regularizer to ubb (overlap with the bounding box). This could bias u toward the initialization and limit discovery of better u when ubb is a poor proxy. Ablations showing the effect of λR and alternative regularizers (e.g., L2 to ubb, entropy to avoid {0,1} extremes) would be informative.
- Optimization details are light: how is u updated efficiently (closed-form per instance, Newton, projected gradient)? What are convergence/stopping criteria and runtimes?
- Comparisons: for segment scoring, gains over SVR are small; for segmentation, reported accuracies are comparable to SVR baselines but do not clearly advance state-of-the-art. For detection, fairness controls are needed (same training data, same feature dimensionality/regularization, with/without bbox regression) and ablations to isolate where the gains come from (e-SVM vs. using segments at all).
- Clarity/typos: some references are incomplete; minor formatting issues (e.g., missing years/pages). Algorithm 1 and equations would benefit from more explicit notation (λw vs λR, normalization by N, etc.).

Qualitative evaluation
- Quality: The core formulation for observed soft labels is technically sound and convex. The latent setting is a reasonable alternating convex scheme with a convex regularizer. However, theoretical guarantees (e.g., convergence to stationary points, effect of λR) and more detailed algorithmic exposition are lacking. Empirical methodology is generally sound, but more ablations and stronger baselines (e.g., logistic regression with soft labels; calibrated SVR; latent SVM variants) would strengthen the case.
- Clarity: The paper is mostly clear and well organized. The motivation and pipeline are easy to follow. Improve rigor in notation, optimization details, and references.
- Originality: Moderate. The expected hinge loss with soft labels and the equivalence to instance weighting/duplicated-label formulation reduces novelty. The KL regularization for latent u and the spatial confidence map feature are practical touches rather than conceptual leaps.
- Significance: Practical significance is reasonable—handling soft labels under weak supervision is widely relevant, and +1.5 mAP on VOC 2007 is useful. Scientific significance is moderate given incremental novelty.

Suggestions for improvement
- Explicitly relate e-SVM to prior “fuzzy/probabilistic label SVMs,” instance-weighted SVM with duplicated examples, and learning from label distributions/label proportions; include empirical comparisons where feasible.
- Study g(u): try calibrated mappings and report sensitivity.
- Provide optimization details (derivation of u-update, complexity) and analyze the effect of λR, initialization, and alternative regularizers.
- Add ablations for the detection pipeline: (i) use of segments without e-SVM; (ii) other ways to aggregate segment scores; (iii) different m × m grids; (iv) effect per class.
- Improve reference list completeness and fix typos.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses an important weakly supervised learning setting with a simple, general, and implementable loss.
- Convex for observed soft labels; easy drop-in replacement for SVC/SVR in pipelines using IoU labels.
- Demonstrates solid empirical gains on a canonical detection benchmark.

Arguments against acceptance
- Conceptual novelty is limited; close to existing soft/probabilistic-label SVM formulations.
- Improvements over strong baselines (SVR) are small on segmentation; detection gains could be due to added features rather than the specific loss.
- Insufficient analysis of the latent formulation’s behavior and sensitivity.

Overall recommendation
Leaning accept. The paper presents a clean, practically useful formulation for learning with soft labels and a reasonable latent extension, with promising empirical results on important tasks. Strengthening the positioning with respect to prior soft-label SVM literature and adding ablations would make the contribution clearer.
