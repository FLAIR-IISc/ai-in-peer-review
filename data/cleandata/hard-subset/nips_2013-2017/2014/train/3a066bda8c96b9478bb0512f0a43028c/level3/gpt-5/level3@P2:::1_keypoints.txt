- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM): convex hinge-loss modification for training with soft labels µi=g(ui) in [0,1], equivalent to weighting positive/negative hinge terms.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM when soft labels are unobserved by alternating optimization over w and latent u with a KL regularizer to initialization ubb.  
- Validated on segment proposal scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection; reports small consistent gains and +1.5 AP over RCNN+BB-reg on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Sits between MIL/latent-SVM and IoU regression approaches; relates to instance-weighted SVMs, probabilistic/fuzzy-label SVMs, and label-distribution learning.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem addressing practical weak supervision and bounded soft labels.  
- Strength: simple, convex observed-soft-label formulation that is easy to implement with standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Strength: latent extension uses a principled convex KL regularizer to stabilize alternating optimization.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation at multiple levels (segment scoring metrics, segmentation accuracy, detection AP) with non-trivial detection gains.  
- Strength: spatial confidence map feature provides a pragmatic, class-specific way to inject segmentation cues into detection.  
- Weakness: novelty is modest — expected hinge loss closely ties to prior fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVMs and can be written as instance-weighted SVM with duplicated examples.  
- Weakness: mapping g(u) set to identity without calibration or justification; ui (IoU proxy) is not a calibrated class probability.  
- Weakness: strong KL regularizer may bias u toward initialization ubb and hinder discovery when ubb is poor; need ablations on λR and alternative regularizers.  
- Weakness: optimization details for updating u (closed-form, Newton, projected gradient), convergence criteria, and runtimes are underspecified.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited — segment scoring gains over SVR are small, segmentation not clearly better than SVR baselines, and detection gains may stem from extra features rather than the loss.  
- Weakness: clarity issues and minor formatting/reference typos; notation in algorithms/equations could be clearer (λw vs λR, normalization).  
- Qualitative: observed soft-label formulation is sound and convex; latent scheme reasonable but lacks theoretical guarantees (convergence, λR effects) and detailed algorithmic exposition.  
- Qualitative: empirical methodology generally sound but needs more ablations and stronger baselines (e.g., logistic with soft labels, calibrated SVR, latent-SVM variants).  
- Qualitative: paper is mostly clear and organized but should improve rigor in notation, optimization, and references.  
- Originality: moderate — main idea incremental but KL regularization for latent u and spatial confidence map are practical contributions.  
- Significance: practical impact reasonable given weak supervision relevance and detection gains; scientific novelty moderate.  
- Suggestions: explicitly relate and compare to prior soft/probabilistic-label SVMs and instance-weighted formulations.  
- Suggestions: study and calibrate g(u) (Platt/isotonic), and report sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions: provide optimization derivation for u-update, complexity, and analyze λR, initialization, and alternative regularizers.  
- Suggestions: add detection ablations isolating e-SVM effect vs. use of segments and feature choices, and per-class analyses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important weakly supervised setting with a simple, convex, implementable loss and shows practical gains on a canonical benchmark.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited conceptual novelty, small improvements over strong baselines, and insufficient analysis of latent formulation behavior and sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on stronger positioning vs prior work and additional ablations/analysis.
