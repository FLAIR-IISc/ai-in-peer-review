- Proposes e-SVM: replace hinge on hard label with expected hinge under Bernoulli(µ=g(u)), yielding a weighted per-example hinge SVM for [0,1] positiveness scores.
- Extends to latent positiveness (ui) with convex KL regularizer toward bounding-box proxy ubb for bounding-box-only training.
- Applies method to segment-proposal confidence prediction and uses learned per-class segment confidences to build spatial confidence-map features concatenated with RCNN.
- Empirical results: e-SVM outperforms SVC/SVR on proposal-confidence metrics (TAPC, NDCG) and yields competitive segmentation accuracy.
- Detection improvement: concatenating spatial confidence maps with RCNN features yields +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC07.
- Connection to prior work: lies between weighted SVMs and latent/transductive SVMs; resembles soft-label and weakly supervised SVM variants.
- Prior-work positioning is weak: needs clearer relation to transductive/semi-supervised SVMs, latent SVM, soft-label SVMs, label-proportion learning, and proper-scoring methods.
- Strength: simple, convex objective that reduces to standard SVM solvers and is easy to implement and scale.
- Strength: practical latent extension with convex KL regularization that avoids degenerate binary ui solutions.
- Strength: method-agnostic to proposal generators and detectors; spatial confidence feature is simple and effective.
- Strength: introduces sensible, task-agnostic metrics (TAPC, NDCG) for proposal-confidence evaluation.
- Weakness: moderate originality — mainly a repackaging of soft-label hinge SVM plus convex prior rather than a major conceptual advance.
- Weakness: missing strong baselines such as logistic regression on soft labels (cross-entropy), Beta regression, squared-loss regression, isotonic regression, and calibration measures.
- Weakness: methodological details are thin — unclear hyperparameter selection (λW, λR), validation protocol, convergence criteria, initialization sensitivity, and runtime.
- Weakness: exposition unbalanced — too much on objective variants and too little on the feature engineering that drives detection gains; needs ablations (m×m, pooling, g(u) choices).
- Clarity: generally readable but needs clearer positioning, more implementation detail, and better organization of detection integration.
- Quality: technically sound; objectives convex in blocks and supported by appropriate KL regularizer; empirical claims supported but missing baselines reduce interpretability.
- Originality: incremental; useful packaging and application but not a large conceptual leap.
- Significance: high practical impact due to simple, effective weak-supervision component that improves detectors.
- Suggestions: add baselines (logistic, squared loss, isotonic), expand related work, detail hyperparameter search/convergence/runtime, and provide ablations on λR, g(u), initialization, and spatial features.
- Minor fixes: correct typos, consistent notation, define acronyms on first use, and ensure Algorithm 1 matches text.
- Acceptance pros: simple, general method with clear empirical gains on prominent benchmarks and community relevance.
- Acceptance cons: limited novelty, incomplete positioning vs. established SVM variants, missing baselines, and insufficient reproducibility details.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on addressing baselines, related-work positioning, and methodological/reproducibility details.
