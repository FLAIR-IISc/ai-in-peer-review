- Proposes e-SVM: weight SVM loss by continuous “positiveness” and extend to latent setting where positiveness is inferred.  
- Shows empirical improvements on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving SOTA on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Addresses an important weakly supervised learning problem with clear motivation about expensive pixel-level annotations versus available bounding boxes.  
- Experimental validation across multiple tasks demonstrates general applicability and competitive results.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the e-SVM formulation and latent extension.  
- Limited originality: weighting examples by confidence is well-studied (weighted SVMs) and the method appears similar to instance-dependent costs in prior work (e.g., SVMlight).  
- Needs clearer differentiation from existing weighted SVM literature and explicit statement of novel contributions.  
- Latent-variable extension lacks novelty; alternating updates for u and w are standard.  
- Optimization is non-convex and the paper provides no theoretical analysis of convergence or optimality.  
- Strong KL regularization prevents trivial solutions but suggests learned weights may be heavily influenced by initialization; sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Incorrect claim: continuous values in [0,1] are not non-standard for regression (e.g., logistic regression); revise this statement.  
- Minor typo in abstract: “continues” should be “continuous.”  
- Acceptance arguments: tackles important problem, consistent cross-task improvements, achieves SOTA detection, and is clearly presented.  
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty, lack of theoretical analysis for latent model, sensitivity to initialization/regularization, and an incorrect regression claim.  
- Overall: technically sound but novelty concerns lead to a weak reject; would reconsider if novelty is clarified and latent-model theory/sensitivity issues are addressed.
