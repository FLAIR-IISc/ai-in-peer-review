- Proposes e-SVM: an SVM variant that weights each example by a “positiveness” value in [0,1], and extends to latent (unobserved) weights.  
- Core idea: incorporate positiveness weights into the loss and treat missing weights as latent variables.  
- Empirical results show improvements on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007 detection.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear experimental setup and relevant tasks.  
- Motivates practical need for learning from weakly supervised data (cost of full annotation).  
- Novelty is limited: weighting examples by confidence mirrors prior work (e.g., example-dependent costs in SVMlight).  
- Latent-weight extension is a straightforward latent-variable application lacking theoretical analysis of convergence or optimality.  
- Heavy reliance on regularization; learned weights staying near initialization suggests potential optimization issues that are not discussed.  
- Incorrect claim that [0,1]-bounded positiveness makes the problem “non-standard regression” (e.g., logistic regression already handles [0,1] outputs).  
- Minor typo: “continues” should be “continuous” in the abstract.  
- Arguments for acceptance: empirical gains over strong baselines, clear presentation, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, lack of theoretical grounding for latent weights, insufficient discussion of regularization/optimization, and an incorrect regression claim.  
- Overall recommendation: lean towards rejection; authors should better contrast prior art and analyze the optimization/theoretical properties.
