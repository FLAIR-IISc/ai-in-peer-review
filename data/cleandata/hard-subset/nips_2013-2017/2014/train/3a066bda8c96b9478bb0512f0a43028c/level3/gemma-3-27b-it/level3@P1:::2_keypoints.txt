- Proposes e-SVM to learn from “positiveness” values and a latent extension for unobserved positiveness, showing strong results including state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007 detection.  
- Situates work in weakly supervised learning and relates to SVC, SVR, MIL, and weighted SVMs.  

Strengths:
- Clear problem formulation and strong motivation for learning from positiveness values.  
- Empirical results: outperforms SVC/SVR on segment proposals and achieves competitive/SOTA results in segmentation and detection.  
- Practical and adaptable to different proposal detectors, features, and classifiers; handles full and weak supervision seamlessly.  
- Well-written and organized presentation.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: approach resembles existing instance-weighting/instance-dependent-cost SVM methods (e.g., SVMlight) and lacks sufficient differentiation.  
- Optimization concerns: latent e-SVM is non-convex, iterative algorithm has no convergence guarantees, and learned weights stay near initialization suggesting sensitivity or instability.  
- Incorrect claim about regression: values in [0,1] are standardly handled (e.g., logistic regression).  
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions (notably the regularization effect in latent e-SVM).  
- Minor typo in abstract: “continues” → “continuous.”  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Practical, effective method with significant empirical gains and clear exposition.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Questionable novelty, insufficient analysis of optimization, and the inaccurate regression claim raise concerns.  

Overall:
- Worthwhile contribution but limited novelty and unresolved optimization issues; recommend borderline reject with major revision to address novelty, optimization analysis, and corrections.
