- Empirical strength: achieves state-of-the-art detection results on PASCAL VOC 2007 when combined with R-CNN features.
- Problem motivation: addresses learning from continuous "positiveness" scores (e.g., IoU) relevant for weak supervision tasks.
- Algorithmic novelty limited: e-SVM resembles instance-weighted SVMs and the paper fails to cite extensive prior work on weighted SVMs.
- Latent e-SVM similarity: the latent formulation is effectively a transductive/semi-supervised SVM with standard alternating optimization, but relevant literature is not discussed.
- Privileged information relation: connections to SVM+ and other privileged-information methods are missing.
- Sum-constraint novelty unclear: the proposed sum constraint on weights may be new but is not sufficiently highlighted or justified.
- Weak baselines: experiments omit standard unit-interval regression baselines (e.g., logistic regression, beta regression), weakening claims about the loss function.
- Hyperparameter transparency: selection and values for critical hyperparameters (λW, λR, etc.) are not described, hurting reproducibility.
- Feature vs. algorithm contribution unclear: the spatial confidence map and engineering may drive gains more than the e-SVM itself, but are under-described.
- Presentation misleading: title and abstract overclaim generality and novelty; bounded [0,1] regression is a standard problem.
- Exposition imbalance: too much space on simple algorithmic formulations and too little on key implementation/feature details needed for reproduction.
- Scholarly omission: fails to cite decades of relevant foundational machine learning literature, misrepresenting contribution.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong benchmark results, addresses an important practical weak-supervision problem, and introduces an effective spatial confidence map feature.
- Arguments for rejection: core algorithms are reformulations of existing methods, missing citations, weak experimental validation, and poor presentation.
- Required revisions: thoroughly review and cite weighted/transductive SVM literature and clearly state what is new.
- Title recommendation: change title to reflect the primarily empirical/application and feature-focused contribution.
- Additional experiments requested: compare e-SVM to standard regression methods on unit-interval targets.
- Reproducibility fixes: disclose hyperparameter tuning procedure and all implementation details (e.g., spatial pooling parameters).
- Feature description: expand and detail the spatial confidence map generation, as it appears central to results.
- Minor edits: fix typos and notation (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", standardize regularizer notation, define l+i/l-i, "datesets"→"datasets").
- Final verdict: not acceptable in current form due to originality and citation issues, but reviewer may raise score after substantial revision; possibly better suited for a top-tier vision venue.
