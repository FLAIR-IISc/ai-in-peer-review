This paper proposes a modification to the SVM learning algorithm related to weak supervision data. Specifically, each training example is assigned a per-example weight (between 0 and 1). The SVM's loss function is modified for this weight. The method is then extended to a situation where the per-example weight is not observed (i.e., there is a hidden variable).

The method is well-described, and experimental results indicate that using such an approach may be more valuable than treating each example with the same weight, especially for semantic segmentation and object recognition tasks. However, I do not see any difference between the proposed method and the existing "example-dependent costs," which are widely known and used in the machine learning community. For example, SVM-Lite provides such a mechanism.

The extension to a situation where the per-example weight is not observed is interesting but straightforward. The problem in this case is non-convex, and no theoretical analysis is provided. In fact, a strong regularization term is used that states the per-example weights do not deviate too much from their initial values. What can the authors comment on?

Minor comment: L050 claims that "this is not even a standard regression problem because the positivity is related to a finite interval [0, 1]." Logistic regression is largely standard and limited to [0, 1].
