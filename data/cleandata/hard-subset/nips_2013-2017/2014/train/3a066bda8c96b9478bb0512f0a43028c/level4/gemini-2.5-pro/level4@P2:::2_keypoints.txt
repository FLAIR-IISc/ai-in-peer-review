- Proposes e-SVM: model true binary labels as Bernoulli with known “positiveness” g(u) and minimize expected hinge loss, yielding a weighted SVM where each example contributes to both classes proportional to g(u) and 1−g(u).

- Introduces latent e-SVM for cases with unknown positiveness scores, alternating optimization of latent scores u and model parameters.

- Evaluated on PASCAL VOC for semantic segmentation and object detection.

- Empirical results: e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR for segment proposal classification.

- Empirical result: features from latent e-SVM combined with R-CNN achieve new state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007 (+1.5% mAP).

- Strength: Tackles an important, practical weak-supervision problem that reduces need for costly pixel-perfect annotations.

- Strength: Strong, competitive empirical improvements support the method’s utility.

- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear motivation and straightforward mathematical formulation.

- Strength: Intuitive, principled framing of labels as random variables avoids ad-hoc thresholding.

- Weakness: Novelty is unclear—e-SVM formulation may be functionally equivalent to per-example/per-class weighted SVMs; needs clearer differentiation and baseline comparison.

- Weakness: Latent model treatment is superficial—optimization is non-convex with no convergence analysis, and heavy KL regularization raises questions about how much u is actually learned; sensitivity analysis for λR and validation that u meaningfully changes are requested.

- Minor: Overlooks existing bounded-output regressors (e.g., logistic regression) and contains a typo in the abstract (“continues” → “continuous”).
