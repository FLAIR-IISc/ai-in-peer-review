- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for training with continuous labels in a bounded interval, applied to segment proposal classification and weakly supervised segment learning.
- Claims extensions to weak supervision using only bounding-box annotations and evaluations on semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Model is related to weighted SVMs and weakly supervised learning but the paper lacks a comprehensive review of pertinent prior work (missing key references like weighted SVM).
- The proposed "latent e-SVM" is essentially a weighted/transductive SVM; authors should explicitly detail connections and differences to existing methods.
- Empirically significant: e-SVM outperforms recent CNN results using a conventional learning approach.
- Object detection system involves heavy engineering; authors should describe how regularization coefficients were chosen.
- Main strength is strong empirical results demonstrating e-SVM’s effectiveness.
- Paper quality suffers from missing crucial references and insufficient comparisons to existing methods.
- Authors should include baseline results (e.g., regularized logistic regression) to justify the sum-constrained weighted SVM formulation.
- Clarity is generally good, but title and abstract misrepresent the core contribution and incorrectly imply regression targets in [0,1] are less informative than binary labels.
- Paper overemphasizes well-known algorithms and underreports feature-engineering and implementation details.
- Minor editorial fixes: “continues”→“continuous” (line 19); “boxes”→“box” (line 94); introduce “AP” (line 98); “SVM”→“SVC” and “with every different values”→“for all threshold values” (line 350).
- With rewriting, additional literature discussion, and more experimental detail, the paper could be suitable for publication in a vision venue.
