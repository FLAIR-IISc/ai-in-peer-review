- Proposes SVM modification that assigns each training example a weight in [0,1] to modulate its loss, with an extension to latent (unobserved) weights.  
- Paper is well-written and the method is clearly described.  
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection indicate improvements over uniform weighting.  
- Primary concern: limited novelty, since per-sample loss modulation is equivalent to existing "example-dependent costs" in SVMs (e.g., SVMlight).  
- Paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contribution from prior work on example-dependent costs.  
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but appears to be a straightforward alternating-optimization approach yielding a non-convex objective.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis: no guarantees on convergence or solution quality for the non-convex formulation.  
- Unclear sensitivity to the strong regularization on weights—may prevent weights from moving far from initialization; need clarification on how much is actually learned.  
- Minor points: claim that the problem is not standard regression is questionable (e.g., logistic regression handles bounded outputs), and there's a typo in the abstract ("continues" → "continuous").
