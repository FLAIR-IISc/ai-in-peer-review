The authors propose an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that accommodates continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem, and they apply it to train visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The approach replaces the binary selection of positive instances in standard multiple instance learning with soft weighting and is formulated for both observed and latent settings. The methodology is validated on semantic segmentation and object detection and yields promising results.

The paper addresses an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning. The motivation is strong, the presentation is clear, and the experiments are interesting and well executed. Although the technical contribution is largely an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework—substituting soft expectations for binary assignments—the formulation is sound, applies in both observed and latent scenarios, and represents a valid contribution for NIPS. Overall, the work is well motivated, clearly presented, and demonstrates relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
