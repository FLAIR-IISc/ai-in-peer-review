- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) assigning per-example weight in [0,1] and extends to latent-weight case.
- Methods clearly described and evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Empirical results show e-SVM can outperform SVC and SVR in some scenarios.
- Novelty questioned because example-dependent costs are already common; clarify differences from existing methods (e.g., SVMlight).
- Latent-weight extension makes the problem non-convex and lacks theoretical analysis of behavior or guarantees.
- Strong regularization likely keeps per-example weights near initial values; authors should comment and provide insight.
- Minor corrections needed (spelling “continues”→“continuous” and clarify line 50 about bounded targets — logistic regression handles bounded intervals).
- Strengths: addresses weak supervision, clear exposition, and promising empirical improvements.
- Weaknesses: questionable novelty, non-convex extension without theory, and minor errors/clarifications.
- For acceptance: novel algorithm for weak supervision with positive empirical results.
- Against acceptance: insufficient novelty and missing theoretical analysis for the non-convex latent case.
- Recommendation: clarify relation to prior work (SVMlight), analyze/interpret latent-weight behavior, and fix minor issues.
- Overall rating: 6/10 (confidence 8/10).
