- Summary: Proposes E-SVM that replaces binary instance indicators with expected (soft) weights to handle continuous attributes in observed and latent MIL settings, applied to segmentation and detection with promising results.

- Strength: Clearly motivated problem—handling continuous/soft positive attributes is important for weak supervision.

- Strength: Methodological contribution—principled generalization of MIL by using expectation-based instance weighting.

- Strength: Broad applicability—validated on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.

- Strength: Empirical results appear carefully designed and supportive of the approach.

- Strength: Presentation is generally clear and intuitive.

- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—soft weighting is a straightforward extension of MIL and needs sharper theoretical/algorithmic insights.

- Weakness: Optimization details are unclear—convexity in observed case, strategy for latent case, convergence guarantees, initialization sensitivity, and complexity are not fully specified.

- Weakness: Calibration and robustness concerns—the method’s sensitivity to mis-specified or noisy continuous attributes is not analyzed and may lead to over-reliance on uncertain weights.

- Weakness: Empirical analysis depth lacking—ablation studies, comparisons to strong baselines, and statistical significance tests are missing or insufficiently reported.

- Question: Provide explicit E-SVM objective and explain how expectations enter loss/margin; clarify convexity for observed setting and convergence properties for latent setting.

- Question: How are continuous attributes/soft weights obtained in practice for each task; are they normalized, calibrated, or modeled with uncertainty?

- Question: Do you regularize or constrain weights (e.g., sum-to-one, bounds, entropy penalties) and how does this affect performance?

- Question: What is the computational cost relative to SVM, MIL-SVM, and latent SVM in training time and memory, and does the method scale to large datasets/high-dimensional features?

- Question: How sensitive is performance to hyperparameters for expectation loss and regularization—please include sensitivity analysis or plots.

- Question: In which regimes do soft weights hurt performance (e.g., highly noisy or biased weights) and are there diagnostics to detect such failure modes?

- Suggestion: Strengthen theory by showing when expected-loss yields tighter bounds or better generalization and relate to label-proportion or soft-label risk frameworks.

- Suggestion: Detail optimization for the latent case—initialization, update rules, subproblem convexity, stopping criteria, and convergence behavior.

- Suggestion: Expand empirical comparisons to include MIL-SVM, latent SVM, recent weakly supervised methods, and ablations (binary vs expectation, observed vs latent, calibration/noise studies) with significance testing.

- Suggestion: Clarify how continuous attributes are derived in weak supervision (e.g., objectness, saliency, proportions) and whether task-specific heuristics are required.

- Suggestion: Include qualitative visualizations (heatmaps of soft weights, segmentation overlays) to illustrate how expectation weighting affects predictions.

- Suggestion: Discuss broader applicability beyond vision, limitations, future directions (e.g., end-to-end deep integration or joint learning of weights and classifiers).

- Minor comment: Use consistent terminology/capitalization (E-SVM vs expectation-loss SVM) and define acronyms at first use.

- Minor comment: Ensure notation consistency across observed and latent formulations and consider releasing code for reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Solid, relevant contribution with promising empirical support but requires clearer optimization details, deeper empirical/ablation studies, and robustness analysis before publication.
