

The authors propose an expectation loss SVM (E-SVM) that handles continuous attributes of positive instances in learning problems and apply it to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding promising results. The paper addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision, with a clear and well-motivated presentation and interesting, well-executed experiments. While the technical content extends the standard multiple instance learning framework by replacing binary with soft weighting of positive instances, it applies effectively in both observed and latent settings, constituting a valid contribution to NIPS. The motivation is strong, the presentation is clear, and the results for semantic segmentation and object detection are relevant.
