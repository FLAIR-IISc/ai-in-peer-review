- Proposes modifying SVM by assigning a per-example weight (in [0,1]) and incorporating it into the loss, with an extension to unobserved (latent) weights.  
- Method is well described and shows empirical benefits for semantic segmentation and object detection compared to equal-weight training.  
- Reviewer questions novelty due to similarity with existing example-dependent costs (e.g., SVMlight supports such mechanisms).  
- Extension to latent per-example weights is interesting but appears straightforward and makes the problem non-convex.  
- No theoretical analysis is provided for the non-convex latent-weight formulation.  
- Strong regularization is used in experiments, implying latent weights remain close to initial values; reviewer asks authors to comment.  
- Line 50 claim that the problem “is not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to [0,1]” is inaccurate; logistic regression handles bounded probabilities.  
- Minor spelling error in abstract: “continues” should be “continuous.”  
- Overall concern: approach may lack sufficient novelty given widespread use of example-dependent costs in ML.
