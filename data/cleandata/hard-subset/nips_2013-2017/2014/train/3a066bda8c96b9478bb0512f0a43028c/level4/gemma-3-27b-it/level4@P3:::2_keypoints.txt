- Proposes e-SVM: weight SVM loss by a continuous “positiveness” score (0–1) for weakly supervised labels.  
- Extends e-SVM to latent (unobserved) positiveness values using an iterative optimization procedure.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Builds on prior work in weak supervision and weighted SVMs; frames contribution as a novel expectation-based loss.  
- Strength: tackles a relevant weakly supervised learning problem where full annotation is costly.  
- Strength: introduces a reasonable and interpretable expectation loss to incorporate continuous confidence.  
- Strength: empirical results are competitive and convincing on benchmarks.  
- Strength: paper is generally clear and well explained.  
- Strength: handles latent positiveness values, broadening applicability.  
- Weakness: core idea (confidence-weighted examples) is incremental and closely related to existing weighted SVM methods.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis or convergence guarantees for the non-convex latent-variable optimization.  
- Weakness: contains minor technical inaccuracies (e.g., mischaracterizing logistic regression).  
- Weakness: limited ablation study on regularization, features, and other hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: claimed generality is overstated due to reliance on segment proposals and IoU for positiveness.  
- For acceptance: addresses a relevant challenge, is well-motivated, and shows clear empirical improvements.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient novelty, limited theoretical treatment, and inadequate comparison to closely related weighted-SVM work.  
- Overall: an incremental but interesting contribution with strong experiments tempered by theoretical and related-work concerns.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, contingent on clearer differentiation from weighted SVMs, theoretical justification for the latent extension, fixing technical errors, and a more thorough ablation study.
