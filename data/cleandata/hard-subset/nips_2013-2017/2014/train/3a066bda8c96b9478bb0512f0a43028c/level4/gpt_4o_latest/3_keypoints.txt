- Method: regress IoU between candidate segments and pixel-accurate ground truth for semantic labeling; treat precise object extent as latent for detection and use weighted SVM / weighted transductive SVM.  
- Results: Competitive semantic labeling and best-reported object detection on VOC 2007.  
- Originality concern: Limited algorithmic novelty; weighted SVM is well-established and key prior work is not cited.  
- Novelty qualifier: The proposed sum constraint on weights might be new but is not thoroughly validated.  
- Latent e-SVM critique: Essentially a weighted transductive SVM and missing citations to foundational work.  
- Significance: Noteworthy that a conventional method outperforms recent CNN approaches — of substantial community interest.  
- Missing baselines: Should compare to standard regularized logistic regression for regression on [0,1].  
- Reproducibility/engineering: Framework involves heavy engineering (normal for field) but paper omits critical reference details and hyperparameter selection (λW, λR).  
- Clarity issues: Title/abstract fail to convey main contributions and make a misleading claim about regression vs binary labels.  
- Presentation balance: Excessive space spent on minor algorithmic variants and insufficient detail on crucial feature engineering.  
- Minor edits: typos and wording fixes (e.g., "continues"→"continuous"; "boxes"→"box"; introduce "AP"; "SVM"→"SVC"; "with every different values"→"for all threshold values").  
- Recommendation: Reviewer raised quality score contingent on added citations; paper has strong empirical results but needs more references, clearer novelty, and clarity improvements — likely publishable after revision and possibly better suited to a vision venue.
