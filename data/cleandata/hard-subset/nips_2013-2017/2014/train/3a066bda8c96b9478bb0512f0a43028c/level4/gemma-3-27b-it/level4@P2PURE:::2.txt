This paper introduces a modification to the SVM learning algorithm designed for weakly supervised data. The approach assigns a weight between 0 and 1 to each training example, incorporating this weighting into the SVM loss function. The method is further extended to handle scenarios where these per-example weights are latent and unobserved. 

The paper is clearly written and the experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest a potential benefit to this weighting scheme compared to treating all examples equally. However, the core idea appears closely related to, and lacks clear distinction from, existing techniques employing example-dependent costs – a well-established practice in machine learning, exemplified by functionality within SVMlight.

The extension to latent per-example weights is a noteworthy contribution, although it introduces a non-convex problem for which no theoretical analysis is provided. The reliance on a strong regularization term, suggesting the learned weights remain close to their initial values, raises a question: could the authors elaborate on the rationale for this regularization and its impact on the solution?

A minor point: the assertion on line 50 that the problem is “not a standard regression problem” due to the bounded output interval [0, 1] is inaccurate, as logistic regression is a standard regression technique with outputs constrained to this same interval. Additionally, a minor spelling error exists in the abstract: "continues" should be "continuous."

In summary, the paper proposes modifying the SVM learning algorithm with a per-example weight in the loss function; however, example-dependent costs are already a common practice in machine learning.
