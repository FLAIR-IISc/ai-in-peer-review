- Proposes modifying SVMs by assigning continuous per-example weights in [0,1] and extending this to a latent setting where weights are learned jointly with the classifier.  
- Method is well-described and experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show advantages over equal weighting.  
- Novelty is unclear relative to existing "example-dependent costs" in SVMs (e.g., SVMlight); authors should clarify what differs from established per-example cost mechanisms.  
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but appears straightforward.  
- Leads to a non-convex optimization problem with no theoretical analysis of convergence or solution quality provided.  
- Strong regularization is used, raising concerns that learned weights may remain near initialization; authors should analyze sensitivity to the regularizer.  
- Minor: Claim that boundedness makes the problem "not a standard regression problem" is inaccurate (e.g., logistic regression outputs in [0,1]).  
- Minor: Typo in abstract—"continues" should be "continuous."
