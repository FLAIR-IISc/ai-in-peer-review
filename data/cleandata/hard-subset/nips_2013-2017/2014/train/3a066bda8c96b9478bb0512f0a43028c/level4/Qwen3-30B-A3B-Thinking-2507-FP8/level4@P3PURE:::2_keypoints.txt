- Proposes modifying SVM loss with per-example weights in [0,1], and extends this to latent per-example weights.  
- Method is clearly described; experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential gains over uniform weighting.  
- Unclear distinction from existing "example-dependent costs" (standard in ML and implemented in tools like SVMlight), making the core contribution unconvincing.  
- Latent-weight extension is conceptually straightforward but results in a non-convex problem and lacks theoretical analysis.  
- The approach relies on strong regularization that keeps weights close to initial values; necessity of this regularization and possible alternatives are not justified.  
- Incorrect claim: saying it is not a standard regression because outputs lie in [0,1] is false (e.g., logistic regression).  
- Minor typo: Abstract uses "continues" instead of "continuous."  
- Overall, the proposal does not clearly advance beyond established example-dependent cost frameworks, and experiments are insufficient to establish novelty.
