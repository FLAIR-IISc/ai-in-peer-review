- Proposes per-example weights in SVM loss and extension to latent (unobserved) per-example weights.  
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential benefit over equal-weighting.  
- Method appears similar to existing "example dependent costs" (e.g., SVMlight) — reviewer sees little novelty.  
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but straightforward and yields a non-convex problem.  
- No theoretical analysis provided for the non-convex latent-weight formulation.  
- Strong regularization is used, implying learned weights stay close to initial values — reviewer asks for comment.  
- Claim L050 about not being a standard regression problem is inaccurate; logistic regression produces bounded [0,1] outputs.  
- Minor typo in abstract: "continues" should be "continuous".  
- Paper is technically sound and experiments are convincing.  
- Writing is clear and easy to follow, though some sentences are long and convoluted.  
- Overall originality is low; method is similar to prior work and extension is simple.  
- Significance is moderate: useful for some tasks but reduced by lack of novelty and theory.  
- Arguments for acceptance: useful method, convincing experiments, well written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: similarity to prior methods, straightforward extension, lack of theoretical analysis, limited originality.
