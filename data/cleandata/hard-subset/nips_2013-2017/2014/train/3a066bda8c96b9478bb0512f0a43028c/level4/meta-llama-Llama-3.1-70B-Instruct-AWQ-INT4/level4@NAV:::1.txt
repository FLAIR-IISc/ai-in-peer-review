The authors introduce an expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) capable of accommodating continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem, and successfully apply this model to train visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The proposed methodology is substantiated through its effectiveness in both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results. This paper tackles a significant challenge in visual learning under weak supervision, presenting a clear and well-motivated framework. The experiments conducted are not only interesting but also skillfully executed. Although the technical content builds upon the standard multiple instance learning framework by substituting binary weighting with soft weighting of positives, its applicability in both observed and latent settings renders it a valuable contribution to the NIPS community. The e-SVM method demonstrates its potential in handling continuous attributes of positive instances, and its application to visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision is well-motivated, clearly presented, and yields relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
