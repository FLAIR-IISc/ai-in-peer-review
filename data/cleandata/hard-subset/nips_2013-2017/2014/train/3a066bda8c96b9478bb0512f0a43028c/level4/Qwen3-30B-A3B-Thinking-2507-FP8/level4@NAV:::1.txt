

The authors introduce an expectation loss SVM (E-SVM) capable of handling continuous attributes of positive instances within learning problems, applying it to train visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is rigorously validated for both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results. The paper effectively addresses a significant challenge in visual learning under weak supervision, supported by clear motivation, a well-structured presentation, and interesting, well-executed experiments. While the technical approach extends the standard multiple instance learning framework by substituting binary with soft weighting of positives, its applicability across both observed and latent settings constitutes a valid and meaningful contribution for NIPS.
