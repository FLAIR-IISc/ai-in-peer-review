- Presents a framework for semantic labeling (IoU regression on candidate segments) and object detection (latent spatial extent with weighted/transductive SVMs), achieving state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; proposed methods are close to established weighted SVM and transductive SVM approaches.  
- Significant omission of relevant prior work and citations (weighted SVM, transductive SVM, SVM+ literature).  
- The introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but is not well contextualized against prior art.  
- Main significance is strong empirical results that outperform recent deep-learning methods using classical techniques.  
- Paper quality suffers from missing citations, insufficient experimental baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression), and lack of implementation details (selection of λW and λR).  
- Feature-engineering complexity is high but not unusually so for the field.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract do not clearly state contributions, and the claim that continuous regression targets are less informative than binary labels is incorrect.  
- Exposition is unbalanced: too much space on minor algorithm variants and too little on crucial feature-pipeline details.  
- Minor editorial/notation errors (typos and unclear abbreviations) listed for correction.  
- Reviewer raised score assuming authors will address citation and related-work concerns; suggests substantial revision and possible better fit for a vision conference despite publication-worthiness.
