This paper introduces an expectation loss SVM (e-svm), a formulation designed to handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning framework. The authors apply this model to the task of learning visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is empirically validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding promising results.

The work addresses a significant and relevant problem in visual learning. The motivation is strong, the presentation is clear, and the experiments are both interesting and well-executed. While the core technical contribution can be viewed as an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework—substituting a binary weighting of positives with a soft one—its applicability in both observed and latent settings makes it a solid and valuable contribution suitable for this conference.
