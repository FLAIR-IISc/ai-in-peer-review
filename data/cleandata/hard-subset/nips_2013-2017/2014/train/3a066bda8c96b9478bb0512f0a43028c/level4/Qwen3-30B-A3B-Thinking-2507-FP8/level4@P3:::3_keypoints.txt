- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) and latent e-SVM for learning from continuous "positiveness" values and unobserved positiveness.
- Applied to semantic segmentation and object detection; integrating spatial confidence maps with RCNN yields +1.5% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Motivated by practical need to leverage abundant bounding-box annotations instead of expensive pixel-level labels.
- Method is essentially a weighted SVM with weights derived from positiveness constrained to sum to 1; latent variant resembles transductive SVMs.
- Fails to cite foundational weighted SVM and transductive SVM work (e.g., Suykens et al. 2002; Yang et al. 2005; Lapin et al. 2014; Vapnik 1998).
- Originality is limited — the main algorithmic change is a minor variation on established weighted SVM approaches.
- Strong empirical results but lacks comparison to natural baselines like regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression.
- Paper omits important methodological details, notably hyperparameter selection (λ_W, λ_R) and feature engineering specifics.
- Title and abstract mischaracterize contribution and overstate the novelty/informativeness of continuous labels.
- Significance is under-argued — the 1.5% gain is notable but not well contextualized against recent deep learning advances.
- Pros: strong empirical performance, clear practical motivation, general framework, effective use of bounding-box annotations.
- Cons: missing key citations, limited novelty, overstated significance, and poorly articulated technical contribution.
- Recommendation: weak acceptance contingent on substantial revision to properly contextualize with prior weighted/transductive SVM literature and clarify contributions.
