- Proposes joint scheme for semantic labeling (regressing IoU of candidate segments) and object detection (using bounding-box labels with precise extent as latent variables), trained with weighted SVMs and weighted transductive SVMs.  
- Empirical results: competitive state-of-the-art semantic labeling and best-known object detection on VOC2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; many ideas (weighted SVMs, transductive SVMs) are established and not sufficiently cited.  
- The sum constraint on weights may be a modest novel contribution but requires literature verification.  
- The proposed "latent e-SVM" appears to be essentially a weighted transductive SVM, not a fundamentally new model.  
- Significance: noteworthy that a conventional method outperforms recent CNN results, showing traditional techniques remain effective.  
- Major quality issues: missing crucial prior-work references and limited methodological novelty beyond engineering choices.  
- Methodological concerns: regression on the unit interval is not especially novel vs. logistic regression; need baseline comparisons for the sum-constrained weighted SVM.  
- Missing experimental details: selection and justification of regularization coefficients λW and λR need to be provided.  
- Clarity problems: title/abstract misrepresent core contributions and contain a misleading claim about regression targets being more informative than binary labels.  
- Paper allocation: too much space on well-known algorithms and too little on critical feature engineering details for a NIPS audience.  
- Minor editorial fixes: several typos and omissions (e.g., "continues"→"continuous"; introduce "AP"; replace "SVM" with "SVC"; wording fixes).  
- Recommendation: publishable after rewriting and better literature/context placement; possibly more suitable for a computer vision venue.
