- Proposes Expectation-SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous attributes of positive instances in weakly supervised learning.  
- Replaces binary MIL assignments with soft expectation-based weighting to leverage continuous features.  
- Extension is applicable in both observed and latent settings, increasing practical utility.  
- Methodology is clearly presented with solid mathematical formulation and helpful figures.  
- Experimental evaluation on semantic segmentation and object detection shows consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Appropriate datasets and rigorous experimental setup; some results are notably strong.  
- Contribution is meaningful but incremental relative to standard MIL extensions.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical analysis differentiating e-SVM from existing approaches.  
- Minor experimental details (e.g., parameter sensitivity) could be clarified or explored.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
