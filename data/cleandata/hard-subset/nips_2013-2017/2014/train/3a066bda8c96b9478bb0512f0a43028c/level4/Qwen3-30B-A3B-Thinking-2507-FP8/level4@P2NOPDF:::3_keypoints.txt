- Proposal: unified framework for semantic labeling (IoU regression over candidate segments) and object detection (treating precise extent as latent, using weighted SVMs); matches SOTA for labeling and best reported VOC2007 detection.

- Originality concern: limited algorithmic novelty and failure to cite key prior work on weighted SVMs and latent SVMs; sum-weight constraint may be novel but does not overcome omission of prior art.

- Significance: strong empirical results—outperforming recent CNNs with non-deep methods—demonstrate continued viability of conventional techniques and are of broad interest.

- Quality issues: critical missing citations, mostly engineering-level detection pipeline, and need to compare to standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression); hyperparameter selection (λ_W, λ_R) not sufficiently described.

- Clarity problems: title/abstract misrepresent contributions and incorrectly claim regression targets are less informative than binary labels; paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants and underreports feature-engineering details.

- Minor editorial fixes: typos and terminology corrections (e.g., "continues"→"continuous"; "boxes"→"box"; define "AP" as Average Precision; use "SVC" vs "SVM"; rephrase threshold wording).

- Overall recommendation: empirically strong but needs better citations and methodological transparency; reviewer would accept if authors address citation gaps, clarify hyperparameters/baselines—suggested venue possibly CVPR but acceptable at NIPS after revision.
