- Proposes e-SVM that minimizes expected hinge loss using soft "positiveness" scores µi = g(ui), implemented with standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Latent e-SVM treats ui as latent, initialized from bbox IoU (ubb) and regularized by KL to ubb, optimized by alternating minimization.  
- Experiments: segment confidence (TAPC/NDCG), semantic segmentation (PASCAL VOC 2011), and detection (PASCAL VOC 2007) with detection gaining ~1.2–1.5 AP by adding spatial confidence maps to RCNN features.  
- Positioning vs prior work is unclear: formulation closely resembles instance-weighted SVMs and soft-label/probabilistic SVMs and needs formal comparison.  
- Observed-label formulation is convex and straightforward; latent extension is non-convex with no convergence guarantees or theoretical analysis.  
- New metrics TAPC and NDCG are sensible for segment confidence prediction but require precise definitions and protocol details for reproducibility.  
- Empirical results: e-SVM outperforms thresholded SVC and slightly beats SVR on segment confidence; comparable to SVR for semantic segmentation.  
- Detection improvements are modest but consistent; the spatial confidence map feature is simple and practical.  
- Notation and terminology need tightening (e.g., prefer "soft label" or "class membership probability" over "positiveness") and minor typos should be fixed.  
- Conceptual novelty is limited given overlap with example-dependent cost and soft-label SVM methods; latent extension is reasonable but straightforward.  
- Practical significance: easy integration with existing SVM tooling, but impact depends on clearer distinctions from established methods.  
- Requested experiments: formal equivalence/ablation vs WSVM/SVMlight-style weighting and probabilistic-label SVMs.  
- Latent e-SVM sensitivity analyses requested: effect of λR, initialization choice, number of alternations, and how much learned ui deviate from ubb.  
- Ask about choice of mapping g(ui) (used g(ui)=ui) and robustness to other monotonic mappings (e.g., temperature scaling).  
- Missing baselines: logistic regression and probabilistic SVM with calibrated probabilities should be included.  
- Detection ablations requested: per-class gains, RCNN alone vs RCNN+e-SVM features vs SVR features, and sensitivity to spatial binning/pooling choices.  
- Evaluation details requested: fully define TAPC/NDCG computation (threshold grid, class averaging, tie handling) and report variance/confidence intervals.  
- Pros: clear, simple formulation for soft labels; practical latent extension with convex regularizer; sensible evaluation; measurable detection gains.  
- Cons: unclear novelty vs existing methods; non-convex latent optimization with heavy regularization may limit learning; missing probabilistic baselines; modest detection gains.  
- Minor fixes: clarify contrast with regression/logistic approaches, fix "continues"→"continuous", standardize "e-SVM"/"eSVM", and correct equation/reference formatting.
