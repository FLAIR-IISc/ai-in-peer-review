- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle training labels that are continuous within a bounded interval.
- e-SVM applied to segment proposal classification and extendable to weak supervision using bounding-box annotations.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.
- Results are impressive, outperforming recent CNN-based object detection methods.
- Main significance: achieves state-of-the-art detection with a conventional learning approach.
- Fails to cite pertinent prior work (weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs) and lacks acknowledgment of related methods.
- The proposed "latent e-SVM" is essentially a weighted/transductive SVM variant, a connection the authors do not discuss.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; more context and references to prior work are needed.
- Paper lacks experimental details (choice of regularization coefficients, feature engineering) that are crucial for reproducibility.
- Clarity issues: title and abstract do not accurately summarize core contributions and too much space spent on well-known algorithms.
- Feature engineering details are glossed over despite being central to the results.
- Quality: strong empirical results but needs to address missing references and provide more methodological context.
- Overall well-written but requires rewriting to add references, context, and experimental details.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept (rating 7/10) provided the authors address the noted concerns.
