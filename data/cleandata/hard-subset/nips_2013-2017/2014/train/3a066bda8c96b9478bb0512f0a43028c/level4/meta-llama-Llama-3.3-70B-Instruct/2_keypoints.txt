- Proposes SVM variant that incorporates a per-example weight in [0,1] into the loss, and generalizes this to latent (unobserved) per-example weights.
- Methodology is clearly described and experiments show potential gains over uniform weighting on semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Fails to clearly distinguish the method from existing example-dependent cost approaches; similar functionality exists in tools like SVMlight.
- The latent-weight extension is straightforward and yields a non-convex problem, but no theoretical analysis of this non-convexity is provided.
- Strong regularization keeps learned weights close to their initial values; the paper lacks insight or justification for this design choice.
- Incorrect claim that bounded [0,1] targets make the problem non-standard regression—logistic regression is a counterexample.
- Minor typo in the abstract: "continues" should be "continuous."
- Overall contribution appears similar to widely known example-dependent cost functions, raising questions about novelty.
