- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from weak labels that are real-valued “positiveness” scores rather than binary labels.
- Core idea: weight each training example in the SVM loss by its positiveness value, using it as a confidence score.
- Extends approach to a latent variable setting when positiveness scores are unobserved, requiring iterative optimization and regularization.
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, with particularly strong results on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Motivated by the practical need to leverage weakly labeled data to avoid costly full annotations.
- Contrasts with ad-hoc binarization heuristics and SVR, arguing e-SVM is more appropriate for outputs bounded in [0,1].
- Strength: clear motivation, well-defined algorithm, and practical extension to latent variables.
- Strength: strong empirical validation and consistent outperformance of SVC and SVR on benchmark tasks.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art object detection results and shows applicability across detectors, features, and base learners.
- Strength: experimental framework appears rigorous and includes both strong and weak supervision scenarios.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relation to existing weighted SVM methods and need to differentiate the contribution more clearly.
- Weakness: uniqueness of weighting labels versus data points should be articulated and compared to prior work [23,27].
- Weakness: latent-variable regularization is crucial but lacks theoretical justification or sensitivity analysis of the regularization parameter.
- Weakness: claim about logistic regression being non-standard for bounded outputs needs a clearer explanation beyond the bounded-interval point.
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, clearly defined method, strong benchmark performance, and thorough experiments.
- Against acceptance: limited novelty due to similarities with existing weighted SVM approaches.
- Against acceptance: reliance on empirical results without theoretical analysis for the latent variable setting.
- Against acceptance: requires more detailed comparisons and discussion of related weighted SVM literature to strengthen the contribution.
