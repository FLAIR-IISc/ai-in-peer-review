- Summary: Proposes a unified IoU-regression framework for semantic labeling and object detection using candidate segments, weighted SVMs, and latent-variable transductive SVMs; reports SOTA detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 and competitive semantic labeling.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance that surpasses recent CNN-based methods using engineered features and SVMs, making a noteworthy contribution to the community.

- Weakness: Limited algorithmic novelty and missing citations—weighted SVM and transductive SVM ideas appear to repeat prior work without adequate attribution.

- Weakness: Insufficient experimental baselines—no comparison to simpler alternatives like regularized logistic regression to justify the chosen model.

- Weakness: Missing methodological details—hyperparameter selection (e.g., λW and λR) is not described, harming reproducibility.

- Weakness: Clarity and presentation problems—title/abstract misstate the core contribution, incorrect claim about continuous vs. binary labels, too much space on SVM variants, too little on critical feature engineering.

- Overall recommendation: Results merit publication but the paper requires substantial revision to address literature review, novelty clarification, and stronger experimental comparisons.

- Minor comments: Typos and phrasing fixes needed (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box", define "AP", likely "SVC" instead of "SVM", rephrase threshold wording).
