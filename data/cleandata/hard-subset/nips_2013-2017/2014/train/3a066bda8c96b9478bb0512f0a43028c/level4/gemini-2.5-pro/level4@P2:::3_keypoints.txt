- Proposal of e-SVM: model binary labels as latent Bernoulli variables with given expected "positiveness" in [0,1], minimizing expected hinge loss yields an instance-weighted SVM formulation.  
- Two settings: strong supervision (known positiveness from pixel annotations) and weak supervision (latent positiveness from bounding boxes optimized alternately, akin to transductive SVM).  
- Applied to segmentation and detection; combining features with R-CNN yields state-of-the-art results, +1.5% AP on PASCAL VOC 2007 vs. R-CNN baseline.  
- Strength — empirical: strong, convincing improvements on a competitive benchmark.  
- Strength — formulation: principled way to incorporate continuous-valued supervision (e.g., IoU) into a max-margin framework.  
- Strength — applicability: framework is general and can integrate different proposal methods, features, and base detectors.  
- Weakness — novelty: method is essentially a weighted SVM and the weakly supervised variant resembles transductive SVM; limited algorithmic novelty.  
- Weakness — missing citations: fails to cite key prior work on weighted SVMs, weighted least squares, SVM+, and transductive learning.  
- Suggestion — contextualization: explicitly discuss and compare e-SVM to weighted SVM and latent e-SVM to transductive SVM, and add the missing references.  
- Suggestion — baselines and evaluation: include regularized logistic regression baseline, describe hyperparameter selection (λ_w, λ_R), and disentangle gains from feature engineering vs. the learning method.  
- Clarity — writing: revise title/abstract to reflect application of established principles rather than claiming a fundamentally new algorithm; rephrase misleading statements about continuous vs. binary labels.  
- Clarity — paper structure: shorten familiar SVM derivations and expand on engineering details (e.g., spatial confidence map, Section 4.2) that drive performance.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos and caption/text issues (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", define "AP", fix Figure 3 caption and phrasing).  
- Conclusion: strong empirical contribution likely worthy of publication if revised to properly contextualize prior work and clarify methodological vs. engineering contributions.
