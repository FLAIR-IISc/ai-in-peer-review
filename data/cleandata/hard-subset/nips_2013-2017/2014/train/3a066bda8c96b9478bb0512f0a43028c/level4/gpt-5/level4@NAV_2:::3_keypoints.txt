- Proposes a framework for segmentation (IoU prediction per candidate segment) and detection (latent fine-grained extent with weighted SVM / latent weighted SVM), matching state-of-the-art segmentation and best VOC2007 detection.
- Methodological novelty is modest; several relevant prior works on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs are not cited.
- The paper’s weighted SVM formulation (sum-constrained complementary weights) may be a small twist but is not shown to be clearly novel.
- The “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted analogue of transductive SVMs and lacks citation to key precedents.
- Empirically significant: outperforming recent CNN-based systems with a classical approach is noteworthy and broadly interesting.
- Important related work is missing and must be addressed to improve the paper’s quality.
- Baseline comparisons are incomplete: regularized logistic regression is a natural baseline and should be reported to justify the proposed formulation.
- Experimental details need clarification, specifically how regularization parameters λW and λR were chosen.
- Clarity issues: title and abstract do not clearly state the core contribution and mistakenly downplay continuous labels; excessive space is spent on minor method variations while feature engineering is under-described.
- Minor editorial fixes: typos (“continues”→“continuous”, “boxes”→“box”), define AP at first use, replace “SVM”→“SVC” at line 350, and correct phrasing for thresholds.
- Recommendation: strong empirical results warrant publication after revisions addressing prior work, baselines, clarity, and presentation.
