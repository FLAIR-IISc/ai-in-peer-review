- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for weak supervision using a “positiveness” confidence per sample.
- Core idea: incorporate positiveness as a weight in the SVM loss and infer it as a latent variable when unobserved.
- Demonstrated strong empirical performance on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, with SOTA on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Motivation is clear: learning from weakly supervised data is important for vision where full annotations are costly.
- Extension to latent variable estimation is practical and a logical progression for weak supervision scenarios.
- Evaluation includes both strong and weak supervision settings, strengthening empirical claims.
- Novelty is limited: approach closely overlaps with existing weighted/instance-dependent cost SVM techniques.
- Paper needs a more thorough discussion of related work on weighted SVMs and clearer statement of specific contributions beyond re-framing.
- Latent-variable extension lacks theoretical justification; optimization may be non-convex with local optima concerns.
- Heavy reliance on a strong regularizer to constrain positiveness values raises questions about sensitivity and choice of regularization.
- Authors should analyze impact of regularization strength and consider alternative regularization strategies.
- Minor technical inaccuracies: mischaracterizes logistic regression and contains a typo in the abstract.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, clear algorithm, competitive benchmarks, practical latent extension.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient theoretical analysis for latent case, and minor technical errors.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting expanded related-work discussion and deeper analysis of latent-variable optimization.
