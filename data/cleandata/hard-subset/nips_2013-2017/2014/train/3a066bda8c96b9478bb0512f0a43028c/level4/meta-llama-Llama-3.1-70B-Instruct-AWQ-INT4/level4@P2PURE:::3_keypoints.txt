- Proposes a framework that regressess segment IoU for semantic labeling and treats precise object extent as latent for detection using weighted SVMs/transductive SVMs.  
- Achieves comparable semantic labeling results and state-of-the-art object detection results on VOC 2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; many components (weighted SVM, transductive SVM) are known and not properly cited.  
- Authors omit pertinent prior work and citations (examples: Suykens et al. 2002; Yang et al. 2005; Vapnik 1998; Lapin et al. 2014).  
- The proposed sum constraint on weights might be novel but requires literature verification.  
- The “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and should be related to existing work.  
- Significant contribution: conventional (non-CNN) approach outperforms recent CNN results, which is of community interest.  
- Major concern: missing crucial references undermines credibility.  
- Paper should include a baseline using regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression to justify the weighted SVM choice.  
- More detail required on selection/tuning of regularization coefficients λW and λR.  
- Title and abstract do not accurately summarize core contributions and contain a misleading statement about information content of regression targets vs. binary labels.  
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variations and underexplains critical feature engineering details.  
- Minor writing/term issues: “continues”→“continuous”; “boxes”→“box”; introduce “AP”; use “SVC” and rephrase thresholding wording.  
- Overall: strong empirical results but lacking references and novelty; recommend publication after rewriting and addressing deficiencies.
