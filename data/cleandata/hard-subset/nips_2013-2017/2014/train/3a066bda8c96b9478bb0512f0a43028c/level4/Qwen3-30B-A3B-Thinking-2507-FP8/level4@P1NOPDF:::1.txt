

The authors propose an expectation-loss SVM (E-SVM) that handles continuous-valued attributes of positive instances in learning problems and apply it to train visual segmentation models under weak supervision. The methodology is validated for both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results. The paper addresses a significant challenge in visual learning under weak supervision, with a well-motivated problem statement, clear presentation, and well-executed experiments. While the technical approach extends the standard multiple-instance learning (MIL) framework by replacing binary weighting with soft weighting of positive instances, it is applicable in both observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution for NIPS. The core idea—leveraging continuous-valued positive instance attributes to refine weakly supervised learning—provides a meaningful extension to existing MIL paradigms. The experimental results on standard benchmarks demonstrate the model’s effectiveness, with clear ablation studies supporting the proposed design choices. The writing remains accessible throughout, and the connection between the methodological extension and its practical impact on segmentation and detection tasks is well articulated. One minor concern is the lack of explicit discussion on computational overhead compared to standard MIL approaches, though this does not significantly undermine the contribution. Overall, the work presents a solid, well-executed advancement in weakly supervised visual learning, and the results are compelling enough to warrant publication at NIPS.
