- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous "positiveness" annotations and extends to latent-variable weak supervision, achieving 60.0 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Claims state-of-the-art object detection, outperforming R-CNN by 1.5 mAP and showing competitive semantic segmentation results.  
- Originality concerns: core weighted SVM idea is well-established and related work (e.g., Lapin et al. 2014, Suykens et al. 2002) is not cited.  
- The sum-constrained weight and "latent e-SVM" offer only minor technical distinctions and resemble existing weighted/transductive SVM formulations.  
- Empirical quality is strong but method rigor is undermined by critical omissions.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to logistic regression, the standard approach for [0,1] targets.  
- Hyperparameters (λ_W, λ_R) lack justification and the regularizer R(u) is not compared to alternatives.  
- Engineering and implementation details (feature extraction, spatial confidence maps) are underdeveloped relative to algorithmic description.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract overstate novelty of continuous labels vs. binary thresholding.  
- Paper prioritizes algorithmic variants (up to p.4) over crucial feature engineering and practical details; key terms (e.g., "AP") are undefined.  
- Significance: achieving competitive SOTA detection without deep learning is practically valuable for annotation-cost reduction.  
- Strengths: strong empirical validation across tasks, clear latent-variable extension, practical applicability to limited-annotation datasets.  
- Weaknesses: omission of foundational weighted-SVM literature, lack of logistic regression baseline, insufficient hyperparameter/engineering detail, overemphasis on algorithms.  
- Recommendation: major revisions required—address prior work, add logistic regression baseline, and clarify feature engineering and hyperparameter choices for acceptance.
