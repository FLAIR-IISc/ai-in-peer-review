- Presents a scheme for semantic labeling via IoU regression on candidate segments and object detection treating precise object extent as a latent variable using weighted SVMs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art semantic labeling and best-known VOC 2007 object detection results.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; core methods (weighted SVM, transductive SVM) are well-established and several relevant works are not cited.  
- The introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but is not thoroughly situated in prior literature.  
- The “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and lacks citation to foundational transductive SVM literature.  
- Outperforming recent CNNs with conventional methods is a significant and noteworthy contribution.  
- Missing baseline: regularized logistic regression for regression-on-[0,1] should be included to justify the weighted SVM choice.  
- Paper lacks details on selection of regularization coefficients λ_W and λ_R.  
- Paper’s strength is strong empirical results, but several prior-work references are missing.  
- Title and abstract do not effectively summarize core contributions and contain misleading phrasing about regression targets.  
- Too much space is spent on minor algorithmic variations while crucial feature-engineering details are covered too briefly.  
- Multiple minor writing/typo issues (e.g., “continues”→“continuous”, define “AP”, “SVM”→“SVC”, phrasing fixes).  
- Reviewer raised quality score expecting authors to address prior-work connections and commentary.  
- Recommendation: publishable (possibly after revisions), potentially a better fit for a computer vision conference.
