- Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models continuous “positiveness” scores for instances.  
- Uses continuous positiveness to train segmentation classifiers under weak supervision.  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with encouraging outcomes.  
- Targets an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning.  
- Exposition is clear and well-motivated.  
- Experiments are interesting and carefully executed.  
- Technical contribution is primarily an extension of multiple-instance learning by replacing binary labels with soft weighting of positives.  
- Method operates in both observed and latent settings.  
- Overall a legitimate NIPS contribution with solid motivation, clear presentation, and relevant results.
