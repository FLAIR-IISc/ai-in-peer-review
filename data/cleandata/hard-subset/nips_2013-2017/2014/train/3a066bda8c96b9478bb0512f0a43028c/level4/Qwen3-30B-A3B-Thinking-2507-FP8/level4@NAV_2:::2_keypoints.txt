- Paper proposes an SVM-based algorithm for examples with continuous "positiveness" in [0,1], incorporating per-example weights and a latent-weight extension for unobserved positiveness.  
- Method is thoroughly described and evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing consistent improvements over SVC and SVR.  
- Main technical idea—incorporating example-dependent weights into the SVM loss—is a special case of well-known weighted SVM techniques.  
- The paper fails to adequately distinguish its approach from existing weighted-SVM literature and implementations (e.g., SVMlight).  
- The weakly supervised (latent positiveness) extension is conceptually straightforward but lacks theoretical analysis.  
- The non-convex optimization requires strong regularization to keep latent weights near initial values, with no justification for this choice or discussion of theoretical implications.  
- Incorrect technical claim that the problem is not standard regression because outputs lie in [0,1]; logistic regression handles bounded outputs.  
- Minor typo in the abstract: "continues" should be "continuous."
