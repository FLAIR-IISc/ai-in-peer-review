- Method: generate candidate segments, extract features, regress IoU with pixel-accurate ground truth for semantic labeling; treat spatial extent as latent for detection and use weighted SVM / weighted transductive SVM.  
- Results: achieves state-of-the-art semantic labeling and best-known object detection on VOC 2007; conventional approach outperforms recent CNNs.  
- Novelty concern: limited algorithmic novelty; many related weighted-SVM works not cited.  
- Latent e-SVM: essentially a weighted version of transductive SVM; not clearly novel.  
- Sum constraint on weights: may be a novel contribution but requires thorough literature check.  
- Referencing: paper lacks crucial citations (e.g., weighted SVM literature R1–R4).  
- Engineering vs innovation: object detection framework is engineering-heavy and not particularly innovative.  
- Missing methodological details: need justification for regularization coefficients λW and λR and results using (regularized) logistic regression baseline for unit-interval regression.  
- Clarity and framing: title/abstract do not accurately state core contribution and mischaracterize informativeness of regression targets; too much space on basic algorithms and too little on important feature engineering.  
- Minor issues: typos and wording errors ("continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box"), AP not defined, "SVM"→"SVC" and phrasing fixes needed.  
- Recommendation: acceptable for publication after rewriting to fix references, clarity, and methodological details; possibly better suited to a computer vision venue.
