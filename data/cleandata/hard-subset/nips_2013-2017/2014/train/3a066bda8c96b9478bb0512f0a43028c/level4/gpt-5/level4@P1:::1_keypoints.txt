- Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models continuous “positiveness” values in [0,1] for binary labels.  
- Supports both settings where positiveness is observed and where it is latent.  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising empirical results.  
- Addresses the important problem of learning from weakly supervised data.  
- Paper is clear, well-motivated, and experimentally thorough.  
- Technical contribution is essentially an extension of multiple-instance learning by replacing binary labels with soft positive weights.  
- Formulation applies cleanly to both observed and latent cases, constituting a valid contribution for NIPS.  
- Results are relevant and demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness on the evaluated tasks.  
- Overall: a well-motivated, clearly presented e-SVM with solid experiments and useful applications.
