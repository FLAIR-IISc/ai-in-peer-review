The paper proposes a modified Support Vector Machine (SVM) learning algorithm designed to handle weakly supervised data. Specifically, the authors introduce a per-example weight, ranging from 0 to 1, for each training example, and subsequently modify the SVM loss function to incorporate this weight. The method is further extended to accommodate scenarios where the per-example weight is not observed, treating it as a latent variable.

The proposed approach is well-described, and the experimental results suggest that, for tasks such as semantic segmentation and object detection, this method may offer advantages over traditional approaches that assign equal weight to each example. However, the distinction between the proposed method and existing "example-dependent costs" is not immediately apparent. These costs are a well-established concept in the machine learning community, and tools like SVMlight already provide a mechanism for incorporating them.

The extension to the case of unobserved per-example weights is an interesting development, albeit a straightforward one. The resulting problem is non-convex, and the authors do not provide a theoretical analysis. Notably, a strong regularization term is employed, suggesting that the per-example weights do not deviate significantly from their initial values. The authors may wish to comment on this aspect.

On a minor note, the claim on line 50 that the problem is not a standard regression problem due to the bounded interval [0, 1] is not entirely accurate. Logistic regression, which is indeed a standard approach, is also bounded to the interval [0, 1]. Additionally, there is a minor spelling error in the abstract, where "continues" should be replaced with "continuous".
