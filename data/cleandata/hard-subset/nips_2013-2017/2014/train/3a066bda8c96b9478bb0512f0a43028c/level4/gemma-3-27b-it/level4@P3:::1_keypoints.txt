- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM), a novel algorithm for training with continuous “positiveness” labels instead of binary labels.  
- Targets weakly supervised learning, especially visual segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Motivation: practical need to learn from weak annotations (e.g., bounding boxes) due to high cost of full pixel-level labels.  
- e-SVM offers a principled alternative to ad-hoc thresholding and addresses limitations of applying SVR to non-regression tasks.  
- Extends to a latent-variable formulation for cases where positiveness is unobserved, broadening applicability.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses equations and Algorithm 1 effectively for clarity.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against relevant baselines (SVC, SVR) and showing consistent improvements.  
- Introduces useful evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment proposal classification.  
- Originality: expectation loss formulation weights examples by positiveness, enabling learning from continuous supervision and generalizing SVM techniques.  
- Authors adequately situate work within related literature (Multiple Instance Learning, weighted SVMs).  
- Significance: substantial practical impact through improved detection performance and potential to reduce annotation costs and improve scalability.  
- Generality: method is compatible with various segment proposal detectors, feature representations, and base detectors.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: addresses important problem, technically sound and novel, strong empirical results, clear presentation, useful generalization of SVMs.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: improvements may not be revolutionary and experiments are primarily limited to PASCAL VOC, requiring broader evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid technical contribution with compelling results, though broader dataset validation would strengthen claims.
