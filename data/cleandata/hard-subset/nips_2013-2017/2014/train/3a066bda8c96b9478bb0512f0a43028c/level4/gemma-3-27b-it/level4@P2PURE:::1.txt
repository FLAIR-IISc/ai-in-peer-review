The authors introduce an Expectation-SVM (e-SVM) capable of handling continuous attributes of positive instances, and demonstrate its effectiveness for learning visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The proposed methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding promising results. This paper tackles the important problem of visual learning with weak supervision; the presentation is clear and well-motivated, and the experiments are both interesting and well-executed. While technically an extension of the standard Multiple Instance Learning framework – specifically, replacing binary weighting of positives with a soft weighting – the e-SVM applies effectively in both observed and latent settings, representing a worthwhile contribution to NIPS. The work offers a strong motivation, clear presentation, and delivers relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
