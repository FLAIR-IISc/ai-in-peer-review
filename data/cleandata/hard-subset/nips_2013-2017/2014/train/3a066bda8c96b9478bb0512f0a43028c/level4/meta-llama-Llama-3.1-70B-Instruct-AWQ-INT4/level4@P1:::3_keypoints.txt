- Presents e-SVM for learning from continuous bounded labels, applied to segment proposal classification, weakly supervised segmentation, and object detection with state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited: resembles weighted SVM and transductive SVM; the sum-constrained weight formulation may be novel but prior work is not sufficiently cited.  
- Results are significant: strong empirical performance that outperforms recent CNN methods and is valuable for weakly supervised learning.  
- Quality concerns: crucial references are missing; object-detection pipeline relies on substantial engineering; must report regularized logistic regression baseline and detail selection of λW and λR.  
- Clarity issues: title and abstract poorly summarize the core contribution and mischaracterize regression targets; paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants and underreports feature-engineering details.  
- Minor editorial fixes needed: typos and terminology (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box", define AP before use, "SVM"→"SVC", clarify threshold wording).  
- Overall verdict: valuable paper that should be published after revisions addressing literature review, baselines, regularization details, clarity, and minor errors.
