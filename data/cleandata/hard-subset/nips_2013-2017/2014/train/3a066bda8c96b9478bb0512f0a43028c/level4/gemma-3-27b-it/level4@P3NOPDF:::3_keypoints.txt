- Presents a regression-based framework for semantic labeling and object detection using IoU regression, latent object extents, weighted SVMs and a “latent e-SVM”, achieving strong empirical results and best-reported VOC2007 performance.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited: weighted SVMs and transductive SVM ideas are well-known and several relevant works are not cited.  
- The introduced sum constraint on SVM weights might be novel but requires a more exhaustive literature search to support the claim.  
- The paper fails to engage adequately with existing literature (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Lapin et al., Vapnik).  
- Significant contribution: demonstrates that engineered features and regression-based methods can match/surpass recent CNN results, which is of interest to the community.  
- Key methodological concern: no justification for using a sum-constrained weighted SVM for regression on the unit interval.  
- Missing baseline: should compare to regularized logistic regression and other natural baselines for unit-interval regression.  
- Hyperparameter reporting: need details on selection and sensitivity of regularization coefficients λ_W and λ_R.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract misrepresent core contribution and incorrectly claim continuous regression targets are less informative than binary labels.  
- Presentation imbalance: excessive space on minor algorithmic variants and insufficient detail on crucial feature engineering; expand feature selection discussion and shorten algorithmic minutiae.  
- Minor editorial/notation fixes required (typos and clarifications: “continues”→“continuous”, “boxes”→“box”, define AP, SVM vs SVC, phrasing for threshold values).  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical work and publishable if authors address missing citations, justify methodological choices, improve presentation, and add baseline/sensitivity analyses.
