The authors propose an expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem and apply the model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated through both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding promising results. The paper addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision, and its presentation is clear and well-motivated. The experiments are interesting and well-executed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Although the technical content is an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework, achieved by replacing a binary weighting of positives with a soft weighting, it applies to both observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution to the NIPS community. The proposed expectation loss SVM method can effectively handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem and is successfully applied to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. Overall, the paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and provides relevant results for both semantic segmentation and object detection.
