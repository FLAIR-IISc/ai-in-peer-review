- Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models continuous attributes of positive instances for weakly supervised learning.
- Applies e-SVM to visual segmentation classifiers and object detection, showing promising empirical results.
- Addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision with sound motivation.
- Presentation is clear, well-argued, and easy to follow.
- Experiments are interesting, well executed, and validate the methodology on semantic segmentation and detection tasks.
- Technically extends multiple instance learning by replacing binary positive labels with soft weighting (expectation-loss).
- Method is applicable in both observed and latent settings.
- Contribution is primarily an extension of existing frameworks rather than a fundamentally new paradigm, but is still a valid and useful contribution.
