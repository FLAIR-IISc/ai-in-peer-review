The authors propose an Expectation-Loss SVM (e-svm), a method capable of handling continuous attributes of positive instances in a weakly supervised learning problem. They apply this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers, validating the methodology on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks with promising results.

The paper addresses the important problem of visual learning under weak supervision. The motivation is strong, the presentation is clear, and the experiments are interesting and well-executed. Although the technical contribution is an extension of the standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) framework—specifically, by replacing a binary weighting of positive instances with a soft weighting—it is a valid contribution for NIPS. The method's applicability in both the observed and latent settings further strengthens its value.
