- Proposal: framework for semantic labeling (candidate segments → features → IoU regression) and object detection (bounding-box labels, spatial extent as latent variable).  
- Methods: uses a weighted SVM for semantic labeling and a weighted transductive SVM ("latent e-SVM") for detection.  
- Results: matches state-of-the-art in semantic labeling and achieves best-reported object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Originality concern: limited algorithmic novelty and omission of key prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs.  
- Specific missing citations: prior weighted SVM literature (e.g., Suykens 2002; Yang 2005; Lapin 2014) and transductive SVM theory (Vapnik 1998).  
- Novelty caveat: the authors’ sum constraint on weights may be new, but this is not established by literature review.  
- Significance: outperforming recent CNN-based methods with conventional techniques is an important and valuable contribution.  
- Quality issues: missing references, reliance on standard engineering for detection, and need to compare against regularized logistic regression as a baseline for unit-interval regression.  
- Experimental detail missing: how regularization hyperparameters (lambda_W, lambda_R) were chosen should be reported.  
- Clarity problems: title/abstract do not accurately summarize core contributions and make a misleading claim about regression targets vs. binary labels.  
- Presentation imbalance: too much space on minor algorithmic variants and insufficient detail on feature engineering.  
- Minor editorial fixes: several typos and phrasing issues (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box", introduce "AP", "SVM"→"SVC", phrasing for threshold values).  
- Recommendation: reviewer raised quality score assuming authors will address missing references and connections; paper merits publication after revisions, possibly better suited for a computer vision venue.
