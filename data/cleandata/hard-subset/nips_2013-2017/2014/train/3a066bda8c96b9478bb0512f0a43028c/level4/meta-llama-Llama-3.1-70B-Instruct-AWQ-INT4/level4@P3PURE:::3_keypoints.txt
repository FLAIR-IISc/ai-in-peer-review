- Proposes semantic labeling and object detection by generating candidate segments, extracting features, and regressing IoU with pixel-accurate ground truth; uses latent variables for precise extents and weighted SVM / weighted transductive SVM; achieves SOTA-level results and best on VOC2007.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited and the paper fails to cite key prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs.
- The introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but requires thorough literature checking.
- The proposed "latent e-SVM" is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and lacks proper citation (e.g., Vapnik).
- Significant result: a conventional learning approach outperforms recent CNN results, which is of high interest.
- Major flaw: omission of crucial references and inadequate contextualization with prior work.
- Should include (regularized) logistic regression as a baseline for regression on the unit interval to justify the sum-constrained weighted SVM.
- Authors must report how regularization coefficients λW and λR were selected.
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variations while glossing over essential feature engineering details.
- Title and abstract do not clearly summarize the core contribution and make an inaccurate implication about regression targets versus binary labels.
- Minor editorial issues and typos: "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box", AP abbreviation missing, "SVM"→"SVC" and phrasing fixes.
- Reviewer raised quality score assuming authors will add extensive commentary on related work; recommends publication after rewriting and perhaps better placement in a vision conference.
