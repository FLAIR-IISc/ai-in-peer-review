- Paper proposes semantic labeling via candidate segment generation, feature extraction, and regression of IoU with ground-truth pixels; object detection treats bounding-box extent as a latent variable and uses weighted SVMs and a weighted transductive SVM.
- Reported results: state-of-the-art semantic labeling and best-known VOC 2007 object detection results.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; many methods (e.g., weighted SVMs, transductive SVMs) are established but not cited.
- The authors’ sum constraint on weights may be novel, but this is a minor algorithmic tweak relative to prior work.
- The proposed “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted variant of transductive SVMs (related prior work not cited).
- Achieving competitive or superior performance to recent CNNs with conventional methods is a significant empirical contribution.
- Crucial prior work citations are missing (e.g., weighted SVM and transductive SVM references R1–R4).
- The object detection system requires substantial engineering but is within typical expectations for the task.
- The paper should include (regularized) logistic regression as a baseline for regression on the unit interval.
- Clarification is needed on how regularization coefficients (λ_W and λ_R) were chosen.
- Title and abstract fail to clearly convey the core contribution; the abstract incorrectly claims regression targets on [0,1] are less informative than binary labels.
- Paper spends excessive space on minor algorithmic variants and glosses over important feature-engineering details.
- Minor editorial fixes needed (typos and abbreviation issues listed by line numbers).
- Reviewer raised quality assessment expecting the authors to address related-work gaps; suggests possible publication in a vision-focused venue.
