- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous positive-instance attributes under weak supervision.
- Applies e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection with promising empirical results.
- Replaces binary positive weighting in MIL with soft weighting, extending standard multiple instance learning.
- Validates both observed and latent settings, with a well-motivated latent extension.
- Thorough comparison to related work, highlighting differences from SVC, SVR, and instance-weighted SVMs.
- Well-formulated loss function and clear optimization algorithm.
- Experiments are impressive and include detailed analysis of strengths and weaknesses.
- Addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision with clear presentation.
- Strength: effectively handles continuous attributes of positives and is well-motivated and written.
- Strength: demonstrates strong empirical performance on segmentation and detection tasks.
- Weakness: technical novelty is mainly an extension of existing MIL framework, which some may view as limited.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the e-SVM model.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution to weakly supervised visual learning, solid formulation, and strong results.
- Arguments for rejection: incremental extension of MIL and insufficient complexity analysis.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS despite minor limitations.
