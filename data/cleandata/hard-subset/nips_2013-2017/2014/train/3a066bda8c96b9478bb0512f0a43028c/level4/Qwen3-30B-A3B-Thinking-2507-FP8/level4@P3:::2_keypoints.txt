- Proposes e-SVM: an SVM variant for weakly supervised learning where training examples have continuous "positiveness" in [0,1].
- Incorporates positiveness by modeling each example as a Bernoulli random variable and embedding expected loss into the SVM objective.
- Extends to latent positiveness values via an alternating minimization that jointly optimizes SVM parameters and latent scores.
- Well-motivated by reducing pixel-level annotation effort in computer vision, leveraging cheaper bounding-box labels.
- Demonstrated on semantic segmentation and object detection with improvements over SVC/SVR baselines.
- Achieves strong object detection results (PASCAL VOC 2007: 60.0 AP, +1.5% vs baseline RCNN) and compelling segmentation performance.
- Experimental design is sound, using appropriate baselines and metrics (TAPC, NDCG) and clear presentation.
- Main novelty is limited: core idea resembles existing weighted SVM approaches and implementations (e.g., SVMlight).
- Paper fails to clearly distinguish the method from standard weighted SVM; the “weighting labels vs data points” distinction is under-justified.
- Latent-case extension is straightforward; optimization is standard alternating minimization with no convergence/theoretical analysis.
- Regularization choice (Equation 3) is used without adequate justification or discussion of alternatives.
- Incorrect claim that regression cannot handle [0,1] outputs—logistic regression already addresses bounded outputs.
- Minor writing issues (typo “continues” in abstract) and need for stronger literature comparison (e.g., refs [23,27]).
- Overall significance: moderate — useful practical application in CV but technically incremental for ML.
- Acceptance pros: well-motivated, strong empirical results, general applicability, clear writing.
- Acceptance cons: limited novelty, inadequate comparison to weighted SVM literature, weak theoretical support, an incorrect related-work claim.
