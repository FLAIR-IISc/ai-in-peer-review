- Presents a method that regresses IoU between candidate segments and pixel-accurate ground truth for semantic labeling, and treats object extent as a latent variable using weighted SVMs/transductive SVMs for detection, achieving state-of-the-art semantic labeling and best-known VOC2007 detection results.

- Algorithmic novelty is limited and several relevant prior works on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs are not cited; the claimed sum constraint may be novel but literature search is insufficient.

- Outperforming recent CNN results with a conventional approach is significant and of strong interest to the community.

- Paper lacks crucial references, relies on substantial but standard engineering for detection, and should compare against regularized logistic regression as a baseline and justify choice of regularization coefficients λW and λR.

- Clarity issues: title and abstract misrepresent core contributions, the abstract makes an incorrect claim about regression targets vs. binary labels, and the paper over-emphasizes minor algorithmic variants while glossing over important feature engineering details.

- Minor corrections: line 19 “continues”→“continuous”; line 94 “boxes”→“box”; line 98 introduce “AP”; line 350 “SVM”→“SVC”; line 350 “with every different values”→“for all threshold values”.

- Reviewer raised quality score conditional on authors addressing related work and discussing connections; recommends publication with revisions and notes suitability for a computer vision venue.
