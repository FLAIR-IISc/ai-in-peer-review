- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models “positiveness” as a continuous [0,1] value and optimizes expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label model.  
- Handles observed positiveness (e.g., IoU with pixel-level masks) and an unobserved case via a latent extension regularized toward bounding-box IoU.  
- Used to train segment classifiers and evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Empirical results are competitive to superior versus SVC/SVR and provide gains when combined with R-CNN.  
- Motivation, formulation, and experimental setup are clear and well presented.  
- Technical novelty is incremental—conceptually similar to replacing binary labels with soft weights, related to MIL/weighted SVMs.  
- The expectation-loss formulation and its latent variant are clean, implementable with standard SVM solvers, and applicable in both fully and weakly supervised regimes.  
- Experiments are thorough and convincingly executed, showing meaningful improvements.  
- Overall: a well-motivated, clearly written paper addressing a relevant weakly supervised setting with solid empirical results; practical impact justifies publication despite limited conceptual novelty.
