- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous attributes of positive instances.  
- Applies e-SVM to weakly supervised visual segmentation, validated on semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Addresses an important and challenging weak-visual-supervision problem with a compelling motivation.  
- Presentation is clear and experiments are well-executed.  
- Technical contribution extends Multiple Instance Learning by replacing binary positive-instance weighting with a soft continuous weighting.  
- The generalization is valuable and non-trivial.  
- Formulation is applicable in both observed and latent variable settings.  
- Demonstrates solid experimental validation with promising results.  
- Overall a well-motivated contribution suitable for NIPS.
