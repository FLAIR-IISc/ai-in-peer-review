- Summary: Proposes e-SVM for learning from weak supervision (only sample "positiveness"), applied to segment proposal classification and weakly supervised bounding-box learning, reporting SOTA object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.

- Originality: Algorithmic novelty limited—weighted SVM and related ideas exist; missing citations (e.g., Suykens et al. 2002, Yang et al. 2005); latent e-SVM resembles weighted/transductive SVM (Vapnik 1998); main contribution is application to vision tasks.

- Significance: Strong—e-SVM outperforms recent CNNs on detection and shows promise for segmentation and real-world applications.

- Quality: Paper omits important prior work, glosses over extensive engineering, lacks detailed feature-engineering description, should report a regularized logistic regression baseline, and better justify regularization hyperparameters (λW, λR).

- Clarity: Title and abstract misrepresent core contribution and make an incorrect claim about regression targets; too much space on known algorithms and too little on feature details—needs clearer, concise exposition for NIPS readers.

- Minor comments: Typos/terminology to fix—"continues"→"continuous"; "boxes"→"box"; introduce AP before first use; SVM→SVC; rephrase "with every different values" to "for all threshold values".
