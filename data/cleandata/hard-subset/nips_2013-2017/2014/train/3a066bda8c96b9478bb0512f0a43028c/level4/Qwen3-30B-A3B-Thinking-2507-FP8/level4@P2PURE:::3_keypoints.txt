- Proposed method: regress IoU of candidate segments for semantic labeling and treat precise object extent as a latent variable for detection using a weighted SVM and a weighted transductive SVM; achieves SOTA semantic labeling and best-reported VOC2007 detection.  
- Originality concern: limited algorithmic novelty and omission of key prior work (weighted SVMs and transductive SVM variants); the sum constraint on weights may be novel but isn’t fully justified or situated in literature.  
- Significance: outperforming recent CNN-based methods with a conventional approach is an important and notable result.  
- Quality issues: critical prior work is missing, the detection system is largely engineering, and the authors should report logistic-regression baselines for unit-interval regression and clarify how λ_W and λ_R were chosen.  
- Clarity problems: title/abstract mischaracterize the contribution and make an incorrect claim about regression targets; too much space on minor algorithmic variations and too little on feature-engineering details.  
- Minor editorial fixes: several typos and phrasing errors (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", fix "boxes"/"box", define "AP", "SVM"→"SVC", rephrase "with every different values").  
- Recommendation: reviewer raised score by one pending major revisions—address missing references and discussion; paper merits publication after revision, potentially better suited for a vision conference.
