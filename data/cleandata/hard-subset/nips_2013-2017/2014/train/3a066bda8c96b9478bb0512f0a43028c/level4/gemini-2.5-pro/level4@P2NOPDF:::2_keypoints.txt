- Proposes SVM with continuous per-example weights in [0,1], extended to a latent-variable setting where weights are learned; shows empirical gains on semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Paper is clearly written and the method is described in sufficient detail.  
- Major novelty concern: when weights are known, the approach is equivalent to instance-dependent/example-dependent costs (e.g., SVMlight); authors must clarify what is fundamentally new.  
- Latent-weight extension yields a non-convex optimization but lacks theoretical analysis (no convergence guarantees or characterization of local minima).  
- Strong regularization on weights raises doubt that weights are truly learned rather than remaining close to initialization; authors should analyze the impact of regularization and optimization behavior.  
- Minor factual issue: claiming the problem is “not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to [0,1]” is misleading—models like logistic and beta regression handle [0,1] targets.  
- Typo in abstract: “continues” should be “continuous.”
