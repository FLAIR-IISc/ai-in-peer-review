- Proposes per-example weights in SVM loss to handle weak supervision, extended to latent (unobserved) per-example weights.
- Method is well described and experimentally improves over standard SVM/SVR on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.
- Latent-weight extension is interesting and practical for weakly supervised learning.
- Novelty is unclear: idea overlaps with existing “example-dependent costs” and weighted SVM implementations (e.g., SVMlight).
- Authors do not adequately differentiate their approach from prior weighted-SVM methods.
- Latent-variable optimization is non-convex and appears straightforward; paper lacks theoretical analysis of optimization properties.
- Strong regularization keeps weights near initialization, suggesting reliance on initialization; sensitivity to regularization parameters is not analyzed.
- Paper should justify choice of regularizer and report sensitivity of results to its parameters.
- Incorrect claim: stating bounded outputs in [0,1] is not a nonstandard regression problem ignores standard logistic regression.
- Minor typo: abstract uses “continues” instead of “continuous.”
- Arguments for acceptance: empirical gains across tasks, practical latent-weight extension, clear and well-organized presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient theoretical treatment of the non-convex problem, and incomplete related-work discussion.
