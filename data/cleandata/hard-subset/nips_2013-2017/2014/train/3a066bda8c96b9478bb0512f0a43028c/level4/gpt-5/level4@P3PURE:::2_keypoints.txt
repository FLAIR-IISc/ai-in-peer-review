- Proposes SVM modification with per-example weights in [0,1], extended to unobserved (latent) per-example weights.
- Method is clearly described and experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection indicate benefits over equal weighting.
- Reviewer questions novelty, noting similarity to existing example-dependent costs / instance-weighted or cost-sensitive SVMs (e.g., SVMlight).
- Requests clarification of meaningful conceptual and algorithmic differences from standard instance-weighting methods.
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but appears straightforward; optimization becomes non-convex and lacks theoretical analysis.
- Strong regularization implies learned weights stay close to initialization; reviewer asks for practical and theoretical implications and conditions where latent weights outperform fixed weights.
- Minor: claim that outputs bounded in [0,1] is incorrect—logistic regression provides such outputs.
- Minor: abstract spelling error—“continues” should be “continuous.”
- Overall: contribution seems limited in novelty without clearer distinction from prior work and theoretical insight into the latent-weight formulation.
