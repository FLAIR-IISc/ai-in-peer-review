- Introduces e-SVM: an SVM hinge loss reframed as an expectation over a latent binary label using a continuous "positiveness" score in [0,1].
- Core idea: weight positive and negative hinge-loss components per example by its observed positiveness score.
- Extends to latent e-SVM where positiveness scores are unobserved and learned jointly with classifier via alternating optimization.
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing e-SVM outperforms SVC/SVR and improves R-CNN by ~1.5% AP on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Strength: paper is clear and well-written with a well-motivated problem formulation for continuous confidence labels.
- Strength: thorough empirical results demonstrating practical value on challenging CV tasks.
- Weakness: novelty concerns — formulation closely resembles existing instance-weighted SVMs; need clearer distinction and justification for decomposing costs into positive/negative loss terms versus single C_i.
- Weakness: latent model lacks theoretical analysis of alternating optimization, convergence, and local minima properties.
- Weakness: optimization seems to rely on strong regularization for latent u; authors should analyze sensitivity to lambda_R and how much u changes from initialization.
- Minor points: claim that modeling [0,1] is "not a standard regression problem" is questionable given logistic regression; and a typo ("continues" → "continuous") in the abstract.
- Summary: solid empirical contribution and clear exposition, but paper should better situate novelty relative to weighted SVMs and provide rigorous analysis of the non-convex latent model.
