- Proposal: Introduces SVM with per-example weights in [0,1] and an extension where weights are latent variables.  
- Clarity & experiments: Method is clearly presented; experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential improvements over uniform weighting.  
- Novelty concern: The technique appears conceptually identical to established example-dependent costs (e.g., SVMlight), so novelty is unclear.  
- Latent-weight extension: The latent-weights idea is interesting but straightforward and leads to a non-convex optimization problem.  
- Lack of theory: No theoretical analysis is provided for the latent-weight non-convex case; reliance on strong regularization to keep weights near initial values needs justification.  
- Minor error: Line L050 wrongly claims the problem isn’t standard regression because positivity is in [0,1]; logistic regression is a standard bounded [0,1] model.  
- Typo: Abstract uses “continues” instead of “continuous.”  
- Overall: Core contribution (weighting examples in SVM loss) does not appear novel given existing standard practice.
