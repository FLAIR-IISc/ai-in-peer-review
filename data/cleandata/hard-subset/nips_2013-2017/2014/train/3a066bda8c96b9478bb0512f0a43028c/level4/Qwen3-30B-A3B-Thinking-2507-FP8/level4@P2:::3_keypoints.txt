- Introduces an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from weak supervision where examples have continuous "positiveness" values in [0,1].
- Applies e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection, outperforming SVC/SVR baselines and achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Core formulation treats segment labels as Bernoulli random variables parameterized by observed positiveness; includes a latent variant when positiveness is hidden.
- Latent e-SVM used for bounding-box supervision and evaluated on segment proposal classification, segmentation, and detection tasks.
- Originality is limited: similar ideas exist in weighted SVM literature (Suykens et al., Yang et al., Lapin et al.) and transductive SVM; authors fail to cite foundational work.
- The imposed sum constraint on weights is a minor variant, and the latent method essentially mirrors weighted transductive SVM, weakening novelty claims.
- Experimental quality is strong with consistent improvements across TAPC and NDCG, and a notable 1.5% AP gain over R-CNN.
- Missing crucial baselines such as logistic regression and poor justification for regularization hyperparameters λw and λR, hurting robustness assessment.
- Significance is high in practice: simple framework achieves top detection results without deep learning and leverages abundant bounding-box annotations.
- Clarity is moderate: title/abstract misleading about unit-interval regression vs binary classification, too much focus on minor algorithmic variants, and insufficient detail on feature engineering.
- Technical explanation of latent e-SVM optimization is clear, but connections to prior work and literature context are inadequately established.
- Paper contains multiple minor typographical and terminology errors that need correction.
- Recommendation: not novel enough for NIPS; better suited to a vision conference unless revised to include related-work citations, logistic-regression baselines, hyperparameter justification, and clearer presentation.
