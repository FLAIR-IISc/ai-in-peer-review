- Proposes SVM modification using continuous per-example weights in [0,1], with observed and latent-weight formulations.  
- Paper is generally well-written and the method is clearly described.  
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest performance gains over standard SVM.  
- Observed-weights formulation lacks novelty—overlaps with cost-sensitive/example-dependent cost SVMs (e.g., SVMlight); authors must clarify differences.  
- Latent-weight extension is an interesting direction but appears straightforward and under-analyzed.  
- Alternating optimization is standard; no theoretical analysis of convergence or quality of local minima is provided.  
- Strong regularization on weights raises concerns about sensitivity and whether learned weights meaningfully diverge from initialization—needs empirical/theoretical probing.  
- Incorrect claim that outputs in [0,1] make the problem "not a standard regression problem" (logistic regression handles this).  
- Minor typo: "continues" should be "continuous" in the abstract.  
- Overall: empirical promise but insufficient positioning versus prior art and inadequate analysis of the non-convex latent optimization.
