**Review Summary**

The authors propose a framework for semantic labeling and object detection, leveraging candidate segment generation, feature extraction, and regression on the Intersection-over-Union overlap ratio. For object detection, they utilize weaker labels in the form of bounding boxes and treat precise spatial extent as a latent variable. The approach employs weighted SVM and weighted transductive SVM for regression, achieving state-of-the-art results in semantic labeling and the best known results for object detection on VOC 2007.

**Originality**

While the authors' approach shows promise, its algorithmic novelty is limited. Unfortunately, they fail to cite relevant prior work, including well-known weighted SVM methods (e.g., [R1, R2, R4]). The introduction of a sum constraint on weights may be a novel contribution, although a thorough literature search is needed to confirm this. The proposed "latent e-SVM" is essentially a weighted version of a transductive SVM (e.g., [R3]), which is not cited.

**Significance**

The fact that the authors' "conventional" learning approach outperforms recent CNN results is a notable achievement and of great interest to the community.

**Quality**

The paper lacks crucial references to prior work, which is a significant drawback. While the object detection framework involves some engineering, it is not unusually complex. The use of a sum-constrained weighted SVM for regression on the unit interval is not thoroughly justified, and the authors should provide more details on their choice of regularization coefficients λW and λR. The paper's main strength lies in its good empirical results.

**Clarity**

The title and abstract do not accurately summarize the paper's core contribution. The abstract also implies that regression targets from the unit interval are less informative than binary class memberships, which is not the case. The paper spends too much space on well-known algorithms and not enough on feature engineering details. Minor errors and suggestions for improvement are noted.

**Minor Comments**

* Line 19: "continues" should be "continuous"
* Line 94: "boxes" should be "box"
* Line 98: AP abbreviation is not introduced
* Line 350: "SVM" should be "SVC" and "with every different values" should be "for all threshold values"

**Overall Assessment**

While the paper presents great results, its deficiencies in referencing prior work and limited algorithmic novelty are notable drawbacks. However, assuming the authors will address these concerns and provide more context, the paper is worthy of publication, possibly after rewriting, and may be more suitable for a computer vision conference.
