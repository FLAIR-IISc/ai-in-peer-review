- Proposes e-SVM: expected hinge loss treating labels as Bernoulli with positiveness µi = g(ui), plus L2 regularization, and a latent variant alternating between w and u with a KL regularizer.

- Equivalence to weighted/example-dependent cost or soft-label SVMs is noted; many toolkits already support per-example weights or duplicating examples with µi and 1−µi.

- Evaluated on segment proposal scoring (new TAPC/NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation, and detection, reporting consistent segment scoring gains and +1.2–1.5 AP over RCNN on VOC07.

- Strength: simple, practical objective compatible with standard SVM solvers and pipelines.

- Strength: natural handling of soft labels and a straightforward latent extension for weak supervision.

- Strength: sensible integration into detection via a spatial confidence map with nontrivial VOC07 improvements.

- Strength: introduces TAPC/NDCG for segment scoring and shows consistent improvement over SVC/SVR on these metrics.

- Weakness: limited originality since expected hinge loss is mathematically equivalent to known weighted/soft-label SVM formulations; reparameterization claim insufficiently justified.

- Weakness: latent alternating optimization is non-convex, lacks theoretical analysis (convergence guarantees, conditions to avoid degenerate u), and depends heavily on KL regularizer.

- Weakness: notation and presentation issues (incorrect regularizer notation, missing algorithmic details for u-update, stopping criteria, runtime, typos, and reference formatting).

- Empirical concern: segmentation results are only comparable to SVR, so practical advantage is unclear.

- Empirical concern: detection gains may be conflated with other implementation tweaks (e.g., modified bbox-regression features); ablations isolating e-SVM contribution are missing.

- Empirical concern: no statistical analysis, multiple runs, per-class breakdown, or error analysis; sensitivity to g(u), λR, and initialization ubb not reported.

- Empirical concern: no comparisons to other bounded regression baselines (e.g., logit-transformed regression, Beta regression) or alternatives to the KL regularizer.

- Quality assessment: conceptually sound for observed-label case; latent case reasonable but under-analyzed; implementation seems practical but novelty is limited.

- Originality assessment: limited — expected hinge/soft-label equivalence known; latent extension and spatial confidence feature are incremental.

- Significance assessment: modest — useful AP improvement but needs stronger ablations to be convincing.

- Key requests: explicitly position relative to weighted/cost-sensitive and soft-label SVMs and state whether duplication with weights reproduces the objective.

- Key requests: provide ablations for g(u), λR, and initialization; test alternatives to KL regularizer.

- Key requests: fully detail the u-update (per-instance optimization, gradients, solver), report convergence behavior, runtime, stopping criteria, and number of alternating steps.

- Key requests for detection: report performance without modified bbox-regression features, vary m and pooling, and compare to SVR-derived confidence maps.

- Key requests: report standard errors/multiple runs and per-class results for segmentation and detection.

- Minor fixes requested: correct typos ("continues"→"continuous"), clarify bounded-regression claim, fix regularization notation, define TAPC/NDCG formally, correct references, state whether µi = ui or g is learned.
