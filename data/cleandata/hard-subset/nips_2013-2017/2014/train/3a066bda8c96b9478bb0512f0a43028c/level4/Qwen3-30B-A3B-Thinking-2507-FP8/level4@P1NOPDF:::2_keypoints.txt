- Paper proposes per-example weights in [0,1] scaling SVM loss and extends this to latent (unobserved) weights.  
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential gains over uniform weighting.  
- Core idea appears to be an application of established example-dependent cost techniques (e.g., SVM implementations like SVMlight).  
- Authors fail to clearly distinguish their method from prior example-dependent cost approaches.  
- Latent-weight extension is straightforward but yields a non-convex problem with no theoretical analysis provided.  
- Heavy regularization keeping weights near initial values suggests reliance on strong priors rather than principled uncertainty handling.  
- Authors should clarify whether regularization is necessary for convergence or merely a practical heuristic and discuss alternative optimization strategies.  
- Minor factual error: line 50’s claim that [0,1]-bounded outputs are non-standard for regression is incorrect (e.g., logistic regression).  
- Abstract contains a spelling error: "continues" should be "continuous."  
- Overall technical contribution lacks novelty relative to existing SVMs with example-dependent costs.  
- The latent-weight idea needs deeper theoretical grounding or clearer justification for the chosen regularization.  
- Empirical results are promising but insufficient to justify a strong novelty claim without better connection to prior work and theoretical/optimization analysis.
