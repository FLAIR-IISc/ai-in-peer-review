The authors propose a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can effectively handle situations where the labels of training data are continuous values within a bounded interval. The e-SVM algorithm can be applied to segment proposal classification tasks and can be easily extended to learn segment classifiers under weak supervision, where only bounding box annotations are available. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two major tasks in computer vision: semantic segmentation and object detection.

The authors' work is closely related to previous research on weakly supervised learning and weighted SVMs. However, they fail to cite pertinent previous work, such as weighted SVMs (e.g., [R1, R2, R4]) and transductive SVMs (e.g., [R3]). The authors' "latent e-SVM" is a weighted version of a transductive SVM, but they do not acknowledge this connection. The algorithmic novelty of the paper is limited, and the authors should provide more context and references to previous work.

Despite these limitations, the authors' results are impressive, outperforming recent CNN results on object detection. The paper's significance lies in its ability to achieve state-of-the-art performance using a conventional learning approach. However, the authors should provide more details on their experimental setup, including the choice of regularization coefficients and the feature engineering process.

The paper's clarity is also a concern. The title and abstract do not accurately summarize the core contribution of the paper, and the authors spend too much space on well-known algorithms. The feature engineering details are also glossed over, which is a crucial aspect of the paper.

In terms of quality, the paper's main strength is its good empirical results. However, the authors should address the missing references to previous work and provide more context for their approach. The paper's engineering aspects are not outside the norm, but the authors should provide more details on their experimental setup.

Overall, the paper is well-written, but it requires some rewriting to address the concerns mentioned above. The authors should provide more context, references, and details on their experimental setup to strengthen their paper.

* The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on object detection using a conventional learning approach.
