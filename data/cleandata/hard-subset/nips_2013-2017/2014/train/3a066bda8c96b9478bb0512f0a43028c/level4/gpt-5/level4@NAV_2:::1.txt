The authors introduce an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models continuous “positiveness” scores for instances and uses this to train segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The method is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection with encouraging outcomes. The paper targets an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning; the exposition is clear and well-motivated, and the experiments are interesting and carefully executed. Although the technical contribution can be viewed as an extension of standard multiple-instance learning (replacing hard binary labels with soft weighting of positives), it operates in both observed and latent settings and constitutes a legitimate contribution for NIPS. Overall, an expectation-loss SVM that accommodates continuous positiveness in training and applies it to weakly supervised segment learning, with solid motivation, clear presentation, and relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
