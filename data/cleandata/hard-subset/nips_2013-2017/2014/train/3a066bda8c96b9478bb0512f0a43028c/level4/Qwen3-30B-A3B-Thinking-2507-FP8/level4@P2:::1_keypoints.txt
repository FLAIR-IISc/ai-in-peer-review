- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from weak supervision with continuous "positiveness" values instead of binary labels.  
- Applies e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection, showing improvements over SVC and SVR baselines.  
- Identifies limitations of multiple instance learning (MIL) for segment proposals and motivates e-SVM as a better fit.  
- Proposes an expectation-based loss that handles both observed (strong) and latent (weak) positiveness values.  
- Method is technically sound and a well-motivated extension of standard SVMs.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation (pixel and bounding-box) and PASCAL VOC 2007 detection (RCNN).  
- Reports consistent improvements, including a reported 1.5% average per-class AP gain for object detection.  
- Introduces two novel evaluation metrics (Threshold Average Precision Curve and NDCG) for segment classification assessment.  
- Ablation studies and comparisons to multiple SVC thresholds show careful analysis of method behavior.  
- Strengths: addresses an important practical problem, clear exposition, comprehensive experiments, and proper contextualization versus prior work.  
- Weaknesses: theoretical analysis of latent e-SVM convergence could be strengthened.  
- Weaknesses: the practical significance of the 1.5% AP improvement could be better contextualized against recent detection advances.  
- Contribution is significant and generalizable, offering a principled approach for continuous positiveness labels in weak supervision.  
- Overall quality, originality, and significance are high; reviewer recommends acceptance for NIPS.
