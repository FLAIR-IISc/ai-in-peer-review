The paper introduces an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that accommodates continuous-valued attributes of positive instances, enabling learning under weak supervision. The method is applied to training visual segmentation classifiers and evaluated on both semantic segmentation and object detection, with promising results.

The work tackles an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning. The motivation is sound, the presentation is clear, and the experiments are well designed and executed. Technically, the approach extends standard multiple-instance learning by replacing binary positive labels with soft weights, and it is formulated for both observed and latent settings. Overall, the contribution is meaningful and appropriate for NIPS, with clear relevance to semantic segmentation and object detection.
