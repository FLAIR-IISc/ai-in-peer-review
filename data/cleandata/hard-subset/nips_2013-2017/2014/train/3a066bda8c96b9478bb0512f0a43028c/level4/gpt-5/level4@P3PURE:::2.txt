Summary:
The paper proposes a modification to the SVM learning algorithm to handle weakly supervised data by assigning a per-example weight in [0, 1] to each training example and modifying the SVM loss to account for these weights. The method is then extended to the case where the per-example weights are unobserved and treated as latent variables.

Evaluation:
- The method is clearly described, and the experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest that there may be value in using per-example weighting compared to treating all examples equally.
- However, I fail to see a substantive difference between the proposed approach and existing “example-dependent costs,” which are widely known and used in the machine learning community. For instance, SVMlight already supports such mechanisms. The authors should clarify how their formulation differs in a meaningful way from standard instance weighting or cost-sensitive SVMs, both conceptually and algorithmically.
- The extension to unobserved per-example weights is interesting but appears straightforward. In this setting, the optimization becomes non-convex, and no theoretical analysis is provided. Moreover, the use of a strong regularization term suggests that the learned weights do not deviate far from their initial values. Can the authors comment on the practical and theoretical implications of this, and under what conditions the latent-weight optimization yields improvements over fixed weights?

Minor comments:
- Line 050: The claim that “it is also not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to a bounded interval [0, 1]” is inaccurate. Logistic regression is a standard method whose outputs are bounded in [0, 1].
- Abstract: spelling error—“continues” should be “continuous.”

Overall:
The paper proposes a modified SVM learning algorithm in which the loss is reweighted per example, but example-dependent costs are already widely used in machine learning. Without a clearer distinction from existing instance-weighted or cost-sensitive SVM formulations and some theoretical insight into the latent-weight extension, the contribution appears limited in novelty.
