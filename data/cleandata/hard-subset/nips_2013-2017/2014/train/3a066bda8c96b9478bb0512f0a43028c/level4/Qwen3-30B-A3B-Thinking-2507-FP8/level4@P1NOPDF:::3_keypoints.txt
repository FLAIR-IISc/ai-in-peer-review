- Proposed framework: semantic labeling via candidate segments + IoU regression; object detection treats precise object extent as latent and uses weighted SVM / weighted transductive SVM.  
- Results: match state-of-the-art for semantic labeling and set a new benchmark on PASCAL VOC 2007 for detection.  
- Originality limited: algorithmic contribution is small and largely engineering-focused.  
- Omitted key prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs (e.g., Suykens et al. 2002; Yang et al. 2005; Vapnik 1998; Lapin et al. 2014).  
- The weighted SVM is well-established; the sum-weight constraint may be novel but is not thoroughly situated in prior literature.  
- Reviewer requests baseline comparisons to regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression to justify the sum-constrained weighted SVM.  
- Need to clarify how regularization parameters (λ_W and λ_R) were chosen.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract misrepresent core contribution and incorrectly claim regression targets are less informative than binary labels.  
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants (first four pages) while omitting critical feature-engineering details.  
- Minor editorial/typo corrections suggested (several line-specific replacements and abbreviation definitions).  
- Recommendation: strong empirical results but requires thorough rewriting, added citations, and clarification; possibly more suitable for a computer-vision venue.
