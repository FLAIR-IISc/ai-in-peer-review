- Summary: Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous “positiveness” labels and a latent variant for unobserved positiveness; evaluated on segmentation, detection, and proposal classification, achieving state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.

- Originality: Algorithmic novelty is incremental; fails to adequately cite related weighted-SVM and transductive learning work; the proposed sum constraint on weights needs further justification and comparison.

- Significance: Empirically important—competitive and SOTA object detection results demonstrate effective use of weakly supervised data, which is valuable for scaling ML.

- Quality: Technically sound with thorough experiments, but weakened by omitted key references; lacks comparison to standard regression baselines and clearer discussion of regularization hyperparameters (λW, λR).

- Clarity: Title/abstract are not sufficiently descriptive and make a misleading claim about continuous vs binary labels; paper overemphasizes minor algorithm variants and underdescribes crucial feature engineering and theoretical discussion.

- Minor comments: Typos and edits—“continues”→“continuous”; “boxes”→“box”; define “AP”; use “SVC” instead of “SVM” in line 350; fix “with every different values”→“for all threshold values”.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with excellent results but limited novelty and insufficient engagement with prior work; recommend publication pending revisions to related work, presentation, and some experimental clarification.
