- Introduces Expectation-Loss SVM (e-SVM) for handling continuous attributes of positive instances in weakly supervised learning.
- Applies e-SVM to learn visual segmentation classifiers using only image-level labels.
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising results.
- Strengths: clear motivation, lucid presentation, and thorough experimental evaluation.
- Addresses a highly relevant weak supervision problem and provides a compelling case for the approach.
- Core technical contribution: extends Multiple Instance Learning by replacing binary instance weighting with a flexible soft weighting scheme.
- Significant advantage: applicable in both observed and latent variable settings, increasing versatility.
- Overall: technically sound, valuable generalization; elegant and effective solution suitable for the NIPS audience.
