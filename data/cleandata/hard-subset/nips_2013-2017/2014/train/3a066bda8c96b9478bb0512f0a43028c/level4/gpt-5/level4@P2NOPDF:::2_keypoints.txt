- Method is clearly described.
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show benefits over uniform weighting, suggesting example-specific weighting is useful.
- Novelty is unclear since approach resembles standard example-dependent / cost-sensitive SVM formulations; substantive differences must be clarified.
- Paper lacks direct empirical comparison to off-the-shelf per-example cost SVMs (e.g., SVMlight).
- Latent per-example-weight extension is interesting but appears straightforward and makes the problem non-convex without theoretical analysis.
- Strong regularization on latent weights raises concerns that weights remain close to initialization, limiting flexibility.
- Need explanation for why strong regularization is necessary and how its strength is chosen.
- Need analysis of sensitivity to initialization and regularization strength.
- Need evidence that the method departs meaningfully from fixed-weight baselines in realistic settings.
- Request for convergence properties or practical diagnostics for the non-convex optimization.
- Specific questions: precise differences from standard per-example cost SVM implementations (constraints, parameterizations, update rules).
- Specific questions: empirical comparison to off-the-shelf instance-specific cost methods on the same datasets.
- Specific questions: ablations isolating latent-weight component versus fixed weights and quantifying benefits beyond initialization.
- Minor: incorrect claim that bounded [0,1] positiveness is non-standard regression — logistic regression contradicts this.
- Minor: abstract typo — replace “continues” with “continuous.”
- Overall: clear presentation and promising results, but contribution seems close to existing methods and the latent-weight extension needs stronger theoretical/empirical justification.
