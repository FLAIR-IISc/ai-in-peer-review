Summary and relation to prior work
This paper introduces the expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a max-margin formulation that trains with “positiveness” scores ui ∈ [0,1] instead of binary labels. The central idea is to replace the standard hinge loss with its expectation under a Bernoulli label whose mean is a function g(ui) (set to ui in the experiments), yielding a convex objective that can be solved with standard linear SVM solvers. The method also extends to weak supervision by treating ui as latent and regularizing it to be close to a bounding-box–derived initializer ubb via a cross-entropy (KL) penalty; optimization alternates between updating w (convex SVM) and u (convex in u given w and convex regularizer).

The approach is applied to segment proposal classification and then instantiated in two vision tasks: (i) semantic segmentation using CPMC proposals and second-order pooling features, and (ii) object detection by deriving a spatial confidence map feature from segment confidences and concatenating it with RCNN features. The authors propose two direct criteria to evaluate segment confidence prediction—TAPC (threshold-averaged AP over IoU thresholds) and NDCG—and report improvements over SVC/SVR baselines. For detection on PASCAL VOC 2007, they report +1.2 mAP over RCNN without box regression and +1.5 mAP with box regression.

The work connects to several lines of research. It sits between fully supervised segmentation methods using pixel labels and MIL-style weak supervision. It is also closely related to instance/label-weighted SVMs, cost-sensitive SVMs, and SVMs trained with probabilistic/soft labels, where expected margin losses are common surrogates. The latent extension relates to latent SVMs and alternating minimization/CCCP techniques used for weak supervision.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The expected hinge loss with soft labels is a sensible convex surrogate, and the alternating optimization for the latent case is standard and implementable. However, some technical details are underspecified or contain typos (e.g., the regularizer and norm terms; the expression “λw·1 wTw” is unclear and likely intended as λ||w||^2). The choice g(u)=u is reasonable but unmotivated; alternative link functions and their impact are not discussed. Convergence properties of the alternating scheme and sensitivity to λR are not analyzed.
- Empirical support: The paper includes experiments on two tasks and proposes task-agnostic evaluation metrics (TAPC/NDCG) for segment confidence. The detection result is noteworthy given the RCNN baseline, though the gain is modest. An important missing piece is ablation: it is unclear how much of the detection improvement comes from e-SVM vs. the new spatial confidence map feature, or whether SVR with the same feature would perform similarly. Comparisons omit baselines from weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs and SVMs with probabilistic labels, which would be the most direct competitors.
- Reproducibility: The approach can be implemented with off-the-shelf SVM solvers, which is a plus. Hyperparameters, optimization details for the u-update, training time, and sensitivity analyses are not fully specified.

Clarity
The paper is generally understandable and well-motivated, with a clear task pipeline. However, there are numerous grammatical errors and notation issues that should be corrected. Definitions of TAPC (threshold range, spacing, and averaging) and the segmentation “accuracy” metric need to be explicit. Algorithm 1 should specify how the u-subproblem is solved (closed-form per i? constrained optimization over ui∈(0,1)?). A brief derivation showing equivalence to weighted hinge terms would aid clarity and highlight the relation to WSVM.

Originality
The expected hinge formulation for soft/probabilistic labels and the use of per-example weights are not new ideas in the SVM literature (e.g., weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs, SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels). The main novelty here is (i) instantiating IoU as a soft “positiveness” label for segments, (ii) a latent extension regularized toward bounding-box–derived priors, and (iii) a practical integration with RCNN via a spatial confidence map. These are reasonable and useful contributions, but the conceptual step beyond existing weighted/soft-label SVM formulations is limited. A more thorough positioning against prior work on probabilistic-label SVMs and weakly supervised segmentation/detection would strengthen the originality claim.

Significance
Weak supervision at the level of bounding boxes is highly relevant for large-scale vision. The method is simple, general, and easy to plug into existing pipelines. The detection gains over RCNN, while modest, suggest practical value. If the authors provide stronger comparisons (especially against WSVM/probabilistic-label SVM baselines and MIL/latent SVM variants) and clearer ablations, the work could have impact as a straightforward tool for learning with soft labels in segmentation/detection pipelines.

Strengths
- Simple, convex objective in the supervised case, solvable with standard SVM solvers.
- Natural extension to weak supervision with an interpretable regularizer toward bounding-box priors.
- General and modular; applicable across features/proposal generators and base detectors.
- Empirical improvements over SVC/SVR for segment confidence prediction; non-trivial mAP gains over RCNN on VOC 2007.
- Introduction of task-agnostic evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG) for segment confidence prediction.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to weighted/cost-sensitive/soft-label SVMs and SVMs with probabilistic labels; related work and comparisons are incomplete.
- Insufficient ablation to isolate the contribution of the loss vs. the new spatial confidence feature in detection.
- Technical clarity issues (typos, notation errors, missing details for u-update, choice of g(u), hyperparameter settings and sensitivity).
- Theoretical analysis is minimal (no generalization or convergence guarantees beyond standard convexity statements).
- Reported detection gains are modest; statistical significance is not discussed.

Suggestions for improvement
- Situate e-SVM against prior work on soft/probabilistic labels and weighted SVMs; include direct baselines (e.g., cost-sensitive SVM, probabilistic-label SVM) and MIL/latent SVM variants where applicable.
- Provide ablations: SVR/SVC/WSVM with the same spatial confidence map; e-SVM without the spatial map; different choices of g(u); effect of λR and initialization ubb.
- Clarify and correct the mathematical notation; precisely define TAPC and the segmentation evaluation metric; report hyperparameters and sensitivity, training times, and implementation details for the u-subproblem.
- Discuss whether the approach extends to non-linear kernels and any practical constraints.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses an important practical problem with a simple, implementable method.
- Works across tasks and improves a strong detection baseline.
- Clear motivation and reasonable empirical evidence.

Arguments against acceptance
- Conceptual novelty over existing soft/weighted SVM formulations is modest.
- Missing key baselines and ablations; limited analysis of the latent optimization.
- Presentation/notation issues that obscure the technical content.

Recommendation
Weak accept, contingent on clarifying the technical presentation and strengthening comparisons/ablations to convincingly establish the benefit of the proposed loss beyond existing weighted/soft-label SVM alternatives.
