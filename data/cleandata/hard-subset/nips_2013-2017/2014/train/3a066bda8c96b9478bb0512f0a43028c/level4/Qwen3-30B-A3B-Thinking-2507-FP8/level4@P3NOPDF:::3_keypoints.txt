- Proposes a unified framework for semantic labeling and object detection, regressing IoU for segments and treating precise extents as latent for detection.  
- Uses weighted SVMs and a proposed "latent e-SVM" for regression, claiming state-of-the-art detection on VOC 2007 and competitive semantic labeling.  
- Core algorithmic contributions are limited; weighted SVM approaches are well-established and key citations are missing.  
- The "latent e-SVM" is a weighted variant of transductive SVM and is not properly contextualized with prior work (e.g., Vapnik’s TSVM).  
- The claimed novelty (e.g., sum constraint on weights) is uncertain and requires verification and clearer positioning in related work.  
- Significant empirical result: a non-deep-learning method can outperform recent CNN-based detectors, which is notable for the community.  
- Lack of crucial baseline comparisons, notably regularized logistic regression for unit-interval targets, undermines justification for the chosen method.  
- Hyperparameters (e.g., λ_W, λ_R) are insufficiently justified or tuned in reported experiments.  
- Paper emphasizes minor algorithmic variations while underreporting important feature engineering and implementation details.  
- Title and abstract misrepresent the core contribution and make incorrect claims about regression targets being "less informative" than binary labels.  
- Several minor writing/terminology errors and unclear definitions (typos, undefined "AP", confusing SVM/SVC usage).  
- Overall recommendation: Accept after major revision to add missing citations, clarify novelty vs. prior work, include missing baselines, and better justify hyperparameters.
