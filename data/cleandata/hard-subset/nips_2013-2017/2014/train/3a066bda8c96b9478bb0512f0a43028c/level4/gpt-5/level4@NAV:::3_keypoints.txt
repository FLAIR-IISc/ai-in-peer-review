- Introduces a pipeline: semantic labeling by regressing candidate-segment features to IoU with pixel-accurate ground truth, and object detection using bounding-box supervision with object extent as a latent variable.  
- Uses weighted SVM for labeling and a weighted transductive SVM (“latent e-SVM”) for detection; reports state-of-the-art detection on VOC2007 and competitive semantic labeling.  
- Algorithmic novelty is modest; key prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs is not cited (several relevant refs given).  
- The imposed sum constraint on weights might be novel, but this is not fully established.  
- Results are significant: outperforming recent CNN baselines with a more traditional approach is of strong community interest.  
- Quality concerns: important references missing; detection system involves substantial engineering but no unusual methodology.  
- Methodological gap: regression on [0,1] should be compared to regularized logistic regression to justify the sum-constrained weighted SVM.  
- Experimental detail needed: specify how regularization parameters λW and λR were chosen.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract do not clearly state the central contribution and make a misleading claim about continuous targets being less informative than binary labels.  
- Paper devotes too much space to minor tweaks of known methods and skims over crucial feature-engineering details.  
- Minor errors/typos listed (e.g., “continues”→“continuous”, “boxes”→“box”, undefined AP, SVM→SVC, phrasing for threshold values).  
- Recommendation: raise quality score contingent on engaging with prior work and clarifying connections; publish after revision, possibly better suited to a computer vision venue.
