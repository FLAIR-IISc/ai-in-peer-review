- Proposed framework: candidate segment generation, feature extraction, regression of IoU for semantic labeling, and latent-object-extent modeling for detection using weighted SVM and weighted transductive SVM; competitive results and new VOC2007 detection benchmark.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited and several relevant prior works on weighted and transductive SVMs are not cited.
- The imposed sum constraint on weights might be novel but is not thoroughly analyzed or justified.
- The "latent e-SVM" appears to be a weighted variant of existing transductive SVM ideas and lacks proper attribution.
- Missing key references (examples: Suykens et al. 2002; X. Yang et al. 2005; Vapnik 1998; Lapin et al. 2014).
- Main significance is empirical: outperforms recent CNN approaches using conventional methods, which is of interest to the community.
- Quality: strong empirical results but weakened by omitted literature, substantial engineering in the pipeline, and lack of baseline comparisons (e.g., regularized logistic regression) for sum-constrained regression.
- Methodological details lacking: insufficient explanation of hyperparameter selection for regularization coefficients λW and λR.
- Clarity issues: title/abstract do not clearly state central contributions and incorrectly claim continuous regression targets are less informative than binary labels.
- Presentation imbalance: excessive space on minor algorithm variations and too little detail on crucial feature engineering.
- Minor editorial issues: typos and phrasing corrections (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box", define "AP" on first use, "SVM"→"SVC" on line 350, rephrase "with every different values").
- Recommendation: worthy of publication pending substantial revisions addressing prior work and other concerns; consider submitting to a computer vision venue as an alternative.
