- Proposes e-SVM: an extension of MIL that replaces binary bag weighting with a continuous expectation-based loss to handle soft supervision.
- Motivated by real-world weak supervision that provides continuous/imprecise cues (e.g., confidence scores), which standard MIL cannot exploit.
- Integrates expectation maximization into the SVM optimization, enabling learning from partial or noisy supervision.
- Applicable in both observed (direct supervision) and latent (inferred supervision) settings, broadening weakly supervised use cases.
- Non-trivial technical derivation: expectation loss integrated into the SVM dual with theoretical justification, convergence guarantees, and complexity analysis.
- Implementation is flexible, adapting seamlessly to segmentation (pixel-level features) and detection (region proposals) pipelines.
- Thorough experimental validation on PASCAL VOC and MS COCO showing consistent gains over strong MIL baselines.
- Notable empirical result: 3.2% mIoU improvement on VOC 2012 segmentation under weak supervision without extra data or complex post-processing.
- Ablation studies and figures convincingly isolate the impact of the expectation loss and illustrate the soft supervision mechanism.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured, with accessible writing and sufficient implementation details.
- Acknowledges limitations such as computational overhead in latent settings and suggests extensions (video, multi-modal).
- Overall significant, practical contribution to weakly supervised learning with a recommendation for acceptance.
