The authors propose an Expectation-SVM (e-SVM) capable of handling continuous attributes of positive instances, and apply this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, demonstrating promising results. This paper addresses an important problem in visual learning – learning with weak supervision – and the presentation is clear, well-motivated, and the experiments are both interesting and well-executed. While the technical content extends the standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) framework by replacing a binary weighting of positives with a soft weighting, it applies effectively in both observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution for NIPS. In summary, this work introduces an Expectation Loss SVM method for handling continuous attributes in positive instances, and successfully applies it to visual segmentation under weak supervision; the motivation is strong, the presentation clear, and the results relevant to semantic segmentation and object detection.
