- Paper proposes segment-based semantic labeling with IoU regression and object detection treating precise object extent as a latent variable, using weighted SVM and weighted transductive SVM; results match SOTA for semantic labeling and achieve best reported detection on VOC2007.

- Algorithmic novelty is limited; weighted SVM is well-known and key related work is omitted, while the authors’ sum-constrained weights may be the only possibly novel element.

- The proposed “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and prior transductive SVM work is not cited.

- Crucial prior references (e.g., Suykens et al. 2002; Yang et al. 2005; Vapnik 1998; Lapin et al. 2014) are missing and should be discussed.

- Significance is high: outperforming recent CNN results with a conventional learning pipeline is notable and broadly interesting.

- Quality concerns: substantial engineering in the detection system but not beyond the norm; missing baseline (regularized logistic regression) for unit-interval regression; insufficient detail on how regularization coefficients (λW, λR) were chosen.

- Main strength is strong empirical performance despite the above concerns.

- Clarity issues: title and abstract do not clearly state the core contribution and make incorrect claims about the informativeness of real-valued regression vs. binary labels.

- Paper spends excessive space on minor algorithmic variants and too little on crucial feature-engineering details (e.g., lines 254ff).

- Minor editorial/notation errors to fix: “continues”→“continuous”; “boxes”→“box”; introduce AP abbreviation; “SVM”→“SVC” at line 350; “with every different values”→“for all threshold values.”

- Reviewer raised quality score assuming authors will thoroughly discuss prior work and clarify relations; recommends publication after rewriting and suggests the paper may be better suited to a computer-vision venue.
