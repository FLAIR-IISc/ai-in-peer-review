- Proposes an expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous attributes of positive instances.
- Applies e-SVM to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision.
- Validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results.
- Addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision with clear motivation.
- Experiments are interesting, well-executed, and demonstrate method effectiveness.
- Technical contribution extends standard multiple instance learning by replacing binary weights with soft weighting of positives.
- Method applies to both observed and latent settings.
- Represents a valid and novel contribution suitable for NIPS.
- Presentation is clear and results are relevant to the target tasks.
