The paper introduces a modification to the Support Vector Machine (SVM) learning algorithm designed for weakly supervised data. The core idea involves assigning a weight – ranging from 0 to 1 – to each training example and incorporating this weight into the SVM’s loss function. This approach is further extended to scenarios where these per-example weights are latent variables and not directly observed.

The method is clearly presented, and the experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest a potential benefit over standard SVM training with uniform example weighting. However, the novelty of this approach appears limited, as it closely resembles existing techniques utilizing example-dependent costs, which are well-established within the machine learning field – for instance, as implemented in SVMlight.

The extension to latent per-example weights is an interesting development, though conceptually straightforward. This extension introduces non-convexity, and the paper lacks theoretical analysis to support the approach. The reliance on a strong regularization term, seemingly to constrain the learned weights near their initial values, raises questions. The authors should address this point in their revision.

A minor point regarding terminology: the statement on line L050 characterizing the problem as “not a standard regression problem” due to the bounded output interval [0, 1] is inaccurate. Logistic regression, a standard regression technique, also produces outputs within this interval. Additionally, a minor spelling correction is needed in the abstract: “continues” should be “continuous.”

In summary, the paper proposes weighting the SVM loss function by per-example weights, but this concept is already present in existing machine learning methods employing example-dependent costs.
