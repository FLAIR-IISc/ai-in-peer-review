- Introduces e-SVM: an SVM variant for labels in [0,1], treating labels as Bernoulli probabilities and using the expected hinge loss.  
- Two variants: supervised e-SVM (known positiveness scores) and latent e-SVM that jointly learns scores and classifier via alternating optimization regularized toward IoU-based initialization.  
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection; outperforms SVC/SVR on segment proposal classification (using TAPC and NDCG) and improves R-CNN by ~1.5% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Strength: Tackles a practical weak supervision problem, enabling use of bounding-box annotations instead of costly pixel-level labels.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated formulation that naturally extends classical SVMs and logically presents the latent model.  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation, including novel evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG), sensible baselines, and a practical detector improvement via spatial confidence maps.  
- Weakness: Modest theoretical novelty—e-SVM is essentially per-sample hinge-loss reweighting related to weighted SVMs; latent extension resembles MIL.  
- Weakness: Alternating optimization only guarantees local minima; robustness to initialization of latent positiveness scores (initialized by IoU) is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: No ablation or sensitivity analysis for the latent regularization hyperparameter (λR), leaving practical tuning guidance incomplete.  
- Overall: Well-executed, impactful paper with comprehensive experiments; strengths outweigh the minor methodological and analysis shortcomings.
