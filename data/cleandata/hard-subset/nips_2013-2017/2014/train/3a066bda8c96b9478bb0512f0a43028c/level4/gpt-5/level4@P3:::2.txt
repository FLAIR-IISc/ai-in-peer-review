Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a variant of the hinge-loss SVM that treats the binary label Yi ∈ {−1,+1} of each training example as a Bernoulli random variable with “positiveness” µi = g(ui) ∈ [0,1], where ui (e.g., IoU) quantifies the affinity to the positive class. The training objective minimizes the expected hinge loss EYi[max(0, 1 − Yi w⊤xi)], which is equivalent to µi max(0, 1 − w⊤xi) + (1 − µi) max(0, 1 + w⊤xi), plus standard L2 regularization. For weak supervision, where ui is unobserved, the authors introduce a latent e-SVM that alternates between optimizing w (convex SVM) and estimating u using a convex regularizer R(u), instantiated as a cross-entropy penalty that keeps u close to an initialization derived from bounding boxes.

The method is evaluated on segment proposal scoring (with new TAPC and NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation (using CPMC segments and second-order pooling), and object detection (augmenting RCNN features with a class-specific spatial confidence map derived from e-SVM predictions). Reported gains include improvements over SVC/SVR on segment scoring, comparable segmentation accuracy to SVR, and a +1.2–1.5 AP improvement over RCNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.

The formulation is closely related to well-known example-dependent cost (weighted SVM) and soft-label SVM settings, where each example contributes weighted positive and negative hinge losses. Many SVM toolkits (e.g., SVMlight) support per-example weights, and equivalent formulations can be obtained by duplicating each example with positive and negative labels and weighting them by µi and (1−µi).

Strengths:
- Simple, practical objective that reduces to standard SVM solvers; clear compatibility with existing pipelines.
- Natural handling of soft labels/continuous “positiveness” and a straightforward latent extension for weak supervision.
- Sensible incorporation into detection via a spatial confidence map; improvements on RCNN are nontrivial for VOC07.
- New direct evaluation criteria (TAPC, NDCG) for segment scoring; consistent improvements over SVC/SVR on these metrics.

Weaknesses and concerns:
- Originality/positioning: The expected hinge loss with µi is mathematically equivalent to using example-dependent costs or soft labels (each sample contributes to both positive and negative hinge terms with weights µi and 1−µi). The paper asserts a distinction from weighted SVMs by “weighting labels” rather than data points, but this is largely a reparameterization. The contribution needs clearer differentiation from cost-sensitive/weighted SVM and soft-label SVM literature and from existing implementations that already support per-example weights.
- Latent variant: The alternating optimization is non-convex and lacks theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence guarantees beyond monotone decrease, conditions under which u remains meaningful). The strong KL regularizer appears critical to avoid degenerate solutions (u ∈ {0,1}); more justification and sensitivity analysis are needed.
- Clarity and presentation: The objective is notationally inconsistent (“λw · 1 wTw” appears to be L2 regularization; please correct to λ/2 ||w||^2 or similar). Algorithmic details for the u-update are missing (closed-form? per-instance Newton? constraints and step sizes?). Stopping criteria and runtime are not reported. Several typos and reference formatting issues remain.
- Empirical analysis: 
  - Semantic segmentation results are “comparable” to SVR; the practical advantage over SVR is thus unclear in this task.
  - For detection, the improvement could conflate the proposed spatial confidence map with other tweaks (e.g., modified bounding-box regression features via additive kernel maps). Ablations isolating the contribution of e-SVM-derived features are needed.
  - No statistical analysis or multiple runs to assess variance; per-class breakdown and error analyses would help.
  - No sensitivity to g(u) (fixed to identity), λR, or initialization ubb; no comparison to alternative bounded regression baselines (e.g., simple logit-transform regression, Beta regression).

Quality:
- Conceptually sound for the observed-label case; the latent case is reasonable but under-analyzed. Implementation appears practical. However, the novelty over established weighted/soft-label SVM formulations is limited and not rigorously delineated.

Clarity:
- Overall understandable, but needs substantial polishing: correct the objective notation, define TAPC/NDCG precisely, specify optimization details, and fix typos and references.

Originality:
- Limited. The expected hinge with soft labels and the equivalence to per-example costs are known. The latent extension and the specific spatial confidence map feature are incremental.

Significance:
- Modest. The +1.5 AP improvement on VOC07 over RCNN (2014 context) is useful but requires stronger ablations. The generality claim is plausible, but the added value over simpler weighted/soft-label baselines is not convincingly established.

Questions and requests for the authors:
1. Precisely position e-SVM relative to weighted/cost-sensitive SVM and soft-label SVM. Can your objective be reproduced by duplicating each example with labels ±1 and weights µi and 1−µi in a standard SVM? If not, what is different?
2. Provide ablations on g(u), λR, and initialization ubb. How sensitive is performance? Do alternatives to the KL regularizer (e.g., L2 on logits) work?
3. Detail the u-update: provide the optimization problem per instance, gradients, and whether a closed-form or efficient solver is used. Report convergence behavior and runtime.
4. For detection, isolate the gain from the spatial confidence map by:
   - Reporting performance without the modified bounding-box regression features.
   - Varying m and pooling choices.
   - Comparing against a baseline that uses SVR-derived confidence maps in the same way.
5. Report standard errors or multiple runs where feasible. Include per-class results for segmentation and detection.
6. Clarify why SVR is “not standard” for bounded targets. Consider comparing to bounded regression alternatives (e.g., logit-transformed regression or Beta regression).

Minor comments:
- Abstract: “continues” → “continuous”.
- L050 claim: “not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to [0,1]” is misleading; bounded regression is standard (e.g., apply a logit transform). If the issue is that IoU is not a probability, please state that.
- Notation: fix the regularization term (likely λ/2 ||w||^2). Define TAPC/NDCG formally in the paper.
- References contain formatting errors and missing fields; please correct.
- State clearly whether µi = ui or if g(u) is learned/calibrated.
- Describe stopping criteria and the number of alternating steps used.

Pros:
- Simple, practical formulation compatible with standard SVM solvers.
- Handles weak supervision with a reasonable latent-variable extension.
- Empirical gains on segment scoring and detection; useful feature for detection.

Cons:
- Limited novelty over established weighted/soft-label SVM formulations.
- Insufficient theoretical and empirical analysis of the latent optimization.
- Clarity and formatting issues; missing ablations and sensitivity studies.
- Ambiguous detection ablation w.r.t. additional feature engineering.
