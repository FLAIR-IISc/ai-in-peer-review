The authors propose an Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) that can effectively handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem, and they apply this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated through experiments on both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results. This paper addresses a significant problem in visual learning under weak supervision, and its presentation is clear and well-motivated. The experiments are interesting and well-executed, demonstrating the efficacy of the proposed approach. Although the technical content is an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework, achieved by replacing a binary weighting with a soft weighting of positives, it is applicable in both observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution to NIPS. The introduction of an e-SVM method capable of handling continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem, along with its application to learning visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, is well-motivated and clearly presented. The paper provides relevant results for both semantic segmentation and object detection.
