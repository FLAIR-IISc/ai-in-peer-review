- Summary: Proposes a unified scheme for semantic labeling and object detection that regresses IoU for candidate segments via weighted SVMs and uses a weighted transductive SVM with latent extents for detection; reports competitive semantic labeling and state-of-the-art VOC2007 detection.

- Originality: Algorithmic novelty is limited—weighted SVMs and transductive SVM ideas are well-known; the sum constraint on weights might be novel but needs clearer positioning.

- Missing prior work: Omits key references on weighted and transductive SVMs (e.g., R1–R4) and should cite/discuss them.

- Significance: Noteworthy that a conventional pipeline outperforms contemporary CNNs; VOC2007 gains are compelling.

- Related-work quality issue: Must acknowledge and compare to prior weighted/latent/transductive SVM formulations and explain the sum-constraint difference.

- Regression baselines missing: Should include regularized logistic/probit regression and ridge (squared-loss) baselines for IoU-in-[0,1] targets.

- Hyperparameter reporting missing: Provide how λW and λR were chosen (cross-validation protocol, ranges, per-class/dataset reuse) and include sensitivity analysis.

- Ablation studies needed: Isolate effects of the sum constraint, latent-variable treatment vs simple heuristics, and per-feature family contributions.

- Engineering complexity: Detection pipeline is engineering-heavy; provide a modular breakdown and guidance on critical components for reproducibility.

- Title/abstract clarity: Title and abstract do not emphasize the core contributions (sum-constrained weighted SVM regression and weighted transductive SVM with latent extents) and should be revised.

- Incorrect claim on informativeness: The paper’s claim that unit-interval regression targets are "less informative" than binary labels is incorrect and should be corrected.

- Exposition balance: Too much space on routine algorithmic derivations; move derivations to appendix and expand feature/implementation/training/computational details in main text.

- Notation/terminology issues: Ensure consistent SVM/SVC usage and define abbreviations (e.g., AP) at first use.

- Minor typos: Line 19 “continues”→“continuous”; Line 94 “boxes”→“box”; Line 98 define AP; Line 350 “SVM”→“SVC” and “with every different values”→“for all threshold values”.

- Strengths: Strong empirical results including VOC2007 SOTA detection; conceptually simple and effective pipeline; sensible use of continuous IoU targets aligning loss with evaluation.

- Weaknesses: Incomplete engagement with prior work, limited algorithmic novelty, and missing standard baselines and hyperparameter details that hinder interpretability.

- Revision recommendations: Add and discuss missing refs (R1–R4), include logistic/probit/ridge baselines, detail hyperparameter selection and sensitivity, expand feature/implementation details, move routine derivations to supplement, and revise title/abstract.

- Overall assessment: Great empirical results but weak referencing and limited novelty; recommend acceptance after revision and suggest it would also fit well at a computer vision venue.
