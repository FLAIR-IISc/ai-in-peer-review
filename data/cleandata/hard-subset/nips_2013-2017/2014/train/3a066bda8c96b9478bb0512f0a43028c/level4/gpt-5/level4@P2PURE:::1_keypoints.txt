Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous attributes of positive instances.  
Applies e-SVM to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision.  
Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising results.  
Addresses an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning; motivation is sound.  
Presentation is clear and the experiments are interesting and well executed.  
Technically extends multiple-instance learning by replacing binary indicators with soft weighting of positives.  
Applies in both observed and latent settings.  
Represents a valid and relevant contribution suitable for NIPS.
