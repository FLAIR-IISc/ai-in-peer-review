The paper proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that accommodates continuous attributes of positive instances and applies this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, with promising results.

The work addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision. The motivation is sound, the presentation is clear and well argued, and the experiments are interesting and well executed. Technically, the method extends the standard multiple instance learning framework by replacing a binary treatment of positives with a soft weighting, and it applies in both observed and latent settings. While the contribution is primarily an extension rather than a fundamentally new paradigm, it is nonetheless a valid and useful contribution for NIPS.

Overall, the paper is well motivated, clearly presented, and demonstrates relevant results for both semantic segmentation and object detection.
