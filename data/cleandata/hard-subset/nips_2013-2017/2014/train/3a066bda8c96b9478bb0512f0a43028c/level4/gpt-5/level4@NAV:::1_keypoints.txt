- Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models continuous attribute weights for positive instances instead of binary indicators.  
- Extends standard multiple-instance learning by replacing hard-positive indicators with soft weighting of positives.  
- Develops the e-SVM formulation for both observed and latent-variable settings.  
- Applies e-SVM to weakly supervised visual learning, specifically semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Experiments are well executed and show encouraging results on both segmentation and detection tasks.  
- Paper addresses an important problem, with a clear, well-motivated narrative and solid technical contribution suitable for NIPS.
