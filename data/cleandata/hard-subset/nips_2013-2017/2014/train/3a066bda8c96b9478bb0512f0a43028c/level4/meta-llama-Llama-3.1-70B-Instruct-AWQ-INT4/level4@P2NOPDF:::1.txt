The authors propose an expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem and apply this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. This methodology is validated through both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding promising results. The paper addresses the important problem of visual learning under weak supervision, presenting a clear and well-motivated approach. The experiments are interesting and well-executed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Although the technical content is an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework, achieved by replacing binary weights with a soft weighting of positives, it applies to both observed and latent settings. This represents a valid contribution to NIPS, as it provides a novel solution to a relevant problem. The expectation loss SVM method can effectively handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem and is successfully applied to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The paper's motivation is well-established, the presentation is clear, and the results are relevant to both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.
