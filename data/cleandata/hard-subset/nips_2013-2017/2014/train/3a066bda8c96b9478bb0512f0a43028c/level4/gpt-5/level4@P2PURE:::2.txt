The paper proposes a modification to the SVM learning algorithm for weakly supervised data: each training example is assigned a weight in [0, 1], and the SVM loss is adjusted to incorporate this weight. The approach is then extended to the case where the per-example weight is unobserved (i.e., treated as a latent variable).

The method is clearly described, and the experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest there may be value in using this approach compared to treating all examples equally. However, I do not see how the proposed method differs from existing example-dependent cost schemes, which are well known and widely used in the machine learning community; for example, SVMlight already provides such a mechanism.

The extension to unobserved per-example weights is interesting but straightforward. In this setting the problem becomes non-convex, and no theoretical analysis is provided. Moreover, the use of a strong regularization term seems to constrain the per-example weights to remain close to their initial values. Can the authors comment?

Minor comments:
- L050: The claim that “it is also not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to a bounded interval [0, 1]” is inaccurate. Logistic regression is a standard method whose output is bounded in [0, 1].
- Abstract: “continues” should be “continuous”.

In summary, the paper introduces an SVM variant whose loss is weighted per example; however, example-dependent costs are already widely used in machine learning.
