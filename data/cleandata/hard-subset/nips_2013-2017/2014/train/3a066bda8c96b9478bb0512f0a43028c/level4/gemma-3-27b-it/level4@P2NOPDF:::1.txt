This paper introduces an Expectation-SVM (E-SVM), a novel approach designed to incorporate continuous attributes of positive instances within a learning framework, and demonstrates its effectiveness in visual segmentation tasks learned under weak supervision. The authors successfully apply E-SVM to both semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving promising results in both domains.

The problem addressed – visual learning with weak supervision – is significant and relevant to the NIPS community. The paper is exceptionally well-written, providing clear motivation for the proposed method and presenting the technical details in a readily understandable manner. The experimental setup is thoughtfully designed and rigorously executed, demonstrating the practical benefits of the E-SVM approach. 

While technically building upon the established multiple instance learning (MIL) framework, this work represents a valuable contribution by extending the standard binary weighting of positive instances to a more nuanced, soft weighting scheme. Crucially, this extension is applicable in both observed and latent settings, broadening the potential impact of the technique. The E-SVM effectively bridges a gap in existing methodologies by directly addressing the challenges posed by continuous-valued positive instance features, a common occurrence in visual learning. 

In summary, the paper presents a well-motivated, clearly articulated, and empirically validated method for visual segmentation under weak supervision. The results are encouraging and suggest the E-SVM offers a compelling alternative to existing approaches.
