### **Summary**

This paper presents a method for semantic labeling and object detection. For semantic labeling, the approach generates candidate segments, extracts features for them, and then performs regression on the Intersection-over-Union (IoU) overlap ratio with the pixel-accurate ground truth. For object detection, where only bounding box labels are available, the precise spatial extent of the object is treated as a latent variable. The regression tasks are addressed using a weighted SVM for semantic labeling and a weighted transductive SVM for object detection. The experimental results are on par with the state-of-the-art for semantic labeling and achieve a new state-of-the-art for object detection on the PASCAL VOC 2007 dataset.

### **Originality**

The algorithmic novelty of the paper is limited. A significant concern is the omission of several key references to prior work. The proposed weighted SVM is a well-established technique (e.g., [R1, R2, R4]), and the "latent e-SVM" appears to be a weighted version of a standard transductive SVM (e.g., [R3]), none of which are cited by the authors. While the sum constraint imposed on the weights may be a novel contribution, the core learning machinery is not new.

[R1] Suykens et al. Weighted least squares support vector machines: robustness and sparse approximation, Neurocomputing, 2002
[R2] X.Yang et al. Weighted Support Vector Machine for Data Classification, IJCNN, 2005
[R3] V. Vapnik, "Statistical Learning Theory", 1998
[R4] M.Lapin et al, Learning Using Privileged Information: SVM+ and Weighted SVM, Neural Networks, 2014

### **Significance**

The paper's primary significance lies in its outstanding empirical results. Achieving performance that surpasses recent deep learning-based (CNN) methods using a more "conventional" machine learning approach is a notable accomplishment and will be of considerable interest to the community.

### **Quality**

The paper's main strength is its strong empirical performance. However, there are several issues regarding its quality. As noted under Originality, the paper is missing crucial references to foundational work on weighted and transductive SVMs. While the object detection framework involves significant engineering, this is not uncharacteristic for the field. A more significant weakness is the lack of a proper baseline for the regression task; the authors should compare their sum-constrained weighted SVM against a standard method for regression on the unit interval, such as regularized logistic regression, to justify their choice. Furthermore, the paper provides insufficient detail on the selection of the regularization coefficients λW and λR.

### **Clarity**

The paper's clarity could be substantially improved. The title and abstract do not effectively summarize the core contributions. The abstract misleadingly suggests that regression targets on the unit interval are less informative than binary class labels, which is incorrect as binary labels can be derived from continuous scores by thresholding. The paper also seems poorly balanced for a NIPS audience: it dedicates excessive space (through page 4) to describing minor variations of well-known learning algorithms, while the crucial details of the feature engineering (lines 254ff), which are likely key to the method's success, are covered too briefly.

### **Overall Assessment**

In summary, this paper presents a method that achieves excellent, state-of-the-art results. However, it suffers from a lack of algorithmic novelty and a serious omission of relevant prior work. While the results are strong enough to warrant publication, the paper requires significant revision. The authors must thoroughly discuss the relationship between their method and existing weighted/transductive SVMs, citing the relevant literature. The paper would also benefit from restructuring to focus more on the novel aspects of the application and feature engineering rather than on standard learning formulations. Given its focus, this work might be better suited for a top-tier computer vision conference.

I have conditionally increased my quality score with the expectation that the authors will address these major concerns constructively in their revision.
