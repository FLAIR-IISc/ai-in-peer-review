The paper proposes a modified SVM with per-example weights (in [0,1]) and a latent-weight extension for weakly supervised data.  
Method is clearly presented and experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential benefits over uniform weighting.  
Major novelty concern: the core idea appears subsumed by existing "example-dependent cost" mechanisms (e.g., SVMlight), so originality is unclear.  
Authors must explicitly explain differences from and the necessity beyond standard example-dependent cost formulations/solvers.  
Latent-weight extension is straightforward conceptually but lacks theoretical grounding and analysis.  
Non-convex latent-weight optimization is acknowledged but no convergence, optimality, or theoretical guarantees are provided.  
Heavy reliance on strong L2 regularization suggests the solution is a small perturbation of standard SVM; this assumption needs justification.  
Authors should clarify whether regularization is required for tractability or is a modeling choice, and compare theoretically to alternatives such as EM for latent weights.  
Minor corrections: incorrect statement about bounded regression (line 50) — logistic regression is a standard [0,1] model; abstract typo "continues" → "continuous".  
Empirical validation should include explicit benchmarking against SVMlight's example-dependent costs to demonstrate added value and strengthen the novelty claim.
