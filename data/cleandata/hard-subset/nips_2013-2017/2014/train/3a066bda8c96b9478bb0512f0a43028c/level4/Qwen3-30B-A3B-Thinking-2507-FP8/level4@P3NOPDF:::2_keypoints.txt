- Paper proposes SVM with per-example weights (in [0,1]) and treats weights as latent optimization variables for weakly supervised learning.
- Empirical results on semantic segmentation and object detection show improvements over uniform weighting.
- Core idea lacks novelty: the weighted-loss formulation is equivalent to established "example-dependent costs" in the literature.
- SVMlight and similar tools already support per-example cost weights (e.g., `--weight`), so the paper fails to distinguish itself from existing implementations.
- Latent-weight extension makes the problem non-convex but the paper provides no theoretical analysis of convergence, optimality, or solution properties.
- The method relies on strong regularization (λ||w − w0||^2) with no justification for why this is necessary or appropriate.
- Authors must clarify why the regularizer is used, whether alternative optimization/regularization (e.g., alternating minimization with guarantees) could work, and how initialization w0 affects outcomes.
- Claim that bounded [0,1] outputs are non-standard is incorrect—logistic regression is a standard approach for such targets.
- Minor technical issues: typo in abstract ("continues" → "continuous") and grammar error in line 50 ("value is using" → "value in using").
- Overall: promising empirical gains but insufficient novelty and theoretical grounding; not ready for acceptance at NIPS without reframing or substantial additional analysis.
- Suggested remedies: (1) demonstrate a mechanism beyond standard example-dependent costs, or (2) provide rigorous theory for latent weights and a detailed comparison to SVMlight.
