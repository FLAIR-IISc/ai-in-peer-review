- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous “positiveness” targets in [0,1] and a latent e-SVM for weak (box-only) supervision via regularized alternating optimization.  
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation (scoring segment proposals, assembling label maps) and object detection (class-specific spatial confidence maps combined with RCNN features), yielding comparable segmentation and improved RCNN detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited: e-SVM closely relates to instance-weighted SVMs and latent e-SVM resembles weighted/transductive/latent SVM formulations; key prior work is not cited.  
- The introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but requires clearer positioning against existing literature.  
- Significance: notable practical impact—1–1.5 AP improvement over RCNN on VOC 2007—showing segment-derived features are complementary and useful.  
- Empirical work is strong and engineering sound, but the paper omits important related citations and comparisons.  
- Missing baseline: regularized logistic regression for targets in [0,1] should be included to justify the specialized loss and weighting scheme.  
- Need clarity on hyperparameter selection (lambda_w, lambda_R) and sensitivity analyses; ablation studies required to quantify the effect of the latent regularizer versus no regularization.  
- Presentation issues: title/abstract underemphasize the core contribution (expectation loss and latent extension) and misleadingly suggest continuous targets are less informative; too much space spent on SVM variants and too little on feature construction and integration (hurting reproducibility).  
- Pros: strong empirical gains in detection, competitive segmentation results, simple and general method, clear motivation for graded positiveness and weak supervision.  
- Cons: limited novelty, missing key citations, incomplete baseline comparisons, and insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and feature engineering.  
- Minor fixes: typos and wording (e.g., “continues”→“continuous”, “boxes”→“box”); define AP on first use; correct SVM→SVC and grammar on threshold phrasing.  
- Overall: recommend acceptance conditional on better prior-art discussion, clearer framing of the main contribution, adding a logistic-regression baseline, and more details/ablations on regularization and feature construction; paper may also fit well at a vision venue after revisions.
