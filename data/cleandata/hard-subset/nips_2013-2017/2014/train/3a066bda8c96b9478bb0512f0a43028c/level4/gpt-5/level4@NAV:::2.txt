Review- The paper introduces a modification to the SVM training procedure for weakly supervised data. Concretely, each training instance is assigned a per-example weight in [0, 1], and the SVM loss is adjusted to incorporate this weight. The approach is then extended to the setting where the per-example weight is unobserved (i.e., treated as a latent variable).
The method is clearly presented, and the experiments suggest that for semantic segmentation and object detection, this approach can be beneficial compared to uniform weighting. However, I do not see how the proposed method differs from established “example-dependent costs,” which are well known and widely used in the machine learning community. For example, SVMlight provides such a mechanism.
The extension to unobserved per-example weights is interesting but fairly straightforward. In this case, the problem becomes non-convex and no theoretical analysis is provided. In fact, a strong regularization term is employed, indicating that the per-example weights are encouraged to remain close to their initial values. Can the authors comment?
Minor comment: The statement on L050 that “it is also not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to a bounded interval [0, 1]” is inaccurate. Logistic regression is a standard method with outputs bounded in [0, 1]. 
Minor spelling error in the abstract: “continues” -> “continuous”
 The paper presents an SVM variant whose loss is weighted per example; however, example-dependent costs are already commonplace in machine learning.
