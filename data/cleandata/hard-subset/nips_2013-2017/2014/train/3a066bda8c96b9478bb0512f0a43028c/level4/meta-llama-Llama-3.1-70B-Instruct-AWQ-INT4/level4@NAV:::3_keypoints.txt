- Proposal: Framework for semantic labeling and object detection using candidate segment generation, feature extraction, and regression on IoU; treats bounding-box supervision as weak with object extent as a latent variable and uses weighted/transductive SVMs, reporting SOTA on semantic labeling and best-known VOC2007 detection results.

- Originality (general): Algorithmic novelty is limited and important related work is not cited.

- Originality (specific): Fails to cite well-known weighted SVM methods and appears to repackage transductive SVM ideas; the introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but requires literature verification.

- Significance: It is notable that a conventional (non-CNN) approach outperforms recent CNN results and is of high community interest.

- Quality (references): The paper lacks crucial prior-work references, which is a significant flaw.

- Quality (method): The object-detection engineering is reasonable but not unusually complex; the use of a sum-constrained weighted SVM for regression on [0,1] is under-justified.

- Quality (details needed): Authors should justify the choice of regularization coefficients λW and λR and provide more methodological detail.

- Clarity (presentation): Title and abstract do not accurately summarize the core contribution and incorrectly suggest regression targets on the unit interval are less informative than binary labels.

- Clarity (focus): Too much space is spent on well-known algorithms and too little on feature engineering details; several minor errors are present.

- Minor comments (typos/edits): "continues"→"continuous" (line 19); "boxes"→"box" (line 94); AP abbreviation not introduced (line 98); "SVM"→"SVC" and grammar fix for threshold wording (line 350).

- Overall assessment: Strong empirical results but limited novelty and missing citations; acceptable for publication if authors address referencing, clarify contributions, and revise—possibly more suitable for a computer vision venue.
