- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for classification when labels are continuous "positiveness" scores in [0,1], formulating SVM loss as an expectation over a latent binary label.  
- Shows e-SVM is equivalent to a weighted SVM solvable with standard solvers.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision by treating positiveness scores as latent and inferring them via alternating optimization.  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection; outperforms SVC and SVR for segment proposal classification.  
- Integrating e-SVM with R-CNN yields a new state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007, improving AP by 1.5%.  
- Strength: addresses practical, relevant weak supervision problem and reduces need for dense annotations.  
- Strength: methodological clarity and clear connection to classical SVMs, with logical extension to latent case.  
- Strength: thorough experiments, principled comparisons, and introduction of TAPC and NDCG metrics for fair evaluation.  
- Weakness: core technical novelty is limited—essentially an instance-weighted SVM with a new problem framing.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of model choices (e.g., choosing g(ui)=ui) and sensitivity to alternative monotonic mappings or regularizers like L2 on u.  
- Weakness: optimization details lacking—missing analysis of initialization sensitivity and typical convergence behavior of the alternating algorithm.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, impactful paper with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance despite somewhat incremental technical novelty.
