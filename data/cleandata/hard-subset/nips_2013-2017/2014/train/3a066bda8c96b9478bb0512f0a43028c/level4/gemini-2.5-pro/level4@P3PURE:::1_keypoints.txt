- Proposes Expectation-Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, enabling continuous attributes for positive instances.
- Replaces binary positive-instance labels with soft weighting (expectation-loss), extending the standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) framework.
- e-SVM can be applied in both observed and latent settings.
- Method validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising empirical results.
- Addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision.
- Presentation is clear and the approach is well-motivated.
- Experiments are interesting and well-executed.
- Technical contribution is a modest but valuable extension of MIL, suitable for NIPS.
