The authors propose an Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM), a novel method designed to handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem. They apply this model to the task of learning visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, delivering promising results.

This paper addresses the important and challenging problem of learning from weak visual supervision. The motivation is compelling, the presentation is clear, and the experiments are both interesting and well-executed.

While the technical contribution can be viewed as an extension of the standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) framework—specifically, by replacing a binary weighting of positive instances with a soft, continuous one—this is a valuable and non-trivial generalization. A key strength of the proposed formulation is its applicability in both the observed and latent variable settings. Overall, this paper presents a well-motivated method with solid experimental validation and represents a valid contribution suitable for NIPS.
