- Introduces e-SVM to handle continuous “positiveness” values for weakly supervised learning.  
- Applied to segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection with promising/SOTA results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Clear, well-motivated presentation.  
- Extends multiple instance learning by using continuous weighting of positives instead of binary assignments.  
- Applicable to both observed and latent positiveness settings.  
- Strong experimental validation on established datasets; experiments are well-executed.  
- Theoretical formulation of SVM with expectation loss appears sound.  
- Flexible approach compatible with different segment proposal detectors and image features.  
- Core idea has limited novelty given prior weighted SVMs and MIL work; main novelty is the expectation-loss framing.  
- Latent e-SVM performance depends on regularization of positiveness values and may require careful parameter tuning.  
- Spatial confidence map construction is somewhat ad-hoc; impact of bin size and pooling needs deeper analysis.  
- Missing discussion/comparison to related confidence-estimation methods that predict scores with bounding boxes.  
- For acceptance: technically sound, compelling results, practical for weak supervision, and clearly written.  
- Against acceptance: incremental novelty and some heuristic components.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution worthy of acceptance with minor revisions (regularization sensitivity and spatial map clarification).  
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
