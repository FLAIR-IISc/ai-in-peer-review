- Proposes semantic labeling via candidate segments, feature extraction, and regression on IoU with pixel-accurate ground truth; treats object extent as latent for detection using only bounding-box labels.  
- Uses weighted SVM for semantic labeling and a weighted transductive SVM ("latent e-SVM") for object detection.  
- Reports competitive semantic labeling results and state-of-the-art object detection on VOC2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited and several key prior works on weighted and transductive SVMs are not cited.  
- The introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but needs broader literature comparison.  
- The "latent e-SVM" appears to be a weighted variant of previously proposed transductive SVMs.  
- Primary significance is strong empirical performance, notably outperforming recent CNN methods with conventional techniques.  
- Paper quality suffers from omitted references and heavy engineering complexity; core contributions rely on empirical results.  
- Authors should compare against standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression) and justify regularization choices (λW, λR).  
- Title and abstract poorly summarize contributions and misleadingly claim continuous targets are less informative than binary labels.  
- Exposition is unbalanced: overemphasizes minor algorithmic variations while underreporting crucial feature engineering details.  
- Minor issues: typos ("continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box"), undefined "AP", possible "SVM"→"SVC" at line 350, and phrasing fixes ("with every different values"→"for all threshold values").  
- Overall: excellent empirical results but weakened by limited novelty and inadequate referencing; addressing these issues is needed and the work may be more suitable for a vision venue.
