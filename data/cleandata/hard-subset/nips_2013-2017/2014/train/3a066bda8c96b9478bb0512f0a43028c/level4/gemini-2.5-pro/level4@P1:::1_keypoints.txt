- Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models labels as Bernoulli variables with known continuous "positiveness" scores and minimizes expected hinge loss solvable via weighted SVMs.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision by treating positiveness scores as latent variables to infer during training.  
- Provides a principled alternative to thresholding heuristics and standard SVR for bounded continuous labels.  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection; features from latent e-SVM improve R-CNN and set state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Addresses a significant practical problem: leveraging cheaper annotations (e.g., bounding boxes) for large-scale datasets.  
- Methodologically sound, clearly motivated, and broadly applicable for continuous-valued supervision.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation, including superiority over SVC/SVR and two new metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for the task.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains, highlighting practical value for segmentation-based features.  
- Paper is well-written and ideas are presented clearly.  
- Weakness: novelty is somewhat incremental—can be viewed as a weighted SVM instance with links to latent SVM and MIL; stronger comparison to related work would help.  
- Overall positive assessment: technically solid, effective, and a valuable contribution with a recommendation to accept.
