- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (E-SVM) to handle continuous-valued attributes of positive instances.  
- Applies E-SVM to weakly supervised visual segmentation and object detection, with empirical validation.  
- Addresses a significant challenge in weakly supervised visual learning with a well-motivated problem statement.  
- Extends MIL by replacing binary weighting with soft weighting of positive instances; applicable in both observed and latent settings.  
- Core idea: leveraging continuous-valued positive-instance attributes to improve weakly supervised learning.  
- Experimental results on standard benchmarks show effectiveness, supported by clear ablation studies.  
- Writing is accessible and the link between method and practical impact is well articulated.  
- Minor concern: lacks explicit discussion of computational overhead versus standard MIL approaches.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed contribution with compelling results, recommended for publication.
