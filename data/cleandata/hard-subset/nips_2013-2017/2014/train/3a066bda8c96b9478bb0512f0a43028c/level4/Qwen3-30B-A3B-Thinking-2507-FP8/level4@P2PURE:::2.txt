

The paper proposes a modification to the SVM learning algorithm that incorporates per-example weights (ranging from 0 to 1) into the loss function. This approach is then extended to scenarios where the weights are unobserved (latent variables). The method is clearly described, and experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest potential benefits over equal-weighting strategies. However, the core contribution appears to be a specific instantiation of well-established "example-dependent costs," which are commonly implemented in machine learning tools such as SVMlight. The proposed method thus lacks novelty in this regard.  

The extension to unobserved weights is interesting but straightforward. The resulting problem is non-convex, yet no theoretical analysis is provided. The authors employ strong regularization to constrain per-example weights near their initial values; they should clarify whether this is necessary or whether alternative approaches could avoid such constraints.  

Minor comments:  
- On line 50, the claim that "it is not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to a bounded interval [0, 1]" is incorrect. Logistic regression is standard and inherently bounded to [0, 1].  
- The abstract contains a spelling error: "continues" should be "continuous."
