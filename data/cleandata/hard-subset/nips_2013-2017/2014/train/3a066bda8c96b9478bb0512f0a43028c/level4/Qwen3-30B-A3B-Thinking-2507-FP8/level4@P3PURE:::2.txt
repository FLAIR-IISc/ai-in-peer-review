

The paper proposes a modification to the SVM learning algorithm for weakly supervised data, where each training example is assigned a per-example weight in [0,1] that modifies the loss function. The approach is extended to scenarios where these weights are latent variables.  

The method is clearly described, and experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest potential benefits over uniform weighting. However, the distinction between the proposed method and existing "example-dependent costs" is unclear. Such mechanisms are standard in machine learning and widely implemented (e.g., in SVMlight), rendering the core contribution unconvincing.  

The extension to latent weights is conceptually straightforward but introduces a non-convex problem without theoretical analysis. The reliance on strong regularization (implying weights remain close to initial values) warrants justification. The authors should address whether this regularization is necessary or if alternative strategies exist.  

Minor issues:  
- Line 50: The claim that "it is not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to [0,1]" is incorrect; logistic regression is standard and bounded to [0,1].  
- Abstract: "continues" should be "continuous."  

The paper's core proposal—modifying SVM loss via per-example weights—does not advance beyond established example-dependent cost frameworks. The experimental findings are insufficient to justify novelty.
