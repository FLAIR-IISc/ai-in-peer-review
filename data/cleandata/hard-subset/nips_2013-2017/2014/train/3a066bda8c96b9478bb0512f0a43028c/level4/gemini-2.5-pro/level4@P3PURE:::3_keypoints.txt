- Proposes IoU regression for semantic labeling using candidate segments and weighted SVM, and treats object extent as latent for detection with a weighted transductive SVM.
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 and competitive semantic labeling results.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; core methods are established weighted SVM/transductive SVM variants.
- Key prior work on weighted and transductive SVMs is omitted and must be cited (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Vapnik, Lapin et al.).
- The sum constraint on weights may be novel, but this alone does not offset the lack of new learning machinery.
- Missing proper baseline comparisons for regression on the unit interval (e.g., regularized logistic regression) to justify the proposed method.
- Insufficient detail on selection and tuning of regularization coefficients λW and λR.
- Paper clarity suffers: title/abstract do not summarize core contributions and make misleading claims about regression vs. binary labels.
- Paper is poorly balanced for the target audience, overemphasizing standard algorithm variants while under-describing crucial feature engineering.
- Minor editorial issues and typos (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", undefined "AP", possible "SVC" needed).
- Overall: strong empirical results justify publication potential, but major revisions required to address novelty claims, missing citations, baselines, methodological details, and restructuring.
