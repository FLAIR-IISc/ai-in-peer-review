The authors propose an expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem and apply the model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated for both semantic segmentation and object detection with promising results.

The main idea of the paper is to develop a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle situations where only the "positiveness" instead of a binary label of each training sample is available. The "positiveness" is a continuous value whose range is a bounded interval, and it quantifies the affiliation of each training data to the positive class.

The authors demonstrate that their e-SVM algorithm can effectively address the segment proposal classification task under both strong supervision (e.g., the pixel-level annotations are available) and weak supervision (e.g., only bounding-box annotations are available), and outperforms alternative approaches. They also validate their method on two major tasks of computer vision: semantic segmentation and object detection. The results show that their method achieves state-of-the-art object detection performance on the PASCAL VOC 2007 dataset.
