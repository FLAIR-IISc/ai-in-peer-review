- Introduces per-instance weights in [0,1] incorporated into the SVM loss, with an extension where weights are latent.
- Methodology is clearly presented and empirical results show potential benefits for tasks like semantic segmentation and object detection versus uniform weighting.
- Novelty is questionable because the approach resembles standard "example-dependent costs" (e.g., in SVMlight); authors should clarify the distinction.
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but straightforward and results in a non-convex optimization problem with no theoretical analysis or guarantees.
- The method appears to rely on strong regularization to keep learned weights near initialization; authors should justify this design choice.
- Authors should explain the absence of theoretical treatment or guarantees for the non-convex formulation.
- Minor: Incorrect claim that outputs in [0,1] make it "not a standard regression problem" — logistic regression produces outputs in this interval.
- Minor: Typo in abstract — "continues" should be "continuous."
