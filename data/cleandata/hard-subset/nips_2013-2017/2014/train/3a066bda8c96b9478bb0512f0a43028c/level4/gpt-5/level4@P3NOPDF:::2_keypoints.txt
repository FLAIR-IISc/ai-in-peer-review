- Proposes an SVM variant incorporating per-example weights in [0,1], with a latent-weight extension for weak supervision.  
- Clear integration of instance weights into the SVM objective and practical motivation for vision tasks with heterogeneous label quality.  
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show instance weighting can improve performance.  
- Core idea is close to well-known instance-dependent cost/weighting schemes (e.g., cost-sensitive SVMs, instance weights in SVM toolkits).  
- Need to explicitly differentiate the proposed loss from standard per-example slack penalties or importance weighting.  
- Latent-weight extension is conceptually straightforward (alternate updates) and yields a non-convex objective with no provided theoretical guarantees.  
- Strong regularization keeping latent weights near initialization raises concern that improvements may stem from initialization rather than learned reliabilities.  
- Request precise statement of how the weighted loss differs from standard per-example weighting or, if equivalent, emphasis on empirical/algorithmic innovations.  
- For the latent model, request details on optimization scheme, stopping criteria, convergence guarantees, and sensitivity to initialization and regularizer strength.  
- Ask for evidence that learned weights meaningfully deviate from initialization (distributions of learned weights and correlation with label-quality proxies).  
- Request baselines comparing to standard per-example weighted SVMs, simple heuristics (e.g., clipping/downweighting), and ablations on weight regularization and constraints.  
- Ask for comparison or positioning relative to weak supervision literature (e.g., Multiple Instance Learning) to clarify the method’s niche.  
- Request practical details on training time, scalability vs. standard SVM, and hyperparameter selection/validation protocol.  
- Recommend a dedicated related-work discussion on instance-weighted learning and a compact mathematical formulation showing equivalence/difference to standard instance weighting.  
- Minor corrections: revise incorrect claim about regression and [0,1] outputs, fix “continues”→“continuous,” and standardize terminology for “weight,” “cost,” and “reliability.”  
- Overall: promising and practically relevant but novelty is unclear; needs clearer positioning, stronger baselines/ablations, and analysis of optimization/sensitivity to be more compelling.
