This paper introduces the Expectation-Loss SVM (e-SVM), a novel framework for handling continuous attributes of positive instances within a weakly supervised learning setting. The authors apply this model to the important problem of learning visual segmentation classifiers using only image-level labels. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection, where it demonstrates promising results.

The paper's strengths lie in its clear motivation, lucid presentation, and thorough experimental evaluation. The problem of learning from weak supervision is highly relevant, and the authors make a compelling case for their approach. The experiments are well-designed and convincingly demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method.

The core technical contribution can be understood as a thoughtful extension of the standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) framework. The key innovation is replacing the conventional binary weighting of positive instances with a more flexible, soft weighting scheme. A significant advantage of this formulation is its applicability in both observed and latent variable settings, enhancing its power and versatility. While building on established concepts, this generalization represents a technically sound and valuable contribution.

In summary, this is a strong paper that addresses an important problem with an elegant and effective solution. The proposed e-SVM framework is a valid contribution to the field of weakly supervised learning and is well-suited for the NIPS audience.
