The authors propose an Expectation-Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, specifically enabling the model to handle continuous attributes of positive instances. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks with promising results.

This paper addresses the important problem of visual learning under weak supervision. The presentation is clear, the approach is well-motivated, and the experiments are both interesting and well-executed. While the technical contribution is an extension of the standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) framework, it is a valuable one. By replacing binary labels for positive instances with a soft weighting, the e-SVM can be applied in both observed and latent settings. This extension represents a valid and worthwhile contribution for NIPS.
