- Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous-valued attributes for positive instances to enable learning under weak supervision.
- Method applied to training visual segmentation classifiers and evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising results.
- Addresses an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning.
- Motivation is sound and presentation is clear.
- Experiments are well designed and well executed.
- Technically extends multiple-instance learning by replacing binary positive labels with soft weights and covers both observed and latent settings.
- Contribution is meaningful, appropriate for NIPS, and clearly relevant to semantic segmentation and object detection.
