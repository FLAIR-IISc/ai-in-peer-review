- Proposes modifying SVM training by assigning per-example weights in [0,1] and incorporating them into the SVM loss, extended to latent (unobserved) weights.  
- Method is clearly described and experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show improvements over uniform weighting.  
- Novelty is questionable relative to existing example-dependent cost formulations (e.g., SVMlight supports per-example cost); paper should explicitly state what is new.  
- The latent-weight extension is interesting but appears straightforward and yields a non-convex objective with no theoretical analysis provided.  
- Strong regularization seems to keep learned weights close to initial values, raising concerns about how much is actually learned versus imposed by the prior.  
- Request authors to comment on the design choice and implications of regularization on learnability of weights.  
- Minor points: incorrect claim that bounded outputs are non-standard regression (logistic regression outputs are in [0,1]); typo “continues” should be “continuous.”  
- Overall: empirical results are promising but contribution seems close to existing frameworks and needs clearer novelty/theoretical justification, especially for the non-convex latent case.
