- Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from examples with continuous "positiveness" scores (0–1) instead of binary labels.  
- Principled framework handles both observed positiveness (strong supervision) and unobserved positiveness (weak supervision), applied to segmentation and detection.  
- Addresses practical gap: pixel-level annotations are costly while bounding box annotations are abundant.  
- Extends SVM to incorporate continuous confidence values, differing from MIL which assumes fully positive instances in positive bags.  
- Treats labels as Bernoulli random variables with positiveness as success probability, yielding a novel expectation-based loss.  
- Latent e-SVM extension for weak supervision is well-motivated and practical.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art segmentation on PASCAL VOC 2011 (36.8% with pixels, 27.7% with boxes) and +1.5% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 detection (60.0% mAP).  
- Notable category-level gains (e.g., "potted plant" +3.65% AP).  
- Introduces two new evaluation metrics for segment classification (TAPC and NDCG).  
- Technical quality is high: clear theoretical foundation, logical derivation, appropriate baselines (SVC, SVR), and thorough ablation studies.  
- Paper clarity is excellent with helpful figures and accessible exposition.  
- Originality: meaningful contribution via expectation-based loss and label weighting, distinct from weighted SVM and MIL approaches.  
- Significance: method can leverage abundant bounding-box data to reduce costly pixel annotations and yields meaningful practical gains.  
- Minor weaknesses: brief theoretical justification for setting µi = ui and limited discussion of relations to other confidence-aware methods.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, well-motivated, significant work — recommend acceptance.
