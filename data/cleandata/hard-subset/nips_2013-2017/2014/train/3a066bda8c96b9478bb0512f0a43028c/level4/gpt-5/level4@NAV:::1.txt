The authors present an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that accommodates continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning task and use it to train visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The approach is evaluated on both semantic segmentation and object detection with encouraging results. The paper targets an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning; the narrative is clear and well-motivated, and the experiments are interesting and well executed. Although the technical contribution is an extension of the standard multiple-instance learning framework (replacing a binary indicator with a soft weighting of positives), it is developed for both observed and latent settings and constitutes a solid contribution for NIPS. An expectation-loss SVM that handles continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem and is applied to train visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. Strong motivation, clear exposition, and relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
