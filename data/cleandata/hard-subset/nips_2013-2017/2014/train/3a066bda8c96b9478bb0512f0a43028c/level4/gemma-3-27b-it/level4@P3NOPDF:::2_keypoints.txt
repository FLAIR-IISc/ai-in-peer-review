- Proposes SVM modification that uses per-example weights (0–1) in the loss to prioritize reliable instances, and extends to latent (inferred) weights.
- Paper is clearly written and the method is well-described.
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show improvements over uniform-weight SVM.
- Novelty concern: example-dependent costs/weights are well-known and supported by tools like SVMlight, so contribution may not be distinct.
- The weighted SVM appears largely equivalent to existing weighted-cost SVM implementations; clearer differentiation from prior work is needed.
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but introduces non-convexity without theoretical analysis of convergence or optimality.
- Strong regularization keeps latent weights near initial values, suggesting reliance on prior knowledge to avoid poor local optima; sensitivity to regularization should be analyzed.
- Authors should explain why standard non-convex optimization techniques were not used or were insufficient.
- Specific factual/comment corrections: the claim that predicting values in [0,1] is “not a standard regression problem” is incorrect (e.g., logistic regression); abstract has a typo (“continues” → “continuous”).
- Recommendation: require stronger argument for novelty and deeper theoretical/empirical analysis of latent-weight formulation before acceptance.
