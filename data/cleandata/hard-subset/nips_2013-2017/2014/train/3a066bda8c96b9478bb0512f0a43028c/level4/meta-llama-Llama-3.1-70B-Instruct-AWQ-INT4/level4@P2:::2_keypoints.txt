- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle weakly supervised data with continuous bounded labels.
- Core idea: modify SVM loss by assigning per-example weights based on a continuous “positiveness” in [0,1].
- Extends algorithm to latent per-example weights when weights are unobserved.
- Experiments: e-SVM outperforms traditional SVM and SVR on segment proposal classification.
- Semantic segmentation: achieves comparable results to state-of-the-art SVR segment confidence model.
- Object detection: improves average precision by 1.2 (before) and 1.5 (after) bounding box regression.
- Strength: algorithm is clearly described with convincing experimental validation.
- Strength: latent-weight extension is interesting and relevant to weakly supervised learning.
- Strength: method is flexible and applicable to multiple computer vision tasks.
- Weakness: per-example weighting is not novel—similar to existing “example dependent costs”—needs clarification of novelty.
- Weakness: latent-weight extension results in a non-convex problem and lacks theoretical analysis; role of strong regularization should be discussed.
- Weakness: inaccurate claim that bounded positiveness makes it not a standard regression problem (logistic regression counters this).
- Minor: spelling error in abstract ("continues" should be "continuous").
- Overall conclusion: promising and effective method but unclear novelty and insufficient theoretical treatment of the non-convex extension.
