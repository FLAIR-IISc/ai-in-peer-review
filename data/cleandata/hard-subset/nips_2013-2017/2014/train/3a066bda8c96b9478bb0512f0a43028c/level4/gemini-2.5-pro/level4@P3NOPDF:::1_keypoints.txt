- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle positive instances with continuous attributes in weakly supervised learning.  
- Applies e-SVM to learn visual segmentation classifiers from image-level labels, treating object locations and boxes as latent variables.  
- Validated on standard semantic segmentation and object detection benchmarks with promising results.  
- Addresses practical problem of reducing reliance on expensive pixel-level annotations.  
- Motivation is clear and presentation is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Experiments are relevant, well-executed, and provide compelling empirical evidence.  
- Technically, e-SVM is an elegant extension of Multiple Instance Learning introducing soft weighting instead of binary instance selection.  
- Frames objective as an expectation over loss across potential positive instances to model continuous attributes like location and scale.  
- Applicable to both observed-attribute settings and more complex latent-attribute training.  
- Builds on established ideas but offers a valuable generalization of MIL with practical utility for weakly supervised segmentation and detection.
