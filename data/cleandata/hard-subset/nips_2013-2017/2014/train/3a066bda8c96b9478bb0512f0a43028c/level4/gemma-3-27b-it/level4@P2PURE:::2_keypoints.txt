- Proposes modifying SVM for weakly supervised data by assigning a weight in [0,1] to each training example and incorporating it into the loss.  
- Extends the approach to handle latent (unobserved) per-example weights.  
- Experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection indicate potential benefit over treating all examples equally.  
- Core idea is closely related to existing example-dependent cost methods and lacks clear distinction from prior work (e.g., SVMlight).  
- Latent-weight extension is notable but yields a non-convex problem and no theoretical analysis is provided.  
- Method appears to rely on strong regularization that keeps learned weights near their initial values; rationale and impact of this regularization are not explained.  
- Incorrect claim that the bounded [0,1] outputs make it “not a standard regression problem”; logistic regression produces outputs in [0,1].  
- Minor typo in the abstract: “continues” should be “continuous.”  
- Overall: modifies SVM with per-example weights, but similar example-dependent cost ideas are already common practice.
