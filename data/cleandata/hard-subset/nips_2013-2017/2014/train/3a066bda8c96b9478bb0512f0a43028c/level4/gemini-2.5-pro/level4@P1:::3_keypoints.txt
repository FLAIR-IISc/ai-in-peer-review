- Proposes e-SVM to train on continuous positiveness labels in [0,1], using weighted SVM for segmentation and a latent weighted (transductive) SVM for weakly supervised detection.  
- Reports state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 and competitive semantic segmentation results.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited—e-SVM is essentially a form of weighted SVM and the latent variant resembles transductive SVMs.  
- Fails to cite and discuss closely related prior work on weighted and transductive SVMs.  
- Main significance is strong empirical performance, notably outperforming recent CNN-based detectors.  
- Strength lies in empirical results; weaknesses include missing references and insufficient methodological comparisons.  
- Should compare against standard baselines for regression on [0,1], e.g., regularized logistic regression.  
- Needs more detail on hyperparameter selection (λ_w and λ_R) and clearer presentation of engineering components.  
- Paper overemphasizes minor algorithmic variants for a NIPS audience while underreporting crucial feature engineering (e.g., spatial confidence map).  
- Title and abstract mischaracterize the core contribution as a fundamental algorithmic advance rather than an applied/engineering success.  
- Incorrect claim in abstract that continuous regression targets are less informative than binary labels.  
- Minor edits: fix typos ("continues"→"continuous"; "bounding boxes annotations"→"bounding box annotations"), define AP, and clarify labels like "SVC-0.0"/"SVC-0.2" in figures.  
- Conclusion: effective, high-performing system but limited novelty and poor literature grounding; requires substantial revision and may be better suited for a top-tier vision conference.
