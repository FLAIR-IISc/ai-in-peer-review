- Proposes a weakly supervised learning framework that regresses candidate segment IoU to pixel-accurate ground truth and treats precise segment extent as a latent variable for box-only annotations.  
- Implements regression via a weighted SVM and a latent-variable extension, using candidate segments and extracted features.  
- Achieves competitive semantic labeling and state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007, outperforming recent CNN-based methods.  
- Builds on established weakly supervised and SVM techniques; weighting samples by “positiveness” is not wholly novel but the specific formulation is useful.  
- Fails to sufficiently cite closely related prior work (weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs), with only partial novelty claimed for the sum constraint on weights.  
- Strong empirical significance: effective across segmentation and detection and potentially more computationally efficient than deep learning approaches.  
- Technical quality is generally sound with a well-designed experimental setup and clear results.  
- Lacks comparison to appropriate baseline regression methods for the unit interval (e.g., regularized logistic regression).  
- Omits details on selection of regularization hyperparameters (λ_W and λ_R), harming reproducibility.  
- Feature engineering in the object detection pipeline is glossed over and needs more detailed explanation.  
- Title and abstract are misleading and underplay the core contribution; abstract contains an inaccurate claim about information content of unit-interval regression vs binary labels.  
- Paper spends excessive space on incremental algorithmic variations while insufficiently detailing key implementation aspects.  
- Contains minor textual and notation errors (typos, undefined “AP”, inconsistent use of “SVM” vs “SVC”, awkward phrasing).  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, versatility, practical weak-supervision focus, and potential efficiency gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing related-work citations, limited algorithmic novelty, inadequate baseline comparisons, and clarity/presentation issues.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with compelling results but requires substantial revision to address literature context, novelty claims, methodological comparisons, and clarity; may suit a vision venue if not revised.  
- Cited missing/related works noted by reviewer: Suykens et al. (2002), Yang et al. (2005), Vapnik (1998), Lapin et al. (2014).
