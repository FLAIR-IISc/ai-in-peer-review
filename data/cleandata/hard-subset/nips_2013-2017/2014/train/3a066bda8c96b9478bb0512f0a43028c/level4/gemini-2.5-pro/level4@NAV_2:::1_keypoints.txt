- Proposes e-SVM: an SVM loss formulated as an expectation over a latent binary label whose positivity probability is a function of a continuous "positiveness" score (e.g., IoU).
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weakly supervised settings by treating unobserved proposal positiveness as latent variables to be optimized.
- Positions the method relative to SVC, SVR, and weighted SVMs, arguing the formulation better matches the problem structure.
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection (PASCAL VOC), showing improvements over baselines and boosting R-CNN detection performance.
- Strength — significance/motivation: addresses practical weak supervision needs and avoids crude thresholding or pure regression approaches.
- Strength — contribution quality: offers an elegant, principled model and natural latent extension, plus new evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG) for segment proposal classification.
- Strength — experiments: e-SVM consistently outperforms SVC/SVR on proposed metrics and provides complementary cues that improve state-of-the-art detection.
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written with clear problem setup, model description, and experimental presentation.
- Weakness — originality/context: formulation is closely related to instance-weighted SVMs and standard latent SVM iterative methods; needs more formal comparison to weighted SVM objectives.
- Weakness — optimization: iterative latent e-SVM algorithm lacks discussion of convergence properties and susceptibility to local minima.
- Weakness — baselines scope: detection improvement demonstrated only with R-CNN; broader detector families should be tested to show generality.
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper with solid results and minor methodological/experimental clarifications needed; suitable for the conference.
