This paper proposes a modification to the Support Vector Machine (SVM) algorithm for weakly supervised learning. The core idea is to associate each training example with a weight between 0 and 1, which is then used to modulate its contribution to the SVM loss function. The method is also extended to handle cases where these per-example weights are unobserved latent variables.

The paper is well-written, and the proposed method is described clearly. The experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks suggest that this approach may offer advantages over the standard approach of weighting all examples equally.

However, my primary concern is the limited novelty of the proposed method. The concept of modulating the loss for each sample is functionally equivalent to the well-established technique of using "example-dependent costs." This feature is widely known, used in the machine learning community, and available in standard SVM implementations (e.g., SVMlight). The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contribution from this existing work.

The extension to latent, unobserved weights is an interesting direction. However, this extension appears to be a straightforward application of alternating optimization, resulting in a non-convex objective function. The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of this formulation, providing no guarantees on convergence or solution quality. Indeed, the use of a strong regularization term on the weights is noted, which may prevent them from deviating significantly from their initial values. Could the authors comment on the sensitivity of the model to this regularization and clarify how much the weights are actually learned versus being fixed by their initialization?

Minor points:
*   The claim on L050 that the problem is "not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to a bounded interval [0, 1]" is questionable. Standard models, such as logistic regression, are routinely used for exactly this type of problem.
*   There is a spelling mistake in the abstract: "continues" should be "continuous".
