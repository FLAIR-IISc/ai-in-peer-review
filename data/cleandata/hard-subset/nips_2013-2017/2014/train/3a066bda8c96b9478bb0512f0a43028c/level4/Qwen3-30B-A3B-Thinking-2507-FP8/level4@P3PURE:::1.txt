

The authors propose an expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous attributes of positive instances in learning problems and applies this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated for both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results. The paper addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision, with strong motivation, clear presentation, and well-executed experiments. Although the technical content extends the standard multiple instance learning framework by replacing binary weighting with soft weighting of positives, it applies effectively in both observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution for NIPS.
