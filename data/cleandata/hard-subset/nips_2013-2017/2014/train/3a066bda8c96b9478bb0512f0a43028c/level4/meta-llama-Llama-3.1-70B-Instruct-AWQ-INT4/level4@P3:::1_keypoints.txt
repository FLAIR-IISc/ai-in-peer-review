- Proposed e-SVM handles continuous positive-instance attributes, suited for weakly supervised visual learning with bounding-box annotations.
- Applied e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection, showing effective performance in both tasks.
- Addresses an important, practical problem in computer vision where dense manual labeling is costly.
- Solution is well-motivated, clearly presented, and experimentally validated with thorough evaluations.
- Technical contribution: extends multiple instance learning by replacing binary positive weighting with soft (expectation) weighting.
- Method is applied in both observed and latent settings, representing a valid extension of MIL frameworks.
- Strengths include strong motivation, clear exposition, and relevant results across scenarios and annotation types.
- Demonstrates compatibility with other object detection systems and robustness under varied supervision.
- Weakness: connection to prior work on multiple instance learning and weighted SVMs is not discussed explicitly enough.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis/insight into the choice of the regularization term and its effect on performance.
- Overall assessment: well-written, thorough evaluation, promising results with potential broad applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; reviewer rating 8/10.
