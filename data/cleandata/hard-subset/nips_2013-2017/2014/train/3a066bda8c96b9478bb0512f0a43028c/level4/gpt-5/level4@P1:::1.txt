The paper proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous “positiveness” values in [0,1] for binary labels and applies it to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The method supports both settings where positiveness is observed and where it is latent, and is validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising results.

The problem—learning from weakly supervised data—is important, and the paper is clear, well-motivated, and experimentally thorough. Although the technical contribution can be seen as an extension of standard multiple-instance learning (replacing binary labels with soft weights on positives), the formulation applies cleanly in both the observed and latent cases and constitutes a valid contribution for NIPS. The results are relevant and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach on semantic segmentation and object detection.

In summary: a well-motivated e-SVM approach for continuous “positiveness” labels, clearly presented and supported by solid experiments, with useful applications to semantic segmentation and object detection.
