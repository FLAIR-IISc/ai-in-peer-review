- Introduces e-SVM: expected hinge loss using per-example positiveness u in [0,1], optimized with standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Latent variant jointly optimizes unobserved u with classifier using a convex regularizer anchored to initialization (e.g., IoU).  
- Evaluated on segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection, showing consistent gains and ~1.5 AP improvement when adding an e-SVM spatial confidence map to R-CNN.  
- Method is closely related to instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs and soft/probabilistic-label SVM formulations; practical distinction from these is not clearly articulated.  
- Clear motivation: avoids heuristic binarization of continuous overlap/IoU targets.  
- Simple, practical objective compatible with off-the-shelf SVM toolkits.  
- Empirical improvements on TAPC/NDCG for segment proposals and measurable downstream benefits.  
- Weakly supervised extension is sensible, using a regularizer tethered to bounding-box–derived IoU.  
- Spatial confidence map feature is simple and effective when combined with R-CNN.  
- Novelty is limited relative to existing example-dependent cost and soft-label SVM methods; theoretical differences are unclear.  
- Latent variant is non-convex with no convergence guarantees or analysis; regularization appears to keep u near initialization.  
- Sensitivity to regularization strength and initialization is not studied; unclear whether u-values meaningfully change.  
- Choice g(u)=u is reasonable but unexplored; effect of alternative link functions is unexamined.  
- Comparisons are mainly to SVC/SVR; missing direct baselines like per-example cost-weighted SVMs and logistic regression with probabilistic labels.  
- Quality: method is sound and easy to implement, but latent optimization lacks theoretical/ablation analysis.  
- Clarity: paper is readable but needs clearer exposition of distinctions from weighted SVMs and minor wording fixes.  
- Originality: incremental; expected hinge-loss perspective is neat but related ideas are established.  
- Significance: moderate; practical and effective but conceptual contribution over prior methods is small.  
- Requests: formally state how e-SVM differs from instance-weighted/probabilistic-label SVMs or acknowledge equivalence, provide sensitivity analyses for g(u), λR, and u initialization, and add matching per-example cost and probabilistic-label baselines.  
- Minor comments: clarify use of “positiveness” (prefer “soft label”/“probabilistic label”/“affinity”), correct typo “continues” → “continuous,” and avoid misleading claims about regression for [0,1] targets.
