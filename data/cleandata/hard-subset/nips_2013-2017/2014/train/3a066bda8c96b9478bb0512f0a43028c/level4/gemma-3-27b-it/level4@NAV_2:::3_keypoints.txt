- Presents a method for semantic labeling and object detection using candidate segments, regression on IoU to pixel-accurate ground truth, and latent-variable handling for weak labels; uses weighted SVM and weighted transductive SVM, achieving competitive semantic labeling and state-of-the-art VOC2007 detection.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; many relevant prior works on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs are not cited.  
- The introduced sum constraint on weights may be novel but its novelty is uncertain without broader literature review.  
- The “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted version of a transductive SVM and echoes earlier work.  
- Outperforming recent CNN-based methods with a conventional approach is a significant and interesting result.  
- Key references are missing, weakening the paper’s contextualization.  
- The approach involves substantial engineering consistent with the field, and its main strength is strong empirical performance.  
- The authors should compare against standard baselines for unit-interval regression (e.g., regularized logistic regression) to justify the weighted SVM choice.  
- More detail is needed on how regularization coefficients (λw and λr) were selected.  
- Title and abstract do not clearly summarize the core contribution and contain misleading statements about regression targets versus binary labels.  
- The paper spends excessive space on minor algorithmic variations and too little on feature engineering details for the intended audience.  
- Minor editorial issues: typos and abbreviation problems (e.g., “continues”→“continuous”, “boxes”→“box”, AP not defined, SVM→SVC, phrasing about threshold values).  
- Pros for acceptance: very strong empirical results, clear method and experiments, and a useful demonstration of weakly supervised learning.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing citations, limited novelty, and a need for a more concise presentation of background material.
