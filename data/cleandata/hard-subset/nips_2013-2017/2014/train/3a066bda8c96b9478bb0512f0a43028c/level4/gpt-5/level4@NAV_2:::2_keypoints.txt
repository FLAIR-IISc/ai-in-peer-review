- Proposes an SVM variant that assigns each example a positiveness score in [0,1] and reforms the SVM loss accordingly, with an extension treating these scores as latent when unobserved.
- Exposition is clear and experiments show potential gains on semantic segmentation and object detection versus treating all examples equally.
- Unclear novelty: the method appears similar to well-known example-dependent cost schemes (e.g., SVMlight supports such mechanisms).
- Latent-weight extension is interesting but fairly straightforward; the resulting objective is non-convex and lacks theoretical analysis.
- In practice a strong regularizer is used, which seems to keep latent weights close to initialization; authors should discuss this choice and its implications.
- Minor factual critique: statement that positiveness in [0,1] is “not a standard regression problem” is debatable since logistic regression produces [0,1] outputs.
- Minor typo: abstract uses “continues” instead of “continuous.”
- Overall: core idea is reweighting SVM loss by per-instance factors, but example-dependent costs are already commonplace in ML.
