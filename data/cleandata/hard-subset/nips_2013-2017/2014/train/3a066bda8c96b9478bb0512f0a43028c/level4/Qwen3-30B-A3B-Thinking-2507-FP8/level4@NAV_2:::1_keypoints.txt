- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous "positiveness" values instead of binary labels.  
- Extends beyond MIL by handling proposals with mixed foreground/background (0 < positiveness < 1).  
- Treats positiveness as observed (strong) or latent (weak supervision) variables in the model.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on segment proposal classification and applies to semantic segmentation and object detection.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 with +1.5% AP over R-CNN.  
- Technical formulation is sound with a clear derivation of the expectation loss function.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with appropriate baselines (SVC, SVR) and new metrics (TAPC, NDCG).  
- Presentation and clarity are excellent, including detailed latent e-SVM extension.  
- Originality: novel use of continuous positiveness and clear distinction from prior weighted SVM approaches.  
- Significance: addresses an important weak-supervision problem and shows tangible improvements on major benchmarks.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with related weighted SVM methods on computational complexity and scalability.  
- Weakness: needs a more concrete discussion of practical implementation using large-scale bounding-box annotations (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Weakness: broader applications beyond computer vision are only briefly mentioned and need more detailed discussion.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant, and original contribution; recommended for acceptance.
