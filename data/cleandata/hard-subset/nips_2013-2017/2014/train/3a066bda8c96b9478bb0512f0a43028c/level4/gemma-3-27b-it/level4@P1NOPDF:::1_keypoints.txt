- Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous attributes of positive instances in learning.
- Applies e-SVM to weakly supervised visual segmentation and object detection.
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection with promising, competitive results.
- Addresses the important problem of learning with weak supervision.
- Presentation is clear, well-motivated, and experiments are interesting and well-executed.
- Extends standard Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) by replacing binary positive weighting with soft/expected weighting.
- Applicability to both observed and latent settings is a valid and notable contribution.
- Soft weighting provides a more nuanced representation of positives, helpful for continuous-valued features.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with clear comparisons to relevant baselines.
- Results sometimes surpass existing weakly supervised methods.
- Lacks detailed discussion/theoretical analysis of computational complexity and scaling of the expectation calculation.
- Would benefit from sensitivity analysis to choice of kernel and expectation estimation techniques.
- Overall, a significant, principled, and effective advance in weakly supervised learning with strong empirical support.
