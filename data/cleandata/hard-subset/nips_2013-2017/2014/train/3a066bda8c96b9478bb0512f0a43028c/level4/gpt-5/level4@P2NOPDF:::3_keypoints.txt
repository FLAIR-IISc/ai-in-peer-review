- Presents a framework for semantic labeling (segment candidates, feature extraction, IoU regression) and detection (precise object extent as latent variable) using weighted SVMs; matches SOTA for labeling and best-known VOC2007 detection.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; fails to cite key prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs (R1–R4); the sum constraint on weights may be the only possibly new element.  
- The “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted transductive SVM variant and lacks proper attribution.  
- Significance: outperforming recent CNN-based methods with a conventional pipeline is notable and of clear community interest.  
- Missing important references and literature discussion; principal strength is empirical performance.  
- Methodological concerns: should include logistic regression baseline for targets in [0,1] and justify advantage of sum-constrained weighted SVM.  
- Experimental details lacking: need clarification on how regularization parameters (lambda_W, lambda_R) were selected.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract do not clearly state contributions and incorrectly claim continuous targets are less informative than binary labels.  
- Paper devotes too much space to minor algorithmic variations and too little to crucial feature-engineering/details.  
- Minor errata and wording fixes suggested (lines 19, 94, 98, 350 and phrasing changes).  
- Overall recommendation: excellent empirical results but insufficient referencing and limited novelty; publishable after revisions, possibly better suited to a computer-vision venue.
