The authors introduce an Expectation-loss Support Vector Machine (e-SVM) designed to accommodate continuous attributes for positive instances, and demonstrate its application to visual segmentation classification under weak supervision. The proposed methodology is empirically validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding encouraging results. This work tackles a significant challenge in visual learning – learning with weak supervision – and is presented with clarity and strong motivation. The experiments are compelling and thoroughly conducted. While technically building upon the established multiple instance learning framework (specifically, through a transition from binary to soft weighting of positive instances), the method’s applicability to both observed and latent settings constitutes a worthwhile contribution to NIPS. The e-SVM effectively addresses continuous positive instance attributes and shows promise in learning visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, supported by relevant results in semantic segmentation and object detection, and a well-articulated motivation.
