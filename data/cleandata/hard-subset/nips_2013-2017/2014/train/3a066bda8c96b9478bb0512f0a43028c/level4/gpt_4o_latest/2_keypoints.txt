- Proposes an SVM variant that assigns a per-example weight (0–1) into the loss and extends this to latent (unobserved) per-example weights.
- Method is clearly presented and experiments (semantic segmentation, object detection) show potential advantages over uniform weighting.
- Hard to distinguish the contribution from well-known "example-dependent costs" approaches (e.g., SVMlight supports such costs).
- Latent-weight extension is straightforward, yields a non-convex problem, lacks theoretical analysis, and strong regularization suggests weights stay near their initial values — authors should clarify.
- Incorrect/misleading claim: stating the problem isn’t a standard regression because outputs lie in [0,1] — logistic regression also produces [0,1].
- Minor typo in abstract: “continues” should be “continuous.”
- Overall: useful empirical idea but conceptually overlaps with established example-dependent cost methods.
