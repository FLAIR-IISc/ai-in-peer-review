- Paper proposes e-SVM: treat labels as Bernoulli with positiveness u∈[0,1] and minimize expected hinge loss so each example contributes weighted positive/negative terms.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM where positiveness scores u are unobserved and optimized jointly with SVM parameters via alternating minimization.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, showing improvements over SVC/SVR and +1.5 AP over R-CNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Introduces task-specific evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment-wise prediction.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical, important problem of learning from continuous “soft” labels common in vision.  
- Strength: Strong, thorough empirical results demonstrating practical gains on multiple tasks.  
- Strength: Elegant expected-loss formulation and sensible latent-variable extension; paper is well written and organized.  
- Main weakness: Insufficient distinction and experimental comparison to standard weighted SVMs (WSVM); need a WSVM baseline with instance-dependent costs.  
- Main weakness: Latent optimization is non-convex with no theoretical analysis of convergence or guarantees.  
- Main weakness: Lack of analysis on how much latent u values change from initialization and sensitivity to KL-regularization λ_R.  
- Minor: Typo in abstract ("continues" → "continuous").  
- Minor: Claim about bounded targets not being a standard regression problem is misleading—methods like logistic or beta regression exist and should be acknowledged.  
- Conclusion: Solid, well-motivated contribution with strong empirical impact, but would be strengthened by clearer positioning versus WSVM and deeper analysis of the latent optimization.
