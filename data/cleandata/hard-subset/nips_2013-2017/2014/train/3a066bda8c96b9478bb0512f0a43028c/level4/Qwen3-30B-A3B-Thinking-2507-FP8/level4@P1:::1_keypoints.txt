- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous "positiveness" values in [0,1].  
- Handles both strong supervision (observed positiveness) and weak supervision (latent positiveness from bounding boxes).  
- Addresses SVC’s need for ad-hoc thresholding and SVR’s unsuitability for bounded targets by modeling labels as Bernoulli random variables with given probabilities.  
- Latent extension allows learning from bounding-box annotations without pixel-level labels, useful for large-scale datasets.  
- Differentiates from MIL and WSVM: problem assumptions and weighting strategy are meaningfully different.  
- Provides a clear theoretical derivation and an optimization procedure solvable with standard SVM solvers.  
- Strong empirical results: +1.5% AP on PASCAL VOC 2007 over R-CNN and competitive semantic segmentation performance.  
- Solid experimental design with appropriate baselines, TAPC/NDCG metrics, and ablation studies on SVC thresholds.  
- Clear presentation and reproducibility details (figures, CPMC segments, spatial confidence map construction).  
- Original and significant: reduces reliance on expensive pixel-level annotations by effectively using bounding boxes.  
- Minor weakness: limited comparison to recent weakly-supervised methods (e.g., Liu et al. 2013, Vezhnevets et al. 2006).  
- Minor weakness: insufficient analysis of interactions with different feature representations and hyperparameter sensitivity of the regularizer.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-presented, and impactful.
