- Paper proposes semantic labeling via candidate segments with IoU regression and object detection treating precise extent as a latent variable, using weighted SVM and a weighted transductive SVM; achieves state-of-the-art detection on VOC 2007 and competitive semantic labeling.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; the methods are largely known and the proposed “latent e-SVM” is essentially a weighted transductive SVM.  
- Crucial prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs is omitted and should be cited (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Vapnik, Lapin et al.).  
- The imposed sum constraint on weights may be new but is not sufficiently motivated or compared to existing approaches.  
- Significant result: outperforming recent CNNs with a conventional approach is notable and of clear community interest.  
- Quality concerns: missing baselines and references, substantial engineering in the detection framework, and lack of justification for using a sum-constrained weighted SVM over natural alternatives like (regularized) logistic regression.  
- Methodological detail lacking: authors must clarify how regularization coefficients (λW and λR) were selected.  
- Clarity issues: title/abstract fail to succinctly state the core contribution and contain misleading claims about unit-interval regression vs. binary labels.  
- Paper spends too much space on minor algorithmic variations and not enough on crucial feature-engineering details.  
- Minor editorial fixes needed: Line 19 “continues”→“continuous”; Line 94 “boxes”→“box”; introduce AP abbreviation at Line 98; Line 350 “SVM”→“SVC” and rephrase “with every different values”→“for all threshold values.”  
- Recommendation: Publish after rewriting to properly relate to prior work, add missing baselines/justifications, and improve clarity; possibly more appropriate for a computer vision conference.
