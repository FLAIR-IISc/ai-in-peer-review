- Introduces Expectation-loss SVM (E-SVM) to handle continuous attributes for positive instances.  
- Targets visual segmentation classification under weak supervision.  
- Validated empirically on semantic segmentation and object detection with encouraging results.  
- Addresses the important problem of learning with limited annotations.  
- Paper is clearly written and well motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates method effectiveness.  
- Extends multiple instance learning by using soft weighting of positive instances rather than binary assignment.  
- Applicable to both observed and latent settings, representing a meaningful contribution.  
- E-SVM’s handling of continuous positive attributes and its weakly supervised applications are well-justified and supported by results.
