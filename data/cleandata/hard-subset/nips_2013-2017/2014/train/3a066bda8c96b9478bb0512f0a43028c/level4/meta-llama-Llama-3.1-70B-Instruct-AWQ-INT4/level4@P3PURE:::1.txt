The authors propose an expectation loss Support Vector Machine (e-SVM) that can handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem, and apply this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated for both semantic segmentation and object detection, yielding promising results. The paper addresses an important problem of visual learning under weak supervision, presenting a clear and well-motivated approach, with interesting and well-executed experiments. Although the technical content is an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework (by replacing a binary weighting with a soft weighting of positives), it applies in both the observed and the latent setting, and represents a valid contribution for NIPS. The expectation loss SVM method effectively handles continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem, and its application to learning visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision is well-motivated. The paper is well-presented, with clear motivation and relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
