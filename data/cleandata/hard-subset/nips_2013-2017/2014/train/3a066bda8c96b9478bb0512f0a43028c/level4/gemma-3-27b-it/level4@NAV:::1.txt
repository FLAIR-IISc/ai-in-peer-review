The authors introduce an Expectation-loss Support Vector Machine (E-SVM) designed to accommodate continuous attributes associated with positive instances, and demonstrate its application to visual segmentation classification under weak supervision. The proposed methodology is empirically validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding encouraging results. This work tackles a significant challenge in visual learning – learning with limited annotation – and is presented with commendable clarity and strong motivation. The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the method’s effectiveness. While building upon the established multiple instance learning framework (specifically, extending it through a soft weighting of positive instances instead of a binary assignment), the approach is applicable to both observed and latent settings, representing a worthwhile contribution to NIPS. The E-SVM’s ability to handle continuous positive attributes, coupled with its application to weak supervision, is well-justified, clearly articulated, and supported by relevant findings in semantic segmentation and object detection.
