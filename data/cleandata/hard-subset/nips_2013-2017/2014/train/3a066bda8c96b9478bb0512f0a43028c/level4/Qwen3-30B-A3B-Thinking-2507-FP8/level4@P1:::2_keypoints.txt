- Proposes e-SVM: an SVM variant that incorporates continuous per-example "positiveness" weights (0–1) and a latent extension when these values are unobserved.  
- Applies e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection, claiming state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivates why standard SVC (requires binarization) and SVR (ignores bounded nature) are suboptimal for bounded continuous labels.  
- e-SVM directly incorporates positiveness into the loss, which addresses the binarization and bounding issues in a practical way.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: improvements over SVC/SVR on segment proposal classification using TAPC and NDCG, and ~1.5% mean AP gain on VOC2007 with convincing ablations.  
- Main novelty is limited: instance-dependent weighting in SVMs is well-known (e.g., SVMlight, LIBLINEAR), so the core idea is an application of established techniques.  
- The paper insufficiently discusses prior work on instance-dependent costs and does not clearly articulate what is novel about this implementation.  
- The latent e-SVM extension (iterative optimization with a strong KL regularizer toward bounding-box estimates) is reasonable but appears straightforward.  
- Missing theoretical analysis: no convergence or optimality guarantees for the non-convex latent optimization and little justification for the regularization choice.  
- The claim that the problem is not standard regression due to boundedness is misleading; logistic/weighted logistic approaches are relevant comparisons.  
- Minor writing issues: typo in abstract ("continues" → "continuous") and lack of a formal mathematical definition for "positiveness."  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with useful empirical gains but insufficient novelty for a NIPS publication; recommend rejection and suggest an application-focused venue.
