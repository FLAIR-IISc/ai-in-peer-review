- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from weak supervision where labels are continuous confidence scores.  
- Extends standard SVM to handle continuous target values and covers supervised and weakly supervised settings.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Tackles an important problem for scaling ML to large, weakly labeled datasets.  
- Core idea—adapting SVM to continuous confidences—is reasonable and addresses limitations of binary classification/regression.  
- Strong empirical results, notably outperforming recent CNN methods in object detection.  
- Paper under-engages with related work and overstates novelty.  
- Omits key prior work in weighted SVMs and transductive learning (e.g., Suykens et al. 2002; Yang et al. 2005; Lapin et al. 2014; Vapnik 1998).  
- Algorithmic novelty is incremental; latent e-SVM resembles weighted transductive SVM and the sum constraint is a minor addition.  
- Method is practically significant due to applicability across supervision levels and competitive performance without deep models.  
- Technically sound with clear mathematical formulation and experiment setup.  
- Lacks comparison to standard regression baselines like regularized logistic regression.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection (λ_W, λ_R) and no sensitivity analysis.  
- Needs a more rigorous ablation study to validate method components.  
- Writing is generally clear but title and abstract misrepresent the main contribution and make misleading claims about continuous confidences.  
- Introduction emphasizes algorithm variations while glossing over crucial feature engineering details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, important problem, sound method, multi-task applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor literature engagement, incremental novelty, missing baseline comparisons, weak hyperparameter discussion, misleading title/abstract.  
- Overall: solid contribution but recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address related work, baselines, hyperparameters, title/abstract, and feature details.  
- Requested revisions: expand related work, compare to logistic regression baseline, detail hyperparameter selection and sensitivity, revise title/abstract, and clarify image feature engineering.  
- Suggested references to add: Vapnik (1998), Lapin et al. (2014), Suykens et al. (2002), Yang et al. (2005).
