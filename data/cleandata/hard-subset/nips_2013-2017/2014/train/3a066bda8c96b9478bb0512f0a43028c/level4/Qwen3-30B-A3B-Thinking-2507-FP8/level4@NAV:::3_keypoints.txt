- Proposes a method for semantic labeling (candidate segments → features → IoU regression) and object detection (spatial extent as a latent variable).  
- Uses weighted SVM for regression and a weighted transductive SVM variant ("latent e-SVM") for detection.  
- Empirical results match state-of-the-art for semantic labeling and achieve best reported detection scores on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; key related methods (weighted SVM, transductive/latent SVM variants) are not cited.  
- The imposed sum constraint on weights may be a minor novelty but literature review is incomplete.  
- Outperforming recent CNN-based methods with conventional ML is significant and of community interest.  
- Missing important references and baseline comparisons (notably regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression).  
- Rationale for regularization parameters (λ_W and λ_R) is insufficient and needs elaboration.  
- Manuscript focuses overly on minor algorithmic variations while under-describing critical feature engineering details.  
- Title and abstract are misleading (mischaracterize unit-interval regression vs binary labels) and should better convey core contributions.  
- Minor editorial issues/typos listed (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", define "AP", SVM→SVC at line 350, phrasing fixes).  
- Reviewer raised quality score by one assuming literature and contextual gaps are addressed; recommends substantial rewriting and likely acceptance at a vision conference after revision.
