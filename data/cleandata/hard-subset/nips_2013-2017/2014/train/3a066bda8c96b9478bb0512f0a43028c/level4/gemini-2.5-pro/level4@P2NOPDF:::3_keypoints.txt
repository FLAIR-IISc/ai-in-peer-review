- Proposes IoU regression on candidate segments for semantic labeling and a latent-variable weighted/transductive SVM approach for object detection with bounding-box supervision.  
- Limited algorithmic novelty; core methods (weighted SVM, transductive SVM variants) are established and several foundational works are not cited.  
- Sum constraint on weights may be a novel element but its originality is not convincingly established.  
- Empirically significant: matches state-of-the-art for semantic labeling and reports best-known results for VOC2007 object detection.  
- Main strength is empirical performance; feature engineering and engineering effort likely drive results.  
- Missing crucial references to prior work on weighted and transductive SVMs (e.g., R1–R4).  
- Missing an important baseline: regularized logistic regression for regression-on-[0,1] should be reported.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection, specifically how λW and λR were chosen.  
- Object detection framework involves substantial engineering complexity.  
- Title and abstract do not clearly state core contributions and contain a misleading claim about regression vs. binary labels.  
- Paper structure is misaligned for NIPS: over-focus on minor algorithm variants and under-reporting of critical feature-engineering details.  
- Several minor editorial/typo issues noted (lines 19, 94, 98, 350, etc.).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing missing citations, clarifying relation to prior work, adding stronger baselines, detailing hyperparameter selection, and rebalancing presentation toward feature engineering.
