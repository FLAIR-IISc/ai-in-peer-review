Summary: Introduces e-SVM that models continuous "positiveness" scores as Bernoulli label uncertainty, extends to latent weak supervision, and achieves strong results (60.0 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007, +1.5 AP over RCNN).

Originality: Proposes a novel expectation-loss that weights labels (not data) via a Bernoulli model, avoiding ad-hoc thresholding and differing fundamentally from weighted SVM/SVR approaches.

Missing related work: Fails to cite key weighted-SVM literature (Lapin et al. 2014; Suykens et al. 2002) and mischaracterizes the state of weighted SVM methods.

Latent extension: Applies a principled EM-style latent-variable extension for weak supervision rather than a transductive SVM variant.

Significance: Empirical gains are meaningful—matches state-of-the-art segmentation and yields a nontrivial detection improvement with minimal engineering, valuable for reducing annotation cost.

Technical quality: Optimization and experiments are sound and clearly presented (Algorithm 1 and thorough evaluations).

Evaluation gaps: Lacks a logistic-regression baseline for [0,1] targets and provides no sensitivity analysis or justification for hyperparameters λ_W and λ_R.

Clarity issues: Abstract/title overstate claims (binary labels can be thresholded), excessive focus on algorithmic variants, and under-explained feature engineering critical to results.

Minor corrections: Several typos and wording fixes needed (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box", define "AP", "SVM"→"SVC", improve phrasing).

Overall assessment: Novel and useful contribution with strong empirical results, but oversight in related work and overstated claims require correction.

Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions—explicitly contrast with weighted SVMs, add logistic baseline, and clarify abstract and novelty.
