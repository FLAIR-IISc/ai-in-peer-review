- Introduces e-SVM framework: regress IoU for segment proposals (semantic segmentation) and latent e-SVM for detection with bounding-box annotations, achieving competitive segmentation and state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.

- Originality limited: e-SVM is a variant of weighted SVM and latent e-SVM a weighted transductive SVM; paper omits citations to relevant prior work.

- Significance rests on strong empirical results, notably outperforming recent CNN-based detectors, which is of high interest.

- Quality concerns: good empirical performance but weak technical presentation, major omission of prior-work citations, and substantial engineering in the detection pipeline.

- Missing baseline and hyperparameter details: should compare to standard regressors (e.g., regularized logistic regression) and report selection of λ_w and λ_R.

- Clarity issues: misleading title/abstract, incorrect claim about continuous vs binary labels, over-focus on SVM variations and under-reporting of crucial feature engineering (spatial confidence map).

- Minor issues: typo in abstract ("continues"→"continuous"), unsupported claim about binarization degrading performance, brief description of spatial confidence map and pooling, unnumbered/missing table caption and referenced figures absent.

- Recommendation: accept only after major revision to properly situate the work within existing literature, improve clarity and technical details; may be better suited to a dedicated computer vision venue.
