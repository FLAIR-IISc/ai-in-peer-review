- Proposes SVM modification that incorporates per-example weights in [0,1], and extends to unobserved (latent) weights.  
- Method is clearly described and experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential gains over equal-weighting.  
- Core contribution is essentially a specific instantiation of well-established “example-dependent costs” (e.g., implemented in SVMlight), so novelty is limited.  
- Extension to latent weights is interesting but straightforward; resulting optimization is non-convex and lacks theoretical analysis.  
- Authors rely on strong regularization to keep per-example weights near initial values; they should clarify if this is necessary or if alternatives could avoid such constraints.  
- Minor: Claim that boundedness to [0,1] makes it “not a standard regression problem” is incorrect — logistic regression produces outputs in [0,1].  
- Minor: Abstract typo — “continues” should be “continuous.”
