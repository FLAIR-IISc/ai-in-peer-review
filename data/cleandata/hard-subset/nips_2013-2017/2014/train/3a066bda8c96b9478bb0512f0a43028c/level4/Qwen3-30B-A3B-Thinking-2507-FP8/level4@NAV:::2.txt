

The paper introduces a modified SVM learning algorithm that incorporates per-example weights (within [0,1]) into the loss function to handle weakly supervised data, further extending the approach to scenarios where weights are latent variables. The method is clearly presented, and experiments on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest potential benefits over uniform weighting. However, the proposed technique appears conceptually identical to established "example-dependent costs," a well-documented mechanism in machine learning (e.g., implemented in SVMlight), making the novelty unclear.  

The extension to latent weights is intriguing but straightforward, resulting in a non-convex optimization problem. The authors provide no theoretical analysis for this case and rely on strong regularization to constrain weight deviations from initial values—this assumption warrants further justification.  

Minor issues:  
- Line L050 incorrectly states that the problem "is not a standard regression problem since the positiveness belongs to [0,1]"; logistic regression is a standard bounded [0,1] model.  
- Abstract typo: "continues" should be "continuous."  

The core contribution—weighting examples in SVM loss—does not represent a novel approach, as example-dependent costs are already standard practice.
