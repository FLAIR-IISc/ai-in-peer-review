- Introduces e-SVM: expected-hinge max-margin training with soft “positiveness” scores ui∈[0,1], convex supervised objective solvable with standard SVMs and a latent, regularized u-update via alternating optimization.  
- Applied to segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation (CPMC + second-order pooling) and object detection (spatial confidence map + RCNN); proposes TAPC and NDCG for segment confidence and reports improvements over SVC/SVR and +1.2/+1.5 mAP vs RCNN.  
- Positions work relative to weighted/cost-sensitive/probabilistic-label SVMs and to latent/MIL-style weak supervision methods.  
- Technical soundness: expected hinge surrogate and alternating optimization are sensible, but notation typos, unclear regularizer expression, unmotivated choice g(u)=u, and missing convergence/sensitivity analysis.  
- Empirical support: experiments and new metrics are useful, detection gains are modest, but key ablations and direct WSVM/probabilistic-label baselines are missing.  
- Reproducibility: implementable with off-the-shelf SVMs, yet hyperparameters, u-update optimization details, training time, and sensitivity analyses are underreported.  
- Clarity: generally understandable but marred by grammatical/notation errors; TAPC, segmentation accuracy, and Algorithm 1’s u-subproblem need explicit definitions and a derivation linking to weighted hinge.  
- Originality: core idea of expected hinge/soft-label SVMs is not new; novelty lies in instantiating IoU as soft labels, the bbox-regularized latent extension, and RCNN spatial-map integration—useful but incremental.  
- Significance: relevant and practical for bounding-box weak supervision; simple and modular method with potential impact if comparisons and ablations are strengthened.  
- Strengths: convex supervised objective, interpretable weak-supervision regularizer, modular applicability, empirical improvements on segment confidence and detection, and new evaluation metrics.  
- Weaknesses: limited conceptual novelty, inadequate ablations to isolate effects, technical/notation clarity issues, minimal theoretical analysis, and modest reported gains without significance testing.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to WSVM/probabilistic-label and MIL/latent SVM baselines; provide ablations (SVR/SVC/WSVM with same spatial map, e-SVM without spatial map, different g(u), λR, ubb); fix notation and define metrics; report hyperparams, sensitivity, u-update implementation, training times; discuss kernel extension.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important practical problem with a simple, implementable method that improves a strong detection baseline.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest novelty over existing soft/weighted SVMs, missing key baselines/ablations, and presentation issues that obscure technical content.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on clarifying technical presentation and strengthening comparisons and ablations.
