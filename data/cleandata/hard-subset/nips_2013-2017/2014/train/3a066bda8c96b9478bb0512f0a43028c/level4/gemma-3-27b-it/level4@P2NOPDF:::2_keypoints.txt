- Paper proposes SVM modification that incorporates per-example weights (0–1) into the loss and extends to latent (unobserved) weights.
- Method is clearly written and well explained.
- Empirical results on semantic segmentation and object detection show potential benefits over uniform-weight SVM.
- Major novelty concern: example-weighting / example-dependent costs are established concepts (e.g., SVMlight), so novelty is unclear.
- Authors should better articulate advantages of their formulation compared to existing example-weighting methods.
- Latent per-example weights are an interesting direction but lead to a non-convex optimization problem.
- Paper lacks theoretical analysis on convergence or optimality for the latent-weight formulation.
- Strong regularization appears to keep learned weights close to initial values, raising concerns about flexibility and the ability to discover informative weights.
- Authors should justify the strong regularization, analyze sensitivity to its parameter, and discuss alternative regularization strategies.
- Minor issues: inaccurate claim that bounded outputs are not standard regression (logistic regression contradicts this) and a typo in the abstract (“continues” → “continuous”).
- Overall: well-described method with promising results, but insufficient differentiation from prior work and missing theoretical justification weaken the contribution.
