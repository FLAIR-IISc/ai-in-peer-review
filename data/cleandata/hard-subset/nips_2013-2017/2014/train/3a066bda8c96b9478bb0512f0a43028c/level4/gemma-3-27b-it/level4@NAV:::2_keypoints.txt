- Proposes SVM modification that assigns a per-example weight in [0,1] incorporated into the loss to handle weak supervision.
- Extends the approach to treat per-example weights as latent variables when weights are unobserved.
- Method is clearly presented and tested on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing potential gains over uniform-weight SVM.
- Novelty is unclear because the idea closely resembles existing example-dependent cost approaches (e.g., SVMlight).
- Latent-weight extension is straightforward but introduces non-convexity and lacks theoretical analysis.
- Strong regularization used suggests an implicit prior that learned weights stay near their initial values; authors should discuss this assumption.
- Minor errors: incorrect claim on L050 that the problem is “not a standard regression problem” (logistic regression is standard with outputs in [0,1]), and a typo in the abstract (“continues” → “continuous”).
- Overall contribution feels incremental given prior work on example-dependent costs in SVMs.
