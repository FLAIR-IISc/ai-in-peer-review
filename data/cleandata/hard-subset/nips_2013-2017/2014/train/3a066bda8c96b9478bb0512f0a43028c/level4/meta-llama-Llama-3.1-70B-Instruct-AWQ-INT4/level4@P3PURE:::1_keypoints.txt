Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous attributes of positive instances.

Replaces binary weighting in multiple instance learning with soft weighting; extends MIL to both observed and latent settings.

Applies e-SVM to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision.

Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing promising results.

Addresses an important weakly-supervised visual learning problem with clear motivation.

Experiments are interesting, well-executed, and the paper is well-presented.

Represents a valid contribution for NIPS despite being an extension of existing MIL frameworks.
