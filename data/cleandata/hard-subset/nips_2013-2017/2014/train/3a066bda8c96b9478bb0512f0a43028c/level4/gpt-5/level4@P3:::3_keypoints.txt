- Method: Proposes e-SVM — minimize expected hinge loss treating labels as Bernoulli(µi=g(ui)) (ui set to scores), yielding a weighted sum of positive/negative hinge losses solvable by standard linear SVMs; latent variant alternates w and u with a convex KL regularizer toward box IoU.
- Applications & results: Applied to proposal scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection; outperforms SVC/SVR on proposal scoring, is competitive for segmentation, and improves RCNN detection AP on VOC2007 by ≈1.5 points via simple feature concatenation.
- Relation to prior work: Closely related to weighted/soft-label SVMs and latent/transductive SVMs; differences from prior art are not clearly delineated and several relevant references are missing.
- Strength: Clear, practical formulation reducible to off-the-shelf SVMs and a straightforward, scalable latent extension with a reasonable convex regularizer.
- Strength: Spatial confidence map is a simple, effective way to inject segmentation cues into window-based detectors; TAPC and NDCG are sensible task-agnostic proposal-scoring metrics.
- Weakness: Limited algorithmic novelty relative to existing weighted/soft-label and latent SVM literature; novelty claims need clarification.
- Weakness: Missing important baselines—logistic regression/cross-entropy (and calibrated variants), beta regression/proper-scoring-rule regressions, and ranking SVM for NDCG.
- Weakness: Insufficient experimental detail and ablations — hyperparameter selection (λw, λR), initialization, convergence/stopping criteria, sensitivity to KL regularizer, and wall-clock comparisons are absent.
- Weakness: Detection engineering choices (binning m, pooling type, choice of segment proposals) are not ablated, leaving robustness of detection gains unclear.
- Clarity: Paper is readable but should emphasize “expected hinge loss with soft labels” in title/abstract, define notation consistently, move variants to appendix, expand feature-integration description, and fix typos.
- Requested analyses: Add logistic regression (L2) and calibrated SVC baselines with calibration metrics (ECE/reliability), include RankSVM, report per-class AP and statistical/significance analyses, and ablate m/pooling/proposal types and alternation counts.
- References: Add and discuss weighted/soft-label SVM literature (e.g., Suykens 2002, Yang 2005, Lapin 2014), transductive/latent SVMs (Vapnik, Felzenszwalb), and proper probabilistic baselines.
- Originality assessment: Core idea (expected hinge with soft labels) is known; contribution is a clean instantiation for segmentation scoring plus a KL IoU regularizer — incremental unless novelty relative to prior work is clarified.
- Significance: Empirically useful and transferable to other domains, but impact depends on adding stronger baselines, ablations, and clearer positioning.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept (leaning accept) conditional on addressing related work, adding probabilistic/ranking baselines, and providing detailed ablations and experimental clarity.
