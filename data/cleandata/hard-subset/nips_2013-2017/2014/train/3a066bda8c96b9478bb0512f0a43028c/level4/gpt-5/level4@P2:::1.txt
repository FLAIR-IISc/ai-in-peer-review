The paper proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that models “positiveness” as a continuous value in [0,1] and optimizes the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label model. The method handles both settings where positiveness is observed (e.g., IoU with pixel-level masks) and where it is unobserved, via a latent extension regularized toward bounding-box IoU. The approach is used to train segment classifiers and is evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing competitive to superior performance compared to SVC/SVR, and yielding gains when combined with RCNN.

The problem of learning under weak supervision is important in computer vision, and the paper’s motivation, formulation, and experimental setup are clear and well presented. While the technical novelty is incremental—conceptually related to replacing binary labels with soft weights, akin to ideas in MIL/weighted SVMs—the specific expectation-loss formulation and its practical latent variant are clean, implementable with standard SVM solvers, and applicable in both fully and weakly supervised regimes. The experiments are thorough and convincingly executed, with meaningful improvements for both semantic segmentation and object detection.

Overall: a well-motivated, clearly written paper addressing a relevant weakly supervised learning setting with solid empirical results. Despite limited conceptual novelty, the formulation’s practicality and demonstrated impact make it a reasonable contribution for NIPS.
