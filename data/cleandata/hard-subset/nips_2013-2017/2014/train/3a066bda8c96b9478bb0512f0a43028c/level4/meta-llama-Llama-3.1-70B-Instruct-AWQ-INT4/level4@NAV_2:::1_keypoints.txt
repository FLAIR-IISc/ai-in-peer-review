- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous "positiveness" of instances under weak supervision.  
- Models each segment label as an unobserved Bernoulli variable; loss is the expectation, effectively weighting samples by positiveness.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification using TAPC and NDCG; e-SVM consistently outperforms SVC and SVR.  
- Applied to semantic segmentation (PASCAL VOC 2011) with comparable state-of-the-art results.  
- Combined with RCNN for object detection, yielding non-trivial detection performance improvements.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and experiments are well-executed with promising results.  
- Technical contribution is primarily an extension of standard multiple instance learning, which may limit perceived novelty.  
- Related work discussion, especially on multiple instance learning, is insufficiently detailed.  
- TAPC and NDCG are not widely used and may be unintuitive to some readers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important weak-supervision problem, presents an effective method, and shows strong experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, weak related-work coverage, and unconventional evaluation metrics.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance if authors address related-work discussion and clarify/evaluate chosen metrics.
