- Introduces e-SVM: an SVM variant using continuous "positiveness" labels and an expectation loss for weak supervision.
- Extends e-SVM to latent (unobserved positiveness) cases via an iterative optimization procedure.
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing improvements over SVC and SVR.

- Builds on recent weakly supervised work, filling the gap between fully supervised and MIL by handling quantified positiveness (e.g., IoU).
- Relates to prior NIPS papers but targets a distinct setting with bounded positiveness values.

Strengths:
- Quality: Technically sound formulation with clear theoretical motivation and thorough experiments including ablations.
- Clarity: Well-structured, clearly written, with a logical latent-variable extension and justified use of IoU as positiveness.
- Originality: Distinguishes weighting labels (e-SVM) from standard weighted SVMs and applies this to segment proposal classification.
- Significance: Addresses annotation cost reduction and achieves strong detection results (PASCAL VOC 2007) using bounding boxes.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical Depth: Lacks rigorous analysis or convergence guarantees for the non-convex latent e-SVM; regularization effects are not fully discussed.
- Comparison to Weighted SVMs: Insufficient differentiation and empirical/algorithmic comparison to existing weighted SVM solvers.
- Experimental Details: Sparse details on the spatial confidence map implementation, parameter choices (e.g., m=5), sensitivity, and broader detector comparisons beyond RCNN.

Pros for acceptance:
- Tackles a meaningful, practical computer vision problem with clear utility in reducing annotation effort.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains over standard SVM baselines across tasks.
- Well-motivated, clearly presented work with thorough experimental validation.
- Clarifies a meaningful conceptual difference from prior weighted SVM approaches.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited theoretical contribution for the latent extension and non-convex optimization.
- Need for stronger elaboration and comparison with existing weighted SVM methods.
- Some experimental and implementation details are under-specified.

Overall assessment:
- Solid technical contribution with practical significance; experimental validation outweighs theoretical/clarity shortcomings.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing theoretical analysis, comparisons to weighted SVMs, and additional experimental details.

Suggested revisions for authors:
- Expand theoretical discussion of the latent e-SVM, addressing non-convexity, convergence, and regularization impact.
- Provide clearer comparisons (theoretical and empirical) with existing weighted SVM frameworks and solvers.
- Add implementation details and sensitivity analyses for the spatial confidence map and other key parameters.
