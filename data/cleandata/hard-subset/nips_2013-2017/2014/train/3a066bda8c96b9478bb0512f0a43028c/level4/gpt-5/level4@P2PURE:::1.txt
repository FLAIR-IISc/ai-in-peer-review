The authors propose an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that accommodates continuous attributes of positive instances and use it to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision. The methodology is validated on both semantic segmentation and object detection, with promising results. The paper addresses an important problem in weakly supervised visual learning; the motivation is sound, the presentation is clear, and the experiments are interesting and well executed. Technically, the approach extends the standard multiple-instance learning framework by replacing a binary indicator with a soft weighting of positives; it applies in both observed and latent settings and represents a valid contribution to NIPS. Overall, the expectation-loss SVM is well motivated, clearly presented, and delivers relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection.
