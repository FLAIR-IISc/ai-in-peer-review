- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous "positiveness" attributes instead of binary labels.  
- Applies e-SVM to visual segmentation and object detection under weak supervision.  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks with promising empirical results.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Method is an extension of multiple instance learning by replacing binary labels with soft weighting.  
- Algorithm applies in both observed and latent settings.  
- Presentation is clear, well-motivated, with well-defined notation and helpful figures.  
- Experiments are interesting, well-executed, and support the claims.  
- Strength: novel learning algorithm for continuous positive-instance attributes.  
- Strength: validated across two major vision tasks with strong results.  
- Weakness: technical novelty may be seen as incremental relative to standard MIL.  
- Weakness: lacks a thorough analysis of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: could discuss potential applications in other fields more extensively.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and clearly presented.  
- Originality assessment: novel approach but fundamentally an MIL extension.  
- Significance assessment: addresses an important weakly-supervised learning problem with impactful results.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for NIPS.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong validation, clear presentation, solid experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived limited novelty, missing complexity analysis, limited discussion of broader applications.
