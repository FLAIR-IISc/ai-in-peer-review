- Method: Proposes semantic labeling via candidate segments, feature extraction, and regression on IoU; object detection treats precise extent as latent and uses weighted SVM/transductive SVMs.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art semantic labeling and best-known VOC 2007 object detection results.  
- Novelty concerns: Algorithmic novelty is limited; proposed latent e-SVM is essentially a weighted transductive SVM.  
- Missing citations: Fails to cite pertinent prior work on weighted SVMs and transductive SVMs (several concrete references noted).  
- Potential novelty: Sum constraint on weights may be novel but not fully justified or researched.  
- Significance: Beating recent CNNs with a conventional approach is notable and of strong interest.  
- Baseline/validation gaps: Should compare to regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression to justify weighted SVM choice.  
- Hyperparameter detail lacking: More information needed on selection of regularization coefficients λ_W and λ_R.  
- Engineering vs. contribution: Framework involves substantial engineering but stays within common practice; main strength is empirical performance.  
- Clarity issues: Title/abstract do not clearly summarize contributions and abstract contains misleading statement about regression vs binary labels.  
- Emphasis imbalance: Paper spends excessive space on minor algorithmic variations and too little on essential feature engineering details.  
- Minor errors: Several typos and notation/abbreviation issues (examples and line numbers provided).  
- Recommendation: Paper merits publication pending revisions to address prior-work connections, clarity, and methodological justification.
