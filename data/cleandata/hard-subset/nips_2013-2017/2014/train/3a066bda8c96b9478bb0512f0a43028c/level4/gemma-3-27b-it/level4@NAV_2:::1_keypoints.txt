- Proposal: Introduces an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous “positiveness” indicators for weakly supervised visual learning.
- Application: Applies e-SVM (with a latent-variable extension) to semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Motivation: Well-motivated extension addressing limitations of using SVC/SVR for continuous affiliation to the positive class.
- Technical quality: Principled, technically sound extension of the standard SVM framework.
- Presentation: Clear, well-structured exposition of the algorithm and latent-variable formulation.
- Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation on standard datasets (PASCAL VOC) with relevant baselines.
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Importance: Tackles an important problem—scaling visual learning with weak labels.
- Strength: Strong empirical results and thorough experimental validation.
- Strength: Clear motivation and solid technical foundation.
- Weakness: Novelty is somewhat incremental, building on SVM and multiple-instance ideas.
- Weakness: Lacks explicit comparisons to recent weakly-supervised approaches (e.g., consistency regularization).
- Weakness: Performance sensitive to the regularization parameter for the latent variable u; lacks detailed sensitivity analysis or selection guidance.
- Weakness: Limited ablation study—insufficient analysis of individual component contributions (regularizer, spatial confidence features).
- Argument for acceptance: Technically sound, well-motivated, strong results, and clear writing.
- Argument against acceptance: Incremental novelty and insufficient exploration of parameter sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: Solid, practical contribution with strong empirical support; recommended for acceptance.
