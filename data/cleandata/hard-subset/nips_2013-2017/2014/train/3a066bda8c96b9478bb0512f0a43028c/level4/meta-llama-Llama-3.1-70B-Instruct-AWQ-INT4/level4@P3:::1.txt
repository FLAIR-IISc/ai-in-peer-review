The authors propose an expectation loss Support Vector Machine (e-SVM) that can handle continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem. This approach is particularly relevant for visual learning under weak supervision, where only bounding-box annotations are available. The authors apply their model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, demonstrating its effectiveness in both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.

The paper addresses an important problem in the field of computer vision, where manually labeled datasets are often expensive and impractical to obtain. The authors' solution is well-motivated, and their presentation is clear and concise. The experiments are interesting and well-executed, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

The technical content of the paper is an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework, where a binary weighting of positives is replaced with a soft weighting. This approach is applied in both the observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution to the field.

The paper's strengths include its good motivation, clear presentation, and relevant results for semantic segmentation and object detection. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in various scenarios, including the use of different types of annotations and the combination with other object detection systems.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the connection to previous work on multiple instance learning and weighted SVMs could be more explicitly discussed. Additionally, the authors could provide more insight into the choice of the regularization term and its impact on the performance of the model.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their proposed method. The results are promising, and the approach has the potential to be applied to a wide range of visual learning tasks under weak supervision.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper addresses an important problem in the field of computer vision.
