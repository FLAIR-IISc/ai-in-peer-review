- Proposes modifying SVMs to assign a per-example weight in [0,1] and incorporate it into the loss.
- Extends the approach to treat per-example weights as latent (unobserved) variables.
- Method is well-described and experiments show potential benefits for semantic segmentation and object detection.
- Unclear distinction from existing "example-dependent costs" (e.g., SVMlight) widely used in ML.
- Latent-weight formulation is straightforward but yields a non-convex problem with no theoretical analysis provided.
- Strong regularization is used, suggesting per-example weights stay close to initial values; authors should comment on this.
- Incorrect claim that bounding to [0,1] makes it non-standard regression — logistic regression already provides such bounded outputs.
- Minor typo in the abstract: "continues" should be "continuous."
- Overall novelty and difference from existing example-dependent cost methods remain unclear.
