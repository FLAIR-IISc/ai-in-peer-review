This paper introduces a modification to the SVM learning algorithm for weakly supervised data. The method assigns a continuous weight (between 0 and 1) to each training example and incorporates this weight into the SVM loss function. This framework is also extended to handle cases where these per-example weights are unobserved and treated as latent variables.

The method is clearly described, and the experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection suggest its potential benefits over a standard SVM that weights all examples equally. However, the core contribution appears to be very similar to the well-established concept of "example-dependent costs," which is widely known and used in the machine learning community. For instance, popular implementations like SVMlight have long supported this functionality. The authors should clarify the novelty of their approach in light of this existing work.

While the extension to latent per-example weights is an interesting direction, the formulation appears to be a straightforward application of alternating optimization. The resulting objective function is non-convex, yet the paper provides no theoretical analysis of the optimization procedure. Furthermore, the use of a strong regularization term on the weights suggests that they may not deviate significantly from their initial values. The authors are requested to comment on the impact of this regularization and the convergence properties of their proposed algorithm.

Minor Comments:
*   The claim on L50 that the problem is "not a standard regression problem" because the output is bounded in [0, 1] is inaccurate. Standard models like logistic regression produce outputs within this exact interval.
*   There is a minor typo in the abstract: "continues" should be "continuous".
