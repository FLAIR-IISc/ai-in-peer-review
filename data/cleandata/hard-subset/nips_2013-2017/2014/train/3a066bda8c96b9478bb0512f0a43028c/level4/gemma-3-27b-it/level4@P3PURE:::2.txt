The paper proposes a modification to the Support Vector Machine (SVM) learning algorithm to handle weakly supervised data. Specifically, a per-example weight – a value between 0 and 1 – is assigned to each training example, and the SVM loss function is modified to incorporate these weights. The method is further extended to the case where these per-example weights are not directly observed, becoming latent variables.

The method is well described, and the experimental results suggest that incorporating these weights can offer improvements over treating all examples equally in semantic segmentation and object detection tasks. However, the core idea appears closely related to existing techniques employing example-dependent costs, which are well-known and widely used in the machine learning community. For instance, SVMlight provides a mechanism for specifying such costs.

The extension to latent per-example weights is interesting, though straightforward. This formulation introduces non-convexity, and the paper lacks theoretical analysis. The use of a strong regularization term, which constrains the per-example weights to remain close to their initial values, suggests an attempt to mitigate challenges arising from this non-convexity. The authors should comment on this regularization and its impact on the learned weights.

Minor comments: On line 50, the claim that this is “not a standard regression problem” due to the bounded interval [0, 1] is inaccurate; logistic regression is a standard regression method with outputs bounded within [0, 1]. Additionally, a spelling error exists in the abstract: “continues” should be “continuous”.

In summary, the paper proposes a modified SVM learning algorithm that incorporates per-example weights into the loss function. However, the fundamental concept of example-dependent costs is already established in the machine learning literature.
