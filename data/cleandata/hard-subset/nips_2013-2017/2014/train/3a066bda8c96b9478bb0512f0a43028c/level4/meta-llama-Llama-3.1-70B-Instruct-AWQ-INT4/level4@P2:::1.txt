This paper proposes a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that is capable of handling continuous attributes of positive instances in a learning problem. The authors apply this model to learn visual segmentation classifiers under weak supervision, where only the "positiveness" of each training sample is available, rather than a binary label. The methodology is validated for both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, yielding promising results.

The paper addresses an important problem in visual learning under weak supervision. The presentation is clear and well-motivated, and the experiments are interesting and well-executed. The technical content is an extension of the standard multiple instance learning framework, where a binary weighting of positives is replaced with a soft weighting. This extension applies to both the observed and latent settings, representing a valid contribution for NIPS.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the related work, highlighting the differences between their approach and existing methods such as Support Vector Classification (SVC) and Support Vector Regression (SVR). They also discuss the connection to instance weighted SVMs in the literature.

The proposed e-SVM model is well-formulated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the loss function and the optimization algorithm. The extension to the latent setting is also well-motivated and executed.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the e-SVM model in both semantic segmentation and object detection tasks. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of visual learning under weak supervision. The proposed e-SVM model is well-formulated, and the experimental results are promising. The paper is well-written, and the presentation is clear and well-motivated.
