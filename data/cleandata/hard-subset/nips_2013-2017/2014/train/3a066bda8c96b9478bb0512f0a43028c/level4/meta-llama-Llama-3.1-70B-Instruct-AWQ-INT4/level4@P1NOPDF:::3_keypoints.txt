- Proposed method: regress IoU between candidate segments and pixel-accurate ground truth for semantic labeling, and treat spatial extent as a latent variable for detection using weighted SVM and weighted transductive SVM.  
- Empirical result: Comparable state-of-the-art for semantic labeling and superior object detection results on VOC 2007.  
- Originality concern: Limited algorithmic novelty; weighted SVM is established and several relevant works are not cited.  
- Possible novelty: Sum constraint on weights may be new but requires literature verification.  
- Method equivalence: The proposed "latent e-SVM" is essentially a weighted version of transductive SVM; prior work not cited.  
- Significance: Notable that a conventional learning approach outperforms recent CNN results, of high community interest.  
- Missing baselines/details: Authors should report (regularized) logistic regression baseline for unit-interval regression and justify the sum-constrained weighted SVM.  
- Hyperparameter reporting: More details needed on selection of regularization coefficients λW and λR.  
- Writing/clarity issues: Title and abstract misrepresent core contributions; abstract's claim about regression targets vs binary labels is misleading; too much space on minor algorithm variants and too little on feature engineering.  
- Minor corrections: Typos and wording fixes (e.g., "continues"→"continuous", "boxes"→"box"), introduce "AP", use "SVC" not "SVM", and reword threshold phrasing.  
- Recommendation: Acceptable for publication pending stronger discussion of related work and clearer exposition/revision.
