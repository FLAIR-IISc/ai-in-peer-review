- Summary: Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for segmentation (predict IoU per segment) and a latent e-SVM for detection with bounding-box supervision; achieves competitive segmentation and state-of-the-art PASCAL VOC 2007 detection with RCNN.

- Originality concern: Algorithmic novelty is limited and several relevant prior works on weighted and transductive SVMs are not cited.

- Literature gap: Must cite and position relative to weighted SVM literature (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Lapin et al.) and transductive/latent SVMs (Vapnik).

- Claimed novelty caveat: The sum constraint on weights might be novel but needs clearer justification and literature search.

- Weighting distinction: Authors should clarify the difference between weighting labels versus weighting examples and how their model differs.

- Significance: Strong empirical gains, especially improving CNN-based detectors with a simple addition; weak-supervision capability is practically valuable.

- Missing baselines: Include natural baselines such as (regularized) logistic regression for targets in [0,1] and discuss calibration.

- Hyperparameters: Provide details on hyperparameter selection (how λW and λR chosen, CV protocol, search ranges, sensitivity).

- Ablations needed: Report effects of KL regularizer, initialization of u_bb, and convergence behavior to strengthen methodological claims.

- Clarity and presentation: Title/abstract should better emphasize (i) learning continuous positiveness, (ii) latent weak-supervision extension, and (iii) detector integration and gains.

- Misleading wording: Rephrase the abstract’s claim that continuous labels are less informative than binary labels (binary are derivable from continuous, not vice versa).

- Exposition balance: Reduce time on well-known formulation variants and expand on feature-engineering, integration details, ablations, and error analysis.

- Make relationships explicit: Explicitly describe connections to instance-weighted and latent/transductive SVMs in main text with proper citations.

- Minor edits: Fix typos and wording (e.g., “continues”→“continuous”; “boxes”→“box”; introduce “AP” before use; SVM→SVC; phrasing for threshold values).

- Recommendation: Conditional accept after revision — strong empirical results but requires comprehensive relation to prior work and presentation improvements.
