- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that minimizes expected hinge loss using per-example soft labels µi = g(ui), yielding a cost-sensitive hinge with weights g(ui) and 1−g(ui).
- Introduces a latent version where ui is unobserved and regularized toward an initial ubb with a convex KL penalty, optimized by alternating convex updates for w and u.
- Evaluated on proposal scoring, semantic segmentation (pixel/box supervision), and object detection, reporting +1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 when augmenting R-CNN with a spatial confidence map feature.
- Related to existing work: similar to fuzzy/probabilistic/soft-label SVMs, cost-sensitive and instance-weighted SVMs, and weakly supervised/latent SVM approaches; paper omits some close prior citations.
- Strength: simple, intuitive objective that’s easy to implement and compatible with linear solvers after transformation.
- Strength: practical weakly supervised extension with convex regularizer that avoids degenerate 0/1 solutions and allows alternating convex optimization.
- Strength: empirical gains over SVC and small gains over SVR on proposal scoring metrics (TAPC, NDCG) and a non-trivial detection improvement when adding segmentation cues.
- Strength: spatial confidence map effectively addresses partial overlap between segments and detection windows and is a simple way to inject segmentation information.
- Weakness: limited originality — expected hinge with soft labels and cost-sensitive variants are well-known and closely related literature is insufficiently cited or positioned.
- Weakness: minimal theory — lacks analysis of calibration, robustness to noisy µi, convergence guarantees beyond biconvexity, and details of the alternating scheme.
- Weakness: clarity and notation problems (typos, undefined constants), and missing explanation of how to map to standard SVM solvers for two label-dependent weights per example.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 omits stopping criteria and optimization details for the u-update; hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analyses are largely absent.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation issues — terse protocol descriptions, missing variances/statistical tests, inconsistent claims versus SVR, lack of per-class IoU and ablations, and missing comparisons to other segmentation-aware detectors.
- Weakness: KL regularizer choice is ad hoc and alternatives (squared loss, beta prior) and justification for using box IoU as prior are not explored.
- Quality: technically sound at a high level (blockwise convexity) but missing important implementation details (solver mapping, u-update, hyperparameter selection, convergence behavior).
- Clarity: readable but needs fixes for typos, complete notation, explicit derivation for solver implementation (e.g., sample duplication and weights), and clear algorithms for u-update and stopping.
- Originality: incremental — method is a practical twist on known ideas and the spatial confidence map is an engineering contribution rather than a deep conceptual advance.
- Significance: moderate — useful for practitioners in weak supervision and a non-trivial detection mAP gain, but broader impact requires stronger evidence and comparisons.
- Suggestions: add missing related work citations, show explicit solver implementation (e.g., example duplication/weights), detail the u-update and convergence, explore g(u) variants, and strengthen experiments (per-class metrics, ablations, runtime).
- Questions for authors: how is expected hinge implemented with LibLinear; what optimizer and convergence behavior for argmin_u; how are λw and λR chosen; were alternative regularizers/g mappings tried; and what is the feature dimensionality and added compute for the confidence map?
- Pros: simple, practical formulation; empirical improvements; broadly applicable idea.
- Cons: limited novelty, incomplete optimization and clarity details, and weaker empirical rigor/ablations.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising and practical but incremental with insufficient positioning, theoretical detail, and experimental rigor for acceptance.
