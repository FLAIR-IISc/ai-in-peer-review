Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a linear large-margin method for training with “soft” labels in [0,1], where the target for each instance is a “positiveness” value (e.g., IoU of a segment with ground-truth). The central idea is to model the label Yi ∈ {−1,+1} as Bernoulli with mean µi = g(ui), and minimize the expected hinge loss E[max(0,1−Yi w^T x_i)], which simplifies to u_i l_i^+ + (1−u_i) l_i^−, a convex combination of positive and negative hinge losses. The authors set g to identity (µ=u). For weak supervision with only bounding boxes, u is treated as latent and regularized to be close to the segment–box IoU u_bb via a convex cross-entropy (KL) penalty; optimization alternates between convex steps in w (standard SVM) and u (per-sample convex problems).

Applications are presented to: (i) segment confidence prediction (compared to SVC and SVR) under two proposed evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG), (ii) semantic segmentation (using CPMC segments and second-order pooling), and (iii) object detection by introducing a spatial confidence map feature derived from segment scores, concatenated with RCNN features, yielding reported +1.2 to +1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.

This setting sits between fully supervised segmentation (pixel labels) and MIL with only image-level labels. There is relevant prior work on MIL for weakly supervised segmentation, and on weighted/instance-weighted SVMs. However, closely related lines not fully covered include soft/fuzzy-label SVMs (membership values in [0,1] that affect the loss), SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels, and latent SVM formulations in detection/segmentation. The alternating optimization and KL regularization resemble MAP estimation with a prior on latent “soft labels.”

Strengths
- Conceptually simple and technically sound base model: the expected hinge-loss with soft labels is convex in w, easy to implement with standard SVM solvers (e.g., via sample reweighting or sample duplication).
- Practical extension to weak supervision via a convex regularizer on latent u, with a clear effect (prevents degenerate 0/1 solutions).
- Empirical improvements over SVC and SVR for segment confidence prediction under the proposed metrics; meaningful detection gains when integrating the spatial confidence map features into RCNN (+1.2 to +1.5 mAP).
- The proposed spatial confidence map feature addresses partial overlap between segments and detection windows in a simple, general way.

Weaknesses
- Originality is limited: minimizing expected hinge loss under probabilistic/soft labels closely matches prior “soft-label” or “uncertain label” SVM formulations; the paper does not adequately position the contribution against that body of work (e.g., fuzzy SVM, SVMs with probabilistic labels/label uncertainty). The main novelty appears to be the particular latent u regularization tied to bounding-box IoU and the specific computer vision integration, which are incremental.
- Clarity and presentation issues: several notation/typographical problems (e.g., the regularization term in Eq. 1 appears malformed; standard is λ||w||^2/2, but the text shows “λw · 1 w^T w”). Details for solving the u-update (closed form vs. numeric, constraints, initialization, convergence criteria) are omitted. The definition and motivation of g(·) are minimal (always identity); no analysis of alternatives or calibration.
- Limited analysis: no theoretical properties (e.g., classification calibration of expected hinge under soft labels, generalization bounds, sensitivity to λ_R). No ablation on the strength of the KL regularizer, the choice of g, or the number/type of segment proposals. Convergence behavior of the alternating scheme is not discussed beyond convexity of subproblems.
- Comparisons could be stronger: missing baselines include logistic regression (cross-entropy) with soft labels, calibrated regression restricted to [0,1], probabilistic large-margin methods, and MIL/latent SVM variants tailored to weakly supervised segmentation. For segmentation, end-task gains are only “comparable” to SVR despite better direct metrics.
- Evaluation metrics: TAPC/NDCG are reasonable, but these are nonstandard for segment proposal scoring; more discussion of their relationship to end-task performance would help. Statistical significance of detection gains is not reported.
- References and formatting are inconsistent, with missing years/authors in multiple entries.

Quality
- The core formulation (fixed u) is technically sound, convex, and easy to implement. The latent extension is a reasonable biconvex optimization with a sensible regularizer; however, important implementation details are missing. Experiments suggest the approach is functional and yields modest gains in detection.

Clarity
- The paper is understandable at a high level but suffers from notation/typos and missing algorithmic details (regularization term form, u-update, hyperparameters λ, λ_R, convergence). Figures/tables are summarized but details on data splits and training protocol are sparse.

Originality
- Moderate to low. The expected hinge-loss with probabilistic labels and “soft/fuzzy” SVM ideas are known; the paper does not differentiate sufficiently from these. The latent KL-regularized extension and the spatial confidence map feature are incremental.

Significance
- Moderate practical significance. The +1.5 mAP on VOC 2007 with RCNN is nontrivial but modest, and the segmentation results are comparable to SVR. The generality claim is plausible, but demonstrated impact is limited to one detector and one segmentation pipeline.

Constructive suggestions
- Clarify and correct the objective notation (regularization term) and provide full details for the u-update (derivation, constraints u∈(0,1), solver, complexity). Report hyperparameters, convergence criteria, and runtime.
- Position against and cite prior soft/fuzzy/uncertain-label SVM work; explain differences formally. Compare empirically to logistic regression with soft labels and to an SVM trained on duplicated samples with weights u and 1−u (the implied implementation).
- Analyze sensitivity to λ_R and to the choice of g(·); consider learning g or calibrating u. Provide ablations on segment proposal method and the parameters of the spatial confidence map (m, pooling).
- Report statistical significance and per-class gains; clarify dataset splits for VOC 2007 (train/val/test). Discuss generalization to VOC 2012/other datasets.
- For weak supervision, compare to MIL/latent-SVM baselines applied to segments and boxes.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, sound formulation; easy to adopt.
- Useful in weakly supervised scenarios; practical detection gains with RCNN.
- Clear empirical improvements over SVC/SVR on proposed segment-scoring metrics.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft/uncertain-label SVM literature.
- Presentation/detail gaps and incomplete related work.
- Modest overall end-task gains; missing baselines and analysis.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The method is sound and practically useful, but the novelty beyond known soft-label SVM formulations is insufficiently established, and the paper needs clearer exposition, stronger comparisons, and deeper analysis to meet NeurIPS standards.
