- Proposes e-SVM: minimize expected hinge loss under probabilistic labels g(u) with L2 penalty; latent u optimized via alternating minimization with KL regularizer to an IoU-based initialization ubb.  
- Strength: Clear, convex-in-w formulation for w and straightforward optimization; natural latent extension.  
- Strength: Practical KL/cross-entropy regularizer that keeps latent u near bounding-box-derived initialization.  
- Strength: Simple spatial confidence-map feature for detection that gives consistent (≈+1.2–1.5 mAP) gains over RCNN.  
- Strength: Introduces new segment-ranking metrics (TAPC, NDCG) aligned with the training objective.  
- Weakness: Novelty is limited—expected-hinge with probabilistic labels is closely related to fuzzy/probabilistic/uncertain-label SVM literature and can be reduced to instance-weighted SVM on duplicated examples.  
- Weakness: Latent alternating scheme and KL prior are standard; similar ideas exist in latent structural SVMs and posterior regularization.  
- Method clarity issue: Link g(u)=u is used without justification, calibration, or exploration of alternatives.  
- Method clarity issue: Claim that any standard SVM solver can be used lacks a concrete reduction or practical details (per-instance weights, duplication).  
- Method clarity issue: Update for latent u is not derived; closed-form, numerical method, complexity, and convergence criteria unspecified.  
- Method clarity issue: Regularization strength λR sensitivity and ablation (optimizing u vs fixing ubb) are not reported.  
- Empirical issue: Segment-ranking gains over SVR are small, variance/error bars are missing, and segmentation results are only “comparable,” contradicting strong claims.  
- Empirical issue: Detection improvements are modest, may depend on feature engineering, and RCNN comparisons are not fully controlled (bounding-box regression altered).  
- Empirical issue: Missing comparisons to other natural baselines (logistic regression with soft labels, calibrated SVMs, ordinal regression, learning-to-rank).  
- Empirical issue: Generality claims (works with any proposals/features/classifiers; beyond vision) are not experimentally validated.  
- Writing/presentation issue: Notational errors and typos (e.g., incorrect regularizer expression) and incomplete/malformed references.  
- Writing/presentation issue: Algorithm 1 is high-level and lacks practical reproduction details (initialization with multiple boxes, multi-instance handling, stopping criteria, runtime).  
- Quality: Core objective is sound and latent variant reasonable, but theoretical analysis is limited (no convergence guarantees beyond monotonicity, no generalization discussion).  
- Clarity: Main idea understandable but marred by notation errors, incomplete algorithmic detail, and weak related-work connections.  
- Originality: Limited—method largely incremental over known soft-label SVM variants; spatial confidence-map feature is simple and incremental.  
- Significance: Addresses an important setting and yields useful practical gains, but incremental contribution and modest empirical improvements limit impact.  
- Questions for authors: Compare and cite fuzzy/probabilistic SVMs and show explicit reduction to instance-weighted SVM on duplicated examples.  
- Questions for authors: Report sensitivity to g(u) and λR; do you learn or calibrate g and u?  
- Questions for authors: Provide exact procedure, complexity, and convergence criteria for optimizing u; closed-form or numerical details.  
- Questions for authors: Ablation: does optimizing u outperform fixing u=ubb?  
- Questions for authors: Provide controlled RCNN comparisons without modifying bbox regression, per-class AP tables, and statistical significance tests.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject—sensible and practical idea but incremental, under-positioned in related work, with insufficient baselines, ablations, and clarity.
