- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous/soft labels (e.g., IoU) by modeling labels as Bernoulli probabilities and extending SVMs to avoid thresholding.
- Proposes a latent-variable extension for weak supervision to handle unlabeled/partially labeled instances.
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection (PASCAL VOC), showing consistent improvements over SVC and SVR baselines.
- Claims practical significance: simple to implement with standard SVM solvers and integrable into object detection pipelines.
- Clear problem motivation and well-organized presentation with sufficient methodological and experimental detail.
- Novel technical contribution addressing a gap in handling bounded continuous labels directly within the SVM framework.
- Limited theoretical analysis: no formal guarantees on convergence, optimality, or generalization bounds.
- Incomplete comparison to related weakly supervised methods (e.g., several MIL/partial-annotation works are not thoroughly compared).
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like the regularizer R(u) and choice of g(ui)=ui.
- Performance gains are modest (≈1.5% AP on detection) and statistical significance is not established; segmentation results not compared to state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods.
- Insufficient implementation/reproducibility details (e.g., optimization iterations, hyperparameter selection, spatial confidence map specifics).
- For acceptance: well-motivated, novel, empirically validated, simple to use, and clearly presented.
- Against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, incomplete literature comparison, modest empirical gains, and reproducibility concerns.
- Overall: useful and well-executed practical contribution but lacks strong theory and transformative performance for NIPS acceptance.
- Recommendation: reject, pending stronger theoretical analysis, more comprehensive comparisons, and ablation/reproducibility improvements.
