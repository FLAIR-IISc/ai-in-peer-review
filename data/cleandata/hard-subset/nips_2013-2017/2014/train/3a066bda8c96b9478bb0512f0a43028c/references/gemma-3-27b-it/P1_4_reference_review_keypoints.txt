- Introduces e-SVM, an SVM variant that incorporates continuous “positiveness” as an expected value in the loss for weakly supervised data.  
- Demonstrates e-SVM on semantic segmentation and object detection using CPMC segments and R-CNN, showing improved performance over SVC/SVR.  
- Clearly positions work relative to prior literature, distinguishing it from Multiple Instance Learning and relating to weighted SVMs.  
- Novel, principled formulation for handling continuous labels within an SVM framework.  
- Versatile: applicable in both strongly and weakly supervised settings; latent e-SVM handles noisy annotations like bounding boxes.  
- Strong empirical results across tasks, including a reported 1.5% average AP gain on object detection with R-CNN.  
- Well-written presentation with clear mathematical formulation and detailed experimental setup.  
- Practical implementation: compatible with standard SVM solvers (e.g., LibLinear) and introduces an effective spatial confidence map for detection integration.  
- Regularization analysis is limited; the KL-divergence regularizer justification appears ad-hoc and needs deeper study.  
- Performance depends on quality of segment proposals; robustness to poor or alternative proposal methods is not explored.  
- Comparisons are limited to SVC/SVR and R-CNN; should compare to more recent detectors (e.g., Faster R-CNN, YOLO).  
- Ablation study is limited; lacks analysis of contributions from components like spatial confidence maps and feature choices.  
- Figure 3 (TAPC) is low resolution and hard to interpret.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution to weakly supervised learning with recommendation to accept under minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: justify regularization choice, analyze segment proposal robustness, compare to newer detectors, improve Figure 3 resolution, and add a brief ablation study.
