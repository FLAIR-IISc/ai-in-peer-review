- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous [0,1] labels by treating labels as Bernoulli probabilities and using an expectation-based loss.  
- Extends e-SVM to a latent setting for weak supervision and applies it to semantic segmentation and object detection on PASCAL VOC.  
- Strength: Clear problem formulation motivating expectation loss over thresholding or regression.  
- Strength: Demonstrates practical value with experiments, including a reported 1.5% AP gain for object detection (RCNN).  
- Strength: Provides a theoretical derivation for the expectation loss and links it to Bernoulli modeling.  
- Strength: Proposes new segment classification metrics (TAPC and NDCG) to address evaluation gaps.  
- Weakness: Latent e-SVM lacks theoretical analysis of the alternating optimization, convergence, and optimality; regularization is only intuitively justified.  
- Weakness: Insufficiently differentiated from weighted SVMs—positiveness acts like instance weights and literature comparisons are weak.  
- Weakness: Performance gains are modest relative to recent detection advances, raising questions about practical impact versus added complexity.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to separate the contributions of expectation loss vs. the regularizer and other components.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of failure cases or situations where improvements are marginal.  
- Weakness: Claim of broad generality (any segments, features, classifiers) is unsubstantiated experimentally.  
- Technical concern: Uses IoU as the positiveness measure without discussing alternatives or its effect on performance.  
- Technical concern: Initialization (ubb IoU) for latent optimization is critical but not analyzed for sensitivity.  
- Technical concern: Latent extension requires alternating optimization—computational cost vs. standard SVMs is not reported.  
- Related work: MIL differences are noted but connections to recent continuous-label weakly supervised methods and weighted SVM literature are underdeveloped.  
- Significance: Addresses an important weak supervision problem with consistent but incremental practical gains.  
- Recommendation: Improve theoretical justification for latent optimization, better contrast with weighted SVMs, add ablations, and analyze failure cases before acceptance.  
- Final assessment: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10 — overall a reasonable contribution but incremental; reviewer recommends weak acceptance.
