Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from “soft” labels in [0,1] that represent the positiveness of a segment proposal (instantiated as IoU with pixel GT). Each example’s label Yi ∈ {±1} is modeled as Bernoulli with success probability μi = g(ui) (set to ui in experiments), and the training objective minimizes the expected hinge loss over Yi: μi max(0,1−w⊤xi) + (1−μi) max(0,1+w⊤xi), plus an L2 regularizer. This can be implemented with standard linear SVM solvers by interpreting it as a weighted combination of positive/negative hinge terms per example. The authors extend e-SVM to weak supervision (only bounding boxes available) by treating ui as latent and adding a convex regularizer R(u) that is the cross-entropy between the segment’s overlap with the GT bounding box, ubb, and ui; training alternates between optimizing w and u.

They evaluate on (i) segment proposal scoring using two proposed criteria (TAPC and NDCG), (ii) semantic segmentation via CPMC segments + second-order pooling, and (iii) object detection, where they derive a per-pixel confidence map from segment scores and pool it over an m×m grid within candidate detection windows, concatenated with RCNN features. On PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation they report e-SVM > SVR > SVC for segment scoring, and segmentation pixel accuracies of 36.8% (pixel labels) and 27.7% (boxes). On VOC 2007 detection, they claim +1.2 AP over RCNN pre-regression and +1.5 AP post-regression, reaching 60.0 mAP.

Relative to prior work, the setup sits between fully supervised segmentation and image-level/MIL formulations. The e-SVM objective is closely related to SVMs with probabilistic/soft/fuzzy labels and to cost-sensitive/instance-weighted SVMs; the weakly supervised extension resembles latent SVM/EM with a data-dependent prior on the latent soft labels. The spatial confidence map feature is related to per-pixel heatmaps and spatial pyramid pooling.

Strengths
- Simple, intuitive formulation: expected hinge loss with soft labels is clean and convex in w; the latent variant is a straightforward alternating scheme with a convex regularizer.
- Practical: can be reduced to standard linear SVM solvers; agnostic to segment proposal and base detector.
- Empirical signal: consistent gains over SVC and small gains over SVR on segment scoring; modest but non-trivial detection mAP improvements when added to RCNN.
- The attempt to evaluate segment scoring directly (TAPC, NDCG) is reasonable in spirit.

Weaknesses
- Originality is limited. Learning with soft/probabilistic labels for SVMs (“fuzzy SVM,” “soft-label SVM,” SVMs with uncertain labels) is well studied; the proposed loss is a standard convex combination of positive/negative hinge losses. The latent extension is akin to latent SVM/EM with a KL prior; related ideas exist in weakly supervised segmentation/detection with soft assignments.
- Justification for μi = g(ui) = ui is ad hoc. No exploration of alternative calibrations or learning g from data; IoU is not a probability of positive class in the usual sense.
- Optimization details are under-specified. For the u-update (Eq. 2 with cross-entropy regularizer), a closed-form or algorithmic description is missing; complexity, convergence behavior, and stopping criteria are not discussed. The claim that “any SVM solver” can be used merits clarification on how the mixed hinge is implemented (e.g., splitting each example into two with weights μi and 1−μi).
- Experimental evaluation lacks key baselines and ablations:
  - Missing comparisons to logistic regression with probabilistic labels, ranking/ordinal regression, and learning-to-rank methods, which are natural for IoU targets.
  - No ablation of the latent regularizer strength λR, the effect of setting λR=0 (claimed to degenerate), or alternative R(u).
  - In detection, gains could be conflated with modifications to bounding-box regression features; an ablation isolating the proposed confidence map is needed.
- Clarity and presentation issues: several typos and notation errors (e.g., λw·1 w⊤w; duplicated N/N factors; incomplete references), and some figures/tables/metrics (TAPC definition, threshold sweep, exact computation of mean AP across thresholds) are insufficiently specified. Semantic segmentation is reported via “pixel accuracy” only, not the standard mean IU on VOC, hindering comparability.

Quality
Technically, the expected hinge objective is sound and convex in w; with the proposed convex R(u) the u-subproblem is convex, but the overall alternation remains non-convex with potential local minima. The weak supervision assumption that u should stay near ubb is reasonable but heuristic. Claims of state-of-the-art on detection rest on small mAP gains without thorough controls; statistical significance and variance are not reported.

Clarity
The core idea is understandable, but the paper would benefit from tighter notation, precise algorithmic details for the u-update, explicit guidance on using standard SVM solvers, and clearer, standard evaluation protocols (e.g., mean IU for segmentation). Several references are incomplete or malformed.

Originality
Moderate to low. The contribution is primarily an application-oriented packaging of soft-label SVM training with a latent soft-label extension and a specific regularizer, plus a straightforward feature integration into RCNN.

Significance
Incremental. The method is practical and could be adopted, but the empirical gains are modest and the novelty limited. The proposed segment-scoring metrics may be useful, but they need clearer definition and broader validation.

Arguments for acceptance
- Simple, general technique for incorporating soft labels with a clear weakly supervised extension.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements on segment scoring and small but positive gains on VOC detection.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft/fuzzy/uncertain-label SVMs and latent SVM approaches.
- Incomplete experimental analysis and missing baselines/ablations; unclear attribution of detection gains.
- Presentation and reproducibility gaps; non-standard or underspecified evaluation for segmentation.

Suggestions for improvement
- Situate the work within soft/fuzzy/uncertain-label SVM literature and weakly supervised segmentation/detection with soft assignments; add appropriate citations and discuss differences.
- Learn or calibrate g(u) (e.g., monotone spline, Platt-style calibration) and analyze sensitivity to g and λR; report convergence behavior and complexity.
- Provide full details for the u-update; include pseudocode/math for the optimizer and implementation notes for using standard SVM solvers.
- Add baselines: logistic regression with probabilistic labels, ranking SVM/ordinal regression on IoU, and deep baselines that regress IoU.
- In detection, isolate the effect of the proposed feature with a clean ablation (no changes to bbox regression); report per-class AP deltas and statistical significance.
- For segmentation, report mean IU and per-class results; compare to recent weakly supervised segmentation baselines.
- Clean up notation, fix references, and clearly define TAPC and how AP is averaged over thresholds.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The idea is practical and results are promising, but the novelty, empirical rigor, and clarity need strengthening for NeurIPS.
