- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous [0,1] labels, with basic (observed positiveness) and latent (unobserved positiveness) variants, applied to semantic segmentation and object detection with improvements over SVC/SVR on PASCAL.

- Positions work relative to weakly supervised learning, differentiating from MIL and weighted SVMs and building on segment-based classification and RCNN-style detection.

- Strength: Addresses an important, well-motivated problem of reducing expensive pixel-level annotations.

- Strength: The e-SVM has a clear, theoretically motivated probabilistic extension of SVMs and a logical derivation of the expectation loss.

- Strength: Proposes new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) suited to segment classification.

- Strength: Shows consistent experimental improvements, including a 1.5% AP gain on PASCAL VOC 2007.

- Strength: Method is practical and can integrate with existing pipelines like RCNN.

- Weakness: Related work comparison is insufficient, missing recent weakly supervised segmentation and advanced MIL variants.

- Weakness: Choice of KL-divergence regularizer R(u) is somewhat ad hoc without stronger theoretical justification or ablation.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical convergence guarantees or analysis for the latent e-SVM optimization (Algorithm 1).

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation lacks per-class breakdown, error analysis, and computational complexity/scalability analysis.

- Weakness: Novelty is somewhat unclear due to similarity with existing “probabilistic SVM” concepts; clearer differentiation is needed.

- Quality/clarity: Paper is technically sound and well-written but needs clearer detail in some parts (e.g., spatial confidence map construction) and better justification of regularization.

- Originality/significance: Meaningful contribution by addressing continuous-label weak supervision and demonstrating practical gains, though conceptually related to prior probabilistic approaches.

- Recommendation: Accept — strengths and empirical gains outweigh the noted weaknesses.

- Suggestion: Expand related work to include recent weakly supervised methods and modern MIL variants.

- Suggestion: Provide stronger justification and ablation studies for the chosen regularizer R(u).

- Suggestion: Add per-class results, error analysis, and computational complexity/runtime details.

- Suggestion: Clarify distinctions from previous probabilistic SVM approaches.

- Suggestion: Improve clarity on implementation details (e.g., spatial confidence map features).

- Pro: Tackles a real-world annotation-cost problem with a sound mathematical formulation and demonstrated benchmark improvements.

- Con: Insufficient comparisons to recent methods, ad-hoc regularization choice, limited experimental depth, and some overlap with prior probabilistic SVM ideas.
