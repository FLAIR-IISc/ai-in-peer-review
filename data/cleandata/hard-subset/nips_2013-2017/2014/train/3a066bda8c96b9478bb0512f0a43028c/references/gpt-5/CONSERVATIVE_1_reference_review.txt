Summary
The paper proposes “Expectation-loss SVM” (e-SVM), which replaces the standard hinge loss with an expected hinge under a Bernoulli label model whose success probability µi is a function of a “positiveness” score ui ∈ [0,1] (in practice, ui is the IoU). The loss per example is µi·max(0,1−f(xi)) + (1−µi)·max(0,1+f(xi)), a convex combination of positive and negative hinge losses. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), ui is treated as latent and optimized jointly with the classifier using alternating minimization and a convex regularizer that keeps ui near the segment–box IoU ubb via a KL/Bernoulli cross-entropy. The method is evaluated on (i) segment scoring (TAPC and NDCG metrics), (ii) semantic segmentation using CPMC segments and second-order pooling, and (iii) object detection by building “spatial confidence map” features from segment scores and concatenating them with RCNN features, reporting up to +1.5 mAP on PASCAL 2007 after bounding-box regression.

Relation to prior work
The idea of training with soft/probabilistic labels and optimizing expected hinge loss is closely related to prior work on SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels and to instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs, as well as soft-label training used with logistic/hinge losses. The latent variant is reminiscent of latent SVM / EM-CCCP-style alternation, with a regularization bias toward a surrogate target derived from boxes. The spatial pooling of segment confidences over detection windows echoes earlier attempts to inject segmentation cues into detection (e.g., bottom-up segmentation for top-down detection, segment-based features, and pooled class-specific maps). The paper cites WSVM and MIL but omits several directly relevant works on SVMs with probabilistic labels/soft labels and weak supervision beyond MIL.

Strengths
- Simple, convex formulation for the fully supervised case; the latent extension is straightforward and computationally tractable (alternating convex problems).
- Practical weakly supervised setting using only boxes; the KL regularization is a sensible way to keep latent ui well-behaved.
- The detection integration is simple and potentially broadly applicable to various proposal/segment pipelines.
- Empirical gains on RCNN (+1.2 to +1.5 mAP) are nontrivial if comparisons are controlled, and segment-scoring metrics (TAPC/NDCG) show improvements over SVC/SVR.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: the expected hinge with soft labels is very close to known “soft-label”/probabilistic-label SVM formulations and can be implemented by duplicating each example with labels ±1 and weights µi and 1−µi. The paper does not position itself clearly against these lines of work nor cite them adequately.
- Claims about using “any standard SVM solver” are under-specified. Standard solvers do not natively support per-example splitting into two label-weighted hinge terms; the practical reduction (e.g., duplicating examples with weights) should be made explicit, with complexity/memory implications.
- Weak supervision: the choice g(u)=u and the strength/role of the KL regularizer are not analyzed. Without R, u collapses to {0,1}; with strong R, u may just track ubb. There is no ablation on λR, alternative g(·), or alternative regularizers. No convergence guarantees or practical stopping criteria are given beyond “until converge.”
- Evaluation controls are insufficient. For detection, the RCNN baseline appears modified (feature augmentation for bbox regression); it is unclear whether all settings are identical aside from the proposed features. Per-class AP tables, statistical significance, and ablations (e.g., grid size m, max vs average pooling, CPMC vs. superpixels) are missing. The reported +1.5 mAP may depend on engineering details rather than the proposed objective.
- Segment/segmentation results are modest (“comparable” to SVR) and do not demonstrate clear advantage under strong supervision; improvements over established baselines are small.
- Clarity/presentation: there are numerous typos, broken references, and incomplete bibliographic entries; figures/tables are referenced but not fully explained here. The optimization over u lacks details (constraints, closed-form updates vs. numeric solver). Reproducibility is limited (hyperparameters, code, and data splits not fully specified).

Quality
Technically sound at a high level (convex risk with soft labels; alternating convex subproblems for latent variables), but theoretical depth is limited (no analysis of bias/consistency or of the latent optimization). Experimental validation is not sufficiently thorough to isolate where gains come from or to rule out confounds.

Clarity
The core idea is understandable, but the write-up needs substantial polishing and more implementation detail. The relation to prior art on soft/probabilistic labels for SVMs should be clarified.

Originality
Incremental. The combination of expected hinge with a KL-regularized latent positiveness tied to boxes is a reasonable engineering variant but close to established paradigms (soft-label SVMs, latent SVMs, weakly supervised segmentation/detection that pool segment scores).

Significance
Potentially useful as a simple plug-in for pipelines that have soft target qualities, but current evidence shows modest gains and limited novelty. Impact will depend on clearer positioning and more rigorous, controlled experiments.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Please position e-SVM against prior work on SVMs with probabilistic/soft labels and expected hinge losses, and clarify what is substantively new.
- Describe precisely how you implement the loss with LibLinear (e.g., duplicating each xi with labels ±1 and weights µi, 1−µi). Provide complexity/runtime and memory implications.
- Provide ablations: λR sweep, alternative g(u), alternative regularizers, initialization sensitivity, and a comparison to using fixed u=ubb (no latent updates).
- For detection, report results with an unmodified RCNN baseline under identical training/testing and provide full per-class AP tables and significance tests. Show the effect of m, pooling type, and segment proposal method.
- For segmentation, report standard metrics and compare to strong baselines beyond SVC/SVR, including regression with bounded targets and logistic regression with soft labels.
- Fix references, typos, and supply enough details to reproduce results.

Recommendation
Given the limited conceptual novelty, missing comparisons to closely related soft/probabilistic-label SVM literature, and insufficiently controlled empirical validation, I do not recommend acceptance at this time. The idea is practical and may be publishable with stronger positioning, clearer technical details, and more rigorous experiments.
