- Proposes e-SVM: expected hinge loss using soft labels µi = g(ui) (IoU as ui) to train on continuous supervision without binarization.  
- Introduces latent e-SVM for weak box supervision, treating ui as latent with a convex KL regularizer toward box-IoU and alternating convex updates in w and u.  
- Optimization details are underspecified: no closed-form update/algorithmic specifics for u (constraints, step sizes, initialization, stopping).  
- Claim that e-SVM “can be solved by any standard SVM solver” is misleading—loss is per-instance weighted hinge and requires instance-weight support or reduction via example duplication, but reduction/solver used is not described.  
- Key hyperparameters (λw, λR, g(·), convergence criteria) and their selection are not reported, limiting reproducibility.  
- Empirical gains: small improvements on segment confidence metrics (TAPC/NDCG) over SVR and larger gains over SVC; semantic segmentation comparable to SVR, contradicting claims of outperforming SVR.  
- Detection shows +1.2–1.5 mAP over RCNN by concatenating spatial confidence maps, but ablations are incomplete and confounded by feature and bbox-regression changes.  
- No variance or statistical significance reported for experimental results.  
- Presentation issues: many typos, broken formulas (e.g., malformed regularizer), overloaded notation (g(ui)=ui), and incomplete references.  
- Algorithm 1 lacks implementation detail; TAPC metric description omits thresholds and averaging details.  
- Novelty is limited: expected hinge loss with probabilistic/fuzzy labels and latent KL-regularized learning overlap with prior SVM/latent SVM and posterior-regularized EM literature; positioning against prior work is weak.  
- Spatial confidence map feature is a plausible engineering contribution but related prior ideas in detection/segmentation are not adequately cited.  
- Practical significance: method is simple, broadly applicable, and yields promising detection gains that could be useful with modern detectors.  
- Scientific significance is modest without stronger theoretical or empirical differentiation from existing soft/latent SVM methods.  
- Pros: addresses weak/soft supervision, convex-in-blocks formulation, task-agnostic evaluation metrics, and non-trivial detection gains when augmenting RCNN.  
- Cons: limited novelty, insufficient technical details for reproduction, inconsistencies between claims and reported results, and insufficient detection ablations.  
- Requests for authors: provide reduction or solver details for e-SVM, derive/describe u-update with KL, report λ values and convergence, run targeted ablations comparing SVC/SVR-based confidence maps, and report sensitivity and variance.  
- Overall recommendation: promising but falls short on clarity, positioning, and rigor; lean reject pending stronger related work, optimization details, and cleaner ablations.
