Summary
The paper proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM), a max-margin method for training with “soft” labels in [0,1], where a segment’s positiveness is defined as its IoU with ground-truth. The core idea is to treat the true binary label Y as Bernoulli with success probability μi = g(ui) (set to ui in experiments) and minimize the expected hinge loss: g(ui) times the positive hinge plus (1−g(ui)) times the negative hinge, plus an L2 regularizer. This yields a convex objective in w when ui is known (pixel-level supervision).

For weak supervision with only bounding boxes, ui is treated as a latent variable, and the paper adds a convex regularizer R(u) equal to the cross-entropy between ui and the IoU with the bounding box ubb, yielding an alternating minimization over w and u. The u-step is convex (linear term in u from the expected hinge plus the convex KL penalty), and the w-step is a standard linear SVM with label-dependent weights.

The method is evaluated on segment proposal confidence prediction (PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation), introducing two metrics (TAPC and NDCG) that directly assess segment ranking/classification. e-SVM outperforms SVC (for multiple thresholds) and slightly outperforms SVR. For semantic segmentation, e-SVM is comparable to SVR with pixel labels (36.8% vs. similar baseline) and to its latent version with only boxes (27.7%). For detection on PASCAL VOC 2007, the authors derive a spatial confidence map from segment scores and concatenate it with RCNN features, reporting +1.2 mAP before box regression and +1.5 mAP after.

Relation to prior work: The approach sits between fully supervised pixel labeling and MIL-style weak supervision and draws analogies to instance-weighted SVMs. It differs from MIL because proposals may not contain fully positive segments. The latent formulation resembles latent SVM with a convex regularizer and connects to weighted SVMs and learning with uncertain/soft labels, though the latter is not thoroughly cited.

Quality
- Technical soundness:
  - The expected hinge loss with μi ∈ [0,1] yields a convex objective in w (nonnegative combination of two hinge losses). The latent extension is bi-convex (convex in each block) with the chosen KL regularizer; alternating minimization should monotonically decrease the objective if subproblems are solved exactly.
  - The claim that it can be “effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver” needs precision: the loss can be implemented by duplicating each example with two labels and instance weights g(ui) and 1−g(ui), requiring per-instance weighting or resampling; not all off-the-shelf solvers support this natively.
  - The choice g(u)=u is a heuristic; no analysis or calibration is provided, and learning g (e.g., monotone link) could matter.
  - The optimization over u is not detailed (closed form vs. numerical solver) nor are convergence criteria and runtimes reported. Sensitivity to λR is not studied.
  - Evaluation for detection does not isolate the contribution of e-SVM vs. the spatial confidence map idea; a baseline using SVR-derived maps is missing.

- Empirical support:
  - Segment confidence prediction: consistent improvements over SVC across thresholds and slight gains over SVR on TAPC/NDCG. Clear and relevant, though more per-class analysis and statistical tests would strengthen claims.
  - Semantic segmentation: results are comparable to SVR rather than clearly better.
  - Detection: +1.5 mAP over RCNN with bbox regression is promising, but ablations (effect of m, pooling, additive kernel mapping, λR, proposal method) and comparisons to alternative soft-label baselines are absent.

Clarity
- The main idea is understandable, but the manuscript has numerous typos, inconsistent notation (e.g., regularizer term formatting “λw · 1 wTw”), and several incomplete/broken references.
- Missing details:
  - Exact mapping to a standard solver (duplication and weighting).
  - The u-step optimization (derivation/solver), initialization, and convergence behavior.
  - Hyperparameters (λw, λR), cross-validation protocol, and computational costs.
  - Full definitions for TAPC (threshold range, averaging) and NDCG construction (relevance definition, discount base).
- Figures/tables referenced but not fully shown in the text; detection results are summarized without per-class table.

Originality
- Using expected hinge loss with soft labels is closely related to SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic/fuzzy labels and instance-weighted SVMs. The paper positions against WSVM but does not adequately discuss these connections or prior formulations that also weight positive/negative hinge terms or train with soft targets.
- The latent extension with a KL penalty towards ubb is straightforward and related to posterior regularization/penalized latent SVM ideas.
- The spatial confidence map for detection is a practical feature-engineering contribution; similar aggregation of segment cues has appeared in segmentation/detection pipelines, and comparisons to RankSVM or soft-label logistic regression are missing.

Significance
- The method is simple, general, and applicable to weak supervision scenarios with soft labels; this is useful in large-scale vision settings.
- Gains on detection are modest but potentially impactful in practice, given ease of integration with existing detectors.
- Given that segmentation results are comparable to SVR and some missing baselines, the advancement over strong existing techniques (SVR, soft-label logistic regression, RankSVM) is not convincingly established.

Strengths
- Conceptually simple convex formulation with soft labels; bi-convex latent extension.
- Works with weak supervision and integrates into modern detection (RCNN) to yield consistent improvements.
- Introduces direct segment-evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG).

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to soft-label/uncertain-label SVM literature; missing citations and positioning.
- Insufficient algorithmic detail (u-step, solver mapping, hyperparameters, runtimes).
- Missing key baselines (soft-label logistic regression, RankSVM, SVR-based spatial maps) and ablations.
- Clarity issues and incomplete references.
- Modest empirical gains on segmentation; detection improvements not dissected.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a precise equivalence to instance-weighted SVM (duplicate examples with weights) and discuss solver support; or provide implementation details.
- Detail the u-step optimization (derivation, closed-form if any, numerical method, convergence).
- Compare against:
  - Logistic regression trained with soft targets (cross-entropy with yi=ui).
  - RankSVM for ranking segments (aligned with TAPC/NDCG).
  - SVR- and SVC-based spatial confidence maps for detection.
- Study sensitivity to λR and explore learning g(u) (monotonic calibration).
- Broaden proposal methods (e.g., MCG/Selective Search) and report runtime.
- Fix references and improve writing clarity.

Arguments for acceptance
- Practical, simple approach for weakly supervised learning with soft labels.
- Convex subproblems and stable optimization.
- Demonstrated improvements on a strong detector with minimal integration effort.

Arguments against acceptance
- Novelty over existing soft-label SVM/uncertain-label methods is limited and not well positioned.
- Missing baselines and ablations prevent isolating the benefit of the proposed loss versus engineering choices.
- Clarity and completeness issues (broken references, missing algorithmic details).

Overall: borderline; promising idea and some positive empirical results, but needs stronger positioning, more thorough comparisons, and improved clarity.
