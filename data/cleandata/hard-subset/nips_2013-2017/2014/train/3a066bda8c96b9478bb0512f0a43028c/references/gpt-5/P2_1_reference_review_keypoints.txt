- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that minimizes expected hinge loss using per-instance positiveness µ∈[0,1]; convex in w when u observed.  
- Extends to weak supervision by treating u as latent with a KL regularizer tethering u to box-IoU and alternating minimization (biconvex).  
- Demonstrates on segment confidence prediction (introduces TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation with CPMC, and object detection by adding a class-specific spatial confidence map to RCNN, reporting ~+1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC07.  
- Method conceptually sits between WSVM and MIL and resembles latent SVM/EM when u is latent.  
- Similarities to prior work on probabilistic/soft-label and fuzzy SVMs are not adequately cited or distinguished.  
- Observed-u objective is convex and equivalent to per-example hinge weighting; latent problem is biconvex and alternating minimization converges to a local minimum.  
- The u-update lacks derivation/algorithmic details (closed-form vs numeric), handling of constraints ui∈(0,1), and complexity analysis.  
- Some claims about outperforming SVR are inconsistent across the paper; segmentation gains are marginal versus SVR.  
- The reported +1.5 mAP gain on VOC07 may be meaningful but is confounded by other changes (bbox regression features) and missing ablation against SVR-based confidence in the same setup.  
- Key experimental details (hyperparameters λw, λR, initialization sensitivity, runtime, convergence criteria) are missing, limiting reproducibility.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks variance/error bars, per-class AP tables, and important ablations (g(u) forms, λR strength, alternative regularizers).  
- No comparison to ranking baselines (e.g., RankSVM) despite using NDCG as an evaluation metric.  
- Exposition of the core idea is clear, but notation and typographical issues, inconsistent scaling, and incomplete references reduce clarity.  
- The claimed reduction to “any standard SVM solver” is asserted but not practically explained (how LibLinear was used with per-instance/per-label weights).  
- Originality is limited: expected-hinge and weighted positive/negative hinge ideas exist in soft-label literature; latent extension and spatial map are incremental relative to prior work.  
- Strengths: simple convex formulation for soft labels, principled latent regularizer, practical RCNN integration with reported gains, and introduction of segment-ranking metrics.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty, missing algorithmic and convergence details for u, reproducibility and notation problems, inadequate ablations and baselines, and no computational cost analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: cite/contrast soft-label SVM literature; give explicit reduction to standard solvers; derive and detail u-update, constraints, complexity, and convergence; run ablations (RankSVM, SVR-in-map, λR, initialization); isolate spatial-map effect; report per-class AP, variances, and release hyperparameters/code.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important weakly supervised setting with a simple, adoptable method and some empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, missing critical technical/experimental details, and modest/confounded empirical improvements.  
- Overall: promising and practical idea but requires substantial revision for rigor, clarity, and reproducibility to meet NeurIPS standards.
