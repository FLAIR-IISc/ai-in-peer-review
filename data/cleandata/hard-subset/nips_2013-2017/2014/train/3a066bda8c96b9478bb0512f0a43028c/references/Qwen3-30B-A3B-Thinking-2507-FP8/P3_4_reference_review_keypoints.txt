- Summary: Proposes e-SVM to learn from continuous "positiveness" labels by modeling labels as Bernoulli variables, handling both observed and latent positiveness; evaluated on segmentation and detection with improvements over SVC/SVR.

- Strength: Well-motivated problem addressing expensive pixel-level annotation and common continuous positiveness labels in vision.

- Strength: Novel expectation-loss formulation that handles continuous labels without arbitrary thresholding or pure regression.

- Strength: Empirical gains—consistent improvements on PASCAL VOC segmentation metrics and a 1.5% AP boost on VOC2007 detection.

- Strength: Clear and useful evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment classification.

- Strength: Practical implementation using standard SVM solvers (e.g., LibLinear), facilitating adoption.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for the alternating optimization in latent e-SVM (Algorithm 1).

- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to MIL and recent weakly supervised methods; claim that MIL is inapplicable is unsubstantiated.

- Weakness: Ambiguous choice of KL-divergence regularizer for latent positiveness with no justification or sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Limited experimental scope—only two vision tasks tested, no cross-domain demonstrations.

- Weakness: Missing implementation details and computational cost/training-time comparisons versus baselines.

- Overall assessment: Novel, well-motivated approach with convincing experiments but needs stronger theory, comparisons, and ablations; quality high, originality moderate, significance high.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Required revisions: add convergence analysis for Algorithm 1; expand related work and comparisons to MIL/recent methods; justify and analyze KL regularization choice; provide computational complexity and runtime comparisons.
