Summary
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a simple modification of the hinge loss that takes an expectation over a Bernoulli label with success probability µi = g(ui), where ui ∈ [0,1] is a “positiveness” score (set to IoU with ground-truth segmentation). This yields, per sample i, a loss µi·max(0,1−f(xi)) + (1−µi)·max(0,1+f(xi)). A latent variant is introduced for weak supervision (only bounding boxes are available) by treating ui as latent and regularizing ui toward the IoU with the box (ubb) using a KL divergence penalty, optimized by alternating minimization. The approach is evaluated for segment proposal scoring (new metrics TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation (with and without pixel labels), and object detection by concatenating a “spatial confidence map” feature to RCNN features, claiming +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC07.

Relation to prior work
The construction is closely related to SVMs with uncertain/soft labels, fuzzy/weighted SVMs, and latent SVMs. The expected hinge decomposes into weighted positive and negative hinge terms, i.e., label-weighted losses per example, which has appeared in variations in the literature on label noise, soft labels, and fuzzy memberships. The latent extension is an EM-like alternating scheme akin to LSVM. The paper positions itself against SVC/SVR and mentions WSVM, but the connection to soft/probabilistic-label SVMs and fuzzy SVMs is incomplete.

Quality
- Objective: The expected hinge derivation is straightforward and correct given µi. However, the mapping µi=g(ui) is fixed ad hoc to µi=ui; no calibration or justification is provided that IoU is a calibrated probability of “positiveness,” nor is g learned/validated.
- Optimization: The claim “can be effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver (e.g., LibLinear)” is underspecified. The per-example objective is a weighted sum of two hinge losses; solving with a standard solver requires either per-example weights for two duplicated samples with labels ±1 or native instance weighting—implementation details are missing.
- Latent e-SVM: The alternating scheme is nonconvex overall; no convergence guarantees or practical details are given (e.g., closed-form update for ui, line search, stopping criteria). With g(u)=u, the per-example update minimizes a convex function on (0,1) but requires solving a 1D problem; no algorithm is specified.
- Regularization: The KL term assumes ui should be near ubb; this is not obviously justified (IoU with the bounding box is a loose proxy for true mask IoU and can be systematically biased).
- Evaluation: For proposal scoring, TAPC and NDCG are defined but not fully specified (threshold ranges, averaging across thresholds and classes, handling class imbalance/positives, confidence calibration). The reported table is poorly formatted and lacks statistical significance/confidence intervals. For semantic segmentation, “accuracy” is undefined (mIoU? pixel accuracy?) and comparisons to strong baselines are weak; the text alternately claims “slightly better than SVR” and “comparable.”
- Object detection: The only comparison is RCNN vs RCNN+e-SVM features; there is no ablation replacing e-SVM by SVR-trained segment confidences in the same pipeline, making attribution unclear. In addition, the authors change the bounding-box regression features (“slightly improved performance”), confounding the baseline. Details such as grid size m, pooling, dimensionality after additive kernels, and classifier settings are partially given, but training hyperparameters (λ, λR) and implementation specifics are missing.

Clarity
The paper is difficult to follow in places and contains numerous notation, grammar, and formatting errors:
- Equation (1): “λw · 1 wTw” is unclear; likely meant λ/2||w||^2. Also “N N∑” appears repeatedly.
- Algorithm 1 uses “until Converge” and omits stopping criteria; no pseudocode for ui update.
- Many typos: “continues” for continuous, “segmentic segmentation,” “one of the advantage,” “lead the algorithm to stuck,” etc.
- Figures are referenced but not shown; the TAPC/NDCG table is garbled.
- References are severely broken: missing years, malformed author lists (“and A, M. Everingham, …”), missing entries (“?”), inconsistent capitalization, incomplete venue info.

Originality
The core idea (expected hinge with probabilistic labels) and the alternating update with a KL regularizer toward a proxy are modestly incremental relative to existing strands: soft/fuzzy-label SVMs, instance/label-weighted hinge losses, latent SVM with weak supervision, and object detection feature augmentation from segments. The paper does not convincingly differentiate from these lines nor provide theoretical insights beyond the straightforward expected loss.

Significance
The empirical gains for detection (+1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC07) could be useful, but without a clean ablation (e-SVM vs SVR in the same pipeline; effect of altered bbox regression; sensitivity to m, λ, λR), it is hard to ascribe improvements to the proposed objective. The segmentation results are not state-of-the-art and are ambiguously reported. The proposal-scoring metrics may be useful if more rigorously defined, but as presented they are hard to assess.

Pros
- Simple, intuitive loss that naturally handles soft labels in [0,1].
- Latent extension with convex per-block subproblems and a reasonable 1D regularizer.
- Practical attempt to integrate segment confidences into detection via a spatial confidence map; modest gains on a standard benchmark.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to prior work on soft/fuzzy-label SVMs and latent SVMs; insufficient related-work discussion.
- Theoretical and optimization details are thin; solver usage is unclear; no convergence or complexity analysis.
- Experimental methodology is under-specified; key ablations are missing; reported metrics/tables are unclear; segmentation results are weak/ambiguous.
- Severe writing/formatting/reference issues that impede understanding and reproducibility.

Questions for authors
1. How exactly do you implement e-SVM with LibLinear? Do you duplicate each xi with labels ±1 and use per-instance weights µi and 1−µi? If so, how are fractional weights handled?
2. How are λ and λR chosen? Please report sensitivity and values per experiment.
3. For the latent ui update, do you use a closed-form solution or numeric 1D optimization? Please provide the formula or algorithm.
4. Why µ=g(u)=u? Did you consider/learn a calibrated g (e.g., monotone link)?
5. Provide a clean ablation on VOC07: RCNN baseline; +spatial confidence map using SVR; using e-SVM; and with/without your modified bbox regression.
6. Precisely define TAPC: threshold grid, averaging scheme, and handling of classes/positives.

Recommendation: Reject (as is). The idea is reasonable and potentially useful, but the paper’s novelty is limited, empirical evidence is insufficiently controlled, and the presentation quality (notation, references, clarity) is far below NeurIPS standards. With thorough revisions (clearer theory/optimization, stronger/cleaner experiments and ablations, rigorous metrics, and corrected formatting/references), this could be a solid workshop or later-conference submission.
