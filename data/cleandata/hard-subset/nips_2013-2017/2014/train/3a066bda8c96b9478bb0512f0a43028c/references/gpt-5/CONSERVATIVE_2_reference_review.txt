Summary
The paper proposes “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM), a linear SVM trained with an expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label model whose mean µi=g(ui) is the “positiveness” of a segment (e.g., IoU). With fully supervised pixel labels, ui is observed; with only bounding boxes, ui is treated as a latent variable and regularized via a cross-entropy term toward an initialization ubb (segment–bbox IoU). Optimization alternates between standard linear SVM training (w) and convex updates in u. The method is applied to (i) segment proposal scoring for semantic segmentation and (ii) object detection by building a spatial confidence map from segment scores and concatenating it with R-CNN features. The authors report modest gains over SVC/SVR on proposal scoring and +1.2 to +1.5 AP over R-CNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Relation to prior work
The formulation reduces to a soft-label SVM: the expected hinge loss equals a convex combination of positive and negative hinge losses weighted by ui and 1−ui. This is closely related to SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels, label-dependent costs, and fuzzy/weighted SVMs. The latent variant is an EM-like alternating procedure akin to latent SVM, with an entropy-style regularizer anchoring u to a bbox-derived proxy. The spatial pooling over a confidence heatmap is reminiscent of spatial pyramid pooling over class-specific score maps.

Strengths
- Simple and implementable: when ui is known, the loss reduces to a weighted sum of standard hinge terms and can be handled by off-the-shelf solvers.
- Practical weak-supervision angle: alternating optimization with a convex-in-u regularizer is straightforward and tends to be stable compared to purely 0/1 latent assignments.
- Empirical signals: consistent (albeit small) improvements over SVC and SVR on proposal scoring; a 1–1.5 mAP gain when concatenating the spatial confidence map with R-CNN is potentially useful.

Weaknesses and concerns
Quality
- Methodological novelty is limited. Expected hinge with soft/probabilistic labels and label-weighted hinge formulations have been studied; the paper neither positions against this literature nor clarifies what is new beyond using ui=IoU and a specific regularizer.
- The latent model is a standard alternating scheme; no convergence analysis (beyond convexity of subproblems) or sensitivity study is provided. Choice of g(u)=u is ad hoc and unexamined.
- Weak empirical validation for the core claim. Comparisons are only to SVC with thresholds and SVR. Missing baselines include: logistic regression with soft targets (cross-entropy with y∈[0,1]), RankSVM/ranking losses, calibrated SVMs, and modern regressors tailored to bounded targets (e.g., sigmoid/beta regression). For weak supervision, comparisons to MIL/latent SVM variants adapted to partial labels would be appropriate.
- The detection improvement may be confounded. The method changes feature dimensionality (concatenation) and also “modified” the bbox regression features; no ablations isolate the contribution of e-SVM vs. architecture changes or feature mapping. It is unclear whether the R-CNN baseline matches the strongest published configuration at the time.
- Hyperparameters (λw, λR), initialization, and optimization details (stopping criteria) are underspecified; no sensitivity analysis is reported. No runtime or scalability discussion.

Clarity
- Writing has numerous grammatical issues and typos; several references are incomplete or malformed. Important details are missing: exact reduction to standard SVM solvers (e.g., duplicating examples with weights?), the definition of “accuracy” for segmentation (pixel accuracy? mIoU?), and precise datasets/splits for each experiment.
- The new evaluation metrics (TAPC) are vaguely specified; NDCG use is reasonable, but setup (gain definition, discount) needs to be fully described.

Originality
- The core idea—expected hinge with soft labels—is not clearly novel. The paper does not adequately differentiate e-SVM from prior weighted/uncertain-label SVMs, nor justify why weighting labels rather than instances is fundamentally distinct (in practice, the proposed loss can be emulated by creating two weighted instances per sample).
- The latent extension with KL regularization toward ubb is a straightforward choice; similar entropy priors are common.

Significance
- Reported gains on detection are modest and lack rigorous ablation; proposal scoring gains over SVR are small. Without broader baselines and clearer analysis, it is hard to conclude that e-SVM substantially advances the state of the art.
- Generality claims (works with any proposals/features/classifiers) are not validated across diverse settings.

Pros
- Simple, convex subproblems; easy to implement.
- Addresses a relevant weakly supervised setting using existing annotations.
- Some empirical improvements on standard datasets.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft/uncertain-label SVM literature.
- Incomplete baselines and ablations; potential confounds in detection experiments.
- Clarity and referencing issues; missing technical details and sensitivity analyses.

Questions for the authors
1. How exactly do you “solve with a standard SVM solver”? Do you duplicate each xi with labels ±1 and weights ui and 1−ui? Does LibLinear support per-instance weights in your setup?
2. Please justify g(u)=u. Did you try other calibrations (e.g., temperature-scaled, nonlinear g)? How sensitive are results to this choice?
3. How are λw and λR chosen? Provide sensitivity plots and convergence behavior for the alternating optimization.
4. What is the precise segmentation metric (mIoU, pixel accuracy)? The reported numbers need to be comparable to prior work.
5. For detection, report: baseline feature dimensionality, the effect of the confidence map alone (without modifying bbox regression), and performance when replacing e-SVM with SVR/logistic regression trained on the same inputs.

Suggestions
- Position the work with respect to SVMs with probabilistic/soft labels, fuzzy SVMs, cost-sensitive SVMs, RankSVM, and cross-entropy with soft targets; add those baselines.
- Provide a clean ablation study for detection, isolating each component (e-SVM vs. architecture changes).
- Explore and report sensitivity to g(·), λR, initialization ubb, and proposal methods.
- Improve clarity, fix references, and detail the evaluation protocols to ensure reproducibility.

Recommendation
Reject. The idea is simple and potentially useful, but the novelty over established soft/uncertain-label SVM formulations is insufficiently demonstrated, and the empirical validation lacks the breadth and rigor needed to support the claims. With stronger positioning, comprehensive baselines, clear ablations, and improved presentation, a revised version could be more compelling.
