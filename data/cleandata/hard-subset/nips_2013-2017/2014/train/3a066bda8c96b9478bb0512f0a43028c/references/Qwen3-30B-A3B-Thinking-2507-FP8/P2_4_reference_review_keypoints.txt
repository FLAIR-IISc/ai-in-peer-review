- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous "positiveness" labels and a latent e-SVM variant for unobserved positiveness.  
- Applies approach to semantic segmentation and object detection on PASCAL VOC, showing modest improvements over SVC/SVR baselines.  
- Mathematically sound formulation with convexity analysis and reasonable extension for latent variables.  
- Introduces two task-appropriate evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG).  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: insufficient comparisons to relevant weakly supervised methods (e.g., MIL and state-of-the-art weakly supervised segmentation).  
- Claims that MIL cannot solve the problem are unsubstantiated and lack empirical/theoretical justification.  
- Reported gains are modest (≈1.5% AP) and not convincingly significant relative to recent methods.  
- Comparisons against detection baselines are outdated (only RCNN), missing newer methods (Fast/Faster R-CNN, Mask R-CNN).  
- Reproducibility is limited by missing implementation details (hyperparameters, computational complexity).  
- Clarity is generally good, but the distinction from weighted SVMs (WSVM) is inadequately explained.  
- Visual result presentation is limited; needs segmentation quality visualizations and error analysis.  
- Novelty is limited due to close connections with existing weighted SVM approaches and lack of broader task demonstrations.  
- Significance is questionable given modest performance gains and unclear computational trade-offs.  
- Overall recommendation: weak rejection; paper needs stronger comparisons, clearer differentiation from related work, more implementation details, and better experimental analysis.
