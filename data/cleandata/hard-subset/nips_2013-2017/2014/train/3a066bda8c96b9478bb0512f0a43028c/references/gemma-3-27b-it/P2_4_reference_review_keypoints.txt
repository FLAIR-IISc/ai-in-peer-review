- Overall: Proposes e-SVM for weakly supervised learning; technically sound but limited novelty and impact → weak accept with major revisions.
- Summary: e-SVM models the expectation over potential labels weighted by a “positiveness” score, bridging SVC/SVR and MIL for continuous label estimates.
- Strength — Technical soundness: Formulation and latent-variable extension are well-defined and optimized using standard SVM solvers.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Shows consistent improvements on PASCAL VOC (segment classification, segmentation, detection) with TAPC, NDCG, and AP metrics.
- Strength — Generality: Method is compatible with various proposal detectors, features, and base classifiers.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized.
- Weakness — Limited novelty: Core idea overlaps with existing weighted SVM literature and lacks strong theoretical justification for the weighting scheme.
- Weakness — Modest significance: Performance gains are small (e.g., ~1.5% AP), raising questions about practical impact.
- Weakness — Evaluation limitations: Experiments are mostly on PASCAL VOC and lack comparisons to more recent weakly supervised methods.
- Weakness — KL regularization: Use of KL divergence feels arbitrary and sensitivity to its parameter is unexplored.
- Weakness — Feature fusion: Concatenating spatial confidence maps with CNN features is simplistic; better fusion methods should be explored.
- NIPS scores summary: Quality 3/5, Clarity 4/5, Originality 2/5, Significance 3/5.
- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem, is well-motivated, and shows empirical improvement over baselines.
- Against acceptance: Incremental contribution, modest gains, single-dataset evaluation, and limited theoretical grounding.
- Recommendations: Expand related work, provide theoretical analysis (convergence/generalization), evaluate on more datasets (e.g., ImageNet/COCO), compare to stronger baselines, analyze regularization sensitivity/alternatives, and improve feature fusion.
- Conclusion: Well-executed and practically useful but incremental; major revisions needed to strengthen novelty, evaluation, and theoretical justification.
