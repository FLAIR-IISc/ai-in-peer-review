- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that minimizes expected hinge loss under soft labels (IoU-based u_i) treating binary labels as Bernoulli with mean μ_i=g(u_i), typically g(u)=u.  
- Weakly-supervised extension treats u_i as latent and regularizes it toward box-IoU u_i^bb with a KL penalty, optimized by alternating minimization over w and u.  
- Applied to segment scoring (CPMC proposals) and detection by adding a pooled spatial confidence map to RCNN features, reporting +1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- The expected hinge loss is equivalent to training a weighted SVM or duplicating examples with weights g(u) and 1−g(u), so core objective novelty is limited.  
- Latent alternating scheme is similar to existing EM-like weakly-supervised segmentation/detection methods; KL prior toward u^bb is a practical but heuristic choice.  
- Strength: clear, intuitive, convex-in-w formulation that is easy to optimize and implement via standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Strength: practical way to incorporate segment quality into learning and couple segmentation cues into detection pipelines.  
- Empirical results show modest but consistent gains in segment scoring (TAPC/NDCG) and small mAP improvement when augmenting RCNN.  
- Weakness: limited novelty given prior work on soft/probabilistic labels, cost-sensitive/weighted SVMs, and expected losses.  
- Weakness: KL regularizer tethering u to u^bb is unjustified; u^bb may be a poor proxy for true mask IoU and no alternatives or ablations (e.g., λ_R) are provided.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis on statistical properties, calibration, robustness, or convergence guarantees beyond convex subproblems.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is incomplete—small gains may be within variance, missing per-class APs, confidence intervals, stronger baselines, and fair isolation of contributions (e.g., bbox regression changes).  
- Weakness: does not model bag-level structure or coverage constraints across proposals, unlike MIL approaches, treating segments independently.  
- Clarity issues: typos, formatting, missing/incomplete references, and omitted figures/tables make verification difficult.  
- Missing implementation details: how u-update is solved, stopping criteria, complexity, hyperparameter selection, and computational/memory overhead of duplication.  
- g(u)=u is ad hoc and not justified or compared to calibrated alternatives (e.g., sigmoid scaling); sensitivity to g(u) is unreported.  
- Questions raised: relation to weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs, sensitivity to g(u), ablations on λ_R and KL prior alternatives, reliability of u^bb, per-class APs and variance, and convergence/time metrics.  
- Minor: fix incomplete references and discuss computational overhead and memory usage.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, implementation-friendly soft-label method with practical latent extension and some empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, heuristic weakly-supervised design, modest/confounded improvements, and presentation/analysis weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: lean reject—practical variant of known ideas with modest gains; needs stronger positioning, ablations, and clearer evaluation to be competitive.
