- Summary: Proposes e-SVM, an expected-hinge extension to handle soft labels µ_i∈[0,1], and a weakly supervised latent-u variant regularized by KL to box IoU; evaluated on segment ranking (TAPC, NDCG), segmentation, and detection (+1.2–1.5 mAP with RCNN fusion).

- Strength: Clear, simple convex objective in w (expected hinge under soft labels) extendable to latent setting with convex subproblems.

- Strength: Weakly supervised continuous u with KL regularizer is computationally straightforward and more stable than hard latent SVM.

- Strength: Spatial confidence map feature is sensible for window-based detection and yields consistent mAP gains when fused with RCNN.

- Strength: Direct task-agnostic segment ranking metrics (TAPC, NDCG) are reasonable and complementary.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — equivalent to weighted hinge via duplicated examples and close to soft/fuzzy/instance-weighted SVMs; prior work not adequately cited or discussed.

- Weakness: Choice µ_i = u_i (IoU) is ad hoc with no calibration, learning of g(.), or sensitivity analysis; IoU is not a true probability.

- Weakness: Latent u optimization is under-specified (no u-update derivation, constraints, closed form, or cost analysis); sensitivity to λ_R and initialization not reported.

- Weakness: Solver claim that standard SVMs (e.g., LibLinear) can effortlessly solve this is dubious — per-instance asymmetric weights require duplication/weights and implementation details are missing.

- Weakness: Empirical evidence is modest and lacks ablations — gains may come from the pooling feature not e-SVM; no comparisons with SVR/SVC using the same pipeline.

- Weakness: Presentation issues — typos, bad references, missing TAPC details, hyperparameters, runtime, and incomplete figures/tables.

- Quality: Technically plausible (convexity arguments hold for fixed u/w), but missing important derivations, solver details, and hyperparameter tuning; detection claim weak without stronger analysis.

- Clarity: Core idea is easy to follow but manuscript needs substantial polishing and explicit details for equivalences, u-step, TAPC, and training settings.

- Originality: Conceptually close to existing probabilistic/fuzzy SVM literature; KL regularizer toward box IoU is a modest twist but not a major novelty.

- Significance: Detection mAP gains are modest and not compelling without ablations; segmentation comparable to SVR, limiting overall impact.

- Suggestions: Cite and position relative to soft/fuzzy/label-noise SVMs and latent SVM; justify or learn g(.); provide u-step derivation, complexity, sensitivity to λ_R and initialization; detail solver implementation and per-instance weighting; add ablations replacing e-SVM with SVR/SVC in the same pipeline; improve writing, figures, and refs.

- Pros: Simple, practical idea with a weakly supervised extension and some detection gains; broadly applicable to soft-label settings.

- Cons: Limited novelty, under-specified optimization and solver claims, modest empirical improvements, missing ablations, and poor presentation.

- Recommendation: Reject (borderline) — could be acceptable after stronger positioning, detailed optimization descriptions, and rigorous ablations.

- Reviewer confidence: High.
