- Summary: Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) to minimize expected hinge loss with soft/latent labels and applies it to segment ranking and detection via a spatial confidence map, reporting modest mAP gains on VOC07.

- Strength: Conceptually simple and general — expected hinge for soft labels is convex in w and integrates with various proposal generators/features.

- Strength: Practical utility — spatial confidence map effectively injects segmentation cues into RCNN and yields consistent AP gains.

- Strength: Empirical signal — e-SVM improves segment-ranking metrics (TAPC, NDCG) over SVC/SVR and gives ~1.2–1.5 mAP gains on VOC07.

- Weakness: Novelty/positioning — formulation is incremental and omits several directly relevant prior works (fuzzy SVMs, probabilistic-label SVMs, ambiguous-label methods).

- Weakness: Solver claim imprecise — stating standard SVM solvers suffice is misleading without explicitly describing the reduction (e.g., duplicate examples with weights) and its numeric/efficiency implications.

- Weakness: Latent optimization details missing — no closed-form u-update, algorithmic details, sensitivity to λR/g(·), convergence guarantees, or empirical convergence plots.

- Weakness: Prior ubb informativeness unexamined — no analysis of correlation between bounding-box IoU prior and true IoU, problematic for thin/irregular objects.

- Weakness: Experimental rigor lacking — missing critical baselines (spatial map derived from SVR/SVC), ablations (latent vs. non-latent, λR, g(·), proposal types), and fuller comparisons to box-supervised methods.

- Weakness: RCNN integration underspecified — training splits, NMS, bbox-regression consistency, hyperparameters, and kernel augmentation details are unclear for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Computational cost unreported — no runtime/memory analysis for per-pixel max over segments and per-window pooling.

- Weakness: Evaluation measures under-specified — TAPC and NDCG definitions and protocols (thresholds, gains, averaging) are incomplete.

- Weakness: Presentation issues — typos, incomplete references, missing figures/tables, and some vague or oversold claims.

- Relation to prior work: Needs to cite and position relative to soft/probabilistic-label SVMs, fuzzy SVMs, ambiguous/partial label learning, and related weak-supervision/MIL literature.

- Pros for acceptance: Simple, broadly applicable formulation; practical spatial confidence map idea; empirical improvements on a strong benchmark.

- Cons for acceptance: Limited methodological novelty, missing baselines/ablations, reproducibility and clarity problems, and imprecise solver/optimization claims.

- Recommendations: Clarify reduction to standard SVM solvers, provide u-update details (closed form or numeric), convergence criteria, and sensitivity analyses for λR and g(·).

- Recommendations: Add critical baselines (spatial map from SVR/SVC), ablations on m/pooling/proposals, runtime/memory reports, and empirical convergence plots.

- Recommendations: Expand related work to include relevant soft-label and ambiguous-label SVM literature, fix references, and improve clarity and figures/tables.

- Final verdict: Weak reject — useful idea with encouraging results but modest novelty and insufficient rigor/clarity for acceptance.
