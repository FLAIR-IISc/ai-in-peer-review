Summary
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from weak or “soft” supervision where each training proposal has a positiveness score in [0,1] (e.g., IoU with a ground-truth mask). Instead of binarizing or regressing IoU, e-SVM treats the label Yi∈{±1} as Bernoulli with mean µi=g(ui), and minimizes the expected hinge loss: µi·hinge(+1) + (1−µi)·hinge(−1), plus an L2 regularizer on w. This yields a convex objective in w and can be implemented using standard linear SVM solvers by treating each proposal as two weighted examples (positive with weight µi, negative with weight 1−µi). For weak supervision where pixel IoU is unknown, the authors introduce a latent e-SVM: u becomes a latent variable per proposal, initialized from the IoU between the proposal and the ground-truth bounding box (u_bb), and updated by alternating minimization with a convex regularizer R(u) defined as the cross entropy D_KL(Bern(u_bb) || Bern(u)). The method is evaluated on segment proposal scoring (introducing TAPC and NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation using CPMC segments, and object detection by concatenating a spatial confidence map feature derived from e-SVM with RCNN features, reporting ~1.2–1.5 mAP improvement on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Relation to prior work
The approach is close to SVMs with probabilistic or soft labels, cost-sensitive/instance-weighted SVMs, and learning with label noise/ambiguity. The latent extension relates to latent SVM and weakly supervised segmentation/detection (MIL, MIM). The detection integration follows a common pattern of adding segmentation-derived cues to window-based detectors (e.g., RCNN), implemented here via a class-specific spatial pooling of per-pixel confidence.

Strengths
- Simple, convex surrogate for soft labels: The expected hinge objective is a natural, well-motivated way to use positiveness scores without ad hoc binarization, and it slots into standard SVM tooling.
- Weakly supervised extension: The alternating scheme with a convex KL regularizer is reasonable and avoids hard assignments that cause local minima, providing a pragmatic bridge from box to mask-level signals.
- Empirical gains in detection: The spatial confidence map is straightforward to compute and yields non-trivial mAP improvements when concatenated with RCNN features.
- Generality: The formulation is generic over proposal generators, features, and base detectors.

Weaknesses and concerns
Quality
- Mathematical clarity: The regularizer term is written as “λw · 1 w^T w” which appears incorrect; presumably it is λ/2 ||w||^2. The paper should fix notation and specify constraints (e.g., u∈(0,1)).
- Missing derivations/algorithms: The u-update (line 4) is not described beyond “argmin.” Given the objective is linear in u plus KL regularization, a closed-form update exists; providing it (and bounds/constraints) would improve rigor. Convergence criteria and hyperparameters (λ, λ_R) selection are underspecified.
- Relationship to weighted/soft-label SVMs: The distinction from WSVM is overstated. The expected hinge loss is exactly equivalent to duplicating each sample into a positive and a negative with instance weights µi and 1−µi; this is a form of instance weighting and soft labels that has prior art. Please cite and compare to SVMs with probabilistic labels/ambiguous labels.
- Baselines: For soft labels, a logistic loss with cross-entropy using soft targets is a strong and appropriate baseline; so is square loss on [0,1] with proper calibration. Only SVC (with thresholds) and SVR are reported.
- Empirical evidence: Segment scoring improvements vs SVR are modest (e.g., TAPC 36.7 vs 35.2; NDCG 0.875 vs 0.865). Semantic segmentation accuracies are comparable, not clearly superior. For detection, a 1.2–1.5 mAP gain is useful but ablations are missing (e.g., effect of latent vs observed u, of KL strength, g(u) choices, bin size m, pooling variant). No runtime analysis.
Clarity
- Writing and references need substantial editing. Several references are malformed; some author names and venues are broken. Figures and tables are referenced but not fully shown here. The algorithm and losses need precise definitions and constraints.
- The “new” evaluation metrics: TAPC is essentially mean AP over thresholds, and NDCG is a standard ranking metric; please position them appropriately and provide details (threshold grid, averaging).
Originality
- The expected hinge loss for soft/probabilistic labels is not new; the contribution is mostly packaging and applying it to segment scoring and introducing a KL-regularized latent variant. The detection feature is a straightforward spatial pooling of per-pixel confidence maps. Novelty is incremental.
Significance
- The idea is simple and likely useful; the ease of implementation is a plus. However, the improvements over strong baselines are limited, and the strongest result is a modest mAP gain in a now-classic RCNN pipeline. Without broader comparisons and deeper analysis, the impact is uncertain.

Questions for the authors
1) Please provide the exact primal objective with correct notation and constraints, and the practical reduction to liblinear (e.g., duplicating instances with weights).
2) What is the explicit update for ui with the KL regularizer? Are there closed forms? What bounds/regularization prevent ui from hitting 0 or 1?
3) How are hyperparameters (λ, λ_R), g(u), and SVM C chosen? Sensitivity plots?
4) Can you compare against soft-label logistic regression (cross-entropy with targets ui) and soft-label linear SVM baselines from the literature? Any calibration analysis?
5) How robust is u_bb as initialization for ui? Any alternatives (e.g., geometric features predicting IoU; shape priors)?
6) Detection ablations: impact of m, pooling, using superpixels vs CPMC, using only the confidence map vs concatenated with RCNN, and category-wise gains.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, general formulation; easy to adopt.
- Addresses a practically important weak supervision regime.
- Some empirical gains on a canonical detection benchmark.
- Clear path to generalization and integration with other systems.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft/weighted SVM ideas.
- Missing theoretical and algorithmic details; mathematical notation issues.
- Modest and uneven empirical gains; baselines incomplete.
- Clarity and referencing problems.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The core idea is sensible and practically valuable, but the paper in its current form overstates novelty, lacks critical details and comparisons, and the empirical evidence is not strong enough to justify acceptance at NeurIPS. Strengthening the theoretical treatment (u-update, equivalence to soft-label SVM), expanding baselines to include soft-label logistic/SVM methods, adding ablations, fixing notation/references, and improving clarity would significantly improve the submission. Reviewer confidence: medium.
