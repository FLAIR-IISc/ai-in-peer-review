- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for weak supervision where labels are continuous "positiveness" values in [0,1].  
- Models label as Bernoulli with success probability equal to observed positiveness and extends to latent e-SVM when positiveness is unobserved.  
- Applies method to segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, showing improvements over SVC/SVR baselines.  
- Claims connections to weighted SVMs, MIL, and recent vision methods (e.g., Carreira et al., RCNN).  

- Strength: Novel problem formulation addressing continuous label values rather than binary labels.  
- Strength: Clear and well-justified derivation of the expectation loss and a logical latent-variable optimization procedure.  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation across tasks and supervision settings with useful metrics (TAPC, NDCG).  
- Strength: Practical impact with a 1.5% mAP gain on PASCAL VOC 2007 and easy integration into existing pipelines.  
- Strength: Well-structured and readable presentation.  

- Weakness: Core idea is a straightforward extension and insufficiently distinguished from existing weighted SVM formulations.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis of convergence or generalization for the latent e-SVM.  
- Weakness: Comparisons omit more recent weakly supervised methods (e.g., weakly-supervised dual clustering).  
- Weakness: Ambiguity in implementation details and unsubstantiated assumption that regularization keeps latent u near u_bb.  
- Weakness: Performance gains are modest for a NIPS contribution and need stronger justification.  

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to practical value despite limited theoretical novelty.  

- Suggestion: Clarify mathematical differences from weighted SVMs.  
- Suggestion: Add discussion or analysis of convergence/generalization for latent e-SVM.  
- Suggestion: Broaden empirical comparisons to recent weakly supervised approaches.  
- Suggestion: Provide evidence and discussion justifying the KL-divergence regularization and its limitations.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the scope of "weak supervision" and discuss relation to MIL more thoroughly.  

- Conclusion: Technically sound, well-written, and practically valuable paper that would benefit from specified revisions to strengthen theoretical and comparative claims.
