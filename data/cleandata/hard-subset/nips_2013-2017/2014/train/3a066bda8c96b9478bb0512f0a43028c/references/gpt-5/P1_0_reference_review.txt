Summary
The paper proposes Expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a simple and general approach for learning from “soft” labels in [0,1], with a latent-variable extension for weak supervision. For each example i with features xi and “positiveness” ui (e.g., IoU of a segment with a ground-truth instance), the method defines a Bernoulli label Yi with success probability µi=g(ui) (set to µi=ui in experiments). The training loss is the expected hinge loss under Yi: g(ui)·max(0,1−w⊤xi) + (1−g(ui))·max(0,1+w⊤xi), plus ℓ2-regularization. This can be seen as a convex combination of positive and negative hinge losses, yielding a convex objective in w when ui is known. For weak supervision (bounding boxes only), the positiveness ui is treated as latent and estimated by alternating minimization over w and u with a convex regularizer R(u) that is the cross-entropy (KL) to the IoU between segment and ground-truth bounding box ubb.

The approach is evaluated on segment scoring (with two proposed metrics, TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation (CPMC + second-order pooling), and object detection by augmenting R-CNN features with a spatial confidence map pooled from per-pixel segment confidences produced by e-SVM. On PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation data, e-SVM outperforms SVC and slightly outperforms SVR on segment scoring; semantic segmentation accuracy is comparable to SVR. On VOC 2007 detection, concatenating e-SVM confidence-map features with R-CNN yields +1.2 mAP without bbox regression and +1.5 with regression (60.0 vs 58.5).

Quality
- Positive: The basic objective is well-motivated and convex in w for known ui. The latent extension is a clear alternating scheme with a convex subproblem in u when using the proposed KL regularizer. The detection integration via spatial pooling is pragmatic and yields modest improvements over a strong baseline.
- Concerns:
  - The claim that the e-SVM “can be effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver” is imprecise. The objective is not a standard hinge loss with a single label per sample; solving it requires either duplicating each sample with y=±1 and weights g(ui),1−g(ui), or implementing per-example weighted hinge terms. Please clarify the exact reduction/implementation (e.g., LibLinear supports instance weights but not two labels per sample).
  - The latent optimization details are under-specified: constraints on u (explicitly u∈(0,1)), exact update for u, convergence criterion, initialization beyond ubb, and sensitivity to λR. Without regularization, the optimal u collapses to {0,1}; with KL, it will still be pulled strongly toward ubb—please characterize behavior and provide ablations.
  - Experimental gains on segmentation are small (e-SVM “slightly” better than SVR; final semantic segmentation accuracy is “comparable” to SVR). The main measurable benefit is the +1.5 mAP on detection via additional features, but the attribution to e-SVM vs the feature design is unclear. A control using SVR to generate the same confidence maps would help isolate the benefit of the learning objective.
  - The new TAPC is insufficiently specified (threshold grid, averaging procedure) and NDCG is standard; please report per-class and aggregate metrics with error bars or statistical tests.
  - Mathematical presentation has several typos/ambiguities (e.g., regularizer term “λw·1 w⊤w,” missing 1/2, inconsistent notation; sometimes w includes bias, sometimes not). The relationship to WSVMs and “soft-label SVM”/uncertain-label SVM literature is discussed briefly but seems incomplete.

Clarity
- The paper is generally understandable but needs significant polishing:
  - Fix equations (regularizer form, consistent definitions of xi augmentation and w).
  - Precisely define g(·), constraints on u, and the optimization steps (closed-form/prox updates for u with KL, or numerical details).
  - Provide hyperparameters (λw, λR, m, number of iterations), convergence tolerance, and runtime.
  - Some references are malformed or missing details; several citations (e.g., soft/probabilistic labels for SVMs, learning with label proportions, ordinal/label-distribution learning) are relevant and should be added.

Originality
- The core idea—minimizing expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli “soft” label—is natural and appears in spirit in prior work on SVMs with uncertain/soft labels and cost-sensitive/weighted hinge losses. The contribution here is a clean, simple formulation with a latent-u extension regularized by KL to bounding-box IoU and a practical integration into detection via spatial pooling of segment confidences. Novelty is moderate; the paper would benefit from a clearer differentiation from:
  - SVMs with probabilistic/soft labels and label-noise models.
  - WSVM/CS-SVM formulations and mixtures of hinge losses.
  - Weakly supervised segmentation/detection approaches (MIL/latent SVM variants).
Include a thorough related work comparison and, where possible, cite and compare empirically to the closest “soft-label SVM” baselines.

Significance
- As a general recipe for exploiting soft/weak supervision with SVMs, the idea is useful and easy to implement. The detection gains over R-CNN, while modest, are non-trivial given the strength of the baseline. However, the limited benefits over SVR for segmentation, lack of ablations isolating components, and unclear novelty relative to soft-label SVM literature reduce the impact.

Pros
- Simple, convex-in-w objective for soft labels; natural latent extension with convex subproblems.
- Applicable to common weakly supervised settings (segments with only bbox annotations).
- Practical integration into object detection yields measurable improvements.
- Task-agnostic idea potentially transferable beyond vision.

Cons
- Novelty over existing soft/uncertain-label SVMs and WSVMs is not clearly established.
- Under-specified optimization details; questionable claim about using off-the-shelf solvers.
- Limited improvements over SVR in segmentation; missing ablations and rigorous analysis.
- Clarity/notation/reference issues.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify and formally derive the reduction to a standard solver or provide a custom optimization algorithm; include complexity and runtime.
- Add ablations: effect of λR, choice of g(u), initialization, number of alternations, using u=ubb without latent updates, and replacing e-SVM with SVR/SVC in the detection confidence-map pipeline.
- Compare against soft-label/uncertain-label SVM baselines and probabilistic logistic regression with soft labels.
- Provide per-class results and statistical significance; detail TAPC protocol.
- Fix notation, equations, and references; improve writing quality.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The idea is sensible and the detection gains are promising, but the novelty over prior soft-label SVMs is not clearly articulated, the empirical advantages over strong baselines are limited or not isolated, and the paper requires substantial clarification and polishing. With stronger positioning, ablations, and clearer optimization details, this could be a solid contribution.
