- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for weakly supervised learning with continuous "positiveness" and a latent variant for unobserved positiveness.  
- Applies e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection, showing consistent improvements over SVC/SVR baselines.  
- Demonstrates a 1.5% AP improvement when integrated with RCNN for object detection.  
- Theoretical derivation is rigorous; latent e-SVM and its iterative optimization are well-justified.  
- Clearly distinguishes e-SVM from weighted SVMs and MIL by weighting labels instead of data points.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive on PASCAL VOC and uses novel metrics (TAPC, NDCG) targeting segmentation performance.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and uses helpful figures and detailed experimental setup.  
- Addresses a significant practical problem—reducing costly pixel-level annotations by leveraging bounding boxes.  
- Originality is moderate: approach resembles weighted SVM ideas and is more an application-specific extension than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical comparison/explanation of why e-SVM outperforms SVR and limited discussion of relation to recent weighted SVM literature.  
- Omits analysis of computational complexity/training time and sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (e.g., λR, spatial bin choice m).  
- Integration with RCNN is simplistic (feature concatenation) and more sophisticated integrations were not explored.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically significant, reproducible work with minor omissions; recommendation to accept.
