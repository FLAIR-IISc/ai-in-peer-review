- Summary: Proposes e-SVM that models continuous "positiveness" labels via Bernoulli expectation loss and extends to latent e-SVM for unobserved positiveness, applied to semantic segmentation and object detection on PASCAL VOC.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation highlighting the need for weak supervision (bounding boxes vs pixel labels).

- Strength: Technical clarity—simple, well-motivated e-SVM and latent extension with an optimization routine implementable via standard SVM solvers.

- Strength: Empirical validation using standard datasets and baselines, plus two new metrics (TAPC, NDCG) suited to continuous labels.

- Strength: Practical impact—reported 1.5% average AP gain on PASCAL VOC 2007 and easy integration with existing systems like R-CNN.

- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification—no rigorous analysis showing expectation loss is preferable to regression or other alternatives.

- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to relevant prior work, including more direct baselines (e.g., Carreira et al. 2012) and recent weakly supervised deep-learning methods.

- Weakness: Overstated claims—improvements are modest (e.g., TAPC 36.69 vs 35.23) and not transformative relative to recent detection advances.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of the KL-divergence regularizer—choice is neither justified nor compared to alternatives.

- Weakness: Lack of computational analysis—no discussion of runtime or cost of latent e-SVM versus SVC/SVR.

- Overall assessment: Technically sound and practically promising but offers modest gains and limited theoretical novelty; not a major advance.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept.

- Suggested improvement: Add theoretical analysis comparing expectation loss to other approaches for continuous labels.

- Suggested improvement: Provide thorough comparisons with recent weakly supervised/deep learning methods using bounding box supervision.

- Suggested improvement: More clearly discuss limitations and scope of the approach.

- Suggested improvement: Empirically justify or compare different regularization choices.
