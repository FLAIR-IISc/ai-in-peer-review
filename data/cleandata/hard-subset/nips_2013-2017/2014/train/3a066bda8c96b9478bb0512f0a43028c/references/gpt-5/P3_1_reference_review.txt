Summary
This paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from weak and soft supervision where each training instance is associated with a “positiveness” score u in [0,1] (e.g., IoU of a segment hypothesis with a ground-truth instance). The key idea is to treat the class label Y ∈ {−1,+1} as Bernoulli with mean µ = g(u), and minimize the expected hinge loss: g(u)·max(0,1−w⊤x) + (1−g(u))·max(0,1+w⊤x). A latent variant is introduced for weak supervision (only bounding boxes), where u is optimized jointly with w using an alternating procedure and regularized via a KL term that penalizes deviation from u_bb (the IoU of a segment with the ground-truth bounding box). The model is evaluated on segment proposal “confidence prediction” with two proposed metrics (TAPC and NDCG), on semantic segmentation (using CPMC proposals and second-order pooling), and on object detection (augmenting R-CNN with a “spatial confidence map” derived from segment scores). Reported gains include small but consistent improvements over SVC/SVR on segment scoring and +1.2–1.5 mAP over R-CNN on VOC’07.

Strengths
- Clear and practical formulation for incorporating soft labels in max-margin training; the expected hinge loss is intuitive, convex in w for fixed u, and easily optimized.
- Latent extension is straightforward and computationally feasible; the KL regularizer is convex and produces a well-behaved BCD scheme.
- The spatial confidence map feature is a simple, general mechanism to inject segmentation-derived cues into window-based detection; works with any proposal method and base detector.
- Empirical evaluation on VOC segmentation/detection demonstrates consistent improvements over SVC/SVR baselines for segment scoring and non-trivial gains on object detection when combined with R-CNN.

Weaknesses and missing related work
- Novelty is limited. Learning with soft/uncertain labels in SVMs is well-studied (e.g., fuzzy SVM, probabilistic/ambiguous labels SVM, cost-sensitive and instance-weighted SVMs). The proposed loss is equivalent to training two weighted hinge terms per instance (one for +1 with weight g(u), one for −1 with weight 1−g(u)), which reduces to an instance-weighted SVM on duplicated examples; this should be acknowledged and positioned relative to fuzzy/soft-label SVMs and “SVM with probabilistic labels.” The paper instead contrasts only with SVC/SVR and WSVM in a way that overstates the conceptual difference.
- The latent model’s KL regularizer anchors u tightly to u_bb, which is a weak proxy for the true IoU with the object mask. This may bias u to be near the box overlap rather than discovering better soft labels; no ablation examines the effect of λ_R or alternative regularizers/priors. A closed-form per-instance update for u exists when g(u)=u (minimizing a linear term plus KL), but it is not derived or discussed.
- Attribution of detection gains to e-SVM is unclear. The proposed spatial confidence map is a feature-level contribution; there is no ablation comparing the same feature constructed from SVR/SVC vs e-SVM scores, nor comparing alternative segmentation cues. Thus, it is hard to isolate the benefit of the e-SVM loss from the benefit of adding any segment-based heatmap.
- Experimental details are insufficient to reproduce and to assess robustness: exact λ_w, λ_R, stopping criteria, initialization, solver settings, training time, and sensitivity to g(u) are missing. For segmentation, the reported pixel accuracy numbers are “comparable” to SVR but earlier sections claimed e-SVM outperforms SVR; class-wise or IoU metrics are not provided. For detection, the baseline R-CNN variant and full per-class AP table are not shown.
- Claims about “any standard SVM solver (e.g., LibLinear)” are imprecise. LibLinear supports class weights but not arbitrary per-example weights; the expected loss requires per-example weights for the duplicated +/− samples. The practical workaround (duplication with instance-specific C_i) or a custom solver should be described.
- The “two new evaluation criteria” are essentially standard: NDCG is well known; averaging AP over thresholds (TAPC) is a reasonable heuristic but methodology details (threshold grid, calibration) are missing.
- Presentation quality: numerous typos/formatting issues; inconsistent notation (e.g., λ_w term written as “λw · 1 w⊤w”); incomplete/misformatted references; ambiguous definition of u_bb (IoU of a segment with a bounding box is not a standard IoU and should be defined precisely).

Quality
Technically sound at a high level; the expected hinge and the convexity properties are correct for g(u)=u and convex R(u). However, theoretical support (calibration, robustness to mis-specified u, convergence guarantees beyond BCD stationary points) is absent. The empirical evaluation suggests usefulness but lacks key ablations to support causal claims.

Clarity
Core idea is easy to grasp, but the manuscript suffers from notation errors, missing derivations, and incomplete experimental details. The latent update for u and its computational form should be provided. The construction of spatial confidence maps deserves a more precise definition and complexity analysis.

Originality
Moderate. The expected hinge over a Bernoulli label with known mean is a natural instantiation of soft-label SVMs; the latent variant with KL anchoring is a straightforward EM-like extension. The spatial confidence feature is a simple application-layer idea.

Significance
Potentially useful as a practical recipe for learning from soft/weak labels without heavy engineering, and for augmenting detectors with segmentation-derived confidence maps. The reported detection gains are promising, but the lack of ablations weakens the generality of the claim.

Suggestions for improvement
- Position the work against soft/fuzzy/ambiguous-label SVM literature and clarify the equivalence to instance-weighted duplicated samples; cite and compare empirically where possible.
- Derive the per-instance u update for the KL-regularized subproblem; report its behavior and sensitivity to λ_R and to initialization u_bb.
- Provide ablations: (i) SVR vs e-SVM for the spatial confidence map, (ii) alternative g(u) (linear vs nonlinear), (iii) effect of λ_R, (iv) using different proposal methods, (v) using segmentation heatmaps without e-SVM.
- Clarify evaluation protocols, report full per-class AP for detection and per-class IoU for segmentation, and include training/inference runtimes.
- Fix notation, typos, and references; define u_bb precisely.

Pros
- Conceptually simple, general, and easy to implement.
- Works with weak supervision and integrates with existing pipelines.
- Empirical gains on a strong detection baseline.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to established soft-label SVMs.
- Insufficient ablations to isolate contributions.
- Presentation and reproducibility gaps.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The idea is reasonable and the results are encouraging, but the paper overstates novelty, lacks critical related work and ablations, and needs clearer derivations and experimental details. Strengthening positioning, analysis, and empirical evidence could make this a solid contribution.
