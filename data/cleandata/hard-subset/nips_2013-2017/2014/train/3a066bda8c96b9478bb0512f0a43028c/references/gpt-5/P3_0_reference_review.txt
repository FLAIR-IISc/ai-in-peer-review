Summary
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a simple extension of the hinge loss to soft labels in [0,1]. Each training example i has a “positiveness” u_i (e.g., segment IoU with pixel-level ground truth). The loss is the expected hinge under a Bernoulli label with success probability µ_i=g(u_i) (set to u_i in experiments), yielding a convex objective in w: sum_i [µ_i max(0,1−f_i) + (1−µ_i) max(0,1+f_i)] + λ||w||^2. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), u_i is treated as latent and optimized jointly with w using alternating minimization, with a convex regularizer that is the cross-entropy between u_i and the segment–box IoU u_i^bb. The method is evaluated for (i) segment proposal “confidence” prediction with new metrics (TAPC and NDCG), (ii) semantic segmentation using CPMC proposals, and (iii) object detection by concatenating a spatial confidence map feature derived from e-SVM with RCNN features, yielding +1.2 to +1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Strengths
- Clear, simple objective: expected hinge under a soft/probabilistic label; convex in w and extendable to a latent-variable setting with convex subproblems.
- Practical weakly supervised extension (continuous u with KL regularization to box IoU) is computationally straightforward and stable compared to hard latent SVM on u∈{0,1}.
- The spatial confidence map feature is a sensible way to aggregate segment confidences for window-based detection and yields consistent mAP improvements when fused with RCNN.
- The direct, task-agnostic segment ranking evaluations (TAPC, NDCG) are reasonable and complementary to downstream metrics.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: the core loss is equivalent to training on two duplicated examples per instance with opposite labels and instance-specific weights µ_i and 1−µ_i. This sits very close to existing “soft-label/probabilistic-label SVM,” “fuzzy SVM,” and cost-sensitive/instance-weighted SVM formulations; these connections are not adequately cited or discussed.
- The choice µ_i = u_i (IoU) is ad hoc; there is no calibration or learning of g(u), and no analysis of sensitivity to g. IoU is not a probability of the segment being a true positive.
- The latent u optimization is under-specified: no derivation of the u-update (closed form? per-sample 1D convex minimization?), constraints (0<u<1), or computational cost; no sensitivity to λ_R or to the initialization u^bb.
- Claims about “effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver (e.g., LibLinear)” are dubious: the per-example asymmetric hinge weights generally require per-instance weighting and/or data duplication, which vanilla LibLinear does not provide by default. Implementation details are missing.
- Empirical evidence is modest and lacks ablations: e-SVM is only slightly better than SVR on segment ranking; for segmentation accuracy, results are comparable to SVR; the detection gains could stem from the spatial pooling feature rather than the e-SVM loss per se. There is no comparison using the same feature pipeline with SVR/SVC-trained segment scorers.
- Clarity and presentation: numerous typos, incomplete/misformatted references, and missing details for TAPC (threshold grid), optimization hyperparameters, and runtime. Figures and tables are referenced but not fully shown in the text excerpt.

Quality
- Technically sound at a high level; the convexity arguments (for fixed u or w) are fine when g(u)=u and the entropy regularizer is used. However, important derivations (u-update), solver details, and hyperparameter tuning are missing. The claim of state-of-the-art detection is based on a small mAP gain and should be supported with stronger statistical and ablation analyses.

Clarity
- The core idea is easy to follow, but the manuscript needs substantial polishing. Provide: (i) explicit equivalence to weighted hinge on duplicated examples; (ii) details for the u-step (objective per sample, derivative, solver, constraints); (iii) precise TAPC definition; (iv) full training details (λ, λ_R, g(.), convergence criteria, compute).

Originality
- The expected hinge with probabilistic labels and related fuzzy/soft-label SVMs are well-studied. Treating segment IoU as a “soft label” is intuitive but not novel. The KL regularization toward box IoU for weak supervision is a reasonable twist, but conceptually close to label-regularized latent SVM. The paper should position itself against prior work on SVMs with uncertain/soft labels and fuzzy SVM, and on weakly supervised segmentation from bounding boxes.

Significance
- The empirical gains in detection (+1.5 mAP) are potentially useful, but not compelling without ablations (e.g., replacing e-SVM by SVR in the same feature pipeline). For segmentation, the method is comparable to SVR. Overall impact is limited by novelty and by modest improvements.

Suggestions for improvement
- Situate e-SVM relative to: soft/probabilistic-label SVM, fuzzy SVM, label-noise robust hinge, and latent SVM; add appropriate citations and a formal equivalence or distinctions.
- Justify/learn the mapping g(u); show sensitivity to g (identity vs. calibrated/logit). Consider learning g from data.
- Provide full optimization details for the u-step and complexity; report sensitivity to λ_R and initialization.
- Clarify how you used standard SVM solvers; if using data duplication with per-instance weights, describe it explicitly.
- Add ablations: (a) use SVR/SVC-trained segment scores to build the same spatial confidence map; (b) vary m, pooling, and feature encoding; (c) per-class analyses and statistical tests.
- Improve writing, figures, and references.

Pros and cons for acceptance
Pros:
- Simple, clear idea with a practical weakly supervised extension.
- Some empirical gains on a strong detector.
- Potentially broadly applicable to other soft-label settings.

Cons:
- Novelty over existing soft/fuzzy/weighted SVMs is limited.
- Under-specified optimization and solver claims.
- Modest empirical improvements and missing ablations.
- Presentation quality issues.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). With stronger positioning vs prior art, clearer optimization details, and rigorous ablations isolating the contribution of e-SVM, this could become a solid contribution.

Reviewer confidence: High.
