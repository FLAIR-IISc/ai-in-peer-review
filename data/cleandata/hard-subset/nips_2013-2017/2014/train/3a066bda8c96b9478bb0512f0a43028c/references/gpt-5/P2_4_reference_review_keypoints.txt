- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) using soft “positiveness” scores µi∈[0,1] and minimizing expected hinge loss, solvable by standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Introduces a latent e-SVM variant for weak supervision where ui are latent and regularized toward IoU-based initialization via a convex KL/cross-entropy term, optimized by alternating between w and u.  
- Applies method to segment scoring (CPMC+O2P features) and object detection by forming spatial confidence maps pooled into m×m bins and concatenated with RCNN features.  
- Proposes two segment-ranking metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for evaluation.  
- Empirically, e-SVM beats SVC and slightly outperforms SVR on segment ranking, achieves 36.8% vs 27.7% in strong vs weak semantic segmentation, and gives ~+1.5 mAP on VOC07 RCNN after bbox regression.  
- Positioned between full supervision and MIL-style weak supervision, but connection to prior soft-label/probabilistic/cost-sensitive SVM literature is underdeveloped.  
- Strength: simple, intuitive expected-hinge formulation that integrates with standard SVM tooling and pipelines.  
- Strength: practical gains in detection and an implementable spatial confidence-map feature for box-based detectors.  
- Strength: introduces task-agnostic segment-ranking metrics that better reflect ranking than binary classification.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—close to existing soft-label/cost-weighted SVM formulations with insufficient clarification of novelty.  
- Weakness: latent scheme appears heuristic—g(u)=u fixed, identifiability and calibration not analyzed, and u-update optimization details omitted.  
- Weakness: clarity and rigor issues—misformatted equations, incomplete references, missing hyperparameters, stopping criteria, initialization, and runtime details.  
- Weakness: experiments lack sufficient ablations, statistical significance, and fair-baseline controls (e.g., SVR in same pipeline, latent vs non-latent effects, pooling choices, bbox regression parity).  
- Quality: technically plausible (block-convex objective) but lacks theoretical support (generalization/calibration) and missing optimization details for u-step.  
- Clarity: paper needs significant editing and full algorithmic/hyperparameter disclosure; TAPC/NDCG computation protocols need clearer description.  
- Originality: moderate to low; core idea aligns with known soft-label SVM work, with incremental latent regularizer and spatial map feature.  
- Significance: potentially useful in practice if mAP gains are robust, but limited by weak ablations and narrow evaluation scope.  
- Key questions: how is the u-update solved (closed form, constraints, stopping criteria), sensitivity to λR and g(u)=u, comparisons using SVR-derived spatial maps, and baseline parity for RCNN bbox regression?  
- Suggestions: strengthen literature positioning and citations, provide full optimization/hyperparameter details and code, add thorough ablations and sensitivity analyses, and substantially improve writing and references.  
- Overall: borderline reject—practical and simple but incremental, unclear novelty, and insufficient clarity/experimental rigor; could be competitive after stronger positioning, cleaner presentation, and thorough ablations.
