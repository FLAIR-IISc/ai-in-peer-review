- Proposes e-SVM, an SVM variant using expectation loss to learn from weakly supervised data with continuous “positiveness” scores.  
- Extends to a latent e-SVM for fully weakly supervised scenarios where positiveness is unobserved.  
- Treats positiveness as a probability from a Bernoulli distribution and optimizes an expectation-based loss.  
- Correctly situates work relative to MIL and weighted SVMs, highlighting that e-SVM weights labels not examples.  
- Builds on existing proposal/detection frameworks (CPMC, superpixels, R-CNN) and integrates as a complementary component.  
- The formulation is novel and well-motivated versus naive binarization (SVC) or regression (SVR).  
- Theoretical derivation and optimization appear sound, with convexity enabling efficient solutions.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements across segment classification, segmentation, and detection tasks.  
- Notable ~1.5% AP improvement on object detection when applied on top of R-CNN.  
- The method is general and flexible, working with different proposal detectors, features, and base detectors.  
- Paper is well-written and illustrated, making the core ideas accessible.  
- Latent e-SVM effectively addresses scenarios with unobserved positiveness, common in weak supervision.  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies to quantify contributions of individual components and design choices.  
- Hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity (e.g., regularization λR) are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Dependence on segment proposal quality is acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed across different generators.  
- Use of NDCG for segment classification is unusual and needs justification versus standard metrics (AUC, precision@k).  
- Detection gains are modest on a strong baseline; more analysis of error types reduced by e-SVM is needed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with solid results but requires minor revisions (ablation, hyperparameter and metric justification).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
