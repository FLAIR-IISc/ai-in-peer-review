Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes an “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM) for learning from soft labels in [0,1] (e.g., IoU-based “positiveness” of a segment) and a latent extension for weak supervision when those soft labels are unobserved (only bounding boxes available). The core idea is to minimize the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label Y with success probability µ=g(u), yielding, per sample, µ·max(0,1−w⊤x)+(1−µ)·max(0,1+w⊤x). For weak supervision, u is treated as a latent variable initialized by IoU to a bounding box (ubb), and optimized jointly with w via alternating minimization with a convex cross-entropy regularizer that keeps u near ubb. The method is evaluated on segment scoring (new TAPC and NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation, and object detection by constructing spatial confidence maps from segment scores and concatenating them with R-CNN features, reporting ~1.2–1.5 AP improvement on PASCAL VOC 2007.

This connects to several existing lines: SVMs with soft/probabilistic/fuzzy labels and uncertain labels; instance-weighted or cost-sensitive SVMs; latent SVMs (alternating minimization); weakly supervised segmentation/detection beyond MIL assumptions; and feature fusion for detection (e.g., segment-based cues with window-based detectors).

Strengths
- Problem relevance: Addressing learning from weak/soft supervision is important for large-scale vision.
- Simple, convex base loss: The observed-positiveness formulation is a clean expected hinge loss that is convex and easy to optimize.
- Practical weak-supervision extension: The KL regularizer on latent u is a reasonable way to avoid hard 0/1 collapse and to encode prior from bounding boxes.
- Empirical signal: On VOC07 detection with R-CNN, the added spatial confidence feature yields small but consistent AP gains; segment scoring metrics show modest improvements over SVR and SVC.
- Generality: The approach is modular with respect to proposal generators, features, and base detectors.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: The “expected hinge with Bernoulli labels” is essentially a well-known soft-label/uncertain-label SVM objective (a convex combination of positive/negative hinge losses), closely related to fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVMs and cost-sensitive SVMs by duplicating examples with weights µ and 1−µ. The paper does not clearly differentiate from this literature nor cite key prior work.
- Methodological gaps: The claim that “it can be solved by any standard SVM solver” is incomplete—standard solvers do not, by default, support two label-dependent per-example weights; one must either implement per-class-weighted hinges or reduce to a weighted duplicated dataset. This reduction and its implications are not shown.
- Latent optimization details: The u-update is left unspecified beyond convexity; there is no closed-form update or numerical scheme described, no constraints on u (0<ui<1), and no convergence analysis beyond alternating minimization. Sensitivity to λR and initialization is not studied.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow and somewhat confounded: 
  - Segment scoring gains over SVR are small; semantic segmentation results are “comparable” to SVR rather than clearly better, contradicting earlier claims.
  - For detection, the improvement could be due to the new spatial confidence feature rather than specifically to e-SVM; there is no ablation replacing e-SVM with SVR/SVC to generate the same feature.
  - Only VOC07/11 are used; no robustness analysis (variance, cross-validation), and hyperparameters (λR, g(u), m, pooling choices) are not reported or tuned transparently.
- Clarity and presentation: Numerous typos, malformed equations (e.g., regularizer notation, odd “λw·1/w⊤w”), missing figure/table references, and incomplete/malformed citations make it hard to reproduce and assess. Important implementation details are missing.

Quality
- Technical soundness of the observed-label case is fine; the loss is convex and standard tools apply. The latent case is plausible but underspecified; without algorithmic details and ablations, it is hard to assess stability and reproducibility. Claims are only modestly supported: small gains on TAPC/NDCG and ~1–1.5 AP on VOC07 without strong controls.

Clarity
- The paper needs substantial editing. Provide a correct, conventional regularizer (e.g., 1/2||w||^2), define g(u), constraints on u, and the exact solver or reduction to standard SVM. Detail the u-update (derivative, projection to (0,1), numerical method), stopping criteria, and hyperparameter selection. Figures/tables and references should be complete and correct.

Originality
- The base e-SVM objective is not novel; it matches prior formulations for SVMs with soft/probabilistic/fuzzy labels and uncertain labels. The latent extension with a KL regularizer and its application to segment scoring is more tailored, but still resembles standard alternating-minimization latent models. The spatial confidence map is a reasonable engineering contribution but similar in spirit to spatial pooling of region cues in detection.

Significance
- Practical impact is limited by small empirical gains and incomplete comparisons. The method could be useful as a simple soft-label SVM baseline, but the contribution falls short of a substantial advance for NeurIPS without stronger, clearer evidence and positioning against prior art.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Precisely relate your loss to fuzzy/soft-label SVMs and uncertain-label SVMs; cite and contrast with that literature. Is your loss identical to weighting two hinge terms? Provide the reduction to a standard solver.
- Describe and analyze the u-update: closed-form? Newton? Constraints and numerical stability near 0/1? Complexity?
- Provide ablations: SVR/SVC-generated confidence maps vs e-SVM for detection; effect of λR, initialization, m, pooling; effect without the modified bbox regression; variance across runs.
- Clarify datasets, splits, and hyperparameter tuning; report per-class gains and statistical significance.
- Improve writing, fix equations and references, and include all figures/tables.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses a relevant weak-supervision setting with a simple, convex objective.
- Some empirical gains on a standard benchmark.
- Potentially useful baseline for learning with soft labels.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft/uncertain/fuzzy-label SVMs and latent SVMs.
- Underspecified optimization for the latent case; unclear reproducibility.
- Modest and confounded empirical improvements; missing ablations and broader comparisons.
- Clarity and presentation issues.

Overall: I lean toward rejection in its current form. With clearer positioning relative to prior work, fuller algorithmic details, and stronger, controlled experiments demonstrating that e-SVM (not just the engineered feature) drives the gains, this could be a solid contribution.
