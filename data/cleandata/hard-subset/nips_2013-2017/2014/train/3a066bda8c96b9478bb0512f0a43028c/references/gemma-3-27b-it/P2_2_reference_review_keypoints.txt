- Proposes e-SVM, which treats proposal "positiveness" as a random variable and optimizes an expectation-based loss for weakly supervised labels.  
- Demonstrates e-SVM on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection using PASCAL VOC.  
- Novel extension of SVMs to continuous/probabilistic labels with a connection to weighted SVMs.  
- Provides a clear theoretical formulation and a latent-variable extension.  
- Strong empirical results, consistently outperforming SVC/SVR, with ~1.5% average improvement in object detection.  
- Method is general and compatible with various proposal methods, features, and base detectors.  
- Paper is well written and well organized.  
- Novelty is incremental—an intelligent adaptation of SVMs rather than a paradigm shift.  
- Comparisons are limited to SVC/SVR; lacks evaluation against more recent state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods and advanced MIL approaches.  
- Regularization in latent e-SVM (KL divergence) seems crucial but is not fully justified; no hyperparameter sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Iterative optimization (Algorithm 1) may be computationally expensive; lacks discussion of complexity and scalability.  
- Missing ablation studies to quantify contributions of individual components (e.g., spatial confidence map).  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Pro arguments: addresses an important problem, theoretically sound, strong empirical evidence, clear exposition.  
- Con arguments: incremental novelty, limited SOTA comparisons, insufficient analysis of hyperparameters and computational cost.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add broader comparisons, discuss computational costs and hyperparameter sensitivity, and include ablation studies.
