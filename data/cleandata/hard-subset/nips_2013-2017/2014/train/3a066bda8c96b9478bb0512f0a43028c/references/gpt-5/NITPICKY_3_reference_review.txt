Summary
The paper proposes “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM) for learning from weak or soft supervision. Given segment proposals with “positiveness” u∈[0,1] (e.g., IoU with ground-truth), labels Y∈{±1} are treated as Bernoulli with P(Y=1)=µ=g(u) (set to u), and the expected hinge loss E[max(0,1−Y f(x))] is minimized, which equals µ·max(0,1−f)+ (1−µ)·max(0,1+f). A latent extension treats u as unobserved when only bounding boxes are available, alternating between optimizing w (a standard linear SVM subproblem) and u with a convex KL regularizer R(u)=∑ KL(Bern(u_bb)||Bern(u)), where u_bb is the IoU between segment and GT box. The method is evaluated on segment proposal scoring (new metrics TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation (CPMC + second-order pooling), and object detection by concatenating a spatial confidence map feature with R-CNN features, reporting up to +1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Relation to prior work
The expected hinge formulation with probabilistic/soft labels is closely related to weighted/uncertain-label SVMs and can be implemented by duplicating each example with labels +1 and −1 and instance weights µ and 1−µ (WSVM). The latent formulation plus an entropy-like regularizer resembles latent SVM with a strong anchoring prior on latent soft labels. In weak supervision for segmentation/detection, there is substantial prior art (MIL, MIM, weakly supervised segmentation/detection); the paper cites some of it but misses directly related “probabilistic labels/soft labels SVM” literature and does not clarify how e-SVM differs in substance from WSVM or soft-label hinge risk.

Strengths
- Simple, convex expected loss per sample; easy to implement with standard SVM machinery by reweighting or duplication.
- Reasonable latent extension with a convex-in-u subproblem using KL regularization.
- Practical application to object detection via a class-specific spatial confidence map that yields modest gains when concatenated with R-CNN features.
- Attempts to evaluate segment scoring directly (TAPC, NDCG), beyond task-specific metrics.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and clarity
- The objective is written ambiguously: “λw · 1 wTw + N N∑…” appears malformed. Please specify the exact regularization (e.g., λ/2||w||^2) and the averaging factor 1/N.
- The claim “can be effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver (e.g., LibLinear)” is not substantiated. LibLinear supports class weights, not arbitrary per-example positive/negative hinge weights. The expected hinge can be implemented by duplicating each sample into two weighted examples; this reduction should be stated explicitly, with any practical implications (memory/time).
- The latent step: the u-update admits a simple closed-form or efficient 1D Newton update when g(u)=u; no update formula, step size, or convergence/stopping criterion is provided. Hyperparameter λ_R and its tuning are not specified.
- Theoretical properties (convexity per block is noted) but no convergence guarantees for the alternating scheme, nor discussion of local minima beyond a brief remark.

Novelty
- The expected hinge loss with probabilistic labels is not new; it is essentially WSVM with duplicated examples or SVM with soft/uncertain labels. The paper asserts a difference from WSVM (“weights labels instead of data points”), but the losses are equivalent up to duplication/reweighting. The novelty claim should be tempered and prior art on soft-label/uncertain-label SVMs cited and contrasted.
- The latent extension is a straightforward alternating scheme with a strong KL prior to the box IoU; similar entropy-regularized latent formulations have appeared in weakly supervised learning.

Empirical evaluation
- Improvements on segment scoring over SVR are small (TAPC +1.46, NDCG +0.0098). For semantic segmentation, performance is only “comparable” to SVR; no per-class breakdown or statistical significance.
- For detection, +1.2 to +1.5 mAP is modest. Crucially, there is no ablation showing the same spatial confidence map built from SVR/SVC instead of e-SVM, so the contribution of e-SVM versus the feature engineering is unclear.
- Computational cost is not discussed: computing per-pixel maxima over many segments and classes, building M×K features per window, and the added mapping (explicit additive kernels) may be substantial. No runtime or memory analysis is provided.
- TAPC/NDCG definitions lack specifics: threshold grid, averaging across classes/images, relevance scaling for NDCG, etc. Reproducibility is hindered.

Weak supervision assumptions
- Using u_bb (IoU with GT bounding box) as an anchor for u is problematic because boxes include background; this biases u upward for background segments within the box. The strong KL regularizer may cement this bias. There's no analysis of sensitivity to λ_R or to box tightness.

Writing and formatting
- Numerous typos/grammar (“continues,” “segmentic,” “positiveness”), inconsistent notation, undefined symbols, and several malformed equations.
- References are severely malformed (missing years, venues, incomplete author lists, stray commas/letters). This is below NIPS standards.
- Algorithm 1 lacks important details (stopping criteria, initialization alternatives, schedule, convergence plot).

Qualitative assessment
- Quality: Partial; core idea is sound but mostly known; derivations and implementation details are incomplete; empirical support is limited and lacks critical ablations.
- Clarity: Below standard; many notation/formatting issues impede understanding; reproducibility details are missing.
- Originality: Limited; the expected hinge with soft labels is essentially equivalent to WSVM with duplication; latent extension is incremental.
- Significance: Modest; small empirical gains; unclear that e-SVM is necessary versus SVR/WSVM; impact likely limited.

Questions for authors
- Provide a precise equivalence/reduction from e-SVM expected hinge to a standard weighted linear SVM (duplication, instance weights), and how you implemented it in LibLinear.
- Give the closed-form or algorithm for the u-update, λ_R values, initialization variants, and convergence behavior.
- Compare detection using the same spatial map built from SVR and from SVC(τ) to isolate the benefit of e-SVM.
- Detail TAPC/NDCG computation, thresholds, and provide code or sufficient details for reproduction.
- Report runtime/memory for training and inference, including the spatial confidence map pipeline.
- Clarify and correct all equations, notation, and references.

Pros
- Simple formulation; convex subproblems.
- Practical weakly supervised extension.
- Some empirical gains on detection.
- Attempts to directly evaluate segment scoring.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to WSVM/soft-label SVM.
- Many missing details; unclear implementation; malformed equations.
- Weak empirical evidence (small gains; missing ablations).
- Poor writing and reference formatting; reproducibility concerns.

Recommendation
Reject. While the idea is sensible and potentially useful, the novelty over existing soft/weighted SVM formulations is minimal, the paper has numerous technical and presentation issues, and the empirical evidence does not convincingly demonstrate a significant advance. Substantial revisions, clear theoretical/algorithmic clarification, stronger and fairer experiments (including ablations), and professional formatting are needed.
