- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous [0,1] labels by treating labels as Bernoulli random variables with expectation loss.  
- Proposes latent e-SVM for settings where "positiveness" is unobserved (e.g., only bounding boxes), with an iterative optimization algorithm.  
- Mathematical formulation is sound, with a regularizer to avoid collapsing to binary solutions.  
- Experimental evaluation on PASCAL VOC 2007/2011 shows consistent improvements over SVC/SVR, including a reported ~1.5% AP gain in detection.  
- Introduces TAPC and NDCG metrics for segment proposal classification.  
- Lacks statistical validation (no significance tests or run variances) weakening confidence in reported gains.  
- Insufficient sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (λ, λR) and some missing implementation details harming reproducibility.  
- Could present more ablation studies to isolate the contribution of e-SVM versus other implementation factors.  
- Clear, well-organized writing and consistent notation, but needs more intuitive explanation of the expectation loss.  
- Original idea is novel and distinct from MIL/WSVM, but the related-work survey omits relevant prior papers (e.g., privileged information, probabilistic SVM).  
- High practical significance: versatile method that can reduce labeling cost and apply across segmentation/detection pipelines.  
- Strengths: novel formulation, clear distinction from related work, new metrics, consistent empirical gains, practical applicability.  
- Weaknesses: missing statistical tests, incomplete related-work comparison, limited theoretical analysis, and omitted implementation/hyperparameter details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add significance testing, hyperparameter details, and expand comparisons to recent weakly supervised methods.
