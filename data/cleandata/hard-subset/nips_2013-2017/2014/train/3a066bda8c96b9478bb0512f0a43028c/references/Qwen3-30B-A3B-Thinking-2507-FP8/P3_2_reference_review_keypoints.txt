- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from continuous "positiveness" labels with basic (observed) and latent (unobserved) variants.  
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection (PASCAL VOC), claiming better performance than SVC and SVR.  
- Technically sound algorithmic formulation with reasonable theoretical foundation extending SVMs to continuous labels.  
- Optimization for latent e-SVM is well-described and appears convex under chosen regularization, but convergence is not proven.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis justifying the chosen regularization and absence of convergence proofs.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive but performance gains are modest (e.g., 1.5% AP on detection).  
- No ablation studies to isolate contributions of key components.  
- Writing and organization are clear; motivation for moving beyond SVC and SVR is compelling.  
- Connections to weighted SVMs are treated superficially and IoU as the positiveness metric is not justified.  
- Hyperparameter choices (λ, λR) are not adequately justified.  
- Idea of expectation loss for continuous labels is somewhat novel but overall contribution is incremental.  
- Insufficient differentiation and comparison with recent weakly supervised and weighted SVM related work.  
- Problem addressed is important, but significance is weakened by modest empirical gains and limited theoretical novelty.  
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, comprehensive experiments, reproducible implementation details, and a clean expectation loss concept.  
- Critical weaknesses: overstated contributions, insufficient comparisons to relevant work, lack of theoretical justification, and modest results.  
- Recommendation: Reject; paper could be improved with stronger theory, more comparisons, ablations, and clearer evidence of impact.
