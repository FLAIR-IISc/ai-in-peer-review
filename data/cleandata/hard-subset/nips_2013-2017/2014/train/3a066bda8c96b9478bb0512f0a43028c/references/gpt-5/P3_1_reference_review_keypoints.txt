- Paper proposes e-SVM: expected hinge loss over Bernoulli labels with mean g(u) for soft/weak supervision, plus a latent variant that jointly optimizes u with KL regularization to u_bb.
- Evaluation on segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection (augmenting R-CNN with spatial confidence maps) shows small consistent gains and +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC’07.
- Strength: clear, practical formulation for incorporating soft labels; expected hinge is intuitive, convex in w for fixed u, and easily optimized.
- Strength: latent extension is straightforward, KL regularizer is convex, and the block-coordinate-descent (BCD) scheme is computationally feasible.
- Strength: spatial confidence map is a simple, general feature to inject segmentation cues into window-based detection.
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate consistent improvements over SVC/SVR baselines and non-trivial detection gains when combined with R-CNN.
- Weakness: novelty is limited—soft/fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVMs and instance-weighted SVMs closely related; expected loss reduces to weighted duplicated hinge terms.
- Weakness: paper fails to position and compare to prior work on soft/ambiguous labels and SVMs with probabilistic labels.
- Weakness: KL regularizer may overly anchor u to u_bb (a weak proxy for true IoU), potentially biasing u and limiting discovery of better soft labels.
- Weakness: no ablation on λ_R, no exploration of alternative regularizers/priors, and no derivation of the closed-form per-instance u update when g(u)=u.
- Weakness: attribution of detection gains is unclear—no ablation comparing spatial maps built from SVR/SVC vs e-SVM or other segmentation cues.
- Weakness: insufficient experimental details for reproducibility (λ_w, λ_R, stopping criteria, initialization, solver settings, training time, sensitivity to g(u)).
- Weakness: segmentation metrics are inconsistently reported (pixel accuracy claims conflict with earlier statements); class-wise IoU or per-class results missing.
- Weakness: claim that standard solvers like LibLinear suffice is imprecise—per-example weights/duplication required and should be described.
- Weakness: the proposed evaluation criteria are not novel (NDCG is standard; TAPC is a heuristic) and methodological details are missing.
- Weakness: presentation issues—typos, inconsistent notation, misformatted references, and ambiguous definition of u_bb.
- Quality: technically sound at a high level, but lacks theoretical analysis (calibration, robustness to mis-specified u, convergence beyond BCD stationary points).
- Clarity: core idea is clear but important derivations (latent u update) and precise construction/complexity of spatial confidence maps are missing.
- Originality: moderate—expected hinge and KL-anchored latent variant are natural/straightforward extensions of existing ideas.
- Significance: potentially useful and practical, but limited by lack of ablations and unclear causal attribution of gains.
- Suggestions: position against soft/fuzzy SVM literature, derive per-instance u update, and empirically compare to instance-weighted/duplicated SVM formulations.
- Suggestions: add ablations for (i) SVR vs e-SVM spatial maps, (ii) alternative g(u), (iii) effect of λ_R, (iv) different proposal methods, and (v) segmentation heatmaps without e-SVM.
- Suggestions: report full per-class AP and IoU, provide training/inference runtimes and solver details, and fix notation/typos and define u_bb precisely.
- Pros: conceptually simple, easy to implement, integrates with existing pipelines, and shows empirical gains on a strong detection baseline.
- Cons: limited novelty relative to established soft-label SVMs, insufficient ablations to isolate contributions, and presentation/reproducibility gaps.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger positioning, derivations, ablations, and improved experimental detail.
