- Overall: Proposes e-SVM to learn from continuous “positiveness” values; theoretically sound, empirically better than SVC/SVR; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem framing: Tackles weak supervision by bridging fully supervised and MIL scenarios using continuous positiveness labels.  
- Relation to prior work: Properly situates e-SVM relative to MIL, SVC, SVR, and weighted SVMs, highlighting key differences.  
- Novelty: e-SVM’s extension to handle continuous positiveness is a meaningful and useful generalization of standard SVMs.  
- Theoretical soundness: Loss derivation and latent-variable extension are clear and well-motivated.  
- Empirical validation: Shows consistent improvements on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection (e.g., ~1.5% AP gain), evaluated on PASCAL VOC.  
- Generality: Method adaptable across segment proposal methods, feature types, and base detectors.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is generally clear, organized, and readable.  
- Modest improvements: Performance gains are statistically significant but not large; possible limits from segment proposals need discussion.  
- Latent-variable regularization: Use of KL-divergence regularizer for u seems arbitrary; requires ablation with alternatives (L1, L2) and sensitivity analysis.  
- Limited baselines: Comparisons restricted to SVC/SVR and MIL variants; should compare to more recent state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods.  
- Spatial confidence map detail: Description of spatial confidence map and additive-kernel approximation is brief and needs expansion and impact analysis.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No analysis of sensitivity (e.g., λR); hyperparameter tuning discussion is missing.  
- Algorithm clarity: Algorithm 1’s stopping criterion (“converge”) should be precisely specified.  
- Equation clarity: Explain the motivation for adding R(u) to the loss more explicitly.  
- Implementation details: Provide more detail on second-order pooling features used for semantic segmentation.  
- Figures: Improve Figure 1 labeling and visual clarity.  
- For acceptance: Novel, sound formulation with consistent gains and broad applicability; well-written.  
- Against acceptance: Gains modest, latent-variable regularization under-justified, and comparisons to recent SOTA are limited.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focusing on deeper analysis of latent-variable regularization and broader comparisons to recent methods.
