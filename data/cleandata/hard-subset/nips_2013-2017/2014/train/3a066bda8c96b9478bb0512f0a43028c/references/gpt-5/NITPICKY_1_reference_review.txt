Summary
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a variant of the hinge-loss SVM that treats the label Y_i ∈ {−1,+1} as a Bernoulli random variable with success probability μ_i = g(u_i), where u_i ∈ [0,1] is a “positiveness” score (IoU in experiments). The expected hinge loss yields per-sample contributions to both positive and negative margins weighted by μ_i and 1−μ_i. A latent extension handles weak supervision (only bounding boxes) by treating u as latent and adding a convex regularizer R(u) that is the cross-entropy between u and an initialization u_bb (segment–box IoU), optimized by block coordinate descent. The method is evaluated on segment proposal “class confidence prediction” with two introduced metrics (TAPC and NDCG), on semantic segmentation (using CPMC and second-order pooling), and on object detection by creating a class-specific spatial confidence map per window that is concatenated with RCNN features, reporting ~1.5 mAP improvement on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Relation to prior work
The work is positioned against SVC and SVR, MIL-style weak supervision, and instance-weighted SVMs. However, there is existing literature on SVMs with probabilistic/soft/uncertain labels, learning with label uncertainty, and learning from ambiguous/partial labels, which is not cited or contrasted. The expected-hinge formulation closely resembles using soft labels or duplicating each instance with opposite labels and instance-specific weights—connections that should be made explicit and compared empirically.

Quality
- Objective/solver correctness: The loss L(w)=λ||w||^2/2 + (1/N)∑[l_i^+ μ_i + l_i^- (1−μ_i)] is convex for fixed μ, but “can be solved by any standard SVM solver” is non-trivial. Standard solvers optimize ∑ C_i max(0,1−y_i f(x_i)) for observed y_i with a single weight per example. Your loss is equivalent to duplicating each x_i twice with labels +1 and −1 and weights μ_i and 1−μ_i, respectively. If this is how you implement it, please state so; otherwise, explain how LibLinear is used without modification.
- Mapping g: You simply set μ_i = u_i. There is no justification, calibration, or sensitivity study for g (e.g., monotone transforms, temperature scaling). This choice is consequential.
- Latent u optimization: For fixed w and g(u)=u, each u_i solves min_{u∈(0,1)} u(l_i^+−l_i^-) + λ_R r_i(u) with r_i being cross-entropy. You do not provide the closed-form update, constraints, or convergence criteria. The statement “u becomes 0/1 if λ_R=0” is true but insufficient. Algorithm 1 omits key details (step 4).
- Regularizer design: Using segment–box IoU as u_bb is a coarse proxy for segment–mask IoU; segments wholly inside the box receive misleading priors. No ablation on λ_R or alternative priors/regularizers is provided.
- Evaluation details: TAPC and NDCG are insufficiently specified (threshold grid, per-class averaging, DCG gain/discount, cutoff). Error bars/variance are missing. Gains over SVR are small and sometimes described as “comparable,” contradicting earlier claims.
- Baselines: Comparisons are limited to SVC and SVR. Missing are probabilistic/soft-label SVM/logistic regression, ranking SVM, isotonic regression, weakly supervised segmentation methods, or more recent MIL/latent segmentation baselines.
- Detection integration: Implementation details are sparse (feature dimensionalities, normalization, SVM hyperparameters, training protocol, cross-validation, class-weighting). Statistical significance of mAP gains is not analyzed. Computational costs and runtime are not reported.

Clarity
The manuscript has many textual, formatting, and referencing issues that hinder reproducibility:
- Equations: Several typos (“λw · 1 wTw” instead of λ/2||w||^2; duplicated N factors; missing vector norms). Notation switches (w, [w b], xi augmented with 1) are imprecise. Please give a clean, consistent primal and dual form.
- Algorithm 1 lacks concrete updates and convergence criteria.
- Figures/tables (Fig. 1–3, Table 1) are referred to but not present; values are partially embedded in text with formatting errors.
- Numerous typos and grammar issues (“continues value,” “segmentic segmentation,” etc.).
- References are incomplete/malformed (missing years/authors, duplicated entries), which is not acceptable for NeurIPS.

Originality
Conceptually, the expected hinge loss with soft labels is a straightforward and known idea; the latent variant with a convex regularizer towards an initialization is also standard. The spatial confidence map feature for detection is a reasonable engineering contribution but ad hoc. The paper’s novelty hinges on packaging and applying these pieces to segmentation/detection; methodological novelty is limited unless connections to and differences from prior soft/uncertain-label large-margin methods are thoroughly established.

Significance
The reported detection gain (~1.5 mAP on VOC07) is potentially useful but needs stronger experimental rigor and breadth of baselines to be convincing. The segmentation results are comparable to SVR, not clearly better. Without clearer theory, ablations, and thorough comparisons, the impact is incremental.

Pros
- Simple, convex-in-blocks formulation; easy to implement as duplicated examples with weights.
- Addresses a practically relevant weakly supervised setting.
- Integration with RCNN yields non-trivial improvements.
- The idea of evaluating segment confidence prediction directly is reasonable.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to soft/uncertain-label SVM literature; missing citations and comparisons.
- Insufficient technical detail on optimization, implementation, and hyperparameters.
- Underdefined evaluation metrics, missing figures/tables, and lack of statistical analysis.
- Many formatting, equation, and reference errors.
- Baselines are too narrow; ablations on g and λ_R absent; runtime not reported.

Questions for authors
1. How exactly do you optimize L(w) with LibLinear? Do you duplicate each instance with ± labels and weights {μ_i, 1−μ_i}?
2. Provide the closed-form update (or solver) for u in step 4 and the convergence criteria.
3. Justify μ=g(u). Did you try calibrated/sigmoid mappings? Provide sensitivity plots.
4. How were λ, λ_R chosen? Per-class or global? Via validation?
5. Fully define TAPC and NDCG settings and provide error bars.
6. Provide complete Table 1 with per-class APs and training protocol; report compute overhead.

Minor notes
- Fix numerous typos and grammar.
- Clean up equations, remove duplicated N factors, and define all symbols.
- Complete and correct the references; add missing related work on soft/uncertain labels.

Recommendation: Reject
While the problem is important and the approach is sensible, the methodological novelty is limited, the empirical evaluation and baselines are insufficient, and the paper quality (clarity, rigor, references) falls below NeurIPS standards. With clearer connections to prior work, rigorous optimization details, comprehensive experiments/ablations, and polished presentation, this could become a solid contribution.
