- Paper proposes e-SVM: minimize expected hinge loss under soft labels µ=g(u)∈[0,1], implementable via standard SVM solvers by weighting.  
- Latent e-SVM alternates between optimizing classifier w and latent soft labels u, regularized by KL around initialization ubb.  
- Applied to segment proposal scoring and to detection by pooling segment scores into spatial confidence maps added to R-CNN features.  
- Empirical gains: modest improvements over SVC/SVR for segment scoring (TAPC/NDCG) and +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC07 when combined with R-CNN.  
- Strength: simple, intuitive objective that is easy to implement with existing SVM solvers.  
- Strength: latent variant is blockwise convex and KL regularizer prevents degenerate hard assignments.  
- Strength: practical, reproducible detection improvement and agnostic to proposal generator, features, and base detector.  
- Weakness (theory): paper fails to position or differentiate from prior soft/probabilistic-label SVMs and does not prove novelty.  
- Weakness (theory): lacking equivalence proof to weighted SVM on duplicated examples to justify solver choice.  
- Weakness (latent opt): u-update is under-specified—no per-sample optimality derivation, closed-form solver, or convergence/sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness (empirics): missing hyperparameter/details (λw, λR, alternations, g(u) choices, runtime, convergence), inconsistent claims about performance.  
- Weakness (empirics): detection tables missing; no per-class analysis or ablations (KL on/off, pooling m×m, superpixels vs CPMC).  
- Weakness (baselines): missing comparisons to RankSVM, logistic regression with soft labels, and MIL variants suited to partial overlap.  
- Clarity issues: typos, broken citations, missing figures/tables, and Algorithm 1 lacks detail on u optimization.  
- Presentation claim that method "weights labels instead of data points" is misleading without explicit equivalence to instance-weighted duplicated samples.  
- Originality: idea is incremental—expected hinge loss and latent alternating optimization are close to existing methods; spatial pooling is an engineering tweak.  
- Significance: practical utility shown but gains are modest and evaluated on a single dataset/detector; broader evaluation needed.  
- Major questions: request derivation/solver for u-update, alternation count and λR sensitivity, and formal equivalence to WSVM on duplicated data.  
- Major questions: ask whether g(u) other than identity was tried or IoU calibrated, and comparisons to RankSVM/logistic regression.  
- Major questions: request full detection tables, per-class gains, and ablations on pooling resolution and m.  
- Minor comments: fix numerous citation/formatting errors, include missing figures/tables, and provide recipe for using standard solvers (e.g., LibLinear weighted construction).  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general, easy-to-implement idea with practical gains and a workable latent extension.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, incomplete experiments/ablation, missing algorithmic details, and light theoretical development.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger positioning against prior work, richer experiments, full algorithmic details, and proofreading.
