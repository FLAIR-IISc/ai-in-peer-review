- Overall: Proposes e-SVM, an SVM variant using continuous "positiveness" scores for weak supervision; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem & method: Tackles weakly supervised learning by weighting labels (positiveness scores) in the SVM loss rather than assuming binary labels.  
- Relation to prior work: Distinguishes from MIL, SVC, SVR, and weighted SVMs by weighting labels instead of instances; situates work within segmentation and detection literature.  
- Novelty: Introducing label-weighted SVM (e-SVM) is a sensible and useful extension of standard SVMs for continuous labels.  
- Theoretical soundness: Loss derivation and latent e-SVM formulation are clear, logically motivated, and well-defined.  
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection show consistent, statistically significant improvements over SVC/SVR.  
- Practicality: Easily implemented using existing SVM solvers (e.g., LibLinear) and generalizes across proposal methods, features, and detectors.  
- Writing & clarity: Paper is well-written and organized, making methods and experiments easy to follow.  
- Modest improvements: Gains are consistent but small (e.g., ~1.5% AP in detection), which may be seen as incremental.  
- Regularization concern: Use of KL-divergence for latent variable u appears somewhat arbitrary; more exploration of alternatives is needed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks detailed analysis or guidance on tuning key hyperparameters (e.g., λR).  
- Figure quality: Some figures (notably Figure 3) need improved readability (larger fonts, clearer labels).  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, is novel and sound, shows consistent improvements, is clearly explained, and is implementable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Improvements are modest, hyperparameter/regularization choices need stronger justification and analysis.  
- Questions for authors: Provide error analysis explaining why e-SVM outperforms SVC/SVR, explore alternative regularizers for u, give hyperparameter tuning guidance, and discuss applicability to other weakly supervised tasks.
