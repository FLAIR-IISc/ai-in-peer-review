Summary and relation to prior work
The paper introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a max-margin formulation that handles “soft” labels in [0,1] by minimizing the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label model with mean µi=g(ui), where ui denotes “positiveness” (e.g., IoU between a segment proposal and the ground-truth instance). With observed ui (pixel-level labels), the objective is convex in w and can be solved with standard linear SVM solvers. For weak supervision (bounding boxes only), ui is treated as a latent variable and is regularized to stay close to the IoU between the segment and the ground-truth box (ubb) via a convex KL (cross-entropy) penalty. Alternating minimization over w and u is used.

Beyond proposal scoring, the paper proposes a spatial confidence map feature: per-pixel scores are the max over overlapping segments’ classifier scores; scores are pooled over an m×m grid within each detection window, yielding class-specific features. These are concatenated with RCNN features, producing an average +1.5 AP on PASCAL VOC 2007 over RCNN with bounding-box regression.

The paper positions e-SVM relative to SVC (thresholding positiveness) and SVR (regressing IoU), and to weakly supervised approaches such as MIL. It also relates to weighted SVMs, claiming e-SVM “weights labels instead of data points.”

Strengths
- Clear formulation for soft labels: The expected hinge loss cleanly incorporates “positiveness” and remains convex when positiveness is observed.
- Straightforward weakly supervised extension: Latent ui with a convex, interpretable regularizer leads to a practical alternating optimization.
- Practical impact: The spatial confidence map is simple and yields non-trivial gains when combined with RCNN on PASCAL 2007.
- Evaluation on both segmentation and detection; introduction of task-agnostic segment-ranking metrics (TAPC and NDCG) is useful.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Originality relative to SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels: Minimizing µi·hinge(+1)+(1−µi)·hinge(−1) closely matches existing “soft/probabilistic label” SVM formulations and cost-weighted/label-uncertainty SVMs; the novelty appears incremental. The paper does not cite this line of work.
- Technical clarity: Equations contain typographical issues (e.g., regularization term formatting, constants), g(ui) is set to ui without justification or sensitivity analysis, and the mapping of the custom loss to “any standard SVM solver” is not described in implementable detail.
- Latent model assumptions: Using ubb (segment–box IoU) as a proxy/anchor for the latent positiveness can be weak, since high overlap with a box does not necessarily imply high foreground purity; the strong KL regularizer may bias u toward ubb even when it is misleading.
- Experimental reporting: Limited ablations (e.g., effect of λR, choice of g, initialization), incomplete per-class breakdowns, and unclear fairness in RCNN comparisons (small changes to bbox regression features are mentioned). No runtime or convergence diagnostics are provided.
- Naming: “e-SVM” collides with the widely-used “Exemplar-SVM” acronym, which may cause confusion.

Quality
- The observed-positiveness objective is convex and sound. The alternating scheme for the latent variant is standard; it will monotonically decrease the objective but offers no global optimality guarantees.
- The regularizer is convex and stabilizes the latent updates; however, the choice and strength of this regularizer are critical and not thoroughly analyzed. Without it, the method degenerates to a hard-assignment latent SVM, as noted by the authors.
- Claims about outperforming SVR on segment scoring are supported by TAPC/NDCG averages, but margins are modest; details about protocol, cross-validation, and statistical significance are missing.
- The detection improvement (+1.5 AP) is promising but would benefit from stronger baselines, ablations (e.g., replacing e-SVM with SVR in the same pipeline), and clearer reporting of exactly matched training/finetuning/bbox regression settings.

Clarity
- The paper is generally readable, but equation formatting issues (“λw · 1 wTw”, duplicated N factors), undefined constants, and several incomplete or malformed references hinder reproducibility.
- Practical details are sparse: how the custom loss is implemented with LibLinear; stopping criteria; sensitivity to λw, λR, m, and to the choice of g; convergence behavior.
- The new metrics TAPC and NDCG are useful; providing precise computation protocols (threshold grid, interpolation, averaging across classes) would improve clarity.

Originality
- The expected hinge-loss formulation with soft labels appears closely related to prior SVM variants handling probabilistic/uncertain labels and cost-weighting; the paper does not delineate theoretical or algorithmic differences clearly.
- The latent extension with a KL prior toward ubb is reasonable but conceptually close to standard biconvex EM/alternate-minimization with regularization.
- The spatial confidence map is a simple pooling idea; related segmentation-aware detection works should be discussed and compared.

Significance
- The approach is practical and could be adopted where soft labels arise naturally (e.g., IoU-style targets), especially with weak supervision.
- The detection gains are incremental but meaningful on a strong baseline; broader adoption would require cleaner evidence and stronger baselines/ablations.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Clarify the exact objective (constants, regularization coefficient, whether 1/2||w||^2 is used). Provide the mapping from Equation (1) to a standard solver (e.g., sample reweighting or sample duplication trick).
- Discuss the relationship to SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels, cost-sensitive SVMs, and learning with label proportions; add appropriate citations and clarify the novelty.
- Justify the choice g(u)=u; report sensitivity to other monotone mappings.
- Provide ablations on λR, initialization (ubb vs alternatives), and show convergence plots. Analyze failure cases where ubb is a poor proxy.
- For detection, report a per-class AP table, an ablation replacing e-SVM with SVR inside the same spatial confidence map pipeline, and ensure the baseline uses identical bbox regression settings.
- Report training/inference time and memory overhead for the latent updates and the spatial confidence features.
- Consider renaming the method to avoid collision with “Exemplar-SVM.”

Arguments for acceptance
- Sound, simple formulation for soft labels with an effective weakly supervised extension.
- Demonstrated improvements on a strong detection baseline.
- Potentially useful, general technique for tasks with bounded soft supervision.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing SVM variants with soft/uncertain labels; missing citations and comparison.
- Incomplete experimental analysis and clarity issues reduce confidence in generality and reproducibility.
- Weak justification for the latent regularizer’s anchor (ubb) and limited analysis of its impact.

Overall, this is a practical paper with a clean idea and some empirical gains, but it needs stronger positioning relative to prior art, clearer exposition, and more rigorous experimental validation.
