- Summary: Proposes e-SVM using expectation of SVM loss over Bernoulli labels to weight segment proposals' positiveness for weakly supervised vision tasks, with a latent extension using KL regularization.

- Strength: Addresses an important practical problem—reducing reliance on costly pixel-level annotations—using bounding-box level supervision.

- Strength: The e-SVM formulation is theoretically well-derived and avoids hard thresholding of positiveness.

- Strength: Can be implemented with standard SVM solvers (e.g., LibLinear), making it practical and easy to adopt.

- Strength: Experiments on segmentation and detection use reasonable baselines (SVC, SVR) and major datasets to evaluate the method.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons—only SVC and SVR are used, missing recent state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods.

- Weakness: Reported performance gains are modest (e.g., 1.5% AP on PASCAL VOC) with no statistical significance testing.

- Weakness: Key design choice (KL regularization on u) is inadequately justified and not compared to alternatives like L2 regularization.

- Weakness: SVC comparisons use arbitrary thresholds; authors don’t show e-SVM vs optimally tuned SVC, weakening the main motivation.

- Weakness: No ablation studies to quantify contributions of expectation loss vs. latent extension and other components.

- Technical concern: Unclear whether IoU used for positiveness is with pixel-level ground truth or bounding boxes, impacting the weak supervision interpretation.

- Technical concern: Paper does not detail optimization with the regularizer or convergence behavior of the alternating optimization in latent e-SVM.

- Significance: Contribution is modest and incremental; stronger claims require broader evaluation and evidence of generalization to other weak supervision problems.

- Overall assessment: The method is technically sound and practical but experimentally limited; suitable for weak acceptance pending stronger validation.

- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on expanded experiments, comparisons, and analyses.

- Revision 1: Compare to recent weakly supervised methods (e.g., [19], [20]) beyond SVC and SVR.

- Revision 2: Provide statistical significance tests for reported performance gains.

- Revision 3: Justify and compare the KL regularizer to alternative regularizations (e.g., L2).

- Revision 4: Clarify whether IoU positiveness is computed against pixel-level ground truth or bounding boxes.

- Revision 5: Include ablation studies to isolate the effects of expectation loss, latent modeling, and other design choices.
