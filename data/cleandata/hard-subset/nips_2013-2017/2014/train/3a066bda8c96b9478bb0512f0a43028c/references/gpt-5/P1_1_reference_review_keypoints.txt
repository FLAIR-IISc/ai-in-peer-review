- Proposes e-SVM: minimize expected hinge loss under Bernoulli labels µi=g(ui), yielding loss µ·hinge(+1) + (1−µ)·hinge(−1) with L2 regularization.
- Introduces a latent-variable variant that alternates between optimizing w and unobserved ui, with cross-entropy regularizer to bounding-box-derived proxy ubb.
- Applies method to segment confidence prediction (new metrics TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation (CPMC segments), and detection by pooling segment confidences into spatial maps concatenated with R-CNN features.
- Reports e-SVM beating SVC/SVR on segment prediction metrics, comparable segmentation performance to SVR, and a +1.2–1.5 mAP boost over R-CNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Relation to prior work: aligns with weakly-supervised vision (MIL, latent SVM) and is closely related to SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels and instance-weighted SVM.
- The expected hinge is effectively realizable by duplicating examples with weights µ and 1−µ, i.e., a special case of weighted SVM.
- Latent alternating updates resemble EM/CCCP-style latent SVM and spatial pooling resembles prior segment-aware detection features.
- Technical soundness: objective is convex in w for known µ and bi-convex for latent u/w, but key details are missing for the ui update.
- Missing derivation/solver/gradients for ui-step, no convergence analysis, step-size, or regularization sensitivity discussed.
- Choice g(u)=u is ad hoc with no calibration, monotone transform exploration, or learned-g considered.
- Using ubb (IoU with bounding box) as a prior is biased; strength of cross-entropy regularizer may overly constrain ui and no ablation on λR is provided.
- Experiments lack crucial ablations separating effects of e-SVM loss vs SVR/SVC for the same features, the spatial pooling design, and the number of alternating iterations.
- Statistical rigor is limited: no error bars, seed variation, or significance testing — reported R-CNN gains may be within run-to-run variance.
- Segmentation results are ambiguous (metric unspecified, e.g., mIoU or pixel accuracy) and lack per-class breakdowns.
- Clarity issues: grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, malformed references, and imprecise definitions for TAPC, hyperparameters, ui solver, convergence, and runtime.
- Originality is limited: core expected-hinge objective is not novel and is essentially weighted SVM; latent extension and regularizer are straightforward.
- Significance is practical but incremental: soft-label approach is sensible and shows modest empirical gains, but lack of analysis reduces broader impact.
- Pros: simple, general formulation usable with standard SVM solvers; naturally handles soft labels; shows empirical improvements and easy-to-integrate spatial features.
- Cons: limited novelty, insufficient technical detail for latent updates, no sensitivity/ablation studies, weak experimental rigor, and poor writing/references.
- Questions for authors requested: clarify segmentation metric and per-class results; provide ui optimization derivation/solver and sensitivity to λR and g(u); clarify equivalence (or not) to WSVM with duplicated examples; report ablations (spatial feature without e-SVM, pooling alternatives, per-class VOC07 gains and variance); provide runtime/scalability and overhead for segment scoring/spatial pooling.
- Recommendation: Weak reject due to limited methodological novelty, missing technical detail, and insufficient evaluation rigor.
