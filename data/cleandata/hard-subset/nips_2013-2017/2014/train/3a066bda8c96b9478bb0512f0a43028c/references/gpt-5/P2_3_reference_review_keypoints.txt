- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for soft labels in [0,1] and a latent variant regularized toward bbox-based surrogates, applied to segmentation ranking, semantic segmentation, and detection (+1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC07).  
- The loss replaces binary hinge with expected hinge over Y∈{±1} using success probability g(u) (set to u), giving per-example loss g(u)·l+ + (1−g(u))·l−.  
- Latent variant alternates convex subproblems in w and u, with a convex cross-entropy prior R(u) pulling u toward bbox IoU ubb.  
- The idea sits near prior work on SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels, instance-weighting/replication, and latent/EM-style SVMs; novelty over these is limited.  
- The e-SVM objective is convex in w for fixed u, and the alternating latent optimization is reasonable, but several technical details are unclear.  
- The claim “effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver” is misleading without explicitly stating the reduction (duplicate samples or per-instance weights) and how LibLinear was used.  
- Choice of g(u)=u is ad hoc; sensitivity to different link/calibration functions is not analyzed.  
- Optimization details for the latent u-step (closed-form update vs numeric solver, constraints, stopping criteria, iteration counts) are missing.  
- Regularization strength λR and its role in avoiding degeneracy (u→0/1) and impact on performance are unablated; comparison to λR=0 is missing.  
- Empirical gains on segmentation are marginal versus SVR; the main result is a modest detection gain, with no statistical variance or training stability reported.  
- Reproducibility is weak: hyperparameters (λ, λR, C), learning rates, convergence criteria, feature specs, ubb computation, and runtime are not provided.  
- Writing contains typos/formatting issues and some references/equations are incomplete, impairing readability.  
- New metrics (TAPC/NDCG) are only briefly defined; precise definitions and error-bar plots are needed.  
- The spatial confidence map feature is pragmatic but not conceptually novel.  
- Pros: simple convex subproblems, clear expected-hinge formulation, principled latent regularizer, practical detector improvement, and plug-and-play generality.  
- Cons: limited novelty relative to prior soft-label and latent SVM work, missing citations/comparisons, sparse optimization/implementation details, modest empirical gains, and potentially misleading solver claims.  
- Suggested improvements: provide explicit solver reduction and code, compare to probabilistic-label SVMs and λR=0 latent SVM/MIL, ablate λR/g(u)/initialization/iterations, report convergence/runtimes and variance, and fix writing/definitions.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — method is clean and practically useful but under-positioned relative to prior work and lacking rigorous ablations and reproducibility details.
