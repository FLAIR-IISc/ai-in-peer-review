- Summary: Proposes e-SVM that models data "positiveness" as a random variable to learn from weak labels, with theory and PASCAL VOC segmentation/detection results showing gains over SVM/SVR.

- Strength: Addresses a practical weakly supervised learning problem relevant due to labeling costs.

- Strength: Novel formulation treating positiveness as a random/latent variable and extending SVMs.

- Strength: Strong empirical validation on standard datasets with consistent improvements over strong baselines.

- Strength: Theoretical soundness with clear mathematical formulation of e-SVM and latent e-SVM.

- Strength: General approach compatible with different proposal detectors, features, and classifiers.

- Strength: Generally clear and well-organized writing.

- Weakness: Applications (segmentation/detection) rely on existing frameworks and yield only incremental improvements (e.g., ~1.5% AP).

- Weakness: Regularization choice for latent e-SVM (KL divergence) seems arbitrary and needs stronger justification or alternatives.

- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to more recent weakly-supervised methods, especially deep learning and contrastive approaches.

- Weakness: Spatial confidence map implementation details (pooling strategy, effect) are vague.

- Weakness: Relevance of introduced evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG) to real-world tasks is not fully explained.

- Qualitative — Quality: Mathematically correct and experimentally supported, but practical significance of modest gains is questionable.

- Qualitative — Clarity: Paper is mostly clear, though some sections (e.g., regularization) need more detail.

- Qualitative — Originality: e-SVM is an original extension of SVMs; applications are less novel.

- Qualitative — Significance: Viable approach with modest impact; potential for broader application but limited current gains.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound algorithm with consistent empirical improvements and general applicability presented clearly.

- Arguments against acceptance: Improvements are incremental for a top conference; missing recent-method comparisons; regularization justification is weak.

- Overall assessment: Above-average, solid contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions.

- Required revisions: Expand related work to include recent weakly-supervised methods; justify or explore alternative regularizers; clarify spatial confidence map implementation; discuss limitations and future work to improve performance gains.
