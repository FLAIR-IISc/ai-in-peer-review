- Introduces e-SVM to handle continuous "positiveness" (e.g., IoU) in weak supervision and a latent-variable variant for unobserved positiveness.
- Extends SVM by weighting labels (not instances), enabling learning from graded correctness of proposals.
- Shows empirical gains over SVC and SVR on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection (≈1.5% AP gain).
- Positions work relative to MIL and weighted SVMs and cites relevant literature.
- Strength: Addresses an important weakly supervised learning problem of continuous feedback on proposals.
- Strength: Novel formulation and latent-extension; intuitive idea of weighting labels by positiveness.
- Strength: Consistent empirical improvements and generality across detectors, features, and classifiers.
- Strength: Clear presentation and helpful algorithm (Algorithm 1) for latent e-SVM optimization.
- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence properties, conditions for superiority over SVC/SVR).
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, notably the KL-regularization coefficient λR.
- Weakness: Evaluation focused mainly on PASCAL VOC; needs tests on additional datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
- Weakness: Lacks detailed ablation studies (impact of feature types, proposal methods, and positiveness function g(ui)).
- Weakness: Comparisons are primarily to SVC/SVR; should include more recent state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods.
- For acceptance: Novel, well-motivated, empirically effective, flexible framework with clear writing.
- Against acceptance: Missing deeper theory, hyperparameter analysis, broader evaluation, ablations, and stronger baselines.
- Overall: Solid contribution but requires more theory and broader/stronger empirical validation to be a strong accept.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions conditioned on adding hyperparameter sensitivity discussion, expanded evaluation, detailed ablations, SOTA comparisons, and brief theoretical discussion.
