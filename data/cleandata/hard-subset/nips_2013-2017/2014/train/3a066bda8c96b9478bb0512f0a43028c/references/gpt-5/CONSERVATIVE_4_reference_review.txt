Summary
The paper proposes “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM) for learning from weak or soft supervision where each instance has a “positiveness” score ui in [0,1] (e.g., IoU with a segmentation ground-truth). The key idea is to treat the label Yi ∈ {−1,+1} as Bernoulli with success probability µi = g(ui) (taken as identity), and minimize the expected hinge loss EYi[max(0,1 − Yi f(xi))] = µi l+ + (1−µi) l−. This yields a convex objective in w and can be implemented using standard linear SVM solvers. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), the paper treats ui as latent and alternates between optimizing w and u, with a KL-based regularizer R(u) that penalizes deviation from an initialization ubb (IoU with bounding boxes). The method is evaluated on segment scoring (new TAPC and NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation, and object detection where e-SVM-derived “spatial confidence map” features are concatenated with RCNN features, yielding reported +1.2 to +1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Strengths
- Simple, convex formulation for soft labels: expected hinge loss provides a neat way to incorporate [0,1]-valued supervision while keeping standard optimization.
- Practical extension to weak supervision via alternating minimization with an entropy/KL regularizer; easy to implement.
- The spatial confidence map feature is a reasonable bridge from segment scores to window-based detectors.
- Empirical results show consistent gains over SVC and small gains over SVR on segment scoring; modest improvements when augmenting RCNN for detection.
- The method is general with respect to proposal generators and features; uses off-the-shelf solvers.

Weaknesses
- Novelty is limited. The expected hinge loss with µ-weighted positive/negative hinge terms is essentially a soft-label or instance-dependent cost-sensitive SVM. This appears equivalent to duplicating each xi into two examples with labels +1/−1 and weights µi/(1−µi), which has been studied under probabilistic/uncertain labels and cost-sensitive SVMs. The paper claims difference from WSVMs but does not convincingly establish a substantive distinction.
- The latent extension is an alternating scheme akin to latent SVM/EM with an ad hoc KL regularizer to a prior ubb; there is no probabilistic derivation, convergence analysis, or ablation to justify this particular choice versus other convex regularizers.
- Experimental validation is not thorough enough given the modest gains:
  - Baselines are weak. Comparing only to SVC (with thresholding) and SVR is insufficient. Natural baselines include logistic regression with soft labels, soft-label/uncertain-label SVMs, ranking SVM/RankNet for TAPC/NDCG, and MIL/latent-SVM style methods for weak segmentation.
  - For the detection experiment, the improvement could stem from adding any segment-based feature. There is no control where the same spatial confidence map is trained with SVR or logistic soft labels to isolate the value of e-SVM itself. The RCNN pipeline is also altered (additive kernel mapping), confounding fairness.
  - Important details are missing: number of thresholds for TAPC, exact NDCG gains per class, settings and sensitivity of λR, m, u initialization, solver and stopping criteria for the u-step, runtime and scalability.
- Clarity and polish need work. There are many typos, incomplete/incorrect references, and missing figures/tables (e.g., Table 1). Some notation is inconsistent (e.g., λw·1/wTw), and the link between ubbi and true ui is only heuristically motivated.
- The claim that MIL “cannot be applied” is too strong; many weakly supervised detection/segmentation methods relax the “at least one pure positive” assumption. The paper should position against that literature more carefully.

Quality
Technically, the basic e-SVM is sound and convex. The latent variant is a heuristic alternating scheme without guarantees. Claims are only modestly supported experimentally; key ablations and stronger baselines are missing. Important implementation details are absent, limiting reproducibility.

Clarity
The paper is generally understandable but suffers from numerous typos, incomplete citations, and missing experimental specifics. The optimization for u and parameter choices require more detail.

Originality
Low to moderate. The core contribution is a specific instantiation of soft-label hinge loss, long explored in cost-sensitive/uncertain-label SVM literature, plus a straightforward latent extension with KL regularization. The spatial confidence map is an engineering contribution.

Significance
Empirical gains over established strong baselines are small. Without stronger positioning against existing soft-label SVMs and more rigorous evaluation, the broader impact is limited.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Please position e-SVM relative to prior work on SVMs with probabilistic/soft/uncertain labels and instance-dependent cost-sensitive SVMs. Is your loss exactly equivalent to training with duplicated weighted examples? If not, clarify the distinction.
- Provide a probabilistic interpretation of the latent model (e.g., posterior regularization) and discuss convergence properties. How is the u-subproblem solved? Any closed form or per-sample Newton? Complexity?
- Add ablations: effect of λR, g(·) choices, initialization, and removing the KL term. Show the collapse to 0/1 without regularization.
- Strengthen baselines: logistic regression with soft labels, soft-label SVMs from the literature, ranking baselines for TAPC/NDCG, and MIL/latent-SVM variants for weak supervision.
- For detection, control for the feature: compare e-SVM vs SVR/logistic-trained confidence maps under identical pipelines. Report per-class results and significance.
- Clarify evaluation metrics (TAPC threshold set, NDCG details), report runtimes, and fix references/typos. Release code if possible.

Pros
- Simple, convex soft-label formulation; easy to implement with standard solvers.
- Practical weakly supervised extension; general framework applicable to segments/proposals.
- Some empirical improvements, including modest mAP gains when augmenting RCNN.

Cons
- Limited novelty versus established soft-label/cost-sensitive SVM literature.
- Heuristic latent optimization with ad hoc regularization; lacks theory and ablations.
- Incomplete baselines and experimental details; modest gains and confounding factors.
- Clarity and citation issues.

Recommendation
Reject. While the idea is practical and the engineering is sensible, the conceptual novelty over existing soft-label/cost-sensitive SVMs is not convincingly established, and the experimental validation is insufficiently rigorous to warrant acceptance at NeurIPS. A revised version with stronger positioning, thorough baselines/ablations, and clearer exposition could be competitive.
