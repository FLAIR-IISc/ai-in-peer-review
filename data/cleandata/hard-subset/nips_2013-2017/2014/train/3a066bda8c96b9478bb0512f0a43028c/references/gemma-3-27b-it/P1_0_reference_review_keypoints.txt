- Summary: Proposes e-SVM, a loss that weights continuous “positiveness” (e.g., IoU) for learning from weakly supervised proposals, with theory and experiments on segmentation/detection.  
- Strength: Tackles a practical weakly supervised learning problem by handling continuous labels directly.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and mathematically sound formulation, with relation to weighted SVMs.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains (e.g., ~1.5% avg improvement in detection) on PASCAL VOC.  
- Strength: Modular design that can integrate with various proposal detectors, features, and base detectors.  
- Strength: Latent-variable extension for truly weak supervision (bounding-box only) with regularization.  
- Strength: Introduces TAPC and NDCG metrics for segment-wise confidence evaluation.  
- Weakness: Experiments limited to a single dataset (PASCAL VOC); needs broader evaluation (e.g., COCO, ImageNet).  
- Weakness: Paper lacks analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, notably the latent e-SVM regularization.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to recent state-of-the-art MIL-based weakly supervised methods.  
- Weakness: Spatial confidence map feature explanation is unclear; additive kernel approximation rationale and visuals are missing.  
- Weakness: Minor typos and awkward phrasing—needs final proofreading.  
- Quality: Technically sound formulation and experiments, but broader validation and sensitivity analysis would improve completeness.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, though some sections require deeper explanation and visual aids.  
- Originality: Novel handling of continuous positiveness and latent extension; new evaluation metrics add value.  
- Significance: Promising results suggesting practical utility for weakly supervised learning in vision.  
- For acceptance: Addresses important problem, well-motivated novel algorithm, strong empirical results, and broad applicability.  
- Against acceptance: Limited dataset scope, missing hyperparameter analysis, and weaker comparisons to cutting-edge weakly supervised methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
