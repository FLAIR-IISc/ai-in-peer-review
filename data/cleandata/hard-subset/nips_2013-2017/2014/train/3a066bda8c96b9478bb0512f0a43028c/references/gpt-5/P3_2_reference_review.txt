Summary
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from “soft” labels in [0,1], where the soft label u_i (positiveness) is the IoU between a segment proposal and ground-truth. The key idea is to treat the unknown binary label Y_i ∈ {−1,+1} as a Bernoulli with success probability μ_i=g(u_i) (taken as μ_i=u_i), and minimize the expected hinge loss E_Y[max(0,1−Y f(x))] = g(u)·max(0,1−f(x))+(1−g(u))·max(0,1+f(x)). For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), u_i is treated as latent and regularized toward u_i^bb (IoU of segment with the GT box) using a KL penalty; alternating minimization over w and u is used. The model is applied to (i) segment-scoring for semantic segmentation using CPMC proposals and (ii) object detection by forming a class-specific “spatial confidence map” pooled over candidate windows and concatenated to RCNN features, reportedly yielding +1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Relation to prior work
The problem of soft/uncertain labels has a substantial literature across SVMs and risk minimization: weighted SVMs, cost-sensitive SVMs, SVMs with probabilistic labels/soft labels, and expected losses w.r.t. label uncertainty. The proposed loss is the expected hinge loss under known posteriors, which is a known construct and equivalent to training a weighted SVM on duplicated examples with opposite labels and weights g(u) and 1−g(u). The latent extension with a convex regularizer is an EM-like alternating scheme akin to prior weakly-supervised segmentation/detection approaches, though the specific KL prior toward u^bb is a practical choice.

Strengths
- Clear, intuitive formulation: the expected hinge loss aligns well with the intuition of soft labels; convex in w for fixed u and easy to optimize.
- Implementation-friendly: reducible to standard linear SVM solvers via per-example weights (or by duplicating examples), with a simple 1-D convex subproblem for each u_i in the weakly supervised case.
- Practical utility: a generic way to incorporate soft segment quality into learning and a simple route to couple segmentation-derived cues into detection via spatial confidence maps.
- Empirical improvements: modest but consistent gains over SVC/SVR for segment scoring metrics (TAPC/NDCG) and a small mAP boost when augmenting RCNN.

Weaknesses and detailed comments
Quality
- Novelty of the core objective appears limited. The expected hinge loss under label uncertainty and learning with soft/probabilistic labels have been studied; the proposed loss is exactly a convex combination of positive and negative hinge losses. It is equivalent to a standard weighted SVM on a dataset where each x_i is duplicated with labels +1 and −1 and weights g(u_i) and 1−g(u_i). The claimed distinction from weighted SVM (“weights labels instead of data points”) is largely semantic; please clarify and cite relevant prior work on soft/probabilistic labels and expected losses.
- The weakly-supervised extension is heuristic. The KL regularizer R(u) tethering u to u^bb is a strong prior with no justification that u^bb is a good proxy for mask IoU; a segment can have high IoU with a box but poor overlap with the object mask. No ablation is provided on λ_R, the choice of g(u), or alternative priors/constraints (e.g., coverage/consistency constraints across proposals within a box).
- Limited theoretical analysis. There is no discussion of the statistical properties of the expected hinge loss with soft labels (calibration, robustness), convergence guarantees for the alternating scheme beyond convexity of subproblems, or sensitivity to initialization u^bb.
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete. Improvements over SVR for TAPC/NDCG are small; semantic segmentation accuracy is only “comparable” to SVR. For detection, the reported +1.2–1.5 mAP gains could fall within variance; per-class APs, confidence intervals, and stronger baselines (e.g., adding hand-crafted features without e-SVM) are needed. The baseline fairness is unclear since the paper also modifies RCNN’s bbox regression (feature augmentation via additive kernels), which conflates contributions.
- The approach does not exploit the “several proposals may be required” insight; training treats segments independently, with no bag/coverage constraints connecting proposals to an instance. This weakens the weakly-supervised story compared to MIL variants that model bag-level structure.

Clarity
- The presentation has numerous typos and formatting issues; several references are incomplete or malformed. Important implementation details are missing: how the u-update is solved (closed-form? Newton?), stopping criteria, complexity, and hyperparameter selection.
- Figures/tables referenced (e.g., Figure 1–3, Table 1) are not shown; this makes it difficult to verify claims.
- The definition and justification for g(u)=u as a “probability” is ad hoc; alternatives (e.g., monotone calibrations, temperature scaling) are not explored.

Originality
- The core contribution (expected hinge loss with soft labels) has close analogs in prior work on soft/probabilistic labels and cost-sensitive SVMs. The latent extension with a KL prior to u^bb is a practical design choice, but not a fundamentally new learning paradigm. The spatial confidence map for detection is a straightforward pooling/concatenation scheme and conceptually similar to using segmentation cues in detection.

Significance
- While the pipeline is practical, the empirical gains are modest and confined to one dataset; it is unclear whether the method advances the state of the art beyond incremental improvements. Without stronger baselines and ablations (e.g., logistic/CRF with soft labels, regression to IoU with bounded losses, calibrating g), the impact is limited.

Questions for authors
- Please clarify the relationship to weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs and SVMs with probabilistic/soft labels; is there any advantage beyond the simple duplication-and-weighting equivalence?
- How sensitive are results to g(u)? Did you try calibrations (e.g., g(u)=σ(a·u+b)) learned on validation data?
- Provide ablations on λ_R and alternatives to the KL prior; how reliable is u^bb as a surrogate?
- For detection, report full per-class APs, standard deviations (e.g., across folds), and isolate the contribution of the confidence map from bbox regression modifications.
- Can you provide convergence behavior (iterations, time) and scalability metrics?

Minor comments
- Many references are incomplete; please fix and add missing related work on soft/probabilistic labels and weakly supervised segmentation/detection.
- Discuss the computational overhead of duplicating examples (if that’s how you implement) and memory usage.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, implementation-friendly method to incorporate soft labels.
- Conceptually clean latent extension; practical for bounding-box-only supervision.
- Some empirical gains in detection.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft-label/weighted SVM literature.
- Heuristic weakly supervised formulation with strong prior; limited analysis and ablation.
- Modest and potentially confounded empirical improvements; clarity and presentation issues.

Recommendation
Lean reject. The paper presents a practical variant of known ideas with modest empirical gains but lacks novelty, rigorous analysis, and a clear, fair evaluation. Strengthening the positioning versus prior work on soft/probabilistic labels, expanding ablations, and clarifying experimental protocols would substantially improve the submission.
