- Proposes e-SVM, an SVM variant that weights samples by continuous "positiveness" (e.g., IoU) for weakly supervised learning.  
- Introduces a latent e-SVM extension for truly weak supervision with only bounding-box annotations.  
- Empirically outperforms SVC and SVR on segment classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection (PASCAL VOC).  
- Differentiates from MIL by handling mixed foreground/background proposals and from prior weighted SVMs by weighting labels rather than data points.  
- Novel and theoretically sound formulation with clear derivation and latent-variable justification.  
- Comprehensive experiments across tasks demonstrating practicality and generality across proposal methods and features.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; Algorithm 1 aids reproducibility.  
- Ablation study is limited; needs deeper analysis of regularization terms and feature choices.  
- Lacks discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning procedures, especially for latent e-SVM regularization.  
- Justification for using NDCG in segment classification is weak and needs clearer rationale.  
- Object detection gains are modest (~1.5% average); requires discussion of reasons and future improvements.  
- Missing comparisons to more recent weakly supervised methods (e.g., consistency regularization, contrastive approaches).  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution; accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: add detailed ablation, hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, clearer NDCG justification, and comparisons to recent methods.
