- Paper proposes e-SVM: an expected-hinge loss for soft labels and a latent KL-regularized extension for weak supervision, evaluated on segment scoring, segmentation, and detection with ~1.2–1.5 AP gains on VOC07.

- Connects to prior work on soft/probabilistic/fuzzy-label SVMs, cost-sensitive/instance-weighted SVMs, latent SVMs, weakly supervised segmentation/detection, and feature-fusion for detection.

- Strength: Addresses a relevant weak/soft supervision problem for large-scale vision.

- Strength: Simple, convex base loss (expected hinge) that is straightforward to optimize.

- Strength: Practical latent extension using a KL regularizer to keep latent u near bounding-box priors.

- Strength: Empirical signal of small but consistent AP improvements on VOC07 and modest gains on segment scoring metrics.

- Strength: Modular approach compatible with different proposal generators, features, and detectors.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—the base objective is essentially equivalent to known soft/probabilistic/fuzzy-label SVM formulations and lacks proper citation/positioning.

- Weakness: Claim about compatibility with standard SVM solvers is incomplete; solvers don’t directly support per-example label-dependent weights without a reduction.

- Weakness: Latent u-update is underspecified—no closed-form or numerical scheme, constraints (0<ui<1), convergence analysis, or sensitivity study provided.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is narrow and confounded—small gains, possible attribution to the spatial confidence feature rather than e-SVM, no ablations, limited datasets, and missing variance/statistical analysis.

- Weakness: Presentation issues—typos, malformed equations, missing/incorrect references and figures, and missing implementation details hinder reproducibility.

- Quality: Observed-label case is technically sound; latent case is plausible but underspecified and claims are modestly supported.

- Clarity: Paper needs substantial editing and to specify regularizer, g(u), constraints, exact solver/reduction, u-update, stopping criteria, and hyperparameter selection.

- Originality: Base e-SVM is not novel; latent extension and spatial confidence map are incremental and resemble standard alternating-minimization latent models.

- Significance: Practical impact is limited by small gains and incomplete comparisons; method could serve as a simple baseline but is not a major advance.

- Key suggestions: Explicitly relate to and differentiate from prior fuzzy/soft-label SVM work; provide solver reduction; detail u-update algorithm and constraints; run ablations (SVR/SVC features, λR, initialization, pooling, bbox regression) and report variance/significance.

- Arguments for acceptance: Relevant problem, simple convex objective, some empirical gains, and a potentially useful baseline.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited novelty, underspecified latent optimization and reproducibility, modest/confounded empirical improvements, and poor clarity.

- Overall: Reviewer leans toward rejection pending clearer positioning relative to prior work, fuller algorithmic details, and stronger controlled experiments demonstrating that e-SVM (not just the engineered feature) drives the gains.
