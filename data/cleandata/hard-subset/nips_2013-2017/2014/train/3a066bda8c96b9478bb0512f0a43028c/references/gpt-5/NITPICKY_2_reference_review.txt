Summary
The paper proposes an “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM) for learning from weak or soft supervision. Each segment proposal Si has features xi and a “positiveness” ui ∈ [0,1], defined as IoU with the ground-truth instance. The model treats the label Yi ∈ {−1,+1} as Bernoulli with success probability µi = g(ui) (taken as µi = ui in experiments) and minimizes the expected hinge loss EYi[max(0, 1 − Yi w⊤xi)] plus ℓ2 regularization. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), ui is treated as latent and learned jointly with w via alternating minimization, regularizing u toward ubb (segment–bounding-box IoU) by the cross-entropy/KL divergence of Bernoulli(ubb) vs Bernoulli(u). The approach is evaluated on segment proposal ranking (new metrics: TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation using CPMC features, and object detection by constructing spatial confidence map features that are concatenated to RCNN features, reporting ~1.2–1.5 mAP gains on VOC07.

Relation to prior work
The loss is the expected hinge loss under probabilistic (soft) labels—an idea that has appeared in “soft-label SVM,” “fuzzy SVM,” “SVM with uncertain/probabilistic labels,” and related robust/weighted SVM variants. The latent extension with a KL regularizer resembles EM-like alternation with a prior on latent soft labels. The object detection coupling via pooled class-specific score maps relates to segmentation-aware detection features and learning-to-rank/overlap regression used in earlier segmentation/detection pipelines.

Strengths
- Simple, intuitive objective: expected hinge loss with soft labels; convex in each block; implementable with off-the-shelf SVMs via data expansion.
- Addresses a practically relevant setting (IoU-like soft supervision; weak supervision via boxes).
- The spatial confidence map feature is a reasonable way to inject segmentation cues into window-based detectors.
- Some empirical gains over SVC/SVR for segment ranking, and modest mAP gains when combined with RCNN.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Technical/methodological
- Novelty: The core idea (minimizing expected hinge with soft labels) is well known (soft/fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVM). The paper does not situate itself against this line of work nor clarify what is substantively new beyond applying µi = IoU and the particular KL regularizer. The difference claimed vs WSVM (“weights labels not data points”) is largely superficial; the objective is exactly equivalent to duplicating each xi as two weighted examples with labels ±1 and weights µi and 1−µi, hence solvable by any weighted SVM. This equivalence should be stated, proved, and compared to prior art.
- Choice of g(u): µi is set to ui without justification or calibration. IoU is not a probability that a proposal is “positive” in the binary sense; it is a continuous overlap proxy. Why not learn g or calibrate µ (e.g., via logistic link)? No sensitivity analysis is provided.
- Latent u optimization: For fixed w and g(u)=u, the u-subproblem has a simple 1D convex objective per sample. The paper neither provides the closed-form update/solver nor details numerical stability (u constrained to (0,1), handling of log terms). Convergence properties of the alternating scheme are not discussed beyond heuristics.
- Regularizer: The KL regularizer is said to be “strong,” anchoring u close to ubb. This risks undoing the benefit of learning u when ubb is a poor surrogate (segments with high box IoU can still be background). There is no ablation over λR nor alternatives (e.g., quadratic, Huber, or learned priors).
- Baselines: Missing comparisons to (i) logistic regression with soft labels (cross-entropy on µ), (ii) ranking methods (e.g., RankSVM optimizing NDCG-like objectives), (iii) bounded regression (e.g., Beta regression, isotonic regression), and (iv) soft-label deep or kernelized methods. Only SVC and SVR are considered.
- Object detection integration: The reported gains conflate multiple modifications: the new features plus a modified bounding-box regression that “slightly improved performance.” The RCNN baseline should be re-run with exactly the same modifications to isolate the contribution of the proposed features. No ablations (effect of m, max vs average pooling, CPMC vs superpixels, identity vs other g, with/without latent u) are provided.

Empirical/reproducibility
- Figures/tables are missing (Figures 1–3, Table 1 referenced but not included), making it impossible to assess results. The small table in Section 5.1.1 is malformed and mixes settings; no per-class or per-threshold breakdowns; no confidence intervals or statistical tests.
- Experimental details are insufficient: preprocessing, feature dimensions, regularization parameters (λw, λR), solver settings, stopping criteria, hardware/time, and implementation of the expected hinge with LibLinear (data duplication vs custom loss) are omitted.
- Dataset protocol for VOC07 detection is unclear (train vs trainval, evaluation split). Claims of “state of the art” are not adequately substantiated.

Clarity and presentation
- Numerous typographical/formatting issues: duplicated symbols (N N∑), odd regularizer term notation (“λw · 1 wTw”), inconsistent spacing and hyphenation, misspellings (“continues value,” “segmentic segmentation,” “segmtation”), inconsistent use of e-SVM/eSVM, and many broken/incomplete references with missing years/authors. This hinders readability and reproduction.
- Algorithm 1 lacks critical details (update formulas, constraints on u, convergence criterion).
- The “new evaluation criterion” TAPC is essentially mean AP over thresholds; the paper should specify the threshold grid and motivate why this differs from standard PR curves or ranking evaluations. NDCG is not new; presenting it as novel is misleading.

Originality
- The formulation aligns closely with soft-label/fuzzy/uncertain-label SVM literature; the paper does not engage with or distinguish itself from this body of work. The latent extension with a KL prior is a straightforward block-coordinate approach.

Significance
- The empirical gains, while potentially useful, are modest and not well-isolated. Without stronger baselines, ablations, and complete result reporting, it is hard to judge impact. The method’s generality claim is not supported by breadth of experiments.

Quality
- The work is technically plausible but underspecified. Key algorithmic details, baselines, and result artifacts are missing, preventing verification.

Pros
- Simple, convex-in-blocks objective; easy to implement via weighted data duplication.
- Addresses a practical weak supervision scenario.
- Sensible idea of pooling segment scores into spatial features for detectors.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft-label SVMs; missing citations and theoretical positioning.
- Incomplete experiments and missing figures/tables; weak baselines and no ablations.
- Poor writing/formatting; broken references.
- Unclear and potentially confounded detection improvements.
- Lack of implementation details and parameter settings.

Questions for authors
- Provide the equivalence to a standard weighted SVM on an expanded dataset and cite related soft/fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVM work.
- Specify u-update derivation and constraints; give λR selection and sensitivity.
- Justify µ = u; consider learning/calibrating g and report sensitivity.
- Include complete detection tables, exact protocols, and ablations isolating each component.
- Clarify how LibLinear was used to optimize the expected hinge.

Recommendation
Reject. While the problem is important, the core idea lacks sufficient novelty relative to established soft-label SVMs, and the paper has substantial methodological, empirical, and presentation deficiencies. A revised version addressing the above, with complete experiments, fair baselines, and proper positioning within prior work, could be much stronger.
