Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes the expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from “soft” labels ui ∈ [0,1], where ui measures positiveness (e.g., IoU with the ground-truth mask). e-SVM replaces the standard hinge loss with its expectation under a Bernoulli label with success probability µi=g(ui), and in practice sets g(ui)=ui. This yields a “soft-label” hinge loss: each example contributes to both positive and negative margins weighted by ui and 1−ui respectively, solvable with standard linear SVM tooling. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes), the paper treats ui as latent and regularizes it toward ubbi (IoU with the GT box) using a Bernoulli KL penalty, optimizing by alternating minimization over w and u. The method is evaluated on segment scoring (with two proposed evaluation criteria, TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation (via CPMC + second-order pooling), and object detection by concatenating a spatial confidence map feature computed from segment scores with RCNN features, yielding ~1.5 mAP improvement on VOC 2007.

The idea connects to a large body of work on SVMs with soft/probabilistic/ambiguous labels and fuzzy SVMs, which also reinterpret the hinge loss under label uncertainty or weight positive/negative losses differently per example. The latent extension relates to latent SVMs/EM-style weak supervision, with a regularizer that anchors latent labels toward a surrogate derived from bounding boxes. The detection integration resembles using class-specific “heatmaps” pooled over spatial grids.

Strengths:
- Clear, simple formulation of a soft-label hinge loss that can be trained with standard SVM solvers; easy to adopt in practice.
- Latent extension is intuitive and convex in each block; the KL regularizer is a sensible choice that stabilizes optimization.
- General framework: agnostic to segment proposal generator and base detector; the spatial confidence map is a practical feature.
- Empirical results show consistent gains over SVC and slight gains over SVR for segment scoring; modest but non-trivial mAP gains when added to RCNN.
- The paper proposes task-agnostic evaluation for segment ranking (TAPC and NDCG), encouraging more direct assessment of segment scoring quality.

Weaknesses:
- Novelty is limited. The expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label and “soft” labels y∈[0,1] has been studied under names such as fuzzy SVM, SVM with probabilistic labels/ambiguous labels, and cost-sensitive SVMs with label-dependent losses. The paper’s distinction from weighted SVMs is not fully convincing; closely related “soft-label SVM” formulations and citations are missing.
- The latent formulation is a straightforward alternating minimization with a strong prior toward ubb. It does not exploit image- or bag-level constraints (e.g., coverage, at-least-one, consistency across proposals), which are common in weakly supervised learning and MIL. As a result, it may not resolve ambiguities beyond what ubb already provides.
- Empirical evidence is mixed and lacks critical ablations. While e-SVM outperforms SVC and slightly SVR on TAPC/NDCG, semantic segmentation accuracy is only “comparable” to SVR. For detection, the 1.5 mAP improvement over RCNN could stem from adding any segment-based spatial feature; there is no ablation comparing e-SVM vs SVR-derived maps or other heatmap features under identical pipelines.
- Missing optimization details. Given w, minimizing over u is convex and separable, but the paper does not provide the closed-form update or numerical procedure, convergence diagnostics, or runtime/scalability with many segments.
- Clarity and presentation issues: several equations/typesetting glitches (e.g., regularization term notation, duplicate N factors), incomplete or malformed references, and insufficient detail for reproduction (hyperparameters, g(ui) alternatives, sensitivity to λR, m, pooling choices).
- The proposed TAPC and NDCG are standard ranking metrics/averaged AP over thresholds; claiming novelty here seems overstated.

Quality:
- The formulation is technically sound and the block-convexity arguments are correct, but theoretical analysis is minimal (no generalization bounds, no convergence guarantees beyond local minima, no robustness analysis to mis-specified ubb).
- Experimental design is reasonable but lacks key ablations (e.g., replacing e-SVM with SVR in the RCNN pipeline; effect of removing KL regularizer; different g(·); per-class breakdown and statistical significance).

Clarity:
- The core idea is understandable, but the paper suffers from typesetting errors, incomplete references, and missing algorithmic details that hinder reproducibility. The description of Algorithm 1 omits the actual u-update.

Originality:
- The core loss is a rephrasing of known ideas on soft/probabilistic labels for SVMs; the latent extension with KL anchoring is incremental. The spatial confidence map is a reasonable engineering feature but not conceptually novel.

Significance:
- Practical: modest mAP gains on VOC 2007 RCNN are encouraging but may be outdated and not clearly attributable to e-SVM vs alternative segment-scoring methods. Without stronger ablations and broader benchmarks, the impact is limited.
- Conceptual: provides a clean baseline for soft labels, but advances over prior art are marginal.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, general, and easy-to-implement soft-label SVM formulation with a weakly supervised extension.
- Some empirical gains, including in object detection when combined with RCNN.
- Encourages direct evaluation of segment ranking.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited novelty relative to prior SVMs with soft/uncertain labels and latent SVMs; missing key citations and comparisons.
- Insufficient ablations to isolate where gains come from; segmentation results not clearly better than strong baselines.
- Presentation and reproducibility issues; lack of optimization details and sensitivity analyses.

Recommendation: Reject.

Constructive suggestions:
- Position the work relative to fuzzy/soft-label SVMs and SVMs with probabilistic or ambiguous labels; compare empirically to these formulations and include proper citations.
- Derive and present the closed-form u-update (with g(u)=u under KL regularization), discuss convergence, and report runtime and iteration counts.
- Add ablations: replace e-SVM with SVR/SVC in the spatial confidence map for RCNN; compare to simpler heatmaps (e.g., objectness, class activation maps) under identical settings; vary λR, g(·), m, and pooling.
- Incorporate weak-supervision constraints beyond KL anchoring (e.g., bag-level constraints, consistency across overlapping proposals, coverage priors) to reduce reliance on ubb.
- Improve clarity: fix equations, provide all hyperparameters, and clean up the reference list.
- Evaluate on additional datasets and with stronger contemporary detectors to establish robustness and significance.
