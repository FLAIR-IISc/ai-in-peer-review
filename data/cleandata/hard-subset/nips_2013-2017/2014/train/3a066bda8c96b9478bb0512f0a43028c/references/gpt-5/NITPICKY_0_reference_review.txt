Summary
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), which replaces binary labels with “positiveness” scores u ∈ [0,1] (e.g., IoU). The loss is the expectation of the hinge loss under Yi ~ Bernoulli(µi), with µi = g(ui) (instantiated as µi = ui), yielding per-sample loss l+·ui + l−·(1−ui). A latent variant handles weak supervision (only bounding boxes): the unknown ui are optimized jointly with w via alternating minimization, regularized by a KL term to keep u near an initialization ubb (IoU with the bounding box). Applications include segment ranking (new TAPC and NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation with CPMC and second-order pooling, and object detection by augmenting RCNN with a “spatial confidence map” feature derived from segment scores; reported improvement is ~1.5 mAP on PASCAL 2007.

Strengths
- Simple, intuitive formulation of soft labels within a max-margin framework; the per-sample loss remains convex in w.
- The latent extension is straightforward and uses a convex-in-u regularizer; the update is likely to have a closed form.
- The spatial confidence map feature is a simple way to integrate segment confidences into window-based detectors.
- Empirical gains over SVC/SVR on proposed segment-ranking metrics; modest mAP gains when plugged into RCNN.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Quality and clarity
- Insufficient derivation/details for the latent e-SVM u-update. With g(u)=u and KL regularizer, the u-subproblem is separable with a closed-form optimum; the paper gives no derivation, no algorithmic details (solver, step size, handling u∈(0,1)), no convergence criterion, and no monotonic decrease proof. Alternating minimization is only heuristically justified.
- Equation (1) is poorly formatted and ambiguous: the regularizer reads “λw · 1 wTw” (likely ½λ||w||2). The duplicated “N N∑” appears multiple times. The notation [w b]→w and [xi 1]→xi overloads symbols and obscures bias handling.
- The claim “solvable by any standard SVM solver” is imprecise. The loss l+·ui + l−·(1−ui) corresponds to instance-dependent weights on class-conditional hinge terms; most solvers support per-instance weights but not per-label splits. Clarify whether you duplicate each xi with labels ±1 and weights ui and 1−ui, or use an instance-weighted solver; specify exact implementation.
- Hyperparameters and evaluation details are missing: values/ranges for λw and λR; how chosen; stopping criteria; initialization ubb definition; thresholds used in TAPC; exact NDCG relevance and discounting; whether scores are calibrated; which features (CPMC parameters) and training protocol.
- Segmentation evaluation is unclear. You report “accuracy 36.8% / 27.7%” without defining the metric (pixel accuracy? mean IU?) and without per-class results. Comparison to standard baselines under identical settings is insufficient; the claim “comparable to SVR” contradicts earlier claims that e-SVM outperforms SVR on TAPC/NDCG.
- Object detection experiments risk confounds. You modify the RCNN baseline (feature mapping for bbox regression) and then compare to the original numbers; ablation is missing (RCNN + your modification without e-SVM; spatial map built from SVR or SVC; effect of m and pooling type). The 1.2–1.5 mAP gain may partly come from the bbox regression change. No statistical significance or per-class analysis beyond a single example.
- Runtime and scalability are not discussed. Computing per-pixel maxima over many segments per image and per-class classifiers could be expensive; practical overhead relative to RCNN is not reported.

Originality
- The core idea—expected hinge loss with probabilistic/soft labels—is not new. Related lines include SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels and instance-dependent costs, soft-label SVMs, learning with label proportions/ambiguity, and cost-sensitive/weighted hinge losses. The paper positions itself mainly against WSVM but overlooks prior work on SVMs with probabilistic labels (and more generally proper composite losses with soft targets). This weakens the novelty claim “weights labels instead of data points.”
- The latent variable formulation with a KL prior to bounding-box-induced soft labels is reasonable but close in spirit to latent SVM with a convex regularizer; similar “posterior regularization”/KL anchoring has prior art. The spatial confidence map is an engineering contribution rather than a core algorithmic novelty.

Significance
- Practical but modest empirical gains. The new segment-ranking metrics are interesting but task-level segmentation results are weakly presented and not clearly superior. The ~1.5 mAP gain on VOC 2007 is small, lacks clean ablation, and is on an outdated baseline by current standards; without careful controls, it’s hard to attribute the gain to e-SVM.
- The method’s generality is claimed broadly (“any classifier”), yet only linear SVMs are used; no guidance on when e-SVM is preferable to, e.g., logistic regression with soft targets or calibrated regressors.

Specific questions and requests for the authors
1) Provide the closed-form update for ui with g(u)=u and the KL regularizer, and the exact alternating algorithm with convergence criterion. Does the objective decrease monotonically?
2) Clarify the implementation of Eq. (1) in a standard SVM solver. Do you duplicate samples with weights? Which solver/options?
3) Define all evaluation metrics precisely (TAPC thresholds, NDCG relevance/discount), and report standard segmentation metrics (mean IU) with strong baselines (including SVR) under identical settings.
4) For detection, provide ablations: (a) RCNN baseline with your bbox regression modification alone; (b) replacing e-SVM with SVR/SVC in the spatial map; (c) varying m and pooling; (d) CPMC vs superpixels. Report runtime overhead.
5) Justify µi=g(ui)=ui; explore/validate other g (e.g., calibrated/monotone transforms).
6) Situate the work against SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels and soft-label learning; add missing references and discuss differences.

Minor issues, typos, and formatting
- Numerous citation and formatting errors (missing years/pages, placeholders “[27, ?]”, malformed author lists; e.g., Everingham et al., Uijlings et al., Deng et al.). Fix all references.
- Typos: “continues”→continuous; “segmentic”→semantic; “segmtation”; “IoU ratio”; “positiveness” is awkward—consider “soft label” or “target score.”
- Equations: fix regularization (e.g., ½λ||w||2), remove duplicated N, define all symbols (l+i, l−i), ensure consistent vector notation, and state convexity conditions clearly.
- Algorithm 1: specify initialization of ubb, stopping conditions, and how feasibility u∈(0,1) is enforced.

Pros
- Simple, intuitive soft-label hinge formulation; convex in each block.
- Practical weakly-supervised extension with a reasonable regularizer.
- Clear engineering idea for integrating segments into detection.
- Some empirical improvements and new evaluation criteria.

Cons
- Novelty over prior soft-label/uncertain-label SVM literature is limited and under-cited.
- Missing derivations, algorithmic details, and ablations; unclear evaluation.
- Small, possibly confounded gains; weak clarity and many formatting issues.

Recommendation
Overall score: 4 (reject). The idea is sensible and potentially useful, but the current presentation has significant clarity, novelty-positioning, and experimental-rigor issues that need to be addressed.

Reviewer confidence: 4/5.
