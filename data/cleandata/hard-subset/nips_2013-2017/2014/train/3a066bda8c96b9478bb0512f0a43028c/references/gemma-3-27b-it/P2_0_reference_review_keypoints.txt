- Introduces the Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle weakly supervised labels that are continuous “positiveness” scores (e.g., IoU).
- Provides a theoretical formulation linking e-SVM to weighted SVMs and describes an iterative optimization for latent variables.
- Shows empirical improvements on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection (including ~1.5% AP gain) using PASCAL VOC.
- e-SVM is a principled, novel way to weight samples by positiveness rather than ad-hoc binarization.
- The approach is general and can integrate different proposal methods, features, and base detectors.
- Addresses an important problem given the cost of pixel-level annotation and prevalence of weak labels.
- Application to segmentation and detection largely follows established pipelines, limiting novelty in applications.
- New evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG) are introduced but their practical significance beyond this task is unclear; more common downstream metrics would help.
- The iterative latent-variable optimization lacks detailed discussion of convergence behavior and computational cost compared to standard SVMs.
- Additional ablation studies are needed (e.g., sensitivity to regularization, hyperparameters).
- Paper writing is somewhat repetitive; experimental and algorithm sections could be more concise for clarity.
- Quality rated 8/10 — technically sound with robust experiments but somewhat incremental in applications.
- Clarity rated 7/10 — generally clear but could be more concise and accessible.
- Originality rated 8/10 — novel e-SVM formulation, reasonable originality despite familiar application contexts.
- Significance rated 8/10 — meaningful impact for scaling learning with weak supervision; tangible performance gains.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and sound algorithm, consistent empirical gains, flexible framework.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental applications, limited discussion of complexity/convergence, needs more ablations.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and discuss computational complexity.
