Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning from “soft” labels in [0,1] (positiveness, e.g., IoU of segment proposals with ground-truth), and a latent variant for weak supervision where positiveness is unobserved and regularized toward a bounding-box-based surrogate. The core loss replaces binary hinge loss with the expectation over an unobserved label Y ∈ {±1} whose success probability is g(u) (set to u), yielding per-example loss l+ g(u) + l− (1 − g(u)). With pixel labels, g(u) is known; with only bounding boxes, u is optimized via alternating minimization with a convex cross-entropy regularizer R(u) that pulls u toward the bbox IoU ubb. The method is evaluated on segment proposal ranking (introducing TAPC and NDCG as metrics), semantic segmentation (with and without full supervision), and object detection by constructing a “spatial confidence map” over windows and concatenating to RCNN features, yielding +1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.

This sits between classical SVC/SVR for segments and weak supervision approaches such as MIL and latent SVM for detection. The “soft-label” hinge is closely related to SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels and to instance replication with fractional weights, and the latent extension resembles EM-style optimization with a convex prior on the latent target. The detection integration is pragmatic and similar in spirit to using segmentation cues to augment window-based detectors.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The basic e-SVM objective is convex in w when u (and g) are fixed since it is a positive linear combination of hinge losses. The latent extension alternates two convex subproblems (w and u), with a sensible convex regularizer on u. However, several points need clarification/strengthening:
  - Solver claim: “Effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver” is only true if one duplicates each sample with positive and negative labels and uses per-instance weights g(u) and 1−g(u), or if the solver supports weighted hinge per-label. Please state the reduction explicitly and how LibLinear was used (duplicate samples? instance weights?).
  - Choice of g(u): Setting g(u)=u is simple but ad hoc. Sensitivity to g (e.g., calibrated link functions) is not analyzed.
  - Optimization details for the latent step are missing (closed-form per-example update? numeric solver? constraints handling u∈(0,1)? stopping criteria and number of iterations).
  - Regularization strength λR and its effect on avoiding degeneracy (u→{0,1}) and on final performance are not ablated. A comparison to the degenerate latent-SVM limit (λR=0) would be informative.
  - Empirical support: On segmentation, e-SVM is only slightly better than SVR on TAPC/NDCG and comparable in pixel accuracy; the main gain is in detection (+1.2–1.5 mAP), which is useful but modest. Statistical variation and training stability are not reported.
- Completeness/reproducibility: Important details are missing (hyperparameters λ, λR, C, learning rates/iterations; convergence criteria; exact feature specs for each task; how ubb is computed; runtime). Figures/tables are sparse and some references/equations have formatting issues.

Clarity
- The paper’s high-level narrative is clear and the loss is easy to understand. However, the writing has numerous typos/formatting issues (e.g., “λw·1/wTw”, missing punctuation, inconsistent references) that hinder readability. The implementation of the solver reduction and the latent-update step need to be made explicit. The new evaluation metrics are only briefly defined; including precise definitions and plots (with error bars) would help.

Originality
- The idea of training SVMs with soft/probabilistic labels (expected hinge loss) has appeared in various forms (e.g., SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels, label smoothing/ambiguous labels, learning with label proportions). The paper does not cite or contrast with this line of work, and the claim that e-SVM is fundamentally different from weighted SVMs is overstated: the per-example expected hinge can be implemented via weighted duplicates (label weighting rather than instance weighting is an implementation detail).
- The latent extension with a convex cross-entropy prior toward a bbox-based surrogate is reasonable but close in spirit to EM with priors or regularized latent SVM. The “spatial confidence map” feature is a simple pooling of per-pixel maxima; useful, but not conceptually novel.

Significance
- Practical significance: A simple, implementable approach for leveraging soft labels and weak supervision that yields a modest but consistent gain when augmenting RCNN (+1.5 mAP on VOC07) is valuable. Introducing TAPC/NDCG for segment ranking is also useful.
- Scientific significance: The conceptual advance over existing soft-label/uncertain-label SVMs and latent SVM/MIL is limited. The segmentation results do not clearly advance the state of the art; the detection improvement, while positive, is incremental and shown for a single base detector and dataset without extensive ablations.

Pros
- Simple, convex subproblems; easy to implement with standard tooling.
- Clear formulation connecting soft labels to expected hinge loss.
- Latent extension with a principled convex regularizer.
- Practical improvement on a strong baseline detector.
- Generality: can plug into different proposal methods, features, and detectors.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to prior soft/probabilistic-label SVMs and latent SVM/MIL; missing citations and comparisons.
- Sparse technical details for optimization and solver reduction; reproducibility concerns.
- Modest empirical gains; limited ablations (no study of λR, g(u), initialization ubb, or comparison to λR=0 latent SVM).
- Claims about solver generality may be misleading without explicit reduction.
- Writing/formatting issues and some incomplete references.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide the explicit reduction to standard SVM (duplicate examples with weights) and implementation details; release code.
- Add comparisons to SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels, ordinal/ranking SVM, and latent SVM (λR=0), and to MIL variants designed for soft labels.
- Ablate λR, choice of g(u), initialization ubb, number of iterations; report convergence and runtimes.
- Broaden detection experiments (other detectors/datasets) and quantify variance.
- Tighten writing and fix references/equations; precisely define TAPC/NDCG.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The method is clean and practically useful, and the RCNN gains are encouraging, but the novelty over established soft-label SVM and latent SVM ideas is limited, related work is underdeveloped, and experimental/optimization details are insufficient for NeurIPS standards. Strengthening the positioning relative to prior art, adding rigorous ablations, and clarifying the implementation could elevate the contribution.
