- Proposal: Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that minimizes expected hinge loss for proposals with positiveness scores µi in [0,1], implementable by duplicating each example with weights µi and 1−µi.  
- Latent extension: Introduces latent e-SVM where per-proposal u is latent, initialized from box IoU and updated by alternating minimization with a KL (cross-entropy) regularizer to the box-derived prior.  
- Implementation claim: e-SVM is convex in w and can be solved using standard linear SVM solvers (e.g., liblinear) by treating proposals as two weighted instances.  
- Evaluation: Tested on segment proposal scoring (TAPC, NDCG), semantic segmentation with CPMC segments, and object detection by adding a spatial confidence map to RCNN, reporting ~1.2–1.5 mAP gains on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Relation to prior work: Closely related to SVMs with probabilistic/soft labels, cost-sensitive/instance-weighted SVMs, and latent SVM/MIL approaches; detection integration resembles prior segmentation-to-window cue methods.  
- Strength — simplicity: Expected hinge loss is a natural, convex surrogate for soft labels and integrates easily with standard SVM tooling.  
- Strength — weak supervision: Alternating optimization with a convex KL regularizer avoids hard assignments and provides a pragmatic bridge from box to mask signals.  
- Strength — empirical utility: Spatial confidence map is inexpensive and yields measurable mAP improvements when combined with RCNN features.  
- Strength — generality: Formulation is agnostic to proposal generators, features, and base detectors.  
- Weakness — mathematical clarity: Notational errors (e.g., regularizer written incorrectly), missing constraints (u∈(0,1)), and overall need to fix the primal objective statement.  
- Weakness — missing derivations/algorithms: u-update is underspecified despite being solvable in closed form; convergence criteria and hyperparameter choices (λ, λ_R) are not detailed.  
- Weakness — overstated novelty: Expected hinge loss is equivalent to instance-weighted duplication and parallels existing soft-label/weighted SVM literature; distinctions from WSVM are not clearly justified or cited.  
- Weakness — inadequate baselines: Missing comparisons to soft-label logistic regression/cross-entropy and calibrated regression losses; only thresholded SVC and SVR reported.  
- Weakness — modest empirical gains and missing ablations: Improvements are small/inconsistent, and ablations (latent vs observed u, KL strength, g(u), pooling/m parameters, runtime) are missing.  
- Weakness — clarity and references: Writing needs substantial editing; several malformed references and unclear figure/table descriptions.  
- Weakness — evaluation metrics: TAPC and NDCG are not novel and need proper positioning and detailed definition (threshold grids, averaging).  
- Originality concern: Contribution is incremental — repackaging expected hinge loss and a KL-regularized latent variant, plus a straightforward spatial pooling detection feature.  
- Significance concern: Practical and easy to adopt but limited impact due to modest gains and incomplete comparisons; broader significance uncertain.  
- Questions for authors: Provide exact primal objective and liblinear reduction; explicit u-update (closed form?) and bounds preventing u→0/1; hyperparameter selection and sensitivity plots.  
- Questions for authors: Compare against soft-label logistic regression and soft-label SVM baselines, and present calibration analyses.  
- Questions for authors: Robustness of u_bb initialization and alternatives (predictive geometric features, shape priors)?  
- Questions for authors: Detection ablations requested — effect of m, pooling variant, superpixel vs CPMC, using only confidence map vs concatenation, and per-category gains.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, general formulation addressing practical weak supervision, easy to adopt, and shows some empirical gains on a canonical detection benchmark.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited novelty vs existing soft/weighted SVM work, missing theoretical/algorithmic details, incomplete baselines and ablations, and clarity/reference problems.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — sensible idea but overstates novelty, lacks critical details and comparisons, and empirical evidence is insufficient for acceptance; improvements suggested to address these issues.
