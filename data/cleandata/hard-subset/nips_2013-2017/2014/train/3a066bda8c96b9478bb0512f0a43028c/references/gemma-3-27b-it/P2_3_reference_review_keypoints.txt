- Summary: Proposes e-SVM that models proposal "positiveness" as a continuous variable and extends SVMs (with a latent-variable version) for weakly supervised learning, evaluated on segmentation and detection.

- Strength: Addresses a practical weakly supervised learning problem relevant for large-scale annotation.

- Strength: Novel formulation treating positiveness as a continuous target and integrating it into the SVM loss with a latent-variable extension.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains across segment classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection (notably +1.5% on PASCAL VOC with RCNN).

- Strength: Solid theoretical justification, connection to weighted SVMs, and convexity analysis.

- Strength: General method flexible to proposal methods, features, and classifiers.

- Strength: Clear and well-written presentation.

- Weakness: Limited novelty in application domains—the contribution is more methodological than in new application areas.

- Weakness: Sensitivity to regularization of the latent variable u; KL regularizer chosen without rigorous justification or ablation.

- Weakness: Insufficient experimental comparison to recent weakly-supervised methods (e.g., consistency or MIL-based approaches).

- Weakness: Missing implementation details and hyperparameter settings, hurting reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Solid, effective, and convincing work with minor weaknesses that do not undermine the contribution.

- Scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 7/10; Significance 7/10.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel and effective algorithm, strong results, clear theory, and practical relevance.

- Arguments against acceptance: Standard application areas, limited comparisons to recent methods, and lack of implementation details.

- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions—expand related work, justify/ablate the regularizer choice, and add hyperparameter/implementation details.
