- Overall assessment: novel SVM-based e-SVM for learning from weak supervision; recommend Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Core idea: weight samples by estimated “positiveness” to handle continuous/weak label estimates.  
- Generality: method can work with any segment proposals, feature representations, and base classifiers.  
- Empirical performance: outperforms SVR/SVC with ~1.5% average improvement on PASCAL VOC; includes ablation studies.  
- Presentation: mathematical formulation and diagrams are clear and easy to follow.  
- Related work limited: insufficient engagement with weakly supervised literature beyond segmentation/detection (e.g., contrastive learning, label ambiguity methods).  
- Regularization concern: KL-divergence regularizer for latent variables seems ad-hoc and needs justification or alternatives and sensitivity analysis.  
- Theoretical analysis lacking: no deep analysis of properties (e.g., bias-variance trade-off, convergence guarantees).  
- Hyperparameter tuning unclear: paper does not detail tuning process or sensitivity to regularization parameter (λR).  
- Dependence on segment proposals: performance tied to proposal quality; experiments focus mainly on CPMC and should explore other methods.  
- Baselines limited: comparisons restricted to SVR/SVC rather than stronger, recent weakly supervised methods.  
- Recommendations: expand related work, justify/compare regularizers, add hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, include theoretical discussion, and evaluate other proposal methods.  
- Significance: addresses important problem of learning with imprecise labels and shows practical gains and compatibility with detectors like R-CNN.
