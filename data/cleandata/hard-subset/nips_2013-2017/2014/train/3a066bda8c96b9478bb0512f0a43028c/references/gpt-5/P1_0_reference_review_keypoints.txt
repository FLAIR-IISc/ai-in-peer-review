- Proposes e-SVM: expected hinge loss for soft labels µi=g(ui) (often µi=ui), convex in w when ui known, plus ℓ2 regularization.
- Extends to weak supervision by treating ui latent and alternating between w and u with a KL (cross-entropy) regularizer to IoU with bbox ubb.
- Evaluated on segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and detection; yields modest gains (e.g., +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC detection when adding e-SVM features).
- Objective is well-motivated and convex-in-w; latent extension yields convex subproblems with proposed KL regularizer.
- Claim that e-SVM “can be effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver” is imprecise; implementation requires per-example weighted hinge terms or sample duplication—needs clarification.
- Latent optimization details are under-specified: constraints on u, exact update rule, convergence criterion, initialization sensitivity, and behavior vs. λR are not characterized.
- Segmentation gains over SVR/SVC are small or comparable; main measurable benefit is detection improvement but attribution to e-SVM vs feature design is unclear.
- Need control experiment using SVR to generate the same confidence maps to isolate e-SVM advantage.
- New TAPC metric is insufficiently specified; must report threshold grid, averaging, per-class and aggregate metrics with error bars or statistical tests; NDCG is standard.
- Mathematical presentation has typos/ambiguities (regularizer notation, missing 1/2, bias handling); notation and equation consistency must be fixed.
- Related work discussion is incomplete; must better position relative to soft/probabilistic-label SVMs, WSVMs, and weakly supervised methods.
- Originality is moderate: expected hinge under Bernoulli soft labels is natural and similar ideas exist; novelty is in the clean formulation, KL-regularized latent extension, and detection pooling integration.
- Significance is limited by small empirical gains on segmentation, lack of ablations, and unclear novelty vs prior soft-label SVM literature.
- Pros: simple convex-in-w objective, applicable to weak supervision, practical detection integration, task-agnostic potential.
- Cons: unclear novelty, under-specified optimization and solver claims, limited segmentation improvements, missing ablations and rigorous analysis, clarity/reference issues.
- Suggested improvements: clarify solver reduction or custom algorithm with complexity/runtime, add ablations (λR, g(u), initialization, alternations), compare to soft-label baselines and logistic regression, report per-class stats and TAPC protocol, fix notation and references.
- Recommendation: Weak reject—promising idea and detection gains but needs clearer positioning, stronger ablations, and substantial polishing.
