- Summary: Proposes semantic labeling via candidate segments + IoU regression and object detection with bounding-box supervision using latent precise extents; uses weighted SVM and weighted transductive SVM; strong results (state-of-the-art on VOC 2007).
- Originality: Algorithmic novelty is limited; many pertinent prior works are not cited.
- Prior work missing: Weighted SVM and transductive SVM literature (e.g., Suykens et al., Yang et al., Vapnik, Lapin et al.) are not referenced.
- Sum-constraint on weights: The imposed sum constraint on weights may be novel, but reviewer did not investigate thoroughly.
- Latent e-SVM: The proposed latent e-SVM is essentially a weighted transductive SVM and not sufficiently differentiated from prior art.
- Significance: Beating recent CNN results with a conventional approach is notable and of high community interest.
- Quality: Missing crucial references; the object detection system involves substantial engineering but is within norms.
- Baselines and methodology: Authors should report standard baselines (e.g., regularized logistic regression for unit-interval regression) to justify using sum-constrained weighted SVM.
- Hyperparameter details: More detail needed on how regularization coefficients (λW and λR) were chosen.
- Clarity: Title and abstract do not clearly convey the core contribution and make a misleading claim about unit-interval targets being less informative than binary labels.
- Focus and exposition: Paper spends excessive space on small algorithmic variations and too little on critical feature-engineering details.
- Minor comments: Several typographical and notation fixes suggested (lines 19, 94, 98, 350, wording fixes).
- Recommendation: Reviewer raised quality score conditional on authors addressing prior-work relations; paper should be published after rewriting and may fit better at a computer-vision venue.
