- Paper proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous "positiveness" labels (0–1), handling observed positiveness and latent positiveness for weak supervision; evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection with claimed SOTA on PASCAL VOC 2007.

- Strength: Clear motivation for weakly supervised CV where dense pixel labels are costly and bounding boxes are more available.

- Strength: e-SVM is a reasonable extension of SVMs treating labels as random variables with success probability given by positiveness.

- Strength: Novel evaluation framework (TAPC and NDCG) for segment proposal classification is a valuable contribution.

- Weakness: Superficial connection to prior work; insufficient explanation of why existing MIL and weighted SVM methods do not apply.

- Weakness: Authors fail to cite and compare to recent relevant weakly supervised methods ([19, 20, 22] and others).

- Weakness: Theoretical contribution is minimal — e-SVM reduces to a weighted SVM and lacks justification for the chosen weighting scheme.

- Weakness: Latent extension resembles standard EM/latent SVM optimization and is not shown to be fundamentally novel.

- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are incomplete — key weakly supervised baselines are missing and the “state-of-the-art” claim is misleading without proper context.

- Weakness: Reported gains (e.g., 1.5% over RCNN) are modest relative to method complexity and lack statistical significance testing.

- Technical issue: Regularization term for latent variables is chosen without theoretical justification or exploration of alternatives.

- Technical issue: Experiments omit discussion of limitations and failure cases, weakening empirical claims.

- Writing: Generally clear, but needs a more thorough related-work discussion and a more nuanced presentation of results.

- Conclusion/recommendation: Contributions are incremental, insufficiently novel or justified for NIPS — recommendation: Weak Reject; authors should revise literature positioning, provide stronger theory, and run more comprehensive comparisons.
