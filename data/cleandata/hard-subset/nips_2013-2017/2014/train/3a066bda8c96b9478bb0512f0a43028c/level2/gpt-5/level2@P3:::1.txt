Summary
The paper addresses learning from weak supervision where examples come with a continuous “positiveness” score in [0,1] rather than a binary label. It proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), which minimizes the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label with success probability given by the positiveness (µ=g(u), set to µ=u in experiments). This yields a convex per-example loss that is a weighted combination of the positive and negative hinge losses and can be implemented using standard linear SVM solvers. A latent extension handles the case where positiveness scores are unobserved (e.g., only bounding boxes exist). It alternates between optimizing classifier parameters and the latent positiveness variables, regularized by a KL divergence that anchors them to initial IoU between segments and bounding boxes. The method is evaluated on segment proposal scoring (introducing TAPC and NDCG as task-agnostic metrics), semantic segmentation with CPMC proposals, and object detection by concatenating a “spatial confidence map” feature with RCNN features. Reported results show consistent improvements over SVC and SVR for segment scoring, competitive segmentation accuracy, and +1.2–1.5 mAP over RCNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Strengths
- Clear formulation that directly aligns the loss with continuous labels; the expected hinge interpretation is simple and practical.
- Latent variant for weak supervision with a sensible convex regularizer on the latent positiveness, leading to block-convex optimization with straightforward alternating minimization.
- Evaluation on multiple tasks with consistent gains over SVC and SVR for segment scoring and a non-trivial improvement for object detection on a strong baseline (RCNN).
- Introduction of task-agnostic metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for evaluating segment confidence prediction is useful and broadly applicable.
- The approach is modular (any proposals, any features, any base detector) and easy to incorporate into existing pipelines.

Weaknesses
- Positioning relative to prior work on soft/probabilistic/fuzzy SVMs and learning with uncertain labels is insufficient. The objective is closely related to training with soft labels and to expected hinge risk; a careful comparison to these formulations and citations to representative works are missing.
- The latent optimization lacks detail. Given g(u)=u and the proposed KL regularizer, the u-update decomposes across samples and appears to admit a simple closed-form or 1D convex optimization; the paper does not provide the derivation, complexity, or convergence diagnostics beyond noting non-convexity overall.
- Claims about outperforming alternative approaches in segmentation are not consistently supported. Reported segmentation accuracies are only “comparable” to SVR rather than better; per-class results and statistical significance are not shown.
- The detection improvements are not adequately disentangled from the feature engineering. An ablation comparing spatial confidence map features built from SVC/SVR vs e-SVM (and vs logistic regression with soft labels) is necessary to attribute gains to the learning objective rather than to the feature design.
- Several presentation issues impede clarity: typos and notation errors in the objective (regularization term), missing or corrupted references, and limited implementation details (e.g., how the expected hinge is implemented with standard solvers, hyperparameter selection, runtimes).
- The choice g(u)=u is reasonable but arbitrary; no study of alternative calibrations or the impact of calibration quality is provided.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The core objective is convex in w for fixed u and vice versa; the latent block-coordinate scheme is standard. However, key algorithmic details (u-update, convergence behavior, initialization beyond IoU) and complexity/runtime are not reported.
- Empirical support: Segment scoring comparisons are appropriate and positive; the detection improvement is promising but requires ablations to isolate the contribution of e-SVM from the proposed feature.
- Reproducibility: Basic ingredients are standard, but missing details and corrupted references hinder reproducibility.

Clarity
- The main ideas are understandable, but the manuscript would benefit from corrected notation, cleaned references, and a more thorough algorithmic description (including pseudocode with explicit per-variable updates and termination criteria).

Originality
- The expected-hinge formulation with soft labels is closely related to prior work on SVMs with probabilistic/fuzzy labels and instance-dependent costs. The latent extension with KL regularization and its application to segment scoring under weak supervision add novelty, but the conceptual increment is moderate.

Significance
- A simple, implementable method for learning from soft/weak labels is valuable. If the detection gains over RCNN are robust and attributable to the learning objective, the impact is meaningful. As presented, significance is moderate due to limited ablation and incomplete positioning.

Suggestions for improvement (feasible within a revision)
- Provide a detailed derivation of the u-update for the latent e-SVM with the KL regularizer, including complexity and convergence criteria; report the number of alternations and sensitivity to initialization.
- Include ablations for object detection: (a) replace e-SVM with SVR/SVC/soft-logistic when building the same spatial confidence map features; (b) vary the spatial binning and pooling; (c) report per-class AP changes.
- Compare to a logistic regression baseline trained with soft labels on [0,1], and to soft-label SVM/fuzzy SVM baselines, to better position e-SVM’s advantage.
- Report sensitivity analyses for g(u) (e.g., identity vs calibrated transformations) and λR; discuss calibration of positiveness estimates.
- Clarify how the expected hinge is implemented with standard solvers (e.g., sample duplication with weights, or solver that supports asymmetric per-sample losses), and provide runtimes.
- Clean up notation and references; fix typos in the objective and ensure all cited works are complete and correct. Add per-class segmentation metrics.

Arguments for acceptance
- Simple, general method for weak supervision with measurable gains on competitive baselines.
- Useful evaluation metrics for segment scoring.
- Practical integration into existing pipelines with modest engineering effort.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to known soft/probabilistic SVM formulations; insufficient related work discussion.
- Incomplete empirical analysis (missing ablations) makes it hard to attribute the detection gains to the proposed learning objective.
- Clarity and presentation issues; some results (segmentation) do not clearly surpass strong baselines.
