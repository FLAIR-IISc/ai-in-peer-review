- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from examples with continuous "positiveness" values in [0,1].  
- Models labels as Bernoulli random variables with success probability equal to the positiveness value.  
- Extends to a latent e-SVM variant for cases where positiveness is unobserved (weak supervision).  
- Applies method to semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.  
- Shows consistent improvements over SVC/SVR on PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation measured by TAPC and NDCG.  
- Reports a 1.5% average AP improvement on PASCAL VOC 2007 detection using RCNN as base.  
- Mathematical formulation and derivation of the expectation loss are rigorous and sound.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, comprehensive experiments, and applicability to both strong and weak supervision.  
- Claiming state-of-the-art is modest given small AP gain and omission of comparisons to more recent detectors (Fast/Faster R-CNN).  
- Lacks demonstration of generality beyond computer vision—no non-vision examples provided.  
- Incomplete analysis of relation to weighted SVMs (WSVMs); distinction of "weighting labels" vs "weighting data points" needs deeper explanation.  
- Technical clarity is generally good but Equation 3 (regularization term) requires more detailed explanation.  
- Originality is moderate; the setting is novel but not clearly differentiated from existing WSVM and weakly supervised methods.  
- Significance is limited by modest empirical gains; the paper would benefit from broader comparisons and complexity analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to recent object detectors, include a non-vision application, thoroughly analyze WSVM relation, and report computational complexity of latent e-SVM training.
