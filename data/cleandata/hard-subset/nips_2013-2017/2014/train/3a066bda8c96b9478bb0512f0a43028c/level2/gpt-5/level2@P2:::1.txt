Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes an “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM) that replaces a binary label with a soft “positiveness” score u ∈ [0,1], interpreted as the probability µ = g(u) that the label is positive. The training objective is the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label with mean µ, yielding a per-example loss µ·max(0, 1 − w⊤x) + (1 − µ)·max(0, 1 + w⊤x) plus L2 regularization. The method is extended to weak supervision by treating u as a latent variable when only bounding boxes are available; an alternating optimization fits w (convex SVM) and u (convex in u with a KL regularizer toward the IoU with the bounding box, ubb). Applications include scoring segment proposals (using IoU to pixel masks as u), semantic segmentation (CPMC segments + second-order pooling), and object detection (augment RCNN window features with pooled per-class confidence maps derived from segment scores). On VOC2011 segmentation, e-SVM outperforms SVC and slightly outperforms SVR on segment-scoring metrics (TAPC/NDCG); on VOC2007 detection, concatenating the new spatial confidence map with RCNN features reportedly improves mean AP by 1.2–1.5 points.

The formulation essentially learns with soft/probabilistic labels via a properly weighted hinge loss. Related lines include fuzzy/weighted SVMs, SVMs with uncertain or probabilistic labels, and MIL for weak supervision. The paper contrasts with instance-weighted SVMs by noting it “weights the labels,” not the examples. The weakly supervised extension regularizes latent soft labels toward box-overlap.

Quality:
- The expected-hinge formulation is sound and convex for fixed µ and straightforward to implement (e.g., as two per-sample hinge losses weighted by µ and 1−µ). However, the writeup of the objective has typographical issues (e.g., regularization term) and omits some details (e.g., how exactly it is mapped to LibLinear; if duplicating examples with different labels and instance weights is used).
- The latent variant is reasonable (alternating minimization between a convex SVM in w and a convex subproblem in u with separable KL regularization), but lacks theoretical analysis (e.g., properties of fixed points, sensitivity to λR and g(·)). The choice µ = u (identity link) and the choice of cross-entropy regularization toward ubb (IoU to the bounding box) are heuristic and not justified empirically beyond a single setting.
- Experiments on segment scoring are appropriate and the introduction of TAPC/NDCG is helpful. Improvements over SVC and SVR are consistent but modest. For semantic segmentation, the final pixel accuracy is reported as “comparable to SVR,” which somewhat tempers the earlier gains in segment scoring.
- The detection results are promising (1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC2007 RCNN), but the experimental controls need clarification: precise baseline numbers, whether any modifications to bounding-box regression or feature mapping are applied equally to both baselines and the proposed method, and ablations (effect of m, pooling, using e-SVM vs SVR for the map, using ubbi-only without latent refinement, etc.). Runtime and memory overhead are not discussed.

Clarity:
- The paper is generally readable and the high-level idea is clear, but several equations and references have formatting errors or missing pieces; the regularization term is inconsistently typeset, and Algorithm 1 is skeletal. Important implementation choices (g(u), λR selection, convergence criteria, closed-form update for u if any) are underspecified. Some references are garbled or incomplete.

Originality:
- The core idea—optimizing the expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label with mean µ—is a natural and known extension of SVMs to probabilistic/soft labels (cf. probabilistic-label SVMs, label-uncertain/fuzzy SVM variants). The paper should more thoroughly position itself against that literature and clarify what is new theoretically. The latent extension with a KL prior toward box-derived soft scores for segment classification under weak supervision and the use of the resulting confidence maps in object detection are more novel in the presented context.

Significance:
- The method is simple, general, and easy to drop into existing pipelines; the detection gains on a strong baseline are potentially valuable. That said, the improvements on semantic segmentation (end-task metric) are not clearly superior to SVR, and the novelty on the learning objective is incremental relative to prior soft-label SVM formulations. The detection augmentation is practically interesting but would benefit from stronger controls and broader comparisons (e.g., to logistic regression with soft labels, calibrated regression, learning-to-rank losses).

Pros:
- Simple, intuitive expected-hinge loss with soft labels; convex when u observed.
- Practical weakly supervised extension with a convex-in-u regularized subproblem.
- Consistent improvements over SVC/SVR on segment scoring; competitive segmentation.
- Non-trivial mAP gain on RCNN via a clean integration (spatial confidence maps).
- Introduces reasonable segment-ranking metrics (TAPC/NDCG).

Cons:
- Limited theoretical novelty; insufficient discussion of closely related soft/probabilistic-label SVM work.
- Heuristic choices (µ = u, KL prior to ubb) not justified or ablated.
- Sparse implementation details; unclear mapping to standard solvers; convergence and sensitivity not analyzed.
- Detection experiments need tighter baselines and ablations; potential confounds with feature and bbox-regression modifications.
- Numerous typos/formatting issues in equations and references.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Position more rigorously relative to prior art on SVMs with uncertain/probabilistic labels, fuzzy SVM, and related convex surrogates; provide citations and discuss differences.
- Clarify g(·) and explore alternatives (e.g., calibrated link functions mapping IoU to probability, class-dependent calibration); include ablations on λR and the role of the KL prior vs other convex priors.
- Provide the exact optimization for u (closed form or 1D convex solver), convergence criteria, and complexity; show convergence behavior.
- Detail how the expected hinge is implemented with standard SVM solvers (e.g., sample duplication and instance weights) and whether class-weighting is used.
- Strengthen experimental controls: report exact RCNN baselines you reproduce; ensure any additional feature mappings or bbox-regression tweaks are applied identically to baselines; add ablations of the spatial pooling design and alternative learners (SVR/logistic with soft labels).
- Improve clarity and fix typographical and citation errors; specify datasets, splits, and hyperparameter selection procedures.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The methodological core is incremental, but the weakly supervised extension and practical detection gains are of interest to the community, provided the paper clarifies related work, strengthens empirical analysis, and improves presentation.

Reviewer confidence: Medium.
