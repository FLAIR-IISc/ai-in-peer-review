- Proposes e-SVM, an extension of SVMs that directly handles continuous "positiveness" supervision in [0,1].
- Extends e-SVM to a latent formulation for weak supervision where positiveness is unobserved (e.g., bounding boxes).
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection tasks.
- Well-motivated problem formulation with a technically sound algorithm and well-justified loss function.
- Provides clear mathematical formulations and pseudocode for the optimization algorithm.
- Introduces two novel evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for better assessment of segment proposal classification.
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple computer vision tasks with statistically significant results.
- Consistent improvements over SVC and SVR baselines on PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation.
- Reports a 1.5% average AP improvement on PASCAL VOC 2007 object detection compared to R-CNN.
- Novelty: expectation loss formulation handles continuous supervision without thresholding, differentiating it from MIL and weighted SVMs.
- Significance: addresses a major bottleneck in vision by reducing reliance on costly pixel-level annotations.
- Limitation: "state-of-the-art" detection claim is overstated without comparisons to more recent detectors.
- Limitation: impact of the regularization term is insufficiently analyzed.
- Limitation: lacks comparisons to more recent weakly supervised learning methods and additional datasets for validation.
- Overall: strong contribution with clear presentation and useful empirical gains; minor clarifications would strengthen the paper for NIPS.
