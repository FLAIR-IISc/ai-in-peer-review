## Review of "Learning From Weakly Supervised Data by The Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm"

This paper proposes a novel Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm designed for learning from data with continuous “positiveness” values instead of binary labels, particularly relevant for weakly supervised learning scenarios like segment proposal classification. The core idea is to weight samples based on their positiveness, effectively bridging the gap between standard binary classification and regression approaches. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of e-SVM on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks, achieving state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.

The paper addresses a practical problem in computer vision – the scarcity of fully labeled datasets. The proposed e-SVM offers a compelling solution by leveraging readily available weak labels (e.g., bounding boxes) and estimating the confidence of segment proposals. The theoretical justification for the loss function is sound, and the extension to a latent variable model for handling unobserved positiveness is well-motivated. The experimental results are comprehensive, showcasing improvements over existing methods (SVC, SVR) on both segment classification and downstream tasks. The inclusion of ablation studies and comparisons with state-of-the-art object detection methods strengthens the claims.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations. While the authors mention the generalizability of the approach, they don’t delve into potential failure cases or scenarios where e-SVM might underperform. The regularization term in the latent e-SVM model, while justified, could be explored with more sensitivity analysis to understand its impact on performance. Additionally, the paper lacks a discussion of computational complexity compared to alternative methods. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a relevant and important problem in weakly supervised learning.
*   Presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm (e-SVM).
*   Demonstrates strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on object detection.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation of the method and experiments.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of limitations and potential failure cases.
*   Lack of analysis of computational complexity.
*   The regularization term in the latent e-SVM could benefit from further exploration.

**Overall:**

Despite minor shortcomings, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of weakly supervised learning. The e-SVM algorithm is a promising approach for leveraging weak labels and improving performance on various computer vision tasks. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations and computational complexity concerns. The authors should also consider expanding the discussion on potential failure cases.
