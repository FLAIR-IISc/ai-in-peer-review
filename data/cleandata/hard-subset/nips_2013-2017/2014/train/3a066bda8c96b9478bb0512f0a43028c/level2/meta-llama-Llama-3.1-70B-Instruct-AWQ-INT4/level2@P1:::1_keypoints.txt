- Tackles a challenging weakly supervised learning problem where labels are continuous within a bounded interval.
- Proposes a novel expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) as an extension of classical SVC models.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on segment proposal classification and shows flexibility to extend for weak supervision.
- Provides thorough evaluation on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Assumes positiveness scores lie in [0,1], which may not hold in all real scenarios.
- Omits detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity for large-scale datasets.
- Lacks comprehensive comparison with other weakly supervised methods (e.g., MIL, weighted SVMs).
- Technically sound with clear description and well-designed experiments, but limited analysis of limitations and applications.
- Well-written with clear notation, though some sections (related work) could be more concise and focused.
- Original contribution as an SVC extension addressing a hard problem, but needs deeper analysis of connections to prior weakly supervised methods.
- Significant potential impact for weakly supervised learning in computer vision, demonstrated on major tasks.
- Pro-acceptance: novel algorithm, thorough evaluation, and potential field impact.
- Con-acceptance: restrictive label assumption, missing complexity analysis, and insufficient method comparisons.
