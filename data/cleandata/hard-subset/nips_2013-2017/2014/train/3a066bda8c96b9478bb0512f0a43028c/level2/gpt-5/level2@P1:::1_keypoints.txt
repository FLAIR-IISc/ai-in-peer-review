- Introduces expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM): extend linear SVM to soft labels in [0,1] by minimizing expected hinge loss as a convex mix of positive/negative hinge terms.  
- Proposes latent e-SVM for weak supervision: jointly optimize u (soft IoU) and w with a KL (cross-entropy) regularizer anchored to u_bb, alternating convex updates.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal scoring (PASCAL VOC 2011) and integrated into RCNN detection (VOC 2007) via a spatial confidence map; yields ≈+1.5 mAP on RCNN.  
- Strength: simple, general, and easy to implement using standard linear SVM solvers (per-instance mixing of hinge losses).  
- Strength: convex u-update regularized by cross-entropy provides a principled weakly-supervised extension.  
- Strength: practical spatial confidence map effectively injects segment-level evidence into window-based detectors.  
- Strength: empirical detection gains are non-trivial and indicate complementary information to RCNN.  
- Weakness: modest novelty—expected hinge for probabilistic/soft labels overlaps with prior work on uncertain/soft/partial labels and related SVM variants.  
- Weakness: latent extension may depend heavily on initialization u_bb and λ_R; no sensitivity analysis or ablation for R(u).  
- Weakness: limited baselines—missing comparisons to logistic regression on soft labels, ranking/ordinal SVMs, calibrated regressors, and simple pooling integration methods.  
- Weakness: light theoretical analysis—no generalization guarantees, calibration rationale for g(u)=u, or convergence discussion for alternating minimization.  
- Weakness: missing practical implementation details (how expected hinge is implemented in LibLinear, u constraints/solver, runtimes, memory).  
- Quality: formulation is sound and convex for fixed u; alternating minimization is standard but global optimality not guaranteed; experiments are reasonable but need more ablations.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and readable; core idea clear but needs more implementation specifics and precise metric definitions (TAPC, NDCG averaging).  
- Originality: moderate—the particular combination and RCNN integration is useful, but core concepts aren’t novel and related literature should be better cited.  
- Significance: addresses an important weakly supervised scenario and is practitioner-friendly, but broader impact limited by modest novelty and gains.  
- Key suggestions: justify g(u)=u or try calibrated mappings, detail u-update solver/constraints, provide sensitivity to λ_R, compare to soft-label/ranking baselines, and ablate spatial map design choices.  
- Additional requests: analyze failure modes when u_bb is misleading, consider spatial coherence priors, report per-class effects, training time, and memory scaling.  
- Pros for acceptance: relevant problem, simple general method, empirical improvement on a strong detector, easy to adopt.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, narrow experimental comparisons and ablations, and reliance on heuristic choices lacking validation.  
- Overall: practical and promising for detection, but needs stronger related-work positioning, broader comparisons, and sensitivity/ablation studies to solidify the contribution.
