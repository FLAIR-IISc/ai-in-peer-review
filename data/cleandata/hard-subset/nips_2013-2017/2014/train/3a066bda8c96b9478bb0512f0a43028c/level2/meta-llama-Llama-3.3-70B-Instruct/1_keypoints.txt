- Proposes a novel expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous training labels within a bounded interval.  
- Demonstrates e-SVM effectiveness for segment proposal classification under both strong and weak supervision, outperforming SVC and SVR.  
- Applies e-SVM to semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear explanation of the algorithm, strengths, and limitations.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and includes detailed comparisons with existing methods.  
- Strength: introduces a new learning algorithm for continuous bounded labels.  
- Strength: shows strong empirical performance across multiple computer vision tasks.  
- Strength: provides comprehensive experimental validation and comparisons.  
- Weakness: assumes segment positiveness is a continuous bounded value, which may not hold in real-world scenarios.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential applications beyond computer vision.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel method, strong empirical results, and thorough experiments support publication.  
- Con-acceptance: realism of label assumption, missing complexity analysis, and narrow application discussion argue for caution.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add computational complexity analysis, discuss broader applications, and include more experiments demonstrating robustness.
