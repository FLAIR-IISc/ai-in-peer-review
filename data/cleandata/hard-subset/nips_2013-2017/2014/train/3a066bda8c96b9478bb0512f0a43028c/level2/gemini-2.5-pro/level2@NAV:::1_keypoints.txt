- Proposes e-SVM, a novel SVM variant for weak supervision where labels are continuous "positiveness" scores (e.g., IoU).  
- Models hinge loss as an expectation over a latent binary label with Bernoulli probability tied to the known positiveness score.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM to handle unobserved positiveness (e.g., only bounding-box supervision) by jointly optimizing the latent variable and classifier.  
- Offers a principled alternative to heuristics like thresholding for SVC or directly using SVR.  
- Originality & quality: elegant, novel max-margin extension with sound technical approach and well-motivated latent-variable formulation.  
- Experimental validation: rigorous tests on PASCAL VOC showing significant empirical gains over SVR and SVC variants.  
- Practical significance: outperforms strong baselines and yields a notable 1.5% AP gain on top of R-CNN for VOC 2007 object detection.  
- Clarity: well-written, well-organized, and introduces useful new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for the task.  
- Weakness: optimization of latent e-SVM needs more discussion (sensitivity to initialization and convergence behavior).  
- Weakness: choice of KL-divergence regularizer R(u) would benefit from comparison or ablation against alternatives.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, significant, and original work with strong experimental support; strong recommendation for acceptance.
