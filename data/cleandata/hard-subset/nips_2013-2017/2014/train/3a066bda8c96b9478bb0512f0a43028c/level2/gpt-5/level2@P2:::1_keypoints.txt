- Proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that replaces binary labels with soft positiveness u∈[0,1] and minimizes expected hinge loss plus L2 regularization.  
- Extends to weak supervision by treating u as latent with alternating minimization: convex SVM update for w and convex u-update regularized toward IoU-derived u_bb.  
- Applies to segment scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection; reports improved TAPC/NDCG for segment scoring and +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC2007 RCNN.  
- Method is essentially learning with probabilistic/soft labels via a weighted hinge loss, related to fuzzy/uncertain-label SVM literature.  
- Expected-hinge is sound and convex for fixed µ and easy to implement, but paper has typographical errors and omits implementation mapping to solvers like LibLinear.  
- Latent variant is reasonable (convex in each block) but lacks theoretical analysis of fixed points, sensitivity to λ_R, and justification for link g(·).  
- Choices µ = u and KL regularizer toward u_bb are heuristic and not empirically justified beyond a single setting.  
- Segment-scoring experiments show consistent but modest gains over SVC/SVR; semantic segmentation end-task accuracy is only comparable to SVR.  
- Detection gains are promising but experimental controls are unclear: missing precise baselines, equal application of bbox-regression/feature changes, and required ablations.  
- Runtime and memory overhead, convergence criteria, and sensitivity analyses are not reported.  
- Paper is readable at a high level but contains formatting errors, incomplete equations, a skeletal algorithm, and underspecified implementation details.  
- Originality is limited at the objective level (ties to prior soft-label SVMs), while the latent box-regularized soft-labels and use in detection are more novel.  
- Significance is practical due to simplicity and detection improvements, but empirical and theoretical support is incomplete and comparisons to other soft-label learners are needed.  
- Pros: simple expected-hinge formulation, convex latent subproblem, consistent segment-scoring gains, non-trivial RCNN mAP improvement, new segment-ranking metrics.  
- Cons: limited theoretical novelty, heuristic/underspecified choices, sparse implementation details, insufficient detection ablations, and many typos/formatting issues.  
- Suggestions: better position relative to prior work, justify/ablate g(·) and λ_R, provide u-optimization details and convergence plots, explain solver mapping, and strengthen detection baselines and ablations.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—incremental methodological novelty but practically interesting weakly supervised extension and detection gains.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
