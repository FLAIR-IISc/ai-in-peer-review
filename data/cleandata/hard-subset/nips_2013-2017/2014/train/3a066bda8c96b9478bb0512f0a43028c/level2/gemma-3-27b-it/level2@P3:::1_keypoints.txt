- Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous “positiveness” scores instead of binary labels.  
- Extends e-SVM to weakly supervised/latent-variable settings for segment proposal classification.  
- Demonstrates strong performance across tasks (segment classification, semantic segmentation, object detection) with SOTA on PASCAL VOC 2007 detection.  
- Core idea of adapting SVMs to continuous confidence scores is practical and well-motivated by labeling costs.  
- Mathematical formulation and latent-variable extension are clear and thoughtfully presented.  
- Experimental validation shows versatility and consistent improvements over SVC and SVR baselines.  
- Spatial confidence map feature combined with an existing CNN detector is a simple yet effective augmentation.  
- Related-work discussion (especially on Multiple Instance Learning) is brief and needs more explicit connections and comparisons.  
- Differences and relationships between e-SVM and weighted SVMs are underexplained; the advantage claim needs deeper justification.  
- Lacks detailed explanation of why e-SVM’s specific weighting scheme is particularly suitable for the problem.  
- Insufficient analysis of the regularization term in latent e-SVM, including sensitivity to regularization parameters.  
- Choice of KL-divergence regularizer is presented without discussion of alternatives or rationale.  
- Reported 1.5% improvement on VOC2007 lacks statistical significance testing to support the claimed gain.  
- Potential use of ImageNet data is mentioned but not explored experimentally.  
- Minor clarity issues: inconsistent notation (e.g., ui) and some overly complex sentences that could be simplified.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and practical contribution; accept with minor revisions to expand related work, regularization analysis, and include statistical tests.
