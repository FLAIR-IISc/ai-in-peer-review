- Proposes e-SVM: minimize expected hinge loss for examples with continuous positiveness µ in [0,1], yielding a convex per-example loss implementable with standard linear SVM solvers.  
- Introduces a latent variant that alternates between optimizing classifier weights and latent positiveness u, regularized by a KL term anchored to IoU.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal scoring (introduces TAPC and NDCG), semantic segmentation with CPMC proposals, and object detection (adds spatial confidence map to RCNN), showing consistent gains for segment scoring and +1.2–1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Strength: Clear, simple formulation that directly aligns loss with continuous labels via expected hinge interpretation.  
- Strength: Latent variant uses a sensible convex KL regularizer and block-convex alternating minimization.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation across multiple tasks with consistent improvements over SVC/SVR for segment scoring.  
- Strength: Introduction of task-agnostic metrics (TAPC, NDCG) for segment confidence prediction is broadly useful.  
- Strength: Method is modular — works with any proposals, features, or base detector and is easy to integrate.  
- Weakness: Poor positioning relative to prior soft/probabilistic/fuzzy SVM and uncertain-label literature; missing comparisons and citations.  
- Weakness: Latent u-update lacks derivation, complexity analysis, and convergence diagnostics despite appearing decomposable and simple.  
- Weakness: Segmentation claims are not clearly stronger than baselines (results often only comparable); per-class results and statistical tests are missing.  
- Weakness: Detection gains may be due to feature engineering (spatial confidence map) rather than e-SVM; ablations comparing features trained with different objectives are missing.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues — typos/notation errors, corrupted references, and insufficient implementation details (solver use, hyperparameters, runtimes).  
- Weakness: Choice g(u)=u is reasonable but arbitrary; no study of calibration alternatives or impact of calibration quality.  
- Quality: Objective is convex in w for fixed u and vice versa; block-coordinate scheme is standard but key algorithmic details and runtimes are unreported.  
- Empirical support: Segment scoring results are positive; detection improvements promising but require ablation to isolate contributions.  
- Reproducibility: Ingredients are standard but missing details and bad references hinder reproducibility.  
- Clarity: Main ideas understandable but manuscript needs corrected notation, cleaner references, and explicit pseudocode with termination criteria.  
- Originality: Close to existing soft/probabilistic SVM formulations; latent KL-regularized extension and applications add moderate novelty.  
- Significance: Simple, implementable method for soft/weak labels is valuable; impact depends on whether detection gains are attributable to the learning objective.  
- Suggested fixes: derive and report u-update details, alternation counts, and convergence; provide detection ablations (SVC/SVR/logistic baselines), per-class AP, and feature-only comparisons.  
- Suggested fixes: compare to soft-label SVM/logistic baselines, study g(u) calibrations and λR sensitivity, and report runtimes and solver implementation details.  
- Suggested fixes: fix notation and references, include pseudocode and per-class segmentation metrics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general method with measurable gains, useful evaluation metrics, and easy integration into pipelines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty vs prior soft-label SVMs, incomplete empirical analysis (missing ablations), and clarity/presentation issues.
