- Novelty: Introduces e-SVM, a novel approach for handling continuous-valued “positiveness” labels in weakly supervised binary classification.  
- Experimental validation: Extensive experiments show e-SVM consistently outperforms SVC and SVR in strong and weak supervision settings.  
- Practical relevance: Demonstrates real-world applicability by improving semantic segmentation and RCNN-based object detection performance.  
- Clarity: Mathematical formulation, latent extension, optimization, and pseudo-code are well-explained and reproducible.  
- Generalizability: e-SVM is flexible and applicable to various classifiers, proposal detectors, and image features.  

- Limited limitations discussion: Paper lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.  
- Insufficient related work context: Needs better comparison to recent weakly supervised and deep learning approaches.  
- Evaluation metrics: Relies on newer metrics (TAPC, NDCG) without enough comparisons using standard metrics.  
- Supervision assumptions: Assumes availability of bounding box annotations and does not explore weaker supervision like image-level labels.  

- Recommendation: Strong contribution suitable for acceptance with minor revisions.  

- Pro acceptance: Novel, practical algorithm; state-of-the-art benchmark results; clear and reproducible methodology.  
- Con acceptance: Limited analysis of efficiency/scalability; insufficient comparisons with recent deep learning methods.  

- Overall: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
