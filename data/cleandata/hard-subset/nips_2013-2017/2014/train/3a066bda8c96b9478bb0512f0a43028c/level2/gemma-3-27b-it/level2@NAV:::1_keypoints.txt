- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous “positiveness” values by treating them as probabilistic labels and adapting the SVM loss.  
- Extends the model to handle unobserved positiveness via a latent variable formulation and an iterative optimization (Algorithm 1).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving SOTA on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive and shows consistent superiority of e-SVM over SVC and SVR.  
- Introduces two new evaluation metrics for segment proposal classification: TAPC and NDCG.  
- Explanations of the latent variable model and the iterative optimization are unclear and need more detail.  
- Motivation for the chosen regularization term on the latent variable is weak.  
- Related work is referenced but lacks a critical discussion of their limitations relative to e-SVM.  
- Ablation studies are limited; the contribution of the spatial confidence map feature and other components is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Paper omits discussion of computational complexity and runtime implications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify technical details, strengthen related-work discussion, and provide deeper experimental analyses.
