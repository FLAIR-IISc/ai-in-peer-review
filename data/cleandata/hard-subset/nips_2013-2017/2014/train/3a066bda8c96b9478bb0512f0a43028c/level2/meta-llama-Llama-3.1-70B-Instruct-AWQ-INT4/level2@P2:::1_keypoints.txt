- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from sample "positiveness" instead of binary labels.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.  
- Provides clear formulation of e-SVM and the optimization algorithm.  
- Experimental results are well-presented with comparisons and discussion of strengths/weaknesses.  
- Effectively addresses weakly labeled data common in computer vision and achieves state-of-the-art performance on several tasks.  
- Lacks thorough theoretical analysis (no convergence guarantees or generalization bounds).  
- Does not compare against relevant weakly supervised methods like multiple instance learning (MIL) or weighted SVMs.  
- Original algorithmic contribution is novel, but the weighted-loss idea is not entirely new.  
- Quality score: 8/10 — solid paper but needs more theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity score: 9/10 — clear and well-organized, minor technical clarifications needed.  
- Originality score: 7/10 — new algorithm but related to existing weighted-loss approaches.  
- Significance score: 8/10 — addresses an important problem and achieves strong empirical results.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel SVM variant, effective for weak labels, well-written presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: missing theoretical analysis, lacking comparisons to other weak-label methods, limited novelty of weighted-loss idea.
