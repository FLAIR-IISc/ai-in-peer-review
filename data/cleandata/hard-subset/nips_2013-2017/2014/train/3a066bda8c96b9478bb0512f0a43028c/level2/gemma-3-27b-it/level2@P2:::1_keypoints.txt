Proposes e-SVM: modify SVM loss to weight examples by continuous "positiveness" scores, treating binary labels as latent variables.

Evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection; achieves state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.

Builds on MIL and weighted SVM literature and acknowledges related work, but could discuss weighting scheme differences more deeply.

Problem framing (learning from continuous positiveness scores) is well-motivated and common in vision tasks with expensive labels.

Novelty: simple yet effective modification to SVM loss for weak supervision.

Strong empirical results and ablation studies showing e-SVM outperforms SVC/SVR and generalizes across tasks.

Clear problem formulation and intuitive explanation of positiveness and its connection to IoU.

Practical algorithm design that leverages existing SVM solvers; iterative optimization for latent variables is clearly described.

Thorough evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks; introduces TAPC and NDCG metrics for segment proposal classification.

Related-work discussion is limited on continuous-label/noise-modeling/label-smoothing approaches.

Needs deeper theoretical comparison between e-SVM weighting and traditional weighted SVMs.

Latent e-SVM relies on regularization to avoid poor optima, but lacks analysis of why certain regularizers work or how to tune them.

Spatial confidence map feature and its integration with RCNN are under-explained; "additive kernels approximation" is vague.

Lacks error analysis: no breakdown of failure modes or cases where e-SVM performs poorly.

Figure 1 is low resolution, making the algorithm flow hard to follow.

Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective method with strong practical results, clear writing, and broad applicability.

Arguments against acceptance: needs more comprehensive related work, regularization analysis, clearer spatial-map details, and error analysis.

Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address related work, regularization, spatial confidence map details, and include error analysis.
