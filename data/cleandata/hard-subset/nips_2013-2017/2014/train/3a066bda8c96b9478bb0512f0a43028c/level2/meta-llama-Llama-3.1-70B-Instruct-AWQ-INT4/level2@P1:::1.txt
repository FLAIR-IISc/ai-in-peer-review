The paper "Learning From Weakly Supervised Data by The Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm" proposes a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that is devoted to problems where only the "positiveness" instead of a binary label of each training sample is available. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm on segment proposal classification tasks under both strong supervision and weak supervision, and further validate their method on two major tasks of computer vision: semantic segmentation and object detection.

Strengths:
1. The paper tackles a challenging problem in weakly supervised learning, where the labels of training data are continuous values whose range is a bounded interval.
2. The authors propose a novel algorithm, e-SVM, which is an extension of the classical SVC models, and demonstrate its effectiveness on segment proposal classification tasks.
3. The algorithm is flexible and can be easily extended to learn segment classifiers under weak supervision.
4. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their algorithm on two major tasks of computer vision: semantic segmentation and object detection, and achieve state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 dataset.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper assumes that the positiveness of each segment proposal is a continuous value in the range of [0, 1], which may not be the case in all scenarios.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their algorithm, which may be an important consideration for large-scale datasets.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other weakly supervised learning methods, such as Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) and weighted SVMs.

Quality:
The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and concise description of their algorithm and its evaluation. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the algorithm and its potential applications.

Clarity:
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise description of their algorithm and its evaluation. The notation is well-defined, and the equations are easy to follow. However, some of the sections, such as the related work section, could be more concise and focused.

The paper proposes a novel algorithm, e-SVM, which is an extension of the classical SVC models. The algorithm is original and addresses a challenging problem in weakly supervised learning. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the connections between e-SVM and other weakly supervised learning methods.
