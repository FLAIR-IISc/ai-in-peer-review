- Introduces e-SVM: models continuous "positiveness" scores as Bernoulli probabilities and minimizes the expected hinge loss, reducing to a weighted SVM.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision by alternating optimization of latent positiveness scores and SVM parameters with a regularizer toward initial estimates (e.g., IoU).  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection; segmentation-trained features integrated into R-CNN yield a significant ~1.5% mAP improvement on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Clearly differentiates from MIL and instance-weighted SVMs: e-SVM weights labels (positive/negative probabilities) rather than instances themselves.  
- Provides a principled alternative to ad-hoc thresholding or direct regression for learning from imperfect overlap of segment proposals.  
- Method is technically sound; expectation-loss formulation and reduction to weighted SVM are principled and practically advantageous.  
- Alternating optimization for latent variables is standard and reasonable for the problem setting.  
- Introduces new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment proposal classification, enabling fairer assessment.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with helpful pseudocode and figures, and clearly explains integration into detection pipelines.  
- The expectation loss idea and latent positiveness formulation are novel contributions with practical relevance.  
- The method is general, simple, effective, and likely to influence future weakly supervised learning work.  
- Suggestion: include explicit SVR baseline numbers in the segmentation results table for direct quantitative comparison.  
- Suggestion: report sensitivity analysis for the KL-divergence regularization parameter (λR) used on latent positiveness u.  
- Suggestion: specify the convergence criterion used in Algorithm 1 (e.g., loss change threshold or max iterations) for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled method, strong empirical support, clear improvement over state-of-the-art, and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reviewer found no major flaws; only minor clarifications suggested.  
- Overall: a valuable, impactful, well-executed contribution to weakly supervised learning and object detection.
