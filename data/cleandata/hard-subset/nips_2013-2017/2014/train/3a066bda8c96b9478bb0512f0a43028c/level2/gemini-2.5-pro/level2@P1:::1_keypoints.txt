- Paper summary: introduces e-SVM that treats continuous "positiveness" as Bernoulli success probabilities and minimizes expected hinge loss, extendable to a latent e-SVM for weak supervision and solvable with standard SVM solvers.  
- Validation: evaluated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, improving R-CNN by 1.5% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 and proposing TAPC/NDCG metrics.  
- Strength — Novelty: e-SVM is a principled, elegant formulation linking continuous positiveness to classification via expected hinge loss.  
- Strength — Practicality/generalizability: method addresses common weak-supervision settings (e.g., bounding boxes) and can be optimized with existing solvers.  
- Strength — Empirical evidence: comprehensive experiments and a notable state-of-the-art gain demonstrate practical value.  
- Strength — Significance: provides a useful tool likely to be adopted and extended in weakly supervised learning.  
- Weakness — Relation to weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs: need a formal comparison showing how e-SVM differs from or improves upon WSVM/cost-sensitive formulations.  
- Weakness — Latent model regularizer: choice of KL-divergence regularizer and sensitivity to λ_R seems arbitrary; other regularizers and hyperparameter effects should be discussed.  
- Weakness — Feature reproducibility: more detail needed on spatial confidence map features and segment proposal generation (e.g., number of segments, pooling, kernel approximations) for reproducibility.  
- Weakness — Evaluation clarity: clarify whether "weakly-labelled bounding-box annotation" corresponds to the latent e-SVM setup in experimental descriptions.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is technically sound, useful, and recommended for acceptance.
