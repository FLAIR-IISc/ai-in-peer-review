- Proposes a novel expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous "positiveness" values instead of binary labels.  
- Demonstrates e-SVM on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection tasks.  
- Claims e-SVM effectively handles segment proposal classification under both strong and weak supervision.  
- Claims e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR and achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Experimental support includes comparisons on PASCAL VOC 2011 segmentation and applications to semantic segmentation and detection.  
- Results show consistent improvement over SVC/SVR using TAPC and NDCG metrics.  
- Method is useful for reducing labeling burden by learning from weakly supervised data common in real-world settings.  
- Paper cites relevant prior work and provides a clear, understandable explanation of the algorithm and assumptions.  
- Novelty includes the expectation loss SVM and a latent extension for unobserved positiveness values.  
- Paper provides sufficient implementation and experimental details to support reproducibility.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, including need for further work using bounding-box annotations to boost segmentation.  
- Concerns: assumes continuous bounded positiveness values, which may not hold in all real-world cases.  
- Concerns: lacks a thorough analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity.  
- Concerns: could discuss relationships to other weakly supervised learning approaches in more depth.  
- Overall rating 9/10 with recommendation to accept subject to minor revisions addressing noted limitations.
