- Proposes e-SVM, a novel algorithm that treats “positiveness” as a probability and weights examples by this value for weakly supervised learning.  
- Validated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Well-motivated solution to learning from weak labels, addressing practical annotation cost issues in vision.  
- Novelty: bridges binary classification and regression by handling continuous confidence values.  
- Practicality: directly targets weakly labeled data common in computer vision applications.  
- Strong empirical performance across multiple tasks, with clear improvements over existing methods.  
- Clear writing and explanation of algorithm, experiments, and links to SVC/SVR limitations.  
- General approach compatible with different proposal detectors, features, and classifiers.  
- Weakness: discussion of latent-variable optimization (Algorithm 1) and regularization (Eq. 3) is brief and lacks implementation/tuning details.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to regularization and potential convergence issues are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: comparisons focus on RCNN, lacking evaluation against more recent detectors (e.g., Faster R-CNN, YOLO).  
- Weakness: absence of an ablation study to quantify contributions of individual components (e.g., spatial confidence features).  
- Weakness: some redundant repetition in motivation and problem setup that could be tightened.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with strong, comprehensive experimental support and clear presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient detail on latent optimization, dated detector comparisons, and missing ablation study limit full assessment.  
- Overall: solid contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand optimization details, add contemporary detector comparisons, and include an ablation study.
