## Review of Learning From Weakly Supervised Data by The Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm

This paper proposes a novel Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm designed for learning from data with continuous “positiveness” values instead of binary labels, particularly useful in weakly supervised scenarios like segment proposal classification. The core idea is to treat the positiveness as a probabilistic label and modify the SVM loss function accordingly, extending it to handle cases where positiveness is unobserved via a latent variable model. The authors demonstrate its effectiveness in segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 for the latter.

The paper addresses a relevant and practical problem – leveraging weakly labeled data is crucial for scaling computer vision applications. The proposed e-SVM is a logical extension of standard SVMs and appears well-motivated, offering a principled way to handle continuous confidence values. The experimental validation is comprehensive, covering different tasks and annotation settings. The results consistently demonstrate the superiority of e-SVM over traditional SVC and SVR approaches, and the achieved SOTA result on PASCAL VOC 2007 is a significant contribution. The inclusion of two new evaluation criteria (TAPC & NDCG) for segment proposal classification is also a positive aspect, recognizing the limitations of existing metrics.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in certain sections. While the core idea is sound, the explanation of the latent variable model and the iterative optimization process (Algorithm 1) could be more detailed. The motivation for the specific regularization term chosen for the latent variable is somewhat weak.  Furthermore, the paper references several related works but lacks a critical discussion of their limitations in the context of the proposed approach.  The gain observed in object detection is presented without extensive ablation studies; a more detailed analysis of the contribution of the spatial confidence map feature would be valuable.  The discussion regarding the computational complexity of the method is also absent.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important problem in weakly supervised learning.
*   Proposes a novel and well-motivated algorithm (e-SVM).
*   Demonstrates strong empirical results, including a SOTA result on PASCAL VOC 2007.
*   Introduces new evaluation criteria for segment proposal classification.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity in the explanation of the latent variable model and optimization.
*   Insufficient discussion of related work and their shortcomings.
*   Limited ablation studies to analyze the contribution of specific components.
*   Missing discussion of computational complexity.

**Overall:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of weakly supervised learning. While there are areas for improvement, the strengths of the work outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, focusing on clarifying the technical details, strengthening the related work discussion, and providing a more in-depth analysis of the experimental results. The authors should be encouraged to address these points to further solidify the impact of their work.
