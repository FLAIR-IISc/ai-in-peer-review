- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous “positiveness” values by incorporating probabilistic labels into SVM loss.  
- Extends e-SVM to a latent variable setting with a regularization term for unobserved positiveness (e.g., bounding-box-only annotations).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007 object detection and improvements across multiple tasks.  
- Builds on MIL, MIM, and weighted SVM literature and correctly identifies limitations of standard SVC/SVR for this problem.  
- Introduces new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment proposal classification.  
- Mathematical formulation is clear, well-motivated, and theoretically sound.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive with comparisons to baselines and state-of-the-art methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written and explains core concepts clearly.  
- Novel contribution addressing a specific gap in weakly supervised SVM approaches.  
- Consistent empirical improvements across tasks demonstrate method efficacy.  
- Extension to latent variables is sensible for bounding-box-only settings.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study to assess contributions of individual components (e.g., latent regularizer).  
- Does not thoroughly analyze hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- No discussion of computational complexity or runtime compared to standard SVMs, especially on large datasets.  
- Related-work section is somewhat overly long and redundant regarding established methods like MIL.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, and sound theoretical formulation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablation/hyperparameter analysis and missing computational-cost discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include ablation studies and computational cost discussion.
