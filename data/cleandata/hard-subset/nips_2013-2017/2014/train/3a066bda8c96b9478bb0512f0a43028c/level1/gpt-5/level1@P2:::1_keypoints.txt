- Paper proposes an expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for soft/latent labels μ=g(u) (g(u)=u), minimizing expected hinge loss (convex in w) and a weakly supervised variant that anchors latent u to box IoU via a convex KL term with alternating optimization.
- The formulation is closely related to prior fuzzy/soft-label and probabilistic-label SVMs and to latent SVMs; novelty is mainly the convex KL anchoring for weak supervision and integrating segment confidence maps with R-CNN.
- Technical soundness: w-step is convex and implementable via reweighting, and the u-step is convex for g=id, but important derivations and numerical safeguards are missing.
- Missing optimization details: derivation/closed form of the u-update, ui ∈ (0,1) constraints, log(0) safeguards, convergence behavior, stopping criteria, and sensitivity to λR are not analyzed.
- Detection experiment lacks crucial ablations isolating e-SVM benefit from added spatial-confidence-map feature and kernel mapping; reported +1.5 mAP may partly come from the feature engineering.
- Empirical support: consistent gains on segment ranking (TAPC, NDCG) and modest gains over SVR; detection boost on VOC07 is promising but needs per-class results, standard deviations, and more ablations.
- Evaluation ambiguity: segmentation metric is unclear (mean IU vs. pixel accuracy vs. class-average) and should be clarified for comparability.
- References and positioning: several citations are incomplete/malformed and key prior work on soft/fuzzy-label SVMs and probabilistic-label SVMs is missing.
- Presentation issues: mathematical notation (define g(u), domains for u), algorithm details (initialization, stopping, complexity), typos, and section organization need polishing.
- Originality: conceptual advance is modest given prior soft-label SVM literature; main contributions are the convex KL regularizer for latent u and practical integration with R-CNN.
- Significance: practically appealing and easy to add to linear SVM pipelines with demonstrated empirical gains, but theoretical novelty is limited.
- Pros: simple convex expected-hinge formulation, principled weakly supervised extension, task-agnostic ranking metrics, and empirical improvements over baselines.
- Cons: limited methodological novelty, sparse optimization and hyperparameter details, ambiguous evaluation reporting, incomplete detection reporting, and presentation/referral problems.
- Suggested fixes: provide u-update derivation and numerical stabilization, clarify segmentation metric and splits, add ablations (e-SVM vs SVR in same pipeline, grid size m, pooling, λR, kernel mapping), add runtime/convergence stats, and add missing citations and clearer positioning.
- Recommendation: Borderline — sound and practically useful but needs stronger related work, clearer optimization details, and fuller experiments/ablations to be a solid contribution.
