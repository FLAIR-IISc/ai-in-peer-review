- Proposes e-SVM that models labels as Bernoulli variables with continuous "positiveness" in [0,1] and optimizes an expectation loss.  
- Handles strong supervision (observed positiveness) and weak supervision (latent positiveness) with an EM-like iterative latent optimization.  
- Distinguishes the setting from binary SVC and MIL, and relates to but claims difference from weighted SVMs (weights on labels vs. data).  
- Provides a clear mathematical formulation and a detailed Algorithm 1 for the latent case.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using SVC/SVR baselines and new metrics (TAPC, NDCG) for segment classification.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements on PASCAL VOC for segmentation and detection, e.g., ~1.5% AP gain on VOC2007.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with helpful figures illustrating the framework and features.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence guarantees for the latent algorithm) and ablation studies isolating the expectation loss effect.  
- The claimed distinction from weighted SVMs is not fully justified; expectation loss may be equivalent to a weighted SVM with label-proportional weights.  
- The regularization term for latent variables (Equation 3) is under-motivated and needs stronger justification.  
- Does not demonstrate applicability beyond computer vision, limiting claims of generality.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify relationship to WSVMs and to better justify the regularizer.
