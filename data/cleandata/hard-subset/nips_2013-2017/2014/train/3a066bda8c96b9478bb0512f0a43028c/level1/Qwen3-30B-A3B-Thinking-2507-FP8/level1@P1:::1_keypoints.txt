- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous "positiveness" values instead of binary labels.
- Models labels as Bernoulli random variables with success probability a function of observed positiveness.
- Extends to weak supervision by treating positiveness as latent variables (latent e-SVM).
- Distinguishes clearly from MIL (requires fully positive instance) and WSVM (weights examples, not labels).
- Theoretical formulation is sound with clear loss derivation and convex optimization properties.
- Latent extension is well-motivated and justified, with note on needing regularization to avoid local minima.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and introduces TAPC and NDCG metrics for continuous target tasks.
- Demonstrates strong results: better segment proposal classification than SVC/SVR, semantic segmentation comparable to SVR, and 1.5% AP gain on PASCAL VOC 2007 (60.0% AP).
- Figures and notation are clear; experimental setup (CPMC, features, evaluation) is thoroughly described.
- Minor clarity issue: connection between theoretical formulation and implementation (specific regularization in Eq.3) could be more explicit.
- Originality: novel formulation that weights labels to handle continuous confidences, addressing segment proposal partial-overlap problem.
- Significance: practically valuable for reducing annotation cost and broadly applicable across detectors, features, and classifiers.
- Suggested improvements: compare to more recent weakly supervised/deep methods, justify or ablate the regularization term, and better contextualize within 2013–2014 NIPS literature.
- Pro acceptance: novel gap-addressing formulation, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, and practical performance gains.
- Con acceptance: limited comparisons to recent methods, unclear regularizer justification, and weaker contextual links to recent NIPS work.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper, recommend acceptance.
