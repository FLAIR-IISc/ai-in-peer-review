- Proposes e-SVM to learn from continuous "positiveness" values instead of binary labels, extendable to weakly supervised segment classifiers.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the algorithm and applications.
- Experimental results show e-SVM outperforms alternatives on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.
- Novel algorithm addressing a specific CV problem and extending classical SVC models to weak supervision.
- Thorough experimental analysis and discussion of implications.
- Assumes positiveness is a continuous value, which may not hold in practice.
- Requires generating a set of proposals for true labels, potentially computationally expensive.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of e-SVM.
- Significant potential applicability to other CV tasks (image classification, object recognition) and to weakly supervised learning more broadly.
- Well-organized presentation with clearly presented experiments and results.
- Technically sound with effective empirical validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical performance, and thorough analysis.
- Arguments for rejection: problematic positiveness assumption, proposal-generation cost, missing complexity analysis.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing weaknesses—provide complexity analysis and discuss the positiveness assumption.
