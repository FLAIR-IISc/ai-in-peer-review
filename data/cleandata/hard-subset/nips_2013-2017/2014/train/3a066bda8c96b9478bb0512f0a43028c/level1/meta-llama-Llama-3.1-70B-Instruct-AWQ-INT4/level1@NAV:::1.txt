This paper proposes a novel learning algorithm called expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle weakly supervised data where only the "positiveness" of each training sample is available. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm on two major tasks in computer vision: semantic segmentation and object detection.

**Summary of main ideas and relation to previous work**

The paper addresses the problem of learning from weakly supervised data, where the labels are continuous values within a bounded interval. This is a common scenario in computer vision, where annotating large datasets with precise labels can be expensive and impractical. The authors propose an extension of the classical Support Vector Classification (SVC) model, called e-SVM, which can handle this type of data. They also introduce a latent extension of e-SVM, which can learn from weakly supervised data where the exact positiveness values are unobserved.

The authors relate their work to previous research on weakly supervised learning, multiple instance learning, and weighted SVMs. They highlight the differences between their approach and existing methods, such as Support Vector Regression (SVR) and Multiple Instance Learning (MIL).
