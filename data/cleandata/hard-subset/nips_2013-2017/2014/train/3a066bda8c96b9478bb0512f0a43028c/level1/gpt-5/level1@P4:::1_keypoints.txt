- Proposes e-SVM: minimize expected hinge loss when only a positiveness score u∈[0,1] is available by modeling labels as Bernoulli(μ=g(u)), yielding L(w)=λ||w||^2 + (1/N)Σ[l+ g(u)+ l−(1−g(u))].  
- For observed u this is convex and reduces to a weighted combination of positive/negative hinge losses (implementable via instance weights or data duplication).  
- Extends to a latent-u setting (only bounding boxes) by adding a convex KL-like regularizer R(u) tying u to initial ubb and alternating optimization between w and u.  
- Applications: segment proposal scoring, semantic segmentation, and object detection; integrates per-pixel confidence maps pooled into window features added to R-CNN, yielding +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC07.  
- Proposes two evaluation metrics for segment confidence: Threshold-Averaged Precision (TAPC) and NDCG, aligned with ranking objectives.  
- Strength: conceptually simple and convex formulation for observed soft labels with interpretable per-sample weights.  
- Strength: practical latent extension with a convex regularizer and straightforward alternating optimization.  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements over SVC and modest gains over SVR for segment confidence; non-trivial mAP gains when integrated into a strong detector.  
- Strength: spatial confidence map feature is a reasonable, general way to combine segment cues with window detectors.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty — expected hinge loss with per-sample probabilities is a natural/known construction closely related to instance-weighted SVMs and prior work on probabilistic/uncertain labels.  
- Weakness: Paper does not position or cite related SVM/probabilistic-label literature sufficiently.  
- Weakness: Sparse technical details — conversion to standard SVM solvers, u-update algorithm, complexity, and convergence analysis are not provided.  
- Weakness: Regularizer ties u strongly to ubb without ablation/sensitivity analysis (λR, initialization, alternative g(·)); robustness versus simply using u=ubb is unclear.  
- Weakness: Empirical gains are modest for segmentation and detection; bounding-box regression modification may confound attribution of improvements.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues — notational inconsistencies, typos, malformed references, and missing practical details in Algorithm 1 (stopping criteria, solver specifics).  
- Quality: Observed-label case is sound and convex; latent alternating scheme is reasonable but lacks theoretical guarantees and stability diagnostics.  
- Experiments: Methodology is standard but needs more baselines (logistic regression with soft labels, RankSVM, other weakly supervised methods), per-class analysis, and statistical significance testing.  
- Originality: Moderate — straightforward expected-loss extension for soft labels; latent KL-regularizer and spatial pooling are incremental.  
- Significance: Practical but limited; small consistent improvements on detection but broader impact requires stronger gains and robustness evidence.  
- Suggestions: Cite and compare to SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels and label-noise literature; clarify theoretical/empirical differences.  
- Suggestions: Explicitly show implementation via instance weights or duplicated samples; provide u-update derivation/algorithm, runtime, convergence criteria, and ablations (λR, g(·), u=ubb baseline).  
- Suggestions: Compare to additional baselines (RankSVM, logistic regression with soft labels, more weakly supervised segmentation methods), isolate spatial feature vs. bbox-regression effects, and report per-class AP/statistical significance.  
- Suggestions: Fix notation/typos, correct references, and add practical algorithmic details to improve clarity and reproducibility.  
- Acceptance arguments: simple, general, convex observed-label formulation; easy to implement; empirically beneficial on a competitive detection baseline; useful evaluation metrics.  
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty relative to prior probabilistic-label SVM work, modest empirical gains, insufficient analysis/ablations, and clarity issues.
