- Proposes e-SVM, a novel SVM extension that learns from continuous “positiveness” scores instead of binary labels.  
- Frames e-SVM as a principled generalization of SVMs versus simple thresholding of confidence values.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across tasks, notably state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Builds on established weakly supervised learning and segment-proposal literature and differentiates from SVC/SVR.  
- Novelty: intuitive and potentially powerful weighting of samples by positiveness.  
- Generality: compatible with different segment proposal detectors, features, and classifiers.  
- Clear presentation and lucid explanation of e-SVM and its latent extension.  
- Thorough evaluation across multiple tasks/datasets and introduction of TAPC and NDCG for segment proposal evaluation.  
- Limited theoretical analysis — lacks discussion of properties like convexity, convergence, and generalization bounds.  
- Regularization choice in latent e-SVM (Eq. 3) appears arbitrary and lacks justification or alternatives; sensitivity to that parameter is unreported.  
- Insufficient details on hyperparameter tuning, harming assessment of robustness and reproducibility.  
- Comparisons are limited to R-CNN; newer detectors (Faster R-CNN, YOLO, SSD) are not considered.  
- Implementation/code not released, further hindering reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with strong empirical results addressing a practical annotation-cost problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theory, weak justification of regularization, missing hyperparameter and code details, and incomplete baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution to weakly supervised learning; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: clarify loss convexity, justify and analyze regularization choice/sensitivity, provide hyperparameter tuning details, compare to newer detection frameworks, and ideally release code.
