## Review of "Learning From Weakly Supervised Data by The Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) algorithm"

This paper introduces the Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM), a novel learning algorithm designed for binary classification problems where only a “positiveness” value (representing confidence in belonging to the positive class) is available instead of a hard binary label. The authors extend this to weakly supervised segment classification, demonstrating its effectiveness in segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection. They achieve state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007.

The work builds upon the limitations of directly applying standard SVMs (SVC and SVR) to this problem, arguing that binarizing the positiveness value (SVC) or treating it as a standard regression target (SVR) are suboptimal. The e-SVM addresses this by weighting examples based on their positiveness, effectively learning a distribution over labels. The extension to weakly supervised learning via a latent variable formulation is a logical and well-presented addition. This approach aligns with a growing body of work in weakly supervised learning, particularly in computer vision where obtaining detailed annotations is expensive (e.g., MIL approaches [7, 26], though the authors correctly differentiate their setting).

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The e-SVM formulation offers a principled approach to handling continuous confidence values as labels, addressing a gap in existing methods.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates consistent improvements over baseline methods (SVC, SVR) across multiple tasks, culminating in state-of-the-art object detection results.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and explains the e-SVM formulation and its extension to the weakly supervised case in a clear and concise manner.
* **Practicality:** The algorithm can be readily implemented using standard SVM solvers, making it accessible to researchers.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors provide intuition for the method, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the convergence properties and generalization bounds would strengthen the paper.
* **Regularization Sensitivity:** The latent e-SVM relies on a regularization term for the positiveness values. The choice of this regularization (KL divergence) and its impact on performance could be explored more thoroughly.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper doesn’t detail the hyperparameter tuning process, which is crucial for achieving optimal performance.
* **Comparison Depth:** While comparisons to SVC and SVR are provided, a comparison to more recent and sophisticated weakly supervised learning methods (beyond those cited) would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a relevant and challenging problem in weakly supervised learning.
* Presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm with strong empirical results.
* Offers a practical solution that can be readily adopted by other researchers.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* Lack of deeper theoretical analysis.
* Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning.
* Could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.

**Overall:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of weakly supervised learning. The e-SVM algorithm is a promising approach with strong empirical support. While some theoretical and experimental refinements would be beneficial, the novelty and practical impact of the work warrant acceptance. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
