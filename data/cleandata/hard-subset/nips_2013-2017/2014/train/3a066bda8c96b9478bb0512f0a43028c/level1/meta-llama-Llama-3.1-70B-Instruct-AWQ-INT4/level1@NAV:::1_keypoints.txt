- Proposes e-SVM, an extension of SVC to learn from weakly supervised data where labels are continuous within a bounded interval.  
- Introduces a latent e-SVM extension to handle cases where positiveness values are unobserved.  
- Positions the work relative to weakly supervised learning, multiple instance learning (MIL), and weighted SVMs, distinguishing it from SVR and MIL.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on semantic segmentation and object detection benchmarks.  
- Provides two new evaluation criteria for segment proposal classification and uses them in a thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Presents detailed result analysis and comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Strength: novel algorithm addressing a common, practical weak supervision scenario in computer vision.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation and clear presentation with helpful tables and figures.  
- Weakness: assumes positiveness values are continuous and bounded, which may not hold in all real datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: does not explore other model families (e.g., deep learning) that might yield better performance.  
- Significance: could reduce labeling effort by enabling learning from weaker annotations.  
- Quality: well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Recommendation: acceptance recommended, subject to addressing the noted weaknesses in future work.
