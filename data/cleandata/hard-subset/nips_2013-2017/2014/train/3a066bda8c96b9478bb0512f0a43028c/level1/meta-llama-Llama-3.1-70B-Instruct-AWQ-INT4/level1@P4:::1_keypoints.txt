- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous "positiveness" scores instead of binary labels for weakly supervised data.  
- Designed for settings where exact positiveness of each example is unobserved, using a latent-variable formulation.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Positions work relative to weakly supervised learning, multiple instance learning, and instance-weighted SVMs, arguing the continuous positiveness setting is distinct.  
- Provides a clear derivation of the loss and optimization, using a latent variable to model unobserved positiveness.  
- Experiments support technical claims and show practical gains across several datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and helpful figures/tables.  
- Presents a novel idea (continuous positiveness) that is original within the weak supervision literature.  
- Significant impact potential by reducing labeling needs and improving detection performance in vision tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experimental validation, and clear significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: may be too specialized for a general ML audience and lacks deeper analysis of limitations and broader implications.
