This paper proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM), a simple extension of linear SVMs that learns from “positiveness” scores in [0,1] rather than binary labels, and a latent variant for weak supervision when positiveness is unobserved. The method replaces the hinge loss by its expectation under a Bernoulli label with success probability g(u), with g(u)=u in experiments. For weak supervision, positiveness is inferred by alternating minimization with a convex regularizer that keeps latent u near IoU with ground-truth boxes. The approach is evaluated on segment proposal classification (with new TAPC and NDCG criteria), semantic segmentation, and object detection, where e-SVM-derived spatial confidence maps are concatenated with RCNN features to improve PASCAL VOC 2007 AP by ~1.5.

Relation to prior work: The formulation is close to SVMs with soft/probabilistic labels and to instance-weighted SVMs; the authors articulate a difference (weighting labels rather than examples) but the expected hinge is mathematically equivalent to a convex combination of positive/negative hinge losses known in probabilistic-label SVM variants. The latent extension is reminiscent of latent SVM and weakly supervised learning beyond MIL, and the application aligns with proposal-based segmentation/detection pipelines (CPMC, Selective Search, RCNN).

Quality: The optimization is sound (block-convex), leverages standard solvers, and the KL regularizer is convex and intuitive. However, theory is light: no generalization or calibration analysis of expected-hinge with soft labels, no convergence guarantees beyond empirical convergence, and limited robustness studies to noisy u or g(u) misspecification. Empirically, gains over SVR on segmentation are modest; the 1.5 AP gain in detection is useful but not deeply analyzed (e.g., per-class calibration, failure modes, runtime).

Clarity: The paper is generally clear, but there are typos, inconsistent notation (e.g., λw·1/wTw), and missing details (choice/sensitivity of λR, stopping criteria, initialization beyond ubb). The related work could better position against probabilistic-label SVMs and ranking SVMs.

Originality: The core loss is incremental relative to soft-label SVM ideas; the latent-u regularization and the spatial confidence map feature are the more novel contributions.

Significance: As a practical recipe, e-SVM is attractive—easy to implement, broadly applicable, and yields measurable detection gains. Broader impact would be strengthened by deeper comparisons (e.g., logistic/probit with soft targets, ranking SVM, calibrated regression) and ablations (g(u) choices, temperature, regularizer forms).

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, general method; uses off-the-shelf solvers
- Addresses an important weakly supervised setting
- Clear block-convex optimization; practical regularizer
- Empirical improvements on VOC detection; new evaluation criteria

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited novelty vs soft-label/weighted SVM literature
- Sparse theory and sensitivity analyses
- Modest gains on segmentation; incomplete baselines
- Clarity/notation issues and missing ablations
