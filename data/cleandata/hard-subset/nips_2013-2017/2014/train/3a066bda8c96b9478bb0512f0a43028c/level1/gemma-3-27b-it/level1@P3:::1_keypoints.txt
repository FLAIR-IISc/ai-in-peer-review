- Introduces e-SVM, which weights SVM training examples by a continuous “positiveness” score instead of binarizing labels for weak supervision.  
- Extends e-SVM to a latent-variable model for fully weak supervision and applies it to segment classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.  
- Core idea is intuitive and addresses limitations of standard SVMs/SVRs when only soft labels are available.  
- Builds on MIL and weighted SVM literature and clearly differentiates e-SVM from prior approaches.  
- Loss function is well-defined, theoretically sound, and compatible with standard SVM solvers; optimization for the latent model is well-described.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive and achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007, with comparisons to SVC and SVR.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and the method is presented logically.  
- Method is flexible with respect to segment proposal detectors, features, and base detectors, increasing its applicability.  
- Insufficient justification for choosing KL-divergence-based regularization in the latent model; alternative schemes are not explored.  
- Lacks thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., λR and latent-variable initialization thresholds), raising robustness concerns.  
- Missing comparisons or discussion regarding integration with newer object detectors (e.g., Faster R-CNN, YOLO, SSD).  
- Results may depend on CPMC segment proposal settings; the effect of varying CPMC parameters is not shown.  
- NDCG is introduced but its specific relevance and justification in this context are under-explained.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution to weakly supervised learning with compelling results; minor revisions recommended to address regularization, hyperparameter analysis, and discussion of newer detectors.
