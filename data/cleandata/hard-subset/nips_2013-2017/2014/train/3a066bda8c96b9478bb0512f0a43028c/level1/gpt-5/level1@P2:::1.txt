Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes an “expectation loss SVM” (e-SVM) for settings where training labels are continuous “positiveness” values u ∈ [0,1] (e.g., IoU of a segment with ground truth). The central idea is to treat the true binary label Y ∈ {−1,+1} as Bernoulli with success probability μ = g(u) (instantiated as g(u) = u), and minimize the expected hinge loss: for sample i, the positive and negative hinge losses are weighted by μi and (1−μi), respectively, plus an L2 regularizer. This yields a convex objective in w equivalent to a soft-label SVM with per-example label probabilities. For weak supervision (only bounding boxes available), the positiveness ui is treated as a latent variable regularized toward the observable segment–box IoU ubb via a convex KL term; alternating minimization over w and u is used. The learned segment scorers are evaluated on segment ranking (TAPC, NDCG), semantic segmentation (via CPMC+pooling), and object detection by forming class-specific spatial confidence maps from segment scores and concatenating them with R-CNN features. Reported gains include improvements over SVC/SVR on segment ranking and a +1.5 mAP boost over R-CNN on PASCAL VOC 2007 when adding the confidence map feature.

The formulation is closely related to prior work on SVMs with uncertain/soft/fuzzy labels and instance-dependent costs (e.g., fuzzy SVM, SVM with probabilistic labels), and to latent SVMs. The weakly supervised extension differs by using a convex KL regularizer that anchors latent u to box-derived pseudo-labels, rather than bag-level constraints as in MIL. The application to detection via spatial confidence maps is reminiscent of integrating segmentation cues into window-based detectors, but the particular feature construction and demonstration on R-CNN are practical contributions.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The expected hinge loss with μ ∈ [0,1] is convex in w and implementable via standard linear SVM solvers by reweighting per-sample positive/negative losses. The weakly supervised variant alternates between a convex SVM in w and a convex optimization in u (with g(u)=u and KL regularization). This is reasonable and standard; however, important details are missing:
  - The u-update (line 4 of Algorithm 1) appears to decouple per sample and has a closed-form solution when g is identity; this derivation and the constraints ui ∈ (0,1) should be given. In practice, KL requires box constraints and numerical safeguards (log 0).
  - Convergence behavior (objective decrease, stopping criteria) and sensitivity to λR are not analyzed.
  - For detection, the ablation isolating the benefit of e-SVM vs. SVR/SVC within the same spatial confidence-map pipeline is missing; the reported mAP gain might partly stem from the added feature and kernel mapping rather than the specific loss.
- Empirical support: The segment-ranking metrics (TAPC, NDCG) are appropriate and show consistent improvements over SVC and modest but consistent gains over SVR. Semantic segmentation accuracy numbers are reported but the exact metric (mean IU vs. pixel accuracy vs. class-avg) is ambiguous; please clarify to ensure comparability. The detection experiment is compelling (+1.5 mAP), but a full per-class table, standard deviations over runs, and ablations (grid size m, pooling type, effect of the additive kernel mapping) would strengthen the claim.
- Carefulness: Several references are incomplete or malformed; related work on fuzzy/soft-label SVMs and SVMs with probabilistic labels is not cited. Claims such as “solved effortlessly by any standard SVM solver” should be restricted to the w-step.

Clarity
The main ideas are understandable, but the presentation needs polishing:
- Mathematical exposition: define g(u) and its properties; specify domains and constraints for u; derive or describe the u-step update; clarify notation (e.g., duplication of λw·1/(w^T w) formatting issues).
- Algorithm details: stopping criteria, initialization (beyond ubb), and computational complexity.
- Experimental details: exact segmentation metric; train/val/test splits; hyperparameters (λ, λR), feature dimensionalities, and implementation details for the u-step.
- Writing and references: fix typos and incomplete citations; improve organization of Sections 3–5.

Originality
The expected hinge formulation with probabilistic labels is known in various guises (fuzzy/soft-label SVMs, SVM with uncertain labels), and the connection to instance-weighted SVMs is tighter than discussed (the proposed loss can be seen as a convex combination of class-conditional hinge terms per example). The paper’s novelty lies primarily in:
- Using a convex KL regularizer to anchor latent soft labels to bounding-box-derived priors in a weakly supervised setting.
- A simple, effective integration of segment-based confidence maps with R-CNN.
The conceptual advance over prior soft-label SVM literature is modest; stronger positioning and citations are needed.

Significance
Practically, the method is appealing: it is easy to implement on top of linear SVMs, handles soft/latent labels naturally, and yields consistent improvements on segment ranking and a meaningful boost for detection on a standard benchmark. If the weakly supervised extension scales and generalizes, it can reduce annotation needs and has potential impact across vision tasks. On the theory/method side, the contribution is incremental.

Pros
- Simple, convex expected hinge formulation; easy to plug into existing pipelines.
- Weakly supervised extension with a principled convex regularizer; straightforward alternating optimization.
- New, task-agnostic evaluation metrics for segment ranking (TAPC, NDCG).
- Empirical improvements over SVC/SVR on segment ranking; non-trivial gains on VOC07 detection when combined with R-CNN.

Cons
- Limited methodological novelty relative to soft/fuzzy-label SVM literature; missing key citations and discussion.
- Sparse optimization details (u-step, constraints, convergence) and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Ambiguity in segmentation evaluation metric; incomplete reporting for detection (lack of ablations and full per-class results).
- Some presentation and referencing issues.

Suggestions and questions
- Provide derivation and algorithm for the u-update; specify constraints and numerical stabilization.
- Clarify the segmentation metric and report standard metrics (mean IU, pixel acc., class avg).
- Add ablations: compare e-SVM vs. SVR within the same spatial confidence map feature; vary m, pooling, λR; show effect of additive kernel mapping.
- Position the work against fuzzy/soft-label SVMs and SVMs with probabilistic labels; add appropriate citations and clarify differences.
- Report runtime and convergence statistics.

Recommendation
Borderline. The paper is sound and practically useful, with clear empirical benefits, but its core loss is close to known soft-label SVM formulations, and the current draft lacks important details and proper positioning. With strengthened related work, clearer optimization details, and fuller experiments/ablations, it would make a solid contribution.
