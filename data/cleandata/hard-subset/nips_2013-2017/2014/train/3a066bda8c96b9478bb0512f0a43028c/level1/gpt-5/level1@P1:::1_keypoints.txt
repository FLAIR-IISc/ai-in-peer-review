- Proposes e-SVM: minimize expected hinge loss under Bernoulli soft labels µi = g(ui) plus L2 regularization, yielding per-example convex combination of positive and negative hinge losses.  
- Implements e-SVM via example duplication/reweighting so standard linear SVM solvers can be used; latent e-SVM alternates over w and u with a convex KL/CE regularizer.  
- Evaluated on VOC segmentation/detection with new proposal scoring metrics (mean AP over thresholds, NDCG); outperforms SVC and slightly beats SVR for proposal scoring.  
- e-SVM gives comparable semantic segmentation accuracy to SVR and improves RCNN detection mAP on VOC2007 by ~1.2–1.5 points when adding a spatial confidence-map feature.  
- Connects to prior work on fuzzy/probabilistic-label SVMs and instance-weighted/cost-sensitive SVMs; latent extension is similar in spirit to latent SVM with a convex regularizer.  
- Positive: expected-hinge formulation is simple, convex in w for known µi, and easily implementable in practice.  
- Positive: latent alternating scheme with convex regularizer is reasonable; observation that λR=0 reduces u to {0,1} (latent SVM behavior) is insightful.  
- Positive: spatial confidence-map feature is a pragmatic way to incorporate segment-level confidences into window-based detectors.  
- Positive: experiments are reasonably thorough with appropriate baselines (SVC/SVR) for segment scoring.  
- Concern: core formulation has limited novelty—essentially per-example label-dependent weighted hinge loss equivalent to duplicating examples, overlapping prior fuzzy/probabilistic SVM literature.  
- Concern: theoretical analysis is minimal—no consistency/risk analysis for expected hinge with µi∈[0,1] or robustness to miscalibrated µi; latent model has no convergence guarantees beyond monotonic decrease.  
- Concern: missing optimization details for u—closed-form or efficient update and complexity analysis are not provided; sensitivity and selection of λR are unclear.  
- Concern: claim that “any standard SVM solver” works is misleading since many solvers lack per-example weight support; duplication/weighting trick and solver modifications should be clarified.  
- Concern: empirical disconnect—better proposal ranking does not clearly translate to improved segmentation, and detection gains lack error bars, ablations, per-class or robustness analyses.  
- Concern: RCNN baseline details (training splits, bbox regression variant) are unclear, affecting reproducibility.  
- Clarity issues: equation formatting, unclear regularizer constants, undefined/justified choice of g(u)=u, incomplete references, and malformed bibliography entries.  
- Presentation missing explicit equivalence to duplicated weighted SVM and exact solver settings (e.g., liblinear options).  
- Originality: most novel parts are latent-u with KL regularization and application to segment scoring/RCNN integration; core expected-hinge loss overlaps existing work and needs clearer citation/comparison.  
- Significance: idea is simple and practical with useful detection gains, but novelty is modest and empirical contributions incremental without stronger analysis/positioning.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple convex formulation, sensible latent extension, new proposal scoring metrics, and demonstrated improvement with RCNN.  
- Cons for acceptance: overlap with prior work, limited theory, incomplete optimization/implementation details, modest segmentation impact, missing ablations, and presentation problems.  
- Suggestions: add baselines (logistic regression, squared-loss regression, ranking methods), ablate g(u), λR, m, pooling and proposal methods, clarify RCNN training, derive u-update, and tighten theory and references.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning to reject in current form due to limited novelty and missing analysis/details, but potentially publishable after stronger positioning, comparisons, and clarity improvements.
