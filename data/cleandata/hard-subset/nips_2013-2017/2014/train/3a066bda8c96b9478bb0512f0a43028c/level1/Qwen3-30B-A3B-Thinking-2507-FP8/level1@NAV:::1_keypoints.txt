- Introduces Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) for training with continuous "positiveness" labels in [0,1] instead of binary labels.  
- Novel extension to SVMs that treats labels as random variables with probabilities given by positiveness values.  
- Handles both strong supervision (observed positiveness) and weak supervision (latent positiveness inferred during training).  
- Validated on semantic segmentation and object detection, showing consistent improvements over SVC and SVR baselines.  
- Strong technical quality with a clear theoretical foundation for the e-SVM formulation.  
- Well-designed experimental evaluation on PASCAL VOC using appropriate metrics (TAPC, NDCG).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection results (60.0 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007).  
- Practical significance: reduces annotation costs by effectively using bounding-box weak supervision.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes helpful figures.  
- Good connection to related work (MIL, weighted SVMs), but theoretical links to weighted SVMs could be deeper.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of convergence properties for the latent e-SVM extension.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations, failure cases, and detailed relation to existing weighted SVM approaches.  
- Overall significance: technically sound, generalizable framework with real-world impact; recommended for acceptance with minor theoretical clarifications.
