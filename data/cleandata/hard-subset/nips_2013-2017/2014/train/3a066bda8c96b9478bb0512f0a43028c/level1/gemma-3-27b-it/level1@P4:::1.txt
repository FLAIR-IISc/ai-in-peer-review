The paper proposes a novel learning algorithm, Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM), for problems where only a “positiveness” value (between 0 and 1) is available for each training sample, rather than a binary label. This is particularly useful for weakly supervised learning scenarios, such as segment proposal classification with bounding box annotations. The authors extend e-SVM to handle latent positiveness values and demonstrate its effectiveness on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 for object detection.

The work builds upon the limitations of using standard SVC and SVR for this type of problem. SVC requires ad-hoc thresholding of the positiveness, while SVR is not ideal as the positiveness value lies within a bounded interval. The proposed e-SVM directly models the expectation over the label given the positiveness, offering a more principled approach. The paper relates to existing weakly supervised learning methods like Multiple Instance Learning, but distinguishes itself by operating in an intermediate space between MIL and strong supervision, and proposes a dedicated solution. Related work on weighted SVMs is also discussed, with the authors highlighting the difference in weighting schemes (labels vs. data points).

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The e-SVM algorithm represents a novel approach to handling continuous “positiveness” values in classification, addressing a gap between standard binary classification and regression techniques. The extension to latent e-SVM for weak supervision is also a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The formulation of the expectation loss and the optimization procedure (for both observed and latent positiveness) appear mathematically sound. The connection to standard SVM solvers is advantageous.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental evaluation on three different tasks (segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, object detection) provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method. Achieving state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 is a significant achievement.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation for the work is clearly stated, and the algorithm is described in a reasonably understandable manner. The figures help illustrate the framework.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Applications:** While the e-SVM algorithm itself is novel, the applications (semantic segmentation and object detection) are relatively standard.  The benefit of e-SVM is demonstrated *within* these existing frameworks, rather than creating entirely new application possibilities.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The regularization term in the latent e-SVM model introduces an additional hyperparameter (lambda\_R) that requires tuning. The sensitivity of the results to this parameter is not adequately discussed.
*   **Computational Complexity:** While the algorithm leverages existing SVM solvers, the iterative optimization for the latent e-SVM model could be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets. This is not discussed in the paper.
*   **Discussion of Limitations:** The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the approach. For example, the assumptions made about the positiveness values (e.g., their distribution) could be addressed.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The e-SVM algorithm is a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in weakly supervised learning.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the method across multiple tasks.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents the algorithm and its evaluation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The applications of the algorithm are relatively standard, and the novelty is primarily in the algorithm itself.
*   The computational complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity of the latent e-SVM model are not adequately addressed.
*   The lack of a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the approach.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of weakly supervised learning. The e-SVM algorithm is a novel and effective approach to handling continuous “positiveness” values, and the empirical results are convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the hyperparameter sensitivity, computational cost, and limitations of the method. Specifically, adding a section discussing these points, and perhaps including some ablation studies showing the impact of lambda\_R on performance, would strengthen the paper significantly.
