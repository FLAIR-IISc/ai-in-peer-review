- Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) that handles continuous "positiveness" u∈[0,1] by treating labels as Bernoulli latent variables and minimizing expected hinge loss.  
- Expected hinge loss reduces to a weighted SVM where each sample contributes to positive and negative losses with weights u and 1−u.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for unknown u_i, optimizing u and model w jointly via alternating optimization with a KL-divergence regularizer to an initial estimate.  
- Connects to weighted SVMs and Multiple Instance Learning, occupying a practical middle ground between pixel-level and image-level supervision.  
- Validated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection (PASCAL VOC), and achieves state-of-the-art detection when combined with R-CNN.  
- Strength — originality/significance: principled, elegant treatment of soft labels that addresses a common practical problem (e.g., IoU-based supervision).  
- Strength — technical quality: clear derivation, reduction to standard weighted SVMs, implementable with existing solvers, and sensible new metrics (TAPC, NDCG).  
- Strength — experiments: consistently outperforms SVC/SVR and provides a 1.5% mAP boost to a strong R-CNN baseline.  
- Weakness — optimization clarity: update rule for latent u is underspecified; a closed-form update likely exists and should be provided.  
- Weakness — relation to weighted SVMs: paper should explicitly clarify equivalence to duplicating points with weights and place work within cost-sensitive/weighted classification literature.  
- Weakness — choice of g(u): using g(u)=u is not justified or ablated; alternatives (sigmoid, power functions) should be evaluated for robustness.  
- Weakness — generality: experiments are vision-focused (IoU); broader applicability to other domains with continuous labels is asserted but not demonstrated.  
- Pro-acceptance summary: novel, principled loss; technically sound and practical; strong empirical gains on a competitive benchmark.  
- Con-acceptance summary: minor but fixable gaps in optimization details, relation to prior weighted methods, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor issues; recommend acceptance.
