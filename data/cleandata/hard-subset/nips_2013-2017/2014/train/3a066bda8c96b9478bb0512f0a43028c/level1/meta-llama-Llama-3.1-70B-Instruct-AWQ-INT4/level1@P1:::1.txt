**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel learning algorithm called Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) that can handle weakly supervised data where the labels are continuous values in a bounded interval. The algorithm is designed to work with segment proposal classification tasks, where the goal is to predict the confidence score of each segment proposal. The authors also extend the algorithm to learn segment classifiers under weak supervision, where only bounding box annotations are available. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the e-SVM algorithm on several computer vision tasks, including semantic segmentation and object detection, and achieves state-of-the-art object detection performance on the PASCAL VOC 2007 dataset.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper is related to previous work on weakly supervised learning and weighted SVMs. However, the authors argue that their problem setting is different from previous work, where the goal is to learn from image-level labels or multiple instance learning. The authors also mention that their approach is different from weighted SVMs, which weight data points instead of labels.
