Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper introduces the expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) for learning when only a “positiveness” score u∈[0,1] is available instead of binary labels. The core idea is to model the (unobserved) binary label Yi∈{−1,+1} for each example as Bernoulli with success probability μi=g(ui), and to minimize the expected hinge loss under this distribution:
L(w)=λ||w||^2 + (1/N) Σi [l+ i g(ui) + l− i (1−g(ui))], where l+ i=max(0,1−w^Txi), l− i=max(0,1+w^Txi), typically g(u)=u. This is convex in w when u is observed and reduces to a weighted combination of positive and negative hinge losses per example. The paper extends this to a weakly supervised setting where u is latent (only bounding boxes available), adding a convex regularizer R(u) based on cross-entropy between the initial estimate ubb (IoU with bounding boxes) and u, and alternates between optimizing w and u.

Applications are shown to segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection. For detection, the authors compute per-pixel “confidence maps” from segment scores and pool them spatially within each candidate window, concatenating these features with R-CNN features, yielding +1.2 to +1.5 mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007. They also propose two evaluation measures for segment confidence prediction: Threshold-Averaged Precision (TAPC) and NDCG.

This work sits between fully supervised segmentation methods (e.g., using CPMC and SVR for IoU prediction) and weakly supervised paradigms such as MIL. It is related to instance-weighted SVMs but differs in that it mixes positive/negative losses per example. There is also relevant literature on SVMs with probabilistic/soft labels and learning under label uncertainty; these connections are not discussed in depth.

Strengths:
- Conceptually simple, convex formulation for the observed-soft-label case; reduces to a standard SVM objective with interpretable weights.
- Practical latent extension with a convex regularizer; alternating optimization is straightforward.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over SVC and modest gains over SVR on segment confidence prediction according to TAPC/NDCG; the detection experiment shows non-trivial mAP gains on a competitive baseline (R-CNN).
- The spatial confidence map feature is a reasonable and general way to integrate segment-level cues with window-based detectors.
- The proposed evaluation metrics (TAPC, NDCG) are aligned with the ranking nature of segment confidence prediction.

Weaknesses:
- Novelty appears limited. The expected hinge loss with per-sample class probabilities is a natural and known construction; it can be implemented by duplicating each example with labels ±1 and weights g(ui) and 1−g(ui), respectively, reducing to an instance-weighted SVM. The paper does not position itself against prior work on SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels or learning with label noise.
- Technical details are sparse in key places. The claim that the loss “can be effortlessly solved by any standard SVM solver” is true for the observed case (via instance weighting), but the conversion is not explicitly described. For the latent case, the u-update is convex but no closed-form, algorithmic detail, or complexity discussion is provided; convergence properties are not analyzed beyond bi-convexity.
- The regularizer R(u) strongly ties u to ubb; ablations on λR, sensitivity to initialization, and alternative g(u) are missing. Without such analysis, it is hard to assess robustness of the latent formulation versus simply using u=ubb.
- Empirical gains on segmentation are small (“comparable” to SVR), and the detection improvements, while positive, are modest. The bounding box regression “modification” introduces a confound; it is unclear how much of the reported +1.5 mAP stems from e-SVM versus this change.
- Clarity and presentation need work: notational inconsistencies (e.g., λw·1 w^T w), typos, and incomplete or malformed references make parts of the paper difficult to follow. Algorithm 1 lacks practical details (stopping criteria, solver specifics).

Quality:
- Observed-label case is technically sound and convex. The latent case is a reasonable alternating scheme with a convex-in-u step due to the chosen R(u), but lacks theoretical guarantees or diagnostic experiments (e.g., stability across runs).
- Experimental methodology is generally standard; however, more thorough baselines (e.g., logistic regression with soft labels, RankSVM, other weakly supervised segmentation methods) and statistical significance or per-class analysis would strengthen the claims.

Clarity:
- The core idea is understandable, but mathematical notation and derivations should be cleaned up. The link between e-SVM and weighted SVM via data duplication or instance weights should be made explicit. The u-update step requires a derivation or at least a clear optimization procedure.

Originality:
- Moderate. The formulation for soft labels is a straightforward expected-loss extension of SVMs and closely related to existing weighted/probabilistic-label SVM variants. The latent extension with a KL-based regularizer and the specific spatial pooling feature are incremental.

Significance:
- Practical but limited. The method slightly improves segment confidence prediction and yields small yet consistent improvements when integrated into R-CNN on VOC07. Broader impact would be enhanced by stronger gains, more extensive comparisons, and clearer evidence of robustness.

Constructive suggestions:
- Position against and cite work on SVMs with probabilistic/uncertain labels and learning with label noise; clarify how e-SVM differs theoretically and empirically.
- Explicitly show how to implement the observed-label objective with standard SVM solvers (instance weights or duplicated samples).
- Provide details and analysis for the u-update (closed-form if available, or per-example convex solver), convergence criteria, runtime, and sensitivity to λR and g(·). Include an ablation with u fixed to ubb as a baseline.
- Compare against additional baselines: RankSVM for ranking-style objectives; logistic regression with soft labels; other weakly supervised segmentation methods beyond SVC/SVR.
- In detection, isolate the contribution of spatial confidence map features from changes to bounding box regression; report per-class AP tables and statistical significance.
- Improve clarity of notation, fix reference formatting, and ensure all equations are correctly typeset.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Simple, general approach; convex observed-label formulation; easy to implement.
- Empirically beneficial on competitive detection baseline; proposed evaluation metrics are useful.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited novelty relative to existing soft/probabilistic-label SVM literature.
- Modest empirical gains; insufficient analysis and ablations; clarity issues.

Overall, this is a practical contribution with some empirical value, but it would benefit from stronger positioning, deeper analysis, and clearer presentation.
