- Proposes e-SVM, a novel algorithm that learns from sample "positiveness" instead of binary labels.  
- Demonstrates e-SVM effectiveness for segment proposal classification under strong and weak supervision, outperforming alternatives.  
- Validates the method on semantic segmentation and object detection tasks.  
- Connects to prior work on weakly supervised learning and weighted SVMs, highlighting differences from WSVMs.  
- Strength: novel approach for positive-only supervision.  
- Strength: strong empirical results across supervision regimes.  
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of the algorithm and its benefits.  
- Weakness: lacks a thorough analysis of e-SVM limitations.  
- Weakness: does not compare against other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on the optimization algorithm used for training.  
- Quality: technically sound with theoretical and experimental support.  
- Clarity: well-written, well-organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Originality: presents a significant and novel contribution addressing positiveness-only labels.  
- Significance: potential to impact semantic segmentation and object detection research.  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses.
