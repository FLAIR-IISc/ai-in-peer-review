- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from weakly supervised data with continuous bounded labels, aimed at predicting segment proposal confidence.  
- Extends e-SVM to learn segment classifiers using only bounding-box supervision.  
- Evaluated on semantic segmentation and object detection, claiming state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Related to weakly supervised learning and weighted SVMs but differs by learning continuous labels rather than image-level/MIL settings and by labeling rather than weighting points.  
- Novel algorithmic contribution addressing weak supervision with continuous labels.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across multiple computer vision tasks.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection results on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Algorithm is flexible and easily extendable to different weak supervision setups.  
- Lacks thorough theoretical analysis, leaving underlying principles and guarantees unclear.  
- Risk of overfitting, particularly with large numbers of parameters.  
- Potentially high computational complexity and scalability concerns for large datasets.  
- Original approach distinct from prior weighted SVM and MIL methods.  
- Significant contribution for handling common weak supervision problems in computer vision with strong empirical gains.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, but theoretical sections need improvement.  
- Suggested improvements: provide theoretical analysis, add regularization to mitigate overfitting, and present efficient implementations to reduce computational cost.
