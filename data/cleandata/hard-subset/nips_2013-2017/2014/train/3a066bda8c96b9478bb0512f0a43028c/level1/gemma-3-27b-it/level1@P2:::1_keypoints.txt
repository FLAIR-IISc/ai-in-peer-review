- Proposes e-SVM: an SVM loss weighted by continuous “positiveness” values for weakly supervised learning.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, including state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Builds on MIL and weighted SVM literature and situates work among segmentation/detection methods like CPMC and R-CNN.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of applying standard SVC (binarization) and SVR (non-binary targets) to this problem.  
- Novelty: conceptually sound modification to SVM loss for continuous-valued labels.  
- Empirical results: consistent improvements over SVR/SVC and thorough ablation/comparisons.  
- Generality: applicable across different proposal detectors, features, and classifiers.  
- Clear problem definition and motivation for learning from positiveness values.  
- Technical soundness: correct mathematical formulation and clear iterative optimization for latent extension.  
- Limited theoretical analysis: lacks deeper understanding of why e-SVM outperforms alternatives.  
- Regularization concerns: KL-divergence regularizer for latent e-SVM is weakly justified and alternatives aren’t explored.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details (e.g., λ selection) are insufficient, harming reproducibility and robustness assessment.  
- Comparison depth: limited comparisons to more recent/advanced weakly supervised methods and confidence modeling techniques.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 2 (spatial confidence map) is hard to interpret and needs clearer visualization.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions requesting more hyperparameter details, justification of regularization, broader comparisons, and discussion of limitations/future work.
