- Proposes e-SVM: models continuous "positiveness" as Bernoulli probability and minimizes expected hinge loss, yielding a weighted SVM solvable by standard solvers.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision by jointly inferring positiveness values and classifier weights via alternating optimization with regularization toward an initial estimate.  
- Validated on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection, showing a notable 1.5 mAP improvement over R-CNN on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- High technical quality: sound formulation, clear connection to hinge loss, and thorough experiments.  
- Introduces two new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) appropriate for ranked predictions against continuous ground truth.  
- Empirically outperforms sensible baselines such as thresholded SVC and SVR.  
- Paper is well-written and logical; Figure 1 gives a helpful overview.  
- Clarify the spatial confidence map feature construction (more detail or diagram needed).  
- Ablation needed to show the impact of the additive kernels approximation mapping on final performance.  
- Novelty: principled and original way to weight positive/negative loss per example using continuous scores, distinct from WSVM heuristics.  
- Significant and practical: addresses common intermediate-strength supervision, is easy to implement, and likely to be widely adopted.  
- Demonstrates impactful combination of segmentation-based cues with CNN features to push detection performance.  
- Need better justification for design choices in latent model, e.g., using g(u)=identity and KL-divergence regularizer instead of alternatives like L2.  
- Alternating optimization may be sensitive to initialization and local minima; stability to u initialization should be discussed.  
- Generalizability claim would be strengthened by a small non-vision experiment with continuous/probabilistic labels.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, elegant formulation; strong empirical gains; practical and extensible to weak supervision.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited justification for some latent-model choices and perceived incremental nature of detection improvement (feature addition to an existing system).  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses.
