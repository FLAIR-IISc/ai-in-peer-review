- Proposes expectation-loss SVM (e-SVM) that trains with soft positiveness scores u∈[0,1] by minimizing expected hinge loss under a Bernoulli label µ=g(u), yielding a per-example weighted-hinge objective with ℓ2 regularization.  
- Extends to latent e-SVM when u is unobserved, regularizing u toward a proxy u_bb (IoU-based) with a KL term and alternating optimization over w and u.  
- Applies method to segment proposal scoring (CPMC) and builds spatial confidence map features pooled into R-CNN, reporting slight improvements on VOC11 (TAPC, NDCG) and +1.2–1.5 mAP on VOC07 detection.  
- Closely related to prior work on fuzzy/soft-label SVMs, cost-sensitive/label-dependent hinge losses, uncertain-label SVMs, posterior regularization, and latent SVMs—literature coverage is insufficient.  

Strengths:
- Simple, intuitive, convex expected-hinge loss compatible with standard SVM solvers and a straightforward latent extension.  
- Targets a practically important weak-supervision regime (IoU-like soft targets / bbox-only labels).  
- Spatial confidence map feature is sensible and yields a practically meaningful mAP increase on a strong R-CNN baseline.  
- Introduces task-agnostic proposal scoring metrics (TAPC, NDCG).

Weaknesses:
- Novelty limited: mathematically equivalent to label-dependent soft class-weighted hinge losses; distinction from weighted SVMs is largely notational.  
- Latent formulation lacks theoretical treatment (identifiability, convergence guarantees, sensitivity to λR) and omits a derivation/efficient solver for the u-update.  
- Choice g(u)=u is ad hoc; no exploration of monotone transforms or calibration despite IoU not being a calibrated probability.  
- Baselines are narrow—missing logistic regression with soft targets, RankSVM, MIL/weakly supervised segmentation methods, and alternative segment+detector integration comparisons.  
- Empirical gains for segment scoring and semantic segmentation are modest; detection gains derive from feature concatenation rather than end-to-end integration.  
- Clarity/organization issues: notation errors, omitted solver/stopping-criterion details, incomplete/malformed references, and inconsistent R-CNN baseline reporting.  
- Reproducibility gaps: missing hyperparameters (λw, λR), optimization details for u, runtime/overhead, and exact TAPC/NDCG definitions.

Quality:
- Technically sound for the convex expected-hinge loss and alternating latent scheme, but under-specified latent optimization and missing critical baselines/ablations weaken empirical claims.

Clarity:
- Readable overall but needs polishing: provide u-update derivation, precise instructions for using SVM solvers, and rigorous metric definitions.

Originality:
- Core idea is incremental given existing soft-label/cost-sensitive SVM literature; spatial pooling feature is an engineering contribution with limited novelty.

Significance:
- Relevant to weak supervision in vision and yields useful detection improvements, but conceptual contribution is modest and would benefit from stronger validation.

Suggestions:
- Position work against prior soft-label and posterior-regularization literature, clarify equivalences/differences.  
- Provide closed-form or efficient u-update, discuss convergence and λR sensitivity, and ablate choices of g(u).  
- Add stronger baselines (soft-target logistic regression, RankSVM, MIL/weakly supervised segmentation) and comparisons to other segment+detector integrations.  
- Report all hyperparameters, optimization details, runtime/overhead, and precise TAPC/NDCG definitions; fix references and baseline inconsistencies.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses a relevant weak-supervision setting with a simple, generalizable loss and shows consistent improvements plus a tangible mAP boost on VOC07.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited novelty relative to prior work, under-specified latent optimization and theoretical analysis, modest empirical gains, and insufficient baselines/ablations.
