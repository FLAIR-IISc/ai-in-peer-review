- Proposes e-SVM: replaces hinge loss with its expectation under a Bernoulli label g(u)∈[0,1], with a latent variant that alternates to infer positiveness u for weak supervision.  
- Uses g(u)=u in experiments and a convex KL regularizer to keep latent u near IoU with ground-truth boxes.  
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification (new TAPC and NDCG metrics), semantic segmentation, and object detection; concatenating e-SVM spatial maps with RCNN features yields ~1.5 AP gain on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Formulation closely relates to existing soft/probabilistic-label SVMs and instance-weighted SVMs; expected hinge is mathematically equivalent to convex combinations used in probabilistic-label SVM variants.  
- Latent extension is reminiscent of latent SVM and other weakly supervised learning approaches; application aligns with proposal-based segmentation/detection pipelines (CPMC, Selective Search, RCNN).  
- Optimization is sound (block-convex), uses standard solvers, and the KL regularizer is convex and intuitive.  
- Theoretical analysis is weak: no generalization, calibration results, or convergence guarantees beyond empirical convergence.  
- Limited robustness and sensitivity studies: lacks experiments on noisy u, g(u) misspecification, or different regularizer choices.  
- Empirical gains over SVR on segmentation are modest; the 1.5 AP detection gain is useful but not deeply analyzed (no per-class calibration, failure-mode analysis, or runtime costs).  
- Clarity issues: typos, inconsistent notation (e.g., λw·1/wTw), and missing implementation details (λR choice, stopping criteria, initialization beyond ubb).  
- Originality is incremental—core expected-hinge loss overlaps prior soft-label SVM work—while latent-u regularization and spatial confidence map feature are more novel.  
- Significance: practical and easy-to-implement recipe with measurable detection gains, but broader impact requires deeper comparisons (logistic/probit, ranking SVM, calibrated regression) and ablations (g(u) choices, temperature, regularizer forms).  
- Pros: simple/general method using off-the-shelf solvers; addresses important weakly supervised setting; clear block-convex optimization and practical regularizer; empirical VOC improvements and new evaluation criteria.  
- Cons: limited novelty relative to soft-label/weighted SVM literature; sparse theory and sensitivity analyses; modest segmentation gains and incomplete baselines; clarity/notation issues and missing ablations.
