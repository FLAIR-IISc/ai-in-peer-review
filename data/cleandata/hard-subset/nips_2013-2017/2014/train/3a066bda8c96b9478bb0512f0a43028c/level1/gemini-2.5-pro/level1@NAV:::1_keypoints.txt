Introduces e-SVM, an SVM variant for labels that are continuous "positiveness" values in [0,1] rather than binary labels.  
Treats true binary labels as Bernoulli latent variables with known success probabilities and defines loss as the expectation of the hinge loss.  
Distinct from SVR by constraining targets to [0,1] and from weighted SVMs by weighting positive/negative loss components per sample.  
Proposes a latent e-SVM for weak supervision where positiveness is treated as a latent variable and optimized with model weights, regularized by a KL term.  
Novel and elegant formulation addressing a significant problem in scaling annotations for vision tasks.  
Technically sound derivations and well-justified KL regularizer to avoid trivial solutions.  
Thorough experimental evaluation, including new metrics (TAPC, NDCG) and validation on multiple large-scale vision tasks.  
Demonstrates practical utility with a +1.5% mAP improvement on R-CNN for PASCAL VOC 2007 object detection.  
In fully supervised segmentation and classification, gains over SVR are modest or comparable, limiting impact claims.  
Object detection improvement is achieved by using e-SVM output as an additional feature for an existing system, not a fully integrated solution.  
Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated loss; strong empirical evidence on a state-of-the-art detector; general applicability beyond evaluated tasks.  
Argument against acceptance: limited performance improvement over a well-tuned SVR in fully supervised settings.  
Overall assessment: clear, novel, and significant contribution with solid theory and experiments; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses.
