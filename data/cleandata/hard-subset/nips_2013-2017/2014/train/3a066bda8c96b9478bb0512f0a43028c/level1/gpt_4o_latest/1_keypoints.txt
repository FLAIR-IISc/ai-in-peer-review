- Introduces expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle continuous “positiveness” labels instead of binary labels for weakly supervised learning.  
- Targets tasks like segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection where pixel-level annotations are unavailable.  
- Proposes weighting labels by positiveness, extending classical SVMs with a novel formulation.  
- Introduces a latent extension of e-SVM to handle weak supervision.  
- Demonstrates e-SVM outperforms SVC and SVR in both strong and weak supervision settings.  
- Validates method on PASCAL VOC and achieves state-of-the-art object detection when combined with RCNN features.  
- Adds new evaluation metrics (TAPC and NDCG) for segment proposal assessment.  
- Strength: clear technical novelty and a method well-suited to weakly supervised problems.  
- Strength: practical impact by reducing reliance on expensive pixel-level annotations.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments showing empirical superiority and integration with existing systems.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis, e.g., no detailed convergence guarantees for the latent extension.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to PASCAL VOC; broader datasets (e.g., ImageNet) not evaluated.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other recent weakly supervised approaches (MIL, deep weak-supervision frameworks).  
- Weakness: some mathematical exposition (latent e-SVM) could be clearer for non-expert readers.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, is novel and experimentally strong with SOTA results.  
- Against acceptance: limited theory and dataset diversity, and needs better positioning within weakly supervised literature.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested future work to address theoretical guarantees, broader evaluations, and clearer exposition.
