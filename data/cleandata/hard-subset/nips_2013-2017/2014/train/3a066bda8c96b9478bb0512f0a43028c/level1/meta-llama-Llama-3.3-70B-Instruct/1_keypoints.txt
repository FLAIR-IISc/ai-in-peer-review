- Proposes expectation loss SVM (e-SVM) to handle training labels that are continuous values within a bounded interval.  
- Applies e-SVM to segment proposal classification and extends it to weakly supervised segment classifier learning from bounding boxes.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on semantic segmentation and object detection, achieving state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Positions the work relative to prior weakly supervised learning and weighted SVM literature, and contrasts with SVC and SVR.  
- Shows e-SVM outperforming existing methods in segment proposal classification tasks.  
- Strength: novel approach for continuous labels with clear, well-organized presentation and detailed optimization description.  
- Strength: effective extension to weak supervision, making it practical for large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: method depends on the quality of segment proposals, which may be unreliable.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the choice and impact of the regularization term in the latent e-SVM model.  
- Weakness: could include more comparisons to related methods like Multiple Instance Learning and weighted SVMs.  
- Recommendation: minor revisions needed but overall ready for acceptance; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Quality score: 8/10 (technically sound with minor issues).  
- Clarity score: 9/10 (well-organized and concise).  
- Originality score: 9/10 (novel and effective).  
- Significance score: 9/10 (high potential impact on object detection and segmentation).
