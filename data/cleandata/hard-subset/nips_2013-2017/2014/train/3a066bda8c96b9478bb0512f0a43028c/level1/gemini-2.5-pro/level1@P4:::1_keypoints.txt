- Introduces e-SVM: an SVM variant for continuous "positiveness" scores that models labels as latent Bernoulli variables and minimizes the expected hinge loss.
- e-SVM yields a convex objective solvable with standard SVM solvers by weighting positive/negative loss components per sample.
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision by treating positiveness scores as latent and optimizing them jointly with model parameters via alternating minimization.
- Adds a KL-divergence regularizer to keep inferred positiveness scores near initial estimates and avoid trivial solutions.
- Evaluated on segment proposal classification with two new metrics (TAPC, NDCG) and shows superiority over SVC and SVR.
- Applied to semantic segmentation and object detection; integrating e-SVM spatial confidence maps with R-CNN features yields state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007.
- Technically sound: derivation and optimization are well-founded and experiments, especially detection, support the claims.
- Generally clear and well-organized, with motivation and derivations easy to follow.
- Minor clarity/reproducibility gaps: optimization of latent variables u in Algorithm 1 and details of the additive-kernel feature mapping are not fully specified.
- Novelty: e-SVM is a new expectation-based max-margin formulation distinct from weighted SVMs and offers a principled way to handle soft labels.
- Practical significance: provides a general tool for non-binary supervision, improves a strong R-CNN baseline (notably ~1.5% mAP), and offers a viable weakly supervised learning approach.
- Weaknesses: segmentation results are only "comparable" without clear quantitative baseline comparisons, and reliance on newly proposed metrics needs further validation.
- Overall recommendation: strengths (novel formulation and strong detection gains) outweigh minor weaknesses, making the work broadly valuable and worthy of acceptance.
