- Paper introduces e-SVM: an SVM formulation minimizing expected hinge loss for continuous "positiveness" scores in [0,1].
- Treats true label as latent Bernoulli variable with known positiveness probability; optimizes expectation of hinge loss.
- Extends to latent e-SVM for weak supervision by jointly estimating positiveness scores and classifier weights via alternating optimization with a KL regularizer.
- Positions e-SVM against SVC (requires thresholding) and SVR (ignores bounded nature), arguing e-SVM is more appropriate for soft labels.
- Empirical validation on segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection is thorough and convincing.
- Proposes two new evaluation metrics for segment classification: TAPC and NDCG.
- Comparisons show e-SVM outperforms strong baselines (SVC with multiple thresholds and SVR) on segment classification.
- Integrating latent e-SVM features into R-CNN yields a new state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007 (≈1.5% mAP improvement).
- Technical quality is high: formulation is sound, optimization for basic e-SVM solvable with standard solvers.
- Latent e-SVM extension is well-motivated; alternating optimization with KL regularization is reasonable for the non-convex problem.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with precise mathematical presentation and helpful figures/algorithm box.
- Minor clarity suggestion: provide more detail on the optimization/update of latent variable u in Algorithm 1 for reproducibility.
- Original contribution: novel expectation-loss SVM distinct from weighted SVMs by weighting positive/negative losses per sample.
- Novel application contributions: spatial confidence map feature for detection and introduction of TAPC/NDCG metrics.
- Significance: addresses practical weak supervision problems, shows impactful results on a challenging benchmark, and is broadly applicable.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled method, effective weak-supervision approach, strong empirical gains, clear writing, general applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance (minor): lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for alternating optimization and omits some implementation details for u-update.
- Overall conclusion: strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
