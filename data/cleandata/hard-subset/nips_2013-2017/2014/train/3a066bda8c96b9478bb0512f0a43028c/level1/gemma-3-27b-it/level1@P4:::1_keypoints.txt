- Proposes Expectation Loss SVM (e-SVM) to learn from continuous “positiveness” values (0–1) instead of binary labels.  
- Extends e-SVM to latent positiveness for weakly supervised tasks like segment proposal classification.  
- Positions e-SVM between Multiple Instance Learning and strong supervision; distinguishes from weighted SVMs by weighting scheme.  
- Provides a principled formulation modeling the expectation over labels given positiveness, avoiding ad-hoc thresholding or unsuitable regression.  
- Optimization ties to standard SVM solvers, making implementation practical.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across segment proposal classification, semantic segmentation, and object detection.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and includes helpful figures.  
- Novel algorithmic contribution, especially for handling continuous weak labels and latent positiveness.  
- Technical formulation and optimization appear mathematically sound.  
- Applications used are standard, so novelty is mainly algorithmic rather than application-level.  
- Latent e-SVM introduces a regularization hyperparameter (lambda_R) with unclear sensitivity and tuning guidance.  
- Iterative optimization for latent e-SVM may be computationally expensive for large datasets; runtime complexity is not discussed.  
- Lacks in-depth discussion of method limitations and assumptions about positiveness value distributions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, sound approach with compelling multi-task empirical validation and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited application novelty, missing analysis on computational cost and hyperparameter sensitivity, and inadequate limitations discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity (ablation for lambda_R), computational cost, and explicit limitations.
