- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments spectral approach for learning Mixture of HMMs to address EM’s computational challenges on large datasets.  
- Leverages MHMMs’ block-diagonal structure and resolves permutation ambiguity using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Clearly identifies limitations of existing MHMM learning methods and positions the contribution as a computationally efficient alternative.  
- Strong mathematical formulation, notably Lemma 1 showing equivalence between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transitions.  
- De-permutation strategy via spectral analysis is complex but appears logically sound and potentially effective.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including conjectures on noise effects and a spectral longevity criterion for determining cluster count.  
- Experimental validation includes synthetic data and a real-world handwritten trajectory dataset, demonstrating a clear speed advantage over EM.  
- Real-data experiment uses a cubic polynomial feature transformation that is not fully justified and whose impact is not analyzed.  
- Improvements on the real dataset are marginal, and specific benefits in that context are not deeply discussed.  
- Discussion of limitations is brief; robustness to various noise types and data characteristics requires more in-depth analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, computationally advantageous, clear derivations, and promising initial experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient real-world experimental validation and limited analysis of robustness and limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experimental validation and provide a more detailed discussion of limitations.
