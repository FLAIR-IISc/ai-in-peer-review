## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a novel learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM), addressing the computational challenges of traditional EM-based methods, particularly for large datasets. The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure inherent in MHMMs and utilize spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguities arising in MoM estimation.

The paper clearly identifies the limitations of existing MHMM learning techniques and positions MoM as a potentially more efficient alternative. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of relevant literature, referencing key works in MoM and MHMM learning. The theoretical contribution – showing the equivalence of an MHMM to an HMM with a specific structure – is well-explained and forms a solid foundation for their approach. The proposed method for de-permuting the parameters using spectral analysis is a clever solution to a known problem in MoM.

The experimental evaluation is a strength. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on both synthetic and real-world data. The experiments effectively illustrate the computational advantages of the spectral learning method compared to EM, especially as the dataset size increases. The inclusion of a real-world dataset (handwritten character trajectories) adds practical relevance.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the theoretical justification is present, the connection between the spectral properties and the robustness of the de-permutation step could be further elaborated. The discussion of the noise conditions (Conjecture 1 and Definitions 1-3) feels somewhat abrupt and lacks intuitive explanation. The experimental section, while comprehensive, could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the parameter sensitivity and a comparison with other recent advances in fast MHMM learning. The discussion of the polynomial feature transformation in the real data experiment feels a bit tacked on and could be better integrated into the narrative.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to MHMM learning with potential for significant computational gains.
*   Solid theoretical foundation and clear explanation of the proposed method.
*   Comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating effectiveness on both synthetic and real data.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical discussion regarding noise and spectral properties could be more accessible.
*   Limited analysis of parameter sensitivity and comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
*   The real-data experiment could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the feature transformation.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of MHMM learning. While some aspects could be further refined, the novelty, theoretical soundness, and promising experimental results warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically focusing on clarifying the theoretical aspects related to noise and expanding the experimental analysis. The authors should also consider addressing the limitations acknowledged in the paper regarding parameter sensitivity.
