- Proposes a spectral/method-of-moments approach to learn Mixtures of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and de-permuting latent states via spectral structure.  
- In noiseless case, the global transition Ā has K unit eigenvalues and Āe reveals K column clusters; in noisy case they reconstruct a rank-K approximation and cluster columns to map states to components.  
- Introduces a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K by tracking persistence of dominant eigenvalues under exponentiation.  
- Reduction of MHMM to block-diagonal HMM is correct and the permutation problem is real; the de-permutation approach is plausible.  
- Important assumptions (each Ak ergodic/aperiodic, Ā diagonalizable) must be stated explicitly early.  
- Theoretical guarantees rely on Conjecture 1 and reused sample-complexity bounds from a different setting, weakening formal claims.  
- Spectral longevity is an interesting heuristic but lacks formal analysis and guarantees.  
- Suggests using matrix perturbation results (Weyl, Davis–Kahan) to convert conjectures into theorems under spectral gap conditions.  
- Algorithm description is clear, but conditions for the underlying MoM HMM routine (Ō full column rank, L ≥ MK, identifiability for continuous emissions) are only partially discussed.  
- Practical implications of feature lifting and noise sensitivity need more detail, since L ≥ MK is restrictive in many applications.  
- Synthetic experiments show de-permutation and speed/accuracy trade-offs versus EM; real-data experiment shows usefulness as an EM initializer.  
- Missing empirical stress tests: vary spectral gaps, similarity of stationary distributions, K-estimation accuracy, and provide runtime breakdowns (moments vs eigendecomposition vs clustering).  
- Writing is generally clear; notation consistency (noisy vs noiseless) and an intuitive/pseudocode explanation of longevity would help.  
- Novelty: using spectral structure of the global transition to resolve permutation for MHMMs and estimating K via eigenvalue longevity appears new relative to EM-based prior work.  
- Significance: method is potentially impactful for scalable sequence clustering but limited by L ≥ MK and effects of feature lifting on identifiability.  
- Essential revisions: explicitly state assumptions, either prove Conjecture 1 under spectral-gap/perturbation conditions or reframe as heuristic, and thoroughly evaluate K-estimation across regimes.  
- Essential revisions (cont.): Discuss failure modes (identical stationary distributions, small gaps, non-diagonalizable Ā) and provide diagnostics/mitigations.  
- Essential revisions (cont.): Detail computational complexity of moment estimation and eigendecomposition and discuss continuous-emission assumptions and feature mapping trade-offs.  
- Optional suggestions: consider more stable numerical methods (real Schur, subspace iterations), add practical baselines (EM+k-means, per-cluster spectral HMM), broaden real datasets, and release code.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear reduction to block-diagonal HMM, novel practical de-permutation strategy, demonstrated computational advantages and value as EM initializer.  
- Arguments against acceptance: central guarantees are conjectural, applicability constrained by strong conditions (L ≥ MK, full-rank Ō), and limited robustness evaluation in challenging spectral regimes.  
- Overall: promising and practically motivated idea that needs stronger theoretical support and broader robustness experiments to be compelling.
