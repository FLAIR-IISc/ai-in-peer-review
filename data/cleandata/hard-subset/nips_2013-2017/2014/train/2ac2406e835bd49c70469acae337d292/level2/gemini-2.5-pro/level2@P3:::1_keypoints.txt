- Introduces a novel spectral/method-of-moments algorithm to learn Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating the mixture as a single HMM with MK states.  
- Identifies permutation ambiguity from spectral learning on the merged HMM as the core challenge for recovering individual HMM components.  
- Main technical contribution: de-permutation method using spectral clustering of columns of a low-rank reconstruction of the global transition matrix.  
- Proposes an eigenvalue-based heuristic ("spectral longevity") to estimate the number of mixture components K.  
- Demonstrates the algorithm on synthetic and real data, showing substantial speed gains over EM and value as an initializer for EM.  
- The noiseless-case theory (Lemmas 1–2) is clear and establishes the foundation for the approach.  
- The noisy-case theoretical analysis is weaker, relying on Conjecture 1 and analogy to prior work rather than a formal sample-complexity proof.  
- Conjecture 1 needs a more formal derivation or justification, even under simplifying assumptions.  
- Lemma 3 and the spectral longevity criterion are heuristic; implementation details (finite-range choice) and robustness are not fully elaborated.  
- Suggests framing model selection more directly via ratios of consecutive eigenvalues (eigengap) as a potentially more robust alternative.  
- Experimental evaluation is solid and honest; shows cases where EM has higher accuracy but highlights spectral method's speed and initializer utility.  
- Recommends additional real-data experiments with K>2 to better demonstrate practical performance.  
- Algorithm 1 is high-level and should specify the clustering method (e.g., k-means) and other implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Notation could be clearer—distinguish estimated quantities (e.g., use hats) to avoid confusion between permuted and noisy estimates.  
- Real-data preprocessing (cubic polynomial feature transform to meet MK < L) is under-described and should be detailed for reproducibility.  
- The contribution is original: first computationally efficient, non-iterative spectral algorithm for MHMMs and the spectral de-permutation idea is novel.  
- The work is significant: offers a fast, consistent alternative to EM and a practical hybrid (spectral init + EM) with wide applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, presents an elegant novel solution, is computationally efficient, and has strong empirical benefits.  
- Arguments for revision: noisy-case theory depends on a conjecture and heuristic model selection; experiments should include more complex real-world scenarios.
