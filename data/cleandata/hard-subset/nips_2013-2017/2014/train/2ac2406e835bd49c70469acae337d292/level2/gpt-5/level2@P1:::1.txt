Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes a spectral, method-of-moments (MoM) algorithm for learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key observation is that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix Ā, obtained by concatenating per-cluster emission matrices and stacking transition matrices along the diagonal. The authors first apply an existing spectral HMM learner (e.g., Hsu–Kakade–Zhang; Anandkumar et al.) to estimate global parameters up to a permutation of the MK latent states. They then propose a “depermutation” step based on spectral properties of Ā: in the noiseless case, Ā has K eigenvalues equal to 1 (one per block), and powering Ā isolates K clusters of columns corresponding to blocks; in the noisy case, they propose a rank-K reconstruction from the leading eigenpairs and cluster columns to recover the block structure. They also propose a heuristic (“spectral longevity”) to estimate K by examining how eigenvalues persist under exponentiation. Experiments on synthetic data investigate sensitivity to data size and compare accuracy/speed to EM; a small real-data experiment (UCI handwritten characters) shows the spectral method is fast and a good initializer for EM.

This builds on spectral learning for HMMs (Hsu, Kakade, Zhang 2009; Anandkumar et al. 2012) and on earlier MHMMs learned by EM (e.g., Smyth 1997 NIPS), and is related to spectral methods for mixtures (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).

Strengths:
- Clear and appealing reduction: reinterpreting MHMMs as block-diagonal HMMs is simple and insightful, and it suggests a natural route to spectral estimation plus a depermutation step.
- Computational motivation is strong: avoiding repeated forward–backward per sequence/iteration is desirable for large datasets. The “moments first, then factorize” paradigm fits the use case.
- The depermutation idea—use the eigenspace associated with eigenvalues near 1 and cluster columns—is intuitive and could be broadly useful.
- Empirical results indicate substantial runtime gains and that the spectral solution can be a strong initializer for EM.

Weaknesses and concerns:
- Theory is incomplete where it matters most. The main robustness result (Conjecture 1) is left as a conjecture, and Lemma 3 depends on separation conditions and an ad hoc “longevity” metric without rigorous guarantees. Standard perturbation tools (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Weyl) could likely yield provable conditions for recovering K and the depermutation under eigengap assumptions.
- Assumptions on Markov chains and emissions are under-specified. The method relies on each Ak being ergodic with a unique eigenvalue at 1 and diagonalizable; periodicity, near-reducibility, or complex eigenpairs are not discussed. For emissions, spectral HMM methods typically require full-column rank and suitable multi-view features; the paper briefly mentions E[x|state] for Gaussian/Poisson/Multinomial but does not detail which moments are used, how continuous outputs are handled, or the exact procedure used in “HMM MethodofMoments.”
- Dimensionality requirement MK < L is restrictive in practice; the real-data experiment circumvents it by a cubic feature map, which may distort temporal structure and complicate interpretation. It is unclear how robust the approach is when L is not much larger than MK, or how to proceed when L < MK without aggressive feature lifting.
- The depermutation step’s reliability under realistic noise/spectral-gap regimes is not thoroughly evaluated. The proposed K estimator (“longevity”) seems heuristic; comparisons to standard eigengap methods or information criteria are missing.
- Empirical evaluation is relatively narrow. Baselines beyond EM (e.g., EM with smarter initialization, spectral clustering on transition matrices, Bayesian nonparametric MHMMs) are not included. Quantitative parameter recovery (e.g., error on A_k and O_k) is not reported. Details necessary for reproduction (moment definitions, SVD tolerances, number of power iterations/eigen-exponentiation range, clustering settings) are sparse.

Quality:
- Technically promising but currently partial. The core reduction is sound; the depermutation/K-estimation steps need stronger analysis or more extensive empirical validation. Claims about computational complexity would benefit from precise costs for moment computation and factorization.

Clarity:
- Overall readable with a helpful high-level narrative and figures. However, the paper omits crucial details of the MoM subroutine for HMMs (moment construction, assumptions, identifiability conditions), the exact depermutation algorithm (distance metric, clustering initialization), and the conditions under which MK < L is required. Adding a concise derivation of the used moments and a clear statement of assumptions would improve clarity and reproducibility.

Originality:
- Moderate. The block-diagonal reinterpretation is straightforward but well-leveraged. The depermutation via eigenstructure and “spectral longevity” appear novel for MHMMs.

Significance:
- Potentially useful in domains with many sequences and moderate state spaces, especially as an EM initializer. The current theoretical gaps and dimensionality constraints limit immediate impact, but the direction is promising.

Questions and suggestions for the authors:
- Specify the exact MoM algorithm used for HMMs: what moments (pairwise/triple), feature maps for continuous observations, whitening/factorization steps, and assumptions (full-rank conditions on Ō, sample complexity).
- Can the MK < L requirement be relaxed via multi-view features (e.g., stack x_{t−1}, x_t, x_{t+1}) as in Anandkumar et al., or via random features, so rank conditions hold without high-dimensional lifting?
- Provide robustness experiments varying (i) spectral gaps of Ak, (ii) chain periodicity/near-reducibility, and (iii) similarity of stationary distributions across clusters.
- Replace or substantiate the “spectral longevity” criterion with a more principled approach (e.g., powered matrix plus eigengap thresholding) and justify thresholds.
- Report parameter recovery error (up to permutation) and downstream clustering performance, and add baselines including EM with k-means initialization on emissions and a spectral clustering approach on columns/rows of A.
- Detail computational complexity and wall-clock time breakdowns (moments vs factorization) as functions of N, T, L, M, K, and memory use.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, computationally attractive approach to a relevant problem.
- Novel depermutation/K-estimation ideas that could spur follow-up work.
- Positive empirical evidence and utility as an EM initializer.

Cons for acceptance:
- Key results are heuristic/unproven; conditions and assumptions not fully specified.
- Practical limitations (MK < L) and limited empirical scope.
- Reproducibility hindered by missing algorithmic details.

Overall, I find the direction valuable and the ideas promising, but I encourage the authors to strengthen the theoretical guarantees for the noisy case, clarify assumptions and the MoM procedure, and broaden/solidify the empirical evaluation.
