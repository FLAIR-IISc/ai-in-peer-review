- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for MHMMs by recasting an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.
- Uses existing spectral HMM estimators to recover parameters up to a global permutation.
- Resolves permutation ambiguity by exploiting spectral structure: reconstruct a rank-K approximation, cluster its columns to recover blocks, and de-permute into individual HMMs.
- Proposes a heuristic K-estimator called “spectral longevity.”
- Builds on spectral learning for HMMs/latent-variable models (Hsu; Anandkumar) and relates to earlier sequence-clustering HMM work (Smyth).
- Formulation and reduction to block-diagonal HMM are sound and the de-permutation intuition is persuasive.
- Theoretical support is incomplete: Conjecture 1 on sample complexity/perturbation is unproven.
- K-identification relies on heuristic/empirical longevity rather than established perturbation bounds (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin).
- Empirically: synthetic studies show de-permutation behavior vs. sequence length and speed–accuracy comparisons to EM; a small real-data test shows value as an EM initializer.
- Runtime and scalability claims need explicit algorithmic complexity and wall-clock timing comparisons.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well organized; the block-diagonal equivalence and algorithm are easy to follow.
- Some notation is dense and references are incomplete/unevenly formatted.
- The MK < L requirement and use of polynomial feature lifting need clearer discussion of modeling consequences and potential mis-specification.
- Originality: novel viewpoint on MHMM learning and a practical spectral criterion for K, but related literature (spectral mixture identification, graph partitioning) could be broadened.
- Significance: promising fast, initialization-free alternative or strong EM initializer if robust, but dependence on eigengaps and MK < L may limit applicability.
- Pros: simple, computationally attractive pipeline; new approach to permutation resolution; competitive accuracy with substantial speedups; useful EM initialization.
- Cons: key guarantees are conjectural and K selection is heuristic; strong assumptions (ergodicity, MK < L); limited real-data evaluation and ablations; incomplete references.
- Suggestions: replace Conjecture 1 with perturbation analysis (Davis–Kahan), compare K-selection to spectral-gap heuristics and information criteria, provide wall-clock scaling and sensitivity to noise/eigengaps, explore graph-partition/assignment formulations for de-permutation, and discuss alternatives when L < MK (discrete/count emissions, tensor methods).
