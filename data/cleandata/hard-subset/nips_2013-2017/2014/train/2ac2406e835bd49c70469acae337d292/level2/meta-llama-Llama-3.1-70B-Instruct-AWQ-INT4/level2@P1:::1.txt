This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM). The authors' approach leverages the spectral properties of a global transition matrix to resolve the permutation ambiguity that arises during the estimation process. The proposed algorithm is computationally more efficient than traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) methods, especially when dealing with large datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach. The use of lemmas and corollaries to establish the theoretical foundations of the algorithm is helpful. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to address the permutation ambiguity issue that arises in MHMM learning. The authors' use of spectral properties to resolve this issue is novel and effective. The proposed algorithm is also computationally efficient, making it suitable for large-scale datasets.

The paper also provides a good comparison with EM methods, demonstrating that the proposed approach can achieve comparable accuracy while being computationally more efficient. The real data experiment on the handwritten character trajectory dataset is also a good demonstration of the proposed approach's effectiveness.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the spectral conditions required for the proposed algorithm to work effectively. The authors mention that the noise level should be sufficiently small, but a more quantitative analysis of this requirement would be helpful.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as Bayesian non-parametric methods or other spectral methods. This would help to establish the proposed approach as a state-of-the-art method for MHMM learning.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is novel and effective. With some additional analysis and comparisons, the paper could be even stronger.
