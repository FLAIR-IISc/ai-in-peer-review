- Proposal: spectral Method of Moments for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs), treating MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition and resolving permutation via spectral properties.  
- Theoretical claim: Lemma 1 establishes block-diagonal equivalence and spectral depermutation to recover component assignments.  
- Algorithm: Algorithm 1 is clearly presented and recovers individual HMM parameters and cluster count K via eigenvalue analysis and clustering.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic experiments show significantly faster runtime (data-size independent) and accuracy comparable to EM; real-data experiment shows usefulness as EM initialization.  
- Strength: The approach has a solid theoretical foundation and offers compelling computational advantages.  
- Weakness: Main noise/error robustness relies on Conjecture 1 without proof or empirical validation, weakening theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: Real-data preprocessing (cubic polynomial transform to satisfy MK < L) is ad hoc and unjustified, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: Paper omits comparisons to recent MHMM methods (e.g., EM variants such as [10]), so novelty and performance vs. state-of-the-art are unclear.  
- Weakness: The MK < L requirement is acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed for practical impact.  
- Suggested fixes: Provide a proof sketch or empirical validation for Conjecture 1, include direct comparisons to recent MHMM techniques, and critically discuss the MK < L constraint.  
- Overall verdict: Well-written and promising but reduced significance due to theoretical gaps and missing comparisons; reviewer recommends weak acceptance with major revisions.
