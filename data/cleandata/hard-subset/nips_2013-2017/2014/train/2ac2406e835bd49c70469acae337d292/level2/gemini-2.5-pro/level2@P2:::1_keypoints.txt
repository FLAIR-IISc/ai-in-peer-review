- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by representing the mixture as one larger HMM with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Applies existing spectral HMM learning to the global model, recovering parameters up to a permutation that mixes component HMMs.  
- Main contribution: a novel de-permutation method using low-rank reconstruction of the global transition matrix and clustering its columns to reveal block structure.  
- Provides a heuristic for estimating the number of mixture components K.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real data, showing substantial speedup over EM and usefulness as an EM initializer.  
- Strength: original and significant — first application of spectral learning to MHMMs and a fast non-iterative alternative to EM.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written with effective figures (e.g., Fig.1) that explain intuition and algorithm behavior.  
- Strength: core idea is clever and well-motivated; noiseless analysis is sound and noisy-case low-rank approach is practical.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification for the noisy case is weak — Conjecture 1 lacks formal proof and relies on hand-wavy arguments from different models.  
- Weakness: Lemma 3 and the “longevity” metric linking to eigenvalue gaps are intuitive but not rigorously proven.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is incomplete — lacks EM variance across restarts, exploration of failure modes (when eigenvalue gap shrinks), and broader real-data tests.  
- Weakness: reliance on the MK < L constraint of spectral HMMs is a practical limitation that is not sufficiently discussed; standalone performance can be worse than EM.  
- Weakness: key assumptions (unique stationary distributions, irreducibility/aperiodicity, spectral gap condition) are not explicitly discussed or analyzed for violations.  
- Suggestion: formalize or empirically validate theoretical claims (e.g., show convergence rate of reconstruction error ‖Ar − Ar_hat‖_F vs TN).  
- Suggestion: report best/worst/average EM performance over restarts, study sensitivity to shrinking eigenvalue gaps, and test on additional real datasets or feature strategies to handle MK ≥ L.  
- Overall: novel, elegant, and computationally attractive approach with promising empirical results, but its robustness is undermined by gaps in theoretical rigor and limited experimental exploration.
