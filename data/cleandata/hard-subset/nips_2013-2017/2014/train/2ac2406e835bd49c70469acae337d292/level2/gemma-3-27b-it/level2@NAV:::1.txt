## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a novel learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM), addressing the computational challenges of traditional EM-based methods, particularly for large datasets. The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure inherent in MHMMs and resolve permutation ambiguity using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.

The paper clearly identifies the limitations of existing MHMM learning techniques and positions its contribution as a computationally efficient alternative. The mathematical formulation, particularly the equivalence established between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices (Lemma 1), is well-presented and forms a strong foundation for the proposed method. The subsequent development of the de-permutation strategy using spectral analysis, while complex, appears logically sound and potentially effective. 

A significant strength of the paper lies in its thorough theoretical analysis, including conjectures regarding the influence of noise and the spectral longevity criteria for determining the number of clusters. The experimental evaluation, involving synthetic data and a real-world dataset (handwritten character trajectories), provides initial validation of the approach. The comparison with EM, showcasing the speed advantage of the proposed method, is particularly compelling.

However, the paper could benefit from more extensive experimental validation. The real-data experiment uses a dimensionality reduction technique (cubic polynomial feature transformation) which isn't fully justified and its impact on the results isn't thoroughly discussed. Furthermore, the results of the real-data experiment show only marginal improvements, and a more detailed analysis of the specific benefits in this context would be valuable. The discussion of limitations is relatively brief. While the authors acknowledge noise, a more in-depth analysis of the method's robustness to various types of noise and data characteristics would strengthen the work. 

**Arguments for acceptance:** The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded approach to MHMM learning, offering a potential computational advantage over existing methods. The mathematical derivations are clear, and the experimental results, though limited, are promising.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The experimental validation is not extensive enough, particularly on real-world datasets. The discussion of limitations is brief, and a more thorough analysis of robustness is needed.

**Overall:** This paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. While further experimental validation and a more detailed discussion of limitations are recommended, the novel approach and strong theoretical foundation justify a positive assessment. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points mentioned above.
