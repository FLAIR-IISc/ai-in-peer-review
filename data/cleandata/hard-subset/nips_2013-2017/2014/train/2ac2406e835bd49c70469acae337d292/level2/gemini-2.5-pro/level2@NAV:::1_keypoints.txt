Introduces a Method of Moments algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs by treating the mixture as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.

Applies standard spectral HMM learning to this formulation, which induces a permutation ambiguity mixing component parameters.

Main contribution is a de-permutation technique that uses spectral properties of the estimated global transition matrix to resolve this ambiguity.

Recovers block-diagonal structure and individual HMM parameters via low-rank reconstruction using the top K eigenvalues.

Original and computationally efficient approach that addresses scaling MHMM learning.

De-permutation is a strong, well-motivated technical insight and the paper is clearly written.

Experimental results show substantial speed-ups over traditional EM on synthetic data.

Theoretical analysis is weakened by reliance on Conjecture 1, which lacks a formal proof.

Method for identifying the number of clusters K depends on a spectral gap condition that may be fragile in practice.

Empirical evaluation is limited (mostly synthetic plus one real dataset) and on that real dataset the method does not consistently outperform randomly initialized EM, suggesting primary utility as an EM initialization.

Recommendation: accept, but authors should strengthen theoretical justification for Conjecture 1 and expand real-data experiments to better compare against baselines.
