- Proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) spectral approach for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by viewing MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Tackles permutation ambiguity by using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Demonstrates on synthetic and real data that the spectral method is computationally faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy.  
- Technically interesting contribution extending spectral HMM learning to MHMMs; Lemma 1 (block-diagonal view) is well-motivated and correct.  
- Algorithm 1 for resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral analysis is a valuable methodological addition.  
- Experimental results (Figure 4) show significant speed improvements over EM without substantial accuracy loss.  
- Real-data experiment indicates practical utility, e.g., as an effective initialization for EM.  
- Paper properly cites relevant prior work on spectral learning for HMMs and MHMMs.  
- Major theoretical gap: Conjecture 1 (central to noise/estimation analysis) lacks a rigorous proof or MHMM-specific justification.  
- Insufficient comparison to existing MHMM learning approaches; differences and advantages over prior methods (e.g., [4]) are not clearly established.  
- Lemma 3’s spectral longevity condition (|λGK| > maxk |λL2,k|) is not well-justified or linked to practical data/model constraints.  
- Real-data experiment limitation: data was transformed to satisfy MK < L, but implications and general applicability of this constraint are not discussed.  
- Recommendations: provide a rigorous proof or limitations for Conjecture 1, include deeper comparisons with prior MHMM methods, explain practical meaning of the spectral condition, and address the data-transformation limitation.  
- Overall: promising, computationally efficient approach with practical utility, but theoretical weaknesses and insufficient comparisons limit its current scientific contribution.
