- Proposes a method-of-moments algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM).  
- Leverages spectral properties of a global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity in MHMM estimation.  
- Algorithm is computationally more efficient than Expectation-Maximization (EM), especially on large datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and helpful mathematical lemmas and corollaries.  
- Experimental results show strong accuracy and computational efficiency.  
- Novel and effective solution to the permutation ambiguity problem via spectral methods.  
- Real-data experiment on handwritten character trajectories demonstrates practical effectiveness.  
- Lacks a detailed, quantitative analysis of the spectral conditions and noise-level requirements for the method to work.  
- Needs broader comparisons with other approaches (e.g., Bayesian nonparametric and other spectral methods).  
- Technically sound with clear notation and useful figures.  
- Potentially significant and widely applicable if spectral condition analysis and comparisons are added.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept (novel, efficient, effective) but caveat due to missing analyses; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10.
