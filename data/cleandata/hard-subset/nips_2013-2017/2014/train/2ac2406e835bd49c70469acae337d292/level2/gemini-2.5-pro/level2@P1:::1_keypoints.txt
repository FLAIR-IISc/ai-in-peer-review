- Proposes a novel Method of Moments (MoM) spectral algorithm to learn Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with KM states and block-diagonal transitions.  
- Identifies permutation ambiguity as the central obstacle: spectral HMM learning recovers KM states only up to a permutation, mixing component parameters.  
- Main contribution is a spectral de-permutation technique: compute a low-rank reconstruction using top K eigenvalues and cluster columns to recover component state assignments.  
- Proposes a heuristic ("spectral longevity") based on the transition matrix spectrum to estimate the number of components K.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show large speedups over EM and comparable clustering accuracy.  
- The method provides an effective, non-random initialization for EM, improving EM performance.  
- Paper is technically sound and self-contained, with a clear distinction between noiseless and noisy analyses.  
- Noiseless-case analysis is clear and correct; noisy-case analysis depends on Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3 with informal justification.  
- Theoretical guarantees for the noisy case are limited; Conjecture 1 is cited rather than fully derived.  
- The "spectral longevity" model-selection heuristic is interesting but lacks full theoretical backing.  
- Method success depends on a spectral gap condition (|λGK| > max_k |λL2,k|); paper offers little discussion on when this holds or failure modes.  
- Accuracy can be slightly lower than EM on small datasets; primary advantage is speed and initialization utility.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized; Lemma 1, Figure 1, and Algorithm 1 effectively explain the approach.  
- Some notation (Section 3.2.2) is dense and would benefit from more intuition for eigenvalues (λG, λL).  
- Real-data experiment description lacks some detail (e.g., the accuracy metric used in Table 1).  
- Original and non-trivial extension of spectral HMM methods to MHMMs; first MoM-based spectral approach for MHMMs to the reviewer’s knowledge.  
- Significant practical value: makes MHMM learning scalable and may inspire spectral methods for other hierarchical latent models.
