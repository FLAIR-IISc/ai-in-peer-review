- Proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) using the block-diagonal structure of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity.  
- Leverages spectral properties to develop a de-permutation technique for MHMM parameter recovery.  
- Demonstrates feasibility both theoretically (with spectral conditions) and empirically on synthetic and real data.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear problem statement, motivation for MoM (scalability), and solution presentation.  
- Key observation: MHMMs can be seen as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices, enabling the proposed approach.  
- The discussion of spectral conditions required for successful de-permutation adds useful practical nuance.  
- Experiments show computational efficiency advantages over EM, particularly on large datasets.  
- Real-data experiment provides a plausible demonstration but is limited by the need for a cubic polynomial feature transformation.  
- Theoretical justification is weak: key results rely on conjectures (eigenvalue separation, spectral longevity) lacking rigorous proofs.  
- Justification for Conjecture 1 depends on sample-complexity results from related work without clear adaptation to MHMMs.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of noise sensitivity and its effect on de-permutation accuracy.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; should vary number of hidden states (M) and mixtures (K) to better characterize performance.  
- Needs more comprehensive comparisons to other MHMM learning techniques beyond EM.  
- Related-work discussion is incomplete; should cite spectral methods for clustering and model selection.  
- The impact and sensitivity of the cubic polynomial feature transformation on real-data results are not analyzed or compared to alternatives.  
- The spectral longevity metric (Lλ̃K′) is not intuitively explained and would benefit from clearer illustration.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel spectral MoM approach, addresses EM scalability, theoretically motivated solution to permutation ambiguity, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on conjectures without rigorous analysis, incomplete experimental validation, and unaddressed feature-transformation effects.  
- Overall: solid and original contribution that would be strengthened by tighter theoretical analysis and more comprehensive experiments; recommend revision.
