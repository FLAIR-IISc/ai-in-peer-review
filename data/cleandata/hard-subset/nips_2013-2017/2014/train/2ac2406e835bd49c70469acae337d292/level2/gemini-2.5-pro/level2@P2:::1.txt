This paper proposes a spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The standard algorithm for learning MHMMs, Expectation-Maximization (EM), can be computationally expensive for large datasets and is sensitive to initialization. This work aims to provide a faster, non-iterative alternative. The key insight is that an MHMM with K components can be represented as a single, larger HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors propose applying an existing spectral HMM learning algorithm to this larger model. However, this procedure recovers the model parameters only up to a permutation of the hidden states, which mixes the parameters of the individual component HMMs. The main contribution of the paper is a novel method to resolve this permutation ambiguity. The proposed solution exploits the spectral properties of the estimated global transition matrix. By performing a low-rank reconstruction using the eigenvectors corresponding to the K largest eigenvalues (which are close to 1 in the noisy case), the authors show that clustering the columns of this reconstructed matrix can reveal the block structure and thus identify the correct permutation. The paper also provides a heuristic for estimating the number of mixture components, K. The method is validated on synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its significant speed advantage over EM.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Originality and Significance:** The paper presents a novel and elegant solution to a relevant problem. To my knowledge, this is the first application of spectral learning techniques to the MHMM. Given the computational burden of EM for sequence clustering on large datasets, a fast, non-iterative alternative is a significant contribution. The method also provides a principled, data-driven way to initialize EM, which is a valuable contribution in its own right, as demonstrated in the real data experiment.

2.  **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and the core ideas are presented very clearly. The formulation of the MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal structure is well-explained and provides a solid foundation for the proposed approach. Figure 1 is particularly effective at providing intuition for the de-permutation algorithm and the effect of estimation noise.

3.  **Soundness of the Core Idea:** The central idea of using the spectral properties of the permuted transition matrix to recover the block structure is clever and well-motivated. The analysis for the noiseless case is sound, and the extension to the noisy case via low-rank approximation is a reasonable and practical approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Theoretical Rigor:** The primary weakness of the paper lies in the theoretical justification for the noisy case. Conjecture 1, which bounds the error of the low-rank reconstruction, is presented without a formal proof. The justification provided is hand-wavy, relying on a sample complexity result from a related but different model (mixture models vs. HMMs) and does not constitute a rigorous argument. This is a critical step, as the success of the entire de-permutation algorithm rests on this reconstruction being accurate enough. Similarly, Lemma 3 provides an interesting heuristic for determining K, but the connection between the proposed "longevity" metric and the eigenvalue gap is more intuitive than formally proven. These theoretical gaps reduce the confidence in the method's robustness.

2.  **Experimental Evaluation:** The experiments demonstrate the promise of the algorithm but could be more thorough.
    *   The comparison with EM in Figure 4 is useful, but it would be strengthened by showing the variance of EM's accuracy across its multiple random restarts. This would give a better sense of how often the proposed method is better than a "lucky" EM initialization.
    *   The experiments do not explore the failure modes of the algorithm. For instance, how does performance degrade as the key condition `|λ_GK| > max_k |λ_L2,k|` is weakened (i.e., when the gap between the "global" and "local" eigenvalues shrinks)?
    *   The real data experiment is on a single dataset and requires a feature transformation to satisfy the `MK < L` constraint. This constraint is a significant practical limitation of the underlying spectral HMM method and deserves more discussion. While the proposed method works well as an initializer here, its performance as a standalone algorithm is not superior to randomly initialized EM.

3.  **Discussion of Assumptions:** The paper does not explicitly discuss all the underlying assumptions. The method implicitly assumes that each component HMM has a unique stationary distribution (i.e., its transition matrix is irreducible and aperiodic) and that the spectral gap condition holds. A discussion about the implications of violating these assumptions would strengthen the paper.

**Constructive Suggestions:**

1.  The authors should try to formalize the theoretical claims in Section 3.2.2. If full proofs are intractable, they should explicitly state this and provide more empirical validation for their conjectures. For example, they could empirically show the convergence rate of the reconstruction error `‖Ar − Ar_hat‖_F` with respect to the amount of data (TN).

2.  For the real data experiment, it would be informative to report the best, worst, and average performance of the randomly initialized EM over its restarts, rather than just the maximum. This would provide a more complete picture of its performance relative to the proposed deterministic method.

3.  A more thorough discussion of the limitations of the approach would be welcome. In particular, the `MK < L` condition is a major hurdle for applying this method to data with low-dimensional observations and/or a large number of states/clusters. The authors should discuss potential workarounds or acknowledge this as a key area for future work.

In summary, this paper introduces a novel and computationally attractive algorithm for a challenging problem. The core idea is elegant and the empirical results are promising, especially regarding computational speed and its utility as an initialization scheme for EM. The main weakness is the lack of theoretical rigor supporting the claims for the noisy case. Despite this, the originality and potential significance of the work make it a valuable contribution to the community.
