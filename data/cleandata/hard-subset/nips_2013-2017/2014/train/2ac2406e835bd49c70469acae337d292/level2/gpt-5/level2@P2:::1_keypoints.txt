- Summary: Proposes a spectral/MoM method to learn MHMMs by viewing them as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions and depermuting a global spectral estimate to recover components.

- Key insight: Reduction of MHMM to an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling use of spectral HMM learners.

- Spectral claim: Global transition matrix has K unit eigenvalues under aperiodicity/ergodicity; eigenvectors cluster by block in the noiseless case.

- Depermutation method: Rank-K reconstruction, a heuristic “spectral longevity” statistic to estimate K, and k-means on reconstructed transition columns to infer permutation.

- Empirical results: Synthetic experiments show large speedups vs EM and competitive accuracy; small real-data experiment suggests spectral estimate is a useful EM initializer.

- Strength: Clean, insightful reduction leveraging existing spectral HMM machinery.

- Strength: Correct and practically useful noiseless spectral characterization.

- Strength: Addresses EM’s computational and initialization pain points with a simple postprocessing step.

- Strength: Demonstrates promising speedups and utility as an EM initializer.

- Major weakness: Central finite-sample claim is only a conjecture (Conjecture 1) with no rigorous perturbation/finite-sample analysis.

- Major weakness: Dependence on strong, under-discussed assumptions (aperiodicity, large spectral gap, L ≥ MK and O full column rank).

- Major weakness: Algorithm inconsistently assumes MK known (MoM HMM takes MK as input) despite claiming to estimate K; experiments use true MK.

- Major weakness: K-estimation and depermutation validation is limited and ad hoc; no quantitative K̂ accuracy or sensitivity analysis.

- Major weakness: MoM HMM step is treated as a black box—missing specifics on moments, whitening, regularization, and identifiability for continuous observations.

- Minor weakness: Notational errors and typos reduce clarity.

- Minor weakness: Computational scalability concerns (MK×MK eigendecomposition, moment storage) are underexplored.

- Minor weakness: Related work coverage incomplete (variational/stochastic EM, HDP-HMM, spectral multi-view methods missing).

- Quality: Moderate — idea sound but noisy-case guarantees conjectural and algorithmic details missing.

- Clarity: Mixed — core message clear but MoM subroutine, assumptions, and longevity measure need clearer formalization.

- Originality: Modest — reduction is known; depermutation via global eigen-structure and longevity heuristic are incremental novelties.

- Significance: Potentially useful for scalable sequence clustering and EM initialization, but limited by lack of theory and incomplete validation.

- Pros for acceptance: Simple, intuitive approach with promising speedups and practical use as EM initialization.

- Cons for acceptance: Core result conjectural, requires true MK, heuristic K-estimation not convincing, strong assumptions and limited experiments.

- Suggested improvements: Provide rigorous perturbation analysis (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin), detail the MoM HMM step for continuous data, and analyze L < MK handling.

- Suggested improvements: Develop principled model selection for K/MK (eigengap tests, stability), report K̂ accuracy, and expand ablation and robustness experiments.

- Suggested improvements: Improve clarity, fix notation/typos, discuss computational scaling, and compare to stronger baselines.

- Recommendation: Reject in current form; revise with stronger theory, clearer method details, and more extensive validation to become a solid contribution.
