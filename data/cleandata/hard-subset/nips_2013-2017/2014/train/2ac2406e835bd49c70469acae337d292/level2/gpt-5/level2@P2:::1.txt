Summary
The paper proposes a spectral/method-of-moments (MoM) approach to learn Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key observation is that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, with one block per component. The authors first apply an existing spectral HMM learner to estimate “global” HMM parameters, which are recovered up to an unknown permutation of the MK latent states. They then propose to “depermute” by exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix: in the noiseless case, there are K unit eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenvectors cluster by block; under noise, they propose a rank-K reconstruction and a heuristic to estimate K via “spectral longevity” of eigenvalues, followed by k-means clustering on columns of the reconstructed transition matrix to infer the permutation. Experiments on synthetic data show computational benefits versus EM and some accuracy comparisons; a small real-data experiment on handwritten character trajectories suggests the spectral solution is a good initialization for EM.

Strengths
- Insightful reduction: recasting MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix is clean and leverages existing spectral HMM machinery.
- The spectral characterization of the global transition matrix (K unit eigenvalues under aperiodicity/ergodicity) is correct and practically useful.
- The paper addresses a core practical pain-point of EM for MHMMs (computational cost and initialization) and offers a simple postprocessing step to recover component parameters from a permuted global estimate.
- Empirical results indicate large speedups and competitive accuracy; using the spectral estimate to initialize EM is a reasonable and useful practice.

Weaknesses
Major:
- The central finite-sample argument is left as Conjecture 1 with an informal justification. There is no rigorous analysis of when the top-K “global” eigen-structure can be reliably separated from the “local” eigenvalues under MoM estimation noise. The proposed longevity statistic is ad hoc and not theoretically grounded.
- The method depends on strong and under-discussed assumptions: (i) each Ak has exactly one eigenvalue of magnitude 1 (requires aperiodicity); (ii) a large spectral gap condition |λG_K| > max_k |λL_2,k|; (iii) L ≥ MK and O full column rank. The last condition is severe; in the real-data experiment the authors add polynomial features to force L > MK, which breaks the generative assumptions without analysis of its effect.
- Inconsistency between problem setting and algorithm inputs: the MoM HMM subroutine is given MK (total number of global states) as input, but the paper claims to estimate K from data. In experiments MK is always set to the true value. There is no demonstrated pipeline that works when MK is unknown.
- Empirical validation of K estimation and depermutation robustness is limited. The synthetic experiment uses the true K for k-means; there is no quantitative evaluation of K̂ accuracy, sensitivity to γ/range of exponents, or failure modes when eigenvalues are close.
- The HMM MoM step is treated as a black box. The paper does not specify which moments are used, the whitening/estimation procedure, regularization, or exact identifiability conditions, particularly for continuous observations. This hinders reproducibility and assessment.

Minor:
- Notational issues and typos (e.g., 1>J vs 1>M, “Ar̄”, inconsistent use of bars/permutes) detract from clarity.
- Computational considerations are underdeveloped: eigen-decomposition on MK×MK can be expensive; moments for continuous observations may require large memory; no discussion of scalability with K, M, L.
- Related work could be expanded: e.g., comparisons to variational/stochastic EM for MHMMs, Bayesian nonparametrics (HDP-HMM) for unknown K, and spectral multi-view methods for mixtures.

Quality
Moderate. The high-level idea is sound and the noiseless spectral characterization is correct, but the key noisy-case guarantees are conjectural, the conditions are strong, and important algorithmic details are missing. Empirical evidence is not sufficient to substantiate the claims about K estimation and robustness.

Clarity
Mixed. The core message is understandable, but the MoM subroutine, assumptions, and the proposed longevity measure need clearer, more formal presentation. Some notation issues remain.

Originality
Modest to moderate. The reduction to a block-diagonal HMM is known; the specific depermutation via global eigen-structure and the longevity heuristic are new but incremental.

Significance
Potentially useful for scalable sequence clustering and as an initialization for EM, but impact is limited by the lack of theoretical guarantees and incomplete empirical validation.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, intuitive approach to a practical problem; promising speed advantages.
- Potentially useful in practice as an EM initializer.
- Leverages and extends spectral HMM methods to a mixture setting.

Cons for acceptance
- Core result is conjectural; no rigorous analysis of recovery under noise.
- Requires knowing MK; K-estimation is heuristic and not convincingly validated.
- Strong assumptions (L ≥ MK, spectral gaps) and limited experiments.

Suggestions for improvement
- Replace Conjecture 1 with a rigorous perturbation analysis (e.g., via Davis–Kahan/Wedin) using explicit bounds on MoM estimation error and required eigengaps; clarify conditions under which global eigenvalues are separable.
- Provide a precise description of the MoM HMM step for continuous observations, including moments used, identifiability conditions, and regularization; discuss how to handle L < MK (e.g., random feature lifts with guarantees).
- Develop a principled model selection criterion for K and MK (e.g., eigengap statistics with hypothesis testing, stability across subsamples), and report K̂ accuracy across synthetic regimes.
- Expand experiments: ablations of depermutation, robustness to small spectral gaps, larger K and M, runtime scaling, and comparisons to strong baselines (variational/stochastic EM, spectral baselines).
- Improve clarity/notation and fix typos.

Recommendation
Reject in current form. With stronger theory, clearer exposition of assumptions and the MoM step, and more thorough experimental validation (especially of K estimation), this could become a solid contribution. Confidence: high for methodological/empirical assessment, medium for completeness of related work coverage.
