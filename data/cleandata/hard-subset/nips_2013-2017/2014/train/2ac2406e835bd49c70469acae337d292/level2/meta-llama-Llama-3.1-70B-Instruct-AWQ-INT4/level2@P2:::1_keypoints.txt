- Proposal: spectral learning for Mixture of HMMs (MHMM) using Method of Moments by viewing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Key method: use existing MoM to learn parameters up to permutation, then estimate a de-permutation mapping via spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Writing/clairety: paper is well-written, clear, and concise.  
- Technical soundness: technically solid with thorough analysis of spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Experimental results: demonstrated effectiveness vs EM on synthetic and real data; much cheaper computationally and comparable in accuracy.  
- Strength: novel solution to permutation ambiguity using spectral de-permutation.  
- Strength: strong experimental evaluation showing practical benefits over EM.  
- Weakness: relies on assumption that local transition matrices have only one eigenvalue of magnitude 1, which may not always hold and needs discussion.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees on algorithm convergence and robustness to noise; needs formal analysis.  
- Originality: approach is novel and impactful, though spectral methods for HMMs are not entirely new and should be better contextualized.  
- Significance: significant contribution—enables learning MHMMs without costly EM.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective method, strong experiments, significant ML contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive eigenvalue assumption, lack of theoretical guarantees, insufficient comparison/context with existing spectral methods.
