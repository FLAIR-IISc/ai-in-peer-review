- Proposes spectral learning for Mixture of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Leverages Method of Moments spectral algorithms for HMMs to learn MHMMs.  
- Identifies and addresses permutation ambiguity in MoM estimation that prevents recovering individual HMM parameters.  
- Introduces a novel procedure using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity, robust to estimation noise.  
- Provides a procedure to estimate the number of HMM clusters K under certain spectral conditions.  
- Theoretical analysis includes well-motivated lemmas and corollaries, establishing mathematical foundations.  
- Some theoretical claims remain conjectural (Conjecture 1) rather than rigorously proven, weakening the theory.  
- Algorithm 1 is clearly described and uses spectral clustering of the transition matrix to handle permutations.  
- Synthetic experiments show correct recovery of block-diagonal structure even with limited data.  
- Empirical comparison with EM demonstrates much faster runtime with comparable accuracy.  
- Real-data (handwriting) experiment shows the method is an effective initialization for EM, enhancing practical utility.  
- Real-data experiment relies on polynomial feature transformation to meet MK < L, which appears like a workaround requiring better justification.  
- Paper builds on and positions itself well relative to prior spectral HMM work, but lacks comparisons to other recent MHMM methods beyond EM.  
- Writing is generally clear, though some theoretical parts could use more intuitive explanation.  
- Overall: technically sound, practically useful, and recommended for acceptance.
