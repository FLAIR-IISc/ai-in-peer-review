- Introduces a novel spectral Method of Moments approach for MHMMs that resolves permutation ambiguity via spectral properties of the global transition matrix.
- Computationally cheaper than EM by avoiding iterative forward-backward computations, making it attractive for large-scale sequential data.
- Empirical results on synthetic data and handwritten character trajectories show effective recovery of clusters and competitive clustering accuracy.
- Serves as a useful initialization scheme for EM, improving practical performance when EM suffers from poor initialization.
- Provides a detailed, step-by-step algorithm description that is implementable by practitioners.
- Some key theoretical claims (e.g., Conjecture 1) lack rigorous proofs, weakening the theoretical foundation.
- Sensitivity to noise is not thoroughly analyzed; robustness and comparisons to EM under high-noise conditions are unclear.
- Real-world evaluation is limited to a single dataset; broader validation on diverse datasets is needed.
- Scalability to more complex hierarchical latent-variable models is not explored.
- Paper is dense in technical detail; sections on spectral properties and eigenvalue analysis could use clearer explanations or visual aids.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical gaps and add more real-world experiments.
- Overall: promising and computationally efficient method with practical value, but requires further theoretical and empirical refinement.
