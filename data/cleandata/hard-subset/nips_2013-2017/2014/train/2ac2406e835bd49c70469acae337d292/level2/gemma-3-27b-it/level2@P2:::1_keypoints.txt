- Proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) algorithm for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Leverages existing MoM techniques to learn global parameters up to permutation, then uses spectral properties of the transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity.  
- Validated on synthetic and real datasets, showing viability and potential as an EM initialization.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with a sound motivation (computational challenges of EM for large data).  
- The block-diagonal MHMM↔HMM connection is clearly explained and forms a solid methodological foundation.  
- Addressing permutation ambiguity is a significant and useful contribution.  
- Experimental results show the method works but do not significantly outperform EM.  
- Potential advantage: faster runtime and usefulness as an EM initializer in large-scale settings.  
- Theoretical justifications (spectral properties, permutation recovery conditions) are somewhat hand-wavy and need more rigor or proofs.  
- Conjecture 1 (error bound) seems optimistic and lacks justification, particularly regarding dependence on N and MK.  
- Discussion of the "low-rank eigendecomposition" is terse and needs more detailed explanation.  
- Experimental analysis is limited; a more thorough ablation study on noise sensitivity and parameter settings is needed.  
- Notation can be dense; more frequent symbol explanations would improve readability.  
- Figure captions are minimal and should provide more context.  
- The link between Lemma 3 and the practical algorithm for estimating K is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Section 3.2.2's argument flow should be improved to more intuitively explain how spectral properties resolve permutation ambiguity.  
- Originality: combining MoM and spectral methods for MHMM permutation recovery is novel, though MoM/spectral methods themselves are not new.  
- Literature review could be more comprehensive—some cited spectral learning work may be less directly relevant and further related work should be discussed.  
- Significance: promising computational alternative to EM with practical relevance, but empirical gains are currently modest.  
- Recommendation: acceptance with minor revisions focused on strengthening theoretical arguments, expanding experiments, and improving clarity/notation.
