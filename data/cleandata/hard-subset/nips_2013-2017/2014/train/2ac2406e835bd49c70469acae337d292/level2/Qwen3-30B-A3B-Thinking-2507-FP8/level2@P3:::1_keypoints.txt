- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) that addresses permutation ambiguity.

- Key insight: an MHMM can be treated as a single HMM with a block-diagonal global transition matrix, enabling spectral resolution of permutations.

- Shows MHMM can be expressed with specific global HMM parameters and develops a procedure to recover individual HMM parameters from permuted global parameters via spectral decomposition.

- Provides solid theoretical foundation with clear lemmas proving MHMM–HMM equivalence and the spectral properties needed for depermutation.

- States a conjectured error bound for estimated transition matrices, grounded in prior literature but lacking a full rigorous proof.

- Introduces “spectral longevity,” a novel metric using eigenvalue ratio convergence to estimate the number of clusters K.

- Synthetic experiments demonstrate recovery of block-diagonal structure and correct K identification even for modest sequence lengths (T=20).

- Empirical comparison with EM shows comparable accuracy while offering significant computational savings, especially on large datasets.

- Real-data experiments on handwritten trajectories indicate the spectral method is effective as an EM initialization, improving practical utility.

- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation, making theoretical results accessible.

- Related work is well-cited and contextualized, notably Anandkumar et al. for MoM and Smyth for MHMMs.

- Experimental setup and metrics are well-described, with appropriate visualizations supporting claims.

- Primary limitation: the method depends on specific spectral conditions for depermutation (|λGK| > maxk |λL2,k|), which may not always hold.

- Paper lacks sufficient analysis of when the spectral conditions fail and how to handle such failure modes.

- Does not explore performance when clusters have non-uniform numbers of states (varying M), a common practical scenario.

- The conjectured error bounds need stronger theoretical justification or further empirical validation.

- Significance: delivers a computationally efficient, theoretically grounded alternative to EM for MHMMs with potential to extend to other hierarchical latent-variable models.

- Distinct contribution from prior spectral HMM work by addressing MHMM permutation ambiguity and advancing MHMM learning methods.
