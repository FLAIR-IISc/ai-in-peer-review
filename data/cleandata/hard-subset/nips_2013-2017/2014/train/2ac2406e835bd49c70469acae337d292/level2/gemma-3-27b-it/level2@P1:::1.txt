This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based learning algorithm for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key idea is to leverage the fact that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors address the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation by exploiting the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, and also provide a method to recover the number of HMMs. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation and technical details.

**Summary of Related Work:** The paper correctly positions itself within the growing body of work on MoM for latent variable models [1, 2, 3] and specifically addresses the challenge of extending these methods to more complex models like MHMMs [4]. The cited applications of MHMMs [5, 6, 7] provide useful context. The reference to spectral learning for HMMs [3] is crucial as the proposed approach builds upon it. The inclusion of [8] related to spectral experts is a nice connection.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The application of MoM to MHMMs and the clever use of spectral properties to resolve the permutation ambiguity is a novel contribution. Addressing this ambiguity is a significant hurdle for applying MoM to MHMMs, and the proposed solution is insightful.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The authors correctly highlight the potential for computational advantages offered by MoM compared to EM, especially for large datasets. The paper convincingly argues that the learning time is independent of the data size after the moments are computed.
*   **Clear Explanation:** The paper is very well-written and the mathematical derivations are clear and easy to follow. The use of matrix notation is helpful and the connection between MHMM and HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix is well explained.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the validity of the approach. The comparisons with EM (both random and MoM initialized) are informative. The experiments show that the proposed method is at least competitive with EM in terms of accuracy while being significantly faster.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The conjecture about the spectral longevity and conditions for accurately estimating K are interesting and provide some theoretical grounding for the algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Conjecture Status:** The central theoretical claim (Conjecture 1) remains unproven. While the justification is provided, a formal proof would significantly strengthen the paper. The reliance on the conjecture limits the confidence in the applicability of the algorithm in very noisy settings.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the feasibility of the approach, they are limited in scope. It would be beneficial to evaluate the performance on a wider range of datasets and with more complex MHMM configurations (e.g., varying the number of states, different observation models).
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm to parameter choices (e.g., the choice of 'e' in the spectral analysis). Investigating the robustness of the algorithm to these parameters would be valuable.
*   **Real Data Preprocessing:** The use of a cubic polynomial feature transformation on the real data seems somewhat ad-hoc. A more detailed explanation of why this transformation is necessary and how it affects the results would be helpful.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The derivations appear correct, and the experimental results support the claims made by the authors. It’s a complete piece of work, though the unproven conjecture leaves some room for caution. The authors are careful about outlining the assumptions and limitations of their approach.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are easy to follow. The figures are helpful in visualizing the concepts.

**Originality:** The approach is original in its application of spectral learning to overcome the permutation ambiguity in MoM-based MHMM estimation. The combination of these techniques is novel and adds to the existing literature.

**Significance:** The results are significant because they offer a potentially more efficient alternative to EM for learning MHMMs, particularly for large datasets. This could have practical applications in various fields where MHMMs are used, such as speech recognition, bioinformatics, and financial modeling.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Novel and insightful approach to learning MHMMs.
*   Clear and well-written paper.
*   Promising experimental results demonstrating competitive performance and computational efficiency.
*   Addresses a significant challenge in applying MoM to complex latent variable models.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The central theoretical claim is currently a conjecture.
*   Limited scope of experiments.
*   Lack of analysis of parameter sensitivity.




**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper presents a novel and promising approach to learning MHMMs. The clarity of the presentation and the encouraging experimental results outweigh the weaknesses. The authors should be encouraged to work towards a formal proof of Conjecture 1 and to expand the experimental evaluation. Addressing the parameter sensitivity would also strengthen the paper.
