**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel learning approach for the Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors show that an MHMM can be expressed as a single Hidden Markov Model (HMM) with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They then use an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters of the HMM, which are then used to recover the parameters of the individual HMMs in the mixture. The authors also propose a method to estimate the number of HMMs in the mixture using spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The approach is evaluated on synthetic and real data, showing that it is computationally more efficient than the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm while achieving comparable accuracy.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new learning approach for MHMMs, which is based on the Method of Moments. This approach is different from traditional EM-based methods and has the potential to be more efficient.
2. **Theoretical foundations**: The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their approach, including lemmas and corollaries that establish the relationship between the MHMM and the HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes a thorough experimental evaluation of the proposed approach, including comparisons with EM and other methods on synthetic and real data.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The approach relies on certain assumptions, such as the existence of a block-diagonal transition matrix, which may not always hold in practice.
2. **Noise sensitivity**: The authors acknowledge that the approach is sensitive to noise in the data, which can affect the accuracy of the estimated parameters.
3. **Limited scope**: The paper focuses on MHMMs with a specific structure, which may not be representative of all possible MHMMs.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel learning approach for MHMMs, which is based on the Method of Moments. While the idea of using MoM for learning latent variable models is not new, the application to MHMMs is original and has the potential to be more efficient than traditional EM-based methods.
