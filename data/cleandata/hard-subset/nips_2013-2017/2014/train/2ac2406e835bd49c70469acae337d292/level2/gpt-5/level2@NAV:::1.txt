This paper proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments (MoM) approach to learn Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key idea is to recast an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and then use existing spectral HMM estimators to recover parameters up to a global permutation. The central claim is that the permutation ambiguity can be resolved by exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix: reconstruct a low-rank (rank-K) approximation, cluster its columns to recover blocks, and thereby de-permute to individual HMMs. A secondary claim is a heuristic for estimating K via “spectral longevity.” The work builds on spectral learning for HMMs and latent-variable models (Hsu et al.; Anandkumar et al.) and relates to earlier sequence clustering with HMMs (Smyth, NIPS).

Quality: The formulation and reduction to a block-diagonal HMM are sound, and the de-permutation intuition via eigenstructure is persuasive. However, the theoretical support is incomplete: Conjecture 1 (sample complexity/perturbation) is unproven, and the K-identification criterion relies on heuristic conditions and empirical longevity rather than standard perturbation tools (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin bounds). Empirically, synthetic studies illustrate de-permutation vs. sequence length and a speed–accuracy comparison against EM; a small real-data test shows the method as a useful EM initializer. Runtime and scalability claims would benefit from explicit complexity and wall-clock comparisons.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear and well organized; the block-diagonal equivalence and algorithm are easy to follow. Some notation is dense; references are incomplete/uniformly formatted. The requirement MK < L and the use of polynomial features to satisfy it deserve clearer discussion of modeling consequences.

Originality: Viewing MHMM learning as spectral HMM plus de-permutation and proposing a practical spectral criterion for K are novel within this setting. Related work is cited but could be broadened to spectral mixture identification and graph partitioning/ block-structure recovery.

Significance: If robust, the approach offers a fast, initialization-free alternative to EM for large sequence corpora and a strong initializer for EM. The dependence on eigengaps and MK < L may limit applicability, but the idea is promising.

Pros:
- Simple, computationally attractive pipeline with clear intuition
- New angle on resolving permutation for MHMMs
- Competitive accuracy with substantial speedups; useful EM initialization

Cons:
- Key guarantees are conjectural; K selection is heuristic
- Strong assumptions (ergodic chains; MK < L); feature lifting may mis-specify emissions
- Limited real-data evaluation and ablations; incomplete references

Suggestions:
- Replace Conjecture 1 with perturbation analysis (e.g., Davis–Kahan) and quantify conditions on eigengaps and sample size
- Compare K-selection to standard spectral gap heuristics and information criteria
- Provide wall-clock scaling, sensitivity to noise/eigengaps, and robustness to approximate block-diagonality
- Explore alternative de-permutation via graph partitioning on |A|^e or assignment optimizing block diagonality
- Discuss practicality when L < MK and alternatives to feature lifting (e.g., discrete/count emissions, tensor methods tailored to MHMMs)
