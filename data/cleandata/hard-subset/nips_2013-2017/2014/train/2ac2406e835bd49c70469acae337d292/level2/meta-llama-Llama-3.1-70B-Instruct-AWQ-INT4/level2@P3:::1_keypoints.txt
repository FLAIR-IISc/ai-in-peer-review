- Proposes a Method-of-Moments (MoM) approach for Mixtures of HMMs by representing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and recovering mixture components from the learned HMM; also estimates number of HMMs via spectral properties and reports faster computation than EM with comparable accuracy.

- Novel approach: introduces a non-EM MoM-based learning method for MHMMs that could be more efficient.

- Theoretical foundations: provides lemmas and corollaries linking MHMMs to HMMs with block-diagonal transitions.

- Experimental evaluation: includes synthetic and real-data experiments comparing the method to EM and other baselines.

- Assumptions: method depends on structural assumptions (existence of block-diagonal transition) that may not hold in practice.

- Noise sensitivity: the approach is sensitive to data noise, affecting parameter estimation accuracy.

- Limited scope: focuses on MHMMs with a specific structure, potentially not covering all MHMM variants.

- Originality: applying MoM to MHMMs is a novel contribution despite MoM being known for other latent models.

- Significance: could impact sequence clustering and latent-variable learning in domains like speech, NLP, and vision.

- Arguments for acceptance: introduces a novel MoM-based method, provides solid theoretical backing, and shows empirical competitiveness and computational gains.

- Arguments against acceptance: relies on potentially strong assumptions, exhibits noise sensitivity, and targets a restricted MHMM subclass.

- Suggestion — robustness: include a more detailed robustness analysis to noise and estimation errors.

- Suggestion — extensions: explore extending the approach to other latent models (e.g., Bayesian networks or deep latent models).

- Suggestion — comparisons: add comparisons to alternative inference methods such as variational inference and MCMC.
