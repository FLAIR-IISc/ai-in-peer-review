

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing the computational limitations of the traditional EM algorithm. The key insight is viewing the MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling application of existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs. The authors then tackle the permutation ambiguity issue by leveraging spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters. The paper demonstrates the approach on synthetic and real data, showing computational advantages over EM while maintaining comparable accuracy.

The paper makes a technically interesting contribution by extending spectral learning methods to MHMMs. The core idea of viewing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure (Lemma 1) is well-motivated and correctly established. The proposed algorithm (Algorithm 1) for resolving permutation ambiguity through spectral analysis is a valuable addition to the literature on latent variable models.

Strengths:
1. The paper clearly establishes the theoretical foundation for viewing MHMM as a special case of HMM, which is the key insight enabling the proposed approach.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the computational advantages of the spectral approach over EM, with Figure 4 showing significant speed improvements without substantial accuracy loss.
3. The real data experiment (Section 4.3) provides practical validation by showing the method can serve as an effective initialization for EM.
4. The paper properly cites relevant prior work on spectral learning for HMMs [1,2,3] and MHMMs [4].

Weaknesses:
1. The theoretical analysis contains significant gaps. Conjecture 1, which is central to the noise analysis, lacks a proper proof. The justification provided is insufficient, as it simply reuses a bound from [1] without adapting it to the MHMM context. The authors should provide a rigorous analysis of the estimation error for their proposed approach.
2. The paper does not clearly establish how their method differs from or improves upon existing MHMM learning approaches. While they cite [4] as prior work on MHMMs, they don't discuss existing spectral learning methods for MHMMs or explain why their approach is superior to those.
3. The spectral longevity approach for determining the number of clusters (Lemma 3) is not thoroughly justified. The condition |λGK| > maxk |λL2,k| is not explained in terms of practical constraints on the data or model parameters.
4. The real data experiment has limitations: the authors transform the data to satisfy MK < L, which is a significant constraint not discussed in terms of how it affects the general applicability of the method.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by:
1. Providing a rigorous proof of Conjecture 1 or clearly stating its limitations.
2. Including a more thorough comparison with existing MHMM learning methods.
3. Discussing the practical implications of the spectral condition |λGK| > maxk |λL2,k| for real-world applications.
4. Addressing the data transformation limitation in the real data experiment.

The paper's main contribution is valuable for the machine learning community as it offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM for MHMM learning. The approach has clear practical utility as demonstrated in the real data experiment. However, the theoretical gaps and insufficient comparison with prior work limit the paper's scientific contribution.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it accessible to the NIPS audience. The experimental results are appropriate and support the main claims, though the theoretical foundation requires strengthening.

Overall, this paper presents an interesting and potentially useful approach to MHMM learning. While the core idea is promising, the theoretical analysis needs significant strengthening for the paper to make a strong contribution to the field. The authors should address the gaps in their theoretical analysis and provide a more thorough comparison with existing methods before the paper can be considered for publication.
