- Proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) algorithm for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Resolves permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates by exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Provides a method to recover the number of component HMMs (K).  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and technical exposition.  
- Correctly positions work within MoM and spectral learning literature and cites relevant MHMM applications.  
- Novelty: first application of spectral/MoM techniques to address permutation ambiguity in MHMM estimation.  
- Computational efficiency: learning time is effectively independent of dataset size after moments are computed, offering advantages over EM.  
- Clear mathematical derivations and helpful matrix notation linking MHMMs to block-diagonal HMMs.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real data shows competitive accuracy with EM and substantially faster runtime.  
- Theoretical justification: conjecture on spectral longevity and conditions for estimating K provides some grounding.  
- Primary weakness: central theoretical claim (Conjecture 1) is unproven, reducing confidence in noisy settings.  
- Limited experiments: evaluation scope is narrow; more datasets and varied MHMM configurations are needed.  
- Parameter sensitivity not analyzed (e.g., choice of spectral parameter 'e'), leaving robustness unclear.  
- Real-data preprocessing (cubic polynomial features) appears ad hoc and lacks justification.  
- Overall quality: technically sound derivations and supportive experiments, but reliance on an unproven conjecture warrants caution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — encourage formal proof of the conjecture, expanded experiments, and analysis of parameter sensitivity.
