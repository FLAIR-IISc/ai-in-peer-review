This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based learning algorithm for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. Existing MoM approaches can learn the global parameters up to a permutation ambiguity, which is then resolved using spectral properties of the transition matrix to recover the individual HMM parameters. The authors demonstrate the validity of their approach on both synthetic and real data.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The motivation for exploring MoM for MHMMs is sound, given the computational challenges of EM, particularly for large datasets. The connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is clearly explained, forming a solid foundation for their approach. The authors acknowledge and attempt to address the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM, which is a significant contribution. The experimental results, while not overwhelmingly superior to EM, demonstrate the viability of the proposed method and highlight its potential as an initialization scheme for EM. 

However, several areas could benefit from further development.

**Quality:** The theoretical justifications, particularly around the spectral properties and the conditions for recovering the permutation, feel somewhat hand-wavy. While conjectures are presented, more rigorous analysis or proofs would significantly strengthen the paper. For example, Conjecture 1 regarding the error bound feels optimistic without further justification, particularly concerning the dependence on the number of sequences (N) and the total number of states (MK). The discussion around the "low-rank eigendecomposition" is also a bit terse and could benefit from a more detailed explanation. While the experimental results show the approach works, the analysis feels somewhat limited.  A more thorough ablation study exploring the sensitivity of the algorithm to noise levels and parameter settings would be valuable.

**Clarity:** The notation, while generally consistent, can be dense. More frequent explanations of symbols, especially in the more mathematically involved sections (3.2.1 & 3.2.2), would enhance readability. The figure captions are minimal and could provide more context. The connection between Lemma 3 and the practical algorithm for estimating K could also be made clearer. The flow of the argument in Section 3.2.2 could be improved to more intuitively explain how the spectral properties are leveraged to resolve the permutation ambiguity.

**Originality:** The use of MoM for latent variable models is not entirely novel (citations [1, 2, 3] are relevant), nor is the application of spectral methods to HMM learning (citation [3]). However, combining these techniques to tackle the specific challenge of MHMM learning, particularly resolving the permutation ambiguity, represents a novel contribution. The authors appropriately acknowledge related work, although the citation of [3] for spectral learning of HMMs seems less directly relevant to the core method. A more comprehensive review of literature on spectral methods in the context of mixture models might reveal further connections and opportunities for comparison.

**Significance:** The potential significance of this work lies in its ability to provide a computationally efficient alternative to EM for learning MHMMs, especially in large-scale settings. While the experimental results don't demonstrate a dramatic performance improvement over EM, the faster runtime and potential for good initialization are valuable. The problem of permutation ambiguity in MoM is a fundamental one, and the authors' approach provides a promising solution. Further research and optimization could potentially unlock the full benefits of this approach. The application to real data, though limited, demonstrates the practical relevance of the method.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to a challenging problem (MHMM learning with MoM).
*   Addresses the key issue of permutation ambiguity in MoM.
*   Demonstrates viability on both synthetic and real data.
*   Potential for computational efficiency compared to EM.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Theoretical justifications are somewhat weak and require further rigor.
*   Experimental results are not overwhelmingly convincing.
*   Limited ablation study and sensitivity analysis.
*   Clarity could be improved in certain sections.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field. While the theoretical aspects could be strengthened and the experimental evaluation expanded, the core idea is novel and potentially impactful. I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically focusing on providing more rigorous justification for the spectral properties and conducting a more thorough experimental evaluation. The authors should address the concerns raised about clarity and notation.
