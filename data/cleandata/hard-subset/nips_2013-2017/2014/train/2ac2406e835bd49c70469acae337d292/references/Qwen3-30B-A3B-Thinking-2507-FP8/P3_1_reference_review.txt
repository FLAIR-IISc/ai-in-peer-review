

# Peer Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing a key computational bottleneck in the traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) approach. The central insight is that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs but must overcome the permutation ambiguity that arises from the MoM estimation process. They propose a novel depermutation algorithm that exploits the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover the parameters of individual HMMs. The paper also introduces a spectral longevity metric to estimate the number of clusters K under certain spectral conditions.

The work builds on Anandkumar et al.'s spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2, 3] and extends it to the more complex MHMM setting. The approach is theoretically grounded in linear algebra and spectral properties, with experimental validation on synthetic data and a real-world handwritten character trajectory dataset. This represents an important step toward more scalable learning algorithms for sequence clustering.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Insight and Novelty**: The key insight that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is elegant and well-motivated. This perspective enables the application of existing spectral learning techniques to MHMMs, which is novel and addresses a significant gap in the literature.

2. **Computational Advantage**: The paper clearly demonstrates that the proposed MoM approach is computationally more efficient than EM, especially for large datasets. The experiments (Section 4.2) show the spectral method is significantly faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy, which is a major practical contribution.

3. **Practical Utility**: The real data experiment (Section 4.3) demonstrates the algorithm's utility as an initialization scheme for EM, which is particularly valuable for practitioners. The results show that spectral learning can provide better initializations than random initialization.

4. **Well-Structured Experiments**: The synthetic experiments are well-designed to test the algorithm's behavior under different conditions (noise, data quantity), providing strong empirical validation of the theoretical claims.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Incompleteness**: The most significant weakness is the reliance on unproven conjectures (Conjecture 1) without rigorous justification. The justification provided is hand-wavy, reusing results from other papers without adapting them to this specific context. The spectral longevity metric (Definition 3) is introduced without sufficient theoretical grounding or analysis of its properties.

2. **Limited Theoretical Analysis**: Lemma 3, which claims that the spectral longevity metric identifies K correctly under certain conditions, lacks thorough analysis. The paper doesn't provide conditions under which the spectral gap condition |λGK| > maxk∈{1,...,K}|λL2,k| is satisfied in practice, nor does it discuss the robustness of the approach when this condition is violated.

3. **Real Data Experiment Limitation**: The real data experiment transforms the input data with a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy the MK < L constraint required for MoM learning. This transformation is not adequately justified, and it's unclear how it affects the validity of the comparison with EM. The paper should have addressed this limitation more thoroughly.

4. **Comparison with Recent Alternatives**: The paper doesn't compare to recent alternatives for MHMM learning, such as variational methods or other spectral approaches. This makes it difficult to assess the relative advantages of the proposed method.

5. **Clarity of Key Concepts**: The spectral longevity metric (Definition 3) is introduced without sufficient intuition or explanation. A more detailed explanation of how this metric works and why it's effective would improve the paper's clarity.

## Quality Assessment

The paper is technically sound in its approach and presents well-designed experiments. However, the theoretical foundation is incomplete due to the reliance on unproven conjectures. The experimental results support the main claims but don't fully address the limitations of the approach. The paper is careful to acknowledge the permutation ambiguity problem but doesn't sufficiently address its practical implications or the conditions under which the depermutation algorithm will fail.

## Clarity Assessment

The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear mathematical notation. However, the explanation of the spectral longevity metric and the depermutation algorithm would benefit from more intuitive explanations. The connection between the theoretical results and the practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) could be made clearer. The paper would be more accessible to readers if it included a more detailed explanation of why the spectral properties of the global transition matrix can be used to recover the individual HMMs.

## Originality Assessment

The core insight of viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is novel and represents a significant contribution to the literature. The depermutation technique using spectral properties is a creative solution to the permutation ambiguity problem. However, the paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate itself from recent spectral learning approaches for latent variable models. It would be helpful to more explicitly contrast the proposed method with techniques like tensor decomposition approaches for mixture models [2, 3].

## Significance Assessment

The significance of this work lies in addressing the computational bottleneck of MHMM learning, which is crucial for large-scale sequence clustering applications. The paper demonstrates that the proposed method is substantially faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. The finding that the method can serve as a good initialization for EM is particularly valuable for practitioners. The extension to other hierarchical latent variable models mentioned in the conclusion is also promising.

## Specific Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Strengthen the Theoretical Analysis**: Provide a rigorous proof of Conjecture 1 or at least a more detailed justification. The paper should analyze the conditions under which the spectral gap condition |λGK| > maxk∈{1,...,K}|λL2,k| holds in practice.

2. **Clarify the Spectral Longevity Metric**: Provide more intuitive explanation and theoretical justification for the spectral longevity metric (Definition 3). Consider including a small example that demonstrates how it works.

3. **Address the Real Data Limitation**: Explain why the cubic polynomial feature transformation was necessary and discuss how it might affect the results. Consider including an experiment with the original data dimensions (if possible) or a discussion of the limitations this transformation introduces.

4. **Expand Related Work**: Compare the proposed method more thoroughly with recent alternatives for MHMM learning, such as variational approaches or other spectral techniques.

5. **Include More Failure Cases**: Present experiments where the spectral conditions aren't met to demonstrate the limitations of the approach.

## Final Recommendation

This paper presents an interesting and potentially useful approach to MHMM learning with clear computational advantages over EM. The core insight is strong, and the experimental results support the main claims. However, the theoretical foundation is incomplete due to the reliance on unproven conjectures, which is a significant weakness for a NeurIPS submission. The paper would be significantly stronger with more rigorous theoretical analysis.

**Recommendation: Weak Accept**

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of latent variable models, particularly for sequence clustering applications. With the theoretical weaknesses addressed, this could be a strong contribution to NeurIPS. As it stands, it's a good paper that would benefit from more thorough theoretical analysis before acceptance.

**Justification for Recommendation:**

- The core insight is novel and well-motivated.
- The computational advantages over EM are clearly demonstrated.
- The real-world application shows practical utility.
- The main weakness is the theoretical incompleteness, which is significant for a NeurIPS paper that should have strong theoretical foundations.
- With the suggested improvements, this paper would be a strong candidate for acceptance.

I would encourage the authors to address the theoretical weaknesses before resubmission. The proposed method has significant potential, and with stronger theoretical grounding, it could make an important contribution to the field.
