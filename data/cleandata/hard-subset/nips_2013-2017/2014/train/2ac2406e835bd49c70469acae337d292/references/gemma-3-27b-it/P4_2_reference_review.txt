This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, applying existing MoM techniques for HMMs, and then using spectral properties to “de-permute” the parameters to recover individual HMMs. The paper includes theoretical justifications, conjectures, and empirical validation on synthetic and real data.

**Quality:** The paper is generally technically sound. The derivation showing the equivalence between MHMM and a block-diagonal HMM is clear and correct. The use of spectral properties to address the permutation ambiguity is a clever idea. However, several aspects lack sufficient rigor. The conjectures regarding the spectral properties, particularly concerning the separation of eigenvalues in the presence of noise, feel hand-wavy and require more detailed analysis or proof sketches. The experimental setup, while including comparisons to EM, doesn’t provide enough detail to fully assess the robustness of the method; more detailed ablation studies regarding noise levels and dataset size are needed. The reliance on existing MoM algorithms for HMMs [1, 2] shifts some of the technical burden to those works, but the authors could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of their limitations and how they affect the overall MHMM learning process. The choice of a cubic polynomial feature transformation in the real data experiment feels somewhat arbitrary and could be better justified.

**Clarity:** The paper is mostly well-written and organized, with clear definitions of the model and the proposed algorithm. However, some sections, particularly those dealing with spectral properties (3.2.1 and 3.2.2) could be more accessible. The notation is consistent, but a glossary of symbols might be helpful. While the algorithm is described clearly, a pseudocode representation could further enhance readability. The flow between lemmas, conjectures, and algorithm steps sometimes feels disjointed, making it difficult to follow the overall argument. Figures 1 and 2 are helpful for visualizing the concepts, but Figure 3 lacks clear labels.

**Originality:** The combination of MoM with spectral methods for MHMM learning is novel. While MoM has been applied to HMMs [1, 2, 3], extending it to the mixture setting and addressing the permutation issue with spectral analysis represents a meaningful contribution. The paper builds upon existing work but presents a unique approach to a challenging problem. However, the novelty is somewhat tempered by the reliance on existing MoM algorithms; the core innovation lies in the de-permutation step. The application of spectral properties feels somewhat heuristic, and exploring connections to related work in spectral clustering or matrix perturbation theory might further strengthen the originality.

**Significance:** The potential significance of this work lies in providing a computationally efficient alternative to EM for learning MHMMs, particularly for large datasets. The reported speed-up in experiments is encouraging. The ability to leverage spectral properties to address the permutation ambiguity is a valuable insight. If the theoretical conjectures can be rigorously established and the method proves robust across a wider range of datasets, it could have a considerable impact on applications such as speech recognition, bioinformatics, and time series analysis. However, the current experiments are not conclusive enough to definitively demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over carefully tuned EM, especially regarding clustering accuracy. The limited scope of the real-world experiment also restricts the assessment of practical significance.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of MoM and spectral methods for MHMM learning.
*   Addresses a key challenge in MHMM learning – the permutation ambiguity.
*   Demonstrates potential for computational efficiency compared to EM.
*   Clear theoretical justification for the core ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Theoretical conjectures lack rigorous proof or detailed analysis.
*   Experimental validation is limited in scope and detail.
*   The significance of the method is not fully established due to the limited comparisons with optimized EM.
*   Some sections are difficult to follow due to a lack of clarity.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a novel and potentially significant approach to learning MHMMs. The technical ideas are sound, and the preliminary experimental results are promising. However, the lack of rigorous theoretical analysis and the limited scope of the experiments raise concerns. I recommend **weak accept** with the caveat that the authors address the reviewer’s concerns regarding the theoretical justifications and experimental validation in a revised version. Specifically, more detailed analysis of the spectral properties, including a discussion of robustness to noise, and more comprehensive experiments with a wider range of datasets and hyperparameter settings are crucial. The authors should also provide a clearer discussion of the limitations of the method and potential future research directions.
