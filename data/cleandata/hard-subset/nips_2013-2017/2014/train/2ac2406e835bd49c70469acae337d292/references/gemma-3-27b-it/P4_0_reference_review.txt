This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, applying an existing MoM algorithm for HMMs and then using spectral properties to “de-permute” the parameters to recover individual HMMs. The paper provides theoretical justification and empirical validation on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Quality:** The paper is generally technically sound. The derivation of the equivalence between MHMM and the specialized HMM is clear and correct. The core idea of using spectral properties to address the permutation ambiguity is novel and potentially valuable, especially for large datasets where EM becomes computationally expensive. However, the theoretical analysis, particularly surrounding Conjecture 1 and Lemmas 2 & 3, relies on assumptions (e.g., noise levels, eigenvalue gaps) that are not fully justified or thoroughly investigated. The experiments provide evidence supporting the approach, but lack detailed ablation studies to isolate the impact of different components of the algorithm. The convergence analysis appears somewhat hand-wavy and relies on citing results from related work without detailed adaptation to the MHMM setting. The claim of computational advantage is reasonable given the MoM framework, but a more rigorous comparison, including memory usage, would strengthen the argument.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is generally clear, and the model definitions are presented concisely. However, the connection between the theoretical results and the practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) could be made more explicit. The explanation of the "spectral longevity" concept (Definition 3) is somewhat convoluted and requires careful reading. While the authors acknowledge the complexity, improving the presentation could significantly enhance understanding. The figures are helpful but could benefit from clearer labeling and more detailed explanations within the captions.

**Originality:** The application of MoM to MHMMs is novel, as is the use of spectral decomposition to address the permutation issue. While MoM has been applied to HMMs previously [3], extending it to the mixture setting and tackling the permutation problem using spectral techniques constitutes a significant contribution. The paper clearly positions itself within the existing literature and highlights the differences from prior work. However, the spectral techniques employed are well-established, so the novelty lies in the specific application and combination with the MoM framework.

**Significance:** The proposed method has the potential to address a significant challenge in MHMM learning, particularly for large datasets. EM, the standard algorithm, can be computationally prohibitive. A faster, MoM-based alternative, especially with demonstrated good performance, is valuable. The application to a real-world dataset (handwritten character trajectories) provides some practical relevance. However, the performance gains over EM are not overwhelmingly significant, and the dependence on specific assumptions (spectral conditions) may limit its widespread applicability. The potential for using the MoM solution as an initialization scheme for EM is a pragmatic and potentially useful outcome.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel application of MoM to MHMMs, addressing a known computational bottleneck.
*   Clever use of spectral properties to overcome the permutation ambiguity.
*   Theoretical justification, although with some caveats.
*   Empirical validation on both synthetic and real datasets.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Theoretical analysis relies on potentially strong and unverified assumptions.
*   Experimental evaluation lacks comprehensive ablation studies.
*   Performance gains over EM are not dramatic.
*   The “spectral longevity” concept is somewhat unclear and needs better explanation.
*   Limited discussion of memory usage and scalability beyond runtime comparison.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to learning MHMMs. While the theoretical analysis has some limitations and the experimental results are not groundbreaking, the potential for significant computational savings and the clever use of spectral techniques make this a worthwhile contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the clarity issues surrounding the spectral longevity concept and to provide more detailed experimental analysis. Specifically, the authors should consider adding an ablation study, investigating the sensitivity of the algorithm to noise, and providing a more thorough discussion of the assumptions underlying the theoretical results.
