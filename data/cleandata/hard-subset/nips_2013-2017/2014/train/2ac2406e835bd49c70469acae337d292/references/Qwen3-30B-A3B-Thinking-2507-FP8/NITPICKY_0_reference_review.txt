

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by viewing the MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage spectral learning for HMMs to estimate global parameters up to permutation ambiguity, then propose a depermutation method based on spectral properties to recover individual HMM parameters. While the problem is important and the approach potentially valuable, the paper contains significant technical flaws, inconsistent notation, and insufficient theoretical justification that undermine its contribution.

## Technical Flaws

The paper contains several critical technical errors. In Lemma 1, the proof incorrectly states that "the first latent state r1 determines what region the entire latent state sequence lies in." This misrepresents MHMM structure - the cluster assignment hn is fixed for the entire sequence, but the initial state r1 is drawn from νk for the cluster k that hn = k. The correct interpretation is that the cluster assignment determines the initial state distribution, not vice versa.

The matrix notation in Lemma 2 is incorrect. The authors write "Ā = V1Λ1V −1 1 . . . 0 0 . . . 0 0 VKΛKV −1 K" which is not standard block-diagonal matrix notation. The correct representation should be a block-diagonal matrix with each block being VkΛkV −1 k.

Conjecture 1 claims "‖Ar − Ar‖F ≤ O(1/ √ TN)" with a justification that is hand-wavy and contains mathematical errors. The justification incorrectly uses "A" to denote both the true transition matrix and its estimate, and the bound derivation is invalid. The authors cite [1] for sample complexity, but the cited paper concerns mixture models, not HMMs, making the bound potentially inapplicable.

The spectral longevity definition (Definition 3) is unnecessarily complex. The authors could have used a simpler measure like the number of eigenvalues above a threshold to estimate K. The justification for Lemma 3 is also weak, as it assumes the eigenvalue ratios determine the spectral longevity without rigorous proof.

## Formatting and Notation Issues

The paper suffers from inconsistent notation throughout. The authors switch between "1>M", "1M", and "1_M" without explanation. The term "depermutation" is misspelled multiple times as "depermutation" or "de-permutation" (e.g., "depermutation mapping" vs. "de-permutation mapping"). The notation "MK" for the total number of states is confusing, as M and K are already defined as the number of states per HMM and number of HMMs, respectively. The authors should use a different symbol like M_total.

In section 2.1, the emission matrix is defined as "O(:, u) = E[xt|rt = u]" which is incorrect. The emission matrix O should parameterize the observation distribution (e.g., O(:, u) = µu for Gaussian), not be equal to the expected value itself.

## Experimental Limitations

The experiments lack sufficient comparison to relevant baselines. The authors compare only to EM, but don't include other MoM-based methods for MHMMs. The real data experiment uses a polynomial feature transformation to increase dimensionality (L=10), but doesn't justify why this transformation is appropriate or how it affects the model's interpretability.

The claim "we require that MK < L" for MoM learning is misleading. Spectral learning for HMMs typically requires the number of states to be less than the observation dimension, but this is not a strict requirement for all cases. The paper doesn't explain why this condition is necessary for their approach.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths**: The core insight of viewing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure is reasonable. The paper identifies a practical computational advantage over EM for large datasets, which is valuable.

**Weaknesses**: The technical errors, inconsistent notation, and lack of rigorous theoretical justification significantly undermine the paper. The proposed depermutation method lacks theoretical guarantees, and the spectral longevity approach is heuristic without proper analysis.

## Recommendation

While the problem addressed is important and the core idea has potential, the paper's technical flaws and insufficient theoretical justification make it difficult to evaluate the contribution's validity. The numerous notation inconsistencies and mathematical errors make the paper hard to follow and undermine confidence in the results. The experimental section lacks sufficient comparisons and details for reproducibility.

The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to correct the mathematical errors, clarify the notation, provide rigorous theoretical justification for the depermutation step, and include more comprehensive experimental comparisons. As written, the paper does not meet the quality standards expected for NIPS.

I recommend rejection due to the substantial technical flaws that compromise the paper's validity. The authors should address these issues before resubmission.
