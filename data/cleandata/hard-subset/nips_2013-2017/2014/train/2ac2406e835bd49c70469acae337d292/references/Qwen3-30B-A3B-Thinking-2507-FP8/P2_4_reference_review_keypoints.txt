- Summary: Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by modelling MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions and resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.

- Theoretical contribution: Establishes MHMM–HMM equivalence (Lemma 1) and a principled depermutation method (Lemmas/Corollary) and introduces "spectral longevity" to estimate number of components K.

- Computational efficiency: Demonstrates the spectral method is much faster than EM on large datasets while achieving comparable accuracy, avoiding repeated forward-backward passes.

- Experimental validation: Experiments on synthetic and real handwritten trajectory data show practical utility and that spectral outputs can initialize EM; polynomial feature transforms used to meet MK < L.

- Theoretical gaps: Conjecture 1 lacks rigorous proof and a formal estimation error bound; sample-complexity for the depermutation step is not fully addressed.

- Limited comparison to prior work: Paper cites earlier sequence-clustering work (Smyth 2000) but lacks explicit comparisons to existing MHMM learning methods and recent spectral approaches.

- Practical limitation: Requirement MK < L is restrictive and the polynomial feature workaround may not be applicable in many settings, limiting generality.

- Clarity issue: The spectral longevity definition and its link to eigenvalue structure need more intuitive explanation and a simple illustrative example.

- Overall assessment: Novel, well-motivated, and practically useful spectral approach to MHMMs that addresses a real computational bottleneck despite some theoretical and practical gaps.

- Recommendation: Accept — contribution is significant, experiments support claims, and theoretical gaps are important but not fatal for publication.
