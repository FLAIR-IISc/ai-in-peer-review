## Peer Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using spectral learning and the Method of Moments (MoM). It addresses a key limitation of traditional EM-based MHMM learning – its computational cost – by leveraging the spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The paper is generally well-written and the core idea is interesting and potentially impactful. However, some aspects of the theoretical justification and experimental validation need further clarification and strengthening. I lean towards **Accept with Minor Revisions**.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The authors correctly identify the computational bottleneck of EM for MHMMs, particularly with large datasets. They build upon recent advances in MoM-based learning for latent variable models, specifically extending these methods to the more complex MHMM setting. The key insight is recognizing an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling the application of existing spectral learning techniques. This connection and the subsequent strategy of “de-permuting” the learned parameters to recover individual HMMs is a novel contribution. The paper appropriately cites relevant work in MoM, HMMs, MHMMs, and spectral learning, establishing its position within the existing literature.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The application of spectral learning and MoM to MHMMs is a significant and original contribution. The idea of leveraging the block-diagonal structure of the MHMM transition matrix is clever and offers a potential computational advantage.
* **Theoretical Insight:** The lemma establishing the equivalence between MHMM and a specific HMM structure is clearly stated and proven. The analysis of eigenvalues and their relation to cluster identification is a strong point.
* **Potential for Scalability:** The core benefit of the MoM approach—computational independence from dataset size—is highly valuable for large-scale applications.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments, while not exhaustive, provide preliminary evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed method. The comparison with EM, particularly in terms of speed, is compelling.  The real-world experiment, even with its limitations, adds to the practical relevance.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written. Mathematical notation is used effectively.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Theoretical Gaps & Conjecture Reliance:**  The justification for the de-permutation algorithm relies heavily on Conjecture 1. While the intuition is sound, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the conditions under which the algorithm reliably recovers the correct permutation is crucial. The claim regarding the error bound O(1/sqrt(TN)) feels a bit hand-wavy without more supporting derivation.
* **Spectral Conditions:** The criteria for identifying the number of clusters (Definition 3 and Lemma 3) feel somewhat ad-hoc and require a stronger theoretical grounding.  How sensitive is this approach to noise? What are realistic expectations for the parameter separation needed for successful cluster recovery?
* **Limited Experimental Scope:** The experiments, while well-executed, are limited in scope.  The Gaussian emission MHMM is a relatively simple setting.  It would be beneficial to see results on more complex datasets (e.g., with different emission distributions, varying sequence lengths, more clusters) and a more thorough comparison against state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods.
* **Feature Transformation in Real Data Experiment:** The cubic polynomial feature transformation applied to the handwritten character data feels like a workaround to satisfy the MoM requirements. It's not immediately clear how this transformation affects the interpretability of the results or the generalizability of the approach to other datasets. The justification for this transformation could be more thorough.
* **Minor Clarifications:**  Throughout the paper, some notation could be made more consistent (e.g. θ̄ vs θ̂ ).



**4. Detailed Comments & Questions:**

* **Section 3.2.1:** The explanation of how to make ĀP block diagonal using low-rank reconstruction is a bit abrupt. A more detailed explanation of the procedure would be helpful.
* **Section 3.2.2:** Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of the noise conditions (αk and βj,k)? How can these parameters be estimated or controlled in practice?
* **Section 4.1:** How was the "total error" metric calculated? A more detailed explanation would be beneficial.
* **Section 4.2:** The comparison with EM is valuable, but it would be helpful to see a breakdown of the computational time for each step of both algorithms.
* **Section 4.3:** How does the choice of the degree of the polynomial feature transformation (cubic) affect the results?



**5. Arguments for & Against Acceptance:**

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and potentially impactful approach to MHMM learning.
* Clear theoretical foundations for the core idea of leveraging the block-diagonal structure.
* Preliminary experimental evidence supporting the effectiveness of the method.
* Addresses a significant limitation of existing MHMM learning techniques.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  Theoretical justification relies heavily on unproven conjectures.
* Limited experimental scope and lack of comparison to more advanced MHMM algorithms.
*  Sensitivity to noise and unclear conditions for reliable cluster recovery.

**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**  I believe this paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly strengthening the theoretical justification and expanding the experimental validation, would significantly enhance the paper’s quality and impact. I recommend focusing on providing a more rigorous analysis of the noise conditions, justifying the conjecture, and conducting more comprehensive experiments with diverse datasets.  The authors should also clarify the role and impact of the feature transformation used in the real-world experiment.
