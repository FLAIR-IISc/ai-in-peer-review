- Proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) algorithm for learning Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Introduces a spectral “de-permutation” technique to resolve permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates and to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Proposes a method to estimate the number of HMMs in the mixture under certain spectral conditions.  
- Provides experimental validation on synthetic and real data showing competitive accuracy and large speedups over EM.  
- Builds on recent spectral MoM literature for latent-variable models and extends spectral HMM methods to the MHMM setting.  
- Positions the work with relevant prior MHMM and application literature, citing spectral experts and related methods.  
- Novelty: applying MoM to MHMMs and the spectral de-permutation approach are original and address computational bottlenecks.  
- Theoretical justification: proves equivalence (Lemma 1) and gives spectral analysis (Lemmas 2–3, Conjecture 1) linking MHMMs to the algorithm.  
- Computational efficiency: demonstrates substantial runtime advantages of MoM over EM, important for large datasets.  
- Experimental strengths: includes real-world experiment, noise and data-size analyses, and shows MoM can initialize EM beneficially.  
- Writing and presentation: generally clear, well-organized, and mathematically explained.  
- Main weakness: key de-permutation step depends on Conjecture 1, which lacks a rigorous proof, weakening theoretical guarantees.  
- Spectral conditions required for recovery are complex, hard to verify in practice, and sensitivity to violations is unclear.  
- Limited empirical comparisons: paper compares to EM but not to other modern MHMM approaches like variational methods or advanced EM inits.  
- Real-data experiment concerns: uses an ad-hoc cubic feature transform that may obscure raw-data performance and weakens primary claims.  
- Recommendation: overall a solid, promising contribution with acceptance recommended, but authors should pursue a rigorous proof of Conjecture 1, simplify/relax spectral conditions, and broaden empirical comparisons.
