- Proposes a novel spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs by exploiting MHMM ≡ HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix and handling permutation ambiguity spectrally.  
- Empirical results show promising performance and significant computational speedups over EM.  
- Builds on recent MoM and spectral HMM literature, extending these techniques to the mixture setting with a de-permutation challenge.  
- Key insight: representing MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM enables application of existing spectral HMM learning methods.  
- Main technical novelty: resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral analysis of the global transition matrix.  
- Theoretical justification is currently insufficient; Conjecture 1 and related lemmas need stronger proofs and clearer adaptation of prior bounds.  
- Spectral conditions (Definitions 1 & 3) are intuitive but require more discussion about practicality, verifiability, and restrictiveness.  
- Missing rigorous guarantees on de-permutation accuracy under varying noise levels and finite sample sizes (T, N).  
- Algorithm may be sensitive to hyperparameters (e.g., exponentiation parameter e); a sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Experimental validation includes synthetic and real-world (handwritten trajectories) datasets and structured comparisons with EM (random and MoM initialization).  
- Comparisons are limited to EM; broader benchmarking against state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods is needed.  
- Figure 1 is unclear and would benefit from improved annotations and explanations.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically organized, with clear model definitions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on strengthening theoretical guarantees, clarifying spectral conditions, adding sensitivity analysis, and expanding benchmarks.  
- The approach is novel and has potential to impact sequence modeling and clustering.
