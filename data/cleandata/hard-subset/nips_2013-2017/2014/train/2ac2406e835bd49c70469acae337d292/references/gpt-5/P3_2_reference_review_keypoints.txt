- Paper proposes a method-of-moments approach to learn MHMMs by reducing to a block-diagonal HMM and de-permuting via eigenstructure of the transition matrix.
- Algorithm: learn global HMM with spectral/MoM, recover top-K eigenpairs to form a rank-K reconstruction, use a "spectral longevity" statistic and k-means on columns to recover blocks.
- Empirically, method is faster than EM and can serve as a good EM initializer on synthetic and UCI character trajectory data.
- Leverages existing spectral HMM literature (Hsu, Anandkumar) and the identifiability requirement that emission feature matrix O has full column rank.
- Novelty claim is limited: block-diagonal MHMM view is classical; main new idea is eigenstructure-based de-permutation and K selection.
- Paper fails to engage with substantial related work on continuous-observation spectral HMMs, RKHS embeddings, observable operator models/PSRs, and perturbation analyses.
- Core noisy-case claims are conjectural and rely on a weak inequality chain mixing incompatible bounds and unverified assumptions.
- The Conjecture 1 argument improperly borrows an O(1/√TN) rate without establishing necessary conditions (stochasticity, diagonalizability, separation, concentration).
- The spectral longevity statistic L uses ad hoc choices (e range, γ threshold) and Lemma 3 assumes untestable eigenvalue ratio conditions.
- K-estimation would be better justified via eigengaps, bootstrap stability, or formal concentration bounds rather than the proposed heuristic.
- Method assumes MK is known to run MoM, implying M is known a priori; practical implications of unknown M are not discussed.
- Missing explicit assumptions needed for de-permutation: distinct stationary distributions per cluster, sufficient spectral gap, and Â closeness to a stochastic diagonalizable matrix.
- Continuous-observation spectral HMM details are incomplete: feature choices, moment constructions, whitening, and conditions for O to be full column rank are unspecified.
- Â from spectral methods may not be stochastic or have spectrum inside the unit disk; no projection or perturbation-theoretic justification is provided.
- Experiments are limited: synthetic tests lack parameter/de-permutation error, variance/error bars, sensitivity analyses; real-data details and baselines are sparse and lack significance testing.
- Writing and notation have glitches and missing details (indices, moment/tensor constructions, whitening), harming reproducibility.
- Suggested theoretical fix: replace conjectures with rigorous perturbation-based results (Weyl, Davis–Kahan) bounding subspace error vs sample size and eigengaps.
- Recommended to state explicit identifiability/separation assumptions and/or projection schemes to stochastic matrices.
- Propose principled K-selection approaches (eigengap + concentration, resampling stability, or information criteria).
- Provide a complete continuous-observation MoM pipeline with citations for RKHS/continuous spectral methods and practical feature guidance.
- Empirical improvements requested: de-permutation accuracy, parameter recovery error, sensitivity to eigengaps and M/K misspecification, error bars, and stronger baselines including EM with smart initializations and PSR methods.
- Discuss practicality of MK < L requirement and impact of polynomial feature lifts on sample complexity and noise.
- Overall assessment: moderate quality and clarity; sound in noiseless case but noisy-case claims are unproven and criteria are ad hoc.
- Originality: moderate—idea is useful but related techniques are standard; significance contingent on theoretical and empirical strengthening.
- Pros for acceptance: tackles a computationally important problem, simple appealing idea, promising speed and EM initialization use.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient theoretical guarantees, limited experiments, and under-discussed practical assumptions.
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; encourage resubmission after substantial theoretical strengthening and expanded empirical validation.
