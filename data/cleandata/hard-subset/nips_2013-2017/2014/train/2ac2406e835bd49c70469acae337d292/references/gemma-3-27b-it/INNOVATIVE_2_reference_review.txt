This paper presents a method-of-moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), cleverly framing the problem as learning a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The core idea – leveraging spectral properties to de-permute the learned HMM states and recover individual cluster parameters – is genuinely novel and potentially impactful. While the validation is currently limited, the boldness of the approach and the clear theoretical connection between MHMMs and a specialized HMM form a compelling narrative. This deviates significantly from standard EM-based approaches and opens a promising new direction.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The spectral de-permutation technique is a genuinely new contribution. Connecting MHMM learning to spectral analysis of the global transition matrix is a clever insight.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper establishes a clear theoretical link between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The promise of avoiding iterative forward-backward message passing, especially for large datasets, is a significant potential benefit. The independence of computation time from data size is a strong advantage.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the mathematical notation is clear. The lemma and proofs contribute to the technical rigor.
*   **Good Initial Experiments:** The initial experiments demonstrate the feasibility of the approach and its potential for use as an initialization scheme for EM.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Validation:** The experimental section feels somewhat underdeveloped. The experiments are relatively simple, and a more comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets (beyond the UCI handwritten character data) is needed. The comparisons to EM are not extensive enough to fully demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach.
*   **Spectral Conditions:** The conditions outlined for successful de-permutation (specifically, the requirements related to eigenvalue separation – Definition 1 and Lemma 3) feel somewhat restrictive and their practical implications aren’t fully explored. How sensitive is the algorithm to noise in real-world applications?
*   **Assumptions:** The assumption of only one eigenvalue equal to 1 in each local transition matrix (used in Lemma 2) might not hold in all practical scenarios. The impact of violating this assumption is not discussed.
*   **Computational Complexity of Spectral Decomposition:** While MoM avoids forward-backward, the spectral decomposition itself can be computationally expensive for very large state spaces (large *M*). This tradeoff is not explicitly addressed.
*   **Missing Details:** Some implementation details are glossed over (e.g., the specifics of the polynomial feature transformation used for the handwritten character data).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a fundamentally new approach to MHMM learning with strong theoretical underpinnings.
*   The potential for significant computational gains over EM is substantial.
*   The clarity of the presentation and the novelty of the idea justify overlooking the current limitations in validation.
*   This work opens a new research direction that could lead to significant advances in latent variable model learning.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The experimental validation is insufficient to convincingly demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed approach.
*   The restrictive spectral conditions raise concerns about the robustness of the algorithm.
*   The paper feels somewhat incomplete, lacking a thorough discussion of the limitations and potential challenges.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a bold and original paper that tackles a challenging problem with a fresh perspective. While the experimental validation is currently limited and some theoretical assumptions need further scrutiny, the novelty of the spectral de-permutation technique and the potential for significant computational gains outweigh the current weaknesses. I believe this work represents a significant step forward in MHMM learning and warrants acceptance, despite not being fully polished. I recommend acceptance with a request for more comprehensive experimental evaluation in the camera-ready version, particularly demonstrating performance on larger, more complex datasets and a sensitivity analysis of the spectral conditions. The authors should also discuss the computational cost of the spectral decomposition and explore potential strategies for mitigating this cost.
