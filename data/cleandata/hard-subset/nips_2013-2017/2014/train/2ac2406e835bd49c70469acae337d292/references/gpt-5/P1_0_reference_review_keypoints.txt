- Proposes spectral/MoM approach to learn Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix and depermuting the global spectral solution.  
- Pipeline: learn global HMM via spectral algorithm, recover component permutation by clustering a rank-K reconstruction from top eigencomponents, and estimate K via a “spectral longevity” heuristic.  
- Sits within existing spectral HMM and latent-variable literature; connections to nearly-reducible Markov chains and PCCA+ are missing and should be cited.  

Strengths:
- Simple, clean reduction of MHMM to block-diagonal HMM enabling reuse of spectral HMM methods.  
- Practical and computationally light depermutation via clustering columns of a low-rank reconstruction.  
- Attempts a fully data-driven pipeline by proposing an estimator for K.  
- Empirical results show faster runtime than EM and usefulness as an initializer for EM.

Weaknesses:
- Core noisy-case theory is missing: key guarantee is stated as a conjecture with no perturbation analysis.  
- Longevity criterion for estimating K is heuristic and lacks principled justification (eigengap or perturbation analysis needed).  
- Dependence on restrictive assumptions (e.g., MK < L, emission full-rank) is not fully discussed or analyzed.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: small settings, few K/M values, no parameter-error metrics, weak baselines, and no robustness tests.  
- Related work on metastability/spectral clustering for Markov chains and mixtures is incompletely cited.  
- Clarity and notation issues, typos, and formatting glitches hinder reproducibility.

Quality:
- Noiseless results are straightforward; main novelty is the noisy depermutation and K estimation but lacks rigorous support via Davis–Kahan/Wedin-style perturbation bounds.

Clarity:
- High-level idea is clear but presentation needs editing and more algorithmic/detail information for reproducibility (assumptions, normalization, clustering details, exponent range, complexity).

Originality:
- Reduction is known; depermutation is sensible but incremental; longevity score is a novel heuristic but similar to standard model-selection methods.

Significance:
- Fast MHMM alternatives to EM are valuable; with rigorous theory and stronger experiments the work could be impactful, but currently premature for a top venue.

Detailed suggestions:
- Provide rigorous perturbation analysis showing invariant-subspace stability and column separability with sample-size/eigengap bounds.  
- Replace or justify longevity with principled K-selection (eigengap, profile likelihood, cross-validation).  
- Analyze sensitivity to similar stationary distributions, varying M across components, near-degenerate eigenvalues, and unknown MK.  
- Expand experiments: larger N/T/K/M, report runtime and parameter error, include VB-EM and stronger EM initializations, and run ablations.  
- Explicitly state assumptions for HMM MoM routine and discuss consequences of polynomial feature expansion on sample complexity.  
- Cite related methods (PCCA+, spectral block recovery, mixtures of Markov models) and fix references and typos.

Pros for acceptance:
- Addresses an important practical problem with a simple, useful pipeline and promising empirical speed and EM initialization benefits.

Cons for acceptance:
- Central claims rest on unproven conjectures and lack theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical validation is limited and baselines are weak.  
- Restrictive assumptions (MK < L) and real-data workaround are not adequately addressed.  
- Presentation, related work coverage, and reproducibility need substantial improvement.

Recommendation:
- Reject in current form; needs rigorous theory, stronger experiments, and clearer presentation to meet conference standards.
