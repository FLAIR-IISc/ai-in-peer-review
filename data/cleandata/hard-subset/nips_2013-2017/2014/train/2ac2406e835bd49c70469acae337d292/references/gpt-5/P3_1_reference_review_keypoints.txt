- Proposes a method-of-moments/spectral approach to learn mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by reducing an MHMM with K components and M states to a block-diagonal HMM with MK states.  
- Uses spectral HMM learning to estimate global HMM parameters up to permutation, then “de-permutes” via spectral structure and clustering of a low-rank reconstruction.  
- Introduces a heuristic “spectral longevity” to estimate K by tracking eigenvalues near 1 under exponentiation.  
- Builds on prior spectral HMM and tensor/MoM literature, but misses citations on nearly uncoupled chains and metastability (e.g., Meyer, PCCA+).  

Strengths:
- Simple, appealing reduction enabling reuse of spectral HMM machinery.  
- Intuitive de-permutation: power iteration and clustering of a rank-K reconstruction isolate block structure.  
- Practical value: avoids repeated forward-backward passes; shows speedups over EM and utility as an EM initializer.  
- Attempts to address model selection (estimating K) from spectral information.  

Weaknesses / technical concerns:
- No rigorous perturbation guarantees for the noisy de-permutation step; Conjecture 1 is informal and unproven.  
- Lacks Davis–Kahan/Wedin-style analysis showing top-K subspace stability and column clustering success under sampling noise.  
- Assumption that the spectral HMM learner recovers Ō, Ā up to only a permutation is unjustified; observable-representation ambiguities are not addressed.  
- Identifiability conditions are missing: requires distinct stationary distributions or column separability but no formal separation bounds.  
- The “spectral longevity” K estimator and Lemma 3 are heuristic; alternative eigengap/stability or likelihood criteria are not considered.  
- Critical condition MK < L is restrictive and only briefly discussed; polynomial feature workarounds change the model and worsen conditioning/sample complexity.  
- No comparison to tensor/multi-view methods that avoid scaling with L; trade-offs in feature expansion and sample complexity are not analyzed.  
- Experiments are limited: small K, M, few sequences, and no stress tests for small eigengaps, model mismatch, mis-specified K/M, or larger MK.  
- Mathematical exposition contains sloppy bounds, dimensional inconsistencies, and notational conflation of Ā and permuted estimates.  

Quality and clarity:
- Readable with helpful intuition, but proofs, assumptions, and MoM procedure details need tightening and explicit statement.  
- Computational complexity in terms of N, T, L, M, K is not fully detailed.

Originality and significance:
- Reduction to block-diagonal HMM is straightforward but useful; the de-permutation via spectral clustering and longevity heuristic are novel in this context.  
- Related work on nearly uncoupled chains should be cited and contrasted to strengthen claims.  
- Approach is promising and could be impactful if made rigorous and robust, but current contribution is preliminary.

Suggestions for authors:
- Provide perturbation analysis with explicit eigengap/separation conditions guaranteeing clustering and de-permutation correctness.  
- Clarify the spectral HMM subroutine, feature maps for continuous observations, and exact recovery guarantees.  
- Replace or support K estimator with standard eigengap/stability or likelihood-based selection, or strengthen its theoretical justification.  
- Discuss identifiability and failure modes when component stationary distributions or mixing rates are similar.  
- Expand experiments to vary eigengaps, K, M, sequence counts/lengths, noise models, and compare to tensor-based MHMM methods.  
- Clean up notation, correct incorrect bounds, assume ergodicity/aperiodicity explicitly, and detail computational complexity.

Pros for acceptance:
- Tackles a relevant problem with a simple, scalable idea and shows promising empirical speedups; useful as an EM initializer.

Cons for acceptance:
- Key steps rely on conjectural heuristics without theory; experiments are limited and MK < L constraint is under-addressed.

Overall verdict:
- Promising direction that requires stronger theory and more comprehensive evaluation before publication at a top venue.
