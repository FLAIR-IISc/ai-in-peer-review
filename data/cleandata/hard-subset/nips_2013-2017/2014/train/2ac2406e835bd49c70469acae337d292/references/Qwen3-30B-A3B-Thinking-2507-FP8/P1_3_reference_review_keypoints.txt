- Summary: Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix to recover components and estimate K.

- Strength: Clever, non-trivial insight that MHMMs can be represented as HMMs with block-diagonal transitions enabling spectral methods.

- Strength: Demonstrates substantial computational speedups over EM, especially on large datasets.

- Strength: Experiments show the depermutation algorithm works on small datasets and provides good initialization for EM.

- Strength: Real-world handwriting trajectories experiment demonstrates practical utility as an EM initializer.

- Strength: Provides theoretical support for the depermutation step via Lemmas 2 and 3.

- Weakness: Relies on Conjecture 1 for low-rank reconstruction error bounds without a rigorous proof.

- Weakness: Limited novelty since the core spectral algorithm is largely an extension of existing HMM learning methods.

- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited—no comparisons to other spectral mixture methods, limited exploration of K and M, real-world test only with K=2.

- Weakness: Lacks implementation details on numerical aspects (eigenvalue decomposition handling, spectral longevity computation).

- Weakness: Fails to compare against recent state-of-the-art (e.g., tensor-based or other moment methods); speed claims need broader qualification.

- Quality: Technically sound and experimentally supportive overall, but theoretical gaps (Conjecture 1) weaken the contribution.

- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized with helpful pseudocode, but spectral properties and Conjecture 1 justification need clearer, more intuitive explanations.

- Originality: Novel application of spectral learning to MHMMs via the block-diagonal insight, but insufficiently distinguished from broader spectral-learning literature.

- Significance: Addresses an important, practical problem (efficient MHMM learning) with notable computational benefits, though accuracy advantages over EM are not clearly established.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Specific recommendation: Provide a rigorous proof for Conjecture 1 or clearly state its limitations.

- Specific recommendation: Expand experimental comparisons to alternative MHMM methods and explore a wider range of parameters (K, M).

- Specific recommendation: More explicitly clarify what aspects of the approach are new versus standard spectral techniques.

- Specific recommendation: Add practical implementation details, particularly numerical eigen-decomposition and spectral longevity computation.

- Specific recommendation: Discuss method limitations, especially the spectral conditions required for successful depermutation.
