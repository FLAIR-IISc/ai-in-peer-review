- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by representing MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and recovering individual HMMs via spectral properties.

- Strength: Novel insight — representing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transitions enables application of existing spectral HMM techniques.

- Strength: Computational efficiency — method runs independent of number of sequences and is significantly faster than EM in experiments.

- Strength: Theoretical contributions — provides lemmas and a spectral longevity metric (e.g., Lemma 3) to analyze conditions for success.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation — experiments on synthetic and real data show comparable accuracy to EM and large speedups.

- Strength: Practical utility — spectral method is effective as an initialization for EM, improving practical performance.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical guarantees — key result (Conjecture 1) is unproven and arguments rely on adaptations from other papers without full rigor.

- Weakness: Spectral conditions requirement — algorithm depends on specific spectral inequalities (|λGK| > max_k |λL2,k|) with no practical verification strategy or failure analysis.

- Weakness: Assumes known M — the number of hidden states per HMM is assumed given and no method to estimate M is provided.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons — evaluation only contrasts with EM and omits other MHMM approaches like variational inference or alternative spectral methods.

- Weakness: Real-data caveat — requires polynomial feature transformation to meet MK < L, which may be inapplicable or distort some datasets.

- Overall: Novel, well-executed, and practically valuable contribution with convincing experiments but weakened by incomplete theoretical proofs and practical condition/assumption gaps.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical analysis and address practical limitations.

- Suggestion: Prove Conjecture 1 or clearly qualify its applicability and limitations.

- Suggestion: Discuss methods to estimate M (number of hidden states) in practice.

- Suggestion: Include comparisons with alternative MHMM learning methods beyond EM.

- Suggestion: Analyze and discuss handling when MK < L is violated and the implications of polynomial feature transforms.

- Suggestion: Provide complexity analysis of the de-permutation step, including scaling with K and M.
