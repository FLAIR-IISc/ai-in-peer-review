

# Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by recognizing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices. The authors leverage spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover the individual HMM parameters after a permutation ambiguity. While the core idea of representing MHMMs as HMMs with structured transition matrices is reasonable, the paper contains numerous technical and formatting issues that undermine its scientific contribution.

**Major Technical Flaws**

The paper contains a critical technical error in Lemma 2: "Assuming that each of the local transition matrices A1:K has only one eigenvalue which is 1." This assumption is incorrect. A row-stochastic transition matrix always has 1 as an eigenvalue (due to the Perron-Frobenius theorem), but it may have other eigenvalues with magnitude 1 (e.g., for periodic Markov chains). The authors' entire spectral analysis relies on this false premise, making their theoretical results invalid for general HMMs.

In Definition 1, the authors define λGk = αkλ1,k where λ1,k is "the original eigenvalue of the kth cluster with magnitude 1" but fail to define αk. This creates significant confusion about how noise affects eigenvalues. Their Conjecture 1 attempts to justify the accuracy of the estimated transition matrix, but the justification is hand-wavy and improperly cites [1] (which addresses mixture models, not HMMs).

The algorithm (Algorithm 1) has a fundamental flaw: it requires "MK : total number of states of global HMM" as input, but MK is precisely what the algorithm is supposed to estimate. The paper claims to "specify a method that can recover the number of HMMs," yet the algorithm assumes this number is known in advance. This contradiction renders the proposed algorithm incomplete.

**Formatting and Notational Issues**

Numerous inconsistent notations and formatting errors undermine the paper's professionalism:

- "K transition matrices1 Ak" (Section 2.2) has a misplaced "1"
- "1>J" (Equation 2) should be "1_M" based on context
- "MK" is used inconsistently to represent both the product M×K and the total number of states
- In Equation (3), the vector "π1ν1 π2ν2 ... πKνK" should be a column vector, not a row vector
- The paper references Figures 1 and 2 but doesn't include them
- In the experiments section, the authors claim "2 sequences with length T for each cluster" but then vary T from 10 to 1000, creating confusion about the experimental setup

**Experimental Section Problems**

The real data experiment has significant methodological issues. The authors state "L = 3" in the original data but transform to "L = 10" using a cubic polynomial feature transformation. They fail to explain why this transformation is valid for their spectral learning approach or how it affects the model's interpretability. The claim that "MK < L" is required for MoM learning is unsupported—this is a common requirement for HMM spectral learning, but the authors don't justify why it applies to MHMMs.

The paper also makes a critical error in the real data experiment: they claim "MK < L" as a requirement, but with K=2 and M=4, MK=8, and L=10 after transformation, which satisfies the requirement. However, they don't explain why the original L=3 was insufficient for MoM learning, which would require MK < L (8 < 3 is false).

**Other Concerns**

The paper lacks proper comparison to existing MHMM learning methods. While the authors claim their approach is "computationally much cheaper than EM," they don't provide a detailed complexity analysis or comparison of computational requirements. The experimental results (Figure 4) show EM outperforming their method on small datasets, but the paper doesn't adequately address this performance gap.

The justification for using spectral longevity (Definition 3) is superficial. Lemma 3 claims that "arg maxK' Lλ̃K' = K" under certain conditions, but the authors don't provide empirical evidence that this works in practice for realistic datasets.

**Conclusion**

While the paper addresses an interesting problem and the core idea of representing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices has merit, the numerous technical errors, inconsistent notation, and fundamental flaws in the algorithm make the paper scientifically unsound. The critical error in Lemma 2 invalidates the entire theoretical foundation, and the algorithm's requirement for knowing MK in advance contradicts the paper's stated goal of recovering K.

The paper would benefit from a complete theoretical revision, fixing the eigenvalue assumption, properly justifying the L > MK requirement, and fixing the algorithm's input requirements. Without these fundamental corrections, the paper cannot be accepted at NIPS.

**Pro and Con for Acceptance**
*Pro*: Interesting idea of viewing MHMMs as structured HMMs, potential computational advantages over EM.
*Con*: Critical theoretical error in Lemma 2, fundamental flaw in algorithm requiring known MK, numerous notational inconsistencies, unsupported claims about data requirements.

Given the severity of these technical flaws, I recommend rejection.
