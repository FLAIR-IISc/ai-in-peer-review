- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM and applying HMM spectral learning.  
- Resolves permutation ambiguity from MoM by leveraging spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Introduces a spectral-based procedure to estimate the number of HMM components (clusters).  
- Provides theoretical support via lemmas and a conjecture about spectral longevity underlying de-permutation.  
- Empirically demonstrates much faster training than EM while achieving comparable accuracy on tested datasets.  
- Suggests the method can serve as an effective initialization for EM, shown on a handwritten character dataset.  
- Builds on recent MoM and spectral HMM work, extending these techniques to the more complex MHMM setting.  
- Connects to sequence clustering literature, offering a computationally efficient alternative to EM.  
- Novel contribution: using spectral techniques specifically to resolve permutation ambiguity in MHMMs.  
- Clear presentation and helpful step-by-step algorithm description.  
- Major theoretical limitation: key reliance on an unproven conjecture weakens guarantees.  
- Experimental limitations: scope is narrow and would benefit from more datasets, varied parameters, and longer sequence ranges.  
- Likely sensitivity of the de-permutation step to noise in estimated transition matrices is acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Method assumes conditions (e.g., each local transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of 1); implications of violations are not explored.  
- Lacks guidance on tuning spectral analysis parameters (e.g., exponentiation parameter e).  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept pending minor revisions to strengthen theory, expand experiments, and analyze sensitivity; rating 8/10.
