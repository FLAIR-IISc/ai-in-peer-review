Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper tackles learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) via a spectral Method of Moments (MoM) approach. The key observation is that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors first apply an existing spectral HMM learner to recover global parameters up to a permutation of the MK latent states. They then propose a de-permutation procedure exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix: in the noiseless case, Ā has K eigenvalues equal to 1 and the powers Āe reveal K clusters; with noise, they define “global” versus “local” noisy eigenvalues, form a rank-K reconstruction from the top-K eigenvectors, and cluster its columns to recover the permutation. They also propose a heuristic to estimate K by “spectral longevity” (counting eigenvalues that persist under exponentiation). Finally, they demonstrate synthetic experiments and a small real-data example where the spectral estimate is a strong initializer for EM.

This work builds on spectral learning for HMMs (Hsu, Kakade, Zhang) and tensor-based MoM for latent variable models (Anandkumar et al.). Mixtures of HMMs have traditionally been learned by EM (e.g., Smyth). To my knowledge, a dedicated spectral method for MHMMs that recovers component HMMs (not just a global HMM) by undoing the permutation and inferring K is not standard in the literature; the “block-diagonal as an HMM” reduction is known, but using the eigen-structure to de-permute components in the presence of noise appears novel. The idea resembles spectral community detection in block models (spectral clustering of block structure), though that literature is not cited.

Strengths:
- Conceptually simple, appealing idea: reduce MHMM to a block-diagonal HMM and use spectral structure to unmix components.
- Emphasis on computational gains over EM. Once moments are computed, learning cost is independent of dataset size; this is valuable for large collections of sequences.
- A concrete algorithmic recipe: MoM for global HMM → estimate K via spectral longevity → low-rank reconstruction → column clustering → de-permutation → recover per-component parameters.
- Generality across several emission families via expected feature moments; practical trick with polynomial features to meet identifiability conditions.
- Experiments show that with modest sequence lengths the block structure and K can be recovered; spectral approach is faster than EM and is a strong initializer on real data.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical gaps: central parts are stated as a conjecture, and the sample complexity bound is borrowed informally from related work without a proof tailored to MHMMs. The key condition |λGK| > maxk |λL2,k| is intuitive but unproven and may be stringent when mixing rates across components are similar.
- Identifiability and assumptions: requiring each Ak to have a single eigenvalue of 1 (ergodic, aperiodic), MK < L, and sufficient spectral separation are important but not fully discussed or justified. Practical failure modes (nearly indistinguishable mixing rates, shared eigenvectors across blocks, non-ergodic Ak) are not explored.
- The K-estimation via “spectral longevity” is heuristic. A comparison against standard eigengap criteria or model selection baselines (BIC/AIC on global HMM, permutation-invariant likelihood) is missing.
- Empirics are limited: synthetic experiments are relatively small-scale and focus on recovering block structure more than end-to-end clustering performance. The real-data result is only for K=2 and relies on feature augmentation to satisfy dimensionality assumptions. No ablations (e.g., sensitivity to noise, sequence lengths, number of components, M mismatch, effect of L) or robustness to model misspecification are provided.
- Some clarity issues: the definition of longevity and how to choose exponent ranges and γ is ad hoc; guidance for practitioners is limited. Algorithm 1 omits details like moment construction for different emissions and how to set MK initially.

Quality:
Technically interesting with sound intuition; however, the lack of rigorous analysis for the two novel steps (rank-K recovery under noise and K estimation) limits the guarantees. The experiments support the claims qualitatively, but broader validation and comparisons are needed.

Clarity:
Generally readable with helpful lemmas. A few derivations are terse; the conjecture section could be tightened. Minor typos/formatting issues exist. More guidance on hyperparameters (exponent range, clustering method/initialization) would improve reproducibility.

Originality:
Moderately high. While the block-diagonal equivalence is standard, the proposed de-permutation via eigenstructure and the K-estimation heuristic for MHMMs form a novel, focused contribution that could open a path for spectral treatment of hierarchical sequence models.

Significance:
Potentially impactful for large-scale sequence clustering where EM is prohibitive. If strengthened theoretically and empirically, this could become a reference approach for MHMM initialization or direct estimation.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide at least partial theoretical support for Conjecture 1 (e.g., perturbation bounds showing when top-K eigenvectors correspond to blocks) and for the longevity criterion (compare to eigengap).
- Empirical phase diagrams characterizing success rates versus spectral gap, number of sequences, and sequence lengths; show failure cases when gaps are small.
- Compare K estimation to alternatives (eigendecomposition-based gap, information criteria on global HMM).
- Evaluate on larger synthetic settings and additional real datasets; report end-to-end clustering accuracy and runtime versus EM and other baselines.
- Discuss relaxing MK < L (e.g., random projections) and handling unequal M across components.
- Clarify hyperparameters and provide implementation details for moments under different emission families.

Reasons to accept:
- Novel and elegant de-permutation mechanism for MHMMs with a practical K-estimator.
- Addresses a meaningful computational pain point; promising speed advantages.
- Clear potential as an initialization scheme with demonstrated benefits.

Reasons to reject:
- Theory is incomplete (central claims left as conjectures).
- Empirical validation is limited in scale and scope; K-estimation is heuristic without comparisons.
- Some assumptions may limit applicability and are not thoroughly examined.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper introduces a fresh idea for spectral learning of MHMMs that is likely to inspire follow-up work. Despite theoretical gaps and limited experiments, the conceptual novelty and potential impact justify inclusion.

Confidence: 0.6
