- Proposal: spectral/method-of-moments approach for mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) via reduction to a single HMM with MK states and block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- De-permutation (noiseless): each component block contributes an eigenvalue 1, so rank-K reconstruction and column clustering can reveal blocks.  
- De-permutation (noisy): use a “spectral longevity” heuristic to choose K and k-means on columns of the rank-K reconstruction to approximate block structure.  
- Novelty: applies spectral HMM learning to MHMMs via block-diagonal reduction and introduces a tailored de-permutation and K-selection method.  
- Correctness: the reduction to block-diagonal HMM is correct and using MoM HMM learners as a black box is reasonable.  
- Theoretical gaps: noisy-case claims rely on conjectures and heuristics; Conjecture 1 is unproven and borrows sample-complexity from unrelated models.  
- K-selection justification: the spectral longevity criterion lacks rigorous justification beyond a lemma that assumes favorable eigenvalue ordering/gaps.  
- Missing identifiability: precise identifiability/regularity conditions (on emissions, transition eigenstructure, rank requirements, MK ≤ L) are not stated.  
- Implicit assumptions: method likely inherits standard spectral HMM assumptions (invertibility/conditioning of moment matrices) but these are not spelled out.  
- Empirics limited: synthetic experiments are small-scale (K ≤ 5, M ≤ 4) and focus on clustering and runtime rather than parameter recovery.  
- Robustness missing: no stress tests on eigenvalue separation, non-ergodic chains, near-reducible blocks, overlapping emissions, or unequal mixing proportions.  
- Real-data issues: required polynomial feature expansion to enforce MK < L, which is awkward and may distort results; spectral method is mainly a useful EM initializer.  
- Clarity: high-level idea and intuition are clear and readable.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing details on moment construction for continuous emissions, whitening/truncation, exact spectral HMM algorithm, and computational complexity.  
- K-selection clarity: algorithmic description, complexity, and numerical stability of the K-selection procedure are not provided.  
- Originality assessment: block-diagonal equivalence is straightforward; contribution is incremental though the MHMM-specific spectral angle is of interest.  
- Practical significance: potential as an EM initializer, but lacking theoretical guarantees, broad validation, and constrained by MK < L in low-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: simple, clear reduction leveraging mature spectral HMM tools.  
- Strength: intuitive de-permutation and a practical, if heuristic, K-selection approach.  
- Strength: demonstrates computational speed advantages over EM on toy data and helps EM initialization on real data.  
- Weakness: no end-to-end recovery or sample-complexity guarantees for MHMM parameters in noisy settings.  
- Weakness: key assumptions and identifiability conditions are unclear; MK < L is a strong limitation.  
- Weakness: insufficient experiments (no parameter estimation metrics, no ablations, limited scales).  
- Weakness: K-selection heuristic may be brittle and is not compared to standard model-selection methods (BIC, spectral gap, cross-validation).  
- Suggestions: state precise identifiability/regularity conditions and uniqueness of de-permutation.  
- Suggestions: provide non-asymptotic analysis relating eigenvalue separation and sample size to de-permutation and K estimation error.  
- Suggestions: clarify moment computation for continuous emissions, discuss MK < L practicality and alternatives (multiple views, random features).  
- Suggestions: expand experiments (larger K/M, multinomial emissions), report parameter estimation error and robustness to misspecification.  
- Suggestions: compare K-selection to alternatives (EM+BIC, spectral gap, cross-validation) and provide time/memory complexity and absolute runtimes.  
- Overall evaluation: moderate quality, fair clarity, modest originality, limited significance; recommend rejection pending stronger theory and more comprehensive experiments.
