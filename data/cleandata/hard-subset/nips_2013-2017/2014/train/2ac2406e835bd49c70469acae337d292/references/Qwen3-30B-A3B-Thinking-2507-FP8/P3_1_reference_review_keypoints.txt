- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs by viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.  
- Introduces a depermutation algorithm that exploits spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Presents a spectral longevity metric to estimate the number of clusters K under certain spectral conditions.  
- Builds on Anandkumar et al.'s spectral HMM work and provides experimental validation on synthetic and handwritten character trajectory data.  
- Key novelty: viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transitions and adapting spectral learning to this setting.  
- Computational advantage: spectral method is significantly faster than EM while achieving comparable accuracy.  
- Practical utility: spectral learning provides useful initializations for EM, outperforming random initialization.  
- Experiments are well-structured, testing noise and data quantity effects to validate theoretical claims.  
- Major weakness: reliance on unproven Conjecture 1 with hand-wavy justification rather than rigorous proof.  
- The spectral longevity metric lacks sufficient theoretical grounding and intuitive explanation.  
- Lemma 3 and the spectral gap condition lack detailed analysis and practical conditions for when they hold.  
- Real-data experiment uses a cubic polynomial feature transform to meet MoM requirements without adequate justification.  
- Paper lacks comparisons to recent alternatives (e.g., variational methods, other spectral approaches).  
- Clarity issues: spectral longevity and depermutation algorithm need more intuition and clearer connection to Algorithm 1.  
- Originality: creative depermutation technique and novel perspective, but insufficient differentiation from recent spectral latent-variable methods.  
- Significance: addresses MHMM computational bottleneck, offers scalable alternative to EM, and shows usefulness as EM initialization.  
- Recommendations: strengthen theoretical analysis (prove Conjecture 1 or justify), clarify spectral longevity, justify feature transforms, expand related work comparisons, and include failure-case experiments.  
- Final recommendation: Weak Accept — promising and practically useful but requires stronger theoretical grounding before stronger acceptance.
