## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, allowing the application of existing MoM algorithms for HMMs. A key contribution lies in addressing the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation through spectral properties of the global transition matrix, enabling recovery of individual HMM parameters and estimation of the number of HMMs.

**Summary and Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the recent resurgence of MoM algorithms for latent variable models [1, 2, 3], which offer computational advantages over traditional EM approaches by focusing on moment matching rather than likelihood maximization.  The authors correctly identify the challenge of applying these methods to more complex models like MHMMs. Existing work on MHMM learning predominantly relies on EM [4], which can be computationally expensive, particularly with large datasets.  The proposed approach offers a compelling alternative, drawing inspiration from spectral methods previously applied to HMMs [3], but extending them to the MHMM context and tackling the permutation problem. The paper's connection to tensor decomposition methods [2] is implicit, as MoM can be seen as a related technique for extracting information from moments.  

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The extension of spectral MoM to MHMMs, and the specific technique for resolving the permutation ambiguity through spectral analysis of the transition matrix, represent a novel contribution. The analysis linking spectral properties to the ability to recover cluster structure is particularly interesting.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The algorithm aims to overcome the computational bottleneck of EM for large datasets by utilizing MoM, which boasts computational complexity independent of the data size once moments are computed.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide theoretical arguments (Lemmas 1-3, Conjecture 1) grounding the approach, even if some remain conjectures requiring further rigorous analysis. The attempt to formalize the spectral conditions for successful cluster recovery is commendable.
* **Empirical Validation:** The paper includes experiments demonstrating the algorithm's performance on synthetic data, comparison with EM in terms of speed and accuracy, and an application to a real-world dataset (handwritten character trajectories). The use of the UCI dataset provides a practical grounding.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The model definitions are clear and concise, and the algorithm is presented in a step-by-step manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture Reliance:** The central theoretical claim regarding the noise threshold (Conjecture 1) relies heavily on unproven assumptions. While the justification is reasonable, the lack of a formal proof significantly weakens the theoretical foundation.  A more thorough discussion of the sensitivity of the algorithm to noise levels would be valuable.
* **Limited Real Data Experiment:** While the UCI dataset experiment is included, the feature transformation applied (cubic polynomial) feels somewhat ad-hoc and lacks detailed justification. The performance gain observed is modest, and the comparison with EM is not particularly decisive. More diverse real-world datasets would strengthen the evaluation.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't delve into the sensitivity of the algorithm's performance to the choice of parameters (e.g., the range of 'e' used in the spectral analysis). A discussion of parameter tuning and its impact on results is needed.
* **Assumptions about HMM Structure:** The algorithm relies on the assumption that the local transition matrices (A1:K) have only one eigenvalue equal to 1. While reasonable for many HMMs, this assumption may not hold in all cases, potentially limiting the algorithm's applicability. This limitation needs to be explicitly acknowledged and discussed.
* **Lack of Comparison with State-of-the-Art MHMM Methods:** The comparison is primarily with EM. A comparison to more recent and advanced MHMM learning techniques (e.g., variational inference methods) would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the proposed approach.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel and potentially efficient learning algorithm for MHMMs.
* The theoretical framework, while containing a conjecture, offers a valuable starting point for understanding the algorithm’s behavior.
* The empirical results, though not overwhelmingly strong, demonstrate the viability of the approach.
* The work addresses a relevant and challenging problem in sequential data modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The central theoretical claim is based on a conjecture, hindering the paper's overall rigor.
* The real-world experiments are limited in scope and lack a strong performance advantage over existing methods.
* The sensitivity analysis and discussion of parameter tuning are lacking.
* The assumption regarding the structure of local transition matrices could limit the algorithm's applicability.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a promising contribution to the field of MHMM learning. The proposed spectral MoM algorithm offers a potentially efficient alternative to EM, and the theoretical insights are valuable. However, the reliance on a conjecture and the limitations of the experimental validation prevent me from strongly recommending acceptance at this time. 

**Recommendation:** **Reject**

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Strengthen the Theoretical Foundation:** Prioritize proving or providing more compelling evidence for Conjecture 1. Explore alternative approaches to address the permutation ambiguity without relying on strict spectral conditions.
* **Expand Empirical Evaluation:** Conduct experiments on more diverse and challenging real-world datasets. Compare the proposed algorithm with state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods.
* **Perform Sensitivity Analysis:** Investigate the impact of parameter choices on the algorithm’s performance.
* **Discuss Limitations:** Explicitly acknowledge and discuss the limitations of the algorithm, particularly the assumption about the structure of local transition matrices.
* **Explore Extensions:** Consider extending the algorithm to handle more general observation models or hierarchical MHMM structures.
