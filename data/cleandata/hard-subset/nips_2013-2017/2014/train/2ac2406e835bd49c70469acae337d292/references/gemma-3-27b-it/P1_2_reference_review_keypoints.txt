- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments / spectral learning approach to estimate Mixtures of HMMs by treating them as an HMM with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Uses spectral HMM algorithms to estimate global parameters and a spectral depermutation step to recover individual HMMs and estimate the number of components.  
- Builds on recent MoM and spectral HMM literature and cites relevant MHMM and tensor-decomposition works.  
- Novel contribution: extending spectral/MoM methods to the more complex MHMM setting.  
- Provides mathematical foundation (lemmas and conjectures) justifying the block-diagonal observation and recovery approach.  
- Main computational benefit: independence of computation time from dataset size, yielding large-scale efficiency.  
- Addresses the critical permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates via a spectral depermutation method.  
- Empirically validated with experiments and a real-world handwriting trajectory dataset, showing comparable performance to EM and faster computation.  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and defines models concisely.  
- Relies on several unproven conjectures, weakening theoretical guarantees.  
- The depermutation step is sensitive to noise, raising robustness concerns for practical use.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: needs more datasets, ablation studies, and comparisons to MHMM methods beyond EM.  
- Implementation and tuning (especially the spectral depermutation) may be complex and are not fully addressed.  
- The polynomial feature transformation used in experiments (cubic) appears ad-hoc and lacks justification.  
- Pros: novel, potentially scalable alternative to EM, solid theoretical intuition, and promising empirical results.  
- Cons: unproven conjectures, noise sensitivity, limited experiments, and potentially high implementation complexity.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential, but requires addressing theoretical and robustness gaps.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: prove key conjectures or narrow claims, study robustness of depermutation to noise, expand experiments and ablations, and discuss implementation practicalities.
