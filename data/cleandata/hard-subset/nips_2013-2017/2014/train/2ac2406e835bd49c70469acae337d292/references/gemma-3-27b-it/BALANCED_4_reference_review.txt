This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. By applying an existing MoM algorithm for HMMs and then exploiting the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, the authors aim to recover the parameters of the individual HMMs and the number of HMMs. 

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a known computational bottleneck in MHMM learning (the E-step of EM). The connection to existing MoM work on HMMs ([1, 2, 3]) is clearly established, and the proposed approach builds upon it in a logical way. The observation that an MHMM can be viewed as a special case of an HMM is insightful and forms the basis of the method. The paper attempts a comprehensive treatment, covering theoretical justification (Lemmas 1 & 2, Conjecture 1), algorithm description, and experimental validation. The inclusion of real-world experiments with the UCI handwritten character dataset is a good addition.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Applying MoM to MHMMs and using spectral properties for de-permutation is a novel contribution. While MoM is gaining traction in latent variable models, extending it to MHMMs and addressing the permutation ambiguity is a valuable step.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The proposed method promises significant computational savings compared to EM, especially for large datasets, as the learning time is independent of the number of sequences.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a theoretical basis for the approach, with lemmas and a conjecture regarding the spectral properties of the transition matrix.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, both in synthetic data and a real-world application. The comparison with EM is helpful.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the model definitions are clear. The use of matrix notation enhances readability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Conjecture Reliance:** A key part of the theoretical justification relies on Conjecture 1. While the authors provide some intuition, a formal proof would significantly strengthen the paper. The condition `|λGK | > max k∈{1,...,K} |λL2,k|` feels somewhat ad-hoc and its practical implications aren't thoroughly discussed.
*   **Spectral Conditions:** The practical applicability of the spectral conditions (specifically Definition 3 and Lemma 3) for determining the number of clusters K is unclear. The algorithm’s sensitivity to noise and the robustness of the spectral properties need further investigation. The connection between the spectral longevity and practical performance isn't fully elucidated.
*   **Limited Experimentation:** While the experiments are a good start, the parameter space explored is relatively limited. More comprehensive comparisons with EM, especially in scenarios with varying numbers of clusters, sequence lengths, and noise levels, would be beneficial.  The handwritten character dataset experiment seems to suggest MoM is a good *initializer* for EM, rather than a standalone solution. This should be emphasized more clearly.
*   **Missing Related Work:** While [4] is cited for MHMMs, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of recent advances in MHMM learning, particularly spectral methods or alternative moment-based approaches.  Also, the connection to spectral clustering (which relies on similar eigenvalue decomposition techniques) could be drawn more explicitly.
*   **Algorithm 1 Clarity:** Algorithm 1 could benefit from more detail, particularly regarding how the "block-diagonal structure" is enforced in practice (beyond simply clustering columns). More specific steps would enhance reproducibility.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important problem (efficient MHMM learning) with a novel and promising approach.
*   The theoretical foundations, while relying on a conjecture, are well-motivated and provide valuable insights.
*   The experimental results are encouraging and demonstrate the potential of the method.
*   The work is well-aligned with the NIPS focus on machine learning theory and algorithms.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The reliance on Conjecture 1 weakens the theoretical guarantees of the approach.
*   The practical implications of the spectral conditions are unclear and require further investigation.
*   The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.
*   The paper lacks a thorough discussion of related work in MHMM learning.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a novel contribution. The proposed MoM-based algorithm for MHMM learning is a promising approach with potential for significant computational benefits. However, the reliance on a conjecture and the limited experimental evaluation prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form.

**Recommendation:**  **Reject with Encouragement to Resubmit.**

The authors should focus on:

1.  **Addressing Conjecture 1:** Attempt to prove or provide stronger justification for the conjecture.
2.  **Clarifying Spectral Conditions:** Investigate the robustness of the spectral properties and provide guidance on how to apply the algorithm in practice with noisy data.
3.  **Expanding Experimental Evaluation:** Conduct more comprehensive experiments with varying parameters and datasets.  Focus on a more detailed comparison of MoM versus EM in different settings.
4.  **Strengthening Related Work:** Provide a more thorough review of related work in MHMM learning, including spectral methods and other moment-based approaches.
5.  **Algorithm Detail:** Add more details to Algorithm 1, especially regarding the implementation of the block-diagonal structure enforcement.
