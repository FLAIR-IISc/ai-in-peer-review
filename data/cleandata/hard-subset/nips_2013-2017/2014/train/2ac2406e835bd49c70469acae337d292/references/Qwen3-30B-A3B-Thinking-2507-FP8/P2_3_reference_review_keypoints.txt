- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for learning Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) as an alternative to EM.  
- Key insight: an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Builds on spectral learning for standard HMMs and recovers individual HMMs by resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.  
- Provides a procedure to estimate the number of clusters K.  
- Theoretical development is solid with clear lemmas (Lemma 1: equivalence; Lemma 2: spectral properties).  
- Reliance on Conjecture 1 for an error bound without formal proof is a significant theoretical gap.  
- Algorithm is clearly presented and mathematically well-justified.  
- Experimental evaluation shows comparable accuracy to EM and much lower computational cost, and spectral method helps initialize EM.  
- Real-world experiment is limited (only K=2) and uses polynomial feature transforms to meet constraints, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Lacks thorough empirical comparison with alternative non-EM methods (e.g., tensor-decomposition approaches).  
- Writing, organization, notation, and figures are excellent and accessible to the target audience.  
- Novelty: the block-diagonal representation and permutation-resolution via spectral properties are meaningful contributions.  
- Would be strengthened by a formal proof of the conjectured error bound and more comprehensive comparisons and speedup quantification.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
