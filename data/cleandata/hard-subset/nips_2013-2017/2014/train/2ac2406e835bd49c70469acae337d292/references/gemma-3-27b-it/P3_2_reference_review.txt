## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and interesting approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using spectral methods, building upon the recent popularity of Method of Moments (MoM) techniques. The core idea of leveraging the block-diagonal structure of the MHMM to adapt existing spectral HMM learning algorithms is clever. The paper is well-written and presents a clear exposition of the method. However, while promising, the theoretical grounding, especially regarding noise robustness and the spectral longevity conjecture, feels somewhat underdeveloped. The experimental validation, while demonstrating improvements in speed, lacks a deeper investigation of the accuracy trade-offs and comparison to state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods beyond EM.  I believe the paper has merit and potential, but needs further strengthening before being considered for acceptance.

**1. Summary and Related Work:**

The paper tackles the computational bottleneck of learning MHMMs, particularly with large datasets, by proposing a MoM-based approach. Traditional EM-based methods scale poorly with the number of sequences. The authors cleverly observe that an MHMM can be represented as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, allowing them to utilize existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs.  A key challenge is the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM, which the authors address by exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a “de-permutation” mapping.  

The paper correctly positions itself within the context of recent advances in MoM learning for latent variable models [1, 2, 3]. It acknowledges the existing challenges with EM for MHMMs [4] and references applications of MHMMs [5, 6, 7].  The connection to spectral learning for HMMs [3] is central to their approach.  A significant related work that isn't explicitly discussed is the literature on parameter identification in mixture models and the difficulties of separating components when they are similar.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The application of spectral learning, specifically tailored to address the permutation issue in MHMMs, is a genuinely novel contribution.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The potential for significant speedup compared to EM, especially with large datasets, is a major strength. The authors demonstrate this experimentally.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and the mathematical derivations (Lemmas 1 & 2) are presented in a clear and understandable manner.
* **Theoretical Insight:** The recognition of the MHMM as a special case of an HMM and the subsequent leveraging of existing algorithms is insightful.
* **Initial Experimental Validation:**  The experiments show promising results, particularly demonstrating the speed advantage and the utility of the algorithm as an EM initializer.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Theoretical Rigor:** The spectral longevity conjecture (Definition 3 and Lemma 3) feels underdeveloped. The conditions required for accurate cluster identification (specifically regarding the eigenvalue separation) are not fully justified, and the practical implications of these conditions are unclear. A more rigorous theoretical analysis of the algorithm’s robustness to noise is needed.
* **Limited Experimental Evaluation:** While the experiments demonstrate speedup, they lack a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods beyond EM.  Specifically:
    *  The comparison with EM initialization is interesting, but doesn’t demonstrate the standalone performance of the spectral method.  
    * The experiments primarily focus on a Gaussian emission model. Testing on other emission distributions (Poisson, Multinomial) would strengthen the evaluation.
    * The real-data experiment uses a feature transformation (cubic polynomial) which might obscure the true performance characteristics of the algorithm.
* **Sensitivity Analysis:** The paper doesn't discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm to the choice of parameters (e.g., the number of eigenvalues used in the reconstruction Ar).
* **Scalability Discussion:** While the authors claim computational independence from data size, the complexity of the eigenvalue decomposition step for large matrices needs to be explicitly addressed. 



**4. Detailed Comments:**

* **Lemma 1:** The proof is clear, but could benefit from a more concise notation.
* **Section 3.2.2:** The explanation regarding the de-permutation mapping and the spectral conditions is complex.  A visual illustration or simplified example would be helpful.
* **Figure 1:** The diagram is helpful for illustrating the spectral properties, but could be clarified with labels indicating the meaning of the different blocks and arrows.
* **Equation 7 (Spectral Longevity):** The notation and the intuition behind this metric are not immediately clear. A more accessible explanation is needed.
* **Table 1 (Real Data):**  The table only shows the "best" accuracy for each method. Including the standard deviation would give a better indication of the reliability of the results.




**5. Arguments for and against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Novel approach to a challenging problem.
* Potential for significant computational efficiency gains.
* Clear and well-written presentation.
* Solid theoretical foundation (despite needing strengthening).
* Promising initial experimental results.

**Con:**

* Theoretical justification for the key conjecture needs more rigor.
* Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art MHMM methods.
* Sensitivity analysis is missing.
* Scalability of the eigenvalue decomposition isn't fully addressed.




**6. Recommendation:**

**Reject with Encouragement for Revision.**

I believe this paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field. However, the current version requires significant revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, the authors should focus on strengthening the theoretical justification, performing a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, and clarifying the practical implications of the spectral conditions.  I would be happy to review a revised version of this paper.
