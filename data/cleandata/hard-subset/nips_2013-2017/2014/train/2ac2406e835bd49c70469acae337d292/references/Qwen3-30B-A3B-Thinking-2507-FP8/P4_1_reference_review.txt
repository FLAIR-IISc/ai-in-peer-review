

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing a computational bottleneck in traditional EM-based learning. The authors make the key observation that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, allowing them to leverage existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs. The main technical contribution is a depermutation algorithm that recovers the individual HMM parameters from the global HMM parameters, along with a spectral longevity-based method to estimate the number of clusters K.

The paper builds upon Anandkumar et al.'s spectral learning for HMMs [1,2,3] and extends it to MHMMs, which is a significant step beyond previous work on spectral learning for latent variable models. The authors relate their approach to related work on MHMMs [4,5,6,7] and spectral learning for HMMs, clearly positioning their contribution within the field.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with strong theoretical foundations. The authors establish the equivalence between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices (Lemma 1), and develop a rigorous theoretical framework for the depermutation process using spectral properties (Lemmas 2-3). The experimental results on synthetic data demonstrate the algorithm's ability to correctly identify the number of clusters K and recover the transition matrix structure even with small datasets (T=20). The real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories shows practical utility, with the spectral method serving as an effective initialization for EM. However, the paper relies on Conjecture 1 without formal proof, which weakens the theoretical foundation. The conjecture about the error bound in the low-rank eigendecomposition lacks rigorous justification, and the paper would benefit from a more thorough analysis of the spectral conditions required for the depermutation to work.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is consistent and clearly defined throughout. The model definitions (Section 2) are thorough and well-structured, making it easy for readers to follow the technical content. The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is clear and step-by-step, and the figures (particularly Figure 1 illustrating the spectral properties) effectively complement the text. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the results, meeting the clarity criterion.

**Originality:** The core insight—recognizing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices—is novel and clever. While the spectral learning approach for HMMs has been established [1,2,3], extending it to MHMMs with the depermutation algorithm represents a significant advancement. The spectral longevity method for estimating K (Definition 3 and Lemma 3) is particularly innovative. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work on MHMMs [4,5,6,7] and spectral learning for HMMs [1,2,3], demonstrating that this is a meaningful extension of the state of the art.

**Significance:** The proposed method addresses a critical computational bottleneck in MHMM learning (the forward-backward message passing required by EM), making it particularly valuable for large datasets. The experiments demonstrate that the spectral method is significantly faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy (Figure 4). The real-world application to handwritten character trajectories shows practical relevance, and the finding that the spectral method serves as a good initialization for EM is a valuable contribution. The approach advances the state of the art in spectral learning for complex latent variable models, with potential applications to other hierarchical models.

**Strengths:** 
- Clever insight connecting MHMMs to HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices
- Rigorous theoretical development of the spectral properties
- Clear and well-organized presentation
- Strong experimental results demonstrating computational advantage
- Practical application to real-world data with meaningful results

**Weaknesses:**
- Reliance on an unproven conjecture (Conjecture 1) weakens theoretical foundation
- Limited discussion of the spectral conditions' practical applicability
- The data transformation requirement (MK < L) for real data limits direct applicability
- Comparison with EM is somewhat limited (e.g., no comparison against other spectral methods for mixture models)

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of spectral learning for latent variable models. The method is technically sound, well-motivated, and addresses a meaningful computational problem in MHMM learning. While the unproven conjecture is a notable limitation, the experimental results strongly support the practical utility of the approach. The paper is clearly written, makes a novel contribution to the literature, and demonstrates significant practical value for sequence clustering applications.

**Recommendation:** Accept. The paper's technical contribution, clarity, and significance outweigh the minor theoretical limitation. It represents a valuable advancement in spectral learning methods for complex latent variable models.
