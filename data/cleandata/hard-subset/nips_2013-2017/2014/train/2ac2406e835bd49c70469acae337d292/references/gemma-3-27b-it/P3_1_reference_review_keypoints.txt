- Overall: Novel spectral/MoM approach to Mixture of HMMs with promising computational advantages; reviewer recommends weak accept pending revisions.  
- Core idea: Treat MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM and apply Method of Moments plus spectral de-permutation to recover components.  
- Contribution novelty: Combining MoM with spectral techniques to resolve permutation ambiguity in MHMMs is original.  
- Computational benefit: Method promises substantial speed/scalability gains over EM once moments are computed.  
- Theoretical insight: Block-diagonal view gives a solid conceptual foundation for the approach.  
- Experimental validation: Initial experiments (including one real dataset) show feasibility and competitive speed.  
- Related work gap: Needs deeper comparison to existing spectral HMM/MHMM methods and scalable alternatives (variational, stochastic EM).  
- Theoretical rigor concern: De-permutation step and Conjecture 1 lack rigorous proofs and detailed error analysis.  
- Fragile conditions: Lemma 3/Definition 3 conditions for cluster recovery appear specific and potentially brittle.  
- Eigenvalue assumption: Lemma 2’s assumption of a single eigenvalue equal to 1 per local transition may not always hold and requires discussion.  
- Error bounds: The stated O(1/√(TN)) error bound seems optimistic and omits dependence on observation dimensionality L.  
- Spectral metric: “Spectral longevity” appears ad-hoc without clear theoretical connection to recovery accuracy.  
- Limited experiments: Need more diverse datasets, non-Gaussian emissions, varied cluster/hidden-state counts, and stronger baselines.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Paper lacks analysis and guidance on tuning algorithm parameters (e.g., exponentiation range in de-permutation).  
- Algorithm clarity: Algorithm 1 is high-level and needs a more detailed, step-by-step description, especially for de-permutation and reconstruction.  
- Practical limitations: Paper should discuss sensitivity to noise, failure modes when spectral conditions fail, and interpretability impacts (e.g., polynomial feature transforms).  
- Complexity analysis: A detailed computational complexity comparison with EM and other methods is missing.  
- Recommended revisions: Strengthen theoretical proofs, expand experiments and baselines, analyze sensitivity, clarify algorithm steps, and discuss limitations.
