

# Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

## Summary

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key insight is recognizing that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs to learn MHMM parameters, then address the permutation ambiguity introduced by the MoM approach through spectral analysis of the global transition matrix. They propose an algorithm to estimate the de-permutation mapping and recover the number of clusters K under specific spectral conditions. The paper includes theoretical analysis and experimental validation on synthetic data and a real-world handwriting trajectory dataset.

The work builds on spectral learning methods for HMMs [1,2,3] and extends them to MHMMs, which is a natural extension given the block-diagonal structure. The paper relates to recent NIPS work on spectral learning for latent variable models, particularly Anandkumar et al.'s work on tensor decompositions for learning latent variable models.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Contribution**: The paper makes a clear technical contribution by recognizing the block-diagonal structure of MHMMs as an HMM, which is a non-trivial insight that enables the application of existing spectral learning methods. The development of the de-permutation algorithm using spectral properties of the global transition matrix is novel and well-motivated.

2. **Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation with several lemmas and corollaries establishing the relationship between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices. The spectral conditions for recovering K are well-defined, and the authors carefully analyze the limitations of their approach.

3. **Experimental Validation**: The experiments are comprehensive and well-designed. The authors demonstrate the algorithm's performance on synthetic data (showing robustness to varying sequence lengths) and a real-world dataset (handwritten character trajectories). The comparison with EM, including the use of the spectral method as an initialization for EM, is particularly valuable.

4. **Computational Efficiency**: The paper convincingly demonstrates that the proposed method is computationally more efficient than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy, which addresses a significant practical limitation of existing MHMM learning approaches.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Limitations**: The paper relies heavily on Conjecture 1 for the theoretical justification of the low-rank eigendecomposition, which is not rigorously proven. While the authors acknowledge this as a limitation, the paper would be stronger with a more complete theoretical analysis of the noise effects on the spectral properties.

2. **Comparison to Related Work**: The paper does not adequately compare with recent methods for learning MHMMs. For instance, there has been work on tensor-based approaches for MHMMs (e.g., "Tensor Decompositions for Learning Latent Variable Models" by Anandkumar et al.) that could have provided a more comprehensive comparison. The authors should explicitly discuss how their approach relates to and differs from these tensor-based methods.

3. **Practical Implementation Details**: While the algorithm is well-described, some implementation details are missing that would help with reproducibility. For example, the paper mentions using a cubic polynomial feature transformation for the real data experiment but doesn't explain how this transformation was chosen or why it was necessary.

4. **Limited Analysis of Spectral Conditions**: The spectral conditions required for the method to work (e.g., |λGK| > max_k∈{1,...,K} |λL2,k|) are not thoroughly analyzed. It would be helpful to include a discussion of how likely these conditions are to hold in practice for common MHMM applications.

## Quality Assessment

The paper is technically sound with a clear theoretical foundation and solid experimental validation. The claims are well-supported by the experiments, though the theoretical analysis could be strengthened with a more rigorous treatment of the noise effects. The work represents a complete piece of research rather than a position paper, and the authors are careful to acknowledge limitations (e.g., the spectral conditions required for the method to work).

## Clarity Assessment

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with a logical flow from problem definition to theoretical results to algorithm to experiments. The mathematical notation is mostly clear, though some of the more complex spectral analysis could be presented more intuitively. The paper provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the experiments, though some implementation specifics would be helpful.

## Originality Assessment

The core insight that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix is not entirely novel (though the paper doesn't explicitly credit this insight to previous work). However, the application of spectral learning to MHMMs and the development of the de-permutation method to recover the individual HMM parameters is novel. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work on spectral learning for HMMs by addressing the specific challenges of the MHMM case.

## Significance Assessment

The paper addresses a practical problem (computational efficiency of MHMM learning) that is important for real-world applications involving sequence clustering. The proposed method offers a significant computational advantage over EM, which is particularly valuable for large datasets. The demonstration that the method can serve as a good initialization for EM is especially significant for practitioners. While the accuracy is comparable to EM rather than superior, the computational efficiency makes this method highly valuable for large-scale applications.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field by providing a computationally efficient alternative to EM for learning MHMMs, with strong experimental validation. The method addresses a real computational bottleneck in sequence clustering applications and could be widely adopted by practitioners.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. Strengthen the theoretical analysis by providing a more rigorous justification for Conjecture 1 or clearly stating the limitations of the current theoretical analysis.

2. Add a more comprehensive comparison with recent methods for learning MHMMs, particularly tensor-based approaches, to better situate the work in the literature.

3. Include a more detailed discussion of the spectral conditions required for the method to work and their practical feasibility in real-world applications.

4. Provide more implementation details for the real data experiment, particularly regarding the polynomial feature transformation.

5. Clarify whether the block-diagonal structure assumption (all HMMs having the same number of hidden states M) is a fundamental limitation or can be relaxed in future work.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a clear contribution to the field of latent variable model learning, with good theoretical grounding and compelling experimental results. It would be a valuable addition to the NeurIPS proceedings.
