- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Uses spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimates.  
- Theoretical analysis shows under certain conditions the global transition matrix has K eigenvalues equal to 1 and provides a depermutation recovery method.  
- Empirical results show the spectral method is computationally faster than EM while achieving comparable accuracy.  
- Demonstrates the method can serve as an effective initialization for EM.  
- Builds on established spectral learning work for HMMs and extends these ideas to the MHMM setting.  
- Core technical contributions are sound (Lemma 1 and subsequent spectral lemmas) and the paper is well-organized and reproducible for experts.  
- Conjecture 1, crucial to the algorithm, is unproven and only heuristically justified using sample-complexity results from prior work.  
- The justification for Conjecture 1 does not properly account for differences between prior mixture-model analyses and the MHMM setting.  
- The paper lacks rigorous analysis or acknowledgement of estimation error and sample-complexity limitations in the MHMM case.  
- Does not discuss robustness when key assumptions fail (e.g., local transition matrices having more than one eigenvalue equal to 1).  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters, particularly the number of states M, is not addressed.  
- Practical requirement MK < L is mentioned but not thoroughly discussed; real-data workaround using cubic features is ad hoc and under-justified.  
- Some implementation details (e.g., how “spectral longevity” is computed) need clarification.  
- Limited comparison to recent alternative MHMM/tensor-based methods, weakening the novelty/empirical positioning.  
- Strengths: elegant block-diagonal insight, clear conditions for success, comprehensive experiments, practical utility as EM initialization, and good writing.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient proof for the key conjecture, limited discussion of limitations and hyperparameter selection, ad hoc feature transform in real-data case, and restricted comparisons to recent methods.  
- Recommendations: strengthen or more carefully justify Conjecture 1, expand discussion of limitations and choice of M, and better justify the real-data feature transform.  
- Final decision: Accept (conditional on addressing the theoretical justification and limitations).
