This paper tackles a challenging problem – applying method of moments (MoM) techniques to Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea of recognizing the MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM and leveraging spectral properties to “de-permute” the learned parameters is quite clever and, importantly, *novel*. While the theoretical grounding isn't ironclad (acknowledged as conjectures), the paper presents a compelling argument and initial empirical validation that warrants serious consideration. The connection to spectral learning and the attempt to provide conditions under which the de-permutation can be reliably estimated are particularly commendable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of applying MoM to MHMMs, combined with the spectral de-permutation technique, is a genuinely new direction. This isn’t just another incremental improvement.
*   **Potential for Scalability:** MoM algorithms inherently offer computational advantages over EM, and this paper attempts to extend those advantages to a more complex model. The potential for scaling to larger datasets is significant.
*   **Interesting Theoretical Insights:** The Lemma demonstrating the MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM is a useful observation, and the exploration of spectral properties adds depth.
*   **Initial Empirical Support:** The experiments, while not exhaustive, demonstrate promising results, particularly highlighting the speed advantage. The inclusion of real-world data is a plus.
*   **Clear Presentation (Mostly):** The core idea is conveyed clearly, and the mathematical notation is generally well-handled.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Conjectural Nature:** Several key claims, particularly those related to the spectral conditions for successful de-permutation, are presented as conjectures. This isn't necessarily a deal-breaker, but it means the theoretical foundation is incomplete. A more rigorous analysis would strengthen the paper considerably.
*   **Limited Empirical Validation:** The experiments are somewhat limited in scope. While they demonstrate the algorithm *works*, they don't fully explore its robustness to different datasets, noise levels, or model configurations. The handwritten character data experiment is good, but the feature transformation adds complexity. More rigorous comparisons with standard EM, beyond just speed, would be beneficial.
*   **Complexity of Spectral Conditions:** The conditions involving spectral longevity (Definition 3) appear quite complex and may be difficult to verify in practice. Further clarification on how to apply these conditions would be useful.
*   **Dependence on Lemma 3's Proof:** The proof of Lemma 3 seems hand-wavy and lacks a formal mathematical argument to support its conclusion.
*   **Overly Optimistic Discussion:** The discussion surrounding the ability to achieve O(1/√TN) error bound seems a bit too strong given the reliance on unproven conjectures.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a truly novel approach to a challenging problem.
*   The potential impact in terms of scalability is significant.
*   The initial experimental results are encouraging.
*   The paper opens up a new research direction with potential for further investigation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical foundation is incomplete, relying heavily on conjectures.
*   The empirical validation is somewhat limited.
*   The complexity of the spectral conditions might hinder practical applicability.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a bold and creative paper. While the theoretical aspects require further development, the novelty of the approach and the potential for significant impact outweigh the current weaknesses. The paper doesn't deliver a fully polished, rigorously proven solution, but it offers a *direction*—a new way of thinking about learning MHMMs—that is highly valuable.  I'm willing to overlook the incomplete validation and the conjectures given the significant novelty.

**Recommendation:**  **Accept**. I believe this paper has the potential to stimulate further research in this area, and its contribution warrants inclusion in NIPS. The authors should be encouraged to address the theoretical gaps and expand the empirical validation in future work.
