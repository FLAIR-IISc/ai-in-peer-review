## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The authors leverage the observation that an MHMM can be represented as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They then adapt existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs, addressing the permutation ambiguity that arises in MoM estimation through a novel spectral de-permutation strategy. The paper also proposes a method for recovering the number of HMMs under certain spectral conditions.  The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world data, highlighting its computational efficiency compared to traditional EM-based methods.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The work builds upon the recent resurgence of MoM-based algorithms for latent variable models (Anandkumar et al., COLT 2014, Anandkumar et al., 2015).  It extends these methods to the more complex setting of MHMMs, which haven’t been addressed effectively by MoM previously. The paper correctly cites relevant work on spectral learning for HMMs (Hsu et al., JCSS 2016) and acknowledges the challenges of applying these techniques directly to MHMMs due to the permutation problem.  The comparison to EM (Smyth, NeurIPS 1997) is appropriate, framing the contribution in terms of computational efficiency.  The reference to Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2012) regarding spectral experts is relevant, showcasing the application of spectral methods in related areas.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Challenging Problem:** Learning MHMMs efficiently is a longstanding problem. This work provides a novel and potentially valuable approach.
* **Sound Technical Foundation:** The reduction of the MHMM to a block-diagonal HMM is a clever and insightful observation. The subsequent use of spectral methods is logically sound.
* **Novelty:** The de-permutation strategy based on spectral properties is a significant contribution and a key component of the method.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The MoM approach offers a potential speedup over EM, which is particularly attractive for large datasets. The experiments demonstrate this advantage.
* **Clear Presentation (mostly):** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear definitions and mathematical notation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture-Based Justification:** A crucial part of the de-permutation strategy relies on “Conjecture 1,” which lacks a formal proof.  While understandable given the complexity of the analysis, it weakens the overall confidence in the method. The assumptions outlined in the conjecture (noise level, spectral gap) are also critical, and it's unclear how easily they hold in practice.
* **Experimental Validation:** While the experiments are helpful, they are somewhat limited.  The real data experiment uses a feature transformation (cubic polynomial) which feels like a workaround and potentially obscures the true benefits of the approach. A more thorough evaluation on diverse real-world datasets is needed.
* **Clarity Issues (minor):** The notation can be dense in certain sections (e.g., equations 6 and 7). More explanation of the variables and their meaning would enhance readability. The discussion of "spectral longevity" (Definition 3) feels abrupt and lacks intuitive explanation.
* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** The paper could benefit from more rigorous theoretical analysis of the error bounds and convergence properties of the algorithm, beyond the conjecture.



**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality (7/10):** The paper presents a technically sound approach with a solid foundation, but the reliance on a conjecture and limited theoretical analysis reduce the score. The experimental results are promising but not conclusive.
* **Clarity (7/10):**  The paper is generally clear, but some sections suffer from dense notation and a lack of intuitive explanation.
* **Originality (8/10):**  The combination of MoM, spectral learning, and the novel de-permutation strategy is a significant contribution.
* **Significance (7/10):**  The potential for efficient MHMM learning is important, but the practical impact depends on the robustness and scalability of the method, which remains to be fully demonstrated.

**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

**Justification:**

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles an important problem with a novel and promising approach.
* The technical contributions (reduction to block-diagonal HMM, spectral de-permutation) are significant.
* The experimental results provide initial evidence of the method's effectiveness.
* It offers a potential computational advantage over existing methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The reliance on a conjecture weakens the theoretical foundation.
* The experimental validation is limited and could be strengthened.
* Some sections lack clarity and intuitive explanation.

I believe the potential benefits of this work outweigh its weaknesses. Addressing the identified weaknesses—particularly by providing a more rigorous justification for Conjecture 1 or acknowledging its limitations more explicitly, and by expanding the experimental evaluation—would significantly strengthen the paper.  Minor revisions to improve clarity would also be beneficial.  The paper represents a worthwhile contribution to the field and is worthy of presentation at NeurIPS.
