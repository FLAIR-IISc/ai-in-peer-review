- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral learning approach for Mixture of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Develops a depermutation algorithm using spectral properties to recover individual HMM parameters and a method to estimate the number of clusters K.  
- The approach is technically sound with a solid foundation; Lemma 1 and the depermutation idea are well-motivated.  
- Theoretical analysis is incomplete: Conjecture 1 lacks rigorous proof and improperly reuses bounds from prior work without proper adaptation.  
- Experiments are promising but limited to small synthetic settings (K=3, M=4) and one real dataset (K=2, M=4), so empirical support is weak.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with consistent notation and a clear Algorithm 1; some implementation details (depermutation) need clarification.  
- The spectral longevity metric is well-motivated but needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Novel contribution: applying spectral learning to MHMMs and the specific depermutation method, despite prior MHMM-as-HMM observation by Smyth.  
- Fails to sufficiently distinguish from prior spectral learning work for mixtures (e.g., Anandkumar et al.).  
- Potentially significant: could speed MHMM learning versus EM and provides useful initialization for EM.  
- Lacks concrete runtime comparisons, scalability analysis with K and M, and evidence of practical computational advantage.  
- Strengths: novel spectral application, clever depermutation, clear writing, useful as EM initialization.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete theory, limited experiments, inadequate related-work comparison, no runtime or scaling analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theory and expand experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: prove or label Conjecture 1 clearly, run larger/diverse experiments, add runtime comparisons with EM, analyze scaling with K and M, and strengthen related-work discussion.
