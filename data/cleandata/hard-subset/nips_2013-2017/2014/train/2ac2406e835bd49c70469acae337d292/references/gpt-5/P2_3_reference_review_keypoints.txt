- Proposes a spectral (method-of-moments) pipeline that treats an MHMM as a block‑diagonal HMM, applies a spectral HMM learner, then de-permutes recovered states and estimates K via an eigenvalue “spectral longevity” heuristic.  
- Novelty mainly in the de-permutation procedure and the eigenvalue-longevity K-selection; the MHMM→block‑diagonal reduction itself is known.  
- Positive: reduction is correct (Lemma 1) and spectral properties for block-diagonal chains are standard and plausibly exploited; pipeline is intuitive and can be fast compared to EM.  
- Positive: synthetic experiments show de-permutation can work and runtime is favorable on large data; method can serve as an EM initializer.  
- Major concern: core practical steps (Conjecture 1, Lemma 3) are heuristic and lack rigorous finite-sample guarantees tailored to block‑diagonal HMMs.  
- Major concern: Conjecture 1 reuses bounds from different settings without proof; claimed sample complexity and stability under noise need concrete statements and proofs.  
- Major concern: condition |λ_G_K| > max_k |λ_L_2,k| is strong, may fail for slow‑mixing local chains, and prevalence in realistic MHMMs is not assessed.  
- Major concern: K-selection via spectral longevity is heuristic, depends on an eigengap ratio, and is not compared to standard methods (eigengap heuristics, BIC/AIC, cross‑validation).  
- Clarity/reproducibility issues: unspecified spectral HMM routine for continuous emissions, exact moment construction, how polynomial feature maps affect identifiability, and missing hyperparameter choices (e, γ, clustering).  
- Proof gaps: Lemma 2/Corollary 1 assume each Ak has a single eigenvalue 1 but lack stated conditions (irreducibility, aperiodicity); Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3 lack rigorous conditions and finite-sample bounds.  
- Originality: interesting de-permutation and longevity ideas, but positioned as heuristics without theoretical backing and lacking discussion of related block‑structure recovery work.  
- Significance: potentially substantial if reliable, but current experiments are limited (one synthetic and one real dataset, small K/M) and do not stress slow-mixing, larger K, or misspecification.  
- Practical limitations: reliance on MK < L and use of polynomial feature expansion may be problematic or bias models; alternatives (multi‑view moments) not fully discussed.  
- Pros summary: simple reduction, intuitive spectral de-permutation, computational advantage potential, promising EM initialization, addresses useful task of sequence clustering.  
- Cons summary: central claims unproven, sensitivity to spectral conditions unquantified, limited experiments and comparisons, under-specified practical requirements and hyperparameters, and reproducibility gaps.  
- Key suggestions: provide a finite-sample theorem for de-permutation with eigenvalue/separation conditions; test robustness to slow‑mixing components; compare K estimation to standard baselines; detail moment construction, spectral routine, complexity, and MK<L handling; and expand experiments and ablations.  
- Overall: clever and relevant idea with potential, but currently falls short on theoretical rigor and empirical validation; needs stronger analysis and broader experiments to be a solid contribution.
