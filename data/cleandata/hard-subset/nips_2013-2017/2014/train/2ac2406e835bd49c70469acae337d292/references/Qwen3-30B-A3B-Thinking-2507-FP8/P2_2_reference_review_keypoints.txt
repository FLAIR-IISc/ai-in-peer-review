- Proposal: Presents a method-of-moments spectral approach for learning Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral de-permutation.  
- Theoretical insight: Recognizes block-diagonal MHMM structure enables use of existing HMM spectral algorithms and analyzes spectral conditions for recovering cluster count K.  
- Novelty: Development of a de-permutation algorithm to recover individual HMM parameters from the global spectral decomposition.  
- Theoretical foundation: Provides lemmas and corollaries linking MHMMs to block-diagonal HMMs and outlines spectral conditions, though relies on an unproven conjecture.  
- Experimental validation: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and handwriting trajectory data showing robustness and usefulness as EM initialization.  
- Computational efficiency: Method is significantly faster than EM while achieving comparable accuracy, making it practical for large datasets.  
- Weakness — conjecture: Heavy reliance on Conjecture 1 for low-rank eigendecomposition without rigorous noise analysis weakens theoretical guarantees.  
- Weakness — related work: Inadequate comparison to recent tensor-based and other MHMM learning methods; relation to existing tensor decompositions not fully discussed.  
- Weakness — implementation: Missing practical details (e.g., choice and role of cubic polynomial feature transform) that hinder reproducibility.  
- Weakness — spectral conditions: Required spectral separation conditions are insufficiently analyzed for how often they hold in practice.  
- Quality: Technically sound with well-supported experimental claims, but theoretical noise analysis needs strengthening.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized overall, though some complex spectral analysis could be made more intuitive.  
- Originality: Core block-diagonal insight is not entirely novel, but applying spectral learning plus de-permutation to MHMMs is a novel contribution.  
- Significance: Addresses an important practical bottleneck (EM's cost) and offers a valuable, scalable alternative useful in practice.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance.  
- Suggested improvement 1: Provide a more rigorous justification or clearly state limitations of Conjecture 1 and analyze noise effects.  
- Suggested improvement 2: Add comprehensive comparisons to tensor-based and recent MHMM learning methods.  
- Suggested improvement 3: Discuss practicality and likelihood of the spectral conditions in real applications.  
- Suggested improvement 4: Include more implementation details for real-data experiments, especially the polynomial feature choice.  
- Suggested improvement 5: Clarify whether the same-number-of-states (M) block-diagonal assumption is fundamental or can be relaxed.
