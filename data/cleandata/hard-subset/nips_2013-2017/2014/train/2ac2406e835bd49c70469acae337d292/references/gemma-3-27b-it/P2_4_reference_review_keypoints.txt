- Tackles learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) using a Method of Moments (MoM) spectral approach viewing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Proposes spectral de-permutation to recover individual HMM components and estimate cluster count under spectral conditions.  
- Novel application of MoM to MHMMs offering computational advantages over EM by avoiding iterative forward-backward passes.  
- Provides theoretical foundation (lemmas showing equivalence to a specific HMM structure) and spectral analysis; conjectures on noise conditions are interesting.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real data showing competitive accuracy and speed versus EM; analyzes noise and data-size effects.  
- Generally well-written and organized, though notation can be dense for non-experts.  
- Directly addresses the computational bottleneck of MHMM learning and offers a potentially scalable alternative.  
- Key algorithmic pieces (permutation estimation and cluster number recovery) rely on unproven conjectures, weakening theoretical rigor.  
- Dependence on “small” noise as defined by spectral conditions may limit practical robustness.  
- Real-data experiment required a cubic polynomial feature transform, raising questions about generalizability to raw data.  
- Spectral de-permutation step appears complex to implement and lacks discussion of practical implementation/tuning.  
- Comparison is limited to EM; missing comparisons to more recent or sophisticated MHMM learning methods.  
- Overall technically sound but weakened by conjectural components and limited empirical scope.  
- Would benefit from more intuitive explanation of spectral de-permutation and stronger empirical/theoretical support for conjectures.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, with encouragement to prove conjectures or provide stronger empirical evidence and to compare with current state-of-the-art methods.
