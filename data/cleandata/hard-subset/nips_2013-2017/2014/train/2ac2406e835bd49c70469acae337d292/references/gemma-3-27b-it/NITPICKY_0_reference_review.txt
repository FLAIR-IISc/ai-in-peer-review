This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and a block-diagonal HMM, apply existing MoM techniques for HMMs, and then utilize spectral properties to de-permute the learned parameters to recover individual HMMs and estimate the number of clusters.

While the problem addressed – efficient MHMM learning – is relevant and the connection to spectral learning is interesting, the paper suffers from a multitude of issues, ranging from minor notational inconsistencies to potentially significant gaps in the theoretical justification and experimental validation. The sheer number of these issues leads me to recommend rejection.

**Strengths:**

*   **Relevant Problem:** MHMMs are useful models, and scaling their learning to large datasets is a valid concern.
*   **Novelty (Partial):** The combination of MoM with spectral techniques for MHMM learning is a novel direction. The observation about the block-diagonal structure is a useful starting point.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The authors clearly articulate the computational drawbacks of EM for MHMMs.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Notational Inconsistencies:** The notation is inconsistent and often confusing. For example, the use of both index and matrix notation (e.g., switching between `xt` and `O(xt)`) within a single equation (Equation 1) is jarring.  The colon notation in MATLAB is mentioned but its usage isn't consistently maintained.
2.  **Lack of Rigor in Lemmas/Proofs:** Lemma 1’s proof feels hand-wavy. It states an equivalence but doesn’t rigorously demonstrate how the MHMM likelihood translates into the HMM likelihood with the block-diagonal structure.  The justification feels like a rearrangement of terms without a complete argument for validity. Lemma 2 relies on the assumption of a single eigenvalue of 1 for each local transition matrix, which isn't universally true and severely limits the applicability of the proposed method.  The Corollary's proof is equally brief and lacks sufficient detail.
3.  **Conjecture 1 – Insufficient Justification:**  The justification for Conjecture 1 relies on a sample complexity result from [1] without establishing the direct applicability to this specific scenario. The connection isn't clearly articulated. The bound O(1/√TN) seems optimistic given the complexity of the parameter estimation and potential for ill-conditioning.
4.  **Spectral Conditions – Weakly Defined:** The definitions of `λGk` and `λLj,k` and the subsequent Lemma 3 are vaguely worded. What constitutes “sufficiently small noise” isn’t quantified.  The criteria for determining `K` based on spectral longevity (Lemma 3) appear ad-hoc and lack theoretical backing.  The statement "if the estimated transition matrix ĀP is not too noisy" is not helpful without a precise definition of "too noisy".
5.  **Experimental Setup – Limited Scope & Control:** The experiments are limited. The synthetic data experiment only varies sequence lengths and the number of sequences but does not explore the impact of different noise levels (as related to the spectral conditions defined). The real-data experiment uses a feature transformation that isn’t fully justified, and the comparison against EM is somewhat incomplete, as EM is presented as sometimes outperforming the proposed method even with the proposed method used for initialization.  No ablation studies are presented.
6.  **Algorithm 1 – Missing Details:** Algorithm 1 lacks crucial implementation details. For example, how is the rank-K reconstruction `Ar` computed? What clustering algorithm is used to identify the permutation mapping `P̃`?
7.  **Related Work – Incomplete:** The related work section, while mentioning some relevant papers, is not exhaustive. For example, recent work on spectral methods for HMM learning beyond [1, 2, 3] should be acknowledged.
8.  **Writing Quality:** The paper contains several instances of awkward phrasing and grammatical errors, hindering clarity.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   The idea of leveraging spectral properties for MHMM parameter recovery is novel.
*   The potential for computational efficiency compared to EM is appealing.

**Con:**

*   Significant lack of theoretical rigor in the proposed lemmas and conjectures.
*   Weak justification for the spectral conditions required for performance.
*   Limited experimental validation and lack of ablation studies.
*   Numerous notational inconsistencies and clarity issues.
*   The sheer quantity of minor flaws and gaps in justification ultimately undermine confidence in the validity of the approach.

**Overall:**

This paper presents an interesting idea, but it is not sufficiently polished or rigorously developed for acceptance at NIPS. The lack of theoretical grounding, the poorly defined spectral conditions, the limited experimental evaluation, and the numerous notational and writing issues collectively weigh against acceptance. I recommend rejection and encourage the authors to address these concerns before resubmitting. The core idea has potential, but substantial work is needed to realize it.
