## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper attempts to apply method of moments (MoM) techniques to the challenging problem of learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea – leveraging the block-diagonal structure of the MHMM’s global transition matrix – is a reasonable starting point, building on recent successes with MoM for simpler latent variable models. The authors propose a spectral approach to “de-permute” the parameters learned via MoM, enabling recovery of individual HMM parameters and the number of components.

While the motivation is sound and the problem addressed is relevant, this paper presents a significant amount of intuition and conjecture without sufficient rigorous justification or compelling experimental validation. My primary concern lies with the substantial reliance on spectral properties and the hand-waving around noise sensitivity. Lemmas 1 and 2 establish the ideal, noise-free case, which is a useful theoretical observation, but the jump to Conjectures 1 and 3—attempting to address noisy scenarios—feels insufficiently grounded. The “spectral longevity” concept (Definition 3) appears ad-hoc and its connection to practical performance isn’t convincingly demonstrated. 

The paper suffers from a lack of thorough comparison to established baselines. While the authors mention EM, the experimental section primarily focuses on comparing against EM *initialized* with their method, rather than a direct speed/accuracy comparison of the MoM-based learning algorithm *versus* a well-tuned EM implementation. This makes it difficult to assess the genuine benefit of the proposed approach. The real data experiment’s conclusion—that the method is “a good initialization scheme for EM”—feels like a significant undersell given the paper’s initial ambitions and framing. Furthermore, the cubic polynomial feature transformation applied to the UCI dataset feels like an attempt to circumvent the limitations of the method rather than a demonstration of its applicability to real-world data.

The writing is generally clear, but suffers from mathematical notation that occasionally obscures rather than clarifies. The use of MATLAB-style notation is acceptable, but should be consistently applied. The organization is logical, presenting the model, method, and experiments in a straightforward manner. However, several sections, particularly those relating to the spectral properties and noise analysis, could benefit from greater mathematical precision and more detailed explanations. 

**Strengths:**

*   Addresses an important and challenging problem.
*   Leverages a potentially efficient MoM framework for MHMM learning.
*   Identifies a connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.
*   Clear presentation of the model and overall approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Lack of rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the spectral de-permutation and number of cluster recovery.
*   Over-reliance on conjectures and insufficiently justified claims.
*   Inadequate comparison to standard EM algorithm without favorable initialization.
*   Experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate a significant advantage over existing methods.
*   The real data experiment relies on a data transformation that masks the method’s limitations.
*   Ad-hoc nature of “spectral longevity” concept.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The approach, if proven sound, could offer a significant speedup for MHMM learning.
*   The identification of the block-diagonal structure is a valuable observation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical foundations are weak and rely heavily on unproven conjectures.
*   The experimental validation is insufficient to justify the claims made.
*   The paper ultimately falls short of demonstrating a compelling advantage over existing methods.
*   The proposed method feels incomplete and requires further development and rigorous analysis.



**Overall Assessment:** Reject. 

This paper presents an interesting idea, but it is not sufficiently developed or validated to warrant acceptance at NIPS. The authors need to provide a more rigorous theoretical analysis, conduct more comprehensive experiments, and demonstrate a clear and compelling advantage over existing methods. While the initial concept is promising, the current manuscript reads more like a preliminary investigation than a complete and convincing contribution to the field. I encourage the authors to address the identified weaknesses and resubmit a revised version with a stronger theoretical foundation and more robust experimental results.
