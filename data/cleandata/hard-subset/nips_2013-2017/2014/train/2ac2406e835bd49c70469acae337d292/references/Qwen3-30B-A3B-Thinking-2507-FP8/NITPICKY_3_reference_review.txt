

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by recognizing that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors then leverage spectral learning techniques to estimate parameters, with a key innovation being the recovery of individual HMM parameters from the permuted global parameters.

**Strengths:** The paper identifies a computationally attractive alternative to EM for MHMM learning, which is particularly valuable for large datasets where EM becomes prohibitively expensive. The theoretical foundation connecting MHMM to HMM with block-diagonal structure is sound. The experimental results demonstrate that the spectral approach provides comparable accuracy to EM with significantly faster runtime, and the real-data experiment shows the method's potential as a good initialization for EM.

**Weaknesses (major technical concerns):**

1. **Critical Notational Inconsistency:** The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout. In Section 2.1, it defines "1>M" as a vector of ones, but in Section 2.2, it incorrectly uses "1>J" without defining J. In Lemma 1's proof, the paper writes "1>MK" (a vector of size MK) instead of "1>M" (size M), creating a dimensionality error that undermines the mathematical foundation of the equivalence between MHMM and HMM.

2. **Mathematical Errors in Key Lemma:** Lemma 2's proof contains a fundamental error in eigenvalue decomposition. The paper claims "Ā = V1Λ1V1^{-1} ... 0 ... 0" which is not a valid block diagonal matrix representation. The correct decomposition should be Ā = diag(V1Λ1V1^{-1}, ..., VKΛKV_K^{-1}), not the incorrectly written form.

3. **Flawed Conjecture Justification:** Conjecture 1's justification contains a mathematical error: "‖Ar - Ar‖F = ‖Ar - A + A - Ar‖F" is nonsensical (the terms cancel to zero). The correct application of the triangle inequality would be ‖Ar - Ar‖F ≤ ‖Ar - A‖F + ‖A - Ar‖F, but the paper's derivation is mathematically invalid.

4. **Algorithm Specification Error:** Algorithm 1 incorrectly requires MK as input, when K is precisely what the algorithm is supposed to estimate. The input should be M (states per HMM), not MK, which contradicts the paper's stated goal of recovering K.

5. **Experimental Ambiguity:** In Section 4.1, the paper states "we have 2 sequences with length T for each cluster" but doesn't clarify whether this means 2 sequences total or 2 sequences per cluster. This ambiguity affects the interpretation of the "amount of data" experiments. Similarly, Section 4.3 states "140 sequences of average length 100 per class" without clarifying whether this is 140 sequences per class or total.

6. **Misunderstanding of Spectral Conditions:** The paper states "to apply MoM learning, we require that MK < L" (Section 4.3), but the requirement for spectral learning of HMMs is that the number of states (MK) should be less than the dimension of observations (L), not that MK < L in general. The polynomial transformation increases L from 3 to 10, making the condition satisfied, but the paper's phrasing is misleading.

7. **Inconsistent Emission Matrix Definition:** The paper defines O(:, u) = E[xt|rt=u] but this is only meaningful for continuous observations. For discrete observations (like multinomial), this conditional expectation isn't well-defined, yet the paper presents a multinomial example without addressing this inconsistency.

**Overall Assessment:** While the paper addresses a meaningful computational challenge in MHMM learning and presents a theoretically interesting approach, the technical errors in the core mathematical arguments and notation inconsistencies are severe enough to undermine the paper's credibility. The mathematical errors in Lemma 1's proof, Lemma 2's eigenvalue analysis, and Conjecture 1's justification are particularly troubling as they form the foundation of the proposed method. These issues suggest the authors may not have thoroughly verified their mathematical derivations.

The paper's contribution would be stronger with more rigorous mathematical presentation and clearer experimental design. The current formulation contains multiple fundamental errors that would require substantial revision before publication. The experimental results, while promising, cannot compensate for the technical inaccuracies in the core method.

**Recommendation:** Reject. The paper's core technical contributions are undermined by multiple fundamental mathematical errors and notational inconsistencies that would confuse readers and prevent proper reproduction of results. The authors should address these issues before considering publication in a venue like NIPS.
