- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Introduces a novel spectral de-permutation strategy to resolve permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates.  
- Proposes a method to recover the number of component HMMs under certain spectral conditions.  
- Demonstrates computational efficiency and faster runtime compared to EM in experiments on synthetic and real data.  
- Builds on prior MoM and spectral HMM work (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) and extends spectral methods to the MHMM setting.  
- Correctly situates comparisons to EM and related spectral experts work (Chaganty & Liang).  
- Strength: tackles a challenging, longstanding problem with a clever block-diagonal reduction.  
- Strength: technically sound use of spectral methods and clear overall presentation.  
- Strength: de-permutation approach is a novel and key contribution.  
- Weakness: crucial de-permutation relies on an unproven Conjecture 1, weakening theoretical guarantees.  
- Weakness: assumptions in the conjecture (noise level, spectral gap) may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: experimental validation is limited and real-data setup (cubic feature transform) may obscure benefits.  
- Weakness: some notation and definitions (e.g., spectral longevity) are dense or abrupt, reducing clarity.  
- Weakness: limited rigorous theoretical analysis of error bounds and convergence beyond the conjecture.  
- Assessment scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: provide stronger justification or acknowledge limits of Conjecture 1, expand diverse real-data experiments, and improve explanatory clarity in dense sections.
