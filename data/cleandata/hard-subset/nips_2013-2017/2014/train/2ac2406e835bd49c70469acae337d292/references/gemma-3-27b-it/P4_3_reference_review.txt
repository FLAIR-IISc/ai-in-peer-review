This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They build upon existing MoM algorithms for HMMs and address the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM by exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters and estimate the number of HMMs.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction to the problem and existing approaches (EM, MoM for HMMs). The mathematical derivations, particularly the proof of Lemma 1 (MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure) are concise and correct. The subsequent discussion of spectral properties and de-permutation is logically presented. The experimental section includes synthetic data experiments assessing the effect of noise and scalability, and a real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Applying MoM to MHMMs is a novel contribution, addressing a computational bottleneck in traditional EM-based approaches. The use of spectral properties to resolve the permutation ambiguity is a key insight.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear sound and the proposed algorithm is logically well-defined. The link to existing MoM for HMMs provides a solid foundation.
*   **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The notation is consistent, and the key ideas are clearly explained. The use of lemmas to break down the proofs enhances readability.
*   **Experiments:**  The paper includes a reasonable set of experiments. Demonstrating that the approach serves as a good initialization scheme for EM is particularly valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Spectral Conditions:** The conditions presented (Conjectures 1 and Lemma 3) for successful de-permutation and cluster number estimation seem somewhat heuristic and lack rigorous theoretical justification. The assumption that the noise is “sufficiently small” is not quantified.  More detailed analysis or theoretical bounds on the noise tolerance would strengthen the work.
*   **Limited Comparison:** While the experiments demonstrate the computational speedup compared to EM, the comparison on accuracy feels incomplete.  The real-world experiment shows the method works as an initialization for EM, but doesn’t clearly demonstrate if the spectral learning approach *alone* provides competitive accuracy. A more thorough comparison against state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Complexity:**  The paper highlights the advantage of MoM (independent of data size), but doesn't explicitly analyze the computational complexity of the spectral de-permutation step. The complexity of the eigendecomposition and clustering (mentioned in Algorithm 1) should be discussed.
*   **Reference [9]:** Reference [9] is cited as "MoM algorithm as explored in [9]", which isn’t ideal. The algorithm from reference [9] should be described or referenced with more detail.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important problem (scaling MHMM learning) with a novel and technically sound approach.
*   The core idea of leveraging spectral properties is insightful and effectively addresses the permutation ambiguity.
*   The experimental results provide evidence of the method's effectiveness and computational advantages.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical justification for the spectral conditions is weak.
*   The comparison to other MHMM learning algorithms is limited.
*   The computational complexity of the de-permutation step is not discussed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of latent variable model learning. The proposed MoM-based approach for MHMMs is novel and has the potential to significantly improve scalability. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened and the experimental comparison broadened, the paper’s strengths outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, the authors should provide a more rigorous justification for the spectral conditions, perform a more comprehensive comparison against state-of-the-art methods, and discuss the computational complexity of the spectral de-permutation step. The reference to [9] should be clarified.
