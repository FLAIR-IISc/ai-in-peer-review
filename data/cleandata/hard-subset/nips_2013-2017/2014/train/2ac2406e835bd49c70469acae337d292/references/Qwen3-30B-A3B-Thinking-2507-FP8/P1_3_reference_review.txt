

# Peer Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

## Summary

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing a significant computational limitation of the traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm. The authors' key insight is recognizing that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. Building on existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs, they develop an algorithm that learns the global parameters up to permutation ambiguity, then use spectral properties of the transition matrix to recover the individual HMM parameters and estimate the number of clusters K. The work extends the line of research on method of moments for latent variable models pioneered by Anandkumar et al. (COLT 2012) and Hsu et al. (JCSS 2013), which has been influential in NIPS.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Contribution**: The paper makes a valuable observation that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with special structure (block-diagonal transition matrices), which enables applying existing spectral learning methods. This is a clever and non-trivial extension of prior work.

2. **Computational Efficiency**: The authors correctly identify the computational bottleneck of EM (requiring forward-backward for each sequence in each iteration) and demonstrate through experiments that their MoM approach is significantly faster, especially for large datasets.

3. **Clear Experimental Design**: The experiments are well-designed to validate the key claims: the depermutation algorithm works even with small datasets, the method is faster than EM, and it provides good initialization for EM.

4. **Practical Value**: The real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories demonstrates practical utility, showing the method serves as a good initialization for EM, which is valuable for practitioners.

5. **Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides solid theoretical foundations for the depermutation step, including Lemmas 2 and 3 that establish the spectral conditions under which the algorithm works.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Gaps**: The paper relies on Conjecture 1 regarding the error bound for the low-rank reconstruction, which is not rigorously proven. The justification provided is somewhat hand-wavy, citing another paper without providing a proper derivation. A more rigorous theoretical analysis would strengthen the paper significantly.

2. **Limited Novelty**: While the application to MHMMs is novel, the core spectral learning algorithm is largely a straightforward extension of existing HMM learning methods. The paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish how much of the contribution is new versus an application of existing techniques.

3. **Experimental Limitations**: The experiments don't compare against other spectral learning methods for mixture models, nor do they explore the full parameter space (e.g., varying K and M). The real-world experiment is limited to a single dataset with K=2, which doesn't fully demonstrate the method's scalability.

4. **Implementation Details**: The paper lacks sufficient detail about the practical implementation of the spectral learning algorithm (e.g., how eigenvalue decomposition is handled numerically, how the spectral longevity is computed in practice).

5. **Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The paper doesn't compare against the most recent advances in MHMM learning (e.g., methods based on tensor decompositions or other moment-based approaches). The claim that "spectral algorithm is much faster" needs to be qualified with a more thorough comparison.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound in its core insight and experimental validation. The methodological approach is well-justified, and the experiments support the main claims. However, the theoretical analysis has notable gaps (Conjecture 1), and the paper would be stronger with more rigorous proofs. The experimental section is adequate but could be expanded to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

## Clarity

The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the flow of ideas is clear. The pseudocode for the algorithm is particularly helpful. However, the spectral properties section could benefit from more intuitive explanations for readers less familiar with spectral methods. Some of the theoretical claims (e.g., the justification of Conjecture 1) are somewhat opaque.

## Originality

The key contribution—applying spectral learning to MHMMs via the block-diagonal transition matrix insight—is novel and valuable. However, the paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish its contribution from the broader line of work on spectral learning for latent variable models. The authors cite relevant prior work but could better articulate how their specific approach to handling permutation ambiguity differs from existing methods.

## Significance

The problem addressed (efficient MHMM learning) is significant for sequence clustering applications. The computational advantage of the proposed method over EM is particularly valuable for large-scale datasets. The real-world experiment demonstrates practical utility, showing the method can serve as an effective initialization for EM. However, the paper doesn't clearly establish that the method outperforms EM in accuracy, which would be necessary for claiming significant advancement over the state of the art.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the efficient learning of MHMMs, which is a well-motivated problem with practical applications. The core insight is clever and the experimental validation is solid. The main weaknesses (theoretical gaps and limited novelty) are not severe enough to warrant rejection, but they should be addressed in the final version.

## Specific Recommendations

1. **Strengthen the theoretical analysis**: Provide a rigorous proof for Conjecture 1 or clearly state the limitations of the current justification.

2. **Expand the experimental comparison**: Include a more thorough comparison against alternative MHMM learning methods and explore a wider range of parameter settings.

3. **Clarify the novelty**: More explicitly differentiate this work from existing spectral learning methods for latent variable models, highlighting what's truly new about the MHMM application.

4. **Add implementation details**: Provide more information about the practical implementation of the spectral learning algorithm, particularly regarding the numerical aspects of the eigenvalue decomposition and spectral longevity calculation.

5. **Discuss limitations**: Acknowledge the limitations of the method more fully, particularly regarding the spectral conditions required for the depermutation step to work well.

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the growing literature on method of moments for latent variable models. With the recommended revisions, it would be a strong addition to NIPS proceedings.
