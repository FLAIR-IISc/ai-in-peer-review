Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper tackles learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) via a method-of-moments/spectral approach. The key observation is that an MHMM with K components of M states each is equivalent to a single HMM with MK states whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, one block per component (Lemma 1). The authors propose to: (i) run a spectral HMM learner (e.g., Hsu et al., JCSS 2012; Anandkumar et al.) to estimate global parameters up to permutation; (ii) exploit spectral properties of the global transition matrix to “depermute” and recover per-component parameters by clustering columns after a low-rank reconstruction based on the K eigenvalues near 1; and (iii) estimate K from the spectrum using a “spectral longevity” heuristic (Definition 3, Lemma 3). They also discuss conditions under which the approach works in the presence of estimation noise (Conjecture 1), essentially requiring a separation between the K near-1 global eigenvalues and the second-largest local eigenvalue magnitudes.

This builds on the spectral literature for HMMs and latent variable models (Hsu et al.; Anandkumar et al.) and leverages classic properties of block-diagonal Markov chains. Prior MHMM work (e.g., Smyth NIPS; Qi et al., IEEE TSP) typically uses EM; to my knowledge, a spectral depermutation strategy specifically for MHMMs is not standard. The idea of using the power method structure of the transition matrix to detect blocks is intuitive and well motivated.

Strengths:
- Clear and simple reduction of MHMM learning to HMM learning with a structured transition matrix. The block-diagonal perspective is sound and useful.
- Practical depermutation strategy via low-rank reconstruction and column clustering, with an intuitive spectral justification.
- Heuristic for estimating K from eigenvalue “longevity” is interesting and aligned with spectral gap intuitions.
- Empirical results suggest substantial speed advantages over EM on synthetic data and that the spectral solution can serve as a strong EM initializer on real data.
- The paper addresses a computational pain point: EM for MHMMs can be costly on large datasets.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical guarantees are limited. The central claims about robustness to noise and correctness of depermutation are left as a conjecture. The “longevity” criterion (Lemma 3) relies on strong gap assumptions and lacks finite-sample analysis.
- Key assumptions are under-specified. The method assumes each local chain has a unique eigenvalue at 1 (irreducible, aperiodic), and requires MK known to run HMM-MoM; yet K is also estimated later. Clarify whether M is assumed known and whether MK is assumed known or estimated.
- Practical requirements are strong. The spectral HMM step appears to require observation dimension L ≥ MK (authors expand L via polynomial features on real data). This can be unrealistic and may distort the observation model; alternatives (e.g., random projections, learned features) are not discussed.
- Algorithmic details are sparse. The “HMM MethodofMoments” step is a black box; moment construction, conditioning, and numerical stability are not described. The depermutation clustering (distance metric, normalization, initialization, stability) is under-specified.
- Output parameters are incomplete. Algorithm 1 returns only Ô1:K̂, Â1:K̂; νk and π are not reported, although ν̄ encodes πkνk. A simple recovery is possible (πk = 1⊤ν̄k, νk = ν̄k/πk) but should be stated and evaluated.
- Empirical evaluation is limited. Synthetic tests are small-scale; robustness to weak spectral gaps, different K and M, and mis-specification is not probed. Real-data experiments are pairwise clustering on a single dataset; statistical significance and ablations are missing.

Quality:
- Technical soundness of the noiseless case is fine. The noisy case hinges on unproven Conjecture 1 and strong spectral separation; no sensitivity analysis is provided. The K-estimation heuristic is plausible but lacks guarantees and practical guidance on hyperparameters (γ, exponent range).
- Claims about computational scaling would benefit from explicit complexities and wall-clock comparisons including moment computation and eigen-decomposition.

Clarity:
- Generally well written, with helpful lemmas, but important implementation details are missing (moment construction, clustering specifics, recovery of ν and π).
- The requirement MK < L and the feature expansion trick deserve a more careful explanation and alternatives.
- Figures and references to them are important for understanding; ensure they are clear and reproducible.

Originality:
- The core idea of using the block-diagonal structure plus spectral depermutation appears novel and nontrivial within MHMMs. However, the reduction to HMM and use of spectral learning are established; the novelty is concentrated in the depermutation and K-estimation heuristics.

Significance:
- If made robust, this could provide a useful alternative or initializer for EM in MHMMs, especially at scale. Current empirical and theoretical support is too limited to claim state-of-the-art impact.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually neat depermutation approach leveraging spectral properties.
- Addresses an important practical problem (efficient MHMM learning).
- Promising empirical speedups; useful as an EM initializer.

Cons for acceptance:
- Lack of rigorous guarantees and reliance on conjecture.
- Strong and somewhat unrealistic assumptions (L ≥ MK; known MK; clear spectral gaps).
- Limited empirical validation and ablation; incomplete parameter recovery discussion.

Constructive suggestions:
- Provide finite-sample perturbation analysis for depermutation correctness under standard Markov chain mixing assumptions; quantify misclustering probability as a function of spectral gaps and sample size.
- Replace or complement the longevity heuristic with a more standard and data-driven K selection (e.g., eigengap statistics with confidence intervals, stability selection).
- Detail the HMM MoM step (moments used, rank conditions, conditioning) and provide guidelines for when L < MK (e.g., random projections with provable rank preservation).
- Explicitly recover and evaluate νk and π; discuss identifiability and normalization.
- Add robustness experiments: small spectral gaps, varying K and M, unbalanced π, short sequences, misspecified M or MK, and near-periodic chains.
- Provide wall-clock complexity and scaling plots, including moment computation, eigen-decomposition, and clustering; compare to batched EM.
- Release code to encourage adoption.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). The idea is promising and could be impactful, but the current submission lacks theoretical guarantees and sufficiently thorough empirical validation for NeurIPS standards. Strengthening the analysis and experiments would make this a strong resubmission.

Confidence: 4/5.
