

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key insight is recognizing that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling the application of existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs. The authors then develop a method to recover the individual HMM parameters by addressing the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM approaches, leveraging spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The paper makes theoretical contributions to understanding the spectral properties of MHMMs and provides experimental validation showing computational advantages over EM-based approaches.

## Strengths

1. **Novel Core Insight**: The paper's central contribution—viewing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix—is clever and well-motivated. This perspective enables the application of existing spectral learning techniques to MHMMs, which has not been previously explored in the literature.

2. **Computational Advantage**: The authors clearly demonstrate that their spectral approach has computational complexity independent of the number of sequences, making it significantly faster than EM for large datasets. This is a practical contribution with potential real-world impact.

3. **Clear Algorithm Presentation**: The proposed Algorithm 1 is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear steps for depermutation and parameter recovery. The theoretical justification for the algorithm is sound, even if some aspects are presented as conjectures.

4. **Experimental Validation**: The experiments effectively demonstrate the computational advantage over EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. The real data experiment on handwritten character trajectories shows practical utility, particularly as an initialization method for EM.

## Weaknesses

1. **Incomplete Theoretical Foundation**: The paper presents key results as conjectures (e.g., Conjecture 1) rather than rigorous proofs. For a NeurIPS paper, this is a significant weakness, as the theoretical guarantees are central to the method of moments approach. The justification for Conjecture 1 relies on a reference to a different model without establishing the necessary connections.

2. **Limited Comparison with Existing Methods**: The paper does not sufficiently compare with other spectral learning approaches for mixture models or alternative MHMM learning methods (e.g., variational inference). This makes it difficult to assess the relative novelty and advantages of the proposed approach.

3. **Insufficient Analysis of Real Data Experiment**: The polynomial feature transformation used to meet the MK < L condition for MoM learning is not thoroughly justified. The paper does not discuss how this transformation affects the representational power or whether it might introduce biases in the clustering results.

4. **Lack of Discussion on Limitations**: The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the approach, such as sensitivity to the spectral assumptions, performance with very noisy data, or how the method scales with the number of clusters K.

## Recommendation

I recommend **minor revision** for this paper. The core idea is promising and addresses a practical problem with potential impact, but the theoretical foundation needs strengthening. The paper would benefit from more rigorous theoretical analysis and a more comprehensive comparison with existing approaches.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Strengthen Theoretical Analysis**: Provide rigorous proofs for the key conjectures, or at least clearly state the assumptions required for them to hold. The authors should explicitly connect their analysis to the sample complexity results from the cited reference.

2. **Expand Comparison with Existing Work**: Include a more detailed comparison with other spectral learning methods for mixture models and other non-EM approaches to MHMMs. This would better establish the novelty and advantages of the proposed method.

3. **Improve Real Data Experiment**: Justify the polynomial feature transformation more thoroughly, and consider alternative approaches to handle the MK < L constraint. Provide a more detailed analysis of how the transformation affects the clustering results.

4. **Discuss Limitations**: Add a subsection discussing the limitations of the approach, including sensitivity to the spectral assumptions, performance with different noise levels, and scalability with respect to K.

5. **Clarify EM Comparison**: Provide more detailed analysis of how the spectral initialization affects EM convergence, including not just accuracy but also the number of iterations required for convergence.

## Overall Assessment

This paper presents an interesting approach to MHMM learning with clear computational advantages over EM. The core insight is novel and well-executed, though the theoretical foundation could be strengthened. With the suggested revisions, this could become a valuable contribution to the field of latent variable model learning. The work aligns with NeurIPS's focus on methodologically sound and practically useful machine learning research, particularly in the area of efficient learning algorithms.
