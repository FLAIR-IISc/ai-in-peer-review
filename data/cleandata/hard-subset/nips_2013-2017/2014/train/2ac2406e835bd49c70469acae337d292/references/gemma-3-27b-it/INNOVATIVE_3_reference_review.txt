This paper tackles the challenging problem of learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using a Method of Moments (MoM) approach, sidestepping the computational burden of traditional EM. The core idea – recognizing the MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM – is clever and offers a potentially significant speedup. The authors build upon existing MoM algorithms for HMMs and ingeniously address the permutation ambiguity through spectral properties of the global transition matrix, even proposing a method to estimate the number of HMMs. 

The paper’s strength lies in its *novelty*. The application of MoM to MHMMs is a genuinely new direction, and the spectral de-permutation trick is a particularly insightful contribution. This isn’t an incremental improvement; it’s a fundamentally different approach to a long-standing problem. The connection between spectral properties and cluster recovery in this context is a worthwhile exploration. The mathematical derivation (Lemma 1, Lemma 2, Corollary 1) is well-structured and aids in understanding the key insights. The discussion of spectral conditions for accurate recovery, while presented as conjectures, demonstrates ambition and a deep understanding of the underlying principles.

However, the paper isn’t without weaknesses. The experimental validation, while present, feels somewhat limited. The results in Figure 3 demonstrate recovery of the block-diagonal structure, but lack a more detailed quantitative analysis. The comparison to EM in Figure 4 highlights speed benefits, but doesn't explore scenarios where EM might outperform the proposed method, or provide a thorough scaling analysis. The real data experiment is encouraging, but the feature transformation to meet the MoM input requirements feels like a workaround and its impact on the results is not fully analyzed. The conjectures regarding the spectral conditions for cluster recovery are not rigorously proven and remain largely intuitive. The paper leans heavily on established results from [1], but a more in-depth discussion of their applicability to this specific context would strengthen the arguments.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **High novelty:** A truly new approach to MHMM learning.
*   **Clever insights:** Recognizing the block-diagonal structure and utilizing spectral properties for de-permutation are significant contributions.
*   **Potential for speedup:** Circumvents the computational bottleneck of EM, particularly for large datasets.
*   **Solid mathematical foundation:** The derivations are generally clear and well-presented.
*   **Ambitious direction:** Tackling a difficult problem with a bold approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited experimental validation:** Quantitative results are lacking, and the analysis could be more comprehensive.
*   **Conjectures without proof:** The critical spectral conditions are not rigorously established.
*   **Workaround in real data experiment:** The feature transformation feels ad-hoc and needs further justification.
*   **Reliance on external results:** The paper heavily relies on [1] without fully contextualizing its application.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the shortcomings in experimental validation and the unproven conjectures, the paper’s originality and boldness are compelling. This is a significant idea that warrants further investigation. The potential benefits of this approach are substantial, and the paper lays a solid foundation for future work. While a more thorough validation and rigorous proof of the spectral conditions would be ideal, I believe the novelty and potential impact of this work outweigh its current limitations. I recommend **acceptance**, with the caveat that the authors address the experimental concerns and explore the theoretical aspects more thoroughly in future work. This paper has the potential to open up a new research direction in MHMM learning.
