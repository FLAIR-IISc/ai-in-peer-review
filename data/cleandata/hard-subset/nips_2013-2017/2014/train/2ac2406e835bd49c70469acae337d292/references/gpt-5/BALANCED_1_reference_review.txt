Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core observation is that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, with each block corresponding to a component HMM. The authors then (i) use an existing spectral/MoM HMM learner (e.g., Hsu, Kakade, Zhang 2012; Anandkumar et al. 2012) to estimate the global parameters up to permutation, and (ii) recover the permutation that disentangles the blocks by exploiting the eigenstructure of the estimated global transition matrix. They further propose a heuristic (“spectral longevity”) to infer the number of mixture components K from the spectrum under noise. Experiments on synthetic data and a UCI handwritten trajectory dataset compare the approach to EM; the proposed method is faster and can serve as a good EM initializer.

This work sits squarely in the spectral learning literature for latent variable models (method of moments, tensor decompositions), adapting these to the MHMM setting. The block-diagonal reduction is known conceptually (mixture components define disconnected regimes) and related clustering-with-HMMs work appears in Smyth (1997), but the de-permutation strategy and the K-selection heuristic are the novel elements.

Strengths
- Clear and appealing reduction: Casting an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix enables re-use of established spectral HMM machinery.
- Practical contribution: A simple de-permutation procedure based on the spectrum of the learned transition matrix; computationally attractive when the number of sequences is large.
- Useful in practice: Even when not superior to EM in accuracy, the spectral estimates provide a good initialization for EM, potentially reducing runtime and variance due to poor local optima.
- Some empirical support: Synthetic studies varying sequence length and sample size, and a real-data experiment on character trajectories.

Weaknesses
Quality
- Theoretical gaps: The main robustness claim (Conjecture 1) is unproven and the justification is heuristic. Recovery guarantees for the de-permutation via column clustering of the rank-K reconstruction are not analyzed. Perturbation results (e.g., Weyl/Davis–Kahan/Wedin) could be used to formalize conditions under which K top eigenvalues are “global” and column clusters remain separable.
- Strong assumptions: Each Ak has a simple eigenvalue at 1 and all others strictly inside the unit disk (often true for ergodic chains) but multiplicity/near-reducibility is not discussed. The method also relies on MK < L in practice (as noted), which can be restrictive; it is not clarified how the MoM learner is adapted for continuous emissions and what moment features are used.
- Determining K: The “spectral longevity” heuristic is ad hoc; Lemma 3 assumes the top-K eigenvalues are global and relies on favorable spectral gaps. No robustness analysis to near-ties or noise is provided.
- Experimental evaluation: Synthetic settings are modest (e.g., two sequences per component in some plots), metrics focus on k-means error rather than parameter or clustering accuracy in 3.1/4.1, and baselines are limited to EM with few restarts. No comparison to other scalable sequence clustering methods (e.g., variational MHMMs, Bayesian nonparametric HMM mixtures, spectral clustering on sequence features). Reproducibility details are sparse (moment choices, whitening, feature maps).

Clarity
- The description of how the spectral HMM learner is instantiated with continuous observations is under-specified. For Gaussian emissions, columns of O are set to means, but the observable moment construction (which moments, how whitened, required ranks) is not clearly laid out.
- Algorithm 1 compresses key steps (moment estimation, eigenspace truncation, clustering) without specifying hyperparameters, complexity, or stabilization techniques.
- Some proofs are straightforward (Lemma 1) while the more critical parts are left as conjectures; separating formal results from heuristic guidance would help.

Originality
- The block-diagonal equivalence is standard; the distinctive contribution is the de-permutation via spectral clustering of the low-rank power of A and the longevity-based K selection. These ideas are interesting but currently heuristic and would benefit from sharper theory and broader empirical validation.

Significance
- If made robust, a fast initializer/estimator for MHMMs would be valuable. As presented, the lack of guarantees and limited empirical scope reduce impact. The requirement MK < L and the reliance on appropriately engineered features may limit applicability.

Specific suggestions for improvement
- Provide a rigorous perturbation analysis: conditions under which the top-K “global” eigenvalues remain separated from “local” eigenvalues under sampling noise; bounds on subspace error (Davis–Kahan) and how it impacts column clustering accuracy for recovering P. Formalize the probability of correct de-permutation in terms of eigengaps, noise level, and sample size.
- Clarify and justify the K-selection criterion. Compare against standard eigengap, profile likelihood, stability-based ranks, or information criteria. Evaluate robustness when eigenvalues are close.
- Detail the MoM pipeline for continuous observations: which moments (time-lagged cross-moments), whitening scheme, rank conditions, and the precise requirement for MK < L. Discuss the discrete-observation case explicitly and report experiments there too.
- Expand experiments: more diverse synthetic regimes (vary K, M, separation, chain mixing rates), parameter recovery metrics, clustering accuracy across sequences, sensitivity to mis-specified M, and to overlaps between components. Include stronger baselines and report wall-clock times broken down by moment computation vs factorization.
- Discuss identifiability/separation: when two components have similar stationary distributions or transition spectra, how does de-permutation fare? Provide failure cases.
- Release code and document hyperparameters (e.g., e-range for exponentiation, k-means settings, whitening regularization).

Arguments for acceptance
- Simple and computationally attractive approach; leverages established spectral HMM tools.
- Novel, intuitive de-permutation and K-estimation ideas tailored to MHMM structure.
- Potentially useful as a fast, practical initializer for EM in large-scale sequence clustering.

Arguments against acceptance
- Key claims rely on unproven conjectures; theoretical contribution is incomplete.
- The K-selection and de-permutation procedures are heuristic without robustness guarantees.
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and baselines; practical constraints (MK < L) are significant.
- Clarity gaps regarding the exact MoM construction for continuous data and reproducibility.

Recommendation
Reject (borderline). The idea is promising and could be impactful with stronger theory and a more thorough empirical study. I encourage the authors to develop rigorous perturbation guarantees for de-permutation and K selection, clarify the continuous-observation MoM setup, and significantly expand the experimental validation.
