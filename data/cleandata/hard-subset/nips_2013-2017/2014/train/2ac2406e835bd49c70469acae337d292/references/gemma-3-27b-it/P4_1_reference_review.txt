## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper presents a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, applying existing MoM techniques for HMMs, and then employing spectral properties to “de-permute” the learned parameters to recover the individual HMM components and the number of HMMs.

**Relation to Previous Work:** The paper builds upon recent work utilizing MoM for latent variable models [1, 2, 3]. It specifically adapts the spectral learning approach for HMMs [3] to the MHMM setting. The authors correctly position their work as addressing the computational challenges of traditional EM approaches for MHMMs [4], particularly with large datasets. The paper also draws connections to related work in areas like music analysis and motion time-series clustering [5, 6, 7], demonstrating an understanding of the application landscape of MHMMs. Reference [8] relating to spectral experts is noted, but the connection to the presented work isn’t immediately clear and could be strengthened in the introduction.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The application of spectral techniques to de-permute the parameters obtained via MoM for MHMMs is a novel contribution. This addresses a key challenge of using MoM in complex models where parameter identification is not straightforward.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The promise of a learning algorithm independent of dataset size (number of sequences and lengths) is a significant strength, particularly when compared to the iterative nature of EM.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a reasonable theoretical justification for the approach, including Lemmas 1 & 2, and conjectures outlining the conditions for successful de-permutation and cluster number estimation.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments provide evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. The comparison with EM in terms of both accuracy and speed is valuable. The inclusion of a real-world dataset experiment further strengthens the evaluation.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The model definitions (Section 2) are concise and clear.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture-Based Approach:** A significant portion of the theoretical justification relies on conjectures. While the provided reasoning is plausible, formal proofs would considerably strengthen the paper. Specifically, the conditions for successful de-permutation (Definitions 1 & 3, Lemma 3) feel somewhat ad-hoc and warrant more rigorous analysis.
* **Limited Explanation of Spectral Conditions:** While the paper outlines spectral conditions for recovering the number of HMMs, the intuition behind these conditions (particularly regarding longevity Lλ̃K′) could be made more accessible.  A visual representation or more detailed explanation of how these conditions manifest in practice would be helpful.
* **Experimental Scope:** The experiments, while providing positive results, could be expanded. The real data experiment uses a feature transformation (cubic polynomial) that feels somewhat disconnected from the core algorithmic contribution.  Exploring the performance on a wider range of datasets and model configurations would increase confidence in the generalizability of the method.
* **Missing Discussion on Limitations:** The paper lacks a dedicated discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach. For example, the sensitivity of the algorithm to noise, or the assumptions about the structure of the transition matrices (only one eigenvalue equal to 1), are not explicitly addressed.
* **Notational Clutter:** While generally clear, the notation, particularly in section 3.2.1, becomes somewhat dense and difficult to follow.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and potentially impactful approach to learning MHMMs, addressing a known computational bottleneck.
* The theoretical justification, while containing conjectures, is well-reasoned and provides a solid foundation for the proposed algorithm.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in comparison to existing methods.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The reliance on conjectures weakens the theoretical contribution.
* The spectral conditions for recovering the number of HMMs could be better explained and justified.
* The experimental scope could be expanded to provide more comprehensive validation.
* The lack of a dedicated discussion on limitations is a minor drawback.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of latent variable modeling. The proposed algorithm is novel, theoretically motivated, and empirically validated. While the reliance on conjectures and limited discussion of limitations are concerns, the strengths of the paper outweigh these weaknesses. I believe that with minor revisions to address the concerns raised, this paper would be a valuable addition to NIPS.

**Recommendation:**  **Accept with minor revisions.** The authors should focus on strengthening the theoretical justification (where possible), providing more intuition behind the spectral conditions, and expanding the experimental evaluation. Addressing these points would significantly enhance the impact and credibility of the work.
