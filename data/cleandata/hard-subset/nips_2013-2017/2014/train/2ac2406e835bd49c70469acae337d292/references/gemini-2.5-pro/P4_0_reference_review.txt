### Summary of the Paper

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based spectral algorithm for learning the parameters of a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM). This stands as an alternative to the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm, which can be computationally expensive for large datasets. The core insight of the paper is that an MHMM with K components, each having M hidden states, can be represented as a single large HMM with KM states. This large HMM possesses a specific structure: its transition matrix is block-diagonal, and its observation and initial state parameters are formed by concatenating the parameters of the individual HMM components.

Building on this observation, the authors propose using an existing spectral algorithm for HMMs [3] to learn the parameters of this equivalent large HMM. However, a known issue with these methods is that they recover parameters only up to a permutation of the hidden states. In the MHMM context, this permutation mixes states from different component HMMs, making it impossible to recover the individual model parameters.

The main contribution of the paper is a "depermutation" procedure to resolve this ambiguity. The authors leverage the spectral properties of the block-diagonal global transition matrix. In the noiseless case, this matrix has K eigenvalues equal to one. The authors show that by raising the estimated transition matrix to a high power, the columns corresponding to the same component HMM will cluster together, allowing for the recovery of the permutation. For the more realistic noisy case, where this property does not hold directly, they propose a low-rank reconstruction of the transition matrix using its top K eigenvectors. Clustering the columns of this reconstructed matrix is shown to effectively identify the blocks and thus find the correct depermutation. The paper also presents a heuristic method based on "spectral longevity" to estimate the number of components, K, from the data by identifying a significant gap in the eigenvalue spectrum. The proposed algorithm is evaluated on synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating significant speed advantages over EM and comparable clustering accuracy.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality (Technical Soundness):**

*   **Strengths:** The technical approach is well-founded. The equivalence between an MHMM and a block-diagonal HMM (Lemma 1) is correct and provides a solid foundation for the method. The depermutation strategy based on the spectral properties of the transition matrix is logical, and the use of a low-rank approximation for the noisy case is a standard and reasonable technique in spectral methods. The empirical results on synthetic data support the claim that the algorithm can recover the block structure effectively.

*   **Weaknesses:** The theoretical justification for the noisy case has a significant gap. Conjecture 1, which bounds the error of the low-rank matrix reconstruction, is central to the method's justification but is not proven. The authors state they are reusing a bound from [1] for a different model (a mixture model) and that further analysis is out of scope. While the intuition is plausible, the lack of a formal proof or a more rigorous justification specific to the MHMM structure weakens the paper's theoretical contribution. The justification provided for the bound is brief and not fully rigorous.

**Clarity:**

*   **Strengths:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The problem is clearly motivated, and the proposed solution is developed step-by-step, starting from the model definition and the key insight in Lemma 1. The figures, particularly Figure 1, are effective at illustrating the core idea of the depermutation algorithm in both noiseless and noisy settings. Algorithm 1 provides a clear summary of the overall procedure.

*   **Weaknesses:** The presentation of the method for estimating K could be improved. The concept of "spectral longevity" (Definition 3) is non-standard and its mathematical formulation is somewhat convoluted. While the underlying idea is to find a persistent gap in the eigenvalues under exponentiation, the explanation could be more intuitive. A simpler motivation, such as directly analyzing the ratio `|λ̃_K'| / |λ̃_{K'+1}|`, might be more accessible to the reader before introducing the more complex longevity formula.

**Originality:**

*   **Strengths:** The paper presents a novel approach to a well-known problem. While it builds directly upon the foundational work of spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2, 3], the application to MHMMs and the proposed solution to the resulting depermutation problem are original. The idea of using the eigenvector structure of the global transition matrix to disentangle the component models is a creative and valuable contribution.

*   **Weaknesses:** The core components (spectral learning for HMMs, using low-rank approximations, clustering eigenvectors) are established techniques. The novelty lies in their specific combination and application to solve the structural problem of learning MHMMs.

**Significance:**

*   **Strengths:** The work is significant as it offers a practical, computationally efficient alternative to the widely used but often slow EM algorithm for MHMMs. The speed-up demonstrated in Figure 4 is substantial. Furthermore, even if its accuracy is not always superior to a well-tuned EM, its use as a principled, non-random initialization scheme for EM (as shown in Table 1) is a very valuable practical contribution that could improve the robustness and speed of existing pipelines. The problem of clustering sequences is important in many domains, making improvements in MHMM learning broadly applicable.

*   **Weaknesses:** The experimental results suggest that the proposed method's accuracy as a standalone algorithm may be lower than EM, especially in low-data regimes (Figure 4). In the real-data experiment, its performance is inconsistent compared to randomly initialized EM, reinforcing its utility primarily as an initialization method rather than a direct replacement. The practical limitation of spectral methods, `MK < L` (total states less than observation dimension), is a critical constraint that is only briefly mentioned and handled with a feature transformation. A more prominent discussion of this limitation and its implications would be beneficial.

### Summary of Arguments

**Arguments for acceptance:**
1.  Provides a novel and elegant spectral learning algorithm for the important and widely used MHMM class of models.
2.  The core contribution—the depermutation algorithm based on spectral clustering of the transition matrix—is a clever solution to a key technical challenge.
3.  Offers a significant computational advantage over the standard EM algorithm, making it scalable to larger datasets.
4.  Provides a principled and effective initialization strategy for EM, which can help overcome EM's sensitivity to initialization and potentially reduce its overall runtime.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
1.  The theoretical support for the algorithm's performance in the noisy case relies on an unproven conjecture with a borrowed and not fully justified error bound.
2.  The method for automatically selecting the number of clusters `K` is presented in a complex manner and its superiority over simpler heuristics is not established.
3.  The experimental evaluation, while showing a clear speed advantage, indicates that the method's accuracy is often lower than EM, positioning it more as an initialization technique than a superior standalone alternative.
