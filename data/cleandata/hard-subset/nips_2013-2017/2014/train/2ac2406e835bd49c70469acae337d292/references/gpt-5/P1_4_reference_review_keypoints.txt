- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for MHMMs by viewing an MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM with MK states and de-permuting the spectral HMM output to recover components.  
- Key de-permutation idea: use eigenvalue multiplicity near 1 and a “spectral longevity” heuristic to identify K and reconstruct a rank-K transition, then cluster columns to recover component assignments.  
- Builds on existing spectral/MoM HMM literature (Anandkumar, Hsu-Kakade-Zhang); block-diagonal reduction is straightforward and partly implicit in prior work.  
- Novelty is algorithmic: applying spectral HMM learning to the global representation and proposing the spectral de-permutation and longevity heuristic.  
- Strength: simple, intuitive reduction enabling reuse of spectral HMM methods.  
- Strength: practical, computationally light de-permutation using eigenproperties and column clustering.  
- Strength: concrete heuristic (spectral longevity) proposed for estimating K in noisy settings.  
- Strength: experiments show large speedups over EM and usefulness as an EM initializer.  
- Strength: addresses an important practical problem—scalable learning for sequence clustering.  
- Weakness: core recovery steps (permutation and K under noise) rely on heuristics and unproven Conjecture 1; sample complexity claims are borrowed, not derived.  
- Weakness: approach assumes a separation |λG_K| > max_k |λL_2,k| and may fail when component mixing is slow or there is periodicity.  
- Weakness: eigenvector instability and non-normality of Ā can undermine eigendecomposition and clustering steps.  
- Weakness: identifiability/assumptions (ergodicity/aperiodicity, identical M across components) are not stated or handled for heterogeneous M_k.  
- Weakness: spectral HMM prerequisites (rank/separability), feature lifting for continuous data, and the MoM routine are insufficiently detailed and unjustified.  
- Weakness: recovery of π and component ν_k from the global stationary vector ν̄ is not explained.  
- Weakness: many implementation and theoretical details omitted (moment definitions, stability, clustering initialization, proofs), with informal sketches and inconsistent references.  
- Originality: moderate — main idea is straightforward but de-permutation heuristic is interesting though heuristic-driven.  
- Significance: potentially useful if robustified, but current theory and experiments (small synthetic + one UCI) are limited.  
- Suggested improvements: provide finite-sample/probabilistic analysis for Conjecture 1 and longevity, formalize identifiability/eigengap conditions, and address eigenvector instability (consider subspace methods).  
- Suggested improvements: clarify π/ν recovery and extension to heterogeneous M_k, detail the MoM algorithm and feature mapping, and strengthen experiments (vary eigengaps, baselines, timings, confidence intervals, ablations).  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles a computationally hard problem, offers an elegant reduction and a practical de-permutation heuristic, and shows promising speed/initialization benefits.  
- Cons for acceptance: key claims are conjectural, sensitivity to spectral gaps is uncharacterized, algorithmic details and experiments are incomplete, and applicability assumptions are restrictive.  
- Overall evaluation: interesting idea with practical promise but currently too heuristic and under-theorized—recommend rejection pending stronger theory, clarity, and empirical validation.
