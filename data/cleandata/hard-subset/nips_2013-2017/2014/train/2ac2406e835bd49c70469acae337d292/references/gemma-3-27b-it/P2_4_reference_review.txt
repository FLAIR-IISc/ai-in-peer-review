## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of learning parameters for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), a computationally expensive task typically addressed with the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm. The authors propose a novel approach based on the Method of Moments (MoM), leveraging the observation that an MHMM can be viewed as a special case of an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  They extend existing MoM algorithms for HMMs and introduce a spectral technique to de-permute the learned parameters, recovering individual HMM components and even estimating the number of clusters under certain spectral conditions. The paper includes theoretical justifications and experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world data.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:**  The application of MoM to MHMMs is a significant contribution. MoM offers potential computational advantages over EM, particularly for large datasets, as it avoids iterative forward-backward message passing.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a clear and concise theoretical foundation, establishing the equivalence between MHMMs and a specific HMM structure. Lemmas 1 and 2 are well-stated and help build the argument for their approach. The spectral analysis and conjectures regarding noise conditions are interesting and potentially valuable.
* **Experimental Validation:** The authors provide a comprehensive set of experiments. They demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed algorithm by comparing it to EM, both in terms of accuracy and speed, on both synthetic and real-world data. The analysis of the impact of noise and data size is particularly useful.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized.  The notation is clear (though potentially dense for readers unfamiliar with HMM notation), and the flow of ideas is logical. 
* **Addresses Computational Bottleneck:** The work directly tackles a major limitation of standard MHMM learning - computational cost - offering a potentially scalable alternative.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture-Based Results:** Several critical components of the algorithm, particularly the estimation of the permutation mapping and the number of clusters, rely on conjectures (Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3). While the justification is provided, the lack of rigorous proof weakens the confidence in these aspects of the method.  The dependence on a "small" amount of noise, as defined by the spectral conditions, is a potential practical limitation.
* **Limited Scope of Real Data Experiment:**  The real data experiment (handwritten character trajectories) involves a feature transformation (cubic polynomial) to meet the model requirements. While justified, it introduces an additional complexity and might affect the generalizability of the results. A demonstration on raw data would be stronger.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** The algorithm, particularly the spectral de-permutation step, appears complex and might require significant engineering effort to implement efficiently. The paper doesn’t adequately discuss the practical considerations of implementing and tuning this algorithm.
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While EM is used as a baseline, the paper does not compare to more recent and sophisticated MHMM learning techniques. This limits the ability to assess the true significance of the proposed method.



**Quality:**

The paper is technically sound overall, though the reliance on conjectures weakens the rigor.  The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation, and the experimental results provide evidence supporting the approach.  It feels like a complete piece of work, presenting a self-contained method with justification and validation.

**Clarity:**

The paper is generally well-written, but the mathematical notation can be dense. A more intuitive explanation of the spectral de-permutation step would be beneficial.

**Originality:**

The combination of MoM with spectral techniques for MHMM learning is novel. The paper builds upon existing work in MoM and spectral learning but applies it in a unique and potentially impactful way. The authors clearly acknowledge related work.

**Significance:**

The potential to learn MHMMs efficiently and accurately, particularly for large datasets, is significant. If the conjectures hold and the algorithm can be implemented effectively, this work could advance the state-of-the-art in sequence clustering and modeling.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Novel and potentially impactful approach to MHMM learning.
* Sound theoretical justification and promising experimental results.
* Addresses a key computational bottleneck in MHMM learning.
* Well-written and organized paper.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Reliance on unproven conjectures weakens the theoretical foundation.
* Limited scope of real-world validation.
* Complexity of implementation may hinder practical adoption.
* Lack of comparison to more recent state-of-the-art MHMM learning techniques.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field, with a novel and potentially significant approach to MHMM learning.  However, the reliance on conjectures and the lack of comparison to recent advancements raise some concerns.  

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **weak accept**.  I believe the paper merits publication, but the authors should be encouraged to address the limitations identified in this review.  Specifically, attempting to prove the conjectures or providing stronger empirical evidence to support them would significantly strengthen the paper.  Addressing the comparison to state-of-the-art methods is also important.
