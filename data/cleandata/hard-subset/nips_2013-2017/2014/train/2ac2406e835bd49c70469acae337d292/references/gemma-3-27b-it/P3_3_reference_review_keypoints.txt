- Presents a novel spectral Method-of-Moments method for learning Mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Introduces a de-permutation strategy to recover individual HMM parameters obscured by permutation ambiguity in spectral methods.  
- Proposes spectral conditions for recovering the number of HMM components.  
- Builds on prior MoM and spectral HMM work (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) and extends these techniques to the MHMM setting.  
- Correctly positions contribution against EM-based MHMM learning (e.g., Smyth 1997) emphasizing computational advantages.  
- Novelty: applying spectral learning and addressing permutation ambiguity is a significant technical contribution.  
- Computational efficiency: runtime independent of number of sequences, offering major savings over EM for large datasets.  
- Theoretical justification: provides lemmas and a conjecture linking MHMM structure to spectral properties, outlining future theoretical work.  
- Experimental validation: comprehensive experiments on synthetic data and UCI handwritten trajectories show promising performance vs EM.  
- Clear presentation: well-written, organized, and uses helpful matrix notation.  
- Reliance on conjecture: key theoretical claims hinge on Conjecture 1 without full proofs, limiting rigor.  
- Sensitivity to noise: de-permutation step is noise-sensitive and lacks a detailed analysis across noise levels.  
- Limited real-world experiments: only one UCI experiment with ad-hoc cubic feature transform; more diverse datasets and less preprocessing needed.  
- Parameter tuning: insufficient guidance on choosing spectral and de-permutation hyperparameters (e.g., exponentiation range).  
- Comparison baseline: comparisons are mainly to EM; should include more recent MHMM methods.  
- For acceptance: addresses scalability, offers a theoretically grounded EM alternative, shows promising empirical results, and is clearly written.  
- Against acceptance: robustness concerns from conjectural theory and noise sensitivity, limited sensitivity analysis and dataset diversity, and sparse tuning guidance.  
- Overall: strong and potentially impactful paper; recommend accept with minor revisions to strengthen theory, sensitivity analysis, and experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.
