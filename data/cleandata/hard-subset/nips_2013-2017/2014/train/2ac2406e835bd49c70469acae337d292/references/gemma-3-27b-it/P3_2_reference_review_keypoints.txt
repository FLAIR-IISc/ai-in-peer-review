- Overall: Novel, well-written spectral MoM approach to MHMMs that is promising but needs stronger theory and experiments.  
- Core idea: Treat MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transitions and apply spectral HMM learning.  
- Permutation issue: Authors propose a de-permutation mapping using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Related work: Positions itself well within MoM and spectral HMM literature but omits parameter-identification work for mixtures.  
- Novelty: Applying spectral learning to resolve permutation ambiguity in MHMMs is a genuine contribution.  
- Computational efficiency: Demonstrated significant speedups over EM, especially on large datasets.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and derivations (Lemmas 1 & 2) are clearly presented.  
- Theoretical insight: Recognizing MHMM as a special HMM and leveraging existing algorithms is insightful.  
- Experimental utility: Shows usefulness as an EM initializer and initial empirical validation.  
- Theoretical weakness: Spectral longevity conjecture and eigenvalue-separation conditions are underdeveloped and lack robustness analysis.  
- Experimental weakness: Limited comparisons—mostly against EM—and lacks evaluations against other state-of-the-art MHMM methods.  
- Emission models: Experiments focus on Gaussian emissions; results on Poisson, Multinomial, etc., are missing.  
- Real-data concern: Using a cubic polynomial feature transform may obscure true algorithm performance.  
- Sensitivity: No analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., number of eigenvalues Ar).  
- Scalability: Claim of data-size independence is undermined by unaddressed eigen-decomposition complexity for large matrices.  
- Presentation issues: Lemma 1 notation could be more concise; Section 3.2.2 is complex and needs a simplified example or visualization.  
- Figures/equations: Figure 1 needs clearer labels; Equation 7 (Spectral Longevity) and its notation/intuitions are unclear.  
- Results reporting: Table 1 shows only best accuracies; standard deviations or variability measures are missing.  
- Acceptance stance: Reject with encouragement to revise—strengthen theory, broaden experiments, add sensitivity and scalability analysis.
