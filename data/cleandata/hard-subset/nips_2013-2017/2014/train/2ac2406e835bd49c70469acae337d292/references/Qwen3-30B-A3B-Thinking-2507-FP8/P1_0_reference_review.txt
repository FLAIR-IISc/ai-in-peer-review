

# Peer Review: "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

## Summary
This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), a useful model for clustering sequences. The authors make the key observation that an MHMM can be expressed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They leverage this insight to apply existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs to MHMMs, addressing the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation by exploiting the spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The paper provides theoretical analysis showing that the global transition matrix has K eigenvalues equal to 1 (under certain conditions), and proposes a method to recover the permutation and identify the individual HMMs' parameters. The authors demonstrate through experiments that their approach is computationally more efficient than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy, and that it can serve as a good initialization for EM.

This work builds on foundational spectral learning papers for HMMs [1, 2, 3] and extends them to the more complex MHMM setting. The approach is a natural extension of recent work on MoM for latent variable models, but the authors make a specific contribution by addressing the permutation problem unique to MHMMs.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound in its core contributions. The theoretical foundation is well-established, showing the equivalence between MHMM and the HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix (Lemma 1). The spectral analysis (Lemma 2, Corollary 1, Lemma 3) provides reasonable conditions under which the depermutation method works. The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the computational advantages of the proposed method over EM.

However, there are some gaps in the theoretical analysis. Conjecture 1, which is crucial for the algorithm's success, is not proven but only justified with a hand-wavy argument. The justification uses the sample complexity result from [1] but does not properly account for the differences between the mixture model in [1] and the MHMM setting. The authors should provide a more rigorous analysis of the estimation error or at least acknowledge the limitations of their current justification.

The paper also lacks a thorough discussion of the method's limitations. For instance, it doesn't address how the approach performs when the key assumption that each local transition matrix has only one eigenvalue equal to 1 is violated, which could be common in practice. The paper should also discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm to the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the number of states M.

## Clarity
The paper is well-written and organized. The notation is consistent, and the key concepts are clearly explained. The algorithm (Algorithm 1) is clearly described, and the figures (Figures 1-4) effectively illustrate the main ideas. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the experiments, though some implementation details (e.g., how exactly the "spectral longevity" is calculated in practice) could be clarified.

The paper could benefit from a clearer discussion of the practical considerations for applying the method. For example, the requirement that MK < L (where L is the dimension of the observations) is mentioned but not thoroughly discussed. The real data experiment circumvents this by using a cubic polynomial feature transformation, which is a bit ad hoc and might be better explained.

## Originality
The paper's main contribution is the clever application of spectral learning to MHMMs by recognizing the block-diagonal structure. While the spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2, 3] is well-established, extending it to MHMMs with the depermutation approach is novel. The authors clearly distinguish their work from prior methods by noting that existing MoM algorithms for latent variable models don't directly apply to MHMMs.

The paper adequately references related work, including the foundational spectral learning papers for HMMs. However, it would have been beneficial to compare more directly with recent approaches to MHMM learning, such as those using tensor methods [10] or other moment-based techniques, to better establish the novelty of their approach.

## Significance
The proposed method addresses a practical problem: the computational bottleneck in EM for MHMMs when dealing with large datasets. The paper convincingly demonstrates that the spectral approach is significantly faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. This computational advantage is particularly valuable for large-scale sequence clustering problems.

The paper's finding that their approach can serve as a good initialization for EM is practically significant, as it provides a way to overcome one of EM's main drawbacks (sensitivity to initialization). The potential extension to other hierarchical latent variable models mentioned in the conclusions is also promising.

## Strengths
1. The core insight of recognizing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure is elegant and well-executed.
2. The theoretical analysis provides a clear set of conditions under which the method works.
3. The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate both accuracy and computational advantages over EM.
4. The practical utility of the method as an initialization for EM is a valuable contribution.
5. The paper is well-organized and clearly written.

## Weaknesses
1. The theoretical justification for Conjecture 1 is insufficient and should be strengthened.
2. The paper doesn't thoroughly discuss the limitations of the approach (e.g., when the spectral conditions aren't met).
3. The real data experiment uses a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy MK < L, which is somewhat ad hoc and not well-justified.
4. The paper doesn't address how to choose the number of states M (a crucial hyperparameter).
5. The comparison to other recent approaches for MHMM learning is limited.

## Recommendations
The paper presents a solid contribution to the field of latent variable model learning. The core idea is novel and well-executed, and the experimental results support the claims. However, the theoretical justification for the key conjecture needs strengthening, and the discussion of limitations should be expanded.

I recommend **acceptance** with the following conditions:
1. The authors should provide a more rigorous justification for Conjecture 1, either by proving it or by providing a more detailed explanation of the conditions under which it holds.
2. The authors should add a discussion of the limitations of the approach, particularly regarding the spectral conditions and the choice of M.
3. The authors should better justify the feature transformation used in the real data experiment.

## Final Decision
The paper makes a significant contribution to the area of latent variable model learning by extending spectral methods to MHMMs. The method is theoretically grounded, well-motivated, and empirically validated. While there are some gaps in the theoretical justification, the core contribution is strong and the paper is well-written. I believe this paper would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.

**Recommendation: Accept**
