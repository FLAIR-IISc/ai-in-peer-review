

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

## Summary
This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by viewing the MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs and propose a depermutation algorithm to recover individual HMM parameters from the permuted global parameters. The paper claims computational advantages over EM, which requires expensive forward-backward message passing for each sequence in every iteration.

## Strengths
The core insight that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix is novel and insightful. The proposed algorithm addresses a real computational bottleneck in MHMM learning, and the experimental results show promising computational speedups while maintaining accuracy comparable to EM. The idea of using spectral properties to recover the depermutation mapping is theoretically interesting.

## Weaknesses

### Critical Technical Errors
1. **Dimensional Inconsistency**: The paper contains a critical error in equation (1) where the dimensions don't match: "1>M ( T∏ t=1 Adiag(O(xt)) ) ν". The expression should be a scalar (probability), but the matrix dimensions (1×M × M×M × M×1) would produce a 1×1 matrix, not a scalar. The paper's interpretation is inconsistent with standard HMM notation.

2. **Incorrect Limit Statement**: In Corollary 1, the paper claims "lim e→∞ Āe = [ v̄11 > M ... v̄k1 > M ... v̄K1 > M ]" but this is dimensionally incorrect. The limit matrix should be MK×M, not MK×MK as implied by the notation. The k'th block should be vk1>M^T (a row vector), not vk1>M.

3. **Unsupported Conjecture**: Conjecture 1 lacks proper justification. The chain of inequalities "‖Ar −Ar‖F ≤‖Ar −A‖F + ‖A−Ar‖F" is circular and mathematically invalid. The paper's "justification" is incomplete and fails to provide the necessary theoretical support.

### Methodological Issues
1. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout, such as "1>M" vs "1_M" and "O(xt)" without clear definition. In Section 2.1, the emission matrix examples for Gaussian, Poisson, and Multinomial are misstated. For Gaussian, it claims "O(:, u) = E[xt|rt = u] = µu" which is correct, but fails to clarify that this is the mean vector, not the covariance matrix.

2. **Misleading Claims**: The introduction states "the rest of the learning work amounts to factorizing a moment matrix whose size is independent of the number of data items." This is misleading - the size depends on M (number of states) and K (number of clusters), not the number of sequences.

3. **Experimental Setup Issues**: In Section 4.1, the experiment uses only 6 sequences total (2 per cluster), which is extremely small for meaningful evaluation. With such limited data, the results showing "correct recovery" for T=20 are not convincing. The paper should clarify whether the "2 sequences per cluster" means 2 sequences total or 2 per cluster.

### Formatting and Presentation
1. **Figure References**: The paper references Figure 1 and Figure 2 but provides no figures, making it impossible to evaluate the claims about "three-cluster structure" and "spectral longevity."

2. **Algorithm Notation**: Algorithm 1 uses "MK : total number of states of global HMM" which is confusing notation (MK could be misinterpreted as M×K). The paper should use a different symbol.

3. **Definition Ambiguity**: Definition 3 introduces "spectral longevity" without explaining why this particular quantity is useful or how it relates to the problem, making it difficult for readers to understand the significance.

## Overall Assessment

This paper contains an interesting core idea but is marred by multiple technical errors, unsupported claims, and inconsistent notation. The dimensional errors in the mathematical formulation are particularly serious, as they undermine the entire theoretical foundation of the approach. The paper's claim of "computational advantage" is misleading due to the incorrect characterization of moment matrix size dependence.

The experimental results are not sufficiently rigorous. The small dataset sizes used in the experiments (only 6 sequences total in Section 4.1) make the results unconvincing for real-world applications. The paper also fails to properly distinguish its contribution from previous work on spectral learning of HMMs.

While the paper's main idea has potential, the technical flaws are too severe to overlook. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to address the dimensional inconsistencies, provide proper theoretical justification for the conjectures, and clarify the experimental setup.

## Recommendation
Reject. The paper contains critical mathematical errors that undermine its technical contribution, and the theoretical justification is insufficient for NIPS standards. The experimental results are not convincing due to extremely small dataset sizes. While the core idea is promising, the current presentation is not rigorous enough to be accepted.
