- Proposes a spectral/MoM method to learn mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM with K components and M states as an HMM with MK states and block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Uses a spectral HMM learner to estimate global parameters up to permutation, then uses eigen-structure of the global transition matrix to “de-permute” and recover K blocks (columns of A^e cluster into K groups in noiseless case).  
- For noisy data, constructs a rank-K reconstruction from top-K eigenpairs, clusters columns, and introduces a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and UCI handwritten trajectory data indicate speed advantages over EM and usefulness as an initializer.  
- Builds on prior spectral HMM and latent-variable work (Hsu, Anandkumar), and notes the straightforward equivalence of MHMM to block-diagonal HMM, but omits related continuous-emission spectral HMM/PSR and perturbation-analysis citations.  
- Strength: simple, practical idea to exploit block-diagonal structure to integrate MHMMs into spectral HMM framework and recover components via eigen-structure.  
- Strength: computationally appealing since learning scales with MK once moments are computed, independent of number of sequences.  
- Strength: empirical suggestion that the method is faster and can initialize EM.  
- Major theoretical gap: Conjecture 1 (finite-sample claim) is unproven and relies on mixture-model bounds that don’t directly apply to HMMs.  
- The “spectral longevity” K-estimation is heuristic; Lemma 3 assumes strong separation without perturbation-robust proofs or standard eigengap analysis.  
- Identifiability/assumption conditions for successful de-permutation (e.g., separation of stationary distributions or eigenspaces) are not formally stated.  
- Notational and model clarity problems: conflation of probabilities and expectations in observation model, unclear feature map and exact moments/tensors used for continuous emissions.  
- Unexplained MK < L requirement for continuous features; practical implications of needing large feature dimension L are not derived or discussed.  
- Practical failure modes unaddressed: similar component stationary distributions/top eigenvectors, slow-mixing or near-reducible HMMs, and reliance on A alone for de-permutation may be fragile.  
- Algorithmic ambiguity: method requires MK input while K is claimed unknown—no guidance on choosing MK or behavior under misspecification.  
- Experimental limitations: small-scale synthetic tests, incomplete metrics (k-means error on columns), few sequences per cluster, and lack of robustness tests (varying K, M, eigen-gaps, noise).  
- Runtime claims lack quantitative wall-clock comparisons or scalability plots.  
- Real-data experiment issues: use of cubic polynomial feature map to meet MK < L changes observation distribution and may make comparisons with EM unfair; dependency on feature map not analyzed.  
- Presentation shortcomings: inconsistent notation, imprecise definitions, and missing important references on continuous spectral HMMs, perturbation theory, and block-permutation recovery.  
- Suggestions: provide rigorous finite-sample analysis using matrix perturbation theory, state explicit identifiability and spectral-gap assumptions, clarify continuous-emission MoM pipeline and feature choices, strengthen K estimation and compare heuristics, expand experiments for robustness and scalability, and consider de-permutation using both A and O.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — idea is promising but currently lacks rigorous guarantees, clear specification, and strong empirical validation.
