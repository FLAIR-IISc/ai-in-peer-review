

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper presents a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using method of moments (MoM) techniques. The key insight is recognizing that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling the application of existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs. The authors then develop a method to overcome the fundamental permutation ambiguity that arises during MoM estimation, which prevents direct recovery of individual HMM parameters. Their approach leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a depermutation mapping and determine the number of clusters K.

The paper makes several significant contributions. First, it extends the spectral learning framework from single HMMs to MHMMs, a substantially more complex model that has previously been difficult to handle with non-EM approaches. Second, it introduces a clever method for handling the permutation ambiguity through spectral analysis, which is a non-trivial technical challenge. Third, it provides theoretical justification for their approach through Lemmas 2 and 3, and Conjecture 1, which establishes conditions under which their method can successfully recover the correct number of clusters and depermutation mapping.

This work represents a bold conceptual leap in the spectral learning of latent variable models. While previous work [1-3] has successfully applied MoM to HMMs, extending this framework to MHMMs is a significant and previously unaddressed challenge. The authors' insight that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is both elegant and powerful, and their subsequent solution to the permutation problem demonstrates a creative application of spectral properties. This is not an incremental improvement but rather a new direction for learning complex latent variable models.

The experimental results support the theoretical claims. The authors demonstrate that their method correctly recovers the block-diagonal structure of the transition matrix even with limited data (T=20), and show that their approach is computationally much faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. The real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories further validates the practical utility of their method, showing that it can serve as an effective initialization for EM.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a logical flow from problem statement to theoretical development to experimental validation. The connection to previous work is adequately established, and the authors are careful to acknowledge the limitations of their approach (e.g., the need for the spectral conditions in Conjecture 1).

The main weaknesses are minor but worth noting. The theoretical analysis contains a conjecture (Conjecture 1) that is not fully proven, and the experimental section could have included more diverse datasets or larger-scale experiments. However, as a reviewer who prioritizes novelty over methodological perfection, I believe these limitations are outweighed by the paper's significant conceptual contribution.

The significance of this work is substantial. MHMMs are an important model for sequence clustering with applications in music analysis, motion capture, and other domains. The computational advantages of the proposed method over EM (which requires expensive forward-backward message passing for each sequence) could enable the application of MHMMs to much larger datasets than previously feasible. The authors' real-world experiment demonstrates this practical impact.

This paper advances the state of the art in learning latent variable models by providing a fundamentally new approach to MHMMs that avoids the computational pitfalls of EM. It opens up new possibilities for applying spectral learning to more complex hierarchical latent variable models, which aligns with NIPS's interest in novel approaches to fundamental machine learning problems.

Strengths:
- Novel conceptual insight connecting MHMMs to HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices
- Creative solution to the permutation ambiguity problem using spectral properties
- Strong theoretical justification with clear lemmas and conjectures
- Good experimental validation on synthetic and real data
- Clear exposition and well-structured paper

Weaknesses:
- Conjecture 1 lacks a full theoretical proof
- Experimental scope could be broader
- Limited discussion of failure cases

Given my prioritization of novelty and bold approaches, I believe this paper makes a significant contribution to the field. It's not just an incremental improvement on existing work but a genuinely new direction for learning MHMMs. The theoretical insight is elegant, the approach is computationally sound, and the results support the claims. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the core idea is sufficiently novel and impactful to warrant acceptance at NIPS.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. It represents a significant step forward in spectral learning for complex latent variable models and opens up new research directions that other researchers will likely build upon. The authors have made a compelling case for the value of their approach, and the novelty of their contribution is clear.
