## Peer Review for "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using spectral methods inspired by recent advances in Method of Moments (MoM) learning for latent variable models. The core idea – leveraging the block-diagonal structure of the MHMM’s global transition matrix – is insightful and potentially offers a significant computational advantage over traditional EM-based approaches, particularly for large datasets. However, the paper relies heavily on conjectures and lacks thorough empirical validation demonstrating the robustness of the proposed method under various noise conditions. The experimental section, while providing some positive results, feels limited in scope and doesn't fully convince that the proposed method consistently outperforms existing techniques.

**1. Summary of Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The authors address the computational bottleneck of learning MHMMs, typically solved with the EM algorithm. They observe that an MHMM can be equivalently viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. Building on this, they apply a spectral MoM learning algorithm, originally developed for HMMs, and then tackle the critical issue of parameter identifiability due to permutation ambiguity. They propose a de-permutation strategy based on the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, aiming to recover the parameters of the individual HMMs within the mixture.  

This work builds directly on the growing body of research around MoM learning for latent variable models [1, 2, 3] and extends the spectral learning techniques for HMMs [3] to the more complex MHMM setting. The connection to spectral graph theory, used for identifying community structure (analogous to the HMM clusters here), is implicit but relevant. The paper also acknowledges related work in sequence clustering using HMMs [4, 6, 7].

**2. Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The application of spectral methods to MHMM learning via the proposed de-permutation strategy is a novel contribution.
*   **Potential Computational Advantage:** The MoM approach promises significant speedups compared to EM, which is particularly valuable for large-scale datasets.
*   **Theoretical Insight:** The observation of the block-diagonal structure and the subsequent analysis of spectral properties provide valuable theoretical grounding for the proposed method.
*   **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of EM and motivates the need for a more efficient learning algorithm.
*   **Well-defined Algorithm:** Algorithm 1 provides a structured overview of the proposed method.

**3. Weaknesses:**

*   **Reliance on Conjectures:** The core of the de-permutation strategy, particularly the conditions for accurate recovery of the permutation matrix (Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3), relies on unproven conjectures. A more rigorous theoretical analysis or more extensive empirical validation is needed.
*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The experimental section is somewhat limited. While the results are promising, they don't convincingly demonstrate the method's robustness under varying noise levels, dataset sizes, or model complexities. The experiments predominantly focus on Gaussian emission models.
*   **Lack of Comparison to State-of-the-Art:**  The comparisons with EM are reasonable, but a more comprehensive benchmark against other MHMM learning techniques (e.g., variational methods) would strengthen the evaluation.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The sensitivity of the algorithm to the choice of parameters (e.g., the range of 'e' used in the spectral analysis) is not discussed.
*   **Clarity Issues:** The discussion surrounding the “spectral longevity” (Definition 2 and Lemma 3) is difficult to follow and lacks sufficient intuition. Further clarification and examples would be helpful.



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

*   **Quality (7/10):** The paper presents a technically sound idea, but the reliance on conjectures and the lack of rigorous theoretical support weaken the overall quality. The analysis feels incomplete.
*   **Clarity (6/10):** While the initial sections are well-written, the discussion of spectral properties and the de-permutation algorithm becomes increasingly dense and difficult to follow.  More intuitive explanations and illustrative examples are needed.
*   **Originality (8/10):** The approach is novel and represents a significant departure from traditional MHMM learning techniques.
*   **Significance (6/10):** The potential for computational speedups is significant. However, the lack of robust empirical validation limits the perceived impact of the work. If the conjectures hold true and the method can be reliably applied to real-world problems, the significance would be much higher.

**5. Arguments for and Against Acceptance:**

**Arguments FOR acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to MHMM learning with potential computational benefits.
*   Insightful observation of the block-diagonal structure and its implications for spectral learning.
*   Clear problem statement and well-defined algorithm.

**Arguments AGAINST acceptance:**

*   Reliance on unproven conjectures.
*   Limited and insufficiently comprehensive empirical evaluation.
*   Lack of rigorous theoretical analysis.
*   Clarity issues in the explanation of key concepts.

**6. Recommendation:**

**Reject with Encouragement to Revise.**

I believe this paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field. However, the current version requires significant revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, the authors should:

*   Investigate the validity of their conjectures through more rigorous theoretical analysis or additional simulations.
*   Conduct more extensive experiments with varying noise levels, dataset sizes, model complexities, and emission distributions.
*   Compare the proposed method to state-of-the-art MHMM learning techniques.
*   Provide clearer explanations of key concepts and parameters.
*   Address the sensitivity of the algorithm to parameter choices.

Addressing these issues would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its chances of acceptance in a future submission.
