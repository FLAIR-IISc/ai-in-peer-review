

# Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by recognizing that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors extend spectral learning techniques for HMMs to MHMMs, addressing the permutation ambiguity that arises during parameter estimation through spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The paper also introduces a method to estimate the number of HMM components (K) based on spectral longevity.

The work builds upon the seminal spectral learning framework for HMMs by Anandkumar et al. [1,2,3] and extends it to MHMMs, which have been previously studied using EM algorithms [4,5,6,7]. The paper distinguishes itself from prior work by providing a computationally efficient alternative to EM that avoids the per-sequence computational burden of forward-backward message passing.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with well-motivated mathematical derivations. Lemmas 1-3 establish the theoretical foundation for viewing MHMMs as structured HMMs and recovering the permutation. The proposed algorithm (Algorithm 1) is clearly described. The experimental validation on synthetic data demonstrates the method's ability to recover the correct block-diagonal structure even with limited data (T=20). The real data experiment on handwritten characters shows the method can serve as a good initialization for EM. However, Conjecture 1 regarding the error bound relies on referencing results from another paper without providing a complete proof, which weakens the theoretical contribution. The paper would benefit from a more rigorous treatment of the error bound.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined. The figures (Figures 1-3) effectively illustrate key concepts like the spectral properties of the transition matrix and the depermutation process. The algorithm is clearly described, and the paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the results. The explanation of spectral longevity (Definition 3) could be more detailed in terms of practical implementation.

**Originality:** The core insight that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is novel and well-justified. The paper provides a novel method to recover the permutation matrix to identify individual HMM components using spectral properties, which distinguishes it from previous work on MHMMs. The approach is not a simple extension of existing NIPS papers but rather a meaningful application of spectral learning techniques to a new class of models. The paper properly cites relevant work and clearly differentiates its contributions.

**Significance:** The proposed method addresses a meaningful practical limitation of traditional EM-based learning for MHMMs (computational cost for large datasets). The experimental results demonstrate clear computational advantages over EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. The real data experiment shows the approach can serve as an effective initialization for EM, which has significant practical value. The problem of MHMM learning is important for sequence clustering applications, and the paper advances the state of the art in spectral learning for latent variable models.

**Strengths:** 
- Novel application of spectral learning to MHMMs
- Clear theoretical foundation with well-motivated mathematical derivations
- Strong experimental validation on both synthetic and real data
- Demonstrated computational advantages over EM
- Practical utility as an initialization method for EM

**Weaknesses:**
- Conjecture 1 regarding the error bound lacks rigorous proof
- Real data experiment requires a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy MK < L, which may limit direct applicability
- Spectral longevity metric (Definition 3) is not sufficiently explained in practical terms
- Limited comparison with alternative methods beyond EM

Overall, this paper makes a solid contribution to the literature on spectral learning of latent variable models. The core idea is novel, the theoretical development is largely sound (with a minor gap in the error bound), and the experimental results are convincing. The paper addresses a meaningful problem in sequence clustering with a theoretically grounded approach that offers practical computational advantages.

The paper would be strengthened by a more rigorous treatment of the error bound, a more detailed explanation of the spectral longevity metric in practice, and a comparison with alternative MHMM learning methods. However, these are relatively minor issues that don't significantly detract from the paper's overall contribution.

The paper is of high technical quality, presents a novel approach to an important problem, and provides significant empirical validation. It advances the state of the art in spectral learning methods for latent variable models and has clear significance for practitioners working with sequence data.

**Recommendation:** Accept
