## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning the parameters of a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and a block-diagonal HMM, apply existing MoM techniques for HMMs, and then utilize spectral properties to “de-permute” the learned parameters to recover individual HMM components. The motivation – computational expense of EM for MHMMs – is well-stated. 

However, while the idea is potentially interesting, the paper suffers from a significant lack of rigorous validation and feels incomplete. The leap from theoretical observations (MHMM as block-diagonal HMM, spectral properties) to a practical, reliable algorithm is not convincingly demonstrated. My primary concern is the heavy reliance on conjectures and unproven assumptions, particularly regarding the noise sensitivity of the de-permutation step (Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3). The presented spectral conditions feel ad-hoc and lack strong justification beyond intuition. 

The paper references previous MoM work [1, 2, 3] extensively, establishing the benefits of this approach for simpler models, but fails to adequately position this work within that context. How does extending MoM to MHMMs address the challenges inherent in applying MoM to more complex models *specifically*? The argument feels superficial; simply stating an observation about block-diagonality isn't enough to claim methodological advancement. Furthermore, the connection to existing spectral learning methods for HMMs [2] is made, but the novelty of *this* spectral approach is not clearly articulated. Is the proposed spectral de-permutation significantly different from, or an improvement upon, existing techniques?

The experimental section is also insufficient. While the effect of noise on de-permutation is investigated (Section 4.1), it’s presented without quantitative metrics – “correctly recovered” is vague. The comparison with EM (Section 4.2) shows speed advantages, but accuracy appears comparable. Crucially, the experimental setup is somewhat artificial; the consistent sequence lengths and controlled Gaussian parameters diminish the relevance to real-world applications. The real data experiment (Section 4.3), using handwritten character trajectories, is a positive step, but its results are inconclusive. Showing that the proposed method can *initialize* EM better doesn't demonstrate its standalone capabilities. Why wasn't the proposed algorithm evaluated directly on clustering accuracy on this dataset?  The transformation to L=10 via polynomial feature expansion feels like a workaround to force the method to work, rather than a demonstration of its robustness.

**Strengths:**

*   The idea of leveraging MoM for MHMMs is conceptually appealing, given the computational limitations of EM.
*   The observation regarding the block-diagonal structure of the MHMM is valid and forms the basis of the proposed approach.
*   The presentation is generally clear, though some sections rely heavily on mathematical notation without sufficient explanation.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The reliance on conjectures and unproven assumptions, particularly concerning noise sensitivity and spectral conditions, significantly undermines confidence in the proposed algorithm.
*   Insufficient experimental validation. The experiments lack quantitative metrics, diverse datasets, and a clear demonstration of the algorithm's standalone performance.
*   Limited novelty. The paper doesn't clearly articulate how its spectral approach differs from, or improves upon, existing techniques.
*   The theoretical justification feels incomplete, and the connection to existing MoM literature isn't sufficiently strong.
*   The paper spends considerable space establishing the MHMM-HMM equivalence, which is known, instead of focusing on the challenging aspects of scaling MoM to this more complex model.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Against:**

*   Lack of rigorous validation and reliance on conjectures.
*   Insufficiently novel contribution.
*   Inconclusive experimental results.
*   The paper feels more like a preliminary exploration of ideas than a complete, well-supported scientific contribution.

**For:**

*   The concept of applying MoM to MHMMs is a worthwhile direction for research.
*   The paper identifies a potential connection between spectral properties and parameter recovery.




**Overall Assessment:** Reject. 

This paper presents an interesting idea, but it is not sufficiently developed or validated to warrant acceptance at NeurIPS. The authors need to provide a more rigorous theoretical analysis, extensive experimental results on diverse datasets, and a clearer articulation of the novelty of their approach.  The current version reads as a proof-of-concept with several unaddressed challenges. I recommend the authors address the concerns raised in this review and resubmit to a future conference.
