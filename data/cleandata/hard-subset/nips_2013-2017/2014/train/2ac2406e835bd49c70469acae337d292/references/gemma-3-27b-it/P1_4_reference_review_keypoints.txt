- Proposes a novel method-of-moments (MoM) algorithm for learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by treating an MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Introduces a spectral de-permutation technique to resolve permutation ambiguity and recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Proposes a spectral criterion to estimate the number of HMM components (clusters).  
- Provides theoretical support via lemmas and a key Conjecture 1 underlying the de-permutation step.  
- Empirically validates the method on synthetic data and a real-world handwriting recognition dataset.  
- Frames the work within prior MoM and spectral HMM literature (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) and contrasts with EM-based approaches.  
- Claims computational advantages of MoM over EM, particularly for large datasets and many sequences.  
- Demonstrates comparable accuracy to EM while offering faster runtime in experiments.  
- Paper is generally well-written, with clear model definitions and algorithm description.  
- Novelty: extending MoM/spectral methods to the more complex MHMM setting is a significant contribution.  
- Dependence on Conjecture 1: de-permutation relies on strong noise assumptions that are not fully validated.  
- Spectral condition assumptions (eigenvalue separation, robustness to noise) are not thoroughly justified or explored.  
- Experimental scope is limited: lacks comparisons to other MHMM learning algorithms and alternative observation models.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., polynomial feature transformation degree) is not fully analyzed.  
- Some proofs and derivations lack full mathematical rigor and feel hand-wavy, especially around the conjecture.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically motivated, empirically supported, and computationally compelling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on an unproven conjecture, limited experiments, and weaker mathematical rigor.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, addressing noise robustness, expanded experiments, and stronger theoretical justification.  
- Suggested revisions: add noise-sensitivity experiments, compare to more MHMM algorithms and observation models, strengthen/concretize Conjecture 1, improve proofs, and analyze feature-transformation sensitivity.
