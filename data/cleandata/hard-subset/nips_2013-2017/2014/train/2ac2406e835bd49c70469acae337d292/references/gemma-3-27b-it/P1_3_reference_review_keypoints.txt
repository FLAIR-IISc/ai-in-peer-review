- Proposes a novel spectral learning / Method of Moments approach to Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) to avoid EM’s computational cost.  
- Key idea: view an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and recover individual HMMs by “de-permuting” learned parameters.  
- Paper is well-written, cites relevant MoM/HMM/MHMM and spectral-learning literature, and positions itself clearly.  
- Lemma proving MHMM ↔ specific HMM structure is clearly stated and proven.  
- Analysis linking eigenvalues to cluster identification is a strong theoretical insight.  
- Main scalability benefit: MoM approach yields computation largely independent of dataset size.  
- Experiments show preliminary empirical support and a compelling speed comparison to EM; includes one real-data experiment.  
- Reliance on Conjecture 1 for de-permutation is a major theoretical gap and needs rigorous justification.  
- The stated error bound O(1/√(TN)) is insufficiently derived and feels hand-wavy.  
- Criteria for identifying cluster number (Definition 3 / Lemma 3) appear ad-hoc and need stronger theoretical grounding.  
- Unclear sensitivity to noise and unclear requirements for parameter separation for successful cluster recovery.  
- Experimental scope is limited (Gaussian emissions, few settings); lacks comparisons to stronger MHMM baselines and more diverse datasets.  
- The cubic polynomial feature transform in the real-data experiment seems like a workaround; its effect on interpretability and generalizability is unclear.  
- Minor presentation issues: some inconsistent notation (e.g., θ̄ vs θ̂).  
- Section 3.2.1 needs a more detailed explanation of making ĀP block-diagonal via low-rank reconstruction.  
- Section 3.2.2 should clarify practical implications and estimation/control of noise parameters (αk and βj,k).  
- Section 4.1 should explain how the “total error” metric is computed in detail.  
- Section 4.2 should provide a breakdown of computational time for each algorithm step when comparing to EM.  
- Section 4.3 should report how the choice of polynomial degree (cubic) affects results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, potentially impactful method; solid core theory on block-diagonal structure; preliminary empirical support; addresses EM’s scalability limits.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on unproven conjectures; limited experimental validation; unclear noise sensitivity and recovery conditions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions — strengthen theoretical justification (esp. conjecture and noise analysis), expand experiments, and clarify the real-data feature transform and minor notational issues.
