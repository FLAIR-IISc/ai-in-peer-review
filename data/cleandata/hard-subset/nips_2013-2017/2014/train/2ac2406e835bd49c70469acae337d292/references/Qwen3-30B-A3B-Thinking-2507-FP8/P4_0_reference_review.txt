

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing a significant computational bottleneck in the traditional EM algorithm. The authors recognize that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices, allowing them to leverage existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs. The key innovation is their method to recover the permutation ambiguity that arises when applying MoM to the block-diagonal structure, enabling recovery of the individual HMM parameters. The paper builds on foundational work in spectral learning for HMMs by Anandkumar et al. (COLT 2012, JCSS 2012) and extends it to the MHMM setting, which has been previously addressed mainly via EM.

## Quality
The paper presents a technically sound approach with solid theoretical foundations. Lemma 1 establishes the equivalence between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices, which is the cornerstone of the proposed method. The authors develop a novel solution for the permutation ambiguity problem using spectral properties of the transition matrix, supported by Lemma 2, Corollary 1, and Lemma 3. The algorithm (Algorithm 1) is well-defined and logically follows from the theoretical analysis. The experimental section provides reasonable validation: Figure 3 demonstrates that the depermutation algorithm recovers the correct block structure even with small sequence lengths (T=20), and Figure 4 shows that the spectral approach achieves comparable accuracy to EM with significantly better computational efficiency. The real data experiment on handwritten character trajectories (Table 1) further supports the practical utility of the approach as an initialization scheme for EM. The only theoretical weakness is Conjecture 1, which is presented without proof but is adequately justified with a sketch of the argument. The paper properly acknowledges the limitation that MK < L is required for the MoM approach, which necessitates feature transformations in the real data experiment.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is consistent and clearly defined throughout, with appropriate notation for matrix operations (e.g., the use of MATLAB colon notation). The paper effectively uses figures (Figures 1-3) to illustrate key concepts like the block structure and spectral properties. The algorithm description is precise and follows logically from the theoretical development. The authors clearly distinguish between the noiseless case and the practical implementation with noise, which enhances readability. The paper adequately informs the reader about the necessary background for understanding the approach.

## Originality
The core contribution of viewing MHMMs as special HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is novel. While spectral learning for HMMs has been established (Anandkumar et al., 2012), extending this to MHMMs with the de-permutation technique is a significant step forward. The paper clearly differentiates its approach from previous work on MHMMs (Smyth, 1996; Qi et al., 2005; Oates et al., 1999), which primarily rely on EM. The authors properly cite relevant work in the field, including the foundational spectral learning papers and MHMM applications. The specific technique for estimating the permutation mapping using spectral longevity (Definition 3 and Lemma 3) is particularly novel and addresses a critical challenge in the MHMM learning problem.

## Significance
The significance of this work is substantial. MHMMs are widely used for sequence clustering, but the computational cost of EM makes them impractical for large datasets. The proposed method provides a computationally efficient alternative with comparable accuracy to EM, as demonstrated in the experiments. The real data experiment showing the method's effectiveness as an initialization scheme for EM is particularly valuable for practitioners. The paper addresses a well-known limitation in the field and offers a method that could significantly broaden the applicability of MHMMs to large-scale sequence clustering tasks. The approach could potentially be extended to other hierarchical latent variable models, as mentioned in the conclusion.

## Strengths
- Solid theoretical foundation with clear lemmas and proofs
- Novel approach to solving the permutation ambiguity problem
- Comprehensive experimental validation on synthetic and real data
- Clear explanation of the method and its advantages over EM
- Well-written and well-organized presentation
- Practical demonstration of the method as an initialization scheme for EM

## Weaknesses
- Conjecture 1 lacks formal proof, though it's adequately justified
- Limited discussion of failure cases when spectral conditions aren't met
- The requirement that MK < L (for MoM to work) is acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed
- Comparison with EM could be more extensive (e.g., with more parameter variations)

## Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance
**Pro:**
- Novel theoretical insight enabling MoM for MHMMs
- Practical method with demonstrable computational advantages
- Well-supported by thorough experiments
- Clear presentation and well-motivated contribution
- Addresses a significant computational bottleneck in MHMM learning

**Con:**
- Conjecture 1 requires more formal justification
- Limited analysis of the method's limitations
- The MK < L constraint may limit applicability without feature transformation

## Overall Assessment
This paper makes a significant contribution to the field by extending spectral learning techniques to MHMMs. The theoretical foundation is sound, the method is well-motivated, and the experimental results demonstrate both theoretical and practical value. The paper addresses a meaningful problem with a novel solution that has clear advantages over existing approaches. While Conjecture 1 could be strengthened with a formal proof, the rest of the theoretical and experimental work is thorough and convincing. The method's computational efficiency and utility as an initialization scheme for EM make it particularly valuable for practitioners dealing with large sequence datasets. The paper meets all NIPS quality standards and makes a strong contribution to the field.
