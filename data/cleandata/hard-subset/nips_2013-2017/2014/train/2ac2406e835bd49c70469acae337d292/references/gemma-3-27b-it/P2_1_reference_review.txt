## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea leverages the equivalence between an MHMM and a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  The authors build on existing MoM algorithms for HMMs, addressing the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation by exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix to “de-permute” the individual HMM parameters.  They also propose a method to estimate the number of HMMs in the mixture under certain spectral conditions. The paper includes experimental validation on synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating competitive performance and significant speed advantages over EM.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds directly on the recent surge of interest in MoM algorithms for latent variable models [1, 2, 3], and particularly on spectral methods for HMM learning [2].  It positions itself as extending these methods to the more complex MHMM setting, where traditional EM often struggles due to computational cost.  The work acknowledges and cites relevant prior work on MHMMs [4] and applications [5, 6, 7] providing a solid contextualization.  Reference [8] on spectral experts is relevant to the core idea of spectral decomposition.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Applying MoM to MHMMs is a novel contribution, addressing a computational bottleneck in standard MHMM learning. The spectral de-permutation technique is a clever way to overcome the ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation.
* **Theoretical Justification:**  The paper provides a clear theoretical connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices (Lemma 1), which forms the foundation for the proposed algorithm. The spectral analysis (Lemmas 2 and 3, Conjecture 1) offers insights into the conditions for successful parameter recovery.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The authors explicitly address the computational advantages of MoM over EM and demonstrate these benefits experimentally (Section 4.2). This is a significant potential benefit, especially for large datasets.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments, including a real-world dataset, provide evidence that the proposed method is competitive with EM in terms of accuracy and offers a considerable speedup.  The analysis of the impact of noise and data size is helpful.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the mathematical concepts and algorithmic steps.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture Dependence:** The core of the de-permutation algorithm relies on Conjecture 1, which is not rigorously proven. While the justification is reasonable, the lack of a formal proof weakens the theoretical foundation. This makes the reliance on noise conditions less convincing.
* **Spectral Conditions:** The spectral conditions for successful estimation (particularly around Definition 3 and Lemma 3) seem complex and potentially difficult to verify in practice. It's unclear how sensitive the algorithm is to violations of these conditions and how they might be relaxed.  More practical guidance on setting parameters or assessing these conditions would be valuable.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the comparison to EM is useful, the paper doesn't compare to other recent advancements in MHMM learning, such as variational inference methods or more sophisticated initialization strategies for EM.
* **Real Data Experiment:** The real data experiment involves a feature transformation (cubic polynomial) which feels a bit ad-hoc and obscures the direct performance of the algorithm on the raw data.  The benefit of MoM initialization for EM, while interesting, isn't the primary claim of the paper.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a relevant and challenging problem in MHMM learning.
* Offers a novel and theoretically motivated solution.
* Demonstrates significant computational advantages over EM.
* Provides empirical evidence of competitive performance.

**Con:**

* Relies on an unproven conjecture.
* Spectral conditions are complex and potentially limiting.
* Limited comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
* Real data experiment could be strengthened.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field. The idea of leveraging spectral properties to overcome the permutation ambiguity in MoM estimation for MHMMs is clever and promising.  While the reliance on a conjecture and the complexity of the spectral conditions are weaknesses, the theoretical motivation, experimental results, and potential for significant speedup make this a worthwhile paper for NIPS. I recommend **acceptance**, with encouragement to the authors to work towards a more rigorous proof of Conjecture 1 and to explore ways to simplify or relax the spectral conditions for practical application.  Adding a more thorough comparison to other contemporary MHMM learning techniques would also strengthen the paper.



**Rating:**  Strong Accept (with minor revisions)
