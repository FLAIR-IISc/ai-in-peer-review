This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), leveraging the observation that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The core idea is to apply existing MoM algorithms for HMMs, then address the resulting permutation ambiguity through spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters. The authors also suggest a method to recover the number of HMMs.

While the motivation—addressing the computational expense of EM for MHMMs—is valid, and the observation about the block-diagonal structure is reasonable, the paper suffers from a lack of rigorous validation and a reliance on conjectures without sufficient supporting evidence. The presentation, while generally clear, feels somewhat hand-wavy in key sections, particularly concerning the spectral de-permutation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses a relevant problem:** Learning MHMMs efficiently is a worthwhile goal, as EM can be computationally expensive for large datasets.
*   **Novel connection:** The connection between MHMMs and block-diagonal HMMs is a sensible starting point for applying MoM techniques.
*   **Clear initial setup:** The model definitions are standard and clearly presented.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of rigorous theoretical grounding:** The central claims regarding spectral properties and de-permutation rely heavily on conjectures (specifically, Conjecture 1) without a thorough theoretical justification. The “sufficiently small noise” condition is vague and undefined.
*   **Insufficient experimental validation:** The experiments presented are limited in scope and don’t provide convincing evidence of the method's robustness or advantage over existing approaches. The Gaussian emission MHMM experiment only shows accuracy *comparable* to EM, and the real-data experiment primarily demonstrates the method’s utility as an initialization scheme, not a standalone learning algorithm. The experiments lack detail (e.g., specific parameter settings, statistical significance testing).
*   **Overreliance on existing work:** The paper heavily relies on citing [1] and [2] for the underlying HMM MoM algorithm without significant extension or novel contribution *within* the MoM framework itself. The primary innovation appears to be the spectral de-permutation, which, again, is not rigorously justified.
*   **Limited scope of experiments**: The experiment using the UCI dataset appears to utilize a feature transformation specifically to make the problem amenable to MoM, rather than demonstrating the method's effectiveness on the original data.
*   **Vague stopping criteria**: The EM algorithm utilizes an “automatic stopping criterion” without defining it.

**Specific Concerns:**

*   The argument connecting Lemma 2 to Corollary 1 is not entirely clear. The reasoning about the limiting behavior of the transition matrix needs more elaboration.
*   Equation (7) (spectral longevity) feels arbitrarily constructed and its justification is weak. The claim that maximizing this quantity will reliably identify K is a strong one and requires substantial proof.
*   The discussion of the amount of data needed for accurate estimation (related to Conjecture 1) is imprecise.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

*   **Against:** The paper presents an interesting idea, but it’s underdeveloped and lacks the rigorous theoretical and experimental validation expected of a NIPS submission. The heavy reliance on conjectures and the limited scope of experiments raise serious concerns about the reliability and generalizability of the proposed approach.
*   **For:** The problem addressed is relevant, and the proposed approach offers a potentially faster alternative to EM. The connection between MHMMs and block-diagonal HMMs is a worthwhile observation. The paper *could* represent a seed for future research if significantly expanded upon.

**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **rejecting** this paper. While the authors identify a potentially interesting direction, the work is currently too speculative and lacks the rigor and compelling evidence needed for acceptance at NIPS. The paper feels more like a preliminary investigation than a fully developed research contribution. The reliance on conjectures, the limited experimental validation, and the lack of substantial novelty within the MoM framework itself are significant drawbacks. Substantial revisions, including a much more rigorous theoretical analysis, more extensive and well-controlled experiments, and a clearer justification for the key conjectures, would be required for reconsideration.
