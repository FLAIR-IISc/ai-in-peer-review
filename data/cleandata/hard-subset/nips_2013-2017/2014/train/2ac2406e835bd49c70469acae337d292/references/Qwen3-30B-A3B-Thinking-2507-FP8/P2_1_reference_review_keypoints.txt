- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral approach for learning Mixture of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and recovering depermutation via spectral analysis.

- Provides theoretical conditions for the method and demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real data, showing faster computation and comparable accuracy to EM.

- Lemma 1 establishes a rigorous equivalence between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.

- Introduces a novel way to resolve permutation ambiguity using spectral properties of the transition matrix and the spectral longevity metric to estimate the number of clusters K.

- Experiments are comprehensive (noise sensitivity, EM comparison, real-world task) and show the spectral method is computationally much faster while maintaining similar accuracy.

- Demonstrates practical value: the spectral method serves as an effective initialization for EM on real data.

- Paper is well-structured, with clear model definitions, theory, and experiments, and uses effective figures to illustrate key concepts.

- Algorithm 1 is clearly described, aiding understanding and implementation.

- Addresses a real computational bottleneck of EM for MHMMs and has potential broader applicability to hierarchical latent-variable models.

- Relies on Conjecture 1 for the low-rank reconstruction error bound without a formal proof, weakening theoretical guarantees.

- Real-data experiment requires transforming inputs to satisfy MK < L, a non-trivial constraint that limits practical applicability and is under-discussed.

- Lacks a formal computational complexity analysis comparing the spectral method and EM.

- Does not sufficiently distinguish the approach from related spectral learning work or other MHMM learning methods; novelty is under-articulated.

- Spectral conditions (Definition 3, Lemma 3) are mathematically complex and not given enough intuitive explanation.

- Algorithm omits how to choose the "sufficiently large range" for eigenvalue exponentiation, affecting reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: technically sound, clever idea, and practically promising, but weakened by theoretical gaps and insufficient discussion of practical constraints.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Recommended revisions: provide a rigorous justification or explicit statement of Conjecture 1 and its limitations; discuss the MK < L constraint and its real-world implications; add a formal complexity analysis; expand related work to better delineate novelty.

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important computational problem, provides theoretically grounded and practically useful solution, demonstrates empirical advantages, and offers a novel permutation-handling approach.

- Arguments against acceptance: depends on an unproven conjecture, has the MK < L limitation, lacks complexity analysis, and does not clearly differentiate from existing MHMM learning approaches.
