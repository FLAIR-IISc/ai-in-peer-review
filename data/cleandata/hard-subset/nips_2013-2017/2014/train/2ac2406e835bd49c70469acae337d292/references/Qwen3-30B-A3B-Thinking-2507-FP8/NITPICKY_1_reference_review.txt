

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by recognizing that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs and propose a depermutation procedure to recover individual HMM parameters. The approach appears to address a meaningful computational bottleneck in MHMM learning, where EM requires expensive forward-backward passes for each sequence.

**Strengths:**
The paper identifies a genuine computational challenge in MHMM learning and proposes a theoretically grounded approach to address it. The connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is well-motivated, and the depermutation procedure based on spectral properties is novel. The experimental results show promising speed improvements over EM, with comparable accuracy, and the real-data experiment demonstrates utility as an initialization scheme.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Notational Inconsistencies:** The paper suffers from significant notational confusion throughout. For instance, the HMM likelihood in equation (1) uses "1>M" without defining this notation, while the text later defines "1M ∈ RM" (a column vector of ones) but doesn't explain the transpose. Similarly, the MHMM likelihood in equation (2) uses "1>J" without defining J, which appears to be a typo for "1>M" based on context. In equation (5), the authors write "ŌP = Ō P >", which is inconsistent with standard matrix notation—P should be a permutation matrix, so the correct expression would be ŌP, not ŌP^T.

2. **Theoretical Flaws:** Lemma 2 claims "each of the local transition matrices A1:K has only one eigenvalue which is 1," but this is not generally true for all HMMs (only for ergodic HMMs). The paper should clarify this critical assumption. More seriously, Corollary 1 states "lim e→∞ Āe = [v̄11>M...v̄K1>M]", but the rows of Ā^e converge to the stationary distribution, not the columns as implied by the notation.

3. **Conjecture 1 Justification:** The justification for Conjecture 1 contains multiple errors. The authors use "A" to represent both the true transition matrix and the estimated one, creating confusion. The bound "‖Ar − Ar‖F ≤ O(1/√TN)" is not properly defined—the paper doesn't specify what Ar and Ar represent, and the notation is inconsistent with standard spectral learning literature.

4. **Spectral Longevity Definition:** Definition 3 introduces "spectral longevity" as a complex measure of eigenvalue behavior, but the justification for why this specific measure is useful is not clear. The proof of Lemma 3 relies on vague statements about convergence rates rather than rigorous mathematical arguments.

5. **Experimental Concerns:** The real data experiment (Section 4.3) transforms the data using a cubic polynomial to increase dimensionality from L=3 to L=10 to satisfy "MK < L." The paper doesn't justify why this transformation is valid for the MoM approach or discuss how it affects model interpretability. The comparison with EM in Figure 4 lacks statistical significance tests or error bars, making it difficult to assess whether the observed differences are meaningful.

6. **Formatting Issues:** The paper contains numerous typos: "depermutation" instead of "depermutation," inconsistent use of "MK" vs "MK," and incomplete reference formatting (e.g., "A method of moments for mixture models and hidden markov models, A. Anandkumar, D. Hsu, S.M. Kakade, In COLT,, , ," has inconsistent punctuation and missing information).

**Recommendation:**

While the paper presents a potentially useful approach to MHMM learning with computational advantages over EM, the theoretical foundations are flawed, the notation is inconsistent and confusing, and the experimental evaluation lacks sufficient rigor. These issues collectively undermine the paper's technical contribution. The authors should address the notational inconsistencies, provide a rigorous justification for their spectral properties claims, and clarify the assumptions about the transition matrices. The experimental section needs statistical validation and better justification for the data transformation.

Given the numerous technical flaws and inconsistencies that prevent the reader from fully evaluating the method's validity, I recommend rejection. The paper's core idea has merit, but the execution does not meet NIPS's standards for technical rigor and clarity. The authors would benefit from revising the paper with a more careful theoretical treatment and consistent notation before resubmission.
