- Overall: Novel spectral MoM approach for Mixture of HMMs; promising computational gains but theoretical justification incomplete — reviewer recommends weak accept conditional on fixes.  
- Core idea: Treat MHMM as HMM with block-diagonal transition and use spectral methods to de-permute and recover components.  
- Positioning: Well-situated among recent MoM and spectral HMM literature; clear advantages over EM in principle.  
- Novelty strength: Applying spectral de-permutation to MHMMs is a significant and original contribution.  
- Efficiency strength: Method avoids iterative EM message passing and shows substantial runtime improvements in experiments.  
- Theoretical insight: Block-diagonal structure and eigenvalue properties (Lemmas 1–2) provide useful foundations for the algorithm.  
- Experimental breadth: Includes synthetic studies (noise and sample size) and one real-world dataset; compares to EM.  
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written and organized.  
- Theoretical gap: Spectral conditions for de-permutation (Conjecture 1, Definitions 2–3, Lemma 3) are ad-hoc and lack rigorous justification.  
- Spectral longevity: Definition and role of Lλ̃K′ (and parameter γ) are unclear and need stronger explanation and linkage to statistics.  
- Applicability of bounds: Claim about Frobenius-norm bound cites prior work without explaining applicability to this setting.  
- Noise sensitivity: Algorithm appears sensitive to noise; robustness not systematically analyzed.  
- Real-data limitation: Requires cubic polynomial feature transform to meet MK < L, which limits practicality and raises concerns about generality.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No discussion of choices like 'e' range or clustering method for de-permutation and their effects.  
- Key questions: Need intuition for spectral gap condition, clarification of Lλ̃K′, practical selection of 'e', and alternatives to cubic feature transforms.  
- Acceptance pros: Computationally efficient, novel spectral idea, feasible results, clear exposition.  
- Acceptance cons: Incomplete theory, noise robustness concerns, and reliance on significant data preprocessing for real data.  
- Required revisions if accepted: Provide rigorous justification of spectral conditions, analyze noise sensitivity, evaluate more real datasets without heavy preprocessing, and discuss hyperparameter selection.
