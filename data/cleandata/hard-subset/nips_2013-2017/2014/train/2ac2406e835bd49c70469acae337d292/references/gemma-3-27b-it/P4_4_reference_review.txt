## Review of “Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models”

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling the application of existing MoM algorithms for HMMs. A key contribution lies in a spectral approach to “de-permute” the learned parameters, recovering individual HMM parameters and estimating the number of HMMs.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the computational limitations of traditional EM-based MHMM learning and positioning MoM as a potentially more efficient alternative. The connection to existing MoM literature ([1, 2, 3]) is explicitly stated.  The problem of applying MoM to MHMMs is well-defined and relevant, as MHMMs are a useful tool for sequence clustering. The connection made to spectral learning of HMMs [3] forms the basis of the proposed solution. The paper correctly references relevant work in MHMMs [4] and applications [5, 6, 7].  The related work section is adequate, though could benefit from more discussion of recent advancements in scalable MHMM learning, beyond just EM.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The application of MoM to MHMMs, combined with the spectral de-permutation technique, presents a novel approach. While MoM for HMMs exists, extending this to mixtures and addressing the permutation issue is a significant contribution.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations, particularly Lemma 1 establishing the equivalence between MHMM and a specific HMM structure, are clear and well-justified. The proposed spectral method seems plausible and the justification provided via lemmas 2 and 3 builds confidence.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The model definitions (Section 2) are standard and clearly presented. The algorithmic steps (Algorithm 1) are easy to follow.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experimental section provides evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed method.  Comparison with EM, including the use of the proposed method as an initialization scheme, is valuable. The tests on both synthetic and real data provide a reasonable evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture Reliance:** The crucial step of estimating the number of clusters (K) relies heavily on Conjecture 1 and Definitions 2 & 3. The justification is based on a sample complexity result from [1], but a more rigorous analysis of the spectral properties and noise sensitivity would strengthen the argument. The statement “further analysis...is out of the scope of this paper” feels like a missed opportunity to bolster a core claim.
* **Limited Empirical Scope:** While the experiments demonstrate the method's potential, the parameter settings and datasets appear relatively limited.  A more extensive evaluation with varying data characteristics (e.g., different numbers of clusters, sequence lengths, noise levels) would enhance the credibility of the results.  The use of a cubic polynomial feature transformation for the UCI dataset is mentioned briefly but lacks sufficient justification for its impact on results.
* **Missing Discussion of Computational Complexity:** While the introduction claims computational advantages, a detailed analysis of the algorithm's complexity compared to EM (especially concerning the spectral de-permutation step) is missing. Is the MoM computation of moments the bottleneck, or is it the spectral analysis?
* **Presentation of Results:** Figures 3 and 4 lack clear labeling of axes and detailed captions explaining the metrics being plotted. Table 1's description is also sparse.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel and potentially efficient alternative to EM for MHMM learning.
* The theoretical justification, despite relying on a conjecture, is reasonably sound and well-presented.
* The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the feasibility of the proposed approach.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The reliance on a conjecture for a critical component (cluster number estimation) weakens the technical soundness.
* The experimental evaluation is limited in scope and lacks detailed analysis.
* The paper lacks a thorough discussion of computational complexity.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of latent variable modeling. The proposed spectral learning algorithm for MHMMs is novel and theoretically grounded. While the reliance on a conjecture and limited empirical validation raise some concerns, the potential benefits in terms of computational efficiency and the clear presentation of the work make it a worthwhile contribution.  

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** The authors should address the following:

1.  Provide a more detailed analysis of the spectral properties underlying Conjecture 1, potentially exploring conditions for its validity.
2.  Expand the experimental evaluation with a broader range of datasets and parameter settings.
3.  Include a discussion of the algorithm's computational complexity.
4.  Improve the clarity of figures and tables with proper labeling and detailed captions.
