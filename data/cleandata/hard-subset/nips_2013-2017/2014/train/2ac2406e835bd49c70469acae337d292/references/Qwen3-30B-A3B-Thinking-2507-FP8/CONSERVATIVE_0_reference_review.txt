

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by leveraging the observation that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors claim their approach avoids the computational burden of EM by using spectral learning techniques for HMMs followed by a depermutation step to recover individual HMM parameters. The paper builds on established spectral learning methods for HMMs [1, 2, 3] but extends them to MHMMs through a novel (though not entirely new) structural insight.

The main technical contribution appears to be the depermutation algorithm based on spectral properties of the transition matrix. The authors propose to identify the number of clusters K by analyzing eigenvalue behavior (Lemma 3 and Definition 3), followed by clustering columns of the estimated transition matrix to recover the permutation. However, the paper's theoretical foundation is significantly weakened by its reliance on Conjecture 1, which states that the estimated low-rank reconstruction will be close to the true matrix with high probability. The justification provided is insufficient, merely citing a result from [1] without demonstrating how it applies to the MHMM setting. The claim that "further analysis of the spectral learning algorithm is out of scope" is unconvincing for a NeurIPS submission, where theoretical rigor is expected.

The experimental validation is notably weak. The authors compare their approach only to standard EM initialization (with random initialization), not to state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods. The synthetic experiments (Section 4.1-4.2) use very small datasets (e.g., 2 sequences per cluster with T=20) and fail to demonstrate robustness to challenging conditions. The real data experiment (Section 4.3) uses a polynomial transformation to satisfy the MK < L constraint (a significant limitation of MoM approaches), which is not properly justified and may distort the data. The claim that "spectral learning serves as a very good initialization scheme for EM" is not substantiated by meaningful comparison with other initialization methods.

The paper's main weakness is its lack of rigorous validation against established baselines. The authors acknowledge that EM is computationally expensive but provide no thorough comparison of running times or accuracy on larger datasets. The speed advantage they claim is not convincingly demonstrated, as the computational complexity of the spectral approach (including moment computation) is not adequately contrasted with EM. The paper also fails to address the practical limitations of MoM approaches for MHMMs, such as the requirement that the number of states MK must be less than the dimensionality L of observations (a constraint that necessitates data transformation in the real experiment).

The paper's originality is marginal. The core idea of viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal structure has been previously noted in the literature (e.g., in Smyth's work [4] cited in the paper). The spectral learning approach for HMMs is well-established in the literature [1, 2, 3], and the depermutation step, while interesting, is not sufficiently novel or rigorously justified to warrant publication at NeurIPS.

In terms of significance, the paper does not demonstrate a meaningful advance over existing methods. The claimed computational advantage is not well-supported, and the accuracy results (Figures 4 and Table 1) show that the spectral method is comparable to EM at best, with no clear advantage in challenging scenarios. The paper's contribution appears to be a specialized application of existing techniques rather than a significant methodological advancement.

The paper is generally well-written and well-organized, though some notation is confusing (e.g., the inconsistent use of K and K̂). The experimental setup is reasonable but lacks the rigor expected for a NeurIPS submission. The authors should provide a more thorough comparison with established MHMM learning methods and address the limitations of their approach more honestly.

Strengths: Clear exposition of the MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure, reasonable experimental design for synthetic data, and useful insights into spectral properties.

Weaknesses: Overreliance on unproven conjectures, inadequate experimental validation against established baselines, failure to address practical limitations of the MoM approach, and limited theoretical justification for key claims.

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting application of spectral learning to MHMMs, the lack of rigorous theoretical foundation, insufficient experimental validation, and marginal novelty make it unsuitable for publication at NeurIPS. The authors would need to substantially strengthen their theoretical claims, provide more comprehensive experimental comparisons, and demonstrate clear advantages over existing methods to warrant acceptance.
