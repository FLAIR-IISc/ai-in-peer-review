- Core idea: Treat MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and apply spectral learning to recover global parameters.  
- Novelty: Introduces a spectral depermutation method that uses spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Practical significance: MoM approach eliminates per-iteration data-dependent forward-backward costs of EM, making learning scalable to large datasets.  
- Experiments: Well-designed empirical validation showing computational advantage over EM and usefulness as an EM initializer, including a real UCI character dataset.  
- Literature connection: Properly builds on spectral HMM MoM literature (Anandkumar et al.) and MHMM work (Smyth).  

- Main theoretical weakness: Lack of rigorous analysis/proof for the depermutation method in noisy settings; Conjecture 1 is presented without proof.  
- Spectral longevity: Definition 3 is introduced with insufficient motivation and theoretical justification for identifying K.  
- MK < L constraint: The requirement MK < L is restrictive and problematic for real applications; the polynomial feature workaround is ad hoc.  
- Known M assumption: The method assumes the number of states per HMM (M) is known, which is often unrealistic and not addressed.  
- Limited baselines: Comparisons only to EM; lacking evaluation against other MoM-based MHMM approaches (e.g., [9]).  
- Convergence: No discussion of convergence guarantees or conditions for global optimality.  
- Depermutation complexity: Computational cost and scalability of the depermutation/clustering step for large K are not analyzed.  

- Ergodicity caveat: Lemma 2/Corollary 1 should clarify ergodicity assumptions; results may fail for non-ergodic HMMs.  
- Conjecture 1 justification: Cited bounds from mixture-model work are not properly adapted or derived for the MHMM setting.  
- Data transformation concern: Transforming L (e.g., from 3 to 10) to meet MK < L can change data distribution and affect performance; implications not discussed.  
- Robustness of K estimation: No demonstration of spectral longevity’s robustness to noise or different noise levels.  
- Complexity of Ar clustering: The paper omits analysis of clustering complexity for columns of Ar, which could be a bottleneck.  

- Overall assessment: Valuable and practically significant contribution with convincing experiments but undermined by insufficient theoretical justification for the core depermutation step and restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision — requires rigorous theoretical analysis (or clear limitation statement) and deeper discussion of MK < L, unknown M, comparisons, and scalability.  

- Revision actions recommended: provide a proof or clearly state Conjecture 1 as open, analyze spectral longevity and its robustness, discuss/mitigate MK < L and unknown-M scenarios, compare to other MoM methods, clarify ergodicity assumptions, and analyze depermutation computational complexity.
