## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and a block-diagonal HMM, apply an existing MoM algorithm for HMMs, and then resolve the resulting permutation ambiguity via spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  The authors also propose a method to estimate the number of HMMs (clusters) based on spectral characteristics.  They provide theoretical justification and empirical evaluation demonstrating the method’s efficiency and competitive accuracy compared to EM, particularly in terms of computational speed.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the recent resurgence of MoM methods for latent variable models [1, 2, 3], and specifically extends the spectral learning approach for HMMs [3] to the more complex MHMM setting. It acknowledges and addresses the challenges inherent in applying MoM to MHMMs—namely, the permutation problem—and proposes a novel spectral solution. The work is connected to clustering sequence data, a topic explored in [4, 5, 6, 7], but offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM.  The reference to spectral experts for regression [8] feels a little distant, as the spectral method used here is different.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The application of spectral techniques to resolve permutation ambiguity in MoM-based MHMM learning is a significant contribution.  The observation that an MHMM can be viewed as a block-diagonal HMM is insightful.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The authors convincingly argue and demonstrate that their approach avoids the computationally expensive iterative forward-backward steps of the EM algorithm, offering a substantial speed-up. This is particularly important for large datasets.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides Lemmas and a Conjecture regarding the spectral properties of the transition matrix, offering a theoretical basis for their de-permutation approach.  While the conjecture requires further investigation, it’s a valuable attempt to formalize the intuition.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm, showing comparable accuracy to EM, while being significantly faster. The real-data experiment on handwritten characters, while limited, suggests the potential for using this approach as an initialization method for EM.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and the mathematical notation is clear. The step-by-step description of the algorithm (Algorithm 1) is helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture vs. Proof:** The reliance on a conjecture regarding the spectral longevity condition is a limitation. While the justification is reasonable, a more rigorous theoretical analysis would strengthen the work.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental section, while adequate, could be more comprehensive. Exploring a wider range of datasets and parameter settings would enhance the confidence in the results.  The comparison to EM in the Gaussian data experiment is limited to a fairly narrow range of sequence lengths and number of sequences.  
* **Sensitivity to Noise:** The performance of the spectral de-permutation step is likely sensitive to noise in the estimated transition matrix. The authors acknowledge this, but a more thorough investigation of this sensitivity, perhaps through controlled experiments with varying noise levels, would be beneficial.
* **Assumptions:** The method relies on assumptions like each local transition matrix having only one eigenvalue of 1. While stated, the implications of violating this assumption aren’t thoroughly discussed.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The paper doesn’t provide much guidance on tuning the parameters related to the spectral analysis (e.g., the choice of *e* in the exponentiation step used to determine spectral longevity).

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and potentially significant contribution to the field of MHMM learning.
* The proposed method offers a substantial improvement in computational efficiency compared to the standard EM algorithm.
* The theoretical justification and empirical validation are reasonably strong.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The reliance on a conjecture weakens the theoretical foundation of the work.
* The experimental section could be more comprehensive and robust.
* The sensitivity to noise and parameter tuning are not fully addressed.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** with minor revisions. The novelty of the approach, combined with the demonstrable computational advantages and reasonably strong theoretical grounding, make it a valuable contribution to NIPS.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above—particularly strengthening the theoretical justification by proving the conjecture or providing tighter bounds, expanding the experimental evaluation, and discussing sensitivity analysis—would further enhance the quality of the paper.



**Rating:** 8/10 (Strong Accept)
