## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a method-of-moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning the parameters of a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM). The core idea leverages the equivalence between an MHMM and a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  The authors build upon existing MoM algorithms for HMMs but address the crucial issue of permutation ambiguity between the individual HMMs within the mixture. They propose a spectral approach, exploiting the structure of the global transition matrix, to estimate a "de-permutation" mapping and recover the parameters of each component HMM.  They also present conditions for recovering the number of HMMs.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds on the growing body of work using MoM for latent variable model learning (Anandkumar et al., 2013, 2015). It acknowledges the limitations of EM for MHMMs, particularly in large datasets.  The use of spectral methods for HMM learning (Hsu et al., 2007) is also clearly acknowledged and forms a key basis for their approach. The connection to spectral clustering and eigenvalue analysis to determine the number of clusters is a relevant link.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a significant problem:** Learning MHMMs efficiently is a longstanding challenge. EM is computationally expensive, and this work offers a potentially much faster alternative.
* **Novel approach:** The combination of MoM with spectral techniques to address the permutation problem is a novel contribution. The observation of MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM is clever and foundational to their method.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper provides theoretical justifications for the spectral de-permutation step, including lemmas relating to eigenvalue behavior and conditions for accurate recovery of the number of clusters.
* **Empirical validation:**  The experiments, though relatively simple, demonstrate the potential benefits of the approach, particularly in terms of speed. The real-data experiment highlights its usefulness as an initialization scheme for EM.
* **Clear presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The equations are clearly presented, and the core ideas are explained in a straightforward manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture-heavy:**  A significant part of the theoretical justification relies on conjectures (specifically Conjecture 1), which are not rigorously proven. While the authors acknowledge this, it weakens the theoretical foundation of the method.
* **Limited experimental scope:** The experiments are somewhat limited. The synthetic data experiments primarily focus on demonstrating the feasibility of the algorithm and its speed, rather than a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods under various conditions.  The real data experiment is also limited to a single dataset and a specific feature transformation.
* **Sensitivity to noise:** The performance of the spectral de-permutation appears highly sensitive to noise levels, as evidenced by the conditions required for accurate recovery (specifically regarding the relative magnitudes of eigenvalues). This is a crucial point that deserves further investigation.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The practical applicability of the approach is unclear. The choice of 'e' in the spectral analysis and the specific criteria for determining K require careful tuning, and the paper doesn't provide clear guidelines.
* **Lack of comparison to other initialization schemes:** The real data experiment compares to random and MoM initialization for EM, but doesn't compare to other common initialization techniques like k-means.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, *given the reliance on conjectures*. The theoretical analysis, while not complete, is a strong attempt to justify the proposed method.  The empirical validation is reasonable but could be more extensive.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.
* **Originality:** The combination of MoM and spectral techniques for MHMM learning is novel.  The approach builds on existing work but offers a distinct contribution.
* **Significance:**  If the conjectures hold and the method proves robust in practice, this could be a significant contribution to the field.  The potential for faster MHMM learning, especially in large-scale applications, is substantial.  



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses an important problem with a novel approach.
* Provides theoretical justification (albeit with some reliance on conjectures).
* Demonstrates promising results in experiments.
* Could lead to significant performance improvements in MHMM learning.

**Con:**

* Heavily relies on unproven conjectures.
* Limited experimental evaluation.
* Potential sensitivity to noise levels.
* Lack of detailed guidance on parameter tuning.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper presents a promising approach to a challenging problem. The combination of MoM and spectral techniques is novel and potentially significant. However, the reliance on conjectures and the limited experimental scope raise concerns. I would rate this as a **weak accept**.  I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the following points:

1. **Strengthen the theoretical justification:**  Prioritize attempting to prove Conjecture 1 or, at the very least, provide a more detailed analysis of its assumptions and limitations.
2. **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Include more comprehensive experiments with varying noise levels, dataset sizes, and different types of data. Compare to other initialization techniques for EM.
3. **Provide guidance on parameter tuning:**  Offer more concrete recommendations for choosing appropriate parameters for the spectral analysis.
4. **Discuss limitations:**  More openly discuss the limitations of the approach, particularly its sensitivity to noise and potential issues with scalability to very high-dimensional data.
