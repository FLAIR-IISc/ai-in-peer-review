

# Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by leveraging spectral learning techniques. The authors recognize that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, and they build on existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs [1,2,3] to develop a novel method for MHMMs. The key innovation is their depermutation approach that uses spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters from the permuted global parameters obtained via standard MoM.

## Strengths

The paper makes a clear theoretical contribution by establishing the equivalence between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices (Lemma 1), which is a crucial insight that enables the application of existing spectral learning techniques. The authors provide a well-structured algorithm (Algorithm 1) that addresses the permutation ambiguity issue through spectral properties of the global transition matrix. This is a significant practical contribution, as the permutation problem is a fundamental challenge in applying MoM methods to MHMMs.

The experimental section is thorough and demonstrates the practical value of the approach. The authors show that their spectral method is computationally much faster than EM while achieving comparable accuracy (Figure 4), and they demonstrate that their method can serve as an effective initialization for EM (Table 1). The real data experiment on the handwritten character trajectory dataset provides valuable empirical validation.

The paper also makes a useful connection to prior work, particularly citing the spectral learning literature for HMMs [1,2,3] and providing appropriate context for their contribution to the MHMM learning problem. The problem statement is well-motivated, highlighting the computational limitations of EM for large datasets.

## Weaknesses

The theoretical analysis contains some significant gaps that undermine the paper's technical contribution. Conjecture 1, which is central to the proposed method, lacks a proper proof and relies on a reference to a different model (mixture models) without justification for the applicability to MHMMs. The authors acknowledge this by labeling it a conjecture, but the main algorithm depends on this unproven result. The justification provided is too vague and does not adequately address the spectral properties under noise.

The conditions for the spectral longevity approach (Lemma 3) are not fully justified. The paper states that "if the noise is sufficiently small" the method works, but it doesn't provide clear conditions for what constitutes "sufficiently small" noise or how this relates to practical applications. The proof sketch for Lemma 3 is incomplete and relies on assumptions not clearly stated.

The real data experiment has a notable limitation: the authors transform the data with a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy the requirement that MK < L for MoM learning. This transformation may significantly alter the underlying data structure, making it difficult to interpret the results in terms of the original problem. The paper does not discuss how this transformation affects the performance compared to the original data.

The paper doesn't adequately compare with other spectral learning approaches for mixture models. For example, recent work on tensor decomposition for mixture models [10] might offer a more direct comparison point, but this is not addressed.

## Overall Assessment

The paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable approach to MHMM learning, with strong empirical results demonstrating computational advantages over EM. The core idea of viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is sound and well-motivated. However, the theoretical foundation is incomplete, and the unproven conjectures weaken the technical contribution.

The significance of the work is moderate - it addresses a practical problem in sequence clustering with a computationally efficient method, but the theoretical gaps limit its impact as a fundamental contribution to the field. The paper would be stronger with a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a clearer discussion of limitations.

## Recommendation

I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper makes a valuable practical contribution to MHMM learning with a sound algorithmic approach, but the theoretical weaknesses prevent it from being a strong NIPS contribution. The authors should address the theoretical gaps (particularly proving Conjecture 1 or providing stronger justification), clarify the conditions under which the method works, and address the data transformation issue in the real data experiment.

Specifically, the authors should:
1. Provide a rigorous proof or stronger justification for Conjecture 1
2. Clarify the noise conditions under which the depermutation method works
3. Discuss the limitations of the method when spectral conditions aren't met
4. Address the data transformation issue in the real data experiment, perhaps by comparing with other approaches that don't require such transformations

The paper is well-written and well-motivated, and with these improvements, it could be a strong contribution to the field.
