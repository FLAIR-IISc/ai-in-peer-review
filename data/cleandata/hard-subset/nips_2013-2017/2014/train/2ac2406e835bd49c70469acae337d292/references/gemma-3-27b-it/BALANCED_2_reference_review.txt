This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, applying an existing MoM algorithm for HMMs, and then using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to “de-permute” the parameters to recover the individual HMMs. The paper addresses a significant practical challenge – the computational cost of EM for MHMMs – and offers a potentially attractive alternative.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Learning MHMMs efficiently is a longstanding challenge. The EM algorithm, while widely used, can be computationally prohibitive for large datasets. This paper tackles this directly.
* **Novel Approach:** The combination of MoM, leveraging the block-diagonal structure, and spectral de-permutation is a clever and novel approach. It provides a different perspective on MHMM learning.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a reasonable theoretical justification for the approach, linking it to spectral properties and establishing conditions under which the de-permutation can be successfully performed. Lemmas 1 & 2 are well stated and contribute to understanding the method.
* **Experimental Validation:**  The experiments, while not extensive, demonstrate the potential of the proposed algorithm. The comparison with EM in terms of speed and accuracy is valuable. The real data experiment, though with a preprocessing step, further supports the viability of the approach, especially as an initialization method.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The model definitions are clear, and the algorithm is presented in a step-by-step manner.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Spectral Conditions are Strong:** The spectral conditions required for successful de-permutation (specifically, the longevity criteria in Definition 3) appear quite strong and may not hold in many practical scenarios. The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t adequately discuss its implications or potential mitigation strategies.  A more detailed sensitivity analysis regarding noise and the practical applicability of these conditions would be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental section feels somewhat limited. The comparison with EM is primarily focused on synthetic data. Expanding the experiments to more diverse real-world datasets (without requiring significant preprocessing) would significantly strengthen the paper.
* **Lack of Comparison with Other MoM Approaches:** While the paper cites MoM work, it doesn't discuss or compare against any recent MoM approaches tailored for mixture models or related latent variable models.  Is this approach competitive with or superior to other MoM-based methods?
* **Computational Complexity Analysis:** While the paper claims computational advantages, a more formal analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, compared to EM, would be valuable.  Specifically, how does the cost scale with the number of sequences, the length of sequences, the number of HMMs, and the number of hidden states?
* **Justification for Conjecture 1:** The justification for Conjecture 1, while intuitive, could be more rigorous. The reasoning relies on bounds that might be loose and doesn’t fully explain why the error term scales as O(1/sqrt(TN)).
* **Preprocessing in Real Data Experiment:** The cubic polynomial feature transformation applied to the handwritten character data seems somewhat ad-hoc and obscures the direct applicability of the method. A more direct application to the raw data, even with potentially lower accuracy, would be more convincing.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and potentially impactful approach to a challenging problem.
* Clear theoretical justification and well-defined algorithm.
* Promising experimental results demonstrating computational advantages.
* The work aligns well with the NIPS focus on machine learning theory and algorithms.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Strong assumptions about the spectral properties of the transition matrix may limit practical applicability.
* Limited scope of experimental validation.
* Lack of comparison to other related MoM approaches.
* The theoretical analysis, particularly for Conjecture 1, could be more rigorous.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of latent variable model learning. The proposed MoM-based algorithm for MHMMs is a novel and potentially efficient alternative to the traditional EM approach. However, the strong spectral conditions required for successful de-permutation and the limited scope of experimental validation raise concerns. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by:

1. **Relaxing Spectral Conditions:** Explore strategies to mitigate the impact of noise and relax the spectral conditions. Could robust estimation techniques be incorporated?
2. **Expanding Experiments:**  Include more diverse real-world datasets and compare against other relevant MoM-based methods.
3. **Providing Complexity Analysis:** Provide a formal analysis of the computational complexity.
4. **Strengthening Theoretical Analysis:**  Provide a more rigorous justification for Conjecture 1.
5. **Investigating Preprocessing Sensitivity:** Analyze the sensitivity of the algorithm to preprocessing steps and attempt a more direct application to the raw data.

Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its likelihood of acceptance in a future submission.
