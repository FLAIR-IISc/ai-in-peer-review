- Summary: Proposes spectral/MoM method to learn MHMMs by treating them as a single HMM with MK states and de-permuting block-diagonal structure via spectral properties and column clustering; includes heuristic “spectral longevity” for estimating K.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on spectral HMM/MoM literature (Hsu, Anandkumar) and classical EM for MHMMs; block-diagonal equivalence is known, novelty is the explicit de-permutation and K heuristic.

Strengths:
- Simple, intuitive recasting of MHMM as block-diagonal HMM enabling spectral learning and potential scalability over EM.
- De-permutation insight: powers of transition matrix yield per-block stationary patterns whose column clustering recovers block structure.
- Clear algorithmic presentation and empirical speed advantage over EM; spectral estimates useful as EM initializers.
- Addresses sequence clustering with experiments on synthetic and real data.

Weaknesses:
- Theory is largely heuristic: Conjecture 1 unproven and key perturbation/stability analyses are missing.
- Spectral longevity K-estimator is ad hoc and lacks justification against standard criteria (eigengap, information criteria).
- Identifiability and regularity assumptions (O, ergodicity/aperiodicity, eigenvalue separation) are not clearly stated or verified.
- Requirement MK < L is a serious practical limitation; polynomial feature lifting workaround is unconvincing and may distort models.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: small-scale synthetics (K≤5, M≤4), missing parameter estimation error, permutation recovery accuracy, robustness tests, and systematic K estimation validation.
- Baselines limited to EM (4 restarts); missing comparisons to other scalable methods (e.g., variational EM).
- Citations incomplete and related work on spectral mixtures/block-structured HMMs under-discussed; inappropriate reuse of mixture sample complexity bounds.

Quality:
- Block-diagonal equivalence and noiseless analysis are correct, but noisy-case claims lack rigorous perturbation bounds (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin not applied).
- Empirical results show promising speedups but mixed accuracy, sometimes worse than EM and data-size dependent.

Clarity:
- Generally well-written and organized with a clear high-level algorithm.
- Spectral longevity description is hard to parse/motivate; MoM step for continuous emissions lacks sufficient reproducibility details.

Originality:
- Main novelty is the de-permutation via clustering a rank-K reconstruction and the heuristic K estimator; ideas are evolutionary and need rigorous guarantees to strengthen novelty.

Significance:
- Potentially valuable alternative/initializer to EM if solid theoretical guarantees and larger-scale empirical validation are provided; current impact limited by missing theory and modest experiments.

Questions for authors:
- What exact identifiability conditions on O and A are required for MoM with continuous emissions; must O be full column rank and how ensured?
- Can you provide perturbation bounds (e.g., Davis–Kahan) relating sample size, eigenvalue separation, and permutation/cluster recovery error?
- How sensitive is de-permutation to small spectral gaps or near-reducible Ak; include stress tests varying gaps and noise?
- Why not use eigengap or information criteria to select K; compare longevity to those baselines?
- Can the approach handle L < MK without feature lifting, e.g., via multi-view constructions or random projections?
- Please report parameter recovery error and cluster assignment accuracy on larger synthetic datasets.

Pros for acceptance:
- Addresses an important problem with potential computational savings.
- Clear, implementable algorithm and useful EM initializer.
- Insightful de-permutation mechanism leveraging HMM spectral structure.

Cons for acceptance:
- Central theoretical claims lack proofs and rest on a conjecture.
- Heuristic, opaque K selection without comparisons to standard methods.
- Limited experiments and unresolved practical constraint MK ≥ L.
- Incomplete related work and references.

Recommendation:
- Weak reject: promising idea but needs stronger theoretical guarantees, broader experiments (including K selection and robustness), and clearer treatment of continuous-emission MoM and the L ≥ MK constraint.
