- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by viewing an MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.
- Main contribution: de-permutation method to recover individual HMM components from global parameters using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.
- Provides conditions and a procedure to estimate the number of clusters K from data.
- Validated on synthetic data (robust to noise and data amount) and a real-world handwritten character trajectories experiment.
- Builds on prior MoM/spectral HMM work and cites relevant MHMM literature.
- Novel theoretical insight: MHMMs as block-diagonal HMMs enabling application of spectral HMM techniques.
- Practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) that avoids iterative EM forward-backward computations and targets computational bottlenecks.
- Offers theoretical justification via Lemmas 1–3 and Conjecture 1 analyzing spectral properties.
- Experimental section is comprehensive, comparing clustering accuracy, speed vs EM, and effect of data size; shows utility as EM initialization.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes helpful illustrative figures.
- Conjecture 1 lacks a formal proof and relies on heuristic justification and references rather than a rigorous derivation.
- Method requires MK < L (hidden states times clusters less than observation dimension), which is restrictive and forced a feature transform in the real experiment.
- No detailed computational complexity analysis comparing spectral method and EM is provided.
- Real-world validation is limited (only K=2), so scalability to larger numbers of clusters is not demonstrated.
- Missing comparisons to more recent MHMM learning methods (e.g., recent variational approaches) beyond EM.
- Recommendation: accept — novel, well-executed approach with strengths outweighing weaknesses.
- Suggested improvements: rigorously justify Conjecture 1, discuss/extend beyond MK < L, add formal complexity analysis, include larger-K real-world experiments, and compare with recent MHMM methods.
- Final verdict: high quality, clarity, originality, and significance; suitable for NIPS.
