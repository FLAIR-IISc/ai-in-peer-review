

# Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The authors establish that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, leveraging this insight to apply existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs. The key contribution lies in addressing the permutation ambiguity that arises when applying standard spectral learning to this augmented HMM, and developing a method to recover the original MHMM parameters through spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The paper also introduces a technique for estimating the number of HMM components under certain spectral conditions.

The work builds upon spectral learning approaches for HMMs (Anandkumar et al., COLT 2012; Hsu et al., Journal of Computer and System Sciences 2012) and extends them to the more complex MHMM setting. It relates to prior MHMM work (Smyth, NIPS 1997) that has primarily relied on EM, which suffers from computational bottlenecks when dealing with large datasets.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with clear theoretical foundations. The key Lemma 1 establishing the equivalence between MHMM and block-diagonal HMM is correctly proven. The spectral analysis in Lemmas 2 and 3, along with Conjecture 1, provides a solid theoretical basis for the depermutation algorithm. The experimental results support the theoretical claims, demonstrating that the method can correctly recover the number of clusters and parameters even with limited data (T=20 in Figure 3). The real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories shows practical utility, particularly as an initialization method for EM. The authors carefully address the limitations of their approach, noting the spectral conditions required for the depermutation algorithm to work. The paper is complete and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The model definitions in Section 2 are precise and well-motivated. The theoretical development flows logically from the block-diagonal representation to the spectral properties and depermutation algorithm. The pseudocode in Algorithm 1 clearly summarizes the proposed method. The figures (Figures 1-3) effectively illustrate key concepts. Some minor improvements could be made to the exposition of the spectral conditions (Definition 3 and Lemma 3), which could be made more intuitive for readers less familiar with spectral analysis.

**Originality:** The paper makes a significant contribution by extending spectral learning methods to MHMMs. The key insight of representing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix is novel and well-justified. The method to recover the permutation using spectral properties of the global transition matrix is a non-trivial extension that addresses a fundamental challenge in applying spectral methods to MHMMs. The paper clearly differentiates itself from prior work on MHMMs (Smyth, NIPS 1997) and spectral learning for HMMs (Anandkumar et al., COLT 2012). The approach represents a novel combination of familiar techniques rather than a simple extension.

**Significance:** The proposed method addresses a significant computational bottleneck in MHMM learning, as EM requires expensive forward-backward message passing for each sequence in every iteration. The experimental results demonstrate that the spectral approach is computationally much cheaper than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. The real data experiment shows the method can serve as a valuable initialization scheme for EM. The paper's approach could potentially be extended to other hierarchical latent variable models, as noted in the conclusion. The results represent a demonstrable advance in the state of the art for MHMM learning.

**Strengths:**
- Novel extension of spectral learning to MHMMs
- Sound theoretical foundation with clear lemmas
- Effective experimental validation showing practical utility
- Demonstrates computational advantages over EM
- Provides a good initialization method for EM

**Weaknesses:**
- Reliance on specific spectral conditions (|λGK| > max_k |λL2,k|) for the depermutation algorithm, with limited discussion of how restrictive these conditions are in practice
- Conjecture 1 is not fully proven, though the authors correctly note this is out of scope
- Limited comparison with alternative methods for MHMM learning that might address computational efficiency
- The polynomial feature transformation used in real data experiment (to satisfy MK < L) might not be ideal for all applications

**Recommendation:**
The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of latent variable models by extending spectral learning methods to MHMMs. The theoretical foundation is sound, the experimental results are compelling, and the approach offers practical computational advantages over existing methods. While the reliance on specific spectral conditions is a limitation, the authors provide a clear characterization of when the method is applicable, and the experimental results demonstrate good performance in practice. The paper is well-written and makes a valuable contribution to the field.

This paper meets all NIPS criteria: it is technically sound, clearly written, original in its approach to extending spectral learning to MHMMs, and significant in addressing a computational bottleneck in MHMM learning. I recommend acceptance.
