

# Peer Review: "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

## Summary

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing a significant computational challenge in sequence clustering. The authors establish a key insight: an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They leverage this observation to apply existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs to MHMMs. However, the MoM approach recovers global parameters up to a permutation ambiguity, making it difficult to identify individual HMM components. The paper's main contribution is a method to recover the de-permutation mapping using spectral properties of the global transition matrix, enabling recovery of the individual HMM parameters. The authors also provide theoretical conditions under which the number of clusters K can be estimated from the data. The approach is validated through synthetic experiments showing robustness to noise and data amount, as well as a real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories.

The paper builds on the growing literature of method of moments approaches for latent variable models (e.g., Anandkumar et al., COLT 2012; Hsu et al., Journal of Computer and System Sciences 2012) and extends these to the more complex MHMM setting. The authors properly cite relevant prior work on MHMMs (Smyth, NIPS 1997) and spectral learning for HMMs.

## Strengths

1. **Novel Theoretical Insight**: The paper's key contribution is the elegant observation that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices. This is a clever theoretical insight that enables the application of existing spectral learning techniques to MHMMs.

2. **Practical Algorithm**: The proposed algorithm (Algorithm 1) is well-structured and addresses a significant computational bottleneck in MHMM learning (the expensive EM algorithm). The paper's approach avoids the iterative forward-backward computations of EM, which is particularly valuable for large datasets.

3. **Theoretical Justification**: The paper provides a reasonable theoretical foundation for the approach, including Lemmas 1-3 and Conjecture 1. The spectral properties of the global transition matrix are well-analyzed, and the conditions for estimating the number of clusters K are clearly specified.

4. **Strong Experimental Validation**: The experimental section is comprehensive, including:
   - A thorough analysis of the effect of data amount on the depermutation algorithm
   - A comparison of clustering accuracy and speed between the spectral approach and EM
   - A real-world experiment showing the method's utility as an initialization for EM

5. **Clear Presentation**: The paper is well-written, logically organized, and uses helpful figures (Figures 1-3) to illustrate key concepts. The algorithm description is particularly clear.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Gaps**: While the theoretical analysis is mostly sound, Conjecture 1 (which is central to the approach) lacks a formal proof. The justification provided is somewhat heuristic and references [1] (Anandkumar et al.) without fully establishing the connection. The paper would be stronger with a more rigorous theoretical foundation.

2. **Practical Limitation**: The requirement that MK < L (where M is number of hidden states per HMM, K is number of clusters, and L is observation dimension) is restrictive. This is particularly problematic for the real-world experiment, where the authors had to apply a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy this condition. This limitation should be discussed more thoroughly, and the authors should consider whether the approach can be extended to cases where MK ≥ L.

3. **Computational Complexity Analysis**: The paper claims the proposed method is "computationally much cheaper" than EM but doesn't provide a detailed complexity analysis. A formal comparison of computational complexity (time and space) between the spectral approach and EM would strengthen the paper.

4. **Limited Real-World Validation**: The real-world experiment uses a small number of clusters (K=2), which doesn't fully demonstrate the approach's utility for larger cluster numbers. A more extensive real-world validation with larger K would be valuable.

5. **Comparison with Recent Methods**: The paper doesn't compare with more recent MHMM learning approaches. For example, there's recent work on variational inference for MHMMs (e.g., from the last 2-3 years of NIPS/ICML) that might provide a more relevant comparison than just EM.

## Overall Assessment

This paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to learning MHMMs using method of moments. The key insight about viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is clever and valuable. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, and the experimental results support the theoretical claims. The paper makes a significant contribution to the literature on efficient learning of latent variable models, particularly for sequence data.

While there are some theoretical gaps and practical limitations, the paper's strengths significantly outweigh these weaknesses. The approach offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM for MHMM learning with comparable accuracy, and the real-world experiment demonstrates its utility as an initialization scheme for EM.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance of this paper at NIPS. The paper addresses an important problem in sequence modeling with a novel and well-executed approach. The theoretical insights are valuable, and the experimental validation is thorough. The limitations noted are important but do not undermine the paper's main contribution.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. Provide a more rigorous justification for Conjecture 1, or at least clarify the assumptions needed for the bound to hold.

2. Discuss the MK < L limitation more thoroughly and consider whether the approach can be extended to cases where MK ≥ L.

3. Add a formal computational complexity analysis comparing the spectral approach and EM.

4. Include a real-world experiment with larger K (e.g., K=5 or 10) to better demonstrate the approach's utility.

5. Compare with more recent MHMM learning approaches beyond EM.

## Final Verdict

**Quality**: High - The paper is technically sound with strong experimental validation, though some theoretical gaps exist.

**Clarity**: High - Well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the key insights.

**Originality**: High - The core insight about viewing MHMMs as block-diagonal HMMs is novel and clever.

**Significance**: High - The approach addresses a meaningful computational bottleneck in MHMM learning with practical implications for sequence clustering applications.

This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of latent variable models and would be of interest to the NIPS community.
