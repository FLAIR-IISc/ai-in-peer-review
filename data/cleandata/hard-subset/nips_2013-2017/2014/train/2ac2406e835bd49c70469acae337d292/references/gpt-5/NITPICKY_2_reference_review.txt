Summary
The paper studies learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using spectral/method-of-moments (MoM) techniques. The key observation is that an MHMM with K HMM components (each with M states) can be represented as a single HMM with MK states and a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors propose to: (i) apply an existing spectral HMM learner to estimate global parameters up to a permutation; (ii) use spectral properties of the estimated block-diagonal transition matrix to “de-permute” and recover the K blocks; and (iii) estimate K via a heuristic based on the decay/longevity of eigenvalues of the estimated transition matrix. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian-emission MHMMs and a UCI character trajectory dataset illustrate speed/accuracy tradeoffs and use as an EM initializer.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on spectral MoM for HMMs and mixtures (e.g., Hsu et al.; Anandkumar et al.). The block-diagonal equivalence is classical. The main novelty lies in the de-permutation procedure and a heuristic to infer K from the spectrum. However, much of the pipeline relies on existing HMM spectral learning methods and standard properties of block-diagonal matrices.

Strengths
- Simple and intuitive reduction of MHMM to an HMM with block-diagonal A, enabling use of off-the-shelf spectral HMM learning.
- Practical de-permutation idea: columns of A^e converge to cluster-specific stationary distributions, which are clusterable; within-block permutations are unimportant for MHMM clustering.
- Potentially faster than EM when many sequences are present; MoM yields data-independent post-moment factorization cost.
- Using spectral learning as an EM initializer is a pragmatic contribution.

Weaknesses and concerns (technical and methodological)
- Reliance on conjectures: The central finite-sample guarantee (Conjecture 1) is not proved, reuses a sample complexity bound from a different model, and omits a perturbation analysis (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin) for eigen-subspace recovery under noise. The paper acknowledges this but does not provide alternatives. This substantially weakens the theoretical contribution.
- Insufficient specification of the MoM step: “HMM MethodofMoments” is a black box. For continuous observations (Gaussian/Poisson), the paper assumes O(:, u) = E[x|r=u] and requires L ≥ MK, but does not specify which observable operators, feature maps, whitening steps, or moment estimators are used. Spectral HMM algorithms differ for discrete vs continuous emissions; the implementation details, identifiability conditions (full-column-rank O, non-degeneracy), and sample complexity are missing.
- Assumptions on Ak not stated precisely: Lemma 2 assumes each Ak has only one eigenvalue equal to 1; this requires irreducible and aperiodic (primitive) stochastic matrices. Without primitivity, A^e may not converge and Corollary 1 may fail. These conditions should be explicit and used consistently (row vs column stochasticity).
- Spectral de-permutation robustness: The method clusters columns of A^∞ (or its low-rank reconstruction Ar) to recover blocks. This will fail when stationary distributions across components are similar, or when the spectral gap separating the K “global” eigenvalues from local ones is small. No identifiability conditions ensuring separable v̄k are stated, nor perturbation bounds quantifying when k-means on columns succeeds.
- Estimating K: The “spectral longevity” criterion is heuristic, depends on an infinite sum of indicator functions, and ignores complex eigenvalues’ phases by taking magnitudes. Lemma 3 assumes an ordering and gap structure that may not hold under noise; there is no empirical ablation vs simpler criteria (e.g., eigengap, information criteria) or sensitivity to γ and the exponent range. The proposed condition arg maxK′ |λ̃K′|^2/(|λ̃K′+1||λ̃K′−1|) is ad hoc and unvalidated theoretically.
- Claim “eigendecomposition is invariant under permutation”: eigenvalues are invariant; eigenvectors are permuted (Pv). This matters for how Ar is reconstructed and clustered.
- Experimental limitations:
  - Synthetic: Very small data (2 sequences per component), limited metrics (k-means centroid distance on A columns), no parameter recovery error, no ablation of spectral gap, similarity of stationary distributions, or noise. Figures referenced but not shown here; quantitative results are vague.
  - EM comparison: No details on exact runtime, tolerance, implementation; accuracy advantage of EM at small data is noted but not quantified; no statistical significance.
  - Real data: The need to increase L via cubic features to satisfy L ≥ MK is a strong practical constraint; no analysis of feature choice sensitivity. Only K=2, M=4 tested. The claim that spectral learning is a good initializer is plausible but only weakly supported; table details and variance across runs are not fully presented.
- Formatting and references:
  - Numerous typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., 1>M vs 1M, colon notation explanations, inconsistent dimensions M vs MK in equations).
  - Footnote marker “1” after Ak not resolved.
  - References are incomplete (missing years/venues/pages; duplicated titles; [9] cited but not listed).
  - Some equations are hard to parse and switch between notations abruptly.
- Scope of novelty: The core block-diagonal observation and use of spectral HMM learning are standard. The main new parts (de-permutation and K estimation) are heuristic and under-theorized.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a fully specified MoM pipeline for continuous emissions, with conditions (rank of O, whitening, moment constructions) and sample complexity tailored to MHMM-as-HMM.
- Replace Conjecture 1 with a rigorous perturbation analysis: show that the top-K invariant subspace of A is stable and that column clustering of the low-rank reconstruction recovers blocks under explicit SNR and separation assumptions (e.g., Davis–Kahan bounds, k-means misclustering guarantees).
- State and use proper Markov chain conditions (irreducibility/aperiodicity) and be consistent about row/column stochasticity; correct claims on permutation invariance of eigenvectors.
- Justify and compare K-selection criteria against baselines (eigengap, profile likelihood/BIC on moments) and provide sensitivity analyses.
- Expand experiments: more realistic data regimes, parameter recovery metrics, robustness to close stationary distributions, varying spectral gaps, alternative emission types, and comparisons to variational MHMMs or Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Clean up notation, equations, and references; include complete figures/tables; fix the [9] citation.

Pros
- Clear and useful reduction to block-diagonal HMM
- Simple, potentially fast de-permutation idea with practical appeal
- Demonstrates use as EM initialization

Cons
- Theoretical backbone is largely conjectural; key steps lack proofs
- MoM details for continuous observations are underspecified
- Heuristic K selection with limited justification
- Experimental evidence is thin and lacks rigorous metrics
- Numerous presentation/formatting issues and incomplete references

Recommendation
Reject. While the idea is appealing and potentially useful, the current paper lacks theoretical rigor, sufficient methodological detail, and comprehensive empirical validation. With stronger analysis, clearer specification, and more substantial experiments, this line of work could be impactful.

Confidence
High (4/5).
