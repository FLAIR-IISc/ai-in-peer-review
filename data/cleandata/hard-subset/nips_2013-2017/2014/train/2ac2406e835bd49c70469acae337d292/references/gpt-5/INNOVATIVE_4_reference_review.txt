Summary
The paper tackles learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) via the method of moments (MoM). The key observation is that an MHMM is equivalent to a single HMM whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, with one block per component HMM. Applying a spectral HMM learner yields parameters up to an unknown permutation over the MK global states. The central technical idea is a de-permutation procedure that exploits spectral properties of the global transition matrix: in the noiseless case, each block contributes an eigenvalue 1, and powers of the transition matrix isolate K column clusters corresponding to the K components. In the noisy case, the authors propose a low-rank reconstruction keeping the K largest-magnitude eigenvalues, then cluster the columns to recover the permutation and thus the individual HMMs. They also propose a heuristic (“spectral longevity”) to estimate K by identifying a stability plateau in the eigenvalue spectrum under exponentiation. Experiments on synthetic data and a UCI trajectory dataset show that the approach is much faster than EM and can serve as an effective initializer for EM.

Relation to prior work
This paper builds on spectral/MoM learning for HMMs and mixtures (Anandkumar et al.; Hsu et al.). Casting MHMMs as HMMs with disconnected components is straightforward, but to my knowledge prior spectral work has not explicitly addressed the permutation unmixing needed to recover component-wise parameters in MHMMs. The de-permutation via eigen-structure of the transition matrix is related to spectral clustering on Markov chains with disconnected components, but its use for unmixing MoM-estimated global parameters and estimating K appears novel.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: The de-permutation idea that leverages the K unit eigenvalues of a block-diagonal chain is simple, elegant, and potentially broadly applicable to other mixture-of-dynamical-model settings.
- Practical benefit: Once moments are computed, the learning time is independent of dataset size; the algorithm is substantially faster than EM in the reported studies and can furnish a good EM initialization.
- Clarity of the core construction: The block-diagonal equivalence and the noiseless spectral rationale are well explained.
- Handles unknown K via a data-driven spectral criterion (albeit heuristic), which is a useful addition.

Weaknesses
- Theory gaps: The most consequential result (robust recovery in noise and the correctness of rank-K reconstruction) is left as Conjecture 1 with an informal bound; no perturbation analysis (e.g., Davis–Kahan, Weyl) is provided to justify eigen-subspace stability or consistency of the permutation recovery. The conditions under which column clustering is guaranteed to recover blocks are not characterized.
- Strong assumptions and edge cases: Success requires clear spectral separation between the K global eigenvalues and all local second eigenvalues, and implicitly that blocks be irreducible/aperiodic with distinct stationary structure. Failure modes (e.g., similar stationary distributions across components, small spectral gaps, or components with identical Ak) are not discussed.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: Mostly synthetic with small K, M, L; one real dataset with a feature map chosen to enforce L > MK. There is no ablation of the K-estimation heuristic, no quantitative evaluation of de-permutation accuracy, and no comparison to other scalable alternatives (e.g., variational Bayes for MHMMs, spectral clustering baselines on sequence features, or recent tensor/HMM extensions for continuous observations).
- Clarity/details: The MoM/HMM step is treated as a black box; it is unclear which variant is used for continuous observations and under what identifiability/feature assumptions. The requirement MK < L is stated but not justified; mapping to higher-dimensional polynomial features alters the observation model, which deserves discussion. Runtime scaling reported includes moment computation but would benefit from a clear complexity analysis in MK and L.
- Notation/definitions: O(xt) is used as E[xt|rt] under different emission families; the connection to sufficient statistic features used by the spectral learner should be made explicit to avoid confusion with likelihoods.

Quality
Technically promising but incomplete. The spectral de-permutation is compelling, yet the main robustness claims are conjectural and the empirical support is modest. Providing a rigorous perturbation analysis and a recovery guarantee under verifiable spectral gap conditions would significantly strengthen the work.

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized. The paper would benefit from: (i) a concise description of the specific MoM algorithm used and its assumptions for continuous outputs; (ii) a formal statement of the permutation recovery algorithm (inputs/outputs, complexity); (iii) a more precise statement of conditions on Ak (irreducibility, aperiodicity) and failure cases.

Originality
Moderate to high. The block-diagonal equivalence is known, but the de-permutation via spectral column clustering and the spectral longevity heuristic for K are novel in this context.

Significance
Potentially impactful. If made robust, this could become a practical alternative to EM for MHMMs and inspire similar treatments for other mixture-of-dynamical-systems (e.g., mixtures of LDS). Current evaluation does not yet establish clear state-of-the-art performance.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Can you provide a perturbation bound (e.g., via Davis–Kahan) that formalizes Conjecture 1 and conditions under which column clustering of Ar yields the true blocks?
- How sensitive is K-estimation to close eigenvalues? Compare “spectral longevity” with standard eigengap criteria or stability across e ranges.
- What happens if two components share very similar stationary distributions or have small spectral gaps? Any empirical stress test?
- Please detail the MoM learner used for continuous observations and the role of features/whitening. Why must L > MK, and how does the polynomial feature map affect identifiability?
- Include additional baselines and larger-scale experiments; report de-permutation accuracy explicitly.

Pros
- Novel, elegant de-permutation mechanism and K-estimation heuristic
- Significant speed advantage; promising initializer for EM
- Clear conceptual contribution that could generalize

Cons
- Key robustness result left as a conjecture; missing guarantees
- Limited empirical validation and baselines
- Some assumptions and implementation details under-specified

Recommendation: Weak accept
Rationale: Despite theoretical gaps and a modest evaluation, the paper introduces a fresh and potentially influential idea for learning MHMMs spectrally by unmixing a permuted global model via transition-matrix eigenstructure. This kind of bold, simple idea is worth discussing at NeurIPS and could catalyze follow-up work to tighten the analysis and broaden applicability.

Confidence: 3/5
