- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Builds on existing spectral HMM learning methods and introduces a depermutation technique using spectral properties to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Provides conditions for estimating the number of clusters K and demonstrates results on synthetic and one real dataset.  

- Key insight: MHMM-as-block-diagonal-HMM is correct and enables use of spectral techniques.  
- Novel algorithmic element: depermutation via spectral properties addresses permutation ambiguity.  
- Experiments show the method can recover block structure with limited sequence length and serve as EM initialization.  

- Relies on an unproven Conjecture 1 (low-rank reconstruction error O(1/√TN)), creating a significant theoretical gap.  
- Spectral conditions (e.g., |λGK| > max_k |λL2,k|) lack intuition and practical motivation.  
- Assumes known number of hidden states M per HMM, which is unrealistic and unaddressed.  
- No detailed computational complexity analysis to substantiate claimed advantages over EM.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (small synthetic sets and a single real dataset) and uses an under-justified polynomial feature transform (L=10).  

- Paper is generally clear and well-organized with effective figures.  
- Needs a fuller description of the underlying spectral HMM learning algorithm for completeness.  
- Experimental and transformation details (e.g., polynomial features) lack reproducibility specifics.  
- The spectral longevity metric (Definition 3) is opaque and needs clearer justification for identifying K.  

- Contribution is a non-trivial extension of spectral learning to MHMMs via the block-diagonal observation.  
- Depermutation technique is the most novel part but lacks strong theoretical justification and comparison to prior permutation recovery methods.  
- Paper does not clearly delineate which parts are novel versus straightforward extensions of prior work (e.g., Smyth 2000).  

- Potential significance: addresses computational bottlenecks of EM for sequence clustering if scalable.  
- Demonstrated computational/accuracy advantages only on small datasets; scalability to large datasets is unclear.  
- Accuracy is comparable to EM but not convincingly better, and no statistical significance analysis is provided.  

- Suggest proving or clearly framing Conjecture 1 and discussing its implications if left as conjecture.  
- Provide intuition/empirical evidence for the spectral conditions and discuss failure modes when they do not hold.  
- Extend the algorithm to estimate M or handle clusters with differing numbers of states.  
- Expand experiments: more diverse real datasets, larger-scale tests, and comparisons to other MHMM methods (variational Bayes, Gibbs).  
- Discuss robustness to imperfect block structure, overlapping clusters, and noisy data.  

- Overall assessment: promising idea with clever depermutation, but weak theoretical foundation and limited experiments; recommend substantial revision.  
- Recommendation: weak rejection pending stronger theory and expanded empirical validation.  

- Specific clarifications needed: correct claim about moment matrix size dependence on MK; properly justify or label Conjecture 1; detail the polynomial feature transform and its impact; discuss effects when spectral conditions fail; address scaling to many clusters or long sequences.
