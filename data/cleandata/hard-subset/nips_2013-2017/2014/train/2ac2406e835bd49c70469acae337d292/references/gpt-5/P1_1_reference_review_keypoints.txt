- Proposes learning Mixtures of HMMs by treating them as a single MK-state HMM, learning it spectrally, then de-permuting to recover K blocks via spectral properties of the transition matrix.
- Uses the limit behavior of powers of the block-diagonal transition to find K rank-1 clusters and a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K.
- Builds on prior spectral HMM and tensor-decomposition work; novelty is the explicit de-permutation scheme and K-estimation heuristic.
- Reduction of MHMM to block-diagonal HMM and noiseless de-permutation intuition are correct under standard ergodicity/aperiodicity assumptions.
- Pipeline is simple, potentially practical, and shows substantial computational gains versus EM in experiments.
- Core perturbation analysis is heuristic and conjectural (Conjecture 1); lacks rigorous bridge between moment estimation error and spectral perturbation.
- The “spectral longevity” K-selection is ad hoc; Lemma 3 glosses over complex eigenvalues and non-normal matrices and lacks a principled eigengap/stability theory.
- Emission model and moment construction for continuous observations are underspecified and sometimes inconsistent (confusing definitions of O and unspecified moments/features).
- Identifiability conditions (e.g., rank conditions, whitening requirements) and exact moment matrices used are not clearly stated.
- Key assumptions for theoretical results (irreducibility/aperiodicity/diagonalizability, distinct stationary distributions) are not explicitly listed.
- Algorithmic details are missing from Algorithm 1: exact moments, whitening, continuous-observation spectral subroutine, and practical clustering steps for Ar.
- Evaluation is limited: small synthetic settings, modest sequence lengths, one real dataset with feature lifting, and no systematic K-estimation or robustness studies.
- No parameter recovery error metrics reported and no ablations on spectral gaps, noise, or similar-stationary-distribution failure modes.
- Theoretical novelty is limited without stronger guarantees; main contribution is a practical de-permutation heuristic rather than deep new theory.
- If made rigorous and validated, the method could be a faster alternative to EM and a useful initializer for EM.
- Pros: simple idea, leverages known spectral tools, computational speedups, practical de-permutation that works in some regimes, useful as EM initializer.
- Cons: theoretical claims are conjectural, continuous-emission MoM unclear, K-estimation ad hoc, limited experiments, and unstated key assumptions.
- Suggested improvements: precisely describe MoM for continuous data, state identifiability assumptions, use standard perturbation bounds (Weyl, Davis–Kahan), and formalize eigenvalue separation conditions.
- Suggested improvements: replace or justify K selection with principled criteria and compare alternatives empirically.
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments across more MHMM variants, stress tests (small eigengaps, varying K and M), K recovery accuracy, parameter error, and stronger EM baselines.
- Clarify practical implications of MK < L and whether alternative feature maps can mitigate this constraint.
- Recommendation: borderline reject — promising idea but lacks rigor and sufficient empirical validation; needs clearer assumptions, perturbation analysis, and stronger experiments.
