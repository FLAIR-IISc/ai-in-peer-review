- Summary: Proposes a spectral MoM method for mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM and de-permuting the recovered MK-state model via spectral clustering of the transition.
- Strength: Clear computational goal — avoid per-sequence forward–backward in EM by using MoM plus a simple post-processing to recover components.
- Strength: Correct formalization of the block-diagonal equivalence and an intuitive de-permutation via spectral structure of the transition.
- Strength: The de-permutation idea (cluster columns of a low-rank reconstruction isolating K “global” eigenvalues) is simple and practically promising.
- Strength: Method applies across emission families where E[x|state] is identifiable (Gaussian/Poisson/Multinomial discussed).
- Strength: Empirical results indicate speed advantages and usefulness as an EM initializer.
- Weakness: Novelty is modest — reduction and spectral ideas are largely known and the contribution is mainly an application plus straightforward clustering.
- Weakness: Core noisy-case claims are left as Conjecture 1 without rigorous perturbation analysis or high-probability guarantees.
- Weakness: Paper assumes permutation-only ambiguity of spectral HMM outputs but does not state or verify conditions; arbitrary invertible transforms would break the de-permutation.
- Weakness: Identifiability assumptions are underspecified — assumptions like single eigenvalue 1 require irreducibility/aperiodicity and complex eigenvalues are not discussed.
- Weakness: The MK < L requirement for full column rank is restrictive; the proposed polynomial feature lifting is ad hoc and may be impractical at scale.
- Weakness: Practical implementation details are underspecified — how to enforce exactly K clusters of size M, whether M is known, and robustness of k-means under noise or complex eigenvectors.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited (small-scale synthetics, single small real dataset), does not test K-identification, spectral gap sensitivity, or compare to stronger baselines.
- Weakness: Runtime claims lack precise numbers and scaling plots.
- Weakness: Writing and references are sloppy in places; longevity criterion is hard to parse and implementation details are missing.
- Quality: Noiseless analysis is sound, but main contributions rely on noisy-case behavior without rigorous guarantees and likely incorrect permutation-only assumption.
- Clarity: Generally readable, but key assumptions, the exact spectral subroutine used, and important implementation choices are not clearly specified.
- Originality: Moderate — reduction and spectral tools are known; longevity score is novel but ad hoc.
- Significance: Potentially useful as a fast initializer for MHMMs, but current theoretical and empirical support is insufficient to claim strong advancement.
- Suggestion: Specify the exact spectral HMM algorithm and conditions guaranteeing permutation-only ambiguity, or add an alignment calibration step.
- Suggestion: Replace Conjecture 1 with a perturbation analysis (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin) proving recovery of the K global eigenvectors and successful column clustering.
- Suggestion: Use a standard eigengap criterion or provide theoretical justification for K-selection; justify or remove the longevity score.
- Suggestion: Clarify how to enforce cluster sizes (M per component), state whether M or K is assumed known, and address complex eigenvectors in clustering.
- Suggestion: Expand experiments (larger K,M, systematic K-recovery, gap sensitivity, mis-specified M), add stronger baselines, and report runtimes and scaling.
- Suggestion: Discuss MK<L requirement and alternatives (multi-view constructions, random projections) and complete/fix references and notation.
- Recommendation: Lean reject — promising idea but needs stronger theory, clearer assumptions, and broader experiments to be a solid NeurIPS contribution.
