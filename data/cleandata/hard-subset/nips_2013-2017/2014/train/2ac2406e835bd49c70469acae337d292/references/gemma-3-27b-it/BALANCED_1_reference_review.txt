## Review of “Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models”

This paper presents a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, utilizing existing MoM techniques for HMMs and then employing spectral properties of the transition matrix to “de-permute” the parameters and recover individual HMM parameters. The paper is well-motivated, addressing the computational limitations of EM for MHMMs, particularly with large datasets.

**Summary of Related Work:** The authors correctly position their work within the recent resurgence of MoM-based algorithms for latent variable models, citing key papers [1, 2, 3]. They appropriately link to existing spectral learning methods for HMMs [3] and acknowledge the broader context of MHMM applications [4, 5, 6, 7]. The reference to spectral methods for mixture models [8] is also relevant, although its connection isn't fully fleshed out.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:**  The combination of MoM with spectral techniques to address the permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning is a novel contribution.  The insight that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with a specific structure is crucial for this approach.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The paper clearly highlights the computational advantages of the MoM approach over EM, especially concerning the independence of computation time from dataset size. This is a significant benefit for large-scale applications.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper attempts to provide a theoretical grounding for the de-permutation step, based on spectral properties of the transition matrix (Lemmas 1 & 2, Conjecture 1).  The exploration of conditions for recovering the number of HMMs (Definition 3, Lemma 3) is a positive step towards a more robust algorithm.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments, while not exhaustive, demonstrate the viability of the proposed approach. The comparison with EM, including initialization strategies, provides useful insights. The results on real data (UCI handwritten characters) are encouraging.
* **Clear Presentation:** The mathematical notation is generally clear, and the paper is well-organized, with a logical flow from model definition to algorithm description and experimental evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Conjecture-Based Core Argument:** The critical step of “de-permutation” relies heavily on Conjecture 1, which lacks a rigorous proof.  The reliance on the noise level being “sufficiently small” is vague and requires more precise quantification. The practical impact of violating this condition isn't clearly discussed.
* **Spectral Condition Clarity:** The spectral conditions for recovering the number of HMMs (Definitions 3 & Lemma 3) are complex and not immediately intuitive.  It is unclear how easily these conditions can be verified in practice. The explanation of 'spectral longevity' is also a bit opaque.
* **Limited Experimentation:** The experimental evaluation is relatively limited. The comparisons with EM are not extensive, and the hyperparameter tuning process is not described in detail. The performance gains are not always substantial.
* **Missing Discussion of Limitations:** The paper doesn’t adequately address potential limitations of the MoM approach for MHMMs, such as sensitivity to model misspecification or difficulties with highly complex datasets.
* **Notation:** The use of multiple notations for the same quantity (e.g., θ̄ and Ā) can be confusing. More consistent notation would improve clarity.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a computationally challenging problem (MHMM learning) with a novel and potentially efficient approach.
* The theoretical arguments, despite being based on a conjecture, are well-motivated and provide a plausible explanation for the algorithm’s behavior.
* The experimental results, while not conclusive, demonstrate the feasibility of the approach and its potential for practical applications.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The core theoretical argument (Conjecture 1) is not rigorously proven, raising concerns about the robustness of the algorithm.
* The spectral conditions for parameter recovery are complex and may be difficult to satisfy in practice.
* The experimental evaluation is limited and doesn’t provide a comprehensive assessment of the algorithm’s performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of latent variable model learning.  The idea of combining MoM with spectral techniques is promising, and the paper provides a solid foundation for future research. However, the reliance on a conjecture and the limited experimental validation are significant concerns.

**Recommendation:**  **Reject, with encouragement to revise and resubmit.**

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Strengthen Theoretical Foundation:** The authors should prioritize proving Conjecture 1 or providing more rigorous bounds on the error introduced by the de-permutation step.
* **Clarify Spectral Conditions:**  Provide a more intuitive explanation of the spectral conditions for parameter recovery and develop practical guidelines for verifying these conditions.
* **Expand Experimental Evaluation:** Conduct more extensive experiments with a wider range of datasets and parameter settings. Compare the proposed approach to state-of-the-art MHMM learning algorithms. Investigate the sensitivity of the algorithm to noise and model misspecification.
* **Address Limitations:**  Discuss the potential limitations of the MoM approach for MHMMs and suggest possible remedies.
* **Improve Notation:** Ensure consistent notation throughout the paper.
* **Sensitivity Analysis:** Explore the sensitivity of the algorithm to the choice of parameters used for the low-rank reconstruction (as mentioned in Section 3.2.1).
