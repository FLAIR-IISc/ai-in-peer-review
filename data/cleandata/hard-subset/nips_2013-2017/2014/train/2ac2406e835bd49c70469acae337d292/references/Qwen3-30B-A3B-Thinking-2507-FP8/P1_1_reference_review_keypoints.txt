- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral approach for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by viewing MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Main contributions: equivalence MHMM↔structured HMM, spectral de-permutation mapping to recover components, and spectral criteria to estimate number of clusters K.  
- Algorithm (Algorithm 1) is clearly described and logically follows the theoretical development.  
- Experiments show the spectral method is computationally faster than EM and provides comparable accuracy; it also makes a good initialization for EM.  
- Builds on prior spectral learning work (Hsu et al., Anandkumar et al.) and extends these techniques to the more complex MHMM setting.  
- Theoretical results (Lemmas 1–3) are well-motivated and technically sound.  
- Major theoretical gap: Conjecture 1 lacks rigorous justification and its cited reference does not directly support the claimed bound.  
- Real-data experiment relies on an ad-hoc cubic polynomial feature transform to satisfy MK < L, which is not well-justified.  
- Definition of “spectral longevity” is complex and insufficiently motivated; needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Clarity is strong overall: well-written, consistent notation, effective figures, and reproducible experiment details (some implementation specifics missing).  
- Original insight: novel framing of MHMM as structured HMM and new approach to handle permutation ambiguity.  
- Significance: addresses a real computational bottleneck in MHMM learning and enables scaling to larger datasets; practical value as EM initializer.  
- Strengths summarized: elegant theory, clear presentation, computational gains, good EM initialization, well-designed experiments, strong literature connection.  
- Weaknesses summarized: incomplete Conjecture 1 proof, poorly motivated spectral longevity, ad-hoc feature engineering, limited discussion of limitations/failure cases, limited EM comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing theoretical gaps and better motivation/justification of key choices.  
- Suggested improvements: provide rigorous support or clarified limits for Conjecture 1, give intuitive/examples for spectral longevity, justify feature transformation, discuss limitations and failure modes, and expand EM comparisons.
