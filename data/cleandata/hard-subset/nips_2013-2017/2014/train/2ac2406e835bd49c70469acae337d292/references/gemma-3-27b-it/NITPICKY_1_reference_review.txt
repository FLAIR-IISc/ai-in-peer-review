## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning the parameters of a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM). The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, applying existing MoM techniques for HMMs, and then employing spectral properties to de-permute the resulting parameters to recover individual HMM components and estimate the number of clusters. 

The paper attempts to address a relevant problem – the computational cost of EM for MHMMs, particularly with large datasets. The motivation is clear and the connection to existing MoM literature is well-established. However, while the core idea is interesting, the paper suffers from a multitude of issues, ranging from lack of rigorous justification to insufficient experimental detail and presentation inconsistencies, ultimately hindering its potential contribution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Problem Relevance:** The computational inefficiency of EM for MHMMs is a well-known issue.
*   **Novelty (Conceptual):** Applying MoM to MHMMs and leveraging spectral properties for de-permutation is a conceptually novel approach.
*   **Clear Model Definitions:** The mathematical definitions of HMMs and MHMMs are standard and clearly presented.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Rigorous Justification:** The leap from the "spectral conditions" outlined for recovering the number of clusters to actual implementability is significant. Conjecture 1, while intuitively reasonable, lacks sufficient theoretical grounding and relies on sample complexity results from another work without proper adaptation or justification in this context. The spectral longevity definition (Definition 3) appears ad-hoc and its connection to practical estimation is unclear.
*   **Insufficient Experimental Detail:** The experiments lack crucial detail.  Specifically:
    *   **Real Data Transformation:**  The use of a cubic polynomial feature transformation on the handwritten character data is unexplained beyond stating it's necessary to meet dimension requirements.  The impact of this transformation on the interpretability and validity of results is completely ignored.
    *   **Hyperparameter Selection:** No discussion of hyperparameter choices, such as the range of 'e' values for exponentiating eigenvalues or the parameters used for the k-means clustering in the first experiment.
    *   **Statistical Significance:**  Results are presented as single data points without error bars or statistical tests.  Differences between algorithms are asserted but not substantiated.
    *   **Baseline Comparison:** While EM is mentioned, the experimental setup lacks a rigorous, fair comparison (e.g., reporting running times for both algorithms with consistent stopping criteria, and standard deviations over multiple runs).
*   **Presentation and Notation Inconsistencies:** The paper suffers from numerous inconsistencies in notation. For example, θ̄ is defined as global parameters but later used in equations without clear component definitions.  Variable indexing is often unclear (e.g., the use of ‘k’ for both cluster index and loop index).  MATLAB notation is used inconsistently (e.g., O(xt) which is not standard MATLAB). The figure captions are often vague or don't fully explain the illustrated content.
*   **Overly Optimistic Claims:** The claim that the proposed approach is "computationally much cheaper than EM" requires concrete substantiation with running times in the experimental section, which is missing.
*   **Missing Proofs and Lemmas:** Lemma 1 is stated without justification for the equality sign, and relies heavily on matrix notation without clear explanations. Lemma 2, while potentially correct, would benefit from a more detailed proof.
*   **Vague Discussion of Noise:** The impact of noise on the spectral decomposition is discussed conceptually, but the sensitivity of the algorithm to noise levels isn't explored experimentally.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Potentially provides a computationally efficient alternative to EM for MHMM learning.
*   Integrates ideas from spectral graph theory and MoM, offering a fresh perspective.

**Con:**

*   Lack of rigorous theoretical justification for key claims.
*   Insufficient experimental detail and analysis to support the authors' conclusions.
*   Numerous presentation and notation inconsistencies.
*   Overly optimistic claims without concrete evidence.
*   The proposed algorithm is not thoroughly validated.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting idea, but its execution is significantly flawed. The lack of theoretical rigor, insufficient experimental detail, and presentation issues undermine its credibility. While the authors identify a relevant problem and propose a potentially promising approach, the paper, in its current form, does not meet the standards of NIPS.  

**Recommendation:** **Reject**.  

The authors need to significantly strengthen the theoretical foundations, provide a comprehensive experimental evaluation with detailed comparisons to established methods, and address the numerous presentation issues before this work would be suitable for NIPS.  A major revision, incorporating these suggestions, would be necessary for reconsideration.
