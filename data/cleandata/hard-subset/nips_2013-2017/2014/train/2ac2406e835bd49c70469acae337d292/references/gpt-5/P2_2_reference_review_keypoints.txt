- Paper proposes learning MHMMs by reducing to an MK-state HMM and using spectral/MoM methods with an eigen-structure de-permutation and a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K.

- Reduction MHMM → block-diagonal HMM is correct and useful; noiseless eigen-based de-permutation is sound.

- Noisy/realistic case depends on an unproven Conjecture 1 and a heuristic longevity metric without formal justification.

- No perturbation bounds (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin) are provided to guarantee eigenvalue separation or correct clustering under noise.

- The “HMM MethodofMoments” is treated as a black box; moment computation details (features, windows, continuous emissions) are missing.

- Identifiability conditions (e.g., requirement L ≥ MK) are stated but not justified or motivated.

- The de-permutation clustering step (distance metric, normalization, sensitivity to eigen-gap) is underspecified.

- Synthetic experiments are small-scale, qualitative, and do not quantify permutation recovery or robustness to noise/eigen-gap.

- EM comparison is limited (few restarts) and stronger baselines (H3M/variational MHMMs, better initializations) are not evaluated.

- The real-data experiment relies on a polynomial feature lift to satisfy L > MK without analyzing its impact on generative assumptions or MoM consistency.

- Paper is generally readable, but references are incomplete/duplicated and notation is sloppy (confusing use of Ā, Ar, Ar̄, etc.).

- Assumptions about the observation matrix O and the MK < L requirement should be stated and motivated more clearly up front.

- Algorithm 1 is high-level and omits practical settings (e-range, longevity thresholding, clustering hyperparameters).

- Conjecture 1 is justified informally and reuses sample-complexity results from mixtures without a clear mapping to this setting.

- Novelty is modest: reduction is known, contribution is exploiting global transition eigen-structure and spectral longevity, which are heuristic.

- Related work on scalable MHMMs and spectral methods is under-discussed and references are sparse/poorly formatted.

- If robust, the method could be a fast, initialization-free MHMM learner and a useful EM initializer; idea is elegant and potentially impactful.

- Current limitations (heuristic de-permutation, unproven spectral conditions, limited empirical validation) reduce confidence in real-world reliability and scalability.

- Strengths: clear reduction to block-diagonal HMM; intuitive eigen-based de-permutation; empirical speed advantage over EM and promise as an initializer.

- Weaknesses: conjectural noisy-case theory; insufficient algorithmic detail for reproducibility; limited experiments and missing ablations; incomplete references and clarity issues.

- Key questions for authors: provide precise longevity hyperparameters and thresholding; derive perturbation guarantees; analyze sensitivity to similar stationary distributions; explain moment computation for continuous emissions and why MK < L is necessary; compare to H3M/variational baselines and report permutation/parameter recovery.

- Pros for acceptance: simple, intuitive idea with practical potential and clear link to spectral HMM learning.

- Cons for acceptance: lacks finite-sample/noisy-case guarantees, insufficient algorithmic and empirical detail, incomplete related work and clarity problems.

- Recommendation: Weak reject; work is promising but requires rigorous noisy-case theory, fuller algorithmic exposition, and broader empirical validation before acceptance.
