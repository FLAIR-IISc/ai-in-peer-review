- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral approach to learn Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Leverages and extends Anandkumar et al.'s spectral HMM techniques and introduces a depermutation method to recover individual HMM components.  
- Novel theoretical insight: connecting MHMMs to structured HMMs enables spectral learning applicability.  
- Provides spectral analysis and lemmas characterizing the global transition matrix's spectral properties.  
- Computational advantage: avoids O(N) per-iteration EM cost, making it suitable for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Empirically demonstrates a clear speed advantage over EM in experiments (Section 4.2).  
- Practical utility: spectral estimates serve as strong initializations for EM, improving non-convex optimization (Section 4.3).  
- Main theoretical weakness: relies on Conjecture 1 (Ar estimation error) without formal proof or rigorous guarantees.  
- Strong spectral assumptions: assumes each local transition matrix has only one eigenvalue of magnitude 1, which may fail for periodic or near-periodic chains.  
- Lacks exploration of robustness when key spectral conditions (e.g., spectral separation) are violated.  
- Limited empirical comparison: only compared to EM, omitting other non-EM methods (variational Bayes, other MoM approaches).  
- Real-data experiment concern: required cubic polynomial feature transform to satisfy MK < L constraint, raising generalization and distortion concerns.  
- Algorithmic detail gap: depermutation algorithm depends on clustering matrix columns but omits specific clustering method and noise robustness analysis.  
- Critical assessment: interesting contribution but weakened by lack of formal guarantees and insufficient robustness/alternative-method comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on addressing theoretical proof, broader experiments (including violated spectral assumptions), comparisons with non-EM methods, and more depermutation details.  
- Overall significance: moderate — addresses a practical computational bottleneck but applicability is limited to cases meeting the spectral conditions.
