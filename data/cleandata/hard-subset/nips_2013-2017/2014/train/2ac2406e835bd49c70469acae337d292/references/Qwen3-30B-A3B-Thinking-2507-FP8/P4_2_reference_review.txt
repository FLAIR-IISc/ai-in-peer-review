

# Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

## Summary
This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), addressing a significant computational bottleneck in the traditional EM-based learning approach. The key insight is recognizing that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. Building on existing spectral learning methods for HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012), the authors develop an algorithm that first learns the global HMM parameters (up to permutation ambiguity) and then applies spectral analysis to recover the individual HMM parameters through a depermutation mapping. The paper also establishes theoretical conditions under which the number of clusters K can be estimated from spectral properties of the transition matrix.

## Quality
The technical approach is sound, with clear theoretical foundations. The equivalence between MHMM and HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix (Lemma 1) is well-established. The spectral analysis (Lemma 2, Corollary 1) provides a solid basis for the depermutation algorithm. The paper demonstrates a reasonable level of completeness, presenting both theoretical conditions and empirical validation. The experimental section supports the claims with synthetic data (showing correct recovery of K even with small sequence lengths) and real-world data (handwritten character trajectories), though the real data experiment has limitations (requiring cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy MK < L).

The authors acknowledge the noise sensitivity of their approach, noting that the spectral conditions (Definition 3) must be satisfied for the depermutation algorithm to work. However, the theoretical analysis is somewhat limited, with Conjecture 1 presented without a formal proof. The paper would benefit from more rigorous theoretical justification for the convergence rate claim in Conjecture 1.

## Clarity
The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively frames the problem and motivation, and the model definitions are precise and well-explained. The key insight about the block-diagonal structure is clearly presented and justified. The algorithm is described with sufficient detail (Algorithm 1), and the experimental results are well-presented with appropriate visualizations. The notation is consistent throughout, and the paper provides enough detail for a knowledgeable reader to understand and potentially reproduce the main algorithmic contributions.

## Originality
The paper makes a novel contribution by extending spectral learning methods to MHMMs, which represents a significant gap in the literature. Previous work on MHMMs has primarily used EM (Smyth, 1997), while spectral learning has been applied to single HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012). The specific approach of using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity is a novel technical contribution. The paper adequately references related work, including Anandkumar's spectral learning for HMMs and Smyth's work on MHMMs, and clearly distinguishes their approach from previous contributions.

## Significance
The significance of this work is substantial. MHMMs are widely used for sequence clustering, but EM-based learning becomes computationally prohibitive for large datasets. The proposed spectral learning approach offers a computationally more efficient alternative that maintains comparable accuracy to EM. The paper demonstrates this through experiments showing the spectral method's speed advantage, particularly for larger datasets. The real-world experiment also shows that the spectral approach can serve as a good initialization for EM, which is practically valuable.

## Strengths
- Clear and novel insight about MHMM as HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix
- Well-motivated approach to address permutation ambiguity
- Comprehensive experimental validation on synthetic and real data
- Demonstrates significant computational advantages over EM
- Provides theoretical conditions for cluster number estimation

## Weaknesses
- Theoretical analysis lacks rigorous proof for key claims (Conjecture 1)
- Limited discussion of computational complexity of the depermutation step
- Real data experiment requires data transformation (cubic polynomial) to satisfy MK < L, which may limit applicability
- Comparison with EM is somewhat limited (focuses on accuracy and speed, but not convergence properties)

## Recommendation
This paper makes a technically sound contribution that extends spectral learning to MHMMs, addressing a practical computational challenge in sequence clustering. The theoretical insights are novel and well-supported by experiments. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened with more rigorous proofs, the practical significance of the approach is clear. The paper meets all four criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance, making it a strong candidate for acceptance at NeurIPS.

Pro: Novel theoretical insight, practical computational advantage, good experimental validation.
Con: Limited theoretical justification for key claims, some experimental constraints.

The paper advances the state of the art in learning latent variable models and provides a valuable computational alternative to EM for MHMMs, with potential applications in sequence clustering across various domains.
