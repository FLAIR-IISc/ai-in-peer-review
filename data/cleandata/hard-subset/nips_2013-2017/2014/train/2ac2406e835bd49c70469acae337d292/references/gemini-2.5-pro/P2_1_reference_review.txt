### Summary of the Paper

This paper proposes a novel method for learning the parameters of a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) based on the method of moments (MoM). The key insight is to re-frame an MHMM with K components, each with M hidden states, as a single large HMM with KM states and a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage an existing spectral MoM algorithm for HMMs to learn the parameters of this large equivalent model. This standard approach, however, recovers the parameters only up to a permutation of the KM states, which mixes the parameters of the individual component HMMs. The main contribution of this paper is a spectral de-permutation algorithm to resolve this ambiguity. By analyzing the spectral properties of the estimated global transition matrix, the authors propose a method to cluster its columns (after a low-rank reconstruction) to recover the block structure, thereby identifying the parameters of the individual HMMs. The paper also presents a heuristic method based on "spectral longevity" to estimate the number of mixture components, K, from the data. Experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrate that the proposed method is significantly faster than the standard Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm and achieves comparable accuracy, while also serving as an effective initialization strategy for EM.

This work builds directly upon the foundational spectral learning algorithms for HMMs developed by Hsu, Kakade, Anandkumar, and others. The novelty lies in extending this framework to the more complex MHMM setting by explicitly addressing and solving the component-mixing problem that arises from the permutation ambiguity.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths (Arguments for Acceptance):**

1.  **Novel and Elegant Approach:** The paper's core idea is both simple and powerful. Re-casting the MHMM as a large HMM with a special structure is an elegant formulation. The subsequent use of the spectral properties of the block-diagonal transition matrix to solve the de-permutation problem is a clever and non-trivial contribution. This provides a principled, non-iterative alternative to EM for a widely used model.

2.  **Computational Efficiency:** The proposed algorithm addresses a major bottleneck of EM for MHMMs, namely the high computational cost of running forward-backward for every sequence in every iteration. Once the moments are computed, the parameter estimation time is independent of the dataset size, making it highly scalable. The experimental results clearly demonstrate this significant speed advantage.

3.  **Addresses Model Selection:** The paper goes beyond just parameter estimation and proposes a method to estimate the number of mixture components (K). While heuristic, this is a valuable addition, as model selection is a critical and often difficult aspect of learning mixture models.

4.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-written and logically structured. The progression from the model definition, to the equivalence with a structured HMM, to the permutation problem, and finally to the spectral solution is clear and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses (Suggestions for Improvement):**

1.  **Weak Theoretical Justification:** The theoretical underpinnings of the de-permutation algorithm in the presence of noise are not fully rigorous. "Conjecture 1" is a key piece of the argument, but its proof is deferred as "out of the scope of this paper," and the error bound is borrowed from a related but distinct model. This weakens the paper's claim of providing provable guarantees. Similarly, the proof of Lemma 3 is more of an intuitive sketch based on spectral gaps rather than a formal argument. The definition of "spectral longevity" feels ad-hoc and could be more clearly connected to established concepts in spectral graph theory or perturbation theory.

2.  **Underwhelming Real-Data Experiment:** The results on the handwritten character dataset are modest. The proposed spectral method does not consistently outperform randomly initialized EM on its own. The primary conclusion drawn from this experiment is that the method serves as a good *initializer* for EM. While this is a valid and useful application, it slightly diminishes the paper's positioning as a complete replacement for EM.

3.  **Unstated Assumptions and Limitations:** The success of the spectral de-permutation relies heavily on the spectral gap condition outlined in Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3: `|λ_GK| > max_k |λ_L2,k|`. This means the "mixing rate" within each component HMM must be faster than the "mixing rate" between components (as perturbed by noise). This is a strong assumption that may not hold in practice, for instance, if some component HMMs have stationary distributions that are slow to converge. The paper would be strengthened by a more explicit discussion of when this condition is likely to hold or fail, and experimental validation of the algorithm's robustness as this condition is weakened.

### Detailed Evaluation Against NIPS Criteria

*   **Quality:** The paper is of good quality. The core idea is technically sound and the proposed algorithm is well-motivated. However, the theoretical analysis is the weakest part, relying on an unproven conjecture and heuristic arguments. The experiments are reasonable and support the main claims about speed and comparable accuracy, though the real-data results could be stronger. It is a complete piece of work, but the authors could be more forthright about the limitations and assumptions of their theoretical claims.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written. The problem is well-defined, and the proposed solution is explained in a step-by-step manner. The figures effectively illustrate the core concept of recovering the block structure from the noisy transition matrix. The paper provides enough information for an expert to understand the method, though reproducing the exact "spectral longevity" calculation might require some interpretation.

*   **Originality:** The work is highly original. While it uses existing MoM techniques for HMMs as a building block, the identification and solution of the de-permutation problem for MHMMs via spectral clustering of the transition matrix is novel. It clearly differentiates itself from prior work by tackling the mixture model scenario, which presents unique challenges not present in single HMM learning.

*   **Significance:** The results are significant. MHMMs are an important class of models for sequence clustering, and EM's drawbacks (speed, local minima) are well-known. This paper provides a new, computationally efficient, and theoretically-grounded learning algorithm that could enable the use of MHMMs on much larger datasets. The core idea of using spectral properties to disentangle components in a mixture learned via MoM could also be influential for other hierarchical latent variable models.

### Final Recommendation

Overall, this is a strong paper with a novel and significant contribution. The proposed spectral learning algorithm for MHMMs is elegant, computationally efficient, and addresses a clear limitation of the standard EM approach. The main weakness is the lack of rigor in the theoretical analysis of the noisy case. Despite this, the novelty of the idea and the promising empirical results make a compelling case for acceptance. I recommend this paper for acceptance, with the strong suggestion that the authors revise the theory section to either provide more rigorous proofs or to be more explicit about the conjectural nature of their claims and the underlying assumptions.
