- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm to learn Mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Central contribution: spectral "de-permutation" to resolve permutation ambiguity between component HMMs and recover individual parameters.  
- Builds on prior MoM and spectral HMM work (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) and connects to spectral clustering/eigenvalue methods for determining cluster count.  
- Addresses an important, computationally challenging problem where EM is slow on large datasets.  
- Novel combination of MoM and spectral techniques; viewing MHMM as block-diagonal HMM is a clever foundational insight.  
- Provides theoretical lemmas supporting the spectral de-permutation and conditions for recovering the number of HMMs.  
- Empirical validation shows speed benefits and usefulness as an EM initialization, though experiments are relatively simple.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and equations are clearly presented.  
- Heavy reliance on unproven Conjecture 1 weakens the theoretical foundation.  
- Experimental scope is limited: synthetic tests mainly show feasibility/speed, and real-data evaluation is on a single dataset with one feature transform.  
- Spectral de-permutation appears sensitive to noise; eigenvalue separation conditions may be fragile in practice.  
- Practical parameter tuning (e.g., choice of 'e' and criteria for K) lacks clear guidance.  
- Lacks comparison to other common EM initialization methods (e.g., k-means) in experiments.  
- Qualitatively: technically sound given conjectures, clear exposition, and original approach with potentially significant impact if robust.  
- Reviewer stance: weak accept conditional on addressing theoretical proofs, broader experiments, parameter guidance, and clearer discussion of limitations.  
- Requested revisions: attempt to prove Conjecture 1 or analyze its assumptions; expand experiments across noise levels/datasets and compare more initializations; provide concrete parameter-tuning recommendations; discuss limitations and scalability.
