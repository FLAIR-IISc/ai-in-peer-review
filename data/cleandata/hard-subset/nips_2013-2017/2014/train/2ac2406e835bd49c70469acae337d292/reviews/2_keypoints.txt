- Presents a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by formulating an MHMM as a special case of an HMM and using Anandkumar et al. (2012) off-the-shelf.  
- Introduces a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides an analysis accounting for estimation noise that leverages eigenvalues (notably Equation (7)) in the algorithm.  
- Interesting and intuitive approach to dodging parameter permutation; the stabilization idea is a key contribution.  
- Notation is confusing in places (e.g., Corollary 1); needs clearer definitions such as 1J^T and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e.  
- Authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced to improve clarity.  
- Lacks sufficient engagement with related work; must cite prior work such as initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty and Liang 2013).  
- Experiments are basic and presentation is poor—Figure 4 is hard to read and results should be reported in tables.  
- Questions about experimental results: EM performs unusually poorly; should report best (not just average) EM accuracy and provide more thorough comparisons.  
- Suggests conforming to established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009: use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden state counts instead of A/L/J).  
- Overall summary: algorithm = existing spectral HMM method + novel stabilization step to undo parameter permutation.
