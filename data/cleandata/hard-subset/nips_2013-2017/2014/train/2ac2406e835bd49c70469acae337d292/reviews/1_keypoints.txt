- Summary: Paper proposes using spectral learning to estimate an HMM then resolving permutation ambiguity to recover a block-diagonal mixture-of-HMMs structure.
- Major 1: Paper is well written but the experimental results need more detail and expansion.
- Major 2: Concern that estimating a full HMM first is impractical for large, sparse mixtures—author should clarify when the method is appropriate versus when it is not.
- Major 3: No guarantee the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse; many full matrices may be close to the desired sparse block structure under noise.
- Major 4: Experiments corrupt permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling observed sequences from a mixture of HMMs; need experiments that learn from observations to reflect real noise and limited-data effects.
- Major 5: Section 4.2 lacks specifics about the mixture HMM parameters (transition and observation matrices); provide full parameter details (e.g., in an appendix).
- Major 6: EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—need explanation since EM should typically improve results.
- Minor 1: Typo in proof: subscript of Λ should be K rather than 2.
- Minor 2: Figures have text and numbers that are too small to read.
- Overall: Interesting ideas and solid theory, but significant doubts remain about practical performance on real datasets.
