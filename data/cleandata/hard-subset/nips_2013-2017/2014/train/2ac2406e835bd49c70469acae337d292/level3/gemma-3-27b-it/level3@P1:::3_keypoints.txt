- Proposes a MoM-based spectral method to learn Mixture of HMMs that resolves permutation ambiguity by exploiting the global transition matrix.
- Uses spectral properties to de-permute and recover individual HMM parameters, offering a computationally efficient alternative to EM.
- Frames MHMM as a structured HMM (Lemma 1) to enable MoM application.
- Provides theoretical analysis (Lemmas 2 & 3, Conjecture 1) to justify the de-permutation algorithm.
- Empirically validated on synthetic data and a handwritten character trajectory dataset, compared against EM (random and MoM init).
- Presents a clear, concise Algorithm 1 for the proposed method.
- Core spectral/MoM technique builds heavily on prior work (limited novelty).
- Omits relevant related work (notably Chaganty & Liang’s spectral experts for mixtures of regressions).
- Contains minor typographical errors and awkward phrasing that need proofreading.
- Key performance claims rely on Conjecture 1, which needs stronger theoretical or empirical support.
- Real-data experiment uses a cubic polynomial feature transform to meet MoM requirements, which feels like a workaround and lacks discussion of implications.
- Overall: solid contribution with a valuable de-permutation approach; merits acceptance with minor revisions.
- For acceptance: addresses a hard problem, is computationally advantageous, has theoretical grounding and empirical support, and is well presented.
- Against acceptance: limited novelty, missing related work, reliance on a conjecture, and minor proofreading issues.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add the missing related work, thoroughly proofread, and strengthen justification for Conjecture 1.
