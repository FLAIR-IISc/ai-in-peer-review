- Proposes a method-of-moments algorithm to learn mixtures of HMMs by treating MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Uses an existing spectral MoM algorithm to recover parameters up to permutation, then estimates a de-permutation mapping via spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Main technical contribution is a stabilization-based de-permutation method that leverages the fact each HMM transition has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides analysis accounting for estimation noise and incorporating eigenvalue information into the algorithm.  
- Approach is interesting, intuitive, and the stabilization idea is a key novelty.  
- Notation is confusing in places; authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced.  
- Corollary 1’s notation is unclear, and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e is not well-defined.  
- The paper lacks sufficient engagement with prior work (e.g., initializing EM with spectral estimates such as Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Experiments are basic, presentation is poor, Figure 4 is hard to read, and results should be reported in tables.  
- Should report best (not just average) EM accuracy and provide more thorough experimental comparisons.  
- Recommend conforming to established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009: use T, n/m for counts instead of A/L/J).  
- Overall technically sound: theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims.  
- Clarity is weak and requires substantial improvement.  
- Originality is moderate: combines existing spectral HMM methods with a novel stabilization step to undo permutation.  
- Significance is good: method is computationally cheaper than EM and can serve as an effective EM initializer.  
- Acceptance pros: novel MHMM approach, key stabilization contribution, computational efficiency vs EM.  
- Acceptance cons: confusing notation, insufficient related-work engagement, basic experiments and poor presentation.  
- Recommended improvements: clarify notation with dimensions, cite and discuss prior spectral-to-EM work, report best EM results, adopt standard HMM notation, and improve experimental presentation.
