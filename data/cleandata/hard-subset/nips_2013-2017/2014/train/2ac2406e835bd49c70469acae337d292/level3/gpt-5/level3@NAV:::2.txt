Summary: The paper proposes a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by (i) reformulating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling application of off-the-shelf spectral HMM learning (e.g., Anandkumar et al., 2012; Hsu et al., 2009), and (ii) introducing a stabilization-based de-permutation step that exploits the eigen-1 structure of each component transition to recover per-HMM parameters and estimate K via a “spectral longevity” statistic (Eq. 7). The idea of undoing permutation through the asymptotic structure of powers of the global transition is intuitive and appealing.

Quality: The high-level construction is sound, and the stabilization idea addresses a real obstacle in MHMM spectral learning. However, the theory is incomplete: Conjecture 1 is central yet unproven; the reuse of bounds from mixtures without a careful adaptation to the HMM setting is weak; and the conditions under which Eq. (7) reliably recovers K need formal guarantees or, at minimum, a systematic sensitivity study (noise level, eigen-gap, periodicity). The assumption that each Ak has a single eigenvalue equal to 1 should be discussed vis-à-vis ergodicity/aperiodicity; how does the method behave for nearly periodic or reducible chains?

Clarity: The notation is often confusing. Corollary 1 uses undefined or ambiguous quantities (e.g., 1J^T, dimensions of v̄k); the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e should be stated with explicit block sizes and dimensions. Please define every matrix/vector with dimensions upon first use. Consider aligning with established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009: T for transitions; m/n for hidden/observation state counts) rather than A/L/J. Algorithmic details needed for reproducibility include the exponent range for e, eigenvalue thresholding, clustering procedure, and how γ in Eq. (7) is set.

Originality: The core estimation step leverages existing spectral HMM methods; the novel contribution is the stabilization-based de-permutation and K-selection via spectral longevity. This is a neat and practical idea, but the paper should better distinguish itself from prior work and cite related approaches that combine spectral estimators with EM (e.g., Chaganty and Liang, 2013).

Significance: If theoretically solidified and empirically validated, the method could make MHMM learning scalable and robust to initialization.

Experiments: Current experiments are basic and presentation is poor. Figure 4 is hard to read; report results in tables with mean/std and best-of-EM, not only averages. EM appears unusually weak—please investigate hyperparameters, restarts, and report accuracy vs. runtime fairly. Add comparisons to EM initialized with spectral estimates and other spectral baselines.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, intuitive stabilization to resolve permutation
- Potentially impactful for scalable MHMM learning

Cons:
- Incomplete theory (unproven conjecture, weak noise analysis)
- Clarity/notation issues
- Limited, poorly presented experiments and weak baselines
