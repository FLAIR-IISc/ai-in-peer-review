- Proposes reframing a K-component MHMM (M states each) as a single HMM with MK states and a block-diagonal transition matrix, then applying existing spectral HMM learning.  
- Identifies permutation ambiguity from spectral methods that mixes component states, preventing recovery of individual HMMs.  
- Main contribution: a de-permutation algorithm using spectral properties—keeping eigenvectors for the top K eigenvalues (≈1) and clustering columns of a low-rank reconstruction to recover block structure and state groupings.  
- Provides a heuristic to estimate the number of components K from spectral gaps of the estimated transition matrix.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and handwritten-character data show significant speed advantages over EM and usefulness as an EM initializer.  
- Strength: Tackles an important, non-trivial alignment problem for spectral learning of mixture models.  
- Strength: De-permutation method is elegant and intuitive, leveraging spectral structure and clustering to recover components.  
- Strength: Method is computationally efficient, avoiding iterative EM passes and scaling well to large datasets.  
- Strength: Practical utility demonstrated—fast standalone method and effective initializer for EM to improve convergence and local optima.  
- Weakness: Paper understates that applying spectral HMM learning to the global model is straightforward and lacks engagement with related component-alignment literature (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Weakness: Theoretical justification in the noisy case is weak—Conjecture 1 lacks proof and Lemma 3’s K-estimation via spectral longevity is heuristic without formal guarantees.  
- Weakness: Presentation needs polishing—occasional typos and grammatical errors detract from clarity.  
- Quality summary: Technically sound with supportive experiments, but theoretical claims are insufficiently proven, making the work feel more like a strong workshop paper.  
- Clarity summary: Generally well-written and organized, but would benefit from careful proofreading.  
- Originality summary: Novel de-permutation algorithm is interesting, though initial spectral HMM application is not novel and comparison to other alignment methods is missing.  
- Significance summary: Important and practical contribution for learning MHMMs; fast algorithm and EM-initializer potential make it valuable for sequence clustering practitioners.
