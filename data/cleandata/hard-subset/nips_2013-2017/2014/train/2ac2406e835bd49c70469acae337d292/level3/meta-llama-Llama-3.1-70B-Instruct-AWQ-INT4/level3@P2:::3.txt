The paper "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models" presents a novel approach to learning mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using the method of moments (MoM). The authors leverage the spectral properties of a global transition matrix to resolve the permutation ambiguity that arises in the estimation process. This approach is an extension of the spectral learning algorithm for HMMs, and it provides a computationally efficient alternative to traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithms.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's properties. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, both in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to address the key question of how to de-permute/align spectral outputs to true components. The authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to work reasonably well, and they provide a thorough analysis of its properties. The paper also provides a good comparison with existing work, including the EM algorithm and other spectral learning approaches.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, including the recent paper by Chaganty and Liang (ICML 2013) on spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions. Secondly, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity and its scalability to large datasets. Finally, the paper could benefit from a more thorough proofreading, as there are occasional typos and proofreading issues.
