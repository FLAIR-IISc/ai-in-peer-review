- Novel spectral method: represents MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition, uses method of moments and spectral analysis to resolve permutation ambiguity as an efficient alternative to EM.

- Builds on prior spectral HMM work (Anandkumar et al.); Lemma 1 formalizes the block-diagonal view and spectral recovery of individual HMMs is the main contribution.

- Theoretical strengths: sound lemmas, novel spectral resolution of permutation ambiguity, and potential computational advantages; shows utility as EM initialization in a real-data experiment.

- Major weakness 1 — experimental details missing: Section 4.2 omits exact mixture-HMM parameters (transition/observation matrices); parameters should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).

- Major weakness 2 — practicality unclear: estimating a full HMM with MK states may be impractical for large, sparse mixtures; the paper doesn’t clarify appropriate application regimes.

- Major weakness 3 — no sparsity guarantee: spectral estimates may not be (nearly) sparse under noise, so block-diagonal structure recovery can fail.

- Major weakness 4 — flawed experimental design: Section 4.1 corrupts permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling sequences from MHMMs, failing to model real-world noise and limited-data effects.

- Major weakness 5 — EM performance anomaly: EM initialized with spectral sometimes performs worse than spectral alone, a counterintuitive result that is unexplained.

- Minor issues: typo in Lemma 2 (Λ subscript should be K, not 2) and figures have text/numbers too small to read.

- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and novel idea, but insufficient empirical validation and practical clarity leave doubts about real-world utility.

- Required revisions suggested: supply full experiment parameters, demonstrate learning from actual sequences, clarify practical limitations/use cases, and explain the EM anomaly.

- Recommendation: reject pending significant experimental and clarity improvements; encourage resubmission after addressing major concerns.
