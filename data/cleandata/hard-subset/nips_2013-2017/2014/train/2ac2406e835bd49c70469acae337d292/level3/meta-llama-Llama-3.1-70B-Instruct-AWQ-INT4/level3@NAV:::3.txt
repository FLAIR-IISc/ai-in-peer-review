This paper proposes a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors show that an MHMM can be expressed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, and then use an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters up to a permutation ambiguity. They propose a de-permutation algorithm that exploits the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping that enables the recovery of the parameters of the individual HMMs.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. However, there are occasional typos and proofreading issues that need to be addressed.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results. However, some sections, such as the introduction, could be improved to better situate the paper in related work.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the use of spectral methods for learning MHMMs. However, the algorithm is largely a trivial variant of Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang's work, and the authors acknowledge this. The de-permutation algorithm is a significant contribution, but its novelty could be better highlighted.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, specifically the issue of permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning. The results are important, and the approach has the potential to be useful for practitioners and researchers. The paper also provides unique insights into the spectral properties of MHMMs.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

Pro:

* The paper presents a novel approach to learning MHMMs that addresses a significant challenge in the field.
* The de-permutation algorithm is a significant contribution that enables the recovery of individual HMM parameters.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.

Con:

* The algorithm is largely a trivial variant of existing work, which may limit its novelty.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to related work, such as Chaganty and Liang's paper.
* There are occasional typos and proofreading issues that need to be addressed.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of the paper, but with revisions to address the issues mentioned above.
