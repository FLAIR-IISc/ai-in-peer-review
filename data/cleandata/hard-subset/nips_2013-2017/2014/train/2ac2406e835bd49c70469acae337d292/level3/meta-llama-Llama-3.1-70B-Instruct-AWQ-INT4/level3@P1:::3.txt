The paper "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models" proposes a novel approach for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (HMMs) using the method of moments. The authors address the key challenge of resolving the permutation ambiguity that arises when learning the parameters of a mixture of HMMs using spectral methods.

The main idea of the paper is to leverage the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping that enables the recovery of the parameters of the individual HMMs. The authors show that the global transition matrix has K eigenvalues that are equal to 1, where K is the number of HMMs in the mixture. By exploiting this property, they propose a de-permutation algorithm that can recover the correct ordering of the HMMs.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach. The authors also provide experimental results on both synthetic and real data, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. The results show that the proposed method is computationally more efficient than the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm, while achieving comparable accuracy.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to address the key question of how to de-permute/align spectral outputs to true components. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated solution to this problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. For example, the authors could provide more context and background on the related work in the field, particularly with respect to the work of Chaganty and Liang (ICML 2013). Additionally, the paper could benefit from more careful proofreading, as there are occasional typos and proofreading issues.

In terms of originality, the paper builds on the work of Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang, but the authors' contribution is significant in that they address the key challenge of resolving the permutation ambiguity. The paper also provides a novel solution to this problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
