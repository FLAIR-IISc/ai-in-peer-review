- Problem & approach: Proposes MoM-based algorithm for learning MHMMs by treating MHMM as a single KM-state HMM and applying spectral HMM learning.  
- Core issue: Direct spectral learning recovers KM states up to an unknown permutation, mixing component parameters and making individual HMMs unidentifiable.  
- Main contribution: Introduces a "de-permutation" algorithm that uses the top-K spectral reconstruction of the global transition matrix to cluster states back into K components.  
- De-permutation method: Compute low-rank approximation using top K eigenvalues, cluster its columns with k-means to recover component state groupings and permutation.  
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-data experiments show computational speed-ups over EM and usefulness as an initialization scheme.  
- Strength 1: Tackles an important practical problem of aligning spectral outputs to mixture components.  
- Strength 2: De-permutation idea is simple, intuitive, and empirically effective.  
- Strength 3: Algorithm is computationally efficient compared to iterative methods like EM.  
- Weakness 1: Insufficient related-work discussion; fails to cite/compare to similar component-alignment work (notably Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Weakness 2: Presentation issues—typos, confusing notation, unclear algorithm descriptions (e.g., "spectral longevity" and K̂ misuse).  
- Weakness 3: Limited theoretical rigor—Conjecture 1 is informal, borrows prior bounds, and omits analysis of k-means clustering performance and column separation.  
- Suggestion 1: Expand related-work section to discuss prior spectral alignment methods and clarify novelty relative to Chaganty & Liang (2013).  
- Suggestion 2: Thorough proofreading and clearer exposition of Algorithm 1, Conjecture 1, and spectral-longevity definition.  
- Suggestion 3: Strengthen theoretical claims or clearly state assumptions/limitations, and analyze clustering separation conditions.  
- Summary assessment: Technically intuitive but informally analyzed; clarity and citation omissions reduce perceived originality; method is practically significant if issues are addressed.  
- Recommendation: Borderline acceptance contingent on addressing related-work omission, clarity problems, and theoretical shortcomings.
