The paper "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models" proposes a novel approach to learning the parameters of a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) using the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors leverage the fact that an MHMM can be expressed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and use an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters up to a permutation ambiguity. They then introduce a stabilization-based method to resolve this ambiguity, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of existing approaches to learning MHMMs. The proposed algorithm is an interesting and intuitive approach to dodging parameter permutation, and the stabilization idea is a key contribution. The authors also provide an analysis accounting for estimation noise that leverages eigenvalues in the algorithm.

However, the paper has several limitations. The notation is confusing in places, and the authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced to improve clarity. For example, Corollary 1 would benefit from clearer definitions of 1J^T and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e. Additionally, the authors should engage more thoroughly with related work, including prior work on initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty and Liang, 2013).

The experimental evaluation of the proposed algorithm is basic and could be improved. The presentation of the results is poor, with Figure 4 being hard to read. The authors should report the results in tables and provide more thorough comparisons with existing approaches. Furthermore, the performance of EM seems unusually poor, and the authors should report the best (not just average) EM accuracy. Finally, the authors should consider conforming to established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009) to improve the clarity of the paper.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and its limitations. However, the paper could benefit from more thorough analysis and evaluation of the algorithm. The authors should also provide more details on the computational complexity of the algorithm and its scalability to large datasets.

The clarity of the paper is generally good, but the notation and presentation of the results could be improved. The authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced and provide clearer explanations of the mathematical derivations.

The originality of the paper is high, as the authors propose a novel approach to learning MHMMs. However, the paper could benefit from more thorough engagement with related work and a clearer explanation of how the proposed algorithm differs from existing approaches.

The significance of the paper is moderate, as the proposed algorithm has the potential to improve the efficiency and accuracy of learning MHMMs. However, the authors should provide more thorough evaluation and analysis of the algorithm to demonstrate its significance.

In conclusion, the paper proposes a novel approach to learning MHMMs using the Method of Moments. While the paper has several limitations, the proposed algorithm is an interesting and intuitive approach to dodging parameter permutation. With more thorough analysis, evaluation, and engagement with related work, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
