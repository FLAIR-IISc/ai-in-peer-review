- Proposes a novel spectral Method-of-Moments approach for Mixture of HMMs by learning a global HMM with block-diagonal transition structure then resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.

- Theoretical derivation clearly connects MHMMs to HMMs with block-diagonal transitions, with a sound proof of Lemma 1.

- Offers an innovative depermutation method using spectral clustering of the transition matrix to avoid iterative EM forward–backward costs.

- Claims potential computational scalability advantages over EM, especially for large datasets.

- Builds on existing MoM and spectral HMM literature and positions the contribution as extending MoM to MHMMs.

- Experimental validation is limited and somewhat artificial, relying mainly on synthetic tests and corrupted estimated matrices rather than learning from observed sequences.

- Experiments do not model real-world noise or finite-sample effects, raising concerns about practical applicability.

- Scalability concern: learning the initial global HMM may be impractical for large/sparse mixtures or high numbers of hidden states (M, K); conditions and limits are not clarified.

- The key conjecture about the transition matrix being "nearly" sparse enough for reliable clustering is fragile and insufficiently justified.

- Robustness of the spectral depermutation to noise is not analyzed; real-world noise could produce full matrices that break the method.

- Section 4.2 lacks details on MHMM parameters (transition and observation matrices), harming reproducibility.

- Unexplained result: EM initialized with spectral estimates sometimes performs worse than spectral learning alone; authors' explanation is unconvincing.

- Minor issues: typographical errors (e.g., subscript of Λ) and small figure font sizes reduce clarity.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretically grounded approach, potential computational efficiency, and clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental validation, scalability concerns, spectral fragility, and missing experimental details.

- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical contribution but insufficient empirical support and robustness analysis; recommendation to reject in current form.

- Suggested revisions: expand experiments on realistic datasets and noise models, analyze scalability limits and spectral robustness, provide full experimental parameters, and investigate the EM-initialization anomaly.
