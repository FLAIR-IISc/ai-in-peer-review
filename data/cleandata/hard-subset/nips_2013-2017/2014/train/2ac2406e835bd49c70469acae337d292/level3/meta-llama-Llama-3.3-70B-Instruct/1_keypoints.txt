- Proposes GUMA, a novel unsupervised manifold alignment method that links correlated datasets without known correspondences.
- Assumes datasets of the same theme have similar manifold structures and formulates alignment as an integer optimization with structure, feature, and geometry-preserving terms.
- Presents an efficient alternating optimization algorithm to solve the model.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, detailed method description.
- Experiments claim effectiveness across set matching, video face recognition, and visual domain adaptation.
- Experimental results lack sufficient detail, discussion, and analysis (e.g., Section 5.2 reports tables without commentary).
- Key parameters (e.g., balance weights γf and γp) are set without explanation or sensitivity analysis.
- Practicality concerns: method depends on accurately estimating full HMM parameters before reversing permutations, which may not hold in real-world data.
- Recovered transition matrices may not be sparse and spectral algorithms offer no sparsity guarantees, risking overfitting and poor generalization.
- Experimental setup may not reflect realistic noise; authors should sample observations from mixtures of HMMs and provide more details on those parameters (Section 4.2 is under-specified).
- Some results are counterintuitive (Table 1: EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes worse than spectral alone), indicating inconsistent advantages and needing deeper analysis.
- Overall: promising contribution but requires more experimental detail, realism, parameter justification, and analysis to validate practical usefulness.
