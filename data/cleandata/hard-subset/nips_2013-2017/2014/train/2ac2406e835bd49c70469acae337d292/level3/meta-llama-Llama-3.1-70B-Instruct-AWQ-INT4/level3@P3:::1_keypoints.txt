- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments + spectral algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs).  
- Resolves permutation ambiguity by exploiting eigenstructure of a global transition matrix: estimate global HMM then use eigen-decomposition to align components.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides a clear explanation of the approach.  
- Technical analysis of the spectral properties of the global transition matrix is thorough and sound.  
- Experiments show the proposed method can effectively estimate MHMM parameters.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks crucial details (hyperparameters, number of restarts, stopping criteria).  
- Unclear when the method is appropriate—no guideline on determining mixture sparsity or when estimating a full HMM is practical.  
- No theoretical or empirical guarantee that the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse; many noisy full matrices may mimic sparse block structure.  
- Lacks analysis of robustness to noise and sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.  
- Section 4.2 experiments use Dirichlet noise on permuted matrices instead of sampling observed sequences; needs experiments using real observations to capture noise and limited-data effects.  
- Missing details about the mixture-HMM parameters (transition and observation matrices) used in experiments (Section 4.2).  
- Unexpected result: EM initialized with spectral sometimes degrades performance relative to spectral alone; requires explanation.  
- Minor issues: typo in proof (Λ subscript should be K, not 2) and some figure text/numbers are too small to read.  
- Novelty is solid: presents a new perspective for resolving permutation ambiguity in MHMM estimation.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing concerns — add experiments with real observations, robustness/hyperparameter analyses, and more experimental detail.
