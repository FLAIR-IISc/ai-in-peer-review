- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixture of HMMs by treating MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Introduces a stabilization method to resolve permutation ambiguity, assuming each HMM transition has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear explanation of limitations of existing MHMM learning approaches.  
- Provides analysis that accounts for estimation noise and leverages eigenvalues in the algorithm.  
- Notation is confusing in places; authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced.  
- Corollary 1 and expressions like 1J^T and lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e need clearer definitions and exposition.  
- Authors should engage more thoroughly with related work, e.g., initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013).  
- Experimental evaluation is basic and presentation is poor (Figure 4 hard to read); results should be reported in tables.  
- Need more thorough comparisons with existing approaches and clearer baselines.  
- Performance of EM in experiments seems unusually poor; authors should report best (not just average) EM accuracy.  
- Recommend conforming to established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009) to improve clarity.  
- Paper lacks sufficient detail on computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Clarity is generally good but mathematical derivations and notation need improvement.  
- Originality is high—the stabilization idea and overall approach are novel and intuitive.  
- Significance is moderate; potential impact contingent on stronger evaluation and analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address notation, related work, and experimental concerns.  
- Rating: 7/10; Confidence: 8/10.
