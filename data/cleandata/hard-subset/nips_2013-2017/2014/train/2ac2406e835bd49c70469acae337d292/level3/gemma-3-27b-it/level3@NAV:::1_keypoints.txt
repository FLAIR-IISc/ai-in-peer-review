- Proposes a novel spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs by estimating a global block-diagonal HMM and resolving permutation ambiguity to recover components.
- Paper is well-written with a clear theoretical foundation building on spectral HMM learning and MoM.

- Major 1: Experimental evaluation is insufficiently detailed and expanded.
- Major 2: Practicality concern about first estimating a full HMM for large or sparse mixtures—may be inefficient or intractable.
- Major 3: Guarantee of near-sparsity in the spectral-learned transition matrix is questionable; many full matrices may be close to the desired block structure, risking incorrect de-permutation.
- Major 4: Experiments use corrupted permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise instead of generating observed sequences from true MHMMs, failing to reflect realistic noise and limited-data conditions.
- Major 5: Synthetic experiment parameter settings (Section 4.2) are not fully specified, hindering reproducibility; a detailed appendix is needed.
- Major 6: EM initialized with the spectral estimate sometimes performs worse than spectral alone; this unexpected degradation needs explanation.

- Minor 1: Typo in proof—subscript of Λ should be K.
- Minor 2: Figures contain unreadably small text and numbers.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theory, potential computational advantages over EM.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental validation, scalability and noise-sensitivity concerns, lack of realistic data/noise experiments, unexplained EM degradation.

- Overall recommendation: reject pending substantial revisions, especially expanded experiments with realistic data and deeper practical analysis.
