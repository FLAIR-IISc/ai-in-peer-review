- Proposes spectral learning for Mixture of HMMs by treating MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.  
- Builds on Anandkumar et al. (2012) and adds a stabilization method to resolve parameter permutation ambiguity using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Main theoretical contribution: analysis of recovering individual HMM parameters from permuted global estimates via the spectral longevity measure (Eq. 7).  
- Theoretical development is technically sound with interesting spectral insights (Lemma 2, Corollary 1).  
- The stabilization idea is intuitive and a meaningful contribution to the permutation problem.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and poorly presented (Figure 4); results should be tabulated for clarity.  
- Comparison with EM is incomplete—only average EM reported; should include best-case EM and cite spectral initialization work (Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation (e.g., 1J^T), matrix dimensions often undefined, and the block-diagonal limit lim_{e→∞} Ā^e needs clearer exposition.  
- Recommend using standard HMM notation (e.g., T for transition matrix) to improve readability.  
- Contribution is incremental: combines existing spectral HMM learning with a novel stabilization step, so significance is limited by modest experimental support.  
- Computational advantage over EM is promising but not convincingly demonstrated in experiments.  
- Real-data experiment indicates spectral learning can be a good EM initializer, but this potential is not fully explored.
