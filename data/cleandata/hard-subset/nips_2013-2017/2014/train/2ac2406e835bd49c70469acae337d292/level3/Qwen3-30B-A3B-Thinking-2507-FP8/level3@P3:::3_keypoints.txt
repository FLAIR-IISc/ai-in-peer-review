- Proposes spectral learning for Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Main technical contribution is a de-permutation algorithm that recovers the block structure from spectral learning output.  
- Builds on Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang (2012) spectral HMM methods; core MHMM procedure largely reduces to applying existing HMM spectral learning.  
- Fails to adequately acknowledge prior related work, notably Chaganty and Liang (ICML 2013), which addressed similar permutation issues for mixtures.  
- Provides a theoretical justification via Conjecture 1, but the analysis is incomplete and relies on external results without full proof.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data show successful recovery of cluster count and block-diagonal structure even with limited data.  
- Real-data experiment (handwritten character trajectories) shows the method is useful as an initialization for EM.  
- De-permutation algorithm appears to work well in practice and is the paper’s main strength.  
- Theoretical claims are presented as conjectures rather than proven results, weakening the paper’s theoretical contribution.  
- Paper contains proofreading, notation, and presentation issues that reduce clarity (e.g., typos and unclear matrix notation).  
- Originality is limited: the core method is a trivial extension of HMM spectral learning; novelty rests mainly on the de-permutation step.  
- Significance is moderate: computational advantages over EM matter mainly for very large datasets, and accuracy gains over well-initialized EM are modest.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a real problem, offers a computationally efficient method, and provides a practical EM initialization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate related work positioning, incomplete theory, limited novelty, and manuscript quality issues.  
- Recommendation: substantially revise to situate against Chaganty & Liang, strengthen theoretical analysis and proofs, expand experiments, and fix proofreading before acceptance.
