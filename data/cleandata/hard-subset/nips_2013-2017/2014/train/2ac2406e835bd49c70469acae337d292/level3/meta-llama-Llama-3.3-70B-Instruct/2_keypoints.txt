- Proposes GUMA: an unsupervised manifold alignment method using explicit integer optimization to match global manifold structures and sample features without known correspondences.
- Optimization alternates between two submodels: integer-constrained alignment learning and transforms to obtain a mutual embedding subspace.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough introduction and detailed method/optimization description.
- Novel and effective approach that discovers and aligns manifold structures without predefined local neighborhoods.
- Uses an efficient optimization algorithm that approximately seeks optima along the descent path of a relaxed objective.
- Demonstrates effectiveness and practicability via extensive experiments on set matching, video face recognition, and visual domain adaptation.
- Notation needs improvement for clarity and consistency, including explicit dimensions for vectors and matrices.
- Experimental results should be presented more comprehensively and comparably (e.g., summary tables instead of only graphs/figures).
- Lacks deeper analysis and discussion of results, including limitations and potential extensions of the method.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: notation clarity, presentation of experimental results, and insufficient analysis/discussion.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
