- Proposes a spectral learning method for MHMMs by representing the mixture as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix and resolving permutation ambiguity via eigendecomposition.  
- Provides an algorithm to recover block structure and estimate the number of clusters K using spectral properties (K eigenvalues equal to 1).  
- Builds on existing spectral HMM methods with a sound theoretical framework and novel extension to mixtures.  
- Claims potential computational advantages over EM and other likelihood-based methods.  
- Lacks empirical validation on real sequence data; experiments only use permuted matrices corrupted with Dirichlet noise, which does not reflect realistic noise or limited-data effects.  
- Fails to address practical scalability: estimating a full HMM first is computationally prohibitive for large, sparse mixtures.  
- No guarantee that the spectral-learned global transition matrix will be nearly sparse, undermining block-recovery in practice.  
- Section 4.2 omits critical parameter details for the mixture HMMs used in experiments, impeding reproducibility.  
- Observed phenomenon that EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes underperforms spectral alone is unexplained and puzzling.  
- Theoretical contribution is interesting, but experimental limitations significantly weaken claims about practical performance.  
- Paper is well-written overall but contains a typo in a proof.  
- Recommendation: include realistic experiments on observed sequences and clarify when the method is appropriate versus when it fails.
