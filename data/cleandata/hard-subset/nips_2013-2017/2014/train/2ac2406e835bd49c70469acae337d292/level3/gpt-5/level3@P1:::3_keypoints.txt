- Paper proposes a spectral method-of-moments approach to learn MHMMs by viewing them as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions and introducing a de-permutation step via eigenstructure.
- Builds on spectral HMM/tensor methods (Hsu et al., Anandkumar et al.); main novelty is the de-permutation/clustering alignment for mixtures.
- De-permutation uses the rank-K reconstruction of the global transition matrix and column clustering to recover block structure; also proposes a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K.
- Reduction to a block-diagonal HMM is correct and convenient; the de-permutation idea is plausible and empirically effective.
- Key components are heuristic: Conjecture 1 (finite-sample closeness) is unproven and the longevity criterion lacks formal guarantees.
- The assumption that each component Ak has exactly one eigenvalue 1 (ergodic, aperiodic) should be stated explicitly and discussed for near-periodic or reducible chains.
- Sensitivity to small spectral gaps (between |λ_GK| and max_k |λ_L2,k|) is not quantified or analyzed.
- Synthetic experiments show the de-permutation works with moderate T and few sequences and that spectral outputs are faster than EM and good as EM initializers.
- Real-data evaluation is limited (binary class pairs, M=4, K=2) and relies on feature lifting to satisfy MK < L; broader datasets and larger K,M are needed.
- Missing ablations: eigen-gap size, noise level, mis-specified MK, and explicit failure-case analysis are not provided.
- Algorithmic steps are clearly described, but code and exact moment/order/regularization details are not supplied for reproducibility.
- Paper is generally well organized with helpful lemmas, but notation is heavy and inconsistent; the spectral longevity concept needs an intuitive example.
- Proofreading issues and late mention of the MK < L requirement; guidance on feature lifting is needed earlier.
- Viewing MHMM as block-diagonal HMM and using spectral MoM is not novel; the de-permutation/alignment strategy and longevity heuristic are the main original contributions.
- If robust, the approach could enable scalable MHMM learning with competitive accuracy and fast runtimes and serve as strong EM initialization.
- Contribution is more algorithmic/engineering than theoretical; providing stronger guarantees or broader empirical validation would increase impact.
- Suggested theoretical work: partial finite-sample analysis of de-permutation (e.g., Davis–Kahan bounds) and conditions on eigen-gaps/noise.
- Suggested comparisons: baselines like Hungarian matching on emissions, clustering stationary distributions, joint diagonalization, and simple thresholding on A.
- Suggested empirical tests: sensitivity to slow-mixing/near-multiple unit eigenvalues, mis-specified MK and K̂, nonuniform priors, and different observation models.
- Clarify computational complexity (moments computation vs eigendecomposition and clustering) and report end-to-end wall-clock times.
- Improve related-work discussion (Chaganty & Liang, Anandkumar et al., Smyth 1997) and compare alternative alignment strategies.
- Pros: addresses practical permutation barrier, simple implementable algorithm, strong speed advantage, useful EM initializer.
- Cons: heuristic steps with missing theoretical guarantees, limited empirical scope, uncharacterized sensitivity to spectral gaps and misspecification, and related-work/formatting issues.
- Recommendation: weak accept if emphasis is on practical spectral algorithms and authors improve clarity, related work, and experiments; otherwise lean weak reject due to missing guarantees and limited robustness evaluation.
