- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs that leverages block-diagonal structure and a de-permutation algorithm to recover individual HMM parameters and component count.  
- Novelty lies in addressing permutation ambiguity in MoM for mixture models via spectral analysis, building on existing spectral HMM methods.  
- Heavy reliance on core ideas from Hsu, Kakade & Zhang [4]; contribution is largely an adaptation to MHMMs and thus feels incremental.  
- Recovered global transition matrix can still reflect arbitrary permutation mixing; de-permutation success depends strongly on noise levels and spectral separation.  
- Theoretical justification for the de-permutation algorithm is weak and relies on conjectures rather than rigorous proofs.  
- Related work section is incomplete and fails to discuss closely related work (notably Chaganty & Liang [8]).  
- Experimental evaluation is reasonable on synthetic and real data but lacks comparison to broader state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods beyond EM.  
- Paper has occasional typos and clarity/proofreading issues that hinder readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles permutation ambiguity, offers a practical de-permutation algorithm, and shows promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, incomplete theoretical guarantees, missing related-work discussion, and clarity issues.  
- Overall assessment: sound but not a major breakthrough; recommend rejection with encouragement to strengthen theory, expand comparisons, and improve related work and presentation.
