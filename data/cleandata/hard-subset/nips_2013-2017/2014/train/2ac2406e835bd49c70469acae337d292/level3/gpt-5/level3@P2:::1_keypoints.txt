- Proposes spectral/MoM learning for Mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single MK-state HMM and depermuting spectral estimates via eigen-structure and clustering.  
- Strength: Clear idea leveraging block-diagonal equivalence and a practical depermutation based on eigenvalues.  
- Strength: Computationally appealing once moments are computed — parameter estimation does not scale with number/length of sequences.  
- Strength: Introduces a heuristic ("spectral longevity") to infer K from sorted eigenvalues.  
- Strength: Real-data experiment suggests spectral estimates can be useful EM initializers.  
- Weakness: Robustness analysis is largely heuristic; Conjecture 1 is unproven and relies on strong spectral-gap assumptions.  
- Weakness: No formal guarantee that spectral HMM estimates will remain close to block-diagonal under realistic noise; clustering may be unstable.  
- Weakness: Synthetic experiments often corrupt permuted matrices rather than re-estimating moments from sampled sequences, bypassing the brittle moment-estimation step.  
- Weakness: Experimental parameterization (Ak, Ok, νk) is underspecified, hurting reproducibility (need sparsity, gaps, emissions, initialization, seeds, code).  
- Clarity issue: Paper is generally readable but misses experimental details and has small, hard-to-read figure text; minor typo in lemma proof subscript.  
- Originality: MHMM-as-block-diagonal is known; novelty lies in depermutation via eigen-structure and the spectral-longevity K-selection heuristic.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for large datasets but practical applicability limited by requirement L ≥ MK and memory/time for MK-state spectral estimation.  
- Significance concern: Lack of end-to-end experiments and robustness guarantees leaves practical performance uncertain.  
- Requested revision: Clarify scope/usage regime, dependence on L ≥ MK, condition number of Ō, and computational/memory complexity vs. EM with wall-clock/memory comparisons.  
- Requested revision: Provide robustness theory replacing or supplementing Conjecture 1 with perturbation-style guarantees relating eigenvalue gaps, mixing rates, sampling error, and noise.  
- Requested revision: Add end-to-end experiments sampling sequences from MHMMs, estimating moments, running full pipeline, and comparing to EM and other baselines.  
- Requested revision: Provide full generative parameter details and code/appendix for reproducibility.  
- Requested revision: Explain unexpected EM behavior when spectral initialization underperforms (training vs. test metrics, monotonicity, regularization/early stopping).  
- Requested revision: Quantitatively report how close learned Â is to block-diagonal and relate that to permutation recovery accuracy.  
- Requested revision: Improve figure readability (larger fonts).  
- Pros for acceptance: Elegant depermutation idea, practical algorithm with speed advantages, and novel K-estimation heuristic.  
- Cons for acceptance: Insufficient theoretical guarantees, incomplete end-to-end empirical validation, applicability constraints (L ≥ MK) not fully addressed, unexplained EM anomalies.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject (weak reject); idea promising but needs stronger theory and end-to-end experiments.
