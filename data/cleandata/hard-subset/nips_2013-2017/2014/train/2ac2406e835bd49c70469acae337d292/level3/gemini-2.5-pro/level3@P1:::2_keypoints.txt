- Summary: Proposes a spectral, method-of-moments algorithm that models a K-component MHMM as a single block-diagonal HMM with MK states and applies spectral HMM learning plus a novel de-permutation step to recover component parameters.

- Key technical contribution: Introduces a de-permutation method using spectral properties and powers of the global transition matrix to cluster columns and recover component assignments, and proposes a "spectral longevity" heuristic to estimate K under noise.

- Strength: Novelty — the de-permutation approach resolves a fundamental barrier to applying spectral HMM learners to MHMMs and enables a computationally efficient alternative to EM.

- Strength: Technical soundness — exploiting powers of the transition matrix to reveal block structure is intuitive and the paper attempts theoretical justification (Conjecture 1, Lemma 3) for the noisy case.

- Weakness: Clarity and notation — inconsistent, sometimes confusing notation with missing dimension declarations and opaque statements (e.g., Corollary 1, lim_{e→∞} Ā^e and vectors v̄k), reducing readability.

- Weakness: Suggests adopting standard HMM notation (e.g., T, n/m) for clarity and explicitly stating matrix/vector dimensions on first use.

- Weakness: Related work — literature review is insufficient, notably omitting work on spectral methods as initializers for EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) despite experiments showing the method is a strong initializer.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation — comparisons with EM are unconvincing; EM's poor performance in plots needs explanation and details (number of restarts, best-of-k vs average) should be provided.

- Weakness: Presentation of results — figures (especially Figure 4) are hard to parse and should include clearer numerical tables for precise comparison.

- Weakness: Real-data findings — spectral method is often outperformed by randomly initialized EM and mainly shines as an EM initializer, contradicting claims that it replaces EM standalone.

- Overall assessment: Interesting and novel de-permutation technique but paper requires substantial revisions in clarity, related-work engagement, and experimental rigor before being publication-ready.

- Arguments for acceptance: The de-permutation method is an elegant, principled solution to a key problem and offers computationally efficient alternatives for large-scale sequence clustering.

- Arguments against acceptance: Major clarity/notational issues, an inadequate literature review on spectral initialization, and insufficiently rigorous experimental comparisons that suggest the method is mainly useful as an EM initializer rather than a full replacement.
