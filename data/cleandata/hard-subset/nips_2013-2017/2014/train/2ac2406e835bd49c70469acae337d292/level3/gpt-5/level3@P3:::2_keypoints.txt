- Proposal: spectral MoM algorithm for MHMMs by reducing to a block-diagonal HMM and using spectral HMM learning plus a stabilization-based depermutation to recover components and estimate K.

- Main novelty: stabilization/depermutation via powers of the estimated global transition matrix and a “spectral longevity” criterion to re-block-diagonalize parameters.

- Relation to prior work: reduction to HMM is standard; connects to spectral HMM methods (Hsu et al., Anandkumar et al.) and spectral initializations (Chaganty & Liang).

- Technical soundness: MHMM→HMM reduction and noiseless depermutation are correct; rank-K reconstruction and column clustering are compelling.

- Theoretical weakness: Conjecture 1 is unproven and bounds reuse mixture-model results without adapting to HMMs; needs perturbation analysis (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin).

- Assumptions concern: requires each Ak have exactly one unit eigenvalue (aperiodicity) and MK < L for MoM, which can be restrictive and needs discussion (near-periodic or reducible chains).

- Empirics limited: synthetic evidence shows feasibility but lacks failure-mode studies (small eigengaps, imbalanced components, emission mismatches, wrong K) and real-data validation is modest.

- Baseline/reporting issues: EM comparisons lack best-of-R restarts, variances, and clearer reporting; EM underperformance unexplained.

- Clarity problems: notation is confusing/inconsistent, dimensions not always defined, Corollary 1 and lim_{e→∞} Ā^e need clearer statements, and figures/tables (e.g., Fig.4) are hard to read.

- Originality: idea of using stabilized limiting behavior to undo permutation is neat, but analysis stops short of rigorous finite-sample guarantees.

- Significance: method could be useful for fast MHMM initialization and estimating K if robustness and theory are strengthened; MK < L requirement limits applicability.

- Pros: clean reduction enabling spectral estimation, intuitive stabilization depermutation, handling of estimation noise, proposed K selection criterion, and shown speed advantages / EM initialization utility.

- Cons: central robustness claim remains a conjecture, strong assumptions (aperiodicity, eigengap, MK < L), limited experiments and ablations, and thin related-work discussion.

- Questions for authors: sensitivity to small eigengaps and near-periodicity, handling reducible chains, stability of k-means clustering vs alternatives, EM restart/variance reporting, and ways to relax MK < L.

- Revision suggestions: replace Conjecture 1 with finite-sample perturbation bounds, justify or replace Eq.(7) with eigengap-based rules, expand related work, fix notation/dimensions, and strengthen experiments (clear tables, EM best/avg, ablations, more datasets).

- Overall recommendation: borderline accept — interesting and practical idea but underdeveloped; recommend rejection unless theory and experiments are substantially strengthened, but suitable for a workshop/poster.
