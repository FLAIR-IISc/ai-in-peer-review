- Proposes learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) via spectral learning by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Uses Method of Moments to estimate HMM parameters, followed by a de-permutation algorithm to recover individual HMMs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and sound theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental results indicate the method is effective on synthetic and real data.  
- Experiments lack detail: datasets, parameter settings, and evaluation metrics need fuller reporting.  
- Missing comparisons with state-of-the-art alternatives to establish empirical superiority.  
- Needs clarification of when the method is appropriate versus when it is impractical (e.g., large, sparse mixtures).  
- Assumes the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse, but offers no guarantee or strategy for near-block but dense matrices under noise.  
- Experiments corrupt permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise instead of learning from sampled observation sequences; should test with realistic observation noise and limited-data effects.  
- Should provide full parameter details (transition and observation matrices) in an appendix or supplement.  
- Originality: novel application of spectral learning to MHMMs, but MoM for HMMs is not new and distinctions from prior work must be clarified.  
- Significance: potentially computationally efficient and accurate, and useful as an initialization for EM.  
- Overall conclusion: promising contribution but requires expanded experiments, clearer limitations, and stronger contextualization with prior work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel MHMM spectral approach, computational efficiency, demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail and comparisons, unclear applicability, unguaranteed sparsity assumption, unrealistic experimental noise model, and missing parameter disclosure.
