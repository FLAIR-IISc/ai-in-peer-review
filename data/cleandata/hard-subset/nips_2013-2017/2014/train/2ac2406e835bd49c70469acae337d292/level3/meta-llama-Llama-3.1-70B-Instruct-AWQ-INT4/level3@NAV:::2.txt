This paper presents a novel spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) by formulating an MHMM as a special case of an HMM and leveraging existing spectral learning techniques. The authors introduce a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1. They also provide an analysis accounting for estimation noise that leverages eigenvalues in the algorithm.

The approach is interesting and intuitive, and the stabilization idea is a key contribution. However, there are some issues with notation, which can be confusing in places. For example, Corollary 1 and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e could be clarified. Additionally, the authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced to improve clarity.

The paper lacks sufficient engagement with related work, particularly in the area of initializing EM with spectral estimates. The authors should cite prior work, such as Chaganty and Liang (2013), to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the field.

The experiments are basic, and the presentation could be improved. For example, Figure 4 is hard to read, and results should be reported in tables. The authors should also report the best (not just average) EM accuracy and provide more thorough comparisons.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, and the stabilization idea is a key contribution. However, the authors could provide more context on how this work differs from previous contributions.

The significance of the paper is that it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The results are important, and other people (practitioners or researchers) are likely to use these ideas or build on them. The paper advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way, providing a unique approach to learning mixtures of HMMs.

Overall, the paper is well-written, but there are some areas that could be improved. The authors should address the issues mentioned above and provide more context on the related work and the significance of the paper.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs.
* The stabilization idea is a key contribution and addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research.
* The results are important, and other people (practitioners or researchers) are likely to use these ideas or build on them.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The notation can be confusing in places, and the authors should clarify certain definitions.
* The paper lacks sufficient engagement with related work, particularly in the area of initializing EM with spectral estimates.
* The experiments are basic, and the presentation could be improved.
* The authors should report the best (not just average) EM accuracy and provide more thorough comparisons.

**Rating:** 7/10

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the issues mentioned above, particularly in terms of notation, related work, and experimental presentation.
