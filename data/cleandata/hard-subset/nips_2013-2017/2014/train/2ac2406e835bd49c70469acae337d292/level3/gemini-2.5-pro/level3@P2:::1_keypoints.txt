- Proposes learning Mixture of HMMs by treating the mixture as a single KM-state HMM with block-diagonal transitions and using Method of Moments spectral learning.  
- Main technical contribution: resolve permutation ambiguity by exponentiating the estimated transition matrix and clustering columns of a low-rank reconstruction to recover component HMMs.  
- Strength — originality/significance: clever, novel framing that could offer a computationally cheaper alternative to EM.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, logically organized, and proofs for the noiseless case are clear.  
- Strength — theory: Lemma 2 and Corollary 1 solidly justify the de-permutation idea in the noiseless setting; conjecture about noisy case is interesting.  
- Weakness — experiments may not simulate realistic estimation error: validation appears to corrupt true permuted matrices with synthetic noise instead of running the full spectral pipeline on sampled sequences.  
- Weakness — missing experimental details: component HMM parameters and other settings are not reported, hindering interpretation and reproducibility.  
- Weakness — suspicious EM result: EM initialized with spectral method performing worse than spectral alone (Table 1) is counterintuitive and suggests a bug, flawed metric, or stopping-criterion issue.  
- Weakness — stability concerns: paper does not sufficiently characterize when a noisy estimate is close enough to a permuted block-diagonal matrix or when clustering will reliably recover components.  
- Weakness — scalability/practicality: method requires estimating a KM-state HMM, so sample and computational costs grow with K and M; paper lacks discussion of feasible regimes.  
- Weakness — presentation issues: figures have illegible text/numbers and there is a minor typo in the proof of Lemma 2 (Λ₂ vs Λ_K).  
- Overall summary: elegant and theoretically motivated approach with solid noiseless analysis, but empirical validation, stability analysis, scalability discussion, and presentation need major revision before acceptance.
