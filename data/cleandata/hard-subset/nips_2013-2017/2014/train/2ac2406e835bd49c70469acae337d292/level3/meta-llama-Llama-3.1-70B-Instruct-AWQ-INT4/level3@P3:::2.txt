This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based algorithm for learning mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The authors leverage the fact that an MHMM can be expressed as a special case of a hidden Markov model (HMM) with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They use an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters up to a permutation ambiguity and then exploit the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping.

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation. This method is based on the observation that each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1. The authors provide an analysis accounting for estimation noise that leverages eigenvalues in the algorithm.

The approach is interesting and intuitive, and the stabilization idea is a key contribution. However, the notation is confusing in places, and the authors should define each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced to improve clarity. For example, the notation in Corollary 1 is unclear, and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e is not well-defined.

The paper lacks sufficient engagement with related work. The authors should cite prior work such as initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty and Liang 2013). The experiments are basic, and the presentation is poor. Figure 4 is hard to read, and the results should be reported in tables. The authors should also report the best (not just average) EM accuracy and provide more thorough comparisons.

The authors should conform to established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009: use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden state counts instead of A/L/J). Overall, the algorithm is a combination of an existing spectral HMM method and a novel stabilization step to undo parameter permutation.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the authors should be more careful in evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Clarity: The paper is not clearly written, and the notation is confusing in places. The authors should improve the clarity of the manuscript by defining each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced.

Originality: The problems or approaches are not entirely new, but the combination of familiar techniques is novel. The paper adequately references related work, but the authors should engage more with prior work.

Significance: The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The approach is computationally cheaper than EM, and the real data experiment shows that it can be used as a good initialization scheme for EM.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for learning MHMMs.
* The stabilization-based method is a key contribution.
* The approach is computationally cheaper than EM.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The notation is confusing in places.
* The paper lacks sufficient engagement with related work.
* The experiments are basic, and the presentation is poor.

Recommendations for improvement:

* Improve the clarity of the manuscript by defining each vector/matrix with dimensions when first introduced.
* Engage more with prior work, including initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty and Liang 2013).
* Report the best (not just average) EM accuracy and provide more thorough comparisons.
* Conform to established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009: use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden state counts instead of A/L/J).
* Improve the presentation of the experiments, including reporting results in tables and making Figure 4 more readable.
