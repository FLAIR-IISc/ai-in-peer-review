- Proposal: Reformulates MHMMs as a special HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix and applies spectral Method of Moments to learn global parameters up to permutation.  
- Novelty: Introduces a spectral stabilization step using eigenstructure to resolve permutation ambiguity across clusters.  
- Key technical contribution: Lemma 3 and Eq. (7) give conditions to estimate number of clusters K and recover individual HMM parameters from permuted global estimates under noise.  
- Motivation: Addresses EM’s high computational cost and sensitivity to initialization for MHMMs.  

- Strength: The spectral depermutation idea is elegant and well-motivated.  
- Strength: Noise analysis (Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3) offers a theoretically grounded way to handle estimation errors.  
- Strength: Empirical speedup over EM is substantial, with comparable accuracy on synthetic data (Figure 4).  
- Strength: Real-data experiment shows the spectral method is a useful initialization for EM, outperforming random starts (Table 1).  

- Weakness: Ambiguous and inconsistent notation (e.g., unclear `1J^T`, dimensions of `1J`, `1>M` vs `1J`) impedes reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Missing explicit dimension specifications for block-diagonal limits (e.g., v̄k ∈ ℝ^{MK}) in key equations.  
- Weakness: Fails to cite relevant prior work (Chaganty & Liang 2013; Smyth 1997), weakening novelty claims.  
- Weakness: Experimental design problems—Figure 4 has overlapping/unclear error bars and axes; Table 1 omits best-of-5 EM accuracy; unclear whether spectral init consistently beats random EM.  
- Weakness: Synthetic experiments are underpowered (only 2 sequences per cluster), limiting robustness claims.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gaps—Conjecture 1’s sample-complexity claim borrows from mixture models without justification for MHMMs; Definition 1’s noise analysis lacks intuitive clarity.  

- Pro: Promising and novel spectral depermutation technique with clear computational benefits.  
- Con: Presentation errors, missing citations, weak experiments, and unverified theoretical claims undermine the paper’s contribution.  

- Recommendation: Reject; paper needs clearer notation with dimensions, comparisons to Chaganty & Liang and Smyth, corrected EM experiment reporting (best-of runs), and stronger theoretical justification for Conjecture 1 before resubmission.
