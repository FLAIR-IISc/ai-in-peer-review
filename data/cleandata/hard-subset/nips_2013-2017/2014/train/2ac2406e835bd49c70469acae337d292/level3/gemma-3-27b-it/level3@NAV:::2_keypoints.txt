- Core idea: Reduce MHMM learning to spectral HMM learning by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and resolve parameter permutation via spectral properties of the global transition matrix.
- Main contribution: A novel stabilization method that uses eigenvalues of the transition matrix to undo parameter permutation.
- Potential benefit: Makes MHMM learning computationally efficient and scalable compared to EM through a Method of Moments approach.
- Strength: Intuitive approach to the permutation ambiguity and a promising stabilization technique.
- Notation issues: Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., unexplained `1J^T`), with missing definitions and unclear Corollary 1.
- Clarity suggestion: Vector and matrix dimensions should be consistently defined on introduction.
- Related work gap: Related work section is sparse and misses directly relevant prior work (e.g., Chaganty and Liang 2013).
- Notation recommendation: Adopt standard HMM notation (e.g., use 'T' for transitions) to improve readability (as in Hsu et al. 2009).
- Experimental weaknesses: Experiments are basic, poorly presented (Figure 4 hard to interpret), and lack summary tables.
- EM results concern: Reported EM performance seems unusually poor; paper should report best and average EM accuracies and explain discrepancies.
- Baselines: Requires more thorough comparisons to baselines and clearer experimental protocols.
- Overall assessment: The algorithm is essentially spectral HMM learning plus a novel stabilization step to resolve permutation.
- Revision needed: Paper is promising but requires substantial revisions for clarity, related work coverage, and stronger experimental validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel stabilization method, computational efficiency potential, tackles a key MHMM challenge.
- Arguments against acceptance: Confusing notation, insufficient related work engagement, and weak experimental evaluation.
