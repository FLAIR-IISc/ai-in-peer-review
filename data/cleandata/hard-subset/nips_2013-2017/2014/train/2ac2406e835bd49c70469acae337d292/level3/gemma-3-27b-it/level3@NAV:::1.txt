## Review of “Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models”

This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by leveraging spectral learning via the Method of Moments (MoM). The core idea is to initially estimate a global HMM (with a block-diagonal transition matrix) and then resolve the permutation ambiguity to recover the individual HMMs within the mixture. This is a compelling approach, particularly for large datasets where traditional EM can be computationally prohibitive. The paper is generally well-written and presents a clear theoretical foundation for the proposed method, building on existing work in spectral learning for HMMs and MoM.

However, despite the theoretical soundness, significant concerns remain regarding the practical performance and applicability of the method. The experimental evaluation lacks sufficient detail and expansion (Major 1). While synthetic data experiments are presented, the parameter settings for the mixture HMMs used in these experiments (Section 4.2) are not fully specified, hindering reproducibility and detailed analysis (Major 5). A comprehensive appendix detailing these parameters would be beneficial.

A fundamental concern is the practicality of estimating a full HMM first, especially for large and sparse mixtures (Major 2). The authors need to clearly delineate scenarios where this approach is appropriate versus when it might be inefficient or intractable. Furthermore, the guarantee of near-sparsity in the spectral-learned transition matrix is questionable (Major 3). The authors acknowledge noise, but don't adequately address the possibility of many full matrices being close to the desired sparse block structure, potentially leading to incorrect de-permutation. 

Critically, the experiments use corrupted permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than generating observed sequences from a true mixture of HMMs (Major 4). This doesn't accurately reflect real-world noise and limits the ability to assess performance under realistic, limited-data conditions. Experiments involving learning from observations are crucial. Lastly, the observation that EM initialized with spectral learning *sometimes* performs worse than spectral learning alone requires a more thorough explanation (Major 6), as EM should generally improve upon an initial estimate. 

Minor issues include a typo in the proof (subscript of Λ should be K, Minor 1) and figures with unreadably small text and numbers (Minor 2).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to MHMM learning.
*   Solid theoretical foundation and clear presentation.
*   Potential computational advantages over EM.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Insufficient experimental validation and detail.
*   Practical concerns regarding scalability and noise sensitivity.
*   Lack of experiments with realistic data generation and noise models.
*   Unexplained performance degradation of EM initialization.



**Overall:** This paper presents interesting ideas with solid theory, but the lack of comprehensive experimental validation and significant practical concerns raise substantial doubts about the method’s performance on real-world datasets. I recommend **rejection** pending substantial revisions addressing the outlined concerns, particularly expanding the experimental evaluation with more realistic data and analysis.
