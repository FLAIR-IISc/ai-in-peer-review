## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a spectral method for learning a Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure inherent in an MHMM to apply existing MoM algorithms for HMMs, and then address the resulting permutation ambiguity through spectral analysis of the global transition matrix. The authors also propose a method to estimate the number of HMMs based on spectral properties.

The work builds upon recent advances in spectral methods for HMM learning (e.g., Hsu, Kakade & Zhang, 2013) and extends them to the more complex MHMM setting. While the application of MoM to MHMMs is a natural extension, the critical contribution lies in tackling the permutation problem, a common challenge in spectral learning. The proposed de-permutation algorithm appears to be a reasonable approach, and the experimental results on both synthetic and real data demonstrate its effectiveness. 

However, the paper suffers from a few weaknesses. The algorithm is described as a “trivial variant” of Hsu, Kakade & Zhang, and the paper doesn’t sufficiently highlight the novelty beyond adapting that work to the MHMM context. A more thorough comparison with existing MHMM learning techniques, particularly those addressing permutation ambiguities, is needed. Crucially, the paper lacks a discussion of Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013), which also addresses spectral learning for mixture models and provides relevant context. The writing also contains occasional typos and grammatical errors (e.g., "For each e, identify of eigenvalues..."), indicating a need for careful proofreading. 

**Strengths:**

*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem: learning MHMMs efficiently.
*   Proposes a practical solution to the permutation ambiguity issue in spectral learning.
*   Demonstrates promising results on both synthetic and real datasets.
*   Offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM-based approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Limited novelty beyond applying existing spectral methods to MHMMs.
*   Insufficiently situates itself within related work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, 2013).
*   Writing quality could be improved with careful proofreading.
*   Theoretical justification for the de-permutation algorithm relies on a conjecture.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a practical problem with a novel approach.
*   The experimental results are encouraging and demonstrate the potential of the method.
*   The work contributes to the growing body of research on spectral learning for latent variable models.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The paper lacks significant novelty and doesn’t sufficiently differentiate itself from existing work.
*   The theoretical analysis is incomplete, relying on a conjecture.
*   The related work section is lacking.

**Overall:**

I believe this paper is borderline. While the technical soundness is reasonable and the experimental results are promising, the lack of significant novelty and the gaps in related work are concerning. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit, addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing a more thorough comparison to existing literature and strengthening the theoretical justification for the proposed algorithm. A more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential failure modes would also be beneficial.
