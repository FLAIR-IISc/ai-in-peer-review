- Proposes a spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by viewing MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transitions.  
- Introduces a de-permutation algorithm using eigenvalue clustering and spectral longevity to resolve permutation ambiguity in spectral estimates.  
- Validated approach on synthetic and real handwritten-character trajectory data, showing practical utility.  
- Empirical results show significant computational advantage over EM, scaling linearly with sequence count while achieving comparable accuracy.  
- Spectral method serves as an effective initialization for EM, improving real-data performance.  
- Experimental design is thorough, exploring noise sensitivity, data quantity vs. accuracy, and real-world applicability.  
- Main novelty is the de-permutation step; core spectral framework largely adapts prior HMM spectral methods.  
- The paper understates prior related work—missing explicit comparison to Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013) which addressed analogous de-permutation issues.  
- Theoretical justification for the de-permutation (Conjecture 1) is incomplete and relies on sample-complexity assumptions from different models.  
- Error bound derivation borrows from mixture-of-Gaussians analysis and lacks rigorous adaptation to MHMMs.  
- Some spectral conditions required for correctness (e.g., eigenvalue separations) may not hold in practice.  
- Clarity issues: related work inadequately discussed and minor proofreading/notation errors reduce readability.  
- Overall quality: strong empirical execution but theoretical claims and novelty framing need strengthening.  
- Significance: addresses a practical scalability bottleneck for sequence clustering and is valuable for large-scale MHMM learning.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept—publishable after revisions clarifying novelty relative to prior work and fortifying theoretical analysis.
