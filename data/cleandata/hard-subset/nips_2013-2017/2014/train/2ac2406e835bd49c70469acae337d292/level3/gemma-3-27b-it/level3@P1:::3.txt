## Review of Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

This paper presents a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM), addressing a key challenge in MHMM learning – the permutation ambiguity in parameter estimation. The authors leverage the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve this ambiguity and recover individual HMM parameters, offering a computationally efficient alternative to traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithms.

The work builds upon recent popularity of MoM-based algorithms [1, 2, 3] for latent variable models, noting their computational advantages.  MHMMs [4] are recognized as powerful tools for sequence clustering with broad applications [5, 6, 7], however, standard EM implementations become computationally prohibitive for large datasets.  The paper correctly identifies the core issue: while MoM can be applied to an MHMM viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, recovering the parameters of the *individual* HMMs requires resolving the permutation ambiguity. The proposed solution, utilizing spectral analysis, is the primary contribution of the paper.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a significant problem:** The permutation ambiguity is a well-known obstacle in MHMM learning. The authors directly tackle this issue with a theoretically grounded and practically motivated approach.
* **Computational Efficiency:** By leveraging MoM, the authors offer a potentially faster learning algorithm, particularly advantageous for large datasets where EM struggles. This is a significant benefit for practical applications.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a mathematical foundation for the approach, demonstrating the equivalence between an MHMM and a specific structured HMM (Lemma 1).  The spectral properties of the transition matrix are analyzed (Lemmas 2 & 3, Conjecture 1) to support the de-permutation algorithm.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results, including synthetic data and a real-world handwritten character trajectory dataset, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.  The comparison with EM, both with random and MoM initialization, provides valuable insights.
* **Clear Presentation of the Algorithm:** Algorithm 1 provides a concise and easily understandable overview of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty:** The core MoM approach builds heavily on existing work like Hsu, Kakade & Zhang [2]. While the adaptation to MHMMs and the de-permutation algorithm represent contributions, the fundamental spectral learning technique is not entirely novel. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of this lineage.
* **Missing Related Work:** The paper overlooks relevant work. For example, Chaganty and Liang [8] present spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions, a closely related approach that should be discussed in the context of this work. This oversight detracts from a complete understanding of the paper's position within the field.
* **Proofreading/Typos:**  There are some minor typographical errors and awkward phrasing throughout the paper (e.g., “For each e, identify of eigenvalues…”). Careful proofreading would significantly improve clarity.
* **Conjectures & Assumptions:**  Key aspects of the algorithm's performance rely on Conjecture 1, which states conditions for accurate parameter recovery. While plausible, this conjecture requires further theoretical justification or empirical support beyond the presented results.
* **Real Data Experiment:** While the real data experiment is valuable, the use of a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy the MoM input requirements feels like a workaround. A discussion on the implications of this transformation, and potential alternative approaches, would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field of MHMM learning. The authors effectively address the critical problem of permutation ambiguity by leveraging spectral analysis, building upon a well-established framework (MoM). While the core spectral learning technique isn't entirely novel, the adaptation to MHMMs and the proposed de-permutation algorithm are valuable contributions. The empirical results are encouraging. The paper would be strengthened by a more thorough discussion of related work and a rigorous examination of the assumptions underpinning Conjecture 1.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a challenging problem in MHMM learning.
* Offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM.
* Provides a theoretically grounded approach with empirical validation.
* Clear and well-structured presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited novelty beyond the specific application to MHMMs.
* Omission of relevant related work.
* Reliance on a conjecture that requires further investigation.
* Minor proofreading issues.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The authors should address the missing related work (specifically [8]) and thoroughly proofread the manuscript.  Strengthening the theoretical justification for Conjecture 1 would also be beneficial.  Addressing these points will elevate the paper and further solidify its contribution to the field.
