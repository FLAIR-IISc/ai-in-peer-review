- Presents a novel Method-of-Moments (MoM) approach to learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by reformulating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.
- Addresses permutation ambiguity by using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping and recover individual HMM parameters.
- Proposes a method to estimate the number of HMM components under certain spectral conditions.
- Builds on recent MoM advances for HMMs and offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM-based methods.
- Positions MHMM learning as sequence clustering with applications across music, motion, and time-series domains.
- The approach is conceptually appealing and frames MHMM learning as a special case of HMM learning with spectral disambiguation.
- Strength: method is novel and avoids the computational bottlenecks of EM while tackling permutation ambiguity effectively.
- Strength: grounded in a solid theoretical framework; insightful discussion on estimation noise and component-count conditions.
- Strength: MoM algorithms provide clear computational efficiency advantages for large datasets.
- Weakness: notation is dense and unclear; matrix/vector dimensions and block-diagonal structure need clearer definitions and standard HMM notation.
- Weakness: related work coverage is insufficient, notably missing discussion of spectral initialization of EM and Chaganty & Liang (2013).
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is basic and presentation is poor (e.g., Figure 4 hard to interpret); results need clear tables and statistical analysis.
- Weakness: EM performance reporting is questionable; authors should report best EM across restarts and provide fuller comparative analysis.
- Weakness: some proofs and justifications (especially Conjecture 1) feel incomplete and need more detail or references.
- Arguments for acceptance: genuinely novel solution to permutation ambiguity, solid theoretical grounding, and strong scalability potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity, incomplete related work, weak experiments, and underdeveloped proofs.
- Overall recommendation: reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit after improving clarity, literature review, experiments, and theoretical justification.
