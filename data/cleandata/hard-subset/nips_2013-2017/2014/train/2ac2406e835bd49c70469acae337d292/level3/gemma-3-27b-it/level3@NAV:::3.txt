## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a spectral method for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure inherent in MHMMs and apply a spectral learning algorithm for HMMs, addressing the permutation ambiguity that arises in MoM estimation. The authors then introduce a de-permutation algorithm based on spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters and the number of components.

The approach builds upon existing MoM techniques [1, 2, 3] and related spectral methods for HMM learning [4], but crucially tackles the permutation problem specific to mixture models. While the underlying MoM framework is not novel, the focus on addressing the permutation issue – and the proposed solution utilizing spectral analysis – represents a worthwhile contribution. The paper correctly identifies the computational advantages of MoM, particularly for large datasets, as a motivation for this work. 

However, the algorithm's reliance on the core ideas presented in Hsu, Kakade & Zhang [4] is significant. The contribution feels incremental, largely focusing on adapting existing techniques to the MHMM context and dealing with the permutation challenge. Furthermore, the recovered transition matrix can still suffer from arbitrary permutation mixing components, and the success of the de-permutation relies heavily on assumptions about the noise level and spectral separation. While the authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to work reasonably well, its theoretical justification feels somewhat hand-wavy, relying on conjectures rather than rigorous proofs. 

The paper suffers from occasional typos and proofreading issues ("For each e, identify of eigenvalues..."). More importantly, the related work section is incomplete. The authors should explicitly discuss Chaganty & Liang [8] and its relevance to spectral methods for mixture models, as it presents a closely related approach. The experimental evaluation is reasonable, demonstrating the method's performance on synthetic and real datasets. However, a more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art MHMM learning techniques (beyond EM) would strengthen the paper. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses the critical issue of permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning via MoM.
*   Presents a reasonable algorithm for de-permuting the spectral outputs.
*   Demonstrates promising results on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Algorithm is largely a variation of Hsu, Kakade & Zhang [4].
*   Theoretical justification of the de-permutation algorithm is incomplete.
*   Related work section is lacking, notably missing Chaganty & Liang [8].
*   Occasional clarity issues due to typos and imprecision.



**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a reasonable contribution to the field but falls short of being a major breakthrough. The core idea is sound, but the novelty is limited, and the theoretical guarantees require further development. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to address the identified weaknesses and strengthen the theoretical foundation. Specifically, a more detailed comparison to existing spectral mixture modeling techniques and a more rigorous analysis of the de-permutation algorithm would be valuable improvements.
