- Proposes treating a Mixture of HMMs as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix and applies spectral learning accordingly.  
- Main technical contribution is a de-permutation algorithm that resolves permutation ambiguity to recover individual HMM components.  
- Practical algorithm effectively addresses a key challenge in spectral learning for mixture models.  
- Experimental results show computational advantages over EM on large datasets and that the method is a good EM initializer on real data.  
- Approach is well-motivated and the technical development is largely sound.  
- Core algorithm is mainly a straightforward extension of Hsu, Kakade & Zhang (2012); novelty is largely the de-permutation step.  
- Paper omits important related work, notably Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013), which addresses similar permutation issues.  
- Theoretical analysis has gaps (e.g., Conjecture 1 lacks justification and borrows results from a different model).  
- Contains several proofreading and presentation errors that reduce professionalism.  
- Overall significance is moderate: computationally useful but limited novelty; not sufficient for acceptance without stronger theory, better literature context, and careful proofreading.
