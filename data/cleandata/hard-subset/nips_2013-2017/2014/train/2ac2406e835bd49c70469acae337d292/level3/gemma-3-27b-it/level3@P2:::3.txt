This paper presents a novel spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea leverages the Method of Moments (MoM) to efficiently learn an MHMM, framing it as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. A key challenge addressed is the permutation ambiguity inherent in the MoM estimation, which the authors tackle through analysis of the global transition matrix’s spectral properties to estimate a de-permutation mapping. The validity of the approach is demonstrated on both synthetic and real data.

The work builds upon a growing body of literature employing MoM for latent variable model learning, citing Anandkumar et al. [1, 2] and Hsu, Kakade & Zhang [3]. It’s well-motivated, recognizing the computational limitations of traditional EM-based MHMM learning, particularly for large datasets. The connection made between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is crucial for applying existing spectral learning techniques. The problem of permutation ambiguity is clearly stated, and the proposed solution, using spectral properties, is a significant contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The application of spectral methods to MHMM learning via a MoM framework is a novel contribution. While building on prior work (e.g., Hsu et al.), it extends these techniques to a more complex model.
* **Efficiency:** The MoM approach, coupled with the spectral de-permutation, offers a potential computational advantage over EM, especially for large datasets, as evidenced in the experimental section.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides lemmas and conjectures attempting to formalize the de-permutation algorithm and establish conditions for its success. While some aspects are presented as conjectures, the attempt at theoretical grounding is commendable.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments on both synthetic and real data (handwritten character trajectories) demonstrate the feasibility and reasonable performance of the proposed approach. The comparison with EM, including MoM-initialized EM, is valuable.
* **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of MHMM learning and the specific issues arising from the MoM estimation process.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Related Work:** The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work. The authors should acknowledge and contrast their approach with Chaganty & Liang [8] on spectral experts for regression mixtures. This would position the paper more effectively within the broader literature.
* **Clarity & Proofreading:** The paper suffers from occasional clarity issues and apparent proofreading errors (e.g., "For each e, identify of eigenvalues..."). While generally understandable, these detract from the overall quality. More careful review is needed.
* **Conjecture Reliance:**  A significant portion of the theoretical justification relies on conjectures. While not necessarily a fatal flaw, these conjectures should be clearly flagged as such, and their limitations discussed.  A more rigorous analysis would strengthen the paper.
* **Experimental Detail:** The experimental section could benefit from more detail. Reporting standard deviations or confidence intervals would provide a more robust assessment of performance. The choice of the cubic polynomial feature transformation in the real data experiment warrants further justification.
* **Spectral Conditions:** The spectral conditions outlined in Definitions 1 and 3, and Lemma 3, while interesting, appear somewhat complex and may limit the practical applicability of the algorithm.  Further discussion on the prevalence of these conditions in real-world scenarios would be helpful.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a genuinely novel approach to a challenging problem (MHMM learning).
* The proposed method offers a potential computational advantage over existing techniques.
* The empirical results are promising and demonstrate the feasibility of the approach.
* The paper attempts to provide theoretical justification for the proposed algorithm, even if incomplete.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper relies heavily on conjectures, lacking a fully rigorous theoretical analysis.
* The clarity and presentation could be improved through careful proofreading and more detailed explanations.
* The related work section is incomplete and doesn’t adequately situate the paper within the broader literature.
* The practical applicability of the approach is potentially limited by the stringent spectral conditions.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of MHMM learning. The proposed spectral approach offers a novel solution to the permutation ambiguity problem and a potential improvement in computational efficiency. However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues, relies on conjectures, and could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work.  

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **weak accept**.  The novelty and potential benefits of the approach, combined with the empirical validation, outweigh the weaknesses. However, I strongly encourage the authors to address the concerns raised regarding clarity, theoretical rigor, and related work before publication. A more comprehensive revision addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper.
