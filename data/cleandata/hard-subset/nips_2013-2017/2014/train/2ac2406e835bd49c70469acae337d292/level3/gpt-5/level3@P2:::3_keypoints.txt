- Proposes a spectral (method-of-moments) approach for learning mixtures of HMMs by reducing an MHMM to a block-diagonal HMM and applying existing spectral HMM learning.  
- Main novelty is a “de-permutation” procedure: rank-K eigen reconstruction of the transition matrix and clustering its columns to recover component blocks, plus a heuristic “spectral longevity” to estimate K.  
- Positions work in the spectral/moment-based literature (Hsu et al., Anandkumar et al.), but needs broader comparison to tensor/spectral mixture alignment methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang).  
- Reduction and use of spectral HMM learning are sound and standard; de-permutation is intuitive and works noiselessly via eigenvalue multiplicity.  
- Key theoretical claims are heuristic: Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3 lack formal proofs, perturbation bounds, and finite-sample guarantees for de-permutation and K estimation.  
- Reusing sample-complexity bounds from other mixture models is not rigorously justified for MHMMs.  
- Method assumes each component Ak has a unique eigenvalue 1; behavior under near-degenerate spectra or slow mixing (small gaps) is insufficiently analyzed.  
- The requirement MK < L (feature dimension constraint) is restrictive and forces feature lifting in experiments; implications are not fully discussed.  
- Synthetic experiments show de-permutation works across sequence lengths and is faster than EM, providing competitive clustering when data is sufficient.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: lacks systematic stress-tests on spectral gaps/noise, ablation studies for de-permutation, robustness to mis-specified K/M, and scalability tests for larger MK.  
- Real-data experiment indicates the spectral method is a good EM initializer, but feature lifting effects (polynomial features, L increase) and distortions are not discussed.  
- Reproducibility issues: missing details (exact e-range for longevity, clustering distance/normalization, handling varying sequence lengths, choice of M) and no code/pseudocode for moments and de-permutation.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear with a helpful equivalence lemma and algorithm summary, but the spectral longevity concept needs more intuition and hyperparameter guidance (γ, e-range).  
- Writing has typos and notation inconsistencies; needs careful proofreading and clearer definitions when introducing quantities and algorithm parameters.  
- Originality is limited: reduction to block-diagonal HMM and spectral HMM learning are not novel; contribution lies in the practical de-permutation/K-estimation heuristic.  
- Significance is practical but constrained by theoretical gaps, MK < L requirement, and limited empirical scope—contribution feels incremental but useful.  
- Pros: addresses an under-discussed alignment problem, offers a simple interpretable eigenstructure-based procedure, is empirically faster than EM, and provides a clear algorithmic recipe.  
- Cons: main claims are conjectural without rigorous perturbation analysis, experiments are narrow, MK < L constraint limits applicability, and related-work coverage is too narrow.  
- Key suggestions: provide formal perturbation guarantees (e.g., Davis–Kahan), replace conjectures with theorems or clarify regimes of consistency, analyze/justify spectral longevity, expand experiments, and broaden related work.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos (e.g., “identify of eigenvalues”), standardize notation, clarify column normalization and clustering metric, and specify algorithm parameter ranges and stopping criteria.  
- Overall assessment: practically motivated and clearly described but incremental and borderline due to lack of theoretical guarantees and limited experiments; stronger analysis and broader evaluation needed.
