- Core idea: spectral learning for Mixture of HMMs by treating MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices and resolving permutation ambiguity via a stabilization/depermutation method.  
- Novelty: leveraging spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters is clever and intuitive.  
- Theoretical support: Lemma 2 and Corollary 1 give a foundation for the approach; Equation 7 provides a noise analysis acknowledging practical limits.  
- Empirical support: experiments show the algorithm can recover the correct number of clusters and block structure with modest data.  
- Notation problems: notation is confusing (e.g., meaning of 1J^T), dimensions are not defined at first use, and some spectral-structure claims (limit of A^e) are not well explained.  
- Missing related work: fails to cite and engage relevant prior work, notably spectral methods for initializing EM (Chaganty & Liang 2013), and does not clearly distinguish from existing MHMM methods.  
- Experimental design issues: Figure 4 is poorly designed and hard to interpret; results should be presented in tables; comparisons are insufficient.  
- EM evaluation flaw: reporting average EM accuracy is misleading given EM’s sensitivity to initialization; best-case results and comparisons to other initializations are needed.  
- Notational conventions: deviates from standard HMM notation (uses A instead of T, nonstandard symbols for state/obs counts) and should adopt common conventions.  
- Theoretical gaps: Conjecture 1 is unproven and the noise analysis lacks rigorous justification.  
- Limited practical significance: experiments are basic and real-data gains over random initialization are marginal, weakening the claims about utility as an EM initializer.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound but incremental; main novelty is the stabilization method, while clarity, related-work engagement, and experiments weaken the contribution.  
- Recommendation: major revisions required before acceptance.  
- Required revisions suggested: clarify notation and dimensions, add related-work discussion (including Chaganty & Liang 2013), replace Figure 4 with tables and report best/average results, provide more rigorous noise/theoretical analysis, adopt standard HMM notation, and run more thorough comparisons with existing methods.
