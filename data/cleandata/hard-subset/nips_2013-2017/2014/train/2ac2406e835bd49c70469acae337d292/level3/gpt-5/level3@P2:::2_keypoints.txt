- Proposes learning Mixtures of HMMs by treating them as a single HMM with MK states and applying spectral/MoM estimators.  
- Main technical contribution is a stabilization-based depermutation: recover block structure from the global transition spectrum and estimate K via a “spectral longevity” heuristic.  
- Theoretical support includes noiseless structural lemmas and a conjectured finite-sample stability under spectral separation.  
- Strength: simple, intuitive observation that MHMMs ≡ HMM with block-diagonal transition, enabling off-the-shelf spectral methods.  
- Strength: clever depermutation via rank-K reconstruction and column clustering to resolve permutation ambiguity in spectral methods.  
- Strength: practical longevity metric for K that matches the block-diagonal spectral picture.  
- Strength: computationally attractive—estimation time independent of dataset size after moment computation, with speedups over EM.  
- Strength: useful as an initialization for EM, improving robustness and runtime in experiments.  
- Weakness: robustness claims under noise are conjectural with no finite-sample perturbation guarantees for depermutation or K selection.  
- Weakness: strong assumption that each component has a single eigenvalue at 1 and others inside the unit disk; no analysis for near-periodicity, defective matrices, or small spectral gaps.  
- Weakness: K-estimation criterion is heuristic, with no guidance on choosing γ or sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: omission of details on recovering π and per-component νk from the stacked global stationary vector.  
- Weakness: requirement MK < L for the MoM estimator is limiting; the cubic feature-expansion workaround may distort the model and lacks analysis.  
- Clarity issue: notation and dimensions are loose; several definitions (e.g., 1M, block structure of limit matrices) are unclear.  
- Clarity issue: recommend adopting standard HMM notation and aligning with Hsu et al. (2009) for readability.  
- Clarity issue: Algorithm 1 lacks explicit description of how clustering yields the permutation P̃, choice of distance, initialization, and k-means stability.  
- Originality: casting MHMM as HMM is known, but adapting spectral HMM estimators with spectral stabilization and a novel K-selection heuristic is a focused contribution.  
- Significance: if robustified and validated, the method could substantially reduce cost of fitting MHMMs and serve as strong EM initializers.  
- Empirical shortcoming: synthetic experiments are basic—need quantitative metrics (ARI/NMI, parameter/block recovery, K accuracy) with confidence intervals.  
- Empirical shortcoming: EM baselines need clearer reporting (best/average over restarts, stopping criteria, initialization, regularization); Fig.4 EM results unclear.  
- Empirical shortcoming: recommend clearer figures/tables, runtime breakdowns, and additional baselines (EM initialized with spectral, variational MHMMs, other spectral mixture methods).  
- Empirical shortcoming: require robustness ablations over eigenvalue gaps, noise, K/M, sequence length, observation dim, and stress tests for depermutation.  
- Specific questions: clarify conditions on Ak (ergodic/aperiodic/diagonalizable) and failure modes for near-periodic or reducible components.  
- Specific questions: explain how γ in Eq.(7) is chosen and sensitivity of K̂ to γ and eigenvalue noise.  
- Specific questions: detail how π and νk are recovered from ν̄ after depermutation, and specify clustering distance/initialization and P̃ stability.  
- Specific questions: provide complexity and memory usage for moment computation as functions of N, T, L, M, K, and cite related spectral initialization work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang).  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant, computationally attractive depermutation with clear intuition and a practical K heuristic; potential impact as EM initializer.  
- Cons for acceptance: key robustness claims are unproven, experimental validation is limited and presentation weak, and exposition/related-work engagement needs improvement.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger theoretical support, clearer exposition, and more thorough, systematic empirical validation.
