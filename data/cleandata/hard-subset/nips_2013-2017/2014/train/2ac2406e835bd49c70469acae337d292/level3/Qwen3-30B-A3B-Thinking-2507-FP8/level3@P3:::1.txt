

# Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

This paper proposes a novel spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by recognizing that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage the method of moments (MoM) to estimate the global HMM parameters up to permutation ambiguity, then resolve this ambiguity using spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The approach provides a computationally efficient alternative to traditional EM-based methods for MHMM learning, which require expensive forward-backward passes for each sequence in every iteration.

The work builds upon Anandkumar et al.'s spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2, 3] by extending it to the more complex MHMM setting. The key insight that MHMMs can be viewed as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is well-motivated and elegantly formalized in Lemma 1. The authors then develop a method to recover the individual HMMs by resolving the permutation ambiguity through spectral analysis of the global transition matrix, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**
The theoretical foundation is sound, with clear lemmas establishing the relationship between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal structure. The spectral approach to resolving permutation ambiguity is novel and well-justified. The method provides a theoretically grounded alternative to EM with computational advantages, particularly for large datasets. The real data experiment demonstrates practical utility by showing the method can serve as an effective initialization for EM.

**Major Weaknesses:**

1. **Experimental Results:** The experimental section lacks sufficient detail. Section 4.2 fails to specify the exact mixture HMM parameters (transition and observation matrices), making it difficult to assess the conditions under which the method works. Parameter details should be provided in an appendix.

2. **Practicality Concerns:** The paper does not adequately address when the method is appropriate versus when it is not. Estimating a full HMM first (with MK states) is likely impractical for large, sparse mixtures where MK is large. The authors should clarify the practical scope of their approach.

3. **Sparsity Guarantee:** There is no guarantee that the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse. Many full matrices may be close to the desired sparse block structure under noise, potentially leading to poor recovery of the block-diagonal structure.

4. **Experimental Design Flaw:** The experiments in Section 4.1 corrupt permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling observed sequences from a mixture of HMMs. This fails to reflect real-world noise and limited-data effects. The authors should demonstrate learning from actual sequences to validate the approach.

5. **EM Performance Anomaly:** Section 4.2 shows that EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone. Since EM should typically improve results, the authors need to explain this counterintuitive observation.

**Minor Weaknesses:**
- Typo in proof: Subscript of Λ should be K rather than 2 (in the proof of Lemma 2).
- Figures have text and numbers that are too small to read, hindering interpretation.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting theoretical contribution with solid mathematical foundation. The spectral approach to resolving permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning is novel and potentially valuable. However, significant doubts remain about the practical performance of the method on real datasets, particularly given the experimental limitations and lack of clarity about the method's appropriate application domains.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by:
1. Providing full parameter details for all experiments
2. Demonstrating learning from actual sequences rather than corrupted matrices
3. Clarifying the practical limitations and appropriate use cases for the method
4. Explaining the EM performance anomaly

While the theoretical ideas are strong and the method shows promise as an initialization strategy for EM, the current experimental validation does not sufficiently demonstrate the approach's practical utility for real-world MHMM learning problems. The authors should address these concerns before the method can be considered a practical alternative to existing approaches.

**Recommendation:**

The paper has strong theoretical contributions but requires significant experimental improvements to demonstrate practical value. I recommend rejection with the understanding that the authors could resubmit a revised version addressing the major concerns about experimental design, practical applicability, and parameter specification. The theoretical insights are valuable but need stronger empirical validation to support their practical utility.
