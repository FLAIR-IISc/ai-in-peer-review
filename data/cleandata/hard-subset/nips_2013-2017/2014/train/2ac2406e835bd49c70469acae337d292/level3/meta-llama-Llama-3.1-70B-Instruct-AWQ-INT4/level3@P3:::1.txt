This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using the Method of Moments (MoM) and spectral learning. The authors address the issue of permutation ambiguity in MHMM estimation by exploiting the spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The proposed algorithm first estimates the parameters of a global HMM using MoM, then resolves the permutation ambiguity using the eigenvalue decomposition of the transition matrix.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed approach. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in estimating the parameters of MHMMs.

However, there are several concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, the experimental results are limited, and the authors need to provide more details about the experiments, such as the choice of hyperparameters, the number of restarts, and the stopping criterion. Additionally, the authors should clarify when the proposed method is appropriate and when it is not. For example, the authors mention that estimating a full HMM first is impractical for large, sparse mixtures, but they do not provide a clear guideline on how to determine the sparsity of the mixture.

Another concern is that there is no guarantee that the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse. Many full matrices may be close to the desired sparse block structure under noise, which could affect the performance of the proposed algorithm. The authors should provide more analysis on the robustness of the algorithm to noise and the choice of hyperparameters.

Furthermore, the experiments in Section 4.2 corrupt the permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling observed sequences from a mixture of HMMs. The authors should provide experiments that learn from observations to reflect real noise and limited-data effects. Additionally, the authors should provide more details about the mixture HMM parameters, such as the transition and observation matrices, in Section 4.2.

It is also surprising that EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone. The authors should provide an explanation for this phenomenon, as EM should typically improve the results.

Minor issues include a typo in the proof, where the subscript of Λ should be K rather than 2, and the figures have text and numbers that are too small to read.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting idea and solid theory, but significant doubts remain about the practical performance of the proposed algorithm on real datasets. The authors need to address the concerns mentioned above to strengthen the paper.
