- Reframes Mixtures of HMMs as a single larger HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix to apply spectral HMM learning.  
- Tackles permutation ambiguity in spectral outputs that arbitrarily shuffles latent states across components.  
- Main contribution is a novel de-permutation algorithm that uses the powered/limiting behavior of the learned transition matrix to cluster permuted states and recover individual HMMs.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical bottleneck for applying spectral methods to mixture models.  
- Originality: de-permutation is novel and elegant; the initial spectral learning step is mostly a straightforward application of prior work.  
- Quality: empirical results show the method is much faster than EM and the de-permutation works well under moderate noise.  
- Framing critique: paper should emphasize the de-permutation contribution more, since the spectral HMM step is a trivial variant.  
- Related-work gap: fails to discuss Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013) and other literature on aligning spectral outputs.  
- Presentation and rigor issues: occasional typos and awkward phrasing; some technical arguments (e.g., justification for Conjecture 1) are sketched and need more rigorous detail.
