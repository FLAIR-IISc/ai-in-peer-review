## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using spectral learning based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure inherent in MHMMs to circumvent the permutation ambiguity typically encountered in MoM-based estimation. The authors demonstrate theoretically how to estimate a de-permutation mapping using spectral properties and present experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets. 

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation and technical details of the proposed method. The connection to existing MoM literature and the explanation of the MHMM as a special case of an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix are particularly well-articulated. The theoretical justification for the de-permutation step, while relying on a conjecture, is presented logically. 

However, significant concerns remain regarding the practical applicability and performance of the method. The experimental evaluation lacks sufficient detail. Specifically, Section 4.2 provides limited information about the parameters of the mixture HMMs used (transition and observation matrices), hindering reproducibility and a thorough assessment of the method's sensitivity to parameter settings. Providing these details, perhaps in an appendix, is crucial. 

A major concern is the assumption that the spectral-learned transition matrix will be *nearly* sparse. The authors don’t adequately address the scenario where many full matrices might exhibit a similar sparse block structure due to noise, potentially leading to incorrect de-permutation. Furthermore, the experiments primarily corrupt permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise, rather than simulating observations directly from a mixture of HMMs. This is a critical limitation, as it doesn’t reflect the noise characteristics of real-world data and the impact of limited data availability.

Interestingly, the authors observe that EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs *worse* than spectral learning alone. This is counterintuitive, as EM should generally refine an initial estimate. A clear explanation for this phenomenon is needed. Finally, a minor typo exists in the proof (subscript of Λ should be K, not 2), and the figures contain text and numbers that are too small to read.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to MHMM learning using spectral methods.
*   Solid theoretical foundation and clear explanation of the core ideas.
*   Demonstrated potential as an initialization scheme for EM.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited experimental detail and lack of experiments with realistic noise.
*   Concerns about the robustness of the spectral de-permutation step.
*   Unexplained performance degradation of EM with spectral initialization.
*   Lack of clarity regarding the method’s applicability to large, sparse mixtures.



**Overall:** While the paper presents interesting ideas and a solid theoretical framework, the significant doubts regarding practical performance and the lack of comprehensive experimental validation prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance at this time. I recommend rejection, but encourage the authors to address the raised concerns and resubmit.
