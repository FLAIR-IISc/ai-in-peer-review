- Proposes a novel spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by formulating an MHMM as a special case of an HMM and using existing spectral techniques.  
- Introduces a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides an analysis that accounts for estimation noise and leverages eigenvalues in the algorithm.  
- Stabilization idea is a key, intuitive contribution that addresses a difficult problem.  
- Notation is confusing in places (e.g., Corollary 1, block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e) and vectors/matrices should be defined with dimensions when first introduced.  
- Paper lacks sufficient engagement with related work, especially on initializing EM with spectral estimates; should cite prior work like Chaganty and Liang (2013).  
- Experiments are basic and presentation needs improvement (Figure 4 hard to read); results should be reported in tables.  
- Authors should report best EM accuracy (not just averages) and provide more thorough empirical comparisons.  
- Originality: a novel combination of familiar techniques, with the stabilization idea as the main novel element.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art on a hard problem and is likely to be useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Overall well-written but requires minor revisions to address notation, related work, and experimental presentation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 7/10.
