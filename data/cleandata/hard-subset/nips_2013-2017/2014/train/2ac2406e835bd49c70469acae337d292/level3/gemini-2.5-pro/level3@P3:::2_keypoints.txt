- Core contribution: Reformulates a K-component MHMM as a single HMM with MK states and applies spectral HMM learning plus a novel stabilization to recover component parameters.  
- Stabilization method: Uses the eigenspectrum of the estimated global transition matrix (K largest eigenvalues) to cluster permuted hidden states and de-permute parameters.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides noise-robustness analysis for the de-permutation step, leveraging eigenvalue separation and spectral gap arguments.  
- Novelty and elegance: The stabilization idea is clever and intuitive, using matrix exponentiation/eigenspectrum to reveal block structure.  
- Problem formulation strength: Casting MHMM as a block-diagonal large HMM cleanly connects to existing spectral learning machinery.  
- Proof/analysis concerns: Some theoretical claims rely on reused sample-complexity bounds (Conjecture 1) without formal justification in this setting.  
- Notation and clarity issues: Confusing notation, missing dimension declarations, and unclear statements (e.g., Corollary 1) hinder readability and require significant rewriting.  
- Related work omission: Fails to adequately cite/discuss spectral initialization for EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) despite using spectral initialization in experiments.  
- Experimental presentation problems: Figures are cluttered and hard to read; quantitative results would be clearer in tables and better-explained plots.  
- Baseline concerns: EM baseline appears unusually poor; authors should report best-of-multiple-restarts rather than averages to provide a fair comparison.  
- Incomplete comparisons: Missing a controlled comparison between random-EM, spectral-only, and spectral-initialized-EM in synthetic experiments (done only on real data).  
- Overall judgement: Promising and valuable main idea, but weakened by unclear exposition, insufficient related-work discussion, and unconvincing/poorly presented experiments; needs substantial revision.
