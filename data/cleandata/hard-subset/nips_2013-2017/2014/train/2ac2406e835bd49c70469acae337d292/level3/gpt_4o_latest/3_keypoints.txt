- Presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs and a novel depermutation algorithm to resolve permutation ambiguities.  
- Builds on the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang spectral algorithm, adapting it to the MHMM setting.  
- Depermutation leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and one real-world dataset, showing computational advantages over EM.  

- Technical contribution: extends spectral methods to MHMMs and proposes a well-motivated depermutation algorithm that performs reasonably under noise.  
- Computational efficiency: avoids iterative EM, making it faster and suitable for large datasets.  
- Experimental validation: thorough synthetic comparisons with EM and application to real data showing competitive accuracy and speed.  
- Potential impact: useful for sequence clustering and may inspire spectral methods for other hierarchical latent models.  

- Clarity and organization: paper has typos and dense theoretical sections that hinder accessibility.  
- Contextualization: insufficient comparison to related work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang) and broader literature.  
- Assumptions and limitations: relies on spectral conditions (e.g., eigenvalue separation) whose practical implications are not fully explored.  
- Real-world applicability: experiments limited to a single real dataset, weakening generalizability claims.  

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem, offers a computationally efficient and practically useful method, and shows promising experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs clearer writing and organization, better contextualization of novelty, and deeper analysis of underlying assumptions and broader experiments.  

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and contextualization.
