- Proposes learning MHMMs via a spectral, moment-based method by reframing an MHMM as a large HMM with block-diagonal transition and then de-permuting the learned transition matrix.
- Novel second step recovers block structure from the permuted global transition matrix using spectral properties.
- Strength: original, elegant idea with solid theoretical justification and clear presentation.
- Requires estimating a full MK x MK HMM intermediate, which seems impractical when K is large and M small; computational and statistical trade-offs/appropriate regimes are not clarified.
- De-permutation critically assumes the estimated transition matrix is nearly sparse/block-diagonal; no guarantees under finite-sample noise and dense near-miss matrices may break clustering.
- Experiments are unconvincing: synthetic setup corrupts an already-permuted matrix with Dirichlet noise instead of learning from sequences, not reflecting real estimation challenges.
- Key experimental details (HMM parameters in Section 4.2) are missing, hindering reproducibility.
- Unexplained empirical finding: EM initialized with the spectral method performs worse than the spectral method alone, contradicting the claimed benefit of initialization.
- Minor: figures contain text too small to be legible.
- Minor: apparent typo in Lemma 2 proof (Λ subscript should be K).
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but practical significance is unclear due to strong assumptions and insufficient empirical validation; needs more realistic, thorough experiments.
