This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs), cleverly leveraging existing spectral HMM learning techniques and adding a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation. The core idea – framing an MHMM as a block-diagonal HMM and using spectral methods – is intuitive and promising, particularly for large datasets where EM becomes computationally expensive. However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, lacks sufficient engagement with related work, and presents experiments that are difficult to interpret.

**Quality:** The technical core of the algorithm appears sound. The lemma establishing the MHMM as a special case of an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix is a crucial step, and the proposed stabilization method based on spectral properties is a valuable contribution. The analysis considering estimation noise (particularly Equation (7)) attempts to provide theoretical grounding, although its justification feels somewhat hand-wavy. While the algorithm builds on existing spectral HMM methods, the permutation handling is a genuine advance. However, the “conjecture” regarding the noise bound feels weak without more rigorous proof, and the dependence on specific eigenvalue properties is concerning – a more robust analysis would be desirable. The paper feels incomplete in its theoretical exploration; further analysis of the algorithm's convergence properties and sensitivity to noise would strengthen the contribution.

**Clarity:** This is a significant weakness. Notation is often confusing. For example, Corollary 1 refers to “1J^T” without prior definition. The meaning of ‘J’ is unclear. The description of the block-diagonal structure in lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e (Equation 6) requires clearer explanation. Crucially, *every* matrix and vector should be defined with its dimensions upon first introduction. This would dramatically improve readability. The transition between Lemma 2 and the discussion of permutation resolution feels abrupt. The progression of ideas, while conceptually sound, is poorly articulated in the text. Figures 1 and 2 are helpful conceptually, but could benefit from more detailed explanations in the captions.

**Originality:** The combination of existing spectral HMM methods with the novel stabilization step constitutes a reasonable degree of originality. The approach to circumventing the parameter permutation problem via spectral properties is a key differentiating factor. However, the paper *completely* neglects prior work on initializing EM with spectral estimates, most notably Chaganty and Liang (2013), which is a significant omission and weakens the claim of novelty.  The paper should explicitly position its contribution relative to this and other related approaches.

**Significance:**  The potential significance is moderate. Addressing the computational limitations of EM for MHMM learning is a worthwhile goal. If the algorithm reliably scales to large datasets and provides competitive accuracy, it could be a valuable tool. However, the current experimental results are insufficient to convincingly demonstrate this. The idea of leveraging spectral properties for permutation resolution could have broader applications beyond MHMMs.  The success of the approach hinges on the practical performance and robustness demonstrated through more comprehensive experiments.

**Specific Comments:**

* **Related Work:** The literature review is insufficient. Citation of Chaganty and Liang (2013) is *essential*.  A more thorough survey of related MHMM learning techniques is needed.
* **Notation:** As mentioned above, improve notation consistency and define all variables (including dimensions). Consider adopting established HMM notation (e.g., T for transition matrix, n/m for observation/hidden state counts) as suggested by Hsu et al. (2009), to improve readability for experts in the field.
* **Experiments:** The experiments are basic and poorly presented. Figure 4 is extremely difficult to read. Results should be presented in clear tables with standard deviations. The authors should report best (not just average) EM accuracy to provide a fair comparison.  The unusually poor performance of EM requires investigation and explanation.  More thorough comparisons with alternative MHMM learning algorithms are needed. The real data experiment utilizes a feature transformation without justification for why it is necessary or how it impacts the results.
* **Equation (7):** The definition of Lλ̃K′ is convoluted and difficult to understand. The intuition behind this spectral longevity metric needs to be clarified.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel approach to resolving parameter permutation in MHMM learning.
* Potential for computational efficiency compared to EM, especially for large datasets.
* Sound theoretical basis leveraging existing spectral HMM methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of clarity in notation and presentation.
* Insufficient engagement with related work (critical omission of Chaganty & Liang 2013).
* Weak experimental validation with poorly presented results.
* Unconvincing theoretical justification for the noise bound (conjecture).




**Overall Assessment:** Reject with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit. The core idea is promising, but the paper requires substantial improvements in clarity, related work discussion, and experimental validation to be considered a significant contribution. Addressing the specific comments outlined above would greatly strengthen the manuscript.
