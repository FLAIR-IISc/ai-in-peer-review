- Proposes a spectral method using method of moments to learn mixtures of HMMs, tackling permutation ambiguity.  
- Leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping.  
- Key observation: the global transition matrix has K eigenvalues equal to 1 (K = number of HMMs), which is exploited.  
- Presents a de-permutation algorithm to recover the correct ordering of mixture components.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the approach.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate effectiveness; method is computationally faster than EM with comparable accuracy.  
- Major strength: effectively resolves de-permutation/alignment of spectral outputs to true components.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient context on related work, notably Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013).  
- Weakness: occasional typos and proofreading/notation issues.  
- Originality: builds on Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang but contributes a significant novel solution to permutation ambiguity.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel permutation solution, solid experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: partly incremental over prior work, minor typos, needs more background/context.
