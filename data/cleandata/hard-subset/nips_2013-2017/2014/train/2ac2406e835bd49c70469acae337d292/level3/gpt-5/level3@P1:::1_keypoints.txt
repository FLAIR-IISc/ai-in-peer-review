- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral approach to learn Mixtures of HMMs by treating them as a single block-diagonal HMM and resolving permutation ambiguity to recover components.
- Key technical insight: in the noiseless case the global transition has K eigenvalues at 1 and powers reveal K rank-one blocks enabling de-permutation.
- For noisy data the authors use low-rank reconstruction and a “spectral longevity” criterion to estimate K and cluster columns for de-permutation.
- Experiments (synthetic and UCI handwriting) show speed advantages over EM and suggest spectral estimates can initialize EM.
- Builds on prior spectral HMM and latent-variable work and classical EM MHMM learning; novelty lies in undoing permutation and K-selection via spectral longevity.
- Theory: block-diagonal equivalence and noiseless de-permutation are clear, but the main finite-sample result is left as a conjecture.
- The separability/spectral-gap assumptions (|λG_K| > max_k |λL_2,k| and unique eigenvalue-1 per A_k) are strong and not empirically stress-tested.
- The spectral longevity criterion and Lemma 3 are only partially justified and rely on possibly small spectral gaps in practice.
- Algorithms: high-level pipeline is specified but crucial implementation details (exponentiation range e, K-means init/restarts, sensitivity to eigen-gaps) are missing.
- Experiments: limited validation — a synthetic test corrupts permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise instead of end-to-end sequence estimation, which is unrepresentative of real noise.
- Section 4.2 uses generated sequences but omits key MHMM parameter details (transitions, emissions, mixing) hindering reproducibility and interpretation.
- Real-data experiment required artificial feature expansion to satisfy MK < L, with no discussion of implications for low-dimensional settings.
- EM sometimes worsens when initialized by the spectral solution; this surprising behavior is unexplained (possible de-permutation mismatch, objective mismatch, overfitting).
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but some figures have unreadable text and some notation (e.g., MK < L) should be stated up front; minor typographical errors noted.
- Originality: block-diagonal MHMM view is known, but leveraging eigen-structure for de-permutation and the spectral longevity heuristic is a novel, incremental contribution.
- Significance: could be a scalable alternative or initializer for EM if robust, but practicality limited by O((MK)^3) eigendecomposition, MK < L requirement, and lack of guarantees under realistic noise.
- Strength: clear statement of permutation problem and an intuitive spectral remedy with potential computational savings and theoretical insight.
- Weakness: core claims rest on unproven conjectures, strong assumptions, insufficient end-to-end experimental validation, and scalability concerns.
- Requests for authors: provide full synthetic MHMM details and sensitivity analyses (eigen-gaps, mixing, persistence), replace Dirichlet corruption tests with end-to-end sequence experiments, and report K-estimation and de-permutation error rates.
- Additional requested evaluations: runtime scaling vs N,T,K,M,L and comparisons to EM, investigate why EM degrades after spectral init and consider constrained EM variants.
- Suggest discussing applicability/failure regimes (near-degenerate eigenvalues, slow mixing, unequal component sizes) and exploring alternative de-permutation strategies and robustness.
- Pros for acceptance: interesting, practical idea addressing a real obstacle for spectral MHMM learning; clear exposition and potential impact as a fast initializer.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient theoretical guarantees, limited and partly non-representative experiments, scalability and applicability not convincingly demonstrated, reproducibility details missing.
- Overall recommendation: promising work but currently a weak reject unless experiments are substantially strengthened and scope/limitations clarified.
