- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) that treats an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.
- Core contribution is a de-permutation algorithm that recovers individual HMM components from permuted global spectral estimates using spectral properties of the transition matrix.
- Builds on prior spectral HMM frameworks (Hsu et al. 2012; Anandkumar et al. 2012) and leverages existing spectral HMM learning algorithms.
- Lacks sufficient contextualization and differentiation from related mixture-model spectral work, notably Chaganty & Liang (2013).
- Technical analysis is strong: provides lemmas establishing conditions for de-permutation success and states a conjecture (Conjecture 1) that remains unproven.
- Algorithmic contribution is sound and appears practically useful.
- Experiments show clear computational advantages over EM, especially on large datasets.
- Real-data experiment (handwritten character trajectories) demonstrates practical utility and that spectral learning can initialize EM effectively.
- Experimental comparisons are somewhat limited — EM was only randomly initialized and additional baselines or spectral mixture comparisons are missing.
- Clarity is generally good with well-defined notation, but the paper has proofreading errors and could use more intuitive explanations of spectral decomposition steps.
- Originality is moderate: adaptation to MHMMs is novel, but core spectral techniques are direct adaptations of existing methods.
- Significance is clear for sequence clustering: offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM with comparable accuracy.
- Pro-acceptance points: meaningful problem solved, theoretically grounded permutation solution, computational benefits, practical experiments, broader applicability of algorithm.
- Con-acceptance points: insufficient differentiation from prior work, proofreading errors, incomplete related-work contextualization, incomplete theoretical proof for the de-permutation, limited experimental baselines.
- Recommended improvements: strengthen comparison to mixture-model literature (esp. Chaganty & Liang), fix proofreading issues, provide more rigorous justification or proof for the de-permutation conjecture, add experimental comparisons to other spectral mixture approaches.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with practical significance; accept with minor revisions.
