

This paper proposes a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by recognizing that an MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors leverage existing spectral methods for HMMs but address the critical permutation ambiguity in the estimated parameters through a de-permutation algorithm based on spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The key contribution lies in resolving the permutation issue to recover individual HMM components.

The paper's main strength is the practical de-permutation algorithm that effectively addresses a significant challenge in spectral learning for mixture models. The experimental results demonstrate the computational advantage over EM (particularly for large datasets) and show that the method serves as a good initialization for EM in real data experiments. The approach is well-motivated and the technical development is largely sound.

However, the paper suffers from several limitations. The core algorithm is a relatively straightforward extension of Hsu, Kakade & Zhang's spectral learning for HMMs (2012), with the main novelty being the de-permutation step. The paper fails to adequately situate itself in related work, notably omitting Chaganty & Liang's ICML 2013 work on spectral learning for mixture models, which addresses similar permutation issues. The theoretical analysis contains gaps, including Conjecture 1 which lacks proper justification and relies on results from a different model. The paper also contains several proofreading errors (e.g., "For each e, identify of eigenvalues...").

The work has moderate significance as it provides a computationally efficient alternative to EM for MHMMs, but the novelty is limited. The clarity is generally good, though the proofreading issues detract from professionalism.

Pro: Practical de-permutation algorithm, computational advantages over EM, good real-world validation.
Con: Minimal novelty beyond existing spectral learning methods, insufficient related work, theoretical gaps, proofreading issues.

The paper makes a useful contribution to spectral learning for mixture models but doesn't advance the state of the art sufficiently to warrant acceptance at NIPS. It would benefit from stronger theoretical justification, better contextualization in the literature, and careful proofreading.
