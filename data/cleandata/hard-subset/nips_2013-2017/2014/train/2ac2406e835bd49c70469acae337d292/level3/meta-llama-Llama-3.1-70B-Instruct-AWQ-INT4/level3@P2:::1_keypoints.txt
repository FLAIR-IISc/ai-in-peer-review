- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm that estimates a full HMM then resolves permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal MHMMs.  
- Builds on prior spectral learning work (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) and situates itself within that literature.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation and clear, well-written algorithm/assumptions.  
- Strength: experiments show the method can be much faster than EM while achieving comparable accuracy.  
- Major concern: estimating a full HMM first may be impractical for large, sparse mixtures—needs clarification on applicability.  
- Major concern: no guarantee the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse; noisy full matrices may masquerade as block structure.  
- Experimental concern: synthetic experiments corrupt permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise instead of sampling observed sequences from MHMMs—does not reflect real noise/limited-data effects.  
- Request: provide experiments that learn from observed sequences to demonstrate performance under realistic data conditions.  
- Request: Section 4.2 lacks specifics about MHMM parameters (transition/observation matrices); full parameter details should be provided (e.g., appendix).  
- Unexplained phenomenon: EM initialized with spectral sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—authors should explain this.  
- Minor presentation issues: typo in proof (Λ subscript should be K not 2) and figures have text/numbers too small to read.  
- Overall: interesting and theoretically solid approach but significant doubts remain about practical performance on real datasets; revisions required.  
- Recommendation/rating: borderline accept (6/10) pending addressing empirical and clarity concerns.
