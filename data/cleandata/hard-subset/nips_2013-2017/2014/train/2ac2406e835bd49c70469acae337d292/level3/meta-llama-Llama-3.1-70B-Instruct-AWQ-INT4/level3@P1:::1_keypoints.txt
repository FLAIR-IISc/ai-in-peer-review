- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs).  
- Clear introduction and critique of existing approaches (e.g., EM).  
- Method: learn an HMM via MoM then resolve permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal MHMM structure.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough theoretical foundations and algorithmic details.  
- Experiments are clear but omit specifics (notably mixture HMM parameters in Section 4.2).  
- Practicality concern for large, sparse mixtures; need clarification of when the method is appropriate.  
- No guarantee the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse; noisy full matrices may mimic block structure.  
- Experimental setup corrupts permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise instead of sampling observed sequences, which may not reflect real noise/limited-data effects.  
- Request experiments that learn from observed sequences to demonstrate robustness.  
- Request full parameter details (e.g., in an appendix) for reproducibility.  
- Ask authors to explain why EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes degrades results.  
- Originality and significance rated high for addressing a challenging problem and showing real-data effectiveness.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
