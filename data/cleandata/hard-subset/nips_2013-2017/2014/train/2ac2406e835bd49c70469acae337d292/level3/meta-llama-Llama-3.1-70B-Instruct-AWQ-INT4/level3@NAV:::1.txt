This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using spectral learning. The method leverages the fact that an MHMM can be represented as a single Hidden Markov Model (HMM) with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors use the Method of Moments (MoM) to estimate the parameters of the HMM and then apply a de-permutation algorithm to recover the individual HMMs.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. The theoretical analysis is sound, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, there are several areas that require improvement.

Firstly, the experimental results need more detail and expansion. For instance, the authors could provide more information about the datasets used, the parameter settings, and the evaluation metrics. Additionally, the authors could compare their method with other state-of-the-art approaches to demonstrate its superiority.

Secondly, the authors should clarify when their method is appropriate versus when it is not. Estimating a full HMM first may be impractical for large, sparse mixtures, and the authors should discuss the limitations of their approach.

Thirdly, the authors assume that the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse, but there is no guarantee that this will be the case. Many full matrices may be close to the desired sparse block structure under noise, and the authors should discuss how to handle such cases.

Fourthly, the experiments corrupt permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling observed sequences from a mixture of HMMs. The authors should conduct experiments that learn from observations to reflect real noise and limited-data effects.

Lastly, the authors should provide full parameter details, such as the transition and observation matrices, in an appendix or supplementary material.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to learning MHMMs using spectral learning. However, the idea of using MoM for learning HMMs is not new, and the authors should clearly discuss how their approach differs from previous contributions.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to provide a computationally efficient and accurate method for learning MHMMs. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used as a good initialization scheme for EM, which is a widely used algorithm for learning HMMs.

In conclusion, while the paper has some strengths, there are several areas that require improvement. The authors should address the limitations of their approach, provide more detail about the experimental results, and discuss the significance of their method in the context of previous contributions.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to learning MHMMs using spectral learning.
* The method is computationally efficient and accurate.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on synthetic and real data.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experimental results need more detail and expansion.
* The authors should clarify when their method is appropriate versus when it is not.
* The assumption that the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse may not always hold.
* The experiments do not reflect real noise and limited-data effects.
* The authors should provide full parameter details in an appendix or supplementary material.
