- Presents a novel spectral Method of Moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by framing MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Addresses permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates by analyzing the global transition matrix’s spectral properties to estimate a de-permutation mapping.  
- Builds on prior MoM and spectral learning literature (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) and extends these techniques to more complex MHMMs.  
- Motivated by computational limitations of EM for MHMMs, proposing a potentially more efficient alternative for large datasets.  
- Provides theoretical lemmas and conjectures to formalize the de-permutation algorithm and conditions for its success.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic data and real handwritten character trajectory data, with comparisons to EM and MoM-initialized EM.  
- Novel application of spectral methods to MHMM learning is a key contribution.  
- Demonstrated computational efficiency benefits over EM in experiments.  
- Theoretical grounding is attempted, though several results are presented as conjectures rather than proofs.  
- Experimental results are promising, but would benefit from reporting variability (std. devs/confidence intervals) and justifying feature choices (e.g., cubic polynomial transform).  
- Related work coverage is incomplete; should compare to Chaganty & Liang on spectral experts for regression mixtures.  
- Paper suffers from clarity and proofreading issues that detract from presentation quality.  
- Reliance on conjectures weakens the theoretical claims and requires clearer labeling and discussion of limitations.  
- Spectral conditions required by the method appear complex and may limit practical applicability; prevalence in real data is unclear.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially valuable contribution but needs clearer presentation, stronger theoretical rigor, and expanded related work.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, conditional on addressing clarity, theoretical gaps, and related-work concerns.
