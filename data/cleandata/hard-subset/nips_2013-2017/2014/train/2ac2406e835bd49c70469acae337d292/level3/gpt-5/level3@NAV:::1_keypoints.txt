- Proposes a method-of-moments approach to learn Mixtures of HMMs by estimating a single MK-state HMM with block-diagonal transition and then de-permuting states to recover per-component parameters.  
- Builds on spectral/moment methods for HMMs and latent-variable models; novelty is exploiting eigen-structure to de-permute states and a “spectral longevity” heuristic to infer K.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and insightful block-diagonal reparameterization.  
- Strength: conceptually elegant use of spectral properties to address permutation ambiguity with plausible small-noise intuition.  
- Strength: potential computational advantages over EM since moments can be computed once and reused.  
- Strength: practical idea of using spectral estimates to initialize EM.  
- Weakness: theoretical support incomplete—central Conjecture 1 is unproven and success conditions are not well characterized.  
- Weakness: key assumptions (e.g., eigenvalue distinguishability) are not tied to observable diagnostics.  
- Weakness: practical scalability concerns—estimating MK-state HMM and eigendecompositions may be infeasible for large, sparse mixtures; complexity and memory costs need quantification.  
- Weakness: no guarantee spectral HMM estimation yields a sparse or block-structured transition; dense matrices near block-diagonal make clustering ambiguous.  
- Experiments weak: synthetic tests perturb matrices rather than sampling sequences, bypassing observation noise and finite-sample effects.  
- Experiments weak: Section 4.2 omits full parameter details and generator code, hindering reproducibility.  
- Experiments weak: on real data, EM initialized with spectral sometimes underperforms spectral alone—paper should report training likelihoods, convergence criteria, and sensitivity analyses to K and M.  
- Presentation: generally clear but Section 4.2 needs precise settings (Ak, Ok, νk, π, sequence lengths, seeds); figures are hard to read.  
- Minor error: typo in proof—Λ subscript should be K, not 2.  
- Originality/significance: de-permutation via eigen-structure and longevity heuristic is novel and potentially useful but limited by weak empirical validation and unclear scalability.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant resolution of permutation ambiguity, clear exposition, and promising EM initialization.  
- Cons against acceptance: conjectural central guarantee, unrealistic experimental design, unclear scalability to large/sparse MHMMs, missing parameter details and poor figure readability.  
- Recommendation: interesting and promising but requires substantial revisions and stronger sequence-based experiments to establish practical viability.
