- Proposes a spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Main novelty: a method to resolve permutation ambiguity in spectral MHMM learning using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Provides theoretical foundations: equivalence result (Lemma 1) and spectral analysis (Lemma 3, Definition 3) to recover clusters and determine number of components.  
- Experiments show the spectral method is computationally more efficient than EM and achieves comparable accuracy on synthetic and real data.  
- Experimental comparison to EM is undermined by missing implementation details (restarts, stopping criteria).  
- Notation and some definitions are inconsistent/confusing (e.g., Corollary 1, 1J^T) and dimensions are often unspecified.  
- Presentation issues: unclear figures (Figure 4 axes/labels) and poor real-data result presentation; a table would help.  
- Originality: viewing MHMM as HMM is known, but the specific permutation-resolution technique is a valuable extension.  
- Lacks sufficient engagement with closely related work, notably Chaganty & Liang (2013), and no direct comparison.  
- Significance: runtime advantage independent of number of sequences makes it useful for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Practical utility: can serve as a good initialization for EM, improving practicality.  
- Limitations: experiments are basic and lack comparisons to other state-of-the-art sequence-clustering methods.  
- Missing analysis of failure modes (e.g., when noise assumptions in Lemma 3 are violated).  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but requires major revisions for clarity, experimental depth, and related-work positioning.
