This paper presents a novel approach to learning mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) using the method of moments (MoM). The authors formulate an MHMM as a special case of an HMM and leverage an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters up to a permutation ambiguity. To resolve this ambiguity, they introduce a stabilization-based method that exploits the spectral properties of the global transition matrix. The paper provides an analysis of the algorithm's behavior in the presence of estimation noise and presents experimental results on synthetic and real data.

The paper's main contributions are the formulation of the MHMM as an HMM and the introduction of the stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation. The authors also provide an analysis of the algorithm's behavior in the presence of estimation noise, which is a significant improvement over previous work.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. However, there are some areas where the notation could be improved. For example, the notation for the block-diagonal structure of the transition matrix could be clearer. Additionally, the authors could provide more explicit definitions for some of the vectors and matrices, such as 1J^T.

The experimental results are basic and could be improved. The presentation of the results is poor, and the figures are difficult to read. The authors should consider reporting their results in tables and providing more thorough comparisons with other methods.

One of the main concerns with the paper is the lack of engagement with related work. The authors should provide more citations to prior work on initializing EM with spectral estimates, such as Chaganty and Liang (2013). Additionally, the authors should consider conforming to established HMM notation, such as using T for transitions and n/m for observation/hidden state counts.

Overall, the paper presents a novel approach to learning mixtures of HMMs and provides a significant improvement over previous work. However, there are some areas where the notation and experimental results could be improved.
