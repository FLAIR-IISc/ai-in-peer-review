- Paper proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for MHMMs that resolves permutation ambiguity via spectral properties of a global transition matrix to recover block-diagonal structure and individual HMM parameters, highlighting computational efficiency and EM-initialization potential.

Strengths:
- Theoretical contribution: frames MHMMs as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions and introduces a novel depermutation algorithm.
- Computational efficiency: method is cheaper than EM and suitable for large datasets.
- Real-world applicability: demonstrated on real data (handwritten character trajectories).
- Clarity: generally well-written with a clear explanation of the spectral algorithm and depermutation steps.

Weaknesses:
- Algorithm feasibility: relies on accurately estimating full HMM parameters before block recovery, which may be impractical in noisy real-world data; conditions for success unclear.
- Sparse transition matrix: no guarantees that the learned transition matrix will be sparse, raising robustness concerns.
- Noise in experiments: evaluations focus on depermuting matrices rather than learning from noisy observed data, limiting realism.
- Experimental details: Section 4.2 lacks specifics on transition and observation matrix parameters, hindering reproducibility.
- EM initialization anomaly: EM initialized with spectral learning performs worse than spectral alone (Table 1) and lacks explanation.
- Minor errors: a subscript error in Section 3.2.1 and some figures have unreadably small text/numbers.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, computationally efficient approach to a challenging MHMM learning problem.
- Theoretical insights and depermutation algorithm are significant and could inspire further work.
- Shown to have practical potential as an EM initialization scheme on real data.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Questionable feasibility in noisy, real-world scenarios and insufficient empirical treatment of noise.
- Lack of sparsity guarantees limits applicability in certain domains.
- Insufficient experimental detail and unclear EM-initialization results reduce confidence.

Recommendation:
- Accept with major revisions focusing on noise robustness, providing sparsity guarantees, and clarifying experimental details.
