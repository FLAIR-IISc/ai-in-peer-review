This paper proposes a spectral method-of-moments (MoM) approach to learn mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, estimating global parameters via the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang (HKZ) spectral algorithm, and then resolving the inherent permutation ambiguity. The core contribution is a de-permutation/aligning procedure that uses the eigenstructure of the estimated global transition matrix and a “spectral longevity” criterion to recover both the number of components K and a permutation mapping that re-assembles block structure. The paper presents synthetic and a small real-data experiment, and argues the approach is computationally attractive compared to EM. The work relates to spectral learning of HMMs (Hsu, Kakade & Zhang, JCSS 2012) and tensor/MoM methods (e.g., Anandkumar et al.), but the connection to spectral methods for mixtures more broadly (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, ICML 2013) should be discussed in more depth.

Quality: The reduction to a block-diagonal HMM is standard; the main novelty is the de-permutation scheme. While intuitively reasonable, the key statements are framed as conjectures rather than theorems. Perturbation guarantees (e.g., via Weyl/Davis–Kahan) could formalize when the top-K “global” eigenvalues separate from “local” ones and when k-means on Ar recovers blocks. Assumptions (unique unit eigenvalue per Ak, mixing, distinct stationary distributions) should be explicit, and sensitivity to small spectral gaps and estimation noise analyzed. Experiments show promise but are limited; comparisons to simple baselines for alignment (e.g., Hungarian matching on emission similarities or stationary distributions) are missing.

Clarity: The paper is generally understandable, but there are typos and proofreading issues (e.g., “For each e, identify of eigenvalues...”), inconsistent notation, and some repeated/unfinished references. A clearer presentation of the longevity statistic and algorithmic complexity would help.

Originality: The spectral MHMM setup closely follows HKZ with an incremental de-permutation heuristic. Novelty is modest but focused on a practical pain point.

Significance: If made robust, the alignment solution could be useful for practitioners seeking scalable MHMM learning. Current theoretical support and empirical breadth limit impact.

Pros for acceptance:
- Addresses a real gap: aligning spectral outputs to true MHMM components.
- Simple, computationally attractive procedure with encouraging empirical results.
- Clear formulation of the permutation issue in spectral MHMM learning.

Cons for acceptance:
- Core claims lack formal guarantees; key results stated as conjectures.
- Limited evaluation and missing baselines; sensitivity to spectral gaps not studied.
- Related work insufficiently situated (e.g., Chaganty & Liang; broader spectral mixture literature).
- Typos and clarity issues reduce polish.

Suggestions: Provide perturbation-based guarantees for K recovery and permutation accuracy; add alignment baselines; expand experiments; strengthen related work and proofreading.
