- Proposes reformulating an MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition and using a stabilization-based de-permutation plus a “spectral longevity” statistic to recover per-HMM parameters and K.  
- Stabilization idea (using asymptotic powers of the global transition) is intuitive and addresses permutation issues in MHMM spectral learning.  
- High-level construction is sound but the theory is incomplete: Conjecture 1 is central and unproven.  
- Reuse of error/bounds from mixture models is weak because they are not carefully adapted to the HMM setting.  
- Eq. (7) for estimating K lacks formal guarantees or a systematic sensitivity study (noise level, eigen-gap, periodicity).  
- The assumption that each Ak has a single eigenvalue equal to 1 needs discussion relative to ergodicity/aperiodicity; behavior for nearly periodic or reducible chains is unclear.  
- Notation is confusing and some quantities are undefined (e.g., 1J^T, dimensions of v̄k); block-diagonal limits should state explicit block sizes.  
- Define every matrix/vector with dimensions on first use and align notation with established HMM conventions.  
- Algorithmic details for reproducibility are missing: exponent range for e, eigenvalue thresholding, clustering procedure, and how γ in Eq. (7) is chosen.  
- Core estimation leverages existing spectral HMM methods; the novel contributions are stabilization-based de-permutation and K-selection via spectral longevity.  
- The paper should better distinguish itself from prior work and cite related spectral+EM approaches (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, 2013).  
- Potentially significant if theoretical gaps are closed and empirical validation is strengthened — could make MHMM learning scalable and robust.  
- Experiments are basic and poorly presented (Figure 4 hard to read); report results as tables with mean/std and include best-of-EM comparisons.  
- EM baseline appears unusually weak — investigate hyperparameters, restarts, and report accuracy vs. runtime fairly.  
- Add comparisons to EM initialized with spectral estimates and to other spectral baselines.  
- Pros: simple, intuitive stabilization and potential impact for scalable MHMM learning.  
- Cons: incomplete theory (unproven conjecture, weak noise analysis), clarity/notation problems, and limited/poorly presented experiments with weak baselines.
