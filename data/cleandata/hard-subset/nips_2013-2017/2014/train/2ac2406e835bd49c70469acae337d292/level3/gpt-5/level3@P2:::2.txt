Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a spectral method for learning Mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core observation is that an MHMM with K components and M hidden states per component is equivalent to a single HMM with MK states and a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors then apply off-the-shelf spectral/MoM estimators for HMMs (e.g., Hsu, Kakade, Zhang, 2009; Anandkumar et al., 2012) to recover the “global” parameters up to a permutation of the MK states. The main contribution is a stabilization-based depermutation procedure: exploiting spectral properties of the estimated global transition matrix to (i) recover the block structure, thereby undoing the permutation and (ii) estimate K by a novel “spectral longevity” heuristic based on the decay behavior of the sorted eigenvalues under exponentiation. Theoretical support includes structural lemmas in the noiseless case and a conjectured finite-sample stability claim under noise when there is sufficient spectral separation. Experiments on synthetic data illustrate the depermutation’s robustness to sample size/sequence length, and a small real-data study on UCI handwritten trajectories suggests the spectral method is a strong initialization for EM.

This work sits at the intersection of spectral learning for latent variable models (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009; Anandkumar et al., 2012) and MHMMs for sequence clustering (e.g., Smyth, 1997). The paper is related to “spectral experts” for mixture models (Chaganty and Liang, 2013) and to work initializing EM with spectral estimates. The novelty here is adapting spectral HMM estimators to MHMMs through a depermutation step based on the transition spectrum and providing a practical K-selection heuristic.

Strengths
- Simple, intuitive, and potentially impactful observation: an MHMM can be learned by viewing it as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.
- The depermutation idea—rank-K reconstruction followed by column clustering of the stabilized transition matrix—is clever and addresses a well-known practical impediment of spectral methods (permutation ambiguity).
- The proposed longevity metric for estimating K is practical and aligns with the block-diagonal spectral picture.
- Computational appeal: once low-order moments are computed, estimation time is independent of dataset size; experiments show clear speedups vs EM.
- The method seems useful as an initialization scheme for EM, improving robustness and runtime.

Weaknesses
Quality
- The key robustness statements under noise are conjectural. Conjecture 1 is plausible but unproven; no finite-sample perturbation guarantees (e.g., via Davis–Kahan/Weyl) are provided for the depermutation accuracy or K selection.
- The assumption that each Ak has exactly one eigenvalue at 1 (and the rest strictly inside the unit disk) is standard for an ergodic, aperiodic chain, but practical robustness to near-periodicity, defective matrices, or small spectral gaps is not analyzed.
- The K-estimation criterion (Eq. 7) is heuristic; there is no guidance on choosing γ, nor ablations showing sensitivity to eigenvalue spreads, noise, or clustering randomness.
- Recovery of π and νk from the stacked ν̄ requires extra steps/normalizations; this is not described.
- The requirement MK < L for the MoM estimator is constraining; the “cubic feature expansion” workaround may distort the observation model. The statistical impact of this transformation is neither analyzed nor evaluated.

Clarity
- Notation needs tightening. Dimensions should be specified when matrices/vectors are introduced. Corollary 1 is hard to parse; define 1M (not 1J), and explicitly show the block structure of lime→∞ Āe.
- Adopting more standard HMM notation (e.g., T for transition, n for observations, m for hidden states) would ease readability. Be consistent with Hsu et al. (2009) where possible.
- Algorithm 1 should more explicitly describe how cluster labels induce a permutation P̃ and how blocks are extracted; clarify distance metric, initialization, and stability of the k-means step.

Originality
- Casting an MHMM as an HMM is known, but using this to enable off-the-shelf spectral HMM learning and then undoing the permutation via spectral stabilization is a nice, focused contribution.
- The K-selection “longevity” criterion is novel to my knowledge, though heuristic.

Significance
- If robustified and better validated, the approach could materially reduce the cost of fitting MHMMs on large sequence collections and provide strong initializations for EM/variational methods.
- Current empirical validation is limited, making it hard to assess impact beyond proof-of-concept.

Empirical evaluation
- Synthetic results are useful but basic; please include quantitative measures (e.g., cluster ARI/NMI, parameter error, block recovery accuracy, K-estimation accuracy) with confidence intervals over multiple seeds.
- EM baselines: report both best-of-restarts and average; ensure fair stopping criteria; include number of restarts typical in practice. In Fig. 4, EM appears unusually weak; provide details (initialization, regularization, convergence thresholds).
- Improve presentation: replace hard-to-read figures with tables for key metrics; expand axes labels and legends; include runtime breakdown (moment computation vs eigendecomposition vs EM iterations).
- Additional baselines: EM initialized with the proposed spectral estimate; spectral methods for mixtures/multi-view; variational MHMMs. Show regimes where spectral-only beats EM and vice versa.
- Robustness ablations: vary eigenvalue gaps, noise, number of states K and M, sequence lengths, and observation dimension L; stress-test depermutation under small spectral gaps.
- Discuss and evaluate the MK < L constraint and the effect of polynomial feature lifts on modeling assumptions.

Specific questions/suggestions
- Clarify the exact conditions on Ak (ergodicity, aperiodicity, diagonalizability). What are failure modes if a component is nearly periodic or reducible?
- How is γ chosen in Eq. (7)? How sensitive is K̂ to γ and to eigenvalue estimation noise?
- How do you recover π and νk separately from ν̄ after depermutation?
- Which distance and initialization does the column-clustering use? How stable is P̃ across runs?
- Provide complexity for moment computation and memory usage as a function of N, T, L, M, K.
- Cite prior work on spectral initialization for EM (e.g., Chaganty and Liang, 2013) and other MHMM clustering approaches (and compare empirically).

Pros for acceptance
- Elegant and computationally attractive depermutation idea with clear intuition.
- Practical K-estimation heuristic consistent with the spectral picture.
- Potentially impactful for large-scale sequence clustering; useful as an EM initializer.

Cons for acceptance
- Key robustness claims are conjectural; lack of finite-sample guarantees for the new steps.
- Limited experimental scope and presentation; EM comparisons not entirely convincing.
- Clarity/notation issues and sparse engagement with related work.

Overall recommendation
Borderline reject. The central idea is interesting and potentially useful, but the paper in its current form needs stronger theoretical support (or at least systematic empirical robustness studies), clearer exposition, and more thorough, well-presented experiments and baselines. I encourage a revision addressing the above points; the approach could be a solid contribution with these improvements.
