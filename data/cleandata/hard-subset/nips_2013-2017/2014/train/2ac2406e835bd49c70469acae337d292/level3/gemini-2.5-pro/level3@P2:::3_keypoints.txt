- Presents a spectral MoM algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with MK states and applying HMM spectral learners.  
- Fundamental challenge: spectral methods recover hidden states up to permutation, which mixes component states and prevents recovering individual HMM parameters.  
- Main contribution: a novel de-permutation algorithm that exploits spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover component alignment.  
- Core idea: in the noiseless case the global transition has K eigenvalues equal to 1; low-rank reconstruction using top-K eigenvectors yields column clusters that reveal the permutation.  
- Proposes a "spectral longevity" heuristic to estimate the number of mixture components K from data.  
- Synthetic experiments show the column-clustering approach successfully recovers the block structure under moderate noise.  
- Method offers significant computational advantages over EM.  
- Real-data results (handwritten characters) show the method is an excellent initializer for EM, though spectral alone does not consistently beat a well-tuned EM.  
- Theoretical weaknesses: central Conjecture 1 is unproven and justification for reusing bounds from prior work is brief and hand-wavy.  
- Lemma 3’s eigenvalue gap condition may be strong; robustness when conditions are violated is unclear.  
- Originality: de-permutation approach appears novel for MHMMs but paper fails to adequately situate or compare with related work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang ICML 2013).  
- Significance: addresses an important, practical problem and provides a fast, potentially broadly applicable technique, especially as EM initialization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, proposes an elegant novel solution, shows practical speedups, and gives a useful initialization strategy for EM.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks comparison to key prior work, relies on an insufficiently justified conjecture, and does not fully replace EM in performance.  
- Recommended revisions: add comparison/discussion of related work (Chaganty & Liang), strengthen theoretical justification for Conjecture 1 (or provide a convincing sketch), reframe emphasis on spectral initialization if appropriate, and proofread for clarity.
