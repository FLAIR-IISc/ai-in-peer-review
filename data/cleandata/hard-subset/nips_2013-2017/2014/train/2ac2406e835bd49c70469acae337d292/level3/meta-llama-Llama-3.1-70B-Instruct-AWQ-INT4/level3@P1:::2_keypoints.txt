- Proposes learning mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a special-case HMM and applying a method-of-moments (MoM) algorithm.  
- MoM recovers parameters up to a permutation ambiguity which the paper addresses.  
- Introduces a stabilization-based method that uses spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve parameter permutation.  
- Provides analysis of the algorithm’s behavior under estimation noise.  
- Presents experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrating the approach.  
- Main contributions: MHMM-as-HMM formulation and the stabilization-based permutation resolution.  
- Strength: novel approach with significant improvement over prior work on MHMM learning.  
- Strength: clear exposition and a noise-robustness analysis not present in earlier work.  
- Weakness: notation is sometimes unclear (e.g., block-diagonal transition structure).  
- Weakness: some vectors/matrices lack explicit definitions (example: 1J^T).  
- Weakness: experimental results are basic, poorly presented, and figures are hard to read.  
- Suggestion: report results in tables and provide more thorough comparisons with other methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with related work (e.g., initializing EM with spectral estimates such as Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Suggestion: conform to standard HMM notation (use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden state counts).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing notation, experimental presentation, and related-work/comparison concerns.
