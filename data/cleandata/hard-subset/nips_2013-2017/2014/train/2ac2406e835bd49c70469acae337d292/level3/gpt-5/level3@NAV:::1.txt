Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes a method-of-moments approach to learn Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by first estimating a single HMM whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, then resolving the permutation ambiguity to recover per-component parameters. The approach builds on spectral/moment methods for HMMs and latent-variable models (e.g., Hsu et al.; Anandkumar et al.) and relates to earlier work on clustering sequences with HMMs (Smyth) and MHMM applications. The novelty lies in exploiting spectral structure of the global transition matrix to de-permute states and in a heuristic “spectral longevity” criterion to infer K.

Strengths:
- Clear problem formulation; the block-diagonal reparameterization is insightful and well explained.
- Conceptually elegant use of spectral properties to address the permutation ambiguity, with reasonable intuition for why it can work under small noise.
- Potential computational advantages over EM when moments can be computed once and reused.
- Interesting practical idea of using spectral estimates to initialize EM.

Weaknesses:
Quality: Theoretical support is incomplete (central Conjecture 1 is unproven) and key assumptions (e.g., when K eigenvalues remain distinguishable) are not tied to observable diagnostics. A major practical concern is that estimating a full MK-state HMM and then de-permuting may be infeasible for large, sparse mixtures; complexity and memory costs (moment size, eigendecomposition) should be quantified, with guidance on regimes where the method is appropriate. There is no guarantee that spectral HMM estimation yields a (nearly) sparse or block-structured transition; many dense matrices can be close to a permuted block-diagonal under noise, making clustering columns ambiguous.

Experiments: Current experiments do not convincingly reflect real use. The synthetic setup perturbs permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling sequences from true MHMMs; this bypasses observation noise and finite-sample effects. Section 4.2 omits full parameter details (transition matrices, exact emissions), hindering reproducibility; please provide all parameters (or generator code) in an appendix. On real data, EM initialized with spectral sometimes underperforms spectral alone; explain why EM does not consistently improve likelihood/accuracy, and report training likelihoods, convergence criteria, and sensitivity to K, M. Figures are hard to read (fonts too small).

Clarity: Generally well written, but Section 4.2 needs specificity; add precise settings for Ak, Ok, νk, π, sequence lengths, and random seeds. Minor: typo in proof—subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.

Originality and significance: The de-permutation via eigen-structure and longevity heuristic is novel and potentially useful, but significance is limited by weak empirical validation and unclear scalability to large, sparse MHMMs.

Pros for acceptance:
- Elegant idea for resolving permutation ambiguity
- Clear exposition and appealing intuition
- Potentially valuable EM initialization

Cons against acceptance:
- Central guarantee conjectural; conditions for success not well characterized
- Experimental design does not learn from observations; limited realism
- Scalability and applicability to large/sparse mixtures unclear
- Missing parameter details; figure readability issues

Recommendation: Interesting and promising, but substantial revisions and stronger experiments on sequence data are needed to establish practical viability.
