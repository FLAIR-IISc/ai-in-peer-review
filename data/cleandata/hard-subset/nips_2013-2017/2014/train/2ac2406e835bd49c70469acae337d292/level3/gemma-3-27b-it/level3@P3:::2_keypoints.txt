- Presents a spectral algorithm for learning MHMMs by framing MHMMs as block-diagonal HMMs and adding a stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation.  
- Core idea is intuitive and promising for large datasets where EM is expensive.  
- Lemma showing MHMM as HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix is crucial and technically sound.  
- Stabilization method based on spectral properties is a valuable, novel contribution to handling permutations.  
- Theoretical analysis (e.g., Eq. (7)) attempts grounding but feels hand-wavy and relies on a weak conjectured noise bound.  
- Dependence on specific eigenvalue properties raises concerns; more rigorous convergence and noise-sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Clarity is poor: confusing notation, undefined symbols (e.g., “1J^T”, meaning of J), and abrupt transitions between arguments.  
- Every matrix and vector should be defined with dimensions at first use to improve readability.  
- Figures 1 and 2 are conceptually helpful but need better captions; Figure 4 is hard to read.  
- Related work coverage is insufficient, notably omitting Chaganty & Liang (2013) and prior spectral-to-EM initialization methods.  
- Originality is moderate: combines existing spectral HMM methods with a new permutation-stabilization step but fails to position against prior work.  
- Experiments are limited, poorly presented, and difficult to interpret; results need clear tables, std devs, and best-EM reporting.  
- Unusually poor EM performance needs investigation and explanation; comparisons to other MHMM algorithms are missing.  
- Real-data experiment uses an unexplained feature transformation that requires justification and analysis of its impact.  
- Definition and intuition behind the spectral longevity metric Lλ̃K′ (Eq. (7)) are convoluted and need clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel permutation resolution, potential computational efficiency, and solid basis in spectral HMM methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation, missing related work, weak experimental validation, and unconvincing theoretical noise bound.  
- Overall recommendation: Reject but encourage revision and resubmission after substantial improvements to clarity, related work, theory, and experiments.
