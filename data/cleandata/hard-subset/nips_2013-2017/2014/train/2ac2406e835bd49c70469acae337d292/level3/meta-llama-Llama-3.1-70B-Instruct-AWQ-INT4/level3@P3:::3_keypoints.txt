- Proposes a spectral Method of Moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs).
- Approach is largely a trivial variant of Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang’s work.
- Recovered global transition matrix can be arbitrarily permuted across components.
- Authors propose a de-permutation algorithm using spectral properties that appears to work well in practice.
- Paper is well-motivated and addresses an important problem (learning MHMMs).
- Clear explanation of challenges with traditional methods (e.g., EM) and how the approach overcomes them.
- Algorithm is easy to follow and clearly described.
- Strength: explicitly tackles de-permutation/alignment of spectral outputs to true components and provides experimental validation.
- Contains occasional typos and proofreading errors that hinder readability (example given from abstract).
- Fails to sufficiently situate itself in related work (e.g., omission of Chaganty and Liang).
- Technically sound overall, with theoretical and experimental support for claims.
- Paper leaves several propositions as conjectures and would benefit from more rigorous analysis of the spectral algorithm.
- Well-organized and reproducible for expert readers but assumes familiarity with MoM/spectral learning and lacks detailed explanations of those concepts.
- Originality: novel combination of existing techniques (MoM for MHMMs + de-permutation).
- Significance: addresses a relevant, computationally efficient solution likely of interest to researchers and practitioners.
- Recommendation summary: good fit for NIPS but needs better related-work placement, proofreading, and deeper theoretical analysis.
