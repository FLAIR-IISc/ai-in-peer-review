- Paper proposes learning an MHMM by treating it as a single MK-state HMM, using spectral MoM to estimate the global HMM, then resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral analysis of the block-diagonal transition matrix.
- Originality: novel and clever pipeline—estimate global HMM then “de-permute” using eigenstructure and spectral clustering.
- Potential significance: a fast, consistent, non-iterative alternative to EM for MHMMs could enable scaling to larger datasets.
- Clarity: theoretical exposition (Lemma 1, Lemma 2, Corollary 1) is well-written and logically presented.
- Scalability/practicality concern: learning a global HMM with MK states can be computationally infeasible for large K, M, or observation dimension L; paper does not characterize applicable regimes.
- Robustness concern: de-permutation assumes Â_perm is close to block-diagonal, but no theoretical guarantees or empirical evidence that MoM estimation noise preserves the necessary spectral structure (Conjecture 1 unproven).
- Experimental validation flawed: key experiment appears to bypass full MoM pipeline by adding noise to the true matrix rather than running spectral learning on sampled observations, failing to test realistic estimation noise.
- Insufficient experimental detail: MHMM parameters used in simulations (A_k, O_k, etc.) are not provided, harming reproducibility and interpretability.
- Inconsistency in reported results: "MoM+EM" sometimes underperforms "MoM" despite EM not decreasing likelihood, suggesting possible implementation issues or needing explanation.
- Minor issue: apparent typo/subscript error in Lemma 2 proof.
- Minor issue: figures (3 and 4) have text/numbers too small to read.
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting but practical viability and robustness not convincingly demonstrated; recommend rejection unless scalability and realistic experimental validation are addressed.
