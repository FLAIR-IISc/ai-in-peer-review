- Proposes a novel spectral Method-of-Moments approach to learn Mixture of HMMs by exploiting block-diagonal structure to resolve permutation ambiguity.
- Theoretical framework is sound with clear mathematical derivations, though some results rely on conjectures (e.g., Conjecture 1).
- Clever use of spectral properties to address permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM-based learning.
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete and lacks depth to validate efficiency, accuracy, and scalability claims.
- Paper is well-written and organized, but Section 3.2.2 (noise and spectral conditions) is dense and needs more intuition.
- Integration of spectral learning, MoM, and MHMMs appears original and addresses computational cost of EM.
- Potentially significant if robust and efficient in practice, with applications across speech, bioinformatics, and time series.
- Reliance on specific spectral conditions and parameter tuning raises concerns about generalizability.
- Strength: novel approach combining spectral learning and MoM for MHMMs.
- Strength: clear theoretical foundation and potential computational efficiency versus EM.
- Strength: directly addresses permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning.
- Major weakness: insufficient experimental detail (parameter settings, preprocessing, reproducibility).
- Major weakness: scalability concerns—estimating a full HMM before resolving permutations may be impractical for large, sparse mixtures.
- Major weakness: spectral robustness unclear—noise may produce dense transition matrices that violate block-diagonal assumptions.
- Major weakness: synthetic experiments use Gaussian emissions and Dirichlet noise, not realistic sequence sampling from MHMMs.
- Major weakness: unexplained cases where EM initialized with spectral learning performs worse than spectral alone.
- Minor issues: typographical error in a proof subscript and unreadable small text/numbers in figures.
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, and potential scalability improvements.
- Cons for acceptance: weak experimental validation, scalability and robustness concerns, and insufficient methodological detail.
- Overall: interesting and theoretically promising but practical shortcomings and inadequate experiments warrant rejection now with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing limitations.
