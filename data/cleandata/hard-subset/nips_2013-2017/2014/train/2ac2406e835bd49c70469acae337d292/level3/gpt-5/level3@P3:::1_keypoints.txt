- Proposes a method-of-moments/spectral approach to learn Mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single MK-state HMM and de-permuting the recovered states.  
- Uses spectral HMM estimation to recover a full MK-state model up to permutation, then exploits eigenstructure and a rank-K reconstruction + “spectral longevity” to detect K and regroup states.  
- Novelty: the specific de-permutation mechanism for recovering MHMM block structure from a full spectral HMM estimate appears new.

- Strength: Clear, well-organized exposition with a clean presentation of the block-diagonal equivalence and spectral intuition.  
- Strength: Insightful recasting of MHMM learning as de-permutation plus low-rank clustering.  
- Strength: Potential computational benefits for modest K and M and useful as EM initialization in some cases.

- Major experimental limitation: Experiments are too limited and lack full parameter details, random seeds, trial counts, and variance reporting.  
- Missing runtime/memory scaling analysis and breakdown (moments vs eigendecomposition vs clustering vs EM).  
- Need additional evaluation metrics (test log-likelihood, block-recovery F1, K-estimation accuracy, sensitivity to class imbalance).

- Practicality concern: Estimating/storing an MK×MK transition matrix and eigendecomposition may be infeasible for large K or M; complexity and appropriate regimes are not clarified.  
- Request clarity on when method is practical (e.g., MK ≤ few hundred, L ≥ MK, strong spectral separation) and whether sparse/randomized solvers or structure can be used.

- No guarantee of sparsity: Spectral estimation can yield dense matrices close to block-diagonal under noise, risking incorrect clustering.  
- Needs discussion of conditions for reliable clustering, failure modes with cross-block leakage, and potential structured/regularized fixes.  
- Request empirical evidence on false-positive cross-block edges as noise increases.

- Noise model issue: Synthetic experiments perturb permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise rather than sampling sequences, bypassing finite-sample and estimation bias effects.  
- Request experiments that sample sequences from MHMMs, vary sequence length/number/SNR/imbalance, and compare to EM (with/without spectral init) on recovery and generalization.

- Reproducibility gap: Section 4.2 lacks full MHMM parameter specifics; generative code or appendix needed.  
- EM initialization anomaly: EM sometimes worsens performance vs spectral alone; request training/test likelihood curves, label alignment confirmation, and details on K/M consistency.

- Theoretical gap: Key identifiability/sample-complexity claim depends on an unproven Conjecture 1 and reused bounds; paper should state limitations and required spectral gap conditions.  
- Practical concern: Requirement MK < L (feature lifting) may limit applicability; discuss feasibility and effect on noise/variance.

- Minor issues: Typo in eigen-decomposition subscript (Λ should be K); figures have small fonts and need clearer legends/vector graphics.

- Overall quality: Sound theoretical intuition but reliance on a conjecture and strong separation assumptions; empirical evidence insufficient for real-world robustness claims.  
- Originality: De-permutation via spectral longevity and low-rank reconstruction is novel.  
- Significance: Potentially useful for moderate MK if scalability and robustness are demonstrated, but current evidence is uncertain.

- Questions for authors: expected practical ranges of (K, M, L, sequences, avg length); sensitivity of K-estimation to small spectral gaps/near-deterministic transitions; ablations comparing alternative permutation-resolution methods.

- Recommendation: Interesting and novel but significant doubts about practical performance and robustness; recommend rejection in current form with encouragement to strengthen end-to-end experiments, clarify applicability, and tighten theory.
