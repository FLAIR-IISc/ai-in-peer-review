- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs that leverages block-diagonal structure and a de-permutation step using eigenvalue analysis.  
- De-permutation algorithm is promising and theoretically motivated, but the proof of Conjecture 1 is hand-wavy and relies on an inapplicable cited result.  
- Technical approach builds on established spectral HMM work (Hsu, Kakade & Zhang, 2014), making the core algorithm a minor variant rather than a major novelty.  
- Fails to adequately situate and compare to relevant prior work (notably Chaganty & Liang, 2013).  
- Experiments show the method works, but synthetic evaluations are limited and the real-data (UCI) results are confounded by feature transformations.  
- Practical impact depends on robustness and scalability of the de-permutation algorithm, which are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Notation is dense and some explanations (e.g., MATLAB colon usage, Lemma 3 → identification of K, Section 3.2.2) are rushed or unclear.  
- Paper contains typos and grammatical errors that hurt readability and suggest insufficient proofreading.  
- Theoretical contribution (error bound/conjecture) lacks rigor and unstated assumptions weaken the argument.  
- Potentially significant if conjecture holds and method scales, offering an EM-free alternative for MHMM learning and extensions to hierarchical latent models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, introduces a novel de-permutation idea, and provides encouraging empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on prior work, missing related-work discussion, clarity and notation issues, weak theoretical justification, and limited experiments.  
- Recommendation: weak reject; improvements needed in related work coverage, theoretical rigor, presentation quality, and experimental evaluation.
