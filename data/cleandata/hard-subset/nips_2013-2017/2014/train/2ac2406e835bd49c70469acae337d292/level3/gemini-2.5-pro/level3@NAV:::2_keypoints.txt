- Formulates MHMM as a single larger HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix to apply Anandkumar et al.’s spectral algorithm.  
- Main contribution is a stabilization method that resolves parameter permutation ambiguity by using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Stabilization leverages the limiting behavior of powers of the transition matrix to cluster hidden states, providing a direct non-iterative alternative to EM.  
- Theoretical analysis accounts for estimation noise via eigenvalue longevity (notably Eq. (7)), offering a principled way to determine the number of mixture components.  
- Strength: intuitive and elegant approach to parameter separation in noisy settings.  
- Weakness: notation is confusing and non-standard; matrix/vector dimensions should be explicitly defined and more common HMM notation adopted.  
- Weakness: key results (e.g., block-diagonal structure in Corollary 1) require clearer, more transparent explanations.  
- Weakness: inadequate engagement with related work, particularly spectral methods used to initialize EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, 2013).  
- Weakness: experimental validation is insufficient and poorly presented (hard-to-read plot); comparisons to EM appear unconvincing and possibly untuned.  
- Suggestion: improve experiments by tuning EM (multiple restarts, report best accuracy), present results in clearer tables/plots, and strengthen related-work discussion.
