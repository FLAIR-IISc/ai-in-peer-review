- Proposes a novel spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs).  
- Formulates an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix to apply existing spectral HMM techniques.  
- Introduces a stabilization technique using spectral properties to resolve permutation ambiguity across mixture components.  
- Assumes each local transition matrix has a dominant eigenvalue of 1, used to align blocks.  
- Provides theoretical support (Lemma 1, Lemma 2) linking MHMMs to block-diagonal HMMs.  
- Analyzes the impact of estimation noise and derives a condition (Equation (7)) for cluster number estimation.  
- Presents a conjectured spectral longevity argument (Lemma 3 / Eq. (7)) rather than a full proof.  
- Novel combination of MoM, spectral HMM learning, and stabilization appears original.  
- Potential for improved scalability and efficiency compared to EM on large datasets.  
- Clear problem statement and motivation for alternatives to EM.  
- Notation and presentation are confusing; dimensions and symbols are inconsistently defined.  
- Related work is insufficiently discussed; misses comparisons to EM-initialized spectral methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: unclear figures, lack of tables/statistical significance, and possibly unfair EM baselines.  
- Dataset and feature choices (UCI handwriting, cubic transform) are not well justified.  
- Strong assumptions about eigenvalues may limit practical applicability and need further discussion.  
- Conjectures and spectral conditions require more rigorous proofs or empirical validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded alternative to EM, and a new stabilization idea.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, weak experiments, strong/unverified assumptions, and incomplete related work.  
- Overall recommendation: rejection in current form with encouragement to improve clarity, experiments, proofs, and related-work coverage before resubmission.
