- Introduces a spectral algorithm for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMM) using the Method of Moments to avoid EM’s computational issues.  
- Models MHMM as a special-case HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix to apply spectral learning techniques.  
- Proposes a stabilization-based de-permutation scheme leveraging spectral properties and assumes each HMM transition has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Empirically validates the algorithm on synthetic and UCI handwritten-character data, reporting faster computation than EM.  

Strengths:
- Stabilization scheme for parameter permutation is novel and intuitive, using eigenvalue-based de-permutation.  
- MoM-based approach gives significant computational advantages over EM for large datasets.  
- Spectral analysis (eigenvalue discussion, Corollary 1, spectral longevity metric) is clear and interesting.  
- Real-data experiment suggests practical utility as an initialization for EM.  

Weaknesses:
- Notation and clarity are poor; vectors/matrices lack consistent dimensional definitions, hindering understanding.  
- Missing references to related work, especially prior spectral initializations for EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Experimental section is shallow; figures are hard to interpret and EM results (Table 1) are suspiciously poor.  
- Corollary 1 and the structure of lim_{e→∞} \bar{A}^e and 1J^T need clearer explanation.  

Pro arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles a computationally challenging problem with a novel, efficient method.  
- Stabilization scheme and spectral insights are theoretically interesting and could inspire future work.  
- Shows practical value as an EM initialization technique.  

Con arguments against acceptance:
- Poor clarity and organization, especially in notation and key-result explanations.  
- Experiments lack depth and rigorous comparison to prior methods.  
- Missing citations to relevant prior work weaken the originality claim.  

Recommendation:
- Weak accept, contingent on addressing clarity/notation, expanding experiments, and better situating the work in related literature.
