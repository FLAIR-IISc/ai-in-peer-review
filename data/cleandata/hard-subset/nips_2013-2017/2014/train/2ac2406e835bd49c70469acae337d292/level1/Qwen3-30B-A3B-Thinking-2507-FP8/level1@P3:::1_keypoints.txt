- Novel insight: MHMM can be represented as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling spectral recovery of mixtures.  
- Technical contribution: Uses spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity in Method-of-Moments for MHMMs.  
- Spectral metric: Introduces a spectral longevity metric for cluster identification (Definition 3) that is elegant and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical foundation: Provides lemmas (Lemma 1, Lemma 2, Corollary 1) connecting MHMM structure to spectral learning for HMMs and builds on prior work.  
- Algorithm/presentation: Algorithm 1 and the paper’s notation, figures, and organization are clear and accessible.  
- Empirical results: Experiments show large computational advantages over EM while maintaining comparable accuracy (Figure 3, Figure 4).  
- Practical utility: Demonstrates usefulness as an initialization for EM on real data (Table 1).  
- Theoretical gaps: Some claims remain conjectural (Conjecture 1) and error bounds rely on results from a different model without full justification.  
- Noise analysis: The paper’s treatment of noise effects on spectral properties and error bounds is insufficiently rigorous.  
- Applicability constraint: The requirement MK < L for MoM application is a significant limitation and only partially addressed by polynomial feature tricks.  
- Comparison to prior work: Lacks adequate comparisons to other recent MHMM approaches (e.g., tensor decomposition, variational methods).  
- Failure modes: Limited discussion and empirical exploration of cases where the spectral approach fails or noise conditions are violated.  
- Significance: Offers a meaningful advance enabling scalable spectral learning for MHMMs and practical EM initialization, but applicability is limited by MK < L.  
- Recommendation: Overall recommendation to accept, citing novelty, solid experiments, and practical impact despite theoretical and applicability weaknesses.
