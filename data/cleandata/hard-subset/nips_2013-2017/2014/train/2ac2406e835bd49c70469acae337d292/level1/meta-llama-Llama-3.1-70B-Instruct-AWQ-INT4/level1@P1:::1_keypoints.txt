- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments (MoM) approach for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) using spectral properties of a global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity, yielding lower computational cost than EM with comparable accuracy.

- Extends recent MoM/spectral algorithms for latent-variable models to the MHMM setting, addressing MHMM-specific challenges like permutation ambiguity in clustering sequences.

- Technically sound with clear, well-supported theoretical analysis and empirical validation on synthetic and real data.

- Authors explicitly discuss strengths and weaknesses, including limitations of the spectral conditions for estimating the number of clusters.

- Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible to readers familiar with HMMs and MoM; notation and derivations are clear.

- Original idea: exploiting spectral properties to resolve permutation ambiguity in MHMM parameter estimation.

- Significant contribution: computationally efficient learning for MHMMs that scales to large datasets and suggests extensions to hierarchical latent models.

- Pro: novel, effective approach with thorough theory and experiments and important implications for large-scale applications.

- Con: approach assumes a specific transition-matrix structure that may not hold in practice.

- Con: spectral conditions for estimating cluster count may be difficult to satisfy in some cases.

- Con: lacks comprehensive comparisons to alternative methods such as variational inference.

- Minor suggestion: provide more details on computational time and memory complexity.

- Minor suggestion: add visualizations (estimated transition matrices, emission probabilities).

- Minor suggestion: discuss broader application implications (e.g., speech recognition, NLP).
