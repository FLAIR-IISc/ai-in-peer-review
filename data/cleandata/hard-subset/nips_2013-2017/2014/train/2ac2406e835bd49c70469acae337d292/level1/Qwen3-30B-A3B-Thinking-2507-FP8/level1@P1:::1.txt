

# Peer Review: Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models

This paper proposes a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors recognize that MHMMs are computationally challenging to learn with EM due to the need for forward-backward message passing for each sequence in every iteration. They cleverly observe that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, allowing them to leverage existing spectral learning algorithms for HMMs [1,2,3]. The key contribution is solving the permutation ambiguity issue that arises when applying standard spectral learning to MHMMs, using the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover the individual HMM parameters. This work builds on Anandkumar et al.'s spectral learning framework for HMMs presented at NIPS and COLT, extending it to the more complex MHMM setting, which Smyth [4] previously addressed using EM.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel insight:** The paper's central contribution—recognizing MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure and using spectral properties to resolve permutation ambiguity—is elegant and well-motivated. This approach directly addresses a fundamental limitation of applying standard spectral learning to MHMMs.

2. **Theoretical grounding:** The authors provide clear theoretical justification through Lemma 1 (MHMM-HMM equivalence) and Lemma 2 (spectral properties of global transition matrix). While some aspects are presented as conjectures (e.g., Conjecture 1), the theoretical analysis is generally sound and well-justified.

3. **Computational advantage:** The paper convincingly demonstrates that the proposed method avoids the computational bottleneck of EM, with learning time independent of data size. The experiments (Section 4) show that the spectral approach is significantly faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy, especially for larger datasets.

4. **Practical application:** The real data experiment on handwritten character trajectories demonstrates practical utility, showing that the spectral method can serve as an effective initialization for EM, a valuable contribution for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Theoretical completeness:** Conjecture 1 regarding the error bound for the low-rank reconstruction is not proven. The justification relies on borrowing bounds from [1], but the authors should provide a more rigorous analysis specific to MHMMs. The spectral longevity definition (Definition 3) is somewhat ad hoc and could benefit from deeper theoretical justification.

2. **Experimental limitations:** The real data experiment requires a cubic polynomial feature transformation to satisfy the MK < L constraint for MoM learning. While this is acknowledged, the paper doesn't thoroughly explore how this transformation affects the performance on the original problem. The comparison with EM in Figure 4 is limited to specific parameter settings (K=5, M=3), and a more comprehensive comparison across different parameter regimes would strengthen the claims.

3. **Lack of comparison with alternative approaches:** The paper doesn't compare with other spectral learning methods for mixture models (e.g., [10]), which would better establish the novelty and advantages of their specific approach for MHMMs.

4. **Practical implementation details:** The paper doesn't discuss how the algorithm handles cases where the spectral conditions (|λGK| > max_k |λL2,k|) are not perfectly satisfied in practice, which could be a significant limitation for real-world applications.

**Overall Evaluation:**

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the spectral learning literature by extending these methods to MHMMs, a model that has previously been difficult to learn efficiently at scale. The theoretical insight is strong, the computational advantages are well-demonstrated, and the practical utility is shown through experiments. While the theoretical analysis could be more complete, the core ideas are sound and represent a meaningful advance over traditional EM-based approaches.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The theoretical arguments are mostly rigorous, though some conjectures could be better justified. The experimental results provide reasonable evidence for the approach's effectiveness. The authors are appropriately cautious about limitations.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with good mathematical notation and consistent terminology. Algorithm 1 is particularly well-described. Some of the spectral analysis (e.g., Definition 3) could be made more intuitive for readers less familiar with spectral methods.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel application of spectral learning to MHMMs, with a clever solution to the permutation ambiguity problem. This represents a meaningful extension of existing spectral learning techniques to a more complex model class, differentiating it from previous NIPS papers on spectral learning for HMMs.

**Significance:** The computational efficiency of the proposed method makes MHMM learning feasible for large datasets where EM would be prohibitively expensive. The demonstrated utility as an initialization scheme for EM further enhances its practical significance. This work could enable new applications in sequence clustering where computational efficiency is critical.

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper makes a solid contribution to the spectral learning literature with clear theoretical insights, practical computational advantages, and empirical validation. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the core ideas are sound and represent a valuable extension to the field. The paper addresses a real problem in sequence modeling with a novel and effective solution that advances the state of the art.
