- Proposes a Method-of-Moments (MoM) spectral approach for learning Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) to avoid costly EM optimization.  
- Leverages the block-diagonal structure of the MHMM transition matrix and spectral techniques to resolve permutation ambiguities from MoM.  
- Clearly proves the connection between MHMMs and an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix (Lemma 1), giving a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Builds on Hsu et al. (2013) and extends spectral HMM learning to the MHMM setting.  
- Novel and technically deep use of spectral properties to depermutate MoM estimates.  
- Theoretical analysis is persuasive but depends on stated conjectures that lack rigorous justification.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show computational efficiency advantages over EM.  
- Real-data experiment uses a cubic polynomial feature transform, raising concerns about preprocessing choices and sensitivity.  
- Comparisons with different EM initializations (random and MoM) are a strength.  
- Transition from idealized spectral theory to noisy, real-world scenarios is not fully addressed.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 under-explained, notation dense in places, and some sections hard to follow.  
- Experimental accuracy does not consistently outperform well-initialized EM, questioning practical benefits when accuracy matters.  
- References are somewhat sparse; broader spectral clustering and recent spectral latent-variable work are under-discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theory (robustness to noise), clarify presentation, and expand discussion of preprocessing and related literature.
