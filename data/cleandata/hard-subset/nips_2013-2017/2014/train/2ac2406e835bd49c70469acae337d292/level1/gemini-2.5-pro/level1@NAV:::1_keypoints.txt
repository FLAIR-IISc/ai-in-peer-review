- Proposes a Method-of-Moments spectral algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by re-framing an MHMM as a single HMM with MK states and a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Applies the Hsu et al. (2012) spectral HMM learning algorithm to this equivalent large HMM.  
- Main technical contribution: resolves the permutation ambiguity from spectral estimation by clustering columns of a low-rank reconstruction of the global transition matrix to recover block structure.  
- Builds on Anandkumar et al.’s spectral latent-variable work, extending applicability from single HMMs to MHMMs.  
- Originality/significance: offers a non-iterative, elegant alternative to EM that addresses permutation ambiguity and is highly practical for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Practical benefit: demonstrates significant speedups over EM and comparable accuracy given sufficient data.  
- Technical quality: equivalence and noiseless de-permutation reasoning are sound; synthetic experiments show robustness to noise.  
- Theoretical weakness: noisy-case analysis relies on an unproven Conjecture 1 and borrows sample-complexity bounds from a different model, weakening guarantees.  
- Theoretical weakness: proof for estimating number of clusters (Lemma 3) is brief and needs strengthening.  
- Experimental weakness: on real data the method is an excellent EM initializer but does not consistently beat the best random-EM run, tempering claims of being a standalone replacement.  
- Experimental weakness: paper should discuss how often the required spectral gap condition holds in practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, computationally efficient MHMM learning algorithm; clever spectral de-permutation; major practical speed advantage.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete theoretical guarantees for the noisy case due to reliance on a conjecture.  
- Overall summary/recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution with practical impact; recommend acceptance despite theoretical gaps.
