- Proposes a spectral Method of Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Key technical contribution: resolves permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates via spectral analysis of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Theoretical foundation (Lemmas 1–3, Conjecture 1) is well-motivated and establishes equivalence between MHMMs and structured HMMs.  
- Addresses estimation noise and provides a clear algorithmic depermutation solution (Algorithm 1).  
- Experiments on synthetic data show robustness to noise and scaling with data size.  
- Real-data experiment on handwritten character trajectories demonstrates practical utility and comparable accuracy to EM.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency over EM (orders-of-magnitude speedup in experiments).  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and accessible with consistent notation and helpful figures.  
- Novel extension of spectral learning to MHMMs, differentiating from prior EM-based and standard HMM spectral work.  
- Introduces spectral conditions for identifying the number of clusters (Definition 3, Lemma 3) as a novel contribution.  
- Practical significance: offers an efficient alternative or initializer for EM in large-scale sequence clustering tasks.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, novel approach, comprehensive experiments, clear writing, and strong computational gains.  
- Weaknesses: relies on Conjecture 1 without formal proof; needs discussion of conditions for the conjecture.  
- Weaknesses: requirement MK < L for MoM limits applicability and needs more discussion of practical impact.  
- Weaknesses: real-data preprocessing (polynomial feature transform to meet MK < L) could alter data structure and needs fuller justification.  
- Pro arguments: solves key computational bottleneck, theoretically sound, and empirically validated with clear benefits.  
- Con arguments: unproven conjecture, MK < L constraint, and reliance on data transformation in real experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance despite minor theoretical and applicability caveats.
