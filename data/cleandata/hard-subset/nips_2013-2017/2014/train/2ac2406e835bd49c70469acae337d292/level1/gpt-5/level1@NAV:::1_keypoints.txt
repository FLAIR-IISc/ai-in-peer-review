- Proposes a spectral method-of-moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Uses an HMM spectral learner to estimate global parameters up to a permutation of states.  
- Main technical contribution: a de-permutation procedure exploiting spectral properties—K eigenvalues at 1 induce K rank-1 blocks in the noiseless case.  
- Under noise, proposes low-rank reconstruction using K “most persistent” eigenvalues via a “spectral longevity” heuristic, then clusters columns to recover block membership and estimate K.  
- Experiments (synthetic + UCI handwriting) show faster runtime than EM, competitive clustering accuracy, and utility as an EM initializer.  
- Builds on MoM/tensor literature and prior MHMM work (Anandkumar et al.; Smyth; Qi et al.).  
- Strength: reduction to block-diagonal HMM is sound and de-permutation idea is intuitive and simple.  
- Weakness: core robustness claims rely on Conjecture 1 and heuristic conditions (e.g., |λGK| > maxk |λL2,k|) without formal guarantees for K recovery or clustering under perturbations.  
- MoM step for continuous emissions is underspecified (which moments/features and whitening not detailed).  
- Sample complexity bound is borrowed rather than derived specifically for this setting.  
- Empirics focus on clustering accuracy and runtime but omit parameter recovery metrics, likelihoods, and robustness to model mismatch.  
- Clarity: generally well organized, but Section 3 needs precise moment construction, computational complexity, and clearer guidance on longevity hyperparameters (γ, exponent range).  
- Originality: casting MHMM learning as spectral de-permutation is a neat synthesis; longevity-based K selection is novel but heuristic.  
- Significance: could be a scalable alternative or EM initializer if made robust, but lack of guarantees limits impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, scalable algorithm; practical speedups over EM; clear NeurIPS relevance; useful EM initializer.  
- Cons for acceptance: theoretical claims are conjectural with no perturbation/consistency guarantees; incomplete moment specification for continuous emissions; limited empirical breadth and diagnostics; sensitivity of K estimation not fully analyzed.  
- Suggested fixes: provide perturbation bounds (e.g., Davis–Kahan/Wedin), compare longevity to eigengap criteria, report parameter error and likelihood, detail moment features and computational costs, and assess robustness to misspecification and overlapping spectra.
