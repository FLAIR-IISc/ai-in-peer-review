- Paper proposes a method-of-moments algorithm for Mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single KM-state HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Main challenge: spectral HMM methods recover KM states up to an unknown permutation that mixes component states.  
- Key contribution: a spectral de-permutation procedure using the top K eigenvectors to produce a low-rank reconstruction whose columns cluster to recover component membership.  
- Proposes an eigenvalue "longevity" heuristic to estimate the number of components K.  
- Demonstrated speed advantages over EM on synthetic and real data.  

- Strength: original and significant idea reframing MHMM-as-HMM and solving the permutation via spectral properties.  
- Strength: paper is clear and well-structured with helpful figures and intuition (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Strength: methodological novelty—leveraging block-diagonality and spectral structure to disentangle mixture components.  

- Weakness: theoretical claims lack full rigor; key results are conjectural rather than formally proved.  
- Weakness: Conjecture 1 (error bound for low-rank reconstruction) is hand-wavy and borrows bounds from different settings without formal derivation.  
- Weakness: Lemma 3 and the model-selection (spectral longevity) approach are heuristic and not thoroughly justified.  
- Weakness: method relies on a strong spectral gap assumption (|λ_GK| > max_k |λ_L2,k|) that may fail for slowly mixing components and is not explicitly discussed.  
- Weakness: experiments are incomplete—model-selection criterion not evaluated in main experiments.  
- Weakness: real-data preprocessing to meet MK < L is ad-hoc (cubic transform) and its effect is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: comparison with EM shows lower accuracy at small data sizes; more experiments for larger N and T and against well-initialized EM are needed.  

- Argument for acceptance: novel, elegant, and computationally efficient alternative to EM with potential practical impact and follow-up theoretical work.  
- Argument for acceptance: spectral de-permutation is a significant methodological contribution that could generalize to other mixture models.  
- Argument for rejection: central theoretical results are conjectural and lack rigorous proofs.  
- Argument for rejection: critical spectral gap assumption is under-discussed and untested.  
- Argument for rejection: empirical validation of automatic K selection is missing.  

- Recommendation: accept—novel, impactful idea with clear weaknesses in theory and experiments but worthwhile as a conference contribution.
