- Proposes a Method of Moments (MoM) algorithm for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Tackles permutation ambiguity in MoM estimates by using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover de-permutation mapping.  
- Presents a method to estimate the number of component HMMs.  
- Well-motivated: aims to reduce computational bottleneck of EM for MHMMs, especially on large datasets.  
- Builds clearly on prior MoM work for HMMs and correctly identifies the permutation ambiguity challenge.  
- Spectral approach to de-permutation is novel and could yield significant computational advantages if effective.  
- Technical approach appears sound and extends established MoM techniques to MHMMs.  
- Theoretical justification includes Lemmas 1 & 2 and Conjecture 1, but Conjecture 1 is somewhat hand-wavy and needs stronger proof.  
- Experimental validation includes synthetic and real data but is limited in scope and depth.  
- Real-data experiments are weakly analyzed and only shown as an initialization for EM rather than compared to state-of-the-art MHMM methods.  
- Handling of noise and its effect on spectral decomposition is acknowledged, but the proposed spectral condition (Definition 3) is complex and practicality is unclear.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear definitions and Algorithm 1 offering a helpful overview.  
- Spectral analysis section (3.2) is dense and requires advanced spectral graph theory background; Equations (5) and (6) are hard to follow.  
- Notation is mostly consistent, though transitions between index and matrix notation could be smoother.  
- Originality: applying spectral learning to resolve permutation ambiguity in MHMMs is a genuine contribution and appropriately cites related work.  
- Overall assessment: promising and technically interesting but limited by weak experimental evaluation and some theoretical gaps; reviewer recommends weak accept.
