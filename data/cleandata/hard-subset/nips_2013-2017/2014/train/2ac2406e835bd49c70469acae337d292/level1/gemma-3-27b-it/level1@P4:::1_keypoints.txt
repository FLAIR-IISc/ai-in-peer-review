- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for learning Mixtures of HMMs by exploiting block-diagonal structure to resolve permutation ambiguity.
- Derives equivalence between an MHMM and a specific HMM structure (Lemma 1), which is central to the algorithm.
- Introduces a de-permutation procedure based on spectral analysis and a spectral longevity concept to estimate the number of HMMs (K).
- Provides a concrete algorithm (Algorithm 1) and validates the approach on synthetic and a real handwritten-character dataset.
- Builds on prior MoM and spectral HMM work, extending those ideas to the MHMM setting.
- Technical approach is sound and well-motivated, with clear mathematical formulation.
- Reliance on Conjecture 1 about noise levels is a notable theoretical weakness.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art MHMM/EM variants and diverse real datasets.
- Real-data experiment is narrow (one dataset and feature transform), limiting generalizability.
- Explanation of spectral longevity and its practical link to estimating K needs clearer exposition.
- Originality is incremental but meaningful: novel application of spectral methods to MHMM permutation ambiguity.
- Potential impact is moderate: offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM, especially when initialization is problematic.
- Experimental results do not clearly demonstrate superior performance over EM with good initialization.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future work, indicating careful consideration of weaknesses.
- Reviewer recommendation: weak accept, conditional on addressing the conjecture and strengthening experimental validation.
