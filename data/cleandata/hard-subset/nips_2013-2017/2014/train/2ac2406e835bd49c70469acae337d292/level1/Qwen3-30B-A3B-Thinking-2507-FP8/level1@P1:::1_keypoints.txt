- Proposal: Spectral Method-of-Moments approach for Mixture of HMMs by viewing an MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition and resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.

- Strength — Novel insight: Recognizing MHMM as block-diagonal HMM and using spectral properties to fix permutation ambiguity is elegant and well-motivated.

- Strength — Theoretical grounding: Lemma 1 and Lemma 2 give clear justification; analysis is generally sound though some aspects are conjectural.

- Strength — Computational advantage: Method avoids per-iteration forward-backward EM cost, yielding learning time independent of data size and much faster performance with comparable accuracy.

- Strength — Practical application: Demonstrated on handwritten character trajectories and effective as an initialization for EM.

- Weakness — Theoretical completeness: Conjecture 1 (error bound) is unproven and the spectral longevity definition is ad hoc and needs stronger justification.

- Weakness — Experimental limitations: Requires cubic polynomial feature transform to meet MK < L; limited EM comparisons (only K=5, M=3) and insufficient exploration of transformation impact and parameter regimes.

- Weakness — Comparison gap: No comparisons with other spectral methods for mixture models, weakening claims of novelty and advantage.

- Weakness — Implementation details: Paper lacks guidance for cases when required spectral conditions are not satisfied in practice.

- Overall: Valuable and meaningful extension of spectral learning to MHMMs with strong insights and empirical gains despite gaps in theory and experiments.

- Quality: Technically sound with mostly rigorous arguments, but some conjectures need better justification.

- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written; some spectral analysis could be made more intuitive.

- Originality: Novel extension of spectral learning to MHMMs with a clever solution to permutation ambiguity.

- Significance: Enables scalable MHMM learning and provides a useful EM initialization, broadening applicability for sequence clustering.

- Recommendation: Accept.
