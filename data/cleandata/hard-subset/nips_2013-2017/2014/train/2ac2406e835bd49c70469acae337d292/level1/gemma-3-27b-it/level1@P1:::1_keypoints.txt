- Proposes a method-of-moments algorithm for MHMMs that uses the block-diagonal structure of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.  
- Addresses a practical bottleneck by offering an alternative to EM that can scale better with dataset size.  
- Clearly connects to prior MoM and spectral HMM work and demonstrates good understanding of related literature and applications.  
- Core idea is technically sound; lemmas and proofs are logically presented though may need specialist scrutiny.  
- Promises significant computational savings compared to EM, especially on large datasets.  
- Novel combination of MoM and spectral techniques applied to the mixture setting.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and handwriting trajectory data shows effectiveness and faster runtime than EM.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; model definitions and algorithmic steps are explained.  
- Several key claims are presented as conjectures (e.g., spectral longevity, noise thresholds) without rigorous proofs, weakening theoretical guarantees.  
- Algorithm performance appears sensitive to noise, and robustness to varying noise levels is not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Real-data experiment relies on a cubic polynomial feature transform, raising questions about generality and possible bias.  
- Algorithm 1 omits specifics about the clustering method used to identify transition-matrix blocks, hurting reproducibility.  
- Lacks benchmarking against other recent MHMM learning approaches beyond EM, limiting assessment of relative performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically grounded approach with encouraging empirical results and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on unproven assumptions, sensitivity to noise, limited real-world validation, and missing comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong borderline accept — promising and original but needs stronger theory and robustness evidence.  
- Recommendation: Borderline Accept, encouraging revisions to address identified weaknesses.  
- Specific suggestions: analyze noise sensitivity, strengthen or prove conjectures, expand real-world datasets and baselines, specify clustering details, and discuss the cubic transform’s limitations.
