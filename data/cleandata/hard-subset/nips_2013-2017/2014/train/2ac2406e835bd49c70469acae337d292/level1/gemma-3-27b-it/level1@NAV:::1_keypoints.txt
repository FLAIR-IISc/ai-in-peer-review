- Proposes a Method-of-Moments spectral approach for learning Mixture of HMMs, using block-diagonal structure and spectral properties to resolve permutation ambiguity and recover number of HMMs.  
- Builds on prior spectral latent-variable work and shows equivalence between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.  
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical derivation and a logically consistent algorithm leveraging established spectral methods.  
- Computational efficiency: method offers potential significant speedups over EM for large datasets.  
- Experimental validation: synthetic and real (handwritten trajectories) experiments demonstrate feasibility and a speed advantage versus EM.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Weakness — spectral conditions: algorithm depends on specific spectral conditions (Definition 1, Lemma 3); robustness to noise/deviations is uncertain.  
- Weakness — real-data preprocessing: using a cubic polynomial feature transform to meet MK < L may confound whether gains come from the transform or the algorithm.  
- Weakness — limited empirical comparison: comparison to EM focuses on speed and lacks detailed accuracy trade-offs across conditions.  
- Weakness — theoretical gap: the key accuracy bound (Conjecture 1) is unproven.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel spectral application to MHMMs, addresses an important computational bottleneck, and shows promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on restrictive spectral assumptions, unproven conjecture, and limited experimental scope.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — request fuller robustness analysis, more detailed EM comparison including accuracy metrics, and effort to address the conjecture.
