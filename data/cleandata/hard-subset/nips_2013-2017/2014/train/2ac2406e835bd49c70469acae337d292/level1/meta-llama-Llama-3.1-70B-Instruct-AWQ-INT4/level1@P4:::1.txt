**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors leverage the spectral properties of a global transition matrix to resolve the permutation ambiguity in the estimation process, allowing for efficient learning of MHMM parameters. The approach is based on the observation that an MHMM can be expressed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors demonstrate the validity of their approach on synthetic and real data.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2] and MoM-based algorithms for learning latent variable models [3]. The authors extend these ideas to MHMMs, which are useful models for clustering sequences. The paper also relates to previous work on clustering sequences with HMMs [4, 5, 6, 7].

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for learning MHMMs, which is computationally efficient and scalable to large datasets.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, which is essential for resolving the permutation ambiguity.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on synthetic and real data.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the number of hidden states for each HMM is the same, which may not be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a comprehensive analysis of the sample complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is an important aspect of MoM-based learning.
* The paper does not compare the proposed approach with other state-of-the-art methods for learning MHMMs.
