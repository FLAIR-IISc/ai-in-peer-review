- Proposes a method-of-moments / spectral approach for Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Resolves the permutation ambiguity in MoM estimation using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Builds on Anandkumar et al. (2012) spectral HMM framework and extends it to the MHMM setting.  
- Provides a theoretical lemma (Lemma 1) showing the block-diagonal representation and a recovery method for individual HMM parameters.  
- Algorithm (Algorithm 1) is precise, implementable, and the paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Synthetic experiments show the algorithm recovers block-diagonal structure even with limited data.  
- Real-data experiments demonstrate the method is a useful initialization for EM and has practical utility.  
- Shows substantial computational advantages over EM for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Key limitation: requires MK < L (observation dimension), which may not hold in practice; authors use a cubic polynomial feature transform as a workaround.  
- Conjecture 1 (error bounds) is not fully proven and relies on justification from prior work.  
- Could better distinguish and compare itself to other spectral methods for mixtures (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Lacks a thorough comparison with other spectral/tensor decomposition approaches for mixture models (e.g., Anandkumar et al., 2014).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen related-work comparisons and more fully discuss the MK < L constraint.
