- Proposes a spectral (method-of-moments) algorithm that reduces an MHMM with K components of size M to a single HMM with MK states having block-diagonal transition and concatenated emissions.  
- Uses existing spectral HMM learners to recover global HMM parameters up to permutation, then de-permutes states via spectral properties of the transition matrix.  
- In the noiseless case, powers of the block-diagonal transition yield rank-1 blocks enabling exact clustering of columns to recover component membership.  
- In the noisy case, proposes selecting top-K “global” eigenvalues (closest to 1) to build a rank-K approximation of A and cluster its columns for de-permutation.  
- Introduces a heuristic “spectral longevity” procedure to estimate K by tracking how long the top-K eigenvalues dominate under exponentiation.  
- Builds on prior spectral HMM/MoM work (Hsu et al., Anandkumar et al.) and MHMM clustering (Smyth 1997); misses citing related PSR/Hilbert embedding spectral lines.  
- Reduction to block-diagonal HMM and the noiseless de-permutation argument are correct and useful.  
- Core noisy-case guarantees are conjectural; Conjecture 1 and Lemma 3 lack formal perturbation/finite-sample analysis.  
- No Davis–Kahan/Wedin-style perturbation analysis quantifying when global eigenvalues remain dominant under estimation noise.  
- K-estimation “longevity” heuristic is ad hoc and not analyzed for robustness to small spectral gaps.  
- Synthetic experiments are small-scale and mainly show de-permutation vs. sequence length; EM comparisons are modest in scale.  
- Real-data experiment (pen-trajectory) required feature lifting (L from 3 to 10), altering generative assumptions and possibly biasing comparisons.  
- No experiments on other emission families (Poisson, multinomial) despite claims of coverage, and limited timing/large-scale results.  
- Important methodological details are omitted: exact MoM moments used, multi-view construction, rank/column-rank assumptions, and the MK<L constraint’s implications.  
- Recovery of mixture weights π and component-specific ν is not clearly described or explicitly reported.  
- Paper is generally readable but has notational overloading, elided steps, and missing explicit assumptions (ergodicity, aperiodicity, simple eigenvalue at 1).  
- The de-permutation via spectrum is novel in this context but related to spectral clustering/graph partitioning and eigenspace clustering literatures.  
- If robustified, the method could speed MHMM training and provide strong EM initializers, but practical impact is limited by MK<L and eigenvalue separation sensitivity.  
- Strengths: simple reduction, intuitive de-permutation, potential computational advantages, and useful EM initialization.  
- Weaknesses: conjectural guarantees, ad hoc K-estimation, MK<L limitation, limited empirical scope (small scale, one real dataset, Gaussian emissions only), and missing algorithmic detail.  
- Suggested improvements: provide perturbation/eigenspace analysis, compare to alternative block-recovery methods, detail the MoM step and rank conditions, discuss MK<L relaxations, report π/ν recovery and clustering accuracy, evaluate more datasets/emission types, and include large-scale timing and ablations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a concrete gap, conceptually simple, potentially useful and empirically promising as an EM initializer.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks theoretical guarantees for noisy case, limited/small-scale empirical validation, MK<L reduces applicability, and incomplete methodological details.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting idea but needs stronger theory and broader experiments; reviewer leans toward rejection in current form.
