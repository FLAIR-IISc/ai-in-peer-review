- Proposes a Method-of-Moments approach for Mixture of HMMs using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping and recover individual HMM parameters; can also recover the number of HMMs under spectral conditions.  
- Problem is important and challenging; paper is well-motivated with adequate related work.  
- Proposed approach is novel and potentially more computationally efficient than EM.  
- Technical soundness is good, with a clear, detailed explanation and thorough analysis of validity conditions.  
- Innovative use of spectral properties to estimate the de-permutation mapping.  
- Experimental results are convincing on both synthetic and real data.  
- Paper clarity and organization are good; notation is clearly explained.  
- High originality and significance.  
- For acceptance: novel problem solution, clear exposition, solid technical analysis, convincing experiments, and innovative spectral method.  
- Against acceptance: assumes equal number of hidden states per HMM, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Against acceptance: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis for large-scale datasets.  
- Against acceptance: lacks detailed empirical comparison with other approaches such as EM.  
- Suggestion: provide a detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Suggestion: include detailed comparisons to EM and other baselines.  
- Suggestion: consider relaxing the equal-hidden-state-size assumption.  
- Suggestion: add more real-world experimental results to further demonstrate effectiveness.
