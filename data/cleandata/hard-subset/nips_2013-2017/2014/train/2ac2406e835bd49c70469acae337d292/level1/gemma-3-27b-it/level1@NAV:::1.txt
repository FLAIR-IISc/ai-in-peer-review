## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM), addressing the computational challenges of traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithms, particularly for large datasets. The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure inherent in MHMMs and employ spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguities arising from the MoM estimation.

The paper builds upon recent work in spectral learning for latent variable models [1, 2, 3], extending these methods to the more complex MHMM setting. It correctly identifies the computational bottleneck of EM for MHMMs [4] and positions MoM as a potentially more scalable alternative. The authors convincingly demonstrate the equivalence between an MHMM and a specific type of HMM, facilitating the application of existing MoM techniques. The novelty lies in tackling the permutation ambiguity through spectral analysis and proposing a method to recover the number of HMMs based on eigenvalue properties. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivation connecting MHMMs to HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices is clear and well-motivated. The proposed algorithm appears logically sound, leveraging established spectral methods.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The potential for significant speedup compared to EM, especially with large datasets, is a major strength.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real data (handwritten character trajectories) demonstrate the feasibility of the approach. The comparison to EM, particularly showcasing the speed advantage, is valuable.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the key concepts and algorithm steps.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Spectral Conditions:** The reliance on specific spectral conditions (Definition 1, Lemma 3) for accurate cluster recovery seems potentially limiting. The robustness of the algorithm to noise and deviations from these conditions requires further investigation.
*   **Real Data Experiment:** The use of a cubic polynomial feature transformation to meet the model requirements (MK < L) in the real data experiment introduces a potential confound. It’s unclear how much the results are influenced by this transformation rather than the core algorithm.
*   **Limited Comparison:** The comparison with EM is somewhat limited, primarily focusing on speed. A more detailed analysis of accuracy trade-offs under different data conditions would strengthen the paper.
*   **Conjecture Status:** The critical result regarding the accuracy bound (Conjecture 1) remains unproven, leaving a gap in the theoretical foundation.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel application of spectral methods to MHMM learning.
*   Addresses a significant computational challenge in a widely-used model.
*   Promising experimental results demonstrating feasibility and speedup.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Reliance on potentially restrictive spectral conditions.
*   Unproven accuracy bound.
*   Limited scope of experimental comparison.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising and technically sound contribution to the field of MHMM learning. While the reliance on spectral conditions and the unproven conjecture raise some concerns, the potential for significant computational gains and the encouraging experimental results warrant acceptance. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more thorough discussion of the robustness of the algorithm to noise and a more detailed comparison with EM, including accuracy metrics under various conditions. Addressing the conjecture would also significantly strengthen the paper.
