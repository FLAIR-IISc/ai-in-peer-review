- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs), addressing computational challenges in MHMM learning.  
- Builds on prior spectral HMM work by viewing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Key innovation: resolves the MoM permutation ambiguity via spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Provides theoretical support (Lemmas 1–3) and a practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) for depermutation and parameter recovery.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets, showing computational advantages over EM, especially on large datasets.  
- Shows practical utility on handwritten character trajectories and as an EM initialization.  
- Theoretical analysis depends on an unproven Conjecture 1, weakening full theoretical guarantees.  
- Some theoretical justifications borrow results from other work without fully adapting them to the MHMM setting.  
- Real-data experiment requires a polynomial feature transform to meet L > MK, which may limit applicability to some datasets.  
- Lacks thorough comparison to alternative spectral methods for mixture models, reducing the strength of empirical significance claims.  
- Clearly written, well-organized, and makes a meaningful computational contribution; overall a strong candidate for acceptance despite theoretical gaps.
