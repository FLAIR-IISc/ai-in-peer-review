**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models" proposes a novel approach to learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors leverage the fact that an MHMM can be represented as a single Hidden Markov Model (HMM) with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They then apply an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters of the HMM, which recovers the parameters up to a permutation ambiguity. To resolve this ambiguity, they exploit the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping. The authors also propose a method to estimate the number of HMM clusters under certain spectral conditions.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2] and MoM-based algorithms for learning latent variable models [3]. The authors extend these ideas to the more complex MHMM model, which has various applications in clustering sequences [4, 5, 6, 7].

The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various settings, including synthetic and real data.

However, there are a few minor issues that could be improved:

* The paper assumes that each local transition matrix has only one eigenvalue with magnitude 1. While this assumption is reasonable in many cases, it would be useful to discuss the implications of relaxing this assumption.
