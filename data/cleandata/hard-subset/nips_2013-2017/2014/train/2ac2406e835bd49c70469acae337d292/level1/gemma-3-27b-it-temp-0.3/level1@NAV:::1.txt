## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a novel method for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM), addressing the computational challenges of traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) approaches, particularly for large datasets. The core idea is to leverage the equivalence between an MHMM and a block-diagonal HMM, then utilize spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation. 

The paper clearly positions itself within the growing body of work on MoM-based learning for latent variable models [1, 2, 3], and correctly identifies the difficulty of extending these methods to more complex models like MHMMs. The connection to existing MHMM learning techniques, primarily EM [4], is well-established. The authors acknowledge the computational burden of EM and motivate their approach effectively. 

**Strengths:** The primary strength lies in the innovative application of spectral techniques to address the permutation problem in MoM-based MHMM learning. The theoretical justification, particularly Lemmas 1 and 2, provides a solid foundation for the proposed method. The experimental validation on both synthetic and real data (handwritten character trajectories [8]) demonstrates the feasibility and potential benefits of the approach, particularly in terms of computational speed. The inclusion of a real-world dataset and comparison to EM, even with different initialization strategies, adds practical relevance.

**Weaknesses:** The paper suffers from some clarity issues. The explanation of the depermutation algorithm using spectral properties (Section 3.2) is dense and requires careful reading. The connection between the spectral longevity (Definition 3) and practical cluster identification could be more clearly articulated. While the experiments demonstrate speed advantages, the accuracy gains over EM are not consistently significant, and the impact of the polynomial feature transformation on the real data experiment isn’t fully discussed. The conjecture regarding error bounds (Conjecture 1) feels somewhat unsupported without further analysis.  Furthermore, the related work section could benefit from a more thorough discussion of recent advances in spectral methods for latent variable models.

**Originality:** The combination of MoM, spectral learning, and MHMMs represents a novel contribution. While spectral methods have been applied to HMMs [3], their use to resolve permutation ambiguities in MoM-based MHMM learning is a new and interesting approach.

**Significance:** The potential for faster MHMM learning, especially with large datasets, is significant. If the method can be further refined to achieve consistently higher accuracy, it could become a valuable tool for applications in areas like speech recognition, bioinformatics, and time series analysis.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to MHMM learning with potential for computational efficiency.
*   Solid theoretical foundation with clear mathematical justifications.
*   Demonstrated feasibility on both synthetic and real data.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Clarity issues in the explanation of the spectral depermutation algorithm.
*   Accuracy gains over EM are not consistently significant.
*   Conjecture lacks sufficient support.




**Overall Assessment:** I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper presents a novel and potentially significant contribution to the field. Addressing the clarity issues and providing further analysis to support the conjecture would strengthen the paper considerably.
