- Proposes a MoM-based learning approach for MHMMs that uses spectral properties of a global block-diagonal transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity and learn parameters efficiently.  
- Validated approach on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Builds on spectral HMM learning and MoM latent-variable algorithms, extending them to MHMMs for sequence clustering.  
- Novel contribution: extends familiar spectral/MoM techniques to MHMMs with analysis of the global transition matrix.  
- Strength: computationally efficient and scalable to large datasets.  
- Strength: thorough spectral analysis crucial for resolving permutation ambiguity.  
- Strength: experimental results demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Weakness: assumes the same number of hidden states for each HMM, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks a comprehensive sample complexity analysis for the algorithm.  
- Weakness: does not compare against other state-of-the-art MHMM learning methods.  
- Quality: technically sound, well-organized, and supported by theory and experiments.  
- Clarity: well-written with consistent notation, though some spectral technicalities require careful reading.  
- Originality: offers a unique combination of spectral/MoM techniques applied to MHMMs.  
- Significance: addresses an important, scalable approach to MHMM learning and provides new insights on global transition matrix spectra.  
- Pro arguments: novelty, spectral analysis, and empirical validation favor acceptance.  
- Con arguments: equal-state assumption, missing sample complexity analysis, and lack of baseline comparisons argue against acceptance.  
- Recommendations: provide sample complexity analysis, compare with state-of-the-art methods, and relax the equal-hidden-state assumption.
