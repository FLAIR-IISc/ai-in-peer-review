- Proposes a spectral (method-of-moments) algorithm that casts an MHMM as a single block-diagonal HMM and uses spectral HMM learning plus a de-permutation step to recover component HMMs and K.
- Key technical idea: in noiseless case the global transition has K eigenvalues equal to 1, producing a block structure in powers of the transition matrix exploited for de-permutation.
- In noisy settings, a rank-K reconstruction from top eigenvalues is used to approximately recover the block structure.
- Introduces a “spectral longevity” heuristic that selects K by tracking dominance of largest eigenvalues as powers increase.
- Builds on prior spectral HMM and latent-variable work; the de-permutation/K-selection angle for MHMMs appears novel.
- Strength: elegant reduction of MHMM learning to HMM learning with a specific de-permutation remedy.
- Strength: clear spectral intuition and correct noiseless lemmas supporting the de-permutation idea.
- Strength: computationally appealing — once moments are computed, estimation cost does not scale with number or length of sequences, unlike EM.
- Strength: practical utility as an EM initializer shown on a small real-data task.
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees incomplete — de-permutation and K selection claims are conjectural or rely on borrowed bounds; no end-to-end finite-sample identifiability.
- Weakness: method depends on strong spectral conditions and gaps; sensitivity to slowly mixing chains or eigenvalues near 1 is not quantified.
- Weakness: requires observation dimension L > MK; real-data used cubic feature expansion to meet this, which may distort data and limits applicability.
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation — lacks thorough parameter recovery metrics, robustness checks, sensitivity analyses, and K-selection experiments.
- Weakness: algorithmic/hyperparameter choices (exponent range e, γ, clustering details, normalization) are under-specified, hindering reproducibility and stability assessment.
- Quality: noiseless technical results are sound, but noisy-case justification is partial and no high-probability recovery guarantees are given.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and intuitions clear, but implementation and experimental details are vague or missing.
- Originality: de-permutation approach and spectral longevity criterion are novel contributions relative to prior spectral HMM/mixture work.
- Significance: potentially valuable if robustness and theoretical guarantees are established, but current strong assumptions and limited validation reduce immediate impact.
- Suggestions: provide finite-sample/high-probability guarantees for de-permutation, analyze/select e and γ, evaluate K selection across varied regimes, report parameter recovery and robustness, compare to alternative methods, and give runtime breakdowns.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually elegant, computational advantages over EM, useful as EM initializer, and solid noiseless spectral rationale.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of rigorous noisy-case guarantees, sensitivity to spectral gaps and L > MK constraint, and weak/small-scale experiments.
- Overall assessment: borderline reject — interesting and potentially impactful idea, but needs stronger analysis and broader empirical validation.
