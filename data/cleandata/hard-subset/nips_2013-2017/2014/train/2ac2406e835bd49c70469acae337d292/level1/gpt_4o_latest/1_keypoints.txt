- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments algorithm for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) that targets EM's computational inefficiency on large datasets.  
- Leverages spectral properties of a global block-diagonal transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity in MoM-based MHMM learning.  
- Represents an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling application of existing spectral learning techniques.  
- Addresses the de-permutation problem in noisy settings, a gap not adequately handled in prior work.  
- Validated on synthetic and real-world (UCI handwritten character) datasets, showing computational efficiency and competitive accuracy versus EM.  
- Builds on prior MoM spectral learning literature and extends applicability to more complex latent-variable models.  
- Technical novelty: combines MoM with spectral methods to overcome a major computational bottleneck in EM.  
- Theoretical contributions: formalizes equivalence of MHMMs to block-diagonal HMMs and analyzes spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Practical utility: algorithm is scalable and reproducible, with a clear, step-by-step Algorithm 1.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: comparisons confined to synthetic data and a single real dataset, needing broader benchmarks.  
- Reliance on an unproven Conjecture 1 undermines some theoretical rigor and requires further validation.  
- Occasional underperformance in clustering accuracy relative to EM, especially in low-data regimes, indicating robustness issues.  
- Some sections (spectral properties, noise handling) are dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with spectral methods.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing empirical breadth, conjecture validation, and clarity improvements.
