**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors leverage the spectral properties of a global transition matrix to resolve the permutation ambiguity in the estimation process, allowing for efficient learning of MHMM parameters. The proposed algorithm is computationally cheaper than traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) approaches and demonstrates comparable accuracy in experiments.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon recent work on MoM-based algorithms for learning latent variable models [1, 2, 3]. The authors extend these ideas to MHMMs, which are useful models for clustering sequences. The proposed approach is related to spectral learning methods for HMMs [1, 2], but addresses the specific challenges of MHMMs, such as permutation ambiguity.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-supported theoretical analysis of their approach. The claims are well-supported by experimental results on synthetic and real data. The authors are careful to discuss the strengths and weaknesses of their approach, including the limitations of the spectral conditions required for estimating the number of clusters.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the background and motivation for their work, making it accessible to readers familiar with HMMs and MoM-based learning approaches. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical derivations are clear and well-explained.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to learning MHMMs, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of spectral properties to resolve permutation ambiguity is a new idea, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in experiments.

**Significance**

The results are important, as they provide a computationally efficient approach to learning MHMMs, which are useful models in many applications. The paper demonstrates the potential of MoM-based learning approaches for large-scale data sets, which is a significant advancement in the field. The authors also provide a clear direction for future work, including the application of their approach to other hierarchical latent variable models.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to learning MHMMs.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and experimental evaluation of their approach.
* The results are important and have significant implications for large-scale data sets.

Con:

* The paper assumes a specific structure for the transition matrix, which may not always hold in practice.
* The spectral conditions required for estimating the number of clusters may be difficult to satisfy in some cases.
* The authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison with other approaches, such as variational inference methods.

**Minor Comments and Suggestions**

* The authors may want to consider providing more details on the computational complexity of their approach, including the time and memory requirements.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the results, such as plots of the estimated transition matrices and emission probabilities.
