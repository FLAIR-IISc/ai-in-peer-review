## Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"

This paper proposes a method of moments (MoM) based learning algorithm for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage the block-diagonal structure of the global transition matrix of an MHMM to overcome the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation. The authors demonstrate the feasibility of this approach, including a method to estimate the number of HMMs, and validate it on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Summary & Related Work:** The paper correctly identifies the computational burden of traditional EM-based MHMM learning, particularly for large datasets. MoM offers a computationally efficient alternative, but its application to MHMMs is hindered by permutation ambiguity. This work builds upon prior MoM approaches for HMMs [3] and mixture models [1, 2], extending them to the more complex MHMM framework. The connection to spectral learning for HMMs [3] is clearly established, providing a foundation for resolving the permutation issue by exploiting spectral properties. The authors appropriately contextualize the problem within the applications of MHMMs for sequence clustering [4, 5, 6, 7].

**Quality:** The technical approach appears sound, and the core ideas are well-motivated. The derivation of the equivalence between an MHMM and a specific HMM structure (Lemma 1) is correct and crucial to the algorithm. The proposed method for de-permutation based on spectral analysis seems plausible, though the reliance on Conjecture 1 regarding noise levels is a potential weakness. The experimental results provide some evidence of the method's efficacy, but the analysis could be more comprehensive (see weaknesses). The paper is not merely a position paper, as it provides a concrete algorithm and evaluation. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the dependence on spectral properties and the need for sufficient data, demonstrating careful consideration of weaknesses.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is reasonably clear, although familiarity with linear algebra and probabilistic models is assumed. The explanation of the spectral properties and de-permutation process could benefit from more intuition or visual aids. The algorithm is summarized clearly in Algorithm 1. The inclusion of equations enhances clarity, but some could be simplified for broader accessibility. The connection between the spectral longevity concept (Definition 3) and the practical determination of K is somewhat unclear and would benefit from further elaboration.

**Originality:** The application of spectral techniques to resolve permutation ambiguity in MoM-based MHMM learning appears novel. While MoM and spectral learning for HMMs are established areas, their combination to address the specific challenges of MHMMs represents a meaningful contribution. The spectral longevity concept (Definition 3) adds another layer of originality to the approach. However, the originality is somewhat incremental, building upon existing work rather than presenting a fundamentally new paradigm.

**Significance:** The potential impact of this work is moderate. A computationally efficient learning algorithm for MHMMs could be beneficial in applications dealing with large sequence datasets. The proposed method offers a potential alternative to EM, particularly when initialization is problematic. The use of spectral properties to address the permutation ambiguity is a potentially generalizable technique. However, the experimental results do not demonstrate a substantial performance advantage over existing methods, limiting the immediate impact. The paper presents a valid and potentially useful technique, but a broader range of applications and more compelling results would enhance its significance.



**Strengths:**

*   Addresses a practical problem – computational cost of MHMM learning.
*   Novel application of spectral methods to resolve permutation ambiguity in MoM.
*   Clear mathematical formulation and algorithm description.
*   Demonstration on both synthetic and real datasets.
*   Acknowledgement of limitations and potential future work.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Reliance on Conjecture 1 regarding noise levels is a major concern. A more rigorous analysis of noise robustness is needed.
*   Experimental evaluation is limited. More extensive comparisons with state-of-the-art MHMM learning algorithms (including different EM initialization strategies) are required.
*   The real data experiment focuses on a specific dataset (handwritten characters) and feature transformation, limiting generalizability.
*   The explanation of spectral longevity and its connection to K estimation could be improved.
*   The performance gain over EM (especially with good initialization) is not clearly established.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a relevant problem.
*   The core idea of leveraging spectral properties to address permutation ambiguity is insightful.
*   The experimental results, while limited, provide preliminary evidence of the method's feasibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The reliance on Conjecture 1 weakens the theoretical foundation.
*   The experimental evaluation is not comprehensive enough to convincingly demonstrate the method’s advantages.
*   The impact of the work is moderate, and the results do not represent a significant breakthrough.



**Overall Assessment:**

I rate this paper as **weak accept**. While the technical contributions are valuable and the idea is interesting, the paper's impact is limited by the reliance on a conjecture and the lack of a compelling experimental evaluation. Addressing these weaknesses would significantly strengthen the paper. A rebuttal addressing the concerns raised, especially regarding the conjecture and experimental validation, would be highly beneficial.
