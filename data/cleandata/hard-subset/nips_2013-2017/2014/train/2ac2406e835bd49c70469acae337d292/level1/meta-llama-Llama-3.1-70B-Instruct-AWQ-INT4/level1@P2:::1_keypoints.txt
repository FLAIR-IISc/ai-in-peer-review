- Proposed method: cast MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition, apply Method of Moments to learn parameters up to permutation, then use spectral properties to de-permute and estimate cluster count under spectral conditions.  
- Builds on prior spectral learning for HMMs and MoM latent-variable algorithms, extending them to MHMMs for sequence clustering.  
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and experiments (synthetic and real data) support the claims.  
- Assumes each local transition matrix has only one eigenvalue of magnitude 1; the paper should discuss relaxing this assumption.  
- Relies on Conjecture 1 for the low-rank reconstruction error bound; needs a formal proof or stronger justification.  
- Well-written with clear mathematical derivations and organized experimental presentation.  
- Suggestion: provide more detailed explanation of the global transition matrix’s spectral properties and their role in de-permutation.  
- Suggestion: add intuition on why the proposed spectral approach effectively resolves permutation ambiguity.  
- Originality: novel and innovative use of spectral properties to de-permute MoM-learned MHMMs.  
- Significance: computationally more efficient than EM, with potential impact on sequence clustering and related applications.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel approach, strong experimental results, contribution to MoM methods.  
- Cons for acceptance: dependence on an unproven conjecture and a potentially restrictive eigenvalue assumption.  
- Overall rating: 9/10, with recommended improvements on theoretical justification and explanatory detail.
