This paper introduces a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The approach leverages an existing spectral method for hidden Markov models (HMMs) – Anandkumar et al., 2012 – by demonstrating that an MHMM can be framed as a specific instance of an HMM. A key contribution is a stabilization technique to address parameter permutation issues, achieved by assuming a single unit eigenvalue within each HMM’s transition matrix.

However, the presentation contains some areas for improvement. The notation requires clarification, and the authors should more thoroughly acknowledge related work, including references such as Chaganty and Liang (2013), which explores EM initialization using spectral estimates. The experimental evaluation is currently limited; presenting results in tabular format, rather than the graphical representation in Figure 4, would significantly enhance clarity.

Despite these points, the proposed method for mitigating parameter permutation is both interesting and intuitively sound. Furthermore, the analysis considering estimation noise, and the resulting use of eigenvalues in Equation (7), is a noteworthy aspect of the algorithm.

Specific comments:

- Corollary 1’s terms are initially unclear. Defining 1J^T (a row vector of ones with length J) and providing further detail on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (specifically, its JK x JK block diagonal form with identical columns within each J x J block) would be beneficial.
- Consistently defining each vector and matrix, including its dimensions, upon first introduction will greatly improve readability.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret; replacing it with tables is strongly recommended. The poor performance of EM in Table 1 is also surprising, as it typically surpasses spectral methods given sufficient iterations, even with random initialization. Reporting the best achieved accuracy for EM, rather than the average, is standard practice and should be included.
- For consistency, adopting the notation established in Hsu et al. (2009) – using T for transition matrices and n, m for the number of observations and hidden states, respectively – would be helpful.

In summary, this paper presents an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models, built upon an existing spectral algorithm for HMMs and augmented with a novel stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation.
