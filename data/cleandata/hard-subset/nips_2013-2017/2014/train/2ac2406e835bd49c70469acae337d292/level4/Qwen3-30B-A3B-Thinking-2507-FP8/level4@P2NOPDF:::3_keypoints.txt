- Introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs based on Hsu et al.; core algorithm is a straightforward adaptation.  
- Recovered transition matrix suffers from permutation ambiguity, conflating component identities.  
- Proposes a de-permutation procedure that appears empirically effective but lacks theoretical guarantees and robustness analysis.  
- Manuscript contains grammatical errors and typos (needs careful proofreading).  
- Needs stronger contextualization in related work, particularly Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013), and connections to de-permutation issues in other mixture models.  
- De-permutation step lacks rigorous justification: no analysis of success conditions, scaling with K/d, or dependence on signal-to-noise ratio.  
- Experiments lack ablation studies and robustness analyses demonstrating the necessity and limits of de-permutation.  
- Computational complexity of the de-permutation step is not clarified or compared to the core spectral algorithm.  
- Writing is imprecise about the permutation ambiguity; should explicitly define the permutation model (e.g., recovers Π T Π^T for unknown Π).  
- Lacks a clear problem motivation and application examples; overall needs substantial revision (proofreading, related work, theory, experiments, clarity) and currently lacks sufficient novelty/technical depth for acceptance.
