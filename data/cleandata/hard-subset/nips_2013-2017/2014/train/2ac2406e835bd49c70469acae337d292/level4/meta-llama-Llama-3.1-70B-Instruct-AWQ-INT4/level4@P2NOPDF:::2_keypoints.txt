- Proposes a novel spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of HMMs by casting an MHMM as a special-case HMM and applying the Anandkumar et al. (2012) spectral method.
- Introduces a stabilization-based step to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue equal to 1.
- Contribution: an intuitive and interesting scheme to mitigate parameter permutation in spectral learning.
- Contribution: analysis of estimation noise and an intriguing use of eigenvalues in the algorithm (Equation (7)).
- Notation is sometimes unclear; authors should define each vector/matrix and its dimensions at first use.
- Authors should clarify terms in Corollary 1 (e.g., 1_J^T) and elaborate the structure of lim_{e->∞} \bar{A}^e (the JK×JK block-diagonal form).
- Paper lacks adequate context and literature references—e.g., acknowledge prior work on initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013).
- Experimental results are basic and presentation is poor (Figure 4 hard to read); results should be reported in tables rather than only graphs.
- EM results in Table 1 are surprising; standard practice is to report best (not average) EM accuracy given multiple restarts/iterations.
- Conform to established notation (use T for transition matrices, n/m for observation/hidden-state counts) as in Hsu et al. (2009) to improve readability.
- Overall assessment: the algorithm (spectral HMM + stabilization) is a valuable contribution but requires revisions in notation, related work, experiments, and presentation to realize its impact.
