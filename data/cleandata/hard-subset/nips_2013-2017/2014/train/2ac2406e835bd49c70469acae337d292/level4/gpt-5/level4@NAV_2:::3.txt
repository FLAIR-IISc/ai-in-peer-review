The paper proposes a spectral, method-of-moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs. Absent the permutation issue, the method would be a straightforward adaptation of the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang spectral HMM algorithm; however, the recovered global transition matrix is arbitrarily permuted, interleaving states from different components. The authors introduce a de-permutation procedure based on the spectral structure of the global transition matrix, which appears to work reasonably well in practice.

There are occasional minor numerical/typographical issues; for example:
“For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.”
Please proofread more carefully.

The paper could better position the contribution within related work, e.g., by discussing:
Chaganty and Liang, Spectral Experts for Estimating Mixtures of Linear Regressions, ICML 2013.

Overall, the paper presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs and addresses the central problem of how to de-permute the estimated parameters.
