- Proposes a spectral (method-of-moments) algorithm for mixtures of HMMs by recasting an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Leverages existing spectral HMM estimators (e.g., Anandkumar et al., 2012) once the MHMM is cast as a single HMM.  
- Introduces a stabilization step to resolve label permutations, assuming each component HMM has exactly one eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Analyzes recovery of the de-permutation under estimation noise and derives an eigenvalue-based criterion (Equation (7)).  
- The permutation-resolution approach is intuitive, novel in this context, and potentially practically useful.  
- Notational issues: many symbols and dimensions are not defined at first use, hurting readability.  
- Related work coverage is weak; should cite and discuss spectral initialization of EM (Chaganty & Liang, 2013) and other relevant literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and Figure 4 is hard to interpret; recommend replacing plots with tables.  
- EM results in Table 1 look unexpectedly weak; report best results over multiple random restarts (not just averages).  
- Clarify Corollary 1: explicitly define 1_J^T and describe the block-structured limit matrix with identical columns in each J×J block.  
- Minor notation suggestion: align with Hsu et al. (2009) by using T for transition matrices and n, m for observation/hidden state counts.  
- Overall pipeline summary: apply off-the-shelf spectral HMM estimator, then apply the new stabilization/de-permutation step.
