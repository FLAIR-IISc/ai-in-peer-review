- Paper proposes spectral learning for HMM mixtures followed by a permutation recovery to reveal block-diagonal structure.  
- Well-written with solid theoretical contributions, but experimental validation is weak.  
- Method first estimates a full HMM then recovers sparsity via permutation, which may be impractical with limited data or high dimensions.  
- Authors should state conditions where the algorithm is effective versus where it will struggle.  
- Spectral learning does not enforce sparsity; dense estimates may mask block-diagonal structure and be sensitive to noise.  
- Need to consider methods to encourage sparsity or analyze sensitivity to noise.  
- Experiment 4.1 is unrealistic: permuting a transition matrix with Dirichlet noise does not capture learning from observations.  
- Evaluation should sample observations from mixture HMMs and attempt full parameter recovery to reflect observational noise and limited data.  
- Section 4.2 lacks crucial details (transition and observation matrix parameters); provide these (e.g., in an appendix).  
- Table 1 is puzzling: EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes worsens performance—this must be investigated and explained.  
- Minor: typo in proof—Lambda subscript should be K not 2.  
- Minor: figure font sizes are too small and hard to read.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical idea but reservations about practical applicability; requires stronger experimental validation on realistic datasets.
