This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The authors demonstrate that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied directly by formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM. They propose a stabilization-based solution to address the issue of parameter permutation by introducing the assumption that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

However, there are some issues with notation that need to be addressed. The authors should provide more context and awareness of related work by including relevant references. For instance, initializing EM with spectral estimates has already been explored in Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experimental evaluation is also quite basic, and I strongly recommend presenting the results in a table format instead of the graphical format used in Figure 4.

That being said, the proposed scheme for addressing the problem of parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) in the algorithm.

Comments:

* I was initially confused by the terms in Corollary 1. It would be helpful to define 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborate on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (that it's a JK x JK block diagonal matrix where the columns within each J x J block are the same).
* Generally, it would be beneficial to define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it is first introduced. This would greatly improve the presentation.
* I find Figure 4 very hard to read; please replace it with tables. Additionally, I am not sure why EM is performing so poorly in Table 1. Typically, even with random initialization, EM beats spectral methods with enough iterations, despite the local optima problem. A standard practice is to report the best (not average) accuracy of EM; please include this result.
* A minor point: it might help readers if the paper conformed to the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009). Specifically, using T for transition matrices (instead of A) and n, m for numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J).

The paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models, which consists of an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models followed by a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation.
