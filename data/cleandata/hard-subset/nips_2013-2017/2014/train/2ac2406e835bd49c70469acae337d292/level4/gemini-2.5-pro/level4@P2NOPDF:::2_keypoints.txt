- Presents a spectral algorithm for MHMMs by formulating an MHMM as a single larger HMM to apply Anandkumar et al. (2012).
- Main contribution is a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation by assuming each component transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue 1 (stationary distribution).
- Stabilization approach is elegant and intuitive; theoretical analysis accounts for estimation noise and uses eigenvalues (e.g., Eq. (7)).
- Notation is inconsistent and many symbols/dimensions are undefined, which harms clarity and readability.
- Corollary 1 is hard to follow; terms like 1_J^T should be defined and the block-diagonal limit matrix structure and its link to stationary distributions should be explained.
- Related work discussion is insufficient — prior use of spectral estimates to initialize EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, 2013) is not acknowledged.
- Recommend adopting standard spectral-learning notation (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009: use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden states) for accessibility.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and presentation is poor; Figure 4 is hard to interpret and should be replaced by a table for quantitative comparison.
- EM results in Table 1 look unexpectedly weak; authors should clarify EM setup and report best-run results over multiple random initializations (not just averages).
- Strengths in algorithmic idea and noise-aware theory, but exposition, literature context, and empirical validation need significant improvement.
