- Proposes spectral learning for MHMMs by representing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.  
- Theoretical contribution is sound: establishes MHMM-HMM equivalence and provides lemmas/conjecture to resolve permutation ambiguity.  
- Mathematical formulations are clear and the exposition is well-organized and accessible.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and handwritten trajectories show ability to recover clusters/block structure and computational advantages over EM.  
- Paper does not clarify when this method is practically preferable to EM, given it first learns a full HMM despite MHMMs’ sparse motivation.  
- Does not analyze whether the learned transition matrix remains (approximately) sparse, which is critical for MHMM utility.  
- Depermutation experiment tests a permuted matrix rather than learning from data generated by an MHMM, misaligning the evaluation with the learning problem.  
- Lacks details on MHMM parameters (transition/observation matrices) in experiments, preventing assessment of problem difficulty.  
- Table 1 result where EM initialized with spectral learning underperforms spectral alone is unexplained and concerning.  
- Real-data evaluation is limited to one dataset with specific choices and lacks broader practical analysis.  
- Theoretical results are technically strong, but experimental evaluation does not fully support the claimed learning pipeline.  
- Writing, clarity, and connection to existing spectral HMM methods are excellent; the extension to MHMMs is novel.  
- Potential significance for large-scale MHMM learning exists, but practical utility is limited by needing to learn the full HMM first.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline acceptance with strong revision requested to address practical applicability, sparsity, experimental realism, and missing details.
