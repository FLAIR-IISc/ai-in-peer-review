- Proposes applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm (Anandkumar et al., 2012) to MHMMs by treating an MHMM as a special-case HMM.  
- Introduces a stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides analysis for estimation noise and uses eigenvalue behavior (Eq. (7)) in the algorithm, which is interesting.  
- Notation is inconsistent/confusing; authors should define each vector/matrix and its dimensions at first use.  
- Corollary 1 needs clarification (e.g., define 1_J^T and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e).  
- Related work coverage is incomplete; cite and discuss methods like EM initialization from spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013).  
- Experimental results are basic and presentation (Figure 4) is hard to read; replace graphs with tables.  
- Report EM performance more appropriately (include best-case accuracy, not just average), since EM can outperform spectral methods with sufficient runs/iterations.  
- Table 1 shows surprisingly poor EM results that need explanation or re-evaluation.  
- Recommend conforming to standard HMM notation (use T for transition matrices, n/m for observation/hidden states as in Hsu et al. (2009)).  
- Overall: algorithm = off-the-shelf spectral HMM learning + novel stabilization to undo parameter permutation; idea is intuitive but needs clarity, better experiments, and stronger literature positioning.
