This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The core idea is to leverage an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models (Anandkumar et al., 2012) by formulating the MHMM as a specific instance of an HMM – one with a block-diagonal transition matrix. A significant contribution lies in addressing the parameter permutation ambiguity inherent in this approach, proposing a stabilization technique based on the spectral properties of the transition matrix and an assumption about the eigenvalues of individual HMM transition matrices.

The paper effectively positions itself within the context of learning latent variable models, noting the recent popularity of Method of Moments (MoM) approaches (references [1-3]) and contrasting them with the computational challenges of Expectation-Maximization (EM) for MHMMs (reference [4]). The motivation for exploring MoM-based approaches for MHMMs, given the limitations of EM, is well articulated.  The work builds upon the HMM spectral learning literature, particularly Hsu et al. (2009), though it isn’t explicitly positioned as such initially. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Approach to Permutation Problem:** The method for resolving the permutation ambiguity using spectral properties is insightful and offers a potentially efficient solution to a core challenge in MHMM learning. The analysis considering estimation noise and the introduction of Equation (7) for eigenvalue-based cluster identification are particularly promising.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The proposed algorithm promises computational advantages over traditional EM-based methods, particularly for large datasets, as highlighted in the introduction and validated through experiments.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The lemmas establishing the equivalence between MHMM and a specific HMM structure, and characterizing the spectral properties of the transition matrix, provide a solid theoretical grounding for the algorithm.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The experiments, while basic, demonstrate the feasibility and competitiveness of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of speed.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity of Notation:** The paper suffers from inconsistent and sometimes unclear notation. The introduction of vectors and matrices often lacks explicit dimension definitions, making it challenging to follow the derivations. For example, the explanation surrounding Corollary 1 requires additional context regarding `1J^T` and the structure of `lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e`.
*   **Limited Related Work Discussion:** The authors need to demonstrate greater awareness of related work. While they cite foundational papers, they miss relevant contributions.  For example, the idea of initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013) is directly relevant and should be discussed. Similarly, mentioning work on spectral clustering applied to sequence data might provide useful context.
*   **Presentation of Experimental Results:**  Figure 4 is difficult to interpret. Replacing it with a table would significantly improve clarity and allow for a more precise comparison of results. Table 1's EM results are surprisingly low and warrant further investigation – the best (rather than average) EM accuracy should be reported, alongside a discussion of the EM parameter tuning.
*   **Notation Consistency:** The paper would benefit from adopting more standard notation common in the HMM literature (e.g., using 'T' for transition matrices, 'n' and 'm' for observation and hidden state numbers, aligning with Hsu et al., 2009). This will facilitate readability and comparison with existing work.
*   **Conjecture Validity:** While the conjecture regarding spectral longevity seems plausible, a more rigorous justification or preliminary proof sketch would strengthen the argument.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant problem in MHMM learning – the computational cost of EM and the permutation ambiguity – with a novel and potentially efficient solution.
*   The theoretical analysis provides a strong foundation for the proposed algorithm.
*   The experiments, although preliminary, demonstrate the feasibility and speed of the approach.
*   The exploration of the impact of noise on eigenvalue-based cluster identification is a valuable contribution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of clarity in notation and the limited discussion of related work detract from the paper's overall quality.
*   The presentation of experimental results hinders a thorough evaluation of the algorithm's performance.
*   The reliance on a conjecture without more substantial justification weakens the theoretical argument.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of MHMM learning. While the theoretical foundations are solid and the proposed approach is promising, the paper suffers from issues with clarity, notation, and presentation.  I believe that addressing these weaknesses, particularly by improving the clarity of the notation, expanding the related work discussion, and providing more informative experimental results, would significantly strengthen the paper. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **weak accept** with a request for major revisions. The authors should focus on improving the clarity of the presentation, expanding the related work section, and providing more compelling experimental results.
