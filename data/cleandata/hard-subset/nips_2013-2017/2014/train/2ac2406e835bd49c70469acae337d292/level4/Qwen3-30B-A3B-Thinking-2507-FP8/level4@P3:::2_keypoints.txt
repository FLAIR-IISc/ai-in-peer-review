- Proposes a spectral MoM algorithm for MHMMs by viewing an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Tackles permutation ambiguity via a novel stabilization/depermutation procedure exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Builds on Anandkumar et al.'s spectral HMM framework and extends it to the MHMM setting.  
- Theoretical development is sound with clear lemmas connecting MHMMs to block-diagonal HMMs and analyzing permutation issues.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data show comparable accuracy to EM with substantially reduced computation time.  
- Noise analysis (Section 3.2.2) is conjectural but offers useful insights into practical algorithm behavior.  
- The depermutation technique is original and addresses a longstanding barrier to applying MoM to MHMMs.  
- The approach is significant for scalable sequence-clustering and serves as a good initialization for EM.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and readable but would benefit from clearer notation and definitions.  
- Notation is inconsistent with standard HMM conventions (e.g., L, J vs. typical symbols), causing confusion—should adopt standard symbols and state dimensions on first use.  
- Corollary 1 and lim_{e→∞} Ā^e need clearer, explicit definitions (e.g., 1_J^T as a J-length row vector; block structure and stationary distributions).  
- Figure 4 is hard to interpret graphically—report accuracy and speed in tables for clarity; Table 1 should include best EM accuracy over runs.  
- Should better acknowledge related work, notably Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013) on spectral initialization for mixture models.  
- Needs more detailed discussion/comparison of computational complexity and the dataset scales where spectral methods outperform EM.  
- Recommendation: solid, novel contribution; accept with minor revisions to notation, result presentation, and literature engagement.  
- Pros summary: novel solution to permutation ambiguity; theoretically grounded; computationally advantageous; validated on synthetic and real data; useful EM initializer.  
- Cons summary: inconsistent notation; confusing graphical presentation of results; insufficient engagement with related spectral-initialization work; limited complexity-scaling discussion.
