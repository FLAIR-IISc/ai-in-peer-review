The paper presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs. The core algorithm is essentially a straightforward variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang approach. As is typical with spectral methods, the recovered transition matrices are only identifiable up to permutation; in the mixture setting, this can mix components. The authors address this by proposing a de-permutation procedure to align the components, which appears to work reasonably well in practice.

Writing and presentation could be improved. There are several typos and grammatical errors. For example: “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.” Please proofread the paper carefully.

The paper would also benefit from a clearer placement within the broader literature on spectral learning and mixtures. In particular, drawing connections to related work such as Chaganty and Liang (ICML 2013), “Spectral Experts for Estimating Mixtures of Linear Regressions,” would help contextualize the contribution and clarify novelty relative to prior spectral approaches.

Overall, the work tackles the key issue of how to de-permute spectral estimates in mixtures of HMMs. While the underlying spectral component closely follows Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang, the proposed alignment step is a useful addition. With more thorough proofreading and stronger contextualization within related work, the paper’s contribution would be clearer and more compelling.
