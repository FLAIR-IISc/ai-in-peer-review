- Main contribution: a depermutation algorithm that resolves arbitrary permutations in the transition matrix recovered by spectral learning for mixtures of HMMs.  
- Approach: represent a mixture of HMMs as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and apply a spectral algorithm to recover individual HMM parameters.  
- Depermutation is crucial because permutation mixing prevents recovery of individual components from the standard spectral output.  
- Theoretical work: thorough spectral analysis of the transition matrix that motivates an efficient depermutation algorithm.  
- Experimental results: the spectral method is much faster than EM and achieves comparable accuracy.  
- Practical impact: effective on a real-world dataset and useful as an initialization scheme for EM.  
- Writing and organization: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and balances theory and experiments.  
- Weakness: insufficient connection to related work (e.g., Chaganty and Liang on spectral experts) and broader literature context.  
- Weakness: depermutation algorithm lacks detailed mathematical analysis and performance guarantees.  
- Weakness: paper needs more careful proofreading to remove typos and minor errors.  
- NIPS criteria: scores well on quality, clarity, and significance but is less strong on originality beyond the depermutation idea.  
- Recommendation: overall recommendation to accept, with listed improvements to address before final version.
