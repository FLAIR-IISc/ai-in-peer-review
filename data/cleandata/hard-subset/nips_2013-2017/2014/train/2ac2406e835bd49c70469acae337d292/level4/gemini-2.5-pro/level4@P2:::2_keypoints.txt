- Proposes a spectral method-of-moments algorithm for learning MHMMs by viewing an MHMM as a single large HMM with a KM-state block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Directly applying spectral HMM learning produces a permutation ambiguity that prevents recovery of individual component HMM parameters.  
- Main contribution: a procedure to resolve the permutation ambiguity by leveraging spectral properties (K eigenvalues ≈1), using matrix powers or low-rank reconstruction and clustering columns to de-permute parameters.  
- Provides analysis for the noiseless case and a partial treatment (Conjecture 1, Lemma 3) for the noisy case, plus a heuristic for estimating the number of components K.  
- Demonstrates method effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: original, elegant, and significant solution that makes spectral learning applicable to MHMMs and is a computationally attractive alternative to EM.  
- Strength: sound theoretical insight connecting block-diagonal structure to spectral properties and introducing a “spectral longevity” metric for model selection.  
- Strength: computationally efficient—core computations (matrix factorizations) scale independently of the number of sequences.  
- Weakness: presentation and notation are unclear; several terms and dimensions are not explicitly defined and should follow standard spectral-HMM notation.  
- Weakness: insufficient positioning relative to prior work—needs explicit discussion of spectral methods as EM initialization (e.g., Chaganty & Liang) and how this work differs.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could be more thorough—Figure 4 is hard to read, results should be tabulated, and EM baselines should report best-of-many random initializations.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification for the noisy case relies on an adapted conjecture without formal proof and should be more carefully framed or acknowledged as a limitation.  
- Summary pro-acceptance: novel, scalable, well-motivated, and practical solution to a key problem in spectral MHMM learning.  
- Summary contra-acceptance: clarity, related-work discussion, and experimental rigor need improvement.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance conditional on revisions to presentation, related-work positioning, and experimental/theoretical clarifications.
