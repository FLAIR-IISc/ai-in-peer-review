- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments algorithm for MHMMs by treating the mixture as one large HMM with a block-diagonal transition and resolving permutation ambiguity spectrally.  
- Novelty/significance: non-iterative spectral approach is original and could offer major computational benefits for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Technical soundness: the MHMM-to-HMM equivalence and the depermutation via eigenvalues near 1 are clever and theoretically justified in the noiseless case.  
- Clarity: the paper is generally well-written and logically organized.  
- Practicality/scalability concern: learning a full MK x MK HMM may negate MHMM sparsity benefits and be statistically/computationally challenging for large K or M.  
- Assumption concern: no guarantee that the spectral HMM estimator from finite data will be a perturbed permuted block-diagonal matrix, so depermutation may fail in practice.  
- Experimental shortcoming 1: depermutation is tested in isolation by adding noise to a permuted matrix instead of end-to-end on generated sequence data.  
- Experimental shortcoming 2: Section 4.2 lacks key parameter details (emission separability, mixing rates), making results hard to interpret.  
- Experimental shortcoming 3: “EM initialized with Spectral” sometimes underperforms Spectral alone; this unexpected result needs explanation.  
- Minor: probable typo in Lemma 2 subscripts (should be K, not 2).  
- Minor: figures (especially 3 and 4) have text/numbers that are too small to read.  
- Overall: interesting and elegant algorithmic contribution, but practical applicability and end-to-end empirical validation are insufficient; reviewer leans toward rejection pending major revisions.
