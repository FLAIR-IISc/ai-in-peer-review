- Proposes applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm to learn mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating MHMM as a special case of HMM.  
- Main contribution: a stabilization-based solution to the parameter permutation problem, relying on the assumption each component HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Notation is inconsistent and unclear; many variables and matrices (and their dimensions) should be defined when first introduced.  
- Corollary 1 is confusing; explicitly define 1_J^T (row vector of J ones) and explain lim_{e->∞} \bar{A}^e as a JK×JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns in each J×J block.  
- Related work is under-cited; specifically, prior use of spectral estimates to initialize EM (Chaganty & Liang, 2013) should be referenced.  
- Experimental evaluation is basic and should present results in tables (not only graphs) for easier comparison and interpretation.  
- EM performance reported is surprisingly poor and unexplained; authors should clarify why EM fares worse than expected.  
- Report standard EM metrics (e.g., best rather than average accuracy across restarts) to give a fair comparison given local optima issues.  
- Minor notation suggestion: follow Hsu et al. (2009) conventions (use T for transitions and n,m for numbers of observation/hidden states) to improve readability.  
- The stabilization/permutation scheme is interesting and intuitive and is a notable strength of the paper.  
- Analysis accounting for estimation noise is valuable; the use of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) in the algorithm is intriguing.  
- With revisions addressing notation, related work, experimental reporting, and EM comparisons, the paper could make a valuable contribution to MHMM learning.
