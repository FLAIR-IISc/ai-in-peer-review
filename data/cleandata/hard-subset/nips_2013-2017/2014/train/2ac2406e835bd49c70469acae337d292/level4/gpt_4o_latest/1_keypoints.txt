- Paper proposes a spectral algorithm to estimate a single HMM and a method to resolve permutation ambiguity to recover a block-diagonal mixture structure.  
- Well-written manuscript but experimental section lacks sufficient detail and elaboration.  
- Method assumes full HMM parameters can be accurately estimated before depermuting, which seems impractical; authors should state where the algorithm is applicable and where it may fail.  
- Unclear whether the spectral learning yields approximately sparse (block-diagonal) transition matrices in practice, especially under noise; many dense matrices could be close to the target sparse structure.  
- Experiments do not adequately test the effect of observational noise; authors should generate data from mixture HMMs and attempt full parameter recovery to assess realistic performance.  
- Section 4.2 partially addresses concerns but omits crucial details (exact transition/observation parameters); include full specifications (e.g., in an appendix).  
- Table 1 anomaly: EM initialized with spectral sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—needs explanation since EM should typically improve results.  
- Minor typo: subscript of Λ in Section 3.2.1 likely incorrect (should be K, not 2).  
- Figures suffer from excessively small text and numeric labels, making them hard to read.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contributions but practical applicability to real-world datasets is questionable.
