This paper presents a spectral method for learning a mixture of HMMs. The core algorithm is essentially a straightforward variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang approach for HMM learning; the main difference is that the recovered transition matrices are obtained only up to an arbitrary permutation, which mixes the components across the mixture. The authors propose a de-permutation procedure to align the estimates with the true components, and this heuristic appears to work reasonably well in their experiments.

While the problem is relevant and the empirical results suggest the de-permutation step is effective, the methodological contribution beyond the standard spectral framework seems limited to this post-processing. The paper would benefit from a clearer discussion of what is genuinely new, how the proposed de-permutation compares to alternative alignment strategies (e.g., based on clustering or anchor features), and under what conditions it is guaranteed to succeed. If there are theoretical guarantees (e.g., identifiability conditions, sample complexity, or robustness to noise) specific to the mixture setting and the de-permutation procedure, they should be stated more explicitly and contrasted with the guarantees of the base spectral method.

The exposition would also be stronger with improved context and related work. In particular, it would help to connect this work to prior spectral approaches for mixture models, such as:
- Chaganty and Liang (2013), “Spectral Experts for Estimating Mixtures of Linear Regressions” (ICML).
Drawing parallels and contrasts—both algorithmic and in terms of identifiability assumptions—would clarify how the current contribution fits within the broader literature on spectral methods for mixtures.

There are also several minor language issues that hinder readability. Please proofread carefully. For example, the sentence:
“For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.”
is ungrammatical and unclear. Such issues appear in multiple places and should be corrected to improve clarity.

In summary:
- Strength: A practical de-permutation step that appears to effectively resolve the permutation ambiguity arising from the spectral estimation in mixture-of-HMM settings.
- Weakness: Beyond this alignment step, the approach is a relatively trivial adaptation of the standard Hsu–Kakade–Zhang algorithm, with limited novelty articulated. More rigorous theoretical characterization and broader empirical comparisons would strengthen the contribution.
- Suggestions: Expand the related work and positioning (e.g., connections to spectral methods for other mixture models), clarify assumptions and guarantees of the de-permutation algorithm, and thoroughly proofread the paper.

Overall, the paper addresses the key question of how to “de-permute” the results when applying spectral methods to mixtures of HMMs, and the proposed solution seems empirically effective, but the novelty and theoretical grounding should be better articulated.
