The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The proposed algorithm is a variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm, with the exception that the recovered transition matrix is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, mixing the various components. To address this issue, the authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to work reasonably well.

However, there are a few minor errors throughout the paper, such as the typo in the sentence "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." A thorough proofread would help eliminate these errors and improve the overall clarity of the paper.

To provide a clearer understanding of the contributions and novelty of this work, it would be beneficial to place it within the context of existing research. For example, a comparison with related papers, such as Chaganty and Liang's "Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions" (ICML, 2013), would be helpful. This would enable readers to better appreciate the significance of the proposed method and its advancements over previous approaches.

Overall, the authors have made a notable contribution to the field by presenting a spectral method for learning a mixture of HMMs and addressing the key question of how to "de-permute" the results.
