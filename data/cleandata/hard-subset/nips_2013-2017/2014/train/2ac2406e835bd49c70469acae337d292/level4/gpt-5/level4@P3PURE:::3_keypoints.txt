- Presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs with a focus on how to “de-permute” recovered components.  
- Algorithm is essentially a direct variant of the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang (HKZ) spectral method.  
- Key technical issue: the recovered transition matrix can be an arbitrary permutation that mixes components.  
- Main contribution: a de-permutation procedure to resolve component mixing, which appears to work reasonably well.  
- Presentation needs improvement—several typos and minor errors hurt readability (example sentence cited).  
- Needs stronger contextualization in the spectral learning literature and discussion of related work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Overall: addresses a practical de-permutation problem on top of a standard HKZ framework; better links to prior work and cleaner writing would clarify the contribution.
