1. Proposes a spectral algorithm to learn a single HMM and a method to resolve permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal mixture structure.

2. Well-written paper but experimental section needs expansion and more details about setup and results.

3. Method requires accurate estimation of full HMM parameters despite mixtures being sparse—may be impractical for large problems or limited data; authors should specify applicability conditions.

4. Unclear whether the spectral algorithm can guarantee or recover near-sparse (block-diagonal) transition matrices in the presence of noise.

5. Experiments using permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise do not mimic real learning from observed data; should sample observations from mixtures to assess noise and data-limited effects.

6. Section 4.2 is a step forward but lacks crucial details (transition/observation parameter values); include these (e.g., in an appendix).

7. Table 1 shows EM initialized with spectral sometimes worse than spectral alone, which is counterintuitive and needs explanation.

8. Proof typo: bottom-right subscript of Λ in Section 3.2.1 should be K, not 2.

9. Figures have text and numbers that are too small and hard to read.

10. Overall: interesting theoretical results but practical effectiveness on real-world datasets is questionable.
