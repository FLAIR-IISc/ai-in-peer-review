1. Paper proposes learning a mixture of HMMs by first estimating a single larger HMM via spectral methods and then resolving permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal structure.

2. Paper is generally well-written but the experimental section needs much more detail and strengthening.

3. The approach appears to contradict the motivation for HMM mixtures—requiring full dense HMM estimation is computationally prohibitive in the sparse/low-data regimes where mixtures are most useful; authors should clarify practical applicability and limitations.

4. There is no theoretical guarantee provided that spectral algorithms will yield a matrix close to a permuted block-diagonal under realistic noise, raising concern that de-permutation may fail.

5. Experiments in Section 4.1 isolate the de-permutation step by perturbing a permuted matrix rather than performing end-to-end learning from generated sequences, so they do not reflect real estimation error propagation.

6. Experiment in Section 4.2 is closer to end-to-end but omits crucial details about the ground-truth HMM mixture (transition and observation matrices), preventing proper assessment.

7. Table 1 shows EM initialized with the spectral solution sometimes performs worse than the spectral solution alone, which is counter-intuitive and requires explanation.

8. Minor typo: in Section 3.2.1 the subscript of Λ in the bottom-right of the first matrix equation should be K, not 2.

9. Figures have text and numbers that are too small and hard to read.

10. Overall: interesting theoretical contributions but significant reservations remain about practical applicability to real-world datasets due to the issues above.
