- Central idea: Treat an MHMM with K components and M states each as one large MK×MK HMM and use spectral HMM learning, then depermutate the global transition matrix via its spectral structure to recover component HMMs.

- Strength — Originality: Novel, theoretically motivated method for depermuting the global transition matrix using spectral properties.

- Strength — Significance: Offers a faster, non-iterative alternative to EM, potentially valuable for large sequence datasets.

- Strength — Clarity: Paper is mostly well-written and clearly explains the MHMM-to-large-HMM connection.

- Weakness — Practicality of core assumption: Method requires accurately estimating a potentially huge MK×MK transition matrix, which may be infeasible when MHMMs are used to avoid that complexity; scalability regimes need discussion.

- Weakness — Robustness to noise: Unclear whether spectral estimates from finite data will be close enough to permuted block-diagonal form for depermutation to succeed, especially in low-data regimes.

- Weakness — Experimental validation: Experiments are insufficiently realistic; Section 4.1 uses artificially noised matrices instead of estimated transitions, and 4.2 lacks crucial ground-truth MHMM parameter details.

- Weakness — Inconsistent results: Table 1 shows EM initialized with Spectral sometimes performing worse than Spectral alone, which contradicts EM likelihood guarantees and needs explanation or debugging.

- Minor: Figures have very small fonts and need larger text for readability.

- Minor: Possible typo in proof (subscript on bottom-right V matrix appears as K not 2); please verify.

- Summary: Interesting and elegant theoretical idea, but practical viability and experimental support are currently insufficient and need to be addressed.
