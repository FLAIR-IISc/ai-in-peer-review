- Paper proposes a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of HMMs by casting MHMMs as a special case of HMMs and using Anandkumar et al. (2012) methods.  
- Authors introduce a stabilization technique to address parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to one.  
- Strength: intuitive approach to parameter permutation and interesting analysis of estimation noise using eigenvalues (Equation 7).  
- Major concern: overall clarity and readability are poor due to inconsistent notation and undefined vectors/matrices and dimensions.  
- Corollary 1 is unclear—terms like 1J^T need definition and lim_{e->inf} Ā^e should have its JK x JK block-diagonal structure explained.  
- Recommendation to adopt established notation (e.g., Hsu et al. (2009): ‘T’ for transitions, ‘n’, ‘m’ for observations/hidden states) to improve readability and comparability.  
- Related work section is insufficient; prior methods such as initializing EM with spectral estimates (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) must be discussed.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: Figure 4 is hard to interpret and should be replaced with tables for clarity.  
- EM performance reported is surprisingly poor; the authors should report best EM accuracy (not average) and investigate why EM underperforms.  
- Specific actionable points: define 1J^T and lim_{e->inf} Ā^e (Corollary 1); consistently define dimensions of all symbols; replace Figure 4 with tables; report best EM results in Table 1; expand related work.
