This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). It shows that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied by formulating an MHMM as a special case of an HMM. The paper proposes a method to address the parameter permutation problem by leveraging the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, assuming each HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

There are some issues with notation and clarity. The authors should demonstrate greater awareness of related work by providing relevant references – for instance, initializing Expectation-Maximization (EM) with spectral estimates has already been explored (Chaganty & Liang, 2013). The experiments are rather basic, and I strongly recommend presenting the results in a table format instead of the graphical representation in Figure 4. 

Despite these points, the proposed scheme to mitigate the parameter permutation problem is interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing use of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) within the algorithm.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   I was initially confused by the terms in Corollary 1. It would be beneficial to define **1**<sub>J</sub><sup>T</sup> (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborate on the structure of lim<sub>e→∞</sub> **Ā**<sup>e</sup>, clarifying that it's a JK x JK block-diagonal matrix where the columns within each J x J block are identical.
*   Generally, please define each vector and matrix, including its dimensions, when it is first introduced. This will significantly improve the presentation’s clarity.
*   I find Figure 4 very difficult to interpret; please replace it with tables. Moreover, I’m unsure why EM is performing so poorly in Table 1. Typically, even with random initialization and sufficient iterations, EM outperforms spectral methods (despite the potential for local optima). A standard practice is to report the best (not average) accuracy achieved by EM; please include that result.
*   Minor point: conforming to the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009) might benefit readers. Specifically, using **T** for transition matrices (instead of **A**) and **n**, **m** for the number of observations and hidden states (instead of **L**, **J**) would enhance consistency with the existing literature.

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   The paper proposes a novel approach to address a critical challenge in MHMM learning: parameter permutation.
*   The analysis of the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, particularly in the presence of estimation noise, is insightful and leads to a potentially effective algorithm component.
*   The authors demonstrate the applicability of an existing HMM learning algorithm to the MHMM setting.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   The notation could be improved for clarity, especially regarding matrix and vector definitions.
*   The related work section is lacking; several relevant publications are not cited.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited, and the presentation of results could be improved.
*   The experimental setup and results for EM seem questionable and require further investigation.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The core idea of leveraging spectral properties to address parameter permutation is novel and potentially impactful.
*   The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed algorithm.
*   The paper tackles a challenging problem in a well-motivated manner.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The presentation suffers from issues with notation and clarity.
*   The experimental validation is insufficient to fully assess the algorithm’s performance.
*   The paper overlooks related work that could strengthen its contributions.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising approach to learning MHMMs. While the presentation and experimental evaluation could be improved, the core idea and theoretical analysis are strong. I believe the paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field, but requires revisions to address the identified weaknesses. I recommend a **weak accept** pending minor revisions to address the clarity and related work issues and to improve the presentation of experimental results.
