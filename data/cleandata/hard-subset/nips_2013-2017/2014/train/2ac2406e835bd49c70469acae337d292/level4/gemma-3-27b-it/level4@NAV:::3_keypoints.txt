- Introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs as an extension of Hsu, Kakade & Zhang’s approach.  
- Core algorithm is a straightforward extension but faces a major challenge: the recovered transition matrix is a permuted version of the true component ordering.  
- This permutation mixes the identified components, complicating component alignment and interpretation.  
- Authors propose and demonstrate a depermutation algorithm that effectively resolves the component-mixing issue.  
- Manuscript contains minor typographical errors and needs careful proofreading (e.g., "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy").  
- Paper would be strengthened by a more thorough related-work discussion, particularly connecting to Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013) on spectral methods for mixtures.  
- Overall, the authors successfully address the important problem of aligning permuted components from their spectral method.
