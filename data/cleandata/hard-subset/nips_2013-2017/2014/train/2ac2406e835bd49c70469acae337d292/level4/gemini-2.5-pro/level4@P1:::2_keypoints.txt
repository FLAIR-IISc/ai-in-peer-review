- Paper proposes a spectral algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by modeling an MHMM as a single larger HMM with a block-diagonal transition structure.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel method to resolve parameter permutation ambiguity by clustering hidden states using eigenvectors of the global transition matrix.  
- Extends analysis to noisy settings and proposes a "spectral longevity" criterion (Eq.7) to estimate the number of components K.  
- Strength: using the top-K eigenvectors to group component hidden states is elegant, intuitive, and effective.  
- Strength: the noisy-case analysis and the spectral longevity model-selection idea are interesting and valuable.  
- Strength: addresses a practical problem—computationally cheaper, moment-based alternative to EM with cost independent of number of sequences after moment computation.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is preliminary and comparisons to EM are insufficiently thorough; reported EM results look unusually weak.  
- Weakness: literature positioning is incomplete—does not cite prior work on spectral initialization for EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Weakness: presentation clarity needs improvement—notations often lack dimension definitions and some figures are hard to interpret.  
- Suggestion: define every vector/matrix and its dimensions at first use (e.g., `1_M` as M-length ones vector).  
- Suggestion: more explicitly explain Corollary 1’s limit structure as MK×MK with K M×M blocks whose columns equal stationary distributions v_k.  
- Suggestion: improve EM baseline by reporting best result over multiple random restarts and clarify experimental setup.  
- Suggestion: replace or supplement Figure 4 with a table for clearer numerical comparisons of accuracy and runtime.  
- Suggestion: cite related works on spectral initialization for EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang) and consider aligning notation with seminal papers like Hsu et al. (2009).
