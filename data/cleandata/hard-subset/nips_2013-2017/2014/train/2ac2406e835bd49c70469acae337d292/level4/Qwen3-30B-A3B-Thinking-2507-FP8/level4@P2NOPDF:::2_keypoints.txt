- Proposes a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs with a stabilization step that resolves parameter permutation using the assumption each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of 1.  
- Adapting Anandkumar et al. (2012) for MHMMs is straightforward; the permutation stabilization is the novel, intuitive contribution.  
- Notation is inconsistent and often undefined (e.g., 1_J^T, structure of lim_{ε→∞} \bar{A}^ε), causing confusion.  
- Authors should define all vectors/matrices with dimensions on first use and explicitly state block-diagonal structures.  
- Recommend adopting standard HMM notation (use T, n, m as in Hsu et al. 2009) to improve clarity and literature consistency.  
- Related work is incomplete—fails to cite/compare to spectral initialization for EM (Chaganty & Liang 2013), weakening novelty claims.  
- Experiments are underdeveloped: Figure 4 is hard to read and should be replaced with tables for clarity.  
- Table 1 reports only average EM accuracy; standard practice is to report EM’s best accuracy across runs and explain any poor EM performance.  
- Theoretical analysis of estimation noise (Eq. 7) is a strength, showing eigenvalue-based robustness.  
- Experimental validation of the theoretical robustness and scalability is insufficient; more thorough tests are needed.  
- Specific actionable revisions: clarify notation, cite Chaganty & Liang (2013), replace Figure 4 with tables, report best EM results, add robustness/scalability experiments, adopt standard notation, and explicitly contrast stabilization with prior permutation methods.  
- Overall: core idea is promising but presentation, context, and experimental rigor currently fall short; revisions are needed for the contribution to be clear and impactful.
