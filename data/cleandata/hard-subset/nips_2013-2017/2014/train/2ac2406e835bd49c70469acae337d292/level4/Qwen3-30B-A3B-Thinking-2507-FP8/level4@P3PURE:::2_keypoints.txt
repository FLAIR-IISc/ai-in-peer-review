- Proposes applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm to learn mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by treating MHMM as a special case of HMM.  
- Main contribution: a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Notation needs clarification: explicitly define all vectors/matrices with dimensions when first introduced.  
- Define 1_J^T (row vector of length J of ones) and clarify structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e as a JK×JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns in each J×J block.  
- Prior work missing: cite Chaganty and Liang (2013) on initializing EM with spectral estimates.  
- Experimental evaluation is simplistic; present results in tables instead of hard-to-interpret graphs (Figure 4).  
- Explain unusually poor EM performance in Table 1 and report best (not average) EM accuracy over multiple runs for fair comparison.  
- Minor notational inconsistencies: follow established conventions (use T for transition matrices, n and m for observation/hidden state counts instead of L and J).  
- Stabilization step for parameter permutation is conceptually sound and well-motivated.  
- Analysis of estimation noise (Equation 7) and its use of eigenvalues is an interesting technical contribution.  
- Core algorithm—spectral HMM learning followed by the novel stabilization step—is clearly presented.
