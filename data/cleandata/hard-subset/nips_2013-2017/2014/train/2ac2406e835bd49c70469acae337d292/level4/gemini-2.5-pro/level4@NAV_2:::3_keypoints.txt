- Proposes a method-of-moments approach for learning Mixtures of HMMs by treating the MHMM as one large HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.
- Leverages existing spectral HMM algorithms (e.g., Hsu et al. 2012) to estimate global model parameters.
- Identifies the main technical challenge: parameter estimates are unique only up to a permutation of hidden states, conflating component HMMs.
- Main contribution is a de-permutation algorithm that uses spectral properties of the estimated global transition matrix to recover per-component state groupings.
- Introduces a clustering approach on columns of a low-rank reconstruction of the transition matrix to identify block structure.
- Provides a method to estimate the number of mixture components K from the eigenspectrum of the transition matrix.
- Experimental results show significant speed advantages over EM and utility as an EM initialization, though experiments are preliminary.
- Theoretical robustness relies on Conjecture 1 about low-rank reconstruction stability; the argument is informal and needs a stronger proof or analysis.
- Manuscript clarity needs improvement (proofreading, clearer intuition for “spectral longevity”/Definition 3).
- Related-work discussion should better connect to prior approaches for resolving permutation/label-switching (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013), and experiments should analyze sensitivity to the spectral gap condition |λGK| > max_k |λL2,k|.
