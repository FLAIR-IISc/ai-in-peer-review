This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM) by formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM and utilizing an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs. The authors propose a stabilization-based solution for parameter permutation by introducing an assumption that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1. This solution is interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues in the algorithm.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors carefully evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. However, there are some issues with notation that need to be addressed. The authors should provide more awareness of related work by including relevant references, such as initializing EM with spectral estimates explored in Chaganty and Liang (2013).

The experiments are quite basic and could be improved by reporting results in a table format rather than the graphical format in Figure 4. Additionally, the authors should consider including the best (not average) accuracy of EM in their results.

The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical. However, the authors could improve the presentation by defining each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced. This would help readers understand the material more easily.

The originality of the paper lies in its novel stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation. The authors provide a unique combination of familiar techniques, and it is clear how this work differs from previous contributions. The related work is adequately referenced, and the authors recommend checking the proceedings of recent NIPS conferences to ensure that each paper is significantly different from papers in previous proceedings.

The results are important, and other researchers may likely use these ideas or build upon them. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way. The authors provide unique data, unique conclusions on existing data, or a unique theoretical or pragmatic approach.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models.
* The authors provide a clear and logical organization of their work.
* The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
* The authors carefully evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Arguments con acceptance:

* There are some issues with notation that need to be addressed.
* The experiments are quite basic and could be improved.
* The authors should consider including the best (not average) accuracy of EM in their results.
* The paper could benefit from more awareness of related work.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors present a novel algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models. With some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
