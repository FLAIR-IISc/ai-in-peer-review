This paper develops a spectral (method-of-moments) algorithm for learning mixtures of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The key observation is that an MHMM can be recast as a single HMM whose transition matrix is block diagonal, which enables direct use of existing spectral HMM estimators (e.g., Anandkumar et al., 2012). The authors then tackle the label permutation problem by a stabilization step grounded in the assumption that each component HMM has exactly one eigenvalue equal to 1, and they analyze how to recover the de-permutation in the presence of estimation noise.

There are some notational issues, and the paper should better situate itself within related work (for example, using spectral estimates to initialize EM has been explored by Chaganty and Liang, 2013). The empirical evaluation is fairly limited; in particular, the results in Figure 4 would be easier to interpret in tabular form.

Nevertheless, the proposed approach to resolve parameter permutation is intuitive and novel in this context. The treatment of the noisy setting and the resulting eigenvalue-based criterion (Equation (7)) are interesting and potentially useful in practice.

Comments:
- Corollary 1 was initially confusing. Please explicitly define 1_J^T as a row vector of ones of length J, and elaborate on the structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e (namely, that it forms a JK × JK block-structured matrix where, within each J × J block, all columns are identical).
- In general, define each vector/matrix and its dimensions at first use; this will significantly improve readability.
- Figure 4 is hard to parse; please replace it with tables. Also, EM’s performance in Table 1 seems unexpectedly weak. It is standard to report the best result over multiple random restarts (rather than the average), since EM typically surpasses spectral methods with sufficient iterations despite local optima; please include that best-run result.
- Minor: consider aligning notation with Hsu et al. (2009)—for instance, use T for transition matrices instead of A, and n, m for the numbers of observation and hidden states instead of L, J.

The paper proposes an MHMM learning pipeline that applies an off-the-shelf spectral HMM estimator followed by a new stabilization step to undo parameter permutation.
