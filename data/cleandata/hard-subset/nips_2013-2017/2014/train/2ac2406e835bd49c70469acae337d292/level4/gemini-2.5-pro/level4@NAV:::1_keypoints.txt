- Proposes a two-stage method: spectral learning of a single large HMM followed by de-permutation to recover block-diagonal mixture structure.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, but experimental evaluation needs more detail.  
- Method may be impractical for real-world settings because it requires accurate estimation of a dense HMM before recovering sparsity; applicability conditions are unclear.  
- Unclear whether spectral methods have theoretical guarantees to recover a transition matrix provably close to a sparse block-diagonal form rather than a nearby dense model.  
- Sec 4.1 experiments isolate de-permutation using Dirichlet-perturbed matrices instead of evaluating the full pipeline from sampled observation sequences, which reduces realism.  
- Sec 4.2 moves toward full-pipeline evaluation but omits crucial details of the ground-truth mixture (transition/observation parameters); these should be provided.  
- In Table 1, EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone, which is counter-intuitive and needs explanation.  
- Typo in proof (Section 3.2.1): Λ subscript in bottom-right block should be K, not 2.  
- Figures have fonts that are too small to read comfortably.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution but significant concerns about practical viability on real-world datasets.
