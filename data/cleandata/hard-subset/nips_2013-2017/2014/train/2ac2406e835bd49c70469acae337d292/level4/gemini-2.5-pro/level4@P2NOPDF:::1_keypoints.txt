- Paper proposes a two-stage method: spectral learning of a single large HMM followed by a de-permutation step to recover block-diagonal mixture structure.
- Theoretical contributions are interesting and the paper is generally well-written, but experiments lack sufficient detail and realism.
- Core concern: method requires accurately estimating a full dense HMM before recovering sparsity, which undermines the motivation for using sparse HMM mixtures in low-data, large-scale settings.
- Unclear whether spectral learning can recover a transition matrix close enough to the true block-diagonal form under realistic noise and limited data.
- The de-permutation algorithm may fail when the input matrix is a noisy, dense approximation rather than a clean permuted block-diagonal matrix.
- Section 4.1 experiments only test de-permutation on a permuted ground-truth plus Dirichlet noise, not the full end-to-end pipeline; a full synthetic end-to-end evaluation is needed.
- Suggested end-to-end experiment: generate sequences from a known HMM mixture, estimate via spectral methods, apply de-permutation, and evaluate recovered blocks against ground truth.
- Section 4.2 attempts end-to-end testing but omits crucial details (ground-truth transition and observation matrices and parameter values) needed for assessing difficulty and reproducibility.
- Table 1 anomaly: initializing EM with the spectral solution sometimes yields worse performance than spectral alone—authors should explain whether a different metric is used or EM is falling into poorer local optima.
- Minor comments: a probable subscript typo in the proof (Λ₂ should be Λ_K) and figure text/numbers are too small for readability.
- Overall: significant practical viability concerns remain; experiments must be strengthened with realistic, end-to-end tests and full experimental details to validate effectiveness on noisy, real-world data.
