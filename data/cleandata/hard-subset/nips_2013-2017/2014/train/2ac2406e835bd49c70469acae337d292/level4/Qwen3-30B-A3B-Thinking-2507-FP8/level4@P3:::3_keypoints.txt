- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) and identifies permutation ambiguity in standard MoM estimates.
- Recasts an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix to enable recovery of individual HMM parameters.
- Introduces a depermutation algorithm that leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover block structure.
- Provides lemmas characterizing spectral properties of the global transition matrix and conjectures conditions for depermutation success.
- Experimental results show computational advantages over EM on large datasets and effectiveness as an EM initialization.
- Paper is technically sound and tackles a non-trivial, previously overlooked problem in MHMM learning.
- Theoretical analysis is promising but incomplete: Lemma 3 proof is unclear and Conjecture 1 lacks rigorous proof and specific noise-condition analysis.
- Experimental validation is well-designed with synthetic recovery of block structure and real-data application to character trajectories.
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized with consistent notation and logical flow.
- Several grammatical errors, typos, and some opaque technical explanations (e.g., spectral longevity) need correction and clarification.
- Novelty lies in the depermutation algorithm for MHMMs, building on but extending Hsu et al. (2012) style MoM approaches.
- Would benefit from stronger contextualization with related spectral mixture literature (e.g., Chaganty & Liang) and clearer differentiation from prior methods.
- Significant practical impact for large-scale sequence clustering due to speed gains and use as robust EM initialization.
- Pros for acceptance: solves a real permutation-ambiguity problem, provides theoretical conditions, shows strong empirical speed/utility, and is applicable to sequence clustering.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient theoretical rigor for key claims, weaker contextual comparison to related work, presentation issues, and limited experimental breadth for EM comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: valuable and promising contribution; accept with revisions to strengthen proofs, clarify exposition, and expand related-work/experimental detail.
