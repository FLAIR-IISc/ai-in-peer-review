

This paper proposes a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). It demonstrates that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be directly applied to MHMMs by formulating the MHMM as a special case of an HMM. The authors introduce a stabilization-based solution for resolving parameter permutation, relying on the assumption that each HMM's transition matrix possesses a single eigenvalue of value 1.

The manuscript exhibits several notational inconsistencies. The authors should demonstrate greater awareness of related work by citing relevant prior studies; for instance, initializing EM with spectral estimates has been explored in Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experimental evaluation is superficial, and results should be reported in tabular format rather than the graphical presentation in Figure 4.

Despite these issues, the proposed approach to circumvent parameter permutation is both interesting and intuitive. The analysis for the case with estimation noise (Equation (7)) is particularly noteworthy for its innovative use of eigenvalues.

Comments:
- Corollary 1 requires clarification: define $1_J^T$ (a row vector of length $J$ filled with ones) and elaborate on the structure of $\lim_{\varepsilon \to \infty} \bar{A}^{\varepsilon}$ (a $JK \times JK$ block diagonal matrix where all columns within each $J \times J$ block are identical).
- Define all vectors and matrices with their dimensions upon first introduction to significantly improve clarity.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret; replace it with tables. Additionally, the poor performance of EM in Table 1 is surprising; typically, even with random initialization, EM achieves better results than spectral methods after sufficient iterations (despite local optima). Report the best (not average) accuracy of EM in Table 1.
- Adopt the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009): use $T$ for transition matrices (not $A$), and $n$, $m$ for the numbers of observation and hidden states (not $L$, $J$).

The paper presents an algorithm for learning MHMMs, combining an existing spectral method for HMMs with a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation.
