- Proposes a spectral algorithm for mixtures of HMMs by modeling MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Addresses parameter permutation ambiguity using a stabilization technique based on spectral properties and eigenvalue assumptions.  
- Motivates Method-of-Moments approaches as computationally attractive alternatives to EM for MHMMs.  
- Builds on prior HMM spectral learning literature (Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al.) though initial positioning could be clearer.  
- Strength: novel spectral method for resolving permutation ambiguity with analysis of estimation noise and eigenvalue-based clustering (Eq. 7).  
- Strength: claims computational efficiency and faster runtime compared to EM, supported by experiments.  
- Strength: provides theoretical lemmas linking MHMM structure to HMM and characterizing transition-matrix spectral properties.  
- Strength: experiments demonstrate feasibility and competitive speed, albeit basic.  
- Weakness: inconsistent and unclear notation, missing explicit dimensions and unclear expressions (e.g., Corollary 1, 1J^T, lim_{e->inf} bar{A}^e).  
- Weakness: limited related-work discussion; misses relevant papers like Chaganty & Liang (2013) and spectral clustering on sequences.  
- Weakness: experimental presentation issues—Figure 4 is hard to read, Table 1’s EM results seem surprisingly low and lack details on EM tuning; report best EM runs.  
- Weakness: notation would benefit from standard HMM conventions (e.g., T, n, m) to improve readability and comparison.  
- Weakness: conjecture on spectral longevity needs stronger justification or a proof sketch.  
- For acceptance: addresses a significant MHMM challenge with a novel, theoretically grounded, and potentially efficient solution.  
- Against acceptance: clarity, related-work gaps, experimental presentation, and reliance on an unproven conjecture weaken the submission.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires major revisions on notation clarity, related work, and experimental evidence.  
- Recommendation: weak accept with request for major revisions focused on presentation, related work expansion, and stronger experiments.
