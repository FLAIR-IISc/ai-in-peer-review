1. Paper is well-written but experimental results need more detail and clearer presentation.  
2. Relying on expressing a mixture of HMMs as a single HMM is problematic: the method needs accurate full-HMM estimation and permutation reversal, which may be infeasible in real-world, data-limited settings; authors should clarify applicability and limits.  
3. Concern that the recovered transition matrix may not be sparse and the spectral algorithm may not guarantee sparsity; multiple dense HMMs could approximate the desired sparse model and noise effects must be discussed.  
4. Section 4.1’s experiment is unrealistic: permuting a noisy transition matrix is not equivalent to learning from noisy observations—authors should sample observation data from a mixture of HMMs and learn parameters.  
5. Section 4.2 lacks crucial experimental details (transition and observation matrices of the mixture); these are needed to assess learning difficulty and results.  
6. Table 1 anomaly: EM initialized with spectral sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—counterintuitive and requires explanation.  
7. Typo in proof (Section 3.2.1): subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.  
8. Figures have text and numbers that are too small and should be enlarged for readability.  
9. Overall: interesting ideas and solid theory but practical applicability is unclear; more comprehensive, realistic experiments are needed to validate the approach.
