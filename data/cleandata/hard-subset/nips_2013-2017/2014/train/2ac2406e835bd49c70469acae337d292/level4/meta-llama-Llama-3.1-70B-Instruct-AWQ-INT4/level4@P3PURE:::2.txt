This paper proposes a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The authors demonstrate that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied off-the-shelf by formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM. They introduce a stabilization-based solution to address the issue of parameter permutation by assuming that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

However, there are some issues with notation that need to be addressed. The authors should provide more references to related work, such as the initialization of EM with spectral estimates, which has been explored in Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experimental results are quite basic and could be improved by presenting them in a table format rather than the graphical format in Figure 4.

On the positive side, the proposed scheme to address the problem of parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The authors provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) in the algorithm.

To improve the clarity of the paper, I have several suggestions. Firstly, the authors should define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it is first introduced. For instance, the terms in Corollary 1 could be clarified by defining 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborating on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (that it's JK x JK block diagonal where the columns within each J x J block are the same).

Furthermore, the authors should consider replacing Figure 4 with tables, as the current graphical format is difficult to read. Additionally, the performance of EM in Table 1 is surprisingly poor, and it would be beneficial to include the best (not average) accuracy of EM, as is standard practice. Typically, even with random initialization, EM can outperform spectral methods with enough iterations, despite the local optima problem.

Finally, conforming to established notation, such as using T for transition matrices (not A) and n, m for numbers of observation and hidden states (not L, J), as in Hsu et al. (2009), would improve the readability of the paper.

Overall, the paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models, which consists of an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models followed by a novel stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation.
