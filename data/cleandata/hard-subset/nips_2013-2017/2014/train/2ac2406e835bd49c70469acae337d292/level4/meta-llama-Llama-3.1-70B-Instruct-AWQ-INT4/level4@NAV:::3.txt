The authors introduce a spectral approach for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). Notably, their algorithm is a variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm, with the exception that the recovered transition matrix is an arbitrary permutation of the true matrix, intermixing the various components. To address this, the authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to yield satisfactory results.

However, upon closer inspection, several minor typos are evident, such as the statement "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." It is essential that the authors conduct a thorough proofread to rectify these errors.

Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive contextualization of the presented work. For instance, a discussion of its relation to existing research, such as Chaganty and Liang's work on spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions (ICML, 2013), would enhance the paper's overall impact. The authors' spectral method for learning a mixture of HMMs effectively tackles the critical issue of "de-permuting" the results, and a clearer connection to the broader literature would strengthen their contribution.
