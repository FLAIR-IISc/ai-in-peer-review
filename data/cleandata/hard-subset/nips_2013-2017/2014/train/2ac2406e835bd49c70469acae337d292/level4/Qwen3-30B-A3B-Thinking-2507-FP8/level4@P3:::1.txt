

**Review of "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models"**

This paper proposes a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors establish that an MHMM can be viewed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling the application of existing spectral HMM learning algorithms. The key contribution lies in resolving the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM-based learning through spectral properties of the global transition matrix, allowing recovery of the individual HMM parameters.

**Summary and Relation to Prior Work**
The paper builds upon recent advances in spectral learning for latent variable models (Anandkumar et al., COLT 2012; Hsu et al., JCSS 2012), extending these methods to the more complex MHMM setting. While previous work has addressed spectral learning for single HMMs, this paper is the first to provide a theoretically grounded approach for MHMMs. The authors correctly note that traditional EM-based learning for MHMMs suffers from computational intractability with large datasets due to the need for sequence-specific forward-backward passes, making their computational advantage significant.

**Strengths**
1. **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear mathematical formulations and logical flow. The key insight (MHMM as an HMM with block-diagonal structure) is elegantly presented and well-motivated.
2. **Originality:** The spectral approach to resolving permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning represents a novel contribution to the literature. The theoretical results (Lemmas 1-3) provide strong guarantees for the algorithm's operation under certain spectral conditions.
3. **Significance:** The computational advantage of the proposed method over EM is substantial, with experiments showing the spectral approach is orders of magnitude faster while maintaining comparable accuracy. The real-data experiment demonstrates practical utility as a good initialization for EM.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Quality (Experimental Validation):** The experimental section lacks sufficient realism to validate the core claim of practical utility. Section 4.1 examines the depermutation algorithm on permuted matrices without generating data from the actual MHMM model, which misrepresents the problem. The noise model (Dirichlet permutation) is artificial and does not reflect observation noise in real data. Section 4.2 begins to address this with synthetic data but fails to provide critical details about the underlying MHMM parameters (e.g., transition matrices, emission parameters), making it impossible to assess the difficulty of the learning task. Section 4.3's real-data experiment is limited in scope (only two classes) and lacks sufficient comparison to establish the method's practical advantage.

2. **Quality (Practical Applicability):** The authors acknowledge that their method requires estimating the full HMM parameters before depermuting, which contradicts the primary motivation for using MHMMs in the first place: the sparsity of the block-diagonal structure. For large-scale problems where MHMMs are typically employed (due to data sparsity), this approach would require estimating a full transition matrix of size MK × MK, which is computationally prohibitive when MK is large. The paper fails to provide concrete guidance on when their method is appropriate (e.g., small K and M) versus when it would be infeasible.

3. **Quality (Sparsity Guarantee):** The paper does not address whether the learned transition matrix will be (close to) sparse in practice, which is the core advantage of MHMMs over single HMMs. Without theoretical or empirical evidence that the spectral approach yields sparse matrices, it's unclear how this method would outperform direct MHMM learning approaches that explicitly enforce block structure.

4. **Significance (Impact Assessment):** While the method is theoretically interesting, the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate how it advances the state of the art beyond existing EM-based MHMM approaches. The experiments fail to show consistent performance improvements over EM, and the real-data results (Table 1) indicate the spectral method alone often underperforms compared to EM with spectral initialization.

**Recommendation**
This paper presents a theoretically sound approach to spectral learning of MHMMs with clear computational advantages over EM. The technical contributions are novel and well-executed, and the paper is exceptionally well-written. However, the experimental validation is insufficient to support the practical claims of the method. The authors need to demonstrate their approach on realistic MHMM data with detailed parameter specifications, properly quantify the trade-offs between computational efficiency and accuracy, and provide clear guidance on the method's practical applicability.

**Pro/Con Acceptance Summary**
*Pro:*
- Novel theoretical approach to MHMM learning
- Strong theoretical guarantees for the depermutation algorithm
- Clear presentation and well-structured paper
- Demonstrated computational advantage over EM

*Con:*
- Experimental section lacks realistic validation
- Insufficient detail on synthetic data generation
- Fails to address practical applicability concerns
- Does not demonstrate clear advantage over existing methods

Given the strong theoretical contribution and writing quality, but significant concerns about experimental validation and practical applicability, I recommend a borderline acceptance. The paper would benefit substantially from more realistic experiments and clearer discussion of the method's limitations.
