

Review Summary: The authors address the problem of learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models. They propose a spectral learning algorithm to estimate parameters for a single HMM and introduce a method to resolve permutation ambiguity in the transition matrix to recover its underlying block-diagonal structure.  

Major Comments:  
1. The paper is well-written overall, though the experimental results section requires further elaboration, particularly regarding [specific details omitted as per original].  
2. The approach assumes a mixture of HMMs can be expressed as a single HMM. However, the primary motivation for using a mixture (as opposed to a single HMM) is the sparsity induced by the block-diagonal structure, which enables scalability for large problems with limited data. The proposed method first estimates full HMM parameters before recovering the block structure—a process that is highly impractical for real-world scenarios where sparsity is essential. The authors should clarify under what conditions their algorithm is viable and where it fails.  
3. In practice, the recovered transition matrix is unlikely to be sparse. Can the spectral learning method guarantee (near) sparsity? I suspect many full-transition HMMs are close to the target block-diagonal structure, making it unclear whether the learned matrix will be sparse even with minimal noise.  
4. The experimental results do not adequately reflect real noise in HMM learning. Section 4.1 applies the algorithm to a permuted transition matrix with Dirichlet-distributed noise—this addresses matrix depermutation, not mixture parameter learning. The authors should instead generate observation data from a true mixture of HMMs and learn parameters from this data to assess how observation noise and limited data impact depermutation.  
5. Section 4.2 begins to address this, but lacks critical details. Without knowledge of the actual mixture parameters (transition/observation matrices), the experiment’s difficulty and results cannot be properly evaluated. Parameter specifics should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).  
6. In Table 1, why does EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes underperform spectral learning alone? EM should not degrade results.  

Minor Comments:  
1. The subscript of Λ in the bottom-right corner of the first matrix in Section 3.2.1 should be K, not 2.  
2. Text and numbers in figures are excessively small.  

The paper presents interesting theoretical contributions and sound methodology, but the approach appears unlikely to generalize to real-world datasets due to its reliance on full HMM estimation prior to sparsity exploitation.
