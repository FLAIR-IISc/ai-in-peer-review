- Summary: Proposes using spectral learning to estimate a single HMM for a mixture, then undoing permutation to recover block-diagonal mixture structure.  
- Writing/theory: Paper is well written with solid theoretical results, but experiments must be expanded (datasets, parameter settings, evaluation metrics).  
- Practicality concern: Algorithm requires accurately estimating the full HMM before revealing sparsity, which may be untenable when data is limited.  
- Applicability guidance: Authors should state when their algorithm is appropriate versus when sparsity-based mixture learning is preferable.  
- Sparsity guarantee: No argument provided that spectral algorithms will produce (approximately) sparse transition matrices; dense nearby HMMs may confound recovery.  
- Experimental realism: Current experiments do not simulate realistic noise—should sample sequences from HMM mixtures and learn parameters from data to assess effects of observation noise and limited data.  
- Missing details (Section 4.2): Provide full specification of the mixture HMMs used (transition and observation parameters) so recovery difficulty can be assessed.  
- EM vs spectral: In Table 1, EM initialized with spectral sometimes performs worse than spectral alone; clarify experimental setup and explain this result.  
- Minor error: In proof (Section 3.2.1) the subscript of Λ in the bottom-right corner should be K, not 2.  
- Presentation issue: Text and numbers in figures are too small.  
- Overall verdict: Interesting ideas and solid theory, but significant concerns remain about practicality on real datasets.
