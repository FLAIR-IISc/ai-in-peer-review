- Proposes a Method of Moments approach for Mixtures of HMMs by viewing an MHMM with K components and M states each as a single HMM with MK states and block-diagonal transitions.

- Leverages existing spectral HMM algorithms (e.g., Hsu, Kakade, Zhang 2012) to learn the parameters of the large MK-state HMM.

- Identifies permutation ambiguity in spectral recovery as the central technical obstacle that mixes states across components.

- Presents a novel de-permutation algorithm that exploits the block-diagonal structure to demix components in the noiseless case via K eigenvalues equal to 1.

- Extends the de-permutation to the noisy case using low-rank reconstruction from the K largest-magnitude eigenvalues and column clustering to restore block structure.

- Proposes a heuristic for estimating the number of components K based on the "spectral longevity" of eigenvalues.

- Empirically demonstrates computational speed advantages over EM and shows the spectral method is an effective initializer for EM.

- Claims the approach is significant and original by making spectral learning practical for MHMMs through de-permutation.

- Argues technical soundness with a clear progression from noiseless analysis to a practical noisy algorithm and helpful visualizations.

- Notes the paper is generally clear and well-organized, with useful figures illustrating de-permutation and noise effects.

- Weakness: theoretical support is incomplete—key steps rely on conjectures, reused results, and assumptions without full proofs or rigorous error bounds.

- Weakness: insufficient contextualization with related spectral demixing work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) and limited discussion of differences.

- Weakness: manuscript needs proofreading and clearer algorithm descriptions (e.g., imprecise wording about estimating K).

- Weakness: experimental validation is limited—real-data tests are narrow and the method as a standalone algorithm sometimes underperforms random-init EM; larger/complex datasets would strengthen claims.
