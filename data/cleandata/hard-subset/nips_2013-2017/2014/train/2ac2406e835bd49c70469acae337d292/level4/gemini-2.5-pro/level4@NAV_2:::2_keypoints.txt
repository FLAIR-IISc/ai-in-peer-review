- Reframes an MHMM with K components as a single larger HMM with block-diagonal transition, enabling use of spectral HMM algorithms.
- Main technical contribution: a novel de-permutation method that clusters columns of a low-rank reconstruction (top K eigenvalues) of the global transition matrix to recover block structure and component parameters.
- Provides analysis of the method under estimation noise and proposes a spectral approach to determine the number of mixture components K.
- Strength: elegant, intuitive, and novel solution to the permutation problem in spectral MHMM learning.
- Strength: includes noisy-case analysis and a practical model-selection procedure.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited and doesn’t fully substantiate claimed speed and accuracy benefits over EM.
- Weakness: manuscript clarity suffers from inconsistent notation and insufficient definitions, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: related-work discussion is incomplete and should better situate the paper relative to spectral initialization methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).
- Argument for acceptance: tackles an important, practical problem of efficient MHMM learning for large datasets.
- Argument for acceptance: de-permutation technique is a significant and clever technical contribution.
- Argument for acceptance: noisy analysis and K-selection increase the algorithm’s practical utility.
- Argument against acceptance: preliminary experiments lack depth to convincingly demonstrate superiority.
- Argument against acceptance: significant improvements needed in clarity and notation.
- Argument against acceptance: inadequate contextualization within existing spectral-method literature.
- Suggestion: improve notation—define each vector/matrix and dimensions at first use and conform to established conventions (e.g., Hsu et al. 2012).
- Suggestion: present accuracy and runtime comparisons in tables for clearer interpretation.
- Suggestion: clarify EM baseline methodology and report the best run over multiple random initializations for a fair comparison.
