- Summary: Proposes learning an MHMM by spectral learning of a single larger HMM then resolving state-permutation via spectral analysis to recover block-diagonal submodels.

- Major 1: Paper is well-written but experimental results need more detail and more realistic evaluations.

- Major 2: Core concern that learning a dense large HMM first undermines the motivation for MHMMs (which exploit sparse block structure); unclear when the two-step approach is practical, especially with limited data.

- Major 3: The learned transition matrix may not be a permuted block-diagonal plus small noise; the paper lacks convincing theoretical or empirical evidence that depermutation will reliably succeed.

- Major 4: Experiment 4.1 is not an end-to-end test; a realistic evaluation should generate sequences from an MHMM, run the full spectral pipeline, then test depermutation to show noise propagation effects.

- Major 5: Experiment 4.2 compares to EM but omits crucial ground-truth parameter details (emission separability, mixing rates); authors should report these settings (e.g., in an appendix).

- Major 6: Table 1 shows EM initialized with MoM sometimes worse than MoM alone, which is counterintuitive; authors should explain whether EM finds higher likelihood but worse clustering/local optima.

- Minor 1: Typo in Lemma 2: bottom-right eigendecomposition subscript should be K (V_K Λ_K V_K⁻¹), not 2.

- Minor 2: Figures have text and numbers that are too small to read.

- Overall: Interesting theoretical idea but significant concerns about practical viability and insufficient experimental evidence that the depermutation approach works in real-world, data-scarce scenarios.
