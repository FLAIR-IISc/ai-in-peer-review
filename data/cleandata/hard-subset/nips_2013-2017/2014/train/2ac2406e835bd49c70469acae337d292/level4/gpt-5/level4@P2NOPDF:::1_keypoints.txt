- Experimental section needs more substance: provide clear dataset descriptions, parameter settings, evaluation protocols, and sensitivity analyses (sample size, noise, number of components, state-space size).

- Clarify practicality of pipeline that first learns a full HMM then de-permutes to recover sparsity; specify regimes where this is appropriate (data size, noise, components, separation) and list required assumptions (identifiability, mixing/separation, eigenvalue gaps).

- Address sparsity: can the method encourage or guarantee near-sparsity in the recovered transition matrix (e.g., thresholding, regularization, constrained factorization) and under what conditions are spectral estimates provably close to a sparse block-diagonal target.

- Experiments understate noise difficulty: evaluate end-to-end by simulating observation sequences from mixtures, applying the spectral estimator, then testing de-permutation to capture noise propagation and finite-sample effects.

- Section 4.2 lacks crucial detail: report true transition/emission parameters or summaries (block sizes, inter-block mass, spectral gaps, emission confusion), sequence lengths, number of sequences, and mixing scheme to enable assessment and reproducibility.

- Explain why EM initialized with spectral estimates sometimes underperforms pure spectral learning: report EM configuration (restarts, damping, regularization, stopping), learning curves, and diagnostics to rule out overfitting or local optima.

- Minor: fix typo in Section 3.2.1 (Λ subscript should be K, not 2).

- Minor: improve figures (increase font sizes and label clarity).

- Overall: paper has solid theory and an appealing permutation-resolution step but practical applicability is questionable; strengthen empirical validation with realistic simulations, detailed ground-truth reporting, and robustness analyses to improve credibility.
