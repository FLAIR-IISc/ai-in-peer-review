This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The authors propose a method of moments based approach, leveraging the information in various moments computed from the data. The main idea is to formulate an MHMM as a special case of HMM and use an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs. The paper also introduces a stabilization-based solution for parameter permutation by assuming that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

The paper relates to previous work on spectral learning for HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) and mixture models (Anandkumar et al., 2013). The authors also mention the use of EM algorithm for learning MHMMs, but they do not provide a comprehensive comparison with other existing methods.

The strengths of the paper include the proposal of a novel stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation, which is an interesting and intuitive solution. The authors also provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues in the algorithm. Additionally, the paper presents experimental results on synthetic and real data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

However, there are some issues with notation and presentation. The authors should define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced. The paper would also benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods, including a discussion of the advantages and limitations of the proposed approach. Furthermore, the experimental results could be presented in a more clear and concise manner, using tables instead of graphs.
