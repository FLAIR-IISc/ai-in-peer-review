- Summary: Proposes spectral learning for MHMMs by modeling MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition and resolving permutation ambiguity to recover block structure, offering computational advantages over EM.

- Strength: The depermutation approach is theoretically sound, leveraging spectral properties of the global transition matrix.

- Strength: The paper clearly derives the connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.

- Strength: Experiments demonstrate the proposed method’s computational efficiency compared to EM, especially on larger datasets.

- Strength: Theoretical analysis outlines spectral conditions required for successful depermutation.

- Weakness 1: Experimental section lacks detailed reporting of parameters and the true MHMM structure used in experiments.

- Weakness 2: Method depends on accurately estimating a full HMM before depermutation, which is impractical when training data is limited—the very setting where MHMM sparsity matters.

- Weakness 3: The paper does not address how to ensure the learned transition matrix is (near) sparse or block-diagonal in practice.

- Weakness 4: Section 4.1’s experiments do not mimic real-world noise and limited-data conditions, since they perturb a transition matrix rather than learning from noisy observations.

- Weakness 5: Section 4.2 lacks sufficient detail on transition and observation matrix parameters, making it hard to assess experimental difficulty and results.

- Weakness 6: Table 1 shows EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performing worse than spectral alone; this counterintuitive outcome is unexplained.

- Minor 1: Typo in Section 3.2.1—subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.

- Minor 2: Figures’ text and numbers are too small to be readable.

- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution but questionable practical applicability under limited-data/noisy settings; needs clearer guidance on applicability and more comprehensive, realistic experiments.
