- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments for MHMMs by reducing an MHMM to an MK-state HMM and using a depermutation procedure to recover component blocks and estimate K.
- Builds on prior spectral/tensor MoM work; block-diagonal reduction is standard and novelty lies in the depermutation and K-estimation heuristics.
- Reduction is correct and clearly presented; depermutation is plausible and works empirically but depends on Conjecture 1.
- No formal theoretical guarantees: missing conditions on noise, spectral gaps, and sample complexity.
- The “spectral longevity” heuristic for selecting K is intuitive but lacks formal justification beyond Lemma 3, which assumes unproven separations.
- Synthetic results show depermutation robustness with longer sequences; real-data (UCI trajectories) shows competitiveness and value as an EM initializer.
- Empirical evaluation needs more systematic reporting (error bars, sensitivity to K/M misspecification, robustness to small spectral gaps).
- Reproducibility issues: unclear how MK is chosen when K is unknown, exponent range/step for longevity, clustering algorithm/hyperparameters, runtime environment, and EM feature setup.
- Paper is readable and well-structured but contains typos/grammatical errors and would benefit from a symbol summary and clearer eigenvalue-multiplicity assumptions.
- Originality is incremental: depermutation and eigenvalue-longevity K estimation are useful extensions of spectral HMM learning.
- Practical significance is moderate: computationally attractive and useful empirically but limited by incomplete theory.
- Strength: clear MHMM→block-diagonal HMM reduction enabling off-the-shelf spectral estimation.
- Strength: practical depermutation procedure and K-selection heuristic that perform well in experiments.
- Strength: computational efficiency relative to EM and effective use as an EM initializer.
- Weakness: central separation and convergence claims remain conjectural; need perturbation/eigenvalue-eigenvector bounds under noise.
- Weakness: assumptions (e.g., exactly one unit-magnitude eigenvalue per Ak) require discussion of violations like near-periodicity.
- Weakness: unclear practical strategy for choosing MK and sensitivity to over/under-specification of MK.
- Weakness: feature expansion used to meet rank conditions may give the spectral method an advantage; need consistent baselines and discussion of feature effects.
- Weakness: experiments lack error bars, runtime/hardware details, ablations (clustering method, exponent range), and parameter-recovery metrics.
- Related-work positioning should be improved with complete citations to Anandkumar et al., Hsu et al., Smyth, Chaganty & Liang, and other spectral/tensor MHMM literature.
- Minor: fix typos, define symbols on first use, standardize notation, and provide clear pseudocode and complexity analysis.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a practical identifiability/permutation hurdle, is simple and scalable, and shows promising empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: theoretical foundations are incomplete, evaluation lacks robustness and reproducibility details, and some assumptions limit applicability.
- Overall recommendation: solid and useful empirical contribution but short on theory and comprehensive evaluation — borderline acceptance contingent on stronger proofs, experiments, and presentation polish.
