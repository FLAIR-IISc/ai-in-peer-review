This paper proposes a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors address the key question of how to "de-permute" the results, as the transition matrix recovered by the algorithm is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, mixing the various components.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and their approach. The algorithm is a variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm, and the authors propose a depermutation algorithm that seems to work reasonably well.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a good balance of theoretical and experimental results. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, including a comparison with the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.

However, there are a few areas for improvement. Occasionally, there seem to be a number of little typos, such as "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." The authors should proofread the paper more carefully to eliminate these errors.

Additionally, the paper could do a better job of putting this work into context. For example, the authors could relate their work to papers such as Chaganty and Liang's "Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions" (ICML, 2013), which also uses spectral methods for learning mixture models.
