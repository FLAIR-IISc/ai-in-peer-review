- Introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs by casting an MHMM as a larger HMM to apply Anandkumar et al. (2012).
- Proposes a novel stabilization-based technique to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each component transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue of 1.
- Notation is inconsistent at times, which reduces readability.
- Related work discussion is insufficient; should cite and discuss prior work like Chaganty and Liang (2013) on using spectral estimates to initialize EM.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and Figure 4 is hard to parse; results should be presented in a standard tabular format.
- Authors should define all vectors and matrices with dimensions on first use and clarify notation such as 1_J^T and the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e.
- The poor EM baseline results are surprising; the paper should clarify this and report best-run EM performance in addition to averages.
- The stabilization technique is a notable and intuitive contribution.
- The theoretical analysis that accounts for estimation noise is valuable and leads to an interesting algorithmic use of eigenvalues (see Eq. 7).
- Recommend adopting standard notational conventions (e.g., T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden counts) to align with prior literature.
