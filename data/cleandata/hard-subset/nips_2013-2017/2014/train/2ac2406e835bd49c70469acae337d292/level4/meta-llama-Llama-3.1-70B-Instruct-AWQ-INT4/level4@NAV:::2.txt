This paper proposes a novel approach to learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM) by leveraging an existing spectral algorithm for hidden Markov models (HMMs) and introducing a stabilization-based solution to address parameter permutation. By reformulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM, the authors demonstrate the applicability of the existing spectral algorithm. The proposed method relies on the assumption that each HMM's transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue of value 1.

While the paper provides a valuable contribution, there are several areas that require improvement. Notably, the notation could be clarified, and the authors should engage more thoroughly with related work, citing relevant studies such as Chaganty and Liang (2013), which explored initializing EM with spectral estimates. The experimental design is somewhat basic, and the results would be more effectively presented in a table format, rather than the graphical format currently used in Figure 4.

The proposed stabilization scheme for parameter permutation is a notable strength of the paper, offering an intuitive solution to this problem. Furthermore, the authors provide a thoughtful analysis of the case with estimation noise, incorporating eigenvalues in a creative manner (Equation (7)). 

To enhance the clarity and readability of the paper, several suggestions are offered:

* Define 1J^T and elaborate on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e to facilitate understanding of Corollary 1.
* Provide explicit definitions and dimensions for vectors and matrices upon their introduction to improve the presentation.
* Replace Figure 4 with tables for enhanced readability and consider reporting the best accuracy of EM, rather than the average, to align with standard practices.
* Adopt the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009) to maintain consistency with existing literature, using T for transition matrices and n, m for numbers of observation and hidden states.

Overall, the paper presents an innovative algorithm for learning MHMM, consisting of an existing spectral algorithm for HMMs and a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation.
