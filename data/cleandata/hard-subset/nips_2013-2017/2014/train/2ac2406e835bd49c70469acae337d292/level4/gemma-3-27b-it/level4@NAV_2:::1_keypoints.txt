- Paper proposes a novel MoM-based approach to MHMM learning using equivalence to a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions and resolves permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.
- Well-written and theoretically grounded, but experimental evaluation is weak and needs strengthening to show practical utility.
- Algorithm requires accurate initial estimation of a full transition matrix, raising concerns about applicability to large-scale, data-limited, or inherently sparse settings.
- Need explicit discussion of appropriate scenarios and limitations, including computational cost of estimating a dense transition matrix.
- No guarantee the spectral method yields a sparse/block-diagonal transition matrix; it may produce a dense approximation and ambiguity over multiple full HMM approximations.
- Suggest exploring regularization or post-processing to enforce/encourage sparsity and clarifying how algorithm handles sparsity ambiguity.
- De-permuting experiments with Dirichlet-perturbed matrices (Sec 4.1) feel detached from real MHMM parameter recovery from observed data.
- Recommend experiments that sample observations from MHMMs with realistic noise and attempt full parameter recovery to assess robustness.
- Section 4.2 lacks critical detail on underlying MHMM parameters; provide specific transition/emission parameter values (e.g., in an appendix).
- EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone (Table 1); this puzzling result requires investigation and explanation (local optima, sensitivity, etc.).
- Minor: Typo in proof (subscript of Λ likely should be K, not 2).
- Minor: Figures have excessively small font and low resolution; increase font size and improve figure quality.
- Overall: The approach is technically solid and promising but practical applicability and robustness concerns temper enthusiasm.
- For acceptance: novel spectral approach to MHMMs, strong theoretical justification, potential for faster learning than EM, and provides a novel EM initialization.
- Against acceptance: scalability/practicality concerns in data-limited settings, no sparsity guarantee in learned transitions, limited/insufficient experimental validation and parameter details, and unexplained EM initialization results.
