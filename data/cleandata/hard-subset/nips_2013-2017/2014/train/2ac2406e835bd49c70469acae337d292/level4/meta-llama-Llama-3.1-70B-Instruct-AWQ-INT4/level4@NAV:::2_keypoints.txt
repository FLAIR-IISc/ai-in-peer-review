- Proposes a novel method to learn a mixture of HMMs by reformulating MHMM as a special-case HMM and applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm with a stabilization step for parameter permutation.  
- Method assumes each component HMM’s transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Stabilization scheme for resolving parameter permutation is a clear strength and provides an intuitive solution.  
- Offers a thoughtful analysis of estimation noise using eigenvalue-based arguments (Equation (7)).  
- Notation is unclear and should be clarified for readability and rigor.  
- Authors should better engage related work, e.g., Chaganty and Liang (2013) on initializing EM with spectral estimates.  
- Experimental design is basic and could be strengthened.  
- Results presentation (Figure 4) is suboptimal; tables would improve readability.  
- Recommend reporting best EM accuracy (not average) to align with standard practice.  
- Ask to define 1J^T and clarify the structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e to support Corollary 1.  
- Request explicit definitions and dimensions for all introduced vectors and matrices.  
- Suggest adopting Hsu et al. (2009) notation (T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden states) for consistency.  
- Overall: an innovative algorithm combining a spectral HMM method with a novel stabilization step, but needs clearer notation, stronger related-work discussion, and improved experiments/results presentation.
