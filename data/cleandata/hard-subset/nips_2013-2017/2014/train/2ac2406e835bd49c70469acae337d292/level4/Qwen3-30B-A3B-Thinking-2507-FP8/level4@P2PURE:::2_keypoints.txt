- Proposes applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm (Anandkumar et al., 2012) to learn mixtures of HMMs by formulating an MHMM as a special-case HMM.
- Introduces a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, relying on the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.
- The proposed stabilization approach to circumvent permutation is interesting and intuitive.
- The analysis of estimation noise (Equation (7)) is noteworthy for its innovative use of eigenvalues.
- Manuscript suffers from notational inconsistencies; all vectors and matrices should be defined with dimensions at first mention.
- Corollary 1 needs clarification: define 1_J^T and describe lim_{ε→∞} \bar{A}^ε (a JK×JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns in each J×J block).
- Related work awareness is weak; cite prior studies such as Chaganty and Liang (2013) on initializing EM with spectral estimates.
- Adopt established HMM notation (e.g., use T for transition matrices, and n, m for observation/hidden state counts) instead of A, L, J.
- Experimental evaluation is superficial: Figure 4 is hard to interpret (replace with tables), and Table 1’s poor EM performance is surprising—report best (not average) EM accuracy and provide explanation.
