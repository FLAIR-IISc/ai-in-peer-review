This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). It demonstrates that an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied directly by formulating an MHMM as a special case of an HMM. A novel stabilization-based solution is proposed to address the parameter permutation problem, relying on the assumption that each HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.

Several areas require attention. The notation is occasionally unclear; the authors should define each vector and matrix, including its dimensions, upon its first introduction to significantly improve readability. Furthermore, the paper lacks sufficient awareness of related work. For example, the use of spectral estimates for initializing Expectation-Maximization (EM) has already been explored (Chaganty and Liang, 2013), and this work should be cited. The experiments are currently quite basic, and I strongly recommend presenting the results in a table format rather than the graphical format used in Figure 4.

Despite these concerns, the proposed scheme for mitigating the parameter permutation problem is both interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis of the algorithm’s performance in the presence of estimation noise, leading to an intriguing application of eigenvalues (Equation (7)).

Specific comments:

- Corollary 1 is initially confusing. Defining 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborating on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (specifically, that it is JK x JK block diagonal, with identical columns within each J x J block) would be beneficial.
- Generally, please define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced. This will greatly enhance the presentation.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret; please replace it with tables. Also, the poor performance of EM in Table 1 is unexpected. Typically, even with random initialization and sufficient iterations, EM outperforms spectral methods, despite the potential for converging to local optima. Reporting the best (rather than average) accuracy achieved by EM would provide a more standard and informative comparison.
- Minor point: conforming to the notation of Hsu et al. (2009) would improve consistency. Specifically, using T for transition matrices (rather than A) and n, m for the numbers of observations and hidden states (rather than L, J) might be helpful. 

In summary, this paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models, comprising an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models followed by a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation.
