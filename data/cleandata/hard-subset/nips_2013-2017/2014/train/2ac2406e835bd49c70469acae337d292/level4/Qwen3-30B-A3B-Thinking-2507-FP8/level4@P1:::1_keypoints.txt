- Paper proposes a Method-of-Moments spectral learning approach for MHMMs by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, resolving permutation ambiguity, and providing theory plus experiments.

- Experimental details are insufficient; synthetic model parameters should be specified to evaluate performance under varied conditions.

- The method requires estimating a full HMM before recovering block structure, which contradicts the sparsity-driven motivation for MHMMs; authors should clarify when their approach is advantageous.

- Key assumption that the estimated transition matrix will be (approximately) block-diagonal is questionable; need analysis of robustness when the matrix is only approximately block-diagonal or with limited observations.

- Section 4.1 experiments are unrealistic: depermutation was applied to a permuted transition matrix rather than learning from generated observation sequences; end-to-end sequence-based experiments are needed.

- Section 4.2 begins to address realism but lacks exact MHMM parameters (transition, emission, initial distributions); these should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).

- In Table 1, spectral learning sometimes outperforms EM initialized with spectral estimates, which is counterintuitive; authors must explain or diagnose possible experimental/implementation issues.

- Minor typo: in Section 3.2.1 the matrix subscript should be K, not 2.

- Minor presentation issue: figure text and numbers are too small to read.

- Overall: theoretical contribution is valuable and sound, but practical relevance is unclear without clearer justification of when the approach is preferable for large-scale or data-limited scenarios.
