- Summary: Proposes estimating a single HMM via spectral methods then resolving permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal mixture structure.

- Strength: Clear exposition and a clean algebraic approach; theory seems sound when block structure is strong and spectral estimates are accurate.

- Major 1 (Scope/experiments): Experimental section is too brief—expand empirical evaluation and include essential setup details or an appendix.

- Major 2 (Practicality): Two-step approach may fail with limited/noisy data—clarify regimes of success, failure cases, and sensitivity of permutation resolution to spectral errors.

- Major 3 (Sparsity/robustness): Spectral estimates can be dense; provide stability bounds or practical sparsity enforcement (e.g., L1, thresholding) and compare against raw depermutation.

- Major 4 (Synthetic setup Sec.4.1): Current Dirichlet perturbation does not mimic end-to-end learning—run experiments that generate sequences, estimate HMMs spectrally, then depermute to evaluate real estimation error propagation.

- Major 5 (Missing details Sec.4.2): Report mixture size, per-component state counts, transition/emission params, mixture weights, alphabet/model, sequence lengths/counts, metrics, runs, variance, and separation assumptions.

- Major 6 (EM vs spectral, Table 1): Clarify whether results are train or test, convergence criteria, iterations, regularization, performance metrics, and report train/test likelihoods plus variability across seeds.

- Minor 1 (Notation): Typo in Section 3.2.1—Λ subscript appears as K instead of 2.

- Minor 2 (Figures): Figures have unreadable text/numbers—enlarge fonts, line widths, and ensure legible axis labels and legends.

- Overall assessment: Attractive theoretical contribution but practical applicability is questionable; strengthen empirical validation and clarify applicable regimes to improve the paper.
