- Summary: Proposes a spectral algorithm to learn mixtures of HMMs by estimating a single HMM then resolving permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal structure.

- Major 1: Paper is well written but experimental section needs more elaboration.

- Major 2: Practicality concern: algorithm requires accurate full HMM estimation before depermuting, which may be impractical; authors should specify applicability conditions and limitations.

- Major 3: Doubt that the learned transition matrix will be (approximately) sparse in practice; no mechanism or guarantee is provided to enforce sparsity.

- Major 4: Experiments insufficiently reflect realistic noise—should evaluate parameter recovery from sampled observation data of HMM mixtures, not just perturbed matrices.

- Major 5: Experiment 4.2 lacks crucial details about the underlying mixture parameters (transition and observation matrices); these should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).

- Major 6: Question why EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone; EM should typically improve initial estimates.

- Minor 1: Typo in proof Section 3.2.1: subscript of Λ likely should be K, not 2.

- Minor 2: Figures have text and numbers that are too small to read comfortably.

- Overall: Interesting ideas and strong theory, but concerns remain about practical effectiveness on real-world datasets.
