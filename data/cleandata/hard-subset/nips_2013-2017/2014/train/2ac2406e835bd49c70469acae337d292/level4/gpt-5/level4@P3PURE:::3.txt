This paper presents a spectral method for learning a mixture of HMMs and focuses on the key question of how to “de-permute” the results.

Technically, the proposed algorithm appears to be a fairly direct variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang (HKZ) spectral method. The main difference is that the recovered transition matrix can be an arbitrary permutation of the true one, effectively mixing the components. The paper’s primary contribution is a de-permutation procedure to resolve this issue, which seems to perform reasonably well.

Presentation could be improved. There are several typos and minor errors that hinder readability. For example: “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.” Please proofread more carefully.

The paper would also benefit from a stronger contextualization within the spectral learning literature, including a discussion of related work such as:
- Chaganty and Liang. Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions. ICML, 2013.

In summary, the paper addresses an important practical issue—how to de-permute components in spectral learning of mixture HMMs—on top of a largely standard HKZ-style framework. Strengthening the connections to prior work and improving the writing quality would make the contribution clearer.
