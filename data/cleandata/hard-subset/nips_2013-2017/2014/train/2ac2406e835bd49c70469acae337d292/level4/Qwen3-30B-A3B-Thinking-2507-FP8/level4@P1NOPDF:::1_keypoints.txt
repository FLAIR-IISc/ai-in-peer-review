- Well-written paper but experimental section lacks sufficient detail and depth to support claims.  
- Method models a mixture of HMMs as a single HMM, contradicting the sparsity motivation and likely impractical with limited/noisy real-world data; authors must specify conditions where the algorithm is viable or fails.  
- No argument or proof that spectral learning yields sparse (or near-sparse) transition matrices; unclear whether full transition models can approximate block-diagonal structure.  
- Sec 4.1 experiment is unrealistic: perturbing the transition matrix with Dirichlet noise does not test learning from observation sequences; need experiments that generate sequences from a mixture of HMMs and recover all parameters.  
- Sec 4.2 omits critical details (true transition/emission matrices); these should be provided (e.g., in an appendix) to assess experiment difficulty and results.  
- Table 1 anomaly: spectral learning alone outperforming EM initialized with spectral estimates is counterintuitive and requires explanation.  
- Minor typo: subscript of Λ in Sec 3.2.1 should be K, not 2.  
- Presentation issue: figure text and numbers are too small for readability.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution and novel depermutation, but lack of sparsity guarantees and realistic experimental validation undermines practical applicability for learning mixture HMMs from real data.
