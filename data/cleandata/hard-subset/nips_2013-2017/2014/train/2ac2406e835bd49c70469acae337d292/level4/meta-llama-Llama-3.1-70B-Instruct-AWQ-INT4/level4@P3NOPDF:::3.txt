This paper proposes a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), which is a variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm. However, the transition matrix recovered by the algorithm is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, mixing the various components. To address this issue, the authors introduce a de-permutation algorithm that appears to work reasonably well.

The paper would benefit from a more thorough proofreading, as there are occasional typos and minor errors. For example, the sentence "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy" contains a grammatical error. A more rigorous proofreading process would help to eliminate these mistakes and improve the overall clarity of the paper.

One of the main contributions of this work is the de-permutation algorithm, which is a crucial component in learning a mixture of HMMs. The authors do a good job of explaining the algorithm and providing experimental results to demonstrate its effectiveness. However, the paper could be improved by providing more context and relating the work to other relevant papers in the field.

For instance, the authors could discuss the relationship between their work and other spectral methods, such as the one proposed by Chaganty and Liang (2013) for estimating mixtures of linear regressions. Providing more background information and comparisons to other methods would help to situate the work in the broader context of machine learning and spectral methods.

In terms of the paper's organization and presentation, the authors do a good job of explaining the technical details of the algorithm and providing experimental results. However, some of the sections could be reorganized or expanded to improve the flow of the paper. For example, the introduction could be expanded to provide more background information on HMMs and the challenges of learning a mixture of HMMs.

Overall, this is a solid paper that makes a useful contribution to the field of machine learning. With some revisions to address the issues mentioned above, the paper has the potential to be even stronger.
