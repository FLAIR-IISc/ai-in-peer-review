1. Paper proposes spectral learning for mixtures of HMMs plus a permutation resolution to recover block-diagonal transition structure.  
2. Well-written and clear, but experiments need more detail and expansion.  
3. Algorithm estimates a full HMM before recovering sparsity, which may be impractical for many real-world scenarios; clarify applicable vs. challenging cases.  
4. Doubts that spectral methods will reliably produce transition matrices close to sparse/block-diagonal, especially with noisy data.  
5. Experimental setup in Section 4.1 tests depermutation with Dirichlet noise rather than full parameter recovery from observations, which is not realistic.  
6. Section 4.2 lacks details on the underlying mixture parameters (transition/observation matrices), hindering evaluation; provide specifics.  
7. Table 1 is puzzling: EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone, which contradicts expectations.  
8. Minor: typo in Section 3.2.1—subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.  
9. Minor: figures have text and numbers that are too small and hard to read.  
10. Overall: interesting theoretical contributions but concerns remain about practical applicability to real-world datasets.
