This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The key idea is to reduce an MHMM to a standard HMM so that an existing spectral method (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied directly. The paper then introduces a stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation, based on the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1. The scheme for addressing parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The analysis under estimation noise is also appreciated and leads to a neat use of eigenvalues (see Equation (7)) within the algorithm.

However, there are several issues that should be addressed to improve clarity, completeness, and empirical evaluation:

- Notation and definitions: The paper has notational inconsistencies and missing definitions. Please define every vector/matrix (including whether it is a row/column) and state its dimensions at first use. This will significantly improve readability.

- Corollary 1: I was initially confused by the terms. Please define 1_J^T explicitly as the row vector of length J filled with ones. Also elaborate on the structure of lim_{e→∞} (bar A)^e: it is a JK-by-JK block-diagonal matrix whose J-by-J diagonal blocks each have identical columns (i.e., each block is rank-1 with all columns equal).

- Related work: The paper should better situate itself within prior literature. For example, initializing EM with spectral estimates has been explored (Chaganty and Liang, 2013). Please include and discuss such references, and clarify how your approach compares (both conceptually and empirically).

- Experiments: The current experiments are basic and the presentation hinders interpretation. Figure 4 is difficult to read; please replace it with tables reporting numerical results. More importantly, the EM baseline appears unusually weak in Table 1. Typically, with enough iterations—even from random initializations—EM outperforms pure spectral estimates despite local optima. Standard practice is to report the best (not only the average) accuracy across multiple EM restarts; please include the best-case EM performance and ensure that EM is run to reasonable convergence with sufficient restarts. This will make the comparison fair and more informative.

- Notational conformity: For consistency with established spectral HMM literature (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009), consider using T for transition matrices (instead of A), and n, m for the numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J).

- Algorithmic framing: While the reduction to an HMM and use of Anandkumar et al. (2012) is straightforward, the added stabilization step is the main novelty. It would help to more clearly delineate what is directly inherited from prior work versus what is new here, including any theoretical guarantees or conditions specific to the permutation-resolution step.

Overall, the paper proposes an MHMM learning procedure that applies an off-the-shelf spectral HMM method followed by a stabilization step to undo parameter permutation. The idea is natural and potentially useful, but the paper would benefit from clearer notation and definitions, a more thorough discussion of related work, and a stronger, more carefully reported empirical evaluation.
