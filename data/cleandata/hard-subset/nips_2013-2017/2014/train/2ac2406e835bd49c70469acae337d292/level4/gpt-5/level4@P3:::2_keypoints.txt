- Proposes spectral (method-of-moments) learning for mixtures of HMMs by reducing an MHMM with K components of M states each to a single HMM with MK states and block-diagonal transitions.  
- Key challenge: spectral estimates of the global HMM are correct only up to an unknown permutation mixing states across components.  
- Novelty: a de-permutation strategy using powers of the transition matrix and a rank-K reconstruction, plus an eigenvalue “longevity” heuristic (Eq. 7) to identify K and recover component assignments.  
- Relation to prior work: reduction to a single HMM and spectral HMM learning are standard; contribution centers on the de-permutation and longevity statistic.  
- Strength: clear high-level idea to recast MHMMs as block-diagonal HMMs and exploit spectral structure to undo permutations.  
- Strength: computationally attractive—parameter recovery after moment computation is data-size independent, scalable for large datasets.  
- Strength: practical post-processing via clustering of columns after rank-K reconstruction shown to work under moderate noise.  
- Strength: eigenvalue longevity is an intriguing, potentially broadly useful model-selection heuristic for spectral methods.  
- Weakness: theoretical support is incomplete—Conjecture 1, noise tolerance claims, and borrowed sample-complexity bounds lack formal proofs.  
- Weakness: identifiability/stability assumptions (ergodicity, aperiodicity, spectral gaps) are not fully specified.  
- Weakness: notation and clarity issues impede readability—many matrices/vectors lack dimensions and some symbols are inconsistent.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—small synthetic settings, narrow real-data test, and weak/under-reported EM baselines.  
- Weakness: MK < L requirement forces a feature expansion in real data; potential biases or invalidity of that transformation are not discussed.  
- Technical soundness: reduction and noiseless de-permutation are sound, but noisy-case results depend on unproven conjectures and heuristics.  
- Empirical support: shows de-permutation can work at moderate sequence lengths and spectral initialization helps EM, but needs stronger baselines, more repeats, and clearer reporting.  
- Clarity suggestions: define every vector/matrix with dimensions, fix inconsistent symbols (e.g., 1J), and explicitly state Corollary 1’s block structure.  
- Notation suggestion: align notation with Hsu et al. (2009) (e.g., T for transitions, m/n for hidden/observed states) to ease comparison.  
- Presentation suggestion: improve Figure 4 readability and add tables summarizing accuracies and runtimes across N and T.  
- Originality: modest—main novelty is the specific spectral de-permutation and longevity statistic rather than the MHMM→HMM reduction.  
- Significance: potentially useful for scalable MHMM learning and EM initialization, but impact limited without stronger guarantees and broader experiments (larger K,M,L; varied emissions; unbalanced mixtures).  
- Specific recommendation: clarify assumptions that each Ak is irreducible/aperiodic and discuss effects of close eigenvalues on recoverability and longevity.  
- Specific recommendation: give practical guidance for choosing γ and exponent ranges in Eq. (7) and compare longevity to spectral-gap or information-criteria methods.  
- Specific recommendation: report runtimes and memory for moments, spectral decomposition, de-permutation, and EM; and improve EM baselines (best-of-R restarts, convergence diagnostics, varied initializations).  
- Specific recommendation: justify polynomial feature map for real data, discuss relaxing MK < L (multi-view or tensor methods), and test additional datasets (speech, motion capture, discrete emissions).  
- Specific recommendation: comment on extension to unequal M across components and methods to detect this case.  
- Related-work suggestion: expand comparisons to prior MHMM learning/clustering and spectral initializations for latent-variable models (e.g., Smyth 1997; Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, intuitive de-permutation strategy; computationally appealing alternative to EM; useful practical initialization for EM.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete theoretical foundations under noise, limited empirical evaluation and weak baseline reporting, and clarity/notation problems.  
- Overall assessment: promising and neat idea for resolving permutation ambiguity in spectral MHMM learning, but needs stronger theory, clearer exposition, and more thorough experiments to be fully convincing.
