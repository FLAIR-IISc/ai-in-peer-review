- Proposes a spectral learning method to resolve permutation ambiguity in method-of-moments estimation for Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs).  
- Observes MHMMs can be represented as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling spectral HMM learning.  
- Identifies the core problem that this representation yields parameters permuted across components, preventing recovery of individual HMMs.  
- Main contribution: a novel depermutation algorithm that leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover block structure.  
- Introduces spectral longevity (Definition 3) and shows conditions under which the number of clusters K can be identified.  
- Shows a depermutation mapping can be estimated from the eigendecomposition of a noisy transition matrix; provides Lemmas 2–3 and Conjecture 1 as theoretical justification.  
- Strength: original and technically meaningful solution to an open problem in MoM for MHMMs, extending prior mixture-model spectral methods.  
- Strength: significant practical impact—scalable alternative to EM with computational cost independent of data size; experiments show comparable accuracy and substantial speedups, and utility as EM initialization.  
- Strength: clear organization, concise executable algorithm, logical progression from model equivalence to depermutation.  
- Weakness: Conjecture 1 lacks formal proof and the cited error bound justification for MHMMs is insufficient.  
- Weakness: real-data experiment uses an unexplained cubic feature transform to increase L, which may alter problem structure and is inadequately analyzed.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited (e.g., comparisons with EM shown mainly for K=2), leaving open performance questions for larger K.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—minor grammatical errors, unclear phrasing, and a need for intuitive explanation of spectral longevity and contrasts with alternative depermutation approaches.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel with compelling computational advantages, but requires stronger theoretical justification and more thorough experimental validation; recommendation: Weak Accept.
