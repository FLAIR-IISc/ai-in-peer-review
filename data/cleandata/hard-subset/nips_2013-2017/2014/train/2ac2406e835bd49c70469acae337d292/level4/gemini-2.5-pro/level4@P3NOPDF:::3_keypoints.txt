- Contribution: Proposes a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs with a novel "de-permutation" algorithm to recover individual transition matrices.
- Technical challenge: Direct application of HKZ spectral methods yields permuted transition matrices that conflate component transitions.
- Methodology: Builds on Hsu, Kakade & Zhang’s spectral HMM approach and introduces post-processing to disentangle components.
- Empirical results: Authors provide empirical evidence indicating the method performs effectively.
- Strength: Tackles a significant problem with many applications and offers a promising spectral alternative to EM with potential computational/theoretical benefits.
- Strength: Correctly identifies permutation ambiguity as the key bottleneck and proposes a reasonable, novel solution.
- Weakness: Lacks sufficient contextualization and comparison with prior spectral mixture-work; needs discussion of related techniques (e.g., Chaganty & Liang ICML 2013).
- Weakness: Presentation requires careful proofreading—grammatical errors and typos occasionally obscure meaning.
- Conclusion: Core idea is sound and well-motivated but would be substantially improved by better literature context and polishing the manuscript.
