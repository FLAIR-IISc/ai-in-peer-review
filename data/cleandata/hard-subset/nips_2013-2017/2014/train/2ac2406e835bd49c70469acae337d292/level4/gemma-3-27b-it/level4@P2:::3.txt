This paper introduces a spectral method for learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core challenge addressed is the permutation ambiguity inherent in Method of Moments (MoM) estimation, which mixes the individual HMM components. The authors propose a depermutation algorithm to resolve this, building upon existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs, notably the work of Hsu, Kakade & Zhang (2013).

The paper clearly identifies a significant obstacle to applying MoM to MHMMs – the inability to directly recover the parameters of individual HMMs due to permutation. Their approach of leveraging the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to estimate a depermutation mapping is a reasonable and potentially valuable contribution. The experimental results on both synthetic and real data suggest the method performs well, and the comparison to EM, highlighting computational speed advantages, is a positive aspect.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an important problem:** The permutation ambiguity in MHMM learning using MoM is a key issue, and this work provides a direct approach to tackling it.
*   **Novelty:** While building on prior work in spectral learning for HMMs, applying this technique to depermute MHMM components represents a novel extension.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The authors emphasize the computational advantages of MoM, making MHMM learning feasible for larger datasets where EM may struggle.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the practical viability of the proposed algorithm.
*   **Clear Presentation of the Model:** The mathematical formulation of MHMMs and the derivation of the spectral properties are presented in a reasonably clear manner.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Contextualization:** The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly regarding spectral methods for mixture models. While Hsu, Kakade & Zhang (2013) are cited, the connection to Chaganty and Liang (2013) – which also addresses spectral learning for mixtures – would be valuable. A discussion of how this work differs from or builds upon these previous approaches is missing.
*   **Typos and Proofreading:** Several minor typographical errors and grammatical inconsistencies detract from the paper's polish. More careful proofreading is needed (e.g., the example provided: "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.").
*   **Conjecture Reliance:** A significant portion of the theoretical justification relies on a conjecture regarding the spectral properties of the transition matrix. While conjectures are acceptable, a more rigorous analysis or further discussion of the conditions under which the conjecture holds would strengthen the work.
*  **Real Data Experiment details:** The real data experiments lack sufficient detail to fully assess the effectiveness of the approach. For example, the rationale behind using a cubic polynomial feature transformation is not fully explained, and the impact of this transformation on the results is unclear.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a genuine and important challenge in MHMM learning.
*   The proposed approach appears to be effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results.
*   The computational benefits of the MoM-based approach are significant.
*   The work extends existing spectral learning techniques to a new and relevant problem.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to related work in spectral mixture modeling.
*   The reliance on a conjecture weakens the theoretical foundation.
*   The clarity of the presentation is hampered by minor errors and a lack of detailed explanation in some areas.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of MHMM learning. The proposed spectral depermutation algorithm addresses a critical issue in applying MoM to this model. While some weaknesses exist, particularly regarding contextualization and theoretical rigor, the potential benefits of the approach and the positive experimental results make it a strong candidate for acceptance.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **weak accept** contingent upon the authors addressing the following:

1.  **Expand the Related Work section:** Provide a more comprehensive overview of existing spectral methods for mixture models, explicitly comparing and contrasting this work with papers like Chaganty and Liang (2013).
2.  **Address the Conjecture:** Either provide a proof of the conjecture or discuss the conditions under which it is likely to hold.
3.  **Proofread Carefully:** Thoroughly proofread the paper to eliminate typographical errors and grammatical inconsistencies.
4.  **Expand Real Data Experiment Details:** Provide a more detailed explanation of the data preprocessing steps (e.g., the rationale for the polynomial feature transformation) and a more thorough analysis of the results.
