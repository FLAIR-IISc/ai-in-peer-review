- Summary: Proposes a spectral method for mixtures of HMMs that adds a de-permutation step to align component parameters; empirically effective.  
- Strength: Addresses a clear and relevant problem of permutation ambiguity when extending spectral HMM learning to mixtures.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple and computationally appealing—builds on existing spectral machinery with a lightweight alignment procedure.  
- Strength: Promising empirical behavior—de-permutation appears to work well in practice and could offer a practical alternative to slow/unstable EM.  
- Weakness: Core estimation largely mirrors Hsu–Kakade–Zhang, so novelty is mainly the de-permutation heuristic.  
- Weakness: Theoretical foundations for de-permutation are unclear; lacks formal identifiability conditions and finite-sample/perturbation guarantees.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is insufficiently strong—needs comparisons to EM, variational methods, and other spectral mixture techniques plus ablations.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues (typos, unclear phrasing) reduce readability and clarity.  
- Clarity: Proofread and fix grammatical errors (example provided).  
- Clarity: Explicitly state identifiability assumptions (full-rank emissions, minimality, transition nondegeneracy, weight/separation bounds).  
- Clarity: Precisely define the permutation ambiguity (per-state, per-component, or global) and how the algorithm achieves consistent global alignment.  
- Clarity: Provide pseudocode for the de-permutation algorithm with IO, objective, complexity, and any matching solver used.  
- Clarity: Detail moment construction for mixtures, whitening/truncation, rank selection, and regularization for small eigen-gaps.  
- Clarity: Give practical guidance on initialization, choosing number of components/states, truncation sensitivity, and preventing degeneracies.  
- Empirical eval: Add comparisons to EM for mixture-of-HMMs, single-HMM baselines, and tensor-based spectral methods.  
- Empirical eval: Report quantitative metrics (parameter recovery error modulo permutation, held-out predictive log-likelihood, sequence prediction accuracy).  
- Empirical eval: Include robustness analyses (sample size, components/states, sequence length, state overlap, model misspecification).  
- Empirical eval: Provide ablations (without de-permutation, random alignment, alternative similarity measures) and report runtime/memory.  
- Related work: Better situate relative to Hsu–Kakade–Zhang, Chaganty & Liang (spectral mixtures alignment), and tensor/multi-view spectral methods.  
- Related work: Clarify what is novel (specific de-permutation strategy) vs. standard tools borrowed (moments, whitening, eigen-decomposition).  
- Questions: What conditions guarantee correct de-permutation and are there perturbation bounds under sampling noise?  
- Questions: Is the alignment deterministic and globally consistent; does it require knowing the number of components and how sensitive is it to misspecification?  
- Questions: How does the method scale with number of components and states; provide computational complexity in relevant terms.  
- Questions: How are eigenvalue thresholds/truncation ranks chosen in practice and are there automated criteria?  
- Minor: Unify notation and introduce symbols before use.  
- Minor: Replace vague terms like “energy” with precise definitions (e.g., proportion of spectral mass).  
- Minor: Discuss limitations and potential failure modes (e.g., highly overlapping components).  
- Overall assessment: Addresses an important practical gap with an incremental contribution; currently borderline due to limited theory and evaluation but promising if substantially strengthened.
