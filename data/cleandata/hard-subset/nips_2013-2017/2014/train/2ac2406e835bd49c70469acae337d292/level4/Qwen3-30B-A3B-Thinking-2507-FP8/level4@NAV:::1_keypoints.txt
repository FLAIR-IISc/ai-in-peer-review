- Summary: Proposes a spectral algorithm for single HMMs plus a method to resolve permutation ambiguity to recover a block-diagonal mixture structure.
- Major 1: Paper is well-written but the experimental section needs more elaboration and missing specifics.
- Major 2: Method assumes a mixture can be expressed as a single HMM, but estimating a full HMM before exploiting sparsity is impractical; clarify viability conditions and failure modes.
- Major 3: Question whether the spectral method can guarantee (near) sparsity in the recovered transition matrix under realistic noise.
- Major 4: Experiments use permuted matrices with Dirichlet noise (depermutation) rather than learning from observation data; need experiments generating observations from true mixtures.
- Major 5: Section 4.2 omits critical true-parameter details (transition/observation matrices); provide parameter specifics to evaluate difficulty and results.
- Major 6: EM initialized with spectral sometimes underperforms spectral alone—needs explanation since EM should not degrade results.
- Minor 1: Typo: subscript of Λ in the matrix in Section 3.2.1 should be K, not 2.
- Minor 2: Text and numbers in figures are excessively small.
- Overall: Interesting theoretical contributions and methodology, but approach likely won’t generalize to real-world datasets due to reliance on full HMM estimation before exploiting sparsity.
