

The authors present a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs. However, the core algorithm constitutes a trivial variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade & Zhang approach, with the key limitation that the recovered transition matrix is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, resulting in permuted component labels. While the proposed de-permutation algorithm appears effective in practice, its necessity and theoretical guarantees warrant clearer justification.  

The paper contains several minor grammatical errors and typos that undermine readability. For instance:  
- "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." → Should read: "For each eigenvalue, identify the eigenvectors that capture all the energy."  
Proofreading should be strengthened to eliminate such errors.  

Furthermore, the work would benefit from stronger contextualization. The authors do not sufficiently connect their method to prior spectral approaches for mixture models. For example, Chaganty and Liang (ICML 2013) developed spectral techniques for mixtures of linear regressions, which shares conceptual similarities with the current problem. Explicitly comparing the HMM mixture setting to this line of work—and clarifying how the proposed de-permutation step advances beyond existing spectral frameworks—would significantly strengthen the contribution. The paper’s positioning as a solution to "who to de-permute" is unclear; it should instead articulate *how* the de-permutation procedure resolves the permutation ambiguity inherent in spectral learning for mixtures, and why this is non-trivial compared to standard HMM estimation.  

The technical argument regarding the permutation issue is valid but undersold. The authors should explicitly state that the Hsu et al. algorithm recovers the transition matrix up to row/column permutation, and that their de-permutation step is essential to recover the *correct* component structure (not merely a relabeled version). Without this, the method’s utility for downstream tasks (e.g., state sequence prediction) remains ambiguous.  

In summary, while the core idea is plausible, the paper requires substantial refinement:  
1. **Technical clarity**: Explicitly define the permutation ambiguity and justify the de-permutation step’s necessity.  
2. **Context**: Anchor the work within existing spectral mixture models (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, ICML 2013) and clarify how it differs.  
3. **Proofreading**: Correct grammatical errors and typos to ensure professionalism.  

The current draft does not convincingly demonstrate how this approach advances the state of the art beyond a straightforward extension of Hsu et al. The contribution would be significantly clearer if the authors more precisely articulated the problem the de-permutation step solves, and how it enables meaningful learning of the underlying HMM mixture structure.
