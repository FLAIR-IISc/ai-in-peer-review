- Introduces a spectral depermutation method to learn Mixture of HMMs, addressing permutation ambiguity in Method of Moments estimation.  
- Leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix, building on Hsu, Kakade & Zhang (2013).  
- Identifies permutation ambiguity as a key obstacle preventing recovery of individual HMM parameters.  
- Proposes a reasonable and potentially valuable depermutation algorithm to resolve component mixing.  
- Demonstrates computational efficiency advantages over EM, making MHMM learning feasible on larger datasets.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data indicate practical viability of the approach.  
- Mathematical formulation and derivation of spectral properties are presented clearly.  
- Limited contextualization: the Related Work section should better cover spectral methods for mixtures, e.g., Chaganty & Liang (2013).  
- Several typographical and grammatical errors reduce polish and readability.  
- Significant theoretical reliance on an unproven conjecture about transition-matrix spectral properties.  
- Real-data experiments lack detail (e.g., unclear rationale and impact of cubic polynomial feature transformation).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, appears effective empirically, offers computational benefits, and extends spectral learning to MHMMs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete comparison to related work, weakened theory due to conjecture reliance, and clarity issues from minor errors and omitted explanations.  
- Overall: worthwhile contribution with promising results but needs stronger contextualization and theoretical support.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, contingent on expanding related work, addressing/proving the conjecture, careful proofreading, and providing more real-data experiment details.
