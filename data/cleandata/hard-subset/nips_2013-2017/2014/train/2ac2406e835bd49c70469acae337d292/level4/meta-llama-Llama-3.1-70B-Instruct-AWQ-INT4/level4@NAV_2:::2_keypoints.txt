- Paper proposes a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a special HMM and using existing HMM spectral methods.
- Introduces a stabilization-based solution to parameter permutation via the assumption that each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.
- Provides analysis under estimation noise, yielding an interesting role for eigenvalues in the algorithm.
- The work is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental results that support the claims.
- Experimental evaluation is basic; results are presented graphically (Figure 4) and would benefit from table format and reporting best (not just average) EM accuracy.
- Notation is problematic in places; vectors/matrices should be defined with dimensions when first introduced.
- The stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation is an original and notable contribution.
- Related work coverage needs strengthening—specifically cite and discuss approaches like initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang 2013) and check recent NIPS proceedings for similar papers.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, making the material accessible with minor presentation fixes.
- Results are important and likely to be useful to other researchers, advancing the state of the art.
- Pros for acceptance: novel algorithm, clear organization, technical soundness, balanced evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.
- Cons for acceptance: notation issues, basic experiments, omission of best-EM results, and incomplete awareness of related work.
