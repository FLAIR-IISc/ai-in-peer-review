- Problem & approach: Learn a mixture of HMMs by expressing it as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition matrix and applying the Method of Moments.  
- Learning pipeline: Use an existing MoM spectral algorithm to recover parameters up to a permutation ambiguity.  
- Permutation resolution: Propose exploiting spectral properties of the global transition matrix to de-permute and recover individual HMMs.  
- Writing & experiments: Paper is well-written and has informative experimental results.  
- Practicality concern: Algorithm requires accurately estimating the full HMM then de-permuting, which may be infeasible for large problems or limited training data.  
- Noise modeling issue: Experimental noise model (Dirichlet-permuted transitions) does not realistically reflect noise when learning MHMMs from observed data.  
- Incomplete experimental details: Section 4.2’s EM comparison lacks critical setup details (transition/observation parameters), hindering evaluation.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides solid theoretical framework for resolving permutation ambiguity.  
- Strengths summary: Novel MoM approach for MHMMs, theoretical permutation-resolution, and informative experiments.  
- Weaknesses summary: Practical scalability/data-efficiency concerns, inadequate noise treatment, and incomplete experimental description.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions requesting clarity on practicality, noise handling, and more detailed experiments.  
- Overall rating: 7/10.
