- Summary: Proposes using spectral learning to estimate an equivalent single HMM then de-permuting its transition matrix to recover block-diagonal mixture structure.

- Major 1: Experiments are insufficiently detailed and need expansion to assess robustness and practical utility.

- Major 2: Concern that estimating a full HMM first may be impractical for large state spaces or limited data; request conditions (sample size, noise, separation) where the method is appropriate.

- Major 3: Questions whether spectral methods will produce (approximate) sparse/block-sparse transition matrices and how to guarantee or encourage sparsity.

- Major 4: Experimental design is unrealistic—should evaluate end-to-end by sampling observation sequences from mixtures and learning parameters to reflect observation noise and estimation error.

- Major 5: Section 4.2 lacks critical mixture parameter details (component counts, states per component, sparsity, emissions, separation); request full specification (e.g., in an appendix).

- Major 6: In Table 1, EM initialized with spectral sometimes degrades performance; request clarification of initialization, stopping criteria, objective being optimized, and any regularization.

- Minor 1: Typo in proof: subscript of Λ in the bottom-right corner should be K, not 2.

- Minor 2: Figures have unreadably small text/lines; increase font sizes and line widths.

- Overall: Interesting theoretical contributions but practical applicability on real datasets is unclear without more realistic, well-documented experiments.
