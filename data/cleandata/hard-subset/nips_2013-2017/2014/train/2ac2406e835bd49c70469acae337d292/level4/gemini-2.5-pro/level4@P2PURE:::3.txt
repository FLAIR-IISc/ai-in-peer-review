This paper presents a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs).

The core algorithm is a variant of the spectral learning method by Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang. The primary technical challenge, which this paper addresses, is that a direct application of this method recovers the correct transition matrices for the HMM components but with their labels arbitrarily permuted. The authors' main contribution is a novel de-permutation algorithm to resolve this component ambiguity, which appears to perform well empirically.

The paper could be strengthened by better contextualizing the work within the existing literature. For instance, a discussion of its relationship to similar spectral approaches for mixture models, such as the work by Chaganty and Liang on mixtures of linear regressions (ICML 2013), would be beneficial.

Finally, the manuscript requires more careful proofreading to address several minor typographical errors. For example, one sentence reads: "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy."
