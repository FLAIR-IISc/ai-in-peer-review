- Proposes a spectral learning method for Mixture of HMMs (MHMMs) exploiting the block-diagonal global transition matrix to resolve permutation ambiguity.
- Extends Hsu et al. (2012) spectral HMM method to MHMMs and addresses permutation resolution specifically for mixtures.
- Novel depermutation approach based on spectral analysis of the global transition matrix tailored to MHMMs.
- Demonstrates up to 100× computational speedup over EM and shows the method is an effective EM initializer on real data.
- Technical approach is sound with theoretical justification via Lemmas 2–3 and Conjecture 1.
- Conjecture 1 lacks a formal proof and relies on sample-complexity arguments; noise analysis in Section 3.2.2 is not rigorously established.
- Experiments on synthetic data and the UCI character dataset support the claims.
- Exposition is generally clear with well-structured model and algorithm descriptions; figures effectively illustrate spectral properties.
- Minor notational inconsistencies and typos reduce readability (e.g., "πkνk" vs. "π_k ν_k", "identify of eigenvalues").
- The block-diagonal insight is straightforward but the depermutation solution is non-trivial and differentiates this work from prior spectral methods.
- Addresses a key computational bottleneck in MHMM learning and has practical significance for sequence clustering and EM initialization.
- Could inspire extensions to other hierarchical latent models.
- Pro-acceptance: solves a known problem with theoretical grounding, provides large computational gains without sacrificing accuracy, and is validated on synthetic and real data.
- Con-acceptance: insufficient comparison to related spectral methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013), Conjecture 1 needs stronger justification, and minor technical errors remain.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically significant contribution that meets NIPS standards and adds value to the spectral learning toolkit.
