- Proposes a spectral Method of Moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs, addressing permutation ambiguity in recovered transition matrices via a spectral depermutation algorithm.
- Builds on Hsu, Kakade & Zhang (2013) spectral HMM algorithm and extends it to the mixture setting; extension is straightforward but significant for applicability.
- Clearly identifies permutation ambiguity as the core problem when applying MoM to MHMMs and leverages spectral properties of the global transition matrix to resolve it.
- Demonstrates empirical validity on synthetic and real datasets, showing competitive performance against EM and usefulness for EM initialization.
- Provides theoretical analysis and conjectures (Defs 1 & 3, Conj.1) that ground the depermutation approach.
- Strength: addresses a key practical challenge in applying MoM to MHMMs with a novel, motivated solution.
- Strength: clear exposition of the problem, solution, and relation to existing HMM spectral learning techniques.
- Strength: empirical validation includes both synthetic and real-world experiments and comparisons to EM.
- Strength: theoretical grounding via spectral property analysis and stated conditions for success.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental rather than a radical departure from prior spectral methods.
- Weakness: mathematical spectral conditions are complex and lack intuitive explanation and discussion of practical implications.
- Weakness: manuscript contains typographical errors and minor imprecisions that reduce polish.
- Weakness: related work is incomplete — should cite Chaganty & Liang (2013) and other spectral mixture-methods.
- Weakness: real-data experiment depends on a feature transformation; impact on results and performance on raw data are not fully explored.
- Argument for acceptance: offers a practical, theoretically grounded solution to a non-trivial MHMM learning problem with promising results.
- Argument for acceptance: method is well-motivated and builds appropriately on established techniques.
- Argument for rejection: contribution may be seen as incremental and the complex spectral conditions could limit applicability.
- Argument for rejection: manuscript requires polishing to address clarity and minor errors.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: proofread to fix typos, expand related work (include Chaganty & Liang), provide more intuitive explanation of spectral conditions and practical implications, and discuss the effect of feature transformations on real-data results.
