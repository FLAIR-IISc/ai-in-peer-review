The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The core idea is to leverage a Method of Moments (MoM) approach, which offers computational advantages for large datasets, but introduces a permutation ambiguity in the learned parameters. The key contribution lies in a novel depermutation algorithm, utilizing the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, to resolve this ambiguity and recover the parameters of individual HMMs. 

This work builds upon existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs, notably the Hsu, Kakade & Zhang [1] algorithm, and extends it to the more complex MHMM setting. The challenge of permutation ambiguity is well-identified, and the proposed spectral approach offers a practical solution. The paper demonstrates the validity of the method through both synthetic and real data experiments, highlighting its computational efficiency compared to traditional Expectation-Maximization (EM) methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a practical challenge:** The permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM-based MHMM learning is a significant obstacle, and the authors provide a concrete and seemingly effective solution.
* **Computational efficiency:**  The MoM approach, combined with the spectral depermutation, offers a compelling alternative to computationally expensive EM algorithms, particularly for large datasets.
* **Clear exposition of the key idea:** The paper clearly explains the connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices, providing a solid foundation for the proposed algorithm.
* **Experimental validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real data demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the approach and provide a comparison with EM.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited novelty in core spectral technique:** While the application to MHMMs is novel, the depermutation algorithm itself relies on established spectral methods.  The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to existing spectral depermutation techniques (if any exist) and a more in-depth justification of the specific spectral properties exploited.
* **Minor typos and clarity issues:** The review noted a few minor typos (e.g., "identify of eigenvalues…").  While not critical, careful proofreading would improve clarity.
* **Missing related work:** The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work.  For example, Chaganty and Liang [2] on spectral experts for mixture models offers a related perspective. The connection between this work and approaches using tensor decompositions [3] could also be discussed.
* **Conjecture without full proof:** While understandable within the scope of a conference paper, the key conjecture regarding the estimation accuracy (Conjecture 1) lacks a complete proof, potentially weakening the theoretical justification of the algorithm.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem in MHMM learning.
* The proposed algorithm is a significant improvement over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency.
* The experimental results provide strong evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The core spectral technique is not entirely novel, and the paper does not fully explore the connection to existing work.
* The theoretical justification relies on a conjecture.
* Minor clarity issues and typos detract from the overall polish. 

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid contribution that addresses a practical challenge in MHMM learning. While the core spectral technique isn't entirely novel, the application to this specific problem and the development of the depermutation algorithm are valuable.  The computational advantages demonstrated in the experiments are compelling. I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the minor weaknesses identified above, particularly through improved proofreading and a more thorough discussion of related work.
