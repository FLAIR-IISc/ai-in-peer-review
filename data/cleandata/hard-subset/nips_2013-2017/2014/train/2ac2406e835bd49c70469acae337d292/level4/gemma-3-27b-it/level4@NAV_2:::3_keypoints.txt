- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs, with a novel depermutation algorithm using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Extends Hsu, Kakade & Zhang’s spectral HMM algorithm to the MHMM setting by modeling MHMMs as HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.  
- Identifies and addresses the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM-based MHMM learning.  
- Demonstrates computational efficiency and scalability compared to EM on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: Provides a concrete, practical solution to the permutation ambiguity problem.  
- Strength: Offers compelling computational advantages for large datasets relative to EM.  
- Strength: Clear exposition of the MHMM–HMM connection and the algorithmic idea.  
- Strength: Experimental validation supports practical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Core spectral technique builds on established methods, so novelty of the depermutation step is limited.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to existing spectral depermutation methods and deeper justification of the specific spectral properties used.  
- Weakness: Contains minor typos and clarity issues that require proofreading.  
- Weakness: Key theoretical claim (Conjecture 1) is unproven, weakening the theoretical guarantees.  
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses a relevant, challenging problem with strong empirical and computational results.  
- Argument against acceptance: Limited methodological novelty, reliance on an unproven conjecture, and presentation polish issues.  
- Overall: Solid, valuable contribution with compelling empirical benefits; recommend acceptance contingent on improved related-work discussion, theoretical justification, and proofreading.
