- Paper proposes applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm (Anandkumar et al., 2012) to learn mixtures of HMMs by formulating MHMM as a special-case HMM and adds a stabilization step assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue 1.  
- Stabilization scheme to resolve parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive, with an analysis under estimation noise that uses eigenvalues (Equation (7)).  
- Notation is unclear in places; authors should define vectors/matrices and their dimensions when first introduced.  
- Corollary 1 needs clarification—define 1J^T and explain lim_{e->inf} bar{A}^e as a JK x JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns within each JxJ block.  
- Related work and context are insufficient; cite prior uses of spectral estimates to initialize EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) and other relevant literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is basic and presentation is poor—Figure 4 is hard to read and results should be shown in tables.  
- EM results are questionable; report best (not just average) EM accuracy and explain why EM performs poorly compared to expectations.  
- Minor notation suggestion: align with Hsu et al. (2009), e.g., use T for transition matrices and n,m for observation/hidden state counts.  
- Overall method = existing spectral HMM learning + novel stabilization to address parameter permutation.
