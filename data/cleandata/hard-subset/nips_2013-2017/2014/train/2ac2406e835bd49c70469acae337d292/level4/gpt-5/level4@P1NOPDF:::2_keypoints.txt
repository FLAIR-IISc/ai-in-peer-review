- Proposes a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by reducing an MHMM to a standard HMM to apply Anandkumar et al. (2012).  
- Introduces a stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each component HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- The permutation-resolution scheme is interesting and intuitive; analysis under estimation noise leverages eigenvalues (see Eq. (7)).  
- Notation and definitions are inconsistent/missing; every vector/matrix and its dimensions should be defined at first use.  
- Corollary 1 is unclear: explicitly define 1_J^T and clarify that lim_{e→∞}(bar A)^e is a JK-by-JK block-diagonal matrix with J-by-J rank-1 blocks of identical columns.  
- Related work discussion is insufficient; include and compare to prior approaches (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) both conceptually and empirically.  
- Experiments are basic and poorly presented; replace hard-to-read figures (e.g., Fig. 4) with numerical tables.  
- EM baseline appears unusually weak; report best-case EM across many restarts, run to convergence, and ensure fair comparisons.  
- Notational conformity: consider using standard spectral HMM notation (T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden state counts).  
- Algorithmic framing needs clarification: clearly separate which parts are inherited from prior work and which (the stabilization) are novel, and state any theoretical guarantees or conditions for permutation resolution.  
- Overall assessment: the approach is natural and potentially useful but needs clearer notation, fuller related-work context, and stronger empirical evaluation.
