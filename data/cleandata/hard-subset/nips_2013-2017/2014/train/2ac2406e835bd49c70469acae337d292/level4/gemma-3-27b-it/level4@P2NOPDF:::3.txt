This paper introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The core idea builds upon the Hsu, Kakade & Zhang algorithm, but addresses a critical challenge: the recovered transition matrix is subject to an arbitrary permutation of the true components. This permutation effectively shuffles the mixture components, necessitating a “depermutation” step. The authors propose an algorithm to address this, which appears to function effectively based on the presented results.

The paper’s presentation could benefit from more rigorous editing. Several minor typos and grammatical errors were observed, for example, “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.” Careful proofreading is recommended before publication.

A stronger connection to related work would enhance the paper’s impact. Specifically, the authors should consider discussing Chaganty and Liang’s work ("Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions," ICML 2013), which shares a similar spectral approach for mixture model estimation. Positioning this work relative to existing spectral methods – particularly those addressing permutation ambiguities – would provide valuable context and demonstrate the novelty of the proposed approach more clearly. 

In essence, the contribution lies in successfully tackling the permutation problem inherent in applying spectral methods to mixtures of HMMs. The depermutation algorithm represents a practical solution to a significant hurdle in this application. However, bolstering the related work discussion and improving the polish of the writing would substantially strengthen the paper.
