- Summary: Proposes a spectral method-of-moments approach to learn mixtures of HMMs by treating the mixture as a single block-diagonal HMM, estimating the global HMM spectrally, then depermuting states via eigen-structure and a "spectral longevity" heuristic; shows good recovery and fast EM initialization on synthetic and UCI data.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on spectral HMM learning (Hsu et al.; Anandkumar et al.), extending to mixtures; depermutation relates to alignment problems in other spectral mixture models and needs clearer positioning vs prior alignment methods.

- Strength — conceptual simplicity: Casting MHMMs as a block-diagonal HMM is intuitive and useful.

- Strength — computational efficiency: After moment computation, learning scales independently of sequence number and length, offering speedups over EM.

- Strength — practical depermutation: Eigen-structure clustering and the K-estimation heuristic are intuitive and work well empirically.

- Strength — empirical utility: Serves as a competitive and fast initializer for EM with good empirical performance.

- Weakness — incomplete theory: Noisy-case steps rely on conjectures and heuristics without formal guarantees or clear conditions.

- Weakness — sensitivity assumptions: Assumes unique eigenvalue at 1 per component and persistent eigenvalue separation; robustness to small spectral gaps is not characterized.

- Weakness — practical constraints: Requires MK < L and the total number of global states MK as input; unclear joint selection of M and K when K unknown.

- Weakness — empirical evaluation: Comparisons lack parameter-recovery metrics, ablations on eigen-gaps, systematic noise/sample-size sensitivity, and variance over runs.

- Weakness — clarity/polish: Typos, awkward phrasing, notation shifts, and an unclearly defined spectral longevity criterion need fixing.

- Quality: Technically sound in the noiseless case; noisy-regime claims are informal and need either proofs or stress tests.

- Clarity: Generally readable, but the noisy-case algorithm and spectral longevity need clearer exposition and examples.

- Originality: Moderate — natural block-diagonal casting with novelty mainly in the depermutation and K-estimation heuristics.

- Significance: Potentially useful for large-scale sequence clustering and EM initialization, but limited by MK < L and eigen-gap sensitivity.

- Suggestion: Provide formal conditions/guarantees or extensive empirical validation for depermutation and K-estimation under noise.

- Suggestion: Study robustness to small spectral gaps and near-nonergodic components with targeted stress tests.

- Suggestion: Clarify model selection when K is unknown and practical diagnostics for choosing M and MK.

- Suggestion: Report parameter estimation errors, include ablations and variance across runs.

- Suggestion: Strengthen related-work discussion on alignment/permutation recovery in spectral methods.

- Suggestion: Explain spectral longevity with intuition and a simple example; streamline notation and proofread.

- Pros for acceptance: Clear, practical, and fast algorithm with an effective depermutation heuristic and real-data applicability as an EM initializer.

- Cons against acceptance: Heuristic theory in noisy regime, applicability limited by MK < L and spectral-gap requirements, and empirical evaluation could be broader.

- Overall: A promising and efficient spectral approach to MHMMs that needs stronger theoretical backing, broader empirical validation, and clearer presentation to be a solid contribution.
