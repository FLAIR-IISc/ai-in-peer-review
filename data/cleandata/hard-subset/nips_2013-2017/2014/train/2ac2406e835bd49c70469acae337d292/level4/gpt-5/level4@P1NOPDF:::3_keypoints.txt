- Presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs, using a variant of the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang approach with recovered transition matrices up to an arbitrary permutation.
- Introduces a de-permutation post-processing heuristic to align estimated components with true components.
- Empirical results indicate the de-permutation step is reasonably effective.
- Methodological novelty is limited mainly to the de-permutation post-processing; core algorithm is a straightforward adaptation of existing spectral methods.
- Paper lacks clear discussion of what is genuinely new and how contributions differ from prior work.
- Needs comparison to alternative alignment strategies (e.g., clustering methods or anchor-feature approaches).
- Requires explicit theoretical guarantees or conditions for the de-permutation step (identifiability, sample complexity, robustness).
- If theoretical results exist, they should be stated and contrasted with guarantees of the base spectral method.
- Exposition and related-work discussion should be improved, notably connecting to prior spectral mixture work such as Chaganty & Liang (2013).
- Multiple language and grammar issues hinder readability; the manuscript should be thoroughly proofread. 
- Overall: addresses an important de-permutation problem with a practical solution, but novelty and theoretical grounding are insufficiently articulated.
