- Proposes a spectral learning algorithm for mixtures of HMMs plus a method to resolve permutation ambiguity and recover block-diagonal structure.  
- Major concern: method relies on accurately estimating full HMM parameters first, which defeats the sparsity/scalability advantages of block-diagonal models in large/limited-data settings.  
- Authors should state when the approach is suitable (e.g., small state spaces or abundant data) and discuss scalability and data requirements.  
- Little discussion on whether the spectral algorithm can produce sparse or near-sparse transition matrices; it may converge to dense approximations under noise.  
- Recommend exploring sparsity-promoting techniques or analyzing sensitivity of learned matrices to noise.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently realistic: depermuting transition matrices (Sec. 4.1) is not equivalent to learning from observation data.  
- Experiments should generate observation data from mixture HMMs and attempt full parameter recovery to reflect observation noise and limited data.  
- Section 4.2 lacks crucial details (specific transition and observation matrices); these should be reported (e.g., in an appendix).  
- Table 1 is puzzling: EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone and requires explanation.  
- Minor issues: typo in proof (Λ subscript should be K not 2) and figures’ text/numbers are too small to read.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution but practical limitations and weak experimental validation mean major revisions are needed for strong impact.
