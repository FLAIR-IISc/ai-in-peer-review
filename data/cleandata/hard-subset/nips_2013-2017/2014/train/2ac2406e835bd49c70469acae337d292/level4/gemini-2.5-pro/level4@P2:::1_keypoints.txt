- Proposes learning an MHMM by treating it as a single MK-state HMM, using spectral MoM to estimate parameters, then resolving state permutation to recover block-diagonal structure.  
- Introduces a de-permutation technique that clusters columns of a high-power estimated transition matrix and a heuristic to infer K from eigenvalues.  
- Novel framing and de-permutation idea that avoids EM’s local optima, offering an interesting new perspective on mixture model learning.  
- Claims significant computational efficiency over EM since core estimation is independent of the number of sequences once moments are computed.  
- Paper is well-written; derivation of MHMM-to-HMM equivalence is clear and elegant.  
- Fundamental practical concern: method requires accurate estimation of a dense MK×MK transition matrix, which may need far more data than estimating K smaller M×M matrices.  
- Asks for analysis of data complexity and scenarios where one could reliably estimate the full MK×MK matrix yet prefer an MHMM.  
- Raises robustness concern: spectral HMM learning may produce a dense matrix close in moments but not block-diagonal, so de-permutation may fail under finite-sample model misspecification.  
- Criticizes noise-robustness experiment for perturbing the true matrix directly instead of running the full pipeline (generate sequences → spectral estimate → de-permute), which would better assess finite-data effects.  
- Calls for more experimental detail and reproducibility: missing ground-truth MHMM parameters and mixing/separability settings that affect task difficulty.  
- Notes counter-intuitive results where EM initialized by spectral sometimes yields lower accuracy than spectral alone and requests explanation for possible EM convergence to worse clustering.  
- Minor fixes: likely typo in proof subscript (should be K), and figures’ text/numbers are too small.  
- Conclusion: theoretically interesting and elegant, but practical applicability and experimental validation are currently unconvincing and need more thorough, realistic evaluation.
