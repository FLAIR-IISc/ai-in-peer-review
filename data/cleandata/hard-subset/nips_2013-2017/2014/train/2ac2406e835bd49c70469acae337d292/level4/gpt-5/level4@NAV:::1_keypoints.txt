Summary: Proposes spectral learning for a single HMM and a method to resolve permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal structure for mixtures of HMMs.

Major 1: Paper is clearly written but the experimental results lack depth and detail needed to interpret findings.

Major 2: Method relies on representing a mixture as a single HMM and requires accurate full-HMM estimation before depermutation, which may be impractical—clarify when the method is appropriate.

Major 3: Unclear whether spectral learning yields approximately sparse (block-diagonal) transition matrices under realistic noise; need ways/guarantees to encourage sparsity.

Major 4: Experiment in Section 4.1 uses Dirichlet-perturbed matrices instead of sampling observation sequences from a mixture—experiments should learn from sequences to reflect realistic noise and limited data.

Major 5: Section 4.2 lacks specifics about the actual mixture HMMs (transition and observation parameters); provide these details (e.g., in an appendix).

Major 6: Table 1 anomaly: EM initialized with spectral estimates sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—explain why EM degrades performance.

Minor 1: In the proof of Section 3.2.1, the Lambda subscript in the bottom-right entry should be K, not 2.

Minor 2: Text and numbers in figures are too small.

Overall: Interesting ideas and strong theoretical results, but concerns remain about practicality on real datasets.
