- Paper proposes learning MHMMs by treating them as a block-diagonal HMM, using spectral/MoM methods to estimate a global HMM and a depermutation procedure to recover component blocks, plus a spectral-longevity criterion to infer K.

- Builds on prior spectral HMM and tensor MoM work; block-diagonal reduction is standard, but the depermutation via eigenstructure and the K heuristic are the main novel contributions.

- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition and a crisp statement of MHMM ↔ block-diagonal HMM equivalence.

- Strength: directly addresses permutation ambiguity that mixes states across components.

- Strength: proposes an efficient, scalable pipeline whose runtime after moment computation is independent of dataset size.

- Strength: empirical results show favorable speed–accuracy trade-offs vs EM and spectral estimates can be strong initializations.

- Major concern: practicality of estimating a full MK-state HMM — need clarity on appropriate regimes (e.g., L ≥ MK, strong spectral gaps, sufficient N and T) and guidance on failure modes.

- Major concern: learned transition matrices will not be exactly sparse in practice; paper lacks guarantees or regularization strategies to promote near-block-diagonality.

- Major concern: noise model in Section 4.1 (Dirichlet perturbation) is not end-to-end; recommend evaluating by simulating sequences from MHMMs and running MoM to assess depermutation under realistic estimation noise.

- Major concern: experimental Section 4.2 omits key generative details (Ak, Ok construction, mixing rates, spectral gaps, emission overlap, initial distributions, variances); include these or an appendix.

- Major concern: unclear why EM initialized with spectral estimates sometimes underperforms spectral alone — clarify EM stopping criteria, regularization, parameter constraints, and numerical issues.

- Major concern: theoretical claims are partly conjectural; the longevity-based K selection depends on spectral gap conditions — provide diagnostics (estimated gaps, sensitivity to parameters) and robustness analysis.

- Quality: method is sound and well-motivated but theoretical support is incomplete and experiments are not always aligned with end-to-end learning.

- Clarity: generally clear but experimental details and figure readability need improvement (fonts, line weights).

- Originality: depermutation using eigenstructure and the spectral-longevity K selection appear novel despite known block-diagonal reduction.

- Significance: potentially a scalable alternative or strong initializer for MHMMs if made robust; current evidence is promising but limited by evaluation design and lack of sparsity mechanisms.

- Pros for acceptance: clear formulation and algorithm, addresses an overlooked permutation issue, fast and useful as an initializer.

- Cons for acceptance: limited end-to-end evaluations, unclear practical regimes, reliance on strong spectral gaps, no mechanism to enforce block sparsity, some conjectural claims.

- Minor comments: fix subscript typo (Λ subscript should be K), enlarge figure fonts/lines, standardize notation and define symbols where first used, ensure complete citations.

- Overall: interesting and well-written with a neat depermutation idea; strengthen end-to-end experiments, provide generative detail and applicability guidance, and add sparsity-aware variants to increase practical impact.
