1. Experiments lack crucial details about data generation and model specifications, limiting result interpretation.  
2. Learning a full HMM before extracting block structure contradicts the scalability/sparsity motivation for mixture HMMs.  
3. Authors must clarify under what conditions (dataset size, noise levels) full-model estimation is viable.  
4. No guarantee spectral learning yields a sparse or unique transition matrix; multiple full HMMs can approximate block-diagonal structure.  
5. Section 4.1’s noise model ignores the observation process; robustness should be tested by sampling observation sequences and evaluating recovery under observation noise and limited data.  
6. Section 4.2 omits actual mixture HMM parameters (transition/emission matrices), which are essential for assessing learnability; include them (e.g., in an appendix).  
7. Table 1 anomaly: spectral learning outperforming EM initialized with spectral suggests implementation flaws or overfitting and requires explanation.  
8. Minor typo in Section 3.2.1 proof: bottom-right subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.  
9. Figures and numerical labels are too small; increase font size.  
10. Overall: theory is sound but practical contribution is limited—either justify feasible use-cases or revise the method to incorporate sparsity during learning.
