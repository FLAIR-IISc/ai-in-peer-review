This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM) by leveraging an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) and formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM. The authors propose a stabilization-based solution to address parameter permutation by introducing an assumption that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

However, there are several issues that need to be addressed. The notation used in the paper could be improved for clarity, and the authors should provide more references to related work, such as the initialization of EM with spectral estimates explored in Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experimental evaluation is also quite basic and could be enhanced by presenting results in a table format instead of the graphical format in Figure 4.

On the positive side, the proposed scheme to address the problem of parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, leading to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues in the algorithm (Equation (7)).

Some specific comments and suggestions for improvement are as follows:

- The terms in Corollary 1 could be clarified by defining 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborating on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (that it's a JK x JK block diagonal matrix where the columns within each J x J block are the same).
- Defining each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced would greatly enhance the presentation.
- Figure 4 is difficult to read and should be replaced with tables. Additionally, the performance of EM in Table 1 is surprisingly poor, and it would be beneficial to include the best (not average) accuracy of EM, as is standard practice.
- A minor suggestion is to conform to the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009) by using T for transition matrices (instead of A) and n, m for numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J).

The paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models, consisting of an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs followed by a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation.
