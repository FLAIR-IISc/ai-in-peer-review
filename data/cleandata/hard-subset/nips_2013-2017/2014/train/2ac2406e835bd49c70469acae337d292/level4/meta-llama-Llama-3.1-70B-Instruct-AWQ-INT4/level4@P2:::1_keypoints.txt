- Proposes a novel Method-of-Moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs by representing an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Uses an existing MoM spectral algorithm to recover parameters up to permutation ambiguity.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the approach.  
- Main concern: requires accurately estimating the full HMM then undoing the permutation, which may be infeasible for large-scale problems or limited data.  
- Authors should clarify applicability conditions and when the algorithm is suitable.  
- Recovered transition matrix may not be sparse despite underlying block-diagonal structure; need analysis of sparsity guarantees.  
- Experiments do not fully reflect realistic noise; recommend sampling observations from MHMMs and learning all parameters from observed data for robustness tests.  
- Section 4.2 lacks sufficient experimental detail—provide transition and observation matrix parameters for the mixtures used.  
- Unexpected result: EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone; authors should investigate and explain.  
- Minor issues: typo in Λ subscript in Section 3.2.1 proof and small/illegible figure text and numbers.  
- Strengths: novel approach, clear exposition, and promising initial experimental results.  
- Weaknesses: scalability/data requirements, potential non-sparsity, limited/noisy experiment realism, insufficient experiment details, and surprising empirical outcomes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: valuable novel contribution and promising results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practicality and robustness concerns and incomplete experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing practicality/robustness issues and providing more experimental details for Section 4.2.
