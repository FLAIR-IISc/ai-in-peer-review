- Paper introduces a spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by casting MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Main contribution: resolves permutation ambiguity in method-of-moments HMM learning via spectral analysis of the global transition matrix, robust to estimation noise.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of spectral properties (Lemmas 2–3, Conjecture 1) and a practical algorithm to estimate cluster count and de-permute the transition matrix.  
- Empirically, method offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM with comparable accuracy and much lower runtime on large datasets.  
- Demonstrates practical utility as an initialization scheme for EM in real data experiments.  
- Strength (quality): clear theoretical foundation and well-designed experiments supporting main claims.  
- Strength (clarity): core idea and algorithm description are generally clear, though some notation issues remain.  
- Strength (originality): meaningful extension of spectral MoM methods to MHMMs with a novel permutation-resolution technique.  
- Strength (significance): valuable computational alternative to EM for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Weakness (theory): noisy-case analysis (Conjecture 1) lacks formal proof and borrows sample-complexity references from a different model.  
- Weakness (experiments): real-data experimental setup (polynomial feature transform) is not well-justified and comparisons with EM are limited.  
- Weakness (clarity): inconsistent and nonstandard notation (e.g., L, J) and undefined terms (e.g., 1J^T, ν̄); Corollary 1’s transition-matrix description is confusing.  
- Weakness (originality overlap): insufficient differentiation from Chaganty & Liang (2013) on spectral initialization for mixture models.  
- Weakness (significance): experiments are narrow; paper needs broader comparisons with state-of-the-art MHMM methods.  
- Specific comment: define all vectors/matrices with dimensions at first use (e.g., 1J^T, ν̄).  
- Specific comment: clarify Corollary 1 to state that lim_{e→∞} Ā^e yields block matrices with identical columns per block.  
- Specific comment: replace Figure 4 with tables to better compare accuracy and speed across data sizes.  
- Specific comment: report best EM accuracy over multiple initializations (not average) in comparisons.  
- Specific comment: adopt standard HMM notation (e.g., T for transition, n for observation states, m for hidden states) for readability.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, well-motivated solution to a key problem in MHMM spectral learning with supportive theory and practical gains.  
- Con acceptance: noisy-case theory needs rigor, experimental EM comparison and presentation need improvement, and notation/presentation require cleanup.
