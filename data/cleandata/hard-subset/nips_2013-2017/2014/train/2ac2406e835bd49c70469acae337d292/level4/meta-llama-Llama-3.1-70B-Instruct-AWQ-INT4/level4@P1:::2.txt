This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). It shows that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be used off-the-shelf by formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM. The authors propose a stabilization-based solution for parameter permutation by introducing an assumption that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed algorithm is interesting and intuitive. The authors provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues in the algorithm. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, especially in terms of computational efficiency.

However, there are some issues with notation and presentation. The authors should define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced to improve the clarity of the presentation. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more awareness of related work by providing references, such as initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty and Liang, 2013).

The experiments are quite basic, and the results could be presented in a more readable format. Instead of using Figure 4, the authors could report the results in a table format. Furthermore, the authors should report the best accuracy of EM, rather than the average accuracy, as a standard practice.

The paper also raises some questions. For instance, the EM algorithm is performing poorly in Table 1, which is unusual. Typically, even with random initialization, EM beats spectral methods with enough iterations. The authors should provide more insights into this result.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

Regarding clarity, the paper is well-organized, but some notations and definitions could be improved. The authors should define 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborate on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (that it's JK x JK block diagonal where the columns within each J x J block are the same).

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation, which is an interesting contribution. However, the authors should provide more context and references to related work to demonstrate the significance of this contribution.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The proposed algorithm is computationally efficient and effective, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed algorithm is interesting and effective. However, the authors should address the issues with notation, presentation, and related work to improve the paper's quality and impact.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation, which is an interesting contribution.
* The proposed algorithm is computationally efficient and effective, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
* The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper has some issues with notation and presentation, which could be improved.
* The authors should provide more context and references to related work to demonstrate the significance of the contribution.
* The EM algorithm is performing poorly in Table 1, which is unusual and requires more insights.

Recommendation:
