- Summary: Proposes a Method-of-Moments approach to learn mixtures of HMMs by viewing them as a single HMM with block-diagonal transition and resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.

- Major 1: Paper is well written but experimental results need more detailed analysis and discussion.

- Major 2: Method relies on estimating a full HMM then recovering block-diagonal structure, which may be impractical for large-scale, sparse-mixture settings; need clarity on applicability conditions.

- Major 3: Unclear whether the spectral learning guarantees produce (or approximate) the needed sparsity; many full HMMs could approximate a sparse block-diagonal model, especially with noise.

- Major 4: Experiments may not reflect real-world observational noise — learning should be evaluated by sampling sequences from MHMMs and estimating parameters from data, not just depermuting noisy matrices.

- Major 5: Section 4.2 lacks details about the HMM mixture parameters; reporting transition/observation matrices (e.g., in an appendix) is needed to assess difficulty and robustness.

- Major 6: Table 1 puzzlingly shows EM sometimes worsening results after spectral initialization; authors should explain why EM does not always improve the estimate.

- Minor 1: Typo in proof Section 3.2.1: Λ subscript should be K, not 2.

- Minor 2: Figures have small text/numbers that hinder readability.

- Overall: Interesting theoretical contribution but practical applicability is questionable due to sparsity and noise concerns.

- For acceptance 1: Novel MoM-based approach to MHMMs offering a potentially efficient alternative to EM.

- For acceptance 2: Strong theoretical analysis supporting the method.

- For acceptance 3: Addresses permutation ambiguity in MoM estimation.

- Against acceptance 1: Sparsity assumptions may not hold in many real-world applications.

- Against acceptance 2: Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate robustness to realistic noise and limited data.

- Against acceptance 3: Practical benefits over existing approaches are not clearly established.
