

The paper proposes a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors recognize that standard MoM approaches for MHMMs suffer from a permutation ambiguity in the estimated global transition matrix, making it impossible to recover the parameters of individual HMMs. They address this by showing that the MHMM can be viewed as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix, and they propose a depermutation algorithm leveraging the spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover the original block structure. The theoretical contributions include lemmas establishing the spectral properties of the global transition matrix and conjectures about the conditions under which the depermutation algorithm succeeds. The experimental evaluation demonstrates the approach's computational advantages over EM, particularly for large datasets, and shows that it can serve as an effective initialization scheme for EM.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach to a non-trivial problem in MHMM learning. The authors correctly identify the permutation ambiguity as a key challenge and provide a theoretically motivated solution. The theoretical analysis, while containing some conjectures (e.g., Conjecture 1), establishes reasonable conditions under which the depermutation algorithm works. The experimental validation is well-designed, with both synthetic data showing recovery of the block structure and real data demonstrating practical utility as an initialization method. However, the theoretical justification for the depermutation algorithm could be strengthened - the proof of Lemma 3 is somewhat opaque, and Conjecture 1 lacks a rigorous proof (the justification provided appears to reuse results from Anandkumar et al. without clear adaptation to this specific context). The paper would benefit from more detailed analysis of the noise conditions under which the algorithm succeeds.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, with a logical flow from problem formulation to solution. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the key concepts (e.g., the block-diagonal transition matrix structure) are well-explained. However, there are several grammatical errors and typos that need correction (e.g., "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy" should be "For each e, identify eigenvalues that contain all of the energy"). Some technical explanations could be clearer - for instance, the explanation of the spectral longevity metric (Definition 3) would benefit from a more intuitive description. The algorithm description is clear, but the connection between the theoretical results and the practical implementation could be made more explicit.

**Originality:** The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the spectral learning literature by addressing a specific challenge in MHMM learning that has been overlooked in previous work. While the core MoM approach builds on Hsu et al. (2012), the depermutation algorithm is novel for MHMMs. The authors could better contextualize their work by more explicitly comparing with related spectral approaches for mixture models, such as Chaganty and Liang (2013) on mixtures of linear regressions, which also deals with permutation issues in spectral learning. The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of how this approach differs from and improves upon existing methods for MHMM learning.

**Significance:** The computational advantages of the proposed approach are significant for large-scale sequence clustering applications, where standard EM becomes prohibitive. The paper demonstrates this clearly in the experiments, showing that the spectral method is substantially faster than EM while maintaining comparable accuracy. The real-world application to character trajectory data further demonstrates practical relevance. The finding that the spectral method can serve as an effective initialization for EM is particularly valuable, as it addresses a key limitation of EM (sensitivity to initialization) without requiring a complete replacement of the standard approach.

**Overall Assessment:**
The paper presents a technically sound and potentially valuable contribution to the spectral learning literature for latent variable models. The core idea of using spectral properties to resolve permutation ambiguity is novel for MHMMs and addresses a genuine limitation of standard MoM approaches. While the theoretical analysis requires some strengthening, the experimental results are compelling and demonstrate clear practical advantages. The paper would benefit from better contextualization within the broader literature on spectral learning for mixture models and more rigorous theoretical justification for the key algorithmic steps.

**Pro Arguments for Acceptance:**
1. The paper identifies and solves a non-trivial problem (permutation ambiguity) in MHMM learning that has been overlooked in previous work.
2. The theoretical analysis provides reasonable conditions for the algorithm's success.
3. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate computational advantages over EM and practical utility as an initialization scheme.
4. The approach is directly applicable to important sequence clustering problems.

**Con Arguments for Acceptance:**
1. The theoretical justification for the depermutation algorithm (particularly Conjecture 1) is insufficiently rigorous.
2. The paper could better contextualize its contribution relative to related work on spectral learning for mixture models.
3. The presentation contains several grammatical errors and typos that need correction.
4. The experimental comparison with EM could be more detailed (e.g., with more initialization methods or larger datasets).

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of spectral learning for latent variable models, with clear practical significance for sequence clustering applications. With minor revisions to strengthen the theoretical justification and improve presentation, this work would make a strong contribution to NIPS. I recommend acceptance.
