The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). 

The algorithm is essentially a trivial variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm, except that the transition matrix recovered is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, mixing the various components. The authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that seems to work reasonably well.

However, the paper could benefit from more careful proofreading, as there are occasional typos and grammatical errors. For instance, the phrase "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy" appears to be incorrect.

Furthermore, the paper could do a better job of putting this work into context. For example, it would be beneficial to relate this work to other relevant papers, such as Chaganty and Liang's "Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions" (ICML, 2013), which also employs spectral methods for estimating mixture models. By situating their work within the broader literature, the authors can provide a clearer understanding of the contributions and novelty of their approach.
