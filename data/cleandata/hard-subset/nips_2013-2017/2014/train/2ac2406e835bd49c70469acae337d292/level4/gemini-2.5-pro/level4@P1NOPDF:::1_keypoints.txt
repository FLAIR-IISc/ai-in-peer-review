1. Summary: Two-step approach — use a spectral algorithm to learn a single large HMM for the mixture, then resolve permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal component HMMs.

2. Experiments need more detail and stronger experimental validation.

3. Method requires estimating a full dense HMM first, which undermines the sparse/block-diagonal motivation and may be infeasible in data-limited, large-scale settings; authors should discuss practical applicability and limitations.

4. Finite-data estimates may not be near a permuted block-diagonal matrix; request theoretical guarantees that the chosen spectral estimator yields a transition matrix close to a permuted block-diagonal target.

5. Section 4.1’s experiment is unrealistic because it perturbs a known permuted matrix rather than performing end-to-end learning from observational sequences; recommend an end-to-end evaluation.

6. Section 4.2 begins end-to-end testing but omits crucial ground-truth HMM parameters (transition/observation matrices); provide these details for reproducibility and proper assessment.

7. Table 1 shows EM initialized with the spectral solution sometimes performs worse than the spectral solution alone — unexpected given EM’s likelihood monotonicity; request an explanation.

8. Minor: In the proof (Section 3.2.1) the bottom-right Λ subscript appears as '2' but should likely be 'K'.

9. Minor: Figures’ text and numbers are too small to read easily.

10. Overall: Interesting theoretical contributions but significant concerns about practical applicability and insufficient, non-rigorous experimental validation.
