This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). It demonstrates that an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models (HMMs) (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be directly applied by formulating an MHMM as a special case of an HMM. To address the parameter permutation problem, the authors propose a stabilization-based solution relying on the assumption that each HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.

There are some notational issues that should be addressed. Furthermore, the authors would significantly benefit from a more thorough review of related work, including references such as Chaganty and Liang (2013), which explores initializing Expectation-Maximization (EM) with spectral estimates. The experiments presented are relatively basic, and I strongly recommend reporting results in tabular format rather than the graphical representation in Figure 4.

That said, the proposed scheme to mitigate the parameter permutation problem is interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis of the algorithm’s performance in the presence of estimation noise, leading to an intriguing application of eigenvalues (Equation (7)). 

Comments:

- I initially found the terms in Corollary 1 unclear. Defining 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) would be helpful. Additionally, elaborating on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e – specifically, that it is a JK x JK block diagonal matrix where the columns within each J x J block are identical – would improve clarity.
- Generally, please define each vector and matrix along with its dimensions the first time it is introduced. This will greatly enhance the presentation.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret; please replace it with tables. Also, the poor performance of EM in Table 1 is surprising. Typically, even with random initialization, EM, given sufficient iterations, outperforms spectral methods despite the potential for convergence to local optima. Reporting the best (rather than average) accuracy achieved by EM would be a standard and valuable addition.
- As a minor point, conforming to the notation of Hsu et al. (2009) might aid readability. Specifically, using T for transition matrices (instead of A) and n, m for the numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J) would be beneficial.

In summary, this paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models. The algorithm leverages an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models, followed by a novel stabilization step designed to resolve the parameter permutation issue.
