The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). Notably, their algorithm is a variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm, with the key difference being that the recovered transition matrix is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, effectively mixing the various components. To address this issue, the authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to work reasonably well in practice.

There are occasional minor typos throughout the paper, such as "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy," which should be corrected through careful proofreading.

To provide a clearer understanding of the contributions and relevance of this work, it would be beneficial to contextualize the research by referencing related papers, such as Chaganty and Liang's "Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions" (ICML, 2013). This would help situate the proposed spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs within the broader landscape of related research, highlighting its key advancements and innovations, particularly in addressing the challenge of "de-permuting" the results.
