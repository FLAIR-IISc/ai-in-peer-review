This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). It shows that the existing spectral method for single HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be used off the shelf by casting an MHMM as a special case of an HMM. To address parameter permutation, the paper proposes a stabilization step that relies on the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue equal to 1.

There are some issues with notation. The paper should also demonstrate stronger awareness of related work with appropriate citations—for example, initializing EM with spectral estimates has already been explored by Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experiments are quite basic, and the results would be clearer in tables rather than the graphical presentation in Figure 4.

That said, the stabilization-based approach to resolve parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The analysis under estimation noise is also helpful and motivates an intriguing use of eigenvalues in Equation (7).

Comments:
- Corollary 1 was initially confusing. Please define 1_J^T explicitly as a row vector of length J filled with ones, and elaborate on the structure of the limit of bar{A}^e as e goes to infinity: it is a JK-by-JK block-diagonal matrix in which, within each J-by-J block, all columns are identical.
- Please define each vector and matrix, along with its dimensions, at first mention; this will significantly improve readability.
- Figure 4 is hard to read; please replace it with tables. Also, EM’s performance in Table 1 seems unusually poor. In practice, even with random initialization, EM often surpasses spectral methods given sufficient iterations (despite local optima). A common practice is to report the best (not just average) EM accuracy across runs; please include that result.
- Minor suggestion: consider adopting the notation of Hsu et al. (2009)—use T for transition matrices (rather than A), and n and m for the numbers of observations and hidden states (rather than L and J).

In summary, the paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models that applies an existing spectral HMM learner followed by a novel stabilization step to undo parameter permutation.
