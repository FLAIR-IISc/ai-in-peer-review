- Proposes a spectral method-of-moments for learning MHMMs by reducing an MHMM to a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition and applying the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang spectral algorithm plus a depermutation step.  
- Depermutation procedure exploits spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM components and, under spectral conditions, estimate the number of mixture components K.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real trajectory data show computational efficiency, reasonable clustering accuracy, and utility as an initializer for EM.  
- Strength: Clear and natural reduction of MHMM learning to a block-diagonal HMM formulation.  
- Strength: Practical depermutation method that works well empirically.  
- Strength: Apparent computational advantages over EM and good scaling with data size.  
- Strength: Real-data results indicate standalone utility and value as an EM initializer.  
- Weakness: Core method largely adapts existing spectral HMM techniques, so novelty is limited beyond the depermutation step.  
- Weakness: Paper contains typos and grammatical errors that hurt clarity and require careful proofreading.  
- Weakness: Insufficient contextualization relative to recent spectral mixture methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang ICML 2013).  
- Quality: Core technical idea is sound and empirically supported, but theoretical guarantees in the noisy setting rely on unproven conditions and conjectures.  
- Clarity: Generally readable but needs clearer separation of prior work vs. novel contributions and streamlined notation.  
- Originality: Main novelty is the depermutation strategy and K-estimation heuristic; overall contribution is useful but incremental.  
- Recommendation: Borderline — practical and computationally appealing but incremental; needs better theoretical support, comparisons, and proofreading.
