- Paper tackles learning mixtures of HMMs via a spectral algorithm plus permutation reversal to recover block-diagonal transition structure.
- Well written overall, but the experimental results need more elaboration and detail.
- The approach estimates a full HMM then attempts to recover block structure, which seems impractical; authors should clarify applicable vs. inapplicable scenarios.
- No mechanism or guarantee is provided that the learned transition matrix will be (approximately) sparse; full transition HMMs may closely approximate sparse models.
- Experiments do not realistically reflect observation/noise effects; evaluation should sample sequences from mixtures and recover all parameters to assess depermutation under realistic noise and limited data.
- Section 4.2 lacks crucial details (exact transition and observation matrix parameters); these should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).
- In Table 1, EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes yields worse results than spectral alone—this counterintuitive outcome needs explanation.
- Minor: probable typo in proof (subscript of Λ should be K, not 2).
- Minor: figures’ text and numbers are too small for comfortable readability.
- Overall skepticism about the method’s practical applicability to real-world datasets despite interesting theory.
