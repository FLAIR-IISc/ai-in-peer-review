This paper presents a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The proposed algorithm is a variant of the standard spectral learning method by Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang. The key challenge in applying this framework directly is that the recovered transition matrix is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, which scrambles the identities of the individual HMM components. The authors' main contribution is a novel de-permutation algorithm to resolve this ambiguity, which appears to perform well in their experiments.

The paper would be strengthened by placing the work in a broader context. A discussion relating this approach to other spectral methods for mixture models would be valuable. For example, connecting it to the work on spectral experts for mixtures of linear regressions by Chaganty and Liang (ICML, 2013) could help clarify the contributions and novelty of the proposed method.

Finally, the manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to correct several minor typos and grammatical errors. For instance, one sentence reads: "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." Addressing such issues would improve the overall clarity and professionalism of the presentation.
