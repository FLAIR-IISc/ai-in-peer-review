- Core methodological flaw: Estimating a full HMM before depermuting defeats the mixture-HMM motivation of exploiting block-diagonal sparsity and is inappropriate for large-scale or low-data settings; the paper must clarify when the algorithm is suitable.  
- Sparsity guarantee missing: Spectral learning typically yields dense matrices, so the method lacks a mechanism (e.g., regularization) or proof to ensure recovered sparsity under noise.  
- Experimental design mismatch: Current noise model permutes matrices rather than generating observation sequences from a mixture HMM; experiments must simulate the true generative process.  
- Experimental requirements: Experiments should (a) generate synthetic sequences from a mixture of HMMs, (b) learn all parameters including block structure from those sequences, and (c) measure how observation/data noise affects block recovery.  
- Insufficient experimental detail: Key mixture-HMM parameters (transition/observation matrices, mixing weights, block sizes, inter-block connectivity) are missing and must be provided for reproducibility and difficulty assessment.  
- EM performance anomaly: EM initialized with spectral estimates degrades performance unexpectedly (Table 1); authors must explain this counterintuitive result (e.g., initialization issues, overfitting).  
- Minor: Typo in Section 3.2.1—subscript for Λ bottom-right should be K, not 2.  
- Minor: Figure text and labels are too small and illegible; increase font sizes.  
- Overall assessment: Theory is solid but practical relevance is undermined by reliance on full-HMM estimation and unrealistic experiments; authors should either remove the full-HMM requirement or provide strong empirical evidence of robustness to noise and data scarcity.
