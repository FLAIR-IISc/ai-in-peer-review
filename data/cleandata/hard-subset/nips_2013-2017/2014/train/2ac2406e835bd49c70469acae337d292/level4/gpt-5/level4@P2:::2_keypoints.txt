- Summary: Proposes a spectral method-of-moments approach for learning mixtures of HMMs by treating the mixture as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and applying spectral HMM learners plus a de-permutation step.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on spectral HMM estimators (Hsu et al., Anandkumar et al.) and suggests spectral initialization for EM (cf. Chaganty & Liang, 2013).

- Strength: Clear reduction of MHMM learning to HMM learning enabling reuse of existing spectral estimators.
- Strength: Novel, practical de-permutation strategy using eigenvalue-1 structure and column clustering of a rank-K reconstruction.
- Strength: Proposes a data-driven “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K that leverages spectral gaps.
- Strength: Potential for large computational savings versus EM once moments are computed.

- Weakness: Theoretical completeness is lacking—several claims are conjectural and reuse bounds from related but different settings without MHMM-specific proofs.
- Weakness: Assumptions about each component having a single eigenvalue of magnitude 1 need to be stated precisely (irreducible, aperiodic) and consequences for violations should be discussed.
- Weakness: The spectral longevity K-estimation is ad hoc; choice of γ and exponent range, sensitivity, and comparisons to standard model-selection methods are missing.
- Weakness: Notation inconsistencies and undefined dimensions make the exposition harder to follow; key objects (e.g., 1J, matrix limits) should be defined explicitly.
- Weakness: Related work coverage is incomplete—prior MHMM and spectral/tensor methods and spectral initialization literature should be discussed and compared.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited—need stronger synthetic baselines, ablations (de-permutation, K estimation), different emission types, best-of-run EM comparisons, and clearer tables/figures.

- Quality: Reduction and de-permutation intuition are sound under assumptions, but noisy-case theory is incomplete and some bounds lack proof.
- Clarity: Paper requires consistent notation, explicit dimensions, clearer descriptions of spectral limits and block structure, and improved figures/tables.
- Originality: Main novelty is the de-permutation mechanism and K-estimation heuristic; reduction to HMM spectral learning is straightforward given prior work.
- Significance: If validated and robust, method could scale MHMM learning and provide useful EM initializations, but current evidence is insufficiently broad.

- Specific recommendation: Provide MHMM-tailored perturbation/perturbation-analysis proofs or stronger empirical quantification of approximation error; justify γ and exponent choices and test sensitivity.
- Specific recommendation: Report best-of-N EM results, runtimes, stopping criteria, and compare alternative model-order selection criteria for K.
- Pros for acceptance: Simple intuitive approach, promising computational gains, and novel de-permutation/K-estimation ideas useful in practice.
- Cons against acceptance: Incomplete noisy-case theory, conjectural key results, limited/unclear empirical validation, and insufficient related-work and notation clarity.

- Overall assessment: Interesting and potentially useful contribution that needs clearer exposition, stronger theoretical or empirical support for noisy-case claims, and more comprehensive experiments to be convincing.
