- Proposes a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs that addresses the de-permutation problem.  
- Builds on prior spectral learning work for HMMs and mixture models.  
- Algorithm is a non-trivial variant of the Hsu–Kakade–Zhang method, with added complexity for arbitrary permutations mixing components.  
- Introduces a de-permutation algorithm that appears to perform reasonably well in experiments.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction and clear algorithm description.  
- Technical soundness is strong, with a solid theoretical foundation and a proof of the key lemma to recover the true transition matrix.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient contextualization with related spectral learning work (e.g., Chaganty & Liang).  
- Weakness: contains a few typos and minor errors that should be corrected.  
- Weakness: assumes a high level of background knowledge, potentially limiting accessibility.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel solution to a challenging problem, strong theory, and empirical validation.  
- Rejection arguments: limited positioning within related work, presentation typos, and steep prerequisite knowledge.  
- Recommendations: add more related-work/context, fix typos, and include more background to improve accessibility.
