- Paper applies an existing spectral HMM algorithm (Anandkumar et al., 2012) to learn mixtures of HMMs by framing MHMMs as a special case of HMMs.  
- Proposes a novel stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to one.  
- The stabilization idea is interesting, intuitive, and supported by an analysis that accounts for estimation noise.  
- Equation (7) and the use of eigenvalues in the algorithm are an intriguing contribution.  
- Notation is unclear and inconsistent; vectors and matrices (and their dimensions) should be explicitly defined when first introduced.  
- Corollary 1 is confusing—define 1_J^T and clarify the block-diagonal structure and identical-column property of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e.  
- Related work is insufficiently cited; e.g., spectral initialization for EM (Chaganty & Liang, 2013) should be discussed.  
- Experimental evaluation is basic; Figure 4 is hard to interpret and results should be reported in tables for clarity.  
- EM results appear surprisingly poor; report best-achieved EM accuracy (not just averages) and ensure adequate iterations.  
- Minor notation suggestions: follow Hsu et al. (2009) conventions (use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden state counts) to improve readability.
