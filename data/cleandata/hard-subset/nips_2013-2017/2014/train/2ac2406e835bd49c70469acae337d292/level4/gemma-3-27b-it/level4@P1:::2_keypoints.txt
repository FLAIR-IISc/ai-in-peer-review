- Proposes a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by casting an MHMM as a special-case HMM.  
- Introduces a method to resolve the parameter permutation problem via spectral properties of the global transition matrix, assuming each component HMM has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides analysis of the algorithm under estimation noise, leveraging eigenvalue behavior (Equation (7)).  
- Demonstrates that an existing spectral HMM learner (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied to the MHMM setting.  
- Notation and clarity are weak; many vectors/matrices and their dimensions are not defined when introduced.  
- Corollary 1 is confusing; the reviewer requests explicit definition of 1_J^T and clarification that lim_{e→∞} Ā^e is a JK×JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns in each J×J block.  
- Related work is incomplete—prior ideas like initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013) should be cited and discussed.  
- Experiments are basic and Figure 4 is hard to interpret; results should be presented in tables rather than the current graphical format.  
- EM results look questionable; report best (not average) EM accuracy and investigate why EM performs poorly compared to expectations.  
- Recommend adopting established notation conventions (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009: T for transitions, n/m for observations/hidden states) for clarity.  
- Strength: novel, intuitive scheme to address parameter permutation in MHMMs.  
- Strength: insightful spectral analysis of the global transition matrix, especially under noise.  
- Strength: shows applicability of tensor/spectral HMM techniques to MHMMs.  
- Weakness: unclear presentation and notation hinder readability.  
- Weakness: related work and citations are lacking.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited and experimental setup (especially for EM) seems problematic.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, solid theoretical analysis, tackles a challenging and meaningful problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation and notation issues, insufficient empirical validation, and overlooked prior work.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept pending minor revisions to improve clarity, related work coverage, and experimental presentation.  
- Key references noted: Anandkumar et al. (2012); Chaganty & Liang (2013); Hsu et al. (2009).
