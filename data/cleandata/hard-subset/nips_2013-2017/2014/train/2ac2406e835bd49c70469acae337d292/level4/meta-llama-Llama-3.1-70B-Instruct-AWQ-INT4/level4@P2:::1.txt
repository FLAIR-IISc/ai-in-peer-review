The paper "Spectral Learning of Mixture of Hidden Markov Models" proposes a novel approach to learning mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) using the Method of Moments (MoM). The authors demonstrate the validity of their approach on synthetic and real data. The main idea is to express an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and then use an existing MoM algorithm to learn the parameters up to a permutation ambiguity.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. However, there are some concerns regarding the practicality and robustness of the method.

One of the main concerns is that the algorithm relies on first accurately estimating the full HMM parameters and then reversing the permutation to find the block-diagonal structure. This may not be feasible for large-scale problems with limited training data. The authors should provide more guidance on when their algorithm is suitable and when it may not be.

Another concern is that the recovered transition matrix may not be sparse, even though the underlying model has a block-diagonal structure. The authors should investigate whether the spectral algorithms suggested in the paper can guarantee a sparse transition matrix.

The experimental results, while promising, do not fully reflect the reality of noise in the transition matrix. The authors should consider sampling observation data from a mixture of HMMs and then learning all the parameters back from the observed data. This would provide a more accurate evaluation of the method's robustness to noise.

Additionally, the experiment in Section 4.2 lacks sufficient information to evaluate the quality of the experiment. The authors should provide more details about the mixture of HMMs used in the experiment, including the parameters of the transition and observation matrices.

In Table 1, it is surprising that EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral learning alone. The authors should investigate this phenomenon and provide an explanation.

Minor comments include a typo in the subscript of Λ in the proof in Section 3.2.1 and the small text and numbers in the figures.

Overall, the paper has some interesting ideas and good theoretical results. However, the method's practicality and robustness need to be further evaluated.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to learning MHMMs using MoM
* Clear explanation of the method
* Promising experimental results

Weaknesses:

* Algorithm may not be feasible for large-scale problems with limited training data
* Recovered transition matrix may not be sparse
* Experimental results do not fully reflect the reality of noise in the transition matrix
* Lack of information in the experiment in Section 4.2
* Unexpected results in Table 1

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to learning MHMMs, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
* The experimental results, while limited, show promise.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The algorithm's practicality and robustness need to be further evaluated.
* The experimental results do not fully reflect the reality of noise in the transition matrix.
* The lack of information in the experiment in Section 4.2 makes it difficult to evaluate the quality of the experiment.

Recommendation:

* The paper should be accepted, but the authors should be asked to address the concerns mentioned above, particularly regarding the algorithm's practicality and robustness, and provide more information about the experiment in Section 4.2.
