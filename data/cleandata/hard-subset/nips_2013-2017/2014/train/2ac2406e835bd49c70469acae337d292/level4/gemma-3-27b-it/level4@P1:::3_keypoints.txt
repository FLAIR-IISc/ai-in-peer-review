- Proposes a spectral Method of Moments algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs that addresses permutation ambiguity.  
- Builds on spectral HMM methods (e.g., Hsu et al. 2013) and models an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Introduces a depermutation algorithm that uses eigenvalues/eigenvectors of the global transition matrix to recover component ordering.  
- Provides a conjecture characterizing conditions needed for successful depermutation and cluster-number recovery.  
- Argues MoM has computational advantages and uniqueness guarantees compared to EM, especially on large datasets.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show comparable accuracy to EM with substantially lower computational cost.  
- Real-world experiment on handwritten character trajectories demonstrates practical relevance.  
- Comparison includes EM with random and MoM initialization, offering insightful baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and clearly explains the algorithmic details.  
- Minor typos and some unclear phrasing (e.g., section 3.2.2)—needs careful proofreading.  
- Lacks a more thorough discussion of related spectral methods for mixture models; should cite and relate to Chaganty & Liang (2013).  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with promising results; reviewer recommends acceptance.
