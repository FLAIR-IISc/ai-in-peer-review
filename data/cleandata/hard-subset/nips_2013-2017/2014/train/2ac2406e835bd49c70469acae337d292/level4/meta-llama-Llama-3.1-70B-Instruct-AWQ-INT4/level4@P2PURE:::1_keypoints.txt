- Summary: Proposes a spectral algorithm to learn HMM parameters and then resolve permutation ambiguity to recover the block-diagonal structure of a mixture of HMMs.

- Writing is good, but the experimental results section needs expansion and more detail.

- Method may be impractical: it requires estimating a full HMM then de-permuting to find a sparse block structure; authors should state when the approach is appropriate.

- Doubt that spectral estimates will be close to sparse: many full transition matrices could approximate the sparse block-diagonal model, especially with noise.

- Experiments do not model realistic noise from observations; should evaluate by sampling observation data from mixtures and re-learning parameters.

- Section 4.2 lacks crucial details about the mixture (transition and observation parameters); these should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).

- In Table 1, it is unclear why EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral learning alone.

- Minor: typographical error in proof—subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.

- Minor: figures have text and numbers that are too small.

- Overall: interesting ideas and solid theory, but significant concerns remain about practical performance on real datasets.
