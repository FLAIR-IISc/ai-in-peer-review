This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The authors demonstrate that a previously established spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be directly applied to MHMMs by framing the latter as a specific instance of the former. A novel stabilization-based solution is proposed to address the parameter permutation problem, relying on the assumption that each HMM’s transition matrix possesses a single eigenvalue equal to one. 

However, the notation requires clarification. The authors should also demonstrate greater awareness of related work by including relevant citations; for example, the use of spectral estimates for initializing Expectation-Maximization (EM) has already been explored (Chaganty and Liang, 2013). The experiments presented are relatively basic, and reporting results in a tabular format—rather than the current graphical representation in Figure 4—would significantly improve clarity. 

Despite these points, the proposed approach for circumventing the parameter permutation problem is both interesting and intuitive. Furthermore, the authors provide an analysis considering estimation noise, leading to an intriguing application of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) within the algorithm.

Comments:
- The terms in Corollary 1 initially caused confusion. Defining 1J^T (as a row vector of length J consisting of ones) and elaborating on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (specifically, its JK x JK block diagonal form with identical columns within each J x J block) would be beneficial.
- Generally, consistently define each vector and matrix, including its dimensions, upon its first introduction to enhance the presentation.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret; please replace it with tables. Additionally, the poor performance of EM in Table 1 is surprising. Typically, EM, even with random initialization, outperforms spectral methods given sufficient iterations, despite the potential for convergence to local optima. Reporting the best achieved accuracy of EM—rather than the average—is standard practice and should be included.
- Minor point: conforming to the notation established by Hsu et al. (2009) may improve readability. Specifically, consider using T for transition matrices (instead of A) and n, m for the numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J). 

In summary, the paper proposes an algorithm for MHMM learning that combines an existing spectral algorithm for HMM learning with a novel stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation issues.
