This paper introduces a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The approach leverages an existing spectral method for hidden Markov models (HMMs) – developed by Anandkumar et al. (2012) – by demonstrating that an MHMM can be framed as a specific instance of an HMM. A key contribution is a stabilization technique to address the parameter permutation problem, achieved by assuming each HMM’s transition matrix possesses a single eigenvalue equal to 1.

However, the notation requires clarification, and the manuscript would benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work. For instance, the use of spectral estimates for initializing Expectation-Maximization (EM) has been previously investigated (e.g., Chaganty and Liang, 2013). The experimental evaluation is currently limited; presenting the results in a tabular format, rather than the graphical representation in Figure 4, is strongly recommended.

Despite these concerns, the proposed method for mitigating parameter permutation is both interesting and intuitively motivated. Furthermore, the authors provide an analysis accounting for estimation noise, leading to a novel and insightful application of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) within the algorithm.

Specific comments include: 
- Corollary 1’s terminology is initially unclear. Defining 1J^T (a row vector of length J consisting of ones) and providing further detail on the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (specifically, its JK x JK block diagonal form with identical columns within each J x J block) would improve clarity.
- Consistently defining each vector and matrix, including its dimensions, upon its first introduction will significantly enhance readability.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret; replacing it with tables is crucial. Additionally, the poor performance of EM in Table 1 is unexpected. EM typically outperforms spectral methods, even with random initialization, given sufficient iterations. Reporting the best observed accuracy of EM, rather than the average, is recommended.
- For consistency, adopting the notation established by Hsu et al. (2009) – using T for transition matrices and n, m for the number of observations and hidden states, respectively – would be beneficial.

In summary, this paper presents an algorithm for MHMM learning, built upon an existing spectral algorithm for HMMs and augmented with a novel stabilization procedure to resolve parameter permutation.
