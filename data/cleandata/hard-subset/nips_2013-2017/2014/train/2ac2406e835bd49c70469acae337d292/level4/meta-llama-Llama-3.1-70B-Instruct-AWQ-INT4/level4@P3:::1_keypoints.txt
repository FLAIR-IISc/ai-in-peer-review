- Proposes a spectral Method-of-Moments approach for MHMMs by viewing the mixture as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions to resolve permutation ambiguity; claims computational efficiency and EM-comparable accuracy.

- Experimental results need more detail (specific MHMM and observation-model parameters) to enable thorough evaluation.

- Scalability/feasibility concern: method depends on accurately estimating full HMM parameters and reversing permutation, which may fail for large-scale problems or limited data; authors should discuss limitations and applicability.

- Transition-matrix sparsity not guaranteed: recovered matrices may be dense and multiple dense HMMs can approximate a sparse block-diagonal model; suggest addressing this and proposing remedies.

- Noise robustness not properly evaluated: authors should sample observations from MHMMs and re-learn all parameters to test sensitivity to noise in transitions.

- Section 4.2 lacks essential experimental details (transition and observation matrix parameters); provide these for reproducibility and evaluation.

- EM initialization issue: EM initialized with spectral estimates sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—explain why and implications for using spectral as EM initialization.

- Minor: typo in proof—Λ subscript should be K, not 2.

- Minor: figures have small text/numbers; increase font size for readability.

- Overall: theoretically interesting and sound, but experiments need more detail, robustness analysis, and discussion of practical limitations.
