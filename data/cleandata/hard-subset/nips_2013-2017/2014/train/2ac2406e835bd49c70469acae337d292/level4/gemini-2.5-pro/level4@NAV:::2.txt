This paper introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The core idea is to frame an MHMM as a specific instance of a larger HMM, which allows for the direct application of an existing spectral learning algorithm (Anandkumar et al., 2012). To resolve the resulting parameter permutation problem, the paper proposes a novel stabilization-based technique, which relies on the assumption that the transition matrix of each component HMM possesses a unique eigenvalue of 1.

However, the paper has several weaknesses. The notation is at times inconsistent, which hinders readability. Furthermore, the discussion of related work is insufficient; for instance, the authors should cite and discuss foundational work on using spectral estimates to initialize EM, such as Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experimental evaluation is limited, and the graphical presentation of results in Figure 4 is difficult to parse and should be replaced with a standard tabular format.

Despite these issues, the paper has notable strengths. The proposed stabilization technique for resolving parameter permutation is novel and intuitive. Additionally, the theoretical analysis that accounts for estimation noise is a valuable contribution, leading to an interesting algorithmic use of eigenvalues as detailed in Equation (7).

Comments:
- The terms used in Corollary 1 are not immediately clear. The authors should explicitly define notation like 1J^T (a J-dimensional row vector of ones) and further explain the structure of lim{e→∞} Ā^e, noting that it is a JK x JK block-diagonal matrix where all columns within a given J x J block are identical.
- For improved clarity, all vectors and matrices should be defined with their corresponding dimensions upon their first appearance. This would significantly enhance the readability of the technical sections.
- Figure 4 is difficult to interpret and should be converted into a table. The poor performance of the EM baseline in Table 1 is also surprising; spectral methods are often outperformed by EM given sufficient iterations, despite local optima. The authors should clarify this result and, following standard practice, report the best-run performance for EM in addition to the average.
- A minor suggestion: Adopting the established notational conventions from seminal works like Hsu et al. (2009) (e.g., using T for transition matrices instead of A, and n, m for observation and hidden state counts instead of L, J) would improve consistency with the literature and aid reader comprehension.
