Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper presents a spectral method-of-moments (MoM) approach for learning mixtures of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). The key observation is that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, with one block per component. The authors first apply an existing spectral HMM learner (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009; Anandkumar et al., 2012) to estimate global parameters up to a permutation of latent states. They then propose a de-permutation procedure that exploits spectral properties of the global transition matrix: in the noiseless case, columns cluster according to blocks when one retains only the eigenvalues equal to 1; in the noisy case, they propose a “spectral longevity” measure over eigenvalues (Eq. 7) to estimate the number of components K and to obtain a rank-K reconstruction whose columns can be clustered to recover the permutation. The paper includes synthetic experiments and a UCI handwritten character trajectory study, and it suggests using the spectral solution as an EM initializer (cf. Chaganty & Liang, 2013).

Strengths:
- Clear and intuitive reduction of MHMM learning to HMM learning via a block-diagonal transition matrix, enabling direct reuse of spectral HMM estimators.
- A practical and novel take on the permutation problem: use eigenvalue-1 structure and column clustering of a rank-K reconstruction to recover component assignments.
- Addresses the noisy-estimation regime and proposes a data-driven way to estimate K via eigenvalue “longevity,” which is an interesting heuristic grounded in spectral gaps.
- Potential for substantial computational savings relative to EM on large datasets, as MoM decouples estimation cost from dataset size once moments are computed.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Technical completeness: Several claims are left as conjectures or re-uses of bounds from related but not identical settings (e.g., Conjecture 1, sample complexity reuse from mixture/tensor results). Either provide a proof sketch tailored to the MHMM setting (including perturbation analysis for the block structure) or add stronger empirical validation that quantifies approximation error versus TN.
- Assumptions: The method relies on each Ak having a single eigenvalue of magnitude 1 (others strictly inside the unit circle). This is standard for ergodic, aperiodic chains, but the paper should state the precise conditions (irreducible, aperiodic) and discuss what happens if components violate them (e.g., near-periodic chains, shared eigenvalues across blocks).
- Estimating K: The spectral longevity criterion (Eq. 7) is appealing but ad hoc. Please justify the choice of γ and the exponentiation range, analyze sensitivity, and compare against standard methods (e.g., eigengap heuristics, information criteria).
- Clarity and notation: Notation is sometimes inconsistent or undefined. Define all vectors/matrices and their dimensions on first use; explicitly introduce 1J as a length-J vector of ones (and its transpose), and clarify the structure of lim e→∞ Āe (i.e., K blocks of size M×M with identical columns within each block). Consider aligning notation with Hsu et al. (2009): T for transitions, n/m for observations/hidden states, etc.
- Related work: The paper should discuss prior MHMM work (e.g., Smyth, NIPS 1997; Qi et al., 2007; Oates et al., 1999) and spectral/tensor methods for mixtures and HMMs (Hsu et al., 2009; Anandkumar et al., 2012). Also note that using spectral estimates to initialize likelihood-based methods has been explored (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, 2013).
- Experiments: The synthetic studies are limited. Include:
  - Stronger comparisons to EM with multiple random restarts, reporting best-of-run accuracy (standard practice) and runtime.
  - Sensitivity to K, M, sequence length, number of sequences, and noise; ablate the de-permutation step and the K-estimation heuristic.
  - Additional emission models (discrete/multinomial, Poisson) to match the method’s generality.
  - Clear tables with numerical results and standard errors; Figure 4 is hard to read.
  - For the UCI data, explain why EM underperforms; report both average and best EM results, stopping criteria, and compute times for all methods.

Quality:
The reduction and the de-permutation intuition are sound under the stated spectral assumptions. However, the noisy-case theory is incomplete and some bounds are asserted without proof. Experimental validation, while suggestive, is not yet strong enough to fully support the claims across regimes.

Clarity:
The paper would benefit from consistent notation, explicit dimensions, and clearer explanations of spectral limits and block structure. Replace hard-to-parse figures with tables and add methodological details (e.g., γ choice, exponent range, clustering settings).

Originality:
Casting MHMM learning as HMM spectral learning is straightforward; the contribution lies in the de-permutation mechanism and the K-estimation heuristic. These ideas appear novel and practically useful, though they build closely on prior spectral HMM work.

Significance:
If robust, the method can make MHMM learning scalable and provide high-quality initializations for EM, which is valuable. Demonstrating consistent gains across broader settings would strengthen impact.

Specific comments:
- Please define 1J (column) and 1J^T (row) and the dimensions of all matrices/vectors upon first appearance.
- In Corollary 1, explicitly show that lim e→∞ Āe yields K rank-1 blocks vk 1M^T.
- Clarify the conditions under which Ā and ĀP share eigenvectors in the presence of estimation noise, and justify the rank-K reconstruction step.
- Report best-of-5 (or more) EM accuracies and runtimes; clarify initialization schemes and stopping criteria.
- Consider citing and comparing against alternative model-order selection criteria for K.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, intuitive approach with promising computational advantages.
- Novel and practical de-permutation strategy; useful heuristic for estimating K.
- Potentially valuable as an EM initializer and for large-scale sequence clustering.

Cons against acceptance:
- Theoretical guarantees in the noisy case are incomplete; key results are conjectural.
- Limited and sometimes unclear empirical evaluation; figures are hard to interpret.
- Related work coverage is insufficient; notation and exposition issues reduce readability.

Overall assessment:
An interesting and potentially useful contribution that leverages spectral HMM learning for MHMMs and proposes a novel de-permutation mechanism. With clearer exposition, stronger related-work discussion, more rigorous analysis (or more extensive empirical evidence), and improved experiments and reporting, the paper would be considerably stronger.
