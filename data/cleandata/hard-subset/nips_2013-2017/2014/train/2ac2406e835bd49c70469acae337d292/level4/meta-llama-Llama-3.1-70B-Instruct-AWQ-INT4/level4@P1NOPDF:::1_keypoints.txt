- Summary: Proposes spectral learning for HMMs followed by permutation recovery to reveal block-diagonal mixture structure.

- Major 1: Paper is well-written but the experimental section needs more detail to properly evaluate the method.

- Major 2: Method depends on first accurately estimating a full HMM, which is impractical for large, sparse block-diagonal mixtures—need guidance on when the algorithm is appropriate.

- Major 3: No guarantee the spectral-learned transition matrix will be (nearly) sparse; many full-matrix HMMs may be close to the sparse target under noise.

- Major 4: Experiments use synthetic permutation noise on transition matrices rather than realistic observation-sampled data; should evaluate learning from observed sequences to reflect real noise and data scarcity.

- Major 5: Section 4.2 lacks specifics about the mixture HMM parameters (transition and observation matrices), which are crucial for judging experiment difficulty; provide details or an appendix.

- Major 6: Table 1 shows EM initialized with spectral sometimes worse than spectral alone, which contradicts expectations and needs explanation.

- Minor 1: Likely typo in proof (subscript of Λ should be K, not 2).

- Minor 2: Figures have text and numbers that are too small to read.

- Overall concern: Interesting theory but substantial doubts remain about practical performance on real datasets.
