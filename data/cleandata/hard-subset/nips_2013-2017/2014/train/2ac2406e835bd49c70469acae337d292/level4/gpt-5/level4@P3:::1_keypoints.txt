- Summary: Proposes learning mixtures of HMMs by treating the MHMM as a single block-diagonal HMM, using spectral HMM estimation followed by a de-permutation step exploiting eigen-structure to recover components and estimate K.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on spectral/method-of-moments HMM estimators; novelty is applying de-permutation and an eigenvalue-based K-selection heuristic to MHMMs.  
- Strength: Clear, modular algorithm: spectral global HMM estimation then eigen-structure de-permutation.  
- Strength: Potential computational advantage over EM when many long sequences make EM expensive.  
- Strength: Intuitive noiseless analysis and a practical “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K.  
- Strength: Useful as an initializer for EM on real data.  
- Weakness: Practicality concern — method requires estimating an MK×MK global HMM first, which may be infeasible when the full model is large or ill-conditioned; need to specify intended operating regimes (M, K, L, samples, mixing/separation).  
- Weakness: Identifiability and assumptions — Lemma 2 assumes each component has a single eigenvalue of magnitude 1; sensitivity to slow mixing, near-periodicity, or near-violations is unclear and Conjecture 1 is unproven.  
- Weakness: Sparsity concerns — spectrally learned transition matrices will be dense in practice; need procedures (thresholding, regularization, projection) and confidence measures for inferred blocks.  
- Weakness: Missing end-to-end experiments — must evaluate full pipeline from observation data through moments to de-permutation to capture real noise and finite-sample effects.  
- Weakness: Experimental detail lacking — need exact generative settings, parameter choices, and reporting of de-permutation/K-estimation accuracy and sensitivity to spectral gaps and sample sizes.  
- Weakness: Real-data setup issue — expanding L via polynomial features to meet MK < L may distort the observation model; alternatives and implications should be discussed.  
- Weakness: EM interaction unexplained — clarify why EM initialized with spectral estimates sometimes underperforms the spectral solution alone.  
- Weakness: Complexity/sample complexity unclear — provide concrete time/memory costs for moments and eigendecomposition and compare to EM, and clarify applicability of borrowed sample-complexity bounds.  
- Quality (soundness): Noiseless arguments are sound, but central robustness claim remains a conjecture needing proof sketch or empirical validation.  
- Quality (empirics): Promising but insufficient to demonstrate robustness across realistic noise levels and larger models.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; figures need larger fonts, legends clearer, and notation tightened; make Lemma 2 assumptions explicit early.  
- Originality: Moderate — reduction of MHMM to HMM is known; main novelty is spectral de-permutation and K-selection heuristic.  
- Significance: Potentially useful for fast approximate MHMM learning or EM initialization, but requires stronger empirical and theoretical support.  
- Specific suggestion: Add end-to-end synthetic studies varying sequences, lengths, spectral gaps, block separation, and report recovery errors for O, A, ν, cluster assignments, and K.  
- Specific suggestion: Evaluate K-estimation vs. eigenvalue separation and sample sizes; compare longevity to eigengap or information-criteria methods.  
- Specific suggestion: Explore post-processing to enforce block-diagonality and report failure modes and guidance for applicability.  
- Minor comments: Fix notation/typos (e.g., subscript in Λ, possessives), enlarge figure fonts, and clarify MK < L requirement.  
- Pros for acceptance: Elegant spectral use to resolve permutation and estimate K; computationally attractive alternative/initializer to EM; clear exposition of core idea.  
- Cons for acceptance: Central robustness claim is conjectural, experiments lack end-to-end validation and detail, and practical applicability is unclear when global estimation is hard.  
- Overall: Interesting and promising idea but needs stronger end-to-end experiments, clearer assumptions/operating regimes, and theoretical or empirical support for the spectral-separation conjecture before acceptance.
