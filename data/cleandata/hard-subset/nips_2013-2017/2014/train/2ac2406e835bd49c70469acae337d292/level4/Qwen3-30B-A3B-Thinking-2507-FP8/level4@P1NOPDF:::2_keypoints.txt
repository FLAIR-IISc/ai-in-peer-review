- Presents a spectral algorithm for learning MHMMs by treating MHMMs as a special case of spectral HMM methods (Anandkumar et al., 2012).  
- Proposes a novel stabilization approach to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Notational inconsistencies throughout the paper need correction and standardization.  
- Authors should more explicitly cite related work (e.g., Chaganty and Liang 2013 on EM initialized with spectral estimates).  
- Experimental presentation is simplistic; Figure 4 should be replaced by a table for clarity.  
- EM results in Table 1 are puzzling—report best (not average) EM accuracy, since EM typically improves with iterations despite local optima.  
- Corollary 1 requires clearer exposition: explicitly define 1_J^T and describe lim_{ε→∞} \bar{A}^ε as a JK×JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns per J×J block.  
- Define all vectors and matrices with their dimensions at first introduction to improve clarity.  
- Minor suggestion: adopt standard HMM notation from Hsu et al. (2009), e.g., use T for transitions and n, m for numbers of observation/hidden states.  
- The stabilization technique is interesting and intuitively sound; worth attention.  
- The analysis under estimation noise is strong and notable, especially the clever application of eigenvalues (Eq. (7)).  
- Overall contribution is well-motivated but presentation (notation, experiments, contextualization) needs substantial refinement to meet conference standards.
