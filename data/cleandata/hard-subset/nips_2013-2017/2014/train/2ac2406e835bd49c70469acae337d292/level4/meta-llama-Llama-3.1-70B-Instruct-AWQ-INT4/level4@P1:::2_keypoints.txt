- Presents a spectral algorithm for learning MHMMs by casting MHMM as an HMM and applying Anandkumar et al. (2012) off-the-shelf.  
- Introduces a stabilization-based solution to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides analysis under estimation noise that leverages eigenvalue behavior in the algorithm.  
- Demonstrates strong computational efficiency in experiments.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, and claims are supported by theory and experiments.  
- Notation and presentation need improvement; vectors/matrices should be defined with dimensions at first use.  
- Missing or insufficient awareness of related work; should cite and discuss methods like initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013).  
- Experiments are basic and presentation could be clearer (prefer tables over Figure 4 and report best EM accuracy instead of average).  
- EM performs unusually poorly in Table 1; authors should investigate and explain this behavior.  
- Clarify specific notations and limits (e.g., define 1J^T as a row vector of ones and explain the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->∞} Ā^e).  
- Novelty: the stabilization step for undoing permutation is an interesting and original contribution.  
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem more effectively and efficiently than prior work.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address notation, presentation, related work, and additional analysis of EM results.
