- Proposes a spectral method to learn mixtures of HMMs that addresses the de-permutation problem.  
- Represents a mixture as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and uses its spectral properties to recover a de-permutation mapping.  
- Builds on standard spectral HMM algorithms with a key modification to handle permutation ambiguity.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear problem statement and proposed solution.  
- Algorithm and underlying theory are clearly and concisely explained.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data are promising and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Presents a novel insight into spectral properties enabling de-permutation, marking an original contribution.  
- Work could have significant impact for domains using HMM mixtures (speech, NLP, bioinformatics).  
- Lacks a detailed empirical comparison with existing methods (e.g., EM for HMM mixtures).  
- More discussion is needed on limitations of the approach and directions for future work.  
- Contains occasional typos and grammatical errors that reduce clarity and professionalism.  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing minor issues such as stronger comparisons and copyediting.
