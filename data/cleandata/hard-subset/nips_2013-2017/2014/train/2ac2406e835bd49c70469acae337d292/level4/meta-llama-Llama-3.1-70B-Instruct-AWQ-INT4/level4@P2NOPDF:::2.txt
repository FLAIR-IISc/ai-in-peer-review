This paper proposes a novel spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The authors demonstrate that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) can be applied directly by formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM. They introduce a stabilization-based solution to address the issue of parameter permutation by assuming that each HMM's transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue of value 1.

The paper has some notable contributions, including the proposed scheme to mitigate the problem of parameter permutation, which is both interesting and intuitive. The authors also provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, leading to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) in the algorithm.

However, there are several areas that require improvement. The notation used throughout the paper is sometimes unclear, and the authors should provide more context and references to related work. For instance, initializing EM with spectral estimates has been explored in Chaganty and Liang (2013), and this should be acknowledged. Additionally, the experimental results are quite basic and could be improved by reporting them in a table format rather than the graphical format in Figure 4.

To enhance the clarity of the paper, the authors should define each vector and matrix along with its dimensions when it is first introduced. This will significantly improve the presentation. Specifically, the terms in Corollary 1, such as 1J^T (a row vector of length J filled with ones), should be clearly defined. Furthermore, the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e (a JK x JK block diagonal matrix where the columns within each J x J block are the same) should be elaborated upon.

The current figure format (Figure 4) is difficult to read and should be replaced with tables. The performance of EM in Table 1 is also surprising, as it typically outperforms spectral methods with sufficient iterations, despite the local optima problem. A standard practice is to report the best (not average) accuracy of EM, and this result should be included.

Finally, conforming to established notation, such as using T for transition matrices (instead of A) and n, m for numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J), as in Hsu et al. (2009), would improve the readability of the paper.

The proposed algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models is a valuable contribution to the field. It consists of an existing spectral algorithm for learning hidden Markov models followed by a novel stabilization step to address parameter permutation. With some revisions to address the mentioned issues, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact.
