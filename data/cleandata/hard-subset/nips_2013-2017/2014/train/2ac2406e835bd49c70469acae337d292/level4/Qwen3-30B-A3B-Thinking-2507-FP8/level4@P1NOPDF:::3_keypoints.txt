- Core algorithm is a trivial variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade & Zhang spectral approach, limiting the novelty.  
- The recovered transition matrix can be an arbitrary permutation of the true matrix, producing permuted component labels.  
- The proposed de-permutation algorithm appears effective empirically but its necessity and theoretical guarantees are not clearly justified.  
- The paper should explicitly justify why de-permutation is required and provide theoretical support for the method.  
- The manuscript contains multiple grammatical errors and typos (example sentence given); needs stronger proofreading.  
- The work lacks sufficient contextualization within prior spectral mixture-model literature.  
- The authors should compare and contrast with related spectral methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, ICML 2013).  
- The paper should explicitly state that Hsu et al.’s algorithm recovers transition matrices only up to row/column permutation.  
- Emphasize that the de-permutation step is needed to recover the correct component structure (not merely a relabeling), which matters for downstream tasks.  
- Overall contribution is not convincingly beyond a straightforward extension of Hsu et al.; clarify precisely what problem the de-permutation solves and how it advances the state of the art.
