This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The authors show that an existing spectral algorithm for learning HMMs can be used off-the-shelf by formulating an MHMM as a special case of HMM. They propose a stabilization-based solution for parameter permutation by introducing an assumption that each HMM's transition matrix has a single eigenvalue of value 1.

The paper is related to previous work on spectral learning of HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) and mixture models (Anandkumar et al., 2013). The authors also draw inspiration from the work of Chaganty and Liang (2013) on initializing EM with spectral estimates.

The proposed scheme to dodge the problem of parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The authors provide an analysis for the case with estimation noise, which leads to an intriguing usage of eigenvalues in the algorithm. However, there are some issues with notation, and the authors could benefit from showing more awareness of related work by providing additional references.

The experiments are quite basic, and I strongly recommend reporting results in a table format rather than the graphical format in Figure 4. Additionally, the authors should consider including the best (not average) accuracy of EM, as it is a standard practice in the field.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the notation could be improved, and the authors should define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, but some sections could be improved. For example, the terms in Corollary 1 could be defined more clearly, and the structure of lim{e->inf} bar{A}^e could be elaborated on. Additionally, the authors could benefit from conforming to the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009).

Originality: The proposed scheme to dodge the problem of parameter permutation is novel and interesting. However, the authors could benefit from showing more awareness of related work and providing additional references.

Significance: The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The algorithm has the potential to be useful in practice, especially in situations where EM is computationally prohibitive.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting solution to the problem of parameter permutation in MHMMs.
* The algorithm has the potential to be useful in practice, especially in situations where EM is computationally prohibitive.
* The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

Arguments For Rejection:

* The notation could be improved, and the authors should define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it's first introduced.
* The experiments are quite basic, and the authors should consider including more comprehensive results.
* The authors could benefit from showing more awareness of related work and providing additional references.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the issues with notation, provide more comprehensive experimental results, and show more awareness of related work.
