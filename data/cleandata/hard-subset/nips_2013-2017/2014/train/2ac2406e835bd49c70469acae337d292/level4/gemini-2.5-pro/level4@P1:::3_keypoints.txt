- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral algorithm for MHMMs by modeling an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Direct spectral recovery yields parameters up to a permutation that mixes component states, preventing individual HMM recovery.  
- Main contribution: a novel depermutation algorithm that clusters eigenvectors of the global transition matrix to reassign states to their original HMM components.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing practical effectiveness.  

Strengths:
- Tackles an important problem and offers an elegant, original extension of spectral methods to hierarchical MHMMs.  
- Offers substantial computational advantages over EM; runtime after moment estimation is dataset-size independent and the method provides strong EM initialization.  
- Clear exposition of the key insight that an MHMM can be represented as a block-diagonal HMM (well explained in Section 3.1).  

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Needs better contextualization within related spectral learning and demixing literature (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013) to clarify novelty.  
- Theoretical support for the depermutation under estimation noise is weak: key claims are conjectural or rely on strong assumptions and sample-complexity is not proven for MHMMs.  
- Manuscript requires proofreading and language polishing to fix typos and awkward phrasing.  
- Experimental evaluation should be broader and analyze sensitivity (e.g., performance as the spectral gap narrows); spectral method does not always outperform randomly initialized EM on real data.  

Overall:
- A novel and computationally efficient approach to MHMM learning with a clever depermutation idea, but it needs stronger theory, broader related-work placement, and more comprehensive experiments.
