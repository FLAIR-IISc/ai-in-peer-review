- Presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs, with the main technical challenge being arbitrarily permuted transition/observation operators that mix component parameters.  
- Central contribution is a novel de-permutation algorithm to disentangle mixed components and recover individual HMMs.  
- Empirical evaluation indicates the proposed de-permutation approach is practically effective.  
- Addresses an important and challenging unsupervised learning problem (mixtures of HMMs).  
- The de-permutation algorithm is a novel solution to a critical bottleneck preventing direct use of existing spectral methods.  
- The initial spectral-step is largely a straightforward extension of prior work (e.g., Hsu et al.), so the paper should emphasize the de-permutation as the primary novelty.  
- Needs better contextualization within related spectral de-mixing literature (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013 and similar works) and clearer comparisons.  
- Manuscript suffers from grammatical errors and typos that reduce clarity; a careful proofreading and rewrite is required.  
- Recommendations: reframe presentation to highlight novelty, expand related-work discussion, and thoroughly proofread to improve readability and positioning.
