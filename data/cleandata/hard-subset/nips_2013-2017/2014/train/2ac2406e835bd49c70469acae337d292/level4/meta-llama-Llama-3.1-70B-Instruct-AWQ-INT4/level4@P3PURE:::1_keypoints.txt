- Summary: Proposes spectral learning for HMM mixtures followed by resolving permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal structure.
- Writing is clear, but the experimental section needs expansion and more experimental detail.
- Core concern: expressing a mixture as a single HMM forces estimation of a full transition matrix, which undermines sparsity advantages of mixture modeling and may be impractical for large/limited-data problems.
- The authors should clarify when their algorithm is suitable versus when it is infeasible due to full-HMM estimation requirements.
- It is unclear whether spectral learning can be guaranteed to recover a transition matrix close to sparse; many full matrices may approximate the desired block-diagonal model.
- Experiments (Section 4.1) use artificial permutation noise on matrices rather than realistic sampling from mixtures of HMMs, so they do not capture noise/limited-data effects on learned parameters.
- Section 4.2 moves toward realistic data but lacks crucial details (transition/observation parameters) needed to assess experiment difficulty and results.
- Table 1: unclear why EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes worsens results—EM would be expected to improve estimates.
- Minor: proof typo—Λ subscript should be K, not 2, in Section 3.2.1.
- Minor: figures have text and numbers that are too small to read.
- Overall: interesting theoretical contributions, but significant concerns remain about practical applicability to real-world datasets.
