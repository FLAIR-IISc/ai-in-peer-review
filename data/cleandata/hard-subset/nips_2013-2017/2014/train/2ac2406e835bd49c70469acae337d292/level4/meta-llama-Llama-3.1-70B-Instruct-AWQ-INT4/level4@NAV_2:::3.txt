The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), addressing the key question of how to "de-permute" the results. This work is related to previous research on spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2, 3] and mixture models [7]. The proposed algorithm is a non-trivial variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade & Zhang algorithm, with the added complexity of dealing with an arbitrary permutation of the true transition matrix, which mixes the various components. The authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that seems to work reasonably well.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem of learning a mixture of HMMs and the challenges associated with it. They also provide a clear explanation of their proposed algorithm and its components, including the de-permutation step.

The technical soundness of the paper is good. The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their algorithm, including a proof of the key lemma that allows them to recover the true transition matrix. They also provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm on both synthetic and real data.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it could do a better job of putting this work into context. For example, the authors could relate their work to other papers on spectral learning for mixture models, such as Chaganty and Liang [8]. Additionally, there are a few typos and minor errors in the paper, such as the phrase "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." These errors do not affect the overall understanding of the paper, but they should be corrected in the final version.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors present a novel algorithm for learning a mixture of HMMs and demonstrate its effectiveness on a range of datasets. With some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
