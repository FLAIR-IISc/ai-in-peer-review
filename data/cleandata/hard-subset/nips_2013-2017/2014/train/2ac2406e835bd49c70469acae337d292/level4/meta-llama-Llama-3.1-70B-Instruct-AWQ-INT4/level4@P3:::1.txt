**Summary**

The authors propose a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) based on the Method of Moments (MoM). They demonstrate that an MHMM can be expressed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and leverage this property to develop a learning algorithm that resolves the permutation ambiguity in the transition matrix. The authors provide experimental results on synthetic and real data, showing that their approach is computationally efficient and comparable in accuracy to Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.

**Major Comments**

1. **Experimental results**: While the experimental results section is well-structured, it could be improved by providing more details about the experimental setup, such as the specific parameters used for the mixture of HMMs and the observation models. This would allow for a more thorough evaluation of the method's performance.
2. **Applicability of the algorithm**: The authors' approach relies on accurately estimating the full HMM parameters and then reversing the permutation to find the block-diagonal structure. However, this may not be feasible for large-scale problems with limited training data. The authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and provide guidance on when it is suitable for practical applications.
3. **Sparsity of the transition matrix**: The authors' algorithm does not guarantee that the learned transition matrix will be sparse. In practice, the recovered transition matrix may not be sparse, and there may be multiple HMM models with full transition matrices that are close to the sparse model with block-diagonal structure. The authors should address this issue and discuss potential solutions.
4. **Noise in the transition matrix**: The experimental results in Section 4.1 do not accurately reflect the reality of noise in the transition matrix. The authors should sample observation data from a mixture of HMMs and learn all the parameters back from the observed data to evaluate the robustness of their approach to noise.
5. **Experimental setup**: The experiment in Section 4.2 lacks details about the mixture of HMMs used, such as the parameters of the transition and observation matrices. The authors should provide more information about the experimental setup to allow for a thorough evaluation of the method's performance.
6. **EM initialization**: In Table 1, EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral learning alone. The authors should explain why this is the case and discuss the implications for using their approach as an initialization scheme for EM.

**Minor Comments**

1. **Typo in the proof**: In Section 3.2.1, the subscript of Λ in the bottom right corner of the first matrix should be K, not 2.
2. **Figure formatting**: The text and numbers in the figures are too small. The authors should increase the font size to improve readability.

**Overall Assessment**

The paper presents an interesting approach to learning MHMMs, and the theoretical results are sound. However, the experimental results could be improved by providing more details about the experimental setup and evaluating the robustness of the approach to noise. Additionally, the authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and provide guidance on when it is suitable for practical applications.
