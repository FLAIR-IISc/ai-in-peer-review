- Proposes applying an existing spectral HMM algorithm (Anandkumar et al., 2012) to learn a mixture of HMMs by formulating an MHMM as a special-case HMM.  
- Introduces a novel stabilization-based solution to the parameter permutation problem, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Notation is sometimes unclear; every vector/matrix and its dimensions should be defined on first introduction.  
- Related work is under-cited—spectral estimates for initializing EM (Chaganty & Liang, 2013) should be discussed and cited.  
- Experiments are basic and presentation is poor (Figure 4 hard to interpret); results should be shown in tables instead of the current graphics.  
- EM’s poor performance in Table 1 is unexpected; report best (not average) EM accuracy and clarify initialization/iteration settings.  
- The stabilization/permutation mitigation scheme is interesting and intuitive and worth highlighting.  
- The analysis of algorithm robustness to estimation noise and the eigenvalue-based result (Equation (7)) is intriguing.  
- Corollary 1 is confusing: explicitly define 1_J^T and clarify the structure of lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e (JK x JK block-diagonal with identical columns in each JxJ block).  
- Minor notation suggestion: adopt Hsu et al. (2009) conventions (use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden-state counts) for consistency.  
- Overall summary: algorithm = existing spectral HMM method + novel stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation.
