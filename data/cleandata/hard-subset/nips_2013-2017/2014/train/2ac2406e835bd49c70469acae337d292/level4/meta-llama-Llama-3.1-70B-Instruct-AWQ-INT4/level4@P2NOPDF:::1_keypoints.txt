1. Paper proposes a spectral algorithm to learn mixtures of HMMs and a method to resolve permutation ambiguity to recover block-diagonal transition structure.

2. Manuscript is well-written but the experiments section needs more detailed descriptions.

3. Algorithm relies on estimating a full HMM then depermuting, raising concerns about feasibility in real-world, large-data-limited settings; guidance on suitability is needed.

4. Unclear whether the spectral method can guarantee sparse or near-sparse recovered transition matrices; multiple full-matrix models may approximate a sparse model, especially with noise.

5. Noise experiments using Dirichlet-permuted transition matrices do not mimic the realistic setting of learning from observed sequences; experiments should sample observations from mixture HMMs and learn parameters.

6. Experiment 4.2 lacks key details about the mixture HMM parameters (transition and observation matrices); these should be reported (e.g., in an appendix).

7. Table 1 shows EM initialized with spectral sometimes worse than spectral alone, which contradicts expectations and requires explanation.

8. Minor: In proof (Section 3.2.1) the Λ subscript in the bottom-right of the first matrix appears incorrect and should be K instead of 2.

9. Minor: Figures have text and numbers that are too small and should be enlarged for readability.

10. Overall: Interesting ideas and solid theory, but practical applicability to real datasets is a concern.
