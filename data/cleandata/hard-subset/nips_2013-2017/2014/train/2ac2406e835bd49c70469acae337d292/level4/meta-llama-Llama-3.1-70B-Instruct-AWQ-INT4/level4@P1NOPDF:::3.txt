The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The algorithm is a variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang algorithm, with the notable exception that the transition matrix recovered is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, mixing the various components. The authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to work reasonably well.

There are occasional typos throughout the paper, such as "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." A thorough proofread is necessary to eliminate these errors.

To provide a clearer understanding of the contributions and relevance of this work, the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of its context. For instance, the authors could relate their work to papers such as Chaganty and Liang's "Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions" (ICML, 2013). By doing so, they can effectively situate their spectral method for learning a mixture of HMMs within the broader literature, highlighting its significance and novelty in addressing the key question of how to "de-permute" the results.
