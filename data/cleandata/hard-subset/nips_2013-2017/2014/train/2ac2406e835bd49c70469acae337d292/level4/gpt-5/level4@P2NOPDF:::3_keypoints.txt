- Presents a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs, essentially a straightforward variant of Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang’s approach.  
- As with spectral methods, recovered transition matrices are identifiable only up to permutation, which in the mixture setting can mix components.  
- Proposes a de-permutation (alignment) procedure to align mixture components; appears to work reasonably well in practice.  
- Writing and presentation need improvement—several typos and grammatical errors (e.g., “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy”); paper should be proofread.  
- Needs clearer placement within broader spectral-learning and mixture literature; should draw connections to related work such as Chaganty and Liang (ICML 2013) to clarify novelty.  
- Overall: addresses the important problem of de-permuting spectral estimates in mixtures of HMMs; alignment step is a useful addition but paper would be stronger with better proofreading and contextualization.
