- Proposes a spectral learning method for Mixtures of HMMs (MHMMs) by reformulating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Introduces a depermutation procedure that uses spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters from permuted outputs.  
- Validated on synthetic and handwritten-character datasets, showing accuracy comparable to EM while being significantly faster.  
- Novel contribution: solves the permutation ambiguity in MHMMs, filling a gap in prior spectral learning literature.  
- The depermutation algorithm is a key technical advance with theoretical grounding (Definitions 1–3, Lemmas 2–3).  
- Demonstrates practical utility: spectral method can serve as an effective and scalable initialization for EM.  
- Technical quality is strong: equivalence claim (Lemma 1) and spectral conditions are rigorously argued.  
- Experiments compare accuracy and speed against appropriate baselines (random and MoM-initialized EM) across data regimes.  
- Conjectured theoretical bound (Conjecture 1) is plausible though not fully proven.  
- Clarity issues: explanations of the global transition matrix and depermutation logic lack intuition and detail.  
- Algorithm 1 omits implementation specifics (how to compute spectral longevity, why clustering avoids brute-force search).  
- Paper understates relation to prior work—needs clearer contrast with Chaganty & Liang (mixtures of regressions) and EM-based MHMM methods (e.g., Qi et al.).  
- Minor presentation errors and typos reduce accessibility.  
- Recommendation: accept pending revisions to exposition (add example/pseudocode for depermutation and strengthen related-work discussion).
