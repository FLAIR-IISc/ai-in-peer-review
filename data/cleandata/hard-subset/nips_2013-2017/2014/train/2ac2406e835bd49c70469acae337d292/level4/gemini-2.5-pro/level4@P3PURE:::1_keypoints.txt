- Summary: Proposes learning a mixture of HMMs by spectral learning of a single equivalent HMM followed by a de-permutation step to recover block-diagonal structure.

- Major 1: Paper is well-written but experiments need more detail and expansion.

- Major 2: Method may be impractical for large-scale problems because it first estimates a large dense HMM, negating the sparsity benefits of mixture models.

- Major 3: No theoretical guarantee is provided that the spectral estimate will be close to a permuted block-diagonal matrix; dense alternatives fitting the data may exist.

- Major 4: Section 4.1's experiment is unrealistic — it perturbs a transition matrix directly instead of performing an end-to-end evaluation from observation sequences.

- Major 5: Section 4.2 is closer to end-to-end but lacks crucial details (ground-truth transition and observation parameters) needed to assess problem difficulty and validity.

- Major 6: Table 1 shows EM sometimes degrading performance after spectral initialization, which is unexpected and requires clarification.

- Minor 1: Typo in proof: subscript of Λ in one matrix should likely be 'K' not '2'.

- Minor 2: Figures have text and numbers that are too small and hard to read.

- Overall: Interesting theoretical contributions but significant concerns remain about practical applicability due to reliance on learning a large, dense HMM.
