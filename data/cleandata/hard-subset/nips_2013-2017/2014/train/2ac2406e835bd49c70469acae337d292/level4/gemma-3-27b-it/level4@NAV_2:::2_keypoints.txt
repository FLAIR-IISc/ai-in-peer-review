- Proposes a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by formulating an MHMM as a specific HMM instance.  
- Leverages Anandkumar et al. (2012) spectral HMM method and applies it to the block-diagonal MHMM transition matrix.  
- Introduces a depermutation method to resolve parameter permutation ambiguity, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to one.  
- Correctly frames MHMM learning challenges and contrasts EM’s computational limits with Method of Moments (MoM) benefits.  
- The block-diagonal formulation is an insightful core idea enabling reuse of existing spectral techniques.  
- Spectral analysis of the global transition matrix under estimation noise is well-motivated and leads to a practical algorithm.  
- Conjectures spectral longevity (Definition 3 and Lemma 3) as a promising way to determine the number of HMMs.  
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded approach to parameter identifiability in MHMMs.  
- Strength: offers a computationally efficient alternative to EM for large datasets.  
- Weakness: notation is sometimes unclear and inconsistent; variable/matrix definitions and dimensions need clarification.  
- Weakness: related work coverage is incomplete—e.g., missing discussion of EM initialized by spectral estimates like Chaganty & Liang (2013).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited; Figure 4 is hard to interpret and should be tabulated.  
- Weakness: EM’s reported performance seems unexpectedly poor; should report best EM accuracy across restarts for fair comparison.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, related work discussion, and experimental reporting.
