This paper presents a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM). The key idea is to formulate an MHMM as a special case of an HMM and then apply the spectral algorithm of Anandkumar et al. (2012) off the shelf. To address parameter permutation, the paper proposes a stabilization step that assumes each component HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1. This scheme for avoiding parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive. The authors also analyze the case with estimation noise, leading to an intriguing use of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) within the algorithm.

There are, however, several presentation and experimental issues. The notation has inconsistencies and gaps, and the paper should demonstrate greater awareness of related work by adding references—e.g., initializing EM with spectral estimates has already been explored by Chaganty and Liang (2013). The experiments are quite basic, and the presentation of results could be improved. I strongly recommend reporting results in table form rather than using the graphical format of Figure 4. Moreover, it is unclear why EM performs so poorly in Table 1. Typically, even with random initialization, EM surpasses spectral methods given enough iterations (despite local optima). A standard practice is to report the best (not average) EM accuracy; please include that result.

Comments:
- I was initially confused by the terms in Corollary 1. Please define 1J^T (as a row vector of length J filled with ones) and elaborate on the structure of lim_{e→∞} bar{A}^e (it is JK × JK block diagonal, where the columns within each J × J block are the same).
- In general, define each vector/matrix along with its dimensions when it is first introduced; this will greatly improve readability.
- Figure 4 is hard to read; please replace it with tables. Also, clarify why EM is performing so badly in Table 1 and include the best EM accuracy across runs, not just the average.
- Nit: It may help readers if the paper conforms to the established notation of Hsu et al. (2009); namely, use T for transition matrices (rather than A) and n, m for the numbers of observation and hidden states (rather than L, J).

Overall, the paper proposes an algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models: it applies an existing spectral algorithm for HMMs and follows it with a novel stabilization step for undoing parameter permutation.
