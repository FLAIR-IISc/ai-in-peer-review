This paper presents a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The proposed algorithm is a variant of the standard method by Hsu, Kakade & Zhang. The key technical challenge addressed is that a naive application of such a spectral method recovers a transition matrix that is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, thus mixing the components of the individual HMMs. The authors propose a de-permutation algorithm to resolve this issue, and the approach appears to work reasonably well.

However, the paper could be improved in a few areas:

*   **Presentation:** The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to correct several typographical and grammatical errors. For example, one sentence reads, "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." These errors detract from the paper's overall clarity.

*   **Context:** The paper could be strengthened by better situating the work within the existing literature. A discussion of its relationship to other spectral methods for mixture models would provide valuable context, such as the following work on mixtures of linear regressions:

    Chaganty, A. and Liang, P. "Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions." In *International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)*, 2013.
