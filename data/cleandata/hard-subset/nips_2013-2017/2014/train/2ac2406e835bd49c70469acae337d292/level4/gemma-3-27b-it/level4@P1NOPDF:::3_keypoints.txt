- Proposes a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs that extends Hsu, Kakade & Zhang by producing a transition matrix permuted across mixture components.  
- Introduces a depermutation algorithm to recover correct component labeling; it appears to work reasonably well in experiments.  
- Presentation contains minor typos and needs careful proofreading (example sentence flagged).  
- Related-work discussion is weak; missing connections to papers like Chaganty & Liang (ICML 2013).  
- Core idea—handling reordering of mixture components in spectral methods—is valuable but conceptually incremental.  
- Unclear advantage of the depermutation algorithm over repeated runs of the standard spectral method with random restarts.  
- Experiments rely mainly on synthetic data, leaving generalization to real-world HMM mixtures untested.  
- Ablation studies are limited; need sensitivity analysis of depermutation parameters and comparisons to simpler permutation strategies.  
- Theoretical analysis is present but shallow; should include conditions for success and error bounds for the depermutation algorithm.  
- Overall technically sound but incremental; improvements in experiments and theory would strengthen the paper.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing empirical, theoretical, and presentation concerns.
