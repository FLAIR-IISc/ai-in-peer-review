The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The algorithm is a subtle, yet important, variant of the standard Hsu, Kakade & Zhang algorithm; however, the recovered transition matrix is an arbitrary permutation of the true one, effectively mixing the mixture components. To address this, the authors propose a depermutation algorithm that appears to perform reasonably well in practice.

The paper would benefit from more thorough proofreading, as several minor typos were observed, for example: “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.” 

Furthermore, the paper could be strengthened by better contextualization within the existing literature. Relating this work to papers such as Chaganty and Liang, “Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions,” *International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)*, 2013, would be particularly beneficial. 

In essence, the authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of HMMs, and crucially, address the fundamental problem of how to de-permute the resulting components.
