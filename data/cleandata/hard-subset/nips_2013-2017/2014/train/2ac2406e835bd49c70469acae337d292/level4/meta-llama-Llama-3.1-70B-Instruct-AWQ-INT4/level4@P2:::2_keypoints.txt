- Presents a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by formulating an MHMM as a special-case HMM usable with existing spectral methods.  
- Proposes a stabilization-based solution to parameter permutation using the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Provides analysis under estimation noise that leverages eigenvalue behavior in the algorithm.  
- Builds on prior spectral HMM and mixture work (Anandkumar et al. 2012/2013) and is inspired by Chaganty & Liang (2013) for EM initialization.  
- The stabilization scheme to avoid parameter permutation is novel and intuitive.  
- Theoretical claims are technically sound and supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Notation is problematic: vectors/matrices and their dimensions are not consistently defined at first introduction.  
- Clarity issues: Corollary 1 terms and the limit structure lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e need clearer definition and alignment with established HMM notation (e.g., Hsu et al. 2009).  
- Experiments are basic; results should be reported in tables rather than only plots and should include best (not just average) EM accuracy.  
- Authors should show greater awareness of related work and add additional references.  
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and could be practically useful where EM is computationally costly.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel permutation solution, technical soundness, practical potential.  
- Arguments for rejection: weak notation, limited experiments, incomplete literature contextualization.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix notation, expand experiments, and cite more related work.
