- Summary: Proposes a spectral algorithm to learn a mixture of HMMs by treating the mixture as a single HMM and resolving permutation ambiguity to recover its block-diagonal structure.

- Experiments: Paper is generally well-written but the experimental section needs expansion and deeper insights.

- Data requirements/feasibility: Algorithm requires accurately estimating full HMM parameters before undoing the permutation, which may be infeasible for large problems or limited training data; authors should discuss real-world limitations.

- Sparsity guarantee: The recovered transition matrix may not remain sparse in practice; need analysis on whether spectral methods can guarantee (or approximate) sparsity given nearby dense models.

- Noise modeling in experiments: Current noise experiments (permuting a matrix with Dirichlet noise) are inadequate; authors should generate observation data from a mixture of HMMs and learn parameters from that to assess noise robustness.

- Experiment details: Section 4.2 lacks crucial details (transition/observation matrix parameters) — include them (e.g., in an appendix) for reproducibility and evaluation.

- EM results anomaly: Table 1 shows EM initialized with spectral learning performing worse than spectral alone, which is counterintuitive and requires explanation.

- Minor typo: In proof of Section 3.2.1, the subscript of Λ in the bottom-right of the first matrix should be K, not 2.

- Figure readability: Text and numbers in figures are too small and should be enlarged.

- Overall assessment: Interesting ideas and solid theory, but practical applicability to real datasets is questionable.
