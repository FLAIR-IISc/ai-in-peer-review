- Proposes a spectral algorithm for learning mixtures of HMMs by reformulating an MHMM as a specific HMM instance.  
- Introduces a stabilization-based method to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Stabilization technique is interesting, intuitive, and a novel contribution to handle permutation.  
- Provides analysis for estimation noise that leverages eigenvalues (Equation (7)) in the algorithm.  
- Notation throughout the paper is unclear; vectors/matrices and their dimensions should be defined when first introduced.  
- Corollary 1 is confusing; explicitly define 1J^T and clarify the structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e (JK×JK block diagonal with identical columns in each J×J block).  
- Related work discussion is weak; must cite prior work such as Chaganty and Liang (2013) on initializing EM with spectral estimates.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and presentation is poor—replace Figure 4 with tables for clearer results.  
- EM results are puzzling (poor performance); report best (not average) EM accuracy and explain why EM underperforms here.  
- Minor notation suggestions: follow Hsu et al. (2009) conventions (use T for transitions, n/m for observation/hidden-state counts instead of A/L/J).  
- Overall summary: combines an existing spectral HMM algorithm with a novel stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation in MHMM learning.
