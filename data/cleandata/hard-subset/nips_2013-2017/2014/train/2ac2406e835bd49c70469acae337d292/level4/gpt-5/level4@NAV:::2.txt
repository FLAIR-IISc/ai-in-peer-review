This paper introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of hidden Markov models (MHMMs). It shows that the spectral HMM learner of Anandkumar et al. (2012) can be used off the shelf by casting an MHMM as a special-case HMM. It also proposes a stabilization-based approach to resolve parameter permutation, relying on the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue at 1.

There appear to be some notation issues. The authors should better situate the work within prior literature by adding missing references (e.g., using spectral estimates to initialize EM has already been studied by Chaganty and Liang (2013)). The experimental evaluation is fairly basic, and I strongly recommend reporting results in tables rather than the plot in Figure 4.

That said, the proposed tactic to avoid parameter permutation is compelling and intuitive. The paper also treats the setting with estimation noise, leading to an interesting role for eigenvalues (Equation (7)) within the algorithm.

Comments:
- I was initially puzzled by the terms in Corollary 1. Please define 1J^T (as a length-J row vector of ones) and expand on the structure of lim_{e->inf} bar{A}^e (namely, a JK-by-JK block-diagonal matrix in which the columns within each J-by-J block are identical).
- In general, define every vector/matrix together with its dimensions at first mention; this will greatly improve readability.
- Figure 4 is hard to interpret; please replace it with tables. Also, I am unsure why EM performs so poorly in Table 1. Typically, even with random initialization, given enough iterations it outperforms spectral methods (despite local optima). A common practice is to report the best (not the average) EM accuracy; please include that.
- Nit: It may help to align with the notation of Hsu et al. (2009): use T for transition matrices (not A), and n, m for the numbers of observation and hidden states (instead of L, J).

Overall, the paper presents an algorithm for learning a mixture of HMMs consisting of an existing spectral HMM learner followed by a new stabilization step to undo parameter permutation.
