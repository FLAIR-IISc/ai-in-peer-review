This paper proposes a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMM). The core idea leverages the Method of Moments (MoM) for initial parameter estimation, recognizing that this approach yields a permutation ambiguity in the recovered transition matrix, effectively mixing the components of the mixture. The authors then introduce a depermutation algorithm based on spectral properties to address this issue and recover individual HMM parameters.

This work builds upon existing spectral learning techniques for HMMs, particularly the Hsu, Kakade & Zhang (2013) algorithm, and extends it to the mixture model setting. While the extension seems conceptually straightforward – addressing the permutation issue – it’s a significant contribution to make MoM applicable to MHMMs. The problem of permutation ambiguity is clearly identified and the proposed solution, exploiting the spectral properties of the global transition matrix, appears well-motivated. The authors demonstrate the validity of their approach through both synthetic and real-world experiments, showing competitive performance against standard EM-based methods.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses a key challenge:** The paper tackles a significant hurdle in applying MoM to MHMMs—the permutation ambiguity—with a novel and seemingly effective solution.
*   **Clear exposition of the problem and solution:** The authors clearly articulate the problem of permutation and provide a well-explained spectral approach for its resolution. The connection to existing HMM spectral learning algorithms is also well established.
*   **Empirical validation:** The experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the practical viability of the proposed method. The comparison with EM, including using the proposed method for EM initialization, is valuable.
*   **Theoretical grounding:** The analysis of spectral properties and the conjectures regarding the conditions for successful depermutation provide a theoretical foundation for the method.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited novelty beyond the extension:** While the contribution of adapting the spectral method to MHMMs and addressing the permutation problem is valuable, the core idea doesn't represent a radical departure from existing spectral learning techniques.
*   **Clarity of spectral conditions:** The conditions for successful depermutation (Definitions 1 & 3 and Conjecture 1) are mathematically complex and could benefit from more intuitive explanations. The practical implications of these conditions are not fully discussed.
*   **Typographical errors and minor imprecisions:** The manuscript contains a few minor typos and phrasing issues (e.g., the example provided in the original review), which detract from the overall polish. More careful proofreading is needed.
*   **Missing related work:** The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, specifically mentioning Chaganty and Liang (2013) on spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions, as pointed out in the original review, and any other relevant spectral methods for mixture models.
*   **Real data experiment limitations:** The real data experiment involves a feature transformation. While justified, it might be beneficial to explore the performance on the raw data or discuss the impact of the transformation more thoroughly.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a practical solution to a non-trivial problem in MHMM learning.
*   The proposed method shows promising results in both synthetic and real-world experiments.
*   The work is well-motivated and builds upon established techniques in the field.
*   The theoretical analysis, while complex, provides a foundation for understanding the method's behavior.

**Arguments for rejection:**

*   The novelty of the contribution is incremental.
*   The spectral conditions for successful depermutation are complex and may limit the method’s practical applicability.
*   The manuscript requires further polishing to address minor errors and improve clarity.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field. The authors have successfully extended existing spectral learning techniques to address a practical challenge in MHMM learning. While the novelty is not groundbreaking, the method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated. The primary concerns relate to the complexity of the spectral conditions and the need for further polishing of the manuscript. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Thoroughly proofread the manuscript to eliminate typographical errors and improve clarity.
2.  Expand the related work section to include Chaganty and Liang (2013) and other relevant spectral methods for mixture models.
3.  Provide a more intuitive explanation of the spectral conditions for successful depermutation and discuss their practical implications.
4.  Consider further discussion on the impact of feature transformations on the results of the real data experiment.
