- Introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs with a new “depermutation” algorithm to fix arbitrary component permutations in recovered transition matrices.  
- Builds on existing spectral techniques (e.g., Hsu, Kakade & Zhang) and targets a critical practical issue when applying those methods to mixtures.  
- Depermutation step is non-trivial and significantly improves the approach’s utility; experiments indicate reasonable practical performance.  
- Key strength: clearly identifies and addresses the permutation problem inherent to spectral learning of mixture models.  
- Weakness: inadequate connection to prior work—needs discussion of related spectral mixture methods, e.g., Chaganty & Liang (2013).  
- Presentation suffers from grammatical errors and typos (example: “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy”); requires thorough proofreading.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate but limited in datasets and baselines; could be more comprehensive.  
- Needs more detailed empirical analysis across conditions (varying numbers of components, noise levels, different HMM structures).  
- Lacks analysis of computational complexity and discussion of scalability to larger datasets and more complex mixtures.  
- Recommendation: with improved related work, clearer presentation, stronger experiments, and complexity analysis, the paper could be a valuable NIPS workshop contribution.
