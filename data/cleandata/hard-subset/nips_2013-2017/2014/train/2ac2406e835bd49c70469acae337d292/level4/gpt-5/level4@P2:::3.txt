Summary
This paper proposes a spectral method-of-moments (MoM) approach to learn mixtures of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The key observation is that an MHMM can be represented as a single HMM whose transition matrix is block-diagonal, with each block corresponding to a component HMM. The authors first estimate the parameters of this “global” HMM using existing spectral HMM techniques (e.g., Hsu, Kakade, and Zhang), which are efficient and avoid local optima, but return parameters only up to an unknown permutation of latent states. They then introduce a depermutation procedure based on spectral properties of the global transition matrix: in the noiseless case, repeated powers of the estimated transition matrix reveal K clusters (one per component HMM); with noise, they propose a low-rank reconstruction using the top K “global” eigenvalues and define a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K and recover the permutation. Experiments on synthetic data and a UCI character trajectory dataset show that the method can accurately recover blocks and serves as a fast initializer for EM.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds directly on spectral learning for HMMs (Hsu, Kakade, Zhang; Anandkumar et al.), extending it to mixtures through a block-diagonal perspective and a post-hoc alignment step. The depermutation problem is closely related to component alignment issues in other spectral mixture models, such as mixtures of linear regressions (Chaganty and Liang, ICML 2013). It would help to position the contribution relative to this broader literature on alignment/permutation recovery in spectral methods and to clarify what is novel compared to prior spectral approaches for mixtures.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity: Representing MHMMs as a block-diagonal HMM is clear and useful.
- Computational efficiency: After moment computation, learning scales independently of the number and length of sequences, which can offer substantial speedups over EM.
- Practical depermutation strategy: The eigen-structure–based clustering is intuitive and empirically effective; the heuristic for estimating K is pragmatic.
- Empirical utility: The method is a competitive initializer for EM and shows good performance on synthetic and real data.

Weaknesses
- Incomplete theory: Key steps in the noisy setting rely on conjectures and heuristics (e.g., separation of “global” vs. “local” eigenvalues, finite-sample bounds reused from related models). The paper would benefit from formal guarantees or clearer conditions.
- Sensitivity assumptions: The method assumes each Ak has a unique eigenvalue at 1 and that eigenvalue separation persists under noise. The robustness of the depermutation and K-estimation to small spectral gaps is not fully characterized.
- Practical constraints: The approach requires MK < L (as noted in the real-data experiment), which limits applicability and led to a polynomial feature mapping. The method also assumes the total number of global states MK as input; when K is unknown, it is unclear how M and MK are selected jointly.
- Empirical evaluation: While timing and accuracy comparisons are presented, more comprehensive metrics would strengthen the case (e.g., parameter recovery error, ablations on eigen-gap size, systematic sensitivity to noise and sample size, variance over runs).
- Clarity and polish: There are typos and phrasing issues (e.g., “For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy.”; “ofwho”). Some notation shifts (matrix vs. index) and the spectral longevity definition could be explained more clearly.

Quality
Technically sound in the noiseless case, with reasonable heuristics in the noisy setting. Proofs for the central claims under noise are informal; adding formal analysis or empirical stress tests would improve the scientific rigor.

Clarity
Generally readable, but the exposition of the noisy-case algorithm and the spectral longevity criterion could be clearer. The paper would benefit from careful proofreading to fix typos and minor grammatical errors.

Originality
Moderate. Casting MHMM learning as HMM learning with block structure is natural; the main novelty lies in the spectral depermutation and K-estimation heuristics. Better comparison to prior alignment techniques in spectral learning would help clarify novelty.

Significance
Potentially useful for large-scale sequence clustering, especially as a fast initializer for EM. Broader impact may be limited by the MK < L constraint and sensitivity to eigenvalue separation.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide formal conditions and guarantees (or, at minimum, systematic empirical validation) for the depermutation and K-estimation steps under noise.
- Discuss robustness to small spectral gaps and near-nonergodic components; include stress tests varying the gap |λG_K| vs. max_k |λL_2,k|.
- Clarify how to choose M and MK when K is unknown; discuss model selection strategies and practical diagnostics.
- Report parameter estimation errors in addition to clustering accuracy, and provide variability across runs.
- Strengthen related work, including discussion of alignment/permutation issues in spectral methods (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, Anandkumar et al. on tensor methods).
- Improve clarity of the spectral longevity definition with a brief intuition and a simple example; streamline notation.
- Proofread to fix typos and grammar and standardize citation formatting.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear idea with a practical, fast algorithm for MHMMs
- Useful depermutation heuristic that works well empirically
- Good potential as an EM initializer on real data

Cons against acceptance
- Theory in the noisy regime is heuristic, with conjectured bounds
- Applicability constrained by MK < L and spectral gap requirements
- Empirical evaluation could be broader and more rigorous

Overall
A promising and efficient spectral approach to MHMMs with a sensible depermutation heuristic. With stronger theoretical backing and a more thorough empirical study, this work would make a solid contribution. As is, it is a useful step forward but would benefit from tightened analysis and presentation.
