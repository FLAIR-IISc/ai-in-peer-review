- Paper proposes learning mixtures of HMMs by spectral estimation of a single HMM followed by depermuting to recover block-diagonal structure.
- Writing is clear, but the experimental section needs more elaboration.
- Method relies on accurately estimating the full HMM then reversing permutation, which seems impractical for large real-world problems; guidance on applicability is needed.
- No guarantee the spectral algorithm will recover (approximate) sparsity; learned full transition matrices may not be block-diagonal under noise.
- Experiments inadequately assess impact of observation noise; should sample observations from a mixture of HMMs and learn parameters from data rather than permuting matrices with Dirichlet noise.
- Section 4.2 addresses concerns but omits crucial details about transition and observation matrix parameters; these should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).
- In Table 1, EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone, which is unexpected and needs explanation.
- Minor typo: in proof (Section 3.2.1) the subscript of Λ should be K, not 2.
- Figures’ text and numbers are too small to read comfortably.
- Overall: interesting theoretical contributions but skepticism about reliable performance on real-world datasets.
