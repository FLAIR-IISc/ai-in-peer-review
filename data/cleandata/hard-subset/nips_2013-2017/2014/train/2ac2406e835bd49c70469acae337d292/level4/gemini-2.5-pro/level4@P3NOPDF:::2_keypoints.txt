- Core idea: Reframe a mixture of HMMs (MHMM) as a single larger HMM and apply an existing spectral learning algorithm.  
- Main technical contribution: Introduce a stabilization method to resolve parameter permutation, relying on each component HMM having a single eigenvalue of one.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides analysis accounting for estimation noise and a principled use of eigenvalues (e.g., Eq. 7).  
- Practical/algorithmic strength: Stabilization approach is intuitive and elegant.  
- Clarity/notation issue: Presentation lacks precision—must define all vectors/matrices (with dimensions) on first use, clarify Corollary 1 and block-diagonal limits, and adopt standard spectral-learning notation.  
- Experimental presentation issue: Figure 4 is hard to interpret; results should be presented clearly (e.g., tables).  
- Baseline evaluation issue: EM results look unusually poor—should run multiple random initializations and report best-of-N (not just averages) and clarify experimental setup.  
- Experimental scope issue: Experiments are preliminary; need more complex datasets and sensitivity analysis to the method’s assumptions.  
- Related work/positioning: Literature review is underdeveloped and missing key citations (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013); should discuss spectral initialization for EM and other relevant prior work.  
- Overall verdict: Promising and clever method but requires substantial revision in presentation, experimental rigor, and contextualization before publication.
