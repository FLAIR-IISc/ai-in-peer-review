- Proposes a spectral algorithm that treats an MHMM with K components as a single block-diagonal HMM to apply Method of Moments for parameter learning.  
- Identifies and addresses a permutation ambiguity that mixes parameters of component HMMs when learning the global model.  
- Main contribution: a de-permutation method that clusters columns of the exponentiated global transition matrix to recover component assignments.  
- Provides analysis for handling estimation noise and a procedure to estimate the number of HMM components (K) via "spectral longevity."  
- Validated on synthetic and real datasets, showing computational advantages over EM and utility as an EM initializer.  
- Novel and elegant de-permutation approach that is intuitive and addresses a core challenge in applying MoM to mixtures.  
- Theoretical development is solid, establishing MHMM-to-HMM equivalence and proving de-permutation in the noiseless case.  
- Extends theory to the noisy setting and derives a principled model-selection criterion (Lemma 3).  
- Potential high impact due to much faster computation than EM once moments are computed, suitable for large-scale sequence clustering.  
- Presentation issues: missing explicit dimensions for many matrices/vectors, hurting readability.  
- Related-work context is insufficient; should cite prior uses of spectral estimates to initialize EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Experimental concerns: unclear whether EM baselines used multiple random restarts and best-log-likelihood reporting.  
- Figure 4 is hard to read; recommend tables for clearer comparisons of accuracy vs. computation time.  
- Real-data experiment required a feature transform to satisfy MK < L; discuss practical implications and sensitivity to this constraint.  
- Detailed fixes: define dimensions at first use (e.g., 1_M), clarify block structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e, and consider aligning notation with prior work (e.g., Hsu et al. 2012).
