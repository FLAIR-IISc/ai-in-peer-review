Summary: The authors consider the problem of learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). They propose first using a spectral learning algorithm to estimate parameters for a single HMM equivalent to the mixture, and then resolving the permutation ambiguity in the transition matrix to recover its underlying block-diagonal structure.

Major Comments:
1. The paper is generally very well written. The experimental section, however, should be expanded. In particular, please provide more detail on datasets, parameter settings, and evaluation metrics.

2. The approach relies on the fact that a mixture of HMMs can be expressed as a single HMM. One major motivation for learning a mixture rather than a single HMM directly is that the mixture’s block-diagonal transition structure is sparse. Such sparsity can be crucial when the full model is too large relative to the available data. In this paper, however, the algorithm first requires accurately estimating the full HMM parameters and only then reversing the permutation to reveal the block-diagonal structure. For most real-world problems this seems untenable. Do the authors have guidance on when their algorithm is appropriate and when it is not?

3. In practice, the recovered transition matrix is unlikely to be sparse. Is there any way to guarantee that the transition matrix learned by the spectral algorithms in [1,2] will be (approximately) sparse? I suspect there may be many HMMs with dense transition matrices that are close, in likelihood, to the sparse block-diagonal models the authors target. It is not obvious that the matrix learned via [1,2] will be sparse under small amounts of noise.

4. I am concerned that the experiments do not reflect realistic noise in the learned transition matrix. In Section 4.1, the algorithm is applied to a transition matrix that has been permuted with noise sampled from a Dirichlet distribution, but the paper is about learning mixtures of HMM parameters, not just depermuting matrices. Wouldn’t it be more informative to sample observation sequences from a mixture of HMMs and then learn all parameters from the observed data? This would give a more accurate picture of how observation noise and limited data affect the learned transition matrix and how difficult it is to undo the permutation in the presence of such noise.

5. The experiment in Section 4.2 moves in this direction, but there is nowhere near enough information to evaluate its quality. I have no idea what the mixture of HMMs actually looked like. The specific transition and observation parameters matter greatly for assessing how easy or hard the recovery problem is. Please provide these details (an appendix would be fine if space is limited).

6. In Table 1, why does EM initialized with spectral learning ever perform worse than spectral learning alone? In principle, EM should only refine and improve the initial estimates. Please clarify the setup and provide an explanation.

Minor Comments:
1. In the proof in Section 3.2.1, the subscript of Λ in the bottom-right corner of the first matrix should be K, not 2.
2. The text and numbers in the figures are too small.

The paper presents interesting ideas and solid theoretical results. I remain concerned, however, about the practicality of the method on real datasets.
