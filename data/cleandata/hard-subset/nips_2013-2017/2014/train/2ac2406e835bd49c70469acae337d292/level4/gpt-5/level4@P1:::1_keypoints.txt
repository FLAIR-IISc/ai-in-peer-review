- Paper proposes learning Mixtures of HMMs by treating them as one HMM with block-diagonal transitions, applying spectral MoM to learn a global model, then depermuting by exploiting eigen-structure; introduces a “spectral longevity” heuristic to estimate K.

- Builds on prior spectral learning for HMMs/mixtures and HMM-based sequence clustering; main novelty is the depermutation scheme and longevity criterion.

- Strength: Clear reduction from MHMM to HMM with supporting lemmas.

- Strength: Computationally attractive alternative to EM with moment computations scaling with data but core estimation independent of data size.

- Strength: Concrete algorithm to resolve permutation ambiguity using eigen-structure.

- Strength: Practical heuristic to estimate number of components K and useful intuition on failure modes of naive eigen-clustering.

- Weakness/Question 1: Practicality concern — estimating a dense MK×MK global HMM first may be infeasible with limited data; need to clarify regimes (N, T, spectral gaps, emission separation) where approach works.

- Weakness/Question 2: Sparsity concern — spectral estimators may yield dense transitions; request for conditions or perturbation bounds guaranteeing near block-diagonality, or a sparsity-promoting post-processing with guarantees.

- Weakness/Question 3: Noise model and experiments — current perturbation experiments are synthetic; request full end-to-end experiments sampling sequences, estimating moments, learning and depermuting, with success rates versus data/noise.

- Weakness/Question 4: Experimental detail and reproducibility — report full generative parameters, moment-estimation details, ablations on spectral gaps and emission overlap, or provide seeds/appendix.

- Weakness/Question 5: EM initialization anomaly — explain why EM initialized with spectral sometimes underperforms spectral alone; report likelihoods, stopping criteria, regularization, and whether EM preserves block structure.

- Weakness/Question 6: Theoretical guarantees — Conjecture 1 is central but unproven; provide more empirical sensitivity analysis (eigenvalue spacing) and clarify longevity criterion reliability when eigenvalues are close.

- NIPS criteria — Quality: solid framing but robustness claims are conjectural and empirical noise realism is lacking.

- NIPS criteria — Clarity: generally readable but experiments and figures need more detail and larger fonts.

- NIPS criteria — Originality: novel combination of reduction plus spectral depermutation and longevity heuristic.

- NIPS criteria — Significance: potentially impactful for scalable sequence clustering if robustness is demonstrated.

- Pros for acceptance: elegant reduction and depermutation algorithm, computational benefits over EM, and novel K-estimation heuristic.

- Cons for acceptance: reliance on accurate dense global HMM estimation without clear conditions, lack of end-to-end realistic experiments, central robustness result left conjectural, and unexplained empirical inconsistencies (EM behavior).

- Minor comments: fix subscript typo (Λ should be K in Sec. 3.2.1), enlarge figure fonts and contrast, and correct typographical/possessive errors (e.g., “it’s” → “its”).
