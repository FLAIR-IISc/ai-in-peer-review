The authors present a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), which addresses the key question of how to "de-permute" the results. This work builds upon the standard spectral learning algorithm for HMMs, but with a crucial modification to handle the permutation ambiguity that arises when learning a mixture of HMMs.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and their proposed solution. The algorithm is based on the observation that a mixture of HMMs can be expressed as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. The authors then use the spectral properties of this transition matrix to estimate a de-permutation mapping that enables them to recover the parameters of the individual HMMs.
