This paper introduces a spectral approach for learning mixtures of Hidden Markov Models. The core of the method is a direct adaptation of the established algorithm by Hsu, Kakade & Zhang. The central challenge, which this work addresses, is that the standard spectral approach recovers a transition matrix that is an unknown permutation of the true one, thus mixing the distinct components of the model. To resolve this ambiguity, the authors propose a de-permutation algorithm that appears to be effective based on the results presented.

The manuscript would benefit from more careful proofreading, as there are several minor typographical errors. For instance, one sentence reads: "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy." The authors are encouraged to conduct a thorough review of the text.

Furthermore, the paper's contribution could be strengthened by better situating the work within the existing literature. A discussion of related spectral methods for mixture models would provide valuable context, for example by relating this work to papers such as:

Chaganty and Liang. Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions. In International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2013.
