

This paper proposes a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of hidden Markov models (MHMM) by leveraging an existing spectral method for HMMs (Anandkumar et al., 2012) as a direct solution, treating MHMM as a special HMM case. It introduces a stabilization-based approach to address parameter permutation, relying on the assumption that each HMM's transition matrix possesses exactly one eigenvalue of 1. While the core idea of circumventing parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive, and the analysis under estimation noise (notably Equation (7)) offers valuable insights, several concerns require attention. The notation lacks consistency and clarity, and the authors should demonstrate greater awareness of related work (e.g., spectral initialization for EM, as in Chaganty and Liang, 2013). The experimental evaluation is insufficiently comprehensive; results should be presented in tabular form instead of the current graphical format (Figure 4). Specific issues include: (1) ambiguous terminology in Corollary 1 (define 1J^T as a row vector of ones and clarify lim_{ε→∞} \bar{A}^ε as a JK × JK block diagonal matrix with identical columns within each J × J block); (2) undefined vectors/matrices and their dimensions upon first introduction; (3) Figure 4 is difficult to interpret (replace with tables); (4) the poor EM performance in Table 1 is unexplained (typically, EM with sufficient iterations achieves better results than spectral methods; report best, not average, accuracy); (5) the paper should adopt the standard notation from Hsu et al. (2009), using T for transition matrices (not A) and n, m for observation and hidden state counts (not L, J). Overall, the algorithm combines a spectral HMM learner with a novel stabilization step for parameter permutation.
