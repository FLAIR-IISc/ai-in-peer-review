- Proposes a spectral algorithm for mixtures of HMMs by casting an MHMM as a special-case HMM and using Anandkumar et al. (2012)’s method.
- Introduces a stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation, relying on the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue equal to 1.
- Notation is inconsistent and unclear; vectors/matrices and their dimensions should be defined at first mention.
- Related work coverage is weak—e.g., prior work on initializing EM with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang 2013) is not cited.
- Experiments are basic and presentation is poor (Figure 4 hard to read); recommend using tables instead of the current graphics.
- EM’s reported performance seems unusually poor; suggest reporting best EM accuracy across runs (not just averages) and clarifying EM setup.
- Corollary 1 is confusing: explicitly define 1_J^T and explain that lim_{e→∞} \bar{A}^e is block-diagonal with identical columns within each J×J block.
- The stabilization idea and the analysis under estimation noise (use of eigenvalues in Eq. (7)) are interesting and valuable.
- Minor notation suggestion: consider adopting Hsu et al. (2009)’s conventions (use T, n, m instead of A, L, J).
- Overall summary: applies an existing spectral HMM learner to MHMMs and adds a novel stabilization step to undo parameter permutation.
