- Proposes a spectral algorithm for learning MHMMs by treating MHMM as a special-case HMM and applying Anandkumar et al. (2012) spectral HMM methods.  
- Introduces a stabilization-based approach to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has exactly one eigenvalue equal to 1.  
- Core idea of avoiding parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive; analysis under estimation noise (notably Eq. (7)) is insightful.  
- Notation is inconsistent and unclear; many vectors/matrices are undefined at first use and dimensions are not provided.  
- Related work coverage is weak — should acknowledge spectral initialization for EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang, 2013) and similar literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently comprehensive and presented poorly; replace Figure 4 graphs with clear tables.  
- Table 1 shows poor EM performance without explanation; EM typically improves with more iterations — report best rather than average accuracy.  
- Corollary 1 contains ambiguous terminology: define 1J^T as a row vector of ones and clarify lim_{ε→∞} \bar{A}^ε as a JK×JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns within each J×J block.  
- Recommend using standard HMM notation from Hsu et al. (2009): T for transition matrices and n,m for observation/hidden state counts (instead of A, L, J).  
- Overall contribution: combines a spectral HMM learner with a novel stabilization step to handle parameter permutation.
