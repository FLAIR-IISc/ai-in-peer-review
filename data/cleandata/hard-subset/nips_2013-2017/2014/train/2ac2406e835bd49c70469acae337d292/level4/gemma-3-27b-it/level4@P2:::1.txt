This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs). The authors propose a method based on the Method of Moments (MoM), leveraging the equivalence between an MHMM and a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix. They tackle the permutation ambiguity inherent in MoM estimation using spectral properties of the global transition matrix to recover individual HMM parameters.

The paper is generally well-written and presents a novel approach to MHMM learning. It builds upon recent work in spectral learning for HMMs [1, 2] and effectively connects it to the MHMM context. The theoretical analysis, particularly concerning the spectral properties of the transition matrix, is a significant strength. However, the practical applicability of the proposed method and the experimental validation raise concerns.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The application of spectral learning techniques to MHMMs, combined with a strategy for resolving permutation ambiguity, is a novel contribution.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear theoretical justification for the approach, demonstrating the connection between MHMMs and HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.  Lemmas 1 and 2, and the associated discussion, are well-articulated.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The MoM-based approach promises computational advantages over traditional EM algorithms, particularly for large datasets, by avoiding iterative forward-backward computations.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and logically structured.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Applicability:** The core assumption of accurately estimating the full HMM parameters *before* attempting to recover the block-diagonal structure seems problematic for many real-world scenarios.  The algorithm might struggle when the underlying block-diagonal structure is truly sparse but the estimated transition matrix is dense due to noise or limited data. This is a crucial point that the authors need to address more thoroughly.
* **Sparsity Concerns:** The paper doesn’t sufficiently address the potential for a dense, learned transition matrix. While the underlying model *is* sparse, there’s no guarantee the spectral algorithm will recover a sparse solution, especially with noisy data. Multiple HMMs with full transition matrices might approximate the true sparse structure, leading to incorrect depermutation.
* **Limited Experimental Validation:** The experiments are insufficient to convincingly demonstrate the method's robustness to realistic noise conditions.  The synthetic experiments in Section 4.1, using a Dirichlet-distributed permutation, don’t adequately simulate the complexities of learning from observed data. 
* **Insufficient Experimental Detail:**  The details of the experimental setup, particularly in Section 4.2, are lacking. Providing the actual parameters of the transition and observation matrices (even in an appendix) would significantly enhance the reproducibility and interpretability of the results. Understanding the specific HMM configurations used is crucial for assessing the algorithm's performance.
* **Unexpected EM Behavior:**  The observation in Table 1 that EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs *worse* than spectral learning alone is counterintuitive and requires explanation. This raises doubts about the quality of the initialization provided by the spectral method.
* **Minor Technical Issue:**  A minor correction is needed in the proof of Section 3.2.1 where the subscript of Λ should be K, not 2. Figure text is also too small.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to MHMM learning.
* The algorithm offers a potential computational advantage over traditional EM.
* The spectral analysis provides a valuable insight into the structure of MHMMs.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The practical applicability of the method is questionable, especially in scenarios with noisy data and weak sparsity.
* The experimental validation is insufficient to demonstrate the algorithm’s robustness.
* The lack of detailed experimental parameters hinders evaluation.
* The unexpected behavior of EM with spectral initialization warrants further investigation.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting idea with solid theoretical foundations. However, the concerns regarding its practical applicability and the limited experimental validation make it difficult to recommend for acceptance in its current form. I believe the authors need to address the sparsity concerns, provide more compelling experimental evidence with realistic noise models, and offer a more thorough discussion of the algorithm's limitations.  A stronger emphasis on *when* this algorithm would be most appropriate – and a clear articulation of scenarios where it would likely fail – would also strengthen the paper.



**Recommendation:** Weak Reject. I would be open to reconsidering with a major revision addressing the points raised above.
