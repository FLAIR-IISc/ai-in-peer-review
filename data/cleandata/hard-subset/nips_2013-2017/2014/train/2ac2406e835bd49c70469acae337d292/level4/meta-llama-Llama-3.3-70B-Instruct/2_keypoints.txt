- Proposes a spectral approach to learning mixtures of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a special-case HMM and leveraging existing spectral HMM algorithms.
- Introduces a stabilization technique to resolve parameter permutation issues by assuming each HMM transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue of 1.
- Innovative and intuitive stabilization step is a key novel contribution.
- Provides analysis of algorithm performance under estimation noise, using eigenvalue arguments (Equation (7)).
- Notation is inconsistent and sometimes unclear (e.g., Corollary 1: undefined 1J^T and unclear lim_{e->inf} bar{A}^e).
- Paper lacks references to related work, notably prior uses of spectral estimates to initialize EM (e.g., Chaganty and Liang, 2013).
- Presentation would benefit from explicitly defining vectors/matrices and their dimensions when introduced.
- Experimental evaluation is basic; Figure 4 is hard to interpret and should be replaced with tables.
- Experiments should report standard comparisons such as the best accuracy result for the EM algorithm.
- Recommend adopting established notation conventions (e.g., Hsu et al., 2009) to improve readability.
- Core contribution: combines an existing spectral HMM learning algorithm with a novel stabilization step to handle parameter permutation in MHMMs.
