- Paper proposes a spectral algorithm for learning a mixture of HMMs by formulating MHMM as a special case of HMM and leveraging Anandkumar et al. (2012).
- Introduces a stabilization-based solution to address parameter permutation, assuming each HMM transition matrix has a single eigenvalue equal to 1.
- Notation is unclear and should be improved; define each vector/matrix and its dimensions when first introduced.
- More related-work citations are needed, e.g., EM initialization with spectral estimates (Chaganty & Liang, 2013).
- Experimental evaluation is basic; Figure 4 is hard to read and should be replaced with tables.
- EM’s performance in Table 1 is surprisingly poor; report best (not average) EM accuracy as standard practice.
- The stabilization scheme to resolve parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive.
- The analysis under estimation noise and the resulting use of eigenvalues (Equation (7)) is intriguing.
- Clarify Corollary 1: define 1_J^T (row vector of J ones) and explain lim_{e->inf} \bar{A}^e as a JK x JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns in each JxJ block.
- Minor suggestion: adopt established HMM notation (use T for transitions and n,m for numbers of observation/hidden states instead of A, L, J).
- Overall method: existing spectral HMM algorithm followed by a novel stabilization step to handle parameter permutation.
