

The paper proposes a spectral learning approach for Mixture of Hidden Markov Models (MHMMs) by leveraging the block-diagonal structure of the global transition matrix. The authors extend the Hsu et al. (2012) spectral method for HMMs to MHMMs, addressing the critical permutation ambiguity in parameter estimation through spectral analysis of the global transition matrix. This work builds upon the NIPS 2012 paper by Hsu et al. and the ICML 2013 work by Chaganty and Liang on spectral mixture models, though the MHMM-specific permutation resolution is novel for this context. The authors demonstrate computational advantages over EM (up to 100× speedup) and show the method serves as effective EM initialization on real data.

**Quality:** The technical approach is sound, with clear theoretical justification for resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties (Lemmas 2–3 and Conjecture 1). The experimental validation on synthetic data (Section 4.1) and the UCI character dataset (Section 4.3) supports the claims. However, Conjecture 1 lacks formal proof (only justified via sample complexity bounds from Anandkumar et al.), and the noise analysis in Section 3.2.2 is not rigorously established. The paper correctly acknowledges the limitations of the depermutation algorithm under high noise.

**Clarity:** The exposition is generally clear, with well-structured model definitions (Sections 2.1–2.2) and algorithm description (Algorithm 1). Minor notational inconsistencies exist (e.g., "πkνk" vs. "π_k ν_k" in Equation 3), and a few typos were noted (e.g., "identify of eigenvalues" in Section 3.2.1). The figures (Figures 1–2) effectively illustrate the spectral properties.

**Originality:** The core contribution—resolving permutation ambiguity in MHMM spectral learning via global transition matrix properties—represents a meaningful extension beyond prior spectral methods for HMMs (Hsu et al.) and mixture models (Chaganty & Liang). While the block-diagonal insight is straightforward, the depermutation solution is non-trivial and specifically tailored to MHMMs, differentiating it from related work.

**Significance:** The method addresses a key computational bottleneck in MHMM learning (EM's O(N T) complexity per iteration), offering a scalable alternative with comparable accuracy. The practical utility as an EM initialization (Table 1) and applicability to real-world sequence clustering (e.g., handwritten characters) demonstrate clear significance for the field. The approach could inspire extensions to other hierarchical latent models.

**Pro-acceptance arguments:**  
- Solves a well-known problem (permutation ambiguity) in MHMM learning with a theoretically grounded solution.  
- Provides substantial computational gains over EM without sacrificing accuracy.  
- Validated on both synthetic and real-world data with practical utility.  

**Con-acceptance arguments:**  
- Insufficient comparison to spectral methods for related models (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Conjecture 1 requires stronger theoretical justification for full rigor.  
- Minor technical errors and notational inconsistencies reduce readability.  

The paper meets NIPS quality standards as a technically sound contribution with practical significance. The proposed method is a valuable addition to the spectral learning toolkit for latent variable models.
