The authors introduce a spectral method for learning a mixture of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). While the core algorithm appears as a straightforward extension of the Hsu, Kakade & Zhang approach, a significant challenge arises in that the recovered transition matrix represents a permutation of the true component ordering, effectively mixing the identified components. The authors propose and demonstrate a depermutation algorithm that appears to effectively address this issue.

The manuscript contains minor typographical errors and would benefit from careful proofreading. For example, the phrase "For each e, identify of eigenvalues that contain all of the energy" requires attention. 

Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly in the context of spectral methods for mixture models. Specifically, relating this work to Chaganty and Liang’s “Spectral experts for estimating mixtures of linear regressions” (ICML, 2013) would provide valuable context. 

The authors successfully tackle the important problem of aligning the permuted components resulting from their spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs.
