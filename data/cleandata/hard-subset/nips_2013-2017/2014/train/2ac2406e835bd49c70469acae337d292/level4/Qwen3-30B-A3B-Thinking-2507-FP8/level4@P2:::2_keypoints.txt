- Proposes a spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as an HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix.  
- Leverages Anandkumar et al. (2012) spectral HMM techniques and resolves parameter permutation ambiguity using spectral properties of the global transition matrix.  
- Provides a method that recovers individual HMM parameters within the mixture and handles estimation noise.  
- Introduces a novel spectral longevity measure (Equation 7) for cluster identification.  
- Theoretical contributions include proofs of MHMM–HMM equivalence (Lemma 1) and spectral permutation resolution (Lemma 2, Corollary 1, Lemma 3).  
- Offers noise analysis with theoretical guarantees about algorithm behavior in the noisy case.  
- Demonstrates computational advantages over EM, especially on large datasets, and suggests utility as an EM initializer.  
- Uses matrix notation effectively to simplify complex expressions, making the technical development logically presented.  
- Requires MK < L for the method to work, which is a noted limitation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: synthetic results are adequate but real-data experiments lack dataset detail and transformation explanation to meet MK < L.  
- Comparison with EM is flawed by reporting average accuracy instead of best-case accuracy from multiple restarts, potentially misleading performance claims.  
- Notation could be clearer and more consistent (e.g., define 1^T_J explicitly, prefer T for transition matrices).  
- Paper should explicitly define dimensions of vectors/matrices on first use and clarify the structure of lim_{e→∞} Ā^e.  
- Presentation suggestions: replace Figure 4 with a table and explain why EM performs poorly in reported results.  
- Needs stronger engagement with related work, particularly Chaganty & Liang (2013) on EM initialization.  
- Originality is meaningful—the permutation solution and longevity measure are clever extensions of prior spectral methods.  
- Significance is promising due to computational benefits and potential generalization to hierarchical latent models, but empirical superiority over EM is not fully demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, experimental rigor, and related-work discussion.
