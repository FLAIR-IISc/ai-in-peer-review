- Proposes a method-of-moments spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by treating the model as a single HMM with block-diagonal transitions and resolving permutation ambiguity via spectral properties.  
- Novel combination of MoM and spectral learning offering a scalable alternative to EM.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound, with lemmas linking MHMMs to single HMMs with block-diagonal transition matrices.  
- Paper is generally well-written and mathematically clear.  
- MoM approach offers potential scalability advantages because it is less dependent on dataset size than EM.  
- Major practical concern: method assumes accurate full HMM parameter estimation before recovering block structure, which may be unrealistic in noisy real-world settings.  
- No guarantee or discussion of sparsity in the learned transition matrix; spectral learning may produce dense matrices that hurt efficiency and interpretability.  
- Experimental design is weak—using a permuted matrix as the primary test is artificial and doesn’t reflect noisy, realistic MHMM data.  
- Experiments lack sufficient detail (e.g., transition probabilities, emission distributions), making results hard to assess or reproduce.  
- EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone (Table 1), which is unexpected and unexplained.  
- Figures are hard to read due to small text and numbers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach; spectral resolution of permutation ambiguity; potential scalability benefits.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable practical applicability, lack of sparsity guarantee, and insufficient experimental validation.  
- Overall: interesting and promising contribution but currently not convincing due to practical and experimental shortcomings.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject with request for significant revisions.  
- Requested revisions: (1) detail conditions for success/failure, (2) investigate sparsity-promoting methods, (3) run realistic experiments with data from true MHMMs and parameter/cluster recovery, (4) provide full experimental parameters and deeper analysis, (5) investigate anomalous Table 1 results, (6) improve figure readability.  
- Minor: fix subscript Λ in Section 3.2.1 (should be K, not 2).  
- Minor: increase font size in figures for readability.
