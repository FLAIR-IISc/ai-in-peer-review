- Proposes learning MHMMs by reformulating as a single HMM with expanded hidden state and applying an existing spectral HMM learner.  
- Introduces a stabilization step to resolve parameter permutation, assuming each component’s transition matrix has a unique eigenvalue at 1.  
- Notation is often unclear; many vectors/matrices are introduced without specifying dimensions.  
- Related-work discussion is thin; omits relevant prior work such as using spectral estimates to initialize EM (e.g., Chaganty & Liang 2013).  
- Experimental evaluation is basic and hard to read; graphical presentation should be replaced or supplemented with tables reporting means, standard deviations, and sample sizes.  
- EM’s poor reported performance is surprising; the paper must clarify EM setup (restarts, iterations, convergence, initialization) and report best EM accuracy across restarts.  
- The stabilization scheme for undoing parameter permutation is interesting and intuitive (positive contribution).  
- The analysis that accounts for estimation noise and motivates using eigenvalues (e.g., Equation (7)) is a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Corollary 1 is hard to parse; explicitly define 1_J^T and describe the block-diagonal structure of lim_{e->∞} \bar{A}^e.  
- Recommend defining every vector/matrix and its dimensions at first introduction to improve readability.  
- Suggest aligning notation with established conventions (e.g., use T for transition matrices and n, m for observation/hidden state counts).  
- Clarify the eigenvalue assumption: state that eigenvalue 1 must be simple and discuss effects of violations (nearly reducible chains, multiple recurrent classes) on stabilization and robustness.  
- Provide more implementation details for reproducibility: regularization, numerical stabilization, handling small singular values, and finite-sample computation of Equation (7).  
- Overall: promising approach but needs clearer notation, stronger experimental methodology/reporting, and deeper positioning within related work.
