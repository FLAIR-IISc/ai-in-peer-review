- Paper proposes spectral learning for a single HMM and a method to resolve permutation ambiguity to recover a block-diagonal mixture-of-HMMs transition structure.  
- Paper is well written but experimental results need expansion, more detail, and deeper analysis.  
- Method relies on expressing a mixture as a single HMM, yet motivation for mixtures is sparsity; requiring accurate full-HMM estimation before depermuting may be infeasible in real problems—authors should specify when the algorithm is appropriate.  
- Concern that the recovered transition matrix may not be (approximately) sparse; no guarantee spectral methods will preserve sparsity under noise.  
- Experiments (Sec. 4.1) use perturbed permuted transition matrices rather than sampling observations from a mixture of HMMs; this does not reflect realistic observational noise or limited-data effects.  
- Sec. 4.2 experiment lacks crucial details about the underlying mixture (transition and observation parameters); these specifics should be provided (appendix acceptable).  
- Table 1: unclear why EM initialized with spectral learning sometimes performs worse than spectral alone—EM should typically improve results.  
- Minor: probable typo—Λ subscript in proof (Sec. 3.2.1) should be K, not 2.  
- Minor: figures have text and numbers that are too small.  
- Overall: interesting ideas and strong theory, but practical applicability on real datasets is questionable.
