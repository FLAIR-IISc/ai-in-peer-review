- Presents a spectral learning algorithm for Mixture of HMMs by treating an MHMM as a single HMM with a block-diagonal transition matrix and adapting Anandkumar et al. (2012) methods.  
- Addresses permutation ambiguity in global parameter estimates by exploiting eigenvalue structure of the global transition matrix and proposing a depermutation algorithm.  
- Provides theoretical analysis (Lemmas 2–3, Conjecture 1) on eigenvalue behavior under noise and introduces "spectral longevity" to estimate cluster count.  
- Empirically evaluates on synthetic data and a handwritten character dataset, showing competitive accuracy with EM and significant computational advantages.  
- Demonstrates practical utility as an initialization strategy for EM.  
- Core technical novelty (permutation resolution via spectral analysis) is well-motivated and novel.  
- Theoretical framework is clear and grounded, with useful lemmas and a plausible conjecture guiding noise behavior analysis.  
- Algorithm design is clean and experiments are generally well-structured, with real-data results supporting claims.  
- Computational efficiency claim is convincing: spectral learning scales independently of data size versus EM’s O(NT) per iteration.  
- Notation and clarity need substantial refinement (define vectors/matrices and dimensions at first use; align with standard HMM notation).  
- Related work omission: fails to discuss Chaganty & Liang (2013) and other spectral initialization approaches, weakening novelty claims.  
- Experimental presentation issues: Figure 4’s scatter plot obscures trends (tables preferred), Table 1 reports average EM rather than best-of-restarts, and synthetic experiments lack error bars/statistical testing.  
- Theoretical rigor concerns: Conjecture 1’s sample complexity claim needs clearer justification or framing as a plausible extension specific to MHMMs.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid contribution with practical and computational significance, but requires revisions on notation, related work, and experimental reporting.
