- Summary: Paper proposes spectral learning for HMM mixtures and a permutation-recovery step to reveal block-diagonal structure.  
- Need much stronger experimental results and more detailed empirical analysis.  
- Method relies on estimating a full HMM then depermuting, which undermines the motivation of using sparse block-diagonal mixtures and may be impractical for large real-world problems.  
- Paper does not address whether spectral learning yields (near) sparse/block-diagonal transition matrices or how robust sparsity is to noise.  
- Experiment in Section 4.1 tests depermutation on a noisy permuted matrix, which does not evaluate learning from observation data and the core mixture-HMM problem.  
- Section 4.2 lacks critical details (mixture HMM parameters), preventing assessment of how parameter choices affect learning difficulty and robustness.  
- Table 1 shows EM initialized with spectral learning performing worse than spectral alone, a counterintuitive result that needs explanation.  
- Minor: typo in Section 3.2.1 (subscript of Λ should be K, not 2).  
- Minor: figure text and numbers are too small and hard to read.  
- Overall: interesting theory but practical feasibility and experimental validation under realistic noise and data scarcity are questionable and need to be addressed.
