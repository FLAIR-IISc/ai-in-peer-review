- Introduces a spectral method for learning mixtures of HMMs by casting MHMMs as special-case HMMs and using the Anandkumar et al. (2012) spectral HMM learner.
- Proposes a stabilization-based approach to resolve parameter permutation, relying on the assumption that each component HMM’s transition matrix has a single eigenvalue at 1.
- Treats estimation noise and highlights an interesting role for eigenvalues within the algorithm (see Eq. (7)).
- Notation issue: define 1_J^T (length-J row vector of ones) and clarify lim_{ε→∞} Ā^ε as a JK-by-JK block-diagonal matrix with identical columns within each J-by-J block.
- Readability issue: define every vector/matrix together with its dimensions at first mention.
- Literature placement: missing references—e.g., spectral estimates to initialize EM have been studied by Chaganty and Liang (2013); better situate the work in prior literature.
- Experimental concerns: evaluation is fairly basic and Figure 4 is hard to interpret; replace plots with tables.
- EM comparison unclear: explain why EM performs poorly in Table 1 and report best (not just average) EM accuracy.
- Notational suggestion: align with Hsu et al. (2009) by using T for transition matrices and n,m for numbers of observation/hidden states (instead of A, L, J).
- Overall summary: the paper combines an existing spectral HMM learner with a new stabilization step to undo parameter permutation; the tactic is compelling and intuitive.
