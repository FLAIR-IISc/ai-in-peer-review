

# Review of "Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis"

## Summary
This paper introduces the Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) model, which extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by providing a generative model for the raw signal x(t) rather than just its STFT. The authors present a novel approach that models the signal as a sum of low-rank time-frequency components, offering advantages in multi-resolution representation compared to traditional NMF approaches. The paper describes two estimation methods (MJLE and MMLE) with corresponding EM algorithms, and demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement.

## Strengths
The paper addresses a meaningful gap in the literature by providing a generative model for the raw signal rather than its STFT. The multi-resolution extension is well-motivated and clearly explained. The connection to sparse Bayesian learning is insightful, and the experimental results in Section 5 demonstrate the practical utility of the approach.

## Weaknesses

1. **Notational inconsistencies**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout. In equation (1), [WH]fn is used to denote the (f,n) element of matrix WH, but later in the paper, the authors write vfn = [WH]fn, where v should be a vector (not a matrix). This creates confusion about the dimensionality of variables. The authors should consistently use either [WH]_{fn} or v_{fn} with proper definitions.

2. **Missing technical details**: The paper states "the negative log-likelihood −log p(x,α|W,H, λ) in the multi-resolution LRTFS model can be optimized using the EM algorithm described in Section 3.1" but fails to provide the specific update rules for the multi-resolution case (equation 31-34 are given, but the derivation is missing). This omission makes it difficult to assess the validity of the approach.

3. **Typographical errors**: In equation (27), the authors write "‖x−Φα(i)‖22" instead of "‖x−Φα(i)‖2^2", which is a critical typographical error that could confuse readers. Similarly, in equation (11), the term "log(|α|2)" should be "log(|α|2)" (with the absolute value squared) but is written without the absolute value, which is mathematically incorrect for complex α.

4. **Inconsistent treatment of complex variables**: The paper refers to Nc for circular complex normal distribution but fails to consistently handle complex conjugates in matrix operations. For instance, in equation (12), they define z = α + √β e1, but for complex variables, the proper form would require Hermitian operations (e.g., Φ* instead of ΦT).

5. **Experimental details**: The paper claims "the estimated components are shown at Fig. 1" but fails to include the figure in the main text, which is a significant omission for a technical paper. The supplementary material should not be required to understand the main contribution.

6. **Reference formatting**: The first reference is incomplete (missing volume, pages, etc.), which is unprofessional for a NIPS submission.

7. **Methodological clarity**: The paper states "In supervised settings where W is known, we may have K > F" but this is technically incorrect. If W is F×K and K > F, then WH has rank at most F, so the low-rank assumption is violated. The authors should clarify whether they mean K > min(F,N) or provide a proper justification.

8. **Inconsistency in IS divergence definition**: The paper defines DIS(A|B) = ∑_{ij} dIS(aij|bij) where dIS(x|y) = (x/y) - log(x/y) - 1, but fails to specify that this is for nonnegative matrices, which is crucial for the NMF context.

## Recommendation

While the paper presents a novel approach with promising applications, the numerous minor technical inconsistencies and formatting errors significantly undermine the paper's quality and credibility. The notational inconsistencies make it difficult to follow the mathematical derivations, and the missing details in the multi-resolution extension prevent proper evaluation of the method. These issues, while seemingly minor, collectively indicate a lack of attention to detail that is unacceptable for a NIPS submission.

The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to address these issues before being considered for publication. The current version does not meet the high standards of technical rigor expected at NIPS.

**Recommendation: Reject**
