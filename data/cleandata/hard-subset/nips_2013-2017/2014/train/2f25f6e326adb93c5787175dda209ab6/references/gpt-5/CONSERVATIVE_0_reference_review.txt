Summary
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative probabilistic model of the raw time-domain signal x(t) in which synthesis coefficients α over a time-frequency frame Φ have circular complex Gaussian priors with variances constrained to be low-rank via WH. This contrasts with the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), which imposes a low-rank model on the variance of analysis (STFT) coefficients. The authors present two estimation strategies: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) using an EM scheme inspired by Figueiredo & Nowak with an auxiliary variable z, leading to Wiener-like updates for α and an IS-NMF step for W,H; and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE), essentially a type-II SBL approach, which is acknowledged as computationally impractical for long signals. They also extend LRTFS to multi-resolution by stacking multiple frames. Experiments include a toy hybrid music decomposition and a semi-supervised speech enhancement task, with modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

Relation to prior work
The work is positioned between NMF on spectrogram magnitudes (e.g., IS-NMF) and probabilistic models of the STFT (GCM), and connects to SBL via Gaussian priors with unknown variances. The distinction—imposing low-rank structure on synthesis coefficient variances rather than STFT variances—is clear. The multi-resolution idea resembles morphological component analysis and prior multi-window STFT approaches, but here combined with the proposed generative synthesis model. However, the paper does not empirically compare to GCM/phase-aware NMF baselines, which seems essential given the stated motivation.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual bridge from analysis (GCM/NMF) to synthesis modeling, with an interpretable Gaussian prior structure that yields simple EM updates.
- The MJLE algorithm is computationally attractive (no large matrix inversions) and leverages standard TF operators; the multi-resolution extension is straightforward.
- The connection to SBL is interesting, highlighting the effect of tying variances via WH rather than independent ARD parameters.
- Preliminary audio examples qualitatively sensible; quantitative speech enhancement shows some improvement over simple baselines.

Weaknesses
- Empirical validation is limited and, in places, methodologically problematic:
  - In the music example, λ is selected post hoc to maximize output SNR (“experimented several values and selected the results leading to best output SNR”), which is not a fair or reproducible evaluation.
  - In speech enhancement, λ is annealed to near zero, undermining the probabilistic model (effectively forcing a near-exact fit) and complicating comparisons.
  - Baselines are weak or mismatched: no comparison to GCM (which already models complex STFT with Gaussian priors), to phase-aware/complex NMF variants, or to multi-resolution NMF. The IS-NMF baseline uses a single window of intermediate length and appears not to leverage the same supervised dictionary setup, making the comparison potentially unfair.
  - Only 10 test signals, only SNR reported, no confidence intervals/variance, and no perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI) that are standard in speech enhancement.
- MMLE, which would be the statistically principled approach aligned with SBL, is declared unscalable and not used; no attempt is made to approximate it (e.g., via matrix-free methods, conjugate gradients, diagonal/low-rank approximations, or message passing beyond a brief mention).
- Theoretical development is light: no convergence or monotonicity proof for the MJLE EM variant beyond citing [6]; the role and selection of β is unclear beyond a bound, and its impact on performance is not studied.
- The extra log(|α|^2) term in the MJLE objective is noted but its influence on updates is not discussed; the derived updates look like DIS(|α|^2|WH) + quadratic data-fit, leaving ambiguity about how that term is handled in EM.
- No runtime/complexity benchmarks or scalability results, despite the practical claims.
- Presentation issues: incomplete references, some notation choices and assumptions (e.g., real-signal constraints via Hermitian symmetry) are not fully elaborated for reproducibility.

Quality
Parts of the model and algorithm are technically sound and consistent with prior Gaussian-latent-factor modeling and IS-NMF. However, the empirical evidence is insufficient to support the claimed advantages; the use of oracle-like λ selection or annealing is particularly concerning. The lack of strong, phase-aware baselines weakens the conclusions. MMLE is not demonstrated at scale, and there is no ablation (e.g., single- vs multi-resolution, effect of K, β, window sizes).

Clarity
Generally well written and organized, with a clear derivation of the MJLE updates and a straightforward multi-resolution extension. However, key implementation details (β choice, λ handling, initialization, stopping) and evaluation protocols need to be made explicit. References are inconsistently formatted and in some cases incomplete.

Originality
The idea of imposing low-rank variance structure on synthesis coefficients rather than analysis coefficients is a novel twist, and the resulting EM procedure tying Wiener-like shrinkage with IS-NMF is interesting. The multi-resolution stacking is not novel per se, but its integration into this generative synthesis framework is a neat combination.

Significance
Potentially useful if it demonstrably improves over phase-aware baselines and scales. As is, the empirical gains are modest and not rigorously established; the paper does not convincingly advance the state of the art.

Questions for authors
- Please compare against GCM/phase-aware NMF (e.g., modeling complex STFT with Gaussian priors) under matched settings, and against multi-resolution NMF baselines.
- How is β chosen in practice, and how sensitive are results to β and λ? Can you avoid oracle λ selection and report results with a fixed, principled schedule?
- Can you provide runtime and memory usage vs IS-NMF and GCM? Any ablation on the benefit of multi-resolution vs single-window LRTFS?
- How is the log(|α|^2) term handled in the EM updates? Does the algorithm optimize the full MJLE objective (monotonic decrease proof)?
- Please report statistical variability and perceptual metrics on speech tasks.

Typos/Minor
- References are missing years/volumes/pages; please fix formatting.
- Some notational overloading between (f, n) and m could be clarified.
- Clarify handling of real signals and Hermitian symmetry in experiments.

Recommendation
I lean toward rejection in its current form due to insufficient and partly unfair empirical validation, absence of strong baselines, and reliance on ad hoc hyperparameter selection that compromises the probabilistic interpretation. The modeling idea and algorithm are promising; with rigorous comparisons (including GCM/phase-aware and multi-resolution baselines), principled hyperparameter handling, broader and statistically sound experiments, and clearer theoretical/implementation details, this could become a solid contribution. Confidence: high.
