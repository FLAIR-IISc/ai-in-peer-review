- Proposes LRTFS: a generative synthesis-model imposing low-rank structure on variances of synthesis coefficients for raw time-domain signals.  
- Contrasts with GCM: low-rank on analysis (STFT) coefficients vs LRTFS on synthesis coefficients.  
- Derives two estimation strategies: MJLE (EM with Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF) and MMLE (SBL-style marginalization, computationally heavy).  
- Extends model to multiresolution by jointly modeling multiple frames (e.g., short and long Gabor frames).  
- Novelty: shifts low-rank prior to synthesis side enabling unified signal-level generative modeling and natural multiresolution extension.  
- Strength: clear conceptual counterpart to GCM/NMF, bridging analysis and synthesis probabilistic models.  
- Strength: MJLE algorithm is practical and scalable, avoiding large matrix inversions and using standard IS-NMF updates.  
- Strength: multiresolution extension is elegant and aligns with known audio representation benefits.  
- Strength: experiments show modest empirical improvements (speech enhancement) and interpretable hybrid decomposition.  
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale with modest gains (+0.6 dB), no statistical tests, runtimes, or ablations.  
- Weakness: missing key comparisons to GCM, phase-aware/complex NMF, and multiresolution analysis baselines.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter λ selection is ad-hoc (SNR tuning/annealing) with no sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of initialization (W, H, α), step-size β choice beyond bound, stopping criteria, and convergence diagnostics.  
- Weakness: claim that a term “favors sparsity” is misleading—algebraic decomposition does not add sparsity beyond the Gaussian prior.  
- Weakness: MMLE/SBL approach is not scalable to audio lengths and alternatives (CG/PCG, variational methods) are not explored.  
- Quality: derivations for MJLE/MMLE are sound and match known identities and SBL methodology.  
- Quality: experimental section lacks robustness checks (sensitivity to frames, K, initialization) and generalization to other noises/tasks.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written; distinction between analysis and synthesis is clear, but several implementation details need clarification (β choice, initialization, stopping, frame normalization, real-signal handling).  
- Originality: synthesis-side low-rank variance modeling and its multiresolution use are novel and insightful for the NeurIPS audience.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for audio representation and denoising, but current empirical support is limited and incomplete.  
- Suggestions: add controlled comparisons to GCM, phase-aware/complex NMF, and multiresolution analysis baselines with identical settings.  
- Suggestions: report runtimes, iterations, sensitivity to λ/β/K/frame choices, and consider evidence-based or hierarchical priors for λ.  
- Suggestions: explore scalable MMLE alternatives (matrix-free CG/PCG, low-rank approximations, EP/AMP/VAMP).  
- Suggestions: analyze identifiability/scaling ambiguities (W↔H) and consider group-ARD priors for automatic K selection.  
- Overall: solid and promising modeling and algorithmic contribution but needs stronger experiments, added baselines, and implementation details to justify claimed advantages; reviewer leans borderline accept.
