- Proposes LRTFS: a novel generative model that synthesizes raw signals with a low-rank time-frequency variance, building on GCM/SBL.
- Models synthesis coefficients as Gaussian with covariance factorized by W and H, enabling direct signal generation and phase preservation.
- Derives MJLE and MMLE estimation approaches and provides EM-based algorithmic details.
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations suited to transient vs. sustained signal components.
- Connects theoretically to sparse Bayesian learning; derivations are clear and mathematically rigorous.
- Demonstrates promising empirical gains in music decomposition and speech enhancement versus IS-NMF and OMLSA.
- MMLE implementation currently does not scale to realistic signal sizes, limiting practical applicability.
- Experimental evaluation is primarily audio-focused; generality to other signal types remains untested.
- Performance is sensitive to the hyperparameter λ, which was tuned manually rather than systematically.
- Lacks thorough ablation studies to quantify the contributions of the low-rank constraint and multi-resolution design.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with standard, easy-to-follow notation.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions (leans strong accept).
- Suggested revisions: discuss MMLE scalability and possible approximate inference remedies, add non-audio experiments, adopt systematic hyperparameter tuning, and include detailed ablation analyses.
