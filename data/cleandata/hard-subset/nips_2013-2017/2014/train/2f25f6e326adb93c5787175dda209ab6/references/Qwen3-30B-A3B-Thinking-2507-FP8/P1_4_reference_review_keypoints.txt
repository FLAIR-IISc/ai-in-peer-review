- Introduces LRTFS: a generative model that synthesizes the raw signal x(t) from low-rank time-frequency components (unlike STFT- or magnitude-only models).
- Proposes two estimation methods: MJLE (practical EM-based) and MMLE (more exact but computationally heavy).
- Presents a multi-resolution extension to allow different time-frequency resolutions for different components.
- Connects the approach to Sparse Bayesian Learning by enforcing a low-rank structure on the variances of synthesis coefficients.
- Validated on tasks: shows strong music decomposition (tonal vs transient separation) and speech enhancement (avg +6.6 dB SNR).
- Novelty: first to model raw signals with low-rank T-F structure within the NMF/GCM-related literature.
- Technical soundness: rigorous derivations, clear MJLE objective and EM algorithm, and natural multi-resolution incorporation.
- Clarity: well-written, logically organized, and accessible mathematical presentation.
- Limited baselines: lacks comparison to recent deep learning / neural audio processing methods.
- Scalability concern: MMLE is impractical for large-scale audio due to matrix inversion requirements (M > T).
- No theoretical guarantees: the paper does not provide convergence or optimality proofs for the EM algorithm.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: regularization λ treated as a hyperparameter with insufficient analysis of selection or sensitivity.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance; advances T-F analysis and offers practical utility.
- Pros summarized: novel raw-signal modeling, practical implementation, convincing experiments, and useful multi-resolution capability.
- Cons summarized: limited deep-learning comparisons, MMLE scalability issues, and missing theoretical convergence guarantees.
