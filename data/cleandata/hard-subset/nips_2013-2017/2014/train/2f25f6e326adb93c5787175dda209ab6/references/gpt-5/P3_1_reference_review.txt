Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative, time-domain model that represents a signal x(t) as a linear combination of time-frequency atoms with complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients αfn whose variances are tied through a low-rank factorization vfn = [WH]fn. This contrasts with the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) that imposes the low-rank structure on the variance of analysis (STFT) coefficients. Two estimation strategies are developed: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) via an EM algorithm using a variable-splitting architecture (Figueiredo & Nowak), yielding practical updates that alternate a Wiener-like shrinkage on α and IS-NMF updates on |α|^2; and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE, type-II ML/SBL) that integrates out α but is computationally prohibitive at audio scale. The framework naturally extends to multi-resolution by stacking multiple frames/bases. Experiments on hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

The work is positioned among probabilistic NMF on STFT magnitude/power, the complex-valued GCM (variance in the analysis domain), sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) with ARD-type Gaussian priors, and multi-resolution/morphological signal models. The key novelty is moving the low-rank variance prior to synthesis coefficients of a redundant time-frequency frame and deriving scalable MJLE updates.

Strengths
- Conceptual contribution: a coherent generative model directly in the time domain, tying Gaussian synthesis coefficient variances via WH. This bridges analysis-domain probabilistic NMF (GCM) and dictionary-based synthesis models.
- Methodological clarity: the EM with auxiliary variable yields simple, matrix-inversion-free updates; the link to IS divergence via DIS(|α|^2|v) is elegant and connects to well-understood IS-NMF machinery.
- Multi-resolution extension is natural in the synthesis view and operationally simple: independent WH factorizations per frame, coupled through the shared residual.
- Component reconstruction and the connection to Wiener filtering are well motivated.
- Practicality: implementation relies on fast analysis/synthesis routines (e.g., LTFAT), making MJLE feasible.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Empirical evaluation is limited. Music: a single 6 s example with λ tuned to maximize SNR; Speech: 10 mixtures with applause noise only, K=500 pre-trained speech dictionary, and average SNR improvement of 6.6 dB vs 6 dB (IS-NMF) and 4.6 dB (OMLSA). No statistical tests, no perceptual metrics (STOI/PESQ), no runtime/memory, and no ablations (e.g., single vs multi-resolution, analysis vs synthesis-domain models) to isolate where gains come from.
- MMLE, arguably the more principled approach, is not scalable; the paper leaves large-scale algorithms as future work. This limits the work’s methodological completeness.
- Probabilistic coherence: in MJLE the λ update used is heuristic; in experiments λ is treated as a hyperparameter or annealed to near zero, and β is not specified. The constraint β ≤ λ/δΦ for a valid covariance λI − βΦΦ* is important; decreasing λ can violate it. The implications for monotonicity and correctness should be clarified.
- The claimed sparsity-inducing log(|α|^2) term is algebraically a re-expression of the Gaussian prior via DIS; the resulting α update α = v/(v+β) z is a smooth shrinkage, not strongly sparsity-promoting. The paper should temper claims about sparsity effects or empirically characterize coefficient distributions.
- Identifiability and scaling: WH has scale ambiguities; with λ and β, additional trade-offs arise. Constraints or normalizations (e.g., sum-to-one columns in W) and initialization strategies are not discussed.
- Related work coverage: phase-aware/complex NMF and other time-domain probabilistic models are only partially referenced; readers would benefit from a clearer contrast to analysis-domain multi-resolution approaches (e.g., concatenated multi-window STFT NMF) and to structured SBL with tied variances.

Quality
- The model and MJLE derivations are mostly sound; the DIS decomposition of the joint likelihood is insightful. Some technical details are missing: explicit derivation of Eq. (11), the exact EM Q function used for the λ update, conditions on Φ (tight/non-tight frames), and how β is set to ensure positive semidefinite covariances. Convergence guarantees are heuristic given inexact M-steps (multiplicative updates) and heuristic λ updates.

Clarity
- Generally well written and organized. However, important implementation details are omitted: initialization for W, H, α; choice of K; stopping criteria; β selection; frame normalization; redundancy of Φ; and computational cost. Figures and audio references are useful but quantitative reporting should be expanded. Some typos and incomplete references (see minor comments).

Originality
- Moving the low-rank prior to synthesis coefficients and exploiting DIS(|α|^2|WH) within an EM with Wiener-type shrinkage is a novel and appealing combination. The multi-resolution synthesis extension is natural but not unique to this framework; the paper does not empirically disentangle novelty-driven gains.

Significance
- For audio ML/SP, the approach is promising: time-domain generative modeling and multi-resolution factorization are relevant, and the MJLE algorithm is practical. Demonstrated gains are modest; broader and more rigorous evaluation would strengthen impact. If a scalable MMLE were provided, significance would increase.

Suggestions and questions for the authors
- Provide ablations: (i) single vs multi-resolution LRTFS; (ii) LRTFS vs GCM (analysis-domain) with same Φ and K; (iii) effect of λ and β; (iv) effect of K and redundancy.
- Report runtime/memory and scaling with T, M, K; clarify Φ properties (tightness, redundancy).
- Detail initialization and constraints on W/H (normalization, smoothness penalties), and stopping criteria.
- Clarify and justify the λ update and β choice; discuss monotonicity and the PSD condition λI − βΦΦ*.
- Consider scalable MMLE approximations (CG solves with implicit Φ, Lanczos/diagonal approximations for Σ, variational bounds, AMP/EP).
- Add perceptual metrics and more noise types; avoid tuning λ on test SNR to reduce optimism.

Minor comments
- Typos: “hit-hat” → “hi-hat”; “other the 10” → “over the 10”; “mentionned” → “mentioned”.
- References are incomplete; provide full bibliographic details.

Pros
- Clear, principled synthesis-domain probabilistic model with low-rank structure.
- Scalable MJLE with simple updates; natural multi-resolution extension.
- Solid connections to IS-NMF and Wiener filtering.

Cons
- Limited and partially tuned empirical validation; modest gains.
- MMLE not scalable; theoretical details (β, λ, PSD condition) under-specified.
- Claims around sparsity and advantages over analysis-domain models not fully substantiated.
