Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model of raw time-domain signals that imposes low-rank structure on the variances of synthesis coefficients in a time-frequency (t-f) frame. Concretely, x(t) is expressed as a sum of atoms φfn(t) with complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients αfn ∼ Nc(0, [WH]fn) and Gaussian residual noise. This contrasts with the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) for STFTs, where the low-rank structure is imposed on the variances of analysis coefficients yfn. The authors derive two estimation strategies: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) via an EM scheme that alternates Wiener-like shrinkage of α and IS-NMF on |α|^2 to fit WH, and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE) in the spirit of Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL), which marginalizes α but is computationally heavy for long signals. They also extend LRTFS to a multi-resolution setting by modeling multiple frames (e.g., short- and long-window Gabor frames) jointly. Experiments on music hybrid decomposition (tonal/transient) and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

The work builds on and connects several lines: NMF and IS-NMF for audio (e.g., Févotte et al.), probabilistic modeling of STFT magnitudes/phases (GCM), SBL/Type-II ML for sparse linear models (Tipping; Wipf & Rao), and hybrid/morphological audio models with multiresolution frames (Daudet/Torrésani). The novelty is to shift the low-rank prior from analysis coefficients to synthesis coefficients of a redundant frame, enabling a unified generative model at the signal level and a natural multiresolution extension.

Strengths
- Conceptual contribution: A clear synthesis-model counterpart to GCM/NMF that models the raw waveform with low-rank variance on synthesis coefficients. This bridges a gap in probabilistic modeling of audio.
- Algorithmic practicality: The MJLE EM algorithm avoids large matrix inversions and uses only analysis/synthesis operations plus standard IS-NMF multiplicative updates, making it scalable to long signals.
- Multi-resolution extension: The model elegantly accommodates multiple frames with straightforward updates, aligning with known advantages of multiresolution audio representations.
- Experimental indications: On speech enhancement, LRTFS slightly outperforms IS-NMF and OMLSA; the hybrid jazz decomposition qualitatively illustrates interpretability of components across resolutions.

Weaknesses and issues
- Limited empirical validation: Experiments are small-scale, with modest average gains (e.g., +0.6 dB over IS-NMF) and without statistical tests, runtime, or ablations. Key comparisons (to GCM/phase-aware NMF/complex NMF) are missing.
- Hyperparameter handling: λ is tuned by SNR or annealed without principled selection; the paper notes poor joint estimation but does not analyze sensitivity.
- Convergence and initialization: No discussion of initialization strategies (W, H, α), step-size β selection beyond the bound, or convergence diagnostics; reproducibility is hindered by missing details.
- Claim about sparsity term: The decomposition of the Gaussian prior into DIS(|α|^2|v) + log(|α|^2) is algebraic; the “additional sparsity-favoring term” cancels the −log(|α|^2) component in DIS and does not introduce extra sparsity beyond the Gaussian prior. The text risks being misleading here.
- MMLE scalability: While the MMLE/SBL connection is interesting, the proposed algorithm is not usable at audio scale; alternatives (CG/PCG, matrix-free solvers, variational approximations) are not explored.
- Missing baselines: No comparison to GCM on STFTs (the closest competitor), complex/phase-aware NMF, or multiresolution NMF baselines. The multiresolution advantage is not quantified against an equally multiresolution analysis model.

Quality
- The modeling and EM derivations for MJLE are sound, leveraging known identities and the Figueiredo–Nowak splitting. The MMLE derivations match standard SBL methodology.
- The complexity discussion for MMLE is honest; however, the experimental section does not sufficiently probe robustness (e.g., sensitivity to frame choices, K, initialization) or generalization to other noises/tasks.
- The lack of comparison to GCM/phase-aware baselines weakens claims that modeling the raw waveform improves performance.

Clarity
- Overall well-written and organized. The distinction between analysis (GCM) and synthesis (LRTFS) is clear.
- Please clarify: (i) precise choice of β in MJLE (fixed vs adaptive), (ii) initialization schemes for W, H, α, (iii) stopping criteria, (iv) frame normalization/tightness assumptions and computation of δΦ, (v) handling of real-valued signals and Hermitian symmetry in practice.
- Rephrase the “third term favors sparsity” claim to avoid confusion; emphasize it is an algebraic decomposition of the Gaussian prior.

Originality
- The synthesis-side low-rank variance modeling and its multiresolution extension constitute a novel and interesting combination of known tools (Gaussian linear models, IS-NMF, EM/Wiener filtering). The connection to SBL with tied variances via WH is insightful.
- The idea of using multiresolution frames within a low-rank generative synthesis model appears new to the NeurIPS audience.

Significance
- Potentially significant for audio representation/denoising/separation, offering a principled way to combine frames and reconstruct time-domain signals.
- Current empirical gains are modest and comparisons incomplete; strengthening experiments would increase impact and adoption.

Questions and suggestions
- Provide a controlled comparison to GCM on the same datasets, with identical frames and K, to isolate the benefit of synthesis modeling.
- Include phase-aware/complex NMF baselines, and a multiresolution analysis-model baseline (e.g., parallel STFTs).
- Report runtimes, iterations, and sensitivity to λ, β, K, and frame choices. Consider cross-validation or evidence-based λ (e.g., Empirical Bayes) or hierarchical priors.
- Explore scalable MMLE: conjugate gradients with matrix-free products, diagonal/low-rank approximations, expectation propagation, or AMP/VAMP-style message passing.
- Analyze identifiability and scaling ambiguities (W↔H), and whether group-ARD priors on columns/rows could enable automatic selection of K.
- Discuss robustness to frame redundancy and non-tight frames; is δΦ estimated or bounded?

Pros
- Sound, elegant modeling contribution bridging analysis vs synthesis.
- Practical, scalable EM algorithm with easy multiresolution extension.
- Clear connections to IS-NMF and SBL; interpretable components.

Cons
- Empirical evaluation limited; modest gains and missing key baselines.
- Some methodological details omitted (hyperparameters, initialization, convergence).
- MMLE not scalable; no alternative approximations tried.
- Potentially misleading discussion of “sparsity” term.

Overall assessment
A solid and promising contribution with clear modeling ideas and a practical algorithm, but the experimental section needs to be strengthened and key comparisons added to substantiate the claimed advantages. I lean toward borderline accept if the authors can address comparisons (especially to GCM/complex NMF), add ablations and runtime/sensitivity analyses, and clarify implementation details; otherwise, this is a strong candidate for revision. Confidence: medium.
