- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): time-domain generative model with synthesis coefficients αfn ~ CG(0, vfn) and vfn = [WH]fn, contrasting with analysis-domain GCM.
- Develops two estimation strategies: scalable MJLE via EM with variable-splitting (yielding Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF updates) and intractable-at-scale MMLE (type-II/SBL).
- Natural multi-resolution extension by stacking frames/bases and separate WH factorizations per frame.
- Experiments (music decomposition, semi-supervised speech enhancement) show modest gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA but limited scope and tuning concerns.
- Strength: coherent time-domain generative model linking analysis-domain probabilistic NMF and synthesis/dictionary approaches.
- Strength: EM with auxiliary variable yields simple, matrix-inversion-free updates and ties to IS divergence and IS-NMF machinery.
- Strength: multi-resolution synthesis view is operationally simple and well motivated.
- Strength: component reconstruction and Wiener-filter connection are well explained.
- Strength: practical implementation feasible using fast analysis/synthesis libraries (e.g., LTFAT).
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited (small datasets, single music clip, narrow noise types, tuning on test SNR, no statistical or perceptual metrics).
- Weakness: MMLE, while principled, is not scalable and left as future work, limiting methodological completeness.
- Weakness: probabilistic coherence issues—heuristic λ update, unspecified β, and PSD condition λI − βΦΦ* may be violated when λ is annealed.
- Weakness: sparsity claims are overstated—log(|α|^2) arises algebraically and updates yield smooth shrinkage rather than strong sparsity.
- Weakness: identifiability and scaling ambiguities in WH (and interactions with λ, β) lack discussion of constraints or normalization strategies.
- Weakness: related work coverage incomplete, particularly phase-aware/complex NMF, time-domain probabilistic models, and structured SBL with tied variances.
- Quality: models and MJLE derivations are largely sound and insightful, but technical details (Eq.11 derivation, EM Q-function, conditions on Φ, β setting) are missing.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and organized but omits important implementation details (initialization, K choice, stopping criteria, frame normalization, computational cost).
- Originality: novel shift of low-rank prior to synthesis coefficients and use of DIS(|α|^2|WH) within EM; multi-resolution extension is natural but not uniquely novel.
- Significance: promising for audio ML/SP, but current modest gains and limited evaluation reduce immediate impact; scalable MMLE would increase significance.
- Suggestions: add ablations (single vs multi-resolution, LRTFS vs GCM, λ/β effects, K/redundancy), runtime/memory scaling, and clarify Φ properties and initialization/constraints.
- Suggestions: justify λ update and β choice, address PSD and monotonicity, and explore scalable MMLE approximations (CG, Lanczos, variational, AMP/EP).
- Suggestions: expand experiments with perceptual metrics (STOI/PESQ), more noise types, avoid tuning λ on test SNR, and report statistical significance.
- Minor comments: fix typos (“hi-hat”, etc.) and complete bibliographic references.
- Pros: principled synthesis-domain model, scalable MJLE updates, clear links to IS-NMF and Wiener filtering.
- Cons: limited empirical validation and tuning, MMLE scalability and theoretical details under-specified, and sparsity/analysis-domain advantages not fully substantiated.
