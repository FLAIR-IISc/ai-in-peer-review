Summary
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative probabilistic model of raw time-domain signals that imposes a low-rank structure on the variances of complex synthesis coefficients over a time-frequency (t-f) dictionary. In contrast to the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), which models the STFT analysis coefficients yfn with variances constrained by WH, LRTFS models αfn in the synthesis domain: x = Φα + e with αfn ~ Nc(0, [WH]fn). This shifts low-rank structure from analysis coefficients (as in NMF/GCM) to synthesis coefficients and enables multi-resolution modeling by stacking multiple t-f frames (e.g., short and long windows).

Two estimation strategies are presented: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE), optimized via an EM architecture adapted from Figueiredo & Nowak that introduces an auxiliary variable z to avoid large inversions, alternating between a Wiener-like shrinkage for α and IS-NMF updates on |α|^2 to estimate W,H; and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE), akin to type-II ML in Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL), with α integrated out and an EM algorithm that requires inverting large matrices (thus impractical here). The paper also shows how to reconstruct latent components, and extends LRTFS to multi-resolution by concatenating multiple frames and separate low-rank priors per layer.

Experiments include: (1) a qualitative hybrid decomposition of a noisy jazz excerpt into tonal/transient layers with distinct Gabor frames and K=3 components; (2) semi-supervised speech enhancement with pretrained speech dictionaries (K=500) and learned noise dictionaries (K=2) in a two-layer (transient/tonal) model, yielding mean SNR improvement of 6.6 dB vs. 6.0 dB for IS-NMF and 4.6 dB for OMLSA.

Relation to prior work
- Extends IS-NMF/GCM beyond modeling the magnitude/power spectrogram to an explicit generative model in the time domain with complex-valued synthesis coefficients and phase retention.
- Connects to SBL: Gaussian scale mixture with diagonal variances; difference is the structured tying via WH (vs. free variances per coefficient in SBL).
- Relates to hybrid/morphological time-frequency representations and multi-resolution audio models, but within a probabilistic low-rank framework.
- EM architecture follows Figueiredo & Nowak’s variable-splitting trick to obtain scalable shrinkage-like iterations without large inversions.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual advance: a principled generative synthesis model with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients, bridging the gap between NMF on spectrograms and time-domain generative modeling.
- Practical, scalable MJLE algorithm using only Φ/Φ* operations and IS-NMF updates; directly applicable with existing Gabor toolboxes.
- Natural multi-resolution extension with separate WH factorizations per frame, which is cumbersome in standard NMF/GCM pipelines.
- Component reconstruction derivation and intuitive Wiener-like update rules.
- Experiments show modest but consistent improvements over single-resolution IS-NMF and a classical enhancement baseline (OMLSA).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Experimental evaluation is limited: small number of signals; primarily SNR as metric; no perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI) or statistical significance; no runtime/memory analysis. The first demo selects λ for best SNR ex post, which risks optimistic tuning.
- Comparisons lack more recent/stronger baselines, e.g., phase-aware NMF/complex NMF, multi-resolution NMF variants, or supervised NMF with temporal continuity/sparsity priors; no ablation against GCM to isolate benefits of synthesis-domain modeling.
- MMLE is theoretically attractive but not scalable; no approximate scalable alternative (e.g., variational/AMP) provided; this reduces the impact of Section 3.2.
- Some derivational clarity issues: the stated “sparsity” effect of the log(|α|^2) term in the MJLE objective is misleading—when expanding DIS, the −log|α|^2 inside DIS cancels with +log|α|^2, leaving no net sparsity-promoting term.
- Notational minor errors (e.g., Eqs. (29)-(30) appear to miss the zero mean in Nc(0, [WH]fn)); incomplete references formatting.
- Convergence/monotonicity claims rely on a hybrid of EM and MM for NMF and a heuristic λ update; a more explicit statement of what is guaranteed to decrease at each block and under what conditions would help.

Quality
- The modeling and algorithmic development are technically sound and well-grounded in Gaussian latent variable modeling and IS-NMF. The EM-like procedure is reasonable for MJLE and computationally practical. However, theoretical properties (monotonicity, convergence) are only partially addressed, and experiments, while promising, are not extensive enough to firmly establish superiority across conditions.

Clarity
- Overall well-written and organized; the link to GCM and SBL is helpful. Derivation details are mostly clear; still, the MJLE objective decomposition and the role of the log(|α|^2) term should be clarified to avoid confusion. Multi-resolution extension is straightforward and clearly described. Provide more implementation details (choice of β, how δΦ or step sizes are set, λ schedules, stopping criteria).

Originality
- The shift to synthesis-domain low-rank variance modeling with multi-resolution capability is novel relative to standard NMF/GCM. The EM architecture in the synthesis setting is a thoughtful adaptation rather than a trivial combination.

Significance
- Potentially significant for audio (and more broadly for inverse problems) due to the ability to model at the waveform level with phase, and to natively support multi-resolution. However, the current empirical evidence is limited. A stronger benchmark suite and comparisons would increase impact.

Questions for authors
- How is β chosen in practice without computing δΦ? Is there an adaptive strategy? Sensitivity to β?
- Can you provide any monotonicity/convergence guarantee for the full alternating scheme (including NMF and λ update) or a practical safeguard?
- How do results compare to complex/phase-aware NMF baselines? Can you include PESQ/STOI?
- For real signals, how do you enforce Hermitian symmetry in practice within Φ/Φ*?
- Any ablation showing the benefit of multi-resolution vs. single best resolution within LRTFS?

Suggestions for improvement
- Add stronger, broader baselines (phase-aware NMF, multiresolution NMF), perceptual metrics, and variance across trials; report computation times.
- Clarify the MJLE objective algebra and remove/qualify the “sparsity” claim.
- Provide practical guidance for β and λ (or cross-validation strategy), and release code.
- Discuss or propose scalable approximations to MMLE (e.g., variational inference, AMP).

Pros
- Novel and principled generative synthesis model; elegant and scalable MJLE algorithm.
- Multi-resolution modeling is natural and effective.
- Clear link to existing probabilistic factorizations and SBL.

Cons
- Limited experiments and baselines; modest gains; tuning choices not fully justified.
- Some clarity and rigor issues; MMLE not scalable.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The modeling contribution and practical algorithm are interesting and relevant to NeurIPS, especially for audio and inverse problems, but the paper would benefit from stronger empirical validation and clearer theoretical/implementation details.
