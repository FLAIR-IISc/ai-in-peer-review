- Introduces LRTFS: models raw signal as synthesis sum with low-rank TF variance [WH]fn, bridging NMF and direct signal synthesis.
- Differs from prior GCM by modeling the signal itself, enabling multi-resolution representations for components like transients and tonals.
- Presents two estimation approaches (MJLE and MMLE) with EM algorithms for parameter inference.
- Demonstrates multi-resolution capability and validates on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.
- Reports experimental improvement: average SNR gain of 6.6 dB, outperforming IS-NMF (6 dB) and OMLSA (4.6 dB).
- Technically sound with clear mathematical derivations and appropriate use of the Itakura-Saito divergence.
- Good connections to prior work (GCM, sparse Bayesian learning) and solid theoretical grounding.
- Acknowledges limitations: MMLE is computationally expensive and λ treated as a hyperparameter rather than estimated.
- Experiments lack comparisons to recent deep learning audio-source-separation methods, limiting relevance to current trends.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and logical development of the multi-resolution extension.
- Minor clarity improvements suggested: include a simple concrete multi-resolution example and clarify Wiener filtering for reconstruction.
- Original contribution: novel direct modeling of raw signals with low-rank TF structure and new multi-resolution capabilities.
- Significant for audio processing due to natural multi-resolution handling and consistent SNR improvements.
- Would be more compelling to the NIPS audience with stronger connections to or comparisons with deep learning frameworks.
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically rigorous, novel, well-presented, with clear strengths outweighing limitations.
