- Introduces LRTFS: a generative model that models raw signals with synthesis coefficients whose variances have a low-rank [W H]fn structure.  
- Proposes two estimation methods (MJLE and MMLE) using EM algorithms; MMLE is noted as computationally prohibitive for audio.  
- Naturally supports multi-resolution time-frequency representations, allowing different resolutions for tonal vs. transient components.  
- Extends and contrasts prior work (GCM) and links to sparse Bayesian learning by imposing low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances.  
- Shows promising experimental results on music decomposition and speech enhancement, outperforming IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound, conceptually novel, and clearly written with solid mathematical exposition.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a major practical advantage over traditional NMF approaches.  
- Lacks comparisons to recent deep learning–based audio source separation methods (missing modern SOTA baselines).  
- Computational scalability is unclear: MMLE impractical and MJLE complexity/time/memory for long signals not quantified.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: λ treated as fixed, automatic estimation performed poorly, and no solutions (e.g., cross-validation) are proposed.  
- MMLE is presented theoretically but not pursued for practical approximations that could make it usable.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, well-executed contribution that would be strengthened by SOTA comparisons and scalability analysis.
