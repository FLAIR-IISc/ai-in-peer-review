- Proposes LRTFS, a novel generative model that imposes low-rank structure on synthesis (rather than analysis) time-frequency coefficients.  
- Builds on GCM and NMF, enabling a true generative model of raw signals and multi-resolution representations.  
- Presents MJLE and MMLE estimation approaches and uses EM-style algorithms for parameter learning.  
- Draws explicit connections to Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL), highlighting tied variances vs. SBL’s free variances.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a key advantage for signals with both transients and sustained tones.  
- MJLE algorithm is efficient and scalable, avoiding matrix inversions.  
- Demonstrates practical applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement with promising results.  
- Speech enhancement results show improvement over the OMLSA baseline.  
- MMLE implementation does not scale to realistic audio problem sizes, a significant limitation.  
- Hyperparameter λ is sensitive and tuned in an ad hoc way; more systematic study is needed.  
- Comparisons are limited—missing state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., deep learning methods) for speech and recent source-separation methods for music.  
- Little discussion on identifiability of factors W and H, an important theoretical omission.  
- Insufficient details about training/test selection from TIMIT are provided.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions, listing needs to: elaborate MMLE scalability issues, systematically study λ, expand comparative analysis, and discuss identifiability.
