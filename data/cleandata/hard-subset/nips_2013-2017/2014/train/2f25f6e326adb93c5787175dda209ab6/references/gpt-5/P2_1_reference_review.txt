Summary
The paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative time-domain model in which a signal is expressed as a linear combination of time-frequency atoms with complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients whose variances are tied through a low-rank factorization WH. This bridges a gap between probabilistic STFT-domain models (e.g., the Gaussian Composite Model, GCM) and signal-level synthesis: rather than imposing a low-rank structure on the variance of analysis coefficients yfn, LRTFS imposes it on synthesis coefficients αfn in x = Φα + e. Two estimation strategies are developed: (i) maximum joint likelihood (MJLE) with an EM scheme using a Figueiredo–Nowak style splitting, alternating a “Wiener-like” shrinkage of α and IS-NMF on |α|2; (ii) maximum marginal likelihood (MMLE) akin to SBL/ARD, treating α as latent and estimating WH via the posterior second moments of α. The authors also show a multi-resolution extension stacking multiple frames. Experiments on hybrid audio decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement demonstrate modest gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

Relation to prior work
- Extends probabilistic IS-NMF (GCM) by modeling the raw signal via synthesis, not only the STFT magnitudes; closely related to SBL/ARD but with structured hyperparameters v = vec(WH) that couple coefficients across time-frequency.
- Multi-resolution modeling relates to morphological component analysis (MCA) and hybrid audio representations (Daudet, Torresani); here the low-rank prior replaces sparsity.
- The connection to complex/phase-aware NMF and probabilistic models of complex STFT (e.g., complex NMF) should be discussed for positioning; LRTFS is different in operating as a synthesis model in time, but empirical comparisons would help.

Strengths
- Clear modeling contribution: tying Gaussian synthesis-coefficient variances through a nonnegative low-rank structure, yielding a principled time-domain generative model that retains phase.
- EM algorithm for MJLE is computationally practical for audio-scale problems and leverages standard toolboxes for Φα/Φ* operations; the multi-resolution extension is natural and elegant.
- Conceptual link to SBL/ARD is well-articulated; MMLE derivations are correct, even if not yet scalable.
- Experiments suggest consistent, if modest, improvements over reasonable baselines; the decomposition examples are intuitive and the model reconstructs components in the time domain.

Weaknesses
- Empirical validation is limited. The music experiment is largely qualitative with parameter tuning selected post hoc for best SNR; the speech enhancement evaluation uses SNR only, small test set, and potentially unfair capacity mismatches across methods. No reporting of variability, ablations (e.g., single vs multi-resolution), or runtime.
- Scalability of MMLE is not addressed beyond noting it is impractical; no approximate inference (e.g., conjugate gradients, Krylov, message passing exploiting Φ structure) is attempted.
- Some practical details are missing: initialization, normalization to handle NMF scale ambiguities, stopping criteria, choice of β satisfying β ≤ λ/δΦ, and how the warm-start schedule for λ maintains the β–λ condition. Reproducibility would benefit from these.
- The interpretation of the “log(|α|2)” term as sparsity-promoting is potentially misleading; it arises from an algebraic rewrite of the Gaussian prior term (DIS + log decomposition), so the overall regularization effect is exactly that of a Gaussian scale model tied by WH.

Quality
- The model and EM derivations for both MJLE and MMLE are technically sound. The MJLE algorithm decreases the joint negative log-likelihood and avoids large matrix inversions. The MMLE equations are standard for ARD/SBL and correct, but the lack of a scalable solver limits practical impact.
- Empirical evidence supports the claims qualitatively but is not extensive. Benchmarks (e.g., SDR/SIR/SAR for source separation, statistical significance, more noise types, controlled ablations) would strengthen the case.
- The authors are honest about MMLE scalability and that λ was treated as a hyperparameter.

Clarity
- Generally well written and organized. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the links to IS divergence, Wiener filtering, and EM are clear.
- Missing practical details (initialization; β, λ handling; convergence criteria; complexity/runtime) hinder reproducibility.
- Suggest clarifying the role of the log(|α|2) term and emphasizing it is part of an exact decomposition of the Gaussian prior contribution rather than an added sparsity penalty.

Originality
- Novelty lies in moving the low-rank variance structure to synthesis coefficients in a time-domain generative model and showing multi-resolution factorization within this framework. The SBL connection with tied hyperparameters WH is interesting and, to my knowledge, not standard in audio LRTF modeling.
- The algorithmic structure (EM with Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF) cleverly blends known pieces but in a new setting. However, links to complex/phase-aware NMF and structured ARD variants could be fleshed out.

Significance
- For audio and time-frequency modeling, the approach is promising: it retains phase, operates in time, and natively supports multi-resolution. This could influence future designs of generative models for audio.
- Current empirical gains are modest and the evaluation is narrow; broader and more rigorous experiments would be needed to convince the NeurIPS community of strong practical impact.

Questions and suggestions
- Provide details on initialization, normalization of W/H, stopping criteria, and computational cost per iteration; report wall-clock times.
- Clarify how β and λ are set to satisfy β ≤ λ/δΦ during warm restarts.
- Compare against complex/phase-aware NMF baselines and multi-resolution sparse MCA to isolate the benefit of low-rank priors versus sparsity.
- Add ablations: single- vs multi-resolution; effect of K; sensitivity to λ; MJLE vs GCM on the same tasks.
- Consider scalable MMLE approximations (CG-based solves using Φ*Φ structure, stochastic trace estimation, or message passing exploiting frame locality).

Pros
- Principled time-domain generative model with low-rank time-frequency structure
- Computationally practical MJLE algorithm; natural multi-resolution extension
- Clear conceptual bridge to SBL/ARD
- Empirical improvements and compelling qualitative decompositions

Cons
- Limited, partly heuristic experimental validation and tuning
- Missing comparisons to closely related complex/phase-aware methods
- MMLE not scalable; practical details missing for reproducibility

Overall assessment
The paper presents a solid and interesting modeling idea with a practical EM algorithm and a neat multi-resolution extension. While the technical quality and clarity are good, the empirical section is not yet strong enough for an unreserved recommendation. With expanded, fairer evaluations and added practical details, this could be a valuable contribution. As is, I would lean borderline accept for NeurIPS if the committee values novel probabilistic signal models, otherwise borderline reject due to limited experimental rigor.
