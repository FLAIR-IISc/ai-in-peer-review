- Overall: Strong, technically sound contribution proposing LRTFS; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Contribution: Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that synthesizes raw signals with low-rank variance on synthesis coefficients.  
- Novelty vs prior work: Improves over NMF and GCM by modeling raw signal (not just STFT magnitudes) and imposing structured low-rank variance unlike SBL.  
- Algorithms: Derives MMLE and MJLE; MJLE (EM) is practical and efficient, MMLE is currently not scalable.  
- Multi-resolution: Synthesis approach naturally supports multi-resolution representations, beneficial for signals with transients and sustained tones.  
- Theoretical grounding: Clear derivations and connections to SBL, Wiener filtering, and related methods.  
- Empirical results: Promising improvements in music decomposition and speech enhancement; supplementary audio examples provided.  
- Writing: Generally clear and well-organized for the target audience.  
- MMLE weakness: Scalability limitations reduce practical impact of the MMLE approach.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance sensitive to regularization parameter λ; current warm-restart strategy insufficient — needs automatic selection methods.  
- Limited comparisons: Experimental comparisons are limited; should include stronger baselines, e.g., modern deep learning source-separation methods.  
- Convergence theory: Lacks rigorous analysis of EM/MJLE convergence beyond local-optimum remarks.  
- Section 2.1: Hermitian symmetry explanation could be clearer for less-familiar readers.  
- Section 3.1: Modified λ update rule lacks full justification; needs elaboration.  
- Section 5.1: Use of output SNR is limited; add perceptual metrics (e.g., PESQ, STOI) for music decomposition.  
- Section 5.2: Discuss generalizability of TIMIT-trained speech dictionaries to other corpora.  
- Acceptance arguments (pro): Novel generative model, addresses NMF/GCM limitations, practical MJLE, demonstrated effectiveness, clear presentation.  
- Acceptance arguments (con): MMLE not scalable, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Requested revisions: Expand hyperparameter discussion, add broader comparisons, include perceptual evaluation, and justify the λ update rule.
