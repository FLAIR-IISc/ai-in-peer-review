- Introduces LRTFS: models raw signal x(t) as sum of TF atoms with synthesis coefficients whose variances follow a low-rank WH structure, unlike GCM/NMF on STFT analysis coefficients.  
- Proposes two estimators (MJLE, MMLE); MJLE is computationally feasible for audio and uses an EM algorithm that avoids large matrix inversions.  
- Multi-resolution extension: supports different TF bases for different components (e.g., tonal vs transient), addressing NMF’s fixed time-resolution limitation.  
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning: instead of free variances that induce sparsity, LRTFS ties variances via low-rank WH.  
- Empirical results: music decomposition and speech enhancement experiments show ~6.6 dB average SNR improvement over IS-NMF baseline.  
- Strength — Originality: novel direct raw-signal low-rank TF modeling and natural multi-resolution handling.  
- Strength — Significance: practical value for audio tasks and potential extension to other signal domains.  
- Strength — Quality: rigorous derivations, well-designed MJLE algorithm, and clear presentation; limitations acknowledged.  
- Weakness — Theory: lacks convergence/optimality guarantees and does not leverage SBL link for theoretical analysis.  
- Weakness — Experiments: limited comparisons to recent methods and insufficient qualitative/perceptual analysis of decomposed components.  
- Weakness — Practicality: no guidance on selecting hyperparameters (K, λ); λ fixed without thorough justification.  
- Weakness — Multi-resolution: scalability to >2 bases and automatic selection of number of bases not discussed.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested additions on hyperparameter selection and more qualitative decomposition analysis.
