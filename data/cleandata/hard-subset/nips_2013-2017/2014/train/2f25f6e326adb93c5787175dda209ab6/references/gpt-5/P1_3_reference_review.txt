Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a generative, time-domain model for signals with low-rank time-frequency structure, termed Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS). The signal x(t) is modeled as a sum of synthesis atoms φfn(t) weighted by complex Gaussian coefficients αfn with zero mean and variances tied through a nonnegative factorization [WH]fn; additive white Gaussian residual e(t) is included. This contrasts with common nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches that factorize a magnitude/power spectrogram (and discard phase), and with the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) of the STFT which is generative in the STFT domain but not in the raw time domain. The authors derive two estimation strategies: (1) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) with a computationally efficient EM algorithm leveraging a variable-splitting trick inspired by Figueiredo & Nowak; and (2) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE, “type-II” ML as in Sparse Bayesian Learning), which is principled but computationally prohibitive at audio scales. They further show a straightforward multi-resolution extension where multiple frames (e.g., short and long windows) are combined, each with its own low-rank prior, and provide an EM algorithm that scales similarly to single-resolution MJLE.

Connections to prior work are clear: to NMF/IS-NMF for modeling power spectra, to the GCM (probabilistic IS-NMF in the complex STFT domain), to Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL/ARD) via a Gaussian scale formulation but with variances tied through WH rather than free per coefficient, and to hybrid/morphological decompositions in audio (transient/tonal separation) that typically rely on sparse synthesis models rather than low-rank ones. The proposed method bridges the gap between synthesis modeling and low-rank statistical structure, with phase handled implicitly by operating on complex coefficients.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A principled generative model in the time domain with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients (rather than on analysis coefficients) is elegant and distinct from GCM and classical NMF. The tying of variances via WH provides a structured ARD prior and a clear link to SBL.
- Algorithmic contribution: The MJLE EM algorithm is simple, avoids large matrix inversions, and reduces to alternating (i) a shrinkage-like Wiener update of α and (ii) an IS-NMF on |α|^2. This is computationally attractive and easy to implement with existing STFT/Gabor toolboxes.
- Multi-resolution modeling: The extension to multiple frames is natural and practically valuable for audio, enabling transient/tonal separation in a low-rank synthesis paradigm; the proposed EM updates remain scalable.
- Experiments: Two applications (hybrid decomposition of music, semi-supervised speech enhancement) show the model can produce interpretable components and deliver modest, consistent SNR gains over strong baselines (IS-NMF and OMLSA).

Weaknesses
- Limited empirical breadth and analysis: Quantitative gains over IS-NMF in speech enhancement are modest (6.6 vs 6.0 dB); the jazz decomposition is mostly qualitative and involves tuning λ to best SNR. Missing are ablations (effect of β and λ, choice of K, dictionary size), runtime/complexity, robustness to frame choices, and convergence behavior.
- Baseline coverage: Comparisons to phase-aware probabilistic models in the STFT domain (e.g., GCM-based separation with MMSE/Wiener filtering), multi-window NMF, or recent structured SBL/group-ARD models are missing. This makes it hard to isolate the benefit of time-domain synthesis vs analysis-domain modeling.
- MMLE scalability: While well motivated, the presented MMLE algorithm does not scale to realistic audio due to matrix inversions; no approximate large-scale alternative (e.g., conjugate gradients, diagonal/low-rank approximations, message passing) is provided experimentally.
- Clarity/details: Some derivations are sketched (e.g., origin of the log(|α|^2) term in MJLE objective) and several implementation details are under-specified: practical choice/schedule of β, handling Hermitian symmetry for real signals, initialization, stopping criteria, and dictionary normalization. Minor notational slips exist in the multi-resolution section.
- Hyperparameter handling: The paper notes λ estimation performs poorly and is treated as a hyperparameter (or annealed to zero), but does not discuss principled selection or impact on results.

Assessment by criteria
- Quality: The model and MJLE derivations are technically sound and align with known Gaussian/EM machinery. The MMLE section is standard but currently impractical. Experimental evidence supports feasibility but is limited in scope and depth.
- Clarity: Generally well written and organized; equations and updates are explicit. Clarity would benefit from a brief derivation of the MJLE objective decomposition, algorithm pseudocode, and more detail on practical settings (β, λ, initialization).
- Originality: The synthesis-domain low-rank variance model and its EM solver are novel combinations of familiar tools; the multi-resolution low-rank synthesis perspective is fresh relative to NMF/GCM.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for audio modeling and other TF-structured signals, especially where multi-resolution synthesis is advantageous. Current empirical gains are moderate; broader validation would strengthen significance.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Provide a derivation sketch showing how DIS(|α|^2|v) + log(|α|^2) arises in MJLE, and discuss the sparsity-inducing effect of the log term.
- Detail the choice/schedule of β and λ; analyze sensitivity and convergence. Can β be adapted (e.g., based on δΦ) for faster convergence?
- Compare to GCM-based methods (phase-aware STFT models) and to multi-window NMF baselines to isolate the benefit of synthesis vs analysis modeling.
- Report computational cost and scaling with T, M, K; include runtime comparisons to IS-NMF.
- Discuss initialization of W, H, α; provide ablations on K and dictionary normalization.
- Consider approximate MMLE solvers (e.g., CG on linear systems for Σ-vector products, diagonal approximations, AMP/EP-style message passing) and report small-scale results.
- Clarify handling of real signals via Hermitian symmetry and any implications for the EM updates.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, principled modeling contribution that bridges NMF, GCM, and SBL in a synthesis framework.
- Simple, efficient algorithm with natural multi-resolution extension.
- Demonstrates consistent (if modest) improvements over standard baselines.

Cons for acceptance
- Empirical evaluation is limited; gains over strong baselines are small.
- MMLE contribution is not realized at scale; practical value centers on MJLE.
- Some clarity and reproducibility gaps (hyperparameters, initialization, runtime).

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a neat and potentially influential modeling idea with a practical algorithm and compelling multi-resolution capability. Strengthening empirical comparisons and practical details would further increase its impact.
