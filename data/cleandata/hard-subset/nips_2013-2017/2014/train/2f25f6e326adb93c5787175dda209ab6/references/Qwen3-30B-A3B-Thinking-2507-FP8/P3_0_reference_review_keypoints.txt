- Introduces LRTFS, a generative model that models raw signals with low-rank time-frequency synthesis, bridging NMF-like structures and time-domain synthesis.  
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Model and sparse Bayesian learning but operates at the signal level rather than STFT coefficients.  
- Novel multi-resolution extension enables different time-frequency resolutions for different components (e.g., transients vs. tonals).  
- Theoretical formulation is sound with clear derivations and an insightful connection to SBL.  
- EM algorithms for MJLE are well-presented with efficient update rules avoiding expensive matrix inversions.  
- Practical experiments (music decomposition, speech enhancement) show clear advantages, including a 6.6 dB average SNR improvement.  
- Paper addresses computational considerations for audio applications appropriately.  
- Insufficient discussion of MMLE scalability and why it fails for large audio problems; message-passing suggestion underdeveloped.  
- Comparative analysis omits recent deep learning source-separation methods, weakening relevance to current baselines.  
- Lacks a thorough theoretical justification that natural signals satisfy the low-rank assumption in synthesis coefficients.  
- Hyperparameter treatment (λ fixed as hyperparameter) is not justified and sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, meaningful practical contribution with valuable multi-resolution capability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address MMLE discussion, broader comparisons, deeper theory, and hyperparameter details.  
- Pro arguments: models raw signals directly, novel multi-resolution, strong empirical results, solid theoretical foundation.  
- Con arguments: limited MMLE analysis, insufficient deep-learning comparisons, and weaker theoretical justification for low-rank assumption.
