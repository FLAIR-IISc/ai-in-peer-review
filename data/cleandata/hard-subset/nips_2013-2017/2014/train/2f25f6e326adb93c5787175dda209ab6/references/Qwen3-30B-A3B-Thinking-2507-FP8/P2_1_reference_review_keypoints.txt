- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that models raw time-domain signals with synthesis coefficients having a low-rank variance structure.  
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) but shifts modeling from STFT magnitude to the raw signal itself.  
- Proposes Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) with a correctly derived EM algorithm and justified update rules.  
- Naturally supports multi-resolution time-frequency representations, enabling different resolutions for transients vs. tonal components.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in music decomposition and speech enhancement, outperforming baselines (e.g., 6.6 dB vs. 6 dB IS-NMF and 4.6 dB OMLSA).  
- The model preserves phase information, offering practical benefits for high-quality audio synthesis and processing.  
- Paper is technically sound, rigorously derived, and situates itself well within related literature.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear notation and reproducible experimental details.  
- Honest discussion of limitations: MMLE does not scale well due to large M×M matrix inversions for long audio.  
- Significant conceptual contribution by modeling raw signals directly rather than transformed-domain magnitudes.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a genuine novelty not achievable with standard NMF approaches.  
- Strengths: conceptual advance, multi-resolution modeling, sound estimation methods, strong experimental evidence, and transparent limitations.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparisons to recent deep learning-based speech enhancement, no detailed analysis of latent dimension K, and limited discussion of computational scaling and complexity-performance trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, well-supported contribution likely to impact audio signal processing and related inverse problems.
