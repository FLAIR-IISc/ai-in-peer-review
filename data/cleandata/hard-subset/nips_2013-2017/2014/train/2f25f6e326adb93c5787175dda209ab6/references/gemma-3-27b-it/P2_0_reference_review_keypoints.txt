- Introduces LRTFS: a generative model that directly models complex STFT coefficients with a low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances.  
- Proposes two estimation approaches: MJLE via an EM algorithm and MMLE; MMLE is computationally heavy.  
- Demonstrates multi-resolution signal representation and applications to music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Builds on GCM and SBL, extending to a generative model of the raw signal and differing in variance and sparsity properties.  
- Multi-resolution aspect is related to prior audio methods but framed in a principled probabilistic model.  
- Strength: provides a full generative model operating on complex STFT rather than magnitude-only NMF.  
- Strength: seamless integration of multi-resolution analysis for transients vs sustained tones.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding with clear derivation of EM algorithms.  
- Strength: empirical results are promising and compare favorably to provided baselines.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation and logical organization.  
- Weakness: MMLE is not scalable for large problems and was not implemented in audio experiments.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the regularization parameter λ; tuning required and selection strategies are not systematic.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited in scope—more datasets, metrics, and ablation studies needed.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to recent state-of-the-art speech enhancement methods.  
- Weakness: MJLE EM may converge to local optima; mitigation strategies (e.g., multiple restarts) are not discussed.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 7.5/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel generative model, integrated multi-resolution capability, strong theory, promising initial experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: MMLE scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited experiments/comparisons, risk of local optima.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; request discussion of hyperparameter selection, comparisons to recent SOTA, strategies to avoid MJLE local optima, and additional experiments.
