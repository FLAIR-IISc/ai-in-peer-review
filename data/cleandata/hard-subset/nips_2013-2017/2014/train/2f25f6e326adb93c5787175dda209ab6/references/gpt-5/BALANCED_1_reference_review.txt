Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model of a raw time-domain signal x(t) expressed as a sum of time-frequency atoms with complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients αfn whose variances are constrained to have a low-rank structure vfn = [WH]fn. This contrasts with the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) that places a low-rank variance model on the analysis/STFT coefficients yfn. Two estimation strategies are discussed: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) with an EM algorithm that leverages a Figueiredo–Nowak auxiliary variable to avoid large matrix inversions and alternates Wiener-type shrinkage of α with IS-NMF updates on |α|^2; (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE), à la sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), where α is marginalized, but this requires inverting large matrices and is deemed impractical for long signals. The model naturally extends to multi-resolution by stacking multiple frames (e.g., short and long STFT windows) with separate low-rank structures. Experiments include a tonal/transient decomposition of a short jazz excerpt and a semi-supervised speech enhancement task, where LRTFS slightly outperforms an IS-NMF baseline and OMLSA.

This work builds on a well-established line: NMF for magnitude/power spectrograms (Lee & Seung; Fevotte et al.), GCM as a probabilistic model for complex STFT magnitudes with IS divergence, SBL for marginal-likelihood-based variance estimation (Tipping; Wipf & Rao), and hybrid/morphological audio decompositions (Daudet & Torresani; Elad et al.). The multi-resolution design aligns with prior sparse synthesis work using multiple frames.

Quality:
- The MJLE derivation appears sound and results in an efficient algorithm with only analysis/synthesis operations and standard IS-NMF updates. The use of the auxiliary variable z and constraint β ≤ λ/δΦ mirrors Figueiredo–Nowak and is appropriate.
- The MMLE formulation is standard for Gaussian latent variable models but currently impractical due to matrix inversions; the paper is transparent about this.
- One potentially misleading point: rewriting −log p(α|WH) in terms of DIS(|α|^2|v) + log(|α|^2) does not add a new sparsity-inducing term beyond a Gaussian prior; the log(|α|^2) cancels the −log(|α|^2) inside DIS, yielding the usual |α|^2/v + log v. The claim that this term “favors sparse solutions” should be clarified or corrected.
- λ is not estimated in practice (or is annealed to near-zero), which undermines the generative story and complicates fair comparisons; oracle tuning on output SNR is not realistic.
- The experimental evidence is limited: small-scale examples, modest gains over a strong IS-NMF baseline (6.6 vs. 6.0 dB), and no statistical significance or ablations. Computational cost and convergence behavior are not quantified.

Clarity:
The manuscript is generally well written and organized. The relation to GCM and SBL is clear. Some notational issues/typos need attention (e.g., in Eqs. 29–30 the Nc(0,·) mean is missing; clarify frame normalization and δΦ; define how β is chosen). A short discussion on numerical stabilization for near-zero |α| (e.g., adding ε in logs) would help.

Originality:
The key novelty is moving the low-rank variance model from analysis to synthesis coefficients and leveraging this for multi-resolution modeling. The MJLE–EM architecture combining Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF is a neat synthesis of known ingredients. While each component is not new, the combination and the multi-resolution extension in a probabilistic synthesis framework seem original relative to NMF/GCM literature. Related phase-aware/complex NMF works are not discussed and should be cited and contrasted.

Significance:
Potentially useful for audio modeling, as synthesis-domain generative models can enable multi-resolution and layered representations. However, the current empirical evidence is not strong enough to conclude clear advantages over established IS-NMF/GCM methods, and the MMLE pathway is not yet scalable.

Strengths:
- Clear, principled generative synthesis model linking NMF, GCM, and SBL.
- Efficient, inversion-free EM algorithm for MJLE with readily available building blocks.
- Natural multi-resolution extension that is difficult in traditional NMF frameworks.
- Conceptually appealing link between Wiener filtering and the proposed updates.

Weaknesses:
- Experimental validation is limited and gains over IS-NMF are small; no comparison to phase-aware/complex NMF or recent probabilistic STFT models.
- λ is tuned/annealed in ways that compromise the generative interpretation and fair benchmarking.
- MMLE is not scalable; the most compelling statistical benefits remain prospective.
- Some derivational/interpretational clarity issues (sparsity claim for log term; missing details on β, frame normalization, numerical stability).

Questions for the authors:
- How are β and δΦ chosen in practice, and how sensitive is performance to β?
- Is Φ assumed tight/normalized? Does performance depend on frame redundancy?
- How do you select λ without oracle access? Can you report robustness to λ and provide a practical selection rule?
- Can you report runtime and iteration counts versus IS-NMF for the reported tasks?
- How does LRTFS compare to GCM applied to STFT (with consistent phase reconstruction), and to complex/phase-aware NMF baselines?

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide stronger, statistically grounded experiments on standard datasets with ablations: single- vs multi-resolution, effect of λ, effect of K, and sensitivity to initialization; report SDR/PESQ/STOI where relevant.
- Include baselines that model phase (complex NMF, phase-constrained NMF, GCM) and a multi-resolution NMF variant if applicable.
- Either estimate λ in a principled way (e.g., empirical Bayes with safeguards) or propose a practical, data-driven selection rule; avoid oracle tuning.
- Clarify the “sparsity” discussion around the log(|α|^2) term; it is just an algebraic decomposition of the Gaussian prior.
- Detail computational complexity per iteration and provide convergence plots; release code if possible.
- Expand the related work to include phase-aware NMF and prior synthesis-domain probabilistic models.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The modeling idea is interesting and the algorithm is elegant and efficient, but the current empirical evidence and clarity around key choices (notably λ) are insufficient for NeurIPS. Strengthening experimental validation and clarifying the theoretical claims would make this a stronger submission.
