- Proposes LRTFS: a time-domain generative model with a low-rank prior on synthesis coefficients α in x = Φα + e, with variances v = vec(WH).

- Two estimation strategies: MJLE via an efficient EM (Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF updates) and MMLE (SBL-like) which is elegant but currently not scalable due to large matrix inversions.

- Natural multi-resolution extension by stacking multiple frames with independent low-rank priors.

- Experiments (hybrid music decomposition, semi-supervised speech enhancement) show modest but consistent SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

- Main conceptual novelty: shifting low-rank structure from analysis (STFT coefficients) to synthesis coefficients enables time-domain modeling and seamless multi-resolution formulations.

- Strong connections to prior work (GCM/probabilistic NMF, SBL, morphological component analysis) but missing empirical comparisons to phase-aware/complex NMF and other time-domain probabilistic models.

- MJLE methodology and EM derivation are sound and practical; MMLE mirrors SBL but faces scalability challenges.

- Algebraic identity means the DIS(|α|^2|v)+log(|α|^2) decomposition does not imply an independent sparsity-promoting penalty—current text misrepresents this as sparsity-inducing.

- Convergence concerns: λ update is not an exact M-step; the paper should provide a monotonicity argument or explicit reference and give guidance/sensitivity analysis for choosing β.

- Identifiability issues persist (NMF scale ambiguity, non-uniqueness in multi-frame decompositions); discussion of normalization and regularization (e.g., smoothness on H) is needed.

- Paper is overall clear and well organized, but has minor notation/indexing errors (Eq.29–30, Eq.32) and needs to clarify the log(|α|^2) interpretation.

- Originality assessed as moderate: known components recombined in a new place; multi-resolution extension is elegant and practical.

- Significance: promising for audio tasks requiring multi-resolution/time-domain synthesis, but wider impact limited by modest empirical gains and weak baselines.

- Empirical strengths: two relevant applications and plausible qualitative decompositions; limitations: small-scale tests, lack of modern metrics (STOI/PESQ/SI-SDR), no runtime/scalability reporting, and missing ablations (multi- vs single-resolution, K, frame choices).

- Pros: principled time-domain low-rank model, scalable MJLE algorithm, easy implementation with TF toolboxes, natural multi-resolution formulation, good theoretical connections.

- Cons: incremental novelty, misleading sparsity claim, non-scalable MMLE, unstable λ estimation and reliance on hyperparameter tuning/annealing, limited empirical evaluation.

- Suggested improvements: clarify log-term role; provide β selection and convergence/monotonicity guidance; add complex/phase-aware baselines and modern metrics; include ablations and runtime results; discuss identifiability and propose regularizers; explore approximate MMLE (low-rank approximations, CG, message passing).

- Recommendation: Weak accept — coherent and practically useful shift to synthesis-side low-rank modeling with an efficient algorithm and multi-resolution extension, but needs clearer objective interpretation and stronger empirical validation.
