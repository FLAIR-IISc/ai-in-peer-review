- Novel, technically sound generative model (LRTFS) that models raw signal as sum of complex TF atoms with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients.  
- Clear contrast with prior GCM/NMF approaches by imposing low-rankness on synthesis rather than analysis coefficients.  
- Two estimation methods proposed (MJLE and MMLE) with EM-based algorithms; MJLE is practical and efficient.  
- Multi-resolution extension is a strong contribution and promising for varied applications.  
- MMLE suffers from poor scalability due to required matrix inversions, limiting practical impact.  
- Experiments (music decomposition, speech enhancement) show promising results but are limited in scope and comparisons.  
- Need more comprehensive evaluation on standardized datasets, additional baselines, and error bars for results.  
- Performance is sensitive to hyperparameter λ; need systematic study and automatic selection strategies.  
- The paper lacks theoretical analysis justifying multi-resolution benefits.  
- Recommend adding discussion of approximation or stochastic methods to address MMLE inversion bottleneck.  
- Request clarification of Eq.(1) (complex normal centered) and motivation for the λ update rule differing from direct EM.  
- Ask for more details on selection of K and Gabor window sizes, and explanation of the λ warm-restart strategy.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing scalability, expanded experiments, and hyperparameter analysis.
