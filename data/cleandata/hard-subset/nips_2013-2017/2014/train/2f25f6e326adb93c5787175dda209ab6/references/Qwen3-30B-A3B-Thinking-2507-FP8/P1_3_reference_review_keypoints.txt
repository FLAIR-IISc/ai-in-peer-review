- Summary: Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model that synthesizes raw signals from low-rank time-frequency components, extending NMF/GCM approaches.  
- Novelty: Modeling the raw signal directly with low-rank time-frequency structure is conceptually new and meaningful.  
- Multi-resolution: Naturally supports multi-resolution representations (e.g., transients vs. tonal components), a clear practical advantage over traditional NMF.  
- Experiments: Demonstrates empirical gains in music decomposition and speech enhancement (e.g., 6.6 dB SNR improvement).  
- Technical rigor: Derivations and links to sparse Bayesian learning are thorough and well justified.  
- Weakness — scope: Paper focuses on audio and lacks discussion of generalization to other signal types (e.g., biomedical, video).  
- Weakness — deep learning comparison: Does not compare to modern deep learning methods (e.g., wave-U-Net, Conv-TasNet), weakening ML relevance.  
- Weakness — MMLE practicality: Insufficient justification for preferring MJLE over the computationally heavier MMLE in applications.  
- Weakness — theory: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis (identifiability, convergence guarantees) expected at a theoretical ML venue.  
- Pro argument: Conceptual bridge between time-frequency analysis and signal synthesis with practical improvements.  
- Pro argument: Multi-resolution capability is a significant and novel contribution.  
- Pro argument: Solid empirical validation in audio tasks.  
- Pro argument: Clear connections to NMF and sparse Bayesian learning literature.  
- Con argument: Omits comparison to dominant deep learning audio approaches.  
- Con argument: Limited discussion of applicability beyond audio signals.  
- Con argument: MMLE limitations not thoroughly addressed for practical ML use.  
- Con argument: Insufficient theoretical guarantees for a NIPS-style audience.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, well-written, and original with strong audio relevance but narrower ML significance without broader comparisons and theory.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request adding deep-learning comparisons (or discussion), broader application discussion, and more justification for choosing MJLE over MMLE.
