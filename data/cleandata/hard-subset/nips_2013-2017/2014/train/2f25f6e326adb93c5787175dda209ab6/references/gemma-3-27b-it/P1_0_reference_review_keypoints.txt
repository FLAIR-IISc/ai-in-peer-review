- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model that directly models raw signals via low-rank time-frequency synthesis coefficients.
- Builds on GCM/NMF and connects to SBL, addressing phase and generative limitations of STFT-based decompositions.
- Presents MJLE and MMLE parameter estimation using EM algorithms for the proposed model.
- MJLE is computationally efficient and avoids expensive matrix inversions, making it practical.
- MMLE implementation does not scale to realistic signal lengths, limiting its applicability.
- Framework naturally supports multi-resolution analysis, demonstrated with hybrid decomposition examples.
- Empirical validation shows competitive performance on music decomposition and speech enhancement versus IS-NMF and OMLSA.
- Theoretical derivations and connection to SBL provide a solid mathematical foundation.
- Writing and presentation are clear and well-organized.
- Sensitive to the hyperparameter λ, and only a warm-restart heuristic is provided rather than systematic tuning.
- Experimental comparisons are limited; lacks broader benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods.
- No ablation study to quantify contributions of low-rank constraint, multi-resolution, and other components.
- Residual is modeled as white Gaussian noise, which may be too simplistic for complex signals.
- Algorithmic novelty is limited since EM is a standard technique applied to the new model.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound generative model with multi-resolution capability and practical MJLE algorithm.
- Arguments against acceptance: MMLE scalability issues, limited experiments and ablation, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with weaknesses outweighed by strengths.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: broaden experimental comparisons, add ablation study, detail hyperparameter selection, and consider more sophisticated residual models.
