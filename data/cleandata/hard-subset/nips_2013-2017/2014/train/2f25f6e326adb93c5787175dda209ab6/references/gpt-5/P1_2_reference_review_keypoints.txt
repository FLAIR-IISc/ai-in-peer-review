- Proposes LRTFS: a generative time-domain model imposing low-rank structure on variances of complex synthesis coefficients (α) using x = Φα + e and αfn ~ Nc(0,[WH]fn).  
- Shifts low-rank modeling from STFT analysis coefficients (as in GCM/NMF) to synthesis coefficients, enabling phase-aware and waveform-level modeling.  
- Supports multi-resolution modeling by stacking multiple time-frequency frames (e.g., short and long windows) with separate low-rank priors.  
- Presents two estimation strategies: scalable MJLE using an EM-like variable-splitting trick and IS-NMF updates, and MMLE (type-II) that requires large matrix inversions and is impractical.  
- MJLE uses Wiener-like shrinkage for α and IS-NMF on |α|^2 to estimate W,H, avoiding expensive inversions via Φ/Φ* operations.  
- Derives component reconstruction formulas and intuitive Wiener-like update rules for latent components.  
- Experiments: hybrid tonal/transient decomposition demo and semi-supervised speech enhancement showing modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (few signals), uses mainly SNR, lacks perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI), statistical significance, and runtime/memory analysis.  
- First demo tunes λ ex post for best SNR, risking optimistic/biased evaluation.  
- Comparisons omit stronger/recent baselines (complex/phase-aware NMF, multiresolution NMF, supervised NMF with temporal priors) and lack an ablation against GCM.  
- MMLE is conceptually appealing but not scalable; no proposed scalable approximations (e.g., variational inference or AMP).  
- Some derivation/notation issues: misleading “sparsity” claim for log(|α|^2) term, minor notational errors (missing mean in Nc(0,·)), and incomplete reference formatting.  
- Convergence/monotonicity guarantees are partially addressed; hybrid EM/NMF/heuristic λ updates need clearer statements on what is guaranteed to decrease.  
- Modeling and algorithms are technically sound and practically implementable; MJLE is computationally attractive via Φ/Φ* operations.  
- Paper is overall clear and well organized, but MJLE objective algebra and role of log(|α|^2) need clarification, and more implementation details (β choice, step sizes, stopping criteria) are required.  
- The synthesis-domain low-rank variance idea with native multi-resolution capability is novel and nontrivial relative to existing NMF/GCM work.  
- Potentially significant for audio and inverse problems due to waveform-level, phase-preserving modeling, but impact limited by thin empirical evidence.  
- Questions for authors include practical β selection, monotonicity/convergence guarantees for the full alternating scheme, comparisons to complex/phase-aware NMF with PESQ/STOI, enforcing Hermitian symmetry for real signals, and ablation for multi-resolution benefits.  
- Suggested improvements: add stronger baselines and perceptual metrics, report computation times and variance across trials, clarify MJLE algebra and sparsity claim, provide guidance for β/λ and release code, and propose scalable MMLE approximations.  
- Pros: principled generative synthesis model, scalable MJLE algorithm, natural and effective multi-resolution modeling, clear links to probabilistic factorizations and SBL.  
- Cons: limited experiments and baselines, modest empirical gains, unclear tuning choices, some clarity/rigor issues, and MMLE not scalable.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — interesting modeling and practical algorithm but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer theoretical/implementation details.
