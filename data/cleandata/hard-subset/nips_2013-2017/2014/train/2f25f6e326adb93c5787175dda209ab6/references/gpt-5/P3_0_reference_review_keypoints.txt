- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a time-domain generative model with synthesis coefficients αfn ∼ Nc(0,[WH]fn) tying variances via low-rank WH.  
- Contrasts with analysis-domain GCM (STFT coefficients modeled) and provides synthesis-domain interpretation linking NMF, Wiener filtering, and signal synthesis.  
- Derives two estimation approaches: scalable MJLE EM (auxiliary-variable Figueiredo–Nowak style) and computationally prohibitive MMLE EM (type-II / SBL-like).  
- MJLE alternates a Wiener-like shrinkage for α and IS-NMF on |α|^2 for W,H; avoids large matrix inversions and is practical.  
- MMLE marginalizes α and parallels SBL but is not made scalable for audio-scale problems.  
- Extends naturally to multi-resolution via a union of frames with separate low-rank factorizations, useful for tonal/transient separation.  
- Conceptual contribution: principled synthesis-domain low-rank variance model that closes gap between probabilistic modeling and time-domain synthesis.  
- Clear theoretical links established to IS-NMF, Wiener filtering, and SBL; log(|α|^2) term emerges as a concave sparsity-promoting penalty.  
- Scalable MJLE algorithm is simple, relies on fast frame analysis/synthesis and standard IS-NMF updates.  
- Multi-resolution extension is elegant and practically relevant with minimal algorithmic changes.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small datasets, modest gains, and hyperparameter tuning (λ) undermines ML interpretation.  
- Baselines are incomplete: missing direct comparison to phase-aware GCM/complex NMF with Wiener reconstruction.  
- MMLE is presented but no practical, scalable approximations (e.g., iterative solvers, CG, preconditioning) are attempted.  
- Identifiability, degeneracy, and regularization issues (scale ambiguity, component death) are under-discussed and not addressed (e.g., no W normalization).  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing implementation details (initialization, K and β selection, stopping criteria, runtime, FFT params, frame normalization).  
- Reported improvements are modest (e.g., 6.6 dB vs 6 dB) with no statistical tests or perceptual metrics (STOI/PESQ); music example is small and qualitative.  
- Some experimental choices (λ handling, warm restarts) are ad hoc and could bias results.  
- Paper is well written, organized, and EM steps are implementable; connections to prior work are clear.  
- Notational clarifications needed (distribution means, role of β and δΦ, frame tightness/normalization).  
- Originality: novel synthesis-domain low-rank variance model and a meaningful tied-variance MMLE twist relative to SBL.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for audio modeling/enhancement, especially multi-resolution and phase-consistent synthesis, but current empirical support is weak.  
- Suggested improvements: add GCM/complex NMF baseline, ablations (single vs multi-res, λ/β/K, log-term), convergence diagnostics, W normalization and simple regularizers.  
- Suggested scalability work: explore CG or matrix-free solvers, trace estimation, or variational approximations for MMLE.  
- Suggested evaluation improvements: report runtime, convergence, perceptual metrics, statistical significance, and avoid tuning λ on test SNR.  
- Overall: solid and interesting modeling contribution with a practical MJLE algorithm and multi-resolution story, but needs stronger baselines, more rigorous evaluation, and clearer implementation details to justify top-tier acceptance.
