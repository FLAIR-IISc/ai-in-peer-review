- Novel generative model (LRTFS) that directly models raw signal x(t) with low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances.  
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and connects to sparse Bayesian learning; extends to multi-resolution hybrid time–frequency representations.  
- Derives an EM-based maximum likelihood estimation framework and practical MJLE algorithm.  
- MJLE EM avoids costly matrix inversions, making it computationally tractable in practice.  
- Demonstrated empirical effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement experiments.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a key strength for handling varying temporal characteristics.  
- Solid theoretical foundation and clear EM derivations grounded in probabilistic principles.  
- MMLE variant suffers scalability issues due to required matrix inversions.  
- Performance sensitive to regularization hyperparameter λ; tuning is required.  
- Treating λ as a fixed hyperparameter lacks theoretical justification and is not estimated.  
- Comparative evaluation is limited — lacks broader state-of-the-art (especially deep learning) baselines.  
- Notation can be dense/inconsistent in places (indexing conventions could be simplified).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss approximate inference for MMLE scalability, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity/auto-tuning, expand comparative evaluation, and justify or discuss estimation of λ.
