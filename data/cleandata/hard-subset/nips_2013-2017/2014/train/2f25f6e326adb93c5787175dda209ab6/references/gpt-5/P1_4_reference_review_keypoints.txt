- Introduces LRTFS: a time-domain generative model x = Φα + e with synthesis coefficients αfn ∼ Nc(0, [WH]fn) enforcing low-rank NMF variance WH across time-frequency.  
- Contrasts with GCM: moves low-rank variance from STFT analysis to synthesis coefficients, enabling direct time-domain likelihood and multi-resolution synthesis.  
- Proposes two estimation strategies: MJLE (efficient EM with auxiliary variable) and MMLE (type-II ML/SBL), with MMLE noted as computationally challenging at audio scale.  
- MJLE EM yields simple updates: Wiener-like shrinkage for α, IS-NMF updates for W,H, and a residual variance update using the Figueiredo–Nowak decomposition.  
- Multi-resolution extension is straightforward: sum multiple frames Φℓ with separate low-rank structures on αℓ, remaining computationally efficient.  
- Builds on prior work (IS-NMF, GCM, SBL, multi-resolution/morphological methods) but is novel in tying a low-rank variance prior to synthesis coefficients.  
- Derivations and likelihoods are clear and technically sound; the MMLE Σ update is correctly identified as infeasible at large scale.  
- The MJLE algorithm is computationally attractive because it avoids large matrix inversions and relies on Φ/Φ* operations.  
- Experiments demonstrate feasibility, modest SNR gains over single-window IS-NMF, and qualitative improvements in separating transient vs tonal components.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small test sets, oracle tuning of λ in one task, modest gains, and missing extensive metrics or ablations.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; the distinction between analysis y and synthesis α is explicit and helpful.  
- Requests for clarification: how β is chosen in practice, initialization strategies for W,H,α, and concrete handling of real signals via Hermitian symmetry.  
- Suggests providing pseudo-code for the MJLE algorithm and the multi-resolution variant for implementability.  
- Originality: novel conceptual move of low-rank variance on synthesis coefficients and linking it to IS-NMF via EM/Wiener-like shrinkage.  
- Significance: promising for audio modeling, source separation, and speech enhancement but currently limited by insufficient empirical validation.  
- Pros: principled time-domain generative model, efficient MJLE algorithm, natural multi-resolution support, and insightful links to IS-NMF and shrinkage.  
- Cons: limited empirical validation, missing comparisons to closest probabilistic baselines (GCM, multi-window NMF), no runtime/complexity benchmarks, and no ablation studies.  
- MMLE section lacks scalable alternatives; no implementation of possible approximations (e.g., conjugate gradients, message passing) is provided.  
- Reviewer suggestions: report wall-clock times and scaling, avoid oracle λ tuning (propose cross-validation or principled schedules), add baselines (GCM, multi-window IS-NMF, MCA), report perceptual metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, STOI/PESQ), and run ablations on resolution, K, and initialization.  
- Ask authors to discuss identifiability issues and potential degeneracies between α and λ and whether low-rank structure sufficiently regularizes α.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept — novel and solid theoretical contribution with an efficient algorithm, but empirical validation needs strengthening.
