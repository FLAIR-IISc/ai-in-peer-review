Summary
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative time-domain model that imposes a low-rank structure on the variance of synthesis coefficients over a time-frequency (t-f) dictionary. Unlike prior Gaussian Composite Models (GCMs) that operate on STFT analysis coefficients and ignore synthesis, LRTFS models the raw signal x(t) as a sum of t-f atoms φfn(t) weighted by complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients αfn with variance [WH]fn, plus Gaussian residual e(t). This bridges NMF-like low-rank modeling and synthesis-based signal representations, and naturally extends to multi-resolution by combining multiple frames (e.g., short and long windows).

Two estimation strategies are presented. The main one (MJLE) uses an EM algorithm with a Figueiredo–Nowak auxiliary-variable split, yielding simple alternating updates: a Wiener-like shrinkage for α and standard IS-NMF multiplicative updates for W,H, with no large matrix inversions. A second approach (MMLE), akin to type-II ML in Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL), marginalizes α but is not scalable for typical audio sizes. The paper also shows how to reconstruct latent components and how multi-resolution modeling is handled seamlessly.

Experiments illustrate a tonal/transient music decomposition and a semi-supervised speech enhancement setup (speech + applause), showing qualitative component separation and modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

Relation to prior work
- Builds on IS-NMF for spectrogram factorization and the GCM that places a low-rank variance on STFT analysis coefficients.
- Connects to SBL: α has Gaussian priors with hierarchical variance, but here variances are tied via WH rather than free per-coefficient (hence structured, not sparsity-inducing in the same way).
- Multi-resolution modeling echoes hybrid/morphological decompositions (Daudet, Torrésani; MCA), but here within a probabilistic low-rank synthesis framework.
- Related work on phase-aware/complex NMF and probabilistic models of complex STFT could be discussed more explicitly.

Strengths
- Novel conceptual shift: a probabilistic, low-rank model in the synthesis (time-domain) space rather than analysis/STFT space. This is a bold, meaningful rethinking of where the low-rank prior should live.
- Elegant and practical inference: the MJLE EM reduces to Wiener-like shrinkage for α and IS-NMF for WH; no large matrix inversions; leverages existing t-f toolboxes. Clear connection to classic shrinkage/denoising viewpoints.
- Multi-resolution comes “for free” by stacking frames; this is a notable advantage over fixed-resolution NMF/GCM pipelines and aligns with audio’s inherently multiscale structure.
- Interpretable latent components and straightforward Wiener-type reconstruction.
- The framework looks extensible (e.g., smoothness or sparsity on W/H, multichannel).

Weaknesses
- Experimental validation is thin and sometimes problematic:
  - Small-scale experiments (6s music, 10 speech clips); modest gains over a decent IS-NMF baseline (6.6 vs 6.0 dB average SNR).
  - λ is tuned by selecting the best output SNR in the music example, which risks test-time overfitting; the speech experiment uses a warm-restart schedule but lacks justification and sensitivity analysis.
  - Limited baselines (no strong modern speech enhancement baselines; only OMLSA and a single-window IS-NMF).
  - No ablations (e.g., effect of the log(|α|^2) term, the multi-resolution vs single resolution within LRTFS, or different K).
  - No runtime or scalability reporting, though complexity is a selling point.
- The MMLE route is conceptually appealing but currently unusable at audio scale; no approximate or iterative alternatives are provided (e.g., conjugate gradients, matrix-free ops, message passing).
- Missing details that affect reproducibility: choice of β, initialization strategies, stopping criteria, handling real-valued signals via Hermitian symmetry, and sensitivity to dictionary/frame choices.
- Possible leakage/identifiability issues between layers in the multi-resolution setting are not analyzed; some structured priors (e.g., temporal smoothness on H, cross-layer penalties) might be necessary.

Quality
Technically sound overall; the derivations and EM updates are reasonable and consistent with prior art. The appearance of the log(|α|^2) term in the joint objective is interesting—derivation could be expanded for clarity. Claims are mostly supported at a proof-of-concept level; stronger empirical evidence would help.

Clarity
Well-organized and generally clear. The link to GCM/SBL and the EM architecture is well explained. Missing practical details (β, initialization, stopping, computational cost). A short pseudo-code block would greatly help practitioners.

Originality
High. Moving low-rank structure into synthesis coefficients in a probabilistic time-domain model and showing a clean, scalable EM procedure plus multi-resolution extension is a fresh contribution relative to standard NMF/GCM pipelines and SBL.

Significance
Promising. If adopted, this could reshape how low-rank audio models are built, enabling coherent multiscale modeling and bridging analysis/synthesis viewpoints. Current empirical gains are modest, but the conceptual advance and practicality make it a strong candidate for follow-up work.

Questions/requests for authors
- How is β chosen in practice, and how sensitive is performance to β and λ? Any rules of thumb using frame bounds?
- Can you report runtimes and memory for MJLE and multi-resolution MJLE on your datasets?
- Any mitigation for cross-layer leakage (e.g., coupling priors, shared H constraints)? Did you observe mode collapse or identifiability issues?
- Please add comparisons to complex/phase-aware NMF and to stronger speech enhancement baselines, and report perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI) and intelligibility.
- Clarify the derivation and role of the log(|α|^2) term and provide an ablation demonstrating its impact.

Reasons to accept
- Clear, novel generative formulation in the synthesis domain with a scalable and elegant EM algorithm.
- Natural, principled multi-resolution extension.
- Bridges influential lines of work (NMF, GCM, SBL) in a new way; likely to inspire follow-up research.

Reasons to reject
- Limited and partially suboptimal experimental methodology; modest gains over strong baselines.
- MMLE remains non-scalable with no practical approximation offered.
- Missing implementation details and ablations reduce reproducibility and clarity of empirical claims.

Recommendation
Leaning accept. The conceptual advance and clean, practical algorithm outweigh the experimental limitations. Strengthening the empirical section and adding missing details would make this a compelling NeurIPS contribution.
