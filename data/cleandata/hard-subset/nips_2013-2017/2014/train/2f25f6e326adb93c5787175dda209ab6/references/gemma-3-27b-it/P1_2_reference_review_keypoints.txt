- Introduces LRTFS, a generative model that synthesizes raw signals as sums of time-frequency atoms with low-rank synthesis coefficients.
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and NMF, differing by modeling the signal directly rather than just magnitude spectrograms.
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL) but uses a low-rank variance structure that does not promote sparsity.
- Proposes an EM-based maximum likelihood estimation (MJLE) algorithm that avoids expensive matrix inversions.
- Enables natural multi-resolution representations, beneficial for signals with varying temporal characteristics (e.g., music transients, speech phonemes).
- Presents empirical validation on music decomposition and speech enhancement with competitive SNR improvements.
- Clear and rigorous mathematical formulation and presentation, with well-articulated links to related work.
- Lacks detailed theoretical analysis of model properties and EM convergence guarantees.
- MMLE approach is acknowledged but currently not scalable, limiting practical benefits of marginal likelihood estimation.
- Experiments show sensitivity to hyperparameter λ, needing more systematic selection or adaptive strategies.
- Experimental scope is limited; evaluation should include more datasets and stronger comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Missing ablation studies to quantify the advantage of multi-resolution over single-resolution models.
- Strengths: novel generative framework, multi-resolution capability, computational practicality, and promising empirical results.
- Weaknesses: limited theory, scalability concerns for MMLE, hyperparameter sensitivity, narrow experimental evaluation, and lack of ablations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting more theoretical discussion, expanded experiments, ablation studies, and hyperparameter investigation.
