- Introduces LRTFS that models raw signals as sums of low-rank time-frequency components, bridging NMF and generative raw-signal models.  
- Extends GCM to the time domain and positions work as a natural extension of NMF and SBL; connection to recent deep-learning audio methods is under-discussed.  
- Strong methodological development: MJLE EM algorithm avoids expensive matrix inversions and yields an objective with an IS divergence term.  
- Multi-resolution extension is a compelling and original solution to fixed time-resolution limitations of NMF.  
- Speech enhancement evaluation is weak: only 10 test samples, insufficient for robust statistical claims.  
- Baseline choices are unclear (e.g., 256-sample window) and not justified.  
- Lacks comparison to recent deep learning state-of-the-art speech enhancement methods.  
- Provides no empirical evidence that MJLE outperforms MMLE in practical audio settings despite claiming scalability advantages.  
- Paper is generally clear with consistent notation; SBL connection is helpful.  
- Needs clarification on treating λ as a hyperparameter, more implementation details on Gabor frames, and additional time-domain component visualizations.  
- Originality is solid: modeling raw signal with low-rank TF structure and multi-resolution capability are novel for NMF-style approaches.  
- Does not fully exploit the SBL connection for deeper theoretical insight or clearly distinguish from deep generative models (e.g., WaveNet).  
- Significance for audio signal processing is substantial, demonstrated by practical benefits (6.6 dB SNR gain) but requires broader validation.  
- Recommended additional work: expand experiments across audio types, analyze computational complexity/scalability, and discuss integration with deep learning.  
- Key strengths: natural GCM→raw extension, efficient MJLE without matrix inversions, multi-resolution capability, and promising experimental results.  
- Key weaknesses: insufficient deep-learning comparisons, limited experimental validation, inadequate empirical MMLE analysis, and limited convergence/theoretical analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; requested revisions include larger/varied speech enhancement experiments, DL baseline comparisons, justification of λ, and clarification of relation to recent deep generative models.
