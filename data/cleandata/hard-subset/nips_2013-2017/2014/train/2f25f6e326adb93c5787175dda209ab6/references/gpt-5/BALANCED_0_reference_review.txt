Summary
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model of time-domain signals that represents x(t) as a linear combination of time-frequency atoms with complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients whose variances follow a low-rank structure WH, plus Gaussian residual noise. This contrasts with prior Gaussian Composite Models (GCM) that impose low-rank structure on the variance of analysis (STFT) coefficients. The authors develop two estimation strategies: (1) Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) via an EM algorithm that uses a variable-splitting trick (after Figueiredo & Nowak) and requires only analysis/synthesis operations (no large matrix inversions); and (2) Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE; type-II ML in the SBL spirit) that is principled but computationally prohibitive in typical audio regimes. A multi-resolution extension is obtained by stacking multiple Gabor frames, enabling layer-wise modeling with different time-frequency resolutions. Experiments on hybrid decomposition (tonal/transient) and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines.

Relation to prior work
The work is closely related to NMF of magnitude/power spectrograms (Lee & Seung; Févotte et al.), the GCM model for complex STFTs with IS-divergence equivalence, and sparse Bayesian learning (Tipping; Wipf & Rao). The novelty lies in modeling the synthesis coefficients directly (time-domain generative model) with a tied low-rank variance structure, rather than analysis coefficients. The multi-resolution idea connects to hybrid/morphological audio models (Daudet & Torrésani; Elad et al.) but is here combined with a low-rank latent variance model rather than sparsity alone.

Strengths
- Conceptual advance: a time-domain generative model tying synthesis coefficients via a low-rank variance; bridges NMF/IS divergence and synthesis modeling.
- Practical MJLE algorithm: efficient EM updates requiring only Φ and Φ* operations, leveraging standard toolboxes; no large matrix inversions.
- Clear connection to Wiener filtering for latent component reconstruction; interpretable latent layers.
- Multi-resolution extension is natural and computationally straightforward; relevant for audio (transients vs. sustained tones).
- Experiments indicate consistent, albeit modest, improvements over standard baselines.

Weaknesses
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small-scale studies, SNR-only metrics, a single noise type emphasized (applause), and modest gains (6.6 dB vs. 6.0 dB for IS-NMF).
- Missing comparisons to stronger phase-aware/complex NMF or GCM-based baselines under identical settings; no ablations (e.g., single vs. multi-resolution; role of K; impact of λ schedule).
- λ is treated as a tuned hyperparameter (and annealed to 0), which undermines the fully generative story and raises concerns about robustness and overfitting.
- MMLE section is largely theoretical; no scalable implementation or approximate inference (e.g., conjugate gradients, iterative solvers, variational/AMP) is attempted.
- Limited discussion of identifiability/scaling ambiguities inherent in NMF-like models, especially with multiple frames/layers.
- Some derivations and notational points would benefit from more clarity (e.g., the emergence and interpretation of the log(|α|^2) term; initialization; convergence guarantees).
- Reproducibility: no code or detailed hyperparameter settings; references are incomplete (missing years/pages).

Quality
Technically sound overall; MJLE derivations follow established EM/variable-splitting methods, and the DIS connection is correct. The MMLE formulation is standard in SBL but currently impractical. Claims are generally supported, but the experimental evidence is not strong enough to establish clear state-of-the-art improvements.

Clarity
The paper is mostly clear and well organized. The description of the EM steps is helpful and practical. However, more detail is needed on initialization, hyperparameter schedules, and how the log(|α|^2) term affects shrinkage/sparsity. Figures and listening examples are useful but not accessible in the paper.

Originality
Moderate to good. The shift from analysis-variance low-rank models to synthesis-variance low-rank modeling is a meaningful conceptual change with practical implications (time-domain generative modeling, multi-resolution). The algorithmic components are combinations of known ideas, but the overall formulation is novel.

Significance
Potentially impactful for audio and other time-series domains where multi-resolution synthesis is beneficial. Current experiments show only modest gains; stronger, broader evaluations would be needed to demonstrate clear practical significance.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel, principled generative model at the synthesis level with low-rank variance.
- Efficient EM algorithm with practical applicability.
- Natural multi-resolution extension with promising qualitative results.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited and somewhat weak empirical validation; modest gains over strong baselines.
- Heavy reliance on tuned/annealed λ; lack of robustness/sensitivity analyses.
- No scalable MMLE or alternative approximate inference despite being a stated contribution.
- Missing comparisons to stronger/phase-aware baselines and ablations demonstrating where the gains come from.

Questions for authors
1. How sensitive is performance to λ and its annealing schedule? Can you provide guidelines or automated selection?
2. How does multi-resolution LRTFS compare directly to single-resolution LRTFS and to GCM/complex NMF under identical conditions?
3. What is the computational cost (runtime/memory) relative to IS-NMF for typical audio lengths?
4. Can you clarify the origin and role of the log(|α|^2) term in CJL, and its impact on shrinkage patterns?
5. Any progress on scalable MMLE (e.g., iterative linear solvers, low-rank approximations, AMP/variational methods)?

Suggestions for improvement
- Add broader benchmarks (more noise types), speech metrics (STOI/PESQ), and ablations (multi- vs single-resolution; window lengths; K; initialization).
- Compare against GCM/phase-aware NMF and other multiresolution decompositions.
- Provide sensitivity analyses for λ, β, and dictionary sizes; discuss identifiability and scaling normalization across layers.
- Explore scalable approximate MMLE (CG-based E-steps, diagonal/low-rank covariance approximations, variational inference).
- Release code and detailed configs for reproducibility.

Recommendation: Weak reject
While the idea is appealing and the MJLE algorithm is practical, the empirical section is not yet strong enough for NeurIPS, and the MMLE contribution remains unrealized at scale. With stronger evaluations and comparisons, this could become a solid contribution.

Confidence: 0.7
