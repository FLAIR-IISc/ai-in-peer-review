- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model that synthesizes the raw signal by imposing a low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances.
- Extends GCM and NMF frameworks and connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning; integrates multi-resolution TF ideas within a principled generative model.
- Proposes two estimation approaches (MJLE and MMLE); MMLE is theoretically interesting but computationally prohibitive for the presented audio tasks.
- Multi-resolution capability allows adaptation to varying transient and tonal signal characteristics.
- Mathematical derivation is sound and EM algorithms are well-described.
- Experiments show effective music decomposition (tonal vs transient separation).
- Speech enhancement results outperform IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines in the reported tests.
- Results are sensitive to the regularization parameter λ; lacks systematic hyperparameter tuning strategies.
- Comparative analysis is limited — missing comparisons to state-of-the-art (e.g., deep learning) methods in music decomposition and speech enhancement.
- No theoretical guarantees provided for EM convergence or quality of local optima.
- Some clarity and notation issues, especially in the multi-resolution extension presentation.
- Overall quality is high: technically sound with supporting experiments, though validation is somewhat limited.
- Clarity is generally good but could be improved in parts; notation inconsistencies can confuse readers.
- Originality is strong: novel bridging of NMF and generative modeling with a multi-resolution extension.
- Significance is high for audio signal processing due to raw-signal modeling and adaptable TF resolution.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel generative model, solid theoretical foundation, promising empirical results, and multi-resolution benefits.
- Arguments against acceptance: MMLE scalability concerns, limited comparisons, lack of theoretical guarantees, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: strong paper that merits acceptance, with recommended revisions to address MMLE scalability and broader comparative evaluation.
- Recommendation: Accept.
