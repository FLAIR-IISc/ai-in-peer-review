- Summary: Proposes LRTFS — a time-domain generative model synthesizing signals from TF atoms with complex Gaussian coefficients whose variances have a low-rank WH structure.  
- Estimation: Presents MJLE (scalable EM with variable-splitting, analysis–synthesis step + Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF on |α|^2) and MMLE (type-II/SBL-style EM that is elegant but not scalable).  
- Multi-resolution: Extends naturally by stacking multiple frames/windows with separate low-rank structures.  
- Experiments: Demonstrates tonal/transient decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

- Technical soundness: Probabilistic formulation is coherent and links to IS-NMF, GCM, and SBL; MJLE derivation plausible though log(|α|^2) term derivation is only sketched.  
- Algorithmic correctness/convergence: MJLE monotonicity arguments rely on MM/NMF and variable-splitting, but the λ update is heuristic and full ML not performed in practice.  
- Empirical evaluation: Limited and narrow — small test sets, SNR-only metrics, modest gains over strong baselines, no statistical analysis, no runtime or ablation studies.  
- Reproducibility: Key implementation choices (β, initialization, stopping, dictionary training, hermitian handling) and λ tuning are under-specified and no code is provided.

- Clarity: Generally well organized with clear equations and algorithms, but some derivations omitted and references appear scrambled/incomplete.  
- Originality: Novel synthesis-side low-rank variance model and combination of known algorithmic ingredients; multi-resolution extension is natural and attractive.  
- Significance: Methodologically promising for waveform-level, phase-aware TF modeling, but empirical impact is currently limited.

- Pros: Principled time-domain probabilistic model that preserves phase.  
- Pros: Scalable MJLE EM avoiding large matrix inversions using TF operators.  
- Pros: Elegant multi-resolution formulation with interpretable updates.  
- Pros: Clear connections to IS-NMF/GCM/SBL and Wiener-like updates.  
- Pros: Feasibility shown with some empirical gains.

- Cons: Evaluation is limited, partly ad hoc (λ tuned/annealed) and uses only SNR on small sets.  
- Cons: No comparisons to phase-aware/complex NMF or other probabilistic TF synthesis models.  
- Cons: Missing implementation details, ablations, runtime and sensitivity analyses.  
- Cons: MMLE is unscalable and no approximate scalable alternatives are explored.  
- Cons: Scrambled/incomplete references and some derivations only sketched.

- Suggestions: Provide principled λ treatment (ML update, cross-validation, or Bayesian approach) and sensitivity analysis.  
- Suggestions: Add ablations (K, β, initialization), single- vs multi-resolution comparisons, and runtime/convergence reports.  
- Suggestions: Compare to complex/phase-aware NMF and STFT-domain probabilistic models that reconstruct waveforms.  
- Suggestions: Use perceptual/intelligibility metrics (PESQ/STOI), larger datasets, and statistical analysis.  
- Suggestions: Clarify derivations (log term, hermitian handling), fix references, and release code.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept (weak accept) — novel and well-motivated method but empirical validation and reproducibility need strengthening.
