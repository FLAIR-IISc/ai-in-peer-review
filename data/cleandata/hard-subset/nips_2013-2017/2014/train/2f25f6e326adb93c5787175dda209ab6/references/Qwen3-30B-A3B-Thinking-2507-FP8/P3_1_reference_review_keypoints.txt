- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): models raw signal via synthesis coefficients with low-rank time-frequency variance, bridging synthesis and NMF approaches.  
- Extends Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to operate on the signal directly rather than its TF transform.  
- Derives MJLE estimation and an EM algorithm; computationally feasible for audio.  
- Connects clearly to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), differing mainly by structured (low-rank) variance parameters.  
- Presents a compelling multi-resolution extension that handles components with different temporal resolutions (e.g., transients vs. tonals).  
- Experiments demonstrate practical gains (speech enhancement: 6.6 dB vs. 6 dB baseline) and music decomposition capability.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and establishes relationships to prior work (GCM, SBL).  
- Lacks sufficient theoretical analysis of the EM/MJLE algorithm—no convergence guarantees or sensitivity discussion.  
- Computational complexity analysis is incomplete; MJLE complexity not detailed or compared to alternatives.  
- Insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art deep learning audio separation methods (e.g., Conv-TasNet, DPRNN).  
- Does not fully differentiate its generative synthesis framework from prior multi-resolution/sparse synthesis methods.  
- Minor presentation issues: inconsistent notation for synthesis coefficients and unclear link between synthesis coefficients and standard STFT analysis coefficients.  
- Needs clearer explanation of why traditional NMF cannot naturally support multi-resolution representations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical analysis, complexity/comparisons (including deep learning), and clarity issues.
