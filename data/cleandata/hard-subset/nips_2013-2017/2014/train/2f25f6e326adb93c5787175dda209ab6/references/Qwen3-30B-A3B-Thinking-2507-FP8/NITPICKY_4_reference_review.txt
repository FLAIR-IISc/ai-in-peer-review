

# Review of "Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis"

This paper proposes a "Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis" (LRTFS) model that extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to model the raw signal directly rather than its STFT. The authors claim this approach bridges a gap between traditional NMF approaches and time-frequency synthesis, enabling multi-resolution representations. While the paper presents a novel framework with potential applications in audio processing, I have identified several technical inconsistencies, formatting issues, and clarity problems that undermine the paper's scientific rigor.

## Technical Inconsistencies

The paper contains several technical inconsistencies that require clarification. In Eq. (1), the authors write "yfn ∼ Nc(0, [WH]fn)" where [WH]fn is a scalar, but the circular complex normal distribution Nc(0, λ) requires λ to be a covariance matrix parameter, not a scalar. This creates confusion about the proper interpretation of the GCM.

In Section 2.2, the authors claim "p(α|v) = Nc(α|0, diag(v))" but the circular complex normal distribution should have a covariance matrix, not a diagonal matrix. The standard complex normal distribution is defined with a covariance matrix, not a diagonal matrix, which suggests a misunderstanding of the distribution.

The paper consistently uses inconsistent notation for squared magnitudes. In Eq. (11), it writes "DIS(|α|2|v)" but |α|2 should be a vector of squared magnitudes, not a matrix. In Eq. (18), it uses |α(i+1)fn|2, which is redundant as |α(i+1)fn| is already a scalar magnitude.

## Formatting and Clarity Issues

The paper contains multiple formatting inconsistencies. It introduces "Itakura-Saito (IS)" in the abstract but later uses "Itakura-Saito (IS) divergence" without consistent abbreviation usage. The paper also uses "t-f" as an abbreviation for "time-frequency" without defining it before first use.

The paper claims "WH must be low-rank such that K < F and K < N" in the footnote, but this is incorrect. When both W and H are estimated, the rank of WH is at most min(F, N, K), so K < min(F, N) is the correct constraint, not K < F and K < N. This is a critical technical error that affects the paper's foundational claim.

The paper states that "the negative log-likelihood − log p(Y|W,H) is equal, up to a constant, to the Itakura-Saito (IS) divergence DIS(S|WH)" but this is only true for the complex Gaussian model, not generally for the GCM. The paper should clarify this limitation.

## Minor Methodological Issues

The paper claims LRTFS "opens doors to multi-resolution representations, that were not possible in the traditional NMF setting." However, multi-resolution representations have been explored in wavelet-based NMF approaches (e.g., [8, 9] cited in the paper), so this claim requires qualification.

The paper references [13] as "a form of warm-restart strategy" but [13] is "Noise spectrum estimation in adverse environments: Improved minima controlled recursive averaging," which does not discuss warm-restart strategies. This citation is mismatched.

The paper states "the coefficients of the STFT can be interpreted as analysis coefficients obtained with a Gabor frame" without explaining the mathematical relationship, which is confusing for readers unfamiliar with time-frequency analysis.

## Strengths

The paper presents a well-structured extension of the GCM to model raw signals directly, which is a meaningful contribution to the time-frequency analysis literature. The multi-resolution extension in Section 4 is conceptually sound and potentially valuable for audio applications. The experimental results in Section 5 demonstrate reasonable improvements over baselines.

## Weaknesses

The technical inconsistencies described above significantly undermine the paper's credibility. As a reviewer, I find it concerning that the paper contains fundamental misunderstandings of complex normal distributions and rank constraints. The formatting inconsistencies and citation errors suggest a lack of careful proofreading. While the overall idea is promising, these issues prevent me from confidently recommending acceptance.

## Recommendation

I recommend rejection. While the paper presents an interesting idea, the technical errors, inconsistent notation, and citation issues are too significant to overlook. A revised version would need to address all these technical inconsistencies before being considered for publication. The authors should clarify the complex normal distribution, correct the rank constraint, fix the notation for squared magnitudes, and verify all citations.

The paper could be significantly improved by:
1. Correcting the fundamental misunderstanding of the complex normal distribution
2. Fixing the rank constraint statement
3. Standardizing notation for squared magnitudes
4. Clarifying the relationship between STFT coefficients and Gabor frames
5. Verifying all citations match the content they're referencing

Without these corrections, the paper's technical foundation is shaky, and the claims about LRTFS being a "step forward" from traditional NMF approaches are undermined by these basic errors.
