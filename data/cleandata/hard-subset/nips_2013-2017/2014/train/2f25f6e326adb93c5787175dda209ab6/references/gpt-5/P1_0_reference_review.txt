Summary
The paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model for raw time-domain signals that combines a synthesis representation with a low-rank latent structure on the complex synthesis coefficients. In contrast to the widely used Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and IS-NMF approaches that model analysis coefficients (e.g., STFT) or magnitudes, LRTFS directly models x(t) as a sum of time-frequency atoms with complex Gaussian coefficients whose variances follow a low-rank factorization WH. Two estimation strategies are presented: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) via an EM algorithm that alternates Wiener-like shrinkage of synthesis coefficients and IS-NMF updates on |α|^2 versus WH; and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE) in the spirit of SBL/type-II ML, which is elegant but computationally heavy due to large matrix inversions. A key advantage of the synthesis view is a natural multi-resolution extension by concatenating multiple frames (e.g., short and long windows) with independent low-rank priors, enabling morphological decompositions (transient/tonal). Experiments illustrate hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement; LRTFS yields modest but consistent SNR improvements over single-resolution IS-NMF and OMLSA.

Relation to prior work
- NMF and probabilistic NMF/GCM: Builds directly on IS-NMF and the GCM (Févotte et al.), but shifts the low-rank model from analysis coefficients to synthesis coefficients and moves from STFT-domain to time-domain generation.
- SBL/RVM: The MMLE derivation mirrors type-II ML with ARD priors, but with structured (tied) variances via WH rather than independent vm, hence not sparse but low-rank-structured.
- Hybrid/morphological decompositions: Extends classical MCA hybrids (Daudet/Torrésani; Elad et al.) from sparse priors to low-rank Gaussian priors, and provides an EM algorithm that scales to audio via fast frame operators (LTFAT).
- Phase-aware/complex NMF: Related in spirit to complex-valued models that keep phase, but differs by modeling in the synthesis domain rather than analysis; a more direct comparison to complex NMF/phase-informed NMF would clarify positioning.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A clean, generative synthesis model with a low-rank variance structure, bridging NMF and synthesis-frame modeling; multi-resolution extension is natural and elegant.
- Algorithmic contribution: Practical EM for MJLE using the Figueiredo–Nowak splitting trick avoids large matrix inversions; reduces to alternating Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF on |α|^2.
- Clarity of derivations: The connection to IS divergence and the reconstruction of latent components via per-component Wiener weights is clearly explained.
- Practical relevance: Time-domain generation with multi-resolution modeling is attractive for audio, and the experiments show consistent gains over strong baselines.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Experimental scope and rigor:
  - Limited datasets and metrics; speech enhancement reports only SNR gain on 10 examples. Consider adding SDR/SIR/SAR (BSS Eval), PESQ/STOI, confidence intervals, and more noise types.
  - For the hybrid music demo, λ is chosen by oracle (best output SNR). This undermines fairness. Provide a principled selection (e.g., CV, marginal likelihood surrogate, or a robust schedule) and report sensitivity.
  - Compare to stronger baselines: multiresolution NMF or complex/phase-aware NMF; morphological component analysis with structured sparsity; the GCM with Wiener filtering and inverse STFT; single-resolution LRTFS ablation.
- MMLE scalability: While the MMLE derivation is insightful, dismissing it as future work leaves the paper with one practical algorithm. Include approximate scalable MMLE (e.g., conjugate gradients with matrix-free ops, variational/message passing, or diagonal/low-rank approximations) or at least provide timing/memory estimates to justify the design choice.
- Interpretation of the objective: The decomposition CJL = data-fit + DIS(|α|^2|v) + log(|α|^2) is mainly algebraic; the log term cancels the −log |α|^2 inside DIS. The claim that it “favors sparse solutions of α” is misleading; the prior is Gaussian and not sparsity-inducing. Please clarify to avoid overclaiming.
- Practical details missing:
  - Initialization of W, H, α; number of iterations; runtime; convergence diagnostics; choice of β (beyond the admissibility constraint β ≤ λ/δΦ); normalization of frames and columns.
  - In the multiresolution setting, discuss potential energy leakage and interactions between layers; any regularization to avoid double counting?
- Typos/clarity: In Eqs. (29–30) Nc should include zero mean; please check notation consistency.

Quality
- Technically solid overall; EM derivations are standard and appropriately adapted. The MJLE algorithm appears correct and scalable. The MMLE section is correct but incomplete in practice. Experimental support is promising but not exhaustive; some choices (λ tuning) are ad hoc.

Clarity
- Generally clear and well organized. The relation to GCM and SBL is helpful. Minor notational issues and the misleading “sparsity” remark should be corrected. Adding pseudocode and practical guidance (β, initialization, stopping) would improve reproducibility.

Originality
- The synthesis-domain low-rank variance model and its multi-resolution extension are novel as a cohesive framework. While components build on known tools (IS-NMF, Wiener filtering, EM), the combination and implications for time-domain modeling are original.

Significance
- Potentially impactful for audio ML and signal processing; modeling in the time domain with multi-resolution is compelling. Current empirical gains are modest but consistent; broader evaluation could strengthen the case.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel and well-motivated modeling perspective (synthesis-domain low-rank).
- Practical EM algorithm that leverages fast frame operators.
- Natural and useful multi-resolution extension with demonstrated benefits.
- Clear links to established probabilistic NMF and SBL literature.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited experimental breadth and somewhat ad hoc hyperparameter handling.
- No comparison to closely related complex/phase-aware NMF or to GCM-inversion pipelines.
- MMLE remains theoretical; no scalable approximation is provided.
- Some interpretational overstatement (sparsity).

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive are results to β and λ? Can you provide a principled selection strategy?
- What are the runtimes and iteration counts for your experiments?
- How does single-resolution LRTFS compare to your multi-resolution variant, isolating the benefit of synthesis modeling versus multi-resolution?
- Can you report results on perceptual metrics for speech (PESQ/STOI) and separation metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR)?

Recommendation
Weak accept, contingent on clarifying the objective interpretation, adding practical guidance, and strengthening the empirical evaluation (or at least adding stronger baselines and ablations). The modeling idea is interesting and relevant for NeurIPS.
