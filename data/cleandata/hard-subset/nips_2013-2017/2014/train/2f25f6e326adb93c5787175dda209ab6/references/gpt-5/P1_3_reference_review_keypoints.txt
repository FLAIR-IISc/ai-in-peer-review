- Summary: Proposes LRTFS, a generative time-domain model with low-rank structure on complex synthesis coefficients whose variances follow a WH factorization; contrasts with analysis-domain NMF/GCM and handles phase implicitly.
- Methods: Derives MJLE with an efficient EM using variable-splitting and a principled but computationally heavy MMLE (type-II), plus a scalable multi-resolution EM extension.
- Relation to prior work: Bridges NMF/IS-NMF, GCM (STFT-domain), and SBL/ARD by tying Gaussian variances via WH rather than per-coefficient priors.
- Strength: Conceptual novelty in modeling low-rank structure in the synthesis domain and linking it to structured ARD/SBL.
- Strength: MJLE EM algorithm is simple, avoids large inversions, and reduces to alternating Wiener-like α updates and IS-NMF on |α|^2.
- Strength: Natural multi-resolution extension enabling transient/tonal separation while keeping scalable updates.
- Strength: Experiments show interpretable decompositions and modest, consistent SNR gains over baselines (IS-NMF, OMLSA).
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited — SNR gains are modest and key ablations (β, λ, K, dictionary size), runtime, convergence, and robustness are missing.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to phase-aware STFT probabilistic models, multi-window NMF, and recent structured SBL/group-ARD baselines, making benefits of synthesis-domain unclear.
- Weakness: MMLE is impractical at audio scale and no approximate large-scale solver is experimentally provided.
- Weakness: Some derivations and implementation details are under-specified (origin of log(|α|^2) term, β/λ schedules, Hermitian symmetry handling, initialization, stopping, normalization).
- Weakness: Hyperparameter handling is weak — λ estimation fails in practice and principled selection/impact is not discussed.
- Quality assessment: Model and MJLE derivations are sound; MMLE is standard but currently impractical; experiments support feasibility but lack depth.
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written but would benefit from a derivation sketch, algorithm pseudocode, and more practical parameter details.
- Originality assessment: Novel combination of synthesis-domain low-rank variance modeling and EM solver; multi-resolution perspective is fresh.
- Significance assessment: Potentially impactful for TF-structured signals, but current moderate empirical gains limit immediate impact.
- Key suggestions: Provide MJLE derivation sketch, analyze β/λ sensitivity and convergence, compare to GCM and multi-window NMF, report computational scaling and runtimes, and study initialization/ablations.
- Acceptance pros: Principled modeling bridge between NMF/GCM/SBL, simple efficient algorithm, and useful multi-resolution extension with consistent improvements.
- Acceptance cons: Limited empirical evaluation and small gains, MMLE not realized at scale, and reproducibility/clarity gaps.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — neat, potentially influential idea needing stronger empirical validation and practical detailing.
