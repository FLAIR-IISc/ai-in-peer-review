- Introduces LRTFS, a generative model that directly models raw audio by imposing a low-rank variance structure (α ~ Nc(0, WH)) on synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-resolution time-frequency modeling.  
- Differs from prior work (NMF, GCM) by modeling the raw signal and from SBL by using structured rather than unstructured variances.  
- Theoretical contribution: clear probabilistic formulation with derived EM algorithms for MJLE and MMLE.  
- Multi-resolution capability: naturally represents components with different temporal resolutions (tonal vs transient) without complex estimation changes.  
- Experimental validation: music decomposition and speech enhancement experiments show improvements, notably a 6.6 dB SNR gain in speech enhancement.  
- Presentation: paper is very clear, well-written, and situates itself well relative to prior work.  
- Lacks comparison to modern deep learning baselines (e.g., Conv-TasNet), making reported gains appear modest in context.  
- Computational scalability concerns: MMLE is infeasible for large signals and no practical acceleration strategies are proposed.  
- Ambiguity in claiming multi-resolution advantage over traditional NMF—needs stronger justification versus multi-STFT NMF approaches.  
- Missing parameter sensitivity analysis for hyperparameter λ.  
- Theoretical gaps: no convergence guarantees for the EM algorithm and no discussion of sample complexity.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and compelling for audio processing but less positioned within current ML/DL trends.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add at least one deep-learning comparison, clarify LRTFS advantage over multi-resolution NMF, include λ sensitivity analysis, and discuss computational optimizations.
