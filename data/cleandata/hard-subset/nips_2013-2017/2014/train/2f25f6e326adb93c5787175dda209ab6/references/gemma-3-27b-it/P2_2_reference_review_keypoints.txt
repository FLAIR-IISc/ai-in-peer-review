- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model that directly models raw signal STFT coefficients via low-rank variance factorization.  
- Proposes MJLE and MMLE parameter estimation using EM algorithms; MMLE requires costly matrix inversions.  
- Supports multi-resolution time-frequency representations adaptable to signal characteristics.  
- Demonstrates promising results on music decomposition and speech enhancement experiments.  
- Positions the work within NMF/GCM literature and explicitly connects to SBL, highlighting tied-variance differences.  
- References and relation to prior work (morphological component analysis, hybrid TF representations) are comprehensive.  
- Theoretical derivation and EM algorithm presentation are sound and clearly explained.  
- MMLE scalability is a major limitation for large datasets and practical use.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope (two simple applications/datasets) and needs broader testing.  
- Method shows sensitivity to hyperparameter λ and relies on careful tuning.  
- The λ update lacks a theoretical EM-derived justification and is only empirically shown to decrease the objective.  
- Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art (notably deep learning) in speech enhancement.  
- Writing quality and mathematical notation are clear and consistent.  
- Authors acknowledge computational limitations and the need for more scalable algorithms.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, including justifying λ update, expanding experiments/comparisons, and automating λ selection.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
