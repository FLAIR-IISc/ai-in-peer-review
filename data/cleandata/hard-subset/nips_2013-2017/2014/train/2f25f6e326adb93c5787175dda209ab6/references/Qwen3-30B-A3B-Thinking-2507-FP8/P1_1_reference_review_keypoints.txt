- Introduces LRTFS: a synthesis-based model that directly models raw time-domain signals with low-rank time-frequency variance, enabling multi-resolution representations.
- Proposes two estimation methods: MJLE (efficient, avoids large matrix inversions) and MMLE (more costly, less practical as presented).
- Shifts the field from analysis-based (STFT magnitude/NMF) to synthesis-based modeling, building on NMF and sparse Bayesian learning.

- Strength: Significant conceptual contribution by modeling raw signals and enabling multi-resolution TF representations.
- Strength: Practical MJLE implementation that scales to audio and uses standard TF analysis/synthesis operations.
- Strength: Strong experimental validation—improvements in music decomposition and ~6.6 dB average SNR gain in speech enhancement.
- Strength: Multi-resolution capability effectively separates transient and tonal components.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis—no convergence or optimality guarantees for the EM algorithm.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity—λ treated as tuned rather than estimated, with no detailed search or sensitivity study.
- Weakness: Limited experimental comparison—omits recent deep learning speech-enhancement baselines.
- Weakness: Incomplete treatment of MMLE—dismissed without thorough empirical or theoretical comparison to MJLE.

- Recommendation: Accept—conceptual advance and effective MJLE-backed experiments outweigh the noted weaknesses.

- Suggestion: Add convergence analysis or empirical evidence that MJLE reaches good local minima.
- Suggestion: Provide details on λ selection and sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.
- Suggestion: Include comparisons with recent neural-network-based speech enhancement methods.
- Suggestion: Expand discussion and empirical comparison of MMLE vs. MJLE across scenarios.

- Final assessment scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10; overall recommendation: Accept.
