- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), modeling the raw signal as a linear combination of time-frequency atoms with low-rank variance structure.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by moving from STFT variance modeling to direct signal synthesis.  
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL) but replaces independent variances with a low-rank constraint.  
- Proposes a unified probabilistic multi-resolution framework allowing different time-frequency bases for different components.  
- Mathematical formulations are sound with clear derivations of the generative model and two estimation approaches (MJLE and MMLE).  
- Provides detailed EM algorithms with explicit update rules and theoretical links to existing work.  
- Experiments show effective music decomposition (tonal vs transient) and speech enhancement with 6.6 dB SNR improvement versus IS-NMF (6 dB) and OMLSA (4.6 dB).  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: MMLE scalability issues for large audio and treating λ as a hyperparameter.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with accessible explanations despite mathematical complexity.  
- Novelty: meaningful conceptual advance over prior TF modeling and particularly novel multi-resolution extension.  
- Significance: preserves phase information and enables natural decomposition by resolution, with practical audio processing benefits.  
- Strengths: clear conceptual advance, solid theory, practical multi-resolution capability, strong experiments, excellent writing.  
- Weaknesses: lacks comparison to state-of-the-art deep learning audio methods.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient detail on MMLE computational complexity (matrix inversion issues) and possible solutions.  
- Weaknesses: experimental section could include more analysis and visualizations of decompositions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — high-quality, novel, technically sound, and practically useful contribution.
