- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that represents raw signals as sums of complex atoms with low-rank-structured coefficients via matrix factorization.  
- Proposes two estimation methods: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) with EM and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE), and extends the model to multi-resolution representations.  
- Demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement with competitive SNR improvements versus IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Builds on and connects to prior work: NMF, the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), hybrid representations, and morphological component analysis.  
- Strength: Provides a true generative model for raw signals, addressing limitations of STFT-focused methods.  
- Strength: Naturally supports multi-resolution time-frequency representations suitable for real-world audio.  
- Strength: Clear mathematical formulation and well-presented EM estimation algorithms.  
- Strength: Promising experimental results that show potential in practical audio tasks.  
- Strength: Thoughtful discussion of relations to established methods (e.g., SBL).  
- Weakness: MMLE is not scalable, limiting applicability to large-scale audio.  
- Weakness: Performance sensitive to the regularization parameter (λ) with no automatic selection strategy discussed.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and lacks comparisons to more advanced state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis (convergence guarantees, consistency) of estimators.  
- Weakness: Implementation complexity (multiple steps, MJLE with NMF updates) and limited discussion of computational considerations.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound generative model with promising results and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability issues (MMLE), limited theory, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address scalability, expand experiments, and add discussion on computational complexity.
