- Summary: Proposes LRTFS, a time-domain generative model with synthesis coefficients whose variances follow a low-rank WH factorization; provides MJLE (EM with Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF) and MMLE (ARD/SBL-style) estimation and a multi-resolution extension; experiments show modest gains.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends probabilistic IS-NMF/GCM by modeling synthesis coefficients in time, relates to SBL/ARD and MCA/hybrid representations, and needs clearer comparison to complex/phase-aware NMF.  
- Strengths: Clear modeling novelty tying low-rank variance to synthesis coefficients, practical MJLE algorithm, correct MMLE derivations, natural multi-resolution extension, and consistent qualitative empirical improvements.  
- Weaknesses: Limited and partly post-hoc empirical validation, small/narrow evaluations and metrics, MMLE scalability not addressed, missing practical implementation details, and potentially misleading wording about the log(|α|2) term.  
- Quality: Theoretical derivations and MJLE algorithm are sound and avoid large matrix inversions; MMLE equations are standard but lack scalable solvers; empirical support is suggestive but not comprehensive.  
- Clarity: Well written and organized with consistent notation, but missing critical practical details (initialization, normalization, stopping criteria, runtime) and needs to clarify the interpretation of the log(|α|2) term.  
- Originality: Novel shift of low-rank structure to synthesis coefficients in a time-domain model and an interesting structured-ARD connection; algorithm combines known pieces in a new setting.  
- Significance: Promising for phase-preserving, time-domain and multi-resolution audio modeling, but practical impact is limited by modest empirical gains and narrow evaluation.  
- Key suggestions: Report initialization/normalization/stopping rules and runtimes; clarify β–λ scheduling; compare to complex/phase-aware NMF and multi-resolution sparse MCA; add ablations (single vs multi-res, K, λ sensitivity, MJLE vs GCM); explore scalable MMLE approximations (CG, Krylov, message passing).  
- Pros (concise): Principled time-domain low-rank model, practical MJLE, SBL conceptual link, and intuitive decompositions.  
- Cons (concise): Sparse/heuristic experiments and tuning, missing comparisons to related methods, MMLE not scalable, and reproducibility details omitted.  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid and interesting modeling contribution with a practical algorithm, but empirical evaluation and practical details need strengthening; currently borderline for acceptance.
