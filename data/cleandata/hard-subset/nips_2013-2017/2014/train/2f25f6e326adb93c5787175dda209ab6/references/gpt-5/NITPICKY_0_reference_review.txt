Summary
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model of the raw time-domain signal x(t) whose synthesis coefficients α over a time-frequency (t-f) frame follow a circular complex Gaussian prior with low-rank variance tied by WH, akin to IS-NMF on |α|^2. The authors develop two estimation strategies: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) via an EM algorithm that avoids large matrix inversions, using a Figueiredo–Nowak-style auxiliary variable z and an IS-NMF M-step on |α|^2; (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE, type-II ML), essentially SBL with a structured prior, but deemed computationally intractable for large T, M. A multi-resolution extension stacks multiple frames Φℓ and runs the same MJLE-EM. Experiments on music hybrid decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest SNR gains over single-resolution IS-NMF and OMLSA.

Relation to prior work
The model bridges GCM [Fevotte et al., 2011]—a generative model on STFT analysis coefficients—with synthesis modeling and connects to SBL [Tipping; Wipf & Rao] by tying the variances via WH instead of independent vm. The multi-resolution construction relates to MCA/hybrid sparse models [Daudet & Torresani; Kowalski & Torresani]. The link between MJLE updates and Wiener filtering is also consistent with classic Gaussian models.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: moving the low-rank prior from STFT analysis coefficients (GCM) to synthesis coefficients, hence modeling x(t) directly and accommodating multiple frames naturally.
- Pragmatic EM algorithm for MJLE avoiding matrix inversions; use of fast Φ, Φ* (e.g., LTFAT) is sensible.
- Multi-resolution extension is straightforward and computationally feasible.
- Clear link to Wiener filtering for component reconstruction; simple shrinkage form α ← v/(v+β) z.

Weaknesses and detailed comments
Technical correctness and derivations
- Objective function (Eq. 11) appears to omit the T log λ term from the likelihood −log p(x|α,λ). Since λ is estimated (Eq. 19), dropping T log λ is not a constant and is inconsistent with claims about likelihood decrease.
- The statement “p(z|x,θJL) = p(z|x,λ)” contradicts Eq. (16), which depends on α(i). The posterior of z given x under the two-stage model (12)-(13) depends on α; please correct the derivation and wording.
- In Sec. 2.1/2.2, Nc is defined with scalar variance, but later multivariate Nc(·|0,Σ) is used. Please formalize the multivariate complex Gaussian notation early. Similarly, e2 in Eq. (13) has a non-scalar covariance (λI − βΦΦ*); ensure consistency with your distributional definitions.
- β selection is unspecified. The EM requires β ≤ λ/δΦ; how is β chosen, updated, and how is δΦ estimated in practice? Without this, the algorithm is under-specified.
- Eq. (29)-(30): Nc([WℓHℓ]fn) is missing the mean and variance arguments; presumably Nc(0, [WℓHℓ]fn). This occurs repeatedly.
- Eq. (31): λ should likely be λ(i). Eq. (32): index mismatch (z(i)fn vs z(i)`,fn).
- Eq. (18): NMF subproblem is under-determined up to scaling; do you normalize W or H each iteration?
- The claim that “log(|α|^2) favors sparsity” is plausible (log-sum penalty), but no analysis is provided. A short justification or reference would help.

Modeling and identifiability
- Scale indeterminacy between α and v=WH (and within W/H) is not discussed. Without constraints/priors or normalization, identifiability is unclear.
- Real-signal handling via Hermitian symmetry is handwavy; how is it enforced in the implementation?
- Coherence in multi-frame unions can be large; any regularization to prevent component swapping or degeneracy?

Algorithmic details and reproducibility
- Initialization (α, W, H), stopping criteria, number of iterations, and runtimes are not reported. For large-scale audio, these are critical.
- In MJLE, the λ update (Eq. 19) is “not exact EM” but “decreases the negative log-likelihood.” Given the omission of T log λ in Eq. (11), this claim needs to be reconciled.
- MMLE is dismissed as intractable, but small-scale demonstrations could validate its behavior and contrast with MJLE.

Experimental design
- λ is treated as a tuned hyperparameter chosen for best output SNR (Sec. 5), which risks optimistic bias. Please fix λ a priori, use validation, or report sensitivity.
- Baselines: Comparing to single-resolution IS-NMF is insufficient. Stronger baselines include:
  - GCM [3] (closest probabilistic competitor).
  - Multi-resolution NMF or multi-window factorization baselines (it is not accurate to claim this is “not possible” in traditional NMF; multiple resolutions can be concatenated and co-factorized).
  - Sparse MCA-style synthesis with group penalties (e.g., [8,9]).
- Speech enhancement: Only 10 mixtures; no confidence intervals; no STOI/PESQ/SI-SDR; unclear SNR setup; applause noise modeled with K=2 per layer seems too low-capacity and possibly biased. The annealing of λ “to negligible” is ad hoc and not justified theoretically.
- No ablations (effect of β, K, window lengths, λ schedule, single vs multi-resolution, component count).

Writing, notation, and formatting
- Numerous typos and grammar: “mentionned,” “hit-hat” (hi-hat), “applauses,” “other the 10 test signals,” etc.
- Notation ambiguities: log(|α|^2) should denote Σm log(|αm|^2); please state explicitly. Also define all dimensions when stacking multi-resolution Φ and α.
- References are incomplete (missing years/venues/pages), inconsistent formatting, and some journal names are misspelled (e.g., “Optimisation”). Please bring references to NeurIPS standards.

Quality: The core idea and EM for MJLE are plausible, but derivational inconsistencies (omitted T log λ, posterior dependence on α) and missing algorithmic details reduce confidence. Experiments show modest gains but are not rigorous enough for strong claims.

Clarity: Generally readable, but many notational slips and missing details harm reproducibility.

Originality: Moving low-rank structure to synthesis coefficients and the multi-resolution EM are interesting, though connections to existing multi-resolution NMF/MCA should be more carefully positioned.

Significance: Potentially useful for audio, but current empirical evidence is limited and comparisons are insufficient.

Pros
- Clear, appealing modeling idea; practical EM with fast operators.
- Natural multi-resolution extension.
- Connections to GCM/SBL clarified; Wiener-style component reconstruction.

Cons
- Derivational gaps and specification issues (λ term, β choice, posterior statement).
- Under-specified algorithm; identifiability and normalization not addressed.
- Weak/limited experimental validation; limited baselines; tuning on test.
- Many typos and incomplete references.

Recommendation: Reject (in current form). The idea is promising, but the accumulation of methodological, derivational, and experimental shortcomings, along with numerous presentation issues, makes it unsuitable for NeurIPS at this time.

Suggestions for revision
- Fix all derivations (include T log λ; correct posterior statements), fully specify β, initialization, normalization, and stopping.
- Provide rigorous baselines (GCM, multi-resolution NMF/MCA), ablations, larger-scale evaluation with standard metrics, and statistical reporting.
- Tighten notation and writing; correct equations and indexing; complete and standardize references.
- Discuss identifiability and impose/describe appropriate constraints. Provide code or pseudocode.
