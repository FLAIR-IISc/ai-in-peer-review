- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that directly models complex STFT coefficients with low-rank variances.
- Proposes MJLE and MMLE estimation approaches with EM algorithms for parameter estimation.
- Extends prior Gaussian Composite Models and relates to sparse Bayesian learning, hybrid audio representations, and semi-supervised source separation.
- Provides a clear positioning within matrix factorization methods in ML and signal processing.
- Main strength: enables a true generative model for raw signals, overcoming magnitude-only NMF limitations.
- Supports multi-resolution representations, useful for signals with varying time scales (transients vs. sustained tones).
- Strong theoretical foundation with derivations of the model and EM algorithms and insightful connection to SBL.
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement show promising, competitive results.
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and mathematically clear.
- Scalability issues with MMLE limit applicability to large-scale problems.
- Experiments show sensitivity to hyperparameter λ, requiring careful tuning.
- Comparative analysis is limited; more comparisons to state-of-the-art methods are needed.
- Lacks detailed ablation studies to isolate contributions of model components (low-rank, multi-resolution).
- Experimental results lack error bars or statistical significance testing for reported SNR improvements.
- Quality rated 8/10, clarity 8.5/10, originality 8/10, significance 7.5/10 — overall positive but with reservations.
- Pros: novel generative model, flexible multi-resolution framework, promising application results, clear presentation.
- Cons: MMLE scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited comparisons, missing ablations and statistical tests.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, theoretically grounded contribution with practical limitations; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions including more comparisons, ablation studies, statistical tests, and discussion of MMLE scalability improvements.
