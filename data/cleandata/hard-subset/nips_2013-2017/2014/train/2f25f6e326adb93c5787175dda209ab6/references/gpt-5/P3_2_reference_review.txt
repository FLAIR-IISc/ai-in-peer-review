Summary
The paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative probabilistic model that moves the low-rank modeling from the STFT analysis domain to the time-domain synthesis coefficients. The signal is synthesized as x = Φα + e, with complex synthesis coefficients αfn drawn from a zero-mean circular Gaussian with variance constrained to have a low-rank structure v = WH (Itakura–Saito NMF prior). Two estimation strategies are presented: (i) maximum joint likelihood (MJLE) with an EM algorithm leveraging a Figueiredo–Nowak auxiliary variable to avoid large matrix inversions, yielding alternating “Wiener-shrinkage” updates for α and standard IS-NMF updates for W,H; and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood (MMLE) in the spirit of SBL/type-II ML, which is elegant but computationally prohibitive at audio scale. The framework naturally extends to multi-resolution by stacking several frames (e.g., short and long Gabor), with separate low-rank priors per layer. Experiments on music hybrid decomposition (tonal/transient) and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines.

Relation to prior work
- NMF/PLCA on magnitude or power spectrograms is standard; modeling complex STFT as zero-mean complex Gaussian with IS divergence (GCM) is known. LRTFS departs by modeling synthesis coefficients in the time domain with the same low-rank variance structure.
- The MMLE connection to SBL (RVM) is clear; LRTFS ties per-coefficient variances through WH rather than independent vm, trading sparsity for low-rank structure.
- Multi-resolution modeling relates to morphological component analysis (MCA) and mixed-norm models, but with a probabilistic low-rank prior rather than sparsity-only.
- The EM scheme via auxiliary variable is in the spirit of Figueiredo–Nowak denoising.

Quality
- The modeling is sound. The derivation of the MJLE objective shows a DIS(|α|^2|v) term and a natural log(|α|^2) term that acts as a sparsity-like penalty on α; this is insightful and correct (reparameterization of the Gaussian prior).
- The MJLE EM updates are coherent: E-step computes a pre-denoised z via analysis/synthesis, M-step performs Wiener-like shrinkage for α and IS-NMF on |α|^2. Complexity is practical since it uses only Φ and Φ*.
- The MMLE algorithm is well-motivated but intractable for long signals due to matrix inversions; the paper is honest about this.
- Experimental validation is limited. The music example is qualitative; the speech enhancement shows a 0.6 dB average SNR improvement over an IS-NMF baseline and 2 dB over OMLSA. There is no statistical analysis, runtime, or ablation to isolate the Contribution of (i) time-domain synthesis vs analysis-domain GCM; (ii) multi-resolution vs single resolution; or (iii) the effect of the log(|α|^2) term.
- A methodological concern: in Sec. 5.1, λ is tuned to maximize output SNR, which uses ground-truth and biases evaluation; in 5.2 λ is annealed to near zero, making the residual negligible—this departs from the generative model and may risk overfitting.

Clarity
- Overall clear and well-structured. The connection to SBL and to Wiener filtering is nicely explained, and the multi-resolution extension is straightforward to follow.
- Some notational slips (e.g., missing zero mean in Eqs. (29)-(30) where Nc(0, ·) is intended), typos, and many references are incomplete (missing years/venues). A brief discussion of how β is chosen (β ≤ λ/δΦ) in practice would help reproducibility.
- The component reconstruction strategy (Eq. 9) is a sensible practical workaround to the intractable MMSE but should be discussed in terms of bias.

Originality
- Modeling low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients (as opposed to analysis coefficients) and combining it with an efficient EM scheme appears novel. The induced log(|α|^2) penalty in MJLE is a nice byproduct with conceptual appeal.
- Multi-resolution probabilistic low-rank synthesis with separate WH per frame is a natural but nontrivial generalization of prior MCA-style models.

Significance
- If scalable and robust, LRTFS could impact audio modeling by enabling multiresolution, phase-aware generative modeling at the signal level, and extensions to multichannel inverse problems. However, the experimental evidence is currently modest and does not clearly demonstrate advantages over well-tuned GCM/IS-NMF or modern complex NMF variants.
- The lack of a scalable MMLE implementation limits broader impact; demonstrating MJLE’s clear superiority over GCM in standard tasks would strengthen significance.

Pros
- Conceptually clean generative model at the signal level; unifies Wiener filtering and IS-NMF within an EM scheme.
- Efficient MJLE algorithm with only Φ/Φ* calls; easy to implement with STFT toolboxes.
- Multi-resolution extension is elegant and practical.
- Honest discussion of computational limitations for MMLE.

Cons
- Experimental validation is limited; improvements over baselines are small and not rigorously analyzed.
- λ selection is ad hoc (and tuned with oracle information in Sec. 5.1), undermining the generative story.
- No ablations to isolate the benefits of synthesis-domain modeling and multi-resolution.
- Missing comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., complex NMF with phase modeling, GCM with multiresolution features, MCA-based methods for hybrid decomposition).
- Incomplete references and minor notational issues.

Questions for authors
1) How sensitive are results to β and λ? Can you provide a principled selection strategy (e.g., evidence maximization for λ, adaptive β scheduling)?
2) Can you compare against GCM on the same multi-resolution front-end to isolate the benefit of moving to synthesis coefficients?
3) Do you observe monotone decrease of the joint objective with your updates in practice? Please include convergence plots and runtime.
4) How do you initialize W,H, and α? How robust are results to initialization?

Suggestions
- Add ablations: single vs multi-resolution; MJLE vs GCM; with/without log(|α|^2).
- Report SDR/SIR/SAR for source separation and statistical significance across more test cases.
- Include runtime and complexity analysis relative to IS-NMF.
- Complete and update references; fix typos and clarify Eq. (29)-(30).

Reproducibility
- Algorithms are sufficiently specified to implement, given Φ/Φ* availability. Missing practical details (β/λ choices, initialization) hinder exact reproduction.
