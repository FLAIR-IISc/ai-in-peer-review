- Introduces LRTFS: a generative model that directly models raw audio via time-frequency synthesis coefficients with low-rank variance structure.  
- Key innovation: low-rank structure on synthesis (not analysis) coefficients enables natural multi-resolution representations.  
- Develops two estimation methods: computationally efficient MJLE (no matrix inversions) and theoretically appealing but intractable MMLE.  
- EM algorithms are derived with monotonic decrease of negative log-likelihood but no global-optimum or local-minima analysis.  
- Technical foundation is sound and implementation details are clear and practical for audio.  
- Experimental evaluation on music decomposition and speech enhancement shows modest gains (e.g., 6.6 dB vs. 6 dB).  
- Limited theoretical analysis: lacks guarantees beyond EM monotonicity and no study of local minima or convergence rates.  
- Experiments are limited in scope: two applications, little sensitivity analysis, and no failure-case exploration.  
- Insufficient comparison with recent deep generative audio models (e.g., WaveNet, WaveGAN) and broader ML literature.  
- Underdeveloped discussion on how LRTFS differs from SBL beyond imposing low-rank variances.  
- Writing and organization are excellent; notation and presentation are precise and supplemented with sound examples.  
- Minor clarity gap: paper does not deeply explain why modeling synthesis coefficients is superior to analysis coefficients.  
- Strengths: novelty in raw-signal low-rank TF modeling, multi-resolution capability, practical MJLE algorithm, clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: limited theory, modest empirical improvements, insufficient comparisons to deep learning baselines, and incomplete SBL relation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, including deeper DL comparisons, more theoretical analysis of MJLE, discussion of limitations/failure cases, and clarification of SBL differences.
