- Proposes LRTFS: a generative probabilistic model synthesizing x(t)=Σ φfn(t) αfn with complex Gaussian αfn whose variances follow a low-rank WH structure and Gaussian residual noise.  
- Bridges deterministic NMF on magnitudes and the GCM on complex STFT by imposing low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients, yielding a full waveform generative model.  
- Develops MJLE EM using Figueiredo–Nowak variable splitting that alternates Wiener-like shrinkage of α and IS-NMF on |α|^2; derives MMLE (type-II) but it is currently not scalable.  
- Introduces a multi-resolution extension to handle multiple time-frequency frames at different time scales.  
- Model specification and MJLE derivation are technically sound; EM steps are consistent and monotonicity is plausible, though the λ update is approximate.  
- MJLE is scalable (no large matrix inversions; uses efficient analysis/synthesis transforms); MMLE is principled but computationally prohibitive for audio-scale data.  
- Experiments demonstrate feasibility and modest improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA on music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small test set, single metric (SNR), oracle tuning of λ, no ablations, and no runtime/convergence plots.  
- Situates well in prior work: connects to IS-NMF, GCM, SBL, and MCA; novelty lies in low-rank Gaussian prior on synthesis variances and an efficient EM avoiding large inversions.  
- Multi-resolution instantiation is unsurprising in spirit, but embedding low-rank variance in a probabilistic synthesis model with a practical EM is novel.  
- Potential significance for audio and 1D signal modeling by modeling waveforms directly with low-rank TF structure, though demonstrated benefits are modest.  
- Pros: principled waveform generative model, scalable and interpretable MJLE algorithm, multi-resolution capability, and solid theoretical connections.  
- Cons: limited and partially oracle-tuned empirical validation, lack of comparisons to complex/phase-aware baselines and multi-window NMF, missing ablations and diagnostics, fragile λ estimation, and insufficient reproducibility details.  
- Requests: report β scheduling and sensitivity, clarify initialization/normalization of α, W, H, and provide stopping criteria and implementation details.  
- Requests: provide runtime, iteration counts, convergence plots, and scalability analysis with respect to K and number of frames.  
- Suggested comparisons: complex NMF/phase-constrained NMF variants, multi-window NMF on STFT magnitudes, and GCM with multi-resolution front-ends.  
- Suggested evaluations: standard speech metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ, STOI), larger test sets, avoid oracle tuning of λ, and run ablations for resolution, K, β, λ, and dictionary choices.  
- Suggestion: include a small-scale MMLE experiment to illustrate potential benefits beyond MJLE.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept (leaning weak accept); interesting modeling and scalable EM but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer implementation details.
