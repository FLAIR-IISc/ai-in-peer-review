- Novelty: Introduces LRTFS, a new generative model that models synthesis coefficients with a low-rank structure rather than analysis/STFT coefficients.  
- Generative model: Provides a model for raw signal x(t) enabling direct synthesis and multi-resolution representations.  
- Multi-resolution: Supports multiple time-frequency bases to better represent transients and sustained tones.  
- Theoretical methods: Derives MJLE and MMLE estimation approaches with associated EM algorithms.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on NMF/GCM and links to SBL, highlighting differences in variance structure and synthesis focus.  
- Experimental validation: Shows promising results on music decomposition and speech enhancement, though experiments are limited.  
- Writing quality: Paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Computational complexity: MMLE is not scalable to practical signal dimensions; MJLE is feasible but may not scale to very long signals.  
- Limited experiments: Evaluations use small datasets and few hyperparameter settings, limiting generality of conclusions.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance depends on regularization parameter λ, which was not estimated automatically.  
- Baseline comparisons: Lacks comparisons to stronger, state-of-the-art speech enhancement baselines beyond OMLSA.  
- Theoretical analysis gap: Needs deeper analysis of EM convergence properties and parameter effects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound generative model with multi-resolution advantages and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical applicability limited by MMLE complexity, narrow experiments, and hyperparameter issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Significant contribution; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand experimental evaluation, compare to stronger baselines, discuss MJLE vs MMLE computational trade-offs and scalability, and detail/sensitivity-analyze hyperparameter (λ) selection.
