- Proposes LRTFS: a time-domain generative model with synthesis TF coefficients αfn having variances constrained by low-rank WH, contrasting with analysis-domain GCM/NMF.  
- Presents two estimation strategies: MJLE via an EM algorithm (scalable, avoids matrix inversions) and MMLE (principled but not scalable in current implementation).  
- MJLE EM cleverly links Wiener-like shrinkage of α with IS-NMF updates on |α|^2 using Figueiredo–Nowak splitting.  
- Framework naturally extends to multi-resolution by stacking frames, suiting tonal/transient audio structure.  
- Experiments show a hybrid tonal/transient decomposition and modest gains in semi-supervised speech enhancement over IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines.  
- Conceptual novelty: moves low-rank modeling from STFT analysis to synthesis coefficients in a time-domain model, bridging synthesis modeling and NMF/GCM literature.  
- Algorithmic strength: practical, computationally efficient MJLE exploiting fast Φ/Φ* operations and avoiding large matrix inversions.  
- Derivations and connections to SBL/IS-NMF/Wiener filtering are clear and traceable.  
- Experimental validation is limited and partially unfair: λ tuned on test instance for first experiment and baseline comparisons are unclear.  
- Baseline comparisons lack fairness: supervised/semi-supervised IS-NMF, complex/GCM baselines, and multiresolution NMF baselines are missing.  
- Metrics are insufficient: only SNR reported; standard speech metrics (STOI, PESQ) and statistical significance are absent.  
- Reported gains (≈0.6 dB over IS-NMF) are modest and lack deeper analysis.  
- Related work coverage is thin: phase-aware/complex NMF, multiresolution NMF, and convolutional/time-domain sparse coding literature are under-discussed.  
- Algorithmic details under-specified: λ update in MJLE is not true EM, β–λ constraint handling and monotonicity guarantees are unclear.  
- Scalability issue: MMLE is impractical at scale and no approximate scalable alternatives are attempted.  
- Model concerns: identifiability and cross-layer competition in multi-resolution stacking are not addressed; redundancy across layers may occur.  
- Sensitivity analyses are missing: effects of K, initialization, β, λ, and ablation single- vs multi-resolution are not studied.  
- Hermitian constraint for real signals is mentioned but not integrated in inference or complexity/accuracy discussion.  
- Paper is technically sound overall, with correct MJLE derivations and solid conceptual framing.  
- Clarity is generally good, though some notational/typographical issues and missing explicit parameter choices remain.  
- Originality is moderate: novel synthesis-domain low-rank variance modeling with efficient EM, but proximity to complex NMF means clearer positioning would help.  
- Significance is promising for audio tasks where phase matters, but current empirical evidence is insufficient to claim broad impact.  
- Suggestions: strengthen experiments with fair supervised baselines, add STOI/PESQ and significance tests, provide sensitivity/ablation studies, clarify λ/β handling, and discuss identifiability and related work more fully.  
- Recommendation: solid methodological contribution but empirical weaknesses and under-specified algorithmic details warrant rejection in current form, with clear paths for improvement.
