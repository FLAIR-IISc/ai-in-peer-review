- Proposes LRTFS: move low-rank modeling to time-domain synthesis coefficients α with x = Φα + e and variance v = WH (IS-NMF prior).  
- Two estimators: MJLE via EM (practical, uses auxiliary variable, yields Wiener-shrinkage + IS-NMF updates) and MMLE (SBL/type-II style, elegant but computationally prohibitive).  
- Multi-resolution extension by stacking frames/layers with separate low-rank WH per layer.  
- Experiments: qualitative music decomposition; speech enhancement gives modest gains (~0.6 dB vs IS-NMF, ~2 dB vs OMLSA) but limited analysis.  
- Relation to prior work: departs from STFT analysis-domain GCM by modeling synthesis coefficients; connects to SBL/RVM and MCA, trading sparsity for low-rank variance tying.  
- MJLE derivation is insightful: DIS(|α|^2|v) plus log(|α|^2) acts like a sparsity penalty; EM updates are coherent and practical using only Φ and Φ*.  
- MMLE is well-motivated but intractable for long signals due to large matrix inversions; authors are transparent about this.  
- Experimental validation is limited: no statistical tests, runtime, or ablation studies to isolate effects of synthesis-domain modeling, multi-resolution, or the log(|α|^2) term.  
- Methodological concern: λ is tuned on ground truth in Sec.5.1 and annealed to near zero in 5.2, biasing evaluation and deviating from the generative model.  
- Clarity: overall clear and well-structured, with nice SBL/Wiener explanations; some notational slips, typos, and incomplete references.  
- Practical detail missing: need guidance on choosing β (β ≤ λ/δΦ) for reproducibility.  
- Component reconstruction via Eq.(9) is a sensible workaround but should be discussed regarding bias.  
- Originality: novel to model low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients and to provide a multi-resolution probabilistic synthesis formulation; log(|α|^2) penalty is a nice byproduct.  
- Significance: could impact audio modeling if scalable and robust, but current evidence is modest and lacks clear superiority over tuned GCM/IS-NMF or modern complex NMF variants.  
- Pros: conceptually clean signal-level generative model, efficient MJLE needing only Φ/Φ*, practical multi-resolution extension, honest about MMLE limits.  
- Cons: limited experiments, ad hoc/oracle λ selection, no ablations, missing comparisons to stronger baselines, incomplete references and minor notation issues.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity and principled selection of β and λ; comparison to GCM with same multi-resolution front-end; convergence plots and runtime; initialization strategy and robustness.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (single vs multi-resolution, MJLE vs GCM, with/without log term), report SDR/SIR/SAR and statistical significance, include runtime/complexity analysis, fix references/typos and clarify Eq.(29)-(30).  
- Reproducibility: algorithms are specified but missing practical details (β/λ choices, initialization) hinder exact reproduction.  
- Overall score: 6 (weak accept); reviewer confidence: 0.8.
