- Introduces LRTFS, a generative model that places low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients, enabling signal reconstruction and multi-resolution representations.  
- Builds on NMF and GCM, extending low-rank time-frequency modeling from STFT coefficients to synthesis-domain variances.  
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL), highlighting a key distinction in variance structure and providing theoretical grounding.  
- Presents two estimation approaches: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE via EM) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- Demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement with competitive SNR gains.  
- Strength: provides a truly generative framework for raw signals beyond spectral-only methods.  
- Strength: naturally supports multi-resolution analysis, useful for signals with varying temporal characteristics.  
- Strength: solid theoretical justification and clear EM derivation linked to SBL.  
- Strength: empirical results show potential and the paper is clearly presented.  
- Weakness: MMLE suffers from scalability and high computational complexity, limiting practical use.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art and narrow noise/dataset coverage.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the regularization parameter λ with no principled selection method provided.  
- Weakness: lacks formal convergence analysis for the EM algorithm and risks local optima.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relations to wavelet-based decomposition methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand experiments, explore robust λ selection, discuss scalability improvements for MMLE, and add connections to wavelet methods.
