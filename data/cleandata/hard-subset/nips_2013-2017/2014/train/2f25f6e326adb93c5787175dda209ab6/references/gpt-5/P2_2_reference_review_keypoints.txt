- Proposes LRTFS: a generative time-domain model that imposes low-rank structure on variances of complex synthesis coefficients (α in x = Φα + e).
- Novelty: moves low-rank Gaussian variance prior from analysis/STFT coefficients to synthesis coefficients, bridging synthesis modeling with NMF/GCM.
- Estimation (MJLE): an EM scheme avoiding large matrix inversions, interleaving Wiener-like shrinkage on α with IS-NMF updates on |α|^2.
- Estimation (MMLE): a type-II SBL–style marginal likelihood approach that is elegant but currently computationally impractical at audio scale.
- Multi-resolution extension: combines multiple frames (e.g., short/long windows) with shared EM updates using the same simple machinery.
- Theoretical/derivational quality: modeling and derivations are sound, including decomposition of −log p(α|v) and scalable EM updates using FFT-based operations.
- MMLE limitation: correct derivation but not scalable, limiting exploration of its potential statistical benefits.
- Experimental evaluation: limited scale; toy jazz demo is small and speech enhancement shows modest gains (6.6 vs 6.0 dB SNR) over IS-NMF.
- Experimental concerns: λ is tuned for best SNR (fairness concerns), no runtime/memory comparisons, no variance across runs, and few ablations reported.
- Missing comparisons: no direct evaluation against phase-aware GCM or other time-domain generative baselines, which are the most relevant priors.
- Clarity: generally well organized and implementable, with clear motivations and explicit EM steps.
- Clarity issues to fix: specify β selection, provide pseudocode and per-iteration complexity, clarify frame normalization/overlap and stopping criteria, and detail λ scheduling.
- Originality: core idea is novel—low-rank variance priors on synthesis coefficients—and the coupled shrinkage+IS-NMF EM is an elegant algorithmic contribution.
- Significance: conceptually important for synthesis-side probabilistic modeling and multi-resolution handling, but empirical impact is modest in current form.
- Pros: principled generative model, scalable MJLE, simple multi-resolution extension, clear links to SBL/GCM, and interpretable decompositions with modest gains.
- Cons: small-scale experiments and possible λ bias, no phase-aware comparisons, MMLE not demonstrated, missing ablations, and some implementation/notation typos.
- Suggestions: add GCM/phase-aware NMF comparisons, report runtime/memory/convergence, provide pseudocode and parameter settings, run ablations (single vs multi-resolution), and report more metrics (SDR/PESQ/STOI).
- Overall: solid, well-motivated methodological contribution with a clean algorithm and useful multi-resolution ideas, but empirical evaluation needs strengthening; reviewer leans weak accept.
