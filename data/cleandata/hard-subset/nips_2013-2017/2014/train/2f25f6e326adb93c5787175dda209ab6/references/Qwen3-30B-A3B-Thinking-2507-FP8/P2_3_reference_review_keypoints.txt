- Introduces LRTFS: models raw signal as a linear combination of time-frequency atoms with low-rank variance in synthesis coefficients and a multi-resolution extension for different TF resolutions per component.  
- Extends prior work (GCM, sparse Bayesian learning, NMF-IS) by modeling the raw signal directly rather than only its STFT.  
- Originality: a significant conceptual advance over STFT-based models; multi-resolution capability is novel and valuable for audio.  
- Quality: solid theoretical derivations, EM estimation, and careful comparisons; experiments show a 6.6 dB average SNR improvement in speech enhancement over an NMF baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible to both newcomers and experts.  
- Weakness — NIPS relevance: work is more focused on audio signal processing and needs stronger connections to broader ML themes (e.g., structured sparsity, generative modeling).  
- Weakness — comparative analysis: lacks comparison to recent deep learning approaches for audio, missing positioning within current audio-ML landscape.  
- Weakness — computational analysis: insufficient detail on MJLE computational complexity and scalability (e.g., runtime benchmarks for long audio).  
- Weakness — theoretical contribution: extends existing techniques to a new domain but lacks formal theoretical guarantees or stronger theoretical novelty.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: strengthen links to broader ML concepts, add discussion/comparison to deep learning audio methods, and provide more detailed computational analysis of MJLE.  
- Pros summary: direct raw-signal modeling, novel multi-resolution capability, strong empirical results, technical soundness, and good literature positioning.  
- Cons summary: limited broader ML relevance, missing deep-learning comparisons, inadequate computational complexity analysis, and somewhat incremental theoretical novelty.
