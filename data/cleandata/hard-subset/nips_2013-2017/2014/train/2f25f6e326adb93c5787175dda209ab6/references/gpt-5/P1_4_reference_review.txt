Summary
This paper introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative probabilistic model for time-domain signals whose time-frequency synthesis coefficients are Gaussian with variances constrained to have a low-rank NMF structure WH across the time-frequency grid. In contrast to the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) that models the STFT analysis coefficients Y with yfn ∼ Nc(0, [WH]fn), LRTFS models the signal x(t) directly via a synthesis expansion x = Φα + e, with αfn ∼ Nc(0, [WH]fn). This leads to two estimation strategies: (i) Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) with an EM algorithm that uses an auxiliary variable to avoid large matrix inversions, and (ii) Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE, type-II ML/SBL), which is shown to be computationally challenging at audio scale. The framework naturally extends to multi-resolution modeling by summing multiple frames Φℓ with separate low-rank structures on αℓ. Experiments on hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement demonstrate feasibility, modest SNR gains over single-window IS-NMF, and qualitative improvements in separating transient/tonal layers.

Relation to prior work
- NMF/IS-NMF: The paper builds on IS-NMF for spectrogram factorization and its probabilistic interpretation. Unlike standard practice (magnitude-only, deterministic fitting), LRTFS is a generative model for x(t), integrating phase through synthesis.
- GCM (probabilistic NMF of complex STFT): LRTFS moves the low-rank variance prior from analysis to synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-resolution synthesis and direct time-domain likelihood.
- Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL): LRTFS echoes SBL’s hierarchical Gaussian prior but imposes a structured variance vm = [WH]fn, tying coefficients instead of per-parameter free variances that induce sparsity. The MJLE objective features a log(|α|^2) term reminiscent of sparsity-promoting penalties while remaining consistent with the Gaussian model.
- Multi-resolution signal models: Prior hybrid/morphological approaches (MCA, multi-window Gabor) and mixed norms assigned transients/tonals to distinct frames with sparsity priors. LRTFS offers a complementary probabilistic, low-rank alternative.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The model and likelihoods are clearly derived. The MJLE EM uses the Figueiredo–Nowak auxiliary-variable decomposition to yield simple updates: a Wiener-like shrinkage for α, IS-NMF updates for W,H, and a residual variance update. The emergence of DIS(|α|^2|v) + log(|α|^2) in the negative joint log-likelihood is coherent with a Gaussian prior and clarifies the shrinkage behavior. The MMLE EM is standard for type-II ML with closed-form posterior moments, but its Σ update is computationally prohibitive at scale (authors correctly note this).
- Algorithms: The MJLE method is computationally attractive (no large inversions; relies on Φ/Φ* operations). The multi-resolution extension is straightforward and remains efficient.
- Empirics: Experiments are illustrative and show the method works in practice. However, they are limited in scope, use oracle-like tuning of λ in one task, and provide modest gains over strong baselines without extensive metrics or ablations.

Clarity
- Generally clear and well organized. The distinction between analysis y and synthesis α is explicit and helpful. The EM steps are stated cleanly with implementable updates.
- Suggestions:
  - Specify how β is chosen in practice (beyond β ≤ λ/δΦ). Is δΦ computed or bounded via frame properties (e.g., tight frame constant)?
  - Clarify initialization strategies for W,H,α and sensitivity to them.
  - Expand on the practical handling of real signals via Hermitian symmetry (what is implemented).
  - Provide pseudo-code for the MJLE algorithm and multi-resolution variant.

Originality
- Novelty lies in moving low-rank variance modeling from analysis to synthesis coefficients, yielding a time-domain generative model that naturally supports multiple frames. To my knowledge, tying a low-rank variance prior across synthesis coefficients in this way and connecting it to IS-NMF via EM/Wiener-like shrinkage is new. The multi-resolution extension is conceptually simple but valuable.

Significance
- Potentially impactful for audio modeling, source separation, and speech enhancement by enabling flexible, multi-resolution, time-domain generative modeling with phase. The practical MJLE algorithm could be adopted in place of or alongside STFT-domain NMF methods.
- Current empirical evidence is promising but not yet definitive: SNR gains over one-window IS-NMF are modest (6.6 vs 6.0 dB) and comparisons omit closely related probabilistic complex STFT models (e.g., GCM) or multi-window NMF baselines. With stronger evaluation, the contribution could be significant.

Pros
- Clear, principled generative model for x(t) with low-rank time-frequency structure on synthesis coefficients.
- Efficient EM algorithm (MJLE) with simple steps and no matrix inversions; easy to implement with fast TF libraries.
- Natural multi-resolution extension; demonstrated on realistic audio tasks.
- Insightful connection between IS-NMF, Wiener filtering, and iterative shrinkage.

Cons
- Limited empirical evaluation: modest gains, small test sets, and oracle tuning of λ in one experiment undermine conclusions.
- Missing comparisons to closest probabilistic baselines (GCM on complex STFT, multi-window NMF).
- No runtime/complexity benchmarks; no ablations (effect of K, λ, β, single- vs multi-resolution).
- MMLE section is not scalable; no approximate scalable alternative (e.g., conjugate gradients, message passing) is implemented.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- How is β selected in practice? Any guidance or adaptive scheme?
- Can you report wall-clock times and scaling with T, F, N, K?
- Please avoid oracle tuning of λ; propose a principled schedule or cross-validation approach. The warm-restart strategy for speech is interesting; can you generalize it?
- Add baselines: (i) GCM-based probabilistic NMF on complex STFT with Wiener reconstruction; (ii) multi-window IS-NMF; (iii) MCA-style sparse hybrid methods. Also report perceptual metrics (e.g., SDR/SIR/SAR, STOI/PESQ).
- Provide an ablation: single vs two resolutions within LRTFS; effect of K on quality; sensitivity to initialization.
- Discuss identifiability issues and whether low-rank structure adequately regularizes α vs potential degeneracies between α and λ.

Overall recommendation
Borderline accept. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated probabilistic model with an efficient algorithm and a compelling multi-resolution story. The theoretical development is solid and the contribution is of interest to the ML-for-signal-processing community. However, the empirical validation is currently not strong enough for a clear accept; adding rigorous comparisons and removing oracle parameter tuning would strengthen the case considerably.

Reviewer confidence: Medium.
