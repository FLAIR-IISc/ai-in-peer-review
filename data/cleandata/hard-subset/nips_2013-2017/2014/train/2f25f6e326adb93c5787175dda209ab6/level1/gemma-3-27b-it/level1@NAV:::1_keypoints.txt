- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model extending the Gaussian Composite Model to synthesize raw signals with low-rank time-frequency structure.  
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations not achievable with traditional NMF.  
- Proposes two EM-based estimation algorithms for the model.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.  
- Clearly situates work within matrix factorization, NMF, probabilistic latent factor models, and relates to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Theoretical derivation and EM algorithms are mathematically well-founded.  
- Multi-resolution extension is a significant and practically useful contribution.  
- Empirical validation is promising but preliminary.  
- MMLE approach is computationally expensive and deemed impractical in current form.  
- Experimental results show sensitivity to hyperparameter λ with limited strategy for selection.  
- Comparative evaluation is limited (only IS-NMF and OMLSA); lacks comparison to more contemporary SOTA methods.  
- Some notation (indices, vector/matrix representations) could be made more consistent for clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid theory, multi-resolution capability, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational limitations, limited experimental scope, and hyperparameter sensitivity; recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter selection and broader baselines.
