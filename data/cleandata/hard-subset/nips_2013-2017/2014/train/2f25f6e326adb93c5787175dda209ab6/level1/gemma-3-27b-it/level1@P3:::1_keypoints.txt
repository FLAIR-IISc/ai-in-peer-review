- Proposes LRTFS, a novel generative model that directly synthesizes raw signals using low-rank time-frequency synthesis coefficients.  
- Extends Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to overcome limitations of modeling only the STFT.  
- Introduces multi-resolution representation via synthesis coefficients, offering advantages over single-resolution methods.  
- Presents two EM algorithms (including MMLE) for parameter estimation.  
- Demonstrates applications on music decomposition and speech enhancement with competitive results.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and connects well to sparse Bayesian learning, clarifying differences in variance treatment and sparsity.  
- Multi-resolution extension is an elegant and compelling contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and uses consistent notation.  
- Builds on solid prior work in NMF, GCM, and matrix factorization, and relates to semi-supervised source separation.  
- Practical relevance is supported by experiments, showing potential in real audio tasks.  
- MMLE approach has serious scalability issues due to large matrix inversions, limiting large-scale applicability.  
- Performance is sensitive to hyperparameter λ, with limited discussion on robust tuning strategies beyond a decreasing schedule.  
- Experimental comparison is limited—missing broader baselines, especially deep learning-based methods.  
- Speech enhancement results lack rigorous statistical analysis to confirm significance (only average SNR over 10 signals reported).  
- Reproducibility could be improved with more implementation details (optimization parameters, EM stopping criteria), despite using LTFAT.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with practical promise; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing robustness and expanding experimental evaluation.
