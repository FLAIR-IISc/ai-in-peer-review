This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative time-domain model in which the signal is synthesized from time-frequency atoms with complex Gaussian synthesis coefficients whose variances have a low-rank structure WH. This bridges the gap between nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) of spectrograms and time-domain modeling: unlike the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) for STFT coefficients (e.g., Févotte et al., Neural Computation), LRTFS is a generative model of x(t). The authors derive two estimation schemes: (i) a scalable EM algorithm for maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) using the Figueiredo–Nowak latent-variable trick that yields alternating “Wiener-like” shrinkage on α and IS-NMF updates on W,H; and (ii) a maximum marginal likelihood (type-II) approach in the spirit of sparse Bayesian learning (Tipping; Wipf & Rao), which is principled but currently not scalable. A key contribution is a straightforward multi-resolution extension using multiple frames (e.g., short/long Gabor), aligning with prior hybrid decompositions (Daudet & Torrésani; Kowalski & Torrésani) while maintaining a probabilistic low-rank structure. Experiments in hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest but consistent SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.

Quality: The modeling is mathematically sound and well-motivated; the MJLE EM is carefully derived and uses efficient Φ/Φ* operations. However, MMLE is not demonstrated at scale, λ is treated as a tuned hyperparameter (and even annealed to near-zero), and quantitative evaluation is limited (SNR only, small test set, no statistical tests). Convergence behavior (e.g., role/schedule of β) and initialization are under-specified.

Clarity: Generally clear and well-organized. Suggestions: specify computational complexity and runtime, initialization of W,H,α, stopping criteria, handling of Hermitian symmetry for real signals, and the exact λ/β schedules.

Originality: The move from low-rank modeling of analysis coefficients (GCM/IS-NMF) to synthesis coefficients in the time domain with a low-rank variance is novel, as is the clean multi-resolution probabilistic formulation. The connections to SBL are insightful.

Significance: The work can influence audio modeling by enabling coherent time-domain, multi-resolution low-rank synthesis with practical algorithms. Demonstrated gains are encouraging, though broader benchmarks and ablations are needed to solidify impact.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Conceptual advance: truly generative time-domain low-rank TF model
- Elegant, scalable EM with clear links to Wiener filtering and IS-NMF
- Natural multi-resolution extension with practical benefits
- Empirical improvements over strong classical baselines

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited evaluation scope and metrics; no modern baselines or ablations
- Reliance on hand-tuned λ/β; lack of robust λ estimation
- MMLE contribution is undeveloped computationally
- Missing details on complexity, initialization, and convergence diagnostics
