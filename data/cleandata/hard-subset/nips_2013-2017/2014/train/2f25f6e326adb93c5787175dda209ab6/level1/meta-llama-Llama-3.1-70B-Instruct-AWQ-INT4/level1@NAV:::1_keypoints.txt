- Introduces LRTFS, a novel model bridging time-frequency synthesis and NMF with multi-resolution atoms.  
- Proposes two EM-based estimators: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- Demonstrates improved results on music decomposition and speech enhancement versus traditional NMF.  
- Novelty: combines strengths of time-frequency synthesis and NMF in a new synthesis model.  
- Theoretical foundation: clear derivation of EM algorithms for MJLE and MMLE.  
- Experimental results support the model’s effectiveness.  
- Provides comparisons to state-of-the-art methods showing improvements.  
- MMLE has high computational complexity due to inversion of a large matrix, limiting scalability.  
- Relies on manual hyperparameter tuning, which may be suboptimal and computationally costly.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two tasks; broader datasets and tasks are needed.  
- Significance: promising advance for multi-resolution signal processing and audio analysis.  
- Quality and clarity: paper is well-written with clear explanations.  
- For acceptance: novel, well-motivated model with solid theory and empirical gains.  
- Against acceptance: MMLE’s computational cost and reliance on manual tuning.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing MMLE complexity and expanding evaluations.
