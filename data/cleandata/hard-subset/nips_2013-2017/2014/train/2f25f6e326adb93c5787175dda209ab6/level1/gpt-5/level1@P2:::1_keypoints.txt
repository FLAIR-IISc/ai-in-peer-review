- Summary: Proposes LRTFS, a generative waveform model that imposes low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients (variance = WH) with MJLE and MMLE estimation and a natural multi-resolution extension.

- Strength: Conceptual advance bridging spectrogram factorizations (GCM/IS-NMF) and generative waveform modeling with tractable inference.

- Strength: Elegant MJLE algorithm using a splitting trick to avoid large inversions; α-update is Wiener-like and W,H updates reduce to standard IS-NMF.

- Strength: Natural and simple multi-resolution extension that linearly combines multiple TF resolutions at the synthesis level.

- Strength: Practical results show modest SNR improvements in speech enhancement and plausible multi-resolution music decomposition.

- Strength: Connects neatly to SBL, framing LRTFS as structured-variance SBL and clarifying sparsity/assumption differences.

- Weakness: MMLE is computationally impractical for typical audio sizes, leaving only MJLE evaluated.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope: few audio tasks, metrics mainly SNR, no perceptual measures or significance testing, and relatively simple baselines.

- Weakness: Missing ablation studies (synthesis vs analysis, single- vs multi-resolution, effect of sparsity log term).

- Weakness: Sensitivity to hyperparameters (λ, β) not systematically studied; λ often tuned/annealed rather than jointly estimated.

- Weakness: Incomplete related-work discussion regarding phase-aware/complex NMF and other models with structured variances.

- Quality: Model and MJLE derivations are sound and consistent; MMLE section is correct but practically incomplete.

- Quality: Experiments indicate benefits, especially for multi-resolution, but stronger comparisons are needed to substantiate claims.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear; distinctions between analysis and synthesis coefficients and index mappings are well explained.

- Clarity: The log(|α|^2) term in the joint objective appears without full derivation—supplementary detail would help.

- Clarity: Multi-resolution explanation and complexity considerations are concise and understandable.

- Originality: Novel idea to impose low-rank structure on synthesis variances yielding a waveform generative model and multi-resolution capability.

- Originality: MJLE EM adapts known techniques in a new setting; the structured SBL perspective is insightful.

- Significance: Likely to influence audio ML by providing a principled synthesis-based low-rank framework and practical EM algorithm.

- Significance: Empirical gains are modest; broader evaluations and stronger baselines would increase practical impact.

- Suggestions: Add ablation studies comparing synthesis vs analysis, single vs multi-resolution, and the sparsity term's effect.

- Suggestions: Include perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI), significance tests, more noise types, and stronger baselines (phase-aware, multi-resolution NMF).

- Suggestions: Discuss identifiability, initialization strategies, and provide sensitivity analysis or heuristics for λ and β.

- Suggestions: Provide supplementary derivations for the joint likelihood (log term) and report per-iteration computational costs.

- Pros for acceptance: Conceptual novelty, scalable MJLE leveraging IS-NMF, clean multi-resolution extension, and clear links to GCM/SBL.

- Cons for acceptance: MMLE not scalable, limited empirical scope and metrics, missing stronger baselines/ablations, and unclear hyperparameter behavior.

- Overall assessment: Original, technically solid, and potentially impactful; recommended for acceptance provided a revised version expands empirical validation and clarifies derivations.
