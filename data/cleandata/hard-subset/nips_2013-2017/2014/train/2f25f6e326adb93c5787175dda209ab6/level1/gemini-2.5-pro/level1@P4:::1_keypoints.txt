- Proposes LRTFS, a generative model for time-domain signals that imposes low-rank structure on the variance of synthesis coefficients.
- Bridges sparse synthesis models and low-rank TF factorization (NMF) by modeling the waveform rather than the STFT.
- Model form: x(t) = sum alpha_fn * phi_fn(t) + e(t), with alpha_fn ~ Nc(0, [W H]_fn).
- Derives two estimation methods: MJLE with an efficient EM algorithm and MMLE that marginalizes coefficients and connects to SBL.
- MJLE is computationally tractable; MMLE is theoretically preferable but not scalable in practice.
- Key advantage: naturally supports multi-resolution representations by combining multiple TF bases with separate low-rank structures.
- Multi-resolution LRTFS better captures transients and tonal components than single-resolution NMF.
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement show improvements over baselines (IS-NMF, OMLSA).
- Quantitative improvement is modest (e.g., ~0.6 dB SNR gain in speech enhancement).
- Theoretical formulation and derivations (MJLE EM) are sound and clearly presented.
- Paper is very well-written and situates the work clearly relative to GCM and SBL.
- Original contribution is the shift from analysis-domain (spectrogram) low-rank modeling to synthesis-domain low-rank variance modeling.
- Significance: principled framework that accounts for phase and enables multi-resolution analysis, with potential beyond audio.
- Limitations include MMLE’s non-scalability and a heuristic update for noise variance in MJLE.
- Practical impact may be limited by modest empirical gains and lack of scalable MMLE approximations.
- Overall recommendation leans toward acceptance for novelty, clarity, and theoretical advance despite computational caveats.
