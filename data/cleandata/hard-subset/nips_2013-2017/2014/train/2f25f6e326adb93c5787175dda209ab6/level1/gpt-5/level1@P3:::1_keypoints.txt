- Proposes LRTFS: a time-domain generative model placing a low-rank complex Gaussian prior on synthesis coefficients and modeling the waveform as their synthesis plus Gaussian noise.  
- Bridges IS-NMF/GCM of the STFT and full waveform generative models by imposing low-rank structure on synthesis (not analysis) coefficients.  
- Presents two estimation strategies: MJLE via an EM algorithm with a Figueiredo–Nowak β-splitting trick to avoid large matrix inversions, and MMLE (type-II/SBL-like) which is currently not scalable.  
- Naturally extends to multi-resolution by stacking frames, enabling layered decompositions with different time supports.  
- Modeling and derivations are technically sound; decomposition into a data-fidelity term plus an IS-NMF penalty on |α|^2 is correct.  
- The paper’s claim that the added log(|α|^2) term “favors sparsity” is misleading since it cancels with an α-dependent component in DIS; net effect remains the original Gaussian prior.  
- MJLE EM algorithm is well-motivated, but the β-splitting constraint (β ≤ λ/δΦ) is important and practical guidance on choosing/scheduling β when λ becomes small is missing.  
- MMLE formulation is elegant and standard but computationally prohibitive in current form; authors acknowledge this limitation.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized with explicit equations and implementation notes (e.g., LTFAT usage).  
- Reproducibility would improve with details on initialization of W/H/α, stopping criteria, β schedule, runtime/memory, and choice of K.  
- The latent component reconstruction rule is sensible, but its relation to the MMSE estimator and the T×T inversion approximation should be discussed more explicitly.  
- Handling of real-valued signals via Hermitian symmetry is only briefly mentioned and needs a short implementation note.  
- Modeling low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients is an original and meaningful shift from prior GCM/IS-NMF work.  
- Multi-resolution extension is valuable and novel in this low-rank synthesis EM estimation context.  
- Potentially significant for audio ML/signal processing by providing waveform-domain NMF-like interpretability and routes to multichannel/inverse problems.  
- Empirical validation is limited: small-scale experiments, modest SNR gains, no ablations, no perceptual metrics, and comparisons to GCM under matched conditions are missing.  
- Specific strengths: principled probabilistic time-domain formulation, efficient MJLE avoiding large inversions, simple multi-resolution EM, and clear links to SBL/Wiener filtering.  
- Specific weaknesses/suggestions: expand experiments (single- vs multi-resolution baseline, GCM/IS-NMF comparisons, perceptual metrics, ablations on λ/β/K, runtime), clarify β selection and sparsity interpretation, discuss identifiability with redundant frames, and propose concrete MMLE approximations (CG, diagonal/low-rank approximations, AMP/EP).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled model, solid EM algorithm, and useful multi-resolution capability with potential to influence future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited/partially confounded experiments, missing practical details, and scalable MMLE not demonstrated.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending stronger experimental validation and clarification of β/λ choices.
