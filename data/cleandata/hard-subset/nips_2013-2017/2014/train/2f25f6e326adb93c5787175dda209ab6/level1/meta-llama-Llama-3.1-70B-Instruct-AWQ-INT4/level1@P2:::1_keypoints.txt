- Proposes LRTFS, a novel generative signal synthesis model bridging time-frequency synthesis and NMF by representing signals as sums of layered, low-rank latent structures with varying time resolution.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough, accessible background and related work section.  
- LRTFS is well-motivated with clear explanations of model components and their relationships.  
- Technical soundness: detailed model derivations and estimation algorithms, including EM for MJLE and MMLE.  
- Clear comparison of conceptual differences between LRTFS and traditional NMF approaches.  
- High originality: novel contribution with new multi-resolution modeling and signal synthesis results.  
- High significance: potential to advance signal processing/audio analysis; demonstrated on music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Limitation: lacks thorough empirical comparison against other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Limitation: does not discuss computational complexity or potential practical limitations.  
- Limitation: would benefit from additional experiments and evaluations to further validate effectiveness.
