- Introduces LRTFS: a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that models raw signals (not just STFT).
- Core idea: represent the signal as a sum of atoms with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients.
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations not easily achievable with traditional NMF.
- Provides two EM algorithms for parameter estimation (MMLE noted as expensive).
- Demonstrated on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks with comparisons to IS-NMF and OMLSA.
- Builds on NMF/GCM literature and connects to sparse Bayesian learning, clarifying differences in variance modeling.
- Strength: genuine novelty by providing a generative model for the raw signal beyond STFT-based methods.
- Strength: clear multi-resolution capability motivated for audio applications.
- Strength: solid theoretical derivation and well-explained connection to SBL.
- Strength: experimental validation shows effectiveness on relevant tasks.
- Strength: generally well-written with clear notation and explanations.
- Weakness: MMLE algorithm is computationally expensive and scalability strategies are insufficiently discussed.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameter λ, requiring manual tuning in experiments.
- Weakness: limited baseline comparisons (no modern deep learning methods) weaken evaluation.
- Weakness: results lack statistical significance testing to support claimed improvements.
- Weakness: missing implementation details (e.g., Gabor frame parameters) reducing reproducibility.
- Recommendation: overall strong work; accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: address computational scaling strategies, robust methods for setting λ, include statistical significance tests, and provide implementation details.
