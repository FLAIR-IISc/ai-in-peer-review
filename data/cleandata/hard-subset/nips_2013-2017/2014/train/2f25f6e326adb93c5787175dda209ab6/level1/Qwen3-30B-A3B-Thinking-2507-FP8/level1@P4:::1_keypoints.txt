- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that models the raw signal directly with low-rank time-frequency components.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) framework by moving from STFT-domain generative modeling to raw-signal synthesis.  
- Proposes two estimation approaches: maximum joint likelihood (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood (MMLE).  
- Derives EM algorithms for estimation with rigorous mathematical justification and attention to computational considerations.  
- Establishes connections to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) and clarifies how LRTFS imposes structured variances.  
- Supports multi-resolution representations, allowing different time-frequency resolutions per component—advancing beyond fixed-resolution NMF.  
- Demonstrates practical applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement with measured improvements (e.g., 6.6 dB vs. 6 dB).  
- Experimental validation is careful and comparisons to baselines show meaningful gains, though baselines in speech enhancement are limited.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written with reproducible mathematical detail.  
- Acknowledges limitations, particularly MMLE scalability which prevented its experimental use.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity for large-scale audio and incomplete experimental use of MMLE.  
- Weakness: could include more baselines for speech enhancement and more detailed explanation/visualization of multi-resolution implementation.  
- Significance: multi-resolution capability is practically valuable for audio and opens new research directions (e.g., multichannel source separation).  
- Overall assessment: meaningful, novel contribution with clear practical impact; recommended for acceptance despite some scalability and evaluation gaps.
