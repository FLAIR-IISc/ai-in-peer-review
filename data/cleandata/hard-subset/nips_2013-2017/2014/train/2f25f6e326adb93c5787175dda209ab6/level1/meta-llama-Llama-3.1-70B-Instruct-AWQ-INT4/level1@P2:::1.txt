This paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model, called Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), which fills in the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional Nonnegative Matrix Factorization (NMF) approaches. The authors present a generative probabilistic model that represents a signal as a sum of layers with their own time resolution and latent low-rank structure.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the background and related work, making it easy to follow for readers not familiar with the topic. The proposed LRTFS model is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the model's components and their relationships.

The technical soundness of the paper is good. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the model and its estimation algorithms, including the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE). The authors also provide a clear explanation of the differences between their approach and traditional NMF approaches.

The originality of the paper is high. The proposed LRTFS model is a novel contribution to the field of signal processing, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its properties and advantages. The paper also presents new results on multi-resolution modeling and signal synthesis, which are relevant to various applications in audio processing.

The significance of the paper is also high. The proposed LRTFS model has the potential to advance the state of the art in signal processing and audio analysis. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement, which are relevant applications in the field.
