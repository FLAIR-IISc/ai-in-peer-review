- Introduces LRTFS: a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure bridging NMF and raw-signal generative models.  
- Proposes two expectation-maximization algorithms for parameter estimation in the LRTFS model.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on audio tasks: hybrid music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with sound mathematical derivations and well-explained algorithms.  
- Main technical novelty: low-rank factorization of the time-frequency transform enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- Strength: novel model offering a new perspective on signal synthesis.  
- Strength: development of two EM-based estimation algorithms.  
- Strength: empirical demonstrations in relevant audio processing applications.  
- Weakness: algorithms appear complex and may be difficult to implement in practice.  
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation (only two experiments).  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Pro-accept: significant and novel contribution with potential for new applications and insights.  
- Pro-accept: proposed algorithms provide a sound estimation framework.  
- Con-accept: practical complexity, limited evaluation, and missing SOTA comparisons weaken impact.  
- Recommendation: accept, with requests for additional experiments, comparisons, algorithm simplification, and exploration of other applications (e.g., multichannel source separation).
