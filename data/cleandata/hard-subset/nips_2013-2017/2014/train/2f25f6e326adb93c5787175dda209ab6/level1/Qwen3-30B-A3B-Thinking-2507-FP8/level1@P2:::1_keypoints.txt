- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that directly models time-domain audio by imposing low-rank variance on synthesis coefficients.  
- Extends Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) from STFT coefficients to raw signal synthesis coefficients and introduces multi-resolution time-frequency representations.  
- Presents two estimation methods: computationally efficient Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- Bridges gap between NMF on STFT magnitudes and generative modeling of raw audio signals.  
- Multi-resolution capability allows modeling components with different time-frequency resolutions (e.g., transients vs. tones).  
- Theoretical framework and EM derivations are sound and well-explained, with connections to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- MJLE is scalable and avoids matrix inversion, suitable for audio-scale problems; implementation details reference LTFAT.  
- Experiments (music decomposition, speech enhancement) show modest SNR improvements (6.6 dB vs. 6 dB for IS-NMF).  
- Limited experimental scope: only two applications and few baselines, with no ablation studies to isolate contributions.  
- Significance is unclear due to marginal performance gains and lack of comparison to recent state-of-the-art (including deep learning methods).  
- No analysis of computational efficiency relative to competing approaches.  
- Paper is technically solid, well-written, and novel, but empirical evidence is insufficient to claim major advance.  
- Recommended for acceptance given technical quality and novelty, but should expand experiments, include ablations, and compare to recent methods in the camera-ready version.
