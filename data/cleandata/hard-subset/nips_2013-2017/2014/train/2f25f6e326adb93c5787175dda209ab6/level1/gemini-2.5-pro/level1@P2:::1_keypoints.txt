- Introduces LRTFS, a generative time-series model that models signals as x = Φα + e and imposes low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients’ variances.  
- Distinguishes from prior work by modeling variances of synthesis (α) rather than analysis (STFT) coefficients, linking NMF to Sparse Bayesian Learning.  
- Derives two estimation methods: a practical MJLE algorithm and a more principled but computationally infeasible MMLE.  
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency analysis by combining different TF bases, a capability not present in standard spectrogram-based NMF.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains on audio tasks (music decomposition, speech enhancement), with multi-resolution LRTFS improving SNR over baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound; probabilistic model and EM derivations are clear and grounded in established literature.  
- Experiments convincingly support claims; reported SNR improvements are meaningful though not huge.  
- Authors transparently acknowledge limitations: MJLE may converge to local minima and MMLE is intractable for realistic signals.  
- Practical issues include treating noise variance λ as a hyperparameter because joint estimation proved difficult.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear motivation, logical presentation, and useful figures/sound examples.  
- Suggestion: provide more justification or citation for the MJLE λ-update rule, which is not the exact EM update.  
- Work is highly original, reframing NMF as a synthesis-variance model and naturally supporting multi-resolution analysis.  
- The approach has substantial significance for audio and other time-series domains by handling phase and multi-scale structure principledly.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled model, resolves key NMF limitations, multi-resolution capability, feasible MJLE, strong empirical results, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: MMLE’s intractability limits theoretical completeness and empirical gains, while positive, are not dramatically large.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — important, well-executed, and influential contribution.
