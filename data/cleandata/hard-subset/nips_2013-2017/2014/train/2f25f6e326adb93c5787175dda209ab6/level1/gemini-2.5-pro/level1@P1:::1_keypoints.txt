- Introduces LRTFS: a probabilistic generative model that synthesizes time-domain signals from TF atoms with synthesis-coefficient variances structured by low-rank NMF (Var(α_fn) = [WH]_fn).

- Fills a gap relative to GCM by modeling the raw waveform (not just STFT coefficients) and linking to Sparse Bayesian Learning through variance priors.

- Naturally supports principled multi-resolution analysis by concatenating TF frames and learning separate low-rank structures for each resolution.

- Presents two estimation approaches: an efficient MJLE-based EM algorithm (scalable) and a more robust MMLE approach that is currently not scalable.

- Demonstrates effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement, showing quantitative improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA (e.g., +0.6 dB SNR vs single-resolution IS-NMF).

- High originality and conceptual advance in shifting from spectrogram analysis to synthesis-based generative modeling.

- Clear, well-organized exposition with detailed and rigorous mathematical derivations.

- Sound, practical methodology that leverages efficient analysis–synthesis operations; authors are transparent about limitations.

- Major weakness: MMLE scalability — inability to apply the more principled estimator to realistic signals.

- Modest empirical gains in some experiments (0.6 dB SNR), suggesting the need for broader evaluation across noise conditions and tasks.

- Hyperparameter handling issue: noise variance λ is fixed rather than estimated due to instability, reducing automation; sensitivity discussion is needed.

- Overall high technical quality, well-supported claims, and significant contribution likely to inspire further research.

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
