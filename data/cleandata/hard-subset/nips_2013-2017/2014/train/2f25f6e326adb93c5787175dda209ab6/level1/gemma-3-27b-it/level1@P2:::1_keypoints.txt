- Proposes LRTFS, a novel generative model extending GCM by modeling the raw signal via atoms with low-rank synthesis coefficients.
- Provides EM algorithms for estimation and demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement with multi-resolution capability.
- Builds on prior matrix factorization work (NMF, GCM) and connects to sparse Bayesian learning while highlighting structural differences.
- Novelty: addresses GCM’s inability to generate raw signals and enables direct signal synthesis from low-rank time-frequency representations.
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation and logically derived EM algorithms grounded in established techniques.
- Clarity: well-written, organized paper with consistent notation and clear motivation.
- Significance: potential broader applications in audio processing and value in multi-resolution analysis.
- Experiments: initial evidence of effectiveness and useful comparisons to IS-NMF and OMLSA, but scope is limited.
- Scalability concern: MMLE does not scale well to large dimensions and an implementation is not presented, raising completeness concerns.
- Hyperparameter tuning: insufficient detail and justification for the λ schedule used in speech enhancement experiments.
- Limited experimentation: needs more datasets, noise types, and metrics; focus is mainly on audio.
- Lack of theoretical analysis: missing deeper discussion of model properties (identifiability, convergence guarantees).
- Computational complexity: MJLE appears efficient but lacks detailed complexity analysis versus existing methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, addresses key limitation of GCM, and is clearly presented with promising experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability issues, limited empirical evaluation, and insufficient theoretical analysis.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking for λ tuning details, expanded experiments, MJLE complexity discussion, and theoretical insights.
