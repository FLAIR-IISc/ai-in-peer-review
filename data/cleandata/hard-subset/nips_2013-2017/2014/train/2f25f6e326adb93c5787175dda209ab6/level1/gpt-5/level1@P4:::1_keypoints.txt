- Proposal: LRTFS — a generative model of time-domain signals imposing low-rank structure on variances of complex synthesis coefficients (αfn ∼ Nc(0, [WH]fn)).
- Key novelty: shifts low-rank modeling from STFT analysis coefficients to synthesis coefficients, enabling multiresolution synthesis with the same low-rank prior.
- Estimation methods: scalable MJLE via an EM algorithm with Wiener-like α updates and IS-NMF updates for W,H; and a computationally heavy MMLE (type-II ML) related to SBL but not used in experiments.
- Multiresolution extension: naturally handled by stacking multiple TF frames and learning separate low-rank structures per frame, which is difficult with conventional NMF pipelines.
- Experiments: hybrid music decomposition (tonal/transient) and semi‑supervised speech enhancement using pre-trained speech dictionaries and online noise dictionaries.
- Reported results: SNR improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines in the demonstrated tasks.
- Relation to prior work: clearly connected to IS-NMF, GCM, SBL, EM augmentation, hybrid audio models, and supervised/semi-supervised separation literature.
- Strength — conceptual advance: provides a principled, probabilistic, signal-level counterpart to GCM/IS-NMF with links to SBL and multiresolution.
- Strength — algorithmic simplicity/scalability: MJLE EM requires only synthesis/analysis ops (Φ, Φ*) and standard IS-NMF updates; avoids large matrix inversions.
- Strength — clear derivations: MJLE updates are well derived, with a log(|α|^2) term emerging that encourages shrinkage.
- Strength — practical relevance: multiresolution modeling is well motivated for audio and shows competitive empirical gains.
- Weakness — hyperparameter selection: λ in the music experiment was tuned using ground-truth SNR (oracle), inflating reported performance; needs validation-independent selection or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — MMLE not validated: MMLE is conceptually valuable but not scalable and not empirically evaluated; provide a reduced-scale demo or defer.
- Weakness — limited evaluation: only SNR on small test sets; lacks perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI), larger statistically analyzed test suites, and objective layer-separation metrics or ablations.
- Weakness — missing implementation details: no details on initialization of W,H,α; choice and use of β; normalization to address W/H scale ambiguity; stopping criteria; runtimes and memory comparisons; training procedure for Wtrain.
- Weakness — identifiability/convergence: no discussion of identifiability under redundant frames, nor convergence guarantees beyond monotonic likelihood decrease; column normalization or priors not specified.
- Weakness — real-signal handling: Hermitian symmetry is mentioned but operational algorithmic handling is not specified.
- Weakness — reference formatting: several references lack years/page numbers.
- Quality assessment: modeling and MJLE are technically sound and supported by derivations and experiments, but λ selection and lack of MMLE validation weaken rigor.
- Clarity assessment: overall clear with explicit equations and steps, but notation is dense and reproducibility details are missing.
- Originality assessment: novel and nontrivial to model low-rank on synthesis coefficients and extend to multiresolution; insightful SBL connection.
- Significance assessment: potentially impactful for audio separation/enhancement, but broader evaluations needed to solidify significance.
- Pros for acceptance: principled signal-level generative model, scalable EM integration with IS-NMF tooling, natural multiresolution extension, strong links to established probabilistic frameworks.
- Cons against acceptance: limited evaluation and oracle hyperparameter tuning, MMLE theoretical-only, missing implementation/complexity details, and unresolved theoretical aspects (identifiability/convergence).
- Requests for clarification: how are W and H initialized and normalized; what stopping criteria are used?
- Requests for clarification: what β is used in practice and is it adapted when λ is annealed?
- Requests for clarification: how are real signals and Hermitian symmetry implemented in algorithms?
- Requests for clarification: how were pre-trained speech dictionaries obtained (data, objective, preprocessing)?
- Requests for clarification: please report runtimes and memory vs IS-NMF and provide sensitivity analyses for λ and K.
- Requests for clarification: add perceptual metrics for speech, larger test sets, and ablation isolating multiresolution benefits.
