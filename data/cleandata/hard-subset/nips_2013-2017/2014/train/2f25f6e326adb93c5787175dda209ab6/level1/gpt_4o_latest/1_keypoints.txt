- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model that directly models raw audio with low-rank time-frequency structure.
- Bridges gap between traditional NMF/STFT-based methods and generative raw-signal models, extending beyond Gaussian Composite Model (GCM).
- Proposes two EM algorithms for parameter estimation: maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE).
- Extends the model to multi-resolution representations, enabling decomposition into layers with different time-frequency resolutions.
- Theoretical connections to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) are well-derived and mathematically rigorous.
- Experimental results show practical utility in music decomposition and speech enhancement, with SNR improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines.
- Supplementary audio examples and clear paper organization aid reproducibility and understanding.
- MMLE is computationally expensive, limiting scalability to large-scale audio tasks.
- Experimental scope is limited (single jazz excerpt for music, applause noise for speech), reducing generalizability.
- Reliance on manual tuning of hyperparameter λ decreases robustness and hinders reproducibility.
- Strong novelty and potential impact by proposing a generative raw-audio model and multi-resolution extension.
- Acceptance arguments: novel contribution, multi-resolution advance, and compelling theoretical/experimental results.
- Rejection concerns: scalability of MMLE, limited experiments, and manual hyperparameter tuning.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestions to address scalability and broaden experimental evaluations in future work.
