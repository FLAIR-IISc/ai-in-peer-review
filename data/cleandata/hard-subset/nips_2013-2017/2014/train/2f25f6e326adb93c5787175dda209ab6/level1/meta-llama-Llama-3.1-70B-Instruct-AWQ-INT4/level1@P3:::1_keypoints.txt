- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative low-rank time-frequency synthesis model bridging time-frequency synthesis and NMF.  
- Represents signals as sums of layers with independent time resolution and latent low-rank structure, modeling the raw signal rather than the spectrogram.  
- Supports multi-resolution representations, useful for audio components with different time-frequency resolutions.  
- Derives and clearly presents estimation algorithms and model formulation; paper is well-written and organized.  
- Proposes two estimators: MJLE (iterative shrinkage with Itakura–Saito NMF penalty) and MMLE (marginalization of synthesis coefficients).  
- MMLE is more robust to overfitting but is significantly more computationally expensive and potentially unsuitable for large-scale use.  
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement show effective tonal/transient separation and improved speech quality under noise.  
- Technical approach is sound and mathematically well-justified.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to music and speech; generalization to other signal types is unclear.  
- Contribution is original and significant, with clear potential for a range of audio applications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting comparisons to baselines and additional experiments on other signal types.
