- Proposes LRTFS, a novel generative signal synthesis model using low-rank time-frequency structure that sums layers with different time resolutions and latent low-rank structure.
- Bridges time-frequency synthesis and traditional NMF approaches by modeling the raw signal rather than only the STFT.
- Relates to prior NMF and Gaussian Composite Models (GCMs) used in audio tasks, positioning LRTFS as an advancement over those methods.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with thorough explanations and relation to previous work.
- Strength: extensive evaluation on music decomposition and speech enhancement demonstrating meaningful component decomposition and improved speech quality.
- Weakness: MMLE estimation is not scalable to large signal processing applications.
- Weakness: estimation of the hyperparameter λ is inadequately addressed and could affect performance.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods to better establish contributions and limitations.
- Pro acceptance: novel, well-motivated model addressing a significant gap, with effective experimental validation and clear writing.
- Con acceptance: practical scalability and hyperparameter estimation issues, plus insufficient benchmarking against current state-of-the-art.
- Overall: significant contribution and suitable for publication if MMLE limitations and comparisons are addressed.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
