This paper introduces a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, referred to as Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS). The model fills the gap between traditional Nonnegative Matrix Factorization (NMF) approaches and generative models of the raw signal itself. The authors propose two expectation-maximization algorithms for estimation in the new model and demonstrate its effectiveness in audio signal processing applications, including music decomposition and speech enhancement.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem and the proposed solution, including a detailed description of the LRTFS model and its relation to previous work. The mathematical derivations are sound, and the algorithms are well-explained.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the LRTFS model, which offers a new perspective on signal synthesis. The model is based on a low-rank factorization of the time-frequency transform, which allows for multi-resolution representations. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the model in two experiments: hybrid decomposition of music and speech enhancement.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The introduction of a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure.
* The proposal of two expectation-maximization algorithms for estimation in the new model.
* The demonstration of the effectiveness of the model in audio signal processing applications.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The complexity of the algorithms, which may be challenging to implement in practice.
* The limited experimental evaluation, which only includes two experiments.
* The lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of signal processing and machine learning. The LRTFS model offers a new perspective on signal synthesis, and the proposed algorithms provide a sound framework for estimation. However, further experimental evaluation and comparison with other methods are necessary to fully assess the effectiveness of the model.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of signal processing and machine learning.
* The LRTFS model offers a new perspective on signal synthesis, which may lead to new applications and insights.
* The proposed algorithms provide a sound framework for estimation, which may be useful in practice.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The complexity of the algorithms may be challenging to implement in practice.
* The limited experimental evaluation may not be sufficient to fully assess the effectiveness of the model.
* The lack of comparison with other state-of-the-art methods may limit the impact of the paper.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but encourage the authors to provide additional experimental evaluation and comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
* Suggest that the authors consider simplifying the algorithms to make them more accessible to practitioners.
* Encourage the authors to explore other applications of the LRTFS model, such as multichannel audio source separation.
