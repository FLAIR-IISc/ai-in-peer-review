- Proposes LRTFS, a generative time-domain model that imposes low-rank structure (W H) on variances of complex synthesis coefficients over a time-frequency dictionary.  
- LRTFS closes the gap between NMF/IS-NMF (analysis variance modeling) and a true waveform generative model and supports multiresolution synthesis by stacking frames.  
- Two estimation strategies: MJLE via an EM algorithm with a denoising split trick (practical, avoids large matrix inversions) and MMLE (type-II ML/SBL style) which is principled but computationally heavy.  
- MJLE alternates Wiener-like shrinkage for α and IS-NMF updates for W and H and is computationally practical for long signals.  
- MMLE requires inversion of large matrices and is therefore not used in experiments due to scalability.  
- Demonstrated applications: tonal/transient/music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Strength: conceptually clean generative formulation linking to GCM/IS-NMF and to SBL.  
- Strength: practical MJLE EM algorithm with convincing monotonic decrease and no large matrix inversions.  
- Strength: straightforward, elegant multiresolution extension with joint estimation across layers.  
- Strength: clear link to Wiener reconstruction and efficient posterior splitting across components.  
- Weakness: derivation of the objective (Eq. 11) and the log(|α|^2) term is only sketched and needs a full derivation appendix.  
- Weakness: critical E-step parameter β (and condition β ≤ λ/δΦ) lacks guidance on selection, estimation of δΦ for redundant frames, and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: experiments limited in scope and metrics; SNR alone is insufficient without SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ, and STOI/ESTOI.  
- Weakness: λ is hand-annealed in speech enhancement, schedule unspecified, and joint estimation performed poorly, indicating hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Weakness: baselines are limited; missing comparisons to supervised IS-NMF with Wiener, complex/phase-aware NMF, multiresolution NMF/MCA, and GCM with time-domain reconstruction.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written with clear synthesis vs. analysis distinction and EM presentation.  
- Clarity: notation is dense (switching (f,n) and m) and would benefit from a mapping table and recap of t-f frame properties.  
- Clarity: practical details missing — initialization, stopping criteria, runtime per iteration, redundancy/normalization choices.  
- Originality: novelty lies in modeling low-rank variance on synthesis coefficients and enabling multiresolution synthesis under a probabilistic model, distinct from IS-NMF/GCM and SBL.  
- Originality: connection to EM denoising and Wiener filtering is insightful; low-rank coupling in synthesis domain appears novel.  
- Significance: opens avenues for phase-consistent generative modeling, seamless multiresolution, and extensions to inverse problems and multichannel audio.  
- Significance: empirical evidence is preliminary; stronger, statistically significant gains on standard datasets are needed to bolster impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: neat conceptual bridge to time-domain generative modeling, practical EM algorithm, and broad potential relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited experimental validation, ad hoc hyperparameter tuning, modest improvements over baselines, and underspecified derivations/implementation affecting reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: add full derivation of Eq. (11), provide guidance and ablations for β and λ (including annealing), and report sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions: include stronger baselines (supervised IS-NMF Wiener, complex NMF, MCA, GCM time-domain), report SDR/SIR/SAR/PESQ/STOI with confidence intervals, and give runtime/scalability numbers.  
- Suggestions: show how redundancy and window choice affect δΦ and convergence, and release code and trained dictionaries for reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and technically solid with a practical algorithm, but needs stronger empirical comparisons and clearer hyperparameter guidance.
