- Proposes LRTFS, a novel signal synthesis model linking time-frequency synthesis and NMF by assuming low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances.
- Extends prior Gaussian Composite Model to a generative model of raw signals rather than just STFT.
- Relates to sparse Bayesian learning but differs by tying variances into a low-rank structure.
- Develops two EM-based estimation algorithms, including a maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE) method.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on audio tasks (music decomposition, speech enhancement).
- Introduces a multi-resolution LRTFS to handle multiple time-frequency bases.
- EM algorithms are complex; MMLE requires inversion of a large matrix, increasing computational burden.
- No scalable MMLE algorithm is provided, limiting applicability to large-scale problems.
- More extensive experimental evaluation is needed to fully assess performance across applications.
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and supporting experiments.
- Well-written, well-organized, and uses clear, consistent notation balancing theory and experiments.
- Original contribution that addresses limitations of traditional NMF approaches.
- Potentially significant impact for audio, image processing, and source separation applications.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but urges the authors to address EM algorithm limitations and develop a scalable MMLE in future work.
- Arguments against acceptance focus on algorithmic complexity, scalability concerns, and the need for further experiments.
