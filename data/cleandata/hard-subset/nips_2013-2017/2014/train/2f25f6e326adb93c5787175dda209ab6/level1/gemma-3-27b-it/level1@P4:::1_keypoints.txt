- Introduces LRTFS: model raw signal synthesis coefficients with a low-rank structure, enabling native multi-resolution representations.  
- Proposes two EM estimation algorithms (MMLE and MJLE) for the LRTFS model.  
- Builds on NMF/GCM literature and connects to sparse Bayesian learning and hybrid/morphological decomposition methods.  
- Novelty: moves beyond STFT coefficient modeling to direct signal synthesis — a clear contribution.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a significant advantage over traditional NMF approaches.  
- Technical soundness: derivations and EM algorithm presentation appear mathematically correct and well-explained.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written, motivated, and equations are clearly presented.  
- Experiments: initial results show improvements in music decomposition and speech enhancement versus baselines; supplementary audio is useful.  
- MMLE scalability issue: matrix inversion prevents scaling to practical signal lengths, limiting applicability.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on λ; choice not well-justified and lacks systematic sensitivity analysis; warm-restart needs more explanation.  
- Limited baseline comparisons: should include stronger/state-of-the-art (e.g., deep learning) speech enhancement methods.  
- MJLE theoretical justification: need clearer explanation why MJLE was used experimentally and discussion of trade-offs with MMLE.  
- Lack of ablations: missing studies isolating effects of low-rank constraint and multi-resolution design.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, multi-resolution advantage, promising empirical evidence, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited baselines, and insufficient component/convergence analysis.  
- Overall: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter analysis, baseline comparisons, and MJLE vs MMLE trade-offs.
