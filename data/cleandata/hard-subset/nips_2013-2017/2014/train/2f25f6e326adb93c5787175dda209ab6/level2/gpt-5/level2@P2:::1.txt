Summary
This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative signal model that operates directly in the time domain while imposing a low-rank structure on the variances of complex synthesis coefficients associated with a time-frequency frame (e.g., Gabor). In contrast to prior work such as IS-NMF and the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), which model the magnitude or complex STFT (analysis coefficients), LRTFS places the low-rank prior on synthesis coefficients α and synthesizes the raw signal x via x = Φα + e, with αm ∼ Nc(0, [WH]m). Two estimation strategies are developed: (i) maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) via an EM algorithm that alternates a Wiener-like shrinkage step on α and an IS-NMF update on W,H; and (ii) maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE), akin to SBL type-II ML, which is elegant but presently not scalable for audio-length signals. A key advantage of the synthesis view is a straightforward multi-resolution extension through the union of frames (e.g., short and long windows) with separate low-rank structures, all estimated within the same MJLE framework. Experiments on tonal/transient music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement show modest improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA baselines.

Relation to prior work
- Builds on IS-NMF and its GCM interpretation (Févotte et al.) by moving from an analysis-variance model to a synthesis-variance model.
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning (Tipping; Wipf & Rao): the prior is Gaussian scale with structured (tied) variances v = WH instead of free variances, thus promoting low-rank structure rather than sparsity per se.
- Multi-resolution modeling relates to hybrid/morphological decompositions combining dictionaries (Daudet & Torrésani; Elad et al.); the paper’s contribution is to endow each resolution with a low-rank generative Gaussian model and estimate all parts jointly from the waveform.
- Compared to prior NMF work, the paper argues that multi-resolution is more natural on the synthesis side, though multi-window/feature-concatenation versions of NMF have been explored; situating against such variants would strengthen the positioning.

Quality
Strengths:
- The MJLE EM derivation is sound and computationally practical for large-scale audio (no large matrix inversions; relies on fast analysis/synthesis with Φ and Φ*).
- The objective decomposes neatly as a time-domain data-fit plus an IS divergence penalizing |α|^2 vs WH, with a log(|α|^2) term emerging naturally; the resulting α-update is a simple Wiener-like shrinkage.
- The multi-resolution extension is cleanly integrated and leads to an efficient coupled EM procedure.
- Component reconstruction is handled coherently via posterior averaging with respect to estimated α.

Concerns:
- The MMLE approach, while principled, is not demonstrated due to scalability; alternatives such as iterative linear solvers, matrix-free methods, or approximations (e.g., Hutchinson trace estimators for diag(Σ)) could be discussed more concretely.
- The role of the log(|α|^2) term in CJL is not reflected in the α-update (Eq. 17) due to the EM architecture; a brief justification of why this does not harm the intended regularization would be helpful.
- Estimation/tuning of λ: in one experiment λ is hand-tuned for best SNR; in another it is annealed to near zero. This undermines the generative story and complicates reproducibility and fairness of comparisons.
- Experiments are limited in scope and metrics (SNR only); differences over IS-NMF are modest (e.g., 6.6 vs 6 dB), and no statistical significance, runtime, or ablations (e.g., single vs multi-resolution; effect of K; effect of λ) are provided.
- Identifiability and scale ambiguities (between α and WH; within W/H) are not discussed; constraints or normalization strategies would increase stability.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well written. The connection to GCM and SBL is clear and instructive. The EM updates are explicit.
- Please specify practical choices and sensitivity for β (requires β ≤ λ/δΦ), how δΦ is computed/approximated for redundant frames, and initialization strategies for W,H,α.
- Provide algorithm pseudocode with stopping criteria and complexity per iteration. Report wall-clock times and memory use.
- Minor issues: some typos (e.g., “hit-hat” → “hi-hat”), and several references have incomplete bibliographic details.

Originality
- The core idea—imposing low-rank Gaussian structure on synthesis coefficients and estimating from the waveform—is novel relative to prior NMF/GCM work. The link to SBL with tied variances is insightful.
- The multi-resolution synthesis with low-rank priors per frame is a natural and useful extension; this is a differentiated formulation compared to concatenated-feature NMF or MCA, though a more explicit comparison would help.

Significance
- Conceptually significant: bridges analysis-based factorization and waveform-level generative modeling; yields straightforward multi-resolution modeling and potentially better phase handling.
- Practical significance is promising but not yet conclusively demonstrated: improvements over IS-NMF are modest on a small set; broader evaluation (standard speech enhancement metrics: STOI, PESQ; BSS Eval SDR/SIR/SAR; more noise types; music source separation benchmarks) would strengthen impact.
- The model may influence future work on multichannel separation, inverse problems, and scalable Bayesian learning with structured variances.

Suggestions for improvement
- Strengthen experiments: more datasets, metrics beyond SNR, statistical analysis, and ablations (single vs multi-resolution; varying K; impact of λ schedule). Include audio examples and code.
- Provide fair multi-resolution baselines: (i) IS-NMF on concatenated short/long-window spectrograms, (ii) MCA-style hybrid decompositions, (iii) GCM with multi-window analysis (if feasible).
- Clarify the practical impact of the log(|α|^2) term and whether alternative priors (e.g., complex Laplacian or hierarchical scale mixtures) could yield better sparsity/robustness.
- Discuss normalization/constraints to address scale indeterminacies and improve stability.
- For MMLE scalability, consider iterative solvers for linear systems involving Φ*Φ + λ diag(v)−1, preconditioning exploiting frame structure, and stochastic approximations for diag(Σ).

Pros and cons for acceptance
Pros:
- Clear, principled generative synthesis model with low-rank structure.
- Efficient, simple EM algorithm with Wiener-like shrinkage and standard IS-NMF updates.
- Elegant and practical multi-resolution extension.
- Conceptual bridge between NMF/GCM and SBL.

Cons:
- Limited empirical validation; improvements over baselines are modest and reported only with SNR.
- λ handling is ad hoc; MMLE not demonstrated; runtime and robustness not reported.
- Positioning versus multi-window NMF/MCA baselines could be more thorough.

Overall recommendation
Borderline accept. The conceptual contribution and algorithmic simplicity are compelling and of interest to the NeurIPS community, but the paper would benefit from stronger empirical evidence, clearer treatment of λ, and more comprehensive comparisons.
