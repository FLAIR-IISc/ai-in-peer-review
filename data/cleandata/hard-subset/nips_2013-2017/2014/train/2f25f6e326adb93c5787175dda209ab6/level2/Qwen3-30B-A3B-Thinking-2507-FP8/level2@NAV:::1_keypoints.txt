- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model bridging NMF approaches and raw-signal modeling.  
- Identifies gap that existing GCMs model STFT magnitudes, not raw signals, and proposes a synthesis-based solution.  
- Contribution 1: generative model operating directly on the raw signal with low-rank time-frequency structure.  
- Contribution 2: multi-resolution extension that models components at different temporal resolutions.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear mathematical derivations and link to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Provides two estimation approaches (MJLE and MMLE); MJLE is computationally feasible for audio.  
- Experimental validation on music decomposition and speech enhancement is well-designed.  
- Speech enhancement yields 6.6 dB average SNR improvement, outperforming IS-NMF (6 dB) and OMLSA (4.6 dB).  
- Multi-resolution capability is particularly compelling for separating tonal vs. transient audio components.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized with appropriate citations and comparison to prior work.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of MMLE computational complexity (acknowledged as future work).  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with recent deep learning-based audio decomposition methods.  
- Weakness: evaluation could include perceptual metrics beyond SNR to better assess audio quality.  
- Overall assessment: significant, novel, practically relevant contribution; recommended for acceptance.
