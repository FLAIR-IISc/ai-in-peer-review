- Summary: Proposes LRTFS, a generative time-domain model x = Φα with α∼Nc(0,diag(v)) and v = vec(WH) low-rank; derives MJLE (EM with Wiener-like α updates + IS-NMF for W,H) and MMLE (ARD-style but costly); supports multi-resolution via concatenated frames and shows music decomposition and speech SNR gains.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on IS-NMF/GCM and links to SBL, but departs by tying variances through a low-rank WH and offering a novel synthesis-domain, multi-resolution perspective.

- Quality (model/algorithms): Model and MJLE derivation are technically sound and well-motivated; MMLE is principled but computationally prohibitive at audio scales.

- Quality (experiments): Demonstrates feasibility and some benefits, but evaluation choices (oracle λ tuning, limited baselines, SNR-only metrics) weaken evidence.

- Clarity: Exposition is generally clear with precise notation; needs clarification on β selection, origin/role of the log(|α|^2) term, initialization, per-iteration complexity, runtime, and K selection.

- Originality: Novel idea to impose low-rank structure on synthesis coefficient variances and to extend this to multi-resolution synthesis priors.

- Significance: Promising for audio and inverse problems (phase-consistent, multi-frame synthesis), but current experiments do not yet establish state-of-the-art impact.

- Strength: Clean conceptual bridge from STFT/IS-NMF to a generative time-domain model with a simple, scalable MJLE algorithm.

- Strength: Seamless multi-resolution extension with separate low-rank factorizations per frame and clear ties to SBL/MMLE.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation (small dataset, SNR-only, oracle-informed λ) and insufficient comparisons to stronger or phase-aware baselines.

- Weakness: Lack of ablation studies isolating synthesis- vs analysis-domain benefits and minimal practical guidance on hyperparameters and initialization.

- Weakness: MMLE is not scalable and no concrete scalable approximations are provided.

- Essential suggestion: Use principled λ selection (EM evidence update, cross-validation, or empirical Bayes) and avoid oracle tuning.

- Essential suggestion: Strengthen evaluations with SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ/STOI, runtimes, varied noise types/SNRs, statistical tests, and include a matched GCM/IS-NMF baseline.

- Essential suggestion: Add ablations (single vs multi-resolution, analysis vs synthesis, effect of K and β) and report initialization/convergence diagnostics.

- Nice-to-have: Provide initialization details, convergence plots, explore scalable MMLE approximations (CG, low-rank/diagonal approximations, message passing), and release code/dictionaries for reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Technically solid and elegant approach with promising multi-resolution synthesis modeling, but empirical validation and hyperparameter handling must be strengthened for full NeurIPS impact.
