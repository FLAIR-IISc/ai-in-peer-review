- Proposes LRTFS, a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that directly models the raw signal instead of the STFT.
- Represents signals as sums of atoms with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-resolution representations.
- Presents two estimation algorithms (MJLE and MMLE) using EM; MJLE is computationally efficient by avoiding complex matrix inversions.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks, comparing to IS-NMF and OMLSA.
- Builds on prior work in NMF, GCM, and sparse Bayesian learning, with clear motivation to move beyond STFT-based modeling.
- Provides a well-motivated generative model that enables principled signal processing applications.
- Mathematical formulation and connection to sparse Bayesian learning are clearly presented; EM derivations are well-explained.
- Multi-resolution capability addresses a key limitation of traditional NMF for signals with varying time-frequency characteristics.
- Empirical results are competitive and validate the proposed approach on relevant audio tasks.
- MJLE’s computational efficiency is a practical advantage for scalability and implementation.
- MMLE suffers from scalability issues, limiting practical applicability to larger datasets.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (especially λ) is not thoroughly analyzed; only heuristic scheduling is discussed.
- Baseline comparisons are limited—speech enhancement evaluation relies mainly on OMLSA and lacks broader state-of-the-art comparisons.
- Choice of K (number of latent components) is not justified or discussed, leaving model selection unclear.
- Reproducibility could be improved by providing code or more detailed implementation instructions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach that enables multi-resolution synthesis with promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns with MMLE, limited baseline comparisons, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong and promising contribution that outweighs weaknesses but would benefit from addressing scalability and experimental depth.
- Recommendation: Accept, with encouragement to improve MMLE scalability and include more thorough experimental analyses in future work.
