- Proposes LRTFS, a novel low-rank time-frequency signal synthesis model.  
- Aims to bridge time-frequency synthesis methods with traditional NMF approaches.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to allow multi-resolution representations unavailable in standard NMF.  
- Provides clear organization and explanation of the model, its relation to sparse Bayesian learning, and estimation algorithms.  
- Introduces two estimation algorithms: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement demonstrate the model’s effectiveness.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental support.  
- Writing is clear, notation consistent, and sufficient detail is given for reproduction by expert readers.  
- Original contribution: multi-resolution extension of GCM and novel synthesis formulation.  
- Significance: results show practical utility and model extensibility (e.g., additional penalties on W or H).  
- Pro: Thorough model discussion and experimentally validated performance.  
- Con: MMLE is not scalable due to large-matrix inversion and high computational cost.  
- Con: Lack of comprehensive empirical comparison between MJLE and MMLE for LRTFS.  
- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed MJLE vs MMLE comparison.  
- Suggestion: Investigate and propose scalable/efficient algorithms for MMLE.  
- Suggestion: Explore additional applications such as multichannel audio source separation.
