1. Introduces LRTFS, a generative model extending the Gaussian Composite Model to synthesize raw signals instead of modeling STFT coefficients.  
2. Bridges a gap between NMF-based STFT modeling and direct time-domain signal modeling.  
3. Provides a generative framework enforcing low-rank time-frequency structure for signal synthesis.  
4. Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations, allowing different resolutions for transient and tonal components.  
5. Connects to sparse Bayesian learning while preserving low-rank constraints.  
6. Proposes two EM-based estimation algorithms: MJLE (computationally feasible) and MMLE (theoretically preferable).  
7. MJLE is practical and efficient for audio applications with clear implementation details.  
8. Experimental results (music decomposition, speech enhancement) show meaningful improvements (e.g., 6.6 dB SNR vs 6 dB for IS-NMF and 4.6 dB for OMLSA).  
9. Paper is well-organized, mathematically sound, and well-contextualized within NMF and signal-processing literature.  
10. Strength: addresses raw-signal modeling gap, offers useful multi-resolution capability, efficient MJLE, convincing experiments, and strong contextualization.  
11. Weakness: lacks comparisons to recent deep-learning audio separation methods.  
12. Weakness: preference for MJLE over MMLE is insufficiently justified despite MMLE’s theoretical advantages.  
13. Weakness: experimental analysis of multi-resolution components is too limited to fully validate the claimed benefits.  
14. Weakness: some experimental details (e.g., λ choices, training procedures) are insufficiently described for replication.  
15. Weakness: limited discussion on generalization to other signal-processing domains and broader NIPS community relevance.  
16. Overall recommendation: accept — novel, technically sound, and practically valuable work with scope for broader comparisons and more experimental detail.
