- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel generative model that directly models the raw signal using low-rank TF structure.  
- Builds on the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and contrasts with STFT-based methods.  
- Presents two EM algorithms for estimation, but their exposition is dense and lacks intuitive motivation.  
- Highlights a connection to sparse Bayesian learning, emphasizing differences in variance structure.  
- Introduces a multi-resolution extension, addressing NMF limitations for audio transients.  
- Experimental results (music decomposition and speech enhancement) are promising and comparable or slightly better than IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and lack comprehensive evaluation metrics (e.g., perceptual audio quality) and diverse datasets.  
- Scalability concerns with the MMLE approach are acknowledged but unresolved.  
- The claim of improved accuracy from modeling at the “lowest level” is insufficiently substantiated.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete; more coverage of other time-frequency synthesis methods is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, addresses clear limitation, initial empirical promise, and multi-resolution capability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability, limited validation, dense algorithm description, and insufficient literature context.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—request clearer EM explanations, expanded experiments and metrics, more comprehensive literature review, and discussion of MMLE scaling and future work.
