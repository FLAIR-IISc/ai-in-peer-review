- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a time-domain generative model with synthesis coefficients having Gaussian priors with low-rank variances WH.  
- Bridges NMF of spectrograms (e.g., IS-NMF, GCM) and a generative model of the raw signal, enabling natural multi-resolution representations via multiple TF frames.  
- Derives two estimation strategies: scalable MJLE via an EM algorithm (Figueiredo–Nowak splitting) and a type-II EM (MMLE) similar to SBL but with tied variances enforcing low rank.  
- Experiments (music hybrid decomposition, semi-supervised speech enhancement) show modest but consistent gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Connects clearly to prior work on probabilistic NMF, SBL/ARD, and hybrid/morphological representations.  
- The model is well-posed and the MJLE-EM derivation is careful; using Wiener-type updates inside EM is sound.  
- Multi-resolution extension is technically straightforward and compatible with standard TF toolboxes.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small datasets, only SNR metrics reported, no statistical tests, and λ tuned post hoc (risking optimistic bias).  
- MMLE path is acknowledged but not shown to scale; no complexity or throughput numbers reported.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized, though notation is dense but standard.  
- Important implementation details are missing or under-specified (β choice, δΦ estimation, initialization, stopping criteria, Hermitian symmetry for real signals).  
- Conceptual novelty: shifting from STFT modeling to synthesis-time-domain low-rank TF variance is a meaningful advance distinct from GCM and SBL.  
- The multi-resolution formulation is elegant and the link to IS-NMF/Wiener filtering as first-iteration analogues is insightful.  
- Potentially impactful for audio and broader inverse problems, but current empirical gains (+0.6 dB) are encouraging yet not compelling at scale.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled generative model filling a gap between NMF and time-domain synthesis; scalable EM and multi-resolution extension; solid theoretical grounding.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical scope and metrics, small gains, oracle-like λ tuning, MMLE not demonstrated at scale, missing ablations and runtime analyses.  
- Suggested improvements: add ablations (single vs multi-resolution, MJLE vs IS-NMF, K and λ effects) and additional speech metrics (PESQ/STOI).  
- Suggested improvements: replace oracle λ selection with validation or hierarchical modeling and sensitivity analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: report computational complexity and wall-clock benchmarks, explore scalable MMLE (conjugate gradients, matrix-free solvers, variational approximations).  
- Suggested improvement: release code to improve reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline, leaning accept for the conceptual contribution and practical EM formulation, but requires stronger empirical validation.
