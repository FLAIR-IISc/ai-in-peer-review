- Proposes LRTFS: a time-domain generative model imposing low-rank Gaussian variances on synthesis TF coefficients α and synthesizing x = Φα + e.  
- Develops two estimators: MJLE (practical EM alternating Wiener-like α shrinkage and IS-NMF updates) and MMLE (principled but currently not scalable).  
- Key advantage: natural, jointly estimated multi-resolution extension via unions of frames with separate low-rank structures.  
- Experimental results show modest improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA on tonal/transient music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement.  
- Builds on IS-NMF/GCM by shifting low-rank modeling from analysis to synthesis coefficients.  
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning: uses tied variances v = WH (promotes low-rank structure) rather than independent ARD variances.  
- Relates to multi-resolution/morphological decompositions but differentiates by endowing each resolution with a low-rank Gaussian generative model.  
- MJLE EM derivation is sound, scalable for audio, and avoids large matrix inversions using fast Φ/Φ* operations.  
- Objective neatly decomposes into time-domain fit plus IS divergence on |α|^2 vs WH, yielding a simple Wiener-like α-update.  
- Multi-resolution extension is cleanly integrated and allows an efficient coupled EM procedure.  
- Component reconstruction via posterior averaging is handled coherently.  
- MMLE is principled but not demonstrated due to scalability; more concrete scalable approaches should be discussed.  
- The log(|α|^2) term in the complete-joint likelihood is not reflected in the α-update due to EM; a justification is needed.  
- λ estimation/tuning is ad hoc (hand-tuned or annealed), undermining the generative claim and complicating reproducibility and fair comparison.  
- Experiments are limited: only SNR reported, modest gains (e.g., 6.6 vs 6 dB), no statistical tests, runtimes, or key ablations (single vs multi-res, K, λ).  
- Identifiability and scale ambiguities between α and WH (and within W/H) are not discussed; normalization/constraints should be proposed.  
- Paper is generally well written; connections to GCM and SBL and explicit EM updates are clear.  
- Practical details missing: choices/sensitivity for β, computation/approximation of δΦ for redundant frames, and initialization strategies for W,H,α.  
- Request algorithm pseudocode with stopping criteria, per-iteration complexity, wall-clock times, and memory usage.  
- Minor issues: typos and incomplete bibliographic entries.  
- Originality: novel synthesis-side low-rank Gaussian modeling and insightful link to SBL with tied variances.  
- Significance: conceptually important bridge between analysis factorization and waveform-level generative modeling, but practical impact not yet convincingly demonstrated.  
- Suggested improvements: stronger experiments (more datasets, STOI/PESQ/BSS metrics, statistical analysis), fair multi-resolution baselines, ablations, audio/code release.  
- For MMLE scalability, suggest iterative/matrix-free solvers, preconditioning exploiting frames, and stochastic/Hutchinson approximations for traces/diagonals.  
- Pros: principled generative model, simple efficient EM, elegant multi-resolution extension, bridges NMF/GCM and SBL.  
- Cons: limited empirical validation, ad hoc λ handling, MMLE not demonstrated, insufficient positioning vs multi-window NMF/MCA baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept — conceptually compelling but needs stronger empirical evidence, clearer λ treatment, and broader comparisons.
