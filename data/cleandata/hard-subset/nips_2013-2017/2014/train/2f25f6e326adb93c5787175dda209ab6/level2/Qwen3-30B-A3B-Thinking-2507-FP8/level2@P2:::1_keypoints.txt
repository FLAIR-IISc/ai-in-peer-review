- Introduces LRTFS: models raw signal directly with low-rank time-frequency synthesis, extending GCM beyond STFT representations.  
- Proposes two EM-based estimation algorithms, focusing on the MJLE approach.  
- Key contribution: generative model enabling multi-resolution representations (e.g., separate time resolutions for tonal vs transient components).  
- Multi-resolution capability addresses a limitation of traditional NMF where time resolution is fixed.  
- Well-structured, clearly written paper with strong theoretical grounding and sound mathematical derivations.  
- Properly connects to prior work on GCM and sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement convincingly demonstrate the multi-resolution advantage.  
- Strength: conceptually advances modeling by operating on raw signals rather than STFT.  
- Strength: well-motivated multi-resolution extension with practical audio implications.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical development and relation to existing approaches.  
- Strength: relevant experimental validation with reasonable baselines.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art audio methods (notably modern deep-learning baselines); 0.6 dB gain over IS-NMF is modest.  
- Weakness: computational complexity concerns not adequately addressed or contextualized for large-scale audio (e.g., T ~ 10^6).  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from existing generative signal synthesis methods; connection to prior work like Elad [15] is weak.  
- Suggested improvements: include comprehensive comparisons to recent state-of-the-art methods.  
- Suggested improvements: provide clearer discussion and analysis of computational trade-offs versus existing approaches.  
- Suggested improvements: deeper analysis of multi-resolution benefits beyond the two presented experiments.  
- Conclusion: technically sound and novel but limited significance due to modest empirical gains and lacking comparisons; suitable for NIPS with the recommended improvements.
