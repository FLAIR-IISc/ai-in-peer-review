- Introduces LRTFS, a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that generatively models the raw signal, enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- Extends prior Gaussian Composite Models (GCMs) by moving from STFT-domain generative modeling to raw-signal synthesis.  
- Proposes two EM estimation algorithms (MMLE and MJLE) for the model.  
- Demonstrates approach on music decomposition and speech enhancement, with supplementary sound examples.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, effectively building on NMF and GCM literature.  
- Connects the model to sparse Bayesian learning and highlights a multi-resolution advantage.  
- Mathematical derivations and EM algorithm presentations appear sound and detailed.  
- Novelty: direct synthesis of the raw signal is a significant advance over STFT-based methods.  
- Insight: relationship to sparse recovery techniques (linked to first iteration of a LASSO solver) is valuable.  
- Scalability concern: MMLE is impractical for large-scale problems.  
- MJLE is computationally efficient but sensitive to initialization and prone to local optima; convergence discussion is insufficient.  
- Experimental evaluation is promising but lacks comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art, especially in speech enhancement.  
- Reported SNR improvement (6.6 dB over OMLSA) is not conclusive without broader testing across noise conditions and signals.  
- Hyperparameter tuning (e.g., λ chosen by “best output SNR”) is ad-hoc; the brief explanation for why λ cannot be estimated in EM needs expansion.  
- Minor wording critique: calling the sparsity-inducing term “unexpected” is misleading since it follows naturally from the model.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting more discussion of EM convergence, broader SOTA evaluation, and strategies/acknowledgment of MMLE scalability issues.
