- Proposes LRTFS, a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that generates the raw signal and bridges time-frequency synthesis with NMF.
- Defines LRTFS via equations relating the signal, synthesis coefficients, and a low-rank latent structure.
- Introduces two estimation methods: MJLE (joint likelihood) and MMLE (marginal likelihood), each with an EM algorithm.
- Shows LRTFS as a special case of sparse Bayesian learning with a low-rank constraint on synthesis-coefficient variances.
- Validated on hybrid music decomposition and speech enhancement, demonstrating successful decomposition and improved signal quality.
- Strength: novel model that connects TF synthesis and traditional NMF approaches.
- Strength: provides two estimation approaches and EM implementations.
- Strength: experimental results are promising.
- Weakness: model and estimation methods are complex, potentially hard to implement and understand.
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation may not fully demonstrate effectiveness.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well-written; Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Acceptance arguments: novel contribution, clear exposition, and effective experiments.
- Rejection arguments: methodological complexity and insufficient experimental breadth.
