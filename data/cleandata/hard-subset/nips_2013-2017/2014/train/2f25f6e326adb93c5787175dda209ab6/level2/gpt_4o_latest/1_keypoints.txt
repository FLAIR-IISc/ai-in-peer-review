- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) that models raw audio signals directly, enabling multi-resolution representations.
- Proposes a probabilistic framework with detailed EM derivations and connections to sparse Bayesian learning.
- Presents two estimation methods (MJLE and MMLE), focusing on MJLE for practicality due to MMLE's computational cost.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement, reporting competitive SNR improvements and convincing qualitative results.
- Novelty: significant advancement over traditional NMF by modeling raw signals and supporting multi-resolution analysis.
- Theoretical rigor: solid probabilistic grounding and insightful links to existing Bayesian methods.
- Practical applications: promising results in real-world tasks, showcasing advantages of multi-resolution modeling.
- Clarity of results: well-presented experiments with clear baseline comparisons.
- MMLE scalability: MMLE is theoretically appealing but computationally prohibitive in current form.
- Limited experimental scope: evaluations limited to two tasks and relatively small datasets.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: reliance on manual tuning of λ reduces reproducibility and robustness.
- Comparative analysis gap: lacks benchmarking against modern generative and deep learning audio models.
- Suggestion: develop a scalable MMLE algorithm to fully realize method’s potential.
- Suggestion: expand experiments to diverse datasets, noise types, and deep-learning baselines.
- Suggestion: provide automated or systematic hyperparameter selection and ablation studies.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing MMLE scalability and expanding experimental evaluation in future work.
