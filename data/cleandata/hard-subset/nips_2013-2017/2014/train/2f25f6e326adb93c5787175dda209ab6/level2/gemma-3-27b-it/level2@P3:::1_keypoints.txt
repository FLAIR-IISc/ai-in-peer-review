- Proposes LRTFS: a generative low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that extends GCM to synthesize the raw signal (not just STFT).
- Introduces two EM algorithms for parameter estimation and demonstrates applications to music decomposition and speech enhancement.
- Novel multi-resolution representation enables flexibility beyond traditional NMF settings.
- Builds directly on prior GCM work and connects to matrix factorization and sparse Bayesian learning literature.
- Technical derivations and probabilistic grounding appear sound and logically motivated.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear notation and helpful relation to sparse Bayesian learning.
- Demonstrated results are promising and suggest applicability to synthesis, editing, and source separation.
- MMLE approach is computationally expensive and likely impractical without scalability improvements.
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and lacks broad comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and perceptual metrics.
- Performance is sensitive to the λ hyperparameter, which was tuned empirically without principled selection.
- Multi-resolution extension lacks theoretical guidance on choosing basis functions and resolutions for different signals.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key limitation of existing models, is novel, technically solid, and shows promising initial results.
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited experiments/benchmarks, hyperparameter sensitivity, and weak theoretical guidance for multi-resolution choices.
- Overall assessment: solid and interesting contribution with notable limitations; benefits outweigh drawbacks.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on (1) discussing scalability strategies for MMLE, (2) expanding experimental comparisons and perceptual metrics, (3) providing guidance/theory for multi-resolution selection, and (4) addressing λ sensitivity and estimation methods.
