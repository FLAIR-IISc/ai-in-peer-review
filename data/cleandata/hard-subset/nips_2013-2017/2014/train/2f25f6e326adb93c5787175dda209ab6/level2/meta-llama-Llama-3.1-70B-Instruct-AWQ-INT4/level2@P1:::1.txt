This paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, which bridges the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches. The authors introduce the Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) model, which is a generative model of the raw signal itself, rather than just its Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT). The LRTFS model is defined by a set of equations that describe the relationship between the signal, the synthesis coefficients, and the low-rank latent structure.

The authors provide two estimation methods for the LRTFS model: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE). The MJLE method relies on the minimization of the negative log-likelihood of the joint distribution of the signal and the synthesis coefficients, while the MMLE method relies on the minimization of the negative log-likelihood of the marginal distribution of the signal. The authors also provide an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for each estimation method.

The paper also discusses the relationship between the LRTFS model and sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), and shows that the LRTFS model can be viewed as a special case of SBL with a low-rank constraint on the variance of the synthesis coefficients.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the LRTFS model on two experiments: a hybrid decomposition of music and a speech enhancement problem. The results show that the LRTFS model can successfully decompose the signal into its components and improve the quality of the signal.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel signal synthesis model that bridges the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional NMF approaches.
* The provision of two estimation methods for the LRTFS model, including an EM algorithm for each method.
* The demonstration of the effectiveness of the LRTFS model on two experiments.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The complexity of the LRTFS model and its estimation methods, which may make it difficult to implement and understand for some readers.
* The limited experimental evaluation of the LRTFS model, which may not be sufficient to fully demonstrate its effectiveness.
