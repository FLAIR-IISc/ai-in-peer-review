- Proposes LRTFS: a novel generative low-rank time-frequency synthesis model modeling the raw signal instead of its STFT.  
- Enables multi-resolution representations via low-rank factorization of the time-frequency transform.  
- Introduces two EM-based estimation algorithms: maximum joint likelihood (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood (MMLE).  
- Thoroughly motivates the approach and contrasts it with traditional NMF limitations.  
- Provides detailed comparison and discussion relative to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-structured with accessible mathematical exposition.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness in music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Reports SNR improvements and outperforms IS-NMF and OMLSA in the presented experiments.  
- Strength: clarity and organization in presenting complex concepts.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed time and space computational complexity analysis of the algorithms.  
- Weakness: limited comparison with other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., ICA, sparse coding).  
- Limitation: contribution is significant but not wholly novel, as it builds on prior work.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, clear exposition, strong empirical results, potential for wide adoption.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing complexity analysis, incomplete method comparisons, partial novelty.
