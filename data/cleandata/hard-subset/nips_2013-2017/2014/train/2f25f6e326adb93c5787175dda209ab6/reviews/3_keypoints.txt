- Proposes a flexible time/frequency factorization framework that applies to arbitrary transforms and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.
- Offers a thoughtful interpretation that departs from STFT-based formulations.
- Paper is easy to read and of good quality.
- Originality is above average, but the paper does not fully communicate the significance.
- Results and evaluation are disappointing; supplemental material is unconvincing.
- Lacks empirical comparisons to the existing methods it builds on.
- Theoretical development is solid and interesting, making the paper valuable despite limited experiments.
- Overall a good, well-written paper that would benefit from more results and comparisons.
