- Paper extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal + transient) decompositions and claiming benefits for source separation and denoising.
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model, expanding implicit convolutions into explicit impulse-response expressions and enabling nonorthogonal bases.
- Presents an EM-based local-optimum estimation procedure; reviewer assumes correctness but found the derivation hard to follow in detail.
- Interesting and potentially impactful idea (applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases), but reviewer is unsure of full implications.
- Concern that overcomplete bases should introduce complications; reviewer didn’t see these issues addressed or clarified.
- Clarity is generally good, but evaluation is very thin: only one illustrative result and one SNR measurement, insufficient to support claims.
- Specific evaluation issues: possible unfair comparison (different audio lengths between methods), perceived higher speech quality of baseline (OMLSA), and significant artifacts in music denoising compared to conventional methods.
- Originality limited by heavy reliance on Fevotte et al., though the time-domain reformulation offers a fresh perspective.
- Significance is plausible but unproven due to weak empirical demonstration; current results fail to convincingly show practical importance.
