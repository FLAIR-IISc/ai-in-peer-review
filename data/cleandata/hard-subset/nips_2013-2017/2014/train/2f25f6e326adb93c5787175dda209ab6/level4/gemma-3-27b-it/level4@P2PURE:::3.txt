This paper offers a compelling reinterpretation of time-frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations based on Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) coefficients, it introduces a more general framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly connected to the time-domain signal. 

The paper is well-written and accessible to researchers in the field, demonstrating good overall quality. The originality is above average; however, the significance of the contribution could be more effectively highlighted. A key limitation lies in the presentation of results and evaluation. The supplemental material is not entirely persuasive, and the paper lacks a comparative analysis with the methods upon which it builds. While this does not represent a critical weakness – the theoretical development alone makes this an interesting work – stronger empirical validation would have significantly strengthened the paper. Overall, this is a solid and well-presented paper with a strong theoretical foundation. I would have appreciated a more comprehensive set of results and comparisons, but their absence does not invalidate the interesting theoretical contribution.
