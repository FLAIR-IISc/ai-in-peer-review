This paper offers a compelling and well-considered re-interpretation of time-frequency factorization. Departing from conventional approaches based on Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) analysis coefficients, the authors propose a more general framework applicable to arbitrary transforms while maintaining a direct connection to the underlying time-domain signal. 

The paper is clearly written and accessible to researchers familiar with time-frequency analysis. The theoretical development is of good quality and demonstrates above-average originality. However, the paper’s significance could be more effectively communicated. The primary area for improvement lies in the presentation of results and the evaluation methodology. The supplemental material does not provide particularly strong evidence to support the claims, and importantly, the paper lacks a comparative analysis against existing methods upon which it builds. 

While this omission isn’t a critical weakness – the theoretical contributions are substantial and interesting in their own right – a more thorough empirical validation would significantly strengthen the work. The proposed framework’s advantages would be more convincingly demonstrated through direct comparison to established techniques. 

Overall, this is a good paper with a solid theoretical foundation. I would have appreciated a more extensive set of results and comparative analyses. However, the lack of these elements does not represent a fatal flaw, given the inherent value and novelty of the theoretical contribution.
