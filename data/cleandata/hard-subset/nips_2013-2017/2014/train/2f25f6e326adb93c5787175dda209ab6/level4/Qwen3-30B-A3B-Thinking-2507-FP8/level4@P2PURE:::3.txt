

This paper offers a thoughtful reinterpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations built upon the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it introduces a more flexible framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly linked to the time-domain signal. The paper is accessible to researchers in the field and demonstrates strong quality. While the originality is above average, the significance of the contribution is not fully articulated. The most disappointing aspect concerns the results and evaluation; the supplemental material lacks compelling evidence, and the paper fails to compare its approach to the foundational methods it extends. This is not a significant weakness—the theoretical development alone suffices to render this paper interesting—but stronger empirical validation and comparisons would have substantially improved it. Overall, it is a well-written, solidly theoretical contribution that would benefit from more extensive results and comparisons, though these omissions do not undermine the paper's core theoretical merit.
