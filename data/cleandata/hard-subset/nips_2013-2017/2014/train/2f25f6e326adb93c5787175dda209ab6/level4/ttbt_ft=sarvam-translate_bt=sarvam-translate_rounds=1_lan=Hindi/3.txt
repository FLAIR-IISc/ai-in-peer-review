This paper presents a thoughtful explanation of time/frequency factorization. Instead of using existing formulations based on analysis coefficients for STFT transforms, it offers a more flexible framework that can be applied to any transform and is directly related to the time domain signal.

The paper is easy to read and of good quality for those in the field. The originality is above average, although the significance is not fully explained in the paper. Perhaps the most disappointing part of this paper is in the context of results and evaluation. The supporting material is not particularly convincing, and the paper itself does not compare this work to the methods it is based on. I don't think this is a significant weakness (the theoretical development itself is sufficient to make this paper an interesting one), but I think it would have made the paper much better. Good paper, well-written, solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, but this is not a fatal flaw in this paper because the theoretical contribution is very interesting.
