- Proposes a general, flexible reinterpretation of time-frequency factorizations beyond STFT.  
- Framework applies to arbitrary transforms and links directly to the time-domain signal.  
- Theoretical development is high-quality, clear, and logically presented.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible to researchers in time-frequency analysis.  
- Originality is above average, offering a new perspective on a fundamental problem.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and does not fully realize the paper’s significance.  
- Supplemental material provides some evidence but is not entirely convincing.  
- Lacks comparative analysis against existing STFT-based and related methods.  
- Missing demonstrations of practical advantages (reconstruction quality, efficiency, applicability).  
- Absence of baseline comparisons hinders assessment of practical value.  
- Authors should clarify scenarios where the framework offers distinct benefits.  
- Theoretical soundness and clarity make the paper a valuable foundation for future work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but encourage addressing empirical/comparative concerns in revisions.
