- Proposal: Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to allow arbitrary, potentially overcomplete bases for multiresolution sound decomposition (e.g., tonal + transient).  
- Model foundations: Builds on Févotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and makes explicit the implicit convolution with Gabor impulse responses per spectrogram row.  
- Time-domain reformulation: Presents a purely time-domain formulation adaptable to nonorthogonal and overcomplete impulse-response sets.  
- Inference: Derives an EM procedure that converges to a local optimum.  
- Novelty: The generalization to nonorthogonal and overcomplete bases within a probabilistic matrix-factorization framework is a notable and interesting angle.  
- Technical quality: Technical development and derivations are impressive, though some steps were hard to follow.  
- Unclear implications: Core idea is interesting but practical implications and benefits are not fully clear.  
- Overcompleteness concerns: Overcomplete dictionaries may introduce complications that the paper does not clearly address.  
- Evaluation weakness: Experiments are very limited (one illustrative demo and one SNR plot), insufficient to support strong claims.  
- Audio examples critique: Audio demos did not convincingly showcase the method; comparisons may be unfair (e.g., OMLSA example much longer).  
- Perceptual quality issues: OMLSA output sounded perceptually higher quality despite more background noise; music denoising produced noticeable artifacts where simpler methods might perform better.  
- Significance uncertain: The approach could be impactful in principle, but current sparse empirical evidence prevents assessing practical importance.
