This paper offers a careful rethinking of time–frequency factorizations. Instead of relying on formulations built around STFT analysis coefficients, it introduces a more general synthesis-based framework that can be applied to arbitrary transforms and ties directly to the time-domain signal.

The manuscript is clear, accessible to readers in this area, and of good technical quality. The originality is above average, though the paper does not fully convey the practical significance. The most disappointing aspect is the experimental section: the supplemental material is not particularly persuasive, and the paper does not compare against the methods it builds upon. I do not see this as a critical flaw—the theoretical development is sufficiently interesting to carry the paper—but stronger empirical validation and comparative baselines would notably strengthen it. Overall, this is a good, well-written paper with solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, but their absence is not fatal given the theoretical contribution.
