- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal/transient) decomposition.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model, generalizing the implicit convolution with multiple Gabor impulse responses to a time-domain formulation.  
- Reformulation supports non-orthogonal, overcomplete impulse-response sets and includes an EM estimation procedure.  
- Insight is interesting, but reviewer remains uncertain about the exact implications and potential complications of the extension.  
- Estimation procedure appears correct and analysis is impressive, though difficult to follow in detail.  
- Paper is well explained and motivated, but empirical evaluation is superficial (one illustrative result and a single SNR measurement).  
- Sound examples are provided but unimpressive; the OMLSA comparison seems unfair due to differing clip lengths and background noise.  
- Denoising results show significant artifacts; reviewer suspects conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield higher perceived quality.  
- Originality largely builds on Fevotte’s work, with the time-domain interpretation as the main novel perspective.  
- Potentially significant impact from applying matrix-factorization to overcomplete bases, but thin evaluation and artifacts prevent demonstration of that importance.
