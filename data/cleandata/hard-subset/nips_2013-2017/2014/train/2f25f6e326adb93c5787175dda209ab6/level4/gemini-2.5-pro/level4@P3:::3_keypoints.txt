- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a generative time-domain signal model with synthesis coefficients whose variances follow a low-rank NMF structure (WH).  
- Conceptual novelty: shifts low-rank modeling from analysis (STFT) coefficients to synthesis coefficients of the signal.  
- Estimation methods: derives EM-based MJLE (practical) and MMLE (theoretically appealing but computationally infeasible for target tasks).  
- Multi-resolution capability: naturally supports combining multiple time-frequency bases, addressing single-resolution limitations of spectrogram factorization.  
- Theoretical quality: derivations are sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented.  
- Connection to prior work: properly links the approach to Sparse Bayesian Learning and situates it relative to GCM/IS-NMF.  
- Experimental validation: demonstrates functionality on music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement.  
- Empirical weakness: experiments do not convincingly show superiority over existing methods.  
- Marginal gains: speech enhancement yields only ~0.6 dB SNR improvement over IS-NMF baseline.  
- Evaluation scope: needs broader comparison to state-of-the-art methods to substantiate practical advantage.  
- Clarity and presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written with consistent notation and accessible algorithms.  
- Originality and significance: highly original conceptual contribution with strong theoretical significance and potential to inspire follow-up work.  
- Practical significance limited: unclear if increased model complexity will be adopted without clearer empirical benefits, especially showing multi-resolution advantages.  
- Recommendation: strong accept recommendation based on theoretical contribution despite modest experimental results.
