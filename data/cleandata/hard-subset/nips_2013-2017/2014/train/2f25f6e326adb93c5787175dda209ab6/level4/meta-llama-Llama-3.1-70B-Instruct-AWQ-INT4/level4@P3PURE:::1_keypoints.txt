- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete multiresolution bases (e.g., tonal + transient) for improved decomposition.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model, reformulating it in the time domain via convolutions with multiple Gabor impulse responses.  
- Presents an EM-based analysis/estimation procedure to find a local optimum for the proposed model.  
- The ability to incorporate overcomplete dictionaries is a welcome and interesting addition.  
- Reviewer found the insight interesting but had trouble grasping the full implications and could not follow the detailed estimation analysis.  
- The paper does not clearly address potential complications introduced by overcompleteness or why they are not problematic.  
- Writing and motivation are generally clear.  
- Evaluation is very superficial: a single illustrative result and one SNR measurement are insufficient to support claims.  
- Sound example comparisons may be unfair (OMLSA example is longer and noisier), and reviewer preferred the OMLSA speech quality.  
- Denoising results show significant artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might give better perceived quality.  
- Originality is limited by heavy reliance on Fevotte’s foundation, though the time-domain interpretation offers a new perspective.  
- Potential significance exists but is not demonstrated due to thin evaluation and artifact problems.
