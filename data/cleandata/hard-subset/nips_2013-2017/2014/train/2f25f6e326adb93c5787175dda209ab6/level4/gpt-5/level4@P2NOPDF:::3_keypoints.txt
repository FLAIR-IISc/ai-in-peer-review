- Proposes a thoughtful reformulation of time–frequency factorizations that works with arbitrary transforms and stays directly tied to the time-domain signal.
- Clearly written and accessible to readers in the area.
- Originality is above average.
- Practical significance is not fully articulated.
- Main weakness: results and evaluation are weak; supplementary material is unpersuasive.
- Lacks direct comparisons to the methods it builds upon.
- Strong theoretical development makes the work interesting despite limited empirical validation.
