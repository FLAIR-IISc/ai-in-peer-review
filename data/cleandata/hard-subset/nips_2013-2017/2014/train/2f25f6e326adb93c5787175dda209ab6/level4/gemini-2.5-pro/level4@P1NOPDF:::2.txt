This paper introduces a compelling extension to the Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) framework. Whereas traditional NMF is based on a generative model of a signal's time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients, the proposed method models the time-domain signal directly through a generative model of its TF synthesis coefficients. This novel formulation is particularly interesting because it enables multi-resolution representations, a significant capability that is not possible within the standard NMF setting. I find the idea of the multi-resolution extension to be a particularly valuable contribution.

While the authors present results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, the paper would be significantly strengthened by also evaluating the proposed method in the single-resolution case. A direct comparison to the well-established Itakura-Saito NMF (IS-NMF) is needed to properly contextualize its performance. Specifically, how does the proposed method perform against IS-NMF in a single-resolution setting? Does it yield similar basis (W) and activation (H) matrices? Furthermore, how does the proposed algorithm's computational speed and numerical stability compare to the standard multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF?
