- Generalizes low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) bases for multiresolution decomposition (e.g., tonal + transient).  
- Reinterprets Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model implicit convolution as a time-domain expression, enabling arbitrary/nonorthogonal impulse responses.  
- Introduces an EM-based estimation procedure that converges to a local optimum.  
- Insightful perspective but implications need clearer exposition; heavy reliance on Fevotte limits perceived novelty.  
- Overcomplete bases introduce potential complications that require deeper analysis.  
- Estimation procedure seems theoretically sound but technical details are not fully transparent.  
- Core methodology is well motivated and clearly explained.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative example and a single SNR metric, insufficient for strong conclusions.  
- Audio examples are helpful but unconvincing; OMLSA comparison is unfair due to different clip lengths and tradeoffs in noise vs. speech quality.  
- Music denoising results show severe audible artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceived quality.  
- Originality is modest: time-domain reinterpretation is novel but not a major departure from prior work.  
- Significance is unsubstantiated: promising potential for overcomplete bases but currently mainly a theoretical reformulation without compelling empirical validation.
