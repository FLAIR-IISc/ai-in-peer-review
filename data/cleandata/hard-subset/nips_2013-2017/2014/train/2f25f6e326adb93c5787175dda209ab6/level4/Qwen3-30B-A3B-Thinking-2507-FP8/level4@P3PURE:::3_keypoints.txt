- Proposes a flexible reinterpretation of time/frequency factorizations that goes beyond STFT-based formulations and applies to arbitrary transforms while preserving a direct link to the time-domain signal.
- Strong theoretical development with above-average originality and solid, clearly written exposition accessible to researchers.
- Practical significance and real-world impact of the proposed framework are not clearly articulated.
- Evaluation is the primary weakness: experimental section is weak and supplemental material lacks sufficient empirical validation.
- Fails to compare the proposed methodology against the foundational approaches it extends, missing important comparative analysis.
- Despite empirical shortcomings, the theoretical contribution is substantial and makes the paper interesting.
