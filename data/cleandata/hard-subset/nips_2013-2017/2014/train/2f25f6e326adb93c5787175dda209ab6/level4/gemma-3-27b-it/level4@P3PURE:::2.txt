This paper presents an interesting approach to Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) by shifting the generative model from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the time-domain signal itself, via a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This allows for multi-resolution representations, a capability not readily available in traditional NMF settings. I find the multi-resolution extension particularly appealing.

The authors demonstrate results on multi-resolution signal decomposition. However, I am curious about the method’s performance in the single-resolution case, specifically in comparison to Iterative Shrinkage NMF (IS-NMF). Do the resulting W and H matrices exhibit similar characteristics? Furthermore, is the proposed algorithm comparable to the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF in terms of speed and stability? To reiterate the core contribution, while standard NMF models the TF analysis coefficients, this work models the time-domain signal directly through TF synthesis coefficients, thereby enabling multi-resolution analysis.
