The standard NMF framework models the TF analysis coefficients of a signal, whereas the proposed approach models the time-domain signal directly by specifying a generative model for the TF synthesis coefficients. This shift enables multi-resolution representations that were not achievable in the traditional NMF setting.

This is an interesting paper; I particularly like the multi-resolution extension.

While the authors present some results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, I would also like to see performance in the single-resolution case, compared with IS-NMF. Does the method produce W and H similar to those from IS-NMF? Is the proposed algorithm as fast and as stable as the multiplicative-update algorithm for IS-NMF?
