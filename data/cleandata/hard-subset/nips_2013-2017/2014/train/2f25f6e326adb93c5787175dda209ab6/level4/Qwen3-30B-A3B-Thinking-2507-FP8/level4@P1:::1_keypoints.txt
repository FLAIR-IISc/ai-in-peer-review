- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), extending GCM to directly model raw audio in time domain.
- Represents signals as sums of components with low-rank time-frequency structure, enabling direct synthesis.
- Proposes two EM estimation algorithms and focuses on a computationally feasible MJLE implementation.
- Supports multi-resolution representations (e.g., separate tonal and transient components) and handles non-orthogonal bases.
- Provides a mathematically rigorous inversion of STFT-based GCM with clear relation to sparse Bayesian learning.
- Technical derivations are sound, well-supported, and reproducible for experts.
- MMLE implementation is not scalable for audio; authors acknowledge and concentrate on MJLE for practicality.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; notation and exposition are consistent.
- Connection between the multi-resolution formulation and practical implementation could be clearer.
- Originality is incremental but meaningful: a natural time-domain extension of GCM with multi-resolution capability.
- Potential significance is substantial for audio processing, especially transient/tonal separation.
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only illustrative music decomposition and one speech enhancement test set.
- Reported gains are marginal (e.g., 6.6 dB vs 6 dB for IS-NMF), insufficient to prove practical advantage.
- Comparisons to baselines lack detail and may be inconsistent (e.g., differing signal lengths for OMLSA).
- No discussion or analysis of computational complexity for large-scale audio applications.
- Perceptual artifacts in the music example are noted but not adequately addressed.
- Overall: technically interesting and promising, but lacks sufficient empirical evidence to demonstrate practical impact.
