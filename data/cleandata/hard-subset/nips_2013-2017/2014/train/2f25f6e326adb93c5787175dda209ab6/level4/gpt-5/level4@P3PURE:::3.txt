This paper offers a thoughtful reinterpretation of time–frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations built on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it proposes a more flexible framework that can be applied to arbitrary transforms and is directly connected to the time-domain signal.

The paper is clearly written for readers in this area and is of good quality. The originality is above average, although the paper does not fully communicate its significance. The most disappointing aspect lies in the results and evaluation: the supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the main text does not compare the proposed approach to the methods on which it builds. I do not view this as a fatal weakness—indeed, the theoretical development alone makes the paper interesting—but these omissions limit the empirical impact and make the contribution feel less fully substantiated than it could be.

Overall, this is a good paper: well written, with solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and direct comparisons, which would substantially strengthen the work, but the theoretical contribution is sufficiently compelling that the lack of empirical depth is not a decisive flaw.
