- Novelty: Proposes a generative NMF framework that models time-domain signals via TF synthesis coefficients instead of TF analysis coefficients.
- Main contribution: This synthesis-based reformulation naturally enables multi-resolution signal representations not easily achievable with traditional NMF.
- Strength: Core idea is elegant and powerful, and initial multi-resolution results are compelling.
- Major gap: Lacks thorough evaluation in the single-resolution setting to compare with established baselines.
- Suggestion 1: Include a direct comparison with Itakura-Saito NMF (IS-NMF) in single-resolution to establish a performance baseline.
- Suggestion 2: Analyze factor similarity—compare learned W and H to those from IS-NMF to see if dictionaries and activations align.
- Suggestion 3: Report computational performance—runtime, convergence speed, and numerical stability versus IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates.
- Overall: Promising research direction that would be significantly strengthened by more comprehensive empirical comparisons and analyses.
