This paper offers a nuanced exploration of time/frequency factorizations, diverging from conventional approaches that rely on analysis coefficients of STFT transforms. Instead, it proposes a more adaptable framework applicable to arbitrary transforms, with a direct connection to the time-domain signal.

The manuscript is well-written and accessible to experts in the field, showcasing good quality and above-average originality. However, the significance of the work could be more effectively conveyed. A notable limitation lies in the results and evaluation sections, where the supplementary material falls short of being persuasive. Moreover, the paper does not provide a comparison with the methods it builds upon. Although this omission is not a critical weakness, as the theoretical development is substantial enough to make the paper engaging, it would have significantly enhanced the manuscript. Overall, the paper is well-crafted, with solid theoretical foundations, but would have benefited from more comprehensive results and comparisons to solidify its impact.
