

This paper offers a thoughtful reinterpretation of time-frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations built upon STFT analysis coefficients, it introduces a more flexible framework applicable to arbitrary transforms while maintaining direct linkage to the time-domain signal. The presentation is clear and accessible to researchers in the field, and the theoretical development is sound. The originality is above average, though the paper does not clearly articulate the significance of its contribution. The most substantial weakness lies in the results and evaluation: the supplemental material fails to convincingly demonstrate the approach's advantages, and the paper lacks comparative analysis against the foundational methods it extends. While this omission is not a major flaw—given the strength of the theoretical contribution—the work would have been significantly strengthened by including such comparisons. The paper itself is well-written, with solid theoretical foundations, and the core ideas are compelling. I recommend acceptance, as the theoretical insight alone makes this a valuable contribution to the field.
