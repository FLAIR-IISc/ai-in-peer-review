This paper presents a novel Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) framework that, distinct from traditional approaches which model time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients, instead models the time-domain signal directly through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This key difference enables the exploration of multi-resolution representations, a capability not inherent in standard NMF. 

The work is compelling, and the potential for multi-resolution analysis is particularly promising. 

However, in addition to the presented multi-resolution decompositions, I would be interested to see a performance comparison against Iterative Shrinkage-NMF (IS-NMF) in a single-resolution setting. Specifically, do the resulting W and H matrices exhibit similar characteristics? Furthermore, an evaluation of the algorithm's speed and stability relative to the well-established multiplicative update rules used for IS-NMF would be valuable. To reiterate the core innovation, the proposed framework shifts the generative modeling focus from TF analysis coefficients – as is typical in NMF – to the time-domain signal itself via a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients, thereby facilitating multi-resolution analysis previously unattainable with traditional NMF.
