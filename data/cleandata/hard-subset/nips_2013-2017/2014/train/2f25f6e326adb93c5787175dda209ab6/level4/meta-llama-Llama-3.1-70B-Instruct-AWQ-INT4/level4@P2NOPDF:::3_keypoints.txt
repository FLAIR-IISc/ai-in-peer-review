- Proposes a thoughtful, novel reinterpretation of time/frequency factorizations that departs from STFT-based analyses.  
- Introduces a flexible framework applicable to arbitrary transforms with a direct link to the time-domain signal.  
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and demonstrates good overall quality.  
- Originality is above average, but the significance of the contributions is not communicated as effectively as it could be.  
- Main shortcoming: results and evaluation are weak and the supplemental material is unconvincing.  
- Fails to compare the proposed method against existing approaches it builds upon.  
- Theoretical development is substantial and intriguing, making the paper interesting despite evaluation gaps.  
- With more thorough evaluation and direct comparisons to prior methods, the paper would be significantly stronger.
