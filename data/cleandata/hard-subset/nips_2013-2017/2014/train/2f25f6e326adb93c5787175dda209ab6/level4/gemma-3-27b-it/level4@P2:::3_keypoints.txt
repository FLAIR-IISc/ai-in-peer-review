- Novelty: Proposes a synthesis-based time-frequency factorization operating on the time-domain signal and arbitrary transforms, a significant perspective shift from STFT-analysis methods.
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and accessible to researchers familiar with signal processing and matrix factorization.
- Theoretical quality: Strong, well-developed, and apparently rigorous mathematical foundation.
- Originality: Above-average originality, providing a compelling alternative to traditional methods.
- Multi-resolution: Naturally supports a multi-resolution framework, useful for applications needing adaptive time-frequency resolution (e.g., audio transients).
- Significance underplayed: The paper does not fully articulate broader applications or the potential impact of the method.
- Limited empirical evaluation: Experiments are illustrative but not comprehensive; supplemental material lacks sufficient detail.
- Missing comparisons: No direct comparison to relevant baselines (e.g., Gaussian Composite Models), so performance advantage is unestablished.
- Overall assessment: Solid and interesting theoretical contribution, recommended for acceptance despite empirical shortcomings.
- Recommended revisions/future work: Prioritize more rigorous empirical validation and clearer articulation of advantages over existing methods.
