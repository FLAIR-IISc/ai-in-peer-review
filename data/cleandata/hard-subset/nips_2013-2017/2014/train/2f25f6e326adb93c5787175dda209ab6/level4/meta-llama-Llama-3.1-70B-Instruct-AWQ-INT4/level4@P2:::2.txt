This paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, which fills the gap between traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches and generative models of the raw signal itself. The authors introduce a low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model that allows for multi-resolution representations, which is not possible in traditional NMF settings.

The LRTFS model is defined by a set of equations that describe the generative process of the signal. The model is an extension of the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and is related to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL). The authors present two expectation-maximization (EM) algorithms for estimation in the LRTFS model: maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE). The MJLE algorithm is computationally friendly and does not require matrix inversion, while the MMLE algorithm is more robust but has a higher computational complexity.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the LRTFS model on two experiments: music decomposition and speech enhancement. The results show that the proposed method can successfully decompose signals into their constituent components and achieve better performance than traditional NMF approaches.
