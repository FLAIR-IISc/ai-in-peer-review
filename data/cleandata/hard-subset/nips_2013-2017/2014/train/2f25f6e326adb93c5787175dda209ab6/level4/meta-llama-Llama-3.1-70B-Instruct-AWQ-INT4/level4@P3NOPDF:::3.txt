This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations, offering a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time domain signal. Unlike existing formulations that rely on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, this approach provides a more comprehensive understanding of the underlying concepts.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers familiar with the subject area. The quality of the paper is good, and the originality of the ideas presented is above average. However, the significance of the work could be more effectively communicated to the reader. One of the most disappointing aspects of the paper is the lack of convincing results and evaluation. The supplemental material does not provide strong evidence to support the claims made in the paper, and the authors fail to compare their work to existing methods that it builds upon.

Although this omission is not a significant weakness, it would have strengthened the paper considerably. The theoretical development presented in the paper is substantial and makes for an interesting read. Nevertheless, the inclusion of more results and comparisons would have enhanced the overall quality of the paper. Despite this limitation, the theoretical contribution is noteworthy, and the paper remains a valuable addition to the field.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept, but encourage the authors to provide more comprehensive results and comparisons to existing methods in future work.
