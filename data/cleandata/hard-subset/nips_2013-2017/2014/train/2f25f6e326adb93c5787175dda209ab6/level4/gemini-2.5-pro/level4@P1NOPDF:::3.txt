This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time-frequency factorizations. In contrast to existing formulations built on the analysis coefficients of the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT), this work introduces a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written, clear, and should be accessible to researchers in this field. The theoretical contribution is solid and demonstrates a good degree of originality. However, the broader significance of the work is not fully communicated.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in its experimental results and evaluation. The empirical evidence provided in the supplementary material is not particularly convincing, and the paper lacks a direct comparison against the foundational methods it builds upon.

Despite this, the shortcomings in the evaluation should not be considered a fatal flaw. The theoretical development is the paper's core strength and is compelling enough on its own to make this an interesting contribution. A more thorough experimental section would have significantly strengthened the submission, but its absence does not negate the value of the work.

In summary, this is a good paper with a strong theoretical foundation. While more extensive results and comparisons would be welcome, the novelty of the proposed framework makes it a valuable contribution.
