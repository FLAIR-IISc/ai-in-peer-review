This paper presents a thoughtful framework for time/frequency factorizations. In contrast to existing formulations built on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, the proposed approach offers greater flexibility, as it can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written and clear for readers familiar with the field. While the work possesses above-average originality, its broader significance is not fully communicated. The primary weakness lies in the experimental evaluation. The results in the supplementary material are not particularly convincing, and the paper fails to compare the proposed method to the prior work on which it builds. This is not a fatal flaw, as the theoretical development is strong enough to make this an interesting contribution. Nevertheless, the paper would have been substantially improved by the inclusion of more comprehensive results and comparisons. In summary, this is a good paper with a solid theoretical contribution that would benefit from a more thorough evaluation.
