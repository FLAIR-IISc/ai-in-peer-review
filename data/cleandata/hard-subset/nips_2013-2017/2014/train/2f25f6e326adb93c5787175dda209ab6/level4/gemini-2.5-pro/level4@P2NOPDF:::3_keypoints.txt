- Introduces a novel, flexible framework for time-frequency factorization that generalizes beyond STFT-based formulations.  
- Operates directly on the time-domain signal and can be applied to arbitrary transforms.  
- Represents a thoughtful reinterpretation of the time-frequency factorization problem.  
- Main strength is a strong, original theoretical contribution and solid foundational development.  
- Paper is well-written and technically clear for experts.  
- Theoretical results alone are significant and of interest to the community.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and underdeveloped, weakening practical validation.  
- Empirical evidence (including supplemental material) is not sufficiently convincing.  
- Critically lacks direct comparisons to STFT-based baselines, making practical advantages unclear.  
- Conclusion: high-quality and novel work, but would be much stronger with rigorous experiments and direct comparisons to established methods.
