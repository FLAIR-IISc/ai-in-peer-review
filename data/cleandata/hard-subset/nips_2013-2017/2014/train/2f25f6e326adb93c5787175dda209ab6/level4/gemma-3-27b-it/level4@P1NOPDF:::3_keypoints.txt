- Proposes a thoughtful, flexible interpretation of time-frequency factorizations applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly tied to the time-domain signal.  
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and of good overall quality.  
- Originality is above average, but the paper does not fully convey the significance of its contribution.  
- Main weakness is weak empirical results and evaluation.  
- Supplemental material is not particularly compelling.  
- Lacks comparisons to the prior methods it builds upon, which would have strengthened the work.  
- Theoretical development is interesting and has solid foundations.  
- Reviewer would have liked more results and comparative analyses, though their absence doesn't invalidate the theoretical contribution.
