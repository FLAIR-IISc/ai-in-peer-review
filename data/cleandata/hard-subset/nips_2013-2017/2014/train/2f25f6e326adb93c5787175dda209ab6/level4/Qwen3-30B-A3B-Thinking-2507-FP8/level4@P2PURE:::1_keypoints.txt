- Introduces an extension of low-rank spectrogram-factorization models that generalizes latent factors over arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) bases.  
- Enables decomposition with multiresolution bases (e.g., separate tonal and transient components) for improved source separation and denoising.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) for modeling spectrogram variances via low-rank factorization.  
- Reformulates the implicit convolution with multiple Gabor impulse responses into a purely time-domain expression for the estimated signal.  
- Reformulation permits adaptation to arbitrary impulse response sets, including nonorthogonal and overcomplete dictionaries.  
- Supports an EM-based optimization procedure derived from the time-domain reinterpretation.  
- Insightful and novel time-domain perspective, but the precise implications and benefits need clearer clarification.  
- The ability to use overcomplete dictionaries is valuable, yet potential complications (e.g., instability, redundancy) are inadequately addressed.  
- The estimation/analysis procedure appears correct and analytically impressive, but its details remain somewhat opaque.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly explained in presentation.  
- Empirical evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement metric, insufficient for strong conclusions.  
- Audio examples are unconvincing; comparisons may be unfair due to differing clip lengths (OMLSA 7s vs proposed 2s).  
- Despite more background noise, OMLSA yields higher perceived speech quality in the provided example.  
- Music denoising results show severe artifacts, suggesting traditional methods (e.g., wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might produce better perceptual quality.  
- Originality is limited by heavy reliance on Fevotte et al., though the time-domain reinterpretation is a fresh contribution.  
- Potential significance in enabling matrix-factorization for overcomplete/multiresolution bases is promising but currently unsubstantiated due to thin evaluation and artifacts.
