- Introduces LRTFS: a generative model that moves probabilistic modeling from STFT analysis coefficients to synthesis coefficients with a low-rank (NMF-like) latent structure.  
- Models the raw time-domain signal directly, contrasting with GCM which imposes low-rank structure on STFT variances.  
- Naturally supports multi-resolution time-frequency representations, addressing the single-window trade-off in spectrogram factorization.  
- Derives two EM-based parameter estimation methods: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- Strength — originality/quality: elegant, novel theoretical formulation linking synthesis-based models and NMF, with clear ties to Sparse Bayesian Learning.  
- Strength — clarity: well-written and organized; motivation and derivations are easy to follow for the target audience.  
- Strength — significance: multi-resolution capability is practically valuable for audio and other signals with diverse time-frequency content.  
- Weakness — evaluation: experimental section is weak and does not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Weakness — lacks direct empirical comparison to GCM, making claimed improvements difficult to verify.  
- Weakness — unconvincing results: modest 0.6 dB gain in speech enhancement and illustrative music demo lacks objective metrics.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter handling: λ is set manually and joint estimation reportedly performs poorly, limiting autonomy and practicality.  
- Recommendation: accept for strong theoretical contribution and potential impact, but authors should substantially strengthen experimental validation and comparisons in future revisions.
