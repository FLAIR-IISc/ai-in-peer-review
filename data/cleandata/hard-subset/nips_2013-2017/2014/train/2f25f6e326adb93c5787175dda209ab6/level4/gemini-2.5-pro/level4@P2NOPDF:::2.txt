This paper introduces a novel and interesting variant of Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF). Unlike conventional NMF frameworks that employ a generative model for the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients of a signal, the proposed method formulates a generative model for the TF synthesis coefficients, thereby modeling the time-domain signal directly. This is a significant conceptual shift that elegantly enables multi-resolution representations, which are not naturally supported in traditional NMF settings. The multi-resolution extension is a particularly compelling contribution.

While the authors present valuable results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, the paper would be significantly strengthened by including a performance comparison against an established baseline in the simpler, single-resolution case. A direct comparison with Itakura-Saito NMF (IS-NMF), for example, would be highly informative for contextualizing the proposed method's behavior and performance. In such a comparison, I would be curious to learn:

1.  Do the learned basis (W) and activation (H) matrices qualitatively resemble those obtained from a standard IS-NMF decomposition?
2.  How does the proposed algorithm's computational performance, specifically its convergence speed and numerical stability, compare to the well-established multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF?

Addressing these points would provide a clearer understanding of the trade-offs and advantages of this new formulation, even in settings where multi-resolution is not strictly required.
