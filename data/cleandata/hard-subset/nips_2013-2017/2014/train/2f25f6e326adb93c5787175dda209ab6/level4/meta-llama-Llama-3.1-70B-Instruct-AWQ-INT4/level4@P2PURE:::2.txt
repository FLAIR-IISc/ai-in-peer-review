The proposed framework offers a distinct departure from the regular Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) framework by considering a generative model of the time-domain signal itself. Specifically, it describes a generative model of Time-Frequency (TF) synthesis coefficients, as opposed to the traditional NMF framework, which models TF analysis coefficients. This shift in perspective enables the exploration of multi-resolution representations that were previously inaccessible within the traditional NMF setting.

This paper presents an intriguing idea, and the concept of a multi-resolution extension is particularly compelling. The authors demonstrate the potential of their approach through results on multi-resolution signal decompositions.

However, to comprehensively evaluate the proposed method, it would be beneficial to assess its performance in the single-resolution case and compare it with Iterative Shrinkage NMF (IS-NMF). Specifically, it would be valuable to investigate whether the proposed method yields similar W and H matrices as IS-NMF and whether its algorithm is equally fast and stable as the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF.
