- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel generative model that directly models the raw time-domain signal.
- Bridges a gap between prior GCM approaches and NMF-based methods by explicitly connecting the time-domain signal to low-rank time-frequency structure.
- Technically sound and well-structured with clear theoretical development building on prior work.
- Establishes a solid connection to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), highlighting key similarities and differences in variance structure.
- Derives two estimation approaches (MJLE and MMLE) and extends the model to multi-resolution representations.
- Correctly notes practical limitations of MMLE for large-scale audio applications.
- MJLE is well-motivated and implemented efficiently without requiring matrix inversions.
- Clarity of exposition is excellent, with appropriate notation, explanations, figures, and supplementary material to aid reproducibility.
- Originality is significant, particularly the shift from STFT modeling to raw-signal modeling and the novel multi-resolution extension.
- Multi-resolution capability addresses limitations of traditional NMF that cannot adapt time resolution across components.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and insufficiently comparative across state-of-the-art baselines.
- Lacks benchmarking against relevant methods (e.g., IS-NMF, hybrid decomposition [8,10], OMLSA) and sufficient quantitative results in supplemental material.
- Strong theoretical contribution could stand alone, but limited experiments diminish practical impact.
- Strengths: strong theoretical foundation, clear exposition, and novel multi-resolution capabilities.
- Weaknesses: limited experimental validation and lack of comprehensive comparisons; authors should address these to maximize impact.
- Recommendation: good fit for NIPS given theoretical novelty, provided experimental shortcomings are addressed in revision.
