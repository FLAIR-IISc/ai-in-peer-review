The paper shifts from the usual NMF perspective—where one models the time–frequency (TF) analysis coefficients—to a framework that models the time-domain signal directly via a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This change of viewpoint naturally enables multi-resolution representations that are not available in the traditional NMF setting.

Overall, this is an interesting paper, and I particularly appreciate the idea of the multi-resolution extension. The presented results on multi-resolution signal decompositions are compelling.

I would, however, like to better understand the method’s behavior in the single-resolution setting:
- How does its performance compare to IS-NMF in that case?
- Does it recover similar W and H?
- Is the proposed algorithm as fast and stable as the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF?
