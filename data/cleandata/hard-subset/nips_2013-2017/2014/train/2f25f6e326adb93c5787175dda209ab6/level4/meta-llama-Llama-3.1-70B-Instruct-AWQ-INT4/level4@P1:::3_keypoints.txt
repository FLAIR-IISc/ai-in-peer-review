- Proposes a thoughtful, flexible time/frequency factorization framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly linked to the time-domain signal.
- Paper is easy to read, well-organized, and of good quality; authors demonstrate strong understanding of the topic.
- Originality is above average, but the significance of the contribution is not fully communicated in the manuscript.
- Theoretical development is solid and interesting, making the paper worthwhile even without strong empirical results.
- LRTFS bridges time-frequency synthesis and traditional NMF, enabling multi-resolution representations that traditional NMF cannot.
- Estimation procedures (including the EM algorithm) are clearly explained and appropriate for the problem.
- Results and evaluation are weak: the main paper lacks comprehensive experiments and comparisons to existing methods.
- Supplemental material is not particularly convincing and does not compensate for the missing comparisons in the paper.
- The lack of thorough empirical comparisons makes it difficult to fully assess the practical significance of the proposed model.
- Recommendation: accept, but the authors should provide more comprehensive results and comparisons to strengthen the paper.
