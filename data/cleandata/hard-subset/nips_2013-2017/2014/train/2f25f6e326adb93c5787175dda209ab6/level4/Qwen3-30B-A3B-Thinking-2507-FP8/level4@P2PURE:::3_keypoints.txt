- Reinterprets time/frequency factorizations with a new framework not limited to STFT analysis coefficients.  
- Introduces a flexible approach applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly tied to the time-domain signal.  
- Paper is accessible and well written for researchers in the field.  
- Theoretical quality is strong and the work is interesting on theoretical grounds.  
- Originality is above average, but the significance and impact of the contribution are not fully articulated.  
- Empirical results and evaluation are weak and disappointing.  
- Supplemental material fails to provide compelling supporting evidence.  
- The paper does not compare its approach to the foundational methods it extends.  
- Stronger empirical validation and comparisons would substantially improve the paper.  
- Omissions in results do not undermine the core theoretical merit, but the paper would benefit from more extensive experiments.
