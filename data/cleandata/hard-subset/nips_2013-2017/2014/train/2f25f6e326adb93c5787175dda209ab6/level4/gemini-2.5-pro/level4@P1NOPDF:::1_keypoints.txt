- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization (GCM) by generalizing latent factors to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete bases for multiresolution decomposition.  
- Derives a time-domain formulation of the STFT convolution, enabling use of arbitrary impulse-response bases (including non-orthogonal/overcomplete).  
- Proposes an EM algorithm to obtain a locally optimal solution for the generalized model.  
- Core insight is interesting and the analytical derivation is impressive, though full implications are not immediately clear.  
- The ability to incorporate overcomplete dictionaries is valuable, but the paper does not fully address complications introduced by overcompleteness.  
- EM-based estimation appears sound despite some difficulty following the derivations in detail.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Evaluation is superficial: based on a single illustrative result and one SNR improvement, making conclusions weak.  
- Sound examples are provided but are not particularly convincing.  
- Baseline comparison fairness is questionable: OMLSA-enhanced baseline example is much longer (~7s vs ~2s) than the proposed method’s example.  
- OMLSA baseline has more residual noise but perceptually higher-quality speech than the proposed method.  
- Music denoising by the proposed method introduces significant audible artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceived quality.  
- Novelty lies in the time-domain reinterpretation of GCM; significance is promising theoretically but undermined by thin evaluation and noticeable artifacts.
