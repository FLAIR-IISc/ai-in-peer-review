The proposed framework presents a novel extension of Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) by considering a generative model of the time-domain signal itself, in contrast to the traditional NMF framework, which models the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients of a signal. This is achieved by describing a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients, allowing for multi-resolution representations that were previously unattainable in the traditional NMF setting.

I find this paper to be interesting, particularly due to the introduction of the multi-resolution extension, which I believe has significant potential. The authors provide some results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, which are promising. However, I am also curious to see the performance of the proposed method in the single-resolution case, compared to IS-NMF. Specifically, I would like to know whether the proposed method yields similar W and H matrices, and whether the algorithm is as fast and stable as the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF.

The key insight of the proposed framework is that it models the time-domain signal directly, rather than its TF analysis coefficients. This fundamental shift enables the exploration of multi-resolution representations, which is a significant departure from the traditional NMF framework. Overall, the paper presents an intriguing idea, and I look forward to seeing further developments and evaluations of the proposed method.
