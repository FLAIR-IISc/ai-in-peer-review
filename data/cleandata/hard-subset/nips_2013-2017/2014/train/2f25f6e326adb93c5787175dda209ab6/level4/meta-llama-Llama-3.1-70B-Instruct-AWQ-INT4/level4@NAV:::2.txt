Here's a paraphrased version of the review:

The proposed framework diverges from the conventional NMF approach by shifting focus from a generative model of time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to a generative model of the time-domain signal itself, achieved through TF synthesis coefficients. This innovation enables exploration of multi-resolution representations, previously unattainable within the traditional NMF framework.

This paper presents an intriguing concept, with the multi-resolution extension being a particularly compelling aspect. While the authors demonstrate promising results in multi-resolution signal decompositions, it would be valuable to investigate the proposed method's performance in the single-resolution case, comparing it to IS-NMF. Specifically, it would be interesting to examine whether the proposed algorithm yields similar W and H matrices, and whether it maintains the speed and stability of the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF.

The distinction between the proposed framework and the traditional NMF approach lies in their generative models: the former targets the time-domain signal through TF synthesis coefficients, whereas the latter focuses on TF analysis coefficients. This fundamental difference unlocks the potential for multi-resolution representations, which were previously inaccessible within the conventional NMF framework.
