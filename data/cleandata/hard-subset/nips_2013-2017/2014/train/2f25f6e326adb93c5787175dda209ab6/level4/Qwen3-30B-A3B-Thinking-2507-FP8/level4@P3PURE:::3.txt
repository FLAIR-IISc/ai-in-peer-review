

This paper offers a thoughtful reinterpretation of time/frequency factorizations, introducing a more flexible framework that moves beyond existing formulations reliant on STFT analysis coefficients. The proposed approach applies to arbitrary transforms and maintains a direct link to the time-domain signal. The paper is well-written and accessible to researchers in the field, with strong theoretical development and above-average originality. However, the practical significance of the contribution is not clearly articulated, and the evaluation section is a primary weakness. The supplemental material lacks sufficient empirical validation, and the paper fails to compare its methodology with foundational approaches it builds upon. While the theoretical contribution alone is substantial enough to render this an interesting paper, the absence of comprehensive results and comparative analysis represents a notable shortcoming. Despite this, the paper presents solid theory, is clearly written, and makes a valuable theoretical contribution.
