- Proposes a flexible time-frequency synthesis framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly tied to the time-domain signal.  
- Readable paper of good quality with clear presentation.  
- Theoretical development is strong and thoughtful.  
- Originality is above average.  
- Enables potential multi-resolution representations beyond traditional NMF.  
- Addresses a fundamental signal-processing problem (signal decomposition and synthesis).  
- Significance not fully demonstrated due to limited empirical validation.  
- Experimental results and supplemental material are not particularly convincing.  
- Lacks comparisons to existing methods it builds upon.  
- More comprehensive results and comparisons would significantly strengthen the manuscript.  
- Overall, a solid and interesting theoretical contribution despite the evaluation weaknesses.
