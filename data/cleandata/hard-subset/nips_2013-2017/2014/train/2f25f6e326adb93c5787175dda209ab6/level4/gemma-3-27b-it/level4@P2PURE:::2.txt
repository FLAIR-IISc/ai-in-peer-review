This paper presents an interesting approach to Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) by shifting the generative model from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the time-domain signal itself, specifically through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This allows for multi-resolution representations, a capability absent in traditional NMF. I find the multi-resolution extension particularly appealing. 

The authors demonstrate results on multi-resolution signal decomposition, but I am also interested in the method’s performance in a single-resolution scenario. A comparison to Iterative Shrinkage-NMF (IS-NMF) would be valuable: does it yield comparable W and H matrices? Furthermore, how does the algorithm’s speed and stability compare to the well-established multiplicative update algorithm used for IS-NMF? To reiterate the core contribution, while standard NMF models the coefficients resulting from TF analysis, this work models the signal directly via a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients, enabling the aforementioned multi-resolution representations not achievable with traditional NMF.
