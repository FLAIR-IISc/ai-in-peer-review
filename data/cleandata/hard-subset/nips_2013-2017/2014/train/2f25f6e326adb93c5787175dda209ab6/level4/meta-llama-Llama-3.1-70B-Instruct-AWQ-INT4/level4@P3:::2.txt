This paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, which bridges the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches. The model, referred to as Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), considers a generative model of the time-domain signal itself by describing a generative model of time-frequency synthesis coefficients. This allows for multi-resolution representations, which were not possible in the traditional NMF setting.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the LRTFS model, its relation to sparse Bayesian learning, and its estimation using maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE) approaches. The multi-resolution extension of LRTFS is also well-explained, and the authors provide a clear example of how it can be applied to audio signal processing.

The experiments presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the LRTFS model in multi-resolution signal decompositions. However, I would like to see a comparison of the proposed method with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case. Specifically, I am curious to know if the proposed algorithm can achieve similar performance to IS-NMF in terms of the quality of the estimated W and H matrices. Additionally, I would like to know if the proposed algorithm is as fast and stable as the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF.

In terms of the quality of the paper, I think it is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the LRTFS model and its estimation approaches. The experiments are well-presented, and the results are easy to understand. However, I think the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case.
