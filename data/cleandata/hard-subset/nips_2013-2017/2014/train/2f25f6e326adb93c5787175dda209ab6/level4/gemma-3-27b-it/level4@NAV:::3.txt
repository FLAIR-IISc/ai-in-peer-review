This paper offers a compelling re-examination of time/frequency factorizations. Departing from conventional approaches based on Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) analysis coefficients, the authors introduce a more versatile framework applicable to a broader range of transforms and demonstrably connected to the underlying time-domain signal. 

The manuscript is clearly written and represents work of good quality, making it accessible to researchers in the field. The originality of the approach is notably strong. However, the paper could benefit from a clearer articulation of the work’s overall significance. A primary area for improvement lies in the presentation and evaluation of results. The supporting material lacks strong evidence, and the paper would be strengthened by a comparative analysis against existing methods upon which it builds. While this lack of comprehensive evaluation isn’t a critical weakness – the theoretical development is a valuable contribution in its own right – incorporating such comparisons would substantially enhance the impact of the work. Overall, this is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation. Further results and comparative analyses would be welcome, but their absence does not preclude acceptance given the intrinsic interest of the theoretical contribution.
