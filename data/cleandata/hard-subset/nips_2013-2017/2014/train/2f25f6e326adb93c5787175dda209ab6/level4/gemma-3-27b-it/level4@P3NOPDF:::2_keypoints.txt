- Introduces a novel NMF framework that shifts the generative model from TF analysis coefficients to the time-domain by modeling TF synthesis coefficients.
- Key distinction enables multi-resolution signal representations not readily available in traditional NMF.
- Core idea is compelling and represents a significant extension to NMF.
- Strong theoretical foundation and potential for novel applications enabled by multi-resolution decomposition.
- Motivation and applicability are clearly articulated.
- Exploration of multi-resolution representations is a valuable contribution.
- Paper lacks a thorough empirical evaluation.
- Needs a comparative analysis against single-resolution NMF methods, specifically IS-NMF, on the same data.
- Should compare learned W and H matrices between the proposed method and IS-NMF at single resolution.
- Requires discussion of computational efficiency and stability (speed and convergence) relative to multiplicative update rules in IS-NMF.
- Repetition of the core concept appears in the draft and should be streamlined for conciseness.
- Addressing single-resolution performance and computational questions would significantly strengthen the paper and is recommended for revision.
