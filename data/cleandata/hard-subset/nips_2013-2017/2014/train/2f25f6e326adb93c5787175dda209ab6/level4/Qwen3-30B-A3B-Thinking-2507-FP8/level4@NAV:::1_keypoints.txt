- Introduces extension of low-rank spectrogram-factorization to overcomplete/multiresolution bases (e.g., tonal + transient), building on Fevotte et al.'s GCM.  
- Reformulates implicit convolution with Gabor impulses into a time-domain expression, allowing nonorthogonal impulse responses and EM-based estimation.  
- Capability to use overcomplete dictionaries is valuable and potentially useful for source separation and denoising.  
- Insight is intriguing but its precise implications are unclear; may be mainly an alternative interpretation of Fevotte’s GCM.  
- Reviewer expects complications from the generalization but these are not discussed or addressed.  
- Estimation procedure appears correct and analytically deep, but the technical derivation was hard to follow.  
- Paper is well-motivated and generally clearly explained.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement metric, insufficient to support claims.  
- Audio examples are appreciated but unconvincing and show severe artifacts in music denoising.  
- Comparison to OMLSA seems unfair (OMLSA clip 7s vs new method 2s) and OMLSA had better perceived speech quality despite more noise/longer duration.  
- Conventional methods (e.g., wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) would likely yield better perceptual quality than the presented denoising results.  
- Originality is limited—heavily based on prior work—though the time-domain reinterpretation provides some novelty.  
- Significance is speculative: potential is there, but thin evaluation and flawed examples prevent demonstrating real impact.
