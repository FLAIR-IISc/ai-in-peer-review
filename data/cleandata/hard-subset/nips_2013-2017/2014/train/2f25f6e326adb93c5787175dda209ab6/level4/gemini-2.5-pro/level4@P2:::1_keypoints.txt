- Introduces LRTFS, a generative probabilistic time-domain model extending low-rank spectrogram factorization.  
- Models signals as linear combinations of time-frequency atoms with synthesis coefficients having a low-rank variance structure.  
- Reformulation naturally supports multi-resolution and overcomplete time-frequency bases (e.g., short windows for transients, long for tonal components).  
- Derives an EM algorithm for maximum joint likelihood estimation; theoretical contribution is strong and technically sound.  
- Experimental validation is weak and primarily illustrative, insufficient to support strong claims.  
- Music decomposition experiment is non-quantitative; speech enhancement reports only a single average SNR over 10 examples.  
- Subjective audio quality is poor in examples: denoised music shows prominent artifacts that may degrade perceptual quality.  
- Comparison to baselines is questionable/unfair (e.g., OMLSA output length mismatch), undermining performance claims.  
- Paper is well-written and the theoretical motivation and progression from GCM to LRTFS are clear.  
- Recommends more extensive evaluation: diverse datasets, additional objective metrics (SDR, SIR, SAR), fair baseline settings, and listening studies.  
- Core idea is novel and addresses a known limitation of STFT-based methods by enabling overcomplete multi-resolution analysis.  
- Potentially high significance for audio processing, but currently more theoretical than practically demonstrated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel elegant reformulation, multi-resolution capability, high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: critically insufficient experiments, perceptual artifacts, and weak/unfair baseline comparisons.
