This paper presents a novel and elegant framework for time-frequency factorizations. The key contribution is a generalization of existing factorization methods. Unlike traditional approaches that are often built upon the analysis coefficients of a specific transform, such as the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT), this work introduces a more flexible formulation that can be applied to arbitrary linear transforms and operates directly on the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written, of high quality, and should be accessible to readers with a background in this area. The theoretical contribution is the primary strength, offering a solid and interesting advancement over previous work.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty and Originality:** The proposed framework, which decouples the factorization from a specific transform and connects it directly to the time-domain signal, is a significant conceptual advance. The originality of this approach is above average and provides a more fundamental perspective on the problem.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The theoretical development is solid, well-reasoned, and provides a strong foundation for the proposed method. The generalization to arbitrary transforms is a particularly valuable contribution that could open up new research directions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and the core concepts are explained logically and effectively, making the theoretical arguments easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Empirical Evaluation:** The most significant weakness of the paper lies in its empirical evaluation. The results presented are not sufficient to fully demonstrate the practical advantages or significance of the proposed framework. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing and fails to provide the necessary depth to bolster the paper's claims.
*   **Lack of Comparisons:** A critical omission is the lack of direct comparison to the established methods that the paper builds upon. Without a baseline comparison, it is difficult for the reader to gauge the practical benefits of the new formulation in terms of performance, computational efficiency, or the interpretability of the resulting factors.
*   **Communication of Significance:** While the theoretical elegance is apparent, the paper does not fully articulate the practical impact of this new framework. The motivation could be strengthened by discussing what specific problems can be solved more effectively with this approach compared to existing ones.

**Conclusion:**

This is a strong paper from a theoretical standpoint. It introduces an elegant and novel generalization for time-frequency factorizations with a solid mathematical foundation. However, the paper is let down by its weak experimental section. While a more comprehensive evaluation with direct comparisons to prior art would have made this a much stronger submission, the theoretical contribution is substantial and interesting enough on its own to warrant attention from the community. It is a valuable, albeit primarily theoretical, addition to the literature.
