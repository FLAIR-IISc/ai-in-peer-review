This paper presents a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, bridging the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches. The proposed model, called Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), is a generative probabilistic latent factor model that can be applied to an arbitrary time-frequency transform and is directly linked to the time domain signal.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to researchers in the field. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the LRTFS model, including its relation to sparse Bayesian learning and its advantages over traditional NMF approaches. The mathematical derivations are sound, and the EM algorithms for estimation in LRTFS are well-described.

The originality of the paper is above average, as it presents a new and flexible framework for time-frequency factorizations. However, the significance of the work is not fully communicated in the paper. The authors could have provided more insights into the potential applications and implications of the LRTFS model.

The results and evaluation of the paper are somewhat disappointing. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper does not provide a thorough comparison with existing methods. While the theoretical development is a significant contribution, the lack of comprehensive results and comparisons is a notable weakness.

Despite this, the paper is still a good contribution to the field, and the theoretical development is interesting and well-presented. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the LRTFS model on two experiments, including a hybrid decomposition of music and a speech enhancement problem. However, more extensive experimental studies would be necessary to fully demonstrate the potential of the LRTFS model.

Strengths:

* Novel and flexible framework for time-frequency factorizations
* Well-written and easy to follow
* Sound mathematical derivations
* EM algorithms for estimation in LRTFS are well-described

Weaknesses:

* Significance of the work is not fully communicated
* Results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing
* Lack of comprehensive comparison with existing methods

Arguments for acceptance:

* Theoretical development is a significant contribution
* LRTFS model has potential applications in various fields
* Paper is well-written and easy to follow

Arguments against acceptance:

* Results and evaluation are not comprehensive
* Lack of comparison with existing methods
* Significance of the work is not fully communicated

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly by providing more insights into the potential applications and implications of the LRTFS model, and by including a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods.
