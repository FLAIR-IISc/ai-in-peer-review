- Paper reformulates probabilistic spectrogram factorization in the time domain, making convolution with analysis filters explicit and enabling overcomplete basis functions.  
- This time-domain view allows multiresolution decompositions (e.g., combined tonal and transient bases) and generalizes Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) approaches.  
- An EM algorithm is derived to estimate the proposed model’s parameters.  
- The time-domain reinterpretation is an elegant, non-trivial, and genuinely novel extension of prior work.  
- Enabling overcomplete dictionaries is a powerful consequence, but raises concerns about non-uniqueness and instability that are not fully addressed.  
- The EM derivation appears sound but is complex and difficult to fully verify from the paper.  
- The manuscript is generally well-written and the motivation/theory are clearly presented.  
- Experimental evaluation is superficial and insufficient—limited to a single illustrative result and one SNR measurement.  
- Audio examples are unconvincing: comparisons (e.g., with OMLSA) are questionable and possibly unfair (unequal clip lengths).  
- Perceptually, the method degrades speech quality more than competing methods and introduces severe, musically unpleasant artifacts in music denoising.  
- Established techniques (Wiener filtering, wavelet shrinkage) likely offer a better noise/artifact trade-off than the proposed method.  
- The idea has significant potential but the paper fails to demonstrate practical value due to thin empirical validation and prominent artifacts.
