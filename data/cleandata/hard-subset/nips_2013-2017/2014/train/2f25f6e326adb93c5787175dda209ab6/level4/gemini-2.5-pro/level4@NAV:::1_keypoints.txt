- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization by allowing latent factors over an arbitrary, potentially overcomplete, dictionary of basis functions.  
- Enables multiresolution decomposition (e.g., combined tonal and transient bases) intended to help source separation and denoising.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) which models spectrogram-bin variance via low-rank factorization.  
- Key technical contribution: reformulates the Gabor impulse-response convolution into an explicit time-domain expression.  
- This reformulation permits use of diverse, including non-orthogonal and overcomplete, impulse-response sets.  
- Provides an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm to compute a locally optimal solution.  
- Core insight is intriguing and valuable, but the full implications and benefits are not clearly established.  
- Paper does not adequately address typical challenges introduced by overcomplete bases; justification is lacking.  
- Derivation of the estimation procedure is impressive and appears credible, though not fully verified by reviewer.  
- Exposition of motivation and method is clear, but the experimental evaluation is very limited.  
- Results consist of a single illustrative example and one SNR metric, insufficient for general conclusions.  
- Audio examples are not compelling; OMLSA comparison appears inequitable (different clip lengths) and OMLSA speech had better subjective quality.  
- Music denoising produced substantial processing artifacts; standard methods (Wiener filtering, wavelet shrinkage) might yield better perceptual quality.  
- Originality: relies heavily on Fevotte’s model, but the time-domain interpretation is a novel and useful perspective.  
- Significance: conceptually high-impact potential, but limited evaluation and observed artifacts prevent demonstrated significance.
