Paper generalizes low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) multiresolution bases for tonal/transient decomposition.

Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model and derives a time-domain expression by expanding implicit convolution with Gabor impulse responses.

Formulation supports different, including non-orthogonal, impulse-response sets and overcomplete dictionaries.

An Expectation-Maximization (EM) estimation procedure is presented, with analysis of its local optima.

Reviewer finds the time-domain perspective an interesting insight but says full implications remain unclear.

Generalization likely introduces complications that the paper does not clearly explain how to mitigate.

Reviewer assumes the estimation is correct but found the detailed derivation hard to follow.

Experimental evaluation is superficial—only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement are reported.

Audio examples are unimpressive and comparisons may be unfair (e.g., OMLSA example is longer and noisier yet sounds better).

Denoising music example shows substantial artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage/Wiener) might yield better perceived quality.

Originality is limited by heavy reliance on Fevotte’s work, though the time-domain interpretation is a novel contribution.

Potential significance is high if overcomplete matrix-factorization works well, but current thin evaluation and artifacts fail to convincingly demonstrate importance.
