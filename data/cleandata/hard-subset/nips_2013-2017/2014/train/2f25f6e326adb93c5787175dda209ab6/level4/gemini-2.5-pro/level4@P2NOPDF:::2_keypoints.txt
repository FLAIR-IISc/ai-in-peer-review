- Introduces a novel NMF variant that models time-frequency synthesis coefficients, directly modeling the time-domain signal rather than TF analysis coefficients.  
- This conceptual shift naturally enables multi-resolution representations absent in traditional NMF.  
- The multi-resolution extension is a particularly compelling and valuable contribution.  
- Authors provide results on multi-resolution signal decompositions.  
- Paper lacks a performance comparison against an established single-resolution baseline.  
- Recommends a direct comparison with Itakura-Saito NMF (IS-NMF) to contextualize behavior and performance.  
- Requests whether learned basis (W) and activations (H) qualitatively resemble those from standard IS-NMF.  
- Requests comparison of computational performance: convergence speed and numerical stability versus IS-NMF multiplicative updates.  
- Addressing these comparisons would clarify trade-offs and advantages even when multi-resolution is unnecessary.
