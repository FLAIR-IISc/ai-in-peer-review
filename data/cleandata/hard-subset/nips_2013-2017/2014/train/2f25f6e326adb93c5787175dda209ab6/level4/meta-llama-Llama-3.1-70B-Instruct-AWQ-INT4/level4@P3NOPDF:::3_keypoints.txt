- Proposes a thoughtful, flexible interpretation of time/frequency factorizations applicable to arbitrary transforms and tied to the time-domain signal.  
- Moves beyond STFT-based formulations by not relying on analysis coefficients, offering a more comprehensive conceptual framework.  
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and of good overall quality.  
- Originality of ideas is above average.  
- Significance of the contribution is under-emphasized and could be communicated more effectively.  
- Major weakness: lack of convincing empirical results and evaluation.  
- Supplemental material fails to provide strong supporting evidence for the paper’s claims.  
- Authors do not compare their method to existing approaches it builds upon.  
- Theoretical development is substantial and interesting.  
- Including more experiments and comparisons would substantially strengthen the paper.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept (7/10) with encouragement to add comprehensive results and baselines in future work.
