- Proposes an extension of low-rank spectrogram-factorization models that generalizes latent factors over arbitrary bases, including overcomplete and multiresolution dictionaries.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) but reformulates it as convolutions with time-domain impulse responses per spectrogram row.  
- Allows non-orthogonal and overcomplete bases and provides an EM analysis procedure for estimation.  
- Conceptually interesting; time-domain interpretation is a fresh perspective on probabilistic matrix factorization.  
- Heavily relies on Fevotte's foundation, so originality is limited by prior work.  
- Estimation procedure appears correct and the analysis is impressive, though some technical details are hard to follow.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement measurement.  
- Sound examples are included but not fully convincing; comparisons seem unfair (different example lengths, differing noise levels).  
- Denoising examples introduce significant artifacts that may be perceived as lower quality than conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering).  
- Potentially significant capability (matrix factorization on overcomplete bases), but current experiments and examples fail to demonstrate practical impact.
