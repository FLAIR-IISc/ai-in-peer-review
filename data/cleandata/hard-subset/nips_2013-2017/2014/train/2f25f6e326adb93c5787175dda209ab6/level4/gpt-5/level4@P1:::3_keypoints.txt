- Proposes LRTFS: a time-domain generative model imposing low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients instead of STFT magnitudes, enabling direct waveform synthesis.  
- Contrasts LRTFS with GCM/IS-NMF which impose low-rank structure on analysis (STFT) coefficients.  
- Derives two estimation schemes: MJLE (practical EM alternating Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF) and MMLE (SBL-style marginalization, currently not scalable).  
- Natural multi-resolution extension by combining multiple time-frequency frames (e.g., short and long Gabor windows) within one model.  
- Connects to SBL: without low-rank tie the prior is diagonal Gaussian; novelty is tying variances via WH to enforce low-rank structure.  
- Provides component reconstruction via Wiener-type formulas and demonstrates hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement.  
- Strength: conceptual advance linking analysis-based NMF/GCM to a generative time-domain synthesis framework.  
- Strength: flexible to arbitrary time-frequency frames and supports multi-resolution modeling important for audio transients vs. harmonics.  
- Strength: MJLE algorithm is simple, practical, avoids large matrix inversions, and leverages efficient Φ/Φ* operations.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding with clear derivations and links to IS divergence, Wiener filtering, and SBL.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited and supplemental results are not fully convincing.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons to baseline methods (e.g., GCM/IS-NMF variants) to quantify practical gains.  
- Weakness: MMLE is conceptually appealing but not computationally practical for large audio problems in its current form.  
- Weakness: some practical estimation choices (treating λ as hyperparameter, warm-restart scheduling) weaken the completeness of the generative treatment.  
- Quality: technical development and MJLE updates are sound, but nonconvexity and approximate λ updates mean convergence to local optima.  
- Clarity: paper is well written; suggestions include clarifying computational costs/memory, β setting, initialization guidance, and choice of Φ impact.  
- Originality: above-average — shifting low-rank structure to synthesis coefficients and enabling multi-resolution synthesis is a novel, coherent contribution.  
- Significance: potentially important for audio processing and inverse problems, but current empirical results do not yet demonstrate clear practical advantages.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant unifying formulation, practical MJLE algorithm, and useful multi-resolution extension.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited experimental validation, non-scalable MMLE, and underplayed empirical significance.  
- Suggestions: add thorough comparisons to baselines, ablation studies (single vs. multi-resolution, K, λ scheduling), runtime/scaling analyses, and reproducibility details.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically solid, well written, and promising; recommend acceptance contingent on stronger empirical evaluation.
