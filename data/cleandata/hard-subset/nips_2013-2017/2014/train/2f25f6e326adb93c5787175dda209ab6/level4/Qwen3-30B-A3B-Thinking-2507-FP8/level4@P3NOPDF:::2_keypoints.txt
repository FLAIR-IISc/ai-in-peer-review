- Proposes a novel NMF extension modeling time-frequency synthesis coefficients instead of analysis coefficients.  
- This shift enables inherent multi-resolution signal representations absent from conventional NMF.  
- Modeling the time-domain signal directly via synthesis coefficients is a significant conceptual advancement with clear application potential.  
- The approach decomposes signals across multiple resolutions without additional post-processing or specialized architectures.  
- Empirical results and visualizations convincingly demonstrate coherent capture of fine- and coarse-scale structures.  
- Work aligns with interest in hierarchical representations for audio and signal processing.  
- Lacks evaluation in the single-resolution setting; should compare factor recovery (W, H) to IS-NMF as a baseline.  
- Recommend quantitative (reconstruction error, divergence) and qualitative (bases/activations) comparisons with IS-NMF.  
- Needs clarification of computational efficiency and numerical stability relative to IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates.  
- Open questions: convergence speed, sensitivity to initialization, and scaling with signal length or dictionary size—suggest ablation/runtime study.  
- Presentation occasionally conflates synthesis and analysis coefficients; require clearer formal definition and role of inverse TF transform.  
- Discussion of why synthesis coefficients enable multi-resolution is superficial; ask for deeper mathematical/structural explanation.  
- Core idea is strong—a paradigm shift with potential applications in audio source separation, biomedical signals, and multi-scale feature extraction.  
- Recommendations: (1) compare with IS-NMF in single-resolution tasks, (2) clarify efficiency/stability, and (3) refine theoretical exposition; then suitable for publication with minor revisions.
