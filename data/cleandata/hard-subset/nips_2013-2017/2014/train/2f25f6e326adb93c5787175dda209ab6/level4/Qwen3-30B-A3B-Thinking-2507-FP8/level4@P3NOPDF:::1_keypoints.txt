- Core contribution: time-domain reinterpretation of Fevotte et al.’s GCM enabling overcomplete/nonorthogonal bases and EM-based multiresolution decomposition.  
- Strength: Conceptually elegant and technically sound time-domain shift that clarifies latent-factor interactions with arbitrary bases.  
- Strength: Nontrivial EM extension for overcomplete bases with a well-motivated, clearly derived formulation.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is severely underdeveloped—only a single illustrative example and one SNR metric.  
- Weakness: Unfair OMLSA comparison (7s vs 2s examples), introducing bias and confounding perceptual assessments.  
- Weakness: Music denoising example produces severe artifacts (audible distortion, phase smearing) where conventional methods likely perform better.  
- Weakness: Paper does not address how overcomplete bases avoid problems like non-uniqueness or convergence instability.  
- Originality: Novel time-domain perspective is the main novelty but remains tightly coupled to prior GCM work and not clearly differentiated from existing overcomplete-dictionary research.  
- Significance: Potentially substantial for source separation/denoising, but current results provide no empirical evidence of real-world gains.  
- Overall: Interesting theoretical extension with a clean formulation but insufficient empirical validation; needs comprehensive experiments, standardized comparisons, and perceptual evaluations to justify significance.
