- Contribution: Generalizes low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary (possibly overcomplete) bases, enabling multiresolution decomposition (e.g., tonal + transient).
- Method: Provides a time-domain formulation accommodating different impulse response sets, including nonorthogonal bases.
- Estimation: Proposes an EM-based estimation procedure that converges to a local optimum.
- Technical merit: Meaningful conceptual extension of GCM; ability to incorporate overcomplete dictionaries is valuable for source separation/denoising.
- Presentation: Well-motivated and clearly written with a solid theoretical foundation, though some derivation details are hard to follow.
- Experiments: Empirical evaluation is very limited—only one illustrative decomposition and a single SNR improvement measurement.
- Comparative fairness: Comparison to OMLSA is questionable due to mismatched example lengths (7s vs. 2s), potentially biasing results.
- Perceptual quality: Music denoising results show noticeable artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceptual outcomes.
- Originality: Builds on Fevotte et al.’s GCM but offers a fresh time-domain perspective and a noteworthy extension to overcomplete bases.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for audio processing, but insufficient evaluation prevents establishing the work’s practical significance.
