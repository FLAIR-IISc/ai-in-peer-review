- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete multiresolution bases (e.g., tonal and transient components).  
- Rewrites Fevotte et al.’s GCM convolution as a time-domain expression allowing non-orthogonal impulse responses.  
- Proposes an EM algorithm for parameter estimation, with susceptibility to local optima.  
- Incorporation of overcomplete dictionaries is a valuable contribution but likely introduces complications that aren’t fully mitigated or explained.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement reported, insufficient for firm conclusions.  
- Audio examples are unconvincing: OMLSA comparison appears unfair (longer duration/more noise) yet sounds better in speech quality.  
- Music denoising produces noticeable artifacts; standard methods (wavelet shrinkage/Wiener) might give better perceived quality.  
- Paper is well motivated and clearly explained, but some technical details were hard to follow.  
- Originality largely builds on Fevotte et al., with the time-domain reinterpretation as the main novel aspect.  
- Potential significance is high if applied to overcomplete bases, but current limited evaluation and artifacts make the impact speculative.
