This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Instead of using existing formulations built upon the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it presents a more flexible framework applicable with an arbitrary transform and directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is readily accessible to researchers in this field and is of good technical quality. The originality is above average, though the significance isn’t fully conveyed within the manuscript. Perhaps the most notable limitation lies in the results and their evaluation. The supplemental material isn't particularly compelling, and the paper lacks comparisons to methods upon which it builds. While not a critically weak point—the theoretical development itself is enough to make this an interesting paper—more thorough results and comparisons would significantly strengthen the contribution. It is a good paper, well-written, and grounded in solid theory. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel framework for time-frequency synthesis directly linked to the raw signal.
*   Addresses a limitation of prior work by providing a generative model for the raw signal, not just the STFT.
*   Opens doors to multi-resolution representations not possible with traditional NMF.
*   Clear and well-structured presentation of the model and EM algorithms.
*   Strong theoretical foundation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experimental evaluation is limited and not entirely convincing.
*   Lack of comparison to established methods.
*   Significance could be better emphasized and demonstrated through more extensive experimentation.
*   The impact and broader applicability beyond the presented examples are not fully clear.
