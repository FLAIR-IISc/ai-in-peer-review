- Proposes an extension of low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary (possibly overcomplete) bases for multiresolution decomposition.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model and generalizes latent factors to nonorthogonal impulse responses.  
- Derives an explicit time-domain expression for the estimated signal by making the implicit convolution with Gabor-like impulses explicit.  
- Presents an EM estimation procedure with claimed convergence to a local optimum.  
- Central insight is interesting and offers a new perspective, but its full implications are unclear.  
- Allowing overcomplete dictionaries is appealing conceptually but may introduce practical complications that are not addressed.  
- The estimation/analysis is impressive technically, though some derivation details are hard to follow.  
- Paper is generally well explained and motivated.  
- Empirical evaluation is very limited: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement measurement.  
- Audio examples are not especially convincing and comparisons (e.g., to OMLSA) appear unfair due to duration mismatch.  
- Speech in the OMLSA example sounds higher quality despite more background noise; raises concerns about comparative claims.  
- Music denoising examples show substantial artifacts; simpler methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceived quality.  
- Originality: extends prior work meaningfully by introducing time-domain interpretation and overcomplete bases within a probabilistic framework.  
- Significance: promising in principle, but current empirical evidence is too thin and anecdotal to demonstrate practical impact.
