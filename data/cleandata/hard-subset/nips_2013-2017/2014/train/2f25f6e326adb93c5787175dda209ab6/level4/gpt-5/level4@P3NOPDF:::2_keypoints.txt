- Proposes a synthesis-based generative model for time-domain signals via TF synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-resolution representations not possible with standard NMF.  
- Conceptual novelty: modeling via synthesis coefficients offers a well-motivated, fresh alternative to analysis-based NMF.  
- Multi-resolution capability: framework naturally supports multiple resolutions, valuable for nonstationary audio.  
- Empirical demonstrations indicate practical utility of the multi-resolution decompositions.  
- Request to compare single-resolution performance against IS-NMF baseline.  
- Ask whether learned W and H resemble IS-NMF factors under same data, initialization, and rank.  
- Ask whether TF-magnitude and time-domain reconstructions are comparable to IS-NMF.  
- Question algorithmic efficiency: how do convergence speed and computational cost compare to IS-NMF multiplicative updates?  
- Question optimization stability and robustness to initialization; whether objective decreases monotonically and avoids pathologies.  
- Request practical guidance on critical hyperparameters and design choices (window sizes, number of resolutions, regularization).  
- Ask about sensitivity to the choice of synthesis dictionary/frame and recommendations for practitioners.  
- Suggest providing a controlled single-resolution experiment reporting objectives, reconstruction errors, factor similarity, convergence traces, and wall-clock time.  
- Suggest ablation/quantitative study isolating multi-resolution benefits (reconstruction vs computation, task metrics like source separation).  
- Recommend discussing when analysis- and synthesis-based models coincide or diverge to interpret differences in learned factors.  
- Recommend addressing identifiability and invariances in the synthesis setting (even qualitatively).  
- Recommend adding implementation details for reproducibility: optimization steps, step sizes/update rules, stopping criteria, and stability tricks.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and promising work with an attractive multi-resolution extension that needs careful single-resolution comparison and deeper analysis of computational efficiency and stability.
