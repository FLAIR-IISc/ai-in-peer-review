

The paper introduces a novel framework that shifts the generative modeling perspective from time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the time-domain signal itself, achieved through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This approach enables multi-resolution representations, which are unattainable within the conventional non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) framework. The concept of multi-resolution extension is particularly compelling and well-motivated.

While the authors demonstrate results for multi-resolution signal decompositions, I have two specific queries regarding the single-resolution case: (1) How does the proposed method perform compared to IS-NMF in terms of the recovered factors W and H? Does it yield similar decompositions? (2) Is the proposed algorithm as computationally efficient and numerically stable as the multiplicative update algorithm used for IS-NMF? Addressing these points would strengthen the evaluation of the method's practical utility.
