

This paper introduces a novel extension to Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) by shifting the generative model from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients (standard NMF) to the time-domain signal itself, achieved through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This fundamental change enables the exploration of multi-resolution signal representations, a capability absent in conventional NMF frameworks.

The core idea is compelling and addresses a meaningful limitation in existing approaches. The proposed method's potential for multi-resolution decomposition is particularly promising and well-motivated.

To strengthen the evaluation, the authors should provide a more thorough comparison in the single-resolution case, which serves as a critical baseline. Specifically:
1.  How do the factor matrices (W and H) from the proposed method compare to those obtained using IS-NMF (a standard single-resolution approach) on the same datasets? Do they converge to similar or distinct solutions?
2.  What is the computational efficiency (speed) and numerical stability of the proposed algorithm relative to the well-established multiplicative update rules for IS-NMF? Is the overhead justified by the multi-resolution benefits?

While the paper demonstrates the multi-resolution capability, a rigorous single-resolution comparison is essential to establish the method's value proposition. Without it, the claim that the proposed framework offers a meaningful advancement over IS-NMF in the standard setting remains unsubstantiated. The authors should address these points to fully validate their approach and clarify its relationship to existing techniques. The theoretical foundation for the synthesis coefficient model is sound, but empirical validation against the single-resolution baseline is the missing piece needed to assess the method's practical significance.
