- Proposes an extension of low-rank spectrogram factorization to general, potentially overcomplete, sets of bases for multiresolution sound decomposition.  
- Enables decomposition using bases that capture both tonal and transient characteristics.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) but derives a novel time-domain expression for estimated parameters.  
- Reformulation supports adaptation to various impulse responses, including non-orthogonal/overcomplete dictionaries.  
- Presents an EM algorithm for parameter estimation and analyzes its local optima.  
- Interesting and potentially valuable insight, but the full implications are not clearly established.  
- Overcompleteness is a significant advantage, yet the paper does not adequately explain why associated complexities are not problematic.  
- Analytical derivations are rigorous but difficult to follow in places.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only a single illustrative example and one SNR improvement metric.  
- Comparison to OMLSA is questionable and possibly unfair due to mismatched example lengths and noise levels.  
- Demonstrated denoising examples show noticeable artifacts; conventional methods might yield better perceptual quality.  
- Originality lies in the time-domain reinterpretation, offering a fresh perspective on existing models.  
- Potentially significant impact from applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases, but current evidence is insufficient; more comprehensive evaluation is required.
