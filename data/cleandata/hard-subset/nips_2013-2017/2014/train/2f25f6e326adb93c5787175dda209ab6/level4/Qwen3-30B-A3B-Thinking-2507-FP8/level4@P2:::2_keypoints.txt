- Introduces LRTFS, a generative model that synthesizes time-domain signals directly rather than modeling only their STFT.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by applying a low-rank variance structure [WH]fn to synthesis coefficients αfn instead of analysis coefficients.  
- Provides two expectation-maximization algorithms for parameter estimation in the new framework.  
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations, allowing different components (transients vs. tonals) to use different resolutions.  
- Bridges a theoretical gap between time-frequency signal processing and generative modeling, linking to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL).  
- Demonstrates practical utility on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.  
- Speech enhancement results show a 6.6 dB average SNR improvement, outperforming IS-NMF (6 dB) and OMLSA (4.6 dB).  
- Paper is well-organized with clear mathematical formulation and explanations of relations to prior work.  
- Lacks sufficient comparison of single-resolution LRTFS performance and computational cost versus traditional IS-NMF.  
- MMLE implementation is reported as computationally infeasible for typical audio sizes due to large matrix inversions.  
- Does not provide detailed runtime/efficiency comparisons between the proposed MJLE algorithm and standard IS-NMF multiplicative updates.  
- Limited discussion of applicability beyond audio, despite potential extensions (e.g., multichannel source separation).  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, significant theoretical advance with compelling multi-resolution capability and strong experiments—worthy of acceptance.  
- Remaining issues to address: single-resolution baselines, MMLE practicality, and more thorough computational and broader-impact analysis.
