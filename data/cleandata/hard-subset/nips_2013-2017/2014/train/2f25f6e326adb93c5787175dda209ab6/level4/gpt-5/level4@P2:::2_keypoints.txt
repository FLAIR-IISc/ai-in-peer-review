- Introduces LRTFS: a time-domain generative model that imposes low-rank variance on complex synthesis coefficients rather than on STFT analysis variances.  
- Enables natural multi-resolution representations by stacking multiple frames to reconstruct waveforms.  
- Proposes two estimation strategies: MJLE (EM with auxiliary variable) and MMLE (type-II SBL that integrates out coefficients).  
- MJLE alternates a Wiener-like shrinkage on synthesis coefficients with IS-NMF updates on their squared magnitudes and avoids matrix inversions.  
- MMLE is principled but currently impractical due to large matrix inversions and scalability issues.  
- Shows applications: tonal/transient/music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with average SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Links the method to prior work: IS-NMF/GCM, NMF, sparse Bayesian learning, and hybrid audio models.  
- Conceptual novelty: modeling low-rank structure on synthesis side (time-domain generative) rather than analysis side.  
- Clean probabilistic formulation with clear connections to Wiener filtering and SBL.  
- Multi-resolution extension is elegant, practical, and requires minimal algorithmic changes.  
- Empirical results indicate benefits and straightforward latent component reconstruction.  
- Lacks a direct single-resolution head-to-head comparison versus IS-NMF on identical data (W/H similarity, quality, speed, stability).  
- Missing computational details: per-iteration complexity, wall-clock times, iteration counts, stopping criteria, and sensitivity to dictionary redundancy.  
- Hyperparameter/stability issues: unclear practical selection of β and λ, handling when λ changes (warm-restart), and treatment of δΦ for overcomplete frames.  
- Joint learning of λ performed poorly; authors should provide guidelines on when to fix λ and good schedules to avoid overfitting.  
- MMLE scalability needs discussion of iterative solvers (CG with Φ/Φ* matvecs), low-rank/diagonal approximations, Krylov methods, or variational/message-passing alternatives.  
- Evaluation is narrow (mostly SNR); recommend adding perceptual metrics (PESQ/STOI), more noise types/conditions, and ablations isolating multi-resolution gains vs best single-resolution.  
- Suggest comparing to a “stacked-STFT IS-NMF” baseline to isolate the benefit of synthesis-side modeling.  
- Reproducibility gaps: initialization details (W, H, α), K selection, β/λ values and schedules, frame redundancy, and code/data availability needed.  
- Presentation issues: minor typos, incomplete references, inconsistent notation, and unclear handling of Hermitian symmetry/Φ assumptions.  
- Derivations and MJLE appear sound; λ update is heuristic but reduces objective; convergence is to local optima and identifiability/initialization effects need more discussion.  
- Overall originality is strong (synthesis-side low-rank variance modeling and tied-variance SBL connection).  
- Significance: promising for audio decomposition/enhancement and broader inverse problems if MMLE can be scaled.  
- Specific requests: add controlled single-frame IS-NMF comparisons, runtime/complexity reports, ablations on K/β/λ/frame choices, additional metrics, and clearer guidelines on hyperparameters and initialization.  
- Pros: principled time-domain generative model, efficient MJLE EM, practical multi-resolution extension, demonstrated baseline gains.  
- Cons: limited single-resolution comparisons, MMLE impractical at present, heuristic hyperparameter choices, and limited reproducibility details.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on stronger empirical comparisons and clearer practical guidance/documentation.
