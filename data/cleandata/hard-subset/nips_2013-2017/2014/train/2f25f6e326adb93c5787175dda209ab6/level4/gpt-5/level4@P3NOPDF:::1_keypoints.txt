- Contribution: Extends GCM spectrogram-factorization to a time-domain convolutional formulation over arbitrary, potentially overcomplete impulse-response dictionaries and derives an EM algorithm for it.
- Strength — Conceptual: Time-domain reinterpretation is insightful and unifies multiresolution and overcomplete-dictionary modeling.
- Strength — Generality: Supports nonorthogonal impulse responses, widening applicability to tonal/transient mixtures and structured bases.
- Strength — Methodology: EM derivation is carefully constructed and potentially a valuable technical contribution.
- Weakness — Evaluation scope: Experiments are extremely limited (one illustrative example, one SNR improvement) and don’t support broad claims.
- Weakness — Audio demos fairness/quality: Comparisons to OMLSA are unequal (different sample lengths) and OMLSA shows higher perceived speech quality despite more noise; music outputs show audible artifacts.
- Weakness — Overcompleteness issues: Paper lacks analysis of identifiability, stability, needed regularization (sparsity/smoothness), and sensitivity to initialization with coherent dictionaries.
- Weakness — Baselines: Missing comparisons to standard NMF/PLCA (STFT), convolutional NMF, sparse coding/K-SVD, and conventional MMSE/Wiener/OMLSA under matched conditions.
- Weakness — Practicality: Insufficient discussion of computational complexity, memory demands, and scalability for large dictionaries/long signals.
- Quality assessment: Technical insight and EM framework promising, but dense derivations hard to verify and empirical evidence is insufficient; audible artifacts suggest need for more constraints or post-processing.
- Clarity: High-level motivation clear but derivations need more guidance (schematics, reduction conditions, EM pseudocode); evaluation protocol should be standardized for reproducibility.
- Originality: Novel time-domain angle on GCM and overcomplete impulse-response sets, but should be positioned relative to convolutional NMF and time-domain factorization work.
- Significance: Potentially impactful if validated, but current weak empirical results and artifacts limit practical significance.
- Questions for authors: How are impulse-response sets chosen/learned and how sensitive are results? What regularization is used against overcompleteness? Does EM reduce exactly to GCM for STFT bases? What are per-iteration time/memory costs and optimizations? How are hyperparameters chosen?
- Suggested improvements: Expand evaluation (multiple datasets/tasks, standard metrics like SDR/SI-SDR/PESQ/STOI), ensure matched fair comparisons, run ablations on overcompleteness and redundancy, address artifacts via priors or post-processing and include a perceptual study, and clarify derivations with pseudocode, convergence discussion, and shared code/audio.
- Overall assessment: Compelling conceptual extension but current empirical validation is too thin and sometimes unfavorable; recommend rejection pending stronger systematic evaluation and clearer treatment of overcomplete-model challenges.
