- Proposes a flexible, nuanced time/frequency factorization framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly tied to the time-domain signal.  
- Offers an interpretation that moves beyond STFT-based formulations.  
- Paper is accessible to specialists and is well written with good quality.  
- Originality is above average, but the significance of the contribution is not fully articulated.  
- Results and evaluation are disappointing; supplementary material is unpersuasive.  
- Paper lacks comparisons to the methods it extends, which would strengthen it.  
- Theoretical development is strong and interesting, making the paper worthwhile despite limited empirical validation.
