This paper presents an interesting approach to Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF). While traditional NMF frameworks model the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients of a signal, this work proposes a generative model of the time-domain signal itself, achieved through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This shift unlocks the potential for multi-resolution representations, which are not readily available in standard NMF settings. I find this multi-resolution extension particularly appealing.

The authors demonstrate results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, and I am curious about the method's performance in a single-resolution scenario. Specifically, how does it compare to Iterative Shrinkage-NMF (IS-NMF)? Do the resulting W and H matrices exhibit similar characteristics? Furthermore, is the proposed algorithm comparable in speed and stability to the multiplicative update algorithm commonly used for IS-NMF? 

To reiterate the core contribution, while regular NMF focuses on modeling TF analysis coefficients, this work models the time-domain signal directly via TF synthesis coefficients, thereby enabling multi-resolution representations previously unattainable with traditional NMF.
