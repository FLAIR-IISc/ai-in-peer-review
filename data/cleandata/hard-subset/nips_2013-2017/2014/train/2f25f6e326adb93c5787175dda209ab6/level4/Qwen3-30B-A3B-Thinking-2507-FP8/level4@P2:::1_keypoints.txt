- Introduces a novel time-domain synthesis framework extending GCM with low-rank variance constraints over arbitrary time-frequency bases.  
- Enables multi-resolution decomposition (e.g., tonal + transient) and handles nonorthogonal/overcomplete bases.  
- Derives two EM-based estimation algorithms for joint and marginal likelihood; only joint-likelihood version implemented in experiments.  
- Technical contribution is sound, rigorously derived, and well-motivated with clear theoretical grounding.  
- Connects to sparse Bayesian learning, but needs clearer explanation of how the low-rank constraint avoids standard SBL pitfalls.  
- Derivation details (notably Eq. (11) and the log(|α|^2) term) require more intuitive explanation.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a logical flow and clear model exposition.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: music decomposition is illustrative with a single example and no quantitative baselines beyond SNR.  
- Speech enhancement shows modest SNR gain (6.6 dB) over IS-NMF (6 dB) and OMLSA (4.6 dB) but lacks statistical tests and perceptual analysis.  
- Comparisons may be unfair/inconsistent (e.g., OMLSA output length discrepancy) and lack ablation studies (e.g., resolution choice effects).  
- Reported audio quality has artifacts (distorted bass/piano) undermining practical utility.  
- Originality is strong in time-domain reinterpretation and multi-resolution extension, but somewhat diminished by reliance on established NMF techniques.  
- Significance is promising for audio applications but not convincingly demonstrated with current empirical evidence.  
- Suggested revisions: add multi- vs single-resolution experiments, ablation studies, perceptual evaluations (e.g., MUSHRA), and clarify OMLSA comparison and Eq. (11).  
- Overall recommendation: technically solid and novel but empirical weaknesses warrant a weak accept conditional on stronger evaluations and clarifications.
