- Proposes a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model bridging time-frequency synthesis and NMF.  
- Models the generative process of time-domain signals via time-frequency synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- Well-written with clear explanation and links to sparse Bayesian learning and the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM).  
- Presents two estimation approaches (MJLE and MMLE) and corresponding EM algorithms.  
- Experiments show effectiveness for multi-resolution decompositions in music and speech enhancement.  
- Semi-supervised speech enhancement comparison shows improved performance over IS-NMF.  
- Requests a comparison with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case to check if W and H are similar and to compare speed/stability with multiplicative updates.  
- Notes that estimating λ with all other parameters free performs poorly in practice; asks for more insight and potential solutions.  
- Recommends additional discussion on algorithm stability and computational complexity.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, novel approach with potential impact; rating 8/10.
