This paper proposes a novel extension to the traditional Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) framework, shifting the focus from a generative model of time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to a generative model of the time-domain signal itself. This is achieved by describing a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients, which opens up new possibilities for multi-resolution representations that were not feasible in the traditional NMF setting.

The idea of a multi-resolution extension is particularly intriguing, and the authors present some promising results on multi-resolution signal decompositions. However, it would be beneficial to also evaluate the performance of the proposed method in the single-resolution case, comparing it to the existing Iterative Shrinkage Non-negative Matrix Factorization (IS-NMF) algorithm. Specifically, it would be interesting to know whether the proposed method yields similar factor matrices (W and H) to IS-NMF and whether it achieves comparable speed and stability to the multiplicative update algorithm used in IS-NMF.

The proposed framework's ability to model the time-domain signal directly is a significant departure from traditional NMF, which models TF analysis coefficients. This shift in perspective enables the exploration of new signal representations, making the paper a valuable contribution to the field. Overall, the paper presents an interesting and innovative approach, and with additional comparisons and evaluations, it has the potential to make a significant impact in the signal processing community.
