- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal + transient) decompositions.  
- Generalizes Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model by expanding the implicit convolution to a time-domain expression for impulse responses.  
- Allows decomposition with nonorthogonal, potentially overcomplete dictionaries.  
- Presents an EM-based local-optimum estimation procedure for the proposed model.  
- Insightful idea; inclusion of overcomplete dictionaries is a notable contribution.  
- Potential complications from the extension are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Estimation procedure appears correct but is difficult to follow in detail.  
- Paper is well-motivated and explained but the empirical evaluation is superficial.  
- Only one illustrative result and one SNR improvement are provided, which is insufficient to draw conclusions.  
- Provided audio examples are appreciated, but the OMLSA comparison seems unfair due to differing durations.  
- Denoising on the music example introduces significant artifacts; conventional fixes (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might mitigate this.  
- Heavy reliance on Fevotte et al. limits perceived originality, though the time-domain interpretation is a new perspective.  
- The approach has significant potential impact if overcomplete bases work well, but the current evaluation fails to demonstrate this importance convincingly.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel GCM extension for multiresolution, time-domain perspective, and EM analysis procedure.  
- Con-acceptance: unclear implications, weak evaluation, unfair baseline comparison, artifact issues, and limited originality.
