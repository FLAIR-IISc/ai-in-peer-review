The proposed framework reformulates Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) by creating a generative model of the time-domain signal itself. This is achieved by modeling the time-frequency (TF) synthesis coefficients, in contrast to the standard NMF approach which models the TF analysis coefficients. A key advantage of this formulation is that it naturally accommodates multi-resolution representations, a capability not present in the traditional NMF setting.

This is an interesting contribution, and the multi-resolution extension is particularly novel and appealing.

While the paper presents results for multi-resolution signal decomposition, its performance in the single-resolution case remains unclear. A direct comparison with a standard method like Itakura-Saito NMF (IS-NMF) would significantly strengthen the paper. For instance, how do the learned factors (W and H) compare to those produced by IS-NMF? Furthermore, is the proposed algorithm comparable in terms of speed and stability to the widely-used multiplicative update rules for IS-NMF?
