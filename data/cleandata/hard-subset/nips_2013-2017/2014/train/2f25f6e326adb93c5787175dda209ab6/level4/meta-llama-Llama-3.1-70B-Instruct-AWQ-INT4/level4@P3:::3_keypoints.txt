- Proposes a novel Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) framework linking time-frequency synthesis to time-domain signals and NMF.  
- Framework is flexible and applicable to arbitrary transforms, including multi-resolution representations.  
- Theoretical development is robust, with a clear generative model and solid foundations.  
- Derivation of EM estimation algorithms is well-explained and sound.  
- Discussion of connections to sparse Bayesian learning is informative.  
- Writing, organization, notation, and figures are clear and accessible to domain readers.  
- Originality is above average due to the bridge between synthesis and NMF approaches.  
- Experimental results are limited and insufficiently comprehensive.  
- Supplemental material is not convincing and lacks depth.  
- Paper does not adequately compare the proposed method to prior related methods it builds on.  
- Significance and potential impact are not clearly communicated or emphasized.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add more experiments, comparisons, and clearer discussion of significance; overall rating 7/10.
