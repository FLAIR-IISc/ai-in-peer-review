

The paper introduces a novel framework that shifts the generative modeling perspective in non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the time-domain signal itself, achieved through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This fundamental reorientation enables multi-resolution signal representations, which were not feasible within the traditional NMF paradigm. The approach is conceptually interesting, particularly the multi-resolution extension, which I find compelling. However, the authors present results primarily on multi-resolution decompositions. I would like to understand the performance of the proposed method in the single-resolution case relative to IS-NMF. Specifically, does it recover similar factor matrices W and H? Additionally, is the proposed algorithm as computationally efficient and numerically stable as the standard multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF? Addressing these points would strengthen the evaluation of the method's practical utility.
