- Introduces extension of low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete, multiresolution bases (tonal/transient).
- Reformulates Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model into a time-domain expression using convolutions with multiple impulse responses.
- Enables adaptation to non-orthogonal, overcomplete impulse response sets and multiresolution analysis.
- Provides an EM-based estimation/analysis procedure, but is susceptible to local optima.
- Inclusion of overcomplete dictionaries is a valuable, novel extension of prior work.
- Paper reads well and is clearly motivated, though some derivation steps are hard to follow.
- The method appears complex and the paper doesn’t clearly explain how anticipated difficulties were mitigated.
- Evaluation is limited to a single illustrative result and one SNR improvement, insufficient for firm conclusions.
- Audio examples are provided but are ultimately unconvincing and show prominent artifacts.
- Comparison to OMLSA is questionable/unfair due to differing example lengths and noise conditions.
- Music denoising example exhibits noticeable artifacts; standard methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener) might yield better perceptual quality.
- Promising potential in applying matrix-factorization to overcomplete bases, but thin evaluation prevents assessing significance.
