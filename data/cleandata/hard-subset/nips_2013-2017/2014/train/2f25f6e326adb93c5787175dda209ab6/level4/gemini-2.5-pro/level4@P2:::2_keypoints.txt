- Paper introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative model for time-domain signals using synthesis coefficients with a low-rank variance (WH) structure.  
- Shifts modeling paradigm from analysis (STFT coefficients) to synthesis (time-domain signal generation).  
- Natural support for multi-resolution representations by combining multiple time-frequency bases of different resolutions.  
- Derives two estimation procedures via EM: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- MJLE derivation is sound and yields computationally feasible updates; MMLE is theoretically valuable but impractical due to large matrix inversion.  
- Experimental results (music decomposition, speech enhancement) show LRTFS is competitive and can outperform standard baselines.  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete: lacks direct comparison of single-resolution LRTFS vs standard GCM/IS-NMF on the same time-frequency grid.  
- Missing quantitative comparisons of learned W and H, computational cost, and convergence speed versus IS-NMF.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation and detailed mathematical derivations.  
- Relationship to related methods (GCM, SBL) is clearly explained; reproducibility is plausible but hyperparameter selection needs more detail.  
- Highly original: novel synthesis-based low-rank variance model and principled integration of multi-resolution analysis.  
- Significant contribution with likely impact on audio and biomedical signal decomposition and related tasks.  
- Strengths: principled generative model, elegant multi-resolution framework, clear and rigorous presentation.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: include single-resolution IS-NMF baseline comparisons, address or propose scalable MMLE approximations, and provide more details/strategy for hyperparameter (e.g., noise variance λ) selection.
