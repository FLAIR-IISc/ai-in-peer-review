- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal + transient) decompositions via time-domain convolutions with Gabor-like impulse responses.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and presents an EM-based local optimization procedure.  
- Inclusion of overcomplete dictionaries is a notable contribution but may introduce complications that the paper does not convincingly address.  
- Estimation procedure appears sound and analytically detailed, but some derivation/details were hard for the reviewer to follow.  
- Paper is well-written and motivated, but the empirical evaluation is very limited (one illustrative example and a single SNR measure).  
- Audio examples are unconvincing: OMLSA comparison seems unfair (different durations) and OMLSA retained higher speech quality despite more background noise.  
- Music denoising example exhibits significant artifacts; reviewer suspects classic methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might give better perceived quality.  
- Time-domain interpretation is an original, thought-provoking perspective despite heavy reliance on prior work.  
- The method has potential significance for applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases, but current results are superficial and the impact remains speculative.  
- Reformulating probabilistic matrix-factorization in time-domain kernels enables EM-based multiresolution analysis, yet thin evaluation and artifacts fail to substantiate its practical importance.
