- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to multiresolution/overcomplete bases (e.g., tonal + transient).
- Starts from Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model and gives a time-domain formulation expanding implicit Gabor convolutions.
- Presents an EM (local optimum) estimation procedure for the proposed model.
- Enables matrix-factorization on non-orthogonal, overcomplete dictionaries.
- EM analysis is well-presented and appears correct, though derivations were hard to follow in detail.
- At first glance seems like a reinterpretation of Fevotte’s GCM; practical implications are still unclear.
- Overcomplete dictionaries likely introduce complications that are not fully addressed or explained.
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement reported.
- Audio examples included but not convincing; denoised music shows significant artifacts.
- Comparison with OMLSA appears unfair (example lengths differ), undermining the comparison.
- Reviewer suspects conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceptual quality for the music example.
- Originality is limited by heavy reliance on prior work, though the time-domain interpretation is a useful new perspective.
- Potentially significant idea (multiresolution matrix factorization), but significance not demonstrated due to thin evaluation.
