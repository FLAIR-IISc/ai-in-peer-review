This paper presents a novel Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) framework that shifts the generative model from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the time-domain signal itself, via a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This approach uniquely enables multi-resolution representations, a capability absent in conventional NMF. 

The work is compelling, and the multi-resolution extension is particularly promising. 

However, alongside the demonstrated multi-resolution decompositions, I would be interested to see a performance comparison with Iterative Shrinkage-NMF (IS-NMF) in a single-resolution setting. Specifically, do the resulting W and H matrices exhibit similar characteristics? Furthermore, an assessment of the algorithm’s computational speed and stability relative to the multiplicative update rules commonly used for IS-NMF would be valuable. To reiterate the core contribution, this work distinguishes itself by modeling the generative process of the time-domain signal directly through TF synthesis coefficients, thereby unlocking multi-resolution analysis previously unattainable with standard NMF.
