1. Proposes LRTFS: a novel generative model for time-domain signals that models time-frequency synthesis coefficients with low-rank variance.  
2. Novelty/significance: shifts the probabilistic model from analysis to synthesis, bridging low-rank NMF and time-domain synthesis models.  
3. Multi-resolution capability: naturally supports combining different time-frequency bases to model transients and sustained components simultaneously.  
4. Methods: derives two EM algorithms for parameter estimation and applies the model to audio decomposition and speech enhancement.  
5. Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly positions the work relative to GCM and related techniques.  
6. Experimental weakness: lacks a direct, single-resolution comparison to the standard IS-NMF baseline, making trade-offs unclear.  
7. Question — single-resolution performance: needs an apples-to-apples evaluation (identical TF parameters) versus IS-NMF to assess benefits beyond multi-resolution.  
8. Question — factor interpretation: unclear whether LRTFS-learned W and H resemble those from IS-NMF; comparison would illuminate representation differences.  
9. Question — computational cost and stability: MJLE’s runtime, convergence speed, and robustness to initialization versus IS-NMF multiplicative updates are not analyzed.  
10. Summary: promising and well-motivated contribution with valuable multi-resolution extension, but requires more thorough single-resolution baselines and practical cost/behavior analysis.
