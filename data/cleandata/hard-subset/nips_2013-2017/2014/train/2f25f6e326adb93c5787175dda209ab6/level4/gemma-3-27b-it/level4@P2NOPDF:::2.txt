This paper presents an interesting approach to Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) by shifting the generative model from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the TF synthesis coefficients of a signal. This key distinction enables the development of multi-resolution representations, a capability not readily available within the standard NMF framework. The potential for multi-resolution analysis is a particularly appealing aspect of this work.

The authors demonstrate the method's application to multi-resolution signal decomposition, and the results are promising. However, I am curious about its performance in the single-resolution scenario. Specifically, a comparison to Iterative Shrinkage-NMF (IS-NMF) would be valuable. Do the resulting W and H matrices exhibit similar characteristics? Furthermore, how does the computational efficiency and stability of the proposed algorithm compare to the well-established multiplicative update algorithm used for IS-NMF? 

The core innovation—modeling the generative process of the time-domain signal through synthesis coefficients rather than analyzing existing TF representations—is a significant departure from traditional NMF. This allows for a more flexible and powerful approach, particularly when considering representations at varying resolutions. The ability to directly generate signals at different resolutions opens up new avenues for application and analysis. 

Overall, the paper presents a novel and potentially impactful extension to NMF. Addressing the questions regarding single-resolution performance and computational complexity relative to IS-NMF would strengthen the contribution and provide a more complete evaluation of the proposed method.
