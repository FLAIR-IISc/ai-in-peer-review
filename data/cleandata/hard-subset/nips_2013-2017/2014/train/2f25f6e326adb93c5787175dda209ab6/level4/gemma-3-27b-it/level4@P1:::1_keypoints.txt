- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to allow latent factors over arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) bases for multiresolution decomposition (e.g., tonal vs transient).  
- Builds on Févotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and derives a time-domain expression by expanding implicit convolution with per-row Gabor impulse responses.  
- Presents an EM algorithm for estimation, acknowledging potential local optima; technical analysis appears sound though some details are hard to follow.  
- Key novelty is the ability to incorporate overcomplete dictionaries and a time-domain reinterpretation of probabilistic matrix factorization.  
- The paper is well-explained and clearly motivated but could discuss the challenges and advantages of overcomplete bases more thoroughly.  
- Evaluation is weak and superficial, relying on a single SNR example and limited illustrative results.  
- Provided audio examples are appreciated but not convincing; comparisons may be unfair (e.g., OMLSA uses a longer segment).  
- OMLSA comparison shows higher speech quality despite more background noise, raising fairness concerns.  
- Music denoising example exhibits noticeable artifacts; conventional methods like wavelet shrinkage or Wiener filtering might yield better perceptual results.  
- Potential significance is high if overcomplete-factorization benefits hold, but current evidence is insufficient to judge impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to GCM, time-domain perspective, clear theoretical framework, and potential for improved source separation/denoising.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak evaluation, artifacts in examples, potentially unfair baselines, and insufficient discussion of limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on substantially strengthening experimental validation (multiple datasets, richer metrics beyond SNR, and fair comparisons).
