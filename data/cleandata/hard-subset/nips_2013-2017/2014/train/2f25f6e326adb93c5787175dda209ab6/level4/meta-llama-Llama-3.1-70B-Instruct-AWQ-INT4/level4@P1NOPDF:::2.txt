While the regular Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) framework models the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients of a signal, the proposed framework takes a more direct approach by modeling the time-domain signal itself through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This key difference enables the extension to multi-resolution representations, which were previously unattainable in traditional NMF settings.

This is an interesting paper, and I personally find the idea of multi-resolution extension particularly appealing. The authors present some results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, which are promising. However, I am also curious to know how the proposed method performs in the single-resolution case, specifically in comparison to IS-NMF. It would be beneficial to see whether the proposed algorithm yields similar W and H matrices, and whether it maintains the same level of speed and stability as the multiplicative update algorithm used in IS-NMF.

The proposed framework's focus on modeling the time-domain signal itself, rather than just its TF analysis coefficients, is a significant departure from the traditional NMF framework. By describing a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients, the authors open up new possibilities for multi-resolution representations, which were previously inaccessible in traditional NMF settings.
