

This paper presents an interesting extension to non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) by shifting the generative modeling perspective from time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients to the time-domain signal itself, achieved through a generative model for TF synthesis coefficients. This approach enables multi-resolution signal decompositions, which were previously unattainable within the standard NMF framework. I find the multi-resolution extension particularly compelling.

The authors demonstrate promising results for multi-resolution decompositions. However, to better assess the method's value, it would be valuable to see a comparison in the single-resolution setting against established approaches like IS-NMF. Specifically: Does the proposed algorithm recover comparable basis matrices (W) and activation matrices (H)? Furthermore, how does its computational speed and numerical stability compare to the multiplicative update algorithm used in IS-NMF? Addressing these points would strengthen the evaluation and clarify the method's practical advantages. The core contribution—enabling multi-resolution analysis through synthesis-based modeling—remains a significant and well-motivated advancement.
