- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete (multiresolution) bases for tonal/transient decomposition.  
- Generalizes Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model by introducing convolution with multiple Gabor impulse responses and a time-domain formulation.  
- Time-domain expression can accommodate different, including non-orthogonal, impulse-response bases and an EM estimation procedure is provided.  
- Idea is interesting and the ability to use overcomplete dictionaries is a welcome contribution.  
- Implications and potential complications of the approach are unclear and not fully addressed.  
- Estimation procedure appears correct and the analysis is impressive, but the technical details are hard to follow.  
- Paper is well-motivated and explained, but evaluation is superficial (single illustrative result and SNR measure only).  
- Audio examples are unconvincing; comparison to OMLSA is unfair due to differing clip lengths and noise levels.  
- Music denoising shows significant artifacts, which might be avoided with conventional techniques (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering).  
- Originality depends heavily on prior work (Fevotte et al.), though the time-domain reinterpretation is novel.  
- Potential significance is high (matrix factorization on overcomplete bases) but thin evaluation and artifacts prevent demonstrating this impact.
