

This paper offers a thoughtful reinterpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on conventional formulations built upon STFT analysis coefficients, it introduces a more versatile framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and explicitly connected to the time-domain signal. The manuscript is accessible to experts in the field and demonstrates strong technical quality. Originality is above average, though the significance of the contribution remains insufficiently articulated. The most notable shortcoming concerns the results and evaluation; the supplemental material lacks persuasiveness, and the paper fails to provide comparative analysis with the foundational methods it extends. While this absence does not constitute a significant weakness—given the robust theoretical development alone makes the work compelling—it would have substantially strengthened the paper. Overall, it is a well-written, theoretically sound contribution. Additional results and comparisons would have been valuable, but this is not a critical deficiency given the interest and strength of the theoretical advances.
