- Proposes extending low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary (possibly overcomplete) bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal+transient) decomposition.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and makes explicit the convolution with Gabor impulse responses.  
- Derives a purely time-domain expression for estimates, allowing adaptation to nonorthogonal impulse responses.  
- Provides an EM-based local-optimum analysis procedure for the proposed model.  
- Central idea is compelling and attractive for incorporating overcomplete dictionaries, though full implications are not yet clear.  
- Reviewer could not follow every analytic detail but is willing to assume the estimator is correct; raises concerns about complications from overcompleteness.  
- Presentation and motivation are clear, but empirical evaluation is minimal (one illustrative example and one SNR figure).  
- Audio demos are unconvincing: OMLSA comparison may be unfair due to differing clip lengths, and OMLSA perceived as higher-quality despite more background noise.  
- Music denoising examples show substantial artifacts; reviewer expects conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener) might yield better perceived quality.  
- Originality limited by heavy reliance on Fevotte’s framework, though the time-domain reframing provides a new perspective.  
- Potentially significant if overcomplete matrix-factorization is practical, but sparse evaluation and demo artifacts do not yet establish impact.
