- Proposes LRTFS: a generative model placing a low-rank prior on synthesis coefficients’ variances to model raw waveform x(t), unlike GCM/IS-NMF that models STFT analysis coefficients.
- Shows LRTFS yields α ~ Gaussian with variance v = WH and x = Φα + e, and connects this to Wiener filtering and SBL.
- Presents two estimators: MJLE via an EM alternating Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF updates, and MMLE (type-II) by integrating out α, though MMLE is not yet scalable to audio-scale problems.
- Highlights natural handling of overcomplete and multi-resolution dictionaries by stacking frames (e.g., long/short Gabor frames).
- EM algorithm is computationally practical (avoids large matrix inversions) assuming fast analysis/synthesis with Φ.
- Derivations are plausible but compressed; missing details include the EM Q-function, surrogate leading to log(|α|^2), justification for λ update, and conditions on β and Φ.
- MMSE component approximation to avoid T×T inversion is reasonable but its trade-offs versus optimal posterior means (especially in overcomplete settings) need discussion.
- Paper is clearly written and situates the work well relative to IS-NMF/GCM, SBL, and MCA, but experiments are thin and raise fairness and robustness questions.
- Main novelty: placing low-rank prior on synthesis coefficients for a fully generative waveform model that supports multi-resolution, overcomplete representations.
- Potential significance is substantial for audio denoising/separation, but empirical evidence is limited and reported SNR gains are modest (0.6 dB over IS-NMF) with audible artifacts.
- Pros: conceptually clean synthesis-oriented model, handles multi-resolution/overcomplete frames, practical EM algorithm, and strong links to Wiener filtering and SBL.
- Cons: limited/partly informal experiments, fairness concerns in comparisons, no strong multi-resolution baselines, missing run-time/memory reporting, MMLE not scalable, and omitted implementation details with λ treated as tuned.
- Recommended evaluation improvements: standardized benchmarks (SiSEC/CHiME), full metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ, STOI), confidence intervals, matched baseline conditions, and additional baselines (multi-frame IS-NMF, MCA, wavelet shrinkage).
- Methodological suggestions: include full EM derivations and conditions, clarify λ and β updates and warm-restart, analyze MMSE approximation trade-offs, and outline scalable MMLE approaches (iterative solvers, AMP, variational methods).
- Reproducibility suggestions: release code and audio, provide pseudocode, parameter settings (β, λ, K), run-times, stopping criteria, and sensitivity analyses.
- Overall assessment: conceptually interesting and promising synthesis formulation with a practical estimator, but under-supported empirically and needing more methodological and experimental detail to be fully convincing.
