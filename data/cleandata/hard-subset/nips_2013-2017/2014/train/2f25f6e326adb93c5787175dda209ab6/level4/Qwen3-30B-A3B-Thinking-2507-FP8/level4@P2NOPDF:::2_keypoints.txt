- Paper proposes shifting NMF’s generative model from TF analysis coefficients to time-domain signal via a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients.  
- This shift enables multi-resolution signal representations not available in standard NMF.  
- The core idea is compelling and addresses a meaningful limitation of existing approaches.  
- Multi-resolution decomposition capability is promising and well-motivated.  
- Evaluation lacks a thorough single-resolution (baseline) comparison to IS-NMF.  
- Need comparison of factor matrices (W, H): do proposed method and IS-NMF converge to similar or distinct solutions on the same data?  
- Need assessment of computational efficiency and numerical stability versus IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates; is the overhead justified?  
- Without rigorous single-resolution comparisons, the claim of improvement over IS-NMF is unsubstantiated.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, but empirical validation against the single-resolution baseline is missing and required.
