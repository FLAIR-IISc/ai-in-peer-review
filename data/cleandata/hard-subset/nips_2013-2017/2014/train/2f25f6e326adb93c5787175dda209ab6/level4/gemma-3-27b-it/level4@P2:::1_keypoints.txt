- Introduces a novel extension of low-rank spectrogram factorization based on Févotte et al.’s GCM.  
- Generalizes latent factors to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete, basis sets enabling multi-resolution decomposition.  
- Reformulates variance modeling in the time domain, allowing diverse impulse responses and non-orthogonal bases.  
- Presents an EM algorithm for parameter estimation in the proposed model.  
- Bridges time-frequency synthesis and traditional NMF, offering a flexible signal representation framework.  
- Theoretical development and EM derivation are strong, though some derivations are hard to follow.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only one illustrative example and a single SNR improvement measurement.  
- Audio examples are included but do not show compelling perceptual improvements.  
- Comparison to OMLSA may be unfair due to differing signal lengths favoring OMLSA.  
- Denoising examples show noticeable artifacts; simpler methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener) might yield better perceptual quality.  
- Originality comes from the time-domain interpretation of GCM and ability to use overcomplete bases.  
- Potential significance is substantial for signal processing, but current evidence is insufficient.  
- Main strengths: novel extension, theoretical soundness, potential multi-resolution benefits, and clear presentation.  
- Main weaknesses: weak experiments, artifacts in results, limited practical demonstration, and reliance on EM without convergence analysis.  
- Recommendation: weak reject with encouragement to expand experiments, address artifacts, and compare against state-of-the-art with standardized datasets and metrics.
