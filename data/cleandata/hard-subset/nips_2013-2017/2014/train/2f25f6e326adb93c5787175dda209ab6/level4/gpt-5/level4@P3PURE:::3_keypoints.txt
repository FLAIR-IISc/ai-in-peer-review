- Proposes a flexible reinterpretation of time–frequency factorizations applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly tied to the time-domain signal.  
- Moves beyond STFT-analysis-coefficient formulations to a more general framework.  
- Paper is clearly written and of good quality for readers in the area.  
- Originality is above average but the paper does not fully communicate the significance of the contribution.  
- Main weakness is empirical: results and evaluation are limited and the supplemental material is unconvincing.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to the existing methods it builds on, reducing empirical substantiation.  
- Theoretical development is solid and interesting, making the paper worthwhile despite empirical gaps.  
- Overall assessment: good paper with compelling theory but would be substantially strengthened by more results and direct comparisons.
