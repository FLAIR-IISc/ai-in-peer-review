- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to allow overcomplete/multiresolution bases (e.g., tonal + transient) for sound decomposition.
- Generalizes Févotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model to a time-domain expression that supports non-orthogonal bases.
- Proposes an EM algorithm for parameter estimation, acknowledging possible convergence to local optima.
- Theoretical development and derivations appear logically consistent and sound.
- Novel contribution: time-domain interpretation and EM-based probabilistic matrix-factorization for overcomplete bases.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and well contextualized in prior work.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and superficial, relying on illustrative examples rather than thorough experiments.
- Speech enhancement result uses a single SNR example, providing weak evidence of effectiveness.
- Included audio examples are useful but their impact is diminished by superficial evaluation.
- Comparison to OMLSA is problematic due to differing signal lengths and perceived better audio quality of OMLSA.
- Music decomposition exhibits noticeable artifacts, raising doubts about practical performance versus established methods (e.g., wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering).
- Significance is potentially high but currently unproven due to insufficient experiments and observed artifacts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, theoretically sound, well-motivated, and potentially impactful.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, observed artifacts, questionable comparisons, and lack of comprehensive experimentation.
