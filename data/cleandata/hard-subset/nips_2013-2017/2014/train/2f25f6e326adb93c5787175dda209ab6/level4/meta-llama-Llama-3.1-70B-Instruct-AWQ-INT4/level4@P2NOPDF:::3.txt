This paper provides a thoughtful and novel interpretation of time/frequency factorizations, departing from existing formulations based on the analysis coefficients of Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) transforms. Instead, it proposes a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform, offering a direct link to the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers familiar with the subject area, demonstrating good quality throughout. The originality of the work is above average, although the significance of the contributions could be more effectively communicated. One of the most notable shortcomings of the paper lies in its results and evaluation. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper fails to compare the proposed method to existing approaches it builds upon. While this omission is not a critical weakness, as the theoretical development is substantial enough to make the paper interesting, it would have significantly strengthened the paper.

Overall, the paper presents solid theory, and the writing is of high quality. Although the lack of comprehensive results and comparisons is a notable limitation, the theoretical contribution is intriguing enough to make this a good paper. With more thorough evaluation and comparison to existing methods, the paper could have been even more compelling.
