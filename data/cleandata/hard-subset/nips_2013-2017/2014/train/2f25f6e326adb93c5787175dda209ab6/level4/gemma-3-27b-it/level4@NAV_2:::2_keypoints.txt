- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model of the raw signal extending NMF beyond spectrogram-only models.  
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations not achievable with conventional NMF.  
- Builds on the known connection between IS-NMF and a Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) of the STFT, addressing GCM’s limitation of modeling only the STFT.  
- Core idea of modeling synthesis coefficients directly is compelling and logically sound.  
- Presents two EM-based estimation algorithms, showing strong theoretical understanding.  
- Experiments (music decomposition and speech enhancement) provide initial validation of the approach.  
- Multi-resolution extension (Section 4) is a notable contribution with interesting application potential.  
- Connection to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) is well-explained and situates the work within existing methods.  
- Strengths: clear mathematical formulation, novelty of the synthesis-based generative model, and multi-resolution capability.  
- Presentation is well-organized and citations are relevant.  
- Limitation: MMLE computational complexity and scalability concerns for large-scale applications.  
- Comparative evaluation is insufficient—missing detailed single-resolution comparisons to standard IS-NMF (e.g., W and H similarity, speed, stability vs multiplicative updates).  
- Experimental scope should include more comprehensive quantitative evaluations and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Recommendation: promising and worthy of acceptance provided authors address comparative evaluation and scalability concerns in a revision.
