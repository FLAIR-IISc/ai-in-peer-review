- Reformulates NMF by building a generative model of the time-domain signal via modeling time–frequency synthesis coefficients (vs. standard analysis-coefficient NMF).  
- Key advantage: naturally supports multi-resolution representations, which traditional NMF does not.  
- Contribution judged interesting; the multi-resolution extension is novel and appealing.  
- Shortcoming: single-resolution performance is unclear and not evaluated.  
- Missing direct comparison to standard methods (e.g., Itakura–Saito NMF) to validate quality of learned factors W and H.  
- Missing evaluation of algorithm speed and stability versus common IS-NMF multiplicative update rules.
