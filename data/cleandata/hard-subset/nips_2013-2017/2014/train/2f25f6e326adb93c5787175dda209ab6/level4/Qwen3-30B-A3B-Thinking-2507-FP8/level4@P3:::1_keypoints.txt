- Paper introduces LRTFS: a generative low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that generates raw audio from basis atoms with Var(α_fn) = [W H]_fn.  
- LRTFS extends GCM by modelling time-domain signals rather than only STFT coefficients.  
- Core novelty: supports multi-resolution representations (e.g., separate tonal and transient bases) absent in traditional NMF approaches.  
- Authors derive two EM estimation algorithms (MJLE and MMLE); MJLE is computationally feasible for audio and avoids costly matrix inversions.  
- Technical derivations are sound and build coherently on GCM and sparse Bayesian learning literature.  
- MJLE is well-motivated and practical for audio processing applications.  
- Paper demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Reported speech enhancement: SNR improvement of 6.6 dB relative to IS-NMF baseline (6.0 dB).  
- Evaluation is limited: music decomposition is illustrative with no quantitative metrics beyond SNR.  
- Speech enhancement experiments use a single dataset (TIMIT) and lack comparisons to recent deep-learning baselines.  
- Claim that music artifacts are “very significant” is unsubstantiated; supplementary audio shows only mild, typical low-rank artifacts.  
- Paper should compare artifacts to conventional methods (e.g., wavelet denoising) and quantify perceptual quality.  
- Minor experimental inconsistencies: OMLSA comparison used a 7s signal vs LRTFS 6s, potentially biasing SNR—signal lengths should be explicitly stated.  
- Lack of visualizations of reconstructed speech beyond spectrograms reduces interpretability.  
- Originality: substantive extension of GCM to time-domain synthesis and multi-resolution modeling; not just a reinterpretation.  
- Significance: multi-resolution capability is promising for audio tasks and could extend to inverse problems (e.g., multichannel separation), but current experiments do not fully prove practical advantage.  
- Missing evaluations: no ablation on resolution choices, limited noise types, and no perceptual metrics or broader baselines.  
- Pros: novel raw-audio generative model, multi-resolution capability, efficient MJLE algorithm, clear and rigorous exposition.  
- Cons: narrow evaluation, marginal SNR gain without context, insufficient artifact analysis and comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — technically solid and novel, but needs expanded experiments (more datasets, modern baselines, perceptual analysis) to strengthen impact.
