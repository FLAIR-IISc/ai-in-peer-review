This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations, offering a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time domain signal, as opposed to existing formulations based on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms.

The paper is well-written and of good quality, making it easily accessible to readers familiar with the area. Its originality is above average, although the significance of the work could be more effectively communicated. One of the most disappointing aspects of the paper is the presentation and evaluation of the results. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper lacks a comparison with the methods it builds upon. While this omission is not a significant weakness, as the theoretical development is substantial enough to make the paper interesting, including such comparisons would have greatly enhanced the paper.

Overall, the paper is well-written, with solid theoretical contributions. Although more comprehensive results and comparisons would have been desirable, the theoretical contribution is engaging and compensates for this limitation, making the paper's shortcomings not fatal.
