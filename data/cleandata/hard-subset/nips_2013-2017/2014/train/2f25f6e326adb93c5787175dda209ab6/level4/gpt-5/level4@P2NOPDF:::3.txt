This paper offers a thoughtful reformulation of time–frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on formulations built on STFT analysis coefficients, it introduces a more flexible framework that can operate on arbitrary transforms and remains directly connected to the time-domain signal.

The paper is clearly written and accessible to readers in this area. Its originality is above average, although the practical significance is not fully articulated. The main weakness lies in the results and evaluation: the supplementary material is not especially persuasive, and the paper does not compare its approach to the very methods it builds upon. I do not view this as a fatal flaw—the theoretical development is strong and, on its own, makes the work interesting—but more comprehensive empirical validation and direct comparisons would significantly strengthen the paper.

Overall, this is a good, well-written paper with solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and head-to-head comparisons, but their absence does not undermine the core theoretical contribution.
