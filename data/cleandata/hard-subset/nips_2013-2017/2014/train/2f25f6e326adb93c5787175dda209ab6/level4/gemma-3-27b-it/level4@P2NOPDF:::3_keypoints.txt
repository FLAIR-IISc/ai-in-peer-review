- Proposes a compelling re-interpretation of time-frequency factorization that generalizes beyond STFT to arbitrary transforms while preserving a link to the time-domain signal.
- Paper is clearly written and accessible to researchers familiar with time-frequency analysis.
- Theoretical development is high quality and shows above-average originality.
- The paper’s significance is not communicated as effectively as it could be.
- Primary area for improvement is the presentation of results and the evaluation methodology.
- Supplemental material does not provide strong evidence to support the paper’s claims.
- The manuscript lacks comparative analysis against existing, related methods.
- Although not fatal, the omission of empirical comparisons weakens the paper’s practical persuasiveness.
- More thorough empirical validation and direct comparisons would significantly strengthen the work.
- Overall assessment: a good paper with a solid theoretical foundation, but would benefit from more extensive results and comparative analyses.
