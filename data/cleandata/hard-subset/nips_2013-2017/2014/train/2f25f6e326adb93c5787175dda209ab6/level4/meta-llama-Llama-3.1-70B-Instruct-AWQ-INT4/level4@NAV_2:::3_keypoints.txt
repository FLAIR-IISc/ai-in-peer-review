- Proposes a flexible time/frequency factorization framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly linked to the time-domain signal.  
- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel generative model of the raw signal rather than STFT coefficients.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to the time domain, enabling layered representations with distinct time resolutions and latent low-rank structure.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to researchers in the area.  
- Theoretical development is solid, novel, and has potential to advance the field.  
- Relates LRTFS to sparse Bayesian learning concepts.  
- Presents estimation approaches using MJLE and MMLE, with well-described EM algorithms.  
- Discusses complexity and limitations of the proposed estimation methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement lack extensive comparisons to existing methods.  
- Supplemental material is not particularly convincing.  
- The paper’s overall significance is not fully communicated.  
- Strengths: novel/flexible framework, clarity, solid theory, potential impact.  
- Weaknesses: limited evaluation/comparisons, weak supplementary material, unclear communicated significance.  
- Recommendation: accept, with the suggestion to provide more extensive evaluation and comparisons in future work.
