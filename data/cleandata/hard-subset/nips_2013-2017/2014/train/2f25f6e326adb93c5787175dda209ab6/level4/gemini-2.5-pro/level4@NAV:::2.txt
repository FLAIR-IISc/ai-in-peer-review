In contrast to conventional NMF approaches that are based on a generative model of a signal's Time-Frequency (TF) analysis coefficients, the proposed framework introduces a generative model for the time-domain signal itself, formulated through its TF synthesis coefficients. A key advantage of this approach is that it enables multi-resolution representations, a capability absent in traditional NMF settings.

This paper presents a compelling contribution. The proposed multi-resolution extension is a particularly novel and promising aspect of the work.

While the evaluation focuses on multi-resolution signal decompositions, the analysis would be strengthened by an examination of the method's performance in the single-resolution scenario. A direct comparison against a standard baseline, such as IS-NMF, is warranted. Specifically, it would be valuable to understand if the resulting W and H factor matrices are comparable and whether the proposed algorithm's convergence speed and stability are competitive with the standard multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF.
