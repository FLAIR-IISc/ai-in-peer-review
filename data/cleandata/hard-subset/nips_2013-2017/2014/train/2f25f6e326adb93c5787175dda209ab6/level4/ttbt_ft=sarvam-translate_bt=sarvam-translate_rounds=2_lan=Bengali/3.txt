This paper presents a thoughtful explanation of time/frequency factorization. Instead of using existing formulas based on STFT transformation analysis coefficients, it presents a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transformation and is directly linked to the time domain signal.

This paper is easy to read and of good quality for those working in this field. The originality is above average, although the significance is not fully revealed in this paper. Perhaps the most disappointing aspect of this paper is the results and evaluation. The supporting elements are not particularly convincing, and the paper itself does not compare this work with its underlying methodology. I don't think it is a significantly weak aspect (theoretical development itself is enough to make it an interesting paper), but I think it would have made the paper even better. Good paper, good writing, solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, but this is not a fatal flaw in this paper, as the theoretical contribution is very interesting.
