- Proposes extending low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete multiresolution bases with a time-domain interpretation and EM-based estimation.  
- Presents a new perspective on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model and enables inclusion of overcomplete dictionaries.  
- Estimation procedure seems correct and the analytical treatment is impressive, though some derivations are hard to follow.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative example and one SNR measurement, insufficient to support claims.  
- Audio examples do not demonstrate a clear advantage; an OMLSA-enhanced example sounds higher quality despite more noise.  
- Denoising on the music example produces significant artifacts that conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) could avoid.  
- Heavy reliance on prior GCM work; unclear what fundamentally new insights this reinterpretation provides.  
- The ability to apply matrix-factorization to overcomplete bases could be significant, but current results do not demonstrate that impact.  
- Recommendation: reject with invitation to resubmit after more thorough evaluation and stronger empirical demonstration.
