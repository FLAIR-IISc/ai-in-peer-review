- Proposes a novel, elegant framework that generalizes time-frequency factorizations to arbitrary linear transforms and operates directly on time-domain signals.  
- Decouples factorization from specific transforms, offering a more fundamental and flexible conceptual advance.  
- Strong theoretical development with a solid, well-reasoned mathematical foundation.  
- Paper is clearly written and accessible to readers with relevant background.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak; presented results and supplemental material do not convincingly demonstrate practical advantages.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to established baselines, making it difficult to assess performance, computational efficiency, or interpretability gains.  
- Fails to clearly articulate the practical impact or specific problems where the method outperforms existing approaches; motivation could be strengthened.  
- Overall: substantial and interesting theoretical contribution, but would be much stronger with more comprehensive experiments and comparisons.
