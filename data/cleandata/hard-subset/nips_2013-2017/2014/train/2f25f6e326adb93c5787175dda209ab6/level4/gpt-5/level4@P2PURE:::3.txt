This paper offers a thoughtful reinterpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations built on the analysis coefficients of STFTs, it proposes a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly tied to the time-domain signal.

The paper is clearly written for readers in this area and is of good quality. Its originality is above average, though the significance of the contribution is not fully conveyed. The most disappointing aspect is the results and evaluation: the supplementary material is not particularly convincing, and the paper does not compare this approach to the methods it builds upon. I do not consider this a major weakness—the theoretical development alone makes the work interesting—but stronger empirical results and comparisons would have substantially improved the paper.

Overall, it is a good, well-written paper with solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, but this is not a fatal flaw given the strength of the theoretical contribution.
