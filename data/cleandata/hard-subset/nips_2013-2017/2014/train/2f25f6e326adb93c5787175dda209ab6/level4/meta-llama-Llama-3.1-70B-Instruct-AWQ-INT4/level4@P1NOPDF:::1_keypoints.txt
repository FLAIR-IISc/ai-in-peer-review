- Paper extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal + transient) decompositions.
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model but provides a time-domain interpretation using multiple Gabor impulse responses.
- Presents an EM estimation procedure for the proposed model and parameter learning in the time-domain formulation.
- The extension to overcomplete dictionaries is appealing but raises potential complications that are not fully explained or justified.
- The authors’ analysis is detailed and impressive, but parts are hard to follow and the correctness of the estimation is assumed rather than fully validated by the reviewer.
- Clarity and motivation are good, but empirical evaluation is very limited and superficial (single illustrative result + SNR improvement).
- Audio examples are included but are not convincing; perceived quality issues and artifacts reduce their impact.
- Comparison fairness is questionable (e.g., OMLSA example is longer and contains more background noise), undermining the evaluation.
- Denoising results exhibit significant artifacts that conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) would likely avoid.
- Originality is moderate: largely builds on prior work but offers a new time-domain perspective.
- Significance is potential but unproven due to thin evaluation and lack of convincing experimental evidence.
