The paper departs from conventional NMF, which models a signal’s time–frequency (TF) analysis coefficients, by instead modeling the time-domain signal through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This shift is compelling because it naturally accommodates multi-resolution representations that are not feasible in the standard NMF framework.

Overall, the work is interesting and well motivated. I find the multi-resolution extension particularly appealing.

Beyond the presented multi-resolution results, I would like to see an evaluation in the single-resolution setting compared with IS-NMF. Specifically:
- Do the learned factors W and H resemble those obtained by IS-NMF under comparable conditions?
- How do convergence speed and numerical stability compare with the standard IS-NMF multiplicative updates?

Addressing these points would clarify the relationship to established methods and strengthen the empirical claims.
