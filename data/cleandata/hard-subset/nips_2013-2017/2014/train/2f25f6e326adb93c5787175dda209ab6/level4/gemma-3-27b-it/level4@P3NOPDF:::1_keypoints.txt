- Extends Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to overcomplete, multiresolution bases using a time-domain Gabor-impulse formulation.  
- Targets improved decomposition into tonal and transient components for source separation and denoising.  
- Derives a purely time-domain expression and generalizes to non-orthogonal impulse responses.  
- Proposes an EM procedure for parameter estimation and analyzes its local optima.  
- Mathematical/analytical development is impressive though some derivations are hard to follow.  
- Ability to use overcomplete dictionaries is a valuable extension but potential complications from overcompleteness are not fully addressed.  
- Paper is well-explained and clearly motivated.  
- Evaluation is weak and preliminary: only one illustrative result and a single SNR measurement.  
- Sound examples are not compelling; OMLSA comparison is unfair (different clip lengths) and OMLSA yields apparently better speech quality.  
- Musical denoising shows significant audible artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) may perform better.  
- Claims of significance are promising but largely conjectural given the limited evaluation.  
- Recommendation: substantially expand experimental comparisons, address artifacting, and analyze computational complexity.
