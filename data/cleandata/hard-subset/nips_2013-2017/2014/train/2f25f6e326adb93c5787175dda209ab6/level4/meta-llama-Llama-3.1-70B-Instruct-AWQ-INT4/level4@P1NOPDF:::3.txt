This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations, offering a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time domain signal, rather than relying on existing formulations based on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow for those familiar with this area, indicating a good level of quality. The originality of the work is above average, although the significance of the contribution could be more effectively communicated. The most disappointing aspect of the paper lies in its results and evaluation. The supplemental material does not provide convincing evidence, and the paper itself lacks a comparison with the methods it builds upon. While this omission is not a significant weakness, it would have strengthened the paper considerably.

Despite this limitation, the theoretical development presented in the paper is substantial enough to make it an interesting contribution. The paper is well-written, with solid theoretical foundations. Although more results and comparisons would have been desirable, this is not a fatal flaw, given the interesting nature of the theoretical contribution.
