This paper offers a compelling re-examination of time/frequency factorizations. Departing from conventional approaches based on Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) analysis coefficients, it introduces a more versatile framework applicable to a wider range of transforms and directly connected to the time-domain signal. 

The paper is clearly written and demonstrates a good understanding of the relevant field. Its originality is notably strong, though the full impact of this work could be more effectively highlighted. A primary area for improvement lies in the presentation of results and evaluation; the supplemental material lacks strong evidence, and the paper would benefit from a comparative analysis against existing methods upon which it builds. While this omission doesn’t fundamentally detract from the paper’s value – the theoretical development alone constitutes a significant contribution – incorporating such comparisons would substantially strengthen the work. Overall, this is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation. More extensive results and comparative analyses would be welcome, but their absence does not invalidate the interesting theoretical advances presented herein.
