- Proposes LRTFS: a novel generative model that directly models raw signals via synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-resolution time-frequency representations absent in NMF-based methods.  
- Extends prior GCM work by shifting from analysis to synthesis coefficients within a low-rank framework.  
- Provides a clear probabilistic formulation and links the method to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Derives EM algorithms for parameter estimation and demonstrates feasibility on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically structured.  
- Missing experimental comparison to IS-NMF in the single-resolution case (no results on W/H similarity, relative speed, or stability).  
- Computational complexity and scalability (especially for MMLE and large signals) are not thoroughly analyzed; MMLE results are not presented.  
- Potential sensitivity to hyperparameter λ and reliance on warm-restart suggest need for more rigorous hyperparameter robustness analysis.  
- Speech enhancement evaluation uses only OMLSA as baseline; lacks comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art methods.  
- For acceptance: presents a genuinely novel, theoretically sound approach with valuable multi-resolution capability.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient comparative evaluation and unresolved scalability concerns weaken the paper’s empirical claims.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires stronger experimental comparisons and a deeper discussion of computational scalability.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, conditional on addressing IS-NMF comparison and scalability issues.
