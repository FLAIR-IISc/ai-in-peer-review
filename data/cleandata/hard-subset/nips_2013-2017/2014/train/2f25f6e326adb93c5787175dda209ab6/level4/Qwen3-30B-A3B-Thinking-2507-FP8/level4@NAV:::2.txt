

The paper introduces a novel framework that shifts the generative modeling perspective from the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients (as in standard NMF) to the time-domain signal itself, achieved through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This approach enables multi-resolution representations, which are unattainable within the conventional NMF paradigm.  
This is an interesting contribution, particularly the multi-resolution extension, which I find compelling.  
While the authors demonstrate results for multi-resolution signal decompositions, I seek clarification on the single-resolution case: how does the proposed method compare to IS-NMF in terms of factor matrices (W and H), computational speed, and numerical stability? Specifically, do the factor matrices align with those from IS-NMF, and is the algorithm as efficient and robust as the multiplicative update scheme for IS-NMF?
