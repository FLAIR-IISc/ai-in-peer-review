This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time-frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations based on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it proposes a more flexible framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly linked to the time-domain signal. 

The paper is well-written and readily accessible to researchers in this area, demonstrating good quality overall. The originality is above average; however, the significance of the contribution is not fully conveyed. A primary weakness lies in the results and evaluation. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper lacks a comparison to the methods upon which it builds. While this does not represent a critical flaw – the theoretical development is interesting in its own right – incorporating such comparisons would have substantially strengthened the work. Overall, this is a good paper with solid theoretical foundations. I would have appreciated a more extensive presentation of results and comparative analyses, but their absence is not a fatal shortcoming given the inherent interest of the theoretical contribution.
