- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to allow decomposition over arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) multiresolution bases (tonal + transient).  
- Generalizes Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model by expanding implicit convolution to a time-domain expression supporting non-orthogonal impulse responses.  
- Presents an EM-based (local optimum) analysis/estimation procedure for the proposed model.  
- Inclusion of overcomplete dictionaries is a significant advantage for source separation and denoising.  
- Paper offers an interesting insight but would benefit from clearer explanation of its broader implications.  
- Unclear why added complications from overcomplete bases do not cause more practical difficulties.  
- Estimation procedure appears correct and the analysis is impressive, though some derivations are hard to follow.  
- Work is well-explained and motivated overall.  
- Evaluation is limited: only one illustrative example and one SNR improvement measurement, insufficient for strong conclusions.  
- Sound examples are appreciated, but comparisons to other methods (e.g., OMLSA) may be unfair.  
- Denoising example introduces significant artifacts that can make results perceptually worse than conventional methods.  
- Originality is moderate: heavily builds on Fevotte et al. but provides a new time-domain interpretation.  
- Potentially significant impact if matrix-factorization can be applied reliably to overcomplete bases, but current experiments are too few to demonstrate this.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel extension enabling overcomplete bases, solid EM analysis, and promising applications to separation/denoising.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited/possibly unfair evaluation, perceptual artifacts in denoising, and need for more comprehensive comparisons.
