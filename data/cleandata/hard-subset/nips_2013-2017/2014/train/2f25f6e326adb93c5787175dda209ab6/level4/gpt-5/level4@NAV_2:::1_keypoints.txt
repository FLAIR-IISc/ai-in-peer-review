- Proposes LRTFS: a synthesis-domain low-rank variance model that generates the waveform from arbitrary time–frequency frames, supporting overcomplete and multiresolution representations (e.g., tonal/transient layers).  
- Moves from the GCM/STFT variance view to a time-domain generative formulation, enabling arbitrary (including nonorthogonal) dictionaries.  
- Presents EM-based estimation: a scalable MJLE scheme and a less scalable MMLE variant.  
- Conceptually interesting and potentially useful; accommodating overcomplete frames and multiresolution is appealing.  
- Largely a reinterpretation/extension of GCM and IS-NMF lineage, but the synthesis-domain angle is a fresh contribution.  
- Derivations appear careful and the estimation procedure seems correct, though the reviewer did not verify every step.  
- Lacks discussion of potential pitfalls (e.g., conditioning/identifiability with highly redundant frames) and how to mitigate them.  
- Presentation and EM explanation are clear, but the experimental section is thin.  
- Empirics are limited to one music decomposition and a single speech SNR-improvement, insufficient to support strong claims.  
- Audio examples are helpful but unconvincing; comparisons may be mismatched (OMLSA output length discrepancy) and could be unfair.  
- Denoising introduces noticeable artifacts; simple baselines (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceived quality.  
- Significance is promising in principle for separation/enhancement, but current limited and artifact-prone evaluations do not substantiate practical impact.
