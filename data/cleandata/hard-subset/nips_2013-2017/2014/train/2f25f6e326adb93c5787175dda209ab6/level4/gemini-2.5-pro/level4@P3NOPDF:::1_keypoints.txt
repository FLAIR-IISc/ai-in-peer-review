- Proposes a reformulation of low-rank spectrogram-factorization that generalizes latent factors to arbitrary, potentially overcomplete, basis functions for multiresolution decomposition (e.g., tonal + transient).
- Builds on the Gaussian Composite Model (Févotte et al.) and expands the STFT convolution into a purely time-domain expression to allow non-orthogonal and overcomplete impulse-response dictionaries.
- Presents an EM algorithm to obtain a local optimum for the proposed model parameters.
- Core technical insight—time-domain kernel reformulation to accommodate overcomplete bases—is novel and conceptually interesting.
- Enables application of matrix factorization techniques to multiresolution dictionaries, offering notable theoretical potential.
- Heavily grounded in prior work by Févotte et al., but the time-domain interpretation is an original advance.
- Paper does not fully explore implications of overcomplete bases, particularly uniqueness and stability issues of the decomposition.
- The EM estimation derivation is rigorous but complex and hard to verify on first reading.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear motivation and logical theoretical development.
- Experimental evaluation is severely limited—only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement measurement—insufficient to support practical claims.
- Audio examples are unconvincing and raise concerns: unfair comparisons (mismatched durations), inferior perceptual quality versus baseline, and significant distracting artifacts in music denoising.
- Conclusion: interesting theoretical idea but lacks the empirical evidence required for publication; needs comprehensive benchmarking against state-of-the-art baselines, objective metrics, and rigorous subjective evaluation.
