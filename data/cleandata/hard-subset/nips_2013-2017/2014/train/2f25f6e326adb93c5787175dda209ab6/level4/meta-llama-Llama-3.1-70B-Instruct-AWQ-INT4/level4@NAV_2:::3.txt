This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Instead of using existing formulations that are built on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it presents a more flexible framework that can be applied on an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time domain signal.

The paper is well-written and easy to read for people in this area. The originality of the proposed Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) model is above average, as it addresses a significant gap in the existing literature by providing a generative model of the raw signal itself, rather than just its STFT. The authors successfully extend the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to the time domain, enabling the representation of signals as a sum of layers with their own time resolution and latent low-rank structure.

The paper's quality is good, with a clear and well-organized structure. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the LRTFS model, including its relation to sparse Bayesian learning and its estimation using maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE) approaches. The EM algorithms for MJLE and MMLE are well-described, and the authors provide a clear discussion of the complexity and limitations of each approach.

The significance of the paper is not fully communicated, however. While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the LRTFS model on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks, the results are not extensively compared to existing methods. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper itself lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Despite this limitation, the theoretical development presented in the paper is interesting and solid. The authors provide a novel perspective on time-frequency factorizations, and their work has the potential to contribute to the advancement of the field. Therefore, I consider this a good paper, with a well-written and solid theory. While I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, this is not a fatal flaw, and the paper's theoretical contribution is valuable in its own right.

Strengths:

* Novel and flexible framework for time-frequency factorizations
* Well-written and easy to read
* Solid theoretical development
* Potential to contribute to the advancement of the field

Weaknesses:

* Limited evaluation and comparison to existing methods
* Supplemental material not particularly convincing
* Significance of the paper not fully communicated

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting interpretation of time-frequency factorizations.
* The theoretical development is solid and well-written.
* The paper has the potential to contribute to the advancement of the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The evaluation and comparison to existing methods are limited.
* The supplemental material is not particularly convincing.
* The significance of the paper is not fully communicated.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of the paper, but with the suggestion that the authors provide more extensive evaluation and comparison to existing methods in future work.
