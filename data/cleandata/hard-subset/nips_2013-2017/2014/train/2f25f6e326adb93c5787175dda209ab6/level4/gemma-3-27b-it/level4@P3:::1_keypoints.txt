- Novel contribution: time-domain reformulation of the Gaussian Composite Model that supports overcomplete bases and multiresolution decomposition (tonal vs transient).
- Algorithmic contribution: derivation of an EM algorithm for parameter estimation in the proposed LRTFS model.
- Theoretical soundness: mathematical derivation appears robust and well-grounded in probabilistic modeling.
- Literature positioning: properly situated within NMF and probabilistic latent factor work, with relevant citations and an SBL connection.
- Potential impact: framework could apply broadly (music analysis, speech enhancement, source separation) due to multiresolution capability.
- Limited empirical evaluation: experiments are superficial, with only illustrative examples and a single speech enhancement test.
- Quantitative weakness: music decomposition lacks objective metrics; speech enhancement comparison is limited and possibly unfair.
- Perceptual issues: music decomposition shows noticeable artifacts, suggesting suboptimal perceptual quality.
- Missing analyses: no ablation studies to quantify contributions of model components (e.g., basis overcompleteness).
- Scalability concerns: MMLE approach may not scale to large audio problems; efficient implementations are not explored.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound extension enabling overcomplete and multiresolution representations with broad applicability.
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient empirical evidence, artifacts in results, and lack of component-wise validation.
- Overall assessment: interesting and theoretically strong but practically undervalidated; needs more experiments and perceptual evaluation.
- Recommendation: weak reject, contingent on expanded quantitative/qualitative experiments, ablations, and scalability work.
