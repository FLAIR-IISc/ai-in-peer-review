- Introduces LRTFS, a novel generative model that models raw time-domain signals, bridging NMF and time-domain synthesis.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by operating on the signal rather than STFT coefficients.  
- Naturally supports multi-resolution time-frequency representations, unlike traditional fixed-resolution NMF.  
- Presents two estimation algorithms (MJLE and MMLE) with well-derived EM update rules.  
- Technical development is rigorous and theoretically sound, properly accounting for time-domain structure.  
- Experimental validation on music decomposition and speech enhancement shows practical utility and improved SNR.  
- Comparison to IS-NMF is limited in single-resolution settings and should be expanded.  
- MMLE is noted to be computationally prohibitive for audio without sufficient practical mitigation details.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., λ) is not thoroughly explored in experiments.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, though it assumes familiarity with complex Gaussian distributions.  
- Connection to sparse Bayesian learning is well-explained; relation to previous NMF and multi-resolution work could be clearer.  
- Multi-resolution extension is clearly described and reproducible; figures (not included) are reported as illustrative.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with a request to include more detailed IS-NMF comparisons and implementation details for large-scale audio in the final version.
