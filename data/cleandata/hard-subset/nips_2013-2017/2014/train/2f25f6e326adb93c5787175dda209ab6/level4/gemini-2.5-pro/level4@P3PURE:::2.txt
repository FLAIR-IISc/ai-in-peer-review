This paper proposes a novel framework for Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF). In contrast to the standard NMF framework, which models a signal's time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients, this work presents a generative model of the time-domain signal itself by modeling the TF synthesis coefficients. A key advantage of this approach is that it enables multi-resolution representations, which are not possible in the traditional NMF setting. This multi-resolution extension is a particularly interesting contribution.

While the authors present results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, the paper would be strengthened by an evaluation of the proposed method in the single-resolution case, including a direct comparison with standard Itakura-Saito NMF (IS-NMF). Specifically, it is unclear if the proposed method yields comparable factor matrices (W and H). Furthermore, an analysis of the proposed algorithm's computational speed and stability relative to the well-established multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF would be valuable.
