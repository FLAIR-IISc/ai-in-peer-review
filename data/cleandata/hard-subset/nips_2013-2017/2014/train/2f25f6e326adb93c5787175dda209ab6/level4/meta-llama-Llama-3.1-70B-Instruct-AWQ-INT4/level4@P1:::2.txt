The authors propose a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model that bridges the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches. This model considers a generative model of the time-domain signal itself, describing a generative model of time-frequency synthesis coefficients. This allows for multi-resolution representations that were not possible in the traditional NMF setting.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough explanation of the proposed model, including its relationship to sparse Bayesian learning and the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM). The authors also present two estimation approaches, maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE), along with their respective EM algorithms.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in multi-resolution signal decompositions, including music decomposition and speech enhancement. The authors also compare their approach with IS-NMF in a semi-supervised speech enhancement task, showing improved performance.

However, I would like to see a comparison of the proposed method with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case. Specifically, I am interested in knowing whether the proposed algorithm yields similar W and H matrices as IS-NMF, and whether it is as fast and stable as the multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF.

Additionally, the authors mention that the estimation of λ with all other parameters free was found to perform poorly in practice, similar to sparse Bayesian learning. It would be helpful to provide more insight into this issue and discuss potential solutions.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that presents a novel approach to time-frequency synthesis. With some additional comparisons and discussions, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting approach to time-frequency synthesis.
* The authors provide a thorough explanation of the proposed model and its relationship to existing approaches.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in multi-resolution signal decompositions.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper lacks a comparison with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case.
* The authors do not provide a thorough discussion of the issues with estimating λ with all other parameters free.
* The paper could benefit from additional insights and discussions on the stability and computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.

Rating: 8/10
