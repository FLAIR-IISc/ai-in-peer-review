- Proposal: LRTFS — a generative waveform model that shifts low-rank priors to synthesis TF coefficients with variances factorized as WH.  
- Model advantage: supports arbitrary/overcomplete TF dictionaries and naturally enables multi-resolution modeling by stacking frames.  
- Estimation methods: MJLE via an EM algorithm (scalable, avoids large T×T inversions) and MMLE/type-II ML related to SBL (insightful but not scalable).  
- Experiments: illustrative tonal/transient decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with reported SNR gains vs IS-NMF and OMLSA.  

- Technical quality: sound modeling move from STFT-domain GCM to waveform synthesis; insightful connection to SBL (structured low-rankness vs per-coefficient variances).  
- MJLE: carefully derived and computationally practical using Φα and Φ* operations.  
- MMLE: derivation consistent with SBL literature but currently impractical for audio-scale problems.  
- Open technical questions: identifiability and conditioning with overcomplete frames remain unclear.  
- Hyperparameter issues: selection and effect of β (subject to β ≤ λ/δΦ) and sensitivity to λ need clarification.  
- Objective structure: role and influence of the implicit log(|α|²) sparsity term in the joint objective require discussion.  

- Clarity: paper is generally well written and situates itself clearly relative to IS-NMF/GCM and SBL.  
- Experimental clarity: evaluation is thin, largely illustrative, with limited quantitative metrics and few baselines; some comparison details (e.g., segment durations) are unclear.  

- Originality: natural but nontrivial extension of GCM/NMF to time-domain synthesis; synthesis viewpoint and EM link to Wiener filtering/IS-NMF are fresh contributions.  

- Significance: combining low-rank modeling with overcomplete/multi-resolution frames is promising for audio tasks, but current empirical support is insufficient.  
- Practical impact: examples show potential but include audible artifacts and do not convincingly outperform strong baselines.  

- Pros: coherent generative waveform model, clear link to prior work, practical MJLE algorithm, and straightforward multi-resolution extension.  
- Cons: sparse experimental evidence, audible artifacts and lack of perceptual/standard separation metrics, no ablation studies, and MMLE not usable at scale.  

- Suggested improvements: broader controlled experiments with standardized metrics (PESQ/STOI/SDR/SIR/SAR), fair comparisons, and additional baselines (PLCA/GCM, morphological methods, sparse coding).  
- Suggested ablations: single vs multi-resolution, orthonormal vs overcomplete frames, K, λ and β effects, and impact of implicit sparsity.  
- Practical guidance: discuss identifiability/conditioning with redundant frames, recommend frame/β choices, and report runtimes and memory usage.  
- Reproducibility: release code and data splits to enable replication.  

- Overall: methodologically solid and potentially impactful, but empirical validation must be strengthened to justify acceptance.
