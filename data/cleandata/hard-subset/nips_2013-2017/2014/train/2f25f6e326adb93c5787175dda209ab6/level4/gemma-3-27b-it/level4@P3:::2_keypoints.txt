- Proposes LRTFS: a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that directly models raw signals rather than spectrograms.  
- Builds on the connection between IS-NMF and the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and generalizes it to full signal generation.  
- Enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations, beneficial for signals with transients and sustained components.  
- Establishes a generative model for synthesis coefficients, potentially useful for downstream tasks.  
- Presents two EM estimation algorithms and demonstrates applications to music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written with concise mathematical derivations.  
- Novelty is a key strength, moving beyond spectrogram-based NMF approaches.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a significant practical advantage for complex audio.  
- Experimental results show feasibility but are limited in scope.  
- Limited comparative evaluation: needs thorough comparison to IS-NMF (single-resolution) and checks whether learned W and H match IS-NMF.  
- Computational complexity concern: MMLE approach appears costly for large-scale audio due to matrix inversion; scalability solutions are not discussed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: reliance on fixed λ raises robustness concerns and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Lack of theoretical justification or ablation for fixing λ; simultaneous estimation reportedly performed poorly but is not explained.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, multi-resolution contribution, and promising experimental results.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient comparative evaluation, computational scalability issues, and sensitivity to fixed hyperparameter λ.  
- Overall: promising approach with important strengths but needs more comparisons, scalability discussion, and justification of λ; reviewer recommends weak accept contingent on addressing these issues.
