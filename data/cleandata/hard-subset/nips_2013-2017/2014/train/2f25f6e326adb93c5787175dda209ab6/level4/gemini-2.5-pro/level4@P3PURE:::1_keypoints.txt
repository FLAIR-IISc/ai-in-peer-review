- Proposes extending low-rank spectrogram factorization to allow latent factors over arbitrary, potentially overcomplete bases for multiresolution decomposition.  
- Reformulates Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model in the time domain, enabling non-orthogonal impulse responses.  
- Presents an EM algorithm to find locally optimal solutions for the proposed model.  
- Central idea is interesting but its full implications are unclear.  
- Paper claims advantage from overcomplete bases but does not clarify why typical complications from overcompleteness are not problematic.  
- Derivations appear rigorous, but the estimation procedure details are difficult to follow.  
- Paper is well-written and motivated, but the experimental evaluation is superficial.  
- Only a single illustrative result and one SNR improvement are reported, insufficient for strong conclusions.  
- Audio examples are not compelling; OMLSA comparison may be unfair due to differing clip lengths.  
- Music denoising shows significant artifacts; conventional methods might yield better perceptual quality.  
- Builds heavily on Fevotte et al., but the time-domain reformulation is a novel perspective.  
- Potentially significant ability to apply matrix factorization to overcomplete bases, but weak evaluation and artifacts limit demonstrated practical importance.
