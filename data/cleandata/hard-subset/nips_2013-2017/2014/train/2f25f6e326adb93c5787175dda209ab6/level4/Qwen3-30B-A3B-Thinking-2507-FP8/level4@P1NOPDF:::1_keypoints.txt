- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) bases, enabling multiresolution decomposition (e.g., tonal + transient).  
- Reinterprets Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model in the time domain, replacing implicit convolution/Gabor impulses with a time-domain expression that handles nonorthogonal bases.  
- Proposes an EM-based inference procedure for the time-domain formulation; analysis is rigorous though some details are hard to follow.  
- Insightful perspective on GCM, but practical implications and benefits remain unclear.  
- Contribution is primarily a reinterpretation of prior work; handling overcomplete bases is a valuable but incremental extension.  
- Evaluation is severely underdeveloped—only one illustrative example and a single SNR metric are reported.  
- Provided audio examples are helpful but unconvincing as evidence for claims.  
- Comparative example with OMLSA appears unfair (different clip lengths and more background noise), and OMLSA yields perceptually better speech.  
- Music denoising example shows significant audible artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) would likely give better perceived quality.  
- Lack of robust experiments across diverse data prevents substantiating claims of improved performance or practical significance.  
- Claims of significance are overstated given the limited and problematic empirical validation.
