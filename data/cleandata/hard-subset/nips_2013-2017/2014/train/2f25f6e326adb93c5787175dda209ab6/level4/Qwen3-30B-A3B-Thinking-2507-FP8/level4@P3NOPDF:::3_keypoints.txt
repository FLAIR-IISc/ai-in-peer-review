- Introduces a novel, flexible time/frequency factorization framework decoupling factorization from specific transform coefficients.  
- Framework applies to arbitrary transforms while maintaining a direct, interpretable link to the time-domain signal.  
- Theoretical contribution is original and addresses a meaningful gap in the literature.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear with precise mathematical formulation and well-structured arguments.  
- Strong derivation of factorization conditions and logical generalization to arbitrary transforms.  
- Major weakness: insufficient demonstration of practical significance and real-world relevance.  
- Experimental validation is lacking: no comparisons against baseline methods like STFT factorizations or competing techniques.  
- Results fail to quantify benefits in reconstruction accuracy, computational efficiency, or robustness.  
- Supplemental experiments are superficial and do not remedy the lack of convincing empirical evidence.  
- Absence of ablation studies on transform choices and benchmarks on standard datasets undermines claims of adaptability.  
- Theoretical depth partially compensates for evaluation shortcomings, but practical impact remains unproven.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance, contingent on adding comparative experiments and clarifying practical implications.
