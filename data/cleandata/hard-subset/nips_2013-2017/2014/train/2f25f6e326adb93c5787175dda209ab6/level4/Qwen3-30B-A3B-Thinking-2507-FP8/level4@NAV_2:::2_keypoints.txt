- Introduces LRTFS, a novel model that directly synthesizes time-domain signals with low-rank variance structure.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by moving from STFT coefficients to time-domain synthesis coefficients.  
- Proposes two expectation-maximization algorithms (MJLE and MMLE) for parameter estimation.  
- Enables multi-resolution audio modeling, allowing different temporal resolutions for different components (e.g., transients vs. tonals).  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear links to prior work (GCM, sparse Bayesian learning) and well-presented mathematical derivations.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation, with a well-motivated introduction and lucid exposition of the multi-resolution extension.  
- Original contribution: modeling the raw signal rather than STFT and enabling multi-resolution capabilities not possible with standard NMF.  
- Experimental results demonstrate practical utility in music decomposition and speech enhancement, showing a 6.6 dB average SNR (vs. 6 dB IS-NMF, 4.6 dB OMLSA).  
- Quality concern: evaluation lacks thorough comparison with IS-NMF in single-resolution settings, especially for W and H quality and computational efficiency.  
- Additional weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability.  
- Limited analysis of how the estimated W and H matrices compare quantitatively to IS-NMF in single-resolution cases.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel generative time-domain model, multi-resolution advance, strong theory, promising experiments, and excellent writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete comparisons with IS-NMF, unclear computational/scalability analysis, and limited W/H comparative analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful contribution to audio processing with minor evaluation improvements needed for acceptance.
