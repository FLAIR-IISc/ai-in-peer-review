This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations, offering a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time domain signal. Unlike existing formulations that rely on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, this approach provides a more versatile and generalizable solution.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow for readers familiar with this area, making it a good quality submission. The originality of the work is above average, although the significance of the contribution could be more effectively communicated. 

One of the most disappointing aspects of the paper is the presentation and evaluation of the results. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper lacks a comparison with the methods it builds upon. While this omission is not a fatal flaw, it would have strengthened the paper considerably.

Despite this limitation, the theoretical development presented in the paper is substantial and interesting. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field, and the paper's solid theoretical foundation makes it a worthwhile read. To further enhance the paper, more comprehensive results and comparisons would be beneficial, but the theoretical contribution is engaging and noteworthy in its own right.
