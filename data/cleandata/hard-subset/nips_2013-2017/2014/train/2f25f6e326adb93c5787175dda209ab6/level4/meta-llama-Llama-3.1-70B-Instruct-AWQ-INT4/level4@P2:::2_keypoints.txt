- Proposes LRTFS, a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model bridging NMF and raw-signal generative models and enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- LRTFS is formalized via generative equations, extends the Gaussian Composite Model, and is related to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Two EM estimation methods are presented: MJLE (computationally efficient, avoids matrix inversion) and MMLE (more robust but computationally heavier).  
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement show successful signal decomposition and improved performance over traditional NMF.  
- Strength: introduces a novel model that fills the gap between NMF and raw-signal generative approaches.  
- Strength: supports multi-resolution representations unavailable in standard NMF.  
- Strength: provides two complementary EM algorithms balancing efficiency and robustness.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness on relevant tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons with traditional NMF, e.g., estimated W and H matrices.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and algorithmic stability.  
- Weakness: limited experimental comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Originality: new LRTFS concept and multi-resolution capability, plus novel EM estimation approaches.  
- Significance: potential impact across music information retrieval, speech enhancement, biomedical signals, and broader signal-processing/ML research.  
- Quality: well-written, clear model and algorithm descriptions; experiments are well-designed.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with consistent notation and terminology.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, multi-resolution contribution, original EM algorithms, and demonstrated empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more comparative experiments, and clearer complexity/stability analysis.
