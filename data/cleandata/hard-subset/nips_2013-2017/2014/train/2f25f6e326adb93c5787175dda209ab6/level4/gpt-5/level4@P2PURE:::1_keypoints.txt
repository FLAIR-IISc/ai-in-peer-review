- Paper extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to arbitrary (possibly overcomplete) bases, enabling multiresolution decompositions (e.g., tonal + transient).
- Starts from Févotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model and derives an explicit time-domain formulation via convolution with Gabor impulse responses.
- Time-domain reformulation allows adapting the estimator to nonorthogonal and overcomplete impulse-response sets.
- Presents an EM algorithm for parameter estimation that converges to a local optimum.
- Core idea is interesting and offers a new way to view Févotte’s GCM, with appealing flexibility to incorporate overcomplete dictionaries.
- Flexibility to use overcomplete bases likely introduces complications, and it’s unclear why these don’t cause greater problems.
- Reviewer assumes the estimation is correct but could not follow all technical details.
- Exposition and motivation are generally clear.
- Experimental evaluation is very superficial: only one illustrative example and a single SNR improvement metric.
- Audio examples are included but not convincing; comparisons may be unfair (e.g., OMLSA example is much longer than the proposed result).
- Denoising examples show significant artifacts (especially in music), where conventional methods might yield higher perceived quality.
- Originality is limited by heavy reliance on Févotte’s work, though the time-domain interpretation offers fresh perspective.
- Potential significance is high if overcomplete matrix-factorization works well, but thin evaluation and artifacts fail to substantiate impact.
