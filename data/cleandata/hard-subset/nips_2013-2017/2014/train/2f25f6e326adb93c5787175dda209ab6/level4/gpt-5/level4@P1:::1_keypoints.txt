- Moves low-rank time–frequency modeling from STFT analysis to a synthesis model on raw waveform coefficients with low-rank variances over a TF dictionary.  
- Produces a generative waveform model that naturally supports overcomplete and multi-resolution dictionaries (e.g., tonal + transient frames).  
- Proposes two estimators: a practical EM-based MJLE with Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF updates, and an elegant but computationally prohibitive MMLE requiring large matrix inversions.  
- Connects formulation to component reconstruction via Wiener filtering and to SBL, with the key twist that variances are tied by a low-rank WH rather than learned independently.  
- Modeling move and EM derivation are technically sound and the EM “plug-in” trick to avoid inversions is a useful practical contribution.  
- MJLE algorithm is computationally friendly (analysis/synthesis ops + IS-NMF) and scales reasonably; MMLE is impractical at audio scale.  
- Overcomplete/nonorthogonal dictionaries introduce identifiability, scaling ambiguities, and local minima; mitigation strategies are under-discussed.  
- The condition β ≤ λ/δΦ is stated but guidance on choosing β and its effect on convergence/quality is missing.  
- Derivations lack some detail; an appendix with step-by-step math, assumptions, and convergence discussion is recommended.  
- Practical implementation choices (initialization, stopping criteria, frame normalization, Hermitian symmetry handling) are insufficiently specified.  
- Experimental evaluation is very limited: one hybrid music example and a single SNR plot for speech are inadequate to support claims.  
- Supplementary audio shows artifacts; comparisons (e.g., to OMLSA) may be unfair due to segment, alignment, or preprocessing mismatches.  
- Missing baselines: wavelet shrinkage, simple Wiener filtering, supervised/semi-supervised NMF variants, PLCA, and other relevant methods.  
- Need larger benchmarks, standard metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ/STOI), ablations (single vs multi-resolution, overcomplete vs complete, K, λ, β), and runtime/memory comparisons.  
- Clarity can be improved with algorithm pseudocode, per-iteration complexity, and explicit discussion of λ sensitivity and how to set it.  
- Expand the SBL connection, explaining how tied variances affect sparsity and interpretability of α.  
- Originality: a distinct and thought-provoking synthesis perspective on GCM/IS-NMF enabling multi-resolution, overcomplete dictionaries.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for audio decomposition/denoising/separation, but current empirical evidence is too thin to demonstrate this.  
- Pros for acceptance: clean synthesis reformulation, practical MJLE EM leveraging IS-NMF, and clear links to SBL and component-wise reconstruction.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak and partially inconclusive experiments with audible artifacts, MMLE unscalable and untested vs MJLE, and insufficient discussion of identifiability and convergence.  
- Overall: promising conceptual contribution with appealing multiresolution extension, but requires substantially stronger empirical validation and clearer treatment of practical/identifiability issues.
