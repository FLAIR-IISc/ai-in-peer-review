The paper departs from the standard NMF framework, which models the time–frequency (TF) analysis coefficients of a signal, by instead modeling the time-domain signal itself through a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This shift enables multi-resolution representations that are not available in traditional NMF.

Overall, this is an interesting paper, and I particularly like the idea of the multi-resolution extension.

While the authors present some results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, I would also like to see an evaluation in the single-resolution setting to allow a direct comparison with IS-NMF. In particular:
- How does the proposed method perform in the single-resolution case compared with IS-NMF?
- Do the learned factors W and H resemble those obtained by IS-NMF?
- Is the proposed algorithm as fast and numerically stable as the standard multiplicative update algorithm for IS-NMF?

The multi-resolution results are promising, but addressing these questions would help clarify the benefits and trade-offs relative to established baselines.
