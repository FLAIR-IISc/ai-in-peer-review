- Proposes a novel, more flexible time-frequency factorization framework that generalizes beyond STFT analysis coefficients.  
- Framework applies to arbitrary transforms and provides explicit links to time-domain signal reconstruction.  
- Exposition is clear and accessible to researchers; theoretical development is sound and well-executed.  
- Originality is above average, offering a conceptually significant alternative to existing formulations.  
- Paper does not sufficiently justify broader significance or practical impact.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and does not provide compelling evidence of performance gains.  
- Supplemental material fails to deliver convincing comparative analysis.  
- Crucially, the method is not benchmarked against foundational techniques it extends, leaving relative advantages unclear.  
- Despite limited experiments, the mathematical rigor makes the theoretical contribution valuable.  
- Omission of thorough empirical validation is a missed opportunity to strengthen impact.  
- Recommendation: include meaningful comparisons with relevant baselines and demonstrate practical benefits in future revisions.
