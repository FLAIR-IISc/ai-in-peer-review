This paper offers a nuanced interpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations built on STFT analysis coefficients, it proposes a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and remains directly connected to the time-domain signal.

The paper is accessible to readers in this area and is of good quality. Its originality is above average, though the significance is not fully articulated. The most disappointing aspect lies in the results and evaluation. The supplementary material is not particularly persuasive, and the paper does not compare this work to the methods it extends. I do not consider this a major weakness (the theoretical development alone makes the paper interesting), but such comparisons would have substantially improved it. Good paper, well written, with solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, but this is not a fatal flaw given the strength of the theoretical contribution.
