- Proposal: Introduces Low-Rank Time–Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative time-domain model with synthesis coefficients having low-rank time–frequency variance WH.  
- Estimation: Presents two estimation strategies — MJLE (scalable EM using analysis/synthesis ops) and MMLE (type-II ML/SBL-style, but computationally heavy).  
- Multi-resolution: Natural multi-resolution extension combining multiple frames (e.g., short for transients, long for tonals).  
- Experiments: Demonstrates hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement, with reported mean SNR gains (6.6 dB) outperforming IS-NMF and OMLSA in the setup.  
- Technical soundness: Modeling is principled, EM derivations consistent, and links to Wiener filtering and SBL are well explained.  
- Empirical limitations: Evaluation is limited — few datasets/metrics, little ablation on λ, β, K, frame choices, and no direct matched comparison to GCM/IS-NMF.  
- Practical limitations: MMLE scalability issues and practical challenges estimating λ jointly are acknowledged.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized, but bibliography entries are incomplete and some algorithmic/parameter choices (β, complexity) need clearer discussion.  
- Originality: Conceptual shift from analysis-side GCM to synthesis-side low-rank variances is novel; structured-variance SBL interpretation and multi-resolution formulation are original.  
- Significance: Potentially influential as a principled time-domain alternative to STFT-NMF with promising results, but broader evaluation needed to substantiate impact.  
- Pros: Conceptually clean generative synthesis model, scalable MJLE, elegant multi-resolution extension, clear links to prior work, and promising speech enhancement results.  
- Cons: Limited experimental comparisons, MMLE impractical as presented, under-explained hyperparameter handling, incomplete bibliography and some missing implementation justifications.  
- Suggested improvements: Add controlled GCM/IS-NMF comparisons, ablation studies (λ, β, K, windows), discuss identifiability/Hermitian symmetry, include complexity/runtime analysis, complete citations, and explore approximate MMLE/variational approximations.  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid and conceptually meaningful contribution with recommended acceptance, contingent on stronger empirical validation and clarifications.
