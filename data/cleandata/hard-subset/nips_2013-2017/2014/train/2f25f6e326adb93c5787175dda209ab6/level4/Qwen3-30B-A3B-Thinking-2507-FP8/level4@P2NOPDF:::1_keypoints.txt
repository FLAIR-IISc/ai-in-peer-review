- Core contribution: Reinterprets Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model in the time domain to allow low-rank spectrogram factorization over arbitrary (including overcomplete) bases for multiresolution sound decomposition.
- Technical soundness: The time-domain reformulation and EM estimation are technically solid and represent a meaningful conceptual advance.
- Overcompleteness concerns: The paper does not adequately address potential issues from overcomplete bases such as identifiability and increased computational cost.
- EM justification: The estimation procedure appears correct but lacks detailed justification that practical complications from overcompleteness are manageable.
- Clarity: Motivation and technical exposition are clear and well-structured.
- Evaluation weakness: The experimental evaluation is severely underdeveloped, relying on only one illustrative example and a single SNR metric.
- Unfair comparison: Audio comparisons are misleading—OMLSA uses a 7s clip versus the proposed method’s 2s clip—raising fairness concerns.
- Perceptual quality: Speech from the baseline appears perceptually better despite noise differences, and music denoising shows severe artifacts like musical noise.
- Missing baselines: Conventional methods (e.g., wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) that likely yield better perceptual results are not adequately compared.
- Originality: The work is an incremental but nontrivial extension of prior GCM work by enabling overcomplete, multiresolution bases.
- Significance: The idea has promising potential for flexible basis decomposition tasks, but current results do not demonstrate practical impact.
- Required improvements: Authors should provide multiple quantitative metrics (e.g., SDR, STOI) across diverse datasets, ensure fair comparisons (equal-length clips), and conduct detailed listening tests to address artifacts.
- Recommendation: Interesting but not ready for acceptance due to weak evaluation and unresolved practical concerns; strengthen experiments before reconsideration.
