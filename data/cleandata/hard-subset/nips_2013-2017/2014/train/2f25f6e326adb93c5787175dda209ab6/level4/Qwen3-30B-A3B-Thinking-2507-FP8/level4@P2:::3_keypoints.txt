- Introduces LRTFS, a generative time-domain model that models raw signals with low-rank time-frequency variance instead of just STFT coefficients.  
- Extends Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) to directly model the time-domain signal, enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- Proposes a generative formulation expressing x(t) as a sum of time-frequency atoms with low-rank variance structure.  
- Derives two EM-based estimation algorithms: MJLE (practical, avoids matrix inversions) and MMLE (theoretical extension with higher computational cost).  
- Theoretical development is sound, with clear links to sparse Bayesian learning and the Itakura-Saito divergence.  
- Technical quality is strong with rigorous derivations and mathematically clear exposition.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized; relation to prior work and multi-resolution extension are clearly explained.  
- Would benefit from more detailed pseudocode to aid reproducibility for non-expert readers.  
- Originality is high: shifts modeling from STFT to time-domain and introduces multi-resolution capability beyond traditional NMF.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks meaningful comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Significance is under-supported: music decomposition and speech enhancement results lack thorough quantitative baselines and analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on future work addressing empirical validation and stronger comparisons to existing methods.
