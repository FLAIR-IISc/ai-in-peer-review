- Proposes extending low-rank spectrogram factorization to general/overcomplete bases, including multiresolution (tonal + transient) bases.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model but introduces time-domain convolution with multiple Gabor impulse responses per spectrogram row.  
- Presents an EM analysis procedure adapted to the time-domain kernel formulation.  
- Claims potential improvements for source separation and denoising by handling overcomplete dictionaries.  
- Idea is intriguing and offers a fresh time-domain perspective, but heavily relies on Fevotte's foundation.  
- The derivation and estimation details are hard to follow, raising concerns about possible unaddressed complications.  
- Presentation and motivation are clear, and the analytical effort is appreciated.  
- Evaluation is very limited: a single illustrative result and SNR measure are insufficient to validate claims.  
- Audio example comparison to OMLSA is questionable due to mismatched clip lengths and differing noise levels.  
- Examples show significant artifacts (especially in music denoising) that weaken the empirical demonstration.  
- Some artifacts could likely be reduced with standard techniques (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering), but this was not explored.  
- Overall potential significance is high, but thin evaluation and artifacts prevent a convincing demonstration.
