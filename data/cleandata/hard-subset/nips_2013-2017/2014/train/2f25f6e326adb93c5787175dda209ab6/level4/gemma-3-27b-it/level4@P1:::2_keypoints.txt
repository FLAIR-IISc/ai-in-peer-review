- Proposes a novel generative model that synthesizes the raw signal with low-rank time-frequency structure, moving beyond STFT-based NMF.  
- Models signal synthesis coefficients directly, enabling multi-resolution representations absent in traditional NMF.  
- Introduces two expectation-maximization (EM) algorithms for parameter estimation within the new model.  
- Demonstrates the method on music decomposition and speech enhancement applications.  
- Theoretical grounding is solid and the EM algorithms are clearly described.  
- Connection to sparse Bayesian learning is well articulated.  
- Experimental results provide a useful initial evaluation of performance.  
- Lacks a direct single-resolution comparison to IS-NMF to assess similarity of W and H factors and relative speed/stability.  
- Choice and impact of the hyperparameter λ are insufficiently explored; more systematic analysis needed.  
- Discussion of the MMLE approach is brief and notes scalability issues that limit experimental evaluation.  
- Strengths: novel synthesis-based approach, multi-resolution capability, solid theory, and demonstrated application performance.  
- Weaknesses: missing direct IS-NMF comparison, limited λ analysis, and MMLE scalability concerns.  
- Overall: strong, novel contribution worth accepting if authors address IS-NMF comparison and provide deeper analysis of λ.
