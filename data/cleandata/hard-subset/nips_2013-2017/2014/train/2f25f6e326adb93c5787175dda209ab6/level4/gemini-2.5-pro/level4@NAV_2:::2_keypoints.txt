- Proposes LRTFS, a novel generative model that directly models time-domain signals with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients.  
- Extends prior low-rank TF work (e.g., GCM/NMF) by placing the low-rank constraint on synthesis (time-domain) coefficients rather than STFT analysis magnitudes.  
- Natural ability to perform multi-resolution modeling by combining multiple time-frequency frames (e.g., short and long windows).  
- Derives two parameter-estimation approaches: MJLE (practical) and MMLE (theoretically appealing but intractable in experiments).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.  
- Paper is well written, technically sound, and connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning for a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Lacks direct, controlled comparison to single-resolution baselines (e.g., GCM/IS-NMF); unclear relative performance and factor differences in single-resolution settings.  
- Questions about computational cost and convergence stability of MJLE versus standard IS-NMF multiplicative updates.  
- MMLE is presented but not scaled to experiments; paper should discuss approximation strategies or future work to make MMLE practical.  
- Noise variance λ is treated as a hyperparameter despite an update rule; more discussion on sensitivity and practical selection guidance is needed.  
- Overall: significant and elegant contribution (analysis→synthesis shift and multi-resolution capability), but experimental evaluation needs stronger baseline comparisons and practical details.
