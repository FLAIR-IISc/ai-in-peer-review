- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete bases, enabling multiresolution (tonal + transient) decomposition for separation/denoising.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model, deriving a time-domain expression by expanding implicit convolution with Gabor impulse responses.  
- Allows use of non-orthogonal, overcomplete impulse-response sets and formulates an EM procedure for parameter estimation.  
- EM estimation may be correct but is potentially subject to local optima; reviewer could not follow all technical details.  
- Time-domain reinterpretation is a novel perspective, though the work heavily relies on Fevotte’s foundation, reducing perceived originality.  
- Paper is well explained and motivated, but some theoretical implications (e.g., why overcompleteness isn’t more problematic) remain unclear.  
- Evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement reported, insufficient to draw firm conclusions.  
- Comparative fairness is questionable (OMLSA example is longer, making comparisons unclear), and baseline speech sounded higher quality despite more noise.  
- Denoising examples exhibit noticeable artifacts; reviewer expects standard methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) to yield better perceived quality.  
- Potentially significant idea (matrix factorization on overcomplete bases), but limited evidence and artifacts prevent a convincing demonstration of practical impact.
