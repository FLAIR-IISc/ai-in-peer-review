This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time-frequency factorizations. Instead of relying on existing formulations built upon the analysis coefficients of STFT, the authors introduce a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written, clear, and should be accessible to readers familiar with this domain. The originality of the proposed approach is a key strength, and the underlying theory is solid.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in its results and evaluation. The broader significance of the work is not fully communicated, as the paper lacks a comparison to the very methods it builds upon. The results in the supplemental material are also not particularly convincing.

While this is a notable weakness, it is not a fatal flaw. The theoretical development is strong enough to stand on its own as an interesting and valuable contribution. A more comprehensive evaluation with comparative results would have significantly strengthened the paper, but its absence does not invalidate the novelty of the core ideas.
