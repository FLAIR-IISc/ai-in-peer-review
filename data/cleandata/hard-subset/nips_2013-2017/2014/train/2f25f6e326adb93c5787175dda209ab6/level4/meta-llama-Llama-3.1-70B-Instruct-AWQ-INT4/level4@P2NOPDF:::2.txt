This paper proposes a novel extension of Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) that considers a generative model of the time-domain signal itself, rather than the traditional approach of modeling the time-frequency (TF) analysis coefficients. This new framework allows for multi-resolution representations, which were previously unattainable in traditional NMF settings.

I find this paper to be particularly interesting, and I appreciate the authors' exploration of multi-resolution extensions. The idea of applying NMF to TF synthesis coefficients, rather than analysis coefficients, is a valuable contribution to the field.

While the authors present results on multi-resolution signal decompositions, I am also interested in the performance of the proposed method in single-resolution cases, specifically in comparison to IS-NMF. It would be beneficial to know whether the proposed algorithm produces similar factorization matrices (W and H) and whether it maintains the same level of speed and stability as the multiplicative update algorithm used in IS-NMF.

The key innovation of this paper lies in its ability to model the time-domain signal directly, using a generative model of TF synthesis coefficients. This approach opens up new possibilities for multi-resolution representations, which were not possible within the traditional NMF framework.
