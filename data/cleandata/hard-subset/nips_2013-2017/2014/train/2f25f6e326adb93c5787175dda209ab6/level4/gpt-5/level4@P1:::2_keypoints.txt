- Proposes LRTFS: a generative model imposing low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances (W,H), enabling direct time-domain signal generation.  
- Shifts from analysis-domain (STFT/GCM/IS-NMF) to synthesis-side modeling, allowing natural multi-resolution decomposition.  
- Multi-resolution extension composes the signal from layers with different time-frequency bases (e.g., Gabor frames of different window lengths).  
- Presents two estimators: MJLE with an EM algorithm (scalable, avoids large matrix inversions) and MMLE (principled but currently not scalable for long signals).  
- Experiments on music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement demonstrate promising results.  
- Strength: synthesis-side generative model conceptually bridges probabilistic modeling and direct signal generation.  
- Strength: multi-resolution extension fits audio structure (transients vs tonal content) and integrates naturally.  
- Strength: MJLE-EM is computationally practical for large signals via efficient analysis/synthesis operations.  
- Strength: experimental demonstrations show potential in music and speech tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks direct single-resolution comparison to IS-NMF to assess whether LRTFS recovers similar W and H.  
- Weakness: missing quantitative comparisons on standard metrics (reconstruction error, separation quality) using the same single-resolution Gabor frame.  
- Weakness: unclear if MJLE matches multiplicative updates for IS-NMF in speed, convergence stability, and robustness to initialization.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and no explicit per-iteration computational complexity comparison between MJLE and IS-NMF.  
- Qualitative: modeling choices are sound; MMLE is principled but impractical for large-scale audio as acknowledged.  
- Qualitative: paper is clear and well-organized; adding complexity analysis and single-resolution comparisons would improve clarity.  
- Overall: interesting and promising contribution, especially for multi-resolution analysis; recommend adding single-resolution IS-NMF comparisons and algorithmic speed/stability analysis.
