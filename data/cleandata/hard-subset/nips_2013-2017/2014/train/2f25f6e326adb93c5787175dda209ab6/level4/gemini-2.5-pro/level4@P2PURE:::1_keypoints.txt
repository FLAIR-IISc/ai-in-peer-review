- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary/overcomplete bases, enabling multiresolution decompositions (e.g., tonal + transient) for source separation and denoising.
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model and reformulates the implicit Gabor convolution into a time-domain expression supporting non-orthogonal impulse responses.
- Derives an EM algorithm to find local optima for the model parameters.
- Core insight is interesting; main novelty is the ability to incorporate overcomplete dictionaries rather than a radical new model.
- Paper does not fully address why overcomplete dictionaries do not introduce expected complications in this framework.
- The estimation derivation is difficult to follow in places, though the analysis appears generally sound.
- Paper is well-explained and motivated overall.
- Experimental evaluation is superficial: only one illustrative result and a single SNR improvement, insufficient for broad conclusions.
- Audio example comparisons are questionable: OMLSA baseline clip is much longer than the proposed method’s clip, undermining fairness.
- Perceptually, OMLSA speech sounds higher quality despite more background noise; music denoising by the proposed method shows significant artifacts.
- Conventional denoising methods (e.g., wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might achieve better perceived quality than the proposed approach in the shown examples.
- Reformulation enabling EM for multiresolution bases is a notable contribution, but the limited evaluation and artifacts prevent clear demonstration of practical significance.
