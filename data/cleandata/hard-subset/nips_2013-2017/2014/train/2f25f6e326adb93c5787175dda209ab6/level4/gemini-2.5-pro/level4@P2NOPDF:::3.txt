This paper introduces a novel and flexible framework for time-frequency factorization. The proposed approach departs from existing formulations, which are typically based on the analysis coefficients of Short-Time Fourier Transforms (STFT). Instead, it offers a more general method that can be applied to arbitrary transforms and operates directly on the time-domain signal, constituting a thoughtful reinterpretation of the problem.

The primary strength of this work lies in its theoretical contribution. The proposed framework is original and well-conceived, offering a solid foundation for a more generalized approach to time-frequency analysis. The paper is well-written and clear, making the technical details accessible to readers with expertise in the field. The theoretical development alone is significant enough to warrant interest from the community.

Despite the theoretical strengths, the paper is limited by its experimental evaluation. The practical significance of the proposed framework is not fully demonstrated, largely due to an underdeveloped results section. The empirical evidence provided, including the supplemental material, is not sufficiently convincing. A critical omission is the lack of a direct comparison with the STFT-based methods that the paper aims to supersede. Without this baseline comparison, it is difficult to assess the practical advantages of the new approach.

In conclusion, this is a high-quality paper with a notable theoretical contribution. While the lack of a comprehensive evaluation and comparison to prior work is a discernible weakness, it is not a fatal flaw, as the novelty and elegance of the proposed framework make the paper a worthwhile contribution on their own. The work would be substantially stronger and its impact more pronounced with the inclusion of more rigorous experimental results and direct comparisons to established methods.
