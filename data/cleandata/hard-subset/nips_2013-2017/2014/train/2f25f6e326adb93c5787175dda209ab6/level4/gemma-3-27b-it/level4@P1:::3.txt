This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time-frequency factorizations. Instead of building upon existing formulations based on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it proposes a more flexible framework applicable to an arbitrary transform and directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is readily accessible to researchers in this area and is of good quality. The originality is above average, although the significance could be more thoroughly demonstrated. Perhaps the most notable area for improvement lies in the results and evaluation. The supplemental material is not particularly compelling, and the paper lacks comparisons to the methods upon which it builds. While not a critical weakness – the theoretical development itself is sufficient to make this an interesting paper – more comprehensive results and comparisons would have significantly strengthened the manuscript. 

Overall, this is a good paper, well written, and with solid theoretical foundations. I would have appreciated seeing more extensive results and comparisons, but their absence does not constitute a fatal flaw given the interesting nature of the theoretical contribution.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel framework for time-frequency synthesis offering greater flexibility than existing approaches.
*   Strong theoretical development and clear presentation.
*   Potential for multi-resolution representations not possible with traditional NMF.
*   Addresses a fundamental problem in signal processing – signal decomposition and synthesis.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experimental results are not particularly convincing.
*   Limited comparison to existing methods.
*   Significance not fully demonstrated through thorough evaluation.
