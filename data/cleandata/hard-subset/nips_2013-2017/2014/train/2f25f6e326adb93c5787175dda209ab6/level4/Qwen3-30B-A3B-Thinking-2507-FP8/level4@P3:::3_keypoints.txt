Paper introduces LRTFS: extends GCM to directly synthesize time-domain signals as low-rank time-frequency syntheses, enabling multi-resolution representations.  
Proposes two EM-based estimators (MJLE and MMLE); MJLE is computationally feasible for audio applications.  
Theoretical development is rigorous, connects to sparse Bayesian learning, and provides a clean MJLE objective derivation.  
EM algorithm is computationally efficient (no matrix inversions) and leverages fast t-f analysis/synthesis (LTFAT).  
Multi-resolution extension is a novel, non-trivial generalization of the core model.  
Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clear; equations and experimental setup are reproducible.  
Originality: fills a gap by modeling the signal with low-rank time-frequency structure rather than only STFT magnitudes.  
Weakness — significance: experimental validation is weak, showing only marginal SNR gains lacking statistical significance or ablations.  
Music decomposition results are qualitative only, without quantitative comparisons to prior multi-resolution methods or perceptual tests.  
Evaluation baselines are superficial; missing comparisons to phase-aware methods and key metrics (e.g., STOI, F1, MOS).  
Crucial gap: paper does not quantify how much LRTFS improves over standard GCM-based pipelines, leaving practical impact unproven.  
Recommendation: weak rejection (score 3/5) — strong theory and originality but insufficient demonstrated significance.  
Suggested revisions: add rigorous phase-aware baselines, perceptual/standard metrics, ablation studies, and clarify when LRTFS is preferable.
