This paper offers a thoughtful interpretation of time-frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations based on the analysis coefficients of STFT transforms, it presents a more flexible framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly linked to the time-domain signal. 

The paper is well-written and accessible to researchers in this field, and is of good quality overall. The originality is above average; however, the paper doesn’t fully convey the significance of its contribution. A key weakness lies in the results and evaluation. The supplemental material is not particularly compelling, and the paper lacks comparisons to the methods upon which it builds. While this isn’t a critical flaw – the theoretical development is interesting in its own right – incorporating such comparisons would have significantly strengthened the work. 

Overall, this is a good paper with solid theoretical foundations. I would have appreciated seeing more results and comparative analyses, but the absence of these doesn't invalidate the paper given the interest of the theoretical contribution.
