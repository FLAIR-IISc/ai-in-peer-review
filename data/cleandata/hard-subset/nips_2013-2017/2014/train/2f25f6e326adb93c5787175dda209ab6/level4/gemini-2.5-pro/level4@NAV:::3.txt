This work provides an insightful formulation for time/frequency factorizations. In contrast to established methods built upon the analysis coefficients of STFT, this paper introduces a more generalized framework applicable to an arbitrary transform that is directly coupled with the time-domain signal. The paper is well-articulated and of high quality, making it readily comprehensible for experts in the domain.

The originality of the work is commendable, though its overall significance could be better communicated. The most pronounced weakness is the experimental results and evaluation. The supplementary material is not fully convincing, and the paper omits a comparative analysis against the very methods it builds upon. This is not a critical failure—the theoretical contribution alone is substantial enough to make this an interesting paper—but it does detract from its overall impact.

In summary, this is a good, well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation. A more thorough set of results and comparisons would have greatly enhanced the work, but its absence is not a fatal flaw given the novelty of the theoretical contribution.
