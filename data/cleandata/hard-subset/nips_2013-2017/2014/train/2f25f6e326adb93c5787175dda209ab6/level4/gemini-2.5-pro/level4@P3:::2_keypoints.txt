- Introduces LRTFS, a synthesis-based probabilistic generative model for time-domain signals that models synthesis coefficients with low-rank variances.  
- Core contribution: shifts from analysis-coefficient (STFT/NMF) modeling to synthesis-coefficient modeling, enabling a generative model on the raw signal.  
- Key practical advantage: inherent multi-resolution synthesis by combining different time-frequency frames for transients and tonal components.  
- Two estimation procedures derived: tractable MJLE (EM) and currently non-scalable MMLE.  
- Demonstrated on audio tasks (music decomposition, speech enhancement).  

- Strength: novel and elegant conceptual shift linking synthesis-based signal models with low-rank factorization.  
- Strength: powerful multi-resolution capability addressing a key limitation of standard NMF/STFT approaches.  
- Strength: technically sound derivations, clear EM algorithms, and relevant connection to Sparse Bayesian Learning.  
- Strength: well-written and clearly motivated paper.  

- Weakness: experiments lack ablation disentangling LRTFS benefits from multi-resolution effects; missing single-resolution LRTFS vs IS-NMF comparison.  
- Weakness: no detailed comparison of computational cost, convergence speed, or stability versus IS-NMF multiplicative updates.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in setting/scheduling the residual variance hyperparameter λ; reproducibility concerns.  

- Suggestion: add a single-resolution experiment directly comparing LRTFS and IS-NMF on the same T-F representation.  
- Suggestion: include a complexity/convergence analysis of MJLE relative to IS-NMF.  
- Suggestion: clarify how λ was selected or scheduled (e.g., the decreasing strategy used) to improve reproducibility.
