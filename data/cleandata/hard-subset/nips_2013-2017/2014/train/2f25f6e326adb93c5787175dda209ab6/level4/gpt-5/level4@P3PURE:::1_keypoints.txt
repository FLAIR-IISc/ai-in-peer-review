- Proposes extending low-rank spectrogram-factorization (GCM) to general/overcomplete bases for multiresolution decomposition (tonal + transient).  
- Derives a time-domain expression by making explicit the convolution with Gabor impulse responses, applicable to nonorthogonal bases.  
- Presents an EM analysis claiming convergence to a local optimum.  
- The idea of incorporating overcomplete dictionaries is appealing but likely to introduce complications that aren’t fully addressed.  
- The estimation derivation is impressive though some mathematical details were hard to follow; reviewer assumes correctness.  
- Method is well explained and motivated, but the empirical evaluation is very limited.  
- Only one illustrative result and one SNR improvement are reported, insufficient to support strong claims.  
- Sound examples are unconvincing: OMLSA comparison appears unfair (different durations) and its speech sounds subjectively higher quality.  
- Music denoising introduces noticeable artifacts; conventional methods (wavelet shrinkage/Wiener) might yield better perceived quality.  
- Relies heavily on Févotte et al.’s foundation, though the time-domain interpretation offers a fresh perspective.  
- Potentially significant if overcomplete matrix-factorization works well, but current examples and evaluation don’t demonstrate practical importance.
