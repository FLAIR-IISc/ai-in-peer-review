This paper presents a thoughtful interpretation of time/frequency factorizations. Rather than relying on existing formulations built on STFT analysis coefficients, it proposes a more flexible framework that can be applied to an arbitrary transform and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is clearly written and accessible to readers in this area, and its overall quality is good. Its originality is above average, though the significance is not fully conveyed.

The most disappointing aspect is the results and evaluation. The supplemental material is not particularly convincing, and the paper does not compare this approach to the methods it builds on. I do not view this as a critical weakness—the theoretical development alone makes the paper interesting—but such comparisons would have substantially strengthened it.

Overall, this is a good, well-written paper with solid theory. I would have liked to see more results and comparisons, but this is not a fatal flaw given the strength of the theoretical contribution.
