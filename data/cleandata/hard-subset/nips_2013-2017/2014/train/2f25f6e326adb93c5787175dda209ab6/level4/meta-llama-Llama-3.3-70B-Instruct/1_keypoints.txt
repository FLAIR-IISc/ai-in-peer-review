- Proposes extending low-rank spectrogram-factorization to generalize latent factors over arbitrary bases, including overcomplete and multiresolution (tonal/transient) bases.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model and low-rank variance modeling of spectrogram cells.  
- Expands the implicit convolution with multiple Gabor impulse responses to a time-domain expression usable with non-orthogonal impulse responses.  
- Presents an EM analysis procedure to estimate model parameters.  
- Idea is intriguing and offers an alternative perspective, but full implications are unclear.  
- Surprising that introducing overcomplete dictionaries does not appear to create major complications.  
- Analytical development is impressive but detailed derivations are hard to follow.  
- Evaluation is thin: relies on a single illustrative result and SNR improvement measure.  
- Sound examples are not convincing; OMLSA comparison seems unfair due to duration mismatch and OMLSA speech sounds better.  
- Music denoising produces notable artifacts; reviewer suggests conventional fixes like wavelet shrinkage or Wiener filtering.  
- Work is not very original (relies heavily on Fevotte), though the time-domain interpretation is a fresh viewpoint.  
- Potential significance is high if applied to overcomplete bases, but current evaluation and artifacts fail to demonstrate that impact.
