- Reformulates the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) in the time domain to model raw signals instead of STFT magnitudes — an interesting and technically sound idea.  
- Derives an MJLE EM algorithm for parameter estimation; the derivation appears correct and provides an elegant estimation procedure.  
- Enables incorporation of overcomplete, multi-resolution bases (e.g., separate tonal and transient dictionaries) in a unified probabilistic framework.  
- Connection to Sparse Bayesian Learning is clearly explained.  
- Paper lacks discussion of potential issues from overcomplete bases (identifiability, stability) and why they do not cause problems here.  
- Experimental evaluation is superficial and insufficient to substantiate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Music decomposition example is illustrative only and the denoising shows significant, audible artifacts.  
- Speech enhancement experiment reports only a single average SNR over a limited test set, which is inadequate.  
- Baseline comparison appears unfair: the OMLSA baseline audio is much longer than the proposed method’s output, raising doubts about equal conditions.  
- Subjectively, OMLSA results sound more natural despite noise; the proposed method’s speech sounds processed and less clear.  
- Conventional denoising methods (wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering) might yield better perceived quality on the same tasks.  
- Conceptual advance is novel and potentially significant, but practical impact is unproven due to weak empirical evidence.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and promising reframing, but limited and problematic experiments reduce the paper’s current impact.
