- Introduces LRTFS, a generative low-rank time-frequency synthesis model linking time-frequency analysis and NMF.  
- Extends previous NMF–GCM STFT link to a generative model of the raw signal.  
- Model is flexible across time-frequency transforms and supports multi-resolution representations.  
- Probabilistic framework is clear and well-grounded.  
- Connects to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) and highlights important similarities and differences.  
- Derives EM algorithms for parameter estimation and acknowledges non-convex optimization issues.  
- Discusses both MJLE and MMLE estimation strategies and their implications.  
- Draws useful connections to soft-thresholding and LASSO in the conclusion.  
- Notation, organization, and clarity of exposition are strong.  
- Multi-resolution capability is an insightful contribution for signals with varying time-frequency content.  
- Originality is above average by providing a generative model for the raw signal absent in prior work.  
- Properly cites relevant prior work (e.g., NMF, SBL, Smaragdis, Tipping).  
- Experimental results are underwhelming, showing only modest SNR gains (≈6.6 dB) over simple baselines.  
- Supplementary audio examples do not convincingly demonstrate substantial improvements.  
- Comparisons are limited and miss stronger state-of-the-art methods, including deep learning baselines.  
- Parameter selection (λ) is manually tuned with no methodology provided, hurting reproducibility.  
- Reviewer lists strong theoretical foundation, clarity, originality, and multi-resolution potential as arguments for acceptance.  
- Reviewer cites limited empirical evidence, lack of comprehensive comparisons, and unclear significance as arguments against acceptance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen experiments, add thorough comparisons (including deep learning), and test other noise types.
