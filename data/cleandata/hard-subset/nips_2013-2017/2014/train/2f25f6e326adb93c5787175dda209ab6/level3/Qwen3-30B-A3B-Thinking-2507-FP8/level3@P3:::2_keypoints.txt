- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) that models time-domain signals via generative synthesis coefficients instead of spectrogram (analysis) coefficients.  
- Builds on the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and extends it to model raw signals directly, enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- Key insight: synthesis-coefficient modeling naturally supports components with different temporal resolutions, unlike traditional NMF.  
- Presents two EM algorithms for parameter estimation with sound mathematical derivations and links to IS-NMF and sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement show promising results and a modest SNR improvement over IS-NMF (6.6 dB vs. 6 dB).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides reproducible technical detail for experts.  
- Originality is strong: paradigm shift from analysis to synthesis coefficients and novel multi-resolution capability.  
- Significance is potentially high for audio tasks (transient vs. tonal modeling), but evidence of consistent multi-resolution benefits is limited.  
- Major strength: clear theoretical connections to prior work (GCM, SBL) and computationally efficient EM algorithms.  
- Major weakness: insufficient quantitative evaluation of multi-resolution benefits and lack of direct comparison to single-resolution IS-NMF.  
- Unclear whether LRTFS reproduces IS-NMF’s W and H in the single-resolution case; authors should clarify.  
- Missing comparisons of algorithm speed, stability, and computational complexity versus IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates.  
- Supplementary material needs more implementation details to improve reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: interesting and novel but requires stronger empirical validation (quantitative multi-resolution comparisons and single-resolution baselines) before acceptance.
