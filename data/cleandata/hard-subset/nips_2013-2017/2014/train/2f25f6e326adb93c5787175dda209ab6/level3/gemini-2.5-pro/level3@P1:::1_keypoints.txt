- Proposes LRTFS: a synthesis-domain extension of the Gaussian Composite Model that models synthesis coefficients’ variance with low-rank structure.
- Main novelty: shifts low-rank probabilistic modeling from analysis (spectrogram) to synthesis (time-domain) representations.
- Key practical benefit: naturally supports multi-resolution decompositions by combining multiple time-frequency bases (e.g., long and short windows).
- Model can handle arbitrary, non-orthogonal, and overcomplete time-frequency dictionaries, not limited to STFT orthogonal bases.
- Two EM estimation schemes derived: maximum joint likelihood (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood (MMLE); experiments use scalable MJLE.
- Well-written presentation with clear relation to GCM and connections to sparse Bayesian learning.
- Experimental validation is thin: music decomposition is only illustrative with no objective metrics or baseline comparisons and shows audible artifacts.
- Speech enhancement shows only a small average SNR gain (0.6 dB) over IS-NMF, which may be insignificant and potentially worse in subjective quality than baselines.
- Evaluation is limited to one noise type (applauses), leaving generalization unclear.
- Overcomplete basis issues (non-uniqueness, identifiability, local minima) are not discussed and could affect estimation.
- Derivation clarity is lacking: MJLE EM derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow and update equations are taken on faith.
- Recommended experiments: comparisons to multiple state-of-the-art baselines, objective metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ/STOI), varied noises, and ablation studies on multi-resolution benefit.
- Originality is incremental—reformulates and generalizes GCM to time-domain rather than a fully novel paradigm.
- Requests clarification on practical choices: how β was set and sensitivity to β (β ≤ λ/δΦ), and reasons for avoiding estimation of λ in experiments.
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially impactful idea but currently a proof-of-concept lacking convincing empirical evidence to demonstrate practical significance.
