- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure.  
- Extends Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by providing a generative model of the raw time-domain signal rather than only the STFT.  
- Offers a thoughtful interpretation that departs from STFT-based formulations and links factorization directly to the time-domain signal.  
- Presents a flexible time/frequency factorization framework applicable to arbitrary transforms.  
- Provides solid theoretical development and derivation.  
- Introduces two expectation-maximization algorithms for parameter estimation in the proposed model.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and contains enough detail for expert reproduction.  
- Experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement are promising and demonstrate approach viability.  
- Results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing and the supplemental material is not convincing.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods it builds on, making significance hard to assess.  
- Hyperparameter λ is treated as fixed rather than estimated, which is a methodological shortcoming.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10); authors should add more results/comparisons and improve λ estimation.
