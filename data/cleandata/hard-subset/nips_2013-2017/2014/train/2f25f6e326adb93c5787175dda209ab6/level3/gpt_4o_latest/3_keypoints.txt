- Contribution: Introduces LRTFS, a probabilistic low-rank time-frequency synthesis framework that directly models time-domain signals with multi-resolution representations and provides two EM estimation algorithms, shown on music decomposition and speech enhancement.
- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Extends the Gaussian Composite Model to raw signals, enabling multi-resolution modeling and a direct time-domain link.
- Strength — Clarity and presentation: Well-written, well-organized, with thorough mathematical derivations and clear connections to related work.
- Strength — Novelty: Original methodological contribution that meaningfully advances multi-resolution time-frequency modeling.
- Strength — Potential applications: Framework could benefit audio tasks like source separation and enhancement despite limited demos.
- Weakness — Significance and impact: Practical advantages are not convincingly demonstrated; experimental results underwhelming.
- Weakness — Lack of comparative analysis: No adequate quantitative comparison to NMF-based or state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness — Experimental evaluation: Experiments are limited and not rigorous (modest speech-enhancement gains, ad hoc hyperparameter choices, insufficient supplementary detail).
- Weakness — Scalability: The MMLE algorithm is not scalable to real-world applications, limiting practical utility.
- Recommendation for improvement: Provide more comprehensive evaluations across datasets/tasks, direct comparisons to state-of-the-art, and clearer communication of broader impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: Solid theoretical foundation, strong writing, and a novel approach with extension potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient experimental evidence and weak comparative analysis reduce the paper's practical impact.
- Final recommendation: Weak Accept — noteworthy theoretical work but needs stronger experimental validation and clearer significance.
