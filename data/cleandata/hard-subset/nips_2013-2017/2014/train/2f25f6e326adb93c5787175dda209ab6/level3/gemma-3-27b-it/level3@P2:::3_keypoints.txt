- Proposes a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model that links factorization with direct raw-signal synthesis.  
- Models the signal as a sum of atoms with low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances, differing from GCM which operates on STFT coefficients.  
- Framework easily incorporates multi-resolution representations and presents EM algorithms for estimation.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and mathematically rigorous.  
- Builds on and situates itself within NMF, GCM, and sparse Bayesian learning literature, clearly articulating differences.  
- References are relevant and show awareness of related work (morphological component analysis, hybrid representations).  
- Strength: genuine novelty and multi-resolution capability beyond traditional NMF methods.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding and connection to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Strength: exceptional clarity, consistent notation, and helpful diagrams.  
- Strength: general framework applicable to arbitrary time-frequency bases and flexible regularization.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited and results (e.g., 6.6 dB SNR) are modest and not clearly superior to existing methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient baselines and lack of comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms for music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Weakness: paper does not convincingly communicate practical advantages or significance of the framework.  
- Weakness: computational complexity of the MMLE approach appears intractable for presented applications and needs more discussion or approximations.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound framework with clear exposition and valuable multi-resolution capability.  
- Against acceptance: weak empirical validation, lack of strong baselines, and limited demonstrated practical impact.  
- Overall: good theoretical contribution and presentation but insufficient experimental evidence to justify acceptance at a top conference.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; encourage stronger experiments, comprehensive comparisons, and focus on an application where LRTFS clearly outperforms alternatives.
