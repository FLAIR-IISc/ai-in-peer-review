- Proposes LRTFS: a novel time-domain synthesis model extending NMF/GCM to generate raw audio with low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s GCM and provides a time-domain interpretation enabling arbitrary (including overcomplete) time–frequency bases.  
- Introduces multi-resolution representations that can model tonal and transient components with different temporal resolutions.  
- Establishes a connection to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL) and clarifies how LRTFS’s low-rank constraint differs from SBL’s sparse variance structure.  
- Derives two estimation algorithms (MJLE and MMLE), with MJLE being computationally efficient and avoiding matrix inversions.  
- Demonstrates applications on music decomposition (tonal + transient + residual) and speech enhancement.  
- Strength: time-domain reformulation is theoretically elegant and offers a fresh perspective.  
- Strength: multi-resolution capability is a genuine conceptual advance for audio processing.  
- Strength: MJLE algorithm is computationally efficient and practical.  
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear mathematical derivations and organization.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is insufficient—only one music example and one speech result, undermining practical claims.  
- Weakness: music decomposition shows significant artifacts compared to conventional methods.  
- Weakness: speech enhancement results (avg. SNR 6.6 dB vs. 6 dB IS-NMF, 4.6 dB OMLSA) do not convincingly outperform baselines.  
- Weakness: comparisons may be unfair—different audio lengths/windows used between methods.  
- Weakness: lack of discussion on complications from overcomplete bases (non-uniqueness, numerical instability).  
- Weakness: theoretical claims about multi-resolution benefits are not empirically validated.  
- Originality: extends GCM meaningfully to raw-signal synthesis and adds multi-resolution capability, but builds substantially on prior work.  
- Significance: potential is plausible, but practical impact is unproven without stronger experiments.  
- Quality: theoretical development is sound, but needs more discussion of overcomplete-basis challenges; empirical work is the main shortcoming.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-presented, though some derivations (e.g., SBL connection) could be more accessible.  
- Recommendation: interesting theoretical contribution but requires substantially more and fairer experiments to validate practical significance before acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel time-domain GCM extension, meaningful multi-resolution advance, and efficient MJLE algorithm.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical evidence, potentially unfair baseline comparisons, unaddressed overcomplete-basis issues, and unproven practical significance.
