- Introduces LRTFS: models time-domain signals via synthesis coefficients instead of TF analysis coefficients, bridging a gap with NMF approaches.  
- Proposes two EM-based parameter estimation algorithms (MJLE and MMLE).  
- Enables multi-resolution representations not feasible with traditional NMF.  
- Demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Strength: Multi-resolution modeling is novel and practically relevant for audio with transients and tonal components.  
- Strength: Solid theoretical grounding linking to sparse Bayesian learning and probabilistic latent factor models, with detailed derivations.  
- Strength: Experimental results, especially in speech enhancement, are competitive with IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct single-resolution comparison to IS-NMF and analysis of W/H similarity.  
- Weakness: Computational concerns—MJLE may converge to local minima; MMLE is impractical for large-scale problems due to matrix inversions.  
- Weakness: No provided scalable alternative to MMLE, limiting applicability to high-dimensional signals.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, but needs supplementary materials (e.g., sound examples) and clearer discussion of MJLE vs MMLE trade-offs.  
- Originality & significance: Highly original contribution with potential to advance generative modeling for audio/time-series.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with encouragement to address single-resolution performance and computational scalability in future work.
