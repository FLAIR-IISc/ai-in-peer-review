- Proposes LRTFS, a novel signal synthesis model that models raw signals with low-rank time-frequency structure, extending the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM).  
- Represents signals as linear combinations of time-frequency atoms with synthesis coefficients that have a low-rank latent structure.  
- Introduces two estimation methods: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE).  
- MJLE is implemented with an EM algorithm that alternates computing posterior means and updating model parameters.  
- MMLE optimizes the marginal likelihood but is more computationally expensive and not used in experiments.  
- Proposes a multi-resolution LRTFS extension using multiple time-frequency bases for different resolutions.  
- Evaluates LRTFS on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks, showing effective decomposition into tonal/transient components and noise reduction.  
- Reports better SNR performance for LRTFS compared to IS-NMF in the experiments.  
- Strength: novel combination of time-frequency synthesis and NMF concepts for low-rank TF modeling.  
- Strength: multi-resolution extension enables representation across different time-frequency scales.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on two audio tasks.  
- Weakness: MMLE is computationally heavy and omitted from experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other state-of-the-art methods beyond IS-NMF.  
- Weakness: insufficient detailed analysis of EM convergence properties for MJLE.  
- Originality: presents a new modeling direction bridging synthesis-based TF models and NMF-like low-rank structure.  
- Significance: has potential applications in music decomposition, speech enhancement, source separation, and audio coding.  
- Quality/Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and provides clear mathematical derivations and experimental descriptions.
