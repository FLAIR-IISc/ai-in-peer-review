- Novel biophysically inspired interpretation of the GLM and introduction of a conductance-based spiking model (CBSM).
- CBSM allows independent excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs, relaxing GLM’s equal-and-opposite conductance constraint.
- Solid theoretical development showing the GLM is a special case of the CBSM.
- CBSM provides a more realistic and flexible model that better captures neural responses.
- Demonstrated ability to estimate excitatory and inhibitory tuning from spike times in retinal ganglion cells.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, clear, and original.
- Experimental results and supplemental material are somewhat unconvincing and need better presentation.
- Lacks thorough comparisons to existing methods and models in the field.
- Needs more detailed discussion of implications, applications, and broader significance.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve evaluation, comparisons, and discussion.
