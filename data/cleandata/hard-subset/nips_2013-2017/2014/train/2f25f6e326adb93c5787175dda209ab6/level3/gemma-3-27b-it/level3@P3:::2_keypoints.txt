- Proposes a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model that models raw signal synthesis coefficients instead of STFT magnitudes.  
- Main contribution: a true generative signal model enabling multi-resolution time-frequency representations.  
- Well-motivated extension of Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) with insightful connection to sparse Bayesian learning; presentation is generally clear.  
- Multi-resolution extension is particularly appealing for handling transients and sustained tones in audio.  
- Core idea of modeling signal synthesis directly is significant and original compared to traditional NMF approaches.  
- Experiments are limited: the jazz example is illustrative but lacks quantitative evaluation.  
- Speech enhancement results show SNR improvements comparable to IS-NMF and OMLSA but need broader comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Missing direct comparison to IS-NMF in the single-resolution case, including whether learned W and H matrices match.  
- Computational-cost claims are underdeveloped: MMLE scaling issues acknowledged, but MJLE’s claimed efficiency needs nuance and empirical timing/complexity analysis.  
- Request for clarity on whether the proposed algorithm matches IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates in speed and stability.  
- Lack of ablation studies to isolate benefits over simpler alternatives like standard NMF.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially significant work, recommended for acceptance only with major revisions.  
- Required revisions: compare to IS-NMF (W/H analysis), provide quantitative multi-resolution evaluation on standardized datasets, include computational complexity/timing comparisons, and add ablation studies.
