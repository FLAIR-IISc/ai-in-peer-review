

This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel framework that models time-domain signals using generative time-frequency synthesis coefficients rather than traditional analysis coefficients used in NMF. The key contribution is enabling multi-resolution representations, which are not possible with conventional NMF approaches. The authors present two EM-based algorithms for estimation and demonstrate applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement.

The work is highly original in shifting from analysis to synthesis coefficients, providing a direct generative model of the raw signal rather than its STFT. This represents a significant advancement over the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) described in prior work [3], as it bridges the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional NMF. The multi-resolution extension is particularly compelling, allowing for different temporal resolutions in different signal components (e.g., transient vs. tonal elements in audio), which is not feasible with standard NMF.

The paper demonstrates promising results in music decomposition and speech enhancement, with the proposed method outperforming IS-NMF baseline by 0.6 dB in speech enhancement. However, the evaluation lacks critical comparisons: the authors should provide a direct comparison with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case to verify whether the learned W and H matrices match (as requested in the key points). Additionally, the paper does not address whether the proposed algorithm matches IS-NMF's multiplicative updates in speed and stability, which would be crucial for practical adoption.

The quality of the paper is strong technically, with well-justified derivations and clear presentation of the algorithms. The clarity is excellent, with a logical structure and thorough explanations. The significance is high for audio signal processing applications, though the potential impact could be better demonstrated with more comprehensive comparisons.

Strengths: Novel synthesis-based approach, elegant multi-resolution extension, strong theoretical foundation, promising experimental results.

Weaknesses: Insufficient comparison with IS-NMF in single-resolution case, lack of speed/stability comparison with existing methods, limited evaluation of the multi-resolution capability's advantages.

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field, but requires additional experiments to fully validate its claims and establish its advantages over existing methods. With the requested comparisons, this would be a strong candidate for acceptance.
