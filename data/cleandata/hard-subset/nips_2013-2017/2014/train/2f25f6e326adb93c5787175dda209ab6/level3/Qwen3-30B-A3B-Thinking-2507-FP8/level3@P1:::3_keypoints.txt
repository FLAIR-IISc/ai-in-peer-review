- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), extending GCM from STFT to modeling raw time-domain signals via synthesis coefficients.  
- Novelty: moves from analysis (STFT) to signal-level generative modeling, enabling direct low-rank variance structure on synthesis coefficients.  
- Enables multi-resolution decomposition (different temporal resolutions for transient vs. tonal components) applicable to arbitrary TF bases (e.g., Gabor frames).  
- Theoretical development is strong, with clear derivations and connections to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Introduces two estimation methods: MJLE (computationally efficient) and MMLE (marginal likelihood), with EM-style algorithms.  
- MJLE avoids matrix inversions using Wiener filtering and multiplicative updates, integrated elegantly with multi-resolution extension.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and technically sound.  
- Empirical validation is weak: only marginal gains over baselines (e.g., 6.6 dB vs 6.0 dB SNR for speech enhancement).  
- Lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (notably deep learning-based source separation) to contextualize practical value.  
- Multi-resolution claims are not substantiated by rigorous baselines or quantitative evidence (no comparison to prior multi-resolution work).  
- Supplemental/high SNR results lack baselines, making their significance unclear.  
- MMLE is dismissed for scalability without investigating whether MJLE improvements justify complexity.  
- Experimental section misses ablations, statistical significance tests, and comparisons to recent audio-processing methods.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — solid theoretical contribution but insufficient empirical evidence; authors should add SOTA comparisons, standard-dataset benchmarks, and rigorous quantitative evaluations to demonstrate practical significance.
