- Proposal: LRTFS — a synthesis-domain generative time-domain model imposing a low-rank WH variance on complex synthesis coefficients to model raw waveforms and handle phase implicitly.

- Multi-resolution: natural multi-resolution extension by stacking multiple frames (e.g., short/long Gabor windows) that STFT-domain NMF cannot easily accommodate.

- Model vs prior GCM: shifts low-rank variance from STFT analysis coefficients (GCM) to synthesis coefficients, linking waveform modeling to NMF-like structure.

- MJLE estimation: auxiliary-variable EM (Figueiredo–Nowak inspired) alternating Wiener-type shrinkage of α and IS-NMF updates of W,H; avoids large matrix inversions and uses efficient analysis/synthesis ops.

- MJLE property: joint likelihood induces a log(|α|^2) term that favors sparsity alongside the low-rank variance penalty.

- MMLE estimation: marginalizes α (type-II ML/SBL) and is theoretically attractive but requires large matrix inversions and is currently not scalable for realistic audio.

- Multi-resolution estimation: the same EM skeleton is reused for the multi-resolution model, keeping algorithmic simplicity.

- Applications demonstrated: jazz tonal/transient hybrid decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with pretrained speech dictionaries and noise adaptation.

- Empirical results: reported average SNR gain +6.6 dB over 10 utterances, modestly above IS-NMF (+6.0 dB) and OMLSA (+4.6 dB); MMLE not shown at scale.

- Relation to prior work: builds on IS-NMF/GCM, SBL/type-II ML, hybrid/multi-resolution decompositions; component reconstruction uses Wiener-style estimators analogous to GCM.

- Strength — concept: principled synthesis-domain low-rank variance model that bridges waveform modeling and NMF-style structure with clear links to GCM and SBL.

- Strength — algorithm: simple, computationally viable EM for MJLE with no large matrix inversions and a compelling multi-resolution extension.

- Strength — exposition: sound, interpretable derivations (Wiener shrinkage + IS-NMF) with clear implementation details.

- Weakness — empirical scope: limited experiments, modest improvements, and lack of standard separation/enhancement metrics.

- Weakness — baselines: missing comparisons to closely related state-of-the-art (multi-window/multi-resolution STFT NMF, structured/temporal NMF, phase-aware/complex NMF, time-domain sparse synthesis).

- Weakness — analysis: no ablation/sensitivity studies (λ, β, K, window sizes, redundancy, initialization) and little discussion of runtime/complexity.

- Weakness — MMLE: presented conceptually but not realized at scale; no exploration of approximate scalable inference strategies.

- Quality: technically solid probabilistic modeling and well-motivated auxiliary-variable EM; empirical validation is insufficient to prove practical advantages.

- Clarity: well written and organized, but needs details on β choice/stability, Hermitian symmetry for real signals, computational costs, initialization, and effect of fixing λ.

- Originality: above average — synthesis-centric low-rank variance model and multi-resolution extension form a novel, coherent framework despite using known components.

- Significance: potentially impactful for audio and time-series with multi-resolution structure, but current results do not decisively outperform mature STFT-domain methods.

- Suggestions: strengthen experiments with standard metrics (BSS Eval, PESQ, STOI), CIs, runtime profiling; compare to more baselines; add ablations; explore scalable MMLE (Woodbury+CG, preconditioning, AMP, variational EM); release code/dictionaries.

- Arguments for acceptance: principled and flexible modeling linking synthesis to low-rank TF structure, simple scalable MJLE EM, and clear exposition likely useful to the community.

- Arguments against acceptance: limited and modest empirical evidence, lack of comprehensive comparisons/ablations, and MMLE left theoretical without scalable implementation.

- Overall evaluation: solid, well-written theoretical contribution; reviewer leans borderline accept — promising ideas but needs stronger and broader empirical validation.
