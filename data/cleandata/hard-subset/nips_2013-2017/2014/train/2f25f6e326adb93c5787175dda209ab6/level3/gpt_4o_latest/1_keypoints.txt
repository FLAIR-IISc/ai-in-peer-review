- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a time-domain generative extension of low-rank spectrogram-factorization models linking NMF to raw signal synthesis.  
- Generalizes latent factors over arbitrary, potentially overcomplete bases, enabling multi-resolution representations not possible with standard NMF.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.'s Gaussian Composite Model and expands Gabor-convolution to a time-domain expression.  
- Introduces two expectation-maximization (EM) algorithms for parameter estimation.  
- Demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Strength: addresses a key NMF limitation by modeling raw signals directly rather than spectrograms.  
- Strength: supports overcomplete and multi-resolution bases, useful for differing time-frequency signal components (transients vs tonal).  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding with connections to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL).  
- Empirical results show flexibility and SNR improvements over baselines like IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Weakness: EM estimation procedure is dense, technical, and hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited and lacks comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: denoised outputs show artifacts and lower perceived quality versus conventional methods (e.g., wavelet shrinkage, Wiener filtering); multi-resolution claims not fully validated.  
- Pro: novel, theoretically sound contribution with promising multi-resolution and overcomplete-basis capabilities.  
- Con: practical significance undermined by insufficient evaluation, result artifacts, and clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to improve clarity of estimation, expand experiments, and address observed artifacts.
