- Proposes LRTFS: a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that uses generative TF synthesis coefficients rather than TF analysis coefficients.  
- Model formulated as a Gaussian mixture where synthesis coefficients are conditionally independent with a low-rank latent structure.  
- Introduces estimation methods: maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE).  
- Enables multi-resolution TF representations that traditional NMF cannot provide.  
- Multi-resolution extension is interesting and appealing; some decomposition results are shown.  
- Reviewer requests more extensive evaluation to fully characterize the method’s capabilities.  
- Asks for direct comparison with single-resolution IS-NMF to assess parity in speed and stability.  
- Requests verification whether LRTFS’s estimated W and H match those obtained by IS-NMF in the single-resolution case.  
- Strengths: novelty, clear and concise presentation, consistent notation, well-organized figures/tables.  
- Paper is technically sound with a thorough related-work discussion and explicit limitations.  
- Originality: presents an innovative bridge between TF synthesis and traditional NMF with potential new insights.  
- Significance: potential impact across audio, image, and biomedical signal processing due to multi-resolution representations.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the requested additional evaluations and IS-NMF comparisons.
