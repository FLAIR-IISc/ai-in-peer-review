- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) to extend low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary bases and multi-resolution decompositions.  
- Aims to bridge time-frequency synthesis methods with traditional NMF approaches for applications like source separation and denoising.  
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), making implicit convolutions explicit and allowing non-orthogonal bases.  
- Presents an EM-based local-optimum estimation procedure that is computationally friendly and avoids matrix inversion.  
- Paper is well-organized and generally clearly written, making the model and estimation procedure understandable.  
- Derivation of the EM estimation is somewhat hard to follow in detail.  
- Evaluation is thin: only one illustrative result and a single SNR measurement, insufficient to support claims.  
- Heavy reliance on prior work by Fevotte et al. reduces perceived originality despite the time-domain reformulation.  
- Empirical significance is unproven due to weak and limited experimental demonstration.  
- Experiments and comparisons are limited and not comprehensive.  
- Baseline (OMLSA) appears to give higher speech quality; music denoising shows artifacts compared to conventional methods.  
- Comparisons may be unfair (e.g., different audio lengths across methods).  
- The approach of applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases is interesting but raises complications that are not addressed.  
- Paper is technically sound but needs more comprehensive experiments and clearer handling of overcomplete-basis implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, potential benefits for separation/denoising, and a practical EM estimator without matrix inversion.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited originality, weak evaluation, and failure to convincingly demonstrate practical importance.
