- Proposes a novel signal synthesis model extending low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary bases, enabling multiresolution decompositions with potential for source separation and denoising.
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), expanding implicit convolutions into explicit impulse-response expressions and supporting non-orthogonal bases.
- Presents an EM algorithm for local-optimum estimation, a significant methodological contribution.
- EM derivation is difficult to follow in detail and needs clearer exposition.
- Claims about applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases are intriguing but the paper does not address complications introduced by overcompleteness.
- Evaluation is very thin: only one illustrative result and a single SNR measurement, insufficient to support the paper’s claims.
- Baseline comparison may be unfair due to different audio lengths used for methods.
- Reviewer perceives higher speech quality for the baseline (OMLSA) and significant artifacts in music denoising from the proposed method.
- Originality is limited by heavy reliance on Fevotte et al., though the time-domain reformulation offers a fresh perspective.
- Significance is plausible but unproven because of weak empirical demonstration.
- Recommendation: paper has potential but needs substantially improved evaluation, clarification of overcomplete-basis issues, and a clearer EM derivation before acceptance.
