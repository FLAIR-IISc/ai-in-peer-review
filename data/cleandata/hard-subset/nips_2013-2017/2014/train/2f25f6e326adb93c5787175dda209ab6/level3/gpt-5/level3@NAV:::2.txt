This paper proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a generative time-domain model that places a low-rank Gaussian prior on TF synthesis coefficients (α) rather than on analysis coefficients (STFT) as in IS-NMF/Gaussian composite models. An EM algorithm for maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) leverages the Figueiredo–Nowak auxiliary variable trick to avoid large matrix inversions, and a type-II ML route (MMLE), akin to SBL, is discussed but not yet scalable. A key contribution is a natural multi-resolution extension by summing over multiple TF frames, enabling joint decomposition across short/long windows. Experiments on hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement illustrate feasibility.

Relation to prior work: The paper is well-situated with respect to IS-NMF and the GCM for STFTs (Févotte et al.), SBL (Tipping; Wipf & Rao), and multi-resolution/morphological decompositions (Daudet & Torrésani; Kowalski). Modeling in the synthesis domain is a clear and original departure from standard NMF pipelines.

Quality: The MJLE derivation is sound, connections to Wiener filtering are clear, and the algorithm uses only Φ/Φ* multiplies. However, the empirical section is limited. λ is treated as a hyperparameter, and there is no systematic analysis of convergence, stability, or runtime. The MMLE path is theoretically appropriate but currently impractical.

Clarity: The paper is generally well organized. Please specify how β is chosen relative to λ/δΦ, report complexity per iteration (in FLOPs) as a function of T, M, and frame redundancy, and add implementation details sufficient for reproduction (windowing, overlap-add, initializations, restarts).

Originality: High. Modeling synthesis coefficients to unlock multi-resolution low-rank structure is novel and conceptually appealing.

Significance: Potentially high for audio ML and beyond; the reported gains are modest but the modeling flexibility is valuable.

Requests for revision:
- Single-resolution comparison to IS-NMF: With a tight/orthonormal STFT and small λ, do W and H learned by LRTFS match those from IS-NMF on |Y|2? Quantify correlations/alignments and reconstruction quality.
- Speed and stability: Provide wall-clock times, iterations to convergence, and failure rates relative to IS-NMF multiplicative updates on the same data; include sensitivity to β, initialization, and K.
- Multi-resolution evaluation: Compare against the best single-window IS-NMF over a sweep of window sizes and against multi-window/structured NMF or MCA baselines; report SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ/STOI, and error bars across multiple seeds.
- Reproducibility: Release code and scripts; detail datasets and hyperparameter selection.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Conceptually clean, generative time-domain framework with a practical EM algorithm.
- Natural and useful multi-resolution extension not available to standard NMF.
- Clear links to established models (IS-NMF, Wiener filtering, SBL).

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited and primarily illustrative evaluation; modest quantitative gains.
- Missing single-resolution equivalence study and runtime/stability comparisons to IS-NMF.
- MMLE contribution remains unscalable in practice.
