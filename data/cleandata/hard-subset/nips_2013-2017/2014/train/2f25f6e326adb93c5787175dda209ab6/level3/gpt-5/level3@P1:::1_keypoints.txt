- Proposes LRTFS: a generative time-domain synthesis model imposing low-rank structure on synthesis-coefficient variances in arbitrary (possibly overcomplete) TF dictionaries, complementing GCM analysis view.  
- Derives two estimators: scalable MJLE via EM with auxiliary variable (alternating Wiener-like shrinkage + IS-NMF updates) and MMLE akin to SBL but impractical at audio scale due to matrix inversions.  
- Presents a natural multi-resolution extension by stacking frames with separate low-rank structures; demonstrated on tonal/transient decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with modest SNR gains.  
- Positions work relative to prior art: bridges probabilistic NMF/GCM and synthesis sparse-coding/MCA; MMLE ties variances via WH (removing sparsity-inducing behavior) rather than independent variances.  
- Strength: clear unified modeling idea that handles non-orthogonal/overcomplete frames in a principled way.  
- Strength: scalable MJLE-EM avoids large matrix inversions and reuses standard IS-NMF updates and TF toolboxes.  
- Strength: elegant and practical multi-resolution modeling useful for tonal/transient separation.  
- Strength: solid connections to GCM and SBL situating the contribution in known probabilistic frameworks.  
- Weakness: dense and insufficiently justified derivations (e.g., origin/role of log|α|2, conditions on β ≤ λ/δΦ, convergence with redundant Φ).  
- Weakness: overcompleteness causes non-uniqueness and conditioning issues; no analysis of identifiability, effect on δΦ, EM stability/speed, or sensitivity to initialization.  
- Weakness: MMLE is presented but lacks practical scalable approximations (no iterative solvers, diagonal/low-rank covariance approximations, or message passing).  
- Weakness: experimental protocol tunes λ to maximize test SNR, risking optimistic and unfair comparisons.  
- Clarity issue: need clearer explanation of probabilistic implications of analysis→synthesis and conditions under which synthesis reduces to GCM (e.g., unitary Φ).  
- Clarity issue: multi-resolution section lacks explicit single-resolution LRTFS control to quantify added value.  
- Originality: interesting and fresh reformulation from analysis-GCM to synthesis with tied low-rank variances, but core algorithmic ingredients are established.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for audio decomposition/source separation, but empirical evidence is thin and qualitative artifacts/questionable speech quality call practical advantage into question.  
- Empirical suggestions: include stronger baselines (multi-window IS-NMF, phase-aware NMF, PLCA, sparse synthesis methods), report standard metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ, STOI) with CIs, and provide runtime/memory.  
- Ablation recommendations: compare single vs multi-resolution LRTFS, vary Φ redundancy, number of components K, learned vs fixed dictionaries, role of residual e and λ, and MMSE vs proposed proxy reconstructions.  
- Methodology recommendation: avoid tuning hyperparameters on test data; use validation splits.  
- Questions for authors: when does LRTFS reduce to GCM/IS-NMF (unitary vs redundant Φ)? How sensitive is MJLE to β and λ and how to set them without test labels? Do multiple local minima arise and how do initializations affect outcomes? Can MMLE be approximated scalably?  
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually neat synthesis reformulation, scalable EM easy for practitioners to adopt, flexible multi-resolution modeling with promising qualitative results.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited and potentially biased empirical validation, insufficient analysis of overcompleteness-related robustness, and incomplete MMLE contribution; overall moderate novelty.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising idea but needs stronger experiments, clearer derivations, robustness analysis, and a scalable MMLE approximation to be convincing.
