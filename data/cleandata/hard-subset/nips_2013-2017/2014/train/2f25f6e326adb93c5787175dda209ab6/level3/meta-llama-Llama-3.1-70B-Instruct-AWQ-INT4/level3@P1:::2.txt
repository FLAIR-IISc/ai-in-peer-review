The paper "Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis" presents a novel signal synthesis model that bridges the gap between time-frequency (TF) synthesis and traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches. The proposed framework models the time-domain signal via generative TF synthesis coefficients, rather than TF analysis coefficients used in regular NMF. This approach enables multi-resolution representations that traditional NMF cannot provide.

The authors introduce a new low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model, which is defined by a set of equations that describe the generative process of the signal. The model is based on a Gaussian mixture model, where the synthesis coefficients are assumed to be conditionally independent and have a low-rank latent structure. The authors also present two estimation methods, maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) and maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE), which are used to estimate the model parameters.

The reviewer finds the multi-resolution extension of the LRTFS model interesting and appealing. The authors present some multi-resolution decomposition results, but more evaluation is desired to fully understand the capabilities of the proposed approach. The reviewer requests a comparison with the single-resolution case, specifically with IS-NMF, to assess whether the proposed algorithm matches IS-NMF's multiplicative updates in speed and stability. Additionally, the reviewer asks whether the proposed algorithm's estimates of W and H match those obtained by IS-NMF.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the proposed approach, the clarity of the presentation, and the potential of the LRTFS model to provide multi-resolution representations. However, the reviewer notes that more evaluation is needed to fully assess the capabilities of the proposed approach.
