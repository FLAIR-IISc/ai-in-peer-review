- Proposes LRTFS: a generative time-domain model where complex TF synthesis coefficients αfn ∼ Nc(0,[WH]fn) produce the waveform x(t)=∑fn αfn φfn(t)+e(t), imposing low-rank covariance on synthesis coefficients.  
- Introduces two estimation schemes: MJLE via an EM algorithm alternating Wiener-type shrinkage of α and an IS-NMF M-step on |α|2, and MMLE (SBL-style) that integrates out α but is currently computationally prohibitive for audio-scale problems.  
- Key novel contribution: multi-resolution extension that stacks multiple TF frames (e.g., short and long windows) to jointly model transients and sustained components, which standard NMF-on-spectrogram cannot natively do.  
- Experiments on hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement show qualitative separation and SNR improvements over IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Builds directly on IS-NMF/GCM by moving the low-rank generative model from STFT analysis coefficients to waveform synthesis coefficients.  
- Connects to SBL/RVM: LRTFS is a structured Gaussian scale model tying variances via WH, trading sparsity for low-rank structure.  
- Multi-resolution decomposition relates to MCA and hybrid audio models but replaces sparsity priors with low-rank priors in a coherent synthesis model.  
- Modeling and MJLE-EM derivation are sound; the log(|α|2) term in the joint objective is unusual and warrants a concise appendix derivation.  
- The EM algorithm is practical, avoiding large matrix inversions and relying only on fast analysis/synthesis routines.  
- MMLE is principled but currently unscalable; the paper appropriately scopes it to smaller problems.  
- Empirical validation is promising but limited in scope and missing several critical baselines and diagnostics.  
- Paper is overall well written but could streamline indexing (m vs (f,n)) and clarify several implementation details.  
- Requests to summarize per-iteration complexity and memory vs IS-NMF, provide MJLE pseudocode (and multi-resolution variant) with β and λ choices, clarify Hermitian symmetry handling for real signals, and state initialization and stopping criteria.  
- Suggests adding a short discussion on identifiability and scale ambiguities (WH, α, window gains).  
- Originality: novel application of low-rank priors to synthesis coefficients with a viable EM procedure and multi-resolution extension; insightful link that IS-NMF+Wiener corresponds to the first MJLE iteration.  
- Significance: directly modeling waveforms with low-rank TF structure and multi-resolution layers is valuable for audio tasks and potentially other signal domains.  
- Strengths: conceptual advance bridging analysis and synthesis, practical EM algorithm, natural multi-resolution extension, clear SBL connections, and promising initial results.  
- Weaknesses: limited evaluation and diagnostics, no single-resolution head-to-head with IS-NMF to check W/H alignment, and missing computational (speed/stability) comparisons.  
- Hyperparameter λ is treated as a tuned hyperparameter due to instability; need guidance and robustness analysis for β and λ selection.  
- MMLE remains impractical at audio scale and no scalable approximate alternative is proposed.  
- Requested experiments: single-resolution equivalence study with factor alignment metrics; per-iteration cost, wall-clock time, convergence plots vs IS-NMF; robustness analysis for β and λ; multi-resolution ablation with equal total K and objective metrics (SNR/SDR/STOI/PESQ).  
- Additional baseline requests: GCM+Wiener, concatenated-spectrogram IS-NMF, and supervised/semi-supervised IS-NMF with identical dictionaries for speech enhancement.  
- Reproducibility: provide pseudocode, parameter settings (windows, hops, K per layer), Hermitian symmetry handling, stopping criteria, and initialization details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated generative model, scalable EM linking to established methods, compelling multi-resolution capability, and promising empirical benefits.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical thoroughness, lack of quantitative runtime/stability comparisons, λ requires ad hoc tuning, and MMLE is largely theoretical at target scales.  
- Overall verdict: conceptually strong and practically promising paper that should be accepted if experiments and computational analyses are substantially strengthened.
