- Introduces LRTFS: a generative model that synthesizes raw audio from low-rank time-frequency coefficients, differing from prior analysis-based STFT low-rank models.  
- Models synthesis coefficients α with low-rank variance (Eqs. 2–4), enabling multi-resolution representations (e.g., separate tonal/transient layers).  
- Presents two EM estimation algorithms (MJLE and MMLE); MJLE is computationally feasible for audio.  
- Demonstrates applications: music decomposition (tonal/transient separation) and speech enhancement, reporting 6.6 dB SNR vs. 6.0 dB for IS-NMF.  
- Strength — originality: fills a gap by modeling the raw signal and providing a synthesis-based, multi-resolution approach novel to the literature.  
- Strength — technical soundness: MJLE derivation is rigorous with links to sparse Bayesian learning and Wiener filtering; multi-resolution extension is well integrated.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-structured with clear equations and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Strength — significance: enables resolution-adaptive decomposition important for transient-rich signals; reported SNR gain is practically meaningful.  
- Weakness — insufficient baseline comparison: no rigorous comparison to multi-resolution baselines like concatenated multi-STFTs + IS-NMF.  
- Weakness — missing IS-NMF equivalence check: authors do not verify whether MJLE reduces to standard IS-NMF under appropriate constraints.  
- Weakness — limited multi-resolution evaluation: lacks quantitative per-layer metrics and ablations isolating multi-resolution benefits (especially in speech experiment).  
- Acceptance pros: novel raw-signal generative framework, elegant multi-resolution solution, solid audio results, robust EM algorithms.  
- Acceptance cons: core claims under-validated (multi-resolution necessity, IS-NMF relation), missing ablations and stronger baselines.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept conditional on adding IS-NMF equivalence tests, multi-STFT+IS-NMF baselines, and ablation/quantitative multi-resolution evaluations.
