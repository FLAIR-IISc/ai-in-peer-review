- Introduces LRTFS: a novel model that directly generates the raw signal using low-rank time-frequency synthesis coefficients instead of decomposing the spectrogram.  
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Model (GCM), extending it from STFT-only modeling to the raw signal domain.  
- Naturally supports multi-resolution representations, a capability absent in standard NMF methods.  
- Presents two EM algorithms for parameter estimation.  
- Demonstrates feasibility on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.  
- Well-grounded theoretically with clear derivations and links to prior matrix factorization literature (NMF, GCM).  
- Thoughtful connection and comparison to sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), highlighting differences in coefficient structure and sparsity implications.  
- Positions work relative to prior multi-resolution analysis literature, strengthening novelty claims.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a significant potential advantage over traditional NMF.  
- Preliminary experimental results are promising but not exhaustive.  
- Lack of rigorous comparison to IS-NMF, especially unclear if single-resolution W and H match between methods.  
- Computational cost and convergence/stability of the EM algorithms are not thoroughly analyzed; local optima and warm-restart for λ are only briefly discussed.  
- No detailed assessment of algorithmic complexity or runtime relative to IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates.  
- Music decomposition lacks quantitative evaluation beyond subjective listening.  
- Speech enhancement shows modest improvement over IS-NMF and OMLSA but needs broader testing across noise types and datasets.  
- Reliance on a fixed λ parameter is a limitation; MMLE dismissed as impractical without thorough scaling attempts.  
- Arguments for acceptance: genuine novelty, theoretical soundness, multi-resolution feature, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental evaluation, missing direct IS-NMF comparison, insufficient analysis of EM complexity/stability, and fixed λ concern.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for (1) direct comparison to IS-NMF in single-resolution case, (2) analysis of EM computational complexity and stability, and (3) expanded experiments with diverse datasets and quantitative metrics.
