- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a time-domain extension of Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM).  
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of the GCM enabling nonorthogonal/overcomplete bases and multi-resolution audio decomposition.  
- Extends prior NMF/GCM approaches by modeling the raw signal directly rather than its STFT.  
- Introduces an EM-based estimation procedure and demonstrates applications in music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Theoretical strength: provides a novel time-domain reformulation that enables natural multi-resolution (tonal + transient) representations.  
- Extends factorization to arbitrary bases, which is an interesting and potentially impactful idea for audio processing.  
- Empirical weakness: evaluation is very limited—only one illustrative example and a single SNR measurement.  
- Comparison concerns: OMLSA comparison may be unfair due to different audio lengths.  
- Methodological concern: paper does not address complications introduced by overcomplete bases.  
- Results: music denoising shows significant artifacts compared to conventional methods.  
- Results: speech enhancement yields minimal improvement (6.6 dB vs. 6 dB for IS-NMF).  
- Presentation issue: EM derivation is technically sound but difficult to follow in detail.  
- Overall originality limited by heavy reliance on prior work, making claims less novel.  
- Significance plausible but unproven due to weak empirical support.  
- Recommendation: needs more comprehensive experiments and clearer exposition of the algorithm.
