- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel signal synthesis model bridging NMF and raw-signal generative models.
- Introduces two expectation-maximization (EM) algorithms for parameter estimation in LRTFS.
- Framework applies to arbitrary transforms and is directly linked to the time-domain signal, departing from STFT-based formulations.
- Theoretical development is solid, with clear links to sparse Bayesian learning and a well-described EM procedure.
- Multi-resolution representation application is a valuable and well-presented extension.
- Paper is well-written, easy to read, well-organized, and uses consistent notation; figures and tables are helpful.
- Experiments (music decomposition, speech enhancement) demonstrate potential but are limited in scope.
- Results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing and the supplemental material is unconvincing.
- Lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods the work builds on.
- Does not fully communicate the significance of the proposed method.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add more extensive experiments, comparisons, clearer significance, and stronger supplemental material.
- Overall rating: 7/10.
