- Introduces CBSM, a biophysically inspired conductance-based extension of the GLM for neural spike trains.  
- Reinterprets the standard GLM as a highly constrained conductance-based model and relaxes those constraints for greater realism.  
- Models independent excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs, allowing separate estimation of conductances.  
- Demonstrates improved spike-rate prediction and conductance estimation on both simulated and real neural data.  
- Captures stimulus-dependent gain control and distinct E/I conductances, outperforming traditional GLMs.  
- Provides clear writing, detailed derivations, and a well-organized presentation.  
- Bridges descriptive statistical models and biophysically interpretable neural models, offering a novel perspective.  
- Relies on numerical optimization methods that may not always converge to true parameters.  
- Uses a penalty term for regularization to avoid filter dominance, but this may be insufficient.  
- Recommends further exploration of optimization techniques and potential pitfalls.  
- Considered original and significant for advancing neural encoding models; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
