- Proposes a novel generative model that synthesizes time-domain signals from TF synthesis coefficients rather than using STFT magnitude analysis coefficients.  
- Correctly situates contribution relative to probabilistic latent factor models (e.g., GCM) and achieves a generative model for the raw signal.  
- Multi-resolution representation capability is a major strength, enabling adaptive TF resolution for transients vs. sustained components.  
- Presentation and mathematical formulation of the LRTFS model are clear and technically sound.  
- EM algorithm derivations are well-explained and the connection to sparse Bayesian learning is insightful.  
- Demonstrations on a jazz excerpt and speech enhancement are promising but limited in scope.  
- Evaluation lacks rigorous quantitative comparisons against state-of-the-art methods for music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Missing a crucial comparison to IS-NMF in the single-resolution case to assess convergence of W and H and solution similarity.  
- No assessment of whether the proposed EM algorithm matches IS-NMF’s speed and numerical stability.  
- Initial implementation suggests higher computational cost than existing methods, raising efficiency concerns.  
- Novelty and potential significance (especially multi-resolution) argue for acceptance.  
- Main weaknesses: limited evaluation, missing baseline comparison, and potential computational inefficiency.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing evaluation, baseline comparison, and efficiency concerns.
