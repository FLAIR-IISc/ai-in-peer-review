- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure.  
- Extends the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) by modeling the raw time-domain signal rather than only the STFT.  
- Introduces a flexible time-frequency factorization framework applicable to arbitrary transforms and directly linked to the time-domain signal.  
- Theoretical development is solid, interesting, and offers a fresh perspective departing from traditional STFT-based formulations.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and of generally high quality.  
- Method can accommodate multi-resolution representations, which traditional NMF cannot.  
- Demonstrated on two experiments: hybrid music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Shows promising performance, especially in speech enhancement where it outperforms other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experimental results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing and limited.  
- Supplemental material is not convincing or sufficiently supportive.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods, making significance hard to assess.  
- Estimation of the hyperparameter λ is poorly explained and appears treated as fixed in experiments.  
- Originality is above average but the paper does not fully communicate the significance and impact of the contributions.  
- Recommendation: good paper deserving acceptance with additional experiments, comparisons, and clearer hyperparameter treatment.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10, Overall 8/10.
