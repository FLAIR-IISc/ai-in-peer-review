- Proposes a novel Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) model linking time-domain synthesis and NMF-style latent factors.  
- LRTFS is a generative probabilistic latent factor model that departs from traditional STFT-based formulations.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Theoretical development is solid, interesting, and thoughtfully interpreted.  
- Supports multi-resolution representations, a notable advantage over traditional NMF.  
- Demonstrated on music decomposition and speech enhancement tasks.  
- Experimental results and overall evaluation are limited and somewhat disappointing.  
- Supplemental material is unconvincing.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods it extends or builds on.  
- MMLE implementation does not scale well to large dimensions, restricting experimental scope.  
- Originality is above average, but the paper does not fully communicate the model’s significance.  
- Technically sound overall.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add more results/comparisons, address MMLE scaling, and improve supplemental material.
