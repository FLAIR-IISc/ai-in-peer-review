- Proposes LRTFS: a low-rank time-frequency synthesis model that operates on raw signals by imposing low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients.  
- Allows multi-resolution representations to separate components with different temporal resolutions (e.g., tonal vs. transient).  
- Introduces two EM algorithms for parameter estimation and demonstrates on music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Builds directly on prior matrix factorization and GCM work (Lee & Seung, Smaragdis, Févotte) and relates to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Conceptually novel and flexible, enabling non-orthogonal bases and a time-domain generative formulation.  
- Potential advantages over STFT-domain methods are suggested but not empirically proven.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and limited in scope (single music example, limited speech tests).  
- Speech enhancement comparison to OMLSA is potentially unfair (different audio lengths) and subjective listening favored the baseline despite SNR claims.  
- Reported significant artifacts in music denoising compared to conventional methods.  
- Lacks broader testing across datasets, noise conditions, and robust objective metrics beyond SNR.  
- Fails to adequately discuss challenges of using overcomplete bases (computational complexity, spurious components, interpretability).  
- Non-convex optimization and risk of local optima are acknowledged but not thoroughly addressed experimentally.  
- Originality is limited by strong reliance on Févotte et al.; time-domain reformulation is incremental.  
- Significance remains unproven; the paper advances the field only marginally without stronger empirical support.  
- Recommendation: reject — authors should strengthen experiments, analyze overcomplete-base issues, and demonstrate clear practical advantages before resubmission.
