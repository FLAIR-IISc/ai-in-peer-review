- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to multi-resolution time-frequency decompositions using a generative model on the raw signal and overcomplete bases.  
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model extended to non-orthogonal bases.  
- Well-written with clear motivation, good connections to prior work, accessible notation, and structured EM algorithms.  
- Novelty is limited and feels incremental due to heavy reliance on Fevotte et al.’s GCM.  
- Paper does not adequately address challenges introduced by overcomplete bases (ambiguity, computational cost, noise sensitivity).  
- Discussion of connections to sparse Bayesian learning is incomplete; differences between tied and free variances are not analyzed.  
- Empirical validation is extremely thin: only one music decomposition and one speech SNR measure.  
- Evaluation setup is problematic (potentially unfair comparisons, differing audio lengths).  
- Authors report higher perceived speech quality for baseline OMLSA and notable artifacts in music denoising.  
- No statistical significance testing and insufficient dataset diversity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel overcomplete extension, useful time-domain GCM view, potential multi-resolution benefits, EM-based estimation appears theoretically sound.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experiments, lack of discussion on overcompleteness, limited originality, unproven practical significance, reported artifacts.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form; require expanded experiments, analysis of overcomplete-basis implications, and deeper SBL comparison.
