- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) that models time-domain signals via generative time-frequency synthesis coefficients rather than traditional analysis coefficients.  
- Main contribution: enables multi-resolution representations that conventional NMF cannot provide.  
- Introduces two EM-based estimation algorithms and demonstrates applications to music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Highly original shift from analysis to synthesis, providing a direct generative model of the raw signal and bridging gap with prior GCM work.  
- Multi-resolution extension allows different temporal resolutions per component (e.g., transient vs. tonal), which is compelling for audio.  
- Empirical results are promising; LRTFS outperforms IS-NMF by 0.6 dB in speech enhancement.  
- Lacks a direct comparison with IS-NMF in the single-resolution case to verify whether learned W and H match.  
- Does not compare algorithm speed and stability to IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates, important for practical adoption.  
- Technical quality and derivations are strong; presentation and clarity are excellent.  
- Significance for audio signal processing is high, but broader impact needs more comprehensive comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient benchmarking against IS-NMF and limited evaluation of the practical benefits of multi-resolution capability.  
- Recommendation: add the requested comparisons and stability/speed analyses; with those, the paper would be a strong acceptance candidate.
