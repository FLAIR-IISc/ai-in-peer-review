- Novel extension: LRTFS moves low-rank time-frequency decompositions from the spectral domain to the raw signal, enabling use of arbitrary/overcomplete time-frequency bases.  
- Generative model: The paper provides a probabilistic generative model for the raw signal, extending GCM and offering a stronger theoretical foundation.  
- Multi-resolution capability: LRTFS naturally supports multi-resolution decompositions, allowing components with different temporal resolutions.  
- Clear formulation: The mathematical presentation is relatively clear and accessible to researchers in matrix factorization and probabilistic modeling.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: Experiments are sparse (one music example, one SNR measure for speech), insufficient to convincingly demonstrate benefits over established methods.  
- Overcompleteness concerns: The paper highlights overcomplete bases but fails to address non-uniqueness, computational cost, or mitigation strategies.  
- Evaluation details problematic: Music decomposition lacks quantitative assessment; speech enhancement comparison to OMLSA may be unfair and shows perceived lower quality/artifacts for LRTFS.  
- Incremental originality: Heavy reliance on Févotte et al. (2013) limits perceived novelty despite the time-domain reformulation.  
- Algorithm complexity/scalability: Discussion of MMLE efficiency and scalability is weak, raising concerns about practical use on real-world signal lengths.  
- Relation to prior work: Connections to NMF, GCM, SBL, hybrid audio representations, and morphological component analysis are acknowledged but reduce novelty.  
- Argument for acceptance — theoretical value: The model is a logical, theoretically sound extension addressing NMF/GCM limitations.  
- Argument for acceptance — potential impact: Multi-resolution generative modeling could significantly benefit audio processing and source separation.  
- Argument against acceptance — weak experiments: Empirical support is inadequate to substantiate claims.  
- Argument against acceptance — unaddressed technical issues: Overcompleteness and scalability concerns are not satisfactorily resolved.  
- Argument against acceptance — limited novelty and comparisons: Dependence on prior work and lack of robust baselines weaken the contribution.  
- Overall assessment: Interesting and promising theoretical contribution but currently under-supported experimentally; recommendation is rejection pending expanded evaluation, addressing overcompleteness, and scalability discussion.
