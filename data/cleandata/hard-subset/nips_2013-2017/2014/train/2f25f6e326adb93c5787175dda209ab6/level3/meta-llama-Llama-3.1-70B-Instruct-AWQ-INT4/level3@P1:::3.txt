The paper "Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis" proposes a novel signal synthesis model that fills the gap between time-frequency synthesis and traditional nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) approaches. The model, called Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), is based on a generative probabilistic latent factor model of the time-domain signal, which is a departure from traditional STFT-based formulations.

The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, making the paper easy to read and understand. The theoretical development is solid and interesting, and the authors provide a thoughtful interpretation of their model.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to accommodate multi-resolution representations, which is a significant advantage over traditional NMF approaches. The authors demonstrate this capability through experiments on music decomposition and speech enhancement.

However, the results and evaluation of the paper are somewhat disappointing. The supplemental material is not convincing, and the paper lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods it builds on. The authors acknowledge that their current implementation of the maximum marginal likelihood estimation (MMLE) approach does not scale well with large dimensions, which limits the scope of their experiments.

Despite these limitations, the paper is still valuable due to its solid theoretical development and interesting ideas. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, making it easy to follow and understand.

In terms of originality, the paper scores above average. The authors propose a new model that departs from traditional STFT-based formulations and offers a thoughtful interpretation of their work. However, the paper does not fully communicate the significance of the proposed model, which is a drawback.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a solid theoretical development. However, it would benefit from more results and comparisons to existing methods to fully demonstrate the potential of the proposed model.
