- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a time-domain generative model with Gaussian synthesis coefficients whose variances are constrained by an NMF (W,H) factorization.  
- Contrasts prior STFT-analysis models (e.g., GCM) by moving low-rank structure to synthesis coefficients, enabling a bona fide waveform generative model.  
- Derives two estimation strategies: scalable MJLE-EM (alternating Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF) and computationally heavy MMLE-EM (type-II ML / SBL-like).  
- Introduces a straightforward multi-resolution extension allowing simultaneous use of multiple frame sizes (short vs long windows).  
- Conceptual novelty: unifies low-rank structure and time-domain synthesis, linking IS-NMF/GCM and SBL in a new way.  
- Derivations are solid and well motivated; MJLE is computationally practical and MMLE clarifies theoretical connections despite being costly.  
- Multi-resolution extension is elegant and easy to implement within the framework.  
- Writing is clear, organized, and connects well to prior work.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; gains over strong IS-NMF baseline are modest and supplemental material lacks convincing qualitative advantages.  
- Comparative breadth is insufficient: missing head-to-head comparisons with GCM/Wiener on STFT, PLCA variants, and multi-resolution sparse synthesis baselines (e.g., MCA).  
- MMLE is not scalable in current form and lacks approximate large-scale solutions, making comparisons to MJLE incomplete.  
- Practical choices (e.g., treating λ as an oracle-tuned hyperparameter) risk optimistic performance assessment and need clearer validation.  
- Modeling and EM derivations appear technically sound; the link between IS-NMF, Wiener filtering, and first EM iteration is insightful.  
- The log(|α|^2) term in MJLE is interesting; authors should discuss its effect (sparsity vs shrinkage) and sensitivity.  
- Algorithmic robustness: MJLE is scalable using Φ/Φ* only; MMLE would benefit from discussion of approximate solvers (CG, preconditioning, variational approximations, AMP).  
- Empirical methodology: reproducible in principle but lacks breadth, ablations, and standard metrics beyond SNR.  
- Clarity: paper is easy to follow but should explicitly address identifiability ambiguities (W/H scaling, Φ redundancy) and guidance on choosing β and λ.  
- Originality: above average — meaningful departure by placing low-rank structure in synthesis coefficients and enabling multi-resolution modeling.  
- Significance: potentially important for multi-resolution needs and time-domain priors, but current experiments do not convincingly demonstrate practical advantages.  
- Suggested improvements: add STFT-domain GCM comparisons, phase-aware NMF variants, multi-resolution sparse baselines, and perceptual/BSS_eval metrics.  
- Suggested ablations: single vs multi-resolution, analysis vs synthesis, K and window size effects, λ and β schedules, and impact of the log(|α|^2) term.  
- For MMLE scalability: provide approximate iterative solvers (matrix-vector products via Φ/Φ*, CG with preconditioning, Hutchinson trace estimates) for medium-scale comparisons.  
- Hyperparameter selection: choose λ via validation or unsupervised criteria, discuss sensitivity, and provide default heuristics instead of oracle tuning.  
- Add an appendix deriving equivalence between IS-NMF+Wiener and the first EM iteration; discuss identifiability and convergence diagnostics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, principled model bridging STFT NMF and time-domain generative modeling; scalable MJLE and multi-resolution extension; strong theoretical development and presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest empirical gains over strong baselines, missing critical comparisons and ablations, and incomplete MMLE contribution without scalable implementation.  
- Overall recommendation: lean weak accept contingent on strengthening experiments with broader comparisons, ablations, and more rigorous evaluation.
