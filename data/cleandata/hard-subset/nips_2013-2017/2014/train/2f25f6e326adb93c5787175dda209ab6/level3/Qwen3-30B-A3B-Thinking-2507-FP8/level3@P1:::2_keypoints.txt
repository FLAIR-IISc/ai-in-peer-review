- Introduces LRTFS: models the raw time-domain signal via synthesis with low-rank variance structure on time-frequency synthesis coefficients, extending the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM).  
- Enables natural multi-resolution representations so different signal components can have different time-frequency resolutions.  
- Main model defined by equations (2)-(4); estimation via MJLE and MMLE, with MJLE presented as computationally feasible for large-scale audio.  
- Strength: principled generative modeling of the raw signal rather than just the spectrogram, advancing over prior NMF approaches.  
- Strength: genuinely novel and useful multi-resolution capability for audio (e.g., handling transients vs. tonal components).  
- Strength: clear connection to sparse Bayesian learning, clarifying how the low-rank constraint differs from sparsity-inducing SBL.  
- Strength: practical utility demonstrated in experiments (music decomposition and speech enhancement), with speech enhancement showing 6.6 dB average SNR vs IS-NMF’s 6 dB.  
- Weakness: lacks a direct comparison to IS-NMF in the single-resolution case to confirm no performance degradation.  
- Weakness: does not analyze speed and stability relative to IS-NMF’s multiplicative updates or discuss differing convergence properties.  
- Weakness: multi-resolution advantage is not systematically quantified to isolate its contribution over single-resolution approaches.  
- Weakness: MMLE is dismissed as infeasible without justification or exploration of scalable/approximate inference options.  
- Overall quality: technically strong, clear, original, and significant in bridging time-frequency analysis and generative modeling.  
- Suggested additions: direct MJLE vs IS-NMF comparison, computational complexity and convergence analysis, systematic multi-resolution evaluation, and discussion of integrating multi-resolution into NMF frameworks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparative and computational-analysis concerns.
