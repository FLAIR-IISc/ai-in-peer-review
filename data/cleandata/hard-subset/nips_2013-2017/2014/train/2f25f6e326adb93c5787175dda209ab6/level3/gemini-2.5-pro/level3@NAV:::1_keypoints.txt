- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a time-domain reformulation of the Gaussian Composite Model enabling arbitrary/overcomplete synthesis bases and multi-resolution decomposition, with an EM-based parameter estimation.  
- Extends low-rank spectrogram factorization to time-domain synthesis coefficients, allowing simultaneous modeling of tonal and transient components.  
- Conceptually elegant and novel: imposing low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients over flexible overcomplete bases offers a fresh perspective.  
- Manuscript is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- EM derivation is presented but difficult to follow in detail.  
- Fails to address theoretical complications of overcomplete bases, notably non-uniqueness of decompositions.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient: only one illustrative example and a single SNR measure for speech enhancement.  
- Comparisons may be unfair (e.g., differing audio lengths), and baseline OMLSA produced subjectively higher-quality speech.  
- Music denoising example showed significant audible artifacts not found in conventional methods.  
- Overall originality is clear but significance is unproven; lacks convincing empirical evidence and does not demonstrate a clear advance over the state of the art.
