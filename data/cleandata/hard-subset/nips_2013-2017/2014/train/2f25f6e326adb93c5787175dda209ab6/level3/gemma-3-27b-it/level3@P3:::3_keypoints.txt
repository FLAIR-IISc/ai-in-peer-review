- Proposes a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) framework that models the raw signal instead of its STFT.  
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Models (GCMs) with low-rank factorization to constrain synthesis coefficient variances.  
- Theoretical development is solid, well-presented, and moves beyond traditional STFT-based formulations.  
- Clear understanding and discussion of related work, especially NMF and probabilistic latent factor models.  
- Originality is above average by extending GCMs to a generative raw-signal model and introducing multi-resolution capability.  
- Multi-resolution extension is a strong theoretical contribution enabling analyses not attainable with conventional NMF.  
- Paper fails to clearly articulate the broader significance and practical implications of modeling the raw signal.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: results are disappointing and supplemental material is unconvincing.  
- Music decomposition lacks quantitative metrics and provides only visual demonstration.  
- Speech enhancement shows modest overall SNR gain (6.6 dB) and minimal improvement over IS-NMF (0.6 dB).  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to established methods like SBL and standard NMF implementations.  
- Reliance on MJLE, limited experiments, and acknowledged scalability issues with MMLE raise practical applicability concerns.  
- Hyperparameter choices (notably λ) appear arbitrary without systematic exploration of their impact.  
- Recommendation: accept only if authors expand experiments with relevant baselines, analyze performance more thoroughly, and better articulate significance.
