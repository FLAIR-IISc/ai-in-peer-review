This paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, called Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS). The model is an extension of the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and addresses the limitation of GCM by providing a generative model of the raw signal itself, rather than just its Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT). The authors introduce a flexible time-frequency factorization framework that applies to arbitrary transforms and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and of good quality. The authors provide a thoughtful interpretation that departs from traditional STFT-based formulations, offering a new perspective on signal synthesis. The theoretical development is solid and interesting, making the paper valuable despite some limitations in the experimental section.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to accommodate multi-resolution representations, which is not possible in traditional Nonnegative Matrix Factorization (NMF) settings. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two experiments: hybrid decomposition of music and speech enhancement. The results show promising performance, especially in the speech enhancement task, where the proposed method outperforms other state-of-the-art methods.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing, and the supplemental material is not convincing. The authors do not provide empirical comparisons to existing methods, which makes it difficult to assess the significance of their contributions. Additionally, the estimation of the hyperparameter λ is not well-explained, and the authors seem to have treated it as a fixed parameter in their experiments.

In terms of originality, the paper scores above average. While the idea of low-rank time-frequency synthesis is not new, the authors' approach is novel and offers a fresh perspective on signal synthesis. However, the paper does not fully communicate the significance of the contributions, and the authors could have done a better job in highlighting the impact of their work.

Overall, this is a good paper that would benefit from more results and comparisons. The authors should consider adding more experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their method.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure.
* The authors provide a thoughtful interpretation that departs from traditional STFT-based formulations.
* The theoretical development is solid and interesting.
* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two experiments.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing.
* The supplemental material is not convincing.
* The authors do not provide empirical comparisons to existing methods.
* The estimation of the hyperparameter λ is not well-explained.
