- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a time-domain generative model imposing low-rank structure on synthesis coefficients with variances factorized as WH.  
- Shifts low-rank assumption from STFT analysis coefficients (GCM) to synthesis coefficients, enabling multi-frame/multi-resolution synthesis.  
- Derives two estimation procedures: a scalable EM for Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) and a non-scalable Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE) akin to SBL.  
- Demonstrates applications in tonal/transient music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement with modest SNR gains over IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Builds on prior work: IS-NMF/GCM, SBL/type-II ML, and multi-resolution frame decompositions; contribution is a synthesis-domain probabilistic formulation.  

Strengths:
- Conceptually clean extension from GCM to a time-domain low-rank generative model.  
- Practical and elegant EM (MJLE) algorithm that avoids large matrix inversions and uses standard analysis/synthesis operators.  
- Naturally handles multi-resolution (tonal/transient) decomposition within a single probabilistic framework.  
- Clear connections to Wiener filtering and SBL, well positioned in the literature.  

Weaknesses:
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only two studies, few metrics, no statistical analysis, and limited baselines.  
- Overcompleteness raises identifiability and leakage concerns that are not analyzed (no discussion of frame bounds/coherence).  
- Claimed practical advantages not convincingly demonstrated; audible artifacts remain and baselines can sound better despite lower SNR.  
- MMLE is not scalable; λ estimation is unstable and guidance on hyperparameters (β, λ) and stopping criteria is limited.  
- Some derivations are terse and the practical effect of the log(|α|^2) term is not fully explained.  

Quality:
- Modeling and MJLE appear technically sound but several derivation steps are hard to verify from the main text.  
- Lacks analysis of identifiability and component leakage for overcomplete/multi-resolution frames.  
- Experimental design may have confounds (window/K choices, pretraining, warm-restarts); hyperparameter sensitivity not explored.  

Clarity:
- Generally well written and organized with useful relation to GCM and SBL.  
- Notation and some derivations are dense; would benefit from pseudo-code, complexity analysis, and convergence discussion.  
- Claim that multi-resolution is impossible in NMF is overstated given prior multi-window NMF work.  

Originality:
- Moderate: combines known ingredients (IS-NMF/GCM, SBL, EM, multi-resolution frames) but the synthesis-domain low-rank formulation is novel and interesting.  

Significance:
- Potentially significant for audio analysis, source separation, and denoising, but current experiments do not establish clear practical advantage over strong baselines.  

Questions for authors:
- What conditions on Φ (frame bounds, redundancy, coherence) affect identifiability and leakage; any design guidance for Φa/Φb?  
- How sensitive are results to β, λ, K, and warm-restart schedule; can you provide ablation studies?  
- Can you quantify convergence behavior and complexity scaling with T, F, N, K?  
- Why is λ estimation unstable and can principled regularizations fix it?  

Suggested additional experiments/analyses:
- Broader benchmarks across noise types/SNRs and report SDR/SIR/SAR, STOI/PESQ with confidence intervals.  
- Ablations comparing single- vs multi-resolution, analysis- vs synthesis-domain, redundancy effects, and different K values.  
- Compare to multi-resolution NMF variants, MCA/structured sparsity hybrids, and supervised/semi-supervised NMF trained identically.  
- Perceptual evaluation (MUSHRA or blind listening tests) and publish code/audio.  
- Analyze identifiability/leakage with respect to frame properties and provide theoretical or empirical guidance.  
- Report hyperparameter sensitivity and runtime/convergence curves.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear, principled modeling contribution extending GCM to time domain and enabling multi-resolution synthesis.  
- Scalable EM algorithm built from simple, practical building blocks likely useful to practitioners.  
- Conceptually impactful and relevant to probabilistic modeling and audio communities.  

Arguments for rejection:
- Insufficient empirical evidence and marginal/uncorroborated improvements over strong baselines.  
- Missing theoretical analysis of issues from overcomplete multi-frame synthesis (identifiability, leakage, sensitivity).  
- MMLE remains impractical, λ instability unresolved, and practical guidance is limited.  

Overall recommendation:
- Weak reject: interesting and potentially impactful idea but empirical and analytical support is insufficient for acceptance.  

Reviewer confidence:
- Moderate.
