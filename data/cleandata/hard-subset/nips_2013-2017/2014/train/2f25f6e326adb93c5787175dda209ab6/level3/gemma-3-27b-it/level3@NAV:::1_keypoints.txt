- Proposes a novel time-frequency synthesis model extending low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary (potentially overcomplete) bases, enabling multi-resolution decompositions.  
- Builds on Fevotte et al.’s Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and derives a time-domain interpretation to support non-orthogonal bases.  
- Presents an EM-based estimation procedure and demonstrates applications to music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Moves beyond magnitude-spectrogram NMF to a generative model of the raw signal, addressing a relevant audio-processing problem.  
- Overcomplete bases and multi-resolution capabilities are potentially impactful for separating transients from tonal components.  
- Time-domain reinterpretation of GCM is a clever contribution that broadens basis selection possibilities.  
- Aligns with trends toward generative models and learned representations, building on established techniques like NMF and sparse coding.  
- Does not sufficiently explore implications of overcomplete bases; lacks discussion of challenges like ambiguity and computational cost.  
- EM algorithm derivation is difficult to follow, which hinders understanding and reproducibility.  
- Empirical evaluation is very thin: only a single illustrative result and one SNR measurement are reported.  
- Experimental setup concerns: audio lengths for different methods are unclear and may skew SNR comparisons.  
- Perceptual results conflict with reported SNR gains: baseline OMLSA provided higher perceived speech quality.  
- Music denoising outputs showed significant artifacts compared to conventional methods.  
- Heavy reliance on Fevotte et al. limits originality, though the time-domain reformulation adds value.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel overcomplete extension, fresh time-domain GCM perspective, and potential for multi-resolution representations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical validation, inadequate discussion of overcomplete-base challenges, unclear EM derivation, and questionable experimental/baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and promising idea but reject for now; request expanded experiments, clearer analysis of overcomplete bases, and improved derivation clarity to demonstrate practical importance.
