- Contribution: Proposes LRTFS, a generative time-domain extension of GCM that models signals via low-rank synthesis coefficients and supports multi-resolution representations.  
- Algorithms: Introduces two EM-based estimators (MJLE practical, MMLE discarded for scalability).  
- Multi-resolution claim: Emphasizes natural support for multi-resolution (tonal vs transient) unlike traditional STFT-NMF approaches.  
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous derivation connecting to Sparse Bayesian Learning and deriving Itakura-Saito NMF as a consequence of the model.  
- Clarity: Exposition is clear with well-motivated equations and a good distinction between analysis/synthesis perspectives.  
- Practicality: MJLE avoids costly matrix inversions and is computationally practical for audio applications.  
- Related work: Properly situates work relative to GCM and SBL and avoids overclaiming novelty.  
- Empirical weakness: Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate the practical benefit of multi-resolution modeling.  
- Music decomposition gap: Results are visually plausible but lack quantitative metrics (e.g., SNR, perceptual scores) to support claims.  
- Speech enhancement gap: Reported 6.6 dB SNR gain is only 0.6 dB better than IS-NMF baseline; comparisons to relevant multi-resolution NMF methods ([8,9,10]) are missing.  
- MMLE and hyperparameters: MMLE is dismissed for scalability without justification and the hyperparameter λ is fixed with no analysis.  
- Originality nuance: Novel model, but insufficient clarification of differences from prior time-domain NMF work (e.g., [15]).  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and well-motivated theoretically, but lacking empirical evidence of significance for practitioners.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject due to insufficient experimental validation and missing critical baselines; stronger empirical comparisons and ablation studies needed.
