- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS) that models raw time-domain signals instead of STFT magnitudes.  
- Builds on the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) but uses a synthesis approach generating signals from low-rank TF coefficients.  
- Key innovation: direct time-domain link and natural support for multi-resolution representations absent in standard NMF.  
- Strong theoretical development: sound mathematical formulation and well-motivated EM algorithms.  
- Clear exposition and good connection to sparse Bayesian learning.  
- Multi-resolution extension (Section 4) is elegant and a significant conceptual advance.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and does not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Speech enhancement shows only marginal improvement (6.6 dB vs. 6 dB) over IS-NMF baseline.  
- Lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art speech enhancement methods and to the methods it builds upon.  
- Supplemental material fails to address empirical shortcomings.  
- Overall technically sound and well-written but requires more comprehensive experiments and clearer articulation of tangible advantages.
