- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): model signal as synthesis atoms with synthesis coefficients whose variances have low-rank W·H structure (NMF-like).  
- Contrasts with prior analysis-based GCM/IS-NMF by moving low-rank modeling to the synthesis (time-domain) domain, yielding a true generative time-domain model.  
- Natural support for multi-resolution representations via a union of time-frequency frames, unlike traditional fixed-resolution spectrogram NMF.  
- Derives two EM-based estimation procedures: MJLE (practical, tractable) and MMLE (noted scalability issues).  
- Experimental demonstrations on audio: music decomposition (illustrative) and speech enhancement showing modest improvement over IS-NMF.  
- Technically sound: probabilistic model well-defined, insightful connection to Sparse Bayesian Learning, and correct EM/MJLE derivation (iterative Wiener + IS-NMF updates).  
- Authors transparently acknowledge MMLE scalability limitations.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; quantitative benefits of multi-resolution vs single-resolution LRTFS are unclear.  
- Lacks direct comparison between single-resolution and multi-resolution LRTFS to isolate source of gains.  
- Music decomposition results are qualitative without objective metrics, making assessment difficult.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; motivation and mathematical derivations are clear, with a lucid multi-resolution section.  
- Would benefit from a direct discussion/empirical comparison of learned factors (W, H) versus those from standard IS-NMF.  
- Highly original: novel idea of imposing low-rank NMF on synthesis coefficient variances, bridging NMF and synthesis models.  
- Significant potential impact: enables flexible multi-resolution low-rank decomposition and broader application to inverse problems.  
- Strengths summarized: novelty, practical multi-resolution capability, and solid technical formulation.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited experimental validation and missing direct comparisons with IS-NMF (both factor similarity and computational behavior).  
- Specific reviewer requests: (1) compare learned W,H to IS-NMF on same data; (2) discuss MJLE computational complexity and convergence vs IS-NMF; (3) report single-resolution LRTFS performance in speech enhancement.
