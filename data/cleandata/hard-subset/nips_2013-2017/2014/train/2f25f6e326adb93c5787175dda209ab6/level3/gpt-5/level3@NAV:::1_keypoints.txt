- Proposes LRTFS: a time-domain generative model synthesizing signals from time–frequency atoms with complex Gaussian coefficients whose variances have low-rank structure WH.  
- Reinterprets Févotte et al.’s GCM in the time domain by expanding STFT-domain convolutions into explicit impulse-response expressions, allowing arbitrary, possibly overcomplete/nonorthogonal bases and multiresolution decompositions.  
- Presents two estimation strategies: a computationally friendly EM for maximum joint likelihood (MJLE) and a less scalable marginal-likelihood (MMLE/SBL-style) variant.  
- Demonstrates applications to source decomposition and denoising.  
- Strength: time-domain reformulation unifies NMF/GCM low-rank variance modeling with synthesis in arbitrary frames.  
- Strength: allows overcomplete, multiresolution dictionaries directly in the generative model, potentially impactful for audio separation/denoising.  
- Strength: MJLE-EM is simple, avoids large matrix inversions, and connects conceptually to Wiener filtering and NMF.  
- Weakness: implications of overcomplete/nonorthogonal bases are underexplored (identifiability, scaling/rotation ambiguities, conditioning of ΦΦ*, effect on EM convergence and variance tying).  
- Weakness: MMLE is intractable at audio scale, so practical approach depends on MJLE with heuristic λ handling; consequences for bias/variance and artifacts are not analyzed.  
- Weakness: derivations are dense and hard to follow; key EM steps (e.g., β ≤ λ/δΦ, monotonicity, initialization sensitivity) need clearer exposition or appendix.  
- Evaluation is too thin: only one music example and a small speech-denoising study, reported mostly with SNR.  
- Experimental concerns: potential unfairness (different audio lengths across methods), perceptual quality discrepancies (OMLSA sounding better despite lower SNR), and audible artifacts for music denoising.  
- Missing evaluations: standard objective metrics (PESQ/STOI/SDR), controlled ablations (single- vs multi-resolution, overcomplete vs orthonormal, effect of λ), and stronger baselines (e.g., STFT-GCM+Wiener, SBL/MCA).  
- Quality: technically promising but empirically incomplete; clarity generally good but derivations dense.  
- Originality: moderate—builds on GCM/SBL but offers a fresh time-domain, overcomplete extension.  
- Significance: plausible but currently unproven due to limited empirical support.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant, general framework enabling multiresolution synthesis; practical EM algorithm and insightful links to Wiener/NMF.  
- Cons against acceptance: weak/unfair evaluation, unanalyzed overcomplete-basis complications, non-scalable MMLE and ad hoc λ handling, and hard-to-follow derivations.  
- Overall: interesting idea with promise but insufficient evidence; reviewer leans toward rejection pending stronger, more rigorous evaluation and clearer treatment of overcompleteness.
