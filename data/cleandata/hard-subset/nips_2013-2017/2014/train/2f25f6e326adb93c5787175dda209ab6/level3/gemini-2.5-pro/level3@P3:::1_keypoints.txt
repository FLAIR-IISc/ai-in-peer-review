- Proposes LRTFS: a synthesis-model extension of GCM that imposes low-rank variance on time-domain synthesis coefficients for arbitrary/overcomplete frames.  
- Key contribution is reformulating spectrogram factorization as a time-domain synthesis model, enabling multi-resolution decompositions.  
- Derives an EM algorithm for maximum joint likelihood estimation; derivation is dense but assumed correct.  
- Theoretical extension to nonorthogonal bases is sound and interesting.  
- Experimental evaluation is severely limited (one music example, one speech task).  
- Reported quantitative gains are marginal (speech SNR 6.6 dB vs IS-NMF 6.0 dB); music results lack baselines.  
- Paper does not address non-uniqueness/ill-posedness issues of overcomplete bases or how the model regularizes them.  
- Generally well-written and motivated, with clear relation to prior work.  
- EM derivation is brief and hard to reproduce; needs more detail.  
- Originality is limited by heavy reliance on GCM, though the synthesis reformulation is novel.  
- Connects algorithmic steps to iterative shrinkage/Wiener filtering insightfully.  
- Potentially significant for audio tasks (source separation, denoising) via multi-resolution low-rank decomposition.  
- Practical significance is unproven due to weak empirical evidence and subjective quality concerns.  
- Pros: elegant time-domain extension, multi-resolution capability, clear exposition.  
- Cons: insufficient experiments, missing theoretical treatment of overcomplete frames, marginal improvements, and questionable perceptual quality.
