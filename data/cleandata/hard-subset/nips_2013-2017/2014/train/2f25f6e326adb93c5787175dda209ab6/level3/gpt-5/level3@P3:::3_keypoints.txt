- Summary: Proposes LRTFS, a generative model imposing low-rank (NMF-like) structure on synthesis-coefficient variances tied to the waveform, with MJLE (EM) and a principled but currently unscalable MMLE, plus multi-resolution extension.  
- Relation to prior work: Conceptual shift from STFT-domain GCM/IS-NMF (analysis-space) to time-domain synthesis variance modeling; relates to MCA and SBL but replaces sparsity with low-rank variance tying.

Strengths
- Models the waveform directly via a flexible synthesis operator applicable to arbitrary and multi-resolution frames.  
- Solid theoretical development: scalable EM-based MJLE and principled MMLE derivation with links to SBL and Wiener filtering.  
- Insightful interpretation: variance tying via WH over synthesis coefficients is an interesting modeling choice.  
- Clear writing and organization, with coherent progression from model to inference to applications.

Weaknesses
- Experimental section is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate advantages proportional to the conceptual contribution.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to core baselines (GCM/IS-NMF with equivalent frames, multi-resolution NMF, MCA, modern speech enhancement).  
- MMLE is not demonstrated due to scalability issues.  
- The practical significance of time-domain vs STFT-domain modeling is not substantiated empirically.

Quality (technical assessment)
- Technical soundness: model and MJLE derivations are well-grounded and explained.  
- Assumptions: standard Gaussian residuals and synthesis priors, independence across TF points; potential mismatch with audio non-Gaussianity and phase correlations.  
- Algorithms: MJLE is computationally attractive; MMLE scalability limitations weaken empirical validation.  
- Evaluation: illustrative but anecdotal hybrid decomposition; speech enhancement shows modest SNR gains but lacks broader metrics, error bars, and fair hyperparameter protocols.

Clarity
- Manuscript is well organized, notation consistent, and relations between analysis/synthesis clear.  
- Suggested clarifications: role of β in EM, initialization/convergence behavior, assumptions on frames, and runtime/complexity reporting.

Originality
- Above-average novelty: shifting low-rank modeling to synthesis variances and multi-resolution extension is novel and conceptually clean.  
- Insightful link to SBL via variance tying departs meaningfully from variance-free SBL.

Significance
- Potentially significant for audio and multi-resolution tasks, but current limited experiments fail to demonstrate robust practical gains across datasets and tasks.

Detailed suggestions for improvement
- Add strong baselines: matched-frame GCM/IS-NMF, multi-resolution NMF, MCA-like sparse synthesis, and modern enhancement methods.  
- Provide ablations: single vs multi-resolution, λ and β effects, implicit sparsity role, dictionary size, supervised vs unsupervised W.  
- Report standard audio metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, STOI, PESQ), error bars, more noise types, and larger test sets.  
- Use fair hyperparameter selection (cross-validation) instead of post-hoc tuning for best SNR.  
- Discuss identifiability, low-rank vs sparsity interactions, overfitting, local minima, and provide initialization heuristics.  
- Include computational cost and runtime comparisons and memory scaling with frame redundancy.  
- If possible, include a small MMLE experiment to illustrate trade-offs.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong theoretical contribution with a flexible, general modeling framework beyond STFT NMF.  
- Clear writing and useful connections to SBL and Wiener filtering.  
- Elegant multi-resolution synthesis modeling.

Cons for acceptance
- Thin empirical validation with modest improvements over baselines and insufficient comparisons.  
- No demonstrated MMLE and unresolved scalability concerns.  
- Insufficient evidence that time-domain modeling yields practical benefits over STFT approaches.

Overall evaluation
- Technically solid and well-written with novel ideas, but empirical weaknesses leave practical impact unclear; borderline/weak accept conditional on stronger experiments.
