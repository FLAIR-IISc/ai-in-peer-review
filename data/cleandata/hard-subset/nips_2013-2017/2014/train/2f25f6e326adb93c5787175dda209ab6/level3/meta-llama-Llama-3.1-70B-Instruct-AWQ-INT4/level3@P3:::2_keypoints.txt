- Proposes LRTFS: a generative model of the raw time-domain signal using low-rank time-frequency synthesis coefficients enabling multi-resolution representations.
- Claims this synthesis-based approach provides multi-resolution capabilities that traditional NMF cannot.
- Defines the model as a sum of time-frequency atoms with low-rank latent structure and provides formal equations.
- Introduces two EM estimation algorithms: MJLE (computationally friendly, avoids matrix inversion) and MMLE (more robust but computationally expensive).
- Presents a multi-resolution extension to capture different temporal resolutions for transients versus sustained components.
- Experiment 1: decomposes a jazz excerpt into tonal, transient, and residual layers to demonstrate layer separation.
- Experiment 2: enhances speech corrupted by texture noise, showing improvements over traditional NMF and some speech enhancement methods.
- Strengths: novel approach, clear presentation, solid theoretical support, and effective experiments.
- Weakness: no comparison with other state-of-the-art audio methods such as ICA or sparse representations.
- Weakness: MMLE is not scalable to large dimensions, limiting practical applicability.
- Quality: technically sound and the claims are supported by analysis and experiments.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow with clear algorithm descriptions.
- Originality: novel combination of NMF-like low-rank structure with time-frequency synthesis and a new multi-resolution extension.
- Significance: potential impact in audio processing (music decomposition, speech enhancement) and possible applicability to other domains.
- Recommendation: add comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and develop a scalable MMLE algorithm.
- Suggestion: explore applying LRTFS to other signal processing areas (image or biomedical signal processing).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel generative raw-signal model, multi-resolution contribution, and supportive experimental results.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons to other methods and scalability concerns with MMLE.
