- Proposal: Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a time-domain generative model putting a low-rank Gaussian prior on TF synthesis coefficients α instead of analysis coefficients.
- Algorithm: Derives an EM algorithm for maximum joint likelihood estimation (MJLE) using the Figueiredo–Nowak auxiliary variable trick to avoid large matrix inversions.
- MMLE/SBL: Discusses a type-II ML (MMLE) route related to SBL but notes it is currently not scalable/practical.
- Multi-resolution: Proposes a natural multi-resolution extension by summing over multiple TF frames for joint short/long window decomposition.
- Experiments: Demonstrates feasibility on hybrid music decomposition and semi-supervised speech enhancement, but empirical evaluation is limited and gains are modest.
- Relation to prior work: Well-situated relative to IS-NMF/GCM, SBL, and multi-resolution/morphological decomposition literature; synthesis-domain modeling is a clear, original departure.
- Theoretical quality: MJLE derivation is sound, with clear links to Wiener filtering and an algorithm relying only on Φ/Φ* multiplies.
- Practical limitations: λ treated as a hyperparameter; no systematic analysis of convergence, stability, runtime, or scalability of MMLE.
- Clarity/actionable details: Paper is organized but should specify β choice relative to λ/δΦ, report per-iteration complexity (FLOPs), and provide implementation details (windowing, overlap-add, initializations, restarts).
- Originality: High — modeling synthesis coefficients to enable multi-resolution low-rank structure is novel and appealing.
- Significance: Potentially high impact for audio ML and beyond, though reported improvements are modest.
- Revision request — single-resolution comparison: Compare LRTFS W/H to IS-NMF on |Y|^2 with tight/orthonormal STFT and small λ, quantify alignments and reconstruction quality.
- Revision request — speed/stability: Report wall-clock times, iterations to convergence, failure rates vs IS-NMF, and sensitivity to β, initialization, and K.
- Revision request — multi-resolution evaluation: Compare to best single-window IS-NMF over window sweeps and to multi-window/structured NMF or MCA baselines with SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ/STOI, and error bars over seeds.
- Revision request — reproducibility: Release code/scripts and detail datasets and hyperparameter selection.
- Arguments for acceptance: Clean generative time-domain framework, practical EM algorithm, and a natural multi-resolution extension with clear links to established models.
- Arguments against acceptance: Evaluation is limited/illustrative with modest gains, missing key single-resolution and runtime/stability comparisons, and MMLE is currently unscalable.
