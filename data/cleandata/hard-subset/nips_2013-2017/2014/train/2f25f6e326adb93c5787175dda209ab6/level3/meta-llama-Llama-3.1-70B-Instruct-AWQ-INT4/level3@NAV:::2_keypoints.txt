- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a novel extension of NMF that models raw signals for multi-resolution representations.
- Introduces two estimation methods: Maximum Joint Likelihood Estimation (MJLE) via EM and Maximum Marginal Likelihood Estimation (MMLE) that marginalizes synthesis coefficients.
- MJLE uses an EM algorithm to iteratively minimize the negative log-likelihood of the observed signal.
- MMLE optimizes the marginal likelihood but requires computing a large matrix inverse, limiting scalability to large datasets.
- Demonstrates LRTFS on music decomposition, successfully separating tonal and transient layers plus a residual.
- Shows speech enhancement results with an average SNR improvement of 6.6 dB.
- Strength: Novel model enabling multi-resolution signal representations beyond magnitude-only NMF.
- Strength: Provides two complementary estimation approaches based on different optimization criteria.
- Strength: Well-designed experiments that validate effectiveness on music decomposition and speech enhancement.
- Weakness: MMLE is not scalable due to expensive matrix inverse computation.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art methods in the field.
- Weakness: Needs more theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence guarantees, stability).
- Quality/Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, with consistent notation and clear explanations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request experimental comparisons to baselines and additional theoretical analysis.
