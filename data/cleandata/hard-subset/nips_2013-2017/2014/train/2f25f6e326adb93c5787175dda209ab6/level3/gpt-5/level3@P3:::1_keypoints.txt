- Proposes LRTFS: time-domain generative model synthesizing signals from TF atoms with Gaussian coefficients whose variances follow low-rank WH, extending GCM/IS-NMF to waveforms.  
- Allows arbitrary (possibly overcomplete/nonorthogonal) synthesis dictionaries and supports multiresolution by stacking frames.  
- Derives two estimators: scalable MJLE via an EM scheme alternating Wiener-like shrinkage and IS-NMF on |α|^2, and an MMLE/SBL-like approach that is not yet scalable.  
- Builds on prior GCM interpretation of IS-NMF and SBL/type-II ML literature; relates to morphological multiresolution methods and NMF-based Wiener filtering.  
- Theory is interesting and plausible; MJLE EM scheme is computationally attractive, but some derivation steps (e.g., Eq.11, role of β) are unclear.  
- Allowing overcomplete, nonorthogonal frames is a key strength but raises unresolved issues: dependencies among α, identifiability (scale/rotation ambiguities), and effects of frame tightness.  
- MJLE treats α priors as diagonal; the paper lacks discussion of posterior covariance and implications of Φ redundancy.  
- MMLE is currently computationally impractical at audio scale; MJLE scales but lacks convergence analysis beyond monotone decrease.  
- Choice and sensitivity of β and the handling/treatment of λ as a hyperparameter need more justification.  
- Empirical evaluation is thin: only one music example and one SNR-style speech comparison, insufficient to support broad claims.  
- Some experimental comparisons may be unfair (different time supports/windows or signal lengths); LRTFS shows audible artifacts vs. cleaner OMLSA in listening.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized, but key derivations are terse and implementation choices (β, initialization, frame normalization, λ scheduling) are under-specified.  
- Originality is moderate: main novelty is applying low-rank variance modeling to synthesis coefficients in time domain with arbitrary frames, conceptually adjacent to existing work.  
- Potential significance if validated: could unify NMF-style low-rank models with multiresolution/overcomplete decompositions for audio tasks.  
- Main weaknesses: weak empirical validation, unaddressed complications from overcomplete bases, and impractical MMLE contribution.  
- Suggest experiments analyzing redundancy/coherence effects (vary redundancy, tight vs non-tight frames) and clarifying identifiability/scaling ambiguities.  
- Recommend either providing scalable MMLE approximations or dropping MMLE claims and focusing on MJLE improvements.  
- Request more algorithmic details: β selection/impact, runtime/memory, α/W/H initialization, sensitivity to K and λ scheduling.  
- Strongly expand evaluation: standardized metrics (SDR/SIR/SAR, PESQ/STOI), more datasets/noise types, matched conditions, listening tests, and comparisons to IS-NMF Wiener, MCA, conv./temporal NMF, and strong denoising baselines (OMLSA/MMSE-LSA).  
- Include ablations: single vs multi-resolution, orthonormal vs overcomplete Φ, and effect of low-rank vs sparsity (log term).  
- Clarify the time-domain interpretation and explicitly connect to GCM’s convolution view and conditions where LRTFS reduces to GCM.  
- Arguments for acceptance: appealing bridge from STFT-NMF to time-domain synthesis, scalable MJLE EM, multiresolution extension, and a fresh perspective likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical evidence, unaddressed overcomplete-base complications, unclear derivations, and incremental novelty.  
- Recommendation: lean reject in current form; encourage revision with clearer derivations, stronger and fair evaluations, and substantive treatment of redundancy/coherence effects.
