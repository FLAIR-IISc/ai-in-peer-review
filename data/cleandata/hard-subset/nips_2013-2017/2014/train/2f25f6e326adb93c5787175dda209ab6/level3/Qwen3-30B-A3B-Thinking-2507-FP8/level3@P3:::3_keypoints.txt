- Proposes LRTFS, a novel low-rank time-domain synthesis model bridging NMF and generative time-domain signal models.  
- Builds on Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and shifts the generative model from STFT to time domain, enabling multi-resolution representations.  
- Establishes a clear theoretical connection between LRTFS and sparse Bayesian learning (SBL), with low-rank variance constraints on synthesis coefficients.  
- Develops two estimation algorithms: computationally efficient MJLE and theoretically appealing MMLE.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically organized.  
- Theoretical development is solid and provides new insights into time-domain synthesis vs frequency-domain factorization.  
- Multi-resolution capability is a compelling strength, demonstrated in music decomposition and speech enhancement examples.  
- Empirical results are limited and unconvincing, showing only marginal gains (e.g., 6.6 dB vs 6 dB baseline).  
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to recent or state-of-the-art audio processing methods.  
- Supplemental material fails to sufficiently bolster empirical claims.  
- Contribution is original and above average theoretically but significance is weak due to poor experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: reject for NIPS due to insufficient empirical evidence; better suited for a signal processing venue.
