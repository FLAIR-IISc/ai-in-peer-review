- Proposes LRTFS: low-rank structure imposed on time-frequency synthesis coefficients instead of analysis spectrograms.
- LRTFS serves as a generative model for the raw time-domain signal.
- Enables natural multi-resolution representations by combining different time-frequency frames.
- Derives an EM algorithm for parameter estimation and demonstrates audio applications.
- Strength: original and significant conceptual shift relative to IS-NMF/Gaussian Composite Model.
- Strength: multi-resolution extension tackles fixed-window STFT limitations for transient and tonal components.
- Strength: paper is clearly written and derivations are easy to follow.
- Weakness: experimental validation is limited and largely illustrative.
- Weakness: lacks quantitative evaluation showing multi-resolution gains over single-resolution on benchmark tasks (e.g., source separation/enhancement).
- Weakness: missing comparison to IS-NMF—unclear whether LRTFS factors W and H converge to IS-NMF solutions on the power spectrogram.
- Weakness: practical aspects of the EM algorithm (convergence speed and stability) versus standard multiplicative updates are not discussed.
- Overall: elegant and significant idea, but needs more rigorous empirical support to establish practical advantages.
