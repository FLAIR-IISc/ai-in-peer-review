- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), extending low-rank spectrogram factorization to arbitrary bases for multiresolution decompositions.
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of the Gaussian Composite Model, expanding implicit convolutions into explicit impulse-response expressions and allowing non-orthogonal bases.
- Presents an EM-based local-optimum estimation procedure for the model.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical derivations and explanations.
- Applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases is an interesting and potentially impactful idea.
- Reviewer is unsure of the full implications and requests more discussion of limitations and challenges.
- Concerns that complications from overcomplete bases are not addressed or clarified.
- Evaluation is very thin: only one illustrative result and a single SNR measurement.
- Comparisons to other methods are potentially unfair due to differing audio lengths.
- Baseline (OMLSA) shows higher speech quality, and the proposed method introduces notable artifacts in music denoising.
- Originality is limited because the work builds heavily on Fevotte et al., despite the fresh time-domain reformulation.
- Significance is plausible but unproven because empirical evidence is weak and results do not convincingly show practical importance.
- Conclusion: paper needs more development, discussion of limitations, and much more comprehensive evaluation to support claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel potentially impactful idea, clear math, and good background/related work presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient evaluation, unfair comparisons, limited originality, and unproven significance.
