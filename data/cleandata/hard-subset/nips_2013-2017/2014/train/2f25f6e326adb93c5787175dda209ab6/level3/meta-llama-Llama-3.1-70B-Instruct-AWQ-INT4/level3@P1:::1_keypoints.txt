- Proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time–frequency structure, extending the GCM of the STFT to raw signals and enabling non-orthogonal bases and multiresolution decompositions.  
- Derives a time-domain interpretation of the GCM and presents an EM algorithm for estimation.  
- Connects to prior work on NMF and sparse Bayesian learning, especially Fevotte et al.  
- Strength: novel application of matrix factorization to overcomplete bases with potential for source separation and denoising.  
- Strength: time-domain reformulation provides a fresh perspective.  
- Strength: EM-based estimation procedure is appropriate for the problem.  
- Weakness: evaluation is very thin—only one illustrative result and a single SNR measurement.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other methods, leaving implications unclear.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Fevotte et al. limits originality.  
- Weakness: empirical demonstration is weak and does not convincingly show practical importance.  
- Quality score 7/10: well-written but evaluation and empirical support are limited.  
- Clarity score 8/10: generally clear, though EM derivation is hard to follow in detail.  
- Originality score 6/10: some novelty (time-domain reformulation) but largely builds on prior work.  
- Significance score 7/10: interesting idea but insufficient empirical evidence.  
- Recommendation: revise and resubmit with more comprehensive evaluation, clearer EM derivation, stronger comparisons, and clarified implications.
