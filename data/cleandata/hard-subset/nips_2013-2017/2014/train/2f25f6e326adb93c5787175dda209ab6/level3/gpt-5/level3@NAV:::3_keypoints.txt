- Proposes Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS): a generative time-domain model with Gaussian synthesis coefficients whose low-rank variance is parametrized by WH.  
- Moves low-rank modeling from analysis/magnitude (STFT/IS-NMF/GCM) to synthesis coefficients and supports arbitrary time-frequency frames.  
- Connects to Sparse Bayesian Learning (SBL) via marginalization (MMLE) or joint estimation (MJLE).  
- Naturally supports multi-resolution representations by combining multiple frames.  
- Derives two EM algorithms: MJLE (computationally practical, uses analysis/synthesis ops) and MMLE (principled but currently impractical).  
- Applications shown for music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Theoretical development, probabilistic construction, and EM derivations are solid and well connected to Wiener filtering and IS-NMF.  
- MJLE algorithm is elegant and computationally reasonable.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: small datasets, limited metrics, modest gains (e.g., 6.6 vs 6.0 dB), and unconvincing supplementary audio.  
- Missing ablations for key hyperparameters (λ scheduling, β, dictionary sizes) and no convergence/runtime complexity analysis.  
- Comparisons omit important baselines (GCM/STFT-domain Wiener filtering with matched dictionaries, SBL variants).  
- Clarity is good: well written with clear notation and links to prior work, but “when to use” guidance is underdeveloped.  
- Originality is above average: meaningful conceptual shift and useful multi-resolution extension.  
- Significance is potential but not demonstrated; current evidence doesn't show clear superiority over STFT-domain methods or modern pipelines.  
- Suggested improvements: add rigorous matched comparisons, SBL baselines, ablations, complexity/convergence plots, more objective metrics on larger datasets, highlight cases of clear advantage, and release code.  
- For acceptance: strong theory, flexible frame-agnostic multi-resolution perspective, and clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: unconvincing empirical validation, missing key comparisons/ablations, and MMLE scalability concerns.
