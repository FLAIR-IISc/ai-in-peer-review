This paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, referred to as Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS). The model is an extension of the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) and addresses the limitation of GCM by providing a generative model of the raw signal itself, rather than just its Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT). The authors provide a thoughtful interpretation that departs from STFT-based formulations and offer a flexible time/frequency factorization framework that applies to arbitrary transforms and is directly linked to the time-domain signal.

The paper is well-written and easy to read, with a clear organization and adequate information for the expert reader to reproduce the results. The authors provide a solid theoretical development, including two expectation-maximization algorithms for estimation in the new model. The results of the experiments with audio applications, namely music decomposition and speech enhancement, are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

However, the paper has some limitations. The results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing, and the supplemental material is not convincing. The paper lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods it builds on, which makes it difficult to assess the significance of the proposed approach. Additionally, the estimation of the hyperparameter λ is not well handled, and the authors treat it as a fixed parameter instead of estimating it with the other parameters.

Overall, this is a good paper that would benefit from more results and comparisons. The originality of the paper is above average, but the authors could do a better job of communicating the significance of their work. The paper is a valuable contribution to the field, and the theoretical development is solid and interesting.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure.
* The authors provide a thoughtful interpretation that departs from STFT-based formulations.
* The paper is well-written and easy to read, with a clear organization and adequate information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.
* The theoretical development is solid and interesting, and the authors provide two expectation-maximization algorithms for estimation in the new model.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The results and evaluation are somewhat disappointing, and the supplemental material is not convincing.
* The paper lacks empirical comparisons to existing methods it builds on, which makes it difficult to assess the significance of the proposed approach.
* The estimation of the hyperparameter λ is not well handled, and the authors treat it as a fixed parameter instead of estimating it with the other parameters.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the limitations mentioned above, provide more results and comparisons, and improve the estimation of the hyperparameter λ.
