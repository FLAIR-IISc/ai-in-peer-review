**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper proposes a novel signal synthesis model with low-rank time-frequency structure, extending the Gaussian Composite Model (GCM) of the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) to the raw signal itself. The authors derive a time-domain interpretation of the GCM, enabling the use of non-orthogonal bases and multiresolution decompositions. The model is related to previous work on nonnegative matrix factorization (NMF) and sparse Bayesian learning (SBL). The authors also present an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for estimation in the new model.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting idea of applying matrix factorization to overcomplete bases, which could have significant implications for source separation and denoising.
* The time-domain reformulation of the GCM offers a fresh perspective on the problem.
* The EM-based estimation procedure is well-suited for the problem.

Weaknesses:

* The evaluation is very thin, with only one illustrative result and one SNR measurement, which is insufficient to support the claims made in the paper.
* The comparison with other methods is limited, and the reviewer is unsure of the full implications of the proposed approach.
* The paper relies heavily on previous work by Fevotte et al., which limits its originality.
