- Proposes LRTFS: a generative model placing a low-rank variance WH on synthesis coefficients α of the time-domain signal, contrasting with IS-NMF/GCM which model analysis STFT coefficients.  
- Develops two estimation strategies: scalable MJLE (EM with Wiener-like α update and IS-NMF for W,H) and MMLE (SBL-style but computationally heavy).  
- Key novelty: moving low-rank probabilistic modeling from analysis to synthesis enables a principled multi-resolution TF decomposition.  
- MJLE derivation is sound and computationally practical (avoids large inversions; uses Φ and Φ*), with an IS divergence penalty and a log(|α|^2) sparsity term from joint likelihood.  
- MMLE/SBL route is elegant conceptually but currently impractical at audio scale; authors acknowledge this.  
- Experiments show appealing tonal/transient decompositions and speech enhancement SNR gains over single-resolution IS-NMF and OMLSA.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small dataset, single metric (SNR), hand-tuned λ, and no runtime or convergence analysis.  
- Minor correctness/clarity issues: Eq.(29)-(30) may miss zero-mean notation; unclear β selection (β ≤ λ/δΦ) and practical λ scheduling.  
- Writing is generally clear; relations to GCM and SBL are well explained and multi-resolution motivation is strong.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: unify indexing (m vs (f,n)), define tensor/matrix shapes early, and restate Φ assumptions (frame tightness/redundancy).  
- Request to include a compact algorithm box for MJLE (single- and multi-resolution) with per-iteration complexity.  
- Ask for explanation of initialization, permutation/scale handling for W,H and identifiability issues.  
- Request discussion and guidelines on practical choice and sensitivity of β and λ; note joint λ estimation performed poorly.  
- Originality: novel synthesis-side low-rank modeling and multi-resolution TF framing; elegant EM that reduces to IS-NMF on |α|^2; interesting structured-SBL connection.  
- Potential significance: could shift waveform-level factorization practices and enable principled multi-resolution decompositions across domains (audio, biomedical, radar).  
- Strengths: principled generative waveform model, multi-resolution capability, scalable EM reusing IS-NMF solvers and TF operators, strong qualitative results.  
- Weaknesses: limited quantitative evaluation/ablations, no runtime or convergence-speed comparison to IS-NMF, no single-resolution sanity check showing alignment with IS-NMF factors, MMLE not scalable here.  
- Requested experiments: single-resolution comparison to IS-NMF using the same frame, check W/H alignment (Hungarian matching), reconstruction errors across redundancies and λ→0.  
- Requested experiments: algorithm speed/stability—plot objective vs time/iterations for MJLE vs multiplicative IS-NMF, report FLOPs, iterations to converge, and monotonicity.  
- Requested experiments: multi-resolution ablations comparing single-resolution LRTFS, IS-NMF, and multi-resolution LRTFS using additional metrics (BSS Eval, PESQ/STOI) and analyze when multi-resolution helps.  
- Requested experiments: hyperparameter study—principled λ schedule (e.g., annealing), sensitivity to β, whether λ is per-sample or global.  
- Reproducibility: report runtimes on tasks and release code/audio to support adoption.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel and elegant synthesis-side factorization, sound derivations, practical EM algorithm, and promising potential impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: experimental section insufficient to establish parity/superiority in single-resolution settings, missing runtime/stability comparisons, and under-specified practical aspects (λ, β, initialization).  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and appealing work; lean accept if authors add single-resolution IS-NMF comparisons, speed/stability evidence, and stronger evaluations/ablations to substantiate multi-resolution benefits.
