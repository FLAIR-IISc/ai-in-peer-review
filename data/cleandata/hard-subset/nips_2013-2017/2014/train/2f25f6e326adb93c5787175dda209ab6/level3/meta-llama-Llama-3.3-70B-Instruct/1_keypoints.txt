- Extends low-rank spectrogram-factorization to generalize latent factors over arbitrary bases for sound decomposition.
- Introduces a time-domain expression of the Gaussian Composite Model that handles different impulse responses, including non-orthogonal bases, with an EM analysis procedure.
- Estimation procedure is impressive but difficult to follow in detail.
- Paper is well-explained and motivated, but implications and potential complications are not clearly articulated.
- Evaluation is superficial, relying on a single illustrative result and one SNR improvement measurement, making conclusions hard to draw.
- Examples exhibit significant artifacts, undermining demonstrated effectiveness.
- Originality is limited due to heavy reliance on Fevotte et al., though the time-domain interpretation provides a new perspective.
- Potential significance is high because it enables matrix-factorization techniques on overcomplete bases, but this is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Pro-acceptance: novel extension, potential to improve source separation/denoising, and offers a new time-domain viewpoint.
- Con-acceptance: unclear implications, thin evaluation, limited originality, and problematic artifacts in examples.
- Overall: interesting extension with promising potential, but clarity, evaluation, and originality concerns require further work to establish impact.
