- Introduces Low-Rank Time-Frequency Synthesis (LRTFS), a time-domain extension of the Gaussian Composite Model enabling low-rank synthesis coefficients and arbitrary (possibly overcomplete) bases.  
- Claims three contributions: (1) time-domain reformulation of GCM for nonorthogonal bases, (2) EM-based parameter estimation (MJLE and MMLE), and (3) a multi-resolution extension for different temporal resolutions.  
- Technical derivations are sound and the MJLE algorithm appears correct, though the EM derivation is hard to follow in places; MMLE is acknowledged as computationally expensive and deferred.  
- Empirical evaluation is severely lacking: only one illustrative figure and a single SNR result (6.6 dB) for speech enhancement across 10 tests, insufficient to support practical claims.  
- Comparison with OMLSA appears unfair and inconsistent (different audio lengths used), and perceived higher baseline quality contradicts reported SNR gains.  
- Music denoising results exhibit significant artifacts compared to conventional methods, raising concerns about practical utility.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear mathematical formulation and relation to prior work, but the EM derivation and some implementation details (e.g., Gabor frame handling, hermitian symmetry) need clarification.  
- The core idea heavily builds on Fevotte et al.'s GCM (NIPS 2009); the time-domain view is interesting but the novelty is incremental rather than transformative.  
- The multi-resolution extension is a valuable application but not a fundamentally new approach.  
- Potential significance is plausible for multi-resolution audio processing, but weak empirical support prevents assessing real-world improvement over existing methods.  
- The paper does not address potential complications of using overcomplete bases (computational cost, overfitting), despite acknowledging they could introduce issues.  
- Strengths: theoretically sound extension of GCM to time domain, clear math, and useful multi-resolution capability.  
- Weaknesses: inadequate evaluation, biased baseline comparisons, hard-to-follow EM derivation, unaddressed overcomplete-base complications, and limited originality.  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient empirical validation; suggest substantially stronger experiments (multiple metrics, diverse examples, fair comparisons) before reconsideration.
