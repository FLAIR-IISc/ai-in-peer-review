- Introduces a novel low-rank time-frequency synthesis (LRTFS) model extending GCMs to directly model raw signals.  
- Low-rank structure on atom variances enables multi-resolution time-frequency representations beyond STFT-based methods.  
- Presents two EM algorithms for parameter estimation and demonstrates applications to music decomposition and speech enhancement.  
- Builds logically on NMF and GCM literature with solid theoretical development and clear exposition.  
- Multi-resolution analysis is a valuable extension addressing standard NMF limitations.  
- Experimental results are disappointing: SNR improvements in speech enhancement are modest and comparable to existing methods (e.g., OMLSA).  
- Supplementary listening examples are not wholly convincing given the modest reported SNR gains.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to other methods that build directly on the GCM framework, a key missing baseline.  
- MMLE approach limited by computational complexity, reducing practical evaluation scope.  
- Significance is not fully communicated; filling the “ultimate gap” does not translate to clear performance gains.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—strengthen experimental validation, include rigorous GCM-based comparisons, and better articulate practical significance.
