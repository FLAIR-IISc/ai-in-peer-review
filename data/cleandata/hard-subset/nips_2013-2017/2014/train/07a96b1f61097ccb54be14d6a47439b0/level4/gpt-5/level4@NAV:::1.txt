This paper studies the challenge of reliably estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when the data are inherently coherent. A common theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods is that performance deteriorates as coherence increases. This work, however, circumvents that barrier in an elegant way. Given that such clustered structure is prevalent in real-world datasets, addressing this issue is clearly highly significant. The proposed method is both theoretically well-grounded and empirically shown to perform strongly.
