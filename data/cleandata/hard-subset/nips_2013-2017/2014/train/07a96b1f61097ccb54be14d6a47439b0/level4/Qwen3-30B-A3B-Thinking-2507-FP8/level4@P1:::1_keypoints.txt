- Problem: RPCA performance degrades with increasing data coherence from clustering; µ2 rises with more clusters and hurts recovery.
- Key theoretical contribution: LRR with a low-rank dictionary A satisfying U0 ⊂ UA achieves exact recovery without relying on µ2.
- Practical contribution: An unsupervised algorithm that builds the required dictionary from RPCA output for real-world use.
- Improved bounds: The method requires less restrictive recovery conditions (O(n2/log n1) vs O(n2/(log n1)^2)).
- µ2-phenomenon demonstrated: Theory and experiments show clustering increases coherence and degrades RPCA.
- Experiments: Comprehensive tests on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences validate the method’s superiority.
- Empirical improvement: About 40% relative reduction in clustering error on motion sequences.
- Theoretical quality: Proofs are rigorous, sound, and clearly presented.
- Experimental quality: Well-designed experiments with controlled parameters and real-world validation.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written with precise notation, clear figures, and implementable algorithm description.
- Originality: Novel theoretical insights on the role of dictionary properties in LRR and on coherence reflecting intrinsic data structure.
- Significance: Highly relevant to real-world applications (computer vision, motion analysis) involving clustered data.
- Insight: Provides a clearer physical interpretation of coherence parameters as tied to data structure.
- Minor suggestion: Expand discussion on connections to subspace clustering/segmentation and direct applications of the dictionary learning approach.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — important problem solved with rigorous theory, practical algorithm, and clear empirical gains; no substantive objections.
