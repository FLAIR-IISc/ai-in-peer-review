This paper tackles the problem of robustly estimating an underlying low-dimensional subspace from contaminated data when the observations are intrinsically coherent (e.g., clustered). A key limitation of many prior RPCA methods is that their performance deteriorates as data coherence increases; this work circumvents that bottleneck with an elegant approach. Because clustered structure is common in real-world datasets, addressing this regime is both practically important and theoretically meaningful. The proposed method is supported by solid analysis and strong empirical results.

One question for the authors: Can the approach be extended to subspace clustering/segmentation, where multiple subspaces are present? Any insights, conditions, or preliminary evidence on this application would be valuable.

Overall, this paper resolves an important challenge in robust PCA. The technical quality and presentation are high, and I recommend acceptance.
