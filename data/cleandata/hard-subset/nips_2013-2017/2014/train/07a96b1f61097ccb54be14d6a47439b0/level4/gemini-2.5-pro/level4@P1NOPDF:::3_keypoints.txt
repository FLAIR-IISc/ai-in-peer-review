- Proposes improving RPCA by learning a dictionary A within the LRR framework to exploit underlying clustering structure.
- Central insight: the incoherence parameter μ can be linked to data clustering and is not an unavoidable bottleneck for recovery.
- Imposing conditions on A lets LRR exploit clustering, yielding stronger theoretical recovery guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank + sparse decomposition.
- Method is novel and significant, building on subspace-clustering literature while making less explicit assumptions about clustering.
- Theoretical analysis is supported by simulations and real-data experiments; authors claim applicability to other domains (e.g., text).
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance at NIPS.
- Request to clarify ambiguous wording around Lines 80–82 and ensure correct sufficiency statements for “perfect recovery.”
- Note of a missing word after “perfect” at Line 88 and general suggestion for a careful proofread for clarity and precision.
- Highlights connection to Wang & Xu’s work: high coherence can help subspace clustering; key distinction is local low-rank clusters vs. global low-rank assumption here.
- Suggests comparing to a two-stage baseline: noisy subspace clustering followed by PCA on each cluster for empirical context.
- Argues the paper should more explicitly emphasize its practical contribution as a high-performing robust subspace-clustering algorithm and lists three main contributions (formalizing clustering structure, exploiting it via dictionary-based LRR, and state-of-the-art robust clustering).
