- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a dictionary A to improve RPCA recovery guarantees by exploiting underlying clustering structure that induces incoherence μ.  
- Shows that under conditions on A, LRR can mitigate dependence on μ, giving stronger theoretical guarantees for exact low-rank/sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.  
- Method is novel and significant, related to union-of-subspaces work but framed more generally (less explicit clustering assumption).  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data corroborate the theoretical claims; authors note the structure likely appears in other applications (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Clarity issue (Lines 80–82): ambiguous phrasing (“our data”) and misleading implication that structural assumptions alone suffice for “perfect recovery”; other conditions should be acknowledged.  
- Incomplete phrase (Line 88): “…is no longer a method of perfect” needs clarification.  
- General readability: manuscript needs careful proofreading to fix similar minor precision/clarity problems.  
- Prior-work connection: relate and contrast to Wang & Xu (Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering) which shows high coherence can help clustering; position this work as synthesizing global low-rank and local union-of-subspaces structures.  
- Experimental suggestion: add a baseline that first performs noisy subspace clustering (e.g., ℓ1-penalized) then PCA per cluster to empirically compare performance.  
- Emphasize Section 4.2 contribution: a new robust subspace-clustering method that substantially outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155; highlight three main contributions (formalizing clustering-origin of coherence, exploiting it via dictionary-based LRR for stronger guarantees, and a new robust subspace-clustering algorithm).
