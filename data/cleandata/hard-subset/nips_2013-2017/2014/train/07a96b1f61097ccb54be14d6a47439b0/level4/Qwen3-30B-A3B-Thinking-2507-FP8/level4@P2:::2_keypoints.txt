- Problem: RPCA's performance degrades on structured/clustered (high-coherence) data.
- Proposal: Use LRR with a carefully designed low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence effects.
- Theoretical contribution: Prove LRR with a low-rank dictionary can avoid coherence parameters and achieve exact recovery.
- Recovery conditions: Guarantees hold when the dictionary is low-rank and satisfies specific subspace alignment conditions.
- Algorithm: Unsupervised dictionary construction by applying RPCA to data and normalizing the resulting low-rank approximation.
- Empirical validation: Synthetic and motion experiments show consistent improvements over RPCA (47% wider success area; 40% relative improvement in motion segmentation error).
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical results for both noiseless and noisy settings with comprehensive experiments.
- Weakness: The dictionary-learning algorithm is heuristic and lacks theoretical guarantees.
- Clarity issues: Explanations of coherence and its relation to clustering are insufficient and the theory is dense and inaccessible to non-specialists.
- Originality: Novel linkage of coherence parameters to clustering structure and demonstration that low-rank dictionaries specifically resolve RPCA's limitation.
- Significance: Addresses a practical RPCA limitation with meaningful empirical gains and useful theoretical insights for robust matrix recovery.
- Recommended improvements: Clarify coherence intuitively, add examples/visualizations, expand discussion on generalizing/improving dictionary learning, and better connect theory to practice.
