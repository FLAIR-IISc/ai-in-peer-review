- Paper studies low-rank recovery for clustered/coherent data and shows RPCA’s guarantees degrade with increasing coherence (µ2); proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary to address this.

- Main theoretical claim: if A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA (and columns of A are unit-norm), LRR recovers low-rank and sparse components without dependence on µ2/µ3 (Theorem 1, noiseless).

- Noisy setting: LRR yields bounded recovery errors proportional to noise level ε and tolerates up to ~35% gross corruptions (Theorem 2).

- Algorithmic contribution: an unsupervised two-stage pipeline—run RPCA, build a rank-truncated column-normalized dictionary from the estimate, then run LRR.

- Empirical results: on synthetic data the success region increases by 47% vs RPCA; on motion data SSC error drops from 22.81% (corrupted) → 9.50% (RPCA) → 5.74% (LRR+dictionary).

- Builds on prior work (RPCA, LRR) and relates to robust subspace clustering (e.g., SSC) and motion segmentation.

- Theoretical analysis and proofs appear sound; rank bounds O(n2/log n1) and corruption tolerances are competitive.

- Practicality concern: the RPCA-derived dictionary heuristic lacks formal guarantees that U0 ⊂ UA, though it helps empirically.

- Computational claims are plausible but runtimes and memory usage are not systematically reported.

- Clarity is weak: heavy notation, insufficient intuition for µ1/µ2/µ3 and the µ2-phenomenon, and poorly motivated assumptions.

- The identification and analysis of the µ2-phenomenon and the decoupling from µ2 via low-rank dictionaries are novel and insightful.

- Significance: advances robust recovery for clustered data and offers a pragmatic recipe with empirical gains, but broader validation is needed for practical uptake.

- Suggestion: add geometric intuition and simple examples for coherence parameters and why µ2 grows with cluster count.

- Suggestion: clarify necessity/practicality of U0 ⊂ UA and analyze robustness to approximate subspace mismatch.

- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters (λ, ε), the rank-thresholding heuristic, and column normalization; include ablation studies.

- Suggestion: report runtimes and memory for RPCA, LRR, and the full pipeline; quantify when the method is faster/slower than RPCA.

- Suggestion: expand empirical comparisons to other robust matrix recovery baselines (e.g., Outlier Pursuit) and better handle missing data (avoid filling with ±1).

- Suggestion: clarify cases when LRR reduces to RPCA (e.g., A orthogonal or A = I).

- Suggestion: thoroughly edit for language, notation, and typos; move key intuition from appendix to main text.

- Pros: addresses an important RPCA failure mode, offers solid theory avoiding µ2/µ3 under clear conditions, and provides a simple algorithm with notable empirical improvement.

- Cons: limited accessibility and heavy notation, strong/heuristic dictionary assumptions without theory, limited experimental breadth, and some questionable experimental choices.

- Overall: publishable and meaningful contributions but requires substantial rewriting, stronger empirical evaluation, and clearer practical guidance; reviewer leans toward weak accept contingent on major revisions.
