This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating the low-dimensional subspace of contaminated observations when the observations are inherently coherent. Performance degradation with increasing data coherence is a standard theoretical bottleneck of previous RPCA methods. This paper, however, circumvents this problem in a clever manner. Considering that such cluster structure is rather common in realistic data, solving this issue is certainly significantly meaningful.

The proposed method is both theoretically sound and well demonstrated to perform well in practice. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem, including the definition of coherence parameters and their impact on RPCA. They also propose a novel approach, Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit, which is able to avoid the coherence parameters that increase with the underlying cluster number.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The experiments are also well-designed, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

I have a curious comment on the submission: Can the proposed method also be applied for solving subspace clustering/segmentation problems? Could the authors provide some comments on this point?

In terms of quality, the paper is very good and should be accepted. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of robust PCA, and their work is likely to have a substantial impact on the community.

Strengths:
- The paper addresses a significant problem in robust PCA.
- The proposed method is both theoretically sound and well demonstrated to perform well in practice.
- The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and a clear explanation of their method.
- The experiments are well-designed, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the proposed method and other subspace clustering/segmentation methods.
