- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA, showing the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure and be partially removed under conditions to yield stronger exact/noisy recovery guarantees.  
- Novel and significant contribution connected to union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering literature (e.g., Wang & Xu, Soltanolkotabi) while making a more implicit/general clustering assumption.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments support the theoretical analysis; the identified structure likely appears in many applications (e.g., text).  
- Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption and yields significant improvements over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.  
- Three key contributions: (1) description of clustering-induced high coherence, (2) demonstrating exploitation of that structure via a dictionary in LRR, (3) partially addressing sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.  
- Strengths: novel/significant idea, solid theoretical analysis, and experimental validation pointing to a new direction for corrupted subspace clustering.  
- Weaknesses: several unclear sentences and phrasing errors; the paper needs careful proofreading and clearer, more precise claims.  
- Specific writing issues to fix: clarify “our data” and the conditions for “perfect recovery” (Lines 80–82) and the unclear sentence at Line 88.  
- Suggestion to compare against a pipeline of noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace, even though no current provable guarantee exists for that approach.  
- Note: Wang & Xu observed higher coherence can help subspace clustering; the paper’s structure blends low-rank and union-of-subspace assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS, contingent on addressing clarity and providing more detailed comparisons to strengthen impact.
