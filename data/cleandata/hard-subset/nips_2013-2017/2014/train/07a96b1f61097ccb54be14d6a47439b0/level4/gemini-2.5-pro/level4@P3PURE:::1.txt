This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations, particularly when the data is inherently coherent. The degradation of performance with increasing data coherence is a well-known theoretical bottleneck for previous Robust PCA (RPCA) methods. This paper circumvents this issue in a clever manner. Given that such cluster structures are common in realistic data, solving this problem is a significant contribution. The proposed method is theoretically sound and is demonstrated to perform well in practice.

I have one question for the authors: Can the proposed method also be applied to solve subspace clustering or segmentation problems? Some comments on this point would be welcome.

In summary, this paper solves an important problem in the field of robust PCA. The quality of the paper is very high, and it should be accepted.
