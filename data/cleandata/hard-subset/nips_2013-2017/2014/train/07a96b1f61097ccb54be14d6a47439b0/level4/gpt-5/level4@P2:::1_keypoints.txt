- Paper tackles robust low-rank matrix recovery in the coherent (clustered) regime where RPCA degrades.  
- Authors identify clustering as a cause of growing coherence parameters (µ2, µ3) that harm RPCA.  
- Proposal: use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a suitably chosen low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space U0.  
- Main theorem: if A is low-rank and U0 ⊆ column space(A), LRR achieves exact (or near-exact under noise) recovery without dependence on µ2.  
- Practical algorithm: initialize with RPCA, build a column-normalized low-rank dictionary from that estimate, then solve LRR.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and corrupted motion sequences show clear improvements over RPCA, especially as coherence increases.  
- Relates to prior work by explaining RPCA’s incoherence assumptions and extending LRR analysis to general dictionaries (not just A = X).  
- Connects to GPCA by highlighting robustness issues in clustered subspace settings.  
- Strength: strong technical contribution with clear noiseless/noisy recovery theorems and looser rank bounds than RPCA.  
- Strength: conceptual insight linking coherence growth to intrinsic clustering rather than proof artifacts.  
- Strength: simple, effective algorithm that leverages RPCA as an initializer with modest extra cost.  
- Strength: empirical validation via phase diagrams and motion segmentation experiments.  
- Weakness: guarantees rely on U0 ⊆ UA and low-rank A; reliance on RPCA initialization may fail when RPCA is already degraded.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to singular-value cutoff, column normalization, and choice of λ (1/√n1) under different noise/corruption regimes is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other robust PCA variants (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, factorization-based methods).  
- Weakness: experiments are limited in scale and datasets; more controlled studies varying cluster count would help.  
- Weakness: potential issues when µ1 is large (clustered rows) are not resolved or discussed in depth.  
- Weakness: need clearer discussion on necessity/practical verification of the projection condition PUA(U0)=U0 and tolerance to violations.  
- Quality: technically sound with coherent algorithmic design, but some implementation details (ε, stopping criteria, complexity) could be elaborated.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized; suggest restating µ2-clustering intuition and separating assumptions from empirical practices.  
- Originality: novel idea to remove µ2 dependence via a low-rank dictionary; convincingly links coherence to clustering.  
- Significance: practically important advance likely to influence robust subspace recovery and dictionary-based methods.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong theory, simple effective algorithm, clear insights and empirical gains.  
- Cons against acceptance: dependence on RPCA initialization in hard regimes, limited experimental breadth, and remaining practical/theoretical sensitivities.  
- Question to authors: can the method be used directly for subspace clustering (e.g., using Z* or AZ* as affinity), and does it outperform LRRx or SSC under high coherence?  
- Recommendation: paper is sound and impactful; the reviewer recommends acceptance.
