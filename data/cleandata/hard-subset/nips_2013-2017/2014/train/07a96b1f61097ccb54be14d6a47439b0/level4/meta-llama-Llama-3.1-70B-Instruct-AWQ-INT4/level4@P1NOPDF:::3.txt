The paper proposes to use Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve the effectiveness of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA). In particular, the authors show that the incoherence parameter μ, often thought of as a bottleneck in the recovery guarantee in RPCA, can in fact correspond to additional clustering structure in the data. Under certain conditions on the dictionary A, the authors demonstrate that one can exploit such structure using LRR and partially remove the dependence on μ, thereby obtaining a theoretically stronger guarantee in both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.

The method is novel and significant to the field. It is closely related to the union-of-subspace structure assumed in previous subspace clustering papers, such as "Wang and Xu: Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC", and "Soltanolkotabi and Candes: A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers" and "Soltanolkotabi et al: Robust subspace clustering". However, these papers are less explicit about the assumption on "clustering", which is reasonable and likely more general. The simulation and real data experiments verify the theoretical analysis, and it is likely that the new structure can be found in many other real applications as well (such as text data, as remarked in the paper).

In summary, the paper contains a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing, and it should be accepted by NIPS.

That said, I do have a number of discussions and stylistic suggestions, which are summarized in the detailed comments below.

1. Lines 80-82: Need some re-writing to make the description clearer. What is meant by "our data"? Additionally, even if the problem captures all the structures in the problem, there is still another condition that needs to be met to qualify for "perfect recovery".

2. Line 88: The sentence is unclear. What is meant by "is no longer a method of perfect"?

3. Please proof-read the paper more carefully for problems like the above two. Be specific and precise about what is being claimed and what is being referred to in the text. In general, please work on clarity of the language in the paper.

4. Wang and Xu also noted in their paper "Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering" that a higher coherence parameter can actually be beneficial for subspace clustering (unlike in RPCA and matrix completion). The key difference from the current paper is that the data need not be low-rank overall, only that every cluster must be low-rank. The structure exploited in this paper can be thought of as a combination of low-rank structure and union-of-subspace cluster structure.

5. It would be interesting to compare the proposed algorithm against first solving noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty and then performing PCA for each subspace. Although no provable guarantee exists for the latter approach to date.

6. Section 4.2 essentially proposes a new method for solving the subspace clustering problem with sparse corruption in the data. The method leads to significant improvements over the standard RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset, which I believe is a hidden contribution of this paper. The authors should highlight this point. This paper should be accepted because it contains at least three important contributions to the field: 1) a description of a clustering structure that leads to high coherence, 2) an example of how one can implicitly exploit such structure by using a dictionary in LRR, and 3) a partial solution to the sparse corruptions problem in subspace clustering.
