- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to strengthen RPCA by leveraging underlying clustering structure.  
- Key insight: the incoherence parameter mu can reflect clustering; under conditions on A the method partially removes dependence on mu, yielding stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank+sparse recovery.  
- Novel and significant; closely related to union-of-subspaces/subspace-clustering literature but assumes a more general structural model.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments support the theory; approach may generalize to other domains (e.g., text).  
- Overall recommendation: accept at NIPS.
