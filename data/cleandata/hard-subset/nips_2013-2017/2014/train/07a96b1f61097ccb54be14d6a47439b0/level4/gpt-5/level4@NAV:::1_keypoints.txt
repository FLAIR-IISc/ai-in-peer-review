- Studies reliable estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when data are highly coherent.  
- Identifies that prior RPCA methods degrade with increasing coherence and presents a solution that circumvents this theoretical limitation.  
- Addresses a significant practical issue since clustered/coherent structure is common in real-world datasets.  
- Proposed method is theoretically well-grounded.  
- Empirically demonstrates strong performance.  
- Minor question: can the method be used for subspace clustering/segmentation—authors should comment.  
- Overall: tackles an important problem, high-quality work, recommendation to accept.
