- Tackles robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations that exhibit inherent coherence.  
- Identifies a standard theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods: performance deteriorates as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes a method that circumvents the coherence-related limitation.  
- Notes high practical significance because cluster structures (which cause coherence) are common in real-world data.  
- Method is theoretically sound.  
- Empirically demonstrated to perform well in practice.  
- Minor comment: asks whether the method can be applied to subspace clustering or segmentation and requests insights.  
- Overall recommendation: paper quality is excellent and worthy of acceptance.
