- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA recovery by exploiting underlying clustering (union-of-subspaces) structure.  
- Shows that the incoherence parameter μ can reflect useful clustering structure and that a suitable dictionary A can reduce μ’s harmful effect, with theoretical guarantees and experiments.

- Novel link between high coherence and latent clustering; reframes μ as exploitable structure rather than only a bottleneck.  
- Connects RPCA to subspace clustering literature and appears more general/practical than some prior clustering-specific methods.  
- Empirical results (including Hopkins155) support theory and suggest broader applicability (e.g., text data).

- Formalizes conditions under which A enables LRR to mitigate high μ in RPCA-style recovery.  
- Provides recovery guarantees for both noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Proposes a robust subspace-clustering method in Section 4.2 that outperforms RPCA+SSC in experiments.

- Paper makes a substantial contribution to robust low-rank modeling and subspace clustering; recommended for acceptance.

- Clarify wording and logical prerequisites (e.g., what “our data” means, and explicit conditions needed for perfect recovery).  
- Fix incomplete/ambiguous sentences (e.g., line 88) and improve overall clarity and rigor.

- Explicitly state how dictionary A is constructed/learned in practice, whether it uses the same data, and if side information is required.  
- Clearly list precise assumptions on A (span, block structure, rank, incoherence with sparse part) and assess realism.  
- Analyze sensitivity to misspecification of A and include ablations comparing learned A vs. SVD-based, random, or identity dictionaries.

- More explicitly position results relative to SSC/LSR methods, prior LRR variants, and state when guarantees strictly improve over RPCA.  
- Compare to pipelines like noisy subspace clustering + per-cluster PCA as a practical baseline.

- Add stronger baseline comparisons and ablation studies (RPCA+SSC, other robust subspace-clustering methods), including at least one non-motion dataset (e.g., text or faces).  
- Make the Section 4.2 subspace-clustering method an explicit contribution in the introduction/abstract and state any (even informal) theoretical claims for it.

- Separate and summarize conditions for exact vs. stable recovery, highlighting dependence on rank, sparsity, sample size, and μ and where improvements occur over RPCA.  
- Provide guidance on hyperparameter choices, sensitivity analyses, and discuss computational complexity and scalability relative to RPCA and SSC.

- Proofread for grammar and incomplete sentences; define notation on first use and avoid ambiguous pronouns.  
- Ensure figures/tables are self-contained, report standard deviations, and include parameter settings for reproducibility.
