- Proposes LRR with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA by exploiting clustering structure in the incoherence parameter μ, yielding stronger theoretical guarantees that reduce dependence on μ for exact and noisy recovery.

- Demonstrates that μ can reflect underlying clustering and that, under conditions on A, LRR can partially remove μ-dependence in recovery guarantees.

- Novel and significant, related to union-of-subspace/subspace clustering literature (Wang & Xu; Soltanolkotabi & Candès) but less explicit about clustering assumptions and potentially more general.

- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical analysis; authors note the structure may appear in many applications (e.g., text).

- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.

- Writing needs improvement: rewrite lines 80–82 for clarity, clarify “our data,” and state additional conditions required for perfect recovery.

- Specific phrasing issue: rephrase line 88 (“method of perfection”) and proofread for precision in claims and references.

- Note that Wang & Xu showed higher coherence can help subspace clustering; key distinction is global low-rank vs per-cluster low-rank—this paper’s structure mixes both.

- Suggest comparing against a two-step baseline: noisy sparse subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA on each subspace, though no provable guarantee currently exists for that pipeline.

- Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption and shows significant gains over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155; this hidden contribution should be highlighted.

- Paper’s three main contributions: (1) identification of clustering-induced high coherence, (2) showing dictionary-based LRR can exploit that structure, (3) partial solution to sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
