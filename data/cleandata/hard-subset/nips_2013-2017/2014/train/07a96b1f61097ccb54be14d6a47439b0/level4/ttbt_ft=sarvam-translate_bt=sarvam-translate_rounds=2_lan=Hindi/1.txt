This paper addresses the problem of accurately estimating the low-dimensional presence of corrupted observations when the observations are naturally consistent. Performance deteriorates with increasing consistency of the data, which is a standard theoretical limitation of previous PCA methods. However, this paper cleverly avoids this problem. Given that such cluster structure is quite common in real data, solving this issue is certainly important. The proposed method is theoretically sound and has also been well demonstrated in practice.

I have a curious comment on the submission:
Could the proposed method also be used to solve unsupervised clustering/ partitioning problems? Could the author provide some comments on this point?

This paper addresses a significant problem in robust PCA tasks. The quality of the paper is very good and should be accepted.
