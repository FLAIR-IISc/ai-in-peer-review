This paper tackles the challenge of accurately estimating the low-dimensional subspace of contaminated data when observations exhibit inherent coherence. Notably, previous RPCA methods typically struggle with performance degradation as data coherence increases, a well-documented theoretical limitation. However, the authors skillfully bypass this issue. Given the prevalence of cluster structures in real-world data, addressing this problem is highly significant. The proposed approach is theoretically rigorous and effectively demonstrated through practical experiments.

I have a minor inquiry regarding the submission:
Is it possible to adapt the proposed method for subspace clustering or segmentation tasks? I would appreciate the authors' insights on this aspect.

This paper addresses a critical issue in robust PCA research. The paper's quality is excellent, and it is deserving of acceptance.
