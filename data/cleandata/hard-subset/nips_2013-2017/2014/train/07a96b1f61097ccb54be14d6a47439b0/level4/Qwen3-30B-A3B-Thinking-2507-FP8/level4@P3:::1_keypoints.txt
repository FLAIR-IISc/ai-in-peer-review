- Paper identifies RPCA's limitation on coherent data, especially when clustering increases coherence parameters (µ2, µ3).  
- Shows clustering structures in real data cause RPCA performance degradation as coherence rises with cluster count.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's coherence sensitivity.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1 & 2) that LRR with a low-rank dictionary is immune to the problematic coherence parameters.  
- Offers a practical unsupervised algorithm (Algorithm 1) that constructs the dictionary from RPCA output.  
- Empirical results: 47% wider success area on synthetic matrices compared to RPCA.  
- Empirical results: ~40% relative improvement in clustering error on real motion data.  
- Clarifies the physical meaning of coherence parameters, linking them to intrinsic clustering structure rather than mere theoretical assumptions.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical rigor, clear exposition, consistent notation, and comprehensive experiments.  
- Strengths: practical algorithm is simple to implement and consistently outperforms RPCA in tests.  
- Weakness: paper could better connect the dictionary-learning approach to subspace clustering applications as a preprocessing integration.  
- Minor weakness: noisy-case theoretical implications (Theorem 2) could be further explored under realistic noise models.  
- Evaluation across criteria: high quality, clear, original (addresses overlooked RPCA limitation), and significant for real-world tasks.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—addresses an important problem with solid theory and convincing empirical validation.
