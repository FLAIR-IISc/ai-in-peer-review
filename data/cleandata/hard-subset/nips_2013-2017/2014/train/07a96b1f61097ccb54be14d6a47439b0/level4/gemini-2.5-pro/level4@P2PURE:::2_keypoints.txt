- Proposes combining robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Central premise: standard robust PCA guarantees are insufficient for structured (e.g., clustered) data; explicit dictionaries can yield stronger recovery bounds.  
- Theoretical contribution: two main theorems giving conditions for successful reconstruction assuming a known dictionary.  
- Practical method: a two-step heuristic—apply robust PCA to corrupted data, then learn a dictionary from the recovered low-rank component; mainly empirically justified.  
- Quality: technically sound for the known-dictionary case; the dictionary-learning step lacks theoretical justification.  
- Clarity: exposition is not accessible to a general ML audience; central claims and impact need clearer, more precise explanation.  
- The concept of “coherence” is poorly explained and pushed to the appendix, hindering interpretation.  
- The claim that dictionaries can sidestep prior-method limitations is hinted at but insufficiently developed.  
- Manuscript requires thorough proofreading to address language and grammar issues.  
- Originality: a novel synthesis of recent methods, supported by original theorems and strong empirical results.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art with rigorous theoretical results and empirically improved performance despite heuristic learning.  
- Post-rebuttal: paper is publishable but requires a major revision to improve accessibility beyond a specialist audience.
