- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA, showing the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure and that, under conditions on A, dependence on μ can be partially removed for stronger exact and noisy recovery guarantees.

- Method is novel and significant, connecting to union-of-subspace models in prior subspace clustering work.

- Related works include Wang & Xu (Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering), “LRR meets SSC”, and Soltanolkotabi & Candes papers, though those are less explicit about the clustering assumption used here.

- Simulations and real-data experiments support the theoretical analysis, and the proposed clustering structure likely appears in many real applications (e.g., text).

- Recommendation: the paper makes a substantial contribution to compressed sensing and should be accepted by NIPS.

- Clarity issue: Lines 80–82 need rewriting — “our data” is vague and the text should clarify that an additional condition is required for “perfect recovery”.

- Clarity issue: Line 88 is unclear (“is no longer a method of perfect”) and needs rephrasing.

- General English/proofreading: the paper needs careful proofreading and more precise statements about claims and references.

- Observation: Wang & Xu note higher coherence can benefit subspace clustering; key difference here is that data need not be low-rank overall, only per cluster, so the paper’s structure blends low-rank and union-of-subspace assumptions.

- Suggested experiment: compare the proposed algorithm to a pipeline that does noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA on each subspace.

- Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption and shows significant improvements over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155; this contribution should be highlighted.

- Summary of main contributions: (1) identification of a clustering structure that leads to high coherence, (2) demonstrating how a dictionary in LRR can exploit this structure, and (3) providing a partial solution to sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
