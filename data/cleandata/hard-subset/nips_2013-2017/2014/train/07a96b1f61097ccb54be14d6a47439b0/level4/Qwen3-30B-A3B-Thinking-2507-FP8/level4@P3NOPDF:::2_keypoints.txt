- Proposes combining robust PCA with a dictionary-based, structure-preserving projection to improve recovery of corrupted observation matrices.  
- Core insight: representing data structure with a dictionary enables stronger recovery guarantees than standard robust PCA for structured (e.g., clustered) data.  
- Provides two theorems rigorously establishing reconstruction conditions given a dictionary.  
- Dictionary learning is implemented as a heuristic: apply robust PCA then represent the result with a dictionary, justified empirically only.  
- Technical content and theorems are sound, but the dictionary-learning step lacks theoretical justification, weakening completeness.  
- Clarity is poor: key concepts (e.g., coherence) lack intuitive explanation and practical interpretation, forcing readers to consult appendices.  
- Paper fails to clearly explain why the dictionary approach resolves robust PCA’s limitations or provide concrete comparative examples.  
- Writing issues and typos further hinder readability and accessibility to non-specialists.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of robust PCA and dictionary methods with meaningful theoretical extensions for structured data.  
- Significance: advances theory and shows empirical gains, suggesting practical value for structured-data applications despite heuristic learning.  
- Post-rebuttal: technical merit confirmed, but clarity remains a critical barrier; authors must substantially rewrite for broader impact.  
- Recommendation: strong accept conditional on a major revision to clarify concepts (e.g., coherence), state practical implications, and restructure the narrative.
