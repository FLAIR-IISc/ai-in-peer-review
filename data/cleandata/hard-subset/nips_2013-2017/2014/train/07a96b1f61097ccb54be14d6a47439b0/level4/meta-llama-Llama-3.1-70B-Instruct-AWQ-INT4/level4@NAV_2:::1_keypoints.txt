- Addresses robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated, inherently coherent observations, overcoming RPCA performance degradation with increasing coherence.  
- Identifies cluster/coherent data structure as common and important to handle in realistic datasets.  
- Proposes a novel approach to learning a suitable dictionary within the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing the method alleviates issues caused by data coherence.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in experiments on coherent data.  
- Clearly summarizes RPCA limitations and motivates the LRR-based solution.  
- Main strengths: novel dictionary-learning approach, theoretical soundness, and convincing experimental results.  
- Main weaknesses: relies on the assumption of a low-rank dictionary and needs careful parameter selection.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Scores highly on quality, clarity, and originality.  
- Authors claim the method could be extended to subspace clustering/segmentation but would require algorithmic and parameter modifications; future work planned.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses and paper should be accepted.
