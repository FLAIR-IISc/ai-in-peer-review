This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces of corrupted observations when the observations are inherently consistent. As the consistency of the data increases, the performance degrades, which is a standard theoretical bottleneck of previous PCA methods. However, this paper avoids this problem in a clever way. Since such cluster formation is quite common in realistic data, the solution to this problem is certainly significant. The proposed method is theoretically sound and has proven to perform well in practice.

I have only one curious comment regarding the submission:
Could the proposed method also be used to solve subspace clustering/segmentation problems? Could the authors comment on this?

This paper solves an important problem in robust PCA work. The paper's quality is very good and it should be accepted.
