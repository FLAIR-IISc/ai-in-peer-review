This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations, particularly when those observations are inherently coherent. A standard theoretical limitation of previous Robust PCA (RPCA) methods is that performance degrades as data coherence increases. However, this paper cleverly circumvents this issue. Given that such clustered structure is common in real-world data, resolving this limitation is a significant and valuable contribution. The proposed method is both theoretically well-founded and empirically demonstrated to perform effectively.

I have one question for the authors: 

Could the proposed method also be applied to subspace clustering or segmentation problems? Any comments on this potential application would be appreciated. 

This paper tackles an important problem within the field of robust PCA. The paper is of high quality and should be accepted.
