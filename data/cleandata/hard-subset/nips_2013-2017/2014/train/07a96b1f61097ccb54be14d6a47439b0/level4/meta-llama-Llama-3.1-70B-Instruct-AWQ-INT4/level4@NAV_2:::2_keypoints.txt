- Novel method: Proposes low-rank dictionary pursuit combining robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to recover coherent data.
- Key insight: Robust PCA guarantees are insufficient for structured data, but representing structure with a dictionary yields stronger bounds.
- Theoretical support: Provides two theorems that specify conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.
- Algorithm: Proposes and empirically validates a heuristic algorithm for learning the dictionary.
- Quality: Technically sound with clear, thorough explanations of theoretical results.
- Clarity concern: Main contributions, impact, and practical interpretation (e.g., coherence) are not explained clearly for non-specialists.
- Conceptual clarity: The notion of coherence is hard to understand and needs more direct interpretation and explanation.
- Originality: Combines recent techniques but contributes new theorems and a novel dictionary-learning approach.
- Significance: Advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results; empirical improvements over alternatives demonstrate usefulness.
- Heuristic limitation: The dictionary-learning algorithm is heuristic rather than formally justified.
- Presentation issues: Paper accessibility and language need improvement.
- Acceptance case: Novel approach, theoretical guarantees, and empirical gains argue for acceptance.
- Rejection concerns: Lack of broader accessibility, unclear coherence concept, and language issues argue for revisions before final acceptance.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarity, interpretation of coherence, and language polishing.
