- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA by exploiting additional clustering structure that mitigates dependence on the incoherence parameter μ.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees showing LRR with suitable A yields stronger recovery results for exact low-rank+sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.  
- The approach is novel and significant, closely related to union-of-subspaces prior work but presented in a potentially more general, less explicit clustering form.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical analysis and suggest applicability to many real-world domains (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (substantial contribution to compressed sensing / NIPS).  
- Writing issues: Lines 80–82 are ambiguous (reference to “our data” and conditions for “perfect recovery”) and need rephrasing for clarity.  
- Writing issues: Line 88 is unclear and should be revised for precision.  
- Paper needs thorough proofreading to improve specificity, precision, and overall clarity of language.  
- Connects to Wang & Xu’s work: higher coherence can help subspace clustering; here the exploited structure is low-rank per cluster (union-of-subspaces) rather than low-rank globally.  
- Suggests empirical comparison to a two-step baseline (noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace) despite lack of provable guarantees for that baseline.  
- Highlights Section 4.2: introduces a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption that outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155; overall the paper makes three key contributions—identifying clustering-induced high coherence, exploiting it via a dictionary in LRR, and partially addressing sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
