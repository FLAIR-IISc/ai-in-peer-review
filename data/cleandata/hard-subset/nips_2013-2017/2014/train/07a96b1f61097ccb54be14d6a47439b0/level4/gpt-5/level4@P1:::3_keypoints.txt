- Problem: Recovery of low-rank matrices with sparse errors when data follow a union-of-subspaces (clustering) structure that induces high coherence and breaks RPCA.
- Observation: The second coherence parameter µ2 increases with number of clusters even if global rank is fixed, explaining RPCA’s failure on coherent data.
- Method: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space U0 to avoid dependence on µ2.
- Theoretical results: Noiseless and noisy recovery guarantees for LRR when A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA, with bounds that avoid µ2 (and µ3) and are slightly looser in rank than RPCA (log factors).
- Algorithmic pipeline: Unsupervised procedure—run RPCA for an initial estimate, form a normalized rank-truncated dictionary from that estimate, then run LRR.
- Empirical validation: Synthetic experiments and corrupted motion sequences show substantially expanded success regions and improved subspace clustering accuracy versus RPCA.
- Relation to prior work: Complements subspace-clustering and RPCA/matrix-completion literature by leveraging extra structure (low-rank dictionary) to bypass incoherence requirements.
- Novelty: The idea of mitigating coherence via a low-rank dictionary in LRR and showing µ2 can be avoided under extra structure is original.
- Strength: Clear identification and explanation of the “µ2-phenomenon.”
- Strength: Solid theoretical advances with both noiseless and noisy guarantees under stated assumptions.
- Strength: Practical, simple unsupervised algorithm with modest additional computation and strong empirical gains.
- Main concern: Exact recovery requires U0 ⊂ UA and low-rank A; sensitivity to imperfect RPCA initialization (especially in highly coherent regimes) is not fully analyzed.
- Baselines missing: No comparison to a natural practical baseline that does noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 regularization, then PCA per cluster and recombination.
- Experimental scope: Limited to motion segmentation; additional real-world domains (e.g., text with clustered rows) would strengthen the work.
- Clarity issues: Several passages are unclear or imprecise; assumptions (column normalization of A, λ choice, ε setting) and reproducibility details need clearer exposition.
- Evaluation summary: Technical claims are plausible and supported by proofs; more discussion on robustness to dictionary misspecification would improve completeness.
- Suggested experiments/analysis: Compare to subspace-clustering-first baselines and analyze sensitivity to imperfect dictionaries; consider iterative refinement (RPCA→LRR→update A).
- Minor reproducibility issues: Specify A’s column normalization, rationale for λ = 1/√n1, choice/sensitivity of ε in noisy LRR, computational complexity, and actual runtimes.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses a known RPCA limitation with principled theory and a practical algorithm; significant relevance to practitioners.
- Cons for acceptance: Dependence on good RPCA initialization, writing/clarity shortcomings, and missing comparisons to natural baselines.
- Recommendation: Accept.
