The paper proposes a novel approach to improve the effectiveness of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) by incorporating Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A. The authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter \mu, often considered a bottleneck in RPCA's recovery guarantee, can be related to additional clustering structure in the data. Under certain conditions on the dictionary A, they show that LRR can exploit this structure, partially removing the dependence on \mu and yielding a stronger theoretical guarantee for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.

The method is significant and novel in the field, closely related to the union-of-subspace structure assumed in previous subspace clustering papers, such as "Wang and Xu: Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC" and "Soltanolkotabi and Candes: A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers" and "Soltanolkotabi et al: Robust subspace clustering". However, this paper is less explicit about the assumption on "clustering", which is reasonable and likely more general. The simulation and real data experiments validate the theoretical analysis, and it is likely that the new structure can be found in many other real applications, such as text data, as remarked in the paper.

In summary, the paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing, and its acceptance by NIPS is warranted.

That being said, I have several discussions and stylistic suggestions, summarized in the detailed comments below.

1. Lines 80-82 require rephrasing to clarify the description. The term "our data" is ambiguous, and even if the problem captures all the structures, an additional condition is necessary to achieve "perfect recovery".
2. Line 88 is unclear and appears to be incomplete. It should be revised to convey a clear message.
3. The authors should proofread the paper more carefully to address issues like the above two. They should strive to be specific and precise in their claims and references throughout the text. In general, the clarity of the language in the paper needs improvement.
4. Wang and Xu's paper "Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering" discusses how a higher coherence parameter can be beneficial for subspace clustering, unlike in RPCA and matrix completion. The key difference lies in the fact that the data need not be low-rank overall, but rather each cluster must be low-rank. The structure exploited in this paper can be seen as a combination of low-rank structure and union-of-subspace cluster structure.
5. It would be interesting to compare the proposed algorithm against an approach that first solves noisy subspace clustering with an \ell_1 penalty and then performs PCA for each subspace. Although no provable guarantee exists for the latter method to date.
6. Section 4.2 essentially proposes a new method for solving the subspace clustering problem with sparse corruption in the data. This method leads to significant improvements over the standard RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset, which I believe is a hidden contribution of this paper. The authors should highlight this point. This paper deserves acceptance due to its at least three important contributions to the field: 1) describing a clustering structure that leads to high coherence, 2) demonstrating how to implicitly exploit such structure using a dictionary in LRR, and 3) partially solving the sparse corruptions problem in subspace clustering.
