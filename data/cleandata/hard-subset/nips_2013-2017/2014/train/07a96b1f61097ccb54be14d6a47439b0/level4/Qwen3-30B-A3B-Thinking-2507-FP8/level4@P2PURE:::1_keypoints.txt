- Addresses robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations with inherent coherence.
- Identifies a standard theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods: performance degrades as data coherence increases.
- Proposes a clever method that circumvents the coherence-related bottleneck.
- Notes high significance because coherent cluster structures are prevalent in realistic data.
- Method is theoretically sound and empirically validated to perform well.
- Minor question: could the proposed method be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation—authors should comment.
- Reviewer rates the work as excellent and strongly recommends acceptance.
