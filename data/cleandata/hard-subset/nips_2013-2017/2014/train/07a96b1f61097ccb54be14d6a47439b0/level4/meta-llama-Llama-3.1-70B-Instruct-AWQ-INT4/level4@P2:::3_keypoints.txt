- Proposal: use Low Rank Representation with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA by exploiting clustering structure and reducing reliance on the incoherence parameter μ.  
- Theoretical result: under conditions on A, LRR can partially remove μ dependence, yielding stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank+sparse recovery.  
- Novelty/significance: method is novel and significant, related to union-of-subspace work but with a more explicit and potentially more general clustering assumption.  
- Contribution 1: identifies a clustering structure that causes high coherence.  
- Contribution 2: shows how a learned dictionary in LRR can implicitly exploit that clustering.  
- Contribution 3: provides a partial solution to the sparse corruptions problem in subspace clustering.  
- Experiments: simulations and real-data results corroborate the theory and show the structure exists in many applications.  
- Writing issues: paper needs rephrasing for clarity (e.g., "our data" in lines 80–82) and careful proofreading to fix imprecise claims and references.  
- Suggested comparison: evaluate against a pipeline of noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace.  
- Empirical claim: authors should emphasize the substantial improvement over standard RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.  
- Recommendation: contains at least three important contributions and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
