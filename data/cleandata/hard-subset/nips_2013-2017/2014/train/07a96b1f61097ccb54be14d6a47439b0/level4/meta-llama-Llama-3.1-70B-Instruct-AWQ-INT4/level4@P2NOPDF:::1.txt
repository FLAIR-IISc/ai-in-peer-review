This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations, particularly when the observations exhibit inherent coherence. A common theoretical limitation of previous Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is their degraded performance with increasing data coherence. The authors of this paper cleverly circumvent this issue, which is significant given the prevalence of cluster structures in realistic data. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and empirically demonstrated to perform well in practice.

I have a comment regarding the submission: it would be interesting to explore whether the proposed method can be applied to solve subspace clustering or segmentation problems. I would appreciate it if the authors could provide some insight into this possibility.

Overall, this paper solves an important problem in the field of robust PCA and is of high quality. It is well-written and effectively conveys the authors' ideas, making it suitable for publication.
