- RPCA performance degrades with increased coherence, which commonly arises from data clustering as the number of clusters grows.  
- Authors propose modeling L as AZ within the LRR framework to avoid direct recovery of L and its coherence issues.  
- Main theoretical result: if A is low-rank and colspace(A) contains colspace(L0), LRR recovery is immune to the problematic coherence parameters (µ2, µ3).  
- This yields a clear dictionary-design principle: construct A low-rank with U0 ⊂ UA.  
- Practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA for an initial L̂0, build a low-rank dictionary A from L̂0, then run LRR for refinement.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and motion segmentation show substantial improvement over standard RPCA and reduced subspace clustering error.  
- Strength: tackles an important, practical limitation of RPCA and links matrix coherence to clustering structure ("µ2-phenomenon").  
- Strength: novel and insightful use of a learned low-rank dictionary in LRR to counteract coherence.  
- Strength: technically sound—Theorems 1 and 2 formally justify the approach by shifting dependence to dictionary coherence.  
- Strength: well-written, clear motivation and illustrations, with convincing visualizations of expanded recovery regions.  
- Strength: practical and efficient algorithm with modest extra computation (~1.2× RPCA).  
- Weakness: dictionary construction is heuristic; paper lacks formal conditions guaranteeing Â satisfies U0 ⊂ UA when built from L̂0.  
- Weakness: unclear positioning regarding subspace clustering—whether the method is general matrix recovery or a robust subspace-clustering approach.  
- For acceptance: addresses significant problem, offers novel theory and a simple effective algorithm with convincing experiments and presentation.  
- Against acceptance: reliance on a heuristic dictionary-learning step without rigorous analysis of when it meets theoretical requirements.
