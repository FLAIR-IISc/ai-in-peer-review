- Problem: Addresses RPCA's limitations in recovering low-rank matrices when data have clustering-induced coherence.
- Observation: RPCA performance degrades as data coherence increases with the number of clusters.
- Proposed solution: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to overcome coherence issues.
- Theoretical contribution: Two theorems establishing recovery guarantees for LRR under specific low-rank dictionary constraints.
- Algorithmic contribution: Presents a practical unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm that empirically improves recovery.
- Experiments: Synthetic data and motion-segmentation results show the proposed method outperforms RPCA.
- Quality assessment: Theoretical results are technically sound and rigorous; the algorithm lacks comparable theoretical guarantees.
- Clarity issues: Paper is hard to access for general audiences; coherence concept and interpretations are underexplained and mostly relegated to the appendix.
- Presentation flaws: Writing has grammatical problems that impede readability and require revision.
- Originality: Novel combination of RPCA and LRR ideas; theorems offer new insights on how dictionary structure affects recovery.
- Significance: Makes a valuable contribution by addressing a previously overlooked RPCA limitation with meaningful theoretical and practical gains.
- Recommendation: Publishable after substantial revisions focused on clarifying key concepts (especially coherence) and improving exposition — recommend major revision.
