- Combines robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Key insight: representing underlying structure with a dictionary yields stronger reconstruction bounds than robust PCA alone.
- Theorems are provided that specify conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.
- Technical quality: paper is sound and rigorous; the two theorems clearly state required conditions.
- Dictionary learning is addressed by a proposed algorithm but its justification is primarily empirical (heuristic).
- Proposed pipeline: perform robust PCA, then represent the recovered low-rank matrix using a dictionary.
- Clarity is a major weakness: main contributions, impact, and claims are not explained clearly enough for specialists.
- Key concepts (e.g., coherence) are difficult to grasp, indirectly interpreted, and relegated to the appendix; language needs careful editing.
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contains novel theorems and empirical results demonstrating benefits.
- Significance: advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous theory and clear empirical improvements despite heuristic dictionary learning.
- After rebuttal: content is publishable but requires substantial rewriting; reviewer would recommend a major revision.
