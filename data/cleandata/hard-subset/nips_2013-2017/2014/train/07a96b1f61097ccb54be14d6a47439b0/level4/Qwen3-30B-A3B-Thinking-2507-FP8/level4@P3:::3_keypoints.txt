- Paper studies RPCA failures on coherent (clustered) data and proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a designed dictionary A to fix this.
- Main theoretical claim: if A is low-rank and U₀ ⊂ U_A, LRR is immune to the coherence parameters that degrade RPCA.
- Provides a theoretical foundation linking clustering structures to high coherence parameters in RPCA.
- Proposes a practical unsupervised algorithm that builds A from RPCA output plus simple post-processing.
- Algorithm is computationally efficient (~1.2× cost of RPCA) and easy to implement.
- Empirical results show clear improvements on synthetic data and a 40% relative reduction in clustering error on Hopkins155.
- Builds on prior RPCA and LRR work and connects to subspace-clustering literature while offering a new perspective.
- Strength: Novel and well-executed theoretical contribution identifying coherence–clustering connection.
- Strength: Practical, efficient algorithm with strong experimental validation.
- Strength: Significant practical impact for applications with natural clustering (motion segmentation, image processing).
- Strength: Generally clear presentation and notation despite minor grammatical issues.
- Weakness: Some statements lack precision and the draft contains ambiguous phrasing and grammatical errors that need fixing.
- Weakness: Comparison to related work could be stronger, especially to Wang & Xu’s noisy sparse subspace clustering.
- Weakness: Theoretical results are focused on LRR (no broader exploration of alternative methods for coherent data).
- Overall assessment: Strong paper meeting NIPS criteria with novel insights and practical gains; recommendation to accept.
