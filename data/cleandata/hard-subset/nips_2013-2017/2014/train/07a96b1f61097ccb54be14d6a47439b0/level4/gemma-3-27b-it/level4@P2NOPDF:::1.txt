This paper tackles the challenging problem of robust low-dimensional subspace estimation from observations corrupted by outliers, specifically in scenarios where the underlying data exhibits inherent coherence. A known theoretical limitation of existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is their performance degradation with increasing data coherence. This work cleverly circumvents this bottleneck, a significant achievement given the prevalence of coherent structures in real-world datasets. Addressing this issue is therefore highly impactful.

The proposed method is both theoretically well-grounded and empirically demonstrated to perform effectively. The authors present a sound theoretical analysis supporting their approach, and the experimental results convincingly demonstrate its practical benefits. 

I have one question for the authors: Given the method’s characteristics, could it also be applicable to subspace clustering or segmentation problems? Any discussion regarding this potential connection would be valuable.

Overall, this paper presents a strong contribution to the field of robust PCA. The quality of the work is excellent, and I recommend acceptance.
