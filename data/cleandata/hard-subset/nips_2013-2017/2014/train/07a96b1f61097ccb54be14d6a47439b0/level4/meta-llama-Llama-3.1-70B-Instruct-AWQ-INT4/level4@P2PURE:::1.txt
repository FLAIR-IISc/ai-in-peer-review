This paper tackles the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations, particularly when the observations exhibit inherent coherence. A standard theoretical limitation of previous Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is that their performance degrades with increasing data coherence. However, the authors of this paper cleverly circumvent this issue. Given that cluster structures are common in realistic data, addressing this problem is highly significant. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and empirically demonstrated to perform well in practice.

I have a minor comment regarding the submission: Could the authors comment on whether their proposed method can also be applied to solving the subspace clustering or segmentation problem?

This paper addresses an important problem in robust PCA research. The overall quality of the paper is excellent, and it is worthy of acceptance.
