This paper presents a novel approach to restoring corrupted observation matrices by integrating robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection. The core idea is that leveraging data structure – such as clustering – through dictionary representation can yield stronger recovery guarantees than robust PCA alone. The authors support this claim with theoretical results in the form of two theorems outlining conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary. The challenge of dictionary learning is addressed with an algorithm whose efficacy is demonstrated empirically, proceeding via robust PCA followed by dictionary representation of the result.

**Quality:**

The paper demonstrates technical soundness, with the theorems providing a solid foundation for understanding reconstruction possibilities. However, the justification for the dictionary learning algorithm relies primarily on empirical evidence.

**Clarity:**

Clarity represents the primary weakness of the manuscript. While the paper’s structure is logical, the key contributions and broader impact are not sufficiently articulated for an audience beyond a highly specialized group. Claims require more precise definition, and the significance of the results needs to be more clearly explained. Specifically, the concept of “coherence” is difficult to grasp, and its interpretation is indirect, residing in the appendix. The potential benefit of circumventing existing challenges through the use of dictionaries is a promising aspect that deserves further emphasis. The language would benefit from careful review. Author feedback on addressing these clarity concerns would be valuable.

**Originality:**

The work builds upon existing techniques, but introduces new theoretical results and empirical validation demonstrating the benefits of this combined approach.

**Significance:**

This paper represents an advancement in the field, offering rigorous results that contribute to the state-of-the-art. Despite the heuristic nature of the dictionary learning algorithm, its clear performance improvements over alternatives highlight the practical utility of the insights presented.

---
**Comment after author rebuttal and reviewer discussion:**

The paper contains publishable content and presents interesting contributions. My remaining concern centers on the substantial effort required to revise the manuscript to ensure accessibility even for specialists. Were a “major revision” option available, that would be my recommendation. The work effectively combines recent advancements and provides both theoretical foundations and a practically effective, albeit heuristic, algorithm.
