This paper studies the robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when the data are inherently coherent. It is well known that performance degrades with increasing data coherence—a standard theoretical bottleneck of previous RPCA methods. This paper, however, circumvents this limitation in a clever way. Since such clustered structure is common in real data, addressing it is highly significant. The proposed method is theoretically sound and convincingly demonstrated to perform well in practice.

A question for the authors:
Can the proposed method also be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation? Could you provide comments on this potential extension?

Overall, this paper tackles an important problem in robust PCA. The quality is very good, and it should be accepted.
