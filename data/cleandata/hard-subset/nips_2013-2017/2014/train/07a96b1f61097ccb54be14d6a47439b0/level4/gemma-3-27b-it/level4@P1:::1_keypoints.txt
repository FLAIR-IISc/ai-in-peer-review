- Addresses robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated observations that exhibit inherent coherence (clustering).  
- Highlights that standard RPCA performance degrades as data coherence (from clustering) increases.  
- Proposes circumventing RPCA’s coherence bottleneck by using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed dictionary.  
- Theoretical result: if the dictionary is low-rank, LRR becomes immune to the coherence parameter that grows with number of clusters.  
- Provides a practical unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm based on the theoretical principle.  
- Validated empirically on synthetic data and real motion sequences, showing improvements over RPCA.  
- Strengths: tackles an important problem, offers rigorous theory, convincing experiments, insightful link between coherence and clustering, and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis focuses mainly on the noiseless case; the noisy-case treatment is less detailed.  
- Weakness: experiments are concentrated on motion sequences; broader real-world dataset evaluation would strengthen claims.  
- Reviewer question: can the proposed method be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation?  
- Overall verdict: high-quality contribution; recommendation for acceptance.
