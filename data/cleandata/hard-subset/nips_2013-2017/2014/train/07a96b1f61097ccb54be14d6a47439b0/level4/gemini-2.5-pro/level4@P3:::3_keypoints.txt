- Paper identifies RPCA's failure on high-coherence data and attributes high coherence to underlying clustering (union of subspaces) in the low-rank matrix.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains that of L0, and analyzes recovery guarantees.  
- Theorem 1 shows LRR with such a dictionary removes dependence on coherence parameters μ2 and μ3, yielding stronger exact-recovery guarantees than RPCA for coherent data.  
- Presents a practical two-stage algorithm: RPCA for an initial estimate, then construct dictionary A from that estimate and run LRR to refine recovery.  
- Experiments on synthetic data show a 47% increase in the success region over RPCA.  
- Motion segmentation experiments demonstrate a ~40% relative improvement over the RPCA+SSC pipeline, evidencing strong practical impact.  
- Contribution: novel physical interpretation of coherence linking it to clustering, providing valuable intuition beyond a technical assumption.  
- Contribution: rigorous theoretical advancement proving LRR can overcome RPCA’s coherence bottleneck under stated conditions.  
- Quality: technically sound with proofs and solid experimental validation.  
- Broader impact: effectively bridges robust matrix recovery and subspace clustering literatures.  
- Clarity suggestion: manuscript needs careful proofreading and clearer, more precise phrasing in several sentences (lines cited).  
- Related-work suggestion: deepen discussion connecting to subspace clustering literature (e.g., Wang & Xu) and contrast when coherence helps vs. hurts.  
- Experimental/pipeline suggestion: acknowledge and discuss alternative robust subspace-clustering pipelines (e.g., cluster then clean) even if lacking guarantees.  
- Framing suggestion: more explicitly highlight the paper’s practical contribution to robust subspace clustering in the introduction and conclusion.  
- Final recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; weaknesses are minor and fixable.
