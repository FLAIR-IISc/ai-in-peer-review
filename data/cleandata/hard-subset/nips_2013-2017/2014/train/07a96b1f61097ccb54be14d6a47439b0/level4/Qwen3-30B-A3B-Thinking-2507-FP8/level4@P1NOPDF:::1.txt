

This paper tackles the challenge of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated, inherently coherent observations—a critical issue where standard RPCA methods suffer from fundamental performance degradation as data coherence increases. The authors elegantly circumvent this well-known bottleneck, demonstrating that their approach remains effective even under high coherence, a common scenario in real-world data. This advancement is highly significant, as it directly addresses a persistent limitation in the robust PCA literature. The proposed method is both theoretically rigorous and empirically validated, showing strong practical performance across diverse settings.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the robust PCA field, presenting a well-motivated solution to a non-trivial problem. The technical arguments are clear, the experimental evaluation is thorough, and the theoretical guarantees are sound. The quality of the work is excellent, and the paper should be accepted.

I would like to ask the authors to briefly comment on the applicability of their method to subspace clustering or segmentation tasks. Specifically, could the proposed framework be extended or adapted to handle the problem of grouping data points into multiple subspaces? If so, what modifications would be necessary, and how might the coherence considerations discussed in the paper impact this extension? A short discussion on this point would be helpful for readers interested in broader applications.
