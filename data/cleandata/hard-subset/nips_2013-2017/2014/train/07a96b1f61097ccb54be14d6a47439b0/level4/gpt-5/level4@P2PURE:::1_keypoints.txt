- Studies robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when the data are inherently coherent.  
- Highlights that increasing data coherence degrades performance and is a standard theoretical bottleneck for prior RPCA methods.  
- Proposes a clever method that circumvents the coherence limitation.  
- Notes that handling clustered/coherent structure is significant because it is common in real data.  
- The proposed method is theoretically sound and empirically convincing.  
- Asks whether the method can be extended to subspace clustering/segmentation and requests comments on that potential extension.  
- Overall assessment: tackles an important problem in robust PCA, high quality work, recommendation to accept.
