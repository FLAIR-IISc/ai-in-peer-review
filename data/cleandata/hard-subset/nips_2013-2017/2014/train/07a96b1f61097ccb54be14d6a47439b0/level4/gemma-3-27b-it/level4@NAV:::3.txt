This paper presents a novel and significant advancement in Robust PCA (RPCA) by integrating Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary. The authors demonstrate a crucial link between the often-limiting incoherence parameter, μ, in RPCA recovery guarantees and underlying clustering structure within the data. By leveraging this connection, and under specific conditions on the dictionary *A*, the authors theoretically show that LRR can mitigate the dependence on μ, leading to stronger recovery guarantees for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition, and its noisy extension.

The work is closely related to existing research in subspace clustering, such as the union-of-subspace models explored in "Wang and Xu: Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC," "Soltanolkotabi and Candes: A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers," and “Soltanolkotabi et al: Robust subspace clustering.” However, this paper offers a more general and less prescriptive approach to the assumption of “clustering,” which is a strength. The empirical validation through simulations and real data experiments supports the theoretical findings, and the authors rightly suggest potential applicability to other domains, like text data.

Overall, this paper represents a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing and is highly recommended for acceptance to NIPS. 

However, I have several suggestions regarding clarity and presentation, detailed below:

1. Lines 80-82 require rewriting for improved clarity. The use of “our data” is vague. Additionally, even capturing all relevant data structure does not automatically guarantee “perfect recovery” – further qualification is needed.
2. Line 88: The sentence “… is no longer a method of perfect…” is incomplete and requires finishing.
3. The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to address imprecisions and ensure clarity in claims and references. Generally, attention to language precision is needed throughout.
4. The paper by Wang and Xu ("Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering") highlights a scenario where a higher coherence parameter can *benefit* subspace clustering, differing from the typical limitations observed in RPCA and matrix completion. The approach presented here effectively combines a global low-rank structure with a union-of-subspace clustering structure.
5. A comparison to a two-step approach – first performing noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ₁ penalty, followed by PCA on each subspace – would be valuable. Although currently lacking provable guarantees, this comparison could provide further insight.
6. Section 4.2 introduces a novel approach to subspace clustering with sparse corruption, demonstrably outperforming standard RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset. This constitutes a significant, yet currently understated, contribution. The paper merits acceptance based on at least three key contributions: 1) the characterization of a clustering structure contributing to high coherence, 2) demonstrating the implicit exploitation of this structure through dictionary learning within LRR, and 3) providing a solution to the sparse corruption challenge in subspace clustering.
