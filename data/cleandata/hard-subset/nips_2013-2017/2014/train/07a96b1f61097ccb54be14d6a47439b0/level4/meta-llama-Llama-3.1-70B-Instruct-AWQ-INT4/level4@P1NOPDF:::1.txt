This paper tackles the problem of robustly estimating the low-dimensional subspace of contaminated observations when the observations exhibit inherent coherence. A standard theoretical limitation of previous Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is that their performance deteriorates with increasing data coherence. However, this paper skillfully circumvents this issue. Given that cluster structures are common in real-world data, addressing this problem is highly significant. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and empirically demonstrated to perform well in practice.

I have a minor comment on the submission: Can the proposed method also be applied to solving the subspace clustering or segmentation problem? The authors may want to provide some insights on this point.

This paper addresses a crucial issue in robust PCA research. The overall quality of the paper is excellent, and it is worthy of acceptance.
