- Combines robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key insight: for structured (e.g., clustered) data, robust PCA’s guarantees are insufficient, but representing structure with a dictionary yields stronger bounds.  
- Theorems are provided that specify conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.  
- Remaining problem: how to learn the dictionary in practice.  
- Proposed algorithm (heuristic): compute robust PCA first, then learn a dictionary to represent the recovered low-rank component; justified empirically.  
- Technical quality: the paper is technically sound and the theorems are reasonably checked.  
- Dictionary-learning step is empirically justified rather than theoretically guaranteed.  
- Clarity is a major weakness: main contributions, impact, and key concepts (e.g., coherence) are not clearly or accessibly explained.  
- Language and exposition need careful revision; the paper should better highlight potential impacts (e.g., circumventing earlier problems).  
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical validation.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements despite heuristic dictionary learning.  
- Post-rebuttal assessment: publishable content and interesting contributions, but requires major revision to be accessible to a broader specialist audience.
