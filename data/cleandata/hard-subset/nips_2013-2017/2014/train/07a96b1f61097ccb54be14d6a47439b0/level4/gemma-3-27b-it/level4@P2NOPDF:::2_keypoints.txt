- Proposes combining robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices by leveraging prior structural knowledge (e.g., clustering).  
- Presents two theorems that specify conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary.  
- Proposes a dictionary-learning algorithm (robust PCA then represent result with a dictionary) and demonstrates its empirical effectiveness.  
- Technically sound and well-researched; theoretical results clearly define feasibility conditions for reconstruction with a pre-defined dictionary.  
- Justification for the proposed dictionary-learning algorithm is primarily empirical; theoretical backing for the learning step is limited.  
- Main weakness is clarity: central contributions, claims, and broader impact are not clearly articulated for non-specialists.  
- The notion of “coherence” is difficult to grasp and is explained indirectly in the appendix rather than up front.  
- Paper’s language and presentation need careful editing and more explicit discussion of implications (e.g., how dictionary learning circumvents prior limitations).  
- Originality: builds on robust PCA and dictionary projection but contributes novel theorems and supporting empirical results.  
- Significance: advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous theoretical results and a practically effective (though heuristic) algorithm.  
- Post-rebuttal: paper is publishable but requires substantial revision to improve accessibility; a major revision would be recommended.
