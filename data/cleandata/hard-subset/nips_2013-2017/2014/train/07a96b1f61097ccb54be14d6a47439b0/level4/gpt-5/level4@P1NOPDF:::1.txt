This paper tackles the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations in the presence of inherent data coherence. A well-known limitation of prior RPCA methods is that their guarantees and performance deteriorate as coherence increases; this work circumvents that bottleneck in a clever and effective way. Since clustered structures that induce coherence are common in real-world data, addressing this issue is both practically important and technically meaningful. The proposed method appears theoretically well founded and is convincingly demonstrated to work well in practice.

I have one question for the authors: Can the proposed method be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation as well? It would be helpful to include a discussion of whether and how the approach could be adapted or extended to that setting.

Overall, this paper addresses an important problem in robust PCA. The quality of the work is high, and I recommend acceptance.
