- Core idea: Combine robust PCA with a learned dictionary-based, structure-preserving projection to improve restoration of corrupted observation matrices for structured data (e.g., clustering).
- Theoretical contribution: Presents theorems that characterize conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary, claiming stronger bounds than robust PCA alone.
- Dictionary learning approach: Uses a heuristic two-step algorithm—first robust PCA, then represent the result with a learned dictionary.
- Empirical validation: Algorithm is validated empirically and shows clear performance improvements over alternatives.
- Technical soundness: Paper is technically sound overall, with theorems clearly stated, pending standard verification.
- Justification weakness: The rationale for the dictionary-learning method is primarily empirical rather than theoretically justified.
- Clarity issues: Main weakness is accessibility—key contributions, claims, and impact are not clearly explained for non-specialists.
- Coherence concept unclear: The notion of “coherence” is difficult to grasp and its interpretation is indirect and pushed to the appendix.
- Writing quality: Language and exposition need careful editing and clearer definitions of claims/terms.
- Originality: Builds on recent techniques but contributes novel theorems and empirical evidence for the combined approach.
- Significance and recommendation: Advances the state of the art with rigorous results and useful heuristics; reviewer recommends substantial revisions for accessibility (would have suggested “major revision”).
