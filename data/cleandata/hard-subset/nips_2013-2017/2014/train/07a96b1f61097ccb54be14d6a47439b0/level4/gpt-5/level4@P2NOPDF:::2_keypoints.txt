- Combines robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key idea: explicit dictionary representation for structured data (e.g., clustered) yields stronger recovery bounds than robust PCA alone.  
- Presents theorems giving conditions for accurate reconstruction assuming an appropriate dictionary is available.  
- Major remaining challenge is learning the dictionary; authors propose a heuristic two-step algorithm (robust PCA → represent with learned dictionary).  
- The dictionary-learning approach is primarily empirically justified rather than theoretically guaranteed.  
- Paper appears technically sound; the two theorems are reasonable and support the main claims.  
- Empirical results show clear improvements over alternative algorithms, indicating practical usefulness.  
- Main weakness is clarity: core contributions, practical significance, and claims are not made accessible beyond specialists.  
- Notion of coherence is unclearly presented and only indirectly treated (mainly in the appendix).  
- Potential contribution—using dictionaries to avoid prior limitations—is underdeveloped and needs clearer articulation.  
- Overall publishable and interesting, but likely requires substantial rewriting/major revision to improve clarity and broader accessibility.
