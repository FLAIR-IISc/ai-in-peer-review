- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA) recovery guarantees.  
- Shows that the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure; LRR can exploit this to reduce dependence on μ.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for exact low-rank + sparse recovery and a noisy extension under conditions on A.  
- Novel and significant contribution, connecting RPCA/LRR to union-of-subspace clustering ideas.  
- More general than prior subspace-clustering work but less explicit about the precise “clustering” assumption.  
- Experiments (simulations and real data) support the theoretical analysis and suggest applicability to many domains (e.g., text).  
- Recommends acceptance to NIPS based on the substantive contribution.  
- Writing issues: lines 80–82 and line 88 need rewriting for clarity; “our data” is ambiguous and claims should be more precise.  
- Authors should proofread carefully and be specific about claims and references to improve clarity.  
- Notes relation to Wang & Xu: higher coherence can help subspace clustering because each cluster, not the whole data, is low-rank.  
- Suggests comparing to an alternative pipeline: noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace, even if lacking current guarantees.  
- Highlights a “hidden” contribution: Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new subspace-clustering method for sparsely corrupted data with strong improvements on Hopkins155; this should be emphasized as one of three main contributions.
