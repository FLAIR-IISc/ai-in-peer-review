This paper tackles the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when the data are inherently coherent. A well-known limitation of prior RPCA methods is that their performance degrades as data coherence increases; this theoretical bottleneck has constrained their utility. The paper proposes a clever approach that effectively circumvents this issue. Given that clustered structure is common in real-world data, addressing coherence is highly significant. The method is theoretically sound and is convincingly demonstrated to perform well in practice.

A question for the authors:
- Can the proposed method also be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation? Please comment on its applicability to that setting.

Overall, this paper addresses an important problem in robust PCA. The quality is very good, and it should be accepted.
