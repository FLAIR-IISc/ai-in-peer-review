- Addresses RPCA’s failure on coherent (clustered) data and its practical significance.  
- Argues standard RPCA is insufficient for low-rank recovery when data coherence increases.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence issues.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical justification (Theorem 1) for the proposed approach.  
- Insightfully connects coherence to underlying clustering structure of the data.  
- Algorithm uses RPCA to initialize dictionary learning for LRR, balancing theory and computational efficiency.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real motion-sequence data demonstrate improved performance.  
- Presentation is clear with well-defined notation and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Paper is novel, significant, and theoretically sound, with strong empirical validation.  
- Scalability discussion for very large datasets is limited and needs expansion.  
- Iterative dictionary refinement’s convergence properties and empirical effectiveness are underexplored.  
- Potential application to subspace clustering/segmentation is relevant but not fully addressed.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
