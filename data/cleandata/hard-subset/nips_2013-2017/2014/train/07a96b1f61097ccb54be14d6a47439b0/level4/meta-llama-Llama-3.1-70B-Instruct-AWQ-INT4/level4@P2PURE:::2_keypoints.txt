- Paper proposes integrating robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to better restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key insight: robust PCA guarantees are insufficient for structured (e.g., clustered) data, and representing structure via a dictionary yields stronger recovery bounds.  
- Authors provide theorems characterizing conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.  
- Learning the dictionary is unresolved; the proposed heuristic algorithm first applies robust PCA then represents the result using a dictionary.  
- Paper is technically sound; theorems and empirical results support the claims, though dictionary-learning justification is empirical.  
- Main clarity issues: contributions, impact, and definitions (e.g., coherence) are not well explained and the language needs revision.  
- Originality: combines recent techniques with new theoretical and empirical contributions.  
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art with rigorous results and empirically improved algorithms despite heuristic dictionary learning.  
- After rebuttal: work is publishable and interesting, with the primary remaining concern being difficulty of rewriting for a broader specialist audience (recommend major revision).
