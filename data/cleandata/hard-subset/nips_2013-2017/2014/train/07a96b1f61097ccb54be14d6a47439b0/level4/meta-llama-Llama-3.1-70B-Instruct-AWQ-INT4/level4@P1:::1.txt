This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating the low-dimensional subspace of contaminated observations when the observations are inherently coherent. The performance of previous Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods degrades with increasing data coherence, which is a standard theoretical bottleneck. This paper, however, circumvents this problem in a clever manner. Considering that such cluster structure is rather common in realistic data, solving this issue is certainly significantly meaningful.

The proposed method is both theoretically sound and well demonstrated to perform well in practice. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem, including a discussion of the limitations of RPCA and the benefits of using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly constructed dictionary. The theoretical results are supported by extensive experiments on randomly generated matrices and real motion sequences, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

One potential area for future work is to explore the application of the proposed method to subspace clustering/segmentation problems. The authors' approach to learning a suitable dictionary under the LRR framework could potentially be used to improve the performance of subspace clustering algorithms. It would be interesting to see the authors provide some comments on this point and explore this direction in future research.

Overall, this paper solves an important problem in robust PCA works, and the quality of the paper is very good. The writing is clear, and the organization is logical and easy to follow. The authors have done a great job of presenting their work, and the paper should be accepted.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a significant problem in robust PCA and provides a clever solution.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and well-supported by extensive experiments.
* The writing is clear, and the organization is logical and easy to follow.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the potential applications of the proposed method to subspace clustering/segmentation problems.
* Some of the notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts in the field, and additional explanations or references could be helpful.
