- Combines robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Core claim: dictionaries capturing data structure yield stronger recovery bounds than vanilla robust PCA for structured (e.g., clustered) data.
- Presents theorems providing conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary.
- Proposes an algorithm: compute robust PCA, then build a dictionary from its output; empirically validated.
- Technically sound overall; mathematical arguments appear reasonable though require detailed verification.
- Dictionary learning algorithm is heuristic and lacks strong theoretical justification.
- Clarity is a major concern: main contributions and overall impact are not clearly articulated for a broad audience.
- Key concepts (e.g., coherence) are hard to grasp and need more precise definitions and explanation of implications.
- Paper’s language and presentation need careful editing and restructuring for accessibility.
- Originality: novel combination of recent robust PCA and dictionary learning advances, supported by new theorems and experiments.
- Significance: advances state of the art for structured matrix recovery; empirical gains justify interest despite heuristic components.
- Post-rebuttal: contributions are clear and publishable but a major revision is required to improve accessibility for a specialized broad audience.
