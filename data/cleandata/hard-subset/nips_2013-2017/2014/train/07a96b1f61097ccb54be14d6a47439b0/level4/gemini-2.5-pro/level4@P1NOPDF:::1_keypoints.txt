- Tackles robust estimation of low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated observations, especially with highly coherent data.  
- Identifies a key theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods: performance degrades as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes a clever approach that successfully circumvents the coherence-related bottleneck.  
- Addresses a practically important problem since clustered/coherent structures are common in real datasets.  
- Method is theoretically well-founded.  
- Empirical results strongly support the method’s practical performance.  
- Asks whether the method could be extended to subspace clustering or segmentation and requests authors’ thoughts.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper solving an important RPCA problem; recommended for acceptance.
