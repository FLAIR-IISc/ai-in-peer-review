- Combines robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key insight: representing data structure with a dictionary yields stronger guarantees than plain robust PCA for structured (e.g., clustered) data.  
- Theorems are provided that state conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.  
- Remaining challenge is learning the dictionary; authors propose a practical algorithm: run robust PCA, then learn a dictionary to represent the result.  
- Algorithm justification is empirical rather than fully theoretical.  
- Technical quality is solid: theorems and analyses appear correct at a reasonable level of checking.  
- Main weakness is clarity: contributions, impact, and key concepts (e.g., coherence) are not explained accessibly and are often relegated to the appendix.  
- Language and presentation need polishing, which harms accessibility to a broader audience.  
- Originality: novel combination of recent techniques with new theoretical and empirical results.  
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art with rigorous results and empirically improves over alternatives despite heuristic dictionary learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel technique combination, supporting theorems, and empirically justified algorithmic improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, unclear interpretation of coherence, and unpolished writing.  
- Recommendation: accept pending a major revision to resolve clarity and accessibility issues.
