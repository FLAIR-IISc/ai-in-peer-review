This paper tackles the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when the data are inherently coherent—for example, due to clustering structure. It identifies a key limitation of RPCA: as data coherence increases, RPCA’s performance degrades. The paper proposes a method that circumvents this bottleneck in a principled way. Because clustered structure is common in real data, addressing this regime is clearly significant. The method is theoretically well founded and is convincingly demonstrated to work well in practice.

I have one question for the authors: Can the proposed approach also be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation? Some comments on this potential application would be helpful.

Overall, this paper addresses an important problem within robust PCA research. The quality of the work is high, and I recommend acceptance.
