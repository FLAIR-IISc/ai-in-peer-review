- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA, reducing dependence on the incoherence parameter μ and giving stronger guarantees for exact and noisy decompositions.

- Shows that clustering (union-of-subspace) structure in data can explain high coherence and can be exploited by LRR under conditions on A.

- The approach is significant and novel, relating to prior subspace clustering work but using a potentially more general clustering assumption.

- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theory and suggest the structure appears in many applications (e.g., text).

- Reviewer recommends acceptance, calling the paper a substantial contribution to compressed sensing.

- Language and clarity issues: Lines 80–82 are ambiguous ("our data") and need rephrasing to state required additional conditions for perfect recovery.

- Line 88 is unclear/incomplete and should be revised for clarity.

- The paper needs careful proofreading to improve specificity and precision of claims and references throughout.

- Related-work nuance: Wang & Xu show higher coherence can help SSC because each cluster is low-rank rather than the whole dataset; the paper’s structure combines low-rank and union-of-subspace ideas.

- Suggestions/highlights: compare to a baseline that does noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty then PCA per subspace; emphasize Section 4.2 as a novel method for subspace clustering with sparse corruptions that improves Hopkins155 results.
