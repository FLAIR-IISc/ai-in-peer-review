- Addresses accurate estimation of low-dimensional subspace from contaminated, coherent observations.  
- Notes existing RPCA methods deteriorate as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes a method that overcomes coherence-related performance degradation.  
- Emphasizes practical importance due to prevalent cluster structures in real-world data.  
- Method is supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results.  
- Reviewer asks whether the approach can be extended to subspace clustering or segmentation and requests authors' insights.  
- Considers the paper a significant contribution to robust PCA.  
- Rates manuscript quality as excellent and strongly recommends acceptance.
