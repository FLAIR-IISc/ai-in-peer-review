- Addresses robust estimation of low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated, inherently coherent observations.  
- Identifies performance degradation with increasing data coherence as a key theoretical bottleneck of prior RPCA methods.  
- Proposes a clever approach that circumvents coherence-related degradation, important given common cluster structures in real data.  
- Introduces Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit, which avoids coherence parameters that grow with the number of underlying clusters.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including definition of coherence parameters and their impact on RPCA.
