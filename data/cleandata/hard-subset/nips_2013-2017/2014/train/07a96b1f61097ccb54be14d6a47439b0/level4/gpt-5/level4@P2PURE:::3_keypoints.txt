- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to strengthen RPCA by reducing dependence on the incoherence parameter μ.  
- Shows that under conditions on A, LRR can exploit clustering structure to yield stronger guarantees for exact low-rank-plus-sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.  
- Method is novel and significant, related to union-of-subspaces prior work but less explicit about a clustering assumption (arguably more general).  
- Simulations and real-data experiments support the theory; proposed structure likely appears in many applications (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Request to clarify Lines 80–82: specify what “our data” means and note that additional conditions are needed for perfect recovery.  
- Line 88 contains an incomplete sentence (“is no longer a method of perfect [WHAT?]”); needs fixing.  
- Paper needs careful proofreading and clearer, more precise language for claims and references.  
- Note relating to Wang and Xu: higher coherence can help subspace clustering; key difference is global low-rank vs per-cluster low-rank structure.  
- Suggest empirically comparing to a pipeline that does noisy ℓ1-based subspace clustering followed by PCA within each cluster.  
- Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new subspace-clustering approach robust to sparse corruption, showing strong improvements over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155 and should be highlighted.  
- Paper’s main contributions: (i) articulating clustering structure that induces high coherence, (ii) showing this structure can be exploited via a dictionary in LRR, (iii) partially addressing sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
