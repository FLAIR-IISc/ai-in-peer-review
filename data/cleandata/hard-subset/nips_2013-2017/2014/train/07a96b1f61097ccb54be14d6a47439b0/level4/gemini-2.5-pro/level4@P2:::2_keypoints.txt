- Problem addressed: RPCA fails to recover low-rank matrices when data is "coherent" (e.g., clustered), because coherence grows with number of clusters.  
- Main idea: Use LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains L0 to remove dependence on coherence parameters µ2 and µ3.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorems 1 and 2 show LRR with a suitable low-rank dictionary can recover L0 without the problematic coherence constraints.  
- Practical algorithm: A two-stage heuristic—use RPCA to get an initial L0 estimate, build a low-rank dictionary A, then run LRR for improved recovery.  
- Limitation of guarantees: Theoretical recovery guarantees apply to the LRR step given a suitable dictionary, not to the full unsupervised two-stage pipeline.  
- Empirical evidence: Strong results—47% larger success area on synthetic data and 40% relative reduction in clustering error on real motion segmentation.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with solid theoretical foundation; algorithmic approach is reasonable though heuristic.  
- Originality: Novel synthesis of RPCA and LRR, linking coherence to clustering and formalizing recovery guarantees for low-rank dictionaries.  
- Significance: Addresses an important, practical limitation of RPCA and offers a method likely useful to researchers and practitioners in subspace recovery and clustering.  
- Clarity issues: Paper is written for specialists, making it hard to follow; coherence (µ1, µ2, µ3) lacks an intuitive explanation.  
- Presentation problems: Introduction and motivation need clearer framing; language and grammar require thorough proofreading.  
- Recommendation for improvement: Add intuitive, high-level explanation and illustrative figures for coherence, state contributions plainly in the introduction, and perform comprehensive editing.
