This paper addresses the task of restoring corrupted observation matrices by combining two recent techniques: robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection. The authors' key insight is that for structured data (e.g., clustered data), the standard recovery guarantees of robust PCA are insufficient. They argue that by explicitly representing this structure with a dictionary, stronger theoretical recovery bounds can be established.

These claims are supported by two theorems that establish the conditions under which reconstruction is possible, given a known dictionary. The practical challenge, then, is how to learn this dictionary from data. To this end, the paper proposes a heuristic algorithm justified by strong empirical results: first, perform robust PCA on the corrupted data, and then learn a dictionary from the recovered low-rank component.

**Quality:**
The paper appears to be technically sound. The two main theorems detail the conditions under which the proposed method can successfully recover the underlying data, assuming the structure-preserving dictionary is known. The proposed algorithm for learning the dictionary, however, is justified empirically rather than theoretically.

**Clarity:**
The primary weakness of the paper is its clarity. While the overall structure is logical, the main contributions and their broader impact are not explained clearly enough. As a result, the paper is likely inaccessible to readers outside a very narrow subfield.

The claims would benefit from more precise definitions, and the impact of the theoretical results needs to be more clearly explicated. For instance, the key concept of 'coherence' is difficult to grasp from the main text, and the means for interpreting it are indirect and relegated to the appendix. The authors should also do more to highlight the potential impact of their work; for example, they could elaborate on how their dictionary-based approach might circumvent challenges faced by previous methods.

Additionally, the manuscript would benefit from a thorough proofreading to improve grammar and phrasing. I would be interested to hear the authors' plans for addressing these clarity issues during the rebuttal period.

**Originality:**
The work is based on a novel combination of recent techniques. The authors contribute new theoretical results and provide strong empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of this combination.

**Significance:**
The paper advances the state of the art by providing rigorous theoretical results for structured data recovery. While the proposed dictionary learning algorithm is heuristic, its clear empirical improvements over alternative methods demonstrate the practical value of the paper's core insights.
