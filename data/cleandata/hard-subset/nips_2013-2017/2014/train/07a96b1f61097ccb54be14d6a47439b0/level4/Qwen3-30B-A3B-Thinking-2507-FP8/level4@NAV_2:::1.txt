

This paper addresses a critical limitation in robust PCA (RPCA) methods when handling inherently coherent data arising from clustering structures, a common scenario in real-world applications. The authors demonstrate that RPCA's performance degrades as coherence increases with cluster count (the "µ2-phenomenon"), as previously observed in [4] but not thoroughly analyzed in NIPS proceedings. They establish a novel theoretical foundation showing that Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a *low-rank dictionary* circumvents coherence dependencies, proving in Theorem 1 that exact recovery is achievable without µ2 constraints when the dictionary spans the true subspace. This extends LRR work from [13] (NIPS 2010) by providing a principled dictionary construction method, verified through extensive experiments on synthetic matrices and motion sequences (Hopkins155), where their unsupervised algorithm outperforms RPCA by 47% in success area and reduces subspace clustering error rates by 40%.

**Strengths:**  
- *Quality:* Theoretical contributions (Theorems 1–2) are rigorous, with clear proof of coherence-avoidance under low-rank dictionary conditions. Experiments comprehensively validate claims across diverse synthetic and real-world settings.  
- *Originality:* First to analyze coherence in clustering contexts and link it to µ2, moving beyond RPCA's incoherence assumption. The unsupervised dictionary learning algorithm (Algorithm 1) is a novel, practical advancement over prior supervised LRR approaches.  
- *Significance:* Directly addresses a fundamental gap in robust PCA for real-world data (e.g., motion segmentation), with demonstrable improvements in subspace clustering error rates. The framework offers a general solution applicable to any low-rank recovery task with clustering structures.  
- *Clarity:* Well-structured with clear notation and intuitive figures (e.g., Fig. 2 demonstrating µ2-phenomenon). Minor notation consistency improvements could be made (e.g., defining µ3 earlier in Sec. 3.1.1).

**Weaknesses:**  
- The focus on clustering structures limits applicability to other coherence sources (e.g., non-uniform sampling in text data), though the authors acknowledge this as future work.  
- Algorithm 1's computational efficiency claim (1.2× RPCA time) relies on sparse dictionary rank; this may not hold if rank(r̂₀) is high in practice.  

**Pro/Con Summary for Acceptance:**  
✅ *Pro:* Solves a significant, well-motivated problem in robust PCA with strong theory and experiments; advances NIPS-relevant LRR framework.  
✅ *Pro:* Unsupervised algorithm is practical and outperforms RPCA in real-world motion data.  
❌ *Con:* Limited to clustering-induced coherence; broader applicability needs exploration.  

**Constructive Question:** The paper shows compelling results for motion segmentation via matrix recovery. Could the authors elaborate on how their dictionary-based LRR approach directly enhances subspace clustering performance beyond RPCA pre-processing (e.g., does it improve cluster separation in the low-rank representation space)? This would strengthen the significance argument for clustering applications.  

This work makes a substantial contribution to robust matrix recovery, meeting all NIPS quality criteria with exceptional technical rigor and practical relevance. It should be accepted.
