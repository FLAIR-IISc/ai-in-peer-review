- Problem addressed: Robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated, coherent (clustered) observations, which breaks classical RPCA methods.  
- Importance: Tackling coherence directly is timely and broadens applicability of robust subspace recovery for real clustered data.  
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous analysis that targets the coherence bottleneck with well-motivated success conditions matching practical scenarios.  
- Empirical strength: Convincing experiments on synthetic and real data showing consistent gains over standard RPCA as coherence increases.  
- Practical relevance: Method addresses a common real-world setting where high-dimensional data form clusters within a low-dimensional subspace.  
- Question — extension: Could the method be adapted to union-of-subspaces problems or used for subspace clustering (e.g., iterative extraction or affinity construction)?  
- Question — limitations: Need discussion of sensitivity to deviations from the assumed clustering/coherence model and expected failure modes.  
- Question — usability: Request guidance on hyperparameter selection and summary of computational complexity relative to standard RPCA.  
- Overall verdict: Well-motivated, theoretically and empirically solid contribution that merits acceptance.
