- Tackles robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations with inherent coherence.  
- Identifies a common limitation of prior RPCA methods: degraded performance as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes a clever approach that circumvents the coherence-related limitation.  
- Addresses a significant practical issue because realistic data often exhibits cluster structures.  
- Method is theoretically grounded and empirically shown to perform well.  
- Reviewer asks whether the method can be applied to subspace clustering or segmentation and requests insight.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-written paper suitable for publication.
