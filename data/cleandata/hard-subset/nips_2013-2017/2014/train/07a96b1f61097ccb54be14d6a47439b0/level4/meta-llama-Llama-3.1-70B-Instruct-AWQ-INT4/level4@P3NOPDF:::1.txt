This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when the observations exhibit inherent coherence. A standard theoretical bottleneck of previous Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is that their performance deteriorates with increasing data coherence. The authors of this paper, however, cleverly circumvent this issue. Given that cluster structures are common in realistic data, resolving this problem is highly significant.

The proposed method is both theoretically sound and well-demonstrated to perform effectively in practice. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the method's strengths and limitations, making a compelling case for its adoption in relevant applications.

One potential extension worth exploring is whether the proposed method can be applied to solving subspace clustering or segmentation problems. The authors may want to consider providing some commentary on this point, as it could further enhance the impact and versatility of their contribution.

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of robust PCA by addressing a significant problem that has hindered the performance of previous methods. The quality of the paper is excellent, and it is strongly recommended for acceptance.
