- Integrates robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Central insight: robust PCA alone is insufficient for structured (e.g., clustered) data; a dictionary can capture structure and strengthen guarantees.  
- Provides theorems establishing conditions under which reconstruction is feasible given a dictionary.  
- Main remaining challenge is learning the dictionary; authors propose a heuristic algorithm (robust PCA followed by dictionary representation).  
- Quality: technically sound and theorems are reasonable, but dictionary-learning justification is primarily empirical.  
- Clarity: paper is logically organized but main contributions and broader implications are not clearly articulated for non-specialists.  
- Claims need more precise definitions; key concepts (e.g., coherence) are hard to grasp and explanations are pushed to the appendix.  
- Potential broader impact (addressing earlier problems via dictionaries) is underemphasized.  
- Language and presentation require refinement; authors should address clarity issues.  
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and supporting empirical evidence.  
- Significance: advances state of the art with rigorous theoretical results; heuristic algorithm shows practical improvements despite lacking full theoretical justification.  
- Post-rebuttal: paper is publishable and interesting, but requires significant rewriting to be accessible; would merit a major revision.
