- Introduces a novel method using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.
- Shows the incoherence parameter is tied to clustering structure and can be partially removed by exploiting that structure with LRR.
- Provides stronger theoretical guarantees for exact low-rank+sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.
- Connects to subspace clustering literature and explicitly addresses the clustering assumption in a more general, reasonable way.
- Simulation and real-data experiments validate the theoretical analysis.
- Demonstrates significant improvement over RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset for subspace clustering with sparse corruption.
- Highlights three main contributions: (1) characterizing clustering-induced high coherence, (2) exploiting that structure via a dictionary in LRR, (3) partially solving sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
- Recommends comparing against alternative pipelines (e.g., noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace) to assess empirical performance.
- Notes clarity and language issues: several vague or ambiguous descriptions (e.g., “our data” lines 80–82) and the need to define “perfect recovery” (line 88).
- Overall assessment: substantial, novel, and significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.
