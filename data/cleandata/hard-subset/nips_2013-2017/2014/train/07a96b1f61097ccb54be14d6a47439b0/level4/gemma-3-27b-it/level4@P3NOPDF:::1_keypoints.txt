- Addresses robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from outlier-corrupted observations with inherent data coherence.  
- Identifies a key limitation of existing RPCA methods: performance degrades as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes a novel approach that circumvents the theoretical coherence bottleneck.  
- Motivates the work by noting the prevalence of cluster structure in real-world datasets, making the problem practically relevant.  
- Method is theoretically grounded with solid analysis supporting the approach.  
- Empirically effective: experiments show superior performance versus existing techniques, especially under high coherence.  
- Experimental setup is well-designed, with fair comparisons on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and positions contributions effectively within RPCA literature.  
- Reviewer asks whether the method can be adapted to subspace clustering or segmentation, given its exploitation of cluster-specific low-rank structure.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with strong theory and experiments; recommendation for acceptance.
