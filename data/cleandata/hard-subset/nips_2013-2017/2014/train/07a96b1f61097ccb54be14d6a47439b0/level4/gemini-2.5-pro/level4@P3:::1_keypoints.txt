- Problem: RPCA performance degrades as the coherence of the low-rank component increases, a common issue when data has clustering structure.
- Insight: High coherence is a practical consequence of data clustering, not just a theoretical artifact.
- Theoretical solution: In the LRR model X = AZ + S, if A is low-rank and its column space contains that of L0, recovery becomes immune to coherence parameters (µ2, µ3).
- Practical algorithm: An unsupervised two-stage method—use RPCA for an initial estimate, build a low-rank dictionary A from it, then apply LRR for refined recovery.
- Empirical results: Synthetic phase-transition diagrams and real-world motion sequence experiments show substantial improvement over standard RPCA.
- Strength — significance: Addresses an important, practical failure mode of a foundational method (RPCA) with clear implications for real applications.
- Strength — originality: Novel use of a low-rank dictionary in LRR to counteract coherence effects, and a simple effective RPCA→LRR bootstrap algorithm.
- Strength — technical quality: Solid theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) and convincing experimental validation, including downstream subspace clustering gains.
- Strength — clarity: Well-written exposition, clear motivation, and illustrative figures (e.g., µ2-phenomenon).
- Weakness — dependence on initialization: The two-stage method relies on an initial RPCA estimate; authors should characterize when a poor initial estimate causes failure or irrecoverable degradation.
- Weakness — integration with clustering: Could explore integrating dictionary learning, robust recovery, and subspace clustering (e.g., using LRR coefficients Z for segmentation) rather than treating recovery as mere preprocessing.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, original, technically sound work with practical impact.
