- Problem: Recover low-rank matrices corrupted by sparse errors when data are coherent due to clustering (union of subspaces).  
- Key observation: RPCA’s incoherence condition breaks down as the second coherence parameter (mu2) increases with number of clusters.  
- Proposal: Use LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space to avoid mu2 dependence.  
- Theoretical result: Prove exact (noiseless) and near-exact (noisy) recovery guarantees for LRR under UA ⊇ U0 that do not depend on mu2.  
- Practical algorithm: Unsupervised pipeline—run RPCA, build a column-normalized low-rank dictionary from RPCA’s output, then solve LRR.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic and motion-data experiments show substantial gains over RPCA and improved downstream subspace clustering (SIM, LRR with A=X, SSC).  
- Conceptual contribution: Connects RPCA, LRR, and union-of-subspaces models and interprets coherence as reflecting clustering structure.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA and LRR and relates to SSC/SIM; contrasts with prior theory where higher coherence can aid clustering.  
- Contribution relative to prior work: Shows how a suitable dictionary in LRR mitigates coherence harms for low-rank+ sparse recovery.

- Strength: Clear theoretical insight linking coherence growth to clustering and how LRR with an appropriate dictionary avoids mu2 issues.  
- Strength: Both noiseless and noisy guarantees with explicit conditions and milder rank scaling than RPCA.  
- Strength: Simple, practical dictionary-learning algorithm with modest overhead relative to RPCA.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical validation and meaningful downstream benefits for subspace clustering.  
- Strength: Clarifies the role and physical meaning of coherence parameters.

- Weakness: Exposition needs polishing; several sentences are unclear or imprecise.  
- Weakness: Exact-recovery assumption UA ⊇ U0 is necessary but nontrivial to ensure; the unsupervised RPCA→LRR heuristic lacks formal guarantees.  
- Weakness: Comparisons are incomplete—missing pipelines that do noisy subspace clustering followed by per-cluster PCA.  
- Weakness: Discussion of coherence’s dual role (harmful for recovery, sometimes helpful for clustering) should be expanded and better situated.  
- Weakness: Limited sensitivity analyses (lambda, rank-thresholding, dictionary conditioning).

- Pros for acceptance: Novel theoretical advance removing mu2 dependence under plausible conditions, practical effective algorithm, and cross-community relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: Clarity issues, lack of formal guarantee for learned dictionary, and missing comparisons to alternative pipelines.

- Detailed suggestions: Clarify early motivation and definitions; reword imprecise sentences (e.g., lines 80–88); proofread for clarity.  
- Detailed suggestions: Explicitly contrast with works showing coherence can aid clustering and emphasize objective differences (global recovery vs. clustering).  
- Detailed suggestions: Evaluate alternative baselines (noisy subspace clustering + PCA) and highlight Section 4.2 as a robust subspace-clustering workflow.  
- Detailed suggestions: Provide guidance on sensitivity to lambda and rank-thresholding, discuss dictionary conditioning effects, and report runtime/scalability details.
