This work addresses the critical challenge of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from corrupted observations, particularly in the difficult setting where the data is highly coherent. A well-known theoretical limitation of conventional Robust PCA (RPCA) methods is the degradation of their performance as data coherence increases. This paper, however, introduces an ingenious approach to circumvent this longstanding bottleneck. As such clustered data structures are frequently encountered in real-world applications, solving this problem represents a significant and meaningful contribution. The proposed methodology is theoretically well-founded, and its practical effectiveness is convincingly demonstrated by the experimental results.

I have one point of curiosity for the authors:
Could the proposed framework be extended or applied to the related problem of subspace clustering and segmentation? Any comments or insights on this potential connection would be welcome.

In conclusion, this paper presents a high-quality solution to an important problem in the field of robust PCA. The work is a strong contribution and warrants acceptance.
