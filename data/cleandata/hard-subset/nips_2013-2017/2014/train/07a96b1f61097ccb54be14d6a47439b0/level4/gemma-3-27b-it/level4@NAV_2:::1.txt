This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating the low-dimensional subspace of contaminated observations when the observations are inherently coherent. A standard theoretical limitation of existing Robust PCA (RPCA) methods is performance degradation with increasing data coherence. This paper circumvents this limitation in a clever manner. Given that clustering structures are common in real-world data, resolving this issue is significantly meaningful. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and empirically demonstrated to perform well.

The authors demonstrate that the challenges posed by coherent data can be alleviated by employing Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with an appropriately configured dictionary. Specifically, they prove that if the dictionary itself is low-rank, LRR becomes immune to the coherence parameter that increases with the number of underlying clusters. This provides a fundamental principle for addressing coherent data and leads to a practical algorithm for obtaining suitable dictionaries in unsupervised settings. Experiments on both synthetic data and real motion sequences validate the authors’ claims.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an Important Problem:** The paper tackles a significant challenge in robust PCA – the limitations of RPCA when dealing with coherent data.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The authors provide a rigorous mathematical proof demonstrating the benefits of using a low-rank dictionary in the LRR framework.
*   **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm is practical and effectively leverages RPCA to construct a suitable dictionary for LRR.
*   **Strong Experimental Validation:** The experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach and its superiority to RPCA.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the authors briefly mention the computational complexity, a more detailed analysis and comparison with RPCA could be beneficial.
*   **Potential for Iterative Refinement:** The authors acknowledge the possibility of an iterative refinement of the dictionary learning process but do not explore its potential benefits in detail.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded solution to a significant problem in robust PCA.
*   The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   The paper is well-written and contributes valuable insights to the field.
*   The work addresses a limitation of existing methods and opens avenues for future research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The computational cost analysis could be more thorough.
*   The potential benefits of iterative dictionary refinement could be further explored.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of robust PCA. The theoretical results are solid, the proposed method is practical, and the experimental validation is compelling. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh its drawbacks. 

**Question for the Authors:**

The paper effectively addresses low-rank recovery in the presence of coherence. Can this method also be applied to the subspace clustering/segmentation problem? Would the authors please provide comments on this point?

I recommend this paper for acceptance to the conference.
