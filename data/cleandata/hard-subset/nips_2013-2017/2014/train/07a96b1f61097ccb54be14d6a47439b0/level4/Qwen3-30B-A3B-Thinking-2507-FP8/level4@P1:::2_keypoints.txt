- Addresses RPCA failure on coherent (clustered) data and proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary as a solution.
- Core insight: if dictionary A is low-rank and U₀ ⊂ U_A, LRR becomes immune to the coherence parameters that degrade RPCA.
- Shows clustering structures increase coherence (μ₂, μ₃), explaining RPCA performance degradation as clusters grow.
- Theoretically establishes that LRR with a low-rank dictionary avoids dependence on those coherence parameters.
- Provides two main theorems (noiseless and noisy recovery) giving conditions for exact or near-exact recovery of the low-rank component.
- Proposes a practical unsupervised algorithm that learns a low-rank dictionary from RPCA outputs.
- Empirical results: synthetic tests show ~47% wider success region than RPCA.
- Empirical results: motion-sequence tests yield ~40% relative error reduction in subspace clustering.
- Computational overhead is modest (≈1.2× RPCA).
- Originality: novel theoretical connection between clustering, coherence parameters, and recovery guarantees.
- Strength: rigorous analysis and comprehensive empirical validation aligning with theory.
- Weakness: presentation is technical; coherence is introduced without enough intuition for non-specialists.
- Weakness: dictionary learning algorithm is empirical/heuristic despite strong results; relation to theory needs clearer articulation.
- Suggestion: clarify differences between their coherence-parameter analysis and the unified μ(L₀) used in RPCA.
- Recommendation: accept with major revision to improve accessibility and better explain practical implications of coherence and the dictionary learning approach.
