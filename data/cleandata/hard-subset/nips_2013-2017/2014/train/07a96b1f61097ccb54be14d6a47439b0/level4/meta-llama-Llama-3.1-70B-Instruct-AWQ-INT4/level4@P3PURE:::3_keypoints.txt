- Paper proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA, showing LRR can exploit additional clustering structure to weaken RPCA’s dependence on the incoherence parameter μ.  
- Authors provide theoretical guarantees under conditions on A for exact low-rank+sparse recovery and a noisy extension, giving a stronger recovery result than standard RPCA.  
- The approach is novel, significant, and connects naturally to the union-of-subspaces assumptions used in subspace clustering literature.  
- Experimental simulations and real-data tests support the theoretical analysis and suggest the discovered structure appears in many applications (e.g., text data).  
- Related work (Wang & Xu; Soltanolkotabi & Candès; Soltanolkotabi et al.) is relevant, though this paper assumes a less explicit and potentially more general clustering assumption.  
- Review recommends acceptance to NIPS based on the paper’s substantive contributions to compressed sensing and subspace clustering.  
- Revise wording in lines 80–82: “our data” is ambiguous and the claim about capturing all data structure needs clarification and an explicit extra condition for perfect recovery.  
- Rewrite the sentence at line 88 to clarify what is no longer a method of perfect recovery.  
- Perform a thorough proofread to improve specificity and precision in claims and references throughout the paper.  
- Note the connection to Wang & Xu: higher coherence can help subspace clustering because clusters, not the whole dataset, need be low-rank; the paper’s structure blends low-rank and union-of-subspace views.  
- Suggest empirical comparison to a two-step baseline: noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace, even if that baseline lacks provable guarantees.  
- Highlight Section 4.2’s new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption, which significantly outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.  
- Summarize the paper’s three key contributions: (1) identification of clustering-induced high coherence, (2) showing a dictionary in LRR can implicitly exploit that structure, and (3) a partial solution to sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
