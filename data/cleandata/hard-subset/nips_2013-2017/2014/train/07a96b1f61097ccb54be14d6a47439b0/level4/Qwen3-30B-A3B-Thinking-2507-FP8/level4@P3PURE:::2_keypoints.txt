- Key insight: Robust PCA alone fails for structured data (e.g., clustered); using a dictionary to represent structure yields stronger theoretical reconstruction guarantees.
- Theorems: Rigorous conditions are proved under which reconstruction is possible given a fixed dictionary.
- Dictionary learning: The proposed two-step algorithm (robust PCA then dictionary representation) is empirically justified but lacks theoretical guarantees.
- Quality: Technically sound with reasonable verification and rigorous theorems, but dictionary-learning is heuristic.
- Clarity: Main contributions, broader impact, and key claims are not well explained for non-specialists; writing needs grammatical improvement.
- Definitions: Important concepts like coherence are poorly defined and only indirectly treated in the appendix.
- Originality: Novel synthesis of recent techniques with new theorems and empirical validation.
- Significance: Advances the state of the art theoretically and shows clear empirical improvements despite the heuristic dictionary-learning.
- Post-rebuttal verdict: Paper is publishable with meaningful contributions; major revision required to improve accessibility and presentation for a broader audience.
