The paper proposes a novel approach to improving the effectiveness of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) by leveraging Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A. The authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter μ, often considered a bottleneck in RPCA's recovery guarantee, can actually correspond to additional clustering structure in the data. Under certain conditions on the dictionary A, they show that LRR can exploit this structure, partially removing the dependence on μ and yielding a theoretically stronger guarantee for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.

The method is significant and novel, making a substantial contribution to the field. It is closely related to the union-of-subspace structure assumed in previous subspace clustering papers, such as "Wang and Xu: Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC" and "Soltanolkotabi and Candes: A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers" "Soltanolkotabi et al: Robust subspace clustering". However, the current paper is more general and less explicit about the assumption on "clustering". The simulation and real data experiments verify the theoretical analysis, and it is likely that this new structure can be found in many other real applications, such as text data, as remarked in the paper.

In summary, the paper contains a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing, and it is recommended for acceptance by NIPS.

However, there are several discussions and stylistic suggestions that can improve the paper. Firstly, the description in lines 80-82 needs re-writing for clarity. The phrase "our data" is unclear, and even if the problem captures all the structures in the problem, there is still another condition required for "perfect recovery". Additionally, line 88 is unclear and needs re-phrasing.

The authors should proof-read the paper more carefully to avoid similar problems. They should strive to be specific and precise about their claims and references in the text. In general, working on the clarity of the language in the paper will improve its overall quality.

It is also worth noting that Wang and Xu have discussed in their paper "Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering" how a higher coherence parameter can be beneficial for subspace clustering, unlike in RPCA and matrix completion. The key difference is that the data need not be low-rank overall, but rather each cluster must be low-rank. The structure exploited in this paper can be seen as a combination of low-rank structure and union-of-subspace cluster structure.

A comparison between the proposed algorithm and an alternative approach, which first solves noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty and then performs PCA for each subspace, would be interesting. Although no provable guarantee exists for the latter approach to date, such a comparison could provide valuable insights.

Section 4.2 essentially proposes a new method for solving the subspace clustering problem with sparse corruption in the data. This method leads to significant improvements over the standard RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset, which is a hidden contribution of this paper. The authors should highlight this contribution, as it is one of the three important contributions of the paper, along with the description of a clustering structure that leads to high coherence and the example of implicitly exploiting such structure by using a dictionary in LRR.

Overall, the paper's contributions make it a strong candidate for acceptance by NIPS.
