- Combines robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Key insight: encoding structure via a dictionary gives stronger recovery bounds than robust PCA alone for structured (e.g., clustered) data.
- Theorems are provided specifying conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.
- Main remaining challenge is learning the dictionary; authors propose a heuristic algorithm: compute robust PCA, then represent the result with a dictionary.
- Paper appears technically sound; the two theorems specify recovery conditions.
- Dictionary-learning step is justified only empirically rather than theoretically.
- Clarity is the main weakness: contributions, impact, and claims are not clearly explained for non-specialists.
- The concept of coherence is hard to understand and guidance is indirect and relegated to the appendix.
- Language and exposition need careful editing; implications and definitions should be made explicit.
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical results.
- Significance: advances the state of the art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements.
- Post-rebuttal judgment: publishable and interesting contributions, but requires substantial revision to be accessible beyond a narrow audience.
