- Proposal: Use Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA) recovery.  
- Main theoretical claim: The incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure; with suitable A, LRR can partially remove μ-dependence and give stronger guarantees for exact and noisy decomposition.  
- Novelty: Method is novel, related to union-of-subspace models, and makes the clustering assumption explicit and plausibly more general.  
- Experimental support: Simulations and real-data experiments verify the theoretical analysis and suggest applicability to other domains (e.g., text).  
- Technical quality: Paper is technically sound; claims are supported by theory and experiments; well-organized and mostly clear.  
- Clarity issues: Minor wording problems (e.g., “our data” lines 80–82) and need for more precise statements of claims and their conditions.  
- Originality assessment: Substantial and original contribution to compressed sensing and subspace modeling.  
- Significance: Likely to have significant impact by improving RPCA guarantees and being broadly applicable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel method, theoretical improvements, empirical validation, clear presentation, and technical rigor.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient precision about claim conditions and lacking detailed comparisons to existing methods (e.g., noisy subspace clustering + ℓ1 then PCA).  
- Recommendations: Rephrase unclear phrasing, state claims and validity conditions more precisely, add comparisons to alternate methods, and emphasize the paper’s significance.
