- Tackles robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from coherently contaminated observations, addressing a known limitation of prior RPCA methods.  
- Identifies that existing RPCA performance degrades as data coherence increases—a well-established theoretical bottleneck.  
- Proposes a novel method that overcomes this coherence-related bottleneck.  
- Notes strong practical relevance due to prevalence of coherence patterns in real-world data.  
- Method is both theoretically rigorous and empirically validated with strong performance.  
- Reviewer asks whether the method can be extended to subspace clustering/segmentation and suggests the authors comment on this possibility.  
- Concludes the work resolves a critical challenge in robust PCA, has excellent technical quality, and should be accepted.
