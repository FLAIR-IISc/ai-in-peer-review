This paper integrates two recent techniques, robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection, to restore corrupted observation matrices. The key insight is that for structured data (e.g., clustered data), the guarantees of robust PCA are not strong enough, and representing the structure using a dictionary can provide stronger bounds. The authors support these claims with theorems.

However, the remaining challenge is learning the dictionary, for which an algorithm is proposed, justified by empirical results. This algorithm first computes robust PCA and then represents the result using a dictionary.

Quality:
The paper is technically sound, as verified through a reasonable level of checking. The two theorems elaborate on the conditions under which reconstruction is possible, given a dictionary. However, the justification for learning the dictionary is empirical.

Clarity:
The primary issue with the paper is its clarity. Although the overall structure is clear, the main contributions and impact of the paper have not been explained clearly enough to be accessible to a broader audience beyond narrow specialists. The claims need to be more well-defined, and the impact of the results should be explicated. For instance, the concept of coherence is difficult to understand, and its interpretation is indirect (and only provided in the appendix). One potential impact of the paper is that using dictionaries may allow sidestepping of earlier problems. Additionally, the language requires checking.

It would be essential to obtain a comment from the authors regarding possible improvements to address these issues.

Originality:
The paper builds upon a combination of recent techniques but includes new theorems and empirical results supporting the usefulness of this combination.

Significance:
The paper advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results. Although the proposed algorithm for finding the dictionary is heuristic, it demonstrates clear improvements over alternative algorithms, indicating that the insights provided in the paper are useful.

Comment after the author rebuttal and reviewer discussion:
There is clearly publishable content and interesting contributions in this paper. My only remaining concern is that rewriting the paper to make it accessible to a broader audience will be a significant undertaking. If a "major revision" option were available, I would vote for it. The paper integrates two recent ideas, providing both theoretical results and a heuristic but empirically well-performing algorithm.
