- RPCA can fail on strictly low-rank data because coherence parameters (µ2) grow with clustering, degrading its guarantees.
- The paper formalizes the µ2-phenomenon: union-of-subspaces (clustered data) increases µ2 (and µ3), harming RPCA.
- Proposal: use LRR with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA).
- Main theoretical result: if A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA, LRR exact recovery guarantees do not depend on µ2; analogous noisy stability results are provided.
- Practical principle: use a low-rank dictionary aligned with the data subspace to neutralize coherence effects.
- Unsupervised algorithm: two-stage RPCA → build normalized low-rank dictionary from RPCA estimate → run LRR.
- Synthetic and motion-segmentation experiments show larger success regions than RPCA and improved clustering accuracy when recovery is used as preprocessing.
- The work connects robust recovery and union-of-subspaces/subspace-clustering literature, bridging RPCA limitations and clustering-based methods like SSC and LRRx.
- Theory quality: noiseless and noisy theorems are clearly stated and plausible; assumptions (low-rank A, U0 ⊂ UA, normalization, sparsity) are transparent.
- The rank condition rank(L0) ≤ O(n2/log n1) is slightly looser than RPCA’s bound, but the removal of µ2 is the main contribution.
- The necessity of U0 ⊂ UA for exact recovery is well explained.
- Algorithm quality: the two-stage RPCA→LRR is simple, well motivated, and empirically does not double runtime.
- Experiments quality: synthetic and Hopkins motion results demonstrate substantive gains and show benefits of learned dictionary versus RPCA alone.
- Clarity: paper is generally readable but needs proofreading, crisper language, earlier statement of key assumptions, and clearer separation of necessary vs convenient assumptions.
- Originality: novel insight linking coherence growth to clustering and the dictionary-design principle for LRR; unsupervised dictionary construction via RPCA is simple and effective.
- Significance: addresses an important RPCA limitation in clustered data, with theory and practical implications for vision and other domains.
- Strengths: clear identification of RPCA failure mode, µ2-free theoretical guarantees for LRR with low-rank A, practical algorithm, and strong empirical gains.
- Weakness — dependence on initial RPCA: exact recovery requires U0 ⊂ UA and the unsupervised method relies on RPCA estimates that may be poor in highly coherent regimes.
- Weakness — empirical comparisons: should include direct robust subspace-clustering pipelines (e.g., noisy SSC + per-cluster PCA), Outlier Pursuit, and LRR with A = X for fuller context.
- Suggestion — ablations: analyze robustness to imperfect A, sensitivity to dictionary rank, column normalization, rank truncation, and one vs two iterations of RPCA→LRR.
- Suggestion — theory vs clustering: explicitly discuss how high coherence helps subspace clustering but harms RPCA, and how the method leverages clustering for recovery.
- Suggestion — clarity and reproducibility: improve wording, define terms earlier, report parameter choices/stopping criteria, and release code.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled solution to RPCA limitation, simple practical algorithm with solid empirical gains, and novel theoretical insight.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on good initial RPCA, incomplete empirical baselines/ablations, and some writing issues reducing clarity.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
