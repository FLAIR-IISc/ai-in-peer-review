This paper addresses the task of restoring corrupted observation matrices by combining two powerful techniques: Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection. The central insight is that for data with inherent structure (e.g., clustered data), the recovery guarantees provided by standard RPCA are suboptimal. By introducing a dictionary to explicitly represent this underlying structure, the authors derive stronger theoretical bounds for data recovery.

The main contributions are twofold. First, the paper presents two theorems that establish the conditions under which perfect reconstruction is possible, given a suitable dictionary that captures the data's structure. Second, it proposes a practical two-step algorithm to learn this dictionary: first, apply RPCA to the corrupted data, and then learn a dictionary representation from the cleaned, low-rank component.

**Quality and Originality**

The paper is technically sound and builds upon recent advances in the field. The combination of RPCA and dictionary-based projection is novel, and the authors support this synthesis with new theoretical results. The theorems rigorously define the conditions under which this structured approach outperforms standard methods. While the proposed algorithm for learning the dictionary is presented as a heuristic, its effectiveness is well-supported by empirical results, demonstrating clear improvements over alternative algorithms. This combination of new theory and compelling empirical evidence constitutes a solid contribution.

**Clarity and Significance**

The most significant weakness of this paper is its clarity. While the overall structure is logical, the exposition of the main contributions and their broader impact is not accessible beyond a very specialized audience. To improve the paper, the authors should focus on the following points:

1.  **Articulating the Core Claims:** The central claims and the precise nature of the theoretical guarantees need to be defined more clearly in the main body of the paper. Key concepts, such as matrix "coherence," are difficult to grasp without intuitive explanations, and their interpretation is currently confined to the appendix. Making these concepts more accessible is crucial for a reader to understand the implications of the theorems.

2.  **Explaining the Impact:** The paper would be much stronger if it explicitly articulated the significance of its findings. For example, the authors could discuss how leveraging dictionaries can help circumvent known challenges or limitations in related matrix recovery problems. This would help a broader audience appreciate the paper's contribution to the state of the art.

3.  **Language and Presentation:** The manuscript would benefit from a thorough check for grammatical errors and awkward phrasing to improve readability.

Overall, the paper introduces valuable insights and advances the field with rigorous theoretical and empirical results. The demonstration that an explicit structural model can strengthen recovery guarantees is an important finding. The primary concern is whether the paper, in its current form, can effectively communicate these insights to the NIPS audience.

**Comment After Author Rebuttal and Reviewer Discussion:**

The author rebuttal and subsequent discussion have reinforced my view that the paper contains publishable and interesting contributions. The core idea of combining RPCA with a learned dictionary is well-motivated, and the work provides both theoretical support for the model (given a dictionary) and a heuristic algorithm that performs very well empirically.

My only remaining concern is the significant revision required to make the paper accessible to an audience beyond a narrow group of specialists. The issues of clarity in defining key concepts and articulating the broader impact still stand. If a "major revision" option were available, I would strongly advocate for it to ensure the authors have the opportunity to substantially improve the exposition. Despite this, the paper's novel combination of techniques, supported by both theory and strong empirical validation, represents a valuable contribution to the field.
