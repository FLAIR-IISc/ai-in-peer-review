- Combines robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Key insight: explicit dictionary representation yields stronger recovery bounds for structured (e.g., clustered) data than standard robust PCA.
- Provides theorems establishing conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a suitable dictionary.
- Main remaining challenge is learning the dictionary; the paper proposes a heuristic two-step algorithm (robust PCA then dictionary representation).
- Empirical results support the proposed dictionary-learning heuristic, but theoretical justification for learning is lacking.
- Paper appears technically sound and offers new theoretical and empirical contributions.
- Clarity is a major limitation: core contributions, impacts, and claims (e.g., coherence) are not explained accessibly and need clearer definition and exposition.
- The interpretation of coherence is obscure and relegated to the appendix, hindering understanding.
- Potentially significant impact—may allow sidestepping earlier problems—needs clearer articulation.
- After rebuttal, reviewer finds the work publishable but requiring substantial rewriting to reach a broader audience; would recommend major revision.
