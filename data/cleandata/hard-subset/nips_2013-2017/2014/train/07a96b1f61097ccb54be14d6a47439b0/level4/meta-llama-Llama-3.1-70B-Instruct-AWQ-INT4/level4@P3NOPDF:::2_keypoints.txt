- Paper integrates robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Key insight: robust PCA guarantees are weak for structured data (e.g., clustered data); using a dictionary representation yields stronger reconstruction bounds.
- Authors provide theorems establishing conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.
- Main practical challenge is learning the dictionary; authors propose a two-step empirical algorithm: compute robust PCA, then represent the result using a learned dictionary.
- Technical quality is good: theorems are sound and the theoretical foundation is well-established.
- Justification for the dictionary-learning algorithm is empirical rather than theoretical.
- Clarity is a major weakness: contributions, claims, and impact are not clearly explained for non-specialists.
- Concepts like coherence are hard to interpret in the paper and are relegated to the appendix.
- Language and presentation need careful revision to improve accessibility and conciseness.
- Originality: combines recent techniques but contributes new theorems and supporting empirical results.
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art with rigorous results; the heuristic dictionary algorithm shows clear empirical improvements.
- Post-rebuttal assessment: work is publishable and interesting, but would require significant revision to be accessible to a broad audience (reviewer would recommend a major revision).
