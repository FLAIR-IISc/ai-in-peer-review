- Integrates robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Key insight: dictionaries representing data structure (e.g., clusters) give stronger reconstruction guarantees than robust PCA alone.
- Theorems provided that formalize conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.
- Learning the dictionary remains a challenge; the proposed algorithm is heuristic and justified empirically (compute robust PCA, then represent result with a dictionary).
- Paper is technically sound; theorems and analysis checked to a reasonable level.
- Main contributions, claims, and impact are not explained clearly enough for a broad audience.
- The concept of coherence is hard to interpret from the main text and requires appendix material.
- Language and presentation need editing for accuracy and fluency; authors should address clarity issues.
- Originality: combines recent techniques but adds new theorems and supporting empirical results.
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art with rigorous results; heuristic algorithm shows clear improvements over alternatives.
- Post-rebuttal: content is publishable and interesting but requires significant rewriting; reviewer would support a major revision.
