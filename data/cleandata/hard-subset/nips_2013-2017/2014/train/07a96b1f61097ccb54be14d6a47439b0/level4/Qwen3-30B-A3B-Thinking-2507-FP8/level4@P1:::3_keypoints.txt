- Paper addresses RPCA's limitation on coherent, clustered data and shows RPCA degrades as data coherence increases.  
- Authors link coherence parameters to intrinsic clustering structures, arguing µ2 grows with clustering while µ1 can remain constant.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary A to avoid dependence on coherence parameters.  
- Theoretical result: if A is low-rank and contains the column space of the true low-rank matrix L0, LRR achieves exact recovery without coherence dependence.  
- Noise analysis extends guarantees to practical settings with dense Gaussian noise.  
- Proof techniques build on existing RPCA theory and distinguish behaviors of µ1, µ2, and µ3.  
- Practical contribution: an unsupervised algorithm to construct the required dictionary A.  
- Synthetic experiments show the proposed method attains a 47% wider success region than RPCA across rank and corruption levels.  
- Hopkins155 motion experiments show a 40% relative improvement in clustering error over RPCA using the learned dictionary.  
- Paper connects to prior subspace-clustering work (Liu et al., Wang & Xu) but fills the gap by explicitly relating clustering to coherence issues in RPCA.  
- Suggestion: clarify the distinct roles of µ1, µ2, and µ3 and better explain why µ2 increases with clustering.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state how the dictionary-learning approach differs from standard LRR with A = X and emphasize the novel subspace-clustering variant.  
- Editorial suggestion: replace unclear phrase "method of perfection" with "method of choice" or "optimal method."  
- Suggestion: discuss contrast with Wang & Xu (2013) showing higher coherence can help subspace clustering, to clarify differing perspectives.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, well-written, significant practical impact; recommendation to accept.
