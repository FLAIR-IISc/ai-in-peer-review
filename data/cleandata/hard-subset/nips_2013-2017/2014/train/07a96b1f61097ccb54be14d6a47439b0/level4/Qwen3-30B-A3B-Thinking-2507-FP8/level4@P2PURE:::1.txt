

This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations that exhibit inherent coherence. A standard theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods is that their performance degrades as data coherence increases. This paper circumvents that bottleneck in a clever manner. Given that such coherent cluster structures are prevalent in realistic data, solving this issue is highly significant. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and empirically validated to perform well.  

A minor question: Could the proposed method also be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation? The authors could comment on this point.  

This paper resolves an important challenge in robust PCA. The quality is excellent, and I strongly recommend acceptance.
