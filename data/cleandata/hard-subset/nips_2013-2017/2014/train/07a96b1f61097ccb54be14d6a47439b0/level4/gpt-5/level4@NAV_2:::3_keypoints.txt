- Paper studies improving RPCA via LRR with a learned dictionary A, showing the coherence parameter µ reflects clustering/union-of-subspaces structure and that LRR can reduce/eliminate harmful µ dependence (including µ2), yielding stronger guarantees for exact and noisy recovery.

- Contribution is novel and significant, connecting to subspace-clustering literature; simulations and real-data experiments support the theory.

- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.

- Revise Lines 80–82 for clarity: specify what “our data” means and precisely state additional conditions needed for perfect recovery.

- Clarify Line 88 wording (e.g., “no longer achieves perfect recovery”).

- Proofread for precision and specificity in claims and references to improve clarity.

- Note Wang & Xu’s point that higher coherence can benefit subspace clustering because only per-cluster low-rankness is required; the paper effectively combines global low-rank modeling with a union-of-subspaces perspective.

- Suggest empirically comparing to a pipeline that does noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA within each cluster.

- Highlight Section 4.2 as proposing a new approach to subspace clustering with sparse corruptions that substantially outperforms the RPCA+SSC baseline on Hopkins155.

- Paper merits acceptance for at least three reasons: (i) it articulates a clustering-induced mechanism for high coherence; (ii) it shows one can exploit this via an LRR dictionary; (iii) it partially addresses sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
