- Proposes a novel method combining RPCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.  
- Key insight: representing structured/coherent data with a dictionary gives stronger recovery guarantees than RPCA alone.  
- Shows RPCA performance degrades as data coherence increases, motivating a new approach.  
- Introduces Low-Rank Representation (LRR) to alleviate challenges from coherent data.  
- Analyzes when coherent data arise and studies recovery of low-rank, coherent matrices from corruptions.  
- Theoretical result: if the dictionary is low-rank and encodes ground-truth structure, LRR can be immune to coherence parameters.  
- Proposes a practical algorithm to learn the dictionary for LRR and demonstrates empirical superiority over RPCA.  
- Strengths: technically sound, novel combination of techniques, thorough analysis of RPCA limitations, clear organization.  
- Weaknesses: language and notation can be confusing and need clarification.  
- Weaknesses: key concepts (e.g., coherence) are not always well-defined.  
- Weaknesses: paper lacks intuitive explanations and illustrative examples for core ideas.  
- Weaknesses: algorithmic justification is largely empirical; more theoretical analysis of the dictionary-learning algorithm is needed.  
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify notation/language, add intuition/examples, and strengthen theoretical support for the dictionary-learning algorithm.
