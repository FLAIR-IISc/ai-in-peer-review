This paper proposes a new approach to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations by combining robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) with dictionary-based Low-Rank Representation (LRR). The authors argue that RPCA is not sufficient for handling coherent data, which is common in many applications, and that LRR can provide better guarantees when the dictionary is properly chosen.

The paper is technically sound, and the two theorems provide conditions under which reconstruction is possible, given a dictionary. The authors also propose a heuristic algorithm to learn the dictionary, which is justified by empirical results. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in handling coherent data and improving the performance of RPCA.

However, the clarity of the paper is a major concern. The structure of the paper is clear, but the main contributions and impact of the paper are not well-explained, making it difficult for non-specialists to understand. The concept of coherence is particularly hard to grasp, and the means of interpreting it are indirect and relegated to the appendix. The language also needs improvement.

The originality of the paper lies in the combination of recent techniques, new theorems, and empirical results supporting the usefulness of the combination. The paper advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results, and the proposed algorithm demonstrates clear improvements over alternative algorithms.

To improve the paper, the authors should focus on clarifying the main contributions and impact of the paper, providing more intuitive explanations of technical concepts, and improving the language. While the paper is technically sound, its accessibility is limited, and significant revisions are needed to make it more readable for a broader audience.

Strengths:

* Technically sound
* New theorems and empirical results
* Advances the state-of-the-art
* Clear improvements over alternative algorithms

Weaknesses:

* Lack of clarity in explaining main contributions and impact
* Technical concepts, such as coherence, are hard to understand
* Language needs improvement
* Accessibility is limited

Recommendation:

* Major revision is needed to improve clarity and accessibility
* Authors should focus on explaining main contributions and impact in a more intuitive way
* Technical concepts should be explained more clearly and concisely
* Language should be improved for better readability
