- Paper tackles low-rank matrix recovery from grossly corrupted observations and identifies RPCA's failure on "coherent" (e.g., clustered) data.  
- Key insight: RPCA performance degrades with data coherence; Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a suitable dictionary can overcome this.  
- Theoretical results: two main theorems give exact (noiseless) and near-exact (noisy) recovery guarantees for LRR assuming a suitable low-rank dictionary.  
- Practical challenge: learning the required dictionary unsupervisedly; authors propose a two-stage algorithm using RPCA to seed a dictionary, then LRR for final recovery.  
- Quality: technically sound and theorems rigorously support the central claim; empirical results justify the proposed dictionary-learning heuristic.  
- Clarity: paper is poorly explained for non-specialists—main contributions, broader impact, and the notion of "coherence" are not conveyed clearly.  
- Suggested improvements: state central claims more precisely, better explain coherence intuition, highlight how the dictionary approach specifically overcomes prior limitations, and edit language.  
- Originality: novel identification of RPCA's failure mode on coherent data and an original synthesis of RPCA and LRR with new theoretical guarantees.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art by addressing an important RPCA limitation; shows clear empirical improvements despite heuristic dictionary learning.  
- Final assessment: publishable and interesting contributions, but requires substantial revision to improve accessibility—would recommend major revision for broader audience.
