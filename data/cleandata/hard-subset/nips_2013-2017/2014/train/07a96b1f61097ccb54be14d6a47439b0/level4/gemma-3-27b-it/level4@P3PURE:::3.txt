The paper proposes to leverage Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to enhance the performance of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA). Specifically, the authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter μ, often considered a limiting factor in RPCA recovery guarantees, can in fact reflect underlying clustering structure within the data. Under certain conditions on the dictionary A, they show that exploiting this structure using LRR can reduce the dependence on μ, leading to theoretically stronger guarantees for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition, and its noisy extension.

This work is novel and represents a significant contribution to the field. It builds upon the union-of-subspace structure commonly assumed in previous subspace clustering papers – such as those by Wang and Xu ("Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC") and Soltanolkotabi and Candes ("A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers") and Soltanolkotabi et al ("Robust subspace clustering") – but is less prescriptive about the assumptions regarding “clustering”, potentially offering greater generality. The simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical analysis, and I believe the identified structure could be applicable in many other domains, including text data as noted by the authors.

In summary, this paper provides a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing and I recommend its acceptance to NIPS. 

However, I have several discussion points and stylistic suggestions detailed below.

1. Lines 80-82 require revision for clarity. The phrase “our data” is vague. Additionally, even if the problem formulation captures all relevant data structure, further conditions are still necessary for “perfect recovery.”
2. Line 88: “… is no longer a method of perfect [recovery/decomposition?]” – please complete the thought.
3. The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to address imprecise language and incomplete statements. Please be specific and precise in claims and references within the text. Overall, improving the clarity of the writing is recommended.
4. Wang and Xu’s work ("Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering") highlights that a higher coherence parameter can actually *benefit* subspace clustering, unlike in RPCA and matrix completion. A key distinction is that their approach does not require overall low-rankness of the data; rather, each cluster is assumed to be low-rank. The structure exploited in this paper can be viewed as a combination of global low-rank structure and a union-of-subspace cluster structure.
5. It would be interesting to compare the proposed algorithm against a two-stage approach: first performing noisy subspace clustering with an \ell_1 penalty, followed by applying PCA to each identified subspace. Although currently lacking provable guarantees, such a comparison would be insightful.
6. Section 4.2 essentially introduces a novel method for solving the subspace clustering problem with sparse corruption. The demonstrated improvements over standard RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset represent a significant, yet currently understated, contribution. The authors should explicitly acknowledge this. This paper should be accepted based on at least three key contributions: 1) characterization of a clustering structure that can lead to high coherence, 2) demonstration of how this structure can be implicitly exploited using a dictionary in LRR, and 3) a partial solution to the sparse corruption problem in subspace clustering.
