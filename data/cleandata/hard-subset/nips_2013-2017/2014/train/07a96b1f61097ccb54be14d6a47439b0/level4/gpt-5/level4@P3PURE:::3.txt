This paper proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to enhance the effectiveness of RPCA. The key insight is that the incoherence parameter μ—often considered a bottleneck in RPCA recovery guarantees—can correspond to additional clustering structure in the data. Under appropriate conditions on the dictionary A, the authors show that one can exploit this structure using LRR, thereby partially removing the dependence on μ and obtaining theoretically stronger guarantees for both exact low-rank plus sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.

The method is novel and significant. It is closely related to the union-of-subspaces structure assumed in prior subspace clustering work, such as Wang and Xu (“Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering” and “Provable Subspace Clustering: When LRR Meets SSC”) and Soltanolkotabi and Candes (“A Geometric Analysis of Subspace Clustering with Outliers”; “Robust Subspace Clustering”). Compared to those works, this paper is less explicit about the precise “clustering” assumption, which seems reasonable and likely more general.

The simulations and real-data experiments support the theoretical analysis, and the proposed structural perspective appears relevant to many real applications (e.g., text data, as the paper notes).

In summary, the paper makes a substantial contribution to compressed sensing and related areas, and I believe it should be accepted to NIPS.

Detailed comments and suggestions
1. Lines 80–82: This passage needs rewriting for clarity. Please clarify what “our data” refers to. Also, even if the model captures all relevant structures, there are still additional conditions required to ensure “perfect recovery.” Please be explicit about these conditions and what “perfect recovery” entails in your setting.

2. Line 88: The phrase “… is no longer a method of perfect (WHAT?)” is incomplete. Please complete or clarify what “perfect” is modifying (e.g., perfect recovery, perfect separation).

3. Please proofread the paper carefully for issues like the above. Be specific and precise about claims and references throughout. Overall, improving the clarity and precision of the language would significantly strengthen the presentation.

4. Related work: Wang and Xu explicitly argue in “Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering” that higher coherence can be beneficial for subspace clustering (in contrast to RPCA and matrix completion). The key difference is that the data need not be globally low-rank; only each cluster is low-rank. The structure exploited in this paper can be viewed as a combination of global low-rank structure and a union-of-subspaces clustering structure.

5. It would be informative to compare the proposed method against a pipeline that first performs noisy subspace clustering with an l1 penalty, followed by PCA within each subspace. While no provable guarantees exist for the latter step to date, such an empirical comparison would contextualize the practical gains of the proposed approach.

6. Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruptions. The approach yields substantial improvements over the standard RPCA+SSC baseline on the Hopkins155 dataset. This seems like a hidden contribution that should be made explicit. In particular, the paper merits acceptance for at least three important contributions:
   - A description of clustering structure that leads to high coherence.
   - An example showing that one can implicitly exploit such structure using a dictionary in LRR, thereby partially mitigating μ-dependence in recovery guarantees.
   - A partial solution to the sparse-corruptions problem in subspace clustering, with strong empirical performance.
