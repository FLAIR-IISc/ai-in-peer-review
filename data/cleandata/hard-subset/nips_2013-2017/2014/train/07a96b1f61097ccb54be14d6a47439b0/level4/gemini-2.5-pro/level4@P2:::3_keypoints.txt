- Problem: RPCA fails when the true low-rank data is "coherent" (e.g., clustered/union-of-subspaces), since coherence µ grows with cluster count and weakens recovery guarantees.  
- Solution: Use LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true L0 to eliminate dependence on coherence parameters µ2 and µ3.  
- Algorithm: Practical two-stage pipeline — run RPCA for an initial L0 estimate, use it to build a low-rank dictionary, then run LRR to refine recovery.  
- Theory: Theorems 1 and 2 (noiseless/noisy) show that if A is low-rank and spans L0, recovery guarantees no longer depend on the problematic coherence parameters.  
- Empirical results (synthetic): Method yields a 47% larger success region than standard RPCA.  
- Empirical results (real data): Achieves state-of-the-art motion segmentation performance, lowering SSC error from 9.50% to 5.74% after recovery.  
- Originality: Provides a novel physical interpretation of coherence as arising from union-of-subspaces and introduces the low-rank dictionary idea to counteract it.  
- Technical quality: Paper is technically sound with formal guarantees; the unsupervised algorithm is simple, intuitive, and well-motivated.  
- Impact: Likely influential across low-rank recovery and subspace clustering applications; provides a practical way to improve RPCA in realistic settings.  
- Clarity issues: Manuscript needs proofreading and tighter phrasing (several sentences/lines cited as imprecise or informal).  
- Related-work/context: Should discuss contrasts with works (e.g., Wang & Xu) where higher coherence can help clustering under different assumptions.  
- Alternative comparisons: Suggest comparing to a pipeline that does robust subspace clustering on corrupted data then PCA per cluster to contextualize advantages.  
- Presentation suggestion: Explicitly highlight the robust subspace clustering improvements (Section 4.2) as a main contribution in the introduction and conclusion.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — novel theory, practical algorithm, strong experiments; only minor polishing needed.  
- Counterarguments: Only notable weakness is minor language/presentation polishing; no substantive technical flaws identified.
