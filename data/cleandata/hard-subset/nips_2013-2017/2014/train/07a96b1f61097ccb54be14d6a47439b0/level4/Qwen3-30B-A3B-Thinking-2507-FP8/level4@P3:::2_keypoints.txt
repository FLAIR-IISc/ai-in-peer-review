- Problem: RPCA fails on coherent (clustered) low-rank data; performance degrades as cluster-induced coherence increases.  
- Main contribution: Proposes using LRR with a properly constructed low-rank dictionary to avoid coherence parameters and recover low-rank structure.  
- Algorithm: Provides an unsupervised method to construct the dictionary using RPCA as a preprocessing step.  
- Theory: Presents two technically sound theorems proving LRR recovery conditions when the dictionary is low-rank.  
- Theoretical gap: Needs clearer connection between the theoretical conditions and practical requirements for the dictionary matrix.  
- Empirical results: Thorough experiments on synthetic data and motion sequences showing clear improvements over RPCA (47% wider success area; 40% relative drop in motion segmentation error).  
- Comparisons: Empirical comparison lacks broader baselines—limited comparison with other recent robust low-rank recovery methods.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense and introduces “coherence” without intuition or concrete visualizations linking it to clustering.  
- Algorithm clarity: Algorithm 1 is clear, but steps like rank estimation and normalization need better justification.  
- Originality: Novel perspective on the µ2-phenomenon and the role of dictionary structure in overcoming coherence limitations.  
- Significance: Addresses a practical gap for real-world clustered data and yields tangible empirical gains.  
- Strengths: Identifies an important limitation of RPCA, provides theoretical insight, and proposes a practical, empirically validated solution.  
- Weaknesses: Poor accessibility for non-specialists, insufficient intuitive explanations, limited baseline comparisons, and weak practical guidance on dictionary construction.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, add intuition/visualizations, and better connect theory to practice.
