- Problem: Recovery of low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, coherent (clustered) data where RPCA degrades as cluster number increases due to growing μ2/μ3.
- Proposal: Use LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space to avoid dependence on μ2.
- Theory: Provides recovery theorems for noiseless and noisy settings showing LRR can recover without μ2 dependence given a suitable A.
- Practical algorithm: Unsupervised pipeline—apply RPCA, build a normalized low-rank dictionary from the estimate, then solve LRR.
- Experiments: Synthetic and corrupted motion data show improved recovery and better downstream subspace clustering vs RPCA.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA/PCP and LRR; extends coherence-framework analysis and studies LRR with general dictionaries (beyond A = X).
- Strength: Clearly identifies a practical RPCA failure mode induced by clustering and growing μ2.
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees that LRR with low-rank A can avoid μ2, with both exact and near-recovery results.
- Strength: Practical, simple unsupervised algorithm leveraging RPCA’s partial success to construct A.
- Strength: Empirical gains on synthetic and real data with meaningful downstream benefits.
- Weakness: Exposition is dense and difficult for non-specialists; coherence and its link to clustering need earlier, clearer intuition.
- Weakness: Key assumption U0 ⊂ UA and low rank of A is necessary but its practical attainability and sensitivity are only informally discussed.
- Weakness: Dictionary-learning procedure is heuristic without theoretical analysis of success probability or required conditions.
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited—needs sensitivity analyses (cluster number, dictionary rank, noise), runtimes, and additional baselines.
- Quality: Technical claims are plausible and appear sound; theorems clearly state conditions, but the practical dictionary step lacks theoretical bridging.
- Clarity suggestions: Add intuitive explanations/toy examples of μ2 growth, separate theorem guarantees from algorithm goals, and summarize assumptions in one place.
- Clarity suggestions: Tighten language, reduce reliance on appendices for core intuition, and improve notation recap and figure captions.
- Originality: Novel combination—analyzing LRR with low-rank dictionaries to circumvent μ2 in coherent data and linking μ2 growth to clustering.
- Significance: Important contribution for robust recovery and subspace learning in clustered regimes with practical algorithmic improvements.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses common clustered-data failure, provides new LRR theory, offers a practical algorithm with empirical gains, and clarifies coherence meaning.
- Cons for acceptance: Requires substantial exposition revision, strong dependence on U0 ⊂ UA with no guarantees for dictionary learning, and limited experimental evaluation.
- Requested improvements: Add diagrams/toy examples, analyze sensitivity to dictionary rank and U0 ⊂ UA mismatch and λ, report runtimes/convergence and ablations, and compare to more baselines (e.g., Outlier Pursuit).
- Overall recommendation: Solid theoretical and practical contributions but needs a major rewrite to improve accessibility and to expand theoretical/experimental validation of the dictionary-learning step.
