- Paper proposes integrating robust PCA with a dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Core claim: leveraging a dictionary yields stronger recovery guarantees than robust PCA alone, especially for structured (e.g., clustered) data.
- Authors provide two theorems that specify reconstruction conditions given a dictionary.
- A practical dictionary-learning algorithm is proposed: run robust PCA first, then represent the result using a dictionary.
- Empirical results support the proposed algorithm’s improvements over existing methods.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, but justification for the dictionary-learning procedure is mainly empirical.
- Main weakness is clarity: key contributions, broader impact, and some claims are not readily accessible to non-specialists.
- The concept of “coherence” is under-explained and presented indirectly in the appendix, reducing interpretability.
- Manuscript needs careful language editing and clearer exposition of significance and how it circumvents prior limitations.
- Overall, the work is original and significant with publishable contributions, but substantial revision for accessibility is recommended (would merit a major revision).
