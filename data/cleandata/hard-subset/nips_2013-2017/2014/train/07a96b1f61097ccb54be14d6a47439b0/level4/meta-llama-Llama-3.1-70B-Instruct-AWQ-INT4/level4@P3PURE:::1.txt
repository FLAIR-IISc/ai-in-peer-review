This paper addresses the problem of robustly estimating the low-dimensional subspace of contaminated observations when the observations are inherently coherent. A standard theoretical bottleneck of previous RPCA methods is that their performance deteriorates with increasing data coherence. The authors, however, cleverly circumvent this issue. Given that such cluster structure is common in realistic data, resolving this problem is certainly highly significant. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and well-demonstrated to perform effectively in practice.

I have a comment on the submission that I would like to bring to the authors' attention: Can the proposed method also be applied to solving the subspace clustering/segmentation problem? I would appreciate it if the authors could provide some comments on this point.

Overall, this paper solves an important problem in robust PCA research. The quality of the paper is very good, and I believe it should be accepted.
