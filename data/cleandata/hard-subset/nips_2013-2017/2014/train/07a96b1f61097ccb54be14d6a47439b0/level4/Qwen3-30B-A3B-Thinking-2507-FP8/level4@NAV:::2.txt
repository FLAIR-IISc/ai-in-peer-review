

Quality:  
The paper is technically sound, with reasonable validation. The two theorems establish conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary. However, the dictionary learning approach relies solely on empirical justification.  

Clarity:  
Clarity is the paper’s primary weakness. While the structure is logical, the main contributions and broader impact remain insufficiently explained for non-specialists. Claims require greater precision, and the significance of results needs explicit elaboration. For instance, the concept of coherence is poorly defined and interpreted only indirectly (via the appendix). A key potential impact—using dictionaries to circumvent prior methodological limitations—remains underdeveloped. Language also requires refinement. Authors must address these issues.  

Originality:  
The work integrates recent techniques with novel theorems and empirical validation demonstrating the combination’s utility.  

Significance:  
The paper advances the state-of-the-art through rigorous theoretical results. Despite the dictionary-learning algorithm being heuristic, clear empirical improvements over alternatives confirm the practical value of the proposed insights.  

---  
Comment after author rebuttal and reviewer discussion:  
The paper contains publishable content and meaningful contributions. The sole remaining concern is the substantial effort required to revise the paper for accessibility beyond a narrow specialist audience. A "major revision" recommendation would be appropriate if available. The work effectively combines two recent ideas, providing both theoretical results and a heuristic algorithm that performs well empirically.
