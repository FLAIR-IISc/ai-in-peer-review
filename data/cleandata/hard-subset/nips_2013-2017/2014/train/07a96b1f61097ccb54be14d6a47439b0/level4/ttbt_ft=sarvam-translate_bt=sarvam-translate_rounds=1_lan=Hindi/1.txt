This paper addresses the problem of accurately estimating the low-dimensional presence of contaminated observations when the observations are naturally consistent. Performance deteriorates as data consistency increases, which is a standard theoretical limitation of previous PCA methods. However, this paper cleverly avoids this problem. Given that such cluster structure is quite common in real data, resolving this issue is certainly important. The proposed method is theoretically sound and also well-demonstrated to perform well in practice.

I have a curious comment to make on the submission:
Could the proposed method also be used to solve the unsupervised clustering/ partitioning problem? Could the authors provide some comments on this point?

This paper addresses a significant problem in robust PCA tasks. The quality of the paper is very good and should be accepted.
