The paper proposes an innovative approach to improving the effectiveness of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) by leveraging Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A. The authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter μ, often considered a bottleneck in RPCA recovery guarantees, can actually correspond to additional clustering structure in the data. Under certain conditions on the dictionary A, they show that LRR can exploit this structure, partially removing the dependence on μ and yielding a theoretically stronger guarantee for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.

The method is novel and significant, contributing substantially to the field. It is closely related to the union-of-subspace structure assumed in previous subspace clustering papers, such as "Wang and Xu: Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC" and "Soltanolkotabi and Candes: A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers" and "Soltanolkotabi et al: Robust subspace clustering". However, this paper is less explicit about the assumption on "clustering", which is reasonable and likely more general. The simulation and real data experiments validate the theoretical analysis, and it is likely that the new structure can be found in many other real applications, such as text data, as remarked in the paper.

In summary, this paper contains a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing, and it should be accepted by NIPS.

However, I have several discussions and stylistic suggestions to improve the paper:

1. Lines 80-82 require rephrasing for clarity. The term "our data" is unclear, and even if the problem captures all the structures, there is still another condition to qualify for "perfect recovery".
2. Line 88 is unclear and requires rephrasing to convey the intended meaning.
3. The authors should proofread the paper more carefully to address issues like the above two. They should be specific and precise about their claims and references in the text. In general, the authors should work on improving the clarity of the language in the paper.
4. Wang and Xu's paper "Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering" notes that a higher coherence parameter can be beneficial for subspace clustering, unlike in RPCA and matrix completion. The key difference is that the data need not be low-rank overall, but each cluster must be low-rank. The structure exploited in this paper can be seen as a combination of low-rank structure and union-of-subspace cluster structure.
5. It would be interesting to compare the proposed algorithm against first solving noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty and then performing PCA for each subspace. Although no provable guarantee exists for the latter approach to date.
6. Section 4.2 essentially proposes a new method for solving subspace clustering problems with sparse corruption in the data. This method leads to significant improvements over the standard RPCA+SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset, which is a hidden contribution of this paper. The authors should highlight this contribution. This paper should be accepted because it contains at least three important contributions to the field: (1) describing a clustering structure that leads to high coherence, (2) demonstrating that one can implicitly exploit such structure using a dictionary in LRR, and (3) partially solving the sparse corruptions problem in subspace clustering.
