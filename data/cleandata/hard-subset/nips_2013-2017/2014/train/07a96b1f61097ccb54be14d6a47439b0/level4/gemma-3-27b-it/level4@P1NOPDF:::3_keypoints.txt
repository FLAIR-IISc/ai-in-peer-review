- Paper proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA) recovery by exploiting clustering structure reflected in the incoherence parameter μ.

- Authors show under conditions on A the dependence on μ can be partially reduced, yielding stronger theoretical guarantees for exact low-rank + sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.

- Method is novel and less restrictive than prior union-of-subspace clustering assumptions, relating to but generalizing work like Wang & Xu and Soltanolkotabi & Candès.

- Simulation and real-data experiments validate the theoretical claims; authors suggest applicability to other domains such as text data.

- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, citing a substantial contribution to compressed sensing/subspace clustering.

- Paper needs clarity improvements and careful proofreading; several sentences are incomplete or ambiguous (e.g., Lines 80–82 and Line 88).

- Specific language/claim precision and reference accuracy must be improved throughout the manuscript.

- Observation: Wang & Xu show higher coherence can benefit subspace clustering; the paper’s exploited structure combines global low-rank and union-of-subspace cluster structure.

- Suggested empirical baseline: compare against a two-step approach (noisy sparse subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty, then PCA per cluster) as a useful benchmark.

- Section 4.2 presents an important, understated new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption and outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.

- Reviewer highlights three main contributions warranting acceptance: identifying clustering-driven high coherence; exploiting it via a dictionary in LRR; and providing a partial solution to sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
