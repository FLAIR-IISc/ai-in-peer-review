- Addresses recovery of low-rank matrices when data has high coherence due to clustering, showing RPCA struggles in this regime.
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence issues.
- Theoretical result: if the dictionary is low-rank, LRR recovery guarantees become less sensitive to the coherence parameter (noiseless and noisy cases).
- Builds on RPCA and LRR literature and connects the problem to subspace clustering and robust dimensionality reduction.
- Explains why coherence arises from clustered data, providing a more general rationale than treating clustering as a separate assumption.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real motion data supports theoretical claims and shows improvements over RPCA.
- Novelty: linking coherence, clustering structure, and effectiveness of LRR with a low-rank dictionary.
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous analysis with conditions for successful recovery under noise and noiseless settings.
- Significance: broad applicability to motion analysis, image processing, and other domains where coherence is problematic.
- Hidden contribution: improved handling of sparse corruptions in subspace clustering with strong results on Hopkins155.
- Weakness: paper language sometimes lacks clarity and precision; needs careful proofreading and rewording.
- Weakness: insufficient depth in discussing differences/relationships with Wang & Xu (2014) regarding beneficial high coherence.
- Weakness: potential iterative refinement of Algorithm 1 is mentioned but not thoroughly investigated.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, practical solution to RPCA limitations, validated experimentally, and impactful hidden contribution.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues and need for a more thorough comparison to related work (especially Wang & Xu).
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with three main takeaways—clustering causes coherence, LRR with a low-rank dictionary exploits this structure, and a partial solution to sparse corruption in subspace clustering—worthy of acceptance despite minor clarity issues.
