- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA by exploiting clustering structure tied to the incoherence parameter μ.  
- Key theoretical insight: under conditions on A, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ, yielding stronger guarantees for exact low-rank+sparse recovery and its noisy extension.  
- Method is novel and significant and relates to union-of-subspaces subspace-clustering literature.  
- Paper is less explicit about the precise clustering assumption, which may be reasonable and more general than prior work.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments support the theory; the structural perspective is relevant to applications (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Revise Lines 80–82: clarify what “our data” means, explicitly state additional conditions required, and define “perfect recovery” in this setting.  
- Fix Line 88’s incomplete phrase (e.g., “perfect recovery” or “perfect separation”) and similar imprecise wording.  
- Proofread and be precise about claims and references throughout to strengthen presentation.  
- Related work note: Wang & Xu argue higher coherence can help subspace clustering; the paper’s structure combines global low-rank and union-of-subspaces viewpoints.  
- Suggest empirically comparing to a pipeline: noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace to contextualize practical gains.  
- Section 4.2 appears to propose a new subspace-clustering-with-sparse-corruptions method and should be highlighted; paper makes three main contributions: (1) characterization of clustering-induced high coherence, (2) showing LRR with a dictionary mitigates μ-dependence, and (3) a partial solution to sparse-corruptions with strong empirical results.
