- Tackles robust estimation of low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated observations with inherent data coherence.  
- Addresses a key limitation of prior RPCA methods whose performance degrades as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes an innovative method that overcomes coherence-related failures in existing approaches.  
- Problem is highly significant because cluster/coherent structures commonly occur in real-world data.  
- Method is theoretically well-founded and empirically demonstrated to be effective.  
- Reviewer asks whether the method could be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation and requests authors' insights.  
- Overall quality judged excellent and recommended for acceptance.
