- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery under gross corruption, focusing on the challenging case of coherent (clustered) data.  
- Identifies that high coherence often arises naturally from clustering structure, explaining RPCA's theoretical bottleneck.  
- Shows Low-Rank Representation (LRR) can avoid RPCA’s coherence-related failures when the LRR dictionary is low-rank and spans the true column space.  
- Proposes a practical two-stage algorithm: use RPCA for an initial low-rank estimate, then construct a dictionary for a final LRR step.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1 and 2) proving insensitivity to problematic coherence parameters in both noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Empirical synthetic experiments demonstrate a substantially expanded region of successful recovery compared to RPCA.  
- Real-world experiments on corrupted motion sequences show improved recovery and significant benefits for downstream subspace clustering.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear motivation, precise theoretical statements, and an easy-to-implement algorithm.  
- Suggests potential extension: investigate using the learned coefficient matrix Z* for affinity graph construction and direct subspace clustering.  
- Overall evaluation: novel, well-supported, practically relevant contribution; strong recommendation for acceptance.
