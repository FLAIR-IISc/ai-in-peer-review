The paper proposes to leverage Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary to enhance the performance of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA). Specifically, the authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter, often considered a limitation in RPCA recovery guarantees, can reflect underlying clustering structures within the data. Under certain conditions on the dictionary, they show that these structures can be exploited using LRR, reducing dependence on the incoherence parameter and yielding theoretically stronger guarantees for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition, and its noisy extension.

This work presents a novel and significant contribution to the field. It connects closely to prior work on union-of-subspace structures in subspace clustering, such as those by Wang and Xu ("Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering & Provable subspace clustering: When LRR meets SSC") and Soltanolkotabi and Candes ("A geometric analysis of subspace clustering with outliers") and Soltanolkotabi et al ("Robust subspace clustering"), yet provides a more general and less explicit assumption regarding “clustering,” which is a reasonable approach. The simulations and experiments with real data validate the theoretical analysis, suggesting potential applicability in diverse areas like text data, as noted by the authors.

In summary, the paper delivers a substantial contribution to compressed sensing and should be accepted to NIPS.

However, I have several points for discussion and stylistic improvement, detailed below:

1. Line 80-82: This section requires rewording for clarity. What is meant by “our data”? Furthermore, even if the method captures all relevant structures, additional conditions are still needed for “perfect recovery.”
2. Line 88: “…is no longer a method of perfection.” (Please clarify.)
3. The paper would benefit from careful proofreading for precision and specificity in its claims and references. In general, enhancing the clarity of the language is recommended.
4. Wang and Xu have shown in their “Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering” paper that higher coherence parameters can actually *benefit* subspace clustering – unlike in RPCA and matrix completion. The crucial difference is that their approach does not require overall low-rankness, only low-rankness within each cluster. The structure exploited in this paper can be viewed as a combination of global low-rank structure and a union-of-subspace cluster structure.
5. It would be interesting to compare the proposed algorithm against a pipeline involving first performing noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty, followed by applying PCA to each resulting subspace. Although currently lacking provable guarantees, this approach could serve as a valuable benchmark.
6. Section 4.2 effectively introduces a new method for addressing the subspace clustering problem with sparse corruption. The significant improvements over standard RPCA + SSC on the Hopkins155 dataset constitute a noteworthy hidden contribution of this work, which the authors should highlight. The paper merits acceptance due to its at least three important contributions: 1) characterization of a clustering structure leading to high coherence, 2) demonstration that this structure can be exploited via a dictionary in LRR, and 3) a partial solution to the sparse corruption problem in subspace clustering.
