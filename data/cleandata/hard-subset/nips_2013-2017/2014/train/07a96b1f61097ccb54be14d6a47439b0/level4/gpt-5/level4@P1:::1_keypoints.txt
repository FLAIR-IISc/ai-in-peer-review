- Tackles robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations when data are inherently coherent (e.g., clustered).  
- Identifies a key limitation of RPCA: its performance degrades as data coherence increases.  
- Proposes a principled method that circumvents this RPCA bottleneck.  
- Notes that addressing the clustered-structure regime is significant because it is common in real data.  
- Method is theoretically well founded and convincingly works in practice.  
- Asks whether the proposed approach can be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation and requests comments on this potential application.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality work and recommends acceptance.
