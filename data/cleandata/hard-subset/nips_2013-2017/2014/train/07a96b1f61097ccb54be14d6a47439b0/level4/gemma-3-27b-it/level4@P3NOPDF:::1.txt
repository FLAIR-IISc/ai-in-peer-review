This paper addresses the challenging problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from observations corrupted by outliers, particularly when those observations exhibit inherent coherence. A key limitation of existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is their performance degradation with increasing data coherence – a well-established theoretical bottleneck. This paper presents a compelling approach that cleverly circumvents this limitation. Given the prevalence of cluster structure in real-world datasets, addressing this coherence issue is both significant and practically relevant. 

The proposed method is both theoretically grounded and demonstrably effective in practice. The authors provide a solid theoretical analysis supporting their approach, and the experimental results convincingly showcase its superior performance compared to existing techniques, particularly in scenarios with high data coherence. The experimental setup appears well-designed, and the comparisons are fair and insightful. The inclusion of both synthetic and real-world datasets further strengthens the validation of the method.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it easy to follow the technical details. The motivation for the work is clearly articulated, and the contribution is well-defined. The authors effectively position their work within the existing literature on RPCA and highlight the novelty of their approach. 

I have only one question for the authors: While this work focuses on robust subspace recovery, could the proposed method also be adapted to tackle subspace clustering or segmentation problems? The inherent structure exploited by the algorithm – identifying and leveraging cluster-specific low-rank structures – seems potentially applicable to these related tasks. Any comments from the authors regarding the potential extensions or limitations in this direction would be greatly appreciated.

Overall, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of robust PCA. It addresses an important open problem, proposes a well-motivated and theoretically sound solution, and demonstrates strong empirical performance. The quality of the paper is excellent, and I recommend acceptance.
