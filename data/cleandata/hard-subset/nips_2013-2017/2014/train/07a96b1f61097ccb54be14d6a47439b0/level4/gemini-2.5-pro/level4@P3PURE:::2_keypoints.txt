- Proposal: combine robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key insight: standard robust PCA guarantees are insufficient for structured (e.g., clustered) data; a dictionary representation can yield stronger recovery bounds.  
- Theory: paper provides theorems that establish reconstruction conditions given a suitable dictionary.  
- Algorithm: dictionary is learned by first computing robust PCA and then constructing a dictionary from that result; this learning method is empirically justified but not theoretically.  
- Quality: technically sound overall, with two main theorems detailing conditions for reconstruction.  
- Limitation: the dictionary-learning algorithm lacks theoretical guarantees and is presented as a heuristic.  
- Clarity issues: main contributions, impact, and definitions (e.g., coherence) are not clearly explained and are often deferred to the appendix; manuscript needs proofreading.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of existing techniques supported by new theoretical and empirical results.  
- Significance: advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous theory and an empirically superior heuristic for dictionary-based recovery.  
- Post-rebuttal judgment: work is publishable but requires substantial revision to make it accessible beyond a narrow subfield (would recommend a major revision).
