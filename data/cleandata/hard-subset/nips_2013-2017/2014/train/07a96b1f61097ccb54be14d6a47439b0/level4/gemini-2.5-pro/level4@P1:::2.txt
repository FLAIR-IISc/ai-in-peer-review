This paper addresses the recovery of a low-rank matrix from grossly corrupted observations. The key insight is that the performance guarantees of Robust PCA (RPCA), a standard method for this task, degrade when the underlying data is "coherent"—for instance, when it exhibits clustering structures. To overcome this limitation, the authors propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR), which models the data's structure via a dictionary. The paper provides theoretical results demonstrating that with a suitable low-rank dictionary, LRR's recovery performance is immune to the coherence parameter that limits RPCA. As learning this dictionary in an unsupervised setting is a key challenge, the authors propose a practical two-stage algorithm: first, an initial estimate of the low-rank component is obtained using RPCA; second, this estimate is used to construct a dictionary for a final, more accurate recovery step with LRR.

**Quality:**
The paper appears to be technically sound. The authors present two main theorems that establish conditions for exact (noiseless case) and near-exact (noisy case) recovery, assuming a suitable dictionary is provided. These theoretical results rigorously support the central claim that LRR can handle coherent data more effectively than RPCA. While the theory assumes a given dictionary, the proposed algorithm for learning the dictionary is justified through strong empirical performance.

**Clarity:**
The primary weakness of the paper is its clarity. While the overall structure is logical, the main contributions and their broader impact are not explained clearly enough, which may limit the paper's accessibility to a narrow specialist audience. The central claims could be stated more precisely, and the implications of the theoretical results should be explicated more thoroughly. For instance, the core concept of "coherence," which is central to the paper's motivation, is difficult to grasp from the text, as its intuitive meaning is not well-developed. The paper would be significantly strengthened by better highlighting its potential impact—for example, by more clearly explaining how the proposed dictionary-based approach circumvents specific, well-understood limitations of previous methods. The prose would also benefit from language editing. It would be helpful if the authors could comment on how they might address these clarity issues in a revision.

**Originality:**
The work builds upon a combination of recent and powerful techniques, namely Robust PCA and Low-Rank Representation. Its originality lies in identifying the specific failure mode of RPCA on coherent data and proposing a novel synthesis of these methods to address it. The paper contributes new theoretical guarantees for LRR with a low-rank dictionary and provides compelling empirical results that demonstrate the value of this combination.

**Significance:**
This paper advances the state of the art with a rigorous analysis of data recovery in the presence of coherence. Although the proposed algorithm for dictionary learning is heuristic, the clear and substantial performance improvements over RPCA on both synthetic and real-world data demonstrate that the paper's core insights are significant and practically useful. The work addresses an important limitation of a widely used method and provides a foundation for other researchers to build upon.

This paper clearly contains publishable content and interesting contributions. My only remaining concern is that a significant amount of revision will be required to make the work accessible to an audience beyond a narrow group of specialists. If a "major revision" were an option, I would recommend it. The paper's strength lies in its effective combination of two recent, powerful ideas, which is supported by both novel theoretical results and a heuristic algorithm that performs very well empirically.
