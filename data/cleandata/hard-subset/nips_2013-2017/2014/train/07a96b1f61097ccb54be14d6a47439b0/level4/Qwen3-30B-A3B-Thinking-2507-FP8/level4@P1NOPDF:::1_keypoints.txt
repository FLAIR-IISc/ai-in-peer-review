- Tackles robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated, inherently coherent observations.
- Addresses a critical failure mode of standard RPCA methods, which degrade as data coherence increases.
- Proposes an approach that remains effective under high coherence, common in real-world data.
- Circumvents a well-known bottleneck in robust PCA, representing a significant advancement.
- Method is theoretically rigorous with sound theoretical guarantees.
- Empirically validated across diverse settings, showing strong practical performance.
- Technical arguments are clear, experiments are thorough, and overall work quality is excellent.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Asks authors to comment on applicability to subspace clustering/segmentation: can the framework be extended to group data into multiple subspaces, what modifications are needed, and how would coherence affect such an extension?
