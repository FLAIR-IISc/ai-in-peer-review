## Review of Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit

This paper investigates the recovery of low-rank matrices corrupted by noise, building on the foundations of Robust PCA (RPCA). The authors identify a limitation of RPCA – its performance degrades with increasing data coherence, a phenomenon often induced by clustering structures. Their key insight is that employing a carefully constructed dictionary within a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework can mitigate this issue. Specifically, they prove that a low-rank dictionary renders LRR immune to coherence parameters tied to underlying cluster number. An algorithm is proposed for dictionary learning, leveraging RPCA as a starting point, and validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound contribution, demonstrating a good understanding of relevant literature in robust matrix recovery. The theorems establishing the relationship between low-rank dictionaries and coherence are mathematically rigorous and well-stated. The proposed algorithm is a reasonable approach to the practical challenge of dictionary learning in an unsupervised setting. However, the theoretical analysis primarily focuses on noiseless scenarios; a more detailed discussion of the algorithm's behavior in the presence of noise, beyond the stated theorem, would strengthen the work. The experimental validation provides support for the claims, but a more thorough comparison to other dictionary learning techniques beyond simply using RPCA as a baseline would be beneficial.

**Clarity:** Clarity is the most significant weakness of the paper. While the overall structure is logical, the presentation suffers from dense writing and a lack of intuitive explanations. The concept of "coherence" is introduced without sufficient context for readers unfamiliar with the area, and its connection to clustering structures is not fully elucidated. The significance of avoiding the coherence parameters is not adequately emphasized, and the paper relies heavily on mathematical notation without providing sufficient narrative guidance. Improving the clarity through more accessible explanations, illustrative examples, and a more streamlined writing style would significantly enhance the paper’s impact. The extensive use of notation (e.g., numerous matrix norms) further complicates readability. Consider moving detailed definitions to an appendix.

**Originality:** The paper’s originality lies in the combination of RPCA and LRR, coupled with the theoretical justification for using low-rank dictionaries to address coherence issues. While both RPCA and LRR are established techniques, the authors’ analysis of coherence and its relationship to dictionary structure represents a novel contribution. The proposed dictionary learning algorithm, while heuristic, offers a practical approach to exploiting this theoretical insight. However, it would be helpful to more explicitly compare the approach to existing methods for handling coherence in low-rank recovery, such as weighted RPCA.

**Significance:** The results presented have the potential to be significant. Addressing the limitations of RPCA in the presence of coherent data is an important step toward more robust and accurate low-rank recovery. The proposed algorithm demonstrates practical improvements over RPCA on both synthetic and real datasets, indicating the value of the authors’ insights. The work could be influential in applications where data exhibits inherent clustering structures, such as motion segmentation, image processing, and sensor network analysis. The theoretical analysis provides a foundational understanding of the interplay between coherence, dictionary structure, and recovery performance.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important limitation of existing low-rank recovery methods.
*   The theoretical analysis provides a novel justification for using low-rank dictionaries.
*   The proposed algorithm demonstrates empirical improvements over RPCA.
*   The work has the potential to impact applications dealing with coherent data.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper’s clarity and accessibility are significantly lacking.
*   The theoretical analysis is limited to noiseless scenarios.
*   The experimental validation could be more comprehensive, including comparisons to alternative dictionary learning approaches.

**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend rejection with a strong encouragement to revise and resubmit. The paper presents a valuable contribution with solid theoretical foundations and promising empirical results. However, substantial improvements in clarity and presentation are necessary to make the work accessible to a wider audience and fully realize its potential impact. Specifically, the authors should focus on providing more intuitive explanations, streamlining the writing style, and expanding the experimental validation. If the authors are willing to undertake a significant revision to address these concerns, this work could become a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
