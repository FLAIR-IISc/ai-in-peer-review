- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery with corruptions, focusing on failures caused by data coherence from clustering.  
- Shows clustering increases coherence, which degrades RPCA performance.  
- Proposes using LRR with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence effects.  
- Proves theoretically that a low-rank dictionary makes LRR immune to the clustering-related coherence parameter.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees and experimental validation on synthetic and real motion-sequence datasets.  
- Demonstrates improved performance of the proposed method compared to RPCA.  
- Novel contribution identifying RPCA’s sensitivity to clustering-induced coherence and a principled LRR-based remedy.  
- Builds on and complements prior RPCA and LRR work, linking coherence to underlying clustering structure.  
- Related to union-of-subspace clustering literature but focuses on exploiting low-rank structure rather than direct clustering.  
- Offers multiple contributions: characterizing clustering–coherence link, leveraging dictionaries in LRR, and improving sparse-corruption removal in subspace clustering.  
- Experimental results are convincing and support theoretical claims.  
- Writing has clarity issues in some sentences/phrases and needs careful proofreading.  
- Would benefit from a deeper, more nuanced comparison to related work (e.g., Wang & Xu on coherence benefits).  
- Lacks detailed discussion and analysis of algorithmic computational complexity relative to RPCA.  
- Suggests potential extension: iterative refinement of the dictionary (Algorithm 1) could improve results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial theoretical and practical contribution, compelling experiments, and additional subspace-clustering advances.  
- Arguments against acceptance: writing clarity and insufficiently nuanced related-work discussion.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Detailed revision suggestions: rephrase ambiguous lines (80–82, 88), expand related-work distinctions, provide complexity analysis for Algorithm 1, and emphasize Section 4.2’s significance.
