

The paper introduces a novel approach leveraging Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary \( \mathbf{A} \) to enhance Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA). The authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter \( \mu \)—typically viewed as a limiting factor in RPCA recovery guarantees—can correspond to inherent clustering structure in the data. Under specific conditions on \( \mathbf{A} \), they show that LRR can exploit this structure to partially eliminate dependence on \( \mu \), yielding theoretically stronger guarantees for both exact low-rank and sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.  

The contribution is significant and novel. While related to union-of-subspace clustering frameworks (e.g., Wang & Xu, Soltanolkotabi & Candes, Soltanolkotabi et al.), the paper more explicitly identifies the clustering structure underlying high coherence, offering a more general perspective. Simulation and real-data experiments validate the theory, and the proposed structure likely applies broadly (e.g., in text data, as noted).  

In summary, the paper makes a substantial contribution to compressed sensing and should be accepted. However, the following revisions are recommended:  
1. Lines 80–82: Clarify ambiguous references (e.g., "our data") and explicitly state the additional condition required for perfect recovery.  
2. Line 88: Correct the incomplete phrase ("is no longer a method of perfect [recovery]").  
3. Improve overall precision: Ensure all technical claims (e.g., "perfect recovery," "coherence") are unambiguously defined and contextualized.  
4. Address Wang & Xu’s observation: Note that higher coherence is beneficial for subspace clustering (unlike RPCA), as their work assumes low-rank clusters rather than global low rank. The paper’s method combines low-rank and union-of-subspace structures.  
5. Compare against a two-stage approach (noisy subspace clustering followed by per-subspace PCA); while no provable guarantees exist for this alternative, such a comparison would strengthen the argument.  
6. Highlight Section 4.2’s hidden contribution: The proposed method significantly outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155 for subspace clustering with sparse corruption. The paper should explicitly emphasize this as a key contribution alongside the three core advances: (i) linking coherence to clustering structure, (ii) using LRR with dictionary \( \mathbf{A} \) to exploit this structure, and (iii) advancing subspace clustering with sparse corruption.
