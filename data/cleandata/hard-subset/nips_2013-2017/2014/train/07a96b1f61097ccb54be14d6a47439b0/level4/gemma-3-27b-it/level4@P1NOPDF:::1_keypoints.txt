- Addresses robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations that are inherently coherent.
- Identifies a standard theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods: performance degrades with increasing data coherence.
- Proposes a clever method that circumvents the coherence-related limitation.
- Notes that clustered/coherent structure is common in real-world data, making this resolution significant and valuable.
- Method is both theoretically well-founded and empirically effective.
- Asks whether the proposed method could be applied to subspace clustering or segmentation problems.
- Concludes the paper tackles an important problem, is high quality, and should be accepted.
