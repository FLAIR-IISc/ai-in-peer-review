- Introduces using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA recovery.
- Argues the incoherence parameter μ can reflect an underlying clustering structure in the data.
- Proves that under certain conditions on A, LRR can mitigate dependence on μ, yielding stronger theoretical guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank+sparse decomposition.
- Novel contribution, conceptually related to union-of-subspaces literature but assuming a more general and reasonable clustering property.
- Simulations and real-data experiments support the theoretical results; authors suggest applicability to other domains (e.g., text).
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance at NIPS.
- Clarity issue: Lines 80–82 use ambiguous phrasing ("our data") and overstate "perfect recovery" without listing necessary conditions.
- Edit needed: incomplete sentence at Line 88.
- Manuscript needs thorough proofreading and more precise claims and definitions throughout.
- Suggested connection to Wang & Xu: higher coherence can aid subspace clustering; contrast that prior work requires cluster-wise low rank while this paper blends global low-rank and local union-of-subspaces structure.
- Proposed experimental extension: compare to a two-stage baseline (noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1, then PCA per subspace), noting that baseline lacks provable guarantees.
- Section 4.2 effectively presents a new subspace-clustering algorithm robust to sparse corruptions with significant gains on Hopkins155 and should be emphasized.
- Summarized key contributions: characterizing clustering-induced high coherence; showing LRR with a learned dictionary exploits this structure; providing a partial solution to sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
