- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA by exploiting clustering structure and reducing dependence on the incoherence parameter μ.  
- Shows theoretically that under conditions on A, LRR gives stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank plus sparse decomposition by leveraging union-of-subspace structure.  
- Novel contribution that builds on subspace-clustering literature while making less prescriptive clustering assumptions and offering greater generality.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical analysis; authors note potential applicability to other domains (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Manuscript needs careful proofreading and clearer, more precise language; several phrases/statements are vague or incomplete (e.g., “our data” in lines 80–82).  
- Complete the unfinished sentence at line 88 about whether the method is one of “perfect recovery/decomposition.”  
- Clarify claims and references throughout; be specific and precise in theoretical and empirical statements.  
- Distinguish from Wang & Xu’s work: high coherence can aid subspace clustering there because they assume per-cluster low-rankness rather than global low-rankness; this paper combines global low-rank and union-of-subspace structures.  
- Suggests comparing to a two-stage baseline: noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA on each identified subspace.  
- Section 4.2 effectively introduces a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption and should be highlighted; paper’s three key contributions are (1) characterizing clustering-induced high coherence, (2) showing how a dictionary in LRR can exploit this, and (3) providing a partial solution to sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
