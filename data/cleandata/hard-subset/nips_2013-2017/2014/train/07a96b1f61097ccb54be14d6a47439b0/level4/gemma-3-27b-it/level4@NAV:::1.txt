This paper tackles the challenging problem of robust low-rank subspace recovery from data corrupted by outliers, specifically addressing scenarios where the underlying data exhibits inherent coherence. A key limitation of existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) techniques is their performance degradation with increasing data coherence; this work elegantly overcomes this theoretical bottleneck. Given the prevalence of clustered data structures in real-world applications, resolving this issue represents a substantial contribution. The proposed approach is rigorously supported by theoretical analysis and convincingly validated through empirical results.

I have a question regarding potential extensions of this work: could the methodology be adapted to address subspace clustering or segmentation problems? The authors’ insights on this possibility would be valuable. 

Overall, this paper presents a significant advancement in robust PCA. The quality of the work is excellent and I recommend acceptance to the conference.
