- Tackles robust low-rank matrix recovery under corruption when data coherence (from clustering) degrades RPCA performance.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully designed low-rank dictionary to circumvent RPCA’s coherence limitation.  
- Proves that a low-rank dictionary makes LRR immune to the coherence parameter that grows with the number of clusters.  
- Derives a practical unsupervised dictionary learning algorithm based on this theory.  
- Provides strong empirical validation on synthetic and real-world motion sequence data, outperforming RPCA.  
- Addresses a meaningful, widely occurring problem (clustered/coherent data) with significant potential impact.  
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous and central to the paper’s claims.  
- The algorithm makes the theoretical insights actionable in practice.  
- Paper is clearly and concisely written, with accessible technical exposition.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.  
- Weakness: dictionary learning relies on an initial RPCA estimate; sensitivity to that initialization and noise isn’t thoroughly explored.  
- Weakness: focus is mainly on clustering-induced coherence; other sources of coherence aren’t evaluated.  
- For acceptance: solid theoretical and empirical improvements over RPCA, addressing a key limitation relevant to the community.  
- Against acceptance: potential sensitivity of dictionary learning and limited complexity analysis could hinder practical impact.  
- Suggestion: comment on applicability or advantages of the dictionary learning strategy for related tasks like subspace clustering/segmentation.  
- Overall: high-quality work with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
