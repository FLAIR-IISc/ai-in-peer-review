- Addresses robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated, inherently coherent observations.
- Overcomes a standard RPCA bottleneck where performance deteriorates as data coherence increases.
- Authors present a clever approach to circumvent the coherence issue.
- Resolving coherence-related failures is significant because cluster structure is common in real data.
- Proposed method is theoretically sound and performs well empirically.
- Reviewer asks whether the method can be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation and requests author comment.
- Overall assessment: solves an important RPCA problem, high-quality work, recommended for acceptance.
