This paper presents a novel approach to recover coherent data via low-rank dictionary pursuit, combining robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection. The authors' key insight is that for structured data, the guarantees of robust PCA are not strong enough, and by representing the structure with a dictionary, stronger bounds can be given. The paper provides theorems to support these claims and proposes an algorithm for learning the dictionary, which is justified by empirical results.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the two theorems elaborate conditions under which reconstruction is possible, given a dictionary. The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their theoretical results.

Clarity: While the structure of the paper is clear, the main contributions and impact of the paper could be explained more clearly to be accessible beyond a very narrow specialist audience. The authors could provide more well-defined claims and explicate the impact of their results. For instance, the concept of coherence is hard to understand, and means of interpreting it are very indirect.

Originality: The paper is based on a combination of recent techniques but includes new theorems and empirical results supporting the usefulness of the combination. The authors' approach to learning the dictionary is novel and contributes to the field.

Significance: The paper advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results. Although the proposed algorithm for finding the dictionary is heuristic, clear improvements over alternative algorithms demonstrate that the insights given in this paper are useful.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a novel approach to recover coherent data via low-rank dictionary pursuit.
- The authors provide theorems to support their claims and propose an algorithm for learning the dictionary.
- The paper advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results.

Weaknesses:
- The paper could be more accessible to a broader audience with clearer explanations of the main contributions and impact.
- The concept of coherence is hard to understand and could be explained more clearly.
- The language needs checking.
