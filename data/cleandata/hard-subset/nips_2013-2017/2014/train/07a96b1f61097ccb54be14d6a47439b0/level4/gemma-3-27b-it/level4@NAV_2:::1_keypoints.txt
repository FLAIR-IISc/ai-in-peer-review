- Tackles robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from coherent, contaminated observations where RPCA degrades with increased coherence.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary to neutralize coherence-related limitations.  
- Proves theoretically that a low-rank dictionary makes LRR immune to the coherence parameter that grows with the number of clusters.  
- Provides a practical, unsupervised algorithm that leverages RPCA to construct suitable dictionaries for LRR.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic data and real motion sequences, showing superiority over RPCA.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized, making the arguments easy to follow.  
- Strength: addresses an important, common real-world issue (clustered/coherent data) in robust PCA.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the proposed approach.  
- Strength: practical algorithm design with strong experimental support.  
- Weakness: limited, brief discussion of computational cost—needs more detailed analysis and RPCA comparison.  
- Weakness: mentions iterative dictionary refinement but does not explore or evaluate its benefits.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically grounded solution with convincing experiments and clear writing.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient computational cost analysis and underexplored iterative refinement options.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.  
- Question for authors: can the method be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation; please comment on applicability.
