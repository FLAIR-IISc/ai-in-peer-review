- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA) recovery by leveraging latent clustering structure.  
- Key insight: the incoherence parameter μ reflects cluster structure and can be mitigated by an appropriate dictionary, yielding stronger theoretical guarantees.  
- Novelty: connects robust low-rank modeling to union-of-subspaces/subspace-clustering literature while being less explicit about clustering assumptions.  
- Theory: provides exact recovery and noisy-case guarantees under conditions on A, reducing dependence on μ.  
- Empirics: simulations and real-data experiments support the theory and show strong performance, including on Hopkins155.  
- Suggestion: rewrite ambiguous sentences (e.g., “our data”) and state precise conditions required for perfect/exact recovery.  
- Fix incomplete phrasing (line 88) to explicitly say “perfect recovery” or “exact recovery.”  
- Overall writing needs careful proofreading, clearer claims, and precise descriptions of what is recovered and how A is constructed/learned.  
- Related work: explicitly contrast with Wang & Xu and SSC results showing high coherence can aid clustering, and clarify differing assumptions and guarantees.  
- Empirical baseline: recommend comparing to a two-stage pipeline (noisy SSC with ℓ1, then PCA per cluster) to contextualize benefits.  
- Highlight Section 4.2 more prominently: it effectively introduces a new subspace-clustering method handling sparse corruption and outperforms RPCA+SSC.  
- Three major advances to emphasize: explains high coherence via clustering, exploits this via dictionary-based LRR to relax μ, and partially solves subspace clustering with sparse corruptions.  
- Clarify assumptions on A: how it is obtained, required properties (alignment, rank, incoherence), sensitivity to violations, and whether A can be learned from corrupted data.  
- Practical guidance: give recommendations for selecting/learning A, discuss computational cost vs RPCA/SSC, and provide ablations on dictionary quality.  
- Noisy setting: specify the noise model and how bounds scale with noise level, rank, sparsity, and μ, and provide theorem-to-interpretation mapping.  
- Terminology: clearly distinguish “coherence,” “clustering structure,” and “union-of-subspaces” to avoid conflating global and local structure.  
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper makes a substantial contribution bridging robust low-rank modeling and subspace clustering, with strong theoretical and empirical results.
