- Proposes using LRR with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA, exploiting clustering structure to reduce dependence on incoherence μ and giving stronger recovery guarantees for exact and noisy cases.

- Novel and significant; related to prior subspace-clustering work (Wang & Xu; Soltanolkotabi & Candes) but assumes a less explicit, arguably more general clustering structure.

- Theoretical analysis is solid and supported by simulations and real-data experiments; identified structure likely present in other domains (e.g., text).

- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, considering the paper a substantial contribution.

- Clarify lines 80–82: ambiguous term "our data" and state additional conditions required for perfect recovery.

- Fix wording at line 88: phrase "...is no longer a method of perfect" is missing a word.

- Paper requires careful proofreading and more precise claims and references to improve clarity.

- Relate finding that high coherence can be beneficial to Wang & Xu’s "Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering"; key distinction is global low-rank vs. per-cluster low-rank assumptions.

- Suggest comparing to a two-stage baseline: noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per identified subspace (theoretical guarantees for this are currently lacking).

- Emphasize Section 4.2 as a major contribution: a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruptions that outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.

- Recommend explicitly stating three main contributions: 1) identification of clustering causing high coherence; 2) showing LRR with a suitable dictionary can exploit it; 3) a novel partial solution to sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
