- Addresses RPCA’s failure on coherent/clustering data, demonstrating performance degradation as coherence increases (Fig. 1).  
- Key insight: Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly designed dictionary can mitigate coherence issues.  
- Shows that if the dictionary is low-rank and contains the true column space (U₀ ⊂ U_A), LRR avoids dependence on the coherence parameter μ₂.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for both noiseless and noisy recovery scenarios.  
- Theoretical advantage: rank constraint in Theorem 1 is looser than RPCA’s (rank(L₀) ≤ O(n₂/log n₁) vs RPCA’s O(n₂/(log n₁)²)).  
- Proposes a practical, computationally efficient dictionary-learning algorithm (Algorithm 1) for unsupervised settings.  
- Empirical gains: synthetic experiments show a 47% wider success area; Hopkins155 motion sequences show ~40% relative improvement in clustering error.  
- Well-positioned within prior work, building on and extending LRR and robust subspace clustering literature.  
- Novelty: first systematic analysis showing dictionary design can address coherence in low-rank recovery.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental RPCA limitation and offers broadly applicable theoretical and practical solutions (including potential text-data applicability).  
- Quality: strong theoretical results supported by comprehensive experiments and careful consideration of strengths/limitations.  
- Clarity issues: some imprecise wording (e.g., “RPCA is no longer a method of perfection”) that should be clarified.  
- Needs clearer explanation of differences from previous union-of-subspaces/subspace-clustering approaches.  
- Should include an explicit comparison to pipelines that perform ℓ₁-based subspace clustering followed by PCA on clusters.  
- Dictionary-learning approach correctly targeted at unsupervised scenarios where U₀ is unknown.  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial contribution with minor clarity fixes suggested for camera-ready.  
- Suggestion: explicitly highlight subspace clustering as a significant secondary application to strengthen impact.
