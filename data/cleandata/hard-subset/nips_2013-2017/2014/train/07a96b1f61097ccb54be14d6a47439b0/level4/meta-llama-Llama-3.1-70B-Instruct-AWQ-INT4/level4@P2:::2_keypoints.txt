- Proposes combining RPCA with dictionary-based LRR to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, targeting coherent data.
- Claims RPCA is insufficient for coherent data and that LRR offers better guarantees when the dictionary is well chosen.
- Paper is technically sound, with two theorems giving conditions for reconstruction given a dictionary.
- Proposes a heuristic algorithm to learn the dictionary, supported by empirical results.
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on coherent data and clear improvements over alternative algorithms (including RPCA).
- Main contributions and impact are not well explained, making the paper hard to follow for non-specialists.
- The concept of coherence is difficult to understand; explanations are indirect and mainly in the appendix.
- Language quality is poor and needs improvement for readability.
- Originality lies in combining recent techniques, providing new theorems, and empirical validation that advance the state-of-the-art.
- Recommendation: major revision to improve clarity, provide intuitive explanations of technical concepts and contributions, and improve language and accessibility.
