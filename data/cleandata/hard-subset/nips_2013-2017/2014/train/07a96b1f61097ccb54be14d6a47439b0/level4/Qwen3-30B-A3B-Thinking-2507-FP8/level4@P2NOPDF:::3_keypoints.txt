- Introduces LRR with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA recovery by leveraging clustering structure.  
- Shows the incoherence parameter μ can encode underlying clustering, reducing its constraining role in recovery guarantees.  
- Proves that under mild conditions on A, LRR weakens dependence on μ for exact and noisy low-rank/sparse decomposition.  
- Bridges RPCA and subspace clustering by formalizing how clustering structure aids robust recovery.  
- Clarifies that data need not be globally low-rank—only each cluster must be low-rank.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real data (including text) strongly supports the theory.  
- Delivers three main contributions: characterization of high μ as clustering indicator, dictionary-based LRR to exploit it, and practical improvement in corrupted subspace clustering.  
- Demonstrates superior performance on Hopkins155 compared to RPCA+SSC, showing real-world applicability.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and the connection to clustering structure is novel.  
- Writing is clear overall but requires minor clarity and precision fixes (e.g., replace vague "our data" and correct incomplete phrase on Line 88).  
- Recommend emphasizing distinction from prior work that views high μ as beneficial for clustering and stressing the combination of local low-rankness with global clustering.  
- Suggest a brief comparative discussion to ℓ1-regularized methods followed by per-cluster PCA, noting lack of provable guarantees for that approach.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, well-executed contribution with immediate utility for NIPS; merits acceptance after minor revisions.
