- Studies robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from grossly corrupted observations when data are intrinsically coherent.  
- Identifies performance degradation of prior RPCA methods as coherence increases.  
- Introduces a clever method that circumvents the coherence-related limitation.  
- Notes the significance because clustered/coherent structures are common in real-world data.  
- Presents a theoretically well-grounded approach.  
- Empirically validates the method convincingly.  
- Asks whether the method can be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation and requests comment on that potential use.  
- Recommends acceptance, calling the paper high quality.
