1. Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA recovery by exploiting clustering structure in data.  
2. Shows that, under conditions on A, LRR can reduce dependence on the incoherence parameter μ and give stronger exact and noisy decomposition guarantees.  
3. The idea is novel, impactful, and related to prior union-of-subspaces/subspace-clustering work (e.g., Wang & Xu, Soltanolkotabi & Candès).  
4. The paper is less explicit about the precise “clustering” assumption; this should be stated more clearly.  
5. Simulations and real-data experiments support the theoretical claims and suggest the structure appears in practical settings (e.g., text).  
6. Recommends acceptance to NIPS due to substantial contribution to compressed sensing.  
7. Needs clarification of Lines 80–82: define “our data” and note that an extra condition is required for certifying perfect recovery.  
8. Correct/complete the incomplete statement at Line 88 (“… is no longer a method of perfect (WHAT?)”).  
9. Requires careful proofreading and more precise claims and references throughout the text.  
10. Notes Wang & Xu’s observation that larger coherence can benefit subspace clustering and positions this work as blending global low-rank and union-of-subspaces structure.  
11. Suggests comparing to a baseline that does noisy ℓ1-based subspace clustering followed by PCA within each subspace, despite lack of formal guarantees for the second stage.  
12. Highlights Section 4.2 as effectively introducing a new subspace-clustering method robust to sparse corruptions that significantly outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.  
13. Summarizes the paper’s three main contributions: (1) articulating clustering-induced high coherence, (2) showing LRR with a dictionary can exploit this, and (3) partially addressing sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
