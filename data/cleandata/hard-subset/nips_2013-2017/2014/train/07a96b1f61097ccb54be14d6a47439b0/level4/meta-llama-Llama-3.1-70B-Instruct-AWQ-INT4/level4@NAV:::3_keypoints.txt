1. Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA recovery.  
2. Argues the incoherence parameter μ can indicate underlying clustering structure rather than being only a recovery bottleneck.  
3. Proves that under conditions on A, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ, yielding stronger guarantees for exact low-rank/sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.  
4. Builds on union-of-subspace clustering literature but is more general and less explicit about the clustering assumption.  
5. Simulation and real-data experiments validate the theory and demonstrate applicability (e.g., text data).  
6. Overall assessment: substantial contribution to compressed sensing/subspace clustering and worthy of acceptance.  
7. Writing concerns: ambiguous phrasing at lines 80–82, unclear line 88, and general need for careful proofreading and clearer, more precise claims.  
8. Relates to Wang & Xu: higher coherence can help subspace clustering because data need only be low-rank per cluster, not globally.  
9. Suggests empirically comparing to a two-step baseline: noisy subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace.  
10. Section 4.2 introduces a method for sparse-corrupted subspace clustering that significantly outperforms RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155 and should be emphasized.  
11. Summarizes three central contributions: identifying clustering-driven high coherence, exploiting it via a dictionary in LRR, and partially addressing sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
