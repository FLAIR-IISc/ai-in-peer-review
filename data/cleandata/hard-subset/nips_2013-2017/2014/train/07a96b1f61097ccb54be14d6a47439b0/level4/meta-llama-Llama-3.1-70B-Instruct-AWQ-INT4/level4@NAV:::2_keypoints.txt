- Integrates robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projections to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key insight: robust PCA guarantees are insufficient for structured data; representing structure with a dictionary yields stronger bounds.  
- Provides two theorems that give conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary.  
- Dictionary learning remains unresolved; authors propose an empirical algorithm: run robust PCA, then represent the result with a dictionary.  
- Technical quality is sound; theorems and empirical support are reasonably checked.  
- Clarity is lacking: main contributions, impact, and claims are not clearly explained or interpreted.  
- The concept of coherence is difficult to follow and mainly explained indirectly in the appendix.  
- Language and exposition need refinement; substantial rewriting required for broader specialist accessibility.  
- Originality: novel combination of recent techniques with new theoretical and empirical results.  
- Significance: advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results and an effective heuristic, but a major revision is recommended for clarity.
