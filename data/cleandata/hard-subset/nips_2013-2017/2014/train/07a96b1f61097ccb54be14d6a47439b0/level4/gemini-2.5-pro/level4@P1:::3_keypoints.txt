- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery from gross sparse errors and links RPCA's limiting incoherence parameter µ to underlying data clustering.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary to exploit clustering and improve recovery.  
- Shows theoretically that, under conditions on the dictionary, the approach can partially remove dependence on µ, yielding stronger guarantees for noiseless and noisy data.  
- Presents a practical two-stage algorithm: RPCA for an initial estimate, then construct a dictionary and run LRR.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real motion data support the theoretical claims.  
- Framework is relatively general, treating "clustering" more broadly than strict union-of-subspaces models.  
- Strong paper with significant contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Novel insight formally connecting coherence issues in RPCA to real-data clustering structures.  
- Principled theoretical demonstration that LRR with a proper dictionary can be immune to the µ2 coherence parameter.  
- Demonstrates a new state-of-the-art preprocessing method for subspace clustering on corrupted data (notably Hopkins155).  
- Suggests careful proofreading to improve clarity and fix ambiguous/incomplete phrasing.  
- Recommends a more detailed discussion linking to subspace clustering literature (e.g., Wang & Xu) and clarifying differences between global low-rank vs. union-of-subspaces perspectives.  
- Recommends adding an empirical baseline: robust subspace clustering to identify clusters followed by per-cluster PCA, for comparison.
