- Presents a novel theoretical and methodological advance: using dictionary-learned LRR to improve robust matrix decomposition.  
- Core insight: the incoherence parameter μ corresponds to inherent clustering structure, which LRR can exploit to relax μ’s role in recovery guarantees.  
- Proves that under mild conditions on dictionary A, LRR can partially eliminate μ’s dependence in exact and noisy recovery, yielding stronger guarantees than RPCA.  
- Builds on subspace clustering literature (e.g., Wang & Xu, Soltanolkotabi & Candes) and clarifies that explicit clustering structure—not just low-rankness—enables μ relaxation.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real data (including Hopkins155 and text) robustly supports the theory and shows likely generalization to domains like document clustering or sensor networks.  
- Strength: substantial theoretical contribution reframing μ through a clustering lens and providing a new pathway for robust decomposition.  
- Strength: elegantly connects and resolves ambiguities in subspace clustering literature about coherence benefiting clustering but hurting RPCA.  
- Strength: Hopkins155 experiments show practical breakthrough—LRR with dictionary significantly outperforms RPCA+SSC on sparse-corrupted subspace clustering.  
- Minor issue: “our data” (lines 80–82) is ambiguous and should specify the dataset or context; claims that “the problem captures all structures” need qualification.  
- Minor issue: typo/unclear phrase at line 88 (“is no longer a method of perfect (WHAT?)”) — clarify as “method for perfect recovery” and align with theorem conditions.  
- Minor issue: general proofreading required; replace vague terms (e.g., “some”, “often”) and explicitly state how μ’s dependence is reduced (e.g., reduction in exponent in recovery bound).  
- Suggestion: explicitly contrast the paper’s clustering-RPCA link with Wang & Xu, emphasizing the distinct dictionary-based mechanism that bypasses μ constraints.  
- Suggestion: emphasize Section 4.2 / Hopkins155 as a major standalone advance showing partial solution to the gap in provable two-step approaches.  
- Suggestion: discuss why intuitive two-step methods (noisy SSC → per-subspace PCA) are suboptimal and argue that LRR+dictionary gives both theoretical guarantees and better empirical performance.  
- Conclusion: makes three high-impact contributions—(1) theoretical (μ–clustering link), (2) methodological (dictionary-based LRR), (3) practical (state-of-the-art sparse-corrupted subspace clustering)—and is recommended for acceptance with minor clarifications.
