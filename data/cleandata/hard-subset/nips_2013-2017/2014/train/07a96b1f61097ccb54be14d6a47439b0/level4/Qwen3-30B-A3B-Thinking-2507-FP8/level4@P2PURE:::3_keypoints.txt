- Introduces Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA) recovery.  
- Shows the incoherence parameter μ can reflect inherent clustering (union-of-subspaces) structure in data.  
- Proves that, under conditions on A, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ, giving stronger guarantees for exact low-rank/sparse decomposition and noisy cases.  
- Builds on prior subspace-clustering work but explicitly characterizes how clustering enables high coherence, a more general and reasonable viewpoint.  
- Simulation and real-data experiments validate the theoretical analysis and suggest applicability to other domains (e.g., text).  
- Recommends acceptance, calling the work significant, novel, and a substantial contribution to compressed sensing.
