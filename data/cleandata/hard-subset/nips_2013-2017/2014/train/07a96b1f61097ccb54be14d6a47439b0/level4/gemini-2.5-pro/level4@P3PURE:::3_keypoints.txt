1. Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary to improve Robust PCA (RPCA).

2. Key insight: the incoherence parameter μ can reflect an underlying clustering structure in the data.

3. Under conditions on the dictionary A, LRR can exploit this structure to partially remove dependence on μ, yielding stronger theoretical recovery guarantees for exact and noisy decompositions.

4. The method is novel and a significant contribution to robust low-rank matrix recovery.

5. Closely related to union-of-subspaces/subspace clustering literature, but uses a less explicit (potentially more general) clustering assumption.

6. Theoretical analysis is supported by simulations and real-data experiments; applicability to other domains (e.g., text) is plausible.

7. Reviewer recommends acceptance.

8. Writing issue: Line 80–82 uses ambiguous phrasing ("our data") and overstates "perfect recovery" without necessary conditions.

9. Typographical/incomplete sentence at Line 88 needs fixing and clarification of what is not perfectly recovered.

10. Overall need for careful proofreading, precise claims, and explicit references.

11. Related work note: Wang & Xu observed high coherence can aid subspace clustering; difference is their per-cluster low-rank assumption versus this paper’s combined global low-rank plus cluster structure.

12. Suggests empirical comparison to a two-stage baseline (noisy subspace clustering with L1 penalty, then PCA) despite lack of provable guarantees.

13. Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new subspace-clustering method robust to sparse corruptions and shows strong improvements over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.

14. Three emphasized contributions: (1) identifying clustering as a cause of high coherence, (2) showing LRR with a learned dictionary can exploit this, and (3) providing a new effective approach to subspace clustering with sparse corruptions.
