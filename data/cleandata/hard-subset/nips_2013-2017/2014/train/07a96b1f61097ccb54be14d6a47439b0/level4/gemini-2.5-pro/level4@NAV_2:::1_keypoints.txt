- Problem: Recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, coherent data where RPCA performance degrades due to clustering-induced coherence.  
- Key observation: RPCA fails as data coherence (µ2) increases, a common issue with clustered/subspace-structured data.  
- Main contribution: Show theoretically and empirically that LRR with a low-rank dictionary whose column space contains the true low-rank data overcomes µ2-related failures.  
- Theoretical result: If the dictionary’s column space contains that of the true low-rank component, LRR recovery guarantees become independent of the problematic µ2 parameter.  
- Practical algorithm: Propose a two-stage method—use RPCA to get an initial low-rank estimate, then build a dictionary from it and run LRR for final recovery.  
- Experiments: Strong validation via synthetic phase-transition plots (larger successful recovery region) and real corrupted motion-sequence experiments (reduced clustering error).  
- Significance: Addresses a well-known RPCA limitation relevant to many applications like motion segmentation.  
- Originality/quality: Novel link between coherence, clustering, and LRR dictionary properties; sound theoretical analysis and an elegant, intuitive algorithm.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and clearly explains the µ2-phenomenon and motivation.  
- Minor weakness 1: Could comment on whether the final LRR coefficient matrix Z* can be used directly for integrated robust subspace clustering.  
- Minor weakness 2: Algorithm depends on a reasonably good RPCA initialization; discussion of failure modes under extreme coherence/corruption would be useful.  
- Overall verdict: High-quality, significant, and well-validated work; recommend acceptance.
