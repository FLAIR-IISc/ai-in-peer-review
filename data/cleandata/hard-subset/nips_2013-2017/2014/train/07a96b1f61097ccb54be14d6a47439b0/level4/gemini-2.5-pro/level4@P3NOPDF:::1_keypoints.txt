- Addresses robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from observations with sparse, gross errors, even when basis vectors are coherent.  
- Overcomes the known RPCA performance degradation caused by increasing data coherence.  
- Tackles a practically and theoretically important problem since coherent/clustered data are common in real applications.  
- Proposes a novel method that offers a new direction for handling coherent subspaces.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical recovery guarantees that do not worsen with increased coherence.  
- Empirically validated with comprehensive synthetic and real-data experiments showing robustness where prior methods fail, with fair comparisons.  
- Asks whether the learned representations could be extended to subspace clustering—e.g., to build affinity matrices for segmentation—and requests discussion of applicability, modifications, and challenges.  
- Overall assessment: original, technically strong, well-presented work with compelling results; strongly recommended for acceptance.
