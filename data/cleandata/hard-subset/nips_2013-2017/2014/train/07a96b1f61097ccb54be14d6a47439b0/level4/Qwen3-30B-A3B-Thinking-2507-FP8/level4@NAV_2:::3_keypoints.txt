- Paper studies RPCA limitations on coherent, cluster-structured data and proposes using LRR with a learned dictionary A to address them.  
- Theoretical claim: when the dictionary A is low-rank, LRR can mitigate coherence parameters (especially μ2 and μ3) that degrade RPCA as cluster count grows.  
- Extends prior NIPS work on RPCA and LRR and ties into subspace clustering literature.  
- Contribution 1: identifies how clustering structures increase coherence parameters.  
- Contribution 2: proves conditions under which LRR with a low-rank dictionary overcomes coherence limitations.  
- Contribution 3: presents a practical unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm that outperforms RPCA.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and motion sequences validate the theory and show significant improvements over RPCA for clustered data.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and offers meaningful insights into data structure and recovery guarantees.  
- The proposed algorithm is practical and demonstrates empirical effectiveness in both synthetic and real-world settings.  
- Work is relevant to applications like motion segmentation where coherent data structures occur.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed.  
- Presentation issue: lines 80–82 are unclear about what “our data” refers to and the precise recovery conditions.  
- Presentation issue: line 88 contains an incomplete phrase (“is no longer a method of perfect (WHAT?)”).  
- Literature/context: should better highlight connections to Wang and Xu on noisy sparse subspace clustering and more explicitly position relative to union-of-subspace work.  
- Overall assessment: substantial technical merit with clear theoretical and practical implications; recommended for acceptance.
