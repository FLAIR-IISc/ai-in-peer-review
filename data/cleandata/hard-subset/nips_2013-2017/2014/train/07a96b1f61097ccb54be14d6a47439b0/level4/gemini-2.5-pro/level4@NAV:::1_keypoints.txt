- Tackles robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from corrupted observations in the challenging, highly coherent data regime.
- Notes a known limitation: conventional RPCA performance degrades as data coherence increases.
- Introduces an ingenious approach to circumvent this longstanding coherence-related bottleneck.
- Emphasizes practical significance since clustered/coherent data structures commonly occur in real-world applications.
- Methodology is theoretically well-founded.
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Asks whether the framework can be extended or applied to subspace clustering and segmentation.
- Concludes the paper is a high-quality, strong contribution that warrants acceptance.
