This paper integrates two recent techniques, robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection, to restore corrupted observation matrices. The key insight is that for structured data (e.g., clustered data), the guarantees of robust PCA are insufficient, and representing the structure with a dictionary can provide stronger bounds. The authors support these claims with theorems, addressing the theoretical foundation of the approach. However, learning the dictionary remains a challenge, and the authors propose an algorithm, justified by empirical results, which first computes robust PCA and then represents the result using a dictionary.

Quality:
The paper is technically sound, with a reasonable level of checking. The two theorems provide conditions under which reconstruction is possible, given a dictionary. While the justification for dictionary learning is empirical, the overall technical framework is well-established.

Clarity:
The primary issue with the paper is its clarity. Although the structure is clear, the main contributions and impact are not explained clearly enough to be accessible to a broad audience beyond specialists. The claims require more precise definition, and the impact of the results needs to be explicated. For instance, the concept of coherence is difficult to understand, and its interpretation is indirect and relegated to the appendix. One potential impact of the paper is that using dictionaries may sidestep earlier problems. Additionally, the language requires checking.

A comment from the authors on addressing these issues would be beneficial.

Originality:
The paper combines recent techniques and presents new theorems and empirical results that support the usefulness of this combination.

Significance:
The paper advances the state-of-the-art with rigorous results. Although the proposed algorithm for finding the dictionary is heuristic, it demonstrates clear improvements over alternative algorithms, showing that the insights provided in the paper are valuable.

Comment after the author rebuttal and reviewer discussion:
The paper has publishable content and interesting contributions. My only remaining concern is that rewriting the paper to make it accessible to a broader audience will be a significant undertaking. If a "major revision" option were available, I would recommend it. The paper successfully integrates two recent ideas, providing both theoretical results and a heuristic yet empirically well-performing algorithm.
