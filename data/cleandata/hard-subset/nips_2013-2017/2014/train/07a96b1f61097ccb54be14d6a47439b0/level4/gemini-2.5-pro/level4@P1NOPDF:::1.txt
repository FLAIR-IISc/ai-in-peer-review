This paper addresses the challenge of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations, particularly when the data is highly coherent. A key theoretical limitation of previous Robust PCA (RPCA) methods is the degradation of their performance with increasing data coherence. This work presents a clever approach that successfully circumvents this bottleneck. Since such clustered structures are prevalent in realistic datasets, addressing this issue is a significant contribution. The proposed method is theoretically sound and is well-supported by empirical results that demonstrate its strong practical performance.

I have one question for the authors regarding potential extensions: Could the proposed method be applied to the problem of subspace clustering or segmentation? I would be interested to hear the authors' thoughts on this potential application.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that solves an important problem in the robust PCA literature. I recommend it for acceptance.
