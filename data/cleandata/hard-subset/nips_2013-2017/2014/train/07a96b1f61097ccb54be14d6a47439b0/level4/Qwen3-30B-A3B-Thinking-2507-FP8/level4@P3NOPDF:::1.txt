

This paper addresses a critical limitation in robust PCA (RPCA) for data with high coherence, where existing methods suffer degraded performance as data coherence increases—a well-known theoretical bottleneck. The authors propose a novel approach that circumvents this issue by leveraging the inherent low-rank structure of coherent observations, offering a significant advancement over prior work. The method is theoretically rigorous, with clear convergence guarantees and error bounds, and is empirically validated through extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating consistent superiority over state-of-the-art baselines. The problem formulation is well-motivated, and the solution is both elegant and practically impactful, particularly for applications involving structured, coherent data such as video surveillance or sensor networks.

The paper’s contributions are substantial: it resolves a persistent challenge in RPCA literature, provides a mathematically sound framework, and delivers strong empirical results without compromising computational efficiency. The clarity of the exposition, combined with thorough experiments and ablation studies, underscores the paper’s high quality and technical depth. This work represents a meaningful step forward for robust subspace learning and deserves recognition at NIPS.

I have one minor point for the authors’ consideration: the proposed framework focuses on robust subspace estimation, but it would be valuable to discuss whether the approach could extend to subspace clustering or segmentation tasks (e.g., grouping data points into disjoint subspaces). While the current method recovers a single subspace, the underlying principles might be adaptable to multi-subspace settings. A brief comment on this potential extension—or limitations thereof—would strengthen the paper’s broader applicability. For instance, could the coherence-aware recovery mechanism be integrated into clustering pipelines, or would additional modifications be required?

Overall, this is an excellent paper that tackles a fundamental problem with a well-executed solution. The theoretical and empirical contributions are compelling, and the writing is clear and concise. I strongly recommend acceptance.
