- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA) recovery by exploiting clustering structure.  
- Shows the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure and under conditions on A, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ, yielding stronger theoretical recovery guarantees (exact and noisy cases).  
- Method is novel, significant, and related to union-of-subspace subspace-clustering literature (e.g., Wang & Xu; Soltanolkotabi & Candès; Soltanolkotabi et al.).  
- Paper is less explicit about the precise “clustering” assumption, which may be reasonable and more general than prior work.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theory; authors note the structure likely appears in many applications (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, citing a substantial contribution to compressed sensing.  
- Stylistic/clarity issues: Lines 80–82 need rephrasing (unclear “our data” and overbroad “perfect recovery” claim).  
- Stylistic/clarity issues: Line 88 is unclear and should be rephrased.  
- General suggestion: Proofread and make claims and references more specific and precise; improve language clarity.  
- Insight: Higher coherence can help subspace clustering (Wang & Xu); difference is overall data vs per-cluster low-rank—paper’s structure combines low-rank and union-of-subspace views.  
- Suggested experiment: Compare to pipeline of noisy sparse subspace clustering with ℓ1 penalty followed by PCA per subspace, despite lack of current guarantees.  
- Hidden contribution: Section 4.2 effectively proposes a new subspace-clustering method robust to sparse corruption and shows significant improvements over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155—this should be highlighted.  
- Summarized key contributions: (1) identifies clustering structure causing high coherence, (2) shows LRR with a dictionary can exploit this structure, (3) partially solves sparse corruptions in subspace clustering.
