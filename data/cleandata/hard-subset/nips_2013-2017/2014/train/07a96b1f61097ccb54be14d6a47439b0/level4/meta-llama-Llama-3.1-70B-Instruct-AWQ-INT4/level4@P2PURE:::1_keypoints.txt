- Tackles robust estimation of low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated observations with inherent coherence.
- Notes standard RPCA methods degrade as data coherence increases.
- Authors propose a clever approach that circumvents this theoretical limitation.
- Emphasizes significance because cluster structures are common in realistic data.
- Method is theoretically sound and empirically performs well.
- Minor comment: request to comment on applicability to subspace clustering or segmentation.
- Overall assessment: important problem, excellent quality, worthy of acceptance.
