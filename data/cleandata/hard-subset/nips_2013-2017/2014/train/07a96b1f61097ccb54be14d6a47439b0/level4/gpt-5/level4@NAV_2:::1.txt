This paper investigates robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from grossly corrupted observations when the data are intrinsically coherent. The well-known performance degradation under increasing coherence is a standard theoretical limitation of prior RPCA methods. This work, however, circumvents that issue in a clever way. Given that such clustered structure is common in real-world data, addressing this challenge is clearly significant. The proposed approach is theoretically well-grounded and is convincingly validated empirically.

I have one question for the authors:
Can the proposed method be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation? Please comment on its potential use in that setting.

This paper tackles an important problem in robust PCA research. The paper is of high quality and should be accepted.
