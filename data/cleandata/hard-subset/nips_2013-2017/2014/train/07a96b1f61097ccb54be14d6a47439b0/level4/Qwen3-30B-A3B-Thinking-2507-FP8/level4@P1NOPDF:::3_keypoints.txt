- Novel approach: uses low-rank representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve RPCA.  
- Theoretical insight: coherence parameter μ can reflect implicit clustering structure rather than a strict recovery bottleneck.  
- Main result: under conditions on A, LRR can reduce dependence on μ, yielding stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank+sparse recovery.  
- Connection to prior work: builds on union-of-subspaces/subspace-clustering literature and provides a more explicit, general clustering assumption.  
- Empirical validation: simulations and real-data experiments robustly support the theoretical claims.  
- Key contributions: (1) identifying clustering as explanation for high coherence, (2) showing LRR with learned A exploits this structure, (3) offering a practical solution for sparse-corruption in subspace clustering.
