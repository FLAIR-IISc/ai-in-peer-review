- Problem: Restore corrupted observation matrices by combining RPCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection.  
- Insight: Standard RPCA recovery guarantees are suboptimal for structured (e.g., clustered) data; an explicit dictionary can strengthen guarantees.  
- Theoretical contribution: Two theorems giving conditions for perfect reconstruction when a suitable dictionary captures data structure.  
- Algorithmic contribution: A practical two-step heuristic—apply RPCA to corrupted data, then learn a dictionary from the cleaned low-rank component.  
- Novelty and soundness: Technically sound and novel combination of RPCA and dictionary projection, supported by rigorous theory.  
- Empirical support: Heuristic dictionary-learning algorithm shows clear empirical improvements over alternatives.  
- Main weakness: Exposition lacks clarity and is accessible only to specialists.  
- Recommendation on clarity: Core claims and theoretical guarantees need clearer presentation in the main text.  
- Conceptual clarity: Key concepts (e.g., matrix coherence) require intuitive explanations rather than relegation to the appendix.  
- Impact statement: Authors should better articulate broader significance and how dictionaries address known matrix-recovery challenges.  
- Presentation: Manuscript needs proofreading for grammar and awkward phrasing.  
- Overall verdict: Publishable and interesting work that requires significant revision to improve accessibility; recommend major revision to enhance exposition.
