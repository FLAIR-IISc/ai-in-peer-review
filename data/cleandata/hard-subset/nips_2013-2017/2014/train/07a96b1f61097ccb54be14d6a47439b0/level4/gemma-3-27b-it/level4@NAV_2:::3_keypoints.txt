- Proposes using Low Rank Representation with a learned dictionary to improve RPCA by exploiting clustering structure to reduce dependence on the incoherence parameter.  
- Shows the incoherence parameter can reflect underlying clustering and that, under dictionary conditions, LRR yields stronger theoretical recovery guarantees for exact low-rank+sparse decomposition and its noisy extension.  
- Connects to prior union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering work (e.g., Wang & Xu; Soltanolkotabi & Candès) while making a more general, less explicit clustering assumption.  
- Experimental simulations and real-data tests validate the theory and suggest applicability to domains like text data.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS as a substantial contribution to compressed sensing.  
- Requested clarification/rewording for Lines 80–82 about “our data” and the claim that capturing structures implies “perfect recovery.”  
- Requested clarification for Line 88: the phrase “…is no longer a method of perfection.”  
- Suggested overall proofreading to improve precision, specificity, and clarity of claims and references.  
- Notes Wang & Xu’s result that higher coherence can help subspace clustering (since they exploit low-rankness within clusters rather than global low-rankness), and frames the paper’s structure as a combination of global low-rank and union-of-subspace structure.  
- Suggests comparing the proposed method to a pipeline of noisy subspace clustering with an ℓ1 penalty followed by per-cluster PCA as a practical benchmark.  
- Highlights Section 4.2 as effectively introducing a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption and significant empirical improvements over RPCA+SSC on Hopkins155.  
- Summarizes three key contributions to emphasize: (1) characterizing clustering-induced high coherence, (2) exploiting that structure via a dictionary in LRR, and (3) providing a partial solution to sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
