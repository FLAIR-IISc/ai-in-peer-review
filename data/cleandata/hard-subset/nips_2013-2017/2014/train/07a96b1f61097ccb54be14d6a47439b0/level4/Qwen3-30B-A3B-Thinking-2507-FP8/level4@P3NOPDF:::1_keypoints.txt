- Tackles RPCA’s key limitation with high-coherence data, addressing a known theoretical bottleneck.
- Introduces a novel coherence-aware method leveraging low-rank structure to circumvent degraded performance.
- Provides rigorous theory with convergence guarantees and error bounds.
- Shows strong empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets, consistently outperforming state-of-the-art baselines.
- Problem formulation is well-motivated; solution is elegant and practically impactful for structured, coherent data (e.g., video, sensor networks).
- Contributions are substantial: resolves persistent RPCA issue, offers a sound mathematical framework, and maintains computational efficiency.
- Clear exposition with thorough experiments and ablation studies demonstrating technical depth.
- Minor critique: authors should comment on potential extensions or limitations for subspace clustering/segmentation (multi-subspace settings).
- Overall judgment: excellent paper with strong theoretical and empirical contributions; strong recommendation for acceptance.
