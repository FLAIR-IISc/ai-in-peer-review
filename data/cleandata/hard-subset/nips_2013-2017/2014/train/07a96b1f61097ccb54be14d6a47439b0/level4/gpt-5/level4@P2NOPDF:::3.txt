This paper proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to enhance Robust PCA (RPCA). The key insight is that the incoherence parameter μ—typically seen as a bottleneck in RPCA recovery guarantees—can reflect latent clustering structure in the data. Under appropriate conditions on A, the method exploits this structure via LRR, reducing the dependence on μ and yielding stronger theoretical guarantees for both exact low-rank-plus-sparse decomposition and its noisy counterpart.

The approach is novel and significant. It connects to the union-of-subspaces perspective used in prior subspace clustering work (e.g., Wang and Xu: Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering; Provable Subspace Clustering: When LRR Meets SSC; Soltanolkotabi and Candès: A Geometric Analysis of Subspace Clustering with Outliers; Soltanolkotabi et al.: Robust Subspace Clustering), but is less explicit about the clustering assumptions. This choice appears reasonable and arguably more general. The simulations and real-data experiments support the theory, and the proposed structure is likely to arise in other domains (e.g., text), as noted by the authors.

Overall, the paper makes a substantial contribution to robust low-rank modeling and related areas and merits acceptance to NIPS.

Detailed comments and suggestions:
1. Lines 80–82: Please rewrite for clarity. The phrase “our data” is ambiguous—clarify whether it refers to observed data, the low-rank component, or the dictionary-based representation. Also, even if the model captures all relevant structure, additional conditions are still needed to ensure “perfect recovery”; please state these conditions precisely.

2. Line 88: The phrase “… is no longer a method of perfect (WHAT?)” is incomplete. Presumably you mean “perfect recovery” or “exact recovery”; please specify.

3. Please proofread carefully and tighten the writing throughout. Be precise about claims and references (e.g., what is being recovered, under which conditions, and how the dictionary is constructed or learned). Improving clarity will make the contributions more accessible.

4. Related work: Wang and Xu (Noisy Sparse Subspace Clustering) discuss how higher coherence can aid subspace clustering (in contrast to its negative impact in RPCA and matrix completion). The distinction is that global low rank is not required; only each cluster (subspace) must be low rank. The present paper effectively combines global low-rank modeling with a union-of-subspaces structure through the dictionary in LRR. Please make this connection explicit and contrast the assumptions and guarantees.

5. Empirical comparison: It would be informative to compare against a two-stage pipeline: first apply noisy SSC with an ℓ1 penalty to obtain clusters, then run PCA within each cluster. While we lack provable guarantees for the second stage, this baseline is natural and may clarify the benefits of the proposed approach, especially in regimes with varying coherence and sparsity.

6. Section 4.2: This section effectively introduces a new method for subspace clustering under sparse corruption, and it substantially improves over the RPCA+SSC pipeline on the Hopkins155 dataset. This is, in my view, an important (and somewhat hidden) contribution and should be highlighted more prominently in the paper. The work provides at least three major advances:
   - It articulates a clustering structure that explains high coherence in RPCA settings.
   - It shows how to implicitly exploit this structure by incorporating a dictionary into LRR, thereby relaxing dependence on μ.
   - It partially resolves subspace clustering with sparse corruptions, both theoretically and empirically.

Additional suggestions:
- Assumptions on the dictionary A: Please state clearly how A is obtained (learned from data, constructed from side information, or both), what properties are required (e.g., alignment with subspaces, rank conditions, mutual incoherence), and how sensitive the guarantees are to violations of these properties. Clarify whether A must be learned from clean data or can be estimated robustly from corrupted observations.

- Practical guidance: Provide recommendations for selecting or learning A in practice and discuss computational complexity relative to RPCA and SSC baselines. If possible, include ablations showing performance versus dictionary quality.

- Noisy setting: When extending to noise, specify the exact noise model and how bounds scale with noise level, rank, sparsity, and μ (before and after introducing A). A concise theorem-to-interpretation mapping would help readers understand the practical regimes where the improvement is most pronounced.

- Terminology: Distinguish clearly among “coherence,” “clustering structure,” and “union-of-subspaces” to avoid conflating global versus local structures.

Recommendation: Accept. The paper provides a compelling theoretical and empirical case that dictionary-based LRR can mitigate incoherence limitations in RPCA by leveraging latent clustering structure. The insights bridge robust low-rank modeling and subspace clustering, and the method shows strong performance on real datasets. With improved clarity and more explicit positioning relative to SSC pipelines, the paper will have significant impact.
