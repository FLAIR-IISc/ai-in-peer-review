This paper tackles the challenging problem of robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from observations corrupted by contamination, particularly when those observations exhibit inherent coherence. A key limitation of existing Robust PCA (RPCA) methods is their performance degradation with increasing data coherence; however, this paper cleverly circumvents this bottleneck. Given the prevalence of cluster structure in real-world data, addressing this issue is highly significant. The proposed method is both theoretically justified and empirically demonstrated to perform well. 

I have one question for the authors: Could this method also be applicable to subspace clustering or segmentation problems? Any insights on this potential connection would be valuable.

Overall, this paper presents a strong solution to an important problem in robust PCA. The quality of the work is excellent, and I recommend acceptance.
