This paper addresses the robust estimation of a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations, particularly in the challenging case where the data is inherently coherent. A key theoretical bottleneck of existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods is that their performance degrades as data coherence increases. The authors propose a novel method that successfully circumvents this limitation. Since coherent or clustered data structures are common in many real-world applications, this contribution is highly significant. The proposed method is theoretically well-founded, and its practical effectiveness is convincingly demonstrated through experiments.

I have one question for the authors:
Could the proposed method also be applied to subspace clustering or segmentation problems? Any comments on this potential connection would be welcome.

In summary, this paper provides a valuable solution to an important problem in robust PCA. The submission is of high quality, and I recommend it for acceptance.
