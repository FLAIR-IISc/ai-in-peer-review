- Combines robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.
- Key insight: for structured data (e.g., clustered), robust PCA guarantees may be insufficient; learned dictionaries can give stronger recovery bounds.
- Provides two theorems specifying conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary.
- Addresses dictionary learning with a heuristic algorithm: robust PCA first, then represent low-rank component with a dictionary.
- Technical quality: sound theoretical development, pending detailed verification of proofs.
- Main weakness: dictionary-learning justification is primarily empirical rather than theoretical.
- Clarity issues: core contributions, impact, and definitions (e.g., “coherence”) are not clearly or accessibly presented.
- Writing needs careful editing and clearer exposition to reach even specialist readers.
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical demonstrations of benefit.
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art with rigorous results; heuristic algorithm shows clear empirical improvements.
- Post-rebuttal assessment: work is publishable but requires substantial revision for accessibility; would merit a major revision.
