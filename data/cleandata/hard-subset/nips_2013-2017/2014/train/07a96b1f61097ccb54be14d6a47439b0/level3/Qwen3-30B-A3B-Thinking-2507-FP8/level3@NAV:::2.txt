

This paper investigates the limitations of Robust PCA (RPCA) when dealing with coherent data (e.g., data with clustering structures) and proposes a dictionary-based Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach to address these limitations. The authors demonstrate that RPCA's performance degrades as data coherence increases with more clusters, while LRR with a properly configured low-rank dictionary can avoid this coherence dependency. The paper provides theoretical guarantees for exact recovery under specific conditions and proposes a heuristic algorithm that uses RPCA output to construct the dictionary.

The paper's strengths are significant. The theoretical contributions are technically sound, with well-justified theorems establishing recovery conditions that improve upon RPCA's constraints. The empirical validation on synthetic and motion sequence data convincingly demonstrates the method's superiority over RPCA, with notable improvements in clustering error rates (40% relative reduction in motion segmentation). The insights about coherence parameters being intrinsically linked to data structures represent an important conceptual advance.

However, the paper suffers from major clarity issues that significantly hinder its accessibility. The core contribution—using dictionary learning to handle coherent data—is not clearly articulated, with key concepts like coherence buried in technical details and the appendix. The writing is dense and specialized, making it difficult for non-experts to grasp the significance of the results. The paper would benefit from substantial rewriting to clarify the main claims and emphasize the broader impact.

The originality is moderate: the paper builds on established RPCA and LRR frameworks but provides novel theoretical insights and empirical validation. The significance is strong in terms of advancing the state of the art for coherent data recovery, though the heuristic dictionary learning approach somewhat limits the theoretical completeness.

Pro: Rigorous theorems, compelling empirical results, important conceptual insights about coherence. Con: Poor exposition, unclear main contribution, inaccessible to broader audience. While the paper makes a valuable technical contribution, the clarity issues prevent it from reaching its full potential. I recommend major revision to address exposition concerns before acceptance.
