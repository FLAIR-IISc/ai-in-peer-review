- Tackles RPCA recovery of low-rank matrices from contaminated observations, relevant to image processing, vision, radar, etc.
- Shows existing RPCA methods degrade as data coherence increases and coherence parameters reflect intrinsic data structure.
- Proposes using a low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework to bypass the coherence-related bottleneck.
- Provides rigorous theory proving exact recovery when the dictionary is low-rank and encodes ground-truth information.
- Presents a practical algorithm implementing the proposed approach.
- Extensive experiments demonstrate significant improvements over RPCA across various scenarios.
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of method and theoretical foundations.
- Suggests investigating applicability to subspace clustering/segmentation as a valuable extension.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, introduces a novel effective method, offers strong theoretical guarantees and impressive experimental results.
- No significant arguments against acceptance; overall recommendation: accept.
