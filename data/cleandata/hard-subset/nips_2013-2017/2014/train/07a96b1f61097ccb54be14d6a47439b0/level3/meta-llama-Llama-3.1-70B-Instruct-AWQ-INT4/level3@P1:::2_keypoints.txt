- Proposes combining RPCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.
- Studies recovery of low-rank, coherent matrices and characterizes the physical regimes that produce coherence.
- Provides new theoretical results and theorems giving conditions for reconstruction when a dictionary is available.
- Introduces a practical algorithm (heuristic dictionary learning: RPCA then dictionary representation) that empirically outperforms RPCA.
- Shows insights into the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) model: LRR with proper dictionaries can handle coherent data.
- Empirical results support the heuristic and show clear improvements over RPCA.
- Proofs and theoretical development are technically sound.
- Major clarity and exposition issues: main contributions, claims, and impact are not well explained and are accessible mainly to specialists.
- The concept of coherence is hard to understand and its interpretation is buried in the appendix.
- Language and presentation require substantial editing for broader accessibility.
- Originality: builds on recent work but contributes novel theorems and empirical validation; dictionary approach is a meaningful innovation.
- Significance: advances the state of the art with rigorous results and practical improvements; potential impact in image processing, computer vision, and ML.
- Overall assessment: interesting and publishable but needs major revision for clarity; reviewer would vote for major revision.
