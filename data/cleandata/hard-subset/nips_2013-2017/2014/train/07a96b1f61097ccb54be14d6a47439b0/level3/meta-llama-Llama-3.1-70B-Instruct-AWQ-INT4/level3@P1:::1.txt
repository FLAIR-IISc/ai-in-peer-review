This paper proposes a novel approach to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, addressing the limitations of existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) methods. The authors demonstrate that RPCA's performance degrades when dealing with coherent data, which is a common occurrence in real-world applications. They introduce a new method, Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit (LRDP), which leverages the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework to effectively handle coherent data.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of RPCA and the benefits of using LRDP. The authors show that LRDP can avoid the coherence parameters that increase with the underlying cluster number, making it a more robust approach for low-rank matrix recovery. They also propose a practical algorithm for learning the dictionary matrix in LRDP, which is demonstrated to outperform RPCA in various experiments.

The strengths of this paper include:

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a new method that addresses the limitations of existing RPCA methods, providing a more robust approach for low-rank matrix recovery.
2. **Theoretical soundness**: The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of RPCA and the benefits of using LRDP, demonstrating the theoretical soundness of the proposed approach.
3. **Empirical performance**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of LRDP through various experiments, showing that it outperforms RPCA in recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.

The weaknesses of this paper include:
