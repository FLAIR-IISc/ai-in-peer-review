- Paper studies low-rank matrix recovery with gross corruptions for coherent (clustered) data and proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary A to avoid RPCA’s µ2 dependence.  
- Main theoretical claim: if A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA then LRR recovers components without dependence on the second coherence parameter µ2; noisy bounds provided.  
- Practical heuristic: run RPCA → build column-normalized low-rank dictionary from the estimate → solve LRR to refine recovery.  
- Empirically, synthetic experiments and corrupted Hopkins motion sequences show improved recovery rates and lower clustering error versus RPCA.  
- Novelty: analyzes LRR with general low-rank dictionaries for coherent data, quantifies how coherence grows with cluster count, and gives a practical dictionary construction.  
- Strength: clearly identifies RPCA’s failure mode for clustered data tied to µ2 and targets it directly.  
- Strength: provides new µ2-free recovery guarantees for LRR under U0 ⊂ UA, including a noisy-case theorem.  
- Strength: proposes a simple, unsupervised, computationally reasonable pipeline that improves empirical recovery.  
- Strength: demonstrates meaningful gains on synthetic and real motion-segmentation data.  
- Strength: offers principled insight for dictionary design in LRR relative to coherence.  
- Weakness: exposition of coherence, intuition, and the µ2-phenomenon is scattered and hard to follow; key intuition is buried.  
- Weakness: core assumptions (U0 ⊂ UA, A low-rank, column-normalized) are strong and only approximately met by the heuristic; theory-practice gap not quantified.  
- Weakness: limited empirical analysis of dictionary choices and ablations (e.g., varying cluster count, comparing A = X).  
- Weakness: parameter sensitivity (λ, ε), effect of column normalization, and robustness to poor initial RPCA are underexplored.  
- Weakness: minor typos and notational overload reduce readability.  
- Quality: theoretical results appear technically sound with reasonable constants and clear noisy/noiseless statements.  
- Quality: empirical evaluation supports claims but would benefit from more thorough experiments and additional baselines.  
- Clarity suggestions: move an intuitive explanation of coherence/µ2 into the main text with a figure, clearly list contributions, provide a high-level proof sketch, and streamline notation and typos.  
- Originality: moderate-to-high — combining µ2 diagnosis, µ2-free LRR analysis with low-rank dictionaries, and a practical construction is novel and relevant.  
- Significance: addresses an important failure mode in robust subspace recovery and gives actionable guidance likely useful to practitioners.  
- Key questions for authors: sensitivity to U0 ⊂ UA accuracy and subspace angle; effect of varying cluster number while keeping rank fixed; performance under higher dense noise and different ε; ablations of Steps 2–3 and comparisons to LRR with A = X; report wall-clock times and rA vs r0.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled fix to a common RPCA failure mode, solid theoretical results, simple effective algorithm, and empirical gains.  
- Cons against acceptance: poor exposition for non-specialists, strong theoretical assumptions with limited quantification of heuristic gap, and insufficient empirical breadth and ablations.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful theoretical and practical advance but needs improved clarity and expanded experiments.
