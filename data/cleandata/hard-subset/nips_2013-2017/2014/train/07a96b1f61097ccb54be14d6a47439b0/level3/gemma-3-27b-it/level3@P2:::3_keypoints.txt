- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery with sparse noise and diagnoses RPCA’s sensitivity to data coherence from clustering.
- Argues coherence reflects underlying clustering structure and is not an unavoidable nuisance.
- Core contribution: using LRR with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to mitigate dependence on coherence.
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2) provide rigorous recovery guarantees, including a noisy case.
- Proposes an unsupervised Algorithm 1 that initializes the dictionary via RPCA as a practical alternative to standard RPCA.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real motion sequences (e.g., Hopkins155) show improved recovery and subspace clustering performance.
- Novelty: new perspective linking coherence and clustering and applying learned-dictionary LRR to overcome RPCA limits.
- Theoretical soundness: clear theorems and proofs that delineate method effectiveness and limitations.
- Significance: demonstrably improves RPCA in high-coherence scenarios and connects low-rank recovery to subspace clustering.
- Clarity: generally well-written, logically organized, with a helpful notation summary.
- Completeness: combines theory, algorithm, and experiments into a well-defined, validated contribution.
- Minor clarity issues: a few ambiguous phrases and one incomplete sentence that need editing.
- Related-work coverage could be more specific, especially comparing to noisy sparse subspace clustering + per-subspace PCA pipelines.
- Dictionary initialization could benefit from exploring alternative or more sophisticated dictionary-learning strategies.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novel idea, rigorous theory, convincing experiments, and strong connections to subspace clustering.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor clarity edits required and the evaluation lacks some comprehensive comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance; main advances are identifying clustering-induced coherence, exploiting it via LRR dictionary, and improving sparse-corruption handling in subspace clustering.
