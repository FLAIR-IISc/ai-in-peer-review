- Problem: RPCA fails on coherent data (high coherence from clustering), with performance degrading as cluster number increases.  
- Proposed solution: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to avoid coherence-related failures.  
- Theoretical contribution 1: Shows clustering structures cause the second coherence parameter µ2 (and µ3) to increase with cluster number, harming RPCA recovery.  
- Theoretical contribution 2: Proves LRR with a low-rank dictionary is immune to coherence parameters, providing exact recovery guarantees without relying on coherence.  
- Theoretical contribution 3: Provides recovery guarantees for both noiseless and noisy cases, with success conditions looser than RPCA’s (O(n2/log n1) vs O(n2/(log n1)2)).  
- Practical algorithm: Proposes an unsupervised method that uses RPCA to construct the dictionary for LRR.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic experiments show a 47% wider success area; real motion-sequence tests show ~40% relative improvement in clustering error.  
- Novelty: Clarifies the physical meaning of coherence and identifies a previously unaddressed coherence limitation of RPCA.  
- Builds on prior work: Extends and complements Candès et al. (RPCA) and Liu et al. (LRR) with a principled fix for coherent data.  
- Strengths: Strong, technically sound proofs and convincing empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses — exposition: Main insight is buried in technical detail; coherence concept is poorly explained and key material relegated to the appendix.  
- Weaknesses — motivation/significance: Lacks clear motivation and articulation of broader ML/data-analysis impact, making significance hard to appreciate.  
- Revision suggestions: State the main insight early, provide intuitive explanations and simple examples of coherence, and restructure for clearer presentation and practical implications.  
- Overall assessment: Meaningful theoretical and empirical contribution but needs substantial rewriting for accessibility; recommend major revision before acceptance.
