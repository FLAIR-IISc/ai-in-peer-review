- Problem: RPCA struggles to recover low-rank matrices when data are coherent due to clustering structures.  
- Proposal: Combine RPCA with dictionary-based LRR using a low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence issues.  
- Theory: The paper provides theorems showing low-rank dictionaries can reduce the adverse effects of coherence.  
- Algorithm: A two-step method—apply RPCA to estimate the low-rank component, then construct a dictionary and refine recovery with LRR.  
- Empirical results: Extensive synthetic and real-world experiments show significant improvements over RPCA, including better motion segmentation.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: Rigorous theoretical contributions with proofs and strong experimental support.  
- Strength — Novelty: Innovative integration of RPCA and dictionary-based LRR to address coherent data.  
- Strength — Significance: Tackles an important problem with likely influence on future robust recovery and subspace clustering work.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments across coherence levels and real sequences that convincingly demonstrate benefits.  
- Weakness — Clarity/accessibility: Paper is dense; key concepts like coherence are not intuitively explained and are pushed to the appendix.  
- Weakness — Presentation of contributions: Relation to existing methods (LRR, RPCA) and clear delineation of contributions are insufficient.  
- Weakness — Heuristic dictionary learning: Dictionary construction is heuristic and lacks theoretical guarantees, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness — Language/organization: Overly technical writing needs significant rewriting for readability and clearer exposition.  
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to improve accessibility, clarify contributions, and state practical implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Advances state-of-the-art, provides rigorous theory and experiments, and shows practical relevance in motion segmentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Poor clarity, reliance on heuristic dictionary learning without guarantees, and requires substantial rewriting.
