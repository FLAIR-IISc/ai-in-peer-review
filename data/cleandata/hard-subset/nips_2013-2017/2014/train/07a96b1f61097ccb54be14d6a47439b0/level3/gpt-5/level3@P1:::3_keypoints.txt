- Paper studies RPCA failure on coherent, clustered low-rank data and proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary to recover low-rank + sparse components.
- Introduces and formalizes the “µ2-phenomenon”: increasing number of subspaces raises second/third coherence parameters, degrading RPCA guarantees.
- Shows LRR can avoid dependence on µ2 if the dictionary A is low-rank and its column space contains the ground-truth column space (U0 ⊂ UA).
- Provides noiseless and noisy theorems giving exact/near-exact recovery with high probability under standard sparsity and λ = 1/√n1.
- Relaxes rank bound from O(n2/log2 n1) (RPCA) to O(n2/log n1), improving theoretical limits.
- Proposes a practical unsupervised two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to estimate L0, build a low-rank column-normalized dictionary from that estimate, then solve LRR.
- Experiments on synthetic coherent data and corrupted motion sequences show clear gains over RPCA and large downstream clustering improvements (SIM, LRRx, SSC).
- Situates work relative to prior RPCA, LRR, SSC, SIM, and Outlier Pursuit literature, bridging robust recovery and subspace clustering.
- Theoretical results are technically sound given assumptions; shifts dependence from data row-space coherence µ2 to dictionary column-space coherence µ1(A).
- Comparison of bounds versus RPCA is informative and the unsupervised procedure is reasonable and empirically supported.
- Clarity is generally good; the µ2-phenomenon is well motivated, but some language issues and ambiguities need fixing and clearer intuition/proof sketches are suggested.
- Original contributions: formalizing clustering-induced coherence, new LRR guarantees with general low-rank dictionaries, and an effective unsupervised dictionary-learning pipeline.
- Significance: addresses important practical problem across applications and gives actionable guidance to use low-rank dictionaries aligned with column space.
- Weakness: main assumption U0 ⊂ UA may be strong when RPCA fails; robustness when learned A is imperfect should be discussed and quantified.
- Empirics need ablations (oracle A vs learned A, A=X vs low-rank A), sensitivity analyses (rank threshold, normalization), and runtime/solver details.
- Baseline comparisons should be expanded (robust SSC variants, Outlier Pursuit, RPCA+SSC) to better position gains.
- Theory–practice gap: dependence on µ1(A) needs empirical measurement for learned dictionaries and analysis of recovery versus dictionary rank/conditioning.
- Minor: streamline notation (unused norms); explicitly state dependence on number of clusters k where possible.
- Pros for acceptance: novel theoretical insight, new LRR guarantees avoiding µ2, simple effective algorithm with strong empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on U0 ⊂ UA assumption, limited ablations/baselines, and some clarity/proofreading issues.
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested minor revisions to clarity and additional empirical comparisons.
