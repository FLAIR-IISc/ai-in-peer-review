- Problem: RPCA's performance degrades as data coherence increases, a common theoretical bottleneck.  
- Key insight: The second coherence parameter μ2 grows with the number of underlying clusters, linking coherence to clustering structure.  
- Physical meaning: Coherence parameters reflect real data structures (e.g., clustering), not just theoretical assumptions.  
- Proposed method: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly constructed low-rank dictionary to handle coherent data.  
- Theoretical result: Theorem 1 proves LRR is immune to coherence parameters when the dictionary itself is low-rank.  
- Practical algorithm: An unsupervised Algorithm 1 builds a suitable low-rank dictionary from RPCA output for real-world use.  
- Theoretical rigor: Careful, rigorous analysis distinguishing μ1, μ2, μ3 and showing how clustering specifically affects μ2.  
- Synthetic experiments: Method achieves a 47% wider success region than RPCA.  
- Motion segmentation experiments: Reduces clustering error from 9.50% to 5.74% on Hopkins155 (≈40% relative improvement).  
- Strengths: Well-written, consistent notation, convincing empirical validation, original and significant contribution.  
- Weakness/limitation: Algorithm 1 is heuristic and could use more analysis of sensitivity to the estimated rank r̂0.  
- Recommendation: Strong acceptance.
