This paper addresses the problem of recovering low-rank matrices corrupted by noise, specifically focusing on cases where the underlying low-rank structure exhibits clustering, leading to higher coherence and degraded performance of standard Robust PCA (RPCA). The authors propose combining RPCA with a dictionary-based low-rank representation (LRR) approach, arguing that a judiciously chosen dictionary can mitigate the effects of coherence. They provide theoretical results demonstrating conditions under which recovery is possible given a low-rank dictionary and propose a heuristic algorithm to learn such a dictionary from the RPCA result.

The paper builds on a strong foundation of recent work in robust PCA [4] and low-rank representation [13]. It correctly identifies a limitation of RPCA – its sensitivity to data coherence arising from clustering – a point not fully explored in prior work. The contribution of demonstrating that a low-rank dictionary can alleviate this issue, along with providing theoretical backing (Theorems 1 & 2), represents a genuine advance. The empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world motion sequences supports their claims, showing improvements over RPCA.  The connections drawn to matrix factorization techniques [20, 23] and GPCA [22] provide useful context.

However, despite the technical soundness and empirical results, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues. The central contributions – the importance of the dictionary and the conditions for its efficacy – are not clearly articulated, especially in the introduction. The impact of the work is downplayed, making it difficult to assess its broader significance without already being deeply familiar with the field.  The concept of “coherence,” while defined, remains conceptually murky. The explanation relies heavily on mathematical notation (µ1, µ2, µ3) which, while rigorous, isn’t intuitively explained. The interpretation of these parameters and their relation to the clustering structure is buried in the appendix and requires significant effort from the reader to unpack. The writing is often dense and verbose, hindering accessibility.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis appears robust, and the proofs (as claimed) are technically sound. The theorems provide concrete conditions for recovery.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The improvements over RPCA are clear.
* **Originality:** While building on existing techniques, the paper offers new theoretical insights and empirical validation of the dictionary-based approach for handling coherent data.
* **Significance:** The work advances the state-of-the-art in robust low-rank recovery, particularly in scenarios where clustering structures are present. The heuristic dictionary learning method, despite its limitations, provides a practical improvement.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity:** The primary weakness. The main contributions are obscured by dense writing and a lack of clear explanation. The paper is challenging to follow for anyone not already an expert in the field.
* **Coherence Explanation:** The concept of coherence and its parameters (µ1, µ2, µ3) are poorly explained and difficult to understand.
* **Heuristic Nature:** The dictionary learning algorithm is a heuristic, and its limitations are not fully discussed.
* **Writing Style:** The language is often convoluted, and the organization could be improved.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a technically sound contribution to a challenging problem in robust matrix recovery.
* The theoretical results are novel and provide valuable insights into the relationship between coherence, dictionary structure, and recovery performance.
* The empirical results demonstrate a clear improvement over existing methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of clarity significantly hinders the paper's accessibility and impact.
* The heuristic nature of the dictionary learning algorithm limits the practical applicability of the approach.
* The writing style and organization need substantial improvement.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper is publishable, and the underlying research is interesting and valuable. However, in its current form, it is likely to be accessible only to specialists in the field. The reviewer’s recommendation for a major revision is justified.  A substantial rewrite is needed to improve clarity, simplify the explanation of coherence, and better articulate the contributions and significance of the work. The authors should specifically address the reviewer’s concerns regarding the lack of clarity and provide a clearer roadmap for readers to understand the key ideas. It would be beneficial to include more intuitive explanations of the coherence parameters and their connection to the clustering structure.
