This paper proposes a novel approach to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, addressing the limitations of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA). The authors introduce a dictionary-based method, Low-Rank Representation (LRR), which can effectively handle coherent data by learning a suitable dictionary. The main contribution of this paper is to provide a theoretical framework for LRR, including theorems that state the conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of RPCA and the need for a new approach. The proposed method is built upon the exploration of the low-rank matrix recovery problem, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the coherence parameters and their properties. The theorems presented in the paper provide a solid foundation for the proposed method, and the empirical results justify the heuristic algorithm used for dictionary learning.

However, the paper suffers from major clarity issues. The main contributions, claims, and impact of the paper are not well explained and are accessible mainly to specialists. The concept of coherence is hard to understand, and its interpretation is indirect and buried in the appendix. The language and exposition require editing, and the authors should comment on how they will address these issues.

In terms of originality, the paper builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical validation. The significance of the paper is that it advances the state of the art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements, despite a heuristic dictionary method.

Overall, I believe that the paper is publishable and interesting, but it needs substantial rewriting for broader accessibility. I would have voted for a major revision.
