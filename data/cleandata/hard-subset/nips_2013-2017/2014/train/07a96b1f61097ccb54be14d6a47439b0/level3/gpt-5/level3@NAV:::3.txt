This paper studies why Robust PCA (RPCA) can fail on “coherent” low-rank data arising from union-of-subspaces structure, and proposes Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit: an LRR formulation with a learned low-rank dictionary A. The key insight is that the coherence parameter μ (especially μ2) reflects clustering in the row space rather than an unavoidable bottleneck; by choosing A to be low-rank and to span the true column space, LRR can avoid dependence on μ2 and yield stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank + sparse recovery. The authors give an unsupervised procedure that first runs RPCA, builds A from the estimated low-rank component, and then solves LRR. Simulations and motion segmentation experiments (Hopkins155 with added sparse corruption) validate the theory. The work is closely related to RPCA (Candès et al.), LRR (Liu et al.), SSC (Elhamifar & Vidal), and the union-of-subspaces literature including GPCA, and connects to observations by Wang & Xu that higher coherence can aid subspace clustering; here, the paper leverages that structure jointly with low-rank + sparse modeling.

Quality: Theorems 1–2 are technically sound and clearly specify conditions on rank(A), μ1(A), sparsity, and noise. The removal of μ2 from guarantees under the stated subspace condition U0 ⊂ UA is significant. Experiments (phase transition showing a 47% larger success region; substantial gains on Hopkins155) support the claims. Section 4.2 effectively proposes a robust subspace clustering method under sparse corruption and shows meaningful improvements.

Clarity: Generally well organized, but there are several proofreading issues (ambiguous phrasing around lines 80–82 and an incomplete phrase at line 88). Please tighten language, be explicit about which coherence terms remain (dependence on μ1(A) persists), and clarify sensitivity to λ, ε, and the normalization of A. A more explicit related-work section would help situate contributions.

Originality: Novel and nontrivial combination of LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence; clear differentiation from LRR with A = X and from standard RPCA.

Significance: Important for compressed sensing/robust PCA and subspace clustering; likely to influence practice.

Suggestions: (1) Add an empirical comparison to a pipeline of noisy SSC (ℓ1 affinities) followed by per-subspace PCA. (2) Discuss the connection to Wang & Xu more explicitly. (3) Include ablations on dictionary quality and violations of U0 ⊂ UA; add more real datasets (e.g., text).

Pros: Strong theory and practice; novel insight into coherence; practical algorithm; clear gains. Cons: Clarity/proofreading; reliance on RPCA to seed A; limited baselines.

Recommendation: Accept.
