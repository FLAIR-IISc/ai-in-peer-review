## Review of “Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit”

This paper addresses the problem of robust low-rank matrix recovery, specifically focusing on the limitations of Robust PCA (RPCA) when dealing with data exhibiting clustering structures, leading to high coherence. The authors argue that incorporating a dictionary learning approach, via Low-Rank Representation (LRR), can alleviate this issue, provided the dictionary itself is low-rank. They provide theoretical guarantees for recovery under certain conditions and propose a heuristic algorithm involving an initial RPCA step to inform dictionary construction. 

The work builds upon recent advances in RPCA and LRR, contributing new theorems that establish conditions under which coherent data can be successfully reconstructed. The empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data appears to justify the effectiveness of the proposed heuristic, demonstrating improvements over standard RPCA. The mathematical analysis appears sound upon initial inspection, and the paper addresses a relevant problem in the field of robust matrix recovery.

However, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues. The central contributions and their impact are not clearly articulated, making it difficult for readers outside of a highly specialized area to fully grasp the significance of the work. The concept of “coherence” – while central to the paper – is explained in a convoluted manner, largely relegated to the appendix, hindering understanding. The language and exposition are often dense and could benefit from substantial editing. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The theorems presented appear mathematically rigorous and provide valuable insights into the conditions for successful recovery.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments demonstrate a clear improvement over RPCA, justifying the proposed approach.
*   **Originality:** The combination of RPCA, dictionary learning, and the theoretical analysis of coherence represents a novel contribution to the field.
*   **Significance:** Addressing the limitations of RPCA with coherent data is a relevant advancement with potential applications in various domains.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity:** The primary and most significant weakness is the lack of clarity in presenting the core ideas, contributions, and their implications.
*   **Accessibility:** The paper is heavily geared towards specialists, making it difficult for a broader audience to appreciate the work.
*   **Coherence Explanation:** The explanation of coherence is obscure and difficult to follow.
*   **Heuristic Nature:** While the proposed algorithm performs well empirically, its reliance on a heuristic approach (RPCA initialization) is not fully justified or discussed in terms of its limitations.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of robust matrix recovery. However, its impact is currently limited by significant clarity issues. While technically sound and empirically validated, the paper requires substantial rewriting to make it accessible to a wider audience.  I would recommend publication *after major revision*, contingent upon addressing the clarity and accessibility concerns. The authors should explicitly outline how they plan to improve the exposition and provide a more intuitive explanation of the concept of coherence.
