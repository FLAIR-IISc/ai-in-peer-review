- Problem: RPCA for low-rank recovery fails when the true low-rank matrix L0 is "coherent."  
- Insight: Coherence µ2 grows with the number of clusters in the data, explaining a practical RPCA failure mode.  
- Method: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) modeling L = A Z with a learned dictionary A to overcome coherence issues.  
- Theoretical result: Theorem 1 shows that if A is low-rank and its column space contains U0, LRR recovery guarantees do not depend on µ2.  
- Algorithm: Two-stage practical method — run RPCA to estimate L0, construct a low-rank dictionary Â from that estimate, then solve LRR to refine recovery.  
- Strength — significance/originality: Reframes an abstract theoretical bottleneck (coherence) as a concrete clustering phenomenon; novel and insightful.  
- Strength — technical quality: Rigorous analysis (Theorems 1 and 2) and a practical algorithm that implements the theory.  
- Strength — empirical validation: Synthetic experiments show a larger phase-transition region; real-motion experiments yield ~40% relative improvement in subspace clustering.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written with a clear motivation and logical flow.  
- Weakness — theory/practice gap: Theoretical guarantees assume ideal A, but the algorithm builds Â from RPCA which may be inaccurate; robustness to dictionary errors needs discussion.  
- Weakness — connection to subspace clustering: Potentially deeper integration of the dictionary-learning strategy into subspace clustering (beyond preprocessing) is not explored.  
- Recommendation: Strong, original, and significant work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
