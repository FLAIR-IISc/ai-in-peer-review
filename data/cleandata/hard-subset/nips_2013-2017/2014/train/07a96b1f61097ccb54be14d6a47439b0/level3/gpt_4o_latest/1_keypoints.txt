- Addresses robust estimation of low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated, coherent observations common in real data.  
- Identifies RPCA's degradation as data coherence (e.g., clustering) increases.  
- Proposes an LRR-based approach that learns a low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence-related issues.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis proving LRR with a low-rank dictionary avoids RPCA's problematic coherence parameters.  
- Offers clear proofs and interprets the physical meaning of coherence parameters.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance with significant improvements over RPCA on synthetic and real datasets (e.g., motion segmentation).  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive and shows superiority particularly for moderate rank and sparsity; also useful for subspace clustering.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and accessible with sufficient RPCA/coherence background.  
- Novel idea: learning a low-rank dictionary within LRR is simple, effective, and a meaningful extension to existing methods.  
- Weakness: limited exploration and experiments specifically on subspace clustering applications.  
- Weakness: scalability to very large datasets is not thoroughly discussed despite claims of marginal extra cost.  
- Weakness: potential iterative refinement of the algorithm is mentioned but not explored.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance based on theoretical and empirical contributions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: advances RPCA limitations, solid theory and experiments, practical and novel approach; arguments against: limited scalability and additional application/iteration exploration.
