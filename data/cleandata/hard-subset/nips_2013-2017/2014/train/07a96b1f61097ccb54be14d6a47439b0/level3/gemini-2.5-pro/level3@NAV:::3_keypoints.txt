- Addresses RPCA's failure on coherent data by linking high coherence to underlying clustering/union-of-subspaces structure.
- Core contribution: use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary A to exploit clustering structure.
- Theoretical result: under conditions on A, the method can remove dependence on the coherence parameter µ, yielding stronger recovery guarantees in noiseless and noisy settings.
- Builds on and synthesizes ideas from RPCA, LRR, and subspace clustering literature.
- Originality/insight: reframes coherence as an exploitable feature (indicator of clustering) rather than just a limitation.
- Technical quality: rigorous proofs showing a low-rank dictionary can make recovery guarantees independent of cluster-related coherence.
- Significance/impact: provides a new effective method for robust subspace clustering under sparse corruption.
- Empirical result: about 40% relative improvement over RPCA-preprocessed methods on the Hopkins155 dataset (Section 4.2).
- Weakness — clarity: manuscript needs proofreading and fixes for ambiguous/incomplete phrasing (e.g., lines 80–82, 88).
- Weakness — experiments: should compare to a direct pipeline baseline (noisy sparse subspace clustering via ℓ1 minimization followed by PCA).
- Overall recommendation: substantial contribution and strongly recommended for acceptance.
