This paper, "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit," addresses a significant problem in robust principal component analysis (RPCA). The authors propose a novel method to recover low-rank matrices from contaminated observations, which is a crucial task in various applications such as image processing, computer vision, and radar imaging.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing RPCA methods, particularly their degradation in performance as data coherence increases. The authors identify that the coherence parameters, which are essential in RPCA, are related to the intrinsic structures of the data and cannot be simply discarded. They propose a clever method that circumvents this coherence-related bottleneck by using a low-rank dictionary in the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework.

The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis of their method, including a proof that the recovery produced by LRR is exact when the dictionary matrix is low-rank and contains information about the ground truth matrix. They also establish a practical algorithm that outperforms RPCA in their extensive experiments.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method and its theoretical foundations. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating a significant improvement over RPCA in various scenarios.

One potential question for the authors is whether their method can be applied to subspace clustering or segmentation tasks. The authors' response to this question would provide valuable insights into the potential applications and extensions of their work.

In summary, this paper tackles an important RPCA problem, is of high quality, and should be accepted. The authors' contributions include:

* A novel method to recover low-rank matrices from contaminated observations
* A thorough analysis of the limitations of existing RPCA methods
* A rigorous theoretical analysis of their method
* A practical algorithm that outperforms RPCA in various scenarios
