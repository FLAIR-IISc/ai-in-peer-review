- Addresses RPCA's limitation on coherent (clustered) data and importance for real-world applications.  
- Shows RPCA performance degrades as clustering increases because coherence parameters (µ2) grow with number of clusters.  
- Key insight: coherence parameters reflect intrinsic data structure, not just theoretical assumptions.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's limitation.  
- Contribution 1: Establishes link between clustering structure and µ2-phenomenon explaining RPCA's performance drop.  
- Contribution 2: Proves LRR with a low-rank dictionary (U0 ⊂ UA) avoids dependence on µ2, giving stronger exact/noisy recovery guarantees than RPCA.  
- Contribution 3: Presents a practical unsupervised dictionary learning algorithm that outperforms RPCA in experiments.  
- Theoretical strength: Theorems 1 and 2 show LRR achieves recovery under looser rank constraints than RPCA, extending RPCA proof techniques.  
- Experimental validation: Synthetic results show a 47% wider success region; Hopkins155 shows ~40% relative improvement in motion segmentation error.  
- Originality: First comprehensive analysis of LRR with general dictionaries for coherent data and valuable connection to subspace clustering.  
- Significance: High practical relevance; method integrates into pipelines with modest overhead and improves real-world tasks like motion segmentation.  
- Minor clarity issues: ambiguous phrasing (lines 80–82), incomplete phrase (line 88), and need to discuss Wang & Xu and compare to SSC+per-subspace PCA.  
- Practicality: Algorithm 1 is computationally efficient, ~20% slower than RPCA, and works best when dictionary approximates true subspaces.  
- Builds on prior work (LRR, RPCA, subspace clustering) and reframes coherence parameters as structural rather than purely theoretical.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel, and well-justified work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
