- Problem & contribution: Addresses robustly recovering low-dimensional subspaces from data made coherent by clustering, shows RPCA fails due to µ2 growth, and proves LRR with a deliberately low-rank dictionary A yields µ2-independent recovery (noiseless and noisy).  
- Algorithm: Proposes a simple unsupervised two-stage method—run RPCA to get a coarse L0, denoise/truncate and normalize columns to form A, then run LRR for final recovery.  
- Theoretical novelty: Provides exact and stable recovery guarantees for LRR under U0 ⊂ UA and rank bounds on A, removing dependence on µ2/µ3.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends RPCA/PCP, Outlier Pursuit, and LRR literature by identifying coherence as clustering and showing low-rank dictionary design circumvents the µ2 bottleneck.  
- Empirical validation: Synthetic and motion-sequence experiments show substantial gains over RPCA (≈47% larger success region; SSC error reduced from 9.50% to 5.74%).  
- Important problem: Tackles a practically meaningful limitation—cluster-induced coherence is common and degrades RPCA.  
- Conceptual insight: Clarifies that coherence parameters reflect extra structure (clustering) rather than mere proof artifacts.  
- Dependence on U0 ⊂ UA: Method relies on an initial RPCA pass to satisfy U0 ⊂ UA; sensitivity to poor initial RPCA estimates (high coherence/high corruption) is a concern.  
- Rank estimation & conditioning: Heuristic thresholding (10^−3 σ1) and conditioning steps are ad hoc—need sensitivity analysis and clearer guidance.  
- Baseline comparisons: Empirical comparisons are limited to RPCA; should include other robust methods (reweighted PCP, truncated/weighted nuclear norms, outlier-pursuit variants).  
- Noise model & robustness: Theory assumes dense Gaussian noise—experiments should test heavy-tailed noise and structured missingness.  
- Scalability: Complexity discussion present but lacks wall-clock timing and scaling experiments on larger datasets.  
- Quality & clarity: Technically sound with clear assumptions and probabilistic guarantees; well written overall, though more intuition on why low-rank A removes µ2 and clarification of constants/λ selection are needed.  
- Originality & significance: Novel identification of clustering-driven coherence and the low-rank dictionary remedy; high significance with likely impact on robust subspace learning and segmentation.  
- Applicability to segmentation: Suggests potential to use learned low-rank A directly for LRR-based clustering and asks for theoretical/empirical study of segmentation when A is low-rank vs A = X.  
- Acceptance arguments: Important problem, sound theory removing a known bottleneck, practical algorithm with strong empirical results, and clear potential impact.  
- Rejection concerns: Limited baselines/sensitivity analyses and reliance on a good initial RPCA estimate could weaken practical robustness.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to add experiments/discussion on segmentation use of A, robustness to initial dictionary quality, and sensitivity to rank estimation.
