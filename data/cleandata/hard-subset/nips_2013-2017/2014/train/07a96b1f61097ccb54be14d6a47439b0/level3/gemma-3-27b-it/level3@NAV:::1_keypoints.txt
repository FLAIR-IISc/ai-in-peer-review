- Addresses RPCA failure when data coherence increases due to cluster structure, an important practical problem.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to circumvent coherence issues.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing a low-rank dictionary can mitigate coherence-related failures.  
- Empirically validates the method on synthetic and real motion-sequence data, outperforming standard RPCA.  
- Connects cluster structure to coherence with insightful, well-motivated analysis.  
- Builds appropriately on prior RPCA and LRR literature (Candès et al., 2009; Liu et al., 2013).  
- Notes valuable connection to Generalized PCA (Vidal et al., 2005).  
- Presentation is clear, notation well-defined, analysis thorough, and algorithm straightforward to implement.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis, particularly for the dictionary learning step.  
- Suggests future work: adapt the LRR/dictionary learning to perform clustering/segmentation directly or encourage cluster-specific dictionaries.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper, strong theoretical and empirical evidence; recommend acceptance.
