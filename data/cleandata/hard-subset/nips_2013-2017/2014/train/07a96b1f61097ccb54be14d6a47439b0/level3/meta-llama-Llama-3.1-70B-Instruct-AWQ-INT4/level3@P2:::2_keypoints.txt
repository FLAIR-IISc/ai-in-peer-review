- Introduces a dictionary-based Low-Rank Representation (LRR) method to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, addressing RPCA limitations.
- Provides a theoretical framework with theorems characterizing conditions for exact reconstruction given a dictionary.
- Analyzes coherence parameters and their properties as part of the theory.
- Offers empirical results that justify the heuristic dictionary-learning algorithm used.
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear explanation of RPCA’s limitations and the need for a new approach.
- Main contributions, claims, and impact are not clearly explained and are accessible mainly to specialists.
- The concept of coherence is hard to understand and its interpretation is indirect and relegated to the appendix.
- Language and exposition require substantial editing and rewriting for broader accessibility.
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical validation (8/10).
- Significance: advances the state of the art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements (9/10).
- Quality: technically sound but clarity issues reduce overall quality (8/10).
- Recommendation: publishable and interesting but requires a major revision to fix clarity, exposition, and coherence explanations.
