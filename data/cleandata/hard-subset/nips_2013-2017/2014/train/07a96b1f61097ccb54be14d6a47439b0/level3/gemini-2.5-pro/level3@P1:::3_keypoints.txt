- RPCA fails when the low-rank matrix is coherent (data on a union of subspaces); coherence parameters μ2 and μ3 grow with cluster count and weaken RPCA guarantees.  
- Main contribution: using LRR with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains that of L0 removes dependence on μ2 and μ3.  
- Practical two-stage algorithm: estimate L0 via RPCA, build dictionary A from that estimate, then run LRR for improved recovery.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 provide formal guarantees that LRR with such A achieves exact/near-exact recovery under much weaker coherence assumptions.  
- Experiments on synthetic data show a much larger success region for the proposed method than RPCA (phase transition plots).  
- Real-world validation on Hopkins155 motion sequences demonstrates substantial improvement in downstream subspace clustering.  
- The work insightfully links coherence parameters to union-of-subspaces structure, bridging low-rank recovery and subspace clustering.  
- Broader impact: improves robust low-rank recovery for structured/clustered data with applications in vision, text, and more.  
- Writing could be more precise (e.g., assertive phrasing about RPCA failure and clearer statements about coherence measuring clustering).  
- Experiments should include an additional baseline pipeline (robust subspace clustering such as SSC on corrupted data followed by PCA) for fuller comparison.  
- Discuss relation to prior work showing coherence can help subspace clustering (e.g., Wang & Xu ICML 2013) to clarify the trade-off addressed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, novel, technically sound, and empirically validated with only minor issues to address.
