- Problem: RPCA struggles with coherent data where low-rank components have clustering structures, causing high coherence parameters and poor recovery.
- Insight: Coherence parameters reflect underlying clustering structure rather than being unavoidable theoretical bottlenecks.
- Approach: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a suitably constructed low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence-induced failure.
- Theory: Theorems 1 and 2 show LRR with a low-rank dictionary can avoid dependence on the coherence parameter μ₂.
- Assumption/limitation: Method requires a low-rank dictionary containing information about the ground truth matrix, which is nontrivial in unsupervised settings.
- Algorithm: Authors provide a practical unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm tailored to the theory.
- Experiments: Synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences convincingly show LRR outperforms RPCA, especially for coherent data.
- Empirical impact: Method reduces clustering error on Hopkins155 by about 40% relative to RPCA.
- Clarity issues: Some ambiguous phrasing (lines 80–82), an incomplete sentence (line 88), and the need for clearer linkage between theory and the practical algorithm.
- Originality: Novel characterization of coherence–clustering relationship and a new theoretical framework for using low-rank dictionaries in LRR.
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental RPCA limitation with broad applicability (motion, images, text) and advances robust low-rank recovery and subspace clustering integration.
- Recommendation: Accept — substantial theoretical and empirical contribution with minor clarity issues to fix.
