## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses the problem of recovering low-rank matrices corrupted by noise, specifically focusing on the challenges posed by data exhibiting clustering structures (high coherence). The authors argue that standard Robust PCA (RPCA) struggles with such coherent data and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach utilizing a carefully constructed dictionary to mitigate this issue. Their key insight is that a low-rank dictionary can effectively decouple coherence from recovery guarantees. They provide theoretical results establishing conditions for successful reconstruction and propose a heuristic algorithm – applying RPCA initially to inform dictionary learning – for unsupervised environments.

The work builds upon recent advances in RPCA and LRR, contributing new theorems regarding recovery conditions with low-rank dictionaries. The empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data demonstrates improvements over RPCA, supporting their claims. The technical soundness of the proofs appears reasonable upon cursory inspection, and the authors acknowledge the heuristic nature of their dictionary learning approach. 

However, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues. While the core idea is potentially valuable, it is presented in a manner that is inaccessible to anyone not already deeply familiar with the field. The main contributions and their impact are not clearly articulated. The concept of “coherence,” while central to the paper, is difficult to grasp, with its detailed explanation relegated to the appendix. The language is dense and the exposition lacks sufficient motivation and intuitive explanations. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel theoretical results extending LRR to handle coherent data.
*   Empirical results demonstrating improvements over RPCA.
*   Addresses a relevant problem in robust matrix recovery.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Severe clarity issues hindering understanding for a broader audience.
*   Heuristic dictionary learning algorithm lacks theoretical justification.
*   The significance of the work is obscured by the presentation.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a technically sound contribution with potentially significant implications. However, its current form is unlikely to be widely appreciated due to substantial clarity problems. A major revision focusing on improved exposition, clearer articulation of contributions, and more intuitive explanations is necessary. The authors should specifically address how they plan to improve accessibility for readers beyond specialists in this niche area. Despite these concerns, the underlying ideas are interesting and warrant further development, making this paper potentially publishable *after* substantial rewriting.
