- Proposes a novel method combining robust PCA with dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Theorems show the approach avoids coherence parameters that grow with the number of underlying clusters.  
- Method is technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Experiments on synthetic matrices and real motion sequences validate effectiveness.  
- Demonstrates clear improvement over alternatives like RPCA.  
- The dictionary-finding algorithm is heuristic but effective in practice.  
- Original contribution advancing state-of-the-art in low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Significant practical solution for recovering coherent data in challenging applications.  
- Writing and organization are unclear, making main contributions and impact hard to follow.  
- Needs more detailed algorithmic/theoretical explanations and intuitive examples.  
- Paper is thorough and honest in evaluation—complete work rather than a position paper.  
- May not be accessible to a broad audience due to technical complexity and lack of clarity.
