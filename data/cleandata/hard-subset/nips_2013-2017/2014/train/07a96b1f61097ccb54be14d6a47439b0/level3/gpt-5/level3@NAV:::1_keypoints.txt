- Problem: Robust subspace recovery under gross corruption and coherent (clustered) data, where RPCA fails as cluster count grows due to coherence.
- Key insight: Identify the coherence bottleneck (µ2-phenomenon) tied to clustered row spaces that degrades RPCA.
- Main theoretical contribution: Show LRR with a suitably low-rank dictionary A can remove dependence on µ2 if U0 ⊂ UA.
- Guarantees: Provide noiseless and noisy recovery proofs with near-optimal rank bounds and explicit probability conditions.
- Practical algorithm: Propose an unsupervised method that uses RPCA to estimate a low-rank dictionary for LRR.
- Experiments: Synthetic and motion-segmentation tests (vs SIM, LRRx, SSC) show clear gains over RPCA in recovery and clustering error.
- Theory quality: Careful, technically sound analysis with explicit conditions and probability bounds.
- Empirical quality: Convincing experiments and meaningful downstream impact demonstrated.
- Sensitivity concerns: Need analysis of dependence on RPCA initialization quality.
- Rank/threshold robustness: Need study of robustness to rank estimation and the 10−3 threshold choice.
- Model mismatch: Require experiments when U0 ⊄ UA to test assumption sensitivity.
- Ablations: Missing ablation studies for Steps 2–4 of Algorithm 1 to isolate contributions.
- Clarity suggestions: Add early intuition for why low-rank dictionaries neutralize µ2 and a brief proof schematic.
- Presentation improvements: Include a short complexity discussion and notation cleanup in Sec. 3.
- Originality: Novel identification of coherence–clustering link and theoretical route to bypass µ2 via dictionary design.
- Significance: Important advance likely to be adopted in practice for union-of-subspaces data.
- Applicability question: Could the learned dictionary be used directly for clustering/affinity construction or a joint recovery–segmentation model?
- Rank adaptation: Request comment on adaptive selection of rank(A).
- Pros summary: Principled address of RPCA limitation, strong theory, practical algorithm, solid experiments.
- Cons summary: Limited sensitivity/ablation analyses and heavy reliance on U0 ⊂ UA assumption.
- Recommendation: Accept.
