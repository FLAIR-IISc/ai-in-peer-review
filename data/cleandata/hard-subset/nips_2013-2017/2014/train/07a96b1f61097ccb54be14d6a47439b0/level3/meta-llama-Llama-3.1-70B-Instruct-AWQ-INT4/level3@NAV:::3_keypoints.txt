- Proposes a novel method to improve robust PCA by incorporating a learned dictionary into the LRR framework.  
- Shows the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure in data rather than being an unavoidable bottleneck.  
- Proves that under certain dictionary A conditions, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ and give stronger exact and noisy low-rank+sparse recovery guarantees.  
- Provides thorough analysis of limitations of existing RPCA methods and how the new approach addresses them.  
- Uses a learned dictionary in LRR, a novel idea not previously explored.  
- Presents rigorous and well-presented mathematical derivations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness with extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and concise explanations.  
- Minor weakness: needs clearer connection to prior union-of-subspace / subspace-clustering work.  
- Minor weakness: should provide more details on computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept to NIPS.  
- Reviewer rating: 9/10, confidence: 9/10.
