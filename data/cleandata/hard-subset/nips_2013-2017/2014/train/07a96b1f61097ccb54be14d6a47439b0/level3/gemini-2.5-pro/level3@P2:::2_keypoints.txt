- Addresses recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations and the failure of RPCA on "coherent" (clustered) data.  
- Identifies coherence (especially high µ2) as a key limitation of RPCA caused by clustering structure.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with representation X = AZ + S to overcome RPCA's coherence sensitivity.  
- Proves that if dictionary A is low-rank and its column space contains that of L0, LRR is immune to the coherence parameter.  
- Main theoretical contributions are formalized in Theorems 1 and 2, which rigorously support the central claim.  
- Offers a practical two-stage algorithm: initial RPCA to estimate L0, then build a low-rank dictionary from that estimate and apply LRR.  
- Empirical results are strong: ~47% larger success region on synthetic data and ~40% relative improvement in clustering error on motion segmentation.  
- Originality lies in connecting coherence to clustering and showing a low-rank dictionary in LRR mitigates RPCA’s limitation.  
- Paper is technically sound and the results are significant and likely impactful for practitioners.  
- Major weakness: poor clarity—dense writing and inaccessible exposition for non-experts.  
- Coherence parameters are defined mathematically but lack intuitive explanation or illustrative examples linking clustering to high µ2.  
- Dictionary-learning step is heuristic; theoretical guarantees apply only to the second LRR step assuming a good dictionary exists.  
- Suggested fixes: rewrite the introduction for accessibility, add intuitive examples/figures for coherence, and substantially improve language and exposition.  
- Overall: valuable and publishable contribution with strong theory and experiments, but requires substantial revision for clarity to reach a broader audience.
