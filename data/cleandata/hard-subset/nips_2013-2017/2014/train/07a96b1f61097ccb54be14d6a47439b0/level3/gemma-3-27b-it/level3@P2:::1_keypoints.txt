- Problem addressed: RPCA performance degrades with increasing data coherence (e.g., clustering); the paper targets this limitation in robust low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Key idea: Using a low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework can mitigate the detrimental effects of coherence.  
- Theoretical contribution: Rigorous analysis showing conditions under which LRR avoids dependence on the coherence parameter.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Practical unsupervised algorithm to learn a suitable low-rank dictionary.  
- Empirical validation: Extensive synthetic and real motion-sequence experiments showing improvements over RPCA.  
- Clarity and relevance: Well-motivated, clearly written, well-organized, and connects coherence to clustering with appropriate citations.  
- Strengths: Strong theoretical grounding, practical impact, valuable insights into coherence–clustering relationship, and convincing empirical results.  
- Minor weaknesses: Lacks detailed computational/runtime analysis and a fuller discussion (or negative result) on iterative dictionary refinement.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — high-quality, novel, and broadly relevant contribution.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
