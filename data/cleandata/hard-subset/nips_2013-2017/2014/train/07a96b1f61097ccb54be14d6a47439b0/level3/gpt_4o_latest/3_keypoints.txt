- Proposes integrating Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary for robust low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Identifies RPCA’s key weakness: reliance on the incoherence parameter μ, which fails on clustered/coherent data.  
- Shows that using LRR with a low-rank dictionary reduces dependence on μ and yields stronger theoretical guarantees for low-rank+sparse decomposition, including noisy cases.  
- Generalizes prior subspace-clustering assumptions, making the method applicable to a broader range of datasets.  
- Introduces a principled method to handle coherent data by learning dictionaries that exploit clustering structure.  
- Extends theoretical understanding of coherence parameters and demonstrates how LRR can bypass RPCA limitations.  
- Proposes a practical algorithm combining RPCA and LRR with superior empirical performance on synthetic and real data (e.g., Hopkins155).  
- Reports substantial empirical gains (up to ~40% reduction in clustering error vs RPCA+SSC) and only ~1.2× RPCA runtime.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with clear proofs and extensive experimental validation.  
- Weakness: some manuscript sections lack clarity (e.g., lines 80–82 and 88) and the writing assumes advanced background, hurting accessibility.  
- Weakness: limited experimental comparisons—should include methods like noisy subspace clustering followed by PCA.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; significant contribution to compressed sensing and subspace clustering.
