- Addresses RPCA limitation: performance degrades as data coherence increases.  
- Notes coherence increases with clustering structures common in real data.  
- Key insight: LRR with a low-rank dictionary that contains subspace information is immune to RPCA’s coherence issues.  
- Provides theoretical proof showing LRR with low-rank dictionaries has stronger recovery conditions than RPCA.  
- Quantifies improvement in recovery conditions (better asymptotic bound for LRR vs RPCA).  
- Proposes a practical algorithm that uses RPCA to construct an appropriate low-rank dictionary for LRR.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real motion data show LRR approach outperforms RPCA.  
- Reports a 40% relative improvement in clustering error rates compared to RPCA on motion sequences.  
- Clarifies that coherence parameters reflect real data structure (e.g., clustering), not just theoretical artifacts.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, making a significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.  
- Main weakness: lacks explicit discussion of how improved recovery translates to subspace clustering/segmentation performance and comparisons to existing clustering approaches.
