- Paper identifies a µ2-phenomenon: clustering (union-of-subspaces) raises row-space coherence µ2, breaking RPCA’s recovery conditions.  
- Shows LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA) yields recovery guarantees independent of µ2 (and µ3).  
- Provides noiseless and noisy theorems with explicit rank and sparsity bounds and standard λ = 1/√n1 scaling.  
- Proposes a simple unsupervised heuristic: run RPCA → rank-truncate & normalize to form Â → run LRR with Â (“low-rank dictionary pursuit”).  
- Empirically expands exact-recovery region by ~47% on synthetic data compared to RPCA.  
- On motion segmentation tasks, using the learned dictionary consistently reduces downstream clustering error (e.g., SSC error 9.50% → 5.74%).  
- Strength: clear conceptual insight linking coherence to clustering and how a low-rank dictionary avoids µ2 dependence.  
- Strength: theorems are precise and align with RPCA/LRR literature; proofs appear technically sound on spot-check.  
- Strength: practical heuristic combines RPCA robustness with LRR’s structure preservation at only ~1.2× RPCA runtime.  
- Weakness: exposition and clarity are poor; intuition for coherence and the µ2-phenomenon is buried in appendices.  
- Weakness: the key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is underexplained and its practical verifiability is unclear.  
- Weakness: heuristic design choices (rank threshold, normalization, non-iterative choice) lack ablation and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited (one synthetic suite and motion segmentation); missing comparisons to other robust subspace methods.  
- Quality: technically solid with standard assumptions and probabilistic scaling; empirical results support theory despite heuristic nature.  
- Clarity recommendations: state contributions up front, move µ intuition to main text, add practitioner checklist for dictionary diagnostics, and streamline notation.  
- Originality: nontrivial new recovery theorems for LRR with low-rank dictionaries and useful articulation of coherence as clustering-induced.  
- Significance: high practical and theoretical relevance for handling coherent/clustered data and improving robust subspace recovery.  
- Suggestions: add figures for coherence intuition, ablations (ranking, normalization, iterative updates), broader baselines, practical guidance for λ/ε, and code; improve language.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, simple effective method, and meaningful empirical gains addressing a common RPCA failure mode.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor exposition hindering accessibility, limited experiments, and strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA needing more justification.  
- Recommendation: publishable and interesting but requires substantial rewriting and expanded experiments; reviewer leans weak accept conditional on improvements.
