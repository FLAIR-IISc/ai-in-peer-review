- Problem: Recover low-rank matrices corrupted by noise and outliers, extending Robust PCA (RPCA).
- Identified limitation: RPCA degrades when underlying low-rank data has clustering structure, increasing coherence.
- Central insight: A well-configured dictionary in the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework can mitigate coherence issues, especially if the dictionary is low-rank.
- Theory: The paper provides Theorems 1 and 2 with conditions guaranteeing successful recovery under the proposed model.
- Algorithm: Proposes a practical method that uses RPCA to learn a suitable low-rank dictionary for LRR.
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-world motion sequence experiments show improved recovery over RPCA in clustered-data scenarios.
- Related work: Builds appropriately on RPCA, matrix completion, and LRR literature with correct citations and positioning.
- Technical soundness: Analysis appears mathematically rigorous and the theorems are noteworthy.
- Originality: Novel combination of RPCA and LRR with focus on dictionary learning to address coherence from clustering.
- Significance: Demonstrates tangible performance gains with potential impact on motion segmentation, image processing, and dimensionality reduction.
- Empirical validation: Experiments provide strong support for theoretical claims.
- Weakness — Clarity: Paper is dense and hard to follow, particularly for non-experts.
- Weakness — Exposition and language: Writing is convoluted; needs more illustrative examples and streamlined presentation.
- Weakness — Accessibility of concepts: Key concept "coherence" is not intuitively explained and is buried in the appendix.
- Weakness — Heuristic dictionary learning: Algorithm relies on RPCA initialization and lacks full theoretical justification.
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with novel approach, solid theory, and convincing empirical evidence.
- Argument against acceptance: Poor clarity limits impact; heuristic nature of algorithm questions generality and robustness; may be too specialized for broad audience without rewrite.
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and potentially significant but requires major revision to improve clarity and accessibility.
- Required revisions: Simplify exposition (esp. coherence), restructure to state problem/solution/contributions up front, improve writing quality, discuss limitations of the heuristic dictionary approach, and provide broader application context.
