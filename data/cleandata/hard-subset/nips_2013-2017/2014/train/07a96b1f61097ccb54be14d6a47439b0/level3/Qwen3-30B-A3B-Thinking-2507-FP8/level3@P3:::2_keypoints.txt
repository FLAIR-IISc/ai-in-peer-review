- Addresses a significant limitation of RPCA: poor performance on coherent (e.g., clustered) data common in practice.
- Key idea: incorporate a low-rank dictionary into LRR to better represent structured/coherent data.
- Theoretical contributions: proves recovery guarantees for LRR with a low-rank dictionary (Theorem 1 noiseless, Theorem 2 noisy).
- Shows LRR with a dictionary satisfying U0 ⊂ UA avoids harmful coherence parameters that degrade RPCA.
- Analyzes coherence parameters (µ1, µ2, µ3) and identifies the "µ2-phenomenon" where µ2 grows with cluster number.
- Establishes a looser rank condition for recovery (O(n2/log n1) vs. RPCA’s O(n2/(log n1)2)).
- Proposes a practical heuristic (Algorithm 1) that uses RPCA to construct the dictionary for LRR.
- Empirical validation: wider success region on synthetic data (47% improvement) and lower clustering error on real motion sequences (40% relative improvement).
- Provides insight that coherence has physical meaning tied to data structures like clustering, not just a theoretical assumption.
- Novelty: theoretically connects dictionary structure to coherence, extending RPCA’s framework for coherent data.
- Technical soundness: proofs and claims are well-supported.
- Major clarity/exposition issues: coherence concept is poorly explained and buried in the appendix, terms like "coherent" vs "less incoherent" are confusing.
- Language and presentation require substantial editing and a more accessible explanation linking theory to practice.
- Recommendation: publishable with major revisions to improve clarity and accessibility.
