This paper proposes a novel approach to recover coherent data via low-rank dictionary pursuit. The authors argue that the Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) method, which is widely used for low-rank matrix recovery, may not be effective for coherent data. They show that the coherence parameter, which is a critical component in RPCA, can be avoided by using a low-rank dictionary in the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the exact and noisy low-rank + sparse recovery using LRR with a learned dictionary.

The main contributions of this paper are threefold. Firstly, the authors identify the clustering-induced high coherence in the data, which can lead to poor performance of RPCA. Secondly, they propose a novel approach to exploit this coherence by using a dictionary in LRR. Thirdly, they partially solve the sparse corruption in subspace clustering by using the learned dictionary.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed approach. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experiments are well-designed to validate the theoretical claims. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of the related work and highlight the significance of their contributions.

However, there are a few areas that can be improved. The language precision can be improved in some places, and the authors should be explicit about their claims and references. For example, the phrase "the coherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure in the data rather than an unavoidable bottleneck" is a bit ambiguous and can be rephrased for better clarity. Additionally, the authors can provide more details about the connection between their work and the prior union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering work.

In terms of the reviewer's suggestions, the authors can consider empirical comparison to a pipeline of noisy sparse subspace clustering (ℓ1) followed by per-subspace PCA. This can provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Overall, this paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing and low-rank matrix recovery. The proposed approach is novel, significant, and has the potential to be applied to various domains, including text analysis. Therefore, I recommend acceptance to NIPS.
