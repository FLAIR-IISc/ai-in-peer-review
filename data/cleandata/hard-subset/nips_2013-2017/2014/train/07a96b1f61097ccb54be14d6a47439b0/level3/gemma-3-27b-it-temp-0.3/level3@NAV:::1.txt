## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses the important problem of robust low-rank matrix recovery in the presence of corruption, specifically focusing on the degradation of Robust PCA (RPCA) performance as data coherence increases. The authors correctly identify a fundamental bottleneck in existing RPCA methods related to data coherence and propose a clever solution leveraging Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed dictionary. The motivation is strong, as real-world data often exhibits clustering structures leading to higher coherence, and overcoming this limitation has significant practical implications.

The core contribution lies in demonstrating that using a low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework circumvents the coherence-related bottleneck. This is supported by a theoretical analysis establishing conditions for successful recovery and validated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data. The paper builds upon the established work of Candès et al. on RPCA [4] and Liu et al. on LRR [13], clearly positioning itself within the existing literature. The authors appropriately cite relevant prior work and provide a thorough discussion of related concepts like coherence parameters. 

The paper is well-written and logically organized, presenting a clear explanation of the problem, proposed solution, and experimental results. The theoretical analysis appears sound, and the experimental validation convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach compared to standard RPCA. The inclusion of results on motion sequences adds practical relevance. 

**Strengths:**
*   Addresses a significant limitation of existing RPCA methods.
*   Proposes a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.
*   Strong connection to relevant prior work.

**Weaknesses:**
*   While the algorithm outperforms RPCA, the gains in the motion sequence experiments are moderate. Further investigation into scenarios where the improvement is more substantial would be beneficial.
*   The discussion of the unsupervised dictionary learning algorithm is somewhat brief. More details on the practical considerations of implementing this step would be helpful.

**Arguments for Acceptance:** This paper tackles an important problem in RPCA, offers a novel and effective solution, and provides strong theoretical and empirical support for its claims. The work is of high quality and represents a significant contribution to the field.

**Question for the Authors:** The proposed method shows promise for handling coherent data. Could this approach be extended or adapted for applications in subspace clustering or segmentation, where identifying underlying subspace structures is a primary goal? We would appreciate your comments on the potential applicability and challenges of using this framework for such tasks.

**Overall Recommendation:** Accept.
