This paper proposes a novel approach to improve the robustness of Principal Component Analysis (PCA) by incorporating a learned dictionary into the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework. The authors demonstrate that the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure in the data, rather than being an unavoidable bottleneck. They prove that under certain conditions on the dictionary A, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ and yield stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank + sparse recovery.

The paper makes significant contributions to the field of compressed sensing and robust PCA. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing RPCA methods and propose a new approach that addresses these limitations. The use of a learned dictionary in LRR is a novel idea that has not been explored before. The paper also provides extensive experimental results on both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The mathematical derivations are rigorous and well-presented. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods.

One minor criticism is that the paper could benefit from more clarity on the connection between the proposed approach and prior work on union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering. Additionally, the authors could provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.

Overall, I highly recommend this paper for acceptance to NIPS. The paper makes significant contributions to the field, and the results are well-presented and thoroughly evaluated.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to improving robust PCA using a learned dictionary in LRR
* Thorough analysis of the limitations of existing RPCA methods
* Extensive experimental results on both synthetic and real-world data
* Rigorous mathematical derivations
* Well-written and clear presentation

Weaknesses:

* Could benefit from more clarity on the connection to prior work on union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering
* Could provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm
