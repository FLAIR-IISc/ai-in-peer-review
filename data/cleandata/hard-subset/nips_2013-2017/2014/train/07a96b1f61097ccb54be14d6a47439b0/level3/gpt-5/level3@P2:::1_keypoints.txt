- Paper studies robust low-rank recovery when clean data are coherent due to clustering and formalizes the µ2-phenomenon where µ2 grows with number of clusters, degrading RPCA.
- Shows that Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space can avoid dependence on µ2 in exact recovery guarantees.
- Provides noiseless and noisy recovery theorems for LRR that depend on µ1(A) but not on µ2(L0), effectively “immunizing” recovery against cluster-induced coherence.
- Proposes a practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to get an initial estimate, build a low-rank column-normalized dictionary from it, then solve LRR.
- Empirical results on synthetic data and corrupted motion sequences show expanded success region and reduced clustering error versus RPCA and LRR with A = X.
- Work is well connected to prior literature (RPCA/PCP, LRR, Outlier Pursuit, SSC, SIM) and clarifies coherence assumptions in RPCA analysis.
- Strength: clear identification and analysis of RPCA’s µ2-related limitation induced by clustering, with practical relevance.
- Strength: solid theory with explicit, interpretable assumptions (low-rank A, U0 ⊂ UA, column normalization).
- Strength: simple, unsupervised, and well-justified two-stage practical algorithm leveraging RPCA as a bootstrap.
- Strength: convincing empirical improvements on synthetic and motion segmentation tasks.
- Strength: offers a principled criterion for dictionary construction in LRR beyond the common A = X choice.
- Weakness: main theoretical guarantees require U0 ⊂ UA, but there is no formal analysis of when the RPCA-based bootstrap achieves this in practice.
- Weakness: sensitivity to λ = 1/√n1 and to imperfect column normalization is not empirically characterized.
- Weakness: limited real-data evaluation (11 sequences); broader datasets and additional metrics (e.g., recovery error vs corruption) would strengthen claims.
- Weakness: does not address cases where µ1 is large (row coherence) or whether analogous remedies exist for µ1-related issues.
- Weakness: algorithmic details (solvers, stopping criteria) and more extensive runtime/complexity analysis are missing for reproducibility.
- Weakness: unclear how the learned dictionary directly improves subspace clustering compared to LRR with A = X or other clustering methods.
- Quality: theory appears sound and fills a gap by designing A to avoid µ2; empirical results are consistent with claims but would benefit from theory for the learned-dictionary setting.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written; more intuition on why low-rank, span-containing dictionaries neutralize µ2 and details on normalization/λ would help.
- Originality: novelty in formalizing µ2-clustering connection, proving µ2-free guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries, and proposing the unsupervised dictionary-learning strategy.
- Significance: results are practically relevant for clustered/coherent data and suggest principled improvements for robust recovery and downstream tasks.
- Requests to authors: comment on using the learned dictionary directly for LRR-based clustering and compare to LRRx/SSC in that context.
- Requests to authors: provide empirical sensitivity analysis for λ and the rank-threshold (σi > 10−3σ1).
- Requests to authors: discuss extending theory to the case where A only approximately spans U0 and to scenarios with large µ1.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a practical RPCA failure mode with new theory and a usable algorithm, demonstrating empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: guarantees depend on conditions only approximately met by the unsupervised procedure, experiments are narrow, and µ1-related issues are underexplored.
- Recommendation: Accept (confidence 4/5).
