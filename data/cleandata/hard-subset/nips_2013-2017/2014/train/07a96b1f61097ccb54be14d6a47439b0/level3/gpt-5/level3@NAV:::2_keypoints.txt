- Problem: Recovers low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations when clustered clean data increase coherence and break RPCA.  
- Key idea: Use a low-rank dictionary A in LRR to eliminate dependence on the problematic µ2 coherence parameter.  
- Theoretical results: Prove noiseless and noisy recovery theorems under conditions (U0 ⊂ UA, rank(A) small relative to n2/µ1(A)) that avoid µ2 and are stronger than RPCA (up to log factors).  
- Unsupervised heuristic: Run RPCA, truncate its SVD to a low-rank estimate, column-normalize to form A, then solve LRR.  
- Empirics: Synthetic phase-transition experiments and corrupted motion-trajectory tests show clear improvements over RPCA and better downstream subspace clustering accuracy.  
- Quality of theory: Theoretical statements and proofs appear technically sound and carefully checked.  
- Empirical limitations: Experiments support theory but lack stronger baselines and ablations (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, Stable PCP, LRR with A=X, GoDec/IRLS, sensitivity to λ/ε, dictionary rank/misspecification, scalability).  
- Clarity weaknesses: Paper fails to foreground contributions, intuition, and impact; coherence/µ2 explanation is hard to parse and mostly in the appendix; language and organization need editing.  
- Suggested clarity fixes: Move µ2 intuition and “µ2-phenomenon” into the main text with figures, explicitly state key theorem assumptions/implications, provide pseudocode with complexity and failure modes, and discuss when dictionary assumption fails.  
- Originality: The low-rank dictionary approach to neutralize coherence and its theoretical analysis is a meaningful advance.  
- Significance: Addresses a real RPCA limitation, provides rigorous guarantees, shows practical gains, and is likely to influence robust subspace recovery and clustering.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theoretical contribution (µ2-independence) with a practical algorithm and clear empirical improvements; principled bridge between RPCA and LRR.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Major clarity/exposition issues, limited ablations and baseline comparisons, scalability not well evaluated, and heuristic lacks analysis of failure cases.  
- Recommendation: Publishable and interesting; recommend acceptance contingent on substantial rewriting for clarity.
