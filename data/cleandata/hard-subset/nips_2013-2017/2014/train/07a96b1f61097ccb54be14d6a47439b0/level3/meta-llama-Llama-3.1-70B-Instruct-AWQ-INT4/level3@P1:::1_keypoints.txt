- Addresses recovery of low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations and highlights RPCA's failure on coherent data.  
- Identifies coherence in real-world data as a key limitation that degrades RPCA performance.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit (LRDP) within the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework to handle coherent data.  
- Shows LRDP can avoid coherence parameters that grow with the number of underlying clusters, improving robustness.  
- Provides a practical algorithm to learn the dictionary matrix for LRDP.  
- Offers theoretical analysis demonstrating LRDP’s advantages over RPCA.  
- Presents empirical experiments where LRDP outperforms RPCA in low-rank recovery.  
- Claims practical significance for applications like image processing, computer vision, and data analysis.  
- Paper is well-written and meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Scope is limited to low-rank matrix recovery; applicability to tasks like subspace clustering or segmentation is unclear.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis despite claiming lower complexity than RPCA.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, based on novelty, theory, empirical results, and practical relevance.
