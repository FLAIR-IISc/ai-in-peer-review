- Problem: RPCA performance degrades when data exhibits high coherence, often due to clustering structures.  
- Bottleneck identified: Data coherence (µ2-phenomenon) limits RPCA’s recovery guarantees.  
- Proposed solution: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to overcome coherence-related failure modes.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorems 1 and 2 show a low-rank dictionary in LRR can circumvent the coherence bottleneck.  
- Practical algorithm: Algorithm 1 provides an unsupervised method to construct the dictionary (based on RPCA output).  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences demonstrate clear improvement over RPCA.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Candès et al. RPCA, extends LRR literature by analyzing the impact of dictionary choice, and connects to matrix factorization work.  
- Strengths summarized: Tackles an important problem, strong theoretical grounding, practical algorithm, convincing experiments, and clear exposition.  
- Key weaknesses: Dictionary depends on initial RPCA (sensitivity if RPCA fails), limited computational complexity analysis, and little exploration of alternative dictionary initializations.  
- Applicability: Method is directly applicable to subspace clustering/segmentation and improves clustering by better low-rank recovery.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Important challenge, novel and effective solution, solid theory and experiments, well-written and well-positioned.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on initial RPCA and incomplete complexity analysis are notable limitations.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality paper with minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
