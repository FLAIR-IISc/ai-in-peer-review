- Problem: RPCA degrades on coherent data common in practice because clustering increases coherence parameters (µ₁, µ₂, µ₃).  
- Key empirical observation: The "µ₂-phenomenon" shows coherence rises with cluster count and harms RPCA (Figure 2).  
- Core contribution: Proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to overcome coherence-driven failure.  
- Theoretical result: Theorem 1 proves that if dictionary A is low-rank and U₀ ⊂ Uₐ, LRR avoids dependence on µ₂.  
- Practical algorithm: Presents an unsupervised Algorithm 1 that constructs A from RPCA output and costs ~20% more computation than RPCA.  
- Synthetic results: Success region expands by 47% compared to RPCA (Figure 4).  
- Real-data results: On Hopkins155 motion sequences, clustering error drops ~40% versus RPCA (Table 1).  
- Originality & theory: First work linking coherence to clustering structure and formally resolving dictionary design for coherent data.  
- Significance: Direct impact on subspace clustering applications and addresses a long-standing RPCA limitation.  
- Quality: Proofs are rigorous with tight bounds; experiments and presentation are clear and well-motivated.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 assumes RPCA’s output is “good enough”; paper lacks analysis of error propagation from L̂₀ to A.  
- Suggestion: Iterative refinement (updating A with ÂZ∗) could improve robustness but is not explored.  
- Theoretical trade-off: Rank bound is weaker (O(n²/log n)) than RPCA’s O(n²/(log n)²), and discussion of when this matters is limited.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Candès et al. (RPCA) and Liu et al. (LRR) by explaining coherence and solving dictionary design beyond A = X.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — minor issues do not undermine the paper’s novelty, rigor, or empirical impact.
