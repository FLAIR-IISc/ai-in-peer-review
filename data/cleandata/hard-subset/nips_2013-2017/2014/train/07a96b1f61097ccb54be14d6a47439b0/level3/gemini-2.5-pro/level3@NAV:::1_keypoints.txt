- Problem: Robustly estimate a low-dimensional subspace from observations with gross, sparse errors; builds on RPCA and targets its coherence-related failure modes.
- Key limitation identified: Standard RPCA performance degrades as the coherence of the low-rank matrix increases, common in clustered real-world data.
- Core contribution: A novel LRR-based method using a learned low-rank dictionary to avoid coherence-related issues.
- Algorithmic contribution: A practical two-stage algorithm for learning the dictionary and performing recovery.
- Theoretical guarantees: Theorems 1 and 2 formally prove the method is immune to the coherence parameters that hinder RPCA.
- Synthetic experiments: Demonstrates a 47% wider success region compared to baseline methods.
- Real-world validation: Strong empirical results on motion segmentation tasks relying on subspace clustering.
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental, practically relevant RPCA limitation and advances the state-of-the-art.
- Originality: Novel and insightful use of a learned low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework to counteract coherence.
- Minor weakness: Method depends on an initial RPCA step; robustness when that initial estimate is poor is not fully discussed.
- Suggestion to authors: Consider integrating dictionary learning with subspace clustering/segmentation rather than treating it solely as preprocessing.
- Overall verdict: High-quality, well-motivated, thoroughly validated work; strong recommendation for acceptance.
