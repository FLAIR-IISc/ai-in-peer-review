- Proposal: use LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary A to circumvent coherence issues.
- Theoretical result: if A is low-rank and contains the true low-rank column space, LRR avoids dependence on coherence μ.
- Key contribution 1: identifies clustering-induced high coherence as a natural phenomenon.
- Key contribution 2: shows how dictionary learning in LRR exploits clustering structure to overcome coherence.
- Key contribution 3: demonstrates the approach solves sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
- Strong theoretical analysis with careful proofs establishing conditions where LRR outperforms RPCA.
- Experimental validation: synthetic + Hopkins155 benchmarks show convincing results, ~40% relative improvement in clustering error over RPCA.
