- Problem: RPCA fails to recover low-rank matrices when the true low-rank component is coherent, a situation arising with clustered data (union of subspaces).
- Core contribution: Show LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains L0 can avoid coherence-driven failures of RPCA.
- Theoretical result: Theorems 1 and 2 provide formal recovery guarantees for LRR immune to growing coherence parameters (µ2, µ3) under the stated dictionary conditions.
- Practical algorithm: Two-stage heuristic—run RPCA to estimate L0, build dictionary A from that estimate, then run LRR to refine recovery.
- Strength (technical): Rigorous, technically sound analysis extending convex recovery frameworks to justify the LRR approach.
- Strength (novelty/significance): Identifies and addresses a practical limitation of RPCA, linking clustering-induced coherence to recovery failure and proposing a principled remedy.
- Strength (empirical): Synthetic phase transitions and corrupted motion-sequence experiments show clear, substantial improvement over RPCA and better downstream subspace-clustering performance.
- Weakness (clarity): Presentation is dense and targeted to specialists, making the main contributions hard to grasp for a general NIPS audience.
- Weakness (coherence explanation): The concept of coherence and the µ2-phenomenon are defined mathematically but lack intuitive, high-level explanation and motivation.
- Weakness (writing/organization): Paper needs a clearer narrative flow from problem to theory to algorithm; overall language and exposition require improvement.
- Summary/recommendation: Technically strong and empirically convincing work but hindered by substantial clarity issues; marginally acceptable pending major rewriting.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important RPCA limitation, provides novel theorems, and demonstrates strong empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: Poor accessibility and insufficient intuitive explanation of core concepts (coherence), weakening the paper’s impact.
