- Paper addresses RPCA's failure for coherent data and the challenge of robustly estimating low-dimensional subspaces from contaminated, coherent observations.
- Identifies that RPCA performance degrades as data coherence increases (e.g., with more clusters), exposing a theoretical bottleneck.
- Proposes using the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework with a carefully designed low-rank dictionary to circumvent coherence-related degradation.
- Shows theoretically that if the dictionary is low-rank and encodes ground-truth information, LRR can be immune to coherence parameters that harm RPCA.
- Provides a practical algorithm based on the LRR approach and a dictionary learning strategy for realistic environments.
- Offers the first formal study of recovering low-rank, coherent matrices from corrupted observations.
- Gives insight into the physical meaning of coherence parameters, arguing they reflect intrinsic data structures rather than mere proof artifacts.
- Reframes LRR as a viable model for handling coherent data, expanding its theoretical and practical interpretation.
- Experimental results: the proposed method outperforms RPCA on synthetic data and real motion-sequence benchmarks.
- Demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed dictionary learning strategy in realistic settings.
- Paper quality: clear, well-written, technically sound, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Originality and significance: high; introduces a new approach and perspective on coherence and LRR.
- Recommendation: strong acceptance (reviewer lists no objections).
- Request to authors: clarify whether the method can be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation and provide comments.
