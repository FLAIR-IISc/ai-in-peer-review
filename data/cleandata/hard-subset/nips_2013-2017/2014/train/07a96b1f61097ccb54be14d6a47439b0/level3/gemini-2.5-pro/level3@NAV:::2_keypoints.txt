- Addresses recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, extending Robust PCA (RPCA).
- Identifies RPCA’s weakness when underlying low-rank data is “coherent” (common for clustered data).
- Proposes combining RPCA with a dictionary-based, structure-preserving projection using Low-Rank Representation (LRR).
- Key insight: a properly chosen low-rank dictionary can make recovery immune to RPCA’s coherence limitation.
- Theoretical analysis is strong, with novel theorems formalizing conditions for success.
- Approach advances state of the art in matrix recovery and is technically significant.
- Dictionary learning is heuristic (RPCA first, then build dictionary) but is reasonably justified.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real motion data show clear performance gains over standard RPCA.
- Original contribution in combining techniques and analyzing a specific RPCA failure mode.
- Major clarity and exposition problems: main contributions, claims, and broader significance are poorly explained.
- The concept of “coherence” lacks intuitive explanation and is inappropriately deferred to the appendix.
- Language and presentation need substantial editing; recommend acceptance contingent on major revision to improve clarity and accessibility.
