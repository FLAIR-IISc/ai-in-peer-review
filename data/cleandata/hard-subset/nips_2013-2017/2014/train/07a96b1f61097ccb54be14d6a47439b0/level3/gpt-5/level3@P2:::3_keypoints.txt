- Paper studies failure of RPCA under clustering-induced high coherence and formalizes how µ2 (and µ3) grows with number of clusters.
- Central theory: LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains U0 removes dependence on µ2, improving the rank bound and giving exact/noisy recovery guarantees.
- Practical pipeline: run RPCA to estimate L0, estimate/truncate its rank, normalize columns to form low-rank A, then solve LRR with A.
- Experiments on synthetic union-of-subspaces and Hopkins155 variants show consistent improvement over RPCA and LRR with A = X.
- Novelty: connects clustering-induced coherence to RPCA limitations and proves LRR with a suitable dictionary avoids µ2 dependence.
- Strength: clear identification and empirical/theoretical characterization of the µ2-phenomenon, highlighting a practical failure mode of RPCA.
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees (noiseless and noisy) for LRR with a low-rank dictionary containing U0.
- Strength: simple, unsupervised algorithm (RPCA-initialized A) that is effective and only modestly more expensive (~1.2×).
- Strength: strong empirical validation showing a 47% larger success region on synthetic data and improved clustering errors on motion data.
- Strength: conceptual insight framing coherence parameters as reflecting union-of-subspaces structure rather than mere technicalities.
- Weakness: assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong and the paper lacks formal guarantees that the learned A satisfies this in the unsupervised pipeline.
- Weakness: heuristic choices (rank threshold, column normalization, single pass) are not ablated or sensitivity-analyzed.
- Weakness: missing baselines and comparisons (Outlier Pursuit, weighted PCP, noisy SSC pipeline) and ablation isolating truncation/normalization effects.
- Weakness: experimental realism concern—replacing missing entries by ±1 is adversarial; more realistic missing-data treatments should be evaluated.
- Weakness: some unclear/ambiguous phrasing and overstrong wording (e.g., “immune to µ2”); related work comparisons need tightening.
- Quality: theoretical contributions are sound and well-motivated; experiments sensible but could be more complete with suggested baselines/ablations.
- Clarity: generally well-organized with helpful discussion of coherence parameters, though a few phrasing and completeness issues remain.
- Originality: original identification of clustering-induced coherence as the RPCA failure cause and nontrivial extension showing LRR avoids µ2.
- Significance: high—advances theory and practice for robust low-rank recovery on clustered data with broad application impact.
- Pro for acceptance: addresses a common RPCA failure mode with new theory and an effective practical fix.
- Pro for acceptance: simple algorithm with strong empirical gains across synthetic and real tasks.
- Pro for acceptance: conceptual contribution clarifying the role of coherence in union-of-subspaces data.
- Con for acceptance: strong U0 ⊂ UA assumption without guarantee for the learned dictionary.
- Con for acceptance: missing comparisons/ablations and limited missing-data experiments.
- Con for acceptance: writing issues and some overstrong claims that should be tempered.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on minor revisions for clarity and expanded experiments.
