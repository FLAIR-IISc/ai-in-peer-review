- Problem addressed: RPCA degrades on coherent data caused by clustering structures.  
- Key insight: Coherence parameters μ2 (and μ3) increase with the number of clusters, so coherence reflects intrinsic clustering rather than a mere theoretical constraint.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proves LRR with a low-rank dictionary can avoid dependence on μ2, providing stronger exact and noisy recovery guarantees (Theorems 1 & 2).  
- Required condition: The dictionary must be low-rank and contain the column space of the true low-rank matrix.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Presents a practical unsupervised algorithm that learns an appropriate dictionary from RPCA outputs.  
- Experiments (synthetic): Synthetic tests validate the theoretical claims about coherence and recovery.  
- Experiments (real): On Hopkins155 motion segmentation, the method achieves ~40% relative error reduction over RPCA.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on LRR and subspace clustering literature and extends Wang & Xu by unifying low-rank and union-of-subspace structure.  
- Originality: Novel use of low-rank dictionaries to handle coherent/clustering data and reframing coherence parameters as reflecting clustering.  
- Clarity issues: Some ambiguous/incomplete phrases (notably lines 80–82 and line 88); conditions for guarantees should be stated more precisely and the link to Wang & Xu made earlier.  
- Limitation / missing baseline: Requires dictionary to contain the true column space (approximation needed in practice) and lacks comparison to a noisy sparse subspace-clustering (ℓ1) + per-subspace PCA pipeline.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, significant contribution with broad practical relevance; recommendation to accept.
