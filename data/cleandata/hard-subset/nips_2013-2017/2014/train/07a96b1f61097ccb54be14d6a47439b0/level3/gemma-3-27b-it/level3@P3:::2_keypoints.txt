- Addresses recovery of low-rank matrices with clustered (coherent) structure where RPCA degrades.
- Proposes combining RPCA with a dictionary-based low-rank representation (LRR) to mitigate coherence effects.
- Argues and proves that a judiciously chosen low-rank dictionary enables successful recovery (Theorems 1 & 2).
- Presents a heuristic algorithm to learn a suitable dictionary from RPCA outputs.
- Builds on recent RPCA and LRR work and relates to matrix factorization and GPCA for context.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real motion data show clear improvements over RPCA.
- Theoretical analysis and proofs are technically sound and provide concrete recovery conditions.
- Contribution is original and advances robust low-rank recovery for clustered data.
- Main weakness: poor clarity—central contributions and importance are not clearly articulated.
- The concept of coherence (µ1, µ2, µ3) is defined but remains conceptually murky and buried in the appendix.
- The dictionary-learning method is heuristic and its limitations are insufficiently discussed.
- Writing is dense, verbose, and the organization hinders accessibility for non-experts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, and empirically validated contribution.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility due to clarity issues and practical concerns about the heuristic algorithm.
- Overall: publishable but requires major revision to improve clarity, explain coherence intuitively, and better discuss algorithmic limitations.
