This paper, "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit", proposes a novel approach to recover low-rank and coherent data from corrupted observations. The authors combine robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore the corrupted observation matrices. The key insight is that using a dictionary to represent structured data yields stronger recovery guarantees than robust PCA alone.

The paper provides theorems that state the conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary. The authors also address dictionary learning with a heuristic algorithm, which computes robust PCA and then represents the result with a dictionary. The empirical results justify the heuristic, and the proofs and theory are technically sound.

The paper's main contributions include studying the problem of recovering low-rank and coherent matrices from their corrupted versions, investigating the physical regime where coherent data arise, and proposing a practical algorithm that outperforms robust PCA in experimental studies.

The strengths of the paper include its originality, as it builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical validation. The significance of the paper is that it advances the state of the art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements, despite using a heuristic dictionary method.

However, the paper has some major clarity issues, as the main contributions, claims, and impact are not well explained and are accessible mainly to specialists. The concept of coherence is hard to understand, and its interpretation is indirect and buried in the appendix. The language and exposition require editing, and the authors should comment on how they will address these issues.

Overall, the paper is publishable and interesting, but needs substantial rewriting for broader accessibility. The reviewer would have voted for a major revision.
