- Paper shows RPCA’s incoherence μ (esp. μ2) reflects clustering and is not an unavoidable bottleneck for low-rank+ sparse recovery.  
- Proposes LRR with a learned, low-rank, column-normalized dictionary A that “absorbs” clustered structure to remove dependence on μ2/μ3.  
- Theoretical guarantees: exact/noisy recovery if U0 ⊂ UA and A is low-rank and column-normalized; bounds depend on μ1(A) (not μ2) and use λ = 1/√n1.  
- Rank/sample complexity bounds improve over RPCA (log n1 factor instead of log^2), and U0 ⊂ UA is necessary for exactness.  
- Practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA, build a low-rank normalized dictionary from the RPCA estimate, then run LRR.  
- Algorithmic point: low-rank A makes LRR computationally cheaper than RPCA in practice.  
- Experiments: synthetic grids show +47% expansion of exact recovery region; Hopkins155 clustering error improved (SSC raw 22.8% → 9.5% with RPCA → 5.7% with learned-dictionary LRR).  
- Paper is well-situated relative to RPCA, LRR, SSC, and union-of-subspaces literature and clarifies the “μ2-phenomenon.”  
- Originality: identifies coherence as clustering-induced and introduces learned low-rank dictionary LRR with recovery guarantees that mitigate μ2.  
- Significance: advances understanding of RPCA failure modes and offers a structure-aware fix with potential broad impact in vision, imaging, and text.  
- Strengths: clear identification of μ2’s role, solid theory, simple implementable pipeline, and strong empirical validation.  
- Main weakness: strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA; need sensitivity analysis when RPCA gives biased U0 estimates.  
- Additional weaknesses/suggestions: compare to noisy SSC+per-subspace PCA and LRR with A = X baselines; report parameter sensitivity (λ, normalization) and runtimes; broaden datasets beyond Hopkins155.  
- Clarity issues: fix ambiguous phrases (lines ~80–88), explicitly state which μ is controlled where, restate assumptions at theorem statements, and avoid imprecise claims like “immune to coherence.”  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical and practical contribution despite some baselines, robustness analyses, and minor writing issues.
