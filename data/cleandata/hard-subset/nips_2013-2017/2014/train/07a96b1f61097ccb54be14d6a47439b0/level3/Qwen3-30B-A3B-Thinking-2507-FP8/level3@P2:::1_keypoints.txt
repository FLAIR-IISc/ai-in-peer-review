- Identifies RPCA's limitation: recovery degrades as data coherence increases, common with clustering structures.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary to bypass the coherence bottleneck.  
- Theoretically proves LRR with a low-rank dictionary is immune to coherence parameters (notably µ2) that grow with cluster number.  
- Shows that if the dictionary is low-rank and aligns with the ground truth, LRR can recover low-rank matrices despite coherence.  
- Presents a practical unsupervised algorithm that learns the dictionary from RPCA outputs.  
- Synthetic experiments: proposed algorithm attains a 47% wider success region versus RPCA.  
- Real-data experiments: reduces clustering error on Hopkins155 by ~40% relative to RPCA.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) are sound and well-motivated, linking coherence to clustering structure.  
- Notes a strong theoretical condition PUA(U0) = U0, which may limit unsupervised applicability but is addressed approximately in practice.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and reproducible with clear figures illustrating key phenomena.  
- Novelty: identifies coherence as a practical issue and connects coherence parameters to clustering structures.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical RPCA limitation and offers a solution with clear empirical benefits.  
- Strengths summarized: meaningful problem identification, solid theory, effective practical algorithm, strong experiments.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited theoretical development of links to subspace clustering and reliance on the strong PUA(U0) = U0 assumption.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is high-quality and recommended for acceptance.
