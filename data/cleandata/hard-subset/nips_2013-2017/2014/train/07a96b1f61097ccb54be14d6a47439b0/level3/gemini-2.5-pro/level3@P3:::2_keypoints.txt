- Paper tackles low-rank matrix recovery under gross corruption, showing RPCA fails on coherent/clustered data and proposing LRR with a low-rank dictionary as a fix.
- Main theoretical contribution: proves LRR with a suitable low-rank dictionary avoids dependence on the µ2 and µ3 coherence parameters that limit RPCA.
- Novelty: first formal link between RPCA degradation and clustering-induced coherence (the “µ2-phenomenon”) and a dictionary-based theoretical remedy.
- Practical algorithm: a heuristic two-stage method — use RPCA to get an initial low-rank estimate, build a dictionary from it, then run LRR.
- Strong empirical validation: synthetic phase transitions and corrupted motion-sequence experiments show substantially wider success regions and ~40% relative improvement in clustering error.
- Importance: addresses a significant problem in machine learning and computer vision by improving robust recovery for structured data.
- Major clarity/exposition issues: dense writing and poorly communicated main contributions make the paper hard to follow for non-experts.
- Coherence concept poorly explained: µ1/µ2/µ3 and why clustering increases µ2 need intuitive, geometric explanation.
- Disconnect between theory and practice: theoretical guarantees require A spanning the true subspace, while the practical algorithm is a heuristic with no formal link to those conditions.
- Accessibility concern: current presentation limits readership to specialists, reducing broader impact.
- Recommended rewrite: restructure introduction to clearly state the problem, cause, solution, and key theoretical/algorithmic results.
- Suggest adding intuitive examples (e.g., 2D/3D geometry) to illustrate coherence and why clustering increases µ2.
- Ask authors to explicitly state in the response how they will address clarity and exposition in the final paper.
- Request discussion of algorithm failure modes, e.g., when initial RPCA estimate is too poor to yield a useful dictionary.
- Overall judgment: technically sound and important work; recommend major revision for clarity, with reviewer leaning toward acceptance contingent on thorough rewrite.
