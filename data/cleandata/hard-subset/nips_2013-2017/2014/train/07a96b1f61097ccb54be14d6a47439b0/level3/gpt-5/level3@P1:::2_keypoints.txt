- Paper identifies that clustering inflates RPCA’s second coherence parameter µ2, degrading recovery (“µ2-phenomenon”).
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the ground truth (U0 ⊂ UA) to eliminate dependence on µ2.
- Theorems prove exact/near-exact recovery under column-normalized A, U0 ⊂ UA, rank bounds involving µ1(A), and sparsity up to a constant fraction.
- This generalizes prior LRR analyses (e.g., A = X) and clarifies when a learned dictionary can “shield” recovery from coherence.
- Practical heuristic: run RPCA to estimate L0, truncate by rank, normalize columns to form A, then run LRR.
- Experiments on synthetic data and corrupted motion sequences show larger success regions and ~40% relative error reduction for SSC after recovery.
- Technical contributions and proofs are sound; λ = 1/√n1 used and high-probability guarantees given.
- Theory still depends on µ1(A), so immunity to coherence is partial rather than absolute.
- The requirement U0 ⊂ UA is strong and hard to verify in practice.
- Heuristic reuses X to build A, raising concerns about sparse-corruption leakage into the dictionary.
- Complexity claims (LRR O(n^2 rA) vs RPCA O(n^3)) are plausible but lack wall-clock comparisons and solver details.
- Exposition is dense and targeted mainly at specialists; intuition for µ2 and geometric motivation should be earlier and clearer.
- Roles of µ1, µ2, µ3 and the µ2-phenomenon are scattered; notation and repetition hurt accessibility.
- Limited ablations: sensitivity to dictionary rank/conditioning, rank-threshold, iterative refinement, and partial UA–U0 overlap not explored.
- Comparisons could be broadened to stronger baselines (Outlier Pursuit, robust NMF/factorization, LRR with A = X).
- Suggested improvements: front-load geometric intuition, add ablations on dictionary construction and rank, test robustness to partial overlap, report runtimes and solver details, and compare broader baselines.
- Clarify scope of “immunity” by explicitly noting dependence on µ1(A) and practical ways to keep µ1(A) small.
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance contingent on substantial rewriting and additional experiments/ablations; otherwise borderline.
