- Proposes a novel method combining robust PCA with a dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to recover low-rank, coherent data from corrupted observations.  
- Key insight: representing structured data with a dictionary gives stronger recovery guarantees than robust PCA alone.  
- Provides theorems specifying conditions for successful reconstruction given a dictionary.  
- Addresses dictionary learning via a heuristic: compute robust PCA, then represent the result with a dictionary.  
- Empirical results support the heuristic and show the proposed algorithm outperforms robust PCA in experiments.  
- Proofs and theoretical analysis are technically sound.  
- Main contributions: formulation of coherent low-rank recovery, analysis of regimes where coherence arises, and a practical algorithm with empirical gains.  
- Strengths: originality, rigorous results, and clear empirical improvements.  
- Weaknesses: major clarity/exposition issues—contributions, claims, and impact are not well explained and are mainly accessible to specialists.  
- The concept of coherence is hard to understand and mainly explained indirectly in the appendix.  
- Language and exposition need editing for broader accessibility.  
- The heuristic dictionary-learning method is practical but may not be optimal.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and publishable but requires substantial rewriting; reviewer recommends major revision.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
