- RPCA's recovery guarantee degrades as the low-rank component's coherence (µ2) increases.  
- The paper shows high coherence naturally arises from intrinsic structures like union-of-subspaces or clustering.  
- As the number of clusters grows, µ2 increases and RPCA performance worsens.  
- Proposal: leverage Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a dictionary A to handle coherent data.  
- Theoretical result: if A is low-rank and Range(L0) ⊆ Range(A), LRR recovery guarantees do not depend on µ2 or µ3.  
- Practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to estimate L0, build a low-rank dictionary from that estimate, then apply LRR to refine recovery.  
- The approach synthesizes RPCA, LRR, and subspace clustering ideas in a novel way.  
- Technical quality: strong theorems (noiseless and noisy settings), sound proofs, and thorough synthetic experiments.  
- Empirical validation: clear performance gains over RPCA on synthetic data and improved motion segmentation on Hopkins155.  
- Clarity: generally well-written and motivated, but needs minor proofreading and tighter phrasing in a few places.  
- Originality: highly original—reframes RPCA's limitation as an exploitable structure and proposes a principled fix.  
- Significance: important contribution likely to influence robust low-rank recovery and subspace clustering research.  
- Suggested related work: discuss Wang & Xu on cases where higher coherence aids subspace clustering for complementary perspective.  
- Suggested experiments: compare to a baseline pipeline (e.g., SSC on corrupted data followed by PCA per cluster) to strengthen validation.  
- Practical impact: the motion segmentation preprocessing algorithm is a key applied contribution and should be emphasized.  
- Acceptance pros: novel conceptual link, strong theory, simple practical algorithm, and substantial empirical improvements.  
- Acceptance cons: minor language issues and experimental comparisons could be broadened.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.
