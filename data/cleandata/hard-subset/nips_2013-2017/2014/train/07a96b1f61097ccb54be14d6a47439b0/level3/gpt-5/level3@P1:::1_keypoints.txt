- Problem: Robust low-rank recovery when the low-rank component is coherent, a regime where standard RPCA degrades.  
- µ2-phenomenon: Column clustering increases the second coherence parameter µ2 (and µ3), explaining RPCA failure on coherent data.  
- Proposed method: Use LRR with a carefully constructed low-rank, column-normalized dictionary A that spans the true column space to avoid µ2 dependence.  
- Main theoretical result: If A is low-rank, column-normalized, and U0 ⊂ UA, LRR yields exact recovery w.h.p. under sparse corruption with rank bound O(n2/log n1), removing µ2 from guarantees.  
- Noisy guarantee: A stability/noisy variant mirrors RPCA-style robustness.  
- Practical algorithm: Unsupervised pipeline — run RPCA, rank-truncate its estimate to form A, then solve LRR to get the final recovery.  
- Synthetic results: Sweeps over rank and sparsity show ≈47% expansion of the success region relative to RPCA.  
- Real-data results: Motion segmentation experiments lower downstream clustering errors (e.g., SSC from 9.50% to 5.74%) and outperform LRR with A = X.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends RPCA/LRR literature by linking clustering-induced coherence to RPCA failure and analyzing LRR with general low-rank dictionaries.  
- Technical soundness: Conditions are clearly stated; proofs replace µ(L0) with µ1(A) and the rank-scaling improvement is plausible.  
- Algorithm-theory alignment: The RPCA-initialization aligns with theory by providing an approximate U0 for constructing A.  
- Key limitation: Strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA — if the initial RPCA estimate is poor (high corruption or extreme coherence), the method may fail.  
- Corruption model limitation: Analysis assumes entrywise sparse corruption; column-sparse/outlier and structured/missing-data models are not treated.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Choice λ = 1/√n1 and the rank threshold (10−3 σ1) are justified but sensitivity analyses and robustness checks are limited.  
- Clarity: Exposition is generally clear; µ1/µ2/µ3 definitions and the µ2-phenomenon are well explained.  
- Suggested experiments/analysis: Provide intuition why low-rank A removes µ2, ablations when A is not low-rank or U0 ⊄ UA, runtime/conditioning reports for A, and sensitivity to rank/λ/ε.  
- Originality: Novel conceptual link between clustering-induced coherence and RPCA failure and a new theoretical regime for LRR with low-rank dictionaries.  
- Significance: Broad implications for vision, recommendation, and text; principle of using a low-rank dictionary to cover underlying subspaces is impactful.  
- Questions for authors (condensed): Can the method integrate directly into clustering, how sensitive is dictionary learning to RPCA errors, how to choose/regularize rank and λ, and do results extend to column-sparse or missing-data models?  
- Pros: Clear identification of RPCA failure mode, solid theory avoiding µ2, simple practical algorithm, and meaningful empirical gains.  
- Cons: Reliance on a good RPCA initialization, strong U0 ⊂ UA and random sparsity assumptions, and limited experimental breadth.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
