- Problem: Recovering a low-rank matrix from observations with gross sparse errors; RPCA fails when the low-rank matrix has high coherence.  
- Insight: High coherence (the "µ2-phenomenon") naturally arises from clustered or multi-subspace structures in real data.  
- Main contribution: Propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A so L = AZ, to bypass coherence-related limits.  
- Theoretical result: Theorem 1 shows exact recovery by LRR when A is low-rank and its column space spans the true low-rank component, eliminating dependence on µ2 and µ3.  
- Practical algorithm: A two-stage "Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit": run RPCA for an initial estimate, build a low-rank dictionary, then perform LRR refinement.  
- Empirical evidence: Demonstrates substantial gains — 47% larger success region on synthetic data and ~40% relative improvement in clustering error on motion segmentation.  
- Strength (significance/originality): Novel and relevant approach that reframes the incoherence bottleneck and leverages dictionary-based methods.  
- Strength (technical quality): Solid theoretical analysis (Theorems 1 & 2), simple/practical algorithm, and convincing experiments.  
- Strength (clarity): Well-written, well-motivated, and clearly presented with effective figures and tables.  
- Weakness/question: Would be stronger if the dictionary learning/representation could be integrated into a unified model for joint subspace learning and segmentation rather than only used as pre-processing.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, original, and significant work with strong theory and empirical support; strong recommendation for acceptance.
