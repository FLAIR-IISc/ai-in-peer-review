- Problem: RPCA fails on data with clustering structures (high coherence), motivating a different approach.  
- Proposal: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary to improve recovery of coherent data.  
- Theoretical contribution: New theorems providing recovery guarantees when the dictionary is low-rank and certain conditions hold.  
- Algorithm: A heuristic method that uses an initial RPCA step to construct the dictionary for LRR.  
- Empirical validation: Synthetic and real-world motion sequence experiments show improvements over standard RPCA.  
- Mathematical soundness: The analysis and proofs appear rigorous and technically solid.  
- Originality: Novel combination of RPCA, dictionary learning, and theoretical analysis of coherence.  
- Significance: Addresses an important limitation of RPCA with potential cross-domain applications.  
- Clarity issues: Core contributions and impact are not clearly articulated, making the paper hard to follow.  
- Coherence explanation: The central concept of coherence is explained poorly and mostly deferred to the appendix.  
- Accessibility: Exposition is dense and specialist-oriented; substantial editing is needed for broader readership.  
- Heuristic limitation: The RPCA-based initialization is heuristic and its limitations are under-discussed.  
- Recommendation: Paper is valuable but should be published only after major revision to improve clarity and intuitive explanation of coherence.
