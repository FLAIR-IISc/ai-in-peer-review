- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve PCA robustness against gross errors in coherent data.  
- Well-motivated explanation of RPCA limitations and how data coherence (μ) can reflect clustering structure.  
- Theoretical analysis shows LRR can partially remove dependence on the incoherence parameter μ, yielding stronger exact/noisy recovery guarantees.  
- Novel contribution connecting union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering ideas to robust low-rank recovery via a dictionary.  
- Section 4.2 proposes a new subspace-clustering method for sparse corruption and shows significant improvements on Hopkins155.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical claims and demonstrate practical applicability (including potential text-domain use).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and provides a thorough analysis of the problem.  
- Originality and significance are high; the work has potential impact across vision, ML, and signal processing.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, rating 9/10 with confidence 8/10, but requests minor revisions.  
- Primary weaknesses: some clarity and proofreading issues (ambiguous phrasing around lines 80–82 and an incomplete phrase at line 88).  
- Experimental suggestion: include empirical comparison to a pipeline of noisy sparse subspace clustering (ℓ1) followed by per-subspace PCA.  
- Paper could benefit from more explicit claims/references and a more detailed comparison with existing methods.
