The paper "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit" presents a novel approach to improve the robustness of Principal Component Analysis (PCA) in the presence of gross errors. The authors propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to alleviate the challenges raised by coherent data, which are ubiquitous in modern applications.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of Robust PCA (RPCA) in handling coherent data. They show that the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure in the data rather than being an unavoidable bottleneck. The theoretical results are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the conditions under which LRR can partially remove dependence on μ and yield stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank + sparse recovery.

The paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing, and the reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS. The authors' approach is novel, significant, and related to prior union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering work. The paper's clustering assumption is less explicit but potentially more general than previous subspace-clustering assumptions.

The simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical claims, and the authors note the applicability of their approach to other domains, such as text. However, there are several clarity and proofreading issues that need to be addressed. For example, the phrasing around lines 80-82 is ambiguous, and there is an incomplete phrase at line 88. The authors should improve language precision and be explicit about claims and references.

The reviewer also suggests an empirical comparison to a pipeline of noisy sparse subspace clustering (ℓ1) followed by per-subspace PCA. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Section 4.2 is particularly effective in proposing a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption, showing significant improvements on Hopkins155. The authors' approach can be summarized into three key contributions: (1) identifying clustering-induced high coherence, (2) exploiting it via a dictionary in LRR, and (3) partially solving sparse corruption in subspace clustering.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach. However, there are some minor issues with clarity and proofreading that need to be addressed.

The originality of the paper is high, and the authors provide a novel approach to improve the robustness of PCA in the presence of gross errors. The paper is related to prior work on union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering, but the authors provide a new perspective on the problem.

The significance of the paper is also high, and the authors provide a substantial contribution to the field of compressed sensing. The paper has the potential to impact a wide range of applications, including computer vision, machine learning, and signal processing.

In conclusion, the paper is a valuable contribution to the field of compressed sensing, and the reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS. The authors should address the minor issues with clarity and proofreading, and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.
