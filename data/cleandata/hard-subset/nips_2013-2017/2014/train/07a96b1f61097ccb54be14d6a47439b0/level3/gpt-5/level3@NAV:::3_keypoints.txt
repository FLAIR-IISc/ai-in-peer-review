- Studies RPCA failure on "coherent" low-rank data and proposes Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit: LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary A.  
- Key insight: the coherence parameter μ2 reflects row-space clustering and can be avoided by choosing A low-rank and spanning the true column space, removing μ2 from recovery guarantees.  
- Practical method: unsupervised pipeline that runs RPCA, builds A from the estimated low-rank component, then solves LRR.  
- Theorems 1–2 are technically sound, clearly state conditions on rank(A), μ1(A), sparsity, and noise; removal of μ2 under U0 ⊂ UA is significant.  
- Experiments show strong empirical support: a phase transition with 47% larger success region and substantial gains on Hopkins155 with added sparse corruption.  
- Proposes a robust subspace clustering approach (Section 4.2) that meaningfully improves performance under sparse corruption.  
- Related to RPCA, LRR, SSC, union-of-subspaces work, and connects to Wang & Xu’s observations about coherence aiding clustering.  
- Clarity issues: proofreading errors and ambiguous phrasing (lines 80–82, 88); need to explicitly state remaining dependence on μ1(A) and clarify sensitivity to λ, ε, and A normalization.  
- Originality: novel and nontrivial use of a learned low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence, distinct from A = X LRR and standard RPCA.  
- Significance: relevant and likely influential for compressed sensing/robust PCA and subspace clustering practice.  
- Suggestions: add empirical comparison to noisy SSC + per-subspace PCA, discuss Wang & Xu more explicitly, include ablations on dictionary quality and U0 ⊂ UA violations, and test more real datasets.  
- Pros: strong theory and practice, novel coherence insight, practical algorithm with clear gains.  
- Cons: clarity/proofreading problems, reliance on RPCA to seed A, and limited baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
