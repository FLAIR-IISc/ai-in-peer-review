- Proposes LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary to make RPCA robust to coherent, clustered data.
- Key insight: the incoherence parameter reflects underlying clustering structure rather than an unavoidable limitation.
- Theoretical guarantee: a low-rank dictionary can reduce dependence on coherence, yielding stronger recovery guarantees.
- Practical algorithm: unsupervised dictionary learning built on an initial RPCA estimate; simple and implementable.
- Strong experimental validation: synthetic and real motion-sequence (Hopkins155) results show substantial improvements over RPCA.
- Situates work well within RPCA, subspace clustering, and low-rank factorization literature; links to Wang & Xu’s coherence observations.
- Writing issues: occasional ambiguity and minor phrasing errors (noted around lines 80–82 and 88) need correction.
- Missing empirical baseline: no comparison to a noisy ℓ1-SSC followed by per-subspace PCA pipeline.
- Limited discussion of alternatives: more analysis needed on other dictionary learning strategies beyond the RPCA-based approach.
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial contribution, rigorous theory, compelling experiments, and strong motivation.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor clarity problems and incomplete empirical comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance, with suggested minor fixes and additional comparisons.
- Notable highlight: Section 4.2 proposes a new subspace clustering method that effectively handles sparse corruption.
- Three main claimed contributions: identifying clustering-induced high coherence, exploiting it via an LRR dictionary, and partially solving sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
