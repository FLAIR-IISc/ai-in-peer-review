- Paper studies RPCA limitations on coherent (clustered) data and proposes a dictionary-based LRR alternative.  
- Shows RPCA performance degrades as data coherence increases with more clusters.  
- Demonstrates LRR with a properly configured low-rank dictionary can avoid coherence dependency.  
- Provides theoretical recovery guarantees under specific conditions improving on RPCA constraints.  
- Presents a heuristic algorithm that constructs the dictionary from RPCA output.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and motion sequence data shows clear superiority of the proposed method.  
- Reports a notable 40% relative reduction in motion segmentation clustering error versus RPCA.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous and technically sound with well-justified theorems.  
- Offers important conceptual insight linking coherence parameters to underlying data structure.  
- Originality is moderate—builds on RPCA/LRR but adds novel theoretical and empirical results.  
- Main weaknesses are major clarity and exposition issues that hinder accessibility.  
- Core contribution and key concepts (e.g., coherence) are poorly articulated and buried in technical details/appendix.  
- Heuristic nature of the dictionary-learning step limits theoretical completeness.  
- Recommendation: major revision to substantially improve exposition and clarify main claims before acceptance.
