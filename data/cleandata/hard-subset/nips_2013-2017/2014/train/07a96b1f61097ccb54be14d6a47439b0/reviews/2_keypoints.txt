- Combines robust PCA and dictionary-based structure-preserving projection to restore corrupted observation matrices.  
- Key insight: using a dictionary to represent structured (e.g., clustered) data yields stronger recovery guarantees than robust PCA alone.  
- Provides theorems that state conditions under which reconstruction is possible given a dictionary.  
- Addresses dictionary learning with a heuristic algorithm: compute robust PCA then represent the result with a dictionary.  
- Empirical results justify the heuristic; proofs and theory are technically sound on reasonable checking.  
- Major clarity issues: main contributions, claims, and impact are not well explained and are accessible mainly to specialists.  
- The concept of coherence is hard to understand and its interpretation is indirect and buried in the appendix.  
- Language and exposition require editing; the authors should comment on how they will address these issues.  
- Originality: builds on recent techniques but contributes new theorems and empirical validation.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art with rigorous results and clear empirical improvements despite a heuristic dictionary method.  
- Post-rebuttal: paper is publishable and interesting, but needs substantial rewriting for broader accessibility (reviewer would have voted for major revision).
