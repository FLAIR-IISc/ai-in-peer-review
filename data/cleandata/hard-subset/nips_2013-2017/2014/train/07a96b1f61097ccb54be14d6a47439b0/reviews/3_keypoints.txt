- Proposes using Low Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary A to improve Robust PCA (RPCA).  
- Shows the incoherence parameter μ can reflect clustering structure in the data rather than an unavoidable bottleneck.  
- Proves that under conditions on dictionary A, LRR can partially remove dependence on μ and yield stronger guarantees for exact and noisy low-rank + sparse recovery.  
- Method is novel, significant, and related to prior union-of-subspace/subspace-clustering work.  
- Paper’s clustering assumption is less explicit but potentially more general than previous subspace-clustering assumptions.  
- Simulations and real-data experiments validate the theoretical claims; authors note applicability to other domains (e.g., text).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS based on substantial contribution to compressed sensing.  
- Several clarity/proofreading issues: ambiguous phrasing around lines 80–82 and an incomplete phrase at line 88.  
- General suggestion to improve language precision and be explicit about claims and references.  
- Notes connection to Wang & Xu: higher coherence can aid subspace clustering; current paper combines low-rank and union-of-subspace structure.  
- Suggests empirical comparison to a pipeline of noisy sparse subspace clustering (ℓ1) followed by per-subspace PCA.  
- Highlights Section 4.2 as effectively proposing a new method for subspace clustering with sparse corruption, showing significant improvements on Hopkins155.  
- Summarizes three key contributions: (1) identifying clustering-induced high coherence, (2) exploiting it via a dictionary in LRR, (3) partially solving sparse corruption in subspace clustering.
