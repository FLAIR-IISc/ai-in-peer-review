- Addresses robustly estimating a low-dimensional subspace from contaminated observations that are inherently coherent.
- Identifies that previous RPCA methods degrade as data coherence increases, which is a standard theoretical bottleneck.
- Proposes a clever method that circumvents the coherence-related bottleneck.
- Notes cluster structure is common in real data, so solving this issue is practically meaningful.
- Method is theoretically sound and empirically performs well.
- Reviewer asks whether the method can be applied to subspace clustering/segmentation and requests authors’ comments.
- Paper tackles an important RPCA problem, is of high quality, and should be accepted.
