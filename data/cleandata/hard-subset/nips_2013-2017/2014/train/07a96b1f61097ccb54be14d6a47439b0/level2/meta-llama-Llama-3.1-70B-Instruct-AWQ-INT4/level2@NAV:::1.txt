This paper proposes a novel approach to recover coherent data via low-rank dictionary pursuit. The authors argue that the existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) method is not sufficient to handle coherent data, which is common in many applications. They introduce a new method called Low-Rank Representation (LRR) that can effectively recover coherent data by learning a suitable dictionary.

The main claims of the paper are: (1) LRR can avoid the coherence parameters that increase with the underlying cluster number, (2) the dictionary matrix A should be low-rank and contain information about the ground truth matrix, and (3) the proposed algorithm outperforms RPCA in extensive experiments.

The support for these claims is provided through theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors prove that LRR can avoid the coherence parameters under certain conditions, and they demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm on both synthetic and real data.
