- Addresses RPCA's limitation with coherent (clustered) data and the resulting performance degradation as coherence increases.
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly designed low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's coherence sensitivity.
- Main theoretical result: LRR is immune to coherence parameters when the dictionary itself is low-rank, giving a clear dictionary-design criterion.
- Introduces a practical unsupervised algorithm that constructs the dictionary using RPCA.
- Empirical results show the proposed method outperforms RPCA on synthetic data and motion sequences.
- Clarifies the physical meaning of coherence parameters by linking them to clustering structures in data.
- Extends prior LRR work beyond the special case A = X by providing a principled low-rank dictionary approach.
- Algorithm is straightforward to implement and yields consistent improvements over RPCA.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and balances theory with experiments.
- Detailed proofs are provided for the main theorems.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using random matrices and the Hopkins155 motion database.
- Demonstrated practical impact: 40% reduction in clustering error on motion sequences.
- Original contribution: identifies a previously unaddressed RPCA limitation and provides a theoretically grounded solution.
- Critique: paper could better explain mechanistically why RPCA fails on coherent data beyond citing coherence bounds.
- Minor weaknesses: theoretical bounds are conservative and may limit applicability; lacks comparison to other coherent-data methods.
- Limitation not discussed: how the dictionary-learning algorithm performs when RPCA recovery is already poor; authors note method helps most when RPCA is reasonably successful.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, claims supported, modest computational overhead (~1.2x RPCA), and recommended for acceptance.
