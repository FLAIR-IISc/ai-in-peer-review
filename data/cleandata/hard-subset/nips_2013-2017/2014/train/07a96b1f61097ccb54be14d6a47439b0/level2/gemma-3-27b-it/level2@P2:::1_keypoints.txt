- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery with corruption and shows RPCA degrades with increasing data coherence and clustering.  
- Proposes using LRR with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence issues, with theoretical justification.  
- Presents an unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm that uses RPCA for initial estimates and then applies LRR.  
- Paper is well-motivated; the RPCA limitations and the clustering–coherence connection are clearly articulated.  
- Theoretical analysis (conditions for avoiding coherence parameters in LRR with a low-rank dictionary) is a significant contribution.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real motion data show the proposed method outperforms RPCA.  
- Heavy reliance on existing work without clearly explaining specific contributions of cited methods and lacking broader contextualization.  
- Relationship to matrix factorization literature ([20, 23]) is treated superficially.  
- Theoretical section is dense and hard to follow; key assumptions (e.g., PUA(U0) = U0) need clearer explanation and practical discussion.  
- Noisy-case analysis (Theorem 2) needs stronger justification for parameter choices and clearer linkage to noise level ε.  
- Ablation study is limited; sensitivity to dictionary-learning strategies and parameter λ (set as 1/√n1) is not thoroughly explored.  
- Missing comparisons to other dictionary-learning and robust matrix-completion methods (e.g., iterative reweighting, alternative sparsity models).  
- Writing is sometimes redundant and the introduction could be more concise.  
- Overall recommendation: worthwhile contribution but requires revisions; reviewer recommends weak accept contingent on addressing literature context, theoretical clarity, and more comprehensive experiments.
