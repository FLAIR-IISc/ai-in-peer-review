- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned dictionary to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, clustered/coherent data.
- Argues RPCA fails on coherent data due to its incoherence assumption.
- Main claims: LRR can handle coherent data by learning a suitable dictionary; the proposed algorithm outperforms RPCA on synthetic and real motion data.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for exact and near recovery of the low-rank matrix under LRR.
- Experiments are extensive and demonstrate superiority over RPCA in multiple settings.
- Paper is well-written with clear introduction, organized technical sections, and proofs in the appendix.
- Strength: Identifies RPCA limitation and proposes a novel solution addressing coherent data.
- Strength: Offers rigorous, well-established theoretical guarantees for the LRR framework.
- Strength: Thorough experimental validation across datasets.
- Weakness: Requires estimating the rank of the low-rank matrix, which is challenging in practice.
- Weakness: Choice of dictionary is critical but lacks practical selection guidelines.
- Weakness: Computational complexity is not thoroughly analyzed, raising scalability concerns.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel approach to a significant limitation, rigorous theory, and strong empirical results.
- Rejection arguments: Practical issues (rank estimation, dictionary choice) and missing complexity analysis could limit applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — provide guidance on dictionary selection and a more thorough computational complexity analysis.
