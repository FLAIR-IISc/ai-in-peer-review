Summary
The paper studies robust recovery of a low-rank matrix L0 from grossly corrupted observations X = L0 + S0 in regimes where L0 is coherent due to clustering. It argues and empirically demonstrates a µ2-phenomenon: when columns of L0 lie in multiple subspaces within a fixed low-dimensional ambient subspace, the second coherence parameter µ2 increases with the number of clusters, degrading RPCA/PCP guarantees and performance. The paper proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a suitably chosen dictionary A: minimize ||Z||* + λ||S||1 subject to X = AZ + S. The central theoretical claim is that if A is itself low rank and its column space contains that of L0 (U0 ⊂ UA), then exact (noiseless) and near-exact (noisy) recovery are possible with bounds that do not depend on µ2 (or µ3). The bounds depend on µ1(A), the rank of A, and the corruption level |Ω|. Building on this principle, the paper suggests an unsupervised two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to estimate L0, normalize its columns to form a low-rank dictionary A, and then run LRR with this A. Experiments on synthetic clustered data and corrupted motion sequences show improved recovery and downstream clustering accuracy over RPCA and LRR with A = X.

Relation to prior work
- RPCA/PCP (Candès et al., JACM 2011) provides exact recovery under incoherence, with bounds depending on a maximum coherence parameter. Outlier Pursuit (Xu et al., NIPS/IT) and related robust PCA methods address different corruption models but retain incoherence-type assumptions.
- LRR (Liu et al., ICML 2010; PAMI 2013) studied subspace segmentation and robust recovery mainly with A = X. This paper analyzes LRR with general dictionaries and identifies conditions under which a low-rank A can neutralize the adverse effect of µ2 stemming from clustered data.
- The work complements matrix factorization approaches by formalizing when replacing L by AZ retains identifiability under sparse corruption.

Quality
- Theory: The noiseless and noisy theorems precisely state conditions under which recovery is exact/near-exact without µ2 dependence. The requirement U0 ⊂ UA is natural for exact recovery and sharpens the role of dictionary design. The rank bounds O(n2/log n1) are comparable or slightly looser than PCP bounds without the µ2 factor, which directly addresses the identified failure mode.
- Assumptions: The probabilistic statements suggest randomness in the corruption model; the submission would benefit from explicitly stating the support/sign distributions (e.g., Bernoulli support and random signs) and the source of probability in the theorems.
- Algorithm: The two-stage RPCA→dictionary→LRR procedure is simple and practical. However, the theoretical guarantees do not cover the unsupervised construction of A, since U0 ⊂ UA is only approximately satisfied by the RPCA estimate when coherence is high. The paper notes tolerance to dictionary errors; quantifying this robustness would strengthen the contribution.
- Experiments: Synthetic studies illustrate the µ2-phenomenon and improved success regions; motion segmentation experiments show tangible downstream gains (e.g., SSC error reduced from 9.50% to 5.74% after LRR with learned A). Additional comparisons (e.g., GoDec or reweighted PCP circa 2011–2013) and timing/conditioning diagnostics would further corroborate claims about efficiency and practical robustness.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized, with clear notation and motivation. The coherence parameters and their behaviors under clustering are explained convincingly. Suggestions:
- State the corruption model and probability space explicitly in the main text (support distribution, independence, sign model).
- Clarify the role of column normalization in the dictionary and how near-zero columns are handled.
- Provide sensitivity analyses for λ and ε, and a brief discussion on how to set these in practice.
- Include more details or a lemma quantifying performance when U0 ⊂ UA approximately (e.g., principal angle bounds).

Originality
- Identifies and formalizes the µ2-phenomenon in clustered low-rank data and its adverse effect on PCP.
- Provides new recovery guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries that remove µ2 dependence under natural subspace inclusion, which, to the best of this reviewer’s knowledge, had not been established for general A.
- The unsupervised dictionary-learning approach is simple; the conceptual novelty lies more in the theoretical insight and prescription for A than in the algorithmic pipeline.

Significance
The work advances understanding of when and why PCP degrades on coherent data and offers a principled remedy via LRR with learned low-rank dictionaries. The results are likely to influence robust subspace recovery and downstream tasks like motion segmentation and clustering under structured data distributions. The theoretical perspective on coherence and dictionary design is of broad interest.

Pros
- Clear identification of a realistic failure mode for RPCA (µ2-phenomenon).
- Strong and relevant recovery guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries, avoiding µ2.
- Practical, easy-to-implement algorithm with empirical gains on synthetic and real data.
- Good positioning relative to RPCA/LRR literature.

Cons
- Recovery guarantees rely on U0 ⊂ UA; no theoretical bridge from the proposed unsupervised learning procedure to this condition.
- Corruption model assumptions are not fully explicit in the main text.
- Experimental scope could be broader, including additional baselines and runtime/conditioning analysis.
- Limited quantitative analysis of robustness to dictionary mismatch.

Suggestions for improvement
- Make the corruption model explicit and state where randomness is used in the proofs.
- Provide perturbation results showing how recovery degrades with principal angles between U0 and UA and with rank(A) > rank(L0).
- Add ablations: effect of the number of clusters on µ2 and recovery; sensitivity to λ and ε; comparison against LRR with simple PCA-derived dictionaries or alternative dictionary constructions.
- Report computational timings and condition numbers of A to support efficiency claims.
- Expand real-data evaluation beyond motion segmentation if space permits, or include more detailed Hopkins results and failure case analyses.

Overall evaluation
A well-motivated and technically solid paper that both deepens theoretical understanding and offers a practical method for coherent data recovery. The main caveat is the gap between the theoretical condition on A and the unsupervised procedure used to obtain it, which could be narrowed with additional analysis and experiments. The contribution is original and significant enough for NeurIPS, contingent on clarifying assumptions and adding modest robustness/ablation evidence.
