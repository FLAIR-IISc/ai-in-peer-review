- Addresses RPCA’s incoherence limitation: clustering/union-of-subspaces inflates µ2 and harms RPCA recovery; proposes LRR with a suitable dictionary A to avoid µ2 dependence.
- Theoretical contribution: provides noiseless/noisy theorems showing LRR with low-rank A (and U0 ⊂ UA) yields exact/near recovery, with conditions depending on µ1(A) only.
- Practical method: two-stage unsupervised pipeline—RPCA to estimate L0, then column-normalized, low-rank dictionary from that estimate for LRR—and consistent λ = 1/√n1.
- Empirics: synthetic and corrupted motion-sequence experiments show tangible gains and improved downstream subspace clustering (SIM, SSC, LRRx).
- Relation to prior work: builds on robust low-rank modeling and subspace segmentation literature (e.g., RPCA, LRR) and is relevant to recent NeurIPS work on low-rank learning.
- Strength: clearly identifies and empirically illustrates the “µ2-phenomenon” explaining when RPCA fails.
- Strength: crisp theoretical insight that LRR can remove µ2 from recovery conditions under a low-rank, inclusive dictionary.
- Strength: simple, unsupervised algorithm with practical parameterization and real-data improvements.
- Weakness: key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is an oracle condition; no theoretical guarantee that the RPCA-derived A satisfies this.
- Suggestion: provide analysis showing conditions under which RPCA-derived A contains U0 with high probability, or quantify degradation when only approximately true (e.g., principal angle bounds).
- Weakness: heuristic choices (rank-threshold 10−3σ1, column normalization) may affect performance.
- Suggestion: report sensitivity studies and consider adaptive rank selection or cross-validation.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks ablations isolating dictionary low-rankness and comparisons to more baselines.
- Suggestion: include ablations (random vs RPCA-derived vs ground-truth A), measure µ1(A), µ2(L0), recovery error vs number of clusters, compare to Outlier Pursuit and factorization methods, and report runtime/scalability.
- Weakness: limited scope discussion on failure modes when rows are clustered (large µ1); experiments or extensions for that regime are needed.
- Pros for acceptance: timely problem, clear theoretical advance, and a practical method with measurable gains.
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on a strong subspace-inclusion condition without guarantees for the learned dictionary and limited empirical breadth/sensitivity analysis.
- Overall: constructive advance that would be much stronger with guarantees for the unsupervised dictionary and broader, more detailed experiments.
