- Addresses RPCA's failure on "coherent data" from clustering by using LRR with a low-rank dictionary and gives theoretical guarantees plus an unsupervised dictionary-construction algorithm.
- Main contributions: (1) links clustering structure to coherence parameters that hurt RPCA, (2) proves LRR with a proper low-rank dictionary can avoid those coherence parameters, (3) proposes an unsupervised algorithm that builds the dictionary from RPCA output.
- Theorems 1 and 2 are generally well-justified but require restrictive conditions for exact recovery.
- The key condition U0 ⊆ UA (true low-rank column space contained in dictionary column space) is strong and may be hard to meet in practice.
- Algorithm 1 uses RPCA to construct the dictionary but does not clearly guarantee the required subspace inclusion; more analysis of how well it satisfies this in practice is needed.
- Experiments show the method outperforms RPCA on synthetic data and motion sequences, supporting the main claims.
- Paper lacks comparisons to other relevant baselines, notably LRR with A = X (LRRx) or other dictionary constructions.
- The claim that the "success area" is 47% wider than RPCA is vague and lacks a clear definition or calculation of "success area."
- Provides valuable insight that coherence parameters reflect intrinsic data structure (clustering), not mere proof assumptions.
- Well-written and organized with clear notation and solid experimental design.
- Connection to prior work is adequate but could better distinguish differences from existing LRR work (e.g., [13]) on general dictionaries.
- Practical significance is moderate: coherent-data problem is important but prevalence in real applications isn’t convincingly demonstrated; more real-world examples would help.
- Strengths summarized: clear problem identification, theoretical insights on coherence, practical unsupervised dictionary algorithm, and supportive experiments.
- Weaknesses summarized: restrictive theoretical conditions, insufficient comparisons to alternatives, vague performance metric, and unclear practical satisfaction of the subspace condition.
- Recommendation implicit: address theoretical practicality, add broader baseline comparisons, clarify the "success area" metric, and analyze Algorithm 1’s effectiveness in meeting U0 ⊆ UA.
