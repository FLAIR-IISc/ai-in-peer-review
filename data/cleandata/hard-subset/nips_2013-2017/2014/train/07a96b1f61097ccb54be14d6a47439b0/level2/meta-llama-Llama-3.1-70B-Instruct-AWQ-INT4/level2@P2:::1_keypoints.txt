- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, clustered (coherent) data.  
- Argues that RPCA is ineffective for clustered/coherent data because it prefers incoherent structures.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves LRR can avoid coherence parameters that grow with cluster number if the dictionary is low-rank and contains information about the ground truth.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear problem statement, approach, and theoretical results.  
- Provides extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world data showing effectiveness over RPCA.  
- Gives insight into the physical meaning of coherence, linking coherence parameters to intrinsic extra structures beyond low-rankness.  
- Practical algorithm: constructs the LRR dictionary from RPCA output; algorithm is simple and effective.  
- Computational complexity analysis shows the method can be much faster than RPCA when the dictionary is fairly low-rank.  
- Strong experimental results, notably improved recovery on Hopkins155 and better subspace clustering performance.  
- Minor issues: lacks detailed discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Minor issues: some notations and symbols need clearer definitions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; technical soundness and empirical/theoretical support are strong.  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
