**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper addresses the problem of recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, particularly when the data exhibits clustering structures. The authors argue that the Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) method, which is widely used for this task, may not perform well when the data is coherent (i.e., has high coherence). They propose an alternative approach based on Low-Rank Representation (LRR) and show that by constructing a low-rank dictionary, LRR can effectively handle coherent data.

The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the performance of LRR and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on randomly generated matrices and real motion sequences. They also propose a practical algorithm for learning the dictionary matrix in LRR, which outperforms RPCA in their experiments.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

1. **Theoretical contributions**: The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of RPCA and introduces a new approach based on LRR, which is supported by theoretical guarantees.
2. **Practical algorithm**: The authors propose a practical algorithm for learning the dictionary matrix in LRR, which is shown to outperform RPCA in experiments.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation of the proposed approach on both synthetic and real data.

Weaknesses:

1. **Limited scope**: The paper focuses on a specific problem (recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations) and may not be directly applicable to other problems.
2. **Assumptions**: The theoretical guarantees provided in the paper rely on certain assumptions about the data, which may not always hold in practice.
3. **Computational complexity**: The proposed algorithm may have higher computational complexity than RPCA, which could be a limitation in certain applications.
