- Addresses RPCA's failure on coherent (clustered) data and identifies clustering-induced coherence as the cause.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA degradation.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves LRR with a low-rank dictionary is immune to the coherence parameter that grows with cluster number.  
- Provides a practical, unsupervised algorithm for constructing the low-rank dictionary.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound with clear proofs showing conditions where LRR outperforms RPCA.  
- Connects clustering structure to coherence parameters and supports this link with theory and experiments.  
- Experiments are comprehensive (synthetic and Hopkins155 motion data) and show significant improvements over RPCA.  
- Empirical gains include up to 47% wider success area on synthetic data and ~40% relative improvement in clustering error on motion sequences.  
- The proposed algorithm is computationally efficient (~1.2× cost of RPCA in practice).  
- Paper is original in identifying RPCA’s coherence-related limitation and providing new theoretical insight on dictionary properties.  
- Presentation quality is high: clear organization, consistent notation, and effective figures.  
- Improvements suggested: clarify intuition for why low-rank dictionaries work and better link theory to practical implementation.  
- Limitations noted: doesn’t establish necessity of low-rank dictionaries and lacks comparisons to other robust low-rank recovery methods.  
- Claim that coherence parameters reflect natural data structure needs stronger support via concrete examples.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, significant contribution with theoretical and practical impact, suitable for NIPS.
