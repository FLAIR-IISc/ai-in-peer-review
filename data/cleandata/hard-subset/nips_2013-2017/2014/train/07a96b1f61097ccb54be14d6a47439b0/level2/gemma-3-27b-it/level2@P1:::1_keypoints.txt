- Problem/motivation: Addresses low-rank matrix recovery with corruptions, focusing on coherence arising from clustering and its challenge for RPCA.  
- Proposed method: Uses Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary and an unsupervised algorithm to learn that dictionary from RPCA output.  
- Theoretical claim: Shows theoretically that a low-rank dictionary in LRR can mitigate coherence issues.  
- Experiments: Validated on synthetic and real data (including Hopkins motion dataset), showing improvements over RPCA.  
- Related work: Thorough citation and positioning relative to RPCA, LRR, and matrix factorization literature.  

- Strength — clear problem statement: Motivation and link to clustering/coherence are well articulated.  
- Strength — theoretical justification: Provides a valuable theoretical result about low-rank dictionaries reducing coherence.  
- Strength — practical algorithm: Offers a sensible, implementable algorithm building on RPCA output.  
- Strength — empirical validation: Experiments support claims and demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Strength — presentation: Paper is well-written and organized.  

- Weakness — limited novelty: Using LRR is not novel; main novelty is the reasoning for a low-rank dictionary, which is modest.  
- Weakness — RPCA dependence: Algorithm relies on RPCA initialization, which may fail under high coherence and hurt subsequent steps.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Comparisons are limited (mainly RPCA and standard LRR) and parameter tuning is not exhaustive.  
- Weakness — strong theorem assumptions: Theorem 1 requires strong conditions (e.g., U0 ⊆ UA) that may not hold in practice.  

- For acceptance: Tackles an important problem, offers theoretical insight on coherence and dictionary learning, shows practical gains, and is well-presented.  
- Against acceptance: Incremental novelty, dependence on RPCA initialization, and restrictive theoretical assumptions may limit impact.  

- Overall: Solid contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  

- Suggested improvements: Tone down novelty claims, discuss RPCA dependence in detail, expand experiments and comparisons, analyze sensitivity to Theorem 1 assumptions, and ensure consistent notation.
