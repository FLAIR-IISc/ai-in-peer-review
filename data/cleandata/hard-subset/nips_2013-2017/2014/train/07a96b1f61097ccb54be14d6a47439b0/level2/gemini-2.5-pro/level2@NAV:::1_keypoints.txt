- Addresses RPCA's limitation on "coherent" data where clustering increases a coherence parameter that breaks RPCA recovery guarantees.  
- Shows coherence (µ2) grows with number of clusters, explaining RPCA failure on union-of-subspaces data.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary whose column space contains the true data.  
- Theoretical result: LRR with such a dictionary is immune to the problematic coherence parameter.  
- Builds on and connects foundational RPCA and LRR literature, clarifying the physical meaning of coherence.  
- Main strength: clear identification and rigorous treatment of the µ2-phenomenon as a practical RPCA failure mode.  
- Strong theoretical justification for the proposed approach.  
- Empirical strength: substantial performance improvements over RPCA on synthetic and real motion-sequence experiments.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: the unsupervised two-step algorithm is heuristic and depends on RPCA producing a sufficiently good initial estimate.  
- Lacks discussion of failure modes when initial RPCA estimate is poor and conditions (corruption/coherence) causing such failure.  
- Rank estimation relies on a simple threshold; sensitivity to this hyperparameter is not analyzed.  
- Overall conclusion: contributions and empirical/theoretical strengths outweigh weaknesses; advances understanding of robust low-rank recovery.
