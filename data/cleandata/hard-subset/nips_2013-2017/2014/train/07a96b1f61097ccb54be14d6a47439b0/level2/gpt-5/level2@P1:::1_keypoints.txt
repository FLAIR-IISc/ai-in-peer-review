- Problem: Robust recovery of low-rank L0 from X = L0 + S0 where RPCA fails under clustered (union-of-subspaces) data due to high row-space coherence µ2.  
- Empirical observation: Increasing number of clusters raises µ2 and degrades RPCA recoverability.  
- Main theoretical contribution: LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains L0’s (U0 ⊂ UA) yields exact recovery/stability bounds that depend on µ1(A) but not on µ2(L0).  
- Algorithmic contribution: Two-stage practical method—(1) run RPCA, truncate to rank r̂0 and normalize columns to form Â; (2) run LRR with Â.  
- Experiments: Synthetic coherent data and corrupted motion trajectories show improved recovery and downstream clustering versus RPCA and baseline clustering.  
- Strength: Clear identification and illustration of the “µ2-phenomenon” linking clustering to RPCA failure.  
- Strength: New recovery guarantees for LRR with general low-rank dictionaries clarifying when LRR handles coherent data.  
- Strength: Practical, simple algorithm using standard convex programs with sensible design choices (rank truncation, column normalization).  
- Strength: Extensive synthetic visualizations of recovery phases and meaningful real-data motion segmentation gains.  
- Strength: Conceptual link between coherence and data geometry (union-of-subspaces).  
- Weakness: The key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong in unsupervised settings and lacks formal robustness analysis when only approximately true.  
- Weakness: No formal guarantee for Algorithm 1 beyond the trivial case when RPCA is exact; need bounds for approximate containment (subspace distance) and effects on theorem constants.  
- Weakness: Probabilistic assumptions are unspecified—“with probability” statements lack clear randomness model for Ω, signs, and independence from U0.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity of heuristic choices (rank threshold 1e-3 σ1, column normalization) is not studied; impact when singular spectra decay slowly is unclear.  
- Weakness: Normalizing near-zero columns of L̃0 can amplify noise; no discussion of pruning or conditioning of Â and effects on µ1(Â).  
- Weakness: Complexity and runtime claims are under-supported—solver details, stopping criteria, and wall-clock comparisons are missing.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited to motion; other coherent domains (faces, background subtraction, text) and direct recovery metrics are missing.  
- Weakness: Limited baselines—should compare to LRR with A = X, structured RPCA variants (Outlier Pursuit, GoDec/AltProj), and RPCA+PCA.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state probabilistic model for support/sign randomness and source of randomness in theorems.  
- Suggestion: Provide bounds or experiments quantifying performance vs. subspace angle between U0 and UA.  
- Suggestion: Add ablations on rank threshold and dictionary preprocessing (column pruning, whitening).  
- Suggestion: Report runtimes, solver details, and consider releasing code.  
- Suggestion: Include more datasets and direct recovery error metrics (e.g., Frobenius norm errors) in experiments.  
- Pro for acceptance: Addresses a practical RPCA limitation with principled theory and a usable method.  
- Pro for acceptance: Novel theoretical insight that LRR with low-rank dictionaries can avoid µ2 dependence.  
- Con for acceptance: Strong dictionary assumption with limited analysis of approximate cases and unclear randomness assumptions.  
- Con for acceptance: Empirical comparisons and theoretical guarantees for the two-stage algorithm are incomplete.  
- Overall assessment: Promising, thoughtful contribution combining theory and practice; leans toward acceptance pending clearer probabilistic assumptions and expanded empirical validation.
