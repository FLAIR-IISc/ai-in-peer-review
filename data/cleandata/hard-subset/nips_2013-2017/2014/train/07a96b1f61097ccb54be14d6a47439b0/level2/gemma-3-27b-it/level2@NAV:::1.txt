## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses the problem of low-rank matrix recovery, specifically focusing on the degradation of Robust PCA (RPCA) performance when dealing with “coherent” data—data exhibiting clustering structures. The authors argue that coherence, often treated as a limitation, is intrinsically linked to these underlying data structures. They propose leveraging Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully designed (low-rank) dictionary to mitigate the effects of coherence, proving theoretically that this approach avoids the coherence parameter issue. An algorithm for unsupervised dictionary learning based on RPCA is also presented, with experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data.

The paper’s core contribution—demonstrating that a low-rank dictionary in LRR can circumvent the coherence challenges in RPCA—is a significant and interesting observation. The theoretical analysis connecting dictionary rank to coherence is valuable, and the unsupervised algorithm provides a practical avenue for implementation. The experiments, while relatively standard, provide supportive evidence for the claims. The authors clearly articulate the limitations of RPCA and position their work as a logical extension. 

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. While the theoretical results are presented, the intuition behind *why* a low-rank dictionary specifically solves the coherence issue isn’t fully fleshed out. The connection to existing work on dictionary learning is somewhat superficial; a more in-depth discussion of relevant literature in this area would strengthen the contribution. The experimental section is rather brief, and while results are positive, more thorough comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods beyond the chosen baselines would be beneficial. Finally, the writing style, while clear, is at times dense, making it harder to follow the technical arguments.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel theoretical insight into the relationship between coherence, low-rank structure, and dictionary learning.
*   A practical algorithm offering improved performance over RPCA in specific scenarios.
*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem in matrix recovery.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of intuitive explanation for the effectiveness of the low-rank dictionary.
*   Limited experimental evaluation.
*   Superficial connection to existing dictionary learning literature.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a solid paper with a novel contribution. While there are areas for improvement, the core idea is compelling and the theoretical analysis is rigorous. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more intuitive explanation of the core mechanism, expanded experimental comparisons, and a deeper discussion of related work on dictionary learning.
