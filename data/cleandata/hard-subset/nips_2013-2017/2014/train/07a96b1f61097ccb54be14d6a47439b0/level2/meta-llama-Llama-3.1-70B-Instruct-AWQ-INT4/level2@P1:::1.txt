This paper proposes a novel method for recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations. The authors identify the limitations of the existing RPCA method, which can fail to produce perfect recovery even when the data is strictly low-rank. They attribute this failure to the ignorance of clustering structures in the data, which can lead to high coherence parameters and degrade the performance of RPCA.

The authors propose to use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured dictionary to alleviate the challenges raised by coherent data. They prove that if the dictionary itself is low-rank, LRR is immune to the coherence parameter that increases with the underlying cluster number. This provides an elementary principle for dealing with coherent data and leads to a practical algorithm for obtaining proper dictionaries in unsupervised environments.

The paper makes several contributions. Firstly, it studies the problem of recovering low-rank and coherent matrices from their corrupted versions, which is a novel and important problem. Secondly, it provides insights into the physical meaning of coherence, which is a widely used concept in various literatures. Thirdly, it provides a practical algorithm for learning the dictionary matrix in LRR, which is a key component of the proposed method.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical foundations. The experimental results are also impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in recovering low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the proposed method and existing methods, such as RPCA and LRR with A = X. Secondly, the authors could provide more insights into the choice of the dictionary matrix A and its impact on the performance of the proposed method. Finally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method and its scalability to large datasets.
