- RPCA fails when the low-rank component L0 is "coherent," degrading recovery from X = L0 + S0.  
- High coherence (µ2, µ3) naturally arises when columns of L0 come from a union of multiple low-dimensional subspaces (clustered columns).  
- Increasing the number of clusters increases coherence, which worsens RPCA performance.  
- Using the LRR model X = AZ + S with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains that of L0 makes recovery immune to µ2/µ3 issues (Theorem 1).  
- The paper proposes a practical two-step unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA for an initial L0 estimate, build a low-rank dictionary Â from it, then solve LRR with Â to refine recovery.  
- Empirical results on synthetic clustered data and real-world motion segmentation show substantial improvement over standard RPCA.  
- Theoretical claims (Theorems 1 and 2) are well-stated and provide a rigorous foundation for the approach.  
- The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides strong motivation for addressing the µ2-phenomenon, aided by an illustrative figure.  
- Experiments are thorough, including large-scale synthetic phase-transition plots and a convincing real-world application.  
- The work is original: first systematic study linking RPCA failure to coherence arising from clustering and proposing an LRR-based remedy.  
- The paper reframes coherence assumptions as properties tied to intrinsic data structure, adding conceptual significance.  
- The proposed method has high practical relevance for common tasks (e.g., motion segmentation) where clustered subspace structure occurs.  
- Insight into using a learned low-rank dictionary A (not equal to X or I) is a valuable contribution to LRR modeling.  
- Strength: addresses an important, practical limitation of a widely used method (RPCA).  
- Strength: clear theoretical insight connecting clustering to µ2 coherence and why a low-rank dictionary helps.  
- Strength: simple, intuitive, and implementable two-step algorithm that leverages existing solvers.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees assume an ideal dictionary A containing U0, but the practical Â from RPCA may be imperfect; robustness to this is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: focus is primarily on µ2/µ3 coherence from clustered columns; high µ1 coherence (clustered rows) is mentioned but not addressed.  
- Weakness: computational-cost claim (~1.2× RPCA) lacks explicit runtime comparisons in experiments.  
- Weakness: implementation detail—dictionary Â is constructed as m×n (d = n)—should be explicitly stated and discussed.  
- Overall: minor clarifications and additional analyses would strengthen the paper, but core contributions are solid and impactful.
