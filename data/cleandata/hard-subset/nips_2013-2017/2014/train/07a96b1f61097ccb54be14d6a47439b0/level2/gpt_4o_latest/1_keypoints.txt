- Paper studies RPCA's failure in recovering low-rank matrices when data have clustering-induced high coherence and proposes using LRR with a learned dictionary to mitigate this.
- Shows theoretically that an appropriately constructed low-rank dictionary makes LRR immune to coherence parameters, enabling better recovery than RPCA.
- Proposes a practical two-stage algorithm: use RPCA to estimate the low-rank component, then construct a dictionary for LRR.
- Algorithm is simple, computationally efficient, and easy to implement.
- Experiments on synthetic data and real-world motion sequences demonstrate the proposed method outperforms RPCA in recovery and clustering tasks.
- Novel contribution addressing a significant limitation of RPCA via a dictionary-based LRR framework.
- Provides rigorous mathematical proofs clarifying conditions under which LRR avoids coherence issues.
- Comprehensive experimental validation with clear, tangible improvements (e.g., success regions, clustering error rates).
- Results and comparisons are presented clearly, making improvements easy to interpret.
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of limitations such as sensitivity to noise levels and parameter choices (e.g., λ).
- Dictionary construction depends on RPCA and may inherit RPCA’s shortcomings; alternative dictionary learning methods are not explored.
- Scalability to very large datasets is not thoroughly analyzed despite claims of computational efficiency.
- Comparisons are primarily against RPCA; broader comparisons with other robust recovery methods are missing.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations and broaden evaluations.
- Pros summarized: novel theory on coherence, practical validated algorithm, strong experimental results.
- Cons summarized: limited alternative dictionary exploration, scalability concerns, and insufficient comparisons with other methods.
- Overall rating given: 8/10.
