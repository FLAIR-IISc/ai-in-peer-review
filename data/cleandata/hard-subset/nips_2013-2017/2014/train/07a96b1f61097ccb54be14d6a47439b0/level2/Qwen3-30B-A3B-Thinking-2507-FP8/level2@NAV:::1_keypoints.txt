- Addresses RPCA's limitation with coherent data arising from clustering and proposes a solution.  
- Claims RPCA performance degrades as data coherence (more clusters) increases.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with an appropriately configured low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's coherence sensitivity.  
- Main contributions: theoretical proof that LRR with low-rank dictionaries avoids coherence parameters.  
- Main contributions: a practical unsupervised algorithm for dictionary learning.  
- Main contributions: experimental validation on synthetic and motion data.  
- Strong theoretical rigor: solid proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) and a novel link between coherence parameters and clustering structure.  
- Experiments show clear improvements: 47% larger success region on synthetic data and 40% relative improvement in motion clustering error rates.  
- Computational cost is reasonable: about 1.2× RPCA runtime.  
- Well-contextualized, properly cited, clearly written, and reproducible.  
- Minor limitation: dictionary learning relies on RPCA output, which may be imperfect for highly coherent data.  
- Could use more discussion on the relationship between dictionary rank and performance and on scaling to very large datasets.  
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality contribution that advances handling of coherent data; recommendation to accept.
