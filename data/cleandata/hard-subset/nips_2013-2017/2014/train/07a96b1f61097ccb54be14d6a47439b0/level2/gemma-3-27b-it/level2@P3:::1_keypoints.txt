- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery with corruptions, focusing on how data coherence degrades RPCA performance.
- Core contribution: shows Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary can mitigate coherence-related failures of RPCA.
- Provides theoretical analysis (notably Theorem 1) supporting the proposed approach.
- Proposes a practical algorithm that uses RPCA to estimate a dictionary for LRR.
- Demonstrates improved performance over RPCA on synthetic data and real-world motion sequence experiments.
- Clearly explains the link between clustering structures and increased data coherence.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and builds on established methods making implementation practical.
- Related work coverage is reasonable and cites key papers in robust PCA and low-rank recovery.
- Does not quantify how much improvement to expect across different data scenarios.
- Theoretical results focus on noiseless data and lack a comprehensive noisy-data analysis.
- The connection to Generalized PCA is mentioned but not explored in depth.
- The intuition for why the dictionary-learning step improves over RPCA could be presented more clearly.
- Experimental comparisons are limited to RPCA and lack other state-of-the-art robust PCA baselines.
- Missing ablation study analyzing the contribution of each algorithmic step, especially dictionary learning.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with clear motivation and promising empirical results.
- Argument against acceptance: limited theoretical scope (noiseless), insufficient quantification, sparse baseline comparisons, and absence of ablation.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with promising results; revision addressing theoretical extension, quantification, broader comparisons, and ablation is recommended.
