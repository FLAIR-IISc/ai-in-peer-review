- Paper identifies a “µ2-phenomenon”: as number of clusters increases (fixed overall rank), µ2 grows and RPCA guarantees degrade.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space to avoid µ2 dependence.  
- Main theoretical result: if U0 ⊂ UA and A is low-rank, LRR recovers L0 exactly (or near-exactly with noise) with bounds depending on µ1(A) not µ2.  
- Practical principle: learn a low-rank dictionary aligned with the underlying column space.  
- Unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA to get L0 estimate, form low-rank approximation, normalize columns to create A, then run LRR.  
- Empirically, synthetic experiments show ~47% expansion in recovery success region using the learned dictionary.  
- Motion segmentation experiments on 11 sequences show consistent downstream clustering improvements.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on RPCA and LRR; extends LRR theory to general dictionaries for robust recovery.  
- Theory quality: theorems are well-motivated and remove µ2 dependence, shifting requirements to µ1(A) with relaxed log factors.  
- Critical assumption: U0 ⊂ UA is necessary for exact recovery and is central to the theoretical guarantees.  
- Circularity risk: in unsupervised settings the algorithm relies on RPCA to provide UA, so guarantees may fail if RPCA fails badly.  
- Suggested theoretical improvement: clarify minimal closeness (e.g., principal angle bounds) between UA and U0 required to still benefit.  
- Experimental limitations: need experiments varying number of clusters at fixed rank to directly validate the µ2-phenomenon mitigation.  
- Experimental robustness: evaluate non-i.i.d. corruptions (heavy-tailed magnitudes, structured/block occlusions) and adversarial settings.  
- Robustness to rank misestimation: test sensitivity to rank-thresholding (10−3σ1) and alternative rank estimation methods.  
- Sensitivity to RPCA stage: empirically quantify how imperfect UA affects final performance and whether iterative refinement helps.  
- Computational claim caveat: reported ~1.2× RPCA runtime depends on solvers; include optimizer details, stopping criteria, and wall-clock times for reproducibility.  
- Clarity: writing and notation are generally clear; proofs and some figures are deferred to a “full paper,” reducing self-containedness.  
- Originality: clearly articulates clustering-induced coherence in terms of µ2 and provides novel theoretical guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries.  
- Significance: addresses a common RPCA failure mode with a simple, theoretically justified remedy that could impact applied pipelines.  
- Weakness: reliance on RPCA initialization and limited analysis of when UA captures U0 undermines practical guarantees.  
- Weakness: experiments are somewhat narrow in corruption types and dataset breadth, limiting confidence in generality.  
- Suggested related work expansion: compare to other robust subspace recovery methods (Outlier Pursuit, Tyler’s M-estimator, nonconvex factorization) in coherent settings.  
- Overall: solid and timely contribution; strengthens understanding and practice for coherent-data recovery but needs broader empirical validation and clarification of assumptions.
