This paper addresses the problem of low-rank matrix recovery in the presence of corruptions, focusing on the challenges posed by data coherence, which arises naturally when data exhibits clustering structures. The authors argue that RPCA, while powerful, can struggle with coherent data and propose a modification based on Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary. They theoretically demonstrate that using a low-rank dictionary in LRR mitigates the coherence issue and propose an algorithm to learn such a dictionary in an unsupervised manner, leveraging the output of RPCA. The paper includes both synthetic and real-world experimental validation.

**Summary of Related Work:** The paper appropriately situates itself within the context of robust PCA, referencing key works like Candes et al. (2009, 2010), Recht & Candes (2011), and the original RPCA paper (Candes et al., 2011). It also correctly connects the approach to LRR (Liu et al., 2013) and highlights the relationship to matrix factorization techniques. The citation of relevant papers is thorough and demonstrates familiarity with the field.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Statement:** The motivation for addressing coherence in low-rank recovery is clearly articulated, and the connection to clustering structures is well-explained.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a theoretical result demonstrating that a low-rank dictionary in LRR can alleviate the coherence problem. This is a valuable contribution.
*   **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm, building upon RPCA to learn the dictionary, appears sensible and relatively straightforward to implement.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real data support the claims of the paper, showing improved performance compared to RPCA. The use of the Hopkins motion dataset is a good choice for demonstrating real-world applicability.
*   **Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized, making it easier to follow the authors' reasoning.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty of LRR Usage:** While the theoretical result regarding the low-rank dictionary is new, the idea of using LRR for robust recovery is not. The primary novelty lies in the *reasoning* behind choosing a low-rank dictionary to address coherence, rather than the application of LRR itself. This feels slightly overstated in the contributions list.
*   **Algorithm's Dependence on RPCA:** The proposed algorithm relies on RPCA as a first step. If RPCA performs poorly due to high coherence, the initial dictionary estimate may be suboptimal, potentially limiting the effectiveness of the subsequent LRR step. The authors acknowledge this implicitly, but a more detailed discussion of this dependency would be beneficial.
*   **Experimental Scope:** The experiments, while positive, could be expanded. It would be valuable to compare the proposed method against other state-of-the-art robust matrix recovery techniques beyond RPCA and standard LRR. The parameter settings for RPCA and LRR were kept consistent, but a more thorough parameter search could showcase the method's robustness.
*   **Assumptions in Theorem 1:** The assumptions in Theorem 1, particularly the requirement that `U0` is a subspace of `UA`, are quite strong.  While mathematically convenient, they may not hold in all practical scenarios. A discussion of how sensitive the results are to violations of these assumptions would be helpful.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant and important problem in robust matrix recovery.
*   The theoretical analysis provides a valuable insight into the role of coherence and dictionary learning.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the approach is somewhat incremental, as it builds upon existing techniques (RPCA and LRR).
*   The algorithm's dependence on the initial RPCA step could be a limitation.
*   The strong assumptions in the theoretical result may limit its applicability.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of robust matrix recovery. While the novelty is not groundbreaking, the theoretical justification and practical demonstration of using a low-rank dictionary in LRR to address coherence are valuable. The strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Specific Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   **Clarify the Novelty:** Tone down the claims of novelty in the introduction and contributions list. Emphasize the theoretical insight regarding the low-rank dictionary rather than just the application of LRR.
*   **Discuss RPCA Dependence:** Elaborate on the potential impact of poor RPCA performance on the dictionary learning step.
*   **Expand Experiments:** Include comparisons to other robust matrix recovery methods and perform a more thorough parameter search.
*   **Address Theorem 1 Assumptions:** Discuss the sensitivity of the results to violations of the assumptions in Theorem 1.
*   **Minor:** Ensure consistent notation throughout the paper (e.g. consistently using `L0` and `S0` vs. `L` and `S`).
