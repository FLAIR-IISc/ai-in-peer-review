- Proposes a novel method to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.  
- Identifies RPCA failure even for strictly low-rank data due to ignoring clustering structure and high coherence.  
- Attributes RPCA degradation to increasing coherence parameters caused by cluster structure.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured dictionary to address coherent data.  
- Theoretical claim: if the dictionary is low-rank, LRR is immune to coherence growth with cluster number.  
- Provides an elementary principle for handling coherent data and a practical unsupervised algorithm to learn dictionaries.  
- Contributions: studies recovery of low-rank coherent matrices, clarifies the physical meaning of coherence, and offers a dictionary-learning algorithm for LRR.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical explanation.  
- Experimental results are strong and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Minor issue: needs more detailed discussion of relationship to existing methods (e.g., RPCA, LRR with A = X).  
- Minor issue: could provide deeper insight into choice and impact of the dictionary matrix A.  
- Minor issue: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.
