- Key idea: Coherence arises from clustering and can be mitigated by using a low-rank dictionary in LRR.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proof that a low-rank dictionary in LRR removes the problematic coherence parameter.  
- Algorithmic contribution: An unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm based on RPCA is proposed.  
- Experiments: Synthetic and real motion-sequence experiments support the claims, though are standard.  
- Strength: Novel theoretical insight connecting coherence, low-rank structure, and dictionary learning.  
- Strength: Provides a practical method that outperforms RPCA in targeted scenarios.  
- Strength: Addresses an important, challenging problem in low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Weakness: Insufficient intuitive explanation for why a low-rank dictionary resolves coherence.
