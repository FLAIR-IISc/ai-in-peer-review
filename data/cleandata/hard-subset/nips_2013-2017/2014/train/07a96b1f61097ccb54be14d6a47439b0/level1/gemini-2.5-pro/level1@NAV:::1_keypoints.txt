- Identifies a key limitation of RPCA: degraded performance on “coherent” data that arises from multi-subspace or clustered structure.  
- Shows that a coherence parameter increases with the number of clusters, violating RPCA’s recovery conditions.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly chosen dictionary to overcome RPCA’s failure mode.  
- Theoretically proves that if the LRR dictionary is low-rank and its column space contains the true data span, recovery is immune to the problematic coherence parameter.  
- Introduces a practical two-stage unsupervised algorithm: initial estimate via RPCA, then construct a dictionary and refine with LRR.  
- Provides sound theoretical analysis linking coherence, clustering, and robust subspace recovery, building on prior foundational work.  
- Supports claims with extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world motion-capture data showing substantial improvement over RPCA.  
- Demonstrates wider successful recovery region and better downstream task performance with the proposed method.  
- Main weakness: the practical algorithm is heuristic and depends on RPCA producing a reasonable initial estimate; behavior under very poor initial outputs is not discussed.  
- Dictionary construction is simple and could be improved; exploring alternative constructions is a suggested extension.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with solid theory and empirical validation, likely to inspire future work.
