- Addresses recovery of low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, coherent (clustered) data using LRR with a properly configured low-rank dictionary.  
- Main contributions: study of recovering low-rank/coherent matrices, analysis of regimes that produce coherence, and a practical unsupervised algorithm to obtain dictionaries.  
- Builds on RPCA and prior LRR work, acknowledging RPCA limitations for coherent data and extending coherence-parameter analysis.  
- Theoretical claims are well-supported with a rigorous proof of the main theorem.  
- Experimental results show the proposed method effectively recovers low-rank matrices from coherent data.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent notation and some equations lack clear explanation.  
- Lacks sufficient details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.  
- Overall clarity and organization are good, but the introduction could be more concise and related work section more detailed.  
- Technical terms and concepts sometimes need more explanation for readability.  
- Originality: the low-rank dictionary approach in LRR is novel and well-motivated.  
- Significance: addresses an important, widely applicable problem (e.g., image processing, computer vision, ML).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve readability, clarify notation/equations, and add complexity details.
