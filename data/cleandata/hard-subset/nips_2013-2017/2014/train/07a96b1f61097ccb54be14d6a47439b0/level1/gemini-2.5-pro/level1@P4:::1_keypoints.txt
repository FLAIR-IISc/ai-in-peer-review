- Identifies RPCA's failure on "coherent" data and the need to recover low-rank L0 from X = L0 + S0 under clustering-induced coherence.  
- Shows that increasing number of underlying clusters raises the row-space coherence parameter µ2, tightening RPCA's recovery conditions ("µ2-phenomenon").  
- Links the µ2-phenomenon to intrinsic clustering structure in real-world data, providing an interpretable cause for RPCA degradation.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) to recover AZ instead of L, where A is a pre-specified dictionary.  
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 1): if A is low-rank and its column space contains L0's column space, LRR recovery is independent of coherence parameters µ2 and µ3.  
- Provides a clear dictionary-design principle: make the dictionary low-rank and ensure it spans the data column space.  
- Presents a practical two-stage unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA to get an initial L0 estimate, build a low-rank dictionary from it, then apply LRR to refine recovery.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic clustered data and real-world motion segmentation demonstrates substantial improvement over RPCA.  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous theoretical analysis, clearly stated theorems, and thorough experiments.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; µ2-phenomenon and implications are well explained with illustrative figures.  
- Original contribution: first systematic study linking RPCA failure to clustering/coherence and repurposing LRR with theoretical justification.  
- Significant impact: addresses a key RPCA limitation and offers a practical, theoretically grounded solution likely to influence future work.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel problem formulation formalizing RPCA's weakness on coherent data.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong theoretical guarantee showing low-rank dictionary immunity to coherence parameters.  
- Argument for acceptance: simple, effective two-step algorithm with convincing empirical gains.  
- Argument for acceptance: high clarity and broad relevance to matrix recovery and subspace learning communities.  
- Argument against acceptance: method depends on the initial RPCA estimate, and failure modes when that estimate is poor are not fully characterized.  
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical assumptions require UA to contain U0, but practical algorithm only approximates this with no theoretical gap analysis.  
- Argument against acceptance: algorithm introduces rank estimation/hyperparameters whose sensitivity and robustness are not deeply explored.  
- Overall assessment: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; the paper makes a clear, well-supported, and significant contribution to robust matrix recovery.
