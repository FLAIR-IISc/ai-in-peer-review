Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses robust low-rank matrix recovery in the presence of gross corruptions when the low-rank component exhibits coherent structure due to clustering of data. Prior work on Robust PCA (RPCA/PCP; Candès et al., JACM 2011) offers exact recovery under incoherence (captured by coherence parameters µ1, µ2, µ3). The authors articulate and empirically illustrate a “µ2-phenomenon”: as the number of underlying clusters increases (union-of-subspaces model with fixed total rank), the row-space coherence µ2 (and thus µ3) grows, degrading RPCA’s guarantees and practice.

They consider Low-Rank Representation (LRR; Liu et al., ICML 2010; T-PAMI 2013), but with a general dictionary A in X = AZ + S. The core theoretical contribution shows that if A is low-rank and its column space contains the true column space U0 (i.e., PUA(U0) = U0), then exact (or near-exact) recovery by LRR can be guaranteed without dependence on µ2, only on µ1(A). Specifically, for λ = 1/√n1 and |Ω| ≤ (0.5−ε)mn (noiseless) or (0.35−ε)mn (noisy), if rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ O(n2/(µ1(A) log n1)), LRR exactly recovers (Z*, S*) = (A+L0, S0) with high probability; in the noisy case, it yields explicit Frobenius error bounds O(√mn ε). This effectively “immunizes” the method against the µ2 increase induced by clustering.

Practically, they propose an unsupervised two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to obtain an estimate L̂0, estimate its rank, form a truncated SVD approximation L̃0, normalize its columns to build a low-rank dictionary Â, and solve LRR with Â. On synthetic data with union-of-subspace structure and sparse corruptions, their method expands the success region by 47% relative to RPCA. On corrupted motion trajectory matrices (Hopkins-like sequences), using matrix recovery as preprocessing reduces subspace clustering errors for SIM/LRRx/SSC; replacing RPCA with the proposed LRR-with-learned-dictionary further reduces SSC error from 9.50% to 5.74%.

Quality
- Theory: The noiseless/noisy theorems are clearly stated, with assumptions explicit (column-normalized A, PUA(U0) = U0, rank bounds, sparsity bounds). The key qualitative advance is eliminating µ2 from the sample complexity bound by leveraging a low-rank dictionary that spans U0. The bounds are plausible and consistent with PCP-style analyses; the improvement from O(n2/(log n1)^2) to O(n2/log n1) relative to RPCA’s worst-case dependence is noted. However, the guarantees rely on A satisfying PUA(U0) = U0; the proposed unsupervised procedure has no formal guarantee to produce such an A unless RPCA already succeeded sufficiently well. The paper claims robustness to dictionary “errors” empirically, but a perturbation analysis (UA approximately containing U0) would strengthen the contribution.
- Empirics: The synthetic study is well matched to the hypothesis (union-of-subspaces plus sparse errors), with a broad sweep over rank and sparsity. The motion segmentation experiment is meaningful and demonstrates practical impact; nevertheless, the corruption model for missing data (replacing with ±1) is somewhat artificial compared to masking-plus-noise, and competing robust-recovery baselines are limited (e.g., no comparison to Outlier Pursuit, GoDec, reweighted PCP, nonconvex surrogates). Runtime claims are qualitative; solvers and convergence details are not deeply discussed.
- Overall, the work is technically solid in its main theorem and sensible in algorithm design; the empirical support is positive but could be broader.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well-written. Coherence parameters µ1, µ2, µ3 are defined clearly, and the µ2-phenomenon is explained with intuition and figures. Assumptions and notations are explicit. Some proofs are deferred to the arXiv version, which is acceptable but weakens self-containedness. The algorithm is presented clearly with reasonable parameter choices. Minor suggestions: briefly discuss solver choices (e.g., ADMM/IALM) and complexity; clarify sensitivity to the rank threshold (10^-3 σ1).

Originality
- Conceptually novel is the explicit link between clustering-induced coherence and RPCA failure, and the theoretical result that LRR with a low-rank dictionary spanning U0 avoids dependence on µ2. While LRR is known, results for general low-rank dictionaries and their effect on coherence are new and insightful. The simple RPCA→dictionary→LRR pipeline is pragmatic; while heuristic, it offers a new angle for robust recovery when incoherence is violated.

Significance
- The problem is important: many real datasets are coherent due to clustering. The theory provides a useful design principle (use low-rank dictionaries aligned with the data subspace), informing both method development and practice. The empirical gains on synthetic and motion data suggest utility in subspace clustering pipelines. If further developed (e.g., approximate-span guarantees, scalable solvers), this line could influence robust subspace learning broadly.

Strengths
- Clear identification and analysis of the coherence issue (µ2-phenomenon) in RPCA under union-of-subspaces structure.
- Theoretical guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries that bypass µ2.
- Practical, simple algorithm with demonstrated performance improvements.
- Good alignment between theory and experiment.

Weaknesses
- Strong assumption PUA(U0) = U0 for guarantees; no theoretical link that the unsupervised procedure achieves this when RPCA is imperfect.
- Limited baselines and somewhat artificial handling of missing data (±1 replacement) in motion experiments; no experiments on truly masked observations.
- Lack of sensitivity analyses (e.g., to rank estimation, λ, dictionary conditioning).
- Proofs deferred to arXiv reduce self-containedness for NIPS.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide perturbation bounds when dist(U0, UA) > 0 to formalize robustness to imperfect dictionaries.
- Evaluate against additional robust recovery baselines and on masked-matrix settings (with explicit masking operators) to reflect missing data realistically.
- Report solver details, convergence, and runtime, and include larger-scale experiments.
- Analyze dependence on µ1(A) and provide guidelines to ensure small µ1(A) in practice.
- Consider an iterative refinement variant and quantify when it helps.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses an important and underexplored failure mode of RPCA with a principled fix.
- Solid theoretical contribution with clear practical implications.
- Demonstrated gains on synthetic and real data.

Arguments against acceptance
- The main algorithm’s success hinges on a preliminary RPCA step, with no end-to-end guarantee.
- Experimental evaluation is not as comprehensive as expected for NIPS (missing baselines and masking setting).
- Some reliance on extended version for proofs.

Overall assessment
This is a thoughtful and well-motivated paper that advances our understanding of coherence in robust low-rank recovery and provides a practical, effective remedy via low-rank dictionaries in LRR. Despite some limitations in experimental breadth and assumptions needed for theory, the contribution is significant and of interest to the NIPS community. I lean toward acceptance.
