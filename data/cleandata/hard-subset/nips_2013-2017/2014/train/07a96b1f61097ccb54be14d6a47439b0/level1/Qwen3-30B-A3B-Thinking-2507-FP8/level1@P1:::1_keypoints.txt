- Identifies RPCA's limitation: performance degrades on coherent, clustered data due to increasing coherence parameters.  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's coherence issue.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorems 1 and 2 prove exact/near-exact recovery for LRR with low-rank dictionaries and show immunity to problematic coherence parameters.  
- Novel insight: coherence parameters reflect intrinsic clustering structure, not just proof assumptions.  
- Practical unsupervised method: uses RPCA output to construct the dictionary for LRR.  
- Strong experimental validation on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences, showing clear improvements (≈40% relative clustering error reduction).  
- Paper is technically sound with well-motivated proofs and clear theoretical foundations.  
- Well-written, well-organized manuscript with consistent notation, clear figures, and a clear algorithm description.  
- Original contribution distinct from prior RPCA/LRR work and confirmed by literature review.  
- Significant practical impact for applications in vision and data analysis where RPCA fails on coherent data.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and practical, simple algorithm likely to be adopted by practitioners.  
- Weakness: theoretical condition U0 ⊂ Ua may be difficult to guarantee in practice.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of interactions with other coherence parameters (µ1, µ3).  
- Weakness: computational complexity of Algorithm 1 requires more detailed analysis and explicit comparison to RPCA.  
- Weakness: scalability to extremely large datasets is not addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses real RPCA limitation, novel and significant theory, convincing experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially restrictive theoretical conditions, incomplete analysis of limitations across data scenarios, insufficient large-scale efficiency validation.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance.
