

**Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"**

This paper investigates a critical limitation of RPCA (Robust Principal Component Analysis) when dealing with coherent data, specifically data exhibiting clustering structures. The authors demonstrate that as the number of clusters increases, the coherence parameter µ2 rises, degrading RPCA's performance. They propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to overcome this issue, proving that when the dictionary is low-rank and contains the column space of the true low-rank matrix, LRR becomes immune to coherence parameters. The paper introduces a practical unsupervised algorithm for dictionary learning based on RPCA results and validates it through synthetic and motion sequence experiments.

**Strengths:**

1. **Problem Relevance:** The paper correctly identifies a significant practical limitation of RPCA in real-world applications where data often exhibits clustering structures (e.g., motion sequences in computer vision). This addresses a genuine gap in the literature, as RPCA is widely used despite its coherence sensitivity.

2. **Theoretical Contributions:** The authors provide valuable theoretical insights (Theorems 1 and 2) establishing recovery guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries. The distinction between coherence parameters (particularly µ2's role) and their relationship to data structures represents an important conceptual advance in understanding low-rank recovery.

3. **Practical Algorithm:** Algorithm 1 is well-motivated and computationally efficient, with a clear explanation of why it typically runs only 1.2× slower than RPCA. The experimental validation on Hopkins155 demonstrates a 40% relative improvement in clustering error rates, which is significant for real-world applications.

4. **Clarity of Motivation:** The paper effectively demonstrates the "µ2-phenomenon" through synthetic experiments (Figure 2), making the problem and its significance clear to readers.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Theoretical Gap in Dictionary Construction:** The paper claims that Algorithm 1 ensures U0 ⊂ UA (necessary for exact recovery), but provides no theoretical guarantee for this condition. The authors state "it is unnecessary for A to satisfy UA = U0" but fail to prove that their dictionary construction satisfies U0 ⊂ UA. This is a critical omission, as the entire theoretical contribution hinges on this condition. The paper should provide a rigorous analysis of when this condition holds for the proposed dictionary.

2. **Limited Differentiation from Existing LRR Work:** While the paper correctly states that LRR with general dictionaries is not fully understood, it doesn't sufficiently distinguish its contribution from Liu et al.'s 2013 LRR paper (which introduced the LRR framework). The authors should clarify how their dictionary learning approach specifically addresses the coherence issue that existing LRR methods might not handle.

3. **Incomplete Explanation of Coherence-Clustering Relationship:** The paper states that clustering increases µ2 but provides only intuitive (not mathematical) justification. A more rigorous explanation of why clustering structures affect µ2 would strengthen the theoretical foundation.

4. **Marginal Practical Improvement:** While the 40% relative improvement on Hopkins155 is meaningful, the paper doesn't explore when this improvement is significant versus negligible. The synthetic experiments show a 47% wider success area, but it's unclear how this translates to real-world data distributions that may not follow the synthetic model.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a valuable contribution by identifying a practical limitation of RPCA and proposing a theoretically grounded solution using LRR with low-rank dictionaries. The theoretical insights about coherence parameters and their relationship to data structures are novel and important. The practical algorithm is well-motivated and demonstrates clear improvements over RPCA in real-world applications.

The main weakness is the lack of theoretical guarantee for the dictionary construction step (U0 ⊂ UA), which is essential for the proposed method's success. This gap needs to be addressed through additional analysis. The paper could also better differentiate its contribution from existing LRR work.

**Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions)

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Identifies and addresses a significant limitation of RPCA in real-world applications
- Provides meaningful theoretical insights about coherence parameters
- Proposes a practical, well-validated algorithm with clear experimental results
- Demonstrates significant improvement over RPCA on real motion data

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- Theoretical gap in dictionary construction (no guarantee of U0 ⊂ UA)
- Insufficient differentiation from existing LRR work
- Limited mathematical explanation of coherence-clustering relationship

The paper is well-written, addresses a relevant problem, and provides valuable insights. With minor revisions to address the theoretical gap in dictionary construction and better differentiate from prior LRR work, this would be a strong contribution to NIPS. The practical significance of handling coherent data in real-world applications makes this an important paper for the field.
