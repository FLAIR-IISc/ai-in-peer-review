- Identifies an important practical limitation of RPCA: clustering in data raises µ2 and degrades RPCA performance.  
- Theoretically shows LRR with a low-rank dictionary that contains the true column space can be immune to coherence parameters (Theorems 1–2).  
- Distinguishes the role of coherence parameters, especially µ2, and links them conceptually to data structure.  
- Proposes a practical, computationally efficient unsupervised dictionary-learning algorithm (Algorithm 1) based on RPCA.  
- Empirically demonstrates substantial improvement: ~40% relative reduction in clustering error on Hopkins155 and wider success region in synthetic tests.  
- Clearly motivates the “µ2-phenomenon” with illustrative synthetic experiments (Figure 2).  
- Lacks theoretical guarantee that the proposed dictionary construction satisfies U0 ⊂ UA, a critical condition for exact recovery.  
- Does not rigorously prove when or why the algorithm’s dictionary will contain the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA).  
- Insufficiently differentiates the contribution from prior LRR work (e.g., Liu et al. 2013) and should clarify novelty.  
- Provides only intuitive, not mathematical, justification for why clustering increases µ2; needs a more rigorous explanation.  
- Unclear how synthetic improvements translate to diverse real-world data distributions; practical significance boundaries are not fully explored.  
- Overall: meaningful and novel contribution with valuable theory and practical gains, but requires minor revisions to close the dictionary-construction theoretical gap and better position relative to prior LRR.  
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
