- Paper proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly configured dictionary to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, clustered (coherent) data.
- Argues RPCA fails on coherent data because it cannot capture structures beyond low-rankness.
- Provides theoretical proof that LRR can avoid coherence parameters that grow with cluster number when the dictionary is low-rank.
- Presents a practical, unsupervised algorithm to learn the dictionary matrix.
- Experimental results show the proposed method outperforms RPCA in extensive tests.
- Paper is well-written with clear problem setup, related work, and method description.
- Strength: thorough analysis of RPCA limitations and benefits of LRR with a suitable dictionary.
- Strength: proposed algorithm is simple, effective, and accompanied by a detailed correctness proof.
- Strength: empirical evidence supports the method’s effectiveness in recovering low-rank matrices from gross corruption.
- Weakness: assumption that the dictionary matrix is low-rank may not hold in all practical scenarios.
- Weakness: algorithm requires estimating the rank of the low-rank matrix, which can be difficult in practice.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other existing robust low-rank recovery methods.
- Pro-accept: novel approach addressing coherent data with significant theoretical contributions and strong experiments.
- Con-accept: reliance on low-rank dictionary assumption, need for rank estimation, and limited baseline comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance.
