- Proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, coherent data.
- Argues RPCA is insufficient for handling coherent data common in modern applications.
- Provides theoretical recovery guarantees for coherent data under the proposed LRR framework.
- Proposes a practical, unsupervised algorithm to learn the low-rank dictionary in LRR.
- Builds on RPCA and prior LRR work, offering new insights into the LRR model for coherent-data recovery.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis supporting recovery claims.
- Strength: practical algorithm suitable for unsupervised settings.
- Strength: extensive experiments on synthetic data and real motion sequences demonstrating effectiveness.
- Weakness: algorithm requires solving two convex programs, which can be computationally expensive.
- Weakness: performance may be sensitive to parameter choices (dictionary rank, regularization).
