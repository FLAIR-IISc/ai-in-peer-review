- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery under corruptions, focusing on how data coherence harms RPCA.
- Shows RPCA performance degrades with increasing coherence arising from clustered data.
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence-related failures.
- Proves a low-rank dictionary makes LRR independent of the data coherence parameter (with the caveat that dependence shifts to the dictionary).
- Presents an unsupervised algorithm to learn the low-rank dictionary and demonstrates empirical improvements.
- Technically sound and builds on established RPCA/LRR theory; proofs appear rigorous but are referenced to arXiv.
- The “immune to coherence” claim is overstated; more accurate to say coherence dependence moves to the dictionary.
- Experimental results show benefits on synthetic and real data but need stronger statistical analysis (confidence intervals, p-values).
- Algorithm is reasonable and practical but relies on correct rank estimation and discusses noise tolerance.
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; notation is dense and full proofs should be included for reproducibility.
- Original contribution: linking coherence to clustering and applying low-rank dictionaries in LRR is novel and insightful.
- Significant potential impact for practitioners in vision, signal processing, and ML dealing with coherent data.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, practical algorithm with demonstrated improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on arXiv for proofs, overstated immunity claim, and weaker statistical validation.
- Overall assessment: strong accept, with recommended improvements to reproducibility and experimental rigor.
