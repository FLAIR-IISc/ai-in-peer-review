## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses the challenge of low-rank matrix recovery when the underlying low-rank matrix exhibits coherence, a phenomenon that degrades the performance of Robust PCA (RPCA). The authors identify that data clustering contributes to higher coherence and propose leveraging Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed dictionary to mitigate this issue. Specifically, they prove that a low-rank dictionary renders LRR immune to the coherence parameter related to clustering. They then present an algorithm leveraging RPCA to estimate the dictionary in an unsupervised manner.

The paper builds upon the established work of RPCA [4] and LRR [13]. It relates to a broader line of research on robust PCA and matrix completion [2, 3, 7] and connects to areas like subspace clustering [5, 6] through the experimental validation. The introduction effectively motivates the problem by highlighting the limitations of RPCA in the presence of clustering and clearly states the core contribution: demonstrating that a low-rank dictionary can alleviate coherence issues in LRR. 

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper provides a mathematical proof linking low-rank dictionaries to immunity against coherence, offering a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed approach.
* **Clear problem identification:**  The authors effectively explain how data clustering affects the coherence of the low-rank matrix and, consequently, the performance of RPCA.
* **Practical algorithm:**  The proposed algorithm offers a practical approach to dictionary learning in unsupervised settings, building on the existing RPCA framework.
* **Experimental validation:**  Experiments on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to RPCA. 

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited novelty beyond the theoretical result:** While the theoretical connection between low-rank dictionaries and coherence is novel, the algorithm itself relies heavily on RPCA, which is a well-established technique. The dictionary learning step feels somewhat heuristic.
* **Clarity of Theorem 1’s conditions:**  The conditions in Theorem 1, particularly the requirement PUA(U0) = U0, are not fully explained intuitively. While mathematically stated, a clearer connection to practical dictionary design would be beneficial.
* **Limited comparison to other LRR variants:** The experiments largely compare against RPCA. A comparison to other LRR approaches with different dictionary learning strategies would provide more context.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel theoretical result demonstrating a crucial link between dictionary design and coherence in LRR.
* It addresses a relevant and practical problem—improving low-rank recovery in the presence of clustering.
* The experimental results support the theoretical claims and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The algorithmic contribution is incremental and heavily relies on existing methods.
* The theoretical conditions for the main result might be difficult to verify in practice.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **weak accept**. The theoretical contribution is significant and the experiments are well-executed. However, the algorithmic novelty is limited and the practical implications of the theoretical conditions require further clarification.  Addressing the clarity of the theorem’s conditions and providing a more comprehensive comparison against related work would significantly strengthen the paper.
