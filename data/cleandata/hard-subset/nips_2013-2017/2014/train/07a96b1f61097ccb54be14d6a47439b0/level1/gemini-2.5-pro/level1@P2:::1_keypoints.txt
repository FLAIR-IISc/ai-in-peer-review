- Addresses recovery of low-rank L0 from X = L0 + S0 and shows RPCA fails when L0 is "coherent".
- Identifies and formalizes the "µ2-phenomenon": clustering increases row-space coherence µ2 and degrades RPCA recovery.
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A and proves (Theorem 1) that if col(U0) ⊂ col(UA) then recovery is independent of µ2.
- Introduces a practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA → build low-rank dictionary from its output → solve LRR for refined recovery.
- Empirical results on synthetic clustered data and real motion segmentation show substantial improvement over standard RPCA.
- Theoretical analysis is strong and isolates the role of µ2, providing a rigorous justification for the LRR approach.
- Experiments are well-designed and directly validate the theoretical predictions in the targeted regime.
- Main weakness: the dictionary-learning step is heuristic—no guarantee the RPCA-derived dictionary satisfies UA ⊇ U0 when the initial estimate is poor.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with intuitive explanations and effective visualizations of the µ2-phenomenon.
- Original contributions: formalizing µ2-phenomenon, proving LRR’s robustness with low-rank dictionaries for general A, and the two-stage dictionary pursuit algorithm.
- Significance: identifies a practical limitation of RPCA, offers a theoretically grounded and easily implemented fix, and is likely to influence future work.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong motivation, novel theory, effective algorithm, and convincing experiments; recommendation to accept.
- Argument against acceptance: reliance on an unguaranteed heuristic for dictionary construction.
