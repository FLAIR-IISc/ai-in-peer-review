- Paper studies robust low-rank matrix recovery when clustering induces high row-space coherence (µ2-phenomenon) that breaks RPCA guarantees.
- Shows LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space U0 can avoid dependence on µ2 and recover exactly/near-exactly.
- Theoretical results: for λ=1/√n1 and sparsity up to ~0.35–0.5 mn, if rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ O(n2/(µ1(A) log n1)), LRR recovers (Z*,S*) with high probability and bounded error in noisy case.
- Practical two-stage algorithm: RPCA → estimate rank and build truncated SVD dictionary Â → solve LRR with Â.
- Empirically expands success region by 47% on synthetic union-of-subspaces data and reduces SSC error from 9.50% to 5.74% on motion trajectory data.
- Key theoretical advance: eliminates µ2 from sample complexity by leveraging a low-rank dictionary that spans U0, relying only on µ1(A).
- Main theoretical caveat: guarantees require the strong assumption PUA(U0) = U0 (A’s column space exactly contains U0).
- Unsupervised procedure has no formal guarantee to produce such an A when initial RPCA is imperfect; robustness to dictionary errors is only empirically shown.
- Empirics are well-matched to the hypothesis and include meaningful motion segmentation tests, but missing-data handling (±1 replacement) is artificial.
- Comparative evaluation is limited: lacking baselines like Outlier Pursuit, GoDec, reweighted PCP, and nonconvex methods.
- Runtime, solver details, and convergence behavior are discussed only qualitatively and lack depth.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear definitions (µ1, µ2, µ3) and intuitive explanation of the µ2-phenomenon; some proofs deferred to arXiv.
- Contribution is conceptually novel: links clustering-induced coherence to RPCA failure and shows LRR with learned low-rank dictionary as a principled fix.
- Significance: addresses an important practical failure mode and gives a useful design principle for robust subspace learning.
- Strengths: clear identification of µ2 issue, solid theorems for LRR with low-rank dictionaries, pragmatic algorithm, and good theory–experiment alignment.
- Weaknesses: strong assumption PUA(U0)=U0, limited baselines, artificial missing-data treatment, lack of sensitivity analyses (rank, λ, conditioning).
- Suggested improvements: provide perturbation bounds for approximate span, evaluate more baselines and masked settings, report solver/runtime details, analyze µ1(A) dependence, and consider iterative refinement.
- Arguments for acceptance: important underexplored failure mode, principled theoretical fix, and demonstrated empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on preliminary RPCA without end-to-end guarantees, incomplete experimental breadth, and proofs not fully contained in the submission.
- Overall assessment: thoughtful, technically solid, practically useful work that leans toward acceptance despite experimental and theoretical limitations.
