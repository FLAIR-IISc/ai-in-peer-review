This paper proposes a new approach to recover coherent data via low-rank dictionary pursuit. The authors argue that the recently established RPCA method is not an ultimate solution to the low-rank matrix recovery problem, especially when the data exhibits clustering structures. They show that the challenges raised by coherent data can be alleviated by Low-Rank Representation (LRR) provided that the dictionary in LRR is configured appropriately. The main contribution of this paper is to prove that if the dictionary itself is low-rank, then LRR is immune to the coherence parameter which increases with the underlying cluster number.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough review of the related work and clearly explain the limitations of the existing methods. The proposed approach is well-motivated and the theoretical analysis is sound. The experiments on randomly generated matrices and real motion sequences demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The strengths of this paper are:

* The authors provide a clear and thorough analysis of the limitations of the existing methods and the advantages of the proposed approach.
* The theoretical analysis is sound and well-motivated.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

* The proposed approach is not significantly different from the existing methods. The authors argue that the dictionary in LRR should be low-rank, but this is not a new idea.
* The experiments are limited to randomly generated matrices and real motion sequences. More experiments on different types of data would be helpful to demonstrate the robustness of the proposed approach.
* The authors do not provide a clear comparison with other state-of-the-art methods. A more detailed comparison would be helpful to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach.
