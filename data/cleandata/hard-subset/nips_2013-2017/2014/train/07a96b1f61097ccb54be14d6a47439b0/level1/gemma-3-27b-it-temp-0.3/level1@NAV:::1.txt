## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses the problem of low-rank matrix recovery, specifically focusing on the degradation of Robust PCA (RPCA) performance when dealing with data exhibiting clustering structures, leading to higher coherence. The authors argue that RPCA’s sensitivity to coherence stems from ignoring underlying data structures and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach with a carefully designed dictionary to mitigate this issue. They theoretically demonstrate that a low-rank dictionary renders LRR immune to coherence and present an algorithm to learn such a dictionary in an unsupervised manner, validated through experiments on synthetic and real-world motion sequence data.

This work builds upon the established framework of RPCA [Candès et al., 2009] and LRR [Liu et al., 2013]. It correctly identifies a limitation of RPCA – its sensitivity to data coherence – and attempts to address it by leveraging the flexibility of LRR. The connection made between coherence and clustering structures is insightful, and the theoretical result demonstrating coherence-independence with a low-rank dictionary is a significant contribution. The proposed algorithm, utilizing RPCA as a starting point for dictionary learning, is a practical approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses a relevant problem:** The paper tackles a known limitation of RPCA, expanding its applicability to more realistic datasets.
*   **Theoretical grounding:** The proof demonstrating coherence-independence with a low-rank dictionary provides a solid theoretical basis for the proposed approach.
*   **Practical algorithm:** The proposed algorithm is relatively straightforward and leverages existing techniques (RPCA) for dictionary learning.
*   **Empirical validation:** Experiments on both synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to RPCA.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity of notation:** The paper uses a large number of notations, some of which are not consistently defined or explained, making it difficult to follow the theoretical derivations.
*   **Limited novelty of LRR usage:** While the application of LRR with a specific dictionary constraint is novel, the core LRR framework itself is well-established. The paper doesn't fully explore the broader landscape of dictionary learning techniques.
*   **Experimental scope:** The experiments, while demonstrating improvement, are somewhat limited in scope. A comparison with more recent and advanced low-rank recovery methods would strengthen the claims.
*   **Discussion of limitations:** The paper lacks a thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach, such as the sensitivity to the initial RPCA solution or the computational cost of dictionary learning for very large datasets.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a theoretically sound and practically viable solution to a known limitation of RPCA.
*   The theoretical results are novel and contribute to a better understanding of the relationship between coherence, clustering, and low-rank recovery.
*   The experimental results provide evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The paper's clarity could be improved through more careful notation and explanation.
*   The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.
*   The novelty of the LRR application is incremental.

**Overall:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of low-rank matrix recovery. While there are areas for improvement, the theoretical insights and practical algorithm presented are valuable. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the experimental evaluation.
