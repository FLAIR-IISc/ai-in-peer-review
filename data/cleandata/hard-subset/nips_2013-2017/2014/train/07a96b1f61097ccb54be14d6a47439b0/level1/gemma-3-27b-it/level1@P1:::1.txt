This paper addresses the problem of low-rank matrix recovery in the presence of corruptions, specifically focusing on the impact of data coherence on the performance of Robust PCA (RPCA). The authors demonstrate that high coherence, often arising from clustering structures in the data, degrades RPCA's performance. They propose leveraging Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to mitigate this issue, theoretically proving that a low-rank dictionary renders LRR immune to coherence. They also present an unsupervised algorithm to learn such a dictionary based on RPCA’s output and validate their approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world motion sequence data.

The paper builds upon the well-established work on RPCA [Candès et al., 2011] and LRR [Liu et al., 2013]. It identifies a limitation of RPCA – its sensitivity to data coherence – which is a crucial observation. The theoretical contribution, linking low-rank dictionaries in LRR to coherence immunity, is a significant step towards understanding the conditions under which LRR outperforms RPCA. The proposed algorithm provides a practical method for exploiting this theoretical finding.  The connection drawn to matrix factorization [Vidal et al., 2008] provides useful context.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of coherent data affecting RPCA’s performance and provides a compelling motivation for addressing it.
* **Theoretical Contribution:** The proof establishing the relationship between low-rank dictionaries and coherence immunity is a valuable contribution to the field. Theorem 1 offers a tangible criterion for dictionary design.
* **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm is relatively straightforward and leverages existing techniques (RPCA and LRR), making it potentially easy to implement and apply.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing consistent improvements over RPCA.  The inclusion of motion sequence data with a standard benchmark (Hopkins155) strengthens the evaluation.
* **Good Related Work:** The paper references relevant work in robust PCA, low-rank matrix recovery, and subspace clustering.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions in Theorems:** Theorem 1 relies on the condition  `PUA(U0) = U0`, which, while necessary, might be restrictive in practice.  The paper acknowledges this, but doesn’t thoroughly discuss the implications or provide alternatives if this condition is not met.
* **Limited Discussion of Dictionary Learning:** While the algorithm proposes a method to learn the dictionary, the discussion is somewhat brief. It would be beneficial to explore more sophisticated dictionary learning techniques and analyze their impact on performance.
* **λ Selection:** The choice of λ = 1/√n1 is adopted directly from RPCA. While this might work well, a more careful justification or investigation of optimal λ selection for LRR with low-rank dictionaries could be valuable.
* **Clarity of µ2-Phenomenon:** The explanation of the µ2-phenomenon and its connection to clustering structures could be more intuitive and visually clear. While Figure 2 illustrates the phenomenon, a more detailed explanation of *why* µ2 increases with the number of clusters would enhance understanding.
* **Comparison to other LRR variants:** The comparison is primarily against RPCA. It would be beneficial to compare the proposed LRR approach to other LRR variants with different dictionary learning strategies.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a relevant and important problem in robust PCA – the impact of data coherence.
* The theoretical results provide a solid foundation for understanding the conditions under which LRR outperforms RPCA.
* The proposed algorithm is practical and demonstrably effective in experiments.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making the technical contributions accessible.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The restrictive assumption in Theorem 1 could limit the applicability of the proposed approach.
* The discussion of dictionary learning and parameter selection could be more in-depth.
* The comparison to other relevant LRR variants is missing.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field of robust PCA. The theoretical insights and practical algorithm offer a valuable advancement in handling coherent data. While some aspects could be further refined, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Discuss the limitations of the `PUA(U0) = U0` condition and explore potential workarounds or alternative dictionary learning strategies.
2.  Expand the discussion of dictionary learning techniques and their impact on performance.
3.  Consider comparing the proposed LRR approach to other LRR variants with different dictionary learning methods.
4.  Provide a more intuitive explanation of the µ2-phenomenon.
