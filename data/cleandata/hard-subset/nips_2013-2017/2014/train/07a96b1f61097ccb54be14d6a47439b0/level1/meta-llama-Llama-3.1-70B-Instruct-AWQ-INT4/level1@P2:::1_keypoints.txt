- Proposes LRR: a novel Low-Rank Representation method that learns a dictionary to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, coherent data.  
- Identifies RPCA limitation: RPCA can fail on strictly low-rank data with clustering/coherence structures.  
- Main contributions: formulation for recovering low-rank coherent matrices, analysis of regimes causing coherence, and a practical algorithm that outperforms RPCA.  
- Builds on prior work: extends RPCA and relates to LRR [13], connecting to matrix factorization ideas.  
- Quality: Technically sound with theoretical results and experiments supporting the claims.  
- Clarity: Well-written, logically organized, consistent notation, with a helpful notation summary.  
- Originality: The use of a learned dictionary to handle coherence is novel and contributes to RPCA research.  
- Significance: Addresses a relevant problem for real-world data and offers insights into the meaning and causes of coherence.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel approach, rigorous proofs and algorithms, convincing experimental validation, and tackles an important problem.  
- Cons against acceptance: May be seen as an incremental RPCA extension; needs more experiments for robustness; some proofs/algorithms are technically dense.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
