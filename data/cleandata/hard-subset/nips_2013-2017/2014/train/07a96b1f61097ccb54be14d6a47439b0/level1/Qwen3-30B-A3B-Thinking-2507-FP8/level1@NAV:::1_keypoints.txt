- Addresses RPCA's failure on data with clustering-induced high coherence and shows performance degrades as cluster count increases (μ2 grows).  
- Proposes using LRR with a properly configured low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's coherence sensitivity.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves that if the dictionary is low-rank and spans the true low-rank column space, LRR avoids dependence on coherence parameters (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Develops an unsupervised algorithm that constructs such a dictionary by leveraging RPCA's output.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic matrices and motion sequences demonstrates clear improvements over RPCA.  
- Strong quality: sound theoretical results and well-executed experiments.  
- Clear exposition: well-organized connection between problem, theory, and algorithm.  
- Originality: identifies and addresses a limitation of RPCA not fully treated in prior work, using a novel LRR approach.  
- Significance: addresses an important real-world issue where clustering is common and shows practical gains.  
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis to parameter choices, notably rank estimation in Step 2.  
- Weakness: could better quantify improvements over RPCA in Figure 4 (e.g., exact success-rate differences).  
- Weakness: should discuss potential failure cases when RPCA's initial estimate used for dictionary construction is poor.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, meaningful contribution; recommend acceptance.
