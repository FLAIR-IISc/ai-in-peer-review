This paper proposes a novel approach to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations, addressing the limitations of the existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) method. The authors argue that RPCA ignores clustering structures in the data, leading to degraded performance when dealing with coherent data. They introduce a new method, Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit (LRDP), which uses a low-rank dictionary to represent the data, and demonstrate its effectiveness in recovering coherent data.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, as well as a thorough review of the existing literature. The proposed method is well-motivated, and the theoretical analysis is sound. The experimental results are also impressive, demonstrating the superiority of LRDP over RPCA in various scenarios.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a significant limitation of RPCA and proposes a novel approach to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a clear understanding of the conditions under which LRDP can recover coherent data.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the dictionary is low-rank, which might not always be the case in practice. It would be interesting to explore the performance of LRDP when the dictionary is not low-rank.
