- Addresses low-rank matrix recovery under corruptions and shows data coherence (e.g., clustering) degrades RPCA performance.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence effects.  
- Theoretical result: a low-rank dictionary makes LRR immune to coherence (Theorem 1).  
- Theorem 1 depends on the restrictive condition PUA(U0) = U0, which may limit practical applicability.  
- Presents an unsupervised algorithm to learn a low-rank dictionary from RPCA output.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences shows consistent improvement over RPCA.  
- Builds on prior work in RPCA and LRR and connects to matrix factorization/subspace clustering literature.  
- Strength: clear problem statement and motivation about coherence impacting RPCA.  
- Strength: significant theoretical contribution linking low-rank dictionaries to coherence immunity.  
- Strength: practical, easy-to-implement algorithm leveraging existing methods.  
- Strength: solid experimental validation including a standard benchmark.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of dictionary-learning alternatives and their potential impact.  
- Weakness: direct adoption of λ = 1/√n1 from RPCA lacks deeper justification for LRR.  
- Weakness: explanation of the µ2-phenomenon and why µ2 grows with cluster number is not intuitive enough.  
- Weakness: comparisons are primarily to RPCA; missing comparisons to other LRR variants/dictionary strategies.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss the PUA(U0)=U0 limitation, expand dictionary-learning/λ discussion, compare to other LRR variants, and clarify the µ2-phenomenon.
