- Paper studies robust low-rank recovery when the low-rank component is clustered, causing high coherence and RPCA failure.
- Identifies a “µ2-phenomenon”: µ2 (and µ3) grow with number of clusters, tightening RPCA rank bounds and degrading recovery.
- Revisits RPCA formulation and explains dependence of success on coherence parameters µ1, µ2, µ3.
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a pre-specified low-rank dictionary A to avoid µ2 dependence.
- Main theoretical claim: if A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA, exact/noisy recovery bounds depend on µ1(A) and rank(A) but not µ2(L0).
- Practical unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA, truncate by estimated rank, column-normalize to form A, then solve LRR with that A.
- Empirical results show improved recovery and subspace-clustering performance on synthetic union-of-subspaces data and motion sequences.
- Novelty: theoretical analysis of LRR with general low-rank dictionaries that mitigates clustering-induced coherence issues.
- Technical soundness: theorems for noiseless/noisy cases are clearly stated; proofs use standard dual-certificate techniques (deferred to full paper).
- Containment condition U0 ⊂ UA is necessary for exact recovery, and the learning algorithm lacks guarantees to enforce it when RPCA is poor.
- Clarity: well-organized exposition of coherence parameters and algorithm; would benefit from proof sketches in the main text.
- Originality: extends LRR literature beyond A = X and formally targets µ2-driven RPCA failures.
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem—clustered subspace data—and offers theory and a simple practical fix.
- Strength: formal identification and treatment of the µ2 issue with theoretical guarantees and a simple empirical method.
- Weakness: strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA and no formal guarantee that the RPCA-based dictionary learning achieves it under heavy corruption.
- Weakness: limited experiments (single synthetic setup, 11 sequences) and missing ablations on clusters vs. µ2, threshold sensitivity, and dense noise robustness.
- Weakness: comparisons could be expanded (LRR with A = X, reweighted PCP, alternative dictionary learning methods).
- Suggestions: include proof sketches, vary number of clusters and measure µ2 empirically, add ablations on rank-threshold and iterative refinement, compare more baselines, and handle missing-data models explicitly.
