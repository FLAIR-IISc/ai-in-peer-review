- Problem: RPCA fails to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted data when data are highly coherent due to clustering.
- Proposed solution: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA's limitations.
- Theory: Authors prove LRR with a low-rank dictionary is immune to the coherence parameter that degrades RPCA as cluster count increases.
- Practical method: A practical unsupervised algorithm is proposed that leverages RPCA to construct the LRR dictionary.
- Experiments: Synthetic data and real-world motion sequence experiments show the method significantly outperforms RPCA on coherent data.
- Strength — Novelty: Tackles an important RPCA limitation by integrating dictionary learning into LRR, a well-motivated and novel idea.
- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous mathematical proofs and conditions under which coherence is avoided.
- Strength — Practicality: Algorithm builds on existing RPCA/LRR methods, making it accessible to practitioners.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments convincingly support the method’s superiority.
- Strength — Motivation clarity: Paper clearly explains RPCA’s limitations and how the proposed method addresses them.
- Weakness — Presentation: Some parts, especially the algorithm description, lack clarity and could use visual aids.
- Weakness — Alternatives: Limited exploration of alternative approaches (e.g., RPCA modifications or hybrid methods).
- Weakness — Scalability: Insufficient analysis of computational scalability on large datasets despite claims of marginal overhead.
- Weakness — Generality: Assumes dictionary can be constructed effectively via RPCA, which may not hold in very noisy or complex scenarios.
- Acceptance arguments: Significant, theoretically sound, and empirically validated contribution likely to inspire further research.
- Rejection concerns: Presentation clarity, limited alternative exploration, and scalability/generalizability questions remain.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address scalability concerns.
