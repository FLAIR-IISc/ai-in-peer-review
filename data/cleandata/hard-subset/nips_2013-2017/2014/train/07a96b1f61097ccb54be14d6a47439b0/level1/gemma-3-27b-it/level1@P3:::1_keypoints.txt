- Paper tackles low-rank matrix recovery with corruptions and identifies RPCA's failure when the low-rank component is highly coherent due to clustering.
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary to overcome coherence-related failures of RPCA.
- Proves a theorem that a low-rank dictionary makes LRR immune to the coherence parameter associated with clustering.
- Presents an algorithm that learns an appropriate low-rank dictionary from data, initialized via RPCA.
- Experiments on synthetic and real motion-sequence data show performance gains over RPCA using standard clustering metrics.
- Clear problem statement highlighting RPCA’s sensitivity to data coherence from clustering.
- Theoretical contribution linking dictionary rank to avoidance of coherence is insightful and valuable.
- Practical algorithm that builds on RPCA for dictionary initialization is sensible and implementable.
- Experimental validation is convincing with both synthetic and real-world datasets and appropriate evaluation metrics.
- Thorough and relevant related work coverage demonstrating awareness of the literature.
- Paper is generally well-written and logically organized.
- Full proof of Theorem 1 is only on arXiv; the conference paper lacks a detailed proof sketch.
- Dictionary learning algorithm is heuristic with limited theoretical justification and unclear robustness if RPCA initialization fails.
- Fixed λ = 1/√n1 is used without sensitivity analysis or parameter tuning experiments.
- Comparisons are mainly to RPCA; broader comparison to other robust matrix completion/dictionary learning methods is missing.
- The µ2 increase with number of clusters is only briefly explained and needs a clearer intuitive discussion.
- Computational complexity, especially of the dictionary learning step, is not analyzed in detail.
- For acceptance: addresses a significant RPCA limitation, provides a novel theoretical insight, offers a practical algorithm, and is well-motivated and referenced.
- Against acceptance: lacks full in-paper proof, uses a heuristic dictionary learning method without deep analysis, and offers limited experimental comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable theoretical and practical contributions; weaknesses are addressable.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on providing a proof sketch, λ sensitivity discussion, broader experimental comparisons, and clearer explanation of the µ2 phenomenon.
