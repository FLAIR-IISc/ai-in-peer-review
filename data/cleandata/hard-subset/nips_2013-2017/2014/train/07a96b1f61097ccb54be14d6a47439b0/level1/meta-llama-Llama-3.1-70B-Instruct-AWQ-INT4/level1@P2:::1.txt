**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit" proposes a novel approach to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations. The authors argue that the existing Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) method is not perfect even when the data is strictly low-rank, especially when the data exhibits clustering structures. They introduce a new method called Low-Rank Representation (LRR) that can handle coherent data by learning a suitable dictionary. The main contributions of the paper include the study of the problem of recovering low-rank and coherent matrices, the investigation of the physical regime that raises coherent data, and the proposal of a practical algorithm that outperforms RPCA in experiments.
