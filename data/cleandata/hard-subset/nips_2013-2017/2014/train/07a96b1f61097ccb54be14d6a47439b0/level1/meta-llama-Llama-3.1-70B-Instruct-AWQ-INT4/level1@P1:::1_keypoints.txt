- Proposes LRDP, a new method to recover low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted, coherent data.  
- Argues RPCA degrades on coherent/clustering-structured data.  
- LRDP models data using a low-rank dictionary.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and specifies conditions under which LRDP succeeds.  
- Experiments show LRDP outperforms RPCA in multiple scenarios.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and well-organized.  
- Strength: addresses a significant limitation of RPCA with a novel approach.  
- Strength: strong theoretical grounding supporting the method.  
- Strength: impressive empirical validation.  
- Weakness: assumes the dictionary is low-rank, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion/comparison with related methods (e.g., matrix factorization).  
- Originality: introducing a low-rank dictionary for recovery is a novel contribution.  
- Significance: method has potential applications in image/video processing, vision, and data analysis and may spur new research.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on addressing weaknesses—add complexity analysis, more comparisons/experiments, and discussion of applications/limitations.
