- RPCA fails on “coherent” data (µ2 increases with number of clusters), degrading recovery guarantees.  
- LRR model X = AZ + S with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains L0 can recover L0 independent of L0’s coherence.  
- Theoretical contributions (Theorems 1–2) rigorously show LRR with a proper A is immune to µ2/µ3 issues.  
- Practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA for an initial L̂0, build a low-rank dictionary Â from it, then run LRR to refine recovery.  
- Experiments (synthetic phase transitions and motion segmentation) show the proposed method outperforms RPCA on coherent data.  

Strengths:
- Addresses a significant, practical limitation of RPCA and explains the “µ2-phenomenon” clearly.  
- Novel idea to trade coherence assumptions on data for controllable assumptions on the dictionary A.  
- Strong technical analysis with sound proofs supporting the main claims.  
- Well-written, logically organized, and moves cleanly from theory to a practical algorithm.  
- Convincing empirical validation demonstrating practical benefit.

Weaknesses:
- Gap between theory and practice: no guarantee that dictionary Â from RPCA contains true subspace U0, especially when RPCA initially fails.  
- Limited discussion of algorithmic choices and parameter sensitivity (e.g., λ = 1/√n1) and lack of detail on iterative refinement behavior.  
- Focused on column-wise coherence (µ2); row-wise coherence (µ1) and other coherence types are left for future work.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Identifies and explains a fundamental RPCA limitation.  
- Presents a principled, novel LRR-based solution.  
- Supported by solid theoretical results.  
- Simple, intuitive algorithm with strong empirical performance.  
- Well-written and broadly relevant.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Theoretical-practical gap in dictionary construction undermines formal guarantees.  
- Algorithmic analysis (parameter sensitivity, iteration) is somewhat brief.

Conclusion:
- Overall an excellent, well-executed paper with minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
