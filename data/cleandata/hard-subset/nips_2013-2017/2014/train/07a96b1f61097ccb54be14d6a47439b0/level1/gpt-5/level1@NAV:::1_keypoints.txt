- Problem: Studies low-rank matrix recovery with coherent/clustered data, showing RPCA/PCP can fail as cluster count (and µ2) grows.  
- Main theoretical claim: LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the ground-truth L0 (U0 ⊂ UA) can recover L0 without dependence on µ2.  
- Recovery guarantees: Noiseless and noisy theorems depend only on µ1(A), with rank(L0) ≤ O(n2 / log n1) and support |Ω| up to ~50% (noiseless) or ~35% (noisy).  
- Dictionary condition: A must be low-rank and satisfy U0 ⊂ UA — a key but strong assumption.  
- Algorithm: Proposes an unsupervised two-stage method — run RPCA, build a low-rank column-normalized dictionary from the estimate, then solve LRR.  
- Experiments: Synthetic and corrupted motion-sequence tests (Hopkins155 extensions) show clear gains over RPCA and improved downstream clustering (SIM, LRRx, SSC).  
- Relation to literature: Connects to NIPS work on robust subspace methods and matrix factorization (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, Srebro & Jaakkola).  
- Quality: Theoretical contributions are technically sound and carefully stated; proofs are standard but nontrivial adaptations for general dictionaries.  
- Limitation in theory: No guarantees are provided for the dictionary learned via the RPCA initialization.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized with clear coherence definitions and a helpful “µ2-phenomenon” narrative.  
- Clarity issues: Some assumptions (notably U0 ⊂ UA) need more context; sensitivity to rank estimation and normalization should be discussed.  
- Originality: Novel analysis of LRR with low-rank dictionaries to avoid µ2, providing fresh insight into coherence and cluster structure.  
- Significance: Addresses a practically important problem (motion segmentation, vision, recommendation) with promising theoretical and empirical impact.  
- Pros: Clear theoretical advance (µ2-agnostic recovery), simple practical algorithm, and empirical improvements.  
- Cons: Strong key assumption (U0 ⊂ UA) without guarantee from RPCA initialization, limited baselines (missing Outlier Pursuit/robust factorization), and no ablation on parameter sensitivity or row-clustering (large µ1) failure modes.
