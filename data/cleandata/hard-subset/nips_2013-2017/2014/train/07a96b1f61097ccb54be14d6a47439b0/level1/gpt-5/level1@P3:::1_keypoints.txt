- Paper addresses RPCA failure when L0 has clustering-induced high coherence and shows LRR with a low-rank dictionary A can avoid dependence on µ2.  
- Main theoretical result: if U0 ⊂ UA and A is low-rank and column-normalized, solving minZ,S ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S recovers L0,S0 without µ2 appearing in conditions.  
- Provides both noiseless and noisy recovery guarantees; conditions depend on rank(A), µ1(A), and sparsity, not µ2.  
- Proposes a simple two-stage unsupervised algorithm: RPCA → estimate low-rank L̃0 → normalize columns to form A → run LRR with A.  
- Synthetic and motion segmentation experiments show larger success regions than RPCA and improved accuracy on corrupted sequences.  
- Relates to RPCA, LRR, SSC, SIM and complements outlier-pursuit and other robust PCA variants targeting different corruption models.  
- Strength: clearly identifies and explains the µ2-phenomenon and how clustering increases coherence parameters.  
- Strength: novel proof that LRR with an appropriate low-rank dictionary avoids µ2, with a rank bound comparable to RPCA’s.  
- Strength: practical, easy-to-implement algorithm with empirical gains over RPCA.  
- Strength: conceptual insight that coherence reflects data structure and can be mitigated by modeling with a low-rank dictionary.  
- Weakness: key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong; unclear when the RPCA-learned A will satisfy this in the regimes where RPCA fails.  
- Weakness: analysis shifts dependence to µ1(A); lacks theory or guidance on when µ1(A) will be small when forming A from RPCA outputs.  
- Experimental limitations: needs comparisons to LRR with A = X, subspace-projection baselines, sensitivity analyses for λ/ε/rank threshold, and failure-mode studies when RPCA is poor.  
- Experimental limitations: motion data handling is nonstandard (replacing missing entries with ±1); should use standard inpainting/masking or larger Hopkins155 evaluation.  
- Presentation issues: some key derivations and intuitions (e.g., µ2 growth with clusters) are deferred to the full paper; notation and minor wording need cleanup.  
- Complexity/reproducibility: clarify solver details, runtimes, and algorithms used for LRR (e.g., ADMM, SVD implementations).  
- Quality: theoretical contributions are sound but rely on strong dictionary assumptions; noisy bounds are meaningful.  
- Originality: novel connection showing LRR with designed low-rank dictionaries avoids µ2; dictionary-design perspective and guarantees beyond A = X are new.  
- Significance: addresses a real RPCA limitation and proposes a practical remedy that could impact robust recovery and subspace clustering pipelines.  
- Questions for authors: can recovery be bounded when Û0 is only approximately equal to U0 (principal angles)?  
- Questions for authors: how does µ1(A) behave under the clustering generative model—does it remain small when µ2(L0) is large?  
- Questions for authors: compare to baseline constraining L to span(Û0) to isolate benefits of AZ parameterization.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear theoretical insight, practical algorithm, and demonstrated empirical improvements.  
- Cons for acceptance: strong dictionary assumptions, limited analysis of unsupervised pipeline robustness, and narrower experimental validation.
