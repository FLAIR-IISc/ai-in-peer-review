- Paper identifies a "µ2-phenomenon": clustering within a fixed-rank subspace increases µ2 and breaks RPCA guarantees.  
- Proposes analyzing Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a chosen dictionary A to avoid dependence on µ2.  
- Main theorem: if A is low-rank and its column space contains L0's column space (U0 ⊂ UA), LRR yields exact/near-exact recovery w.h.p. without µ2 assumptions.  
- The U0 ⊂ UA condition is strong and effectively oracle-like, acknowledged as guidance for dictionary design.  
- Authors propose a simple unsupervised pipeline: RPCA → form low-rank, column-normalized dictionary Â → solve LRR for final recovery.  
- Theory assumptions and bounds are clearly stated; rank bound slightly looser than RPCA but avoids µ2.  
- The paper lacks quantitative robustness analysis to approximate span containment (e.g., principal angle perturbation bounds).  
- Practical algorithm details (rank thresholding, normalization) are given but sensitivity to thresholds and λ is not analyzed.  
- Synthetic experiments show a larger success region than RPCA (claimed 47% area increase) but need ablations on dictionary purity and cluster count.  
- Motion segmentation experiments show improved clustering after preprocessing, but scope is limited (11 sequences, one corruption model).  
- Missing comparisons to other robust PCA variants and omitted runtime/sensitivity studies weaken empirical claims.  
- Exposition on coherence and the µ2-clustering link is clear and insightful, though notation is heavy and minor typos remain.  
- Including intuition for why a low-rank dictionary spanning U0 removes µ2 dependence would improve clarity.  
- Contribution is novel in analyzing LRR with general low-rank dictionaries and linking coherence to clustering.  
- The RPCA-seeded dictionary heuristic is pragmatic and aligns with the theoretical guidance.  
- Significance: diagnosis and prescription for µ2 avoidance are valuable and potentially impactful if assumptions are relaxed.  
- Major cons: strong dictionary assumption, limited experiments/ablations, reliance on RPCA where RPCA may fail, and some incomplete references.  
- Suggested improvements: provide perturbation guarantees for approximate containment, run ablations (imperfect A, iterative refinement, λ/rank sensitivity), include runtimes, and vary cluster number to test µ2 mitigation.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, novel, and practically useful work; recommend acceptance conditional on strengthening empirical validation and relaxing/quantifying the dictionary assumption.
