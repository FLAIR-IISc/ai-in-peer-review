Summary
The paper studies recovery of a low-rank matrix L0 and sparse corruption S0 from X = L0 + S0 in regimes where L0 is coherent, particularly when data lie in multiple clusters (union-of-subspaces) within the same low-dimensional subspace. The authors argue that standard RPCA/PCP fails as coherence parameters (especially µ2) increase with the number of clusters (“µ2-phenomenon”). They propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) minZ,S ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains that of L0 (U0 ⊂ UA). They prove noiseless and noisy recovery theorems that eliminate dependence on µ2 (and thus on the number of clusters) and give a rank bound rank(L0) ≤ O(n2/log n1) with probability guarantees, depending only on µ1(A). They then present a simple unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA to estimate L0, truncate its SVD to get a low-rank approximation, normalize columns to form A, and run LRR with A to produce the final estimate. Experiments on synthetic clustered data and corrupted motion sequences show improved recovery and downstream clustering performance compared to RPCA.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on RPCA/PCP (Candès et al.) and LRR (Liu et al.). Prior LRR papers focused on subspace segmentation (often with A = X) and outlier handling, but the present paper studies recovery with general dictionaries and coherence explicitly. The identification of the µ2-phenomenon with clustered sampling is consistent with the incoherence conditions in matrix completion/RPCA, but the prescription to avoid µ2 by choosing a low-rank dictionary that contains U0 seems new. The empirical section relates to motion segmentation pipelines using SIM/LRRx/SSC.

Strengths
- Clear articulation of a practical failure mode for RPCA under clustered sampling; helpful interpretation of coherence parameters, especially µ2.
- Theoretical contribution: recovery guarantees for LRR with general low-rank dictionary A that remove dependence on µ2 and permit a looser rank bound O(n2/log n1), under U0 ⊂ UA and column normalization.
- Simple, pragmatic algorithm that leverages RPCA to build a dictionary and improves performance on both synthetic and real data.
- The connection between dictionary rank/column space and robustness to coherence is insightful and could guide practice in robust subspace learning.

Weaknesses and limitations
- Assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong and requires supervision or a good estimate of U0; the unsupervised algorithm uses RPCA to obtain A, but there is no theory guaranteeing that RPCA yields UA containing U0 when coherence is high (the regime where RPCA struggles). This circularity may limit reliability in the hardest cases.
- The theoretical results depend on µ1(A); guidance on how µ1(A) behaves for the proposed construction (from RPCA output) is missing, and no bounds relate µ1(A) to properties of L0 and the RPCA error.
- Algorithmic choices (rank threshold at 10^-3 σ1, column normalization, λ = 1/√n1, ε setting) lack sensitivity analyses; robustness to these choices is unclear.
- Experimental scope is limited: only entrywise sparse corruptions are considered. No evaluation under column-sparse outliers, mixed noise models, or heavy-tailed noise, and no comparison to Outlier Pursuit, Trimmed PCP, GoDec, or more recent robust PCA variants that address coherence.
- The noisy recovery bound (||AZ* − L0||F ≤ 8√mn ε) is quite loose and scales with √mn; no bounds on Z* or on support recovery are provided.
- Some important details (figures referenced, full proofs) are omitted; clarity could be improved in places.

Quality
Technically sound at a high level; theorems are plausible and consistent with PCP analysis, though proofs are deferred. The proposed algorithm is reasonable and empirically helpful. However, theoretical guarantees do not cover the unsupervised construction of A, which is the main practical contribution, and experimental comparisons could be broader and more rigorous (runtime, sensitivity, ablations).

Clarity
Overall well organized with useful notation and background on coherence. The narrative on the µ2-phenomenon is clear. The manuscript would benefit from:
- Including (or summarizing) key proof ideas to justify why µ2 can be removed under U0 ⊂ UA.
- Adding algorithmic details (parameter choices, stopping criteria, solver specs) and runtime plots.
- Cleaning minor grammatical issues and ensuring all referenced figures are present.

Originality
Moderate. Using LRR with a learned dictionary is known, but the specific insight that a low-rank dictionary spanning U0 removes µ2 from the guarantees, plus corresponding theorems, is a novel and valuable perspective. The RPCA→LRR pipeline is simple but pragmatic.

Significance
Potentially significant for robust subspace learning with clustered data. The idea of dictionary design to mitigate coherence is broadly applicable and may influence practice. The empirical improvements on motion segmentation are encouraging, though more comprehensive evaluations are needed to establish broad impact.

Questions for the authors
- Can you characterize conditions under which the RPCA-derived dictionary satisfies U0 ⊂ UA with high probability? Any empirical measure of principal angle between U0 and UA across trials?
- How sensitive is performance to the rank threshold and column normalization? What happens if A is built from randomized SVD or with different truncation rules?
- Can you compare against Outlier Pursuit (column-sparse), Trimmed PCP, and robust matrix factorization baselines under clustered sampling?
- Do you have ablation results isolating the contributions of steps 2–4 in Algorithm 1?
- Can you report wall-clock times and memory usage for RPCA vs. LRR and the combined pipeline?

Pros
- Important problem; clear articulation of coherence-induced failures.
- Theoretical results for general dictionaries avoiding µ2.
- Simple, practical method with promising gains on synthetic and real data.

Cons
- Strong assumptions (U0 ⊂ UA) and lack of guarantees for the unsupervised construction.
- Limited experimental breadth and analysis; missing comparisons and sensitivity.
- Loose noisy bounds; limited discussion of failure modes.

Recommendation: Weak accept
The conceptual and theoretical contributions are interesting and likely to stimulate follow-up work; the practical pipeline shows benefits. Strengthening experiments and clarifying analysis would further solidify the paper.

Confidence: Medium-high
