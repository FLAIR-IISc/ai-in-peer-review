- Paper studies recovery of low-rank matrices with sparse gross errors when the low-rank part is coherent, a regime where PCP can fail.
- Empirically and conceptually links clustering in columns (union of subspaces) to increases in the second coherence parameter µ2 (“µ2-phenomenon”).
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary A: solve min_Z,S ||Z||_* + λ||S||_1 s.t. X = AZ + S.
- Theoretically shows exact/noisy recovery when A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA, with bounds depending on µ1(A) but not µ2(L0).
- Presents a practical unsupervised pipeline: run RPCA, truncate SVD to form a low-rank approximation, normalize columns to build A, then run LRR.
- Builds on PCP/RPCA and LRR literature, extending analysis to general dictionaries rather than A = X.
- Strength: clear conceptual insight and empirical illustration of why PCP fails under clustering-induced coherence.
- Strength: provides noiseless and noisy recovery guarantees for LRR that remove dependence on µ2 under stated conditions.
- Strength: proposes a simple, implementable algorithm with modest extra runtime (~1.2× PCP).
- Strength: empirical results show a larger synthetic success region and reduced clustering error on corrupted motion sequences.
- Weakness: main theorems assume U0 ⊂ UA, but no theory is provided for the realistic unsupervised two-stage pipeline when PCP is imperfect.
- Weakness: sparsity model for S0 and independence assumptions are not precisely specified in the manuscript.
- Weakness: guarantees depend on µ1(A) but there is no analysis or practical guidance to ensure or reduce µ1(A) when A is learned.
- Weakness: experiments are limited—lack quantitative recovery metrics, statistical variation, more baselines, and sensitivity/ablation studies.
- Weakness: potential circularity—algorithm relies on PCP to build A even though PCP is claimed to fail in high-µ2 regimes.
- Weakness: some speculative claims and missing experimental reproducibility details (parameter choices, data generation).
- Quality: technical claims plausible and build on established techniques; main novelty is µ2-free recovery via low-rank dictionaries.
- Clarity: generally well organized, but some definitions/assumptions and experimental details should be made explicit; minor grammar issues.
- Originality: formalizing the µ2-phenomenon and proving µ2-free LRR recovery is a novel and useful angle; pipeline is incremental but practical.
- Significance: approach could materially improve robust recovery in coherent settings if generalized and empirically validated more broadly.
- Key questions: clarify S0 model and independence, provide perturbation analysis for U0 ⊄ UA, sensitivity to rank threshold, methods to reduce µ1(A), and behavior when rows are clustered.
- Pros for acceptance: theoretical insight, practical algorithm, bridges theory and practice with encouraging initial results.
- Cons for acceptance: strong/unrealistic assumptions, missing theory for the unsupervised pipeline, limited experiments and baselines.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—novel and promising but needs clearer assumptions and expanded experiments.
