- Summary: RPCA fails on coherent, clustered data (the "µ2-phenomenon"); using LRR with a low-rank dictionary that contains L0's column space can avoid coherence parameters and exactly recover data; an unsupervised algorithm builds such a dictionary from RPCA and shows strong empirical gains.

- Strength — Quality: Theorems 1–2 provide sound proofs that LRR with an appropriate low-rank dictionary avoids coherence issues; experiments validate large empirical improvements.

- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-organized, notationally consistent, and explains coherence and the µ2-phenomenon with intuitive examples enabling reproducibility.

- Strength — Originality: Identifies the µ2-phenomenon and provides new theoretical foundations explaining when and why LRR outperforms RPCA on coherent data.

- Strength — Significance: Addresses a practical, widely relevant problem (clustered/coherent data) and demonstrates substantial performance gains on synthetic and real motion-segmentation data.

- Weakness 1: The key theoretical requirement U0 ⊂ UA is strong, and the proposed heuristic dictionary construction does not guarantee this condition in unsupervised settings.

- Weakness 2: The paper lacks analysis of trade-offs between dictionary rank and recovery performance (how low the dictionary rank can be while preserving recovery).

- Weakness 3: Computational analysis is limited; more detailed complexity/scalability analysis of Algorithm 1 and the dictionary construction step is needed.

- Recommendation: Strong accept for NIPS — technically solid, experimentally convincing, and practically significant despite some practical challenges in dictionary construction.

- Minor suggestions: Discuss dictionary-rank vs. recovery trade-offs, provide a more detailed computational complexity analysis of Algorithm 1, and further explore links between dictionary rank and coherence conditions.
