- Identifies RPCA's failure mode: clustering in the low-rank component increases µ2 and degrades RPCA performance.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary and proves exact recovery without dependence on µ2 when U0 ⊂ UA.  
- Offers an unsupervised algorithm that builds the dictionary from RPCA and reports empirical improvements on synthetic and motion data.  

- Strength: Clearly pinpoints a practical limitation (µ2-phenomenon) of RPCA often ignored in theory.  
- Strength: Provides a solid theoretical result (Theorem 1) showing LRR can avoid µ2 dependence under conditions.  
- Strength: Presents a simple, scalable algorithm with claimed complexity O(n² r_A) vs RPCA's O(n³).  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation, including ~40% relative reduction in clustering error on real data.  
- Strength: Effectively links coherence theory to real-world clustering structures.  

- Weakness: Overstates novelty; related work on coherence and subspace clustering is not sufficiently contrasted.  
- Weakness: Theoretical guarantees still depend on µ1(A), so coherence is replaced, not eliminated.  
- Weakness: Relies on the strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA and does not analyze sensitivity to violations.  
- Weakness: Limited baseline comparisons—only compared to RPCA, omitting other robust PCA methods.  
- Weakness: Scalability claim depends on r_A, which may grow and negate the complexity advantage.  

- Technical concern: Figure 2 lacks a clear mathematical derivation explaining why µ2 increases with number of clusters.  
- Technical concern: Algorithm 1’s step forming a rank-r̂0 approximation may be redundant since RPCA already yields a low-rank estimate.  
- Technical concern: The paper’s statement about bounds is misleading—its rank bound is looser (i.e., theoretically weaker) than RPCA’s and should be clarified.  
- Technical concern: The motion-data corruption model (10% missing replaced by ±1 Bernoulli) is unusual and needs justification.  

- Overall assessment: Well-written, technically sound, meaningful contribution addressing a real RPCA limitation; minor revisions recommended.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify coherence claims, add baselines, analyze U0 ⊂ UA sensitivity, and justify algorithmic steps.  

- Pro-acceptance: Identifies an important failure mode, gives a theoretically justified remedy, shows clear empirical gains, and provides a practical algorithm.  
- Con-acceptance: Gains may not always justify added complexity, method still depends on a coherence parameter, sensitivity to assumptions is unclear, and comparisons are limited.
