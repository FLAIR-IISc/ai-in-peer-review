- Paper shows RPCA fails on coherent (clustered) data and proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A containing the true column space to avoid μ2-dependence, with proofs for noiseless/noisy cases and an unsupervised Algorithm 1.  
- Theorems 1–2 provide novel theoretical guarantees that avoid μ2 when U0 ⊂ UA, relaxing RPCA requirements.  
- Clarifies that coherence parameters (μ2) reflect real data structures like clustering, not just theoretical artifacts.  
- Strong empirical validation on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences, showing substantial improvements (≈40% relative reduction in clustering error).  
- Proposes a simple, effective, computationally efficient unsupervised algorithm (Algorithm 1) that is only ~1.2× slower than RPCA.  
- Clearly motivates the problem by linking ubiquitous clustering to increased coherence and RPCA degradation (the "μ2-phenomenon").  
- Lacks comparisons to more recent robust PCA variants (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, L1-based methods), weakening claims against state-of-the-art.  
- Provides empirical evidence that μ2 grows with cluster number but offers no rigorous mathematical explanation for this phenomenon.  
- Dictionary construction in Algorithm 1 is described as heuristic with no sensitivity analysis on Steps 2–3.  
- Claims of computational advantage lack detailed complexity analysis across problem sizes.  
- Recommendation: Accept — paper is a significant, well-supported contribution with minor weaknesses to address.  
- Final note: Well-suited for NIPS; suggested minor revisions include comparisons to recent methods and deeper analysis of μ2 and dictionary sensitivity.
