

# Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses a limitation of Robust PCA (RPCA) when dealing with coherent data (data with clustering structures that increase coherence parameters), proposing a Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit approach within the LRR framework. The authors claim that when the dictionary A is low-rank, LRR can avoid the coherence parameters that degrade RPCA's performance, and they provide theoretical guarantees and an algorithm for dictionary learning.

## Quality

The paper contains several critical technical errors that undermine the theoretical claims:

1. Theorem 1 states: "rank (L0) ≤ rank (A) ≤ ǫ²n₂/caµ₁(A) log n₁" - this appears to be a typo where "rank(A) ≤" should not be present in the middle of the inequality. This is a fundamental error in the theorem statement that invalidates the theoretical contribution.

2. The paper claims "LRR falls back to RPCA when A = I (identity matrix)" - this is redundant since the paper already states this relationship in the introduction. The phrasing "falls back to RPCA" is confusing and should be removed.

3. Figure 2(a) shows µ₁ increasing with cluster number, contradicting the text's claim that "µ₁ is invariant to the variation of the clustering number." This inconsistency needs resolution.

4. Theorem 2 uses a threshold of (0.35 - ε)mn for |Ω|, while Theorem 1 uses (0.5 - ε)mn without justification for the change in constant. This arbitrary change requires explanation.

5. The paper states "the rank of L₀ is fixed and the underlying cluster number goes large, the coherence of L₀ keeps heightening" but fails to provide a mathematical explanation for this relationship, which is central to the paper's premise.

6. The claim that "the coherence parameters are not 'assumptions' for a proof, but rather some excellent quantities that relate in nature to the extra structures" is philosophically stated but lacks supporting evidence.

## Clarity

Several notational inconsistencies and unclear explanations reduce clarity:

1. The paper uses both "L₀" and "L̃₀" for low-rank matrices without clear distinction, causing confusion.

2. The requirement for "column-wisely unit-normed dictionary" is stated as "purely for complying the parameter estimate of λ = 1/√n₁," but RPCA doesn't typically use this specific λ value, creating confusion.

3. Algorithm 1's Step 2 uses a heuristic rank estimation with "σᵢ > 10⁻³σ₁" without justification for the 10⁻³ threshold.

4. The paper claims LRR "is actually tolerant to the 'errors' possibly existing in the dictionary" but provides no evidence or analysis to support this.

5. The phrase "the row space, which is characterized by µ₂" is misleading; µ₂ characterizes the row space, but the paper doesn't clarify this relationship.

## Originality

The paper's claims of novelty require scrutiny:

1. The paper states it's "the first to study the problem of recovering low-rank, and coherent (or less incoherent as equal) matrices from their corrupted versions," but this appears overstated. The relationship between clustering structures and coherence has been discussed in other works (e.g., [22] on GPCA).

2. The paper references LRR [13] but doesn't clearly differentiate its approach from previous LRR work, particularly [14] which uses A = X.

3. The "physical meaning of coherence" claim is not sufficiently supported by the paper's analysis.

## Significance

The significance is limited by the paper's scope and unaddressed limitations:

1. The claim that "the success area of our algorithm is 47% wider than that of RPCA" is unsubstantiated without clear definition of "success area" or how it's calculated.

2. The experiments are limited to synthetic data and a single motion database (Hopkins155), lacking broader validation.

3. The paper doesn't address how to learn a low-rank dictionary in truly unsupervised settings where "U₀ ⊂ U_A" is unknown.

4. The computational time claim ("merely about 1.2 times as much as RPCA") lacks details about implementation specifics and hardware.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper addresses a genuine limitation of RPCA and proposes an interesting approach, the numerous technical errors, inconsistencies, and unclear claims significantly undermine its contribution. The typo in Theorem 1 is particularly problematic as it invalidates a central theoretical claim. The contradiction between Figure 2 and the text regarding µ₁'s behavior is also concerning.

The paper would benefit from:
- Correcting the critical typo in Theorem 1
- Resolving the contradiction between Figure 2 and the text
- Providing better justification for the dictionary learning approach
- Clarifying the relationship between clustering structures and coherence parameters
- Expanding the experimental validation

Given the paper's critical technical errors and the fact that it makes strong claims without sufficient supporting evidence, I recommend rejection. The paper has potential but requires substantial revision to address these fundamental issues before it can be considered for publication. The minor flaws identified here are substantial enough to outweigh the paper's potential contribution.
