## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper addresses an important and practical problem – the limitations of RPCA when dealing with data exhibiting clustering structures (high coherence). The authors convincingly argue that coherence isn’t simply a necessary assumption, but rather reflects underlying data structure. Their proposed approach, leveraging a low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework, offers a theoretically sound and empirically validated improvement over standard RPCA. While the presentation could be more concise, and the experimental evaluation, while solid, could benefit from broader datasets, I believe this paper represents a **strong contribution** to the field of robust PCA and is worthy of acceptance.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the well-established framework of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) – decomposing data into low-rank and sparse components. It identifies a key limitation of RPCA: its sensitivity to data coherence, particularly when stemming from clustering.  The authors correctly point out that high coherence, often arising from inherent data structure, hinders RPCA's performance.  They propose utilizing the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) model with a *low-rank dictionary* to mitigate this issue. This is a clever connection - by imposing a low-rank constraint on the dictionary itself, they aim to circumvent the coherence problems that plague RPCA. This work effectively bridges the gap between RPCA and dictionary learning, providing theoretical justification and practical guidance for dictionary design.  The paper appropriately references relevant work in RPCA, matrix completion, and dictionary learning.

**2. Strengths and Weaknesses:**

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of coherent data and its impact on RPCA performance. The “µ2-phenomenon” is well-explained and illustrated.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis demonstrating how a low-rank dictionary can alleviate coherence issues is a significant contribution. Theorem 1 provides a clear criterion for dictionary selection.
*   **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm, utilizing RPCA to initialize dictionary learning, is simple yet effective.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on both randomly generated data and motion sequences consistently demonstrate the improvement over RPCA. The motion sequence experiments, in particular, showcase the practical relevance of the approach.
*   **Insightful Discussion of Coherence:** The paper successfully reframes coherence not as a mere assumption but as a reflection of underlying data structures. This is a valuable conceptual contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Length & Redundancy:** The paper is somewhat verbose. Certain explanations are repeated, and the writing style could be more concise. The extensive notation section (Section 2) while comprehensive, feels slightly overwhelming.
*   **Experimental Scope:** While the experiments are well-executed, the datasets used are relatively limited. Expanding the evaluation to include more diverse and real-world datasets would strengthen the conclusions.  A comparison to other state-of-the-art robust PCA methods beyond just RPCA would also be beneficial.
*   **Algorithm Complexity:** The discussion of computational complexity is a bit hand-wavy. A more detailed analysis comparing the computational cost of the proposed algorithm with RPCA and other alternatives would be valuable.
*   **Minor Technical Details:** The statement about Theorem 1 offering a “looser” bound than RPCA (footnote on page 8) requires careful scrutiny. A more detailed comparison of the conditions would be helpful.



**3. Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

*   **Quality (8/10):**  The paper is technically sound, with rigorous theoretical analysis and well-supported experimental results. The proposed approach is a logical extension of existing work, and the theoretical results are convincing.
*   **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally clear, but suffers from verbosity and a sometimes dense writing style.  The notation section is comprehensive but could be streamlined.
*   **Originality (8/10):**  While building on established techniques (RPCA and LRR), the combination of these with a focus on coherence and low-rank dictionary learning is novel and insightful.  The theoretical analysis connecting coherence to data structure is a significant original contribution.
*   **Significance (8/10):**  The problem of robust PCA in the presence of coherent data is important and widely applicable. The proposed approach offers a practical solution with demonstrable improvements over existing methods. The insights into the meaning of coherence have broader implications for data analysis.

**4. Arguments for and against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Addresses a real and important limitation of RPCA.
*   Provides a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution.
*   Offers a new perspective on the role of coherence in data analysis.
*   The proposed algorithm is relatively simple and effective.

**Con:**

*   The paper is somewhat verbose and could benefit from more concise writing.
*   The experimental evaluation, while solid, could be expanded.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept**.  Despite minor weaknesses in presentation and scope of experimental validation, the paper’s technical soundness, originality, and significance make it a valuable contribution to the NIPS community. The authors have identified an important problem and proposed a well-reasoned and effective solution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the clarity and experimental scope concerns.
