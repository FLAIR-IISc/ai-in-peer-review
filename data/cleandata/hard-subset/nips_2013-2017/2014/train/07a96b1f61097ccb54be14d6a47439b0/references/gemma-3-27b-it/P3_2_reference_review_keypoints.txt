- Overall: Tackles an important practical limitation of RPCA and proposes LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary; recommendation: weak accept pending major revisions.
- Core idea: Use a low-rank dictionary A in LRR (min ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S) to avoid RPCA’s coherence limitations.
- Theoretical contribution: Proves conditions (Theorems 1–2) showing a low-rank dictionary can circumvent coherence-related failures.
- Algorithmic proposal: Practical method that initializes the dictionary using RPCA’s output to improve recovery.
- Relation to prior work: Cites RPCA and LRR but under-discusses connections to structured low-rank recovery and dictionary learning literature (e.g., K-SVD, online methods).
- Strength — problem identification: Correctly identifies coherence from clustering as a critical RPCA limitation.
- Strength — theory: Provides valuable theoretical justification and concrete guidance for recovery.
- Strength — practicality: Simple, sensible algorithm leveraging RPCA initialization that shows empirical improvements.
- Strength — experiments: Synthetic and real-world motion data show consistent improvement over RPCA.
- Strength — interpretation: Clarifies the link between coherence parameters and underlying data structure.
- Weakness — clarity: Presentation is dense with heavy notation and insufficient intuitive explanations (e.g., column-wise unit norm constraint justification).
- Weakness — novelty framing: Using a learned dictionary in LRR is not fully positioned against existing dictionary learning / structured low-rank methods.
- Weakness — dictionary learning detail: Lacks discussion on choosing dictionary rank, alternatives to RPCA initialization, and behavior under poor initialization.
- Weakness — noise model: Theorem 2 assumes dense Gaussian noise, which may be too restrictive for many real scenarios.
- Weakness — experiments: Missing detailed parameter tuning and sensitivity analysis (e.g., λ, dictionary rank).
- For acceptance: Well-motivated, theoretically justified, empirically better than RPCA, and improves understanding of coherence.
- Against acceptance: Needs clearer presentation, deeper related-work comparison, clearer novelty claims, and more realistic noise analysis.
- Suggested revisions: Improve clarity and notation, expand related work on dictionary learning/structured low-rank recovery, discuss dictionary rank and initialization robustness, include sensitivity analyses, and address the strong noise assumptions in Theorem 2.
