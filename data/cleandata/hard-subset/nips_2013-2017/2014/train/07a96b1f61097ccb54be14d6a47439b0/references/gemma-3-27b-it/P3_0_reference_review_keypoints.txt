- Overall assessment: Weak accept conditional on significant revisions to clarity and novelty arguments.  
- Main idea: RPCA fails with coherent, clustered low-rank data; propose learning a low-rank dictionary within LRR to mitigate coherence and enable recovery.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous analysis (Theorem 1) showing conditions for recovery and explains the µ2-phenomenon linking coherence to data structure.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA and LRR (Liu et al. 2013) and connects to matrix factorization, but extension may be incremental.  
- Strength: Identifies a practical limitation of RPCA and links clustering to increased coherence.  
- Strength: Solid theoretical foundation with recovery guarantees under specified conditions.  
- Strength: Promising empirical results on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences showing improvement over RPCA.  
- Strength: Clear connection to established frameworks and a practical, computationally reasonable Algorithm 1 that uses RPCA as a starting point.  
- Weakness: Paper presentation is dense, notation-heavy, and lacks intuitive motivation for coherence parameters (µ1, µ2, µ3).  
- Weakness: Novelty seems incremental; authors must clarify what is specifically new about their dictionary-learning constraint (e.g., PUA(U0)=U0).  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of alternative dictionary learning methods (e.g., K-SVD) and trade-offs versus using RPCA output.  
- Weakness: Experimental details are insufficient—missing λ selection, dictionary tuning, and rank estimation (r̂0) explanations.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 (noisy case) is weakly justified and lacks detailed analysis of noise impact (ε).  
- Acceptance pros: Addresses a real problem, gives theoretical guarantees, shows empirical gains, and offers a practical algorithm.  
- Acceptance cons: Hindered by clarity issues, incremental novelty concerns, limited exploration of dictionary methods, and sparse experimental detail.  
- Required revisions: Rewrite Sections 3.1.1–3.1.2 for intuition, better articulate novelty of dictionary learning, discuss alternative dictionary approaches, provide full experimental settings and rank-selection criteria, and strengthen Theorem 2’s noise analysis.
