- Paper addresses failure of RPCA on clustered/union-of-subspaces data where coherence parameter μ2 grows with cluster count ("μ2-phenomenon"), harming RPCA guarantees.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A (min ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S) and shows exact/near recovery if U0 ⊂ UA, removing dependence on μ2 and replacing it with μ1(A).  
- Theoretical results cover noiseless and noisy settings with corruption fractions up to 0.5/0.35 and λ = 1/√n1, relaxing rank bounds compared to PCP under the subspace inclusion assumption.  
- Practical two-stage unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA to estimate L0, truncate SVD to get low-rank L̃0, normalize columns to form A, then solve LRR for final estimate.  
- Empirical gains shown on synthetic clustered data and corrupted motion trajectory (Hopkins) datasets, improving downstream subspace clustering performance.  
- Novelty: focuses on coherence due to clustering, analyzes LRR with general low-rank dictionaries (not just A = X), and gives a simple recipe to learn A from RPCA.  
- Strength: clearly identifies and articulates a practical failure mode of RPCA (μ2-phenomenon) and provides an insightful theoretical remedy.  
- Strength: technically nontrivial recovery theorems that avoid μ2 and connect coherence to dictionary design.  
- Strength: simple, practical algorithm that is plausible in many applications and shows nontrivial empirical gains.  
- Weakness: central theoretical assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong and effectively supervised; the unsupervised algorithm lacks theoretical guarantees.  
- Weakness: no theory provided for approximate subspace inclusion (principal angle bounds) or for end-to-end behavior of the proposed Algorithm 1.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are limited—missing comparisons to other robust PCA variants (Outlier Pursuit, trimmed/structured RPCA, nonconvex methods, weighted PCP) and dictionary-based methods.  
- Weakness: motion data handling (replacing missing entries with random ±1) is unrealistic; recovery metrics (reconstruction error, support recovery) are not reported, only clustering error.  
- Weakness: several derivations (e.g., μ2-phenomenon) and proof details are deferred to a future full paper; NeurIPS submission should be self-contained with proofs in supplement.  
- Quality concern: noisy-case bounds scale with √(mn)ε and appear loose; discussion of tightness and runtime (wall-clock) and ablations (rank threshold, normalization, λ sensitivity) are missing.  
- Clarity issues: some typos/notation problems (e.g., "PRCA"), deferred definitions/claims, and unexplained parameter choices (rank threshold, fixed λ for A) need fixing.  
- Specific suggestions: provide a theorem for approximate subspace inclusion, analyze Algorithm 1's end-to-end behavior given RPCA error/subspace distance, and add comparisons to other methods and realistic missing-data handling.  
- Specific suggestions: report direct recovery metrics (||L̂−L0||F, support precision/recall), runtime, sensitivity analyses for λ and SVD truncation, and include a lemma quantifying μ2 vs. number of clusters.  
- Overall assessment: conceptually and theoretically valuable with promising practical gains, but strong assumptions, limited experiments, and missing end-to-end theory make the contribution borderline; recommendation: borderline accept.
