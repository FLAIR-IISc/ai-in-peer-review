- Problem: RPCA fails on coherent (clustered) data because increased µ2 breaks its recovery conditions.  
- Proposal: Use LRR with a low-rank dictionary A satisfying U₀ ⊂ U_A to avoid dependence on µ2.  
- Theory: Theorems 1–2 show LRR with such a dictionary can recover coherent data and give looser rank bounds than RPCA.  
- Algorithm: An unsupervised practical method (Algorithm 1) builds A from RPCA output to enable LRR.  
- Contribution: Novel identification of the µ2-phenomenon linking coherence parameters to clustering structure.  
- Strength — Theory: Provides significant theoretical improvement (rank bound O(n₂/log n₁) vs RPCA’s O(n₂/log² n₁)).  
- Strength — Practical: Simple, effective algorithm with strong empirical gains (47% larger success region, 40% improvement on motion segmentation).  
- Strength — Insight: Clarifies physical meaning of coherence parameters, not just technical assumptions.  
- Strength — Validation: Well-designed experiments on synthetic data and Hopkins155 that demonstrate benefits.  
- Weakness — Related work: Inadequate comparison to dictionary learning and adaptive dictionary methods for low-rank recovery/subspace clustering.  
- Weakness — Theory–algorithm gap: Practical construction of A from RPCA isn’t clearly tied to theoretical conditions (U₀ ⊂ U_A, A low-rank).  
- Weakness — Complexity claim: Computational time claim (~1.2× RPCA) lacks detail and relies on simplifying assumptions (m=n).  
- Weakness — Dictionary analysis: No theoretical guarantees that RPCA-derived A satisfies required conditions; empirical success doesn’t fully justify assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Accept — paper is well-written, technically sound, addresses an important limitation, and provides theoretical and practical advances.  
- Revision suggestions: Add comparisons to dictionary learning literature and clarify/bridge theoretical conditions with the practical dictionary construction.
