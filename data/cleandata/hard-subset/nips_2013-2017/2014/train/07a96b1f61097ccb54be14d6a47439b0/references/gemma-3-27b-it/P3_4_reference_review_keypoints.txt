- Motivation: Identifies the µ2-coherence issue caused by clustering and links coherence to intrinsic data structure rather than a mere technical assumption.
- Theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 provides recovery conditions for LRR with a low-rank dictionary, connecting dictionary design to coherence reduction.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA and LRR literature and links to matrix factorization, positioning the contribution as a novel combination addressing coherence.
- Practical algorithm: Proposes a simple, efficient Algorithm 1 that uses RPCA to initialize the dictionary for LRR.
- Empirical results: Synthetic and real-motion experiments show clear improvements over RPCA in recovery performance.
- Strength: Clear motivation, solid theoretical analysis, practical algorithm, convincing empirical validation, and insightful discussion of coherence.
- Weakness: Strong dictionary condition U0 ⊂ UA — practical sensitivity and implications not fully explored.
- Weakness: Theoretical noise model assumes dense Gaussian noise, which may not match real-world sparse noise or missing-data scenarios.
- Weakness: Limited experimental comparison to other LRR variants and dictionary-learning strategies.
- Weakness: Iterative dictionary refinement was dismissed without rigorous investigation; potential gains not fully evaluated.
- Evaluation: Quality strong, clarity excellent, originality and significance good — meaningful and practical contribution.
- Pros: Addresses practical RPCA limitation, principled approach, theoretical support, effective algorithm, useful coherence insights.
- Cons: Strong dictionary requirement and limited comparisons to other LRR methods.
- Recommendation: Strong accept with minor revisions.
