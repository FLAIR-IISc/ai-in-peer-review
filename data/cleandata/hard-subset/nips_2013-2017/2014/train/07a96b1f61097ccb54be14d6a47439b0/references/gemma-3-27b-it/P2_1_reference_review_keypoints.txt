- Addresses robust low-rank recovery when the true low-rank matrix is coherent due to data structure and proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary to mitigate RPCA failures.
- Theoretically shows that a low-rank dictionary in LRR can avoid coherence-related limitations of RPCA (Theorem 1) and gives conditions for successful recovery.
- Proposes a practical algorithm that uses RPCA to learn a suitable dictionary for LRR; claimed to be simple and computationally efficient.
- Empirical results on synthetic and motion-capture data demonstrate improved recovery over standard RPCA.
- Clearly connects to and builds on existing RPCA and LRR literature, providing insight into coherence and data structure.
- Strength: Tackles a relevant and important limitation of RPCA with solid theoretical support.
- Strength: Provides a practical, implementable algorithm with convincing experimental validation.
- Weakness: Core LRR framework is not novel; main novelty is the dictionary-learning strategy, which should be emphasized.
- Weakness: Theoretical assumptions (e.g., column-wise unit-norm dictionary and rank–coherence conditions) may be restrictive and their practical sensitivity is not fully discussed.
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are limited (mostly vs RPCA); lacks comparisons to other robust low-rank methods and parameter sensitivity analyses.
- Weakness: Heavy notation and some dense phrasing reduce readability for non-experts.
- Weakness: Insufficient exploration of pipeline sensitivity to RPCA quality and practical guidance for choosing RPCA parameters (e.g., lambda).
- Quality: Technically sound and rigorous but slightly reduced by strong theoretical assumptions and limited experiments (8/10).
- Clarity: Generally well-written but notation density and complexity hinder readability (7/10).
- Originality: Moderate — LRR use is known, but the dictionary-learning strategy and theoretical justification are novel (7/10).
- Significance: High — addresses a common RPCA limitation with practical impact (8/10).
- Pro: Significant practical problem addressed, solid theory, clear empirical gains, and a simple efficient algorithm.
- Con: Limited novelty in core framework, potentially restrictive theoretical assumptions, and incomplete experimental evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing assumptions, expanding experiments, and improving clarity.
