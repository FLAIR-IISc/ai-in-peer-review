This paper tackles a crucial problem: the brittleness of PCA in the presence of corruptions and the limitations of RPCA in handling coherent data. The authors identify a connection between data coherence and underlying clustering structures, proposing a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach with a carefully designed dictionary to mitigate the issue. This is a conceptually bold and potentially impactful direction. The core idea – that coherence isn’t merely a mathematical artifact but reflects data structure – is a significant insight.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The connection made between data coherence, clustering structures, and the limitations of RPCA is a genuinely novel contribution. Framing coherence not as an assumption but as a characteristic reflecting underlying data organization is a compelling argument.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis, demonstrating how a low-rank dictionary in LRR can avoid the pitfalls associated with high coherence. Theorem 1 provides a clear, if somewhat restrictive, condition for recovery.
* **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm, building on RPCA to initialize the dictionary for LRR, is a pragmatic approach. Its relatively low computational overhead is also a plus.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on both randomly generated matrices and motion sequences demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, consistently outperforming RPCA. The results on motion sequences with realistic corruptions are particularly convincing.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, technical details, and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions:** Theorem 1 relies on fairly strong assumptions, including a column-wise unit norm dictionary and the condition PUA(U0) = U0. While these might be achievable in some scenarios, their generality is limited.  The sensitivity analysis to these assumptions is lacking.
* **Dictionary Learning:** The heuristic for dictionary learning (using the output of RPCA) is relatively simple. Exploring more sophisticated dictionary learning techniques could potentially yield further improvements.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are well-executed, the range of datasets and the comparisons with other robust PCA methods are somewhat limited. Exploring more challenging real-world datasets would strengthen the claims.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The theoretical analysis primarily focuses on the noiseless case.  While Theorem 2 addresses noise, the results are less strong and could benefit from further exploration. The connection between the theoretical analysis and the observed improvements in the experiments could be made more explicit.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a genuinely novel perspective on robust PCA and addresses a significant limitation of existing methods.
* The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed approach, and the empirical results support the claims.
* The approach is practical and offers a tangible improvement over RPCA in realistic scenarios.
* The insights gained into the relationship between coherence and data structure are valuable contributions to the field.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The strong assumptions in the theoretical analysis limit the generality of the results.
* The dictionary learning strategy is relatively simple and could potentially be improved.
* The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a significant and novel contribution to the field of robust PCA. While the theoretical assumptions are somewhat restrictive and the experimental evaluation could be expanded, the core idea – connecting coherence to underlying data structure and leveraging this insight to improve recovery – is exceptionally promising.  The paper introduces a conceptually important new direction, and the practical algorithm demonstrates its effectiveness. I lean heavily towards acceptance, prioritizing the novelty and potential impact of the work over the limitations in methodological rigor. I believe the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses, making this a valuable addition to the NIPS program. 

**Recommendation:** Accept.  I encourage the authors to address the reviewer’s concerns regarding the assumptions and the scope of the experiments in a future version of the paper.
