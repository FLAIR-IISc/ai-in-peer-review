Summary
The paper studies recovery of low-rank plus sparse matrices when the low-rank component is coherent (e.g., exhibits clustering). The authors argue RPCA’s guarantees degrade with increasing coherence (especially via μ2) and propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A to “avoid” μ2. The main theoretical statements (Theorems 1 and 2) claim exact (noiseless) and near-exact (noisy) recovery via LRR under conditions: A is column-normalized, low-rank, and its column space contains the true left singular space U0 (U0 ⊂ UA); rank(A) upper-bounded by n2/(μ1(A) log n1); and the sparse support is not too dense. To build such A in an unsupervised setting, they propose a two-stage heuristic: run RPCA, estimate rank, truncate the RPCA low-rank factor, column-normalize to form A, then solve LRR. Experiments on synthetic data and corrupted motion sequences show improved success regions or clustering error over RPCA.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on RPCA/PCP [Candès et al. 2011] and LRR [Liu et al. 2010–2013]. The discussion of coherence parameters follows matrix completion/robust PCA literature (Candès, Recht, Gross). The idea of using a learned dictionary resembles factorization methods. The specific claim of mitigating the μ2 phenomenon via a low-rank dictionary with U0 ⊂ UA is the main conceptual novelty.

Strengths
- Clearly identifies and explains the practical failure mode of RPCA under clustered data (μ2-phenomenon).
- Theorems formalize conditions under which LRR with a low-rank dictionary sidesteps μ2 (depends only on μ1(A)).
- Simple, practical two-stage algorithm; empirically improves over RPCA on synthetic and a motion dataset.
- Tolerates high corruption fractions in statements (up to ~50% in noiseless theorem).

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Quality/technical soundness
- The key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong and is the very information one typically lacks. The unsupervised procedure builds A via RPCA, but there is no analysis that (a) RPCA approximates U0 closely enough in the coherent regime where it is known to struggle, or (b) quantifies how approximation error affects PUA(U0) = U0 and recovery. The claim that the algorithm’s “success probability is ≥ that of RPCA” is informal and unproven.
- Theorems are underspecified: “with probability at least 1 − n^−101” is unusual and the source of randomness is not clearly stated (support Ω uniform? random signs? independence assumptions?). Standard RPCA theory specifies these precisely.
- The bounds depend on μ1(A), but the paper does not analyze how the proposed construction controls μ1(A), nor how column normalization relates to μ1(A) quantitatively. “Encouraging a well-conditioned dictionary” is vague.
- λ = 1/√n1 is justified by column-normalization, but for general A the optimal λ can depend on operator norms; no sensitivity analysis is provided.
- Algorithmic details are ad hoc: rank threshold at 10^−3 σ1, ε fixed at 10^−6‖X‖F, no robustness study to these choices.
- Claims like “LRR same as RPCA whenever A is orthogonal” are stated without proof or citation; this equivalence is nontrivial and needs justification.
- The statement “O(n2/log n1) is probably the finest bound” is speculative.
- Complexity claims (LRR O(n^2 rA) vs RPCA O(n^3)) depend on implementation specifics and hidden constants; no wall-clock results are reported.

Clarity
- Many missing details: no proof sketches; theorems refer to “full paper”; assumptions on Ω/S0 not fully specified; solver, stopping criteria, and implementation specifics are absent.
- Figures/Tables are referenced but not included here; the description alone is insufficient to assess rigor (e.g., no quantitative statistics).
- Notation/grammar issues throughout: “column-wisely” (should be columnwise), inconsistent spacing “rank (L0)”, several typos (“principle component analysis”, “besides of”, “heightening”, “criteria” used as singular), and awkward footnotes embedded in text (e.g., “PRCA3”, “SVD)2”).
- Reference formatting is inconsistent/incomplete (missing years/venues; “Arxiv” capitalization; many entries lack details).

Originality
- The insight to leverage a low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence is interesting and, to my knowledge, not fully addressed in earlier LRR papers with general dictionaries. However, LRR with A = X and factorization-based robust methods are well-studied; the theoretical contribution rests heavily on the U0 ⊂ UA assumption. The unsupervised two-stage construction is a natural heuristic but not theoretically novel without analysis.

Significance
- If fully substantiated, the approach could be useful for coherent data regimes. Current empirical evidence is limited: synthetic study shows a larger “success area” but lacks comparisons beyond RPCA; real-data study uses only 11 sequences and an artificial corruption process (replacing missing entries by ±1 rather than modeling missingness), and no standard robust subspace baselines beyond RPCA are included.

Experimental concerns
- Synthetic setup fixes k = 5 clusters; since the μ2-phenomenon is central, more systematic variation of k and its effect on performance should be shown.
- No ablations: e.g., oracle A built from true U0 vs RPCA-derived A; sensitivity to rank threshold; effect of dictionary rank.
- Missing baselines: AltProj/GoDec, Outlier Pursuit (column-sparse), robust MF, more recent RPCA variants; and LRR with different A (e.g., A = X) under the same settings.
- No reporting of variance, statistical significance, or runtimes.
- Motion experiment corruption model is questionable (replacing missing with ±1). Missing data should be handled with matrix completion or RPCA variants designed for missing entries.

Pros
- Addresses an important practical limitation (coherence) in RPCA.
- Clear high-level idea; simple pipeline; some empirical gains.
- Theoretical statements target removal of μ2 from conditions.

Cons
- Strong, arguably impractical assumption (U0 ⊂ UA) with no theoretical bridge from the unsupervised procedure to the theorem’s conditions.
- Underspecified theorems (randomness, constants), no proofs provided.
- Limited, somewhat contrived experiments; minimal baselines; lack of ablations and runtime.
- Numerous clarity, notation, and reference issues.

Recommendation
- Score: 4/10 (Reject)
- Confidence: 0.8

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide complete proofs (in appendix) with precise probabilistic models and constants; clarify the dependence on μ1(A).
- Theoretically analyze the two-stage procedure: conditions under which RPCA yields UA that contains/approximates U0; robustness of Theorems 1–2 to UA ≈ U0 and to errors in A.
- Add ablations and stronger baselines; vary number of clusters; include oracle and noisy settings; report runtimes.
- Handle missing data properly in motion experiments; increase dataset size and realism.
- Justify parameter choices (λ, ε, rank threshold) with sensitivity analyses.
- Clean up writing, notation, and references; fix typos and footnotes; ensure reproducibility details.
