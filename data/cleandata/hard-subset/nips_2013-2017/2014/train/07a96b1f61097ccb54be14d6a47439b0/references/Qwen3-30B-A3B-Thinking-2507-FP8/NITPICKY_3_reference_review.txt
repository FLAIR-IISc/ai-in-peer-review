

# Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

## Summary

This paper addresses the limitation of RPCA (Robust Principal Component Analysis) when dealing with coherent data (data with clustering structures), which causes RPCA's performance to degrade as the number of clusters increases. The authors propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary matrix A to avoid the coherence parameters that negatively impact RPCA. The key theoretical contribution is Theorem 1, which shows that LRR can recover the low-rank component exactly when the dictionary A is low-rank and contains the column space of L0. The authors also propose Algorithm 1 for unsupervised dictionary learning.

## Strengths

1. The paper identifies an important limitation of RPCA that has not been extensively explored in the literature, particularly regarding how clustering structures affect coherence parameters.
2. The connection between clustering structures and coherence parameters (the "µ2-phenomenon") is well-documented with clear examples.
3. The experimental results on both synthetic data and motion sequences demonstrate a consistent improvement over RPCA, with a 40% relative improvement in clustering error rates.
4. The paper provides a useful theoretical foundation for understanding why dictionary learning can help with coherent data.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Flaws**: Theorem 1 requires that U0 ⊆ UA (the column space of L0 is a subspace of the column space of A). This is a strong condition that essentially requires prior knowledge about the column space of L0, which contradicts the claim that the method works in unsupervised settings. The paper states "it is unnecessary for A to satisfy UA = U0," but the theorem's condition is U0 ⊆ UA, not UA = U0. This is a critical theoretical flaw that undermines the paper's main contribution.

2. **Algorithmic Circular Dependency**: The paper claims Algorithm 1 is "more efficient" than RPCA, stating "the computational time of Algorithm 1 is merely about 1.2 times as much as RPCA." However, the efficiency depends on A being low-rank (rA small), which is exactly what the algorithm is trying to achieve. This creates a circular dependency that is not addressed.

3. **Arbitrary Thresholds**: The algorithm uses an arbitrary threshold of 10⁻³ for rank estimation (Step 2: "r̂₀ = #{i : σᵢ > 10⁻³σ₁}"). This value is not justified or tested for sensitivity, making the algorithm's performance highly dependent on this arbitrary choice.

4. **Lack of Precision in Claims**: The paper claims "the success area of our algorithm is 47% wider than that of RPCA" but never defines what "success area" means or how it's calculated. This is a vague, unsupported claim that lacks scientific rigor.

5. **Inconsistent Notation and Definitions**: The paper uses inconsistent notation for coherence parameters (e.g., defining µ(L0) = max{µ₁(L₀), µ₂(L₀), µ₃(L₀)} but then discussing them individually). The relationship between µ₂ and µ₃ is misstated as "the variation of µ₃ is mainly due to the variation of the row space, which is characterized by µ₂," when µ₃ actually depends on both U₀ and V₀.

6. **Missing Figures**: The paper references Figures 1-3 multiple times but does not include them in the provided text, making it impossible to verify the claims about the "µ2-phenomenon" and the performance of the proposed method.

7. **Experimental Ambiguity**: The paper claims "a 40% relative improvement" in clustering error rates, but fails to specify the baseline (RPCA vs. another method) or how the improvement is calculated. This lack of precision makes the experimental results difficult to interpret.

8. **Theoretical Claims Without Justification**: The paper states "O(n₂/log n₁) is probably the 'finest' bound one could accomplish in theory" without providing any justification or comparison to existing bounds in the literature.

9. **Formatting and Reference Issues**: The paper has multiple formatting inconsistencies (e.g., inconsistent spacing around inequality symbols, inconsistent use of matrix norms) and incomplete references (e.g., "Arxiv,, , ," in the references section).

10. **Overstated Contributions**: The paper claims to "show that the coherence parameters are not 'assumptions' for a proof, but rather some excellent quantities that relate in nature to the extra structures (beyond low-rankness) intrinsically existing in L₀," but this is a philosophical claim without empirical or theoretical support.

## Recommendation

This paper identifies an interesting limitation of RPCA and proposes a method that shows empirical improvement over RPCA in some scenarios. However, the theoretical foundation is severely undermined by the strong condition U₀ ⊆ UA, which is not practically achievable in unsupervised settings as claimed. The algorithmic circular dependency, arbitrary thresholds, and lack of precise claims significantly weaken the paper's contribution.

The paper contains multiple minor methodological and formatting errors that collectively indicate a lack of rigorous attention to detail. While the empirical results are promising, the theoretical claims are not sufficiently supported, and the paper fails to adequately address the practical challenges of implementing the proposed method.

Given the stringent requirements for NIPS papers and the significant theoretical flaws in the paper, I recommend rejection. The paper would benefit from a complete theoretical revision addressing the U₀ ⊆ UA condition, a rigorous justification for the arbitrary thresholds, and precise definitions of all claims.
