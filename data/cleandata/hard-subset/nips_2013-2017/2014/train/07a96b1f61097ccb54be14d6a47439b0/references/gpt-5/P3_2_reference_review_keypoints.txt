- Paper addresses the documented µ2-phenomenon: clustered/union-of-subspaces structure inflates coherence (µ2, µ3) and degrades PCP/RPCA guarantees.
- Proposal: use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a learned low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space U0 to avoid dependence on µ2.
- Theoretical results: noiseless and noisy recovery guarantees for LRR with λ = 1/√n1, requiring rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ O(n2/(µ1(A) log n1) and |Ω| ≤ c·m·n sparse corruptions.
- Key condition: U0 ⊂ UA (dictionary column space contains true column space) and low µ1(A); under this the dependence on µ2 (and µ3) is removed.
- Practical algorithm: two-stage unsupervised pipeline—run RPCA, truncate to top r̂0 components, normalize columns to form Â, then solve LRR with Â.
- Empirical gains: on synthetic clustered subspaces and corrupted motion data the method outperforms RPCA (e.g., 47% larger success region; 40% relative error reduction in motion segmentation).
- Strength: clear articulation and empirical demonstration of the µ2-phenomenon and its practical importance.
- Strength: new recovery guarantees for LRR with general low-rank dictionaries shifting dependence to µ1(A); rank bound favorable versus PCP’s standard.
- Strength: simple, practical algorithm that yields nontrivial empirical improvements.
- Strength: good discussion of physical meaning of coherence and how extra structure drives it.
- Weakness: strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA is close to assuming access to the column space in unsupervised settings; RPCA initializer may fail in the problematic regime.
- Weakness: no analysis of approximate containment—missing quantification of how principal-angle deviation between U0 and UA affects recovery.
- Weakness: guarantees replace µ2 dependence with µ1(A); paper lacks empirical reporting of µ1(Â) vs µ2(L0) to substantiate that dictionaries are well-conditioned.
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to random entrywise-sparse corruption; lacks comparisons to methods handling structured corruption, column-sparse outliers, or missing data (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, robust matrix completion, RASL/TILT).
- Weakness: solver and complexity claims (e.g., O(n^2 rA)) lack specificity; algorithmic details (ADMM, stopping criteria) and runtime comparisons are missing.
- Weakness: theoretical exposition defers proof details; a high-level dual-certificate sketch and formal clustered-subspace model for µ2 growth are requested.
- Weakness: fixed hyperparameters (singular threshold, ε) and column normalization raise sensitivity/noise-amplification concerns without ablation or handling guidance.
- Quality: theoretical claims plausible and consistent with prior work; empirical results encouraging but limited and lacking ablations on dictionary rank and RPCA initialization quality.
- Clarity: generally well written and organized; notation heavy but standard; some claims overly strong and should be qualified.
- Originality: novelty lies in theory and practice of using low-rank dictionaries to mitigate coherence, and the RPCA→LRR pipeline with conditioned guarantees.
- Significance: addresses a real PCP failure mode with a simple remedy that could influence robust subspace learning if further validated.
- Pros for acceptance: formalizes PCP shortcoming, gives new LRR guarantees avoiding µ2, provides a practical algorithm with empirical benefits, and clarifies coherence role.
- Cons for acceptance: strong U0 ⊂ UA assumption, narrow experimental scope, and deferred theoretical/algorithmic details.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (high reviewer confidence).
- Suggested improvements: add experiments with block/structured corruptions and missing data baselines; report µ1(Â), µ2(L0), and UA–U0 principal angles; provide sensitivity/ablation on r̂0 and iterative refinements; clarify solver, runtime, and handling of near-zero columns.
