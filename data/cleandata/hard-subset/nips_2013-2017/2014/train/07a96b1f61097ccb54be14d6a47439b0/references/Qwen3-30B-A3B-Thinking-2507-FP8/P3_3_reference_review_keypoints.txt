- Summary: Shows RPCA fails on coherent/clustering data (μ₂-phenomenon) and proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary to avoid dependence on coherence parameters, providing novel theoretical analysis.

- Strength: Theoretical insight that coherence parameters reflect data structures (e.g., clustering) rather than arbitrary assumptions.

- Strength: Proposes a simple, computationally efficient Algorithm 1 with a practical unsupervised dictionary construction.

- Strength: Strong experimental validation: wider success region on synthetic data and substantial error reduction on real motion sequences (Hopkins155).

- Strength: Clear empirical motivation linking clustering to increased μ₂ (Figure 2).

- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison—omits recent robust low-rank methods (e.g., RSC, Outlier Pursuit, OR-PCA).

- Weakness: Key theoretical condition P_UA(U₀) = U₀ is assumed; paper lacks evidence that dictionary construction reliably enforces this in practice.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of failure modes, such as complex data structures or poor initial RPCA dictionaries.

- Weakness: Theoretical rank bound improvement is marginal and not clearly tied to practical gains.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable theory and practical algorithm, but needs minor additional validation and analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Revision request: Add comparisons to 2–3 recent state-of-the-art robust recovery methods.

- Revision request: Provide more detailed analysis or evidence that the dictionary construction satisfies P_UA(U₀) = U₀ in practice.

- Revision request: Include a brief discussion of method limitations and potential failure cases.

- Acceptance arguments: Identifies and addresses an important RPCA limitation; deepens theoretical understanding of coherence; offers a simple and effective algorithm; demonstrates significant empirical improvements; well-motivated and clearly written.

- Rejection arguments: Lacks comparisons to recent methods; relies on an unverified theoretical assumption; limited discussion of failure cases.
