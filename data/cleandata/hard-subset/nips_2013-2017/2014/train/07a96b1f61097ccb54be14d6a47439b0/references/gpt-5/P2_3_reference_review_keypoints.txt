- Problem: Robust recovery of a low-rank matrix from gross corruption fails when the matrix is coherent (μ2-phenomenon).
- Proposal: Use LRR with a low-rank dictionary A (min ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S) to avoid μ2 dependence.
- Main theoretical result: If column space of A contains the true column space (PUA(U0)=U0) and A is low-rank, exact recovery is possible without μ2(L0) dependence, with improved rank bounds (O(n2/log n1)).
- Noisy extension: Frobenius-norm error bounds O(√(mn) ε) are provided for the noisy setting.
- Practical algorithm: Unsupervised two-step heuristic—run RPCA, form low-rank approximation of L̂0, column-normalize to get Â, then run LRR with Â.
- Experiments: Synthetic union-of-subspaces and corrupted motion trajectories show larger success regions (≈47% gain) and improved clustering accuracy over RPCA.
- Theoretical quality: Theorems are clear and plausible, trading μ2(L0) for μ1(A) and improving logarithmic factors vs. RPCA.
- Key assumption weakness: The requirement PUA(U0)=U0 is strong; unsupervised heuristic lacks formal guarantees to meet it.
- Experimental limitations: Missing ablations (rank truncation, column normalization), no sensitivity analysis for λ/ε, few baselines, and no evaluation when initial RPCA is poor.
- Reproducibility concerns: Algorithm is specified but solver choices, stopping criteria, and computational costs are under-detailed (1.2× overhead claim is qualitative).
- Clarity: Paper is well written with standard notation; some proofs deferred and some references/in-figure claims incomplete.
- Originality: Conceptually related to self-expressive methods, but novelty lies in theoretically showing LRR with a proper low-rank dictionary avoids μ2 and yields improved bounds.
- Significance: Addresses an important practical issue (coherent data); dictionary-design insight is valuable and likely to influence follow-up work.
- Pros: Clear identification of μ2-phenomenon, strong theoretical results removing μ2, practical algorithm that improves over RPCA, and principled bridge between RPCA and LRR.
- Cons: Strong assumption on A, limited empirical breadth and baselines, nonstandard handling of missing data, and presentation/implementation gaps.
- Suggested improvements: Provide ablations for rank truncation/normalization/L̂0 quality; compare to Outlier Pursuit, nonconvex RPCA, PCA-based dictionaries; try iterative refinement (RPCA→LRR→update A); clarify missing-data handling; add solver/runtime details.
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance pending stronger experiments and clearer practical/implementation details.
