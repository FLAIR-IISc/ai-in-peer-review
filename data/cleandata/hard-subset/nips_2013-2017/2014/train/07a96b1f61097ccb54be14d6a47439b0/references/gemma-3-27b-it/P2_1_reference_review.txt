## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of robust low-rank matrix recovery, specifically focusing on scenarios where the underlying low-rank matrix exhibits coherence due to intrinsic data structure (e.g., clustering). The authors argue that standard Robust PCA (RPCA) can struggle in these cases and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach with a carefully chosen dictionary to mitigate the issue. They theoretically demonstrate that using a low-rank dictionary in LRR can avoid the coherence-related limitations of RPCA, and present an algorithm that leverages RPCA to learn a suitable dictionary.  Experimental results on both synthetic and motion capture data demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach compared to RPCA.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a relevant problem:** Robust PCA is a cornerstone of many applications, and the issue of coherence arising from underlying data structure is a significant limitation.  Addressing this is a valuable contribution.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper provides a theoretical justification for using a low-rank dictionary in LRR to address coherence. Theorem 1 is a solid result, clarifying conditions for successful recovery.
* **Practical algorithm:** The proposed algorithm offers a practical way to leverage existing RPCA solutions to construct a more effective dictionary for LRR. The algorithm is relatively simple and computationally efficient (as discussed by the authors).
* **Empirical validation:** Experiments on both synthetic data and a real-world dataset (motion capture) demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over RPCA. The results appear convincing.
* **Clear connection to existing work:** The paper explicitly connects to and builds upon the foundations of RPCA and LRR, providing context and clarity.  The discussion regarding the relationship between coherence and data structure is insightful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited novelty in the core LRR framework:** The use of LRR itself isn't novel. The primary contribution lies in the *dictionary learning* strategy to address coherence within the LRR framework. While important, this should be emphasized more explicitly.
* **Theoretical assumptions:** The theoretical results rely on assumptions such as a column-wisely unit-normed dictionary and a specific condition relating rank to coherence (Theorem 1). While these assumptions are stated, their practical implications and sensitivity to violations are not thoroughly discussed.
* **Experimental scope:** While the experiments are well-executed, they are somewhat limited. It would be beneficial to see comparisons against other robust low-rank recovery methods beyond RPCA, such as those based on outlier pursuit.  A more thorough analysis of the parameters (e.g., lambda) and their impact on performance would also be valuable.
* **Clarity in notation:** While generally well-written, the paper uses a large number of notations, and some may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with the field to readily grasp. A more concise notation or a more gradual introduction of symbols would improve readability.
* **Discussion of practical dictionary learning:** The paper suggests leveraging RPCA to create the dictionary.  However, the sensitivity of the entire pipeline to the quality of the RPCA solution isn't deeply explored.  A discussion of practical considerations like choosing the RPCA parameter (lambda) for dictionary learning would be beneficial.



**Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound, with a clear theoretical justification and supporting experimental results. The analysis appears rigorous. However, the assumptions made in the theoretical results and the limited scope of the experimental comparison slightly reduce the quality score.
* **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally well-written, but the density of notation and occasional complex phrasing can hinder readability. More careful attention to clarity would improve the paper.
* **Originality (7/10):** The use of LRR is not new, but the specific dictionary learning strategy and its theoretical justification are novel contributions.  The paper provides a valuable insight into the relationship between coherence and data structure in the context of low-rank recovery.
* **Significance (8/10):** The results are significant because they address a common limitation of RPCA and offer a practical solution for handling coherent data. The potential impact on applications relying on robust low-rank recovery is substantial.

**Arguments for and against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a significant practical limitation of a widely used technique (RPCA).
* Provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed approach.
* Demonstrates clear improvements over RPCA in experiments.
* Offers a relatively simple and efficient algorithm.

**Con:**

* Limited novelty in the core LRR framework.
* Theoretical results rely on potentially restrictive assumptions.
* Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

This is a solid contribution that addresses a relevant problem and provides a valuable theoretical and practical solution. While the paper isn’t groundbreaking in terms of introducing entirely new techniques, the insight into coherence and the proposed dictionary learning strategy within LRR are significant. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above (particularly regarding the discussion of assumptions, experimental scope, and clarity of notation) will further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
