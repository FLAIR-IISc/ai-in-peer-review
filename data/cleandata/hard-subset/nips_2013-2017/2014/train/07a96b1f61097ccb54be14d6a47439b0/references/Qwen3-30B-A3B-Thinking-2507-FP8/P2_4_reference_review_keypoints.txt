- Identifies RPCA failure on clustered/coherent data and links clustering to increased coherence parameters (µ₂, µ₃).  
- Contributions: (1) formal connection between clustering structures and coherence, (2) proofs that LRR with a low-rank dictionary avoids those coherence parameters, (3) an unsupervised dictionary-construction algorithm using RPCA preprocessing.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2) are rigorous and provide clear recovery conditions for LRR with a low-rank dictionary.  
- Extensive experiments: 4000 synthetic trials and real-world validation on Hopkins155 motion database.  
- Empirical gains: 47% wider success area on synthetic data and 40% relative improvement in motion segmentation error rates.  
- Computational cost is practical: method runs at about 1.2× the time of RPCA.  
- Writing and presentation are excellent: clear motivation, consistent notation, helpful figures, and a precise algorithm description.  
- Original insight: treats coherence as structure-driven (clustering) rather than an arbitrary assumption, novel theoretical analysis of LRR with general dictionaries.  
- Proposed unsupervised dictionary-learning approach is novel in the context of coherent data recovery.  
- Significance: addresses a common real-world issue (clustered data) with theoretical and practical impact for vision and other applications.  
- Limitation: reliance on RPCA for dictionary construction—performance degrades when RPCA itself fails (dictionary imperfection not fully analyzed).  
- Missing comparisons: experimental baselines could include newer robust recovery methods (e.g., Outlier Pursuit variants).  
- Unaddressed scenario: handling high coherence arising from non-clustering causes is not explored and left to future work.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, practically relevant, and well-presented, with suggested extensions to robustness analysis and broader comparisons.
