Summary
The paper studies recovery of a low-rank matrix L0 in the presence of gross sparse corruptions S0 when the low-rank component is coherent (e.g., arises from a union-of-subspaces/clustered model). The authors observe that RPCA’s guarantees depend on incoherence (via µ1, µ2, µ3). They claim that “µ2-phenomenon” occurs when the number of clusters increases, degrading RPCA. They propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A to “avoid µ2” under the condition PUA(U0) = U0 (i.e., col(U0) ⊂ col(A)). They state noiseless and noisy recovery theorems where success depends on µ1(A), rank(A), and sparsity of S0, but not µ2(L0). For unsupervised settings, they propose a practical two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to estimate L0, build a normalized dictionary from it, then run LRR. Experiments on synthetic unions of subspaces and corrupted motion trajectories show improved recovery over RPCA and downstream clustering error reductions.

Strengths
- Addresses an important and common failure mode of RPCA in clustered/union-of-subspaces settings.
- Clear statement of the “µ2-phenomenon” and its empirical impact.
- LRR with a low-rank dictionary is a natural, interpretable idea; theorems formalize conditions under which µ2 can be avoided.
- Simple practical algorithm with promising empirical improvement over RPCA on both synthetic and motion segmentation data.
- Discussion connects to factorization methods and provides some interpretability about coherence parameters.

Weaknesses
- Core theoretical assumption PUA(U0) = U0 is very strong and effectively requires the dictionary to already contain the true subspace. The proposed unsupervised construction achieves this only when RPCA is already reasonably accurate; otherwise, guarantees do not apply. The circularity is not adequately acknowledged or analyzed.
- Limited empirical evaluation: only one synthetic setting and one application domain; no stress tests where RPCA fails badly; no ablations (e.g., sensitivity to the threshold 10−3σ1, dictionary rank, or iterative refinement).
- Experimental corruption model mismatch: the synthetic data uses replacement X = PΩ⊥(L0) + PΩ(S0), while the optimization model assumes additive X = L0 + S0. This is acceptable if S0 absorbs −PΩ(L0), but then S0 is not Bernoulli ±1 as generated; please clarify how additivity is ensured or evaluate under an additive model.
- Parameterization choices lack justification/sensitivity: λ is fixed to 1/√n1 for all settings; ε is fixed to 10−6‖X‖F regardless of actual noise; no cross-validation or robustness analysis.
- Claims about computational complexity and runtime (LRR O(n2rA) vs RPCA O(n3); 1.2× walltime) are not supported with solver details, datasets, hardware, or profiles. Algorithmic details (e.g., stopping criteria, proximal steps) are missing.
- Novelty relative to prior LRR literature is underspecified. The case A = X and benefits of low-rank dictionaries have been discussed; the main novelty appears to be the specific coherence-based analysis. Related work (e.g., Outlier Pursuit; robust matrix completion; stable PCP; nonconvex RPCA) is not fully compared empirically.
- Several technical imprecisions: randomness/modeling assumptions for Ω and S0 in Theorems 1–2 are not stated; success probability terms are malformed; conditions on A (dimensions, orthogonality cases) are underspecified.
- Writing and formatting issues are numerous (see below), impacting clarity.

Quality
- Partial. The high-level idea is reasonable, and theorems are plausible given the assumption U0 ⊂ UA. However, crucial assumptions are strong, and the experimental validation is limited and partially misaligned with the model. Lacking thorough baselines and sensitivity analyses reduces confidence.

Clarity
- Moderate. The narrative is understandable, but the paper contains many grammatical errors, undefined/misdefined notation, and formatting issues. Key assumptions (distribution of Ω, independence, exact dimensionalities) should be made explicit. Figures referred to (e.g., Figure 1–4) are not present here, limiting assessment.

Originality
- Somewhat incremental. The LRR model is established; constructing dictionaries from data is known. The novelty is the coherence-focused analysis showing avoidance of µ2 under low-rank A containing U0. This is interesting but relies on a strong prerequisite and is close to known intuitions about factorization invariance.

Significance
- Potentially useful if stronger practical procedures to estimate A without RPCA were provided. As is, empirical gains over RPCA are promising but limited in scope. The theoretical perspective on coherence is conceptually valuable.

Detailed comments and questions
- Please formalize the distributional assumptions on Ω and S0 in Theorems 1–2 (uniform support? independence? sign/magnitude distribution?). The probability statement “with probability at least 1 − n−101” appears malformed; do you mean 1 − n−10?
- The claim “LRR equals RPCA when A is orthogonal” requires precise dimensionality: if A has orthonormal columns and is square, then nuclear norm is invariant under left-orthogonal transforms; otherwise not. Please clarify the exact conditions.
- How sensitive is Algorithm 1 to the rank threshold 10−3σ1? Provide sensitivity plots.
- Provide ablations: (a) skip Steps 2–3; (b) try iterative refinement; (c) vary dictionary rank; (d) mismatched dictionaries where UA only partially overlaps U0 to test “tolerance to errors.”
- Compare against additional baselines: Outlier Pursuit, Robust Matrix Completion (with missing entries), Stable PCP, AltProj/nonconvex RPCA, GoDec, as well as LRR with different dictionaries (e.g., random low-rank, PCA-based).
- On motion data, replacing missing entries with Bernoulli ±1 is atypical; evaluate with standard missing-at-random and outlier corruption models; report standard deviations over multiple trials; detail parameter choices for SSC/LRRx to ensure fairness.
- Provide solver details (algorithm, penalties, stopping, SVD acceleration) and actual runtime plots to support the 1.2× claim.

Minor/formatting issues
- Numerous grammatical issues (“column-wisely,” “heightening,” “success area,” “ca” not defined), typos (“PRCA3” likely RPCA; “In terms of exact recovery, O(n2/ log n1) is probably the ‘finest’ bound” is speculative), inconsistent notation (n1, n2 introduced twice; SVD footnote repeated).
- References are incomplete (missing years/venues; inconsistent formatting; many commas and empty fields). Please fix to NeurIPS style.
- Define all norms upon first use; ℓ2,∞ notation missing subscript 2 in text; ensure consistent bold/roman for operators.
- Clarify whether Figures 2–4 show averaged results with error bars.

Pros for acceptance
- Interesting observation on coherence and clustered data impacting RPCA; clear articulation of µ2-phenomenon.
- Theoretical result that isolates µ1(A) under a low-rank dictionary; conceptually insightful.
- Simple algorithm with promising empirical improvements.

Cons for acceptance
- Strong assumptions and circularity (dictionary must contain U0; obtained via RPCA).
- Limited experiments, missing strong baselines and sensitivity analyses.
- Model/experiment mismatch and many presentation errors reduce confidence.

Recommendation
Lean reject. The idea is appealing and could be impactful with stronger, cleaner theory-to-practice linkage, broader experiments, and a thoroughly revised manuscript.
