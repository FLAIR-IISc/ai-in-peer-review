- Paper studies recovery of low-rank matrices when the low-rank part is coherent (large µ2), showing RPCA degrades in clustered/union-of-subspaces data.
- Authors identify a “µ2-phenomenon”: clustering increases µ2 and hurts RPCA guarantees.
- They propose using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA) to avoid µ2 in recovery conditions.
- Theorems show exact and noisy recovery via LRR if A is low-rank, column-normalized, and U0 ⊂ UA, with rank bounds depending on rank(A), µ1(A), n1, n2, and sparsity |Ω|.
- Rank bounds improve over RPCA scaling (O(n2/log n1) vs RPCA’s O(n2/(log n1)^2)), but conditions require strong U0 ⊂ UA.
- Practical unsupervised algorithm: RPCA → build low-rank column-normalized dictionary Â → run LRR, relying on RPCA to approximate U0.
- No formal guarantees given for the unsupervised pipeline except when RPCA is already exact.
- Experiments on synthetic clustered data show substantially larger exact-recovery region (reported ~47% wider).
- Motion segmentation experiments on 11 sequences with randomized imputations show consistent error reduction when used as preprocessing.
- Experimental setups are reasonable but small; more baselines and diagnostics would strengthen evidence.
- Connects to prior RPCA work by explaining RPCA’s incoherence assumption failures via µ2 growth with clusters.
- Extends LRR theory from A = X to general learned low-rank dictionaries, shifting coherence dependence to µ1(A).
- Relates to robust matrix factorization and Outlier Pursuit but emphasizes dictionary choice mitigating coherence failures.
- Theory is clean and formal, removing µ2 but relying on µ1(A) and the strong column-space inclusion assumption.
- Authors claim tolerance to imperfect A but provide no formal analysis of this robustness.
- Algorithmic idea is simple and plausible, leveraging RPCA as a dictionary initializer.
- Experimental evidence is consistent but limited: small sample sizes, lack of error bars, and few baselines (missing Outlier Pursuit, robust MF).
- Heuristic parameter choices (rank threshold, λ, ε) are not sensitivity-analyzed.
- No ablation studies on dictionary design (random A, A = X, iterative refinement) or normalization impact.
- Suggested additions: measure µ1(A) and µ2(L0) empirically, add baselines, report runtimes, and provide sensitivity analyses.
- Recommend clarifying noisy guarantee tightness and discussing other corruption models (column outliers, MNAR missingness).
- Minor fixes needed: typos, awkward terminology, and full proofs/citations.
- Overall assessment: conceptually and theoretically novel with practical gains; recommendation: Weak accept pending stronger experiments and practical guidance.
