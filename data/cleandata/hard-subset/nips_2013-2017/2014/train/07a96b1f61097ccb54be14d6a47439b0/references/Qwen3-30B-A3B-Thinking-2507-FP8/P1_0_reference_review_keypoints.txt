- Paper identifies RPCA's failure on clustered/coherent data and introduces the "µ2-phenomenon" linking clustering to increased coherence that degrades RPCA.
- Shows LRR with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary can avoid harmful coherence parameters and improve recovery.
- Builds on RPCA and LRR literature, clarifying that coherence reflects intrinsic data structure rather than a mere proof assumption.
- Proves rigorous recovery results (Theorem 1 noiseless, Theorem 2 noisy) with clear conditions under which LRR succeeds.
- Provides an unsupervised, simple-to-implement Algorithm 1 that empirically outperforms RPCA.
- Thorough experimental validation on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion sequences, showing large improvement over RPCA (47% wider success area in synthetic tests).
- Strong theoretical insight linking clustering structure to coherence parameters µ2 and µ3.
- Clear interpretation of coherence parameters and their practical implications for matrix recovery methods.
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs and well-supported claims.
- Originality: novel focus on dictionary rank in LRR and the coherence–clustering connection.
- Significance: relevant to practitioners working with real-world clustered data; results likely to influence future robust recovery work.
- Weakness: insufficient explanation/derivation of why clustering increases µ2; should be included in the paper.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 depends on an initial RPCA estimate without analysis of error propagation or sensitivity to poor initial estimates.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to alternative structured-data methods (e.g., subspace clustering like SSC or SIM).
- Weakness: Overstated claim about the "finest" rank bound O(n2/log n1) without matching lower bound or justification.
- Weakness: Terminology/confusion around "less incoherent" vs. "coherent" needs correction for clarity.
- Weakness: Missing detailed computational complexity and runtime comparisons, especially since the method solves two convex programs.
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to clarifying µ2 explanation, analyzing RPCA-dependence, fixing terminology, and adding at least one comparison to alternative methods.
