- Overall: Addresses RPCA's failure on coherent (clustered) data by using a learned low-rank dictionary in LRR, with theoretical and empirical support; recommends accept with minor revisions.
- Main idea: Coherence arises from clustering and degrades RPCA; a suitably low-rank dictionary in LRR can avoid coherence-related failure.
- Algorithm: Proposes a simple, efficient dictionary-learning algorithm initialized from RPCA outputs.
- Theory: Theorem 1 shows conditions under which a low-rank dictionary circumvents coherence parameters (especially µ2); Theorem 2 treats noise briefly.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA, LRR, matrix completion, and dictionary learning; extends LRR analysis to general dictionaries.
- Strength — Motivation: Clearly motivates why coherence is meaningful and problematic rather than a mere technicality.
- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous justification for using low-rank dictionaries to enable recovery under coherence.
- Strength — Practicality: Algorithm is simple, efficient, and leverages existing RPCA methods.
- Strength — Empirics: Synthetic and real-motion experiments consistently outperform RPCA.
- Strength — Context: Effectively situates contribution within related literature and subspace clustering.
- Weakness — Theory accessibility: Theoretical analysis and proofs are dense and need more intuitive exposition.
- Weakness — Assumptions: The column-wise unit-normed dictionary assumption in Theorem 1 seems arbitrary and insufficiently justified.
- Weakness — Dictionary learning: The proposed learning procedure is basic; more sophisticated approaches could improve results.
- Weakness — Noise treatment: Discussion and empirical treatment of robustness to noise (Theorem 2) are brief.
- Weakness — Comparisons: Experiments compare mainly to RPCA; broader comparisons to recent robust PCA methods are missing.
- Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance: Rated 8/10, 7/10, 8/10, and 8/10 respectively.
- Pro-accept arguments: Addresses major RPCA limitation, offers theory-backed practical solution, shows empirical gains, and reframes coherence as structure.
- Con-accept arguments: Needs clearer theory, stronger dictionary learning and comparisons.
- Recommendation & fixes: Accept with minor revisions — improve theoretical clarity, justify unit-norm assumption, and expand experimental comparisons and noise analysis.
