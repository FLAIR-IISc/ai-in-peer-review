- Overall assessment: Strong contribution that convincingly addresses RPCA limitations on coherent (clustered) data and is worthy of acceptance.  
- Main idea: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to mitigate RPCA’s sensitivity to data coherence.  
- Conceptual contribution: Reframes coherence as reflecting underlying data structure (µ2-phenomenon) rather than a mere restrictive assumption.  
- Theoretical result: Theorem 1 gives a clear criterion for selecting a low-rank dictionary to alleviate coherence issues.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Proposes a simple, practical algorithm that uses RPCA to initialize dictionary learning.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on synthetic data and motion sequences show consistent improvement over RPCA.  
- Experimental limitation: Datasets are relatively limited; broader, more diverse real-world evaluations and comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods are needed.  
- Presentation issues: Paper is somewhat verbose with repetitive explanations and an overwhelming notation section; could be made more concise.  
- Computational analysis: Discussion of algorithmic complexity is hand-wavy and requires a more detailed comparison with RPCA and alternatives.  
- Technical caveat: Claim that Theorem 1 provides a “looser” bound than RPCA needs more careful scrutiny and detailed comparison.  
- Significance and originality: Novel and insightful bridge between RPCA and dictionary learning with broader implications for robust PCA.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and tighten technical/computational comparisons.
