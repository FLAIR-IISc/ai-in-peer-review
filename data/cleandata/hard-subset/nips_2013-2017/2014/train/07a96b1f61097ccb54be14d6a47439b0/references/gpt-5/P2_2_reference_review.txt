Summary
The paper studies robust recovery of a low-rank matrix L0 and sparse corruptions S0 from X = L0 + S0 in regimes where L0 is coherent (large coherence parameters), which is known to hurt RPCA’s guarantees. The authors diagnose a “µ2-phenomenon”: when data exhibit clustering (union-of-subspaces) structure within a fixed-rank subspace, the second coherence parameter µ2 increases with the number of clusters, tightening RPCA’s recoverability condition. To address this, they analyze Low-Rank Representation (LRR): minZ,S ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S, with a dictionary A chosen a priori. Their main theoretical result shows that if A itself is low-rank and its column space contains the true column space of L0 (U0 ⊂ UA), then exact (noiseless) or near-exact (noisy) recovery is possible with high probability without assumptions on µ2—i.e., LRR “avoids µ2.” Building on this, they propose an unsupervised two-stage algorithm: use RPCA to obtain a denoised low-rank estimate L̂0, build a column-normalized low-rank dictionary Â from it, and then solve LRR to produce the final recovery. Experiments on synthetic data (phase transition plots) and motion segmentation (Hopkins-derived sequences with occlusions) show improved recovery and downstream clustering accuracy compared to RPCA preprocessing.

Quality
- Theory: Theorems 1–2 are clearly stated and address a recognized weakness of RPCA in coherent regimes. The assumptions are transparent: column-normalized dictionary, U0 ⊂ UA, rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ O(n2/(µ1(A) log n1)), and random support of S0 up to a constant fraction. Avoiding dependence on µ2 is meaningful, and the rank bound is slightly looser than RPCA’s (log vs. log^2). However, the U0 ⊂ UA condition is strong and essentially oracle-like for exact recovery; the paper acknowledges this and positions the condition as guidance for dictionary learning. It would be valuable to quantify robustness to approximate containment (e.g., principal angle bounds) since the unsupervised algorithm can only approximate U0.
- Algorithm: The RPCA→dictionary→LRR pipeline is simple and justified by the theory’s guidance (low-rank A with UA approximating U0). The practicalities (rank thresholding at 10^-3σ1, normalization) are described. A discussion of sensitivity to the rank threshold and to λ, and whether an iterative refinement beyond two passes adds value, would strengthen the empirical section.
- Experiments: The synthetic study (200×1000, 5 subspaces, varying subspace dimensions and sparsity) shows a larger success region (claimed 47% area increase) than RPCA. This is a convincing directionally correct demonstration, but it would benefit from: (i) ablations versus the purity/conditioning of A, (ii) varying the number of clusters to directly illustrate the µ2-phenomenon being mitigated, and (iii) runtime comparisons to substantiate the O(n^2 rA) vs. O(n^3) claim. The motion segmentation study shows substantial improvements in clustering error when using the proposed preprocessing; this is compelling but limited in scope (11 sequences and a particular corruption model). Comparisons to other robust PCA variants (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, high-dimensional robust PCA) as preprocessors would add context.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written and well organized. The exposition on coherence (µ1, µ2, µ3), their different behaviors, and how clustering drives µ2 is clear and insightful. Notation is heavy but standard and defined. The assumptions (e.g., column-wise unit norm) and parameter choices (λ = 1/√n1) are explicit. A few stylistic issues remain (typos, some informal phrasing); ensuring figures referenced (e.g., Fig. 2 and 3) concretely demonstrate the µ2 trend would help. Including a short intuition for why a low-rank dictionary that spans U0 removes dependence on µ2 (e.g., row-space alignment and representation in AZ) would aid readers.

Originality
- Conceptually, LRR is not new, but analyzing LRR with general low-rank dictionaries and showing that the success conditions can avoid µ2 under a span-inclusion assumption is novel and insightful. It connects coherence to data clustering and positions dictionary design as a way to overcome RPCA’s limitations.
- The pragmatic RPCA-seeded dictionary-learning heuristic is simple, but it is a reasonable and original instantiation of the theoretical guidance.
- The work appears distinct from prior LRR analyses focused on A = X and from alternative robust PCA formulations.

Significance
- The diagnosis of the µ2-phenomenon and the theoretical prescription for avoiding it are valuable contributions that may influence how the community approaches robust low-rank recovery in clustered data. If the assumptions can be relaxed (approximate containment) and the approach extended, this line of work could have broad impact in subspace clustering, vision, and other applications where union-of-subspaces structure is common.
- Empirically, the improvements over RPCA are meaningful on both synthetic and application data, and the method is easy to adopt.
- The strength of the significance is somewhat tempered by the strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA and by modest experimental breadth; still, the paper advances understanding and provides a practical method.

Pros
- Clear identification and explanation of a real limitation of RPCA due to coherence.
- Theoretical guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries that avoid µ2.
- Simple, practical algorithm with empirical gains over RPCA in recovery and downstream tasks.
- Insightful connection between coherence and clustering structures.

Cons
- Strong dictionary assumption (U0 ⊂ UA) for exact recovery; no quantitative analysis for approximate span containment.
- Limited experimental scope and ablations; missing runtime and sensitivity analyses.
- Dependence on RPCA for dictionary construction may falter in highly coherent regimes where RPCA is weakest.
- Some references are incomplete; minor clarity/notation issues.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide perturbation-style guarantees when UA approximately contains U0 (e.g., in terms of principal angles).
- Add ablations: effect of imperfect dictionaries, iterative refinement beyond one LRR step, and sensitivity to rank threshold and λ.
- Expand comparisons to other robust PCA variants and include runtime.
- Empirically vary the number of clusters to directly test the µ2-phenomenon and its mitigation.

Overall assessment
The paper meets the NeurIPS reviewing criteria well. It is technically sound with clear theorems, offers novel and relevant insights, and provides a practical method with demonstrated benefits. I recommend acceptance, with the caveat that strengthening the empirical section and relaxing/quantifying the dictionary assumption would substantially enhance the contribution. Confidence: medium-high.
