

# Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses a limitation of RPCA (Robust Principal Component Analysis) when dealing with coherent data, particularly when data exhibits clustering structures that increase coherence parameters. The authors propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to avoid the coherence parameters, especially the µ2 parameter that increases with cluster number. The main contribution is a theoretical analysis showing that LRR with a low-rank dictionary can avoid coherence parameters, along with an unsupervised algorithm for dictionary construction.

## Strengths

The paper identifies a meaningful limitation of RPCA in practical scenarios where data exhibits clustering structures. The connection between clustering structures and coherence parameters is well-motivated and supported by empirical evidence. The proposed algorithm is simple to implement and shows clear improvements over RPCA in both synthetic and real-world experiments. The theoretical analysis provides a clear condition for exact recovery, and the experimental results are thorough and convincing.

## Weaknesses

While the paper presents a novel perspective on coherence parameters, it contains several minor but significant technical inconsistencies and unclear statements that undermine the precision of the contributions:

1. **Inconsistent terminology**: The paper uses "coherent data" and "less incoherent data" interchangeably without clear definition. In the abstract, it states "recovery of coherent data," but in the introduction, it says "recovery of low-rank, and coherent (or less incoherent as equal) matrices." This confusion should be resolved.

2. **Theoretical inconsistencies**: In Theorem 1, the condition "rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ ǫ²n2/caµ1(A) log n1" is claimed to be looser than RPCA's "O(n2/(log n1)²)" requirement. However, the paper states "O(n2/log n1)" is the bound, which is indeed looser than RPCA's bound, but this claim is not clearly justified. The exact relationship between the constants should be clarified.

3. **Figure-text inconsistency**: The paper states "µ2(L0) (and so µ3(L0)) will increase with the number of clusters" (Section 3.1.2), but Figure 2(a) shows µ1 varying while Figure 2(b) shows µ2 increasing. The text contradicts the figure regarding which coherence parameter is invariant to cluster number.

4. **Arbitrary threshold**: In Algorithm 1, the rank estimation uses a threshold of "σi > 10−3σ1" without justification. Why 10^-3? This arbitrary choice affects the dictionary construction and should be explained or validated.

5. **Redundant condition**: The condition "PUA(U0) = U0" (i.e., U0 is a subspace of UA) is stated as "indispensable" for exact recovery. However, if U0 is a subspace of UA, then PUA(U0) = U0 is automatically true. This condition is redundant and should be removed or better justified.

6. **Experimental details**: In Section 4.2, the paper states "We replace each missed entry with a number from Bernoulli ±1," but this creates large outliers rather than handling missing values properly. The authors should clarify whether they're treating missing values as gross corruptions or using a different strategy.

7. **Inconsistent threshold values**: The paper uses |Ω| ≤ (0.5−ǫ)mn for the noiseless case and |Ω| ≤ (0.35−ǫ)mn for the noisy case without explaining why the threshold changes from 0.5 to 0.35. This discrepancy should be addressed.

## Recommendation

The paper addresses a meaningful limitation of RPCA and provides a theoretically grounded approach with empirical validation. However, the technical inconsistencies, redundant conditions, and lack of justification for arbitrary choices significantly undermine the paper's precision and rigor. These are not major conceptual flaws but represent a lack of meticulous attention to detail that would be expected in a NIPS submission.

The paper's contributions are interesting but suffer from minor but pervasive technical inconsistencies that detract from its overall quality. While the ideas are novel and the experiments are well-executed, the paper would benefit from careful revision to address these minor but important issues before publication.

I recommend rejection due to the significant number of minor technical inconsistencies that, while not undermining the core ideas, demonstrate a lack of the meticulous attention to detail expected at NIPS. These issues suggest the authors may not have thoroughly vetted the technical details of their work.
