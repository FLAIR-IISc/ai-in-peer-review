## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper addresses an important and practical problem in robust PCA – the impact of data coherence on recovery performance. The authors correctly identify the limitations of standard RPCA when dealing with data exhibiting inherent clustering structures (leading to higher coherence) and propose a principled approach using low-rank dictionaries within the LRR framework. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the connection between coherence, clustering, and recovery guarantees. While the theoretical contributions build upon existing work, they provide valuable insights and a practical algorithm that demonstrates promising results. I believe this paper is a **strong accept** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds on the well-established framework of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) and Low-Rank Representation (LRR). It starts by highlighting that RPCA, while powerful, struggles with data possessing inherent structures beyond simple low-rankness – specifically, clustering. This clustering increases the coherence of the low-rank component, negatively impacting RPCA's performance. The core idea is to mitigate this by employing a low-rank dictionary *A* in LRR, effectively pre-conditioning the data to reduce coherence.  The authors theoretically analyze the conditions under which this approach avoids the limitations imposed by coherence.  This work nicely bridges the gap between RPCA, LRR, and the understanding of data structure’s influence on matrix recovery.

The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of robust PCA (references [2, 3, 4, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 24, 13, 16, 19, 25]). They build upon the LRR framework [13] and relate it to matrix factorization techniques [20, 23].  The paper distinguishes itself by specifically addressing the coherence issue, linking it directly to underlying data structures and offering a practical solution.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of coherent data and why it poses a challenge for RPCA. The explanation of the "µ2-phenomenon" and its connection to clustering is intuitive and well-illustrated.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a theoretical analysis (Theorem 1) outlining the conditions for successful recovery using LRR with a low-rank dictionary.  This provides a solid foundation for the proposed approach. While the conditions are not drastically different from existing RPCA recovery guarantees, the connection to dictionary learning and coherence reduction is novel.
* **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm (Algorithm 1) is simple yet effective.  Leveraging RPCA to initialize the dictionary for LRR is a smart and computationally efficient approach.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real-world motion data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. The comparison with RPCA is convincing, showing a tangible improvement in recovery performance.
* **Insightful Discussion of Coherence:** The paper offers a valuable perspective on the meaning of coherence, moving beyond it being a mere technical assumption and linking it to intrinsic data structures. This is a significant contribution to understanding the underlying principles of these methods.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Condition on Dictionary:** The requirement that *U0 ⊂ UA* (U0 being the subspace spanned by the true low-rank component and UA the column space of the learned dictionary) is strong.  While the authors acknowledge this, the practical implications are not fully explored. How sensitive is the performance to deviations from this condition? 
* **Noise Assumption:** The theoretical analysis (Theorem 2) considers dense Gaussian noise, which might not always be representative of real-world scenarios (e.g., sparse noise or missing data).
* **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art LRR Methods:** The experiments focus primarily on comparing against RPCA.  A more thorough comparison against other LRR variants with different dictionary learning strategies would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Iterative Refinement:** While the authors mention iterative refinement of the dictionary, they dismiss it as not substantially improving performance. A more rigorous investigation of this might reveal further gains.



**4. Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (Strong):** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported. The analysis and experiments are well-executed. The theoretical results build upon existing work in a meaningful way.
* **Clarity (Excellent):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The explanations are clear and concise, making the concepts accessible to a broad audience.
* **Originality (Good):** While the individual components (RPCA, LRR, dictionary learning) are not novel, the combination of these ideas to address the specific problem of coherent data is a valuable contribution. The connection between coherence, clustering, and dictionary design is a novel insight.
* **Significance (Good):** The results are significant because they address a practical limitation of RPCA and provide a more robust approach for handling real-world data. The insights into coherence are also likely to be useful to other researchers in the field.

**5. Arguments for and Against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses an important and practical problem.
* Provides a principled approach with theoretical justification.
* Offers a simple and effective algorithm.
* Demonstrates clear performance improvements over RPCA.
* Provides valuable insights into the meaning of coherence.

**Con:**

* Strong condition on the dictionary (U0 ⊂ UA).
* Limited comparison to other LRR methods.



**6. Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept** with minor revisions.

**Suggested Revisions:**

* **Discuss the practical implications of the *U0 ⊂ UA* condition.** How can this condition be approximated in practice? What is the sensitivity of the algorithm to deviations from this condition?
* **Consider extending the experiments to include a broader comparison with other LRR variants.**
* **Re-evaluate the potential benefits of iterative dictionary refinement.** A more rigorous investigation might reveal further improvements.
* **Acknowledge the limitations related to the noise assumption and potential future work to address other noise models.**




I believe that addressing these minor points will further strengthen an already compelling paper and make it an even more valuable contribution to the NIPS community.
