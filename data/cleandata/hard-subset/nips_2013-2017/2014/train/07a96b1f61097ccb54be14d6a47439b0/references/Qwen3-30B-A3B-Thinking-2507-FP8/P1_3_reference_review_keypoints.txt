- Addresses RPCA's failure on coherent data (e.g., clustered structures) and links degradation to increased coherence parameters, especially µ2.  
- Proposes Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit (LRR) using a low-rank dictionary A to avoid dependence on coherence parameters.  
- Theorem 1: if A is low-rank and contains the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA), LRR can exactly recover the low-rank component without coherence assumptions.  
- Provides a practical Algorithm 1 that constructs a dictionary from RPCA's output to enable LRR in unsupervised settings.  
- Experimental results (synthetic and Hopkins155 motion data) show clear improvements over RPCA, including a 47% wider synthetic success region and ~40% relative reduction in motion segmentation error.  
- Technical contributions are rigorous with detailed proofs and comprehensive experiments validating the theory.  
- Novel insight: coherence parameters reflect intrinsic data structures (like clustering) rather than arbitrary assumptions.  
- Extends prior LRR/RPCA work by giving the first theoretical analysis of LRR with general low-rank dictionaries for coherent data recovery.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation and helpful visualizations illustrating key concepts.  
- Minor clarity issue: inconsistent use of the term "coherent data" (high coherence vs. clustering structures).  
- Main limitation: method requires a dictionary that contains the true column space, which is hard to verify in practice.  
- Theoretical guarantees impose strict conditions (e.g., column-wise unit-norm dictionary) that may limit practical applicability.  
- Connection between Algorithm 1's dictionary construction and the theoretical condition (U0 ⊂ UA) could be better justified.  
- Paper does not investigate the effect of dictionary size (d) on performance.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, original, significant, and practically useful — recommendation: Accept.
