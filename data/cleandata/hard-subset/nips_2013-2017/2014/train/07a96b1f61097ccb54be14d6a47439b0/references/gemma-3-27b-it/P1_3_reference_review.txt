## Peer Review for "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper addresses an important and practical problem – the limitations of Robust PCA (RPCA) when dealing with data exhibiting intrinsic structures beyond low-rankness, specifically clustering. The authors identify the role of coherence in this limitation and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach with a learned dictionary to mitigate the issue. The theoretical analysis provides insights into the conditions under which LRR can succeed, and the experimental results demonstrate promising performance gains over RPCA, especially in motion data. While the theoretical contributions could be strengthened with tighter bounds and more nuanced analysis, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of robust matrix recovery and provides a practical solution for handling coherent data. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the well-established framework of RPCA for low-rank matrix recovery.  It correctly identifies the sensitivity of RPCA to high coherence, which arises when the low-rank component contains inherent structures like clustering.  This is a crucial observation, as real-world datasets often deviate from the idealized random projections assumed by standard RPCA theory. The authors leverage the LRR framework (originally proposed in [13]) as a means to overcome the limitations of RPCA. The key innovation lies in demonstrating that a carefully chosen, low-rank dictionary can effectively address coherence issues, thereby enhancing recovery performance.  This work connects to a broad range of research including matrix completion ([3]), robust PCA ([4]), subspace clustering ([6, 14]), and matrix factorization ([20, 23]).  The authors appropriately cite relevant prior work and situate their contributions within the existing literature.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** Addressing the limitations of RPCA in real-world scenarios with structured data is a significant contribution.
* **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly explains why RPCA struggles with coherent data and provides a compelling motivation for exploring alternative approaches. The µ2-phenomenon is a useful conceptualization.
* **Theoretical Insights:** The analysis demonstrating how a low-rank dictionary can circumvent coherence issues is valuable and provides a theoretical justification for the proposed approach. Theorem 1, while having restrictive conditions, is a good starting point.
* **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm, which leverages RPCA to initialize the dictionary for LRR, is relatively simple and computationally efficient, making it practical for real-world applications.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real-world motion data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing performance improvements over RPCA.
* **Good Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized, making it relatively easy to follow the technical details.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Theoretical Assumptions:** The conditions for Theorem 1 (specifically regarding the dictionary A satisfying PUA(U0) = U0) seem quite restrictive and may not hold in many practical scenarios. While necessary for *exact* recovery, it’s not clear how sensitive the performance is to deviations from this condition. Exploring more relaxed conditions would be beneficial.
* **Tightness of Bounds:** The bounds presented in Theorems 1 and 2 are fairly loose and it’s unclear how tight they are. Investigating tighter bounds or providing empirical evidence to support the practicality of the presented bounds would strengthen the theoretical contributions.
* **Limited Exploration of Dictionary Learning:** The paper primarily focuses on using RPCA to initialize the dictionary. While effective, exploring other dictionary learning techniques, particularly those suitable for unsupervised settings, could further improve performance.
* **Experimental Scope:** While the results are promising, the experimental evaluation could be expanded to include a wider range of datasets and comparison with other state-of-the-art robust matrix recovery methods.
* **Discussion of Noise:** Theorem 2 addresses noisy data, but the discussion feels somewhat brief. A more detailed analysis of the impact of noise on performance and a sensitivity analysis of the noise level parameter (ε) would be valuable.

**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound, with a clear theoretical foundation and supporting experimental results. However, the theoretical bounds could be tighter, and the assumptions more realistic.
* **Clarity (9/10):** The paper is well-written and organized. The authors do a good job of explaining the technical concepts and motivating their approach.
* **Originality (7/10):** The idea of using a learned dictionary within the LRR framework to address coherence issues is novel and contributes to the ongoing research in robust matrix recovery. However, the underlying components (RPCA, LRR) are established techniques.
* **Significance (8/10):** The problem addressed is significant, and the proposed solution has the potential to be widely applicable in various domains dealing with noisy and structured data. The empirical results suggest a demonstrable improvement over existing methods.



**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a relevant and important problem in robust matrix recovery.
* Provides a theoretically grounded solution with practical implementation.
* Demonstrates clear performance improvements over a strong baseline (RPCA).
* Well-written and clearly organized.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Theoretical assumptions are restrictive.
* Theoretical bounds are loose.
* Limited exploration of dictionary learning techniques.



**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. I recommend the authors address the following points in a revised version:

* **Relax Theoretical Assumptions:** Investigate the sensitivity of the approach to deviations from the condition PUA(U0) = U0 and discuss potential alternatives for dictionary initialization in cases where this condition is not met.
* **Discuss Bound Tightness:** Provide a discussion on the tightness of the theoretical bounds and, if possible, explore opportunities for improvement.
* **Expand Experimental Evaluation:** Include a more comprehensive experimental evaluation with a wider range of datasets and comparison with other relevant methods.
* **Detail Noise Analysis:**  Provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of noise and the sensitivity of the algorithm to the noise level parameter.



This paper offers a valuable contribution to the field. Addressing the minor revisions outlined above would further strengthen its impact and solidify its place as a significant piece of research.
