- Paper studies recovery of low-rank L0 from gross corruption X = L0 + S0 when L0 is coherent/clustered, showing RPCA fails as µ2 grows.  
- Proposes LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary A: min_Z,S ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S, and shows recovery can avoid dependence on µ2 if U0 ⊂ UA.  
- Main theoretical result: exact and stable recovery possible when A is low-rank, column-normalized, and contains L0’s column space; only µ1(A) matters.  
- Provides a noisy-data stability theorem analogous to the exact recovery result.  
- Practical unsupervised algorithm: two-stage bootstrap—RPCA estimate, truncate SVD to get A, normalize columns, then run LRR.  
- Experiments: synthetic clustered subspaces and corrupted motion trajectories show significant gains over RPCA in exact recovery and downstream clustering error.  
- Relates to prior work: builds on RPCA/PCP and LRR, extending guarantees from A = X to general low-rank dictionaries and decoupling from µ2.  
- Strength: addresses a real failure mode (µ2-phenomenon) relevant in vision and trajectories.  
- Strength: clear sufficient theoretical conditions that avoid µ2 dependence and relax rank bounds compared to PCP.  
- Strength: simple, practical dictionary-learning pipeline with complexity benefits when A is low-rank.  
- Strength: extensive synthetic study and motion segmentation experiments demonstrating improved downstream accuracy.  
- Weakness: circularity—dictionary learning relies on RPCA; unclear if Algorithm 1 yields U0 ⊂ UA when RPCA fails under high µ2.  
- Weakness: theorems assume random uniform support for S0; robustness to structured corruptions (block/missing/column-sparse) is unclear.  
- Weakness: lack of formal characterization linking µ2 to number of clusters, subspace dims, and sampling; only empirical evidence provided.  
- Weakness: noisy-theorem not thoroughly validated—experiments mostly use gross corruptions and imputation rather than true dense noise or PΩ modeling.  
- Weakness: motion data preprocessing (±1 imputation) is nonstandard and may bias results; missing-data formulation would be fairer.  
- Weakness: missing sensitivity/ablation studies for λ, SVD truncation threshold, column normalization, iterative refinements, and runtime claims.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit PCP variants for specific corruption models and nonconvex/factorized RPCA baselines.  
- Quality: theoretical claims plausible but full proofs are deferred and should be in the supplement for NeurIPS.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written but needs explicit randomness assumptions, verifiability discussion of U0 ⊂ UA, and minor typos fixed.  
- Originality: novelty in providing guarantees for LRR with general low-rank dictionaries that avoid µ2; bootstrapping dictionary learning is practical but incremental.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for theory and practice if empirical backing and analysis of learned dictionary validity are strengthened.  
- Questions for authors include formal bounds for µ2 vs cluster count, conditions ensuring U0 ⊂ UA from RPCA output, performance under structured corruptions/missingness (PΩ), and sensitivity to λ/SVD truncation.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important RPCA failure mode, offers a clear theoretical advance and a simple effective algorithm.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on RPCA for dictionary learning in hard regimes, nonstandard experimental choices, limited baselines, and missing proofs.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on providing full proofs and strengthening empirical evaluation (handle missing data without ad hoc imputation and add sensitivity/ablation studies).
