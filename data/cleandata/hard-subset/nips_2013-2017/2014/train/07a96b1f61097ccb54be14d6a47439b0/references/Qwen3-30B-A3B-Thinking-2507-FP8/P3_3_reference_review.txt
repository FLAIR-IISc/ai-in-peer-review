

# NIPS Review: "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

## Summary and Relation to Previous Work
This paper addresses a critical limitation of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) when dealing with coherent data - specifically, data exhibiting clustering structures that increase coherence parameters like μ₂. The authors demonstrate that RPCA's performance degrades as clustering increases (the "μ₂-phenomenon"), and propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to avoid this issue. The work builds on the LRR framework [13] but makes a novel theoretical contribution by showing that LRR with a low-rank dictionary can circumvent coherence parameters that RPCA depends on. This extends the theoretical understanding of RPCA's limitations beyond the standard coherence assumptions.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Insight**: The paper makes a significant contribution by revealing that coherence parameters are not arbitrary assumptions but are intrinsically related to extra data structures (like clustering). This deepens our understanding of why RPCA fails in certain practical scenarios, which is a valuable theoretical contribution.

2. **Practical Algorithm**: The proposed Algorithm 1 is simple, effective, and computationally efficient (only 1.2x more expensive than RPCA). The unsupervised dictionary construction method is particularly well-motivated and practical.

3. **Strong Experimental Validation**: The experiments are comprehensive, showing clear improvements over RPCA on both synthetic data (47% wider success area) and real motion sequences (40% relative error reduction in clustering). The Hopkins155 database results are particularly convincing for real-world applicability.

4. **Clear Motivation**: The paper effectively demonstrates through Figure 2 that clustering structures increase μ₂, directly linking data structure to RPCA's failure mode - a connection previously unexplored in the literature.

## Weaknesses and Concerns

1. **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art**: While the paper compares to RPCA and LRR with A=X, it omits comparison to more recent robust low-rank recovery methods like Robust Subspace Clustering (RSC) [14], Outlier Pursuit [24], or the more recent OR-PCA [25]. This weakens the significance claim, as the paper doesn't demonstrate superiority over other modern approaches.

2. **Theoretical Assumption**: The condition P_UA(U₀) = U₀ (U₀ is a subspace of U_A) is crucial for the theoretical guarantees. The paper claims Algorithm 1 satisfies this condition, but doesn't provide evidence that the dictionary construction actually ensures this relationship in practice, especially for complex data.

3. **Limited Failure Analysis**: The paper doesn't explore the failure cases of their approach. For instance, how does the method perform when the data has complex structures beyond simple clustering, or when the dictionary construction from RPCA is poor?

4. **Theoretical Bound Comparison**: While the paper claims the rank bound is looser (O(n₂/log n₁) vs RPCA's O(n₂/(log n₁)²)), this is only marginally better in practice, and the paper doesn't clearly demonstrate how this translates to practical performance gains.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable theoretical contribution to understanding RPCA's limitations and provides a practical solution. The connection between coherence parameters and data structures is insightful, and the experimental results convincingly demonstrate the method's superiority in relevant scenarios. The paper's main weakness is the limited comparison to more recent methods, which is particularly important given the rapid evolution of robust low-rank recovery techniques.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper makes a clear, significant contribution to the field that aligns well with NIPS' focus on theoretical and practical advances in machine learning. The minor weaknesses can be addressed by:
1. Adding a comparison to 2-3 recent state-of-the-art robust recovery methods
2. Providing more detailed analysis of how the dictionary construction satisfies the theoretical assumption P_UA(U₀) = U₀
3. Including a brief discussion of the method's limitations

The paper's insights about coherence parameters being related to data structures (not just theoretical artifacts) are particularly valuable for the community, as this understanding can guide future work on robust low-rank recovery. The practical algorithm is simple, effective, and well-validated, making this a meaningful contribution to the field.

**Summary of Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Identifies and solves an important practical limitation of RPCA
- Provides deep theoretical insight about coherence parameters
- Offers a simple, effective, and efficient algorithm
- Demonstrates significant improvements in both synthetic and real-world experiments
- Well-motivated and clearly written

**Summary of Arguments for Rejection:**
- Limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods
- Theoretical assumption lacks practical verification
- Limited discussion of failure cases

The paper's strengths substantially outweigh its weaknesses, and it makes a clear contribution to the field that would be valuable to the NIPS community.
