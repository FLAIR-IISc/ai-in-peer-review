- Identifies RPCA's limitation: clustering structures increase coherence parameters (µ2, µ3) and degrade RPCA performance.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A and condition U0 ⊂ UA to avoid reliance on coherence parameters.  
- Provides Algorithm 1 that uses RPCA to construct the LRR dictionary in practice.  
- Theorems 1–2 prove LRR with a low-rank dictionary can avoid coherence parameters under stated conditions.  
- Theoretical rank bound provided: rank(L0) ≤ O(n2 / log n1).  
- Empirical results: synthetic experiments show a 47% wider success area; motion data shows ~40% relative improvement in clustering error.  
- Gives physical interpretation: coherence parameters relate to clustering structure, not just theoretical assumptions.  
- Novelty: formally analyzes the µ2-phenomenon and the clustering–coherence connection.  
- Theoretical contribution: builds on RPCA theory to derive new guarantees for LRR with low-rank dictionaries.  
- Practical algorithm: simple, intuitive, and claimed to be only ~1.2× the runtime of RPCA.  
- Strong experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world data.  
- Terminology concern: calling datasets "coherent data" is ambiguous and should be clarified.  
- Sensitivity concern: paper lacks analysis of performance when the estimated dictionary does not perfectly contain U0.  
- Scalability concern: unclear how the method performs on extremely large-scale (e.g., billion-scale) matrices.  
- Related work: insufficient contextualization relative to other structured low-rank recovery approaches (e.g., Zhou et al. NIPS 2015).  
- Dictionary construction issue: column normalization in Algorithm 1 may not be optimal for all data types; alternatives should be discussed.  
- Bound tightness: the provided rank bound is looser than RPCA's; discussion on whether tighter bounds are possible is missing.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound, well-supported theory and experiments.  
- Clarity assessment: well-written and organized, but should define "coherent data" earlier.  
- Originality assessment: novel angle on dictionary learning for coherent/clustering data despite building on RPCA and LRR.  
- Significance: addresses a practical limitation of RPCA with an easy-to-integrate, effective method.  
- Recommendations: clarify terminology, expand limitations and scalability discussion, compare to related work, analyze normalization choices, and consider extensions for more complex structures.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful, well-supported contribution; minor revisions recommended.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Acceptance arguments: identifies real RPCA limitation, supplies theory and a practical algorithm, and shows strong empirical gains.  
- Minor counterarguments: terminology confusion, limited discussion of limitations and related work, and a looser theoretical bound.
