- Overall: Above-average paper that convincingly addresses RPCA failures on coherent (clustered) data and merits acceptance with minor revisions.
- Main idea: Use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully chosen low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence problems that break standard RPCA.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA (Candès et al.) and LRR (Liu et al.), extending LRR by linking coherence, clustering, and dictionary design.
- Insight: Reframes coherence not as a nuisance assumption but as a signal of underlying clustering structure that should be modeled.
- Theoretical contribution: Provides a sufficient-condition theorem showing how a low-rank dictionary enables recovery, though largely incremental on existing LRR theory.
- Practical algorithm: Proposes Algorithm 1 for unsupervised dictionary learning using RPCA for initialization.
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-world (motion sequence) results show clear improvement over RPCA, especially on motion data.
- Clarity: Paper is very well-written, well-organized, and uses clear notation.
- Originality/significance: Novel insight on coherence and dictionary design; significance is high for robust PCA applications despite moderate theoretical novelty.
- Weakness — theory: Theoretical results are limited and mainly extend existing LRR analyses rather than presenting a breakthrough.
- Weakness — dictionary learning: Algorithm 1 is heuristic (RPCA-based); more sophisticated dictionary learning could improve performance.
- Weakness — parameters: Experimental choice of λ and noise ε lacks sensitivity analysis or detailed justification.
- Weakness — noise model: Theorems focus on noiseless or small-noise regimes and do not fully address realistic noise scenarios.
- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem, offers theoretical justification, provides empirical validation, and is clearly presented.
- Against acceptance: Main theoretical advances are incremental and the dictionary learning approach is heuristic.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: Add parameter selection/sensitivity discussion, mention or explore more advanced dictionary learning options, and acknowledge theoretical limitations (noise assumptions) with future directions.
