- Addresses robust low-rank matrix recovery under coherent data and proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary to mitigate coherence effects.  
- Novelty: connects data coherence to underlying structure (e.g., clustering) and shows a low-rank dictionary avoids harmful µ2 dependence.  
- Builds on RPCA and LRR literature, expanding understanding of LRR with general dictionaries.  
- Clear motivation: RPCA fails on coherent data; introduces the "µ2-phenomenon" to explain clustering-induced coherence.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorems 1 and 2 give recovery conditions for LRR with a low-rank dictionary in noiseless and noisy settings.  
- Practical algorithm: simple dictionary-learning approach using RPCA as an initialization.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic and real motion-data experiments show substantial improvements over RPCA.  
- Insight: reframes coherence as reflecting data structure rather than an arbitrary assumption.  
- Well-written and well-organized presentation.  
- Simplicity concern: the dictionary-learning algorithm may be too reliant on the initial RPCA solution; more advanced refinement could help.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: limited exploration of λ choice and other RPCA hyperparameters.  
- Limited experiments: needs broader application domains (e.g., images, documents) where coherence matters.  
- Strong theoretical assumptions: relies on conditions like PUA(U0)=U0; lacks discussion of robustness to violations.  
- Overstated claim: asserting success probability ≥ RPCA is too strong without nuance since RPCA can succeed outside the theorem’s conditions.  
- Overall assessment: solid, significant contribution with convincing theory and experiments, deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, offers theoretical guarantees, shows empirical gains, provides conceptual insights, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: simplistic dictionary learning, limited hyperparameter study, narrow experimental scope, and potentially restrictive theoretical assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
