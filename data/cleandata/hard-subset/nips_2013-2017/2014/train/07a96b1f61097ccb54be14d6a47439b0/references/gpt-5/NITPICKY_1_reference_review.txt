Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies robust recovery of a low-rank matrix L0 and a sparse corruption S0 from X = L0 + S0 when L0 is coherent (high coherence parameters), a regime where RPCA/PCP may fail. It argues that clustering (union-of-subspaces) structure inflates µ2 (and µ3), degrading RPCA performance (“µ2-phenomenon”). The main proposal is to use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A, solving minZ,S ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S. Theorems claim that if A is low-rank, column-normalized, and its column space contains that of L0 (U0 ⊂ UA), then exact recovery holds without dependence on µ2, with noisy analog giving stability bounds. An unsupervised two-stage algorithm first runs RPCA to estimate L0, constructs a normalized low-rank dictionary A from that estimate, and then runs LRR. Experiments on synthetic matrices and corrupted motion sequences suggest improvements over RPCA, and downstream gains for subspace clustering.

This work builds on PCP/RPCA (Candès et al.) and LRR (Liu et al.). The idea of general dictionaries in LRR is known; here the contribution is the theoretical condition “low-rank A with U0 ⊂ UA avoids µ2” and a simple dictionary-learning heuristic.

Strengths
- Addresses an important failure mode of RPCA (coherence) tied to realistic cluster structure.
- Clear conceptual message: factorization via AZ with appropriately chosen A can mitigate µ2 dependence.
- The noiseless and noisy theorems are intuitively plausible and align with empirical observations; the condition U0 ⊂ UA is natural for exact recovery.
- Simple practical algorithm; minimal parameter tuning (λ = 1/√n1).
- Experiments indicate improved recovery regions and downstream clustering accuracy.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical/theoretical
- Key assumptions are strong and practicality is unclear: Theorems require U0 ⊂ UA and column-wise unit normalization; no guarantees are provided for the proposed two-stage estimator to produce such an A when RPCA is significantly degraded by coherence (the paper asserts RPCA is “not brittle” but provides no quantitative bound).
- Randomness and probability statements are under-specified. Theorems claim success with probability ≥ 1 − n^−101, but the distributional assumptions (e.g., support Ω drawn uniformly, signs of S0 entries, independence) are not stated with precision in the theorem statements. As written, they appear to be deterministic statements with probabilistic conclusions.
- Only µ2 is “avoided”; µ1(A) still appears in the bounds (rank(A) ≤ ε^2 n2 / (ca µ1(A) log n1)). Since A is constructed from L̂0, µ1(A) may remain large in row-clustered scenarios; the limits of this are not analyzed.
- Claims like “O(n^2/log n1) is probably the finest” lack citations or a formal lower bound. Several constants (e.g., 8√mn in the noisy bounds) are loose and unmotivated.
- Several results and key explanations are deferred to a “full paper” (e.g., proof of µ2-phenomenon, proofs of the theorems). For NeurIPS, the submission should be self-contained.

Algorithmic/practical
- The two-stage algorithm’s success hinges on the quality of the initial RPCA when coherence is high—the very regime where RPCA is weak. No ablations quantify how often U0 ⊂ UA holds after Step 1, nor sensitivity to the rank threshold r̂0 = #{i : σi > 10^-3 σ1}.
- Setting ε = 10^-6||X||F in the noisy program is arbitrary; no sensitivity analysis is shown. Similarly, fixing λ = 1/√n1 for general A is inherited from PCP with A = I and may be suboptimal; there is no guidance or analysis on λ for general dictionaries.
- Computational complexity claims are cursory. Stating O(n^3) for RPCA vs O(n^2 rA) for LRR ignores dependence on m, d, operator norms, and the cost of SVD/prox steps. No wall-clock times or solver details are provided.
- Normalizing columns of L̃0 to form A may distort scaling and conditioning; the rationale is thin, and failure modes (e.g., near-zero columns) are not discussed.

Experiments
- Synthetic setup: L0 generated from a union of 5 subspaces is appropriate, but details are insufficient (distribution of subspaces, sampling per subspace, exact corruption model). “Success area 47% wider” is a coarse metric; no confidence intervals, no curves vs. µ2, and figures are missing. Provide numerical success rates and clarify the success criterion.
- Motion sequences: Replacing missing entries by Bernoulli ±1 is unrealistic and conflates missing-data modeling with sparse corruption. Proper formulations use masking operators; comparing to robust matrix completion or RPCA-with-missing entries would be more appropriate. Dataset details are scant (which 11 sequences, preprocessing, tracking errors). Table 1 is referenced but not included; hyperparameters for SSC/LRRx/SIM are omitted.
- Baselines: Comparisons are limited. Consider PCP variants addressing coherence (e.g., weighted PCP/IRLS, nonconvex surrogates), Outlier Pursuit (column-sparse), GoDec, or recent robust matrix completion methods that relax incoherence assumptions. Also compare to LRR with A = X and other dictionary choices.

Clarity/presentation
- The paper is difficult to verify without included figures/tables (Figures 1–4, Table 1 not present).
- Numerous typos/grammar: “Besides of”, “column-wisely”, “need be”, “as equal”, “PRCA” (typo), subject-verb agreement issues. Step numbering in Algorithm 1 has a formatting error (“. Estimate”).
- Notation: n1, n2 are defined but mixed in statements; µ3 discussion is vague. Define all assumptions in theorem statements.
- Several claims are promotional (“for the first time”) though LRR with general dictionaries has been studied; temper and better situate the novelty.

References/formatting
- Many references are incomplete (missing years/venues/pages), inconsistent capitalization, and lack of precise citations for key claims. Please fix formatting to NeurIPS standards and add missing related work.

Questions for the authors
- Under what quantitative conditions on RPCA’s initial error does your two-stage algorithm guarantee U0 ⊂ UA with high probability? Can you provide a perturbation analysis?
- How sensitive are results to the rank threshold 10^-3 σ1? Any adaptive or information criteria?
- Can you handle missing entries properly (mask operator) and report results? How does your method integrate with robust matrix completion?
- How does performance vary with the number of clusters and µ2 explicitly? Please include plots vs. k and µ2.
- What is the effect of λ and ε choices for general A?

Pros
- Addresses an important and realistic limitation of RPCA.
- Clear conceptual insight; simple practical algorithm.
- Promising empirical improvements.

Cons
- Strong assumptions with limited justification for the practical algorithm; missing theoretical guarantees for learned A.
- Insufficient experimental rigor and missing figures/tables; unrealistic handling of missing data.
- Incomplete proofs and reliance on a “full paper”; clarity/formatting issues.

Recommendation
Given the combination of promising ideas with substantial theoretical gaps for the proposed unsupervised procedure, under-specified assumptions, limited baselines, and significant clarity/formatting issues, I recommend rejection at this stage. With a self-contained presentation (full proofs or at least precise assumptions), rigorous experiments (including proper missing data handling and stronger baselines), and analysis of the learned dictionary’s guarantees, this work could be a strong contribution.

Confidence: High.
