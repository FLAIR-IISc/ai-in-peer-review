- Paper identifies a µ2-phenomenon: RPCA fails for coherent low-rank matrices when columns form multiple clusters, causing µ2 (and µ3) to grow with cluster count.  
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A whose column space contains U0 to remove dependence on µ2 in recovery guarantees.  
- Proves exact/noiseless and near-exact/noisy recovery bounds for LRR with low-rank A, yielding a looser rank bound r(L0) ≤ O(n2/log n1)/µ1(A).  
- Practical unsupervised pipeline: run RPCA to get L̂0, build a low-rank column-normalized dictionary Â from L̂0, then run LRR with Â.  
- Experiments on synthetic union-of-subspaces data and corrupted motion sequences show improved recovery and clustering over RPCA and several baselines.  
- Theoretical contributions are meaningful but the probabilistic assumptions (“with probability ≥ 1 − n−10”) lack precise specification of randomness (support/signs).  
- The key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong; authors acknowledge it and rely on a heuristic to approximate it in practice.  
- Algorithm is simple and plausible; claimed ~1.2× overhead lacks concrete timing, solver details, and stopping criteria.  
- Experimental evaluation shows notable gains but is limited in scope (small real dataset) and misses ablations (λ/ε sensitivity, iterative refinements).  
- Missing comparisons to other robust PCA variants (Outlier Pursuit, column-sparse models, nonconvex methods) weaken empirical claims.  
- Paper is generally well organized with clear motivation and notation; some proofs deferred and a few references appear incomplete.  
- Originality: novel theoretical analysis of LRR with general low-rank dictionaries in the coherent regime and a useful bootstrap algorithm.  
- Significance: addresses a realistic failure mode of RPCA and shows that structure-aware dictionaries can neutralize harmful coherence, relevant for subspace clustering and vision.  
- Pros: clear identification of µ2 failure mode, new LRR guarantees avoiding µ2, simple practical algorithm, and empirical validation.  
- Cons: strong theoretical assumption, underspecified probabilistic model, limited experiments and missing baselines/ablations, and some incomplete references.  
- Suggestions: clarify randomness assumptions in theorems, provide solver/runtime/code details, add ablations (λ/ε, dictionary rank, iterations), test robustness when RPCA fails, and compare to more robust PCA baselines.  
- Overall rating: 7 (weak accept); Reviewer confidence: 3/5.
