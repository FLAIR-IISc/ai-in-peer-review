

# Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

## Summary and Relation to Prior Work

This paper addresses a limitation of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) when dealing with coherent data, specifically data exhibiting clustering structures. The authors demonstrate that clustering structures increase the second coherence parameter (µ2), which degrades RPCA's performance as shown in Equation (3.7). They propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a properly constructed dictionary matrix to avoid this issue, showing theoretically that a low-rank dictionary containing the column space of the true low-rank matrix L0 can recover data without being affected by coherence parameters. The paper contributes a theoretical analysis of coherence parameters' relationship with data structures, a new theoretical guarantee for LRR with low-rank dictionaries, and a practical unsupervised algorithm for dictionary construction.

This work extends the RPCA literature [4] and LRR [13] by addressing a specific limitation of RPCA that has not been thoroughly explored. While RPCA has been widely adopted, its performance degradation with coherent data (particularly due to clustering) has not been systematically analyzed. The authors build on the coherence framework established in [4], but provide new insights into the physical meaning of coherence parameters.

## Strengths

1. **Problem Identification**: The paper successfully identifies a meaningful limitation of RPCA that is relevant to real-world data with clustering structures, which is not adequately addressed in current literature.

2. **Theoretical Insights**: The connection between clustering structures and coherence parameters (µ2-phenomenon) is well-motivated and theoretically grounded. The paper provides valuable insights into the physical meaning of coherence parameters, showing they are not just "assumptions" but relate to intrinsic data structures.

3. **Practical Algorithm**: The proposed unsupervised algorithm (Algorithm 1) is simple, practical, and demonstrates significant improvements over RPCA on both synthetic and real-world motion data (Hopkins155 database), with a 40% relative improvement in clustering error rates.

4. **Experimental Validation**: The experiments are well-designed, covering both random matrix generation and real-world motion sequences, and provide convincing evidence of the proposed method's superiority over RPCA in the coherent data regime.

## Weaknesses

1. **Incomplete Theoretical Analysis**: The theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2) are presented without sufficient detail in the submitted paper. The paper refers to "the full paper" for detailed proofs, which is not acceptable for a conference submission. The reviewers should be able to evaluate the theoretical claims without needing to consult additional material.

2. **Lack of Comparison with Recent Work**: The paper does not sufficiently compare with recent advances in robust matrix recovery, such as [24, 25, 26] which propose alternative approaches to handling coherent data. This weakens the novelty claim and makes it difficult to assess the relative contribution.

3. **Algorithm Dependency on RPCA**: The proposed unsupervised algorithm relies on RPCA as a first step. While the paper argues that RPCA is not "brittle" with coherent data (though its performance is depressed), this creates a potential circular dependency: RPCA may fail to provide a good initial estimate when coherence is high, which is precisely the regime where the proposed method is needed.

4. **Limited Discussion of Practical Constraints**: The paper does not thoroughly discuss practical limitations, such as how the rank of the dictionary matrix A should be chosen in practice, or how sensitive the algorithm is to parameter choices beyond λ = 1/√n1.

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution by identifying a specific limitation of RPCA related to coherent data with clustering structures, and proposing a theoretically grounded solution using LRR with a low-rank dictionary. The theoretical insights about coherence parameters are particularly compelling, and the experimental results demonstrate significant practical improvements.

However, the incomplete theoretical presentation and lack of comparison with recent work prevent this from being a strong NIPS submission. The paper would benefit from:
1. Providing complete theoretical proofs in the submission
2. Adding a thorough comparison with recent robust matrix recovery methods
3. Addressing the dependency of the algorithm on RPCA's initial estimate
4. Discussing practical implementation considerations more thoroughly

## Recommendation

The paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to robust matrix recovery. While the core idea is sound and the empirical results are convincing, the incomplete theoretical analysis and insufficient comparison with recent work are significant weaknesses that need to be addressed. The authors should strengthen the theoretical presentation, add a comparison with recent methods, and address the dependency on RPCA.

I recommend **weak acceptance** with the understanding that the authors must substantially revise the paper to address the theoretical completeness and comparison issues before acceptance. The paper has strong potential but requires significant improvements to meet NIPS standards.
