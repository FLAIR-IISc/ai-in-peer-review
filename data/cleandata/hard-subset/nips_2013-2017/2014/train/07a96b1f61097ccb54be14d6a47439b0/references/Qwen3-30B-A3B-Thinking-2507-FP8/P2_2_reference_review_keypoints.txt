- Identifies that RPCA fails on coherent/clustering data because clustering increases coherence (µ₂-phenomenon).
- Shows theoretically that µ₂ grows with the number of clusters, explaining RPCA breakdown.
- Proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary A containing U₀ (U₀ ⊂ U_A) to recover L₀ and S₀ without coherence assumptions.
- Provides Algorithm 1 to learn the dictionary in an unsupervised way.
- First theoretical analysis of LRR with general dictionaries for handling coherent data, extending prior LRR work.
- Theorems 1 and 2 give rigorous exact and near-exact recovery guarantees independent of coherence parameters.
- Experiments (synthetic and motion segmentation) demonstrate clear empirical gains (≈40% relative error reduction).
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and figures effectively illustrate key concepts like the µ₂-phenomenon.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and present a practical algorithm applicable to real data.
- Lacks theoretical analysis of robustness when the learned dictionary A only approximates U₀ (dictionary errors).
- Scalability concerns: dictionary construction may be expensive for very large matrices and no large-scale experiments are shown.
- Could better position and compare to other recent robust PCA methods addressing coherence (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, Outlier-Robust PCA).
- Overall assessment: significant, original, and practically relevant contribution to low-rank recovery.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Suggested improvements: compare to recent coherence-aware RPCA methods, provide scalability/complexity experiments for larger matrices, and add theory on tolerance to dictionary approximation errors.
