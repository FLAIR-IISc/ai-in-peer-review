- Paper studies robust low-rank recovery for coherent L0 and highlights a µ2-phenomenon that degrades RPCA/PCP guarantees for union-of-subspaces/clustered data.
- Authors analyze LRR with a general low-rank dictionary A and prove recovery guarantees that avoid dependence on µ2, instead depending on µ1(A).
- They propose an unsupervised pipeline: run RPCA to estimate L0, build a low-rank column-normalized dictionary A, then run LRR to refine recovery.
- Experiments on synthetic data and corrupted motion trajectories show improvements over RPCA and reduced downstream clustering errors.
- Strength: clearly articulates a practical RPCA failure mode under clustered data and connects coherence to intrinsic data structure.
- Strength: provides theoretical contribution for LRR with general low-rank dictionaries and exact/noisy recovery guarantees under sparsity assumptions.
- Strength: simple, practical algorithm leveraging RPCA to construct A with empirical gains and demonstrated impact on subspace clustering.
- Weakness: key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong and effectively requires the dictionary to already capture the ground-truth column space.
- Weakness: the unsupervised construction depends on RPCA to estimate U0, which is exactly where RPCA struggles; no formal bootstrapping/perturbation analysis is provided.
- Weakness: theoretical bounds depend on µ1(A), which could be large if A is built from estimated L̂0 in clustered settings; µ1(A) is not measured empirically.
- Weakness: noisy recovery bound scales as O(√mn ε), which may be loose in high dimensions and is not compared to PCP’s noisy bounds or improved constants.
- Weakness: experiments are limited—do not systematically vary number of clusters, subspace angles, or sample imbalance to validate the µ2-phenomenon and theory.
- Weakness: motion experiment uses unrealistic Bernoulli ±1 imputation for missing entries and omits comparisons to robust matrix completion or RPCA-with-missing-data baselines.
- Weakness: parameter choices (λ = 1/√n1, rank threshold 10−3σ1) are ad hoc and sensitivity/robustness analyses are missing.
- Weakness: some claims are deferred to a full paper (e.g., formal µ2 growth analysis); the submission lacks precise statements or proof sketches for these.
- Weakness: minor clarity problems and typos (e.g., “PRCA”, “column-wisely”), incomplete references, and an unanalyzed conditioning motivation for Steps 2–3.
- Quality: main theorems are plausible and align with convex analysis for LRR/PCP, but rely on strong assumptions and lack theoretical backing for the unsupervised pipeline.
- Clarity: paper is generally readable and organized but needs to include deferred statements, clearer proofs, and consistent notation.
- Originality: identifying coherence inflation from clustering and proposing LRR with low-rank dictionaries that avoid µ2 is novel and timely.
- Significance: moderate—addresses a real RPCA limitation with a conceptually clean remedy, but needs stronger unsupervised guarantees and broader evaluation.
- Suggested improvement: provide perturbation analysis quantifying recovery when A approximately spans U0 and formalize the “no worse than RPCA” claim.
- Suggested improvement: empirically report µ1(A), µ2(L0), µ3 before/after dictionary construction and vary clusters, subspace angles, and imbalance.
- Suggested improvement: compare to additional baselines (robust matrix completion, RPCA-with-missing-data, feature-based methods, sampling schemes).
- Suggested improvement: study sensitivity to λ and rank threshold, include ablations on normalization and conditioning heuristics, and report runtimes.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a real RPCA limitation, offers theoretically interesting LRR results, and presents a simple algorithm with demonstrated empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: strong theoretical assumptions, lack of guarantees for the learned dictionary, limited/unrealistic experiments, and presentation issues.
- Overall: promising and relevant work that would benefit from strengthened analysis for the learned dictionary and broader, more diagnostic experiments.
