Summary
The paper addresses a real limitation of RPCA/PCP: its reliance on incoherence, which degrades in the presence of common “extra structures” such as clustering of data points within the low-rank subspace. The authors articulate and empirically demonstrate the µ2-phenomenon: as the number of clusters increases (while global rank is fixed), the second coherence parameter µ2 grows, tightening RPCA’s conditions and degrading performance.

To mitigate this, the paper studies Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a general dictionary A (beyond the commonly analyzed A = X case) and proves that if A is low-rank and its column space contains the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA), then exact/near-exact recovery is possible without invoking µ2 (Theorems 1–2). This cleanly separates the role of coherence and shows that a properly chosen low-rank dictionary can “absorb” the structured row-space variability that inflates µ2.

Building on this, the authors propose a simple unsupervised algorithm: run RPCA to obtain an estimate of L0, rank-truncate and column-normalize it to form a well-conditioned low-rank dictionary Â, then solve LRR with Â. Experiments on synthetic data and motion segmentation (Hopkins155 with injected corruptions) show consistent improvements over RPCA, and down-stream clustering error reductions when using the recovered matrices.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: Clear identification of how clustering induces increased µ2 and a principled route to avoid µ2 via a low-rank dictionary that spans U0. This reframes coherence as a property induced by extra structures, not just a technical assumption.
- Theoretical contribution: Recovery guarantees for LRR with general low-rank dictionaries that do not depend on µ2, both noiseless and noisy, with explicit conditions and sharp scaling (O(n2/log n1) vs. O(n2/(log n1)2) in RPCA).
- Practical algorithm: A simple, scalable two-stage approach that leverages RPCA as a bootstrap to learn a dictionary, and yields tangible gains with modest additional computation.
- Empirical validation: Synthetic experiments show a substantial expansion of the success region; motion data experiments show meaningful downstream benefits (∼40% relative error reduction for SSC).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Strength of assumptions: The condition U0 ⊂ UA is necessary for exactness but strong. The proposed algorithm has no formal guarantee when RPCA is imperfect; a robustness analysis (e.g., with subspace angle bounds between U0 and UA) would strengthen the story.
- Limited experimental breadth: Comparisons are mostly to RPCA. It would be informative to compare to alternatives that mitigate coherence or exploit structure (e.g., IRLS-type RPCA, variants with adaptive incoherence, Outlier Pursuit for column-sparse corruptions, nonconvex factorizations).
- Sensitivity and design choices: The rank threshold (1e-3 σ1), column normalization, and fixed λ = 1/√n1 are chosen heuristically. A sensitivity study would help, as would guidance for λ when A is not unit-normed or when noise levels vary.
- Clarity/polish: Some typos (e.g., “PRCA”), occasional notational overloading, and reliance on a “full paper” for proofs. Including sketch proofs or clearer intuition for why low-rank A neutralizes µ2 would improve readability.

Quality
- The theorems are plausible and align with known RPCA/LRR analyses, but full proofs are deferred. The algorithm is well-specified and experiments are convincing though not exhaustive. Claims about complexity (O(n2 rA) vs O(n3)) are reasonable; reporting wall-clock times would help.

Clarity
- Generally well-written with a cogent narrative connecting coherence to clustering. Some sections could benefit from tighter exposition (especially around the intuition for avoiding µ2 and the role of column normalization). The notation section is thorough.

Originality
- High. While LRR is not new, the theoretical treatment for general low-rank dictionaries that provably avoids µ2, and the explicit connection between clustering and coherence, constitute a fresh and valuable angle. The simple RPCA→LRR pipeline is pragmatic and novel in this context.

Significance
- Significant. Many real datasets are clustered; making robust low-rank recovery work in the coherent regime is practically important. The ideas are likely to influence both theory (reframing coherence) and practice (dictionary-guided robust recovery).

Questions and requests for the authors
- Can you provide a robustness theorem when U0 is only approximately contained in UA (e.g., principal angles bounded), quantifying recovery error as a function of subspace misalignment?
- How sensitive is performance to the rank-threshold 1e-3 σ1 and to column normalization? Please include a brief sensitivity analysis.
- Guidance for λ when A’s columns are not unit-norm or when ε is not known?
- Could you report wall-clock run times and rA in experiments to substantiate the complexity claims?
- Comparisons to additional baselines (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, IRLS/Nonconvex RPCA) would strengthen the empirical case.

Minor suggestions
- Fix typos and unify notation (e.g., “RPCA,” “PCP,” “PRCA”).
- Briefly include the intuition for “why” low-rank A removes µ2 dependence (e.g., A projects onto U0, so the effective row-space variability that induces large µ2 is not penalized in Z).
- Release code and data splits for reproducibility.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel and insightful theoretical contribution (µ2 avoidance via low-rank dictionaries).
- Simple, practical algorithm with clear empirical gains.
- Addresses an important, underexplored failure mode of RPCA.

Cons for acceptance
- Strong assumptions with limited robustness analysis for the unsupervised algorithm.
- Experimental comparisons could be broader; some clarity issues remain.

Overall recommendation
- Accept (leaning to weak accept if proofs are only in the supplement). The paper introduces a compelling conceptual advance with practical impact, and the bold idea of sidestepping coherence via learned low-rank dictionaries is precisely the kind of direction worth encouraging, despite some incomplete validations.

Reviewer confidence
- Moderate: familiar with RPCA/LRR literature; would like to see full proofs and broader empirical baselines.
