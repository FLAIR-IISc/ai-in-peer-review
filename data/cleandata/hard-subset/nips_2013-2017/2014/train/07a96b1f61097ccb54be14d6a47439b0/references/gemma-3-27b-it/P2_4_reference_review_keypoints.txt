- Addresses RPCA’s limitation with coherent/clustering data and proposes mitigating it via a learned low-rank dictionary in the LRR framework.  
- Core idea: construct a low-rank dictionary reflecting data distribution to reduce effective coherence and improve recovery.  
- Connects coherence behavior to underlying data structure (clustering) and situates work relative to RPCA, LRR, matrix factorization, and dictionary learning.  
- Strength: correctly identifies a real weakness of RPCA for structured/coherent data.  
- Strength: intuitive, implementable approach—using a learned dictionary inside LRR to reduce coherence.  
- Strength: provides theoretical insight into coherence vs. data structure, even if limited.  
- Strength: generally clear writing and some supportive experiments, notably on motion sequences.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 relies on strong, restrictive assumptions (e.g., P_UA(U0)=U0, column-wise unit-norm dictionary) that limit practical applicability.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 for noisy data does not sufficiently relax or address those restrictive conditions.  
- Weakness: Experiments on synthetic data show only modest gains over RPCA; motion experiments are better but need broader, stronger comparisons.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 is essentially RPCA followed by LRR on the RPCA result, suggesting limited algorithmic novelty.  
- Weakness: Sections defining coherence parameters (µ1, µ2, µ3) are dense and notation-heavy, harming clarity.  
- Weakness: Related-work discussion is somewhat superficial; needs deeper comparison to existing dictionary-learning methods for RPCA.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 6/10.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important limitation, offers an intuitive solution, supplies theoretical connections, and shows some empirical improvement.  
- Against acceptance: restrictive theoretical assumptions, unconvincing experimental gains, incremental algorithmic contribution, and limited practical advance over state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on clarifying Theorem 1 assumptions, relaxing them if possible, and discussing practical implications.  
- Requested revisions: strengthen empirical validation with more datasets and baselines, analyze learned dictionaries in detail, simplify/cohere notation for coherence parameters, and expand related-work comparisons.
