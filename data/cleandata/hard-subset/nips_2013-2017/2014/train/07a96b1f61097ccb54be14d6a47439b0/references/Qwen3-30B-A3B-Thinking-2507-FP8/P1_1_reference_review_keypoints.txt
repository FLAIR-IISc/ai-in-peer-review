- Addresses RPCA's failure on clustered/coherent low-rank data and proposes LRR with a low-rank dictionary to overcome it.
- Key insight: if dictionary A is low-rank and its column space contains L0's column space, LRR can recover L0 without being harmed by coherence parameters.
- Identifies the "µ2-phenomenon": clustering increases coherence (µ2/µ3), degrading RPCA performance.
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1 and 2) showing LRR with a low-rank dictionary avoids coherence-based failures.
- Proposes a simple unsupervised algorithm that uses RPCA's output to construct the dictionary.
- Algorithm is computationally practical (≈1.2× cost of RPCA) and typically converges quickly.
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic data and Hopkins155 motion data demonstrate clear empirical superiority over RPCA.
- Demonstrates significant real-world impact: substantial reduction in clustering error (≈40% relative improvement).
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution linking data clustering to coherence parameters.
- Strength: technically sound proofs building on established matrix recovery theory.
- Strength: practical, efficient algorithm with careful computational analysis.
- Strength: robust experimental validation across controlled and real datasets.
- Weakness: limited experimental comparison with more recent robust low-rank recovery methods.
- Weakness: reliance on RPCA output to build the dictionary may fail in highly coherent settings, challenging true unsupervised use.
- Weakness: theoretical requirement PUA(U0) = U0 is strong and effectively requires supervision information.
- Weakness: the claim "LRR falls back to RPCA when A = I" is technically correct but potentially misleading given standard LRR typically uses A = X.
- Suggestion: include comparisons with more recent methods to better position contributions.
- Suggestion: clarify and empirically test dictionary construction in truly unsupervised or RPCA-failure scenarios.
- Suggestion: rephrase/clarify the A = I statement, noting standard LRR uses A = X.
- Suggestion: discuss handling of slower convergence cases beyond the typical two iterations.
- Suggestion: provide more intuition on why normalized columns of the rank-r̂0 approximation of L̂0 form a good dictionary.
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical and practical contribution that addresses a fundamental RPCA limitation.
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to comparisons and clarifications.
- Arguments for acceptance: reveals a fundamental RPCA limitation, gives rigorous guarantees, proposes a practical algorithm, and shows strong empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to recent methods, potentially unrealistic theoretical assumption, and a potentially misleading LRR-RPCA statement.
