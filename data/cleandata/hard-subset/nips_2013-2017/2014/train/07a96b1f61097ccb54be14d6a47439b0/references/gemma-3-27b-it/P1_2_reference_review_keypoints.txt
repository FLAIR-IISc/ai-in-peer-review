- Summary: Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary to overcome RPCA’s failures on coherent (clustered) data, with theory and an RPCA-informed dictionary-learning algorithm and strong empirical results.
- Relation to previous work: Builds on RPCA and LRR literature, situating contributions among robust PCA and matrix factorization methods and addressing known incoherence limitations.
- Strength: Addresses an important practical problem of coherence in low-rank recovery.
- Strength: Provides solid theoretical analysis (notably Theorem 1) showing conditions for successful recovery with LRR and a low-rank dictionary.
- Strength: Proposes a practical algorithm that uses RPCA output to construct the dictionary and discusses computational overhead.
- Strength: Empirical validation on synthetic and motion data shows clear improvements over RPCA.
- Strength: Clear explanation of coherence and the “µ2-phenomenon,” with well-written, organized presentation.
- Weakness: Dictionary-learning heuristic depends on RPCA output with limited justification; it’s unclear if RPCA is always the best starting point.
- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on specific assumptions (e.g., column-wise unit-norm dictionary) without sensitivity analysis for assumption violations.
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison to more recent or sophisticated robust PCA alternatives.
- Weakness: Theorem 2 and the noisy-data discussion are less developed than the noiseless case.
- Weakness: Some repetitive discussion of coherence parameters and clustering that could be streamlined.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with solid theory, a practical algorithm, and convincing experiments; minor issues do not outweigh contributions.
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles a real limitation of RPCA, offers theoretical grounding, practical algorithm, and compelling empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on an RPCA-based heuristic, potentially restrictive theoretical assumptions, and limited comparisons to newer methods.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
