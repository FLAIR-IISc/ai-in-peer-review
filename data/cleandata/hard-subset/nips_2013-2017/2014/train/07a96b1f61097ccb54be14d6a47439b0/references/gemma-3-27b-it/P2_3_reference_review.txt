## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of robust low-rank matrix recovery in the presence of coherent data, a scenario where standard Robust PCA (RPCA) struggles due to increasing coherence parameters linked to underlying data structures like clustering. The authors argue that coherence isn’t an inherent limitation but a reflection of these extra structures. They propose leveraging the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework with a carefully chosen, low-rank dictionary to mitigate the coherence issue, and provide theoretical justification and an algorithm utilizing RPCA for dictionary learning.  Experimental results on synthetic and motion data demonstrate improvements over RPCA.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of robust PCA, addressing a known limitation of RPCA and offering a practical solution. The core idea of linking coherence to data structure and using a tailored dictionary within the LRR framework is insightful. While the theoretical analysis is somewhat dense, the experimental validation is convincing.  I believe the paper meets the NIPS acceptance criteria, leaning towards a strong accept.

**Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper presents a theoretically grounded approach.  The theorems establishing conditions for recovery with the proposed LRR framework are a strong point.  However, the derivations are quite technical and could benefit from simplification or more intuitive explanations. The proof of Theorem 1, while mathematically sound, is not clearly linked to intuition. The experimental setup seems reasonable, and the results demonstrably outperform RPCA. The discussion of the limitations of the proposed method could be expanded.  The noisy data analysis in Theorem 2 is a welcome addition but feels somewhat brief.
* **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally well-written, but suffers from density and technical jargon. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and contextualizes it within existing work.  However, sections 3.1 and 3.2 (particularly the mathematical formulations) are challenging to follow without a strong background in matrix theory.  More intuitive explanations of the coherence parameters and their relationship to clustering would be beneficial.  The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is clear, though.  The notation is consistent, but the use of many different norms (six in total!) adds to the cognitive load.
* **Originality (8/10):**  While LRR itself isn't new, the specific insight of linking coherence to data structure and using a low-rank dictionary to address it *is* original. The connection between coherence and the underlying clustering structure is a novel contribution. The algorithm for dictionary learning, which leverages RPCA as a starting point, is a clever and practical approach.  The paper appropriately cites related work, acknowledging the foundations upon which it builds.
* **Significance (8/10):**  The problem of robust PCA in the presence of coherent data is important, as it extends the applicability of these techniques to a wider range of real-world datasets exhibiting structure beyond simple low-rankness. The proposed solution is practical and demonstrably improves performance.  The idea of tailoring the dictionary in LRR has broader implications for matrix factorization techniques.  The experiments on motion sequences provide a relevant and compelling application.

**Strengths:**

* Addresses a known limitation of RPCA.
* Provides theoretical justification for the proposed approach.
* Offers a practical algorithm with clear steps.
* Demonstrates improved performance over RPCA on both synthetic and real datasets.
* Insightful connection between coherence and data structure.

**Weaknesses:**

* Technical density and challenging mathematical derivations.
* Limited intuitive explanation of the coherence parameters.
*  Could benefit from a more thorough discussion of limitations and potential failure cases.
* No discussion of computational complexity beyond mentioning it is "not really double" that of RPCA. A more rigorous analysis would be helpful.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and insightful approach to addressing a significant problem in robust PCA.
* Solid theoretical foundation and convincing experimental validation.
* Practical algorithm with demonstrated improvements over existing methods.
* The work has the potential to impact researchers and practitioners working with high-dimensional data.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The technical complexity might limit accessibility for some readers.
* The theoretical analysis could be more streamlined and intuitively explained.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.**  This paper offers a valuable contribution to the field and is well-suited for presentation at NIPS.  I recommend the authors consider simplifying the mathematical derivations and providing more intuitive explanations to enhance clarity.  Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper.
