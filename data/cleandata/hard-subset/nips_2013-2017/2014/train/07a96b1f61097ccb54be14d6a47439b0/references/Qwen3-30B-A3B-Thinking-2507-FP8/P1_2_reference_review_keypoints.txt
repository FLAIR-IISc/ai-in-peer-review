- Paper identifies RPCA's limitation: coherent structures (clustering) increase coherence parameters (μ₂, μ₃) and degrade RPCA performance.  
- Establishes theoretical connection between clustering and rising coherence parameters ("μ₂-phenomenon").  
- Proposes using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A and condition U₀ ⊂ U_A to avoid harmful coherence effects.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1 and 2) for exact and near-exact recovery under stated conditions.  
- Offers a practical unsupervised Algorithm 1 that uses RPCA to construct a dictionary for LRR.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains: 47% wider success region in synthetic tests and ~40% relative improvement on Hopkins155 motion data.  
- Presents a novel interpretation that coherence parameters reflect extra data structure (e.g., clustering) beyond mere low-rankness.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive (4000 synthetic trials plus real-world experiments).  
- Theorem 1’s U₀ ⊂ U_A requirement is strong and hard to verify in unsupervised settings; dictionary rank selection is heuristic.  
- Limited empirical comparison: primarily benchmarked against RPCA, not against more recent robust recovery methods (e.g., outlier pursuit).  
- Column-wise unit-norm constraint on dictionary A seems tied to parameter choice (λ = 1/√n₁) but its necessity isn’t fully explored.  
- Analysis focuses on μ₂ while μ₁’s behavior under clustering is not fully analyzed and could also be problematic.  
- Claims about theoretical bound tightness (O(n₂ / log n₁)) lack supporting evidence or discussion of optimality.  
- Suggest expanding experiments to compare with more recent robust matrix recovery methods.  
- Suggest providing a more principled method for choosing dictionary rank A rather than the current RPCA-based heuristic.  
- Suggest extending theoretical analysis to characterize μ₁ under clustering and discuss implications for the method.  
- Suggest discussing tightness of theoretical bounds and possible improvements.  
- Suggest more detailed empirical validation across varying coherence, clustering, and noise levels to map method limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original work that identifies a practical RPCA limitation, provides theoretical insights, and offers an effective algorithm.  
- Recommendation: accept — paper is technically sound, practically relevant, and well-validated, despite some practical and comparative limitations.
