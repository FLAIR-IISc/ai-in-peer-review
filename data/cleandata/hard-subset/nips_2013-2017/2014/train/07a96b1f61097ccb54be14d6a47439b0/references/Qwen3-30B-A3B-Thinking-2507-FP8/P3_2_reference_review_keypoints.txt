- RPCA fails on "coherent" (clustered) data because the second coherence parameter μ₂(L₀) increases with cluster number, degrading performance ("μ₂-phenomenon").
- Proposal: use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a low-rank dictionary A satisfying U₀ ⊂ U_A to avoid coherence-related failures.
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2) provide exact and near-exact recovery guarantees; rank(L₀) bound is looser than RPCA's.
- Critical omission: the key explanation of why μ₂ increases with cluster number is deferred to a "full paper" and must be included here.
- The claimed relationship between μ₂ and μ₃ is asserted without mathematical derivation and needs explicit justification.
- Algorithmic concern: post-processing steps in Algorithm 1 (Steps 2–3) are insufficiently explained—how they yield a well-conditioned dictionary and affect guarantees is unclear.
- Experimental limitations: motion-sequence results are interesting but limited—no comparisons with other dictionary-learning methods for LRR.
- Unclear how the RPCA-constructed dictionary quality influences final performance; the observed error reduction lacks mechanistic analysis.
- Clarity issues: the link between clustering and coherence parameters needs clearer exposition and a simple example; terminology ("coherent"/"less incoherent") is inconsistent.
- Originality: novel insight that low-rank dictionaries can mitigate coherence issues—related to prior matrix factorization work but a distinct focus on coherence parameters.
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem (many datasets are clustered/coherent), but prevalence and scenarios of failure/improvement are not quantified.
- Strengths: identifies a genuine RPCA limitation, provides sound theory under reasonable conditions, offers a simple practical algorithm with modest overhead, and supports claims with synthetic and real data.
- Weaknesses: reliance on external "full paper" for key arguments, missing derivations, vague algorithmic details, incomplete experimental comparisons, and limited analysis of why method improves over RPCA.
- Recommendation: weak acceptance contingent on adding the μ₂ explanation, deriving the μ₂–μ₃ link, clarifying Algorithm 1, comparing to other dictionary methods, and deeper experimental/ practical analysis.
- Overall: promising and valuable contribution if made self-contained and supported by stronger theoretical exposition and broader empirical validation.
