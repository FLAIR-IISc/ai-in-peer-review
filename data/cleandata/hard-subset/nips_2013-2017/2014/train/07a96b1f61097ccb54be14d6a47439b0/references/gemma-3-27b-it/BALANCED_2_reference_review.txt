This paper tackles the problem of robust PCA in the presence of coherent data, arguing that standard RPCA struggles when the underlying low-rank structure exhibits clustering. The authors identify the coherence parameter µ2 as particularly sensitive to clustering and propose using a low-rank dictionary within a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework to mitigate this issue. They provide theoretical analysis supporting their approach and demonstrate its effectiveness through both synthetic and real-world experiments.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** The limitations of RPCA with coherent data are well-recognized, and this paper offers a compelling approach to address this weakness. Clustering is a common characteristic of real-world data, making the issue highly relevant.
* **Clear theoretical contribution:** The paper provides a theoretical justification for using a low-rank dictionary in LRR to avoid the issues caused by high coherence. Theorem 1, in particular, is a valuable result outlining the conditions for recovery.
* **Connection to existing work:** The authors clearly connect their work to RPCA, LRR, and matrix factorization techniques, placing their contribution within the broader context of the field. They also acknowledge and build upon previous insights regarding coherence parameters.
* **Empirical validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and motion sequence data support the claims made in the paper. The improvement over RPCA on the motion sequence data is particularly convincing.
* **Well-written and organized:** The paper is generally well-written and follows a logical structure, making it relatively easy to understand the authors’ ideas. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical derivations appear sound.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **µ2-phenomenon explanation lacks depth:** While the paper claims µ2 increases with cluster number, the explanation and visual evidence (Figure 2) are somewhat superficial. A more rigorous derivation or a more detailed intuitive explanation would strengthen this claim. It’s not immediately clear *why* µ2 is specifically affected by clustering beyond a hand-wavy argument.
* **Dictionary learning algorithm is heuristic:** Algorithm 1 relies on an initial RPCA solution to construct the dictionary. While empirically effective, this approach lacks a strong theoretical guarantee and might be sensitive to the quality of the initial RPCA recovery. A more principled dictionary learning strategy could be explored.
* **Limited comparison with other robust PCA methods:** The paper primarily compares against RPCA. There are other robust PCA algorithms (e.g., those based on outlier pursuit or reweighted least squares) that could provide a more comprehensive benchmark.
* **Theorem 1's assumptions are strong:** The assumption that U0 is a subspace of UA (P_UA(U0) = U0) is quite strong and might not hold in practical scenarios. While the paper acknowledges this, its practical implications are not fully discussed. How sensitive is the performance to deviations from this assumption?
* **Experimental details are scarce:**  More details about the experimental setup, parameter tuning, and statistical significance of the results would be helpful. For example, how was λ chosen for RPCA and LRR? What statistical tests were used to determine the significance of the improvements observed in the experiments?

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a relevant and important problem in robust PCA.
* It provides a novel theoretical insight into the relationship between coherence, clustering, and low-rank recovery.
* The empirical results are encouraging and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The explanation of the µ2-phenomenon is not fully convincing.
* The dictionary learning algorithm is heuristic and lacks theoretical guarantees.
* The comparison with other robust PCA methods is limited.
* The strong assumptions of Theorem 1 might limit its applicability.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of robust PCA. While the paper has some weaknesses, the strengths outweigh them, and the proposed approach is promising.  I believe the paper is of sufficient quality for NIPS, but some revisions would significantly improve it.

**Recommendations for Improvement:**

* **Strengthen the explanation of the µ2-phenomenon:** Provide a more rigorous analysis or a more detailed intuitive explanation of why clustering leads to an increase in µ2.
* **Explore alternative dictionary learning strategies:** Investigate more principled dictionary learning methods that do not rely on an initial RPCA solution.
* **Expand the experimental comparison:** Include comparisons with other state-of-the-art robust PCA algorithms.
* **Discuss the sensitivity to the assumption P_UA(U0) = U0:**  Analyze the impact of deviations from this assumption on the performance of the proposed method.
* **Provide more detailed experimental details:**  Include more information about the experimental setup, parameter tuning, and statistical significance of the results.



**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions.
