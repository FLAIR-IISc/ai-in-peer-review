This paper addresses the problem of low-rank matrix recovery in the presence of corruptions, specifically focusing on scenarios where the underlying low-rank matrix exhibits coherence due to clustering structures. The authors argue that standard Robust PCA (RPCA) struggles with such coherence, and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach utilizing a carefully chosen dictionary to mitigate this issue. They provide theoretical analysis demonstrating conditions under which LRR avoids the coherence problems of RPCA and present an algorithm to learn the dictionary in an unsupervised manner, demonstrating improved performance on both synthetic and real-world motion data.

**Quality:** The paper presents a solid technical contribution. The theoretical analysis appears sound, building upon existing RPCA theory and extending it to the LRR framework. The conditions for avoiding coherence issues are clearly stated (Theorem 1), and the noisy case analysis (Theorem 2) adds practical relevance. The proposed algorithm is well-defined and logically motivated. The experimental validation, while limited in scope, demonstrates improvements over RPCA in both synthetic and real data scenarios. However, the proofs are referred to rather than included, making a complete assessment of their validity challenging for the reviewer. A minor weakness is the discussion of computational complexity; while stating the algorithm doesn’t *double* the work of RPCA, a more precise comparison of computational costs would be beneficial.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and outlines the contributions. The mathematical notation is standard and consistently used. The explanation of the “µ2-phenomenon” and its relation to clustering structures is insightful and well-illustrated with Figure 2. However, the density of mathematical notation in Section 3.1 could be reduced by breaking down complex expressions into smaller, more manageable steps. Some definitions, like the various matrix norms, are presented compactly and might benefit from slightly more elaboration for readers less familiar with matrix analysis.

**Originality:** The problem of addressing coherence in low-rank recovery is relevant and the approach of leveraging a dictionary within the LRR framework is a novel contribution. While LRR itself is not new, the authors’ analysis of coherence and the specific conditions for dictionary design to overcome this issue are original. The connection made between coherence and intrinsic data structures like clustering is a valuable insight. The algorithm for unsupervised dictionary learning is a practical extension of the theoretical findings. The authors correctly situate their work within the existing literature on RPCA, matrix completion, and subspace learning, and acknowledge related work.

**Significance:** The results have the potential to be significant, particularly in applications where data exhibits inherent clustering or coherence. Robust low-rank matrix recovery is a fundamental problem with applications across numerous domains, including image processing, computer vision, and machine learning. The insights into the relationship between coherence and data structure could guide the development of more robust and efficient algorithms. The demonstrated improvements on motion sequence data, a practical application, lend further weight to the potential impact of the work. The analysis provides a clearer understanding of the limitations of RPCA and offers a pathway toward overcoming them.  The connection between coherence and matrix factorization is also a potentially impactful contribution.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and insightful analysis of coherence in low-rank matrix recovery.
*   Theoretical guarantees for the proposed LRR approach.
*   Demonstrated improvements over RPCA on both synthetic and real data.
*   Clear articulation of the problem and its relevance to various applications.
*   The connection to the physical meaning of coherence and its relation to data structure.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Proofs are not included in the paper, limiting the ability to fully verify the theoretical claims.
*   Experimental evaluation is somewhat limited in scope; more extensive evaluation on diverse datasets would strengthen the findings.
*   The computational complexity analysis could be more detailed.
*   The discussion regarding iterative improvements to the algorithm is brief; exploring this further could be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a solid technical contribution. The analysis of coherence in low-rank recovery is insightful and the proposed approach offers a promising solution. While the lack of included proofs and the limited experimental evaluation are minor drawbacks, the originality, clarity, and potential significance of the work outweigh these concerns. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions – specifically, providing a more detailed computational complexity analysis and, if possible, including key parts of the proofs or providing a more detailed roadmap to their verification.
