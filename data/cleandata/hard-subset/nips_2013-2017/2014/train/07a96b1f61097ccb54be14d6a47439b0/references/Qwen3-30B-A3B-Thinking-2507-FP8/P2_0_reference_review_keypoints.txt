- Paper identifies RPCA's failure on coherent/clustering low-rank data due to increased coherence parameters (µ₂, µ₃).
- Proposes using LRR with a low-rank dictionary A and proves (Theorem 1) that if U₀ ⊂ U_A and A is low-rank, LRR avoids harmful coherence parameters.
- Presents Algorithm 1: unsupervised method that uses RPCA to construct a dictionary for LRR, improving recovery empirically.
- Theoretical contribution: formalizes the "µ₂-phenomenon" linking coherence parameters to clustering structure in real data.
- Novel algorithmic idea: low-rank dictionary pursuit to bypass coherence-related failures of RPCA, backed by Theorems 1 and 2.
- Experimental validation: thorough synthetic experiments varying rank/sparsity and real motion-data tests showing significant improvements.
- Practical impact: demonstrates ~40% relative improvement in clustering error on motion data, tying theory to real-world relevance.
- Authors acknowledge RPCA still performs well when rank is very low or corruption is very sparse.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 requires the strong condition U₀ ⊂ U_A, which may be hard to satisfy or verify in unsupervised settings.
- Weakness: Limited computational complexity analysis; runtime depends on dictionary rank and may be problematic at large scale.
- Weakness: Dictionary construction (normalizing RPCA output) may be suboptimal for some data; alternatives not explored.
- Weakness: Missing empirical comparisons to other recent robust low-rank recovery methods (e.g., Xu et al. 2012; Wang et al. 2015).
- Overall quality: technically sound with solid theory and experiments (score 9/10).
- Clarity: well-written and well-organized exposition (score 9/10).
- Originality: novel identification of µ₂-phenomenon and principled low-rank dictionary solution (score 9/10).
- Significance: addresses a practical RPCA limitation with potential broad impact (score 8/10).
- Recommendation: accept at NIPS — significant theoretical and practical contribution despite some limitations.
- Suggested improvements: discuss verifying/sensitivity of U₀ ⊂ U_A, compare with recent methods, provide guidance on choosing dictionary rank, and report empirical convergence for iterative variants.
