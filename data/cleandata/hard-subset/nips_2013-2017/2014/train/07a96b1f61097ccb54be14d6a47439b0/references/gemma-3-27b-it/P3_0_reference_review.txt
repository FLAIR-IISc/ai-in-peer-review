## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper addresses an important and practical problem – the limitations of RPCA when dealing with data exhibiting inherent clustering structure, leading to coherence issues. The authors identify a connection between coherence and underlying data structure, and propose a modification to the LRR framework leveraging a learned dictionary to mitigate these issues. While the paper presents a sound theoretical analysis and demonstrates promising experimental results, the presentation could be significantly improved for clarity, and the novelty, while present, feels incremental given the existing literature on LRR and dictionary learning. I recommend **weak accept** contingent on significant revisions addressing clarity and strengthening the novelty argument.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon the foundation of Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) and Low-Rank Representation (LRR). It identifies that RPCA’s performance degrades when the low-rank component possesses significant coherence, a phenomenon exacerbated by clustering structures within the data.  The core contribution lies in recognizing that the coherence parameter isn’t an unavoidable assumption, but rather a manifestation of underlying data structure.  The authors propose learning a low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework to circumvent the coherence issue, theoretically demonstrating that this approach can recover low-rank data even with high coherence.  This builds directly on the LRR work of Liu et al. (2013), but extends it to explicitly address coherence concerns and provide a dictionary learning strategy tailored to this problem.  The connection to matrix factorization techniques (as alluded to by the authors) is also relevant, placing the work within a broader context of dimensionality reduction methods.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Identifies a practical limitation of RPCA:** The paper accurately points out a common issue in real-world applications where data rarely conforms to the idealized assumptions of RPCA.  The link between clustering and coherence is insightful.
* **Solid Theoretical Foundation:** The theoretical analysis, particularly Theorem 1, provides a rigorous justification for the proposed approach and defines conditions for successful recovery.  The discussion of the 'µ2-phenomenon' offers a clear explanation of the problem.
* **Promising Experimental Results:**  The experimental validation, both on synthetic data and motion sequences, demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm compared to RPCA.  The improvement on the Hopkins155 dataset is a strong indicator of practical relevance.
* **Connection to Existing Frameworks:** The work builds upon well-established frameworks (RPCA, LRR) and provides a clear pathway for incorporating dictionary learning to enhance their robustness.
* **Algorithm 1 is practical:** The proposed algorithm is straightforward and computationally reasonable, leveraging RPCA as a starting point for dictionary learning.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper suffers from a dense writing style and a lack of intuitive explanations. Many concepts are introduced without sufficient motivation.  For instance, the sudden introduction of multiple coherence parameters (µ1, µ2, µ3) feels abrupt and lacks clear justification for their individual relevance *before* diving into the µ2-phenomenon.  The notation is also quite heavy, making it difficult to follow the theoretical derivations.
* **Incremental Novelty:** While the idea of using a learned dictionary within LRR to address coherence isn't entirely new, the specific contribution feels incremental. Dictionary learning is a well-studied area, and applying it to mitigate coherence in LRR, while sound, doesn’t represent a significant paradigm shift.  The authors need to better articulate *what* specifically is novel about their dictionary learning strategy beyond simply being low-rank.  Is the constraint  PUA(U0) = U0 truly a novel contribution, or a natural consequence of wanting the learned dictionary to align with the underlying data subspace?
* **Limited Discussion of Dictionary Learning:**  The paper focuses heavily on the theoretical guarantees but provides limited discussion on the *choice* of dictionary learning methods. Algorithm 1 uses the output of RPCA to construct the dictionary, but doesn't explore alternatives like K-SVD, or other dictionary learning algorithms that might be more effective. A discussion on the trade-offs between different dictionary learning approaches would strengthen the work.
* **Experimental Setup Details:** The experimental section lacks sufficient detail.  Specifically, the choice of λ, the parameter tuning for the dictionary learning step (beyond using the RPCA output), and the criteria for determining rank (r̂0) in Algorithm 1 are not thoroughly explained.
* **Theorem 2 (Noisy Case) is Weakly Justified:** The justification for Theorem 2 feels abrupt and lacks sufficient detail on how the noise level (ε) impacts the analysis.



**4. Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a real-world limitation of RPCA.
* Provides theoretical guarantees for recovery.
* Demonstrates empirical improvements over RPCA.
* Offers a practical algorithm for implementation.

**Con:**

* Clarity and presentation issues hinder understanding.
* Incremental novelty – the core idea isn’t radically new.
* Limited exploration of dictionary learning methods.
* Insufficient experimental details.




**5. Recommendation:**

**Weak Accept.** I believe the paper addresses a relevant problem and presents a technically sound solution. However, significant revisions are needed to improve clarity, strengthen the novelty argument, and provide more detailed experimental analysis. Specifically, the authors should:

* **Rewrite sections 3.1.1 and 3.1.2 for clarity:** Provide intuitive explanations for the coherence parameters before diving into mathematical details.
* **Elaborate on the novelty of the dictionary learning strategy:** Clearly articulate what differentiates their approach from existing dictionary learning methods.
* **Discuss alternative dictionary learning approaches:** Explore the trade-offs between different methods and justify the choice of using the RPCA output.
* **Provide more detailed experimental setup information:** Include parameter settings, rank selection criteria, and a more thorough analysis of results.
* **Strengthen the justification for Theorem 2:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of noise on recovery guarantees.

Addressing these issues would significantly enhance the paper's impact and make it a valuable contribution to the NIPS community.
