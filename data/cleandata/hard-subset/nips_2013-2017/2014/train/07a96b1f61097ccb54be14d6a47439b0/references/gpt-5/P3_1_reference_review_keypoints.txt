- Problem: Recover low-rank L0 from X = L0 + S0 (and noisy variant), focusing on “coherent” L0 where RPCA/PCP fails due to large µ2.
- Main observation: µ2-phenomenon — µ2(L0) grows with number of clusters when columns lie in a union of subspaces, harming RPCA guarantees.
- Method: Use LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary A via min ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S.
- Main theorem: If A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA, LRR achieves exact/near-exact recovery without dependence on µ2(L0), with rank bounds depending on µ1(A).
- Practical algorithm: Run RPCA to estimate L̂0, truncate to estimated rank, column-normalize to form Â, then run LRR with Â.
- Empirics: On synthetic clustered subspaces, method expands RPCA success region by 47%; improves subspace clustering on corrupted motion data.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on RPCA/PCP, coherence-based analysis, and LRR literature; novelty is analyzing LRR with general dictionaries to avoid µ2 dependence.
- Strength: Clearly articulates a real RPCA failure mode tied to µ2 and clustering structure.
- Strength: Provides theoretical results removing µ2 dependence under the U0 ⊂ UA condition, with noisy stability bounds.
- Strength: Simple, practical two-stage algorithm leveraging existing RPCA; computationally efficient when A is low-rank.
- Strength: Empirical evidence shows consistent improvements over RPCA on synthetic and real data.
- Weakness: Key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong in unsupervised settings; theory does not cover the learned Â and may fail if RPCA is inaccurate.
- Weakness: Randomness/identifiability assumptions (support, sign, independence) for probability guarantees are not clearly specified.
- Weakness: Bounds depend on µ1(A); unclear why µ1(A) should be small after forming Â — need intuition or methods to control µ1(A).
- Weakness: Experimental baselines are limited to RPCA; should include Outlier Pursuit, reweighted/weighted RPCA, AltProj/GoDec, and LRR with A = X.
- Weakness: Synthetic experiments fix k=5; should sweep number of clusters k (keeping rank fixed), report coherence parameters, and success rates.
- Weakness: Motion experiments need reconstruction metrics, ablations on corruption/noise, and runtime comparisons.
- Weakness: Fixed λ = 1/√n1 for all experiments; need sensitivity studies and practical tuning guidance for general A.
- Weakness: Normalization step may leak sparse corruption into Â when RPCA is imperfect; suggest robust column selection or projecting onto Û before normalization.
- Clarity issues: Define n1,n2 consistently, fix truncated footnotes, include referenced figures, and provide solver/stopping details for reproducibility.
- Quality assessment: Theoretical contributions appear sound and nontrivial but proofs are deferred; empirical results promising but need broader baselines and robustness analyses.
- Clarity assessment: Well-written overall but needs clearer assumptions and expanded discussion around µ1(A) and guarantees for learned dictionaries.
- Originality: Novel and interesting focus on coherent data and analysis of LRR with low-rank dictionaries to remove µ2 dependence.
- Significance: Addresses a common failure mode in robust low-rank recovery and could influence practice if experiments and theory are strengthened.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying assumptions, providing end-to-end guarantees for learned A, and strengthening experiments; reviewer confidence: high.
