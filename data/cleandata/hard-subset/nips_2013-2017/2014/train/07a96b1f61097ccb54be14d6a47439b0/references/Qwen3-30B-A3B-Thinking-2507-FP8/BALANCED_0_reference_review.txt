

# Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

This paper addresses a fundamental limitation of Robust PCA (RPCA) when dealing with coherent data, specifically data exhibiting clustering structures that increase coherence parameters. The authors propose using Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a carefully constructed low-rank dictionary to avoid the coherence parameters that degrade RPCA's performance. They provide theoretical guarantees showing that LRR with a low-rank dictionary can recover coherent data without requiring coherence assumptions, and propose a practical unsupervised algorithm to learn such a dictionary. The work builds on prior NIPS contributions like RPCA (Candès et al., 2011) and LRR (Liu et al., 2013), but offers novel insights about the physical meaning of coherence parameters.

## Strengths

1. **Novel Insight on Coherence Parameters**: The paper makes a significant contribution by connecting coherence parameters to physical data structures (specifically clustering). The "µ2-phenomenon" (Figure 2) clearly demonstrates how clustering structures increase coherence, providing a much-needed physical interpretation of these parameters that are commonly used in RPCA literature.

2. **Strong Theoretical Contributions**: Theorems 1 and 2 provide rigorous guarantees for the proposed approach without requiring coherence assumptions. The paper establishes that when the dictionary A is low-rank and contains the column space of L0, LRR can recover coherent data. This is a meaningful theoretical advance over RPCA.

3. **Practical Algorithm**: The unsupervised algorithm (Algorithm 1) is well-motivated and effective. The authors demonstrate that it outperforms RPCA on both synthetic data (47% wider success area) and real motion sequences (40% relative improvement in clustering error rates).

4. **Comprehensive Experiments**: The experiments are thorough, covering both synthetic data with controlled parameters and real-world motion sequences from the Hopkins155 database, providing strong empirical validation of the claims.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Assumptions**: The key condition PUA(U0) = U0 (that the column space of L0 is contained in the column space of A) is strong and may be difficult to satisfy in practice without prior knowledge. While the paper proposes an algorithm to approximately satisfy this condition, it's unclear how often this condition is actually met in real-world applications.

2. **Connection to Matrix Factorization**: The paper mentions that the approach relates to matrix factorization (point 4 in contributions), but this connection is not developed in depth. A more thorough discussion of how this work relates to and advances the broader matrix factorization literature would strengthen the paper.

3. **Limited Comparison with Recent Methods**: The paper compares primarily with RPCA and LRR with A=X, but doesn't compare with recent robust PCA variants like R-PCA (Xu et al., 2012) or other state-of-the-art methods that might also handle coherent data.

4. **Practical Dictionary Construction**: While Algorithm 1 provides a practical method for dictionary learning, the paper doesn't thoroughly analyze the sensitivity of the approach to the specific steps of the algorithm (e.g., the rank estimation step in Step 2).

## Overall Assessment

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of coherence in low-rank matrix recovery and provides a practical approach to handle coherent data. The connection between coherence parameters and clustering structures is particularly insightful and fills an important gap in the literature. The theoretical results are sound, and the experimental validation is comprehensive.

However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the following points:

1. Provide more detailed analysis of how often the condition PUA(U0) = U0 holds in practice, and how robust the approach is to violations of this condition.

2. Include comparisons with recent robust PCA methods beyond RPCA to better establish the relative advantages of the proposed approach.

3. Expand the discussion on the connection to matrix factorization to better position this work within the broader literature.

4. Include a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed algorithm for very large datasets.

## Recommendation

This paper presents a meaningful theoretical advance with practical implications for robust matrix recovery. While the theoretical assumptions require careful consideration in practice, the insights about coherence parameters and the proposed algorithm are compelling. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the robust matrix recovery literature and is well-suited for NeurIPS.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses noted above, particularly regarding the practicality of the theoretical assumptions and expanding the comparison with recent methods. The paper's theoretical insights and practical algorithm represent a significant step forward in understanding and handling coherent data in low-rank matrix recovery.
