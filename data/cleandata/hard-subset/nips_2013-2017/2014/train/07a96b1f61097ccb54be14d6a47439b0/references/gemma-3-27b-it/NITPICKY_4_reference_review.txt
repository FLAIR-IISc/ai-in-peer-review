This paper addresses the problem of recovering low-rank matrices corrupted by sparse errors, focusing on scenarios where the underlying low-rank structure exhibits coherence – specifically, clustering. The authors identify a limitation of Robust PCA (RPCA) in such settings, arguing that coherence parameters increase with clustering, leading to performance degradation. They propose leveraging the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) framework with a pre-defined low-rank dictionary to mitigate this issue, and provide theoretical justification and an algorithm based on initializing the dictionary using an RPCA solution.

While the motivation is sound and the problem relevant, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws that, cumulatively, significantly detract from its impact. The clarity of presentation is frequently hampered by imprecise language, redundancy, and a lack of rigorous definitions. The technical contributions, while potentially interesting, are presented with insufficient detail and lack convincing empirical validation in all scenarios.

**Strengths:**

*   **Problem Identification:** The paper correctly identifies the issue of coherence in the context of RPCA and its relationship to clustering structure within the low-rank component. This is a valuable observation.
*   **LRR Framework:** Utilizing the LRR framework with a carefully chosen dictionary is a reasonable approach to addressing the coherence issue.
*   **Algorithm Proposal:** The proposed algorithm, leveraging RPCA to initialize the dictionary for LRR, is a practical and intuitively appealing idea.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistency & Imprecision:** Numerous notational issues exist. For example, the use of both *U* and *U* (with and without subscripts) to denote matrices *and* the spaces they span is confusing. The definition of µ3 is unnecessarily complex and could benefit from simplification. The paper frequently refers to “less incoherent” data without a clear definition of what constitutes “less incoherent” in relation to the presented analysis.
*   **Theoretical Gaps:** Theorem 1's conditions, particularly the relationship between rank(A), µ1(A), and n, are not thoroughly explained or justified. The claim that the proposed approach offers a “looser” bound than RPCA (footnote on page 8) is weakly supported. The proof sketch mentions “detailed proof” being in the full paper, but a proper NIPS submission should contain *sufficient* detail for reviewers to assess the soundness of the arguments.
*   **Experimental Rigor:** The experiments on randomly generated matrices are somewhat convincing, but lack detailed parameter tuning information. The improvement over RPCA, while reported as 47%, is not accompanied by statistical significance testing. More importantly, the experiments on motion sequences are inadequately described. The preprocessing steps used on the Hopkins155 dataset, the specific implementation details of the subspace clustering algorithms, and the evaluation metrics are not sufficiently clear. Asserting a “40% relative improvement” without appropriate statistical testing is misleading. It is also not clear why the performance of RPCA is so poor *before* any clustering is applied.
*   **Redundancy & Verbosity:** The introduction is overly lengthy and repetitive. The connections drawn to related work, while present, are often superficial. Several statements are rephrased multiple times without adding significant value. For example, the discussion around coherence parameters being “not assumptions” is belabored.
* **Minor errors**: The sentence “Readers can also refer to the full paper to see why the second coherence parameter increases with the cluster number underlying L0” (page 7) is not helpful and suggests incompleteness.

**Specific Issues/Questions:**

*   Page 3: “Interestingly, we show…” is unnecessarily weak phrasing for a technical contribution.
*   Page 4: What is meant by "1 is a size-d dictionary matrix constructed in advance"? This is unclear.
*   Page 7: The µ2-phenomenon – the connection between clustering and µ2 increase isn’t fully explained. A more rigorous derivation or visual explanation is needed.
*   Page 8: The condition PUA(U0) = U0 – why is this necessary? Provide more intuition.
*   Table 1: The reported standard deviations are missing.
*   Algorithm 1:  The choice of λ = 1/√n1 seems arbitrary and should be justified.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Against Acceptance:**

*   Numerous notational and clarity issues.
*   Insufficient theoretical rigor and detail.
*   Weak empirical validation, particularly regarding statistical significance and experimental setup details.
*   Redundancy and verbosity detract from the impact of the paper.
*   Missing information (e.g., proof details, parameter settings).

**For Acceptance:**

*   Identifies a relevant and potentially important problem.
*   Proposes a reasonable approach using LRR with a learned dictionary.
*   The algorithm is practical and intuitively appealing.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite identifying a valid problem and proposing a plausible solution, this paper is ultimately flawed by numerous deficiencies in clarity, technical rigor, and experimental validation. The cumulative effect of these issues is substantial. While the ideas are potentially valuable, the paper, in its current form, does not meet the standards for acceptance at NIPS. I recommend **rejection**. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, focusing on improving clarity, providing more rigorous theoretical justification, and conducting more thorough and statistically sound experiments before resubmitting.  A major rewrite focusing on conciseness and precision is also strongly recommended.
