Summary
The paper studies robust recovery of a low-rank matrix L0 from a grossly corrupted observation X = L0 + S0 (plus optional dense noise), with a focus on “coherent” L0 where classical RPCA/PCP guarantees degrade due to large coherence parameters. The authors (i) empirically and conceptually highlight a “µ2-phenomenon”: when data are union-of-subspaces (clustered) within a fixed global rank, the second coherence parameter µ2(L0) grows with the number of clusters, weakening RPCA’s success conditions; (ii) analyze Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a general dictionary X ≈ AZ + S and prove that, if A is low-rank and spans the column space of L0 (U0 ⊂ UA), exact (or near-exact) recovery via LRR can be achieved without dependence on µ2 (bounds depend on µ1(A) instead); (iii) propose a simple unsupervised pipeline: run RPCA to estimate L0, build a low-rank, column-normalized dictionary A from that estimate, and then solve LRR to refine recovery. Experiments on synthetic data and corrupted motion trajectories show improvements over RPCA, and reduced clustering errors downstream.

Strengths
- Clear articulation of a practical failure mode of RPCA under clustered/union-of-subspaces data; the µ2-phenomenon is well motivated and aligns with coherence-based theory.
- Theoretical contribution for LRR with general (low-rank) dictionaries that avoid µ2 and provide exact/noisy recovery guarantees under standard sparsity assumptions.
- Simple, practical algorithm leveraging RPCA to construct the dictionary, with empirical gains on synthetic and real motion data; downstream impact on subspace clustering is demonstrated.
- Insightful discussion connecting coherence to intrinsic data structure rather than a mere technical assumption.

Weaknesses and concerns
- The key condition U0 ⊂ UA is strong and amounts to requiring the dictionary to already capture the ground-truth column space. The proposed unsupervised construction relies on RPCA to estimate U0, precisely where RPCA is challenged by high µ2. The claim that the algorithm’s success probability is at least that of RPCA is plausible but not formally established; providing a formal “bootstrapping” result (tolerance to subspace estimation error) would materially strengthen the paper.
- The theoretical bounds depend on µ1(A). When A is constructed from L̂0, µ1(A) might itself be large in clustered settings (e.g., if rows of L0 are also structured). The paper should measure µ1(A) in experiments and clarify when µ1(A) is indeed smaller/more benign than µ2(L0).
- The noisy recovery bound scales as O(√mn ε), which can be loose in high dimensions. A discussion of tightness relative to PCP’s noisy bounds and whether constants can be improved would help.
- Experimental evaluation, while encouraging, is limited. Synthetic data vary rank and sparsity but do not systematically vary the number of clusters, subspace angles, or sample imbalance to directly validate the µ2-phenomenon and the theory. The motion experiment corrupts missing entries with Bernoulli ±1, which is not a realistic imputation model; comparisons to robust matrix completion or RPCA-with-missing-data baselines are missing.
- Parameter selection: adopting λ = 1/√n1 from PCP for general A is justified by column normalization, but the sensitivity to this choice is not studied. Similarly, the rank selection threshold 10−3σ1 is ad hoc; robustness analysis or cross-validation guidance would help.
- Some claims are deferred to the “full paper” (e.g., formal analysis of the µ2 growth); the current submission would benefit from including at least a precise statement and proof sketch.
- Clarity/minor issues: typos (e.g., “PRCA” for “RPCA”), “column-wisely”, probability “n−101”, incomplete references. The conditioning motivation for Steps 2–3 is not analyzed.

Quality
The core technical claims (Theorems 1–2) are plausible and in line with convex analysis for LRR/PCP; however, key assumptions (U0 ⊂ UA) are strong and the unsupervised algorithm lacks theoretical backing. Experiments support the approach but are not exhaustive.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable and well organized, but several important statements are deferred, and some presentation aspects need polishing (proof sketches, parameter choices, and notation consistency).

Originality
The identification of coherence inflation due to clustering in RPCA is timely, and the LRR-with-low-rank-dictionary perspective avoiding µ2 appears novel relative to prior LRR work that focused on A = X. The algorithmic idea is simple and incremental but practically relevant.

Significance
Moderate. The work addresses a real limitation of RPCA and suggests a viable remedy with both theory (under side information) and practice (unsupervised construction). Stronger guarantees for the unsupervised case and broader evaluation would increase impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a perturbation analysis: if ∥PUA⊥U0∥ is small (A approximately spans U0), quantify recovery error or conditions for exactness; formalize the “success probability no worse than RPCA” claim.
- Empirically report µ1(A), µ2(L0), and µ3 before/after dictionary construction; vary number of clusters, subspace angles, and imbalance to validate the µ2-phenomenon and demonstrate robustness.
- Compare against additional baselines: (i) PCP with missing entries/robust matrix completion; (ii) alternatives that leverage side information/features; (iii) column/row sampling to reduce coherence.
- Study sensitivity to λ and the rank threshold; include ablations on normalization and the conditioning heuristic.
- Discuss computational complexity with actual runtimes on larger problems and modern PCP solvers.
- Clean up typos and complete references.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses a real and under-discussed limitation of RPCA with a conceptually clean fix.
- Theoretical results for LRR with low-rank dictionaries are of independent interest.
- Simple algorithm with demonstrated gains on synthetic and real data; relevance to subspace clustering.

Cons for acceptance
- Strong assumptions for theory; lack of guarantees for the proposed unsupervised pipeline.
- Limited and partially unrealistic experimental setup; missing key comparisons and diagnostics.
- Some claims deferred; clarity and presentation issues remain.

Overall: promising and relevant work; with strengthened analysis for the learned dictionary and broader, more diagnostic experiments, it would make a strong contribution.
