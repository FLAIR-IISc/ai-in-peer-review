- Paper identifies a µ2-phenomenon: µ2(L0) grows with number of clusters (union of subspaces), hurting RPCA guarantees.
- Proposes analyzing LRR with a general dictionary A: min_Z,S ||Z||* + λ||S||1 s.t. X = AZ + S to avoid dependence on µ2.
- Main theorem: if A is low-rank and U0 ⊂ UA, exact recovery is possible without µ2 dependence, with λ = 1/√n1 after column-normalizing A.
- Noisy result: near-recovery with Frobenius error O(√mn ε) under analogous conditions.
- Theoretical rank/sparsity conditions are interpretable and slightly looser than RPCA’s bounds (rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ ε^2 n2 /(c μ1(A) log n1) and |Ω| ≤ (0.5−ε)mn).
- Key assumption U0 ⊂ UA is strong but necessary for exact recovery, restricting scope of guarantees.
- The paper is honest about using RPCA to build A in practice but provides no theory for error propagation in the two-stage estimator (RPCA → A → LRR).
- Practical two-stage algorithm (RPCA → build low-rank column-normalized A → LRR) shows improved recovery and downstream subspace-clustering on synthetic and Hopkins155 variants.
- Empirics are promising but limited: no real-data recovery metrics, no ablations on dictionary quality, and missing comparisons to Outlier Pursuit and robust MF variants.
- Treatment of missing entries by ±1 imputation is nonstandard and may bias results; masked formulations or matrix-completion baselines are preferable.
- Clarity is generally good; separation and intuition for µ1, µ2, µ3 are helpful.
- Paper should include a quantitative plot/table of µ2 vs. number of clusters and show corresponding RPCA failure rates.
- Need clarification on whether success probability is over random support Ω only and on randomness assumptions for A when learned from RPCA.
- Discuss impact of column normalization on UA (it should not change UA) and conditioning/implications for A+.
- Complexity claims need empirical runtimes and discussion of dependence on rank(A).
- Originality: making the µ2-phenomenon explicit and proving LRR recovery with general low-rank dictionaries containing U0 is a meaningful refinement.
- Significance: advances understanding of RPCA failures on coherent data and offers a simple, adoptable remedy, though impact limited by lack of learned-dictionary guarantees and broader empirics.
- Suggestions: run ablations varying A quality (add controlled noise to A), compare to RPCA variants for missing data, Outlier Pursuit, and LRR with A = X.
- Suggestions: analyze parameter sensitivity (λ, rank threshold), justify/assess column normalization effects, and provide a perturbation-style guarantee for the two-stage algorithm if possible.
