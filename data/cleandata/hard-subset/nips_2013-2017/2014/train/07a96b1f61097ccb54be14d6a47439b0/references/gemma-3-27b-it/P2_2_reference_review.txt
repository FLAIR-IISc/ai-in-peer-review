## Review of "Recovery of Coherent Data via Low-Rank Dictionary Pursuit"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of robust low-rank matrix recovery, specifically focusing on the limitations of Robust PCA (RPCA) when dealing with data exhibiting clustering structures – leading to higher coherence. The authors argue that RPCA’s performance degrades as coherence increases due to these inherent data structures and propose a solution leveraging the Low-Rank Representation (LRR) model with a carefully designed dictionary. They prove that using a low-rank dictionary in LRR can mitigate the effects of coherence, providing theoretical guarantees and demonstrating empirical improvements over RPCA on both synthetic and real-world motion data.

**Strengths:**

* **Identifies a relevant limitation of RPCA:** The paper clearly articulates a weakness of RPCA—its sensitivity to data coherence caused by underlying clustering structures—which is a practical concern in many applications.
* **Provides theoretical justification:** The authors provide a theorem establishing conditions under which LRR with a low-rank dictionary can avoid the coherence issues hindering RPCA, contributing to a deeper understanding of the recovery process.  The theoretical analysis seems rigorous (though a detailed check is beyond the scope of this review).
* **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm for constructing the dictionary from an RPCA estimate is simple, intuitive, and demonstrably improves performance.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets support the claims made in the paper. The results on the motion sequence data are particularly convincing.
* **Clear Writing and Organization:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem, and the notation is well-defined.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty of LRR:** The core idea of using LRR isn’t novel; the contribution lies in *how* it’s applied (specifically, the dictionary learning strategy) to address coherence.  While the dictionary learning approach is insightful, the paper doesn't fully emphasize this nuance.
* **Dictionary Learning Heuristic:**  The dictionary learning algorithm is based on an RPCA estimate, which itself is susceptible to coherence issues. While the experiments show improvement, the theoretical justification doesn't explicitly address the potential for RPCA's initial estimate to negatively impact the learned dictionary. A discussion of the robustness of the dictionary learning process to initial errors would strengthen the paper.
* **Theoretical Assumptions:**  The theorems rely on several assumptions (e.g., column-wise unit-normed dictionary, noise level) that might not always hold in practice. A discussion of the sensitivity of the results to violations of these assumptions would be valuable.
* **Comparison to Other Robust Methods:** While the comparison to RPCA is strong, the paper could benefit from a comparison to other recent robust matrix completion methods, beyond just LRR with the X dictionary.  Methods exploiting other prior knowledge or different regularizations might provide further context.



**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound and provides solid theoretical and empirical evidence. The analysis is rigorous, and the results are well-supported. However, the reliance on RPCA for dictionary learning introduces a potential weakness.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it relatively easy to follow. Some sections (particularly the theoretical ones) could benefit from further simplification.
* **Originality (6/10):** The core idea of using LRR isn't entirely new. The novelty lies in the specific dictionary learning approach, which is a valuable contribution but not groundbreaking.
* **Significance (7/10):** The results are significant in that they address a practical limitation of RPCA and provide a concrete improvement in performance, especially for datasets with inherent clustering structures. The impact is potentially broad, particularly in applications like motion analysis and image processing.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Addresses a practical limitation of a widely used method (RPCA).
* Provides a theoretically grounded solution with empirical validation.
* Offers a simple and effective algorithm.
* Results are promising on both synthetic and real-world data.

**Against:**

* The core idea of LRR isn't novel.
* The dictionary learning strategy relies on an RPCA estimate, which could be problematic.
* Limited comparison to alternative robust methods.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of robust matrix recovery.  While the core idea of using LRR isn’t entirely novel, the proposed dictionary learning strategy is insightful and effectively addresses a practical limitation of RPCA.  The theoretical analysis is sound, and the empirical results are convincing.  I recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above – specifically, a more detailed discussion of the dictionary learning process’s robustness and a comparison to a broader range of robust matrix completion methods.  Also, clarifying the level of novelty in the introduction would be beneficial.
