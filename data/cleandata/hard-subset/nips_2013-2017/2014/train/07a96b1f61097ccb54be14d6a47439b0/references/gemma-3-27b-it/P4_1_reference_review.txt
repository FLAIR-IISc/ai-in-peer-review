This paper addresses the problem of recovering low-rank matrices corrupted by sparse errors, focusing on the case where the underlying low-rank matrix exhibits coherence due to clustering structures. The authors argue that standard Robust PCA (RPCA) suffers in such scenarios due to its sensitivity to coherence and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach with a carefully chosen dictionary to mitigate this issue. They provide theoretical analysis demonstrating conditions under which LRR can avoid the coherence parameter limitations of RPCA and an algorithm leveraging RPCA to construct the dictionary for LRR. Experimental results on both synthetic and real-world motion sequence data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis, while dense, appears well-motivated and logically presented. The theorems are clearly stated, and the derivations seem reasonable. The authors rigorously compare their method to RPCA and demonstrate performance improvements. However, the analysis focuses heavily on asymptotic conditions (e.g., large n) which may limit its practical interpretation. The experiments, while demonstrating the method's effectiveness, could benefit from a more thorough statistical analysis (e.g., confidence intervals or statistical significance tests) to support the claims of improvement. The choice of λ = 1/sqrt(n1) seems somewhat arbitrary and could be justified with more explanation. The discussion of the noisy case is brief, and more detailed analysis on the robustness to noise levels could strengthen the work.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, but suffers from density, particularly in the mathematical derivations. The notation is extensive, and while a notation section is provided, it can be overwhelming to follow. The connection between the different coherence parameters (µ1, µ2, µ3) could be explained more intuitively. The motivation for using LRR and the connection to matrix factorization are clearly articulated, but a more visually intuitive explanation of the “µ2-phenomenon” (e.g., a more illustrative Figure 1) could improve understanding. The algorithm description is concise, but a pseudocode representation would enhance clarity.

**Originality:** The core idea of using a dictionary in LRR to address coherence issues is novel and represents a meaningful extension of existing work on RPCA and LRR. While LRR itself is not new [13], applying it specifically to address the coherence problem and deriving the theoretical conditions for dictionary selection are significant contributions. The analysis of the relationship between coherence and clustering structures is a valuable insight. The work appropriately references relevant literature, including RPCA, LRR, and related matrix factorization techniques. The paper successfully distinguishes itself from previous work by focusing on a specific limitation of RPCA and offering a targeted solution.

**Significance:** The problem of robust low-rank matrix recovery is fundamental in many areas of machine learning and data analysis, including computer vision, signal processing, and bioinformatics. Addressing the limitations of RPCA in the presence of coherent data is a significant step forward. The proposed method and theoretical insights have the potential to impact a wide range of applications where data exhibit inherent clustering structures. The empirical results on motion sequences demonstrate the practical relevance of the work. The connection between coherence and underlying data structure provides a deeper understanding of the assumptions and limitations of low-rank matrix recovery techniques.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to addressing coherence issues in low-rank matrix recovery.
*   Strong theoretical foundation and analysis.
*   Demonstrated improvement over RPCA on both synthetic and real-world data.
*   Insightful connection between coherence and underlying data structure.
*   Potentially significant impact on various applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Density of mathematical derivations may hinder accessibility.
*   Lack of detailed statistical analysis in experiments.
*   Limited discussion of robustness to noise.
*   Asymptotic conditions may limit practical interpretability.
*   Arbitrary choice of parameter λ without further justification.



**Overall Assessment:**  This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of robust low-rank matrix recovery. The technical soundness, originality, and potential significance of the work are compelling. While the paper could benefit from improved clarity and more thorough experimental analysis, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points mentioned above, particularly improving clarity and adding statistical significance testing to the results.
