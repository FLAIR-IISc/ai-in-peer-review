The paper addresses the problem of robust PCA in the presence of coherent data, arguing that standard RPCA can struggle when the underlying low-rank structure exhibits clustering. The authors propose using a low-rank dictionary within the LRR framework to mitigate this issue, and provide theoretical analysis supporting this approach. They also present an algorithm utilizing RPCA to estimate the dictionary and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on both synthetic and real-world motion data.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the limitations of RPCA when dealing with coherent data structures. The connection between coherence parameters and underlying data structure (clustering) is insightful and well-explained, particularly the "µ2-phenomenon". The theoretical results, particularly Theorem 1, provide a strong foundation for the proposed approach. The algorithm is reasonably straightforward and builds on existing work (RPCA and LRR). The experimental validation is thorough, with results on both synthetic data and a real-world dataset (motion sequences), demonstrating improvement over RPCA.  The related work section is comprehensive, citing relevant papers in the field. The notation is generally clear, and the summary of notations section is helpful.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. The theoretical analysis, while present, is quite dense and requires significant effort to follow. The conditions for Theorem 1, particularly regarding the rank of A and the dictionary construction, seem restrictive and might limit practical applicability. The discussion of noise (Theorem 2) feels somewhat tacked on and lacks the same level of detail as the noiseless case.  While the algorithm leverages RPCA, the improvement presented seems incremental, rather than a dramatic breakthrough. The paper doesn’t fully explore potential challenges of dictionary learning in the unsupervised setting, and relies heavily on the assumption that RPCA provides a good starting point.  The link between the proposed method and existing matrix factorization techniques, mentioned in the introduction, could be further elaborated. While the authors acknowledge that their approach is preliminary, a more detailed discussion of future work directions (e.g., exploring different dictionary learning methods, addressing the noise model more comprehensively) would be beneficial.  The experimental section's focus is heavily geared towards demonstrating improvement over RPCA, and could benefit from comparison with other state-of-the-art robust PCA methods beyond RPCA and LRR.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novelty: The paper identifies a weakness in existing RPCA methods (sensitivity to data coherence) and proposes a theoretically sound solution using low-rank dictionaries within the LRR framework.
*   Theoretical Soundness:  The theoretical analysis provides justification for the proposed approach and establishes conditions for exact recovery.
*   Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and results.
*   Significance: The problem addressed (robust PCA) is important, and the proposed approach has the potential to improve performance in applications where data coherence is a concern.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Restrictive Conditions: The theoretical guarantees rely on conditions that may be difficult to satisfy in practice.
*   Incremental Improvement:  The experimental results, while positive, demonstrate a relatively incremental improvement over existing methods.
*   Limited Exploration: The paper does not fully explore the challenges and potential extensions of the proposed approach.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper is a solid contribution to the field of robust PCA. While the theoretical results have some limitations, the authors clearly identify an important problem, propose a reasonable solution, and provide evidence of its effectiveness. The paper meets the criteria for NIPS acceptance, despite not being a groundbreaking advance.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, focusing on clarifying the practical implications of the theoretical conditions and expanding the discussion of future work.  Specifically, more discussion on alternative dictionary learning techniques would strengthen the work.
