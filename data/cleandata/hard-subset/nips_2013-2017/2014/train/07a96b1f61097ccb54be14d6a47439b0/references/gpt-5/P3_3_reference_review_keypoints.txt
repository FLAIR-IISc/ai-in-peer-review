- Paper studies recovering low-rank L0 and sparse S0 from X=L0+S0 when L0 is coherent, arguing PCP degrades due to inflated row-space coherence μ2 in clustered/union-of-subspaces data.  
- Proposes using LRR with a learned low-rank dictionary A and proves exact/near recovery if A is low-rank and its column space spans L0 (U0 ⊆ UA), eliminating dependence on μ2.  
- Practical two-stage algorithm: run PCP to estimate L0, form a normalized low-rank dictionary from that estimate, then run LRR with this A.  
- Theorems state noiseless/noisy recovery conditions (rank(L0) ≤ rank(A) ≤ O(n2/(μ1(A) log n1)); |Ω| ≤ 0.5mn/0.35mn) and explicitly remove μ2 from bounds.  
- Main theoretical gap: assumption U0 ⊆ UA is strong and acts like supervision/oracle knowledge, but no analysis showing PCP-derived A satisfies this when PCP is imperfect.  
- Adaptivity concern: proofs assume A fixed and independent of Ω, but the practical algorithm makes A adaptive to X and S0, and no guarantees show adaptivity doesn’t break results.  
- Noisy-case bounds scale as O(√mn ε), which may be loose; no discussion on whether dependence on rA is achievable or tightness of bounds.  
- Randomness/modeling assumptions for S0 (support distribution, independence from A, magnitude) are only sketched and should be explicitly stated in theorem statements.  
- Methodologically, the two-stage PCP→LRR is simple and sensible, and λ=1/√n1 is standard, but sensitivity to rank truncation r̂0 and dictionary conditioning is not analyzed.  
- Complexity claims (LRR cost O(n2 rA)) are plausible for low-rank A, but per-iteration cost and dependence on dictionary width d (often d=n) need clarification.  
- Synthetic experiments show larger success regions than PCP (+47% claimed), aligning with theory, but success criteria and variability (confidence intervals over trials) are not fully reported.  
- Experimental baselines are limited; recommend comparisons to Outlier Pursuit, OP/OR-PCA, IRLS/nonconvex methods, and LRR with A=X used as a recovery method.  
- Real-data motion experiments use ±1 imputation for missing entries, which is unrealistic; recommend testing on genuine missingness and reporting recovery metrics (relative L-error, precision/recall) besides clustering error.  
- Runtime comparisons need more detail to support the “1.2× PCP” efficiency claim.  
- Paper is generally well written; exposition on coherence and the μ2-phenomenon is clear and insightful.  
- Clarity suggestions: explicitly state probabilistic models/independence in Theorems 1–2, note relations (A=I or orthogonal A reduces to PCP), move important footnotes into main text, and fix minor typos.  
- Include a brief proof sketch explaining why μ2 disappears under U0 ⊆ UA and why μ1(A) remains in bounds.  
- Originality: framing coherent data via union-of-subspaces and showing LRR can avoid μ2 is a valuable, novel perspective despite LRR itself being prior work.  
- Significance: work could impact robust subspace learning if the theory–practice gap (adaptive A) is bridged; empirical results are promising but need broader validation.  
- Pros: clearly identifies μ2-phenomenon, provides theoretical guarantees for low-rank dictionaries, offers a practical two-stage algorithm, and shows relevant application gains.  
- Cons: strong U0 ⊆ UA assumption without unsupervised guarantees, limited baselines and some questionable experimental choices, no sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters, potentially loose noisy bounds, and underspecified complexity.  
- Key recommendations: provide theory for the two-stage adaptive procedure (stability guarantees if PCP is δ-close), compare to more robust PCA baselines including LRR with A=X, report standard errors/success criteria and runtimes, use realistic missingness and recovery metrics on real data, and discuss/mitigate cases where μ1(A) could be large.
