Summary
This paper addresses recovery of low-rank matrices from grossly corrupted observations when the low-rank component is coherent (i.e., exhibits large coherence parameters), a regime where RPCA is known to degrade. The authors analyze how common data structures—especially clustering (union-of-subspaces)—inflate the second coherence parameter µ2 and consequently worsen RPCA’s guarantees (“µ2-phenomenon”). They propose to use Low-Rank Representation (LRR) with a pre-constructed dictionary A, and prove that if A is low-rank and its column space contains the true column space (U0 ⊂ UA), then exact (noiseless) and near-exact (noisy) recovery are possible without invoking µ2 in the conditions. They give bounds on rank(L0) in terms of rank(A), µ1(A), n1, n2 and the corruption level |Ω|. Practically, they propose an unsupervised two-stage algorithm: run RPCA to obtain an estimate of L0, use it to build a low-rank, column-normalized dictionary Â, and then run LRR to recover the final low-rank component. Experiments on synthetic clustered data and corrupted motion trajectories (Hopkins-like sequences) show that the proposed pipeline improves over RPCA and further improves subspace clustering errors when used as preprocessing for SIM/LRRx/SSC.

Relation to prior work
- RPCA (Candès et al.) provides convex recovery guarantees under incoherence; this paper argues that µ2 naturally increases with the number of subspaces, explaining RPCA’s limitations on clustered data.
- LRR (Liu et al.) was introduced primarily with A = X for subspace segmentation; here the authors analyze LRR with general, learned dictionaries, and show a new recovery theory that avoids µ2 if A is low-rank and contains U0.
- The work relates to robust matrix factorization and Outlier Pursuit/robust subspace recovery, but differs by emphasizing how dictionary choice mitigates coherence-induced failures.

Quality
- Theory: The main theorems formally establish exact and noisy recovery via LRR with low-rank dictionaries, under column normalization, U0 ⊂ UA, and standard sparsity assumptions (≤ ~50% entry corruption). The results cleanly remove µ2 from the conditions and provide improved rank bounds O(n2/log n1) vs RPCA’s O(n2/(log n1)^2). The conditions are strong—U0 ⊂ UA is necessary for exact equality AZ∗ = L0—and the guarantees depend on µ1(A), shifting the coherence control onto the chosen dictionary. The paper claims tolerance to imperfect A.
- Algorithm: The RPCA→dictionary→LRR heuristic is simple and plausible: RPCA is often “good enough” to recover a basis that approximately contains U0; then LRR leverages that basis to avoid µ2. However, there is no formal guarantee for the unsupervised algorithm beyond the special case when RPCA is already exact.
- Experiments: Synthetic experiments systematically vary rank and corruption, showing a substantially larger exact-recovery region (reported as 47% wider). Motion segmentation experiments on 11 sequences with random ±1 imputation for missing entries show consistent error reduction when using the proposed recovery as preprocessing. The setups are reasonable but relatively small; additional baselines and diagnostics would strengthen the evidence.

Clarity
The manuscript is generally well organized. The exposition of coherence parameters and the µ2-phenomenon is clear and useful. Notation is dense but standard. Some terminology is awkward (“column-wisely”), and there are minor typos (e.g., “PRCA”). Theorems are stated clearly, though proofs are deferred to the full paper. Figures are referenced appropriately (not available in the excerpt).

Originality
- Conceptually novel link: identifying the dependence of µ2 on the number of clusters and showing how an appropriately chosen dictionary can eliminate this dependence in LRR’s guarantees.
- Theoretical novelty: recovery conditions for LRR with general low-rank dictionaries that avoid µ2 and slightly improve rank scaling.
- Practical novelty: a simple, effective two-stage recovery pipeline leveraging RPCA to build the dictionary. While incremental, it addresses an important failure mode.

Significance
Robust recovery in coherent regimes is practically significant in vision, imaging, and recommendation when data cluster in subspaces. The theory provides a new lens on coherence and dictates dictionary design principles. The practical algorithm is simple to adopt and appears to give tangible gains. The ideas are likely to spur follow-on work on dictionary learning for robust recovery and robust GPCA.

Pros
- Clear identification and empirical demonstration of the µ2-phenomenon due to clustering.
- Recovery theory for LRR with low-rank dictionaries that avoids µ2 and slightly improves rank bounds.
- Simple, practical algorithm with consistent improvements over RPCA in synthetic and real(istic) data.
- Insightful reinterpretation of coherence as reflecting intrinsic structure, not just a proof device.

Cons
- Strong assumption U0 ⊂ UA in the theorems; unsupervised algorithm lacks corresponding guarantees unless RPCA is already exact.
- Theoretical dependence shifts to µ1(A); how to ensure small µ1(A) in practice is not analyzed.
- Experimental scope is limited: small number of real sequences, synthetic setting lacks variance/error bars, and limited baselines (e.g., Outlier Pursuit, robust MF).
- Heuristic choices (rank threshold 1e-3 σ1, λ = 1/√n1, ε setting) lack sensitivity analysis.
- No ablations on dictionary choice (random low-rank A, A = X, iterative refinement).

Suggestions and questions
- Provide empirical measurements of µ1(A), µ2(L0), and how they change across experiments to directly support the “avoid µ2” claim.
- Add baselines: Outlier Pursuit, robust matrix factorization, LRR with A = X, random low-rank A, and an ablation of the normalization/low-rank truncation steps.
- Report runtime with solver details, scaling with n and rA, and show that the 1.2× overhead is typical.
- Sensitivity analyses for λ, rank threshold, and ε; discuss guidance when A is not orthonormal.
- Clarify the noisy guarantee: bounds scale as 8√(mn)ε, which can be loose; can tighter, instance-dependent bounds be shown?
- Discuss non-entrywise corruption models (e.g., column outliers) and missing-not-at-random patterns.
- Minor edits: fix typos; improve wording; ensure complete citations.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a clear and useful insight into coherence, a solid theoretical contribution for LRR with low-rank dictionaries that addresses a real shortcoming of RPCA, and a simple algorithm that improves practice. Strengthening the experimental evaluation and clarifying practical conditions for small µ1(A) would further improve the paper.
