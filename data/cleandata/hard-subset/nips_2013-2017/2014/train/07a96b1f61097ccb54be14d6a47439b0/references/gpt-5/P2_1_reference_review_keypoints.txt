- Summary: Paper studies low-rank+ sparse recovery when the low-rank part is coherent and identifies a µ2-phenomenon where right-space coherence grows with subspace clustering, harming RPCA.
- Proposed solution: Analyze LRR with a learned low-rank, column-normalized dictionary A whose column space contains U0, yielding recovery guarantees independent of µ2 (and µ3).
- Theoretical claims: Under U0 ⊂ UA and column-normalized low-rank A, LRR exactly recovers AZ = L0 (noiseless) and is stable in noise, with bounds depending on µ1(A), rank(A), and log factors.
- Practical algorithm: Unsupervised two-stage heuristic—run RPCA to estimate L0, truncate/normalize to form A, then run LRR to obtain final recovery.
- Empirical results: Synthetic union-of-subspaces experiments show substantially larger recovery region than RPCA; motion segmentation experiments show consistent downstream clustering improvements.
- Theory quality: Theorems are plausible and identify the key mechanism, but proofs are deferred and a proof sketch or supplemental proofs are needed to assess tightness and novelty of technical arguments.
- Algorithm quality: Heuristic is simple and well motivated; the informal claim that success probability ≥ RPCA’s lacks formal analysis under approximate subspace inclusion.
- Experimental quality: Strong synthetic match to hypothesis and positive real-data gains, but limited baselines, no sensitivity analyses (rank, λ, ε, conditioning), and unusual handling of missing data.
- Clarity: Manuscript is generally well written with clear definitions and motivation, but needs proof sketches, clarification on µ3 behavior, solver/complexity details, and minor typo fixes.
- Originality: Novel theoretical analysis of LRR with general low-rank dictionaries and formal noisy stability; µ2-phenomenon identification and the two-stage heuristic are useful contributions.
- Significance: Addresses an important practical failure mode of RPCA and offers a potentially influential fix, though U0 ⊂ UA limits theoretical generality.
- Pros: Clear identification of µ2 failure mode, new µ2-free LRR guarantees, simple practical algorithm with empirical gains, and useful insights on coherence.
- Cons: Strong U0 ⊂ UA assumption without approximate-analysis, missing proofs, limited baselines and ablations, and presentation issues including handling of missing data.
- Key suggestions: Provide analysis for approximate subspace inclusion, ablation studies on rank/normalization, comparisons to more robust subspace recovery baselines, and include proof sketches or full proofs.
- Overall assessment: Solid and timely contribution likely acceptable if strengthened with proofs, approximate-inclusion theory, and broader empirical validation; reviewer leans toward acceptance contingent on addressing concerns.
