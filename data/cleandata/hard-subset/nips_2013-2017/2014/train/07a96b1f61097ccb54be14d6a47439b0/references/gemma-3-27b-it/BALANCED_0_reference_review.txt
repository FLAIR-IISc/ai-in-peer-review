This paper addresses the problem of recovering low-rank matrices corrupted by sparse errors, focusing on scenarios where the low-rank component exhibits clustering structures, leading to high coherence. The authors argue that standard Robust PCA (RPCA) struggles in these cases and propose a Low-Rank Representation (LRR) approach with a carefully designed dictionary to mitigate the issue. They provide theoretical analysis demonstrating conditions under which LRR avoids the coherence limitations of RPCA, and an algorithm that leverages RPCA to construct the dictionary for LRR. Extensive experiments on both synthetic and real motion data demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly identifies a limitation of RPCA – its sensitivity to coherence arising from clustering structures in the low-rank component – and frames it as a significant problem.
* **Theoretical Contribution:** The paper provides theoretical results establishing conditions for LRR to overcome the coherence limitations of RPCA. The connection between dictionary design, low rankness, and coherence is insightful.  The theorems relating to both noiseless and noisy recovery are valuable.
* **Practical Algorithm:** The proposed algorithm builds on existing methods (RPCA and LRR) and offers a practical way to address the identified problem. Leveraging RPCA for dictionary construction is a sensible approach.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing improvements over RPCA. The inclusion of motion data adds relevance.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized.  The notation is clear and the mathematical derivations seem sound (though a detailed verification would require significant effort). The inclusion of a detailed notation section is helpful.
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The paper positions itself well within the context of existing literature on RPCA, LRR, and matrix factorization. It properly cites relevant works. The discussion connecting coherence to underlying data structures is particularly valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Incremental Improvement:** While the empirical results show improvements over RPCA, the magnitude of the gains sometimes appears modest, particularly on the synthetic data. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of the practical impact of these improvements.
* **Dictionary Learning Heuristic:** The algorithm for dictionary construction relies on RPCA, which itself might struggle in highly coherent scenarios.  The paper doesn't fully address potential failure modes of this approach if the initial RPCA estimate is poor. More robust dictionary learning techniques could be explored.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance likely depends on the choice of parameters like λ. A more detailed analysis of parameter sensitivity and guidelines for parameter selection would be beneficial. While λ = 1/sqrt(n1) is used consistently, a justification beyond that being a standard choice would be helpful.
* **Theoretical Assumptions:** The theoretical analysis relies on assumptions about the dictionary (column-wise unit norm, subspace containment). The practicality and robustness of these assumptions in real-world scenarios should be discussed further.  Is there a mechanism to verify or approximate these conditions?
* **Limited Comparison to Related Work:** The comparison to related work is good, but could be expanded.  For example, other dictionary learning techniques or robust subspace learning methods could be considered. Are there alternative approaches for handling high coherence data that were not explored and why?
* **Figure Clarity:** Figure 2, demonstrating the “µ2-phenomenon,” is difficult to interpret. The axes labels and visual representation could be improved for better clarity.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses an important limitation of a widely used technique (RPCA).
* The theoretical contributions provide valuable insights into the relationship between coherence, data structure, and matrix recovery.
* The proposed algorithm is practical and demonstrates empirical improvements.
* The paper is well-written and thoroughly researched.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical improvements are sometimes modest.
* The dictionary learning approach relies on a potentially unstable initial RPCA estimate.
* The theoretical assumptions may be restrictive.
* The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to alternative methods.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of robust matrix recovery.  The authors have identified a meaningful problem, provided theoretical justification for their approach, and demonstrated its effectiveness through experiments. While the improvements over existing methods are not always dramatic, and the algorithm relies on some potentially limiting assumptions, the overall quality of the work is high. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Expand the empirical analysis:** Include a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the observed improvements.
* **Investigate alternative dictionary learning strategies:** Explore more robust approaches for constructing the dictionary matrix.
* **Analyze parameter sensitivity:** Provide guidelines for choosing appropriate parameters.
* **Discuss the limitations of the theoretical assumptions:**  Address the practicality and robustness of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.
* **Enhance Figure 2:** Improve the clarity and interpretability of Figure 2.
* **Broaden the comparison to related work:** Include a more comprehensive comparison to alternative methods.
