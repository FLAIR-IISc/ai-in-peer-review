- Paper proposes a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, extending kernel-based supervised optimization to include unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral.  
- High technical quality with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Introduces a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information.  
- Resulting classifier differs from the classical representer-theorem solution but remains elegant and computationally tractable.  
- Question: Equation 3’s validity when the associated kernel is not PSD—does it still hold, and a derivation/proof is missing.  
- Question: Experimental protocol unclear—how were the "optimal" parameters chosen and what precisely is meant by "optimal"?  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, making main ideas easy to follow.  
- Several typos and minor writing errors need correction (listed examples: Line 82, Line 83, Section 4 title, Lines 247, 283, 352).  
- Related to a recent NIPS paper but novel in essence, with clear differences from prior work.  
- Results are significant; extensive experiments across multiple datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.
