- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and introducing data-dependent "Fredholm kernels."  
- Formalizes a new "noise assumption" for leveraging unlabeled data, distinct from manifold and cluster assumptions.  
- Theoretical contributions (Theorems 2–4) show noise suppression via re-weighting principal components and improved kernel approximations; connects Fredholm kernels to PCA.  
- Derivation of Fredholm kernels (Equation 4) is elegant and provides a fresh perspective complementary to existing methods.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic experiments violating cluster assumptions and real-world text/digit tasks show competitive performance and robustness to added noise.  
- Main theoretical limitation: analysis assumes infinite unlabeled data (continuous kernels) and does not fully address effects of finite-sample discretization (Equation 2).  
- Insufficient clarification on how practical discretization affects noise suppression properties and algorithmic behavior.  
- Limited comparisons to key related and recent semi-supervised methods (e.g., graph-based and deep semi-supervised approaches).  
- Does not clearly distinguish the noise assumption from manifold assumptions in practice despite claiming compatibility.  
- Lacks practical guidance for selecting kernel parameters (e.g., t in Equation 4) and for understanding the impact of normalization (Equation 5).  
- Potential overstatement: synthetic examples demonstrating the noise assumption may be artificial and do not prove prevalence in real-world data.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and novel; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to bridge theory-practice gap, expand comparisons, and provide practical parameter guidance.
