- Proposes a semi-supervised framework casting prediction as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent Fredholm kernel KF = K(l+u) KH K(l+u)ᵀ (PSD if KH PSD).  
- Unlabeled data enter via K(l+u) and normalization; motivated as denoising under a “noise assumption” that low-variance directions are label-uninformative.  
- Theoretically, for linear outer kernel and Gaussian kH, the method shrinks low-variance principal directions (“soft-thresholded PCA”).  
- Theoretically, under a Gaussian signal-plus-orthogonal-noise model with infinite unlabeled data, Fredholm kernels give unbiased, lower-variance estimators of the true kernel for linear and Gaussian targets.  
- Empirically competitive or superior to RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC on synthetic and some real datasets, especially under added noise.  
- Places work relative to integral operators/RKHS, Fredholm-based density ratio estimation, kernel mean shrinkage, and data-dependent/manifold regularization kernels.  
- Positive: the reduction to a PSD KF and the normalized variant are elegant and correct.  
- Positive: variance-reduction results are plausible and the PCA connection is intuitive.  
- Positive: empirical gains are tangible when noise is present and cluster assumptions fail.  
- Concern: theory assumes access to the true pX (infinite unlabeled); no finite-sample error quantification for empirical integrals.  
- Concern: no generalization bounds or consistency rates provided for finite unlabeled sample size u.  
- Concern: the “noise assumption” is informal and lacks analysis of failure modes where low-variance directions carry label information.  
- Concern: scalability not analyzed — constructing KF costs O((l+u)^2) memory and O((l+u)^3) time; prediction needs O(l+u) kernel evaluations.  
- Concern: experimental methodology lacks hyperparameter tuning details, variance reporting (no error bars), and broader baselines (e.g., label propagation, diffusion kernels).  
- Clarity: generally readable with a clear operator viewpoint, but has typos/notation issues and many proofs deferred (need sketches).  
- Originality: Fredholm-equation derivation and PSD-insight are nice but the construction is closely related to existing data-dependent/diffusion kernels and needs tighter positioning/citations.  
- Significance: simple, general, and potentially useful with an insightful variance-reduction perspective, but impact limited by overlap with prior work and missing finite-sample/scalability analysis.  
- Pros: clear/principled PSD unlabeled-data-dependent kernel; theoretical variance reduction; soft-threshold PCA view; empirical gains; easy to implement.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs diffusion/data-dependent kernels; reliance on infinite unlabeled data; no finite-sample guarantees; scalability and prediction-time costs not addressed; limited baselines and uncertainty reporting.  
- Suggested improvements: provide finite-sample consistency/rates, formalize the noise assumption and failure modes, and better position vs diffusion/manifold kernels with additional comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: include computational complexity, propose scalable approximations (Nyström, random features), add proof sketches, tighten notation, evaluate other losses, and report error bars and tuning details.  
- Overall: a compelling, well-motivated semi-supervised kernel construction with promising results under noise but needing stronger positioning, finite-sample and scalability analysis; reviewer leans borderline accept pending revisions.
