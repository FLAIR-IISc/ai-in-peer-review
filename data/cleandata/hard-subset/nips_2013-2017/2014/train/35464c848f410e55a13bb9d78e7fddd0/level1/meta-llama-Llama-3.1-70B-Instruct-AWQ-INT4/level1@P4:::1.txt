**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. This approach leads to the construction of new data-dependent kernels, called Fredholm kernels, which can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning. The authors provide theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels can improve the performance of classifiers in the standard semi-supervised learning setting.

**Related Work**

The paper is related to previous work on kernel and integral methods in machine learning, particularly [10], which introduced Fredholm integral equations for density ratio estimation and covariate shift. The authors also mention related work on kernel mean embedding and semi-supervised learning techniques, such as manifold regularization.
