- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by formulating learning as solving a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding "Fredholm kernels."
- Connects the framework to existing kernel methods and derives a data-dependent kernel interpretation.
- Provides theoretical analysis under a “noise assumption,” arguing noise suppression benefits.
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real-world datasets with comparisons to several baselines.
- Strength: novel perspective integrating unlabeled data and introducing a new class of kernels.
- Strength: theoretical grounding on noise suppression, albeit under restrictive conditions.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and a well-structured related work section.
- Weakness: the "noise assumption" is vaguely defined and needs a more precise mathematical formulation.
- Weakness: theoretical results rely on strong assumptions (e.g., Gaussian distributions, specific kernels) limiting generalizability.
- Weakness: experimental section lacks an ablation study on kernel parameters and amount of unlabeled data.
- Weakness: dense and sometimes inconsistent notation (multiple definitions/normalizations of kF) hinders readability.
- Minor issue: explanation of naming conflict for "Fredholm kernel" feels cursory.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical attempt, empirical performance, and clear connections to prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: vague noise assumption, limited theory generality, missing ablations, and complex notation.
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but recommends weak accept pending clearer noise assumption, fuller ablation studies, and simplified notation.
