- Introduces a novel Fredholm learning framework reformulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Proposes Fredholm kernels, a new class of data-dependent kernels leveraging both labeled and unlabeled data via density information.  
- Introduces a novel noise assumption that provides an alternative perspective beyond cluster and manifold assumptions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including variance reduction properties and approximation of target kernels under noise.  
- Demonstrates noise suppression mechanisms and theoretical grounding for the new semi-supervised paradigm.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real-world datasets (text classification, sentiment, digit recognition) with consistent improvements, notably in noisy settings.  
- Presents normalized Fredholm kernels and offers clear, well-organized exposition of framework, theory, and experiments.  
- Practical implementation may be complex due to computational overhead from density-dependent kernel formulation.  
- Related-work discussion is limited; lacks detailed comparison with recent semi-supervised and noise-robust methods.  
- Scalability is unclear: experiments are on moderate-size datasets, leaving large-scale and high-dimensional performance open.  
- Validation of the noise assumption is mostly synthetic; more real-world evaluation is needed to confirm applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, and empirically effective, especially with noisy/low-label data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved computational/scalability concerns and limited real-data validation of the noise assumption.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability concerns and expand related-work discussion.
