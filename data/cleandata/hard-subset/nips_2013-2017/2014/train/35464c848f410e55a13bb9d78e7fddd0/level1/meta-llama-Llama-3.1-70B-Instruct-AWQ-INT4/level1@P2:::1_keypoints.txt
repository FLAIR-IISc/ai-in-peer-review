- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces the Fredholm kernel, a data-dependent kernel combining labeled and unlabeled data.  
- Provides theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels effectively utilize unlabeled data under a newly proposed "noise assumption."  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains main ideas and contributions.  
- Thorough related-work review and clear positioning within existing research.  
- Technical content is sound with a clear derivation of the Fredholm learning framework and kernel.  
- Main strength: offers a new perspective on semi-supervised learning and a novel kernel that leverages unlabeled data.  
- Clearly explains the "noise assumption" and its relevance to semi-supervised learning.  
- Experimental results are strong, showing Fredholm kernel outperforming state-of-the-art on several benchmarks.  
- Provides thorough analysis and discussion of experimental findings.  
- Limitation: lacks clear guidance for choosing optimal Fredholm kernel parameters (kernel width, regularization).  
- Limitation: does not clearly relate the Fredholm kernel to standard kernels (e.g., Gaussian, polynomial).  
- Limitation: insufficient explanation of the mechanisms behind the kernel's strong empirical performance.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address parameter selection and kernel comparisons.
