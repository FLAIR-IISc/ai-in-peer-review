- Introduces a novel framework that formulates supervised and semi-supervised learning as solving a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a new data-dependent "Fredholm kernel."

- The Fredholm kernel combines an "outer" kernel k and an "inner" kernel kH and naturally incorporates the unlabeled data distribution pX.

- Proposes the "noise assumption": low-variance directions in the data distribution are uninformative, providing a new rationale for when unlabeled data helps beyond cluster/manifold assumptions.

- Theoretical results show the linear Fredholm kernel performs a data-driven re-weighting of principal components (soft-thresholding PCA), giving clear intuition (Theorem 2).

- Proves under a signal-plus-orthogonal-Gaussian-noise model that linear and Gaussian Fredholm kernels can yield lower-variance, unbiased estimates of the true kernel compared to naive kernels (Theorems 3 and 4).

- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrates competitive performance, especially on tasks designed to validate the noise assumption where cluster-based SSL methods fail.

- The formulation is original, elegant, and well-motivated, and the paper is clearly written and well-organized.

- Major practical concern: computational scalability — direct solution requires inverting an (l+u)x(l+u) matrix and naively constructing KF costs O(l^2*(l+u)^2*D), which is prohibitive for large u and not addressed.

- Hyperparameter complexity: requires selecting both inner and outer kernels and their parameters (e.g., widths r and t), making model selection non-trivial and potentially sensitive.

- Relationship to existing methods could be deeper: connections to metric learning, whitening, and other data preprocessing techniques deserve further comparison to clarify unique contributions.

- Theoretical analysis relies on a specific linear signal-plus-orthogonal-noise model; discussion of extensions to more general manifolds or noise distributions and a formal statement of the noise assumption would strengthen generality.
