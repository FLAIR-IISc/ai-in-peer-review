- Proposes a new supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces "Fredholm kernels" that construct new data-dependent kernels.  
- Introduces the "noise assumption" offering a new perspective on how unlabeled data helps semi-supervised learning.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis for linear and Gaussian Fredholm kernels.  
- Shows theoretical and empirical evidence that Fredholm kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the noise assumption.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets are impressive and show competitive performance.  
- Comparisons with Transductive SVM and LapRLSC help evaluate method performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and notation and derivations are explained in detail.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of connections to other semi-supervised methods.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could include more datasets and comparisons with additional state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: assumes feature space X is Euclidean RD, which may limit practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to expand comparative discussion and experiments.
