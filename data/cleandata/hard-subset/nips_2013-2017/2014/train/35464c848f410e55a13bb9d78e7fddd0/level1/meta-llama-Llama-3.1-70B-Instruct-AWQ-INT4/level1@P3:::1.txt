This paper proposes a new framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. The authors introduce the concept of Fredholm kernels, which can be interpreted as constructing new data-dependent kernels. The paper provides both theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" and demonstrate competitive performance in the standard semi-supervised learning setting.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed framework and its connections to previous work. The introduction of the "noise assumption" is a valuable contribution, as it provides a new perspective on the role of unlabeled data in semi-supervised learning. The theoretical results, including the analysis of linear and Gaussian Fredholm kernels, are rigorous and provide insight into the behavior of the proposed method.

The experimental results are also impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of Fredholm kernels on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The comparison with other semi-supervised learning methods, such as Transductive SVM and LapRLSC, is useful in evaluating the performance of the proposed method.
