- Proposes reformulating supervised/semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" built from unlabeled data.  
- Introduces a new "noise assumption": low-variance directions in unlabeled data are uninformative for classification (treated as noise).  
- Theoretically and empirically shows Fredholm kernels can suppress noise and improve classification performance.  
- Builds on kernel methods and integral operators, distinguishing itself from manifold/cluster assumptions and related prior work.  
- Strength: technically sound with rigorous derivation and insightful theoretical analysis (Section 4) on noise suppression.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear writing, organization, consistent notation, and effective illustrative figures.  
- Strength: original contribution — new noise assumption and Fredholm kernels derived from integral equations offer a fresh perspective.  
- Strength: significant potential — addresses leveraging unlabeled data and shows competitive results on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees are limited to specific cases (e.g., Gaussian noise, particular kernels) and lack broader generality.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability of Fredholm kernels are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with recent state-of-the-art semi-supervised methods (e.g., contemporary deep learning approaches).  
- Weakness: applicability of the noise assumption to diverse real-world problems is not clearly established.  
- Weakness: clarification needed on practical differences between normalized and unnormalized Fredholm kernels and their costs.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, mathematically rigorous framework with clear empirical support and potential to inspire new research directions.  
- Con acceptance: limited theoretical scope, missing SOTA comparisons, and unresolved computational/ applicability concerns.  
- Overall: high-quality, meaningful contribution that merits acceptance despite noted limitations.
