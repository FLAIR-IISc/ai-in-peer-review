- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that naturally incorporate unlabeled data into kernel construction.  
- Proposes the "noise assumption": low-variance directions are uninformative and should be suppressed, offering a new SSL motivation distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions.  
- Connects to prior work on Fredholm equations for density ratio estimation and data-dependent kernels for manifold regularization, situating the contribution in existing literature.  
- Originality and significance: Fredholm formulation and noise assumption are novel, principled, and broaden understanding of when SSL helps.  
- Technical quality: Solid theoretical analysis showing Fredholm kernels can act like a soft-thresholding PCA to suppress noise and yield lower-variance estimators.  
- Clarity and empirical support: Well-written derivations, convincing synthetic examples, and strong performance on diverse text and image datasets.  
- Practical guidance weakness: Limited intuition and recommendations for choosing the inner/outer kernels and associated hyperparameters.  
- Scalability concern: No discussion of computational cost for constructing the Fredholm kernel matrix that depends on total labeled+unlabeled points (l+u).  
- Theoretical scope limitation: Analysis relies on idealized conditions (e.g., infinite unlabeled data); limited discussion of finite-sample guarantees.  
- Overall: High-quality, original contribution with solid theory and experiments, but with open questions on parameter selection and scalability.
