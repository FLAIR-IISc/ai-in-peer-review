This paper describes a framework specifically useful for semi-supervised learning based on the Fredholm kernel. The classical supervised learning optimization problem being solved in kernel-based methods has been extended to incorporate unlabeled data, resulting in a discrete version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is of high technical quality, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and solid experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled data. The classifying function is different from the classical represent theory solution but is still elegant and easy to compute.

I have a few comments: One is related to equation 3, where the proposed optimization problem solution is described. Since the authors mentioned that the associated kernel does not necessarily have PSD, does equation 3 still apply in this case? It would also be good to see how equation 3 was derived. Another comment is related to the experimental section, where the authors chose an "optimal" parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were those parameters determined? What is meant by "optimal"?

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part. It is easy to understand, and the main ideas are adequately explained. However, there are some typos, for example:
* Line 82: "... a Euclidean will be ..." -> ... a Euclidean will be ...
* Line 83: "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredholm" -> Fredholm
* Line 247: "proportional" -> proportional
* Line 283: "i.e., or ..." -> i.e., for ...
* Line 352: The sentence needs to be rewritten.

Originality
The proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is new in essence. It is clear how this work differs from this publication and others.

Significance
The results are significant and meaningful. A substantial effort was made to test the proposed method on several datasets in various application domains.

A well-written paper with a good theoretical foundation and a solid experimental section.
