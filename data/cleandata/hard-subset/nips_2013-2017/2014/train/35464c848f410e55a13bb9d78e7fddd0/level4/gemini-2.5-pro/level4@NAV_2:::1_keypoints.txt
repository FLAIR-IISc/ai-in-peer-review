- Introduces a framework reformulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation that yields data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" using unlabeled data.  
- The method is technically sound with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experiments demonstrating denoising and improved generalization.  
- Derivation of the predictive solution is clear and computationally tractable, and the Fredholm-kernel idea is elegant and powerful.  
- Clarification needed: confirm whether the solution (involving matrix inversion) remains valid when the outer kernel k is not positive semi-definite.  
- Clarification needed: include the detailed derivation of Equation 3 from Equation 2 (e.g., in an appendix) for completeness.  
- Clarification needed: specify how "optimal" experimental parameters were chosen (e.g., cross-validation protocol, held-out set, and optimization criterion).  
- Writing and exposition are generally clear and well organized, but a final proofreading is recommended.  
- Minor typographical errors to fix (examples): Line 82 "will a Euclidean" → "will be a Euclidean"; Line 83 incomplete phrase about label set Y; Section 4 title "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"; Line 247 "porportional" → "proportional"; Line 283 "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."; rephrase sentence at Line 352 for flow.  
- Originality: the approach is novel and distinct from prior work (e.g., density ratio estimation, manifold regularization), with related work adequately cited.  
- Significance: the formalized "noise assumption" and empirical results suggest strong potential impact and usefulness for future semi-supervised learning research.  
- Overall: a well-written paper with strong theory and experiments that makes a valuable contribution to semi-supervised learning.
