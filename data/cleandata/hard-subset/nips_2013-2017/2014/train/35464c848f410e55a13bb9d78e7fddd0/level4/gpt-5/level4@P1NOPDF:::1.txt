This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. It extends the classical kernel-based supervised optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled data, yielding a discretized version of a Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is technically strong, with claims supported by solid theoretical analysis and convincing experiments. The proposed formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information. The resulting classifier departs from the classical representer theorem solution, yet remains elegant and computationally simple.

I have a few comments:
- Equation (3): The authors state that the associated kernel need not be PSD. Does Equation (3) still hold when the kernel is indefinite? Clarifying the conditions under which the solution in (3) is valid would strengthen the paper. A brief derivation or a pointer to a proof of how Equation (3) is obtained would also be valuable.
- Experimental protocol: The authors report using an “optimal” parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were these parameters selected? Was “optimal” determined via cross-validation on a validation set, a fixed search grid, or another criterion? Please clarify the selection procedure and whether the same search space and protocol were used across methods.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. The main ideas are easy to follow and are explained adequately. There are, however, several minor typographical and phrasing issues:
- Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” → “… will be a Euclidean …”
- Line 83: “or” is missing.
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” → “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or …” → “That is, for …”
- Line 352: The sentence is unclear and should be rewritten.

Originality
The approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is substantively novel. The distinctions from [9] and other related work are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are meaningful and suggest practical impact. The authors invested significant effort in evaluating the method on multiple datasets across diverse application domains.

Overall
A well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and convincing empirical results. Addressing the questions about Equation (3) in the indefinite-kernel setting and clarifying the “optimal” parameter selection procedure would further improve the paper’s rigor and reproducibility.
