

The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, and the paper is overall very interesting.  

Nevertheless, I have a few suggestions and questions for clarification:  

- **Section 2 (equations 1 to 99)**: The Nyström method also approximates integral equations (e.g., in kernel PCA). The authors should explicitly mention this connection and clarify in what specific sense their approach is similar to or differs from the Nyström method.  

- **Section 2, Equation (2)**: Approaches using operator equations have been studied in the context of support vector machines and kernel methods. The authors should cite relevant literature and explain how Equation (2) relates to these prior works—whether it constitutes a special case or differs in key aspects.  

- **Section 2, Line 128 (Representer Theorem)**: Equation (2) is less common in learning theory. The authors assume the existence of a representer theorem for their formulation. They should either provide a proof of the representer theorem for this setting or supply a precise reference and clarify how Equation (2) fits within existing representer theorem frameworks.  

- **Section 4, Equation (8)**: The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. How does this approach differ from established methods like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels in terms of formulation, assumptions, or practical performance?  

**Typos**:  
- Line 165: "Not that" → "Note that"  
- Line 221: "achived" → "achieved"  
- Line 341: "chossing" → "choosing"  
- Line 672: "Table ??" → "Table 1" (assuming standard paper structure)  

The core contribution—a new approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels—remains promising, but addressing these points would strengthen the paper’s technical rigor and contextualization.
