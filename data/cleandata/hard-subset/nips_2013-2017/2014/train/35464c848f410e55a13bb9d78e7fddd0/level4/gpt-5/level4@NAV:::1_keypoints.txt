- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, leading to a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Technically strong: solid theoretical analysis and persuasive experiments showing a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled data.  
- The resulting classifier departs from the standard representer-theorem solution but remains elegant and computationally straightforward.  
- Question about Equation 3: does the stated solution hold when the associated kernel is not PSD, and a derivation/proof is needed.  
- Experimental concern: unclear how the “optimal” parameter for all methods was selected and what “optimal” precisely means.  
- Clarity is good and organization is strong; main ideas are adequately explained.  
- Several typos and minor errors to fix (examples: Line 82 “will a Euclidean” → “will be a Euclidean”; Line 83 missing “or”; Section 4 “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”; Line 247 “porportional” → “proportional”; Line 283 “That is, or…” → “That is, for…”; Line 352 needs rewriting).  
- Related to a recent NIPS paper but essentially novel, with distinctions clearly articulated.  
- Results are significant and impactful, validated across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Overall: well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and convincing experimental validation.
