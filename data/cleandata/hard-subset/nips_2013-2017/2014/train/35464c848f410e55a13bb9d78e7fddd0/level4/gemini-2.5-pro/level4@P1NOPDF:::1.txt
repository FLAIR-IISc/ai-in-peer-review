This paper introduces a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The authors extend the classical optimization problem for supervised kernel methods to incorporate unlabeled data, which leads to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

### Quality

The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a new, data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates information from unlabeled examples. The resulting classification function, while different from the solution given by the classical Representer Theorem, is elegant and straightforward to compute.

I have a few comments and questions for the authors:

1.  My primary concern relates to Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem. The authors state that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). Does Equation 3 still hold in the non-PSD case? A short derivation or proof of Equation 3, particularly addressing this point, would be a valuable addition and would strengthen the paper's theoretical claims.

2.  My second comment concerns the experimental setup. The authors state that an "optimal" parameter was chosen for all benchmarked methods. The paper would be improved by clarifying how these parameters were determined. What is meant by "optimal" in this context? For example, were they tuned on a held-out validation set for each dataset, or were standard values from the literature used?

### Clarity

The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and well-organized. The main ideas are explained adequately, and the overall narrative is easy to follow.

However, I did notice several minor typos that should be corrected:
*   Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
*   Line 83: A conjunction (e.g., "or") appears to be missing.
*   Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
*   Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional".
*   Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for...".
*   Line 352: This sentence is unclear and should be rephrased for clarity.

### Originality

The proposed approach is novel, although it relates to recent work in the area (e.g., [9]). The authors do a good job of clearly differentiating their contributions from this and other related publications.

### Significance

The results presented are significant. The authors have made a commendable effort to validate their method extensively across several datasets from different application domains.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation and a convincing set of experiments.
