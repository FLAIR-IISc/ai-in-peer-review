- Proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework using discretized Fredholm integral equations to create a data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled data.  
- High technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis and strong experimental validation; resulting classifier is distinct from but efficient compared to classical representer-theorem solutions.  
- Concern about Equation 3: unclear if it holds when the associated kernel is not PSD; authors should better justify the claim that the kernel need not be PSD and provide a derivation/proof of Eq. 3.  
- Experimental methodology needs clarification: explain how "optimal" parameters were chosen for all benchmarked methods and define "optimal."  
- Paper is well-written and organized but contains minor errors to fix (Line 82: "will a" → "will be a"; Line 83: missing "or"; Section 4 title: "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"; Line 247: "porportional" → "proportional"; Line 283: "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."; Line 352: sentence needs rephrasing).  
- Originality: similar in spirit to a recent NIPS work ([9]) but introduces clear, novel distinctions from existing literature.  
- Significance: results are impactful and broadly validated across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Overall: strong theoretical foundation and convincing experiments make this a valuable contribution.
