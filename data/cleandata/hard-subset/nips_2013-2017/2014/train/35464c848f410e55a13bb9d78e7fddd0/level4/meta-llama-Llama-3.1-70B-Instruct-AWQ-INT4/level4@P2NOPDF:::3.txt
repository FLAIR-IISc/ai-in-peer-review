The authors propose a data-dependent kernel called Fredholm kernels, which leverages unlabeled datasets in a natural manner. The novelty of this kernel lies in its ability to utilize unlabeled data effectively. The authors demonstrate several theoretical properties of the kernel and experimentally verify its competitiveness with existing state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand their contributions.

Detailed comments:

1. The authors may want to consider providing a systematic approach to selecting the kernel width for Fredholm kernels. For instance, under certain assumptions, can the kernel width be determined through cross-validation? A clear guideline on kernel width selection would enhance the practical applicability of the proposed method.
2. Including toy experiments to demonstrate scenarios where the proposed method may not perform well would be beneficial. This would provide insight into the settings in which the proposed method is most effective, allowing for a more comprehensive understanding of its strengths and limitations.

Overall, the paper is interesting, clearly written, and effectively communicates the authors' ideas. Based on its quality and contributions, I vote for acceptance.
