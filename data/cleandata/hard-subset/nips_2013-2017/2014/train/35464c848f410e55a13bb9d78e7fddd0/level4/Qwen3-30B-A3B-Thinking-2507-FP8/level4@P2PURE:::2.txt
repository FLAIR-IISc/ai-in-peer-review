

The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels, with many aspects discussed in detail. Overall, I find the paper highly interesting.  
Nevertheless, I have a few suggestions and questions:  
- Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to line 99): The Nyström method also approximates integral equations (e.g., in kernel PCA). The authors should clarify how their approach relates to this, specifying similarities and differences.  
- Section 2, Eq. 2: Approaches based on operator equations exist in support vector machines and kernel methods literature. The authors should cite relevant work and explain whether Eq. 2 is a special case or how it differs.  
- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem): Eq. 2 is non-standard in learning theory. The authors assume a representer theorem holds; they should either prove it or provide a precise reference and clarify how Eq. 2 fits into existing representer theorems.  
- Section 4, Eq. 8: The soft-thresholding PCA interpretation is insightful. How does this differ from methods like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?
