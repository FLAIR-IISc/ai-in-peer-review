The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, making the paper very engaging.

However, I still have a few suggestions and questions that I believe would strengthen the paper:

- In Section 2 (from Equation 1 to the equation on line 99):
The Nystrom method also considers an approximation to an integral equation, specifically kernel PCA. I think it would be beneficial if the authors could mention this and explain in what sense their approach is similar to or differs from the Nystrom method.

- Section 2, Equation 2:
Operator equations have been presented in the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. It would be good to mention this work and explain how Equation 2 relates to it, i.e., whether it is a special case or how it differs.

- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):
While Equation 2 is less common in learning theory, the authors assume the existence of a representer theorem. To strengthen this section, they should either prove a representer theorem or provide a precise reference and explain how Equation 2 is a special case of it.

- Section 4, Equation 8:
The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is nice and interesting. However, it would be helpful to discuss how this approach differs from other methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

Additionally, I have identified a few typos that should be corrected:
- line 165: "Not that" should be "Note that"
- line 221: "achived" should be "achieved"
- line 341: "chossing" should be "choosing"
- line 672: "Table ??" should be replaced with the correct table number.

Overall, the authors propose a new approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using Fredholm kernels, which are data-dependent. With some revisions to address the mentioned points, the paper has the potential to be even more impactful.
