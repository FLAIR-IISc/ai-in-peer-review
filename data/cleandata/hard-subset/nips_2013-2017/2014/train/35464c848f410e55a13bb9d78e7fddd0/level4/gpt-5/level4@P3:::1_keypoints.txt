- Proposes learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and discretizes an operator K_pX to induce a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" on labeled points.  
- Shows the Fredholm kernel is PSD when the RKHS inner kernel kH is PSD, making the method equivalent to a standard kernel method with an unlabeled-data–dependent kernel matrix.  
- Introduces a "noise assumption": locally low-variance directions in the marginal are uninformative and should be suppressed.  
- Theoretically, linear outer kernels yield PCA-like soft-thresholding of principal components under Gaussian marginals.  
- With Gaussian inner/outer kernels, the Fredholm kernel provides a variance-reduced estimator of a target kernel on a latent clean space.  
- Positions the work relative to kernel mean embeddings, Fredholm-based density-ratio estimation, RKHS shrinkage, and data-dependent/manifold kernels.  
- Empirically strong: performs well on synthetic data violating cluster assumptions and on text/sentiment/digit datasets, with robustness to injected noise.  
- Strength: elegant operator-to-kernel reduction that cleanly integrates unlabeled data and is computationally useful.  
- Strength: clear theoretical insights demonstrating noise suppression and variance reduction.  
- Strength: PSD property and representer-based equivalence give solid foundations for the method.  
- Weakness/ask: Clarify the representer theorem application and give a proof sketch, especially when the outer kernel k may be non-PSD and state explicitly that KF is PSD when KH is.  
- Weakness/ask: Specify hyperparameter/validation protocols (splits, search grids for t,r,λ, normalization choices), whether equal validation budgets were used, and how scaling constants/normalization are chosen.  
- Weakness/ask: Discuss computational complexity and scalability as u grows, memory/runtime costs, and applicability of Nyström/random-feature or sparsification approximations.  
- Weakness/ask: Clarify inductive prediction for out-of-sample test points and whether new unlabeled data can be incorporated without retraining.  
- Weakness/ask: Theory relies on Gaussianity and orthogonal-noise assumptions; discuss how conclusions might extend to non-Gaussian or manifold+noise settings.  
- Clarity issues: several typos and wording fixes suggested; request guidance on kernel width selection and when to use normalized vs. unnormalized k̂F.  
- Originality: novel reframing of learning via Fredholm equations to induce unlabeled-data-shaped kernels.  
- Significance: practical and principled approach to leverage unlabeled data, likely useful with clearer scalability and hyperparameter details.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed contribution with a lean-toward-accept recommendation pending clarifications on representer theorem, experiments, and scalability.
