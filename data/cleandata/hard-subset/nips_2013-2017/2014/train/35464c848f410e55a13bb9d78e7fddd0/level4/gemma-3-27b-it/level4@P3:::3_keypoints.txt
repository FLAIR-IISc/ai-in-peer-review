- Introduces Fredholm kernels: reformulates learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation to naturally incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Provides theoretical analysis under a "noise assumption," showing variance reduction benefits.  
- Empirical results are comprehensive and competitive on synthetic and real-world benchmarks, with comparisons to TSVM and LapRLSC.  
- Situates the work well within prior kernel and integral-operator literature (density ratio estimation, kernel mean embeddings, manifold regularization).  
- Novelty: offers a fresh semi-supervised learning perspective by explicitly addressing noise in unlabeled data.  
- Theoretical foundation: solid analysis supporting the proposed method under stated assumptions.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clear, and explains mathematical and algorithmic details effectively.  
- Weakness: no systematic method for selecting kernel width (t or r); only cross-validation is mentioned without sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of negative results, limitations, and potential failure modes.  
- Weakness: the "noise assumption" is intuitively appealing but needs a more rigorous mathematical formulation and relation to existing assumptions (e.g., manifold assumption).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with solid theory, empirical support, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: practical limitation in kernel parameter selection, missing failure-mode analysis, and imprecise noise-assumption characterization.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand parameter-selection discussion and analyze limitations/failure modes.
