- Proposes a Fredholm-kernel framework for semi-supervised learning by extending kernel-based supervised optimization to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral.  
- High technical quality with rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the claims.  
- Convincing experimental results that validate the proposed approach.  
- Introduces a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Classifier deviates from the classical representer theorem solution but remains elegant and computationally efficient.  
- Question: Equation 3’s associated kernel may not be PSD—does Equation 3 still hold in that case; a derivation would strengthen the paper.  
- Question: Experimental “optimal” parameters—how were they chosen and what precisely defines “optimal”?  
- Well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Minor typographical errors to correct (examples: “will a Euclidean” → “will be a Euclidean”; missing “or”; “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”; “porportional” → “proportional”; “That is, or…” → “That is, for…”; revise line 352).  
- Builds on recent NIPS work [9] but is fundamentally novel, with clear distinctions from related work.  
- Results are significant and broadly evaluated across diverse datasets and application domains.  
- Overall: strong theoretical foundation combined with compelling experimental validation.
