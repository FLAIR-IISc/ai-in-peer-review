The authors propose a novel data-dependent kernel, known as Fredholm kernels, which naturally incorporates unlabeled data into the learning process. The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the development of the ideas.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the Fredholm kernel, which is derived from the Fredholm integral equation. The authors show that this kernel has several desirable properties, including the ability to suppress non-informative variations in the data. They also provide theoretical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the Fredholm kernel in certain settings.

The experimental results are impressive, with the Fredholm kernel outperforming existing state-of-the-art methods on several real-world datasets. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the "noise assumption" and how it relates to the cluster and manifold assumptions commonly used in semi-supervised learning.

Some minor suggestions for improvement:

1. The authors could provide more guidance on how to select the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel. While they mention that cross-validation can be used, it would be helpful to have more concrete advice on how to choose the optimal kernel width.
2. It would be interesting to see some toy experiments that demonstrate the limitations of the proposed method. This would help to clarify in which settings the Fredholm kernel is most effective.

Overall, the paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning. I vote for acceptance.
