This paper presents a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, extending the classical supervised learning optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled information and resulting in a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality:
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims backed by thorough theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information. The classifying function, although differing from the classical representer theorem solution, is elegant and easy to compute. 

However, I have a few comments that require clarification. Firstly, Equation 3 describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, but the authors mention that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). It would be beneficial to provide a proof or explanation of how Equation 3 was obtained, as it is unclear whether it still holds in the non-PSD case. 

Additionally, in the experimental section, the authors chose an "optimal" parameter for all benchmarked methods. It would be helpful to elaborate on how these parameters were determined and what is meant by "optimal" in this context.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow and understand the main ideas. However, there are a few typos and minor errors that need attention. Specifically:

* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: An "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be corrected to "Fredholm."
* Line 247: "porportional" should be spelled as "proportional."
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be rephrased as "That is, for..."
* Line 352: The sentence requires rewriting for better clarity.

Originality:
Although the proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), it is novel in essence. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others, demonstrating a good understanding of the existing literature.

Significance:
The results presented in the paper are important and significant. The authors have made a considerable effort to test the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains, which strengthens the validity of their claims.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a good theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.
