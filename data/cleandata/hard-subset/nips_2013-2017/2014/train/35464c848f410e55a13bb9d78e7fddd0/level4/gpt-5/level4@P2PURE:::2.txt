The authors propose an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, and overall I find the paper compelling. Nevertheless, I have a few suggestions and questions:

- Section 2 (from Eq. (1) to the equation on line 99):
In the Nyström method, one also approximates an integral equation (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be helpful to mention this connection and explain in what sense your approach is similar to or different from Nyström approximations.

- Section 2, Eq. (2):
Within support vector machines and kernel methods, operator-equation–based approaches have appeared in the literature. Please cite this prior work and clarify whether Eq. (2) is a special case of those formulations or, if not, how it differs.

- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):
Because Eq. (2) is less common in learning theory, you assume a representer theorem. You should either prove the relevant representer theorem here or provide a precise reference and explain explicitly how Eq. (2) is a special case of it.

- Section 4, Eq. (8):
The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is appealing and interesting. How does it differ from related approaches such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?

- Typos and editorial issues:
  - line 165: “Not that”
  - line 221: “achived”
  - line 341: “chossing”
  - line 672: “Table ??”
  - “New approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using Fredholm kernels, which are data-dependent.” (clarify or rephrase)
