The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail. Overall, I find the paper very interesting.

The paper starts by formulating a new framework for learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. This formulation shares some similarities with the usual kernel learning framework but also allows for easy incorporation of unlabeled data. The authors discuss reasons why incorporation of unlabeled data may be desirable, concentrating in particular on what may be termed “the noise assumption” for semi-supervised learning. They provide both theoretical and empirical results showing that the Fredholm formulation allows for efficient denoising of classifiers.

The main contributions of the paper are threefold. First, the authors formulate a new framework based on solving a regularized Fredholm equation. The framework naturally combines labeled and unlabeled data. Second, they discuss “the noise assumption” in semi-supervised learning and provide some theoretical evidence that Fredholm kernels are able to improve performance of classifiers under this assumption. Third, they show that Fredholm kernels perform well on synthetic examples designed to illustrate the noise assumption as well as on a number of real-world datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical background. The experimental results are also convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, I still have a few suggestions and questions:

- In section 2, the authors should mention the Nystrom method, which also considers an approximation to an integral equation (in that case, kernel PCA). The authors should explain in what sense their approach is similar or different.

- In section 2, the authors should mention the work on operator equations in the area of support vector machines and kernel methods. They should explain in which sense equation 2 is a special case of it (or explain how it differs).

- In section 2, the authors assume that a representer theorem exists for equation 2. They should either prove a representer theorem here or provide a precise reference and explain how equation 2 is a special case of it.

- In section 4, the interpretation of the Fredholm kernel as soft-thresholding PCA is nice and interesting. The authors should discuss how it differs from other methods like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

- There are a few typos in the paper (e.g., "Not that" instead of "Note that", "achived" instead of "achieved", "chossing" instead of "choosing", and "Table ??" which should be replaced with the actual table number).

Overall, I think the paper presents an interesting and novel approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. With some minor revisions to address the above comments, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels
* Clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical background
* Convincing experimental results

Weaknesses:

* Lack of comparison with other methods (e.g., Nystrom method, operator equations in SVMs and kernel methods)
* Assumption of a representer theorem without proof or reference
* Limited discussion of the relationship with other methods (e.g., Fisher kernels, probability product kernels)

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning.
* The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks comparison with other methods and discussion of the relationship with other approaches.
* The assumption of a representer theorem without proof or reference is a weakness.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed method.

Recommendation:

I recommend acceptance of the paper, but with minor revisions to address the above comments. The authors should provide more comparison with other methods, discuss the relationship with other approaches, and either prove a representer theorem or provide a precise reference.
