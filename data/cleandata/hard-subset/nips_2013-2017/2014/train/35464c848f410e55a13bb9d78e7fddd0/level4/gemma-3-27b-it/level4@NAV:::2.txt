The authors introduce a novel method for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, providing a detailed discussion of its various components. I found the paper to be highly interesting overall.

However, I have several suggestions and questions that could further strengthen the work:

- **Section 2 (Equations 1 – line 99):** The Nyström method also relies on approximating an integral equation (e.g., in kernel PCA). A discussion of the similarities and differences between the proposed approach and Nyström methods would be valuable.
- **Section 2, Equation 2:**  Operator equation formulations exist within the fields of support vector machines and kernel methods. It would be beneficial to acknowledge this prior work and clarify how Equation 2 relates to it – either as a special case or by detailing the key distinctions.
- **Section 2, line 128 (Representer Theorem):**  Equation 2 represents a less conventional formulation in learning theory, implicitly relying on the existence of a representer theorem. The authors should either provide a proof of such a theorem within the context of this work, or offer a precise reference and explain how Equation 2 fits within the existing theoretical framework.
- **Section 4, Equation 8:** The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful.  However, it would be helpful to elaborate on how this approach differs from related techniques such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.
