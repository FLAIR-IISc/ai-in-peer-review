The authors present a novel method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels, which is a data-dependent approach. The paper provides an in-depth discussion of various aspects of the method, making it a very interesting read.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions to further improve the paper:

In Section 2, the authors could benefit from comparing their approach to the Nystrom method, which also approximates an integral equation, specifically kernel PCA. A discussion on the similarities and differences between these two approaches would be beneficial.

Additionally, the authors should acknowledge the existing literature on operator equations in the context of support vector machines and kernel methods. Specifically, they should explain how Equation 2 relates to these previous works, either as a special case or as a distinct approach.

The authors assume the existence of a representer theorem for Equation 2, which is less common in learning theory. To strengthen their argument, they should either provide a proof of the representer theorem or offer a precise reference and explain how their equation is a special case of it.

The interpretation of Equation 8 as soft-thresholding PCA is a valuable contribution. However, the authors should discuss how this approach differs from other methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

Finally, there are a few typos that need to be corrected: "Not that" (line 165), "achived" (line 221), "chossing" (line 341), and "Table ??" (line 672).

Overall, the paper presents a new and interesting approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using Fredholm kernels. With some revisions to address the aforementioned points, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
