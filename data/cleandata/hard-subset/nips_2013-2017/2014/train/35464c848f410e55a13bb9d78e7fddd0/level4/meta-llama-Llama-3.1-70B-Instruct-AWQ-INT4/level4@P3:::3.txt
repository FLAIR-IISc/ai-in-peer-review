The authors propose a novel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. This approach naturally fits into the kernel framework and can be interpreted as constructing new data-dependent kernels, which they call Fredholm kernels. The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and understand the proposed method.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of Fredholm kernels, which can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning. The authors provide both theoretical and experimental evidence to support the effectiveness of their approach. The theoretical results show that Fredholm kernels can suppress principal components with small variance and provide a better approximation to the "true" kernel on the hidden underlying space. The experimental results demonstrate that Fredholm kernels can outperform existing state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.

Strengths of the paper:

* The authors propose a novel and interesting approach to semi-supervised learning, which can effectively utilize unlabeled data.
* The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and understand the proposed method.
* The authors provide both theoretical and experimental evidence to support the effectiveness of their approach.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The authors do not provide a systematic approach to select the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel. It would be nice to have some guidance on how to choose the kernel width, for example, under some assumption, can you select a kernel width by cross-validation?
* The authors do not provide any toy experiments to show the cases where the proposed method might not work. It would be nice to have some insight into the limitations of the proposed method and in which setting it is most effective.

Overall, the paper is interesting and well-written, and I vote for acceptance. However, I would like to see the authors address the above-mentioned weaknesses in their final version.
