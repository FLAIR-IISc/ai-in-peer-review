Summary
The paper proposes a novel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, with a detailed treatment of the method and insightful connections to spectral interpretations (e.g., soft-thresholding PCA). The approach is data-dependent and potentially unifies several ideas across kernel methods and operator-based formulations. I found the paper interesting and well-motivated.

Strengths
- Presents a new learning framework using Fredholm kernels with both supervised and semi-supervised applications.
- Provides substantial technical detail and a compelling interpretation of Section 4 (Eq. 8) as soft-thresholding PCA.
- The data-dependent nature of the kernels is appealing and may offer practical benefits.

Questions and suggestions
- Section 2 (Eq. 1 to line 99): Please discuss the relation to Nyström methods. In Nyström approximations—e.g., as used in kernel PCA—one also approximates solutions to integral equations via data-derived quadrature. It would help to explain the precise similarities and differences: Are you approximating the same operator? Is your Fredholm construction equivalent to a particular Nyström scheme under certain sampling or weighting assumptions? If not, where does it differ (e.g., in the induced RKHS, regularization, or bias-variance trade-off)?

- Section 2, Eq. 2: There is prior work in SVMs and kernel methods that formulates learning via operator equations (e.g., integral operator perspectives on regularization, spectral filtering, and inverse problems). Please cite representative works and clarify how Eq. 2 relates to or specializes these formulations. Is Eq. 2 a special case of a known operator-regularized objective, or does it introduce a distinct operator or penalty? A concise mapping would aid readers in situating your contribution.

- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem): Since Eq. 2 is less common in standard learning-theoretic treatments, the assumption of a representer theorem needs justification. Please either:
  - Prove a representer theorem under your assumptions (specifying the exact functional, admissible hypothesis space, and regularizer), or
  - Provide a precise citation to an applicable representer theorem and explain how Eq. 2 fits its conditions (e.g., that the objective depends on f only through pointwise evaluations and a nondecreasing function of the Hilbert norm or an operator-induced norm). A short sketch showing that your operator-regularized setup satisfies these conditions would be very helpful.

- Section 4, Eq. 8 (soft-thresholding PCA): The soft-thresholding PCA interpretation is elegant. To help position the approach, please compare it with:
  - Fisher kernels: Both build models from generative structures; how does your operator/kernel construction differ in terms of feature extraction, parameterization, and computational pipeline? Are there conditions under which your method reduces to or subsumes Fisher kernels?
  - Probability product kernels: These define kernels via integrals over product powers of probability densities. How does the Fredholm construction compare in expressivity and robustness to distributional mismatch? Can your framework recover PPKs as special cases with particular operators or weights, or is it fundamentally different?

Minor presentation issues and typos
- Line 165: “Not that” → “Note that”
- Line 221: “achived” → “achieved”
- Line 341: “chossing” → “choosing”
- Line 672: “Table ??” → Please fix the missing table reference
- Consider briefly reiterating in the introduction that the proposed Fredholm kernels are data-dependent and clarify the implications for generalization and stability (you already hint at this, but a concise statement upfront would improve readability).

Overall assessment
This is a promising and thoughtfully developed approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning with interesting spectral insights and a clear connection to integral operator methods. Addressing the points above—especially the relationship to Nyström approximations and operator-based learning, and providing a representer theorem proof or precise citation—would significantly strengthen the paper’s theoretical grounding and help readers understand where it sits within the broader kernel methods literature. The clarifications around Eq. 8 in relation to Fisher and probability product kernels would further enhance the paper’s positioning.
