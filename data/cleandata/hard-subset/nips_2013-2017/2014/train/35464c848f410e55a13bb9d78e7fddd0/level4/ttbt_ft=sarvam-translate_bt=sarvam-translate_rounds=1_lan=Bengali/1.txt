This paper describes a framework specifically adapted for semi-supervised learning based on the Fredholm kernel. The classic supervised learning optimization problem, solved using kernel-based methods, is extended to incorporate unlabeled data, leading to a discrete version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper has a high technical standard, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and reliable experimental results. The proposed formula leads to a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled data. The classification function differs from the classical representation theorem solution, but it is still refined and easy to compute.

I have a few comments: one is related to equation 3, where the proposed optimization problem solution is described. Since the authors mention that the associated kernel does not need to be PSD, is equation 3 still applicable in this case? It would be nice to see a proof of how equation 3 was derived. Another comment is about the experimental section, where the authors have chosen an "optimal" parameter for all the methods in the benchmark. How were those parameters determined? What is meant by "optimal"?

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and organized in most parts. It is easy to follow, and the main concepts are adequately explained. Although there are some typos, for example:
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." -> ... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" -> Fredholm
* Line 247: "porportional" -> proportional
* Line 283: "That is, or..." -> That is, for...
* Line 352: The sentence needs to be rewritten.

Originality
The proposed method is related to a recently published NIPS publication ([9]), but it is fundamentally new. It is clear how this work differs from this publication and other publications.

Importance
The results are important and significant. A significant effort was made to test the proposed method on several datasets in various application domains.

A well-written paper with good theoretical foundation and reliable experimental section.
