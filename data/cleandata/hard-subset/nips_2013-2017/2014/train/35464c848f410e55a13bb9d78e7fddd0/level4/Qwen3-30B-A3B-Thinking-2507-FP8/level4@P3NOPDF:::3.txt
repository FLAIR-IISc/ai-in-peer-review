

This paper introduces Fredholm kernels, a novel data-dependent kernel construction that effectively leverages unlabeled datasets in a principled manner. The authors establish several theoretical properties of the kernel and demonstrate its competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods through comprehensive experiments. The paper is exceptionally well-written, logically structured, and clearly articulates both the technical contributions and empirical findings. The core novelty—integrating unlabeled data without ad-hoc modifications—represents a meaningful advancement in kernel learning, particularly for scenarios where labeled data is scarce.

**Major Strengths:**  
The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-motivated, providing clear insights into the kernel’s behavior. The experimental section convincingly validates the approach across multiple benchmarks, with results that consistently match or exceed existing methods. The writing is precise, with minimal jargon and excellent flow from problem statement to evaluation.

**Constructive Comments:**  
1. **Bandwidth Selection:** The paper does not elaborate on how to systematically choose the kernel bandwidth parameter. Given its critical role in performance, it would strengthen the work to include a discussion (or empirical analysis) of bandwidth selection strategies. For instance, under what assumptions could cross-validation or theoretical bounds guide this choice? A brief exploration of this in the experiments or supplementary material would greatly enhance reproducibility and practical utility.  

2. **Limitations and Scope:** While the experiments showcase the kernel’s strengths, adding a small set of synthetic toy examples could help clarify the method’s effective scope. Specifically, illustrating a scenario where the Fredholm kernel underperforms relative to alternatives (e.g., in highly noisy or structurally complex data) would provide valuable context for practitioners. This would not undermine the contribution but instead help readers identify the most suitable application domains.

**Overall Assessment:**  
The paper makes a compelling contribution to kernel methods with clear theoretical and empirical merit. The writing is exemplary, and the proposed approach is both novel and practically relevant. The minor suggestions above are intended to further solidify the work’s impact, but they do not detract from the paper’s overall quality. The authors have successfully addressed a meaningful gap in unsupervised kernel design, and the results are robust and well-supported.  

**Recommendation:** Strongly recommend acceptance.
