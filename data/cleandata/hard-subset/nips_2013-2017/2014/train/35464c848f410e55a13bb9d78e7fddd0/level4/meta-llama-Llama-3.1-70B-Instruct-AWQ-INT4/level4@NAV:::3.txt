The authors introduce the Fredholm kernel, a data-dependent kernel that innovatively leverages unlabeled datasets. The kernel's uniqueness lies in its natural integration of unlabeled data. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the kernel's theoretical properties and demonstrate its competitiveness with state-of-the-art methods through experimental evaluations.

The paper is well-structured and effectively communicated.

Detailed comments:

1. Is there a systematic method for determining the optimal kernel width for the Fredholm kernel? For instance, under specific assumptions, can cross-validation be employed to select the kernel width?
2. Incorporating simple experiments to illustrate scenarios where the proposed method may not be effective would be beneficial. This would provide insight into the settings in which the proposed method is most efficacious.

Overall, the paper is engaging and exceptionally well-written, warranting consideration for acceptance.
