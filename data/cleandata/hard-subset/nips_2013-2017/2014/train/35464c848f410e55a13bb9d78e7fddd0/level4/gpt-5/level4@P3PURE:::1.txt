This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. It extends the classical supervised kernel optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled data, yielding a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is of high technical quality, with claims well supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation induces a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The resulting classifier differs from the classical representer-theorem solution, yet remains elegant and easy to compute.

I have a few comments:
- Equation 3: The authors state that the associated kernel need not be PSD. Does Equation 3 still hold in that case? A derivation or proof explaining how Equation 3 is obtained would be helpful.
- Experimental protocol: The authors chose an “optimal” parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were these parameters determined? What precisely is meant by “optimal” in this context?

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. It is easy to follow, and the main ideas are adequately explained. There are a few typos and minor issues:
- Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” -> “… will be a Euclidean …”
- Line 83: missing “or.”
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” -> “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” -> “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or …” -> “That is, for …”
- Line 352: Sentence needs to be rewritten.

Originality
The approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is essentially novel. The differences from that work and others are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are important and significant. There was a substantial effort to evaluate the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section.
