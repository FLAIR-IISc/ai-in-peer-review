The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning utilizing Fredholm kernels. The paper discusses many aspects of the method in considerable detail, and overall I find it very compelling. 

Nevertheless, I have a few suggestions and questions that would strengthen the work:

- **Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to Eq. on line 99):** The Nyström method also considers an approximation to an integral equation (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be beneficial to discuss this connection, and clearly articulate how the proposed approach is similar to or differs from Nyström-based techniques.

- **Section 2, Eq. 2:** Approaches employing operator equations have appeared in the literature within the contexts of support vector machines and kernel methods. A discussion of related work and an explanation of how Eq. 2 relates to these existing formulations – whether as a special case or through key differences – would be valuable.

- **Section 2, line 128 (Representer Theorem):** Eq. 2 represents a less common formulation in learning theory. The authors implicitly assume the existence of a representer theorem. I recommend either proving a representer theorem within the paper, or providing a precise reference and explaining how Eq. 2 fits within the established framework of existing representer theorems.

- **Section 4, Eq. 8:** The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful and interesting. However, it would be helpful to delineate how this approach differs from other related techniques such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

    - Line 221: "achived" should be "achieved".
    - Line 341: "chossing" should be "choosing".
    - Line 672: "Table ??" should be clarified with the correct table number.
