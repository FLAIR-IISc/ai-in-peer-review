- Proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels that incorporates unlabeled data into a data-dependent kernel.  
- Extends kernel-based supervised optimization to a discretized Fredholm integral formulation yielding a computationally tractable classification function.  
- Resulting classifier differs from traditional representer-theorem solutions but retains an elegant, practical form.  
- High technical quality with rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling experimental results.  
- Main theoretical concern: Equation 3’s associated kernel may not be PSD — need clarification whether Eq.3 remains valid in that case and a brief proof sketch/derivation.  
- Experimental concern: unclear how “optimal” parameter settings were chosen — request details on metric and parameter search procedure (e.g., grid search, cross-validation).  
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and logically organized with consistent notation.  
- Minor typos and wording issues to fix (examples: Line 82 “will a Euclidean” → “will be a Euclidean”; Line 83 missing conjunction; Section 4 “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”; Line 247 “porportional” → “proportional”; Line 283 “That is, or…” → “That is, for…”; Line 352 awkward sentence).  
- Builds on prior work (including NIPS 2013 [9]) but clearly distinguishes novel contributions and direction.  
- Results are significant and broadly validated across diverse datasets, demonstrating practical potential.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance pending clarification of Eq.3 and parameter selection details.
