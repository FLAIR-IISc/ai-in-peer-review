This paper presents a new and interesting method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels. The authors provide a detailed discussion of many aspects of the method, and overall, I find this to be a compelling contribution.

Nevertheless, I have a few suggestions and questions that could help strengthen the manuscript:

*   **Section 2 (Relationship to the Nystrom Method):** In the development from Eq. (1) to line 99, the authors propose an approximation to an integral equation. The Nystrom method, particularly in the context of kernel PCA, also relies on approximating a similar integral equation. It would strengthen the paper if the authors could discuss this connection, clarifying the similarities and differences between their approach and the Nystrom method.

*   **Section 2, Eq. (2) (Context within Kernel Methods):** The use of operator equations is well-established in the literature on Support Vector Machines and other kernel methods. The authors should situate their work within this context by citing relevant literature and explaining whether Eq. (2) can be seen as a special case of these more general operator equations, or how it fundamentally differs.

*   **Section 2, line 128 (Representer Theorem):** The formulation in Eq. (2) is not standard in learning theory, and the authors appear to assume that a representer theorem holds for it. This assumption requires justification. The authors should either provide a proof for the representer theorem in this specific context or cite a more general theorem and explicitly demonstrate how the formulation in Eq. (2) satisfies its conditions.

*   **Section 4, Eq. (8) (Comparison to Other Kernels):** The interpretation of the method as a form of soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. To better position this contribution, could the authors elaborate on how their approach differs from related methods such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?
