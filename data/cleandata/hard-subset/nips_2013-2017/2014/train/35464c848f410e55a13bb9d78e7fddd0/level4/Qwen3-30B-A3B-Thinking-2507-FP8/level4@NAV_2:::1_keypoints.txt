- Introduces a novel framework reformulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and defines data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Builds on kernel methods and Fredholm-based density ratio estimation and shares conceptual links with kernel mean embedding approaches.  
- Contrasts with manifold regularization and cluster-assumption methods by proposing a new "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning.  
- High technical quality: solid theoretical claims, an elegant and computationally feasible classifier formulation, and compelling experimental results.  
- Section 4 theoretical analysis explains how Fredholm kernels suppress noise and connects their behavior to principal component analysis.  
- Minor theoretical concern: Equation 3 appears to assume the kernel is PSD despite the claim it need not be—needs a brief derivation/clarification.  
- Experimental reporting concern: statement that "optimal" parameters were chosen lacks detail on how parameters were selected (e.g., cross-validation procedure).  
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized, with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Several minor typographical errors (e.g., Line 82 "will a Euclidean" → "will be a Euclidean"; Line 83 missing "or"; "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"; Line 247 "porportional" → "proportional"; Line 283 "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."; Line 352 sentence needs restructuring).  
- Original and meaningful contribution distinct from related work (e.g., NIPS kernel mean shrinkage), offering a new perspective on semi-supervised learning.  
- Significant empirical impact: strong performance on synthetic tests and diverse real-world datasets (text, sentiment, images), showing advantages when unlabeled data meets the noise assumption.
