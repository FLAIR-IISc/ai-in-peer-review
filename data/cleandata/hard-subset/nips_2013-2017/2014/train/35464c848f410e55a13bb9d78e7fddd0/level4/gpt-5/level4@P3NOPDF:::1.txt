Summary
The paper presents a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels. By extending the classical supervised kernel optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled data, the authors arrive at a discretized Fredholm integral equation and a resulting data-dependent kernel. The approach is theoretically grounded and computationally practical, and the experimental results are compelling across multiple datasets.

Quality
The technical quality is high. The formulation yields a novel data-dependent kernel that effectively leverages unlabeled information. The resulting classifier departs from the standard representer theorem solution yet remains elegant and efficient to compute. The theoretical analysis is solid, and the empirical evaluation is convincing.

I have a few points that would benefit from clarification:
- Equation (3) and PSD assumptions: The paper notes that the associated kernel need not be positive semidefinite. Does Equation (3) still hold in this case? Please specify the conditions under which the solution remains valid with an indefinite kernel, and clarify whether the optimization objective remains convex. If convexity is lost, explain how the solution is computed (e.g., through regularization, spectral correction, or solving in a Krein space) and whether uniqueness is guaranteed. A concise derivation or a pointer to an appendix proving Equation (3) would be very helpful.
- Parameter selection: The experimental section mentions using an “optimal” parameter for all methods. How were these parameters determined? Please describe the model selection protocol in detail: the search space, validation procedure (e.g., a held-out set, cross-validation), and whether any test labels or transductive information were used in tuning. Clarify whether unlabeled data were used in parameter selection for both the proposed method and baselines to ensure fairness. If feasible, add a brief sensitivity analysis or report performance as a function of key hyperparameters.
- Computational complexity: It would be useful to discuss the computational and memory cost relative to standard kernel methods, especially the dependence on the number of unlabeled samples. Is the data-dependent kernel constructed once and reused, and what is the asymptotic cost? Including runtime or scalability comparisons would strengthen the empirical section.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written and well organized. The main ideas are clearly explained and the method is easy to follow. A few minor issues to fix:
- Line 82: “... will a Euclidean ...” → “... will be a Euclidean ...”
- Line 83: missing “or.”
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” → “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or ...” → “That is, for ...”
- Line 352: The sentence is unclear and should be rewritten for readability.

Additional clarity suggestions:
- Clearly state all notation and assumptions when introducing the discretized Fredholm integral equation, including the role and weighting of unlabeled samples.
- If the kernel can be indefinite, briefly discuss the implications for theory (e.g., generalization bounds) and practice (e.g., whether you apply any spectral clipping or flip heuristics).
- Consider adding a short algorithmic summary to make the pipeline from data to classifier entirely transparent.

Originality
The approach is closely related to but distinct from prior work (including [9]). The paper clearly articulates how it differs in formulation and in the way unlabeled information is integrated via the Fredholm kernel perspective. The contribution is, in my view, novel and nontrivial.

Significance
The method is potentially impactful: it offers a principled, flexible way to integrate unlabeled data into kernel methods. The experiments span diverse datasets and domains and generally support the claimed benefits. With the added clarifications on parameter selection and computational profile, the empirical evidence would be even more compelling and more easily reproducible.

Overall Assessment
This is a well-written paper with strong theoretical grounding and convincing experiments. Addressing the questions about Equation (3) under indefinite kernels, providing a clear model selection protocol (and ideally sensitivity analyses), and briefly discussing computational complexity would further strengthen the submission. I recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
