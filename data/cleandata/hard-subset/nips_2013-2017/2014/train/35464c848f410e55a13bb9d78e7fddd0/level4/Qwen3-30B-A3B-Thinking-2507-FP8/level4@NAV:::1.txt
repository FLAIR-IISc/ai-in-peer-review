

This paper presents a framework particularly valuable for semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. The classical supervised learning optimization problem in kernel-based methods is extended to integrate unlabeled information, yielding a discretized form of the Fredholm integral equation.  

**Quality**  
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims through rigorous theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation introduces a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled data. While the classifying function diverges from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains elegant and computationally efficient.  
I have two minor comments: First, regarding Equation 3, the authors note the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). Does Equation 3 remain valid in this case? A derivation of Equation 3 would strengthen the presentation. Second, in the experimental section, the authors select an "optimal" parameter for all benchmarked methods. How were these parameters determined, and what precisely constitutes "optimal"?  

**Clarity**  
The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and easy to follow, with adequate explanation of core concepts. Minor typographical errors require correction:  
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." → "... will be a Euclidean ..."  
* Line 83: Missing "or".  
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" → "Fredholm".  
* Line 247: "porportional" → "proportional".  
* Line 283: "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."  
* Line 352: Sentence requires revision.  

**Originality**  
The approach builds upon a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is fundamentally novel. The distinctions from [9] and other related work are clearly articulated.  

**Significance**  
The results are important and significant, with substantial effort expended to evaluate the method across diverse datasets and application domains.  

This well-written paper offers a strong theoretical foundation and compelling experimental validation.
