The authors propose a data-dependent kernel, referred to as the Fredholm kernel, which leverages an unlabeled dataset in a natural way. The novelty of this kernel lies in its ability to incorporate unlabeled data, setting it apart from existing methods. The authors demonstrate several theoretical properties of the kernel and experimentally verify that it compares favorably to state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology. The clarity of the writing and the logical flow of the paper contribute to its overall quality.

Detailed comments:

1. A systematic approach to selecting the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel would be beneficial. For instance, under certain assumptions, it would be useful to explore whether cross-validation can be employed to determine an optimal kernel width. Providing guidance on this aspect would enhance the practical applicability of the proposed kernel.

2. Including toy experiments to illustrate scenarios where the proposed method may not perform well would be valuable. These experiments would help to elucidate the settings in which the proposed method is most effective, thereby providing a more comprehensive understanding of its strengths and limitations.

Overall, the paper is interesting, and its clarity makes it a compelling read. Based on its merits, I vote for acceptance.
