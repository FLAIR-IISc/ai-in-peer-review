

The authors introduce a novel data-dependent kernel, termed Fredholm kernels, which leverages unlabeled data in a principled manner to enhance kernel-based learning. The core innovation lies in its natural integration of unlabeled data without requiring additional assumptions, distinguishing it from prior approaches. The paper establishes key theoretical properties of the kernel and demonstrates competitive empirical performance against state-of-the-art methods across multiple benchmarks. The contribution is significant, well-motivated, and presented with clarity and precision.  

The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured, making it accessible to a broad audience. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experimental results are thorough and convincing, providing strong evidence for the kernel's effectiveness.  

**Detailed Comments:**  
1. **Kernel Width Selection:** The manuscript does not specify a systematic procedure for selecting the kernel width. Given its critical role in performance, it would be valuable to clarify whether the width can be determined via cross-validation under standard assumptions (e.g., minimizing empirical risk on a validation set). A brief discussion of this practical implementation detail would strengthen the work.  
2. **Limitations and Scope:** To better contextualize the method’s strengths, it would be beneficial to include a small-scale toy experiment illustrating scenarios where Fredholm kernels may underperform relative to baseline approaches. This would help readers identify the specific conditions under which the method is most effective, as well as its potential limitations.  

Overall, the paper makes a compelling contribution to kernel learning with clear theoretical and empirical foundations. The proposed Fredholm kernel offers a practical and principled way to harness unlabeled data, and the results validate its utility across diverse tasks. The writing is exemplary, and the technical content is both novel and impactful. I strongly recommend acceptance.
