

Quality  
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims through rigorous theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed framework extends classical supervised learning optimization in kernel-based methods to incorporate unlabeled data, yielding a novel data-dependent kernel that integrates unlabeled information. While the resulting classifying function differs from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains elegant and computationally efficient.  

I have a few comments: First, regarding Equation 3, the authors note that the associated kernel need not be positive semidefinite (PSD). Does Equation 3 retain validity in this case? A derivation or proof of Equation 3 would strengthen the presentation. Second, in the experimental section, the authors report using an "optimal" parameter for all benchmark methods. How were these parameters determined, and what criteria define "optimal"?

The paper is clearly written and well-organized overall, with the main ideas adequately explained. Minor typographical errors require correction:

* Line 83: Missing "or"  
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"  
* Line 247: "porportional" → "proportional"  
* Line 283: "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."  
* Line 352: Sentence requires rewriting.  

Originality  
The proposed approach builds upon a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but represents a novel conceptual contribution. The distinctions from [9] and other related work are clearly articulated.  

Significance  
The results are important and significant. The authors demonstrate substantial effort in evaluating the method across multiple datasets spanning diverse application domains.  

Well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and compelling experimental validation.
