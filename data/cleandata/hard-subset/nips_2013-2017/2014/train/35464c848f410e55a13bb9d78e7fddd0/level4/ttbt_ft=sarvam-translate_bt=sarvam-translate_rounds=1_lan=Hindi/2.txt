The authors, Fredholm and Kernell, present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using kernels. Several aspects of the method are discussed in detail. Overall, I found this paper very interesting.

However, I have some suggestions and questions:

- Section 2 (equation 1 to the equation at line 99):
Furthermore, the NISTROM method considers the approximation of an integral equation (in that case, kernel PCA). I think it would be good if the authors could mention this and also explain how this approach is similar or different.
- Section 2 equation 2:
Approaches with operator equations have also been presented in the literature in the field of support vector machines and kernel methods. It would be good to mention this work and explain how equation 2 is a special case of it (or how it differs).
- Section 2 line 128 Representative Theorem:
Equation 2 is less common in learning theory. The authors believe that a representative theorem exists. They should prove a representative theorem here or otherwise provide an accurate reference and explain how equation 2 is a special case of it.
- Section 4 equation 8:
The explanation as a soft-thresholding PCA is good and interesting.
How does this differ from other methods such as the Fisher kernel and the probability product kernel?
