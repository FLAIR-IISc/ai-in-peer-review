### **Summary**

This paper introduces a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The authors extend the classical optimization problem for supervised kernel methods to incorporate information from unlabeled data, which leads to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

### **Quality**

The paper is of high technical quality, with claims that are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that effectively leverages unlabeled data. The resulting classifying function, while different from the solution given by the classical representer theorem, remains elegant and computationally efficient.

I have a few comments and questions for the authors:

1.  **Regarding the solution in Equation 3:** The authors state that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). It is unclear if Equation 3, which presents the solution to the optimization problem, still holds in this non-PSD case. The paper would be strengthened by the inclusion of a proof or derivation showing how Equation 3 is obtained, particularly for the non-PSD scenario.

2.  **Regarding the experimental setup:** In the experimental section, the authors mention that an "optimal" parameter was chosen for all methods in the benchmark. It would be beneficial to clarify this process. Specifically, how were these parameters determined? For example, was a validation set used for tuning? Furthermore, what criterion was used to define "optimality"?

### **Clarity**

For the most part, the paper is clearly written, well-organized, and easy to follow. The main ideas are explained adequately. However, I noticed several typos and grammatical issues that should be corrected in a final version:

*   **Line 82:** "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
*   **Line 83:** A connecting word, likely "or", seems to be missing.
*   **Section 4 Title:** "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
*   **Line 247:** "porportional" should be "proportional".
*   **Line 283:** "That is, or..." should be "That is, for...".
*   **Line 352:** This sentence is unclear and should be rewritten for better readability.

### **Originality**

The proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), but the work is fundamentally novel. The authors do an excellent job of positioning their contribution and clearly articulating how it differs from and advances upon prior art.

### **Significance**

The results presented are important and significant. The authors have undertaken a considerable effort to test the proposed method on diverse datasets spanning multiple application domains, demonstrating its broad applicability. The work represents a valuable contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning.

### **Overall Recommendation**

This is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section. I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the minor points raised above.
