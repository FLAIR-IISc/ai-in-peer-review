This paper introduces a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The classical optimization problem for supervised kernel methods is extended to incorporate unlabeled information, leading to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with claims that are well-supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates information from unlabeled data. Although the resulting classifying function differs from the solution given by the classical representer theorem, it remains elegant and straightforward to compute.

I have a few comments:
1.  Regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the optimization problem: The authors state that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). Does the solution presented in Equation 3 still hold in the non-PSD case? The inclusion of a proof or derivation for Equation 3 would be beneficial.
2.  In the experimental section, the authors mention choosing an "optimal" parameter for all methods in the benchmark. Could the authors please clarify how these parameters were determined and provide a precise definition of "optimal" in this context?

**Clarity**
For the most part, the paper is clearly written, well-organized, and easy to follow, with the main ideas explained adequately. However, the manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to correct several typos and minor grammatical issues. For example:
*   Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
*   Line 83: A word (perhaps "or") appears to be missing.
*   Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
*   Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional".
*   Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for...".
*   Line 352: This sentence is unclear and needs to be rewritten.

**Originality**
While the proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication [9], it is fundamentally novel. The authors clearly distinguish their work from this and other related publications.

**Significance**
The results are important and significant. The authors have demonstrated a considerable effort to test the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains, highlighting its practical value.

In summary, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.
