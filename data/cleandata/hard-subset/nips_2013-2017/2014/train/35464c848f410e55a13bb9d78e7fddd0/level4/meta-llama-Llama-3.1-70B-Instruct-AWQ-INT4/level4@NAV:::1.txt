This paper presents a novel framework for semi-supervised learning, leveraging Fredholm kernels to incorporate unlabeled data into the classical supervised learning optimization problem. The proposed approach discretizes the Fredholm integral equation, yielding a new data-dependent kernel that effectively utilizes unlabeled information.

Quality
The paper exhibits high technical quality, with claims substantiated by rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling experimental results. The formulation of the proposed method leads to a unique data-dependent kernel, and the resulting classifying function, although distinct from the classical representer theorem solution, remains elegant and computationally efficient.

I have a few comments for the authors to consider: Firstly, in Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, it is unclear whether the equation holds when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD). The authors' assertion that the kernel need not be PSD warrants further explanation. Additionally, a proof detailing the derivation of Equation 3 would be beneficial. Secondly, in the experimental section, the authors selected an "optimal" parameter for all benchmarked methods. It would be helpful to elaborate on the process used to determine these parameters and clarify the definition of "optimal" in this context.

Clarity
The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the main ideas and understand the key concepts. However, there are a few minor errors that require attention:
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be corrected to "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: An "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be corrected to "Fredholm".
* Line 247: "porportional" should be corrected to "proportional".
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be corrected to "That is, for...".
* Line 352: The sentence requires rephrasing.

Originality
Although the proposed approach bears some resemblance to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), it introduces novel elements that distinguish it from existing work. The authors have clearly articulated the differences between their approach and other related publications.

Significance
The results presented in the paper are significant and impactful. The authors have made a considerable effort to evaluate the proposed method on multiple datasets across various application domains, demonstrating its efficacy and versatility.

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper with a strong theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
