- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels and a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Strong technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis backed by convincing experiments.  
- Introduces a novel, data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Resulting classifier departs from the classical representer theorem solution but remains computationally tractable and elegant.  
- Clarification requested on Equation 3: need derivation/justification, especially when the associated kernel is not PSD.  
- Clarify experimental protocol: precisely how "optimal" parameters were selected (cross-validation, grid search, etc.) and the definition of "optimal."  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and demonstrates significant, impactful results across diverse datasets.  
- Builds on related work (including a recent NIPS paper) but presents a clearly novel contribution and distinctions.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, but contains minor typos and grammatical errors to fix (listed in review).  
- Overall assessment: well-written, strong theoretical foundation, compelling experimental validation, and a valuable contribution to semi-supervised learning.
