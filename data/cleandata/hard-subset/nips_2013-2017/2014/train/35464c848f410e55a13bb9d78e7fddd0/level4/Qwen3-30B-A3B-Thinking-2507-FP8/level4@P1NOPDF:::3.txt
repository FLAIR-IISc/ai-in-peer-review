

The authors propose a data-dependent kernel termed the Fredholm kernel, which integrates unlabeled data in a principled manner. The paper establishes several theoretical properties of the kernel and demonstrates competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods through empirical evaluation. The manuscript is well-written and clearly organized.  

Detailed comments:  
1. The paper does not specify a systematic approach for selecting the kernel width. Could the authors clarify whether cross-validation under standard assumptions (e.g., smoothness constraints) could be used to determine this parameter?  
2. Including a few toy examples illustrating scenarios where the proposed method underperforms would be valuable. This would help characterize the settings in which the Fredholm kernel is most effective.  

The paper presents a compelling idea with strong theoretical and empirical support. It is clearly articulated and makes a meaningful contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
