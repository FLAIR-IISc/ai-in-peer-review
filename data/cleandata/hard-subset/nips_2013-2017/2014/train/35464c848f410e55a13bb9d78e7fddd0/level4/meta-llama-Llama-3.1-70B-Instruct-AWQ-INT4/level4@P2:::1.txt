This paper proposes a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, which is a significant extension of the classical supervised learning optimization problem in kernel-based methods. The authors incorporate unlabeled information into the optimization problem, leading to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality: 8/10
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims through theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information. However, I have a few minor comments. Firstly, it would be beneficial to clarify whether Equation 3 still holds when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD). Additionally, a proof of how Equation 3 was obtained would be a valuable addition. Furthermore, in the experimental section, it is unclear how the "optimal" parameters for all methods were determined. Providing more details on the parameter selection process would strengthen the experimental results.

Clarity: 9/10
The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the main ideas. However, there are a few typos and minor errors that need to be corrected. For instance, "will a Euclidean" should be "will be a Euclidean" (Line 82), and "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm" (Section 4 title).

Originality: 8/10
The proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), but it is novel in essence. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others.

Significance: 9/10
The results are important and significant, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on several datasets in different application domains. The authors have made a considerable effort to test their method thoroughly, which strengthens the paper's contributions.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a good theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section. With some minor revisions to address the comments mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of semi-supervised learning.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.
* The proposed method is effective in incorporating unlabeled information into the optimization problem.

Arguments against acceptance:

* Minor typos and errors need to be corrected.
* Clarification is needed on the applicability of Equation 3 when the associated kernel is not PSD.
* More details on the parameter selection process in the experimental section would be beneficial.
