- Introduces a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, extending supervised kernel optimization to include unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral equation.
- High technical quality with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.
- Proposes a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information and yields an elegant, computable classifier different from the classical representer theorem solution.
- Question: clarify whether Equation 3 remains valid when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite and include a proof/derivation of Equation 3.
- Question: clarify how the "optimal" parameters in experiments were chosen and provide a precise definition of "optimal" for benchmark methods.
- Manuscript is mostly clear and well-organized but requires careful proofreading to fix several typos and unclear sentences.
- Specific typographical/clarity issues: lines 82, 83, Section 4 title ("Fredhom"), lines 247, 283, and an unclear sentence at line 352 need correction.
- Approach is fundamentally novel despite relation to a recent NIPS paper, and the authors distinguish their work from related publications.
- Results are significant and practically valuable, demonstrated across multiple datasets and application domains.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with strong theory and convincing experiments.
