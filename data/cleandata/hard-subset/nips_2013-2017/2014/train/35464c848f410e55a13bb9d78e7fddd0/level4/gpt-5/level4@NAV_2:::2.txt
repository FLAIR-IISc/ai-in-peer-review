The authors introduce a compelling new framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The method is presented in substantial detail. Overall, I found the paper engaging and thought-provoking.

Nevertheless, I have several comments and questions:
- Section 2 (from Eq. (1) to the equation on line 99):
The Nyström method also constructs approximations to integral operators (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be helpful to acknowledge this connection and clarify in what respects your approach aligns with or diverges from Nyström-based approximations.
- Section 2, Eq. (2):
There is prior work in SVMs and kernel methods that formulates learning via operator equations. Please cite this literature and explain whether Eq. (2) is a special case of those formulations or, if not, precisely how it differs.
- Section 2, around line 128 (Representer Theorem):
Because the objective in Eq. (2) is not the standard form commonly used in learning theory, the invocation of a representer theorem needs justification. Please either provide a proof tailored to this setting or give an exact citation and demonstrate how Eq. (2) meets the theorem’s assumptions.
- Section 4, Eq. (8):
The soft-thresholding PCA interpretation is appealing. Could you discuss how this relates to or differs from established constructions such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?
