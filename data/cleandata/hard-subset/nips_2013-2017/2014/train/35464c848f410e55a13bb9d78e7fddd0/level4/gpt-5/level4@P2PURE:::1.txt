This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. It extends the classical kernel-based supervised learning optimization to incorporate unlabeled data, yielding a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is technically strong, with claims supported by solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation induces a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information. The resulting classifier differs from the classical representer theorem solution, yet remains elegant and computationally straightforward.
I have a few comments: First, regarding Equation 3, which gives the solution to the proposed optimization problem: since the authors mention that the associated kernel need not be PSD, does Equation 3 still hold in that case? A proof or derivation of Equation 3 would also have been helpful. Second, in the experimental section, the authors select an “optimal” parameter for all benchmarked methods. How were these parameters determined, and what precisely is meant by “optimal”?

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and largely well organized. It is easy to follow, and the main ideas are explained adequately. There are, however, a few typos:
- Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” -> “… will be a Euclidean …”
- Line 83: “or” is missing.
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” -> “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” -> “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or …” -> “That is, for …”
- Line 352: Sentence needs to be rewritten.

Originality
The approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is essentially novel. The distinctions from that work and others are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are important and meaningful. There was a significant effort to evaluate the proposed method on multiple datasets across different application domains.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section.
