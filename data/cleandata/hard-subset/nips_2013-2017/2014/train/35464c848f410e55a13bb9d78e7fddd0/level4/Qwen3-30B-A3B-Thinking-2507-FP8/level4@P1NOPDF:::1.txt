

Quality  
This paper presents a novel framework for semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels, extending classical kernel-based supervised optimization to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral equation. The technical quality is high, with well-supported theoretical claims and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a data-dependent kernel that integrates unlabeled information, and the resulting classifier remains elegant and computationally straightforward despite differing from the standard representer theorem solution.  

I have a few comments: First, regarding Equation 3, the authors note the associated kernel need not be PSD. Does Equation 3 remain valid in this case? A derivation or proof of Equation 3 would strengthen the argument. Second, in the experimental section, the authors report using a single "optimal" parameter for all benchmark methods. How were these parameters determined? What precisely constitutes "optimal" in this context?  

Clarity  
The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, with clear exposition of core ideas. Minor typos require correction:  
- Line 82: "... will a Euclidean..." → "... will be a Euclidean..."  
- Line 83: Missing "or"  
- Section 4 title: "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"  
- Line 283: "porportional" → "proportional"  
- Line 283: "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."  
- Line 352: Sentence requires revision.  

Originality  
The approach builds on recent work (e.g., [9] at NIPS), but the core contribution is novel. The distinctions from [9] and other related work are clearly articulated.  

Significance  
The results are significant, with substantial effort devoted to evaluating the method across diverse datasets and application domains. The theoretical foundation and experimental validation collectively demonstrate strong potential for semi-supervised learning.  

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper with a solid theoretical basis and compelling empirical results. The minor technical and editorial points noted above are easily addressable.
