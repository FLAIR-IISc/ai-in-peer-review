- Presents a technically sound semi-supervised framework using Fredholm kernels, extending kernel optimization via a discretized Fredholm integral with rigorous theory and convincing experiments.  
- Introduces a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled data; resulting classifier departs from the standard representer theorem but is elegant and computationally efficient.  
- Minor concern: Equation 3 may assume PSD kernels—need a brief derivation/justification for validity when the kernel is not PSD.  
- Minor concern: “Optimal” experimental parameters are not explained—clarify how they were chosen (e.g., cross-validation, grid search) to improve reproducibility.  
- Well-written and logically structured overall, but several typographical errors should be corrected (Line 82, 83, Section 4 title, Lines 247, 283, 352).  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., NIPS [9]) but clearly articulates differences, establishing the approach’s novelty.  
- Results are significant and broadly evaluated, addressing an important gap in kernel-based semi-supervised learning with strong empirical support.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission with solid theoretical foundation and compelling experiments; addressing the minor points would further enhance impact.
