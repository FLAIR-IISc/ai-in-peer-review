- Introduces a novel supervised and semi-supervised framework using a regularized Fredholm integral equation and data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Proposes a "noise assumption" under which unlabeled data are theoretically beneficial.  
- Connects to kernel methods and integral operators; formulation is distinct from prior density ratio estimation and kernel mean embedding work.  
- Main contributions: general Fredholm-equation learning framework, theory showing noise-driven variance suppression, and empirical validation.  
- Derives interpretation as a standard kernel method with a novel data-dependent kernel and provides variance-reduction guarantees under specific noise models.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 4) is rigorous with clear theorems on variance reduction.  
- Empirical results are comprehensive and show improvements on synthetic and real datasets (text classification, digit recognition), especially when the noise assumption holds.  
- Clarity is generally good but the paper needs stronger discussion of connections to the Nystrom method and operator-based kernel approaches.  
- Relationship to operator equations in SVM/kernel PCA and to Fisher/probability product kernels is insufficiently explained.  
- The paper does not clearly distinguish how Eq. (2) differs from standard kernel methods; this should be clarified.  
- The representer theorem for the proposed optimization is not properly justified; a proof or citation is needed.  
- Experimental setup is clear, but the authors should explicitly discuss practical situations where the noise assumption holds.  
- Minor typos present (e.g., "Not that", "achived") that should be corrected.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical framework, strong empirical support, clear integral-equation connection, well-motivated noise assumption.  
- Weaknesses: limited connection to related kernel/operator methods, representer theorem justification lacking, and comparisons to related kernel-based methods are limited.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
