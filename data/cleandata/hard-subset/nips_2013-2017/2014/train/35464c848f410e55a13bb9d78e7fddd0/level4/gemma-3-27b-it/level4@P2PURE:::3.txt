The authors propose Fredholm kernels, a data-dependent kernel method that leverages unlabeled data in a natural way. They demonstrate several theoretical properties of this kernel and empirically show its competitive performance against existing state-of-the-art methods. 

The paper is well-written and organized.

Detailed comments:

1. Is there a systematic approach for selecting the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel? For instance, can a kernel width be chosen via cross-validation under certain assumptions?
2. It would be beneficial to include some toy experiments illustrating scenarios where the proposed method might *not* perform well. Understanding the settings in which the method is most effective would be valuable.

The paper is interesting and clearly written, and I recommend acceptance.
