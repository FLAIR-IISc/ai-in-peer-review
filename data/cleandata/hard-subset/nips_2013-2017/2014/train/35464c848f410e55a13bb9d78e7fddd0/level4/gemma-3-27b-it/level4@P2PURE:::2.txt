The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning utilizing Fredholm kernels. The paper provides a detailed discussion of many aspects of this method, and I find it quite compelling overall.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions that could strengthen the work:

- Section 2 (from equation 1 to equation on line 99):
The approach bears a resemblance to the Nyström method, which also approximates an integral equation (as seen in kernel PCA). It would be beneficial for the authors to acknowledge this connection and clarify the similarities and differences between the two approaches.

- Section 2, equation 2:
Operator equation formulations also exist within the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. I suggest the authors cite relevant work and explain whether equation 2 represents a special case of these formulations, or detail how it diverges from them.

- Section 2, line 128, regarding the representer theorem:
Equation 2 is not a standard formulation in learning theory. The authors implicitly assume the existence of a representer theorem; therefore, they should either prove such a theorem in this context or provide a precise reference and explain how equation 2 fits within the existing theoretical framework.

- Section 4, equation 8:
The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. However, it would be valuable to discuss how this differs from related approaches, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

Line 165: “Not that” should be corrected.
Line 221: “achived” should be “achieved”.
Line 341: “chossing” should be “choosing”.
Line 672: Table reference “??“ needs to be corrected.
