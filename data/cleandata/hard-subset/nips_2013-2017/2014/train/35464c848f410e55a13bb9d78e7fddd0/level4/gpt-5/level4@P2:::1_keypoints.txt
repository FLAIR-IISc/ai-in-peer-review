- Proposes reframing supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding data-dependent "Fredholm kernels."
- Estimates operator K_pX from unlabeled data to inject marginal-distribution information into predictors.
- Shows method is equivalent to a standard kernel algorithm with an induced, unlabeled-data–dependent kernel.
- Introduces a "noise assumption" that low-variance directions in unlabeled data are uninformative and should be attenuated.
- Theoretically demonstrates Fredholm kernels perform soft-thresholding on low-variance principal components and reduce estimator variance in noisy settings.
- Empirically performs well on synthetic datasets violating the cluster assumption and on real text, sentiment, and digit tasks.
- Connects to prior work on Fredholm equations, density-ratio estimation, kernel mean embeddings, shrinkage in RKHS, and manifold-regularization data-dependent kernels.
- Technical quality is high: sound formulation and clear analyses linking Fredholm kernels to variance reduction and PCA-like reweighting.
- Induced-kernel interpretation is elegant and leads to a practical algorithm.
- Request clarification: specify precise assumptions under which Eq. (3) and the representer-theorem derivation hold when the outer kernel k may be indefinite.
- Request derivation details: include a brief proof or appendix deriving Eq. (3) from Eq. (2), with matrix identities and the role of unlabeled points in Kl+u and KH.
- Request experimental protocol details: specify validation protocol, parameter search spaces, labeled validation budget, and whether unlabeled data was used for model selection.
- Clarity is generally good but contains minor typos and a few unclear sentences needing rewrite.
- Suggest explicitly define the "noise assumption" formally and summarize computational complexity and memory footprint for constructing k̂F and its normalized variant.
- Originality: novel focus on semi-supervised learning under the noise assumption and a specific induced-kernel construction integrating unlabeled data.
- Significance: provides a principled way to leverage unlabeled data beyond cluster/manifold assumptions, with theory explaining when unlabeled data helps.
- Pros: principled reformulation, solid theoretical linking to noise suppression, strong empirical performance, PSD of induced kernel under mild conditions.
- Cons: need explicit assumptions/proofs for Eq. (3) with indefinite k, unclear experimental model-selection details, minor typographical and clarity issues.
- Overall evaluation: well-written paper with strong theory and convincing experiments; addressing Eq. (3) assumptions and experimental protocol would strengthen the submission.
