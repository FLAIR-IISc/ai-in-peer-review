- Proposes reformulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral using data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" (compose inner RKHS kernel kH with outer kernel k integrated over pX).  
- Shows the method is equivalent to a standard kernel algorithm with an induced unlabeled-data–dependent kernel that is PSD when KH is PSD, and introduces a normalized variant for stability.  
- Introduces a "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning: local low-variance directions are uninformative for labels and can be treated as noise.  
- Theoretical: with a linear outer kernel the Fredholm kernel performs soft-thresholding PCA down-weighting low-variance directions.  
- Theoretical: with Gaussian outer kernels it yields unbiased estimates of a true kernel on the signal subspace and reduces variance versus using noisy observations.  
- Empirical: strong performance on synthetic examples (designed to violate cluster assumption) and real datasets (text, sentiment with linear kernels; digits with Gaussian kernels); normalized variants improve stability.  
- Relates to prior work on Fredholm formulations for density-ratio estimation and kernel mean embeddings, and parallels manifold-regularization/data-dependent kernels but targets a different (noise) assumption.  
- Technical quality is high: clear derivations and proofs (PSD property, variance reduction), elegant representer-style solution, and computational tractability.  
- Needs clarification whether Equation (3) and the representer-style solution/matrix identities remain valid when the outer kernel k is not PSD or symmetric.  
- Needs detailed description of hyperparameter selection: how "optimal" parameters were determined (validation protocol, search ranges, use of unlabeled data, equal tuning budget across methods).  
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized but contains several minor typos and one sentence needing rewriting (specific line edits provided).  
- Originality: novel construction of Fredholm kernels for semi-supervised learning and theoretical variance-reduction analyses distinct from prior RKHS shrinkage and Fredholm work.  
- Significance: provides a principled way to leverage unlabeled data beyond manifold/cluster assumptions with theoretical insights and practical impact.  
- Pros: conceptually clean framework incorporating unlabeled data, solid theory (PSD, PCA-like reweighting, unbiasedness, variance reduction), strong empirical results, and normalized stability variant.  
- Cons: unclear conditions for representer solution with non-PSD outer kernel, insufficient hyperparameter-tuning details, and minor typographical issues.
