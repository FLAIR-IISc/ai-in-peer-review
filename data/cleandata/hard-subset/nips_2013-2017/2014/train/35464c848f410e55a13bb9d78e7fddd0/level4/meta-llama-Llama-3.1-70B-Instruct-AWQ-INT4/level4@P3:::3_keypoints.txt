- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that fit naturally into the kernel framework.  
- Method can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption."  
- Theoretical results show Fredholm kernels suppress low-variance principal components and better approximate the true kernel on the hidden space.  
- Experimental results demonstrate performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, making the method easy to follow.  
- Strength: novel and interesting approach to semi-supervised learning.  
- Strength: provides both theoretical and empirical evidence supporting the approach.  
- Weakness: lacks a systematic procedure or guidance for selecting the Fredholm kernel width (e.g., cross-validation under assumptions).  
- Weakness: no toy experiments illustrating failure cases or limitations and the settings where the method is less effective.  
- Overall recommendation: vote for acceptance, but request addressing the above weaknesses in the final version.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
