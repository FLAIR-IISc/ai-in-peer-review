This paper presents a framework well-suited for semi-supervised learning built on Fredholm kernels. It extends the classical kernel-based supervised learning objective to leverage unlabeled data, resulting in a discretized Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is technically strong, with claims backed by solid theoretical analysis and persuasive experiments. The proposed formulation induces a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The resulting classifier departs from the standard representer-theorem solution yet remains elegant and computationally straightforward.
I have a few comments: First, concerning Equation 3, which states the solution to the proposed optimization problem. Since the authors note that the associated kernel need not be PSD, does Equation 3 still hold under that condition? A derivation or proof of Equation 3 would also have been helpful. Second, in the experimental section, the authors choose an “optimal” parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were these parameters selected, and what precisely is meant by “optimal”?

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. It is easy to follow, and the main ideas are adequately explained. There are some typos, for example:
- Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” -> “… will be a Euclidean …”
- Line 83: missing “or”.
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” -> “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” -> “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or…” -> “That is, for…”
- Line 352: sentence needs to be rewritten.

Originality
The approach is related to a recent NIPS paper ([9]) but is essentially novel. The distinctions from that work and others are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are important and impactful. There was substantial effort to evaluate the method on multiple datasets across different application domains.
Overall, a well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section.
