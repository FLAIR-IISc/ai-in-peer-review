This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning leveraging Fredholm kernels. It extends the standard optimization problem in kernel methods to integrate unlabeled data, resulting in a discretized Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The paper demonstrates strong technical merit, with claims substantiated by rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling experimental validation. The proposed formulation introduces a novel, data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information. While the resulting classification function departs from the traditional representer theorem solution, it remains both mathematically sound and computationally efficient. 

I have a couple of specific questions. Regarding Equation 3, which details the solution to the optimization problem, could the authors clarify if this equation remains valid when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD), as noted in the paper? Providing a derivation or justification for Equation 3 would be beneficial. Additionally, in the experimental section, the authors mention selecting an "optimal" parameter setting for all benchmark methods. Could they elaborate on the process used to determine these parameters and define what constitutes "optimal" in this context?

**Clarity**

The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it easy to understand the core concepts. However, several minor typographical errors should be addressed:

* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: A conjunction ("or") is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm"
* Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional"
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for..."
* Line 352: The sentence requires rewriting for clarity.

**Originality**

The presented approach builds upon related work, including a recent publication in NIPS ([9]), but constitutes a distinct and novel contribution. The authors clearly articulate the differences between their work and existing methods.

**Significance**

The findings of this research are important and impactful. The authors have made a considerable effort to thoroughly evaluate the proposed method across a diverse range of datasets and application areas. 

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper with a solid theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental evaluation.
