- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, extending kernel methods via a discretized Fredholm integral to incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Produces a novel, data-dependent kernel and a resulting classifier that differs from the classical representer-theorem solution.  
- High technical quality: claims supported by solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- The proposed solution remains elegant and computationally efficient.  
- Concern: Equation 3’s validity is unclear when the associated kernel is not PSD; a derivation/proof of Equation 3 is requested.  
- Concern: Experimental parameter selection is unclear—authors should explain how “optimal” parameters were chosen and define “optimal.”  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clearly written and well-organized.  
- Multiple minor typos and wording issues to fix (examples: line 82, line 83, Section 4 title “Fredhom”, line 247, line 283, line 352).  
- Originality: Related to a recent NIPS paper but novel; differences from prior work are clearly articulated.  
- Significance: Results are important and significant, supported by extensive tests across diverse datasets.  
- Overall: Well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and compelling experimental validation.
