This paper introduces a framework—well-suited to semi-supervised learning—built on Fredholm kernels. It extends the standard kernel-based supervised objective to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized, regularized Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The technical quality is strong: claims are backed by analysis and sound empirical evidence. The formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled data. While the resulting classifier deviates from the standard representer-theorem form, it remains concise and computationally tractable.
A few comments: In Equation 3, the authors note the “outer” kernel need not be PSD; does the stated solution still apply in that case? It would be helpful to see a derivation of Eq. 3. In the experiments, parameters are chosen “optimally” for all methods—how were these selected, and what precisely is meant by “optimal”?

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-structured, with the main ideas explained adequately. A few minor issues:
- Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” → “… will be a Euclidean …”
- Line 83: missing “or”
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” → “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or …” → “That is, for …”
- Line 352: the sentence is unclear and should be revised.

Originality
Although related to a recent NIPS paper ([9]), the core approach is novel. The distinctions from prior work are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are meaningful and impactful. The authors undertook substantial experimentation across diverse datasets and application areas.
Overall, a well-written paper with solid theoretical grounding and persuasive empirical validation.
