The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail. Overall, I find the paper very interesting.

Nevertheless, I still have a few suggestions and questions:

- In section 2 (from equation 1 to equation 99), the authors could further clarify the connection to the Nystrom method, which also considers an approximation to an integral equation (in that case, kernel PCA). It would be beneficial if the authors could explain in what sense their approach is similar to or differs from the Nystrom method.

- In section 2, equation 2, the authors should acknowledge the existing literature on operator equations in the context of support vector machines and kernel methods. They should explain how equation 2 relates to these approaches, specifically whether it is a special case or how it differs from them.

- In section 2, line 128, the authors invoke the representer theorem, which is less common in learning theory. To strengthen the paper, the authors should either prove a representer theorem for their specific case or provide a precise reference and explain how equation 2 is a special case of it.

- In section 4, equation 8, the interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is nice and interesting. However, the authors should discuss how this approach differs from other methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

- There are a few typos that need to be corrected: "Not that" (line 165) should be "Note that", "achived" (line 221) should be "achieved", and "chossing" (line 341) should be "choosing". Additionally, the table reference on line 672 is incomplete.

Overall, the paper presents a new approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels, which is a promising direction for future research.
