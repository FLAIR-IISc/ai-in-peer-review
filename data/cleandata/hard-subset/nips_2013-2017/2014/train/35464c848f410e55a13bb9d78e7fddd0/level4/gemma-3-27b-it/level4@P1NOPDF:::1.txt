This paper presents a framework particularly well-suited for semi-supervised learning, building on Fredholm kernels. The classical supervised learning optimization problem in kernel-based methods is extended to incorporate unlabeled data, resulting in a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The paper is of high technical quality, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a novel, data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The resulting classifying function differs from the standard representer theorem solution, yet remains elegant and computationally efficient.

I have a few comments. First, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem: given that the authors state the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD), does Equation 3 still hold? A proof outlining the derivation of Equation 3 would be beneficial. Second, the experimental section mentions choosing an “optimal” parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were these parameters determined? Could the authors clarify what is meant by “optimal” in this context?

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow and adequately explaining the main ideas. However, there are a few minor typos:

* Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” should read “… will be a Euclidean …”
* Line 83: An “or” is missing.
* Section 4 title: “Fredhom” should be “Fredholm”
* Line 247: “porportional” should be “proportional”
* Line 283: “That is, or…” should be “That is, for…”
* Line 352: This sentence requires rewriting for clarity.

**Originality**

The proposed approach relates to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), but is fundamentally novel. The authors clearly articulate how their work differs from this and other related publications.

**Significance**

The results are important and significant. The authors invested considerable effort in testing the proposed method on a variety of datasets across different application domains. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and a compelling experimental section.
