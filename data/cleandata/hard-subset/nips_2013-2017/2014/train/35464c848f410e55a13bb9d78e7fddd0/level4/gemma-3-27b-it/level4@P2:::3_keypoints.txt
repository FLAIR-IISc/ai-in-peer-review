- Introduces Fredholm kernels: a data-dependent kernel derived by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation that incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Provides theoretical analysis supporting benefits of Fredholm kernels, especially under a stated “noise assumption.”  
- Shows variance reduction in certain estimators (Section 4), strengthening the theoretical claims.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on synthetic and real-world datasets, including linear and Gaussian kernel variants.  
- Well-written and logically organized; clearly explains motivation, methodology, and experiments.  
- Positions work within kernel methods, semi-supervised learning, and integral operator literature, citing relevant prior work.  
- Novelty: offers a fresh perspective on semi-supervised learning by framing it as a Fredholm problem.  
- Theoretical foundation: solid analysis and justification for the proposed approach.  
- Experimental validation: comprehensive experiments that show competitive results versus state-of-the-art.  
- Clarity: effectively communicates complex mathematical ideas and connections to related approaches.  
- Weakness: lacks a systematic strategy or practical guidelines for selecting the Fredholm kernel width parameter (t).  
- Weakness: does not discuss scenarios or toy examples where the method may fail or be less effective.  
- Weakness: the “noise assumption” is somewhat intuitive and could be more formally justified or empirically examined for real datasets.  
- Recommendation: overall a strong contribution with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
