- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces a new data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that combines labeled and unlabeled data naturally.  
- Presents the "noise assumption" and provides theoretical results showing Fredholm kernels can leverage unlabeled data under this assumption.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid and gives foundational understanding of the Fredholm kernel.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincingly demonstrates effectiveness.  
- Novel contribution to semi-supervised learning through the Fredholm framework and the noise assumption.  
- Potentially significant impact for applications with abundant unlabeled data.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed guidance on choosing the Fredholm kernel width beyond cross-validation.  
- Weakness: could include more toy experiments to illustrate method limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
