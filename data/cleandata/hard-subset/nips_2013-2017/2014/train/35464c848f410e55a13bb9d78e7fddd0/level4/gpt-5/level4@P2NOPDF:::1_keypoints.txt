- Presents a semi-supervised learning framework using discretized Fredholm integral equations to incorporate unlabeled data into a new data-dependent kernel.  
- Technically strong with rigorous analysis and convincing experiments; yields an elegant, computationally efficient classifier that departs from the classical representer-theorem form.  
- Needs clarification/proof for Equation (3): does it hold when the associated kernel is indefinite (not PSD), and what are the existence/uniqueness/stability conditions?  
- Experimental protocol unclear: how were “optimal” parameters chosen (cross-validation/validation split/grid search), what ranges and search budgets were used, and was the same procedure applied to all methods?  
- Generally clear and well organized, but several typographical and wording errors need correction (examples: Line 82, Line 83, Section 4 title, Lines 247, 283, 352).  
- Approach is substantively novel despite relation to a recent NIPS paper, with distinctions from prior work clearly articulated.  
- Results are meaningful and significant, showing clear benefits from incorporating unlabeled data across diverse datasets.  
- Overall: a well-written paper with solid theoretical foundations and a compelling experimental section.
