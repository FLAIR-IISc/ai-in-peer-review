- Proposes a novel supervised/semi-supervised framework based on a regularized Fredholm integral equation and introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that incorporate unlabeled data.
- High technical quality with strong theoretical analysis and convincing experimental validation.
- Fredholm kernels effectively integrate unlabeled information; resulting classification function is elegant and computationally feasible.
- Technical concern: Equation 3’s validity is unclear when the associated kernel is not PSD; a derivation/justification for using the representer theorem in this setting is needed.
- Experimental clarification needed: specify how "optimal" parameters were selected (e.g., cross-validation on a validation set) and which metric determined optimality.
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized, with a clear introduction situating the work in context.
- Several minor typos and phrasing errors should be corrected (specific line numbers provided).
- Originality: distinct from recent kernel mean embedding and density-ratio work; introduces a new "noise assumption" that differs from manifold/cluster assumptions.
- Significance: results are important and broadly applicable, demonstrating utility of unlabeled data especially when labeled examples are scarce.
- Theoretical impact: shows Fredholm kernels can suppress noise and reduce variance; includes compelling connections to PCA (Theorem 2) and kernel approximation results (Theorems 3–4).
- Recommendation: address the Equation 3 clarification and parameter-selection details; with these minor revisions the paper is an excellent contribution.
