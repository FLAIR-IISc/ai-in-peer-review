## Review of "Learning with Fredholm Kernels"

This paper introduces Fredholm kernels, a novel data-dependent kernel method for supervised and semi-supervised learning. The core idea is to reformulate the learning problem as solving a regularized Fredholm integral equation, naturally incorporating unlabeled data. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating the benefits of this approach under a "noise assumption," and empirical results showing competitive performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The paper builds upon a rich history of kernel methods and integral operator approaches in machine learning [11, 12]. It is particularly related to prior work on density ratio estimation using Fredholm equations [10] and kernel mean embeddings [5, 6, 7], as well as data-dependent kernels for manifold regularization [13]. The authors clearly situate their work within this landscape, highlighting the connections and distinctions. This is a well-written and organized paper that proposes a potentially valuable contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The Fredholm kernel framework offers a fresh perspective on semi-supervised learning by explicitly addressing the role of noise in unlabeled data.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The authors provide theoretical analysis supporting the effectiveness of Fredholm kernels under the proposed “noise assumption,” exploring variance reduction properties.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are comprehensive, demonstrating competitive performance on synthetic datasets designed to illustrate the noise assumption and on several real-world benchmark datasets. The inclusion of comparisons against established semi-supervised learning methods (TSVM, LapRLSC) is valuable.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the mathematical concepts and algorithmic details. The connection to standard kernel methods via the induced data-dependent kernel is particularly well articulated.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Kernel Width Selection:** The paper does not provide a systematic approach for selecting the kernel width parameter *t* (or *r* for the Gaussian kernel). While cross-validation is mentioned in the experiments section, a more detailed discussion of this parameter selection process and its sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.
* **Negative Results/Limitations:** While the synthetic experiments demonstrate scenarios where the method performs well, the paper lacks a clear discussion of settings where Fredholm kernels *might* be less effective. Exploring potential limitations and failure modes would strengthen the work.
* **Noise Assumption Specificity:** The “noise assumption” is intuitively appealing, but its precise mathematical formulation could be more rigorous. Clarifying the conditions under which this assumption holds and its relation to existing assumptions like the manifold assumption would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to semi-supervised learning.
* The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed method.
* The empirical results demonstrate competitive performance and support the theoretical findings.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The lack of a systematic approach for kernel width selection is a practical limitation.
* The absence of a discussion of potential failure modes weakens the overall assessment of the method.
* While the “noise assumption” is interesting, its precise mathematical characterization could be improved.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite minor weaknesses, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning. The novelty of the approach, the solid theoretical foundation, and the compelling empirical results outweigh the limitations. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest the authors expand the discussion of kernel parameter selection and include a more thorough analysis of potential limitations and failure modes.
