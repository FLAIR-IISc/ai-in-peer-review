- Proposes a semi-supervised framework reformulating prediction as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, inducing a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel."
- Fredholm kernel composes an outer kernel k with an inner kernel kH via an empirical integral operator over unlabeled data; PSD if kH is PSD, even when k is not.
- Introduces a normalized variant to address numerical instability for small-bandwidth Gaussian k.
- Proposes a "noise assumption": low-variance local directions are uninfluential for labels and should be suppressed.
- Theoretically shows Fredholm kernels perform soft PCA reweighting (linear k) and reduce variance of clean-kernel estimates (linear and Gaussian targets) under Gaussian mixture/linear subspace models with additive noise.
- Empirically reports improvements over RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC on synthetic (cluster-violation) and real benchmarks (text, sentiment, digits).
- Correctly relates work to Fredholm methods, kernel mean embeddings, shrinkage in RKHS, and data-dependent/manifold kernels; novelty is integral-equation formulation for general supervised losses.
- Representer theorem yields finite solution; PSD result and theoretical variance/noise suppression arguments are sound.
- Some proofs and the hinge-loss extension are deferred to the full version and should be included in the supplement for completeness.
- Practical scalability concerns: time and memory scale with l + u due to KH and KF; no discussion of Nyström, random features, or mini-batch approximations.
- Hyperparameter selection (bandwidths, normalization) is only briefly discussed and sensitivity analysis is limited.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; the Fredholm objective to induced kernel link is well explained.
- Minor clarity issues: occasional misuse of "unsupervised" instead of "semi-supervised" and unclear inductive dependence on the unlabeled set for predictions.
- Recommends adding an explicit algorithm box with inputs/outputs, complexity, and parameter-selection guidance to improve reproducibility.
- Composition of outer/inner kernels with unlabeled-data–weighted integration offers a fresh perspective and appears novel.
- The "noise assumption" is a useful alternative to cluster/manifold assumptions and the variance-reduction theory is valuable.
- Empirical gains are promising but would benefit from stronger baselines, ablations, and larger-scale evaluations to establish impact.
- Pros: principled integral-equation to kernel connection, natural unlabeled-data incorporation, theoretical noise/variance support, competitive empirical performance, normalized stabilization.
- Cons: computational/memory scaling with l+u, limited guidance on bandwidth/normalization selection, deferred key details, limited failure-mode analysis, comparisons could include more recent baselines and ablations.
- Requests for authors: clarify systematic bandwidth selection (CV/median heuristics/marginal-likelihood), show sensitivity curves for t and r.
- Requests: include toy failure-mode examples where noise aligns with informative directions or pX is poorly estimated with small u.
- Requests: provide complexity analysis and demonstrate scalable approximations (Nyström, inducing points, random features).
- Requests: clarify inductive vs transductive prediction behavior and robustness to changes in unlabeled data; evaluate test-time dependence.
- Requests: include hinge-loss/SVM variant derivation and experiments to show benefits beyond square loss.
- Requests: add ablations isolating unlabeled-data contribution and comparisons to label propagation/graph diffusion and recent semi-supervised methods.
- Requests: fix notational/terminology issues, advise when to use normalized vs unnormalized variants, and clarify scaling constants' interaction with regularization.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying parameter selection, adding a brief complexity/scalability discussion, and including supplemental proofs and the hinge-loss extension.
