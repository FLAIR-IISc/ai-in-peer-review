

The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. The paper details numerous aspects of the method thoroughly. Overall, I find this paper highly interesting.  
Nevertheless, I have a few suggestions and questions:  
- Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to line 99):  
  The Nystrom method also approximates integral equations (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be beneficial for the authors to reference this connection and clarify how their approach aligns with or differs from such approximations.  
- Section 2, Eq. 2:  
  Operator-based approaches for support vector machines and kernel methods exist in the literature. The authors should cite relevant work and explain whether Eq. 2 constitutes a special case or how it diverges from established operator formulations.  
- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):  
  Eq. 2 is less standard in learning theory. The authors assume a representer theorem holds; they must either prove it here, provide a precise reference, or clarify how Eq. 2 relates to existing representer theorems.  
- Section 4, Eq. 8:  
  The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. How does this differ from methods such as Fisher kernels or probability product kernels?
