- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels, discretizing a Fredholm integral to incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Induces a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information.  
- Resulting classifier departs from the classical representer-theorem solution but remains elegant and computationally simple.  
- High technical quality: claims are supported by solid theoretical analysis and convincing experiments.  
- Question: Clarify Equation 3 derivation and whether it holds when the associated kernel is not PSD.  
- Question: Explain the experimental protocol — how were the “optimal” parameters chosen and what is meant by “optimal”?  
- Originality: Related to a recent NIPS paper but essentially novel, with clear articulation of differences.  
- Significance: Results are important, with substantial evaluation across multiple datasets and domains.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well organized.  
- Minor typos/issues to fix (examples): Line 82 “will a Euclidean”→“will be a Euclidean”; Line 83 missing “or”; Section 4 “Fredhom”→“Fredholm”; Line 247 “porportional”→“proportional”; Line 283 “That is, or”→“That is, for”; Line 352 needs rewriting.  
- Overall: Well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section.
