- Proposal: Reformulate supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, introducing data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that incorporate unlabeled data under a "noise assumption."  
- Contribution 1: A general Fredholm-equation framework that naturally combines labeled and unlabeled data.  
- Contribution 2: Theoretical analysis of the noise assumption showing Fredholm kernels can suppress noise via principal-component reweighting and reduce variance versus standard kernels.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets (text classification, digit recognition) showing competitive or improved performance.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical development, especially Section 4’s demonstration of noise suppression and variance reduction.  
- Strength: The noise assumption is well-motivated and distinct from manifold/cluster assumptions, and the method works when cluster assumptions are violated.  
- Strength: Elegant derivation of a data-dependent Fredholm kernel implementable within standard kernel methods.  
- Clarification needed: Explicitly connect to prior work on operator equations in kernel methods (e.g., kernel PCA, Nyström) and clarify how Equation 2 differs.  
- Theoretical gap: Provide a proof or precise reference for the representer theorem claimed for Equation 2.  
- Comparison needed: Relate the "soft-thresholding PCA" interpretation to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels, and clarify how noise suppression differs and leverages unlabeled data.  
- Related-work and assumptions: Strengthen comparison with kernel-based semi-supervised methods (e.g., manifold regularization, LapRLSC) and clarify differing assumptions about data structure.  
- Minor issues: Fix typos ("Not that"→"Note that", "achived"→"achieved", "chossing"→"choosing") and correct the "Table ??" reference.  
- Overall assessment: High significance, strong theoretical and empirical contributions; recommend acceptance pending clarifications.
