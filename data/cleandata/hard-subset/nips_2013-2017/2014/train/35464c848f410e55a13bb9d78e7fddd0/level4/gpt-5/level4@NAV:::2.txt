The authors introduce a compelling new approach for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. Many components of the method are described in depth. Overall, I find the paper highly interesting.

That said, I have a few suggestions and questions:
- Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to the equation on line 99):
In the Nyström method, one also approximates an integral equation (as in kernel PCA). It would be helpful to mention this connection and clarify in what ways the proposed approach is similar or different.
- Section 2, Eq. 2:
Within the SVM and kernel-methods literature, formulations using operator equations have been studied. Please cite this work and explain whether Eq. 2 is a special case of those formulations (or, if not, how it differs).
- Section 2, line 128, representer theorem:
Eq. 2 is less common in learning theory. The paper assumes a representer theorem; the authors should either prove it here or provide a precise citation and explain how Eq. 2 fits as a special case.
- Section 4, Eq. 8:
The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is appealing. How does it compare to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?
