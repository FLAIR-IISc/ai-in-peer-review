- Introduces a novel framework reformulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a new data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Proposes a "noise assumption" that unlabeled data can help identify and suppress uninformative/noisy feature dimensions.  
- Provides theoretical analysis (e.g., variance reduction for specific kernels) supporting the approach.  
- Presents extensive experiments showing Fredholm kernels effectively leverage unlabeled data and achieve strong performance on diverse tasks.  
- High technical quality: claims are well-supported by theory and convincing empirical results; solution is elegant and computationally accessible despite deviating from the standard Representer Theorem.  
- Request for clarification: explain derivation of Equation (3) when the outer kernel k is not PSD, perhaps via a brief derivation in the appendix.  
- Request for clarification: detail how "optimal" parameters were chosen in experiments (fixed validation set vs. cross-validation) to improve reproducibility.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Minor typos/grammar to correct: Line 82 "will a" → "will be a"; Line 83 missing "or" between classification/regression; Section 4 title "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"; Line 247 "porportional" → "proportional"; Line 283 "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."; Line 352 rephrase to "The experiments use subsets...".  
- Originality: novel application of Fredholm integral equations to create data-dependent kernels for semi-supervised classification and regression, distinct from manifold regularization and related work.  
- Significance: noise assumption complements cluster/manifold assumptions; Fredholm kernels are a principled, practical method with broad applicability demonstrated on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Overall: well-written paper with solid theoretical basis and convincing experiments, representing a valuable contribution to semi-supervised learning.
