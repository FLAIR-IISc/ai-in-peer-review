- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, discretizing the Fredholm integral to incorporate unlabeled data.  
- High technical quality with rigorous theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Introduces a novel, data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information and yields a classifier different from the standard representer-theorem solution.  
- Claims the method is elegant and computationally efficient.  
- Questions whether Equation 3 remains valid when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite and requests a derivation/proof of Equation 3.  
- Requests clarification on how the “optimal” parameter for all benchmarked methods was chosen and what “optimal” means in this context.  
- Paper is mostly clear and well-organized, making the main ideas easy to follow.  
- Notes several minor typos and phrasing errors that should be corrected (lines 82, 83, section title, 247, 283, 352).  
- Work is fundamentally novel though related to a recent NIPS paper; authors clearly explain differences from prior work.  
- Results are significant and supported by extensive testing across diverse datasets and application domains.
