

Quality  
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims through rigorous theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed framework extends classical supervised learning optimization in kernel-based methods to incorporate unlabeled information, yielding a novel data-dependent kernel that integrates unlabeled data. The resulting classifying function, while differing from the classical representer theorem solution, remains elegant and computationally efficient.  

I have a few comments: First, regarding Equation 3, the authors note that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). Does Equation 3 retain validity in this case? A derivation or proof of how Equation 3 was obtained would strengthen the presentation. Second, in the experimental section, the authors report using an "optimal" parameter for all benchmark methods. How were these parameters determined? What specific criterion defines "optimal" in this context?

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with ideas presented logically and accessibly. The main contributions are adequately explained. Minor typographical errors require correction:

Originality  
The proposed approach builds on recent work (e.g., reference [9]), but introduces a novel conceptual foundation. The distinctions from reference [9] and other related works are clearly articulated.  

Significance  
The results are important and significant. The authors demonstrate substantial effort in evaluating the method across multiple datasets spanning diverse application domains.  

Well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and compelling experimental validation.
