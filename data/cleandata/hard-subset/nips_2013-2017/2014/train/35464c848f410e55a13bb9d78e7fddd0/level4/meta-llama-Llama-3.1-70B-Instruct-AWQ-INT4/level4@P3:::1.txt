This paper proposes a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, leading to the introduction of Fredholm kernels. The authors demonstrate that this approach effectively incorporates unlabeled data and provides a more robust classifier, especially in the presence of noise.

Quality:
The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims through theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The classifying function, although differing from the classical representer theorem solution, is elegant and easy to compute. However, I would like to see a proof of how Equation 3 was obtained, especially considering that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). Additionally, the authors should clarify how the "optimal" parameters were determined for the benchmark methods in the experimental section.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow and understand the main ideas. However, there are some minor typos and grammatical errors, such as "will a Euclidean" instead of "will be a Euclidean" (Line 82), a missing "or" (Line 83), "Fredhom" instead of "Fredholm" (Section 4 title), and "porportional" instead of "proportional" (Line 247). Furthermore, some sentences, such as the one on Line 352, require rephrasing for better clarity.

Originality:
The proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is novel in essence. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others, demonstrating a good understanding of the existing literature.

Significance:
The results are important and significant, with a substantial effort to test the proposed method on several datasets in different application domains. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Fredholm kernels in various scenarios, including semi-supervised learning and noise robustness.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and technically sound framework for semi-supervised learning.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various scenarios.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed proof of Equation 3, especially considering the non-PSD kernel case.
* The authors should clarify the parameter selection process for the benchmark methods in the experimental section.
* Minor typos and grammatical errors need to be corrected.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper, as it presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning. However, the authors should address the minor issues mentioned above to further improve the quality and clarity of their work.
