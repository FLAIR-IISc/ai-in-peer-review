This paper introduces a framework for semi-supervised learning leveraging Fredholm kernels. It extends the standard kernel-based supervised learning optimization problem to integrate unlabeled data, resulting in a discretized Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The paper demonstrates strong technical merit, with claims substantiated by rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling experimental validation. The proposed formulation yields a novel, data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information. While the resulting classifying function deviates from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains computationally efficient and elegantly expressed. 

I have a couple of questions. Regarding Equation 3, which details the solution to the optimization problem: given the authors’ statement that the associated kernel is not necessarily positive semi-definite (PSD), does Equation 3 still hold? Providing a derivation of Equation 3 would be beneficial. Additionally, in the experimental section, the authors report using an “optimal” parameter setting for all benchmark methods. Clarification on how these parameters were determined, and a precise definition of “optimal” in this context, would be appreciated.

**Clarity**

The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making it easy to understand the core concepts. However, several minor typographical errors were identified:

* Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” should read “… will be a Euclidean …”
* Line 83: A conjunction ("or") is missing.
* Section 4 title: “Fredhom” should be “Fredholm”
* Line 247: “porportional” should be “proportional”
* Line 283: “That is, or…” should be “That is, for…”
* Line 352: The sentence requires revision for clarity.

**Originality**

The proposed approach builds upon related work, including a recent publication in NIPS ([9]), but presents a fundamentally novel contribution. The distinctions between this work and existing literature are clearly articulated.

**Significance**

The results presented are both important and significant. The authors have invested considerable effort in evaluating the proposed method across a diverse set of datasets and application domains. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental evaluation.
