This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning that leverages Fredholm kernels. The authors extend the standard supervised learning optimization problem from kernel methods to integrate unlabeled data, which results in a discretized form of the Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The technical quality of this work is high, with claims substantiated by both rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling experimental evidence. The formulation yields a novel data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates information from unlabeled samples. While the resulting classifying function deviates from the solution given by the classical representer theorem, it remains elegant in its form and computationally straightforward.

I have a few points for clarification:
First, concerning the solution described in Equation 3. The authors state that the associated kernel is not required to be positive semi-definite (PSD). Does the solution presented in Equation 3 remain valid under this relaxed condition? The inclusion of a proof or derivation for Equation 3 would be a valuable addition.
Second, in the experimental section, it is mentioned that an "optimal" parameter was chosen for every method in the comparison. Could the authors elaborate on how these parameters were determined and provide a precise definition of what "optimal" signifies in this context?

**Clarity**

The manuscript is, for the most part, clearly written and logically structured. The exposition is easy to follow, and the core ideas are communicated effectively. However, there are several minor typographical errors that should be corrected:

*   Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
*   Line 83: A connecting "or" appears to be missing.
*   Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
*   Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional".
*   Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for...".
*   Line 352: This sentence requires rephrasing for clarity.

**Originality**

While this work is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), the proposed approach is fundamentally novel. The authors do an excellent job of positioning their contribution and clearly distinguishing it from this and other existing methods.

**Significance**

The findings of this paper are both important and impactful. The authors have demonstrated the method's effectiveness through a comprehensive set of experiments across multiple datasets from diverse application domains, which underscores the significance of their contribution.

Overall, this is a strong paper, characterized by a solid theoretical foundation and persuasive experimental validation.
