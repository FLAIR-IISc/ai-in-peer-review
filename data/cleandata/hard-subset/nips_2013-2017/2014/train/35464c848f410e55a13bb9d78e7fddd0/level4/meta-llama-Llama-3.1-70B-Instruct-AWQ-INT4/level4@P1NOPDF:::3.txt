The authors propose a data-dependent kernel called Fredholm kernels, which leverages unlabeled data in a natural way. The novelty of this kernel lies in its ability to incorporate unlabeled data, and the authors demonstrate several theoretical properties of the kernel. They also experimentally verify that the kernel compares favorably with existing state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand their contributions.

Detailed comments:

1. Is there a systematic approach to selecting the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel? For instance, under certain assumptions, can cross-validation be used to determine an optimal kernel width? Clarifying this point would strengthen the paper's methodology.

2. It would be beneficial to include some toy experiments that illustrate scenarios where the proposed method may not perform well. This would provide insight into the settings in which the proposed method is most effective, allowing for a more nuanced understanding of its strengths and limitations.

Overall, the paper is interesting and clearly written. I vote for acceptance.
