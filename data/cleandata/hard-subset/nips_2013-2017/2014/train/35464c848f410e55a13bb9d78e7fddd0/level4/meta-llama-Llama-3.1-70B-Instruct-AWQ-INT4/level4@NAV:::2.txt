The authors introduce a novel approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning, leveraging Fredholm kernels, which presents an innovative data-dependent method. The paper provides an in-depth discussion of various aspects of this approach, rendering it highly engaging.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions to further enhance the paper:

- In Section 2 (equations 1-99), the authors may benefit from drawing a parallel with the Nystrom method, which also involves an approximation to an integral equation, specifically kernel PCA. A clarification on the similarities and differences between these approaches would be beneficial.
- Equation 2 in Section 2 has precedents in operator equations within the realm of support vector machines and kernel methods. The authors should acknowledge this existing work and elucidate how their equation 2 relates to or diverges from these established methods.
- The representer theorem, as stated in line 128 of Section 2, is less common in learning theory. To substantiate their assumption, the authors should either provide a proof of the representer theorem or offer a precise reference and explain how equation 2 is a special case or differs from it.
- The interpretation of equation 8 in Section 4 as soft-thresholding PCA is intriguing. To provide a more comprehensive understanding, the authors should discuss how this approach differs from other methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.
- Several typos were noted: "Not that" (line 165), "achived" (line 221), "chossing" (line 341), and "Table ??" (line 672).

Overall, the paper presents a new and captivating approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels.
