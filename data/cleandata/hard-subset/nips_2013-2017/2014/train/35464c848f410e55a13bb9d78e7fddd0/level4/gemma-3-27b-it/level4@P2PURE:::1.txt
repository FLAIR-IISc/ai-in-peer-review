This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The standard supervised learning optimization problem in kernel methods is extended to integrate unlabeled data, resulting in a discretized Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is technically sound, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and compelling experimental results. The proposed formulation introduces a novel, data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information. While the resulting classifying function deviates from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains elegant and computationally efficient. 
I have a few questions and suggestions: Regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, does this equation still hold given the authors’ statement that the associated kernel is not necessarily positive semi-definite (PSD)? Providing a derivation of Equation 3 would strengthen the paper. Additionally, in the experimental section, the authors report using an "optimal" parameter setting for all benchmark methods. Could they elaborate on how these parameters were determined and clarify the meaning of “optimal” in this context?

Clarity
The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the main ideas. However, there are a few minor typographical errors:
* Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” should be “… will be a Euclidean …”
* Line 83: A conjunction ("or") is missing.
* Section 4 title: “Fredhom” should be “Fredholm”
* Line 247: “porportional” should be “proportional”
* Line 283: “That is, or…” should be “That is, for…”
* Line 352: The sentence requires rewriting for clarity.

Originality
The proposed approach builds on related work, including a recent NIPS publication ([9]), but is fundamentally novel. The distinctions between this work and existing literature are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are important and demonstrate the significance of this work. The authors have made a considerable effort to evaluate the proposed method across a variety of datasets and application domains. 
Overall, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental evaluation.
