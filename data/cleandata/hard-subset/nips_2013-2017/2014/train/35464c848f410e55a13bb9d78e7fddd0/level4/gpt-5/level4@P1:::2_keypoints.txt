- Proposes supervised and semi-supervised learning via a regularized Fredholm integral equation that yields data-dependent “Fredholm kernels.”  
- Shows the learned predictor can be viewed as a standard kernel method using a new kernel built from labeled and unlabeled data.  
- Introduces a distinct “noise assumption” for semi-supervised learning (different from manifold/cluster assumptions).  
- Theoretically argues Fredholm kernels can suppress low-variance (uninformative) directions and reduce estimator variance.  
- Empirical results are competitive on synthetic tasks tailored to the noise assumption and on several real datasets.  
- Connects to prior work on integral operators, RKHS methods, density ratio estimation, kernel mean embeddings, and data-dependent kernels.  
- Novel theoretical perspective on noise suppression and variance reduction in semi-supervised learning.  
- Strength: clear, principled formulation producing a PSD data-dependent kernel when the inner kernel is PSD.  
- Strength: intuitive and solid theory—linear outer kernel reweights and soft-thresholds small-variance directions; Gaussian choices reduce variance.  
- Strength: practical reduction to standard kernel machinery via kernel matrix KF and normalization to handle narrow outer kernels.  
- Strength: empirical evidence supports benefits when the noise assumption holds.  
- Weakness: connection to other operator-based and data-dependent kernel methods (Nyström, kernel PCA, Fisher/PPK) is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: representer theorem for Eq. (2) is assumed rather than proved or precisely cited; needs formal justification.  
- Weakness: some exposition, notation choices, line references, and typos reduce clarity.  
- Detailed request: discuss Nyström method and clarify similarities/differences with using unlabeled data through Kp̂X.  
- Detailed request: relate Eq. (2) to operator-equation formulations (Tikhonov regularization, linear operators on RKHS) and cite relevant work.  
- Detailed request: provide a tailored representer theorem or explicit citation showing Eq. (2) fits generalized representer conditions.  
- Detailed request: compare the soft-thresholding PCA interpretation (Eq. 8) to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels, clarifying shared/different assumptions and effects.  
- Minor edits: fix typos (“Not that”→“Note that”, “achived”→“achieved”, “chossing”→“choosing”), table reference, Nyström capitalization, spacing, and kF/k̂F consistency.  
- Evaluation: methodology is sound but missing representer-theorem justification; clarity is good but could be tightened; originality is high; significance is potentially strong.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled framework linking integral equations and data-dependent kernels, theoretically motivated noise suppression, competitive empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of detailed theoretical justification for Eq. (2), insufficient comparison to Nyström/Fisher/PPK, and minor clarity issues.  
- Overall: interesting and promising paper that would be strengthened by addressing the representer-theorem gap and clarifying relations to related operator-based and data-dependent kernel methods.
