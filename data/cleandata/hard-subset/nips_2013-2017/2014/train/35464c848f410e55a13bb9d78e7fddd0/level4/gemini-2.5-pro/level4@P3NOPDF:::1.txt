This paper proposes a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The authors extend the classical supervised learning optimization problem for kernel-based methods to incorporate information from unlabeled data, which results in a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The technical quality of this paper is high. The claims are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. A key contribution is a novel formulation that yields a new data-dependent kernel incorporating information from unlabeled data. While the resulting classifying function deviates from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains elegant and computationally efficient.

I have a few questions for the authors that could further strengthen the paper:

1.  Regarding Equation 3, which presents the solution to the optimization problem: The authors state that the associated kernel is not required to be positive semi-definite (PSD). Could they clarify if Equation 3 remains valid in the non-PSD case? Including a derivation or proof for Equation 3, perhaps in the supplementary material, would strengthen the paper's theoretical underpinnings.

2.  In the experimental section, the authors mention that an "optimal" parameter was chosen for all benchmarked methods. It would be beneficial to elaborate on this parameter selection process. Specifically, what procedure was used to determine these parameters (e.g., cross-validation on the labeled set?), and what was the precise definition of "optimal" in this context?

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and organized for the most part, making it easy to follow. The core ideas are explained adequately. However, there are several minor typos that should be corrected:

*   Line 82: "...will a Euclidean..." should be "...will be a Euclidean..."
*   Line 83: An "or" seems to be missing in the phrase "a weighted graph Laplacians a diffusion kernel."
*   Section 4 Title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
*   Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional".
*   Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for...".
*   Line 352: The sentence "The first remark is that our kernel depends on the statistics of the marginal distribution p(x) and not only data, through the integral operator L_K." is slightly awkward and could be rephrased for clarity.

**Originality**

The proposed approach is novel, although it relates to recent work, notably [9]. The authors do a commendable job of clearly situating their contribution and differentiating it from prior art.

**Significance**

The work is significant due to its potential impact on semi-supervised learning. The authors have made a considerable effort to validate their method, testing it extensively on several datasets across different application domains, which underscores the practical relevance of their findings.

**Overall**

This is a well-written paper presenting a novel and significant contribution to semi-supervised learning. It is grounded in solid theory and supported by a convincing experimental evaluation. I recommend acceptance, provided the authors address the minor points and questions raised above.
