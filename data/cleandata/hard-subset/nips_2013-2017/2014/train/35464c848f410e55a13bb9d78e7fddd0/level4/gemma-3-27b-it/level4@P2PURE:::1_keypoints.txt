- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels, turning kernel optimization into a discretized Fredholm integral equation.
- Technically sound with solid theoretical analysis and strong experimental results.
- Introduces a novel, data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled data.
- Resulting classifier differs from the classical representer theorem solution but is elegant and computationally efficient.
- Questions whether Equation 3 remains valid if the associated kernel is not PSD and requests a derivation of Equation 3.
- Asks for clarification on how the reported “optimal” parameter settings for benchmarks were determined and what “optimal” means.
- Generally well-written and organized, making the main ideas easy to follow.
- Lists specific minor typos and phrasing errors to correct (Lines 82, 83, Section 4 title, 247, 283, 352).
- Builds on related work (including a recent NIPS paper) but is fundamentally novel and clearly distinguishes itself from prior literature.
- Demonstrates significance through extensive evaluation across multiple datasets and application domains.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical foundation, convincing experiments, and a well-written paper.
