This paper introduces Fredholm kernels, a data-dependent kernel method with the appealing characteristic of naturally incorporating unlabeled data. The authors provide theoretical justification for the kernel’s properties and demonstrate its competitive performance against existing state-of-the-art techniques through experimentation. 

The manuscript is well-structured and clearly presented.

Specific comments for the authors:

1. Could the authors elaborate on a systematic strategy for kernel width selection for the Fredholm kernel? For instance, is it possible to determine an appropriate kernel width based on certain assumptions, perhaps utilizing cross-validation?
2. Including some illustrative toy experiments demonstrating scenarios where the proposed method *might* underperform would be beneficial. This would help delineate the conditions under which the method is most effective.

Overall, this is an interesting and well-written paper, and I recommend acceptance to the conference.
