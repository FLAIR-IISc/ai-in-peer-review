## Review of “New approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using Fredholm kernels, which are data-dependent”

This paper presents an interesting and novel method for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The approach is thoroughly discussed, and the paper as a whole is quite compelling. However, several points regarding related work and theoretical justification would benefit from clarification.

**Summary:** The authors introduce a kernel-based learning framework leveraging data-dependent Fredholm kernels. This allows for a formulation of learning problems as operator equations, potentially leading to new insights and algorithms. The paper explores both supervised and semi-supervised applications, along with a compelling connection to soft-thresholding PCA.

**Strengths:** The core idea of utilizing Fredholm kernels is original and potentially powerful. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the mathematical foundations and successfully apply the method to various learning tasks. The connection to soft-thresholding PCA (Section 4, Eq. 8) provides a valuable and intuitive interpretation of the approach. The experiments appear well-designed and showcase promising results.

**Weaknesses & Questions:**

* **Section 2 (Eq. 1 – Line 99):** The formulation presented bears a strong resemblance to integral equation approaches, such as those used in Nyström methods for kernel PCA. While the authors should not be expected to provide an exhaustive literature review, acknowledging this connection and explicitly delineating the similarities and differences would strengthen the presentation. How does this approach generalize beyond, or differ from, standard Nyström approximations?

* **Section 2 (Eq. 2):** Operator equation formulations are not uncommon in kernel methods and support vector machines. Several works have explored learning problems through this lens. The authors should contextualize Eq. 2 within this existing body of work. Specifically, is Eq. 2 a special case of a more general operator equation formulation? If so, under what conditions? If not, what key distinctions justify this new approach? 

* **Section 2 (Line 128 – Representer Theorem):**  The assumption of a representer theorem underlying Eq. 2 is crucial. The standard representer theorem relies on specific assumptions about the regularization term. Given that Eq. 2 represents a less conventional formulation, the authors need to either provide a proof of a suitable representer theorem for their setup or offer a precise citation to existing work that justifies this assumption.  A clear explanation of how the standard representer theorem applies (or doesn’t apply) to this particular formulation is essential for theoretical rigor.

* **Section 4 (Eq. 8 – Soft-Thresholding PCA):** The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. However, the authors should further discuss how this approach compares to other dimensionality reduction and feature learning techniques that also utilize sparsity-inducing penalties, such as Fisher kernels or probability product kernels. What are the advantages of this Fredholm kernel approach in terms of computational efficiency, performance, or theoretical properties compared to these alternatives?

**Typos and Minor Issues:**

* Line 165: “Not that” should likely be “Note that”.
* Line 221: “achived” should be “achieved”.
* Line 341: “chossing” should be “choosing”.
* Line 672: “Table ??” should be replaced with the correct table number.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses outlined above, I find this paper to be a valuable contribution to the field. The authors present a novel approach with a strong theoretical foundation and promising empirical results. Addressing the questions regarding related work and the representer theorem would significantly enhance the clarity and impact of the paper. I believe that with these revisions, this work has the potential to be a significant contribution to NIPS. 

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the points raised concerning related work (Nyström methods, operator equation formulations in SVMs), the representer theorem, and the comparison with alternative soft-thresholding methods.
