This paper introduces a novel and interesting framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The core contribution is a method for learning a data-dependent kernel by solving a Fredholm integral equation of the second kind. The authors provide a detailed theoretical discussion, an algorithm for its implementation, and an insightful interpretation of the learned solution as a form of soft-thresholding PCA. Overall, the paper is well-motivated and presents a theoretically rich approach to an important problem in kernel methods.

While I find the paper to be a valuable contribution, I have several questions and suggestions that I believe would help strengthen the manuscript and clarify its relationship to existing work.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Relation to the Nyström Method:**
    The formulation of the learning problem as the solution to an integral equation (from Eq. 1 to the equation on line 99) is reminiscent of the Nyström method. The Nyström method is also based on finding an approximate solution to an integral eigenvalue problem, which is fundamental to spectral methods like Kernel PCA. To better situate this work within the broader literature, it would be beneficial for the authors to explicitly discuss the connection to the Nyström method. A clarification of the key similarities and, more importantly, the fundamental differences in both the problem formulation and the resulting approximation would provide valuable context for the reader.

2.  **Contextualization with Operator-Theoretic Kernel Methods:**
    In Section 2, the authors formulate the learning problem using an operator equation (Eq. 2). The use of operator equations in Hilbert spaces is an established technique in the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. The paper would be strengthened by acknowledging this body of work and discussing how the proposed formulation relates to it. Is Equation (2) a special case of these more general operator-theoretic approaches, does it represent a new class of problems, or does it differ in some other fundamental way? Placing this work in the context of prior operator-based formulations would help clarify its specific contribution.

3.  **Justification for the Representer Theorem:**
    A critical theoretical point arises on line 128 concerning the representer theorem. The functional to be minimized, which leads to Equation (2), is less common than the standard Tikhonov regularization framework for which representer theorems are typically proven. The authors currently state that a representer theorem exists for their formulation without providing a proof or a specific reference. This is a significant assumption that underpins the validity of the proposed solution form. The authors should either provide a self-contained proof of a representer theorem that applies to their specific setting or, alternatively, cite an existing theorem and rigorously demonstrate that their formulation satisfies all of its required conditions. Without this justification, the theoretical foundation of the method remains incomplete.

4.  **Comparison with other Data-Dependent Kernels:**
    The interpretation of the proposed method as "soft-thresholding PCA" in Section 4 is elegant and provides excellent intuition. This interpretation makes it clear that the method is effectively learning a data-dependent kernel or similarity measure. This naturally invites a comparison to other well-known families of data-dependent kernels, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels, which also derive kernels from underlying probabilistic models of the data. Could the authors elaborate on the conceptual differences between their approach and these methods? A discussion of the potential advantages and disadvantages would help readers better understand the unique properties of the proposed Fredholm kernel approach.
