- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm integral equations that yields a new data-dependent kernel (not necessarily PSD).  
- High technical quality with strong theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Elegant formulation that naturally incorporates unlabeled data; resulting classifier differs from standard representer-theorem solutions yet is computationally tractable.  
- Key theoretical contribution: investigation of a “noise assumption” and theoretical evidence that Fredholm kernels perform well under it.  
- Major clarification needed: justify/sketch why Equation 3 holds when the kernel is not PSD or cite results guaranteeing existence/form under non-PSD kernels.  
- Experimental clarification needed: specify how “optimal” parameters were chosen (e.g., cross-validation) and define “optimal” (validation accuracy, etc.).  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Minor typos/grammar issues hurt polish (e.g., "will a"→"will be a"; missing "or"; "Fredhom"→"Fredholm"; "porportional"→"proportional"; "That is, or..."→"That is, for..."; awkward sentence at line 352).  
- Originality: builds on kernel and integral-operator literature but presents a distinct Fredholm-based approach and links to data-dependent kernels/manifold regularization.  
- Significance: broad empirical evaluation (synthetic, text, sentiment, digits) shows potential performance gains, especially when the noise assumption holds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel approach, strong experiments, addresses effective use of unlabeled data, generally clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need stronger justification for Eq.3 with non-PSD kernels, clarify parameter selection methodology, fix minor typos.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and empirically validated paper; minor clarifications and corrections needed—recommend acceptance.
