- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised framework reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation using data-dependent "Fredholm kernels."
- Introduces the "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning: low-variance directions in unlabeled data are uninformative for classification.
- Extends kernel methods by embedding them in an integral equation framework, distinct from manifold regularization and prior Fredholm-based density ratio work.
- Strong theoretical contributions: proves noise suppression via PCA with Fredholm kernels (Theorem 2).
- Provides variance reduction results for kernel approximations (Theorems 3 and 4).
- Experiments show competitive performance on synthetic tests tailored to the noise assumption and on real-world datasets.
- Main theoretical analyses assume infinite unlabeled data, which may limit practical applicability with finite unlabeled samples.
- Well-written and organized with clear mathematical formulations and a good link between Fredholm learning and standard kernel methods.
- Could use more detailed comparisons to related methods (e.g., Nyström, operator-equation approaches).
- The relation to operator equation approaches like [10] and to Nyström approximations is insufficiently clarified.
- Representer theorem justification for Eq. 2 needs more detail or a precise citation.
- Distinction between Fredholm kernels and other data-dependent kernels (e.g., Fisher kernels) is not adequately explained.
- Original and significant: provides a new conceptual advance and a complementary assumption to manifold/cluster assumptions.
- Empirical results indicate practical benefit when the noise assumption holds, demonstrating real-world relevance.
- Minor issues: several typos and small clarity problems throughout the manuscript.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution with strong theory and experiments; recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
