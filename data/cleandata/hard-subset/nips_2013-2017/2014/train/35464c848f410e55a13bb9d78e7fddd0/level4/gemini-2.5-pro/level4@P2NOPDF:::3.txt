This paper introduces Fredholm kernels, a novel class of data-dependent kernels designed to leverage unlabeled data in a principled manner. The key novelty lies in the natural integration of an unlabeled dataset into the kernel's construction. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the kernel's properties and present compelling experimental results demonstrating that the proposed method compares favorably with existing state-of-the-art approaches. The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.

**Detailed Comments:**

1.  A practical concern is the selection of the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of whether a systematic approach exists for tuning this hyperparameter. For instance, can the kernel width be effectively selected via cross-validation, perhaps under certain assumptions? Clarification on the recommended practice for this would be valuable for practitioners.

2.  To better characterize the proposed method, it would be beneficial to include experiments that explore its limitations. Illustrating scenarios where the Fredholm kernel might not perform well, perhaps on synthetic or toy datasets, would provide valuable insight into the settings and data distributions for which this approach is most effective. This would help readers understand not just when the method works, but also its potential failure modes.

**Recommendation:**

This paper presents an interesting and well-motivated contribution. The clear presentation and solid results make a strong case for the method. Despite the minor points for clarification raised above, I recommend acceptance.
