This paper introduces Fredholm kernels, a novel, data-dependent kernel method that effectively leverages unlabeled data. The key innovation lies in its natural incorporation of the unlabeled dataset into the kernel construction. The authors support their approach with both theoretical analysis, demonstrating key properties of the kernel, and empirical validation, showing competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning.

Here are some detailed comments and suggestions for improvement:

1. **Kernel Width Selection:** The paper would benefit from a discussion of strategies for selecting the kernel width parameter. While practical performance is demonstrated, a systematic approach—perhaps informed by theoretical assumptions or utilizing cross-validation—would enhance the method’s usability and robustness. Investigating and outlining such a method would be a valuable contribution.

2. **Limitations and Failure Modes:** To provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method, it would be helpful to include toy experiments or a discussion of scenarios where the Fredholm kernel might *not* perform well. Identifying the settings in which the method is most effective, and conversely, where it may struggle, will strengthen the paper and guide future research. Understanding these limitations will also help practitioners appropriately apply the method.

Overall, this is an interesting and promising contribution. The clear presentation and solid results lead me to recommend acceptance.
