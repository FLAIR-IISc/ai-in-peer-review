- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels that extends kernel-based supervised optimization to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral.
- High technical quality with strong theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.
- Introduces a novel data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information and yields a classifying function differing from the classical representer theorem solution while remaining computationally tractable.
- Unclear whether Equation 3 holds when the associated kernel is not PSD; a brief justification or proof sketch is requested.
- Experimental section uses an “optimal” parameter for benchmarks but lacks clarification on how “optimal” parameters were determined.
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, making main ideas easy to follow.
- Contains several minor typographical errors that should be corrected (e.g., Line 82, Line 83, Section 4 title, Line 247, Line 283, Line 352).
- Related to recent work (including a NIPS paper) but sufficiently novel and properly cites related research.
- Results are significant, with extensive evaluation across synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and theoretically sound framework; clear ties to kernel methods and integral operator theory; convincing empirical validation; addresses a relevant ML problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: need stronger justification for Equation 3 under relaxed PSD assumption; insufficient detail on parameter selection; minor typos slightly detract from clarity.
