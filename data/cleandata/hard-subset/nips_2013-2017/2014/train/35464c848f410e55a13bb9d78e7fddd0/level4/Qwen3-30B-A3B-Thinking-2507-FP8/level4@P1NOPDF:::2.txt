

The authors present an interesting new approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using Fredholm kernels. The method is discussed in considerable detail, and the paper is overall compelling. However, I have a few suggestions to strengthen the technical exposition.

- **Section 2 (equations 1–99):** The authors should clarify how their integral approximation relates to the Nyström method (used in kernel PCA for approximating integral equations). Specifically, it would be valuable to explicitly state the similarities or differences between the two approaches.

- **Section 2, Equation 2:** The literature on support vector machines and kernel methods includes several works employing operator equations. The authors should cite these and explain whether Equation 2 constitutes a special case or how it differs from prior formulations.

- **Section 2, Line 128 (Representer Theorem):** Equation 2 is not standard in learning theory. The authors assume a representer theorem holds but do not prove it. They should either establish this theorem for their formulation or provide a precise reference and clearly justify how Equation 2 arises from it.

- **Section 4, Equation 8:** The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. However, the authors should clarify how this approach differs from established methods like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels, particularly in terms of their underlying assumptions and computational mechanisms.

**Typos to correct:**  
- Line 165: "Not that" → "Note that"  
- Line 221: "achived" → "achieved"  
- Line 341: "chossing" → "choosing"  
- Line 672: "Table ??" → Specify the correct table number (e.g., "Table 1")  

The core contribution—leveraging data-dependent Fredholm kernels for learning—remains novel and promising. Addressing these points would significantly enhance the paper’s technical rigor and contextual clarity.
