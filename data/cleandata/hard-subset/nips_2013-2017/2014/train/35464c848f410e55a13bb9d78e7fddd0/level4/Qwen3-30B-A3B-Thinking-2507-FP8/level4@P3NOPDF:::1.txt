

This paper introduces a novel semi-supervised learning framework leveraging Fredholm integral equations, extending classical kernel-based optimization to incorporate unlabeled data. The resulting formulation yields a data-dependent kernel that integrates unlabeled information, producing a classifying function distinct from the standard representer theorem solution yet retaining computational elegance. The technical quality is high, supported by rigorous theoretical analysis and comprehensive experimental validation across diverse datasets. The method demonstrates significant empirical gains over baselines, underscoring its practical relevance.

**Technical Quality**  
The theoretical contribution is substantial, with clear justification for the proposed extension of the optimization problem. However, two points require clarification. First, Equation 3 presents the solution under the assumption that the kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). The authors should explicitly address whether Equation 3 remains valid without the PSD constraint, and provide a derivation or proof sketch to justify the solution’s form. Second, the experimental section states that "optimal" parameters were selected for all benchmark methods. It is unclear how these parameters were determined (e.g., via cross-validation, grid search, or domain-specific tuning). Clarifying the procedure for parameter selection—especially the meaning of "optimal" in this context—would strengthen the reproducibility and interpretability of the results.

**Clarity and Presentation**  
The paper is well-structured and largely clear, with logical flow from problem formulation to experiments. Minor typographical and grammatical errors, however, detract from readability:  
- Line 82: "...will a Euclidean..." → "...will be a Euclidean..."  
- Line 83: Missing "or" (e.g., "or" after "is")  
- Section 4 title: "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"  
- Line 247: "porportional" → "proportional"  
- Line 283: "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."  
- Line 352: Sentence requires restructuring for clarity.  
These should be corrected in the final version.

**Originality and Significance**  
The work builds on related ideas (e.g., reference [9]), but makes a distinct contribution by reformulating semi-supervised learning through Fredholm equations rather than relying on graph-based or manifold assumptions. The authors clearly articulate the conceptual differences from prior work, establishing novelty. The significance is evident in the method’s effectiveness across multiple domains (e.g., image, text, bioinformatics), with extensive experiments demonstrating robust performance improvements. The effort to validate on varied datasets—beyond standard benchmarks—highlights the framework’s versatility and potential impact.

**Recommendation**  
This is a strong submission with a solid theoretical foundation and compelling empirical results. Addressing the two technical clarifications (Equation 3’s validity without PSD and parameter selection methodology) would substantially enhance the paper. The minor editorial issues are easily rectified. The work merits acceptance, as it advances semi-supervised learning through a fresh mathematical perspective with demonstrable practical value. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify the methodological points raised above.
