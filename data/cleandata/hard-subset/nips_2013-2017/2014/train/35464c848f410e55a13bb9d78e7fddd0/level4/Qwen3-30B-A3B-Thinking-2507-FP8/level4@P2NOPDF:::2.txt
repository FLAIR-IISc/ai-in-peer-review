

This paper introduces a novel framework for supervised and unsupervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels, offering a promising approach with significant theoretical depth. The authors thoroughly cover multiple aspects of their method, including connections to integral equations and operator theory, making the work highly relevant to kernel-based learning. The soft-thresholding PCA interpretation (Section 4, Eq. 8) is particularly insightful. Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field.

To strengthen the manuscript, the following points require clarification or expansion:

1.  **Section 2 (Lines 1-99):** The paper discusses approximating solutions to Fredholm integral equations. The Nystrom method (used in kernel PCA) also approximates integral equations. The authors should explicitly compare their approach to the Nystrom method, clarifying key similarities and differences in the approximation strategy and its implications for learning.

2.  **Section 2, Equation (2):** Operator-theoretic formulations are well-established in kernel methods (e.g., for SVMs and regularization networks). The authors should cite relevant work (e.g., Schölkopf et al. on regularization operators) and precisely explain how Equation (2) relates to or generalizes these existing frameworks—specifically, whether it represents a special case or a distinct formulation.

3.  **Section 2, Line 128 (Representer Theorem):** The paper assumes a representer theorem for the solution structure (Equation 2) without proof or reference. This is a critical gap. The authors must either provide a rigorous proof of the representer theorem applicable to their setup or cite a precise, existing reference that directly justifies Equation (2) as a special case of a known theorem. Without this, the theoretical foundation is incomplete.

4.  **Section 4, Equation (8):** The soft-thresholding PCA interpretation is compelling. To highlight its novelty, the authors should explicitly contrast it with related methods like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels. Specifically, clarify how the Fredholm kernel approach differs in its underlying assumptions, optimization objective, or resulting feature space properties.

The paper’s core idea—leveraging Fredholm kernels for flexible learning—holds significant promise. Addressing these points will substantially enhance the theoretical rigor and contextual clarity, making the contribution even stronger for the NIPS audience. The work is well-motivated and technically sound, and these revisions are essential for full impact.
