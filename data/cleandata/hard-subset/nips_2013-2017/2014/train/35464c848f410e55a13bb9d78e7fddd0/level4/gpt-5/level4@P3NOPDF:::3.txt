Summary
The paper introduces a data-dependent kernel, the Fredholm kernel, that leverages unlabeled data in a natural way. The authors provide theoretical analysis of the kernel and present empirical results showing competitive or superior performance relative to strong baselines. The manuscript is well written and clearly organized.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A principled, intuitive construction that incorporates unlabeled data directly into the kernel.
- Theoretical grounding: The paper establishes key properties of the proposed kernel and situates it within a sound analytical framework.
- Empirical validation: Experiments indicate that the Fredholm kernel performs favorably against state-of-the-art methods.
- Clarity: The exposition is clear, and the structure of the paper makes it easy to follow.

Questions and Suggestions
1) Hyperparameter selection (kernel width):
- Is there a systematic approach to selecting the kernel width (and any other key hyperparameters) for the Fredholm kernel?
- In practice, can these be chosen via cross-validation, and if so, how should unlabeled data be incorporated in that process to avoid bias or leakage?
- If there are theoretical guidelines (e.g., scaling with sample size or bandwidth selection under smoothness assumptions), it would be very helpful to include them, even as heuristics.

2) Failure modes and scope of applicability:
- It would strengthen the paper to include a small set of toy experiments illustrating scenarios where the method is less effective or fails (e.g., when the unlabeled distribution differs substantially from the labeled distribution, or when the unlabeled data are very sparse or noisy).
- More generally, please characterize the settings in which the Fredholm kernel is most beneficial (e.g., regimes with abundant unlabeled data, particular manifold structures, or certain signal-to-noise conditions).

3) Sensitivity analyses:
- Provide sensitivity curves for key hyperparameters (e.g., kernel width, regularization parameters) to show robustness.
- Analyze the effect of the quantity and quality of unlabeled data (e.g., performance as a function of the number of unlabeled samples, and robustness to distribution shift between labeled and unlabeled sets).

4) Computational considerations:
- Clarify the computational complexity, memory requirements, and scalability with respect to the number of unlabeled samples.
- If approximations (e.g., sampling, low-rank approximations) are applicable, briefly discuss their impact on accuracy and runtime.

5) Baseline fairness and implementation details:
- Ensure all baselines are tuned comparably and, where relevant, are allowed to use the same unlabeled data.
- Include brief details sufficient for reproducibility (or provide code, if possible).

6) Relation to prior data-dependent kernels:
- A short discussion contrasting the Fredholm kernel with related approaches (e.g., graph/diffusion kernels, Laplacian-based semi-supervised methods, Nyström/data-dependent embeddings) would help position the contribution and clarify the unique advantages and trade-offs.

Minor comments
- Please correct minor grammatical issues (e.g., “several theoretical property” → “several theoretical properties”).
- Consistently use “Fredholm kernel” throughout.

Overall assessment
This is an interesting, well-presented paper with a clear contribution: a data-dependent kernel that naturally leverages unlabeled data, supported by theory and experiments. Addressing the practical guidance for hyperparameter selection and adding illustrative failure cases would further improve the work. I recommend acceptance.
