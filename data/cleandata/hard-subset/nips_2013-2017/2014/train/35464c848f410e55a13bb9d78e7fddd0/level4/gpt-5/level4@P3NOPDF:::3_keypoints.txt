- Introduces a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that leverages unlabeled data; well written and organized.  
- Conceptual novelty: principled, intuitive construction incorporating unlabeled data directly.  
- Theoretical grounding: key properties established within a sound analytical framework.  
- Empirical validation: competitive or superior performance vs. strong baselines.  
- Clarity: exposition and paper structure are clear and accessible.  
- Hyperparameter selection: request for systematic guidance (kernel width, other hyperparameters), cross-validation procedures that avoid leakage, and theoretical/heurstic scaling rules.  
- Failure modes/scope: ask for toy experiments or characterization of when the method fails or is less effective (distribution mismatch, sparse/noisy unlabeled data) and regimes where it is most beneficial.  
- Sensitivity analyses: request sensitivity curves for key hyperparameters and studies of performance vs. quantity/quality of unlabeled data and robustness to distribution shift.  
- Computational considerations: clarify complexity, memory, scalability with unlabeled sample size, and discuss approximations (sampling, low-rank) and their accuracy/runtime trade-offs.  
- Baseline fairness & implementation: ensure baselines are comparably tuned and can use the same unlabeled data; include reproducibility details or code.  
- Relation to prior work: add a short comparison to graph/diffusion kernels, Laplacian semi-supervised methods, Nyström/data-dependent embeddings to highlight differences and trade-offs.  
- Minor comments: fix small grammatical errors and consistently use the term "Fredholm kernel."  
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-presented contribution with theory and experiments; recommend acceptance pending added practical guidance and illustrative failure cases.
