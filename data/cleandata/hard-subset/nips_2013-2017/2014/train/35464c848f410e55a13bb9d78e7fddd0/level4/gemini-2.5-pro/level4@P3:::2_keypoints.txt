- Paper reformulates supervised/semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, defining predictions via an integral operator K_{pX} approximated from data.  
- Introduces "Fredholm kernels": a data-dependent kernel built from an inner kernel k_H and outer kernel k using l+u data points.  
- Proposes a new "noise assumption": low-variance local directions are uninformative, distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions.  
- Theoretical results: linear Fredholm kernel acts like "soft-thresholding PCA," down-weighting low-variance directions (Theorem 2).  
- Variance-reduction theory: Fredholm kernels give lower-variance estimates of the true clean-data kernel under specific noise models (Theorems 3 and 4).  
- Empirically outperforms standard supervised and SSL methods on synthetic data designed to violate cluster assumptions but satisfy the noise assumption.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on several real-world text and image classification tasks.  
- Strength: original and elegant formulation casting learning as an inverse problem and creating a new class of data-dependent kernels.  
- Strength: the "noise assumption" is a valuable new perspective for when unlabeled data helps.  
- Strength: strong theoretical support providing intuition and formal guarantees for denoising and dimensional weighting.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments, especially synthetic ones tailored to test the noise assumption.  
- Weakness: needs a clearer justification or proof sketch showing the Representer Theorem applies to the nonstandard objective in Eq. (2).  
- Weakness: should better contextualize connections to existing integral-operator and data-dependent kernel methods (e.g., Nyström approximations, other operator-theoretic regularizations).  
- Weakness: recommend contrasting the soft-thresholding mechanism with other data-dependent kernels (e.g., Fisher kernels, probability product kernels).  
- Minor edits: fix typos ("Not that"→"Note that", "achived"→"achieved", "chossing"→"choosing") and remove incorrect "Table ??" reference.  
- Conclusion: solid, novel, and well-supported work with addressable clarifications; reviewer recommends acceptance.
