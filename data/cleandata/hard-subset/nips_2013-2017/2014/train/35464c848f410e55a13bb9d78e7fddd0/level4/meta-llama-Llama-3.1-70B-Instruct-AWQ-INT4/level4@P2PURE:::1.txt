This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, extending the classical supervised learning optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled information and resulting in a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims through theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information, and the resulting classifying function, while differing from the classical representer theorem solution, remains elegant and easy to compute.

I have a few comments: Firstly, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, it is unclear whether this equation still holds when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD), as mentioned by the authors. A proof detailing the derivation of Equation 3 would be beneficial. Secondly, in the experimental section, the authors select an "optimal" parameter for all benchmarked methods, but the process of determining these parameters and the definition of "optimal" are not clearly explained.

Clarity
The paper is well-written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow, with the main ideas adequately explained. However, there are some minor errors, including typos: 
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: an "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
* Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional".
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for..."
* Line 352: the sentence requires rewriting.

Originality
Although the proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), it is novel in essence. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others.

Significance
The results are important and significant, demonstrating a substantial effort to test the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains. Overall, the paper is well-written, with a solid theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section.
