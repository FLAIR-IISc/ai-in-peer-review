This paper introduces a novel and interesting approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning based on data-dependent Fredholm kernels. The method is presented with considerable detail, making for a compelling contribution to the field.

However, I have several suggestions and questions for the authors:

-   **Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to line 99):** The Nystrom method also relies on approximating an integral equation (e.g., in kernel PCA). The authors are encouraged to discuss this connection and clarify the similarities and distinctions between their proposed method and the Nystrom approximation.

-   **Section 2, Eq. 2:** The literature on support vector machines and kernel methods includes various approaches based on operator equations. The discussion would be strengthened by referencing this body of work and positioning Equation 2 within it—for instance, by explaining if it constitutes a special case or how it fundamentally differs.

-   **Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):** The formulation in Equation 2 is less conventional in learning theory. The assumption of an existing representer theorem requires justification. The authors should either provide a proof for a representer theorem in this context or cite a specific theorem from the literature and demonstrate how Equation 2 satisfies its conditions.

-   **Section 4, Eq. 8:** The interpretation of the method as a form of soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. To further contextualize this finding, could the authors elaborate on how their approach differs from related methods like Fisher kernels or probability product kernels?

    -   line 165: "Not that" should likely be "Note that".
    -   line 221: "achived" should be "achieved".
    -   line 341: "chossing" should be "choosing".
    -   line 672: The reference to "Table ??" is incomplete.
