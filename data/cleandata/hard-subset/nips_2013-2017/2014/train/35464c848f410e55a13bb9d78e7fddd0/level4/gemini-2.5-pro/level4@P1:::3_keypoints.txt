- Introduces a novel supervised/semi-supervised framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Constructs new data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that naturally incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Proposes the "noise assumption": low-variance local directions are uninformative and can be treated as noise.  
- Theoretical analysis (linear and Gaussian Fredholm kernels) shows variance reduction under certain noise models.  
- Empirical results: strong performance on synthetic datasets designed for the noise assumption and on various real-world text/image tasks.  
- Originality/significance: novel connection between Fredholm equations and semi-supervised kernel learning; noise assumption complements manifold/cluster assumptions.  
- Technical quality: solid theoretical justification and thorough experiments supporting claims.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and derivations are easy to follow.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter selection: lacks systematic guidelines or sensitivity analysis for choosing inner/outer kernels and their parameters.  
- Weakness — limitations: needs experiments or discussion of failure modes where the noise assumption is violated.  
- Overall conclusion: interesting, well-supported work; recommendation to accept.
