- Presents a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels that extends kernel-based supervised optimization to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- High technical quality with strong theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Introduces a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information; resulting classifier departs from the classical representer theorem but remains elegant and computationally tractable.  
- Question raised about Equation 3: does it hold when the associated kernel is not PSD; a brief derivation or proof sketch is requested.  
- Experimental details unclear: how were the “optimal” parameters chosen (e.g., cross-validation procedure) and needs clarification.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with main ideas adequately explained.  
- Several minor typos/phrasing issues noted (lines 82, 83, section title “Fredhom”, line 247, line 283, line 352) that should be fixed.  
- Originality: related to recent work but sufficiently novel and properly referenced.  
- Significance: important results with extensive testing across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel formulation, clear links to kernel/integral operator theory, and convincing experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Equation 3 derivation needs more clarity, parameter selection details should be specified, and minor editorial fixes needed.
