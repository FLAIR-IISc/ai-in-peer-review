This paper introduces a novel and interesting method for supervised, semi-supervised, and unsupervised learning based on data-dependent Fredholm kernels. The authors discuss many aspects of their approach in detail, and the overall contribution is compelling.

However, I have several suggestions and questions that could strengthen the manuscript:

*   **Relation to the Nystrom Method (Section 2):** The proposed approach is based on approximating an integral equation. The Nystrom method, particularly in the context of kernel PCA, also relies on this principle. The authors should discuss this connection and clarify the similarities and differences between their method and the Nystrom approximation.

*   **Relation to Operator Equations in Kernel Methods (Section 2, Eq. 2):** The use of operator equations, such as the one presented in Equation (2), is not novel within the literature on Support Vector Machines and kernel methods. The authors should situate their work in this context by discussing relevant prior work and clarifying whether Equation (2) can be considered a special case of these existing formulations, or how it fundamentally differs.

*   **Justification for the Representer Theorem (Section 2, line 128):** The formulation in Equation (2) is non-standard in learning theory. The authors currently assume that a representer theorem holds for their objective function without providing a justification. This is a critical point that needs to be addressed. The authors must either include a proof for the representer theorem in this context or provide a precise reference to an existing theorem and demonstrate how their formulation satisfies its conditions.

*   **Comparison to Other Data-Dependent Kernels (Section 4, Eq. 8):** The interpretation of the method as a form of soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. To further contextualize this contribution, how does this approach compare to other data-dependent kernel methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?
