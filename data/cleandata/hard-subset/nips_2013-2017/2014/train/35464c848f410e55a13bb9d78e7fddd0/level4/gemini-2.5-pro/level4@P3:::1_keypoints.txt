- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Defines predictors via an integral operator dependent on the marginal data distribution, enabling natural use of unlabeled data.  
- Introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" built from inner and outer kernels using all labeled and unlabeled points.  
- Formalizes a "noise assumption" stating low-variance local directions are uninformative and can be suppressed using unlabeled data.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing Fredholm kernels act as variance-reduction estimators for linear and Gaussian kernels under a specific data model.  
- Shows the method re-weights/smooths kernel evaluations to better approximate the noise-free true kernel.  
- Presents comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world (text, image) datasets with competitive performance versus strong SSL baselines.  
- High technical quality: elegant formulation, solid theoretical support, and extensive empirical validation.
