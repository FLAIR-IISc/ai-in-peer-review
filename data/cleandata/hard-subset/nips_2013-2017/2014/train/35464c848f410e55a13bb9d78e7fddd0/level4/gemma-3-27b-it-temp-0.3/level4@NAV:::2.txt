The authors introduce a novel method for supervised and semi-supervised learning leveraging Fredholm kernels. The paper provides a detailed discussion of many facets of this approach, and I find the work to be quite compelling overall. 

However, I have several suggestions and questions that could further strengthen the manuscript:

- Regarding section 2 (from equation 1 to the equation on line 99): The Nyström method also relies on approximating an integral equation (as seen in kernel PCA, for example). It would be beneficial to acknowledge this connection and clarify the similarities and differences between the proposed approach and Nyström-based techniques.
- Concerning section 2, equation 2: Operator equation formulations exist within the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. Including a discussion of this related work and explaining how equation 2 relates – either as a special case or through its distinctions – would be valuable.
- In section 2, around line 128, concerning the representer theorem: Equation 2 represents a less conventional formulation in learning theory. As the authors implicitly assume the existence of a representer theorem, either a proof should be included or a precise reference provided, alongside an explanation of how equation 2 fits within the established framework.
- With respect to section 4, equation 8: The interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful. However, a discussion differentiating it from related techniques such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels would be helpful.
- Finally, I noted a few minor typographical errors:
    - line 165: “Not that”
    - line 221: “achived”
    - line 341: “chossing”
    - line 672: “Table ??”
