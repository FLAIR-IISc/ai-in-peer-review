- Introduces a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Proposes data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that naturally incorporate unlabeled data for semi-supervised learning.  
- Introduces a new "noise assumption" distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions, arguing it better motivates use of unlabeled data in some settings.  
- Main contributions: (1) Fredholm-equation-based learning framework combining labeled and unlabeled data.  
- Main contributions: (2) Formalization and theoretical support for the noise assumption showing Fredholm kernels can improve performance under this assumption.  
- Main contributions: (3) Empirical validation on synthetic and multiple real-world datasets demonstrating competitive performance.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong—Theorem 2 shows noise suppression via principal component re-scaling; Theorems 3–4 show better approximation to the true kernel under noise.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, testing cases that violate cluster assumptions and multiple domains (text, sentiment, digits).  
- Concern: Mathematical derivation around Equation 3 is unclear, especially whether the Representer Theorem applies when the kernel is not PSD—needs justification.  
- Concern: Experimental section states "optimal parameters" without describing the selection procedure (cross-validation, grid search, etc.).  
- Clarity issues: The noise assumption needs a clearer, earlier definition and a more explicit explanation of how Fredholm kernels differ from traditional approaches.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos (e.g., "Fredhom", "porportional") and some sections need greater precision; strengthen link between theory and implementation.  
- Originality: The approach is genuinely novel and well-positioned relative to related work (e.g., [9], [10]) by focusing on the noise assumption.  
- Significance: Provides a new principled mechanism for leveraging unlabeled data under different assumptions with promising empirical gains.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept with revisions to clarify Equation 3, parameter selection methodology, tighten definitions, and fix minor typos.
