This paper introduces a novel method for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The approach is well-motivated and presented with a good level of detail, making it a compelling read. Overall, I found the paper to be interesting and potentially significant. However, I have several suggestions and questions that, if addressed, would further strengthen the work.

Regarding Section 2 (from Equation 1 to line 99), the methodology shares conceptual similarities with the Nyström method, which also approximates integral equations – particularly in the context of kernel PCA. It would benefit the paper to acknowledge this connection explicitly and clarify how the proposed approach compares and contrasts with Nyström-based methods. Specifically, discussing the nuances and potential advantages of using Fredholm kernels in this manner would be valuable.

Concerning Equation 2, the formulation utilizes an operator equation, a technique previously explored within the support vector machine and kernel methods literature. It would be helpful to situate this work within that broader context. The authors should either demonstrate how Equation 2 represents a specific case of existing operator equation approaches or clearly articulate the key differences and novel contributions of their formulation.

In Section 2, around line 128, the authors invoke the representer theorem. While the theorem is fundamental, Equation 2 represents a less conventional application. Given its centrality to the theoretical justification, I recommend either providing a proof of the representer theorem within the context of this specific Fredholm kernel formulation *or* supplying a precise citation with a detailed explanation of how Equation 2 aligns with – or extends – the referenced theorem. Justifying the applicability of the theorem is crucial for a rigorous understanding of the method.

Section 4, specifically Equation 8, presents a compelling interpretation of the method as soft-thresholding PCA. This connection is insightful. However, it would be valuable to contrast this interpretation with related techniques like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels. A discussion outlining the differences in their underlying principles and potential advantages/disadvantages would further illuminate the strengths of this approach.

Finally, I noted a few minor typographical errors that should be corrected:

*   Line 165: “Not that” should likely be “Note that.”
*   Line 221: “achived” should be “achieved.”
*   Line 341: “chossing” should be “choosing.”
*   Line 672: “Table ??” needs to be updated with the correct table number. 

The core contribution of this paper is a new approach to supervised and unsupervised learning employing data-dependent Fredholm kernels, and resolving the points mentioned above will substantially enhance its clarity and impact.
