The authors propose a data-dependent kernel called Fredholm kernels, which leverages unlabeled data in a natural way. The novelty of this kernel lies in its ability to effectively utilize unlabeled data, and the authors demonstrate several theoretical properties of the kernel. They also experimentally verify that the kernel compares favorably with existing state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand their contributions.

Detailed comments:

1. The authors may want to consider providing a systematic approach for selecting the kernel width for the Fredholm kernel. For instance, under certain assumptions, can the kernel width be selected using cross-validation? A clear methodology for choosing this hyperparameter would strengthen the paper.
2. It would be beneficial to include some toy experiments that demonstrate scenarios where the proposed method may not perform well. This would provide valuable insights into the settings in which the proposed method is most effective.

Overall, the paper is interesting, and the authors' writing style is very clear. Based on the strengths of the paper, I vote for acceptance.
