This paper proposes a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, leading to a new data-dependent kernel, referred to as the Fredholm kernel. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach through theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.

Quality
The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a clear explanation of the proposed formulation. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the Fredholm kernel, including its properties and advantages over traditional kernels. The experimental results are also convincing, showing the superiority of the proposed approach over other semi-supervised learning methods.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the authors claim that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD), but it is not clear if Equation 3 still holds in this case. A proof or further explanation would be helpful. Additionally, the experimental section could be improved by providing more details on how the optimal parameters were determined for each method.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the main ideas. However, there are a few typos and minor errors that need to be corrected. For example, "will a Euclidean" should be "will be a Euclidean", and "porportional" should be "proportional".

Originality
The proposed approach is novel and distinct from other related work, including a recent NIPS publication. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from others and provide a thorough analysis of the advantages of their approach.

Significance
The results are significant and important, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach on several datasets in different application domains. The authors have made a substantial effort to test their method and provide a comprehensive evaluation.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective approach for semi-supervised learning.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed formulation, including its properties and advantages.
* The experimental results are convincing, showing the superiority of the proposed approach over other semi-supervised learning methods.
