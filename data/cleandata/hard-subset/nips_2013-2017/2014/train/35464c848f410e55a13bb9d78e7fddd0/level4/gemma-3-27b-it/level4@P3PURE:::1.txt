This paper describes a framework particularly well-suited for semi-supervised learning, based on Fredholm kernels. The classical supervised learning optimization problem solved in kernel-based methods is extended to incorporate unlabeled information, leading to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The paper exhibits high technical quality, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation results in a novel, data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled data. The resulting classifying function differs from the classical representer theorem solution, yet remains elegant and computationally efficient. 

I have a few comments. First, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution of the proposed optimization problem: since the authors state that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD), it is unclear whether Equation 3 still holds. A proof outlining the derivation of Equation 3 would be beneficial. Second, concerning the experimental section, the authors select an “optimal” parameter for all methods in the benchmark. The method used to determine these parameters should be clarified, and the precise meaning of “optimal” defined.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow and understand the main ideas. However, several minor typos were identified:

* Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” should be “… will be a Euclidean …”
* Line 83: An “or” is missing.
* Section 4 title: “Fredhom” should be “Fredholm”
* Line 247: “porportional” should be “proportional”
* Line 283: “That is, or…” should be “That is, for…”
* Line 352: The sentence requires rewriting for clarity.

**Originality**

The proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), but is novel in its essence. The paper clearly articulates how this work differs from prior work, including publication [9] and others in the field. 

**Significance**

The results are important and significant. A substantial effort was made to test the proposed method on several datasets across diverse application domains. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical foundation and a compelling experimental validation.
