- Proposes a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that naturally incorporates unlabeled data by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Novel framing of semi-supervised learning as an inverse problem using Fredholm integral equations.  
- Demonstrates theoretical properties, notably variance reduction and analysis under a “noise assumption.”  
- Empirically competitive with state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and situates contributions within relevant literature.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments but lacks explicit negative/failure-case examples.  
- Reviewer asks for a systematic approach to selecting the Fredholm kernel width (e.g., cross-validation under assumptions).  
- Reviewer requests toy experiments showing scenarios where the method fails to clarify limitations and validity of the noise assumption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear novelty, rigorous theoretical analysis, strong empirical validation, clarity, and potential significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential sensitivity to kernel parameter choices and insufficient demonstration of failure modes.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper with minor limitations; reviewer recommends acceptance.
