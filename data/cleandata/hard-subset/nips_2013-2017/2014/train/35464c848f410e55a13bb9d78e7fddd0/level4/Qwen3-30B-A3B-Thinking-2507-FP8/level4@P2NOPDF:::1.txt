

Quality  
The paper presents a technically sound framework for semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels, extending classical kernel-based optimization to integrate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral equation. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experimental results convincingly demonstrate the method's efficacy. The proposed formulation yields a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information, and the resulting classifier, while deviating from the standard representer theorem solution, remains elegant and computationally efficient.  

I have a few minor concerns:  
1. **Equation 3**: The authors note the kernel need not be PSD. Does Equation 3 remain valid in this case? A brief derivation or justification for this step would strengthen the theoretical foundation.  
2. **Experimental parameters**: The benchmark reports "optimal" parameters for all methods. How were these parameters determined? What criteria defined "optimal" (e.g., cross-validation on labeled data, grid search)? Clarifying this would improve reproducibility.  

Clarity  
The paper is well-written and logically structured, with clear exposition of core ideas. However, minor typographical errors require correction:  
- Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." → "... will be a Euclidean ..."  
- Line 83: Missing "or" (e.g., "A or B")  
- Section 4 title: "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"  
- Line 247: "porportional" → "proportional"  
- Line 283: "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."  
- Line 352: Rewrite sentence for clarity (e.g., "The optimization problem..." instead of current phrasing).  

Originality  
The approach builds on related work (e.g., NIPS [9]) but introduces a distinct formulation. The authors clearly articulate differences from prior literature, establishing novelty in the core mechanism.  

Significance  
The results are significant, with substantial effort devoted to evaluating the method across diverse datasets and application domains. The semi-supervised framework addresses a meaningful gap in kernel-based learning, and the empirical validation supports its practical utility.  

Overall, this is a strong submission with a solid theoretical foundation, compelling experiments, and clear presentation. Addressing the minor points above would further enhance the paper's impact. The work represents a valuable contribution to semi-supervised learning research.
