- Presents a novel semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels, leading to a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- High technical quality: theoretical analysis supports claims and experiments are convincing.  
- Introduces a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Resulting classifier departs from the classical representer theorem but remains elegant and computationally simple.  
- Concern: Equation (3)’s validity when the associated kernel is not PSD is unclear; a derivation or proof is needed.  
- Concern: Experimental selection of an "optimal" parameter for benchmarked methods is not clearly described or defined.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and organized.  
- Minor corrections: Line 82 "will a Euclidean" → "will be a Euclidean"; Line 83 missing "or"; Section 4 title "Fredhom" → "Fredholm"; Line 247 "porportional" → "proportional"; Line 283 "That is, or..." → "That is, for..."; Line 352 sentence needs rewriting for clarity.  
- Originality: Novel contribution despite similarities to a recent NIPS paper ([9]); authors clearly explain differences.  
- Significance: Results are impactful and thoroughly tested across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Overall: Well-written paper with strong theory and convincing experiments.
