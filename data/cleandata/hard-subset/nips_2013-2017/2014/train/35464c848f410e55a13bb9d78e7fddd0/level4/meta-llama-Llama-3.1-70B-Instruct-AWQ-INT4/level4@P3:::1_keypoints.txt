- Proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework via a regularized Fredholm integral equation, introducing Fredholm kernels.  
- Technically sound with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental validation; produces a new data-dependent kernel incorporating unlabeled data.  
- Classifier differs from classical representer-theorem solutions but is elegant and computationally simple.  
- Reviewer requests a derivation/proof of Equation 3, noting the associated kernel need not be PSD.  
- Authors should clarify how “optimal” parameters were selected for benchmark methods in experiments.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized but contains minor typos and some sentences that require rephrasing (several examples cited).  
- Approach is novel relative to a recent NIPS paper and differences from prior work are well explained.  
- Results are significant and demonstrate effectiveness and noise robustness across multiple datasets and domains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound framework; strong experimental results; clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing detailed proof for Eq. 3, unclear baseline parameter selection, and minor language/typo issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the listed issues.
