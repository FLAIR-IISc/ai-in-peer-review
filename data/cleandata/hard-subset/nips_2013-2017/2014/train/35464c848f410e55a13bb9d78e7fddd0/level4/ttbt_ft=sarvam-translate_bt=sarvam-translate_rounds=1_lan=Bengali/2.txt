The authors have presented an interesting new approach for supervised and semi-supervised learning using the Fredholm kernel. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail. Overall, I find the article very interesting.

However, I have some suggestions and questions:

- Section 2 (from equation 1 to the equation in line 99):
Also, the NISTROM method considers the approximation of an integral equation (in that case, kernel PCA). I think the authors should mention this and explain in what sense this method is similar or different.
- Section 2 Equation 2:
Various methods, including the operator equation, have been presented in the literature in the context of the support vector machine and the kernel method. It would be good to mention this work and explain how equation 2 is a specific example of it (or how it differs).
- Section 2 Line 128 Representative Theorem:
Equation 2 is less common in learning theory. The authors assume that a representative theorem exists. They should either prove a representative theorem here or otherwise provide a specific reference and explain how equation 2 is a specific example of it.
- Section 4 Equation 8:
Its explanation as a soft-thresholding PCA is beautiful and interesting.
How does it differ from other methods such as the Fisher kernel and the probability product kernel?
