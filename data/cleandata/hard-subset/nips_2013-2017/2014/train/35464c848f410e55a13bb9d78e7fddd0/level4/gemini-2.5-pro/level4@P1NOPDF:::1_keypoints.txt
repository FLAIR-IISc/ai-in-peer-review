- Introduces a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, yielding a discretized Fredholm integral formulation.  
- High technical quality: theoretical analysis and experiments convincingly support the claims.  
- Proposes a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled data; resulting classifier differs from classical Representer Theorem but is simple to compute.  
- Major concern: Equation 3—authors claim the kernel need not be PSD; a derivation/proof that Eq3 holds in the non-PSD case is required.  
- Experimental concern: unclear what "optimal" parameter selection means—need clarification on whether parameters were tuned on held-out validation sets or taken from literature.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and well organized.  
- Minor typos and phrasing fixes needed (Line 82, Line 83, Section 4 title "Fredhom" → "Fredholm", Line 247, Line 283, Line 352).  
- Originality: approach is novel and appropriately differentiated from recent related work.  
- Significance: results are significant and validated across multiple datasets and domains.  
- Overall: well-written paper with solid theoretical foundation and convincing experiments.
