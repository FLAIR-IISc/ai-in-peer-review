This paper introduces a novel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning by reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. This approach leads to the construction of new, data-dependent kernels, which the authors term "Fredholm kernels." The method is motivated by a "noise assumption," which posits that low-variance directions in the unlabeled data distribution are uninformative for the learning task. The authors provide a theoretical analysis suggesting that Fredholm kernels can effectively suppress such noise, including an insightful interpretation of a linear Fredholm kernel as a form of "soft-thresholding" PCA. The paper supports its claims with experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating competitive performance.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and well-motivated contribution. The proposed method is elegant, and the discussion is detailed. I have a few suggestions and questions that could further strengthen the manuscript:

*   **Relation to the Nystrom Method:** The approximation of the integral operator in Section 2 is reminiscent of the Nystrom method, which is also used to approximate integral equations (for example, in the context of kernel PCA). It would be beneficial for the authors to discuss the relationship between their approach and the Nystrom method, clarifying both the similarities and key differences.

*   **Context within Operator-Theoretic Methods:** The use of operator equations in the optimization problem (Eq. 2) has precedents in the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. The paper would be strengthened by positioning this work within that broader context. The authors should explain how their formulation differs from, or is a special case of, prior operator-theoretic approaches.

*   **Justification for the Representer Theorem:** The authors derive their solution (Eq. 3) by invoking the Representer Theorem on the optimization problem in Eq. 2. However, given that this formulation is non-standard, the direct application of the standard theorem is not obvious. The authors should either provide a proof (or a sketch) for a representer theorem applicable to their specific problem or cite a more general version of the theorem and explicitly demonstrate how their formulation satisfies its conditions.

*   **Comparison with other Data-Dependent Kernels:** The interpretation of the linear Fredholm kernel as a form of soft-thresholding PCA is a valuable insight. To further contextualize this contribution, it would be helpful to discuss how this method compares to other techniques that create data-dependent kernels, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels, which also incorporate information from the data distribution.
