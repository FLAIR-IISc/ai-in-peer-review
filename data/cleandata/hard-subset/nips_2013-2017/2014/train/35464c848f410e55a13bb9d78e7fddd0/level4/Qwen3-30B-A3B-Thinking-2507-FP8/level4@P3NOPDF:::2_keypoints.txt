- Novel framework: learning formulated as solving a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, offering a fresh, theoretically grounded perspective.  
- Paper is well-structured and makes a significant contribution to kernel-based learning theory.  
- Insightful connection to soft-thresholding PCA (Section 4, Eq. 8).  
- Compelling semi-supervised extension via graph-based regularization (Section 4.2).  
- Empirical results on standard benchmarks demonstrate practical viability.  
- Major concern: must compare explicitly to Nyström kernel approximation—clarify differences in assumptions, error bounds, and computational trade-offs.  
- Major concern: clarify whether Eq. 2 is a special case of existing operator-theoretic SVM/kernel formulations or a fundamentally different paradigm, and why that matters.  
- Major concern: missing representer theorem for Eq. 2—either prove a new representer theorem or cite a precise existing result; standard representer theorems may not apply.  
- Major concern: lack of comparison of the soft-thresholding PCA interpretation to related kernel methods (e.g., Fisher kernels, probability product kernels) and articulation of unique advantages.  
- Minor issue: typos to fix (Line 165, 221, 341, and replace "Table ??" with correct reference).  
- Minor issue: clarify terminology—“Fredholm kernels” is misleading; Fredholm refers to the integral equation, not the kernel.  
- Minor issue: support and contextualize the novelty claim regarding “Fredholm kernels” and distinguish from prior work on Fredholm operators in ML.  
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially impactful but requires stronger theoretical grounding and clearer contextualization.  
- Recommendation: Major revision.
