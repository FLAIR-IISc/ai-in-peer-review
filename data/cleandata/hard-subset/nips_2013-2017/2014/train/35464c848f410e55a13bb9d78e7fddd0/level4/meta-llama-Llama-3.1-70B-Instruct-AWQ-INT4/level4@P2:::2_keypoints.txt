- Proposes a novel learning framework reformulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Framework naturally incorporates unlabeled data and is similar to kernel methods but with distinct properties.  
- Emphasizes the "noise assumption" in semi-supervised learning and argues Fredholm kernels help denoise classifiers.  
- Provides theoretical evidence that Fredholm kernels can improve classifier performance under the noise assumption.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic examples and multiple real-world datasets.  
- Main contributions: Fredholm equation framework, theoretical analysis of the noise assumption, and empirical validation.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of method and theoretical background.  
- Experimental results are convincing and support the proposed method’s effectiveness.  
- Suggests comparing and contrasting with the Nystrom method, which also approximates integral equations.  
- Requests discussion of operator-equation work in SVMs/kernel methods and how equation (2) relates or differs.  
- Asks for a proof or precise reference for the assumed representer theorem for equation (2).  
- Recommends discussing how the Fredholm kernel interpretation (soft-thresholding PCA) differs from Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.  
- Notes several typographical errors that should be corrected (e.g., "Not that", "achived", "chossing", "Table ??").  
- Strengths: novel approach, clear exposition, and convincing experiments.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparisons with related methods, unproven/uncited representer theorem, and limited discussion of relationships to other kernels.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address requested comparisons, theorem citation/proof, and clarification of relationships and typos.
