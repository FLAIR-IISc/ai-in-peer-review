- Proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels that yields a discretized Fredholm integral equation and a novel data-dependent kernel incorporating unlabeled data.  
- High technical quality with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experiments; classification function is elegant and computationally simple despite differing from the classical representer-theorem form.  
- Question about Equation 3: unclear if it holds when the associated kernel is not PSD; a derivation/proof of Equation 3 is needed.  
- Experimental concern: authors claim an "optimal" parameter for all methods but do not explain how parameters were selected or what "optimal" means.  
- Clarity is generally good and well-organized, but several typos must be fixed (e.g., "will a Euclidean" → "will be a Euclidean", missing "or", "Fredhom" → "Fredholm", "porportional" → "proportional", "That is, or..." → "That is, for...", and another sentence at Line 352 needs rewriting).  
- Originality: approach is novel and clearly differentiated from a related recent NIPS paper ([9]).  
- Significance: results are important and well-validated across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with a strong theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.
