The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, and overall I find the paper compelling.

I do, however, have several suggestions and questions:

- Section 2 (from Eq. (1) to the equation on line 99):
The Nyström method also approximates an integral equation (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be helpful to acknowledge this connection and explain in what sense your approach is similar to, or different from, Nyström approximations—both conceptually (integral-operator viewpoint) and practically (approximation quality, computational cost, and implications for generalization).

- Section 2, Eq. (2):
Within SVMs and kernel methods, related formulations involving operator equations have appeared. Please cite this line of work and clarify the relationship. Is Eq. (2) a special case of an existing operator-equation framework, or does it introduce a substantive difference (e.g., in the choice of operator, regularization, or data-dependence)? An explicit mapping or counterexample would make the distinction clear.

- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):
Because Eq. (2) is less common in learning theory, the assumed representer theorem needs justification. Please either provide a proof tailored to your setting (including assumptions on the Fredholm operator, kernel, and regularizer) or give a precise reference to an existing theorem and explain exactly how Eq. (2) falls under its scope. In particular, specify the functional space, regularization functional, and conditions ensuring that solutions admit finite expansions.

- Section 4, Eq. (8):
The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is elegant and interesting. How does this compare to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels? Please discuss similarities and differences in:
  - The induced feature maps and metrics,
  - The role of data-dependent operators,
  - Statistical efficiency and robustness,
  - Computational requirements,
  - Empirical performance if available.

- Typos and minor issues:
  - Line 165: “Not that” → likely “Note that.”
  - Line 221: “achived” → “achieved.”
  - Line 341: “chossing” → “choosing.”
  - Line 672: “Table ??” → please provide the correct table reference.

In summary, this is a promising new approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels. Clarifying the connections to Nyström approximations, operator-equation formulations in kernel methods, and the representer theorem, as well as positioning the soft-thresholding PCA view relative to Fisher and probability product kernels, would significantly strengthen the paper.
