- Paper proposes a semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm kernels, yielding a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- High technical quality with thorough theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Introduces a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information.  
- Classifying function differs from classical representer-theorem solutions but remains elegant and computationally simple.  
- Concern: Equation 3’s validity is unclear when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD); a derivation/proof of Equation 3 is requested.  
- Concern: Experimental section uses an "optimal" parameter for benchmarks without explaining how "optimal" was determined or selected.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized.  
- Several minor typographical errors and wording issues need correction (lines 82, 83, Section 4 title, 247, 283, 352).  
- Approach is novel despite relation to a recent NIPS paper; differences from prior work are clearly explained.  
- Results are significant and well-validated across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with solid theory and convincing experiments.
