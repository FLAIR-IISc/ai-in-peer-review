This paper presents a novel framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, extending the classical supervised learning optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled information. This extension leads to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality:
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information. Although the resulting classifying function deviates from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains elegant and easy to compute.

I have a few comments that warrant further clarification. Firstly, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, it is unclear whether this equation remains valid when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD). The authors mention that the kernel need not be PSD, but it would be beneficial to provide a proof or explanation for how Equation 3 was derived. Secondly, in the experimental section, the authors select an "optimal" parameter for all benchmarked methods. However, the process for determining these parameters and the definition of "optimal" in this context are not clearly explained.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the main ideas. However, there are some minor errors and areas for improvement. Specifically, the following corrections are suggested:
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: An "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be corrected to "Fredholm".
* Line 247: "porportional" should be spelled "proportional".
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be rephrased as "That is, for...".
* Line 352: The sentence requires rewriting for improved clarity.

Originality:
Although the proposed approach shares similarities with a recent NIPS publication ([9]), it presents a novel contribution. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others.

Significance:
The results are significant and impactful, demonstrating a substantial effort to test the proposed method on multiple datasets across various application domains.

Overall, the paper is well-written, with a strong theoretical foundation and a convincing experimental section.
