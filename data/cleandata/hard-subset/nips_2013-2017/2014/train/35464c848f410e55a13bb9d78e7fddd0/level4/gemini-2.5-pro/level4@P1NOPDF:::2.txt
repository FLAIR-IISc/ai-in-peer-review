The authors present a compelling new approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels. The method is presented in detail, and the paper is well-written and interesting.

While the overall contribution is valuable, I have a few suggestions and questions that could help strengthen the paper's exposition and theoretical grounding.

*   **Section 2 (Relationship to the Nyström Method):** The paper's formulation, based on an integral equation (from Eq. 1 to the equation on line 99), bears a resemblance to the Nyström method. The Nyström method also approximates an integral equation, most notably in the context of kernel PCA. It would be beneficial for the authors to acknowledge this connection and discuss the similarities and differences between their proposed framework and the Nyström approximation.

*   **Section 2 (Context within Operator-based Kernel Methods):** The use of operator equations (Eq. 2) has precedents in the literature on kernel methods, particularly in the development of Support Vector Machines. The authors should position their work relative to this existing body of literature. Specifically, it would be helpful to clarify whether the formulation in Equation 2 is a special case of these prior operator-based approaches or how it fundamentally differs.

*   **Section 2 (Representer Theorem, line 128):** The formulation in Equation 2 is non-standard, and the paper's solution seems to implicitly assume that a representer theorem holds for this setting. For the theoretical grounding of the method to be complete, this assumption must be formally justified. The authors should either provide a proof of the representer theorem for their specific formulation or provide a precise reference to a more general theorem and explicitly demonstrate how Equation 2 satisfies its conditions.

*   **Section 4 (Comparison to other Data-Dependent Kernels):** The interpretation of the method as a form of soft-thresholding PCA (Eq. 8) is insightful and interesting. To better contextualize this contribution, could the authors elaborate on how this approach differs from other well-known methods for constructing data-dependent kernels, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?

*   line 165: "Not that" should likely be "Note that".

*   line 341: "chossing" should be "choosing".
