- Introduces a novel framework casting supervised/semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding “Fredholm kernels.”
- Provides a principled way to incorporate unlabeled data via approximation of an integral operator, producing data-dependent kernels.
- Offers solid theoretical analysis, especially regarding a “noise assumption” that justifies improved performance.
- Presents comprehensive empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets showing competitive semi-supervised performance.
- Paper is generally well-written and explains complex ideas clearly.
- Lacks sufficient contextualization with related work; needs deeper comparison to Nyström methods and kernel PCA.
- Should relate Eqn. 2 and the formulation to existing operator-equation approaches in SVM and kernel methods with appropriate references.
- Application of the representer theorem is weakly justified; requires a proof or clear authoritative reference.
- Section 4’s view of Fredholm kernel as soft-thresholding PCA is interesting but needs comparison to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.
- Minor typos and presentation issues (multiple line-specific errors and a missing table reference) need fixing.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of Fredholm kernel, theoretical soundness, strong empirical results, and overall clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited literature discussion, insufficient representer theorem justification, missing comparisons to related techniques, and minor typos.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — expand related work, justify representer theorem, contrast with existing dimensionality-reduction/feature-selection kernels, and correct typos.
