This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, extending the classical supervised learning optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled information and resulting in a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper demonstrates high technical quality, with well-supported claims through theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation yields a novel, data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. Although the classifying function differs from the classical representer theorem solution, it remains elegant and easy to compute.

I have a few comments. Firstly, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, it is unclear whether this equation still holds when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD), as mentioned by the authors. A proof detailing how Equation 3 was obtained would have been a welcome addition. Secondly, in the experimental section, the authors selected an "optimal" parameter for all benchmarked methods. It would be beneficial to elaborate on how these parameters were determined and what is meant by "optimal" in this context.

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow and understand the main ideas. However, there are some typos that need correction:
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: An "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm"
* Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional"
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for..."
* Line 352: The sentence requires rewriting.

Originality
Although the proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), it is novel in essence. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others.

Significance
The results are important and significant, demonstrating a substantial effort to test the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a good theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.
