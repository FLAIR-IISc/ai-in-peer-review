A framework specifically adapted for semi-theoretical learning based on the Fredholm kernel is described. The classic supervisory learning optimization problem, solved using kernel-based methods, is extended to include unlabeled data, resulting in a discrete version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper has a high technical standard, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and reliable experimental results. The proposed formula leads to a new data-dependent kernel that includes unlabeled data. The classification function is different from the classical representation theorem solution, but it is still refined and easy to compute.

I have a few comments: one related to equation 3, where the proposed optimization problem solution is described. Since the authors mention that the associated kernel does not need to be PSD, is equation 3 still applicable in this case? It would be good to see a proof of how equation 3 arose. Another comment is about the experimental section, where the authors have chosen a "best" parameter for all the methods in the benchmark. How were those parameters determined? What is meant by "best"?

Clarity
The paper is written and organized clearly for the most part. It is easy to follow and the main concepts are adequately explained. Although there are some typos, for example:
Line 82: "... a Euclidean will be ..." -> ... a Euclidean will be ...
Line 83: "or" is missing.
Paragraph 4 title: "Fredholm" -> Fredholm
Line 247: "asymmetric" -> "symmetric"
Line 283: "i.e., or ..." -> i.e., for ...
Line 352: The sentence needs to be rewritten.

Originality
The proposed method is related to a recently published NIPS publication ([9]), but it is fundamentally new. It is clear how this work differs from this publication and other publications.

Significance
The results are important and significant. A substantial effort was made to test the proposed method on several datasets in various application domains.

A well-written paper with a good theoretical foundation and reliable experimental section.
