- Proposes a supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding data-dependent "Fredholm kernels."  
- Fits naturally into the kernel framework and can be interpreted as constructing new kernels adapted to the data.  
- Provides theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels can effectively leverage unlabeled data under the "noise assumption."  
- Shows competitive performance of Fredholm-based methods in standard semi-supervised settings on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Connects the approach to prior kernel and semi-supervised work, situating it within existing literature.  
- Discusses the "noise assumption" and its relation to cluster and manifold assumptions, with results showing suppression of non-informative data variations.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and offers clear, detailed explanations of the framework and results.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach that balances theoretical analysis and empirical validation.  
- Strength: thorough relation to prior research, clarifying the method’s place in the field.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of the method’s limitations and scenarios where it may fail.  
- Weakness: some theoretical results (e.g., proof of Proposition 1) are omitted from the paper and deferred to the full version.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—strong contribution with a recommendation for acceptance despite noted weaknesses.
