This paper describes a framework particularly useful for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The classical supervised learning optimization problem solved in kernel-based methods is extended to incorporate unlabeled information, leading to a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

**Quality**

The paper has high technical quality, with well-supported claims through theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The resulting classifying function differs from the classical representer theorem solution, but remains elegant and computationally tractable. 

I have a few comments: First, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem. Since the authors mention that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD), does Equation 3 still hold in this case? A brief explanation or proof sketch of how Equation 3 is derived would be beneficial. Second, concerning the experimental section, the authors choose an “optimal” parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were these parameters determined? Clarifying the definition of “optimal” – e.g., cross-validation details – would strengthen the evaluation.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part. It is easy to follow, and the main ideas are explained adequately. However, there are some minor typos:

* Line 82: “…will a Euclidean…” should be “…will be a Euclidean…”
* Line 83: The word “or” is missing.
* Section 4 title: “Fredhom” should be “Fredholm”.
* Line 247: “porportional” should be “proportional”.
* Line 283: “That is, or…” should be “That is, for…”
* Line 352: This sentence is somewhat awkward and could be rewritten for clarity.

**Originality**

The proposed approach is related to recent work, including a NIPS publication [9], but is novel in its essence. It is clear how this work differs from existing contributions and adequately references related work.

**Significance**

The results are important and significant. The authors have put considerable effort into testing the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation and novel formulation of semi-supervised learning.
*   Clear connection to existing kernel methods and integral operator theory.
*   Convincing experimental results across a variety of datasets.
*   Addresses a relevant problem in semi-supervised learning and provides a potentially effective solution.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The derivation of Equation 3 could be more explicitly explained.
*   The details of parameter selection in the experiments could be clarified.
*   Minor typos and grammatical issues detract slightly from the overall polish of the paper.
