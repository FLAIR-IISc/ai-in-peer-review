The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels, which are data-dependent. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, making the paper very engaging.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions to further improve the manuscript:

- In Section 2 (from equation 1 to the equation on line 99), the authors could benefit from drawing a connection to the Nystrom method, which also considers an approximation to an integral equation (specifically, kernel PCA). A discussion on the similarities and differences between the two approaches would be enlightening.

- Equation 2 in Section 2 bears resemblance to operator equations found in the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. The authors should acknowledge this existing work and clarify how their equation 2 relates to it, either as a special case or as a distinct approach.

- On line 128 of Section 2, the authors invoke the representer theorem, which is less common in learning theory. To strengthen their argument, they should either provide a proof of the representer theorem in this context or offer a precise reference and explain how equation 2 is a special case of it.

- The interpretation of equation 8 in Section 4 as soft-thresholding PCA is a valuable contribution. To further contextualize this result, the authors should discuss how it differs from other methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

Additionally, I have identified a few typos that should be corrected:
- Line 165: "Not that" should be replaced with "Note that."
- Line 221: "achived" should be spelled "achieved."
- Line 341: "chossing" should be spelled "choosing."
- Line 672: The table reference appears to be incomplete and should be revised.

Overall, the paper introduces a novel approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using Fredholm kernels, which are data-dependent. With some revisions to address the above points, the manuscript has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
