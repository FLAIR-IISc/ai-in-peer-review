- Introduces a novel framework framing learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and defining “Fredholm kernels.”  
- Provides solid theoretical foundations, including analysis under a “noise assumption” and related results.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-written and explains core concepts accessibly.  
- Lacks explicit discussion of the connection to the Nyström method, which also approximates integral equations.  
- Equation 2 resembles operator-equation formulations used in SVM/kernel literature and needs contextual comparison to prior work.  
- The Representer Theorem’s applicability to Equation 2 is not clearly justified—requires a proof or precise reference and explanation.  
- Section 4’s interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful but should be contrasted with other dimensionality/feature methods (e.g., Fisher kernels, probability product kernels).  
- Needs a more thorough related-work section to position the approach relative to existing techniques.  
- Comparison to established methods (feature selection/dimensionality reduction and kernel approaches) should be expanded.  
- Minor typographical errors to fix: line 165 “Not that,” line 221 “achived,” line 341 “chossing,” and line 672 “Table ??.”  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with promising experiments and practical potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of related work and incomplete theoretical justification in places.  
- Overall recommendation: promising paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address context and theoretical clarifications.
