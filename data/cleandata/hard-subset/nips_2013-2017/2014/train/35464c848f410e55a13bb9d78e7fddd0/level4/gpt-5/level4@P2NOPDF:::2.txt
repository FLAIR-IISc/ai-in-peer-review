This paper introduces a new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on data-dependent Fredholm kernels. The approach is well motivated and many aspects are described in commendable detail. Overall, I found the paper interesting and promising.

That said, I have several suggestions and questions:

- Section 2 (from Eq. (1) to the equation on line 99):
Please discuss the relationship to Nyström methods, which also approximate integral equations (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be helpful to articulate in what sense your formulation is similar to, or departs from, standard Nyström approximations—both conceptually and in terms of approximation error and computational cost.

- Section 2, Eq. (2):
Operator-equation formulations also appear in the SVM and broader kernel methods literature (e.g., learning with integral operators or operator-valued regularization). Please cite the most relevant prior work and clarify whether Eq. (2) can be viewed as a special case of these frameworks, or explain the key differences (assumptions, objectives, or regularizers) that set your approach apart.

- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):
Because Eq. (2) is less standard in learning theory, the manuscript assumes a representer theorem without stating it formally. Please either provide a proof tailored to your setting or give a precise citation to a theorem that applies here. In either case, explain the assumptions under which the representer property holds and how Eq. (2) satisfies them.

- Section 4, Eq. (8):
The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is appealing. Could you elaborate on how this construction differs from Fisher kernels and probability product kernels? In particular, please compare the objective, the nature of feature/embedding construction, and any implications for statistical efficiency and computational complexity.

- Typos and minor issues:
  - Line 165: “Not that” → “Note that”
  - Line 221: “achived” → “achieved”
  - Line 341: “chossing” → “choosing”
  - Line 672: “Table ??” → provide the correct table reference/number

In summary, the paper proposes an interesting data-dependent Fredholm kernel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning, with a compelling interpretation and potentially broad applicability. Clarifying the connections to Nyström approximations and operator-based formulations, and providing a rigorous treatment (or precise citation) of the representer theorem, would significantly strengthen the contribution.
