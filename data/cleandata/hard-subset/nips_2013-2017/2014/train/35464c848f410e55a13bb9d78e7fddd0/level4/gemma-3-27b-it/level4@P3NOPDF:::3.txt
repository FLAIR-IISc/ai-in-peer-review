## Review of NIPS 2014 Submission: Fredholm Kernels

This paper introduces Fredholm kernels, a novel data-dependent kernel method that effectively leverages unlabeled data. The key strength of this approach lies in its natural incorporation of the unlabeled dataset into the kernel construction. The authors support their proposal with compelling theoretical analysis, demonstrating several desirable properties of the kernel, and substantiate these findings with experimental results showing performance competitive with, and often exceeding, state-of-the-art methods. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ line of reasoning and understand the technical details. Overall, this is a strong contribution with significant potential impact. I recommend acceptance.

**Detailed Comments:**

1. **Kernel Width Selection:** A practical concern for any kernel method is the selection of appropriate hyperparameters, in this case, the kernel width. The paper would benefit from a discussion of strategies for selecting this parameter. While a grid search or cross-validation approach is likely applicable, exploring whether there are theoretical justifications – perhaps under specific assumptions about the data distribution – for a particular width selection rule would strengthen the contribution. Providing guidance on parameter tuning is crucial for practical adoption of the proposed kernel.

2. **Limitations and Failure Modes:** While the experimental results are promising, it would be valuable to understand the scenarios where the Fredholm kernel might *not* perform well. Including “toy experiments” or analysis demonstrating such cases would provide a more complete picture of the method’s strengths and weaknesses. Specifically, it would be helpful to identify the types of datasets or problem settings where the benefits of incorporating unlabeled data are minimized, or where the assumptions underlying the kernel’s theoretical properties are violated. Knowing in which settings the proposed method is most effective, and conversely, where it might struggle, will greatly enhance the paper’s impact and inform future research directions.
