This paper introduces a framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning by reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. This approach naturally extends the standard kernel methods paradigm by constructing new, data-dependent kernels—termed Fredholm kernels—which effectively incorporate information from unlabeled data. The authors motivate this framework with a "noise assumption," providing theoretical and empirical evidence that their method can leverage unlabeled data to denoise classifiers and improve generalization.

**Quality**
The paper is technically sound, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The central idea of deriving a new data-dependent kernel from the Fredholm formulation is both elegant and powerful. The resulting solution for the predictive function is clearly presented and computationally tractable.

A few points could be clarified to strengthen the paper:
1.  Regarding Equation 3, the authors mention that the "outer" kernel `k` need not be positive semi-definite. It would be helpful to confirm if the solution, which involves matrix inversion, remains valid under this general condition.
2.  The derivation of the solution in Equation 3 from the optimization problem in Equation 2 is central to the method. For completeness, including this derivation (perhaps in an appendix) would be beneficial for the reader.
3.  The experimental section states that "optimal" parameters were chosen for all methods. Could the authors specify how these parameters were determined (e.g., via cross-validation on a held-out set) and what criterion defined optimality?

**Clarity**
The paper is well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the main ideas. The exposition of the framework and its motivation is particularly clear. A final proofreading would be beneficial to correct a few minor typographical errors:
*   Line 82: "will a Euclidean" -> "will be a Euclidean"
*   Line 83: The phrase seems incomplete; perhaps "the label set Y is either {-1, 1} for binary classification or the real line R for regression."
*   Section 4 title: "Fredhom" -> "Fredholm"
*   Line 247: "porportional" -> "proportional"
*   Line 283: "That is, or..." -> "That is, for..."
*   Line 352: This sentence could be rephrased for better flow.

**Originality**
The proposed approach is novel and makes a distinct contribution to the literature. While Fredholm equations have been explored in machine learning for related problems like density ratio estimation [10], this paper's formulation as a general framework for semi-supervised learning is new. The work is clearly distinguished from previous contributions on data-dependent kernels and manifold regularization, and related work is adequately referenced.

**Significance**
The results are important and likely to have a significant impact. The formalization of the "noise assumption" offers a valuable new perspective for designing semi-supervised learning algorithms. The comprehensive experimental evaluation across several synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates the method's practical effectiveness and potential for broad applicability. Researchers and practitioners are likely to find these ideas useful and may build upon them in future work.

In summary, this is a well-written paper with a strong theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section that presents a valuable contribution to semi-supervised learning.
