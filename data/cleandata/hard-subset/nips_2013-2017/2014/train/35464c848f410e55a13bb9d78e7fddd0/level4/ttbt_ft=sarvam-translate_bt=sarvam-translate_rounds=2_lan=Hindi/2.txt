The authors, Fredholm and Kern, present an interesting new approach to supervised and semi-supervised learning using kernels. Several aspects of the method are discussed in detail. Overall, I found this paper very interesting.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions:

- Section 2 (Equation 1 to Equation 99):
Furthermore, the NISTROM method considers the approximation of an integral equation (in that case, the kernel PCA). I think it would be good if the authors could mention this and also explain how this approach is similar or different.
- Section 2 Equation 2:
Approaches with operator equations have also been presented in the literature in the field of vector machines and kernel methods. It would be good to mention this work and explain how Equation 2 is a special case of it (or how it differs).
- Section 2 Line 128 Representative Theorem:
Equation 2 is less common in learning theory. The authors believe that a representative theorem exists. They should prove a representative theorem here or otherwise provide an accurate reference and explain how Equation 2 is a special case of it.
- Section 4 Equation 8:
The explanation as a soft-bound PCA is good and interesting.
How does it differ from other methods such as the Fisher kernel and the probability product kernel?
