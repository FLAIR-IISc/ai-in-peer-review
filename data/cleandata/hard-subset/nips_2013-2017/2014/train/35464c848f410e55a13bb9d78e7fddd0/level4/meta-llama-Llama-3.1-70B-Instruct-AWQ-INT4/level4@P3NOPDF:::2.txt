The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, making the paper engaging and thought-provoking. Overall, I find the paper very interesting and appreciate the authors' efforts in exploring this novel approach.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions to further enhance the paper's clarity and impact:

In Section 2, the authors introduce their method, which bears some resemblance to the Nystrom approximation technique used in kernel PCA. It would be beneficial to explicitly mention this connection and explain in what sense their approach is similar or different. This comparison would provide readers with a deeper understanding of the proposed method's novelty and advantages.

Furthermore, the use of operator equations in Equation 2 is reminiscent of approaches found in the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. The authors should mention this related work and clarify how Equation 2 is a special case of these existing methods or how it differs from them. This would help readers appreciate the unique contributions of the paper.

The authors assume the existence of a representer theorem for Equation 2, which is less common in learning theory. To strengthen the paper, they should either provide a proof of the representer theorem or offer a precise reference and explain how Equation 2 is a special case of it. This would ensure that the theoretical foundations of the method are solid and well-established.

In Section 4, the interpretation of the proposed method as soft-thresholding PCA is a valuable contribution. To further contextualize this result, the authors should discuss how it differs from other methods, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels. This comparison would help readers understand the advantages and limitations of the proposed approach.

Finally, I noticed a few typos that should be corrected: "Not that" (line 165) should be "Note that," "achived" (line 221) should be "achieved," and "chossing" (line 341) should be "choosing." Additionally, the table reference on line 672 should be corrected to provide a clear citation.

In conclusion, the authors have proposed a novel approach to supervised and unsupervised learning using data-dependent Fredholm kernels. With some revisions to address the mentioned suggestions and questions, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
