This paper presents a semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels. It extends the standard kernel-based supervised optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper is technically strong, with claims supported by rigorous analysis and convincing experiments. The formulation yields a new data-dependent kernel that leverages unlabeled information. The resulting classifier departs from the classical representer-theorem form yet remains elegant and computationally efficient.

Comments:
- Equation (3): Since the associated kernel need not be PSD, does Equation (3) still hold in the indefinite case? Please provide a derivation or proof of Equation (3), and clarify conditions for existence/uniqueness and stability when the kernel is not PSD.
- Experimental protocol: The paper states that an “optimal” parameter was chosen for all benchmark methods. How were these parameters selected (e.g., cross-validation, validation split, grid search)? Over which ranges, and was the same search budget used across methods? What precisely does “optimal” mean operationally?

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized; the main ideas are easy to follow. A few typographical and wording issues:
- Line 82: “… will a Euclidean …” → “… will be a Euclidean …”
- Line 83: missing “or.”
- Section 4 title: “Fredhom” → “Fredholm”
- Line 247: “porportional” → “proportional”
- Line 283: “That is, or …” → “That is, for …”
- Line 352: sentence should be rewritten for clarity.

Originality
Although related to a recent NIPS paper ([9]), the approach is substantively novel, and the distinctions from prior work are clearly articulated.

Significance
The results are meaningful and significant. The method is evaluated on diverse datasets from different domains, demonstrating clear benefits from incorporating unlabeled data.

Overall
A well-written paper with a solid theoretical foundation and a compelling experimental section.
