The paper introduces a data-dependent family of kernels, termed Fredholm kernels, that leverage unlabeled data in a natural and principled way. The authors establish several theoretical properties and present experiments showing that the approach performs competitively—often favorably—against state-of-the-art methods. The paper is clearly written and well organized.

Detailed comments:
1) Kernel width selection: Is there a systematic procedure for choosing the kernel bandwidth(s) for the Fredholm kernel? For example, under reasonable assumptions, can one select the width via cross-validation or another principled criterion? Guidance on this would improve practical usability.
2) Scope and failure cases: It would be helpful to include toy experiments that illustrate scenarios where the proposed method is less effective. This would clarify the conditions under which the method excels and where it may struggle.

Overall, the paper is interesting, well presented, and makes a meaningful contribution. I recommend acceptance.
