This paper describes a framework particularly useful for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels. The classical supervised learning optimization problem solved in kernel-based methods is extended to incorporate unlabeled information leading to discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper has high technical quality with well-supported claims by theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a new data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The classifying function differs from the classical represent theorem solution but it is still elegant and easy to compute. 
I have a few comments: One is related to Equation 3 where the solution of the proposed optimization problem is described. Since the authors mentioned that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD), does Equation 3 still hold in this case? A proof on how Equation 3 was obtained would have been nice to see as well. Another comment concerns the experimental section where the authors chose an "optimal" parameter for all methods in the benchmark. How were those parameters determined? What is meant by "optimal"?

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part. It is easy to follow, and the main ideas are explained adequately. However, there are some typos that need to be corrected, such as:
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: An "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm"
* Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional"
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for..."
* Line 352: The sentence needs to be rewritten for clarity.

Originality
The proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]) but is novel in essence. It is clear how the work differs from this publication and others.

Significance
The results are important and significant. There was a significant effort to test the proposed method on several datasets in different application domains.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a good theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.
