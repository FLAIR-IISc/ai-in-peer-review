The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, and overall I find the paper very interesting.

Suggestions and questions:
- Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to the equation on line 99):
  In the Nyström method, one also considers an approximation to an integral equation (e.g., in kernel PCA). It would be helpful to mention this connection and to explain in what sense your approach is similar to or different from Nyström-style integral-equation approximations.
- Section 2, Eq. 2:
  In the SVM/kernel methods literature there are approaches formulated via operator equations. Please cite this line of work and clarify whether Eq. 2 is a special case of those operator-equation formulations or, if not, precisely how it differs.
- Section 2, line 128 (representer theorem):
  Eq. 2 is less common in learning theory. You appear to assume a representer theorem. Please provide a proof of the relevant representer theorem for your setting, or give a precise reference, and explain clearly how Eq. 2 follows as a special case.
- Section 4, Eq. 8:
  The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is nice and interesting. How does this compare to other methods such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels?
