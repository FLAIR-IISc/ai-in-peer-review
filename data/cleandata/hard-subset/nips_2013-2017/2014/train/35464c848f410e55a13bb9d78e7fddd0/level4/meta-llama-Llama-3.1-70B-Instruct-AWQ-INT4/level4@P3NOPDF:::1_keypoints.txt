- Proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, leading to a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- High technical quality: thorough theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.  
- Introduces a new data-dependent kernel that effectively incorporates unlabeled information.  
- The classifying function is different from the classical representer-theorem solution but is elegant and easy to compute.  
- Clarification needed: provide proof/explanation that Equation 3 remains valid when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (non-PSD).  
- Experimental concern: clarify how the “optimal” parameter for all benchmarked methods was chosen and what “optimal” means.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Several typos/minor errors require correction (Line 82, Line 83, Section 4 title, Line 247, Line 283, Line 352).  
- Novelty is solid: method is related to a recent NIPS paper but offers distinct, original contributions.  
- Significance: results are important and validated across multiple datasets and application domains.  
- Overall: a well-written paper with strong theoretical basis and convincing experiments.
