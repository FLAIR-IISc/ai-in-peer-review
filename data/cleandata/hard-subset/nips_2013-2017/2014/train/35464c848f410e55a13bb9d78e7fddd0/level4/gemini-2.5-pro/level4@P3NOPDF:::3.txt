### Summary

This paper introduces a novel data-dependent kernel, termed the Fredholm kernel, which provides a principled way to incorporate information from unlabeled data into the kernel's construction. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the kernel's properties and demonstrate its practical utility through a series of experiments. The empirical results show that the Fredholm kernel compares favorably against several state-of-the-art methods, particularly in semi-supervised settings.

### Strengths

1.  **Novelty and Significance:** The proposed Fredholm kernel is a novel and significant contribution to the literature on kernel methods. The idea of leveraging the entire data distribution (including unlabeled points) to define the kernel is both elegant and powerful, addressing a key challenge in semi-supervised learning.
2.  **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper is well-grounded in theory. The authors derive and discuss several important properties of the kernel, providing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
3.  **Empirical Validation:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincing. The proposed kernel demonstrates strong performance against competitive baselines across various datasets, validating its practical effectiveness.
4.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The core concepts are explained clearly, making the work accessible and easy to follow.

### Questions and Suggestions for Improvement

While the paper is strong, I have a few questions and suggestions that could further enhance its impact:

1.  **Hyperparameter Selection:** Could the authors elaborate on the selection of hyperparameters for the Fredholm kernel, such as the kernel width? Is there a systematic approach for tuning these parameters? For instance, would a standard cross-validation procedure be theoretically justified or empirically effective under certain assumptions? Guidance on this practical aspect would be very valuable for practitioners.

2.  **Analysis of Limitations:** To better understand the applicability and potential limitations of the proposed method, it would be beneficial to include an analysis of its failure modes. For example, a toy experiment illustrating a scenario where the Fredholm kernel might not perform well could provide valuable insights. This would help clarify the specific settings or data distributions where the method is most effective.

### Recommendation

Overall, this is a high-quality paper presenting an interesting and well-supported contribution. The proposed Fredholm kernel is a valuable addition to the semi-supervised learning toolkit. Despite the minor points raised above, which can be addressed in the final version, the paper's strengths are compelling. I recommend acceptance.
