The authors present an interesting new method for supervised and semi-supervised learning using Fredholm kernels. Many aspects of the method are discussed in detail, and overall, I find the paper very compelling.

However, I have a few suggestions and questions that would strengthen the presentation:

- **Section 2 (from Eq. 1 to Eq. on line 99):**
The Nyström method also considers an approximation to an integral equation (in the context of kernel PCA, for example). It would be beneficial if the authors could acknowledge this and explain how their approach is similar to, or differs from, the Nyström method. 

- **Section 2, Eq. 2:**
Operator equation approaches are also present in the literature on support vector machines and kernel methods. Mentioning this related work and clarifying how Eq. 2 is a special case of it (or detailing the differences) would be valuable.

- **Section 2, line 128 (Representer Theorem):**
Eq. 2 represents a less common formulation in learning theory. The authors implicitly assume the existence of a representer theorem. Either proving a representer theorem here, or providing a precise reference and explaining how Eq. 2 falls within its framework, would be crucial.

- **Section 4, Eq. 8:**
The interpretation as soft-thresholding PCA is insightful and interesting. However, it would be helpful to understand how this approach differs from other methods like Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.

    - Line 221: Change "achived" to "achieved".
    - Line 341: Change "chossing" to "choosing".
    - Line 672: Replace "Table ??" with the correct table number.
