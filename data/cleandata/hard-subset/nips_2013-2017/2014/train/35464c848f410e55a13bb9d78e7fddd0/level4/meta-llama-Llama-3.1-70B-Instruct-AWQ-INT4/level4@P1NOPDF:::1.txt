This paper presents a framework for semi-supervised learning based on Fredholm kernels, which extends the classical supervised learning optimization problem to incorporate unlabeled information, resulting in a discretized version of the Fredholm integral equation.

Quality
The paper exhibits high technical quality, with well-supported claims backed by thorough theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The proposed formulation leads to a novel data-dependent kernel that incorporates unlabeled information. The classifying function, although differing from the classical representer theorem solution, remains elegant and easy to compute.

I have a few comments: Firstly, regarding Equation 3, which describes the solution to the proposed optimization problem, the authors mention that the associated kernel need not be positive semi-definite (PSD). In this case, does Equation 3 still hold? A proof detailing how Equation 3 was obtained would have been a welcome addition. Secondly, in the experimental section, the authors chose an "optimal" parameter for all methods in the benchmark. It would be beneficial to know how these parameters were determined and what is meant by "optimal" in this context.

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well-organized for the most part, making it easy to follow and understand the main ideas. However, there are some minor errors: 
* Line 82: "... will a Euclidean ..." should be "... will be a Euclidean ..."
* Line 83: an "or" is missing.
* Section 4 title: "Fredhom" should be "Fredholm".
* Line 247: "porportional" should be "proportional".
* Line 283: "That is, or..." should be "That is, for...".
* Line 352: the sentence requires rephrasing.

Originality
Although the proposed approach is related to a recent NIPS publication ([9]), it is novel in essence. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from this publication and others.

Significance
The results are important and significant, demonstrating a considerable effort to test the proposed method on several datasets across different application domains.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a solid theoretical basis and a convincing experimental section.
