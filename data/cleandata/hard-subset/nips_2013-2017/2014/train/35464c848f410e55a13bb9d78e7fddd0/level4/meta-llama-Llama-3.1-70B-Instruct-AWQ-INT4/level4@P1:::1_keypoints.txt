- Paper proposes a Fredholm-kernel-based semi-supervised learning framework that extends kernel optimization to a discretized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Strong technical quality: solid theoretical analysis and convincing experiments; introduces a data-dependent kernel using unlabeled data and an elegant, easy-to-compute classifier differing from the classical representer solution.  
- Question about Equation 3: unclear whether it holds when the associated kernel is not PSD and a derivation/proof of Equation 3 is requested.  
- Experimental concern: authors used an "optimal" parameter for all methods—need clarification on how parameters were chosen and what "optimal" means.  
- Clarity is good overall, but several typos and wording issues must be fixed (Line 82 "will a"→"will be a"; Line 83 missing "or"; Section 4 "Fredhom"→"Fredholm"; Line 247 "porportional"→"proportional"; Line 283 "That is, or..."→"That is, for..."; Line 352 sentence needs rewriting).  
- Originality: related to a recent NIPS paper [9] but novel in essence and differences are clearly explained.  
- Significance: important results with extensive testing across multiple datasets and domains.  
- Overall verdict: well-written paper with strong theory and convincing experimental support.
