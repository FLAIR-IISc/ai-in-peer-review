- Paper reframes supervised/semi‑supervised learning as solving a regularized Fredholm integral equation and derives a data‑dependent “Fredholm kernel” k̂F used in a kernel method.  
- Framework handles square loss and, in principle, margin losses; motivates a “noise assumption” that low‑variance local directions are uninformative.  
- Theoretical lenses: (i) linear outer kernel yields soft‑thresholding of principal directions; (ii) under Gaussian signal+noise, Fredholm kernels give unbiased, lower‑variance approximations to a target kernel.  
- Empirically competitive on synthetic tasks matching assumptions and several real datasets.  
- Position relative to operator‑theoretic learning (Tikhonov regularization in RKHS, integral-operator literature) is missing and should be cited (Caponnetto & De Vito, Smale & Zhou, Rosasco et al., Engl et al.).  
- Clarify relation to Nyström/kernel PCA: both use empirical integral operators, but differ in goal (prediction vs eigendecomposition) and in whether PSD outer kernels/spectrum approximation are required.  
- Clarify relation to diffusion/graph heat kernels and diffusion maps; normalized Fredholm kernel is especially close and should be positioned/cited accordingly.  
- Explain Eq.2 as Tikhonov regularization for a linear operator L formed from empirical integral operators and state it in that RKHS regularization framework.  
- Provide a precise representer‑theorem statement or proof/citation showing L_i(f) are bounded linear functionals and the optimizer lies in the finite span, with conditions when outer kernel is non‑PSD.  
- State boundedness of K_p̂X as an operator from H to L2 and assumptions under which Proposition 1 holds for non‑PSD outer kernels.  
- Compare the linear‑outer (ΣF‑reweighted) view to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels, clarifying when Fredholm kernels may outperform or underperform distributional kernels.  
- Expand proofs or move full proofs to appendix and state technical assumptions (bounded kernels, operator norms, consistency as u→∞).  
- Clarify inductive vs transductive use: k̂F depends on the unlabeled pool—explain handling of new test points if unlabeled set changes.  
- Report computational cost and scalability: forming K̂F is O((l+u)^2) memory and O((l+u)^3) time; discuss approximations (Nyström, random features).  
- Add a positioning subsection covering Nyström, diffusion kernels, and operator‑theoretic regularization.  
- Provide guidance on when to use normalized vs unnormalized k̂F and how to choose t vs r beyond cross‑validation.  
- Originality: Fredholm‑kernel construction is novel and distinct from graph Laplacian regularization and prior Fredholm uses in density‑ratio estimation.  
- Significance: simple to implement (K̂F = K K_H K^T) and broadly applicable, especially in noise‑dominated regimes, but needs scalability and positioning fixes.  
- Pros: novel reformulation, clear kernelization, theoretical insight (soft PCA/variance reduction), competitive experiments, flexible kernel choices.  
- Cons: missing formal representer theorem and operator assumptions, limited discussion of Nyström/diffusion/operator literature, scalability and inductive use not fully addressed, noise assumption needs further formalization.
