- Introduces a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Proposes a data-dependent "Fredholm" kernel that naturally incorporates unlabeled data, a significant methodological contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the theoretical properties of the kernel.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the Fredholm kernel, including its connection to the noise assumption in semi-supervised learning.  
- Shows that the Fredholm kernel effectively utilizes unlabeled data under the noise assumption.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods on several synthetic and real-world experiments.  
- Offers a comprehensive related-work review that situates the contribution within the broader ML literature.  
- Weakness: lacks a systematic method for selecting the Fredholm kernel width (authors recommend cross-validation).  
- Weakness: could benefit from more toy experiments to illustrate the settings where the method is most effective.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 9/10.
