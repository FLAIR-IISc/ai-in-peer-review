- Proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and introducing "Fredholm kernels."
- Naturally extends kernel methods to incorporate unlabeled data, with theoretical justification under a “noise assumption.”
- High technical quality with solid theoretical analysis; Theorems 2 and 4 are strong under their assumptions.
- Introduces potential variance reduction and connects to density ratio estimation and kernel mean embedding.
- Extensive experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets showing strong performance versus TSVM, LapRLSC, and RLSC.
- Generally well-written and organized; main ideas and connections to prior work are clearly presented.
- Significant contribution addressing leveraging unlabeled data, particularly promising for noisy datasets.
- Major concern: Equation 3’s validity when the associated kernel k_H is not PSD lacks justification; Representer Theorem application is unclear.
- Experimental protocol description is ambiguous—unclear how “optimal” parameters were selected, validation procedure, and hyperparameter search details.
- Manuscript contains several typographical errors that detract from polish.
- Limited discussion of computational cost and scalability (matrix computation/inversion complexity) for large datasets.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, convincing experiments, clear exposition, and significance of the problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved Eq.3/PSD issue, ambiguous experimental reproducibility, and presentation typos.
- Recommendation: accept contingent on addressing: rigorous justification for Eq.3 with non‑PSD k_H, complete experimental protocol details, and correction of typos.
