This paper introduces a novel semi-supervised learning framework based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, leading to the concept of “Fredholm kernels.” The authors demonstrate a strong theoretical foundation, coupled with compelling experimental results, positioning this work as a significant contribution to the field.

The core idea is to extend kernel-based supervised learning to incorporate unlabeled data by discretizing and regularizing the Fredholm integral. This results in a data-dependent kernel that naturally integrates information from both labeled and unlabeled datasets. The framework connects to existing kernel methods while offering a distinct approach to leveraging unlabeled data, particularly under what the authors term the "noise assumption" – a perspective on semi-supervised learning complementary to the more common manifold and cluster assumptions.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper exhibits a high level of technical rigor. The derivation of the Fredholm kernels and the accompanying theoretical analysis, particularly in Section 4, are well-presented and appear sound. The connection to existing kernel methods is clearly articulated.
* **Originality:** The formulation of learning as a Fredholm integral equation and the resulting data-dependent kernels represent a novel approach to semi-supervised learning. While building on existing work in kernel methods and integral operators, this paper presents a unique and insightful perspective. The authors clearly differentiate their work from prior contributions (e.g., [10, 13]), demonstrating awareness of the related literature.
* **Significance:** The experimental results, spanning both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The consistent performance improvements over baseline algorithms (RLSC, TSVM, LapRLSC) suggest a meaningful contribution to the field. The exploration of the “noise assumption” provides a valuable new lens through which to understand semi-supervised learning.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The authors effectively convey the main ideas, making the framework accessible to readers familiar with kernel methods. The use of equations and illustrative examples aids comprehension.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Equation 3 Validity:** A crucial aspect requiring clarification is the validity of Equation 3 when the underlying kernel *k* is not positive semi-definite (PSD). The authors acknowledge this possibility, but a formal derivation or proof addressing its applicability in such cases is missing. This is a significant point, as the framework’s flexibility in accepting non-PSD kernels is a key feature.
* **Experimental Details:** The description of the experimental protocol could be more precise. The meaning of “optimal” parameters and the specific methodology employed to select them requires further elaboration. Providing details on the validation sets used and the search space for parameter tuning would enhance reproducibility and strengthen the conclusions.
* **Minor Errors:** The paper contains several minor typos and writing errors that detract from its polish. Examples include Line 82 (“the square loss or hinge loss”), Line 83 (“we will use functions”), Section 4 title (missing a “for”), Lines 247 (“the noise assumption”), 283, and 352. These should be addressed during the revision process.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound framework for semi-supervised learning.
* The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing methods.
* The introduction of Fredholm kernels provides a valuable new tool for researchers in the field.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a formal proof regarding Equation 3’s validity for non-PSD kernels is a concern.
* The experimental details require further clarification to ensure reproducibility and strengthen the conclusions.
* The presence of minor errors detracts from the overall polish of the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, this is a strong paper with significant potential. The originality, technical soundness, and compelling experimental results outweigh the concerns. The proposed Fredholm kernel framework offers a valuable contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning, and I believe it warrants acceptance. However, addressing the concerns regarding Equation 3 and clarifying the experimental details are crucial for enhancing the paper’s impact and ensuring its long-term value to the research community.

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. Specifically, I recommend:

1.  Providing a derivation or proof for the validity of Equation 3 when the underlying kernel is not PSD.
2.  Elaborating on the experimental protocol, detailing the parameter selection process and the validation sets used.
3.  Correcting the minor typos and writing errors identified.
