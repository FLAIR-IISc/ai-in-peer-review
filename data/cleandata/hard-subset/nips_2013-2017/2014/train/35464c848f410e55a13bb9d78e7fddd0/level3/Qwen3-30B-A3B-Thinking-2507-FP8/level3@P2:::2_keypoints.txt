- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework via a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that naturally incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Proposes a new "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning that differs from manifold and cluster assumptions.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing noise suppression via principal-component re-weighting (Theorem 2) and improved kernel approximations (Theorems 3–4).  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show consistent improvements over baseline methods.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear derivation of the Fredholm framework and connection to data-dependent kernels.  
- Lacks a complete representer theorem for Eq. 2 or a precise reference proving Eq. 2 as a special case.  
- Connection to Nystrom/kernel PCA integral-equation approximations and broader integral-operator literature needs clarification.  
- Generally well-written and organized; Section 4.2 could use more intuition to improve accessibility.  
- Several typos to fix (line 165 "Not that" → "Note that"; line 221 "achived" → "achieved"; line 341 "chossing" → "choosing"; line 672 unresolved table reference).  
- Core idea of Fredholm kernels is original and distinguishes itself from existing approaches.  
- Should compare more explicitly to other data-dependent kernel methods (e.g., Fisher kernels, probability product kernels).  
- The "soft-thresholding PCA" interpretation is an interesting, novel perspective on using unlabeled data to suppress noise.  
- Significant contribution for semi-supervised learning when manifold/cluster assumptions fail; useful with scarce labels.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address representer theorem, literature comparisons, and clarifications.
