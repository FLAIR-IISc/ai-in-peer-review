- Proposal: Introduces a semi-supervised framework casting prediction as a regularized Fredholm integral, yielding a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” KF built from an outer kernel, an inner RKHS kernel, and the empirical marginal p̂X.  
- Algorithmic consequence: Reduces semi-supervised learning to standard kernel methods (RLS/SVM) using KF = Kl+u KH KTl+u with representer-theorem solutions.  
- Variants and properties: Analyzes unnormalized and normalized Fredholm kernels, proves PSDness and provides explicit solutions.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to Fredholm-based density-ratio/covariate-shift methods, kernel mean embedding/conditional operators, and data-dependent/manifold kernels.  
- Novelty: Presents a simple operator-composition that integrates unlabeled data without graph Laplacians and formalizes a new “noise assumption.”  
- Theoretical contributions: Shows PCA-like soft-thresholding with linear outer kernel, unbiasedness and variance reduction under Gaussian/orthogonal-noise models, and normalization/PSD properties.  
- Empirics: Demonstrates favorable performance versus RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC on synthetic and real text, sentiment, and vision datasets; normalized variant improves stability.  
- Technical soundness: Operator construction and PSD proof are solid; denoising analysis is rigorous under stated assumptions.  
- Completeness: Provides algorithmic details and practical variants, though some proofs are deferred to the full version.  
- Empirical quality: Uses standard baselines and careful synthetic setups; benchmarks are convincing.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is well written, organized, and explains operator-to-kernel reduction, kernels, and the noise assumption clearly.  
- Originality: The specific Fredholm-kernel construction and formal noise assumption are novel and distinct from existing Laplacian/Fredholm work.  
- Significance: Method is simple, broadly applicable, easy to adopt, and likely to be extended given strong baseline performance in common domains.  
- Question — kernel width selection: Asks for systematic methods to choose outer width t (e.g., analytic mapping from target width, marginal-likelihood, or CV consistency).  
- Question — sensitivity/stability: Requests sensitivity analysis for t, especially for instability in the unnormalized variant.  
- Question — computational complexity: Notes KF formation is costly (O((l+u)^2) memory/time) and asks about Nyström, random features, or low-rank approximations and timing comparisons.  
- Question — generalization analysis: Suggests discussing bounds/stability treating the unlabeled set as fixed or transductive generalization.  
- Suggestions — failure modes: Add toy failure cases (cluster assumption holds but noise assumption fails, covariate shift, small u/high-d, multimodal pX, kernel mismatch).  
- Suggestions — sensitivity study: Provide brief sensitivity study over t, normalization, and λ plus a rule-of-thumb initializer (e.g., median heuristic).  
- Suggestions — computational accelerations: Discuss or demonstrate Nyström/random features and their accuracy tradeoffs.  
- Suggestions — reproducibility: Unify notation (p vs pX) and add a reproducibility paragraph (code, datasets, parameter grids).  
- Pros: Elegant operator-to-kernel reduction, solid theoretical denoising results and PCA interpretation, strong empirical results, practical normalized variant.  
- Cons: Parameter selection and sensitivity not systematized, theory limited to Gaussian/orthogonal noise, potentially high computational cost, limited discussion of failure modes.  
- Overall assessment: Novel, well-executed, and practically useful semi-supervised kernel construction with solid theory and competitive empirical performance; recommended for acceptance.
