- Proposes Fredholm kernel: a novel data-dependent kernel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning that naturally incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Provides a thorough introduction and review of semi-supervised learning, including manifold regularization and cluster assumptions.  
- Introduces a "noise assumption": low-variance directions in unlabeled-data neighborhoods are uninformative for labels and can be treated as noise.  
- Defines the Fredholm kernel as a non-standard, data-dependent kernel usable in standard kernel methods.  
- Shows the Fredholm kernel is positive semi-definite and gives a convenient reformulation via a new kernel matrix.  
- Offers two theoretical views of noise suppression: filtering small-variance principal components and approximating the true kernel on the hidden space.  
- Provides quantitative analysis demonstrating better noise-robust approximation to the target kernel than using noisy-point kernels.  
- Presents experiments on synthetic and real datasets showing competitive or superior performance, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains technical details clearly for readers familiar with kernels.  
- Technically sound work with convincing experimental results.  
- Novel and original approach with potential broad significance for semi-supervised learning applications.  
- Lacks a systematic procedure for selecting the Fredholm kernel width; authors recommend cross-validation but give little guidance.  
- Would benefit from more practical guidance on choosing kernel width and applying the method.  
- Could include toy experiments showing failure cases to clarify regimes where the method is less effective.
