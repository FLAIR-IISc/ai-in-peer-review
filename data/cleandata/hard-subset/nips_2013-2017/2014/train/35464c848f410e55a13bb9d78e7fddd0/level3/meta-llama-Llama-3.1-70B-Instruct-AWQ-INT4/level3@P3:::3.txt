The authors of this paper propose a novel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning, based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. This approach naturally fits into the kernel framework and can be interpreted as constructing new data-dependent kernels, which they call Fredholm kernels. The paper presents several theoretical properties of the kernel and demonstrates its effectiveness in utilizing unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning.

The main contribution of the paper is the formulation of a new framework for learning based on solving a regularized Fredholm equation. This framework combines labeled and unlabeled data in a natural way, allowing for the incorporation of unlabeled data into the learning process. The authors show that this framework can be expressed as a kernel method with a non-standard data-dependent kernel, which they call the Fredholm kernel.

The paper also discusses the "noise assumption" in semi-supervised learning, which is that the directions with low variance in the unlabeled data are uninformative with respect to the class labels and can be regarded as noise. The authors provide theoretical evidence that Fredholm kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under this assumption, and demonstrate that methods based on Fredholm learning show competitive performance in the standard semi-supervised learning setting.

The authors present several theoretical results for Fredholm kernels, including the fact that they can be used to suppress principal components with small variance, and that they can provide a more accurate estimate of the "true" kernel on the hidden underlying space. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of Fredholm kernels on synthetic examples and real-world datasets, including text categorization, sentiment analysis, and hand-written digit recognition.
