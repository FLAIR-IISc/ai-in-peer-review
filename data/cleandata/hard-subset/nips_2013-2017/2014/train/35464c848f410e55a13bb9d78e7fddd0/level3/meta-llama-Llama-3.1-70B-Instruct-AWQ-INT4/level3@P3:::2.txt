The paper "Learning with Fredholm Kernels" presents a novel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. The authors propose a new kernel method, called Fredholm kernels, which can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption". The paper provides both theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels can improve the performance of classifiers.

The main contributions of the paper are: (1) a new framework for learning based on solving a regularized Fredholm equation, which naturally combines labeled and unlabeled data; (2) a discussion of the "noise assumption" in semi-supervised learning and its relation to the cluster and manifold assumptions; and (3) theoretical and empirical results showing that Fredholm kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the noise assumption.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed framework and its relation to previous work. The theoretical results are impressive, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of Fredholm kernels in improving the performance of classifiers.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning. The authors show that the proposed framework can be interpreted as a standard kernel method with a non-standard data-dependent kernel, which is a novel contribution to the field.
