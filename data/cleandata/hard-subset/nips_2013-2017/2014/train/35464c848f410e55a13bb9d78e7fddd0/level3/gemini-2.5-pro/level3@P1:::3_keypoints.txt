- Reformulates supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation to define predictors via an integral operator on an RKHS function.
- Introduces a new data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that incorporates marginal distribution information from unlabeled data.
- Connects to density ratio estimation and kernel mean embeddings, while distinguishing from manifold-regularization approaches that use graph Laplacians.
- Formalizes a "noise assumption": low-variance local data directions are uninformative for classification and should be suppressed.
- The framework provides a principled mechanism for leveraging unlabeled data to suppress noise and improve generalization.
- The derivation of the Fredholm kernel is clear and technically sound, with solid theoretical backing.
- Theorem 2 links the linear Fredholm kernel to soft-thresholding PCA, offering an intuitive noise-suppression interpretation.
- Theorems 3 and 4 show that for linear and Gaussian target kernels the Fredholm kernel yields a variance-reduced estimator of the true kernel.
- The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized, with clear exposition of inner vs. outer kernels.
- Empirical results are convincing: synthetic experiments target the noise assumption and real datasets (text, image, MNIST with added noise) show strong performance versus baselines.
- Weakness: sensitivity to inner and outer kernel parameters (e.g., widths); cross-validation is used but a more principled selection strategy informed by theory would help.
- Weakness: lacks discussion or experiments showing failure cases when the noise assumption is violated and low-variance directions are informative.
- Overall: high-quality, novel, technically solid work with strong empirical support; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
