- Novel structured spike-and-slab prior that incorporates prior knowledge about sparsity patterns.  
- Hierarchical prior using a Gaussian process on spike-and-slab probabilities to encode prior information via covariance functions.  
- Bayesian inference scheme based on expectation propagation for the proposed model.  
- Strength: new prior formulation can capture structured sparsity, a significant contribution.  
- Strength: efficient EP-based inference capable of handling high-dimensional problems.  
- Strength: numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relationship to existing approaches (e.g., Nystrom method).  
- Weakness: limited insight into choice of covariance function, parameter settings, and sensitivity.  
- Weakness: high computational complexity may limit applicability to very large-scale problems.  
- Weakness: several typos and errors in the paper that need correction.  
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Originality: presents a novel approach to sparse signal recovery.  
- Significance: results have potential impact on sparse recovery and related fields.  
- Suggested improvements: add more context/background, discuss limitations and extensions, and detail experimental setup and parameters.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel method, efficient for high dimensions, promising empirical results.  
- Con-accept arguments: needs deeper relation to prior work, high computational cost, and proofreading.
