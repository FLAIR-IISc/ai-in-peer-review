- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that naturally incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Shows Fredholm kernels can leverage unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" with theoretical support.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Distinguishes the approach from prior kernel and integral methods, emphasizing natural incorporation of unlabeled data.  
- Main contributions: new Fredholm-equation-based framework, noise-assumption analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Paper quality is high: clear presentation, well-supported claims, and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Clarity is excellent, with reproducible-level detail and appropriate citations.  
- Originality is high due to the novel use of Fredholm integral equations for semi-supervised learning.  
- Significance is high because the method addresses a challenging semi-supervised problem with practical potential.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid theoretical analysis, convincing experiments, and strong writing.  
- Main weaknesses: reliance on the "noise assumption" which may not hold broadly, and no systematic method for selecting Fredholm kernel width.  
- Suggested improvements: include toy failure-case experiments and provide a systematic kernel-width selection method (e.g., cross-validation under assumptions).  
- Overall recommendation: interesting, well-written paper recommended for acceptance despite some limitations.
