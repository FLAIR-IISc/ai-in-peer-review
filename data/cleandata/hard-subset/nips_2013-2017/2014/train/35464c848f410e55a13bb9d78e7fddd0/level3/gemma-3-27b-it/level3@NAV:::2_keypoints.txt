- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, producing “Fredholm kernels.”  
- Leverages a “noise assumption” as an alternative justification for semi-supervised learning, which is interesting and potentially valuable.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and empirical results supporting the effectiveness of the Fredholm-kernel approach.  
- Positions the work well within kernel methods and semi-supervised learning literature, citing manifold regularization and kernel mean embedding.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets show competitive performance.  
- Fails to fully discuss connections to prior operator-equation approaches in SVM/kernel literature; relation to existing representer-theorem-based methods is unclear.  
- Eq. 2 needs explicit discussion about how it generalizes or differs from prior integral-equation formulations.  
- A representer theorem for Eq. 2 must be proven or a precise reference supplied to justify the formulation.  
- The “soft-thresholding PCA” interpretation is intriguing but requires detailed comparison to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.  
- Needs clarity on differences in theoretical guarantees and computational complexity versus related kernel techniques.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors (“Not that”, “achived”, “chossing”) and a broken table reference (Table ??).  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution with solid presentation and results, but acceptance hinges on stronger theoretical justification and comparative analysis.
