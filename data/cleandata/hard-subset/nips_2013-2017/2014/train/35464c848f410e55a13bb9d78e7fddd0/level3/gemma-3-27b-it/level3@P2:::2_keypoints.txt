- Introduces a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework using Fredholm integral equations and defines “Fredholm kernels.”
- Core idea: use unlabeled data to denoise classifiers by interpreting learning via integral operators.
- Provides theoretical justification under a plausible “noise assumption.”
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Positions work within kernel methods and integral operators, citing relevant prior work.
- Needs expanded discussion of related integral-equation literature, especially Nyström methods and kernel PCA.
- Should acknowledge and compare to operator-equation approaches in SVM/kernel literature and clarify relation of Eq. 2 to established formulations.
- Missing a formal proof or precise reference for the representer theorem justification of the proposed solution (computational tractability not fully justified).
- The “soft-thresholding PCA” interpretation is interesting but requires deeper comparison to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels.
- Normalization of the Fredholm kernel (Eq. 5) appears crucial for stability and needs more explanation of necessity and conditions.
- Strengths: novel theoretical framing, intuitive noise assumption, solid theoretical results, thorough experiments and ablation studies.
- Weaknesses: missing representer theorem justification, insufficient related-work discussion, lack of detailed comparisons to related feature-weighting methods.
- Minor typographical errors throughout the manuscript that should be fixed (examples provided).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions conditioned on addressing the representer theorem, expanding related-work/discussion, and fixing typos.
