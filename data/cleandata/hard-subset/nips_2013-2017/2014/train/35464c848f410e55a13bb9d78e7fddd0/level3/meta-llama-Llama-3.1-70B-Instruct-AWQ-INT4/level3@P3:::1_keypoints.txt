- Proposes a novel semi-supervised framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral and introducing a discretized Fredholm kernel.  
- Extends kernel-based supervised optimization to incorporate unlabeled data via a new data-dependent Fredholm kernel.  
- Main contributions: new semi-supervised framework, introduction of Fredholm kernel, and demonstration of its effectiveness for using unlabeled data.  
- Strong theoretical analysis, including discussion of the "noise assumption" and evidence that Fredholm kernels can improve classifier performance under that assumption.  
- High technical quality: clear derivation of the Fredholm kernel and an elegant, computationally tractable resulting classifier.  
- Extensive experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets with convincing empirical results.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and situates the work effectively relative to prior research; the approach is novel.  
- Concern: lack of derivation or proof for Equation 3 when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD); needs clarification.  
- Concern: experimental protocol details are insufficient—unclear how "optimal" parameters were chosen, harming reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to minor revisions to address the theoretical justification for Eq.3 and to clarify experimental protocol.
