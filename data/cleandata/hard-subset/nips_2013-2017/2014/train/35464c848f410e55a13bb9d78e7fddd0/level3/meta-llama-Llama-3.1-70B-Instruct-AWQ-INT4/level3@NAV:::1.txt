**Review of "Learning with Fredholm Kernels"**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm integral equations, which can be interpreted as constructing new data-dependent kernels, called Fredholm kernels. The authors demonstrate that these kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" and show competitive performance in various experiments.

**Technical Quality**

The paper exhibits high technical quality, with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their ideas, making it easy to follow their arguments. The mathematical derivations are rigorous and well-explained, and the experimental results are thorough and well-illustrated.

**Originality**

The paper introduces a new data-dependent kernel, the Fredholm kernel, which is a novel combination of familiar techniques. The authors also propose a new assumption, the "noise assumption," which is orthogonal to existing assumptions in semi-supervised learning. While the paper builds upon previous work, it presents a significant departure from existing methods and provides a fresh perspective on semi-supervised learning.

**Significance**

The results presented in the paper are significant, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework in various settings. The authors show that their method can improve performance over existing semi-supervised learning algorithms, especially in cases where the noise assumption holds. The paper also provides a new perspective on the role of unlabeled data in semi-supervised learning, which can inspire further research in this area.

**Weaknesses**

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of a clear derivation or proof for Equation 3's validity when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite. Additionally, the experimental protocol could be clarified, particularly with regards to how the "optimal" parameters were chosen. Finally, there are several minor typos and writing errors throughout the paper that should be corrected.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated framework for semi-supervised learning.
* The technical quality of the paper is high, with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.
* The paper introduces a new data-dependent kernel and a new assumption, which can inspire further research in this area.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a clearer derivation or proof for Equation 3's validity when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite.
* The experimental protocol could be clarified, particularly with regards to how the "optimal" parameters were chosen.
* The paper contains several minor typos and writing errors that should be corrected.

**Conclusion**

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning, introducing a novel framework and a new data-dependent kernel. While there are some minor weaknesses, the paper's technical quality, originality, and significance make it a strong candidate for acceptance.
