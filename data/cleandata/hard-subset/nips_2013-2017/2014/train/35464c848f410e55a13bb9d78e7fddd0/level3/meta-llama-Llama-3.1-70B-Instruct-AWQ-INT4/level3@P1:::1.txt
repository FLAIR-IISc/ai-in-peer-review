This paper proposes a novel semi-supervised learning framework based on Fredholm kernels, which extends kernel-based supervised optimization to incorporate unlabeled data via a discretized Fredholm integral. The authors demonstrate high technical quality with solid theoretical analysis and convincing experimental results.

The main idea of the paper is to reformulate the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, which naturally combines labeled and unlabeled data. The authors show that this framework can be expressed as a kernel method with a non-standard data-dependent kernel, which they call the Fredholm kernel. This kernel is induced from the Fredholm problem and can be viewed as a "soft thresholding" PCA, suppressing the directions with low variance.

The authors provide theoretical results for Fredholm kernels, including the analysis of linear Fredholm kernels and inner products, and kernel approximation with noise. They also present extensive experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The paper is generally clear and well-organized, making the main ideas easy to follow. However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. For example, there are several typos and minor writing errors that need correction. Additionally, the experimental protocol is unclear, and it would be helpful to provide more details on how the "optimal" parameters were chosen.

One potential question is the validity of Equation 3 when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD). The authors do not provide a derivation or proof for this case, and it is unclear whether the equation still holds.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of semi-supervised learning, and the results are convincing. The proposed method is novel and has clear differences from prior work. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the method across multiple datasets, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Weaknesses:
