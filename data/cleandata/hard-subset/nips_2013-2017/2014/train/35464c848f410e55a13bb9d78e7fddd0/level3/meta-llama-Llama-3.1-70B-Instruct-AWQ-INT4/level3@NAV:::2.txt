This paper proposes a novel framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. The approach fits naturally into the kernel framework and can be interpreted as constructing new data-dependent kernels, which are called Fredholm kernels. The authors demonstrate that methods based on Fredholm learning show very competitive performance in the standard semi-supervised learning setting.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly presented. The authors provide both theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the noise assumption. The "soft-thresholding PCA" interpretation of the Fredholm kernel is interesting and provides a new perspective on the problem.

However, there are a few areas that need improvement. Firstly, the authors should mention Nystrom/kernel PCA integral-equation approximations and clarify similarities/differences with the proposed approach. Additionally, they should cite operator-equation approaches in SVM/kernel literature and explain how Eq. 2 relates or differs from these approaches. A representer theorem should also be provided, either by proving it for Eq. 2 or giving a precise reference and showing Eq. 2 as a special case.

Furthermore, the authors should compare the Fredholm kernel with other data-dependent kernels, such as Fisher kernels and probability product kernels. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

There are also a few typos that need to be fixed, including "Not that" on line 165, "achived" on line 221, "chossing" on line 341, and "Table ??" on line 672.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. With some minor revisions, it has the potential to be a top-rated paper at the conference.
