- Introduces a semi-supervised framework reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, producing "Fredholm kernels" that incorporate unlabeled data via data-dependent kernels.  
- Proposes a new "noise assumption" (distinct from manifold/cluster assumptions) and provides theoretical and empirical evidence that Fredholm kernels exploit unlabeled data under this assumption.  
- Shows linear Fredholm kernels implement a "soft thresholding" PCA, suppressing principal components with small variance.  
- Shows Gaussian Fredholm kernels give more precise estimators of target kernels and reduce variance in noisy settings.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets (20 Newsgroups, WebKB, IMDB, digit recognition) demonstrate consistent improvements over Transductive SVM and LapRLSC, especially when cluster assumptions fail.  
- Original work that extends integral operator methods and is distinct from prior density-ratio and manifold-regularization approaches.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with sufficient detail for expert reproduction.  
- Minor writing issues: several typos (Lines 82, 83, 247, 283, 352), Section 4 title, inconsistent notation for normalized Fredholm kernels, and some unclear experimental phrasing.  
- Major technical concern: Equation (3) uses the Representer Theorem assuming the kernel is PSD; the paper must clarify validity or provide a derivation/proof for non-PSD kernels.  
- Experimental protocol concern: insufficient detail on how "optimal" parameters were chosen—need exact parameter selection processes per dataset.  
- Limited discussion of computational complexity and runtime comparison against baseline methods.  
- Overall assessment: substantial and significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing technical completeness and experimental details.
