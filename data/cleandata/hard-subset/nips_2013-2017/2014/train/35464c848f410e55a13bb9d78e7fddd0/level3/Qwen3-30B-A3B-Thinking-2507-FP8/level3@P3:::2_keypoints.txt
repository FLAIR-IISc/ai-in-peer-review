- Proposes a novel Fredholm kernel framework that reformulates learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation and incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Introduces a new "noise assumption" that low-variance directions in unlabeled data are noise, explaining when unlabeled data helps.  
- Theoretical contribution: "soft-thresholding PCA" interpretation (Theorem 2) and noise-suppression results (Theorems 3–4) show how Fredholm kernels attenuate low-variance components.  
- Experimental validation: synthetic experiments (designed to violate cluster assumption) and real-world text/image results show competitive improvements over baselines.  
- Originality: framework is novel and meaningfully extends semi‑supervised learning literature beyond manifold/cluster assumptions.  
- Connection to prior work: relates to Fredholm equations for density-ratio estimation, kernel mean embeddings, shrinkage in RKHS, and manifold regularization.  
- Critical missing piece: no proof or precise reference for the claimed Representer Theorem application to Eq. 2 — a significant technical gap.  
- Insufficient comparisons to related methods: needs explicit comparison to Nystrom approximation and kernel PCA given the PCA-like interpretation.  
- Broader operator-equation context is underdeveloped: should clarify relationships to other kernel operator/inverse-problem formulations (e.g., SVM as regularized inverse problem).  
- Lacks comparison to other noise-handling kernels (e.g., Fisher kernels, probability product kernels) that also incorporate additional information.  
- Writing/typos: several minor errors to fix (e.g., "Not that"→"Note that", "achived"→"achieved", "chossing"→"choosing", "Table ??"→"Table 1").  
- Quality: theoretical claims are generally sound but undermined by the missing representer result; experiments are well-conducted and supportive.  
- Clarity: well-organized and clear, but would benefit from stronger contextualization and more precise comparisons to existing methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions — require a proof/citation for the representer theorem, stronger comparisons to Nystrom and Fisher kernels, and better literature contextualization.
