## Review of “Learning with Fredholm Kernels”

This paper introduces a novel semi-supervised learning framework based on reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, leading to the definition of “Fredholm kernels.” The approach elegantly extends kernel methods to incorporate unlabeled data, and the authors provide a strong theoretical analysis alongside compelling experimental results demonstrating the method’s effectiveness. The central idea of constructing data-dependent kernels via a discretized Fredholm integral is a significant contribution, and the paper positions itself well within the broader landscape of semi-supervised learning, specifically drawing connections to manifold regularization and noise modeling. 

The paper’s strengths lie in its technical depth and clarity. The derivation of the Fredholm kernels and the associated Representer Theorem solution are well-explained, and the discussion of the “noise assumption” provides a valuable perspective on when and why incorporating unlabeled data is beneficial. The extensive experimental validation, across both synthetic and real-world datasets, convincingly demonstrates the performance gains achieved by the proposed method. The authors also acknowledge related work, particularly the NIPS paper by Que and Belkin, and clearly articulate the differences and novel contributions of their work. 

However, several points require attention. Firstly, the validity of Equation 3 is questioned when the associated kernel is not positive semi-definite (PSD). A clarification or proof regarding this condition is crucial. Secondly, the experimental protocol lacks sufficient detail. The notion of “optimal” parameters is used without a clear definition of the optimization process – were grid searches used? What metrics were optimized? Furthermore, the paper suffers from several typos and minor writing errors (as noted in the provided list - lines 82, 83, Section 4 title, lines 247, 283, 352). While minor, these detract from the overall polish.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound framework for semi-supervised learning.
*   Strong experimental results across diverse datasets.
*   Clear connection to existing literature and a well-defined contribution.
*   Elegant mathematical formulation and tractable computational solution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of proof/clarification for Equation 3 when kernel is not PSD.
*   Insufficient detail regarding the experimental setup and parameter optimization.
*   Presence of typos and minor writing errors.



Overall, this is a high-quality paper with significant potential. Addressing the aforementioned concerns, particularly regarding the validity of Equation 3 and providing greater clarity in the experimental methodology, would strengthen the manuscript and warrant strong acceptance. I recommend acceptance contingent upon these revisions.
