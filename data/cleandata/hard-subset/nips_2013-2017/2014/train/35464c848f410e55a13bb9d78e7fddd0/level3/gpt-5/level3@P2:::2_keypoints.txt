- Paper reformulates supervised/semi-supervised learning as solving a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” k̂F built from inner kernel kH and outer kernel k.  
- The authors introduce a “noise assumption” that low-variance directions under pX are label-uninformative and analyze its implications for semi-supervised learning.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 2–4) show soft-thresholding PCA behavior for linear k and variance reduction/unbiasedness for linear and Gaussian target kernels under Gaussian models with infinite unlabeled data.  
- Empirical results on synthetic (violating cluster assumptions) and real text/digit datasets show competitive or improved performance vs RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC.  
- Strength: elegant, principled reduction to a PSD data-dependent kernel (Prop. 1) that incorporates unlabeled density into the hypothesis class.  
- Strength: clear mechanism leveraging unlabeled data via the empirical operator Kp̂X producing k̂F.  
- Strength: the noise-assumption provides a useful, intuitively geometric taxonomy orthogonal to cluster/manifold assumptions.  
- Strength: clean, interpretable Gaussian-model theory (PCA soft-thresholding and variance-reduction results).  
- Weakness: Missing representer-theorem proof or precise reference for Eq. (3); need assumptions on H, k, and Kp̂X (boundedness/compactness).  
- Weakness: Prop. 1 needs a provided proof (or inclusion in main text) and conditions for normalization preserving PSD.  
- Weakness: Theory assumes infinite unlabeled data; no finite-sample bound for replacing KpX with Kp̂X (operator norm or excess risk) and missing l,u trade-off analysis.  
- Weakness: Noise-assumption results are limited to Gaussian additive orthogonal noise; robustness to non-Gaussian tails, multimodality, or manifold structure unclear.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity not discussed — building k̂F and KH is O((l+u)^2) and solving systems is costly; no discussion of Nyström/low-rank approximations.  
- Clarity issue: Paper relies on a “full version” for key derivations; essential proofs and a self-contained algorithm description should be included.  
- Clarity issue: Inductive vs transductive deployment unclear — how to handle new test points or changing unlabeled data?  
- Notation issue: Need explicit PSD/symmetry requirements on k vs kH, clear matrix definitions in Eq. (3), and consistent normalization between Eq. (4) and Eq. (5).  
- Originality: Composition k̂F = k • kH • k and noise-suppression analysis appear novel, but clearer differentiation from manifold-regularization/cluster kernels, Nyström/kernel PCA, and integral-operator/Tikhonov approaches is needed.  
- Significance: Method is broadly useful for injecting unlabeled density and shows promising gains, especially when cluster assumptions fail.  
- To increase impact: add stronger baselines (label propagation, consistency regularization, recent data-dependent kernels), report variances/std errors, ablations (normalized vs unnormalized, k vs kH), and runtimes.  
- Specific question: Can the authors provide learning-rate bounds accounting for both labeled and unlabeled sample sizes via operator concentration?  
- Specific question: How sensitive is performance to mis-specification of k and kH and guidance beyond cross-validation for choosing them?  
- Specific question: Is k̂F interpretable as convolution/smoothing of kH w.r.t. pX or as preconditioning/composition of integral operators K and KH?  
- Specific question: How does Eq. (2) relate to operator-equation approaches in SVM/KRR and what is genuinely new?  
- Pros summary: principled semi-supervised kernel construction, theoretical variance reduction, competitive empirical performance, conceptually orthogonal to cluster/manifold assumptions.  
- Cons summary: missing representer-theorem proof and finite-sample unlabeled analysis, limited comparisons to related data-dependent kernels and operator approximations, scalability/deployment details absent.  
- Minor: compare to Fisher/probability-product kernels and Nyström/kernel PCA; cite prior operator-equation kernel methods; fix typos (e.g., “Not that”→“Note that”, “achived”→“achieved”, “chossing”→“choosing”, Table ??) and complete incomplete references.  
- Overall recommendation: Interesting and promising; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying representer/PSD arguments, adding finite-sample analysis or discussion, strengthening positioning and experiments; reviewer confidence medium.
