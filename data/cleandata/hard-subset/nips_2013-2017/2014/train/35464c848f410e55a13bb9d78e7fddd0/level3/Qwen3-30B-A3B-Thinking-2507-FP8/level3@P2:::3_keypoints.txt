- Introduces a novel supervised and semi-supervised framework using regularized Fredholm integral equations and data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Proposes a new "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning, distinct from manifold and cluster assumptions.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorems 2–4) shows Fredholm kernels can suppress noise and provide a principled way to use unlabeled data.  
- Empirical validation shows competitive performance on synthetic tests designed for the noise assumption and on multiple real-world datasets.  
- Clearly connects to prior work (Que & Belkin 2012, kernel mean embeddings) and positions the contribution relative to manifold regularization.  
- Technically sound approach with well-designed experiments comparing against strong baselines.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear mathematical derivations and effective figures (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Provides sufficient detail for reproduction, though computational complexity is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Originality: meaningful new perspective via the noise assumption and use of Fredholm integral equations to build data-dependent kernels.  
- Significance: promising new method to leverage unlabeled data, particularly when the noise assumption holds, with empirical improvements shown.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical framework, strong theoretical results, convincing empirical evidence, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: lacks systematic guidance for selecting Fredholm kernel width and would benefit from experiments showing failure cases.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
