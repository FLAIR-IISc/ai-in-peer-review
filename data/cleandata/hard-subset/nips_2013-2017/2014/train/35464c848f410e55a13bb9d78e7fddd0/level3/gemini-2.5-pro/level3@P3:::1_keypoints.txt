- Introduces a novel SSL framework reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation that incorporates unlabeled data via a discretized integral operator.  
- Proposes a new data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that leverages the distribution of unlabeled points.  
- Introduces a "noise assumption" where unlabeled data help identify and suppress uninformative variance directions, distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions.  
- Connects to prior Fredholm use in density ratio estimation but novelly applies it to general SSL with theoretical and empirical development.  
- Strong originality and significance: creative framework with potential practical and research impact.  
- High technical quality: theoretical analysis (Theorems 2–4) shows variance reduction and a soft-thresholding PCA interpretation.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation: extensive experiments on synthetic, text, and image datasets with competitive or superior performance vs. strong baselines.  
- Clear and well-organized writing that builds intuition and supports claims with theory and experiments.  
- Missing derivation for Equation 3 (Representer Theorem step) — needs at least a sketch or appendix for correctness and reproducibility.  
- Unclear hyperparameter selection protocol — clarify validation set creation and optimization metric for reproducibility.  
- Several minor typographical errors noted that should be corrected in a final proofreading pass.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper, accept with requested clarifications on derivation and experimental details.
