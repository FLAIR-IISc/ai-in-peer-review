- Proposal: semi-supervised framework reformulating supervised prediction as a regularized Fredholm integral equation that induces a data-dependent "Fredholm" kernel incorporating unlabeled data.  
- Algorithm: Fredholm kernel can be implemented as a standard kernel method (with a normalized variant) and is PSD when the inner kernel is PSD.  
- Noise assumption: low-variance directions around each point are uninformative and should be suppressed.  
- Theoretical result 1: linear outer + Gaussian inner kernel yields a soft-thresholded PCA inner product that downweights low-variance directions.  
- Theoretical result 2: under a Gaussian subspace-with-noise model, Fredholm kernels give unbiased estimators of the clean kernel with reduced variance versus noisy inputs.  
- Empirics: strong performance on synthetic noise-focused tasks and real datasets (text/sentiment with linear kernels; digits with Gaussian kernels) compared to RLSC, TSVM, LapRLSC.  
- Relation to prior work: connects to Fredholm equations for density ratio, kernel mean embeddings, RKHS shrinkage, manifold regularization, and classical SSL methods.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition and principled derivation from integral equations to an implementable kernel.  
- Strength: conceptually appealing PSD, data-dependent kernel with a simple matrix form integrating unlabeled data.  
- Strength: well-motivated theoretical guarantees (PSD, PCA interpretation, variance-reduction proofs).  
- Strength: solid empirical evidence showing noise-robustness and competitive performance.  
- Strength: compatible with standard kernel tooling and normalized variant improves numerical stability.  
- Weakness: theory uses restrictive assumptions (Gaussianity, orthogonal additive noise, infinite unlabeled asymptotics) with unclear sensitivity to real-world deviations.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter dependence (inner/outer kernels and widths); limited principled guidance beyond cross-validation.  
- Weakness: computational scalability concerns—KF requires O((l+u)^2) evaluations and solving a system of size l for large u.  
- Weakness: lack of generalization guarantees (e.g., excess-risk or stability bounds) for the induced predictor.  
- Weakness: unclear role and impact of non-PSD outer kernels and pre-normalization numerical effects.  
- Weakness: relation to other data-dependent kernels (e.g., prior graph-based constructions) could be more fully clarified.  
- Suggestion: add failure-case experiments where the noise assumption fails, cluster assumptions hold, or unlabeled marginal is mismatched.  
- Suggestion: provide practical hyperparameter guidance or ablations (bandwidth sensitivity, normalization effects).  
- Suggestion: discuss and empirically demonstrate scalable approximations (Nyström, random features) that preserve PSD/theory if possible.  
- Suggestion: include brief comparisons to label propagation/graph-based methods in cluster-favorable settings.  
- NIPS-quality: technically sound derivations and experiments, but theory assumptions are strong and scalability discussion is limited.  
- NIPS-clarity: very clearly written; Fredholm-to-kernel derivation and inner/outer roles are easy to follow.  
- NIPS-originality: novel Fredholm-equation-based SSL kernel construction distinct from prior density-ratio and manifold approaches.  
- NIPS-significance: provides a practical, conceptually clean way to leverage unlabeled data for noise suppression and variance reduction.  
- Reasons to accept: elegant integration of unlabeled data via a PSD data-dependent kernel, matching theoretical insights, and strong empirical results.  
- Reasons to reject: restrictive theoretical assumptions, limited hyperparameter guidance, and insufficient scalability discussion.  
- Questions for authors: can inner/outer bandwidths be selected systematically using unlabeled data (e.g., density-adaptive rules or spectral criteria)?  
- Questions for authors: how does the method perform with multimodal marginals or label-conditional heteroskedastic noise?  
- Questions for authors: provide complexity/memory analysis and practical approximations that preserve PSD and theoretical properties?  
- Overall assessment: interesting, clearly written work with solid theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance pending expanded discussion of assumptions, hyperparameter guidance, and scalability.
