1. Proposes a novel supervised/semi-supervised framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.  
2. Defines a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" (outer + inner kernels) that incorporates unlabeled data into the kernel itself.  
3. Introduces the "noise assumption": low-variance local directions are uninformative for classification, as an alternative to cluster/manifold assumptions.  
4. Shows for a linear outer kernel the Fredholm kernel performs soft re-weighting of principal components (soft thresholding PCA).  
5. Proves for linear and Gaussian target kernels that, under a data+noise model, the Fredholm kernel yields variance-reduced estimates of the true noiseless kernel.  
6. Connects to prior work on Fredholm equations, density ratio estimation, covariate shift, and data-dependent kernels/manifold regularization.  
7. Empirical validation is thorough: synthetic datasets (satisfying noise but violating cluster assumptions) and diverse real text/image datasets show strong performance versus SSL baselines.  
8. Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, derivation, and linkage between theory and method.  
9. Weakness: limited practical guidance on selecting kernel width t and regularization λ given reliance on large unlabeled data; sensitivity and cross-validation robustness need discussion.  
10. Weakness: lacks an explicit failure-case experiment illustrating when the noise assumption is violated (e.g., low-variance directions are informative).  
11. Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel, theoretically sound work with strong empirical support; minor revisions on practical guidance and limitations suggested; recommend acceptance.
