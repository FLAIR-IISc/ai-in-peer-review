- Introduces "Fredholm kernels": a reformulation of learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation yielding data-dependent kernels that incorporate unlabeled data.
- Proposes the "noise assumption": low-variance directions in unlabeled data correspond to label noise and can be suppressed.
- Derives the Fredholm kernel from integral operator theory, connecting it rigorously to kernel methods.
- Theorem 2: Fredholm kernels suppress noise by reweighting principal components.
- Theorems 3–4: Fredholm kernels give more precise kernel estimates than traditional kernels under noisy conditions.
- Positions the approach as complementary to existing semi-supervised methods and distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions.
- Strong experimental support on synthetic data designed for the noise assumption.
- Strong empirical results on real datasets (20 Newsgroups, WebKB, IMDB, Twitter sentiment, digit recognition).
- Well-written and well-organized presentation that makes complex math accessible.
- Clear experimental comparisons against relevant baselines, showing competitive state-of-the-art performance.
- Interesting connections to prior work in kernel methods and density estimation.
- Strength: novel theoretical framework that naturally integrates unlabeled data.
- Strength: clear theoretical justification and compelling experimental validation.
- Weakness: no systematic procedure for selecting the Fredholm kernel width (cross-validation not fully specified).
- Weakness: lacks toy experiments showing failure cases and limitations.
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity compared to standard kernel methods.
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, mathematically sound, and empirically effective contribution to semi-supervised learning.
