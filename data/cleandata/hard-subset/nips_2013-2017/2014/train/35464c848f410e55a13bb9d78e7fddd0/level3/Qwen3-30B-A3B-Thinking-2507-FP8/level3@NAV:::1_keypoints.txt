- Introduces a novel semi-supervised learning framework using discretized Fredholm integral equations to incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Proposes "Fredholm kernels" — data-dependent kernels designed to leverage unlabeled data under a new "noise assumption."  
- Framework naturally integrates labeled and unlabeled samples and differentiates from manifold regularization and cluster assumptions.  
- Theoretical analysis (Proposition 1, Theorems 2–4) shows Fredholm kernels can suppress noise and reduce variance in high-dimensional feature spaces.  
- Connects to prior kernel-method work ([10], [13]) while clearly positioning its distinct approach.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets (20 Newsgroups, WebKB, IMDB, MNIST) show improved performance over baseline semi-supervised methods, especially with few labels.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making complex ideas accessible.  
- Critical issue: validity of Equation 3 for non-PSD kernels is not addressed; a derivation or proof is missing.  
- Critical issue: experimental protocol lacks clarity on how "optimal" parameters were selected, harming reproducibility.  
- Several minor typos and writing errors need correction (Lines 82, 83, Section 4 title, Lines 247, 283, 352).  
- Work demonstrates strong originality and practical significance; addressing the theoretical and methodological concerns would substantially strengthen the paper.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with significant potential impact in semi-supervised learning.
