Proposes a novel structured spike-and-slab prior that encodes sparsity patterns using generic covariance functions.  
Provides a Bayesian inference scheme based on expectation propagation for the proposed model.  
Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the methodology easy to follow.  
Numerical experiments on synthetic data show improved performance over LARS and BG-AMP.  
Detailed description of the expectation propagation algorithm aids implementation.  
Strengths: novel prior formulation, clarity of presentation, and thorough numerical evaluation.  
Weakness: no systematic method for selecting the Fredholm kernel width; cross-validation is suggested but not explored.  
Weakness: limited toy experiments to demonstrate limitations and performance across different settings.  
Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.  
Originality is high; the approach is a novel combination of established techniques with proper citations.  
Significance is high; the work advances the state of the art in sparse signal recovery.  
Overall recommendation: accept—the strengths outweigh the weaknesses.
