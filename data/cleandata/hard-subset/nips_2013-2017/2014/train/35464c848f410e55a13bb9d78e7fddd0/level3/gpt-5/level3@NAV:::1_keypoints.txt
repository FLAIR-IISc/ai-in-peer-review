- Proposes a semi‑supervised framework reformulating prediction as a regularized Fredholm integral equation that yields a data‑dependent “Fredholm kernel” incorporating unlabeled data via a discretized operator.  
- Learning occurs in an RKHS H with an “inner” PSD kernel kH while an “outer” kernel k (using unlabeled samples) defines the Fredholm operator, producing an equivalent classifier using a new PSD kernel k̂F.  
- Theoretical analysis addresses a “noise assumption” (low‑variance directions uninformative) and proves Fredholm kernels suppress low‑variance components (linear case) and reduce estimator variance (linear and Gaussian cases).  
- Empirically competitive with strong semi‑supervised baselines on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Well positioned relative to manifold/cluster assumptions and data‑dependent kernels (Belkin et al., Sindhwani et al.) and related to Fredholm density ratio estimation (Que & Belkin).  
- Connects to kernel mean shrinkage via a shrinkage/variance‑reduction perspective but is distinct in formulating semi‑supervised prediction through Fredholm operators and producing a practical PSD data‑dependent kernel.  
- Technical development is solid and insightful; theorems are clear and align with the claimed noise assumption.  
- Reformulation to a PSD k̂F (given PSD kH) is compelling and computationally tractable.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; main ideas are easy to follow, but minor typos and labeling errors need fixing (specific line/section references provided).  
- Originality: extends kernel‑based optimization to a Fredholm setting with unlabeled data and novel variance‑reduction analysis relative to prior work.  
- Significance: strong results across multiple datasets and offers a broadly applicable mechanism to leverage unlabeled data with standard kernel solvers.  
- Request clarification: provide a careful derivation and required conditions for Equation (3) when the “outer” kernel k is not PSD, since representer arguments use H induced by kH but Eq. (3) uses Kl+u from k.  
- Request experimental details: specify how “optimal” parameters were chosen (search grids, validation splits, criterion), number of trials/seeds, error bars, computational costs, and sensitivity to u and kernel bandwidth(s).  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant semi‑supervised framework with strong theory and results; clear novelty and relation to prior work; practical PSD data‑dependent kernel with tractable implementation.  
- Cons against acceptance: missing derivation/details for Eq. (3) under non‑PSD k and insufficiently specified experimental selection criteria and reproducibility details.
