This paper proposes learning via a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” that naturally incorporates unlabeled data. The key idea is to learn f in an RKHS H, but apply an integral operator KpX built from the marginal distribution before fitting to labels, which induces a new PSD kernel k̂F(x,z)=∑i,j k(x,xi) kH(xi,xj) k(z,xj)/(l+u)2. The authors analyze semi-supervised benefits under a “noise assumption,” show soft-thresholding of low-variance directions in a linear/Gaussian setting, and provide variance-reduction results for approximating target kernels under Gaussian models. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets indicate competitive performance against RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLS.

Strengths
- Conceptually interesting operator-theoretic view that unifies supervised and semi-supervised learning via data-dependent kernels.
- Clear PSD construction (KF=K_l+u KH K_l+u^T) and useful “soft-thresholding PCA” intuition.
- Theoretical variance reduction results under explicit generative assumptions.
- Empirical improvements on noise-sensitive and some real tasks.

Weaknesses and requests for clarification
- Relation to classical integral-equation approximations (Nyström) and kernel PCA is not discussed. Please compare: (i) Nyström’s approximation of integral operators and how Kp̂X relates, and (ii) similarities/differences to kernel PCA’s integral-operator viewpoint and to diffusion/double-kernel constructions.
- Operator-equation approaches in SVM/kernel literature should be cited and positioned (e.g., regularization networks/Tikhonov with general regularization operators, representer theorem with operator penalties). Please explain precisely how Eq. 2 fits or differs.
- Representer theorem: you invoke it for Eq. 2; please either prove it under your operator Kp̂X (assumptions on boundedness/compactness and PSD structure) or give a precise reference and show Eq. 2 is a special case, including conditions on k, kH, and p̂X.
- The “soft-thresholding PCA” view is appealing. Please compare to Fisher kernels and probability product kernels (and related convolution/diffusion kernels): what is shared (data/model dependence, kernel composition) and what is fundamentally different?
- Scalability: computing KF=K_l+u KH K_l+u^T is O(l(l+u)^2) in time and O(l(l+u)) memory; please discuss complexity, sparsification, or Nyström-style approximations for u large, and sensitivity to t/normalization.

Quality: Sound under stated assumptions; more rigorous representer justification is needed.
Clarity: Generally clear; please fix typos: line 165 “Not that”→“Note that”; line 221 “achived”→“achieved”; line 341 “chossing”→“choosing”; line 672 “Table ??”.
Originality: Novel and well-motivated composition yielding a data-dependent kernel; clarify novelty vs Nyström/kernel PCA/diffusion kernels.
Significance: Potentially useful semi-supervised mechanism; practical impact hinges on scalability and broader empirical validation.

Arguments for acceptance: novel operator-kernel perspective, solid intuition and theory, promising results. Arguments against: incomplete positioning to operator/Nyström literature, representer theorem gap, limited scalability discussion.
