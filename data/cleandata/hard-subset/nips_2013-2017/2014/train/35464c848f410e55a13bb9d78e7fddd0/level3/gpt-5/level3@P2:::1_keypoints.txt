- Proposes a semi-supervised framework reformulating supervised risk as a regularized Fredholm integral and predicting via a data-dependent “Fredholm” kernel constructed from labeled and unlabeled data.
- Derives a closed-form representer-style solution equivalent to a standard kernel method using the Fredholm kernel k̂F and a normalized variant.
- Motivates semi-supervised gains with a “noise assumption”: low-variance directions under pX are label-uninformative and should be suppressed.
- Theory (linear outer kernel) shows the Fredholm kernel induces a soft-thresholding effect on small-variance principal components.
- Under a Gaussian manifold+noise model, Fredholm kernels give unbiased (up to scaling) but lower-variance estimators than applying the target kernel to noisy observations.
- Empirically strong on synthetic tasks that violate the cluster assumption and competitive or better than RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC on real datasets (text, sentiment, digits).
- Relates to manifold regularization, mean embeddings, density-ratio/Fredholm methods, and data-dependent kernels but is distinct in formulation and noise-suppression perspective.
- Proposition 1 (PSD of Fredholm kernel when kH is PSD) is important and should be moved into the main paper.
- Major technical concern: need a precise derivation of Eqn. (3) and representer solution when the outer kernel k is non-PSD or non-symmetric, plus conditions for convexity and invertibility.
- Theory is insightful but relies on stylized assumptions (Gaussian noise, infinite unlabeled data); finite-sample bounds/discussion would strengthen it.
- Experiments cover diverse settings and show consistent gains, especially with added noise.
- Experimental protocol is under-specified: parameter selection, split strategy, use of unlabeled data in validation, and fairness across baselines need clarification.
- Clarify usage and effects of unnormalized vs. normalized Fredholm kernels and relevant scaling constants in theorems.
- Include a consolidated algorithm box to help practitioners reproduce the method.
- Scalability concern: constructing KH and KF costs O((l+u)^2) memory and up to O((l+u)^3) time; discuss approximations (Nyström, random features, block methods).
- Provide training/prediction time and memory vs. l and u, and guidance for large-u scenarios.
- Request full answers to specific experimental questions: parameter grids, validation protocol, number of splits, seeds, implementations and tuning for TSVM/LapRLSC, and whether unlabeled data include test inputs (inductive vs transductive).
- Ask for practical guidance on when to prefer the normalized Fredholm kernel and stability issues with narrow Gaussian k.
- Question whether non-symmetric k is viable in practice and whether it causes empirical issues.
- Minor typos and wording fixes are needed throughout (several examples provided).
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on addressing derivation/convexity details and clarifying experimental protocol.
