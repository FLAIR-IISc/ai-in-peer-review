- Proposes Fredholm kernels by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation for supervised and semi‑supervised learning.  
- Naturally incorporates unlabeled data and offers a new perspective within kernel methods.  
- Builds on prior integral-operator and kernel mean embedding work and relates to data‑dependent kernels for manifold regularization.  
- Strong theoretical grounding, focusing on the “noise assumption” distinct from manifold/cluster assumptions.  
- Shows unlabeled data can reduce variance in estimation, analogous to shrinkage estimators.  
- Section 4 provides compelling theoretical results on noise suppression and improved estimates under specific conditions.  
- Empirical validation uses synthetic and real datasets and compares favorably to RLSC, Transductive SVM, and LapRLSC.  
- Experiments varying labeled data and added noise support the theoretical claims; presentation and organization are clear.  
- Practical weakness: insufficient discussion of systematic choices for the kernel width parameter (t or r), beyond cross‑validation.  
- Methodological weakness: absence of failure‑case experiments or toy examples illustrating limitations and applicability boundaries.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, strong empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of parameter‑selection guidance and missing experiments demonstrating failure scenarios.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
