- Proposes an SSL framework that builds a PSD, data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" k̂F by composing an outer kernel k with an inner RKHS kernel kH via an integral operator approximated from unlabeled data.  
- Motivates SSL via a new "noise assumption" complementary to cluster/manifold assumptions and shows Fredholm kernels can suppress low-variance directions (soft-PCA) and reduce variance under latent Gaussian models.  
- Theoretical contributions are solid: Proposition 1 proves PSD of k̂F; Theorems 2–4 give PCA-like shrinkage and variance-reduction/unbiasedness results.  
- Eq. (3) and representer-theorem usage need a clear derivation and explicit assumptions: KH must be PSD and symmetric, evaluation functionals bounded; k need not be PSD.  
- Provide the matrix identity derivation (push-through/Woodbury) and state that convexity requires KH PSD and λ>0; clarify that k can be indefinite/asymmetric.  
- Experiments are broad and show gains where the noise assumption holds, but the hyperparameter/validation protocol is under-specified.  
- Specify which hyperparameters were tuned per method, grid ranges, whether tuning used only labeled CV or a held-out labeled validation set, and whether unlabeled labels were used.  
- Report number of random splits/seeds and provide mean ± std; ensure all baselines had equal tuning budgets and only standard access to unlabeled data.  
- Complexity/scalability concerns: forming KF costs O((l+u)^2) memory and naive solves O((l+u)^3); discuss practical complexity and use of low-rank/Nyström/random features and sensitivity to u.  
- Clarity is generally good; move a concise Eq. (3) derivation into main text, add an algorithm box (inputs, KF construction, training), and include a complexity discussion.  
- List of minor typos and wording fixes provided for correction.  
- Novelty: distinct Fredholm-based construction producing a PSD, data-dependent kernel for general losses and a new noise-assumption perspective.  
- Significance: broadly applicable when unlabeled data is plentiful, integrates into standard kernel pipelines, and provides useful theoretical insights for SSL under noise.  
- Ask authors to add formal derivation/conditions for Eq. (3), discuss pathological k where boundedness fails, and clarify when normalization (Eq. (5)) is preferable and sensitive to kernel widths.  
- Request sensitivity analyses for performance/compute as u varies, behavior under mild distribution shift of unlabeled data, and an ablation replacing unlabeled data with noise.  
- Pros: elegant Fredholm-to-kernel reduction, strong relevant theory, empirical effectiveness across domains, clear distinction from related methods.  
- Cons: missing derivation/assumptions for Eq. (3), incomplete hyperparameter/validation documentation, limited scalability discussion, and several typos.  
- Recommendation: lean accept contingent on adding Eq. (3) derivation, transparent experimental protocol, complexity discussion, and minor edits.
