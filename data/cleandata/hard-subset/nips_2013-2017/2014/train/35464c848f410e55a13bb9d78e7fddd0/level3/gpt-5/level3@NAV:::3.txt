This paper reformulates supervised and semi-supervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent “Fredholm” kernel that naturally incorporates unlabeled data via an integral operator estimated from the marginal pX. The authors show that the resulting predictor is equivalent to a standard kernel method using a PSD, unlabeled-data–dependent kernel matrix, and relate the approach to prior work on integral operators and kernel mean embeddings, density-ratio estimation via Fredholm equations (Que & Belkin, NIPS), and data-dependent kernels/manifold regularization (Belkin, Niyogi, Sindhwani). The “noise assumption” is introduced as an alternative to cluster/manifold assumptions, with theory demonstrating variance reduction and soft-thresholding of low-variance directions (linear outer kernel) and better approximation of a target kernel under Gaussian noise (Gaussian inner/outer kernels). Empirically, the method compares favorably to RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC on synthetic and several real datasets.

Quality: The paper is technically sound. The representer-form-based derivation, PSD property, and analyses under Gaussian models are clear and plausible; the variance reduction results are particularly compelling. Experiments are broad and generally controlled. Some practical aspects deserve deeper treatment: bandwidth/regularization selection, computational cost with large unlabeled sets, and sensitivity when the noise assumption is violated.

Clarity: The paper is very well written and well organized. The reformulation as a kernel method and the inner/outer kernel roles are clearly explained. Including pseudocode and complexity analysis would further aid reproducibility.

Originality: Using a Fredholm operator to induce a PSD data-dependent kernel for semi-supervised learning is a neat and principled contribution that differs from graph-Laplacian regularization and prior Fredholm uses for density ratios. The “noise assumption” provides a fresh lens distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions.

Significance: The method advances semi-supervised learning by offering a simple drop-in kernel that leverages unlabeled data with theoretical support and competitive performance. It is likely to be adopted and extended.

Questions and suggestions:
- Is there a systematic way to select the Fredholm kernel width (for both inner and outer kernels) beyond grid search—e.g., cross-validation using labeled data only, marginal-likelihood surrogates, or bilevel optimization under the noise model?
- Please report sensitivity to u and provide scalable approximations (Nyström, random features) and time/memory costs.
- Add toy failure cases where the noise assumption is violated (e.g., anisotropic class-conditional structure, multimodality) to delineate scope.
- Guidance on when to prefer normalized vs unnormalized variants would be useful.

Pros: clear, principled, theoretically grounded, empirically strong. Cons: hyperparameter guidance and scalability details are limited; missing failure-mode analyses.

Overall: Interesting and well-executed; I recommend acceptance.
