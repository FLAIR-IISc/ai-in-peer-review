Summary
The paper proposes a semi-supervised learning framework that casts supervised/unsupervised learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation of the first kind. Using an integral operator KpX built from an “outer” kernel k and the marginal pX (approximated by unlabeled data), the method minimizes squared loss on labeled data with RKHS regularization on f, but predicts via KpX f. By applying a representer-theorem-style reduction, the authors show that the approach is equivalent to kernel ridge regression with a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” kF(x, z) that combines k, an “inner” kernel kH, and unlabeled samples. They provide a normalized variant for stability. A “noise assumption” is introduced: locally low-variance directions are uninformative for labels. Theoretically, under Gaussian models, they claim: (i) with linear outer kernel, kF induces an inner product that downweights low-variance directions (“soft-thresholding”); (ii) with linear or Gaussian target kernels and additive orthogonal Gaussian noise, kF yields unbiased (up to scaling) estimates of the clean target kernel with lower variance than computing the target kernel on noisy observations. Experiments on synthetic data (violating cluster assumption) and several text and vision datasets suggest improvements over RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC.

Relation to prior work
The formulation sits close to integral operators in RKHS, kernel mean embeddings, conditional mean embeddings, and data-dependent/diffusion kernels used in manifold regularization. The construction kF = K KHK⊤ with K built from k on labeled/unlabeled points is a specific instantiation of smoothing kH by the unlabeled-data geometry (akin to diffusion/data-dependent kernels). The connection to [Que & Belkin, NIPS’09] on Fredholm equations for density ratio estimation is noted. The paper would benefit from a clearer positioning versus diffusion kernels, Nyström/data-dependent kernels for SSL, and conditional mean embedding regressors.

Strengths
- Simple, unifying construction that turns unlabeled data into a PSD, data-dependent kernel usable by standard kernel methods.
- Clear intuition for noise suppression and how unlabeled geometry can denoise classifiers without relying on cluster assumption.
- Theoretical variance-reduction results under Gaussian models; linear case insight via induced inner product x⊤ΣF z.
- Empirically promising on synthetic and several real datasets.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and clarity
- Representer theorem/closed form: Eq. (3) relies on an identity (KTK KH + λI)−1 KT = KT (K KH KT + λI)−1 which is not generally valid without conditions (e.g., symmetry/commutation or reweighting with M1/2). Please provide a correct derivation (e.g., via normal equations) and state required assumptions. Dimensions and symmetry should be checked carefully.
- Notation conflict: λ is used as the regularization parameter in Eq. (2) and also as the standard deviation of the “signal” in Theorems 3–4. This is confusing and may invalidate statements like “when λ > σ.” Use distinct symbols.
- Scaling inconsistencies: Eq. (2) uses “l ∑” rather than (1/l)∑; Eq. (4) uses 1/(l+u)2 but the scaling in c*(x) and α = (KF+λI)−1y seems to drop factors. Please standardize all normalizations (including the normalized variant Eq. (5)) and state how scaling interacts with regularization.
- Normalized kernel PSD claim: For Eq. (5), the PSD claim is “easy to check,” but a brief argument (e.g., kNF(x,z)=α(x)⊤KH α(z)) should be included. Also clarify symmetry when k is not symmetric.
- Theorem 2: The stated “rescaling factor” includes a square root that seems inconsistent with ΣF as written and ignores the global product factor; please correct. Also ensure parameterizations of Gaussian kernels (denominator t vs 2r) are consistent across sections.
- Theorems 3–4: Precise assumptions and constants c1, c2 and the mapping from r to t (cubic equation) should be fully derived. The condition “λ > σ” (with the symbol conflict) needs justification and discussion of tightness. Proofs are deferred to the “full version”; however, NeurIPS requires sufficient detail to verify main claims.

Novelty and positioning
- The proposed kF is closely related to data-dependent/diffusion kernels, kernel smoothing of kernels, and conditional mean embedding style regressors. The paper should more crisply articulate what is genuinely new (beyond a particular two-stage smoothing) and compare theoretically/empirically to diffusion kernels/data-dependent kernels (e.g., Sindhwani et al., 2005; diffusion maps; graph diffusion kernels), not just LapRLSC.

Experimental details and rigor
- Many critical details are missing: exact numbers of labeled/unlabeled samples per dataset; parameter search ranges and selection; whether results include mean±std over multiple splits; computational costs and memory; runtime vs baselines. Tables/figures are referenced but not included; error bars absent.
- Baselines: Consider including label propagation/diffusion kernel machines, graph-based kernel ridge with data-dependent kernels, and strong modern semi-supervised baselines for images (if applicable).
- Potential bias: Using unlabeled data to build kernels and a labeled validation set is fine, but please clarify protocol to avoid test leakage. Also specify whether kF at test time uses only training unlabeled data.
- Scalability: Computing KH and K on l+u points is O((l+u)2) memory and O((l+u)3) time if not approximated; no discussion of Nyström/approximate schemes for scalability.

Presentation and editorial issues
- Numerous typos/grammar: “distint,” “senario,” “kernle,” “unormalized,” “on on,” etc. Footnote markers appear without content. Some symbols (L2 vs L2(X,p)) and measures are informal.
- References are incomplete and inconsistent (missing years/venues, author names misspelled, duplicated authors, missing titles). Please fix to NeurIPS style.
- Several statements defer key material to a “full version,” which is not acceptable for evaluation.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually clean, practical way to inject unlabeled geometry into kernels; potentially useful and easy to adopt.
- Intuitive and plausible noise-suppression mechanism; some theory backing.
- Promising empirical results on varied tasks.

Cons for acceptance
- Technical derivations incomplete or possibly incorrect (matrix identity, scaling, Theorem 2 details).
- Novelty relative to existing data-dependent/diffusion kernel literature is not convincingly established.
- Experimental section lacks crucial details, stronger baselines, and scalability analysis.
- Significant notation/editorial issues hinder verification and reproducibility.

Questions for authors
1) Provide a rigorous derivation of Eq. (3) and the identity relating (KTK KH + λI)−1KT and KT(K KHK⊤ + λI)−1. Under what conditions does it hold?
2) Clarify all scalings and normalizations in Eqns (2)–(5), and how they affect α and λ.
3) Resolve the λ notation conflict (regularization vs signal variance) and restate Theorems 3–4 precisely with complete proofs in the paper.
4) How does kF relate precisely to diffusion/data-dependent kernels? Can you provide comparisons?
5) Provide full experimental protocols, statistics across splits, and complexity/runtime. How does the method scale to large u?
6) Can the method be extended to other losses (hinge/logistic) with proofs, not just a statement?

Recommendation
Reject (borderline). The idea is interesting and could be impactful, but the paper in its current form has too many technical, clarity, and completeness issues to meet NeurIPS standards. A revised version addressing the derivations, positioning, experiments, and presentation could be much stronger.
