- Proposes a novel supervised/unsupervised learning framework based on solving Fredholm integral equations of the first kind.  
- Introduces a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" that naturally incorporates unlabeled data.  
- Proposes a new "noise assumption": low-variance directions in unlabeled data are uninformative for classification.  
- Theoretically shows Fredholm kernels can suppress such noise via variance reduction (Theorems 2–4).  
- Mathematical framework is sound and proofs appear correct, but theory assumes infinite unlabeled data (unrealistic).  
- Empirically strong improvements on synthetic, noise-designed datasets that violate the cluster assumption.  
- Real-world experiments show only modest improvements and lack analysis of dataset-specific behavior.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; notation and key concepts are clearly defined.  
- Needs clearer distinction between the noise assumption and manifold/cluster assumptions, and a better illustration (Figure 1 insufficient).  
- Could better explain connection between Fredholm kernels and standard kernel methods for non-expert readers.  
- Core contribution and noise assumption are novel and clearly differentiated from prior related work ([10], [1], [9]).  
- Practical significance is limited by lack of evidence that the noise assumption commonly holds in real data.  
- Computational complexity and scalability with large unlabeled sets are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Overall: interesting and theoretically solid contribution with limited practical evidence; recommended accept (borderline) for its novel theoretical perspective.
