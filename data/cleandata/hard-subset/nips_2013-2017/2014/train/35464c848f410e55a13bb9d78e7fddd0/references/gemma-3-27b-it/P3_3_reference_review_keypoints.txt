- Proposes a novel learning framework formulating supervised and semi-supervised learning as Fredholm integral equations, yielding data-dependent "Fredholm kernels."
- Shows theoretically that Fredholm kernels can reduce variance under a “noise assumption” and connects to soft-thresholding PCA in the linear case.
- Builds on prior work in kernel methods, integral operators, density ratio estimation, kernel mean embeddings, and relates to manifold regularization and shrinkage estimators.
- Strength: Offers a fresh, unified framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning with clear derivation and easy implementation via existing kernel methods.
- Strength: Provides theoretical variance-reduction results and insightful connections to known methods (e.g., PCA shrinkage).
- Strength: Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets support effectiveness, especially when the noise assumption holds, and demonstrate robustness to noise.
- Strength: Paper is well-written and logically organized, with clear derivations of the Fredholm kernel.
- Weakness: The generality and real-world prevalence of the “noise assumption” are not thoroughly explored; limitations need discussion.
- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability (notably the matrix inversion in Equation 3) are not sufficiently addressed.
- Weakness: Method appears sensitive to kernel parameter choices (e.g., Gaussian width t) without detailed tuning strategies.
- Weakness: Comparisons to semi-supervised baselines are limited; more recent state-of-the-art methods should be included.
- Weakness: Some explanations are repetitive, restating the connection to standard kernel methods multiple times.
- Question: How does the noise assumption complement cluster/manifold assumptions, rather than being orthogonal?
- Question: How robust are the theoretical results to violations of strong assumptions (e.g., Gaussian noise, known marginal)?
- Question: Is the normalization in Equation 7 needed for empirical performance or only theoretical unbiasedness?
- Experiment suggestions: Clarify evaluation metrics, add ablation studies (inner vs. outer kernel), and include standard kernel baselines like Gaussian SVM.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound framework with meaningful variance-reduction results and practical implementation potential.
- Against acceptance: Noise assumption needs more validation, computational complexity is unclear, and baseline comparisons are insufficient.
- Overall: Strong contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address computational complexity, parameter tuning, and broader baseline comparisons.
