- Proposes a semi-supervised framework predicting via the Fredholm integral operator K_pX f and learns f in an RKHS, yielding a data-dependent "Fredholm kernel" KF = K K_H K^T.  
- Uses unlabeled data to approximate the integral operator and introduces a normalized variant for stability.  
- Argues a "noise assumption": low-variance local directions are uninformative for labels and should be suppressed.  
- Provides two analyses supporting noise suppression: linear outer kernel gives variance-dependent reweighting (soft-threshold PCA).  
- Shows under a Gaussian manifold+orthogonal noise model with infinite unlabeled data, Fredholm kernels give unbiased, lower-variance estimators than computing target kernel on noisy inputs.  
- Algebraic reduction to a data-dependent kernel is clean and correct; KF is PSD when K_H is PSD, independent of outer k being PSD.  
- Representer-style solution and equivalence to kernel ridge regression with k̂F are standard and valid.  
- Theoretical variance-reduction results rely on strong, transparent assumptions (Gaussian pX, infinite unlabeled data, linear subspace + orthogonal noise).  
- Lacks generalization guarantees for finite unlabeled data, non-Gaussian distributions, and arbitrary kernel choices.  
- Experiments on synthetic, text, and digit data show improvements—especially under added noise—but experimental rigor/details are insufficiently reported.  
- Missing experimental details: hyperparameter ranges, validation protocol with few labels, error bars, and computational cost reporting.  
- Paper is generally well written with helpful outer vs inner kernel notation, but some notation needs clarification and many proofs are deferred to a full version.  
- A complexity discussion (cost of forming KF and scaling with u) is absent and would aid understanding.  
- The composition K K_H K^T and variance-reduction angle are an original, nontrivial contribution despite connections to prior operator- and data-dependent-kernel work.  
- If scalable and robust, the method is practically useful as a drop-in data-dependent kernel for standard learners; impact depends on scalability and robustness to violated assumptions.  
- Pros: principled PSD data-dependent kernel, theoretical noise-suppression/variance-reduction insights, natural use of unlabeled data, empirical gains in noisy settings.  
- Cons: reliance on full-version proofs, limited theoretical scope, missing scalability analysis, and incomplete experimental protocol and baselines/ablations.  
- Key suggestions: include proof sketches and finite-sample analyses, discuss and propose scalable approximations (Nyström, low-rank), add error bars/hyperparameter details/ablations, and clarify tuning with few labels and transductive vs inductive use.  
- Overall: technically interesting and relevant with promising results; reviewer leans toward acceptance conditional on stronger proofs and improved experimental/scalability details.
