

# Review of "Learning with Fredholm Kernels"

This paper proposes a new framework for supervised and unsupervised learning based on solving Fredholm integral equations, which the authors term "Fredholm kernels." The main claim is that this framework naturally incorporates unlabeled data and provides better performance under a novel "noise assumption" compared to traditional kernel methods.

## Major Concerns

### Formatting and Technical Inconsistencies

The paper contains numerous technical and formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility:

1. **Equation formatting errors**: The paper consistently writes "l l∑ i=1" instead of "1/l ∑_{i=1}^l" in Eqn 2, and "K T l+u" instead of "K_{l+u}^T" in Eqn 3. These are not mere typos but fundamental notational errors that would confuse readers and reviewers.

2. **Incorrect matrix dimension description**: The paper claims "Kl+u is a l × (l + u) matrix" when defining it as (Kl+u)ij = k(xi, xj) for 1 ≤ i ≤ l, 1 ≤ j ≤ l + u. This is correct, but the description is confusingly phrased and should be clarified.

3. **Inconsistent notation**: The paper uses both "Fredholm kernel" (lowercase) and "Fredholm Kernel" (capitalized) interchangeably, which is confusing for readers and reviewers.

4. **Missing references**: The paper cites [4] as "the primal TSVM proposed in [4]" but doesn't include this reference in the bibliography. Similarly, it references "implementation of LapRLSC given in [1]" but [1] refers to a different work (manifold regularization).

5. **Figure references without figures**: The paper states "the first figure on right" and "See the right figure for an illustration" but no figures are included in the submission, making it impossible to assess the claims about the noise assumption and synthetic examples.

### Theoretical and Methodological Issues

1. **Incomplete theoretical claims**: The paper claims "the Fredholm kernel defined in Eqn 4 is positive semi-definite as long as KH is positive semi-definite" but states the proof is in the "full version" without providing it. This is unacceptable for a NIPS submission.

2. **Contradictory assumptions**: In Theorem 2, the paper assumes "N(µ, diag(σ²₁, ..., σ²d))" but then claims "Assuming that the data is mean-subtracted, i.e. µ = 0." This inconsistency undermines the theorem's validity.

3. **Confusing kernel specifications**: The paper states "We will set kH in Fredholm kernel to also be linear" in Theorem 3, but earlier in Theorem 2, kH was a Gaussian kernel. This inconsistency in kernel specifications across theorems is confusing and potentially misleading.

4. **Unjustified novelty claims**: The paper claims "the noise assumption has not been explicitly formulated in the context of semi-supervised learning algorithms, nor applied to theoretical analysis" without providing sufficient evidence. This claim requires more substantial justification and comparison to existing work.

### Experimental and Presentation Issues

1. **Incomplete experimental details**: The paper mentions using "Webkb" and "IMDB movie review" datasets but doesn't provide sufficient details about the preprocessing steps, feature extraction, or parameter selection.

2. **Missing implementation details**: The paper states "we will use the implementation of LapRLSC given in [1]" but [1] refers to a paper on manifold regularization, not LapRLSC. This is a significant disconnect.

3. **Inadequate comparison**: The paper compares with "Transductive SVM and LapRLSC" but doesn't explain why these specific methods were chosen for comparison or how they relate to the noise assumption.

4. **Overstated claims**: The paper claims "Fredholm kernels show clear improvement over other methods for synthetic examples in term of classification error" but doesn't provide statistical significance testing or error bars to support these claims.

## Strengths

The paper does make an interesting connection between Fredholm integral equations and kernel methods, and the "noise assumption" concept is potentially valuable. The theoretical analysis in Section 4 provides some insights into how unlabeled data might help suppress noise in classification.

## Weaknesses

The paper's weaknesses significantly outweigh its strengths due to the numerous technical inconsistencies, incomplete theoretical claims, and lack of necessary details. The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to address these issues before submission to a top-tier conference like NIPS.

## Recommendation

I recommend rejection. While the paper presents an interesting theoretical connection between Fredholm equations and kernel methods, the numerous technical and formatting errors, inconsistent notation, incomplete theoretical claims, and missing experimental details make it impossible to assess the validity of the core contributions. The paper would benefit from a complete revision to address these issues before resubmission.

The minor flaws, when accumulated, create significant barriers to understanding the paper's technical contributions and assessing its validity. Given NIPS' high standards for technical precision and clarity, these issues are sufficient grounds for rejection.
