- Overall assessment: Novel, interesting semi-supervised framework (Fredholm Learning) with strong kernel connection; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Summary: Casts semi-supervised learning as solving a Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” that incorporates unlabeled data.
- Strength — Novelty: Fresh formulation linking integral equations to kernel learning, extending beyond standard kernel and manifold methods.
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Provides theorems (2 and 4) analyzing a “noise assumption” showing noise suppression and improved kernel estimates under specific conditions.
- Strength — Kernel interpretation: Equivalence to a standard kernel method with a derived Fredholm kernel is clear and leverages existing tools.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Synthetic experiments effectively illustrate benefits under the noise assumption; real-world results are competitive or improved.
- Weakness — Theoretical assumptions: Key results rely on strong assumptions (e.g., Gaussian data, specific kernels), raising concerns about robustness.
- Weakness — Incomplete theoretical analysis: The explanation for why the Fredholm kernel suppresses noise and the link to PCA needs more intuition and detail.
- Weakness — Experimental rigor: Parameter tuning details are sparse, statistical significance is not reported, and baselines are limited.
- Experimental detail — Parameter tuning: Cross-validation mentioned but ranges and selection criteria are not provided, hurting reproducibility.
- Experimental detail — Statistical significance: Reported performance gains are modest and lack significance testing (e.g., t-tests).
- Experimental detail — Baseline comparison: Compared mainly to TSVM and LapRLSC; should include modern semi-supervised methods like MixMatch/ReMixMatch.
- Weakness — Kernel choice justification: Rationale for choosing specific kernels (Gaussian, linear) is unclear; systematic exploration is missing.
- Weakness — Computational complexity: Complexity and scalability (matrix inversion in Eqn.3) are not discussed; optimizations absent.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded, kernel-accessible framework with promising empirical results and important problem focus.
- Arguments against acceptance: Theoretical generalizability is limited by assumptions, empirical evaluation needs strengthening, and complexity concerns remain.
- Questions for authors: How robust are theoretical results beyond Gaussian assumptions?
- Questions for authors: Can you provide an intuitive explanation for Fredholm kernel noise suppression?
- Questions for authors: Please detail parameter tuning and provide statistical significance tests for results.
- Questions for authors: What is the computational complexity and are there scalability optimizations?
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying theoretical limits, strengthening experiments, and addressing computational concerns.
