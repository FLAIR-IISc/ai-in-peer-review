- Overall: Novel, interesting Fredholm integral formulation for semi‑supervised learning with promising theory and experiments; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Proposal: Recasts semi‑supervised learning as solving a Fredholm integral equation and defines a data‑dependent “Fredholm kernel” that incorporates unlabeled data.
- Noise assumption: Introduces a “noise assumption” that low‑variance directions are uninformative, giving motivation for using unlabeled data.
- Theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous analysis showing noise suppression under specific conditions and links to soft‑thresholding PCA (Theorems 2 and 4).
- Kernel connection: Elegantly connects the Fredholm framework to standard kernel methods via a derived Fredholm kernel.
- Empirical results: Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show improvement in noise‑prone scenarios.
- Writing: Well written and organized, though theoretical parts require careful reading.
- Assumption limits: Theoretical results depend heavily on strong assumptions (Gaussian unlabeled data, specific kernel choices like linear/Gaussian), limiting generality.
- Complexity of theory: Derivations are involved and may be inaccessible; would benefit from more intuitive explanations.
- Limited comparisons: Experimental evaluation omits many modern semi‑supervised baselines (e.g., MixMatch, FixMatch) and is not comprehensive.
- Inconsistent gains: Performance improvements on real datasets are not consistently large; lacks analysis of where method excels.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No thorough sensitivity analysis for key parameters (kernel width t, regularization λ).
- Scalability concerns: Computing the Fredholm kernel may be computationally costly for large datasets; scalability and optimizations are not discussed.
- Redundancy: Some sections repeat material (e.g., discussion of Fredholm kernel in Sections 2 & 3) and should be streamlined.
- Eqn 4/normalization: Question whether normalized Fredholm kernel (Eqn 5) is always necessary and how common instability with small kernel widths is in practice.
- Formalizing noise assumption: The “noise assumption” needs a clearer formal definition and practical methods to assess it on datasets.
- Theorem 4 sensitivity: The crucial relationship between t and r in Theorem 4 raises questions about performance sensitivity to choosing t.
- Recommendation for authors: Discuss theoretical limitations and extensions, expand experiments to include state‑of‑the‑art baselines and sensitivity analyses, address scalability/optimizations, and remove redundancies.
