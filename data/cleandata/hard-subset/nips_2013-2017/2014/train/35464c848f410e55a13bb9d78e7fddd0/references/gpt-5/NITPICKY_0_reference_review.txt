Summary
This paper proposes a semi-supervised learning framework by formulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation of the first kind. Given an outer kernel k and an RKHS with inner kernel kH, the method fits f in H by minimizing square loss on labeled points of K_p̂X f (an empirical integral operator estimated from unlabeled data) plus RKHS regularization. The resulting predictor can be written as a standard kernel method using a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” k̂F(x, z) that “sandwiches” kH by the (empirical) integral operator induced by k and the marginal pX. A normalized variant is introduced for stability. The paper motivates a “noise assumption” for semi-supervised learning (low-variance directions are uninformative) and gives two theoretical results: (i) with linear outer kernel, the induced kernel is an inner product with a data-dependent metric that soft-thresholds small-variance directions; (ii) under a Gaussian subspace-plus-orthogonal-noise model, Fredholm kernels yield unbiased approximations (up to constants) to the target kernel with lower variance than the noisy direct kernel, for both linear and Gaussian targets. Experiments on synthetic and several real datasets are claimed to demonstrate benefits over RLSC, TSVM, and LapRLSC.

Strengths
- Conceptually simple reduction: a semi-supervised Fredholm formulation that reduces to a standard kernel machine with a data-dependent kernel KF = K KH K^T; PSD by construction.
- Provides a clear avenue to incorporate unlabeled data into otherwise standard supervised pipelines.
- The “noise assumption” is intuitive and distinct from cluster/manifold assumptions, and the theory aligns with its intuition (variance suppression).
- The linear-k outer case gives an interpretable geometry (re-weighting principal directions), and the Gaussian case shows variance reduction under a clean generative model.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and clarity:
- Notation and scaling inconsistencies. There is an unresolved 1/(l+u) scaling throughout. Eqn (1) defines K_p̂X with 1/(l+u), but the matrices K, KH, KF in Eqn (3) and later do not carry these factors consistently. As written, KF = K KH K^T does not match the kernel in Eqn (4) which includes 1/(l+u)^2. Please fix dimensions and scaling rigorously.
- Eqn (2) appears to have a typo “l l∑”; unclear whether a 1/l factor is intended.
- Representer theorem: the statement “standard derivation” is deferred to the full version. Because the loss depends on f via evaluations at both labeled and unlabeled points, the precise statement and conditions (especially with k not PSD/symmetric) should appear in the paper.
- Proposition 1: PSD of k̂F follows since KF = K KH K^T with KH PSD. Fine, but the normalized variant (Eqn (5)) needs a careful matrix-level argument (B KH B^T with B = D^{-1}K) and explicit assumptions (nonzero row-sums; add epsilon?). As written, “easy to check” is insufficient.
- Theorem 2: The expression for ΣF and the “rescaling factor” are inconsistent dimensionally and notationally. The presence of a global product term over d is suspicious; the square root in the rescaling factor seems unjustified given that the inner product uses ΣF directly. This section requires a cleaned derivation and careful statement (assumptions, centering, exact coefficients).
- Theorems 3 and 4: All proofs are deferred; nontrivial details (choice of constants, independence assumptions, exact variance expressions, the condition λ>σ) are critical to assess the claims. Also, the choice t(t+λ^2)(t+3λ^2)/λ^4 = r in Theorem 4 needs derivation and discussion of existence/uniqueness/conditioning.

Originality and relation to prior work:
- The construction k̂F = E_u,v[k(x,u) kH(u,v) k(z,v)] is closely related to data-dependent kernels, diffusion kernels/heat kernels on data, marginalized kernels, and kernel smoothing/conditional mean embeddings. The connection to Sindhwani–Niyogi–Belkin’s data-dependent kernels is mentioned but references are inconsistent; diffusion maps/heat kernels (Coifman–Lafon) and marginalized kernels literature are not cited/discussed. Please position the contribution more precisely against these lines and clarify what is genuinely new (e.g., the specific Fredholm regularization objective versus post-hoc smoothing; the variance analysis under the noise model).
- The paper references are inconsistent: numbering mismatches (e.g., [13] in text vs list), incomplete metadata (years, venues), and several typos. This undermines clarity.

Empirical evaluation:
- Tables 1–6 are referenced but not included; without quantitative results, the empirical claims are not verifiable.
- Experimental protocol lacks critical details: sizes of labeled/unlabeled sets, exact hyperparameter search spaces and validation splits, whether unlabeled data used for normalization includes test points, computational overheads, and standard deviations/confidence intervals.
- Baselines: For linear settings, compare also to strong linear SVM; for Gaussian settings, include LapSVM and recent semi-supervised baselines beyond TSVM/LapRLSC. Ablations (outer vs inner kernel choices; normalized vs unnormalized; effect of u) are missing.
- No runtime/memory analysis. Building KF scales as O(l(l+u)^2) in time and O((l+u)^2) in memory; with large u typical in semi-supervised learning, this can be prohibitive. Practical workarounds (Nyström, sparsification, mini-batching) should be discussed.

Presentation:
- Many typos and grammatical errors: “distint”, “on on”, “senario”, “kernle”, “unormalized”, “neural” vs “neutral”, etc.
- Footnotes (“1”, “2”, “3”) are referenced but content is missing. Several claims are deferred to the “full version” without sufficient in-paper detail.
- Some figures are referenced but not present. The text repeatedly says “see right figure,” which is not available here.

Qualitative evaluation
- Quality: The core idea is promising, but the paper has multiple technical/notation issues, missing proofs, and incomplete experiments. As is, the work is not yet scientifically solid.
- Clarity: Moderate to low; numerous inconsistencies and missing content hinder understanding and reproducibility.
- Originality: Moderate; the particular Fredholm-regularized objective plus the KF = K KH K^T reduction is nice, but the idea of data-dependent kernel smoothing via unlabeled data is not entirely new. The specific variance analyses are potentially novel if fully proved.
- Significance: Potentially useful if made rigorous and scalable. Current presentation and evaluation limit impact.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, general mechanism to incorporate unlabeled data into kernel methods via a PSD data-dependent kernel.
- Intuitive “noise assumption” with matching theoretical variance-reduction results.
- Potentially broad applicability.

Cons for acceptance
- Mathematical inconsistencies and missing proofs for central claims.
- Incomplete, non-reproducible experiments; key tables/figures absent.
- Poor presentation quality (typos, references, footnotes).
- Limited positioning w.r.t. related data-dependent/diffusion/marginalized kernels.

Recommendation
Reject. The paper contains an interesting and potentially impactful idea, but the accumulation of technical, empirical, and presentation issues prevents acceptance. I encourage the authors to: (i) fix notation/scaling rigorously; (ii) include full proofs or at least detailed sketches; (iii) add comprehensive, reproducible experiments with strong baselines and scalability analysis; (iv) clean up writing and references; and (v) better situate the work within existing data-dependent kernel literature.
