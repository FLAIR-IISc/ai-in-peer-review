- Proposal: semi-supervised framework treating prediction as a regularized inverse problem for a Fredholm integral equation, producing a data-dependent kernel KF = Kl+u KH KTl+u (with a normalized variant).
- Motivation: unlabeled data should denoise kernel evaluations under a “noise assumption” that low-variance directions in pX are uninformative for labels.
- Theoretical insights: in Gaussian models, KF yields soft-PCA-like downweighting of low-variance directions and variance reduction of noisy kernels (under specific conditions λ > σ).
- Representer result: solution equivalent to standard KRR/SVM using the PSD Fredholm kernel k̂F, with an interpretation as inner products of smoothed H-features.
- Empirics: improvements over RLSC, TSVM and LapRLSC on synthetic noise-heavy tasks and gains on text and digit datasets, especially as noise increases.
- Strength: conceptual clarity and a simple, implementable construction that injects unlabeled density information while keeping standard learners.
- Strength: solid operator-theoretic grounding showing the kernel is PSD and the normalized variant preserves PSDness.
- Strength: transparent Gaussian-model theory that gives tangible justification for the denoising effect.
- Strength: practical relevance supported by empirical evidence on multiple datasets.
- Weakness: theory scope limited to infinite unlabeled data and idealized Gaussian models; no finite-sample concentration or rates for empirical k̂F.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and comparison to related data-dependent kernels (diffusion/Laplacian kernels, Nyström features, Nadaraya–Watson smoothing).
- Weakness: computational and scalability concerns—forming KF requires handling dense (l+u)^2 matrices and O(l(l+u)^2) time; no approximations or runtimes reported.
- Weakness: the “noise assumption” is informal and can fail (e.g., rare predictive low-variance features); failure modes and diagnostics are not discussed.
- Weakness: sensitivity to choices of outer/inner kernels and bandwidths is not analyzed; normalized variant not systematically compared.
- Weakness: missing baselines and ablations (PCA/whitening, local covariance shrinkage, diffusion kernels, role of k vs kH, normalized vs unnormalized).
- Weakness: lack of generalization guarantees or discussion of stability/Rademacher bounds for data-dependent kernels.
- Suggestions: add finite-sample concentration bounds and excess-risk analysis as u grows, even under mild assumptions.
- Suggestions: strengthen related work to clarify connections/distinctions with diffusion/graph kernels, two-stage kernels, and density-ratio Fredholm methods.
- Suggestions: provide computational details, approximations (Nyström/RFF), iterative solvers, and empirical runtimes.
- Suggestions: add targeted baselines/ablations aligned with the noise motivation and study performance versus u and covariate shift.
- Suggestions: formalize the noise assumption, provide diagnostics for when it holds, and discuss potential harms.
- Clarity fixes: clarify Eq. (3) constants/dimensions, add a short proof sketch for Proposition 1, verify Theorem 2 notation/rescaling, and give full experimental protocol details.
- Acceptance pros: elegant, implementable semi-supervised kernel construction with illuminating theory and empirical gains.
- Acceptance cons: limited finite-sample theory, scalability and comparison gaps, and an informal noise assumption that could hide failure modes.
