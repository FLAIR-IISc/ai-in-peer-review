- Proposes a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation.  
- Introduces data-dependent "Fredholm kernels" that naturally incorporate unlabeled data.  
- Claims unlabeled data utility under a new "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning.  
- Main contributions: new Fredholm-equation-based framework, theoretical analysis of noise suppression, and comprehensive experiments.  
- Correctly situates work within kernel methods, integral operators, manifold regularization, density ratio estimation, and kernel mean embeddings.  
- Section 4 provides strong theoretical evidence that Fredholm kernels reduce noise variance in classification.  
- Theorem 2 shows linear Fredholm kernels implement a “soft thresholding” PCA, an insightful and well-supported result.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and show competitive improvements on synthetic and multiple real-world datasets.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and parameter sensitivity (e.g., kernel width t).  
- Needs clearer guidance on when the proposed noise assumption holds in practice to assess applicability.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and balances theory and experiments.  
- Novelty is moderate due to similarities with existing kernel methods, but the focus on noise suppression is a meaningful advancement.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with solid theory and empirical support; recommendation for acceptance.
