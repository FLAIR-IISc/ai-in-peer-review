- Introduces Fredholm kernels: a novel semi-supervised framework using a regularized Fredholm integral equation to build data-dependent kernels incorporating unlabeled data.  
- Proposes a new "noise assumption" that low-variance directions in unlabeled data are noise-agnostic for classification.  
- Claims theoretical variance reduction from Fredholm kernels under the noise assumption.  
- Linear kernel analysis (Sec. 4.1) rigorously shows suppression of low-variance principal components.  
- Derivation of Fredholm kernels (Eq. 4) is mathematically sound and Proposition 1 proves positive semi-definiteness.  
- Synthetic experiments (Sec. 5) effectively isolate the noise assumption and outperform TSVM and LapRLSC where cluster assumptions fail.  
- Empirical gains on real-world datasets are marginal (e.g., 0.5–1% error reduction on WebKB), overstating significance.  
- Lacks ablation studies on kernel choices (e.g., why FredLin1 beats FredLin2) to explain practical performance differences.  
- Gaussian kernel analysis (Sec. 4.2) is incomplete and depends on a specific Gaussian noise model that may not generalize to real data.  
- Theorem 4 assumes identical kernel widths for k and kH, which is unrealistic and undermines practical applicability.  
- The claim that unlabeled data "provably improves inference" (Sec. 1) is only supported under strict Gaussian assumptions (Theorem 3).  
- The normalized Fredholm kernel (Eq. 5) appears ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification versus the unnormalized form.  
- Connection to related work is underdeveloped; fails to clearly distinguish Fredholm kernels from prior data-dependent kernels like [13].  
- Computational cost and scalability (O((l+u)^2) storage) are unaddressed, posing concerns for large-scale use.  
- Revision recommendations: add kernel ablations, discuss Gaussian assumption limits, explicitly contrast with [13], include complexity analysis, and broaden noisy-data experiments beyond MNIST.  
- Overall: technically solid and theoretically interesting, but empirical significance is modest and requires stronger experiments and clearer theoretical scope to justify claims.
