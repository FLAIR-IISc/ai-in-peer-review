- Proposes a new supervised and semi-supervised learning framework based on regularized Fredholm integral equations, yielding data-dependent "Fredholm kernels."
- Fits naturally into the kernel framework and can be interpreted as constructing new kernels from data.
- Theoretically shows Fredholm kernels are positive semi-definite and can leverage unlabeled data under a "noise assumption."
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate competitive semi-supervised performance.
- Novel approach offering a fresh perspective on kernel methods via Fredholm integral equations.
- Solid theoretical foundation, including rigorous proofs supporting the method.
- Empirical validation supports effectiveness of the proposed method.
- Clearly situates the work within existing literature on kernels, semi-supervised learning, and manifold regularization.
- Framework complexity may hinder understanding and implementation for readers without strong functional analysis and kernel-method backgrounds.
- Insufficient exploration and guidance on hyperparameter choices, especially inner and outer kernels.
- Comparisons to existing methods are limited; more comprehensive benchmarking against state-of-the-art is needed.
- The contribution is original and significant for kernel-based and semi-supervised learning research.
- Paper is well-organized with a clear flow and balanced presentation of theory and experiments.
- Writing is clear, concise, and uses appropriate technical notation and formal tone.
- Recommendation to provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and selection.
- Recommendation to include broader and stronger comparisons to other state-of-the-art semi-supervised methods.
- Recommendation to explore applications in other domains (e.g., computer vision, NLP) to demonstrate wider applicability.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address the suggested improvements.
