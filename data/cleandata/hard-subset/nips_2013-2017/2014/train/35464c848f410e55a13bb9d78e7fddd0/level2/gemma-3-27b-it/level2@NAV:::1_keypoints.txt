- Introduces a novel supervised and semi-supervised learning framework by reformulating learning as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, defining “Fredholm kernels.”  
- Core idea leverages the Fredholm formulation to naturally incorporate unlabeled data and denoise classifiers under a proposed “noise assumption.”  
- Derivation of Fredholm kernels as a data-dependent kernel is clearly presented and well connected to existing kernel methods and density estimation.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, notably the noise suppression results under a Gaussian noise model (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical validation includes synthetic examples tailored to the noise assumption and real-world benchmarks, showing competitive performance.  
- Prior work is appropriately cited, including links to kernel mean embeddings and manifold regularization.  
- The “noise assumption” is intuitively appealing but lacks a precise, rigorous definition beyond being the opposite of the cluster assumption.  
- Performance improvements over strong baselines like LapRLSC are often marginal, limiting practical impact.  
- The paper would benefit from a more extensive ablation study to isolate effects of components such as inner/outer kernel choices.  
- Presentation is dense and sometimes hard to follow; clearer, more concise explanations and illustrative examples are needed.  
- Practical guidance for choosing the regularization parameter t in the Gaussian kernel setting is insufficiently explored.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and warranting acceptance contingent on revisions to clarify the noise assumption, add ablations, and improve clarity.
