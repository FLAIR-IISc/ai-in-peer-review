**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a new framework for supervised and semi-supervised learning based on reformulating the learning problem as a regularized Fredholm integral equation. The approach fits naturally into the kernel framework and can be interpreted as constructing new data-dependent kernels, called Fredholm kernels. The authors provide both theoretical and experimental evidence that Fredholm kernels can effectively utilize unlabeled data under the "noise assumption" for semi-supervised learning.

2. Fredholm kernels can be used to replace traditional kernels to inject them with "noise-suppression" power with the help of unlabeled data.
3. The "noise assumption" is a new assumption that is compatible with the manifold assumption and can be used to analyze the behavior of Fredholm kernels.
