- Summary: Proposes a semi-supervised framework casting supervised loss as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent PSD "Fredholm kernel" built from an outer kernel k and inner RKHS kernel kH with unlabeled data entering via Monte Carlo; predictor reduces to kernel ridge/SVM with kernel kF that leverages unlabeled data under a "noise assumption."  
- Summary (theory/regimes): Analyzes linear outer kernels (PCA-style soft-thresholding/reweighting of principal directions) and Gaussian latent-subspace noise (Fredholm kernels approximate a clean kernel with lower variance than standard kernels).  
- Summary (empirics): Experiments on synthetic, text, and digit tasks show competitive or improved performance versus RLSC, TSVM, LapRLSC, especially when noise is injected.  

- Relation to prior work: Connects to kernel integral operators/mean embeddings, density-ratio via Fredholm equations, and data-dependent kernels/manifold regularization; differs from Laplacian methods by denoising via operator smoothing and enforces PSD via kH.  
- Relation to prior work: Closest works include Que & Belkin (density-ratio Fredholm) and data-dependent/manifold regularization literature; variance-reduction reminiscent of RKHS mean shrinkage.  

- Quality — Theory: Propositions/theorems are plausible and insightful (linear PCA view, variance-reduction), but rely on access to pX and strong Gaussian assumptions; lacks finite-sample concentration or excess-risk bounds for finite u and l.  
- Quality — Empirics: Experiments are diverse and show noise robustness, but lack key details (size of u, sensitivity to t and λ, computation/memory, ablations on u) and comparisons to other data-dependent kernels.  
- Quality — Correctness: PSD property and algebraic reduction from (l+u) to l systems are sound; several technical steps/proofs deferred to full version.  

- Clarity: Generally clear and well-structured with intuitive figures; has typos, omitted proofs, and needs a clearer computational complexity discussion.  

- Originality: Novel formulation deriving a PSD data-dependent kernel from a regularized Fredholm equation and variance-reduction analysis under noise models.  

- Significance: Offers a potentially general plug-in kernel to exploit unlabeled data without cluster/manifold assumptions and gives a clear noise-based rationale for when unlabeled data help; impact limited by missing finite-sample guarantees and broader baselines.  

- Strengths: Principled derivation of PSD data-dependent kernel; intuitive noise-suppression (soft PCA); variance-reduction under Gaussian models; integrates with standard kernel learners and shows empirical gains under added noise.  

- Weaknesses & questions: Assumes access to pX (infinite unlabeled data) with no finite-sample analysis; strong Gaussian assumptions limit generality; limited comparisons to diffusion/propagation/self-tuning kernels; scalability concerns (computational/storage cost O(l(l+u)^2), O((l+u)^2) memory); hyperparameter selection/sensitivity and validation details unclear.  

- Suggestions for improvement: Provide finite-sample concentration and excess-risk bounds; experiments varying u and stronger baselines (label propagation, diffusion, self-tuning kernels); demonstrate scalable implementations (sparsification, Nyström, random features, iterative solvers); add sensitivity plots for t, r, λ and clearer validation protocol; move key proofs into main text and fix typos.  

- Reasons to accept: Conceptually novel and principled semi-supervised kernel with PSD guarantee; theoretical evidence that unlabeled data can help under realistic noise; empirical competitiveness in noisy settings.  

- Reasons to reject: Lack of finite-sample theory and scalability discussion; limited experimental breadth and moderate real-data gains without added noise; dependence on Gaussian assumptions for theoretical claims.  

- Overall verdict: Weak accept; reviewer confidence: Moderate.
