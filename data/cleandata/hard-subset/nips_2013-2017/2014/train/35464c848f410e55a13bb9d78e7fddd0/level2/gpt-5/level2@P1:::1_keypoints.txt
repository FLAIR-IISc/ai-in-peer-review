- Proposal: recast prediction as a regularized Fredholm integral equation, yielding a data-dependent “Fredholm kernel” k̂F = K_out K_H K_out^T (sandwich form) that is PSD if kH is PSD.  
- Use of unlabeled data: approximates the integral operator with unlabeled samples, giving an inductive plug-in kernel compatible with any supervised kernel method.  
- Noise assumption: low-variance directions under pX are uninformative and should be suppressed; this complements manifold/cluster assumptions.  
- Theoretical results: PCA-like soft-thresholding for linear outer kernel (Gaussian case) and variance reduction/unbiasedness (under Gaussian signal-plus-orthogonal-noise model, with infinite unlabeled data).  
- Empirical results: competitive performance on synthetic, text/sentiment, and digit tasks, with larger gains under added noise.  
- Relation to prior work: connects to integral operator methods, kernel mean embeddings, shrinkage in RKHS, density ratio estimation, diffusion/marginalized/convolutional data-dependent kernels.  
- Strength — simplicity: straightforward construction and clear PSD guarantee from the sandwich form.  
- Strength — unlabeled data use: integrates unlabeled data without extra graph regularizers or changing objectives.  
- Strength — intuitive assumption: noise-centric assumption is well-motivated and made precise in tractable Gaussian models.  
- Strength — insight: theory explains when/why unlabeled data helps (variance reduction, reweighting PCs).  
- Weakness — theoretical scope: relies on Gaussian assumptions and infinite unlabeled data; lacks finite-sample/concentration bounds and robustness analysis when assumptions fail.  
- Weakness — scalability: naive memory/time O((l+u)^2) / O(l(l+u)^2) is prohibitive for large u; needs discussion of Nyström, random features, sparsification, or low-rank approximations.  
- Weakness — normalization: normalized Fredholm kernel is PSD on training Gram via congruence, but formal proof/conditions for being a Mercer kernel over X are missing.  
- Weakness — relation to related kernels: insufficient deeper conceptual/empirical comparison to diffusion, marginalized, and convolutional kernels.  
- Weakness — practical guidance: multiple kernel choices and scales introduced with limited ablation/sensitivity analysis and tuning heuristics.  
- Quality: operator formulation and PSD claim are sound; proofs deferred to full version—proof sketches should be included for self-containment.  
- Empirical reproducibility: more details needed on hyperparameter selection, unlabeled set sizes, computational cost, and runtime.  
- Originality/significance: neat Fredholm derivation and noise-centric analysis add novelty despite resemblance to known data-dependent kernels; potential practical impact if scalability/tuning are addressed.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance, conditional on clarifying normalization/PSD, adding scalability/approximation details, and stronger comparisons to related kernels.  
- Questions for authors: provide complexity/approximation schemes used, prove validity of k̂NF on test points, report sensitivity to kernel choices/bandwidths, give finite-sample bounds for ||k̂F − kF||, and evaluate cases where low-variance directions are informative.
