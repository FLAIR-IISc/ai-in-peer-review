- Proposes "deep" recursive networks by adding depth across space with parameter sharing across heights within each layer.  
- Uses dropout and ReLUs and keeps word representations fixed to pre-trained embeddings.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and achieves very strong performance.  
- Method is novel but essentially a generalization of depth used in recurrent networks.  
- Performance gains from dropout and ReLUs are interesting and noteworthy.  
- Some model and experimental design choices are questionable or not well justified.  
- Paper is clearly written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Undefined notation: W^eta and W^xh are not explained; equations should be split for inputs vs hidden layers and parameter sets/dimensions summarized.  
- Statements in lines 148–154 are speculative and need empirical backing.  
- Missing baseline: should include results from Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model.  
- Why were word vectors not fine-tuned? This could confound results—fine-tuning might allow a single-layer RNN to match or exceed performance.  
- Need to justify the use of shared dropout units (line 251).  
- For stability, suggest weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation as alternatives to the current approach.  
- Binary classification results are problematic because training included neutral examples; must be redone following standard protocol.  
- Question whether depth across space helps when only a single root label exists; recommend additional experiments on global-label datasets and ablations (fine-tuning embeddings, proper binary protocol) to strengthen claims.
