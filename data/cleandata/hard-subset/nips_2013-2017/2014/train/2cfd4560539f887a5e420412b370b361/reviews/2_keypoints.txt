- Introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset.  
- Results are impressive and the paper’s exposition is very clear.  
- Proposed changes are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated.  
- Untying leaf-node and nonterminal-node parameters improves modeling.  
- Using rectified linear units (ReLUs) in the recursive setting is effective.  
- Leveraging large unsupervised word vectors while keeping smaller hidden nonterminal representations is beneficial.  
- The deep architecture introduces inter-layer and inter-node connections rather than simply stacking layers.  
- Missing ablation/comparison: whether parent inputs should come only from the last hidden layer (i.e., dropping the V matrix connection).  
- Table 1(a) improperly reports results on the final test set instead of the development set, implying tuning on the test set.  
- Paper contains typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that needs fixing.
