- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks: stacking recursive layers on trees with per-node inter-layer (V) connections to add “depth-in-space.”
- Novelty: explicit per-node skip connections across layers rather than stacking independent recursive modules or widening.
- Architectural benefit: unties leaf vs nonterminal parameters to allow large fixed pretrained word vectors and smaller phrase representations.
- Uses ReLUs, dropout with shared masks across tree nodes, AdaGrad, and careful initialization; claims these choices improve performance and training.
- Strong empirical results on SST: consistent gains from depth under fixed parameter budgets and state-of-the-art fine-grained accuracy at the time.
- Analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects (syntactic vs task-relevant).
- Exposition and training details are generally clear and reproducible.
- Missing critical ablation: evaluate model without V connections to test whether gains come from inter-layer skip connections versus stacking.
- Missing comparison: connect classifier to all layers vs only last layer to assess supervision distribution effects.
- Potential test-set leakage: Table 1(a) seems to report multiple hyperparameter settings on the test set; model selection should be done on dev and only one final test evaluation reported.
- Fairness concern: pretrained embeddings are fixed here but some baselines fine-tune; include baselines that use same fixed embeddings and versions that fine-tune for completeness.
- Robustness concern: no variance/standard deviation over multiple random seeds; deep ReLU+dropout models can be sensitive to initialization.
- Additional comparisons requested: ReLU vs tanh under the untied scheme, quantify gain from untying leaves, and complementary depth-vs-width experiments at matched widths/parameter increases.
- Minor issues: typos and malformed sentences (e.g., “All other weights are initialized as .”) and unclear dropout mask-sharing description need fixes.
- Practical concerns: report computational cost and training time as depth increases and clarify importance of identity-plus-noise initialization.
- Recommendation: accept if authors fix evaluation protocol and add the key V-connection ablation; otherwise borderline/weak accept despite strong novelty and empirical gains.
