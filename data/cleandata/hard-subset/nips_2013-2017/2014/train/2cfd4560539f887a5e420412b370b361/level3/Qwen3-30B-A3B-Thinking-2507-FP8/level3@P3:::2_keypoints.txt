- Proposes a deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers to better model compositionality, distinguishing “depth in structure” vs “depth in space.”  
- Novelly combines recursive processing with hierarchical stacking and untied leaf/nonterminal parameters to leverage large pretrained word vectors and ReLUs.  
- Introduces inter-layer and inter-node connections (V matrix) so each layer captures different aspects of compositionality.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and achieves new state-of-the-art fine-grained accuracy (49.5% vs prior 48.1%).  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) indicate layer specialization: layer 1 lexical, layer 2 syntactic/semantic, layer 3 sentiment.  
- Technical contributions are clever, well-motivated, and validated empirically; exposition is clear with helpful figures.  
- Paper is original, significant, well-organized, and provides reproducible details and theoretical motivations.  
- Lacks an ablation study testing the necessity of the V matrix/inter-layer connections; authors should evaluate dropping it.  
- Table 1(a) improperly reports final test-set results instead of development-set results, suggesting hyperparameter tuning on the test set and requiring correction.  
- Contains a few typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that need fixing.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, provided the authors address the methodological error and minor fixes for the camera-ready version.
