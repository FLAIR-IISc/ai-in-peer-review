

This paper introduces a novel deep recursive neural network architecture that stacks multiple recursive layers to achieve state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank for fine-grained sentiment classification. The authors make several key innovations: untangling leaf-node and nonterminal-node parameters to allow for more effective use of pretrained word vectors and ReLU activations, and introducing inter-layer connections that create a deeper hierarchical representation compared to traditional recursive networks (which are "deep in structure" but not "deep in space"). The paper effectively demonstrates that deeper architectures capture different aspects of linguistic compositionality, with each layer learning distinct representational features.

The paper excels in clarity, with well-organized exposition and excellent experimental validation. The results are impressive, showing consistent improvements over both shallow recursive networks and previous state-of-the-art baselines. The authors' exploration of how different layers capture different compositional aspects through input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis provides valuable theoretical insights.

However, the paper has several critical issues that need addressing. The most serious is Table 1(a) reporting results on the final test set instead of the development set, which implies test set tuning and undermines the validity of the claims. Additionally, the authors miss an important ablation study examining whether parent inputs should come only from the last hidden layer (i.e., dropping the V matrix connection), which would clarify the necessity of their proposed inter-layer connections.

The paper contains minor typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that should be corrected. While the architecture is novel and the results compelling, the test set reporting error is particularly problematic for reproducibility and fair comparison.

Overall, this is a strong paper with significant contributions to recursive neural network architectures. The quality of the work is high, the significance is clear given the state-of-the-art results, and the originality is evident in the architecture design. The clarity is excellent, though the test set reporting issue must be resolved before acceptance. The paper represents a meaningful advance in modeling compositionality in language, and with the identified revisions, would make a valuable contribution to the field.
