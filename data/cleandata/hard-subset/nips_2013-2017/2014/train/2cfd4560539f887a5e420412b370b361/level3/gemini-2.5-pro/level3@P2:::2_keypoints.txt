- Proposes a novel Deep RNN that stacks multiple recursive layers with direct node-to-node inter-layer connections.  
- Each node receives input from its children and from its corresponding node in the layer below, enabling hierarchical tree processing.  
- Unties leaf and internal-node parameters so large pre-trained word vectors can be used with smaller phrase representations and ReLUs.  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, outperforming RNTN and Paragraph Vectors.  
- Strength — Originality/significance: well-motivated method for adding depth to recursive networks with broad applicability.  
- Strength — Technical quality: clever modeling choices, successful use of ReLU/dropout, and qualitative analyses showing distinct layer behaviors.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is very well written with clear figures and detailed experimental setup.  
- Weakness — Experimental reporting: Table 1(a) suggests hyperparameter/model selection may have used the test set; authors must clarify and report development-set results.  
- Weakness — Missing ablation: no comparison to a stacked baseline with the inter-layer connection removed (V=0) to isolate its benefit.  
- Weakness — Minor clarity/typos: small errors (e.g., incomplete weight initialization description) require proofreading.  
- Pro-accept reasons: novel architecture, strong empirical results, impactful methodological contributions, clear presentation.  
- Con-accept reasons: potential test-set tuning and missing key ablation study raise significant concerns.  
- Conclusion: strong and elegant paper but requires clarification on experimental methodology before final acceptance.
