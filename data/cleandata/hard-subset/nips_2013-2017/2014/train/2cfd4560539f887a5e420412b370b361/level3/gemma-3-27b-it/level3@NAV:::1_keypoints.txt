- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (stacked recursive layers) to capture hierarchical representations for compositionality in language.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment) and reports state-of-the-art performance.
- Methodology is clearly explained and the paper is technically sound and well-presented.
- Experimental setup is thorough, including comparisons to strong baselines and ablation studies.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) provide insights into layer-wise behavior.
- Empirical results convincingly show benefits of increased depth vs. shallow recursive networks.
- Properly situates the work within prior RNN/deep learning literature (Socher et al., Bengio et al., Mikolov et al.).
- Lacks deeper theoretical justification for why increased depth yields the observed compositional benefits.
- Architectural choices (number of layers, hidden unit sizes) seem empirically chosen without clear rationale.
- The untying of leaf and internal parameters, while effective, needs more discussion on its impact on representations.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on authors addressing theoretical justification and architectural rationale in revision.
