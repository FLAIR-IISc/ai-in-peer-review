- Paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks: stacking multiple recursive layers on parse trees with depth in structure and space, leaf/internal parameter untying, ReLU activations, and dropout, evaluated on SST with AdaGrad and early stopping.  
- Under a fixed parameter budget, deeper-but-narrower models outperform shallow counterparts and achieve state-of-the-art on SST; layer analyses suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects.  
- Well-positioned relative to prior recursive models, word/document vectors, and stacked/recursive depth literature.  
- Clear, well-written exposition; useful distinction between “depth in structure” and “depth in space.”  
- Simple, appealing architectural extension: stacked recursive layers with cross-layer connections and leaf/internal untying enabling ReLU use with pretrained embeddings.  
- Solid empirical grounding on a canonical benchmark with parameter-controlled comparisons and standard regularization/optimization.  
- Achieves new SOTA against strong baselines and provides qualitative analyses indicating layer specialization.  
- Training details (activations, dropout sharing, AdaGrad, initialization) are sufficiently specified to aid reproducibility.  
- Limited theoretical/diagnostic analysis of why stacked depth helps in tree recursion; more principled discussion would strengthen the work.  
- Incomplete ablation study—needs systematic isolation of contributions from untying leaves/internals, ReLU vs. tanh, classifier connectivity to layers, dropout, and fixed vs. fine-tuned embeddings.  
- Evaluation limited to a single dataset/task (SST); generality to other structured NLP tasks is untested.  
- No statistical significance testing or variability estimates across runs despite relatively small absolute gains.  
- Practical considerations (training speed, memory, complexity vs. depth, impact of parse errors) are not discussed.  
- Comparisons to DCNN and Paragraph Vectors mix supervision and input-structure regimes; fairness caveat or controlled comparisons are needed.  
- Technically sound and well-defined; parameter-controlled depth comparison is a strong experimental design, but lacking thorough diagnostics.  
- Very clear presentation; recommended to report per-model parameter counts, runtime stats, and clarify shared dropout mask and initialization details.  
- Originality is moderate—stacking is known, but adapting depth-in-space to tree recursion with untying and ReLUs is a sensible, useful contribution; qualitative layer-role analyses are novel.  
- Significance is good due to SOTA and controlled gains, but broader impact depends on generalization to other tasks.  
- Questions for authors: provide ablations isolating key components; test multi-layer supervision; sensitivity to shared dropout mask; training stability/gradient diagnostics as depth grows; and report training time/memory vs. depth.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear, motivated extension with strong empirical results, SOTA performance, insightful qualitative analyses, and practical recipe likely to be adopted.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical explanation, incomplete ablations, single-task evaluation, and no significance/variance reporting.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
