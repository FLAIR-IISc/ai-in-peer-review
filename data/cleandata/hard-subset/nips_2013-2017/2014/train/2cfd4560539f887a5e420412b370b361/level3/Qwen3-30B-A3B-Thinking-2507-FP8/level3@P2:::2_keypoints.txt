- Introduces a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks recursive layers to model hierarchical compositionality, distinguishing "depth in structure" vs. "depth in space".  
- Proposes inter-layer connections (V^(i)) so each stacked layer captures distinct aspects of linguistic composition.  
- Reports state-of-the-art on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (49.5% fine-grained accuracy), outperforming shallow RNNs, RNTN, and paragraph vectors.  
- Offers qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) showing successive layers encode increasingly abstract sentiment representations.  

Strengths:
- Strong technical execution; untied leaf/internal parameterization fixes word/phrase space mismatch and enables effective ReLU use, supported by ablations.  
- Well-justified use of pretrained fixed 300D embeddings and dropout; comprehensive comparisons and clear figures/tables.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and helpful visualizations; experimental setup gives good reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: extending deep recurrent ideas to recursive (tree-structured) models is a meaningful conceptual advance.  
- Significant impact: new SOTA on SST and insightful layer-specific compositionality findings that may generalize beyond sentiment.  

Weaknesses:
- Critical reporting error: Table 1(a) reports test-set results as development-set results, implying test-set tuning and undermining claimed performance gains.  
- Missing ablation: no experiment removing inter-layer connections V^(i) (i.e., output connected only to final layer) to verify V^(i)’s benefit.  
- Minor clarity issues and typos, including a malformed sentence in the abstract.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance pending fixes: correct test/dev reporting immediately, add the V^(i) ablation study, and fix typos.
