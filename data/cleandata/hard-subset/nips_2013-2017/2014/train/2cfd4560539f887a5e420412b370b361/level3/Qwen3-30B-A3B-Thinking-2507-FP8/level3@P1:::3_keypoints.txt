- Proposal: Introduces "Deep Recursive Neural Networks" by stacking multiple recursive layers to add spatial depth to tree-structured recursive models.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Socher et al. (2013) and extends ideas from deep recurrent networks [10-12].  
- Main claim: Layer stacking produces hierarchical compositional representations that improve sentiment classification.  

Strengths:
- Writing and reproducibility: Exceptionally well-written with clear architecture diagrams and sufficient implementation detail.  
- Strong empirical result: Achieves state-of-the-art SST fine-grained accuracy (49.5%), outperforming shallow RNNs (48.1%) and prior baselines.  
- Regularization choices: Use of ReLU and dropout is well-justified and shown to be crucial (3.4% gain over L2).  
- Qualitative analyses: Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase studies indicate deeper layers capture different compositional aspects (word dominance, intensity, sentiment).  
- Conceptual insight: Correctly distinguishes depth in space (layer stacking) from depth in structure (tree recursion) and shows they complement each other.

Weaknesses:
- Fixed embeddings: Keeping word vectors fixed is unjustified; fine-tuning could alter the attribution of gains to depth.  
- Missing baseline: Omits Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN baseline (47.5% on SST), weakening comparative claims.  
- Binary protocol issue: Reports binary results by discarding neutral examples while training on 5 classes, which is not standard and is misleading.  
- Notation problems: Equations (3)-(4) use undefined terms (e.g., W^η, W^{xh}) and conflate input vs. hidden parameters; parameter dimensions are not summarized.  
- Speculative layer claims: Assertions that layers capture different aspects are qualitative; no quantitative layer-wise evidence is provided.  
- Stability remedy is ad hoc: Using a fixed L2 penalty for exploding activations is arbitrary; standard alternatives like gradient clipping or weight normalization are not explored.

Originality & significance:
- Contribution scope: Incremental but meaningful extension of deep RNN ideas to recursive architectures, yielding SOTA on SST.  
- Limitations: Significance is mostly within sentiment analysis; broader applicability (e.g., unsupervised tasks) is not demonstrated.

Recommendation:
- Decision: Accept with major revisions.  
- Required revisions: Fine-tune embeddings and compare single-layer RNN under identical conditions; add Kalchbrenner’s CNN baseline; fix binary training/evaluation protocol; clarify notation and parameter dimensions; provide quantitative layer-wise analyses; consider standard stability techniques.
