- Paper proposes a deep recursive neural network that stacks recursive layers to bring hierarchical representation learning to structured inputs, evaluated on SST with reported state-of-the-art performance.
- Motivation ties to successes of deep recurrent networks and leverages prior work (Socher et al., Chung et al.) with sensible design choices like pretrained word vectors and ReLU.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results: reported SOTA on SST for fine-grained sentiment classification.
- Clear, well-motivated approach: stacking recursive layers and untying leaf/internal parameters are logically justified.
- Thorough experimental setup: systematic variation of depth/width, baselines, dropout, and L2 regularization.
- Qualitative analyses: input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase experiments suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects.
- Excellent clarity of exposition with clear figures and diagrams.

Weaknesses:
- Critical reporting error: Table 1(a) lists test results as development set results, implying potential test-set tuning.
- Missing ablation: no study on the impact of the V matrix connection (Eq. 4), leaving its necessity unclear.
- Minor presentation issues: typos and a malformed/repeated sentence need proofreading.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, well-motivated architecture with strong empirical performance.
- Comprehensive experiments and insightful qualitative analyses.
- Very clear and well-written manuscript.

Arguments for rejection:
- Serious validity concern due to development/test reporting error.
- Unclear contribution of the V matrix without ablation.
- Presentation errors detract from polish.

Overall assessment:
- Strong and promising paper but the test-vs-dev reporting error is a serious issue that must be corrected before acceptance.

Recommendation:
- Conditional acceptance pending correction of the development-set reporting error and a response or preliminary ablation results regarding the V matrix; otherwise recommend rejection.
