- Introduces the scaled latent trace norm for tensors tailored to multitask learning.
- Claims existing norms (overlapped, latent trace) are suboptimal when dimensions or multilinear ranks are heterogeneous.
- Shows improved dependence on multilinear rank and dimensions, yielding better sample complexity for matrix completion, multitask, and multilinear multitask learning.
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition of problem, method, and theory.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including sample complexity and excess risk bounds.
- Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed norm.
- Includes detailed comparisons with existing norms and methods, highlighting advantages of the scaled latent trace norm.
- Strength: Proposes a new norm addressing limitations of prior norms in multitask learning.
- Strength: Delivers comprehensive theoretical guarantees.
- Strength: Empirically validates improvements across multiple settings.
- Weakness: Assumes heterogeneous dimensions and multilinear rank, which may not hold in all practical scenarios.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a few datasets; broader evaluation is needed.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper discussion of practical implications and application guidance.
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses a significant limitation of existing norms with solid theory and supporting experiments.
- Argument against acceptance: Reliance on heterogeneity assumption and limited empirical breadth weaken practical impact.
