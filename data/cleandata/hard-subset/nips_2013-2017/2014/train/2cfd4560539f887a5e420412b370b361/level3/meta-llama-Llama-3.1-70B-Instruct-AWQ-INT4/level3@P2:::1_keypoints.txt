- Proposes deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to model compositionality in language.
- Paper is well-written and pleasant to read.
- Provides a thorough background on recursive neural networks and their NLP applications.
- Motivates deepening recursive nets by discussing limitations of shallow recursive architectures.
- The proposed deep RNN architecture is technically sound with a clear explanation of its operation.
- Evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).
- Experimental results: deep RNNs outperform same-size shallow recursive nets and previous baselines (including multiplicative RNN and paragraph vectors).
- Qualitative analysis shows different layers capture different aspects of compositionality and notions of similarity.
- Shows that adding depth differs qualitatively from adding width, with each layer focusing on different meaning aspects.
- Strength: simple, reasonable idea of stacking recursive nets with clear presentation.
- Strength: experiments are reasonable and cover various analyses of the method.
- Weakness: lacks discussion on why deeper networks help and rationale for the specific architecture choice.
- Weakness: no clear comparison to other deep architectures (e.g., CNNs, LSTMs).
- Overall significance: interesting idea with reasonable experimental support.
- Ratings summary: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Acceptance arguments: novel architecture, empirical gains on SST, thorough background and analyses.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient explanation of depth benefits, missing comparisons to other deep models, unclear how architecture captures hierarchical representations.
