- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture combining depth in structure and depth in space.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank using binary parse trees.  
- Model outperforms shallow recursive nets of comparable size in experiments.  
- Paper is well-written and provides sufficient implementation detail for reproduction.  
- Use of dropout and rectified linear units (ReLUs) is a notable implementation choice.  
- Qualitative analyses suggest different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- No clear justification for keeping word vectors fixed and not fine-tuning, which may confound results.  
- Use of shared dropout units is not well-motivated or explained.  
- Lacks comparison to convolutional sentence model baselines (e.g., Kalchbrenner et al.), limiting evaluation breadth.  
- Claim that each layer captures distinct compositional aspects is not fully supported by rigorous experiments.  
- Stacking multiple layers itself is not novel; more discussion needed on how this work differs from prior stacking approaches.  
- Overall, the paper makes a significant NLP contribution but has methodological and experimental concerns that should be addressed.
