- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network (stacked recursive layers) for modeling compositionality in language.  
- Approach is essentially a depth-generalization of existing recurrent/recursive network ideas.  
- Paper is clearly written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art fine-grained sentiment performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Use of dropout and ReLUs is notable and appears to contribute to performance gains.  
- Some notation (e.g., W^eta, W^xh) and equation organization are unclear and need clarification.  
- Equations should distinguish inputs vs hidden layers and summarize parameter sets/dimensions.  
- Statements in lines 148–154 are speculative and require empirical support.  
- Missing baseline comparison with Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model.  
- Authors do not justify not fine-tuning word vectors; this omission could confound results and allow a single-layer RNN to match performance.  
- Use of shared dropout units is not well justified; alternatives (weight-norm constraints, gradient truncation) should be considered.  
- Binary classification results are flawed because training included neutral examples; experiments should follow the standard protocol.  
- Main contribution is the deep recursive architecture, but many design choices need stronger justification and additional experiments.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with supported claims, but some design choices and notations lack explanation.  
- Clarity: generally clear, but notational and equation improvements are needed.  
- Originality and significance: novel application of depth to recursive networks with strong empirical impact, but concept is a generalization of existing depth ideas.
