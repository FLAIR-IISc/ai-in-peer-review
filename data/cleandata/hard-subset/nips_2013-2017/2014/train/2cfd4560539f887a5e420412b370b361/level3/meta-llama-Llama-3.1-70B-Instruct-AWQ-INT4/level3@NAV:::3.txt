This paper proposes a novel architecture, deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs), which combines the concepts of depth in structure and depth in space. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this architecture on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees as the structure. The proposed model outperforms shallow recursive nets and achieves state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide sufficient detail for reproduction. The idea of stacking multiple recursive layers to create a deep RNN is novel and interesting. The use of dropout and rectifier linear activation units is also noteworthy. The experimental results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size, and the authors provide qualitative analyses to demonstrate that each layer captures different aspects of compositionality.

However, there are some concerns regarding the model and experimental design choices. The authors do not provide a clear justification for keeping the word vectors fixed and not fine-tuning them. This could potentially confound the results, as fine-tuning the word vectors might allow a single-layer RNN to match or exceed the performance of the deep RNN. Additionally, the use of shared dropout units is not well-justified, and the authors should provide more explanation for this choice.

The paper also lacks a comparison to a baseline model that uses a convolutional sentence model, such as the one proposed by Kalchbrenner et al. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed architecture.

Furthermore, the authors' claim that the deep RNN captures different aspects of compositionality at each layer is not fully supported by the experimental results. While the qualitative analyses provide some insight into the operation of the deep RNN, more rigorous experiments are needed to fully demonstrate the benefits of the proposed architecture.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel architecture that combines the concepts of depth in structure and depth in space. However, the idea of stacking multiple layers is not new, and the authors should provide more discussion on how their approach differs from previous work.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing, but there are some concerns regarding the model and experimental design choices that need to be addressed.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel architecture that combines the concepts of depth in structure and depth in space.
* The experimental results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size.
* The use of dropout and rectifier linear activation units is noteworthy.
* The paper provides sufficient detail for reproduction.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The authors do not provide a clear justification for keeping the word vectors fixed and not fine-tuning them.
* The use of shared dropout units is not well-justified.
* The paper lacks a comparison to a baseline model that uses a convolutional sentence model.
* The authors' claim that the deep RNN captures different aspects of compositionality at each layer is not fully supported by the experimental results.
