- Proposes stacking multiple recursive layers to form a deep recursive neural network for compositional semantics.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and achieves state-of-the-art performance over baselines.
- Builds logically on prior work in recursive/recurrent nets and depth in neural networks, with clear motivation and literature grounding.
- Technical formulation is sound, with appropriate use of dropout and L2 regularization to address stability concerns.
- Experimental setup is rigorous, comparing against strong baselines on a standard dataset.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear explanations and effective illustrative figures.
- Novelty lies in applying depth to the structural dimension of recursive networks—a promising research direction.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrase analysis) show different layers capture distinct compositional aspects.
- Lacks deeper theoretical justification for why depth helps specifically in this recursive setting and for certain architectural choices (layers, hidden sizes).
- Missing targeted ablation studies (e.g., impact of untying leaves vs internals, activation choices) to isolate contributions.
- Error analysis is limited and would benefit from a more systematic investigation of failure modes.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for expanded justification of design choices and a focused ablation study.
