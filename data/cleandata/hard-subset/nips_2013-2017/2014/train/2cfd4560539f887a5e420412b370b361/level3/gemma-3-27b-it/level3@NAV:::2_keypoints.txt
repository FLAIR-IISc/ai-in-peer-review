- Introduces a deep recursive neural network architecture by stacking multiple recursive layers to model compositionality in language.  
- Motivates the approach with parallels to successful deep architectures and aims to capture more abstract compositional representations.  
- Evaluates on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and reports state-of-the-art results for fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Builds on prior recursive neural network work and draws inspiration from deep recurrent networks, making the extension logical and promising.  
- Design choices (untied leaf vs. non-terminal parameters and use of ReLUs) are well-motivated given word vector properties and deep training challenges.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrases) provide useful insights into layer-specific representations.  
- Strengths: clear exposition, strong empirical performance, and insightful qualitative analysis.  
- Critical weakness: missing ablation study on the V matrix connection (Eq. 4) to isolate benefits of multi-layer recursion.  
- Reporting error: Table 1(a) incorrectly shows test set results as development set results, raising potential test-set tuning concerns.  
- Minor issues: typos and a repeated sentence need correction.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on correcting Table 1(a) and including the requested ablation study.
