- Novel deep recursive neural network achieving state-of-the-art on SST for fine-grained sentiment.  
- Combines depth in structure and space to model hierarchical compositionality, building on prior RNN work.  
- Employs multiple recursive layers, ReLUs, and large pre-trained word vectors to address shallow RNN limitations.  
- Paper is clearly written with thorough methodology and detailed experimental results.  
- Innovation: untied leaf and nonterminal nodes allowing smaller hidden layers and rectifiers.  
- Innovation: inter-layer connections to capture hierarchical representations.  
- Deeper networks consistently outperform shallow ones, with optimal performance at specific depths.  
- Dropout regularization and pre-trained embeddings enhance robustness and generalization.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) show layers capture distinct compositionality/sentiment aspects.  
- Suggestion: include ablation study removing inter-layer connections (matrix V) to isolate their contribution.  
- Criticism: Table 1(a) reports results on the test set (risk of implicit tuning); should report on development set.  
- Minor typographical errors need correction.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution; recommendation—accept with minor revisions.
