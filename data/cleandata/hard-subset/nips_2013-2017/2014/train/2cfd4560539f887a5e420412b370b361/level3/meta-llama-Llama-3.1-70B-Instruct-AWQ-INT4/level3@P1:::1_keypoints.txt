- Proposes a deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers for fine-grained sentiment classification.
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and easy to read.
- Technically sound with an appealing core idea of adding depth to recursive nets.
- Experimental evaluation is reasonable, including comparisons to prior work and analysis of layer effects.
- Builds on prior RNN/NLP work with a clear background and architecture description.
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and outperforms several baselines, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Lacks a clear theoretical explanation of why deeper recursive networks improve performance.
- Choice of specific architecture appears driven by intuition rather than rigorous analysis.
- Analysis of results is limited; insufficient investigation of learned representations and their relation to sentiment detection.
- Pro-acceptance points: novel idea, clear presentation, solid experimental support, potential to advance NLP.
- Con-acceptance points: missing theoretical justification, weak architectural rationale, and shallow analysis of learned features.
- Overall: promising and suitable for NIPS if the authors address the noted theoretical and analysis shortcomings in the final version.
