- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers to achieve SOTA on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained).  
- Untangles leaf-node and nonterminal-node parameters to better leverage pretrained word vectors and ReLU activations.  
- Introduces inter-layer connections to produce a deeper hierarchical (deep-in-space) representation than traditional recursive networks.  
- Demonstrates that different layers capture distinct aspects of linguistic compositionality.  
- Clear exposition with strong experimental validation and consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Provides theoretical insight via input perturbation and nearest-neighbor analyses of layer representations.  
- Major flaw: Table 1(a) reports final test set results instead of development set, implying test-set tuning and undermining validity.  
- Missing critical ablation: no study on whether parent inputs should come only from the last hidden layer (dropping the V matrix).  
- Minor issues: typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that need correction.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and significant contribution, but the test-set reporting error must be fixed before acceptance.
