- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network (stacking multiple recursive layers).  
- Applied to fine-grained sentiment classification and outperforms shallow recursive nets and previous baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear background and reasonable experimental support.  
- Main idea: combine depth in structure and depth in space to capture hierarchical representations not possible with shallow RNNs.  
- Architecture is technically sound with thorough methodology and experimental setup explanation.  
- Strength: exploratory analyses show different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Strength: uses pre-trained word vectors, rectifiers, and dropout, contributing to improved performance.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear explanation of why deeper networks help specifically for this task.  
- Weakness: no comparison to other deep models (e.g., CNNs, LSTMs) or justification for choosing this specific architecture.  
- Originality: stacking layers is not new, but applying it to recursive networks and combining specific techniques is a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: advances state of the art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and suggests applicability to other tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—authors should provide deeper analysis of depth benefits and justify architecture choices.  
- Rating: 8/10.
