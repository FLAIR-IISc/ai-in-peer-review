This paper proposes a new deep recursive neural network (Deep RNN) architecture for modeling compositionality in language. The key idea is to introduce depth "in space" by stacking multiple recursive layers, analogous to how deep recurrent networks stack multiple recurrent layers to add depth "in time". The proposed architecture is novel in that each node in a given layer receives input not only from its children within that same layer but also from its corresponding node in the layer below. This creates a rich set of connections that allows for hierarchical processing of the tree structure. The authors also introduce a well-motivated "untying" of parameters for leaf (word) nodes and internal (phrase) nodes in the first layer. This enables the use of large, pre-trained word vectors with smaller hidden phrase representations and makes the use of rectified linear units (ReLUs) more natural. The model is evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, where it outperforms previous models, including the Recursive Neural Tensor Network (RNTN) and Paragraph Vectors, establishing a new state-of-the-art result for this task.

**Strengths:**

*   **Originality and Significance:** The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for adding depth to recursive neural networks. While recursive networks are inherently "deep in structure," this work provides an effective method for creating a hierarchy of representations, a key feature of other successful deep learning models. The strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark demonstrate the significance of this contribution and suggest that this architecture could be beneficial for other tasks involving structured data.
*   **Technical Quality:** The modeling choices are clever and effective. The untying of leaf and internal node parameters is a particularly valuable contribution, as it decouples the word embedding size from the phrase representation size, offering greater modeling flexibility and efficiency. The successful application of ReLUs and dropout in this recursive setting further adds to the paper's technical merit. The quantitative results are impressive, and the qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis) provide plausible evidence that different layers learn distinct aspects of compositionality.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation is clearly laid out, the model is described precisely with helpful figures, and the experimental setup is detailed. The authors do a good job of placing their work in the context of prior research on both recursive and deep networks.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Experimental Reporting:** There is a potentially serious issue with the reporting of results in Table 1(a). This table shows the performance of models with varying depths and widths on the final test set. Standard practice requires that hyperparameter tuning and model selection be performed on a validation/development set, with the final test set used only once for evaluating the single best model. Reporting a grid of results on the test set suggests that model selection may have been based on test set performance, which would invalidate the main quantitative claims of the paper. The authors should clarify their procedure and, ideally, replace the test set results in Table 1(a) with the corresponding development set results that were used for model selection.
*   **Missing Ablation Study:** The proposed deep model in Equation (4) includes the term `V(i)h(i-1)η`, which connects a node to its counterpart in the layer below. A crucial ablation study is missing: a comparison to a simpler stacked model where this connection is removed (i.e., `V=0`). In such a model, information would still flow from layer to layer, but only through the compositional process (i.e., a parent's representation at layer `i` would depend on its children's representations at layer `i`, which in turn depend on their own children, eventually linking back to layer `i-1`). Comparing against this baseline would more clearly isolate the benefit of the direct inter-layer, inter-node connections, which are a central part of the proposed architecture.
*   **Minor Clarity Issues:** The paper contains a few typos and minor ambiguities. For instance, in the "Network Training" section, the weight initialization is described as "0.5I + ", with a missing term. A final proofread would be beneficial.

**Summary of arguments:**

*   **Pro-acceptance:**
    1.  Introduces a novel, intuitive, and powerful deep architecture for recursive neural networks.
    2.  Achieves new state-of-the-art results on a standard, challenging sentiment analysis benchmark.
    3.  The architectural and methodological contributions (e.g., untying parameters) are well-motivated and likely to be impactful for future work on structured data.
    4.  The paper is very clearly written and presented.

*   **Con-acceptance:**
    1.  The reporting of results in Table 1(a) raises significant concerns about proper experimental methodology (i.e., potential tuning on the test set). This point must be addressed by the authors.
    2.  A key ablation study that would better justify the specific form of the deep architecture is missing.

In conclusion, this is a strong paper that presents a novel and highly effective architecture. The ideas are elegant, and the results are impressive. However, the concern regarding the experimental reporting on the test set is significant and requires clarification. Assuming this can be satisfactorily addressed in the author response, the paper is a clear contribution to the field.
