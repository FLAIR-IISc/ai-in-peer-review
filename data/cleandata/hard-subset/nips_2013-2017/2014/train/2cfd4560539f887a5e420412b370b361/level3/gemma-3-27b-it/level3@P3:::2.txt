This paper introduces a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture for sentiment classification, achieving state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST). The key idea is to stack multiple recursive layers, analogous to deep feedforward networks, to capture hierarchical representations of compositionality in language. The authors demonstrate the benefits of untying leaf and non-terminal node parameters, using rectified linear units (ReLUs), and leveraging large pre-trained word vectors.

The work builds upon previous research in recursive neural networks (RNNs) [6, 7, 8] and deep recurrent neural networks [10, 11, 12], extending the notion of depth to the recursive setting.  It also draws connections to deep feedforward networks [1] and the importance of hierarchical representation learning. The paper relates to recent advances in word embeddings [2, 19] and regularization techniques like dropout [20]. The authors appropriately cite relevant prior work, positioning their contribution within the existing landscape of NLP and deep learning.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Performance:** The paper presents compelling experimental results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SST dataset. The improvements over existing baselines are significant and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture.
* **Clear Exposition:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the motivation behind the deep RNN architecture and the design choices made. The diagrams (Figures 1 and 2) are particularly helpful in understanding the network structure.
* **Well-Motivated Changes:** The proposed modifications to traditional RNNs, such as untying leaf and internal node parameters and utilizing ReLUs, are well-justified and experimentally validated. The authors convincingly argue for the benefits of each change.
* **Insightful Analysis:** The input perturbation experiment (Section 3.2) and the nearest neighbor phrase analysis provide valuable qualitative insights into how different layers of the deep RNN capture different aspects of compositionality.
* **Thorough Experimentation:** The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, comparing the deep RNN to a variety of baselines and exploring the impact of different hyperparameters.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Missing Ablation Study:** A crucial missing experiment is an ablation study to assess the importance of the V matrix connection (Equation 4) that connects layers. It's unclear whether feeding parent inputs *only* from the last hidden layer (effectively dropping this connection) would significantly impact performance. This would help isolate the contribution of the stacked recursive layers versus simply passing information forward.
* **Test Set Tuning:** Table 1(a) incorrectly reports results on the final test set instead of the development set. This is a serious issue, as it suggests that the models were tuned on the test data, leading to an overly optimistic evaluation.  The authors should re-report results using the development set for model selection and only report the final test set performance *once* for the best model.
* **Typos and Malformed Sentence:** The paper contains minor typos and at least one repeated/malformed sentence that needs to be carefully reviewed and corrected before publication. (Specific location not indicated to avoid unnecessary searching, but proofreading is recommended.)



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of recursive neural networks and sentiment analysis. The proposed deep RNN architecture is novel, well-motivated, and demonstrably effective. The clarity of the writing and the insightful analysis further strengthen the paper. However, the issue with test set reporting is a critical flaw that needs to be addressed. The missing ablation study would also be a valuable addition.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* State-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark.
* Novel and well-motivated architecture.
* Clear and insightful exposition.
* Thorough experimental evaluation (aside from the missing ablation study).

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Incorrect reporting of results on the test set (a serious flaw).
* Lack of an ablation study on the V matrix connection.
* Minor typos and malformed sentence.

**Recommendation:**

Conditional Acceptance.  The paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to NIPS. However, acceptance is contingent upon the authors: 1) Correcting the error in Table 1(a) by re-reporting results using the development set for model selection and reporting the final test set performance only once. 2) Conducting and reporting the results of an ablation study to assess the importance of the V matrix connection. 3) Proofreading the paper carefully to correct typos and the malformed sentence.  Once these issues are addressed, the paper should be accepted.
