- Introduces a deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers on parse-tree structure, adding “depth in space” alongside structural depth, with untied leaf/internal weights, ReLU, and dropout.  
- Positioned against prior recursive sentiment models and deep RNNs; evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and compares to strong baselines.  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on SST and outperforms shallow models with comparable parameter budgets; qualitative probes suggest different layers capture different compositional roles (intensity vs. polarity).  
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with concrete figures/equations.  
- Strength: Technically sound training and stability measures (AdaGrad, dropout, L2, initialization) and careful parameter-matched comparisons.  
- Strength: Simple, appealing architectural idea bridging deep RNNs and tree-structured nets.  
- Strength: Solid empirical support and informative qualitative analyses.  
- Strength: Sensible engineering choices (untied leaves, fixed pretrained embeddings, shared dropout masks) that yield gains.  
- Weakness: Limited principled explanation for why depth helps; ablations isolating contributing factors are missing.  
- Weakness: Key architectural choices under-explained (top-only supervision, specific inter-layer affine connection) and not ablated against alternatives (residual/skip, tying).  
- Weakness: Evaluation scope limited to SST—no other tree-structured tasks, parse-noise robustness, or constituency vs. dependency comparisons.  
- Weakness: No reporting of variance across runs or statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: No quantification of efficiency (training/inference time, memory, convergence vs. depth).  
- Quality: Technically solid with good depth-controlled study, but would benefit from more ablations and statistical rigor.  
- Clarity: Very clear and well-organized; distinction between depth in structure vs. space well articulated; suggest adding complexity discussion and dropout schematic.  
- Originality: Natural yet novel extension of recursive models by stacking layers; clearly placed relative to prior work.  
- Significance: Moderate to high—likely to influence follow-up work, but broader impact depends on generalization beyond sentiment.  
- Suggestions: Add controlled ablations (ReLU, tied/untied, dropout, supervision schemes), report mean±std and significance, include efficiency metrics, test parse sensitivity and other tasks, perform linguistic error analyses, and consider residual/gated inter-layer variants.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, novel idea; strong empirical results (SOTA); careful experiments and clear writing.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited analysis of mechanisms, single-dataset evaluation, missing variance/efficiency reporting, and unablated architectural choices.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
