Summary and relation to prior work: This paper proposes deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs) that add depth in “space” to tree-structured models by stacking recursive layers with inter-layer connections (V), in addition to the usual inter-node (structural) connections. The model unties leaf and nonterminal parameters, uses ReLUs for hidden units, and leverages large pretrained word vectors while keeping smaller hidden sizes for nonterminals. Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, the approach achieves state-of-the-art performance over prior recursive models such as RNN/RNTN and non-tree baselines like Paragraph Vectors and DCNN. The work is well situated relative to recursive models for compositionality (e.g., Socher et al.) and deep recurrent architectures that distinguish depth in time vs. space (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio; Hermans & Schrauwen), and extends these ideas to tree structures with careful engineering.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and most design choices (untied leaf/internal parameters, ReLUs, dropout, fixed large word vectors, and stacked recursive layers) are well-motivated and empirically validated. The analyses (input perturbation and nearest neighbors across layers) are insightful. However, a key ablation is missing: the role of the V connection. Please report results when parent inputs come only from the last hidden layer within the structural computation (i.e., dropping V), to quantify how much the inter-layer skip contributes beyond stacking. Also clarify the impact of connecting the classifier only to the top layer versus to all layers.

Clarity: The exposition is very clear and reproducibility is strong (training details, regularization, initialization). Please fix typos and the repeated/malformed sentence noted by the reviewers. Importantly, Table 1(a) appears to report model selection comparisons on the final test set; this risks test-set tuning. Hyperparameter sweeps and model selection should be on the development set, with a single final test evaluation.

Originality: The combination of inter-node and inter-layer recursion for trees is a clever and nontrivial extension of deep recurrent ideas to structured inputs. The untying of leaf/nonterminal parameters and effective use of ReLUs in recursive settings are well-justified.

Significance: Results are strong and likely to influence subsequent work on hierarchical composition and tree-structured deep models.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, well-motivated architecture with new inter-layer connections for trees
- Strong empirical gains and solid analyses
- Practical insights: ReLUs in recursive nets; leveraging large word vectors with small hidden states

Cons against acceptance:
- Potential test-set tuning in Table 1(a); needs correction
- Missing ablation on the V connection (and output-layer wiring)
- Minor writing/formatting issues to fix
