- Introduces deep recursive neural networks (stacked recursive layers) to add spatial depth to recursive models.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), achieving state-of-the-art fine-grained sentiment performance.  
- Builds on prior recursive/recurrent and deep learning work and targets a gap: lack of hierarchical depth in recursive models.  
- Novelty: explicit stacking of recursive layers to better capture compositional hierarchies.  
- Strong empirical results that outperform shallow RNNs and prior published baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility.  
- Ablation and analysis (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) give preliminary insights into layer-specific representations.  
- Practical choices (ReLUs, dropout, pre-trained word vectors) contribute to performance gains.  
- Insufficient justification for several design choices (e.g., keeping word vectors fixed, shared dropout across a sentence).  
- Missing important baseline comparisons, notably Kalchbrenner et al.’s dynamic CNN.  
- Stability fixes (L2 regularization) seem ad-hoc; alternatives like weight normalization or gradient clipping should be considered.  
- Some claims about layered compositionality are speculative and need stronger empirical support.  
- Binary classification evaluation is flawed because neutral examples were included in training; needs standard binary protocol.  
- Limited scope/generalizability: results shown only on supervised fine-grained labels; need experiments on global-label datasets and additional ablations (e.g., fine-tuning embeddings).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach; strong results; clear writing; useful exploratory analyses.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unjustified design choices, incomplete comparisons, flawed binary protocol, and uncertain generalizability.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution deserving acceptance pending minor revisions to address methodological and evaluation concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add missing baseline, correct binary protocol, justify design choices, explore alternative regularization, and discuss limitations/future work.
