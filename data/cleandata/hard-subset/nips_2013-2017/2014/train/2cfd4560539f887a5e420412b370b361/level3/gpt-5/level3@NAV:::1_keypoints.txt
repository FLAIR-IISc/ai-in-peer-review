- Proposal: Introduces deep recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive tree layers, separating tree-structure depth from stacking depth.  
- Architecture: Within-layer propagation follows parse-tree structure; vertical inter-layer connections transfer information; supervision applied only at the top layer for all nodes.  
- Evaluation: Tested on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained) against shallow recursive nets, MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, and DCNN.  
- Results: Depth yields consistent gains under parameter-matched comparisons and achieves a new state-of-the-art on SST.  
- Qualitative analysis: Input perturbations and nearest-neighbor phrase retrieval show layers capture complementary aspects of compositionality and sentiment intensity.  
- Technical quality: Work is technically sound with well-motivated training/regularization (rectifiers, dropout, AdaGrad, untied parameters) contributing to robustness.  
- Experimental rigor: Parameter-controlled comparisons are appreciated, but statistical significance is not reported and experiments are limited to one dataset.  
- Ablation concern: Insufficient disentanglement of depth effects from other factors (rectifiers, dropout, untied parameters).  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and clearly describes architecture, training, and qualitative probes.  
- Explanatory gap: Needs deeper discussion of why stacking helps tree composition and rationale for design choices (top-layer supervision, rectifiers, width–depth trade-offs).  
- Originality: Conceptually simple and incremental but novel within tree-structured models, with insightful qualitative analyses.  
- Significance: Practical and likely adoptable, strengthened by SST SOTA, but broader impact limited by single-task evaluation.  
- Pros for acceptance: Clear, reproducible architecture; strong parameter-matched empirical gains and SOTA on SST; informative qualitative layer analyses.  
- Cons against acceptance: Limited dataset/task scope, no significance testing, and inadequate disentanglement of depth vs. other design choices.  
- Suggested additions: Add statistical tests, multi-dataset evaluations (e.g., paraphrase, entailment), thorough ablations for rectifiers/dropout/untying, and comparisons to alternative inter-layer designs (residuals, gated/tree-LSTM).  
- Practical considerations: Include discussion of computational cost and inference speed.
