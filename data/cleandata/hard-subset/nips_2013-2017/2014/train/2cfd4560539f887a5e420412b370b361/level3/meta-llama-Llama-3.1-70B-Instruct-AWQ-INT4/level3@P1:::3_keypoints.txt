- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network architecture by stacking multiple recursive layers to add depth and hierarchical representation.
- Evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification (Stanford Sentiment Treebank) and reports state-of-the-art performance over shallow models.
- Paper is well-written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Provides thorough analysis of how added depth affects compositionality and layer-wise meaning capture.
- Uses dropout and ReLUs, shown to improve performance and produce sparse representations.
- Uses pre-trained word embeddings kept fixed during training as a baseline.
- Use of shared dropout units is not clearly justified.
- Decision not to fine-tune word vectors could confound results and is questionable.
- Binary classification results are problematic because neutral examples were included in training; experiments should follow standard protocol.
- Lacks comparison with relevant baselines (e.g., Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model).
- More empirical evidence is needed to support claims about benefits of adding depth across space, especially with single root labels.
- Notation and equation presentation could be clearer; weights/parameter sets and dimensions should be summarized and inputs versus hidden layers distinguished.
- Technically sound with supported claims, but several experimental and presentation issues need addressing.
- Strengths: novel architecture, state-of-the-art results, and thorough depth analysis; Weaknesses: missing baselines, questionable design choices, and insufficient empirical justification for some claims.
