- Introduces a deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers for sentiment classification, achieving state-of-the-art on SST.  
- Key architectural ideas: stack recursive layers, untie leaf and non-terminal parameters, use ReLUs, and leverage large pre-trained word vectors.  
- Builds on prior recursive and deep recurrent network work and connects to deep feedforward networks and word embedding advances.  
- Strong empirical performance with significant improvements over baselines on SST.  
- Exceptionally clear exposition and helpful diagrams explaining the model.  
- Well-motivated architectural modifications that are experimentally validated.  
- Insightful qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analysis) showing layer-wise compositionality.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons and hyperparameter exploration.  
- Missing a crucial ablation study on the V matrix connection that links layers, leaving its contribution unclear.  
- Serious evaluation flaw: Table 1(a) reports final test-set results instead of development-set results used for model selection (suggesting test-set tuning).  
- Contains minor typos and at least one malformed/repeated sentence needing proofreading.  
- Overall: strong and novel contribution but marred by the test-reporting error and missing ablation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA results, novel/meaningful architecture, clear writing, and broad experimentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incorrect test reporting, absent V-matrix ablation, and minor editorial issues.  
- Recommendation: Conditional acceptance pending correction of test-set reporting, inclusion of the V-matrix ablation study, and careful proofreading.
