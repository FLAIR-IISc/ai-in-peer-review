- Proposal: stack multiple recursive layers with learned node-to-node V connections to separate tree-structural depth from representational depth.  
- Architecture details: untie leaf vs. internal parameters in first layer, use ReLUs, fixed large pretrained word vectors, and small hidden phrase representations.  
- Evaluation: tested on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (5-way and binary), compared to MV-RNN, RNTN, DCNN, Paragraph Vectors, and shallow RNN baselines.  
- Main empirical claim: deeper, narrower deep RNNs outperform parameter-matched shallow RNNs and achieve state-of-the-art on SST.  
- Qualitative finding: lower layers capture local/lexical and syntactic patterns; upper layers capture sentiment intensity and polarity.  
- Novelty: stacking recursive layers with explicit across-layer node connections for tree-structured computation is a natural and novel extension.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated architecture disentangling structural and representational depth with documented training details.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance with parameter-matched comparisons showing depth benefits up to a point.  
- Strength: pragmatic engineering choices (untied params, ReLUs, dropout, AdaGrad, fixed embeddings) that improve performance.  
- Strength: insightful qualitative analyses supporting layer-wise specialization claims.  
- Weakness: possible hyperparameter selection on the test set (Table 1a) — protocol must use dev set for selection and report a single test result.  
- Weakness: missing ablations on the inter-layer V connection, alternative output connectivity (deep supervision), and component-wise contributions (untying, ReLU vs tanh, dropout vs L2, embedding tuning).  
- Weakness: no variance reporting or statistical significance testing across runs/seeds, reducing robustness of claims.  
- Weakness: unclear parameter-budget matching details and missing training time/complexity reporting.  
- Weakness: minor clarity issues and typos/malformed sentences (incomplete initialization descriptions and reference errors).  
- Request: re-run or re-report model selection strictly on the development set and provide averaged results over multiple seeds with standard deviations if possible.  
- Request: provide ablations removing/modifying V, attaching outputs to all layers, and isolating effects of untying, activation choice, regularization, and embedding fine-tuning.  
- Request: report exact parameter counts per configuration, brief training time/compute notes, and fix typos/missing initialization details.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, and significant work with strong empirical and qualitative contributions; recommend acceptance pending correction of evaluation protocol and key ablations.
