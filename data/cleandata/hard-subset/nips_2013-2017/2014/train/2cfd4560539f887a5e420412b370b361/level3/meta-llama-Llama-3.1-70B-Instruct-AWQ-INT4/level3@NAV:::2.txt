**Paper Review: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language**

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a new deep recursive neural architecture for modeling compositionality in natural language. The authors introduce a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, which allows for a hierarchical representation of language. They evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank dataset. The authors also provide exploratory analyses of the effect of multiple layers and show that they capture different aspects of compositionality in language.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a novel deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized exposition of their work, including a detailed description of the proposed architecture and its components.
* The experimental results are impressive, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits of using deep recursive neural networks.
* The use of rectified linear units (ReLUs) in the recursive setting is effective, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the benefits of using ReLUs.

Weaknesses:

* The paper is missing an ablation study on the importance of the V matrix connection in the proposed architecture.
* The authors report results on the final test set instead of the development set in Table 1(a), which implies tuning on the test set.
* There are typos and a repeated/malformed sentence in the paper that need to be fixed.

**Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance**

* Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits of using deep recursive neural networks. However, the missing ablation study and the reporting of results on the final test set instead of the development set are notable weaknesses.
* Clarity: The paper is clearly written, and the authors provide a detailed description of the proposed architecture and its components. However, there are some typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that need to be fixed.
* Originality: The paper proposes a novel deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The use of ReLUs in the recursive setting is also a notable contribution.
* Significance: The paper addresses a difficult problem in natural language processing and provides a new state-of-the-art result on a challenging dataset. The proposed architecture has the potential to be applied to other tasks in natural language processing.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits of using deep recursive neural networks.
* The paper addresses a difficult problem in natural language processing and provides a new state-of-the-art result on a challenging dataset.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper is missing an ablation study on the importance of the V matrix connection in the proposed architecture.
* The authors report results on the final test set instead of the development set in Table 1(a), which implies tuning on the test set.
* There are typos and a repeated/malformed sentence in the paper that need to be fixed.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions. The authors should address the missing ablation study, report results on the development set instead of the final test set, and fix the typos and repeated/malformed sentence.
