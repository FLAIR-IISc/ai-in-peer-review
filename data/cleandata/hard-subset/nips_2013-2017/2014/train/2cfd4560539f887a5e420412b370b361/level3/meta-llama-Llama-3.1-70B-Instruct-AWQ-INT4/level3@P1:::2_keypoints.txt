- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network architecture built by stacking multiple recursive layers.  
- Applied to fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset.  
- Key innovations: untied leaf vs. nonterminal parameters, use of ReLUs in recursive setting, and large unsupervised word vectors with smaller nonterminal representations.  
- Deep architecture uses inter-layer and inter-node connections rather than simple layer stacking.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing to shallow recursive nets, multiplicative RNNs, and paragraph vectors.  
- Results show the deep RNN outperforms all baselines.  
- Missing ablation study on the design choice of parent inputs coming only from the last hidden layer (dropping the V matrix).  
- Table 1(a) incorrectly reports test-set results instead of development-set results, implying tuning on the test set.  
- Contains minor typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that need correction.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Reproducible for expert readers given provided details.  
- Original and significant contribution with potential for wide adoption in NLP.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
