- Introduces a deep recursive neural network that stacks recursive layers (depth in structure + depth in space) and achieves state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Originality/significance: novel, natural extension of recursive/recurrent nets that yields progressively more abstract phrase representations and is likely influential.  
- Technical quality: well-motivated architecture; untied leaf/internal parameters elegantly enable pre-trained word vectors and ReLU phrase activations.  
- Solid engineering: successful integration of ReLUs, dropout, and pre-trained embeddings into a recursive framework driving strong performance.  
- Clarity/exposition: paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, precise equations, helpful diagram, and generally well-designed experiments.  
- Qualitative analysis: input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analyses show different layers capture word-level, syntactic, and abstract sentiment features.  
- Major experimental concern: Table 1(a) suggests hyperparameter/model selection used the test set; authors must clarify and report hyperparameter sweeps on the development set.  
- Missing ablation: no experiment removing the inter-layer V connection to isolate the contribution of direct node-wise inter-layer links versus simple stacking.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos and at least one malformed/repeated sentence need proofreading.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, accept; correct the test-set reporting and, if possible, add the ablation to strengthen the paper.
