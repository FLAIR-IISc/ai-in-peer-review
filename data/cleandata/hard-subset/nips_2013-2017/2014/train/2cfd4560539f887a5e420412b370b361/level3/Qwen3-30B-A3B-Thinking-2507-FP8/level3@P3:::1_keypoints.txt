- Proposes "deep recursive neural networks" that stack multiple recursive layers to combine structural depth with spatial (layer) depth.  
- Extends established recursive neural network frameworks by adapting ideas from deep recurrent networks to the recursive setting.  
- Claims enhanced hierarchical and compositional representations for language processing.  
- Thorough evaluation on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank demonstrating outperforming shallow recursive nets and prior state-of-the-art.  
- Experiments include quantitative fine-grained sentiment results and qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases).  
- Technically sound with reproducible details provided (network configs, hyperparameters, training procedures).  
- Exceptionally clear writing and effective visualizations explaining architecture and information flow.  
- Original contribution in stacking recursive layers, clearly differentiated from prior work.  
- Significant impact: addresses a key limitation of recursive networks and establishes a new SST benchmark.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper theoretical or empirical explanation for why increased depth improves performance.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification and exploration for choosing 2–4 layers and limited analysis of depth vs. width trade-offs.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept.
