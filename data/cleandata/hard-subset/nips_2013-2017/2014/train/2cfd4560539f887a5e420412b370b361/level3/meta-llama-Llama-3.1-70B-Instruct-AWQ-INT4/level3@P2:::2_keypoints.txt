- Introduces a new deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers for hierarchical representations.  
- Applies the architecture to fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and shows deep RNNs outperform equally sized shallow RNNs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming multiplicative RNNs and paragraph vectors.  
- Provides a clear, well-motivated description of the model as an extension of traditional recursive networks.  
- Stacking layers yields more hierarchical representations; ReLUs enable sparse, nonnegative intermediate representations.  
- Leverages large unsupervised word vectors while keeping small hidden nonterminal representations for efficiency.  
- Thorough experimental comparison and qualitative analyses suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects and similarities.  
- Serious issue: Table 1(a) reports final test results instead of development-set results, implying tuning on the test set.  
- Paper contains typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that require correction.  
- Lacks sufficient ablation studies (e.g., effect of the V matrix connection) to isolate contributions.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on fixing test-reporting, adding ablation studies and experimental details (hyperparameter tuning, compute), and improving qualitative analyses.
