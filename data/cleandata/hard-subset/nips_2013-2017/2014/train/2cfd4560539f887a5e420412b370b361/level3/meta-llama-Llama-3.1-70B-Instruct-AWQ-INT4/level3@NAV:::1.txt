**Paper Review: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language**

**Summary:**
This paper introduces a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture, constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, to model compositionality in natural language. The authors evaluate their proposed model on fine-grained sentiment classification and demonstrate its effectiveness in outperforming shallow recursive nets and previous baselines.

**Relation to Previous Work:**
The paper builds upon previous work on recursive neural networks (RNNs) and their application to natural language processing tasks. The authors draw inspiration from recent developments in deep recurrent neural networks and propose a similar distinction between depth in structure and depth in space for RNNs.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technically sound work**: The paper presents a well-defined architecture and provides a clear explanation of the proposed deep RNN model.
2. **Good experimental results**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on fine-grained sentiment classification, outperforming shallow recursive nets and previous baselines.
3. **Well-written paper**: The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of discussion on architecture choice**: The authors do not provide a detailed explanation of why they chose to stack multiple recursive layers, and how this architecture was selected.
2. **Limited analysis of layer contributions**: While the authors provide some qualitative analysis of the contributions of each layer, a more in-depth analysis would be beneficial in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed architecture.

**Originality:**
The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, namely the stacking of multiple recursive layers to form a deep RNN architecture. While the idea is simple, it is well-executed and demonstrates promising results.

**Significance:**
The paper addresses a significant problem in natural language processing, namely the modeling of compositionality in language. The proposed architecture shows promise in improving the state-of-the-art in fine-grained sentiment classification, and its application to other NLP tasks is an exciting area of future research.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

Pro:

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for modeling compositionality in language.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed model over shallow recursive nets and previous baselines.

Con:

* The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the architecture choice and the contributions of each layer.
* The analysis of the proposed model is limited to fine-grained sentiment classification, and its application to other NLP tasks is not explored.

**Recommendation:**
I recommend accepting the paper, but with the suggestion that the authors provide a more detailed explanation of their architecture choice and a more in-depth analysis of the contributions of each layer.
