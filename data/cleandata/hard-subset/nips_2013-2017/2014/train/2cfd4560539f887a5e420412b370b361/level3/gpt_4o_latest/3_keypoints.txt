Strengths:
- Novel architecture: Deep RNN extends recursive networks by adding depth across space, capturing hierarchical compositionality in language.
- Strong performance: Achieves state-of-the-art results on SST, benefiting from dropout and ReLUs for improved convergence.
- Qualitative analysis: Provides insightful analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) showing distinct layer roles.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written with sufficient experimental and training details.

Weaknesses:
- Design justification lacking: Choices like shared dropout units and connecting output only to final layer are not well motivated or validated.
- Missing parameter definitions: Key parameters (e.g., W^η, W^{xh}) and their dimensionalities are not clearly specified.
- Speculative claims: Assertions about hierarchical representations are not fully supported by empirical evidence.
- Incomplete baselines: Omits comparison to Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN, weakening baseline comprehensiveness.
- Word embedding protocol: Decision not to fine-tune pre-trained embeddings is questionable and may affect results.
- Nonstandard binary protocol: Including neutral examples in binary classification departs from standard evaluation, harming comparability.
- Stability techniques not explored: No investigation of weight norm constraints, gradient truncation, or other methods to address training stability.

Recommendations:
- Empirically justify or validate architectural design choices (shared dropout, output connections).
- Provide clear parameter definitions and dimensionalities.
- Include comparisons with Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN and follow standard binary classification protocols.
- Evaluate the impact of fine-tuning word embeddings and explore stability techniques for training.
- Run additional experiments on global-label datasets and unsupervised tasks to test generality.

Pro / Con:
- Pro: Novel, impactful Deep RNN with strong empirical results and contributions to modeling hierarchical compositionality.
- Con: Insufficient justification of design choices, unclear parameters, and protocol deviations reduce rigor.

Conclusion:
- Conditional acceptance recommended: paper is strong in novelty and performance but should address identified weaknesses to improve scientific rigor and broader impact.
