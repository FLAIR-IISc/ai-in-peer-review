- Introduces a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers for hierarchical representations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition.  
- Proposed modifications to traditional recursive nets are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated.  
- Untying leaf-node and nonterminal-node parameters enables use of large pretrained word vectors with smaller nonterminal representations.  
- Using ReLU activations in the recursive setting is effective.  
- Leveraging large unsupervised word vectors while keeping smaller hidden nonterminal representations is beneficial.  
- Deep architecture adds inter-layer and inter-node connections (not just stacked layers) for richer representations.  
- Exploratory analysis shows multiple layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Deep RNN outperforms equally sized shallow recursive networks.  
- Qualitative analysis suggests each layer learns distinct compositional features.  
- Missing an ablation study on the V matrix connection to quantify its contribution.  
- Table 1(a) incorrectly reports final test results instead of development results, implying test-set tuning.  
- Paper contains a few typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that need fixing.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, significant contribution with recommendation to accept.
