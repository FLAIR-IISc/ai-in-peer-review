- Proposes a deep recursive neural network architecture by stacking multiple recursive layers for structural processing.  
- Applies the architecture to fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees.  
- Paper is well-written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Architecture generalizes deep feedforward networks by adding structural processing inside each layer.  
- Multiple layers enable hierarchical representations absent in conventional recursive nets.  
- Uses dropout and rectifier linear units to improve performance.  
- Experiments on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank show deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of comparable size.  
- Compares to prior work (multiplicative RNN, Paragraph Vectors) and reports state-of-the-art results on SST.  
- Strength: clear explanation, good organization, and thorough experimental analysis.  
- Limitation: unclear justification for using shared dropout units per sentence and layer.  
- Limitation: no explanation for not fine-tuning word vectors, which could confound comparisons.  
- Limitation: lacks baseline comparison to Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model.  
- Originality: novel combination of deep feedforward benefits with recursive structural processing, though components like dropout/ReLU are not entirely new.  
- Significance: addresses a challenging NLP task and achieves strong practical results on SST.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, SOTA performance, reproducibility, and thorough analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing justification for shared dropout, unexplained absence of word-vector fine-tuning, and missing conv-net baseline.
