This paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on the challenging sentiment classification dataset, Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST). The authors propose a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, which allows for a hierarchical representation of the data. The paper is well-written, and the exposition is very clear.

The proposed changes to the traditional recursive neural network architecture are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits of untying leaf-node and nonterminal-node parameters, which allows for the use of large pretrained word vectors and smaller hidden nonterminal representations. The use of rectified linear units (ReLUs) in the recursive setting is also shown to be effective. The authors demonstrate that leveraging large unsupervised word vectors while keeping smaller hidden nonterminal representations is beneficial.

The deep architecture introduces inter-layer and inter-node connections rather than simply stacking layers, which allows for a more complex representation of the data. The authors provide an exploratory analysis of the effect of multiple layers and show that they capture different aspects of compositionality in language.

The results are impressive, with the deep RNN outperforming shallow recursive nets of the same size and achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SST dataset. The authors also provide a qualitative analysis of the representations learned by the deep RNN, which suggests that each layer captures a different aspect of compositionality.

However, there are a few areas for improvement. The paper is missing an ablation study on the effect of the V matrix connection, which would help to understand the contribution of the inter-layer connections to the performance of the deep RNN. Additionally, Table 1(a) improperly reports results on the final test set instead of the development set, implying tuning on the test set. Finally, the paper contains a few typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that needs fixing.

Overall, this paper is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing, and the proposed deep recursive neural network architecture has the potential to be widely adopted. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits and limitations of the architecture, and the results are impressive.
