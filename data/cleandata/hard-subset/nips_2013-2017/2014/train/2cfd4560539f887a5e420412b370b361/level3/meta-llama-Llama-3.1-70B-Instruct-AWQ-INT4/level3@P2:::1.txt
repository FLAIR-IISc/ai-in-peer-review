The paper "Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language" proposes a new architecture for modeling compositionality in natural language, called deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs). The authors introduce a novel way of stacking multiple recursive layers to capture hierarchical representations in language. The paper is well-written and pleasant to read, and the core idea of stacking multiple recursive nets is appealing.

The authors provide a thorough background on recursive neural networks and their applications in natural language processing. They also discuss the limitations of shallow recursive neural networks and the benefits of deepening them. The proposed deep RNN architecture is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how it works.

The paper evaluates the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) dataset. The experimental results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size, and also outperform previous baselines on the sentiment analysis task, including a multiplicative RNN variant and paragraph vectors.

The authors also provide qualitative experiments to analyze the effect of multiple layers on the compositionality of language. They show that each layer captures a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity. The results suggest that adding depth to a recursive net is different from adding width, and that each layer focuses on different aspects of meaning.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of stacking multiple recursive nets is simple and reasonable, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how it works. Secondly, the experimental results are reasonable and cover various aspects of the method. Finally, the paper provides a thorough background on recursive neural networks and their applications in natural language processing.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. One of the main limitations is the lack of discussion on why deeper networks help and why the specific architecture was chosen. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the deep RNN architecture captures hierarchical representations in language, and why it is better than other architectures.

Another limitation is that the paper does not provide a clear comparison with other deep learning architectures, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) or long short-term memory (LSTM) networks. The authors only compare their results with previous baselines on the sentiment analysis task, but do not provide a clear discussion of how their architecture compares to other deep learning architectures.
