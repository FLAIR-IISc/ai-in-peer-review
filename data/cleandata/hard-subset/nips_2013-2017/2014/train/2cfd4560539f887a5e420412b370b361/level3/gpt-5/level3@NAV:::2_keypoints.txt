- Proposal: Introduces deep recursive neural networks that add spatial depth to tree models by stacking recursive layers with inter-layer (V) connections.  
- Model design: Unties leaf and nonterminal parameters, uses ReLUs, fixes large pretrained word vectors, and uses smaller hidden sizes for nonterminals.  
- Empirical result: Achieves state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank versus prior recursive models and non-tree baselines.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends deep recurrent ideas (depth in time vs. space) to tree structures and relates to Socher et al.'s compositional models.  
- Technical quality: Methodology is sound, design choices are well-motivated, and analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors across layers) are insightful.  
- Missing ablation: Key experiment missing—quantify the contribution of the V inter-layer connection by dropping it and reporting results.  
- Classifier wiring: Need clarification/ablation on connecting the classifier to only the top layer versus all layers.  
- Reproducibility and clarity: Exposition and training details are clear, but fix typos and a repeated/malformed sentence.  
- Evaluation concern: Table 1(a) appears to use test set for model selection—risk of test-set tuning; hyperparameter/model selection should be done on dev set with a single final test evaluation.  
- Originality: Clever, nontrivial extension combining inter-node and inter-layer recursion for trees; untying leaf/nonterminal and using ReLUs are well-justified.  
- Significance: Strong results likely to influence future hierarchical composition and tree-structured deep model work.  
- Pros: Clear architecture, strong empirical gains, and practical insights (ReLUs in recursive nets; leveraging large word vectors with small hidden states).  
- Cons: Potential test-set tuning, missing ablations (V and output wiring), and minor writing/formatting issues to address.
