- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers and distinguishes structural depth (parse tree) from spatial depth (stacked layers).  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank for fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Original and significant extension of recursive models: stacking with inter-node connections yields more powerful hierarchical representations.  
- Strong experimental support: untying leaf/internal parameters, applying ReLUs, and showing deeper models outperform shallow ones with similar parameter counts.  
- Qualitative analyses indicate different layers capture distinct aspects of compositionality.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Missing key ablation: no comparison removing the V matrix that links corresponding nodes across layers to justify that architectural choice.  
- Experimental reporting concern: Table 1(a) reports multiple test accuracies, suggesting possible tuning on the test set instead of using development set for model selection.  
- Contains minor typos and at least one malformed sentence—needs proofreading.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, impactful paper with addressable weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
