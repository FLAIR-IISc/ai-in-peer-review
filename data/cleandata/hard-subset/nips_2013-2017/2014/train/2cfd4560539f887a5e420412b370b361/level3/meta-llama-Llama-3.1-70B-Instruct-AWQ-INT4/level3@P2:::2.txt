This paper introduces a new deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture, which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors apply this architecture to the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees as the structure. They evaluate the proposed model on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) dataset and show that deep RNNs outperform their shallow counterparts of the same size. Furthermore, their approach outperforms previous baselines on the sentiment analysis task, including a multiplicative RNN variant and the recently introduced paragraph vectors, achieving new state-of-the-art results.

The authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of their proposed architecture, which is an extension of the traditional recursive neural network. They introduce a new way of processing the input by stacking multiple recursive layers, which allows for a more hierarchical representation of the data. The use of rectified linear units (ReLUs) in the recursive setting is also a clever choice, as it allows for sparse and nonnegative intermediate representations. Additionally, the authors leverage large unsupervised word vectors while keeping smaller hidden nonterminal representations, which is beneficial for the model.

The experimental results are impressive, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model. They compare their approach to several baselines, including shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and paragraph vectors. The results show that deep RNNs outperform their shallow counterparts and achieve state-of-the-art performance on the sentiment analysis task. The authors also provide qualitative experiments that suggest that each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity.

However, there are a few issues with the paper. One of the main concerns is that Table 1(a) improperly reports results on the final test set instead of the development set, implying tuning on the test set. This is a serious issue, as it can lead to overfitting and biased results. Additionally, the paper contains typos and a repeated/malformed sentence that needs fixing.

In terms of the criteria for evaluation, the paper scores high on originality, as it introduces a new architecture that is an extension of the traditional recursive neural network. The paper also scores high on significance, as it achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The clarity of the paper is also good, as the authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of their proposed architecture. However, the paper could benefit from more ablation studies, such as comparing the performance of the model with and without the V matrix connection.

Overall, this is a strong paper that introduces a new and effective architecture for sentiment classification. The experimental results are impressive, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model. However, there are a few issues with the paper that need to be addressed, including the improper reporting of results on the final test set and the lack of ablation studies.
