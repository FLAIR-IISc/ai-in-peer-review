This paper proposes a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture, constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, and evaluates its performance on fine-grained sentiment classification. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, making it a pleasure to read.

The paper is technically sound, and the core idea of stacking multiple recursive nets is appealing. The authors provide reasonable experimental results that cover various aspects of their method, including a comparison with previous work and an analysis of the effect of multiple layers on the task of fine-grained sentiment detection.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to build upon previous work in the field of natural language processing (NLP) and recursive neural networks (RNNs). The authors provide a clear background on the topic, discussing the limitations of traditional RNNs and the benefits of introducing depth into these models. They also provide a thorough explanation of their proposed architecture and its components.

The experimental results presented in the paper are also a strength. The authors evaluate their model on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) dataset and compare its performance to several baselines, including shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and paragraph vectors. The results show that the deep RNN outperforms these baselines, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the task.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One of the main weaknesses is the lack of discussion on why deeper networks help and why the specific architecture was chosen. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the theoretical benefits of stacking multiple recursive layers, and the choice of architecture seems to be based on intuition rather than a rigorous analysis.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the results. While the authors provide some qualitative experiments to analyze the effect of multiple layers on the task, they do not provide a detailed analysis of the learned representations and how they relate to the task of sentiment detection.

Despite these limitations, the paper presents an interesting idea with clear background and reasonable experimental support. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed architecture on a challenging task, and their results have the potential to advance the state of the art in NLP.
