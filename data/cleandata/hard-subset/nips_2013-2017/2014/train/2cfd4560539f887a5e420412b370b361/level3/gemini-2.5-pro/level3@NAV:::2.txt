This paper introduces a novel deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) for modeling compositionality in language, extending prior work on recursive neural networks (e.g., Socher et al., 2013) by stacking multiple recursive layers. Drawing inspiration from deep recurrent networks, the authors distinguish between depth in structure (inherent in the parse tree) and depth in space (from stacked layers). The model is evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank for fine-grained sentiment classification, where it achieves new state-of-the-art results.

**Strengths:**
*   **Originality and Significance:** The core contribution is a novel, well-motivated architecture that naturally extends existing recursive models. The concept of adding depth by stacking layers with inter-node connections is a clever way to build more powerful hierarchical representations. The impressive state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark demonstrates the significance of this approach and its potential to influence future work on compositional semantics.
*   **Quality:** The paper’s claims are well-supported by strong experimental results. The authors introduce several beneficial design choices, including untying leaf and internal node parameters to effectively leverage large, pre-trained word vectors, and successfully applying rectified linear units (ReLUs) in the recursive setting. The experiments in Table 1(a) clearly demonstrate that deeper models outperform their shallow counterparts with a similar number of parameters. The qualitative analyses provide compelling intuition for how different layers learn distinct aspects of compositionality.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation is clear, the model is described precisely, and the experimental setup is easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Missing Ablation:** A key architectural choice is the inclusion of the `V` matrix, which creates connections between a node in layer `i-1` and the corresponding node in layer `i`. A crucial ablation study is missing to justify this connection. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a comparison to a model without the `V` matrix, which would clarify the specific contribution of these inter-layer, inter-node connections versus a more standard stacking approach.
*   **Experimental Reporting:** Table 1(a) reports test set accuracies for a range of model configurations, which could imply that the model architecture (depth and width) was tuned on the test set. Standard practice is to report development set performance for model selection and only report test set accuracy for the single best model chosen. This should be clarified or corrected.
*   **Minor Errors:** The manuscript contains minor typos and at least one malformed sentence that require proofreading.

**Summary for Acceptance:**
**Pro:** A novel and significant deep recursive architecture, state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark, strong experimental validation, and excellent clarity.
**Con:** Missing a key ablation study, and the experimental reporting in Table 1(a) raises concerns about tuning on the test set.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper with a strong central contribution. The weaknesses are addressable and do not invalidate the impressive results. I recommend acceptance.
