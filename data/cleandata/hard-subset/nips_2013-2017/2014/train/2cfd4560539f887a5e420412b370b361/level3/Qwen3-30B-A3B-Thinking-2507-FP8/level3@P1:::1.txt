

The paper introduces deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers to combine hierarchical structural processing with depth in space. This extends prior work on recursive neural networks (RNNs) for compositional language modeling (e.g., Socher et al., 2013) and deep recurrent networks (Hermans & Schrauwen, 2013), addressing a key gap: while standard RNNs are "deep in structure" via parse trees, they lack the hierarchical representation benefits of stacked feedforward layers. The authors evaluate deep RNNs on fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating state-of-the-art results over shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and paragraph vectors.

Strengths are substantial. The core idea—stacking recursive layers—is technically sound and well-motivated, directly addressing the limitations of prior compositional models. The architecture (Equation 4) cleanly integrates structural recursion with depth, and the untying of leaf/internal node representations (Section 2.2) is a practical and well-justified enhancement that enables efficient use of rectifier activations and pretrained word vectors. The experimental design is thorough: extensive ablation studies (Table 1a) confirm that deeper networks outperform shallow counterparts with identical parameter counts, and the qualitative analyses (input perturbation in Figure 3 and nearest-neighbor phrase comparisons in Table 2) compellingly show that each layer captures distinct aspects of compositionality (e.g., layer 1: word dominance; layer 2: syntactic similarity; layer 3: sentiment-focused semantics). Results are robust, with the best model achieving 49.5% accuracy on SST fine-grained classification—significantly outperforming prior work (e.g., 48.1% for shallow RNNs here, 46.06% for 3-layer L2-regularized RNN in their ablation). The writing is clear, well-organized, and the paper is a pleasure to read, with precise technical descriptions and insightful figures.

Key weaknesses, however, warrant attention. The paper lacks a rigorous theoretical or empirical explanation for *why* depth improves performance beyond empirical observation. While the qualitative analyses suggest layered compositionality, they do not establish *how* depth resolves issues like vanishing gradients or enables hierarchical abstraction—critical for a "deep" architecture claim. The authors note depth "starts to cause a degrade" beyond a certain point (Table 1a), but do not investigate why (e.g., width vs. depth trade-offs, gradient dynamics). Additionally, the choice of the specific architecture (Equation 4, with weight matrix V^(i)) is not sufficiently justified. Alternatives like skip connections or layer-wise pretraining (as in deep feedforward networks) could be explored but are omitted without discussion. The paper also does not address whether the gains from depth would hold on tasks without rich hierarchical supervision (e.g., SST's node-level labels), which is a limitation for broader applicability.

Significance is high: the work provides a clear, effective path to deeper compositional modeling in NLP, with strong empirical validation and state-of-the-art results on a standard benchmark. The insights into layered compositionality advance understanding of how neural networks process language structure. While the idea is not radically novel (extending recursive nets with depth), its execution is rigorous and the results are compelling. The paper aligns well with NIPS 2013-2014 themes on deep learning for structured data, and the related work is appropriately contextualized (citing Socher et al., 2013, and Hermans & Schrauwen, 2013).

Pro: Well-motivated architecture; thorough experiments with strong results; clear qualitative analyses; excellent writing; significant improvement over baselines.  
Con: Insufficient explanation for *why* depth helps; limited discussion of architectural alternatives; underdeveloped analysis of depth limitations (e.g., gradient flow).

This paper makes a solid, incremental contribution to compositional modeling in NLP. The technical execution is sound, the results are convincing, and the insights into layered compositionality are valuable. The minor weaknesses—particularly the lack of mechanistic explanation for depth—are outweighed by the paper's strengths. It should be accepted as a high-quality, impactful contribution to the field.
