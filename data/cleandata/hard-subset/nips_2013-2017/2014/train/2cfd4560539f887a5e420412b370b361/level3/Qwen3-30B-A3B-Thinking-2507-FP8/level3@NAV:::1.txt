

This paper introduces "deep recursive neural networks" (Deep RNNs), a novel architecture constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers on top of each other. The authors build upon established recursive neural networks (RNNs) that process structured input like parse trees, extending them with the notion of "depth in space" analogous to deep feedforward networks. This work is positioned as a natural extension of recent developments in deep recurrent networks, addressing a gap in hierarchical representation capacity that traditional recursive networks lack.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, making complex concepts accessible. The methodology is technically sound, with thorough experimental evaluation on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank for fine-grained sentiment classification. Results demonstrate that Deep RNNs outperform both shallow recursive networks with equivalent parameter counts and previous state-of-the-art baselines, including multiplicative RNN variants and paragraph vectors. The exploratory analyses on input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrases provide compelling evidence that each layer captures different aspects of compositionality.

Strengths include the clear presentation, strong experimental validation, and the appealing core idea of stacking recursive layers. The significance is clear: the approach advances sentiment analysis with demonstrable improvements, and the architecture could potentially generalize to other NLP tasks involving structured input.

However, the paper has notable weaknesses. While the architecture is simple and effective, it lacks sufficient discussion on *why* deeper networks help—specifically, the authors don't provide theoretical justification or empirical analysis to explain the observed performance gains with depth. The choice of architecture (e.g., why not use residual connections or different layer types) is also not thoroughly justified. The paper would benefit from deeper exploration of the representational differences between layers beyond the qualitative analyses provided.

Overall, this is a technically sound, well-executed paper with clear significance. The lack of theoretical depth regarding the benefits of depth and architectural choices is the primary weakness. I recommend acceptance as it represents a meaningful incremental advance with strong empirical support, though future work should address the theoretical gaps. The paper would be strengthened by a more rigorous exploration of the depth-performance relationship.
