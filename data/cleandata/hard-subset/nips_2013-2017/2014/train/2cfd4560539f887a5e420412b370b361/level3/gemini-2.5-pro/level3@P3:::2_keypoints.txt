- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks recursive layers and adds feed-forward inter-layer connections to model compositionality.  
- Untying parameters for leaf and non-terminal nodes allows use of large pre-trained word vectors and different representation spaces, facilitating ReLU activations.  
- Empirical comparison shows depth (deep, narrow) is more beneficial than width (shallow, wide) for this architecture given similar parameter counts.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrases) indicate layers capture increasing abstraction from lexical to syntactic to semantic/sentiment features.  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, outperforming prior models like RNTN and paragraph vectors.  
- Architectural and modeling changes are well-motivated and experimentally validated.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with a strong visual (Figure 2) explaining weight sharing and connectivity.  
- Missing ablation: no experiment removing the V inter-layer connection to test whether the direct pass-through is essential.  
- Methodological issue: Table 1(a) appears to use the test set for hyperparameter/model selection, which can bias reported results and should use the development set instead.  
- Minor clarity/presentation issues (typos, a missing symbol in weight initialization) that hurt polish and reproducibility.
