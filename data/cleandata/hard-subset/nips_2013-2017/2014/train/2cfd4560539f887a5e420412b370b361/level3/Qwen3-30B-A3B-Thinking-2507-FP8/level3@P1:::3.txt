

This paper proposes "deep recursive neural networks" (Deep RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers, extending the concept of depth in structure (via recursive connections) with depth in space (via layer stacking), inspired by recent advances in deep recurrent networks. The work builds directly on Socher et al.'s 2013 NIPS paper on recursive neural networks for sentiment analysis, and aligns with the trend of exploring depth in neural architectures (e.g., deep RNNs [10-12]). The key innovation is stacking recursive layers to create hierarchical representations within the tree-structured processing, rather than relying solely on structural depth.

**Strengths:**  
The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear explanations of the architecture (Figures 1-2) and sufficient detail for reproducibility. The empirical results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) are compelling: Deep RNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance (49.5% fine-grained accuracy), significantly outperforming shallow recursive networks (48.1% with identical parameter counts) and prior baselines (e.g., RNTN, Paragraph Vectors). The use of ReLU activations and dropout is well-justified and crucial for the gains (a preliminary experiment shows a 3.4% improvement over L2 regularization). The qualitative analyses—input perturbation (Fig. 3) and nearest-neighbor phrases (Table 2)—effectively demonstrate that deeper layers capture distinct aspects of compositionality (e.g., first layer: word dominance; second layer: intensity; third layer: sentiment), validating the architectural hypothesis. The paper also correctly notes that depth in space (layer stacking) complements depth in structure (recursive processing), a subtle but important distinction from prior work.

**Weaknesses:**  
1. **Methodological gaps:** The decision to keep word vectors fixed (Section 3.1) is not justified. Fine-tuning embeddings could significantly impact performance (e.g., a single-layer RNN might match Deep RNNs if embeddings adapt to sentiment). This omission weakens the claim that depth *alone* drives gains.  
2. **Missing baseline:** The paper omits Kalchbrenner et al.’s 2014 convolutional sentence model (ACL), which achieved 47.5% on SST—critical for establishing the novelty of the approach against strong CNN baselines.  
3. **Binary classification protocol:** The binary results (Table 1b) are misleading. The paper trains on 5 classes (including neutral) but reports binary performance by discarding neutral cases. Standard practice requires separate binary training; the current approach "solves a harder problem" without proper comparison.  
4. **Unexplained notation:** Equations (3)-(4) use undefined terms (e.g., $W^{\eta}$, $W^{xh}$) and conflates input vs. hidden layer parameters. A summary of parameter dimensions/dimensions would clarify the model.  
5. **Speculative claims:** Lines 148–154 assert that deeper layers capture "different aspects" without quantifying this beyond qualitative examples. Empirical evidence (e.g., layer-wise classification accuracy on intermediate nodes) would strengthen this.  
6. **Stability concerns:** The fixed L2 penalty (10$^{-5}$) for exploding activations is ad hoc; alternatives like gradient clipping or weight normalization (e.g., [25]) are standard and should be considered.

**Originality & Significance:**  
The architecture is a natural extension of deep RNNs to recursive structures, making it incremental but meaningful. It advances the state of the art on SST (49.5% vs. prior 48.1%), demonstrating that depth in space enhances compositional modeling. However, the significance is limited to sentiment analysis; the paper lacks exploration of broader applicability (e.g., unsupervised tasks, as suggested in Section 4). The core idea—stacking recursive layers—is novel within recursive networks but conceptually similar to deep RNNs [10-12], so the contribution is more "generalization" than "breakthrough."

**Recommendation:**  
**Accept with major revisions.** The paper presents strong empirical evidence for the benefits of depth in recursive architectures, with clear writing and insightful analyses. However, the methodological gaps (fixed embeddings, missing baseline, binary protocol) must be addressed to ensure claims are fully supported. Specifically:  
- Re-evaluate with fine-tuned embeddings and report single-layer RNN performance under identical conditions.  
- Add Kalchbrenner’s CNN baseline and clarify the binary classification protocol.  
- Resolve notation issues in Equations (3)-(4) and provide parameter dimension summaries.  
- Include quantitative evidence for layer-wise compositional differences (e.g., layer-wise accuracy on SST subtrees).  

The work is technically sound, significant for sentiment analysis, and aligns with NIPS’s focus on deep learning innovations. With the suggested revisions, it would be a strong contribution. The minor weaknesses are fixable and do not undermine the core results.
