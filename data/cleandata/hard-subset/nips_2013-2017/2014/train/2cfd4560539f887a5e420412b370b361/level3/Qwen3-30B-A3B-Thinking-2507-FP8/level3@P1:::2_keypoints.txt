- Introduces deep recursive neural networks that stack recursive layers (with inter-layer matrix V) to capture hierarchical compositionality, distinct from temporal stacking in deep recurrent nets.  
- Novelty: provides "depth in space" for recursive architectures, advancing beyond Socher et al. and RNTN.  
- Technical insight: untied leaf/internal parameters enable effective ReLU use by resolving mismatch between dense word vectors and sparse phrase representations.  
- Efficient parameterization: uses pretrained 300D word vectors with modest hidden size (|h|=340) to avoid quadratic complexity.  
- Empirical strength: achieves SOTA on SST and outperforms shallow counterparts with equal parameter counts (e.g., 49.5% vs. 48.1% on SST 5-class).  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) convincingly show layered compositionality: low layers capture lexical similarity, middle layers add syntactic/semantic nuance, top layers encode sentiment.  
- Major methodological flaw: Table 1a appears to use test-set results for hyperparameter tuning (depth/width), risking test-set overfitting.  
- Missing ablation: no experiment isolating the necessity of inter-layer matrix V—critical to validate the claimed novelty of stacking.  
- Writing and reproducibility are strong (clear figures, details), though there are minor typos/repeated sentences.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—re-run experiments using development-set tuning and add an ablation comparing models with and without V.
