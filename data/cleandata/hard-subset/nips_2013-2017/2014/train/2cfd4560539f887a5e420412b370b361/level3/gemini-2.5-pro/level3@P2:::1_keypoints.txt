- Contribution: Proposes a deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers, adding representational depth to traditional recursive architectures.  
- Architecture detail: Each parse-tree node receives inputs from its children in the same layer and from its corresponding node in the layer below.  
- Motivation: Inspired by deep recurrent networks’ distinction between temporal and stacked depth; applies stacking to recursive models for compositionality.  
- Evaluation: Tested on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained and binary sentiment classification).  
- Results: Deep RNNs outperform shallow counterparts with comparable parameter counts and achieve new state-of-the-art on the benchmark.  
- Baseline comparison: Surpasses strong baselines including RNTN and Paragraph Vectors.  
- Qualitative analysis: Different layers appear to capture distinct aspects of compositionality (lower layers lexical/syntactic, top layers semantic/sentiment).  
- Technical quality: Method is sound, well-motivated, and uses modern training practices (rectifiers, dropout, AdaGrad); experiments control for depth/width fairly.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible; helpful figures (e.g., weight-sharing visualization).  
- Originality: Novel application of stacking to recursive networks; non-trivial architectural extension building on prior recursive and recurrent work.  
- Significance: Findings are impactful—demonstrates benefits of representational depth for compositional language models and likely to influence future work.  
- Main weakness 1: Lacks deeper theoretical or linguistic justification for the specific formulation (e.g., choice in Eq.4 and one-to-one inter-layer connections).  
- Main weakness 2: Limited architectural exploration (e.g., did not report variants connecting output to all hidden layers).  
- Recommendation rationale: Strong empirical and qualitative contributions argue for acceptance, while the novelty is partly an expected application of stacking and some architectural choices need more justification.
