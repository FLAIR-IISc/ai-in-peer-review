- Proposes deep recursive neural networks that stack multiple recursive layers ("depth in space") with parameter sharing within each layer and untied leaf/internal weights.  
- Uses ReLUs, dropout, supervision only at the top layer, AdaGrad, and fixed pretrained word embeddings for training.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained and binary) and shows improvements over shallow recursive baselines and prior systems.  
- Exploratory analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) suggest different layers capture complementary compositional aspects.  
- Generalizes stacked recurrent models to tree-structured computation and complements RAE/MV-RNN/RNTN approaches that increase per-layer capacity.  
- ReLU and dropout choices follow trends from deep feed-forward/CNN literature.  
- Strength: clear core idea—stacking recursive layers induces hierarchical abstraction independent of tree depth.  
- Strength: strong empirical SST performance with parameter-matched depth comparisons showing depth benefits beyond width.  
- Strength: practical contributions—untied leaves enable ReLUs and large embeddings; dropout improves generalization.  
- Strength: good exposition and qualitative analyses; sufficient training details for rough reproduction.  
- Weakness: attribution of gains unclear—pretrained embeddings, ReLUs, and dropout may account for improvements besides depth; limited ablations.  
- Weakness: fixed embeddings may disadvantage shallow models; need controlled comparisons with embedding fine-tuning.  
- Weakness: non-standard binary evaluation (mapping 5-way posteriors) instead of the usual train/test without neutrals; must report standard binary protocol.  
- Weakness: unclear/missing baselines—DCNN results and preprocessing/tokenization alignment not clearly reported; include same-split DCNN and other strong CNN baselines.  
- Weakness: dropout design (shared/locked masks across nodes) needs justification and ablation against independent per-node masks.  
- Weakness: stability fix (L2 on activations) is unusual; compare with gradient norm clipping and weight norm constraints.  
- Weakness: some layer-interpretation claims are speculative and need targeted probing (e.g., linear classifiers on frozen layer representations).  
- Weakness: notation/clarity issues in untying section—overloaded symbols and missing parameter/shape table; recommend clearer equations and a parameter summary.  
- Quality: model and optimization choices are sound; experiments mostly fair but some protocols and ablations limit causal claims about depth.  
- Clarity: generally well written; fix notation and baseline reporting for reproducibility.  
- Originality: natural but novel extension of stacked RNNs to tree structures; combination and analysis are new.  
- Significance: likely to influence tree-structured modeling, but broader evidence on other tasks/datasets would strengthen impact.  
- Requested experiments: proper SST binary protocol; ablations for fine-tuning embeddings, ReLU vs tanh, shared vs independent dropout, output at last vs all layers; additional root-label datasets (MR, CR, SUBJ, MPQA, TREC); diagnostic probes for layer roles.  
- Pros: clear idea, solid engineering, competitive results, insightful qualitative analyses, reproducible.  
- Cons: causal role of depth not isolated, non-standard binary evaluation, unclear CNN baseline reporting, some speculative claims and notation issues.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept—timely, useful contribution with solid results; addressing evaluation, baselines, and targeted ablations would strengthen the paper.
