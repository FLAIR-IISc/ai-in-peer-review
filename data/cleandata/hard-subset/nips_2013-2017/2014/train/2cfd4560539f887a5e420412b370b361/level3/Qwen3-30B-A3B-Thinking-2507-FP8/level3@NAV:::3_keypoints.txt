- Paper proposes "deep recursive neural networks" by stacking recursive layers with shared parameters, claiming better hierarchical compositionality and SOTA on SST.
- Strength: Clearly articulates relationship between structural depth (recursive nets) and spatial depth (stacked layers), generalizing deep recurrent advances.
- Strength: Use of dropout and ReLU with fixed pre-trained embeddings yields notable performance gains.
- Strength: Paper is well-written with sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Weakness: Core innovation is incremental—a generalization of depth concepts from recurrent networks, not a fundamentally novel approach.
- Weakness: Key methodological choices lack justification, notably fixing word vectors instead of exploring fine-tuning.
- Weakness: Shared dropout units (line 251) are not explained or justified.
- Weakness: Binary classification protocol is flawed because neutral examples are included during training, deviating from standard practice.
- Weakness: Missing an important baseline (Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model).
- Weakness: Speculative claims (lines 148–154) are made without empirical support.
- Weakness: Undefined notation (W^eta, W^xh) and inadequate equation explanations reduce clarity.
- Recommendation: Binary classification results must be revised to follow standard protocols and include proper baselines.
- Recommendation: Additional experiments needed (ablations on fine-tuning, proper binary setup, tests on global-label datasets) to validate depth benefits.
- Overall: Interesting empirical results but limited significance due to questionable experimental design, lack of clear novelty, and unresolved methodological issues; needs substantial revision before publication.
