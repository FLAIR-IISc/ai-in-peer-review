

This paper proposes "deep recursive neural networks" (deep RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers, where each layer processes the same tree structure with parameter sharing across heights. The authors claim this approach captures hierarchical compositionality better than shallow recursive networks, evaluating on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) where they achieve state-of-the-art results.

The paper demonstrates several strengths: it clearly articulates the relationship between depth in structure (recursive networks) and depth in space (stacked layers), which generalizes recent advances in deep recurrent networks. The use of dropout and ReLUs with fixed pre-trained embeddings yields notable performance gains, and the paper is well-written with sufficient detail for reproduction.

However, several weaknesses undermine the scientific contribution. The core innovation is essentially a generalization of depth concepts from recurrent networks, not a fundamentally novel approach. Critical methodological choices lack justification: word vectors are fixed without exploration of fine-tuning (which could significantly impact results), shared dropout units (line 251) are not explained, and the binary classification protocol is problematic as it includes neutral examples during training. The paper fails to include a key baseline (Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model) and makes speculative claims (lines 148-154) without empirical support. Notably, undefined notation (W^eta, W^xh) and inadequate equation explanations compromise clarity.

The binary classification results require substantial revision to follow standard protocols, and the authors should conduct additional experiments to validate their claims about depth's benefits (e.g., ablations on fine-tuning, proper binary setup, and global-label datasets). While the qualitative analyses provide interesting insights about layer-specific compositionality, they don't fully address whether depth across space truly helps with the single-root-label task.

Overall, this work demonstrates interesting empirical results but suffers from questionable experimental design and insufficient justification for key choices. The technical quality is adequate, but the significance is limited by the lack of clear novelty and unresolved methodological issues. The paper would benefit substantially from addressing these concerns before publication.
