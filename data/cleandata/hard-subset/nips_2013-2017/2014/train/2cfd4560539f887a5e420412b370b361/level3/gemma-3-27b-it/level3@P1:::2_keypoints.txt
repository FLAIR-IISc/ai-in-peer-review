- Introduces a deep recursive neural network by stacking recursive layers to improve compositionality in language modeling.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) with reported state-of-the-art performance and exploratory analysis of layering effects.
- Builds on prior recursive/recurrent work and modern techniques (pre-trained embeddings, dropout, ReLU).
- Strong empirical results: deep RNNs outperform shallow counterparts with comparable parameter counts.
- Careful experimental design with comparisons to strong baselines increases credibility.
- Clear exposition and helpful visualizations explaining architecture and design choices.
- Untying leaf and internal node parameters, ReLU activations, and pre-trained vectors contribute to performance gains.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) show different layers capture different compositional aspects.
- Missing ablation study on the V matrix connection linking layers; unclear if that specific design is necessary.
- Critical methodological flaw: Table 1(a) appears to use the test set during hyperparameter tuning, risking overfitting and invalidating reported gains.
- Minor polish issues: typos and a repeated sentence need correction.
- Originality: novel combination of stacking recursive layers to bridge depth in time and structure.
- Significance: likely impactful for other compositional NLP tasks; recommend acceptance pending correction of the test-set reporting and minor revisions.
