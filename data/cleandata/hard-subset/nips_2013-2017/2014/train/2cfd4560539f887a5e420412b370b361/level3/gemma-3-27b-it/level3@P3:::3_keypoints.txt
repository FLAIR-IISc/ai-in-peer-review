- Proposal: Stack multiple recursive layers to form deep recursive neural networks to improve compositionality; strong results on SST.  
- Quality: Technically sound with comprehensive experiments, but some design choices weaken robustness of conclusions.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and well-written, but notation issues (e.g., undefined W^eta, W^xh) and equation splitting/parameter table are needed.  
- Originality: Novel generalization—stacking recursive layers—combining prior RNN/recursive ideas in a new way.  
- Significance: State-of-the-art SST performance suggests practical value and potential impact on NLP research.  
- Strengths: Strong empirical performance, clear architecture presentation, logical extension of prior work, useful qualitative analyses, and reproducibility.  
- Weaknesses: Undefined notation; unclear why word vectors weren’t fine-tuned; binary results possibly flawed by including neutrals; missing DCNN baseline; unjustified shared dropout; speculative claims without evidence; limited analysis of depth benefits with single root labels.  
- Pro-acceptance: Novel, effective architecture with strong empirical results and sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Con-acceptance: Methodological choices and missing critical baselines raise concerns about robustness and generalizability.  
- Recommendations: Define missing notation and add a parameter table; include Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) baseline; justify design choices (no fine-tuning, shared dropout); retrain binary classifier excluding neutrals; provide empirical support for speculative claims; run ablations/global-label experiments; consider stabilization alternatives like weight normalization or gradient clipping.
