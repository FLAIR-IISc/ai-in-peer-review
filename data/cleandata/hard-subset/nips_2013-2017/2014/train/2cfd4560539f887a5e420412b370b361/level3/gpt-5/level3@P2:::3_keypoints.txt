- Proposes stacking multiple recursive layers "in space" on top of tree-structured depth, distinguishing leaf vs internal mappings in the first layer and sharing phrase space in higher layers.  
- Uses ReLUs, large fixed pretrained embeddings, softmax supervision at all nodes but classifier only on top layer, AdaGrad, shared-mask dropout, and small L2 penalties for stability.  
- Evaluated mainly on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (5-way and binary); deeper-but-narrower models outperform single-layer nets under a fixed-parameter budget and achieve SOTA on SST.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects (e.g., intensity vs polarity).  
- Builds naturally on deep recurrent networks and prior recursive nets, and compares to RNN, MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, and cites DCNN.  
- Empirical gains are consistent, but several experimental choices reduce conclusiveness (see ablation and protocol issues).  
- Binary SST evaluation is nonstandard (decoded from 5-way models including neutral); must re-run with standard pos/neg training for fair comparison.  
- Word embeddings are kept fixed; unclear if gains are from depth or from not fine-tuning—ablation with fine-tuned embeddings needed.  
- Shared-mask dropout across nodes is nonstandard; an ablation versus independent per-node dropout should be reported.  
- Stability handled via small L2 on weights/activations; alternatives like gradient clipping or weight-norm constraints should be evaluated or discussed.  
- Results appear to be single runs; need variance/confidence intervals and explicit parameter counts per configuration.  
- Parameter-budget experiments would benefit from depth vs width trade-offs at equal width as well as explicit parameter numbers.  
- DCNN baseline is cited but unclear whether comparisons use identical SST splits and protocols; clarify or include matched numbers.  
- Notational/exposition issues in Section 2.2—define all symbols, parameter shapes, and separate leaf vs internal composition equations.  
- Clarify whether V(i) is tied across leaves/internals in higher layers and specify its dimensions.  
- Temper speculative claims about hierarchical interpretation and layer-specific semantics or add quantitative probes to support them.  
- Justify connecting the classifier only to the final layer versus to all layers, and include an ablation.  
- Contribution is an incremental but useful extension of deep RNN ideas to trees with practical engineering design choices.  
- Significance is moderate: consistent gains on SST are valuable, but generality beyond SST (e.g., tasks with only root labels) is untested.  
- Pros: simple well-motivated architecture, strong SST results, sensible training recipe, thoughtful qualitative analyses, generally clear and reproducible.  
- Cons: nonstandard binary SST protocol, missing ablations (embedding fine-tuning, dropout type, classifier attachment), limited task scope, speculative claims, lack of multiple runs/parameter counts, and notation ambiguities.  
- Requests: re-run binary SST with standard protocol; ablations for embedding fine-tuning, dropout sharing, and classifier attachments; report parameter counts and variance; compare DCNN on same splits; test stability alternatives; evaluate on root-only supervision tasks or mask internal losses.  
- Minor fixes: explicitly define W^η, W^xh, matrix dimensions; clarify V(2) blocks and tying; justify shared dropout masks.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—incremental but useful work with strong results; acceptance contingent on addressing protocol and ablation concerns.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
