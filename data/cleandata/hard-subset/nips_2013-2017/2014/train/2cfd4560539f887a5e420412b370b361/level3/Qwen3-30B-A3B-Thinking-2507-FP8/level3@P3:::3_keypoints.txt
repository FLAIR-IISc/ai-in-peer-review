- Introduces "deep recursive neural networks" by stacking recursive layers to add hierarchical depth across structured inputs.  
- Claims stacking recursive layers yields better hierarchical representations than traditional (shallow) recursive nets.  
- Shows deeper recursive nets outperform shallow counterparts with the same parameter count on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results versus several baselines (including multiplicative RNN variants and paragraph vectors).  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) suggest different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Paper is clearly written with sufficient implementation detail for reproduction (ReLUs, dropout, fixed pre-trained embeddings).  
- Use of SST with node-level labels provides richer supervision appropriate for evaluating hierarchical representations.  
- Dropout and ReLU yield substantial empirical gains (e.g., 3-layer RNN with dropout: 49.5 vs 46.06 with L2 on fine-grained SST).  
- Methodological concern: word vectors were kept fixed (not fine-tuned), which could confound comparisons and favor deeper models.  
- Missing important baseline: Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model is not included.  
- Binary classification results are flawed: training included neutral examples while testing discarded them, violating standard protocol.  
- Some notation is undefined (e.g., W^eta, W^xh) and certain claims (lines 148–154) are speculative without empirical support.  
- Use of shared dropout units and stability via small L2 penalty lack justification; more standard alternatives (weight-norm, gradient truncation) not considered.  
- No experiments on global-label datasets to test whether depth-across-space helps when only a root label exists.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-executed, but evaluation weakened by methodological issues.  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but reduced by unexplained notation and key design choices.  
- Originality: a natural generalization of depth from recurrent nets; contribution not clearly distinguished from prior deep recurrent work.  
- Significance: meaningful empirical and qualitative contributions on SST, but impact limited by missing experiments and methodological gaps.  
- Recommendation: lean accept if authors address concerns about fine-tuning, missing baselines, and proper binary classification protocol.
