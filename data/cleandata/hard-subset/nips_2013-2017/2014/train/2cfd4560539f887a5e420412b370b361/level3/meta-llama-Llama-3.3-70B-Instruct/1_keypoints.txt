- Introduces the scaled latent trace norm for tensors in multitask learning, addressing shortcomings of overlapped and latent trace norms.  
- Claims improved performance in matrix completion, multitask learning, and multilinear multitask learning.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise technical explanations.  
- Provides a thorough excess-risk analysis for the proposed and existing norms using Rademacher complexity.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the proposed norm’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: new norm targets limitations arising from heterogeneous tensor dimensions.  
- Strength: strong theoretical treatment of excess risk.  
- Strength: empirical validation across multiple settings.  
- Weakness: assumes heterogeneous tensor dimensions, which may not always hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Rademacher-complexity-based bounds may not be tight in all cases.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a few datasets; more extensive evaluation needed.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions to address limitations.  
- Rating: 8/10.
