- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Network: stacks multiple recursive layers to add depth in structure and space.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment); outperforms shallow RNNs and achieves state-of-the-art vs. baselines.  
- Provides qualitative analyses showing different layers capture distinct aspects of compositionality (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrase similarity).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized.  
- Stacking recursive layers is technically sound and empirically supported.  
- Thorough quantitative comparisons with strong baselines and robust experimental setup (dropout, rectifiers, pre-trained word vectors).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark, underscoring significance.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical discussion/intuition for why deeper recursive networks suit linguistic compositionality.  
- Claims about different layers’ roles need further theoretical substantiation or additional qualitative experiments.  
- Does not explore limitations such as computational complexity, scalability to larger datasets, or applicability beyond sentiment classification.  
- Related work discussion is adequate but could include more recent hierarchical/compositional models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension; strong experimental results; insightful qualitative analyses; clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical grounding; limited exploration of limitations and broader applicability; novelty viewed as incremental.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to expand theoretical motivation and discuss limitations in the final version.
