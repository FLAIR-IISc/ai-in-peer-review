- Proposes deep recursive neural networks combining recursive nets and deep feedforward nets for fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Uses binary parse trees as structure and reports state-of-the-art performance on the task.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a clear explanation of the architecture and components.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are presented clearly and concisely.  
- Provides qualitative analyses suggesting different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Main strength: novel architecture that outperforms shallow recursive nets of comparable size.  
- Main weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art architectures (e.g., CNNs, LSTMs).  
- Additional weakness: unclear how the architecture generalizes or can be adapted to other NLP tasks.  
- Originality: novel combination of recursive and deep feedforward ideas, but limited by missing architecture comparisons.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to NLP with strong task performance and useful layer analyses.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel architecture, thorough experiments, state-of-the-art results.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient comparisons to competing models, unclear applicability to other tasks, need for deeper layer analysis.  
- Recommendations: add detailed comparisons with CNNs/LSTMs, explain adaptation to other NLP tasks, and expand analysis of layer-wise compositionality.
