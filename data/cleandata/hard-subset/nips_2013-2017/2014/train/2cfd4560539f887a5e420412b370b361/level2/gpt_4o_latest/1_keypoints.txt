- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN), stacking recursive layers for hierarchical representations.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) showing Deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets and several baselines.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results compared to multiplicative RNNs and Paragraph Vectors on SST.
- Provides qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) showing different layers capture distinct compositional aspects.
- Uses pre-trained word embeddings, rectifier activations, and dropout, strengthening empirical robustness.
- Paper is well-structured with clear methodology and helpful visualizations (parse trees, response graphs).
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (SST), reducing claims about generalizability.
- Lacks comparisons with more recent architectures like transformers.
- Some implementation/hyperparameter details (for varying depths/widths) are insufficient, risking reproducibility issues.
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of dropout, rectifiers, and leaf/internal node parameterization.
- Suggests evaluating additional datasets/tasks, comparing to transformer-based models, releasing detailed hyperparameters and code, and conducting ablation studies.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scope, comparisons, reproducibility, and ablation analyses.
