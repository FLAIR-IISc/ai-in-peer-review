- Proposes deep recursive neural network (stacked recursive layers) to separate "depth in structure" from "depth in space" for richer hierarchical representations.  
- Introduces weight-untied variant for leaf vs. internal nodes, enabling different dimensionalities and rectifier activations.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) for fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Demonstrates deeper models outperform shallower ones with comparable parameter budgets.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SST, surpassing RNTN and Paragraph Vectors.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures explaining the model.  
- Thorough experimental setup using modern training techniques (AdaGrad, dropout, rectifiers) and strong baselines.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) indicate layers capture lexical, syntactic, and task-specific semantics.  
- Part of the performance gain may stem from improved training techniques and not solely from added depth.  
- Contribution is an incremental but useful application of established deep-learning principles to recursive networks.  
- Model depends on external syntactic parsers, limiting applicability to parsed text.  
- Overall: technically sound, well-motivated, empirically strong work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
