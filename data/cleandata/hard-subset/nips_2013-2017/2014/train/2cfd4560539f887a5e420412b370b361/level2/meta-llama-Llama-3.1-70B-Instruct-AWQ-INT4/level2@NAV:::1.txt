**Summary**

This paper introduces a new architecture called deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs), which combines the benefits of recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose stacking multiple recursive layers on top of each other to capture hierarchical representations of data. They evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees as the structure. The results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets and previous baselines, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the task.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its benefits. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model. The use of dropout regularization and rectifier linear activation units is well-justified.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its benefits. The notation is consistent, and the figures are helpful in understanding the architecture. The writing style is clear and concise, making it easy for readers to understand the paper.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, namely recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose a new architecture that combines the benefits of both approaches. The use of multiple recursive layers is a new contribution, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.

**Significance**

The results are important and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification. The paper provides new insights into the benefits of using deep recursive neural networks for natural language processing tasks. The proposed model has the potential to be applied to other tasks that require hierarchical representations of data.

**Arguments For Acceptance**

* The paper presents a novel architecture that combines the benefits of recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.
* The paper provides new insights into the benefits of using deep recursive neural networks for natural language processing tasks.
* The proposed model has the potential to be applied to other tasks that require hierarchical representations of data.

**Arguments Against Acceptance**

* The paper assumes that the reader is familiar with recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks.
* The experimental results are limited to a single task, and it is unclear how the proposed model will perform on other tasks.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed model.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

* Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed model.
* Evaluate the proposed model on other tasks that require hierarchical representations of data.
* Provide more insights into the benefits of using multiple recursive layers.
* Consider using other regularization techniques, such as L1 regularization, to compare with dropout regularization.
