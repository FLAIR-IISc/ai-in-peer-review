- Proposes deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to combine structural and spatial depth, addressing hierarchical representation limits of traditional RNNs.  
- Introduces key innovations: untied leaf/internal node representations for rectifier compatibility and layer-specific compositionality analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor).  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), achieving state-of-the-art results and a significant +6.3% improvement in fine-grained accuracy over prior RNNs.  
- Ablation studies show deeper networks outperform shallower ones with the same parameter count, validating the core hypothesis.  
- Methodology is clearly explained with precise equations, visualizations, detailed experimental setup, and reproducible hyperparameters.  
- Qualitative layer-wise analyses demonstrate distinct roles for layers (e.g., lexical similarity at lower layers, sentiment at higher layers).  
- Technical choices (rectifiers, dropout, pretrained vectors) follow modern best practices and yield statistically significant improvements.  
- Limited analysis of depth degradation: explanation for underperformance of very deep models is superficial and lacks gradient or activation analyses.  
- Binary sentiment results are underexplored; paper does not examine whether a dedicated binary model would improve performance.  
- Baseline comparisons omit some recent deep learning models (e.g., DCNN), weakening the "new SOTA" contextualization.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address depth degradation mechanism and clarify the binary classification approach.
