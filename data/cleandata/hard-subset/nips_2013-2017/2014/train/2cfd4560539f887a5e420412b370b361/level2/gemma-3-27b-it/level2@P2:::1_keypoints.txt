- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks by stacking recursive layers to better model linguistic compositionality.
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and reports improved, state-of-the-art performance over shallow RNNs and baselines.
- Exploratory analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbor phrases) suggest different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.
- Well-motivated work with a clear connection to recurrent and recursive networks and a sound rationale for deeper architectures.
- Methodology is detailed and the paper is clearly written and well-organized.
- Major weakness: insufficient analysis of why deeper networks perform better; layer behavior analysis is qualitative rather than quantitative.
- Suggestion to visualize activations or learned weights and provide quantitative assessments of layer representations.
- Ablation studies are incomplete; need experiments on varying hidden sizes, activation functions, and other architectural choices.
- Comparison to recent deep models is limited; omission of Transformer-based models (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa) is a significant oversight.
- Regularization approach (dropout + fixed L2) appears ad-hoc; a more systematic evaluation of regularization techniques is needed.
- Heavy reliance on SST is a concern; dataset size and binary parse structure may bias results—evaluation on additional datasets is recommended.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear approach, insightful analyses, and good presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited explanatory analysis, incomplete SOTA comparisons, lacking ablations, ad-hoc regularization, and single-dataset reliance.
- Overall assessment: valuable and promising contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions requesting deeper analyses, discussion of SST limitations, and broader comparisons.
