- Proposes deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs) built by stacking multiple recursive layers.  
- Applies the architecture to fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees.  
- Main claim: deep RNNs outperform equally sized shallow recursive nets and achieve state-of-the-art results.  
- Paper provides theoretical analysis and experimental results supporting the claims.  
- Clear explanation of architecture components, including recursive nets and untying leaves vs internals.  
- Qualitative analyses show different layers capture distinct aspects of compositionality.  
- Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analyses offer additional operational insights.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and contains a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, thoroughness, and well-presented experimental results.  
- Limitation: evaluation restricted to a single task (fine-grained sentiment classification).  
- Limitation: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale use.  
- Recommendation: accept, given the significant contribution and strong empirical support.
