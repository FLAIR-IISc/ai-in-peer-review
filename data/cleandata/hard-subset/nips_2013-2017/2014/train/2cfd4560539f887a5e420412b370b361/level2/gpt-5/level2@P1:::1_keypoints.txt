- Proposes stacking multiple recursive layers ("depth in space") on tree-structured composition, with untied leaf/internal parameters and ReLUs.
- Trains with Adagrad, shared-mask dropout, and small activation/weight penalties; passes node states upward across layers.
- Achieves new SOTA on SST fine-grained sentiment and competitive binary results, outperforming several prior models.
- Exploratory analyses suggest different layers capture complementary compositional aspects (e.g., intensity vs polarity).

- Builds on recursive nets and the "depth in time vs depth in space" idea; novelty is architectural stacking and practical untied parametrization.
- Places contribution relative to MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, and DCNN.

- Clear, well-motivated architectural extension that is nontrivial to implement with backprop-through-structure.
- Thoughtful and well-documented training choices that aid reproducibility.
- Convincing parameter-matched comparisons showing depth benefits independent of model size.
- Strong empirical result (SST SOTA) and insightful qualitative layer analyses.

- Empirical evaluation is narrow: single dataset/task (SST) with phrase-level supervision, limiting generality.
- Baseline fairness concerns: training improvements applied only to the proposed model, so published baseline numbers may be incomparable.
- Limited ablation study; key factors (tanh vs ReLU, dropout, untying, finetuning embeddings, output tapping) not systematically quantified.
- No variance or significance analysis across multiple random restarts.
- Computational/resource costs (training time, memory, scaling) not reported.

- Technically sound and carefully executed; parameter-controlled depth comparison is convincing.
- Training details are thorough, but broader validation and systematic ablations are needed to isolate causes of gains.

- Generally clear and well-organized; math and implementation details are easy to follow.
- Would benefit from complexity analysis and pseudocode for interleaved backprop through structure and layers.

- Moderate originality: main novelty is stacking tree composition; untied leaves with ReLUs is practical but incremental.
- Qualitative analyses add value.

- Significant contribution due to SOTA on a common benchmark and empirical evidence that deepening tree composition helps.
- Impact would increase with broader tasks/datasets and stronger, matched baselines.

- Question: How sensitive are results to random initialization? Please report mean/std over runs.
- Question: What is the effect of fine-tuning word vectors vs keeping them fixed?
- Question: Did you try deep supervision (connecting output to all hidden layers) and its effects?
- Question: What are training time and memory costs as functions of depth/width/tree size? Any efficiency tricks?
- Question: How do results change without phrase-level supervision (sentence-level only)?

- Suggestion: Add systematic ablations for ReLU vs tanh, dropout, untying, pretrained vs random/finetuned embeddings, and output strategy.
- Suggestion: Re-run at least one strong tree baseline (e.g., RNTN) with the same training tricks for fair comparison.
- Suggestion: Report variability across seeds and perform significance testing.
- Suggestion: Include training/runtime comparisons and brief complexity discussion.
- Suggestion: Evaluate on a second dataset/task (e.g., paraphrase detection, entailment) to test generality.

- Pros: Simple, clear idea with solid execution, SOTA results, parameter-controlled depth comparisons, and useful analyses/training details.
- Cons: Narrow empirical scope, baseline fairness and missing ablations, and modest conceptual novelty.
