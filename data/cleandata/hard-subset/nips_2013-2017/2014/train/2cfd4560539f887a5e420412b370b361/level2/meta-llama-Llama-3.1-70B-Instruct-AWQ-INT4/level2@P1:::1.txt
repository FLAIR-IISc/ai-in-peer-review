This paper introduces a new architecture, deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees as the structure. The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its application to the task.

The authors provide a thorough review of previous work on recursive neural networks and their application to natural language processing tasks. They also provide a detailed explanation of the proposed architecture, including the untying of leaves and internal nodes, and the use of rectifier activation units.

The experimental results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size, and also outperform previous baselines on the sentiment analysis task, including a multiplicative RNN variant and the recently introduced paragraph vectors. The authors also provide qualitative experiments that suggest that each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a new architecture that combines the benefits of recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks.
* The thorough evaluation of the proposed model on a challenging task, including a comparison with previous baselines.
* The qualitative experiments that provide insights into the operation of the proposed architecture.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The lack of a clear explanation of the intuition behind the proposed architecture, and how it differs from previous work.
* The limited exploration of the hyperparameter space, including the number of layers and the number of hidden units.
* The use of a fixed learning rate and a simple regularization technique, which may not be optimal for the task.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its application to the task. The experimental results are thorough and provide a good evaluation of the proposed model. However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved, including a clearer explanation of the intuition behind the proposed architecture and a more thorough exploration of the hyperparameter space.
