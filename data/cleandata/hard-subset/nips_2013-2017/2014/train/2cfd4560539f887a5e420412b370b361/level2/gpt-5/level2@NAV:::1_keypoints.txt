- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks that stack recursive layers to add “depth in space” on tree structures and untie leaf/internal transforms to allow ReLU/dropout.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (5-way), achieving state-of-the-art and outperforming several baselines with matched-parameter comparisons.  
- Exploratory analyses indicate layer specialization: lower layers track lexical/syntactic info, middle layers capture intensity, top layer focuses on sentiment polarity.  
- Model and training are clearly specified (equations, AdaGrad, dropout, initialization) and results are compelling.  
- Missing statistical significance, run-to-run variance, and ablation studies, making it hard to isolate which design choices drive improvements.  
- Some baseline comparisons use published numbers; fairness of those comparisons (training parity) is not fully discussed.  
- Paper is generally well written; distinctions between depths and untying rationale are clearly explained.  
- Would benefit from explicit parameter counts per configuration to aid reproducibility.  
- Stacking recursive layers is a natural but underexplored extension; combining with untying/ReLU/dropout is incremental yet effective.  
- Achieving SOTA on SST and demonstrating depth-vs-width tradeoffs for tree models is significant for parse-based NLP tasks.  
- Main empirical limitation: evaluated on a single dataset (SST) and only English binary trees.  
- No analysis of computational cost or inference speed is provided.  
- Pros: conceptually simple, well-motivated architecture; strong empirical gains with matched-parameter budgets; insightful qualitative analyses; practical training recipe.  
- Cons: single-dataset evaluation; lack of statistical tests and ablations; reliance on reported baseline numbers without full parity; no computational-cost analysis.  
- Suggestions: add ablations isolating depth, untying, ReLU, dropout, and embedding fine-tuning with multiple seeds and confidence intervals.  
- Suggestions: evaluate on additional tree-structured tasks/datasets (paraphrase, entailment, other sentiment corpora) and discuss parsing dependency/cost.  
- Suggestions: provide parameter counts per configuration and release code for reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: analyze intermediate-layer outputs further (e.g., attach outputs to probes) to probe layer roles.  
- Overall: solid, timely contribution with clear promise; strengthening experimental rigor and breadth would improve the paper.
