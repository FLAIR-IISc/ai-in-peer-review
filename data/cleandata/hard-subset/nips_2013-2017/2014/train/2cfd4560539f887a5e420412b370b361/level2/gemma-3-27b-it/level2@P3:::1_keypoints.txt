- Introduces deep recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive layers to improve compositionality modeling.
- Extends existing recursive networks with depth, analogous to deep feedforward and recurrent architectures.
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and achieves state-of-the-art fine-grained sentiment classification.
- Well-motivated and clearly presented, with a convincing parallel to developments in deep recurrent networks.
- Thorough experimental setup including multiple baselines and ablation studies on depth.
- Exploratory analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor) provide useful qualitative insights into layer behavior.
- Regularization techniques are used to address stability issues of deep networks; funding acknowledgements included.
- Untying leaf and internal nodes is a thoughtful design that enables effective use of pre-trained embeddings and rectifier activations.
- Empirical results show clear improvement over shallow counterparts and prior methods.
- Limited discussion of computational complexity, training time, and memory implications despite constant parameter count.
- Input perturbation experiment depends on manual word selection; a more systematic perturbation strategy is recommended.
- Insufficient analysis of why increasing depth beyond a point degrades performance (overfitting, vanishing gradients, etc.).
- Would benefit from discussion of optimizer choices (AdaGrad vs. alternatives) and rationale for selection.
- Connection to compositional distributional semantics could be made more explicit.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, viewing weaknesses as minor and addressable in revisions.
