- Introduces "deep recursive neural networks" by stacking recursive layers and reports SOTA on SST fine-grained (49.5%, +2.8%).  
- Well-written paper with strong empirical validation and reproducible choices (pretrained vectors, ReLU, dropout).  
- Qualitative analyses show lower layers capture lexical/syntactic similarity while higher layers capture sentiment.  
- Terminology confusion: using "RNN" for recursive networks conflicts with standard usage for recurrent networks and obscures clarity.  
- Novelty overstated: insufficiently distinguished from prior recursive deep models (e.g., Socher et al. 2012) that exploit tree depth.  
- Parameter-comparison flaw: reported "same parameter count" comparisons are not actually controlled for identical total parameters.  
- Lacks theoretical justification explaining why stacking layers outperforms increasing width; qualitative results don’t prove causation.  
- Omits relevant related work (e.g., Socher et al. 2013 dynamic pooling/unfolding recursive autoencoders) and needs clearer differentiation.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify terminology, rerun controlled parameter-matched experiments, strengthen related-work discussion, and add analysis of why depth helps.  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution but incremental novelty; recommendation — Weak Accept, contingent on revisions.
