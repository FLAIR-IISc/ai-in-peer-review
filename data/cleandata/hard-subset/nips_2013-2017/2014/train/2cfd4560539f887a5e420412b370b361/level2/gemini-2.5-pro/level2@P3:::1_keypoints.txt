- Proposes deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers to add "depth in space" to tree-structured models.  
- Motivated as analogous to deep recurrent networks, enabling hierarchical representations of compositionality.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment classification).  
- Deeper models outperform shallow ones under a fixed parameter budget.  
- Best deep RNN achieves new state-of-the-art on SST, beating RNTN and strong baselines like Paragraph Vectors.  
- Model formulation is clear, technically sound, and a logical extension of existing recursive networks.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous, uses standard benchmark and strong baselines, and controls for parameter count.  
- Includes implementation details (AdaGrad, dropout, rectifiers, pre-trained embeddings) that aid reproducibility.  
- Figures effectively illustrate model structure and weight-sharing scheme; paper is well-written and organized.  
- Novelty: stacking layers for recursive/tree-structured data and “untying” leaf vs. internal node weights is a new contribution.  
- Qualitative analyses indicate different layers capture distinct compositional aspects, from word-level to abstract sentiment.  
- Suggestions: perform systematic probing of layer representations across dataset, run ablation studies to isolate gains from untied weights/activations/embeddings, and evaluate or discuss generalizability to other structured NLP tasks.
