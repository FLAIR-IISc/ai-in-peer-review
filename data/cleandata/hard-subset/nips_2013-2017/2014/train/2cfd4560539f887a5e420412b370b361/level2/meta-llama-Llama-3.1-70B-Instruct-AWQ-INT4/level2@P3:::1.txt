**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a new architecture called Deep Recursive Neural Networks (Deep RNNs) for modeling compositionality in natural language. Deep RNNs are constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, allowing for a hierarchical representation of the input data. The authors evaluate their model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank dataset. They show that Deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets and previous baselines, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the task.
