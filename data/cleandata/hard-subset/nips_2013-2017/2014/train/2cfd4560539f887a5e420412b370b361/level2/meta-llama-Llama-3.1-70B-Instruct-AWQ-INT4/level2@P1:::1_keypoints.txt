- Introduces deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers for fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of architecture and task application.  
- Thorough literature review of recursive neural networks and NLP applications.  
- Detailed description of architectural choices: untying leaves vs internal nodes and use of rectifier activations.  
- Experimental results: deep RNNs outperform equal-size shallow recursive nets and prior baselines (including multiplicative RNNs and paragraph vectors).  
- Qualitative analyses indicate different layers capture different aspects of compositionality and similarity.  
- Strength: novel architecture combining recursive and deep feedforward benefits.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation on a challenging sentiment task with baseline comparisons.  
- Strength: qualitative experiments provide interpretability insights.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuition/explanation of how the architecture differs from prior work.  
- Weakness: limited hyperparameter exploration (number of layers, hidden units).  
- Weakness: uses fixed learning rate and simple regularization that may be suboptimal.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; overall strong (rating 8/10).  
- Minor revisions suggested: clarify architectural intuition, expand hyperparameter search, adopt stronger regularization (e.g., dropout/L1), and consider advanced optimizers (e.g., Adam, RMSProp).
