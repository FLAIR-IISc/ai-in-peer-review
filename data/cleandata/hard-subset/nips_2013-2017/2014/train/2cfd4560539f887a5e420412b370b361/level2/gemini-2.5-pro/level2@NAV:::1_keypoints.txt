- Proposes a novel "deep recursive neural network" by stacking recursive layers, distinguishing "depth in structure" vs "depth in space."
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, outperforming RNTN and Paragraph Vectors.
- Technically sound with well-designed experiments, including ablations that control for parameter count.
- Compares depth vs width and shows benefits of stacked recursive layers for hierarchical representation learning.
- Strong qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrases) showing deeper layers learn more abstract, sentiment-focused representations.
- Exceptionally clear writing, precise model explanation, and thorough experimental setup.
- Limited evaluation scope—results are shown only on sentiment analysis (SST), weakening claims about general compositionality.
- Multiple simultaneous implementation changes (untying nodes, rectifiers, pre-trained vectors, dropout) make it hard to isolate the effect of depth alone.
- Overall: a strong, influential paper with minor scope/ablation limitations; recommendation for acceptance.
