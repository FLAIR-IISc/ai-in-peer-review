This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (Deep RNNs), an architecture extending traditional Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers. The core idea is to capture hierarchical compositional semantics in language by allowing each layer to learn different aspects of compositionality. The authors evaluate this approach on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating improvements over shallow RNNs and existing state-of-the-art methods.

The paper builds on a strong foundation of prior work in deep learning and NLP, particularly RNNs [6, 7, 8], deep recurrent networks [10, 11, 12], and distributed word representations [2, 19]. The motivation for combining depth in structure (RNNs) with depth in space (deep feedforward networks) is well-articulated, drawing a clear analogy to the advancements in deep recurrent networks.  The authors correctly identify the limitations of traditional RNNs in capturing hierarchical abstraction and propose a solution that addresses this. The choice of the SST dataset is excellent, as its fine-grained sentiment annotations provide a rich supervised signal for training deep models and allow for detailed analysis of intermediate representations.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of stacking recursive layers to create Deep RNNs is a novel contribution. While the individual components (RNNs, deep networks) are well-established, their combination in this manner is a significant step forward.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates convincingly that Deep RNNs outperform shallow RNNs and achieve state-of-the-art results on the SST dataset. The ablation studies and comparisons with established baselines provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Clear Explanation & Motivation:** The authors clearly explain the motivation behind their work and provide a well-structured and easy-to-understand explanation of the Deep RNN architecture. The analogy to deep recurrent networks is particularly helpful.
* **Qualitative Analysis:**  The exploratory analyses, specifically the input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrase experiments, offer valuable insights into how different layers of the network capture different aspects of compositionality. These analyses go beyond mere quantitative results and provide a deeper understanding of the model’s behavior.
* **Careful Consideration of Implementation Details:** The authors meticulously address important implementation details such as activation functions (rectifiers), regularization (dropout and L2 penalty), and optimization (AdaGrad), demonstrating a thorough understanding of best practices in deep learning.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Exploration of Hyperparameters:** While the authors mention tuning the dropout rate, the paper lacks a detailed discussion of the hyperparameter search process. Exploring a wider range of hyperparameters, such as learning rates, batch sizes, and network widths/depths, could potentially lead to even better performance.
* **Untying Leaves and Internals – Justification:** The authors provide a rationale for untying leaves and internals, but the explanation feels somewhat post-hoc. A more rigorous analysis, perhaps comparing performance with and without untying under controlled conditions, would strengthen this design choice.
* **Scalability Concerns:**  The computational cost of training deep RNNs, especially with large parse trees, is not explicitly addressed. A discussion of scalability issues and potential solutions (e.g., distributed training, approximation techniques) would be beneficial.
* **Sensitivity to Parse Tree Quality:** The performance of RNNs is inherently dependent on the quality of the underlying parse trees. The paper does not address the potential impact of parse tree errors on the results.  Exploring the robustness of Deep RNNs to noisy or inaccurate parse trees would be a valuable addition.
* **Lack of Error Analysis:** While the qualitative analyses offer some insights, a more systematic error analysis could reveal specific types of errors that the Deep RNNs struggle with. This could inform future research directions.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to capturing compositional semantics in language.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods.
* The qualitative analyses provide valuable insights into the model's behavior.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation and methodology.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The hyperparameter search is not sufficiently detailed.
* The justification for untying leaves and internals could be strengthened.
* The scalability concerns are not adequately addressed.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field of NLP and deserves to be accepted to NIPS. The proposed Deep RNN architecture is a novel and promising approach to capturing hierarchical compositional semantics, and the empirical results are compelling. The qualitative analyses add significant value, providing insights into the inner workings of the model. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, particularly regarding hyperparameter tuning and scalability.  Specifically, adding a paragraph in the appendix discussing the hyperparameter search space and limitations would be helpful.
