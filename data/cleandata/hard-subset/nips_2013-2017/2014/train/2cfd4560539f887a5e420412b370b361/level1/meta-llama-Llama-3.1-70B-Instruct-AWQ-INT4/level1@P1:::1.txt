This paper introduces a new architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers on top of each other. The authors apply this architecture to the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees as the structure. The proposed model is evaluated against shallow recursive nets, as well as previous work on the task, including a multiplicative RNN and the more recent Paragraph Vectors method.

The main idea of the paper is to combine the concepts of depth in structure and depth in space, which are distinguished for recurrent neural networks. The authors propose a deep recursive neural network, which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, allowing for a hierarchical representation of the data. This is different from shallow recursive neural networks, where a single layer is responsible for learning a representation of composition that is both useful and sufficient for the final decision.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its components. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results show that the deep RNN outperforms its shallow counterparts, as well as previous work on the task. The authors also provide qualitative experiments that suggest that each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity.

The paper is related to previous work on recursive neural networks, which have been applied to various natural language processing tasks, such as parsing, sentence-level sentiment analysis, and paraphrase detection. The authors also draw inspiration from recent developments in deep recurrent neural networks, which have been shown to be effective in modeling temporal dependencies.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a new architecture that combines the concepts of depth in structure and depth in space.
* A thorough experimental evaluation that shows the effectiveness of the proposed model.
* Qualitative experiments that provide insights into how the different layers of the network capture different aspects of compositionality.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with recursive neural networks and deep learning architectures, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The experimental evaluation is limited to a single task, fine-grained sentiment classification, and it would be interesting to see how the proposed model performs on other tasks.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed model, which is an important consideration for practical applications.
