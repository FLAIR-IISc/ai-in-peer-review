- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks: stacking multiple recursive layers to capture hierarchical compositional semantics.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), showing improvements over shallow RNNs and state-of-the-art methods.  
- Builds on prior RNN, deep recurrent network, and word representation work with clear motivation for structural and spatial depth.  
- Novelty: combining stacked recursive layers is a significant and original contribution.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms baselines with convincing ablation studies.  
- Clear explanation and motivation, with a helpful analogy to deep recurrent networks.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) give insight into layer-wise compositionality.  
- Careful implementation: considered activation functions, regularization, and optimization best practices.  
- Weakness: limited exploration and reporting of hyperparameter search (dropout, learning rates, batch sizes, widths/depths).  
- Weakness: rationale for untying leaves and internals feels post-hoc and lacks controlled comparisons.  
- Weakness: scalability and computational cost, especially for large parse trees, are not addressed.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to parse tree quality not analyzed; robustness to noisy parses is unknown.  
- Weakness: lacks systematic error analysis to identify specific failure modes.  
- Recommendation: overall strong contribution; accept with minor revisions, especially adding hyperparameter search details (e.g., appendix paragraph).
