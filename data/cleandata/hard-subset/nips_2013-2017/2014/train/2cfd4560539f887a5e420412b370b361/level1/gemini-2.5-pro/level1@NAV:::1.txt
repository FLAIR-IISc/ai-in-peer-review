This paper introduces the Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN), a novel architecture that extends standard Recursive Neural Networks by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors draw a clear analogy to the development of deep recurrent networks, distinguishing between the "depth in structure" inherent in parsing a tree and the "depth in space" created by their multi-layer model. This work builds directly on the foundational recursive models for compositionality from Socher et al. (2011, 2013) and applies the well-established deep learning principle of hierarchical representation to this structural domain. The model is evaluated on the fine-grained sentiment classification task using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, a standard benchmark for this area.

**Strengths (Pro-Acceptance):**

*   **Significance & Quality:** The paper presents a significant empirical contribution, achieving new state-of-the-art results on a competitive benchmark. The carefully controlled experiments convincingly demonstrate that deeper, narrower networks outperform shallower, wider counterparts with a comparable number of parameters, providing strong evidence for the benefits of the proposed architecture.
*   **Quality & Clarity:** The work is technically sound and very well-executed. Beyond just reporting performance metrics, the authors provide insightful qualitative analyses. The input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase experiments offer compelling evidence that the stacked layers learn a hierarchy of representations, progressing from lexical and syntactic features in lower layers to more abstract, sentiment-oriented semantics in the final layer. This analysis greatly strengthens the paper.
*   **Originality:** The core idea of stacking recursive layers is a simple, elegant, and novel extension to an important class of models. The architectural choice to "untie" leaf and internal nodes is a practical and well-justified modification that enables the effective use of modern techniques like pre-trained word vectors and rectifier units.

**Weaknesses (Con-Acceptance):**

*   **Originality/Scope:** While the application to recursive networks is novel, the core concept of stacking layers to add representational depth is a standard technique in deep learning. The contribution is thus more of a successful and well-reasoned application of a known principle to a new model class rather than a fundamentally new paradigm.
*   **Significance:** The empirical validation is currently limited to a single dataset (SST) and a single task (sentiment analysis). While this is the most appropriate benchmark for initial validation, the paper’s broader claims about modeling compositionality in language would be significantly stronger if the benefits were also demonstrated on other tree-based NLP tasks, such as parsing or paraphrase detection.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that presents a clear, well-motivated model, strong empirical results, and insightful analysis. The strengths, particularly the new state-of-the-art performance and the qualitative evidence for hierarchical representation, far outweigh the minor weaknesses. The work represents a solid and valuable contribution to the field.
