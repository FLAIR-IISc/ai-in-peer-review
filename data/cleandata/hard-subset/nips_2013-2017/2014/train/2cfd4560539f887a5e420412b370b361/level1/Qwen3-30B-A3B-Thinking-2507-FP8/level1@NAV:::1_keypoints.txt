- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks that stack recursive layers to combine "depth in structure" with "depth in space."  
- Builds on prior recursive and deep recurrent work and processes parse trees hierarchically across multiple layers.  
- Empirically outperforms shallow recursive networks with the same parameter count on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), achieving state-of-the-art.  
- Provides thorough quantitative evaluations and insightful qualitative analyses.  
- Qualitative analysis shows different layers capture distinct compositional aspects (e.g., layer 1: word-level similarity; layer 3: sentiment-level similarity).  
- Methodology is technically sound, experiments are well-designed, and baselines are appropriate.  
- Conceptually valuable distinction between structural depth and spatial depth.  
- Novel architecture effectively combines structural and hierarchical depth with strong empirical support.  
- Weak: application is somewhat incremental, focusing mainly on sentiment analysis.  
- Weak: limited theoretical justification for why stacked recursive layers outperform standard deep networks.  
- Weak: does not adequately address why deeper networks degrade in performance or discuss failure cases and limitations.  
- Paper is well-written, reproducible, has broader potential for structured language tasks, and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
