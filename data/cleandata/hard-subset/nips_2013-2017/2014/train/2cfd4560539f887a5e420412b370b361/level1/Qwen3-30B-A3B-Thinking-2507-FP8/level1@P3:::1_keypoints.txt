- Introduces deep recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive layers to add hierarchical representation capacity beyond traditional RNNs.  
- Builds on Socher et al.'s recursive sentiment architecture, combining "depth in structure" with "depth in space."  
- Technically sound: clear architecture, detailed untied leaves/internals mechanism, and thorough experimental validation.  
- Strong experiments: ablation studies across depths/widths, comparisons to strong baselines, and statistically significant SOTA (49.5% fine-grained accuracy).  
- Excellent clarity and presentation: well-written, good visualizations (Fig.2), and transparent experimental details.  
- Compelling qualitative analyses showing different layers capture lexical similarity, sentiment intensity, and sentiment polarity.  
- Significant contribution: demonstrates stacking layers ("depth in space") yields benefits beyond recursive depth for compositionality.  
- Novelty is incremental: stacking layers is standard in deep learning and this extension to recursive nets is a natural progression.  
- Lacks theoretical justification: no formal analysis of why deep stacking improves representational capacity over shallow recursive models.  
- Practical limitations: uses fixed word vectors without fine-tuning and omits discussion of computational costs for deeper recursive models.  
- Limited comparisons to other deep tree-structured architectures (e.g., deep recursive tensor/compositional models).  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with strong empirical and qualitative contributions.  
- Pro arguments: SOTA results, insightful layer analyses, sound methodology, and continuity with established NIPS work.  
- Con arguments: incremental novelty, weak theoretical grounding, no fine-tuning exploration, and limited related-work comparison.  
- Recommendation: Accept — empirical strength and interpretability outweigh the minor novelty and theoretical limitations.
