- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network architecture that stacks multiple recursive layers to capture hierarchical language representations.  
- Provides thorough background on recursive neural networks and motivations for deeper architectures over shallow models.  
- Clearly explains the deep RNN design, including weight sharing and layer-wise updates.  
- Conducts thorough experiments on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, comparing to shallow RNNs, multiplicative variants, and paragraph vectors.  
- Shows deep RNNs outperform shallow counterparts and achieve state-of-the-art results on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Presents insightful qualitative analyses (layer responses to perturbations and nearest-neighbor phrase inspections) indicating each layer captures different compositional aspects.  
- Strength: novel architecture addressing compositionality in NLP.  
- Strength: comprehensive and well-executed experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: qualitative analysis sheds light on model behavior across layers.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the architecture’s limitations.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter effects on performance.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed comparisons to other state-of-the-art NLP architectures.  
- Originality: deep RNN is a significant and novel contribution to modeling compositionality.  
- Significance: achieves SOTA results and has potential impact on NLP research.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, easy to follow, and descriptions are clear.  
- Quality: technically sound, claims supported by theory and experiments, and evaluations are honest.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong experiments, and informative qualitative findings.  
- Rejection concerns: missing limitations discussion, hyperparameter study, and broader SOTA comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
