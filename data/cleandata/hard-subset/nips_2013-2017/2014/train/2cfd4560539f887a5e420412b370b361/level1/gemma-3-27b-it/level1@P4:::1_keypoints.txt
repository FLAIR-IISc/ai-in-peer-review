Deep RNNs extend recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive layers to better model compositionality in language.  
Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, the model achieves state-of-the-art performance with analyses of layer effects.  
Well-motivated idea that naturally extends prior RNN and deep learning work.  
Methodology and experimental setup are clear and technically sound, using appropriate baselines and regularization.  
Ablation studies on depth and width, plus input perturbation and nearest-neighbor analyses, provide useful interpretability.  
Novel architectural contribution by stacking recursive layers.  
Use of dropout, L2 regularization, and ablations demonstrates practical training attention.  
Paper is well-written and figures effectively illustrate the architecture.  
Significant result: state-of-the-art on a standard fine-grained sentiment benchmark.  
Depth exploration is limited (only up to 4 layers), leaving open whether more depth helps or hurts.  
Lacks detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity beyond dropout tuning.  
Justification for untying leaves and internals is empirical and would benefit from stronger theoretical rationale.  
Paper focuses on sentiment analysis with limited discussion of generalization to other compositional tasks.  
Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with valuable analyses; recommendation for acceptance despite areas for improvement.
