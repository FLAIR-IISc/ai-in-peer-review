

# Peer Review

## Summary
This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (Deep RNNs), a novel architecture that combines the "depth in structure" of recursive neural networks (which operate on parse trees) with "depth in space" (stacked layers), analogous to deep feedforward networks. The authors argue that while traditional recursive neural networks are "deep in structure" due to their recursive application, they lack the hierarchical representation capabilities of deep feedforward networks. The proposed Deep RNN stacks multiple recursive layers, where each layer processes the same parse tree structure but passes intermediate representations to the next layer. The authors evaluate their approach on fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating that Deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive networks with the same number of parameters and previous state-of-the-art baselines, including multiplicative RNN variants and paragraph vectors. The paper includes exploratory analyses showing that different layers capture distinct aspects of language compositionality.

The work builds on Socher et al.'s recursive neural networks (NIPS 2011, 2013) and recent advances in deep learning for NLP (e.g., word embeddings from Mikolov et al., NIPS 2013). It also relates to deep recurrent networks (Hermans & Schrauwen, NIPS 2013), though the authors distinguish between depth in time (recurrent) and depth in structure (recursive).

## Strengths
1. **Technical Soundness**: The paper presents a well-structured methodology with clear mathematical formulations. The architecture is carefully designed to distinguish between leaf (word) and internal (phrase) nodes, addressing an important limitation in previous recursive networks. The experimental setup is thorough, with appropriate baselines, regularization techniques (dropout), and evaluation metrics.

2. **Novelty**: The distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" is conceptually valuable. While stacking layers in feedforward networks is common, applying this to recursive architectures represents a meaningful extension of the field. The paper properly situates this contribution relative to prior NIPS work.

3. **Empirical Results**: The results are compelling, with the Deep RNN achieving state-of-the-art performance on SST. The ablation studies showing consistent improvement with increased depth (up to a point) provide strong evidence for the value of the proposed architecture. The qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbor phrases) offer valuable insights into how different layers process compositionality.

4. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and well-organized, with helpful figures (Figures 1-3) that clarify the architecture and analysis. The methodology section provides sufficient detail for reproduction, and the writing is accessible to NIPS readers.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Justification for Architecture Choice**: The paper doesn't sufficiently explain why stacking recursive layers is preferable to alternative approaches for adding depth to recursive networks (e.g., deeper parse trees or more complex composition functions). The distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" is conceptually useful, but the paper doesn't fully demonstrate why this particular combination is superior to other possible extensions.

2. **Comparison with Deep Recurrent Networks**: The paper references deep recurrent networks (Hermans & Schrauwen, NIPS 2013) but doesn't directly compare with them or explain why their recursive architecture is better suited for language compositionality than deep recurrent approaches.

3. **Scope of Analysis**: The qualitative analyses are insightful but limited to sentiment analysis. The paper doesn't sufficiently explore how the architecture might generalize to other NLP tasks beyond sentiment classification, which would strengthen the significance claim.

4. **"State-of-the-Art" Claims**: While the paper reports improvements over previous baselines, the magnitude of improvement (e.g., 49.5% vs. 48.1% on SST fine-grained classification) is modest. The paper could better quantify the significance of these improvements and more precisely situate them relative to the previous state-of-the-art.

## Overall Assessment

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The methodology is sound, the experiments are well-designed, and the results are consistent with the claims. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations (e.g., fixed word vectors, supervised task focus).

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and well-written. The figures effectively complement the text, and the methodology is sufficiently detailed for reproduction.

**Originality**: The Deep RNN architecture represents a meaningful extension of recursive neural networks, with the "depth in structure vs. depth in space" distinction providing valuable conceptual clarity. While not a radical departure from existing work, it's a significant and novel contribution to the specific area of recursive architectures for language.

**Significance**: The work makes a valuable contribution to the field of NLP, particularly for modeling compositionality in language. The improvements over previous baselines are meaningful, and the qualitative analyses provide useful insights into how deep recursive networks process language. While the scope is currently limited to sentiment analysis, the approach could potentially generalize to other NLP tasks.

## Pro/Con for Acceptance

**Pro**:
- Novel architecture that combines two types of depth (structure and space) in recursive networks
- Strong empirical results showing consistent improvement over baselines
- Insightful qualitative analyses demonstrating different layers capture different aspects of compositionality
- Well-written and technically sound paper with clear methodology

**Con**:
- Limited exploration of generalization beyond sentiment analysis
- Modest improvements over previous state-of-the-art
- Insufficient justification for why stacking recursive layers is better than alternative approaches to adding depth

## Recommendation
This paper makes a solid contribution to the field of deep learning for NLP, particularly in modeling compositionality through recursive architectures. While the improvements are incremental rather than revolutionary, the paper is technically sound, well-written, and provides valuable insights into how deep recursive networks process language. The distinction between depth in structure and depth in space is conceptually important and clarifies a key aspect of deep learning in structured domains.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the following:
1. Better clarify why stacking recursive layers is preferable to alternative approaches for adding depth (e.g., deeper parse trees)
2. More precisely quantify the significance of the improvements over previous state-of-the-art
3. Expand the discussion on potential generalization to other NLP tasks

The paper is a strong candidate for NIPS, advancing the state of the art in a meaningful way while providing valuable theoretical and empirical insights.
