- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Network that stacks recursive layers to add “depth in space” to tree-structured models.  
- Node representations combine children's representations in the same layer with the node’s representation from the layer below.  
- Inspired by deep recurrent networks; combines ideas from recursive nets and deep feed-forward stacking.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment) and uses strong modern training practices.  
- Introduces untied weights for leaf vs. internal nodes in first layer to leverage pre-trained embeddings and ReLUs.  
- Deep RNNs consistently outperform shallow counterparts with a comparable parameter budget.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SST, beating RNTN and Paragraph Vectors.  
- Qualitative analyses suggest layers learn a compositional hierarchy: lexical → syntactic → semantic/sentiment.  
- Experimental setup is thorough (pretrained vectors, dropout, AdaGrad) and comparisons are fair.  
- Paper is very clear, well-written, and includes effective illustrative figures (e.g., weight sharing/ connectivity).  
- Much of the gain over older RNN baselines comes from modernization of the shallow baseline (untied weights, ReLU, dropout, word2vec).  
- Absolute improvement from shallow modern baseline to 4-layer deep model is modest (~1.7 percentage points).  
- Relies on precomputed parse trees, making performance dependent on external parsers and limiting applicability in some domains.  
- Novelty is a natural combination of existing ideas but is well-executed and empirically justified.  
- Overall: strong, well-supported contribution with clear writing; recommended for acceptance.
