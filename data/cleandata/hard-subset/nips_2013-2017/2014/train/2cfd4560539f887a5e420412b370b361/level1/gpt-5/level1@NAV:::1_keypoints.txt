- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks that stack multiple recursive layers to add “depth in space” and untie leaves from internal nodes to enable ReLU and fixed pretrained word vectors.  
- Trained with dropout and AdaGrad; evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank for 5-class and binary sentiment classification.  
- Under a fixed parameter budget, deeper-but-narrower models outperform shallow counterparts.  
- Best configuration achieves state-of-the-art versus RNTN, MV-RNN, DCNN, and Paragraph Vectors.  
- Layer-wise analyses (input perturbations, nearest neighbors) indicate lower layers track lexical/syntactic patterns while higher layers capture sentiment intensity/polarity.  
- Positions work relative to prior temporal vs spatial depth literature and extends tree-structured composition models (Socher et al. 2013).  
- Technical contribution is clearly specified with equations, training details, and principled parameter-matched depth comparisons.  
- Empirical methodology is sound but limited to a single dataset and lacks statistical significance testing.  
- Missing ablation studies (impact of untying, ReLU, dropout individually) that would strengthen causal claims.  
- Computational costs and training stability are not quantified beyond a small L2 on activations.  
- Paper is well organized and generally clear; needs explicit complexity analysis, runtimes, and fuller description of dropout sharing.  
- Novelty is moderate but substantive; untying leaves/internals with ReLU is a useful design choice.  
- Significance: advances SOTA on SST and suggests a general design pattern, but empirical scope is narrow.  
- Pros: clear architectural advance, strong baseline comparisons, and insightful qualitative analyses.  
- Cons: single dataset, no statistical tests, missing ablations and cost analysis, relies on gold parses without robustness tests.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending minor revisions addressing ablations and additional reporting.
