- Proposes deep recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive layers over parse trees with vertical state passing and shared within-layer parameters.
- Introduces untied parameterization between leaves and internal nodes to enable ReLU usage and large pretrained embeddings without quadratic parameter growth.
- Trains with backpropagation through structure, AdaGrad, dropout (shared masks per sentence-layer), and small L2 penalties; supervision only at the top layer.
- Experiments on SST show deeper-and-narrower RecNNs outperform shallow-and-wider ones under matched parameter budgets and achieve state-of-the-art results.
- Qualitative analyses indicate different layers capture distinct compositional phenomena (e.g., intensity vs. polarity) though evidence is anecdotal.
- Technically sound: coherent extension of RecNNs with well-motivated training and clear untie scheme.
- Empirical support is promising but lacking some controls: explicit parameter counts, run-to-run variability, and statistical significance testing are missing.
- Some baseline comparisons mix modeling/training choices (e.g., fixed vs. fine-tuned embeddings), raising fairness concerns.
- Clarity is good overall with clear equations and figures, but terminology (“RNN” vs. “RecNN”), parameter/training details, and parser assumptions need clarification.
- Originality lies in stacking recursive layers (depth in space) and the practical untie/ReLU design; components are known but their combination and evaluation are nontrivial.
- Significance: advances SOTA on an important benchmark and suggests depth helps tree compositionality, but evaluation is limited to a single dataset and gold parses.
- Pros: principled model extension, consistent empirical gains under comparable budgets, new SST SOTA, and insightful layer analyses.
- Cons: narrow evaluation scope, no robustness to parser errors, incomplete experimental controls, and limited analysis of computational cost/scalability.
- Suggested improvements: report exact parameter counts, training time, memory; provide multi-seed results with std and significance tests.
- Suggested improvements: add ablations for untying, activation choice, supervision placement, and embedding fine-tuning.
- Suggested improvements: evaluate robustness to parsing noise, test on additional structured tasks, and ensure parameter-matched baseline comparisons.
- Suggested improvements: expand systematic probing of layer behavior with controlled diagnostics (negation, intensity, templates).
- Overall: a solid, clearly presented contribution that shows depth benefits in tree-based models but would be stronger with broader evaluation and tighter experimental controls.
