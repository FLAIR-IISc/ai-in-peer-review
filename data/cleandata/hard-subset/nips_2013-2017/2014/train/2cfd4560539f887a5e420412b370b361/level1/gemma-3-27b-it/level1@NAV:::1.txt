## Review of “Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language”

This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (Deep RNNs), extending the traditional Recursive Neural Network (RNN) framework by stacking multiple recursive layers. The motivation stems from the desire to incorporate the benefits of depth, observed in deep feedforward and recurrent networks, into compositional models of language. The authors hypothesize that multiple layers can capture different aspects of compositionality. They evaluate their model on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating state-of-the-art results in fine-grained sentiment classification, outperforming shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and Paragraph Vectors. The paper also includes exploratory analysis examining the effect of different layers and their captured representations. 

The work builds upon a strong foundation in recursive and recurrent neural networks (Socher et al., 2013, 2012; Elman, 1991). It directly addresses the limitations of shallow RNNs by integrating ideas from deep learning, specifically inspired by Deep Recurrent Networks (Schmidhuber, 2011).  The authors clearly position their contribution within the existing literature, referencing relevant prior work on compositional semantics and deep learning architectures. The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the core concepts and methodology.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of stacking recursive layers to create a Deep RNN is a novel contribution, bridging the gap between traditional RNNs and deep learning paradigms.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The authors demonstrate significant performance improvements on a challenging benchmark dataset (SST), achieving state-of-the-art results.
* **Ablation and Analysis:** The included exploratory analyses, such as input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis, provide valuable insights into the behavior of different layers and the type of information they capture.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand, effectively explaining the technical details of the proposed architecture.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Analysis:** While the analyses are insightful, they are somewhat limited. A more detailed investigation of the learned representations (e.g., visualization of hidden unit activations) could offer a deeper understanding of the model's inner workings.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The results highlight a sensitivity to network depth and width. More discussion on hyperparameter tuning and generalizability to other datasets would be beneficial.
* **Computational Cost:** Stacking layers inherently increases computational complexity. A discussion on scalability and efficiency of the proposed model, particularly for larger datasets, is missing.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to compositional modeling.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate a significant advancement in the field.
* The exploratory analyses provide valuable insights and contribute to a better understanding of the model.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The analysis, while informative, could be more comprehensive.
*  Scalability and computational cost concerns are not adequately addressed.

**Overall:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing. The proposed Deep RNN architecture is a promising approach to compositional modeling, and the empirical results are impressive. While some aspects of the analysis and scalability could be further explored, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
