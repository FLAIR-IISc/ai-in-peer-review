- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks by stacking multiple recursive layers to capture hierarchical language representations.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and achieves state-of-the-art improvements over shallow RNNs and strong baselines.  
- Exploratory analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) suggest different layers capture distinct aspects of compositionality.  
- Strong connection to prior work on recursive/recurrent nets and compositional semantics; motivation is well-articulated.  
- Technical soundness: architecture is well-defined, including untying of leaves and internals.  
- Empirical results are compelling, showing clear performance gains on a challenging benchmark.  
- Exploratory analyses provide useful qualitative insight into layer behavior.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with effective figures illustrating the model.  
- Authors acknowledge and address vanishing gradient concerns using fine-grained labels and regularization.  
- Weak ablation study: more analysis needed on components like untying leaves vs internals.  
- Limited hyperparameter tuning details and sensitivity analysis (learning rates, regularization, configurations).  
- Missing discussion of computational cost and training practicality compared to baselines.  
- Qualitative analyses are somewhat limited in scope and could be deeper to validate claims.  
- Recommendation: solid contribution with strong results; accept with minor revisions to expand ablation, hyperparameter details, and computational cost discussion.
