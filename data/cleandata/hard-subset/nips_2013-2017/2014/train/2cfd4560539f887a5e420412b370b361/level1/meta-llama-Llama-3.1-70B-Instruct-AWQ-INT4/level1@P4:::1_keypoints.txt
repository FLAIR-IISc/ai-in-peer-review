- Proposes deep recursive neural networks (deep RNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to combine benefits of recursive and deep feedforward networks.  
- Uses binary parse trees for structure and introduces an untied variant distinguishing leaf and internal nodes to leverage pre-trained word vectors and rectifier activations.  
- Demonstrates improved hierarchical compositional representations compared to traditional (shallow) RNNs.  
- Evaluates on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and reports state-of-the-art performance versus prior baselines.  
- Provides qualitative analyses showing different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the proposed architecture and benefits effectively.  
- Strength: novel architecture combining recursive and deep feedforward ideas with practical performance gains.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation on a challenging sentiment task.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with RNNs and deep feedforward networks, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis (time and space).  
- Weakness: does not compare in depth to other deep architectures like CNNs or LSTMs.  
- Originality: introduces a new architecture and novel analysis of multilayer effects on compositionality.  
- Significance: achieves SOTA on SST and offers a new perspective on linguistic compositionality.  
- Overall: strong, well-executed paper with empirical and qualitative contributions, tempered by gaps in complexity analysis and comparative evaluation.
