This paper proposes a novel deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture, which stacks multiple recursive layers to model compositionality in natural language. The authors evaluate their model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) dataset.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper introduces a deep RNN architecture that combines the benefits of recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose a new way of stacking multiple recursive layers, which allows for a hierarchical representation of the input data. They also introduce an untied variant of the recursive neural network, which distinguishes between leaf and internal nodes. The authors evaluate their model on the SST dataset and show that it outperforms shallow recursive nets and previous state-of-the-art models.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel architecture that combines the benefits of recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on the SST dataset, including a comparison with previous state-of-the-art models.
3. The paper includes a qualitative analysis of the model's performance, including an investigation of the effect of multiple layers on the model's ability to capture different aspects of compositionality.
4. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, including a detailed description of their methodology and experimental setup.
