- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated extension of recursive neural networks that achieves state-of-the-art on SST; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

- Summary: Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (stacked recursive layers) to learn hierarchical compositional representations, building on prior RNN and deep recurrent work.

- Relation to prior work: Connects directly to existing RNN, deep recurrent, and compositional semantics literature and analogizes to deep feedforward networks.

- Strength — Motivation: Clear intuition that stacking recursive layers lets different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.

- Strength — Empirical results: DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs and achieve state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

- Strength — Experiments: Extensive experiments varying depth and width with constant parameter budgets and useful ablation studies on dropout and activations.

- Strength — Qualitative analysis: Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analyses give insight into layer-specific representations.

- Strength — Presentation: Well-written, well-organized, and methodologically detailed.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Clear mathematical formulation; untying leaves and internals is justified and empirically helpful.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Experiments are confined to SST, limiting claims about generalizability to other tasks/datasets.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Optimal depth depends on width; trade-offs need more discussion and systematic search.

- Weakness — Computational cost: Additional recursive layers likely increase compute and memory, but this is not quantified or discussed.

- Weakness — Error analysis: Lacks detailed analysis of failure cases and specific model errors.

- Suggestion — Clarify SST benefit: Explicitly explain how SST’s richer supervised signal helps alleviate vanishing gradients.

- Suggestion — Untying interpretability: Discuss whether untying leaves and internals affects interpretability of learned representations.

- Suggestion — L2 penalty justification: Provide rationale for using a small fixed L2 penalty to prevent parameter explosions.

- Suggestion — Report computational cost: Include training time and memory usage for different network configurations.

- Suggestion — Explain layer behavior: Give a more detailed explanation for why the second layer focuses on intensity rather than direction in input perturbation results.

- Suggestion — Distance metrics: Discuss limitations of one-norm nearest-neighbor comparisons and consider alternative metrics.

- For acceptance: SOTA results, sound architecture extension, insightful qualitative analyses, and clear writing.

- Against acceptance: Single-dataset evaluation, unexplored hyperparameter/computational trade-offs, and insufficient error analysis.

- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing the above points.
