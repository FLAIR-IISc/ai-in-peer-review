- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to combine "depth in structure" with "depth in space" for compositional language understanding.  
- Provides a clear mathematical formulation (Equation 4) and implementation details enabling reproducibility.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) with strong quantitative results (2-layer and 4-layer models ~49.5% fine-grained accuracy).  
- Experimental design is thorough: standard benchmark, relevant baselines (including Socher et al., 2013), quantitative and qualitative analyses.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbor phrases) support the claim that different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-executed, and well written with effective figures and organization.  
- Did not fine-tune word vectors in experiments (noted as a limitation/future direction).  
- Minor clarity improvement: should more explicitly connect and compare to "deep recurrent networks" mentioned in the introduction.  
- Novelty is incremental: stacking layers is a familiar deep-learning idea, but application to recursive networks and hierarchical representations is meaningful.  
- Could better distinguish DRNNs from naive stacking of multiple recursive networks (weight-sharing nuance).  
- Significance is clear for sentiment analysis (state-of-the-art) but limited evidence of generalization to other NLP tasks.  
- Strengths: clear concept, strong empirical results, insightful qualitative analysis, and solid experimental design.  
- Weaknesses: incremental novelty, limited broader applicability, insufficient comparison to naive stacking, and lack of word-vector fine-tuning.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
