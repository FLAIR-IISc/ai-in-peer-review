This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs), stacking recursive layers to combine depth in structure with depth in space, inspired by recent work on deep recurrent networks. The core idea—applying multiple layers of recursive composition—is a compelling and intuitively appealing extension of existing recursive neural network approaches. While the empirical evaluation focuses on sentiment analysis using the SST dataset, the potential applicability to other structured data scenarios is clear. This represents a genuinely novel direction within the field of compositional semantics.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture is a significant departure from standard shallow recursive networks and offers a potentially powerful way to capture hierarchical compositional semantics. The explicit motivation drawn from the advancements in deep recurrent networks strengthens the justification.
*   **Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates clear improvements over shallow RNNs and existing state-of-the-art methods on the SST dataset. The ablation studies, while not extensive, provide some evidence for the benefits of depth.
*   **Qualitative Analysis:** The input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrase analyses provide valuable insights into how different layers of the network are processing information. This goes beyond purely quantitative results and offers a glimpse into the internal workings of the model.
*   **Sound Methodology:** The methodology is generally well-defined and utilizes established techniques like dropout and AdaGrad. The untying of leaf and internal nodes is a sensible extension and contributes to the model’s performance.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and well-organized. The explanations of the architecture and methodology are easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The evaluation is currently limited to a single task (sentiment analysis) and a single dataset (SST). While SST is a commonly used benchmark, it would be beneficial to see the DRNN applied to other tasks involving structured data, such as parsing or semantic relation extraction.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance seems sensitive to the number of layers, with diminishing returns beyond a certain depth. A more thorough exploration of the hyperparameter space and a discussion of the factors influencing optimal depth would be valuable.
*   **Ablation Study Depth:** While the ablation studies are a good start, they could be more comprehensive. For example, analyzing the impact of different activation functions or regularization techniques in the deeper layers could provide further insights.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the motivation is intuitive, a more rigorous theoretical justification for the benefits of deep recursive networks would strengthen the paper. For instance, exploring connections to hierarchical Bayesian models or compositional distributional semantics could be beneficial.
*   **Limited discussion on computational cost:** Stacking layers increases the computational burden. A discussion on the efficiency of the proposed architecture, compared to alternatives, is missing.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a truly novel architectural contribution that has the potential to significantly impact the field of compositional semantics.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods.
*   The qualitative analyses provide valuable insights into the model’s behavior.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
*   The potential for broader application beyond sentiment analysis is significant.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a single task and dataset.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity and lack of a comprehensive ablation study raise some concerns about the robustness of the results.
*   The theoretical justification could be stronger.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that introduces a compelling new architectural approach. While there are some areas for improvement, the novelty and potential impact of the work outweigh the weaknesses. I believe that the DRNN architecture represents a significant step forward in the field of compositional semantics, and its exploration is a valuable contribution to the NIPS community. I am willing to overlook the relatively limited scope of the evaluation and the lack of a fully comprehensive ablation study, given the originality of the idea and the promising empirical results.

**Recommendation:** Accept. I would encourage the authors to address the limitations outlined above in future work. Particularly, broadening the evaluation to other tasks and datasets would further strengthen the paper's contribution.
