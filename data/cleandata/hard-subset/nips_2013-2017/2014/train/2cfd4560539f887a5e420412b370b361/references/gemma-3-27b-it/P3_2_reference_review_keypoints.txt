- Overall assessment: Proposes a deep extension of recursive neural networks with strong SST results, clear writing, and a recommendation to accept with minor revisions due to limited qualitative/theoretical analysis.

- Summary & related work: Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) by stacking recursive layers to model compositionality and situates the work well among RNN and tree-structured literature.

- Strength — Strong empirical results: DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs and prior state-of-the-art on SST with statistically significant gains.

- Strength — Well-motivated architecture: Clear rationale for stacking recursive layers and a useful framing of “depth in structure” vs. “depth in space.”

- Strength — Clear writing and figures: Paper is readable and Figures (e.g., Fig. 2) effectively illustrate the model.

- Strength — Careful experimental setup: Uses pre-trained embeddings, dropout, diagonal AdaGrad, and comprehensive baseline comparisons.

- Strength — Untying leaves and internals: Separating leaf/internal representations enables rectifiers and larger embeddings, improving performance.

- Weakness — Limited theoretical justification: Lacks deeper analysis of why the specific deep recursive form improves representation learning or gradient/expressivity properties.

- Weakness — Weak qualitative analysis: Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor analyses are interesting but anecdotal and not comprehensive.

- Weakness — Missing sensitivity analysis: No systematic study of hyperparameters like layer count, hidden size, or dropout and limited description of tuning.

- Weakness — Computational cost unaddressed: Paper does not report training cost or compare efficiency to shallow RNNs.

- For acceptance: Significant empirical improvement, novel and well-motivated architecture, clear presentation, and rigorous baselines.

- Against acceptance: Insufficient theoretical grounding, limited qualitative analysis, and no discussion of computational cost.

- Revision suggestion — Theoretical analysis: Add discussion or brief analysis relating depth to representational power or learnability compared to shallow RNNs.

- Revision suggestion — Enhanced qualitative analysis: Visualize hidden activations/weights and provide quantitative metrics for phrase similarity to better explain layer-wise behavior.

- Revision suggestion — Hyperparameter/sensitivity details: Report hyperparameter tuning procedures and include sensitivity experiments (layers, hidden size, dropout).

- Revision suggestion — Report computational cost: Provide training time, memory requirements, and comparison to shallow RNNs for practical assessment.

- Revision suggestion — Minor edits: Rephrase awkward conclusion sentence (“Since our task was supervised...”) for clarity and connection to future work.
