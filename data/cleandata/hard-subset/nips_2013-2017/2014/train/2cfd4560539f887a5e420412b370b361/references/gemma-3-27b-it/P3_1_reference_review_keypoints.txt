- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack recursive layers to capture hierarchical compositional semantics.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements and state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) show lower layers capture word sentiment while higher layers capture abstract composition.  
- Well-situated in prior RNN literature and motivated by depth-in-space vs depth-in-time distinctions.  
- Methodology and training (untying leaves/internals, dropout, AdaGrad) are clearly described and technically sound.  
- Writing and organization are clear, making ideas accessible.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to SST; need broader tasks/datasets to demonstrate generalization.  
- Hyperparameter selection details (learning rate, L2, hidden size) and sensitivity analysis are lacking.  
- Comparisons use somewhat dated baselines; should include more recent models (e.g., transformers, GNNs).  
- Missing systematic ablation studies on key architectural choices (untying, activation functions, depth).  
- No analysis of computational cost (training time, memory) for deeper architectures.  
- Section 2.3 (V(i) connections) needs clearer explanation regarding leaf/internal handling across layers.  
- Justify the use of fixed word embeddings or evaluate finetuning.  
- Table 1 should include standard deviations/confidence intervals to assess significance.  
- Make perturbation experiments more systematic and quantitative.  
- Nearest-neighbor analysis would benefit from examples of incorrect neighbors and error discussion.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on expanding experiments and clarifying methodological details.
