- Summary: Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (stacked recursive layers) and shows improved fine-grained sentiment performance on SST with qualitative analysis of layer roles.  
- Novelty: Stacking recursive layers is a reasonable, logical extension of RNNs to increase hierarchical composition capacity.  
- Strong empirical results: DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs and prior state-of-the-art on the SST dataset.  
- Thorough experimentation: Includes baselines, ablations (depth/width), and qualitative analyses.  
- Qualitative analysis: Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase experiments suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and methodologically detailed for reproducibility.  
- Incremental contribution: Idea feels incremental—natural extension rather than a paradigm shift, with modest gains.  
- Limited evaluation scope: Experiments only on SST and sentiment; unclear generalization to other tasks or structural representations.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results depend on dropout and other hyperparameters; more sensitivity analysis (learning rate, L2) is needed.  
- Untying leaves/internals justification: Rationale for untying leaf and internal node parameters is weak and needs more rigorous analysis of its impact.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound and careful but slightly weakened by limited scope and incremental novelty.  
- Originality assessment: Novel in stacking recursive layers; untying is a minor additional idea.  
- Significance assessment: Advances SST state-of-the-art and shows depth benefits for recursive nets but broader NLP impact is uncertain.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: Clear improvement on a challenging task, logical extension, thorough experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: Incremental work, single-task evaluation, and insufficient hyperparameter/design justification.  
- Overall assessment: Weak accept contingent on addressing limitations, discussing generalization, and analyzing hyperparameter/design choices.
