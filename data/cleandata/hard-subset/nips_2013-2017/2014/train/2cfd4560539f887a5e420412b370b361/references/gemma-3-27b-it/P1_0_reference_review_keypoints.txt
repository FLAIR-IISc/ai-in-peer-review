- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) by stacking recursive layers to better capture hierarchical compositionality in language, evaluated on SST for fine-grained sentiment.

- Demonstrates DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and prior state-of-the-art on SST, with qualitative analysis showing different layers learn different compositional aspects.

- Builds on prior recursive-network work (Socher et al.) and parallels depth in temporal RNNs, situating contributions against existing SST benchmarks.

- Novelty: logically extends depth into the structural/recursive domain, a worthwhile and promising idea.

- Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art performance on SST with informative ablation studies across depth and width.

- Thorough experimental setup: detailed hyperparameters, regularization, and word vector initialization enabling reproducibility.

- Qualitative analysis: input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase experiments provide insight into layer-wise representations beyond metrics.

- Clear writing and organization: motivation and methodology are presented accessibly.

- Limited scope: evaluation restricted to a single dataset and task (SST/fine-grained sentiment), raising generalizability concerns.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: tuning focused on dropout; sensitivity to other hyperparameters (learning rate, L2, layer sizes) is underexplored.

- Theoretical justification: lacks rigorous analysis of why stacking recursive layers increases representational capacity versus shallow RNNs.

- Limited error analysis: insufficient examination of specific failure cases to identify model limitations.

- Untying leaves vs internals: justification provided but impact and necessity need stronger empirical comparison, especially controlling for model size.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, strong empirical evidence, valuable qualitative insights, and clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation breadth, weak theoretical grounding, and incomplete hyperparameter analysis.

- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommended accept with minor revisions.

- Revision requests: acknowledge evaluation limitations and future directions for generalizability.

- Revision requests: add discussion comparing computational complexity (training time, memory) of DRNNs vs shallow RNNs.

- Revision requests: include a table comparing performance of tied vs untied leaves/internals (controlled for model size).
