- Overall: Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack recursive layers, shows improved performance on SST, recommends accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: DRNN is a logical extension of RNNs combining “depth in structure” and “depth in space.”  
- Empirical Results: Consistent, statistically significant improvements over shallow RNNs and prior methods on SST.  
- Qualitative Analysis: Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analyses indicate different layers capture different compositional aspects; visualizations are informative.  
- Methodology: Uses standard best practices (dropout, AdaGrad, rectifiers) and sensible modifications like untying leaves/internals.  
- Writing: Paper is generally clear and well organized.  
- Baseline Comparison Weakness: Lacks comparison to standard deep architectures (e.g., deep feedforward nets, Transformers).  
- Dataset Specificity Weakness: Evaluation limited to SST, which may not represent broader compositional semantics.  
- Depth/Width Tradeoff Weakness: Notes performance degradation with increased depth but lacks systematic exploration of depth vs. width.  
- Theoretical Weakness: No formal theoretical analysis explaining why stacking recursive layers improves compositionality.  
- Hyperparameter Weakness: Missing discussion of sensitivity to hyperparameters affecting reproducibility and robustness.  
- Specific Comment: Justification for not connecting the output layer to all hidden layers is unconvincing.  
- Specific Comment: Explanation of untying leaves and internals is well articulated.  
- Specific Request: Report exact sizes of weight matrices (W(i)L, W(i)R, V(i)) for configurations.  
- Specific Request: Perturbation experiment should include quantitative measures (e.g., magnitude of hidden representation change).  
- Specific Request: Provide more examples in Table 2 to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Pro-Accept Arguments: Novel, well-motivated architecture; strong empirical performance; insightful qualitative analyses; sound methodology and clear writing.  
- Con-Accept Arguments: Limited baseline comparisons, potential generalizability issues, and lack of deeper theoretical analysis.
