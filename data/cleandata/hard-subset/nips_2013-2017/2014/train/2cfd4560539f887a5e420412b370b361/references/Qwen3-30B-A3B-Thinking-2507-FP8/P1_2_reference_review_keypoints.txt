- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to combine "depth in structure" with "depth in space."
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) with state-of-the-art but modest improvements and qualitative analysis showing layer-wise compositionality.

- Technically sound with clear architecture equations and reproducible implementation details.
- Experimental setup is standard and comparisons include strong baselines.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) provide insight into layer behavior.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and directions for future work.

- Fails to clearly distinguish DRNNs from prior deep recursive/recurrent network variants.
- Weak theoretical justification for why stacking recursive layers improves performance.
- Does not address computational/training cost trade-offs versus shallow RNNs.
- The "depth in structure" vs. "depth in space" distinction is confusingly presented.
- Uses pretrained word vectors without fine-tuning and does not analyze the impact of fine-tuning.

- Paper is well-written, organized, and supported by helpful figures.
- Methodology section gives sufficient detail for reproduction.

- Explanation of "depth in structure" vs. "depth in space" is unclear and muddles the novelty.
- Differences from prior deep recursive/recurrent networks are not sufficiently clarified.
- "Untying leaves and internals" section is confusing and its benefits are not well connected to the task.
- Input perturbation experiment design choices lack clear justification.

- Novel architecture applying layer stacking to recursive networks is a useful extension.
- The "untied" leaves vs. internals variant is a beneficial modification.

- Stacking layers to create depth is a standard idea; novelty is limited to its application to recursive networks.
- Literature review is insufficient; related deep recursive work is not thoroughly discussed or cited.
- Prior deep recursive/recurrent work that might be similar is not addressed.

- Demonstrates SOTA on SST and offers insights into compositionality across layers.
- Qualitative analysis adds value to understanding layer-specific behavior.
- Contributes to research on linguistic compositionality in NLP.

- Improvement over prior SOTA is modest (49.0% → 49.5%), questioning significance.
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (SST), so broader impact is unclear.
- Does not compare DRNNs to equivalently sized deeper feedforward alternatives or justify why DRNNs are preferable.

- Overall, a reasonable and technically sound extension with modest empirical gains and interesting qualitative findings.
- Significant weaknesses in clarity, originality claims, theoretical grounding, and practical evaluation limit the contribution.

- Recommendation: Reject.

- Clarify the novelty and more clearly define "depth in structure" vs. "depth in space."
- Provide theoretical justification for stacking recursive layers.
- Discuss computational cost relative to shallow RNNs.
- Justify or experiment with fine-tuning pretrained word vectors.
- Strengthen and better connect qualitative analyses to main claims.
- Acknowledge and analyze the modest performance improvement and dataset limitations.
