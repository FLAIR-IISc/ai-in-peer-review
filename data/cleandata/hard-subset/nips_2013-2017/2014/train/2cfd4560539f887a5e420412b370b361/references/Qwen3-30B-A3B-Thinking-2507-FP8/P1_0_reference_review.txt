

# NIPS Peer Review

## Summary

This paper proposes "Deep Recursive Neural Networks" (DRNNs), a novel architecture that combines the concepts of depth in structure (from recursive neural networks) and depth in space (from stacked feedforward networks). The authors argue that while recursive neural networks are "deep in structure" (as deep as the tree structure), they lack the hierarchical representation benefits of traditional deep feedforward networks. To address this, they stack multiple recursive layers on top of each other, creating a deep network that processes information both through the structure (recursively within each layer) and through the layers (spatially across layers).

The paper makes a clear distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space," which is a novel conceptual contribution. The authors evaluate their DRNNs on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), which includes sentiment labels for every node in the parse trees, allowing for richer supervised error signals. They show that DRNNs outperform shallow recursive networks of the same size and previous state-of-the-art models on fine-grained sentiment prediction. The paper also provides qualitative evidence through input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis that different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.

This work builds on the foundational recursive neural network architecture from Socher et al. (2011) and extends it by incorporating the notion of depth in space, which is inspired by recent advances in deep recurrent networks. The paper properly cites and differentiates from related work, including the earlier recursive neural network papers and the more recent deep recurrent network literature.

## Strengths

1. **Novel Architecture**: The core contribution of DRNNs is conceptually sound and well-motivated. The distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" is clear and provides a valuable theoretical framework for understanding how to combine recursive and deep architectures.

2. **Strong Experimental Results**: The paper demonstrates clear empirical advantages of DRNNs over shallow recursive networks and previous state-of-the-art methods on the SST benchmark. The quantitative results are well-presented and the ablation studies on depth and width provide strong evidence for the benefits of the proposed architecture.

3. **Thoughtful Qualitative Analysis**: The input perturbation analysis and nearest neighbor phrase examination provide compelling evidence that different layers capture different aspects of compositionality. This is a significant contribution that goes beyond just reporting accuracy numbers.

4. **Good Experimental Design**: The paper carefully controls for relevant variables (e.g., keeping the total number of parameters constant while varying depth), uses appropriate baselines, and employs standard techniques like pretrained word vectors and dropout regularization.

5. **Clear Writing and Organization**: The paper is well-written, with clear figures that effectively illustrate the key concepts. The methodology is well-explained, and the experimental setup provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: While the empirical results are strong, the paper doesn't provide a theoretical justification for why stacking recursive layers would work better than simply increasing the width of a single layer. The paper states that "deeper networks become narrower" when keeping parameters constant, but doesn't explain why this trade-off is beneficial for compositionality.

2. **Limited Scope of Qualitative Analysis**: The qualitative analysis is focused almost exclusively on sentiment analysis. While this is a good demonstration, it would be stronger to show similar analysis on a different task or dataset to demonstrate the broader applicability of the layered compositionality hypothesis.

3. **Computational Cost Consideration**: The paper doesn't discuss the computational cost of the deeper networks, despite mentioning that they keep the total number of parameters constant. It would be valuable to know whether the improvements come with a reasonable increase in training or inference time.

4. **Misleading Claim About "Same Size"**: The paper states that DRNNs outperform "shallow recursive nets of the same size," but actually compares networks with the same number of parameters, not the same number of layers. This is a subtle but important distinction that could confuse readers.

5. **Limited Discussion of Broader Implications**: While the paper mentions future work on unsupervised tasks, it doesn't deeply explore how DRNNs might be applied to other NLP tasks beyond sentiment analysis, such as semantic role labeling or machine translation.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a clear conceptual contribution to the field of compositional neural networks. The DRNN architecture is novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated with compelling results on a standard benchmark. The qualitative analysis provides valuable insights into how compositionality is processed across layers, which is an important question in compositional language understanding.

The paper meets the high standards of NIPS in terms of technical quality, clarity, and significance. The results represent a meaningful advancement in the state-of-the-art for compositional language processing, and the insights about layered compositionality could influence future work in the field.

## Recommendations for Improvement

1. Clarify the claim about "same size" networks to specify that they mean "same number of parameters" rather than "same number of layers."

2. Add a brief discussion about the computational cost of the deeper networks compared to shallower ones with the same number of parameters.

3. Expand the qualitative analysis to include a brief example from a different task or dataset to strengthen the claim about layered compositionality being a general phenomenon.

4. Include a short theoretical discussion about why depth might be beneficial for compositional tasks, even when keeping the total number of parameters constant.

## Final Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The contributions are significant, the methodology is sound, and the results are compelling. The paper makes a clear advancement in the understanding of how to build deep architectures for compositional language processing, and the insights about layered compositionality are valuable to the field.

The paper is well-written, properly contextualized within the literature, and provides both quantitative and qualitative evidence for its claims. While there are some minor weaknesses in the presentation and scope, these do not detract from the overall quality and significance of the contribution. This work would be valuable for both researchers interested in compositional language models and those studying deep learning architectures more broadly.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Novel architecture that combines depth in structure and depth in space
- Clear empirical improvements over previous state-of-the-art
- Thoughtful qualitative analysis demonstrating layered compositionality
- Well-written, well-organized paper with sufficient detail for reproducibility
- Addresses an important question in compositional language understanding

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- Limited theoretical justification for the depth advantage
- Qualitative analysis restricted to sentiment task
- Misleading phrasing about "same size" networks
- No discussion of computational cost
- Limited exploration of broader applications beyond sentiment analysis

Overall, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses, and the contribution represents a meaningful advancement in the field.
