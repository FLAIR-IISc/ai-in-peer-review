- Summary: Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack recursive layers for parse-tree inputs, improving SST fine-grained sentiment performance and showing layer-wise compositionality.

Strengths:
- Clear technical contribution: DRNN formulation extends recursive networks by adding "depth in space" to "depth in structure."
- Thorough experiments: Comprehensive SST comparisons with strong baselines and controlled depth/width studies.
- Meaningful qualitative analysis: Layer-wise input perturbation and nearest-neighbor analyses show different layers capture different compositional aspects.
- Strong baselines: Beats prior state-of-the-art and reports clear comparative improvements.
- Practical implementation details: Provides sufficient reproducibility details (word vectors, regularization, training).

Weaknesses:
- Limited theoretical justification: Lacks analysis explaining why depth helps for recursive structures.
- Incremental novelty: Stacking layers is standard; distinction from deep recurrent nets could be clearer.
- Limited evaluation scope: Experiments only on SST, limiting generalizability to other NLP tasks.
- Insufficient discussion of limitations: Does not address reliance on provided parse trees or applicability to unstructured input.
- Ambiguous performance explanation: Attribution of gains to layer specialization is qualitative, not quantitatively validated.

Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance:
- Quality: Technically sound and well-executed but reduced by lack of theory and limitations discussion.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized; some technical nuances (e.g., untied leaves/internals) could be clearer.
- Originality: Novel combination of ideas but conceptually incremental.
- Significance: Advances SST SOTA and offers useful insights into compositionality, though impact is task-limited.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

Specific suggestions:
- Expand theoretical justification and links to hierarchical representation learning.
- Discuss potential generalization to other structured NLP tasks.
- Explicitly address limitations of requiring parse trees and possible mitigations.
- Clarify distinction between DRNNs and deep recurrent networks in intro/related work.
- Add ablation (e.g., activation functions across layers) to support design choices.

Final assessment:
- Solid, well-executed contribution with strong qualitative insights; with suggested revisions it would be a strong NIPS paper.
