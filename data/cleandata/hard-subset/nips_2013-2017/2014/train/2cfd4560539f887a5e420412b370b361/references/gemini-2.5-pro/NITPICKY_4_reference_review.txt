### NIPS Review

**Summary**
This paper introduces the Deep Recursive Neural Network, an architecture constructed by stacking multiple layers of recursive neural networks. The motivation draws an analogy from deep recurrent neural networks, which distinguish between depth in time (recurrence) and depth in space (stacking layers). Similarly, this work proposes to combine the structural depth of a standard recursive net with spatial depth by feeding the representations from each node in a tree to a corresponding node in the layer above. The authors also propose an "untied" variant for the first layer that uses different transformations for leaf nodes (words) and internal nodes (phrases). The model is evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank for fine-grained sentiment classification, where it is shown to outperform shallow recursive models with a similar number of parameters and to achieve a new state-of-the-art result, surpassing previous methods like the Recursive Neural Tensor Network (RNTN). The paper concludes with qualitative analyses intended to show that different layers learn hierarchical representations of compositionality.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

The paper's primary strength is the introduction of a novel and intuitive architecture that achieves impressive state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark. The core idea of stacking recursive layers is a natural extension of prior work on both recursive and recurrent networks. The empirical evaluation is strong, with comparisons to a solid set of contemporary baselines, and the experiments comparing deep versus shallow models while controlling for parameter count are well-designed.

However, the paper is undermined by a significant number of minor flaws, methodological ambiguities, and a general lack of rigor in its analysis and presentation.

**Quality:**
The technical quality of the work is mixed. While the model achieves strong results, the claims are not always well-supported.
- The qualitative analyses in Section 3.2 are based on anecdotal evidence (one perturbation example, two nearest-neighbor examples) yet are presented as general findings. For instance, concluding that the second layer "remembers the intensity of the sentiment, rather than direction" based on Figure 3 is highly speculative and overstates the evidence. Such claims require a much more systematic and large-scale analysis to be convincing.
- Key methodological choices lack sufficient justification. The choice of the one-norm for distance measurement in the qualitative analyses is not explained, whereas L2-norm or cosine similarity are more standard for dense vectors. The justification for untying leaf and internal nodes (Section 2.2)—specifically that it makes using ReLUs more "natural"—is hand-wavy and not supported by an ablation study comparing tied versus untied models.
- The contribution of dropout is highlighted by referencing an unpublished preliminary experiment (score of 46.06). A formal ablation study comparing the final model with and without dropout, and against other regularizers, should have been included in the main results table for a rigorous evaluation.

**Clarity:**
The paper is generally readable, but several key details are either unclear or imprecise.
- The description of the dropout implementation is ambiguous. The authors state that "for a single sentence and a layer, we drop the same units of the hidden layer at each node." This suggests a non-standard dropout variant where the same mask is applied to all nodes in a tree for a given layer. This detail is crucial for reproducibility and requires a clearer explanation and justification.
- The update equation for the deep model (Eq. 4) is slightly confusing. The presence of `h(i)` on both sides of the equation, while standard for recursive definitions, could be made clearer to emphasize the bottom-up computation order within each layer.
- The paper suffers from poor presentation quality. The References section is riddled with formatting errors: missing venue and year information, stray commas, and inconsistent formatting. This level of carelessness is concerning for a submission to a premier venue. There are also minor grammatical errors, such as "starts to cause a degrade" in Section 3.2.

**Originality:**
The core architectural idea is novel. While stacking layers is a common theme in deep learning, its application to recursive neural networks in this specific manner—with connections between corresponding nodes across layers—is a new contribution. It represents a thoughtful combination of ideas from deep feedforward, recurrent, and recursive models.

**Significance:**
The work is significant in that it demonstrably advances the state of the art on an important NLP task. The results suggest that adding "spatial" depth to recursive models is a promising direction for learning compositional representations. Other researchers are likely to build upon this idea. However, the significance is tempered by the weak analytical part of the paper. Without a deeper, more rigorous understanding of *why* the model works, the contribution leans more towards an engineering success than a fundamental scientific insight into compositional semantics.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
1.  Presents a novel and well-motivated architecture (Deep Recursive NN).
2.  Achieves a new state-of-the-art result on a standard and challenging benchmark (SST).
3.  The comparison against shallow models of similar size provides convincing evidence for the benefits of depth.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
1.  The qualitative analysis is anecdotal and the conclusions drawn are speculative and overstated.
2.  Key methodological choices (e.g., untying leaves/internals, dropout variant, one-norm distance) are poorly justified and lack proper ablation studies.
3.  The paper exhibits a lack of polish and attention to detail, most notably in the severely misformatted bibliography.
4.  Several technical descriptions lack the precision required for easy reproducibility.

**Recommendation: Reject**

While the paper presents a promising idea and strong empirical results, the accumulation of minor flaws, methodological ambiguities, and overstated claims from weak evidence detracts significantly from its overall quality. The lack of rigor in the analysis and the poor state of the manuscript, particularly the references, suggest a rushed submission. I cannot recommend this paper for acceptance in its current form. The authors are encouraged to strengthen their analysis with more systematic experiments, clarify the methodological details, and thoroughly proofread the manuscript before resubmission.
