- Summary: Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks—stacked tree-structured recursive layers with vertical connections and untied leaf/internal mappings using ReLUs; trains with fixed embeddings, dropout, AdaGrad, and L2 on activations; top-layer output only; achieves SOTA on SST and shows layer-wise compositional differences.

- Relation to prior work: Extends TreeRNNs by adding vertical stacked depth (analogous to stacked RNNs); untying and ReLU/dropout borrow from deep-net best practices; stacking recursive layers with vertical V(i) links is the main incremental novelty.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated architecture combining structural recursion and vertical depth; leverages untied leaves to use ReLU.

- Strength: Strong empirical results with parameter-controlled comparisons and SOTA on 5-way and binary SST without embedding fine-tuning.

- Strength: Solid training recipe (ReLU + dropout + AdaGrad, activation L2, identity-ish init) and practical stability measures.

- Strength: Qualitative analyses provide insight into different layers capturing intensity vs polarity.

- Strength: Simple and reproducible setup using public embeddings and standard optimizers without pretraining.

- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—natural extension of stacked RNNs with no new composition operator or theory.

- Weakness: Comparison fairness concerns—model uses phrase-level supervision while some baselines may not, possibly biasing results.

- Weakness: Limited ablations—insufficient disentangling of contributions from untying, ReLU, dropout, and depth; shallow baseline with full modern recipe is only partially reported.

- Weakness: No reporting of statistical significance or variance across random seeds; results appear from single runs with early stopping.

- Weakness: Missing experimental details—exact parameter counts, model sizes, training time, precise dropout application, and inference scaling are unclear.

- Weakness: Generalization concerns—performance without internal-node supervision is not evaluated.

- Quality: Technically sound and carefully engineered; empirical claims supported by parameter-matched comparisons but potentially confounded by supervision and embedding differences.

- Clarity: Generally well written and organized; would benefit from explicit hyperparameter listings, per-model parameter counts, and exact dropout details.

- Originality: Moderately novel—stacked recursive layers are a straightforward but meaningful extension; untying/ReLU are sensible adaptations.

- Significance: Advances SOTA on a prominent benchmark and provides a stronger tree-model baseline, though broader impact is limited by reliance on parses and phrase supervision.

- Suggestion: Provide systematic ablations isolating untying, ReLU, dropout, and depth, including a shallow model with the same training recipe.

- Suggestion: Report averages and standard deviations over multiple seeds and include significance tests.

- Suggestion: Clarify supervision used by baselines and, if possible, train a sentence-only supervision variant for fairness.

- Suggestion: Report model sizes, parameter counts, training/inference time, learning rates, and exact dropout placement/scaling.

- Suggestion: Explore embedding fine-tuning and compare fixed vs tuned embedding variants for baselines like RNTN/DCNN.

- Suggestion: Consider adding outputs to intermediate layers as an ablation to support claims about layer roles.

- Question: Is dropout applied before or after ReLU, and is inverted-dropout used at test time?

- Question: Are reported baselines trained with phrase-level supervision, and how does that affect comparisons?

- Question: What are the exact parameter counts and hidden sizes (|h|) for each depth reported, and sensitivity of results to |h|?

- Question: What is performance variance across 5–10 random seeds?

- Question: How does the model perform when trained with only sentence-level labels?

- Arguments for acceptance: Simple, well-motivated extension with clear empirical gains, new SOTA on SST, and useful qualitative analysis.

- Arguments against acceptance: Incremental novelty, fairness and completeness of comparisons need strengthening, and limited evidence of generalization beyond SST.

- Recommendation: Weak accept.
