- Proposal: Deep Recursive Neural Networks stack multiple recursive layers over tree-structured composition with untied leaf/internal transforms, ReLU activations, vertical connections, shared-layer dropout, AdaGrad, and activity regularization.
- Empirical claim: Improved results on fine-grained and binary sentiment classification on SST, establishing a new published SOTA and showing different layers capture different compositional aspects.
- Positioning: Bridges recursive tree RNN literature and deep/stacked recurrent networks, importing ReLU/dropout/pretrained embeddings ideas from feedforward nets to tree models.
- Novelty: Stacking recursive layers (depth in space) atop tree-structured depth, plus leaf/internal parameter untie to enable ReLU, is an original integration.
- Strength: Conceptually simple, well-motivated architectural extension that increases representational hierarchy beyond uniform single-layer composition.
- Strength: Sensible engineering/training recipe (pretrained vectors, ReLU, AdaGrad, shared dropout) improving trainability of tree models.
- Strength: Careful within-family experiments controlling parameter budget, showing deeper-but-narrower models can outperform shallow ones with an optimal depth depending on capacity.
- Strength: Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) support the claim that different layers capture different semantic aspects.
- Strength: Strong empirical results on SST supporting the approach's effectiveness.
- Weakness: External baseline fairness — RNTN/MV-RNN baselines lack modern training choices (pretrained embeddings, ReLU, dropout), conflating architecture gains with improved training regimen.
- Weakness: Limited ablation study — multiple factors (untied leaves, activation, dropout, pretrained embeddings, stacking) changed simultaneously with only partial ablations reported.
- Weakness: Statistical robustness missing — single-run results with early stopping and no variance/confidence intervals across seeds.
- Weakness: Depth/width trade-off confound — keeping parameter count fixed makes deeper models narrower; need fixed-width curves and parameter-efficiency plots to isolate depth effects.
- Weakness: Limited scope — evaluation only on SST; generality would be strengthened by additional compositional tasks or settings without phrase-level supervision.
- Weakness: Some design choices lack justification or comparison — top-layer-only outputs vs deep supervision, activity L2 penalty vs gradient clipping, and shared-dropout mask vs independent dropout need ablations.
- Weakness: Computational costs not reported — training time and memory comparisons versus shallow trees and tensor models are missing.
- Quality: Technically sound and reproducible from provided details, but experimental methodology needs stronger ablations and matched baselines.
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized; notation around leaf/internal and layers should be tightened and parameter tying summarized compactly.
- Significance: Moderate to high — useful, adoptable improvement for compositional tree models, but would benefit from stronger baselines and ablations to cement impact.
- Pros for acceptance: Clear principled idea, strong training recipe for tree models, and insightful qualitative layer analyses.
- Cons for acceptance: Ambiguity from baseline unfairness, single-dataset evaluation, limited statistical reporting, and missing runtime analysis.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept; authors should add matched-regimen RNTN/MV-RNN baselines or thorough ablations, report variance across runs, and include a second dataset or further ablations.
