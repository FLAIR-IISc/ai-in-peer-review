### NIPS Review

**Summary**

This paper proposes the Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN), a novel architecture for processing structured linguistic data. The core idea is to extend standard Recursive Neural Networks by stacking multiple recursive layers, creating a distinction between "depth in structure" (inherent to the recursive processing over a tree) and "depth in space" (achieved by stacking layers). This is analogous to the development of deep recurrent networks from shallow ones. The authors hypothesize that this stacking allows for a hierarchy of representations, where each layer can learn different aspects of semantic composition.

The authors introduce a key modification to the standard RNN, "untying" the weights for leaf nodes (words) and internal nodes (phrases), which allows for the use of large pre-trained word vectors and non-saturating activation functions like ReLUs. They evaluate their model on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) for fine-grained sentiment classification. The results demonstrate that Deep RNNs outperform shallow counterparts with a similar number of parameters. Furthermore, the proposed model achieves a new state-of-the-art result on this benchmark, surpassing previous models like the Recursive Neural Tensor Network (RNTN). The paper supports its quantitative results with qualitative analyses, including an input perturbation study and nearest neighbor analysis, which suggest that different layers indeed capture different levels of abstraction, from word-level similarity to syntactic patterns to overall sentiment.

**Evaluation**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution, solid empirical results, and insightful analysis. It addresses an important problem in NLP—learning compositional representations of language—with a well-motivated and elegant architectural extension.

**Quality: High**
The paper is technically sound. The claims are well-supported by rigorous experiments.
*   **Methodology:** The experimental setup is strong. The authors carefully control for the number of parameters when comparing deep versus shallow models, which is crucial for demonstrating the benefit of depth itself, rather than just a larger model. The use of modern techniques (pre-trained word vectors, ReLU activations, dropout, AdaGrad) constitutes a strong baseline and reflects good practice.
*   **Evaluation:** The quantitative evaluation is thorough. The model is compared against a comprehensive set of strong baselines on a standard, challenging dataset. The reported state-of-the-art result is a significant achievement.
*   **Analysis:** A major strength of the paper is that it goes beyond simply reporting performance metrics. The qualitative analyses in sections 3.2 (Input Perturbation and Nearest Neighbor Phrases) provide compelling, intuitive evidence for the central hypothesis that stacked layers learn a hierarchy of representations. This analysis adds significant value and insight. The authors are also honest about the contributions of different components, noting the significant boost from using dropout.

**Clarity: High**
The paper is very well-written and organized.
*   The motivation for Deep RNNs is clearly explained through the analogy with deep recurrent networks and the distinction between depth in structure and depth in space.
*   The methodology section is clear, and the core equation (4) for the Deep RNN is presented directly. The figures, particularly Figure 2 illustrating the weight sharing and tying scheme, are effective and aid understanding.
*   The description of the experimental setup is detailed enough that an expert could reasonably reproduce the work.

**Originality: Good**
The paper presents a novel combination of existing ideas in a new context, leading to a new and effective architecture.
*   The central idea of stacking layers to add depth is, of course, the foundational concept of deep learning. It has also been previously applied to recurrent networks.
*   However, its application to *recursive* neural networks operating on tree structures is novel. The formulation of how information flows both recursively within a layer and sequentially between layers at each node is a non-trivial architectural contribution.
*   The concept of distinguishing "depth in structure" from "depth in space" provides a clear and useful conceptual framework for thinking about this class of models. The "untying" of leaf and internal node transformations is also a simple but clever and well-justified modeling choice that facilitates the use of modern components like ReLUs.

**Significance: High**
The results and ideas presented in this paper are important and likely to be influential.
*   **State-of-the-Art:** The paper advances the state of the art on a widely used benchmark for semantic compositionality. This is a clear and demonstrable contribution.
*   **Generality:** While tested on sentiment analysis, the Deep RNN architecture is general and could be applied to any task involving tree-structured data, such as parsing, logical inference, or code analysis. The insights about hierarchical representation learning are broadly applicable.
*   **Influence:** The work provides a strong impetus for exploring depth in other structured-prediction models. The clear success of this straightforward "stacking" approach encourages similar investigations in other domains where recursive or graph-based models are used. The qualitative analysis techniques also set a good example for how to probe and understand what deep models are learning.

**Strengths (Arguments for acceptance):**
1.  **Novel Architecture:** Proposes a simple, elegant, and well-motivated extension to recursive neural networks by adding depth in space.
2.  **State-of-the-Art Performance:** Achieves SOTA results on the challenging Stanford Sentiment Treebank, outperforming strong, contemporary models.
3.  **Strong Empirical Validation:** The benefit of depth is clearly shown through controlled experiments comparing deep/narrow vs. shallow/wide models with similar parameter counts.
4.  **Insightful Qualitative Analysis:** The input perturbation and nearest neighbor studies provide compelling evidence that stacked layers learn a hierarchy of representations, moving from surface/lexical features to more abstract semantic ones (specifically, sentiment). This significantly strengthens the paper's claims.
5.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, making the concepts easy to understand.

**Weaknesses (Minor points for improvement):**
1.  **Limited Task Scope:** The model is only evaluated on sentiment analysis. While the authors acknowledge this as future work, demonstrating its effectiveness on a more structurally-focused task (e.g., parsing) or a different semantic task (e.g., paraphrase detection) would have made the claims about general compositionality even stronger.
2.  **Incremental Originality:** The core idea of stacking layers is not new to neural networks. While its application here is novel and effective, one could argue it is an incremental step building on the established success of deep models in other areas. However, I believe the successful execution and analysis make this a valuable contribution regardless.

**Conclusion:**

This is an excellent paper that introduces a novel and effective architecture, backs it up with state-of-the-art empirical results, and provides valuable insights through qualitative analysis. The strengths far outweigh the minor weaknesses. It represents a clear step forward in learning compositional representations of language and is a perfect fit for NIPS. I strongly recommend acceptance.
