- Paper proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recursive layers combining "depth in structure" and "depth in space" for compositional language representations.
- DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and show qualitatively that different layers capture different compositional aspects.
- Technical formulation is sound with clear equations and justified design choices (e.g., untied leaves vs internals).
- Experiments are thorough with ablation studies on depth/width and comparisons to strong baselines.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) provide valuable insights beyond accuracy metrics.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility.
- Novelty: stacking recursive layers and the untied leaf/internal variant are useful contributions in the recursive network context.
- Lack of theoretical analysis explaining why stacked layers improve performance weakens the scientific contribution.
- Limited comparison to other deep architectures (e.g., LSTMs, transformers) leaves the position of DRNNs in the broader landscape unclear.
- Computational costs of deeper DRNNs are not addressed, which could hinder practical applicability.
- Related work discussion is incomplete and does not clearly position DRNNs relative to recent deep recurrent models.
- The distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" should be articulated earlier and more clearly.
- Some terms (e.g., "meaning space") are used imprecisely and need clearer definitions.
- Paper should better explain how DRNN depth differs from simply increasing a single-layer RNN’s width.
- Significance: strong empirical and qualitative contributions to compositional semantics, but impact is limited by focus on a single task (sentiment).
- Scope limited to sentiment analysis; applicability to broader NLP tasks (e.g., MT, QA) is not demonstrated.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel within recursive nets, strong results and analysis; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommended revisions: add brief theoretical discussion, expand comparisons to LSTMs/transformers, clarify distinctions from other stacked approaches, and broaden discussion of applicability.
- Specific suggestions: clarify depth distinctions in the introduction (consider a comparison table), include a baseline LSTM/transformer on SST in Section 3.2, expand future-work applications beyond sentiment, and highlight how layer-wise compositional insights inform future hierarchical representation work.
