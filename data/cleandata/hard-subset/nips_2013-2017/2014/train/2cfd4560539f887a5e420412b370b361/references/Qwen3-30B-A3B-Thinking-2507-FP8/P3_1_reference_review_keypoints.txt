- Paper proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs): stack multiple recursive layers to add "depth in space" to tree-structured recursive processing, evaluated on SST with SOTA results.

- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures illustrating the architecture and untied variant.

- Strength: Strong empirical results—improved SST performance (49.5% fine-grained accuracy) over previous recursive models.

- Strength: Meaningful qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor) showing different layers capture different compositional aspects.

- Strength: Appropriate technical choices (rectifier activations, dropout, pretrained word vectors).

- Strength: Good comparisons to several relevant baselines (MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, DCNN).

- Weakness (conceptual): Mischaracterizes recursive networks as inherently "deep in structure"; DRNN is essentially stacked feedforward processing of trees, not a fundamentally new notion of depth.

- Weakness (conceptual): Fails to distinguish DRNN from the simpler alternative of a single recursive layer followed by stacked feedforward layers.

- Weakness (technical): Inadequate comparison to other deep tree-structured architectures (deep tree-LSTMs, deep GNNs on parse trees, deep RNTNs).

- Weakness (technical): Incomplete explanation of the untied leaves/internals variant and its role across stacked layers.

- Weakness (methodological): Lacks ablation studies isolating contributions of stacking vs. untied variant, activations, and dropout.

- Weakness (qualitative): Input perturbation analysis is limited to a single sentence/word, reducing strength of qualitative claims.

- Weakness (claim): Overstates novelty—claims to combine "depth in structure" and "depth in space" misleadingly.

- Suggestion: Reframe conceptual framework to acknowledge DRNN is deep feedforward processing applied per tree layer, not a novel depth combination.

- Suggestion: Add baseline comparing single recursive layer followed by multiple feedforward layers.

- Suggestion: Expand qualitative analysis with more perturbation and nearest-neighbor examples.

- Suggestion: Add ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution to performance gains.

- Suggestion: Compare to relevant deep tree-structured models (deep tree-LSTMs, deep GNNs, deep RNTNs).

- Overall quality score: 7/10 — technically sound with promising results but flawed conceptual framing and missing comparisons.

- Overall clarity score: 8/10 — well-written and clearly organized with clear mathematics and figures.

- Overall originality score: 6/10 — stacking recursive layers is not highly novel; untied variant is modestly new.

- Overall significance score: 7/10 — strong empirical results for sentiment analysis but limited broader conceptual impact.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept — solid empirical contribution but needs clearer conceptual framing and more comprehensive comparisons/ablations.

- Key arguments for acceptance: clear empirical improvements, useful qualitative insights, sound technical choices, and clear presentation.

- Key arguments against acceptance: conceptual confusion about depth, missing comparisons to simpler and deep tree-structured baselines, incomplete ablation and qualitative analyses.
