

This paper proposes "deep recursive neural networks" (DRNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers on top of each other, creating a novel architecture that combines depth in structure (from recursive networks) with depth in space (from stacked layers). The authors argue that while recursive neural networks are "deep in structure" (as deep as the parse tree), they lack the hierarchical representation benefits of standard deep feedforward networks. By stacking recursive layers, they create a model where each layer can learn different aspects of compositionality.

The paper builds on recursive neural networks introduced by Socher et al. (2011), but extends them by stacking recursive layers rather than just increasing the width of a single layer. This is a clear conceptual advance over previous recursive network work, which has primarily focused on shallow architectures. The authors provide a detailed description of their architecture (equation 4), which handles the distinction between leaf nodes (words) and internal nodes (phrases) through weight untying (Section 2.2), and they validate their approach on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) for fine-grained sentiment analysis.

The main strengths of this paper are its novelty and boldness. The core idea of stacking recursive layers is a fresh perspective on how to build hierarchical representations for structured data, and it's not merely an incremental extension of existing work. The authors make a compelling case for the importance of this distinction between depth in structure and depth in space, which is a conceptual leap forward. Their empirical results show that DRNNs outperform shallow recursive networks and previous baselines (including RNTN and Paragraph Vectors), achieving new state-of-the-art results on SST. The qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrases) provide strong evidence that different layers capture different aspects of compositionality, which supports their theoretical claims.

I note that the paper could be clearer about how their approach differs from existing stacked recursive networks. While the authors cite some related work on stacked RNNs (e.g., Hermans & Schrauwen 2013), they don't sufficiently contrast their architecture with those approaches. However, this is a minor issue that doesn't undermine the main contribution.

The paper has some minor technical issues, such as the claim that DRNNs "are constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers" (which is true, but might be confused with stacked RNNs that operate on sequences rather than trees). However, these are not major flaws that would prevent acceptance given the paper's novelty.

The significance of this work is high. The authors have introduced a new architecture with clear theoretical foundations that advances the field of recursive neural networks. Their approach opens up new avenues for research on hierarchical compositionality in language, and the qualitative analysis provides valuable insights into how different layers contribute to the overall representation. This work could inspire future research on deeper recursive architectures for structured data, potentially extending beyond sentiment analysis to other NLP tasks involving structured inputs.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for replication. The experimental setup is sound, with appropriate baselines and careful evaluation on the SST dataset. The authors have also appropriately acknowledged the limitations of their approach (e.g., the need for supervised training, potential for better performance with fine-tuned word vectors).

In summary, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field by introducing a novel architecture that combines two forms of depth (in structure and in space) for recursive neural networks. The work is conceptually bold, empirically validated, and provides important insights into how compositionality is captured across layers. While the approach is not without minor technical limitations, these do not detract from the paper's novelty and significance.

I recommend acceptance of this paper for NIPS. It represents a clear step forward in the understanding and application of recursive neural networks, and it introduces a new direction that other researchers will likely build upon. The novelty of the core idea, combined with strong empirical validation and insightful qualitative analysis, makes this a valuable contribution to the field.
