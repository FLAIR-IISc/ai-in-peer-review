- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs): stack multiple recursive layers to separate structural (tree) depth from spatial (stacked) depth.  
- Within-layer uses standard binary TreeRNN composition; across layers node states feed vertically via layer-to-layer weights.  
- First layer unties leaf vs internal parameters to allow large fixed pretrained embeddings and ReLUs.  
- Uses ReLUs, shared-mask dropout per sentence/layer, AdaGrad, and small L2 on weights/activations to stabilize training.  
- Supervises every node’s representation at the final stacked layer.  
- Reports empirical gains on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), outperforming matched-parameter shallow baselines and claiming SOTA vs MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, DCNN.  
- Qualitative analyses show layer specialization (e.g., intensity vs polarity) and different similarity notions across layers.  
- Novelty: combining structural recursion with stacked depth and untied leaf/internal parametrization for ReLU compatibility.  
- Strength: clear, nontrivial architectural idea that naturally extends TreeRNNs.  
- Strength: sound engineering and sensible training design (untying, ReLUs, dropout, AdaGrad, per-node supervision).  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements under matched parameter budgets and informative qualitative analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation to attribute gains to depth versus ReLU, dropout, or pretraining.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single dataset/task (SST); generality to other tree tasks is untested.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical rigor—no variance across seeds, confidence intervals, or significance testing; external baselines not parameter-normalized.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to stronger gated/recurrent alternatives (LSTM-based trees, residual/skip connections).  
- Weakness: some training choices are ad hoc (shared dropout masks, L2 on activations) and need justification/ablation.  
- Clarity gaps: missing binarization/parsing details, computational complexity, training times, and resource usage.  
- Quality: technically sound formulation and training; pragmatic optimization fixes, but comparisons to alternatives (e.g., gradient clipping) are missing.  
- Requests: provide full quantitative tables with parameter counts, run-time, and stddevs; detail parser/binarization, OOV handling, class weighting, and dropout scheme.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (untied vs tied, ReLU vs tanh, dropout on/off, depth control), try skip/residual connections, evaluate on additional tree tasks, and report multi-run confidence intervals.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, well-motivated idea with empirical gains and useful qualitative insights likely to influence follow-up work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation, incomplete ablations, lack of statistical rigor, and missing comparisons to alternative depth mechanisms.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept contingent on stronger experimental rigor and broader evaluation.
