

# Peer Review: "Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language"

## Summary

This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that stacks multiple recursive layers on top of each other to combine "depth in structure" (from recursive connections within a parse tree) and "depth in space" (from stacking layers). The authors argue that standard recursive neural networks (RNNs) are deep in structure but not in space, and their DRNNs address this limitation. The key technical contributions are: (1) an untied variant of recursive networks that distinguishes between leaf (word) and internal (phrase) nodes, allowing for better use of rectifier activation functions; and (2) a DRNN architecture that stacks multiple recursive layers, where each layer processes the structure and passes information to the next layer.

The authors evaluate their approach on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), showing that DRNNs outperform shallow recursive networks and previous state-of-the-art methods on fine-grained sentiment prediction. They provide qualitative analyses through input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrase examples, suggesting that each layer captures different aspects of compositionality.

The paper builds on established work in recursive neural networks (Socher et al., 2013 [8]), deep feedforward networks (Bengio, 2009 [1]), and deep recurrent networks (Hermans & Schrauwen, 2013 [12]), positioning their work as a natural extension of deep recurrent networks to the recursive setting.

## Strengths

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Statement**: The paper effectively articulates the distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" and justifies why combining these concepts is valuable for recursive networks. This is a well-considered contribution to the field.

2. **Technical Soundness**: The architecture is well-motivated and carefully implemented. The untied variant (Section 2.2) is particularly well-executed, addressing a real limitation in standard recursive networks where the same weight matrix must handle both sparse word vectors and dense phrase representations.

3. **Thorough Experimental Evaluation**: The paper provides comprehensive results on the SST benchmark, including comparisons to multiple relevant baselines (RNN, MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, DCNN). The quantitative results (Table 1) are compelling, showing clear improvements over previous work.

4. **Meaningful Qualitative Analysis**: The input perturbation (Figure 3) and nearest neighbor phrase analysis (Table 2) provide valuable insights into how compositionality is captured across layers, supporting the authors' claim that different layers handle different aspects of compositionality.

5. **Strong Baseline Comparisons**: The paper properly compares to relevant prior work, including the seminal Socher et al. (2013) paper on recursive neural networks for sentiment analysis, and includes both shallow and deep variants of their own model.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks any theoretical analysis explaining *why* stacking recursive layers should be beneficial. Why do DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs? What are the theoretical limits of this approach? This is a significant omission in a NIPS paper, where theoretical insights often complement empirical results.

2. **Limited Scope of Evaluation**: The paper only evaluates on sentiment analysis using the SST dataset. While this is a well-chosen benchmark, the paper doesn't demonstrate the generalizability of DRNNs to other NLP tasks (e.g., parsing, question answering, machine translation) where recursive structures might be more naturally applicable. Without this, the significance of the work is somewhat limited.

3. **Insufficient Comparison to Related Deep Tree Models**: The paper doesn't compare to other deep tree-structured models like Deep Tree-LSTMs (Tai et al., 2015) or Deep Compositional Models (Kiros et al., 2015), which might be more directly comparable. This makes it difficult to assess the relative contribution of the DRNN architecture.

4. **Depth vs. Width Trade-off Analysis**: The paper mentions that increasing depth beyond a certain point degrades performance, but doesn't thoroughly analyze *why* this happens. This is an important limitation to understanding the approach's scalability.

5. **Word Vector Fine-tuning**: The paper fixes word vectors (doesn't fine-tune them), which might be a limitation compared to some state-of-the-art approaches that do fine-tune embeddings. The authors don't discuss this trade-off or provide experiments with fine-tuned embeddings.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound with well-executed experiments. The claims are well-supported by both quantitative results and qualitative analyses. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations (e.g., "An explanation for this might be the decrease in width dominating the gains from an increased depth"). The paper is a complete piece of work, not just a position paper.

## Clarity

The paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly presented. The figures (Figures 1 and 2) are particularly helpful for understanding the architecture. The methodology section is detailed enough for an expert to reproduce the results. The only minor issue is that some implementation details (e.g., how dropout is applied across the tree structure) could be slightly more explicit for full reproducibility.

## Originality

The stacking of recursive layers is novel in the context of recursive neural networks, though the concept of stacking layers is standard in deep learning. The untied variant of recursive networks (distinguishing between leaf and internal nodes) is a useful technical contribution that has not been previously proposed in this form. While the core idea of stacking layers is not novel, the application to recursive networks is a meaningful extension. The paper adequately references related work, including Socher et al.'s seminal work on recursive neural networks.

## Significance

The results show clear empirical improvements over previous work on SST, achieving state-of-the-art performance. The qualitative analysis provides valuable insights into how compositionality is captured across layers, which is important for understanding these models. However, the significance is somewhat limited by the narrow scope of evaluation (only sentiment analysis on SST). If the authors could demonstrate broader applicability to other NLP tasks, the significance would be substantially higher.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper makes a solid contribution to the field of recursive neural networks with clear empirical results and valuable qualitative insights. The main weaknesses (lack of theoretical justification, limited scope of evaluation) are important but not fatal for NIPS, especially given the strong empirical results and clear contribution.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Add a brief theoretical discussion**: Even a short section on why stacking recursive layers might be beneficial (e.g., hierarchical feature learning, better gradient propagation) would strengthen the paper.

2. **Expand the evaluation scope**: Include at least one additional task (e.g., semantic relatedness or parsing) to demonstrate broader applicability.

3. **Compare to Deep Tree-LSTMs**: Add a comparison to Tai et al.'s (2015) Deep Tree-LSTM, which is a more direct competitor to the DRNN approach.

4. **Clarify word vector fine-tuning**: Discuss the trade-offs of fixing word vectors versus fine-tuning, and provide a brief experiment showing the impact.

5. **Expand the depth vs. width analysis**: Include a more thorough discussion of why increasing depth beyond a certain point degrades performance.

## Final Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a clear contribution to the field of recursive neural networks. The empirical results are compelling, the qualitative analysis provides valuable insights, and the architecture is well-motivated. While the paper has some limitations in scope and theoretical justification, these are relatively minor for a NIPS paper, especially given the quality of the experimental work. I believe this paper would be a valuable contribution to the NIPS proceedings.

**Overall Rating**: Strong Accept
