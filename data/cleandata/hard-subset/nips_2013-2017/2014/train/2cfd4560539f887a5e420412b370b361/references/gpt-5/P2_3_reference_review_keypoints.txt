- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (Deep RvNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers over parse trees, separating “depth in structure” and “depth in space.”
- Within each layer parents are computed recursively from children; across layers node-wise feedforward connections pass representations upward.
- Unties leaf and internal-node parameters and uses ReLUs, dropout, and AdaGrad for training.
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) with labels at all tree nodes; reports state-of-the-art on fine-grained and binary sentiment.
- Deeper (narrower) stacks outperform shallow RvNNs under a matched-parameter budget.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) indicate layer-wise specialization in compositionality (e.g., intensity vs polarity).
- Builds on prior recursive nets and deep recurrent ideas; novelty is stacking recursive layers rather than a new composition operator.
- Strength: clear, simple architectural idea with sensible untying to enable ReLUs.
- Strength: solid empirical signal with matched-parameter comparisons and reproducible training choices.
- Strength: insightful qualitative analyses supporting the depth hypothesis.
- Weakness: incremental novelty; gains may derive partly from training refinements (ReLU, dropout, untied params) rather than depth alone.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single dataset (SST), reducing claims about generality and compositionality.
- Weakness: lacks reporting of variance across seeds and statistical significance for SOTA claim.
- Weakness: baseline fairness concerns—fixed embeddings vs possible fine-tuning in prior work; stronger reimplementations needed.
- Weakness: design choices under-explored (multi-layer supervision, L2 on activations, gradient clipping, depth/width trade-offs).
- Weakness: practicality concerns—assumes gold binarized parses and omits sensitivity to parse errors, computational cost, and training time.
- Quality: technically sound and coherent, but causal attribution to depth needs stronger ablations and statistical reporting.
- Clarity: well written and organized, though missing exact parameter counts, training time, and seed variance; minor typos present.
- Originality: moderate—natural extension of deep RNN ideas with practical refinements; qualitative probing is a nice addition.
- Significance: moderate—useful SST improvements but impact is incremental without broader validation.
- Suggestions: provide ablations isolating depth from ReLU/dropout/untied params, report mean/std and significance, evaluate additional datasets, compare supervision at all layers, and report parameter/time/memory and parser sensitivity.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, well-motivated architecture with consistent gains and clear qualitative evidence.
- Cons for acceptance: incremental novelty, single-dataset evaluation, limited statistical rigor, and missing computational/robustness analyses.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept; strengthen ablations and statistical reporting to justify acceptance.
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
