- Paper proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacking multiple recursive layers to capture hierarchical compositionality in language.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and shows state-of-the-art performance vs. shallow RNNs and other baselines.  
- Methodology is detailed and reproducible, including untying leaf/internal nodes, dropout, L2, and AdaGrad.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) indicate different layers learn different aspects of compositionality.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, making the contributions easy to follow.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset/task (SST), so generalizability to other NLP tasks is untested.  
- Paper lacks a thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis beyond dropout tuning.  
- Missing theoretical justification for why stacking recursive layers improves representational capacity.  
- Insufficient discussion connecting DRNNs to broader deep learning ideas (e.g., residuals, attention).  
- Quality score 8/10: technically sound but held back by limited scope and theory.  
- Clarity score 9/10: very clear exposition and helpful figures.  
- Originality score 7/10: novel stacking idea but builds on existing RNN/deep learning work.  
- Significance score 8/10: promising results and potential impact, but broader evaluation needed.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on more datasets/tasks, provide hyperparameter study, add theoretical discussion of representational benefits, and relate DRNNs to other deep learning techniques.
