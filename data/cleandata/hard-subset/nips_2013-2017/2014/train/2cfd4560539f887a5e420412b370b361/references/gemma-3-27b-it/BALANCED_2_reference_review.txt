This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture extending Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers. The motivation stems from recent advances in deep learning, particularly the successful application of stacking in recurrent networks (Deep RNNs), and the desire to combine “depth in structure” (RNNs) with “depth in space” (deep feedforward nets). The authors apply DRNNs to fine-grained sentiment analysis on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating performance improvements over shallow RNNs and existing state-of-the-art methods. The paper also includes insightful qualitative analyses exploring how different layers capture compositionality and similarity.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The idea of stacking recursive layers to create DRNNs is a natural and potentially powerful extension of existing RNN architectures. It logically extends the concept of depth explored in Deep RNNs to the structural domain.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The authors demonstrate consistent performance gains on the SST dataset, achieving new state-of-the-art results. The ablation studies clearly show the benefits of increased depth.
* **Thorough Experimental Setup:** The paper presents a well-designed experimental setup, including a comparison to a strong set of baselines and careful attention to regularization techniques (dropout, L2 penalty) and hyperparameter tuning.
* **Qualitative Analysis:** The input perturbation and nearest neighbor analyses provide valuable insights into the behavior of the DRNNs, suggesting that different layers learn different aspects of compositionality and semantic similarity. These analyses go beyond simply reporting numbers and help to understand *why* the model works.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ ideas and understand the experimental results. The figures are helpful in illustrating the architecture and experimental setup.
* **Solid Theoretical Foundation:** The paper builds upon established concepts in RNNs and deep learning, providing a clear connection to related work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** While the results on SST are strong, the evaluation is limited to a single task and dataset. It would be beneficial to see how the DRNNs perform on other tasks that involve structured inputs, such as parsing or semantic role labeling.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The authors mention tuning dropout rates, but a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and the strategies used for optimization would be helpful.  How does performance change with varying numbers of hidden units in each layer?
* **Computational Cost:** Stacking recursive layers likely increases computational cost compared to shallow RNNs. The paper does not explicitly discuss the computational trade-offs. A brief analysis of training time and memory requirements would be valuable.
* **Untying Leaves and Internals Justification:** While the untying of leaves and internals improves performance, the reasoning presented – related to rectifier activations and dense vs. sparse vectors – feels somewhat superficial. A more rigorous justification, perhaps with a theoretical analysis or additional experiments, would be stronger.
* **Layer-wise Analysis Limitations:** The input perturbation analysis is interesting, but its scope is limited to a single sentence and a small set of word replacements. A more systematic analysis, perhaps involving a larger set of examples and different types of perturbations, would provide more convincing evidence.
* **Connection to Tensor Networks:** The paper compares to RNTNs, but doesn't thoroughly discuss how DRNNs differ conceptually.  Both approaches aim to capture compositional semantics, but through different mechanisms.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture (DRNNs) that extends existing work in a logical and promising direction.
* The empirical results are strong, demonstrating significant performance improvements on a challenging task (SST).
* The qualitative analyses provide valuable insights into the behavior of the DRNNs and help to understand *why* they work.
* The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and builds upon a solid theoretical foundation.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The evaluation is limited to a single task and dataset.
* The computational cost of DRNNs is not fully addressed.
* The justification for the untying of leaves and internals could be strengthened.
* The layer-wise analysis, while insightful, is limited in scope.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning and natural language processing. The DRNN architecture is a novel and promising extension of existing RNNs, and the empirical results are compelling. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not significantly detract from the overall quality of the work.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses related to the scope of evaluation, computational cost, and justification for the untying of leaves and internals. A more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity would also be beneficial.  Expanding the layer-wise analysis would further strengthen the paper.
