## Peer Review: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs), extending traditional Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers. The motivation stems from the success of deep architectures in other domains (e.g., deep recurrent networks) and aims to capture hierarchical compositional structure in language more effectively. The authors apply DRNNs to fine-grained sentiment detection on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating improved performance compared to shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and other state-of-the-art methods.  They also provide qualitative analysis suggesting different layers learn different aspects of compositionality and similarity.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds directly on the established field of Recursive Neural Networks (Socher et al., 2013, 2014).  It appropriately acknowledges the evolution from RNNs to deep RNNs in the temporal domain (Schmidhuber, 1992; El Hihi & Bengio, 2014; Hermans & Schrauwen, 2014) and draws a parallel to extend depth into the structural dimension. The comparison to existing SST benchmarks (Socher et al., 2013; Le et al., 2014; Kalchbrenner et al., 2014) is also well-placed, providing a clear context for the contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The idea of stacking recursive layers to create deep structural representations is a logical and promising extension of existing RNN architectures. While the concept of depth is well-established in other areas of deep learning, applying it to recursive networks in this way is a worthwhile contribution.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The DRNNs demonstrably outperform previous state-of-the-art methods on the SST dataset, providing strong evidence of their effectiveness. The ablation studies comparing varying depths and widths are informative.
* **Thorough Experimental Setup:** The authors provide a detailed description of their experimental setup, including hyperparameters, regularization techniques (dropout and L2 regularization), and word vector initialization.  This allows for reproducibility.
* **Qualitative Analysis:** The input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrase experiments provide valuable insights into what each layer of the DRNN is learning. This goes beyond simple performance metrics and helps build intuition about the model's behavior.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly articulated, and the methodology is explained in a reasonably accessible manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope:** The evaluation is restricted to a single dataset (SST) and a single task (fine-grained sentiment analysis). While SST is a standard benchmark, it would be beneficial to see results on other datasets or tasks to assess the generalizability of the approach.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions tuning the dropout rate, but the sensitivity of the model to other hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, L2 regularization strength, layer sizes) is not extensively explored. More thorough hyperparameter analysis would strengthen the claims.
* **Theoretical Justification:** While the motivation for DRNNs is intuitively sound, the paper lacks a strong theoretical justification for *why* stacking recursive layers should lead to improved performance. A more detailed analysis of the representational capacity of DRNNs compared to shallow RNNs would be valuable.
* **Limited Error Analysis:**  A more detailed error analysis, identifying specific cases where the DRNNs fail, could provide further insights into their limitations and potential areas for improvement.
* **Untying Leaves and Internals:** While the authors justify untying leaves and internals, its necessity and impact deserve a more robust exploration. A comparison between tied and untied variants with equivalent model size would be beneficial.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and potentially significant extension to recursive neural networks.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark dataset.
* The qualitative analysis provides valuable insights into the learned representations.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear and detailed description of the methodology and experiments.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset and task.
* The paper lacks a strong theoretical justification for the proposed approach.
* The hyperparameter sensitivity is not thoroughly explored.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid contribution to the field of recursive neural networks.  The proposed Deep Recursive Neural Networks show promising results on the SST dataset, and the qualitative analysis provides valuable insights.  While the limitations regarding scope, theoretical justification, and hyperparameter sensitivity should be addressed, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  **Discuss limitations:** Acknowledge the limited scope of the evaluation and suggest future directions for exploring the generalizability of the DRNNs to other datasets and tasks.
2.  **Add a brief discussion about computational complexity:** Compare the computational requirements (training time, memory usage) of DRNNs with those of shallow RNNs.
3. **Consider a table comparing performance with and without the "untied leaves and internals" approach.** This will help show the benefit of this design choice.
