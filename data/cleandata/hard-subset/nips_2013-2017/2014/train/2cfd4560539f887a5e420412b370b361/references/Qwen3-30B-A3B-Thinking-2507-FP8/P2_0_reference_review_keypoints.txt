- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to gain hierarchical (spatial) depth in addition to tree-structured (structural) depth.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs and previous state-of-the-art on fine-grained and binary sentiment classification.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) suggest different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Well-motivated, technically sound formulation with clear equations and a useful distinction between structure-depth and space-depth.  
- Strong experimental results on a standard benchmark with multiple baselines, demonstrating new state-of-the-art performance.  
- Insightful qualitative experiments that support claims about layer-wise compositional processing.  
- Clear writing, logical organization, and effective figures illustrating concepts and results.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations such as performance degradation when networks become too deep and discuss possible causes.  
- No ablation studies isolating the effect of depth versus other factors (e.g., rectifier activations from untied leaves).  
- Experiments are limited to SST, reducing evidence for broader applicability across NLP tasks.  
- Missing analysis of computational costs (training time, memory) for deeper versus shallower models.  
- Explanation for performance degradation with excessive depth is superficial and lacks detailed analysis.  
- Limited direct comparison to deep recurrent/stacked recurrent networks to clarify relative advantages.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid contribution with strong results and valuable qualitative insights into compositionality.  
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper meets NIPS standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Suggestion: Add ablation studies to isolate depth’s contribution from other design choices.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate DRNNs on at least one additional NLP task to demonstrate generality.  
- Suggestion: Report computational cost and compare training/inference times with shallower models.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis explaining why excessive depth degrades performance (e.g., width vs. depth trade-offs).  
- Suggestion: Include direct comparisons to deep recurrent networks (stacked recurrent layers) to position DRNNs relative to existing approaches.
