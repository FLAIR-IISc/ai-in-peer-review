## Review of "Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs), extending traditional Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) by stacking multiple recursive layers. The motivation is to capture hierarchical compositionality in language, drawing parallels to the advancements in deep recurrent networks. The authors apply DRNNs to fine-grained sentiment detection on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating performance improvements over shallow RNNs, multiplicative RNNs, and achieving new state-of-the-art results. The paper also provides qualitative analysis through input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrase analysis, suggesting that each layer learns different aspects of compositional meaning.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds directly upon the foundation of RNNs [6, 7, 8] and the concept of depth in neural networks, inspired by deep recurrent networks [10, 11, 12]. It acknowledges and improves upon existing approaches for sentiment analysis on SST [8, 16, 17, 18]. The paper appropriately positions itself within the broader context of deep learning for NLP, citing relevant work on word embeddings [2, 3, 4] and general deep architectures [1].

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The concept of stacking recursive layers to create DRNNs is a reasonable and potentially impactful extension to existing RNN architectures. While the idea of depth is common, applying it specifically to the structural dimension of recursive networks is a valuable contribution.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides convincing quantitative results on SST, demonstrating that DRNNs outperform existing baselines, including state-of-the-art methods.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors perform not only quantitative evaluation but also qualitative analysis through input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis, providing insights into the behavior of different layers.
* **Clear Methodology:** The paper provides a clear and well-defined methodology, with detailed explanations of the architecture, training procedure, and hyperparameter settings. The untying of leaf and internal nodes is a sensible modification, well-motivated and clearly explained.
* **Good Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to understand the proposed approach and its evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The experiments are limited to a single dataset (SST) and a single task (fine-grained sentiment analysis). While achieving SOTA on SST is a positive result, it’s crucial to assess the generalizability of the DRNNs to other tasks and datasets.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions tuning the dropout rate, but lacks details regarding the sensitivity of performance to other hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, layer sizes). A more thorough hyperparameter analysis would strengthen the findings.
* **Qualitative Analysis Could be Deeper:** The qualitative analysis, while insightful, feels somewhat superficial. For example, the input perturbation results are presented without a more comprehensive discussion of *why* certain layers respond in specific ways. 
* **Justification for Layer Depth:** The paper notes that performance degrades with increasing depth beyond a certain point. While this is acknowledged, the reasons for this aren't fully explored. Is it due to vanishing gradients, overfitting, or limitations in the expressiveness of the architecture?
* **Computational Cost:** The paper doesn't discuss the computational cost of training DRNNs compared to shallower models.  This is relevant given the increased complexity.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated extension to recursive neural networks.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant improvements on a challenging benchmark.
* The qualitative analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed architecture.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset and task.
* The hyperparameter analysis is not comprehensive.
* The qualitative analysis could be more in-depth.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning for NLP. The DRNN architecture is a promising approach for capturing compositional semantics, and the experimental results are compelling. While the limitations regarding scope of evaluation and hyperparameter analysis are valid concerns, they do not outweigh the strengths of the work. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** 

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

*   Discuss the computational cost of DRNNs.
*   Provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of hyperparameters on performance.
*   Expand the qualitative analysis with a more thorough discussion of the observed behavior of different layers, and why the performance degrades with extreme depth.
*   Briefly discuss potential avenues for future work extending this research to other datasets or tasks.
