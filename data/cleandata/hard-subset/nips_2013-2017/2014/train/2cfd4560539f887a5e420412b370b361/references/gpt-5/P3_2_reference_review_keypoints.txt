- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (stacked tree composition layers with untied leaf/internal parameters, ReLUs, and pretrained embeddings) and shows SOTA on SST fine-grained sentiment.
- Within-layer composition uses shared bottom-up recursive weights on binary parse trees; cross-layer feedforward connections pass node states upward; supervision only at top layer.
- Training uses AdaGrad, dropout with shared masks per sentence/layer, L2 weight and activation penalties; many training details are provided aiding reproducibility.
- Builds on prior tree-structured and stacked RNN work and contrasts with multiplicative/tensor and matrix-vector composition approaches.
- Uses sensible integration of pretrained word2vec embeddings and untied leaf/internal parameters to enable ReLU activations at leaves.
- Empirical finding: increasing depth (under fixed parameter budget) improves tree model performance on SST compared to wider/shallow models.
- Qualitative analyses (word replacement along paths, nearest neighbors across layers) suggest layer specialization for lexical/syntactic vs. sentiment features.
- Strength: clear, simple architectural idea that yields practical gains without fine-tuning embeddings.
- Strength: careful regularization and training choices produce stable optimization with ReLUs.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a single dataset/task (SST), weakening generality claims about compositionality.
- Weakness: comparisons to baselines may be unfair—differences in embedding tuning and parameter counts are not matched or reported.
- Weakness: incomplete ablations (tied vs. untied, ReLU vs. tanh, dropout vs. L2, supervision schemes, connecting outputs across layers) leave the source of gains ambiguous.
- Weakness: stability measures (activation L2 penalty, shared dropout masks) seem ad hoc and lack comparison to alternatives like gradient clipping or gated units.
- Weakness: qualitative results are anecdotal without aggregate statistics or human evaluation.
- Weakness: notation inconsistencies and missing parameter/dimensionality details hinder clarity; Eq. (4) doesn't reflect stated distinctions.
- Weakness: no runtime/memory analysis or training time comparisons; no variance estimates or statistical significance tests.
- Suggests missing comparisons to gated tree models (e.g., Tree-LSTM) as natural baselines.
- Requests for authors: provide ablations isolating depth vs. embeddings/dropout/ReLUs, fine-tuning experiments, parameter counts and wall-clock times, supervision/connectivity variants, gradient clipping tests, and sensitivity to dropout mask sharing.
- Pros for acceptance: simple effective extension, new SST SOTA, useful qualitative insights, and reproducible training details.
- Cons for acceptance: narrow evaluation, incomplete ablations and baseline fairness concerns, lack of gated-model comparisons, and missing statistical/runtime analyses.
- Recommendation: weak accept — promising and effective but needs broader evaluation and stronger ablations.
- Reviewer confidence: high.
