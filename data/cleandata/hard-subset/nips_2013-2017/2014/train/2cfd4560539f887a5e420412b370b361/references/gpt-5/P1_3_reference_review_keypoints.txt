- Proposal: Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack recursive layers with vertical V connections to separate tree recursion depth from spatial depth.  
- Leaf untying and ReLU recipe: Unties leaf word vectors from internal phrase vectors, uses ReLUs, and allows fixed high-dim pretrained embeddings without quadratic parameter blowup.  
- Training details: Uses AdaGrad, shared-mask dropout per layer/sentence, small L2 on weights/activations, careful initialization, and early stopping.  
- Empirical result: On SST, deeper-and-narrower DRNNs outperform shallow recursive nets at matched parameter budgets and achieve state-of-the-art 5-way and binary sentiment accuracy.  
- Qualitative analysis: Layer-wise perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analysis suggest different layers capture lexical dominance, syntactic type, and sentiment polarity/intensity.  
- Strength — clarity and simplicity: Clear, simple architectural idea (vertical connections) that is easy to understand and implement.  
- Strength — practical recipe: Provides a thorough, practical training recipe (untying, ReLU, fixed embeddings, dropout, AdaGrad).  
- Strength — empirical rigor: Fair protocol on SST (phrase-level supervision, standard split) with consistent gains and SOTA claims.  
- Strength — interpretability: Useful qualitative analyses that illuminate layer-specific functions.  
- Strength — exposition: Well written with specified training details.  
- Weakness — limited ablations: No systematic isolation of key design choices (untying, ReLU vs tanh, fixing vs fine-tuning embeddings, output-only-top-layer).  
- Weakness — statistical robustness: No variance across random seeds or significance testing; results may vary at the 1–2% level.  
- Weakness — fairness of baselines: Unclear whether external baselines used phrase-level supervision or pretrained embeddings and whether parameter budgets were matched.  
- Weakness — limited generality: Evaluation only on SST; no experiments on other structured NLP tasks (parsing, entailment) to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Weakness — optimization/stability: Uses L2 on activations to prevent explosions but does not explore standard alternatives (gradient clipping, norm constraints, normalization); final-layer-only supervision may hinder gradient flow in other settings.  
- Quality: Technically sound and reproducible; main gap is absence of systematic ablation to identify essential design choices.  
- Clarity suggestions: Unify notation for x vs h, provide complexity analysis and training time, add pseudocode for backprop-through-structure-and-space, and report parameter counts and exact hyperparameters.  
- Originality: Moderately novel—stacking known from sequential RNNs but natural and useful extension to tree-structured nets with practical untying/ReLU recipe.  
- Significance: Empirically meaningful with consistent gains and SOTA on a key benchmark; methodological influence likely, but broader impact limited by single-dataset focus.  
- Key questions for authors: Provide ablations (stacking w/out untying/ReLU; untying with tanh; fixed vs fine-tuned embeddings; dropout effects; supervision to all layers), sensitivity to shared dropout mask, variance across seeds, parameter matching details vs RNTN/MV-RNN, root-only results, and experiments on other datasets/tasks.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, well-motivated extension with consistent gains, SOTA results, and useful practical guidance.  
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete ablation/robustness analysis, single-task evaluation, and ambiguity in baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (high reviewer confidence).
