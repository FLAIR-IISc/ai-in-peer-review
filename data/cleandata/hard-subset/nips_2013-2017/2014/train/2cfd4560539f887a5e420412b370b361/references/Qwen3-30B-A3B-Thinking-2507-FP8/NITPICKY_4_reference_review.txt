

# Review of "Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language"

## Summary
This paper proposes "deep recursive neural networks" (DRNNs) that stack multiple recursive layers to address compositionality in language. The authors argue that while recursive neural networks (RNNs) are "deep in structure," they lack the hierarchical representation benefits of stacked feedforward networks. By stacking recursive layers, they create a model that combines structural processing with depth in space. The authors evaluate DRNNs on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), showing improvements over shallow recursive networks and previous state-of-the-art methods.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an interesting gap in recursive neural network architectures by introducing depth in space alongside depth in structure. The qualitative analysis of different layers capturing different aspects of compositionality (input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrases) provides valuable insights into the model's behavior. The experimental results are generally convincing, with DRNNs outperforming shallow RNNs of comparable parameter count and previous state-of-the-art methods.

## Weaknesses

1. **Terminology Confusion**: The paper consistently uses "RNN" to refer to both Recurrent Neural Networks and Recursive Neural Networks, creating significant confusion. This is particularly problematic in the introduction where the authors distinguish between "deep recurrent networks" and "deep recursive neural networks." This terminology inconsistency should be resolved immediately.

2. **Mathematical Notation**: The paper contains multiple inconsistent and confusing notations:
   - Equation (1) uses "xη = f(WLxl(η) +WRxr(η) + b)" but doesn't specify dimensions or how the weight matrices operate on the vectors.
   - Equation (3) uses "W l(η) L" which is confusing notation (should be W^{l(η)}_L or similar).
   - Equation (4) uses "h(i)η" which should be "h^{(i)}_η" to indicate layer index.
   - The paper uses "RNN" for Recursive Neural Networks in section 2.1 but "recursive neural networks" elsewhere, creating inconsistency.

3. **Figure References**: The paper references "Figure 1" and "Figure 2" multiple times but provides no actual figures. This is unacceptable for a NIPS submission, as figures are essential for understanding the architecture.

4. **Experimental Details**: Several critical experimental details are missing:
   - The specific dropout rate used in the best model isn't stated (only that it was tuned over {0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5}).
   - The exact word vectors used (GloVe, word2vec, etc.) and training corpus aren't specified.
   - The paper states "we keep the overall number of parameters constant" but doesn't explain how this is achieved mathematically.
   - The claim that "deeper networks become narrower" is confusing since same number of hidden units across layers would increase parameter count with depth.

5. **Formatting Issues**:
   - The paper contains typos ("evaulate" in section 4).
   - The reference list is inconsistent (some entries have "In Proceedings of" while others don't).
   - The paper cites [13] for "alleviating vanishing gradients" but doesn't explain how DRNNs specifically address this compared to other deep architectures.

6. **Methodological Clarity**: The untying of leaves and internals (section 2.2) is presented as beneficial but the justification is weak. The claim that "we can use large pretrained word vectors and a small number of hidden units without a quadratic dependence" is unclear and not supported with mathematical explanation.

7. **Comparison to Prior Work**: The paper compares to "shallow RNN" but doesn't clearly establish how this differs from their own shallow RNN implementation. The baseline comparison lacks a clear explanation of why their shallow RNN outperforms previous work.

## Recommendation
While the paper presents an interesting architectural extension to recursive neural networks and provides some convincing empirical results, the technical presentation contains too many inconsistencies and omissions to be considered acceptable for NIPS. The terminology confusion between "RNN" for Recurrent vs. Recursive Neural Networks is particularly problematic, as is the lack of figures to illustrate the proposed architecture. The mathematical notation is inconsistent and sometimes confusing, and critical experimental details are missing.

The paper's claims about "depth in space" and the benefits of stacking recursive layers are not sufficiently clarified, and the paper would benefit from a more careful explanation of how the proposed architecture differs from existing stacked recurrent or recursive approaches. The qualitative analysis is promising but lacks sufficient detail to fully evaluate.

Given the paper's significant technical inconsistencies and the fact that the presentation does not meet the high standards expected at NIPS, I recommend rejection. The core idea has potential but the current presentation is insufficiently rigorous and clear for publication.
