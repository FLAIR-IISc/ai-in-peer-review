

# NIPS Peer Review: "Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language"

## Summary

This paper proposes "Deep Recursive Neural Networks" (DRNNs), which stack multiple recursive layers on top of each other to combine the concepts of "depth in structure" (from recursive neural networks) and "depth in space" (from stacked feedforward networks). The authors argue that while traditional recursive neural networks are "deep in structure" (processing the tree depth), they lack the hierarchical representation benefits of deep feedforward networks. Their DRNN architecture processes the tree structure within each layer and passes representations between layers. The paper applies this to sentiment analysis on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), demonstrating state-of-the-art performance compared to shallow recursive networks and other baselines.

The paper builds on several key lines of work: recursive neural networks (RNNs) [6, 7, 8], deep recurrent networks [10-12], and the untied variant of recursive networks [2]. However, the paper's conceptual framework for "depth in structure" versus "depth in space" appears to conflate two distinct concepts, as traditional recursive networks aren't truly "deep" in the same way as stacked feedforward networks.

## Strengths

1. **Clear and well-structured presentation**: The paper is well-organized with clear figures (Figures 1 and 2) that effectively illustrate the proposed architecture and the untied variant of recursive networks.

2. **Strong empirical results**: The paper demonstrates clear improvements over previous recursive network approaches on the SST benchmark. The state-of-the-art results (49.5% fine-grained accuracy) are significant and well-supported by thorough experiments.

3. **Meaningful qualitative analysis**: The input perturbation and nearest neighbor analyses provide valuable insights into how different layers capture different aspects of compositionality. This goes beyond simple performance metrics and offers deeper understanding of the model's behavior.

4. **Appropriate technical choices**: The use of rectifier activations, dropout, and pretrained word vectors is well-justified and aligns with current best practices in deep learning.

5. **Good comparison to relevant baselines**: The paper compares against multiple relevant baselines (including MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, and DCNN), providing a comprehensive evaluation.

## Weaknesses

### Conceptual Issues

1. **Misunderstanding of "depth" in recursive networks**: The paper's core premise that recursive networks are "deep in structure" is conceptually flawed. Traditional recursive networks process tree structures through recursive application of a single layer, so they are not "deep" in the same way as stacked feedforward networks. A recursive network with a tree depth of d is still a single-layer network that processes the tree structure. The authors' "deep recursive" network is simply a deep feedforward network where each layer processes the tree structure, not a fundamentally new concept. This conceptual confusion undermines the paper's main contribution.

2. **Lack of distinction from simpler alternatives**: The paper doesn't adequately distinguish their approach from the much simpler alternative of stacking feedforward layers on top of the output of a single recursive network (i.e., having a single recursive layer followed by multiple feedforward layers). This simpler architecture would achieve the same "depth in space" benefit without the additional complexity of recursive layers. The paper should have included experiments comparing to this baseline.

### Technical and Methodological Issues

3. **Inadequate comparison to relevant deep tree-structured architectures**: The paper fails to compare to other deep architectures designed specifically for tree-structured data, such as:
   - Deep versions of tree-LSTMs [24]
   - Deep graph neural networks (GNNs) applied to parse trees
   - Deep Recursive Neural Tensor Networks (RNTN)

   Without these comparisons, it's unclear whether the proposed DRNN offers advantages over existing methods for processing tree structures.

4. **Incomplete explanation of the untied variant**: The paper introduces "untied leaves and internals" as a key modification, but the explanation in Section 2.2 doesn't sufficiently address why this is necessary for the stacked architecture. The explanation focuses on the first layer, but the paper doesn't clarify how this affects the stacking mechanism.

5. **Lack of ablation studies**: The paper doesn't perform thorough ablation studies to isolate the contributions of each component (stacking, untied variant, rectifier activation, dropout). For example, it's unclear how much of the performance gain comes from stacking versus the other modifications.

6. **Qualitative analysis limitations**: The input perturbation example is limited to a single sentence and word. More examples would strengthen the qualitative claims about different layers capturing different aspects of compositionality.

7. **Overstated significance**: The paper claims their work "combines depth in structure and depth in space," but this is misleading. Their model doesn't truly combine these concepts in a novel way; it simply stacks recursive layers, which is a straightforward extension rather than a meaningful combination.

## Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Clarify the conceptual framework**: The authors should reframe their contribution to acknowledge that their model is essentially a deep feedforward network with recursive processing within each layer, rather than a novel combination of "depth in structure" and "depth in space."

2. **Add comparison to simpler baseline**: Include experiments comparing to the architecture of a single recursive layer followed by multiple feedforward layers (not recursive layers), which would be the most direct alternative.

3. **Expand qualitative analysis**: Provide more examples of input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis to better support the claim that different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.

4. **Add ablation studies**: Perform ablation studies to isolate the contribution of stacking versus other components (untied variant, rectifier activation, dropout).

5. **Compare to relevant deep tree-structured architectures**: Add comparisons to deep tree-LSTMs and other deep architectures designed for tree structures.

## Overall Assessment

### Quality: 7/10
The paper is technically sound and the experiments are well-conducted. The results are promising, but the conceptual framework is flawed, and the paper doesn't adequately compare to the most relevant baselines.

### Clarity: 8/10
The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with helpful figures. The mathematical formulations are clear.

### Originality: 6/10
The core idea of stacking recursive layers is not novel. While the untied variant is reasonable, it's not particularly innovative, and the paper fails to distinguish their approach from simpler alternatives.

### Significance: 7/10
The empirical results on SST are significant for the sentiment analysis task, and the qualitative analysis provides interesting insights. However, the conceptual contribution is limited, and the paper doesn't address broader implications for NLP.

## Recommendation

**Weak Accept**

The paper makes a modest contribution to the field by demonstrating that stacking recursive layers improves performance on the SST benchmark. The results are solid and the qualitative analysis adds value. However, the conceptual framework is flawed, and the paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish its approach from simpler alternatives or compare to relevant deep tree-structured architectures. The paper would be significantly stronger with a clearer conceptual foundation and more comprehensive comparisons.

The paper is technically sound and makes a useful contribution to sentiment analysis, but the conceptual claims are overstated. With the suggested improvements (particularly clarifying the conceptual framework and adding the missing comparisons), this could be a stronger paper.

## Key Arguments for Acceptance

- Demonstrates clear improvements over previous recursive network approaches on a standard benchmark
- Provides meaningful qualitative analysis of how different layers capture compositionality
- Uses appropriate technical choices (rectifiers, dropout, pretrained vectors)
- Well-written with clear figures and explanations

## Key Arguments Against Acceptance

- Conceptual confusion about "depth in structure" versus "depth in space"
- Fails to distinguish from simpler alternatives (single recursive layer + feedforward layers)
- Lacks comparison to relevant deep tree-structured architectures
- Incomplete ablation studies
- Overstated claims about the novelty of combining depth concepts

## Final Note

This paper makes a modest contribution to the field, but its conceptual framework is flawed, and it doesn't sufficiently establish the novelty of its approach. With revisions to clarify the conceptual foundation and include the missing comparisons, it could be a strong contribution. As it stands, it's a marginal accept that would benefit from addressing the conceptual and methodological issues noted above.
