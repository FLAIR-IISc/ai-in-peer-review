- Proposal: Stack multiple recursive layers “in space” on tree-structured inputs, passing node hidden states upward across layers via V connections to form deep RNNs for trees.
- Model details: Within-layer bottom-up recursion with shared WL/WR, untied leaf/internal transforms in first layer, ReLUs, and top-layer outputs only.
- Training setup: Supervision at every tree node on SST, fixed pretrained word vectors, layer-shared dropout masks, AdaGrad, and small L2 penalties on weights and activations.
- Main empirical finding: With fixed parameter budget, deeper-and-narrower models outperform shallower-and-wider ones up to an optimal depth (2–4 layers); reported SOTA on SST fine-grained and binary sentiment.
- Qualitative analysis: Layerwise perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analyses suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects (e.g., intensity vs polarity).
- Relation to prior work: Natural extension of recursive tree RNNs (Goller & Küchler; Socher et al.) analogous to deep recurrent nets; contrasts with multiplicative MV-RNN/RNTN and non-tree models (DCNN, Paragraph Vectors).
- Practical twist: Untying leaf/internal transforms with ReLU and dropout is an engineering refinement not emphasized in prior tree RNN work.
- Technical soundness: Architecture and training details are well specified; shared dropout masks and L2 on activations are reasonable and empirically justified.
- Empirical support: Comparisons across depths/widths back the central claim, and improvements from pretrained embeddings, ReLUs, and dropout over older baselines are shown.
- Missing/weak evidence: Ablation study is incomplete (e.g., untie vs tie, ReLU vs tanh, dropout on/off, activation L2, output at all layers), and parameter-count/details are not fully tabulated.
- Limited evaluation: Results are reported on a single dataset (SST) with no replication on other tree tasks like paraphrase or entailment.
- Statistical robustness: No multiple random seeds or significance tests reported, raising concerns about variance and reliability.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and reproducible for an expert, though it should explicitly list parameter counts, report means/variances, state parse sources, embedding fine-tuning status, and discuss computational cost.
- Originality: Moderately novel—straightforward extension of known ideas to stacked tree layers with some nontrivial analysis, but not a major conceptual leap.
- Significance: Valuable empirical finding (depth-in-space helps) and useful SOTA result, but impact limited by narrow evaluation and missing ablations.
- Questions for authors: Provide ablations (depth vs width, ReLU vs tanh, dropout, tied vs untied leaves, output connections); sensitivity to embedding freezing vs fine-tuning; results across random seeds and significance tests; parse source and robustness to parsing errors; computational overhead with depth.
- Pros: Clear, well-motivated formulation; strong SST results under fixed-parameter comparisons; practical training recipe; informative qualitative analyses.
- Cons: Limited novelty, single-dataset evaluation, missing ablations and variance reporting, unclear computational/memory costs.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—technically sound and interesting empirically but would benefit from stronger ablations and broader evaluation.
- Reviewer confidence: High on technical correctness and clarity; medium on claims of broader significance due to limited experiments.
