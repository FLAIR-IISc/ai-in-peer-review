- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks: stacks of recursive layers over parse trees with vertical connections (V) to add “depth in space” to structural depth.
- First layer unties leaf vs internal node parameters to enable ReLUs with large fixed pretrained embeddings without quadratic parameter blow-up.
- Uses shared-per-sentence dropout masks, AdaGrad, early stopping, and small L2 penalties to stabilize ReLU training on trees.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (supervision at all nodes); deep models outperform shallow models at comparable parameter budgets and reach reported state-of-the-art.
- Qualitative probes (input perturbations, nearest neighbors) suggest different layers capture complementary aspects of composition (e.g., intensity vs polarity).
- Technically sound and well-motivated extension of recursive nets; vertical connections and per-layer parameters are standard stacking mechanisms.
- Empirical design is strong by holding total parameters constant across depth/width, directly testing the benefit of depth in space.
- Missing statistical significance, variance reporting, and full visibility of absolute gains versus strongest baselines limits confidence in robustness.
- Ablation study is limited: effects of untying leaves, ReLU vs tanh, pretrained vs learned embeddings, and dropout vs L2 are not fully disentangled.
- Qualitative analyses are sensible but anecdotal; more systematic probing across controlled linguistic phenomena would be stronger.
- Paper is generally clear and organized; equations and main training details are mostly reproducible.
- Some clarity issues: truncated/missing initialization detail, exact parameter counts per configuration, and missing table context.
- Conceptual novelty is incremental (analogous to stacking recurrent layers) but the combination and empirical demonstration on SST are new and useful.
- Significance: advances compositional modeling and offers practical training recipes, but evaluation is limited to one dataset with gold parses.
- Strengths: simple implementable architecture, fair shallow-vs-deep comparisons, strong SST results, practical ReLU training tricks, and informative qualitative analysis.
- Weaknesses: limited ablations, single split/no significance reporting, potentially uncontrolled baseline comparisons, single-dataset evaluation, and missing computational cost analysis.
- Requests for authors: report parameter counts and training/inference time; provide ablations for activation, tying, vertical connections, dropout, and embeddings; clarify weight initializations; add significance tests; analyze performance with automatic parses.
- Reproducibility: most hyperparameters given (optimizer, LR, batch size, dropout grid) but missing exact non-Whh initialization, final dev-tuned hyperparameters, and parameter counts; code or supplement recommended.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—technically sound, clearly written, useful incremental contribution with state-of-the-art results, but needs broader evaluation and deeper ablations.
