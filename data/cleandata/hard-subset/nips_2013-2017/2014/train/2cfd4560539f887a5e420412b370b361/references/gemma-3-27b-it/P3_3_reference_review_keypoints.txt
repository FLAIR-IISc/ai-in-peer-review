- Overall assessment: paper introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs), shows strong SST results, well-written, recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Main idea: stack layers on recursive neural networks to combine structural recursion with spatial depth for hierarchical compositional representations.  
- Relation to prior work: builds naturally on RNNs and deep recurrent networks and cites relevant prior compositional models.  
- Empirical strength: DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs and prior state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Clarity and presentation: motivation, architecture, and figures are clear and well-explained.  
- Layer-wise analysis: qualitative experiments show different layers capture different aspects (e.g., intensity vs. direction of sentiment).  
- Practical training choices: authors use pre-trained vectors, dropout, L2 regularization, rectifiers, and untied leaf/internal parameters to stabilize training.  
- Experimental rigor: uses standard SST benchmark and strong baselines, strengthening result validity.  
- Limited evaluation scope: experiments are only on SST, raising concerns about generalizability and applicability to freer-word-order languages.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper lacks detailed description of hyperparameter search and sensitivity analysis.  
- Theoretical justification: missing deeper theoretical analysis explaining why DRNNs outperform shallower models.  
- Computational cost: paper does not adequately report training time or memory requirements for DRNNs versus shallow RNNs.  
- Clarify Equation 4: the role and sharing of V(i) across nodes/layers is insufficiently explained.  
- Input perturbation critique: chosen perturbation words seem arbitrary; a systematic selection (e.g., sentiment lexicon) would be better.  
- Nearest-neighbor analysis: qualitative observations are interesting but would benefit from quantitative measures of similarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical performance, clear method, insightful analysis, practical considerations, logical extension of RNN work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation, insufficient hyperparameter/theoretical analysis.  
- Required minor revisions: discuss computational cost, detail hyperparameter tuning, acknowledge tree-structure limitations, and clarify V(i) in Equation 4.
