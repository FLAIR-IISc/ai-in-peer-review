- Overall assessment: Compelling extension of recursive nets to deep stacked layers that outperforms shallow RNNs and SOTA on SST; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Summary & related work: Introduces depth into recursive frameworks to capture hierarchical compositionality and situates the work well among RNN/RNTN and related NLP advances.
- Strength — Novelty: Stacking recursive layers to form a deep RNN is a valuable and natural extension addressing limits of shallow recursive models.
- Strength — Empirical results: Consistently achieves strong, state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and aided by helpful diagrams.
- Strength — Qualitative analysis: Perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase analyses suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects.
- Strength — Practical details: Addresses regularization, initialization, and uses pre-trained word vectors for reproducibility.
- Weakness — Limited qualitative scope: Qualitative analyses are insightful but too narrow; deeper neuron-level or broader perturbation studies are needed.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient details on tuning (learning rate, weight decay) beyond dropout.
- Weakness — Computational cost: No explicit discussion of increased training time or memory trade-offs from added depth.
- Weakness — Generalizability: Evaluation is restricted to a single dataset (SST), limiting claims about broader applicability.
- Detailed question — Eq.4: Clarify whether V(i) is a linear transform applied before the activation f.
- Detailed question — Regularization: Provide a clearer rationale for combining fixed L2 penalty with dropout and why it stabilizes training.
- Detailed suggestion — Figure 3: Include error bars or confidence intervals to show variability in perturbation results.
- Detailed suggestion — Table 2: Quantify phrase similarity (e.g., cosine) to make nearest-neighbor examples more objective.
- Pro arguments: Novel contribution, strong benchmark results, supportive qualitative analyses, and clear exposition.
- Con arguments: Incomplete qualitative analysis, sparse hyperparameter/compute discussion, and single-dataset evaluation.
- Recommendation/action: Accept with minor revisions — expand qualitative experiments, detail hyperparameter optimization, acknowledge computational costs, and discuss generalization/future work.
