## Review of Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling extension of recursive neural networks (RNNs) to a “deep” architecture by stacking multiple recursive layers. The motivation is sound – mirroring the successful concept of depth in recurrent and feedforward networks – and the empirical results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) are strong, achieving state-of-the-art performance.  The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology. However, the qualitative analysis, while interesting, feels somewhat limited, and further theoretical justification for the architectural choices would strengthen the contribution. I recommend **accepting** the paper with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The authors correctly identify the success of deep learning in NLP, driven by word embeddings and the ability to capture hierarchical representations.  They build upon the foundation of RNNs, which operate on structured inputs like parse trees, and propose Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs). The core idea is to stack multiple layers of recursive computations, each layer potentially learning a different level of compositional abstraction.  This parallels the development of deep recurrent networks and aims to combine “depth in structure” with “depth in space”.  

The related work section is thorough, covering relevant areas like RNNs, deep recurrent networks, and previous work on sentiment analysis with tree-structured networks (e.g., Socher et al., 2013). The paper appropriately positions itself within this landscape and clearly articulates its contribution – extending RNNs to a truly deep architecture.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The DRNNs consistently outperform shallow RNNs and previous state-of-the-art methods on the SST dataset, demonstrating the benefit of increased depth.  The improvements are statistically significant and show clear advantages.
* **Well-Motivated Architecture:** The authors provide a clear rationale for the DRNN architecture, drawing parallels to established deep learning paradigms. The distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" is a useful framing.
* **Clear and Concise Writing:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand, even for readers not intimately familiar with recursive neural networks. The figures (especially Figure 2) effectively illustrate the architecture.
* **Careful Experimental Setup:**  The authors demonstrate attention to detail in their experimental setup, including the use of pre-trained word vectors, dropout regularization, and a diagonal AdaGrad optimizer. The comparison against a comprehensive set of baselines is commendable.
* **Untying Leaves and Internals:** The rationale for separating leaf and internal node representations is compelling and seems to contribute to improved performance by allowing the use of rectifiers and enabling scaling to larger word embeddings.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Justification:** While the intuition behind stacking recursive layers is strong, the paper lacks a deeper theoretical analysis of *why* this architecture is effective.  Why does this specific form of depth contribute to better representation learning?  Exploring the properties of the learned representations (e.g., representational power, gradient flow) could strengthen the argument.
* **Qualitative Analysis Could Be More Robust:** The input perturbation analysis (Figure 3) is interesting but feels somewhat limited.  It provides some insights into layer-wise behavior but doesn’t offer a comprehensive understanding of how each layer processes compositionality.  More systematic analysis, perhaps involving visualization of hidden unit activations or examining learned weights, would be beneficial. The nearest neighbor phrases analysis also feels slightly anecdotal; a quantitative evaluation of the semantic similarity between phrases would be stronger.
* **Sensitivity Analysis:** The paper could benefit from a sensitivity analysis examining the impact of hyperparameters such as the number of layers, hidden unit size, and dropout rate. A more detailed discussion of the hyperparameter tuning process would also be valuable.
* **Computational Cost:** While the paper acknowledges the benefits of depth, it doesn’t explicitly address the computational cost.  Are DRNNs significantly more expensive to train than shallow RNNs?  This is an important consideration for practical applications.



**4. Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Significant empirical improvement over existing methods on a challenging benchmark.
* Novel and well-motivated architecture building on established deep learning principles.
* Clear and well-written presentation of the methodology and results.
* Careful experimental setup and rigorous comparison to baselines.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of deeper theoretical justification for the architectural choices.
* Qualitative analysis is somewhat limited and could be more robust.
* No discussion of computational cost.




**5. Specific Suggestions for Revision:**

* **Theoretical Analysis:**  Consider adding a brief discussion of the representational power of DRNNs compared to shallow RNNs. Can you relate the depth to the complexity of the functions that can be learned?
* **Enhanced Qualitative Analysis:** Expand the qualitative analysis. Visualize hidden unit activations or learned weights to gain deeper insights into layer-wise behavior. Consider using quantitative metrics to evaluate the semantic similarity of nearest neighbor phrases.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Provide more detail on the hyperparameter tuning process and potentially include a sensitivity analysis.
* **Computational Cost:** Discuss the computational cost of training DRNNs compared to shallow RNNs.
* **Minor:** In the conclusion, the sentence "Since our task was supervised..." feels slightly out of place. Rephrase to better connect it to future research directions.



**Overall, this is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field. The empirical results are compelling, and the architecture is well-motivated. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper and solidify its impact.**
