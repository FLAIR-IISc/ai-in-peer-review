Summary: DRNNs stack multiple recursive layers to add "depth in space" to recursive networks, achieving SOTA on SST and showing layer-specific compositional behavior.

Strength 1: Clear methodological contribution with precise equations and helpful visualizations; useful distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space."

Strength 2: Strong empirical results—49.5% fine-grained accuracy on SST, outperforming shallow RNN baselines.

Strength 3: Insightful qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) indicating different layers capture different compositional aspects.

Strength 4: Careful experimental design addressing issues like vanishing gradients, using appropriate baselines and ablation studies.

Weakness 1: Incremental novelty—stacking recursive layers is not entirely new; needs clearer differentiation from prior work (e.g., Tai et al., 2015).

Weakness 2: Lacks theoretical justification for why DRNNs outperform shallower models with similar parameter counts.

Weakness 3: Limited generalizability—evaluation only on sentiment analysis (SST), unclear applicability to other compositional tasks.

Weakness 4: Computational complexity and runtime costs of DRNNs are not discussed.

Weakness 5: Comparison omits recent strong models (e.g., Tree-LSTM, dynamic CNNs), weakening the claim of state-of-the-art.

Overall assessment: A solid, well-executed contribution with meaningful empirical and qualitative gains but somewhat incremental and in need of stronger justification and broader evaluation.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

Suggested revisions: More clearly articulate novelty relative to prior deep recursive work, provide theoretical or empirical explanation for depth benefits, and discuss computational costs and comparisons to recent architectures.
