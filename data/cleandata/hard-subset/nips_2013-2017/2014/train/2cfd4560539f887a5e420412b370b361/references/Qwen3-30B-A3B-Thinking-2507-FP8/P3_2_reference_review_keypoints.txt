- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack recursive layers to combine "depth in structure" and "depth in space."  
- Key contributions: an untied variant distinguishing leaf vs internal nodes, and a stacked recursive-layer architecture.  
- Empirical result: DRNNs outperform shallow recursive networks and prior state-of-the-art on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment).  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) suggest different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Strength: Clear motivation articulating the distinction between structural and spatial depth.  
- Strength: Technically sound implementation; untied variant effectively handles sparse word vs dense phrase representations.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation on SST with strong quantitative improvements over multiple baselines.  
- Strength: Meaningful qualitative analyses that support the model’s claims about compositionality.  
- Strength: Strong baseline comparisons, including comparisons to seminal RNN work.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification explaining why stacking recursive layers helps and what the limits are.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope — only tested on sentiment analysis (SST), so generalizability is unclear.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to related deep tree-structured models (e.g., Deep Tree-LSTMs, deep compositional models).  
- Weakness: Incomplete analysis of why increasing depth beyond a point degrades performance (depth vs width trade-offs).  
- Weakness: Word vectors are fixed (not fine-tuned); trade-offs and effects of fine-tuning are not explored.  
- Quality: Paper is technically solid, claims are well-supported by experiments and analyses.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized; figures aid understanding; minor reproducibility detail (dropout across tree) could be clearer.  
- Originality: Stacking recursive layers and the untied leaf/internal variant are novel applications of known deep-learning ideas to recursive networks.  
- Significance: Achieves state-of-the-art on SST, but impact is limited by narrow task evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested improvement: Add a brief theoretical discussion on why stacking recursive layers might be beneficial.  
- Suggested improvement: Expand evaluation to at least one additional NLP task (e.g., semantic relatedness or parsing).  
- Suggested improvement: Compare experimentally to Deep Tree-LSTMs and other deep tree models.  
- Suggested improvement: Clarify the word-vector fine-tuning trade-off and include a fine-tuning experiment.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide a deeper analysis of the depth vs width trade-off to explain performance degradation with increased depth.  
- Final verdict: Strong paper with clear contribution; Overall Rating — Strong Accept; Reviewer confidence: high (4/5).
