## Review for Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper presents a novel neural network architecture – the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) – built by stacking multiple layers of recursive networks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

**Clarity:**

The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The presentation is clear and the arguments are readily understandable.

**Quality:**

The proposed approach appears technically sound. The core idea of constructing a deep RNN by layering individual recursive networks is conceptually strong. The authors support their method with a reasonable set of experiments that explore different facets of the model. The experimental results seem convincing and adequately evaluate the proposed architecture.

**Originality:**

The deep RNN architecture is a simple yet effective contribution. While building on existing recursive neural network frameworks, the depth extension provides a natural way to improve compositional representation learning. However, the paper would benefit from further discussion regarding the intuition behind leveraging increased depth for this particular task. Specifically, elaborating on *why* a deeper network is expected to be advantageous for capturing fine-grained sentiment would strengthen the paper. Additionally, a more detailed justification for the specific network architecture chosen – beyond its recursive nature – would be valuable. Exploring alternative architectures or providing a comparative analysis would further highlight the strengths of the proposed approach.

**Significance:**

This paper introduces an interesting and well-motivated idea, backed by a clear explanation of the relevant background and reasonable experimental validation. The work demonstrates the potential of deep recursive networks for tasks requiring compositional reasoning, and the results contribute to a better understanding of how network depth can influence performance in natural language processing.
