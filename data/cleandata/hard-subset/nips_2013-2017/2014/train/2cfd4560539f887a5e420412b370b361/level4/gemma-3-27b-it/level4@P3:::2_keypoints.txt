- Proposes a deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers and achieves state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).
- Builds on prior RNN work and recent deep recurrent nets, translating depth to the recursive setting.
- Key contributions: deep RNN architecture, untied leaf/internal nodes, ReLUs, specific layer connection scheme, and empirical improvements.
- Strong empirical results: consistently outperforms shallow models and baselines on SST, setting a new state-of-the-art.
- Well-motivated design choices (untying nodes, ReLUs, pre-trained vectors, fewer hidden units) that plausibly drive gains.
- Thorough evaluation with ablation studies across depth/width, input perturbation experiment, and nearest-neighbor qualitative analysis.
- Clear, well-written paper with helpful diagrams that explain the architecture.
- Major weakness: reporting and tuning on the test set rather than a held-out development set risks overfitting and inflates generalization claims.
- Suggested ablation: evaluate variant removing intermediate-layer (V) connections to clarify their role.
- Limited scope of analysis: further probing/visualization of learned representations would deepen understanding.
- Minor typo: “comprise a class of architecture” should be corrected.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective architecture with strong empirical support, clear writing, and informative qualitative analyses.
- Arguments against acceptance: serious methodological flaw in test set reporting and need for additional architectural/representation analyses.
- Overall: strong contribution but conditional—recommend accept if test-set reporting issue is addressed; otherwise request revision or reject.
