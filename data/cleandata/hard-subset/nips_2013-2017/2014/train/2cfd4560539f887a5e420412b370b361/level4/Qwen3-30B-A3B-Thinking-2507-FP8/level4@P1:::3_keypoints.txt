- Proposal: Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack recursive layers to add spatial depth while preserving structural depth.  
- Empirical result: DRNNs outperform shallow recursive networks with similar parameter counts and achieve state-of-the-art fine-grained SST performance.  
- Key techniques: Uses ReLU activations, fixed pretrained word embeddings, and dropout; qualitative analyses show distinct compositional roles across layers.  
- Strength: The spatial+structural depth idea is well-motivated and supported by compelling empirical and qualitative evidence.  
- Strength: Writing is clear and sufficient implementation details are provided for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Claim about rectifier benefits lacks citation or empirical support (e.g., Glorot et al. 2011) or ablation.  
- Weakness: Missing important baseline—Kalchbrenner et al.’s 2014 CNN model—weakening comparative claims.  
- Weakness: Fixing word embeddings is unjustified; fine-tuning could alter results and may explain DRNN gains.  
- Weakness: Binary classification results are invalid because neutral examples were included; a separate binary training protocol is required.  
- Weakness: Shared dropout masks across tree nodes are unexplained; alternative designs (per-node dropout) should be considered.  
- Weakness: Lack of experiments on single-root-label tasks leaves the claim that spatial depth (vs. node-wise prediction) drives gains unproven.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending additional experiments (add CNN baseline, test fine-tuning vs fixed embeddings, redo binary protocol, and evaluate on single-label sentiment) to solidify the core claim.
