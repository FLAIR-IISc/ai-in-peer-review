- Proposes Deep RNN: stacking recursive layers where a phrase’s representation depends on same-layer constituents and its lower-layer representation.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, outperforming strong baselines (RNTN, Paragraph Vectors).  
- Model is elegant and well-motivated, distinguishing "depth in structure" vs "depth in space."  
- Unties parameters for leaf (words) and internal (phrases) nodes, enabling large pre-trained embeddings without quadratic parameter blowup.  
- Successfully applies rectified linear units and dropout in a recursive setting.  
- Introduces direct cross-layer connections (connection matrix V) as a core architectural innovation.  
- Exposition is clear and qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) convincingly show hierarchical representations.  
- Major methodological concern: Table 1(a) reports architectures’ results on the test set, implying possible tuning on the test set.  
- Authors should clarify procedure and report development-set performance for architectural choices, and report only the single best model on the final test set.  
- Suggests an ablation study for the connection matrix V (e.g., V=0) to quantify its contribution.  
- For acceptance: novel, high-quality work with strong empirical and qualitative results likely to be impactful.  
- Against acceptance: potential test-set tuning undermines the reported results and requires clarification.
