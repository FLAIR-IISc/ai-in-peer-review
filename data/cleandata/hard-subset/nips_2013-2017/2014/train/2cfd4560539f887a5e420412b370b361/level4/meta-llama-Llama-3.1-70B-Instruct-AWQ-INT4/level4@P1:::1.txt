Paper For paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a new architecture — deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) — which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The authors propose a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture, which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. This architecture is applied to the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees as the structure. The idea of deep RNNs is inspired by the success of deep feedforward neural networks and deep recurrent neural networks in various tasks. The authors also draw on previous work on recursive neural networks, which have been applied to parsing, sentence-level sentiment analysis, and paraphrase detection.

**Clarity**

In general, this paper is well-written and pleasant to read. The authors provide clear explanations of their proposed architecture and experimental setup. However, there are some minor issues with clarity, such as the use of acronyms without explicit definitions (e.g., "RNN" is not explicitly defined until later in the paper).

**Quality**

The paper seems technically sound. The authors provide a clear description of their proposed architecture and experimental setup. The major idea of deep recursive neural networks is well-motivated, and the authors provide reasonable amounts of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method.

**Originality**

The proposed idea is simple and reasonable. However, it might be better to add more discussions about:

* Why intuitively deeper networks are good for the target task? The authors could provide more insights into why they expect deep RNNs to perform better than shallow RNNs on fine-grained sentiment classification.
* Why choose the selected network architecture? The authors could provide more justification for their choice of network architecture, including the use of rectifier linear activation units and the specific way of stacking recursive layers.

**Significance**

The paper has presented an interesting idea, clear background description, and reasonable experimental supports. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of deep RNNs on fine-grained sentiment classification, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank dataset. The paper also provides some insights into the operation of different layers in the deep RNN, including the use of input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrases to evaluate the different notions of similarity in the meaning space captured by multiple layers.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a new and interesting architecture for deep recursive neural networks.
* The authors provide clear explanations of their proposed architecture and experimental setup.
* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of deep RNNs on fine-grained sentiment classification, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank dataset.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from more discussions about the intuition behind deep RNNs and the choice of network architecture.
* The paper could provide more insights into the operation of different layers in the deep RNN, including more detailed analysis of the input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrases experiments.

Overall, I believe that the paper is well-written and provides a clear contribution to the field of natural language processing. With some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, I would recommend accepting the paper.
