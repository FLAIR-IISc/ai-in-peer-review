- Introduces "deep" recursive networks extending depth across spatial dimensions with independently parameterized layers and parameter sharing across heights.  
- Incorporates dropout and ReLUs while keeping word embeddings fixed to pre-trained vectors; evaluated on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank.  
- Method is novel but can be seen as a generalization of approaches used in recurrent networks.  
- Reports strong performance and notable gains from dropout and ReLUs.  
- Modeling and experimental choices lack sufficient justification; additional experiments are required before acceptance.  
- Paper is generally well-written and provides enough detail for replication.  
- Definitions of W^η and W^{xh} are unclear; recommend separate equations for inputs vs. hidden layers and explicit parameter sets and matrix dimensionalities.  
- Lines 148–154 appear speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence or clarified.  
- Missing an important baseline: Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) CNN for modeling sentences should be included.  
- Question why word vectors were not fine-tuned; fine-tuning could explain performance gains and should be controlled for (e.g., single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings).  
- Clarify motivation for using shared dropout units.  
- For stability, suggest considering weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation as alternatives to the current approach.  
- Binary-class experiments are improperly conducted with neutrals included; binary classification should be evaluated separately following standard protocol.  
- Question whether deep RNNs help when only a single root-level label is used; recommend testing on 1–2 additional datasets with only global labels and rerunning corrected experiments.
