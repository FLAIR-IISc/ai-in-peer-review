- Proposes adding depth across space in recursive neural networks, with per-layer parameters shared across heights.  
- Incorporates dropout and ReLUs and uses fixed pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford sentiment treebank and reports impressive performance gains.  
- Method is innovative and builds reasonably on deepening recurrent networks.  
- Performance improvements from dropout and ReLUs are noteworthy.  
- Several model and experiment design choices lack sufficient justification and require more experiments.  
- Notation unclear: W^eta and W^xh are undefined—separate equations for inputs/hidden layers and parameter dimensionalities are needed.  
- Discussion in lines 148–154 appears speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence.  
- Missing baseline comparison with Kalchbrenner et al.’s convolutional sentence model (ACL).  
- Surprising decision not to fine-tune word embeddings; fine-tuning could explain gains and must be controlled for.  
- Motivation for using shared dropout units (line 251) is unclear and needs justification.  
- Alternative regularization (weight norm constraints or gradient truncation) should be considered instead of shared dropout.  
- Binary classification setup deviates from standard protocol by including neutral examples in training—redo separately following standard protocol.  
- The deep-RNN analogy is reasonable for node-level predictions, but authors should test tasks with only a global/root label, redo binary experiments, fine-tune embeddings, and add 1–2 global-label datasets to strengthen claims.
