- Proposal: Stack multiple recursive layers to add “depth in space” to tree-structured models, with learned across-layer connections (V) that pass node representations upward.  
- Architecture details: Untied parameters for leaves vs. internal nodes, ReLU activations, dropout regularization, fixed pretrained word vectors, and inter-layer connections at each node.  
- Evaluation: Tested on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained and binary), outperforming matched-parameter shallow recursive baselines and prior recursive models (MV-RNN, RNTN) and paragraph vectors.  
- Analysis: Exploratory input perturbations and nearest-neighbor phrase analysis indicate successive layers capture different aspects of compositionality and sentiment intensity.  
- Novelty: Meaningful extension of recursive nets by introducing stacked depth with explicit per-node inter-layer connections; using ReLUs and dropout in trees is incremental but effective.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, precise equations, helpful figures, and a well-motivated across-layer connection V.  
- Strength: Solid empirical gains under a controlled parameter budget and a sensible training recipe (ReLUs, dropout, AdaGrad, identity initialization).  
- Strength: Qualitative analyses give interpretable insights into layer-wise behavior (lower layers lexical/syntactic, higher layers sentiment-focused).  
- Main methodological concern: Possible test-set tuning—Table 1(a) may report model-selection results on the test set rather than the dev set and needs clarification or re-reporting.  
- Missing ablations: Need comparisons to stacking per-node MLPs without V and to removing V to verify that inter-layer shortcuts (V) drive gains.  
- Output ablation request: Compare predicting from all layers (auxiliary losses) or from intermediate layers to test whether only the final layer is necessary.  
- Component ablations requested: Isolate effects of untying leaves vs. internals, ReLU vs. tanh, and dropout vs. L2 to quantify design choices.  
- Embedding issue: Since word vectors are fixed, add experiments fine-tuning embeddings to assess impact and possible overfitting given rich node supervision.  
- Notation suggestion: Use “RecNN” instead of “RNN” to avoid confusion with recurrent neural networks.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound and careful training, but empirical claims need stronger ablation support and dev/test hygiene.  
- Clarity assessment: Paper is clear and well-structured; minor notation cleanup and explicit statement of data-split usage for Table 1(a) recommended.  
- Significance: Advances state of the art on SST and demonstrates value of added tree-composition depth under a fixed parameter budget.  
- Minor issues/typos: Fix phrasing (“class of architecture” → “class of architectures”), “classificationd dataset” → “classification dataset,” and ensure hyperparameters selected on dev set.  
- Overall: Strong, clearly presented contribution likely to influence tree-structured modeling; requires proper dev/test separation and the suggested ablations to fully attribute gains.
