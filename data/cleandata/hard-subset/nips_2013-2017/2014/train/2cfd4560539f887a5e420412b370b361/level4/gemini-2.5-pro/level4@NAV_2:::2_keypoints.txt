- Introduces Deep RNN: stacks multiple recursive layers to combine structural depth with spatial depth, learning hierarchical representations.  
- Applied to fine-grained sentiment classification on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and reportedly outperforms shallow counterparts and prior SOTA (RNTN, Paragraph Vectors).  
- Originality/significance: novel, well-motivated extension of recursive models that adapts spatial depth ideas to recursion.  
- Empirical quality: strong experimental results and effective modifications (untied leaf/internal representations, rectifier units).  
- Qualitative analysis: input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase studies support that different layers capture distinct compositional aspects.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, precisely described, and figures effectively illustrate the model.  
- Methodological concern: Table 1a appears to use test-set results for model selection, risking test-set overfitting and inflated performance — authors should clarify and report dev-set selection.  
- Missing ablation: no experiment removing the vertical node-to-node connections (matrix V) to isolate their contribution versus mere stacking.  
- Minor typo: abstract phrase "comprise a class of architecture" should be corrected (e.g., "architectures").  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, new SOTA on a challenging benchmark, technically sound, and insightful qualitative analyses.  
- Argument against acceptance: potential serious methodological flaw (hyperparameter tuning on test set) that could invalidate the main quantitative claims.
