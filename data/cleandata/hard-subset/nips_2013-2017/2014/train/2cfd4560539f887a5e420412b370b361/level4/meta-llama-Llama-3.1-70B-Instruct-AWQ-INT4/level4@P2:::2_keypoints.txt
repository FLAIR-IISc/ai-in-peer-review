- Introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset.  
- Exposition is clear and the paper situates the work relative to prior research in deep connectionist architectures, word embeddings, and recursive neural networks.  
- Architecture: stacks multiple recursive layers to form hierarchical representations that capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Key technical changes: untied leaf vs. non-terminal nodes, use of rectified linear units in a recursive setting, large unsupervised word vectors with smaller hidden non-terminals, and deep inter-layer/tree connections.  
- Empirical: deep RNN outperforms shallow recursive nets of the same size and achieves SOTA on the sentiment task.  
- Qualitative analysis: different layers appear to handle different compositional aspects and capture distinct notions of similarity.  
- Major methodological issue: Table 1(a) reports results on the test set while hyperparameters were tuned on that test set—should report on a development set.  
- Minor issues: several typos and wording errors (e.g., "comprise a class of architecture" should be plural).  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with impressive results that require only minor revisions to address the noted issues.
