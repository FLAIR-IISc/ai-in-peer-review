This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that obtains state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results from their new architecture are impressive, and the exposition is very clear.

The changes they propose to previous RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally demonstrated to work well. Specifically, they introduce several key modifications: untying leaf nodes and non-terminal nodes, using rectified linear units in the recursive setting, using large unsupervised word vectors but smaller hidden non-terminal nodes, and using a deep architecture that is not simply replacing a single neural network RNN layer with a deep layer but introducing connections between the layers and tree nodes. One potential comparison that could have been explored is whether the outputs of the last hidden layer at each node could be the only input to the next parent, effectively dropping the connection from matrix V.

The authors evaluate their proposed architecture on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) dataset. They demonstrate that their deep recursive neural networks outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size, as well as several baseline models, including a multiplicative RNN variant and the recently introduced paragraph vectors. The results show that deep RNNs achieve new state-of-the-art performance on the task.

The authors also conduct qualitative experiments to investigate the effect of multiple layers on the representation of compositionality in language. They find that each layer captures different aspects of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity. Specifically, they observe that the first layer focuses on local word-level features, while the second layer captures more abstract features that are related to sentiment intensity. The third layer, which is the final layer, captures the most abstract features that are related to sentiment.

One minor criticism is that the results in Table 1 (a) should have been reported on the development set, not the final test set. The authors are tuning hyperparameters on the test set, which could potentially lead to overfitting.

There are a few typos in the paper, including "comprise a class of architecture" which should be "comprises a class of architecture".

In terms of the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, the main strength is the introduction of a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The paper is also well-written and easy to follow. One potential weakness is that the authors could have explored more comparisons with other models, such as the effect of dropping the connection from matrix V.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.

* The paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
