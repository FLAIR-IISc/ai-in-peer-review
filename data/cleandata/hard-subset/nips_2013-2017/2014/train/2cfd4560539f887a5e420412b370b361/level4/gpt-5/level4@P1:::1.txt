Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

Summary
The paper proposes a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture formed by stacking multiple recursive layers. Each layer performs recursive composition over a parse-tree structure, and higher layers refine intermediate representations passed from lower layers. The model is evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification, with experiments exploring different depths and widths. The authors provide a clear background on recursive neural networks and position their model relative to prior work on compositionality in language.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
- The motivation and background are clearly presented.

Quality
- The work appears technically sound.
- The main contribution—stacking multiple recursive layers to create a deep RNN—is well motivated and clearly described.
- The experimental evaluation is reasonable and covers multiple aspects of the method.

Originality
- The idea is simple and reasonable: extend recursive neural networks by adding depth via stacked recursive layers.
- It would strengthen the paper to include more discussion of:
  - Why a deeper architecture is intuitively beneficial for the target task (e.g., what kinds of compositional phenomena different layers are expected to capture).
  - Why the specific architectural choices were made among possible deep recursive variants (e.g., design alternatives and tradeoffs).

Significance
- The paper presents an interesting idea, provides clear background, and supports its claims with reasonable experiments.

Pros
- Clear problem formulation and motivation.
- Straightforward, well-explained extension of recursive networks to a deeper architecture by stacking layers.
- Empirical results are presented in sufficient quantity to evaluate the approach across settings.
- Writing is clear and the paper is pleasant to read.

Cons
- The paper would benefit from a deeper intuitive explanation of how and why added depth improves compositional modeling for sentiment, beyond empirical gains.
- The rationale for the chosen architecture (as opposed to other ways to make recursive models deeper) could be elaborated—for example, why connect the classifier only to the final layer, and what alternatives were considered.
- Additional discussion of architectural design choices and their implications would improve the reader’s understanding.

Overall Assessment
This is a clearly written and technically sound paper that introduces a natural and useful extension of recursive neural networks by stacking recursive layers. The idea is straightforward yet meaningful, and the experiments provide reasonable evidence that deeper recursive models can offer benefits for fine-grained sentiment classification. The main areas for improvement are a stronger intuitive justification for depth in this setting and a more detailed discussion of architectural choices and alternatives.
