This paper introduces a novel deep recursive network architecture, extending the successful concept of depth found in recurrent neural networks to a spatial dimension. The model utilizes shared parameters within each layer across different tree heights, and incorporates dropout and ReLU activations with fixed, pre-trained word embeddings. Experiments are conducted on the sentiment treebank dataset.

The approach is a logical generalization of existing recurrent network methods and yields strong performance on the chosen dataset. The observed gains from dropout and ReLU are noteworthy. However, certain modeling and experimental choices lack sufficient justification, and further investigation is needed before a strong recommendation for acceptance can be made.

The paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient detail to enable reproducibility of the reported results.

Detailed comments / questions:

- Line 138: The variables W^eta and W^xh are undefined. Clarification regarding their relationship to W^{eta}L and W^{eta}R is needed. Presenting separate equations for input and hidden layer calculations, and a comprehensive summary of the parameter space (e.g., {WL, WR, U, b, c}) including the dimensionality of each matrix, would improve clarity.
- Lines 148-154: This discussion appears speculative. If based on empirical findings, please state so explicitly. The assertion itself is not entirely convincing without supporting evidence.
- Baseline: Please include a comparison to the results reported in “A convolutional neural network for modelling sentences” (Kalchbrenner et al., ACL 2014).
- Word Embeddings: The decision to not fine-tune the word vectors is questionable. Allowing word embeddings to adapt to the sentiment task often improves performance. The current setup creates a discrepancy between discriminative phrase/sentence vectors and non-sentiment-specific word vectors. Investigating whether the observed improvements are dependent on this lack of fine-tuning – potentially by comparing against a single-layer RNN *with* fine-tuned embeddings – is crucial.
- Line 251 (shared dropout units): Please explain the rationale behind this design choice.
- Lines 255-256 (stability): Consider exploring alternative stabilization techniques such as weight norm constraints or gradient clipping, commonly employed in recurrent network training.
- Binary Classification: The binary classification experiment should be performed independently. Inclusion of neutral class examples during training deviates from standard protocol, rendering the results incomparable to existing work.
- Generalization to Root-Only Prediction: The analogy to deep recurrent networks is well-suited for node-wise prediction in tree structures. However, it would be valuable to evaluate performance when predicting solely a root-level label. Does the benefit of depth still hold in this scenario, and how does it compare to a single-layer RNN? Further experiments, specifically a re-evaluation of the binary classification with the correct protocol, an investigation of the effects of fine-tuning word embeddings, and the inclusion of 1-2 datasets utilizing global labels only, would significantly strengthen the paper and provide more robust evidence supporting the value of depth across space.
