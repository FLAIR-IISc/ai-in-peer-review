- Proposes "deep" recursive networks by adding depth across space with layer-wise parameter sharing across heights.  
- Uses dropout and ReLUs, and fixes word representations to pre-trained embeddings.  
- Experiments conducted on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank.  
- Method is novel but effectively a generalization of depth-in-time approaches from recurrent nets.  
- Achieves very strong performance on the sentiment treebank.  
- Performance gains from dropout and ReLUs are noteworthy.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written and reproducible with sufficient detail.  
- Technically sound; theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims.  
- Some model and experimental choices (e.g., fixing embeddings without fine-tuning) are insufficiently justified.  
- Minor clarity/notation issues (definitions of W^eta, W^xh, and parameter-space summary) need improvement.  
- Significance would be increased by additional experiments (fine-tuning embeddings, more datasets with only global labels).  
- Recommendation to include Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN as a baseline.  
- Recommendation to study fine-tuning word embeddings and redo the binary classification per proper protocol.  
- Recommendation to add 1–2 additional global-label datasets to better demonstrate the method's usefulness.
