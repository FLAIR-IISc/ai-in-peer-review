- Proposal: introduces “deep” recursive networks by adding depth across tree space, with each layer having its own parameters shared across heights; uses dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank.
- Novelty: method effectively generalizes depth ideas from recurrent nets to recursive/tree architectures.
- Empirical: reports strong performance on the sentiment treebank and notable gains from dropout and ReLUs.
- Clarity/reproducibility: paper is clearly written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Unclear notation: definitions of W^eta and W^xh are ambiguous; suggest separate equations for inputs vs. hidden layers and explicit parameter/dimensionality summary.
- Speculative claims: statements in lines 148–154 seem speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence or clarified.
- Missing baseline: should include results from Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) CNN sentence model as a baseline.
- Word embeddings: authors do not fine-tune pretrained word vectors—this may be inconsistent with discriminative phrase/sentence vectors and could explain improvements; needs investigation/control.
- Shared dropout: motivation for using shared dropout units (line 251) is not justified.
- Stability: instead of the current approach, consider standard RNN techniques like weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation for stability (lines 255–256).
- Binary classification protocol: including neutral examples during training deviates from standard protocols; binary evaluation should be performed separately.
- Tree/root-label question: it’s unclear whether depth advantages persist when only root/global labels are used—need experiments that use only a global label.
- Recommended experiments: redo binary classification per correct protocol, test fine-tuning embeddings (and a single-layer RNN baseline with fine-tuned embeddings), and add 1–2 additional datasets with only global labels to strengthen claims.
