- Proposes “deep” recursive networks by adding depth across spatial layers, sharing layer-specific parameters across heights.  
- Employs dropout and ReLUs with fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on Socher et al.’s sentiment treebank.  
- Novel generalization of recurrent depth strategies and shows strong performance on the sentiment treebank.  
- Notable performance gains attributed to dropout and ReLUs.  
- Several model and experimental design choices lack sufficient justification and require more experiments before acceptance.  
- Paper is clearly written and provides enough detail for reproducibility.  
- Clarify notation: define W^η and W^{xh}, likely W^η_L and W^η_R; present separate equations for inputs vs. hidden layers and summarize parameter set with dimensionalities.  
- Claim in lines 148–154 is speculative and needs explicit empirical citation or stronger justification.  
- Include baseline results from Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) CNN for sentence modeling.  
- Justify why word embeddings were not fine-tuned; control experiments are needed since fine-tuning can affect sentiment performance.  
- Explain motivation for shared dropout units used in the model.  
- Consider and/or justify alternatives for training stability (weight norm constraints, gradient truncation commonly used in recurrent nets).  
- Binary classification experiment violates standard protocol by including neutral examples and must be re-run per protocol for comparability.  
- Demonstrate generalization beyond node-wise labels: redo binary experiment correctly, test embedding fine-tuning effects, and evaluate on 1–2 datasets with only global/root labels to validate spatial depth benefits.
