Review - Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper presents a novel architecture – a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) – built through the stacking of multiple recursive layers. The model is evaluated on the problem of fine-grained sentiment classification.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

Quality
- The work appears technically solid.
- The core concept, a deep RNN constructed by layering individual recursive networks, is well-conceived.
- The authors provide a sufficient quantity of experimental results to assess different facets of the proposed method.

Originality
- The proposed idea is straightforward and logical.
- Further discussion would be beneficial regarding:
 + The intuitive rationale for why a deeper network is advantageous for this specific task.
 + The motivations behind the chosen network architecture.

Significance
- The paper introduces an interesting idea, accompanied by a clear background explanation and supported by reasonable experimental evidence.
