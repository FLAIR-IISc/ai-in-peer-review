- Paper proposes deep recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive layers to add “depth in space” atop tree recursion.
- Each layer has its own parameters shared across tree positions; hidden states pass upward to the next layer.
- First layer distinguishes leaf and internal nodes (untied parameters), enabling ReLUs and fixed pretrained embeddings.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank with supervision at all tree nodes; deeper (narrower) models outperform shallow baselines with matched parameters.
- Achieves state-of-the-art vs. RNTN, MV-RNN, and Paragraph Vectors; qualitative analyses suggest layer-wise specialization.
- Relates closely to stacked recurrent networks and prior recursive/parsing models, and compares to document/sentence encoders like Paragraph Vectors and dynamic CNNs.
- Strength: Conceptually clear extension that introduces representational hierarchy in tree-structured models.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with careful parameter-matched comparisons and useful practical training choices (ReLU, dropout, AdaGrad).
- Strength: Qualitative analyses and generally clear exposition sufficient for reimplementation.
- Weakness: Some modeling and experimental choices lack justification and key ablations are missing (notably embedding fine-tuning).
- Weakness: Binary sentiment evaluation deviates from standard SST protocol, hindering direct comparison.
- Weakness: Notational and clarity issues (undefined symbols, missing dimensions) impede full reproducibility.
- Weakness: No evaluation when supervision is only at the root, limiting claims about utility of depth across space.
- Request: Clarify notation (e.g., W^η, W^xh) and provide a concise parameter summary with dimensions for reproducibility.
- Request: Provide evidence or temper claims about why separating leaf/internal spaces “enables” rectifiers; include ablations if observed.
- Request: Ensure dynamic CNN baseline uses the same SST split or explicitly state differences in splits.
- Request: Compare fixed vs. fine-tuned embeddings for shallow and deep models to control for embedding effects.
- Request: Justify shared dropout mask across nodes and compare to independent/variational/dropconnect alternatives.
- Suggestion: Mention other stability techniques (gradient clipping, spectral init, norm constraints, layer norm) beyond L2 regularization.
- Request: Train and report a binary model following the standard SST protocol rather than mapping fine-grained outputs to binary.
- Suggestion: Evaluate on at least one root-only supervised dataset or additional sentence-level tasks to test generality.
- Pros for acceptance: Timely conceptual advance, strong results on a benchmark, insightful qualitative analyses, and generally reproducible write-up.
- Cons against acceptance: Missing controls (embedding fine-tuning), protocol deviations for binary results, limited evaluation beyond SST, and some unclear/under-justified design choices.
- Overall: Promising and well-executed paper demonstrating gains from depth across space; addressing binary evaluation, embedding fine-tuning, and root-only experiments would substantially strengthen the work.
