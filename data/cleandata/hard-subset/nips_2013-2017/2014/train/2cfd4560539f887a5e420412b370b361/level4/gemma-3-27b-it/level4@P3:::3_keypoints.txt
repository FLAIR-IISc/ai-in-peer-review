- Proposal: Introduces "deep" recursive networks by adding depth across space with parameter-sharing across heights.  
- Novelty: Concept is a logical and valuable generalization of depth used in recurrent nets.  
- Methods: Uses dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Empirical result: Achieves strong / state-of-the-art performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Qualitative analysis: Input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase experiments give insight into layer-wise compositionality.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Justification gap: Several model/design choices (e.g., not fine-tuning embeddings) lack adequate justification.  
- Limited evaluation: Experiments are restricted to a single dataset/task (SST), limiting claims of generality.  
- Binary protocol issue: Binary classification setup includes neutral examples during training, deviating from standard practice and harming comparability.  
- Missing baseline: Paper omits comparison to a relevant recent model (Kalchbrenner et al., 2014 CNN).  
- Speculative claims: Statements about different layers' behaviors are presented as facts without sufficient empirical support.  
- Experimental suggestions: Authors should test fine-tuning embeddings, include a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings, and evaluate on additional datasets/tasks.  
- Protocol fix: Re-run binary classification with a proper protocol (exclude neutrals during training or decode posteriors only on binary test set).  
- Root-label test: Investigate performance when only a single root label is available to match traditional sentiment settings.  
- Notation/detail request: Clarify W^η and W^xh, separate input/hidden equations, and summarize parameter set and matrix dimensionalities.  
- Dropout question: Explain motivation for sharing dropout units across nodes within a layer.  
- Stability alternatives: Consider other stabilization techniques (weight norm constraints, gradient clipping) used in recurrent nets.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends rejection in current form but encourages revision addressing experimental design, baselines, and evidential support.
