- Paper proposes a "deep" recursive neural network with multiple spatial layers, layer-wise parameter sharing, dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings, achieving strong results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

- Overall assessment: novel and logical extension with state-of-the-art performance, clear writing, and reproducible details, but several claims and design choices lack sufficient justification, preventing unconditional acceptance.

- Equation and notation clarity is insufficient (e.g., undefined W^eta and W^xh); full parameter sets and matrix/vector dimensionalities should be specified.

- Claims about vanishing gradients in single-layer RNNs are speculative and need empirical support or clarification.

- Missing a key baseline comparison to Kalchbrenner et al.'s convolutional sentence model (ACL 2014).

- Decision not to fine-tune pre-trained word embeddings requires justification; fixed embeddings may inflate apparent gains from depth.

- Single-layer baseline without embedding fine-tuning may be unfair; experiments should include fine-tuning for both shallow and deep models.

- Binary classification experiment deviates from standard protocol by including neutral examples in training, making results incomparable to prior work and needing re-run on only positive/negative classes.

- Request motivation for using shared dropout units across child nodes; the intuition for this design choice is unclear.

- Suggest exploring stability techniques used in recurrent nets (e.g., weight norm constraints, gradient clipping) and report any experiments with them.

- Central analogy to deep recurrent networks is weaker for tasks with only root-level labels; it's unclear if spatial depth helps when only a single root prediction is made.

- Strongly recommended additional experiments: re-run binary task with correct protocol, ablation study on embedding fine-tuning for both models, and evaluations on 1–2 additional sentence classification datasets to test generality.
