- Proposal: introduce "deep" recursive networks by adding depth across space with parameter sharing across heights within each layer.  
- Implementation choices: use dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Results: strong performance on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank; dropout and ReLUs yield notable gains.  
- Novelty: method is novel but is an extension of deep recurrent-net ideas.  
- Writing/reproducibility: paper is mostly clear and provides sufficient detail to reproduce results.  
- Major concern: several model and experimental design choices are questionable and lack justification; additional experiments needed.  
- Clarity issue: definitions of W^eta and W^xh are unclear—need separate equations for inputs vs hidden layers and a parameter/dimensionality summary.  
- Speculation: discussion in lines 148–154 appears speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence.  
- Missing baseline: should include results from Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN paper for comprehensive comparison.  
- Word embeddings: puzzling decision not to fine-tune embeddings—this could explain gains and must be controlled (compare single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings).  
- Dropout design: motivation for using shared dropout units is unclear and needs explanation.  
- Regularization alternatives: authors should consider weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation instead of shared dropout units.  
- Binary classification protocol: current binary experiment includes neutral examples during training and is not comparable to standard protocol—redo separately.  
- Scope/generalization: need experiments on datasets with only global/root labels to test whether deep RNNs help when node-level predictions are absent.  
- Recommendation: redo binary experiment correctly, test fine-tuning embeddings, and add 1–2 global-label datasets to strengthen evidence for depth across space.
