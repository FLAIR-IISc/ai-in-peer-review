- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network (stacking multiple recursive layers) to increase hierarchical representation capacity.  
- Evaluates the model on fine-grained sentiment classification and reports state-of-the-art performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear background and related work.  
- Technical presentation of the deep RNN architecture is clear and sound.  
- Experiments compare to previous work and analyze the effect of multiple layers.  
- Results show deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of comparable size.  
- Qualitative analysis suggests different layers capture different aspects of compositionality and similarity.  
- Authors provide reasonable experimental support demonstrating effectiveness for modeling compositionality.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of the intuition for why deeper networks suit the target task.  
- Needs more analysis on how multiple layers capture distinct compositional phenomena.  
- Would benefit from additional comparisons with prior work and deeper result analysis.  
- Overall, valuable contribution with potential applicability to broader NLP tasks.
