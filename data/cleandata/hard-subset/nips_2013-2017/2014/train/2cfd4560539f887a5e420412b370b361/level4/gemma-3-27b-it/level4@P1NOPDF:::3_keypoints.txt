- Paper proposes deep recursive networks that add depth across tree structure, using shared layer parameters, dropout, ReLU, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank.

- Novel and natural generalization of recurrent-net depth; shows strong performance and notable gains from dropout and ReLU.

- Paper is well-written and reproducible, but several model and experimental choices lack justification and need more experiments.

- Unclear notation: W^eta and W^xh are not defined; recommend separate equations for inputs vs. hidden layers and a parameter/dimensionality summary.

- Section (lines 148–154) reads speculative—if claims are empirical, state that explicitly and provide evidence.

- Missing baseline: should include Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN sentence model as a comparison.

- Word vectors were not fine-tuned—question whether this choice is justified; fine-tuning could capture sentiment-specific info and affect comparisons.

- Possible confound: improvements from deep RNNs might stem from not fine-tuning embeddings; compare against a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings.

- Shared dropout-unit design (line 251) needs motivation and explanation.

- Stability suggestions (lines 255–256): consider alternative stabilization techniques such as weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation.

- Binary classification protocol is incorrect: including neutral examples deviates from standard practice, so results are not comparable—redo binary experiments separately.

- Need to test whether depth advantage holds when only a single (root-level) label is available; recommend additional experiments on 1–2 datasets with only global labels.

- Overall recommendation: redo binary experiments per protocol, investigate embedding fine-tuning, add CNN baseline and 1–2 single-label datasets to strengthen claims.
