Summary
This paper proposes a deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The contributions are clearly explained, well motivated, and supported by strong empirical results. The paper is very well written and easy to follow.

Key contributions and what I found compelling
- Untying parameters between leaf and nonterminal nodes to better model their distinct roles.
- Employing rectified linear units (ReLUs) in a recursive setting, which improves optimization and representational capacity.
- Combining large, unsupervised word embeddings with smaller hidden dimensions for nonterminal nodes, yielding an efficient allocation of capacity.
- Introducing a genuinely deep recursive architecture that is more than a simple deepening of a single RNN layer: it adds connections both across layers and along the tree, enabling richer compositional representations.

The architectural ideas are clever and empirically validated. The experiments indicate clear gains over strong baselines, and the exposition provides sufficient intuition for why the changes help.

Main concerns and suggestions
- Evaluation protocol (dev vs. test): Table 1(a) appears to report results on the final test set in a context where hyperparameters or model choices may have been tuned. This risks implicit test-set tuning. Please report model selection and ablation results on a development set, and reserve the final test set strictly for a single, untouched evaluation. If any settings were in fact chosen using test performance, the reported test results should be recomputed under a proper protocol.
- Ablation on deep connections: A particularly informative comparison would be a variant where only the output of the last hidden layer at each node is passed to its parent (i.e., remove the direct connection from matrix V). This would isolate the benefit of the proposed inter-layer/skip connections versus a conventional “deep per-node” composition. Including this ablation would strengthen the causal story for the architecture’s gains.
- Capacity and parameterization: The choice to pair large pre-trained word vectors with smaller hidden nonterminal dimensions makes sense from an efficiency standpoint. If space permits, a brief sensitivity analysis (e.g., varying hidden size while fixing word vector size) would help quantify the trade-off and reassure readers that the gains are not primarily due to increased capacity at the leaves.

Clarity and presentation
- The paper is very clearly written, with a logical flow from motivation to model to experiments. Figures and equations are easy to parse.
- The discussion of why ReLUs help in the recursive setting is helpful; consider adding a short note on any observed regularization needs (e.g., norm clipping, dropout) specific to ReLUs in trees.
- Training details (optimization, initialization, regularization, and parameter counts) are generally adequate; exact parameter counts for the main model and key baselines would further aid reproducibility.

Minor comments and typos
- “comprise a class of architecture” → “comprise a class of architectures.”
- “classificationd” → “classification.”
- Please proofread for a few minor grammatical slips and duplicated phrases.

Overall assessment
This is an exciting and timely paper. It advances recursive neural modeling with a thoughtfully designed deep architecture and demonstrates clear state-of-the-art results on a hard sentiment dataset. The contributions are well motivated and experimentally validated, and the paper is a pleasure to read.

Provided the evaluation protocol is corrected to avoid any test-set tuning and, if possible, the suggested ablation on the inter-layer connections is added, I strongly support acceptance.
