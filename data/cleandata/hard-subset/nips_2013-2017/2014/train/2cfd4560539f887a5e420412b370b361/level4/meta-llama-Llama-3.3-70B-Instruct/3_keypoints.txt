- Proposes a novel "depth across space" deep recursive network where each layer has its own parameters shared across heights, uses dropout/ReLUs, and pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the sentiment treebank.  
- Method is innovative and generalizes ideas from recurrent networks, showing strong performance and gains from dropout and ReLUs.  
- Some model and experimental design choices are questionable or insufficiently justified; additional experiments needed before acceptance.  
- Paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient detail to replicate results.  
- Clarify definitions of W^{η} and W^{xh} (line 138); consider separate equations for inputs/hidden layers and provide a parameter/dimensionality summary.  
- Discussion in lines 148–154 is speculative and lacks empirical evidence; if based on authors' results this should be stated and justified.  
- Include a baseline comparison to Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN for modelling sentences (ACL).  
- The decision not to fine-tune word vectors is puzzling; fine-tuning could capture sentiment and should be controlled for (a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings may rival the proposed model).  
- Explain the motivation for using shared dropout units (line 251).  
- Consider alternative regularization/stability methods (lines 255–256) such as weight norm constraints or gradient truncation used in recurrent nets.  
- Binary classification should be run as a separate experiment using the standard protocol (exclude neutral examples during training) to allow fair comparison.  
- The deep RNN analogy is reasonable for node-level predictions, but it is unclear if deep RNNs help when only a single root label exists; additional experiments are needed.  
- Recommended additional experiments: redo binary classification with proper protocol, evaluate the effect of fine-tuning embeddings, and add one or two datasets with only global labels to strengthen evidence.
