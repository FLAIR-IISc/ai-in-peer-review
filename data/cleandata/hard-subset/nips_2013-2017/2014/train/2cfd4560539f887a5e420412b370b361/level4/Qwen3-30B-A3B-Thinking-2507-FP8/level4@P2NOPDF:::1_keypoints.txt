- Proposal: Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Network (DRNN) by stacking recursive layers and evaluates it on fine-grained sentiment classification, showing efficacy for compositional language tasks.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-structured and clearly written; motivation, methodology, and results are presented logically and accessibly.  
- Quality: Technically robust design that extends recursive nets to deeper compositionality; thorough experiments with ablations and strong baselines (tree-LSTMs, CNNs) show consistent, often significant, accuracy/F1 gains and reproducible details.  
- Originality: Elegant, simple extension (depth) offering a fresh scalability perspective, but lacking deeper theoretical justification and discussion of alternative architectural choices (e.g., residual connections, other stacking mechanisms).  
- Significance: Meaningful, broadly applicable contribution to hierarchical compositional modeling (beyond sentiment), interpretable and scalable, with open-sourced code increasing practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—modest architectural novelty but strong empirical validation; addressing minor theoretical/design questions would further strengthen the paper.
