- Proposes "deep" recursive networks sharing layer parameters across heights, inspired by deep recurrent nets, and uses dropout, ReLUs, and pre-trained embeddings on the sentiment treebank.  
- Novel extension of recurrent deepening to recursive nets with strong performance gains on the sentiment treebank.  
- Performance improvements from dropout and ReLUs are notable, but some model/experimental choices need stronger justification.  
- Paper is well-written and provides sufficient detail for replication.  
- Undefined variables (W^eta and W^xh) and unclear parameter/matrix dimensionalities; request separate equations for inputs vs. hidden layers and a parameter summary.  
- Section (lines 148–154) is speculative; unclear if claims are empirically supported.  
- Missing comparison to the recent CNN sentence model (Kalchbrenner et al., ACL) as a baseline.  
- Not fine-tuning word vectors is puzzling—word vectors should be controlled/fine-tuned for sentiment tasks as this may affect gains attributed to deep RNNs.  
- Motivation for shared dropout units (line 251) is unclear and needs justification.  
- Stability concerns: suggest alternative remedies like weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation (as used in RNNs).  
- Binary classification protocol deviates by including neutral examples during training; binary task should be evaluated separately.  
- Recommend more experiments: redo binary classification properly, study fine-tuning embeddings, evaluate root-only label tasks, and add 1–2 additional global-label datasets to strengthen claims.
