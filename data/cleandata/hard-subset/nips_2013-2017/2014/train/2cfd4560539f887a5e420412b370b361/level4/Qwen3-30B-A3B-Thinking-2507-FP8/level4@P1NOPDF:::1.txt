

Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language  

This paper introduces a deep recursive neural network (DRNN) architecture, built by stacking multiple recursive layers, and evaluates it on fine-grained sentiment classification.  

**Clarity**  
The paper is well-written and clearly structured, making the core contributions accessible.  

**Quality**  
The technical approach appears sound. The central idea—constructing a deep model via stacked recursive layers—is well-motivated and effectively implemented. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering multiple facets of the method with appropriate baselines and metrics.  

**Originality**  
While the core concept is straightforward, the application to compositionality in language is promising. However, the authors should strengthen the discussion by addressing two key points:  
1. Why deeper architectures specifically benefit fine-grained sentiment tasks (beyond general depth advantages).  
2. The rationale for the chosen architectural design (e.g., layer structure, activation choices) relative to alternatives.  

**Significance**  
The paper presents a novel and well-supported approach to modeling compositional language structures. It offers a clear problem statement, thorough background context, and robust experimental validation. The work provides a meaningful contribution to the literature on neural architectures for linguistic compositionality.
