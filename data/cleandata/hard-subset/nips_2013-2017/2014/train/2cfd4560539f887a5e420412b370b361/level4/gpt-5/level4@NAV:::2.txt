This compelling paper presents a novel deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results of the proposed model are strong, and the exposition is very clear.

The modifications to prior RNN models are inventive, well-justified, and empirically shown to be effective:
- decoupling parameters for leaf nodes and internal (nonterminal) nodes
- employing rectified linear units within the recursive framework
- using large unsupervised word vectors while keeping the hidden size for nonterminal nodes smaller
- adopting a deep architecture that does more than simply replace a single RNN layer with a deeper one by introducing connections across layers and tree nodes — it would be an interesting ablation to compare a variant where only the final hidden layer at each node is passed to its parent (i.e., dropping the connection from matrix V)
