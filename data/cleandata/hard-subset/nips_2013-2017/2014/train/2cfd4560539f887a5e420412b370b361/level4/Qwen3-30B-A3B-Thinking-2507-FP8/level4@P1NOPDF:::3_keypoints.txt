- Introduces depth-across-space extension for recursive networks with shared-layer parameters, dropout, ReLU, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings, evaluated on the Socher sentiment treebank.

- Core idea is a meaningful generalization of depth in recursive architectures and achieves state-of-the-art on the sentiment treebank; dropout and ReLU give notable gains.

- Paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

- Model notation unclear (Line 138): explicitly define W^η and W^{xh}, clarify left/right variants, present separate input/hidden equations, and summarize parameter set and matrix dimensions.

- Claim about the necessity of depth (Lines 148–154) is speculative and needs citation or empirical support.

- Missing baseline: include comparison to Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) convolutional sentence model.

- Word embeddings left fixed without justification; must test whether results stem from not fine-tuning and include controlled experiments with fine-tuned embeddings (single-layer RNN baseline with fine-tuning).

- Shared dropout choice (Line 251) needs justification versus layer-specific dropout.

- Stability/regularization (Lines 255–256): consider and report alternative methods like weight norm constraints or gradient clipping.

- Binary classification protocol is incorrect (neutral examples included in training); re-run binary experiments following the standard protocol to ensure comparability.

- Depth utility question: test whether depth helps when only a single root label is predicted (datasets with global/root-level labels) to validate the core contribution.

- Recommendation: promising but requires three critical additions—(1) proper binary classification experiment, (2) controlled fine-tuning comparison, and (3) root-level-label experiments—otherwise reject pending revision.
