This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that obtains state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results from their new architecture are impressive, and the exposition is very clear.

The authors relate their work to previous research in the field, specifically highlighting the contributions of deep connectionist architectures, word embeddings, and recursive neural networks. They also provide a thorough explanation of their proposed architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. This allows for a hierarchical representation of the data, enabling the network to capture different aspects of compositionality in language.

The authors' changes to previous RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally demonstrated to work well. These changes include untying leaf nodes and non-terminal nodes, using rectified linear units in the recursive setting, using large unsupervised word vectors but smaller hidden non-terminal nodes, and using a deep architecture that introduces connections between the layers and tree nodes.

The experimental results are impressive, with the deep RNN outperforming shallow recursive nets of the same size and achieving state-of-the-art performance on the sentiment analysis task. The authors also conduct qualitative experiments that suggest each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity.

However, there are a few issues with the paper. Specifically, the results in Table 1(a) should have been reported on the development set, not the final test set, as the authors are tuning hyperparameters on the test set. Additionally, there are a few typos throughout the paper, such as "comprise a class of architecture" instead of "comprise a class of architectures."

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes significant contributions to the field of natural language processing. The authors' proposed architecture is well-motivated, and the experimental results are impressive. With a few minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, this paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field.
