- Introduces Deep RNN: stacked recursive layers with inter-layer matrices V^(i) and untied leaf/internal node representations to add spatial depth to tree-structured RNNs.  
- Achieves SOTA on Stanford Sentiment Treebank fine-grained (49.5%), outperforming shallow RNNs (49.5 vs 48.1) and prior baselines; dropout is crucial (49.5 vs 46.06 with L2).  
- Technical rigor: resolves dimensionality/activation issues via untied leaves/internal nodes and rectifiers.  
- Strong ablation study: systematically compares depth/width trade-offs while controlling parameter counts.  
- Convincing qualitative analyses: input perturbation and nearest neighbors show distinct layer roles (e.g., Layer 1 word dominance, Layer 3 sentiment).  
- Clear presentation: well-organized motivation, effective architecture visualizations, and transparent experimental setup.  
- Originality: novel combination of structural depth (tree recursion) with spatial depth (stacked layers and V^(i) connections).  
- Significance: advances hierarchical compositionality modeling with interpretable layer functions and implications for phrase-level NLP tasks.  
- Major flaw: hyperparameter tuning reported on the test set (Table 1a), risking overfitting and invalidating comparisons—must retune on dev and report test only for final model.  
- Missing ablation: no variant evaluating Deep RNN without inter-layer V^(i) connections to confirm their necessity.  
- Minor clarifications needed: explain Layer 2 convergence in input-perturbation (e.g., "coolest" vs "worst") and provide concrete direction for proposed unsupervised applications.  
- Recommendation: accept pending revision to fix the test-set evaluation and add the requested ablation; overall a high-quality, original contribution.
