This paper introduces the deep Recursive Neural Network (deep RNN), a novel architecture for learning compositional representations of language. The core idea is to extend the standard Recursive Neural Network by stacking multiple recursive layers, thereby increasing the model's capacity for non-linear transformations. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of this approach on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification, evaluating it on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

**Clarity and Quality**

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow. The central contribution—the deep RNN—is both elegant and intuitive. By extending the well-established recursive neural network framework with added depth, the authors propose a straightforward yet powerful method for capturing more complex compositional semantics. The approach appears technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by a comprehensive set of experiments that investigate various aspects of the model, including the impact of depth and the visualization of learned representations.

**Originality and Significance**

The proposed deep RNN is a simple and reasonable extension of existing models, and it represents a valuable contribution to the field. The paper effectively demonstrates that increasing the depth of recursive models can lead to improved performance on a challenging NLP task. The clear exposition, coupled with solid experimental validation, makes this a strong submission.

While the core idea is compelling, the paper could be strengthened by a more detailed discussion in a few key areas to further clarify its contribution and impact:

1.  **Intuition for Depth:** The motivation for depth could be further elaborated. While deep architectures have proven successful in other domains, the specific benefits for recursive models in NLP deserve more exploration. What linguistic phenomena or compositional structures does a deeper network capture that a shallower one cannot? Providing qualitative examples, perhaps by analyzing specific phrases or sentences where the deeper model excels, would provide valuable intuition and bolster the paper's claims. For instance, how does a deep RNN handle complex negation, sarcasm, or nested clauses differently from a single-layer model?

2.  **Architectural Design Choices:** The paper would benefit from a discussion of the architectural design space. Why was this particular method of stacking layers chosen? Were alternative architectures, such as different connectivity patterns between layers or alternative non-linearities, considered? A brief justification for these choices would help the reader understand the design process and the robustness of the proposed model.

In summary, this paper presents a significant and interesting contribution. The introduction of the deep Recursive Neural Network is a logical and valuable extension of prior work. Addressing the points above regarding the intuition for depth and architectural choices would further enhance the impact and completeness of this solid research.
