This work presents a novel deep recursive neural architecture, achieving state-of-the-art results on a challenging sentiment classification benchmark. The empirical performance of the proposed model is commendable, and the paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.

The architectural modifications to existing RNN frameworks are insightful, well-justified, and empirically validated to be effective. Key contributions include:
- Using separate parameterizations for leaf and non-terminal nodes.
- The application of Rectified Linear Units (ReLUs) within the recursive model.
- Employing large, pre-trained word embeddings while maintaining smaller hidden representations for non-terminal nodes.
- The introduction of a deep architecture that goes beyond simple layer stacking by incorporating connections between different layers at each node in the tree. A potentially valuable ablation study here would be to evaluate performance when these connections (from matrix V) are removed, leaving only the output of the final hidden layer at each node as input to its parent.

A significant concern arises from Table 1(a), where model selection results appear to be reported on the final test set. This constitutes tuning on the test set and should be corrected by reporting these results on the development set.

Finally, the manuscript would benefit from a final proofreading to correct a few minor typographical errors.
