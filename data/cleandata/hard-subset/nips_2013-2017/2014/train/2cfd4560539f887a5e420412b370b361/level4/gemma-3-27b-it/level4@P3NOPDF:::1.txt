## Review of Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture – the Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN) – constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate this model on the challenging task of fine-grained sentiment classification, demonstrating its potential for capturing compositional semantics.

**Clarity:**

The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The exposition is clear and the concepts are presented in a logical manner. The notation is consistent and aids understanding.

**Quality:**

The technical approach appears sound and well-motivated. The core idea of building a Deep RNN by stacking individual recursive networks is intuitive and represents a logical extension of existing recursive neural network models. The authors provide a substantial set of experimental results, exploring various aspects of the proposed method and demonstrating its effectiveness. The ablation studies are particularly valuable in understanding the contribution of different components of the architecture. The chosen baselines are relevant and appropriate for comparison.

**Originality:**

The proposed Deep RNN architecture is a simple, yet effective, contribution. While recursive neural networks are not entirely novel, the application of depth – inspired by successes in other areas of deep learning – to recursive models for compositionality is a valuable extension. The idea of explicitly stacking recursive layers to build a deeper representation is a reasonable and promising direction. 

However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the theoretical motivations behind the choice of a deeper architecture for this specific task. While the experimental results demonstrate empirical improvements, further elaboration on *why* a deeper network is intuitively expected to be beneficial for capturing compositional semantics in language would strengthen the paper. Additionally, a more detailed justification for the specific architectural choices – beyond stating it’s a stacking of recursive nets – would be helpful. Exploring alternatives and articulating the rationale for the chosen configuration would increase the paper's impact. For example, discussing how this architecture relates to tree-structured LSTMs or other compositional models would position the work within the broader landscape of research.

**Significance:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially impactful idea. The clear background description establishes the context of the work effectively, and the reasonable experimental support validates the proposed approach. The results demonstrate that Deep RNNs can achieve competitive performance on a challenging sentiment classification task.  Success in this task suggests potential applications in other areas requiring compositional understanding, such as semantic parsing or question answering. 

**Minor Issues & Suggestions:**

*   While the experimental section is thorough, it would be helpful to include error analysis. Examining examples where the Deep RNN fails could provide insights into limitations of the model and suggest future research directions.
*   The authors might consider discussing the computational complexity of the Deep RNN compared to shallower recursive models. Does the increased depth introduce significant computational challenges?
*   Consider briefly addressing the potential for overfitting with deeper networks and how this was mitigated in the experiments (e.g., regularization techniques).



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper presenting a valuable contribution to the field of compositional semantics. The Deep RNN architecture is a reasonable and promising extension of existing recursive neural network models. While some further discussion of the theoretical motivations and architectural choices would strengthen the paper, the clear presentation and strong experimental results make this a worthwhile contribution to NIPS 2017. I recommend acceptance.
