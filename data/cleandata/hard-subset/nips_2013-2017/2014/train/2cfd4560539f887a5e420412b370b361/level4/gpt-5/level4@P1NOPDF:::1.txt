Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, and evaluates it on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

Clarity
- Overall, the paper is clearly written and pleasant to read.

Quality
- The work appears technically sound.
- The main idea is appealing: a deep recursive neural network formed by stacking multiple layers of individual recursive nets.
- The authors provide a reasonable amount of experimental evidence evaluating various aspects of the method.

Originality
- The proposed idea is simple and sensible.
- The paper would benefit from additional discussion of:
  - Why, intuitively, a deeper network is advantageous for the target task.
  - Why the specific network architecture was chosen over alternatives.

Significance
- The paper presents an interesting idea, a clear background description, and reasonable experimental support.
