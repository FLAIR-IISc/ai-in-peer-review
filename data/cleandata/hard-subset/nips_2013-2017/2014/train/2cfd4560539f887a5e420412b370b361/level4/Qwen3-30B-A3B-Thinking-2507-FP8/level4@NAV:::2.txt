

This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results are impressive and the exposition is exceptionally clear. The proposed modifications to prior RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated:  
- Untying leaf and non-terminal nodes  
- Employing rectified linear units within the recursive framework  
- Utilizing large unsupervised word vectors alongside smaller hidden non-terminal node dimensions  
- Implementing a deep architecture that extends beyond simple layer replacement by introducing inter-layer and tree-node connections (a comparison omitting the matrix V connection from child to parent nodes would have been insightful).
