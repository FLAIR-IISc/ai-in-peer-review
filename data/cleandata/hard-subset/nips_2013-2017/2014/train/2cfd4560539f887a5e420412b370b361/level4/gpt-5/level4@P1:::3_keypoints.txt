- Proposal: stack multiple recursive layers to add “depth in space,” with per-layer shared parameters, untied leaf/internal params, ReLU/dropout, fixed pretrained embeddings, and final-layer outputs for hierarchical abstraction.  
- Evaluation: tested on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), showing improvements over shallow recursive models and prior baselines, plus qualitative analyses of layer-specific compositionality.  
- Relation to prior work: natural extension of recursive neural nets and parallels stacked deep recurrent networks for temporal modeling.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated idea connecting stacked recurrent nets to structural computation.  
- Strength: strong empirical SST results with careful parameter-matched comparisons showing consistent gains from depth.  
- Strength: effective practical choices (ReLUs, dropout, pretrained embeddings) that improve even shallow models.  
- Strength: useful qualitative analyses suggesting deeper layers separate sentiment polarity and intensity.  
- Strength: generally clear exposition with enough detail for reproduction of most experiments.  
- Weakness: several modeling and experimental choices are insufficiently justified, making it hard to attribute gains specifically to depth.  
- Notation concern: W^η and W^xh undefined—request explicit leaf vs internal equations, full parameter set, and matrix/vector dimensionalities.  
- Claim about rectifiers vs dense word vectors is speculative—ask for supporting ablations or to temper the claim.  
- Missing baseline: include Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN results as a contemporary sentence-modeling comparison.  
- Embedding fine-tuning: challenge keeping embeddings fixed—request comparisons to well-regularized single-layer RNNs with fine-tuned embeddings and to deep RNNs with fine-tuning.  
- Dropout design: ask justification for using the same dropout mask across all nodes in a layer and a comparison to independent masks per node.  
- Stability/optimization: ask whether norm constraints or gradient clipping were tried in addition to L2 penalties on weights/activations.  
- Binary evaluation protocol: binary results should be obtained by training specifically on the binary task (exclude neutrals), not by decoding a 5-way model.  
- Generalization beyond node supervision: request experiments on datasets with only sentence-level labels to test whether depth across space helps without phrase-level annotations.  
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually clean extension, strong SST performance, and insightful qualitative analyses.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing CNN baseline, nonstandard binary evaluation, lack of embedding fine-tuning ablations, and limited evidence of generalization beyond SST.  
- Overall assessment: promising and well-executed paper, but needs (1) standard binary results, (2) controlled fine-tuning/strong single-layer baselines, and (3) at least one sentence-level-only dataset and CNN baseline to fully justify acceptance.
