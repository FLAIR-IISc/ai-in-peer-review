This paper introduces the Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN), a novel architecture for modeling compositional structure in language. Inspired by recent work on deep recurrent neural networks, the authors construct their model by stacking multiple recursive layers. In this architecture, the representation of a phrase at a given layer depends on both the representations of its constituent sub-phrases within the same layer (depth in structure) and its own representation from the layer below (depth in space). The model is evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank for fine-grained sentiment classification, where it outperforms previous methods, including strong baselines like the Recursive Neural Tensor Network and Paragraph Vectors, to achieve a new state-of-the-art result.

**Strengths**

The paper's primary strength is its elegant and well-motivated model. The Deep RNN is a natural extension of existing recursive networks, and the distinction between "depth in structure" and "depth in space" provides a clear conceptual framework for the architecture. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating a significant improvement over strong baselines on a difficult task.

The authors also introduce several clever and effective modeling choices that contribute to this success:
1.  Untying the parameters for leaf nodes (words) and internal nodes (phrases), which allows for the use of large, pre-trained word embeddings without a quadratic increase in parameters for the phrase composition function.
2.  The successful application of rectified linear units and dropout regularization in the recursive setting.
3.  A novel deep architecture that introduces direct connections between a node's representations across different layers.

The paper's exposition is exceptionally clear. The qualitative analyses in Section 3.2 (Input Perturbation and Nearest Neighbor Phrases) are particularly insightful, providing compelling and intuitive evidence that the stacked layers learn a hierarchy of representations, capturing different aspects of syntax and semantics.

**Weaknesses**

The main concern with the paper relates to the experimental methodology. Table 1(a) presents results for models with varying depths and widths directly on the test set. This practice is problematic as it can be interpreted as tuning hyperparameters (in this case, model architecture) on the test set, which can lead to over-optimistic results. Standard practice requires that such architectural exploration and model selection be performed exclusively on the development set. The authors should clarify this procedure and, ideally, present the development set performance for the configurations in Table 1(a), reporting only the single best-performing model's score on the final test set.

**Questions and Suggestions**

The proposed architecture's key innovation is the connection matrix V, which links each node in the tree across layers. It would have been interesting to see an ablation study that quantifies the importance of this specific component. For instance, what would the performance be if these connections were removed (i.e., setting V=0)? This comparison would help isolate the benefits of the proposed deep architecture over other potential strategies for stacking recursive layers.

**Summary of Arguments**

*   **For acceptance:** The paper presents a novel, well-motivated, and elegant deep recursive architecture. It achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging and widely used benchmark. The presentation is very clear, and the qualitative analysis provides valuable insights into the model's behavior. The work is of high quality and is likely to be significant for future research in compositional semantics.
*   **Against acceptance:** The primary concern is the potential methodological flaw in reporting results from an architectural search on the test set. This issue should be clarified by the authors.
