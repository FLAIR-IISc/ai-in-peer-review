- Proposes “deep” recursive networks by adding depth across tree space, sharing layer parameters across heights.
- Uses dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; experiments on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank.
- Novel extension of recurrent-net depth; achieves strong performance and notable gains from dropout and ReLUs.
- Some model and experimental design choices are questionable or not well-justified, requiring more experiments before acceptance.
- Paper is mostly clearly written and reproducible, but several clarifications and improvements are needed.
- Undefined variables (W^eta and W^xh) and unclear equation organization; request summary of parameter dimensionalities.
- Discussion in lines 148–154 appears speculative and not clearly tied to empirical evidence.
- Missing baseline: should compare to Kalchbrenner et al.’s convolutional sentence model from ACL this year.
- Decision not to fine-tune word vectors is puzzling; fine-tuning may be essential for sentiment tasks and could account for gains.
- Motivation for using shared dropout units is unclear and should be explained.
- Suggest alternative stability methods (weight norm constraints or gradient truncation) instead of shared dropout reliance.
- Binary classification experiment deviates from standard protocol by including neutral class examples, making comparisons invalid.
- Recommend experiments: redo binary protocol properly, study effect of fine-tuning embeddings, test on 1–2 additional datasets with only global/root labels to validate depth-across-space benefits.
