- Introduces a novel spatially deep recursive neural network with per-depth shared parameters, motivated by deep recurrent architectures.  
- Incorporates dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on SST and attributes gains partly to dropout and ReLUs.  
- Paper is generally well-written and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Notation issues: W^eta and W^xh are undefined; full parameter set and matrix dimensionalities should be explicitly presented.  
- Some claims (lines 148–154) are speculative and lack empirical support.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) convolutional sentence model.  
- Word embedding decision: not fine-tuning embeddings is unjustified; need to test whether fixed embeddings explain gains and compare to a single-layer model with fine-tuning.  
- Shared dropout: motivation for sharing dropout units across compositions is not provided.  
- Training stability: authors should consider standard techniques (weight-norm constraints, gradient clipping) and discuss their use.  
- Binary classification experiment is flawed by including neutral examples during training, making results incomparable to prior work; must rerun with standard protocol.  
- Recommended additional experiments: rerun binary setup correctly, evaluate fine-tuning vs. fixed embeddings with single-layer baselines, and test on one or two sentence-level (root-only) datasets to assess general utility of spatial depth.
