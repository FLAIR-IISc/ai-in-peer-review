- Novel architecture: extends depth to the spatial dimension of recursive networks by stacking recursive layers with layer-wise parameter sharing.  
- Implementation details: incorporates dropout, ReLU activations, and pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Empirical result: achieves strong, state-of-the-art performance on SST with notable gains from dropout and ReLU.  
- Notation issue: W^eta and W^xh are unclear; need separate equations for leaf vs internal transforms and a parameter-dimension summary.  
- Untying leaves vs internals: rationale for separate parameters is speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence.  
- Missing baseline: should compare against Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN sentence model as a relevant competitive baseline.  
- Word vector fine-tuning: decision not to fine-tune embeddings is questionable and could confound results; experiments controlling for this are needed.  
- Shared dropout units: need justification for sharing dropout masks across nodes within a layer.  
- Stability concerns: L2 regularization is used, but alternatives like weight norm constraints or gradient clipping should be considered.  
- Binary classification protocol flawed: training with neutral examples prevents valid comparison to prior work; a separate model excluding neutrals is required.  
- Generalizability: benefits are demonstrated mainly with node-level labels (SST); need experiments with only root-level labels to show broader applicability.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is generally well-written and reproducible, though some notation clarifications would improve readability.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, logical extension of recursive nets; strong benchmark performance; clear presentation; informative ablation (dropout/ReLU).  
- Argument against acceptance: several design choices lack justification, experiments are incomplete/missing baselines, and generalizability is limited.  
- Overall assessment: promising paper but concerns about experimental design and justification warrant revisions; reviewer leans to weak accept pending fixes (fine-tuning experiments, corrected binary eval, broader evaluation).
