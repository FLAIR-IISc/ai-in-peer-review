- Proposes a deep recursive neural network architecture by stacking multiple recursive layers.  
- Evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification, showing improvements over shallow models and state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and helpful figures.  
- The core idea is well-motivated and technically sound, analogous to deep recurrent networks.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Ablation studies vary depth and width, strengthening empirical analysis.  
- Regularization (dropout and L2) is used appropriately to mitigate deep-network instability.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline potential future work.  
- Idea is a reasonable, natural extension of prior work but somewhat incremental in originality.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison to simply increasing hidden-layer size in standard recursive networks.  
- Could benefit from a deeper theoretical analysis of why depth helps.  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank, indicating significance.  
- Exploratory analyses of compositionality across layers are insightful and relevant to NLP researchers.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution with strong results and clear presentation—recommend accept.
