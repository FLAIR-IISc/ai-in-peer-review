- New deep recursive neural architecture achieves state-of-the-art sentiment classification with strong motivation, compelling empirical results, and exceptionally clear exposition.
- Innovation 1: untied parameters for leaf and non-terminal nodes.
- Innovation 2: use of rectified linear units (ReLU) in the recursive setting.
- Innovation 3: leveraging large unsupervised word vectors while using smaller hidden-state dimensions for non-terminal nodes.
- Innovation 4: deep architecture with explicit inter-layer connections between tree nodes (not just stacked layers); ablation suggested removing V connections to isolate this effect.
- Critical evaluation error: Table 1(a) reports test-set results instead of development-set results, risking test-set leakage and overestimated generalization.
- Minor typographical issues: "classificationd" → "classification" and the fragment "comprise a class of architecture".
- Overall verdict: significant, well-validated technical contribution and meaningful advancement in recursive neural networks, but evaluation protocol must be fixed before publication.
