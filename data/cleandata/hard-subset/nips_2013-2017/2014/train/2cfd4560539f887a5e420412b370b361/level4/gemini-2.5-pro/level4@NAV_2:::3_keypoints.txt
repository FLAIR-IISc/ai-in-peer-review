- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network architecture that stacks multiple recursive layers, extending prior recursive/recurrent models.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and shows gains from ReLUs and dropout.  
- Manuscript is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Notation in Section 2.2 is unclear (e.g., `W^{η·}`, `W^{xh·}`) and should explicitly define leaf vs. internal node computations and parameter dimensionalities.  
- The rationale for untying leaf and internal parameters is speculative and needs empirical support (ablation).  
- A relevant baseline (Kalchbrenner et al., 2014 CNN for sentences) is missing from comparisons.  
- Not fine-tuning pretrained word vectors may confound results; a shallow RNN with fine-tuned embeddings could match or exceed the deep RNN’s performance.  
- Sharing dropout masks across all nodes in a layer/sentence is unexplained and needs justification over independent dropout.  
- Stability measures for ReLU activations are limited to a small L2 penalty; other techniques (gradient clipping, weight norm constraints) should be considered or reported.  
- Binary classification evaluation differs from prior work by training on five classes then decoding to binary; a proper binary-only training experiment is needed for fair comparison.  
- Experiments are limited to a dataset with per-node supervision; it’s unclear whether gains generalize to root-level (single-label) tasks.  
- Recommend re-running: (1) proper binary-training experiment, (2) control with fine-tuned embeddings, and (3) evaluation on sentence-level datasets to demonstrate broader applicability.
