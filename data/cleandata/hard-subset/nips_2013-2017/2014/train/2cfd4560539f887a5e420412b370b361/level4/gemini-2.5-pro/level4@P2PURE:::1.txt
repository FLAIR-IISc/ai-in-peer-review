This paper introduces the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), a novel architecture constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate this model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

**Clarity**
- The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

**Quality**
- The work appears to be technically sound. The central idea of creating a deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers is compelling. The authors provide sufficient experimental results to evaluate various aspects of their proposed method.

**Originality**
- The proposed idea is simple and intuitive. However, the paper would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion on two points:
    1. The intuition behind why a deeper network is beneficial for this task.
    2. The justification for the specific architectural choices made.

**Significance**
- The paper presents a compelling idea, provides clear background, and is supported by solid experimental results.
