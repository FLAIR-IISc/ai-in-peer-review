- Contribution: Proposes deep recursive neural networks that stack recursive layers to add spatial and structural depth, applied to fine-grained sentiment on SST with ReLUs, dropout, and fixed pretrained embeddings.  
- Main empirical claim: Deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets (with equal parameter counts) and report state-of-the-art on SST.  
- Key innovations: Use of ReLUs, dropout regularization, untied leaf/internal weights, and fixed pretrained word vectors; qualitative analyses show distinct compositional roles per layer.  
- Technical quality: Methodology is sound and clearly explained, with well-motivated design choices enabling ReLU use.  
- Experimental rigor: Authors evaluate depth/width trade-offs and include many baselines, plus insightful qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor phrase comparisons).  
- Reproducibility & clarity: Paper is well-written with sufficient detail for reproducibility, though some notation issues remain.  
- Significance: Results on SST are strong and the architecture is a principled generalization of recursive networks.  
- Critical omission: Fails to compare to Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN baseline, undermining the state-of-the-art claim.  
- Embedding treatment concern: No justification for fixing pretrained word vectors — failing to test fine-tuning means depth benefits may be confounded with embedding choices.  
- Binary protocol error: Binary sentiment results are derived by thresholding a 5-class model (including neutrals), violating standard protocol and invalidating comparisons to prior work.  
- Limited evaluation scope: Experiments are restricted to SST with tree-structured labels; single-label/root-only tasks are not evaluated, limiting generality.  
- Notation/clarity issues: Some parameter definitions (e.g., W^η, W^{xh}, dimensions of W^L/W^R) and the shared-dropout rationale need clearer explanation.  
- Overall assessment: Idea is natural and promising and qualitative analyses are strong, but experimental validation lacks rigor due to omitted baseline, embedding/fine-tuning concerns, and protocol errors.  
- Recommendation: Reject as submitted; major fixes needed (add Kalchbrenner baseline, test fine-tuning, retrain proper binary classifier, broaden evaluations) before acceptance.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pro — novel architecture, strong SST results, insightful qualitative analyses, clear writing; Con — missing critical baseline, unjustified embedding choices, flawed binary protocol, limited task scope.
