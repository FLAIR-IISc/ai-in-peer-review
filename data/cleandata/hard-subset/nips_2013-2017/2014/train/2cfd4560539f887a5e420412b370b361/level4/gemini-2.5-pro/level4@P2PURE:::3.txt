This paper introduces a 'deep' recursive neural network, extending the model's depth spatially. This approach is motivated by the success of similar deep architectures in recurrent neural networks. In the proposed model, each layer has its own set of parameters, which are shared across all nodes at that depth. The authors also incorporate dropout and Rectified Linear Units (ReLUs) and use fixed, pre-trained word embeddings. The model is evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

While the proposed method is a straightforward generalization of adding depth to recurrent networks, its application to recursive networks is novel. The authors report state-of-the-art results on the sentiment treebank, and the performance gains attributed to dropout and ReLUs are interesting in their own right. However, several model and experimental design choices are questionable or lack sufficient justification. For this reason, I believe additional experiments are required before the paper can be recommended for acceptance. On a positive note, the paper is generally well-written and provides enough detail for reproducibility.

**Detailed Comments and Questions:**

*   **Clarity (line 138):** The notation W^eta and W^xh is used without prior definition. It is unclear if these are meant to be general placeholders for W^L and W^R. For clarity, I suggest presenting separate equations for the input and hidden layers. Additionally, it would be beneficial to explicitly define the full parameter space (e.g., {W^L, W^R, U, b, c}) and to include the dimensionalities of all matrices upon their introduction.
*   **Speculation (lines 148-154):** The claims made in these lines appear speculative. If this reasoning is based on empirical observations, the authors should state this and provide supporting evidence. As presented, the argument that this is a significant issue is not convincing.
*   **Missing Baseline:** The results should be compared against the convolutional neural network model for sentence modeling proposed by Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014).
*   **Word Vector Fine-Tuning:** The decision not to fine-tune the word embeddings is not well-justified. Fine-tuning on a sentiment task typically allows word vectors to capture sentiment-specific semantics. A crucial question is whether the performance gains from the deep model are an artifact of using fixed embeddings. It is plausible that a single-layer recursive network with fine-tuned embeddings could outperform the proposed model. This is a critical comparison that should be included as a controlled experiment.
*   **Shared Dropout (line 251):** Could the authors provide the motivation for sharing dropout units across compositions?
*   **Training Stability (lines 255-256):** Regarding stability, have the authors considered alternative techniques common in recurrent network training, such as constraining the norms of the weight matrices or applying gradient clipping?
*   **Binary Classification Experiment:** The experimental setup for binary classification appears flawed. By including neutral examples during training, the protocol deviates from the standard setup for this task. This makes the reported results incomparable to prior work. The experiment should be re-run following the established protocol.

**Summary and Required Experiments:**
The analogy to deep recurrent networks is most direct for tasks requiring predictions at every node in the tree, such as the sentiment treebank task. To better assess the general utility of spatial depth, it is important to evaluate its effectiveness on tasks with only a single label at the root. Would the deep model still outperform a single-layer model in this setting?

While the proposed approach is promising, its contribution would be significantly strengthened by additional experiments. Specifically, I recommend that the authors:
1.  Re-run the binary classification experiment using the correct protocol.
2.  Investigate the effect of fine-tuning word embeddings, comparing against a single-layer model with fine-tuning.
3.  Evaluate the model on one or two additional datasets that involve only a single, sentence-level label.

These experiments are necessary to provide stronger evidence for the benefits of spatial depth in recursive networks and would result in a much stronger paper.
