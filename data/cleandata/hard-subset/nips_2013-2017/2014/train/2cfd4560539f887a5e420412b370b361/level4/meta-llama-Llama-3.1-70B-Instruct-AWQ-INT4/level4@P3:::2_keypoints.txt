- Introduces a novel deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) that achieves state-of-the-art on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Proposes well-motivated changes: untied leaf vs non-terminal nodes, ReLU in recursive setting, large unsupervised word vectors with smaller hidden nodes, and deep layer/tree connections.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture and benefits.  
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate effectiveness on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Provides useful qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrase experiments) offering insight into model behavior.  
- Strength: novel deep RNN architecture with strong empirical performance on a challenging dataset.  
- Strength: clear motivation and explanation of modifications over prior RNN models.  
- Strength: qualitative analyses that illuminate model operation.  
- Weakness: authors tuned model on the final test set instead of using a development set, risking overfitting.  
- Weakness: minor typos and grammatical errors present in the manuscript.  
- Suggestion: compare to a variant that drops the V matrix connection (only use last hidden layer outputs to parent).  
- Suggestion: report development-set results and reserve test set for final evaluation.  
- Suggestion: carefully proofread to remove typos and grammatical mistakes.  
- Overall: strong paper with minor revisions needed; recommended for acceptance (Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10).
