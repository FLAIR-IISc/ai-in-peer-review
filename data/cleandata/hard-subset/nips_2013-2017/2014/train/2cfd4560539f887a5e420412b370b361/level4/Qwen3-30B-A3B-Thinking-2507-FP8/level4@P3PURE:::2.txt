

This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results of the proposed architecture are impressive, and the exposition is exceptionally clear.

The modifications to prior RNN models are clever, well motivated, and experimentally validated:
- Untying leaf and nonterminal nodes.
- Employing rectified linear units within the recursive framework.
- Utilizing large unsupervised word vectors alongside smaller hidden nonterminal nodes.
- Implementing a deep architecture that extends beyond simply replacing a single-layer RNN with a deep layer; instead, it introduces connections between layers and tree nodes. It would have been interesting to compare the scenario where only the output of the last hidden layer at each node serves as input to the parent (i.e., dropping the connection from matrix V).
