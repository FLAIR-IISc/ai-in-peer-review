- Paper proposes deep recursive networks extending recurrent depth to tree-structured spatial hierarchies, using dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings with strong sentiment treebank results.  
- Major concern: fixing word embeddings is unexplained and may hide gains—fine-tuning embeddings is standard and could change conclusions.  
- Need baseline: a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings might outperform the proposed deep model and must be evaluated.  
- Binary classification experiment incorrectly includes neutral examples; must be re-run excluding neutrals to match standard protocol.  
- Omission of Kalchbrenner et al.’s 2014 convolutional sentence model is a significant missing baseline and should be included.  
- Model should be evaluated on root-label-only datasets (e.g., IMDB) to test the utility of spatial depth beyond node-level/tree-structured labels.  
- Notation ambiguity: undefined symbols (W^η, W^{xh}) and unclear whether they indicate left/right or hidden-layer weights—provide explicit equations and parameter dimensions.  
- Clarify the rationale and empirical justification for sharing dropout units across layers.  
- The claim that "stability is not an issue" lacks evidence; compare against weight-norm constraints or gradient clipping to support this.  
- Overall recommendation: idea is promising but current evidence is insufficient to attribute gains to spatial depth due to experimental gaps.  
- Required revisions: re-run binary task excluding neutrals; add Kalchbrenner et al. CNN baseline; test on a root-label-only dataset; include fine-tuned embedding baseline (single-layer RNN); clarify all technical notation and parameter dimensions.
