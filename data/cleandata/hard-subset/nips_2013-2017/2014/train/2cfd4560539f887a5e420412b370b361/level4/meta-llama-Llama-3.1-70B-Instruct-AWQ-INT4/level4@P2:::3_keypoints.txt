- Proposes deep recursive networks adding depth across tree space, with layer-wise parameter sharing across heights.
- Uses dropout and ReLU activations while keeping word vectors fixed to pre-trained embeddings.
- Experiments on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank show very strong performance.
- Method is novel but essentially a generalization of techniques used in deep recurrent nets.
- Performance gains from combining dropout and ReLUs with deep recursive networks are interesting.
- Some model and experimental design choices are questionable or not well justified.
- Paper is clearly written and reproducible overall, though some notation and equation definitions need clarification.
- Technically sound with supported claims, but several justifications and controls are missing.
- Originality limited by prior use of dropout/ReLU and recurrent-net ideas, though their combination here is notable.
- Significance promising for sentiment treebank but additional datasets/experiments are needed to demonstrate broader impact.
- Missing baseline: should include Kalchbrenner et al.’s CNN sentence model from ACL this year.
- Key control missing: authors did not fine-tune word embeddings; this could explain some performance gains and needs to be tested.
- Notation issues: undefined terms (W^eta, W^xh) and parameter/matrix dimensionalities should be clearly specified.
- Need justification for using shared dropout units and the chosen stability techniques.
- Suggest alternative stability methods (weight norm constraints or gradient truncation) as done in recurrent nets.
- Binary classification experiment deviates from protocol by including neutral examples and should be redone per standard protocol.
- Recommend additional experiments on datasets with only global/root labels to test whether deep RNNs help without node-level supervision.
