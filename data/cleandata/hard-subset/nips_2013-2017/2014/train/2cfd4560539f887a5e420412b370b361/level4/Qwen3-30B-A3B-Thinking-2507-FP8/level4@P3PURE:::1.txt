

Paper ID: 1180  
Title: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language  

This paper proposes a deep recursive neural network (DRNN) architecture, constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers, and evaluates it on fine-grained sentiment classification.  

**Clarity**  
The paper is well-structured and clearly written, facilitating easy comprehension of the proposed methodology and results.  

**Quality**  
The technical approach appears sound. The core idea—stacking recursive layers to form a deep architecture—is conceptually straightforward and well-motivated. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with sufficient ablation studies and comparisons to baseline methods.  

**Originality**  
The proposed DRNN framework is simple yet effective. To strengthen the contribution, the authors should expand the discussion to address two key points:  
- *Why deeper networks specifically benefit the target task of fine-grained sentiment analysis?*  
- *What justifies the chosen architectural choices (e.g., layer composition, activation functions)?*  

**Significance**  
The paper presents a promising approach with a clear problem statement, thorough background, and robust experimental validation. The results demonstrate meaningful progress in leveraging compositional structure for sentiment analysis.
