- Paper proposes “deep” recursive networks by increasing depth across tree structure and tying layer parameters across heights.  
- Uses dropout, ReLU activations, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on Socher et al. sentiment treebank.  
- Method is novel as a generalization of deep recurrent nets, and achieves very strong results on the sentiment treebank.  
- Notable empirical gains from dropout and ReLU activations.  
- Several modeling and experimental choices are questionable or insufficiently justified, requiring further experiments before endorsement.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written with enough detail for reproduction.  
- Notation issues: W^eta and W^xh are undefined; recommend separating input vs hidden equations and summarizing parameter set with dimensions.  
- Speculative claims (lines 148–154) need to be stated as empirical observations or supported by evidence.  
- Missing baseline: include Kalchbrenner et al.’s convolutional neural network for sentence modeling (ACL).  
- Major concern: word embeddings are not fine-tuned—this could bias results and should be controlled (a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings might outperform the proposed model).  
- Clarify motivation for shared dropout units (line 251).  
- For stability issues, consider standard fixes like constraining weight norms or gradient clipping (lines 255–256).  
- Binary classification should be evaluated separately using the standard protocol (exclude neutral examples) to ensure comparability to prior work.  
- Test whether deep RNNs help when only a single root label is available (global-label datasets); add 1–2 such datasets and redo binary classification and embedding fine-tuning ablations to strengthen the claim.
