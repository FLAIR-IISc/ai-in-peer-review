This paper introduces a novel and compelling deep recursive neural network architecture, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification benchmark. The results are impressive, and the paper is very well-written and clear.

The proposed modifications to previous recursive neural network models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated. Key strengths include:

1.  **Untied Parameters:** The use of separate parameter matrices for leaf nodes (words) and non-terminal nodes (phrases) is a simple yet effective idea that allows the model to learn distinct compositional functions for initial word embeddings versus intermediate phrasal representations.

2.  **Rectified Linear Units (ReLUs):** The authors successfully apply ReLUS in a recursive setting, showing their effectiveness in this type of architecture.

3.  **Efficient Representation Sizing:** The model insightfully combines large, pre-trained unsupervised word vectors with smaller-dimensional hidden representations for non-terminal nodes. This approach effectively balances rich input semantics with computational efficiency in the compositional layers.

4.  **Novel Deep Architecture:** The deep architecture is a significant contribution. Rather than simply stacking layers, the model introduces connections between corresponding layers of parent and child nodes throughout the parse tree. This creates a more integrated form of deep compositionality that proves highly effective.

**Major Concern:**

My primary concern relates to the experimental methodology. The results presented in Table 1(a) appear to have been generated after tuning hyperparameters on the final test set. This is a serious methodological issue, as it conflates model development with final evaluation and can lead to an overestimation of the model's true generalization performance. All hyperparameter tuning should be conducted strictly on the development set, with the test set used only for a single, final evaluation run. The authors should clarify their procedure and, if necessary, re-evaluate using a proper split.

**Question for the Authors:**

Regarding the novel deep connections, it would be very interesting to see an ablation study on the contribution of the skip-layer connections (i.e., the connections parameterized by matrix V). For instance, an interesting comparison would be a model where each parent node only receives input from the final hidden layer of its children. Such an experiment would help isolate and quantify the benefits of the proposed deep connection scheme versus a more standard layer-stacking approach.
