- Summary: Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks by stacking recursive layers with node-wise inter-layer connections V, untying leaves from internal nodes, using ReLUs and fixed pretrained word vectors; evaluated on SST with qualitative analyses.

- Key architectural choices: stack recursive layers with shared WL/WR and vertical V connections; untie leaf/internal representations; use ReLUs and fixed large pretrained embeddings with smaller nonterminal hidden states.

- Empirical result: Achieves new state-of-the-art on SST (5-way and binary) versus RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, and other baselines under a fixed parameter budget.

- Qualitative analysis: Layer-wise analyses (input perturbations, nearest-neighbor phrases) suggest different layers capture different compositional aspects.

- Strength — exposition: Clear, well-organized writing and figures that make the architecture easy to understand.

- Strength — practical modifications: Simple changes (untying leaves, ReLUs, fixed pretrained vectors, stacking with V) are effective in practice.

- Strength — training: Technically sound implementation with appropriate training details (ReLUs, dropout, AdaGrad, initialization).

- Weakness — evaluation protocol: Model selection appears to have been done on the test set (Table 1a); must use dev set and reserve test for final evaluation.

- Weakness — ablation: Missing comparison to a no-V variant to isolate the effect of inter-layer node-wise connections versus depth alone.

- Weakness — minor issues: Small typos (e.g., “comprise a class of architecture” → “architectures”).

- Originality: Novel and clean extension of recursive models by stacking layers over trees with explicit inter-layer connections; untying leaves to enable ReLUs is a practical innovation.

- Significance: Demonstrates that added depth improves compositional modeling and achieves SOTA on a widely used benchmark, likely to influence future structured deep models.

- Pros for acceptance: New well-motivated architecture, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, and insightful qualitative analyses.

- Cons for acceptance: Test-set tuning concern and missing no-V ablation weaken the claims and should be addressed.

- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with compelling idea and results; resolve evaluation protocol and add the suggested comparison to strengthen the claims.
