- Introduces a deep Recursive Neural Network that stacks multiple recursive layers to increase non-linear capacity for compositional language representations.  
- Demonstrates efficacy on fine-grained sentiment classification using the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and the deep RNN idea is elegant and intuitive.  
- Method appears technically sound, with comprehensive experiments (impact of depth, visualization of representations).  
- Novelty: a simple, reasonable extension of recursive models that meaningfully improves performance.  
- Suggestion: provide stronger intuition for why depth helps in NLP, with qualitative examples (e.g., negation, sarcasm, nested clauses).  
- Suggestion: discuss and justify architectural design choices and alternatives (connectivity patterns, non-linearities).  
- Overall: significant and interesting contribution; addressing the above points would further strengthen the paper.
