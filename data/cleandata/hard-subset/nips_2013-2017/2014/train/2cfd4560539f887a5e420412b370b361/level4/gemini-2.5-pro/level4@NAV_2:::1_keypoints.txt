- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Network: stacking multiple recursive layers to build hierarchical, more abstract compositional representations.  
- Evaluation on Stanford Sentiment Treebank shows Deep RNN outperforms shallow counterparts and previous state-of-the-art.  
- Technical quality: model is well-formulated and experiments are thorough with quantitative and qualitative analyses supporting the central claims.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written, organized, and motivates depth-in-space for recursive models clearly with easy-to-follow model descriptions and figures.  
- Originality: novel and intuitive contribution—first systematic exploration of stacking recursive layers vs simply increasing width.  
- Significance: achieves new state-of-the-art and shows depth is more beneficial than width under a fixed parameter budget, offering impactful insights for compositional models.  
- Analysis strength: input perturbation and nearest-neighbor analyses substantiate that deeper layers learn increasingly abstract representations.  
- Suggestion: justify or empirically compare the architectural choice to connect only the final hidden layer to the output versus connecting all layers.  
- Suggestion: further investigate why performance degrades past a certain depth—consider optimization issues or vanishing gradients in addition to reduced layer width.
