- Introduces a compelling deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a sentiment classification dataset; paper is very well written.  
- Proposes untied parameters for leaf nodes versus nonterminal nodes.  
- Employs rectified linear units (ReLUs) within the recursive architecture.  
- Uses large pre-trained word vectors for inputs and smaller hidden representations for nonterminal nodes.  
- Implements depth via cross-layer and tree-node connections rather than by stacking layers.  
- Suggests an informative ablation: remove the composition matrix V so parent input comes only from children's final hidden layer.  
- Major methodological concern: Table 1(a) appears to report test-set results after tuning on that same test set — improper; such results should be reported on the development set.  
- Minor issues: several typos (e.g., "classificationd").
