- Introduces a deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers for fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented with a motivating introduction linking depth benefits in NLP.  
- Methodology is comprehensively described and the experimental setup is well-defined.  
- Technically sound: deep RNN is a logical extension of existing recursive frameworks.  
- Training considerations (dropout, L2) are included, showing careful implementation.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using several baselines on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Results show deep RNNs outperform shallow models and achieve new state-of-the-art performance.  
- Core idea is simple and reasonable for capturing hierarchical compositional semantics.  
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbations, nearest-neighbor phrases) provide useful insights into learned representations.  
- Could benefit from more detailed justification for architectural choices and why depth helps this task.  
- Needs broader exploration of alternative architectures and deeper qualitative analysis to better understand representations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear contribution, thorough evaluation, and relevance to compositionality in NLP.  
- Arguments against acceptance: architectural novelty is relatively straightforward and the analysis could be expanded.
