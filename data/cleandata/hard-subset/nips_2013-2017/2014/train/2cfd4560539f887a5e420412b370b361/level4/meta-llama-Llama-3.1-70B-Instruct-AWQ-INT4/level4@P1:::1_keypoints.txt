- Proposes a deep recursive neural network (stacking multiple recursive layers) for compositionality in language.  
- Applies the architecture to fine-grained sentiment classification using binary parse trees.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear descriptions of architecture and experiments.  
- Minor clarity issue: acronym “RNN” not defined early enough.  
- Technically sound with reasonable experimental evaluation.  
- The core idea is simple and reasonable but lacks deeper intuition for why deeper networks help this task.  
- The choice of specific architecture elements (e.g., ReLU, stacking method) requires more justification.  
- Provides layer-level analysis (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) but this analysis could be more detailed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, clear exposition, strong empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of intuition and limited analysis of layer operations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address intuition and architectural justification.
