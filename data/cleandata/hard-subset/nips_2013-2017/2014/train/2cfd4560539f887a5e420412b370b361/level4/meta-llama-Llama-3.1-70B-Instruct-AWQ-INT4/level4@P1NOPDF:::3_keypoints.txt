- Proposes deep recursive networks by adding depth across space with layer-wise parameter sharing; tested on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Uses dropout and ReLUs and keeps word embeddings fixed (pre-trained) during training.  
- Method is novel and a natural generalization of deep recurrent nets; achieves strong performance on the dataset.  
- Gains from dropout and ReLUs are notable.  
- Some model and experimental choices are not well-justified and require additional experiments before acceptance.  
- Paper is mostly clear and reproducible, with sufficient implementation detail.  
- Missing definitions for W^eta and W^xh and no summary of parameter dimensionalities (WL, WR, U, b, c).  
- Section (lines 148–154) reads speculative and should indicate whether it’s empirically supported.  
- Should compare against Kalchbrenner et al.’s sentence CNN from ACL as a baseline.  
- Surprising choice not to fine-tune word embeddings; this could explain improvements and must be controlled for (single-layer RNN + fine-tuned embeddings baseline).  
- Motivation for using shared dropout units is not explained.  
- For training stability, suggest also trying weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation as in recurrent nets.  
- Binary classification protocol deviates from standard (neutrals included in training); results are not directly comparable and binary experiment should be redone separately.  
- Unclear if deep RNNs help when only a single root/global label exists; recommend testing on 1–2 global-label datasets.  
- Recommended experiments: redo proper binary protocol, study fine-tuning embeddings, and add datasets with only global labels to strengthen claims.
