- Paper proposes deep recursive neural networks that add depth across parse-tree spatial dimensions with shared parameters and uses dropout, ReLU, and pre-trained embeddings on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

- Approach is novel and a logical generalization of recurrent-depth to tree structures; reported performance is strong and gains from dropout/ReLU are notable.

- Paper is well-written and reproducible, but several modeling and experimental choices lack sufficient justification.

- Notation is unclear (e.g., W^eta, W^xh); separate equations for input/hidden transforms and a concise parameter/dimensionality summary are needed.

- Claims about limitations of shallow models are speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence.

- Missing a key baseline: comparison to Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) convolutional sentence model is required.

- Decision not to fine-tune word embeddings is questionable; fine-tuning may capture sentiment-specific signals and confound depth benefits.

- Include a single-layer recursive network with fine-tuned embeddings as a control to isolate depth effects from embedding tuning.

- Motivation for using shared dropout units across layers is unclear and should be explained.

- Consider and discuss alternative stabilization/regularization techniques (e.g., weight norm constraints, gradient clipping).

- Binary classification protocol is incorrect (neutral class included); redo by excluding neutrals in training to match standard practice.

- Evaluate generalization beyond node-level labels by testing whether depth helps when only global/root labels are available.

- Recommendation: redo binary experiment properly, test fine-tuning embeddings, and run 1–2 additional global-label datasets to strengthen evidence for spatial depth benefits.
