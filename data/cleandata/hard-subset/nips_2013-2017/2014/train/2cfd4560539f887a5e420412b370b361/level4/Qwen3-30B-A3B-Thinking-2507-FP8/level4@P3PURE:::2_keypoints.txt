- Exciting paper introducing a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art sentiment classification and is very clearly written.
- Untying leaf and nonterminal node parameters is a clever, well-motivated modification.
- Using rectified linear units (ReLUs) within the recursive framework is an effective change.
- Combining large unsupervised word vectors with smaller hidden nonterminal nodes improves performance.
- The deep architecture adds inter-layer and tree-node connections beyond simply stacking layers; an ablation comparing removal of the V matrix (only last hidden layer passed to parent) would be informative.
- The proposed modifications are experimentally validated with impressive results.
- Table 1(a) appears to report results on the test set (test-set tuning); results should be reported on the development set instead.
