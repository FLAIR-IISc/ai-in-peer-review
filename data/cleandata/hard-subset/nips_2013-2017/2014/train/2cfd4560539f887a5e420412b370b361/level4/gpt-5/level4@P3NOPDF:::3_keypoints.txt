- Summary: Proposes “deep” recursive nets by adding spatial (tree-depth) layers with layer-specific shared parameters, uses ReLUs/dropout/fixed embeddings, and shows strong SST results.

- Strength: Clear motivation and intuitive generalization of deep recurrent nets to trees.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains on SST; ReLUs and dropout appear beneficial.

- Strength: Writing and procedural detail are generally clear and reproducible.

- Concern: Conceptual novelty is incremental; stronger empirical validation needed.

- Concern: Several modeling and experimental choices lack justification (e.g., fixed embeddings, shared dropout masks).

- Concern: Notation is unclear and protocol deviations (binary task) undermine comparability.

- Notation/detail request: Clarify undefined symbols (W^η, W^xh), separate bottom-layer vs inter-layer equations, and list full parameter set with dimensionalities.

- Speculation: Remove or support speculative claims (lines 148–154) with empirical evidence or soften wording.

- Missing baseline: Include Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN results to situate performance among strong contemporaries.

- Embedding fine-tuning: Test fine-tuning embeddings and include controls: single-layer w/ fine-tuning, deep model w/ fine-tuning, and current fixed setup to isolate depth effects.

- Dropout clarification: Explain whether dropout masks are shared across nodes/layers, report per-layer dropout rates, and provide ablation vs independent masks.

- Training stability: Report use (or try) standard techniques like weight-norm constraints and gradient clipping for robustness/comparability.

- Binary protocol: Retrain and report strictly according to SST binary train/dev/test splits; separate from fine-grained results.

- Supervision granularity: Evaluate root-only supervision (or root-only SST variant) to test whether spatial depth helps with sparse labels.

- Model capacity/efficiency: Report parameter counts, training/inference time per depth, scaling of gains with layers, and discussion of overfitting vs capacity.

- Ablations: Provide a table quantifying contributions of ReLU vs other activations, dropout, and depth.

- Implementation details: Document optimizer, learning-rate schedule, initialization, gradient clipping thresholds, early stopping, L2 regularization, and dev set usage; release code/config if possible.

- Error analysis: Include qualitative examples where deeper layers correct shallower errors to illustrate what depth captures.

- Recommendation: Paper is promising but borderline; acceptability requires adding the requested baselines, standard binary protocol results, embedding fine-tuning studies (including strong single-layer baseline), and at least one root-only evaluation.
