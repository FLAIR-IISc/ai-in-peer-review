This paper presents a novel deep recursive neural architecture that delivers state-of-the-art performance on a challenging fine-grained sentiment classification dataset. The empirical improvements are compelling, and the presentation is very clear.

The proposed modifications to prior recursive/RNN models are thoughtful, well-motivated, and empirically validated:
- disentangling leaf nodes from internal (nonterminal) nodes
- using rectified linear units in the recursive setting
- leveraging large pretrained word vectors while using smaller hidden dimensions for internal nodes
- introducing depth by stacking recursive layers with explicit inter-layer connections tied to tree nodes, rather than merely replacing a single recursive layer with a deeper block. It would be informative to include a comparison where only the final hidden layer at each node feeds the parent (i.e., ablate the V connection).

Table 1(a) should report development-set performance; as written, it appears that choices are being made based on the final test set, which amounts to tuning on the test set.
