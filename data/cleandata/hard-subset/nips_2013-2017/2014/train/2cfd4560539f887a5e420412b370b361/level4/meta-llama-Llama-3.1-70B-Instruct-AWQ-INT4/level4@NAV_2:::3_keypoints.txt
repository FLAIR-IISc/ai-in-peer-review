- Proposes "deep" recursive neural networks by adding depth across tree space with layer-wise parameter sharing.
- Uses dropout and ReLUs and keeps word representations fixed to pre-trained embeddings.
- Experiments are conducted on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank.
- Method is novel but is essentially a generalization of approaches used in recurrent nets.
- Authors report very strong performance on the sentiment treebank.
- Performance gains from applying dropout and ReLUs are notable.
- Paper is mostly clearly written and reproducible from provided details.
- Some notation and terminology (e.g., W^eta, W^xh) are undefined or unclear and need clarification and dimensionalities.
- Several model and experimental design choices are questionable or not well justified.
- Additional experiments are necessary before full acceptance can be recommended.
- Missing baseline: include results from Kalchbrenner et al.'s ACL CNN sentence model.
- Question why word vectors were not fine-tuned; this could confound gains attributed to deep RNNs.
- Binary classification setup is improper (neutral class included during training) and should follow standard protocol.
- Motivation for using shared dropout units is not explained.
- Stability concerns could be addressed by weight norm constraints or gradient truncation, not discussed.
- The deep-RNN vs. single-layer advantage should be tested on tasks with only a global/root label and on additional datasets.
- Recommend redoing binary classification per protocol, testing fine-tuning of embeddings, and adding 1–2 root-label-only datasets.
