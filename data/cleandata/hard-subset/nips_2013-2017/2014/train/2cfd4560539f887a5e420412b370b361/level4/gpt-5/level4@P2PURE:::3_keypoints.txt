- Paper proposes “deep” recursive networks with layer-wise shared parameters, dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings, evaluated on the Socher sentiment treebank.  
- The method is novel (a generalization of recurrent networks) and shows strong performance; dropout and ReLUs yield notable gains.  
- Several modeling and experimental choices are questionable or insufficiently justified, requiring additional experiments before acceptance.  
- Paper is largely clear and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Notation issues: W^eta and W^xh are undefined; recommend separate equations for inputs vs. hidden layers and a summarized parameter set with matrix dimensionalities.  
- Section (lines 148–154) reads speculative; ask for explicit empirical support if available.  
- Missing baseline: include results from Kalchbrenner et al.’s CNN (ACL this year).  
- Word embeddings were not fine-tuned; this is odd for a sentiment task and could confound results—fine-tuning should be tested as a control.  
- Unclear motivation for shared dropout units (line 251); request justification.  
- For stability, recommend alternatives like constraining weight norms or gradient clipping (common in RNNs).  
- Binary classification was not evaluated separately and including neutral examples deviates from standard protocol, harming comparability.  
- Question whether deep RNNs still help when only a single root label is available; request experiments on datasets with only global labels.  
- Recommended additional experiments: (1) redo binary classification per standard protocol, (2) analyze effect of fine-tuning word embeddings, and (3) add 1–2 datasets with only global labels to strengthen evidence for spatial depth.
