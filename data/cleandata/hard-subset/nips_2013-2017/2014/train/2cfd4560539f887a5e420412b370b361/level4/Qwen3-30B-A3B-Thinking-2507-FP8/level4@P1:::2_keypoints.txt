- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks recursive layers to better capture compositional meaning.  
- Addresses limitation of conventional RNNs being structurally deep but lacking hierarchical representation by adding stacked layers and inter-layer matrix V connections.  
- Introduces an untied variant that separates leaf (word) and internal (phrase) representations, enabling rectifier activations.  
- Shows using large pretrained word vectors with smaller hidden units avoids quadratic dependence and yields more efficient models.  
- Architecture explicitly connects intermediate layer outputs via matrix V, not just deepening a single layer, allowing richer information flow.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and achieves new state-of-the-art results; deep RNNs outperform shallow RNNs with same parameter count (e.g., 49.5% vs. 48.1%).  
- Provides thorough ablation studies and exploratory analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrase) showing different layers capture distinct compositional aspects.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, reproducible, and properly situated relative to prior work (RNTN, multiplicative RNNs, paragraph vectors).  
- Major experimental concern: possible test-set leakage—authors must clarify whether dropout/model selection used only the development set or inadvertently used the test set.  
- Suggests adding a comparison to the alternative design that passes only the final hidden layer (i.e., removing V connections) to justify architectural choices.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution that meets NIPS quality criteria.
