Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a novel architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and pleasant to read. The authors have done a good job of presenting their ideas in a clear and concise manner.

Quality:
The paper appears to be technically sound. The major contribution, the deep recursive neural network, is an intriguing idea that involves stacking multiple layers of individual recursive nets. The authors provide a reasonable amount of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method.

Originality:
The proposed idea is simple yet reasonable. However, it would be beneficial to include more discussions on the following points:
+ An intuitive explanation for why a deeper network is better suited for the target task would be helpful.
+ A justification for the chosen network architecture would add more depth to the paper.

Significance:
The paper presents an interesting idea, provides a clear background description, and offers reasonable experimental support. Overall, the authors have made a compelling case for the effectiveness of their proposed model.
