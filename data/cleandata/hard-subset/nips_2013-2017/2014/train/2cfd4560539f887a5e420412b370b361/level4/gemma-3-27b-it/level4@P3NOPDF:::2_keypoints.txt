- Novel, exciting deep recursive neural network that achieves state-of-the-art sentiment classification performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.  
- Untying leaf and nonterminal nodes provides greater flexibility and representational power.  
- Successfully adapts rectified linear units (ReLUs) to the recursive setting, improving training.  
- Uses large pre-trained word vectors while keeping smaller nonterminal hidden sizes to reduce overfitting and improve generalization.  
- Introduces a truly deep recursive architecture with inter-layer and inter-node connections, enabling richer feature interactions.  
- Suggests an important ablation: compare to a variant where each parent receives only the last hidden layer outputs of children (remove direct V connection) to isolate benefits of inter-layer connectivity.  
- Critical methodological flaw: results in Table 1(a) appear reported on the final test set (model effectively tuned on test), which biases performance estimates; results should be reported on a development set.  
- Minor typographical errors to fix (“comprise a class of architecture” and “classificationd” → “classification”).  
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative paper with demonstrable impact, contingent on correcting the test-set reporting issue.
