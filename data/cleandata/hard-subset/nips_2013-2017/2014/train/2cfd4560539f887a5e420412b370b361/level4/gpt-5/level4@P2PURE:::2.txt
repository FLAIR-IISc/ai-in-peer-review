This is an exciting paper that introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results are impressive and the exposition is very clear.

The proposed modifications to prior RNN-style models are clever, well motivated, and empirically validated:
- Untying parameters for leaf nodes versus nonterminal nodes
- Using rectified linear units in the recursive setting
- Employing large unsupervised word vectors while keeping smaller hidden sizes for nonterminal nodes
- Adopting a deep architecture that does more than simply replace a single RNN layer with a deeper one by introducing connections both across layers and along the tree structure. It would be an informative comparison to evaluate a variant where only the output of the last hidden layer at each node is passed to its parent, i.e., dropping the connection from matrix V.

One concern: the results in Table 1(a) should be reported on a development set rather than the final test set. As presented, this amounts to tuning on the test set.
