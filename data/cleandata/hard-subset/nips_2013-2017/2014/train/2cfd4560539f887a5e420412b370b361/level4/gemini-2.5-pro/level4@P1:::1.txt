This paper introduces the Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN), a novel architecture constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. Drawing an analogy to deep recurrent networks that distinguish between depth in time and depth in space, this work proposes a similar distinction for recursive models between depth in structure (from recursion) and depth in space (from stacking). The authors apply this model to the task of fine-grained sentiment classification on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank. Their empirical results demonstrate that deep, narrow recursive networks outperform their shallow, wide counterparts with a similar number of parameters. Furthermore, the proposed model achieves new state-of-the-art results on this benchmark, surpassing previous methods including the Recursive Neural Tensor Network (RNTN) and Paragraph Vectors.

**Clarity**
The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, making the proposed architecture and experimental setup easy to understand. The motivation, methodology, and results are presented in a logical and accessible manner.

**Quality**
The paper is technically sound. The proposed Deep RNN architecture is well-defined, and the experimental methodology is rigorous. The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation that includes a fair comparison against several strong baselines. The design choices, such as untying leaf and internal nodes and using modern regularization techniques like dropout, are well-motivated and their effectiveness is demonstrated. The qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest neighbor analysis) are particularly strong, providing valuable insights into the model's inner workings and supporting the central claim that different layers learn different aspects of composition.

**Originality**
The central idea of creating a deep recursive network by stacking layers is an elegant and intuitive extension of existing work on both recursive and recurrent neural networks. While the concept of "depth" is not new, its specific application to recursive models in this stacked manner is novel.

However, the paper could be strengthened with more discussion on the following points:
*   While the model is well-motivated by analogy to other deep architectures, a more in-depth discussion of the linguistic intuition would be beneficial. Why should a multi-stage, hierarchical composition function be superior to a single, more complex function for a task like sentiment analysis? Expanding on the theoretical or cognitive reasons why depth is beneficial for composing sentiment would add significant value.
*   The justification for some architectural choices could be expanded. For example, the decision to connect the output layer only to the final hidden layer is explained as a way to facilitate analysis. It would be helpful to include a brief discussion of the alternative (e.g., connecting to all layers) and why the chosen approach is preferable, perhaps with a comparative experiment on the development set, to further solidify this design decision.

**Significance**
This work makes a significant contribution to the field. The paper presents a novel architecture that demonstrably advances the state of the art on a challenging and widely used benchmark. The thorough experimental evaluation, including the insightful qualitative analyses, provides compelling evidence for the benefits of adding depth to recursive models. The finding that different layers learn a hierarchy of representations is an important result that other researchers are likely to build upon, not only in sentiment analysis but also in other NLP tasks requiring an understanding of semantic compositionality.

**Summary of Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Strengths:**
*   Presents a novel and intuitive architecture, the Deep RNN, which is a natural extension of prior work.
*   Provides a thorough and convincing experimental evaluation on a standard benchmark, achieving state-of-the-art results.
*   Includes excellent qualitative analyses that offer insights into how the model works and support the claim that depth creates a representational hierarchy.
*   The paper is very clearly written and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The paper could benefit from a deeper discussion of the linguistic or cognitive intuition for why a stacked, hierarchical model is particularly well-suited for sentiment composition.
*   Further justification for specific architectural choices (e.g., connecting the classifier only to the top layer) would strengthen the paper.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper presenting a novel and effective architecture with strong empirical support. It makes a clear and significant contribution to deep learning for natural language processing.
