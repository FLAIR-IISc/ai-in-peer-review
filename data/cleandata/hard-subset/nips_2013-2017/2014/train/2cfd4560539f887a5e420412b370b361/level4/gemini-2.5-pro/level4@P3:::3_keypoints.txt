- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Network: stack recursive layers to add spatial depth for compositional language modeling.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank using ReLUs and dropout with fixed pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results; deeper narrower networks outperform shallower wider ones with similar parameter counts.  
- Qualitative analyses suggest lower layers capture local/word-level features while higher layers capture abstract sentiment semantics.  

- Strength: novel, well-motivated extension of recursive nets by analogy to deep recurrent models.  
- Strength: strong quantitative results and ablation evidence supporting the benefit of spatial depth.  
- Strength: paper is generally clear and provides sufficient training/hyperparameter details for reproduction.  
- Strength: insightful exploratory analyses indicating a hierarchy of learned representations.  

- Weakness: word embeddings were kept fixed; lack of fine-tuning control may confound claimed benefits of depth.  
- Weakness: binary classification protocol is incorrect (trained with neutrals, evaluated after removing them), making comparisons unfair.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to SST with supervision at every parse node — unclear if benefits generalize to typical sentence-level labels.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to a key contemporaneous baseline (DCNN, Kalchbrenner et al. 2014).  

- Comment: notation in Equation 3 is ambiguous; selection mechanism and matrix dimensionalities should be made explicit.  
- Comment: claim that untying leaf/internal weights helps with ReLUs is speculative and needs empirical evidence or citations.  
- Comment: motivation for using the same dropout mask per sentence (shared across nodes) is not justified.  
- Comment: instead of L2 on activations for stability, consider standard alternatives like gradient clipping or weight-norm constraints.  

- Conclusion: promising architecture but conclusions are weakened by missing controls (embedding fine-tuning), incorrect protocol, and limited dataset scope; additional experiments are recommended.
