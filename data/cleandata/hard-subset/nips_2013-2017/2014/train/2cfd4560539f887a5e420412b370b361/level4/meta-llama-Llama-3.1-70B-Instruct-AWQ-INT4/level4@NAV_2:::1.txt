Paper For paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a novel architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), which is constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

The main idea of this paper is related to previous work on recursive neural networks (RNNs) and their application to natural language processing tasks. RNNs have been successfully used to model compositionality in language using parse-tree-based structural representations. However, these architectures lack the capacity for hierarchical representation that exists in conventional deep feed-forward networks and deep recurrent neural networks. The authors propose a deep RNN to address this limitation.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The background description and related work are clearly presented, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their proposed architecture.

The technical soundness of the paper is evident in the clear and concise presentation of the deep RNN architecture and its application to the task of fine-grained sentiment classification. The authors provide reasonable amounts of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method, including comparisons with previous work and an analysis of the effect of multiple layers.

The proposed idea is simple and reasonable, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their intuition behind the deep RNN architecture. However, it might be beneficial to include more discussions on why a deeper network is intuitively good for the target task and why the selected network architecture was chosen.

The paper presents an interesting idea, clear background description, and reasonable experimental supports. The results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size and achieve state-of-the-art performance on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification. The qualitative experiments suggest that each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity.

Overall, this paper is a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing and deep learning. The proposed deep RNN architecture has the potential to be applied to a wide range of NLP tasks, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness in modeling compositionality in language.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel architecture that addresses a limitation of previous recursive neural networks.
* The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of the deep RNN architecture and its application to the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture in modeling compositionality in language.
* The paper presents an interesting idea, clear background description, and reasonable experimental supports.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from more discussions on the intuition behind the deep RNN architecture and why it is well-suited for the target task.
* The authors could provide more analysis on the effect of multiple layers and how they capture different aspects of compositionality.
* The paper could be improved by including more comparisons with previous work and a more detailed analysis of the results.
