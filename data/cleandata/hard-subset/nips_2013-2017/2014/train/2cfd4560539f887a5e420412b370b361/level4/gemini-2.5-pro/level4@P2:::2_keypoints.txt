- Introduces a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks recursive layers so each parse-node receives input from children and the corresponding node in the layer below.  
- Evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and outperforms shallow counterparts, achieving new state-of-the-art results.  
- Qualitative analyses suggest lower layers capture local/syntactic phenomena while higher layers capture more abstract semantic/sentiment information.  
- Strength: original and well-motivated idea that combines recursive structure with deep hierarchical representation learning.  
- Strength: strong technical contributions—untie leaf and internal node spaces to use large pre-trained word vectors without parameter explosion.  
- Strength: effective application of modern practices (ReLUs, dropout, AdaGrad) adapted to the recursive setting.  
- Strength: useful qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest neighbors) that support the claim about layer-wise specialization.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written, well-organized, and explains the model and experiments precisely.  
- Major weakness: apparent model selection on the test set (choosing optimal depth by test accuracy) risks overfitting and inflating reported performance; model selection should use the development set and report a single final test score.  
- Weakness: missing ablation study on the inter-layer connection (matrix V) to isolate the benefit of the "depth in space" links.  
- Minor issue: typographical error in the abstract—“comprise a class of architecture” should be corrected.
