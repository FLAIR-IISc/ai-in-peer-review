- Introduces a deep recursive neural network extending the notion of depth to tree-structured (spatial) models with depth-specific parameters shared across nodes.  
- Uses ReLUs, dropout, and static pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Claims novelty and reports state-of-the-art results; finds performance gains from using dropout and ReLUs.  
- Several design and experimental choices lack sufficient justification and require further experimentation before acceptance.  
- Manuscript is well-written and provides enough detail for reproduction.  
- Notation is unclear for W^eta and W^xh; authors should provide separate input-to-hidden and hidden-to-hidden equations and a complete parameter/dimension summary.  
- The argument in lines 148–154 is speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence.  
- Missing an important baseline comparison to Kalchbrenner et al., "A convolutional neural network for modelling sentences" (ACL 2014).  
- Rationale for not fine-tuning word embeddings is unclear; need control experiments (e.g., single-layer model with fine-tuned embeddings) to rule out artifact effects.  
- Need to justify sharing dropout units across the network.  
- For stability, consider standard recurrent-network techniques (weight-norm constraints, gradient clipping) as alternatives to the current approach.  
- Binary classification experiment includes neutral examples and is not comparable to prior work; redo under the standard protocol.  
- Core motivation (analogy to deep recurrent nets) is strongest when predicting at every node; authors should test whether depth benefits hold for root-only prediction tasks and on additional datasets.  
- Recommended next steps: redo binary experiment per protocol, analyze the impact of fine-tuning embeddings, and evaluate on one or two additional single-label datasets to strengthen the paper’s claims.
