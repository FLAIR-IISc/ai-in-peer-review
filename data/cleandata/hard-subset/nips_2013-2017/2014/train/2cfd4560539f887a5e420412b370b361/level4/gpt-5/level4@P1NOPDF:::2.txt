This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results are impressive, and the exposition is very clear.

The proposed modifications to prior RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and empirically validated:
- Untying parameters between leaf nodes and nonterminal nodes.
- Using rectified linear units in the recursive setting.
- Leveraging large unsupervised word vectors while using smaller hidden sizes for nonterminal nodes.
- Employing a deep architecture that does not simply replace a single RNN layer with a deep layer, but also introduces connections between layers and tree nodes. It would be an interesting comparison to consider a variant where the output of the last hidden layer at each node is the only input to its parent (i.e., drop the connection from matrix V).
