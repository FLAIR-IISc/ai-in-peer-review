- Proposes a novel “deep” recursive neural network that extends depth to tree structures by introducing spatially layered parameters shared across nodes in each layer.  
- Uses dropout and ReLU activations while keeping input word embeddings fixed to pre-trained vectors; evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Results show very strong performance on SST and useful analysis of gains from dropout and ReLUs.  
- Contribution is novel but can be seen as a natural generalization of deepening recurrent networks.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Notation is unclear (W^eta, W^xh undefined); authors should explicitly define layer-specific matrices and summarize the full parameter space and dimensions.  
- A speculative argument (lines 148–154) lacks empirical support and should be clarified or substantiated.  
- Missing a relevant baseline: Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) convolutional sentence model should be included.  
- Decision not to fine-tune word vectors is insufficiently justified; fine-tuning may affect results and could explain improvements—need control experiments (e.g., single-layer model with fine-tuned embeddings).  
- Motivation for sharing dropout units across nodes is not explained and should be elaborated.  
- Questions about model stability—authors should consider or report alternatives like weight-norm constraints or gradient truncation used in recurrent training.  
- Binary classification experiment deviates from standard protocol by including neutral examples in training; results are thus not directly comparable and should be redone per established protocol.  
- Unclear whether depth advantages generalize to tasks with only a single root-level label; additional experiments are needed to test global-label settings.  
- Recommended experiments to strengthen the paper: redo binary protocol correctly, study effect of fine-tuning embeddings, and include 1–2 additional datasets with only global labels.
