- Introduces a new deep recursive neural network architecture by stacking multiple recursive layers.  
- Evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification, achieving new state-of-the-art results.  
- Provides a clear, concise background on recursive nets and limitations of existing architectures.  
- Motivates the approach by relating benefits of deep feedforward and recurrent networks.  
- Well-organized paper with thorough methodology (including an untied variant) and experimental setup.  
- Figures and tables effectively illustrate the architecture and results.  
- Technically sound with reasonable experimental support and comparisons to baselines.  
- Results show deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size and prior baselines.  
- Offers qualitative analyses suggesting different layers capture different aspects of compositionality.  
- Idea is simple, reasonable, and intuitively motivated, but intuition for why depth helps is underdeveloped.  
- Lacks sufficient explanation for the specific architectural choices made.  
- Needs more comparisons with other deep learning architectures.  
- Overall well-written and promising; reviewer rating 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
