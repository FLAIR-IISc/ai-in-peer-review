- Paper proposes “deep” recursive neural nets: multiple recursive layers with layer-wise parameter sharing, ReLU/dropout, fixed pretrained word embeddings, evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).
- Novel as an extension of deep recurrent ideas to tree-structured models and obtains strong SST results, with notable gains from dropout and ReLUs.
- Overall clarity and reproducibility are good.
- Several modeling and experimental choices lack motivation and additional controls are needed before strong acceptance can be recommended.
- Notation is unclear (e.g., W^η, W^xh); recommend separate equations for leaf-to-hidden vs. hidden-to-hidden, explicit parameter set, and matrix dimensionalities.
- Some claims (lines 148–154) are speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence or rephrased.
- Add the Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN baseline for completeness and comparability.
- Justify not fine-tuning word embeddings; compare to a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings since fixed embeddings may inflate the apparent benefit of depth.
- Explain the choice to use the same dropout mask across all nodes in a layer for a sentence or provide an ablation comparing shared vs. independent masks.
- Consider additional stabilization techniques (e.g., weight norm constraints, gradient clipping) beyond small L2 penalties on weights and activations.
- Binary sentiment evaluation should follow the established protocol and be trained separately (exclude neutrals); also test whether deep RNNs help when only root/sentence-level labels are available and add 1–2 sentence-only datasets.
