This paper introduces a compelling new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results are impressive and the paper is exceptionally well-written.

The authors propose several clever and well-motivated modifications to previous recursive neural network models, and their efficacy is demonstrated experimentally:
- Untying the parameters for leaf nodes and nonterminal nodes.
- Employing rectified linear units (ReLUs) within the recursive architecture.
- Utilizing large, pre-trained word vectors for input while using smaller hidden representations for nonterminal nodes.
- Implementing a deep architecture where depth is achieved not by simply stacking layers, but by introducing connections between different layers and the tree nodes.

Regarding the deep architecture, an interesting comparison would have been to evaluate a model where the input to a parent node is solely the output of its children's final hidden layer (i.e., dropping the connection from the composition matrix V). This ablation would help clarify the contribution of these specific cross-layer connections.

A significant concern is the experimental methodology for the results in Table 1(a). It appears these results are reported on the final test set, which was also used for hyperparameter tuning. This is improper experimental practice, as it can lead to an overestimation of performance. These results should have been reported on the development set.

Finally, there are several minor typos (e.g., "classificationd" in the introduction).
