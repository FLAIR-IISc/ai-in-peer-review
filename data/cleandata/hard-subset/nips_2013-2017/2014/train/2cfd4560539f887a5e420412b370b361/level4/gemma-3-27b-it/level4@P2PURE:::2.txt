This paper presents an exciting new deep recursive neural network architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. 
The results obtained with this architecture are impressive, and the paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
The authors’ modifications to previous recurrent neural network (RNN) models are insightful, well-motivated, and demonstrably effective through experimentation:
- disentangling the weights of leaf and non-terminal nodes;
- employing rectified linear units within the recursive framework;
- leveraging large, pre-trained word vectors in conjunction with smaller hidden representations for non-terminal nodes;
- implementing a genuinely deep architecture, extending beyond a simple replacement of RNN layers with deep layers by introducing connections between layers and tree nodes. It would be valuable to compare this approach to one where only the output of the last hidden layer at each node is used as input to its parent, effectively removing the connection mediated by matrix V.
