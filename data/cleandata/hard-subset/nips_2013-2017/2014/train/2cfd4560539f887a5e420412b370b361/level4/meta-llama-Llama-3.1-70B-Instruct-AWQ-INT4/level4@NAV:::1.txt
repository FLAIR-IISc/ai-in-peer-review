Review of Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This manuscript proposes a novel deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture, comprising multiple stacked recursive layers, and assesses its performance on fine-grained sentiment classification tasks.

Clarity
- Overall, the paper is well-structured and engaging, making it an enjoyable read.

Quality
- The technical aspects of the paper appear sound.
- The core concept of the deep RNN, achieved by stacking individual recursive nets, is noteworthy.
- The authors provide a sufficient amount of experimental results to evaluate various facets of their method.

Originality
- The proposed concept is straightforward and reasonable.
- To enhance the paper's impact, it would be beneficial to include more in-depth discussions on:
  + The intuitive advantages of deeper networks for the target task.
  + The rationale behind the chosen network architecture.

Significance
- The paper presents an intriguing idea, accompanied by a clear background description and reasonable experimental support.
- The authors successfully demonstrate the potential of their proposed architecture through comprehensive experimental evaluations and a well-grounded theoretical foundation.
