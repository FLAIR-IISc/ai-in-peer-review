- Introduces deep recursive neural networks by stacking multiple recursive layers to add "depth in space" to recursive architectures.  
- Motivated by deep recurrent networks and applied to fine-grained sentiment analysis on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST).  
- Main contributions: a novel stacked-recursive architecture and empirical evidence that deeper models outperform shallow ones with comparable parameter counts, achieving new SST state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: technically well-motivated and builds naturally on prior recursive and deep recurrent work.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies showing consistent improvements from increased depth.  
- Strength: qualitative analyses indicate different layers capture distinct aspects of compositional meaning.  
- Strength: methodology and writing are clear with sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Major concern: authors do not fine-tune pretrained word embeddings, creating an inconsistency that could confound reported gains.  
- Required: compare to a shallow RNN with fine-tuned embeddings to verify that depth, not embedding strategy, drives improvements.  
- Methodological oversight: binary sentiment results derived from a 5-class-trained model (including neutrals) rather than using the standard protocol that excludes neutral examples.  
- Required: re-evaluate binary classification using the proper protocol for fair comparison to prior work.  
- Baseline comparison incomplete: missing Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN (ACL 2014), a strong recent baseline for sentence-level sentiment.  
- Notation clarity issue: several parameters (e.g., W^eta, W^xh) are not clearly defined; summarize all parameters and dimensionalities when introduced.  
- Generality concern: claim about depth-in-structure vs. depth-in-space should be validated on datasets with only root/global labels (no per-node labels) to test broader applicability.  
- Regularization choice issue: shared dropout units across nodes are used without justification; authors should justify this choice and contrast with alternatives (weight norms, gradient clipping).  
- Overall: paper makes a meaningful contribution and presents compelling qualitative/empirical evidence, but embedding fine-tuning, binary protocol, and baseline omissions must be addressed before acceptance.
