Review - Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper presents a novel architecture – a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) – built by stacking multiple recursive layers. The model is evaluated on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

Quality
- The paper appears technically robust.
- The central concept, a deep RNN constructed by layering individual recursive networks, is well-defined.
- The authors include sufficient experimental results to assess various facets of their approach.

Originality
- The proposed idea is straightforward and logically sound. 
- Expanding the discussion with further insights would be beneficial, specifically addressing:
 + The intuitive rationale for why a deeper network architecture is advantageous for this task?
 + The motivations behind selecting the specific network architecture employed?

Significance
- This work introduces an interesting idea, provides a clear contextual background, and supports its claims with reasonable experimental evidence.
- The paper has presented interesting idea, clear background description and reasonable experimental supports.
