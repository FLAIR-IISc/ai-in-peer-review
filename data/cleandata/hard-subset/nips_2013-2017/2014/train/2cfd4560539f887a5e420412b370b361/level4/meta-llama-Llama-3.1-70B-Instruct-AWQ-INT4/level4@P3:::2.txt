This paper introduces a novel deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on the challenging Stanford Sentiment Treebank dataset. The authors' proposed changes to previous recursive neural network (RNN) models are well-motivated and experimentally validated, including untying leaf nodes and non-terminal nodes, utilizing rectified linear units in the recursive setting, employing large unsupervised word vectors with smaller hidden non-terminal nodes, and introducing a deep architecture that incorporates connections between layers and tree nodes.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed architecture and its benefits. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the deep RNN in fine-grained sentiment classification. The authors also provide qualitative analyses, including input perturbation and nearest neighbor phrase experiments, which offer valuable insights into the operation of the deep RNN.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The introduction of a novel deep RNN architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging dataset.
* A clear and well-motivated explanation of the proposed changes to previous RNN models.
* Impressive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the deep RNN.
* Qualitative analyses that provide valuable insights into the operation of the deep RNN.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The authors tune their model on the final test set, which is not ideal. It would be better to report results on the development set and use the test set only for final evaluation.
* There are a few typos in the paper, such as "comprise a class of architecture" instead of "comprises a class of architecture."

Suggestions for improvement:

* Consider adding a comparison to a variant of the deep RNN where the outputs of the last hidden layer at each node are the only input to the next parent, i.e., dropping the connection from matrix V.
* Report results on the development set instead of the final test set to avoid overfitting.
* Proofread the paper carefully to eliminate typos and grammatical errors.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing. With some minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, it has the potential to be an excellent paper.

* The paper introduces a novel deep RNN architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging dataset.
* The authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of their proposed changes to previous RNN models.
* The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the deep RNN.

Arguments for rejection:
