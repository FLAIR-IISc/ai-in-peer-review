This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results from their new architecture are impressive, and the exposition is very clear.

The changes they propose to previous RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally demonstrated to work well. Specifically, they introduce the following innovations:

- Untying the weights of leaf nodes and non-terminal nodes, allowing for more flexibility in the model.
- Using rectified linear units in the recursive setting, which helps to improve the flow of information through the network.
- Leveraging large unsupervised word vectors while using smaller hidden non-terminal nodes, striking a balance between representation capacity and computational efficiency.
- Employing a deep architecture that goes beyond simply replacing a single neural network RNN layer with a deep layer. Instead, they introduce connections between the layers and tree nodes, enabling more effective information exchange.

It would have been an interesting comparison to investigate the effect of dropping the connection from matrix V, such that the outputs of the last hidden layer at each node are the only inputs to the next parent.

One methodological concern is that the results in Table 1 (a) are reported on the final test set, rather than the development set. This raises the issue of tuning on the test set, which can lead to overly optimistic estimates of the model's performance. To avoid this, it would be better to report results on the development set.

Additionally, there are a few minor typos that should be corrected, including "comprise a class of architecture" and "sentiment classificationd dataset".
