- Introduces deep recursive neural networks that stack parameter-shared layers across tree height, extending depth into the spatial/tree dimension.  
- Novel and effective generalization of depth from recurrent networks; strong performance on the sentiment treebank with notable gains from dropout and ReLU.  
- Paper is generally well-written and reproducible.  
- Notation/clarity issues: undefined variables (W^eta, W^xh); recommend separate input/hidden equations and a parameter list with matrix dimensionalities.  
- Claims about word order interacting with tree structure are speculative and need empirical support or clearer framing.  
- Missing comparison to Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN sentence modeling; should be included in related work/experiments.  
- Questionable choice not to fine-tune pre-trained word embeddings; fine-tuning could yield sentiment-aware word vectors and is an important control (single-layer RNN with fine-tuning).  
- Shared dropout motivation unclear—explain the rationale and expected benefit.  
- Training stability concerns—suggest exploring regularizers like weight norm constraints or gradient clipping.  
- Binary classification protocol is flawed: training with neutrals deviates from standard evaluation and makes results incomparable; conduct binary experiments separately.  
- Unclear if depth helps when only a single root-level label is needed; test on datasets with only global labels to demonstrate generalization beyond treebanks.  
- Recommended revisions/experiments: redo binary protocol correctly, investigate fine-tuning embeddings, and add 1–2 global-label datasets to strengthen evidence for spatial depth benefits.
