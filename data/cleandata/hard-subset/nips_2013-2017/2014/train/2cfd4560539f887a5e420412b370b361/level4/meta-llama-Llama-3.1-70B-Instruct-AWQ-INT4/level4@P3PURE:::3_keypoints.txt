- Proposes "deep" recursive networks by adding depth across tree-space, with per-layer shared parameters and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; uses dropout and ReLUs.  
- Method is novel (extension of deep recurrent ideas) and achieves strong results on the Sentiment Treebank; gains from dropout and ReLUs are interesting.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written and gives sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Missing/unclear definitions: variables W^eta and W^xh are not defined; recommend separate equations for inputs vs hidden layers and a summarized parameter space (e.g., {WL, WR, U, b, c} with matrix dimensionalities).  
- Section Lines 148–154 reads speculative; author claims should be supported by empirical evidence if available.  
- Baseline omission: should include Kalchbrenner et al.'s CNN (ACL) for a more comprehensive comparison.  
- Decision not to fine-tune word vectors is puzzling and unjustified; lack of fine-tuning may explain improvements and needs to be controlled for (single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings could match/exceed results).  
- Shared dropout units (Line 251): motivation for using shared units is not explained.  
- Stability handling (Lines 255–256): authors should consider or compare alternative techniques like weight norm constraints or gradient truncation as used in RNNs.  
- Binary classification protocol deviates from standard by including neutral examples during training; results are not directly comparable and binary task should be rerun with the standard protocol.  
- Question about generality: unclear whether deep RNNs help when only a single root/global label exists — experiments on root-only label datasets are needed.  
- Recommended additional experiments: redo binary protocol properly, test fine-tuning embeddings, and evaluate on 1–2 datasets with only global labels to strengthen evidence for depth across space.
