- Proposes "deep recursive networks" extending depth across spatial layers, uses layer-specific shared parameters, dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Achieves strong results on the sentiment treebank but is more a generalization of recurrent techniques than a fundamentally novel idea.  
- Major methodological concern: word embeddings are fixed (not fine-tuned), creating inconsistent representations and potentially inflating apparent gains from depth.  
- Authors must compare against baselines with fine-tuned embeddings; a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings may outperform the proposed deep model.  
- Binary classification protocol is incorrect: neutral examples were included in training, preventing valid comparison with prior work; re-run excluding neutrals.  
- Unclear whether depth benefits tasks with only root-level labels; need experiments on sentence-level datasets (e.g., SST-1) to test utility beyond node-wise predictions.  
- Missing relevant baseline: Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) convolutional neural network for sentence modeling should be included.  
- Equations lack clarity: undefined matrices (e.g., W^η, W^{xh}); authors should explicitly define all parameters and dimensionalities and separate input/hidden equations.  
- Some claims are speculative/unverified (lines 148–154) and should be backed by evidence or removed.  
- Motivation for "shared dropout units" is unclear and needs explicit justification.  
- Stability concerns are addressed ad-hoc; recommend standard remedies (gradient clipping, weight norm constraints) instead.  
- Required experiments to strengthen the paper: (1) re-run binary classification excluding neutral labels, (2) compare deep models with and without fine-tuned embeddings, (3) evaluate on datasets with only global labels to isolate depth effects.  
- Paper is well-written and reproducible, but unresolved methodological gaps mean current results do not convincingly attribute gains to spatial depth; additional experiments are needed before acceptance.
