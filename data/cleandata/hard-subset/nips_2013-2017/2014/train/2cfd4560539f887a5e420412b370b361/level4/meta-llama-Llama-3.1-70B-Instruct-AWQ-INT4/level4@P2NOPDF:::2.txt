This paper introduces a novel deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results obtained using this new architecture are impressive, and the authors present their ideas with exceptional clarity.

The proposed modifications to previous RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated to be effective. Specifically, the authors:

- Decouple the leaf nodes and non-terminal nodes, allowing for more flexible representations.
- Employ rectified linear units in the recursive setting, which helps to improve the model's expressiveness.
- Utilize large unsupervised word vectors in conjunction with smaller hidden non-terminal nodes, striking a balance between representation capacity and computational efficiency.
- Design a deep architecture that goes beyond simply replacing a single neural network RNN layer with a deep layer. Instead, they introduce connections between the layers and tree nodes, enabling a more nuanced representation of the input data.

It would be interesting to see a comparison where the outputs of the last hidden layer at each node are the only inputs to the next parent node, effectively dropping the connection from matrix V. This ablation study could provide further insights into the importance of the proposed architecture.

The authors should note that the results in Table 1 (a) should be reported on the development set, rather than the final test set. This is because the test set should be used solely for evaluation, and reporting results on it may give an overly optimistic estimate of the model's performance.

Additionally, there are a few minor typos that need to be corrected, such as "comprise a class of architecture" and "sentiment classificationd dataset".
