- Proposes “deep” recursive networks adding depth across tree structure with per-layer shared parameters, dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Method is novel but a natural extension of deep recurrent nets; reported results are strong and gains from dropout/ReLUs are noteworthy.  
- Several modeling and experimental choices are weakly justified and additional experiments are required before recommendation.  
- Paper is generally well written and provides enough detail for reproduction.  
- Undefined notation (W^η, W^xh); request clearer equations, separate input/hidden formulations, full parameter set, and matrix dimensions.  
- Claims in lines 148–154 appear speculative and need empirical support or evidence.  
- Missing key baseline: include Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN results.  
- Word embeddings were not fine-tuned; need to justify or compare since fine-tuning can affect sentiment polarity and performance.  
- Shared dropout masks across positions need motivation and comparison to independent dropout.  
- Stability fixes: ask whether standard alternatives (weight norm constraints, gradient clipping) were tried and compared.  
- Binary classification protocol deviates from standard (neutrals included); re-run without neutrals to be comparable to prior work.  
- Scope question: test whether depth across space helps when only root labels are available—suggest re-running binary experiment with standard protocol, studying embedding fine-tuning vs fixed, and adding 1–2 global-label datasets.  
- Overall: promising approach and strong results, but requires clearer definitions, justified design choices, key baselines, and control experiments to strengthen the paper.
