- Proposes stacking multiple recursive layers to form a deep RNN that composes over parse trees; evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Paper is generally well written; motivation and background are clearly presented.  
- Work appears technically sound; main contribution (stacked recursive layers) is well motivated and clearly described.  
- Experimental evaluation is reasonable, exploring different depths and widths and providing sufficient empirical evidence.  
- Idea is a simple, reasonable extension of recursive networks to add depth.  
- Paper lacks intuitive explanation of why added depth helps compositional modeling and what phenomena different layers capture.  
- Rationale for specific architectural choices (e.g., connecting classifier only to final layer) and alternatives is underdeveloped.  
- More discussion of design alternatives and tradeoffs among deep recursive variants would strengthen the paper.  
- Significance: interesting and useful extension with meaningful empirical support for benefits in sentiment classification.  
- Pros: clear problem formulation, straightforward extension, adequate experimental coverage, and pleasant writing.  
- Cons: needs stronger intuitive justification for depth and more detailed discussion of architectural choices and alternatives.  
- Overall: technically sound and clearly presented; main improvements should target intuition for depth and elaboration of architecture design decisions.
