- Introduces a novel deep recursive network extending recurrent depth to spatial/tree structures with shared layer parameters.  
- Uses shared parameters across tree heights, dropout, ReLU, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the sentiment treebank.  
- Logical generalization of recurrent methods that achieves strong performance on the chosen dataset; gains from dropout and ReLU are notable.  
- Modeling and experimental choices lack sufficient justification, requiring further investigation before strong acceptance.  
- Paper is well-written and provides enough detail for reproducibility.  
- Clarify undefined variables (W^eta, W^xh) and present separate input/hidden equations plus a full parameter/dimensionality summary.  
- Lines 148–154 read speculative; state when claims are based on empirical findings and provide supporting evidence.  
- Include baseline comparison to Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) convolutional sentence model.  
- Questionable decision not to fine-tune word embeddings; recommend experiments allowing embeddings to adapt and comparing to single-layer RNN with fine-tuning.  
- Explain the rationale for using shared dropout units (line 251).  
- Consider alternative stabilization methods (weight norm constraints, gradient clipping) beyond the current approach (lines 255–256).  
- Binary classification protocol is flawed: neutral examples were included during training; binary experiments should be run independently to be comparable to prior work.  
- Test generalization to root-only (global) prediction to see if depth benefits persist versus a single-layer RNN.  
- Recommended additional experiments: re-run binary classification with correct protocol, evaluate fine-tuning embeddings, and add 1–2 datasets with only global labels to strengthen claims.
