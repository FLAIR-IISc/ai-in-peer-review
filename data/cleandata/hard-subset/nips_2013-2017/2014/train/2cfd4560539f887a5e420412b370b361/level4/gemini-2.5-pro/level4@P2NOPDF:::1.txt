### **Review for Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language**

#### **Summary**
This paper introduces the Deep Recursive Neural Network (Deep RNN), a novel architecture constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate this model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification, arguing that the increased depth allows the network to learn more complex compositional representations of language.

#### **Clarity**
The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors present their proposed architecture, the motivation behind it, and the experimental setup with a high degree of clarity, making the work accessible and pleasant to read.

#### **Quality**
The methodology presented in the paper appears to be technically sound. The core contribution—extending recursive networks through depth by stacking layers—is an elegant and logical progression of prior work in this area. The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation, with sufficient experimental results to substantiate their claims and analyze various aspects of the proposed model. The comparisons against relevant baselines effectively demonstrate the advantages of their approach.

#### **Originality**
The proposed Deep RNN architecture is a novel and intuitive extension of existing recursive models. While the concept of adding depth to neural networks is well-established in other domains, its application to recursive structures for modeling natural language compositionality is a valuable contribution.

The paper could be strengthened, however, by a more detailed discussion on the following points:
*   **Intuition for Depth:** While the results convincingly demonstrate the benefits of a deeper architecture, the paper would benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the intuition behind *why* this depth is particularly well-suited for capturing the complex, hierarchical nature of sentiment. A clearer conceptual motivation would enhance the impact of the empirical findings.
*   **Architectural Justification:** A more explicit justification for certain architectural choices would be helpful. For instance, providing the rationale behind the specific number of layers chosen or the type of non-linearities used would improve the paper's completeness and reproducibility.

#### **Significance**
This paper presents an interesting and well-supported contribution to the field. By demonstrating that adding depth to recursive neural networks leads to significant performance gains on a challenging NLP task, the work provides strong evidence for the benefits of deep representations in modeling linguistic compositionality. This is a significant finding that is likely to influence and inspire future research in representation learning for natural language.
