- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network that stacks multiple recursive layers for hierarchical compositionality.  
- Builds on prior recursive and deep recurrent work and clearly distinguishes structural depth vs. spatial depth.  
- Outperforms shallow recursive networks with the same number of parameters.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on fine-grained sentiment classification (Stanford Sentiment Treebank).  
- Strong empirical support with rigorous experiments and ablation studies.  
- Insightful qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest neighbors) showing different layers capture different compositional aspects.  
- Technically sound design choices: untied leaf/internal representations, rectifier activations, and dropout.  
- Clear exposition and reproducibility: well-organized text, clear figures, and sufficient implementation detail.  
- Original and significant contribution by meaningfully extending shallow recursive architectures.  
- Issue: Table 1(a) appears to report test-set results rather than development-set results, risking hyperparameter-tuning leakage.  
- Suggestion: Include an ablation study removing V (skip) connections to quantify their contribution.
