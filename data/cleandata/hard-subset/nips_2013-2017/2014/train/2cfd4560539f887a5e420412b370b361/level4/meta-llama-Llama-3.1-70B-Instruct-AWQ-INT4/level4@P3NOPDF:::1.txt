Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a novel architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

**Clarity**
The paper is generally well-written and pleasant to read. The authors have done a commendable job in presenting their ideas in a clear and concise manner. The text is free of major grammatical errors, and the language is accessible to readers with a basic understanding of neural networks.

**Quality**
The paper appears to be technically sound. The major idea of the deep recursive neural network, which is constructed by stacking multiple layers of individual recursive nets, is well-motivated and intuitive. The authors provide a reasonable amount of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method, demonstrating a thorough understanding of the subject matter.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical foundations with empirical evaluations. The authors have made a genuine effort to validate their claims through experiments, which adds credibility to their work.

**Originality**
While the proposed idea is simple and reasonable, it is not particularly groundbreaking. The concept of stacking multiple layers of recursive nets is a natural extension of existing recursive neural network architectures. However, the authors' implementation and evaluation of this idea are thorough and well-executed.

To further enhance the paper's originality, it would be beneficial to include more discussions on the following aspects:

1. Intuitive explanations for why deeper networks are better suited for the target task of fine-grained sentiment classification. Providing a deeper understanding of the underlying mechanisms would make the paper more compelling.
2. Justifications for the selected network architecture. The authors could elaborate on why they chose to stack multiple recursive layers, as opposed to other possible architectures.

**Significance**
The paper presents an interesting idea, provides a clear background description, and offers reasonable experimental support. The authors have made a genuine contribution to the field of natural language processing, and their work has the potential to inspire further research in this area.

One potential area for improvement is to more explicitly highlight the significance and impact of the proposed approach. While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method, they could further emphasize its potential applications and implications for the broader field of NLP.

Overall, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a valuable contribution to the field. With some minor revisions to address the above-mentioned points, it has the potential to become an even stronger contribution to the conference.
