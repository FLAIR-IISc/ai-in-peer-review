- Novelty: Introduces "deep" recursive networks generalizing recurrent-depth ideas across spatial/tree dimensions.  
- Empirical result: Strong performance on the Stanford sentiment treebank; dropout and ReLUs appear effective.  
- Reproducibility: Paper is well-written with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Main concern: Several model and experimental choices lack justification and require additional experiments before acceptance.  
- Parameter clarity: Clarify definitions of W^eta and W^xh (e.g., whether they refer to W^{eta}L and W^{eta}R) and provide separate equations and parameter dimensionalities.  
- Speculative claim: Remove or support the claim in lines 148–154 with empirical evidence or citations.  
- Missing baseline: Include results from Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) convolutional sentence model as a baseline.  
- Embeddings: Justify not fine-tuning pre-trained word embeddings; compare to a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings to isolate depth effects.  
- Shared dropout: State the motivation for using shared dropout (line 251).  
- Stability method: Consider and/or compare alternatives like weight norm constraints or gradient clipping instead of the proposed stability approach (lines 255–256).  
- Binary classification protocol: Re-run binary sentiment experiments excluding neutral examples during training to match prior work.  
- Depth utility and experiments: Demonstrate advantage of deep RNNs for root-only (global) prediction, test fine-tuning effects, and add 1–2 additional datasets with only global labels to strengthen claims.
