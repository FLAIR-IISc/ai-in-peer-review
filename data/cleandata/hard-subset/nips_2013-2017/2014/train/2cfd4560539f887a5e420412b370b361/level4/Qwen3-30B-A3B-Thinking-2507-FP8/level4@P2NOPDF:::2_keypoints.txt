- Introduces a novel deep recursive neural network that achieves state-of-the-art sentiment classification results.  
- Four key architectural innovations: untied leaf vs. nonterminal weights for flexibility.  
- Innovation: adaptation of ReLUs to the recursive setting to increase representational capacity.  
- Innovation: use of large pre-trained word vectors with compact nonterminal hidden states for efficiency and expressiveness.  
- Innovation: deep architecture with explicit inter-layer connections between tree nodes (not naive stacking) for richer hierarchical flow.  
- Empirical results are strong, outperforming strong baselines across multiple metrics.  
- Critical methodological flaw: Table 1(a) reports test-set results implying hyperparameter tuning on the test set; tuning must use the development set and final results reported on a held-out test set.  
- Suggested additional experiment: ablate/remove the V (inter-layer) connection to test whether inter-layer connections are essential versus using only the top hidden layer.  
- Paper strengths: exceptionally clear writing, solid motivation, technical soundness, and convincing ablation studies.  
- Practical design choice praised: unsupervised embeddings with small hidden states aligns with efficient NLP trends.  
- Minor improvements requested: clarify Section 3.2 connectivity vs. naive stacking and add discussion of computational efficiency (training time/memory).  
- Recommendation: high-quality contribution; accept pending correction of the evaluation protocol.
