

This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results are impressive, and the exposition is very clear. The proposed modifications to prior RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally validated:  
- Untying leaf and nonterminal node parameters.  
- Employing rectified linear units within the recursive framework.  
- Utilizing large unsupervised word vectors alongside smaller hidden nonterminal nodes.  
- Implementing a deep architecture that extends beyond simply replacing a single-layer RNN with a deep layer; it introduces connections between layers and tree nodes. It would be interesting to compare against a variant where only the output of the last hidden layer at each node feeds into the parent (i.e., dropping the connection from matrix V).
