- Proposes a deep recursive neural architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset, with clear motivation and strong empirical support.
- Unties parameters between leaf and nonterminal nodes to better model their distinct roles.
- Uses ReLUs in a recursive setting to improve optimization and representational capacity.
- Combines large unsupervised word embeddings with smaller hidden nonterminal dimensions for efficient capacity allocation.
- Introduces a genuinely deep recursive architecture with both across-layer and along-tree connections for richer compositional representations.
- Concern: potential test-set tuning—report dev-set model selection and reserve test set for a single untouched evaluation.
- Suggestion: add an ablation that removes inter-layer/skip connections (passing only last hidden layer output to parent) to isolate their contribution.
- Suggestion: include a sensitivity analysis varying hidden size (with fixed word vectors) to show gains aren’t solely from leaf capacity.
- Writing and presentation are clear; figures and equations are easy to follow.
- Suggest adding notes on ReLU-specific regularization (e.g., norm clipping, dropout) and exact parameter counts for reproducibility.
- Minor typos: change “comprise a class of architecture” → “comprise a class of architectures” and fix “classificationd.”
- Overall: exciting, timely work with strong support for acceptance pending correction of evaluation protocol and inclusion of the suggested ablation.
