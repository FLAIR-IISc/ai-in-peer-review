- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Networks (DRNNs): stack recursive layers to add hierarchical (spatial) depth to structural recursion.  
- Builds on Socher et al. (2013) and achieves state-of-the-art on SST (49.5% fine-grained accuracy).  
- Technical approach is sound with appropriate regularization and pretrained vectors; deeper DRNNs outperform shallower models with equivalent parameters.  
- Ablation (Table 1a) convincingly shows depth benefits over width; qualitative analyses suggest layer specialization.  
- Paper is well-written with clear figures and reproducible methodological details; minor clarifications needed (output-layer connections, depth vs. width discussion).  
- The idea is a natural but novel extension of recursive models, distinguishing structural depth from temporal depth in prior deep RNN work.  
- Results are significant for NLP and likely generalizable, though current evaluation focuses narrowly on sentiment.  
- Underexplored theoretical justification for why depth produces layer specialization; needs stronger theoretical linkage (e.g., hierarchical feature abstraction).  
- Limited task scope: evaluation only on sentiment limits evidence of broader applicability; more tasks would strengthen claims.  
- Some analyses are qualitative and limited (single-example input perturbation, nearest-neighbor table) and would benefit from larger-scale quantitative validation.  
- Pros: novel architecture, strong empirical results, thorough ablations, clear writing, useful qualitative insights.  
- Cons: modest theoretical development, narrow evaluation, underdeveloped implications of layer specialization.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand theoretical discussion and broaden evaluation experiments.
