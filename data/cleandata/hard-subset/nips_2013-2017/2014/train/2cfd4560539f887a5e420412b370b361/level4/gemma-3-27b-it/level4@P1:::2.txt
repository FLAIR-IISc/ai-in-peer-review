This paper introduces a novel deep recursive neural network architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging fine-grained sentiment classification dataset. The results are impressive, and the exposition is remarkably clear.

The modifications proposed to previous recursive neural network (RNN) models are clever, well-motivated, and convincingly demonstrated through experiments: untying leaf and nonterminal nodes, utilizing rectified linear units within the recursive setting, leveraging large unsupervised word vectors with smaller hidden nonterminal nodes, and introducing a genuinely deep architecture with connections between layers and tree nodes. It would have been interesting to see a comparison where the outputs of the last hidden layer at each node were the *only* inputs to the next parent node, effectively removing the connection mediated by matrix V. This would clarify the contribution of the interlayer connections. 

A concern is that the results presented in Table 1(a) appear to be reported on the test set, rather than a dedicated development set. Reporting results on the test set during model selection constitutes tuning on the test set, which can lead to overly optimistic performance estimates. 

There are a few minor typographical issues: “comprise a class of architecture” should be “comprises a class of architectures” or “is a class of architectures.”

**Summary of the Paper:**

The authors address the limitations of traditional recursive neural networks in capturing hierarchical representations by introducing a deep recursive neural network (deep RNN). This architecture stacks multiple recursive layers, similar to deep recurrent networks, to combine depth in structure and depth in space. They evaluate their model on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST) and demonstrate superior performance compared to shallow counterparts and existing baselines, including multiplicative RNNs and Paragraph Vectors. Exploratory analyses suggest that different layers capture varying aspects of compositionality.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Performance:** The proposed deep RNN achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging sentiment classification task.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The authors clearly articulate the limitations of existing recursive networks and provide a compelling motivation for their approach.
*   **Well-Designed Architecture:** The architecture is thoughtfully designed, combining feedforward and recursive components effectively. The untying of leaf and internal nodes is a particularly insightful modification.
*   **Thorough Evaluation:** The experiments are comprehensive, including comparisons to several strong baselines and analyses of layer-specific behavior.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand, despite the technical complexity of the topic.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Test Set Evaluation:** Reporting results on the test set for model selection is a significant concern. A dedicated development set should be used for hyperparameter tuning and model selection.
*   **Ablation Study:** While the authors justify their design choices, a more thorough ablation study could further solidify their claims. For example, the impact of the interlayer connections (matrix V) could be explicitly evaluated.
*   **Limited Generalization Discussion:** The discussion of generalizability beyond the sentiment analysis task is brief. Exploring the potential of deep RNNs in other domains would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture that achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging task.
*   The authors provide a clear and comprehensive evaluation of their model.
*   The paper makes a significant contribution to the understanding of deep learning for natural language processing.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The reporting of results on the test set raises concerns about the validity of the performance claims.
*   The lack of a more thorough ablation study limits the insight into the contribution of individual components.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the concern regarding test set evaluation, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. The proposed deep RNN architecture is innovative, well-motivated, and demonstrably effective. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the issue of test set reporting by re-evaluating and reporting results using a dedicated development set.
