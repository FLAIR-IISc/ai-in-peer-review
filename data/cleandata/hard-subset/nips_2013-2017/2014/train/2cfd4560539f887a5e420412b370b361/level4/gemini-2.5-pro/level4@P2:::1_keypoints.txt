- Proposes Deep RNN: stack multiple recursive layers to make tree-structured RNNs "deep in space" as well as "deep in structure."
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment); Deep RNNs outperform shallow models with similar parameter budgets.
- Best model achieves new state-of-the-art, surpassing RNTN and Paragraph Vectors.
- Qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) indicate different layers capture lexical/syntactic features in lower layers and higher-level sentiment in upper layers.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with novel, intuitive architecture, solid empirical results, and useful analysis.
- Technical quality: well-motivated model, sound experimental setup (dropout, AdaGrad, pre-trained vectors), and fair, thorough comparisons.
- Experimental design: controlled comparison between deep/narrow and shallow/wide models provides compelling evidence for the benefits of depth.
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written; figures and examples make methodology and analyses easy to follow.
- Originality: novel extension of recursive models by stacking layers; distinguishes itself from prior single-layer recursive work.
- Significance: advances recursive model capacity, sets a benchmark result, and offers insights into hierarchical compositionality.
- Strength: novel and elegant architecture combining tree structure with deep hierarchical representation learning.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging, widely used benchmark.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation with parameter-controlled comparisons supporting central claims.
- Strength: insightful qualitative analyses demonstrating functional hierarchy across layers.
- Weakness: limited discussion of alternative architectural choices (e.g., skip connections or connecting outputs to all layers).
- Weakness: could provide more a priori linguistic intuition for why sentiment benefits from hierarchical composition (e.g., negation, contrast).
- Weakness: evaluation restricted to a single task (sentiment); broader tasks like paraphrase detection or entailment would strengthen generality claims.
