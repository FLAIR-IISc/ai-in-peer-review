- Introduces deep recursive neural networks with layer-wise parameters shared across tree nodes, using dropout, ReLUs, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank.  
- Method is a novel, natural generalization of deep stacking for recurrent networks and shows strong performance; dropout and ReLU gains are noteworthy.  
- Paper is well-written and reproducible, but model and experimental design lack sufficient justification.  
- Notation issues: W^eta and W^xh are undefined; recommend clearer separate equations and an explicit parameter list with matrix dimensionalities.  
- Claim in lines 148–154 is speculative and needs empirical support or explicit evidence from experiments.  
- Missing key baseline: should compare against Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2014) CNN sentence model.  
- Critical omission: word embeddings are not fine-tuned; this choice needs justification and an ablation, since fine-tuning could negate depth benefits.  
- Concern that performance gains may be an artifact of fixed embeddings; compare to a single-layer model with fine-tuned vectors.  
- Clarify motivation for sharing the same dropout mask across child vectors (line 251).  
- Regarding stability (lines 255–256), consider standard alternatives like constraining weight norms or gradient clipping.  
- Binary classification protocol deviates from standard by including neutrals in training; re-run excluding neutral examples for fair comparison.  
- Unclear if depth benefits generalize to tasks with only root labels; test on sentence-level (root-only) datasets.  
- Recommended additional experiments: re-run binary experiment with standard protocol, ablation on embedding fine-tuning (including single-layer baseline), and evaluation on 1–2 additional sentence-level datasets.  
- Conclusion: architecture is promising but its general utility is not yet fully established; additional experiments needed to strengthen claims.
