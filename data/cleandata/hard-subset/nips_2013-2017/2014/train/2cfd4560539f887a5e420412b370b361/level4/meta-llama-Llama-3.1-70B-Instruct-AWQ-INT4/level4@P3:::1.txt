Paper For paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a new architecture — deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) — constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

The authors provide a clear and concise background on recursive neural networks, their applications, and the limitations of existing architectures. They also discuss the benefits of deep feedforward neural networks and deep recurrent neural networks, which serves as a motivation for their proposed deep recursive neural network architecture.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their methodology, including the untied variant of recursive neural networks, the deep recursive neural network architecture, and the experimental setup. The use of figures and tables to illustrate the architecture and present the results is helpful.

The paper seems technically sound, and the authors provide reasonable amounts of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method. The results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size and previous baselines on the sentiment analysis task, achieving new state-of-the-art results.

The proposed idea is simple and reasonable, and the authors provide some intuitive explanations for why deeper networks might be beneficial for the target task. However, it would be helpful to have more discussions on why intuitively deeper networks are good for the target task and why the selected network architecture is chosen.

The paper has presented an interesting idea, clear background description, and reasonable experimental supports. The authors also provide some qualitative analyses of the effect of multiple layers, which suggest that each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality, and representations at each layer capture different notions of similarity.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their methodology and results. The idea of deep recursive neural networks is interesting, and the experimental results are promising.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

1. The paper introduces a new architecture that combines the benefits of recursive neural networks and deep feedforward neural networks.
2. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their methodology and experimental setup.
3. The results show that deep RNNs outperform shallow recursive nets of the same size and previous baselines on the sentiment analysis task.
4. The paper provides some qualitative analyses of the effect of multiple layers, which suggest that each layer handles a different aspect of compositionality.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

1. The paper could benefit from more discussions on why intuitively deeper networks are good for the target task.
2. The authors could provide more explanations for why the selected network architecture is chosen.
3. The paper could benefit from more comparisons with other deep learning architectures.
