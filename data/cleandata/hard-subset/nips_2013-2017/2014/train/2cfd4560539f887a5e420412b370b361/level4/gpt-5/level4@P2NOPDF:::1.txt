Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

Summary
The paper proposes a deep recursive neural network (DRNN) architecture formed by stacking multiple recursive layers and evaluates it on fine-grained sentiment classification.

Clarity
- Generally well written and easy to follow.
- Background and motivation are presented clearly.

Quality
- The technical approach appears sound.
- The central idea—building depth by stacking recursive networks—is appealing and well justified within the proposed framework.
- The experimental evaluation covers several aspects of the method with a reasonable amount of evidence.

Originality
- The idea is straightforward and sensible rather than radically novel.
- The paper would benefit from additional discussion on:
  - The intuitive reasons depth should help for the target task.
  - The rationale behind the specific architectural choices and design alternatives considered (or not considered).

Significance
- The work offers an interesting contribution with clear exposition and solid empirical support.
- Given the clarity of the presentation and the demonstrated performance on fine-grained sentiment analysis, the approach is likely to be of interest to the community.
