- Introduces "deep" recursive networks with spatial depth and per-layer parameters shared vertically; novel generalization of depth in recurrent nets and strong performance on the sentiment treebank.  
- Uses dropout and ReLUs and fixes word embeddings to pre-trained vectors; reported gains from dropout and ReLUs are noteworthy.  
- Paper is generally well-written and provides enough detail for reproducibility.  
- Model/experimental-design choices (e.g., parameter definitions, why embeddings are fixed) lack sufficient justification.  
- Clarify notation: define W^η and W^{xh} (likely W^η_L and W^η_R), present separate input/hidden equations, and list parameter set with dimensionalities.  
- A speculative claim (Lines 148–154) must be stated as empirical if supported by results or substantiated with evidence.  
- Include baseline results from Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2013) for fair comparison.  
- Explain why word embeddings are not fine-tuned since fine-tuning is standard for sentiment tasks and may affect gains; compare to a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings.  
- Justify the shared-dropout-unit design (Line 251) or explain its rationale.  
- Consider alternative stability methods (weight norm constraints, gradient clipping) instead of or in addition to the current approach (Lines 255–256).  
- Binary classification experiment is flawed: training includes neutral examples, so results are not comparable to prior work; run a proper binary experiment separately.  
- Demonstrate whether spatial depth helps global/sentence-level classification: rerun binary experiment per standard protocol, test fine-tuning effects, and add 1–2 additional datasets with only global labels to strengthen claims about depth.
