- Proposes stacking multiple recursive layers to model compositionality; evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Manuscript is clear, well organized, and the idea of deepening recursive composition is intuitive and timely.  
- Methodologically sound: stacking recursive layers is a straightforward, reasonable extension of recursive models.  
- Empirical evaluation shows potential benefits of increased depth for sentiment tasks.  
- Contribution: systematically explores depth in recursive networks, advancing compositionality modeling.  
- Needs clearer intuition for why depth helps and which linguistic phenomena (negation, intensification, contrast, scope) it captures.  
- Architectural justification is lacking: why stack full layers versus deepening the per-node composition, and how do sharing, nonlinearities, gating/skip connections affect results?  
- Comparative baselines are insufficient: missing parameter-matched single-layer, intra-node multi-layer composition, and non-recursive deep sequential baselines.  
- Lacks depth analysis: no ablation over number of recursive layers or performance vs. depth plot to reveal diminishing returns or optimal depth.  
- Training dynamics and regularization are under-discussed: optimization stability, gradient clipping, pretraining, normalization, and their effects are unclear.  
- Efficiency concerns: no analysis of computational time/memory scaling with depth or effects on batch size and convergence.  
- Missing error analysis: need qualitative/targeted quantitative breakdown (e.g., with negation or long-distance dependencies) to show where depth helps.  
- Generality is underexplored: discuss applicability to other structured NLP tasks (entailment, semantic parsing).  
- Suggest adding concise examples showing how extra layers enrich representations at different tree levels.  
- Suggest providing detailed architectural specs: parameter sharing, activations, initialization, regularization, and how intermediate representations are combined.  
- Suggest including ablations: depth sweep, parameter-matched baselines, alternative deepening methods, and reporting validation/stability metrics.  
- Suggest expanding discussion of computational trade-offs and memory footprint.  
- Suggest adding error analysis by linguistic phenomenon and clarifying the impact of supervision granularity (phrase vs sentence labels).  
- Originality: idea is incremental but meaningful—a natural evolution toward deeper recursive architectures.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising; positive but contingent on stronger justification, deeper analyses, and more comprehensive baselines.
