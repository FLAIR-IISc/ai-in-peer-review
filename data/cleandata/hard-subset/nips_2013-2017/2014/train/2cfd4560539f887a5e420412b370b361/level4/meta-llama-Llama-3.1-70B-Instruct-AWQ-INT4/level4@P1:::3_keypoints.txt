- Proposes "deep" recursive networks by adding depth across space with layer-wise parameter sharing across heights.  
- Uses dropout and ReLUs while keeping word representations fixed to pre-trained embeddings.  
- Achieves very strong performance on the Socher et al. sentiment treebank; improvements from dropout and ReLUs are interesting.  
- Method is novel as an extension of recursive nets but essentially generalizes approaches used with recurrent networks.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written and gives sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Technically sound overall, but several model and experimental choices are not well justified.  
- Questionable choice: authors do not fine-tune word embeddings and provide no clear justification.  
- Questionable choice: use of shared dropout units lacks adequate motivation.  
- Binary classification experiment is not run according to the standard protocol, making comparisons difficult.  
- Results would be more convincing with additional experiments (e.g., fine-tuning embeddings, more datasets with global labels).  
- Recommendation: authors should justify freezing embeddings and shared dropout choices more clearly.  
- Recommendation: redo the binary classification experiment using the standard protocol.  
- Recommendation: conduct extra experiments (fine-tune embeddings, include other datasets) and fix minor notation/terminology issues.
