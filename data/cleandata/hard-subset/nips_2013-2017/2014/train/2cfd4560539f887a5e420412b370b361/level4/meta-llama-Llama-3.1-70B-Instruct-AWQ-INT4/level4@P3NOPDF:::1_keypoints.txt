- Introduces a deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers and evaluates it on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clear, concise, and free of major grammatical errors.  
- Quality: technically sound, well-motivated idea with reasonable and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Balance: good mix of theoretical grounding and empirical validation that supports the claims.  
- Originality: concept is a natural extension of existing recursive nets and not groundbreaking, though implementation and evaluation are solid.  
- Suggested improvements: add intuitive explanations for why deeper networks help the target task.  
- Suggested improvements: justify the chosen architecture and why stacking recursive layers was preferred over alternatives.  
- Significance: interesting contribution with potential to inspire further NLP research but should more explicitly highlight its broader impact and applications.  
- Overall: solid, valuable paper that could be strengthened with minor revisions addressing the above points.
