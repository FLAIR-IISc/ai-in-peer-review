- Proposal: Introduces “deep” recursive networks by adding depth across tree structure, with layer-wise shared parameters, ReLUs, dropout, and fixed pre-trained word embeddings.
- Novelty and results: Novel but natural extension of recurrent-net techniques; achieves very strong results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and reports interesting gains from ReLUs and dropout.
- Main concern: Several modeling and experimental design choices are insufficiently justified, requiring additional experiments before confident acceptance.
- Reproducibility: Paper is generally clear and contains enough detail for reproduction.
- Notation/definitions: Some notation (W^η, W^xh) is undefined and full parameter set and matrix dimensionalities should be summarized.
- Speculation: Certain discussions (lines 148–154) are speculative and need to be supported by empirical observations or removed.
- Missing baseline: Should include results from Kalchbrenner et al. (2014) CNN baseline.
- Word embeddings: Not fine-tuning word vectors is questionable; need experiments controlling for fine-tuned vs. fixed embeddings as gains might stem from this choice.
- Dropout choice: Rationale for using shared dropout units (line 251) is not explained.
- Stabilization alternatives: Consider comparing to standard stabilization methods (weight-norm constraints, gradient clipping) used in recurrent nets.
- Binary protocol deviation: Binary classification experiment includes neutral examples in training and deviates from established protocol, making comparisons invalid—should be redone per standard setup.
- Scope of evaluation: Need experiments on datasets with only global/root labels to test whether depth across space helps when internal node labels are unavailable.
- Recommended experiments: Redo binary task with standard protocol, test fine-tuning vs. fixed embeddings, and include 1–2 additional root-label-only datasets.
