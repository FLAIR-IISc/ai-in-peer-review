This paper presents a novel deep recursive neural network architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The authors' modifications to previous recursive neural network (RNN) models are well-motivated and experimentally validated, including untying leaf nodes and non-terminal nodes, utilizing rectified linear units in the recursive setting, leveraging large unsupervised word vectors with smaller hidden non-terminal nodes, and introducing a deep architecture that incorporates connections between layers and tree nodes.

The proposed deep recursive neural network (deep RNN) architecture is a significant departure from traditional RNN models, which are deep in structure but lack the hierarchical representation capacity of conventional deep feed-forward networks. By stacking multiple recursive layers, the authors enable the network to learn a hierarchical representation of compositionality in language, which is essential for tasks like sentiment classification.

The paper provides a clear and well-structured exposition of the proposed architecture, including its components, training procedure, and experimental evaluation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive experiments on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank dataset, showcasing the benefits of deep RNNs over shallow RNNs and other baseline models.

One potential area for improvement is the evaluation protocol. The authors report results on the test set, which may not accurately reflect the model's performance due to overfitting. It would be more appropriate to report results on the development set, which is typically used for hyperparameter tuning.

Additionally, the authors may want to consider an ablation study to investigate the impact of the connections between layers and tree nodes. Specifically, they could examine the effect of dropping the connection from matrix V, which would allow them to isolate the contribution of the deep architecture.

Minor typos and grammatical errors were found throughout the paper, including the phrase "comprise a class of architecture" which should be "comprises a class of architecture."

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing, and its findings have important implications for the development of more effective sentiment classification models.
