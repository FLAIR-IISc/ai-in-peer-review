- Proposes Deep Recursive Neural Network by stacking recursive layers and tests it on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with accessible presentation of architecture and experiments.  
- Methodology is technically sound; depth is a natural extension of prior recursive models with thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Comparisons against relevant baselines demonstrate the proposed approach’s advantages.  
- Novelty: applying increased depth to recursive structures for modeling linguistic compositionality.  
- Lacks deeper conceptual intuition for why added depth specifically captures hierarchical sentiment complexity.  
- Needs clearer architectural justification for choices (number of layers, types of non-linearities) to aid completeness and reproducibility.  
- Findings are significant: depth yields notable performance gains and will likely influence future NLP representation learning research.
