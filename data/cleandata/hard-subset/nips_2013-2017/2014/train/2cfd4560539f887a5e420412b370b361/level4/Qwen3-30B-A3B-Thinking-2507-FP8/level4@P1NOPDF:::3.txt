

This paper introduces a novel approach to deep recursive networks by extending depth across spatial dimensions, drawing inspiration from successful depth augmentation in recurrent networks. Each layer employs shared parameters across all positions within that layer, while incorporating dropout and ReLU activations with fixed pre-trained word embeddings. The method is evaluated on Socher et al.'s sentiment treebank.

The core idea is a meaningful generalization of depth in recursive architectures, and the results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the sentiment treebank. The observed gains from dropout and ReLUs are particularly noteworthy. However, several model and experimental design choices lack sufficient justification, and additional experiments are needed before I can recommend acceptance.

The paper is generally well-written with adequate detail for reproducibility. Below are specific concerns requiring clarification or extension:

- **Line 138**: Define $W^\eta$ and $W^{xh}$ explicitly. Are these intended to represent $W^\eta_L$ and $W^\eta_R$? Presenting separate equations for input and hidden-layer transformations would improve clarity. Also, explicitly summarize the parameter set $\{W_L, W_R, U, b, c\}$ and specify matrix dimensions upon definition.

- **Lines 148–154**: This claim about the necessity of depth appears speculative. If based on empirical findings, cite specific results. Without evidence, this reasoning is unconvincing.

- **Baseline comparison**: Include results from Kalchbrenner et al.'s "A convolutional neural network for modelling sentences" (ACL 2014), a relevant recent baseline.

- **Word embedding fine-tuning**: Explain why embeddings remain fixed despite sentiment-specific fine-tuning being standard practice. Could performance gains stem from *not* fine-tuning embeddings? A single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings may outperform the current setup. This confound requires explicit control.

- **Line 251 (shared dropout)**: Justify the choice of shared dropout units over layer-specific dropout.

- **Lines 255–256 (stability)**: Consider alternative regularization strategies like weight norm constraints or gradient clipping, common in recurrent networks.

- **Binary classification protocol**: The current implementation incorrectly includes neutral-class examples during training, violating the standard protocol. This renders results incomparable to prior work. Re-run this experiment strictly adhering to the protocol.

- **Depth utility**: The analogy to deep recurrent networks holds for node-wise predictions (as in the treebank). Crucially, the authors should test whether depth provides an advantage when only a *single root label* is predicted (e.g., on datasets with global sentiment labels). Does depth still improve performance over a single-layer RNN? This is essential to validate the core contribution.

**Recommendation**: The paper presents a promising extension of recursive networks, but requires three critical additions to strengthen its claims: (1) a proper binary classification experiment adhering to the standard protocol, (2) a controlled comparison with fine-tuned embeddings, and (3) experiments on datasets using only root-level labels. These additions would provide robust evidence for depth across space and significantly elevate the paper's impact. Without them, the current evidence for depth's utility remains preliminary. I recommend rejection pending these essential revisions.
