- Proposal: Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Networks that stack recursive layers with per-node vertical matrices V(i), untied leaf/internal parameters, ReLUs, shared-mask dropout, L2 on weights/activations, and AdaGrad with fixed pretrained word vectors.
- Evaluation: Tested on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (5-way) with comparisons to shallow RNNs, MV-RNN, RNTN, Paragraph Vectors, and DCNNs; reports new state-of-the-art and layer-wise qualitative analyses.
- Strength: Well-motivated separation of "depth in structure" vs "depth in space" with natural vertical connections analogous to stacked RNNs.
- Strength: Practical engineering choices—untied leaves/internals, ReLUs, and shared-mask dropout—are sensible for structured inputs and large pretrained embeddings.
- Strength: Empirical improvements: consistent gains of deep over shallow models at matched parameter budgets and new SST state-of-the-art.
- Strength: Clear training details (AdaGrad, initialization, early stopping) that aid reproducibility.
- Strength: Insightful qualitative analyses showing layer specialization (e.g., intensity vs polarity).
- Weakness: Possible test-set tuning leakage—many depth/width/dropout sweeps appear run on the test set; model selection protocol needs clarification.
- Weakness: Missing statistical reliability—no variance across random seeds or significance testing for small absolute differences (~0.5–1%).
- Weakness: Insufficient ablations—need controlled tests for (i) tying vs untying leaves, (ii) ReLU vs tanh within untied setup, (iii) dropout on/off, and (iv) word-vector fine-tuning vs freezing.
- Weakness: Vertical connection design unclear—should evaluate variants without per-node V(i) or with residual/normalized connections to isolate stacking benefit.
- Weakness: Training stability discussion incomplete—recommend exploring/reporting gradient clipping and other stabilization methods.
- Weakness: Notation/terminology risk—uses "RNN" for recursive networks, which can be confused with recurrent networks.
- Quality assessment: Technical claims well supported but main concerns are model-selection rigor and lack of variance estimates.
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written with intuitive figures; clarify positional DAGs and dev vs test usage for sweeps.
- Originality assessment: Stacking recursive layers with per-node vertical connections and leaf/untying is a novel and useful extension.
- Significance assessment: Advances SST state-of-the-art and offers transferable practical contributions for structured architectures.
- For acceptance: Simple, well-motivated idea; controlled parameter-budget comparisons; practical techniques (ReLUs, dropout) and illuminating qualitative analyses.
- Against acceptance: Potential test-set tuning, lack of statistical significance reporting, limited ablations, and single-dataset evaluation.
- Questions for authors: Clarify which hyperparameters were selected on dev vs test in Table 1(a) and provide dev-only selection if needed.
- Questions for authors: Did you try fine-tuning word vectors, and how did it affect performance and stability?
- Questions for authors: How sensitive are results to shared-dropout masks vs per-node independent dropout?
- Questions for authors: Did you explore gradient clipping, layer normalization, or other stabilization methods?
- Minor comments/typos: Fix typos ("classificationd", "evaulate"), rephrase "comprise a class of architecture(s)", and avoid tuning on the final test set.
- Minor suggestion: Briefly disambiguate "recursive neural networks (RNNs)" from "recurrent neural networks" early in the paper.
