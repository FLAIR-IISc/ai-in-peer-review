- Introduces Deep Recursive Neural Network (DRNN) by stacking recursive layers to model linguistic compositionality.  
- Evaluated on fine-grained sentiment classification and shows strong performance on standard benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; motivation and technical exposition are accessible.  
- Experimental setup and results are detailed enough to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Technical approach is sound with rigorous implementation and thorough evaluation.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies isolate the contribution of depth; results support that deeper recursive structures improve compositional performance.  
- Comparisons to strong baselines (standard RNNs, tree-LSTMs) are appropriate and convincing.  
- Empirical analyses, including error analysis and layer-wise visualizations, add valuable insight.  
- Originality is incremental: stacking depth is a straightforward extension of RNNs, though well-motivated for sentiment tasks.  
- Needs stronger discussion of why depth helps sentiment compositionality (link to linguistic phenomena like negation/intensifiers and failure cases of shallow models).  
- Requires clearer justification for architectural choices (no residuals/gating) and whether depth alone suffices versus other mechanisms.  
- Significant contribution as a simpler, interpretable alternative with potential applicability beyond sentiment; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions on depth and architecture rationale.
