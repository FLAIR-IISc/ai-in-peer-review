Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a new architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

The paper relates to previous work on recursive neural networks and their application to natural language processing tasks. The authors build upon the idea of recursive neural networks, which operate on structured inputs, and propose a deep version of this architecture. This is a natural extension of the original idea, as deep neural networks have been shown to be effective in many tasks.

The paper is well-written and pleasant to read. The authors provide a clear background description of recursive neural networks and their application to natural language processing tasks. The major idea of the paper, the deep recursive neural network, is nicely presented and easy to understand.

The paper seems technically sound, and the authors provide reasonable amounts of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are clearly presented.

The proposed idea is simple and reasonable, and the authors provide some intuition about why deeper networks might be beneficial for the target task. However, it would be beneficial to have more discussions about why intuitively deeper networks are good for the task and why the authors chose the selected network architecture.

The paper has presented an interesting idea, clear background description, and reasonable experimental supports. The authors also provide some qualitative analysis of the results, which helps to understand the behavior of the deep recursive neural network.

Arguments for acceptance:
- The paper introduces a new architecture, the deep recursive neural network, which is a natural extension of the original recursive neural network idea.
- The authors provide clear background descriptions and reasonable experimental results to evaluate the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and pleasant to read.

Arguments against acceptance:
- The idea, although simple and reasonable, might not be considered novel enough by some reviewers.
- The authors could provide more discussions about the intuition behind the deep recursive neural network and why it is beneficial for the target task.
- The experimental results, although reasonable, might not be considered extensive enough by some reviewers.

Overall, I think the paper is a good fit for NIPS, and I would recommend acceptance. However, I would like to see the authors address some of the minor issues mentioned above to strengthen the paper.
