Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper introduces a new architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and pleasant to read. The authors present their ideas in a clear and concise manner, making it easy to follow their argument.

Quality:
The paper appears to be technically sound. The major idea of the deep recursive neural network, which is constructed by stacking multiple layers of individual recursive nets, is a nice contribution. The authors provide reasonable amounts of experimental results to evaluate various aspects of the method. However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the results and their implications.

Originality:
The proposed idea is simple and reasonable. However, it would be beneficial to include more discussions on the following aspects:
- Intuitive explanations for why deeper networks are better suited for the target task would strengthen the authors' argument.
- A more detailed justification for the selected network architecture would provide valuable insights into the design choices made by the authors.

Significance:
The paper presents an interesting idea, provides a clear background description, and offers reasonable experimental support. The authors demonstrate the potential of deep recursive neural networks for compositionality in language, making a valuable contribution to the field. However, the impact of the paper could be further enhanced by addressing the aforementioned points and providing a more comprehensive analysis of the results.
