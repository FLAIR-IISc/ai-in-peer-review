This exciting paper introduces a new deep recursive neural architecture that obtains state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. The results from their new architecture are impressive, and the exposition is very clear.

The changes they propose to previous RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and experimentally demonstrated to work well. Specifically, they introduce the following key modifications:

- Untying the leaf nodes and nonterminal nodes, allowing for more flexible representations.
- Utilizing rectified linear units in the recursive setting, which enables more efficient training.
- Employing large unsupervised word vectors in conjunction with smaller hidden nonterminal nodes, effectively balancing the trade-off between representation capacity and model complexity.
- Designing a deep architecture that is not simply a straightforward replacement of a single neural network RNN layer with a deep layer, but rather introduces connections between the layers and tree nodes. It would have been an interesting comparison to investigate the impact of dropping the connection from matrix V, such that the outputs of the last hidden layer at each node are the only inputs to the next parent.

However, there is one methodological concern: the results in Table 1 (a) should have been reported on the development set, not the final test set. This is because the authors are effectively tuning hyperparameters on the test set, which may lead to overly optimistic performance estimates.

Additionally, there are a few minor typos that need to be corrected, including "comprise a class of architecture" and "sentiment classificationd".
