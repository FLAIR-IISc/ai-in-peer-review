- Introduces deep recursive networks extending depth into the spatial/tree dimension, using shared-parameter layers, dropout, ReLUs, and pre-trained word embeddings; evaluated on the sentiment treebank.  
- Method is novel and a logical generalization of recurrent techniques, showing strong performance and notable gains from dropout and ReLU.  
- Several modeling and experimental choices lack sufficient justification, warranting further investigation before acceptance.  
- Paper is well-written and provides enough detail to reproduce results.  
- Notation unclear (e.g., W^eta and W^xh); recommend separate equations for input vs. hidden computations and a summary of parameter matrices with dimensionalities.  
- Lines 148–154 are speculative; if based on data, state that explicitly and provide supporting evidence.  
- Missing baseline: include results from Kalchbrenner et al. (ACL 2013) convolutional sentence model for comparison.  
- Word embeddings were not fine-tuned; justify this choice or evaluate fine-tuning since it may affect task-specific representations and the observed gains.  
- Question the motivation for sharing dropout units across positions; explain or ablate this design choice.  
- For stability, consider and compare common alternatives like weight norm regularization or gradient clipping.  
- Binary classification setup is flawed: neutral examples should be excluded and the experiment run separately to be comparable to prior work.  
- Assess whether deep recursive networks help when only root/global labels are available by re-running experiments (including correct binary protocol, embedding fine-tuning) and testing on one or two additional datasets with only global labels.
