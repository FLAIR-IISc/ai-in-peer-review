- Introduces deep recursive neural networks that stack multiple recursive layers, using ReLUs, dropout, and fixed pre-trained embeddings, and reports strong state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank.

- Novel extension of recursive networks with reproducible results and useful analysis of architectural choices like ReLU and dropout.

- Model definition needs clarity: Equation (3) uses undefined notation (W^{η·}, W^{xh·}); separate equations for leaf vs. internal nodes and a parameter/dimensionality summary are requested.

- The justification for untying leaf and internal node weights is speculative and should be supported by empirical evidence.

- Not fine-tuning word vectors is a major experimental choice; a control comparing deep RNNs to a single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings is necessary to isolate the benefit of depth.

- Binary classification evaluation is incorrect (5-class model adapted post-hoc, neutrals included); a dedicated positive/negative-trained model is required for fair comparison.

- Generalizability is unclear because experiments are limited to SST with node-level supervision; tests on tasks with only root-level labels are needed to show broader applicability.

- Minor methodological queries: why share the same dropout units across all nodes in a sentence/layer, and why rely on L2 penalties on weights/activations rather than techniques like gradient clipping or weight-norm constraints.

- Recommendation: run the fine-tuning control, redo the binary experiment with correct protocol, and evaluate on sentence-level-label datasets to strengthen the paper's claims.
