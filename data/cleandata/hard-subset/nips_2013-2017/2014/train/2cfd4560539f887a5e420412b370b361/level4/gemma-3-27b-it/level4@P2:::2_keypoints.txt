- Introduces a deep recursive neural network that achieves state-of-the-art results on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank and is very well written.  
- Extends prior recursive/recurrent work with several motivated modifications: untied leaf vs. non-terminal representations, ReLUs, large pre-trained word vectors with smaller hidden units, and true depth with interlayer tree connections.  
- Suggests an informative ablation: remove the direct interlayer matrix V so parents receive only the last hidden layer output to clarify interlayer contribution.  
- Properly situates work within related literature, citing relevant prior work (e.g., Socher et al., Mikolov et al.).  
- Strength: strong empirical results that consistently outperform shallow and prior state-of-the-art models on SST.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated design choices with sound reasoning for each modification.  
- Strength: excellent writing and clarity, making the architecture and experiments easy to understand.  
- Strength: thorough experimentation (varying depth/width) and ablation-style analyses.  
- Strength: insightful qualitative analyses (input perturbation and nearest-neighbor analyses) showing layered compositionality.  
- Weakness: reporting results on the test set in Table 1(a) raises methodological concerns about hyperparameter tuning and potential test-set overfitting; development-set performance should be clarified/reported.  
- Weakness: minor typo—“comprise a class of architecture” should be “comprises a class of architectures.”  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, effective architecture with strong results, clear exposition, and valuable qualitative insights.  
- Argument for rejection: critical methodological issue around test-set reporting without clear development tuning invalidates confidence in results unless clarified.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant contribution to compositional semantics and deep learning; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying/correcting test-set tuning/reporting.
