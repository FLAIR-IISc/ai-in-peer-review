This paper introduces an exciting new deep recursive neural network architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. 

The results obtained with this new architecture are impressive, and the exposition is remarkably clear. The modifications the authors propose to previous RNN models are clever, well-motivated, and demonstrably effective through rigorous experimentation:

*   Untying the weights of leaf and nonterminal nodes.
*   Employing rectified linear units within the recursive framework.
*   Leveraging large, unsupervised word vectors in conjunction with smaller hidden representations for nonterminal nodes.
*   Introducing a genuinely deep architecture – one that goes beyond simply replacing a single neural network layer in a traditional RNN with a deep layer, and instead establishes connections *between* the layers and tree nodes. It would have been a valuable comparison to see results if the only input to each parent node was the output of the last hidden layer of its children, effectively removing the connection mediated by matrix V.

One concern is that the results presented in Table 1(a) appear to be reported on the final test set, rather than the development set. This suggests potential overfitting, as the model is being tuned based on performance on the test set.
