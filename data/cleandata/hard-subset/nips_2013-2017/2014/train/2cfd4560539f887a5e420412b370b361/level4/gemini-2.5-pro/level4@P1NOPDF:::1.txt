This paper introduces the deep Recursive Neural Network (deep RNN), a novel architecture constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate this model on the task of fine-grained sentiment classification.

**Clarity**
The paper is well-written and generally easy to follow. The background, proposed method, and experimental setup are all presented clearly.

**Quality**
The work appears to be technically sound. The central idea—creating a deep recursive neural network by stacking multiple recursive layers—is elegant and well-motivated. The authors support their claims with a sufficient set of experiments that evaluate various aspects of the proposed method, providing solid empirical validation for their approach.

**Originality**
The proposed idea is a simple yet effective extension of existing recursive models. While the empirical results are promising, the paper could be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of the underlying motivations for the architectural choices. Specifically, the paper would benefit from:

1.  A more thorough intuitive explanation of *why* adding depth to a recursive network is particularly advantageous for capturing the compositional semantics required for sentiment analysis. While the results demonstrate that it works, a more in-depth motivation would be valuable.
2.  A clearer rationale for the specific network architecture chosen. Expanding on the design decisions would help the reader understand why this particular configuration was selected over other potential alternatives for introducing depth.

**Significance**
This paper presents an interesting and novel idea, supported by a clear description of the background and reasonable experimental validation. The proposed deep RNN architecture is a valuable contribution to the field and is likely to be of interest to researchers working on compositional models of language.
