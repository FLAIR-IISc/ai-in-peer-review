- Introduces a novel deep recursive neural network for sentiment classification with state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with strong exposition of architecture and motivations.  
- Achieves impressive empirical performance, claiming a new state-of-the-art on fine-grained sentiment classification.  
- Proposes parameter untying (separate matrices for leaf vs. non-terminal nodes) to learn specialized word vs. phrase representations.  
- Successfully incorporates ReLU activations into the recursive architecture, a non-trivial and valuable contribution.  
- Uses large pre-trained unsupervised word vectors for inputs while keeping smaller hidden representations to control parameter growth.  
- Introduces a novel deep architecture with direct connections from input vectors to composition functions at each layer, which appears crucial to success.  
- Major methodological concern: hyperparameter tuning/model selection appears to have been done using the final test set, risking over-optimistic results and undermining SOTA claims.  
- Suggests an ablation study removing the direct input-to-composition (matrix V) connection to isolate its contribution.  
- Notes minor typographical errors (e.g., duplicated first sentence of the introduction) to correct in the final version.
