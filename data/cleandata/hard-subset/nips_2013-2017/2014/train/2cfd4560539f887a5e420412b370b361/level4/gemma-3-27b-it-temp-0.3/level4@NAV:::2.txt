This paper presents a novel deep recursive neural network architecture achieving state-of-the-art results on a challenging sentiment classification dataset. 
The reported performance is strong, and the paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
The modifications to existing recurrent neural network (RNN) models are insightful, well-justified, and validated through rigorous experimentation:
- Decoupling of leaf and nonterminal nodes.
- Implementation of rectified linear units within the recursive framework.
- Utilization of large, pre-trained word vectors alongside smaller hidden representations for nonterminal nodes.
- Development of a genuinely deep architecture, establishing inter-layer and tree node connections, rather than simply substituting a deep layer for a single RNN layer. A potentially valuable extension would be to compare performance with a configuration where only the final hidden layer output at each node serves as input to the parent node, effectively removing the connection mediated by matrix V.
