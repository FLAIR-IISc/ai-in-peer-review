- Proposes Deep RNN: stack multiple recursive layers with inter-layer weights to combine recursive structural processing and hierarchical representation learning.  
- Addresses limitation of conventional recursive nets being deep in structure but shallow in learned hierarchical representations.  
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (fine-grained sentiment with labels at every parse node).  
- Introduces an untied variant that distinguishes leaf (word) and internal (phrase) nodes, improving ReLU use and word/phrase handling.  
- Shows deep recursive networks outperform shallow counterparts with similar parameter counts; optimal performance depends on depth–width tradeoffs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art 49.5% accuracy on fine-grained SST, beating previous baselines (multiplicative RNNs, paragraph vectors).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with careful ablation studies demonstrating impacts of architectural choices.  
- Qualitative analyses indicate layer-wise compositionality: first layer captures lexical similarity, second syntactic structure, third sentiment.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, with effective figures and reproducible detail.  
- Positions work clearly relative to prior recursive network literature (Socher et al.) and NIPS structured neural net work.  
- Major methodological concern: Table 1(a) appears to tune on the test set; results should be reported on the development set.  
- Secondary concern: Lacks comparison to a constrained architecture that drops V connections to isolate depth vs width effects.  
- Rated highly across NIPS criteria: quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept with minor revision to address the development/test set issue.  
- Broader impact: Architecture is relevant to modeling compositionality and likely applicable to other structured prediction tasks beyond sentiment.
