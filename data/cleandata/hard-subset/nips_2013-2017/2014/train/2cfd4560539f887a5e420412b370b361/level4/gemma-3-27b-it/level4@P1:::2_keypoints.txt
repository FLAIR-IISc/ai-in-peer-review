- Proposes a novel deep recursive neural network that achieves state-of-the-art results on fine-grained sentiment classification with clear exposition.  
- Key technical changes: untied leaf vs. nonterminal parameters, ReLUs in recursive setting, large unsupervised word vectors with smaller nonterminal hidden nodes, and genuinely deep interlayer/tree connections.  
- Experimental results are strong and include layer-wise analyses showing different layers capture different compositional aspects.  
- Suggests an ablation: test variant where only last hidden-layer outputs feed the parent (removing matrix V) to clarify interlayer connection contributions.  
- Major concern: results in Table 1(a) appear reported on the test set, implying tuning on the test set and potentially optimistic performance estimates.  
- Weakness: ablation study is insufficient to isolate the effects of individual components (e.g., interlayer connections).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of generalization beyond sentiment analysis and other domains.  
- Minor typo: “comprise a class of architecture” should be “comprises a class of architectures” (or similar).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated architecture with comprehensive evaluation and significant contribution to NLP deep learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: test-set tuning issue undermines reported claims and lack of thorough ablation reduces insight into component contributions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance pending re-evaluation/reporting with a dedicated development set.
