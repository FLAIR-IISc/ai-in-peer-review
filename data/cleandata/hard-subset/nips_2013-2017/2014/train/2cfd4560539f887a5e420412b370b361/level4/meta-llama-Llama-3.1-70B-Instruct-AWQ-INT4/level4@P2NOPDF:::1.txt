Paper 1180: Deep Recursive Neural Networks for Compositionality in Language

This paper proposes a novel architecture, the deep recursive neural network (deep RNN), constructed by stacking multiple recursive layers. The authors evaluate the proposed model on fine-grained sentiment classification tasks.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and enjoyable to read. The authors effectively convey their ideas, making the content accessible to the target audience.

Quality:
The paper appears to be technically sound, presenting a coherent and well-structured argument. The core idea of the deep RNN, formed by stacking individual recursive nets, is a valuable contribution. The authors provide a reasonable amount of experimental results, allowing for a thorough evaluation of their method.

Originality:
The proposed idea is straightforward and reasonable. However, to further enhance the paper, the authors could provide additional discussions on the following points:

* Intuitive explanations for why deeper networks are beneficial for the target task would be helpful.
* A more in-depth justification for the chosen network architecture would strengthen the paper.

Significance:
The paper presents an interesting idea, provides a clear background description, and offers reasonable experimental support. Overall, the authors have made a notable contribution to the field, and their work has the potential to inspire further research in this area.
