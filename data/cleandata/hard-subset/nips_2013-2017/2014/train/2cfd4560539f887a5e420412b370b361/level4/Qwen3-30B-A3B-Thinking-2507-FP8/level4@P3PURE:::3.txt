

This paper proposes the use of 'deep' recursive networks by adding depth across spatial layers, motivated by the success of depth in recurrent networks. Each layer possesses its own parameters shared across different heights within that layer. The authors employ dropout and ReLUs while fixing word representations to pre-trained embeddings, evaluating on Socher et al.'s sentiment treebank.  

The approach is novel as a generalization of recurrent network depth strategies, achieving strong performance on the sentiment treebank. The gains from dropout and ReLUs are noteworthy. However, several model and experimental design choices lack sufficient justification, and additional experimentation is required before I can wholeheartedly recommend acceptance. The paper is clearly written with sufficient detail for reproducibility.  

**Detailed comments/questions:**  
- *Line 138:* Define $W^{\eta}$ and $W^{xh}$. Are these intended to refer to $W^{\eta}_L$ and $W^{\eta}_R$? Present separate equations for inputs and hidden layers, and summarize the parameter space $\{W_L, W_R, U, b, c\}$ with dimensionalities.  
- *Lines 148–154:* This claim appears speculative. If based on empirical results, cite them explicitly; otherwise, it lacks justification.  
- *Baseline:* Include results from "A convolutional neural network for modelling sentences" (Kalchbrenner et al., ACL 2014).  
- *Word embeddings:* Why not fine-tune embeddings? Fine-tuning on sentiment tasks makes word vectors sentiment-reflective, contrasting with discriminative phrase/sentence vectors. Could performance gains stem from not fine-tuning? A single-layer RNN with fine-tuned embeddings may outperform the current setup; this requires control.  
- *Line 251 (shared dropout):* Clarify the motivation for shared dropout units.  
- *Lines 255–256 (stability):* Consider weight norm constraints or gradient truncation (common in recurrent nets) as alternatives.  
- *Binary classification:* This must be re-run per the standard protocol. Training includes neutral-class examples, violating the experiment setup and making results incomparable to prior work.  
- *Generalization:* The analogy to deep recurrent nets holds for treebank experiments (node-wise predictions). However, demonstrate whether depth across space improves performance when only a root label exists (e.g., on datasets with global labels). Redo the binary classification experiment per protocol, test fine-tuning effects, and include 1–2 datasets using global labels. These additions would substantially strengthen the paper and evidence for spatial depth.
