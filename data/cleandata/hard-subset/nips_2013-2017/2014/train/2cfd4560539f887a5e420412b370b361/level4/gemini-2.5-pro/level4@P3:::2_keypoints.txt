- Introduces Deep RNN: stacks multiple recursive layers to add "depth in space" for hierarchical compositional representations.
- Untying leaf and internal node weights in the first layer enables use of large pre-trained word vectors and ReLU activations.
- Evaluated on Stanford Sentiment Treebank and achieves new state-of-the-art, outperforming strong baselines.
- Experiments show depth benefits over width and different layers capture progressively abstract syntax/semantics.
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and figures effectively illustrate model structure and information flow.
- Provides insightful qualitative analyses (input perturbation, nearest-neighbor phrases) supporting the hierarchical representation claim.
- Major methodological concern: hyperparameter/model selection appears to have been performed on the test set, risking overestimated generalization.
- Missing architectural ablation: unclear contribution of per-node vertical (V) connections versus simpler alternatives like applying an MLP after a shallow RNN.
- Question about justification for using a shared dropout mask across all nodes within a sentence layer versus per-node masks.
- Positive note on regularization analysis: dropout substantially improves performance over L2 in the 3-layer RNN comparison.
- Minor wording/typo suggestion for the abstract phrasing of "Recursive neural networks comprise a class of architecture."
