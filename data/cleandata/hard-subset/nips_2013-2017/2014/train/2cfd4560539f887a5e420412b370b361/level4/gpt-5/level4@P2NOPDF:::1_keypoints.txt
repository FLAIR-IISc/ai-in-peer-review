- Proposes a deep recursive neural network (DRNN) formed by stacking multiple recursive layers and evaluates it on fine-grained sentiment classification.
- Generally well written; background and motivation are clearly presented.
- Technical approach appears sound.
- Central idea—building depth by stacking recursive networks—is appealing and well justified.
- Experimental evaluation covers several aspects and provides reasonable empirical evidence.
- Idea is straightforward and sensible rather than radically novel.
- Needs more discussion on the intuitive reasons depth should help for the target task.
- Lacks rationale for the specific architectural choices and discussion of design alternatives.
- Offers an interesting contribution with clear exposition and solid empirical support.
- Likely to be of interest to the community given demonstrated performance on fine-grained sentiment analysis.
