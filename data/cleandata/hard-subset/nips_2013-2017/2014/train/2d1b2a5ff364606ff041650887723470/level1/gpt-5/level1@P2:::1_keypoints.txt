- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to enforce per-source time-sum priors in AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.
- Shows naive inclusion of non‑Markovian sum constraints breaks dynamic programming and derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation using auxiliary pairwise variables Hit.
- Connects the SAC objective to posterior regularization, clarifying the probabilistic interpretation.
- Empirically evaluates AFHMM+SAC on a toy dataset and UK HES (100 synthetic-mains, 9 real-mains households) versus AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Using national-average totals (NTC) as µ0 yields consistent, statistically significant improvements; oracle µ0 (TTC) gives larger gains.
- Convexity and formulation arguments are sound; using CVX for the studied scales is reasonable.
- The paper solves a relaxed CQP but does not specify or evaluate a rounding/projection procedure to obtain discrete states.
- Hyperparameters λi are fixed to 1 with no sensitivity analysis or cross-validated tuning reported.
- Parameter estimation is simplistic (k-means for means; fixed K=3 for appliances) and robustness to model order is not evaluated.
- Empirical gains (e.g., 29–45% relative improvements) are meaningful, but use of NTC as prior could advantage the method unless explicitly framed.
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 5 notation is dense; missing solver settings, stopping criteria, and post-processing details.
- Introducing global aggregate constraints in AFHMMs and the CQP relaxation is an original and useful contribution.
- SACs are practically relevant for energy disaggregation and transferable to other BSS tasks with aggregate priors.
- Pros: clear, well-motivated idea; principled convex relaxation; posterior-regularization link; empirical improvements on realistic data.
- Cons: no rounding strategy or fractionality analysis; limited λi and µ0 robustness tests; scalability concerns with CVX; simplistic model/training choices; missing comparisons to duration-based baselines and public benchmarks.
- Suggested improvements: describe/evaluate rounding, sensitivity to λi and µ0 (including hierarchical priors), per-appliance errors and failure cases, scalable solver (e.g., ADMM) and runtimes, and evaluation on public datasets (e.g., REDD).
- Overall recommendation: solid, relevant contribution with promising results — reviewer leans toward acceptance contingent on addressing clarity, rounding, sensitivity, and scalability issues.
