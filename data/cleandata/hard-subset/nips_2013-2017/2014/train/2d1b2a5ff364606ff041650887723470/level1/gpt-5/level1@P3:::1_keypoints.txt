- Paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to encode per-source total-signal knowledge and derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation using auxiliary transition variables; also links the objective to posterior regularization.

- Empirical results on synthetic and UK HES data show AFHMM+SAC improves NDE and SAE versus AFHMM and AFAMAP, including realistic mains-included settings.

Strengths
- Conceptually simple and general: SACs use widely available aggregate priors applicable beyond NILM.
- Sound convex relaxation: auxiliary variables yield a tractable CQP and PR derivation gives probabilistic semantics.
- Empirical value: consistent improvements on real data and an oracle variant showing headroom.
- Reported computational competitiveness with AFHMM/AFAMAP at considered scales.

Weaknesses / Questions
- Relaxation and rounding: CQP solutions are fractional; no rounding/projection procedure or quantification of resulting loss is provided.
- Sensitivity to µ0 and λ: performance may degrade with mis-specified µ0 and λi are fixed without tuning or sensitivity analysis.
- Parameter learning: means/transitions set heuristically (k-means); no joint learning/uncertainty treatment or identifiability discussion.
- Comparisons limited: other strong NILM baselines (semi-Markov, non-homogeneous FHMMs, Parson et al.) not evaluated; SACs not combined with AFAMAP’s one-switch constraint.
- Scalability concerns: polynomial but potentially large; no complexity bounds, problem sizes, solver details, or specialized solvers (e.g., ADMM) discussed.

Clarity / Reproducibility
- Notation is heavy; would benefit from a concise variable/dimension summary.
- Missing minor details: treatment of σt, numerical stability, initialization, stopping criteria, and consistency constraints linking Sit and Hit.

Originality
- Moderate: aggregate constraints relate to PR/GE/measurement-based learning, but the AFHMM-specific convex relaxation and NILM application are novel.

Significance
- Relevant to NILM and broader BSS tasks where totals are known, but dependence on accurate µ0 and limited baselines temper claims of broad impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide and evaluate rounding/projection schemes and report their effect.
- Combine SACs with transition/duration constraints (e.g., AFAMAP) and test.
- Model uncertainty in µ0 (slack variables, inequality constraints) and tune/learn λi.
- Evaluate on additional public NILM datasets (e.g., REDD) and report solver scaling metrics.
- Discuss household-specific priors and small calibration strategies.

Arguments for acceptance
- Simple, general idea with principled probabilistic grounding and a practical convex formulation; consistent empirical gains on realistic data.

Arguments against acceptance
- Reliance on accurate µ0, lack of rounding/interpretability discussion, limited baselines and combination with best practices, and scalability/implementation gaps.

Overall
- Borderline accept: useful and implementable contribution with promising results, but needs stronger robustness analysis, rounding strategy, broader comparisons, and scalability details.
