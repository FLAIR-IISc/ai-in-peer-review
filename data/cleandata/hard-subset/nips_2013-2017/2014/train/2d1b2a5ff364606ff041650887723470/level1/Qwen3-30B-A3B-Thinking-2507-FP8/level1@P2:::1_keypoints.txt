- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation, implemented in an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) framework.  
- Formulates SAC-constrained inference as a convex quadratic program, enabling efficient inference.  
- Demonstrates large empirical improvements: 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error vs. baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement vs. AFAMAP on real-world energy data.  
- Theoretical derivation is clear and correct; experiments include synthetic robustness tests and real HES dataset evaluation with statistically significant gains.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible with consistent notation and logical presentation.  
- SACs are novel for BSS and connected to posterior regularization, suggesting broader applicability beyond energy disaggregation.  
- Results are significant for energy disaggregation, with potential real-world impact for energy monitoring and demand management.  
- Limited demonstration of generalization: only applied to energy disaggregation, no experiments in other BSS domains (e.g., audio or biology).  
- Reliance on oracle "true total consumption" (TTC) in experiments raises concerns; robustness to real-world errors in constraint estimates (µi0) is not thoroughly explored.  
- Lacks comparisons to more recent energy disaggregation methods (e.g., deep learning or newer HMM variants from 2013–2014).  
- Insufficient runtime and scalability analysis; no detailed computational comparisons for larger numbers of appliances or longer time series.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel constraint type, sound theory, strong empirical validation, and clear writing.  
- Con-accept arguments: limited generalizability demonstration, practical issues in estimating constraints, and incomplete baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: strong, technically sound contribution with significant empirical gains; recommended for acceptance with high confidence.
