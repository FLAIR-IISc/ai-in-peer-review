This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to address the problem of blind source separation (BSS), which is inherently unidentifiable. The authors incorporate SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) and apply it to the energy disaggregation problem. The paper makes three main contributions: (1) introducing SACs for BSS problems, (2) developing a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs, and (3) evaluating the use of SACs on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results. The introduction provides a good overview of the BSS problem and the challenges associated with it. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good context for the proposed approach.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors propose a novel approach to incorporating domain knowledge into the BSS problem, which is a key challenge in this area. The use of SACs is a clever way to address the identifiability problem, and the development of a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs is a significant technical achievement.

The experimental results are impressive, with the proposed approach outperforming state-of-the-art methods on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison with other methods and an analysis of the computational complexity.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to incorporating domain knowledge into the BSS problem
* Significant technical contributions, including the development of a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs
* Impressive experimental results, with the proposed approach outperforming state-of-the-art methods on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of prior knowledge about the BSS problem and the AFHMM. While the authors provide a good overview of the problem and the proposed solution, the paper may be difficult to follow for readers without a strong background in this area.
* The experimental results are based on a single dataset, and it is unclear how well the proposed approach will generalize to other datasets.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes significant technical contributions to the area of BSS and energy disaggregation.
* The proposed approach is novel and has the potential to make a significant impact in this area.
* The experimental results are impressive, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach.

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of prior knowledge about the BSS problem and the AFHMM, which may make it difficult for readers without a strong background in this area to follow.
* The experimental results are based on a single dataset, and it is unclear how well the proposed approach will generalize to other datasets.

Overall, I believe that the paper makes significant technical contributions to the area of BSS and energy disaggregation, and the proposed approach has the potential to make a significant impact in this area. While there are some weaknesses to the paper, I believe that the strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend acceptance.
