- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to encode total-per-source priors (e.g., daily Wh) as quadratic penalties for blind source separation.  
- Instantiates SACs in an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation.  
- Introduces pairwise transition variables and derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation of a MAP problem.  
- Shows the CQP objective can also be obtained via posterior regularization, linking to constraint-based learning.  
- Empirically, AFHMM+SAC yields substantial, statistically significant improvements in NDE and SAE on the UK HES ultra-low-frequency dataset.  
- Experiments are thorough: multiple households, oracle vs. practical priors (TTC vs. NTC), significance testing, and runtime comparisons.  
- Technical contribution is sound: SAC regularizer is well motivated and CQP relaxation is carefully constructed with PSD structure ensuring convexity.  
- Posterior-regularization derivation provides useful conceptual clarity.  
- Clarity is generally good but notation is dense; optimization section lacks discussion of integrality/rounding and solver specifics (tolerances, convergence).  
- Reproducibility would benefit from a short algorithm box and more solver details.  
- Originality: novel encoding of aggregate-signal priors as SACs and an efficient convex relaxation for AFHMM inference.  
- Significance: broadly applicable idea with meaningful gains on realistic data, but reliance on aggregate priors and supervised parameter estimation may limit deployability.  
- Weakness: dependence on µi0 (aggregate priors) needs guidance for estimation under domain shift; limited robustness ablations beyond a toy study.  
- Weakness: no comparisons to duration-aware or k-segment baselines (e.g., semi-Markov models, Titsias et al.).  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—use of CVX for long sequences/many devices is unclear; a custom solver or block-coordinate scheme would help.  
- Weakness: rounding/integrality gap and its impact on discrete state recovery are not discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general, effective constraint addressing identifiability; solid convex relaxation with PR connection; significant empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical dependence on accurate aggregate priors, missing strong duration-based baselines, and underexplored scalability/rounding details.
