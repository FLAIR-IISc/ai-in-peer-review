- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge for identifiability in blind source separation by constraining each source’s total sum to a known prior.  
- Incorporates non-Markovian SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM).  
- Derives a tractable approximate MAP inference algorithm via a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation.  
- Novelty: global, aggregate constraints based on signal totals, differing from prior local-transition constrained FHMMs (e.g., AFAMAP).  
- Empirical impact: shows a 29% error reduction over a state-of-the-art baseline on a challenging low-frequency energy disaggregation dataset.  
- Generality: SACs are simple, intuitive, and likely applicable to other BSS domains where aggregate priors are known.  
- Technical quality: CQP formulation is well-derived and linked to posterior regularization, providing sound theoretical grounding.  
- Experimental rigor: thorough evaluation on synthetic and real data, including comparisons to an oracle and a realistic prior.  
- Weakness: CQP inference is an adaptation of existing methods rather than a wholly new algorithmic technique.  
- Weakness: regularization hyperparameter was set heuristically instead of being systematically tuned.  
- Overall verdict: excellent, significant, and clearly presented work with strong empirical results and broad potential impact.
