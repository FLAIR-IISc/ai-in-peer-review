

This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation using additive factorial hidden Markov models (AFHMMs). The authors address the identifiability problem in BSS by incorporating domain knowledge about expected total signal values for each source (e.g., a toaster's expected daily energy consumption). SACs are integrated into AFHMM through a convex quadratic program, avoiding the unidentifiability issues that plague standard BSS approaches. The work relates to prior NIPS contributions on posterior regularization (Ganchev et al., 2010) and energy disaggregation (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012), while offering a novel constraint framework that differs from previous approaches like AFAMAP, which constrains state transitions rather than signal aggregates.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with a clear derivation of the convex optimization problem and thorough experimental validation on real-world energy data. The SAC approach significantly improves performance over the baseline AFHMM (45% lower normalized disaggregation error) and a state-of-the-art method (29% improvement), with statistically significant results across multiple households. The authors carefully distinguish between using ideal (TTC) and realistic (NTC) constraint values, showing SACs remain effective even with imperfect prior knowledge.

Clarity is excellent, with well-organized sections, detailed mathematical derivations, and clear explanations of the novel constraint mechanism. The paper adequately references related work, including relevant NIPS publications.

Originality is strong, as SACs represent a new class of constraints for BSS that are general across applications and leverage domain knowledge in a novel way. The authors convincingly differentiate their approach from prior work, particularly in how they handle non-Markovian constraints.

Significance is high, as energy disaggregation has practical importance for energy conservation, and the SAC framework could extend to other BSS applications. The computational efficiency (comparable to existing methods) enhances practical applicability.

Weaknesses include limited discussion of constraint selection beyond the energy domain and minimal exploration of alternative constraint formulations. The toy data experiments, while informative, don't fully demonstrate scalability to larger problems.

Strengths: Novel constraint framework, strong empirical results, clear technical presentation, practical significance.
Weaknesses: Limited generalization discussion, minimal exploration of constraint selection strategies.

The paper makes a clear, significant contribution to the field of BSS with well-supported technical results. It advances the state-of-the-art in energy disaggregation while offering a broadly applicable method for incorporating domain knowledge into probabilistic models. I recommend acceptance.
