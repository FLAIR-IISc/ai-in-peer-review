- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in an AFHMM to incorporate prior aggregate energy knowledge and address BSS identifiability.
- Formulates SACs as a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.
- Shows significant performance improvements on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset versus the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.
- Well-motivated and clearly connects BSS challenges to practical energy disaggregation and interpretability needs.
- Related work is comprehensive and ties the approach to posterior regularization.
- Mathematical formulation and QP derivation are sound and clearly presented, though some derivation details could be expanded.
- Empirical results are convincing and statistically significant, including tests with realistic and oracle-like constraints.
- Novelty: focuses on aggregate-value constraints (readily available in many domains) rather than HMM state constraints.
- Technical soundness: approach is justified and relaxation effects are acknowledged.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, making key ideas accessible.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential real-world impact for energy management and consumer awareness.
- Weakness: limited analysis of computational complexity and scaling with number of states/chains.
- Weakness: insufficient sensitivity analysis of the tuning parameter δ (or λ).
- Weakness: experiments focus mainly on energy disaggregation; generalizability to other BSS domains is not demonstrated.
- Weakness: limited discussion of how SACs resolve identifiability and remaining uniqueness challenges.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel, technically solid, strong empirical gains, clear writing, and practical relevance.
- Con-acceptance arguments: need deeper computational and sensitivity analyses and broader validation across BSS domains.
- Overall assessment: reviewer recommends Strong Accept.
