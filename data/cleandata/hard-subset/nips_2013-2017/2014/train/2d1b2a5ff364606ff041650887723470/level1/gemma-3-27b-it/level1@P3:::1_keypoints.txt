- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within AFHMM to incorporate prior aggregate energy knowledge for improved energy disaggregation.
- Core idea is intuitive and addresses identifiability issues in blind source separation by leveraging approximate aggregate signals.
- Well-motivated connection to prior work in energy disaggregation and posterior regularization.
- Mathematical formulation is rigorous, reducing inference to a convex quadratic program solvable with CVX.
- Empirical results on the HES dataset show substantial improvement over AFHMM and Kolter & Jaakkola, including tests with/without mains readings.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with strong practical significance for energy conservation and smart homes.
- Novelty: SACs are a new and relevant way to inject domain knowledge into BSS problems.
- Technical soundness: derivations and CQP formulation are solid and practically implementable.
- Sensitivity: performance depends on accuracy of aggregate constraint values (µ0); robustness to misestimation is underexplored.
- Limited exploration of constraint generalizations (e.g., time-varying constraints) and no experiments on these extensions.
- Computational complexity: authors claim similar cost to other methods but lack detailed scaling analysis in Ki and T.
- Missing comparisons with other constraint-based methods (e.g., semi-Markov, k-segment) for a fuller benchmark.
- Hyperparameter tuning: choice of λ is ad hoc and would benefit from systematic selection (e.g., cross-validation).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical gains, well-presented, high practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to constraint values, limited generalization experiments, and insufficient complexity/benchmark analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution recommended for acceptance, though some aspects (robustness, extensions, complexity analysis) should be strengthened.
