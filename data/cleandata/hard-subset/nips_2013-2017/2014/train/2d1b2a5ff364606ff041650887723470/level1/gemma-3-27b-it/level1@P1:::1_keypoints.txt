- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate prior knowledge of appliance total energy into blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- Implements SACs within Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs) to address identifiability issues.  
- Formulates approximate inference with SACs as a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Demonstrates significant empirical gains on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset versus AFHMM and a recent SOTA.  
- Positions SACs as a specific, broadly applicable class of constraints and connects them to posterior regularization.  
- Builds on established BSS and energy disaggregation literature and uses AFHMM as a sensible baseline.  
- Novelty: framing aggregate domain knowledge as SACs is a clear and useful contribution.  
- Technical soundness: CQP derivation is well-motivated and mathematically reasonable, with relaxation acknowledged.  
- Empirical results: large improvements (45% decrease in NDE, 29% improvement over [12]) are compelling.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and concepts are explained clearly.  
- Evaluation: tested on simulated and real data with appropriate metrics (NDE, SAE) and baselines.  
- Sensitivity: performance degrades when the constraint value µ0 is inaccurate, raising robustness concerns.  
- Parameter tuning: fixed λi=1 across experiments; lack of systematic tuning or analysis of λi’s impact.  
- Computational cost: limited discussion of CQP solver scaling with number of appliances and time series length.  
- Generalizability: claims of broad applicability are not strongly supported by evaluations outside energy disaggregation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical gains, clear write-up, and theoretical grounding.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to prior accuracy, insufficient parameter tuning and scalability analysis, limited cross-domain validation.  
- Recommendation: overall strong paper; accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss handling uncertainty in µ0, analyze impact/tuning of λi, detail CQP scalability, and expand discussion of SACs’ applicability to other BSS domains.
