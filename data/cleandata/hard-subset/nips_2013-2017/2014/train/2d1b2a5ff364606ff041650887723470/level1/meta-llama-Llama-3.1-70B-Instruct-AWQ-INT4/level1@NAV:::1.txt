This paper introduces a novel approach to blind source separation problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem through signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach in the context of energy disaggregation, a challenging problem in which the goal is to separate the total household electricity usage into the consumption of individual appliances.

The main idea of the paper is to encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value, which is often available in advance. This is achieved by incorporating SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM), a commonly used model for blind source separation. The authors show that the use of SACs dramatically improves the performance of the AFHMM, resulting in a 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to the original AFHMM, and a significant improvement (29%) in performance compared to a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem, the proposed approach, and the experimental results. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and demonstrate a good understanding of the field. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear headings and concise paragraphs.
