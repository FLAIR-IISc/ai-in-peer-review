- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve AFHMMs for blind source separation.  
- Applies SACs to energy disaggregation and demonstrates significant improvements over AFHMM and recent state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough related-work review.  
- Technical soundness is strong with concise mathematical formulations and algorithms.  
- Main contribution: SACs encourage each source’s total signal to match specified values, addressing identifiability.  
- Presents a convex quadratic program for approximate inference in AFHMMs with SACs.  
- The proposed QP can be efficiently solved using standard optimization techniques.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough on both simulated and real-world data, showing significant gains.  
- Strength: novel approach to the identifiability problem in blind source separation.  
- Strength: clear and concise mathematical/algorithmic presentation.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation.  
- Strength: significant technical contribution in the form of the convex QP.  
- Weakness: assumes model parameters are known, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, and experimentally validated approach.  
- Arguments for rejection: practical parameter assumptions, missing complexity analysis, and limited discussion of limitations.  
- Overall: paper is significant and worthy of acceptance despite some limitations.
