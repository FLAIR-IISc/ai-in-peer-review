- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) to incorporate aggregate domain knowledge into AFHMM for blind source separation.  
- Introduces AFHMM+SAC by adding a quadratic penalty to the AFHMM posterior to enforce aggregate totals.  
- Penalty breaks Markov structure, so the authors relax the MAP integer program to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient approximate inference.  
- Links the penalized objective to posterior regularization via a KL-divergence minimization with expectation constraints.  
- Evaluated on toy data and HES energy disaggregation data, showing large error reductions (≈45% vs AFHMM, ≈29% vs AFAMAP).  
- CQP derivation is sound and adapts known relaxation techniques to the non-Markovian objective.  
- Experimental design is strong: toy experiments show performance vs aggregate accuracy; real data uses both synthetic and real mains and oracle vs practical constraints.  
- Reported improvements are substantial and statistically significant, supporting the method’s efficacy.  
- Assumes model parameters are known/pre-trained; lacks discussion of integrating SACs into EM-style parameter learning.  
- Regularization hyperparameter λi is fixed to 1 in experiments; no sensitivity analysis or principled tuning method is provided.  
- The CQP solves a relaxation; the paper does not quantify how close continuous solutions are to integral MAP solutions.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, consistent notation, and clear experiments.  
- Section 5 (CQP formulation) is dense; needs a higher-level intuitive explanation of variable transformations for broader readability.  
- The SAC idea is novel, simple, interpretable, and complementary to existing HMM constraints.  
- Casting the problem as a CQP is an original technical contribution distinct from prior constrained-HMM work.  
- The method is practically significant for energy disaggregation and likely generalizes to other domains with available aggregates.  
- Provides a clear, adoptable recipe for incorporating aggregate priors that should inspire follow-up work.  
- Pros: novel effective idea, sound convex relaxation, strong empirical gains, and clear presentation.  
- Cons: limited hyperparameter exploration, evaluation focused on one domain, and no quantitative assessment of CQP approximation quality.  
- Overall conclusion: strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses; strongly recommends acceptance.
