- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to enforce known total signal values for sources, addressing identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM and reformulates inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient optimization.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and Household Electricity Survey (HES) data, showing a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error over AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP.  
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization and situates the method within constraint-based learning literature.  
- Theoretical formulation and CQP transformation are mathematically precise and technically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, uses appropriate metrics (NDE, SAE) and statistical tests, and demonstrates robustness to approximate domain knowledge.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient technical detail and implementation information for reproducibility.  
- Figures (e.g., impact of constraint specification errors) effectively illustrate key findings.  
- SACs are a novel contribution to BSS, specifically targeting aggregate values not previously emphasized in the literature.  
- Approach is potentially generalizable to other domains (audio, biology) where total signal knowledge exists.  
- Main limitation: requires domain knowledge of total signal values and sensitivity to constraint specification errors needs further analysis.  
- Computational complexity analysis is brief and could be expanded, particularly for the CQP solution.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent energy disaggregation methods post-AFAMAP (2012) and does not explore unsupervised integration of SACs in depth.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, original, significant, and well-validated for the NIPS community.
