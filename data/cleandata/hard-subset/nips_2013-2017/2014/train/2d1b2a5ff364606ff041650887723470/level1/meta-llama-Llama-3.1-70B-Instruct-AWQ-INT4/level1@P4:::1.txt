This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve the performance of additive factorial hidden Markov models (AFHMMs) in blind source separation problems. The authors apply this approach to the energy disaggregation problem and demonstrate significant improvements over the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly presented. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and adequately reference previous contributions to the field. The technical soundness of the paper is strong, with a clear and concise presentation of the mathematical formulations and algorithms.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of SACs, which encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. This is a novel approach that addresses the identifiability problem in blind source separation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of SACs in improving the performance of AFHMMs in energy disaggregation tasks.

The paper also presents a convex quadratic program for approximate inference in AFHMMs with SACs, which is a significant technical contribution. The authors show that this approach can be efficiently solved using standard optimization techniques.

The experimental results presented in the paper are thorough and well-executed. The authors evaluate the performance of AFHMM+SAC on both simulated and real-world data, demonstrating significant improvements over the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to addressing the identifiability problem in blind source separation
* Clear and concise presentation of mathematical formulations and algorithms
* Thorough experimental evaluation on both simulated and real-world data
* Significant technical contribution in the form of a convex quadratic program for approximate inference

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the model parameters are known, which may not always be the case in practice
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed approach

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach to addressing the identifiability problem in blind source separation
* The technical soundness of the paper is strong, with a clear and concise presentation of mathematical formulations and algorithms
* The experimental results are thorough and well-executed, demonstrating significant improvements over existing approaches

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper assumes that the model parameters are known, which may not always be the case in practice
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed approach

Overall, I believe that the paper is well-written and presents a significant technical contribution to the field of blind source separation. While there are some limitations and potential areas for improvement, I believe that the paper is worthy of acceptance.
