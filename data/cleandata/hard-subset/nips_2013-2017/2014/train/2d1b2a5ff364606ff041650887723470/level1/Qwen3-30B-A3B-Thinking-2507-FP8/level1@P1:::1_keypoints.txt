- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation by adding SACs to an AFHMM and converting inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Situates work relative to AFHMM, posterior regularization, and AFAMAP, noting SACs are a specific, tractable instance of posterior regularization and orthogonal to AFAMAP’s constraints.  

Strengths:
- Novel and well-motivated idea: total-energy priors are practical and address a key identifiability issue in BSS.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: clear derivation of the CQP and explicit connection to posterior regularization.  
- Thorough experiments: synthetic and real-world evaluations with NDE and SAE metrics showing large gains (≈45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM, 29% vs AFAMAP).  
- Practical relevance: method works with approximate national consumption data (NTC), increasing real-world applicability.  
- Computationally efficient: AFHMM+SAC scales comparably to AFHMM and AFAMAP, making it practical.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited sensitivity analysis: paper lacks detailed quantification of how tolerant performance is to errors in constraint values.  
- Sparse discussion of constraint acquisition: no general strategies for obtaining µi0 in domains beyond energy where priors are limited.  
- Vague complexity claims: states "polynomial time" but does not provide explicit complexity bounds or formulas.  
- Limited exploration of complementary constraints: does not investigate combining SACs with other constraint types (e.g., AFAMAP’s state-change constraints).  

Overall assessment:
- Strong, original, and significant contribution with sound methodology, clear presentation, and convincing results.  

Recommendation:
- Accept; encourage adding sensitivity analysis and a clearer discussion on acquiring constraints in practice (areas for future improvement).
