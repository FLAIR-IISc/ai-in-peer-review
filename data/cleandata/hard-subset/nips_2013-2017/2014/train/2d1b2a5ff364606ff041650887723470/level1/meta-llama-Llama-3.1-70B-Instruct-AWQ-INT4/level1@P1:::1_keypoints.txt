- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge into blind source separation within additive factorial HMMs for energy disaggregation.
- Incorporates SACs into an AFHMM and uses a convex quadratic program for approximate inference from a single observed mixture.
- Main ideas are well-motivated, clearly explained, and balanced between theory and experiments.
- Provides a thorough related-work review and is well-organized with clear writing.
- Strength: Introduction of SACs as a novel way to guide source recovery toward known aggregate values.
- Strength: Development of a convex QP for inference that is computationally efficient and scalable in practice.
- Strength: Experimental validation on real-world energy disaggregation data demonstrating effectiveness.
- Weakness: Assumes a finite set of states per Markov chain, which may be unrealistic for many applications.
- Weakness: Uses a simple Gaussian noise model that may not capture real-world noise complexity.
- Weakness: Lacks a thorough analysis of the computational complexity, raising concerns for large-scale use.
- Overall assessment: Significant, novel contribution to blind source separation and energy disaggregation.
