This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve the performance of additive factorial hidden Markov models (AFHMMs) in blind source separation problems, specifically in the context of energy disaggregation. The authors propose a novel way to incorporate domain knowledge into the AFHMM by adding constraints on the aggregate values of the source signals. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of SACs, which encourages the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. This is a reasonable assumption in many applications, including energy disaggregation, where the total energy consumption of each appliance is often known in advance. The authors show that the use of SACs significantly improves the performance of the AFHMM, resulting in a 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to the original AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the related work, the problem setting, and the proposed solution. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed method using both simulated and real-world data.

One of the strengths of the paper is the use of a convex quadratic program for approximate inference, which makes the solution computationally efficient. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the optimization problem and the relaxation of the problem, which makes the solution more tractable.

However, there are a few limitations to the paper. Firstly, the authors assume that the model parameters are known, which may not be the case in practice. Secondly, the authors use a simple k-means clustering algorithm to estimate the model parameters, which may not be the most effective way to estimate the parameters. Finally, the authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is an important consideration in practice.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed solution and the experimental results. The introduction of SACs is a novel contribution to the field, and the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of AFHMMs in blind source separation problems.
