1. Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs), a simple, intuitive, and novel way to incorporate aggregate-domain knowledge into blind source separation.

2. Applies SACs to energy disaggregation by integrating them into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) to separate household total electricity into appliance-level signals.

3. SACs create non‑Markovian dependencies across time, making standard inference (e.g., Viterbi) inapplicable.

4. Formulates MAP inference with SACs as a penalized optimization and derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation to make inference tractable.

5. Connects the penalized objective to posterior regularization, providing a principled Bayesian interpretation.

6. Differs from prior constraints approaches (e.g., AFAMAP, semi‑Markov) by constraining aggregate signal values rather than transitions or durations.

7. Demonstrates strong empirical performance: ~29% error reduction over a state‑of‑the‑art method on a challenging real-world HES dataset.

8. Experimental evaluation is thorough: toy robustness tests, benchmarking against AFHMM and AFAMAP, oracle TTC experiment, and statistical significance testing (t‑tests).

9. Paper is well written, clearly motivated, and logically presents the model and CQP formulation.

10. Hyperparameter treatment is weak: penalty λi fixed to 1 with no sensitivity analysis or principled tuning procedure discussed.

11. Generality claim is under-supported: evaluation and discussion are limited to energy disaggregation with no examples from other BSS domains.

12. Training details for underlying HMM parameters are sparse (e.g., k‑means clustering, Ki=3 choice), which hampers reproducibility.

13. Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and impactful contribution with minor issues; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
