- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation (BSS).
- Proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) augmented with SACs to push each source’s total signal toward specified values.
- Motivated for applications where source totals vary widely and prior total-value knowledge exists.
- Well-written paper with clear motivation and concise presentation of the problem.
- Provides a detailed derivation of a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.
- Experimental results on toy and real-world energy disaggregation data show significant improvements over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.
- Strength: novel and effective approach to BSS using domain knowledge via SACs.
- Strength: clear technical exposition and derivation.
- Strength: strong empirical performance on both synthetic and real data.
- Weakness: assumes model parameters are known, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: simplified modeling assumption that each source is a finite-state HMM may not capture complex real-world signals.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis despite some discussion of efficiency.
- Overall: significant contribution with promising applicability, but concerns about parameter assumptions, modeling realism, and complexity analysis inform arguments against acceptance.
