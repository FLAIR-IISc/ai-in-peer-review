- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate appliance-level total energy priors into blind source separation for AFHMMs.  
- Converts the constrained inference problem into a convex quadratic program enabling efficient inference (solved with CVX).  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. AFHMM and 29% improvement vs. a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Novelty: focuses on aggregate-value constraints as a practical and previously underexplored prior for disaggregation.  
- Technical soundness: optimization formulation and convex relaxation appear correct and well-explained.  
- The connection to the posterior regularization framework is clearly articulated and provides theoretical grounding.  
- Writing and presentation are strong: clear motivation, notation, and organization.  
- Strength: addresses identifiability issues by incorporating domain knowledge, yielding significant performance improvements.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (λi, δi) and their effect on performance.  
- Weakness: reliance on known aggregate values (µi0) may limit applicability; methods for estimating these values are not explored.  
- Weakness: scalability claims lack detailed empirical or theoretical analysis for many appliances or large state spaces.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit other constraint-based methods (e.g., k-segment constraints, non-homogeneous HMMs) beyond AFAMAP.  
- Recommendation: overall positive — strengths outweigh weaknesses and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
