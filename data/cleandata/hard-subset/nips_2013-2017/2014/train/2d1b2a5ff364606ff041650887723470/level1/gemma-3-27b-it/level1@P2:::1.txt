This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) within an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to improve blind source separation, specifically in the context of energy disaggregation. The core idea is to incorporate prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of different appliances, addressing the inherent identifiability issues in BSS. The authors formulate this as a convex quadratic program for approximate inference and demonstrate significant performance improvements on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset compared to the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the challenges of blind source separation and the importance of incorporating domain knowledge. The introduction effectively connects the problem to the practical application of energy disaggregation, highlighting the need for interpretable source signals. The related work section is comprehensive, covering relevant literature in energy disaggregation, probabilistic modeling, and posterior regularization. The mathematical formulation of the AFHMM+SAC model and the derived convex quadratic program are presented clearly, though the derivation could potentially benefit from slightly more detailed explanations for readers less familiar with the underlying optimization techniques. The experimental results are convincing, showing substantial improvements in disaggregation accuracy with SACs, both with realistic and oracle-like constraint values. The comparison to existing methods like AFAMAP is valuable.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of signal aggregate constraints is a novel approach to incorporating domain knowledge into BSS. While prior work explores constraints on HMM states, this paper focuses on aggregate values, which are often readily available in many applications.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation and the derivation of the convex quadratic program appear sound and well-justified. The authors acknowledge the relaxation involved and its potential impact on optimality.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on real-world energy disaggregation data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach and its superiority to existing methods. The results are statistically significant and clearly presented.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the key ideas and technical details. The connection to posterior regularization is a helpful addition.
* **Significance:** The problem addressed is important, and the results suggest a practical solution with potential for real-world impact in energy management and consumer awareness.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While the authors mention computational scaling, a more detailed analysis of the computational cost associated with solving the convex quadratic program for larger problems would be beneficial. The impact of the number of states and chains on runtime should be discussed more explicitly.
* **Sensitivity Analysis:** The sensitivity of the results to the choice of the tuning parameter δ (or equivalently λ) in the SAC formulation is not thoroughly investigated. A more systematic evaluation of the parameter's impact on performance would strengthen the analysis.
* **Generalizability:** The experiments primarily focus on energy disaggregation. While the authors argue for the general applicability of SACs, exploring their effectiveness in other BSS domains would further demonstrate their broader impact.
* **Limited Discussion of Identifiability:** While acknowledging the identifiability problem, the paper could delve deeper into how SACs help mitigate this issue and the remaining challenges in obtaining unique source signal estimates.



**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to address a challenging problem in blind source separation.
* The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods on a real-world dataset.
* The clear writing and organization make the paper accessible and understandable.
* The proposed method has potential for practical impact in energy management and related applications.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

* The computational complexity analysis could be more detailed.
* A more thorough sensitivity analysis of the tuning parameter is needed.
* The generalizability of the approach to other BSS domains remains largely unexplored.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field and deserves acceptance to NIPS. The proposed approach is novel, technically sound, and empirically validated. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. The paper addresses an important problem and offers a promising solution with potential for real-world impact. I would rate this paper as a **Strong Accept**.
