- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation by aligning each source’s total signal with expected aggregates.
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model, forming a novel AFHMM+SAC model.
- Proposes a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation to efficiently solve the MAP inference problem.
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains on energy disaggregation datasets, notably large reductions in NDE and SAE versus AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Novelty: SACs are a creative, domain-knowledge-driven approach that can generalize where aggregate signal information exists.
- Technical soundness: Rigorous mathematical formulation and a justified, computationally efficient CQP inference method.
- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world data showing significant performance improvements (e.g., ~45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM).
- Clarity: Contributions (SACs, CQP derivation, evaluation) are clearly presented.
- Relevance: Addresses an important, practical problem in energy disaggregation with implications for efficiency and sustainability.
- Limited scope: Paper does not explore SAC applicability to other BSS domains like audio or biology, limiting generalizability.
- Dependence on prior knowledge: Method requires accurate aggregate priors, which may not be available or reliable in many real-world settings.
- Lack of broader comparisons: Missing detailed comparisons with alternative constraint types (e.g., semi-Markov or k-segment constraints).
- Implementation complexity: CQP solution may need specialized tools (e.g., CVX), potentially limiting practitioner accessibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, rigorously developed, empirically strong, and relevant to the community.
- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on prior knowledge and limited comparisons/applicability could constrain impact.
- Recommendation: Accept, with a suggestion to investigate SAC generalizability and robustness to limited prior knowledge in future work.
