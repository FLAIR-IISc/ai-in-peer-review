- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to address identifiability in blind source separation by constraining each source's total signal.  
- Incorporates SACs into Additive Factorial HMMs and formulates inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient optimization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% NDE reduction over AFHMM and 29% over AFAMAP on real-world energy disaggregation data, evaluated with NDE and SAE.  
- Connects SACs to the posterior regularization framework, providing theoretical grounding and viewing SACs as a prior over source signals.  
- Well-written and clearly organized with precise mathematical formulation and logical flow.  
- SACs are practically motivated for energy disaggregation where domain knowledge about appliance energy totals exists (e.g., daily Wh).  
- Main limitation: assumes known true total signals (µi0), which are rarely available in practice and makes oracle results (TTC) unrealistic.  
- Uses national total consumption (NTC) as a proxy but does not provide robust methods for estimating µi0 for new households or appliances.  
- The CQP is a relaxation of the original problem, but the paper lacks analysis quantifying the gap between the relaxed solution and the true optimal solution.  
- Comparison to related constraint approaches (e.g., k-segment constraints, non-homogeneous HMMs) is limited and could better distinguish SACs' novelty and complementarity.  
- Claims polynomial-time complexity but does not specify exact complexity or scalability with number of appliances, time steps, or states.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and practically useful contribution with solid empirical and theoretical support, but practical applicability is limited by reliance on known aggregates.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strengths in novelty, results, and theory outweigh weaknesses, with clear avenues for future work on estimating aggregate values.
