- Addresses unidentifiability in single-channel blind source separation by introducing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).  
- Formalizes SACs within an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM).  
- Core technical contribution: MAP inference in AFHMM with SACs via penalized log-likelihood and a continuous relaxation to a Convex Quadratic Program (CQP).  
- Establishes a connection between the penalized objective and the posterior regularization framework.  
- Empirical evaluation on energy disaggregation shows substantial gains, reducing error by >29% versus AFAMAP.  
- Experimental setup is thorough, using realistic (NTC) and oracle (TTC) settings and two error metrics (NDE and SAE).  
- High technical quality: well-motivated method and sound CQP derivation.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with transparent presentation of experiments.  
- Original idea: SACs are a novel, simple, and effective way to address label switching/unidentifiability.  
- CQP relaxation application to constrained AFHMMs is a solid technical contribution.  
- Significant impact for energy disaggregation and potential applicability to other BSS problems.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter λ set to 1 without sensitivity analysis or cross-validation exploration.  
- Weakness: missing explicit details on converting fractional CQP state assignments into final disaggregated signals and on solution “peakedness.”  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—CQP size grows with time T and limits for longer time series are not discussed.  
- Weakness: assumes pre-trained HMM parameters; lacks discussion of incorporating SACs into parameter learning (e.g., EM).  
- Overall conclusion: strong paper with novel idea and convincing results; recommend acceptance subject to clarifying hyperparameter choice, CQP output usage, scalability, and learning integration.
