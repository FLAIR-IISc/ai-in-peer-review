- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation with AFHMMs.  
- Tackles BSS identifiability by incorporating domain knowledge of expected total signal values per source.  
- Integrates SACs into AFHMM via a convex quadratic program, avoiding standard BSS unidentifiability.  
- Positions work relative to posterior regularization (Ganchev et al., 2010) and energy disaggregation literature (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012).  
- Novel constraint framework distinct from AFAMAP, focusing on signal aggregates rather than state-transition constraints.  
- Strong technical derivation and clear formulation of the convex optimization problem.  
- Thorough experimental validation on real-world energy data with statistically significant results.  
- Substantial empirical gains: 45% lower normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method.  
- Demonstrates robustness of SACs with both ideal (TTC) and realistic/noisy (NTC) constraint values.  
- Clear, well-organized exposition with detailed math and appropriate citations.  
- High originality and generality: SACs represent a new class of constraints for BSS and handle non-Markovian information.  
- High significance and practical applicability to energy disaggregation and other BSS problems; computational cost comparable to existing methods.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on selecting constraints beyond the energy domain and minimal exploration of alternative constraint formulations.  
- Weaknesses: toy experiments are informative but do not fully prove scalability to larger problems.  
- Overall verdict: clear, significant contribution with strong empirical support; recommends acceptance.
