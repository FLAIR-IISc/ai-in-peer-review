- Introduces SACs (signal aggregate constraints) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.
- Method enforces each source’s total signal to match a specified value by integrating SACs into an AFHMM.
- Empirical results: SAC-AFHMM reduces normalized disaggregation error by 45% versus AFHMM and improves 29% over a recent state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and well-organized with thorough related work.
- Strength: novel approach that effectively leverages domain knowledge for a challenging problem.
- Strength: strong experimental demonstration in the energy disaggregation context.
- Weakness: assumes per-source total signals are known in advance, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Weakness: does not compare against approaches that do not use SACs.
- Originality: use of SACs is a new idea in energy disaggregation and blind source separation.
- Significance: has potential impact on blind source separation and energy disaggregation research.
- Quality: technically sound and demonstrates good command of the field.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear exposition, and strong empirical gains.
- Arguments for rejection: problematic data assumption, missing complexity analysis, incomplete comparisons.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
- Rating: 8/10.
