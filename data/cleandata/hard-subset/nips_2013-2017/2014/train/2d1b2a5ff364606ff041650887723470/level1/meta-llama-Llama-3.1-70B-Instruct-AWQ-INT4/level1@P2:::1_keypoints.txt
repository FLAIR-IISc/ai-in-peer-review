- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS) and integrates them into an AFHMM for energy disaggregation.  
- Three main contributions: (1) SACs for BSS, (2) convex quadratic program for relaxed AFHMM with SACs, (3) evaluation on a real-world dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a comprehensive related work section.  
- Novel technique for incorporating domain knowledge to address identifiability in BSS.  
- Significant technical contribution: development of a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs.  
- Experimental results outperform state-of-the-art on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including comparisons with other methods and computational complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial prior knowledge of BSS and AFHMM, may be hard for non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset, so generalization is unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant technical novelty, strong empirical performance, and potential impact.  
- Arguments for rejection: accessibility concerns for non-experts and limited experimental validation across datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
