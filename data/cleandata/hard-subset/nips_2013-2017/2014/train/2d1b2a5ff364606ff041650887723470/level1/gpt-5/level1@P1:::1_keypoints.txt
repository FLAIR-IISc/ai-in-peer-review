- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs): soft priors on total signal (e.g., energy) per source to aid blind source separation in AFHMMs.  
- Derives a relaxed convex quadratic program (CQP) for MAP inference using auxiliary variables and links the objective to posterior regularization.  
- Tests on low-sampling-rate household electricity (HES) data and reports substantial NDE/SAE improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP, including with measured mains.  
- Novelty: identifies aggregate-over-time constraints as a practical prior for BSS and gives a tractable convex relaxation for AFHMMs.  
- Strength: clear, realistic motivation—aggregate priors are easier to estimate and applicable beyond energy (audio/bio).  
- Strength: sound convex relaxation with principled probabilistic interpretation via posterior regularization.  
- Strength: evaluation on a challenging, realistic dataset with statistical significance tests and two scenarios (with/without mains).  
- Strength: simple to incorporate, orthogonal to other constraints, and extensible to windowed SACs.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on accurate priors µi0; limited study of sensitivity to λ and misspecification on real data.  
- Weakness: CQP yields fractional solutions; paper omits how fractional Si/Hi are converted to discrete sequences or final signals.  
- Weakness: scalability and solver details are sparse (uses CVX); per-iteration complexity and feasibility for larger problems are unreported.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons—no evaluation against duration-aware HSMMs, non-homogeneous transition models, or AFAMAP+SAC baseline.  
- Weakness: simplistic modeling choices (Ki=3 via k-means, sparse details on σt and parameter estimation), hindering reproducibility.  
- Quality: formulation is technically sound and empirically supported, but implementation details (rounding, λ selection, solver tolerances) are missing.  
- Clarity: generally well written but dense notation in Section 5; needs variable dependency schematic and pseudocode/algorithm box.  
- Clarity: must explain post-processing from relaxed solutions to final estimates and whether outputs are continuous or discretized.  
- Originality: not novel as a framework (posterior regularization instantiation) but contributes a useful, non-trivial application of aggregate constraints and convex relaxation.  
- Significance: large reported improvements (~29–45% in NDE/SAE) on low-rate HES data are practically meaningful and transferable to other domains.  
- Suggested improvements: detail rounding/projection steps, evaluate AFAMAP+SAC or HSMM baselines, report λ sensitivity and selection strategy, and add scalable optimization (e.g., ADMM) and complexity analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical, domain-relevant prior with principled convex formulation and significant empirical gains on realistic data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited methodological novelty, missing comparisons and scalability/rounding details, and under-explored hyperparameter robustness.  
- Recommendation: lean toward accept, conditional on clarifications about rounding, scalability, additional baselines, and hyperparameter handling.
