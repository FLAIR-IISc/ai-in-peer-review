- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into AFHMMs for blind source separation/energy disaggregation.  
- SACs encourage each source’s total signal to match a specified aggregate, a realistic assumption in many applications.  
- Use of SACs yields large empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs original AFHMM and 29% improvement vs a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Presents experiments on both simulated and real-world data with clear, well-presented results.  
- Uses a convex quadratic program for approximate inference, making the solution computationally efficient and tractable.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and notation/derivations are clearly explained.  
- Main limitation: assumes model parameters are known, which may not hold in practice.  
- Parameter estimation relies on simple k-means clustering, which may be suboptimal.  
- Does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.  
- Quality score: 8/10 — technically sound with some practical limitations.  
- Clarity score: 9/10 — clear exposition and explanations.  
- Originality score: 8/10 — SACs are novel, but convex QP inference is not new.  
- Significance score: 9/10 — results are impactful for energy disaggregation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel incorporation of domain knowledge, strong empirical improvements, potential field impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic parameter assumptions, simplistic parameter estimation, missing complexity analysis.
