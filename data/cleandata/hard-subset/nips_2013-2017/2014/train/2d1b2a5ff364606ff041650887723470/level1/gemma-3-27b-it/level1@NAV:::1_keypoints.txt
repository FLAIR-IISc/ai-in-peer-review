- Tackles blind source separation for energy disaggregation by introducing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to encourage source totals to match expected values.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM and formulates approximate inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Evaluated on the HES dataset, showing substantial disaggregation improvements over baseline AFHMM and Kolter & Jaakkola (2012).  
- SACs are an intuitive, novel way to address identifiability, building on prior ideas like posterior regularization and measurement-based learning.  
- The CQP formulation is a significant technical contribution, making inference computationally tractable.  
- Empirical results are compelling and indicate potential real-world impact for energy disaggregation.  
- Paper insufficiently explores limitations, notably sensitivity to inaccurate or unavailable aggregate priors (µi0).  
- The choice of a quadratic penalty in the optimization is not well-justified; alternative penalties and sensitivity analysis are needed.  
- The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is tenuous and adds little insight.  
- Presentation lacks statistical detail (e.g., error bars or confidence intervals) to assess result variability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on revisions addressing the noted limitations and strengthening empirical/statistical analysis.
