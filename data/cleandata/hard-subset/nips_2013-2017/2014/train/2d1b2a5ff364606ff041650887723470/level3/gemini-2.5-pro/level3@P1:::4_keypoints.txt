- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) as a quadratic penalty to encode known per-source aggregate totals into AFHMMs, solved via a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation.  
- Applies AFHMM+SAC to energy disaggregation and shows significant improvement over AFHMM baseline and AFAMAP on large, real-world UK datasets.  
- Tackles the important unidentifiability in blind source separation by leveraging practical, easily available domain knowledge (e.g., average appliance consumption).  
- SACs are a novel, intuitive global regularizer that constrain long-horizon aggregate behavior, complementing existing local constraints.  
- The method has a solid theoretical grounding through a connection to posterior regularization.  
- Strong experimental evaluation: 100-household synthetic aggregation and 9-household real mains tests, plus an oracle true-total-consumption (TTC) experiment.  
- Empirical gains over a strong baseline (AFAMAP) are substantial and statistically significant.  
- Paper could be clearer about terminology and baselines—explicitly state that AFHMM is the model and AFAMAP/AFHMM+SAC are inference objectives, and specify the AFHMM baseline setup (e.g., λi=0).  
- Missing analysis of CQP solution integrality—unclear whether SACs produce more integral (near-discrete) solutions or succeed with fractional solutions.  
- SACs are largely orthogonal to AFAMAP constraints; the paper should evaluate a combined AFAMAP+SAC approach to test complementarity.  
- For acceptance: simple, novel idea with thorough, convincing empirical validation and practical impact for the community.  
- Against acceptance: idea is a relatively straightforward extension of existing CQP AFHMM inference and lacks deeper analysis and combination experiments.  
- Conclusion: solid, impactful paper with strong empirical support; accept with suggested clarifications and additional experiments.
