- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to address identifiability in blind source separation using domain knowledge about total signal aggregates.  
- Proposes SACAFHMM, which constrains each hidden chain’s sum to known aggregates and yields a convex quadratic program for efficient MAP inference.  
- Applies the method to large-scale energy disaggregation (100 low-frequency and 10 higher-frequency UK homes) with significant improvements over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- The convex relaxation is technically sound and well-justified via a connection to posterior regularization.  
- Empirical results are convincing: 33% and 16% improvements in NDE and SAE over AFAMAP, with comparable computational complexity.  
- Paper conflates model names and inference procedures; must clarify AFAMAP is an approximate MAP inference algorithm for AFHMM and what inference produced the AFHMM results.  
- Unclear whether SACAFHMM produces more integral solutions (like AFAMAP’s “one chain state at a time”) or simply lower error via non-integral solutions; this should be investigated.  
- Combining SAC constraints with AFAMAP is a promising direction and could yield further improvements; the paper should explore this.  
- Overall: well-executed, significant practical contribution with strong empirical validation; minor clarifications would improve technical precision.
