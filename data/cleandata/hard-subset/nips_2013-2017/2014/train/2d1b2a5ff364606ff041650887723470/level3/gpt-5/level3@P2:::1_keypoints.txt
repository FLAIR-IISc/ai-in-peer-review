- Summary: Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to encode known source totals, formulating MAP with SACs and a relaxed convex quadratic program (CQP) solved via CVX; applied to energy disaggregation with gains over AFAMAP.

- Relation to prior work: Connects SACs to posterior regularization and prior constrained latent-variable methods (e.g., Ganchev et al., Mann & McCallum, Liang et al.).

- Key idea: Enforce ∑t xit ≈ µi0 to improve identifiability without micromanaging transitions.

- Inference method: Introduces auxiliary transition variables Hit to relax to a CQP enabling tractable optimization with standard solvers.

- Empirical results: Strong, statistically significant improvements in NDE and SAE on toy and HES datasets while maintaining comparable runtime to AFAMAP.

- Strength — modeling: Simple, useful global-aggregate constraint directly addresses identifiability.

- Strength — integration: Solid integration with AFHMMs and an efficient relaxed inference; posterior-regularization derivation situates the method in existing frameworks.

- Strength — empirical: Robust improvements on realistic low-frequency data; results consistent and significant.

- Strength — generality: Likely transferable to other BSS domains where aggregate priors exist.

- Weakness — convexity: Paper lacks a formal proof/statement that the relaxed objective in (6) is convex; request to provide a formal argument and assumptions.

- Weakness — complexity/scalability: Needs explicit complexity analysis (T, I, Ki), variable/nonzero counts, and discussion of interior-point solver limits vs AFAMAP/Viterbi.

- Weakness — toy realism/SNR: Toy noise σ2=0.01 may be unrealistically low; report SNRs and sensitivity of results to noise levels for toy and mains experiments.

- Weakness — hyperparameters: λi fixed to 1; request sensitivity analysis, cross-validation guidance, and robustness to misspecified µi0.

- Weakness — baseline fairness: Provide full details on AFAMAP/AFHMM parameter estimation and tuning; clarify “oracle” TTC framing and add ablations like time-windowed constraints.

- Weakness — reproducibility: Specify CVX solver/settings, stopping criteria, warm starts, wall-clock times, and problem sizes.

- Quality: Technically sound and empirically supported; main gaps are convexity proof and deeper complexity analysis.

- Clarity: Generally clear but request a concise symbol/dimensions table, clearer explanation of avoiding bilinear terms, and minor typo fixes.

- Originality: Novel use of global aggregate constraints in AFHMMs and relaxed CQP in NILM; posterior-regularization perspective differentiates contribution.

- Significance: Practically meaningful improvements on real homes; broadly applicable to other BSS problems with aggregate priors, though dependent on µi0 quality.

- Pros for acceptance: Simple, general idea addressing identifiability; strong empirical gains with significance tests; tractable inference and runtime comparable to baseline.

- Cons for acceptance: Missing formal convexity proof, limited scalability analysis, sensitivity/SNR exploration, and dependence on CVX may limit scalability.

- Questions for authors: Provide convexity proof; report performance vs SNR and typical mains SNRs; analyze sensitivity to λi and µi0 errors; discuss scalability beyond CVX; report experiments with time-windowed SACs.

- Post-rebuttal note: Authors addressed toy SNR and convexity intuitions; request integration of formal convexity argument and SNR reporting into final version.

- Overall recommendation: Accept (borderline/weak accept) — novel, useful idea with solid empirical results; addressing convexity and scalability concerns will strengthen the paper.
