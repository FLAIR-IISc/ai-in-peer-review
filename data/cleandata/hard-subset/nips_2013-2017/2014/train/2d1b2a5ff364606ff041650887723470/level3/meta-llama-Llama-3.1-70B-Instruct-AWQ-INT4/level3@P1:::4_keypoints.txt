- Introduces a novel AFHMM extension that incorporates signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS).  
- Demonstrates significant improvements on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Core idea: inject domain knowledge by constraining each source’s total signal to be close to expected values.  
- Embedding SACs into AFHMM yields a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Balances theoretical development with a practical, real-world application and clear summary of contributions.  
- Highlights importance of distinguishing models from inference procedures (e.g., AFAMAP vs AFHMM/alternating Viterbi).  
- Recommends exploring combining SACAFHMM constraints with AFAMAP as a promising experiment for improved performance.  
- Suggests clarifying the relationship between models and inference methods in the manuscript.  
- Quality: technically sound with convincing experimental validation.  
- Clarity: clear exposition and organization.  
- Originality: novel extension of AFHMM using SACs.  
- Significance: meaningful empirical gains on a large real-world dataset.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing experiments combining algorithmic strategies and need for clarification on model vs inference distinctions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
