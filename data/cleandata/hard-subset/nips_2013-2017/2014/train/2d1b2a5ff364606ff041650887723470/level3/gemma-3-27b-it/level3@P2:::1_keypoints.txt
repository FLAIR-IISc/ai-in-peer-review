- Proposes extending Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve blind source separation, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.
- Core idea: use domain knowledge of expected total signal values per source to encourage aggregate signals to match specified values.
- Formulates the SAC-regularized inference as a convex quadratic program enabling efficient approximate inference.
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements over standard AFHMM and AFAMAP on simulated and real-world energy datasets, notably the HES dataset.
- Empirical results include both a toy dataset and real energy data, with statistical significance tests supporting improvement claims.
- Computational efficiency is comparable to existing methods, indicating tractability.
- Well-motivated problem with clear articulation of blind source separation challenges and benefits of incorporating domain knowledge.
- Novel contribution: applying SACs to AFHMMs and transforming the problem into a convex optimization formulation.
- Strong experimental validation and thorough evaluation demonstrating clear improvements.
- Writing is clear and accessible, with good organization, notation, figures, and tables.
- Theoretical grounding is strengthened by connecting the approach to posterior regularization.
- Lacks a formal proof or more detailed justification of the claimed convexity (Eq. 6).
- Toy experiment discussion needs strengthening via SNR analysis or justification of noise variance realism.
- Missing theoretical scaling/complexity analysis detailing how computational cost scales with time steps and hidden states.
- More detailed comparison of scalability and memory requirements versus AFAMAP would be valuable.
- Minor notation issue: extra commas in a subscript on page 4 (line 202).
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, technically sound, well-executed work with minor fixable issues and substantial practical impact.
