- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into AFHMMs by penalizing deviations from known time-aggregated source outputs.
- Formulates SACs as a penalized log-posterior and derives a convex relaxation solvable as a convex quadratic program (CQP).
- Positions the work relative to AFHMM and AFAMAP, noting AFAMAP uses a different structural constraint.
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements on energy disaggregation using two large UK HES datasets, outperforming AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Strength: SACs are simple, intuitive, and leverage commonly available priors (e.g., appliance total consumption).
- Strength: Convincing large-scale empirical evaluation on realistic, low-sampling-frequency data with statistically significant gains.
- Strength: Combines a clear algorithmic contribution (CQP for SACs) with an impactful application, relevant to the community.
- Strength: Technical formulation and relaxation are sound and connected to posterior regularization.
- Weakness: Unclear whether SACs encourage more integral (binary) relaxed solutions; analysis of integrality gap or S_itk distributions is missing.
- Weakness: Occasional conflation of models and inference procedures; AFAMAP should be framed as an approximate MAP inference algorithm and baseline AFHMM tied to a specific inference method.
- Suggestion: Combining aggregate (SAC) and transition (AFAMAP) constraints is promising and could further improve performance; experiments or discussion recommended.
- Overall: Well-written, effective contribution with minor clarifications and additional analysis suggested; strong candidate for acceptance.
