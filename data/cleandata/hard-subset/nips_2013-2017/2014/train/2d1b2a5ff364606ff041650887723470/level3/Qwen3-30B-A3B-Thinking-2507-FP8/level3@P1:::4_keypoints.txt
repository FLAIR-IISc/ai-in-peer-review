- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation, applied to AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs enforce that each source’s total signal matches domain-specified aggregate values (e.g., national average consumption).  
- SACs target global signal aggregates, distinct from prior posterior-regularization or AFAMAP single-chain state-change constraints.  
- SACs are formulated as convex quadratic programs (CQPs) to enable efficient approximate MAP inference under non-Markovian constraints.  
- On UK HES data (100 homes), SAC-AFHMM achieves 33% lower NDE than AFAMAP and 45% lower than baseline AFHMM, with p<0.05.  
- Method demonstrates robustness to moderate errors in aggregate specifications.  
- Empirical validation on large-scale, real-world data is compelling and practically relevant for smart energy monitoring.  
- The CQP derivation is rigorous and leverages convex optimization while retaining the probabilistic model’s structure.  
- The paper clearly presents SACs as a novel constraint class and connects them to posterior regularization for theoretical grounding.  
- Weakness: The paper conflates model vs. inference—AFHMM is a model and AFAMAP is an inference algorithm—terminology needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Uses a relaxed CQP with S_it ∈ [0,1] and does not analyze whether non-integral solutions harm practical utility.  
- Weakness: Misses a simple experiment combining SACs with AFAMAP’s single-chain constraint, which could yield additional gains.  
- Overall significance and originality are high; experiments and exposition are strong.  
- Recommendation: Accept pending minor fixes (terminology precision and brief integrality analysis).
