- Proposes extending the Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) by incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) for blind source separation.  
- Introduces a convex quadratic program for approximate inference in AFHMM with SAC.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset with strong empirical gains.  
- Paper is well-written with solid mathematical foundations and correct derivations.  
- Main idea: inject domain knowledge via constraints on aggregate source values to improve separation.  
- SACs dramatically improve AFHMM performance and outperform a recent state-of-the-art approach.  
- Provides a clear and thorough related-work overview situating the contribution.  
- Experimental results and figures are clear, informative, and well-presented.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline directions for future work.  
- Potential weakness: baseline comparisons may be unfair because baselines use less data than SAC-AFHMM.  
- Originality: novel incorporation of SACs into AFHMM, representing a significant contribution.  
- Significance: approach could meaningfully impact energy disaggregation and blind source separation applications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.  
- Suggest evaluating other methods that could incorporate SACs, even if less efficient.  
- Suggest extending the algorithm to handle more complex constraint types (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration).  
- Suggest generalizing to broader classes of constraints and expanding the discussion of limitations and future work.
