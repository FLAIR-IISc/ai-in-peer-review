- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation (BSS).
- Applies SACs to energy disaggregation, separating household aggregate electricity into appliance signals.
- Constrains an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) by prior knowledge of an appliance’s total energy over a period.
- Because the constraint is non‑Markovian, exact inference is intractable; authors cast MAP as a penalized optimization.
- Solve the relaxation via a convex quadratic program (CQP), making inference practical.
- Related to constrained HMM work (e.g., AFAMAP) but introduces a novel global aggregate‑value constraint.
- Experimental results show SACs significantly improve disaggregation over AFHMM and AFAMAP on real low‑frequency data.
- Paper is technically sound; CQP formulation and empirical evaluation are solid and convincing.
- The probabilistic interpretation is unclear: authors should explicitly state SAC as an a priori constraint on latent state sequences shaping P(S|Y).
- Major clarity issues: dense prose and poor narrative structure hinder readability.
- Excessive and cumbersome use of acronyms (AFHMM, AFAMAP, AFHMM+SAC, NDE, SAE, NTC, TTC) disrupts flow and comprehension.
- Original and generalizable idea: using aggregate signal values as constraints is novel and applicable beyond energy disaggregation.
- Significant practical impact: strong empirical gains using easily obtainable priors (e.g., national averages) make the method useful.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel intuitive method, tractable CQP inference, state‑of‑the‑art results, high potential impact.
- Arguments for rejection: very poor writing and overwhelming acronym usage that obscure contributions.
