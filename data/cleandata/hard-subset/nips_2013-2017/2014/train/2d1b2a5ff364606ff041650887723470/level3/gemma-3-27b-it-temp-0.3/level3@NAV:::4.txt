## Review of “Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation”

This paper introduces a compelling and straightforward extension to Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs) by incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs). The core idea – leveraging domain knowledge about expected total energy consumption of appliances – is well-motivated and addresses a key challenge in blind source separation, particularly in energy disaggregation. The authors formulate the problem as a convex quadratic program for approximate MAP inference, enabling efficient computation. The application to large-scale, real-world datasets (100 UK homes with low-frequency data, and 10 homes with higher-frequency data) is a significant strength, demonstrating the practical relevance of the approach. 

The work builds upon existing research in AFHMMs and related techniques like AFAMAP, clearly positioning itself within the field. The authors appropriately reference relevant work in energy disaggregation and posterior regularization. The empirical results are convincing, showing substantial improvements over the baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method. The computational efficiency appears comparable to existing approaches, which is a positive aspect.

However, the paper could benefit from clarification regarding the nature of the solutions obtained with SACAFHMM. Specifically, the reviewer notes a request for detail on whether the formulation yields more integral (integer) solutions, akin to AFAMAP’s “one chain state at a time” constraint, or achieves lower error despite potentially non-integral solutions. This is a crucial point for understanding the trade-offs involved. Furthermore, the paper occasionally conflates models and inference procedures (e.g., referring to “AFAMAP” when discussing an approximate MAP algorithm *for* AFHMM). Precision in terminology is needed.

A particularly promising direction for future work, highlighted by the reviewer, is combining the SACAFHMM constraints *within* the AFAMAP framework. This could potentially leverage the strengths of both approaches. The suggestion to explore combining different algorithmic strategies to further improve performance is also well-taken.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem with a practical solution.
*   Strong empirical results on real-world datasets.
*   Clear and concise presentation of the method.
*   Straightforward extension with a simple optimization formulation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Requires clarification on the integrality of solutions.
*   Occasional imprecision in terminology.
*   Potential for further improvement through combination with AFAMAP.



**Overall:** This is a reasonable and successful paper. Addressing the requested clarifications and exploring the suggested experiments would significantly strengthen the contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
