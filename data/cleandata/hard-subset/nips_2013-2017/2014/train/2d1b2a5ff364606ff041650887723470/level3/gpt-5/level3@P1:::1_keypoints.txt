- Paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for BSS and integrates them into AFHMM via quadratic penalties on latent-source totals.  
- Reformulates non-Markovian MAP inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) over relaxed state and transition variables, with connection to posterior regularization.  
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP on toy and UK HES datasets with significant NDE and SAE gains and comparable runtime to AFAMAP.  
- Builds on factorial HMMs and constrained inference frameworks (posterior regularization, GE), and complements prior AFHMM local-dynamics constraints by regularizing global aggregates.  
- Relaxation to CQP is well motivated and the posterior-regularization link strengthens interpretation.  
- Missing a clear, formal convexity argument for Eq. 6; need a short proof sketch (Hessian structure, PSD arguments, optimization direction).  
- The rounding/decoding step from relaxed solutions to discrete states is not described; clarify whether soft states or vertex projection/flow-preserving rounding is used and its metric impact.  
- Complexity discussion is incomplete: provide theoretical scaling in T, I, {K_i} (e.g., |S| = O(T ΣK_i), |H| = O(T ΣK_i^2)), per-iteration complexity and memory vs AFAMAP.  
- Exposition is generally clear; posterior regularization section is concise and accurate.  
- Minor notational/typo issues (extra commas in subscripts); suggest a symbol mapping table for S, H, µ*, V_t, Λ_i.  
- Explicitly state V_t and Λ_i are PSD and move convexity proof to appendix.  
- Novelty: global aggregate constraints in AFHMMs are a novel and general idea with potential beyond energy disaggregation.  
- Significance: meaningful gains on realistic low-frequency data; practical adoption may depend on scalability and solver choices (first-order/ADMM).  
- Empirical concerns: toy experiment uses very low noise (σ^2=0.01) — add SNR sweep and justify representativeness.  
- Recommend reporting SNRs in dB, fraction of unmodeled loads, robustness to missing appliances, and sensitivity/cross-validation for λ_i and µ_0.  
- Statistical reporting: include effect sizes and confidence intervals in addition to t-tests.  
- Suggested extensions: test multiple-interval SACs (e.g., daytime/nighttime) and consider hierarchical priors on µ_0 for per-household deviations.  
- Pros: principled convex formulation, statistically significant improvements, general applicability, solid theoretical framing.  
- Cons: missing convexity proof and decoding details, limited scaling analysis and reliance on generic solvers, toy experiment SNR may be unrealistic, minor notation issues.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending clarifications (convexity proof, decoding, scalability); reviewer confidence medium.
