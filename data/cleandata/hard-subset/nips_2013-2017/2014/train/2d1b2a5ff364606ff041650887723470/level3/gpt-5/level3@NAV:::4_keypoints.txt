- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and instantiates them in an AFHMM, yielding a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate MAP inference.
- Leverages prior knowledge of each source’s total energy over a window to encourage per-chain aggregates to match targets.
- Connects method to posterior regularization and complements existing disaggregation constraints (e.g., AFAMAP’s one-change heuristic, k-segment, semi-Markov priors).
- Empirically evaluated on large-scale energy disaggregation (100 UK homes, plus 10 higher-frequency homes) with consistent gains over AFHMM and improvements over AFAMAP.
- Optimization is technically sound; the CQP is well-motivated and posterior-regularization view is appropriate.
- Results show sizable error reductions at realistic scale, indicating strong empirical impact and practical adoptability.

- Avoid conflating models and inference procedures (e.g., AFAMAP vs. AFHMM); clearly label baselines and algorithms used.

- Discuss runtime and scalability beyond CVX and demonstrate solver independence or alternatives for large-scale use.
