- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: extends additive factorial HMM by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation.  
- Introduces a convex quadratic program for approximate inference in the AFHMM+SAC model.  
- Applies the method to energy disaggregation and demonstrates practical applicability.  
- SACs force each source’s total signal to be close to a known value, a reasonable assumption in many domains.  
- Empirical results on a large real-world dataset show significant improvement over AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and presents ideas clearly.  
- Contribution is original and significant for blind source separation and energy disaggregation.  
- Relation to prior work: method is orthogonal to AFAMAP; authors suggest combining SACs with AFAMAP as future work.  
- Technical soundness: claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Weakness: occasional conflation of models vs inference procedures (AFAMAP vs AFHMM) needs clarification.  
- Weakness: more detail needed on the optimization formulation and the convex relaxation used for MAP inference.  
- Weakness: some notation could be improved for clarity.
