- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: integrates signal aggregate constraints into additive factorial HMM for blind source separation.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation datasets.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and solid mathematical foundation.
- Shows significant improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).
- Compares favorably to AFAMAP, claiming superiority of AFHMM+SAC.
- Comparison may be unfair: uses less data than competing SACAFHMM method.
- Fails to consider alternative SAC-based approaches that are less computationally efficient.
- Figure 1 is unclear and may suggest poor constraints can drastically worsen performance.
- Algorithm could be improved by adding more complex constraints (time constraints, limits on simultaneous activities).
- Strengths: originality, significance, and both theoretical and pragmatic contributions to blind source separation.
- Weaknesses: potentially unfair comparisons and lack of consideration of alternative methods.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound and reproducible.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, since strengths outweigh weaknesses.
