- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: additive factorial HMM with soft aggregate constraints for blind source separation/energy disaggregation from a single meter.  
- Injects domain knowledge about per-source total usage as soft constraints (e.g., daily Wh per appliance).  
- MAP inference is relaxed to a convex quadratic program by introducing transition indicator variables, enabling efficient optimization with CVX.  
- Method related to factorial HMMs, AFAMAP (Kolter & Jaakkola ’12), posterior regularization, and generalized expectation frameworks.  
- Experiments on HES data show substantial improvements in normalized disaggregation error and aggregate error over AFHMM and AFAMAP, plus oracle upper-bound results.  
- Modeling idea is well-motivated by identifiability and the relaxation appears correct (concave objective via negative semidefinite quadratic terms).  
- Paper fixes λi=1 without tuning and provides limited sensitivity analysis beyond a small toy example.  
- Scalability claims relying on CVX need more careful reporting (problem sizes, solver settings); discuss potential for specialized solvers.  
- Manuscript is hard to read: excessive/acronym overload, heavy notation; suggest moving notation to appendix and adding an intuitive running example.  
- Ambiguity about whether SACs are imposed as priors (a priori) or as posterior constraints; text alternates interpretations and should choose one.  
- Using aggregate cross-time constraints as soft priors within factorial HMMs is a novel and practically meaningful contribution.  
- Results are significant for NILM and likely to influence constrained inference approaches for BSS.  
- Pros: clear general idea, principled convex relaxation, empirical gains on realistic low-frequency data.  
- Cons: readability and acronym issues, ambiguity about constraint interpretation, limited sensitivity/scalability analysis, and missing comparisons to more recent/non-HMM NILM baselines.
