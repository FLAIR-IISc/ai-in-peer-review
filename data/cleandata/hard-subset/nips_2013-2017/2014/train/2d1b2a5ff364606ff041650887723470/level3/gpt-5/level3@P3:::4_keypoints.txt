- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and integrates them into AFHMM to form AFHMM+SAC, imposing a convex quadratic penalty on chain aggregates.
- Proposes a convex relaxation using auxiliary transition variables (H) to yield a convex quadratic program (CQP) and connects the approach to posterior regularization.
- Positions work relative to AFHMM/AFAMAP, semi-Markov/non-homogeneous FHMMs, and general constraint/weak-supervision frameworks.
- Clarifies AFHMM+SAC is orthogonal to AFAMAP: AFAMAP enforces temporal structure, SACs add global aggregate priors.
- Evaluates on HES: 100 homes with synthesized mains and 9 homes with measured mains and unmodeled loads.
- Shows AFHMM+SAC reduces normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) versus AFHMM and AFAMAP, often significantly, with comparable runtime.
- Strength: clear, focused contribution tying aggregate priors to AFHMMs via a scalable convex relaxation and posterior-regularization view.
- Strength: high practical relevance—aggregate priors are natural in NILM and the optimization is straightforward to implement.
- Strength: strong, realistic empirical scale (100-house, ultra-low frequency) and statistically significant improvements over AFAMAP.
- Strength: clear methodological exposition of the CQP, PSD structure, and complexity analysis.
- Strength: includes useful analysis of robustness to mis-specified aggregates on toy data.
- Weakness: integrality and rounding not sufficiently analyzed—need quantification of near-integral solutions and sensitivity to rounding.
- Weakness: attribution of gains unclear—should compare AFHMM + alternating Viterbi, AFHMM (CQP, λ=0), and AFHMM+SAC (CQP, λ>0) to isolate effects of SACs vs. relaxation.
- Weakness: hyperparameter treatment limited—λi fixed to 1; requires sensitivity analysis, cross-validation, or per-appliance scaling/learning.
- Weakness: aggregate prior quality concerns—NTC prior estimated across households; need robustness analysis to domain shift and stratified results by NTC–TTC mismatch.
- Weakness: occasional terminological imprecision between model (AFHMM) and inference algorithm (AFAMAP); baseline/inference labels should be precise.
- Weakness: relies on supervised per-appliance training; limited discussion of how SACs integrate with unsupervised NILM.
- Quality: technically sound, correct and scalable optimization; empirical evidence strong but additional baselines would clarify contributions.
- Clarity: generally well written with clear derivations; requests to document rounding procedure, λ settings, and provide pseudocode for reproducibility.
- Originality: moderate—SACs are a natural, domain-informed instantiation of posterior regularization; convex relaxation is neat but conceptually simple.
- Significance: high for NILM and related BSS applications—easy-to-adopt method with consistent gains on challenging data.
- Suggested experiments: report integrality metrics and rounded vs. relaxed objectives, compare AFHMM variants to decouple relaxation vs. SAC benefits, test AFAMAP+SAC, explore multiple-interval SACs, and analyze λ/NTC sensitivity.
- Implementation suggestion: provide a lightweight solver implementation (beyond CVX) for larger T.
- Recommendation: Accept, pending requested clarifications and additional experiments (especially AFAMAP+SAC and integrality analyses).
