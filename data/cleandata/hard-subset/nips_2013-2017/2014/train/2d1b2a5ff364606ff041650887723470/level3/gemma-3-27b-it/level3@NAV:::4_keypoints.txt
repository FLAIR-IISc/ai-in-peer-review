- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in an AFHMM to incorporate expected total appliance energy and improve identifiability.
- Formulates SAC-based inference as a convex quadratic program for approximate MAP estimation.
- Shows substantial performance gains over a state-of-the-art method on simulated and real-world datasets (100 low-frequency UK homes, 10 high-frequency).
- Grounded in and clearly connected to related techniques (AFAMAP, posterior regularization).
- Demonstrates practical relevance by scaling to large real-world energy disaggregation problems beyond toy examples.
- Optimization approach is straightforward but effectively leveraged to tackle the problem.
- Empirical results are compelling and show noticeable improvement over existing methods.
- Unclear whether SACAFHMM produces more integer-like (integral) solutions or achieves low error with non-integral state assignments — needs clarification.
- Potential synergy with AFAMAP: combining SAC constraints within AFAMAP is a promising avenue for further improvement.
- Paper occasionally conflates models (AFHMM) and inference procedures (AFAMAP); calls for more precise terminology and consistent references.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a challenging problem with a novel, intuitive approach and solid theoretical grounding.
- Argument for acceptance: demonstrates significant empirical improvements at scale on real data.
- Argument against acceptance: lack of clarity about solution integrality and mechanism of improvement.
- Argument against acceptance: missed opportunity to explore or evaluate hybridization with AFAMAP.
- Argument against acceptance: occasional terminological imprecision that reduces clarity.
- Overall: promising, well-executed paper that would be strengthened by clarifying integrality behavior and exploring SAC+AFAMAP experiments.
