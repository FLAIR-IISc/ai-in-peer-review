- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge into AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs constrain each hidden source’s total output over a period to match a known aggregate value.  
- Uses an approximate MAP inference procedure via a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation for tractability.  
- Builds on factorial HMM blind source separation work and is orthogonal to AFAMAP (which constrains transitions).  
- Strong empirical results on large, real-world low-frequency energy datasets (100+ homes), outperforming state-of-the-art.  
- SACs are simple, intuitive, novel, and the optimization formulation is straightforward and technically sound.  
- Presentation issue: paper conflates models and inference methods—AFAMAP is an inference algorithm, and baseline AFHMM likely used alternating Viterbi; clarify for reproducibility.  
- Analysis gap: should investigate why SACs work (e.g., whether SACs encourage more integral solutions or perform well even with fractional solutions).  
- Missed experiment: combine SACs with AFAMAP’s inference framework to test complementary strengths and potentially achieve better results.  
- Overall: solid, practically significant contribution with strong empirical backing; clarify methodology and add combination experiments to strengthen the paper.
