- Problem & contribution: Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into an AFHMM for energy disaggregation, turning a non-Markovian posterior into a tractable convex quadratic program for approximate MAP inference.  
- Novelty & motivation: Novel, intuitive use of domain knowledge (expected total signal per source) to regularize an ill-posed blind source separation problem.  
- Technical quality: Elegant convex optimization formulation enabling efficient inference.  
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC significantly outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP on real-world energy data (improvements validated by t-tests).  
- Computational cost: Performance gains achieved with computational cost comparable to AFAMAP, making the method practical.  
- Theoretical gap: Needs an explicit proof that the relaxed optimization in Eq.(6) is convex (show quadratic forms are PSD).  
- Scalability concern: Should include theoretical complexity analysis in T and number of hidden states and discuss tractability on larger datasets versus AFAMAP.  
- Experimental clarification: Clarify realism of very low noise (σ²=0.01) in toy experiment and report/discuss signal-to-noise ratios in real-world experiments.  
- Minor issue: Notational typo with extra commas in subscripts on page 4 (line 202).  
- Overall recommendation: Original, significant, and empirically strong paper with addressable weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
