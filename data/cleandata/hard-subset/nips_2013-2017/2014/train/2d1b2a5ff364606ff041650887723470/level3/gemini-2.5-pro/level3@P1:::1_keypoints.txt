- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge and address identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) and formulates MAP inference as a penalized objective.  
- Relaxes integer constraints and solves the resulting convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference.  
- Draws a connection between the penalized objective and the posterior regularization framework.  
- Motivated by and evaluated on energy disaggregation, where appliance daily energy priors are available.  
- Evaluated on synthetic toy data and real-world HES dataset, including synthetically aggregated and actual mains settings.  
- AFHMM+SAC significantly outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP, reducing normalized disaggregation error by 33–45% and signal aggregate error by 16–29%, with paired t-test significance.  
- Strength: Novel, intuitive, and generalizable idea that addresses a fundamental BSS challenge.  
- Strength: Sound technical contribution turning a non-Markovian constraint into a tractable CQP with similar runtimes to baselines.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and well-structured exposition.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental validation and robustness analysis to errors in constraint values.  
- Weakness: Toy experiment uses very low noise (σ² = 0.01); need rationale and noise-sensitivity results.  
- Weakness: Real-data experiments should discuss effective SNR given unmodeled appliances.  
- Weakness: Convexity claim needs a more formal justification (proof that Λi and Vt are PSD).  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical complexity analysis in terms of T, I, and Ki to assess scalability.  
- Overall: Strong, well-motivated contribution with convincing empirical gains; minor clarifications would further strengthen the paper—strong accept recommendation.
