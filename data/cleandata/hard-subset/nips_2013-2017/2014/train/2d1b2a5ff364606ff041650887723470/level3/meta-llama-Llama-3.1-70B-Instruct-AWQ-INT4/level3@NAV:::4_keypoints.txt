- Proposes extending AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Implements SACs via a convex quadratic program for efficient approximate inference.  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Evaluated on real-world datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (high-frequency).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Method is technically sound with thorough derivations and theoretical/experimental support.  
- Novelty: introducing SACs to AFHMM is an original and meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: addresses an important energy disaggregation problem with potential practical impact.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear, detailed comparison with other related work and how it differs/improves.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and potential extensions of the approach.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication based on contribution and empirical results.  
- Suggestions: include a more detailed related-work comparison, discuss limitations/extensions, and add more experiments to show robustness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid real-world evaluation, and potential impact on energy management.
