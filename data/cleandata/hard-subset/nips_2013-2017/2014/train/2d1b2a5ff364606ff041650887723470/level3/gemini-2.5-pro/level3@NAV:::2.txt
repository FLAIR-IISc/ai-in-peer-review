This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) as a method for incorporating domain knowledge into additive factorial hidden Markov models (AFHMMs) to address the well-known identifiability problem in blind source separation. The application focus is on energy disaggregation. The core idea is to constrain the total signal from each unobserved source to be close to a known value (e.g., the expected daily consumption of an appliance). The authors formulate MAP inference with these constraints as a convex quadratic program, enabling efficient approximate inference. This work builds upon prior research using AFHMMs for energy disaggregation (e.g., Kolter & Jaakkola, AISTATS 2012) by proposing a novel and intuitive type of constraint to improve source identification.

**Strengths and Weaknesses:**

**Quality & Significance:** The paper makes a valuable contribution to the important problem of energy disaggregation. The proposed SAC is an intuitive and practical way to leverage prior knowledge, directly targeting the key weakness of identifiability in factorial models. The technical approach, using a CQP relaxation for the non-Markovian optimization problem, is sound. The experimental validation is a clear strength; the authors demonstrate significant performance gains over a standard AFHMM and a state-of-the-art method (AFAMAP) on a real-world dataset. The inclusion of an "oracle" experiment (using TTC) effectively illustrates the potential of this approach. The results are significant and the method is likely to be adopted by other researchers in this field.

**Originality:** While building on existing models like AFHMMs, the specific formulation of Signal Aggregate Constraints and the method for incorporating them are novel. The paper clearly delineates its contribution from related work on model constraints.

**Clarity:** The primary weakness of the paper is its clarity. The manuscript is dense and very difficult to read, which significantly hinders the appreciation of its strong technical contributions. There is an excessive use of acronyms (AFHMM, AFAMAP, AFHMM+SAC, NDE, SAE), some of which become unusually long and make the text cumbersome to follow for those not deeply embedded in this specific niche. The organization could be improved to build intuition before diving into the dense mathematical formulations.

Furthermore, the conceptual framing of the constraint could be clearer. The authors correctly note that the SAC can be viewed as a prior over the states, but this is a critical point that should be made more explicitly. Stating upfront that this is an *a priori* constraint on the aggregate statistics of the state sequences, which thereby constrains the posterior, would greatly improve the reader's understanding of the mechanism.

**Conclusion:**
This paper presents a novel, well-motivated, and empirically successful method for improving energy disaggregation models. The core idea is strong and the results are significant. However, the paper is severely hampered by poor readability. I would be willing to raise my score if the authors undertake a major revision to improve the clarity and presentation of their work.
