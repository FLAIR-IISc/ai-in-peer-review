- Paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation with AFHMMs by adding a convex quadratic regularizer that yields a solvable CQP for MAP inference.
- Key insight: total signal per source often has known expected values and using these aggregates addresses identifiability in BSS.
- Evaluated on synthetic and real Household Electricity Survey data, showing significant improvements over AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP (≈30% reduction in NDE).
- SACs are a novel class of constraints leveraging aggregate signal properties rather than temporal dynamics or state transitions.
- The approach has a solid theoretical link to posterior regularization, providing grounding for the method.
- Strength: tackles an important real-world problem (energy disaggregation) with clear empirical gains and computationally efficient inference.
- Strength: thorough evaluation including robustness to constraint misspecification and appropriate metrics (NDE, SAE).
- Weakness: excessive and long acronyms harm readability; notation choices impede comprehension.
- Weakness: dense, hard-to-follow mathematical presentation with many new variables lacking clear motivation; CQP transformation needs more intuitive explanation.
- Weakness: confusion in the text about whether SACs are priors or posterior regularizers — should be clarified as a priori constraints that constrain the posterior.
- Weakness: overstated claim that SACs “dramatically improve” AFHMM without making clear that SACs are complementary to existing constraints (comparison with AFAMAP is the biggest gain).
- Quality assessment: technically sound, well-justified, and experimentally convincing.
- Clarity assessment: manuscript suffers from poor readability and needs substantial revision.
- Originality assessment: SACs are a novel and meaningful contribution to BSS/energy disaggregation literature.
- Significance assessment: practical impact is high given measurable improvements over state-of-the-art.
- Recommendation summary: strong technical and empirical contribution but acceptance contingent on major clarity and presentation revisions and clearer theoretical positioning of SACs.
