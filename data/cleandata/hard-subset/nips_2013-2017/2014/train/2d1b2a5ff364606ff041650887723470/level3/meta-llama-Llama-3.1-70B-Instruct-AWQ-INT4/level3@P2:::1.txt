This paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors introduce SACs, which encourage the aggregate values of the source signals to be close to specified values, often available from domain knowledge. They incorporate SACs into an AFHMM and transform the posterior log objective into a convex optimization problem, which is efficiently solvable.

The authors relate their work to previous research in blind source separation and energy disaggregation, highlighting the challenges of identifiability and the need for incorporating domain knowledge. They also discuss the connection to posterior regularization, showing that their objective function can be derived from this framework.

The paper is well-organized, and the technical presentation is clear. The authors provide a thorough description of their model, the optimization problem, and the solution method. The use of convex quadratic programming is a significant contribution, as it enables efficient inference in the AFHMM+SAC model.

The experimental results are strong, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on both a toy dataset and real domestic energy data. The authors show that their method outperforms the AFHMM and AFAMAP, with significant improvements in terms of normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE). The optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP, which is an important consideration for practical applications.

However, there are a few areas that require clarification or improvement. The toy experiment uses a very low noise variance, which may not be realistic. The authors should discuss the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for the mains versus aggregate appliance signals to provide a better understanding of the experimental setup. Additionally, a formal proof that the procedure in equation (6) is convex would strengthen the theoretical presentation.

The authors should also provide a theoretical scaling and complexity analysis, discussing how the method performs with increasing data length and number of hidden states. A tractability comparison to AFAMAP for larger datasets would be valuable, as it would help readers understand the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.

Minor notation issues, such as extra commas in subscripts, should be corrected to improve the overall clarity of the paper.

Overall, this is a novel and original work that addresses an important problem in blind source separation and energy disaggregation. While the reviewer is not an expert in the HMM community or energy applications, the paper appears to make a significant contribution to the field. The authors have satisfactorily addressed reviewer queries in their feedback, and incorporating those clarifications into the paper would further improve its quality.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective method for blind source separation and energy disaggregation.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical presentation and strong experimental results.
* The use of convex quadratic programming is a significant contribution to the field.
* The paper is well-organized, and the technical presentation is clear.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The toy experiment uses a very low noise variance, which may not be realistic.
* A formal proof of convexity for the procedure in equation (6) is missing.
* Theoretical scaling and complexity analysis are not provided.
* Minor notation issues need to be corrected.

Recommendation: Accept, pending minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above.
