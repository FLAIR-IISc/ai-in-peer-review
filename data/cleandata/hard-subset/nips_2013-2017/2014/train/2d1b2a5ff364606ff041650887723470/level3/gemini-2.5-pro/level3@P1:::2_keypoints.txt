- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to use known/expected total energy per source to address identifiability in blind source separation (energy disaggregation).  
- Incorporates SACs into AFHMM by adding an aggregate-sum penalty and relaxes inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for tractability.  
- Provides theoretical grounding by relating the approach to the posterior regularization framework.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and the HES low-frequency dataset, showing large improvements (≈45% error reduction vs AFHMM, ≈29% vs AFAMAP).  
- Includes an informative oracle experiment using true total consumption to bound potential gains.  
- Strength: SACs are a novel, intuitive, and practically significant form of prior knowledge for many BSS problems.  
- Strength: Technical formulation and CQP relaxation are sound and make the method computationally feasible.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation is strong, uses relevant baselines, and demonstrates robustness on challenging data.  
- Weakness: Paper readability is poor—dense writing and overuse of nonstandard acronyms hinder comprehension.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity in model framing—SACs are presented as a posterior constraint/penalty rather than clearly as an a priori non-Markovian prior over state sequences.  
- Detailed comment: The proposed generalization to different time periods (e.g., day/night) should include a brief example showing the altered CQP formulation.  
- Detailed comment: Choice of λi=1 is arbitrary; the paper needs a sensitivity analysis or practical guidance for setting this hyperparameter.  
- Recommendation: Conditional acceptance—strong contribution but requires substantial revision to improve clarity (reduce acronyms, explicitly frame SACs as a prior).
