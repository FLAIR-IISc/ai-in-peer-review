- Introduces AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge and address BSS unidentifiability, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate inference that is computationally efficient and scalable.  
- Empirical results on real-world data show strong gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error over baseline AFHMM and 29% over a state-of-the-art method.  
- Novelty: SACs provide a generalizable framework for embedding aggregate signal priors into BSS problems, advancing identifiability.  
- Technical rigor: thorough model derivation, convex formulation, and connection to posterior regularization.  
- Empirical validation: evaluated on synthetic and real datasets; national total consumption used effectively as a practical SAC proxy.  
- Computational efficiency: added SACs do not materially increase computational cost compared to baselines.  
- Clarity/readability issues: excessive, frequent acronyms and dense notation impede accessibility, especially for non-experts.  
- Prior density constraint is not clearly interpreted as an a priori/posterior regularization, risking confusion for readers unfamiliar with Bayesian regularization.  
- Limited discussion of limitations: lacks analysis of sensitivity to inaccurate priors and scalability to larger appliance sets/datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem with novel, broadly applicable SACs and strong theoretical/empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity and insufficient discussion of SAC limitations and sensitivity to priors.  
- Suggested improvements: reduce acronym use and simplify presentation; explicitly link prior density constraint to posterior regularization; discuss limitations and future work (e.g., inaccurate priors, larger-scale settings).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing clarity and interpretability concerns in the final version.
