- Proposal: Introduces Additive Factorial HMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation for MAP inference in blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Key idea: Injects domain knowledge of per-source totals (e.g., daily Wh) via quadratic penalties and solves a relaxed CQP using transition variables.  
- Related work: Frames the approach as an instance of posterior regularization and builds on AFHMM/AFAMAP and prior disaggregation constraints (duration, one-change, k-segment).  
- Technical quality: Solid mathematical development—SAC penalty well-motivated, CQP carefully constructed with PSD structure and clear link to posterior regularization.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic and HES experiments show improvements in NDE and SAE on realistic low-frequency data.  
- Comparison fairness: Baselines (AFHMM/AFAMAP) lack the extra side information used by AFHMM+SAC; TTC used is an oracle—comparisons may be unfair.  
- Robustness concerns: Sensitivity to misspecified constraints only partially explored; Figure 1 indicates SAC can degrade performance under large mismatch.  
- Unanalyzed aspects: Relaxation gap and the choice/tuning of λ (fixed to 1) are not analyzed or justified.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is well written overall, but Figure 1 is unclear and claims that SAC “always helps” should be tempered; hyperparameter selection, computational scaling beyond CVX, and reproduction details are missing.  
- Originality: Novel and useful instantiation of posterior regularization for AFHMMs in BSS, though it leverages known ideas.  
- Significance: Broad applicability when per-source totals are known/estimable; promising gains, but impact would grow with richer constraint classes and fairer baselines.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled SAC contribution with convex relaxation, demonstrated empirical gains, and strong connection to posterior regularization.  
- Cons for acceptance: Potentially unfair baselines, insufficient characterization of robustness/failure modes, and limited constraint class (only aggregates).  
- Reviewer suggestions: Evaluate baselines augmented with SAC (e.g., AFAMAP+SAC) to control information budget, perform sensitivity studies for λ and µ0 (and mis-specification), analyze relaxation gap, and extend/discuss richer constraint types (mutual exclusivity, duration, cardinality).
