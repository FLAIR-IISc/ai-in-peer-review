- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: Additive Factorial HMM augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints for blind source separation in energy disaggregation.  
- Introduces SACs to incorporate domain knowledge by encouraging each source’s total signal to match a specified value.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with thorough related-work coverage and clear explanations of models and optimization.  
- Use of SACs is a novel approach to improving identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Strong simulation results: outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP on toy and real domestic datasets.  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Provides a theoretical presentation of a convex quadratic program for approximate inference, a notable contribution.  
- Toy experiment used very low noise variance; authors should clarify realism vs. simplification for easier separation.  
- Lacks discussion of Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) differences between mains and aggregate appliance signals.  
- Needs a formal proof that the procedure in equation (6) is convex.  
- Should include theoretical scaling/complexity analysis (data length, number of hidden states).  
- Requires a tractability/comparison analysis to AFAMAP for larger datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the issues above.  
- Key revision requests: clarify toy noise assumption, prove convexity of eq.(6), discuss SNR, and add scaling/tractability analysis vs AFAMAP.
