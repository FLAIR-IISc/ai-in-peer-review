- Proposes AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to incorporate domain knowledge for blind source separation/energy disaggregation.
- Uses SAC to push each unknown source’s total signal toward specified values.
- Integrates SAC into AFHMM and performs approximate inference via a convex quadratic program for efficiency.
- Paper is well-written with a solid mathematical foundation and clear motivation.
- Demonstrates significant improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) on synthetic and real data.
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair because AFHMM and AFAMAP used less data than SACAFHMM, potentially biasing results.
- Figure 1 is unclear; it suggests AFAMAP may outperform SACAFHMM at large distances, indicating bad constraints can hurt performance.
- The claim that SAC always helps is contradicted by the Figure 1 observation and needs clarification.
- Recommends evaluating other methods that can incorporate SAC (even if computationally heavier) for a more comprehensive comparison.
- Suggests future extensions to more complex constraint types (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration limits) for greater modeling flexibility.
- Paper demonstrates thorough related-work discussion and domain understanding.
- Minor issues with writing style: some long/convoluted sentences and typographical/grammatical errors.
- Overall assessment: novel and promising approach with important empirical gains but comparisons and figure clarity need improvement.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
