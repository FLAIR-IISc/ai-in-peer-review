- Paper introduces AFHMM+SAC: adds signal aggregate constraints to AFHMM, reformulates inference as a convex quadratic program, and applies it to energy disaggregation with strong empirical gains.  
- Novelty: SACs are a new, practical way to inject domain knowledge into blind source separation.  
- Technical approach: relaxed MAP inference via CQP is mathematically sound and computationally efficient.  
- Empirical results: significant improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Theoretical grounding: links method to posterior regularization and provides robust justification.  
- Writing and motivation: clear exposition and strong problem motivation.  
- Comparison fairness concern: baselines (AFHMM, AFAMAP) may be disadvantaged because AFHMM+SAC uses extra domain knowledge.  
- Baseline/alternative methods: paper should explore other SAC-based approaches for a fairer comparison.  
- Constraint robustness: results suggest poorly specified constraints can degrade performance, contradicting claims of consistent improvement.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 is unclear about the relationship between constraint accuracy and performance.  
- Generalizability: paper lacks discussion on handling diverse constraint types and more complex/practical constraints (e.g., appliance usage rules).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparison fairness, clarify results/figures, and explore more complex constraint types.
