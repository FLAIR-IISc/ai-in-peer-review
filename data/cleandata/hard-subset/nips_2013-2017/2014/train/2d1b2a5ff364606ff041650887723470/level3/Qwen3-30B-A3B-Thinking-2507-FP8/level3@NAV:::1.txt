

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation within an additive factorial HMM framework (AFHMM), specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating domain knowledge about expected total energy consumption per appliance into the model, transforming the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program. This approach significantly improves performance over baseline AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP on both synthetic and real-world energy data, with statistically significant gains (33% NDE improvement over AFAMAP).

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality through thorough experimental validation on both toy data and the Household Electricity Survey dataset. The convex optimization formulation is well-motivated and computationally efficient, with inference times comparable to AFAMAP. The theoretical foundation is sound, though the formal proof of convexity for the procedure in equation (6) is missing. The authors appropriately relate their work to posterior regularization, establishing theoretical connections.

Strengths include the novel application of SACs to energy disaggregation, clear experimental results with proper statistical validation, and the practical relevance of incorporating domain knowledge. The paper addresses a meaningful challenge in energy disaggregation where interpretability of results is crucial for end-users.

Key weaknesses include the toy experiment's unrealistically low noise variance (σ²=0.01), which requires clarification on whether this reflects realistic scenarios or was chosen to simplify separation. The paper also lacks a detailed SNR analysis for mains vs. aggregate appliance signals. The theoretical complexity analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and tractability comparison with AFAMAP for larger datasets are missing, which would strengthen the claims about scalability.

Minor issues include a possible notation error with extra commas in subscripts (page 4, line 202). While the authors addressed these concerns satisfactorily in their response, incorporating these clarifications would significantly improve the paper.

The work is novel within the energy disaggregation literature, though its broader significance to the HMM community requires further discussion. Given the strong empirical results, clear methodology, and practical application, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the theoretical and experimental clarifications. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field of energy disaggregation by providing a principled way to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation.
