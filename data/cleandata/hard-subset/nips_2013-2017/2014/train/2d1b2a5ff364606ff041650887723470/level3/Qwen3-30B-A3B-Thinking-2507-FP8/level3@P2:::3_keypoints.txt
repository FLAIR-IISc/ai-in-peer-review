- Paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs incorporate domain knowledge by constraining per-source total signal over time, differing from state-transition constraints like AFAMAP.  
- The constrained optimization is transformed into a convex quadratic program that can be solved efficiently.  
- Theoretical connection to posterior regularization is established, strengthening the approach's justification.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show large improvements: 45% reduction vs AFHMM and 29% vs AFAMAP.  
- Computational complexity scales similarly to existing methods.  
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair because SAC-AFHMM uses additional national consumption data that baselines do not.  
- Figure 1 shows AFAMAP outperforming SAC-AFHMM when constraints are far from true values, contradicting the claim that SACs "always help."  
- The paper is well-written, well-organized, and presents clear mathematical formulations.  
- SACs represent a novel, practically important class of constraints not previously explored in NIPS work.  
- The method has potential broader applicability beyond energy disaggregation (e.g., audio, biology).  
- Weakness: limited exploration of more complex constraint types (mutual exclusivity, duration limits).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify baseline data use, explain counterintuitive Figure 1 results, and discuss constraint limitations.  
- Overall: strong technical contribution and empirical gains, but claims about universal benefit of SACs need qualification and fairer comparisons.
