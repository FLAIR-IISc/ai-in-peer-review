- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge into blind source separation — novel idea.  
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation with only one observed mixture.  
- Transforms the optimization into a convex quadratic program for computational efficiency.  
- Demonstrates method on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset and reports improvements over a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Provides a solid theoretical foundation, including a convex QP formulation for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares against a strong baseline.  
- Toy experiment uses very low noise variance; need clarification whether this is realistic or chosen to ease separation.  
- Paper should include discussion of SNR for mains versus aggregate appliance signals.  
- Authors should provide a formal proof that the procedure in equation (6) is convex.  
- Lacks theoretical scaling/complexity analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and comparison of tractability to AFAMAP on larger datasets.  
- Minor notation issue: extra commas in subscripts before p on page 4 (line 202).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
