- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation with AFHMMs.  
- SACs enforce known total sum per source to address identifiability in BSS.  
- Formulates approximate MAP inference as a convex quadratic program that is computationally efficient.  
- Relates the SAC objective to posterior regularization and shows derivation in Section 6.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains: ~45% improvement over AFHMM and ~29% over AFAMAP on real UK household data.  
- Shows improvements in both normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE), important for energy applications.  
- Validates results on multiple datasets (100 households low-frequency, 9 households mains) with thorough experiments.  
- SAC formulation is simple, easy to implement, and integrates cleanly with existing AFHMM infrastructure.  
- Technical approach and convex relaxation are sound with clear theoretical grounding.  
- Paper sometimes conflates models and inference procedures; needs clearer distinction between AFHMM, AFAMAP (approximate MAP), and SACAFHMM.  
- Unclear whether SACAFHMM yields more integral solutions or achieves gains despite fractional solutions—needs clarification.  
- SACs and AFAMAP appear orthogonal; authors should discuss or explore combining them in future work.  
- Limited discussion of how SACs interact with other constraint-based approaches and the requirement for prior aggregate knowledge.  
- Overall strong, original, and significant contribution with recommendation for acceptance; suggested clarifications are minor.
