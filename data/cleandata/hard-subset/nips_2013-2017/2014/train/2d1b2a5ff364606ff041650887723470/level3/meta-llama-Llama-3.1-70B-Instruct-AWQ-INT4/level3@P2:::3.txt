This paper proposes an extension to the Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) by incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) to improve the performance of blind source separation problems. The authors introduce a convex quadratic program for approximate inference in the AFHMM with SAC, and demonstrate its effectiveness on a real-world energy disaggregation data set. The paper is well-written, and the mathematical foundation is solid.

The main idea of the paper is to incorporate domain knowledge into the blind source separation problem by adding constraints on the aggregate values of the source signals. This is based on the observation that the total signal often varies widely across the unknown sources, and we often have a good idea of what total values to expect. The authors show that the use of SACs dramatically improves the original AFHMM, and significantly improves over a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The paper is related to previous work on blind source separation, energy disaggregation, and probabilistic modeling. The authors provide a clear overview of the related work and position their contribution within the existing literature.

The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of the AFHMM with SAC, and demonstrate its effectiveness on a real-world data set. The paper also provides a good discussion of the limitations of the approach and suggests directions for future work.
