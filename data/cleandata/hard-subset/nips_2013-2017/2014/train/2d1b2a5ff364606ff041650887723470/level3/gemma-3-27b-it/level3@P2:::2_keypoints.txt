- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) within AFHMMs to incorporate appliance-level energy priors for improved blind source separation/energy disaggregation.  
- Formulates approximate inference as a convex quadratic program and connects the approach to posterior regularization.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical gains on the HES dataset, outperforming baseline AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP, robust across mains/no-mains settings.  
- Novel and practically relevant contribution that addresses identifiability in BSS by specifying what prior knowledge to incorporate.  
- Technical soundness backed by optimization formulation and theoretical justification.  
- Builds appropriately on prior work in BSS and energy disaggregation and contextualizes related constrained AFHMM methods.  
- Clarity and readability suffer from excessive acronyms and dense writing, hindering accessibility.  
- Lacks analysis of sensitivity to inaccuracies in the aggregate prior (µi0) and how mis-specified priors affect performance.  
- Fails to explicitly state that SACs are applied a priori to the prior distribution, requiring clearer framing (a priori vs. a posteriori).  
- Limited discussion of scalability with respect to number of appliances and time-series length despite cost claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, empirically strong, and computationally tractable.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor clarity, limited prior-sensitivity discussion, and missing explicit prior framing.  
- Overall: valuable contribution but needs improved readability and clearer treatment of the prior; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
