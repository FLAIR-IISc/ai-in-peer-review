This paper introduces a novel approach to blind source separation problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem, called signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The authors propose an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) with SACs to formulate the energy disaggregation problem, where only one mixture signal is assumed to be observed. The paper is well-structured, and the ideas are clearly presented.

The problem of energy disaggregation, also known as non-intrusive load monitoring, is a challenging task due to its inherent unidentifiability. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work in this area, including various approaches to improve the basic AFHMM by constraining the states of the HMMs. The introduction of SACs is a significant contribution, as it provides a way to incorporate prior knowledge about the total signal of each source.

The technical quality of the paper is high, and the authors provide a thorough derivation of the convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs. The use of a convex quadratic program is a significant improvement over the original AFHMM, as it allows for efficient computation. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the relation to posterior regularization, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

The experimental results are impressive, showing a significant improvement in performance compared to the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach. The use of real-world data from the Household Electricity Survey (HES) adds credibility to the results. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of the method, including a discussion of the computational complexity and the scalability of the approach.

However, the paper has some drawbacks. The excessive use of acronyms, some of which are unusually long, harms readability. The authors should consider using more descriptive variable names and reducing the number of acronyms used. Additionally, the manuscript is very hard to read, and the authors could improve the clarity of the text by breaking up long paragraphs and using more headings.

Furthermore, the authors appear to place the constraint on the prior rather than directly on the posterior. While this is a valid approach, the authors should explicitly state that it is an a priori constraint, thereby constraining the posterior. This clarification would improve the understanding of the method and its implications.

In conclusion, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The introduction of SACs and the use of a convex quadratic program are valuable contributions. While the paper has some drawbacks, the overall quality is good, and the ideas are clearly presented.
