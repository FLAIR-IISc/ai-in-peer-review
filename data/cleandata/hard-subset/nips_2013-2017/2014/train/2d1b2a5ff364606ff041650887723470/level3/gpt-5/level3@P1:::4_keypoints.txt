- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and integrates them into AFHMM to match each source’s total signal to a known/estimated aggregate.
- Derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation for MAP inference by augmenting AFHMM with transition-indicator variables.
- Shows SAC objective is equivalent to posterior regularization, clarifying its probabilistic interpretation.
- Empirical evaluation on UK HES: simulated mains from 100 households and true mains from 9 households using NDE and SAE metrics.
- Reports sizable, statistically significant gains (~33% NDE and ~16% SAE over AFAMAP with national total consumption) with similar runtime to baselines.
- Strength: clear, intuitive idea—many BSS/NILM tasks have reliable total-energy priors that SACs naturally encode.
- Strength: solid algorithmic framing—a PSD convex relaxation and equivalence to posterior regularization.
- Strength: strong, large-scale empirical evidence on realistic, low-frequency data.
- Strength: practical relevance—constraints reflect deployable domain knowledge and address identifiability.
- Weakness: CQP relaxes binary variables—need clarification on rounding, prevalence/magnitude of fractional S, and whether SACs promote integrality.
- Suggestion: include a small study quantifying the integrality gap and performance after rounding.
- Weakness: orthogonality to AFAMAP—AFAMAP’s one-change constraint addresses different failure modes; propose combined AFAMAP+SAC baseline.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters—report sensitivity to λ, robustness to µ0 mis-specification, and how µ0 would be estimated in deployment.
- Weakness: supervision scope—clarify benefit in unsupervised/weakly supervised settings and effect of K (number of states).
- Weakness: scaling and solver dependence—CVX may not scale; report scaling behavior in T, I, K and consider specialized solvers/warm starts.
- Suggestion: be precise in nomenclature—distinguish AFHMM model vs AFAMAP inference algorithm and state experimental details clearly.
- Suggestion: add baselines/ablations (semi-Markov/duration-constrained, AFHMM+SAC vs AFHMM under same relaxation) to isolate contributions.
- Quality: technically sound derivation and principled connection to posterior regularization; main open point is integrality/rounding impact.
- Clarity: generally well-organized but tighten model vs inference distinction and detail rounding/reconstruction from fractional variables.
- Originality: moderate—aggregate constraints are natural but the CQP relaxation and posterior-regularization view are well executed.
- Significance: high for NILM and other BSS tasks with aggregate priors; empirical gains indicate practical value.
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general, principled inference with convincing large-scale improvements and similar runtime.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited analysis of relaxation integrality, missing combined AFAMAP baseline, limited hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability analysis.
- Minor: provide details on converting fractional solutions to discrete states, multi-interval SACs tried, computing environment, per-instance runtimes, and fix minor notation/PSD justifications.
