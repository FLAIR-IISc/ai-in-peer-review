- Introduces "signal aggregate constraints" (SACs) to incorporate prior knowledge of total source aggregates into blind source separation.  
- Formalizes SACs by adding a penalty on the sum of each source to the AFHMM objective.  
- SACs are non‑Markovian, making exact MAP inference intractable.  
- Proposes an efficient approximate inference scheme using a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation.  
- Connects the formulation to the posterior regularization framework.  
- Empirical evaluation on a toy dataset and the HES energy disaggregation dataset shows large gains (≈45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM, ≈29% vs AFAMAP).  
- Technical quality is high: sound mathematics and clear CQP derivation.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair because AFHMM+SAC is given extra prior information (µ_i0) that baselines lack.  
- Sensitivity to mis‑specified priors is underexplored; poorly specified SACs can degrade performance and the paper lacks thresholds for harm.  
- Paper is very clear, well organized, and reproducible for experts.  
- Original: global non‑Markovian aggregate constraint and the specific CQP relaxation are novel contributions.  
- Significant for energy disaggregation and other BSS tasks; method is practical and computationally efficient.  
- Could be strengthened by discussing generality and extensions (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration constraints).  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing fairness of comparisons, robustness to inaccurate priors, and discussion of extending the framework.
