- Proposal: AFHMM+SAC augments additive factorial HMMs with signal-aggregate constraints (SACs) to match each source’s total energy to targets.  
- Formulation: Relaxed MAP inference is written as a convex quadratic program (CQP) via transition-indicator variables and PSD structure.  
- Conceptual link: The method is connected to posterior regularization, showing penalized posterior arises from constraining expectations of aggregate functions.  
- Experiments: Tests on toy and UK HES data show AFHMM+SAC lowers NDE and SAE versus AFHMM and AFAMAP, with paired t-test significance and comparable runtime.  
- Novelty: SACs provide global, cross-time aggregate priors distinct from prior local temporal constraints and duration models.  
- Strength: Broadly applicable idea addressing identifiability in BSS/NILM using readily available aggregate priors.  
- Strength: Sound optimization — relaxed problem solvable efficiently as a CQP; derivation tied to posterior regularization.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains on realistic, low-frequency data and robustness to imperfect aggregate targets in toy study.  
- Strength: Practical relevance — easily incorporates survey/expert priors and extends to multiple time-window constraints.  
- Main weakness: Missing formal convexity/concavity proof for the relaxed program and equivalence to disciplined convex programming.  
- Main weakness: No clear description of rounding/decoding from fractional S and H to discrete states and its effect on accuracy/objective.  
- Main weakness: Lack of theoretical complexity and memory-scaling analysis in T, total states K, I, and number of constraints J; comparison to AFAMAP scaling.  
- Main weakness: Toy experiment uses very low noise (σ2=0.01); need justification and reporting of SNRs/residual variance for realism.  
- Main weakness: Sensitivity analysis absent — λ fixed at 1; need plots varying λ and perturbing µ0 on real data.  
- Main weakness: Handling of missing/unmodeled loads unclear — how SACs behave when modeled totals cannot match mains and whether SACs should be softened.  
- Minor issues: Clarify equivalence of maximizing L to minimizing convex −L under linear constraints; report solver tolerances/stopping criteria.  
- Minor issues: Notation/typos and inconsistent indexing (extra commas, H_it dimensions, Kronecker constructs) need cleanup.  
- Quality assessment: Technically solid with rigorous link to posterior regularization and efficient relaxation; main gaps are convexity proof and rounding strategy.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally well-written but optimization derivation is dense; requested convexity proof and decoding description would improve readability.  
- Acceptance pros: Simple, general idea with practical value; solid optimization grounding; strong empirical improvements; comparable runtime to AFAMAP.  
- Acceptance cons: Missing formal convexity proof, rounding details, sensitivity analyses, SNR reporting, and more thorough scaling analysis.  
- Questions for authors: Provide convexity/concavity proof and rounding method; report SNRs and justify σ2; show performance vs λ and biased µ0; give per-iteration complexity and memory; discuss unmodeled-appliance handling.  
- Post-rebuttal note: Authors addressed main questions satisfactorily; incorporate convexity, rounding, noise/SNR, and scaling clarifications in camera-ready.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept — novel, well-motivated, empirically strong with requested clarifications to address remaining gaps.
