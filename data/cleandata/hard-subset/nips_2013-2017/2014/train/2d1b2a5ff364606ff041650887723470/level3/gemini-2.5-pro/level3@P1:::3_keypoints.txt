- Tackles underdetermined blind source separation for energy disaggregation by decomposing a single household power signal into appliance-level signals.  
- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) that use known/estimated per-source total energy to regularize BSS.  
- Incorporates SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) to improve identifiability.  
- Uses a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation to perform efficient approximate MAP inference under non‑Markovian SACs.  
- Connects the penalized objective to the posterior regularization framework, providing theoretical justification.  
- Evaluates the AFHMM+SAC method on synthetic data and the HES real-world dataset, showing substantial gains over AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- SACs are a simple, intuitive, and potentially broadly applicable form of domain knowledge beyond energy (e.g., bioinformatics, audio).  
- The technical formulation and CQP derivation are sound, and experiments are thorough.  
- The paper is clearly written with well-explained motivation and methodology.  
- Experimental comparisons are potentially unfair because AFHMM+SAC is given aggregate targets (µi0) not available to baselines.  
- The method is sensitive to inaccurate aggregate constraints: poor µi0 can degrade performance below baselines.  
- The constraint framework is currently limited to linear sum-based SACs and lacks discussion of handling other useful constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration).  
- Overall recommendation: strong and suitable for NIPS, but needs fairer baseline comparisons and deeper analysis of sensitivity and generalizability.
