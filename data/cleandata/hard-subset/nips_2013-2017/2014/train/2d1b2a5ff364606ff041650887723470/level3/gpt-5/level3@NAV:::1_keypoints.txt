- Proposes AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge (e.g., daily Wh per appliance) for blind source separation/energy disaggregation.
- Reformulates MAP inference in AFHMMs using auxiliary transition variables and a binary-relaxation to obtain a convex quadratic program (CQP) solvable with CVX.
- Empirical results: outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP on a toy example and HES dataset, with paired t-tests showing statistical significance.
- Runtime is reported comparable to AFAMAP.
- Connects to posterior regularization and builds on FHMMs and AFAMAP, introducing a new practically motivated constraint class.
- Quality: modeling and optimization are sound and experiments thorough for the target application.
- Major methodological request: provide a formal proof that the relaxed objective (Eq. 6) is convex (show Λi and Vt PSD and constraints affine) and clearly separate the nonconvex IQP from its convex relaxation.
- Major evaluation request: include theoretical complexity analysis in T (sequence length), I (number of chains), and Ki (states per chain), and compare scaling to AFAMAP for large T, I.
- Clarity: exposition generally clear; fix minor notation error (extra commas in subscripts on p.4, line 202) and incorporate prior response clarifications in the camera-ready.
- Originality: SACs are a simple but novel constraint class for BSS and the convex relaxation in AFHMMs appears original relative to prior NILM work.
- Significance: demonstrated gains and comparable runtime suggest practical impact for energy disaggregation and potential transfer to other BSS problems.
- Experimental detail requests: justify toy experiment noise level (σ2=0.01) and report SNRs for mains and aggregate appliance signals in both toy and real data.
- Scalability requests: report wall-clock and memory scaling on larger instances and discuss tractability relative to AFAMAP.
- Pros: clear/simple idea, strong empirical gains with statistical tests, practical convex relaxation and solver use, useful connection to posterior regularization.
- Cons: missing formal convexity proof and scaling analysis, limited discussion of noise realism/SNR, reliance on quality of µ0 with limited guidance on obtaining robust priors.
- Overall: novel, well-executed contribution; reviewer leans positive contingent on addressing theoretical, clarity, and evaluation concerns.
