- Introduces AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- Proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate inference that enables efficient computation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with a good balance of theory and experiments.  
- Thorough discussion of problem setting and related work, situating the approach within energy disaggregation research.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on simulated and real-world data; AFHMM+SAC outperforms original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art on normalized disaggregation and signal aggregate errors.  
- Readability suffers from excessive and overly long acronyms (e.g., AFHMM+SAC, AFAMAP, NTC).  
- Authors incorrectly place a constraint on the prior density instead of stating an a priori constraint that constrains the posterior.  
- Originality is high: SACs provide a novel way to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation.  
- Significance is high: method addresses a hard energy-disaggregation problem and has broader applicability for embedding domain knowledge in probabilistic models.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to reduce acronyms, correct the constraint placement, and expand discussion of implications and applications.
