- Summary: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in an AFHMM for electricity disaggregation, penalizing deviation from known per-source total energy and solving a convex quadratic program relaxation.  
- Strength: Clear, intuitive constraint class (aggregate energy totals) that addresses identifiability in BSS/NILM.  
- Strength: Neat convex relaxation derivation using transition-indicator variables and PSD structure.  
- Strength: Substantial empirical improvement over AFHMM and AFAMAP on low-frequency real-world HES data.  
- Strength: Practical relevance by leveraging externally available aggregate statistics.  
- Weakness: Manuscript is notation-heavy and hard to read with excessive acronyms.  
- Weakness: Confusing posterior vs prior framing—SACs act as an a priori quadratic potential and should be explicitly described as such.  
- Weakness: Paper omits clear description of how fractional CQP solutions are converted to discrete state sequences and lacks complexity bounds.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter λ fixed to 1 without principled selection; limited sensitivity analysis to λ and µ0 errors.  
- Weakness: Evaluation relies on TTC oracle and lacks thorough ablations (states, NTC quality, windowed SACs).  
- Quality: CQP derivation appears technically sound with PSD matrices making the relaxation convex.  
- Quality concern 1: Need details on discretization—how continuous Sit and hitjk are mapped back and effect on metrics.  
- Quality concern 2: Need complexity/scalability analysis, per-problem sizes, runtimes, and discussion of scalable solvers (e.g., ADMM).  
- Quality concern 3: Require principled hyperparameter selection (cross-validation/sensitivity sweep) on real data.  
- Quality concern 4: Clarify parameter learning protocol for θ, initialization, regularization, and sensitivity to Ki.  
- Clarity suggestion: Reduce/standardize acronyms, add a glossary, and include a notational table.  
- Clarity suggestion: Add a high-level overview figure of AFHMM+SAC, reorder Sections 4–6 to present intuition first, and include an algorithmic summary.  
- Originality: Formulation of signal-total constraints within an AFHMM plus the convex relaxation is novel and a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: Demonstrated gains on challenging, low-frequency data are practically important and broadly applicable to BSS tasks.  
- Specific request 1: Describe discrete reconstruction from relaxed solutions and report fractionality and performance impact.  
- Specific request 2: Provide a λ grid sweep on HES and consider methods to learn λ (evidence maximization/L-curve).  
- Specific request 3: Explore modeling uncertainty in µ0 or jointly learning µ0 with weak priors.  
- Specific request 4: Experiment with windowed SACs (e.g., daytime/nighttime aggregates).  
- Specific request 5: Compare AFAMAP augmented with SACs to isolate the value of aggregate information vs convex relaxation.  
- Specific request 6: Report problem sizes, wall-clock runtimes, and alternatives to CVX for scalability.  
- Specific request 7: Release code, preprocessing scripts, and precise dataset/training/test splits for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Simple, well-motivated idea; sound convex formulation; strong empirical gains on real data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Poor readability, missing discretization/hyperparameter/scalability details, limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Leaning accept.
