Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation in an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) applied to energy disaggregation.  
Transforms the constrained optimization into a convex quadratic program for efficient inference.  
Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP, including a 33% NDE improvement vs AFAMAP.  
Thorough experimental validation on toy data and the Household Electricity Survey dataset with statistical significance testing.  
Convex optimization formulation is well-motivated and inference times are comparable to AFAMAP.  
Theoretical foundation is sound and connects appropriately to posterior regularization.  
Missing formal proof of convexity for the procedure described in equation (6).  
Novel and practically relevant application of SACs to energy disaggregation with improved interpretability for end users.  
Toy experiment uses an unrealistically low noise variance (σ² = 0.01), requiring clarification on realism vs simplification.  
Lacks a detailed SNR analysis for mains vs. aggregate appliance signals.  
No theoretical complexity analysis (dependence on data length, number of hidden states) or scalability/tractability comparison with AFAMAP on larger datasets.  
Minor notation issue (extra commas in subscripts, page 4, line 202) that should be fixed.  
Authors addressed reviewer concerns in response, but incorporating clarifications into the paper is recommended.  
Novel within the energy disaggregation literature, but broader significance to the HMM community needs more discussion.  
Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical and experimental clarifications.
