- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: extends AFHMM by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation.  
- SACs encourage each source’s total signal to be close to a specified expected value based on prior knowledge.  
- Derives an approximate inference method as a convex quadratic program for AFHMM+SAC.  
- Evaluates on two real-world energy disaggregation datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).  
- AFHMM+SAC outperforms original AFHMM and AFAMAP on normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and places the work in context of prior literature.  
- Evaluation is thorough, results are convincing, and experimental setup is reproducible for experts.  
- Technically sound with theory and experiments supporting claims.  
- Novel contribution: combining SACs with AFHMM is not a trivial extension and is well-motivated.  
- Significant application to energy disaggregation with potential practical impact.  
- Requests clarification on whether SACAFHMM yields more integral solutions (like AFAMAP’s “one chain state at a time”) or attains lower error with non-integral solutions.  
- Notes occasional conflation of models and inference procedures; recommends explicitly distinguishing AFAMAP as an approximate MAP algorithm for AFHMM and clarifying inference methods (e.g., alternating Viterbi).  
- Suggests exploring combinations of different algorithmic strategies in future work to further improve performance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarifications on comparisons, notation, and precise terminology.
