- Paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- SACs constrain each source's total signal (sum over time) to be close to a specified value to address identifiability.  
- Authors integrate SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM and transform the constrained problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient solution.  
- Empirical results on real-world data show a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error versus original AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP.  
- SACs leverage domain knowledge (e.g., appliance average energy) and are an intuitive, practical prior-style constraint.  
- Technical approach is sound and computationally efficient, with statistically significant experimental results.  
- Connection to posterior regularization is claimed but presented unclearly and somewhat incorrectly.  
- Manuscript overuses acronyms (e.g., repeated "AFHMM+SAC"), harming readability.  
- The paper frames SACs as placed on the prior rather than explicitly as posterior constraints, creating conceptual confusion that must be clarified.  
- Lack of discussion about limitations and sensitivity: when SACs fail and how errors in aggregate values affect results are not explored.  
- Toy experiments are simplistic and do not sufficiently test SAC behavior under varying constraint specifications.  
- Originality: SACs are a novel constraint type distinct from prior transition/duration constraints and meaningfully advance constrained HMM approaches.  
- Significance: substantial empirical gains and broad applicability to other blind source separation problems make the work practically valuable.  
- Recommendation: promising paper but requires revision for clarity (reduce acronyms) and conceptual fixes regarding the prior/posterior framing before acceptance.
