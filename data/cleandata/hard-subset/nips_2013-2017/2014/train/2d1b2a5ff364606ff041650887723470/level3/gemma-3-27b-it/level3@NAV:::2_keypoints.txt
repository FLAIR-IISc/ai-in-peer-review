- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into Blind Source Separation, applied to energy disaggregation with an AFHMM.  
- SACs encourage each source’s total signal to match predefined expected values from domain knowledge.  
- Formulates SACs as constraints in the optimization and proposes a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate inference.  
- Reports strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error and 29% improvement over a recent SOTA on a real-world dataset.  
- Positions the contribution within existing BSS/AFHMM literature and connects to posterior regularization and measurement-based learning.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, novel constraint formulation, substantial empirical improvements, and real-world evaluation.  
- Readability is poor due to excessive and long acronyms (e.g., AFHMM+SAC, TTC, NTC).  
- Conceptual ambiguity: unclear whether the constraint is applied to the prior or the posterior—this must be explicitly clarified.  
- Technical exposition is rushed: the CQP derivation and the relaxed problem need more detailed explanation.  
- Reviewer recommendation: conditional acceptance contingent on substantially improved clarity, reduced acronym usage, and a precise explanation of how the constraint affects inference.
