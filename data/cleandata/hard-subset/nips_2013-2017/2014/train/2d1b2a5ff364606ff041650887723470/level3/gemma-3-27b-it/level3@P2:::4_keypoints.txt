- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation within AFHMMs.  
- Formulates SACs as a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on the HES energy disaggregation dataset (45% reduction vs AFHMM, 29% vs AFAMAP).  
- Builds on and cites relevant BSS and energy disaggregation literature, situating SACs among prior methods like posterior regularization and generalized expectation.  
- Practical relevance: tackles a real-world, impactful problem (energy disaggregation) with a large representative dataset.  
- Simple, intuitive idea with tractable mathematical formulation.  
- Clear presentation and accessible mathematical notation.  
- Computationally efficient and scales similarly to existing approaches.  
- Open question whether SACs yield more integral solutions or achieve improvements despite non-integral state occupancies—needs clarification.  
- Occasional conflation of models and inference algorithms (e.g., AFAMAP vs AFHMM results) reduces precision.  
- Limited exploration of combining SACs with other techniques (e.g., incorporating SACs into AFAMAP) — promising future direction.  
- Hyperparameter tuning (λ) process and sensitivity analysis are under-described.  
- Original contribution: novel use of aggregate constraints and convex inference for identifiability in BSS.  
- Significant potential beyond energy disaggregation; broadly applicable where source totals are known.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on clarifying solution integrality, improving model/inference precision, and discussing combination with methods like AFAMAP.
