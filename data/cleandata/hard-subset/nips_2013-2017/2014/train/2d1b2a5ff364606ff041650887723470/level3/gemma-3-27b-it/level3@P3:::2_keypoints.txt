- Proposes AFHMM augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve energy disaggregation by incorporating prior knowledge of appliances’ total consumption.  
- Formulates approximate inference as a convex quadratic program for efficient optimization.  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains on a real-world dataset (HES), outperforming standard AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Problem is well-motivated and positioned within prior work on blind source separation and identifiability.  
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization, providing useful theoretical grounding.  
- Strength: SACs are a practical, effective way to inject domain knowledge and improve identifiability.  
- Strength: Experimental validation includes both simulated and real-world data, increasing credibility.  
- Strength: Conversion to a convex QP enables efficient inference.  
- Weakness: Paper readability suffers from excessive and long acronyms and dense, hard-to-follow writing.  
- Critical implementation concern: the SACs are applied via a penalized prior (eq. 5), which effectively biases the posterior rather than directly enforcing aggregate constraints—this needs explicit clarification.  
- Suggestion: Consider alternative implementations (post-processing to enforce constraints or incorporating constraints into the likelihood) or explicitly state the prior/posterior interpretation.  
- Originality: AFHMM itself is not new, but combining AFHMM with SACs and deriving the convex QP is novel.  
- Significance: Method is broadly applicable to other blind source separation problems and has real-world energy-saving implications.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance contingent on substantially improving clarity (fewer acronyms, clearer exposition) and explicitly addressing the prior vs. posterior constraint formulation.
