- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into AFHMMs to address unidentifiability in blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.
- Leverages prior knowledge of total signal by encouraging source aggregates to match specified values.
- Formulates approximate MAP inference as a convex quadratic program for tractable optimization.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on a real-world HES dataset: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. AFHMM and 29% improvement vs. AFAMAP.
- Positions work within blind source separation and prior-knowledge literature, citing Hart (1978), Kolter & Jaakkola (2012), posterior regularization, and measurement-based learning.
- Introduces SACs with a clear, intuitive mathematical formulation and connects to posterior regularization for theoretical grounding.
- Claims computational efficiency by converting SACs into a solvable convex QP with similar cost to comparable methods.
- Evaluation is thorough, using both simulated robustness tests and realistic energy-disaggregation experiments.
- Unclear whether SAC solutions are closer to integer-valued (interpretable) states or rely on non-integral relaxations; needs clarification.
- Paper sometimes conflates model (AFHMM) and inference method (AFAMAP); should consistently distinguish AFAMAP as an approximate MAP algorithm for AFHMM.
- Limited exploration of combining SACs with orthogonal constraints like AFAMAP; integrating SACs into AFAMAP is suggested but not investigated.
- Hyperparameter tuning discussion is limited; sensitivity to λ (or δ) requires more detailed analysis.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a hard problem with a novel, well-motivated approach, strong empirical results, clear math, and solid writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor issues around solution integrality and lack of exploration of combined inference approaches.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address interpretability, combination with other methods, and parameter sensitivity.
