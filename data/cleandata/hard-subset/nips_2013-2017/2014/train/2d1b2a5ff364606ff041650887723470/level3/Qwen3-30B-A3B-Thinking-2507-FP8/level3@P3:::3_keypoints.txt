- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Incorporates known expected total signal values as constraints within the Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) framework.  
- Main technical contribution: a convex quadratic programming relaxation enabling efficient inference despite non‑Markovian constraints.  
- Builds on prior AFHMM and constraint-based methods (posterior regularization, AFAMAP) and differentiates SACs by domain knowledge and convex tractability.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with rigorous derivation and connection to posterior regularization.  
- Clearly motivates the problem and why aggregate totals are natural constraints in energy disaggregation.  
- Empirical results show large improvements (45% NDE decrease vs AFHMM, 29% vs AFAMAP) and synthetic experiments linking constraint accuracy to performance.  
- Paper is well‑written, organized, and presents detailed mathematical derivations.  
- Comparison with baselines may be unfair: SACAFHMM uses NTC from training data while AFAMAP does not, possibly biasing results.  
- Claim that SACs “always help” is misleading—when constraints are very inaccurate AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM, so poorly specified constraints can harm performance.  
- Focus is limited to simple aggregate constraints and does not address more complex constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration).  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis comparing SACAFHMM to other constraint-based approaches.  
- Suggested fixes: ensure fair baseline comparisons (e.g., incorporate constraints into AFAMAP), acknowledge risk of poor constraints and provide guidance, discuss extending to complex constraints, and add complexity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and significant contribution with strong theory and promising results; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
