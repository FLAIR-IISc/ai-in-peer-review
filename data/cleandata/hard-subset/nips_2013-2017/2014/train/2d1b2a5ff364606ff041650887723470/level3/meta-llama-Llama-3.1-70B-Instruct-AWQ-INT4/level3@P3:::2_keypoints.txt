- Proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for electricity usage disaggregation.  
- Key idea: incorporate domain knowledge by constraining aggregate values of source signals.  
- Empirical results on a real-world dataset show significant improvements over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and provides a clear introduction to blind source separation and motivation for SACs.  
- Thorough related work review and clear explanation of the proposed model and its components.  
- Experimental results are well-presented and demonstrate the benefits of using SACs.  
- Excessive use of acronyms (some very long) harms readability; recommend defining them at first use and using sparingly.  
- Authors appear to place the constraint on the prior rather than directly on the posterior; clarify that it is an a priori constraint affecting the posterior.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experiments supporting the claims.  
- Needs a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed approach.  
- Clarity is generally good for experts, but readability suffers for non-experts due to acronym usage.  
- Originality is strong: SACs are a novel contribution to incorporating domain knowledge into AFHMM.  
- Significance is high: tackles a difficult real-world energy disaggregation problem with an impactful solution.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address acronym usage, clarify prior/posterior relationship, and expand limitations discussion.
