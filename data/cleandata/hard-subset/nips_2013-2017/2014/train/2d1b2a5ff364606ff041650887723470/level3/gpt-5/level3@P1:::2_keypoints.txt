- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs, enforcing per-chain total energy via quadratic penalties to improve blind source separation.
- Derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation for MAP inference by augmenting the AFHMM with auxiliary transition variables.
- Connects the SAC objective to posterior regularization, clarifying the statistical interpretation of the penalty.
- Shows substantial empirical gains on low-frequency energy disaggregation (HES), outperforming AFHMM and AFAMAP, with oracle results indicating upper-bound benefits.
- Simple, general idea that directly addresses identifiability in BSS/NILM and is broadly applicable.
- Clean, tractable optimization exploiting PSD structure; implemented with CVX and similar runtime to baselines in experiments.
- Empirical improvements are statistically significant and shown on a realistic, challenging dataset.
- Useful extension: supports multiple time-window (windowed) constraints for practical relevance.
- Manuscript suffers from heavy notation and excessive acronyms, reducing readability.
- Recommend moving dense derivations to an appendix and adding a high-level algorithm box and intuition-first walkthrough.
- Need to reconcile presentation of SACs as an a priori quadratic prior vs. posterior-regularization derivation explicitly.
- Hyperparameter selection is under-explored: λi fixed to 1 with no cross-validation or sensitivity analysis on real data.
- Robustness to misspecified aggregate targets µ0 is insufficiently studied beyond a toy example.
- Rounding/discreteness is unclear: paper relaxes binary variables but does not describe how discrete state sequences are recovered or performance differences when rounding.
- Baselines and ablations are limited; comparisons to HSMM/duration models and AFAMAP with added priors, plus ablation on windowed SACs, are missing.
- External-knowledge portability concerns: need discussion on learning µ0 from limited metadata, handling missing/unseen appliances, and demographic/seasonal bias.
- Experimental reporting is thin: only nine houses, limited variability/CI reporting, unclear train/test splits, uniform state-count choice, and scaling analysis absent.
- Technically sound formulation and correct optimization/PSD structure, but reproducibility would improve with more rounding and hyperparameter details.
- Originality: novel application of aggregate-total constraints in AFHMM with CQP relaxation and PR link, incremental but practically meaningful relative to prior work.
- Significance: strong empirical gains on a real benchmark and broadly useful idea for domains where aggregates are known or estimable.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, tractable solution to identifiability with significant empirical improvements and theoretical framing.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor presentation, limited hyperparameter/robustness analysis, unclear rounding, and insufficient comparison to alternative strong baselines.
- Actionable improvements: reduce acronyms, add algorithm box, move algebra to appendix, explicitly reconcile prior vs posterior views, provide λ and µ0 sensitivity analyses, detail rounding, expand baselines/ablations, and discuss practical estimation of µ0 and robustness to misspecification.
