- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to regularize blind source separation by forcing each source’s total signal toward a known value; implemented in AFHMM with a CQP relaxation and linked to posterior regularization.

- Originality & significance: novel, intuitive, and practical use of aggregate priors that yield substantial real-world improvements (energy disaggregation).

- Technical quality: solid mathematical formulation of constrained MAP and a tractable CQP relaxation with theoretical justification via posterior regularization.

- Clarity: well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow; motivation, model, and experiments are presented precisely.

- Empirical results: SACAFHMM shows significant improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP on NDE and SAE in both synthetic and real datasets.

- Fairness of comparison: baselines did not receive the national total consumption (NTC) vector used by SACAFHMM, so gains may partly reflect extra data rather than the SAC formulation alone.

- Impact of inaccurate constraints: performance degrades as the provided aggregate (µ0) deviates from truth; authors should report the crossover point where constraints become harmful.

- Limited scope of constraint types: current work focuses on sum-over-time aggregates; should discuss/extend to other useful constraints (e.g., mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration limits).

- Recommendation: High-quality, novel contribution with sound execution; recommend acceptance pending improved experimental framing and discussion of constraint scope.
