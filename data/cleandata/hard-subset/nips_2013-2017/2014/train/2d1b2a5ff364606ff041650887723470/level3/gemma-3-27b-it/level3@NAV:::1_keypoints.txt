- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within AFHMMs to address identifiability in blind source separation (energy disaggregation).  
- SACs enforce expected aggregate signal values per source as a form of domain knowledge.  
- Reformulates the resulting inference problem as a convex quadratic program for efficient approximate inference (convexity proof not provided).  
- Validated on a toy dataset and a real household energy dataset.  
- Shows consistent empirical improvements and a statistically significant improvement over AFAMAP.  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Positions the work within BSS/AFHMM literature and relates to posterior regularization; novelty lies in aggregate-value constraints.  
- Theoretical presentation is clear and well-structured.  
- Concern: toy experiment uses very low noise variance—needs SNR discussion for mains and individual appliances.  
- Concern: missing formal convexity proof and missing theoretical scaling/complexity analysis (data length, number of hidden states).  
- Concern: need tractability comparison to AFAMAP on larger datasets; minor notation issue (extra commas in subscripts on p.4 line 202).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing the above points.
