- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation in an Additive Factorial HMM to enforce known aggregate totals (e.g., appliance daily energy) and address identifiability.  
- Formulates a convex quadratic program (convex relaxation) to efficiently solve the constrained optimization.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical gains: SACAFHMM achieves 45% lower NDE and 29% better SAE than AFAMAP on real-world data, with paired t-test significance.  
- Paper is clear and mathematically well-founded; motivation for energy disaggregation is strong and domain knowledge use is sensible.  
- Technical approach is sound and implemented using standard optimization tools.  
- Comparison methodology is potentially unfair: baselines (AFHMM, AFAMAP) used less training data than SACAFHMM, possibly inflating improvements.  
- Figure 1 contradicts the “SACs always help” claim: AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM at large constraint distances, implying poorly specified constraints can hurt performance.  
- Authors should acknowledge the limitation and provide guidance on selecting appropriate constraint values.  
- Suggest extending SACs to richer constraint types (mutual exclusivity, duration limits) to broaden applicability beyond aggregate sums.  
- Relation to posterior regularization is noted but should be contrasted more explicitly with related work.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel contribution with strong validation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify limitations and improve methodological transparency.
