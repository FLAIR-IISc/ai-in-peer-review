- Introduces SACs to AFHMMs for energy disaggregation, a practical and compelling extension.  
- Uses a convex relaxation for efficient inference and shows significant empirical improvements on real-world datasets.  
- Leverages prior knowledge of total appliance energy to address identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Well-situated in related work; connection to posterior regularization provides theoretical grounding.  
- Strong empirical contribution: evaluated on 100 UK homes (low-frequency) plus smaller high-frequency set.  
- Demonstrates improvements over baseline AFHMM (alternating Viterbi) and state-of-the-art AFAMAP.  
- Clear presentation of optimization formulation and use of CVX.  
- Conflation between the AFHMM model and AFAMAP inference procedure introduces ambiguity and needs clarification.  
- Unresolved whether SACAFHMM produces more integral (physically interpretable) solutions or simply lower error; requires analysis.  
- Combining AFAMAP’s state-change constraints with SACAFHMM and exploring other constraint types is a promising direction.  
- Claim of similar runtime to AFHMM/AFAMAP needs concrete runtime comparisons and complexity details.  
- Technically sound: convex relaxation is justified and experiments are robust.  
- Generally well-written, but occasional ambiguity due to model/inference conflation.  
- Original and novel: SACs as a general approach to BSS applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Significant practical impact with likely adoption by researchers/practitioners.  
- For acceptance: novel, strong empirical results, clear presentation, and promising follow-ups.  
- Against acceptance: requires clarification on model vs inference, integrality analysis, and more runtime evidence.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
