This paper proposes an extension to the additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) by incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve the performance of blind source separation problems. The authors introduce a convex quadratic program for approximate inference in the AFHMM+SAC model and apply it to the energy disaggregation problem. The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly presented.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of SACs, which encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. This is a reasonable assumption in many applications, including energy disaggregation, where the total energy consumption of each appliance is often known in advance. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of SACs in improving the performance of the AFHMM on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset.

The paper is related to previous work on energy disaggregation, including the additive factorial approximate maximum a posteriori (AFAMAP) algorithm, which introduces a constraint that at most one chain can change state at any one time point. The authors show that their approach is orthogonal to AFAMAP and suggest combining SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP as a promising experiment for future work.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the AFHMM+SAC model, including the convex quadratic program for approximate inference. The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating a significant improvement in performance over the AFHMM and AFAMAP on a large real-world dataset.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. The authors occasionally conflate models and inference procedures, labeling AFAMAP vs AFHMM. It would be helpful to clarify that AFAMAP is an approximate MAP algorithm for AFHMM and that AFHMM results likely refer to alternating Viterbi. Additionally, the authors could provide more detail on the optimization formulation and the convex relaxation used for MAP inference.
