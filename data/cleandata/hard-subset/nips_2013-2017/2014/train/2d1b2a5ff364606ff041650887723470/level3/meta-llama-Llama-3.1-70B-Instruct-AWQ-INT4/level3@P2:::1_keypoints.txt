- Proposes an AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs enforce source signal aggregates to be close to known domain values, improving identifiability.  
- Incorporates SACs into the AFHMM and reformulates the posterior log objective into a convex optimization problem.  
- Uses convex quadratic programming for efficient inference in the AFHMM+SAC model.  
- Connects the approach to prior work in blind source separation and energy disaggregation, highlighting identifiability challenges.  
- Shows relationship to posterior regularization and derives the objective from that framework.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear technical presentation and thorough model/optimization descriptions.  
- Experimental results on a toy dataset and real domestic data show strong performance gains.  
- Method outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP, making it practically viable.  
- Concerns: toy experiment uses very low noise variance; authors should report mains vs. appliance SNR to clarify realism.  
- Theoretical gap: a formal proof of convexity for the procedure in equation (6) is missing.  
- Scalability gap: need theoretical scaling/complexity analysis and tractability comparison with AFAMAP for larger datasets.  
- Minor issues: notation errors (e.g., extra commas in subscripts) should be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: novel, significant contribution to the field; recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the above points.
