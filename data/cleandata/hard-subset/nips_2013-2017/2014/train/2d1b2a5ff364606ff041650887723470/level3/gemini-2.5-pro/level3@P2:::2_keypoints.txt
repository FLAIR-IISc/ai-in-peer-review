- Problem: Blind source separation for energy disaggregation is highly unidentifiable, making separated signals hard to interpret.  
- Proposal: Introduce Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge about total energy consumption per source.  
- Model integration: SACs are incorporated into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM), but are non‑Markovian and complicate inference.  
- Optimization: Formulate MAP inference as a penalized objective and derive a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation for efficient solution.  
- Theoretical link: Authors relate the formulation to posterior regularization, offering an alternative perspective on the penalty term.  
- Empirical results: Strong improvements on a real low-frequency energy dataset, including a 29% NDE gain over state-of-the-art AFAMAP.  
- Experimental design: Uses both oracle (True Total Consumption) and practical (National Total Consumption) constraints to demonstrate utility.  
- Strength — originality: SACs are simple, intuitive, and broadly applicable to other BSS problems where aggregate statistics are known.  
- Strength — technical quality: The constrained optimization and CQP relaxation are well-executed and technically sound.  
- Strength — empirical evaluation: Convincing results that substantiate the method’s practical impact.  
- Weakness — clarity: Manuscript is dense and difficult to read, significantly detracting from the work.  
- Weakness — acronyms: Excessive and cumbersome acronyms (e.g., AFAMAP, AFHMM+SAC, NDE, SAE) impede readability.  
- Weakness — ambiguity: Unclear statistical interpretation whether the objective is a MAP under a new prior or a posterior-regularized variational approximation.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, effective method addressing an important real-world problem with strong empirical support.  
- Argument against acceptance: Poor writing and presentation risk obscuring contributions; needs major revision.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on a major revision focused on improving clarity and resolving the technical interpretation ambiguity.
