This paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for electricity usage disaggregation. The key idea is to incorporate domain knowledge into the model by adding constraints on the aggregate values of the source signals. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset, showing significant improvements over the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear introduction to the problem of blind source separation and the motivation for incorporating domain knowledge. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain the proposed model and its components. The experimental results are well-presented and demonstrate the benefits of using SACs.

However, the paper suffers from an excessive use of acronyms, some of which are unusually long (≈11 letters). This makes the text difficult to read and understand, particularly for non-experts in the field. It would be helpful to define each acronym when it is first introduced and to use them sparingly throughout the paper.

Furthermore, the authors appear to place the constraint on the prior rather than directly on the posterior. It would be more accurate to explicitly state that this is an a priori constraint, thereby constraining the posterior. This would help to clarify the relationship between the prior and posterior distributions.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for their approach. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed approach.

The clarity of the paper is good, but as mentioned earlier, the excessive use of acronyms detracts from the overall readability. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide enough information for an expert reader to reproduce the results.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to incorporating domain knowledge into the AFHMM. The use of SACs is a new contribution, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in improving the performance of the model.

The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses a difficult problem in energy disaggregation and provides a novel solution that can be applied to real-world data. The results are important, and the paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to incorporating domain knowledge into the AFHMM.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset.
* The paper addresses a difficult problem in energy disaggregation and provides a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The excessive use of acronyms detracts from the overall readability of the paper.
* The authors could provide a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed approach.
* The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the relationship between the prior and posterior distributions.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, but with revisions to address the issues mentioned above.
