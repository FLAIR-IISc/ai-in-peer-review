- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within an AFHMM for energy disaggregation to address identifiability issues.  
- Incorporation of prior knowledge about total appliance energy is sensible and potentially valuable.  
- Mathematical formulation is sound and the paper is well-written with a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Results on synthetic and real-world data show promising improvements in NDE and SAE.  
- Experimental setup concern: baselines use less data, making comparisons potentially unfair and confounding reported gains.  
- Figure 1 is misleading: at large distances of µ0 AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM, showing poorly chosen constraints can worsen performance.  
- Paper should more prominently discuss and acknowledge the risk that inaccurate priors (µ0) can be detrimental.  
- Limited scope: focuses only on simple aggregate constraints and needs extension to broader constraint types.  
- Suggested extensions: mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, and duration limits to broaden applicability.  
- Relation to posterior regularization is well-established and strengthens theoretical grounding.  
- Computational complexity analysis is reassuring: similar scaling across methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of SACs, solid theory, demonstrated empirical improvements, clear writing, and promising direction.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially unfair baseline comparison, misleading presentation of Figure 1, limited scope, and insufficient sensitivity analysis to µ0 accuracy.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — equalize dataset usage for fair comparisons and explicitly address Figure 1 and SAC sensitivity/limitations.
