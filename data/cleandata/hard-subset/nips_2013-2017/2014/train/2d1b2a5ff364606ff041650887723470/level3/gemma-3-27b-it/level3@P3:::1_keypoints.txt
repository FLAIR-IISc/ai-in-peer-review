- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into AFHMM for energy disaggregation, leveraging prior total energy per appliance.  
- Transforms the inference problem into a convex quadratic program for efficient computation.  
- Positions work within BSS and energy disaggregation literature and prior-knowledge probabilistic models.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements in simulations and real-world data over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Practical approach that improves interpretability and trustworthiness of disaggregated results.  
- Computational efficiency shown to be comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Clear presentation and well-structured derivation of the optimization.  
- Toy experiment uses very low noise variance (σ2 = 0.01); realism and SNR context are unclear and need clarification.  
- Missing formal proof of convexity for the procedure/equation (6).  
- Lacks theoretical scalability/complexity analysis (scaling with T and Ki) and tractability comparisons for larger datasets.  
- Minor notation issue: extra commas in subscripts before ‘p’ (page 4, line 20).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, strong empirical results, significant technical contribution, rebuttal concerns addressed.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absent convexity proof, absent scalability analysis, questionable toy noise realism.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance contingent on addressing noise realism, providing convexity proof, and adding scalability clarifications.
