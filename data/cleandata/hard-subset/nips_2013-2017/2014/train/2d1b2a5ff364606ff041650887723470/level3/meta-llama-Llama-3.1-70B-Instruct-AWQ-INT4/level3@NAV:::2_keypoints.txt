- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: an additive factorial HMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints to improve identifiability in energy disaggregation.
- Clear, concise introduction and thorough related-work review framing the blind source separation challenge and need for domain knowledge.
- AFHMM+SAC is well-motivated and theoretically sound with a detailed model derivation.
- Optimization is formulated as a convex quadratic program and solved accordingly.
- Empirical results on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset show significant improvement over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-organized with clear headings and concise sections.
- Excessive and unusually long acronyms severely harm readability, especially for non-experts.
- Authors appear to impose the constraint on the prior rather than directly on the posterior; this should be explicitly stated as an a priori constraint.
- Overall, a strong scientific contribution to energy disaggregation and blind source separation using SACs.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel domain-knowledge incorporation, impressive real-world results, and solid theoretical grounding.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor readability due to acronyms and unclear statement about prior vs posterior constraint.
- Recommendations: reduce acronyms and define them clearly.
- Recommendations: explicitly state that the constraint is on the prior (and thus constrains the posterior).
- Recommendations: provide more details on computational complexity and optimization costs.
