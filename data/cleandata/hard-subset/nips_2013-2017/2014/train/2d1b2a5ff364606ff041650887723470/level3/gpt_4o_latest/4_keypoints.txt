- Introduces SACs into AFHMM for blind source separation/energy disaggregation and uses a CQP relaxation for MAP inference with real-world UK household data.

- Novelty/practical relevance: SACs creatively address identifiability and apply to a real-world problem.

- Technical soundness: Rigorous CQP MAP derivation, clear connection to posterior regularization, and scalable computation comparable to AFHMM/AFAMAP.

- Empirical validation: Extensive synthetic and UK Household Electricity Survey experiments showing substantial NDE and SAE improvements.

- Clarity of results: Clear comparisons to AFHMM and AFAMAP and analysis of robustness to SAC specification errors.

- Conflation issue: Paper confuses the AFHMM model with the AFAMAP optimization procedure, reducing clarity.

- Non-integral solutions: Insufficient discussion on whether improved errors stem from non-integral solutions of the convex relaxation and the associated trade-offs.

- Orthogonality unexplored: Does not investigate combining AFAMAP and SACAFHMM despite claiming orthogonality.

- Limited SAC sensitivity analysis: Assumes SAC values are known and does not deeply explore sensitivity to inaccurate or unavailable SACs.

- For acceptance: Addresses a significant real problem, offers novel technical contributions, and demonstrates compelling empirical gains.

- Against acceptance: Presentation confusions and missing discussions on non-integral solutions and method integration raise unanswered questions.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify model vs. optimization distinctions and expand discussions on non-integral solutions and method interplay.
