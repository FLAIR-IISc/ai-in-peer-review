- Problem and approach: Introduces per-source signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs and a convex quadratic relaxation using pairwise transition variables to solve the resulting non-decomposable MAP.  
- Theoretical connection: Shows the same objective follows from posterior regularization, and proves the relaxation is convex given PSD quadratic terms.  
- Empirical claim: Reports improvements in NDE and SAE on synthetic and HES energy-disaggregation data compared to AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Methodological gaps: Underspecified how fractional posteriors are converted to discrete sequences and no sensitivity analysis for the penalty weight λ (fixed to 1).  
- Baseline fairness: Comparisons may be unfair because SACAFHMM uses extra aggregate information (NTC/TTC) that AFHMM/AFAMAP do not; oracle conditions need clear separation.  
- Robustness concern: Figure 1 indicates misspecified µ0 can hurt performance (AFAMAP can outperform SACAFHMM), contradicting claims of robustness; figure needs clearer labels, units, and CIs.  
- Novelty: Per-source SACs for AFHMMs and the specific convex surrogate are novel and timely, despite posterior regularization being known.  
- Significance: Approach is relevant to energy disaggregation and other BSS tasks; global aggregate priors could have broad impact.  
- Strengths: Clear motivation, principled derivation (optimization + posterior regularization), implementable convex QP, and promising real-data gains.  
- Constraint expressivity: Paper should discuss/extensions to other useful constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration/semi-Markov constraints) and whether the CQP can encode them.  
- Rounding/interpretablity: Need clarification on how fractional outputs are rounded and how rounding affects NDE/SAE and interpretability.  
- Hyperparameters/uncertainty: Recommend sensitivity curves for λ and methods to set λ or model µ0 uncertainty (e.g., hierarchical priors, variance-weighted penalties, robust losses).  
- Scaling/run-time: Provide runtime and memory scaling vs T, number of chains, and states; CVX may not scale and practical heuristics should be discussed.  
- Suggested experiments: Augment AFAMAP/AFHMM with SAC via posterior regularization, compare HSMM/k-segment methods with aggregate penalties, and run ablations (relaxation-only, λ=0, sensitivity to µ0).  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel, principled integration of global aggregates into factorial HMMs, solid math, reusable convex surrogate, and meaningful NILM results.  
- Cons against acceptance: Fairness of comparisons, insufficient robustness analysis to mis-specified constraints, limited exploration of richer constraint types and λ/scaling.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper but should be revised to address baseline fairness, robustness to µ0/λ, and extensions/clarifications before final acceptance.
