- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge and resolve identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Applies SACs to an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation.  
- Main technical contribution: tractable MAP inference by relaxing the non‑Markovian posterior into a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Authors relax integer constraints to enable efficient optimization and link the approach to posterior regularization.  
- Novelty: SACs are intuitive and exploit available priors on total source contribution, moving beyond structural constraints like AFAMAP.  
- Technical quality: solid formulation of AFHMM+SAC and a nontrivial derivation of a solvable CQP despite broken Markov structure.  
- Significance: strong real-world impact demonstrated—29% reduction in disaggregation error vs. AFAMAP on low-frequency mains data.  
- Practicality: optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP, making the method feasible for real applications.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, logically presented, and experiments are clearly described.  
- Weakness: should include a short formal proof or more detailed justification that the relaxed problem is convex.  
- Weakness: toy experiment uses very low noise (σ² = 0.01); authors should justify realism and discuss typical SNR in real energy data.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical complexity analysis—need scaling of CQP solve time with T, I, and number of hidden states and comparison to AFAMAP.  
- Minor issue: typographical/notational error (extra commas in subscripts like `Si,τ(i)j`) that should be fixed.  
- Final recommendation: strong, novel, and effective work; incorporate clarifications and revisions and recommend acceptance.
