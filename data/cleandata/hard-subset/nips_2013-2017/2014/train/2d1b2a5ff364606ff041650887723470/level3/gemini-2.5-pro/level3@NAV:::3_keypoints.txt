- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within an Additive Factorial HMM (SACAFHMM) to improve identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Introduces a convex quadratic programming relaxation to make MAP inference tractable.  
- Builds on prior constrained AFHMM work (e.g., AFAMAP) by adding a complementary form of prior knowledge.  
- Technically sound with a solid mathematical foundation and clear, well-written exposition.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical improvements in Normalized Disaggregation Error (NDE) and Signal Aggregate Error (SAE) on real-world energy data.  
- Novel contribution: modeling SACs as a quadratic penalty and solving via convex quadratic programming.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair because SACAFHMM is given aggregate targets (µi0) that baselines do not receive.  
- Claim that SACs consistently help is unsupported; poor constraints can degrade performance (AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM when constraints are bad).  
- Paper lacks explicit discussion of the negative impact of inaccurate priors and should acknowledge this limitation.  
- Evaluation scope is limited—should compare to other methods that can incorporate SACs (even if less efficient).  
- Framework could be extended to handle more realistic constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, state duration limits).  
- Overall: original and valuable idea, but claims of superiority should be tempered and comparisons reframed; suggest expanding evaluation and generalizing constraints in future work.
