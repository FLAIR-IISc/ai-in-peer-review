- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within AFHMM for energy disaggregation, using domain knowledge about total appliance energy — a novel, valuable idea.  
- Derives a convex quadratic program for approximate inference that is mathematically sound and clearly presented.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements (reduced NDE and SAE) on the HES dataset versus AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Situates the work well in related BSS and energy disaggregation literature and emphasizes novelty in identifying useful constraint classes.  
- Paper is well-written, motivated, and addresses practical interpretability concerns in energy disaggregation.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair: AFHMM+SAC used 15–30 days of data while baselines appear to use less, potentially inflating gains.  
- Claims of consistent improvement are overstated: AFAMAP can outperform SAC-AFHMM when the constraint (µ0) is far from the true aggregate, so poorly chosen constraints can harm performance.  
- Limited exploration of other constraint types (e.g., time-of-day, exclusivity) and extensions to more complex constraints.  
- More detailed analysis of computational cost and sensitivity to parameters (e.g., δ in eq.4) is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses identifiability with a novel, practical approach, strong empirical and technical contributions, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: fairness of experimental setup and overstated generality of benefits weaken the claims.  
- Overall: strong paper with promising results but requires clarifications and additional analyses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — clarify experiments, temper claims, discuss extensions and robustness, and analyze sensitivity to δ.
