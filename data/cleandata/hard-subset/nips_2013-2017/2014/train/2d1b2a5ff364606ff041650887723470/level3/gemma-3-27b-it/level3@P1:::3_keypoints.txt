- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within an AFHMM to incorporate expected total signal values per source for blind source separation/energy disaggregation.  
- Derives a convex quadratic program for approximate MAP inference, enabling efficient computation.  
- Demonstrates significant reductions in Normalized Disaggregation Error (NDE) and Signal Aggregate Error (SAE) over AFHMM and AFAMAP on a real-world dataset.  
- Strong motivation: addresses identifiability in BSS by integrating domain knowledge (aggregate constraints).  
- Builds on prior constraint-based approaches (posterior regularization, generalized expectation) and leverages the established AFHMM framework.  
- Technical development is sound; derivation of the QP is a notable contribution.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and makes mathematical details accessible.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline directions for future work; connection to posterior regularization clarified.  
- Potentially unfair baseline comparison: SAC-AFHMM was trained on a longer time series than AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Need for controlled comparisons using identical training data and evaluation of other methods that could incorporate SACs.  
- Figure 1 shows SAC can degrade performance when constraint values are poorly specified; AFAMAP can outperform SAC in that regime.  
- Claim that SAC always improves results is misleading; robustness to mis-specified constraints requires deeper discussion.  
- Current work uses a single global constraint per component; more complex constraints (time-varying, exclusivity, cardinality, duration) are unexplored.  
- Generalization to other BSS models and broader constraint classes is not fully explored and would increase impact.  
- Overall assessment: novel, theoretically solid, and empirically promising; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing baseline fairness and clarifying conditions for SAC effectiveness.
