This paper proposes an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model with Signal Aggregate Constraints (AFHMM+SAC) for blind source separation, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors introduce Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into the problem, encouraging the total signal for each unknown source to be close to a specified value.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain the problem setting, models, and optimization techniques used. The use of SACs is a novel approach to addressing the identifiability problem in blind source separation.

The paper presents strong simulation results, outperforming the original AFHMM and AFAMAP on a toy dataset and real domestic energy data. The optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. The authors also provide a theoretical presentation of the convex quadratic program for approximate inference, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed. The toy experiment has a very low noise variance, which may not be realistic. The authors should clarify whether this is a realistic assumption or if it was chosen to make the separation easier. Additionally, a discussion on the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) for mains vs aggregate appliance signals would be beneficial.

Furthermore, while the theoretical presentation is good, a formal proof that the procedure in equation (6) is convex is needed. The authors should also provide a theoretical scaling/complexity analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and a tractability comparison to AFAMAP for larger datasets.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the incorporation of SACs into an AFHMM. The work is significant and has the potential to impact the field of energy disaggregation and blind source separation.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper, pending minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and original work
* Strong simulation results
* Well-structured and clearly written paper
* Significant contribution to the field of energy disaggregation and blind source separation

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Minor issues with the toy experiment and noise variance
* Lack of formal proof for the convexity of the procedure in equation (6)
* Limited discussion on SNR for mains vs aggregate appliance signals
* No theoretical scaling/complexity analysis or tractability comparison to AFAMAP for larger datasets

**Recommendations for Revision:**

* Clarify the assumption on noise variance in the toy experiment
* Provide a formal proof for the convexity of the procedure in equation (6)
* Discuss SNR for mains vs aggregate appliance signals
* Include a theoretical scaling/complexity analysis and tractability comparison to AFAMAP for larger datasets
