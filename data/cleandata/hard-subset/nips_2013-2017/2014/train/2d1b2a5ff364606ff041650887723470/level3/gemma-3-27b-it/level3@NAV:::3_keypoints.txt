- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within AFHMMs to incorporate domain knowledge about total appliance energy for better identifiability.
- Proposes a convex quadratic relaxation for approximate inference in SAC-AFHMM.
- Shows improved Normalized Disaggregation Error (NDE) and Signal Aggregate Error (SAE) on synthetic and HES real-world data versus AFAMAP.
- Paper is well-written with solid mathematical foundations and clear connection to posterior regularization literature.
- Demonstrates tangible benefits of aggregate constraints for energy disaggregation and blind source separation.
- Experimental fairness concern: baselines (AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP) appear trained on less data than SAC-AFHMM, possibly inflating gains.
- Suggests evaluating whether other methods can similarly benefit from SACs to establish the proposed approach’s unique contribution.
- Figure 1 anomaly: AFAMAP outperforms SAC-AFHMM at distances >1e4, implying poorly specified constraints can worsen performance and contradict the “SACs always help” claim.
- Calls for a more nuanced discussion of limitations and investigation into when SACs harm performance.
- Recommends extending SACs to more complex constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration limits) in future work.
- Suggests generalizing the algorithm to handle broader classes of constraints to increase applicability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel domain-knowledge application, tractable convex formulation, empirical improvements, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential unfair experimental comparisons, evidence SACs aren’t universally beneficial, and limited exploration of alternative incorporation methods.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing experimental fairness and Figure 1 implications.
