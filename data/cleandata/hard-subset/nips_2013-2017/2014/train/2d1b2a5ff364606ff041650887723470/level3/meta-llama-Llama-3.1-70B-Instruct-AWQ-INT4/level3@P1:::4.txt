This paper introduces a novel extension to the additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) by incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve the performance of blind source separation (BSS) problems. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on a real-world energy disaggregation problem, achieving significant improvements over the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The main idea of the paper is to incorporate domain knowledge into the BSS problem by adding constraints that encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. This is based on the observation that the total signal often varies widely across the unknown sources, and we often have a good idea of what total values to expect. The authors show that this approach can be effectively incorporated into an AFHMM, resulting in a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.
