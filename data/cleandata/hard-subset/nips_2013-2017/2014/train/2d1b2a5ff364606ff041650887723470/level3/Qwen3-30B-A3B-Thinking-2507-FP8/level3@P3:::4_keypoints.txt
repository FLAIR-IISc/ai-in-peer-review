- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, constraining each source's total signal to a known value.  
- Formulates a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate inference in additive factorial HMMs (AFHMMs) incorporating SACs.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains: SACAFHMM reduces normalized disaggregation error by ~45% vs AFHMM and ~29% vs AFAMAP on real datasets.  
- Evaluated on two large-scale, realistic energy-disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency, 10 homes higher-frequency).  
- Computationally efficient: inference times comparable to baseline methods despite additional constraints.  
- Builds on posterior regularization literature and extends ideas from AFAMAP (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012).  
- SACs are a novel constraint type focused on aggregate signal values, distinct from temporal/state constraints used previously.  
- Empirical results are statistically validated (paired t-tests) and the theoretical connection to posterior regularization is sound.  
- Weakness: paper often conflates AFAMAP (inference algorithm) with the AFHMM model; needs clearer model vs inference distinctions.  
- Critical open question: does SACAFHMM produce more integral (integer) solutions than AFAMAP’s "one chain state at a time" constraint?  
- Unclear whether performance gains stem from better approximate solutions or from the SAC constraint itself; needs clarification.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss and experiment with integrating SACs into AFAMAP as a promising orthogonal combination.  
- Paper is practically significant and broadly applicable beyond energy disaggregation, but needs minor clarifications on solution quality and methodological distinctions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, well-motivated contribution with convincing results, pending clarifications on integrality and model/inference attribution.
