- Contribution: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) on AFHMMs for energy disaggregation, relaxing MAP to a convex quadratic program using auxiliary variables and box relaxation.
- Interpretation: Shows the objective can be derived via posterior regularization or as an implicit prior over state sequences penalizing aggregate deviations.
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC yields substantial NDE and SAE improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP on toy and HES low-frequency datasets with comparable runtime.
- Relation to prior work: Novel focus on long-horizon global aggregate constraints distinct from prior FHMM constraints (local transitions, durations).
- Technical soundness: Relaxation is carefully formulated; concave quadratic objective under linear constraints and correct posterior-regularization derivation.
- Empirical support quality: Uses appropriate metrics (NDE, SAE) and oracle constraints as upper bounds; results are consistent and realistic.
- Missing methodological details: Unclear how relaxed (fractional) Sit are converted to discrete states or power traces and the observed relaxation gap.
- Hyperparameter treatment: λi fixed to 1; lacks sensitivity analysis or cross-validation guidance.
- Scalability concerns: CVX used for proofs-of-concept; needs bespoke solvers (e.g., ADMM, block-coordinate) and complexity/runtime scaling with T and ∑i Ki.
- Clarity issues: Heavy notation and many acronyms hinder readability; recommendation to simplify naming and add a notation table and schematic.
- Presentation suggestions: Move dense vectorization to appendix, state aggregate constraint as prior/regularizer early, and add an algorithm box.
- Originality: Novel embedding of global aggregate constraints into FHMMs via tractable convex relaxation; connection to posterior regularization is appropriate.
- Significance: Meaningful performance gains for low-frequency NILM and potential applicability to other BSS domains; practical impact depends on how µ0 is obtained.
- Practical robustness: Need discussion/experiments on adapting or learning µ0 per household and robustness to biased µ0.
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, broadly applicable idea; principled convex relaxation; strong empirical gains on realistic data.
- Cons against acceptance: Poor readability, limited analysis of relaxation tightness and mapping to discrete solutions, sparse guidance on λ and µ0, and narrow evaluation breadth.
- Key questions for authors: How are fractional Sit post-processed? How sensitive are results to λi and mis-specified µ0? Can µ0 be learned jointly? What problem sizes were solved without CVX timing out?
- Suggested improvements: Clarify prior vs posterior role, reduce acronyms, add notation crib sheet and algorithm summary, analyze relaxation tightness and λ/µ0 sensitivity, and compare to duration/semi-Markov baselines.
- Overall recommendation: Lean weak accept pending improved presentation and additional analysis of relaxation, hyperparameters, and scalability.
