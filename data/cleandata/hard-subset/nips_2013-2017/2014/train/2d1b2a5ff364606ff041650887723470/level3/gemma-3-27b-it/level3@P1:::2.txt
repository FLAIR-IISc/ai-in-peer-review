This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) within an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to improve blind source separation, specifically in the context of energy disaggregation. The core idea is to incorporate prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of different appliances, addressing the inherent identifiability problem in BSS. The authors formulate this as a convex quadratic program for approximate inference and demonstrate significant performance gains on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset, surpassing both the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The work builds upon a substantial body of research in blind source separation and energy disaggregation [8, 12, 15, 22, 24]. The AFHMM itself is a well-established model in this field [17]. The novelty lies in the introduction of SACs as a means of incorporating domain-specific knowledge to address the identifiability issues inherent in BSS. While previous works have explored prior constraints [4, 13, 14], this paper specifically focuses on the utility of aggregate constraints, a potentially valuable addition to the toolkit of available constraints. The authors correctly identify the challenge of identifiability and attempt to address it through informed priors.  Related works constraining the states of the HMMs such as [10, 11, 12, 21] are well cited.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of signal aggregate constraints is a valuable contribution to the field of BSS, offering a principled way to incorporate domain knowledge.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results demonstrate a significant improvement in disaggregation accuracy compared to both the baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method [12]. The 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error is substantial.
* **Theoretical Formulation:** The formulation of the problem as a convex quadratic program provides a computationally efficient approach to approximate inference.
* **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of identifiability in BSS and the need for incorporating domain knowledge.
* **Connection to Posterior Regularization:**  The authors establish a clear connection between their approach and posterior regularization, adding theoretical depth.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Readability:** The manuscript suffers significantly from poor readability. The excessive use of acronyms (SAC, AFHMM, AFAMAP, NDE, SAE, etc.) becomes overwhelming, especially considering some are quite long. While acronyms are common in this field, their density hinders comprehension.  Several acronyms appear without initial definition.
* **Technical Writing:** The mathematical notation, while correct, could be presented more clearly. The flow between equations and the accompanying text sometimes feels disjointed.
* **Implicit Prior:** The paper treats the constraint as being implemented on the posterior distribution, but does not explicitly state that the regularizer is the logarithm of the prior distribution. It would improve the paper to be more explicit that the SACs effectively represent an a priori constraint on the hidden states.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** While the paper briefly mentions tuning the complexity parameters λi, the details of how this was done (e.g., cross-validation) are lacking.  A more detailed discussion of hyperparameter selection would strengthen the results.
* **Generalizability:** The results are primarily demonstrated on the energy disaggregation domain. While this is a compelling application, it would be beneficial to discuss the potential applicability of SACs to other BSS problems.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to addressing a core challenge in blind source separation.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a clear improvement over existing methods.
* The theoretical formulation and connection to posterior regularization provide a solid foundation for the work.
* The problem is significant and has real-world implications, particularly in the context of energy conservation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The poor readability of the manuscript significantly hinders comprehension and makes it difficult to fully appreciate the technical contributions.
* The implicit treatment of the prior and lack of detail regarding hyperparameter tuning weaken the paper's rigor.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation. The introduction of signal aggregate constraints is a clever and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge and improve disaggregation accuracy. However, the poor readability of the manuscript is a significant drawback.  If the authors can address the clarity issues, particularly by reducing the reliance on acronyms and improving the flow of the technical writing, this paper would be a strong candidate for acceptance.  

**Recommendation:**

Conditional acceptance pending major revisions to improve clarity and readability.  Specifically, a substantial rewrite to reduce acronym use, clarify the mathematical notation, and explicitly state the prior regularization framework would be necessary. I recommend the authors consider providing a glossary of acronyms.
