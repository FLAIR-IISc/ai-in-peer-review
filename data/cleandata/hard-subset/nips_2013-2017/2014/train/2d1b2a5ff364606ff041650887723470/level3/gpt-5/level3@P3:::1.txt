Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes AFHMM+SAC, an additive factorial HMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) that encourage each source’s total energy over a period to match a target value. The idea is well-motivated by energy disaggregation (NILM), where appliance totals differ substantially and rough prior totals are often available (e.g., national statistics). The authors derive a relaxed MAP inference objective and show it can be written as a convex quadratic program (CQP) by introducing transition-indicator variables and exploiting the PSD structure of the quadratic terms. They also connect their formulation to posterior regularization (Ganchev et al., 2010), showing the penalized posterior arises from constraining expectations of aggregate functions. Experiments on a toy dataset and the UK HES domestic energy data compare AFHMM+SAC against AFHMM and AFAMAP (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012). AFHMM+SAC produces lower normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE), with improvements significant by paired t-tests, while maintaining comparable runtime to AFAMAP.

Relative to prior NILM methods that impose local temporal constraints (e.g., AFAMAP’s single-chain change constraint, semi-Markov duration models), the proposed SACs encode global, cross-time information about each chain’s aggregate mass—an intuitively powerful and general type of domain knowledge that complements existing constraints. The link to posterior regularization and to aggregate constraints in databases is appropriate and helps situate the work within general frameworks for constrained inference.

Strengths
- A clear, broadly applicable idea (aggregate constraints) that addresses identifiability in BSS and NILM.
- Sound optimization formulation: the relaxed problem is solvable efficiently as a CQP; derivation tied to posterior regularization adds conceptual clarity.
- Strong empirical results on realistic, low-frequency HES data: consistent gains over AFHMM and AFAMAP; statistical significance demonstrated; runtime comparable to AFAMAP.
- The method is robust to imperfect aggregate targets (toy study varying µ0).
- Practical relevance: easily incorporates priors available from surveys or domain experts; extensible to multiple time-window constraints.

Weaknesses and requests for clarification
- Convexity claim: The paper states that the relaxed objective leads to a CQP (Eq. 6), relying on PSD matrices Λi and Vt. Please provide a formal proof sketch: (i) show that after relaxing Sitk, hitjk ∈ [0,1] the objective is concave in (S,H) when maximizing (equivalently, convex when minimizing −L); (ii) argue linearity of all constraints and equivalence between the augmented-variable formulation and the original MAP objective. This will remove any ambiguity about convexity/concavity and disciplined convex programming compliance.
- Rounding/integer recovery: How are fractional S and H mapped back to discrete states for evaluation? Describe the rounding/decoding procedure and its impact on objective value and accuracy.
- Complexity/scaling: Provide a theoretical complexity analysis in terms of T and total states K = ∑i Ki (and number of constraints J if time-window SACs are used). Include memory requirements and discuss tractability versus AFAMAP as I, K, and T grow. A per-iteration complexity for the solver or a custom optimization routine would be helpful.
- Toy experiment noise: The toy data use σ2 = 0.01, which seems very low. Is this meant to mimic realistic NILM noise or to isolate the effect of SACs? Please discuss realism and provide SNR values. More generally, for the mains experiments with unmodeled appliances, report observed SNRs (mains vs modeled aggregate) or residual variance to contextualize difficulty.
- Sensitivity to µ0 and λ: You fix λi = 1 throughout. Add sensitivity plots on real data varying λ and perturbing µ0 (e.g., using biased NTC) to quantify robustness and guide practitioners.
- Missing/unmodeled loads: In “with mains” experiments, some loads are absent from the model. Clarify how SACs behave when the modeled totals cannot match the mains because of missing appliances; discuss whether SACs should be softened or reweighted in such cases.
- Minor clarity: Make explicit that maximizing L(S,H) is equivalent to minimizing a convex quadratic (−L) under linear constraints; note solver tolerances and stopping criteria.
- Notation/typos: There appear to be extra commas in subscripts before p on p.4 (line ~202), e.g., around p(i)jk. Please fix. Also ensure consistent indexing and dimensions for H it (rows/columns) and define all Kronecker constructs once.

Quality
Technically solid overall, with a rigorous link to posterior regularization and an efficient relaxation. The main gap is the absence of a formal convexity proof for the relaxed program and of a clear rounding strategy. Empirical evaluation is careful, with appropriate baselines, statistical tests, and realistic data.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized. The optimization derivation is dense; the requested convexity proof and a brief decoding description would significantly improve readability. A small notation cleanup is needed.

Originality
Introducing SACs as global aggregate priors within AFHMMs is novel and distinct from prior local-transition constraints and duration modeling. The posterior-regularization lens is a nice unification.

Significance
Likely to be useful for NILM practitioners and potentially other BSS domains where aggregate priors are available. Demonstrated gains on challenging, low-frequency data are meaningful.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, general idea with clear practical value
- Solid optimization and conceptual grounding
- Strong, statistically significant empirical improvements
- Comparable runtime to a widely used baseline (AFAMAP)

Cons against acceptance
- Missing formal convexity proof and rounding details
- Limited analysis of sensitivity to µ0 and λ on real data
- Toy experiment noise realism not discussed; no SNR reporting
- Scaling analysis could be more thorough

Questions for the authors
- Please add a formal convexity/concavity proof for Eq. (6) after relaxation, and detail the rounding method.
- Can you report SNRs for toy and mains data, and comment on σ2 choices?
- How does performance vary with λ and with biased µ0 on real data?
- What is the per-iteration complexity and memory footprint versus AFAMAP as T and K scale?
- Any issues when some appliances are unmodeled? Would adaptive λi or slack variables help?
