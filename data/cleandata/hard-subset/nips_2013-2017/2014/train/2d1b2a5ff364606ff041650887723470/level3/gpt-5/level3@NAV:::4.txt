This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and instantiates them in an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM), yielding a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate MAP inference. The core idea is to leverage prior knowledge of each source’s total energy over a window, encouraging per-chain aggregates to match specified targets. The method is related to posterior regularization (Ganchev et al., 2010) and complements previous constraints for disaggregation such as AFAMAP’s “at most one chain changes at a time” heuristic (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012), k-segment constraints, and semi-Markov duration priors. Empirically, the approach is evaluated on large-scale energy disaggregation: 100 UK homes with low-frequency data and a second set of 10 homes with higher frequency but without per-appliance monitoring, demonstrating consistent gains over AFHMM and improvements over AFAMAP.

Quality: The optimization is technically sound and the CQP is well-motivated. The posterior-regularization view is appropriate. Results show sizable error reductions at realistic scale. However, the paper should clarify whether the relaxed CQP tends to produce integral solutions, how fractional solutions are post-processed (projection/rounding/DP refinement), and whether lower disaggregation error can be achieved despite non-integrality. Please also state clearly whether “AFHMM” baselines are alternating/chainwise Viterbi.

Clarity: Generally well written with a simple, transparent objective. There is occasional conflation of models and inference procedures (e.g., AFAMAP vs. AFHMM); please be precise that AFAMAP is an approximate MAP algorithm for AFHMM. Better label the baselines and document λ selection, the provenance of µ0 in practice, and sensitivity analyses.

Originality: The SAC concept is a straightforward but novel and useful constraint class for BSS; combining it with a convex relaxation for AFHMM is a neat applied/algorithmic contribution, orthogonal to AFAMAP-style dynamics constraints.

Significance: Strong empirical impact on sizable, realistic datasets. Likely to be adopted by practitioners; the constraint is generic and could apply beyond energy.

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple idea with convincing real-world gains at scale
- Clear optimization and posterior-regularization grounding
- Orthogonal to AFAMAP; practical and general

Cons / requests:
- Clarify integrality, rounding, and relation to AFAMAP’s integral solutions
- Avoid model/algorithm conflation; specify AFHMM inference used
- Analyze sensitivity to mis-specified µ0 and choice of λ
- Runtime/scalability beyond CVX; solver independence

Promising experiment for final version: combine SAC constraints with AFAMAP’s one-change-per-time constraint and report whether the hybrid improves further.
