- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: extends additive factorial HMM by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs encourage each source’s total signal to match a specified value, incorporated into the AFHMM framework.  
- Optimization formulated and solved via convex quadratic programming; technical contributions are sound.  
- Empirical results show AFHMM+SAC outperforms AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP on real-world datasets.  
- Significant improvements reported in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and technically well-organized.  
- Related work is thorough and differences from existing methods are clearly discussed.  
- Originality: novel combination of known techniques (SACs + AFHMM) applied to a real-world problem.  
- Significance: potential to improve blind source separation and energy management systems.  
- Recommends acceptance based on technical soundness and empirical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: assumes total signal for each source is known a priori, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of other potential limitations of the SACs approach.
