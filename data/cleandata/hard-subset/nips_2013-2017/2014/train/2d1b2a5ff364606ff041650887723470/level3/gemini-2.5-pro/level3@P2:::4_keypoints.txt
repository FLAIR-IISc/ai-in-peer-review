- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate prior knowledge of total aggregate values of hidden sources into BSS/energy disaggregation.
- Integrates SACs into an AFHMM and formulates MAP inference as a penalized optimization solved via a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation.
- Evaluated on a large UK HES dataset, showing 29–45% error reduction over AFHMM baseline and state-of-the-art AFAMAP.
- SACs offer a novel, complementary form of regularization compared to transition/duration constraints (e.g., AFAMAP, semi-Markov models).
- Significance: simple, intuitive approach that addresses identifiability and is broadly applicable beyond energy disaggregation.
- Quality: strong empirical validation on a challenging, large-scale real-world dataset.
- Originality: novel application of SACs and a practical CQP relaxation within the AFHMM framework.
- Weakness: unclear whether the CQP relaxation yields more integral (discrete) solutions—needs analysis of the integrality gap and solution discreteness.
- Weakness: should consider or discuss combining SACs with AFAMAP-style local transition constraints to assess potential complementary gains.
- Weakness: terminology needs precision distinguishing models from inference algorithms (e.g., AFHMM model vs AFAMAP inference).
- Overall: well-executed, convincing contribution with recommendation for acceptance pending the suggested clarifications/experiments.
