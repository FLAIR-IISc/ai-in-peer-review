- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge into blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) formulation.  
- Derives a convex quadratic program for approximate inference that is computationally efficient.  
- Real-world dataset results: SAC-enhanced AFHMM significantly improves baseline AFHMM and outperforms a recent state-of-the-art method.  
- Paper is well-structured with a formal technical writing style.  
- Readability suffers from excessive and unusually long acronyms, making the manuscript hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Potential methodological issue: SAC appears to be applied to the prior rather than directly to the posterior — needs explicit clarification.  
- Suggests explicitly stating that SAC is an a priori constraint which then constrains the posterior.  
- Recommends a more detailed discussion of the relationship between prior and posterior distributions.  
- Originality rated high: novel and reasonable approach for many applications.  
- Significance rated high: promising potential to improve energy disaggregation accuracy and practical impact.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel domain-knowledge incorporation, reasonable assumption, clear comparisons to existing work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy acronym use harming readability, and need to clarify/apply constraint on posterior and discuss prior–posterior relationship.
