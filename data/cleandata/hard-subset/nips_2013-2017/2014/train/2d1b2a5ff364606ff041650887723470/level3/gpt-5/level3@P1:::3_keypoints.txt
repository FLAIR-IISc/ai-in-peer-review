- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and integrates them into AFHMM, producing AFHMM+SAC to enforce total-usage targets per source.
- Encodes non-Markovian aggregate knowledge by encouraging each chain’s aggregate to match provided targets (e.g., daily totals).
- Derives a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation for MAP inference via auxiliary transition variables and binary-relaxation.
- Shows equivalence between the CQP penalty approach and posterior regularization, clarifying the probabilistic interpretation.
- Empirically improves normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) on toy data and HES dataset, with large gains when constraints are accurate.
- Builds on factorial HMMs, AFAMAP, semi-Markov NILM models, change-at-one-chain constraints, and general constraint frameworks; SACs target global aggregates unlike prior transition constraints.
- The convex relaxation adapts prior relaxed formulations but is tailored to aggregate penalties.
- Technical soundness is convincing: quadratic terms are PSD and the relaxation yields a convex program; link to posterior regularization is correct.
- Missing methodological detail: how relaxed continuous solutions are rounded back to valid discrete sequences while preserving transition consistency.
- Empirical comparisons may be unfair because AFHMM+SAC uses extra side information (NTC/oracle TTC) that baselines do not receive.
- Suggests stronger baselines that also receive aggregate information (e.g., posterior-regularized AFAMAP or AFHMM with aggregate priors).
- Recommends reporting performance versus constraint misspecification and including confidence intervals to assess robustness.
- Robustness concern: performance degrades as constraint targets become inaccurate; AFAMAP can outperform AFHMM+SAC when targets are substantially wrong.
- Claims that SACs "dramatically improve" should be tempered and conditions where they help or harm clearly articulated.
- Scalability concern: solving CQP with CVX may be slow for many chains/time steps; suggest complexity analysis and consideration of specialized solvers (e.g., ADMM).
- Clarity issues: notation around μi vs μi0 should be clarified, Figure 1 axes/baselines need explanation, and the rounding step must be described.
- Originality: aggregate constraints are a useful, distinct prior for BSS and the CQP relaxation is a careful adaptation of known tools.
- Significance: practically impactful for NILM and potentially other domains, but significance depends on robustness and fair comparisons.
- Pros: simple, well-motivated constraint; solid math and posterior-regularization link; consistent empirical gains; broadly applicable.
- Cons: potentially unfair comparisons; sensitivity to misspecified constraints; missing rounding details; limited exploration of richer constraint classes.
- Suggested improvements: add fair baselines using aggregate info, sweep μ0 errors and report breakpoints, include confidence intervals, evaluate more constraint types, detail rounding, and discuss/offer scalable solvers.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — clear useful idea with solid theory and promising results but needs fairness and robustness analyses to strengthen impact.
