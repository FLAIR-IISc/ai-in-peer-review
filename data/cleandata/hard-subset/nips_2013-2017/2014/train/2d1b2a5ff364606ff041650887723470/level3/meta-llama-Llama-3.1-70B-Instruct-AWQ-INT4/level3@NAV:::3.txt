This paper proposes an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) for blind source separation problems, specifically energy disaggregation. The authors introduce SACs to incorporate domain knowledge into the problem, which encourages the total signal for each unknown source to be close to a specified value. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on synthetic and real-world data, achieving significant improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) compared to existing methods.

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound, with a solid mathematical foundation and good problem motivation. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach, including a detailed derivation of the convex quadratic program for approximate inference. The experimental results are thorough and well-evaluated, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and its components. The authors provide sufficient background information and context, making the paper accessible to readers familiar with hidden Markov models and blind source separation.

**Originality**: The paper proposes a novel combination of familiar techniques, incorporating SACs into an AFHMM for blind source separation. While the individual components are not new, the authors' contribution lies in the way they integrate these components to address the identifiability problem in blind source separation.

**Significance**: The results presented in the paper are significant, demonstrating a substantial improvement in performance compared to existing methods. The authors' approach has the potential to impact various applications, including energy disaggregation, audio processing, and computational biology.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**:

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to blind source separation, incorporating domain knowledge through SACs.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach on synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating significant improvements in performance.

Weaknesses:

* The comparison to existing methods may be unfair, as the baselines use less data than the proposed method.
* The authors could consider evaluating other methods that could incorporate SACs, even if they are less computationally efficient.
* Figure 1 is unclear, and the claim that SAC always helps may be misleading.

**Recommendations for Future Work**:

* Extend the approach to more complex constraint types, such as mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, and duration limits.
* Generalize the algorithm to handle broader classes of constraints.
* Consider applying the proposed approach to other applications, such as audio processing and computational biology.

**Acceptance**: Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I recommend accepting it for publication. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of blind source separation, and the authors' approach has the potential to impact various applications. With some minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, the paper is ready for publication.
