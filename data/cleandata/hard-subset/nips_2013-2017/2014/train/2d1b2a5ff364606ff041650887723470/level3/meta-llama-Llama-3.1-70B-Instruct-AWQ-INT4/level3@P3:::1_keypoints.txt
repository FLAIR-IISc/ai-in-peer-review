- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: an additive factorial HMM with signal aggregate constraints to incorporate domain knowledge for blind source separation (energy disaggregation).
- Introduces SACs to encourage each unknown source’s total signal to be close to known/expected values, leveraging prior knowledge.
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement, motivation, and related work overview.
- AFHMM+SAC is a natural extension of AFHMM with a clear derivation of the model and parameters.
- SACs are a novel contribution and empirically improve AFHMM performance on simulated and real-world data.
- Experimental results show AFHMM+SAC outperforms AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP on NDE and SAE metrics.
- Good discussion and comparison to related methods, with strengths and weaknesses articulated.
- Lacks a formal proof that the optimization in equation (6) is convex despite deriving a convex quadratic program.
- Toy experiment uses very low noise variance; realism and robustness to higher/noise SNR levels are unclear.
- Needs more discussion of mains vs. aggregate appliance signal SNR and its impact on performance.
- Computational complexity analysis is insufficient; lacks detailed scaling analysis with hidden states and data length.
- Minor notation issue noted that should be fixed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution, well-motivated approach, strong empirical results, clear presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: missing convexity proof, unrealistic toy experiment/noise assumptions, insufficient complexity analysis.
- Recommended improvements: provide convexity proof, clarify toy experiment and SNR assumptions, add detailed computational complexity analysis, and fix notation.
