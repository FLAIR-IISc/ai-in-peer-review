- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) within an AFHMM to improve blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- Uses prior knowledge of total appliance energy to address identifiability in BSS.  
- Frames inference as a convex quadratic program for efficient approximate inference.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on a real dataset, outperforming AFHMM and a recent SOTA (≈45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error).  
- Builds on substantial prior work in BSS and energy disaggregation; AFHMM is a well-established base model.  
- Novelty lies in explicitly using aggregate constraints as domain-specific priors to improve identifiability.  
- Establishes a clear connection to posterior regularization, providing theoretical grounding.  
- Strength: clear motivation for incorporating domain knowledge to solve identifiability.  
- Strength: computationally tractable formulation via convex optimization.  
- Weakness: manuscript readability suffers from excessive and sometimes undefined acronyms.  
- Weakness: mathematical notation and exposition could be clearer; equation-text flow is disjointed.  
- Weakness: paper treats constraint on the posterior implicitly and doesn’t explicitly state the regularizer is the log-prior.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning (λi selection and validation procedure).  
- Weakness: empirical validation is confined to energy disaggregation; generalizability to other BSS tasks is not well discussed.  
- For acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical and theoretical support and real-world relevance.  
- Against acceptance: clarity/readability issues and insufficient explicitness on prior interpretation and hyperparameter methodology.  
- Overall: valuable contribution but requires major revisions to improve clarity, reduce acronyms, clarify math, and state the prior/regularization explicitly.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending major revisions (rewrite for clarity, explicit prior framing, better hyperparameter discussion, consider an acronym glossary).
