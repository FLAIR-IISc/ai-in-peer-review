- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation with AFHMMs.
- Formulates the constrained inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference.
- Connects SACs and the CQP formulation to posterior regularization.
- Novel constraint type well-motivated by known total appliance energy consumption.
- Clear and well-executed theoretical derivation of the CQP (Section 5).
- Empirical evaluation shows robustness to constraint errors on a toy dataset (Figure 1).
- Real-world results: 45% NDE reduction vs baseline AFHMM and 29% NDE improvement vs AFAMAP (p<0.05).
- Computational efficiency comparable to AFAMAP, making the method practical.
- Toy experiment uses unrealistically low noise variance (σ²=0.01), creating very high SNR and needs clarification and SNR discussion for real data.
- Claims of convexity for equation (6) lack a formal proof and should be provided.
- Complexity claim ("polynomial time...") lacks explicit theoretical scaling analysis and comparison to AFAMAP for larger datasets.
- Minor notation issue: extra comma before p in "p(i)jk" on page 4 (line 202).
- Overall high quality and originality; significant practical impact for energy disaggregation and recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
