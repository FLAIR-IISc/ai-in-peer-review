1. Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to address identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
2. SACs constrain each source’s total signal to be near known values (e.g., expected daily appliance energy), incorporating domain knowledge.  
3. Formulates inference as a convex quadratic program for tractable optimization.  
4. Empirical gains: 45% lower normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement vs a recent state-of-the-art on real-world data.  
5. Theoretically connects SACs to posterior regularization, providing a sound approach to the identifiability problem.  
6. Demonstrates strong empirical performance across multiple energy disaggregation scenarios.  
7. Extends prior constraint methods (e.g., AFAMAP) by leveraging aggregate signal knowledge in a more domain-aware way.  
8. Major readability issues: excessive, often unexplained acronyms that make the paper hard to follow.  
9. Ambiguity in presentation: constraint is placed on the prior rather than directly on the posterior, and this a priori vs posterior effect is not clearly clarified (Section 4.2).  
10. Overall assessment: strong technical quality, significance, and originality for energy disaggregation.  
11. Recommendation: accept, conditional on substantial clarity improvements and fixing the constraint explanation before publication.
