- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and integrates them into an AFHMM to improve identifiability.  
- Proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate inference that is computationally efficient.  
- Paper is well-written with a solid mathematical foundation and clear problem motivation.  
- SACs are novel: they encourage each unknown source’s total signal to match a specified value, incorporating domain knowledge.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset with significant improvement over AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- AFHMM+SAC outperforms baselines on normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Method shows robustness to errors in the specified constraint values.  
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair because the baselines use less data than AFHMM+SAC.  
- Authors should evaluate other methods that could incorporate SACs, even if less computationally efficient.  
- Figure 1 is unclear and needs more detail/explanation.  
- Figure 1 suggests AFAMAP may outperform AFHMM+SAC at large distances, implying bad constraints can worsen performance and contradicting claims that SACs always help.  
- Recommend extending the algorithm to handle more complex constraint types (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration limits).  
- Overall assessment: significant and novel contribution with promising results, but comparisons and plot clarity need improvement.  
- Quality: technically sound and claims are well-supported.  
- Clarity: clear exposition and sufficient algorithm/experimental details.  
- Originality: SACs represent a new and meaningful way to inject domain knowledge.  
- Significance: addresses a challenging problem and offers substantial contributions.
