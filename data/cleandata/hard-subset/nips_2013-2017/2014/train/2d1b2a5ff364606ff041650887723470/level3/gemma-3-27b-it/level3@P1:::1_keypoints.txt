- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into AFHMMs to improve blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- Incorporates domain knowledge of expected total appliance energy to address identifiability in BSS.  
- Formulates approximate inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient optimization.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical gains on both a toy dataset and a real-world energy disaggregation dataset.  
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and recent AFAMAP baseline, with improvements validated by paired t-tests.  
- Situates work well within prior BSS and energy-disaggregation literature and connects to posterior regularization.  
- Novelty lies in the specific SAC constraint and its effective integration via convex relaxation.  
- Mathematical formulation and derivation appear technically sound and well-presented.  
- Computational cost reported comparable to AFAMAP, suggesting practical applicability.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and organized.  
- Lacks a formal proof of convexity for the stated optimization problem.  
- Missing theoretical complexity analysis and scalability comparison to AFAMAP for larger datasets.  
- Toy dataset uses very low noise variance; SNR representativeness and realism need clarification.  
- Minor notation issue (extra commas in subscripts) noted on page 4.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem addressed, sound theory, convincing empirical results, real-world impact potential, responsive authors.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing convexity proof and scalability analysis; reviewer notes limited domain expertise but still favors contribution.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.
