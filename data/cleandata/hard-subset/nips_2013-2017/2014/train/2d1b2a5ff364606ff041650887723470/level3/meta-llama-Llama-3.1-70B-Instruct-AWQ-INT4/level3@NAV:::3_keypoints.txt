- Proposes AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) to incorporate domain knowledge for blind source separation, especially energy disaggregation.  
- SAC encourages each unknown source’s total signal to be close to a specified aggregate value.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) on synthetic and real data.  
- Technically sound with solid mathematical foundation and detailed derivation of a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Well-organized presentation and clear explanations, accessible to readers familiar with HMMs and blind source separation.  
- Novelty lies in integrating SACs into AFHMMs to address identifiability, though components individually are familiar.  
- Results are significant and have potential impact on energy disaggregation, audio processing, and computational biology.  
- Strength: novel, effective approach that incorporates domain knowledge and is thoroughly evaluated.  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons may be unfair because baselines use less data than the proposed method.  
- Weakness: should evaluate other methods that could incorporate SACs, even if less computationally efficient.  
- Weakness: Figure 1 is unclear and the claim that SAC always helps may be misleading.  
- Recommended future work: extend to more complex constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration) and generalize the algorithm to broader constraint classes.  
- Recommended future work: explore applications beyond energy disaggregation, such as audio and computational biology.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
