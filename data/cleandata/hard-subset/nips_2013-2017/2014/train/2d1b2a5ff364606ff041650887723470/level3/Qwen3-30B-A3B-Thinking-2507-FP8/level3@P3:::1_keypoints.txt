- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, using expected total signal values as domain knowledge.  
- Integrates SACs into Additive Factorial HMMs and reformulates inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Builds on posterior regularization and offers a novel, practically useful constraint class that is theoretically tractable.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements (45% and 29% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and AFAMAP).  
- Validated on synthetic and real-world Household Electricity Survey (HES) data, showing practical utility for energy disaggregation.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with good explanations of model, optimization, and experiments.  
- Toy experiment uses extremely low noise variance (σ² = 0.01), raising questions about realism and SNR justification.  
- Lacks a formal proof that the optimization in equation (6) is convex, despite claiming convexity.  
- Missing theoretical complexity/scaling analysis (data length, number of states) and tractability comparison to AFAMAP.  
- Minor notation error (extra comma in subscripts before p on page 4, line 202).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address convexity proof, experiment realism, and complexity analysis.  
- Pro: novel constraint mechanism, strong empirical results, convex optimization approach, clear presentation; Con: missing formal proof, unrealistic toy noise, absent complexity analysis, minor notation issue.
