- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and integrates them into AFHMMs via a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- SACs enforce that each source’s total signal is close to a known value, addressing identifiability in BSS.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on real energy-disaggregation data (≈45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM, ≈29% vs AFAMAP).  
- Builds on AFHMM/AFAMAP literature and relates to posterior regularization and generalized expectation, but focuses on aggregate-signal constraints.  
- Convex relaxation and theoretical connection to posterior regularization are notable strengths.  
- Paper lacks theoretical guarantees for the convex relaxation and should explicitly state the constraint is placed on the prior (a priori), not the posterior.  
- Readability is poor due to excessive and long acronyms (e.g., AFHMM+SAC, NTC, TTC, NDE, SAE) and dense mathematical notation.  
- Presentation needs more intuitive explanations and clearer differentiation of when SACs are appropriate versus other constraint types.  
- Novel and practically significant contribution with potential applicability beyond energy disaggregation.  
- Recommendation: promising and technically sound but needs substantial revision to improve clarity, reduce acronyms, and clarify probabilistic interpretation.
