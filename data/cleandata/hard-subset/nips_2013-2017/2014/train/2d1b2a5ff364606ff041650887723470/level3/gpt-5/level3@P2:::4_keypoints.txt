- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to enforce ∑t xit ≈ μi0 in AFHMMs via a quadratic penalty.  
- Introduces auxiliary transition variables and a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation over state and transition marginals, solvable with CVX.  
- Provides an alternative derivation linking the objective to posterior regularization.  
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC improves NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP on simulated and measured UK HES low-frequency data.  
- Reports statistical significance with paired t-tests and comparable runtimes to baselines.  
- Novelty: identifies total-signal constraints as practical, task-agnostic priors and extends aggregate constraints to temporal HMMs.  
- Method is well-motivated; CQP derivation and convexity arguments (PSD structure) are sound.  
- Key missing detail: how fractional marginals from the relaxation are converted to discrete state sequences (rounding/projection strategy).  
- Missing integrality reporting: need fraction of integral Sit/Hit, effect of SAC on integrality, and post-rounding constraint satisfaction.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: λi fixed to 1 in experiments; needs sensitivity analysis or cross-validation.  
- Comparisons incomplete: should evaluate SAC combined with AFAMAP and include semi-Markov/duration-model baselines.  
- Statistical analysis needs detail: pairing scheme, number of test days, normality assumptions, and effect sizes; report per-appliance SAE.  
- Clarity issues: conflate model vs inference (AFHMM vs AFAMAP); standardize terminology and state inference procedures used.  
- Complexity/runtime: provide explicit complexity in T, I, Ki, CVX solver settings, and practical scalability limits.  
- Robustness: show sensitivity to mis-specified μ0 on real HES data (e.g., ± perturbations).  
- Strength: simple, practical prior addressing identifiability with a principled convex relaxation and link to posterior regularization.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains on realistic low-frequency dataset, with statistical significance.  
- Strength: approach is generalizable (windowed SACs, time-of-day priors) and likely adoptable in BSS/NILM.  
- Weakness: underspecified rounding/integrality and lack of combined AFAMAP+SAC experiments.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of λ and μ0 robustness on real data; missing ablations.  
- Questions for authors: Do SACs increase integrality? How are solutions rounded and how well are SACs satisfied after rounding?  
- Questions: How are λi chosen in practice and how is μ0 estimated per household/day for NTC?  
- Suggestions: add AFAMAP+SAC baseline, report integrality/feasibility metrics, include μ0 robustness study, and give detailed runtime/scaling info.  
- Acceptance arguments: practical, novel, methodologically sound with strong real-data gains and broad applicability.  
- Rejection arguments: underspecified methodological details, incomplete baselines/ablations, and limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (7/10) contingent on clarifying rounding/integrality, sensitivity analyses, and expanded comparisons.
