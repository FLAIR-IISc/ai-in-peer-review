- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to add domain knowledge to AFHMMs and address identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs constrain the total signal from each unobserved source to be close to known values (e.g., expected daily appliance consumption).  
- MAP inference with SACs is formulated as a convex quadratic program (CQP), enabling efficient approximate inference.  
- Builds on prior AFHMM work (e.g., Kolter & Jaakkola) while proposing a novel, intuitive constraint formulation.  
- Experimental results show significant gains over a standard AFHMM and AFAMAP on a real-world dataset.  
- Includes an "oracle" (TTC) experiment that effectively demonstrates the method’s potential.  
- Technical approach (CQP relaxation for the non‑Markovian optimization) is sound and likely to be adopted by others.  
- Main weakness: poor clarity—manuscript is dense, hard to read, overuses long acronyms, and needs better organization to build intuition.  
- Conceptual framing should be clearer: SACs function as an a priori constraint on aggregate state statistics that thereby constrain the posterior.  
- Conclusion: novel, well-motivated, and empirically strong work, but severely hampered by readability; reviewer would raise score if clarity is substantially improved.
