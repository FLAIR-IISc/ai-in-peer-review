- Proposes extending additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with constraints forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate and uses a convex relaxation for MAP inference.
- Applies the method to energy disaggregation on large real-world datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).
- Approach is straightforward with a simple optimization formulation but is compelling due to real-world scale and combined applied/algorithmic contribution.
- Requests clarification on whether SACAFHMM’s formulation yields more integral (integer) solutions like AFAMAP’s “one chain state at a time” constraint, or whether it attains lower error despite non-integral solutions.
- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are fundamentally orthogonal and suggests combining SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP as a promising experiment for a final version.
- Points out occasional conflation of models and inference procedures (e.g., labeling AFAMAP vs AFHMM), asking for precision that AFAMAP is an approximate MAP algorithm for AFHMM and AFHMM results likely refer to alternating Viterbi.
- Suggests that combining the different algorithmic strategies might improve performance and should be explored.
- Overall assessment: simple extension and optimization but convincing empirical results; paper is reasonable and successful with suggested clarifications and experiments.
