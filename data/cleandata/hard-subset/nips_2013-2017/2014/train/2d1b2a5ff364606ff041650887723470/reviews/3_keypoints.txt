- Proposes AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation for blind source separation.  
- Demonstrates promising synthetic and real-world results with improvements in NDE and SAE.  
- Paper is well written and has a solid mathematical foundation and good problem motivation.  
- Comparison may be unfair: baselines (AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP) use less data than SACAFHMM.  
- Suggests evaluating other methods that could incorporate SAC even if they are less computationally efficient.  
- Figure 1 is unclear: AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM at distances >1e4, implying bad constraints can worsen performance and the claim that SAC always helps is misleading.  
- Requests extension to more complex constraint types (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration limits).  
- Recommends future work to generalize the algorithm to handle broader classes of constraints.
