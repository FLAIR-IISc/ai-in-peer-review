## Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of identifiability in blind source separation (BSS), specifically within the context of energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating “Signal Aggregate Constraints” (SACs) into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM).  SACs leverage prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of individual appliances.  They demonstrate a mathematically sound approach to incorporate these constraints into the AFHMM via a convex quadratic program, and show significant performance improvements on both simulated and real-world energy disaggregation datasets compared to baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method (AFAMAP).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature on energy disaggregation and BSS. It acknowledges the challenges of identifiability and builds upon previous work that attempts to incorporate prior knowledge into probabilistic models like AFHMMs. The authors acknowledge related work in posterior regularization and other constraint-based approaches, and appropriately highlight the novelty of focusing on *aggregate* constraints. They also correctly cite relevant work on AFHMM variations (AFAMAP, semi-Markov models) and related techniques (k-segment constraints).  The connection to posterior regularization is a strong point, demonstrating a deeper theoretical grounding for their approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a specific class of constraints for BSS, particularly leveraging readily available prior knowledge about total energy usage, is a valuable contribution.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the convex quadratic program for solving the relaxed optimization problem is rigorous and provides a computationally efficient solution.  The connection to posterior regularization further solidifies the theoretical foundation.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results, both on simulated data and the HES dataset, are compelling. The 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error compared to the standard AFHMM and the 29% improvement over AFAMAP are substantial.  The use of both synthetic and real data strengthens the validity of the findings.
* **Practical Relevance:** Energy disaggregation is a practically important problem, and this work provides a tangible improvement in disaggregation accuracy, potentially leading to more useful energy consumption insights for consumers.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations of the models and algorithms are relatively easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Sensitivity Analysis:** While the toy data experiments demonstrate the effect of errors in the constraint values (µ0), a more thorough sensitivity analysis on the real-world data would be beneficial. How robust are the gains observed when the assumed aggregate values are inaccurate?
* **Parameter Tuning:** The paper states λi was set to 1 for all experiments. While acknowledging the possibility of cross-validation, a discussion of how the choice of λi affects performance would be valuable.  A sensitivity analysis of this parameter would increase confidence in the results.
* **Computational Complexity Discussion:** The authors state the CQP scales polynomially, which is good. However, a more concrete discussion of the scalability of the method to significantly larger numbers of appliances and longer time series would be helpful. Is there a practical limit to the number of hidden states or appliances that can be handled?
* **Limited Comparison to Other State-of-the-Art Methods:** While the comparison to AFAMAP is relevant, a brief comparison to other contemporary disaggregation approaches (e.g., deep learning-based methods) might provide additional context.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*  The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem.
*  The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
*  The work has practical relevance and the potential to impact the field of energy disaggregation.
*  The authors clearly articulate the contribution and relate it to existing literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The sensitivity analysis is limited.
* The parameter tuning discussion is somewhat lacking.
* The scalability discussion could be more concrete.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of BSS and energy disaggregation. The proposed SACs offer a practical and effective way to incorporate prior knowledge into AFHMMs, and the resulting improvements in disaggregation accuracy are significant. While some areas could be strengthened (sensitivity analysis, parameter tuning discussion, scalability analysis), the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses.  I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.

**Rating:**  Strong Accept (8/10)
