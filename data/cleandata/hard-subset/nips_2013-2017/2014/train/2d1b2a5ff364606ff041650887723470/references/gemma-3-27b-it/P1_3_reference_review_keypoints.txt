- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for Blind Source Separation within Additive Factorial HMMs to use aggregate priors (e.g., daily appliance usage) for better identifiability.
- Casts inference with SACs as a convex quadratic program (CQP) to enable efficient computation with non‑Markovian constraints.
- Reports strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs standard AFHMM and 29% improvement vs a recent state‑of‑the‑art method on real energy data.
- Places the work in context of BSS and energy disaggregation literature and connects SACs to posterior regularization and related constrained HMM methods.
- Novelty: SACs represent a new, broadly applicable class of constraints leveraging aggregate signal properties.
- Practicality: CQP formulation addresses computational challenges and achieves comparable runtime to other AFHMM variants.
- Evaluation is thorough, including toy experiments and real‑world HES dataset with varying data availability (with/without mains readings).
- Paper is well‑written, clearly motivated, and uses consistent mathematical notation.
- Weakness: sensitivity to the accuracy of the specified aggregate constraint value (µ0), which may be hard to obtain in practice.
- Weakness: insufficient guidance and detailed analysis on parameter tuning (δ, λ) and reliance on unspecified cross‑validation procedures.
- Weakness: scalability concerns — the paper does not explicitly discuss CQP limitations as number of appliance chains and states increases.
- Weakness: limited exploration of generalized or time‑varying SAC variants despite mentioning them as possible extensions.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important identifiability problem with a novel, practical, theoretically sound method and strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to µ0, limited parameter‑tuning/scalability discussion, and limited exploration of SAC generalizations.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution; minor weaknesses do not outweigh strengths.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggested revisions to address parameter tuning guidance and explore robust/Bayesian estimation for µ0.
