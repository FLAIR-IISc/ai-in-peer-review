

# Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS) problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors address the identifiability problem in BSS by incorporating domain knowledge about expected aggregate signal values (e.g., total daily energy consumption of appliances). SACs constrain the sum of each source signal over time to be close to known values, which is particularly relevant for energy disaggregation where such values can be estimated from national surveys. The paper integrates SACs into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) framework, transforming the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program that can be efficiently solved. The authors demonstrate significant performance improvements over baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach (AFAMAP) on real-world energy disaggregation data.

The paper relates well to prior work in energy disaggregation (e.g., [8, 12, 22]) and probabilistic modeling (posterior regularization [4], generalized expectation criterion [14]). It distinguishes itself from previous constraint-based approaches that focused on state transitions (e.g., AFAMAP [12]) by introducing a novel constraint type on aggregate signal values, which is both domain-specific and computationally tractable.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach. The theoretical connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) provides strong justification for the proposed method. The convex quadratic programming formulation (Section 5) is well-justified and correctly derived, with proper handling of the non-Markovian nature of SACs. The experimental evaluation on both synthetic data (Section 7.1) and real-world energy data (Section 7.2) is thorough and well-designed. The use of both normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) provides comprehensive evaluation metrics. The results are statistically validated through paired t-tests. The paper acknowledges limitations (e.g., the need for accurate prior knowledge of aggregate values) and provides a clear comparison to relevant baselines.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The problem statement (Section 3), model definitions (Section 4), and technical contributions (Section 5) are presented with sufficient mathematical rigor. The connection between SACs and domain knowledge is clearly articulated through the energy disaggregation example. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility, including data sources (HES dataset), parameter settings, and evaluation metrics. The paper would benefit from additional visualizations of disaggregation results (e.g., actual appliance energy usage patterns) to help readers better understand the qualitative improvements.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear and significant contribution to the field. The concept of signal aggregate constraints is novel for BSS problems. While previous work has constrained state transitions in HMMs for energy disaggregation (e.g., AFAMAP [12]), SACs represent a fundamentally different constraint type based on aggregate signal values. The authors clearly articulate how their approach differs from prior work in Section 2 and provide a convincing theoretical justification. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) further strengthens the novelty by situating the work within a broader framework for incorporating domain knowledge.

**Significance:** The results demonstrate substantial practical significance. The AFHMM+SAC method achieves a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error compared to the baseline AFHMM and a 29% improvement over AFAMAP on real-world energy data. This represents a meaningful advancement in energy disaggregation, a problem with significant real-world applications for energy conservation. The approach is generalizable to other BSS domains where aggregate constraints are known (e.g., audio processing, computational biology), extending the potential impact of the work.

**Strengths:**
- Novel constraint type (SACs) that addresses a fundamental identifiability problem in BSS
- Theoretical connection to posterior regularization provides strong justification
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real-world data
- Significant performance improvements over baselines (45% NDE reduction vs. AFHMM)
- Clear presentation of technical contributions with proper mathematical formulation
- Practical relevance to energy disaggregation with clear domain knowledge integration

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of the sensitivity to hyperparameter λi (only used λi=1 in experiments)
- The paper does not fully explore the computational complexity trade-offs compared to other methods
- While the connection to domain knowledge is well-explained, the paper could more explicitly discuss how to obtain the constraint values µi0 in other domains beyond energy disaggregation
- The visualizations of disaggregation results would benefit from additional examples to illustrate the qualitative improvements

**Pro/Con for Acceptance:**
Pro: The paper presents a novel, theoretically sound, and practically significant approach to a well-known problem in BSS. The technical contribution is clear, the experiments are thorough, and the results demonstrate substantial improvements over existing methods. The work has potential for broad application beyond energy disaggregation.

Con: The paper does not fully explore the sensitivity of the method to constraint parameter values, and the computational complexity analysis is somewhat limited.

Overall, this paper makes a strong contribution to the field of probabilistic modeling for BSS. The introduction of signal aggregate constraints provides a new and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge into the learning process, addressing a critical identifiability problem. The technical approach is sound, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results demonstrate significant practical improvements. The work is well-positioned to impact both energy disaggregation and broader BSS applications. I recommend acceptance.
