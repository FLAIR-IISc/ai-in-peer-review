- Summary: Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for BSS in AFHMMs, formulates them as a convex quadratic program, and shows large gains on synthetic and real energy-disaggregation data.

- Quality: Technically sound with a clear derivation from posterior regularization, efficient convex QP formulation, and thorough experiments showing statistically significant improvements.

- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and reproducible experimental details.

- Originality: SACs are a novel constraint type targeting aggregate signal properties, distinct from existing temporal/state-change constraints.

- Significance: Substantial practical improvements (45% vs AFHMM, 29% vs AFAMAP in SAE) and potential applicability to other BSS problems.

- Weakness — Limited scope: Experiments use only a single aggregate constraint per appliance and do not evaluate multi-period constraints.

- Weakness — Practical acquisition: Evaluation relies on oracle "true total consumption" and does not fully assess robustness to realistic, noisy prior constraints.

- Weakness — Comparative methods: Does not explore combining SACs with other constraint-based methods (e.g., k-segment constraints) or integrated approaches.

- Recommendation: Accept — strong contribution meeting quality, clarity, originality, and significance criteria with important but non-fatal limitations.
