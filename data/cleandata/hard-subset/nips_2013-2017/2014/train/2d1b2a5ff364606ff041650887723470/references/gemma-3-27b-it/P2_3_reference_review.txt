## Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of blind source separation, specifically within the context of energy disaggregation. The authors identify the challenge of identifiability and propose incorporating “Signal Aggregate Constraints” (SACs) into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM). SACs leverage prior knowledge about expected total consumption of individual sources (e.g., appliances) to improve disaggregation accuracy. They demonstrate a convex quadratic program solution for efficient inference with these constraints and showcase a significant performance improvement (45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error) on real-world energy disaggregation data compared to the baseline AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Core Problem:** Identifiability is a fundamental issue in BSS and energy disaggregation.  The paper directly tackles this, offering a practical approach to incorporate domain knowledge.
* **Novelty of SACs:** The introduction of Signal Aggregate Constraints as a general class of priors applicable to BSS is a valuable contribution.  It's a logical and intuitive extension to existing methods.
* **Theoretical Soundness:**  The formulation of the constrained optimization problem and its relaxation into a convex quadratic program are well-motivated and clearly presented.  The connection to posterior regularization is also insightful.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results on real-world data are compelling, demonstrating a substantial improvement over existing techniques. The comparison to a recent state-of-the-art method (AFAMAP) strengthens the claims.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The authors explicitly address computational concerns, noting the scalability of their CQP approach and providing timing results.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and organized, making the technical details accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Exploration of Constraint Generalization:** While the authors mention generalizing the SACs to series of constraints over time periods, this extension is only briefly discussed and not empirically evaluated. Exploring its benefits (and potential computational costs) could strengthen the work.
* **Sensitivity Analysis:**  The sensitivity of the results to the tuning parameter δ (or equivalently, λ) is not thoroughly investigated. While they use a fixed λ in their experiments, a more detailed analysis of its impact on performance would be beneficial.
* **Dependence on Prior Knowledge:** The performance relies on accurate prior knowledge of the aggregate values (µi0). The paper acknowledges this but doesn't fully address how robust the method is to inaccuracies in this prior information.  Investigating scenarios with imperfect priors would be valuable.
* **Comparison with Simpler Baselines:** While a comparison with AFAMAP is strong, including simpler baselines (e.g., a regular AFHMM without any constraints) in the experiments would provide more context for the performance gains.



**Evaluation of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper presents a technically sound approach with a clear theoretical grounding. The experimental validation is convincing. The relaxation to a CQP is a smart move to enable efficient inference. However, the lack of sensitivity analysis and deeper exploration of the constraint generalization hold it back from a higher score.
* **Clarity (9/10):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is clear, and the explanations are concise and understandable. 
* **Originality (7/10):**  The concept of SACs is novel and represents a sensible way to incorporate domain knowledge. However, the core technique (AFHMM) is well-established, and the CQP relaxation is a standard optimization approach. The originality lies primarily in the *application* of these techniques to this specific problem and the formulation of the SACs.
* **Significance (8/10):**  The results are important and demonstrate a practical improvement in a real-world application. Energy disaggregation is a growing field with significant implications for energy conservation and user awareness. The potential for wider application of SACs in other BSS contexts is also promising.


**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a crucial issue (identifiability) in BSS and energy disaggregation.
* Presents a novel and theoretically sound approach (SACs).
* Demonstrates strong empirical results on real-world data.
*  Clear and well-written paper.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited exploration of the generalization of SACs.
* Lack of detailed sensitivity analysis.
* Reliance on accurate prior knowledge.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept.** 

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The proposed SACs offer a practical and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge and improve disaggregation accuracy. While some areas could be further investigated (sensitivity analysis, constraint generalization), the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh its weaknesses. The clear writing and strong empirical results make it a worthwhile addition to the NIPS program.
