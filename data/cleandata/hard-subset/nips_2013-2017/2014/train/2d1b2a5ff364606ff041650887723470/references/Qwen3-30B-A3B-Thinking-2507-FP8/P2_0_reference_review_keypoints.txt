- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, integrated into AFHMMs and solved via a convex quadratic program.  
- Provides clear theoretical justification and links SACs to posterior regularization.  
- Empirical evaluation uses synthetic and real-world energy data, showing strong improvements over baselines (NDE and SAE gains).  
- Mathematical derivation and computational complexity analysis are sound and reproducible.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible to experts.  
- SACs are a novel constraint type focused on aggregate values, distinct from prior transition-based constraints.  
- Demonstrates significant practical impact for energy disaggregation (large error reductions vs baselines).  
- Claims general applicability to other BSS domains but evaluation is limited to energy disaggregation.  
- Assumes known aggregate values; sensitivity to errors in these values is only partially explored and may be problematic in practice.  
- Generalized SACs (multiple time periods) are proposed but not empirically evaluated.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, significant, and clear—recommended for acceptance.
