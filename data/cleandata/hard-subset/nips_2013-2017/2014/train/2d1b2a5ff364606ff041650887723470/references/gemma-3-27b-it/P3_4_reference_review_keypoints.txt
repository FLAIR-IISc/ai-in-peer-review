- Overall assessment: novel, technically sound method (SACs in AFHMM) that improves energy disaggregation; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: introduce Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) and a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation to incorporate aggregate priors into AFHMMs.  
- Motivation: leverages practical prior knowledge about aggregate signal properties to address identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Related work: situates SACs relative to posterior regularization, generalized expectation, measurement-based learning, and AFAMAP.  
- Novelty & insight: SACs are a new, practical idea reframing BSS to constrain aggregates rather than force perfect separation.  
- Technical soundness: CQP derivation is clear and justified, with connections to posterior regularization acknowledged.  
- Empirical validation: significant improvements — 45% reduction vs AFHMM baseline and 29% vs AFAMAP on real HES data; ablation study on true vs estimated aggregates included.  
- Clarity & presentation: paper is well-written, mathematically clear, and figures are informative.  
- Computational efficiency: CQP approach maintains comparable runtime to existing methods like AFAMAP.  
- Sensitivity concern: performance dependence on accuracy of prior aggregate values (µ_i0) needs deeper analysis and robustness strategies.  
- Generalizability concern: paper focuses mostly on energy disaggregation; broader BSS applications (e.g., audio) are only briefly discussed.  
- Constraint generalization: limited exploration of using multiple/temporal constraints per chain and trade-offs with computational cost.  
- Baselines: lacks comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art disaggregation methods beyond AFAMAP.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, presents a novel method with strong empirical gains, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to prior accuracy and narrow application focus warrant more investigation.  
- Suggested revisions: analyze µ_i0 sensitivity and robust/Bayesian alternatives, discuss broader applicability beyond energy, compare to newer methods, and elaborate trade-offs for generalized constraints.
