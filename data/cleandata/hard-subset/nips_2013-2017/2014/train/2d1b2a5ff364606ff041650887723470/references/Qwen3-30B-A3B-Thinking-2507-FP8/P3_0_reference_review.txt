

# Peer Review: "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to address the identifiability problem in Blind Source Separation (BSS) applications, particularly for energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating constraints on the aggregate values (e.g., total energy consumption) of source signals into Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMMs). They transform the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) that can be efficiently solved, demonstrating significant improvements in energy disaggregation performance over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP approaches.

## Strengths

The paper makes a well-motivated contribution to the energy disaggregation literature. The identifiability problem in BSS is well-known, and the authors correctly identify that incorporating domain-specific constraints (like expected total appliance energy consumption) can improve results. Their connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) provides a nice theoretical foundation for their approach. The empirical evaluation on real-world energy data is thorough, with both simulated data (Section 7.1) and real-world household data (Section 7.2) showing consistent improvements (33% and 16% over AFAMAP in NDE and SAE, respectively). The computational efficiency claim is supported by similar inference times across methods, which is important for practical applications.

## Weaknesses

**Theoretical Limitations:** The paper lacks sufficient theoretical analysis of the convex relaxation. While they state that the relaxed problem is a CQP, they don't analyze the optimality gap between the relaxed solution and the true MAP solution. This is particularly important given that the identifiability problem means the true solution is not unique. The sensitivity of performance to the choice of λi (the complexity parameter) is not adequately explored - the paper simply sets λi=1 for all experiments.

**Practical Implementation Concerns:** The paper assumes that the aggregate values (µi0) are known in advance, but doesn't discuss how to estimate these in practice. While they use national survey data (NTC) as a proxy for µi0, the paper doesn't analyze how sensitive results are to errors in this estimate. In Section 7.1, they show that performance degrades as the constraint value diverges from the true aggregate, but they don't quantify how much error in µi0 would be tolerable in practice.

**Limited Discussion of Broader Applicability:** While the paper claims SACs are "general across a wide variety of domains," the experimental evaluation is limited to energy disaggregation. The paper doesn't demonstrate how SACs could be applied to other BSS problems (e.g., audio source separation), which would strengthen the claim of generality. The related work section (Section 2) mentions similar constraints like "k-segment constraints" [19], but doesn't sufficiently distinguish SACs from these prior approaches.

**Inadequate Comparison to Other Constraint-Based Approaches:** The paper compares to AFHMM and AFAMAP, but doesn't compare to other constraint-based approaches in the literature. For example, [21] uses non-homogeneous HMMs with constraints on state changes, and [12] uses constraints on state transitions. A comparison to these methods would help contextualize the contribution of SACs.

## Recommendations

1. Add theoretical analysis of the optimality gap between the relaxed solution and the true MAP solution for the constrained problem.

2. Include an analysis of how sensitive the results are to errors in the constraint values (µi0), particularly for the NTC approach.

3. Expand the evaluation to include a comparison with other constraint-based approaches (e.g., [21], [12]) to better demonstrate the unique value of SACs.

4. Discuss potential limitations of SACs and scenarios where they might not be applicable.

## Overall Assessment

The paper presents a practical approach to a well-known problem in BSS with demonstrated improvements in a relevant application domain. While the theoretical foundation is somewhat thin and the broader applicability is not sufficiently demonstrated, the empirical results are compelling and the method is computationally efficient. The paper would be strengthened by addressing the theoretical and practical limitations noted above.

**Quality:** 7/10 - Solid empirical results but theoretical analysis is insufficient.

**Clarity:** 8/10 - Well-written with clear motivation and methodology, but some implementation details could be clearer.

**Originality:** 6/10 - The concept of SACs is novel for BSS, but the approach builds on existing ideas (posterior regularization, constraint-based HMMs) without sufficiently distinguishing itself.

**Significance:** 7/10 - Demonstrates meaningful improvement in a practical application, but the broader impact beyond energy disaggregation is not established.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the energy disaggregation literature and presents a useful technique for incorporating domain knowledge into BSS problems. With the suggested improvements, it could be a strong contribution to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the theoretical and comparative limitations.
