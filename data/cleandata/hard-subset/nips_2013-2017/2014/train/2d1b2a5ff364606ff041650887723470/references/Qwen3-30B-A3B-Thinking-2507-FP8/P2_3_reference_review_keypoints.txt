- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to improve identifiability in blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) to efficiently solve the relaxed optimization with SACs.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% improvement vs AFAMAP.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using toy data and real-world datasets with paired t-tests for significance.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid and connects SACs to posterior regularization.  
- Computational cost is practical—runtime similar to baseline methods.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear notation; CQP derivation is dense and could use more intuition.  
- Main limitation: SACs require prior knowledge of aggregate values, limiting use in fully unsupervised settings.  
- Sensitivity to constraint parameter λi is under-explored beyond a single toy experiment; authors used λi = 1 in reported experiments.  
- Toy experiment is small (only two chains), leaving scalability demonstrations limited.  
- SACs represent a novel class of constraints (aggregate signal properties) distinct from prior transition-based constraints.  
- Method has broader applicability to other BSS problems where aggregate signal priors exist (e.g., audio).  
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality, original, significant contribution with practical impact.  
- Suggestion: briefly discuss integration with recent deep learning approaches to energy disaggregation.
