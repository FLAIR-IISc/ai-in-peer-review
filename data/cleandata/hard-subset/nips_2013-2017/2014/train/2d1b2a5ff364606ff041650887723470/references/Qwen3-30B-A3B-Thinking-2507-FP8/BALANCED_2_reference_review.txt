

# Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS) problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors address the identifiability problem in BSS by incorporating prior knowledge about expected aggregate signal values (e.g., total daily energy consumption for specific appliances) into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM). They transform the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP), enabling efficient computation. The approach is evaluated on synthetic data and real-world energy disaggregation tasks, showing significant improvements over baseline methods.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel and Well-Motivated Contribution:** The SAC concept is a clear and practical solution to the identifiability problem in BSS. The energy disaggregation application provides a strong motivation for the constraints, as appliance energy consumption totals are well-documented in prior research. This is a meaningful extension beyond existing constraint approaches like AFAMAP [12], which constrains state transitions rather than aggregate values.

2. **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the CQP formulation is technically rigorous and correctly addresses the non-Markovian nature of the SACs. The connection to posterior regularization [4] provides a solid theoretical foundation. The experimental results are convincing, with a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error compared to AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP on real-world data.

3. **Clear Experimental Evaluation:** The paper provides thorough evaluation on both synthetic data (demonstrating robustness to constraint specification errors) and real-world energy data (HES dataset). The use of both NDE and SAE metrics appropriately captures different aspects of disaggregation performance. The statistical significance testing (paired t-tests) adds credibility to the results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Exploration of Broader Applicability:** While the paper mentions potential applications beyond energy disaggregation, it doesn't provide concrete examples or discuss how SACs might be adapted to other BSS domains (e.g., audio processing or computational biology). A brief case study demonstrating SACs in another application would strengthen the contribution's significance.

2. **Simplistic Hyperparameter Treatment:** The paper sets λi = 1 for all experiments without justification or exploration of how these parameters should be tuned in practice. This is a significant limitation since the effectiveness of SACs depends on appropriate constraint strength. A discussion of cross-validation strategies or sensitivity analysis would improve the practical utility of the approach.

3. **Incomplete Comparison with Related Constraint Approaches:** The paper mentions k-segment constraints [19] but doesn't compare SACs to these or other constraint-based methods in the experimental evaluation. A more comprehensive comparison would better establish the relative merits of SACs.

4. **Theoretical Limitations:** The paper acknowledges that the relaxed solution isn't guaranteed optimal due to identifiability, but doesn't explore how the SACs specifically address the exchangeability problem in the AFHMM. A more detailed theoretical discussion of how SACs break the identifiability symmetry would strengthen the contribution.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a technically sound and practically useful approach to incorporating domain knowledge into BSS through signal aggregate constraints. The energy disaggregation application is well-chosen, and the results demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods. The technical contribution of transforming the constrained optimization into a CQP is well-executed.

However, the paper's significance would be substantially enhanced by addressing the weaknesses noted above. The limited exploration of broader applicability, simplistic hyperparameter treatment, and incomplete comparison with related methods prevent this from being a truly transformative contribution to the field.

**Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions)

**Key Suggestions for Revision:**

1. Add a brief case study demonstrating SACs in a different BSS domain (e.g., audio processing) to showcase broader applicability.

2. Include a sensitivity analysis showing how performance varies with different λi values and discuss practical tuning strategies.

3. Add a comparison with k-segment constraints [19] in the energy disaggregation experiments.

4. Expand the theoretical discussion on how SACs specifically address the identifiability problem in AFHMMs.

5. Clarify the notation in Section 5 (e.g., define all symbols consistently) to improve readability.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the energy disaggregation literature, and with these revisions, it would be a strong candidate for NIPS. The technical approach is sound, the experimental results are convincing, and the application to a real-world problem with significant societal impact makes this work particularly relevant.
