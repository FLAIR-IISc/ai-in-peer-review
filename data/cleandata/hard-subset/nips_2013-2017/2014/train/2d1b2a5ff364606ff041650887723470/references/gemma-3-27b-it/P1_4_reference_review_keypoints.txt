- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) in Additive Factorial HMMs to improve identifiability in blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.
- Formulates SACs as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference.
- Reports strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs standard AFHMM and 29% vs a recent state-of-the-art.
- Leverages prior domain knowledge (aggregate expected values) and connects to posterior regularization for theoretical grounding.
- Positions work relative to prior energy disaggregation and constrained HMM literature, claiming practical and computational novelty.
- Addresses a core BSS problem with a clear motivating example ("franken-appliance") to illustrate need for constraints.
- Presents a principled, domain-informed method that is computationally tractable for realistic datasets.
- Demonstrates robustness via toy experiments exploring sensitivity to constraint value specification.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and mathematically accessible with mostly consistent notation.
- Key limitation: lack of systematic exploration and justification for hyperparameter λ (λi = 1 used without detailed tuning analysis).
- Reliance on accurate µi0 constraint values is problematic; paper under-explores impact of estimation errors and real-world availability.
- Evaluation scope is narrow—almost exclusively energy disaggregation—limiting claims of general applicability to other BSS domains.
- The CQP uses an integer relaxation (Sitk ∈ {0,1} relaxed), and the potential impact of this relaxation on solution quality is not discussed in depth.
- Reproducibility could be improved by providing more detailed CVX setup and configuration information.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles important problem, theoretically sound SACs, efficient CQP formulation, and substantial empirical improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient hyperparameter treatment, dependency on accurate constraint values, and limited demonstration of generalization.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that outweighs weaknesses; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommended revisions: analyze hyperparameter sensitivity (λ), test sensitivity to µi0 errors, demonstrate or discuss broader BSS applications, detail CQP relaxation impacts and mitigation, and provide reproducibility details for CVX.
