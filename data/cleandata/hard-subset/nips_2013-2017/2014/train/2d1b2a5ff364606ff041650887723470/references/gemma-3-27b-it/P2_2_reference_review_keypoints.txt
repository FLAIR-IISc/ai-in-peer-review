- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) in an Additive Factorial HMM to improve identifiability in energy disaggregation.
- Formulates inference with SACs as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient computation.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM baseline and 29% improvement over AFAMAP on real data.
- Positions work within prior literature on AFHMMs, posterior regularization, generalized expectation, and measurement-based learning.
- Argues SACs are a novel, practical way to incorporate domain aggregate knowledge unavailable to prior graphical-model approaches.
- Strength: directly tackles identifiability, a core blind source separation problem.
- Strength: SACs provide a principled, easily available source of prior information for applications like energy disaggregation.
- Strength: CQP formulation makes the approach computationally scalable to real-world datasets.
- Strength: Clear presentation and accessible mathematical derivations.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including toy robustness experiments and real-world dataset results.
- Weakness: Method is sensitive to accuracy of prior aggregate values (µi0), with degraded performance when priors are noisy (NTC results).
- Weakness: Limited experimental exploration of more complex constraint structures (e.g., multiple time-window constraints).
- Weakness: Minimal parameter tuning analysis—λi fixed to 1 without cross-validation studies.
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of runtime complexity scaling with number of states and chains.
- Weakness: No justification or model selection analysis for assumed number of states (three).
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded solution with significant empirical improvements and efficient computation.
- Against acceptance: relies on accurate priors, lacks exploration of richer constraints, and omits comprehensive parameter tuning.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing sensitivity to µi0, constraint-structure experiments, parameter tuning, and justification/model selection for number of states.
- Qualitative scores: Quality 8.5/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8.5/10.
