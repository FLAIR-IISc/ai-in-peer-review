- Tackles blind source separation/energy disaggregation by addressing identifiability via Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).
- Proposes incorporating SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM to use prior aggregate consumption knowledge per source.
- Formulates inference as a constrained optimization and relaxes it to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient solution.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM baseline, 29% improvement vs AFAMAP.
- Shows computational scalability and provides timing results for the CQP approach.
- Draws a clear connection to posterior regularization and presents a well-motivated theoretical formulation.
- Paper is well written, organized, and technically accessible.
- SACs are a novel, intuitive class of priors with potential applicability beyond energy disaggregation.
- Limited empirical exploration of generalized/temporal SACs — discussed but not evaluated.
- Missing thorough sensitivity analysis for the tuning parameter δ/λ and its impact on results.
- Method depends on accurate prior aggregate values (µi0); robustness to inaccurate priors is not fully investigated.
- Lacks comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., unconstrained AFHMM) for fuller context.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strengths and empirical results outweigh the noted limitations.
