- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to enforce per-chain time-sum targets µ0 and improve identifiability.  
- Targets energy disaggregation (NILM), using survey-derived appliance totals (NTC) as aggregate priors.  
- Integrates SACs via a quadratic penalty on deviation from µ0.  
- Handles non‑Markovian constraints by introducing transition variables Hit and relaxing integrality to obtain a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Shows equivalence of the relaxation to posterior regularization.  
- Toy experiments indicate robustness to misspecified µ0.  
- On HES data, AFHMM+SAC outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP, with ~45% NDE reduction over AFHMM and ~29–33% over AFAMAP in some settings.  
- Builds on FHMM, AFAMAP, semi‑Markov NILM, and posterior regularization; novelty lies in global sum constraints and convex relaxation for structured latent models.  
- SAC modeling is well-motivated and clearly integrated; posterior‑regularization view is helpful.  
- Optimization is technically sound: relaxation yields a convex quadratic objective with PSD matrices.  
- Missing detail on how fractional Sit/Hit are converted to discrete sequences or final predictions and implications for metrics/interpretability.  
- Empirics: dataset choice and metrics are appropriate, but baselines are limited (no semi‑Markov or recent constrained FHMM comparisons).  
- Hyperparameters λi are fixed to 1; need sensitivity analysis and practical tuning guidance without appliance labels.  
- Clarity requests: specify how θ (µi, transitions, σt) are learned; how outputs are generated from fractional S; typical T/I/K values; solver settings and runtimes; how µ0 is computed in “with mains.”  
- Originality: specific combo of global aggregate constraints, convex relaxation, and NILM application appears novel; posterior regularization connection not the main novelty.  
- Significance: addresses identifiability in BSS/NILM with practical impact and transfer potential, but depends on µ0 quality and CQP scalability.  
- Strengths: principled incorporation of domain knowledge, tractable convex relaxation, empirical gains on real-world low-frequency data, and usefulness of coarse aggregate priors.  
- Key weaknesses/questions: decoding/rounding procedure, hyperparameter setting without labels, sensitivity to biased/noisy µ0, omitted baselines, solver scalability (CVX), handling of unmodeled loads in mains, and sparse parameter‑learning details.  
- Minor comments: tighten Section 5 notation, standardize references, and consider releasing code or a minimal solver.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel/principled method, solid technical grounding, and strong empirical improvements with statistical tests.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing decoding and scalability details, limited baselines/ablations, and some reproducibility gaps.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on addressing decoding, scalability, and baseline breadth (can be improved in rebuttal).
