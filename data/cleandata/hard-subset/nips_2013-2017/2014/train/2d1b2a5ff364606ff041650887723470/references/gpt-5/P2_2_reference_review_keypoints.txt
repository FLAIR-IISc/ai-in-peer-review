- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to encourage each source’s total energy over a horizon to match a prior, added as a quadratic penalty to the AFHMM MAP objective.  
- Derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation by introducing pairwise transition variables H and relaxing binary state indicators S to [0,1].  
- Shows the penalized MAP objective is equivalent to posterior regularization (MAP under reweighted posterior exp(-Σλiϕi)).  
- Reports substantial empirical gains on synthetic and UK HES ultra-low-frequency mains data, e.g., ~45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM and ~29% vs AFAMAP in one setting.  
- Uses both practical national totals (NTC) and oracle targets (TTC) as priors; NTC is a reasonable, available prior in practice.  
- Technical formulation is well-motivated and the relaxation is carefully set up leading to a convex program.  
- Completeness concern: paper does not discuss rounding fractional S to discrete state sequences; evaluation uses fractional S for energy reconstruction.  
- Empirics are substantive (100-household and 9-household mains experiments) with standard NILM metrics (NDE, SAE) and significance tests.  
- Hyperparameter concern: λi are fixed to 1 without systematic tuning or extensive sensitivity analysis on real data.  
- Runtime claim unclear: implementation uses a general-purpose solver (CVX); scaling behavior and complexity constants (T, I, Ki) are not provided.  
- Notational density and some derivations (Section 5) are dense; minor typos and reference formatting issues present.  
- Requests clarification on how x̂ is computed from relaxed S and whether any rounding is applied.  
- Requests clarification on the one-to-one correspondence between δi and λi and guidance for setting λi in practice.  
- Requests more detail on parameter learning for θ and the amount of labeled appliance data assumed.  
- Originality is moderate-to-good: global aggregate constraints in AFHMMs are novel and complementary to prior constraints.  
- Significance: practical and interpretable method valuable for NILM and other BSS tasks when per-source totals are available, but broader impact would be stronger with another domain or analysis of relaxation tightness.  
- Strength: simple, interpretable prior matching practitioner needs and a clean convex relaxation with strong empirical improvements.  
- Weakness: results depend on prior quality (µ0); sensitivity to prior bias/variance needs more exploration.  
- Weakness: lack of rounding/integrality strategy for producing discrete state sequences if needed.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns with CVX and lack of specialized solver exploiting block structure.  
- Weakness: baseline tuning fairness and whether competing methods could benefit from additional constraints are not fully addressed.  
- Minor: clean up references, clarify training requirements, and discuss combining SACs with other constraints (e.g., duration, at-most-one-change).  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses AFHMM identifiability, principled convex formulation, and strong empirical gains on realistic data.  
- Rejection arguments: limited sensitivity/scalability analysis, no non-NILM evaluation, and gaps in experimental clarity (rounding, baseline tuning).
