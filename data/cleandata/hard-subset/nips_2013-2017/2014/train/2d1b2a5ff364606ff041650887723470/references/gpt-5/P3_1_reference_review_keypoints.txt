- Summary: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) as quadratic penalties to match each chain’s total energy, relaxes AFHMM inference via transition marginals Hit to a concave quadratic program, connects to posterior regularization, and shows improved NDE/SAE on synthetic and HES data with similar runtimes.

- Relation to prior work: Well-situated among NILM and constrained HMM methods; novel in using global per-chain aggregate constraints for AFHMMs and correctly links to PR, but should cite label-proportions/aggregate supervision and constrained conditional models.

- Formulation and convexity: Reparameterization with Hit and relaxation yields a concave quadratic objective over a linear polytope (i.e., convex QP); PSD properties of matrices are correct.

- Soundness: Uses a standard local-polytope relaxation extended with quadratic penalties; paper omits discussion of integrality gaps and rounding strategies for state sequences.

- Parameter estimation: θ assumed known; µ estimated by k-means and transitions from data; λ fixed to 1 with little sensitivity analysis, though PR dual suggests principled λ learning.

- Complexity and scalability: QP has O(T∑i Ki + T∑i Ki^2) variables; empirical runtimes comparable on short, low-frequency windows but reliance on CVX raises scaling concerns for longer/higher-rate traces.

- Experimental validation: Shows consistent gains over AFHMM/AFAMAP and an oracle upper bound; missing per-appliance breakdowns, household variability, ablations on mis-specified µ0 and λ, and comparisons to duration-aware baselines.

- Clarity: Generally clear and well-organized; clarify vector notation (1T vs eT), state explicitly that you maximize a concave quadratic, and describe how fractional solutions are used or rounded.

- Implementation details: Need solver/tolerances/warm-start/convergence information for reproducibility.

- Originality: Novel application of global per-chain aggregate constraints in AFHMMs; moderate technical novelty but valuable in NILM context.

- Significance: Practically useful for NILM, improving identifiability using realistic priors; impact depends on availability/quality of µ0 but priors are often obtainable.

- Pros: Simple, principled incorporation of domain knowledge aligned with user metrics; clean convex formulation with PR interpretation; consistent empirical gains; natural extension to multiple time-window constraints.

- Cons — integrality: No discussion of integrality gap or rounding, limiting interpretability of on/off trajectories.

- Cons — robustness: Limited analysis of sensitivity to λ and mis-specified µ0; potential degradation when mains include unknown loads.

- Cons — baselines: Missing comparisons to HSMM/duration-aware or other constrained-inference approaches; unclear complementarity with duration constraints.

- Cons — scalability & priors: Unclear scalability with CVX to long/high-rate data and unclear handling/renormalization of µ0 when unmodeled loads exist.

- Minor issues: References missing years and contain typos.

- Suggested experiments: Vary λ and deliberately mis-specify µ0 to quantify robustness; provide per-appliance breakdowns with error bars and household variance.

- Suggested methodology additions: Compare to HSMM/duration models or combine SACs with duration constraints; provide a simple rounding heuristic and report its effect on metrics and interpretability.

- Suggested engineering improvements: Discuss or provide a specialized solver (e.g., projected gradient/ADMM) exploiting problem structure and report scaling with T.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated, practically relevant contribution with measurable gains on challenging data; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity, experimental breadth, and scalability concerns.
