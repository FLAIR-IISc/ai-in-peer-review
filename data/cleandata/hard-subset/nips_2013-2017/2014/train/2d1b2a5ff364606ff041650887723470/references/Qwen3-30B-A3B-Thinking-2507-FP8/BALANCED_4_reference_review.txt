

# Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to address the identifiability problem in blind source separation (BSS) applications, with a focus on energy disaggregation. The authors observe that in many BSS applications, the total signal values across source components are known in advance (e.g., average energy consumption per appliance), and propose incorporating these as constraints into Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMMs). SACs encourage the sum of each component's time series to match expected aggregate values, transforming the optimization problem into a convex quadratic program. The paper demonstrates significant performance improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP on energy disaggregation data.

The paper relates well to prior work at NIPS, particularly in the energy disaggregation literature (e.g., [12, 22]) and posterior regularization frameworks [4]. It extends recent work on constrained HMMs (e.g., AFAMAP [12]) by introducing a new constraint type that addresses the aggregate signal values rather than state transitions.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach. The derivation of the convex quadratic program for the SACs is well-supported and the relaxation to a CQP is justified. The experimental evaluation on real-world energy data is thorough, with appropriate metrics (NDE and SAE) and comparisons to strong baselines. The authors acknowledge the limitations of their relaxed solution due to identifiability issues, which demonstrates honesty in evaluation. However, the paper doesn't sufficiently analyze how the relaxation affects the quality of solutions, nor does it provide a thorough discussion of how to select the constraint values (µi0) in practice beyond using national averages.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulations are clear and well-motivated, with sufficient detail for reproducibility. The experimental setup, including data sources and evaluation metrics, is well-described. The figures effectively illustrate the relationship between constraint specification and performance.

**Originality:** SACs represent a novel constraint type for BSS that differs from existing approaches (e.g., AFAMAP [12] constrains state transitions, while SACs constrain aggregate values). The connection to posterior regularization provides theoretical grounding. While the paper acknowledges related work, the specific constraint type is new and well-motivated by the energy disaggregation application.

**Significance:** The results are significant, with a 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to AFHMM and 29% improvement over state-of-the-art. Energy disaggregation is an important practical problem, and the approach is generalizable to other BSS applications where aggregate constraints are known. The paper advances the state of the art in energy disaggregation and provides a useful framework for incorporating domain knowledge into BSS.

**Strengths:**
1. Novel constraint type (SACs) that addresses a meaningful problem in BSS
2. Solid theoretical foundation connecting SACs to posterior regularization
3. Compelling experimental results showing significant performance improvements
4. Clear, well-organized writing with sufficient detail for reproducibility
5. Practical application to a real-world problem with clear societal impact

**Weaknesses:**
1. Insufficient discussion of how to obtain constraint values (µi0) in practice beyond national averages
2. Limited analysis of the trade-offs between constraint accuracy and performance
3. No comparison to other constraint-based approaches beyond AFAMAP
4. The relaxed solution (CQP) may not yield optimal results, but this limitation isn't thoroughly analyzed
5. The paper doesn't discuss how SACs would perform in more challenging BSS scenarios (e.g., with more sources than observations)

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- The problem addressed (identifiability in BSS) is important and well-motivated
- SACs represent a genuinely novel constraint type with clear theoretical grounding
- The results demonstrate significant improvements over strong baselines
- The application to energy disaggregation is relevant and impactful
- The paper is technically sound and well-written

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- The paper doesn't sufficiently address how to obtain constraint values in general cases
- The evaluation is limited to energy disaggregation without broader validation
- The relaxation to a CQP is not thoroughly analyzed for its impact on solution quality

**Recommendation:** Accept

**Suggestions for improvement:**
1. Include a discussion of how to obtain constraint values (µi0) in different application domains beyond energy disaggregation.
2. Add a comparison to other constraint-based approaches (e.g., k-segment constraints [19]) to better position the novelty.
3. Provide more analysis of the trade-offs between constraint accuracy and disaggregation performance.
4. Discuss potential limitations of SACs in more challenging BSS scenarios (e.g., when aggregate values are unknown or highly variable).
5. Consider adding a brief discussion of how SACs might be integrated into unsupervised settings where aggregate values aren't known a priori.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the BSS literature with a novel constraint approach that has demonstrated significant practical impact in energy disaggregation. With the suggested improvements, the paper would be even stronger, but as it stands, it represents a high-quality contribution to the field.
