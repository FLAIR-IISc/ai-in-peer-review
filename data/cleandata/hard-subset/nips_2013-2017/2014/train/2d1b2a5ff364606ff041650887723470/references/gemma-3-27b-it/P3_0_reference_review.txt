## Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenge of identifiability in blind source separation (BSS), specifically within the context of energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating *signal aggregate constraints (SACs)* – prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of individual appliances – into an additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM). They develop a convex quadratic program (CQP) to efficiently solve the resulting inference problem and demonstrate significant performance improvements on both simulated and real-world energy disaggregation datasets, achieving a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error compared to a baseline AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art method.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature on energy disaggregation and BSS. It acknowledges the long history of AFHMMs in this area and the recent push to incorporate prior knowledge, referencing relevant work on posterior regularization, generalized expectation criteria, and constrained HMMs (e.g., AFAMAP, non-homogeneous HMMs, semi-Markov models). The connection to probabilistic databases and aggregate constraints is also appropriately noted, highlighting the novelty of applying such constraints within a dynamic Bayesian network framework like the AFHMM.  The authors effectively frame SACs as a specific, well-motivated type of prior knowledge that can be efficiently integrated.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Motivation:** The introduction of SACs is a genuinely novel approach to address the identifiability issue in energy disaggregation. The motivation – leveraging domain expertise about expected appliance usage – is strong and practical. The observation that aggregate signal values are often well-defined is insightful.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the CQP for the relaxed AFHMM+SAC model appears mathematically sound. The transformation to a convex program allows for efficient optimization.  The connection to posterior regularization further strengthens the theoretical underpinnings.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling. The consistent improvements over both the baseline AFHMM and the AFAMAP method on both simulated and real-world data provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of SACs. The use of both NDE and SAE metrics is appropriate.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized. The mathematical notation is mostly standard, and the explanations are concise and understandable. The authors provide sufficient detail for a reader familiar with HMMs to follow the methodology.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The authors explicitly address the computational complexity and demonstrate that the proposed method scales similarly to existing approaches, which is a critical practical consideration.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Sensitivity to Prior Specification:** While the results demonstrate the benefit of SACs, the sensitivity to the accuracy of the prior aggregate values (µi0) is a concern.  The toy experiment highlights this, and the results with TTC as a prior (while impressive) are unrealistic in a real-world scenario. More analysis of the robustness to errors in the prior specification is needed. How does performance degrade with increasing noise in the µi0 values?
* **Limited Exploration of Constraint Generalization:** The authors mention the possibility of generalizing the SACs to time-varying constraints (e.g., using different aggregate values for daytime vs. nighttime). While acknowledging this extension, they do not present any experimental results. Investigating the performance gains from such richer constraint structures would be valuable.
* **Lack of Comparison to More Recent Disaggregation Methods:** The comparison to AFAMAP is reasonable, but there have been more recent advances in energy disaggregation, particularly those leveraging deep learning techniques. A comparison to these methods, even if brief, would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the proposed approach.  Specifically, comparing to methods that directly predict appliance-level energy usage would be insightful.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The statement that lambda was simply set to 1 for all experiments feels a bit dismissive. While cross-validation is not explicitly detailed, clarifying *how* the hyperparameter was chosen (or why it wasn't further tuned) would be beneficial.



**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Pro-Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem (identifiability in BSS/energy disaggregation).
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant performance improvements.
*   The methodology is clearly explained and the computational complexity is addressed.
*   The connection to posterior regularization provides a solid theoretical foundation.

**Con-Acceptance:**

*   The sensitivity to prior specification is a potential limitation.
*   The exploration of constraint generalization is limited.
*   The lack of comparison to more recent disaggregation methods makes it difficult to fully assess the significance of the contribution.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of energy disaggregation. The proposed SACs offer a practical and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge into the AFHMM framework, leading to significant performance improvements. While some limitations exist (particularly regarding the sensitivity to prior specification and the lack of comparison to cutting-edge deep learning methods), the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept with Minor Revisions.**  

I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the following minor revisions:

1.  **Robustness Analysis:** Include a more thorough analysis of the sensitivity of the results to errors in the prior aggregate values (µi0).
2.  **Discussion of Time-Varying Constraints:** Expand the discussion on the potential for time-varying constraints and potentially include preliminary experiments.
3.  **Comparison to Recent Methods:** Briefly discuss and, if possible, compare the performance to more recent deep learning-based energy disaggregation methods.
4.  **Hyperparameter Justification:** Clarify the rationale for setting lambda = 1 and whether any hyperparameter tuning was performed.
