- Paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) as a novel prior for blind source separation within AFHMMs to leverage known total energy/value per source.
- SACs are formulated to yield a computationally efficient convex quadratic program for inference.
- Method evaluated on simulated data and the HES energy disaggregation dataset, showing large improvements over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Positions work correctly within BSS and energy disaggregation literature and distinguishes focus on the type of prior knowledge rather than inference mechanisms.
- Connects to related ideas like posterior regularization and measurement-based learning, and appropriately cites AFAMAP and semi-Markov models.
- Novelty: SACs provide a principled, domain-knowledge-driven way to improve identifiability in BSS.
- Practicality: Convex quadratic-program formulation enables efficient, real-world applicable inference.
- Empirical validation: Strong experiments, including NDE and SAE metrics, and convincing comparison to a strong baseline (AFAMAP).
- Presentation: Well-written with accessible notation and clear connection to posterior regularization.
- Analysis: Ablation study on prior accuracy and computational complexity discussion demonstrate robustness and practical considerations.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on tuning parameter δi/λi selection and lack of sensitivity analysis for these parameters.
- Weakness: Current implementation uses a single aggregate constraint per chain; limited discussion of multi-constraint tradeoffs (accuracy vs computational cost).
- Weakness: Comparison scope could be broadened to include more recent disaggregation techniques.
- Weakness: The relaxation used for computational tractability is not fully analyzed for its impact on theoretical identifiability guarantees.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10 (noting ~45% reduction in NDE).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, efficient convex solution, strong real-world results, clear and thorough evaluation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited parameter tuning discussion, narrower comparison set, and insufficient exploration of relaxation implications.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address parameter tuning details and deeper discussion of the relaxation’s implications.
