This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, specifically within the context of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs), and applies this to the problem of energy disaggregation. The core idea is to incorporate prior knowledge about expected aggregate values of source signals (e.g., a toaster uses approximately 50 Wh per day) as constraints during inference. The authors formulate this as a convex quadratic program, demonstrating computational efficiency, and demonstrate significant performance improvements on real-world energy disaggregation data compared to existing methods.

The paper builds upon a substantial body of work in blind source separation, energy disaggregation, and probabilistic modeling. It correctly positions itself within the existing literature, referencing key papers in AFHMMs ([17]), energy disaggregation surveys ([8, 22]), and related constraint-based approaches ([4, 12, 13]). The connection to posterior regularization [6] is also well articulated.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a general class of constraints for BSS is a meaningful contribution. While prior work has explored various constraints on HMM states ([12, 21]), explicitly focusing on aggregate values represents a novel direction.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the convex quadratic program appears correct and offers a computationally efficient solution to the constrained inference problem. The relaxation approach is a pragmatic way to handle the non-Markovian nature of the constraints.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experimental results on real-world energy disaggregation data are compelling. A 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to the original AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method [12] demonstrate the practical benefit of SACs.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The motivation is clear, and the mathematical derivations are presented in a readable manner.
*   **Completeness:** The paper provides a complete description of the model, inference procedure, and experimental setup, allowing for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Sensitivity Analysis:** While the toy data experiment touches upon the sensitivity of performance to the accuracy of the constraint values (µ0), a more thorough sensitivity analysis would be beneficial. How robust is the method to *inaccurate* prior knowledge?
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper states that λi was set to 1. While acknowledging cross-validation as a possibility, a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter selection and its impact on performance is needed.
*   **Generalizability:** The empirical evaluation focuses solely on energy disaggregation. While this provides a strong demonstration of the method’s effectiveness in this domain, it would be valuable to explore its performance on other BSS problems where aggregate constraints might be applicable.
*   **Comparison with Stronger Baselines:** While comparison to [12] is relevant, exploring comparisons with more recent and potentially stronger energy disaggregation methods might be beneficial to fully assess the contribution of the paper.
*   **Limited Discussion of Identifiability:** The paper acknowledges the identifiability problem but doesn’t fully explore how SACs help mitigate it. A more detailed discussion of how the constraints inform the solution and potentially reduce ambiguity would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a novel and technically sound approach to incorporating prior knowledge into BSS models.
*   The empirical results demonstrate a significant performance improvement on a real-world problem.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents its contributions.
*   The formulation as a convex quadratic program allows for efficient computation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a single application domain.
*   The sensitivity analysis of the constraint values could be more thorough.
*   Hyperparameter tuning is not discussed in detail.
*   Further exploration of the connection to identifiability is needed.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The introduction of SACs is a novel idea, and the empirical results are promising. While some aspects of the evaluation and analysis could be strengthened, the paper’s strengths outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points mentioned above, particularly concerning sensitivity analysis and a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter selection and identifiability.
