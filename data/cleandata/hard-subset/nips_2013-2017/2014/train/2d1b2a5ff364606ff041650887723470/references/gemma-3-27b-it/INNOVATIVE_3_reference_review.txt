This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) within an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) framework, motivated by the blind source separation problem and specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The core idea – incorporating prior knowledge about expected total energy consumption of appliances – is quite compelling and addresses a crucial practical issue in disaggregation: identifiability and interpretability of the separated signals. The authors convincingly argue that such aggregate constraints are broadly applicable beyond energy disaggregation, making this a potentially impactful contribution. They formulate the constrained optimization as a convex quadratic program, enabling efficient computation. Results on both simulated and real-world (HES dataset) data demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods, including a substantial 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a general approach to incorporating prior knowledge into BSS, and specifically within the AFHMM context, is genuinely novel. While prior incorporation techniques exist (posterior regularization, etc., as discussed in the introduction), the focus on *aggregate* constraints and their specific formulation are new.
*   **Practical Relevance:**  Energy disaggregation is a well-motivated and challenging problem with clear practical applications. The identifiability issue addressed by SACs is a significant hurdle in real-world deployment.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the CQP for the relaxed problem appears sound, and the computational tractability is a major advantage. The authors demonstrate scalability in their experiments.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported performance gains on the HES dataset are impressive, particularly the 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error and the 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making the core ideas accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors justify the relaxed optimization problem, a more thorough theoretical analysis of the approximation introduced by the relaxation would be beneficial. Understanding the conditions under which the relaxation is tight or leads to significant error is important.
*   **Sensitivity to Constraint Values:** The results demonstrate performance is sensitive to the accuracy of the aggregate constraint values (µi0), particularly in the toy data. While this is acknowledged, a deeper exploration of robustness to imperfect prior knowledge would strengthen the paper. The authors note this in Section 7.1, but a more rigorous analysis would be better.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The choice of λ (or equivalently δ) is crucial, but the paper lacks a detailed discussion of how these parameters were tuned. Cross-validation is mentioned, but the details are missing.
*   **Limited Ablation Study:** While comparisons to AFHMM and AFAMAP are provided, a more focused ablation study to isolate the impact of the SACs would be valuable. For instance, exploring different types of aggregate constraints (beyond simple sums) could provide further insights.
*   **Generalization beyond Energy Disaggregation:** While the authors claim the approach is general, the evaluation is heavily focused on energy disaggregation. Demonstrating the effectiveness of SACs on a different BSS application (e.g., audio source separation) would bolster the generality claim.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a genuinely novel and potentially impactful approach to BSS.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant performance improvements.
*   The technical contribution (CQP formulation) enables efficient computation.
*   The paper addresses a critical practical challenge (identifiability) in energy disaggregation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis of the relaxation is limited.
*   The sensitivity to constraint values and hyperparameter tuning requires further investigation.
*   The evaluation is primarily focused on a single application domain.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper is a strong submission. The novelty of the SAC approach, coupled with the impressive empirical results, outweighs the minor weaknesses. While further theoretical analysis and broader application evaluation would be desirable, the current work represents a significant step forward in BSS and energy disaggregation.  I believe the paper warrants acceptance, as the core idea is strong and the potential impact is substantial. I lean towards acceptance. The paper is pushing a novel idea, and methodological perfection isn't strictly necessary at this stage.
