- Paper tackles blind source separation for energy disaggregation by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM).
- Proposes a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference and connects SACs to posterior regularization.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method.
- Positions work well within prior literature, acknowledging related HMM constraint methods and prior-knowledge frameworks.
- Novelty: explicit, structured incorporation of aggregate energy priors (SACs) into AFHMMs.
- Theoretical contribution: derivation of a computationally tractable CQP and formal grounding via posterior regularization.
- Strong empirical evaluation including an ablation study (TTC vs NTC).
- Clear writing, organization, and presentation of equations and explanations.
- Weakness: method’s reliance on accurate prior aggregate values (µi0) may limit real-world applicability.
- Weakness: limited empirical exploration of generalized/multi-period constraints (J constraints).
- Weakness: superficial discussion of computational scaling with number of states and chains for large problems.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other common regularization techniques (e.g., L1/L2) within AFHMM.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with solid theory and compelling results.
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on accurate priors, limited exploration of generalization and complexity, and incomplete regularization comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written paper with convincing results; recommendation: Strong Accept.
