

# Review: Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs), addressing the critical identifiability problem in blind source separation (BSS) applications like energy disaggregation. The authors observe that in many BSS domains, we have prior knowledge about the total energy consumption of individual appliances (e.g., a toaster uses ~50 Wh/day), and they incorporate this knowledge as constraints on the sum of source signals. The key technical contribution is transforming the non-Markovian constraint optimization problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) that can be efficiently solved.

The paper builds on prior work in energy disaggregation (e.g., [8, 12, 22]) and constraint-based approaches to HMMs (e.g., [12, 19]), but makes a novel contribution by formalizing SACs as a general class of constraints applicable across BSS domains. The authors establish a theoretical connection to posterior regularization [4], showing their approach is a special case of this framework, which strengthens the theoretical grounding.

**Quality (Strong):** The paper is technically sound with well-justified claims. The CQP formulation (Section 5) is properly derived and the convexity properties are correctly established. The empirical evaluation (Section 7) is comprehensive, using both synthetic data (Section 7.1) and real-world energy data (Section 7.2). The results are compelling: AFHMM+SAC achieves a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error over the baseline AFHMM and a 29% improvement over AFAMAP (a recent state-of-the-art method). The authors appropriately acknowledge limitations (e.g., SACs require prior knowledge of aggregate values, which may not always be available).

**Clarity (Strong):** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The problem statement (Section 3) and motivation (Section 1) clearly establish the importance of the identifiability issue in energy disaggregation. Technical details are presented with appropriate mathematical rigor, and the empirical results are clearly presented in tables and figures. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is particularly well-explained, making an abstract concept more accessible.

**Originality (Moderate):** While the SAC concept is novel for BSS, the paper could better distinguish itself from related constraint-based approaches. The connection to posterior regularization is interesting but not entirely new (the paper cites [4] for this framework). The paper mentions "k-segment constraints" [19] in related work but doesn't explicitly compare with this approach in experiments. The application to energy disaggregation is well-motivated, but the core technique (SACs) could potentially be applied to other BSS domains, which the authors could have emphasized more.

**Significance (Strong):** The results on energy disaggregation are significant, with a 45% improvement over the baseline. The paper makes a concrete contribution to a practical problem with real-world impact. The authors demonstrate that SACs can be incorporated into existing AFHMM frameworks without significant computational overhead (Table 1 shows similar inference times across methods), making the approach practically viable. The work provides a clear path for incorporating domain knowledge into BSS models, which is valuable for many applications beyond energy disaggregation.

**Weaknesses:** The paper could better address the sensitivity of SACs to errors in the constraint values (µi0). While Figure 1 shows some results for synthetic data, the authors don't thoroughly analyze how performance degrades when the constraint values are inaccurate (a common issue in real-world applications). The paper also doesn't sufficiently address how to obtain constraint values when expert knowledge is unavailable, beyond mentioning "national surveys" (which may not be applicable to all appliances or regions). The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is theoretically sound but somewhat superficial; the authors could have discussed how this perspective might lead to further extensions or alternative constraint formulations.

**Overall Assessment:**
This is a strong paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of blind source separation. The SAC approach is novel, well-motivated, and effectively addresses a significant problem in energy disaggregation. The technical approach is sound, the evaluation is thorough, and the results are compelling. While there are some limitations regarding constraint sensitivity and the practical acquisition of constraint values, these don't undermine the core contribution. The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it combines theoretical insight with practical application, and makes a clear advancement in a well-established research area.

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper demonstrates a novel technique with clear theoretical grounding, strong empirical validation, and significant practical impact in an important application domain. The contributions are substantial and well-justified, making this a valuable addition to the NIPS literature.
