- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to use known aggregate source values and transforms inference into a convex quadratic program.
- Demonstrates large empirical gains: ~45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and ~29% vs AFAMAP.
- Claims computational efficiency with inference times similar to baseline methods.
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic and real-world data, comparisons to AFAMAP, and paired t-tests for significance.
- Shows SACs are robust to errors in constraint specification (Section 7.1).
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization, situating the method within a broader theoretical framework.
- Major weakness: insufficient theoretical treatment of identifiability and no quantification of how SACs mitigate exchangeability.
- Hyperparameter choice concern: λi fixed to 1 with no tuning or ablation studies to assess sensitivity.
- Limited generalizability: claims beyond energy disaggregation lack empirical evidence or demonstrations in other domains.
- Superficial comparison to related constraint-based approaches (e.g., k-segment constraints) without clear differentiation or advantages.
- Theoretical analysis of the convex QP is thin: no complexity analysis or discussion of scaling with number of appliances/time steps.
- Practical concern: obtaining accurate aggregate constraints (µi0) for new appliances/households is not adequately addressed.
- Overall quality: technically sound and well-written but needs more theory and hyperparameter/ablation analysis.
- Originality: novel application of SACs and QP formulation but insufficiently contrasted with prior constraint methods.
- Significance: strong empirical impact for energy disaggregation, but broader applicability claims are overstated without more evidence.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions, requesting deeper identifiability analysis, λi ablation/sensitivity studies, stronger comparisons to related methods, and discussion of practical constraint acquisition.
