- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to address identifiability in blind source separation by enforcing known aggregate signal values.
- Formulates SACs as constraints and transforms the inference problem into a convex quadratic program for efficient optimization.
- Derives SACs from posterior regularization, providing a strong theoretical foundation linking to existing probabilistic constraint frameworks.
- Demonstrates empirical gains on synthetic and real energy disaggregation data using NDE and SAE metrics.
- Reports substantial improvements: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error over AFHMM and 29% over AFAMAP.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient experimental detail for reproducibility.
- SACs are novel in targeting aggregate signal values, complementary to prior approaches like AFAMAP and semi-Markov duration constraints.
- The approach has practical significance for energy disaggregation and is potentially generalizable to other BSS problems.
- Practical challenge: unclear how to obtain accurate appliance aggregate priors (µi0) when domain data is unavailable or inaccurate.
- Limited sensitivity analysis of the regularization parameter λi (only evaluated at λi = 1).
- Insufficient comparison and discussion of conceptual overlap with other constraint methods (e.g., k-segment constraints).
- Evaluation scope is narrow, focused mainly on energy disaggregation without demonstration on other BSS applications.
- Recommendation: accept—paper is theoretically sound, empirically strong, and makes a meaningful contribution.
- Pro arguments: novel/practical solution, solid theory, significant empirical improvements, clear writing, real-world impact.
- Con arguments: limited parameter exploration, constrained generalizability evidence, and incomplete comparison to related constraints.
