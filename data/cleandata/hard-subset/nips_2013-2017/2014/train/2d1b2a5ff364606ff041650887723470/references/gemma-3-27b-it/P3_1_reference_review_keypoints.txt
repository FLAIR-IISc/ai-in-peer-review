- Tackles blind source separation for energy disaggregation by incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMMs).  
- Leverages prior knowledge of total appliance energy usage to improve identifiability and guide inference.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient convex quadratic program relaxation solved with CVX.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on the HES dataset: ~45% reduction vs baseline AFHMM and ~29% improvement vs AFAMAP.  
- Performs ablation study using TTC and NTC to assess sensitivity to constraint accuracy.  
- Situates work within prior BSS and energy disaggregation literature and links to posterior regularization approaches.  
- Novel contribution: focus on aggregate-level constraints rather than state-level constraints.  
- Technical soundness: clear derivation and tractable optimization formulation.  
- Empirical validation: convincing real-world results and meaningful error reductions.  
- Clarity and organization: well-written with clear notation and explanations.  
- Computational efficiency: scales similarly to existing methods and addresses practical concerns.  
- Weakness: limited systematic analysis of performance degradation when assumed aggregate values are incorrect.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of generalized constraints (multiple/time-varying) without empirical exploration.  
- Weakness: limited justification or sensitivity analysis for regularization parameter λi (set to 1).  
- Weakness: reliance on HMM assumptions for components without discussing extensions to more flexible models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically strong, efficient, and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient sensitivity analysis and limited exploration of more complex constraint structures.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with impactful idea and solid execution.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
