## Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the identifiability problem in Blind Source Separation (BSS), specifically within the context of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs) applied to energy disaggregation. The authors propose Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) – priors on the total energy consumption of individual sources – to improve disaggregation accuracy. They formulate incorporating these constraints as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for a relaxed version of the MAP inference problem and demonstrate significant performance gains on both simulated and real-world energy disaggregation data.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon a substantial body of work in energy disaggregation and BSS. It acknowledges existing techniques like AFAMAP and semi-Markov models that constrain the HMM states.  The novelty lies in the specific *type* of constraint – aggregate signal values – and its efficient implementation via a CQP.  The work also connects to broader research in probabilistic modeling with prior constraints (posterior regularization, generalized expectation criteria, measurement-based learning), framing SACs as a specific instantiation of these techniques.  The authors appropriately cite relevant literature across these areas.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** The identifiability issue is a critical hurdle for practical BSS and energy disaggregation, where interpretability of the disaggregated signals is essential.
* **Novelty of Approach:** The introduction of SACs, particularly the focus on aggregate signal values, is a sensible and potentially impactful constraint for this domain. It reflects a realistic prior knowledge that domain experts possess.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the CQP for the relaxed optimization problem is clearly presented and justified. The connection to posterior regularization provides a strong theoretical grounding.
* **Empirical Validation:** The results on both the toy dataset and the real-world HES data are compelling. A 45% decrease in NDE compared to the standard AFHMM and a 29% improvement over AFAMAP is significant.  The ablation study with TTC (true total consumption) illustrates the potential of the SAC approach.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The authors acknowledge and discuss the computational complexity, stating that the CQP scales polynomially and comparable to existing methods.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the methodology and results easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Relaxation & Optimality:** The authors acknowledge that solving the relaxed problem does not guarantee optimality.  While understandable given the complexity, a more detailed discussion of the impact of relaxation on the quality of the solution would be beneficial. How sensitive are the results to the choice of the relaxation parameter?
* **Parameter Tuning:** The choice of λ (and δ, equivalently) in the SAC is presented as being set to 1. While noting cross-validation could be used, the paper lacks exploration of different parameter settings and their impact on performance.  A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Generalizability:** While the energy disaggregation domain provides a strong demonstration, the paper could benefit from exploring the applicability of SACs to other BSS problems mentioned in the introduction (audio processing, computational biology). Even a brief discussion of potential challenges and adaptations would enhance the work's broader impact.
* **Limited Comparison:** While compared to AFAMAP, a comparison with more recent state-of-the-art disaggregation methods, beyond just [12], would be valuable for contextualizing the results.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles an important and challenging problem in BSS.
* The proposed SACs are a novel and well-motivated approach to address identifiability.
* The CQP formulation provides an efficient means of incorporating these constraints.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant improvements in disaggregation performance.
* The paper is theoretically sound and clearly written.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The relaxation step introduces a potential suboptimality that is not thoroughly investigated.
* The limited exploration of parameter tuning and generalizability leaves some questions unanswered.
* The comparison to related work could be more comprehensive.


**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**.  The novelty of the approach, coupled with the compelling empirical results and theoretical justification, make a significant contribution to the field of BSS and energy disaggregation. While there are some minor weaknesses regarding parameter sensitivity, optimality of the relaxed solution and limited generalizability discussion, these do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. The paper meets all the NIPS review criteria and is likely to be of interest to a broad audience.
