This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, specifically within the context of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs), and applies this to the energy disaggregation problem. The core idea is to incorporate prior knowledge about the total energy consumption of appliances by constraining the aggregate values of the hidden source signals. The authors formulate this as a convex quadratic program, offering a computationally efficient solution.

The work builds upon existing research in BSS, energy disaggregation [8, 22, 15], and incorporating prior knowledge into probabilistic models [4, 14, 13].  It differentiates itself by focusing on a specific class of constraints – aggregate constraints – and demonstrating their effectiveness in a practical application.  Related work focuses on constraining the *states* of the HMMs [12, 21, 11, 10, 16], whereas this paper constrains the *sums* of states.  The authors acknowledge and connect to work on aggregate constraints in probabilistic databases [20], but highlight the limitations of that work to simpler graphical models.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a principled way to incorporate domain knowledge into AFHMMs is a novel contribution.  The application to energy disaggregation is also well-motivated and practical.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM+SAC model appears mathematically sound. The connection to posterior regularization [4] provides further justification for the approach.
* **Experimental Validation:** The paper provides a thorough experimental evaluation on both simulated data and a real-world energy disaggregation dataset (HES).  The results demonstrate a significant performance improvement (45% decrease in NDE) compared to the original AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art method [12].
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation for the work is clear, and the mathematical derivations are reasonably easy to follow.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The authors explicitly address the computational complexity and report that the proposed method scales similarly to existing approaches (AFHMM, AFAMAP).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of SACs:** While the authors acknowledge that the SACs can be generalized (e.g., to multiple time periods), the paper focuses solely on a single aggregate constraint per chain over the entire time series.  Exploring more complex constraint structures could potentially further improve performance.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The choice of the regularization parameter (λi or δi) is not discussed in detail. While the paper mentions using a single value (λi = 1) and suggests cross-validation, a more systematic exploration of parameter sensitivity would be beneficial.
* **Dependence on Prior Knowledge:** The performance of AFHMM+SAC relies on accurate prior knowledge of the expected aggregate values (µi0). The paper acknowledges this, but a more in-depth discussion of how to obtain this information in practice (beyond referencing national surveys) would be helpful. The impact of inaccuracies in µi0 is demonstrated with the toy data, but further investigation is needed.
* **Oracle Performance:** The results using the TTC in the toy data demonstrate the potential of the approach, but this is an unrealistic scenario.  The gap between the NTC and TTC results highlights the sensitivity to the accuracy of the prior information.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the comparison to [12] is valuable, a broader comparison to other state-of-the-art energy disaggregation methods would strengthen the evaluation.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to incorporating prior knowledge into AFHMMs.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement in energy disaggregation performance.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methods, and results.
* The problem addressed is important and relevant to the NIPS community.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The scope of the SACs is relatively limited, and exploring more complex constraint structures could potentially yield further improvements.
* The sensitivity to prior knowledge (µi0) needs to be carefully considered and addressed.
* The lack of a more extensive comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation.  The proposed SACs provide a principled and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge into AFHMMs, and the experimental results are convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding parameter tuning, prior knowledge sensitivity, and comparison to other methods.
