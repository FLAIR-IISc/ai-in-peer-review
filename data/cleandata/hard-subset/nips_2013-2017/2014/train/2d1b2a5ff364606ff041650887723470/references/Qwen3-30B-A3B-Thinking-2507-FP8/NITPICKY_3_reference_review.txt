

# Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

## Summary
This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of appliances into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM), formulating this as a constrained optimization problem that can be transformed into a convex quadratic program. The method is evaluated on both toy data and real-world energy disaggregation tasks, showing improvements over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP approaches.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an important practical problem in energy disaggregation with a well-motivated application. The idea of incorporating prior knowledge about signal aggregates is intuitively appealing and could be useful in other domains. The experimental results on energy disaggregation data are promising, showing significant improvements (33% NDE reduction) when using SACs with national average consumption estimates. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is well-made and provides theoretical grounding.

## Weaknesses

### Technical and Mathematical Issues
1. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout, which significantly hinders readability and understanding. For example:
   - "µ" is used for both mean values and matrices (e.g., "µi" vs "µ_i" in different sections)
   - "Sitk" is sometimes written as "S_{itk}" and sometimes as "Sit_k"
   - "µi0" is written as "μ_{i0}" in some places but "µi0" in others
   - The transition matrix is denoted as "P (i)" in equation (3) but "P^{(i)}" in equation (5), and "P (i)" in equation (6)

2. **Mathematical Errors and Ambiguities**:
   - Equation (1) should be written as "Y_t = ∑_{i=1}^I x_{it} + ε_t" with proper summation notation and ε for noise.
   - Equation (3) has multiple notational errors: "STit ( logP (i) ) Si,t−1" should be "S_{it}^T log(P^{(i)}) S_{i,t-1}" and the notation is inconsistent.
   - Equation (5) is miswritten: "maximize S L(S) = logP (S|Y )− I∑ i=1 λi ( T∑ t=1 µTi Sit − µi0 )2" should be "maximize_S L(S) = log P(S|Y) - ∑_{i=1}^I λ_i (∑_{t=1}^T μ_i^T S_{it} - μ_{i0})^2"
   - The derivation of the convex quadratic program in Section 5 lacks sufficient mathematical detail. The transformation from the non-convex problem to the CQP is not clearly explained, and key steps are missing.

3. **Experimental Details**:
   - The paper states "We simply assume 3 states for all the appliances" without justification. The number of states is a critical hyperparameter that significantly affects performance, and the paper should discuss why 3 states are appropriate for energy disaggregation.
   - The training data description in Section 7.2.1 is ambiguous: "The model parameters were estimated by using 15-26 days' data as the training data." Is this 15-26 days per household or total across households?
   - The paper claims "AFHMM+SAC using NTC had improved 33% and 16% over state-of-the-art AFAMAP" but doesn't provide the absolute NDE values, making it difficult to assess the practical significance.

4. **Theoretical Grounding**:
   - The paper claims the SACs are "novel" but Section 6 shows they are a special case of posterior regularization, which has been previously studied in [4]. The novelty claim is overstated.
   - The paper states "the SAC assumes ∑T t=1 xit ≈ µi0" but doesn't provide a theoretical justification for why this constraint would improve identifiability beyond what's already achieved by posterior regularization.

### Clarity and Presentation
- The paper contains numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing (e.g., "the constraint values µi0 (i = 1, 2, · · · , I) could be obtained from expert knowledge or by experiments" - "could be" should be "can be").
- The mathematical notation is inconsistent and confusing, making it difficult to follow the key contributions.
- The comparison with AFAMAP in Table 1 is misleading because the paper doesn't explain why AFAMAP's performance is better in SAE but worse in NDE.

## Recommendation
This paper addresses a relevant problem with a promising approach, but the technical execution is severely compromised by consistent notation errors, mathematical ambiguities, and insufficient theoretical grounding. The claims of novelty are overstated, and the experimental details are insufficiently explained. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite with consistent notation, clearer mathematical derivations, and more careful justification of methodological choices.

While the results are promising, the technical flaws and lack of rigor in the presentation undermine the paper's scientific contribution. The paper does not provide sufficient evidence to demonstrate that SACs represent a significant advance over existing posterior regularization approaches. Given the high standards for NeurIPS, I recommend rejection due to these substantial technical and presentation issues.

The paper has potential but needs significant revision to address these fundamental problems before it could be considered for publication at NeurIPS.
