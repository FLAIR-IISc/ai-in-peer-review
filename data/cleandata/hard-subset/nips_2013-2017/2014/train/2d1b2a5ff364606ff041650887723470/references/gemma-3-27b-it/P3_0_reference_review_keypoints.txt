- Proposes incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into AFHMMs for energy disaggregation to improve identifiability.  
- Develops a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference in the relaxed AFHMM+SAC model.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: ~45% reduction in NDE vs baseline AFHMM and ~29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Correctly situates work relative to AFHMM literature and methods like posterior regularization, generalized expectation, and constrained HMMs.  
- Novel idea: using known appliance aggregate energy as a practical, well-motivated prior (SACs).  
- Technical derivation and transformation to a convex program are mathematically sound and theoretically grounded.  
- Experimental evaluation is compelling, using both simulated and real-world datasets with appropriate metrics (NDE, SAE).  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to readers familiar with HMMs.  
- Computational complexity is addressed and shown to scale comparably to existing approaches.  
- Main weakness: sensitivity to accuracy of prior aggregate values (µi0); robustness to noisy priors needs more analysis.  
- Limited exploration of richer constraint forms (e.g., time-varying/day vs. night SACs) and no experimental results for these extensions.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent deep learning–based disaggregation methods that predict appliance-level usage directly.  
- Hyperparameter treatment is unclear—lambda was set to 1 without detailed justification or tuning procedure.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with significant empirical improvements; weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add robustness analysis to prior errors, expand/discuss time-varying constraints (ideally with experiments), compare or discuss recent deep-learning methods, and clarify hyperparameter (lambda) selection.
