- Summary: Augments AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (quadratic penalties) and uses auxiliary variables plus integrality relaxation to get a convex quadratic program; links to posterior regularization and reports improved NDE/SAE on HES data with similar runtimes.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on AFHMM/AFAMAP and connects to posterior regularization, generalized expectation, and measurement-based learning, differing in sequence-level aggregate constraints and a tailored convex relaxation.  
- Strength: Application-motivated constraint—expected aggregate usage is natural and often available in BSS/NILM.  
- Strength: Sound optimization—auxiliary variables and relaxation yield a concave quadratic objective under linear constraints (solvable as a convex program).  
- Strength: Clear link to posterior regularization, clarifying statistical interpretation.  
- Strength: Empirical gains—consistent improvements on realistic, low-frequency HES data; toy experiments show sensitivity to prior quality.  
- Strength: Practical extension—discusses multi-window SACs (e.g., day/night) relevant for NILM.  
- Weakness: Rounding/discretization not specified—unclear how fractional solutions are converted to discrete sequences for evaluation.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning missing—λi fixed to 1 with no sensitivity analysis or cross-validation.  
- Weakness: Source of µ0 and fairness—mains-based µ0 uses extra side information not given to AFHMM/AFAMAP baselines, potentially biasing comparisons.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—CVX used without detailed complexity/scale analysis; need discussion of T, I, K, variable counts and solver scalability or first-order alternatives.  
- Weakness: Identifiability and conflict risk—SACs may produce pathological allocations when appliances have similar aggregates or priors are inaccurate; more stress tests needed.  
- Weakness: Missing ablations—no results for relaxed AFHMM without SAC (λ=0) to isolate effects of relaxation vs constraints.  
- Clarity: Generally clear but Section 5 is dense; needs an algorithm box, explicit post-processing/rounding description, and cleaned notation/citations.  
- Quality: Technical formulation and convexity arguments appear sound, but reproducibility is limited by missing rounding and hyperparameter details.  
- Originality: Moderate—SACs are a natural yet novel addition to AFHMMs and the specific convex relaxation is a useful technical contribution.  
- Significance: Moderate-to-high for NILM and other BSS tasks where aggregate priors exist; empirical improvements indicate practical impact.  
- Suggestions: Specify and evaluate rounding method; add sensitivity analyses for λ and µ0; provide ablations and baselines augmented with aggregate priors; include complexity analysis and discuss scalable solvers; clean references/notation.  
- Pros for acceptance: Well-motivated constraint, convex relaxation enabling efficient inference, strong empirical results, and general applicability beyond energy.  
- Cons for acceptance: Missing rounding/hyperparameter details, possible unfair baseline comparisons due to extra side information, and unresolved scalability concerns.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
