This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) framework, specifically targeting the energy disaggregation problem. The core idea – leveraging prior knowledge about expected total energy consumption of appliances – is a compelling and intuitively sound approach to address the inherent identifiability issues in blind source separation. It’s a relatively simple yet potentially powerful constraint that hasn’t been explicitly explored in this form within the AFHMM context, distinguishing it from existing work which focuses on constraining *state transitions* rather than aggregate values. The authors correctly position SACs within the broader landscape of incorporating prior knowledge into probabilistic models.

The technical contribution – formulating the constrained problem as a convex quadratic program – is solid and allows for efficient computation. The connection drawn to posterior regularization further strengthens the theoretical grounding. The empirical results on both simulated and real-world energy data are convincing, demonstrating a substantial (45%) reduction in disaggregation error compared to the standard AFHMM, and a noticeable improvement over a recent state-of-the-art method. The authors acknowledge the limitations surrounding the need for accurate prior knowledge (µi0) and perform experiments exploring the sensitivity to this parameter.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The SAC concept is a genuinely novel approach to addressing the identifiability problem in BSS, and specifically in energy disaggregation. The focus on aggregate constraints, as opposed to state-level constraints, is a significant departure from prior work.
*   **Intuition:** The idea of incorporating known total energy consumption is highly intuitive and directly addresses a key challenge in the disaggregation problem – producing interpretable results.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The formulation of the constrained optimization problem and its relaxation into a computationally tractable convex program are well-executed.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The significant performance improvements demonstrated on real-world data, particularly the 45% reduction in NDE, provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, technical details, and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Prior Knowledge Dependency:** The reliance on accurate prior knowledge (µi0) is a significant limitation. While the paper explores the sensitivity to errors in this prior, the practical challenges of obtaining reliable estimates for all appliances are not fully addressed. The real-world results depend on a somewhat idealized setup where these values are known.
*   **Limited Generalization Discussion:** While the introduction claims the SAC concept is generalizable, the discussion largely remains focused on the energy disaggregation domain. Expanding on potential applications in other BSS contexts (audio processing, bioinformatics) would further strengthen the impact.
*   **Relaxation Impact:** The relaxation of the optimization problem introduces a potential suboptimality. The paper acknowledges this, but a more in-depth analysis of the tightness of the relaxation and the impact on performance would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the SAC concept and its intuitive appeal make this a significant contribution.
*   The technical approach is sound and the results are compelling.
*   The paper clearly identifies the limitations and acknowledges potential areas for future work.
*   The significant performance improvements on real-world data demonstrate practical value.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The strong dependence on accurate prior knowledge limits the immediate applicability of the method.
*   The lack of extensive exploration of applications beyond energy disaggregation diminishes the broader impact.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a novel and promising approach to the challenging problem of blind source separation, specifically energy disaggregation. While the dependence on accurate prior knowledge is a limitation, the significant performance improvements and the inherent intuitiveness of the SAC concept outweigh this concern. This is a bold idea, executed with technical competence, and has the potential to impact the field. I believe the novelty and potential significance justify acceptance despite some minor limitations. I recommend **accepting** this paper.
