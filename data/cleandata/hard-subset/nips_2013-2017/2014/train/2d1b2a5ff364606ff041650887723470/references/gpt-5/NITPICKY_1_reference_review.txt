Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation with additive factorial HMMs (AFHMMs): penalizing deviations of each source’s total signal from a prior total µi0. The authors incorporate SACs into MAP inference for AFHMM by adding quadratic penalties on aggregate sums, introduce auxiliary transition variables H to linearize transition terms, and relax the binary constraints to obtain a concave quadratic objective optimized under linear constraints—solved via CVX. They connect the formulation to posterior regularization. Experiments on toy data and UK Household Electricity Survey (HES) show improved normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) over a basic AFHMM and AFAMAP [Kolter & Jaakkola, AISTATS’12]. Related work is reasonably covered (AFAMAP, duration constraints, semi-Markov, posterior regularization), though some important baselines/extensions are missing in experiments.

Quality
- Formulation: With Λi = λi µ∗i µ∗Ti and Vt = σ−2t μ→μ→T (both PSD rank-1), the quadratic terms enter L(S,H) with negative signs, making the objective concave in the relaxed variables. This justifies solving a convex program by maximizing a concave quadratic (equivalently minimizing a convex quadratic). However:
  - The paper says “convex quadratic program” while presenting a maximization; it should explicitly state the sign flip used for CVX (which minimizes).
  - “Equivalent” to the original discrete MAP is only true before relaxation; after relaxing Sit, hit to [0,1], the solution need not be integral. There is no discussion of rounding/projection to discrete states, nor clarity on whether evaluation uses soft assignments (x̂it = μi⊤Sit) or rounded sequences. This is critical for fairness vs discrete baselines and reproducibility.
  - The constraints on H and S ensure row/column sum consistency, but in the relaxed problem they allow fractional flows; no analysis of relaxation tightness or conditions under which integral solutions arise.
- Statistical/hyperparameter details are insufficient:
  - λi are fixed to 1 everywhere; there is no tuning nor sensitivity analysis. Claims of state-of-the-art improvement should control for hyperparameter tuning across methods.
  - σt^2 estimation is omitted; this variance directly affects quadratic weighting Vt and u_t. Clarify how σt is set/estimated.
  - Transition probabilities/log terms imply log p(i)jk; handling of zeros (smoothing) is not described.
  - The choice of Ki = 3 for all appliances is arbitrary; no justification or sensitivity testing.
- Experiments:
  - The toy example is minimal (I=2, T=100) and explores only the effect of µ0 misspecification, not noise levels, number of chains/states, or mis-specified model parameters.
  - For HES without mains, NTC is computed “across all households in the data set,” which risks leakage if test households contribute to NTC priors. For the “with mains” case, NTC is from training data, which is appropriate; ensure the same rigor for the first setting.
  - Statistical testing: paired t-tests are mentioned, but without variances, effect sizes, or multiple-comparison considerations across houses/days. Figures/tables referred to (Tables 1–3, Fig. 2) are not provided here; ensure they include detailed statistics, confidence intervals, and per-house variability.
  - Compute time: claims that AFHMM, AFAMAP, and AFHMM+SAC “scale similarly” and have “similar time” need concrete numbers. CVX-based QP with O(T∑iKi + (T−1)∑iKi^2) variables can be heavy; report wall-clock times, solver settings, and complexity vs T, I, Ki.
- Theoretical framing: The posterior regularization link is sketchy; PR typically yields a penalized log-likelihood with expected constraints. Here, the MAP objective uses the constraint applied to S, not its expectation; the connection should be made precise (e.g., limiting case of delta posterior/MAP). Uniqueness claims for P̃∗ should be qualified.

Clarity
- The paper is generally readable but contains notation and formatting issues:
  - Several typos and inconsistencies (“equavalent,” “distibution,” missing indices, inconsistent bold/arrow notation).
  - eT is defined as a T×1 vector with first element 1 and others 0—confusing notation; traditionally e denotes all-ones.
  - The time-index ranges for H constraints should be explicit (t = 2,…,T). The initial-state handling via π̃i is awkward; consider cleaner factorization.
  - The extension to multiple time-window constraints uses undefined symbols (τ(i)j) and unclear indices; needs revision.
  - References are poorly formatted and incomplete (missing years, venues, page numbers; duplicate/garbled author entries). This is not NeurIPS style compliant.

Originality
- The idea of imposing priors on aggregate statistics and casting AFHMM inference as a relaxed QP is a sensible and, to my knowledge, novel combination in this context. The relation to posterior regularization is appropriate, though related “learning from measurements” (Liang et al.) and generalized expectation are cited.
- That said, aggregate constraints per chain are a conceptually straightforward regularizer; the main novelty is the particular convex relaxation for AFHMM+SAC.

Significance
- Energy disaggregation is important, and low-frequency HES is a challenging dataset. If validated rigorously, an approach that exploits aggregate priors to improve NDE/SAE is useful to practitioners.
- However, the current empirical evidence is not fully convincing due to the lack of hyperparameter tuning fairness, limited robustness studies, unclear rounding/evaluation procedure, and potential leakage in NTC computation in one setting. The method’s reliance on reasonably accurate µ0 is shown in toy data, but robustness on real data (where µ0 can be badly off) is not analyzed.

Pros
- Clear motivation: aggregate priors are available in many domains.
- Sound convex relaxation (objective is concave quadratic under PSD terms).
- Demonstrates improvements on a realistic dataset and task.
- Connection to posterior regularization.

Cons
- Missing details on rounding/decoding from relaxed solution; unclear evaluation protocol.
- No hyperparameter tuning/sensitivity (λi, Ki, σt^2); unclear fairness vs baselines.
- Limited ablations and robustness analysis.
- Potential leakage in NTC computation for the “without mains” setting.
- Scalability claims lack concrete evidence; reliance on CVX may limit practicality.
- Numerous notation/formatting/reference issues; not up to NeurIPS standards.

Suggestions for improvement
- Precisely describe post-processing from relaxed S,H to discrete sequences (or justify soft evaluation) and compare both.
- Provide sensitivity analyses for λi, Ki, σt^2, and misspecified µ0 on real data.
- Ensure NTC priors are computed without test leakage; consider household-specific vs population priors.
- Report detailed runtime and memory vs T, I, Ki; compare to a tuned AFAMAP implementation.
- Add baselines with duration/one-change constraints combined with SAC to isolate SAC’s contribution.
- Tightness analysis or empirical integrality rates of the relaxation; if needed, add cutting planes or rounding schemes with guarantees.
- Fix notation and fully correct references per NeurIPS style.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The idea is promising and likely useful, but the current submission has important methodological and empirical gaps, and significant clarity/formatting issues that detract from its reliability and reproducibility.
