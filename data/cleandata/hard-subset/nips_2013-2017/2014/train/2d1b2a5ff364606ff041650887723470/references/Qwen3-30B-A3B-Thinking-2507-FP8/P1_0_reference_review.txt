

# Peer Review: "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

## Summary
This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS) problems, with a focus on energy disaggregation. The authors address the critical identifiability problem in BSS by incorporating domain knowledge about expected aggregate values of source signals (e.g., total daily energy consumption of appliances). They integrate SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM), transforming the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) that can be efficiently solved despite the non-Markovian nature of the constraints. The approach is evaluated on synthetic data and real-world energy disaggregation data from the Household Electricity Survey (HES), demonstrating significant improvements over baseline AFHMM and a state-of-the-art AFAMAP approach.

The paper relates well to previous work on energy disaggregation [8, 12, 22] and posterior regularization [4], positioning SACs as a novel class of constraints that complement rather than replace existing approaches that constrain HMM state transitions.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound and well-executed. The SAC formulation is logically motivated by the need to address identifiability in BSS, with clear examples from energy disaggregation. The transformation of the constrained optimization problem into a CQP is mathematically rigorous and well-justified. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using both synthetic data to validate the method's robustness to constraint specification errors and real-world data to demonstrate practical impact. The paper acknowledges limitations (e.g., the need for prior knowledge about expected aggregates) and demonstrates SACs' robustness to moderate errors in constraint values (Section 7.1). The use of both normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) provides a balanced evaluation, and statistical significance testing (paired t-tests) strengthens the claims about performance improvements.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The problem statement, technical contributions, and experimental results are presented logically with consistent mathematical notation. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is particularly helpful for readers familiar with that framework. The paper would benefit from slightly more detail on how constraint values μi0 are practically obtained beyond "national surveys" (though this is acknowledged as a limitation), but this is a minor point given the focus on the technical contribution.

## Originality
The core idea of SACs represents a novel contribution to BSS literature. While previous work has incorporated constraints on HMM state transitions (e.g., AFAMAP [12] constrains at most one chain to change state at a time), SACs operate on aggregate values across the entire sequence, representing a fundamentally different class of constraints. The transformation to a CQP to handle these non-Markovian constraints is a significant technical contribution that appears to be new. The paper clearly differentiates itself from related work in Section 2 and demonstrates how SACs complement rather than replace previous constraint approaches.

## Significance
The significance of this work is substantial. Energy disaggregation is an important application domain with clear real-world impact, and the demonstrated 29% improvement over AFAMAP (a state-of-the-art method) is significant. The approach is generalizable to other BSS problems beyond energy disaggregation, making it potentially valuable for other domains. The paper successfully bridges the gap between theoretical modeling (AFHMM) and practical application, showing how domain knowledge can be effectively incorporated into probabilistic models to improve performance.

## Strengths
1. Clear and compelling motivation for SACs addressing the identifiability problem in BSS
2. Novel technical contribution of transforming SAC-constrained optimization into a CQP
3. Comprehensive evaluation showing substantial performance improvements on real-world data
4. Clear connection to posterior regularization framework
5. Good balance between theoretical and practical contributions

## Weaknesses
1. The paper could provide more detail on practical challenges of obtaining accurate constraint values μi0 for different applications (beyond "national surveys")
2. The computational complexity analysis is somewhat brief, though the paper does note polynomial scaling
3. The extension to multiple time periods (Section 4.2) is mentioned but not explored in experiments

## Recommendation
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The technical approach is novel and well-executed, and the empirical results demonstrate significant improvements over previous methods. The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a compelling case for the value of signal aggregate constraints. I recommend acceptance.

## Minor Suggestions
1. Consider adding a brief discussion on practical challenges of obtaining accurate constraint values μi0 for different applications.
2. In Section 7.2.2, clarify why the results for "Energy Data with Mains Readings" show larger absolute improvements than "Energy Data without Mains Readings" (Table 1 vs Table 3).
3. The paper could briefly mention how SACs might interact with other constraint approaches (e.g., AFAMAP's constraint on state changes).

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. It is technically sound, well-written, and demonstrates clear practical impact. I believe it would be a valuable addition to NIPS proceedings.
