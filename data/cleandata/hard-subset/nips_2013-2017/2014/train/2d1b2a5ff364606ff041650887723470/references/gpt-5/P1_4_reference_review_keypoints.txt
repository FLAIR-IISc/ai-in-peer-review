- Paper addresses blind source separation for additive sources modeled by AFHMMs, introducing signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to match per-source aggregate targets over time.
- SACs are implemented as quadratic penalties on aggregate deviations, producing non-Markovian coupling across time steps.
- Authors introduce auxiliary transition variables and relax the integer problem to obtain a concave quadratic objective with linear constraints, solvable as a convex QP.
- The formulation can be derived from Posterior Regularization, connecting SACs to established regularization frameworks.
- Empirically, AFHMM+SAC improves performance on synthetic data as aggregate priors approach ground truth.
- On UK-DALE low-frequency electricity data, AFHMM+SAC yields significant NDE and SAE reductions versus AFHMM and AFAMAP, with similar runtime.
- The relaxation construction is technically sound, leveraging PSD structure to make the problem convex.
- Important practical details are missing: how discrete states are recovered from relaxed solutions and whether evaluations use soft or discretized outputs.
- Missing methodological details on HMM parameter estimation (transitions/initials); only k-means for emissions is described.
- Sensitivity analysis to the penalty weight λ is lacking beyond a single fixed value.
- Empirical comparisons are limited to AFHMM and AFAMAP; no comparison to semi-Markov/duration models or newer NILM baselines.
- Robustness to mis-specified aggregates is only shown on a toy example, not on real data.
- Paper is generally readable but notation is heavy and contains typos; convexity/concavity arguments could be stated more clearly.
- Some notational choices (e.g., eT definition) are nonstandard and could be clarified.
- Experimental setup needs more detail: transition estimation, train/test splits, time-step granularity, handling of unknown loads in mains data, and whether outputs are hard/soft.
- The SAC idea is novel and useful; linking it to Posterior Regularization is appropriate though not itself novel.
- SACs are complementary to prior AFHMM constraints like duration models and one-change constraints.
- The approach is significant for NILM and general additive BSS where aggregate priors are available, but scalability to long horizons and many sources is not demonstrated.
- Strengths: well-motivated constraint class, sound convex relaxation, PR interpretation, and strong empirical gains on realistic data.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: clarify rounding/inference, perform λ sensitivity analysis, compare to more baselines, test robustness to biased aggregates, and report scalability and solver details.
- Specific requests: describe discrete sequence recovery, transition estimation method, sensitivity to λ and µ0, combining SACs with AFAMAP constraints, and typical problem sizes and solver timings.
- Pros for acceptance: practical novel constraint, principled convex approach, and strong empirical evidence with significance testing.
- Cons for acceptance: missing methodological/experimental details, limited baselines and robustness checks, and incomplete exploration of scalability and hyperparameter selection.
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing clarity, evaluation, and experimental gaps.
