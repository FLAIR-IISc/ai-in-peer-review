- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability and performance of AFHMMs for energy disaggregation by incorporating known aggregate signal values.
- Formulates a relaxed optimization solved as a convex quadratic program, implemented with CVX.
- Evaluated on toy and real HES data, reporting large improvements (45% reduction vs AFHMM, 29% vs a recent state-of-the-art).
- Positions work within BSS/NILM literature, relating to AFHMM variants and posterior regularization; focuses on what priors to use.
- Novel idea: using aggregate signal information is an intuitive and practically motivated way to address identifiability in BSS.
- Technical formulation appears sound and grounded theoretically via connections to posterior regularization.
- Strong empirical evidence supports the method’s effectiveness on both synthetic and real data.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and mostly accessible mathematically.
- Authors discuss computational complexity and show the CQP scales polynomially with favorable runtime comparisons.
- Sensitivity analysis is limited—regularization parameter (λ) effects and systematic selection are not thoroughly explored.
- Real-world dataset description (households, monitored appliances, preprocessing) is too brief for full reproducibility.
- Generalization to multi-period constraints is mentioned but not empirically or thoroughly analyzed.
- Comparisons are limited; broader benchmarks (e.g., deep-learning NILM methods) are missing.
- Identifiability implications of SACs are not deeply analyzed; potential scenarios where constraints could worsen identifiability are not addressed.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with valuable idea and solid results, but needs minor revisions on analysis, dataset detail, and broader comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking for expanded λ sensitivity study, more dataset/preprocessing details, exploration of generalized constraints, and discussion of limitations.
