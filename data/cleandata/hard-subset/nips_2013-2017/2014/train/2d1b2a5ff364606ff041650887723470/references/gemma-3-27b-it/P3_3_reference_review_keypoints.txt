- Summary: Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) in Additive Factorial HMMs to improve identifiability for energy disaggregation, solved via a convex quadratic program (CQP).
- Main result: CQP-based SACs yield large empirical gains—45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. base AFHMM and 29% vs. a state-of-the-art method on real HES data.
- Related work: Well-situated among energy disaggregation, constrained AFHMM variants, and connects to posterior regularization and aggregate constraints in probabilistic databases.
- Novelty: SACs offer a principled, practically useful way to inject domain prior knowledge into AFHMMs to address blind source separation identifiability.
- Technical soundness: Derivation of the relaxed CQP is mathematically solid and implemented efficiently (CVX).
- Empirical strength: Convincing experiments on challenging low-frequency real-world data with strong baselines (AFAMAP).
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear exposition.
- Theoretical grounding: Links to posterior regularization provide a strong Bayesian interpretation for SACs.
- Weakness — sensitivity analysis: Needs more thorough exploration of the hyperparameter λ’s effect on real-world performance.
- Weakness — prior dependence: Relies on known total energy µi0 from surveys; impact of errors in µi0 is insufficiently evaluated.
- Weakness — generalizability: Evaluation is confined to energy disaggregation; broader BSS applications (e.g., audio) are not tested.
- Weakness — computational cost: Lacks detailed complexity analysis (scaling with number of states/appliances).
- Weakness — relaxation impact: Limited discussion of how the relaxation may introduce suboptimality or affect results.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses identifiability with a novel, theoretically grounded method and strong empirical improvements using an efficient CQP.
- Arguments against acceptance: Single-domain evaluation and insufficient analysis of sensitivity to priors and relaxation effects.
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution with some gaps in sensitivity, generalizability, and computational discussion.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add sensitivity analysis for λ and discussion/experiments on errors in µi0.
