- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to constrain source signal aggregates for identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) and reformulates the constrained problem as a convex quadratic program.  
- Reports large empirical gains: ~45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error over AFHMM and ~29% improvement over AFAMAP [12] on real data.  
- Method is technically sound with clear theoretical grounding and correct identification of the BSS identifiability issue.  
- Provides a clear connection to posterior regularization, giving theoretical justification for the approach.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive (synthetic and real-world experiments) using appropriate metrics (NDE and SAE).  
- Computational complexity analysis is reasonable, with the CQP formulation scaling polynomially in time steps and states.  
- Requires prior knowledge of aggregate values (SACs), which is a valid limitation of the approach.  
- Sensitivity to constraint parameters (δi or λi) is underanalyzed; synthetic experiments partially address this but a more systematic study is needed.  
- Mathematical formulation and optimization derivation are precise but Section 5 is somewhat dense.  
- Presentation and experimental figures/tables are clear and well-organized.  
- Discussion of applying SACs to BSS domains beyond energy disaggregation is limited and could be expanded.  
- Novelty lies in focusing on aggregate-value constraints rather than temporal transition constraints, extending prior work to HMM-based BSS.  
- Connection to related aggregate-constraint work (e.g., probabilistic databases) is noted, but the HMM extension is novel.  
- Potential loss of accuracy due to relaxation of integer constraints to convex form is a noted weakness.  
- Missing comparisons to other constraint-based BSS approaches (e.g., k-segment constraints [19]).  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with practical significance for energy disaggregation; recommendation to accept.  
- Meets NIPS criteria with high scores for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
