- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation for AFHMMs by enforcing known aggregate values of source signals.  
- Formulates SACs as constraints and transforms the resulting optimization into a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. AFHMM baseline and 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization and situates the method within existing probabilistic modeling frameworks.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using synthetic data to test robustness to constraint errors and real-world energy disaggregation datasets.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound and the CQP formulation addresses key computational challenges.  
- Paper is very clear, well-organized, and accessible to ML/probabilistic modeling readers; notation and presentation are consistent.  
- Novelty lies in identifying a practical, widely-available constraint type (aggregate values) and efficiently incorporating it into AFHMMs.  
- SAC framework is general and potentially applicable to other BSS problems beyond energy disaggregation.  
- Strength: strong technical contribution with clear practical impact on a real-world application.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation and explicit discussion of limitations.  
- Weakness: limited comparison with other constraint-based approaches (e.g., AFAMAP) and how SACs interact with them.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity (λi) not explored; experiments use λi=1 without guidance on selection.  
- Weakness: extension to multi-period constraints mentioned but not experimentally investigated.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of scenarios where aggregate domain knowledge is unavailable or inaccurate.  
- Recommendation: accept—strong, well-grounded contribution with significant empirical improvements and broad applicability.
