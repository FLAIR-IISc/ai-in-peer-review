## Peer Review: Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) as a novel prior for Blind Source Separation (BSS) problems, specifically within the framework of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs). The authors observe that in many BSS applications, prior knowledge about the total energy or value of each source signal exists and can be leveraged to improve identifiability. They formulate SACs to enforce this knowledge, leading to a computationally efficient convex quadratic program for inference.  The method is evaluated on both simulated data and a real-world energy disaggregation dataset (HES), demonstrating a significant performance improvement compared to baseline AFHMM and a state-of-the-art alternative (AFAMAP).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature on BSS and energy disaggregation. It acknowledges the challenges of identifiability in BSS and the existing approaches to incorporating prior knowledge like posterior regularization and measurement-based learning.  The authors clearly differentiate their work by focusing on *what* kind of prior knowledge to incorporate, rather than the general mechanisms for doing so. They also accurately cite relevant work in energy disaggregation, including AFAMAP and semi-Markov models. The connection to probabilistic databases with aggregate constraints is noted, but appropriately dismissed due to the simplicity of models considered in that work.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a principled way to incorporate domain knowledge is a valuable contribution. It addresses a key limitation of existing BSS approaches – the lack of effective mechanisms to guide identifiability.
* **Practicality:** The formulation of the SAC-constrained optimization problem as a convex quadratic program is a significant strength. This allows for efficient inference, a critical factor for real-world applications like energy disaggregation.
* **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental evaluation, particularly on the challenging HES dataset, provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  The comparison with AFAMAP, a strong baseline, is particularly convincing.  The inclusion of both normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) provides a comprehensive evaluation.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core ideas. The mathematical notation is reasonably accessible, and the connection to posterior regularization provides a broader theoretical context.
* **Thorough Analysis:** The ablation study on the toy data, varying the accuracy of the prior knowledge (µ0), is insightful and demonstrates the robustness of the approach. The computational complexity discussion is also valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Parameter Tuning:** The paper briefly mentions the tuning parameter δi (and corresponding λi), but doesn’t dedicate sufficient space to discussing how these parameters were chosen in practice. Cross-validation is mentioned, but more detail on the procedure would be beneficial.  A sensitivity analysis to these parameters would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Generalizability of SACs:** While the authors acknowledge the potential to generalize SACs to multiple constraints over time periods, the current implementation uses only a single constraint per chain. A more in-depth discussion of the tradeoffs associated with different constraint configurations (e.g., computational cost vs. accuracy) would be valuable.
* **Limited Scope of Comparison:**  While comparison to AFAMAP is strong, comparing to more recent disaggregation techniques (beyond those cited in the related work) might offer a more comprehensive benchmark.
* **Identifiability Discussion:** The paper acknowledges the identifiability problem but doesn't fully explore the implications of the relaxation used to achieve computational efficiency. A deeper discussion of how the relaxation impacts the theoretical guarantees of the solution would be helpful.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete piece of work. The theoretical justification is solid, and the experimental results are convincing.  The relaxation step is a reasonable trade-off for computational tractability, but its implications warrant further discussion.
* **Clarity (9/10):** The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are concise yet comprehensive.
* **Originality (8/10):** The introduction of SACs is a novel and significant contribution. While AFHMMs are well-established, the integration of domain-specific constraints in this manner is a new approach.
* **Significance (9/10):** The results are important and have the potential to be widely adopted in the field of energy disaggregation and potentially other BSS applications. The 45% reduction in NDE is a substantial improvement.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Introduces a novel and effective method (SACs) for incorporating prior knowledge into BSS problems.
* Provides a computationally efficient solution (convex quadratic program).
* Demonstrates significant performance improvements on a challenging real-world dataset.
* Is well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly evaluated.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited discussion of parameter tuning and sensitivity analysis.
* Scope of comparison could be broadened to include more recent disaggregation techniques.
* Deeper exploration of the implications of the relaxation step for identifiability is needed.

**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**  This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding parameter tuning and a more thorough discussion of the relaxation, would further strengthen the manuscript and increase its impact. I believe that this paper is well-suited for NIPS.
