This paper addresses the problem of blind source separation, specifically within the context of energy disaggregation, and proposes a novel approach incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM). The motivation – improving identifiability and interpretability of source signals – is well-articulated, and the problem setting is clearly defined. The authors connect their work to existing literature on probabilistic modeling and BSS, and position SACs as a generalizable approach applicable across various domains. 

However, while the core idea of SACs is sensible and the reported results demonstrate improvement over baselines, the paper suffers from a surprising number of minor but persistent flaws that detract from its overall quality. The presentation, while generally clear, is often imprecise and lacks sufficient rigor. Several areas require significant polishing before the paper would be suitable for NIPS.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a method for incorporating domain knowledge into AFHMMs is a worthwhile contribution. It provides a principled way to leverage prior information about expected signal aggregates.
* **Empirical Results:** The reported performance gains (45% decrease in NDE and 29% improvement over AFAMAP) on real-world energy disaggregation data are substantial and demonstrate the practical benefit of the proposed approach.
* **Connection to Existing Work:** The authors appropriately situate their work within the broader context of BSS, energy disaggregation, and probabilistic modeling.
* **Mathematical Formulation:** The conversion to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference is a significant practical achievement.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Rigor in Mathematical Notation:** Numerous inconsistencies and imprecise definitions plague the mathematical formulations. For example, the Kronecker product notation appears abruptly without prior definition, and the meaning of several vector/matrix notations (e.g., µ∗i, Vt) is not immediately clear even after reading the preceding text. The definitions seem to evolve or are simply assumed.
* **Inconsistent Variable Usage:** Variables are sometimes redefined without explicit notice, causing confusion.  For instance, the meaning of 'δi' shifts between a tuning parameter and being related to the complexity parameter 'λi'. 
* **Insufficient Justification for Relaxations:** The relaxation of the integer constraints in the CQP is stated, but the potential impact of this relaxation on solution optimality isn't adequately discussed. The authors acknowledge it isn't optimal but don’t sufficiently explain *why* or if there’s any analysis of the error introduced.
* **Overly Brief Discussion of Hyperparameter Tuning:**  The paper states that λi = 1 was used for all experiments, but provides no justification or exploration of alternative tuning strategies. Cross-validation is mentioned but not performed. The sensitivity of performance to λi is unknown.
* **Limited Ablation Study:** While the comparison to AFAMAP is valuable, a more detailed ablation study exploring the contribution of individual components of the AFHMM+SAC (e.g., the specific form of the SAC constraint) would strengthen the analysis.
* **Redundant Sections:** The "Relation to Posterior Regularization" section largely re-derives known results and doesn't add significant insight beyond what's already established in the field. It feels somewhat tacked on.
* **Formatting Issues:** Several minor formatting inconsistencies are present (e.g., inconsistent spacing in equations, awkward phrasing). While not major, they contribute to the impression of a rushed or carelessly prepared manuscript.
* **Missing Details in Experiments:** The description of the HES data is somewhat cursory. More specifics about the appliances monitored and the data preprocessing steps would be beneficial.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:** The core idea is novel, the empirical results are compelling, and the mathematical formulation demonstrates a viable solution.

**Con:** The paper suffers from a significant number of minor flaws – inconsistencies in notation, lack of rigor, insufficient discussion of hyperparameter tuning, and unnecessary redundancy – that undermine its overall quality. The level of polish expected for NIPS is not currently met.



**Overall Assessment:**

I am leaning towards **rejection**. While the core contribution of SACs is promising, the numerous flaws in presentation and the lack of complete rigor significantly detract from the paper’s quality.  Addressing these issues would require substantial revision. The current manuscript feels like a preliminary report rather than a polished NIPS submission. The authors should focus on meticulous editing, clarifying the mathematical notation, and conducting a more thorough experimental analysis.
