- Paper adds per-source sum (Signal Aggregate Constraints, SACs) to additive FHMMs to improve identifiability for NILM.
- Authors enforce SACs via quadratic penalties and derive a convex quadratic program (CQP) by relaxing transition indicators.
- They connect the penalized objective to posterior regularization (KL-projection) for interpretation.
- Experiments on synthetic and UK HES ultra-low-rate data show substantial NDE and SAE improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Technical formulation and derivation to a CQP are sound; PSD quadratic terms and linear transition terms are correct.
- Missing detail: how fractional relaxed solutions are mapped back to discrete state sequences or used to compute x̂it.
- Hyperparameters λi fixed to 1 without tuning, hiding sensitivity and potential bias in comparisons.
- Toy study shows performance is sensitive to accuracy of SAC targets µi0, raising robustness concerns.
- HES experiments are valuable, but statistical claims need more detail (p-values, confidence intervals).
- Some relevant stronger baselines (semi-Markov NILM, recent methods) are cited but not compared empirically.
- Paper is generally readable but notation-heavy; a variable/shape table would improve clarity.
- Clarify rounding/projection from relaxed S,H to discrete states and how x̂it is computed from fractional S.
- Clarify treatment and estimation of σt.
- Extension to multiple time-window constraints is reasonable; note that they add PSD terms to the CQP.
- Novelty: per-chain aggregate constraints in additive FHMMs plus convex relaxation appears original.
- Practical significance is high for low-frequency NILM, but depends on availability and accuracy of µi0.
- Strength: simple, interpretable prior directly addressing identifiability.
- Strength: principled convex relaxation solvable with off-the-shelf solvers and linked to posterior regularization.
- Strength: demonstrated empirical gains on challenging low-rate data and extendable to multi-window SACs.
- Weakness: no specified rounding/decoding procedure or evaluation of discrete outputs vs soft outputs.
- Weakness: scalability concerns using CVX for long sequences; need complexity, runtimes, hardware, or specialized solvers.
- Recommendations: evaluate rounding methods, tune/explore λi selection, discuss/prioritize SAC acquisition methods, include stronger baselines, and provide reproducibility details (code, solver settings, σt, training days).
- Key author questions: how are S,H rounded; how are σt and λi chosen and validated; how often µi0 misestimates true totals and impact; can SACs combine with AFAMAP constraints?
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — promising, principled idea with real-data gains but needs better treatment of decoding, robustness, baselines, and scalability.
