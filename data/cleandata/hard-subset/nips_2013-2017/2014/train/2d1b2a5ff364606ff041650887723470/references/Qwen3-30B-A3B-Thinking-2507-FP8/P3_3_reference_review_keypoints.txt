- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for additive factorial HMMs to incorporate domain knowledge (e.g., total appliance energy) into blind source separation.
- Reformulates the problem as a convex quadratic program that can be solved efficiently and scales similarly to existing methods.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on energy disaggregation: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement vs AFAMAP, validated on synthetic and real HES data.
- Connects the SAC approach to the posterior regularization framework, providing theoretical grounding.
- Well-motivated application (avoiding "franken-appliance" outputs) where aggregate domain knowledge is naturally available.
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the convex relaxation’s quality or bounds on relaxation-induced error, raising identifiability concerns.
- Uses λi = 1 in experiments without tuning or sensitivity analysis, and reports results with "true" total consumption (TTC) that are unrealistic in practice.
- Related work coverage is incomplete; misses comparisons to methods using appliance power profiles or duration constraints and overclaims generality beyond energy disaggregation.
- Does not sufficiently address practical limitations of SACs, including how to obtain accurate aggregates or handle uncertain/misaligned constraints (performance degrades when aggregates deviate).
- Overstates significance by implying AFAMAP is state-of-the-art for the task without fully justifying that claim.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and practically significant, but would benefit from theoretical guarantees, hyperparameter tuning, and a nuanced discussion of practical constraints.
- Recommendation: Accept, with revisions to address theoretical, experimental tuning, and related-work/practical limitation concerns.
