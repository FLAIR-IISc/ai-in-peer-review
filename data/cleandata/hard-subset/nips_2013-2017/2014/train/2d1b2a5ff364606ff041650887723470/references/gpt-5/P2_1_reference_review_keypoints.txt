- Paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation of a single aggregated time series and applies them to AFHMMs.  
- Derives a convex quadratic program (relaxation of discrete variables with transition indicators) to handle non‑Markovian aggregate constraints and solves via CVX.  
- Shows SAC objective is an instance of posterior regularization (PR), connecting to prior constrained-latent-variable frameworks.  
- Empirical results on toy data and HES energy dataset show substantial improvements in NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP with paired t-tests.  
- Convexity argument is sound: quadratic terms are negative semidefinite and constraints linear after relaxation, yielding a convex optimization.  
- Transition-indicator trick enforces Markov consistency in the integer case and becomes linear consistency constraints in the relaxation.  
- Toy experiment sensibly examines sensitivity to mismatch in aggregate targets µ0.  
- Missing/unclear details: how fractional S from the relaxation are rounded or decoded to discrete states and interpretability implications.  
- Missing/unclear details: hyperparameter choices—λi fixed to 1, noise variances, and AFAMAP tuning—raise fairness concerns in comparisons.  
- Scalability concerns: solving CQPs via CVX at minute-level T and many appliances needs quantified runtimes, problem sizes, solver tolerances and iteration counts.  
- Completeness: focuses on MAP inference with fixed θ (k‑means for emissions, simple transition estimates); stronger baselines (duration models, tuned AFAMAP) and ablations (windowed SACs) are missing.  
- Clarity: high-level ideas are clear but notation is dense with typos; an algorithmic summary and clearer convexity/constraint exposition would aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel identification of appliance-level totals as reusable side information and a tailored convex relaxation for AFHMMs with aggregate constraints.  
- Significance: practical impact is promising (29–45% gains on real low-frequency data) and idea likely generalizes beyond energy to other domains.  
- Limitation: dependence on quality of µ0 and potential loss of discrete interpretability due to relaxation; robustness to biased priors not fully tested.  
- Pros: well-motivated idea, sound convex formulation, connection to PR, significant empirical gains, and simple implementation with off‑the‑shelf solvers.  
- Cons: reliance on CVX (scalability), limited hyperparameter tuning, lack of decoding/rounding discussion, narrow evaluation metrics (no event-level/per-appliance breakdowns), minor clarity issues.  
- Suggested experiments/analyses: clarify rounding/decoding, report runtime scaling and solver details or use specialized solvers, tune λi and test sensitivity/uncertainty-aware priors, evaluate windowed SACs and robustness to biased µ0, compare to duration/semi‑Markov and better‑tuned AFAMAP.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — novel, principled, and effective approach with convincing gains but concerns about scalability, baseline tuning, and decoding clarity.
