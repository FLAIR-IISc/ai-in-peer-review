- Overall: Compelling, well-motivated paper introducing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into AFHMMs; technically sound and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.

- Problem & approach: Tackles energy disaggregation (BSS) by encoding expected aggregate usage per source as SACs within an AFHMM and solving inference via a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation.

- Empirical claim: Demonstrates substantial improvements (≈45% reduction vs baseline AFHMM, ≈29% vs AFAMAP) on real-world HES dataset and simulated data.

- Relation to prior work: Properly situates among energy disaggregation and constrained HMM literature, distinguishing its focus on what constraints to impose and connecting to posterior regularization and measurement-based learning.

- Strength — motivation: Clear articulation of identifiability issues and why aggregate constraints are needed (avoids “franken-appliance” solutions).

- Strength — methodology: SACs are a logical domain-knowledge incorporation and the CQP relaxation provides an efficient tractable solution.

- Strength — evaluation breadth: Thorough evaluation on simulated and real data, including analysis of constraint value effects.

- Strength — computation & writing: Computational cost comparable to AFAMAP/AFHMM and paper is well-written and organized.

- Weakness — constraint selection: Limited discussion on how to automatically learn or refine µ0 values beyond brief mention of cross-validation.

- Weakness — sensitivity: Method shows sensitivity to accuracy of µ0; lacks deeper robustness analysis to noisy/uncertain aggregate estimates.

- Weakness — model assumptions: Reliance on AFHMM assumptions (factorization, Markov property) not deeply examined for real-world limitations.

- Weakness — generalizability: Evaluation focused on energy disaggregation; broader applicability to other BSS domains remains unproven.

- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem, proposes an effective and novel application of constraints, shows strong empirical gains, and is technically solid.

- Against acceptance: Narrow application focus, limited treatment of constraint selection/robustness, and dependence on AFHMM assumptions.

- Recommendations: Expand discussion of automatic/uncertain constraint selection, analyze robustness to constraint errors, acknowledge AFHMM limitations, and briefly discuss applications beyond energy disaggregation.

- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
