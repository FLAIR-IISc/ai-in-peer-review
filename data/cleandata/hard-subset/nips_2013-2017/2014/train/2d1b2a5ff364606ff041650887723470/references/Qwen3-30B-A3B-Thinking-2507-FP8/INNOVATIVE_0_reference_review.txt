

# Review: Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation

This paper introduces "Signal Aggregate Constraints" (SACs), a novel approach to address the identifiability problem in blind source separation (BSS), particularly in the energy disaggregation domain. The authors observe that in many BSS applications, domain knowledge about the total energy consumption of appliances (e.g., "a toaster uses about 50 Wh daily") can be leveraged to improve source separation. SACs constrain the aggregate values of source signals to be close to specified values, transforming the problem into a convex quadratic program that can be efficiently solved.

The paper makes a significant contribution by identifying a class of constraints that are both practically useful across domains and computationally feasible. While posterior regularization and other constraint-based approaches exist, this work specifically addresses the question of *what types of constraints* should be used, which has been overlooked in previous literature. The SAC concept is clearly motivated by the energy disaggregation application, where interpretability of results is critical—showing a "franken-appliance" would undermine user trust.

The authors demonstrate substantial empirical improvements: a 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) compared to the baseline AFHMM, and a 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art approach (AFAMAP) on real-world energy data. The theoretical grounding in posterior regularization (Section 6) is a nice touch that strengthens the methodological foundation.

This work stands out for its bold conceptual contribution. While the convex relaxation of the original problem is a minor technical limitation, the paper clearly acknowledges this and demonstrates that the relaxed solution still provides significant practical benefits. The authors avoid the trap of incremental improvements—they're not just tweaking an existing model, but introducing a fundamentally new constraint mechanism that addresses a core challenge in BSS.

The paper is well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the problem, methodology, and results. The evaluation on both toy data (showing robustness to constraint specification errors) and real-world energy data (with both national survey-based and oracle constraints) provides strong evidence for the approach's utility.

Minor weaknesses include:
1. Limited exploration of how to obtain constraint values (µi0) in practice beyond national surveys, which is a significant practical challenge.
2. The paper doesn't fully explore the sensitivity of results to constraint values beyond the toy data experiment.
3. The generalizability claim to other domains is somewhat underdeveloped, with most evaluation focused on energy disaggregation.

However, these are minor concerns compared to the paper's substantial contribution. The SAC concept is novel, well-motivated, and demonstrates significant practical impact. The authors have clearly identified an important gap in the literature and provided a compelling solution that addresses a real-world problem where interpretability matters.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold conceptual contributions over methodological perfection, I find this paper to be a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS. The SAC framework represents a significant new direction for blind source separation that could inspire future work across multiple domains. The 45% improvement in NDE is not just incremental—it's a substantial advance that would likely be adopted by practitioners in energy disaggregation.

The paper's strengths (novel constraint mechanism, significant empirical results, clear theoretical grounding) far outweigh its minor weaknesses. It addresses a fundamental problem in BSS with a creative and practical solution, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Recommendation:** Accept

**Strengths:**
- Novel concept of Signal Aggregate Constraints for BSS
- Addresses the critical identifiability problem with practical domain knowledge
- Significant empirical improvements (45% NDE reduction over baseline)
- Clear theoretical grounding in posterior regularization
- Well-motivated application to energy disaggregation (where interpretability matters)

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited exploration of constraint value acquisition in practice
- Some generalizability claims could be better supported
- Convex relaxation is a minor methodological limitation, but the paper acknowledges this

This paper represents a clear step forward in making blind source separation more interpretable and practical. The SAC framework is a valuable new tool for researchers and practitioners working on BSS problems, and I believe it will be influential in the field.
