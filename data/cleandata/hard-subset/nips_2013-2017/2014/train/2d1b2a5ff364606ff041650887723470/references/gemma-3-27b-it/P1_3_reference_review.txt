## Peer Review: Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for Blind Source Separation (BSS) problems, specifically within the framework of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs). The core idea is to leverage domain knowledge about expected total values of source signals (e.g., daily energy usage of a toaster) to improve the identifiability and accuracy of source separation. The authors formulate the problem as a convex quadratic program (CQP) to enable efficient computation and demonstrate significant performance gains on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset, achieving a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error compared to a standard AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the broader context of BSS and energy disaggregation. It acknowledges the inherent challenges of identifiability in BSS and the existing approaches for incorporating prior knowledge (posterior regularization, generalized expectation criterion, measurement-based learning).  It differentiates itself by focusing on *what* kind of prior knowledge is most useful and proposing SACs as a particularly effective constraint.  The related work section is comprehensive, citing relevant publications in energy disaggregation (Hart, Ziefman) and constrained HMMs (Kolter, Kim, Titsias). The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is a valuable addition, highlighting the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a specific, broadly applicable constraint for BSS is a novel contribution. While constraints have been applied to AFHMMs before (AFAMAP, non-homogeneous HMMs), SACs represent a different class of constraint leveraging aggregate signal properties.
* **Practicality & Efficiency:**  The formulation of the problem as a CQP is a significant strength. It addresses the computational challenges associated with incorporating non-Markovian constraints and allows for efficient inference, as demonstrated by the comparable runtime to other AFHMM variants.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results on the HES dataset are compelling. A 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error is a substantial improvement and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method. The comparison to a recent state-of-the-art approach (Kolter et al.) further strengthens the claim of significance.
* **Clear Motivation & Justification:**  The paper clearly articulates the problem of identifiability in BSS and the motivation behind using domain knowledge to address it. The energy disaggregation example provides a concrete and relatable application.
* **Thorough Evaluation:**  The evaluation includes both toy data and real-world energy data, with varying levels of data availability (with and without mains readings).  This demonstrates the robustness and adaptability of the method.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is clear and consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Sensitivity to Constraint Value (µ0):** The toy data experiment (Section 7.1) reveals a sensitivity to the accuracy of the specified constraint value (µ0). While understandable, this is a potential limitation in real-world scenarios where obtaining accurate prior knowledge can be difficult.  The paper acknowledges this but could benefit from a more detailed discussion of strategies for dealing with uncertain or inaccurate prior information (e.g., robust optimization, Bayesian approaches to estimate µ0).
* **Parameter Tuning:** While the paper mentions tuning parameter δ, it doesn't provide detailed guidance on how to select appropriate values. Cross-validation is mentioned but not described in detail. A more systematic exploration of the sensitivity to δ and λ would be beneficial.
* **Scalability Considerations:** While the CQP formulation enables efficient computation compared to potentially intractable exact inference, the paper doesn't explicitly discuss the scalability limitations of the CQP approach as the number of appliances (chains) and states increases.
* **Limited Exploration of Constraint Generalizations:** While the authors briefly mention the possibility of extending SACs with time-varying constraints (Section 4.2), they don't explore this in detail. This could be a fruitful area for future research.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses an important problem (identifiability in BSS) with a novel and practical solution (SACs).
* The theoretical formulation is sound and the CQP implementation enables efficient computation.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivations and contributions.
* The work has clear potential for impact in the energy disaggregation and broader BSS communities.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The sensitivity to the constraint value (µ0) is a potential limitation.
* The paper could benefit from more detailed discussion of parameter tuning and scalability.
* The exploration of more complex SAC variations is limited.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of BSS and energy disaggregation. The proposed SACs are a novel and effective constraint that addresses a critical challenge in these domains. The CQP formulation enables efficient computation, and the empirical results are compelling. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not outweigh the strengths of the paper.

**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.**  I recommend this paper for acceptance to NIPS. The work is original, significant, and well-executed.  I encourage the authors to address the minor weaknesses in a revised version, particularly providing more guidance on parameter tuning and exploring the potential for robust optimization or Bayesian estimation of the constraint value (µ0).
