- Paper proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to improve identifiability by penalizing deviations of each source’s total energy from a prior.
- Formulates MAP inference with a quadratic aggregate penalty, interprets it as posterior regularization, and derives a convex quadratic program via relaxation and auxiliary transition variables.
- Solves the relaxed CQP with CVX and evaluates on a toy dataset and the HES NILM dataset, reporting improved NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Novelty lies in specializing global aggregate expectation constraints to AFHMMs and providing a tractable convex relaxation for NILM.
- Clear motivation: per-appliance aggregate priors are often available and directly address identifiability issues.
- Convex formulation is sound: relaxation yields a concave quadratic objective with linear/box constraints solvable by off-the-shelf solvers.
- Correct and insightful connection to posterior regularization / KL-projection under expected quadratic constraints.
- Empirical gains on realistic, low-rate NILM (HES) are meaningful, improving both pointwise and aggregate metrics with similar reported runtimes.
- Natural extension to multiple time-window constraints (e.g., day/night) increases modeling flexibility.
- Major concern: the paper does not describe how discrete state sequences are recovered from the relaxed (possibly fractional) solution or the impact of rounding on metrics.
- Sensitivity to mis-specified aggregate priors is only shown on toy data; robustness on HES (real-world prior mismatch) is not quantified.
- Hyperparameter λ selection is unexplored (all λi set to 1); no ablation or practical tuning strategy is provided.
- Scalability concerns: variable count grows as O(T∑i Ki + T∑i Ki^2) and using CVX may be slow; more timing and solver strategy details are needed.
- Noise/model mismatch: handling of unmodeled loads, choice of σt, and whether a residual “other” component exists is unclear and may cause misattribution.
- Clarity issues: some dense definitions (Λi, Vt) need more intuition; methodology for estimating µi, transition matrices, and σt is unspecified.
- Evaluation details missing: how metrics are computed from relaxed outputs and whether µ0 is estimated from disjoint training data should be clarified to avoid leakage.
- Originality is incremental: global expectation constraints are known, but their specialization and convex relaxation for AFHMMs in NILM is a useful contribution.
- Significance: improvements are practically important for NILM and likely transferable to other BSS problems with known totals (e.g., deconvolution tasks).
- Key questions for authors: how discrete sequences are obtained; how σt and transitions are learned/tied across houses; sensitivity to λ and µ0 on HES; absolute runtimes and memory as function of T and appliances; compatibility with AFAMAP constraints.
- Suggested improvements: add a rounding/post-processing step and evaluate its effect, include sensitivity studies for λ and µ0 on real data, report per-appliance SAE/NDE, and provide a lightweight or specialized solver/warm-start strategies.
- Consider adding an explicit residual component to absorb unmodeled loads.
- Minor fixes: correct typos, clarify that maximizing a concave quadratic under linear constraints is convex, and state whether µ0 comes from disjoint training days.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated contribution with promising empirical results; addressing decoding, sensitivity, and scalability would strengthen the paper — recommendation: weak accept (high reviewer confidence).
