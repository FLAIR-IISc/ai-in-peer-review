- Tackles blind source separation in AFHMMs for NILM by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) as soft quadratic penalties to match per-appliance totals µi0.
- Derives penalized MAP objective equivalent to posterior-regularized AFHMM and a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation using pairwise transition variables H and [0,1] relaxations.
- Empirically improves normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) over AFHMM and AFAMAP on UK HES dataset and toy data.
- Positions work within Posterior Regularization, Generalized Expectation, and measurement-learning literature; novelty is a global, cross-time aggregate constraint with a tractable relaxation.
- Methodology is sound: relaxed objective is concave given PSD quadratics and linear HMM consistency constraints; CVX use is reasonable at reported scales.
- Posterior-regularization derivation is correct and clarifies λ as Lagrange multipliers.
- Missing implementation details: how fractional solutions are converted to discrete states or whether fractional S is acceptable.
- Unspecified parameter estimation: σt^2, transition matrices, and priors beyond using k-means for µ need description.
- Runtime claims depend on CVX settings and problem sizes; solver and complexity details are needed for reproducibility.
- Empirics show consistent gains and report paired t-tests, but sensitivity analysis is limited (λ fixed to 1; µ0 variation only in toy study).
- Requires robustness studies: sweep λ, test mis-specified µ0 on real data, and evaluate household- vs national-level priors.
- Missing comparisons to stronger baselines (HSMM-based NILM, interleaved non-homogeneous FHMM) and to AFAMAP+SAC.
- Notation can be dense and unclear (shapes of Vt, ut, construction of µ*); need to state objective concavity explicitly.
- Need explanation of relationship between δi and λi and practical guidance for selecting λi.
- Clarify whether fractional S is accepted and, if so, justify fairness of comparisons to discrete baselines.
- Several references are incomplete/missing years; proofreading and added pseudo-code would improve clarity.
- Originality is incremental: instantiation of posterior-regularization/measurement learning to AFHMMs with a useful combination of CQP and aggregate penalties.
- Significance: aligns with user-facing NILM goals and shows gains on a challenging, low-frequency dataset; approach could generalize to other BSS tasks.
- Broader impact would be strengthened by multi-domain demos, theory on µ0 mismatch effects, and demonstrations of scalability beyond general-purpose solvers.
- Strengths: simple, principled constraint class; convex relaxation enabling global penalties; clear PR connection; empirical gains and extendability to time-localized SACs.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: perform λ and µ0 sensitivity analyses, add stronger baselines including AFAMAP+SAC, report event/timing metrics, state discretization strategy, solver/time vs T,I,K, and provide theoretical bounds on µ0 mismatch.
- Pros for acceptance: practical, well-motivated, principled derivation, demonstrated real-data gains, likely practitioner adoption.
- Cons for acceptance: limited sensitivity/ablations, missing discretization and scalability details, narrower comparisons, absent theoretical guarantees, presentation issues.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — incremental but well-executed and practically impactful; needs stronger experimental analysis and implementation clarity to be a solid accept.
