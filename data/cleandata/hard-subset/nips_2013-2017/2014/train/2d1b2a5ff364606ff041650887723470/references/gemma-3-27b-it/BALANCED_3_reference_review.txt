This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) within an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) framework, specifically targeting the challenging problem of energy disaggregation. The authors motivate the work by highlighting the identifiability issues inherent in blind source separation and the need to incorporate domain knowledge. The core idea – constraining the aggregate values of source signals based on prior expectations – is intuitive and potentially impactful. The authors formulate this as a convex quadratic program, enabling efficient computation, and demonstrate significant performance improvements on real-world energy disaggregation data.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively sets the stage, motivating the problem and positioning the contribution within related work. The related work section provides a concise overview of existing approaches to energy disaggregation and relevant probabilistic modeling techniques. The mathematical formulation of the AFHMM+SAC model and its optimization are presented clearly, although a deeper understanding of optimization might be required for full comprehension. The experimental results are convincing, showing substantial gains over baseline methods, including a state-of-the-art approach.  The inclusion of both toy data experiments and real-world data analysis strengthens the evaluation.  The connection to posterior regularization is also a nice addition, highlighting the theoretical grounding of the approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of signal aggregate constraints is a valuable contribution to the field of BSS and, specifically, energy disaggregation.  While constraints have been applied to AFHMMs before (as discussed in the related work), SACs represent a distinct and reasonable type of prior knowledge to incorporate.
* **Practicality:**  The formulation of the optimization problem as a convex quadratic program is a significant strength.  This allows for efficient and scalable inference, making the approach applicable to real-world datasets.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The substantial performance improvements demonstrated on the HES dataset are compelling evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed method. The 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error is noteworthy.
* **Clear Writing & Structure:** The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is generally clear, and the explanations are concise.
* **Connection to Existing Frameworks:**  Linking the approach to posterior regularization provides a broader theoretical context.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Sensitivity Analysis:** While the toy data experiments demonstrate robustness to errors in the constraint value, a more thorough sensitivity analysis on the real-world data would be beneficial. How sensitive is the performance to inaccuracies in the prior expectations of appliance consumption (µi0)?
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper states that λi was simply set to 1.  A more rigorous hyperparameter tuning strategy, such as cross-validation, could potentially further improve performance. While this is mentioned as a potential future direction, it's a significant omission in the present evaluation.
* **Computational Complexity Discussion:** While the authors state that AFHMM, AFAMAP, and AFHMM+SAC scale similarly, a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity, particularly concerning the size of the problem (number of appliances, length of the time series), would be helpful. How does the runtime scale with these parameters?
* **Limited Comparison to Other Approaches:** While the comparison to AFAMAP is valuable, comparing to other state-of-the-art energy disaggregation techniques (beyond [12]) would strengthen the evaluation. There are other approaches, such as those based on deep learning, that could provide a useful benchmark.
* **Assumptions:** The assumption of a finite number of states (Ki) for each Markov chain, while common, could be a limitation. Discussing the potential impact of this assumption and exploring methods to determine an appropriate value for Ki would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem with a novel and well-motivated approach.
* The proposed method demonstrates significant performance improvements on a real-world dataset.
* The formulation as a convex quadratic program ensures computational efficiency.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The sensitivity analysis is limited.
* The hyperparameter tuning strategy is not rigorous.
* The comparison to other state-of-the-art methods is incomplete.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of energy disaggregation and BSS. The introduction of signal aggregate constraints is a clever idea, and the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through convincing experiments. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the concerns outlined above (particularly a more thorough sensitivity analysis and a discussion of hyperparameter tuning). Adding a comparison to a more diverse set of state-of-the-art disaggregation methods would also be helpful.
