This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation problems, specifically within the context of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs), and demonstrates their effectiveness in energy disaggregation. The core idea is to leverage prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of appliances to improve the accuracy of disaggregation. The authors formulate the problem as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for a relaxed version of the MAP inference, enabling efficient computation.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the challenges of identifiability in BSS and the importance of incorporating domain knowledge. It builds upon existing work in probabilistic models with constraints (posterior regularization, generalized expectation criterion, measurement-based learning) and identifies a useful class of constraints – SACs – that are applicable across diverse domains. The connection to posterior regularization is insightful, providing a theoretical justification for the proposed approach. The empirical evaluation on both simulated and real-world energy disaggregation data demonstrates a significant performance improvement compared to the baseline AFHMM and a state-of-the-art method (AFAMAP). The computational efficiency is also addressed, showing comparable running times.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a specific type of constraint for BSS is novel and addresses a practical challenge in the field.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the CQP for the relaxed problem appears mathematically sound, and the conversion to a computationally efficient form is a significant contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and results. Equations are well-defined, and the flow of ideas is logical.
*   **Significance:** The 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error and 29% improvement over AFAMAP on real-world data demonstrate the practical significance of the proposed approach. This has potential implications for energy conservation and consumer awareness.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The thorough experimental evaluation, including both toy data and real-world data with and without mains readings, strengthens the validity of the results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Exploration of Constraint Generalization:** While the paper mentions the possibility of generalizing SACs beyond a single constraint per chain, the exploration of this generalization (e.g., multiple constraints over different time periods as mentioned in Section 4.2) is limited. The computational impact of such generalization isn't discussed.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The choice of λ (or δ) is not thoroughly discussed. While the paper mentions it can be tuned via cross-validation, there’s no discussion of how sensitive the results are to this parameter. The paper simply sets λi = 1 in experiments.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumption of known µi0 values, while realistic in some scenarios, isn't always valid. The paper acknowledges this but doesn't explore methods for estimating these values from data. A brief discussion about the robustness of the approach to errors in µi0 would be beneficial.
* **Redundancy:** There is some redundancy in sections 6 and 7 when discussing the benefits of SAC.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem in BSS and energy disaggregation.
*   The proposed SACs offer a practical and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge into the model.
*   The technical contributions (CQP formulation and experimental validation) are substantial.
*   The results demonstrate a significant improvement in performance compared to existing methods.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The limited exploration of constraint generalization and hyperparameter tuning.
*   The sensitivity to the accuracy of the µi0 values is not fully investigated.
*   Some redundancy in the presentation.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The proposed SACs are a novel and effective way to incorporate prior knowledge into AFHMMs, and the experimental results demonstrate their practical significance. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, particularly regarding hyperparameter tuning, the impact of inaccurate prior knowledge, and further exploration of constraint generalization.
