- Summary: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs, relaxed to a convex quadratic program via auxiliary transition variables, with gains on toy and UK HES data.

- Relation to prior work: Differs from local-dynamics AFHMM variants by imposing global aggregate constraints; closely linked to Posterior Regularization and measurement-based learning.

- Technical soundness: Relaxation and convexity arguments are plausible; CQP formulation appears standard for relaxed HMM inference.

- Missing detail — decoding/rounding: Paper omits how relaxed soft solutions are converted to discrete states/signals and whether baselines are evaluated comparably.

- Missing detail — complexity/scalability: No concrete complexity analysis, timing breakdowns, or solver/hardware details despite scalability concerns with CVX.

- Missing detail — hyperparameter sensitivity: λi fixed to 1 in experiments; no sensitivity study to λi or robustness to µ0 mis-specification on real data.

- Missing detail — training/evaluation protocol: Ambiguity about handling unmodeled mains loads, evaluation metrics (NDE/SAE) scope, and whether AFHMM baseline uses chainwise Viterbi or relaxed CQP.

- Empirics: Results are encouraging with paired t-tests and realistic national totals, but more analysis needed on when SACs hurt under severe µ0 bias.

- Notation and reproducibility: Notation is heavy; a compact restatement of the final CQP, explicit decoding procedure, and fuller parameter-estimation details are needed.

- Figures/tables and references: Ensure figures include error bars and problem sizes; several references are incomplete.

- Originality: Novel application of per-source aggregate constraints and CQP construction for AFHMMs; moderate originality given links to prior measurement/PR work.

- Significance: Addresses identifiability in low-frequency disaggregation and is broadly applicable, but impact threatened by solver scalability and reliance on reasonable µ0.

- Pros: Simple, general idea with clear motivation; principled PR interpretation; strong empirical gains on low-frequency data; extensible to multiple windows.

- Cons: Missing decoding/rounding details and baseline fairness; scalability concerns with CVX; limited sensitivity analysis to λi/µ0; supervised per-appliance training reduces realism; formatting/clarity gaps.

- Question 1: How are relaxed S/H solutions decoded to states/signals, and are baselines evaluated in the same soft/hard manner?

- Question 2: What are typical problem sizes (I, Ki, T), variable counts, and per-house runtimes with solver/hardware details?

- Question 3: How sensitive are results to λi and to biased µ0 (e.g., ±25–100%) on HES data?

- Question 4: In the mains setting, how are unmodeled loads handled when computing NDE/SAE?

- Question 5: Could a specialized first-order/ADMM solver exploiting chain structure scale the method beyond CVX?

- Question 6: Can uncertainty in µ0 (e.g., Gaussian priors) be incorporated while preserving convexity?

- Minor comments: Fix reference details; clarify that local consistency constraints define the chain marginal polytope; provide quantitative evidence for “similar runtime” claims.

- Recommendation: Promising and practically useful; recommend acceptance if authors address decoding, scalability, and sensitivity issues, otherwise borderline but positive.
