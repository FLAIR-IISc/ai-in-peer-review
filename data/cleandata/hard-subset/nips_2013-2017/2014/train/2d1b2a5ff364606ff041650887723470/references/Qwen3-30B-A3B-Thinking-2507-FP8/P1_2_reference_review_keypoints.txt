- Paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to improve identifiability in blind source separation by enforcing source-sum constraints over time.  
- SACs transform the AFHMM inference problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) and are linked theoretically to posterior regularization.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and Household Electricity Survey (HES) real-world data, showing 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% vs AFAMAP.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, includes robustness tests to constraint errors and statistical significance testing (paired t-tests).  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with precise math, helpful figures/tables, and a useful connection to posterior regularization.  
- The SAC concept is original relative to prior constraint approaches (which focused on state transitions) and generalizable beyond energy disaggregation.  
- The work is practically significant for energy disaggregation and other BSS domains where aggregate knowledge exists.  
- Claim of computational efficiency via CQP needs stronger justification and concrete complexity comparisons to alternatives like chainwise Viterbi.  
- Practical specification of constraints is under-discussed: "true total consumption" is unrealistic and the National Total Consumption proxy may not reflect individual households.  
- Paper lacks ablation/exploration of other constraint types (e.g., time-specific or more complex constraints) and their impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel aggregate-constraint approach, strong theoretical grounding, substantial empirical gains, clear presentation, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited real-world constraint specification discussion, underdeveloped computational complexity justification, and no exploration of richer constraint forms.  
- Final recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance, with minor camera-ready suggestions to address computational complexity justification and real-world constraint specification.
