- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to address identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs constrain appliance-level aggregate energy to known values and convert the problem to a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: ~45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and ~29% improvement vs AFAMAP on real data.  
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization, providing theoretical grounding.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using synthetic robustness tests and real-world datasets with appropriate metrics (NDE, SAE).  
- Paper is technically sound, mathematically rigorous, and clearly written with reproducible detail.  
- Main weakness: insufficient treatment of how to obtain constraint values (μi0) in practice and sensitivity to errors in these estimates.  
- Real-world experiments use oracle "true total consumption" (TTC), limiting practical relevance without analysis of estimated μi0 from limited data.  
- Does not fully address SACs’ limitations under household/time variability (seasonal or cross-house heterogeneity) or dynamic updating of constraints.  
- Related work section could better position SACs relative to other aggregate- or physics-based constraint methods in BSS.  
- Claim of generality across domains is overstated, as experiments are limited to energy disaggregation without examples in other BSS domains.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address practical estimation of constraints, robustness to constraint errors, limitations, and clearer positioning in literature.
