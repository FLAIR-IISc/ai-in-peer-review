- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to address identifiability in blind source separation, especially energy disaggregation.  
- Incorporates prior knowledge of total appliance energy (aggregate sums) as constraints on source signals.  
- Transforms non-Markovian constrained optimization into a convex quadratic program (CQP) that can be solved efficiently.  
- Formalizes SACs as a general class of constraints applicable across BSS domains.  
- Theoretically connects SACs to posterior regularization, framing the approach within an established framework.  
- Technical quality is strong: CQP derivation and convexity properties are correctly established.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, using both synthetic and real-world energy datasets.  
- Strong empirical results: AFHMM+SAC yields a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% improvement vs AFAMAP.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; problem motivation and technical details are well presented.  
- Originality rated moderate: SACs are novel for BSS but not clearly distinguished from related constraint-based methods; connection to posterior regularization is not novel.  
- Significance strong: practical impact on energy disaggregation, low computational overhead, and applicability to other domains.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to errors in constraint values (µi0) is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: practical methods for obtaining constraint values are insufficiently addressed beyond citing surveys.  
- Weakness: the posterior regularization connection is somewhat superficial and could be expanded for extensions.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: strong paper, significant contribution, accept.
