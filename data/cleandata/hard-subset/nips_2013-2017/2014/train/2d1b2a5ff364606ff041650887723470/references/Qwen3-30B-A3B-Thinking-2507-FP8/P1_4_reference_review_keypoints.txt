- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation by constraining source-sum aggregates.  
- Reformulates non‑Markovian constrained AFHMM inference as a convex quadratic program, enabling efficient optimization.  
- Provides theoretical grounding and links the method to posterior regularization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements: 45% decrease in NDE vs AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP on real datasets.  
- Shows practical applicability by using national appliance usage statistics to set constraints.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and technical sections are accessible to readers familiar with HMMs and optimization.  
- Limited analysis of sensitivity to constraint values (µi0) and regularization parameters (λi).  
- Evaluation scope is narrow — experiments only on energy disaggregation despite claims of general applicability.  
- Lacks direct comparisons with other types of prior/constraint incorporation beyond AFAMAP.  
- Missing detailed computational complexity bounds or thorough runtime comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution, recommended for acceptance.  
- Suggested improvements: add sensitivity analysis and a brief discussion or demonstration of broader applicability.
