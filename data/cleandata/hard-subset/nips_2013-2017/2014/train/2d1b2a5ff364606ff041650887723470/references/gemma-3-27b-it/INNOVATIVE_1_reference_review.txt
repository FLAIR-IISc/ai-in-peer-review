This paper tackles the challenging problem of blind source separation, specifically within the context of energy disaggregation, by introducing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM). The core idea – leveraging prior knowledge about expected total energy consumption for different appliances – is insightful and addresses a critical issue in BSS: identifiability and interpretability of results. The authors convincingly argue that incorporating such domain knowledge can lead to more meaningful and reliable disaggregation, rather than producing “franken-appliances.”

The technical contribution, formulating SACs and relaxing the resulting optimization problem into a computationally tractable convex quadratic program, is solid. The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of related work in probabilistic modeling, particularly posterior regularization and constrained HMMs. The experimental results on both a toy dataset and real-world energy data are compelling, showing a substantial improvement over the baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method. A 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error is a significant result.  The fact that the computational cost scales similarly to existing methods is also a practical strength. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs is a genuinely novel idea for addressing the identifiability problem in BSS. While prior constraints have been used before, the focus on aggregate values representing realistic expectations for appliance consumption is a specific and potentially broadly applicable contribution.
* **Practical Relevance:** The energy disaggregation application is highly relevant, and the authors clearly articulate the practical benefits of interpretable results for end-users.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The significant performance gains on both simulated and real data provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Technical Soundness:** The formulation and relaxation into a CQP are well-presented and justified. The connection to posterior regularization further strengthens the theoretical foundation.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the key ideas and technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of SACs empirically, a deeper theoretical understanding of *why* they work so well would be beneficial. For example, exploring the impact of different constraint tightness (δi or λi) on the solution and its robustness to noise.
* **Constraint Specification:** The paper acknowledges that obtaining accurate prior knowledge (µi0) can be challenging. While they demonstrate results with both national averages and ground truth values, further discussion of practical methods for estimating these values in real-world scenarios would be helpful. The reliance on national averages, while realistic, is a potential limitation.
* **Generalizability Beyond Energy Disaggregation:** While the authors state SACs are general, the experiments are heavily focused on energy disaggregation. Demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach on another BSS application domain would strengthen the claim of broad applicability.
* **Relaxation Impact:** The relaxation to a CQP, while necessary for tractability, introduces a potential sub-optimality. Quantifying the impact of this relaxation on the accuracy of the disaggregation would be valuable.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel and potentially impactful approach to BSS by incorporating signal aggregate constraints.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
* The technical formulation is sound, and the connection to posterior regularization provides a solid theoretical basis.
* The problem addressed is highly relevant, and the proposed solution has practical implications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis is limited, and a deeper understanding of the underlying mechanisms would be beneficial.
* The evaluation is primarily focused on energy disaggregation, and further demonstration of generalizability is needed.
* The impact of the relaxation to a CQP on the optimality of the solution is not fully explored.


**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some minor weaknesses, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of blind source separation. The novelty of the SAC concept, combined with the strong empirical results, outweighs the limitations. The approach has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and interpretability of BSS in a variety of applications.  I believe this paper is a strong fit for NIPS and recommend **acceptance**. The authors should be encouraged to further explore the theoretical underpinnings of SACs and investigate their applicability to other BSS problems.
