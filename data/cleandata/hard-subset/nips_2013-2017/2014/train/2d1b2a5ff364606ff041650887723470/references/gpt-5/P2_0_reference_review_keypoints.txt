- Paper augments AFHMM for energy disaggregation with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) that penalize deviations of each source’s total energy over a window.
- Authors derive a convex quadratic program (CQP) via relaxed joint transition variables and show equivalence to posterior regularization (expected quadratic penalties).
- Empirical results on a toy dataset and UK HES low-frequency data show consistent NDE and SAE improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP, with similar runtimes.
- Technique relates to PR/GE/measurement-based learning, AFAMAP/semi-Markov duration constraints, and prior aggregate constraints in other probabilistic models.
- Main novelty is applying aggregate-of-emissions constraints to AFHMM and deriving a tractable convex relaxation for that model.
- Technical formulation is sound: log posterior becomes concave quadratic with PSD matrices, yielding a convex program after relaxation.
- The relaxation produces fractional states that reconstruct signals as convex combinations of state means but reduces discrete-state interpretability and duration structure.
- Paper lacks a described rounding procedure and does not analyze its effect on discrete-state recovery.
- Empirical evaluation includes statistical tests and an oracle total (TTC) upper bound, but sensitivity analysis on real data (NTC mis-specification, λ variation) is limited.
- Computational scalability is under-analyzed: variable counts scale as O(T ΣKi + T ΣKi^2) and solver details, tolerances, and concrete runtimes are sparse.
- Presentation is generally clear but notation is heavy in Section 5; practical details (λ choice, solver settings, rounding) are missing and references have formatting issues.
- Originality is moderate: uses known posterior-constraint ideas but novel instantiation and convex formulation for AFHMMs.
- Significance is solid for the AFHMM/NILM community due to intuitive, widely available constraints and meaningful error reductions, with potential broader applicability.
- Pros: principled domain-knowledge injection, convex relaxation with PR link, consistent real-data gains, and easy extension to multiple time windows.
- Cons: limited sensitivity/λ analysis, no rounding strategy, under-specified scalability and solver details, limited baseline comparisons, and some presentation issues.
- Suggested improvements: provide a rounding heuristic and its impact, sensitivity analyses for λ and NTC mis-specification, scalability characterization and solver choices, comparisons to non-HMM NILM baselines, and clarify NTC computation.
- Reviewer recommendation: Weak accept — useful, incremental contribution that would be strengthened by addressing evaluation, clarity, and scalability gaps.
