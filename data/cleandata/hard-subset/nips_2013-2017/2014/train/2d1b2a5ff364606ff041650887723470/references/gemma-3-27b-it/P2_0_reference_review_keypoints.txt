- Addresses identifiability in BSS/AFHMMs for energy disaggregation by proposing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) as priors on total energy per source.  
- Formulates SACs as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for a relaxed MAP inference, showing improved disaggregation accuracy.  
- Novelty: specific use of aggregate signal constraints and an efficient CQP implementation; situates SACs within posterior regularization/measurement-based learning.  
- Builds on prior work (AFAMAP, semi-Markov models) and appropriately cites related literature.  
- Strength: tackles an important practical identifiability problem in energy disaggregation.  
- Strength: theoretical derivation of the CQP is clear and well-justified.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains—45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP; informative ablation with true total consumption.  
- Strength: CQP scales polynomially and is computationally comparable to existing methods.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and organized.  
- Weakness: relaxation of the MAP problem may yield suboptimal solutions; impact of relaxation not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: limited exploration and sensitivity analysis of SAC hyperparameters (λ/δ).  
- Weakness: unclear generalizability beyond energy disaggregation; lacks discussion of applying SACs to other BSS domains.  
- Weakness: comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art disaggregation methods are limited.  
- Recommendation: reviewer rates the paper a strong accept based on novelty, theoretical grounding, and empirical results.  
- Overall ratings: Quality—Excellent; Clarity—Very Good; Originality—Good; Significance—Good.
