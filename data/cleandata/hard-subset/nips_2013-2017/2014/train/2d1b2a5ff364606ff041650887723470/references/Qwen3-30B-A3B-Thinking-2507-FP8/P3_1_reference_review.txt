

# Peer Review: "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS) problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. SACs constrain the aggregate values of source signals (e.g., total daily energy usage for appliances) to known prior values, addressing the identifiability problem in BSS. The authors incorporate SACs into Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMMs), formulate a relaxed optimization problem as a convex quadratic program, and demonstrate significant improvements on real-world energy disaggregation data.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear problem motivation:** The paper effectively articulates the identifiability challenge in BSS, particularly in energy disaggregation, where "franken-appliances" would undermine user trust. The SAC concept is intuitive and directly addresses this practical concern.

2. **Strong technical contribution:** The paper successfully transforms a non-Markovian constraint into a convex quadratic program (Section 5), enabling efficient computation. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) provides theoretical grounding, showing SACs can be viewed as a specific case of this framework.

3. **Compelling empirical results:** The 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP on real-world data (HES dataset) are significant. The use of paired t-tests to establish statistical significance strengthens the claims. The results are well-presented in Tables 1 and 3.

4. **Practical relevance:** Energy disaggregation is an important real-world problem with tangible societal impact. The approach is computationally efficient (similar to AFHMM/AFAMAP), making it suitable for practical deployment.

**Weaknesses and areas for improvement:**

1. **Theoretical limitations:** The paper doesn't analyze the quality of the relaxation (SACs transform the problem into a CQP, but the relationship between the relaxed solution and the true MAP solution isn't examined). It would be valuable to discuss how sensitive results are to λi/δi and whether principled methods exist for setting these parameters.

2. **Comparative evaluation:** While the paper shows improvement over AFAMAP, it doesn't compare with more recent state-of-the-art methods beyond AFAMAP. The REDD dataset (used in [12]) is mentioned but not utilized for comparison, limiting the significance of the 29% improvement claim. A more comprehensive comparison would strengthen the contribution.

3. **Practical feasibility of SACs:** The paper uses "true total consumption" (TTC) as an oracle, but doesn't adequately discuss how realistic it is to obtain such precise prior information in practice. The National Total Consumption (NTC) approach is reasonable, but the paper should quantify how much prior knowledge is needed for SACs to be effective.

4. **Generality of approach:** The paper focuses almost exclusively on energy disaggregation. While this is a strong application, it would strengthen the paper to briefly discuss how SACs might generalize to other BSS problems (e.g., audio separation) and what limitations might exist.

**Relation to prior work:**
The paper correctly cites relevant work on energy disaggregation (e.g., [8, 12, 22]) and posterior regularization ([4]). However, it could better distinguish itself from other constraint-based approaches. For example, the "k-segment constraints" mentioned in Section 2 [19] are similar in spirit but focus on state durations rather than aggregate values. A more detailed comparison would strengthen the originality claim.

**Overall assessment:**
This is a strong paper with a clear, well-motivated contribution to the BSS field. The SAC concept is novel and well-executed, the technical approach is sound, and the empirical results are impressive. The paper meets all NIPS criteria: it's technically sound, clearly written, makes a novel contribution, and demonstrates significant improvements over prior work.

**Recommendation:**
Accept. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to a practical problem with a well-motivated method that is both theoretically grounded and empirically validated. The improvements over state-of-the-art are significant and well-demonstrated.

**Suggested improvements for camera-ready version:**
1. Add a brief sensitivity analysis for λi/δi parameters.
2. Compare with more recent state-of-the-art methods (beyond AFAMAP) on standard datasets.
3. Discuss practical feasibility of obtaining aggregate constraints in real-world settings.
4. Expand on how SACs might generalize to other BSS problems.

The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it combines a novel theoretical contribution (SACs) with a compelling empirical application. The method is simple yet effective, and the results demonstrate clear advances in the energy disaggregation domain. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field that should be of interest to both researchers and practitioners.
