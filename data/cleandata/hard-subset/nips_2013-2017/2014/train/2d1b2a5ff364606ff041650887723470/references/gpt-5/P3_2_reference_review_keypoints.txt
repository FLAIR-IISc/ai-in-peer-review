- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to match each latent source’s total activity to a target.  
- Casts MAP inference with SACs as maximizing a concave quadratic objective via auxiliary transition variables H, yielding a convex quadratic program after relaxation.  
- Shows the objective can be derived from the posterior regularization framework.  
- Experiments on a toy problem and UK HES dataset show sizable improvements in NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP baselines.  
- Situates work within NILM/factorial models, weak supervision frameworks (posterior regularization, generalized expectation), and aggregate-constraint literature.  
- Novelty: identifies reusable aggregate-sum constraints for additive BSS and derives a tractable convex relaxation for AFHMMs.  
- Relaxation is sound: quadratic terms are concave (negative PSD forms) so maximizing over a polytope is a convex problem.  
- Posterior-regularization derivation is correct for fixed model parameters θ.  
- Underspecified rounding/interpretation: relaxed solutions are fractional and no discrete rounding procedure or integrality-gap metrics are provided.  
- Scalability concerns: general CQP in CVX can create many variables (O(T Σi Ki + T Σi Ki^2)), with no specialized solver or sparsity exploitation discussed.  
- HES experiments are valuable and improvements are consistent with significance testing; TTC oracle provides an informative upper bound.  
- Sensitivity to µ0 and λ is not analyzed on real data, despite likely per-appliance daily total variability across households.  
- λ is fixed to 1 in experiments; adaptive tuning or robustness analysis is lacking.  
- Parameter learning is simplistic (k-means, fixed 3 states per appliance) and effects are not explored; comparisons to semi-Markov/duration models are missing.  
- Mains-with-unmodeled-loads case is not addressed; no slack chain or heavy-tailed noise modeling discussed.  
- Clarity is generally good; recommend explicitly noting that maximizing a concave quadratic is a convex program and justifying PSD/concavity.  
- Suggest adding an algorithmic summary (inputs/outputs/rounding) and clarifying estimation of σt^2 and θ across methods.  
- Minor notation typos/inconsistencies should be cleaned (e.g., p(i)jk indexing).  
- Core idea and convex relaxation are novel combinations of known techniques and relevant beyond NILM (e.g., biology, audio).  
- Practical significance: simple, general, and extensible constraint class with non-trivial empirical gains; impact depends on addressing robustness and scalability.  
- Pros: simple general idea, solid optimization formulation, consistent empirical gains, extensible to multiple windows and other priors.  
- Cons: reliance on accurate aggregates and limited robustness/λ analysis; no discrete rounding strategy; scalability concerns with CVX; limited baseline comparisons and θ ablations.  
- Suggested improvements: principled λ selection (CV/dual learning/Bayesian), rounding/post-processing (e.g., Viterbi or randomized rounding) and integrality-gap reporting.  
- Further suggested improvements: sensitivity analyses for µ0/λ on real data, interval/soft bounds for µ0, combine SAC with duration/one-change constraints, handle unmodeled loads, and include scaling experiments or an ADMM/first-order solver.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — compelling, broadly useful constraint class with solid formulation and promising results, but needs robustness, rounding, and scalability work.
