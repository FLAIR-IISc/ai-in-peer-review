This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, specifically within the context of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs), and applies this to the energy disaggregation problem. The core idea is to incorporate prior knowledge about the expected total energy consumption of appliances to improve disaggregation accuracy. The authors formulate this as a convex quadratic program (CQP) and demonstrate significant performance gains on real-world energy disaggregation data compared to existing methods.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing the crucial issue of identifiability in BSS and energy disaggregation. The introduction clearly articulates the problem and the limitations of existing approaches. The connection to posterior regularization is also a valuable addition, providing a broader theoretical grounding for the proposed SACs. The authors appropriately position their work within the existing literature on energy disaggregation and probabilistic modeling.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of SACs as a general constraint class for BSS, and its specific application to AFHMMs, represents a novel contribution. While constraints have been previously used with AFHMMs (e.g., AFAMAP), the SAC approach offers a different and potentially more informed way to incorporate prior knowledge.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the CQP for the relaxed optimization problem appears mathematically sound. The connection to posterior regularization is also well-established.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experimental results on real-world energy disaggregation data are compelling, demonstrating a substantial improvement (45% decrease in NDE) over the baseline AFHMM and a noticeable improvement (29%) over a state-of-the-art method (AFAMAP).
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clearly defined, and the explanation of the CQP derivation is relatively easy to follow.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Energy disaggregation is a significant practical problem, and the proposed approach has the potential to improve the accuracy and interpretability of disaggregation results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of the AFHMM+SAC appears sensitive to the choice of the constraint value (µi0) and the tuning parameter (δi or λi). While the toy data experiment explores this sensitivity, more detailed analysis and guidance on selecting these parameters in real-world scenarios would be beneficial. The authors acknowledge this by noting that TTC is an oracle and is unavailable in reality, but provide minimal guidance on practical estimation of µ0 beyond national averages.
*   **Computational Complexity:** While the authors claim similar computational time to AFHMM and AFAMAP, the computational complexity of solving the CQP may become a bottleneck for large-scale problems with many appliances or long time series. A more detailed discussion of the computational cost and scalability would be valuable.
*   **Limited Scope of Constraints:** The paper focuses on simple aggregate constraints (sums). Exploring more complex constraints, such as constraints on the timing or duration of appliance usage (mentioned briefly in Section 4.2), could potentially further improve performance.
*   **Lack of Comparison to Other Regularization Techniques:** The paper primarily compares against AFHMM and AFAMAP. Comparing against other relevant regularization techniques, such as L1 regularization or sparsity-inducing priors, would help to better contextualize the benefits of the SAC approach.
*   **Presentation of results:** The results section could benefit from more in depth analysis. For example, there is minimal discussion of what the differences between the successes/failures of the different methods were, and why.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to address a challenging problem in blind source separation and energy disaggregation.
*   The empirical results demonstrate significant performance gains on real-world data.
*   The connection to posterior regularization provides a strong theoretical foundation.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The hyperparameter sensitivity and computational complexity of the approach require further investigation.
*   The scope of the constraints is limited.
*   The comparison to other regularization techniques is incomplete.




**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some minor weaknesses, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The proposed SACs offer a principled and effective way to incorporate prior knowledge into AFHMMs, leading to significant performance improvements. The technical soundness of the approach and the compelling empirical results justify acceptance to NIPS. However, the authors should address the limitations regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and computational complexity in future work.
