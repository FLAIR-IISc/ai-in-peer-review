- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, focused on energy disaggregation.
- Uses aggregate domain knowledge (e.g., total daily appliance energy) to address identifiability in BSS.
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM and converts the constrained problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP).
- Demonstrates efficient solution of non‑Markovian constraints via the CQP transformation.
- Evaluated on synthetic and Household Electricity Survey (HES) data, outperforming baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Positions SACs relative to prior work and posterior regularization, showing they complement rather than replace state-transition constraints.
- Technical approach is sound with a rigorous mathematical derivation of the CQP.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including robustness to constraint errors and use of NDE/SAE metrics with statistical significance tests.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized with consistent notation.
- Would benefit from more detail on how practical constraint values (μi0) are obtained beyond national surveys.
- SACs are a novel class of constraints for BSS; the CQP transformation is a significant technical contribution.
- The approach is significant—29% improvement over a state-of-the-art method and generalizable beyond energy disaggregation.
- Strengths: clear motivation, novel CQP transformation, strong empirical gains, link to posterior regularization, good theory–practice balance.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on obtaining μi0, brief computational complexity analysis, and multi-period extension not tested experimentally.
- Recommendation: accept—strong, novel, well-executed work with clear practical impact.
- Minor suggestions: add discussion on obtaining μi0, clarify results difference with/without mains readings, and discuss interaction with other constraint methods.
