## NIPS Paper Review: Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within the framework of Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs) to address the identifiability problem in blind source separation (BSS), specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors observe that prior knowledge often exists regarding the total expected value of source signals and leverage this through constraints on the aggregate values of the hidden states.  They formulate this as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient computation and demonstrate a significant performance improvement (45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error) on real-world energy disaggregation data compared to the standard AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art approach.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon the established field of energy disaggregation (Hart, 1992; Zoha et al., 2014) and probabilistic models for BSS like AFHMMs (Ghahramani & Jordan, 1997). It acknowledges existing approaches to incorporating prior knowledge (posterior regularization, generalized expectation criteria, measurement-based learning) but focuses specifically on the design and utility of SACs. The paper appropriately cites related work on constrained HMMs (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012; Johnson & Willsky, 2007) and connections to probabilistic databases with aggregate constraints, acknowledging limitations of the latter in its simplicity. This work distinguishes itself by presenting a practically effective constraint that is both informed by domain knowledge and computationally tractable.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Core Problem:** The paper tackles a fundamental issue in BSS – identifiability – with a novel and practically motivated approach.  The "franken-appliance" example vividly illustrates the need for constraints to make disaggregation results interpretable.
* **Principled Approach:** Introducing SACs is a well-reasoned attempt to incorporate domain expertise into a probabilistic model. The connection to posterior regularization provides a solid theoretical grounding.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The derivation of the CQP is a significant contribution, making the method scalable to realistic datasets.  The authors acknowledge and quantify the computational cost, demonstrating awareness of practical limitations.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The 45% improvement in disaggregation error on real-world data is compelling.  The comparison to a recent state-of-the-art method (Kolter & Jaakkola, 2012) strengthens the findings.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the model and methodology. The notation is mostly consistent, and the mathematical derivations are reasonably accessible.
* **Robustness Analysis:** The toy data experiments exploring the sensitivity to constraint value specification (Figure 1) demonstrate a thoughtful evaluation of the method’s robustness.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Parameter Tuning:**  The choice of λ (controlling the strength of the SAC) is crucial. The paper simply sets λi = 1 for all experiments, lacking a systematic exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity and a clear justification for this choice. Cross-validation, while mentioned, wasn’t detailed in the results.
* **Constraint Value Estimation:** The success of SACs relies on accurate estimation of µi0. While the paper acknowledges the use of national consumption surveys (Zimmermann et al., n.d.), it doesn't delve into the potential impact of inaccuracies in these values. In practice, these values may not be readily available or accurate for specific households.
* **Limited Scope of Generalization:** While the authors claim SACs are generally applicable, the evaluation is almost entirely focused on energy disaggregation.  Demonstrating the effectiveness of SACs on other BSS problems (audio processing, computational biology) would bolster the claim of broad applicability.
* **CQP Relaxation:** The relaxation of the integer constraint (Sitk ∈ {0,1}) in the CQP is acknowledged as potentially suboptimal.  A discussion of the potential impact of this relaxation on the solution quality would be beneficial.
* **Reproducibility:** While the authors mention CVX, providing more specific details regarding the setup and configuration would improve reproducibility.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant practical problem with a novel and theoretically sound approach.
* The CQP formulation enables efficient computation on real-world data.
* The empirical results demonstrate a substantial improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The hyperparameter tuning is not adequately addressed.
* The reliance on accurate constraint values is a potential limitation.
* The generalization beyond energy disaggregation is not demonstrated.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. The introduction of SACs is a clever and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge and improve the identifiability of the model. While there are some weaknesses, particularly regarding parameter tuning and the scope of generalization, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**Recommendations for Revision:**

* **Hyperparameter Tuning:**  Include a discussion of the impact of different λ values on performance, potentially using cross-validation.
* **Constraint Value Sensitivity:**  Conduct experiments to assess the sensitivity of the method to errors in the estimation of µi0.
* **Generalization:** Briefly discuss potential applications of SACs to other BSS problems or include preliminary results on a different dataset.
* **CQP Discussion:** Elaborate on the potential impact of the CQP relaxation and consider techniques for mitigating its effects.
* **Reproducibility:** Provide more detailed information regarding the CVX setup and configuration.
