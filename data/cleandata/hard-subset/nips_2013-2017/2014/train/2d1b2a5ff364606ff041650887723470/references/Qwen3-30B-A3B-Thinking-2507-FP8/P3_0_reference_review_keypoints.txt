- Well-motivated contribution: introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in BSS for energy disaggregation.  
- Theoretical link: connects SACs to posterior regularization, providing theoretical grounding.  
- Empirical gains: shows large improvements over baselines (33% and 16% over AFAMAP in NDE and SAE respectively) on simulated and real household data.  
- Computationally efficient: similar inference times to baseline methods, supporting practical applicability.  
- Theoretical limitation: lacks analysis of the optimality gap between the convex relaxation (CQP) and the true MAP solution.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: does not explore sensitivity to the λi complexity parameter (λi fixed to 1 in experiments).  
- Practical concern: assumes aggregate values (µi0) are known and does not show how to estimate them or quantify tolerance to estimation errors.  
- Robustness gap: shows performance degrades when constraint value diverges from truth but does not quantify acceptable error levels in µi0.  
- Limited generality evidence: claims SACs are broadly applicable but evaluation is restricted to energy disaggregation.  
- Insufficient differentiation: does not clearly distinguish SACs from related constraints (e.g., k-segment constraints) in prior work.  
- Incomplete comparisons: lacks empirical comparison to other constraint-based HMM approaches (e.g., non-homogeneous HMMs, state-transition constraint methods).  
- Recommendation 1: provide theoretical analysis of the optimality gap for the convex relaxation.  
- Recommendation 2: analyze sensitivity to errors in constraint values (µi0), especially when using proxies like NTC.  
- Recommendation 3: expand evaluation to include other constraint-based methods for comparison.  
- Recommendation 4: discuss limitations and scenarios where SACs may not apply.  
- Overall: solid empirical contribution with computational practicality but needs stronger theoretical analysis and broader evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.
