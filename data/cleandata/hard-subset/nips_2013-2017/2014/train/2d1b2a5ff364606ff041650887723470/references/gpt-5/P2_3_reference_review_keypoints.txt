- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to encode priors on per-source totals via a quadratic penalty encouraging ∑t xit ≈ µi0.
- Relaxes non-Markovian MAP by using transition-indicator variables to derive a concave quadratic objective with affine/box constraints, yielding a solvable convex quadratic program (CQP).
- Shows the formulation corresponds to posterior regularization and provides a tractable convex relaxation for additive factorial models.
- Builds on AFHMM and AFAMAP but adds novel global aggregate constraints across time; conceptually fits within posterior regularization and “learning from measurements.”
- Related to other HMM constraint work but focuses on inherently global, non-Markovian aggregate constraints.
- Technical derivation appears sound: quadratic terms use PSD matrices, objective is concave, constraints produce a valid CQP, and posterior-regularization equivalence is correctly derived.
- Relaxation produces fractional S; x̂ = µT_i S_it is used but the paper should explicitly state how fractional S is converted to evaluated signals.
- Empirical results are strong: consistent improvements in NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP across many households, with significance testing reported.
- Runtime claims are plausible for the evaluated small settings (K=3, short T), but more solver/problem-size details are needed to validate scalability.
- Hyperparameters λi are fixed to 1 without tuning; sensitivity study is limited and lacks cross-validated analysis on real data.
- Model parameters θ are pre-estimated; joint learning of θ with SACs is not explored.
- Paper is generally clear and well organized though derivations are dense; some notation is heavy in Section 5.
- Missing reproducibility details: decoding from fractional solutions, exact T/I/K per experiment, CVX solver settings, and handling of unmodeled loads.
- The SAC constraint form and CQP integration into AFHMM inference is a useful and apparently novel instantiation with practical relevance.
- Method has significant application potential in energy disaggregation and other BSS problems where aggregate priors exist.
- Scalability beyond reported settings is unclear: variable count and reliance on generic convex solvers may limit larger deployments.
- Pros: well-motivated constraint class, sound convex relaxation, consistent empirical gains, and general applicability beyond energy.
- Cons: limited hyperparameter exploration, missing comparisons to some duration/semi-Markov baselines, qualitative scalability discussion, and underspecified implementation details.
- Suggestions: clarify x̂ computation from fractional S, show sensitivity to λ on real data, report problem sizes and runtimes, consider a specialized QP solver, compare to duration-model baselines, and evaluate windowed SACs.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound and empirically validated contribution that would benefit from stronger scalability and experimental breadth.
