

# Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

## Summary
This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for addressing identifiability in blind source separation (BSS) problems, with a focus on energy disaggregation. SACs constrain the total aggregate values of source signals (e.g., daily energy consumption of appliances) to known prior values. The authors incorporate these constraints into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) framework, transforming the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program that can be efficiently solved. The paper demonstrates significant improvements (45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error) over the standard AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a state-of-the-art approach [12] on real-world energy disaggregation data.

## Quality
The paper presents a technically sound approach with clear theoretical grounding. The authors correctly identify the identifiability problem in BSS and propose a principled way to incorporate domain knowledge through SACs. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) is well-executed and provides theoretical justification for the proposed method. The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, using both synthetic data (Section 7.1) and real-world energy data (Section 7.2), with appropriate metrics (NDE and SAE).

The computational complexity analysis is reasonable, noting that the CQP formulation scales polynomially with time steps and states. The authors acknowledge that SACs require prior knowledge of aggregate values, which is a valid limitation. However, the paper could have provided more detailed analysis of the sensitivity to the constraint parameters (δi or λi), particularly how errors in the prior aggregate values affect performance. The synthetic data experiment (Section 7.1) partially addresses this, but a more systematic analysis would strengthen the quality assessment.

## Clarity
The paper is well-written and logically organized. The problem statement in Section 3 and related work in Section 2 provide sufficient context for the contribution. The mathematical formulation in Sections 4-5 is precise, though the optimization derivation in Section 5 is somewhat dense. The presentation of the convex quadratic program formulation is clear, and the authors appropriately use CVX to solve the relaxed problem.

The experimental results (Sections 7.1-7.2) are presented clearly with tables and figures that effectively communicate the performance improvements. The paper adequately explains the energy disaggregation problem and the relevance of SACs to this application. One minor weakness is that the connection to other BSS applications beyond energy disaggregation is not developed as thoroughly as it could be.

## Originality
This paper makes a meaningful contribution to the BSS literature by addressing a gap identified in the introduction: "what types of domain knowledge we should include" for constraint-based approaches. SACs represent a novel constraint type that is simple yet effective, and the authors correctly position their work against related approaches that constrain HMM state transitions (e.g., AFAMAP [12]) rather than aggregate signal values.

The paper distinguishes itself from prior work by focusing on aggregate constraints rather than temporal constraints on state transitions. While some related work has considered aggregate constraints in probabilistic databases [20], those models are limited to simple graphical structures, making the authors' extension to HMM-based BSS novel. The connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) further strengthens the theoretical novelty.

## Significance
The significance of this work is substantial for both the energy disaggregation domain and broader BSS applications. The 45% improvement in normalized disaggregation error over AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP [12] are impressive and demonstrate clear practical value. Energy disaggregation has real-world impact for energy conservation, making this application highly relevant.

The paper's framework for incorporating domain knowledge through SACs could be applied to other BSS problems beyond energy disaggregation, suggesting broader significance. The authors appropriately note that "the main goal of interest is for people to interpret the resulting source signals" - a common goal in many BSS applications where interpretability matters. The method provides a principled way to guide the solution toward more interpretable sources, addressing a fundamental challenge in the field.

## Strengths and Weaknesses
**Strengths:**
1. Addresses a meaningful problem (identifiability) with a simple yet effective constraint type
2. Strong empirical results showing significant improvements over state-of-the-art
3. Clear connection to posterior regularization framework
4. Well-organized and well-written paper with appropriate evaluation

**Weaknesses:**
1. Limited discussion of sensitivity to constraint parameters (though partially addressed in Section 7.1)
2. Superficial treatment of potential applications beyond energy disaggregation
3. No comparison against other constraint-based approaches to BSS (e.g., k-segment constraints [19])
4. The relaxation to a convex problem may lose some accuracy due to the integer constraints being relaxed

## Recommendation
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of BSS with practical significance for energy disaggregation. The technical approach is sound, the empirical results are convincing, and the method is well-motivated. While the paper has minor weaknesses in discussing sensitivity to constraint parameters and broader applicability, these do not significantly undermine the overall contribution.

I recommend acceptance. The work is technically rigorous, addresses an important problem with a novel solution, and demonstrates significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods in a meaningful application domain. The paper's contributions to both theory (connection to posterior regularization) and practice (energy disaggregation results) make it a valuable addition to NIPS proceedings.

The paper meets all four NIPS criteria:
- Quality: High (sound technical approach with strong empirical validation)
- Clarity: High (well-organized with clear presentation of results)
- Originality: High (novel constraint type addressing a gap in the literature)
- Significance: High (practical impact in energy disaggregation with potential for broader application)

This work advances the state of the art in BSS and provides a principled framework for incorporating domain knowledge that could benefit many other applications.
