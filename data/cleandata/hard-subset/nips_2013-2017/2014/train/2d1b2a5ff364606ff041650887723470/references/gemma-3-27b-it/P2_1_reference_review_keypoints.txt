- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to improve identifiability in energy disaggregation using prior total-energy knowledge.  
- Incorporates SACs into AFHMM via a convex quadratic program with a connection to posterior regularization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs standard AFHMM and 29% improvement over AFAMAP on synthetic and HES data.  
- Correctly situates work within prior literature on BSS, AFHMM variants, and constraint-based/posterior-regularization approaches.  
- Novel contribution: focusing on aggregate constraints as a practical, readily available prior for disaggregation.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous derivation of a computationally efficient convex relaxation.  
- Practical relevance: meaningful improvements for real-world energy disaggregation applications.  
- Clarity: well-written, clear mathematical notation and explanations.  
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis on real-world data regarding errors in assumed aggregate values.  
- Weakness: lack of exploration of the effect of λi (set to 1) and limited parameter-tuning discussion.  
- Weakness: scalability discussion is vague; practicality for many appliances or long time series is unclear.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit other contemporary methods (e.g., deep learning–based approaches).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, strong empirical results, and practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient sensitivity analysis, limited parameter-tuning discussion, and unclear scalability.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper deserving acceptance; reviewer rating Strong Accept (8/10).
