

# Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for additive factorial hidden Markov models (AFHMMs), addressing the identifiability problem in blind source separation (BSS) applications like energy disaggregation. The authors observe that in many BSS applications, we often have prior knowledge about the total aggregate values of source signals (e.g., average daily energy consumption of appliances), and they propose incorporating these as constraints. The key contribution is transforming the SAC-informed optimization problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) that can be efficiently solved, while connecting the approach to posterior regularization [4].

The paper builds upon established work in energy disaggregation [8, 12, 22] and BSS [17, 2], while extending the AFHMM framework [5] to incorporate aggregate constraints. It differentiates itself from prior approaches that constrain HMM state transitions (e.g., AFAMAP [12]) by focusing on aggregate signal properties rather than temporal dynamics.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel and meaningful contribution:** SACs address a critical practical problem in energy disaggregation where unidentifiability leads to unrealistic "franken-appliances." The idea of leveraging aggregate signal knowledge is well-motivated and generalizable beyond energy disaggregation.

2. **Strong theoretical foundation:** The connection to posterior regularization [4] provides a solid theoretical basis. The derivation of the CQP in Section 5, while mathematically dense, appears technically sound.

3. **Compelling empirical results:** The energy disaggregation experiments demonstrate significant improvements (45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to baseline AFHMM, and 29% improvement over AFAMAP [12]). The use of both synthetic and real-world data strengthens the claims.

4. **Practical implementation:** The computational complexity is reasonable (polynomial in time steps and states), and the implementation scales similarly to previous methods, making it practical for real applications.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited analysis of the relaxation:** The paper solves a relaxed problem (with Sitk ∈ [0,1]) rather than the exact integer problem. While acknowledged, the authors don't thoroughly analyze how this relaxation affects the results or provide bounds on the optimality gap. This is particularly important for a method that claims to improve performance.

2. **Insufficient justification for hyperparameters:** The paper sets λi = 1 for all experiments without justification or exploration of parameter sensitivity. While they mention cross-validation could be used, the lack of such analysis weakens the empirical claims.

3. **Limited synthetic evaluation:** The synthetic experiments (Section 7.1) use only two chains with simple parameters, which doesn't fully demonstrate the approach's scalability or robustness to more complex scenarios.

4. **Practical constraint acquisition:** The paper doesn't adequately address how to obtain the aggregate constraints (µi0) in real-world applications beyond energy disaggregation. While national surveys are mentioned for energy data, this approach may not generalize well to other domains.

5. **Comparison scope:** The comparison to state-of-the-art is limited to AFAMAP [12]. Including more recent energy disaggregation methods (e.g., [16, 10]) would strengthen the significance claims.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to a significant problem in BSS applications. The theoretical foundation is sound, and the empirical results are convincing. The work is technically solid and addresses a meaningful practical challenge in energy disaggregation.

However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the weaknesses noted above. Specifically:
1. A deeper analysis of the relaxation's impact on results
2. More thorough exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity
3. Expanded synthetic experiments to demonstrate scalability
4. Better discussion of how to obtain constraint values in practice

The significance of the work is high for energy disaggregation applications, and the SAC framework could potentially be applied to other BSS domains. The paper makes a clear contribution to the field, though the current version would benefit from the suggested improvements before being accepted at NIPS.

**Decision:**
I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper makes a novel contribution with strong empirical results, but the current version has some gaps in the theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation that prevent it from being a strong candidate for acceptance. With the suggested revisions, it would be an excellent NIPS paper.
