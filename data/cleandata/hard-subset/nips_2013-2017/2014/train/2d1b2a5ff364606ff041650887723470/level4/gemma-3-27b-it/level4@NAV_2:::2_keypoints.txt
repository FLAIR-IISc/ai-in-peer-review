- Proposes an additive factorial HMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for electricity usage modeling.  
- Incorporates domain knowledge into blind source separation to address unidentifiability in energy disaggregation.  
- Provides a well-motivated theoretical justification for SACs.  
- Formulates a convex quadratic program for approximate inference, a notable technical contribution.  
- Demonstrates substantial performance improvements on a real-world HES dataset over AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Novelty lies in specifying what types of domain knowledge to include (SACs) and applying them to AFHMM in a tractable way.  
- Use of a high-resolution real-world dataset enhances practical relevance.  
- Potentially significant impact for energy conservation and broader blind source separation problems.  
- Presentation suffers from excessive and dense acronyms, reducing readability.  
- Connection to posterior regularization is mentioned but not explicitly or thoroughly articulated.  
- Emphasizing the interpretation of SACs as an a priori constraint would strengthen conceptual clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-motivated, strong empirical gains, novel, practically relevant, thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation due to acronyms and insufficient linkage to related posterior-regularization methods.
