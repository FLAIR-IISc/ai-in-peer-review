- Problem addressed: blind source separation for energy disaggregation—separating household aggregate electricity into appliance signals despite inherent unidentifiability.  
- Main contribution: introduce Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject prior knowledge about total source values over a period.  
- Model integration: incorporate SACs into Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM), breaking Markov property and invalidating standard inference.  
- Inference method: relax MAP inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient optimization.  
- Theoretical grounding: connect SACs formulation to posterior regularization, giving solid theoretical justification.  
- Empirical results: large improvements on HES dataset—~45% error reduction vs. AFHMM and ~29% vs. AFAMAP; includes oracle (TTC) experiments showing upper-bound gains.  
- Originality/significance: SACs are simple, general, and broadly applicable to other BSS problems with known signal totals.  
- Technical quality: sound formulation; CQP relaxation makes the approach practical and tractable.  
- Clarity issue: Section 5 is dense with new notation (µ∗i, Λi, etc.) and lacks intuition, hurting readability.  
- Presentation suggestion: include a variables table and clearer explanations to ease parsing of the CQP formulation.  
- Acronym overload: heavy use of acronyms (AFHMM, AFAMAP, CQP, NTC, TTC, NDE, SAE) reduces accessibility; remind or reduce usage.  
- Conceptual framing: explicitly state early that the penalty is equivalent to a Gaussian prior on aggregate signals to improve narrative clarity.  
- Hyperparameter concern: complexity parameter λ fixed at 1 without justification; need guidance on selection and sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall evaluation: technically strong, novel, and significant with convincing results; primary weakness is presentation/clarity which can be fixed to strengthen the paper.
