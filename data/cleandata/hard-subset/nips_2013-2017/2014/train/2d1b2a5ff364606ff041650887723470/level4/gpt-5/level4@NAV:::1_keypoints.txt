- Introduces AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, leveraging domain knowledge to improve decomposition.  
- Motivated by energy disaggregation where appliance-level totals over time windows are available as priors.  
- Reformulates the log-posterior inference as a convex optimization problem.  
- Demonstrates method on synthetic toy data and real domestic energy consumption readings.  
- Method outperforms standard AFMM and AFAMAP baselines.  
- Improvements shown statistically significant via t-tests.  
- Optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Concern about toy experiment: very low noise variance chosen—may make setup overly simplistic; request clarification whether SNRs are typical in real data.  
- Suggest authors report SNR between mains readings and aggregate appliance outputs for the energy application.  
- Request a formal proof that equation (6) leads to a convex procedure, as this is not fully evident.  
- Ask for scaling analysis w.r.t. sequence length and number of hidden states and tractability comparison to AFAMAP on larger datasets.  
- Manuscript is well written, organized, and motivates the problem effectively.  
- Minor notation issue noted on page 4 (commas in subscripts preceding equation (6)).  
- Reviewer believes the work is novel and original but cannot fully judge domain significance.  
- Authors addressed reviewer questions satisfactorily during feedback; reviewer encourages inclusion of those clarifications in the revision.
