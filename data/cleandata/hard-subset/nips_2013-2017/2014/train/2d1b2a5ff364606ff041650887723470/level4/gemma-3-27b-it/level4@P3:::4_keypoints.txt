Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation by encouraging each source’s total signal to match a specified aggregate.

Applies SACs within additive factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs) for energy disaggregation and formulates a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate inference.

Demonstrates substantial performance improvements on the real-world HES low-frequency energy disaggregation dataset compared to baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method.

Addresses a well-motivated, practical problem (identifiability in blind source separation) and connects to related work like posterior regularization and AFHMM constraint literature.

Use of low-frequency HES data is a notable contribution since many studies rely on higher-frequency readings.

Mathematical formulation and clarity of the CQP derivation are strengths of the paper.

Main weakness: lacks deeper theoretical justification for why SACs improve inference beyond empirical results.

Potential conflation of model vs. algorithm: AFAMAP is an inference algorithm for AFHMM and comparisons should clarify this distinction.

Suggests exploring combining SACs with other inference methods (e.g., AFAMAP) rather than treating them as competing models.

Needs sensitivity analysis of the tuning parameter δ (or λ); justification for chosen λ=1 is missing.

Should expand discussion of relationship to aggregate-constraint work in probabilistic databases and clarify differences in model complexity.

Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, novel intuitive approach, efficient solvable optimization, strong real-world results, clear presentation.

Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical grounding, model/algorithm conflation, insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity discussion, lack of exploration with other inference techniques.

Overall recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing theoretical justification, clarifying model vs. algorithm comparisons, adding sensitivity analysis, and acknowledging/ exploring SACs+AFAMAP.
