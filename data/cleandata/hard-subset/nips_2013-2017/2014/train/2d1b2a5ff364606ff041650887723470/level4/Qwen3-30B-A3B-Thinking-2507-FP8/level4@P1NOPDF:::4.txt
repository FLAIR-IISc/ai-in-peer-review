

The paper extends the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) by incorporating constraints to ensure that the sum of individual chain outputs aligns with a known aggregate value. The authors propose a convex relaxation for MAP inference and apply the method to real-world energy disaggregation datasets: 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings, and 10 homes with higher-frequency readings (without appliance-level monitoring). While the core idea is straightforward and the optimization formulation is relatively simple, the work remains compelling due to its application to large-scale real-world data. As a paper bridging applied and algorithmic contributions, it successfully demonstrates practical relevance. The following points, however, warrant clarification to strengthen the presentation:

First, the motivation for AFAMAP to enforce per-chain state constraints appears to improve the relaxation’s ability to yield integer solutions. Does the SACAFHMM formulation achieve a similar effect, or do its solutions maintain lower error despite non-integer values? A discussion addressing this would clarify the relative merits of the two approaches and sharpen the technical distinction.

Second, AFAMAP and SACAFHMM seem orthogonal in design: the SACAFHMM constraints could readily be incorporated into the AFAMAP optimization framework. While I do not expect the authors to address this in their response, I strongly encourage them to explore this combination for the final version, as it may yield improved performance.

Third, the paper conflates model names and optimization procedures. For instance, comparing "AFHMM" versus "AFAMAP" is imprecise: AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP solutions under AFHMM (which itself relies on alternating Viterbi optimization), not a distinct model. The authors should clarify this distinction throughout. This issue also connects to the previous point—since the algorithmic strategies are complementary rather than mutually exclusive, combining them could enhance results beyond the current approach. The paper proposes a simple extension to AFHMM with a tailored optimization, but the real-world results are convincing. Overall, the work is sound, though precise terminology would strengthen its technical contribution.
