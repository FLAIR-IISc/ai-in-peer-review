- Extends AFHMM by adding soft aggregate constraints to encourage chain outputs to match known aggregate signals (SACAFHMM) and develops a convex MAP-relaxation.
- Evaluated on two real-world energy disaggregation settings: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency without submetering).
- Strength: Aggregate-constraint idea is practically relevant for energy disaggregation and similar source-separation problems.
- Strength: Proposed convex relaxation is simple, scalable, and attractive for large datasets.
- Strength: Empirical evaluation on sizable real-world datasets increases credibility and impact.
- Strength: Motivation is clear and intuitive—using known aggregate totals to regularize disaggregation.
- Question: How often does the relaxation return integral solutions in practice, and how does this frequency compare to AFAMAP?
- Question: When solutions are fractional, do they still yield lower reconstruction error than AFAMAP’s typically more integral solutions?
- Request: Empirically discuss the trade-off between integrality and accuracy and whether aggregate constraints tighten the relaxation.

- Request: Specify which inference method each baseline uses, how hyperparameters are tuned, and, if possible, report results using a common set of constraints to isolate inference method effects.
