This paper introduces a novel method for blind source separation (BSS) by formulating the problem within an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) framework and augmenting it with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs). The core motivation is to leverage domain-specific knowledge to achieve more accurate separation of time-series components. The authors effectively illustrate this concept using the application of energy disaggregation, where constraints on the total expected energy consumption of individual appliances over a time period significantly improve the decomposition of the aggregate household energy signal. The resulting objective function, which corresponds to the log-posterior of the hidden states, is then transformed into a tractable convex optimization problem. The method's efficacy is convincingly demonstrated on both a synthetic toy dataset and real-world domestic energy data.

**Strengths**

The paper has several significant strengths:
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented. The idea of incorporating aggregate constraints is intuitive and powerful.
*   **Strong Empirical Evaluation:** The experimental section is thorough, providing a clear validation of the proposed method. The authors demonstrate superior performance against both a standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, a state-of-the-art alternative.
*   **Rigorous Comparison:** The performance improvements are shown to be statistically significant using t-tests, adding rigor to the empirical claims.
*   **Practical Viability:** The authors demonstrate that the optimization speed of their method is comparable to that of AFAMAP, which is crucial for practical applications.
*   **Clarity and Structure:** The paper is well-written and logically structured. The introduction provides excellent motivation and situates the work effectively within the relevant literature.

**Areas for Improvement**

While the work is strong, several points could be clarified or expanded upon to further improve the paper.

1.  **Experimental Setup:** The choice of a very low noise variance in the toy example warrants further discussion. While this setup effectively illustrates the method's superior separation capabilities in Figure 2, it could be perceived as an idealized scenario. It would be beneficial for the authors to comment on whether such high signal-to-noise ratios are representative of real-world data. A related clarification on the signal-to-noise ratio between the mains readings and the aggregate appliance output in the energy disaggregation application would also add valuable context.

2.  **Theoretical Justification:** The paper asserts that the objective function is transformed into a convex optimization problem. However, the reasoning that establishes the convexity of the final procedure in equation (6) is not immediately obvious from the text provided. Including a brief proof or a more detailed justification, perhaps in an appendix, would solidify this central and important claim.

3.  **Scalability Analysis:** The empirical results demonstrate a computational speed comparable to AFAMAP, but the paper would be enhanced by a theoretical analysis of the method's scalability. Discussing the computational complexity with respect to the length of the time series and the number of hidden states would provide valuable insight into its performance on much larger datasets and state spaces, offering a more complete comparison with AFAMAP from a theoretical standpoint.

**Minor Comments**

I noted a minor potential notational inconsistency on page 4 (line 202). In the unnumbered equation preceding equation (6), the subscripts for the object `p` contain commas. This appears to deviate from the notation established on page 3, which does not use commas in this context. Please verify if this is an intentional notational change or a minor typo.

**Conclusion and Recommendation**

To my knowledge, the work is novel and original. The method of incorporating signal aggregate constraints is an elegant and effective extension to the AFHMM framework for blind source separation.
