The paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation problems, specifically energy disaggregation. The model incorporates domain knowledge by constraining the total signal of each unknown source to be close to a specified value, which is often available in many applications.

The authors provide a clear summary of the related work in energy disaggregation and introduce the concept of SACs, which is a novel contribution to the field. The paper is well-organized, and the notation is properly defined. However, the text is dense and contains many acronyms, which can make it challenging to follow for non-experts.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model on both synthetic and real-world data, showing significant improvements over the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach. The evaluation metrics, such as the normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE), are well-defined and provide a clear understanding of the model's performance.

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of SACs, which is a general framework for incorporating prior knowledge into probabilistic models. The authors show that the proposed model can be derived from the posterior regularization framework, providing a theoretical foundation for the approach.

One potential criticism is that the paper assumes a priori knowledge of the total signal values for each source, which may not always be available in practice. However, the authors acknowledge this limitation and suggest that the proposed model can be extended to incorporate uncertainty in the prior knowledge.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their work. The proposed model has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in energy disaggregation, and the introduction of SACs is a valuable contribution to the field.
