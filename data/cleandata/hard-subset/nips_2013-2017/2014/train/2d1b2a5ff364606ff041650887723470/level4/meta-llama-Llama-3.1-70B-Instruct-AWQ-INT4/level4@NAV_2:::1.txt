This paper develops a novel method of performing blind source separation by formulating the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and applying signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The motivation behind this approach is to incorporate additional domain knowledge to improve the separation of the time series into components. The example used throughout the paper is energy disaggregation, where the components of domestic energy use (relating to individual appliances) can be better separated when information relating to total (expected) usage of each appliance in a time period is incorporated.

The paper is technically sound, with a strong simulation section. The method, as expected, performs favorably in contrast to regular AFHMM and also AFAMAP (a recently developed alternative method) - neither of which incorporate the SACs. The improvements are shown to be significant using t-tests. The authors also demonstrate that the speed of optimization is comparable to AFAMAP.

The theoretical aspects of the paper are well-presented and structured. However, a proof that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex would strengthen the paper, as it is not entirely clear from the provided reasoning. Additionally, theoretical scaling arguments (with respect to data length and number of hidden states) would complement the simulation findings and provide insight into the tractability of the method for larger data sets and state spaces.

The paper is well-written and structured, with a clear description of relevant literature in the introduction. However, a minor error is noted on page 4 (line 202), in the unnumbered equation before (6), where a comma appears to be missing in the subscripts preceding the object p, which is inconsistent with earlier notation on page 3.

The work appears to be novel and original, and the authors have satisfactorily addressed the reviewer's questions in the feedback phase. Incorporating similar clarifications in the next version of the paper would be beneficial.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the method performs well in comparison to state-of-the-art approaches. The paper's clarity is good, with a clear description of the problem and relevant literature. However, some minor errors and areas for improvement in the theoretical aspects and presentation have been identified.

The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a novel method for performing blind source separation using AFHMMs with SACs. The significance of the results to the HMM community and the energy application studied is also high, as demonstrated by the paper's performance in comparison to state-of-the-art approaches.

The paper's relevance to NIPS is high, as it addresses a challenging problem in the field of machine learning and presents a novel solution. The paper's impact is also high, as it has the potential to be applied to various real-world problems, including energy disaggregation.
