- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) integrated into an AFHMM to improve identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- Enforces constraints on total signal magnitudes per source to match domain expectations (e.g., expected daily appliance consumption).  
- Formulates inference as a convex quadratic program for efficient optimization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on real energy data: 33% reduction in normalized disaggregation error and 16% improvement in signal aggregate error vs AFAMAP, and 45% improvement over original AFHMM.  
- Novel contribution: focuses on aggregate signal constraints rather than traditional HMM state/transition constraints.  
- Links SACs to posterior regularization for theoretical grounding, but the connection is not fully developed.  
- Major readability issues due to excessive unexplained acronyms (SAC, AFHMM, NDE, SAE, etc.).  
- Insufficient explanation of how constraint values μi0 would be obtained in real deployments; experiments use oracle true totals (TTC) and an imperfect national total consumption (NTC) proxy.  
- Lacks discussion on the reliability and estimation methods for μi0 and could cite appliance energy modeling literature for practical approaches.  
- Inadequate comparison with more recent NIPS/deep-learning work on energy disaggregation.  
- Writing quality problems: grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and heavy passive voice reduce clarity.  
- Suggested improvements: add an acronym glossary, clarify SAC–posterior regularization relationship, detail practical estimation of μi0, and broaden empirical comparisons.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel, and empirically compelling approach, but weakened by poor exposition and insufficient practical discussion—would be strong after revisions.
