- Proposes modeling blind source separation as an AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).
- Key idea: incorporate domain knowledge (aggregate signal priors) to improve time-series decomposition.
- Focuses on energy disaggregation: separating appliance-level consumption from mains readings using prior total-usage info.
- Reformulates the posterior maximization over hidden chains as a convex optimization problem.
- Validated on a toy dataset and real household energy data.
- Empirical results: outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with statistically significant gains and comparable optimization speed.
- Concern: toy experiment uses very low noise variance—need clarification if this is realistic or chosen for visual effect.
- Suggestion: report typical signal-to-noise ratios in real data, especially SNR between mains and appliance outputs.
- Theoretical clarity request: provide a proof or clearer argument that equation (6) is convex.
- Suggestion: include theoretical scaling/complexity analysis (sequence length, number of hidden states) and tractability comparison to AFAMAP.
- Minor issue: potential inconsistent notation in the unnumbered equation before (6) (subscripts/commas).
- Overall: well written, novel and compelling method with strong empirical performance; reviewer’s questions were addressed in feedback and should be included in revisions.
