- Introduces a constrained extension to the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) enforcing chain outputs sum to a known aggregate value.  
- Proposes a convex relaxation for MAP inference to handle the constraint.  
- Evaluates on two real-world energy disaggregation datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency, no appliance monitoring).  
- Core ideas are straightforward and use a simple optimization framework, yet the work is compelling due to large-scale real-world application.  
- Strong applied contribution combining practical algorithmic development with empirical validation.  
- Recommendation 1: Explicitly link AFAMAP’s sequential state constraint to SACAFHMM’s behavior—clarify whether SACAFHMM attains similar integer solution recovery or yields lower error despite non-integer solutions.  
- Recommendation 2: Note AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are largely orthogonal; consider integrating SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP’s optimization for potential performance gains.  
- Recommendation 3: Avoid conflating the model (AFHMM) with the optimization procedure (AFAMAP); consistently distinguish AFHMM as the model and AFAMAP as an MAP approximation algorithm.  
- Empirical results are robust and well-motivated, offering convincing evidence of the method’s utility in practice.  
- Overall assessment: modest but effective extension, technically sound and well-executed, suitable for NIPS with minor clarifications to improve impact.
