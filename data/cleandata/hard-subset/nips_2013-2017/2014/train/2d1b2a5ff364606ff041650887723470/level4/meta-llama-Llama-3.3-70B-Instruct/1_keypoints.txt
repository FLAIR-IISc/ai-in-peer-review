- Proposes a novel blind source separation method: additive factorial HMM with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).  
- Leverages domain knowledge about total expected appliance usage to improve component separation, demonstrated for energy disaggregation.  
- Converts the posterior maximization objective into a convex optimization problem.  
- Validated on both synthetic and real domestic energy datasets.  
- Technically sound with robust simulations; outperforms AFMM and AFAMAP with statistically significant improvements and comparable optimization speed to AFAMAP.  
- Unclear choice of low noise variance in the toy example; needs clarification and discussion of signal-to-noise ratio in the energy application.  
- Theoretical presentation is strong but requires a formal proof of convexity for equation (6).  
- Recommend adding theoretical scaling analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and comparing scalability to AFAMAP.  
- Minor notation issue: possible incorrect subscripts in the unnumbered equation before (6) (page 4, line 202).  
- Authors’ rebuttal responses were satisfactory; suggest incorporating those clarifications in the manuscript.  
- Overall novel and effective approach, though its broader significance to the HMM community and energy experts merits further assessment.
