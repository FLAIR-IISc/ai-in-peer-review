- Problem: Blind source separation for time series (energy disaggregation) using signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM).
- Method: Introduces soft SACs encouraging each source’s total signal to match specified targets.
- Inference: Proposes a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation via pairwise transition variables with PSD matrices.
- Theory: Connects the SAC-regularized objective to posterior regularization and interprets the penalty as a prior on state sequences.
- Empirics: Shows substantial NDE and SAE improvements on a toy example and UK HES low-frequency data versus AFHMM and AFAMAP, with similar runtimes.
- Novelty: The application of aggregate constraints to AFHMMs and the specific CQP relaxation are new.
- Strength — conceptual clarity/general applicability: SACs are natural for many BSS settings and aid identifiability without constraining fine-grained dynamics.
- Strength — principled formulation: Posterior-regularization link gives solid theoretical grounding.
- Strength — tractable inference: CQP relaxation is well-motivated, explicit PSD structure, solvable with standard solvers.
- Strength — empirical gains and robustness: Marked improvements and resilience to modest mis-specification of aggregate targets.
- Strength — practical relevance: Targets challenging low-frequency smart meter NILM problems.
- Weakness — rounding/integrality: Unclear how fractional CQP solutions are converted to discrete state sequences and how this affects NDE/SAE; needs ablation.
- Weakness — hyperparameter setting: λi fixed to 1; requires guidance and sensitivity analysis for λ selection.
- Weakness — estimating µi0: Lacks practical procedures and sensitivity analysis for estimating aggregate targets when true totals are unknown.
- Weakness — baselines/scope: Comparisons limited to AFAMAP; should include semi-Markov/duration models and interleaved FHMMs.
- Weakness — scalability: CVX solvers may not scale to longer series or larger state spaces; discuss complexity and possible specialized solvers (ADMM, projected Newton, exploiting block structure).
- Clarity — notation/acronyms: Paper is dense with acronyms and heavy notation; recommend reducing acronyms and adding a notation table/appendix.
- Clarity — intuition for SACs: Add a concrete numerical example to illustrate why SACs help and clarify hard vs. soft constraints.
- Clarity — prior vs posterior: Explicitly state whether the penalty is treated as a prior or posterior regularization early in the paper.
- Clarity — dataset/protocol details: Need details on handling unmodeled appliances, train/test leakage, distribution of state counts per appliance, and effect of K.
- Originality emphasis: Should highlight how SACs differ from change-sparsity or duration constraints and their unique benefits for NILM.
- Significance: Improvements on low-frequency data and generality of SACs suggest impact for NILM and other BSS domains.
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, general idea with theoretical grounding; convex relaxation makes inference practical; consistent empirical gains; addresses low-frequency identifiability.
- Cons for acceptance: Readability issues; missing details on rounding, hyperparameter tuning, and µi0 estimation; limited baselines and unclear scalability.
- Overall assessment: Timely, well-motivated contribution with empirical value; recommend revisions on clarity and experimental details — reviewer leans weak accept.
