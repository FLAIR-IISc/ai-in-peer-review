- Proposes framing blind source separation as an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge.  
- Key idea: use expected usage totals over intervals to improve identifiability of latent time-series components.  
- Main application: energy disaggregation—separating appliance-level consumption from mains readings with aggregate usage priors.  
- Formulates MAP estimation for hidden chains and reformulates the objective as a convex optimization problem.  
- Demonstrates the method on synthetic and real household energy data.  
- Experiments are thorough and show the method outperforms AFHMM baseline and AFAMAP; gains are statistically significant and optimization time is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Question: toy example uses very low observation noise—need clarification whether this was chosen to highlight separation and whether such SNRs reflect real settings.  
- Suggest reporting typical SNRs between mains readings and summed appliance outputs for the energy experiments.  
- Theoretical development is clear but would be strengthened by a formal proof of convexity for equation (6).  
- Request complexity and scaling analysis (sequence length, number of hidden states) and comparison of scaling to AFAMAP.  
- Manuscript is well written; minor issues include a possible notational inconsistency on page 4 before eq. (6).  
- Approach appears novel and original; rebuttal addressed reviewer questions satisfactorily and clarifications should be incorporated in revision.
