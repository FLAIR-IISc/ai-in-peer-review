This paper introduces a new approach to blind source separation by casting the task as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The motivation is to leverage domain knowledge to improve decomposition of time series into constituent components. The running example is energy disaggregation, where appliance-level usage can be more accurately separated when prior information about each appliance’s total (expected) consumption over a time window is available. The objective optimized for separation—the log posterior of the latent chains given the observations—is reformulated as a convex optimization problem. The method is demonstrated on both a synthetic toy dataset and real domestic energy consumption readings.

The paper is technically sound with a robust simulation section. As expected, the method outperforms a standard AFMM and AFAMAP—neither of which uses SACs. Improvements are shown to be statistically significant via t-tests, and the optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. One question about the results: why was such a low noise variance chosen in the toy example? Was this intended to make Figure 2 clearly show that the proposed method separates signals well while others do not? Clarification would be helpful; otherwise the setup risks being overly simplistic. Are these signal-to-noise ratios typical in real data? Relatedly, the authors could comment on the SNR between the mains readings and the aggregate appliance outputs in the energy application.

The theoretical development is generally well presented and structured. The paper would benefit from a proof that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex, as this is not fully evident from the current reasoning. In addition, scaling arguments with respect to sequence length and the number of hidden states would strengthen the work and complement the simulations. Are the methods tractable for substantially larger datasets and state spaces, and how does this compare with AFAMAP?

The manuscript is well written and organized. The introduction motivates the problem effectively and provides a clear review of relevant literature, though I am not sufficiently familiar with the area to guarantee completeness. I note a small potential error on page 4 (line 202): in the unnumbered equation preceding (6), the subscripts following p may not require commas, to be consistent with the earlier notation on page 3 (unless new notation is being introduced).

To my knowledge, the work is novel and original, although I am not an expert and cannot fully judge its significance for the HMM community or the energy application domain.

Addendum: The authors satisfactorily addressed my questions during the feedback phase, and I encourage them to include similar clarifications in the revised manuscript. This paper proposes a blind source separation method based on Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints, and it performs strongly compared to the state of the art, particularly on real energy disaggregation data.
