- Proposes AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation.
- Motivated by using domain knowledge (aggregate signal totals) to improve time-series component separation.
- Main application is energy disaggregation: splitting household mains into appliance-level usage.
- Reformulates the log-posterior objective as a convex optimization problem.
- Evaluated on both a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.
- Strong simulation section; method outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with improvements shown as statistically significant.
- Optimization speed reported to be comparable to AFAMAP.
- Question about toy experiment: noise variance appears very low—clarify if this was to make results visually clear or if such SNRs are typical in real data.
- Request comment on signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and aggregated appliance outputs in the energy application.
- Theoretical presentation is clear but needs a proof or self-contained argument that equation (6) is convex.
- Ask for theoretical scaling analysis with respect to data length and number of hidden states and tractability comparison to AFAMAP for larger problems.
- Paper is well written and organized; minor notation issue flagged (commas in subscripts before eq. (6)).
- Work appears novel, authors addressed reviewer questions during feedback, and reviewer encourages incorporating clarifications in the revision.
