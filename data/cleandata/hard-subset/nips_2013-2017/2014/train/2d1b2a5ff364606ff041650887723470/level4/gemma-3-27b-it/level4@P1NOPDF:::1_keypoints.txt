- Proposes a new blind source separation method: additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).  
- Motivates approach with domain knowledge improving time-series component separation, exemplified by energy disaggregation.  
- Formulates inference by maximizing the log-posterior of hidden chains and transforms the objective into a convex optimization problem.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on both a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.  
- Technically sound with a strong experimental section and clear writing.  
- Method outperforms standard AFHMM and recent AFAMAP, which do not use SACs.  
- Improvements are statistically significant (t-tests) and optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Primary empirical concern: toy example uses very low noise variance—clarification needed whether this is for illustration or reflects real-world SNR; also request SNR discussion between mains and aggregate appliance signals.  
- Theoretical request: provide a formal proof of convexity for equation (6), since current reasoning is not fully convincing.  
- Additional theoretical request: scaling analysis for data length and number of hidden states to assess tractability on much larger datasets and comparison to AFAMAP.  
- Minor noted issue: potential notation inconsistency in the unnumbered equation preceding (6) (page 4, line 202).  
- Reviewer judges the work novel and original but defers full significance assessment to domain experts.  
- Authors addressed reviewer questions in feedback; reviewer asks that clarifications be incorporated into the next version.
