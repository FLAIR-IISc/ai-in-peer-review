- Introduces AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.  
- Leverages domain knowledge (expected total appliance usage) to improve separation of time-series components.  
- Transforms log-posterior maximization into a convex optimization problem and validates on synthetic and real-world energy data.  
- Technical contributions are sound and supported by a thorough simulation study.  
- Method consistently outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with statistically significant gains (t-tests).  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP, a practical advantage.  
- Concern: toy example uses unusually low noise variance—clarify whether this was deliberate or reflective of real-world SNR.  
- Request clarification of signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and aggregate appliance outputs in the energy application.  
- Theoretical exposition needs a formal proof that the procedure in Equation (6) is convex; current reasoning is insufficiently rigorous.  
- Request theoretical scaling analysis (e.g., with sequence length and hidden state count) to assess scalability vs AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated; literature review appears adequate though reviewer is not a domain specialist.  
- Minor notational inconsistency: commas in subscripts (e.g., p_{i,j,k}) before Eq (6) should be standardized.  
- Novel within the AFHMM literature, but broader significance to the HMM and energy-disaggregation communities needs clearer contextualization.  
- Authors addressed reviewer queries satisfactorily; recommend incorporating clarifications (noise/SNR) into the final version.
