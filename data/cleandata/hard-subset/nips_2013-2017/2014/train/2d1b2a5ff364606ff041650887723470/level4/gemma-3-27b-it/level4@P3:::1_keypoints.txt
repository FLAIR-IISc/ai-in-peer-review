- Proposes a novel blind source separation method: AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge.  
- Targets energy disaggregation: decomposes total household energy into appliance-level usage.  
- Frames inference as maximizing posterior over hidden states and shows equivalence to a convex program for efficient solution.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over baseline AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP on simulated and HES real-world data.  
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and comparable in computational cost to alternatives.  
- SACs address identifiability by incorporating sensible prior information about aggregate signals.  
- The convex formulation and connection to posterior regularization provide solid theoretical grounding.  
- Strengths include convex program formulation, empirical evaluation on real dataset, and balanced toy + realistic experiments.  
- Limitation: method relies on accurate prior estimates for aggregate signal values.  
- Weak justification for the low noise variance choice in the toy example; robustness to noise needs clarification.  
- Needs more discussion and analysis of signal-to-noise ratio effects and methods for handling noisy data.  
- Missing a formal proof of convexity for the optimization (equation 6) to strengthen theoretical rigor.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability (scaling with time steps, hidden states) and comparison to AFAMAP.  
- Minor notation inconsistency (comma in subscripts on p at page 4, line 202).  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with clear contributions and practical impact; reviewer recommends acceptance pending addressing the cited weaknesses.
