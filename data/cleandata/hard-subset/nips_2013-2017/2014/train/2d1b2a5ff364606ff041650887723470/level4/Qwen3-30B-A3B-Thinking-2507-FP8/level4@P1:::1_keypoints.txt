- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve blind source separation in AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs constrain total expected energy per appliance over time to address identifiability issues in BSS.  
- Formulates SAC inference as convex quadratic programs (CQPs) to efficiently handle non-Markovian constraints.  
- Provides a theoretical connection between SACs and posterior regularization, strengthening the method’s foundation.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP on synthetic and real (HES) datasets.  
- AFHMM+SAC with National Total Consumption (NTC) constraints reduces NDE by 33% and SAE by 16% versus AFAMAP (p<0.05).  
- Reports a 45% NDE reduction over AFHMM in Section 7.2.2, indicating substantial practical gains.  
- Computational efficiency matches AFAMAP, making SACs practical for large-scale applications.  
- Toy experiments illustrate SACs’ sensitivity to constraint accuracy, reinforcing their utility.  
- Paper omits a formal proof or discussion of convexity for the relaxed problem (Equation 6), weakening theoretical rigor.  
- The toy experiment uses a low noise variance (σ²=0.01) without justification; SNR relevance to real data should be clarified.  
- Contains minor notation inconsistencies (e.g., "AFMM" vs. "AFHMM") that should be corrected.  
- SAC concept is novel and likely generalizes beyond energy disaggregation (e.g., to audio processing).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting issues are minor and addressed in rebuttal.
