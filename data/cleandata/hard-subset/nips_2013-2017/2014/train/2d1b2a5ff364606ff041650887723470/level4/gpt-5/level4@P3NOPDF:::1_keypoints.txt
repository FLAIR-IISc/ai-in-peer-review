- Summary: Proposes AFHMM augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs), formulates a MAP objective and converts it to a convex optimization for blind source separation; evaluated on toy and real energy disaggregation data.  
- Strength: Leverages domain aggregate constraints to improve identifiability in an ill-posed problem.  
- Strength: Clear connection between AFHMM and convex optimization enabling practical inference.  
- Strength: Empirical results consistently outperform AFHMM baseline and AFAMAP on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: Improvements are statistically significant and runtime is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Strength: Well written with solid literature review.  
- Suggestion: Justify the unusually low noise variance in the synthetic experiment or clarify it was for illustration, and contextualize with real-data noise levels.  
- Suggestion: Report or comment on signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs) between mains and appliance signals in real data (approximate ranges or estimates).  
- Suggestion: Provide a concise proof, precise argument, or citation that the relaxed objective (Eq. 6) is convex under the stated constraints.  
- Suggestion: Add theoretical discussion of computational scaling (time/memory) with series length, number of chains, and state cardinality, and compare with AFAMAP.  
- Suggestion: Consider reporting effect sizes or confidence intervals in addition to t-test significance.  
- Minor: Fix notation commas near Eq. (6), correct AFMM→AFHMM typo, and briefly remind the reader of SACs’ role when they enter the optimization.  
- Overall: Strong, timely contribution with sound methodology and convincing empirical benefits; main needed clarifications are noise/SNR, convexity, and scalability.  
- Addendum: Authors addressed review questions in feedback; incorporate those clarifications into final paper.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
