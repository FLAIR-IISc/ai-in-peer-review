

This paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) for electricity usage modeling, incorporating a novel "signal aggregate constraint." The core contribution addresses a specific challenge in energy disaggregation, and the technical approach demonstrates potential utility for the field. However, significant issues with clarity and exposition currently undermine the paper's impact and readability.

The primary weakness lies in the paper's presentation. The exposition is dense and unnecessarily jargon-heavy, hindering comprehension. The authors introduce an excessive number of acronyms without sufficient justification or clear definitions upon first use. Several acronyms are unreasonably long (e.g., an 11-letter acronym for a constraint mechanism), creating a barrier for readers and diminishing the paper's accessibility. For instance, the constraint is referred to as "FHM-AGC" early on without adequate explanation of its components or necessity. The reader is forced to constantly refer back to definitions, disrupting the flow of understanding. The authors should rigorously review all acronyms, eliminate non-essential ones, and ensure any necessary long acronyms are introduced with a clear, concise explanation of their meaning and purpose. Defining all acronyms at first use is non-negotiable for clarity.

A more substantive concern pertains to the handling of the constraint. The paper describes imposing a "signal aggregate constraint" but the precise relationship between this constraint and the model's probabilistic structure requires greater precision. The current phrasing suggests the authors are attempting to constrain the *posterior* density directly. However, the constraint is inherently imposed on the *prior* density over the hidden states (or their sufficient statistics), which then propagates to the posterior via Bayes' rule. The authors should explicitly clarify this point. The statement "they place a constraint on a prior density" is accurate but needs to be stated clearly and early, framing it as an *a priori* constraint imposed to enforce a specific structural property (e.g., ensuring the sum of disaggregated signals matches the total meter reading). This is a critical technical detail that needs unambiguous articulation to avoid confusion about the model's mechanics. The paper would benefit from a dedicated subsection or a clearer paragraph early in the methodology describing the *origin* and *rationale* for the constraint within the Bayesian framework.

While the model's application to electricity usage is relevant, the paper lacks sufficient detail on the practical implementation and computational considerations. How is the constraint enforced numerically within the inference algorithm (e.g., the EM procedure)? What is the computational overhead compared to standard FHMMs? A brief discussion of these points, even if preliminary, would strengthen the practical contribution. Similarly, the experimental evaluation, while present, could be more compelling. The comparison to baselines is limited (e.g., only one or two competitors). Including results on a larger or more diverse dataset, or providing more nuanced analysis (e.g., disaggregation accuracy per appliance type, robustness to noise), would better demonstrate the model's advantages. The current results, while showing improvement, do not fully convince of the constraint's necessity or the model's superiority over simpler alternatives.

The writing, while technically sound in parts, suffers from grammatical inconsistencies and awkward phrasing that further impede readability. For example, sentences like "the Authors are looking for a constraint to be placed on the posterior density and so they place a constraint on a prior density" are grammatically imprecise and logically confusing. This should be rephrased to clearly state the authors' action (imposing a prior constraint) and its consequence (indirectly constraining the posterior). Careful proofreading for grammar, sentence structure, and logical flow is essential.

In summary, the paper addresses a relevant problem with a potentially useful model extension. The core idea has merit, and the technical approach is plausible. However, the current presentation severely limits its impact. The excessive jargon, poorly explained acronyms, and lack of clarity regarding the constraint's precise probabilistic foundation are major barriers to understanding. The experimental section also lacks sufficient depth to fully validate the claimed benefits.

**Recommendation:** The paper requires substantial revision before it can be accepted. The authors must significantly improve the clarity of exposition by:
1.  Drastically reducing the number of acronyms and ensuring all are clearly defined at first use (eliminating unnecessarily long ones).
2.  Explicitly and unambiguously stating that the signal aggregate constraint is an *a priori* constraint imposed on the prior density, explaining its necessity and how it propagates to the posterior.
3.  Providing clearer details on the implementation of the constraint during inference and a more robust experimental evaluation.
4.  Carefully revising the language for grammatical correctness and logical flow.

Addressing these points would transform the paper from one with a good idea but poor presentation into a valuable contribution to the field. As it stands, the current version does not meet the standards for publication at NIPS.
