- Proposes additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic program for relaxed AFHMM with SAC.  
- Shows promising improvements on synthetic and real-world data, with significant gains in NDE and SAE versus AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-written with solid mathematical foundation and clear motivation.  
- Evaluation indicates substantial performance boost compared to baseline and recent state-of-the-art.  
- Comparison to AFHMM and AFAMAP may be unfair because those methods use less data than SAC AFHMM.  
- Suggests exploring other methods that could utilize SAC (even if less computationally efficient) for a more comprehensive comparison.  
- Figure 1 results are unclear and may show AFAMAP outperforming SAC AFHMM at larger distances, implying sensitivity to poorly chosen constraints.  
- Discussion can be misleading by implying SAC always improves performance; needs analysis of constraint quality impact.  
- Paper focuses mostly on simple aggregate constraints and lacks discussion on more complex constraints (e.g., mutual exclusivity, time-duration limits).  
- Recommends extending the algorithm to incorporate richer constraint types and analyze their integration.  
- Overall technically sound with supported claims, but discussion and comparisons need strengthening.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong empirical gains, clear exposition; Rejection arguments: potentially unfair comparisons, limited constraint scope, insufficient analysis of constraint effects.
