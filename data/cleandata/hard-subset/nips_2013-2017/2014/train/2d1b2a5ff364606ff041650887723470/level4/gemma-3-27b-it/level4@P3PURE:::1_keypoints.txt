- Proposes a novel blind source separation method: Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).
- Motivation: leverage domain knowledge (aggregate signal information) to improve time-series component separation.
- Application: demonstrated on energy disaggregation to better recover individual appliance usage using expected total usage constraints.
- Method: converts the log-posterior objective of hidden chains into a convex optimization problem.
- Empirical evaluation: tested on both synthetic data and real domestic energy consumption datasets.
- Results: outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with statistically significant improvements (t-tests).
- Computational performance: optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP.
- Concern: synthetic experiments use a relatively low noise variance—clarification needed whether this is for illustration or representative of real-world SNRs; also request SNR comment between mains and appliance aggregates.
- Theory: a formal proof of convexity for equation (6) would strengthen the paper; current reasoning is not fully conclusive.
- Scalability: recommend theoretical scaling analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and empirical tractability tests on larger datasets/state spaces, with comparisons to AFAMAP.
- Minor issue: potential notation inconsistency in the unnumbered equation before (6) (page 4, line 202) regarding commas in subscripts.
- Presentation: paper is well-written, organized, and the literature review appears comprehensive.
- Novelty: work appears original, though reviewer (non-expert) cannot fully assess its significance to the HMM or energy-disaggregation communities.
- Addendum: authors satisfactorily addressed reviewers’ questions during feedback; incorporate those clarifications into the final version.
