- Proposes blind source separation via Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMM) augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).  
- Motivation: leverage domain knowledge (aggregate signal totals) to improve time-series component separation.  
- Application example: energy disaggregation, using total expected appliance usage to aid separation.  
- Reformulates the MAP objective into a convex optimization problem for inference.  
- Demonstrates method on a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.  
- Empirical results: method outperforms AFMM and AFAMAP, with improvements statistically significant by t-tests.  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Reviewer questions use of low noise variance in the toy example and whether the chosen SNR reflects real-world data; requests clarification on mains vs. appliance aggregate SNR.  
- Theoretical presentation is clear but needs a formal proof that equation (6) is convex.  
- Reviewer requests theoretical scaling analysis (data length and number of hidden states) and comparison of scalability with AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-written and has a comprehensive literature review (reviewer not an expert).  
- Minor issue: possible notation/subscript inconsistency in the equation before (6) on page 4 (line 202).  
- Novel and original work, but reviewer cannot fully judge significance to the HMM and energy communities.  
- Authors addressed reviewer queries satisfactorily; reviewer encourages incorporating those clarifications in the next version.
