- Paper proposes AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation, showing promising synthetic and real-world results.  
- The manuscript is well-written with a solid mathematical foundation and clear problem motivation.  
- Evaluation reports significant improvements in normalized displacement error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Comparisons are potentially unfair: baselines (AFHMM without SAC, AFAMAP) use less data than SACAFHMM.  
- Recommend comparing to other methods that also exploit SAC, even if they are less computationally efficient.  
- Figure 1 results are unclear; AFAMAP may outperform SACAFHMM for distances >10^4, contradicting claims of consistent improvement.  
- Need a more nuanced discussion of results, including limitations and cases where constraints hurt performance.  
- Suggest investigating the impact of poor or noisy constraints on algorithm performance.  
- Recommend extending the algorithm to handle more complex constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality limits).  
- Suggest adding temporal constraints (e.g., max usage duration) as an extension.  
- Overall assessment: solid foundation with clear future-work directions to incorporate richer constraints.
