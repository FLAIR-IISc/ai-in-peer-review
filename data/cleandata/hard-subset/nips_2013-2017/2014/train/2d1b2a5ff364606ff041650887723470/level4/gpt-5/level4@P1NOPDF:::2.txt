This paper develops an additive factorial hidden Markov model to model electricity usage, incorporating a “signal aggregate” constraint. The technical quality appears good, but the paper is very difficult to read. There are too many acronyms, and several are extremely long (some up to 11 letters). Please reduce the number of acronyms, shorten them where possible, and define each clearly at first use. Consider adding a brief glossary and simplifying notation to improve readability.

A central point of confusion concerns how the constraint is imposed. It appears that the authors aim to constrain the posterior density but implement this by constraining the prior. If the intention is to place a restriction on the posterior, the paper should explain precisely how that restriction is enforced in the posterior (e.g., via constrained inference, posterior projection, or likelihood-based constraints). If, instead, the constraint is introduced a priori through the prior, then the paper should state this explicitly: you have an a priori constraint, and consequently the posterior is constrained as a result. Clarifying this distinction is important for both interpretability and correctness.

In summary, the contribution is potentially valuable, but the presentation needs substantial polishing. Streamline the acronyms and notation, and clearly articulate whether the constraint is imposed a priori (via the prior) or directly on the posterior, including the implications for inference.
