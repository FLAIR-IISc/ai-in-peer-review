The paper proposes an extension to the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) model by incorporating additional constraints that require the sum of individual chain outputs to be close to a known aggregate value. The authors introduce a convex relaxation to perform MAP inference and apply the algorithm to real-world datasets, including energy disaggregation tasks across 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings and 10 homes with higher-frequency readings.

Although the ideas presented in the paper are relatively straightforward, and the authors employ a simple optimization formulation to solve the problems, the work remains compelling due to its application to large real-world datasets. As a paper that combines both applied and algorithmic elements, it is successful in demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

To further improve the paper, I suggest addressing the following points:

The authors motivate the use of AFAMAP by highlighting its ability to enforce the constraint that one chain stage is active at a time, which can lead to more integer solutions. It would be helpful to discuss whether the SACAFHMM optimization formulation achieves a similar effect or if the resulting solutions still exhibit lower error despite being non-integral.

It appears that the AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas are orthogonal, and it is possible to combine them by incorporating the SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization formulation. Although I do not expect a response to this suggestion during the author rebuttal, I strongly encourage the authors to explore this combination in a future version of the paper.

The authors occasionally conflate models and optimization procedures, which can lead to imprecision. For instance, comparing the results of "AFHMM" versus "AFAMAP" is not accurate, as AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP solutions to the AFHMM. I believe the authors actually refer to alternating Viterbi optimization when mentioning AFHMM. Clarifying this distinction would be beneficial, as it highlights the potential for combining the strategies of different algorithms to achieve better performance.

Overall, the paper proposes a simple yet effective extension and optimization to solve a variant of the AFHMM, and the results on real-world data are compelling. With some revisions to address the mentioned points, the paper has the potential to be even stronger.
