- Proposes extending additive factorial HMM by adding constraints so sum of chain outputs matches a known aggregate, and presents a convex relaxation for MAP inference applied to 100 low-frequency and 10 high-frequency UK-home datasets.

- Methodology is straightforward and uses a simple optimization formulation, but is compelling due to application to large real-world datasets and combined applied/algorithmic contribution.

- Request clarification whether SACAFHMM’s enforcement of single-chain states similarly improves the relaxation (yielding more integral solutions) or reduces error even when solutions are non-integral.

- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas appear orthogonal and recommends investigating incorporation of SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP’s optimization in a final version.

- Points out the authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures; asks for precise distinction between the AFHMM model and AFAMAP algorithm and whether reported “AFHMM” results are actually alternating Viterbi.

- Suggests exploring combinations of the different algorithmic strategies, as hybrid approaches might outperform the presented methods.

- Overall assessment: a simple extension with compelling real-world results; paper is reasonable and successful.
