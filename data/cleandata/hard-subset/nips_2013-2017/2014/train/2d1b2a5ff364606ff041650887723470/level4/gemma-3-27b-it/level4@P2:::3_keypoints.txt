- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in an AFHMM and formulates approximate inference as a convex quadratic program, showing substantial improvements on simulated and real data.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and the mathematical formulation is sound.  
- Experimental results show significant reductions in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Connection to posterior regularization (Section 6) strengthens the theoretical grounding.  
- Comparison may be unfair because AFHMM+SAC uses information (µi0) not provided to baseline models, possibly inflating gains.  
- Authors should compare to or discuss other methods that also leverage prior knowledge and consider computational trade-offs.  
- Figure 1 is unclear and potentially misleading: AFHMM+SAC can perform worse than AFAMAP when constraint distance > 10^4, so sensitivity to inaccurate constraints needs discussion.  
- The paper only explores simple total-energy constraints; extending to richer constraints (co-occurrence, duration) and describing how the QP would adapt would increase impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel SAC incorporation, strong empirical gains, sound formulation, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially unfair baseline comparison, misleading presentation of results (Figure 1), and limited exploration of more complex constraints.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; accept with minor revisions addressing comparison fairness, Figure 1 interpretation, and extensions to richer constraints.
