This paper introduces a novel approach to blind source separation by formulating it as an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) and incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The key motivation is to leverage domain knowledge to improve the decomposition of time series data, as demonstrated through the application of energy disaggregation. Specifically, the method aims to better separate individual appliance energy usage by incorporating information about the expected total consumption of each appliance over time. The resulting objective function – maximizing the log posterior distribution of hidden states given observed data – is transformed into a convex optimization problem. The method’s effectiveness is empirically validated using both a synthetic dataset and real-world domestic energy consumption data.

The paper is technically sound and includes a robust experimental evaluation. As anticipated, the proposed method outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP (a recent alternative) due to the inclusion of SACs, with statistically significant improvements demonstrated via t-tests. The authors also report comparable optimization speeds to AFAMAP. Regarding the results, I question the choice of a low noise variance in the synthetic example. Clarification is needed to understand whether this was intended to clearly illustrate the method’s separation capabilities, and whether the resulting problem setup is representative of real-world scenarios. Alternatively, the authors could discuss the typical signal-to-noise ratio encountered between mains readings and aggregate appliance outputs in the energy disaggregation application.

The theoretical framework is well-presented and organized. However, a proof demonstrating the convexity of the final procedure in equation (6) would strengthen the paper, as the current reasoning is not entirely convincing. Furthermore, including theoretical scaling analysis – concerning data length and the number of hidden states – would complement the simulation results. Specifically, it would be valuable to understand the tractability of this method with significantly larger datasets and state spaces, and how it compares to AFAMAP in such scenarios. 

The paper is well-written and structured, with a clear motivation and thorough description of related work. While I am not deeply familiar with this specific problem area, the introduction provides adequate context. I note a potential minor typographical error on page 4 (line 202), in the unnumbered equation preceding (6), where the commas in the subscripts of 'p' appear inconsistent with the notation used on page 3, unless a new convention has been introduced. 

To the best of my knowledge, the work is novel and original, although I cannot assess its broader significance within the HMM community or the energy application domain due to my limited expertise.

Addendum: The authors have satisfactorily addressed my concerns during the feedback phase, and I recommend incorporating these clarifications into the final version. This paper presents a method for performing blind source separation using Additive Factorial HMMs augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints. The method exhibits strong performance, particularly when applied to real data from an energy disaggregation problem, exceeding the performance of current state-of-the-art techniques.
