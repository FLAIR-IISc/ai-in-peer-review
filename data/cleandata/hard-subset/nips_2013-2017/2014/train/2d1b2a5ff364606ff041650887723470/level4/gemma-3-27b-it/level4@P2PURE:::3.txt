This paper introduces an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model with Signal Aggregate Constraints (AFHMM-SAC) for blind source separation. The authors formulate a convex quadratic problem to efficiently approximate the AFHMM-SAC model, and demonstrate promising performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written and grounded in solid mathematical principles.
- The problem motivation is clear and compelling.
- Evaluation results demonstrate a significant improvement in performance metrics, specifically Normalized Distance Error (NDE) and Source Allocation Error (SAE).

**Areas for Improvement:**
- The reported results compare AFHMM-SAC to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which utilize less data. This discrepancy raises concerns about the fairness of the comparison. Are there alternative approaches that could also leverage SAC but are computationally less demanding?
- Figure 1 lacks clarity. The data suggest that AFAMAP outperforms AFHMM-SAC when the distance exceeds 10<sup>4</sup>. This implies that poorly specified constraints can lead to significantly degraded performance – a nuance that is not adequately discussed, and which contradicts the paper’s overall claim of consistent improvement.
- While the current constraints are effective, I anticipated the exploration of more complex constraint types. For example, constraints prohibiting simultaneous use of conflicting appliances (e.g., heater and cooler) or limiting the number of active devices (e.g., no more than 3 out of 10 light bulbs), or imposing time limits on appliance usage (e.g., grinders operating for less than 10 minutes) would broaden the applicability of the model. Could the algorithm be extended to accommodate such diverse constraint types? The paper is well-written and provides a strong motivation for the proposed work; the authors correctly identify extending the model to encompass a wider range of constraints as a direction for future research.
