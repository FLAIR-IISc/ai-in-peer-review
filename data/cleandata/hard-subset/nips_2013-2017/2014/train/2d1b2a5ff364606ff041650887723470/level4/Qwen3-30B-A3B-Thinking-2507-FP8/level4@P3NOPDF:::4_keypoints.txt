- Proposes AFHMM extension by adding constraints that force sum of chain outputs to approximate a known aggregate value.
- Develops a convex relaxation for MAP inference (SACAFHMM) and applies it to energy disaggregation on two real-world datasets.
- Empirical results on large-scale data are compelling, bridging applied and algorithmic contributions.
- Clarify how SACAFHMM’s relaxation affects integrality: does it encourage integer solutions like AFAMAP’s per-chain state constraint, or just lower error with non-integral outputs?
- Correctly distinguish model vs algorithm: AFHMM is the model; AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are optimization algorithms—use consistent terminology in comparisons.
- Note orthogonality of AFAMAP and SACAFHMM: their constraints/optimizers can be combined and should be described as modular rather than competing.
- Strengthen ablation study: isolate impact of the aggregate constraint versus the relaxation (e.g., SACAFHMM with/without constraint, AFAMAP with added constraint).
- For the high-frequency (10-home) dataset, explicitly discuss how missing appliance-level monitoring is handled and the resulting limitations.
- Better justify why the aggregate constraint is well-suited to energy disaggregation (smart meters provide known total household consumption).
- Improve writing precision—e.g., explicitly call the aggregate value “known” and fix minor grammatical errors.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-motivated, and empirically convincing; addressing the above clarifications would make the paper stronger.
