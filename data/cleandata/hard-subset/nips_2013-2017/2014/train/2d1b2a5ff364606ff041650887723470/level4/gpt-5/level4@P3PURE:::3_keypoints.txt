- Proposes AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and formulates a convex quadratic program for a relaxed AFHMM.  
- Demonstrates promising results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well written and grounded in solid mathematical foundations.  
- Problem motivation is clear and compelling.  
- Empirical results show notable improvements in NDE and SAE metrics.  
- Concern about fairness of comparisons: baselines may use less information than SACAFHMM; request SAC-enabled alternative baselines for apples-to-apples comparison.  
- Figure 1 interpretation unclear: AFAMAP appears to outperform SACAFHMM for distances >10^4, suggesting poorly specified constraints can hurt; ask for clarification on when SAC helps vs. hurts and sensitivity to constraint quality.  
- Ask whether more expressive constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration) can be supported and what formulation/optimization and computational changes that would require.  
- Overall assessment: promising and empirically effective approach; future work should extend constraint classes and clarify comparative evaluation.
