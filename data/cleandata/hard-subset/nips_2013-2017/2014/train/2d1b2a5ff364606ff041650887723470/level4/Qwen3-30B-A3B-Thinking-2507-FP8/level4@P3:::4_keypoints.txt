- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to enforce known total signal values per source and address identifiability in BSS.  
- Motivates SACs by observing that total signal magnitudes vary across sources and domain knowledge often provides reasonable aggregate estimates.  
- Incorporates SACs into AFHMMs and reformulates the constrained inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient inference.  
- Evaluates on synthetic data and 100-household HES energy-disaggregation data, showing robust performance and real-world impact.  
- Reports that AFHMM+SAC with National Total Consumption constraints outperforms AFAMAP by 33% in NDE and 16% in SAE across 100 households.  
- Connects to prior work: builds on AFHMM (Ghahramani & Jordan) and AFAMAP (Kolter & Jaakkola) and relates formulation to posterior regularization.  
- Presents SACs as a novel, domain-knowledge constraint type effectively situated within broader BSS and energy-disaggregation literature.  
- Strength: directly addresses identifiability with intuitive motivation that resonates with practitioners.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation, including robustness to constraint errors and practical efficiency comparable to AFHMM/AFAMAP.  
- Criticism: conflates model names with optimization procedures—should clarify AFHMM vs AFAMAP (algorithm vs model).  
- Missed opportunity: does not explore combining SACs with AFAMAP’s state-change constraint, despite being orthogonal and easily integrable.  
- Unclear whether SACs improve the quality of relaxed (continuous) solutions toward integers or if gains come solely from the constraint itself.  
- Needs more explanation of why SACs are especially effective for energy disaggregation and their applicability to other BSS domains.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically relevant, clear empirical gains; recommendation to accept with suggested clarifications and additional analyses.
