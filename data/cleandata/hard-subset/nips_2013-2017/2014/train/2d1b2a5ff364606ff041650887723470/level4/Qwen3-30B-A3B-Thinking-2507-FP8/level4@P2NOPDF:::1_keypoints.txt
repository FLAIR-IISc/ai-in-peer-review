- Introduces AFHMM enhanced with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.  
- Core insight: incorporating domain-specific priors (expected appliance usage) improves time-series separation.  
- SACs leverage prior appliance consumption knowledge to isolate appliance-level signatures from aggregate readings.  
- Objective derived from log-posterior of hidden states is reformulated as a convex optimization problem.  
- Validated on synthetic and real-world energy datasets, showing clear advantages over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Statistical significance established via t-tests; computational efficiency comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Concern: toy experiment uses very low noise variance (σ²=0.01); needs clarification whether this reflects realistic SNRs for mains vs. appliance readings.  
- The convexity claim for Equation (6) lacks a rigorous proof and needs stronger justification.  
- Missing theoretical scaling analysis with respect to data length and number of hidden states to assess tractability and scalability.  
- Writing is precise and well-structured with strong motivation and literature context.  
- Minor notation inconsistency on page 4 (line 202) around subscripts p_{i,j,k} that should be standardized.  
- Method appears novel within AFHMM literature, though reviewer has limited domain expertise in energy disaggregation.  
- Empirical results—especially on real energy data—are strong and suggest practical value of the SAC framework.  
- Overall: technically solid contribution; addressing convexity proof, scaling analysis, and noise-variance justification would substantially strengthen the paper.
