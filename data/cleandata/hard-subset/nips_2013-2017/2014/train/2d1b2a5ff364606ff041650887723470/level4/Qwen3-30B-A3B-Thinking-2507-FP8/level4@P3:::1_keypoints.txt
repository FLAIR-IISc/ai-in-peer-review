- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and integrates them into an additive factorial HMM with a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Targets energy disaggregation and demonstrates SACs constrain each source's total signal to be near a specified value (e.g., appliance daily energy).  
- Empirical results show large improvements on real data: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. AFHMM and 29% vs. AFAMAP, with paired t-tests for significance.  
- Technical approach is sound with a well-motivated convex relaxation for inference.  
- Needs clearer theoretical justification for the convexity of the optimization (equation 6).  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity scaling with data length and number of hidden states for assessing practicality.  
- Paper is well-written and structured, with a clearly described experimental setup; minor notation inconsistency noted in transition probability subscripts.  
- SACs are an original, practically useful class of constraints and can be derived within posterior regularization, strengthening theoretical ties.  
- Results are significant and likely generalize beyond energy disaggregation; suggests future work integrating SACs into unsupervised methods.  
- Weaknesses include convexity justification, scaling analysis, a minor notational error, and a toy example with unrealistically low noise variance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically valuable contribution; recommendation—Accept.
