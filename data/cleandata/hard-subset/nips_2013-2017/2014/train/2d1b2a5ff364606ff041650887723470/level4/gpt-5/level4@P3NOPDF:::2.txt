Summary:
The paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (FHMM) for modeling electricity usage, augmented with a “signal aggregate constraint” to ensure consistency between the sum of component signals and the observed aggregate consumption. The idea is well-motivated by the structure of energy data and addresses a central challenge in disaggregation and structured time-series modeling.

Strengths:
- The modeling choice is natural for electricity usage, where aggregate consumption is the sum of multiple underlying sources.
- Introducing an explicit aggregate constraint is conceptually appealing and likely to improve identifiability and inference stability in additive FHMMs.
- The technical development appears sound overall, and the work has clear practical relevance.

Weaknesses and clarity concerns:
- The paper is difficult to read. There are too many acronyms, some of them very long, which impedes comprehension. Many definitions could be replaced with descriptive phrases or shorter names. If acronyms must be used, define them once, use them consistently, and consider a notation/acronym table.
- The probabilistic status of the “signal aggregate constraint” is not clearly articulated. It reads as though the authors intend to constrain the posterior but implement this by constraining the prior. If the approach restricts the support of the latent states/parameters via the prior, then this is an a priori (hard) constraint; the posterior is thereby implicitly constrained because it inherits the prior’s support. If instead the approach adds a penalty or soft preference, this is regularization and should be framed as such (e.g., via a prior with specific mass or a modified likelihood). Please be explicit about:
  - Whether the constraint is hard (support-restricting) or soft (penalty/regularizer).
  - Where the constraint enters the model: in the prior, the likelihood, or the variational objective.
  - How the constraint is enforced during inference (e.g., projected updates, augmented messages, Lagrange multipliers).
- The inference procedure needs clearer exposition. Provide a high-level algorithmic summary before diving into equations. Explain message passing and factorization assumptions for the factorial structure, and detail computational scaling with the number of factors and state cardinalities.
- The motivation and intuition for the constraint should be introduced earlier with a simple illustrative example showing how it narrows the hypothesis space and improves disaggregation.
- If the model targets disaggregation, identifiability is a serious concern in additive FHMMs. Please discuss what conditions or priors/constraints make the components recoverable, even if only heuristically, and provide references or empirical diagnostics.
- The experimental section (if present) would benefit from:
  - An ablation contrasting the model with and without the aggregate constraint.
  - Sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters controlling the constraint strength (if soft).
  - Clear baselines (e.g., standard FHMM, HMM mixtures, or nonprobabilistic disaggregation methods) and consistent metrics.
  - Runtime and scalability results as factors/components increase.

Specific suggestions for improving readability:
- Reduce acronym usage and avoid overly long acronyms. Prefer short, memorable names or descriptive phrases.
- Include a brief “generative story” of the model up front, followed by the constraint’s role in that story.
- Provide a table of notation and acronyms.
- Use a running toy example to connect equations to intuition.
- Clearly separate modeling assumptions, inference method, and implementation details.

Technical clarifications requested:
- Precisely define the “signal aggregate constraint.” Is it equality at each time step, equality in expectation, or an integral/energy-matching constraint over windows?
- If the constraint is implemented via the prior, specify the prior’s form and how it restricts support. If via the likelihood, show the modified likelihood and any normalization issues.
- For variational or approximate inference, show the objective being optimized and where the constraint appears. If Lagrangian methods are used, describe multiplier updates and convergence guarantees, if any.
- Discuss how missing data, sensor noise, or outliers are handled under the constraint.
- Comment on initialization and the possibility of local minima given the factorial structure and constraints.

Overall evaluation:
This is a timely and potentially impactful contribution: an additive FHMM with an explicit aggregate constraint is a sensible and useful idea for electricity usage modeling. However, the current presentation is unnecessarily opaque, primarily due to heavy acronym usage and an unclear probabilistic framing of the constraint. Clarifying that the constraint is a priori (if implemented in the prior) and making its role in the posterior explicit, together with a streamlined exposition and stronger empirical diagnostics, would significantly improve the paper’s accessibility and credibility. I encourage the authors to revise the text for clarity, provide a precise mathematical statement of the constraint and its enforcement in inference, and strengthen the experimental validation and ablations.
