- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation in AFHMMs, using domain knowledge of total source magnitudes.  
- Applies SACs to energy disaggregation, constraining per-appliance expected consumption.  
- Formulates constrained MAP inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) that is computationally efficient.  
- Reports empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and 29% vs AFAMAP, with paired t-test significance.  
- Validates robustness on a toy dataset showing resilience to constraint specification errors.  
- Strong technical quality and significance; CQP derivation and results are compelling.  
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization, strengthening theoretical grounding.  
- Writing is clear, well-structured, and appropriately motivated with solid related work coverage.  
- Novelty: SACs represent a generalizable constraint class distinct from prior HMM state/duration constraints.  
- Strengths highlighted: significant empirical improvements, practical utility in energy disaggregation, theoretical justification, competitive computational efficiency, and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: toy dataset uses very low noise variance (σ²=0.01), possibly unrealistically simplifying separation.  
- Weakness: convexity of the relaxed problem (Eq. 6) needs a formal proof for a NIPS audience.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical scaling/complexity analysis for longer sequences or larger state spaces.  
- Minor issue: notation inconsistency on page 4 (line 202) with commas separating subscripts in transition probabilities.
