- Introduces SACAFHMM: an AFHMM extension with a sum-to-aggregate constraint and a convex relaxation for MAP, evaluated on two real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 low-frequency UK homes; 10 high-frequency homes).  
- Strength: effective integration of method development and a practical, large-scale application, yielding impressive empirical results.  
- Clarification needed on relaxed-solution properties: does the convex relaxation produce integer-valued solutions like AFAMAP, do sum-to-aggregate constraints encourage integrality, or are gains from non-integral solutions?  
- Terminology/experimental clarity: avoid conflating models and inference algorithms—specify whether baselines refer to the AFHMM model or to particular inference methods (e.g., AFAMAP) for reproducibility.  
- Orthogonality/combination potential: AFAMAP ideas and SACAFHMM constraints appear modular and could likely be combined; authors should acknowledge and consider this hybrid approach.  
- Summary: well-motivated and promising work whose impact would increase by clarifying optimization behavior, solution nature, and precise algorithm/model distinctions.
