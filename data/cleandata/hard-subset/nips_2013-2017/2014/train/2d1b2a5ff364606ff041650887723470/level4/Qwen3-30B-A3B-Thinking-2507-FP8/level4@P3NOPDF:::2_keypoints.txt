- Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation with a novel "signal aggregate constraint."  
- Core idea is relevant and technically plausible but presentation severely undermines impact.  
- Exposition is dense, jargon-heavy, and uses excessive, poorly justified acronyms (including an 11-letter example).  
- Acronyms are often not defined on first use, forcing readers to backtrack and disrupting comprehension.  
- The relationship between the constraint and the model’s probabilistic structure is unclear—specifically whether it’s imposed on the prior or posterior.  
- Reviewer asserts the constraint is an a priori constraint on the prior density and this must be stated clearly and early.  
- Paper needs a dedicated subsection explaining the origin, rationale, and Bayesian framing of the signal aggregate constraint.  
- Lacks detail on numerical enforcement of the constraint within inference (e.g., EM) and on computational overhead versus standard FHMMs.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (few baselines, small/diverse dataset coverage) and does not fully justify the constraint’s necessity.  
- Missing more granular analyses (per-appliance accuracy, robustness to noise) to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Writing contains grammatical imprecision and awkward phrasing that further impede readability.  
- Major revisions required: drastically reduce/define acronyms, explicitly state the prior constraint and its propagation to the posterior, add implementation and computational details, strengthen experiments, and proofread for clarity.
