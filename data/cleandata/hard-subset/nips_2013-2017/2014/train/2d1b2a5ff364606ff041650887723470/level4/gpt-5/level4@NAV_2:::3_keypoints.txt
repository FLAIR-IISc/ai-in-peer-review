- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and formulates a convex quadratic program for a relaxed AFHMM+SAC inference.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation datasets (NDE and SAE).  
- Clear exposition and solid mathematical treatment with strong problem motivation.  
- Comparisons may be unfair: AFHMM+SAC uses extra prior aggregate information that baselines (AFHMM, AFAMAP) do not, raising apples-to-apples concerns.  
- Suggests exploring alternative methods that can incorporate SAC-like information (even with less efficient solvers) for fairer comparisons.  
- Figure 1 trends are hard to interpret; when aggregate mismatch > ~10^4 AFAMAP can outperform AFHMM+SAC, implying poorly specified constraints may harm performance.  
- Calls for a more nuanced robustness analysis to characterize sensitivity to constraint misspecification.  
- Expects integration of a broader class of domain constraints (mutual exclusivity, global activity limits, duration constraints) and asks whether the proposed framework can be extended to handle these richer types.  
- Overall: well written, compelling motivation for SACs, and a clear direction for future work to extend the constraint types.
