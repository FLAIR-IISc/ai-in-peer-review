- Proposes AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation, showing promising results on synthetic and real data.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and mathematically strong.
- Problem motivation is well-explained and sets up the approach effectively.
- Evaluation shows notable improvements on NDE and SAE metrics.
- Comparison is potentially unfair: AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP use less data; need comparisons to methods that also use SAC.
- Figure 1 is unclear: for distances >10^4 AFAMAP appears to outperform SAC-AFHMM, suggesting poorly chosen constraints can harm performance and contradict claims of consistent improvement.
- The discrepancy between results and discussion needs to be addressed and explained.
- The method should explore and discuss incorporation of more complex constraints (e.g., mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, time-duration limits like grinders’ max usage).
- A future direction is extending the model to accommodate diverse constraint types to improve applicability.
