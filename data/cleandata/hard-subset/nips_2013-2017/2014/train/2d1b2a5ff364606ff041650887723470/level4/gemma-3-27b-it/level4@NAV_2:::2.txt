An additive factorial hidden Markov model is developed to model electricity usage. The model incorporates a novel approach termed signal aggregate constraints (SACs). 

The paper presents a technically sound approach to incorporating domain knowledge into blind source separation, specifically within the context of energy disaggregation. The authors clearly identify the unidentifiability problem inherent in these tasks and propose a principled method for leveraging prior knowledge about expected aggregate signal values. The theoretical justification for the SACs is well-motivated, and the formulation of the convex quadratic program for approximate inference is a significant contribution. The experimental results on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset demonstrate substantial improvements over both the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method, validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in its presentation. The extensive use of acronyms, some of considerable length (e.g., AFHMM+SAC), hinders readability. While definitions are provided, the density of acronyms makes it challenging to follow the technical details. The connection between the introduced constraints and existing posterior regularization techniques, though acknowledged, could be more explicitly articulated. While the authors mention an a priori constraint, further emphasis on this interpretation would strengthen the conceptual clarity. 

The originality of the work lies in the specific application of signal aggregate constraints to the AFHMM framework and the demonstration of their effectiveness in energy disaggregation. While prior work exists on incorporating constraints into probabilistic models [4, 14, 13], this paper uniquely addresses the question of *what* types of domain knowledge to include, proposing SACs as a general and computationally tractable solution. The use of a real-world, high-resolution dataset (HES) is also commendable, as it addresses a practical challenge in the field.

The significance of this work is considerable. Energy disaggregation is a growing field with the potential to significantly impact energy conservation efforts. The demonstrated improvements in disaggregation accuracy suggest that the proposed method could be valuable for practical applications. More broadly, the concept of signal aggregate constraints could be applicable to other blind source separation problems across various domains. The paper advances the state of the art by providing a novel and effective technique for addressing the inherent challenges of identifiability in BSS.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Technically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Significant performance improvements on a real-world dataset.
*   Novel application of signal aggregate constraints.
*   Addresses a practically relevant and important problem.
*   Clear and thorough experimental evaluation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity due to excessive acronyms.
*   Conceptual link to posterior regularization could be strengthened.
