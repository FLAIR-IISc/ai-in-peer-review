

The paper introduces an extension to the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) by incorporating constraints that enforce the sum of individual chain outputs to approximate a known aggregate value. The authors propose a convex relaxation for MAP inference and apply this algorithm to real-world energy disaggregation datasets: one involving 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings, and another involving 10 homes with higher-frequency readings (though without per-appliance monitoring).  

While the core ideas are straightforward and the optimization formulation is relatively simple, the work remains compelling due to its application to large-scale real-world datasets. As a paper blending applied and algorithmic contributions, it is successful. The following points could strengthen the paper:  

The motivation for AFAMAP to enforce constraints on one chain's state at a time is to improve the relaxation's tendency to yield integer solutions. Does the SACAFHMM optimization formulation achieve a similar effect, or do its solutions retain lower error despite non-integral values? Clarifying this would further elucidate the distinctions between the approaches.  

Fundamentally, AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal: SACAFHMM constraints could readily be incorporated into AFAMAP's optimization. While I do not expect this to be addressed in the author response, I strongly encourage the authors to explore this combination for the final version.  

The paper occasionally conflates models and optimization procedures. For instance, comparing "AFHMM" versus "AFAMAP" is imprecise: AFAMAP is an algorithm for (approximately) computing MAP solutions to the AFHMM, and the term "AFHMM" here likely refers to alternating Viterbi optimization. This clarification is essential, as the strategies of these algorithms could be combined to potentially outperform the current approaches. The paper proposes a simple extension and optimization for a variant of the AFHMM, and while the real-world results are compelling, the distinction between model and algorithm must be made precise. Overall, the paper is reasonable.
