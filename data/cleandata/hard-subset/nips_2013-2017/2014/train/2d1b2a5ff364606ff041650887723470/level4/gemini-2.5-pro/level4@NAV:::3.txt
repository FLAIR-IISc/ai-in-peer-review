This work presents an additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors formulate the inference as a convex quadratic problem for a relaxed version of the model. The proposed technique demonstrates compelling performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
*   The manuscript is clearly written and rests on a solid mathematical foundation.
*   The motivation for the problem is well-articulated and compelling.
*   The empirical evaluation shows a significant improvement in key performance metrics, namely the Normalized D-error (NDE) and Signal-to-Artifact Error (SAE).

**Areas for Improvement:**
*   The experimental comparison seems somewhat imbalanced. The proposed SACAFHMM is benchmarked against AFHMM (without SAC) and AFAMAP, neither of which leverages the additional aggregate signal information. This raises a question of fairness, as the performance gain may stem primarily from access to more data. A more convincing comparison would involve baselines that can also incorporate SACs, even if they are less computationally efficient.
*   The results presented in Figure 1 are difficult to interpret and potentially contradict the paper's claims. For distances greater than 10^4, the AFAMAP baseline appears to outperform SACAFHMM. This possibility is not addressed in the discussion, which implies consistent superiority. Does this suggest that poorly-chosen constraints could degrade performance? The current narrative is misleading without acknowledging this potential outcome.
*   The work could be strengthened by exploring a richer set of constraints. For instance, real-world scenarios often involve logical or temporal constraints, such as mutual exclusion (e.g., a heater and a cooler cannot be active simultaneously), cardinality limits (e.g., no more than 3 out of 10 lights can be on), or duration constraints (e.g., an appliance running for less than 10 minutes). A discussion on whether the proposed framework can be extended to handle these more complex constraint types would significantly enhance the paper's contribution and better outline avenues for future work.
