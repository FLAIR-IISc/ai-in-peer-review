- Proposes a new blind source separation method: AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs), motivated by energy disaggregation.  
- Reformulates the log-posterior objective and converts it into a convex optimization problem.  
- Validated on toy data and real domestic energy readings, showing favorable performance relative to AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Performance improvements reported as statistically significant (t-tests); optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Raises concern whether the low noise variance in the toy example was chosen to highlight the method and whether it reflects realistic SNRs.  
- Requests clarification on the signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and aggregate appliance output in the real data.  
- Theoretical exposition is strong but the convexity argument for equation (6) is not fully conclusive.  
- Recommends adding a formal proof of convexity to strengthen the manuscript.  
- Suggests including theoretical scaling analysis with respect to data length and number of hidden states and comparison to AFAMAP for large datasets.  
- Notes the paper is well written, organized, and has a comprehensive literature review despite reviewer not being an expert.  
- Points out a minor notation correction: unnecessary commas in subscripts before p in the unnumbered equation before (6) on page 4.  
- Judges the work novel and original but cannot definitively assess its significance for the HMM community and energy domain.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-motivated, significant empirical gains, and computationally efficient.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of formal convexity proof, absence of scaling analysis, and need for SNR clarifications.
