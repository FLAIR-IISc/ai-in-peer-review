- Proposes extending additive factorial HMM by adding constraints that the sum of chain outputs match a known aggregate, with a convex relaxation for MAP inference.  
- Applies the method to real-world energy disaggregation: 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings and 10 homes with higher-frequency readings (no individual-appliance monitoring).  
- Ideas are straightforward but compelling due to application to large, real-world datasets and a mix of applied and algorithmic contributions.  
- Key motivation of AFAMAP is enforcing one-chain-change-at-a-time to improve relaxation integrality; paper should discuss whether SACAFHMM achieves similar integrality or reduced error.  
- AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal; authors should explore incorporating SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP’s optimization formulation.  
- Authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures—e.g., comparing “AFHMM” versus “AFAMAP” is imprecise since AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP on the AFHMM.  
- Likely use of “AFHMM” in comparisons actually refers to alternating Viterbi optimization; this needs clarification.  
- Combining the algorithmic strategies from the presented methods could yield improved performance and should be investigated.  
- Overall: a simple, well-applied extension to AFHMM with compelling real-data results, but presentation and clarity could be improved by addressing the above points.
