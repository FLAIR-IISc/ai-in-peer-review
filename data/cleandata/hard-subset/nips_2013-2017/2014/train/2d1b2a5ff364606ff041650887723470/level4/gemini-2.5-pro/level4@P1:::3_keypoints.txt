- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) within AFHMM to leverage known total source statistics and address identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Formulates constrained MAP inference and derives a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation, linked to posterior regularization.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation data, showing substantial improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP in NDE and SAE.  
- Novel and significant: provides an intuitive, generalizable way to incorporate practical domain knowledge into BSS.  
- Technically strong: elegant constrained MAP formulation and practical CQP relaxation with solid theoretical grounding.  
- Clearly written and well-organized, with precise model description and transparent experimental setup.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and compelling, demonstrating robustness and real-world utility.  
- Comparison fairness concern: AFHMM+SAC uses additional prior information (µi0) not given to baselines, complicating direct algorithmic comparisons.  
- Suggestion to examine whether other inference methods (e.g., particle-based) could incorporate SACs to isolate CQP’s contribution from the added prior.  
- Sensitivity issue: performance degrades with inaccurate priors and may be worse than unconstrained methods beyond a crossover point; more analysis and guidance on prior quality needed.  
- Generalization question: paper focuses on aggregate constraints but should discuss extending the CQP framework to other constraint types (mutual exclusion, cardinality, duration).  
- Overall conclusion: solid, novel contribution with strong results, but would benefit from addressing comparison fairness, prior robustness, and broader constraint generalization.
