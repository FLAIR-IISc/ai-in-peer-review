- Proposes a novel blind source separation method using Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMM) augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).  
- Motivation: leverage domain knowledge about component-wise aggregates to improve time-series separation; illustrated with energy disaggregation.  
- Reformulates the log-posterior over hidden states into a convex optimization problem.  
- Technical presentation is strong and supported by a comprehensive simulation section.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic and real-world domestic energy consumption data.  
- Consistently outperforms standard AFHMM and recent AFAMAP; improvements are statistically validated via t-tests.  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Minor experimental concern: toy example uses relatively low noise variance—clarify whether this is to highlight separation capability and whether it reflects real-world SNR; discuss SNR between total readings and appliance aggregates.  
- Theoretical gap: provide a formal proof of convexity for equation (6) as current argument is not fully conclusive.  
- Request theoretical scaling analysis (data length and number of hidden states) to assess tractability and compare scalability with AFAMAP.  
- Minor notation inconsistency on page 4 (line 202) with unnecessary commas in subscripts.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and appears novel, though reviewer lacks full expertise to judge broader significance.  
- Authors addressed reviewer queries during feedback; reviewer urges incorporating those clarifications into the revised manuscript.
