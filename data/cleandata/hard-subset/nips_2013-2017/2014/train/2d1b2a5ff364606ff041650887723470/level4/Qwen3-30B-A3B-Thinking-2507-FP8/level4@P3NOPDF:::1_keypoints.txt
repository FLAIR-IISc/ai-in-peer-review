- Proposes AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.
- Key innovation: use domain-specific aggregate information to improve separation of time-series components.
- Formulates method by maximizing log-posterior and casting the objective as a convex optimization problem.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real energy data show significant improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP, with statistical significance via t-tests.
- Computational efficiency is comparable to baselines.
- Simulation framework is robust and convincingly shows SACs improve separation, especially for appliance-level disaggregation.
- Concern: toy example uses very low noise variance (σ² = 0.01), which may make the task artificially easy; authors should clarify if this reflects real SNR or justify the choice and compare SNRs between mains and appliance aggregates.
- Theoretical clarity would improve with a formal convexity proof for Equation (6) or a reference to convexity-preserving operations.
- Missing theoretical scaling analysis: discuss computational complexity versus data length and hidden-state count and compare SACs’ scalability to AFAMAP.
- Writing is clear with a strong introduction; fix minor notation inconsistency (p_{i,j,k} vs p_{ijk}) for readability.
- Novelty: integrating SACs into AFHMM appears original and practically relevant despite reviewer’s limited domain expertise.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance pending the above clarifications and minor revisions.
