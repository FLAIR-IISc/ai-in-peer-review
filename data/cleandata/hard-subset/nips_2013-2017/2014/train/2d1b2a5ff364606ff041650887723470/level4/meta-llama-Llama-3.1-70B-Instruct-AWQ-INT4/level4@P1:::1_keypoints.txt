- Proposes a novel blind source separation method using additive factorial HMMs with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).
- Motivated by energy disaggregation: incorporating expected per-appliance usage improves component separation.
- Method is technically sound with a strong simulation section.
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP in experiments; improvements significant by t-tests.
- Optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP.
- Question: toy example uses very low noise variance—need clarification if this is for illustrative clarity or realistic SNR.
- Request to comment on signal/noise ratio between mains readings and aggregate appliance outputs in real energy data.
- Theoretical presentation is clear, but request a proof that the final procedure (eq. 6) is convex.
- Request theoretical scaling analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and tractability for larger datasets/state spaces, and comparison to AFAMAP.
- Minor notation issue on page 4 (line 202): commas in subscripts of p may be inconsistent with earlier notation.
- Reviewer not an HMM expert but considers the work novel and original.
- Strengths cited: novel method, strong real-data performance, well written and structured.
- Weaknesses cited: overly simplistic toy example with low noise, missing convexity proof, lacking theoretical scaling arguments.
- Overall: good contribution likely acceptable with minor revisions.
