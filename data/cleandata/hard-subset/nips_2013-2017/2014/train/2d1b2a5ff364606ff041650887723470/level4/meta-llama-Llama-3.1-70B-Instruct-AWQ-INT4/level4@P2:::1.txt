This paper proposes a novel approach to blind source separation by incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM). The method leverages domain knowledge to improve the separation of time series into components, with a focus on energy disaggregation. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their approach through simulations and real-world data from domestic energy output readings.

The paper is technically sound, with a strong simulation section that showcases the method's performance compared to regular AFHMM and AFAMAP. The results indicate significant improvements, confirmed by t-tests. The authors also demonstrate that the optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. However, it would be beneficial to clarify the choice of low noise variance in the toy example. Is this a typical signal-to-noise ratio in real data, or was it chosen to illustrate the method's performance?

The theoretical aspects of the paper are well-presented, but a proof that the final procedure in equation (6) is convex would strengthen the argument. Additionally, theoretical scaling arguments regarding data length and number of hidden states would complement the simulation findings. It would be valuable to discuss the tractability of the method for larger data sets and state spaces, and how it compares to AFAMAP.

The paper is well-written and structured, with a clear introduction that motivates the problem and provides relevant background literature. However, there may be a small error on page 4 (line 202) in the unnumbered equation before (6), where the notation appears inconsistent with earlier notation on page 3.

To the best of my knowledge, the work is novel and original. While I am not an expert in the field, the results appear significant, particularly in the context of energy disaggregation. The authors' responses to my questions during the feedback phase were satisfactory, and I encourage them to incorporate similar clarifications in the next version of the paper.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to blind source separation using SACs in AFHMM
* Strong simulation section demonstrating the method's performance
* Comparable optimization speed to AFAMAP
* Well-written and structured paper

Weaknesses:

* Clarification needed on the choice of low noise variance in the toy example
* Proof of convexity for the final procedure in equation (6) would be beneficial
* Theoretical scaling arguments would complement simulation findings
* Potential error in notation on page 4 (line 202)

Arguments for acceptance:

* Novel and original contribution to the field
* Strong performance demonstrated through simulations and real-world data
* Well-written and structured paper

Arguments against acceptance:

* Minor issues with notation and clarity in the toy example
* Lack of theoretical scaling arguments and proof of convexity

Overall, I recommend acceptance of the paper, pending minor revisions to address the mentioned issues.
