- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation (BSS).
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation.
- Main idea: use prior knowledge of total signal values to address BSS identifiability issues.
- Observes that total signals often vary across sources and expected totals are often known.
- SACs encourage each unknown source’s total signal to be close to a specified value.
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and easy to follow with a thorough related-work review.
- Strong empirical evaluation on real-world data demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.
- Novel approach that meaningfully addresses an important problem in BSS.
- Weakness: complex and sometimes overwhelming notation.
- Weakness: overuse of acronyms, some undefined.
- Weakness: unclear explanation of the relationship between prior density and posterior density.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to simplify notation, reduce/define acronyms, and clarify prior–posterior relationship.
