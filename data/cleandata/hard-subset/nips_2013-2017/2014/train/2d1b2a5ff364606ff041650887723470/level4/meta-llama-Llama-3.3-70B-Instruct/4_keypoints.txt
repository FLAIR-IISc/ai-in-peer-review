- Paper proposes an enhanced additive factorial HMM (SACAFHMM) that adds constraints forcing the sum of individual chain outputs to approximate a known aggregate.

- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference and applies it to energy disaggregation on real-world datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency, 10 homes high-frequency) without per-appliance monitoring.

- Method is conceptually simple (a straightforward optimization formulation) but compelling due to large-scale real data experiments and integration of applied and algorithmic aspects.

- Reviewer asks whether SACAFHMM’s optimization yields improved relaxation tightness or more frequent integer solutions, similar to AFAMAP’s one-chain-change constraint, and requests analysis of resulting error rates when solutions are non-integral.

- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas appear orthogonal and recommends exploring incorporation of SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization in future work.

- Critiques conflation of models and algorithms in the manuscript (e.g., comparing “AFHMM” and “AFAMAP” imprecisely), suggests clarifying that AFAMAP is an approximation algorithm (and that alternating Viterbi may be meant), since combining algorithmic strategies could improve performance.

- Overall assessment: a reasonable, straightforward extension of AFHMM with convincing real-world results, but needs clarifications and additional comparative/analytic discussion.
