- Problem: Blind source separation for energy disaggregation is highly underdetermined due to source unidentifiability.  
- Proposal: Introduce Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) that constrain each source’s total sum over a period to a known/expected value.  
- Integration: SACs incorporated into Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM), making the model non‑Markovian.  
- Inference: Relax MAP inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP) to enable efficient optimization.  
- Theory: Formulation is technically sound and connects to the posterior regularization framework (SACs can be seen as a penalty/log‑prior).  
- Empirics: Substantial performance gains over baseline AFHMM and state‑of‑the‑art AFAMAP on real low‑frequency dataset; oracle experiments show potential with accurate priors.  
- Computation: Reported computational cost is comparable to existing methods.  
- Strength: SACs are a simple, intuitive, novel, and broadly applicable way to inject domain knowledge into BSS.  
- Strength: Solid technical contribution in problem formulation and relaxation approach.  
- Weakness: Clarity/readability is poor due to dense writing and excessive acronyms; reduce acronyms or remind readers more.  
- Weakness: Framing of the constraint is indirect; recommend presenting it up front as an informative prior over aggregate states and then noting the posterior‑regularization view.  
- Overall: Strong contribution with compelling results, but needs clearer presentation to improve accessibility.
