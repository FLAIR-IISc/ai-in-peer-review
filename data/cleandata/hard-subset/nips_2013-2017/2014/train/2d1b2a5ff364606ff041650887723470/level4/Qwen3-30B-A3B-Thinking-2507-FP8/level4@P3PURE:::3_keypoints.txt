- Proposes AFHMM with signal aggregation constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic program relaxation, showing promising synthetic and real-data results.  
- Well-written paper with a strong mathematical foundation.  
- Clear and compelling problem motivation.  
- Evaluation reports significant improvements in NDE and SAE over baselines.  
- Comparison may be unfair: baselines use less data than SACAFHMM, so gains could reflect data differences rather than method superiority.  
- Figure 1 shows AFAMAP outperforming SACAFHMM for distances >1e4, contradicting the claim that SAC always helps and suggesting poorly designed constraints can hurt performance.  
- Limited constraint scope: paper focuses on a specific constraint type and does not address complex real-world constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, time-based constraints).  
- Unclear whether the framework can be efficiently extended to diverse constraint types; extending to such constraints would significantly strengthen the contribution.
