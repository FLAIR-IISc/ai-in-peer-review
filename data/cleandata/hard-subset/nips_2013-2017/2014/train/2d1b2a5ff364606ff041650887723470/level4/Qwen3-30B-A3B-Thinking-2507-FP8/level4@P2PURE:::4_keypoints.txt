- Introduces AFHMM extension that enforces sum-of-chains to match a known aggregate and proposes a convex relaxation for MAP inference.  
- Applies the method to two real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).  
- Core ideas and optimization are straightforward but compelling due to large-scale real-world application.  
- Suggest clarifying whether SACAFHMM achieves similar integrality improvements as AFAMAP’s one-chain-at-a-time constraint or maintains lower error despite non-integral solutions.  
- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are fundamentally orthogonal; recommends exploring combining SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP for the final version.  
- Criticizes conflation of model vs. optimization: AFHMM (model) vs. AFAMAP (algorithm) distinctions are unclear and should be made precise.  
- Points out that algorithmic strategies could be combined and that clearer terminology/definitions are essential.  
- Overall assessment: reasonable paper with applied and algorithmic contributions, but needs clarification and exploration of algorithm combinations.
