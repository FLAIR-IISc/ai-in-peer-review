- Proposes SACAFHMM: an extension to additive factorial HMMs that enforces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) so chain outputs sum close to a known aggregate.
- Develops a convex relaxation for MAP inference for the SACAFHMM formulation.
- Applies the method to large real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency, 9 homes high-frequency).
- Main ideas are straightforward but compelling due to scale and real-world application.
- Results on energy disaggregation are strong and demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness.
- Needs discussion on how SACAFHMM’s optimization affects the relaxation toward integer solutions and comparisons with non-integral solutions.
- Suggests comparing SACAFHMM to AFAMAP’s enforcement of single-state-per-chain constraints to elucidate differences in relaxation behavior.
- Observes that AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are largely orthogonal and SAC constraints could be integrated into AFAMAP as an extension.
- Authors sometimes conflate models (AFHMM) with optimization procedures (AFAMAP), requiring clearer terminology and distinctions.
- Recommends clarifying that AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximate MAP inference rather than a separate model.
- Suggests exploring combinations of the different algorithmic strategies, which may outperform current methods.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity on optimization/relaxation, model vs. algorithm distinctions, and possible method combinations.
