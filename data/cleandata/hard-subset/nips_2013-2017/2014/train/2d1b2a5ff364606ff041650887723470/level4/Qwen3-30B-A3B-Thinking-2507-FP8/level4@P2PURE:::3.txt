

The paper introduces an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) for blind source separation. The authors formulate a convex quadratic relaxation for the AFHMM with SAC and demonstrate promising results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  

Pros:  
- The paper is well-written with a strong mathematical foundation.  
- The problem motivation is compelling.  
- Evaluation results show significant improvements in performance metrics (NDE and SAE).  

Areas for Improvement:  
- The results are presented as improvements over AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, but SACAFHMM uses more data than these baselines, making the comparison potentially unfair. Are there alternative approaches that could incorporate SAC while maintaining computational efficiency?  
- Figure 1 is unclear: for distances exceeding \(10^4\), AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM. This contradicts the paper's claim that SACAFHMM consistently yields better results, suggesting that poor constraints may lead to significantly worse performance. The discussion should address this discrepancy.  
- The paper does not explore complex constraints beyond those tested. For instance, real-world constraints include "a person cannot use the heater and cooler simultaneously," "no more than 3 of 10 house bulbs active at once," or "grinders must operate for less than 10 minutes." The algorithm’s applicability to such diverse constraint types is not addressed. Can the framework be extended to handle these broader constraint categories? The paper provides strong motivation and is well-written; future work should investigate this extension.
