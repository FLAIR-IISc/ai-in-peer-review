- Introduces SACAFHMM: a sum-constrained extension of AFHMM with a convex relaxation for MAP inference.
- Applies the method to large real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes high-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).
- The approach is conceptually simple and uses a straightforward optimization formulation but is compelling due to scale and practical application.
- Recommendation to evaluate whether SACAFHMM encourages single-active-chain solutions like AFAMAP and whether it yields lower errors even when solutions aren’t fully integral.
- Suggests exploring integration of SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization for potential improvements.
- Critique that the paper conflates models and optimization procedures (e.g., AFHMM vs AFAMAP) and needs clearer terminology and comparisons.
- Notes potential for synergistic gains by combining strategies from the different algorithms presented.
- Overall assessment: a solid, incremental contribution with strong real-world results that justify acceptance.
