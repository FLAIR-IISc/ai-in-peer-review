- Novel method (SACAFHMM) adds aggregate-signal constraints to AFHMM and formulates relaxed inference as a convex quadratic program for efficient optimization.

- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and mathematically rigorous.

- Motivation is strong and practical: leveraging readily available aggregate signals (e.g., energy disaggregation) is intuitive and impactful.

- Empirical results show significant improvements on NDE and SAE across synthetic and real-world datasets.

- Experimental fairness concern: baselines do not use the aggregate signal, so performance gains may stem from extra information rather than model superiority.

- Results need nuance: Figure 1 suggests AFAMAP can outperform SACAFHMM at large distances, so sensitivity to noisy/inaccurate constraints should be discussed.

- Extensibility question: paper should address whether the QP framework can handle richer constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, temporal/operational limits) or requires reformulation.

- Conclusion: valuable and well-executed contribution but would be strengthened by fairer comparisons, clearer limitation discussion, and exploration of more complex constraints.
