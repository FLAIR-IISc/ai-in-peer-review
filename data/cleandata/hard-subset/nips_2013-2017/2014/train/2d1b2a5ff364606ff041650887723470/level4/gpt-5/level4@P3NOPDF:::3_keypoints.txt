- Introduces AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation for efficient blind source separation; shows gains over AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP.  
- Strength: clear motivation and mathematically well-grounded formulation.  
- Strength: elegant convex relaxation with linear constraints that appears practically useful.  
- Strength: empirical improvements on synthetic and real data; relevant to energy disaggregation.  
- Concern: fairness of comparisons—proposed method uses extra SAC information; need baselines that also incorporate same constraints (e.g., constrained-MAP, MIP/Viterbi, Lagrangian-penalized inference).  
- Concern: sensitivity to constraint quality—Figure 1 suggests SAC can hurt when constraints are poor; request clarification of plotted metrics/axes and threshold at 10^4.  
- Request: provide systematic robustness analysis varying constraint noise/misspecification and report whether soft penalties or regularization can downweight unreliable constraints and how weights are chosen.  
- Concern: constraint expressiveness—ask whether richer constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration/temporal logic) can be encoded as linear inequalities compatible with the convex relaxation or require integer variables and what the computational impact is.  
- Presentation: improve Figure 1 labeling, add error bars/confidence intervals, and align textual claims with what figures actually support.  
- Computational reporting: provide runtimes, scaling with number of chains/time steps/constraints, solver details, tolerances, warm starts, and details on rounding/integer recovery and integrality gaps.  
- Hyperparameters: clarify how constraint penalty weights are selected (cross-validation/held-out) and sensitivity to these choices.  
- Data realism: clarify how aggregate constraints are derived in practice and whether there is any risk of test-time leakage.  
- Suggested experiments: add a baseline using the same constraints (even if slower), ablation over quantity/accuracy of constraints, and examples/integration strategies for richer constraint types (or a hybrid convex+binary approach).  
- Minor: expand NDE and SAE on first use and standardize terminology/symbols (e.g., SAC-AFHMM).  
- Overall: technically solid and promising, but needs fairer baselines, robustness/expressiveness analyses, and computational details; reviewer leans toward acceptance if addressed.
