- Proposes an additive FHMM for electricity disaggregation augmented with a “signal aggregate constraint” to align component sums with observed aggregate consumption.  
- Modeling choice is natural for aggregate electricity usage (sum of multiple sources).  
- Explicit aggregate constraint is conceptually appealing and likely improves identifiability and inference stability.  
- Technical development appears sound and the work has clear practical relevance.  
- Paper is hard to read due to excessive and long acronyms; recommend defining/limiting acronyms and adding a notation/acronym table.  
- The probabilistic status of the aggregate constraint is unclear: need to state whether it is a hard (support-restricting) or soft (regularizer) constraint.  
- Clarify where the constraint enters the model—prior, likelihood, or variational objective—and the implications of that choice.  
- Explain how the constraint is enforced during inference (e.g., projected updates, augmented messages, Lagrange multipliers).  
- Inference exposition is unclear; provide a high-level algorithmic summary, message-passing details, factorization assumptions, and computational scaling with number of factors and state sizes.  
- Introduce the constraint’s motivation and intuition earlier, ideally with a simple illustrative example showing how it narrows hypotheses and aids disaggregation.  
- Address identifiability: discuss conditions, priors, or constraints that make components recoverable and provide references or empirical diagnostics.  
- Experiments need ablations (with/without constraint), sensitivity analyses for constraint strength, clear baselines and consistent metrics, and runtime/scalability results.  
- Readability improvements: reduce acronyms, present a brief generative story up front, use a running toy example, and separate modeling assumptions, inference method, and implementation details.  
- Technical clarifications requested: precisely define the aggregate constraint (per-time equality, expectation, or windowed energy), specify prior/likelihood forms and normalization, show the variational/objective formulation and any Lagrangian updates and convergence guarantees.  
- Also clarify handling of missing data, sensor noise, outliers, and initialization/local minima risks given factorial structure and constraints.  
- Overall: timely and potentially impactful idea, but presentation must be clarified—make the constraint’s probabilistic role explicit, formalize enforcement in inference, and strengthen exposition and empirical validation.
