- Proposes extending additive factorial HMMs with constraints that aggregate chain outputs match a known total, using a convex relaxation for MAP inference and evaluating on real energy-disaggregation data (100 low-frequency homes, 9 high-frequency homes).  
- The idea and optimization are straightforward but effective; applying them to large real-world datasets makes the work compelling and successful.  
- Ask whether SAC-AFHMM achieves the same “only one chain changes at a time” effect as AFAMAP, or whether SAC solutions remain non-integral yet still yield lower error — more discussion needed to clarify differences.  
- AFAMAP and SAC-AFHMM appear orthogonal; the reviewer suggests exploring combining SAC constraints with AFAMAP in future work.  
- The paper sometimes conflates models and optimization methods (e.g., treating AFHMM vs AFAMAP as model differences); clarify that AFAMAP is an MAP algorithm for AFHMM (likely compared to alternating Viterbi).  
- Overall, the extension is simple but the empirical results are compelling and the paper is reasonable.
