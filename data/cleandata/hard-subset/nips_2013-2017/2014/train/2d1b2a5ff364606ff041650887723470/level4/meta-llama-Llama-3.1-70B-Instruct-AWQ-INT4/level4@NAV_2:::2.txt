This paper proposes a novel approach to blind source separation (BSS) problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem through signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The authors introduce SACs, which encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value, and incorporate these constraints into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to formulate the energy disaggregation problem.

The main idea of the paper is to address the identifiability problem in BSS by incorporating prior knowledge about the total signal values of the unknown sources. The authors observe that in many applications of BSS, the total signal often varies widely across the different unknown sources, and we often have a good idea of what total values to expect. They propose using SACs to capture this prior knowledge and improve the performance of the AFHMM.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear introduction to the problem, a thorough explanation of the proposed approach, and a detailed evaluation of the method on real-world data. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work and clearly explain the contributions of their paper.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the proposed approach, the thorough evaluation of the method, and the clear presentation of the results. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on real-world data and provide a detailed analysis of the results.

The weaknesses of the paper include the complexity of the notation and the use of too many acronyms. Some of the acronyms are not clearly defined, and the notation can be overwhelming at times. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the relationship between the prior density and the posterior density.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel approach to BSS problems. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method and demonstrate its effectiveness on real-world data. With some minor revisions to address the complexity of the notation and the use of acronyms, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
