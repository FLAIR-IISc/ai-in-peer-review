This research paper proposes an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) to address the problem of blind source separation. The authors propose a convex quadratic problem for relaxed AFHMM with SAC. This technique shows promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

Pros:
- The paper is well-written and has a good mathematical foundation.
- The problem motivation is good.
- Evaluation results indicate good improvements in performance metrics, NDE, and SAE.

Areas of Improvement:
- The results are shown as improvements compared to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP. Both of these use less data than SACAFHMM, which seems like an unfair comparison. Are there other approaches that could use SAC but are not as efficient in computation?
- Figure 1 does not clearly show the results. It seems that AFAMAP beats SACAFHMM for distances greater than 10^4. Does this indicate that very poor performance can result from poor constraints? The discussion suggests that the results are always better, which seems misleading.
- I expected more complex constraints to be included in the algorithm. For example, a person cannot use a heater and a cooler at the same time. What about having no more than 3 out of 10 bulbs active in the house at the same time? Things like grinders have constraints of less than 10 minutes. In short, there can be different types of constraints that can be specified. Is it possible to extend the algorithm to other types of constraints as well? The paper is well-written and provides good motivation in the problem. Future work involves extending the model to other types of constraints as well.
