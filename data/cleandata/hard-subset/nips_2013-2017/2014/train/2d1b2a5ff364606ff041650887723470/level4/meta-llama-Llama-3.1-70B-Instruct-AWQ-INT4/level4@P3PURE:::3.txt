The paper proposes an additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors formulate a convex quadratic problem for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper has several strengths:
- The writing is clear and well-organized, with a solid mathematical foundation.
- The problem motivation is well-articulated, providing a compelling context for the proposed approach.
- The evaluation results show a notable improvement in performance metrics, including NDE and SAE.

However, there are some areas that require attention:
- The comparison of results appears to be biased, as SACAFHMM is evaluated against AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which use less data. To provide a more comprehensive understanding, it would be beneficial to compare SACAFHMM with other approaches that utilize SAC but are less computationally efficient.
- The results presented in Figure 1 are unclear, suggesting that AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM for distances greater than 10^4. This raises concerns that poor constraints may lead to significantly poorer performance. The discussion seems to imply that the results are consistently better, which may be misleading.
- The incorporation of more complex constraints into the algorithm would enhance its applicability. For instance, constraints such as "a person cannot use the heater and cooler simultaneously" or "no more than 3 out of 10 bulbs can be active at the same time" could be explored. Additionally, time constraints, like those for grinders with usage limits of less than 10 minutes, could be integrated. It would be valuable to discuss the possibility of extending the algorithm to accommodate various types of constraints.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear motivation for the problem. Future work should focus on extending the model to incorporate more complex constraints, increasing its versatility and potential applications.
