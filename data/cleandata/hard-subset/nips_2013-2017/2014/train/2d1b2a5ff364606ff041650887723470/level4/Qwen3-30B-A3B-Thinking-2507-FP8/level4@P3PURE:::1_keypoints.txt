- Introduces a novel blind source separation method: Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).
- Motivates using domain knowledge (expected total appliance usage) to improve energy disaggregation.
- Reformulates the MAP objective for hidden chains into a convex optimization problem.
- Validated on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation datasets.
- Method outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP with statistically significant improvements (t-tests).
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.
- Concerns about the low noise variance in the toy example (Figure 2); authors should clarify if this choice is deliberate or realistic.
- Request to comment on typical signal-to-noise ratios between mains readings and appliance aggregates in practice.
- Theoretical exposition is clear but needs a formal proof that Equation (6) is convex.
- Suggests adding theoretical scaling analysis (data length and hidden state count) to complement simulations.
- Ask authors to discuss tractability for larger datasets/state spaces and compare scalability with AFAMAP.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and literature review appears comprehensive.
- Minor notation inconsistency: remove commas in subscripts for consistency (page 4 vs page 3).
- Work appears novel in HMM and energy disaggregation contexts; broader HMM significance uncertain.
- Authors addressed reviewer queries; recommend incorporating requested clarifications in the final version.
