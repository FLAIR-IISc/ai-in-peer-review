- Proposes an additive factorial HMM for electricity usage that incorporates a novel signal aggregate constraint (SAC).  
- Uses SAC to inject domain knowledge and address identifiability in blind source separation/energy disaggregation.  
- Mathematical formulation is rigorous and converted into a convex quadratic program for efficient computation.  
- Empirical results on a real-world dataset show significant improvement over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method.  
- Strength: Novelty — SAC is a fresh approach to incorporating domain knowledge.  
- Strength: Technical soundness — derivations and convex formulation are well-presented.  
- Strength: Empirical validation — clear performance gains on real data.  
- Strength: Problem statement — challenges and need for identifiability solutions are clearly articulated.  
- Weakness: Clarity — excessive acronyms and complex writing hinder readability.  
- Weakness: The paper does not clearly explain how imposing a prior constraint affects the posterior.  
- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical discussion of how SAC mitigates identifiability beyond empirical gains.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed guidance on selecting constraint parameters (δi or λi).  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem with a novel, sound approach and strong experimental results with practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, limited theoretical analysis, and underdeveloped constraint-parameter selection reduce potential impact.  
- Overall: Solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve readability and expand theoretical explanation.
