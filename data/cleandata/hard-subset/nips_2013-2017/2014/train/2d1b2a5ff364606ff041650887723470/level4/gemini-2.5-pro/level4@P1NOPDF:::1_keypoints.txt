- Proposes incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) for blind source separation, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.  
- Leverages constraints on expected total appliance usage over a period to improve decomposition accuracy.  
- Reformulates the log-posterior of hidden states given observations into a convex optimization problem for inference.  
- Validated on both a toy dataset and real-world household energy consumption data.  
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP; improvements shown statistically significant via t-tests.  
- Computational runtime comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Reviewer questions the low noise variance used in the toy example and asks if it was chosen to favor the method or reflects real-world SNR.  
- Recommends authors comment on the signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and appliance signals in the real data.  
- Requests a formal proof of convexity for Equation (6), as the provided justification is not obvious.  
- Suggests theoretical analysis of scalability with respect to data length and number of hidden states, and comparison of scaling properties to AFAMAP.  
- Notes a minor notational inconsistency on page 4 (commas in subscripts after p) to be corrected.  
- States the paper is well-written, novel and effective, though reviewer cannot fully judge broader significance in the subfield.  
- Addendum: authors satisfactorily answered reviewer queries in feedback; incorporate those clarifications in the final version.
