- Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC.  
- Demonstrates promising performance on synthetic and real-world data with improved NDE and SAE metrics.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and clearly motivated.  
- Comparison is potentially unfair: SAC AFHMM uses more data than baselines (AFHMM without SAC, AFAMAP), so improvements may stem from extra information.  
- Suggests comparing to other methods that could also use SAC, even if less computationally efficient.  
- Figure 1 is unclear; for distances >10^4 AFAMAP seems to outperform SAC AFHMM, indicating poor constraints can hurt performance.  
- Recommends a deeper analysis of when and why SAC degrades performance (impact of poor constraints).  
- Encourages exploring more complex constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, time-duration limits) and discussing algorithmic extensions to accommodate them.
