- Summary: Proposes AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) encoding per-source total energy; formulates penalized MAP, relaxes to a convex quadratic program, links to posterior regularization, and shows improved disaggregation on real data.

- Strength (intuition): Encoding aggregate totals as constraints is intuitive, practical, and encodes strong prior knowledge useful for energy disaggregation.

- Strength (computation): Casting constrained MAP as a convex QP yields an efficient approximate inference method.

- Strength (empirics): Experiments show meaningful improvements over a baseline AFHMM and a recent strong method, suggesting SACs help identifiability.

- Weakness (readability): Paper is hard to read due to too many and very long acronyms that interrupt flow.

- Weakness (terminology): Confusing framing between “constraints on the posterior” and “prior constraints”; authors should clarify that SACs act as priors/regularizers that constrain the posterior.

- Quality: Technical approach and connection to posterior regularization are sound; convex relaxation is reasonable for intractable exact inference.

- Clarity suggestions: Reduce acronyms, state early that SACs encode prior knowledge via regularization, and keep consistent terminology between penalized MAP and posterior-regularization views.

- Originality: Idea is a natural but useful combination of SACs with AFHMMs and convex inference; relates to prior work but adds a valuable formulation for this application.

- Significance: Improved disaggregation accuracy on real data indicates practical utility and possible applicability to other blind source separation tasks.

- Pros for acceptance: Well-motivated use of domain knowledge, computationally efficient relaxation, and empirical gains over strong baselines.

- Cons for acceptance: Excessive/long acronyms and imprecise Bayesian/posterior-regularization framing harm readability and clarity.

- Recommendations: Simplify acronyms, explicitly state SACs as a priori regularizers, streamline the penalized MAP vs posterior-regularization explanation (choose one primary narrative), and add a short intuitive example early.
