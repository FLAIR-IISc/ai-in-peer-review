- Paper introduces AFHMM augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.  
- Method leverages domain-specific aggregate energy constraints to improve time-series component separation.  
- Objective (log-posterior) is reformulated into a tractable convex optimization problem.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on both synthetic toy data and real-world domestic energy datasets.  
- Technical approach is sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented.  
- Strong empirical evaluation showing superior performance to standard AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Performance improvements are statistically validated using t-tests.  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP, supporting practical viability.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and situates contributions within related work.  
- Experimental setup: clarify the very low noise variance in the toy example and provide SNR context for real data.  
- Theoretical justification: include a brief proof or detailed appendix justification for convexity of equation (6).  
- Scalability: add theoretical analysis of computational complexity w.r.t. time-series length and number of hidden states.  
- Minor notation issue: check comma usage in subscripts of p in the equation preceding (6).  
- Conclusion/recommendation: work is novel and valuable; authors addressed reviewer queries satisfactorily and the paper is recommended for acceptance with requested clarifications.
