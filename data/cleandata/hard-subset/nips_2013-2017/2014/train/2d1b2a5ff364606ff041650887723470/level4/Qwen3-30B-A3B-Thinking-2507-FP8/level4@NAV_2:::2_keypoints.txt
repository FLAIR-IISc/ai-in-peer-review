- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation.  
- SACs force each source’s total signal to align with domain-specific expectations (e.g., appliance daily energy).  
- Builds on AFHMM (Kolter & Jaakkola 2012) but introduces a novel constraint class distinct from AFAMAP and k-segment constraints.  
- Formulates SACs as a convex quadratic program (CQP) to enable efficient inference.  
- Provides theoretical grounding via posterior regularization, clarifying constraints act on the posterior.  
- Empirical results show 45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM and 29% NDE improvement vs AFAMAP on real-world data (100 households, paired t-tests).  
- Strength — Originality: novel, domain-knowledge-driven constraint class addressing prior gap.  
- Strength — Quality: technically sound CQP solution with robust empirical validation.  
- Strength — Significance: substantial practical improvement for energy disaggregation with deployment potential.  
- Weakness — Clarity: SACs not formally defined in abstract/introduction and acronym density (e.g., "AFHMM+SAC") hampers readability.  
- Weakness — Theoretical depth: relaxed CQP lacks discussion of approximation/error bounds.  
- Weakness — Scope: paper uses single-constraint SAC formulation; multi-period extensions (day/night) are only briefly noted.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pro — novel constraint class, strong empirical gains, technical rigor; Con — minor clarity issues and need for stronger theoretical guarantees.  
- Recommendation: With minor clarifications on SAC definition and posterior regularization mechanics, the paper is a good fit for the conference.
