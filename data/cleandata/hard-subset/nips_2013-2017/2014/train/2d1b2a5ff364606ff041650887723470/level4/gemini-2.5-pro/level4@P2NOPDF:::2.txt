This paper develops an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to model aggregate electricity usage, incorporating what the authors term a "signal aggregate constraint."

The technical quality of the work is good, and the proposed model appears to be a novel and sound approach to an important problem. However, the paper in its current form is very difficult to read, which significantly obscures its contributions.

The primary issue is a lack of clarity in the presentation. The manuscript is laden with an excessive number of acronyms, many of which are exceptionally long (some up to 11 letters). This forces the reader to constantly reference their definitions, disrupting the logical flow and making the core arguments challenging to follow. The authors are strongly encouraged to reduce their use of acronyms and spell out terms where feasible to improve readability.

Furthermore, the motivation for placing the constraint on the prior density is not clearly articulated. It appears the authors' goal is to constrain the posterior density, and they achieve this by introducing a constraint on the prior. The paper would be strengthened if the authors explicitly stated that they are imposing an *a priori* constraint based on prior knowledge of the system. Clarifying that this modeling choice consequently—and intentionally—constrains the posterior distribution would provide a clearer and more robust justification for their methodology.

In summary, while the core contribution is valuable, the paper would benefit significantly from a thorough revision focused on improving its clarity and exposition. Addressing these presentational issues would make the work more accessible and impactful for the NIPS audience.
