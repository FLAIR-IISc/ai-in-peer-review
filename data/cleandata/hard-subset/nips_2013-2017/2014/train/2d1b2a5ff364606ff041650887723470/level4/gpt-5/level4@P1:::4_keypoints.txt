- Paper extends AFHMM by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to bias each source’s total activity and formulates MAP inference with a convex quadratic relaxation solved efficiently.

- Evaluated on energy disaggregation with large low-frequency UK dataset and smaller mains dataset, showing substantial gains over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP.

- Idea is straightforward and well-motivated: aggregate priors improve identifiability in blind source separation.

- Optimization is simple, practical, and empirically effective on real data, blending methodological and applied contributions.

- Request clarification on integrality and relaxation behavior: do SACs make solutions more integral or do fractional solutions still yield improved accuracy?

- Ask for diagnostics on fraction of integral assignments, rounding strategy, and effect of rounding on NDE/SAE.

- Suggest exploring orthogonality of ideas by combining SACs with AFAMAP’s temporal-change constraint, which may yield further gains.

- Recommend clarifying model vs. algorithm terminology: specify that AFAMAP is an inference algorithm and what is meant by “AFHMM” in comparisons.

- Empirical clarifications requested: sensitivity to number of appliance states, justification or analysis of λi hyperparameters, description of rounding/feasibility post-relaxation, and computational scaling with T and total states.

- Suggest expanding discussion connecting SACs to posterior regularization and differentiating SACs from other moment constraints like generalized expectation.

- Advise standardizing method naming (e.g., AFHMM+SAC) across the paper, figures, and tables to avoid confusion.

- Quality: technically sound formulation and convex relaxation with thorough empirical evaluation and clear improvements over strong baselines.

- Clarity: generally well written but needs clearer separation of model versus inference, consistent naming, and explicit rounding description.

- Originality: SAC idea is simple but novel in this context and provides a useful connection to posterior regularization.

- Significance: meaningful results on large-scale low-frequency data with likely adoption potential, especially if combined with other constraints.

- Pros: practical effective constraint, clean convex relaxation, efficient implementation, strong empirical gains, and clear extension path with other constraints.

- Cons: limited discussion of integrality and rounding impacts, lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and occasional conflation of model and algorithm in exposition.

- Overall: incremental but well-executed contribution with practical improvements on challenging real-world data; recommended as a solid, useful paper.
