- Proposes extending AFHMM by adding constraints forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate, with a convex relaxation for MAP inference.
- Applies the method to real-world energy disaggregation datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency, no per-appliance monitoring).
- Paper’s ideas and optimization are straightforward but compelling, and methods scale to relatively large real data.
- AFAMAP is motivated by enforcing one chain changes at a time to improve relaxation and produce integer solutions more often.
- Reviewer asks whether SACAFHMM achieves similar benefits or yields lower error despite non-integral solutions—clarification needed.
- AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal; SACAFHMM constraints could likely be incorporated into AFAMAP’s formulation.
- Authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures—e.g., comparing “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP” is imprecise since AFAMAP is an algorithm for AFHMM.
- Likely “AFHMM” references alternating Viterbi; the paper should clarify this terminology and distinctions.
- Overall the paper is reasonable and compelling; addressing the above points and exploring method combinations would strengthen the work.
