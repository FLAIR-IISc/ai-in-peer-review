This paper introduces an extension to the additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (HMM) incorporating constraints to ensure the sum of individual chain outputs closely matches a known aggregate value. The authors develop a convex relaxation for Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference and demonstrate its application to energy disaggregation problems using both low-frequency data from 100 UK homes and higher-frequency data from 10 homes (without appliance-level monitoring).

While the presented concepts are relatively direct and utilize a straightforward optimization approach, the work is nonetheless impactful. The application of these methods to substantial real-world datasets, coupled with the combination of algorithmic and applied contributions, makes this a successful paper. The following suggestions could further strengthen the manuscript:

The authors motivate the single-chain-state constraint in AFAMAP by suggesting it enhances the relaxation, leading to more frequent integer solutions. It would be valuable to understand whether the SACAFHMM optimization also achieves a similar effect, or if its improved performance stems from accurate solutions even with non-integer values. A discussion clarifying these aspects would be insightful and further differentiate the two approaches.

The AFAMAP and SACAFHMM approaches appear fundamentally complementary; the SACAFHMM constraints could conceivably be integrated directly into the AFAMAP optimization framework. While not expected in a response to review, exploring this combination for a revised version is strongly encouraged.

The paper occasionally blurs the distinction between models and optimization methods. Specifically, comparing “AFHMM” and “AFAMAP” lacks precision, as AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP estimates within the AFHMM framework, and “AFHMM” seems to refer to alternating Viterbi optimization. Clarification on this point would be beneficial, and ties into the earlier observation that the algorithmic strategies could be combined for potentially superior performance. In summary, this work presents a straightforward extension and optimization for a variant of the AFHMM, and the promising results on real-world data render it a sound and reasonable contribution.
