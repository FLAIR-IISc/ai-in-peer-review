- Introduces an additive factorial HMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and formulates a convex quadratic optimization for a relaxed AFHMM with SAC.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and grounded in strong mathematical principles.  
- Problem is well-motivated.  
- Evaluation shows significant improvements in NDE and SAE metrics.  
- Comparison fairness concern: baseline methods (AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP) use less data than SACAFHMM, raising questions about apples-to-apples comparison.  
- Requests alternative approaches that could leverage SAC with lower computational cost.  
- Figure 1 is unclear: for distances > 10^4 AFAMAP appears to outperform SACAFHMM, implying poorly chosen constraints can worsen performance and the claim of consistent improvement may be misleading.  
- Algorithm currently targets a specific constraint type; it's unclear if it can handle more complex, diverse constraints.  
- Suggests extending the model to support richer constraints (e.g., mutually exclusive appliance usage, limits on number of active devices, maximum run-time constraints) as future work.
