- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate known total signal aggregates into blind source separation for AFHMMs.
- Implements SACs by adding a penalty to the MAP objective that encourages each inferred source’s aggregate to match a target.
- Uses a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation to make inference tractable after the new objective breaks dynamic programming.
- Evaluated on real-world energy disaggregation with a large, low-frequency UK homes dataset (HES).
- Shows large empirical gains: ~45% error reduction vs baseline AFHMM and ~29% improvement vs state-of-the-art AFAMAP on mains data.
- Method is practical, technically sound, and computationally efficient for the evaluated task.
- SACs are general and likely applicable to other BSS problems where aggregate priors exist.
- Correctly relates the approach to posterior regularization and frames SACs as a novel, useful class of constraints.
- Clear writing and motivation, but some ambiguity between model definitions and inference algorithms.
- Needs explicit clarification which inference algorithm was used for the "AFHMM" baseline to avoid confusion.
- Suggests analyzing whether the CQP relaxation yields integral solutions or improves performance despite fractional assignments.
- Proposes exploring combining SACs with AFAMAP’s "at most one chain changes" constraint as a promising, orthogonal extension.
- Minor revision: improve precision in terminology distinguishing model vs inference and add analysis of CQP solution properties.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant empirical impact and minor clarifications needed for the final version.
