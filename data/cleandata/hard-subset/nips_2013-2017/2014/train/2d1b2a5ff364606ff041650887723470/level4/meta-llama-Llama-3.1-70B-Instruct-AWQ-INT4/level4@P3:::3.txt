The paper proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to deal with blind source separation problems. The authors propose a convex quadratic program for relaxed AFHMM with SAC. The technique shows promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper is well-written, with a good mathematical foundation and clear problem motivation. The evaluation results indicate a significant boost in performance metrics, such as normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE), compared to the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach, AFAMAP.

However, there are a few areas that require further discussion. Firstly, the comparison with AFHMM and AFAMAP seems unfair, as these methods use less data than the proposed SAC AFHMM. It would be beneficial to explore other approaches that could utilize SAC but may not be as efficient in computation. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's strengths and weaknesses.

Additionally, the results presented in Figure 1 are not entirely clear. It appears that for larger distances, AFAMAP outperforms SAC AFHMM. This suggests that poorly chosen constraints can result in significantly poorer performance. The discussion seems to indicate that the results are always better, which may be misleading. A more detailed analysis of the impact of constraint quality on performance would be helpful.

Furthermore, the paper primarily focuses on simple aggregate constraints. However, there are various other types of constraints that could be specified, such as mutual exclusivity constraints (e.g., a person cannot use a heater and a cooler at the same time) or time constraints (e.g., a grinder can only be active for less than 10 minutes). It would be beneficial to discuss the possibility of extending the algorithm to incorporate these more complex constraints.

In conclusion, the paper provides a good motivation for the problem and proposes a novel approach to address it. The evaluation results are promising, but further discussion is required to fully understand the strengths and limitations of the proposed method. Future work should focus on extending the model to incorporate more complex constraints and exploring other approaches that can utilize SAC.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors carefully evaluate the performance of the proposed method and compare it to existing approaches. However, the discussion could be improved by addressing the concerns mentioned above.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written, and the mathematical derivations are clear. However, some of the results, such as those presented in Figure 1, could be better explained.

**Originality:** The paper proposes a novel approach to blind source separation problems by incorporating signal aggregate constraints into an additive factorial HMM. The use of convex quadratic programming for relaxed AFHMM with SAC is also a new contribution.

**Significance:** The results presented in the paper are significant, as they demonstrate a substantial improvement in performance metrics compared to existing approaches. The proposed method has the potential to be applied to various real-world problems, such as energy disaggregation, and could lead to advancements in the field.
