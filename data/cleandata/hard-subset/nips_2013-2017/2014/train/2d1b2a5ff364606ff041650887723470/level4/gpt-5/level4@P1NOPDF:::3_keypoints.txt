- Proposes AFHMM augmented with signal-aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation, with promising synthetic and real-world results.  
- Paper is clearly written and grounded in solid mathematics.  
- Problem motivation is convincing.  
- Evaluation shows notable gains in NDE and SAE.  
- Primary baselines (AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP) use less information than SACAFHMM, making comparisons potentially unfair—need baselines that also incorporate SAC or control for informational advantage.  
- Figure 1 is unclear and suggests AFAMAP may outperform SACAFHMM for large distances, raising concern that poorly chosen or inaccurate constraints can degrade performance.  
- Authors should analyze when constraints help versus when they hurt (robustness to misspecified/adversarial constraints).  
- Expected richer constraint types (mutual exclusion, cardinality limits, duration/temporal constraints) are missing; need discussion of whether and how these can be encoded in the relaxation and the computational/convexity implications.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising, but should address fairness of comparisons, clarify behavior under misspecified constraints, and outline/extensions to broader practical constraint families.
