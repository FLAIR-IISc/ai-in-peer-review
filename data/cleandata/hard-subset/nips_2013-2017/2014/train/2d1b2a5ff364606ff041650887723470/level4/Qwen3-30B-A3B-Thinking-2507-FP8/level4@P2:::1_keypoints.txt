- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation in AFHMMs, leveraging domain knowledge of total source signals.  
- Incorporates SACs into AFHMMs to address identifiability issues in additive BSS.  
- Formulates a convex quadratic program as a convex relaxation for approximate inference.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% improvement vs state-of-the-art on real energy data.  
- Validates results on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation datasets with paired t-tests for statistical significance.  
- The method is technically sound with clear theoretical justification for the convex relaxation.  
- Needs a more explicit proof that the transformed optimization problem (eq. 6) is convex.  
- Lacks detailed scaling analysis with respect to time series length and number of hidden states for larger problems.  
- Robustness shown on toy data to constraint specification errors; figures effectively illustrate performance (e.g., relation between constraint error and disaggregation error).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, though the transition from constrained optimization (eq. 4) to convex relaxation (eq. 5) could be clearer.  
- Minor notation inconsistency on page 4 (unnumbered equation before eq. 6) with unnecessary commas in subscripts.  
- Novel contribution: SACs are a new, general class of constraints distinct from prior posterior-regularization and temporal/structural constraints.  
- Significant practical impact for energy disaggregation and broader BSS domains where total magnitudes are known or estimable.  
- Some experimental details needed: justification for low noise variance in toy example and discussion of signal-to-noise ratios in real data.  
- Authors addressed reviewer concerns in feedback, strengthening the paper’s arguments; overall recommendation is acceptance.
