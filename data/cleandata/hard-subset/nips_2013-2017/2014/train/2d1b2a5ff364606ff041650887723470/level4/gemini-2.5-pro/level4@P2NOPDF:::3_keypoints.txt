- Introduces SAC-AFHMM for blind source separation in energy disaggregation, using aggregate signal constraints.
- Formulates relaxed inference as a convex quadratic program with a clear mathematical derivation.
- Paper is clearly written and builds on a solid mathematical foundation.
- Leveraging an easily obtainable aggregate signal is a practical and effective idea.
- Empirical evaluation shows significant improvements over baselines on synthetic and real datasets (NDE and SAE metrics).
- Comparison fairness concern: baselines lack access to the aggregate signal, so gains may reflect extra information rather than model superiority.
- Results ambiguity: Figure 1 suggests AFAMAP outperforms SAC-AFHMM for large distances, indicating constraints can hurt performance if inaccurate; failure modes need discussion.
- Extensibility question: paper should address whether the convex QP framework can handle richer constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality, temporal) or explain theoretical/computational barriers.
