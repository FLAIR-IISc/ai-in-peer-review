- Proposes AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) for blind source separation and a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Shows promising results on synthetic and real-world (energy disaggregation) data.  
- Contribution: integrates domain knowledge via SAC into AFHMM, enabling computationally efficient convex inference and improved performance.  
- Strong technical foundation: well-written, mathematically sound model and optimization.  
- Well-motivated problem: identifiability and interpretability issues in energy disaggregation are clearly explained.  
- Empirical validation: substantial improvements in NDE and SAE compared to baselines.  
- Novelty: introduction of SAC within AFHMM and derivation of the convex quadratic program.  
- Comparison fairness concern: AFHMM+SAC uses aggregate information not used by other methods, so fairness of comparisons is unclear.  
- Figure 1 clarity/sensitivity: AFAMAP may outperform AFHMM+SAC at large distances, suggesting poor constraints can degrade performance and sensitivity needs discussion.  
- Constraint complexity limitation: paper focuses on simple aggregate constraints and does not explore more complex or mutually exclusive/temporal constraints.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, principled contribution with strong experiments, but needs further discussion on fairness and constraint sensitivity.  
- Arguments pro-accept: novel domain-knowledge integration, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, potentially impactful.  
- Arguments con-accept: debatable comparison fairness, unexamined sensitivity to inaccurate constraints, limited scope of constraint complexity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparison fairness, constraint sensitivity, and extension to more complex constraints.
