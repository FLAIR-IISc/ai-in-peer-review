- Paper extends the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with constraints that keep the sum of chain outputs close to a known aggregate.  
- Authors introduce a convex relaxation for MAP inference (SACAFHMM) and evaluate it on energy disaggregation.  
- Experiments use two real-world datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency, no per-appliance monitoring).  
- Methodological ideas and optimization formulation are straightforward, but the contribution and application to large datasets are compelling.  
- The paper is a successful blend of algorithmic and applied work with strong real-world results.  
- Reviewer asks whether SACAFHMM’s constraints produce more integral (vs. fractional) solutions similar to AFAMAP’s “one chain changes” constraint; discussion needed.  
- AFAMAP and SACAFHMM seem orthogonal—encouraged to explore combining SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP in the final version.  
- The manuscript sometimes conflates models and optimization procedures; comparisons like “AFHMM vs AFAMAP” are imprecise and should be clarified.  
- Clarify that AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP in AFHMM, and specify what is meant by “AFHMM” (e.g., alternating Viterbi).  
- Overall assessment: a modest extension with a solid optimization method and compelling empirical evaluation.
