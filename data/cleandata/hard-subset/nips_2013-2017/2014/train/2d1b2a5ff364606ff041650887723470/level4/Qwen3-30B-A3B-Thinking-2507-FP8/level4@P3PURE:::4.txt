

The paper introduces an extension to the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) by incorporating constraints that enforce the sum of individual chain outputs to approximate a known aggregate value. The authors propose a convex relaxation for MAP inference and apply the resulting algorithm to real-world energy disaggregation datasets, including 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings and 10 homes with higher-frequency readings (where individual appliance monitoring is absent).  

The core ideas are relatively straightforward, employing a simple optimization formulation, yet the work remains compelling due to its application to large-scale real-world datasets. As a paper bridging applied and algorithmic contributions, it successfully demonstrates practical relevance. The following points would strengthen the paper:  

1. **Clarify SACAFHMM's integer solution behavior**: The motivation for AFAMAP to enforce one-chain-at-a-time state staging is to improve the relaxation's tendency toward integer solutions. Does SACAFHMM achieve a similar effect, or do its solutions maintain lower error despite non-integral values? A discussion addressing this would clarify the distinction between the two approaches.  

2. **Address orthogonality of approaches**: AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear fundamentally orthogonal; SACAFHMM constraints could readily be integrated into AFAMAP's optimization. While this is not required for the author response, I strongly encourage the authors to explore this combination for the final version, as it may yield superior performance.  

3. **Distinguish models from optimization procedures**: The paper occasionally conflates model specifications with optimization algorithms. For instance, comparing "AFHMM" versus "AFAMAP" is imprecise: AFAMAP is an algorithm for (approximately) computing MAP solutions to the AFHMM, and the term "AFHMM" here likely refers to the alternating Viterbi optimization variant. This clarification is essential for accurately framing the contributions. These points further underscore that the strategies presented could be combined for enhanced results.  

The paper proposes a simple extension and optimization for a variant of AFHMM, but the compelling empirical results on real-world data make it a reasonable contribution.
