This paper develops a new method for performing blind source separation by formulating the problem using an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs). This approach is motivated by the idea that domain knowledge can significantly improve the separation of a time series into its constituent components. The primary application demonstrated is energy disaggregation, where prior knowledge about the total expected energy consumption of individual appliances helps to better separate their respective signals from the aggregate household usage. The authors formulate MAP inference as an optimization problem and relax the non-convex objective into a convex quadratic program that can be solved efficiently. The efficacy of the method is demonstrated on both a toy dataset and real-world energy disaggregation data.

The paper is technically sound and supported by a strong experimental section. As expected, the proposed method (AFHMM+SAC) convincingly outperforms both the standard AFHMM and the state-of-the-art AFAMAP baseline, neither of which incorporate such constraints. The performance improvements are shown to be statistically significant via paired t-tests. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate that the computational cost of their approach is comparable to that of AFAMAP.

My main questions and suggestions for improvement are as follows:

1.  **Experimental Setup:** In the toy example, the noise variance (σ² = 0.01) seems quite low. Was this chosen primarily for illustrative purposes? Without further justification, the problem setup feels somewhat simplistic. It would be helpful if the authors could clarify whether such signal-to-noise ratios are representative of real-world scenarios. Relatedly, a comment on the signal-to-noise ratio in the real-world energy application (i.e., the ratio between the aggregate appliance signals and the noise present in the mains readings) would add valuable context.

2.  **Theoretical Justification:** The theoretical exposition is generally clear. However, the paper would be strengthened by a more formal proof of the convexity of the relaxed optimization problem in equation (6). The current reasoning is brief, and a more detailed derivation would improve clarity. Additionally, the paper would benefit from a theoretical analysis of the computational complexity with respect to the length of the time series (T) and the number of hidden states. This would complement the empirical runtime results and provide a clearer picture of the method's scalability compared to AFAMAP.

The paper is well-written and organized. The introduction provides good motivation for the problem and includes a review of related work. While I am not an expert in this specific subfield, the literature review appears to be adequate. I noticed a potential notational inconsistency on page 4 (line 202) in the equation before (6): the term `log p(i)jk` seems inconsistent with the earlier notation `p(i)jk` on page 3. Please clarify if this is a typo or new notation.

To my knowledge, the proposed method of incorporating signal aggregate constraints into AFHMMs is novel and original. While I cannot definitively comment on its significance to the broader HMM and energy disaggregation communities, the contribution seems solid and the results are promising.

**Addendum:**
The authors have satisfactorily addressed my questions in their response. I encourage them to incorporate these clarifications into the final version of the paper. This paper proposes a novel and effective method for blind source separation using Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints. The approach performs very well against state-of-the-art methods, particularly on the challenging real-world energy disaggregation task.
