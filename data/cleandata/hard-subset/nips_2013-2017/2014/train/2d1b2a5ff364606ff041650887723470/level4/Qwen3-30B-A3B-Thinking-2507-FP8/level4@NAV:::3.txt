

The paper introduces additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) for blind source separation. Authors formulate a convex quadratic problem for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating promising results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
Pros:  
- Well-written with strong mathematical foundation.  
- Effective problem motivation.  
- Evaluation shows significant performance gains in key metrics (NDE and SAE).  
Areas of improvement:  
- Results are compared against AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which use less data than SACAFHMM, creating an unfair benchmark. Are there alternative methods that could incorporate SAC but are computationally less efficient?  
- Figure 1 results are ambiguous: AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM for distances >10⁴. This contradicts the discussion claiming consistent superiority, suggesting poorly designed constraints may drastically degrade performance.  
- The paper lacks exploration of complex constraints (e.g., "heater and cooler cannot operate simultaneously," "maximum 3 of 10 bulbs active," or "grinder usage <10 minutes"). Future work should address extending the model to handle such diverse constraint types. The paper provides good motivation and writing; future work should focus on broader constraint generalization.
