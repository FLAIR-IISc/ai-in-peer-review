- Addresses unidentifiability in blind source separation (BSS) for AFHMMs by introducing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) that encode prior knowledge about total source signal over a period.
- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: augments AFHMM objective with a penalty forcing inferred sources to match known aggregates.
- Shows global SACs break the Markov property, making standard inference (e.g., Viterbi) inapplicable.
- Introduces an efficient approximate inference: relaxes MAP integer program to a convex quadratic program (CQP) solvable efficiently.
- Evaluated on synthetic and real energy-disaggregation data, demonstrating substantial gains over AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC yields ~45% error reduction vs AFHMM and ~29% vs AFAMAP on real-world data.
- Strength — Quality & Clarity: well-written, rigorous formulation and strong motivation addressing a fundamental practical barrier.
- Strength — Originality: novel use of signal-aggregate priors in AFHMMs and a clean CQP-based inference formulation.
- Strength — Significance: compelling experimental improvements suggesting practical adoption potential.
- Weakness — Experimental framing: comparisons are imbalanced because baselines do not have access to the SAC prior information.
- Weakness — Sensitivity: performance degrades when provided aggregate priors are inaccurate; poor priors can harm results substantially.
- Weakness — Generality: paper focuses only on aggregate-sum constraints and lacks discussion on extending the CQP framework to other useful constraints (mutual exclusion, temporal duration, cardinality).
- Recommendation/summary: strong, well-motivated paper with minor issues in framing, sensitivity analysis, and discussion of generalization; clear candidate for acceptance.
