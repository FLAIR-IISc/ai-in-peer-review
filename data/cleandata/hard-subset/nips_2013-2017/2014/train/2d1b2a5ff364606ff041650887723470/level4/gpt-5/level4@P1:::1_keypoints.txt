- Proposal: Augment AFHMMs with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to encode domain knowledge about total source energy and relax MAP inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP); show equivalence to posterior regularization with quadratic constraints.  
- Empirical result: Method outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP on synthetic and real household electricity data (significant improvements in NDE and SAE) with comparable runtime.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on AFHMMs, AFAMAP, semi-Markov models and posterior regularization; novelty is global aggregate constraints and the specific convex relaxation.  
- Strength: Clear motivation for SACs where per-source totals are informative (e.g., appliances).  
- Strength: Principled formulation tying penalties to a posterior-regularized objective.  
- Strength: Convex relaxation (CQP) enabling efficient inference and an implementation that scales similarly to baselines in experiments.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance on challenging low-frequency data with statistical significance.  
- Weakness/question: Toy data uses very small noise variance—need discussion of whether this is for visualization or representative of real SNRs, and SNRs in mains-vs-appliance data.  
- Weakness/question: Convexity/relaxation—paper should include a brief proof or pointer that quadratic terms are PSD and constraints yield a convex CQP.  
- Weakness/question: Scaling/tractability—need explicit complexity in T, I, Ki and comparison to AFAMAP; clarify practical limits (number of appliances, horizon length).  
- Minor issue: Notation inconsistency on page 4 (commas in subscripts) should be fixed.  
- Quality: Method is technically sound and experiments are careful; adding convexity proof and clearer complexity analysis would strengthen the paper.  
- Clarity: Generally well written and organized; would benefit from added clarifications on noise/SNRs, convexity, and scaling for reproducibility.  
- Originality: Introducing SACs for AFHMMs and the particular convex relaxation is novel; linking to posterior regularization helps position the work.  
- Significance: Compelling, practically useful gains for energy disaggregation and likely applicable to other BSS problems with aggregate priors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant empirical gains, principled domain-knowledge incorporation, and efficient convex relaxation with comparable runtime.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited theoretical exposition on convexity and scaling, toy example noise concerns, and minor notation issues.  
- Addendum: Authors addressed rebuttal questions; recommend incorporating clarifications (noise/SNR, convexity, scaling) into the final version.
