- Proposes an additive factorial HMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation for blind source separation.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and backed by solid mathematical formulation.  
- Problem motivation is well-articulated and compelling.  
- Evaluation shows notable improvements in metrics such as NDE and SAE.  
- Comparison appears biased: SACAFHMM is compared to AFHMM and AFAMAP that use less data.  
- Missing comparisons to other methods that use SAC but are less computationally efficient.  
- Figure 1 is unclear and suggests AFAMAP may outperform SACAFHMM for distances > 10^4, raising concerns about poor constraints causing worse performance.  
- Discussion may be misleading by implying consistently better results despite ambiguous figure behavior.  
- Model currently lacks incorporation of more complex, realistic constraints (e.g., mutually exclusive devices, cardinality limits, short-duration usage caps).  
- Future work should extend the algorithm to support various constraint types to increase applicability.
