The paper proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors develop a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC, which demonstrates promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper is well-written and has a solid mathematical foundation. The problem motivation is clear, and the evaluation results indicate a significant boost in performance metrics, specifically the normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).

However, there are a few areas that require improvement. Firstly, the results are presented as an improvement over AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which use less data than SAC AFHMM. This comparison seems unfair, as it does not account for the additional information provided by the SAC. It would be beneficial to compare the proposed method with other approaches that could utilize SAC but are not as efficient in computation.

Secondly, the results in Figure 1 are not entirely clear. It appears that for distances greater than 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms SAC AFHMM. This suggests that poor constraints can lead to significantly poorer performance, which contradicts the discussion that implies the results are always better. A more detailed analysis of this phenomenon would be helpful.

Lastly, the paper could benefit from exploring more complex constraints that could be incorporated into the algorithm. For instance, constraints such as "a person cannot use the heater and cooler at the same time" or "no more than 3 out of 10 bulbs in the house can be active at the same time" could be considered. Additionally, time constraints, such as "grinders can only be used for less than 10 minutes," could also be examined. The authors should discuss the possibility of extending the algorithm to accommodate these types of constraints.

Overall, the paper provides good motivation for the problem and is well-written. Future work should focus on extending the model to incorporate more complex constraints, which would further enhance the applicability and effectiveness of the proposed method.
