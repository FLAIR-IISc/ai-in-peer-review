The paper proposes an extension to the additive factorial HMM model, incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) that require the sum of individual chain outputs to be close to some reasonable (known) aggregate value. The authors develop a convex relaxation for MAP inference and apply the algorithm to real-world data sets involving energy disaggregation, both across 100 UK homes with low frequency readings, and 9 homes with higher frequency readings.

The main ideas presented in this paper are straightforward, yet the work is still compelling due to its application to relatively large real-world data sets. The authors combine both applied and algorithmic elements, making this paper a success. However, some points could be clarified to improve the paper.

The authors should discuss how the SACAFHMM optimization formulation affects the relaxation to achieve integer solutions. Specifically, they should compare the performance of SACAFHMM with non-integral solutions to AFAMAP, which enforces the constraint that one chain stages state at a time to improve the relaxation. This discussion would help elucidate the distinctions between the two approaches.

Furthermore, it seems that the AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas are orthogonal, and one could easily include the SACAFHMM constraints in the optimization formulation for AFAMAP. Although this is not expected to be explored in the author response, it would be a valuable extension for a final version.

The authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures. For instance, comparing the results of "AFHMM" versus "AFAMAP" is not precise, as AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximately computing MAP solutions to the AFHMM. The authors likely mean alternating Viterbi optimization by AFHMM. Clarifying this point would also highlight the potential for combining the strategies of the different algorithms presented, which might perform better than the current approaches.

The paper proposes a simple extension and optimization to solve a variant of the AFHMM. While the results on real-world data are compelling, the authors could further improve the paper by addressing the above points.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel extension to the additive factorial HMM model, incorporating signal aggregate constraints.
* The authors develop a convex relaxation for MAP inference and apply the algorithm to real-world data sets.
* The results on energy disaggregation data sets are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how the SACAFHMM optimization formulation affects the relaxation to achieve integer solutions.
* The authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures, which could be clarified.
* The potential for combining the strategies of the different algorithms presented is not fully explored.

Acceptance:

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should address the points mentioned above to improve the clarity and impact of the paper.
