- Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation, showing promising results on synthetic and real data.
- Paper is well-written with a strong mathematical foundation.
- Problem motivation is clear and well-articulated.
- Evaluation shows significant improvement in performance metrics (NDE and SAE).
- Comparisons may be unfair because SACAFHMM uses more data than baselines (AFHMM, AFAMAP); additional-data effects aren’t isolated.
- Suggests comparing to alternative methods that can use SAC or are computationally cheaper to use SAC.
- Figure 1 is unclear and may show AFAMAP outperforming SACAFHMM for distances > 10^4, indicating poorly chosen constraints can degrade performance and that claims of consistent improvement may be misleading.
- Reviewer expected exploration of more complex constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality limits, time/duration constraints like usage limits).
- Recommends future work to extend the model/algorithm to accommodate a wider variety of constraint types.
