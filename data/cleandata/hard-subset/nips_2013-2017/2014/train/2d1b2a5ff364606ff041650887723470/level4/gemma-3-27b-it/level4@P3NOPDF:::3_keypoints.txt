- Introduces AFHMM-SAC with a convex quadratic relaxation for blind source separation, showing promising synthetic and real-world results.  
- Paper is well-written with rigorous mathematical formulation and clear motivation for signal aggregate constraints.  
- Convex relaxation provides a practical, tractable solution to the AFHMM-SAC model.  
- Experimental results show improvements in Normalized Distance Error (NDE) and Source Amplitudes Error (SAE).  
- Fairness concern: AFHMM-SAC used significantly more training data than compared baselines, possibly inflating observed gains.  
- Recommend comparing against alternative methods that also leverage signal aggregate constraints, even if less efficient.  
- Figure 1 is unclear and may indicate AFAMAP outperforms AFHMM-SAC at large distance values; this contradicts claims of consistent superiority.  
- Authors should analyze conditions that cause performance drops at higher distances and the impact of poorly chosen constraints.  
- Current implementation uses simple aggregate constraints (e.g., total power); paper should discuss extending to more complex/realistic constraints.  
- Need discussion of challenges and potential approaches (and whether the convex relaxation remains applicable) for constraints like mutual exclusivity, resource limits, and temporal limits.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing fairness of comparisons, clarifying Figure 1 results, and expanding discussion on complex constraint incorporation.
