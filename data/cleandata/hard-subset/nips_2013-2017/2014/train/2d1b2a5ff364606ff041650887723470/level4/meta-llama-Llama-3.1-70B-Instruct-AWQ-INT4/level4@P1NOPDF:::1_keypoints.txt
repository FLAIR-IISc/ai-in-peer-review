- Proposes a novel blind source separation method using Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).  
- Motivation: incorporate domain knowledge (e.g., expected per-appliance energy totals) to improve component separation.  
- Uses energy disaggregation as the motivating application and example.  
- Transforms the log-posterior objective into a convex optimization problem for separation.  
- Empirically validated on a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.  
- Technically sound with a strong simulation section; method outperforms AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Improvements shown significant via t-tests; optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Reviewer questions the toy example’s low noise variance and requests clarification on whether such SNRs are realistic and on mains vs. appliance SNR.  
- Requests a formal proof that equation (6) is convex, as current reasoning is not fully convincing.  
- Suggests adding theoretical scaling analysis (data length, number of hidden states) and comparison of scalability to AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well written and well motivated, with clear literature discussion; minor notation issue noted on page 4.  
- Reviewer believes the work is novel and encourages authors to include clarifications addressed during feedback.
