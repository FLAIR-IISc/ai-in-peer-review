- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: an additive factorial HMM incorporating signal aggregate constraints for blind source separation, aimed at energy disaggregation.  
- Clear, concise introduction motivating the need for domain knowledge in blind source separation.  
- Thorough related-work review that highlights the novelty of incorporating SACs.  
- Technical derivation of AFHMM+SAC is sound and detailed.  
- Presents a convex quadratic program for the relaxed inference problem and discusses computational complexity.  
- Evaluation shows significant improvements in NDE and SAE over AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Demonstrates robustness to errors in the specified constraint value.  
- Comparison is limited: baselines use less data than AFHMM+SAC, so fairer comparisons to similarly trained methods are needed.  
- Figure 1 is unclear; for distances >10^4 AFAMAP appears to outperform AFHMM+SAC, suggesting poor constraints can hurt performance.  
- Paper does not fully discuss the impact of poor or mis-specified constraints.  
- Mentions promising extensions to person-specific or time constraints but lacks implementation/details on how to incorporate them.  
- Well-written overall with strong motivation and potential to make a significant contribution.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, thorough literature review, sound technical work, promising empirical results, extensibility.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient comparisons, unclear figure/results interpretation, limited discussion on handling poor constraints.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach with solid improvements and field relevance.  
- Rejection arguments: current comparisons and discussion of failure modes are insufficient.
