- Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, focused on energy disaggregation.  
- SACs constrain each unknown source’s total signal to be close to a specified value, injecting domain knowledge.  
- Introduces SACs as a novel, general framework for incorporating prior knowledge into probabilistic models.  
- Shows the SAC-augmented model can be derived from the posterior regularization framework, giving a theoretical basis.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Uses well-defined evaluation metrics (normalized disaggregation error and signal aggregate error) to quantify performance.  
- Paper is well-organized and notation is properly defined.  
- Text is dense and uses many acronyms, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Assumes a priori knowledge of total signal values for each source, which may not always be available in practice.  
- Authors acknowledge the prior-knowledge limitation and suggest extensions to model its uncertainty.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective framework with clear presentation and potential to advance state-of-the-art.  
- Arguments for rejection: reliance on known total signals and dense, acronym-heavy text could limit practical applicability and readability.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestions to clarify assumptions/limitations and add more examples or case studies.
