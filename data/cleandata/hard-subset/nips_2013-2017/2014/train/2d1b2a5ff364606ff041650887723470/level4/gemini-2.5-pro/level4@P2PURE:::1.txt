This paper proposes a new method for blind source separation by formulating the problem as an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) and incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs). The core motivation is to leverage domain-specific knowledge—such as the expected total usage of an appliance over a time period in energy disaggregation—to improve the separation of a time series into its constituent components. The objective function, corresponding to the log-posterior of the hidden states, is transformed into a tractable convex optimization problem. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated on both a synthetic dataset and real-world domestic energy readings.

The paper is technically sound and supported by a strong experimental section. As expected, the proposed method outperforms both the standard AFHMM and a recent alternative, AFAMAP, neither of which incorporate SACs. The improvements are shown to be statistically significant via t-tests, and the optimization speed is demonstrated to be comparable to AFAMAP. My only question regarding the results concerns the low noise variance in the synthetic example. It would be helpful if the authors clarified whether this was chosen specifically to highlight the performance gap in Figure 2, as it makes the problem setup seem artificially simple. A comment on whether such signal-to-noise ratios are typical in real-world data would be valuable. Similarly, for the energy application, discussing the signal-to-noise ratio between the aggregate appliance output and the mains readings would strengthen the analysis.

The theoretical aspects of the paper are generally well-structured and presented. However, the paper would be strengthened by a formal proof that the final optimization procedure in equation (6) is convex, as the provided reasoning is not immediately obvious. Additionally, including a theoretical complexity analysis with respect to data length and the number of hidden states would be a valuable addition. Such an analysis would complement the empirical timing results and provide insight into the method's scalability for larger datasets and state spaces, particularly in comparison to AFAMAP.

The paper is well-written and organized. The introduction provides good motivation and a clear overview of the relevant literature, although I cannot definitively comment on its completeness as I am not an expert in this specific problem domain. I noticed a minor potential notational error on page 4 (line 202) in the unnumbered equation preceding (6); the commas in the subscripts for the object *p* appear to be inconsistent with the notation established on page 3.

To my knowledge, the work is novel and original. However, as a non-expert, I cannot fully assess the significance of these results for the HMM community or the specific energy application studied.

Addendum: The authors satisfactorily answered my questions in the feedback phase, and I encourage them to incorporate these clarifications into the final version of the paper. This paper proposes a method for blind source separation using Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints. The method performs very well when compared with the state-of-the-art, particularly on real data from an energy disaggregation problem.
