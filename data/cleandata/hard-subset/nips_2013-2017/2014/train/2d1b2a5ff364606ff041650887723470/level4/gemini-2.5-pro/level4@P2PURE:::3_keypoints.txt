- Proposes SAC-AFHMM for blind source separation with inference cast as a convex quadratic problem.  
- Demonstrates promising results on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and grounded in strong mathematical foundations.  
- Problem formulation and motivation are clear and compelling.  
- Experimental results show substantial improvements in NDE and SAE metrics.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited/unfair, as they exclude methods that can use SAC.  
- Should compare to alternatives that leverage SAC to separate gains from added data vs. optimization method.  
- Figure 1 is unclear: AFAMAP appears to outperform SAC-AFHMM for distances >10^4, implying noisy/inaccurate constraints can hurt performance and contradict claims of consistent improvement.  
- Model would be stronger if extended/discussed for richer constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality, temporal constraints).
