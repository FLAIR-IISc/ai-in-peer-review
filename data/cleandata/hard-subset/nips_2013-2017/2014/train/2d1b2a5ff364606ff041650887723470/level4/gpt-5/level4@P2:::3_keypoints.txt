- Paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation in AFHMMs, encoding aggregate activity targets and encouraging source aggregates to match specified totals.
- Authors derive a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate MAP inference by relaxing integer constraints and adding auxiliary transition variables.
- They relate the SAC objective to posterior regularization and provide a PSD CQP formulation.
- Experiments on synthetic and large real-world HES data show consistent improvements in NDE and SAE versus standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with similar runtime.
- Builds on AFHMM and AFAMAP and connects to posterior regularization, contrasting SACs with duration, interleaving, and k-segment constraints.
- Strength: clear motivation—aggregate priors address identifiability in BSS and are realistic/practical.
- Strength: solid technical development—a principled convex relaxation with clean theoretical connections.
- Strength: strong empirical results and a robustness study showing performance under constraint misspecification.
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons—AFHMM+SAC uses extra side information; need baselines that also exploit SACs (e.g., AFAMAP+SAC, posterior-regularized AFHMM, enriched semi-Markov methods).
- Weakness: sensitivity to mis-specified constraints—need quantified breakpoints where SACs harm performance and guidance on λ tuning relative to misspecification.
- Weakness: oracle TTC results should be emphasized as oracle; clarify how NTC µ0 estimates are obtained and avoid train-test leakage.
- Weakness: convex relaxation rounding—need details on how fractional solutions are rounded, integrality gap statistics, and whether Markov structure is preserved after rounding.
- Weakness: scalability—CVX may not scale; discuss specialized solvers, decomposition (ADMM), or exploiting banded structure and provide empirical runtime vs T and state count.
- Weakness: discussion of richer realistic constraints (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, duration limits) is brief—state which constraints fit the linear/quadratic CQP framework and which break convexity.
- Presentation: improve Figure 1 (axes, units, error bars) and clarify when AFAMAP outperforms AFHMM+SAC at large target errors.
- Presentation: provide more training details—number of states per appliance, learning of µi and transition matrices, and how λi were chosen and tuned.
- Reproducibility: specify CVX settings, stopping criteria, rounding/post-processing steps, and provide public code if possible.
- Pro for acceptance: addresses identifiability with a practical prior, offers principled convex formulation, and shows strong empirical gains with similar cost.
- Con for acceptance: comparisons don’t isolate SAC effect, limited analysis of failure modes and hyperparameter guidance, and brief treatment of richer constraints.
- Overall: well-written, technically sound, and practically relevant; strengthening fairness of comparisons, sensitivity analysis, and extensibility/scalability discussion would improve the paper.
