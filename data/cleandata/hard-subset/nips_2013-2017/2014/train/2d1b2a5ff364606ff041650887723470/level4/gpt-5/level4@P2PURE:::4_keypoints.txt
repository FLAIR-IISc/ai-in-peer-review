- Extends additive factorial HMM with constraint forcing sum of chains to stay close to a known aggregate signal.
- Proposes a convex relaxation for MAP inference and applies it to two real-world energy-disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes high-frequency without submetering).
- Ideas and optimization are relatively straightforward but compelling, with solid empirical evaluation on sizable datasets.
- Ask whether AFAMAP’s “at most one chain changes” constraint yields integral solutions and whether SACAFHMM achieves a similar tightening or remains fractional while lowering error.
- Suggest exploring incorporation of SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP’s optimization, as the approaches appear orthogonal.
- Paper sometimes conflates models and algorithms: AFAMAP is an algorithm for MAP on AFHMM, while the labeled “AFHMM” implementation seems to be alternating Viterbi—this should be clarified.
- Note that combining strategies from the different algorithms could likely yield better performance; encourage investigating hybrid methods.
- Overall assessment: simple extension plus optimization method, compelling real-world results, reasonable and promising submission.
