- Proposes blind source separation using Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).  
- Motivation: use domain knowledge (aggregate/expected usage) to improve decomposition, demonstrated on energy disaggregation.  
- Reformulates the log-posterior of hidden chains into a convex optimization problem.  
- Evaluated on both a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.  
- Empirical results: outperforms standard AFMM and AFAMAP; improvements are statistically significant (t-tests).  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Reviewer questions the low noise variance in the toy example and asks if those SNRs are realistic; requests clarification on mains vs. appliance signal/noise ratio in real data.  
- Requests a formal proof that the final procedure (eq. (6)) is convex, as this is not fully evident.  
- Requests theoretical scaling arguments (data length, number of hidden states) and discussion of tractability for larger datasets/state spaces compared to AFAMAP.  
- Paper is technically sound, well written, and organized; literature review seems adequate though reviewer is not an expert.  
- Notes a minor notation issue on page 4 (commas in subscripts before eq. (6)).  
- Work appears novel and original; authors addressed reviewer questions during feedback and should include those clarifications in the next version.
