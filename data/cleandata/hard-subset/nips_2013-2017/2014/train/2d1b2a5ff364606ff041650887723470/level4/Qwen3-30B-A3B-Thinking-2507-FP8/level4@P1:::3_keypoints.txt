- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) framework to encourage each source's total signal to match specified aggregate values.
- Transforms the constrained inference problem into a convex quadratic program for efficient inference.
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation data (45% reduction vs AFHMM, 29% vs AFAMAP).
- Claims three main contributions: SAC framework, convex QP inference, and strong empirical improvements.
- Situates work well within prior energy disaggregation and BSS literature, relating to posterior regularization and generalized expectation criteria.
- Technical approach is sound with a clear derivation of the convex quadratic program.
- Experiments are thorough, showing robustness to constraint errors and strong real-data performance.
- Acknowledges limitations: requires prior aggregate knowledge and solution not guaranteed optimal due to identifiability.
- Evaluation could be strengthened by comparisons to other constraint-based methods (e.g., k-segment constraints, non-homogeneous HMMs).
- Paper is well-written with clear math and figures, though Figure 1 needs clearer axis labels and interpretation.
- SACs are a novel, generally applicable constraint class, but the paper should better differentiate SACs from other HMM constraint approaches.
- Results are significant for non-intrusive load monitoring and potentially other BSS domains, but broader applicability discussion is limited.
- Strengths: strong technical foundation, clear derivation, comprehensive evaluation, practical relevance, good relation to posterior regularization.
- Weaknesses: potentially unfair AFAMAP comparison, unclear behavior for large constraint errors, limited discussion of SACs' limitations and extensions, and no demonstration of other constraint types.
