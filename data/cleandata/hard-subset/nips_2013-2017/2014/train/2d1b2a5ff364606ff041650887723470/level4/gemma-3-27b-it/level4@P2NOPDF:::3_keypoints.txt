- Paper introduces AFHMM-SAC, a convex-relaxed AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints for blind source separation.  
- Strong mathematical foundation and clear motivation; paper is well-written.  
- Experimental results show notable improvements in NDE and SAE.  
- Comparisons may be unfair because AFHMM-SAC uses more data than baselines, potentially biasing results.  
- Unclear whether performance gains stem from SAC specifically or from extra data; need experiments equalizing data.  
- Question whether other methods can incorporate SAC and how their computational efficiency compares to AFHMM-SAC.  
- Figure 1 suggests AFAMAP may outperform AFHMM-SAC at large distances, indicating possible degraded performance with poor constraints.  
- Current discussion may overstate consistent superiority of AFHMM-SAC; should acknowledge risks from suboptimal constraints.  
- Paper only explores simple aggregate constraints; extension to more realistic constraints (mutual exclusivity, resource limits, time limits) is needed.  
- Need analysis of generalizability to diverse constraint structures and associated computational challenges.  
- Overall: valuable, promising contribution, but should address fairness of comparisons, impact of poorly specified constraints, and extension to complex constraint types; authors note this as future work.
