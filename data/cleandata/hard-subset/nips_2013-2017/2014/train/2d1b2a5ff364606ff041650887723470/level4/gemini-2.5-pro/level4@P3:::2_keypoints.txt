- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge about total source signal over time into blind source separation (BSS).
- Applies SACs to energy disaggregation by estimating typical appliance daily energy and enforcing global sum constraints.
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) to form AFHMM+SAC for MAP estimation of hidden states under aggregate constraints.
- SACs are non-Markovian, so standard algorithms (e.g., Viterbi) are inapplicable.
- Authors relax the MAP problem to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient approximate inference.
- Evaluated on a large real-world HES dataset with two realistic scenarios (synthesized aggregate and real mains with unmodeled sources).
- AFHMM+SAC reduces normalized disaggregation error by 45% over AFHMM and 29% over AFAMAP in one experimental setup.
- Includes an oracle experiment using true total consumption (TTC) to show an upper bound on performance gains from SACs.
- Theoretical connection: penalized objective can be derived from posterior regularization framework.
- Builds on prior constraint-based identifiability work but proposes a novel global, non-Markovian aggregate constraint.
- Method is computationally efficient and scales similarly to existing approaches, increasing practical adoption potential.
- Significant impact for energy disaggregation and potential generalizability to other BSS domains (audio, bioinformatics).
- Strength: technically sound formulation and clear, correct derivation of the CQP with thorough empirical support.
- Weakness: paper clarity suffers from proliferation of acronyms and a convoluted framing of constraints versus priors.
- Recommendation: strong paper with novel idea and robust results; main revisions needed are presentational (clarify framing and reduce acronym overload).
