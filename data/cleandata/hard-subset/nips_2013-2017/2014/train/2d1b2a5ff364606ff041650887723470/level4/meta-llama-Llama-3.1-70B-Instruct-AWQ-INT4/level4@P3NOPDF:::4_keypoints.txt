- Proposes SACAFHMM: extending AFHMM with sum constraints and solving MAP via convex relaxation.  
- Applied algorithm to real-world energy disaggregation data (100 UK homes low-frequency, 10 homes higher-frequency).  
- Method is straightforward but compelling due to scale and practical application.  
- Paper successfully combines applied and algorithmic contributions.  
- Suggests evaluating whether SACAFHMM yields more integral solutions or lower error despite non-integral relaxations, compared to AFAMAP.  
- Notes AFAMAP enforces single-chain state changes to improve relaxation; asks if SACAFHMM achieves similar benefits.  
- Points out AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas are orthogonal and could be combined for potential gains.  
- Encourages exploring incorporation of SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP in future work.  
- Criticizes occasional conflation of models and optimization methods (e.g., “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP”); requests clarification (likely alternating Viterbi reference).  
- Overall conclusion: simple, effective extension with strong real-world results and generally well-written presentation.
