This paper introduces a novel blind source separation technique, framing the problem as an additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) and incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs). The core idea is to leverage domain knowledge – specifically, expected usage patterns – to enhance the decomposition of time series data into constituent components. The application demonstrated is energy disaggregation, aiming to better isolate individual appliance energy consumption from total household readings. The resulting objective function, maximizing the posterior distribution of hidden states, is reformulated as a convex optimization problem. The method’s performance is validated through simulations and analysis of real-world domestic energy data.

The paper is technically rigorous and features a strong experimental evaluation. As anticipated, the proposed method outperforms standard AFHMM and the AFAMAP algorithm (which lacks SACs), with statistically significant improvements confirmed via t-tests. Computational efficiency is shown to be comparable to AFAMAP. A point of clarification is needed regarding the low noise variance used in the toy example; it would be helpful to understand if this was intended to highlight the method’s separation capabilities, or if such signal-to-noise ratios are representative of real-world scenarios. Further discussion of the signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and appliance output in the energy application would also be beneficial.

The theoretical development is generally clear and well-organized. A formal proof demonstrating the convexity of the procedure outlined in equation (6) would strengthen the paper. Additionally, including theoretical scaling analysis – considering data length and the number of hidden states – would complement the empirical findings. Investigating the method’s tractability with larger datasets and state spaces, and comparing it to AFAMAP in this context, would be valuable.

The paper is well-written and logically structured. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and provides a comprehensive review of related work, though my familiarity with the field is limited. I noted a potential notational inconsistency on page 4 (line 202) in the equation preceding (6), where the subscripts of ‘p’ appear to include unnecessary commas.

To the best of my knowledge, the work presents a novel and original contribution. However, I lack the specialized expertise to fully assess the significance of these results within the HMM community or the energy application domain. 

Addendum: The authors have addressed my initial questions satisfactorily through the feedback process, and I recommend incorporating these clarifications into the revised manuscript. This work introduces a promising method for blind source separation based on Additive Factorial HMMs enhanced with Signal Aggregate Constraints, demonstrating strong performance – particularly in the context of energy disaggregation – when compared to existing state-of-the-art techniques.
