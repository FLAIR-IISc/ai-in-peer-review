- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation, applied to AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.  
- Encourages each unknown source’s total signal to match a pre-specified (known or estimable) aggregate value.  
- Frames approximate inference as a convex quadratic program for efficient computation.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset (HES).  
- Builds on established BSS and AFHMM literature and differs from recent AFHMM improvements that constrain state transitions.  
- Relates to posterior regularization and measurement-based learning, but the specific aggregate constraint is underexplored.  
- Strength — Novelty: SACs are a sensible and potentially widely applicable prior for BSS.  
- Strength — Practicality: Convex QP formulation enables scalable, efficient computation.  
- Strength — Empirical: Strong performance gains on large-scale, low-frequency real data.  
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is generally well-written and clearly explained.  
- Weakness — Limited theoretical analysis of SAC properties and their effect on model identifiability.  
- Weakness — Authors do not fully explore combining SACs with other methods (e.g., AFAMAP), which may yield further gains.  
- Weakness — Occasional conflation of model vs. inference algorithms (e.g., AFHMM vs. Viterbi/AFAMAP) harms clarity.  
- Weakness — Lack of discussion on whether the convex relaxation yields non-integral state solutions and how that impacts interpretation/performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a practical challenge with a novel, efficient method and compelling empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theory, insufficient exploration of method combinations, and some presentation confusions.  
- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution worth accepting with minor revisions to clarify inference vs. model distinctions and address convex relaxation/integrality concerns.
