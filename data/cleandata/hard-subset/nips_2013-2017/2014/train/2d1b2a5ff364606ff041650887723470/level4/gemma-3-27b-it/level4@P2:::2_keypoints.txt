- Problem: Tackles blind source separation for energy disaggregation by leveraging domain knowledge about appliance energy totals.
- Method: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate expected total consumption per appliance into models.
- Model integration: Embeds SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM).
- Inference: Formulates the constrained problem as a convex quadratic program, enabling efficient inference.
- Empirical results: Shows substantial improvements on the HES dataset, outperforming original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.
- Metrics: Demonstrates gains in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).
- Motivation: Well-motivated by identifiability issues in energy disaggregation — priors on aggregates help separation.
- Originality: SACs are a novel, general framework for incorporating aggregate-domain knowledge into BSS.
- Broader impact: Approach is applicable to other BSS problems where aggregate priors exist and has practical significance for energy conservation.
- Connection to prior work: Relates to posterior regularization, adding conceptual depth.
- Clarity issues: Writing is dense and frequently uses acronyms (SAC, AFHMM, AFAMAP), hurting readability.
- Presentation problems: Derivation of the relaxed optimization and convex program feels rushed and needs clearer exposition.
- Parameter interpretation: Needs more detailed explanation of how constraint parameters (δi, λi) affect results.
- Recommendation: Overall positive — accept with minor revisions focused on clarity, reduced acronym use, and clearer technical explanations.
