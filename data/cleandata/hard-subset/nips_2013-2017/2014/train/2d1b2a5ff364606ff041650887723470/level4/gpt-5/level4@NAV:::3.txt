The paper introduces an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to tackle blind source separation. The authors formulate a convex quadratic optimization problem for a relaxed AFHMM under SAC and report promising results on both synthetic and real datasets.

Pros:
- The manuscript is clearly written and rests on a solid mathematical foundation.
- The problem is well motivated.
- The evaluation shows notable gains in NDE and SAE.

Areas of improvement:
- The comparisons are against AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which appear to use less data than SACAFHMM, making the comparison potentially unfair. Are there other approaches that can exploit SAC but are less computationally efficient?
- The results in Figure 1 are unclear. It seems that for distances greater than 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM. Does this suggest that poorly chosen constraints can lead to substantially worse performance? The discussion implies results are always better, which may be misleading.
- I expected more complex constraints to be incorporated. For example, a person cannot run the heater and cooler simultaneously; no more than 3 of 10 bulbs on at once; grinders limited to under 10 minutes of operation. There are many such constraint types one could specify. Can the algorithm be extended to accommodate these additional constraints?

Overall, the paper is well written and provides strong motivation for the problem. Future work should extend the model to broader classes of constraints.
