- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation by using domain knowledge of total source signals.  
- Incorporates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) and reformulates constrained inference as a convex quadratic program.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation data, showing significant improvements over baseline AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP (≈33% improvement in normalized disaggregation error).  
- Connects to prior AFHMM work (Ghahramani & Jordan) and constrained approaches like AFAMAP, and shows SACs can be derived via posterior regularization.  
- Theoretical derivation of SACs and convex QP formulation is sound and computationally efficient.  
- Experimental comparisons are careful and include statistical validation (paired t-tests).  
- Novel contribution: aggregate constraints on total signal are a principled and previously underexplored class of constraints for BSS/energy disaggregation.  
- Significant potential impact for energy disaggregation and other BSS domains (audio, biology); approach could be used in unsupervised settings with survey data.  
- Main clarity issues: excessive jargon, inconsistent notation, many undefined acronyms, and overly complex mathematical exposition.  
- Abrupt transition between theory and implementation (Sections 4→5) and insufficient intuitive explanation of SACs before formalism.  
- Key limitation: sensitivity to choice of constraint values (μi₀) is not sufficiently explored, which is important for practical use.  
- Practical assumption concern: method assumes known total energy consumption (μi₀), which may not be available in all scenarios.  
- Weaknesses include limited discussion of failure cases and need for clearer presentation and notation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking authors to define acronyms, simplify math, add sensitivity analysis for μi₀, clarify SAC–posterior-regularization links, and provide a more intuitive SAC explanation.
