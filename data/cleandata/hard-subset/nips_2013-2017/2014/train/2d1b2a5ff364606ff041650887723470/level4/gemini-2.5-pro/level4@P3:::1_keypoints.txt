- Summary: Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge into blind source separation, integrated into an AFHMM and solved via a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation for MAP inference; evaluated on synthetic and real energy-disaggregation data with significant accuracy gains.

- Strength 1 (Novelty): SACs are a simple, intuitive, and novel regularizer that captures common aggregate prior knowledge missing from standard models.

- Strength 2 (Empirical results): AFHMM+SAC reduces normalized disaggregation error substantially (45% vs AFHMM, 29% vs AFAMAP) with statistically significant improvements on real data.

- Strength 3 (Technical soundness): Reformulating the non‑Markovian inference as a CQP is technically solid, making inference tractable and competitively efficient.

- Strength 4 (Presentation): Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and connects to posterior regularization for stronger theoretical grounding.

- Weakness 1 (Experiment parameters): Need justification for low noise variance in toy experiments and context on signal-to-noise (unmodeled appliance contribution) in real-data experiments.

- Weakness 1a (suggestion): Briefly state whether chosen SNRs reflect realistic settings or were for illustrative clarity.

- Weakness 2 (Theoretical clarity): Convexity claim of the relaxed problem should be made more explicit with a brief justification.

- Weakness 2a (scalability): Include theoretical complexity analysis describing how CQP runtime scales with T (time), I (sources), and K (states).

- Minor comment (clarity): Overall clarity is high.

- Minor comment (originality/significance): Work appears original and practically significant, especially for energy disaggregation.

- Minor comment (typo): Check notational inconsistency in the transition probability subscript before Eq. (6) (p(i)jk vs p(i)jk).

- Conclusion: Paper is novel, effective, and recommended for acceptance pending incorporation of clarifications from the author response.
