This paper proposes a new approach to blind source separation by modeling the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The key idea is to incorporate domain knowledge to improve the decomposition of time series into component signals. The paper focuses on energy disaggregation: appliance-level consumption is separated from mains data more effectively when prior information about each appliance’s total expected usage over a time window is included. The objective function—maximizing the log posterior of the hidden chains given the observations—is reformulated as a convex optimization problem. The method is validated on both a toy dataset and real household energy data.

The paper is technically sound and includes a strong simulation section. As expected, the proposed method outperforms a standard AFHMM and AFAMAP (a recent alternative), neither of which uses SACs. The performance gains are reported as statistically significant via t-tests, and the optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. My only concern about the experimental section is the use of a very low noise variance in the toy example. Was this chosen primarily to make Figure 2 visually compelling by highlighting the separation quality, or is this representative of realistic settings? Without clarification, the setup risks looking “overly toy” and too easy. Relatedly, it would help to comment on typical signal-to-noise ratios in the real data, particularly the SNR between the mains readings and the aggregate appliance outputs in the energy disaggregation task.

The theoretical development is generally clear and well structured. However, the paper would benefit from a proof (or a more explicit argument) that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex; the current reasoning did not make this fully clear to me. In addition, some theoretical scaling analysis—characterizing computational complexity as a function of sequence length and the number of hidden states—would complement the simulations. Are the methods tractable for substantially larger datasets and state spaces, and how does their scalability compare to AFAMAP?

The paper is well written and well organized. The introduction motivates the problem nicely and situates it within the literature. I am not deeply familiar with all related work, so I cannot definitively assess coverage, but the discussion appears appropriate. I did notice a small potential notation issue on page 4 (line 202): in the unnumbered equation before (6), the subscripts following p seem to include commas that are inconsistent with the earlier notation on page 3 (unless new notation is being introduced there).

To the best of my knowledge, the work is novel and original. That said, I am not an expert in this specific subarea, so I cannot fully assess the significance of the contribution for the HMM community or the energy disaggregation domain.

Addendum: The authors addressed my questions satisfactorily during the feedback phase, and I encourage them to incorporate similar clarifications in the next revision. Overall, this paper proposes a compelling method for blind source separation using additive factorial HMMs with signal aggregate constraints, and it demonstrates strong performance compared to the state of the art, particularly on real-world energy disaggregation data.
