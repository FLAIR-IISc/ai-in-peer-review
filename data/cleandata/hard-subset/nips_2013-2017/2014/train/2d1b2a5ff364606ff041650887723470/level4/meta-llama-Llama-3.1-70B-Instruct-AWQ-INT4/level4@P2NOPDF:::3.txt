The paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to tackle the blind source separation problem. The authors develop a convex quadratic formulation for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, it is well-written and provides a solid mathematical foundation for the proposed technique. The problem motivation is clear and effectively conveyed. Furthermore, the evaluation results show a significant improvement in performance metrics, including normalized displacement error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).

However, there are some areas that require improvement. One concern is that the results are compared to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which utilize less data than SACAFHMM. This comparison may be unfair, as it does not account for the additional data used by SACAFHMM. It would be beneficial to compare the proposed technique to other approaches that also utilize SAC but may not be as computationally efficient.

Additionally, the results presented in Figure 1 are unclear. It appears that for distances greater than 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM. This raises questions about the impact of poor constraints on performance. The discussion suggests that the results are consistently better, which may be misleading. It would be helpful to provide a more nuanced discussion of the results, including potential limitations and areas for improvement.

Another area for improvement is the incorporation of more complex constraints into the algorithm. For instance, real-world scenarios often involve constraints such as "a person cannot use a heater and cooler simultaneously" or "no more than three out of ten bulbs can be active at the same time." The algorithm could also be extended to accommodate time constraints, such as "grinders cannot be used for more than 10 minutes." While the paper provides a good foundation for the problem, it would be beneficial to explore the possibility of extending the algorithm to incorporate these more complex constraints.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear motivation for the problem. The future work section effectively outlines potential avenues for extending the model to incorporate more complex constraints.
