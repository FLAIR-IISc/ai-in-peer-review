The paper proposes Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) to solve blind source separation problems. The authors propose convex quadratic problems for relaxed AFHMM with SAC. This technique shows promising results in both synthetic and real-world data.

Advantages:
- The paper is well-written and has a good mathematical foundation.
- The motivation for the problem is good.
- Evaluation results indicate good improvements in performance metrics, NDE, and SAE.

Areas for improvement:
- The results are shown as improvements compared to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP. Both of these use less data than SACAFHMM, which seems like an unfair comparison. Is there any other method that can use SAC but is not as efficient in computation?
- Figure 1 results are not clear. It seems that AFAMAP has outperformed SACAFHMM for distances greater than 10^4. Does this suggest that performance may worsen due to poor constraints? The discussion seems to suggest that the results are always good, which seems misleading.
- I expected the algorithm to include more complex constraints. For example, a person cannot use a heater and a cooler at the same time. What if no more than 3 out of 10 bulbs are active in the house at the same time? There may be a constraint of less than 10 minutes for things like grinders. In short, different types of constraints can be specified. Is it possible to extend the algorithm to other types of constraints as well? The paper is well-written and provides a good motivation for the problem. Future work is to further extend the model to other types of constraints.
