- Proposes a new blind source separation method: AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).  
- Motivation: incorporating domain knowledge (aggregate signals) improves time-series component separation.  
- Application example: energy disaggregation (separating appliance usage from aggregate mains readings).  
- Formulates posterior log-probability of hidden chains and transforms it into a convex optimization problem.  
- Validated on a toy dataset and real domestic energy data.  
- Technically sound with a strong experimental section.  
- Method outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP; improvements are statistically significant (t-tests).  
- Computational speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Question: toy example uses low noise variance—need clarification whether this is illustrative or realistic for actual data.  
- Suggest reporting signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and aggregate appliance outputs in the energy application.  
- Theoretical presentation is clear and well structured.  
- Request: provide a formal proof of convexity for the procedure in equation (6), as current reasoning is unclear.  
- Request: include theoretical scaling arguments for data length and number of hidden states to assess tractability on larger datasets/state spaces.  
- Ask for comparison of scalability and tractability versus AFAMAP for larger problems.  
- Paper is well written and organized with a clear introduction and literature review.  
- Minor typographical/notation issue on page 4 (line 202) in the unnumbered equation before (6).  
- Work appears novel and original, though reviewer is not an expert to fully judge significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, strong real-world performance, comparable efficiency, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: convexity proof needs rigor, scaling analysis lacking, toy-data/SNR clarifications needed.
