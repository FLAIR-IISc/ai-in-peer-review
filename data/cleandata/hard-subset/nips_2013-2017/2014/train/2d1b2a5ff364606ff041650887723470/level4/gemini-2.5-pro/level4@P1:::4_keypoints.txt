- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into AFHMM-based blind source separation by encouraging each hidden source's total sum to match known aggregates.  
- Formulates MAP inference as an integer quadratic program and solves a convex relaxation via a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Evaluated on two real-world UK energy disaggregation datasets, showing ~45% error reduction vs. baseline AFHMM and ~29% improvement over AFAMAP.  
- Practical significance: addresses identifiability in blind source separation with a well-motivated, real-world application.  
- Strong empirical results on large, realistic datasets demonstrating substantial improvements.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clearly explained motivation, model, and inference.  
- Suggest clarifying whether SACs improve integrality of relaxed solutions (like AFAMAP) or reduce error even with non-integral solutions, to explain the mechanism of improvement.  
- Suggest exploring combining SACs with AFAMAP’s "at most one chain change" constraint, since the constraints appear orthogonal and may yield further gains.  
- Suggest more precise terminology: clarify comparisons are between inference algorithms/relaxations (e.g., unconstrained CQP for AFHMM vs. AFAMAP vs. AFHMM+SAC), not distinct models.
