- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: an additive factorial HMM extension with signal aggregate constraints and a convex relaxation for MAP inference applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Approach is conceptually straightforward with a simple optimization formulation but compelling due to applied + algorithmic combination.  
- Strong motivation and clear, thorough introduction to blind source separation and need for domain knowledge.  
- AFHMM+SAC model and optimization are well-explained and clearly presented.  
- Experimental results on toy and real-world datasets are impressive, showing improvements over original AFHMM.  
- Comparison with AFAMAP is informative and highlights benefits of incorporating SACs.  
- Paper is technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Writing and presentation are clear; experiments and comparisons are well-presented.  
- Originality: novel use of SACs in AFHMM and novel application to real-world energy disaggregation.  
- Significance: promising practical improvements and potential for notable contribution to the field.  
- Suggestion: discuss relationship between SACAFHMM formulation and AFAMAP, including whether it yields integer solutions more often.  
- Suggestion: consider combining SAC constraints with AFAMAP’s optimization as a future direction.  
- Suggestion: be precise in terminology—distinguish model vs optimization (e.g., AFHMM vs alternating Viterbi).  
- Against acceptance: optimization is relatively simple; could explore more complex formulations and deeper connection/integration with AFAMAP.
