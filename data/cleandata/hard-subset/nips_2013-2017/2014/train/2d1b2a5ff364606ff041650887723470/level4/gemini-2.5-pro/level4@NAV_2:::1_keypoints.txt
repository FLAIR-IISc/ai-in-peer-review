- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to encode prior aggregate knowledge and tackle identifiability in blind source separation.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) motivated by known/estimable total source aggregates.  
- Applies method to energy disaggregation, using expected daily appliance consumption to separate signatures from mains.  
- Formulates MAP inference as a penalized objective and derives an efficient approximate inference via a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation.  
- Validates the approach on synthetic and real-world energy datasets with strong empirical performance.  
- Demonstrates substantial, statistically significant gains over standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with comparable runtime to existing methods.  
- Novel and general-purpose technique likely applicable to other domains with aggregate information.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and adequately positions related work.  
- Suggest adding a formal proof/justification that the relaxed objective is convex.  
- Recommend including a theoretical complexity analysis (time/space) in terms of time steps, source chains, and states.  
- Request clarification on experimental SNR choices (toy and real data) and proportion of mains signal explained by modeled appliances.  
- Note a minor notational inconsistency in transition probability notation near Equation (6).  
- Overall assessment: excellent, novel, effective work with strong empirical support; strongly recommend acceptance.
