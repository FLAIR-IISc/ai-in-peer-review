

The paper introduces a constrained extension to the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM), enforcing that individual chain outputs sum to a known aggregate value. The authors propose a convex relaxation for MAP inference and evaluate the approach on energy disaggregation tasks using two real-world datasets: 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings and 10 homes with higher-frequency readings (where individual appliance monitoring is absent). While the core ideas are straightforward and rely on a relatively simple optimization framework, the work remains compelling due to its successful application to large-scale real-world problems. The integration of practical algorithmic contributions with empirical validation makes this a strong applied contribution. To further strengthen the paper, the following points are recommended:  

First, the motivation for AFAMAP’s sequential state constraint (to improve integer solution recovery) should be explicitly linked to SACAFHMM’s behavior. Specifically, does SACAFHMM achieve similar integer solution benefits, or do its non-integer solutions still yield lower error? Clarifying this distinction would enhance the theoretical justification and distinguish the two methods more precisely.  

Second, the AFAMAP and SACAFHMM approaches appear fundamentally orthogonal—SACAFHMM constraints could readily be incorporated into AFAMAP’s optimization framework. While this is not required for the current revision, the authors should consider this combination for a final version, as it may yield improved performance.  

Third, the paper conflates the model (AFHMM) with its optimization procedure (AFAMAP). For instance, comparing "AFHMM" and "AFAMAP" results is misleading: AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP solutions to AFHMM (which itself relies on alternating Viterbi optimization), not a distinct model. The authors should consistently distinguish between the model (AFHMM) and the algorithm (AFAMAP) to avoid confusion. This clarification would also underscore the potential for combining the two strategies, as noted above.  

The paper’s empirical results on real-world energy disaggregation data are robust and well-motivated, providing a convincing case for the method’s utility. The core extension to AFHMM is modest but effectively addresses a practical constraint in large-scale applications. The authors’ focus on real-world validation, rather than purely theoretical novelty, aligns well with the conference’s applied focus. Minor revisions to clarify the model-algorithm distinction and strengthen the theoretical discussion of integer solution behavior would significantly elevate the paper’s impact. Overall, the work is technically sound, well-executed, and appropriately scoped for NIPS.
