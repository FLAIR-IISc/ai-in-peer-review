

This paper introduces a novel approach to blind source separation by modeling the problem as an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs). The core innovation lies in incorporating domain-specific aggregate information—exemplified through energy disaggregation—to enhance the separation of time-series components (e.g., individual appliance usage from household energy readings). The method maximizes the log-posterior of hidden states, transforming the objective into a convex optimization problem. Empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world energy data demonstrates significant performance gains over baseline AFHMM and AFAMAP (a recent SAC-agnostic alternative), with statistical significance confirmed via t-tests and comparable computational efficiency.

The paper’s technical contributions are well-executed. The simulation framework is robust, and the results convincingly show that SACs enable superior separation, particularly in the energy disaggregation application where aggregate appliance usage constraints are naturally available. The real-data results are compelling, highlighting the method’s practical relevance. However, a minor concern arises regarding the toy example: the exceptionally low noise variance (σ² = 0.01) appears deliberately chosen to accentuate separation performance (Figure 2), potentially making the problem seem artificially simple. The authors should clarify whether this noise level reflects typical real-world signal-to-noise ratios (SNR) in energy data or is merely a pedagogical choice. If the latter, they should explicitly justify the SNR selection and discuss its implications for real-world applicability. A brief comparison of SNR between mains readings and appliance aggregates in the energy application would further strengthen the case.

The theoretical exposition is generally clear but would benefit from two refinements. First, while Equation (6) is presented as convex, the argument lacks a formal proof. The paper states convexity "follows from the structure," but a concise derivation or reference to convexity-preserving operations (e.g., composition of affine maps and log-concave functions) would bolster rigor. Second, the authors omit theoretical scaling analysis with respect to data length and hidden state count. Given the computational claims, it would be valuable to discuss tractability for larger datasets (e.g., scaling in O(n) vs. O(n²) for n observations) and how SACs compare to AFAMAP in this regard. Such analysis would complement the empirical results and provide guidance for broader deployment.

The writing is clear and well-structured, with a strong introduction that effectively motivates the problem and contextualizes the work within relevant literature. A minor notational inconsistency should be addressed: on page 4, line 202, the unnumbered equation preceding (6) uses subscripts like `p_{i,j,k}` with commas (e.g., `p_{i,j,k}`), whereas prior notation (page 3) omits commas (e.g., `p_{ijk}`). Standardizing to `p_{ijk}` would improve consistency. This is a trivial fix but enhances readability.

Regarding novelty, the integration of SACs into AFHMM for blind source separation appears original within the specified domain. While the reviewer lacks deep expertise in HMMs or energy disaggregation, the method’s distinct use of aggregate constraints—unlike AFAMAP or standard AFHMM—suggests meaningful contribution to both fields. The energy disaggregation application is particularly timely, and the method’s demonstrated efficacy there adds practical weight to the theoretical advances.

In summary, this is a strong paper with clear technical merits and real-world relevance. The proposed SAC framework effectively leverages domain knowledge to improve separation accuracy without sacrificing computational efficiency. Addressing the minor points above—specifically clarifying the toy example’s noise variance, providing a convexity proof for Equation (6), and adding scaling analysis—would elevate the work to a high standard. The authors’ responsive feedback on these points during revision is commendable. With these refinements, the paper will make a valuable contribution to the blind source separation and energy analytics communities. I recommend acceptance.
