

The paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for additive factorial hidden Markov models (AFHMMs) to address identifiability challenges in blind source separation problems. The authors develop a convex quadratic programming approach for approximate inference under these constraints, demonstrating significant performance improvements on both synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation datasets.

Strengths:
- The paper is well-written with a strong mathematical foundation.
- The problem motivation is compelling and well-justified through the energy disaggregation application.
- The evaluation shows substantial improvements in both normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE) metrics compared to baseline methods.

Weaknesses:
- The comparison against AFHMM and AFAMAP appears potentially unfair, as these baselines do not incorporate SACs and thus use less information, raising questions about the computational efficiency of other approaches that might also utilize SACs.
- Figure 1's results are ambiguous; it appears that for constraint distances exceeding 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms the SAC-enhanced AFHMM. This contradicts the paper's claim that SACs consistently improve performance, suggesting that poorly specified constraints could substantially degrade results.
- The paper focuses exclusively on aggregate constraints, but the authors could have explored more complex domain-specific constraints (e.g., mutually exclusive appliance usage, limits on the number of active devices, or temporal constraints on appliance operation). The potential for extending the framework to accommodate such constraints remains unexplored, representing a significant opportunity for future work.
