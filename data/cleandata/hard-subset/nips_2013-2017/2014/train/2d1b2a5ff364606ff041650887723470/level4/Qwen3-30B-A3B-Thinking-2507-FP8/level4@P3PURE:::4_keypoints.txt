- Introduces an AFHMM extension that enforces the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate and uses a convex relaxation for MAP inference, evaluated on 100 low-frequency and 10 higher-frequency UK homes.  
- Core idea is straightforward (simple optimization) but compelling due to large-scale real-world application and practical relevance.  
- Clarify whether SACAFHMM promotes integer solutions like AFAMAP or achieves lower error despite producing non-integral solutions.  
- Note that AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are fundamentally orthogonal and SACAFHMM constraints could be integrated into AFAMAP; authors should explore this combination.  
- Distinguish clearly between the AFHMM model and optimization procedures (e.g., AFAMAP is an algorithm for MAP inference, not a separate model); comparisons should be precise.  
- Conclusion: a modest methodological extension but justified by strong empirical results on real-world datasets.
