- Introduces AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve blind source separation.  
- Applies method to energy disaggregation by incorporating expected per-appliance total usage.  
- Reformulates log-posterior maximization of hidden chains into a convex optimization problem.  
- Validated on a toy dataset and real domestic energy consumption data.  
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP; improvements shown to be statistically significant via t-tests.  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Reviewer questions choice of low noise variance in the toy example and asks for clarification or evidence that such SNRs reflect real-world data; suggests reporting SNR between mains and appliance outputs.  
- Requests a formal proof that the final procedure (equation (6)) is convex, as current argument is not fully convincing.  
- Asks for theoretical scaling arguments on data length and number of hidden states and discussion of tractability for larger datasets relative to AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; introduction motivates the problem and summarizes literature adequately.  
- Notes a potential minor notation inconsistency on page 4 (comma in subscripts before equation (6)).  
- Work appears novel and original, though reviewer is not an expert enough to fully assess broader significance.  
- Authors addressed reviewer questions during feedback; reviewer encourages including those clarifications in the revised paper.
