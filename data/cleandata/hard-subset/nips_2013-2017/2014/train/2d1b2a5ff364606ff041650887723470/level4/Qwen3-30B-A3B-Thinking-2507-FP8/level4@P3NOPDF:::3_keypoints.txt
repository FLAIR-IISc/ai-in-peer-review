- Introduces AFHMM-SAC: a novel convex-quadratic relaxation for AFHMM that enforces signal aggregation constraints for blind source separation.  
- Strong mathematical foundation and clear motivation; writing is precise and accessible for the NIPS audience.  
- Empirical results show substantial improvements (Normalized Distance Error, Source Activity Error) on synthetic and real-world sensor data.  
- Major concern: baseline comparisons are unfair because SACAFHMM uses more training data than competing methods, potentially inflating gains.  
- Recommend comparing against baselines that also incorporate SAC (or matching data volumes) to isolate the effect of constraint handling vs. data size.  
- Figure 1 is ambiguous: SACAFHMM appears to underperform AFAMAP at high signal distances (>10^4), contradicting claims of consistent improvement.  
- Authors should analyze whether SAC degrades under specific conditions (e.g., high noise, sparsity) or if the figure is misinterpreted.  
- Limitation: framework focuses on linear aggregation constraints and does not address richer real-world constraints (disjunctive, cardinality, temporal).  
- Need discussion (or methods) for extending the convex relaxation to non-linear, combinatorial, or temporal constraints (e.g., Lagrangian duality, piecewise relaxations).  
- Revision recommendations: re-run controlled baseline experiments, reconcile Figure 1 discrepancy, and explicitly outline how to support diverse constraint types.  
- Conclusion: AFHMM-SAC is a promising theoretical and empirical advance but currently risks overstating SAC benefits; addressing methodological and applicability gaps would substantially strengthen the paper.
