- Proposes extension to additive factorial HMM by adding constraints forcing sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate value.
- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference and applies it to energy disaggregation on two real-world datasets (100 low-frequency UK homes; 9 high-frequency monitored homes).
- Method is straightforward but compelling, combining simple optimization with large-scale real data.
- Empirical results show significant performance improvements and computational efficiency from the convex relaxation.
- Motivation of AFAMAP is to enforce single-chain state changes to improve relaxation and encourage integral solutions.
- Question whether the SAC-AFHMM formulation also enforces single-chain changes or achieves lower error despite non-integral solutions.
- AFAMAP and SAC-AFHMM appear orthogonal; combining their constraints could be beneficial and is encouraged for future work.
- Authors sometimes conflate models and optimization methods (e.g., using “AFHMM” to mean alternating Viterbi optimization vs. AFAMAP algorithm).
- Clarifying terminology and distinctions between model and optimization procedure would strengthen the paper.
- Theoretical novelty is limited, as the work builds on existing AFHMM literature.
- Paper is clearly written, well-presented, and practically valuable.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution worthy of acceptance, with suggested clarifications and exploration of method combinations.
