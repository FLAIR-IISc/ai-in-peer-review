- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation/energy disaggregation by constraining each source’s total signal.  
- Incorporates SACs into AFHMMs and converts the constrained MAP problem into an efficient convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate inference.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real Household Electricity Survey data, showing significant improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with a clear, precise derivation of the CQP formulation.  
- Robust experimental design validating robustness to constraint specification and providing detailed implementation choices.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements in NDE and SAE, with statistically significant gains (e.g., 33% and 16% vs AFAMAP; 45% NDE reduction vs AFHMM; 29% improvement over AFAMAP).  
- Exceptionally clear writing, well-organized presentation, and effective figures (though one figure’s interpretation needs clarification).  
- SACs are a novel, practically relevant constraint class distinct from prior work and well situated in the literature.  
- Significant practical impact with minimal computational overhead and potential applicability to other BSS domains.  
- Strengths: compelling motivation, elegant CQP solution, and strong empirical validation on meaningful metrics.  
- Improvement 1: strengthen comparisons by including other recent methods and clarify fairness given AFAMAP uses fewer constraints.  
- Improvement 2: clarify Figure 1’s case where AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM for large constraint distances and report statistical significance/failure analysis.  
- Improvement 3: discuss how SACs generalize to more complex constraint types (e.g., mutually exclusive devices, duration limits) and the computational implications.  
- Overall: high-quality paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing comparison fairness, figure interpretation, and extension discussion.
