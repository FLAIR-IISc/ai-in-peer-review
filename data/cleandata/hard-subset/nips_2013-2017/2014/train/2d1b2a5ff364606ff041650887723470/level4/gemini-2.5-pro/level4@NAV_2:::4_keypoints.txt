- Introduces AFHMM+SAC: an AFHMM extension that regularizes each latent source by encouraging its total sum to match a known expected value.  
- Performs inference via a convex relaxation of MAP formulated as a convex quadratic program.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on real-world energy disaggregation using two large UK household electricity datasets.  
- Shows significant empirical improvements over standard AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP.  
- Highlights that a simple aggregate-domain constraint can yield substantial gains on an underdetermined problem.  
- Critique: the paper sometimes conflates the probabilistic model with the inference algorithm—clarify what “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP” comparisons represent.  
- Suggestion: analyze whether SACs tighten the convex relaxation (yield more integral solutions) or merely steer solutions toward better regions.  
- Observation: SACs appear orthogonal to AFAMAP’s structural constraints; combining both could be a promising future direction.  
- Overall: solid, practically significant contribution that would benefit from clearer exposition and deeper analysis of the relaxation effects.
