- Proposes adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to additive factorial HMMs to enforce that chain outputs sum close to a known aggregate.  
- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference to incorporate SACs.  
- Applies method (AFHMM+SAC) to toy data and real energy-disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency, 9 homes higher-frequency).  
- Reports substantial empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. AFHMM and 29% improvement vs. AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the method and experiments, aided by figures and tables.  
- Thorough discussion of related work and prior approaches to constraining HMM states.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and experiments supporting claims.  
- Demonstrates robustness and effectiveness across toy and real-world datasets.  
- Original contribution: introducing SACs for blind source separation/energy disaggregation.  
- Significant potential impact on energy disaggregation and related fields.  
- Needs clearer discussion of the relationship between AFAMAP and SACAFHMM and exploration of combining the approaches.  
- Sometimes conflates models and optimization procedures; should clarify AFHMM as a model vs. AFAMAP as an approximate MAP solver.  
- Could benefit from additional experiments or comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Authors already discuss limitations and potential future work, but might expand on combining constraints with alternative optimization formulations.
