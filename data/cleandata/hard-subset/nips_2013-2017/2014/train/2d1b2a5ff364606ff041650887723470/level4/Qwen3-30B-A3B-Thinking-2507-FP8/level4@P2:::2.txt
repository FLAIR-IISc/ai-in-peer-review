

This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to constrain the total signal for each source to be close to specified values. The method transforms the constrained optimization problem into a convex quadratic program for efficient inference. The authors demonstrate significant improvements over the baseline AFHMM (45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error) and a recent state-of-the-art approach (29% improvement) on real-world energy disaggregation data.

The paper is technically sound and addresses an important practical problem in energy disaggregation. The authors make a strong case for SACs as a domain-specific constraint that can improve identifiability in blind source separation. The theoretical development of SACs as a prior distribution over chain states is well-motivated and connects to the posterior regularization framework (Section 6), which strengthens the methodological contribution. The empirical results are compelling, showing consistent improvements across multiple evaluation metrics and datasets.

The paper's main strengths lie in its practical significance and methodological contribution. Energy disaggregation is an increasingly important problem with real-world impact, and the authors provide a novel constraint that directly addresses the identifiability challenge in this domain. The SAC approach is generalizable beyond energy disaggregation to any blind source separation problem where domain knowledge about total signal magnitudes is available. The demonstration of how SACs can be incorporated into existing models like AFHMM represents a meaningful advancement.

However, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues that hinder its readability and impact. The excessive use of acronyms (e.g., AFHMM, SAC, NTC, TTC, NDE, SAE) without sufficient explanation creates unnecessary barriers for readers. Some acronyms are unnecessarily long (e.g., "Signal Aggregate Constraint" abbreviated as SAC, which is reasonable, but "Normalized Disaggregation Error" as NDE is problematic). The paper would benefit from defining all acronyms upon first use and minimizing their use throughout the text.

The theoretical development in Sections 4 and 5 is particularly difficult to follow due to complex notation and insufficient explanations of key concepts. For instance, the transformation to the convex quadratic program in Section 5 is presented without sufficient motivation for the additional variables used. The connection between the constraint formulation and the posterior regularization framework (Section 6) is valuable but could be presented more clearly.

The paper's originality is strong, as SACs represent a novel constraint type that differs from previous approaches like posterior regularization or state constraints (Section 2). The authors clearly articulate how SACs differ from existing methods and provide evidence of their effectiveness. The approach is sufficiently distinct from recent NIPS papers on energy disaggregation (e.g., [12, 22]), making it a significant contribution to the field.

The significance of the work is high due to the practical importance of energy disaggregation and the demonstrated improvements over state-of-the-art methods. The 45% error reduction compared to the baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement over [12] are substantial gains that would be valuable for practitioners. The paper also demonstrates how domain knowledge (e.g., average appliance energy consumption) can be effectively incorporated into probabilistic models, which has broader implications beyond energy disaggregation.

To improve the paper, I recommend the following specific changes: 1) Reduce acronyms and define all terms upon first use; 2) Simplify the notation in Sections 4 and 5, providing more intuitive explanations of the mathematical transformations; 3) Clarify the connection between the prior knowledge and the SAC formulation in Section 4; 4) Add a brief discussion of potential limitations and future directions for SACs.

Overall, this is a strong paper with significant contributions to blind source separation and energy disaggregation. While the technical content is sound and the results are compelling, the clarity issues currently prevent the paper from reaching its full potential. With improved presentation and explanation, this work would make an excellent contribution to NIPS.

Pro: Novel constraint type addressing a real identifiability problem, significant empirical improvements, clear connection to posterior regularization framework, strong practical significance.
Con: Excessive acronyms, complex notation, unclear explanations of key mathematical transformations, difficult to follow for non-experts.
