This paper introduces the Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC-AFHMM) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors formulate the inference for a relaxed version of this model as a convex quadratic problem. The proposed technique demonstrates promising results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper is well-written and builds upon a strong mathematical foundation.
*   The problem is well-motivated.
*   The experimental results show substantial improvements in performance metrics, specifically Normalized Decomposition Error (NDE) and Source Aggregate Error (SAE).

**Areas for Improvement:**
*   The primary comparison is against AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP. This comparison seems potentially unfair, as both baseline methods utilize less information (i.e., they do not incorporate the signal aggregate constraints). A more compelling evaluation would include comparisons against alternative approaches that can also leverage SAC, even if they are less computationally efficient. This would help to isolate the benefits of the proposed optimization framework from the benefits of simply using additional data.

*   The results presented in Figure 1 are not entirely clear. It appears that for distances greater than 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms SAC-AFHMM. This raises the question of whether noisy or inaccurate constraints can degrade performance. The discussion in the paper suggests that the proposed method consistently yields better results, which seems misleading in light of this observation.

*   The model could be strengthened by considering a richer set of constraints beyond simple signal aggregates. For example, many real-world scenarios involve mutual exclusion (e.g., a heater and a cooler cannot be active simultaneously), cardinality constraints (e.g., no more than 3 out of 10 lights can be on at once), or temporal constraints (e.g., an appliance has a maximum run time). A discussion on whether the proposed framework can be extended to incorporate these more complex constraint types would significantly enhance the paper's contribution.
