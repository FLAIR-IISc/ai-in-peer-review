This paper proposes a novel method for blind source separation (BSS) by incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM). The authors demonstrate the efficacy of this approach using both a toy data set and real-world data from domestic energy output readings, showcasing significant improvements over existing methods.

The paper is technically sound, with a strong simulation section that effectively evaluates the proposed method. The results demonstrate that the AFHMM with SACs (AFHMM+SAC) outperforms both the regular AFHMM and the recently developed AFAMAP method, which does not incorporate SACs. The improvements are statistically significant, as confirmed by t-tests. Furthermore, the authors show that the optimization speed of AFHMM+SAC is comparable to that of AFAMAP.

The paper's theoretical aspects are well-presented and structured. However, a formal proof that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex would strengthen the paper. Additionally, providing theoretical scaling arguments with respect to data length and number of hidden states would complement the simulation findings and offer insights into the method's tractability for larger data sets and state spaces.

The paper is well-written and structured, with a clear introduction that motivates the problem and provides a concise overview of relevant literature. The authors effectively explain the proposed method and its application to energy disaggregation. However, a minor correction is needed on page 4 (line 202), where the subscripts in the unnumbered equation before (6) should be consistent with the earlier notation on page 3.

The work appears to be novel and original, and the results are significant, particularly in the context of the energy application studied. However, as a non-expert, I cannot definitively comment on the significance of these results to the HMM community.

To further improve the paper, the authors could address the following points:

* Clarify the choice of low noise variance in the toy example and discuss the typical signal-to-noise ratios in real data.
* Comment on the signal-to-noise ratio between the mains readings and the aggregate appliance output in the energy application.
* Provide a formal proof of convexity for the final procedure in equation (6).
* Offer theoretical scaling arguments with respect to data length and number of hidden states.
* Correct the minor notation error on page 4 (line 202).

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach to BSS using AFHMMs with SACs, and with some minor revisions, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Strengths:

* Novel and effective approach to BSS using AFHMMs with SACs
* Strong simulation section with significant improvements over existing methods
* Well-written and structured paper with clear explanations

Weaknesses:

* Minor notation error on page 4 (line 202)
* Lack of formal proof of convexity for the final procedure in equation (6)
* Limited discussion on theoretical scaling arguments and tractability for larger data sets and state spaces

Acceptance:

Based on the strengths of the paper, I would recommend acceptance. However, I suggest that the authors address the minor issues mentioned above to further improve the paper's clarity and robustness.
