- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation, focused on energy disaggregation.  
- Extends the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) by enforcing that each source’s total signal approximates a domain-specified value.  
- Formulates a convex quadratic program as an approximate MAP inference method for the constrained model.  
- Shows strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% improvement vs AFAMAP on real-world data.  
- Derives SACs from the posterior regularization framework and presents a convex relaxation enabling efficient inference.  
- Identifies SACs as a practically useful and computationally tractable class of constraints for problems with widely varying source totals.  
- Builds on prior domain-knowledge frameworks but fills the gap of specifying which constraints are most useful in practice.  
- Strength: clear identification of a practical constraint that addresses a real BSS problem.  
- Strength: careful theoretical derivation linking SACs to posterior regularization.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: significant performance improvements over existing methods.  
- Weakness: unclear distinction between AFHMM as a model and AFAMAP as an inference algorithm—comparison should be clarified.  
- Weakness: paper suggests combining SACs with AFAMAP but does not explore this natural extension.  
- Weakness: unclear whether improvements come from SACs guiding optimization away from local optima or from the convex relaxation itself—needs clarification.  
- Well-written, technically clear, significant for energy disaggregation, generalizable to other BSS problems, and recommended for acceptance.
