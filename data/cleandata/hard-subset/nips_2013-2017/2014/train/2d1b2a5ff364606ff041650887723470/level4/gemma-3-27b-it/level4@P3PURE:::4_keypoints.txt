- Proposes extending additive factorial HMM by adding constraints that force sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate.  
- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference and applies it to energy disaggregation.  
- Evaluated on real-world datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency, no appliance monitoring).  
- Contributions are straightforward but compelling, using a simple optimization formulation.  
- Strong applied and algorithmic contribution through scaling to relatively large, real-world data.  
- Question: Does SACAFHMM’s formulation improve integrality like AFAMAP’s single-chain-stage constraint, or does it yield lower error despite non-integral solutions?  
- Suggestion: SACAFHMM constraints appear orthogonal to AFAMAP and could be incorporated into AFAMAP—worth investigating.  
- Criticism: Authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures (e.g., AFHMM vs AFAMAP); comparisons should be clarified (possible use of alternating Viterbi).  
- Overall: Simple extension and optimization with compelling real-world results; paper is reasonable but would benefit from the clarifications and combinations suggested.
