- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation, applied within an AFHMM for energy disaggregation.  
- Handles intractable MAP inference by relaxing SACs to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient approximate inference.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real energy datasets show significant improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP, with an oracle experiment illustrating potential upper bound gains.  
- Strength: novel, intuitive, and generalizable idea that addresses source identifiability and is practically relevant for low-frequency energy disaggregation.  
- Strength: technically sound formulation of SACs as a penalty in AFHMM and derivation of an efficient CQP relaxation.  
- Strength: strong, convincing experiments with statistically significant improvements and an informative oracle study.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured, and well-written presentation.  
- Weakness: toy experiment uses very low noise (σ² = 0.01) without justification; needs discussion of robustness to higher noise.  
- Weakness: energy experiments should report typical signal-to-noise ratio and proportion of mains signal explained by modeled appliances vs background load.  
- Weakness: central convexity/concavity claim for the relaxed objective lacks an explicit proof or proof sketch.  
- Weakness: would benefit from theoretical complexity analysis describing scaling with T (time), I (HMM chains), and K (states per chain).  
- Minor: notational inconsistency in transition probability subscripts (commas in p(i)jk) should be corrected.  
- Conclusion: minor issues only; authors’ responses were satisfactory; recommends acceptance.
