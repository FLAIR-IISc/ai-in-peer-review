- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to improve identifiability in blind source separation.
- Proposes a convex quadratic programming approximation for inference under SACs.
- Demonstrates substantial NDE and SAE improvements on synthetic and real energy disaggregation datasets.
- Paper is well-written with a strong mathematical foundation.
- Problem motivation is compelling and well-justified via the energy disaggregation application.
- Comparisons may be unfair because baseline methods (AFHMM, AFAMAP) do not use SACs, raising questions about computational efficiency of alternative SAC-enabled approaches.
- Figure 1 is ambiguous: for large constraint distances (>10^4) AFAMAP appears to outperform SAC-enhanced AFHMM, contradicting claims of consistent improvement and suggesting poorly specified constraints can harm performance.
- Scope is limited to aggregate constraints; paper does not explore richer domain-specific constraints (mutual exclusivity, limits on active devices, temporal constraints), leaving significant extension opportunities.
