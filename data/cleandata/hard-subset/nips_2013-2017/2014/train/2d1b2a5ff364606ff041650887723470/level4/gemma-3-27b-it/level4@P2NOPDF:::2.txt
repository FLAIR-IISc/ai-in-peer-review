This paper introduces an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) for modeling electricity usage, incorporating a signal aggregate constraint. While the technical quality of the work appears sound, the presentation significantly hinders comprehension. The manuscript suffers from excessive and often unwieldy acronyms; several are unnecessarily long (e.g., exceeding 11 letters), creating a substantial cognitive load for the reader. 

The core idea, as I understand it, involves imposing a constraint on the posterior density function. However, the authors currently present this as a constraint directly on the prior density. This is a subtle but important distinction. The paper would benefit from explicitly stating that the imposed constraint is *a priori*, and therefore indirectly constrains the resulting posterior distribution. Clarifying this point would improve the logical flow and transparency of the methodology.

Specifically, repeatedly encountering long and obscure acronyms interrupts the reader’s focus on the model’s underlying principles and practical implications. A more judicious use of full terms, especially upon initial introduction of a concept, would dramatically improve readability. Consider a table defining all acronyms, and a critical review of whether each acronym is truly necessary, or if the full term would be clearer in context.

The AFHMM itself seems a reasonable approach for capturing the complexities of electricity usage, and the inclusion of the signal aggregate constraint is a potentially valuable contribution. However, these contributions are obscured by the difficulties in navigating the manuscript. Addressing the issues of acronym overload and clarifying the constraint's placement (prior vs. posterior) are crucial for making this work accessible and impactful.
