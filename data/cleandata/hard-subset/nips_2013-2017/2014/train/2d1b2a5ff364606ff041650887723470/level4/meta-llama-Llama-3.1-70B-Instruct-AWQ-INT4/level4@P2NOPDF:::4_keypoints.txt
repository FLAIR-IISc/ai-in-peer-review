- Proposes extending AFHMM with constraints forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate and uses a convex relaxation for MAP inference.  
- Applies the method to large real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency, 10 homes higher-frequency).  
- Ideas are straightforward but compelling due to their practical application and simple optimization formulation.  
- Strength: combines applied and algorithmic elements and demonstrates effectiveness on real data.  
- Request: discuss whether the SACAFHMM formulation yields the same “one-stage-active” / more-integral behavior as AFAMAP or still attains lower error despite non-integral solutions.  
- Suggestion: AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal; consider combining SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization in future work.  
- Critique: authors sometimes conflate models and optimization methods (e.g., AFHMM vs AFAMAP); clarify distinctions and likely refer to alternating Viterbi when mentioning AFHMM.  
- Overall: promising simple extension with strong empirical results; addressing the above points would further strengthen the paper.
