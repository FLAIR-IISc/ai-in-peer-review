- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to enforce that each latent source’s total signal matches a target, applied to energy disaggregation.
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM+SAC) and performs inference via a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation with auxiliary variables.
- Shows equivalence of the objective to posterior regularization with quadratic aggregate constraints.
- Demonstrates empirical gains over plain AFHMM and improvements over AFAMAP on synthetic data and the HES dataset.
- Novel practical idea: using aggregate signal constraints for AFHMMs is a new, well-motivated approach for low-frequency energy data.
- Clear and useful modeling intuition: aggregate totals are often known or estimable and help identifiability.
- Solid computational approach: the CQP relaxation is convex and tractable, with sensible auxiliary-variable construction.
- Conceptual clarity via linking to posterior regularization situates the method within established frameworks.
- Manuscript clarity is poor: heavy notation, many acronyms, and limited intuition make it hard to follow.
- Probabilistic interpretation is inconsistent; needs upfront clarification that SACs are implemented as quadratic penalties equivalent to PR, not conventional priors.
- Method’s performance depends on aggregate targets µ0; paper lacks guidance on estimating µ0, sensitivity to bias, and robust selection strategies.
- The relaxation and rounding procedure is under-specified: no details on converting fractional solutions to discrete sequences or impact on accuracy.
- Experimental scope is limited: only 3-state appliances, some experiments use aggregated submetered sums, and scalability to more appliances/states is unexplored.
- Hyperparameter treatment is weak: λi values fixed without tuning or sensitivity analysis; cross-validation strategies are not discussed.
- Reproducibility concerns: missing implementation details (solver settings, convergence criteria, rounding scheme) and code availability.
- Technically sound derivations for the relaxed CQP and PR connection; claims are reasonably supported by experiments.
- Clarity issues lower the paper to below conference standards; needs notation streamlining, fewer acronyms, and a running example.
- Moderately original: constraining factorial HMMs isn’t new, but aggregate constraints plus convex relaxation is practically valuable.
- Significant potential impact for low-rate energy disaggregation and other BSS domains where aggregate quantities are known.
- Suggested improvements: state SACs as quadratic PR penalties early, add a notation table and intuitive explanations, and detail rounding/post-processing.
- Additional suggestions: discuss practical µ0 estimation, provide sensitivity analyses, vary/tune λi, expand scalability experiments, and include timing/complexity.
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general, effective idea with empirical benefits and a convex, theoretically grounded inference method.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor presentation, insufficient treatment of µ0 estimation and relaxation rounding, and limited scalability/hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending significant improvements in clarity and more thorough treatment of practical considerations.
