- Addresses blind source separation for energy disaggregation by incorporating domain knowledge into AFHMM via signal aggregate constraints (SACs).  
- Introduces SACs as a general technique to encode expected total energy consumption of unknown appliances.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Provides theoretical grounding: derives a convex quadratic program for approximate inference and links the method to posterior regularization.  
- Clearly motivates the problem and the need for priors due to identifiability issues in energy disaggregation.  
- Novelty is a meaningful contribution to blind source separation and well-motivated for the energy domain.  
- Paper suffers from clarity and readability problems, with excessive and cumbersome acronyms and dense writing.  
- Limited discussion on how to select or learn constraint values (µi0) and sensitivity to constraint accuracy.  
- Claims of broad generality are under-supported because experiments focus almost exclusively on energy disaggregation.  
- Confusion in framing: authors claim to constrain the posterior but appear to be placing constraints on the prior.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated technique with compelling empirical and theoretical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity/readability and insufficient discussion of constraint selection and generalization.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires significant revisions to clarity and discussion of constraint choice.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on substantial improvements in presentation and discussion.
