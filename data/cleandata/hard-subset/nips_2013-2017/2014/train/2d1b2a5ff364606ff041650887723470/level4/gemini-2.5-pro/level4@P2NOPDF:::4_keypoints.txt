- Proposes SACAFHMM: an AFHMM extension adding sum-to-aggregate constraints so individual chain outputs sum to a known aggregate.  
- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference in SACAFHMM.  
- Evaluated empirically on two real-world energy-disaggregation datasets: low-frequency (100 UK homes) and higher-frequency (10 homes).  
- Strength: well-motivated, intuitive model extension with standard optimization, delivering compelling results on large-scale real data.  
- Suggestion 1: Clarify distinction between probabilistic models (AFHMM) and inference algorithms (AFAMAP, alternating Viterbi) and specify which inference method baselines use.  
- Suggestion 2: Discuss whether the SACAFHMM relaxation encourages integral solutions like AFAMAP’s one-change constraint, and analyze trade-offs between integrality and error.  
- Suggestion 3: Explore combining sum-to-aggregate constraints with AFAMAP’s one-state-change-at-a-time constraints into a single optimization for potential performance gains.  
- Overall: Straightforward but effective extension with convincing results; addressing clarity and algorithmic-comparison points would strengthen the paper.
