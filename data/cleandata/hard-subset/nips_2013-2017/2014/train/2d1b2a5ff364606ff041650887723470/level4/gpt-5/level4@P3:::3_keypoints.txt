- Summary: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs, formulates MAP inference with quadratic penalties on source totals, derives a convex QP relaxation, links to posterior regularization, and shows NDE/SAE improvements on synthetic and HES data (including low-frequency).

- Relation to prior work: Positions against AFAMAP, non-homogeneous FHMMs, semi-Markov and k-segment constraints, and side-information frameworks; novelty is sequence-level aggregate constraints with a tractable convex relaxation.

- Strength: Clear, practical motivation—uses plausible aggregate knowledge (e.g., daily appliance totals) to address identifiability.

- Strength: Conceptually simple and interpretable constraint that is easy for practitioners to state and use.

- Strength: Solid technical development—auxiliary variables, PSD structure, convex relaxation, and explicit posterior-regularization interpretation.

- Strength: Empirical gains on realistic, low-frequency energy data and oracle experiments showing upper-bound benefits.

- Strength: Implementation appears practical with reported runtimes similar to AFHMM/AFAMAP in tested settings.

- Weakness: Fairness of comparisons—AFHMM+SAC uses side information (NTC/TTC) that baselines do not; need baselines that incorporate the same aggregate penalty to isolate SAC value.

- Suggestion: Include ablations/baselines: AFHMM+SAC with chainwise Viterbi (no relaxation), AFAMAP augmented with the same quadratic/hard total penalty, or posterior regularization with AFAMAP.

- Weakness: Sensitivity to misspecified constraints—performance degrades as µ0 deviates; constrained method can underperform unconstrained baselines at large errors.

- Suggestion: Provide sensitivity curves with AFHMM/AFAMAP references, quantify regimes where AFHMM+SAC underperforms, and give guidance for trusting NTC-derived µ0.

- Weakness: Sensitivity to λi—fixing λi=1 is likely suboptimal; need cross-validation or a principled heuristic tied to expected µ0 error.

- Weakness: Inference and scalability details missing—how relaxed solutions are discretized, observed integrality gap, problem sizes (T, I, Ki), solver iterations/tolerances, and complexity comparison to AFAMAP’s DP.

- Suggestion: Report discretization/rounding method, empirical integrality gap, and runtime scaling with problem size; consider specialized QP solvers for larger instances.

- Weakness: Scope of constraints limited in discussion—many practical constraints (mutex, capacity, duration, time windows) may be nonconvex or combinatorial.

- Suggestion: Discuss which additional constraints remain convex (e.g., linear knapsack-type, simple mutex via linear inequalities) and which require relaxations or nonconvex handling.

- Weakness: Clarity and presentation issues—Figure 1 needs clearer labels/metrics; explicitly flag cases where AFAMAP outperforms under large µ0 error; fix typos and reference formatting.

- Evaluation (quality/clarity/originality/significance): Technically sound and well organized; originality moderate (posterior regularization known) but significant and practically promising.

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses identifiability with an interpretable constraint, clean convex formulation with probabilistic view, demonstrated real-data improvements, and likely broader applicability.

- Cons for acceptance: Uncontrolled comparisons for side information, limited sensitivity and lambda analysis, limited rounding/integrality and large-scale solver discussion, minimal exploration of other constraint classes.

- Questions for authors: Provide AFHMM/AFAMAP with same aggregate penalty, describe discretization and integrality gap, show sensitivity to λi with principled settings, give empirical distribution of NTC total errors and regimes of SAC benefit/harm, and state which additional constraint types remain convex vs nonconvex.

- Overall assessment: Well-motivated and technically solid with practical impact; leaning toward acceptance contingent on addressing comparison fairness and sensitivity/analysis concerns.
