This paper introduces an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model with Signal Aggregate Constraints (AFHMM-SAC) as a novel approach to blind source separation. The authors formulate a convex quadratic program for a relaxed version of the AFHMM-SAC model, and demonstrate encouraging performance gains on both simulated and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**

- The paper is clearly written and grounded in a strong mathematical framework.
- The problem motivation is well-articulated and compelling.
- The experimental evaluation demonstrates a notable improvement in performance metrics, specifically Normalized Distance Error (NDE) and Source-to-Artifact Ratio (SAE).

**Areas for Improvement:**

- The comparison is primarily against AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which appear to utilize less data than the proposed SAC-AFHMM. This difference in data usage may create an imbalanced comparison. It would be valuable to consider whether alternative approaches exist that could also leverage SAC, and to assess their computational efficiency relative to the proposed method.
- The results presented in Figure 1 require clarification. The figure suggests that AFAMAP outperforms SAC-AFHMM for distances exceeding 10<sup>4</sup>. This observation raises the question of whether poorly defined constraints can lead to significantly degraded performance, a point that is not adequately addressed in the discussion, which currently implies consistent performance improvements.
- While the current constraints are effectively handled, the paper would benefit from a discussion of more complex constraint types. For example, constraints representing mutually exclusive events (e.g., simultaneous heater and cooler usage) or resource limitations (e.g., a maximum number of active light bulbs, or time limits on appliance usage) could be considered. Exploring the potential for extending the algorithm to accommodate a wider range of constraint types would strengthen the work. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a strong foundation and clear motivation. The authors acknowledge future work involving model extension to more complex constraints, which represents a promising direction for further research.
