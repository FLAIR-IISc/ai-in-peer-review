- Novel approach: formulates blind source separation as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).
- Motivation: leverages domain knowledge (aggregate constraints) to improve separation of time series components.
- Application: demonstrated on energy disaggregation, using expected appliance usage aggregates to improve separation.
- Technical soundness: method is well-founded and the theoretical presentation is clear and structured.
- Empirical performance: strong simulation results showing the proposed method outperforms regular AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Statistical significance: improvements confirmed by t-tests.
- Computation: optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.
- Clarification needed: justification for the low noise variance used in the toy example and typical signal-to-noise ratios in real data.
- Theoretical gap: provide a formal proof of convexity for the final procedure (equation (6)).
- Scalability: request theoretical scaling arguments with respect to data length and number of hidden states, and comparison with AFAMAP.
- Minor issues: possible notation/subscript inconsistency before equation (6) (page 4, line 202).
- Presentation and novelty: paper is well-written, likely novel and original, and author responses during review were satisfactory.
