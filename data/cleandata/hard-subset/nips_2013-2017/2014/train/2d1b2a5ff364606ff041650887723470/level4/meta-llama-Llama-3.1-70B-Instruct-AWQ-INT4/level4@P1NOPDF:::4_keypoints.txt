- Proposes extending AFHMM by adding constraints forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate and uses a convex relaxation for MAP inference.  
- Applies the method to real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).  
- The idea and optimization formulation are straightforward but compelling, with strong empirical application to large datasets.  
- The paper successfully combines applied and algorithmic elements, making it a valuable contribution.  
- Clarify whether SACAFHMM enforces the “one chain active at a time” effect like AFAMAP, or if non-integral solutions still yield lower errors.  
- AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal; SACAFHMM constraints could likely be incorporated into AFAMAP—authors should explore this combination.  
- Authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures; comparisons labeled “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP” are imprecise since AFAMAP is an algorithm for AFHMM MAP inference.  
- Likely the term “AFHMM” in comparisons refers to alternating Viterbi; clarifying terminology would highlight opportunities to combine algorithmic strategies.  
- Overall assessment: a reasonable paper with promising results, but would benefit from clearer distinctions and discussion of integrality and algorithmic combinations.
