- Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with signal-aggregate constraints (SAC) and formulates a convex quadratic program for a relaxed AFHMM with SAC.
- Shows promising results on synthetic and real-world datasets with substantial gains in NDE and SAE.
- Paper is well written and grounded in solid mathematics.
- Problem motivation is clear and compelling.
- Comparisons are mainly to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, which use less information and may make the comparison unfair; stronger baselines that can also exploit SAC are needed.
- Figure 1 is unclear: AFAMAP appears to outperform SAC-AFHMM at large distances (>10^4), implying poorly specified constraints can degrade performance; clarify when constraints help versus hurt.
- The method is expected to support more complex constraint types (mutual exclusivity, cardinality limits, duration constraints); request discussion or extension to these broader constraints in future work.
