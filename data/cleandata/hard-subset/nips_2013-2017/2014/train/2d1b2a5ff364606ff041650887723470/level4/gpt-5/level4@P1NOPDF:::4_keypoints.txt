- Proposal: Extend AFHMM with a constraint forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate, derive a convex MAP relaxation, and apply it to energy disaggregation on two real datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes high-frequency without submetering).

- Strength: Ideas are straightforward and the optimization formulation is simple but compelling; evaluated on relatively large, real-world data and successfully blends applied and algorithmic elements.

- Question: Does SACAFHMM replicate AFAMAP’s effect of tightening relaxation via the “at most one chain changes” constraint and producing integral solutions, or does it yield fractional solutions while still reducing error?

- Suggestion: Since AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal, the authors should try incorporating SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP’s optimization for the final version.

- Critique: The paper sometimes conflates models and optimization procedures—AFAMAP is an algorithm for MAP inference on AFHMM, whereas the authors seem to use “AFHMM” to mean alternating Viterbi; this needs clarification.

- Opportunity: Combining strategies from the different algorithms could likely outperform the current standalone approaches.

- Overall judgment: A simple, reasonable extension and corresponding optimization for an AFHMM variant with compelling empirical results.
