The paper proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors formulate a convex quadratic problem for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

Pros:
- The paper is well-written, with a strong mathematical foundation.
- The problem motivation is clear and well-articulated.
- The evaluation results indicate a significant improvement in performance metrics, including NDE and SAE.

Areas of improvement:
- The results are presented as an improvement over AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP, both of which use less data than SACAFHMM. This comparison may be unfair, as it does not account for the additional data used by SACAFHMM. It would be beneficial to consider alternative approaches that could utilize SAC but are less efficient computationally.
- The results in Figure 1 are unclear. It appears that for distances greater than 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM. This suggests that poorly chosen constraints can lead to significantly worse performance. However, the discussion implies that the results are consistently better, which may be misleading.
- The reviewer expected to see more complex constraints incorporated into the algorithm. For example, constraints such as "a person cannot use the heater and cooler simultaneously" or "no more than 3 out of 10 bulbs in the house can be active at the same time" could be explored. Additionally, time constraints, such as "grinders can only be used for less than 10 minutes," could be considered. In summary, various types of constraints could be specified. It would be valuable to investigate the possibility of extending the algorithm to accommodate other kinds of constraints.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear motivation for the problem. Future work should focus on extending the model to incorporate more complex constraints, as mentioned above.
