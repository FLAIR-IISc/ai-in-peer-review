- Introduces AFHMM augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation.  
- Motivates method with energy disaggregation: using known appliance total usage to improve decomposition of aggregate mains signal.  
- Reformulates inference (maximizing log-posterior of hidden states) as a convex optimization problem.  
- Demonstrates method on synthetic toy data and real-world domestic energy readings.  
- Technically sound with a strong experimental section.  
- Method outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP, with improvements shown as statistically significant (t-tests).  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Concern: toy example uses a low noise variance—need clarification whether this SNR is representative of real-world data.  
- Request: discuss signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and aggregate appliance output in the real-world experiment.  
- Suggestion: include a formal proof of convexity for the final objective (equation (6)).  
- Suggestion: provide theoretical scaling/complexity analysis w.r.t. data length and number of hidden states to complement empirical timing and compare tractability to AFAMAP.  
- Writing is clear and well-structured; introduction effectively motivates the problem.  
- Minor notational inconsistency noted on page 4 (line 202) regarding commas in subscripts of p.  
- Novel and original approach, though reviewer cannot definitively assess broader significance in the subfield.  
- Authors addressed reviewer questions satisfactorily in feedback and should incorporate those clarifications into the final paper.  
- Overall: method shows excellent performance, especially on real-world energy disaggregation, and compares favorably to state-of-the-art.
