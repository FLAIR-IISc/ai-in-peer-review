- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) to constrain each source’s total signal.  
- Reformulates the constrained inference as a convex quadratic program for efficient optimization.  
- Empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM baseline and 29% improvement vs a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Technically sound and tackles an important practical problem in energy disaggregation.  
- Frames SACs as a prior over chain states and connects the approach to posterior regularization.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements across multiple metrics and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: practical significance and a novel, generalizable constraint that improves identifiability.  
- Strength: shows how SACs can be incorporated into existing models like AFHMM.  
- Weakness: excessive and poorly explained acronyms hinder readability.  
- Weakness: Sections 4–5 use complex notation and lack sufficient motivation for introduced variables.  
- Weakness: the transformation to a convex quadratic program is presented without enough explanation.  
- Weakness: the connection to posterior regularization could be clearer and better motivated.  
- Originality: SACs are distinct from prior constraints and recent related work, representing a novel contribution.  
- Significance: high, given the practical impact and sizable empirical improvements.  
- Recommendation: define and reduce acronyms, especially on first use.  
- Recommendation: simplify notation and provide more intuitive explanations in Sections 4–5.  
- Recommendation: clarify how prior knowledge maps to the SAC formulation.  
- Recommendation: add a brief discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Overall: strong, impactful paper that needs improved clarity to reach its full potential.
