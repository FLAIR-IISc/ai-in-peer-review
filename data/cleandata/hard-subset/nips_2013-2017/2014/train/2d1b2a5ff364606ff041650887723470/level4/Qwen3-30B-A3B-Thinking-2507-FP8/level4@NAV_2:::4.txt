

The paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to enhance additive factorial HMMs (AFHMMs) for blind source separation, particularly in energy disaggregation. SACs enforce that the sum of each source signal over the observation period aligns with domain-specified values (e.g., average appliance energy consumption), addressing identifiability challenges in unobserved mixture problems. The authors formulate a convex quadratic program for approximate MAP inference under SACs, demonstrating significant improvements on real-world energy data from 100 UK households (100 households with low-frequency readings) and 9 homes with mains-level measurements. Compared to the baseline AFHMM, SACs reduce normalized disaggregation error (NDE) by 45% and outperform state-of-the-art AFAMAP by 29% in NDE and 16% in signal aggregate error (SAE) using national consumption estimates as constraints.

**Strengths:**  
- **Quality & Significance:** The method is technically sound, with rigorous validation on large-scale real-world datasets. The 33% NDE improvement over AFAMAP (using national consumption constraints) demonstrates clear practical impact for energy disaggregation, a high-stakes application.  
- **Clarity & Originality:** SACs represent a novel, domain-aware constraint for BSS, distinct from prior work (e.g., posterior regularization). The convex relaxation solution is well-motivated and efficiently implemented.  
- **Experimental Rigor:** Results include statistically significant paired t-tests (p < 0.05) and thorough error metrics (NDE/SAE), with computational efficiency comparable to AFHMM/AFAMAP.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- **Terminology Precision:** The paper conflates model specifications (e.g., "AFHMM" vs. "AFAMAP") and inference procedures. AFAMAP is an *algorithm* for approximate MAP inference in AFHMMs (using alternating Viterbi), not a distinct model. Clarifying this would strengthen the comparison.  
- **Orthogonality of Constraints:** SACs and AFAMAP’s single-chain-state-change constraint are orthogonal; combining them (e.g., SACs + AFAMAP) could yield further gains. The authors should address this possibility in future work.  
- **SAC Robustness Analysis:** The toy data experiments show SACs degrade gracefully with constraint errors, but the real-world robustness (e.g., when national averages deviate from household-specific usage) warrants deeper discussion.  

**Pro/Con Summary:**  
- *Pro:* Novel SAC framework, strong empirical results on large-scale energy data, computationally efficient, significant real-world impact.  
- *Con:* Terminology inconsistencies, missed opportunity to explore SACs combined with AFAMAP, limited discussion of SAC robustness under imperfect domain knowledge.  

The work is a high-quality contribution to BSS, directly addressing identifiability through domain-aware constraints. With minor clarifications on model/algorithm distinctions and exploration of constraint combinations, this paper would be a strong fit for NIPS. The significance of energy disaggregation results—particularly the 33% NDE improvement over AFAMAP—makes it compelling for the community.
