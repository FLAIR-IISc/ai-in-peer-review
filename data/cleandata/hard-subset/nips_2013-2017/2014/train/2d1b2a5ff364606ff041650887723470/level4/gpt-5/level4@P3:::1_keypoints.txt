- Problem and contribution: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for AFHMMs to improve blind source separation from a single aggregate signal, with application to energy disaggregation.  
- Optimization: Formulates MAP inference with transition-indicator variables and relaxes the integer quadratic program to a convex quadratic program (CQP) that is efficiently solvable.  
- Theory connection: Shows SACs arise naturally from a posterior-regularization view by constraining posterior moments.  
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC substantially improves NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP on synthetic and UK HES data, with comparable runtime and statistical significance.  
- Relation to prior work: Complements state-dynamics constraints (e.g., AFAMAP, semi-Markov models) by encoding readily available aggregate information applicable across domains.  
- Soundness: Modeling and relaxation are well motivated; convexity follows from negative semidefinite Hessians and linear S–H coupling, but a concise formal convexity proof would strengthen the paper.  
- Empirics critique: Results are convincing and significant; toy experiment demonstrating robustness to SAC misspecification is useful.  
- Open question on noise: Toy experiment uses very low noise—need justification or performance across a range of SNRs and characterization of real-data SNR.  
- Clarity suggestions: Add a short convexity argument or pointer, fix minor notation/typo, and clarify subscripts in transition probability notation.  
- Scaling: Provide explicit complexity/accounting (variables and constraints in terms of T and ∑i Ki) and compare to AFAMAP’s scaling.  
- Hyperparameters: Explain choice λi = 1, provide sensitivity analysis or cross-validation advice, especially when µ0 is noisy.  
- Originality: Novel and practical integration of aggregate-total constraints into AFHMMs with a tractable relaxation; posterior-regularization framing is a solid theoretical anchor.  
- Significance: Method is practically impactful for energy disaggregation and transferable to other BSS domains, though reliance on general-purpose CQP solvers may limit scalability.  
- Pros: Clear modeling idea, principled derivation, convex relaxation, strong empirical gains on realistic low-frequency data, and comparable runtime to baselines.  
- Cons: Convexity and scalability arguments need strengthening, dependence on CVX may hinder large-scale use, limited SNR and µ0 sensitivity analysis, and a minor notation issue.  
- Suggested improvements: Add convexity proof and complexity accounting, report noise sensitivity and real-data noise characterization, show robustness to imperfect µ0 and discuss λi selection, and report effect sizes or nonparametric tests.  
- Identifiability note: SACs improve identifiability but do not fully resolve source exchangeability—clarify remaining ambiguities.  
- Overall recommendation: Well-executed, novel, and significant paper; recommend acceptance with minor clarifications on convexity, scaling, and noise robustness.
