- Proposes SACAFHMM: an AFHMM extension that enforces sum-to-aggregate constraints and uses a convex relaxation for MAP inference.  
- Applied to two real-world energy-disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes, low-frequency; 10 homes, higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).  
- Core ideas and optimization are straightforward but made compelling by successful large-scale real-world application.  
- Request to clarify whether SACAFHMM encourages integer solutions like AFAMAP’s single-chain-change constraint, or instead achieves lower error despite non-integral solutions.  
- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are fundamentally orthogonal; recommends investigating combining AFAMAP’s constraints with SACAFHMM’s sum-to-aggregate constraints.  
- Criticism that authors sometimes conflate probabilistic models with optimization procedures—e.g., labeling a baseline “AFHMM” may actually be an alternating Viterbi/AFAMAP method; needs clarification.  
- Observation that the different algorithmic strategies are not mutually exclusive and could likely be combined to improve performance.  
- Overall: a simple, effective extension with compelling empirical results; a solid contribution and reasonable conference submission.
