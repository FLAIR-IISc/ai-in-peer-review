

This paper introduces a novel blind source separation method by formulating the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The approach leverages domain knowledge—specifically, expected appliance usage patterns—to enhance energy disaggregation, where components representing individual appliance energy consumption are better separated when total usage constraints are incorporated. The objective function, derived from the log posterior distribution of hidden chains given observations, is transformed into a convex optimization problem. Performance is validated using both synthetic data and real domestic energy readings.  

The paper is technically sound with robust simulations. The proposed method significantly outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP (a recent SAC-free alternative) in separation accuracy, as confirmed by t-tests, while maintaining comparable optimization speed to AFAMAP. A minor concern arises regarding the low noise variance in the toy example: clarification is needed on whether this choice was deliberate to emphasize separation capability in Figure 2 (potentially making the problem overly simplistic) or reflects realistic signal-to-noise ratios in energy data. The authors should also address the signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and appliance aggregates in the energy application.  

The theoretical presentation is clear, though a formal proof of convexity for the procedure in Equation (6) would strengthen the argument. Additionally, theoretical scaling analysis (e.g., with respect to data length and hidden states) would complement the simulations and clarify tractability for larger datasets compared to AFAMAP.  

The paper is well-written and structured, with a strong problem motivation and literature review. A minor notation inconsistency is noted on page 4 (line 202), where subscripts in the unnumbered equation preceding Equation (6) should align with earlier notation on page 3 (removing unnecessary commas).  

The work appears novel within the HMM and energy disaggregation domains, though the reviewer’s limited expertise prevents definitive assessment of its broader significance.  

*Addendum: The authors satisfactorily addressed all concerns in the feedback phase. The paper merits acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the toy example’s noise variance and signal-to-noise context, and to include the requested convexity proof and scaling analysis.*
