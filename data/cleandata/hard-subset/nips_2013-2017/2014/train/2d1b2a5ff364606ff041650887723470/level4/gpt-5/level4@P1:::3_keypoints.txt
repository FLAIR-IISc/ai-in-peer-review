- Paper introduces AFHMM+SAC: additive factorial HMM with signal aggregate constraints to match source energy over time horizons.
- Authors derive a convex quadratic program (CQP) via transition variables yielding a PSD quadratic objective for relaxed MAP inference.
- They show an equivalent interpretation under the posterior regularization framework.
- Experiments (toy + HES low-frequency data) show notable improvements in NDE and SAE versus AFHMM and AFAMAP.
- Relation to prior work is well framed: SACs are complementary to temporal-structure constraints (AFAMAP, semi-Markov, etc.).
- Posterior-regularization and measurement-based learning connections clarify SACs as soft aggregate priors.
- Technical soundness: relaxation produces a convex objective and the posterior-regularization equivalence is correctly derived.
- Clarify handling of fractional CQP solutions: rounding/decoding procedure and its impact on objectives and performance are missing.
- Empirical evaluation: reported gains are meaningful (e.g., ~45% NDE vs AFHMM, ~29–33% vs AFAMAP) and toy experiment shows sensitivity to misspecification.
- Recommend reporting performance as a function of λ and sensitivity to µ0 accuracy (scaling/noise) on real data.
- Computational aspects: CVX solves CQP at shown scales with comparable run times; need problem sizes (T, I, ∑Ki), solver tolerances, and scalability discussion.
- Figure 1 is hard to interpret; axes/scales and regimes should be clarified and behavior discussed when µ0 deviates strongly.
- Minor typos/notation issues (e.g., “equavalent,” “distibution,” overloaded symbols) should be fixed.
- Originality: instantiating aggregate priors for AFHMM and deriving a tractable CQP for BSS/NILM is novel and practical.
- Significance: statistically significant, realistic-data gains indicate practical impact and general applicability to other BSS domains.
- Main concern—fairness: AFHMM+SAC uses extra prior info (NTC/TTC); include baselines that use the same aggregate priors (e.g., AFAMAP+aggregate or AFHMM relaxed with λ=0).
- Main concern—robustness: badly chosen constraints can harm performance; show sensitivity curves on real data, discuss, and consider adaptive λ per appliance.
- Suggest discussing richer constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality, duration, rate-of-change) and whether they fit the convex framework.
- Request guidance on model assumptions/hyperparameters: sensitivity to Ki, θ estimation errors, and how to set λ (possibly per-appliance).
- Pros: clear motivation, solid math, practical convex inference, strong low-frequency results, useful posterior-regularization link.
- Cons: comparisons asymmetric due to extra information, limited robustness/sensitivity analysis, Figure 1 presentation weak, extensions only sketched.
- Overall: technically sound and promising paper; addressing fairness of comparisons, robustness to misspecification, and tractability of richer constraints would strengthen the contribution.
