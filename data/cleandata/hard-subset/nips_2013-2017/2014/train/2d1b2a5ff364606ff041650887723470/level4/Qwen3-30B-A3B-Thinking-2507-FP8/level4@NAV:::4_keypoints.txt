- Introduces an extension to additive factorial HMMs that enforces the sum of chain outputs to approximate a known aggregate.
- Develops a convex relaxation for MAP inference.
- Demonstrates the approach on two real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes, low-frequency; 10 homes, higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).
- Core ideas and optimization are straightforward but compelling due to large-scale, real-world application; the paper successfully bridges applied and algorithmic work.
- Ask whether AFAMAP’s enforcement of sequential state transitions improves relaxation toward integer solutions, and whether SACAFHMM achieves similar improvement or lower error despite non-integral solutions—clarification needed to distinguish methods.
- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are conceptually orthogonal and suggests integrating SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP should be explored.
- Points out authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures (e.g., calling “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP” imprecise); AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP on AFHMM, not a separate model.
- Overall assessment: clear extension and optimization for an AFHMM variant with compelling real-world results; contribution is reasonable.
