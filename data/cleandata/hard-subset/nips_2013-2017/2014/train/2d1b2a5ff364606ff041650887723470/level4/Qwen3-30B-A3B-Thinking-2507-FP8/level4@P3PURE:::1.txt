

This paper introduces a novel method for blind source separation by formulating the problem as an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs). The authors motivate the approach by demonstrating how domain knowledge—specifically, expected total usage of individual appliances over time—improves energy disaggregation, where the goal is to separate domestic energy consumption into appliance-level components. The core objective, maximizing the log posterior distribution of hidden chains given observations, is transformed into a convex optimization problem. The method is validated on both synthetic data and real-world energy disaggregation datasets.

The paper is technically sound, with a strong simulation section. As expected, the proposed method outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP (a recent alternative that lacks SACs), with statistically significant improvements confirmed via t-tests. Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. However, the low noise variance in the toy example (used for Figure 2) warrants clarification: is this choice deliberate to ensure clear signal separation in the illustration (making other methods appear ineffective), or does it reflect realistic signal-to-noise ratios in energy data? The authors should address whether the noise level is typical in practice and comment on the signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and appliance aggregates in the energy application.

The theoretical exposition is generally well-structured. A proof that the procedure in Equation (6) is convex would strengthen the paper, as the provided reasoning is insufficiently clear. Additionally, theoretical scaling analysis (with respect to data length and hidden state count) would complement the simulations. The authors should address tractability for larger datasets and state spaces, and contrast this with AFAMAP's scalability.

The writing is clear and well-organized, with a strong introduction that contextualizes the problem and relevant literature. While I am not a domain expert, the literature review appears comprehensive. A minor notation inconsistency exists on page 4, line 202: the unnumbered equation preceding (6) uses commas in subscripts (e.g., $p_{k,t}$) inconsistent with earlier notation on page 3 (which omits commas; e.g., $p_{k,t}$). Commas should be omitted for consistency.

The work appears novel within the HMM literature and energy disaggregation domain, though I cannot assess its significance to the broader HMM community due to my limited expertise. The authors satisfactorily addressed my queries in the feedback phase, and I encourage incorporating these clarifications in the final version. Overall, the method is well-motivated, performs strongly against state-of-the-art baselines (especially on real energy data), and represents a solid contribution to blind source separation.
