- Proposes AFHMM+SAC: incorporate signal aggregate constraints into additive factorial HMMs for blind source separation.  
- Motivated by using domain knowledge to improve decomposition; primary application is energy disaggregation.  
- Formulates MAP inference as an optimization and relaxes the non-convex objective to a convex quadratic program.  
- Method is demonstrated on both a toy dataset and real-world energy disaggregation data.  
- Empirically, AFHMM+SAC convincingly outperforms standard AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP.  
- Performance improvements are shown to be statistically significant via paired t-tests.  
- Computational cost of AFHMM+SAC is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Concern: toy example uses very low noise variance (σ² = 0.01); authors should justify if this SNR is realistic and report SNR for real-world mains readings.  
- Suggestion: provide a formal proof of convexity for the relaxed problem (eq. 6) and a theoretical runtime/complexity analysis in terms of T and number of hidden states.  
- Minor issue: potential notational inconsistency (log p(i)jk vs p(i)jk) — clarify if typo or new notation.  
- Overall assessment: paper is well-written, literature review adequate, method appears novel and promising for the domain.  
- Addendum: authors satisfactorily addressed reviewer questions; incorporate clarifications in the final version.
