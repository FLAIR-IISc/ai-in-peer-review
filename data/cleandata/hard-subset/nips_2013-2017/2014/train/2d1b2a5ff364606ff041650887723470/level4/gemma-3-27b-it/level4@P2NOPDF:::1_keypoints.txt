- Proposes blind source separation via Additive Factorial HMMs augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).
- Motivated by leveraging domain knowledge of expected aggregate behavior to improve separation.
- Demonstrates approach on energy disaggregation to decompose household meter readings into appliance contributions.
- Transforms the log-posterior objective into a convex optimization problem for efficient solution.
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real domestic energy datasets.
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and recent AFAMAP, with statistically significant gains and comparable computational cost.
- Requests clarification on the low noise variance used in the toy example and whether such SNRs occur in real data; suggests reporting observed SNRs for the application.
- Recommends a formal proof of convexity for equation (6), as current reasoning is not fully conclusive.
- Suggests theoretical scaling analyses (data length, number of states) to assess tractability and compare scalability with AFAMAP.
- Writing is clear and well-organized; introduction and literature overview are effective.
- Notes a potential typographical/notation error on page 4 (commas in subscripts before eq. (6)).
- Reviewer sees the contribution as novel but is not expert enough to judge broader significance; authors’ responses were satisfactory and should be incorporated.
