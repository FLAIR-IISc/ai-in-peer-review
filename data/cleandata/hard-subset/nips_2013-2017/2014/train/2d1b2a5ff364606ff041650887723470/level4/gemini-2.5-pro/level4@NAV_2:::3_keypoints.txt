- Problem & contribution: Tackles identifiability in blind source separation by introducing Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) that enforce each source's total sum to match a specified value and integrating them into AFHMM.  
- Inference method: SACs break Markov structure, so authors relax the MAP problem into a convex quadratic program (CQP) to enable efficient approximate inference.  
- Empirical results: AFHMM+SAC shows significant improvements on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset and includes an oracle experiment using True Total Consumption (TTC).  
- Significance & originality: Proposes a novel, general, and intuitive way to inject domain knowledge via aggregate constraints to address identifiability.  
- Technical quality: Formulation and relaxation into a solvable CQP are rigorous, with a thoughtful connection to posterior regularization.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explanations of model and inference are easy to follow.  
- Empirical evaluation quality: Experiments are thorough and results are compelling, particularly on challenging low-frequency data.  
- Fairness of comparison: Baselines (AFHMM, AFAMAP) do not use aggregate information, making comparisons potentially inequitable; need comparisons/discussion with methods that can incorporate similar priors.  
- Sensitivity to constraints: Performance can degrade when the provided aggregate µ0 is inaccurate; this limitation needs explicit acknowledgement and discussion.  
- Generality of constraints: Work focuses on sum-based constraints only; should discuss extending the CQP framework to other constraint types (mutual exclusivity, cardinality, temporal) to broaden applicability.
