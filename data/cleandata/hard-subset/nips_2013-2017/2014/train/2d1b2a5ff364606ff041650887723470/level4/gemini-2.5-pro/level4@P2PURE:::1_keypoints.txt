- Proposes AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation / energy disaggregation.
- Motivated by using domain-specific aggregate knowledge (e.g., expected total appliance usage) to improve separation.
- Converts the log-posterior objective into a tractable convex optimization problem.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on both synthetic data and real domestic energy readings.
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP with statistically significant gains; optimization speed comparable to AFAMAP.
- Concern about low noise variance in synthetic example — request clarification if this was chosen to exaggerate performance and whether such SNRs are realistic.
- Suggestion to report signal-to-noise ratio between aggregate appliance output and mains in the energy experiments.
- Needs a formal proof that the optimization in equation (6) is convex (current reasoning not immediately obvious).
- Request for theoretical complexity analysis with respect to data length and number of hidden states to assess scalability and compare to AFAMAP.
- Paper is well-written and organized with a clear introduction and literature overview, though reviewer is not an expert in the domain.
- Minor notational inconsistency on page 4 (line 202) in the subscripts of p before equation (6).
- Work appears novel and original, but reviewer cannot fully assess broader significance to the HMM community or energy application.
