Summary
The paper proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation that incorporates signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The key idea is to encode domain knowledge about the total energy consumed by each latent source (e.g., appliance) over a time horizon. These constraints encourage each source’s aggregate signal to be close to a specified target, reflecting prior expectations (e.g., a toaster’s daily consumption). The authors formulate MAP inference with these constraints as a penalized objective and relax it to a convex quadratic program for efficient approximate inference. They also connect the formulation to the posterior regularization framework. Experiments on a real-world energy data set show substantially improved normalized disaggregation error compared to a baseline AFHMM and improvements over a recent state-of-the-art approach.

Strengths
- The core idea—constraining source aggregates to encode strong, readily available prior knowledge—is intuitive and practically motivated for energy disaggregation.
- Casting the constrained MAP problem as a convex quadratic program provides a computationally efficient approximate inference method.
- The empirical results indicate meaningful improvements over a baseline AFHMM and a strong prior method, suggesting that SACs effectively mitigate identifiability issues.

Weaknesses and clarity concerns
- The paper is difficult to read. There are too many acronyms, and some are very long (even up to 11 letters), which interrupts the flow and makes it hard to follow the main ideas. Please reduce the number of acronyms, avoid introducing unnecessary ones, and consider using descriptive names where possible.
- Conceptual framing around “constraints on the posterior” versus “prior constraints” is confusing. It appears that the authors wish to constrain the posterior distribution, but implement this via a penalty that is equivalent to placing an a priori constraint (i.e., a prior or regularizer) that in turn induces a constrained posterior. The manuscript should explicitly state that the constraint is a priori (reflecting prior knowledge about aggregate energy use), and that this prior/regularizer constrains the posterior through Bayes’ rule. Maintaining consistent terminology here would greatly improve clarity.

Quality
- The technical approach appears sound, and the connection to posterior regularization is appropriate. The convex relaxation is a reasonable strategy given the intractability of exact inference in AFHMMs with non-Markovian constraints.

Clarity
- As noted, the readability suffers from excessive and lengthy acronyms and from imprecise language around the nature of the constraints (prior vs posterior). The structure and notation are otherwise standard for AFHMMs, but the presentation would benefit from:
  - Minimizing acronyms and defining each abbreviation once, clearly.
  - Clearly stating early on that aggregate constraints encode prior knowledge and are implemented as a regularization term, which implies a constrained posterior.
  - Keeping terminology consistent with either the penalized MAP or posterior-regularization perspective, and explaining their equivalence succinctly.

Originality
- Using signal aggregate constraints in AFHMMs for energy disaggregation is a natural but useful idea. While it relates to prior work on AFHMMs and posterior regularization, the specific formulation of SACs and the convex program for inference are a valuable combination for this application.

Significance
- The improvements in disaggregation accuracy on real data suggest that SACs could be practically useful for energy disaggregation and potentially other blind source separation settings where total signals are informative.

Pros for acceptance
- Practical and well-motivated use of domain knowledge (aggregate totals).
- Computationally efficient convex relaxation for inference.
- Empirical improvements over strong baselines.

Cons for acceptance
- The manuscript is hard to read due to excessive/long acronyms and imprecise framing of constraints.
- The paper would benefit from a clearer, more concise exposition of the Bayesian and posterior-regularization perspectives to avoid confusion.

Recommendations to improve the manuscript
- Reduce and simplify acronyms; prefer descriptive names where feasible.
- Explicitly state that SACs are a priori constraints (reflecting prior knowledge), implemented via a regularizer that consequently constrains the posterior.
- Streamline the explanation connecting the penalized MAP view and the posterior regularization view; choose one as the primary narrative and mention the other as an equivalent interpretation.
- Provide a brief intuitive example early (e.g., toaster daily kWh) to anchor readers before introducing dense notation.
