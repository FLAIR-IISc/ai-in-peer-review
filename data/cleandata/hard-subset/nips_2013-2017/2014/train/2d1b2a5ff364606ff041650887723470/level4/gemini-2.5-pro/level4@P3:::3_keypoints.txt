- Addresses identifiability in blind source separation and proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate prior knowledge about each source's total value over a period.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM); because global constraints break the Markov property, MAP inference is cast as a penalized optimization and relaxed to a convex quadratic program (CQP).  
- Introduces AFHMM+SAC and shows large empirical gains on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset, reducing normalized disaggregation error by 45% vs AFHMM and 29% vs AFAMAP.  
- Well-motivated and novel idea; the "franken-appliance" example effectively illustrates the need and SACs are generalizable to other BSS domains.  
- Mathematically rigorous: principled incorporation of SACs, a tractable CQP relaxation for inference, and a connection to posterior regularization.  
- Strong empirical evaluation on synthetic and ultra-low-frequency HES data, including oracle and practical experiments demonstrating benefits with realistically obtainable priors.  
- Comparison caveat: AFHMM+SAC uses additional aggregate priors unavailable to baselines; authors should discuss whether and how baselines could also leverage this information (e.g., initialization or post-processing).  
- Sensitivity concern: performance degrades as specified aggregates deviate from truth; need discussion on tolerance to prior inaccuracies and guidance for managing uncertain µi0.  
- Scope limitation: paper focuses only on aggregate constraints; should discuss feasibility of incorporating other realistic constraints (mutual exclusivity, temporal, cardinality) into the CQP framework.  
- Overall: technically solid and impactful contribution with suggestions to better contextualize comparisons and explore broader constraint types.
