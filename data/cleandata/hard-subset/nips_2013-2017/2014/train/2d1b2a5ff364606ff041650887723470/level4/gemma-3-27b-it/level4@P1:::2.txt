An additive factorial hidden Markov model is developed to model electricity usage. The model incorporates a novel constraint, termed the signal aggregate constraint (SAC).

The paper presents a technically sound approach to a challenging problem – blind source separation, specifically in the context of energy disaggregation. The core idea of incorporating domain knowledge through signal aggregate constraints is well-motivated and addresses the inherent identifiability issues in this field. The mathematical formulation is rigorous, and the conversion to a convex quadratic program provides a computationally efficient solution. The experimental results on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset demonstrate a significant improvement over both the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art method, highlighting the practical benefits of the proposed approach. 

However, the paper suffers from clarity issues that hinder readability. The frequent use of acronyms, some quite lengthy (e.g., AFHMM+SAC), makes it difficult to follow the technical details. While acronyms are common in this field, excessive usage obscures the core ideas. The writing style could benefit from simplification and more explicit explanations, particularly when introducing new concepts. For instance, the connection between imposing a constraint on a prior and its effect on the posterior is not clearly articulated and could be strengthened. While the authors acknowledge the identifiability problem, a more in-depth discussion of how the SAC mitigates this issue – beyond simply improving performance metrics – would be beneficial. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of signal aggregate constraints is a fresh approach to incorporating domain knowledge into blind source separation.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation and the derivation of the convex quadratic program are rigorous and well-presented.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The results on a real-world dataset demonstrate a significant improvement in disaggregation accuracy.
*   **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of energy disaggregation and the need for methods that address identifiability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity:** The paper is often difficult to read due to excessive acronyms and a complex writing style.
*   **Explanation of Prior Constraint:** The connection between the prior constraint and the resulting posterior is not fully explained.
*   **Discussion of Identifiability:** A more detailed discussion of how the SAC addresses the identifiability problem would be beneficial.
*   **Limited Discussion of Constraint Selection:** The method for selecting appropriate values for the constraint parameters (δi or λi) is not discussed in detail.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a challenging and important problem in energy disaggregation.
*   The proposed approach is novel and technically sound.
*   The experimental results demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
*   The work has the potential to impact both research and practical applications in the field.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of clarity hinders understanding and limits the potential impact of the work.
*   The paper lacks a deeper discussion of the theoretical implications of the proposed approach.
*   The method for selecting constraint parameters requires further refinement and explanation.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of energy disaggregation and blind source separation. The core idea is novel, the technical approach is sound, and the results are promising. However, the paper would greatly benefit from improved clarity and more in-depth discussion of the underlying principles. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions** focused on improving readability and providing more detailed explanations. Specifically, the authors should consider reducing the number of acronyms, simplifying the writing style, and expanding the discussion of the theoretical implications of the proposed approach.
