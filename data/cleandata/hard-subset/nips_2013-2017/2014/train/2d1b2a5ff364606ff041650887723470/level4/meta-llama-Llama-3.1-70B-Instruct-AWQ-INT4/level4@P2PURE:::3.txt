The paper proposes an additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors formulate a convex quadratic problem for the relaxed AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper has several strengths:
- The writing is clear and well-organized, with a strong mathematical foundation.
- The problem motivation is well-explained and effectively sets the stage for the proposed approach.
- The evaluation results show a notable improvement in performance metrics, including NDE and SAE.

However, there are several areas that require improvement:
- The comparison of the proposed SAC-AFHMM with AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP is not entirely fair, as the latter two methods use less data. It would be beneficial to compare the proposed method with other approaches that utilize SAC but may not be as computationally efficient.
- The results presented in Figure 1 are unclear. It appears that for distances greater than 10^4, AFAMAP outperforms SAC-AFHMM. This suggests that poorly chosen constraints can significantly degrade performance, contradicting the discussion that implies consistent improvement. This discrepancy should be addressed.
- The incorporation of more complex constraints into the algorithm would enhance its applicability. For instance, constraints such as "a person cannot use a heater and cooler simultaneously" or "no more than 3 out of 10 bulbs can be active at the same time" could be explored. Additionally, time constraints, like those for grinders with a maximum usage of 10 minutes, could be integrated. The possibility of extending the algorithm to accommodate various types of constraints should be discussed.

Overall, the paper is well-written and effectively motivates the problem. A potential direction for future work is to extend the model to incorporate more complex constraints, increasing its versatility and real-world applicability.
