- Introduces a novel blind source separation method using Additive Factorial HMMs enhanced with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs).
- Leverages domain knowledge (expected appliance usage) to improve time-series decomposition.
- Targets energy disaggregation to separate household appliance energy consumption.
- Recasts the log-posterior objective as a convex optimization problem for efficient solution.
- Demonstrates performance on synthetic simulations and real-world domestic energy data.
- Empirical results show statistically significant improvements over standard AFHMM and AFAMAP (t-tests).
- Reports computational efficiency comparable to AFAMAP.
- Requests clarification on the toy example’s low noise variance and on realistic mains vs. appliance SNRs.
- Suggests a formal proof of convexity for equation (6) would strengthen the manuscript.
- Recommends adding theoretical scaling analysis relating complexity to data length and number of hidden states.
- Advises discussing scalability to larger datasets/state spaces and comparative performance versus AFAMAP in such settings.
- Notes the paper is well-written with a comprehensive literature review, but flags a minor notation inconsistency on page 4.
- Considers the contribution original but defers broader significance judgment to HMM and energy-domain experts.
- States authors’ responses to initial queries were satisfactory and suggests incorporating those clarifications in revision.
