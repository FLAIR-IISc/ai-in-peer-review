This paper introduces an extension to the Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) by incorporating a sum-to-aggregate constraint, which enforces that the sum of the outputs from the individual HMM chains must align with a known aggregate signal. To perform inference, the authors formulate a convex relaxation for the Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) problem. The efficacy of this approach, termed Sum-to-Aggregate-Constrained AFHMM (SACAFHMM), is demonstrated on two real-world energy disaggregation datasets: one comprising low-frequency readings from 100 UK homes, and another with higher-frequency readings from 10 homes.

The paper's primary strength lies in its successful integration of algorithmic development with a compelling real-world application. While the proposed extension and optimization method are conceptually straightforward, their application to large-scale, practical datasets is commendable and yields impressive results. The work serves as a strong example of an applied machine learning paper that is both methodologically sound and practically relevant.

While the overall work is strong, the following suggestions could help to improve its clarity and impact:

1.  **Clarification on the Nature of the Relaxed Solution:** The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion regarding the properties of the solutions obtained from the proposed convex relaxation. The authors note that a key motivation for the related AFAMAP formulation is that its constraints can lead the relaxation to produce integer-valued solutions more frequently. A crucial point for clarification is whether the proposed SACAFHMM formulation shares this property. Do the sum-to-aggregate constraints also encourage integrality? Or does the method achieve lower error even when yielding non-integral solutions? Expanding on this would help to clarify the mechanisms behind the performance gains and further elucidate the distinctions between the proposed approach and prior work.

2.  **Distinction Between Models and Inference Algorithms:** A notable point of confusion arises from the occasional conflation of probabilistic models with their corresponding inference algorithms. For instance, the paper presents experimental comparisons between "AFAMAP" and "AFHMM." This terminology is imprecise, as AFAMAP is an *algorithm* for approximate MAP inference in the AFHMM *model*. It is presumed that the baseline labeled "AFHMM" refers to the AFHMM model with inference performed via a different algorithm, perhaps an alternating Viterbi-style optimization. Clarifying this distinction throughout the paper is essential for reproducibility and a clear understanding of the contributions.

3.  **Orthogonality of Contributions:** It appears that the core ideas of AFAMAP and the proposed SACAFHMM are orthogonal. Specifically, the sum-to-aggregate constraints introduced in this work could likely be incorporated directly into the AFAMAP optimization formulation. This also relates to the point above, where the algorithmic strategies presented are modular and could potentially be combined. While an experimental evaluation of this hybrid approach may be beyond the scope of the author response period, I would strongly encourage the authors to acknowledge this possibility and consider exploring it for the final version of the paper, as it has the potential to combine the strengths of both methods.

In summary, this paper presents a valuable and well-motivated extension to the AFHMM for problems with known aggregate signals. The empirical results on challenging, real-world energy disaggregation tasks are compelling. By addressing the points of clarification regarding the optimization, the nature of the solutions, and the precise terminology used, the authors can significantly strengthen an already promising paper.
