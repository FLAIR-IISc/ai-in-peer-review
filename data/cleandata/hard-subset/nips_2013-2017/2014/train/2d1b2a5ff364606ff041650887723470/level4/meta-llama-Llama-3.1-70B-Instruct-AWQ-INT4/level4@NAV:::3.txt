The authors introduce an additive factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the challenge of blind source separation. They propose a relaxed AFHMM with SAC, formulated as a convex quadratic problem, which demonstrates promising performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

Strengths:
- The paper is well-structured and grounded in solid mathematical principles.
- The problem motivation is clear and well-articulated.
- The evaluation results show a significant improvement in performance metrics, including NDE and SAE.

Suggestions for improvement:
- The comparison of the proposed method to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP may be biased, as the latter two methods utilize less data. It would be beneficial to explore alternative approaches that incorporate SAC but are less computationally efficient.
- Figure 1 appears to indicate that AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM at distances greater than 10^4, which may suggest that poorly chosen constraints can lead to degraded performance. This contradicts the discussion, which implies consistent improvement. Clarification on this point would be appreciated.
- The algorithm's potential for incorporating more complex constraints, such as mutual exclusivity (e.g., a person cannot use a heater and cooler simultaneously) or time constraints (e.g., a grinder's operation limited to 10 minutes), could be explored. The possibility of extending the algorithm to accommodate various types of constraints would enhance its versatility.
