This paper presents a new blind source separation approach by casting the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and imposing signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The motivation is to leverage domain knowledge to improve decomposition of time series into components. The running example is energy disaggregation: components of household energy use (individual appliances) can be separated more effectively when constraints reflecting total (expected) appliance usage over a time window are incorporated. The objective maximized for separation—the log posterior of the hidden chains given the observations—is reformulated as a convex optimization problem. The method is evaluated on both a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.

The paper is technically sound with a strong empirical section. As expected, the method outperforms a standard AFMM and AFAMAP (a recent alternative), neither of which use SACs. Improvements are statistically significant by t-tests, and the optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. My only question about the results is why such a low noise variance was used in the toy example. Was this chosen to make Figure 2 more illustrative—i.e., to clearly show that the proposed method separates the signal well while others do not? Clarification would help; otherwise, the setup seems overly toy-like and rather easy. Are these signal-to-noise ratios typical in real data? Relatedly, please comment on the signal/noise ratio between the mains readings and the aggregate appliance outputs in the energy application.

The theoretical development is generally clear and well structured. The paper would benefit from a proof that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex, as this is not fully evident from the current reasoning. In addition, theoretical scaling arguments (in data length and number of hidden states) would complement the simulations. Are the methods tractable for much larger datasets and state spaces, and how does this compare with AFAMAP?

The paper is well written and organized. The introduction motivates the problem nicely and reviews relevant literature, though I am not an expert and cannot be certain nothing was missed. I also note a small potential error on page 4 (line 202): in the unnumbered equation before (6), it seems there should be no commas in the subscripts following p, to remain consistent with the notation on page 3 (unless new notation is being introduced).

To my knowledge, the work is novel and original, although I am not in a position to fully assess its significance to the HMM community or the specific energy application.

Addendum: The authors satisfactorily addressed my questions during the feedback phase, and I encourage them to include similar clarifications in the next version. This paper proposes blind source separation using Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints. The method performs very well relative to the state of the art, particularly on real data from an energy disaggregation task.
