This paper introduces an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) incorporating Signal Aggregate Constraints (SAC) as a novel approach to blind source separation. The authors formulate a convex quadratic program for a relaxed version of the AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating encouraging performance gains on both simulated and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**

- The paper is clearly written and demonstrates a solid mathematical foundation.
- The problem motivation is well-articulated and justified.
- Evaluation results demonstrate a notable improvement in performance metrics, specifically Normalized Distance Error (NDE) and Source-to-Artifact Ratio (SAE).

**Areas for Improvement:**

- The comparative evaluation primarily focuses on improvements over AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP. The significantly larger data requirements of the SAC-AFHMM compared to these baselines raise concerns about the fairness of the comparison. It would be valuable to benchmark against alternative approaches that also leverage SAC, even if they are less computationally efficient.
- The visualization of results in Figure 1 lacks clarity. The figure suggests that AFAMAP outperforms SAC-AFHMM when the distance exceeds 10<sup>4</sup>, potentially indicating that poorly defined constraints can lead to degraded performance. The current discussion implies consistent performance improvements, which may be misleading given these observations. A more nuanced discussion of these findings is needed.
- While the presented constraints are effective, the algorithm’s capacity to handle more complex, real-world constraints remains unexplored. Examples such as mutually exclusive device usage (e.g., heater and cooler) or limitations on the number of simultaneously active devices (e.g., 3 out of 10 lights) or time-based constraints (e.g., grinder usage under 10 minutes) could significantly broaden the applicability of the method.  Exploring the potential for extending the algorithm to accommodate such diverse constraint types would strengthen the work.

The paper provides a strong foundation and compelling motivation for the proposed method. The authors acknowledge the potential for future work to expand the model’s capabilities to encompass a wider range of constraints.
