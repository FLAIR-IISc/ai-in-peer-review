- Proposes an AFHMM extension that enforces the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate, with a convex relaxation for MAP inference.
- Applies the method to two real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes higher-frequency without per-appliance monitoring).
- Although conceptually straightforward, the method is compelling and provides a strong algorithmic + applied contribution due to successful large-scale real-data results.
- Request clarification on whether the SACAFHMM formulation produces integral solutions (like AFAMAP’s one-chain-change assumption) or achieves lower error despite non-integrality.
- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas seem orthogonal and suggests investigating combining SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization for the final version.
- Points out the paper sometimes conflates models and optimization procedures (e.g., “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP”) and asks the authors to clarify which algorithms correspond to which model baselines.
- Overall positive: useful extension to AFHMM with compelling empirical results, but needs clarity and exploration of combined methods.
