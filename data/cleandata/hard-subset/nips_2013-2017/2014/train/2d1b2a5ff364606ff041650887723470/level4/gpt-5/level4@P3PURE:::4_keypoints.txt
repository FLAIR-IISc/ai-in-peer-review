- Paper extends AFHMM with constraints forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate and proposes a convex relaxation for MAP inference, applied to two real-world energy-disaggregation datasets.

- The idea and optimization are straightforward but compelling, with successful application to sizable real data and a good blend of applied and algorithmic contributions.

- Clarify the role of integer (integral) solutions: does SACAFHMM produce more integral MAP solutions like AFAMAP’s “one-chain-change” constraint, or remain fractional while lowering error?

- Discuss orthogonality and possible combination: AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal and could likely be combined (e.g., add SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP) to potentially improve performance.

- Be precise about models versus algorithms: avoid conflating AFHMM (the model) with AFAMAP (an MAP-optimization algorithm); clarify what baseline labeled “AFHMM” actually implements (e.g., alternating Viterbi).

- Overall assessment: a simple, reasonable extension with compelling results; addressing the above clarifications would strengthen contribution, reproducibility, and interpretability.
