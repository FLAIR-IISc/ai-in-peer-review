- Introduces a novel blind source separation method framing the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).  
- Leverages domain knowledge of expected appliance usage to improve energy disaggregation by incorporating total-usage constraints.  
- Derives an objective from the log posterior of hidden chains and converts it into a convex optimization problem.  
- Validated method on both synthetic data and real domestic energy readings.  
- Technically sound with robust simulations demonstrating the approach.  
- Significantly outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP in separation accuracy, with t-test confirmation, while matching AFAMAP’s optimization speed.  
- Minor concern: toy example uses low noise variance—authors should clarify if this was deliberate or realistic and discuss SNR between mains readings and appliance aggregates.  
- Suggests adding a formal convexity proof for Equation (6) to strengthen the theoretical claim.  
- Requests theoretical scaling analysis (e.g., with respect to data length and hidden states) to assess tractability on larger datasets.  
- Well-written and structured paper with strong motivation and literature review; minor notation inconsistency on page 4 should be fixed.  
- Appears novel in HMM and energy disaggregation domains; reviewer’s limited expertise prevents full judgment of broader significance.  
- Authors addressed feedback satisfactorily; paper merits acceptance with minor revisions to clarify noise/SNR, include the convexity proof, and add scaling analysis.
