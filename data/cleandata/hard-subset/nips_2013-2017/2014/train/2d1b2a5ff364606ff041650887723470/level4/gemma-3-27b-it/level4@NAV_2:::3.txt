The paper proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to address the blind source separation problem. The authors formulate an efficient convex quadratic program for approximate inference with the relaxed AFHMM+SAC model. The technique demonstrates promising results on both synthetic and real-world data.

This paper is well-written and grounded in strong mathematical foundations. The problem motivation is clearly articulated, and the evaluation results indicate substantial improvements in performance metrics, namely normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).

However, several points warrant consideration. The reported improvements are primarily demonstrated relative to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP. These comparisons might be somewhat imbalanced, as the AFHMM+SAC approach appears to leverage more data during the estimation process. It would be valuable to consider and discuss the existence of alternative approaches that could potentially benefit from SAC but are limited by computational efficiency. 

Furthermore, the results presented in Figure 1 require clarification. The figure suggests that beyond a distance of 10<sup>4</sup>, AFAMAP may outperform AFHMM+SAC. This raises the question of whether poorly specified constraints can lead to significantly degraded performance, a point that isn’t fully addressed in the discussion, which generally implies consistent performance gains.

The paper focuses on relatively simple aggregate constraints. It would be interesting to explore the potential for incorporating more complex, domain-specific constraints. For example, constraints reflecting appliance co-occurrence (e.g., a user would likely not simultaneously operate a heater and air conditioner) or temporal limitations (e.g., a grinder's operation is typically less than 10 minutes) could be considered. Investigating the extensibility of the proposed framework to accommodate such diverse constraints would strengthen the work. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper that provides compelling motivation for the problem and a sound methodology for addressing it. Future work could focus on extending the model to accommodate a wider range of constraints and exploring its applicability to other domains.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of signal aggregate constraints to improve blind source separation within an AFHMM framework.
*   Technically sound formulation and efficient convex relaxation for inference.
*   Demonstrated performance improvements on both simulated and real-world energy disaggregation datasets.
*   Clear writing and well-defined contributions.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Potential imbalance in comparisons with baseline methods regarding data usage.
*   Limited discussion of the sensitivity of the approach to inaccurate constraint specification.
*   Scope for expanding the types of constraints incorporated into the model.
