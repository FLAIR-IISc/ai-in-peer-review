This paper proposes a novel approach to blind source separation by formulating the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The motivation behind this approach is to leverage additional domain knowledge to improve the separation of time series into components. The authors use the example of energy disaggregation, where incorporating information about the total expected usage of each appliance in a time period enhances the separation of domestic energy use components. The objective function, which maximizes the log of the posterior distribution of the hidden chains given the observed data, is transformed into a convex optimization problem. The efficacy of the method is demonstrated using both a toy dataset and real data from domestic energy output readings.

The paper is technically sound, with a strong simulation section. The proposed method outperforms regular AFMM and AFAMAP, a recently developed alternative method that does not incorporate SACs. The improvements are statistically significant, as confirmed by t-tests. The authors also show that the optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP. However, I have a question regarding the results section: why did the authors use a low noise variance in the toy example? Is this to illustrate the method's ability to separate the signal effectively, as shown in Figure 2? Clarification on this point would be helpful, as the problem setup seems overly simplistic. Additionally, it would be useful to know whether the chosen signal-to-noise ratio is representative of real-world data. The authors could also comment on the signal-to-noise ratio between the mains readings and the aggregate appliance output in the energy application.

The theoretical aspects of the paper are well-presented and structured. However, a proof that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex would strengthen the paper, as the provided reasoning is not entirely clear. Furthermore, theoretical scaling arguments regarding data length and the number of hidden states would complement the simulation findings and provide insight into the method's tractability for larger datasets and state spaces. How does the proposed method compare to AFAMAP in terms of scalability?

The paper is well-written and structured, with a clear introduction that motivates the problem and provides relevant literature background. Although I am not an expert in this field, the literature review appears comprehensive. I did notice a potential error on page 4 (line 202), in the unnumbered equation before (6), where the subscripts of the object p seem to be inconsistent with earlier notation on page 3.

To the best of my knowledge, the work is novel and original. However, as a non-expert, I am unable to assess the significance of these results to the HMM community and the energy application studied.

Addendum: The authors have satisfactorily addressed my questions in the feedback phase, and I encourage them to incorporate similar clarifications in the next version of the paper. This paper proposes a method for performing blind source separation using Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints, which demonstrates excellent performance, particularly with real data from an energy disaggregation problem, when compared to state-of-the-art methods.
