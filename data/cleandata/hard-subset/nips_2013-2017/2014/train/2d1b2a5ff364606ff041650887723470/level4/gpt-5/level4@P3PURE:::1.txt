This paper proposes a method for blind source separation by modeling the problem as an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The key motivation is to incorporate domain knowledge to improve separation of time series into components. The primary application throughout is energy disaggregation, where knowledge of total (expected) appliance usage over a time period helps separate household energy consumption into appliance-level components. The objective function being maximized—the log-posterior of the hidden chains given the observations—is reformulated as a convex optimization problem. The method is evaluated on both a toy dataset and real domestic energy readings.

The paper is technically sound and includes a strong simulation section. As expected, the method performs favorably compared to a standard AFHMM and AFAMAP (a recent alternative), neither of which incorporate SACs. Improvements are shown to be statistically significant using t-tests, and the optimization speed is reported to be comparable to AFAMAP.

One question about the results: why is the noise variance so low in the toy example? Is this primarily to make Figure 2 visually clear in showing that the proposed method separates the signal well while other methods do not? Some clarification would help, as the current setup appears “overly toy” and potentially too easy. Alternatively, if these signal-to-noise ratios are typical in real data, please state this. In the same spirit, it would be helpful to comment on the signal-to-noise ratio between the mains readings and the aggregated appliance outputs in the energy application.

The theoretical presentation is generally clear and well structured. The paper would benefit from a proof (or a self-contained argument) that the final procedure in equation (6) is indeed convex, as the current reasoning leaves this point somewhat unclear. In addition, theoretical scaling arguments with respect to data length and the number of hidden states would nicely complement the simulation results. Are the methods tractable for much larger datasets and state spaces, and how would this compare to AFAMAP?

The paper is well written and organized. The problem is well motivated in the introduction with an appropriate discussion of related work, though I am not deeply familiar with this literature and cannot definitively assess completeness. A small potential notation issue: on page 4 (line 202), in the unnumbered equation before (6), it appears there should be no commas in the subscripts following p, to remain consistent with the notation on page 3 (unless new notation is being introduced there).

To my knowledge, the work is novel and original, though I am not an expert and therefore cannot fully assess the significance of these results for the HMM community or the energy application.

Addendum: The authors satisfactorily addressed my questions during the feedback phase, and I encourage them to incorporate similar clarifications in the next revision. Overall, this paper introduces an AFHMM approach with signal aggregate constraints for blind source separation and demonstrates strong empirical performance relative to the state of the art, particularly on real energy disaggregation data.
