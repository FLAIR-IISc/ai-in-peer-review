- Introduces AFHMM augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to enforce expected total energy per source for blind source separation.  
- Motivating application: energy disaggregation recovering per-appliance usage from a single aggregate meter by matching daily consumption targets.  
- Performs MAP inference and reformulates the log-posterior into a convex quadratic program via relaxation with auxiliary variables.  
- Empirically outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP on synthetic and real household electricity data, with improvements verified by t-tests.  
- Optimization runtime is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Question about very small noise variance in the toy experiment—clarify whether it was chosen to make the illustrative separation especially clear or if it makes the setup overly simplistic.  
- Request to report signal-to-noise ratios between mains readings and latent appliance signals in the real energy data.  
- Theoretical presentation is clear but needs an explicit proof or citation that the relaxed program (eq.6) is convex, since the current argument is not fully transparent.  
- Recommend including scaling analysis w.r.t. time-series length and number of HMM states to assess tractability at larger scales and compare to AFAMAP.  
- Minor notation inconsistency: commas in subscripts before eq.(6) may conflict with earlier notation.  
- Paper is well written, appears novel and original; rebuttal addressed reviewer questions satisfactorily and clarifications should be incorporated into the revision.
