

The paper introduces an additive factorial hidden Markov model for electricity usage modeling, incorporating a "signal aggregate constraint." While the technical contribution is sound and the paper's quality is generally good, significant readability issues undermine its impact. The primary concern is the excessive use of acronyms, including several unnecessarily long ones (e.g., exceeding 11 characters), which impedes comprehension and disrupts the flow of the argument. The authors should adopt a more restrained approach to acronym usage, defining only essential terms and avoiding proliferation of obscure abbreviations.

A more substantive issue lies in the conceptual treatment of the "signal aggregate constraint." The paper describes this as a constraint imposed on the *posterior* density. However, the constraint is actually derived from an *a priori constraint* on the prior density, which subsequently induces a constraint on the posterior. The authors fail to explicitly clarify this distinction, leading to confusion about the constraint's origin and justification. This ambiguity is critical: the constraint is not imposed directly on the posterior via the model structure but arises from the choice of prior. The authors must explicitly state that they impose an *a priori constraint* on the prior density, thereby necessitating the constrained posterior. Without this clarification, the constraint's theoretical foundation appears misplaced, and the paper risks misrepresenting the model's mechanics.

The paper's core idea—using a factorial HMM with additive structure for electricity demand—is potentially valuable for energy analytics. However, the current presentation obscures this contribution. The methodological details are buried under excessive jargon and unclear notation, making it difficult to assess the model's novelty or practical utility. For instance, the rationale for the specific form of the signal aggregate constraint is not sufficiently motivated, and its relationship to the underlying electricity usage patterns remains underdeveloped. A clearer exposition of how the constraint aligns with domain knowledge (e.g., physical limits on aggregate consumption) would strengthen the work significantly.

To address these issues, the authors should: (1) drastically reduce acronym usage, defining only indispensable terms and avoiding lengthy, non-intuitive abbreviations; (2) explicitly reframe the constraint as an *a priori* condition on the prior density, clearly distinguishing it from a posterior constraint; and (3) provide a more concrete justification for the constraint's form, linking it to observable electricity usage properties. These revisions would resolve the central conceptual ambiguity and greatly improve readability.

While the model's potential merits are evident, the current draft's presentation hinders its evaluation. The technical content is promising but currently obscured by poor exposition and a critical logical gap. Addressing the acronyms and clarifying the constraint's origin are essential for the paper to make a clear contribution. As written, the work risks being dismissed due to accessibility issues rather than conceptual weakness. With the suggested revisions, the paper could make a meaningful impact on energy modeling applications.
