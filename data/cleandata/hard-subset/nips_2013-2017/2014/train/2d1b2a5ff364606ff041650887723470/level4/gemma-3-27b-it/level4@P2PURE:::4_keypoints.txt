- Introduces an AFHMM extension that enforces the sum of chain outputs to approximate a known aggregate value.  
- Develops a convex relaxation for MAP inference on the constrained AFHMM.  
- Demonstrates the method on real-world energy disaggregation datasets (100 UK homes low-frequency; 10 homes higher-frequency without appliance monitoring).  
- Ideas are conceptually clear and the optimization framework is straightforward.  
- The work is compelling due to application to large, real-world datasets and a mix of algorithmic and applied contributions.  
- Request to clarify whether SACAFHMM promotes single active-chain states or achieves low error even with non-integer solutions.  
- Notes AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear orthogonal and suggests exploring incorporation of SACAFHMM constraints into AFAMAP.  
- Points out occasional conflation of models and algorithms (e.g., “AFHMM” vs “AFAMAP”) and asks for precision—likely AFHMM comparisons refer to alternating Viterbi.  
- Suggests combining strategies from different algorithms could yield better performance.  
- Summary: a simple, valuable extension with strong real-data results, but needs clarity on algorithmic distinctions and potential method combinations.
