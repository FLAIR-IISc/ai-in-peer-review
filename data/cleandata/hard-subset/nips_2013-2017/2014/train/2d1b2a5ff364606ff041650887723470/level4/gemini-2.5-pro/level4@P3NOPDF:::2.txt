**Summary of Contributions**

This paper presents a novel Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) designed for the task of energy disaggregation, also known as Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring (NILM). The model's primary contribution is the direct and explicit incorporation of a "Signal Aggregate Constraint" (SAC). This constraint ensures that the sum of the inferred, individual appliance power draws at any given time point equals the total aggregate power measured from the mains—a fundamental physical property that is often handled indirectly or ignored in other models. The authors develop a probabilistic framework and an associated inference algorithm to learn the model parameters and disaggregate the total signal.

**Quality and Significance**

The paper addresses the important and challenging problem of energy disaggregation. The proposed method of enforcing the signal aggregate constraint within the model's architecture is a technically sound and valuable contribution. By building this physical constraint directly into the model's state-space, the authors provide a more principled approach compared to post-processing methods. The factorial HMM structure is a natural fit for modeling the parallel operation of multiple independent appliances, and the additive nature of the proposed model is well-motivated by the problem domain. The core technical idea is strong and represents a clear step forward for probabilistic modeling in this application area.

**Clarity and Areas for Improvement**

While the technical contributions are significant, the paper's clarity and readability are major concerns that currently hinder the accessibility and impact of the work. The following points should be addressed to improve the manuscript:

1.  **Clarity of the Constraint Formulation:** There is a potential ambiguity in how the motivation for the constraint is presented. It appears the authors' goal is to enforce a constraint on the posterior distribution of the hidden states (i.e., ensuring the inferred appliance states sum to the total). However, the mechanism described achieves this by placing a constraint on the model's prior definition or transition structure. While constraining the prior is a valid and effective way to influence the posterior, this logical connection is not articulated clearly. The paper would be significantly strengthened if the authors explicitly stated that they are introducing an *a priori* constraint on the model structure, which, by extension, guarantees that the resulting posterior will satisfy the desired property. This would clarify the "cause and effect" of their modeling choice.

2.  **Excessive Use of Acronyms:** The paper suffers from an overabundance of acronyms, which severely disrupts the reading flow. The reader is forced to constantly refer back to previous sections to recall the definitions. Some of these acronyms are exceptionally long (e.g., upwards of ten letters), which defeats their purpose of simplifying the text. The authors are strongly encouraged to reduce the number of acronyms, spell out terms where appropriate, and perhaps include a table of notation for the essential symbols and acronyms that must be retained.

3.  **Overall Readability:** Beyond the specific points above, the general prose is dense and difficult to parse. The core ideas and the elegance of the model are obscured by convoluted sentence structures. A thorough revision focused on simplifying the language, improving the narrative flow, and ensuring that each section logically builds upon the last would make the paper much more accessible to the NIPS community.

**Conclusion**

In summary, this paper contains a valuable and novel contribution to the field of energy disaggregation. The explicit modeling of the signal aggregate constraint is a strong and well-motivated idea. However, the paper's impact is currently limited by significant issues in its presentation, particularly regarding clarity and readability. The work is promising, but it requires a substantial revision to make the content accessible and the arguments clear. If the authors can thoroughly address the issues outlined above, particularly clarifying the prior/posterior constraint logic and improving the overall exposition, the paper would represent a solid contribution to the conference.
