- Proposes SACAFHMM: AFHMM with Signal Aggregate Constraints and convex quadratic-program inference for blind source separation.  
- Demonstrates promising synthetic and real-world results, improving NDE and SAE metrics.  
- Paper is well-written with a strong mathematical foundation and clear motivation.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair because SACAFHMM uses aggregate signal information unavailable to the baselines.  
- Recommend comparing to other methods that can incorporate aggregate constraints to isolate benefits of the convex formulation.  
- Figure 1 appears to contradict claims: AFAMAP outperforms SACAFHMM at large distances, suggesting possible sensitivity to noisy/incorrect aggregate constraints.  
- Authors should clarify the discrepancy and analyze robustness to constraint quality.  
- Suggest exploring extension to more realistic constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality, temporal) and discuss feasibility.  
- Addressing these concerns and constraint extensions would significantly strengthen the paper.
