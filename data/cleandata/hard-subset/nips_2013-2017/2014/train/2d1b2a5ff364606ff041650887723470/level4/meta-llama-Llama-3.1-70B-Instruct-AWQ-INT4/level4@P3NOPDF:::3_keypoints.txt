1. Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) and a convex quadratic relaxation.  
2. Method is well-written with a solid mathematical foundation and clear problem motivation.  
3. Convex formulation shows promising results on synthetic and real-world data, improving NDE and SAE metrics.  
4. Comparison fairness is questionable because baseline methods (AFHMM without SAC, AFAMAP) use less data than SACAFHMM.  
5. Needs comparison to other approaches that also use SAC, even if less computationally efficient.  
6. Figure 1 is unclear: AFAMAP appears to outperform SACAFHMM at large distances (>10^4), suggesting possible weaknesses.  
7. Poorly chosen constraints might harm performance; the paper’s discussion implying universal improvement is potentially misleading.  
8. Paper should include a more nuanced discussion of SAC’s limitations and failure modes.  
9. Recommend exploring more complex constraints (mutual exclusion, cardinality, time-limited usage) to increase applicability.  
10. Future work should focus on extending the algorithm to handle varied constraint types and provide more comprehensive comparisons.
