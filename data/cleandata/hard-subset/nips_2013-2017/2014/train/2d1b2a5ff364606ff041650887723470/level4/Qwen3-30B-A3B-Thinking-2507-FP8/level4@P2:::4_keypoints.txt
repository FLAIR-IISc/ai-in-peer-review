- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to enforce each source's total signal be close to a specified value, addressing identifiability in blind source separation/energy disaggregation.
- Integrates SACs into Additive Factorial HMMs (AFHMMs) and formulates a convex quadratic program for approximate inference despite non-Markovian constraints.
- Evaluated on real-world datasets (100 UK households low-frequency and 9 homes with mains) showing large improvements over baselines.
- Reported performance gains include AFHMM+SAC achieving a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% improvement vs AFAMAP.
- Using national consumption data, AFHMM+SAC yields 33% better NDE and 16% better SAE compared to AFAMAP.
- Novel contribution: SACs provide a general, domain-knowledgeable constraint type complementary to existing approaches like posterior regularization.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem statement linking SACs to practical energy disaggregation needs.
- Strength: technically sound convex optimization enabling efficient inference under non-Markovian SACs.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation using meaningful metrics (NDE, SAE) on multiple real-world datasets.
- Weakness: conflates model and algorithm terminology—AFAMAP is an inference algorithm for AFHMM; baseline AFHMM needs clearer definition.
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of how SACs relate to or can be combined with other constraint approaches (e.g., AFAMAP's transition constraints) and their comparative computational trade-offs.
- Weakness: needs more detail on the integer-to-relaxation step—whether non-integral QP solutions are practically meaningful and how they compare to exact integer inference.
- Weakness: unclear why SACs improve over AFAMAP—whether they capture complementary information or address different identifiability aspects.
- Suggested next step: implement SACs within the AFAMAP framework to evaluate combined performance and clarify relationships to other constraint methods.
- Overall verdict: strong, practically valuable, and technically sound paper that requires minor clarifications on terminology and comparisons to related constraint methods.
