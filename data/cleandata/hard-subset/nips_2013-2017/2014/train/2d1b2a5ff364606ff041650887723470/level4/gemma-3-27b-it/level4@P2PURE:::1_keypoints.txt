- Introduces AFHMM-based blind source separation augmented with Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to leverage domain knowledge.  
- Applies method to energy disaggregation, using expected appliance total consumption to improve separation.  
- Reformulates MAP objective into a convex optimization problem for hidden-state inference.  
- Validated on synthetic and real domestic energy datasets, showing empirical effectiveness.  
- Outperforms standard AFHMM and AFAMAP with statistically significant improvements (t-tests).  
- Reports optimization speeds comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Questions choice of low noise variance in the synthetic example and requests clarification on representativeness and typical mains vs. appliance SNR.  
- Requests a formal proof of convexity for the final procedure (equation (6)), as current reasoning is not fully convincing.  
- Suggests adding theoretical scaling analysis (data length and number of hidden states) to assess tractability and compare to AFAMAP on larger problems.  
- Notes a minor typographical/notation inconsistency in the subscripts of 'p' near equation (6).  
- Deems the paper technically sound, well-written, and novel, but unsure about broader significance within HMM or energy-disaggregation communities.  
- Authors addressed reviewer concerns in feedback; reviewer recommends incorporating clarifications into the final version and endorses the paper.
