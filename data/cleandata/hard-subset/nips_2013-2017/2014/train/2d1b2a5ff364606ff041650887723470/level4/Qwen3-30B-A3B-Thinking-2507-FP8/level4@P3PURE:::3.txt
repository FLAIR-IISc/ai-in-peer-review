

The paper introduces an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregation constraints (SAC) for blind source separation. The authors formulate a convex quadratic program to relax the AFHMM with SAC, demonstrating promising results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  

**Strengths:**  
- The paper is well-written with a strong mathematical foundation.  
- The problem motivation is clear and compelling.  
- Evaluation results show significant improvements in key metrics (NDE and SAE) over baselines.  

**Areas for Improvement:**  
- The performance gains are reported relative to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP. However, both baselines utilize less data than SACAFHMM, creating an unfair comparison. It is unclear whether other approaches could incorporate SAC while maintaining computational efficiency.  
- Figure 1 shows SACAFHMM being outperformed by AFAMAP for distances exceeding \(10^4\). This contradicts the paper’s claim that SAC consistently improves performance, suggesting that poorly designed constraints could degrade results. The discussion’s assertion that SAC always yields better outcomes appears misleading.  
- The paper focuses on a specific constraint type but does not address more complex real-world constraints (e.g., a person cannot operate both the heater and cooler simultaneously, or no more than three of ten bulbs may be active at once). Incorporating time-based constraints (e.g., grinders operating for <10 minutes) would broaden applicability. The authors should clarify whether the framework can be extended to such diverse constraint types.  

The paper provides a well-motivated approach and strong foundational work. Future extensions to handle diverse constraint structures would significantly strengthen the contribution.
