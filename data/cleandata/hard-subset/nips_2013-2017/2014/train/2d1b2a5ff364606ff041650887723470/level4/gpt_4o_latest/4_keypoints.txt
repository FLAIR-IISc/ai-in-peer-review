- Extends the additive factorial HMM by adding constraints that force the sum of chain outputs to align with a known aggregate value.
- Proposes a convex relaxation approach for MAP inference to handle the added constraints.
- Evaluated on real-world energy disaggregation datasets: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (high-frequency) without appliance-level monitoring.
- Methods and concepts are straightforward and use a simple optimization framework, but the work is compelling due to large-scale, real-world evaluation.
- Paper combines applied and algorithmic contributions and is overall successful.
- Question: does SACAFHMM produce the same benefit as AFAMAP’s “one chain active” constraint (i.e., more integer solutions) or do non-integral SACAFHMM solutions still yield lower error? Discussion needed.
- AFAMAP and SACAFHMM appear fundamentally orthogonal; SACAFHMM constraints could in principle be incorporated into the AFAMAP framework—worth exploring.
- The paper sometimes conflates models and optimization methods; comparing “AFHMM” and “AFAMAP” is imprecise because AFAMAP is an algorithm for solving the AFHMM MAP problem.
- Reviewer suspects that the paper’s “AFHMM” baseline corresponds to alternating Viterbi; the authors should clarify.
- Suggestion: explore combining strategies from different algorithms, which might outperform current approaches.
