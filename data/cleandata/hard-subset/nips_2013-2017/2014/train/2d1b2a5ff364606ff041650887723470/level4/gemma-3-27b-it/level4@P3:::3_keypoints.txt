- Addresses blind source separation for energy disaggregation by adding signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM).
- Proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate inference with SACs.
- Leverages domain knowledge by encouraging each source’s total signal to match expected aggregate values.
- Clear motivation explaining identifiability issues in blind source separation and need for priors.
- Mathematical formulation and derivation of the convex QP are sound and technically notable.
- Experimental results show substantial improvements: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% vs AFAMAP.
- Comparison to AFAMAP and baseline AFHMM may be uneven because of different training data sizes.
- Lack of detailed analysis of computational cost and scalability as number of appliances/sources increases.
- Figure 1 discussion is misleading; results suggest AFAMAP can outperform when SACs are poorly specified.
- Method sensitivity to inaccuracies in SAC values needs thorough exploration.
- Paper briefly mentions richer constraints (time-of-day, co-occurrence) but lacks depth on incorporating them or generalizing the QP.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, well-motivated approach; strong empirical gains; solid math and clear presentation.
- Rejection concerns: uneven method comparisons, potentially misleading result interpretation, limited exploration of more complex constraints.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify comparisons and discuss limitations and sensitivity to constraint accuracy.
