- Paper extends AFHMM by adding constraints to match the sum of chain outputs to a known aggregate signal, proposes a convex relaxation for MAP inference, and evaluates on two real-world energy disaggregation datasets.  
- The idea and optimization are conceptually simple and straightforward, and the application to substantial real data is compelling.  
- Clarify integrality and relaxation behavior: does SACAFHMM tend to produce integral solutions like AFAMAP’s “one chain changes at a time” constraint, or non-integral solutions with lower error; discuss when/why integrality occurs and its relation to error.  
- Discuss orthogonality and potential combinations: SACAFHMM constraints appear orthogonal to AFAMAP and could potentially be integrated with AFAMAP’s framework—testing this combination is encouraged.  
- Use precise terminology when comparing models and algorithms: distinguish the AFHMM model from inference methods (AFAMAP, alternating Viterbi, the proposed convex relaxation) and state explicitly which algorithm is used in comparisons.  
- Overall: clean extension and efficient optimization with convincing empirical results; clearer distinctions and a discussion of relaxation effects would strengthen the paper.
