- Proposes blind source separation via additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) augmented with signal aggregate constraints (SACs).  
- Motivation: incorporate domain knowledge (aggregate signal info) to improve time-series component separation.  
- Demonstrated on energy disaggregation: separates appliance-level usage using expected total appliance usage per period.  
- Paper is technically sound with a strong simulation section validating the method.  
- Empirical results: method outperforms regular AFHMM and AFAMAP; improvements are statistically significant (t-tests).  
- Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.  
- Concern: toy example uses a low noise variance—unclear if this SNR is realistic or chosen to best illustrate separation.  
- Suggestion: clarify signal-to-noise ratio between mains readings and aggregate appliance outputs in the real-data experiments.  
- Theory is well-presented but needs a formal proof that equation (6)’s final procedure is convex.  
- Request theoretical scaling analysis (with respect to data length and number of hidden states) and comparison with AFAMAP for tractability.  
- Minor notation issue: possible erroneous commas in subscripts on page 4 (unnumbered equation before (6)).  
- Overall assessment: work is novel, authors’ responses were satisfactory, and method shows excellent performance on real energy-disaggregation data.
