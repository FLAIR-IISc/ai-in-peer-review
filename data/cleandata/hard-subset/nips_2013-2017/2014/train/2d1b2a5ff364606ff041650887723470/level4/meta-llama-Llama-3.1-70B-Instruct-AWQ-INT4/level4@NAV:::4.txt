This paper presents an extension of the additive factorial HMM model by incorporating additional constraints to ensure that individual chain outputs sum up to a reasonable aggregate value. The authors propose a convex relaxation for MAP inference and demonstrate its application on real-world datasets, specifically energy disaggregation across 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings and 10 homes with higher frequency readings, albeit without monitoring individual appliances.

Although the ideas presented are relatively straightforward, and the optimization formulation is simple, the work remains compelling due to its application to large real-world datasets. As a paper that combines both applied and algorithmic elements, it is successful. To further improve the paper, the following comments are offered:

It would be beneficial to discuss whether the SACAFHMM optimization formulation achieves similar results to AFAMAP in terms of enforcing the constraint that one chain stages state at a time, and whether the resulting solutions still exhibit lower error despite being non-integral. This discussion would help elucidate the distinctions between the two approaches.

It appears that the AFAMAP and SACAFHMM ideas are orthogonal, and it is possible to incorporate SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization formulation. Although not expected in the author response, it is strongly encouraged to explore this combination in future work.

There are instances where the authors seem to conflate models and optimization procedures. For example, comparing "AFHMM" and "AFAMAP" results lacks precision, as AFAMAP is an algorithm for approximating MAP solutions to the AFHMM. It is believed that the authors actually refer to alternating Viterbi optimization when mentioning AFHMM. Clarifying this distinction would highlight the potential for combining strategies from different algorithms, which might yield better performance than the current approaches.

Overall, the paper proposes a simple yet effective extension and optimization to solve a variant of the AFHMM, and the results on real-world data are compelling, making the paper reasonable in its current form.
