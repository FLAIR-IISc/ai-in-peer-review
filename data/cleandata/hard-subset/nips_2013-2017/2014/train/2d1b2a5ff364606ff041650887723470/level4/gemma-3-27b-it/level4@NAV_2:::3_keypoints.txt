- Proposes AFHMM with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) for blind source separation.
- Formulates an efficient convex quadratic program for approximate inference in the relaxed AFHMM+SAC.
- Shows promising results on synthetic and real-world energy disaggregation data.
- Paper is well-written with strong mathematical foundations and clear motivation.
- Evaluation reports substantial improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).
- Comparisons may be imbalanced since AFHMM+SAC appears to use more data than some baselines.
- Recommend discussing alternative methods that could use SAC but are limited by computational cost.
- Figure 1 needs clarification: beyond distance 1e4 AFAMAP may outperform AFHMM+SAC.
- Raises concern that poorly specified constraints could substantially degrade performance, not fully analyzed.
- Uses relatively simple aggregate constraints; scope to incorporate more complex, domain-specific constraints.
- Suggests exploring constraints like appliance co-occurrence and temporal duration limits.
- Recommends evaluating the framework’s extensibility to diverse constraint types and other domains.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel SAC application, technically sound convex relaxation, demonstrated empirical gains, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential imbalance in baseline comparisons, limited sensitivity analysis to constraint mis-specification, narrow constraint scope.
