1. Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge by constraining each source’s total signal to a known/expected aggregate.

2. Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM and solves the resulting MAP inference via a convex quadratic programming (CQP) relaxation.

3. Demonstrates on a large real-world energy dataset that AFHMM+SAC substantially reduces disaggregation error versus standard AFHMM and AFAMAP.

4. Strength: Addresses an important, practical BSS problem and uses aggregate constraints to mitigate inherent unidentifiability.

5. Strength: SACs are a simple, novel, and elegant regularization idea likely adaptable to other BSS tasks.

6. Strength: Thorough empirical validation against strong baselines with convincing performance gains.

7. Weakness: Terminology/experimental clarity—AFAMAP is an approximate MAP inference algorithm for AFHMM, not a distinct model; the inference procedure used for the "AFHMM" baseline should be explicitly stated (e.g., chainwise Viterbi).

8. Weakness: Missing analysis of the CQP relaxation behavior—unclear if SACs make solutions more integral or merely guide them to better regions.

9. Weakness: SACs (global constraints) appear orthogonal to AFAMAP’s local temporal constraints; combining both could improve results and should be noted as future work.

10. Conclusion: A solid, well-motivated contribution with strong empirical results; minor clarifications and deeper optimization analysis would strengthen the paper.
