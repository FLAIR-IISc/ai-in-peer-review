1. Proposes a novel blind source separation method using Additive Factorial HMMs with Signal Aggregate Constraints, motivated by energy disaggregation.

2. Transforms the log-posterior objective into a convex optimization problem to perform the separation.

3. Paper is technically sound with a strong simulation section.

4. Method outperforms regular AFHMM and AFAMAP, with improvements significant by t-tests.

5. Optimization speed is comparable to AFAMAP.

6. Reviewer questions the toy example's low noise variance and requests clarification on whether the signal-to-noise ratios are realistic and typical in the energy application (mains vs appliance aggregate).

7. Theoretical presentation is good but needs a formal proof that the final procedure (equation (6)) is convex.

8. Requests theoretical scaling analysis with respect to data length and number of hidden states and comparison of tractability to AFAMAP.

9. Notes a potential notation error on page 4 (line 202) regarding commas in subscripts before p.

10. Reviewer views the work as novel and original but cannot fully assess its broader significance to the HMM community.

11. Authors addressed reviewer questions in feedback; reviewer recommends incorporating those clarifications and notes strong real-data performance.
