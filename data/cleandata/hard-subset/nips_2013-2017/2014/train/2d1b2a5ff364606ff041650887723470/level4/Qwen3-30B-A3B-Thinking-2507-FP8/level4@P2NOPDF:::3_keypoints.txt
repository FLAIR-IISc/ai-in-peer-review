- Introduces an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) framed as a convex quadratic relaxation, showing promising synthetic and real-world results.  
- Strength: well-structured paper with strong mathematical foundation and clear motivation.  
- Strength: evaluation (Normalized Distortion Error and Signal-to-Artifact Error) shows consistent performance gains over baselines.  
- Concern: Baseline comparison may be unfair—AFAMAP appears to use a reduced dataset; authors must clarify whether SAC-AFHMM used identical data and discuss alternative SAC-incorporating methods and their complexity.  
- Concern: Figure 1 suggests SAC-AFHMM underperforms AFAMAP for distances >10⁴, contradicting the claim that SAC always helps; paper should discuss constraint sensitivity and edge cases where constraints can harm performance.  
- Concern: Limited constraint types—unclear if SAC’s convex relaxation can handle richer real-world constraints (temporal limits, mutual exclusion, cardinality) or if model extensions are needed.  
- Recommendation: Novel, technically sound contribution, but needs rigorous justification of comparisons and expanded discussion/handling of broader constraint types to strengthen real-world applicability.
