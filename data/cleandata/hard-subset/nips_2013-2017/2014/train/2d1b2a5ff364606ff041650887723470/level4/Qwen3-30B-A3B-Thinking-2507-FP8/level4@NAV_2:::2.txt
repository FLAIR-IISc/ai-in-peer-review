

The paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to address identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation, extending the Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM) framework. SACs enforce that the total signal for each source (e.g., appliance energy consumption) aligns with domain-specific expectations (e.g., toaster ≈50 Wh/day), leveraging prior knowledge to resolve unidentifiability. This work builds directly on Kolter & Jaakkola’s AFHMM (AISTATS 2012) and related NIPS energy disaggregation studies [12, 22], but introduces a novel constraint class distinct from prior approaches like AFAMAP [12] or k-segment constraints [19]. The authors formulate SACs as a convex quadratic program (Section 5), enabling efficient inference, and demonstrate significant improvements over AFHMM (45% NDE reduction) and AFAMAP (29% NDE improvement) on real-world energy data (Section 7.2.2).

**Strengths:**  
- **Originality (High):** SACs represent a novel, domain-knowledge-driven constraint class for BSS, addressing a gap in prior work [4, 13, 14] that focused on generic regularization without specifying *what* constraints to use.  
- **Quality (High):** The CQP solution (Section 5) is technically sound, with clear theoretical grounding via posterior regularization [4] (Section 6). Empirical results are robust (paired t-tests, 100 households) and significantly outperform state-of-the-art [12].  
- **Significance (High):** The 29% NDE improvement over AFAMAP [12] demonstrates tangible impact for energy disaggregation, a high-impact application with real-world deployment potential (e.g., household energy monitoring).  
- **Clarity (Moderate):** The core methodology is well-motivated and logically presented, though acronym density (e.g., "AFHMM+SAC" used without expansion in Section 4.2) slightly impedes readability.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- **Clarity (Moderate):** SACs are introduced without formal definition in the abstract/introduction (first defined in Section 4.2), and Section 6’s posterior regularization derivation could more explicitly clarify that constraints are imposed on the *posterior* via the regularized objective (not the prior), avoiding confusion.  
- **Theoretical Depth (Moderate):** The relaxed CQP solution’s optimality guarantees (beyond empirical validation) could be strengthened with a discussion of approximation error bounds.  
- **Scope (Minor):** The single-constraint SAC formulation (Section 4.2) is sufficient for energy disaggregation, but the potential for multi-period constraints (e.g., daytime vs. nighttime usage) is noted as an extension (Section 4.2), which could be briefly elaborated.  

**Pro/Con Acceptance Summary:**  
- **Pro:** Novel constraint class with strong empirical validation (45% NDE improvement over AFHMM), technically rigorous CQP solution, significant practical impact for energy disaggregation (a key NIPS application area).  
- **Con:** Minor clarity issues with acronym usage and posterior regularization explanation; theoretical guarantees for relaxed inference could be expanded.  

The paper makes a valuable contribution to energy disaggregation and BSS, meeting all NIPS quality criteria. With minor revisions to clarify SAC definition and posterior regularization mechanics, it would be an excellent fit for the conference.
