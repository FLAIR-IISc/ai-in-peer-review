- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to enforce that each source’s summed signal matches domain-specified values, improving identifiability in AFHMMs for energy disaggregation.  
- Proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate MAP inference incorporating SACs.  
- Validated on real-world data (100 UK households low-frequency; 9 homes mains-level) showing practical applicability.  
- Shows 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) versus baseline AFHMM.  
- Outperforms AFAMAP by 29% in NDE and 16% in signal aggregate error (SAE) when using national consumption estimates.  
- Results are statistically significant (paired t-tests, p < 0.05) and empirically rigorous.  
- SACs are novel and distinct from prior constraint methods; the convex relaxation is well-motivated and computationally efficient.  
- Experiments report thorough error metrics (NDE/SAE) and comparable runtime to AFHMM/AFAMAP.  
- Terminology issues: paper conflates AFHMM model and AFAMAP inference algorithm; requires clarification.  
- Orthogonality point: SACs and AFAMAP’s single-chain-state-change constraint are complementary; combining them could yield further improvements.  
- Robustness concerns: toy experiments show graceful degradation, but real-world sensitivity to incorrect domain aggregates (e.g., national vs household) needs deeper analysis.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful BSS contribution with minor fixes (terminology, combined-constraint experiments, robustness discussion) suggested.
