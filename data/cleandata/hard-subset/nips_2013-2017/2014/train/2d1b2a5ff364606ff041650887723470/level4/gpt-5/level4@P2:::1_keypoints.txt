- Contribution: Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into AFHMMs for blind source separation/energy disaggregation, enforced as soft constraints during inference.
- Method: Formulates MAP inference with SACs, introduces transition indicators, and derives a convex quadratic program (CQP) under binary-relaxation.
- Interpretation: Frames the SAC-penalized objective via posterior regularization as an optimally constrained posterior.
- Empirics: Shows sizable, statistically significant improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP on synthetic and UK HES data with comparable runtime.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on AFHMMs and AFAMAP, and leverages posterior regularization; SACs are presented as a distinct, practical constraint class.
- Strength — technical soundness: Modeling and CQP relaxation are coherent and inference uses standard convex tools; experiments use realistic low-frequency data.
- Strength — originality: SACs are a simple, novel way to inject widely-available domain knowledge; convex-relaxation + posterior-regularization link is appealing.
- Strength — significance: Substantial empirical gains and applicability to broader BSS problems make the method useful to practitioners.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is generally well written with good overview of related work and applications.
- Weakness — synthetic noise: Synthetic experiment uses very low observation noise; needs justification and sensitivity analysis to realistic SNRs (report typical SNRs in HES).
- Weakness — convexity/relaxation: Request for a formal convexity argument for Eq. (6) and clarification on handling/rounding of fractional solutions and impact on performance.
- Weakness — computational scaling: Ask for explicit complexity in terms of I, Ki, T and practical scalability/solver-time discussion compared to AFAMAP.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Experiments fix λi=1; request sensitivity/ablation on λi and guidance for setting them in practice.
- Weakness — dependence on aggregate priors: Need discussion of robustness to biased/uncertain µi0 and potential modeling of µi0 as random/uncertain.
- Weakness — comparative breadth: Suggest comparison or discussion on combining SACs with duration/non-homogeneous HMM constraints.
- Clarity/minor issue — notation: Inconsistent subscript/comma use in one equation; request standardization or explanation.
- Clarity/minor issue — practical details: Summarize how µi are learned (k-means) and whether more than three states affects performance-runtime trade-offs.
- Pros for acceptance: Clear, generalizable idea with solid theoretical grounding, efficient convex relaxation, strong empirical gains, and applicability beyond energy.
- Cons for acceptance: Limited analysis of convexity/relaxation tightness and rounding, sparse complexity/scalability analysis, and insufficient sensitivity/robustness studies.
- Overall assessment: Well-executed, practically motivated paper; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications on convexity, scaling, and noise/parameter sensitivity.
- Addendum: Post-rebuttal responses addressed reviewer concerns; recommend incorporating SNR, convexity, and complexity clarifications into final version.
