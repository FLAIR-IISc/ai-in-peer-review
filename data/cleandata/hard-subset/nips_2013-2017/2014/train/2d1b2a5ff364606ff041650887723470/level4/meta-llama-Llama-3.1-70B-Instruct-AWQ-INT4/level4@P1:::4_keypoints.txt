- Proposes extending the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with aggregate-sum constraints forcing the sum of chain outputs to match a known aggregate value.  
- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference in this constrained AFHMM.  
- Applies the method to real-world energy disaggregation: 100 UK homes with low-frequency readings and 9 homes with higher-frequency readings (without per-appliance monitoring).  
- Overall approach is straightforward and uses a simple optimization formulation, but is compelling due to scale and practical application.  
- Question: does SACAFHMM promote integral (integer) solutions like AFAMAP, or do non-integral solutions still yield lower error—discussion needed.  
- Observation: AFAMAP and SACAFHMM seem orthogonal; the SACAFHMM constraints could likely be incorporated into AFAMAP.  
- Critique: authors sometimes conflate models and optimization procedures (e.g., AFHMM model vs AFAMAP algorithm); clarity is needed.  
- Implication: different algorithmic strategies could be combined and might outperform the presented approaches.  
- Strength: novel extension to AFHMM incorporating signal aggregate constraints.  
- Strength: convex relaxation to MAP inference is a noteworthy contribution.  
- Strength: empirical validation on practical, large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: ideas and optimization formulation are relatively straightforward/simple.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the relationship and potential combination of AFAMAP and SACAFHMM.  
- Recommendation: accept the paper for publication.  
- Recommended improvements: add discussion on AFAMAP vs SACAFHMM interaction/combination, clarify model vs algorithm distinctions, and include additional experiments/results.
