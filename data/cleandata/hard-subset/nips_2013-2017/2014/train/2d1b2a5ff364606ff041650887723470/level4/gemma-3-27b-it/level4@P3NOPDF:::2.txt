## Review of NIPS_2014 Submission – Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model for Electricity Usage

This paper introduces an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) designed for modeling electricity usage, incorporating a novel “signal aggregate constraint.” While the core idea appears promising and the technical quality of the work is generally sound, the paper suffers significantly from readability issues that hinder a full appreciation of the contributions.

The primary weakness lies in the excessive and often unnecessarily complex notation. The authors employ a multitude of acronyms, many of which are lengthy and obscure (some exceeding 11 letters), making it difficult to follow the derivations and understand the model’s components. This density of jargon creates a steep learning curve for the reader and detracts from the clarity of the presentation. A conscious effort to expand commonly used terms, especially upon their first appearance, and to simplify acronyms where possible would substantially improve comprehension.

The core technical contribution – the signal aggregate constraint – is interesting. However, its presentation lacks precision in describing *where* the constraint is being applied. The paper frames the constraint as directly impacting the posterior density, but the mathematical formulation suggests it is actually imposed on the prior distribution. This distinction is crucial; a constraint on the prior will naturally propagate to the posterior, but stating it as a posterior constraint is misleading. The authors should explicitly clarify that they are imposing an *a priori* constraint, and therefore, the resulting posterior distribution will implicitly reflect this constraint.  Addressing this conceptual clarity would significantly strengthen the paper.

Beyond the notational burden, several sections could benefit from more detailed explanations. For example, the motivation behind the specific factorial structure of the HMM is not fully articulated. While the authors hint at computational advantages, a more explicit discussion of why this structure is suitable for electricity load modeling, and what assumptions it embodies, would be valuable. Similarly, the relationship between this approach and existing load forecasting methods (e.g., those utilizing similar factor models or HMMs) is not thoroughly explored.  A dedicated comparison, outlining the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed AFHMM relative to existing techniques, would contextualize the contribution and demonstrate its novelty more effectively.

The experimental results, while demonstrating the model's performance on real-world electricity usage data, could be presented more effectively. The choice of baseline models for comparison is reasonable, but the discussion of the results lacks depth.  A more detailed analysis of the performance differences, potentially including statistical significance testing, would strengthen the conclusions. Furthermore, visualizing the learned latent factors and their relationship to the observed electricity usage patterns could provide valuable insight into the model’s behavior and interpretability.

**Strengths:**

*   The proposed AFHMM framework is a potentially valuable approach to electricity load modeling.
*   The signal aggregate constraint is a novel contribution that addresses a relevant aspect of electricity usage data.
*   The experiments demonstrate promising results on real-world data.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Poor readability due to excessive and complex notation and acronyms.
*   Imprecise description of the constraint – it’s applied to the prior, not the posterior.
*   Insufficient explanation of the motivation for the factorial HMM structure.
*   Limited comparison to existing load forecasting methods.
*   Experimental results lack in-depth analysis and visualization.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a potentially interesting and technically sound contribution. However, the significant readability issues severely limit its impact. I recommend **rejection** in its current form. If the authors address the concerns regarding notation, clarity of the constraint application, and provide more detailed explanations and analysis, the paper could become a valuable addition to the NIPS conference. A substantial revision focusing on clarity and comprehensibility is essential.
