- Proposes an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) with signal aggregate constraints (SAC) to tackle blind source separation.  
- Introduces a convex quadratic program for a relaxed AFHMM+SAC formulation.  
- Shows promising performance on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Paper is well-written with a solid mathematical foundation and clear motivation.  
- Evaluation demonstrates significant improvements in normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).  
- Strength: SAC enables incorporation of domain knowledge, helping address identifiability issues.  
- Strength: Clear explanation of the connection between the method and posterior regularization.  
- Concern: Comparisons to AFHMM without SAC and AFAMAP may be unfair because SACAFHMM uses more data.  
- Suggestion: Compare against other methods that could also utilize SAC, even if less computationally efficient.  
- Concern: Figure 1 is unclear; AFAMAP may outperform SACAFHMM at larger distances, suggesting poor constraints can worsen performance.  
- Critique: Discussion implying results are always better may be misleading.  
- Suggestion: Explore more complex, realistic constraints (e.g., mutually exclusive devices, limited simultaneous activations).  
- Suggestion: Consider time-limited constraints (e.g., grinders max 10 minutes) and extend the algorithm to handle such constraints.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address comparison fairness and explore more complex constraints.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, promising empirical results, clear relation to posterior regularization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially unfair comparisons, unclear/misleading results in Figure 1, lack of exploration of complex constraints.
