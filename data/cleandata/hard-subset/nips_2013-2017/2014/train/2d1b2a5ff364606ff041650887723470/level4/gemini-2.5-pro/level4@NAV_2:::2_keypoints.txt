- Proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge into blind source separation for energy disaggregation by constraining each source’s total aggregate over time.  
- Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM (AFHMM), but global constraints break the Markov property making exact MAP inference intractable.  
- Relaxes the constrained MAP inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for efficient approximate inference.  
- Method is complementary to prior constrained AFHMM work (e.g., AFAMAP).  
- Empirical results on a real low-frequency energy dataset show large gains: ~45% error reduction vs AFHMM and ~29% vs AFAMAP.  
- Strength: idea is intuitive, novel, addresses BSS non-identifiability, and is practically relevant to energy disaggregation.  
- Strength: technically sound formulation and relaxation; thorough experiments including oracle (TTC) and realistic (NTC) constraints.  
- Strength: connects the penalized objective to the posterior regularization framework, providing theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: paper readability suffers from dense acronyms and compressed notation; CQP section needs clearer explanation or a notation guide.  
- Weakness: model framing could be improved by presenting the SAC penalty as a prior on latent sequences (Bayesian perspective) earlier for clearer probabilistic motivation.
