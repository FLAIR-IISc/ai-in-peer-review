- Proposes an extension of the additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) that adds constraints so individual chain outputs sum to a reasonable aggregate.  
- Introduces a convex relaxation for MAP inference.  
- Demonstrates method on real-world energy-disaggregation data: 100 UK homes (low-frequency) and 10 homes (higher-frequency, no per-appliance monitoring).  
- Ideas and optimization are straightforward but compelling because of application to large real datasets.  
- Paper successfully combines applied and algorithmic contributions.  
- Request to discuss whether SACAFHMM enforces the “one state per chain at a time” constraint as effectively as AFAMAP and whether non-integral solutions still yield lower error.  
- Suggests AFAMAP and SACAFHMM are orthogonal and recommends exploring incorporating SACAFHMM constraints into the AFAMAP optimization in future work.  
- Critiques conflation of models and optimization procedures—AFHMM (model) vs AFAMAP (algorithm) should be clarified; authors likely mean alternating Viterbi when referring to AFHMM results.  
- Clarifying distinctions could reveal opportunities to combine algorithmic strategies for better performance.  
- Overall verdict: a simple, effective extension with compelling real-world results; paper is reasonable in current form.
