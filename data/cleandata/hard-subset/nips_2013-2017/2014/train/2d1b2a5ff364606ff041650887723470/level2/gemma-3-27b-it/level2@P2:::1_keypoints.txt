- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) in AFHMMs to improve blind source separation by leveraging prior total-energy knowledge.  
- Proposes a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate inference under SACs, connected to posterior regularization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% vs a recent state-of-the-art on the HES dataset.  
- The SAC idea is novel, intuitive, and addresses identifiability in energy disaggregation.  
- Theoretical justification and CQP formulation are well-explained and computationally tractable.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, making the method and experiments easy to follow.  
- Computational complexity is comparable to existing methods (AFHMM, AFAMAP), supporting practical applicability.  
- Related work discussion is limited; connections to constrained HMMs and comparisons to other prior choices need expansion.  
- Performance is sensitive to the accuracy of constraint values (µi0); more guidance on obtaining reliable estimates is needed.  
- Assumes known model parameters (θ), which is a strong and potentially unrealistic assumption; impact of parameter estimation errors is not analyzed.  
- Experimental evaluation is dataset-specific (HES); additional datasets would help assess generalizability.  
- The role and tuning of the δi parameter are not fully explored; practitioners need more analysis on its impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical and theoretical support and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete related work coverage, sensitivity to µi0, and limiting assumption of known θ.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to expand related work and provide guidance on constraint estimation and parameter assumptions.
