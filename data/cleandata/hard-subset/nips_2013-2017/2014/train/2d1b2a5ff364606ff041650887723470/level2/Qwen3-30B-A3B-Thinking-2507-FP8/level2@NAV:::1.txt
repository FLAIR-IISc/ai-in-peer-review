

This paper introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation problems, specifically applied to energy disaggregation using Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Models (AFHMMs). SACs constrain the total signal for each source to be close to a specified value based on domain knowledge (e.g., average appliance energy consumption), addressing the critical identifiability problem in BSS. The authors formulate SACs as a constrained optimization problem, transform it into a convex quadratic program for efficient inference, and demonstrate significant improvements on real-world energy disaggregation data.

The paper's strengths are substantial. The SAC concept is novel and well-motivated, providing a practical way to incorporate domain knowledge into BSS problems. The technical derivation is sound, with the convex quadratic program offering a computationally efficient solution to an otherwise intractable problem. The experimental evaluation is thorough and compelling: on real energy data, AFHMM+SAC achieves a 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error over the original AFHMM and a 29% improvement over the state-of-the-art AFAMAP, with statistical significance confirmed through paired t-tests. The paper properly contextualizes its contribution within related work on posterior regularization and constraint-based HMM approaches.

The primary limitation is the reliance on domain knowledge for constraint values (µi0), which may not be readily available in all applications. While the paper acknowledges this limitation, it would strengthen the contribution by discussing potential strategies for estimating constraint values when expert knowledge is unavailable (e.g., using historical data or transfer learning). The paper also doesn't explore how SACs might interact with other constraint methods.

Overall, this is a high-quality, well-executed contribution with clear significance for energy disaggregation and broader BSS applications. The method is technically sound, the experiments are convincing, and the paper is clearly written. The minor limitation regarding constraint value estimation does not diminish the paper's substantial contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
