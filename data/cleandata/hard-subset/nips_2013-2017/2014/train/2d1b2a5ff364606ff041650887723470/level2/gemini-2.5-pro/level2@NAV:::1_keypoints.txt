- Tackles identifiability in blind source separation for energy disaggregation using AFHMMs.
- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs): prior on total aggregated signal per source over a time window.
- Integrates SACs into the AFHMM objective and relaxes the problem to a convex quadratic program (CQP) for tractable inference.
- Approach is simple, intuitive, novel, and applicable beyond energy disaggregation.
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements over a strong baseline (AFAMAP) on real-world HES data.
- Technical formulation and relaxation are sound and well-executed.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincing (toy data + real dataset).
- Uses both realistic (national consumption) and oracle priors to bound method performance and includes statistical tests.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and well-organized.
- Primary weakness: method depends on the quality of the aggregate prior (µi0); poor priors degrade performance.
- Secondary weakness: complexity parameter λ fixed at 1 with no sensitivity analysis or principled selection.
- For acceptance: novel idea, strong empirical gains, tractable algorithm, and rigorous experiments.
- Against acceptance: lack of principled hyperparameter (λ) selection (minor concern).
- Reviewer recommendation: strong accept / recommend acceptance.
