- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to inject domain knowledge about total source signals into blind source separation for AFHMMs.  
- Formulates SACs as constraints on per-source total signal and proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Leverages prior knowledge of appliance total energy to address identifiability issues in energy disaggregation.  
- Connects SACs to posterior regularization, situating the method within existing frameworks for incorporating priors.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset over baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Shows substantial reductions in Normalized Disaggregation Error (NDE) and Signal Aggregate Error (SAE).  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.  
- Convex relaxation is computationally efficient but does not guarantee optimality; the optimality gap is not quantified.  
- Sensitivity to regularization (λ) and slack (δ) is acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Assumes known aggregate values (µi0), which may limit practical applicability; estimating these from data or population statistics is not explored.  
- Recommendation: accept, given novelty, strong results, and clear presentation, with encouragement to address limitations in future work.
