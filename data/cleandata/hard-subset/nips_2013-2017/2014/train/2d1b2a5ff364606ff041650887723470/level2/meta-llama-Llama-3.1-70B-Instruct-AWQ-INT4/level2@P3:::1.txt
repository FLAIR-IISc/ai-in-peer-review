**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper introduces a new approach to blind source separation (BSS) problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem, called signal aggregate constraints (SACs). SACs encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. The authors incorporate SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to formulate the energy disaggregation problems. They employ a convex quadratic program for approximate inference to recover the source signals. The paper evaluates the performance of the proposed approach on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset and shows that it significantly improves the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on BSS, AFHMMs, and energy disaggregation. The authors mention that previous approaches to BSS have focused on constraining the states of the hidden Markov chains, but have not addressed the issue of identifiability. They also mention that previous work on energy disaggregation has used various approaches, including AFAMAP and semi-Markov models, but have not incorporated domain knowledge into the problem.
