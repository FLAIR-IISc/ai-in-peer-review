- Paper addresses identifiability in blind source separation for NILM by adding per-source total (SAC) constraints to AFHMM, yielding AFHMM+SAC and a CQP relaxation for MAP inference.
- Aggregate constraints break Markov structure; authors introduce transition-indicator variables and relax binary/simplex constraints to obtain a tractable convex quadratic program.
- The objective is connected to posterior regularization, situating the method within constrained-learning frameworks.
- Experiments on toy data and the HES low-frequency real dataset show substantial, statistically significant improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP across synthetic and real households.
- Clear, general motivation: per-source totals address common identifiability failures and aid interpretability in energy disaggregation.
- Sound formulation: relaxation leads to concave quadratic plus linear terms with linear constraints; auxiliary variables make relaxation tractable.
- Practical impact: meaningful error reductions on realistic low-frequency NILM data, consistent across many households with significance testing.
- Reasonable computational profile reported: runtimes comparable to AFHMM/AFAMAP at evaluated scales despite using CVX.
- Modular approach: SACs are orthogonal to other constraints (duration, interleaving) and can be combined with them.
- Essential weakness — sensitivity/calibration: robustness to mis-specified µ0 on real data is not quantified; need experiments varying µ0 and reporting performance vs. λ.
- Essential weakness — relaxed-to-discrete decoding: paper omits how discrete sequences/continuous estimates are obtained from fractional solutions and lacks ablation on rounding effects.
- Essential weakness — unmodeled/“other” loads: unclear whether an “other” chain or slack handling exists and how SACs interact with mismatch between modeled appliances and mains.
- Essential weakness — comparative baselines: missing comparisons to HSMMs and interleaved/non-homogeneous FHMMs or justification for their exclusion on HES.
- Optional improvement — interval/robust SACs: squared penalty assumes precise targets; interval/hinge/Huber or Gaussian-variance formulations should be discussed or tested.
- Optional improvement — specialized optimization: CVX may not scale; authors should outline tailored solvers exploiting structure and provide complexity comparisons to AFAMAP.
- Optional improvement — broader/windowed SACs: experimenting with windowed constraints (day/night) would demonstrate generality and practical relevance.
- Quality: technical development and posterior-regularization derivation are sound; empirical evidence is positive but some methodological details need clarification.
- Clarity: generally well-organized but heavy notation around CQP; a compact summary of dimensions/variables and explicit decoding procedure would aid readability.
- Originality: novel combination of aggregate constraints with FHMMs and application to NILM; connection to PR is known but aggregate form is new in this temporal setting.
- Significance: simple, broadly applicable mechanism yielding meaningful gains on realistic data, relevant to BSS and structured prediction communities.
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated idea, solid convex formulation, strong empirical gains, likely adoptable and extensible.
- Cons for acceptance: limited robustness analysis to prior misspecification, missing decoding details, omitted related baselines, scalability concerns due to generic solver reliance.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and practically impactful paper that would be strong with clarifications on decoding, robustness, and baseline comparisons.
