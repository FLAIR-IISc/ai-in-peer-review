The paper "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation" presents a novel approach to blind source separation problems, specifically in the context of energy disaggregation. The authors introduce signal aggregate constraints (SACs) that encourage the total signal for each unknown source to be close to a specified value. This approach is based on the observation that the total signal often varies widely across unknown sources, and we often have a good idea of what total values to expect.

The paper's main claims are:

1. The introduction of SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) improves the performance of the model in energy disaggregation tasks.
2. The use of SACs results in a significant improvement over a recent state-of-the-art approach to the disaggregation problem.
3. The proposed approach can be applied to other blind source separation problems where domain knowledge is available.

The support for these claims is provided through experiments on both simulated and real-world data. The results show that the AFHMM+SAC model outperforms the original AFHMM and the AFAMAP algorithm in terms of normalized disaggregation error (NDE) and signal aggregate error (SAE).

The paper's strengths include:

* The introduction of a novel approach to blind source separation problems that leverages domain knowledge.
* The use of a convex quadratic program for approximate inference, which is computationally efficient.
* The evaluation of the proposed approach on real-world data from the energy disaggregation domain.

However, there are some limitations to the paper:

* The authors assume that the model parameters are known, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.
* The evaluation of the proposed approach is limited to a specific domain (energy disaggregation), and it is not clear how well the approach will generalize to other domains.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and promising approach to blind source separation problems. However, further research is needed to fully explore the potential of this approach and to address the limitations mentioned above.
