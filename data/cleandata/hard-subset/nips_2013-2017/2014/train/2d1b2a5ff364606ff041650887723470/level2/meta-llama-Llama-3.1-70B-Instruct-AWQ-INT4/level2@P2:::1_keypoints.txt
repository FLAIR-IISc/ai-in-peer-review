- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation.
- Integrates SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation.
- Derives a convex quadratic program for approximate inference that is computationally efficient.
- Main contributions: (a) SACs for blind source separation, (b) convex QP for relaxed AFHMM with SACs, (c) real-world energy disaggregation evaluation.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivates the problem and approach.
- Related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of existing methods.
- Technical details are clearly explained and accessible.
- Experimental results are impressive; SACs significantly improve AFHMM performance and outperform a recent state-of-the-art.
- Strength: novel method of incorporating domain knowledge via SACs.
- Strength: strong empirical validation and clear presentation.
- Weakness: assumes model parameters are known, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Weakness: does not compare against other blind source separation approaches.
- Suggestion: provide a detailed computational complexity analysis.
- Suggestion: include comparisons with alternative blind source separation methods.
- Suggestion: investigate SACs in other application domains and expand discussion of limitations and future work.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, effective approach with strong experiments and clear writing.
- Argument against acceptance: practical assumptions (known parameters), missing complexity analysis, and limited comparative evaluation.
