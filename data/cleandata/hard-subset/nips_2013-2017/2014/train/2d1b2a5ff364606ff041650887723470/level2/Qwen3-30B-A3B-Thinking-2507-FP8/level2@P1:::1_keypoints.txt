- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation.  
- Incorporates SACs into an additive factorial HMM and formulates a relaxed convex quadratic program for inference.  
- Shows significant empirical gains (33% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFAMAP) on real-world data with statistical significance.  
- Theoretical foundation strong via connection to posterior regularization.  
- Well-motivated by identifiability issues in energy disaggregation and practical domain knowledge about aggregate totals.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including synthetic validation and real-world benchmarks.  
- Computational efficiency is competitive, with inference times comparable to baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound; convex QP derivation is correct though could use more intuition.  
- Writing and organization are clear; problem statement and experiments are well presented.  
- SACs are original in focusing on aggregate signal values rather than transition/duration constraints.  
- High practical significance given energy conservation relevance and potential applicability to other BSS problems.  
- Weakness: Lack of explicit contrast between SACs and other constraint approaches (e.g., AFAMAP) in terms of knowledge needs and overhead.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to errors in constraint values (μi0) not thoroughly explored on real-world data.  
- Weakness: Does not explore combining SACs with other constraints (e.g., single-state-change) which might further improve results.  
- Weakness: Generalization to multiple time periods is mentioned but not evaluated experimentally.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
