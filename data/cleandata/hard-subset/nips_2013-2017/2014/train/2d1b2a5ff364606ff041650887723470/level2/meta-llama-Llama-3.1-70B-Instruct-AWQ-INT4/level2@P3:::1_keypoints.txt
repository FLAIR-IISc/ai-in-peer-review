- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation (BSS).
- Incorporates SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) for energy disaggregation.
- Uses a convex quadratic program for approximate inference to recover source signals.
- Empirical evaluation on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset shows significant improvement over original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.
- Builds on prior work in BSS, AFHMMs, and energy disaggregation but addresses identifiability by adding domain knowledge.
- Notes prior methods constrained HMM states but did not incorporate aggregate signal constraints.
- Novel contribution: domain-knowledge-based constraint (SACs) applied to BSS problems.
- Strong theoretical foundation: derivation of objective and proof of convexity.
- Thorough empirical evaluation demonstrating effectiveness on real data.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.
- Insufficient detail on hyper-parameter tuning methodology and its effects.
- Comparison to other existing approaches is limited and not comprehensive.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.

- Recommendation: provide more comprehensive comparisons to other methods.
