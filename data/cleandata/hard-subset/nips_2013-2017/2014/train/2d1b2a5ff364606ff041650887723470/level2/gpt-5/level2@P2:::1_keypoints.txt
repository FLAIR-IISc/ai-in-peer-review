- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to penalize deviations of each latent source’s total energy and instantiates this in an AFHMM (AFHMM+SAC).
- Shows MAP inference with non-Markovian SAC penalties can be relaxed to a convex quadratic program (CQP) via auxiliary transition variables.
- Provides a posterior-regularization interpretation of the objective.
- Empirical results on a toy problem and UK HES dataset show substantial, statistically significant improvements in NDE and SAE over AFHMM and AFAMAP with similar runtimes.
- Situates work relative to prior AFHMM/NILM methods and PR/expectation-constraint literature; novelty is targeting sum-over-time constraints in coupled latent time-series and a tractable relaxation.
- Strength: conceptually simple, broadly applicable constraint family that matches practical domain knowledge about total per-source usage.
- Strength: solid mathematical formulation—relaxed objective is concave in variables leading to a tractable CQP; PR derivation strengthens grounding.
- Strength: strong empirical gains on realistic, challenging dataset, including cases with unmodeled loads and significance testing provided.
- Strength: practical use of national averages as SAC targets and robustness to moderate mis-specification in toy study.
- Strength: implementation and runtime claims are reasonable and compared empirically.
- Major weakness: dependence on accuracy of µ0 (target totals); real-data robustness to realistic per-appliance perturbations is insufficiently explored.
- Major weakness: evaluation focuses on energy reconstruction (NDE/SAE) but omits event-detection and temporal localization metrics, leaving segmentation behavior unclear.
- Major weakness: scalability concerns—variable count grows substantially due to H variables, and reliance on CVX may limit large-scale deployment; complexity/memory analysis missing.
- Major weakness: hyperparameters λi fixed to 1 without tuning or guidance; selection and sensitivity analysis are missing.
- Major weakness: modeling choices (3-state k-means, unspecified σt) and presence of unmodeled loads are not deeply analyzed for their interaction with SAC.
- Minor: some notational density in derivations; would benefit from an explicit concavity proposition and mapping from relaxed solutions to evaluated signals.
- Minor: related-work coverage could include other long-horizon constraints in time-series and additional NILM baselines.
- Quality: technical formulation and convexity argument are sound; experiments are carefully executed with significance testing.
- Clarity: generally well written but CQP derivation is dense; solver settings, stopping criteria, and σt/preprocessing details should be clearer.
- Originality: moderately novel—combines known PR ideas with novel sum-over-time constraints and an efficient convex relaxation for AFHMMs.
- Significance: likely useful for NILM and other BSS problems where per-source totals are available; simple to adopt and empirically effective.
- Suggestions: add robustness experiments perturbing NTC, report event-detection/segmentation metrics, explore windowed SACs, provide λ selection guidance, include complexity/solver details, and address unmodeled loads (slack/other component).
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated idea, strong empirical gains, principled optimization and practical applicability.
- Cons for acceptance: limited robustness analysis, missing temporal/event-level evaluation, and scalability/hyperparameter selection not fully addressed.
- Overall: solid, practical contribution that advances AFHMM disaggregation via aggregate priors with a tractable relaxation; leans toward acceptance pending additional robustness and evaluation analyses.
