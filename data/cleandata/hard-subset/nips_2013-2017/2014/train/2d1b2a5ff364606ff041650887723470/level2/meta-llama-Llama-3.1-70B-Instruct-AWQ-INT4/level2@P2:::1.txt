This paper proposes a novel approach to blind source separation problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem through signal aggregate constraints (SACs). SACs encourage the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. The authors incorporate SACs into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) and formulate the energy disaggregation problem. They also derive a convex quadratic program for approximate inference, which is computationally efficient.

The main contributions of this paper are: (a) introducing signal aggregate constraints for blind source separation problems, (b) a convex quadratic program for the relaxed AFHMM with SACs, and (c) an evaluation of the use of SACs on a real-world problem in energy disaggregation.

The paper is well-written and well-organized. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed approach. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of existing approaches to energy disaggregation. The proposed approach is well-motivated and the technical details are clearly explained.

The experimental results are impressive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors show that the use of SACs significantly improves the performance of the AFHMM and outperforms a recent state-of-the-art approach to the disaggregation problem.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to blind source separation problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem through signal aggregate constraints.
* The authors provide a clear and comprehensive introduction to the problem and the proposed approach.
* The technical details are clearly explained and the experimental results are impressive.
* The paper is well-written and well-organized.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the model parameters are known, which may not be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.
* The paper does not provide a comparison with other approaches to blind source separation problems.

Suggestions for improvement:
