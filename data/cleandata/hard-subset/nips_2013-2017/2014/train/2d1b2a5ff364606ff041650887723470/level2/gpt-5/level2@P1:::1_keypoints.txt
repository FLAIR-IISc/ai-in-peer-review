- Paper proposes Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation/NILM, incorporated into an AFHMM (AFHMM+SAC).
- Derives a relaxed convex quadratic program (CQP) using auxiliary transition variables and PSD structure for efficient approximate MAP inference.
- Connects the objective to the posterior regularization framework, giving a probabilistic interpretation.
- Empirical results on UK HES low-frequency data show AFHMM+SAC outperforms vanilla AFHMM and AFAMAP, with reported statistical tests.
- Toy experiments show robustness to moderate mismatch in aggregate priors.
- The optimization derivation is careful: concave objective in relaxed variables and solvable via convex optimization (CVX).
- SACs assume reasonable per-source aggregate priors (µ0); toy and real-data priors (NTC, TTC) are plausible and informative.
- Runtime at evaluated scales is comparable to baselines, but scalability beyond that is not fully characterized.
- Novel contribution: instantiation of global aggregate constraints for factorial HMMs and the specific CQP formulation.
- Potentially broadly applicable beyond NILM (e.g., deconvolution, budget-constrained mixtures).
- Main clarification needed: how fractional Sit from relaxation are rounded/decoded to discrete appliance traces and impact on metrics.
- Hyperparameter concern: λi fixed to 1; need sensitivity analysis and principled selection (cross-validation, uncertainty-weighting).
- Practical deployment question: how to acquire context-aware µ0 (seasonality, weather, occupancy, recent history) beyond national averages.
- Scalability concern: CQP variable count is O(T∑iKi + T∑iKi^2); need runtime vs T and appliance count analysis and specialized solver use.
- Robustness to model misspecification: states chosen by k-means with K=3 may not suit multi-modal/continuous loads; sensitivity to K and σt should be studied.
- Clarity is good overall; add a short algorithmic summary (inputs, outputs, solver, rounding) to aid reproducibility.
- Pros: simple well-motivated idea, sound convex relaxation with probabilistic view, consistent empirical improvements, comparable runtime at tested scales.
- Cons: dependence on aggregate priors, no systematic λi tuning, limited discussion of rounding/decoding and scalability, missing comparisons to additional duration-constrained baselines.
- Key questions posed: handling fractional Sit, sensitivity to λi and K, treatment of unmodeled appliances in mains data, runtime scaling and solver options, and testing time-of-day SACs on HES.
- Suggested improvements: learn/adapt µ0 from context or model uncertainty, release code and reproducible example, combine SACs with duration models or AFAMAP constraints, and explore automatic rounding (e.g., Viterbi projection).
- Overall evaluation: solid, novel, and practically useful contribution recommending acceptance contingent on clarifying decoding, λi sensitivity, and scalability details.
