- Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation with AFHMMs.  
- SACs force each source’s total signal to be close to a domain-informed value, addressing identifiability in BSS.  
- Formulates SACs as a constrained optimization and converts it into a convex quadratic program for efficient inference.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. AFHMM and 29% improvement vs. AFAMAP.  
- Statistical significance of improvements confirmed via paired t-tests.  
- Novel and well-motivated approach that incorporates domain knowledge practically into BSS.  
- Technical derivation is sound and computationally efficient due to convex formulation.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, uses real-world energy data, and is convincing.  
- Properly situates work within related literature on posterior regularization and constraint-based HMMs.  
- Main limitation: reliance on domain knowledge for constraint values (µi0), which may not always be available.  
- Suggestion (not explored): discuss or evaluate strategies to estimate constraint values (e.g., historical data, transfer learning).  
- Does not analyze interactions between SACs and other constraint methods.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, well-executed contribution with recommendation for acceptance; minor limitation does not undermine significance.
