- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation, applied to energy disaggregation with AFHMMs to encourage source totals to match specified values.  
- Incorporates SACs into AFHMM and derives a convex quadratic program for approximate inference.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs. baseline AFHMM and 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Provides theoretical grounding by connecting SACs to posterior regularization.  
- Novelty: SACs target aggregate signal values across time, unlike prior AFHMM constraints that focus on temporal structure (e.g., state transitions).  
- Technical contribution is strong with clear mathematical formulation and inference derivation.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using synthetic data for robustness checks and real-world datasets for practical validation.  
- Results include appropriate statistical validation (paired t-tests) and clear presentation of metrics (NDE and SAE) with figures/tables.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with problem setting and model formulation well-defined.  
- Significance is high: approach is practical for energy disaggregation and generalizable to other blind source separation tasks with known/estimable aggregates.  
- Limitation: reliance on accurate SAC values (µi0) and limited discussion on how to obtain them when prior knowledge is scarce.  
- Suggestion: discuss systematic methods for estimating SAC values (e.g., small labeled datasets or transfer learning) when surveys/experts are unavailable.  
- Minor suggestion: provide a clearer computational complexity/time comparison with AFHMM and AFAMAP for practitioner guidance.
