This paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and instantiates them in an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM+SAC) for energy disaggregation. The central claims are: (1) aggregate priors on source totals (e.g., appliance energy over a day) resolve identifiability in underdetermined mixtures; (2) MAP inference with SACs can be solved via a convex quadratic program (CQP) relaxation using auxiliary transition variables; (3) on a large, real, low-frequency dataset (HES), SACs yield substantial improvements over AFHMM and AFAMAP (Kolter & Jaakkola, AISTATS’12). The method is related to posterior regularization (Ganchev et al., JMLR’10), to which the authors provide a clean equivalence derivation.

Quality: The technical formulation is sound; the relaxation yields a PSD quadratic objective with linear constraints, and the posterior-regularization derivation is correct. Empirically, improvements are sizable (≈45% NDE over AFHMM; ≈29% over AFAMAP), with paired t-tests across households. However, key details are missing for reproducibility: how fractional solutions are used or rounded; sensitivity to λ; how µ0 (NTC) is estimated and its variance; and solver settings/initialization. The supervised estimation of appliance HMMs and fixed 3-state assumption are pragmatic but under-justified.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written, with clear notation and metrics (NDE, SAE). Some references are incomplete, and the evaluation section would benefit from more methodological detail.

Originality: Using aggregate constraints in factorial models is a simple yet novel and practically useful idea; relating it to PR is appropriate. The CQP construction resembles AFAMAP’s convex relaxation but targets a different prior; novelty is moderate but meaningful.

Significance: The approach is likely to be used by practitioners in NILM and potentially other BSS settings where totals are known or estimable. Improvements on low-frequency mains data are valuable for deployment.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, general idea with strong empirical gains on challenging real data.
- Solid connection to posterior regularization and convex optimization.
- Practical relevance to NILM and broader BSS.

Cons for acceptance:
- Dependence on accurate µ0; limited robustness analysis beyond toy study.
- Missing details on rounding/integrality and λ selection; potential interpretability issues from fractional states.
- Scalability concerns with generic CVX; no specialized solver or complexity study.

Constructive suggestions:
- Report sensitivity to λ and mis-specified µ0; consider interval/bounded SACs or learned hierarchical priors on totals.
- Describe or add a principled rounding scheme to recover discrete states and assess impact on interpretability.
- Provide ablations (AFHMM+SAC with chainwise Viterbi vs CQP), and scaling experiments in I, T, Ki; consider an ADMM-based solver.
- Compare to duration-constrained/semi-Markov baselines and to higher-frequency datasets (e.g., REDD).
- Clarify training/test splits and NTC estimation; complete references.
