- Problem: single-channel blind source separation (BSS) where an observed time series is a sum of hidden source signals; unidentifiability is the core issue.
- Proposal: introduce Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to encode prior knowledge about each source’s total value over a time period.
- Method: add a quadratic penalty on aggregate mismatch to the AFHMM MAP objective, breaking Markov property.
- Inference: derive a convex relaxation of the constrained MAP into a convex quadratic program (CQP) solvable efficiently.
- Evaluation: applied to energy disaggregation on a large household electricity dataset, showing substantial gains.
- Empirical result: AFHMM+SAC yields a 29% reduction in normalized disaggregation error versus a strong recent baseline (AFAMAP).
- Strength — originality/simplicity: SACs are novel, intuitive, and connect to posterior regularization.
- Strength — significance: tackles a practical, challenging problem with results that demonstrate real-world impact and general applicability.
- Strength — technical quality: sound formulation, clear relaxation to CQP, and thorough experiments (toy, oracle, real data).
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and organized.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: penalty λi fixed to 1; need discussion/sensitivity analysis and guidance for setting λi in practice.
- Weakness — quality of priors: method depends on accurate aggregate priors (µi0); limitations for households deviating from national averages should be discussed.
- Weakness — computational complexity: claim of polynomial-time CQP and similar runtime to baselines needs a more formal complexity analysis (scaling with T, I, K).
- Weakness — presentation: Section 5’s CQP notation is dense; add intuitive explanations for new variables (e.g., Hit) and Kronecker products.
- Overall: a high-quality, novel, and effective contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
