- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation (BSS).
- Proposes an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) augmented with SACs to enforce total-signal priors for unknown sources.
- Provides clear problem setup, model description, and motivation for SACs.
- Mathematical derivations are sound and the optimization reduces to a convex quadratic program with detailed analysis.
- Paper is well-written and explanations are clear and concise.
- Extensive evaluation on simulated and real-world data demonstrates AFHMM+SAC outperforms AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art on normalized disaggregation error and signal aggregate error.
- Strength: Novel introduction of SACs as a new approach to BSS.
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis and correct mathematical treatment.
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical evaluation across datasets.
- Weakness: Assumes model parameters are known, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Weakness: No comparison to other constraint-based methods (e.g., posterior regularization).
- Overall contribution is significant to BSS and energy disaggregation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address complexity analysis and comparisons to related constrained approaches.
