- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to incorporate domain knowledge into blind source separation for AFHMM-based energy disaggregation.  
- Core idea: approximate total energy per appliance type improves disaggregation identifiability.  
- Proposes a convex quadratic program for approximate inference with SACs.  
- Empirical results show substantial performance gains on a real-world HES dataset, outperforming AFHMM and state-of-the-art AFAMAP.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presents the problem, and connects SACs to posterior regularization.  
- Claims computational efficiency comparable to existing methods, important for practicality.  
- No discussion of how hyperparameter λ (or δ) is selected or tuned; lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Comparison to probabilistic databases is cursory; needs more detailed comparison of aggregate constraint types.  
- Related work section could be broadened (e.g., recent sparsity-inducing priors for energy disaggregation).  
- Derivation of the convex QP is concise but would benefit from more intermediate steps; notation is dense in Section 5.  
- Lacks in-depth analysis of failure cases—unclear which appliance types or patterns cause poor performance.  
- Heavy reliance on the HES dataset; results on additional (including synthetic) datasets needed for generalizability.  
- Scalability claim unsupported by runtime numbers across dataset sizes; a scalability analysis is missing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical gains, clear exposition, and theoretical grounding.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited hyperparameter discussion, superficial related work, dense mathematics, and missing failure/scalability analyses.
