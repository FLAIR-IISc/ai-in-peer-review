- Problem: Underdetermined blind source separation (single aggregate time series from multiple unknown sources) is ill-posed due to identifiability issues.  
- Main contribution: Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) to incorporate known/estimated total aggregate values of each source over a period.  
- Application: Applies SACs to energy disaggregation, separating household mains into individual appliance consumption.  
- Model: Integrates SACs into an Additive Factorial HMM to form AFHMM+SAC.  
- Inference challenge: SACs are non-Markovian and couple all time steps, making exact MAP inference intractable.  
- Solution: Relaxed MAP inference to a convex quadratic program (CQP) that can be solved efficiently.  
- Theory: Provides a connection between the penalized objective and posterior regularization.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on a toy dataset and the real-world HES dataset, including synthetic aggregates and true mains readings.  
- Baselines and results: Compared to AFHMM and AFAMAP (plus an oracle), showing large, statistically significant reductions in point-wise and total-energy errors.  
- Strength — Novelty: SACs are simple, elegant, broadly applicable, and complement existing constraints to address identifiability.  
- Strength — Technical quality: Sound formulation and principled relaxation to CQP with theoretical grounding.  
- Strength — Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments demonstrating robustness to noisy constraints and strong real-world performance.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, motivations and experiments are clear.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Regularization weights λi fixed to 1; need discussion or sensitivity analysis and guidance for setting them in practice.  
- Weakness — Training clarity: Unclear description of how HMM parameters are estimated; appears supervised—should clarify training assumptions and unsupervised prospects.  
- Weakness — Relation to other constraints: Should discuss compatibility or combination of SACs with local temporal constraints like AFAMAP’s “one chain change” constraint.  
- Weakness — Generalization: Briefly mentions temporally segmented SACs (e.g., day/night); expanding this idea and use cases would strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: Strong, practical contribution with sound methods and convincing results; minor clarifications and analyses would further improve the work.
