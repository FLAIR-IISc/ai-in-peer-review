- Contribution: Introduces Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) for blind source separation (BSS), applied to energy disaggregation.
- Contribution: Formulates a convex quadratic program for MAP inference in AFHMM+SAC.
- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates large improvements over baselines on synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: SACs address identifiability in BSS and are a timely contribution to non-intrusive load monitoring.
- Strength: Convex quadratic program derivation is rigorous and connected to posterior regularization.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments show significant NDE and SAE improvements (45% vs AFHMM, 29% vs SOTA).
- Strength: Paper is well-organized with clear math and reproducible experimental protocols.
- Weakness: Generality claim of SACs is under-supported because experiments are limited to energy disaggregation.
- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (e.g., λi set to 1 without exploration).
- Weakness: Computational complexity and scalability of the QP approach are not thoroughly analyzed for large/high-dimensional data.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations, including reliance on accurate prior SAC values and potential noise sensitivity.
- Recommendation: Paper is high-quality, convincing, and should be accepted, but would benefit from broader evaluation and deeper analysis of sensitivity and scalability.
- Pro arguments: Novel, impactful, theoretically solid, empirically validated, and clearly written.
- Con arguments: Limited cross-domain evaluation and insufficient discussion of parameter sensitivity and scalability.
- Overall rating: Strong Accept.
