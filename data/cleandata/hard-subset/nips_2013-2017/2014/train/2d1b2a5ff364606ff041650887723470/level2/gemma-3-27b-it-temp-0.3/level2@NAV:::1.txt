## Review of "Signal Aggregate Constraints in Additive Factorial HMMs, with Application to Energy Disaggregation"

This paper addresses the challenging problem of blind source separation, specifically within the context of energy disaggregation. The authors identify the inherent unidentifiability of these problems and propose a novel approach – Signal Aggregate Constraints (SACs) – to incorporate domain knowledge by encouraging the total signal for each source to align with expected values. They integrate SACs into an Additive Factorial Hidden Markov Model (AFHMM) and develop a convex quadratic program for approximate inference. The method is evaluated on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset, demonstrating significant improvements over both the baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach.

The core idea of leveraging SACs is compelling and addresses a key limitation of standard BSS techniques. The observation that prior knowledge about total energy consumption exists and can be effectively utilized is insightful. The formulation of the problem as a convex quadratic program is a significant contribution, enabling efficient inference. The experimental results are convincing, showcasing a substantial 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to the original AFHMM and a 29% improvement over a strong baseline. The authors clearly articulate the contributions and relate their work to existing literature in BSS, energy disaggregation, and probabilistic modeling.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations. While the authors acknowledge the identifiability problem, a deeper exploration of scenarios where SACs might be ineffective or misleading would be valuable. For example, how robust are the results to inaccurate prior estimates of the total signal? Furthermore, the choice of the tuning parameter δ (or equivalently, λ) in the SAC is not thoroughly discussed; a sensitivity analysis or justification for the chosen value would strengthen the work. The connection to posterior regularization, while interesting, feels somewhat tacked on and could be more deeply integrated into the discussion.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and well-motivated approach to address a fundamental challenge in BSS.
*   Strong experimental results on a real-world dataset, demonstrating significant improvements.
*   Clear and concise writing, with a good overview of related work.
*   Efficient and scalable inference via convex quadratic programming.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of the limitations and potential failure modes of SACs.
*   Insufficient justification for the choice of the regularization parameter.
*   The connection to posterior regularization feels somewhat superficial.

**Overall:**

Despite minor weaknesses, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of energy disaggregation and BSS. The proposed SACs offer a practical and effective way to incorporate domain knowledge, leading to significant performance gains. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations and parameter selection discussion. The idea is strong and has the potential to be impactful in this area.
