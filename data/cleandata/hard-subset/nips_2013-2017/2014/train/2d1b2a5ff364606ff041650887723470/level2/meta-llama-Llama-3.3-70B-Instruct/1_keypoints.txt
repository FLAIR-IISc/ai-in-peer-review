- Introduces SACs (signal aggregate constraints) integrated into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to improve blind source separation (BSS).  
- SACs encourage each unknown source’s total signal to be close to a specified value, injecting domain knowledge.  
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and provides a concise treatment of problem, related work, and proposed method.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset support the main claims.  
- SAC-enhanced AFHMM reduces normalized disaggregation error by 45% versus original AFHMM and by 29% versus a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Figures and tables effectively illustrate results and support claims.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of strengths and weaknesses and show strong understanding of underlying theory.  
- Originality is high: SACs are a novel and useful contribution to BSS.  
- Significance is high: SACs have potential to improve BSS performance across applications.  
- Limitation: assumes total signal for each source is close to a specified value, which may not hold in practice.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, which could impact practicality.  
- Limitation: does not compare against a broader set of state-of-the-art BSS approaches, limiting evaluation scope.
