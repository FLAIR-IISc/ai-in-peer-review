- Paper introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) for blind source separation and an AFHMM+SAC instantiation for energy disaggregation.
- Central claim: aggregate priors on source totals resolve identifiability in underdetermined mixtures.
- Central claim: MAP inference with SACs can be relaxed to a convex quadratic program (CQP) using auxiliary transition variables.
- Central claim: On the HES low-frequency dataset, SACs yield large empirical gains (~45% NDE over AFHMM; ~29% over AFAMAP) with paired t-tests across households.
- Relation: The method is correctly derived as equivalent to posterior regularization (PR).
- Technical quality: Formulation is sound; relaxation gives a PSD quadratic objective with linear constraints and a correct PR derivation.
- Reproducibility issues: Missing details on how fractional CQP solutions are used/rounded to discrete states.
- Reproducibility issues: No sensitivity analysis reported for the regularization weight λ.
- Reproducibility issues: Unclear how µ0 (NTC) and its variance are estimated.
- Reproducibility issues: Solver settings, initialization, and implementation details (e.g., CVX usage) are not provided.
- Modeling choices: Supervised estimation of appliance HMMs and fixed 3-state assumption are pragmatic but under-justified.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written with clear notation and metrics, but some references are incomplete and evaluation lacks methodological detail.
- Originality: Using aggregate constraints in factorial models is simple, moderately novel, and practically useful; CQP resembles AFAMAP’s relaxation but targets a different prior.
- Significance: Likely to be useful in NILM and other BSS settings where totals are known or estimable, especially for low-frequency mains data.
- Pros for acceptance: clear, general idea with strong empirical gains; solid PR and convex optimization connections; practical relevance to deployment.
- Cons for acceptance: Dependence on accurate µ0 and limited robustness analysis; missing rounding/λ selection details and interpretability concerns from fractional states; scalability concerns with generic CVX and no complexity study.
- Constructive suggestions: Report sensitivity to λ and µ0 mis-specification; consider interval/bounded SACs or hierarchical priors.
- Constructive suggestions: Provide a principled rounding scheme and assess its impact on interpretability.
- Constructive suggestions: Add ablations (e.g., chainwise Viterbi vs CQP), scaling experiments (I, T, Ki), and consider an ADMM-based solver.
- Constructive suggestions: Compare to duration-constrained/semi-Markov baselines and higher-frequency datasets (e.g., REDD), and clarify training/test splits and NTC estimation; complete references.
