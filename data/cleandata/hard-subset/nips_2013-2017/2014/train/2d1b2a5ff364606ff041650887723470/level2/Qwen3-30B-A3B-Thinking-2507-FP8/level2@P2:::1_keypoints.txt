- Proposes signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation (BSS) by forcing each source's total signal toward a specified value.  
- Applies SACs to energy disaggregation via an additive factorial HMM (AFHMM) and solves approximate inference with a convex quadratic program.  
- Reports strong empirical gains: 45% reduction in normalized disaggregation error vs AFHMM and 29% improvement over a recent state-of-the-art on real data.  
- Strength: tackles a practical identifiability problem in energy disaggregation with a simple, effective approach and clear mathematical formulation.  
- Strength: computationally efficient solution and thorough experiments (synthetic and real-world) with proper statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for why SACs are better or different than other constraint-based methods (e.g., AFAMAP).  
- Weakness: claim that total signals vary widely across sources in many BSS applications is unsubstantiated beyond the energy domain.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to constraint values μi0 is not adequately explored in real experiments; reliance on oracle "true total consumption" (TTC) is unrealistic.  
- Weakness: connection to posterior regularization is interesting but feels tacked on and not integrated into the main narrative.  
- Weakness: overstatement of SACs' generality across BSS domains without supporting evidence beyond energy disaggregation.  
- Clarity issue: introduction and motivation for why identifiability matters could be more concise for end users; Section 5 math is dense and needs more intuition.  
- Suggestion: provide a detailed comparison with other constraint-based approaches (e.g., AFAMAP) to clarify novelty.  
- Suggestion: include a sensitivity analysis showing performance as μi0 deviates from true values in real-world experiments.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion or give examples of how SACs might generalize to other BSS applications.  
- Suggestion: integrate the SACs–posterior regularization relationship into the main text rather than as an addendum.  
- Suggestion: address practical methods for obtaining accurate constraint values in real settings instead of relying on TTC.  
- Overall: solid empirical contribution to energy disaggregation but theoretical justification and claims of general applicability are weak; needs better comparison and substantiation to strengthen the paper.
