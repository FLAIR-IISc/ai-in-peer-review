This paper addresses the challenging problem of blind source separation, specifically in the context of energy disaggregation. The authors propose incorporating signal aggregate constraints (SACs) into an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) to improve performance. The core idea—leveraging prior knowledge about the total energy consumption of appliances—is intuitive and well-motivated. The paper presents a convex quadratic program (CQP) for approximate inference under these constraints and demonstrates significant improvements over both the baseline AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach on a real-world energy disaggregation dataset.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Intuition:** The introduction of SACs is a novel and intuitively appealing way to incorporate domain knowledge into the BSS problem. It directly addresses the identifiability issue by leveraging realistic constraints on total energy consumption.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results on the HES dataset are compelling. The reported 45% decrease in normalized disaggregation error compared to the original AFHMM and 29% improvement over the recent state-of-the-art method demonstrate a significant advance.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The formulation of the problem as a CQP is well-explained and provides a computationally tractable solution. Connecting the approach to posterior regularization strengthens the theoretical foundation.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly structured. The problem statement, proposed method, and experimental setup are all easy to understand.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The authors acknowledge and demonstrate that the computational complexity of their approach is comparable to existing methods (AFHMM, AFAMAP), which is important for practical applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Prior Work:** While the paper references related work in energy disaggregation and BSS, the discussion feels somewhat limited. For instance, the connection to work on constrained HMMs (specifically k-segment constraints [19]) could be expanded, exploring potential synergies or differences.  The paper states that previous approaches have not sufficiently considered *what* domain knowledge to include, but doesn't fully address how SACs specifically compare to other possible prior choices within those frameworks (e.g. [4], [14], [13]).
* **Sensitivity to Constraint Values (µi0):** The results in Section 7.1 show that performance is sensitive to the accuracy of the constraint values (µi0).  While the authors acknowledge this, a more in-depth discussion of strategies for obtaining reliable estimates of these values in real-world scenarios would be beneficial. The use of national averages (NTC) is a reasonable starting point, but its limitations should be further explored.  
* **Assumption of Known Parameters (θ):** The paper assumes that the model parameters (θ) are known. This is a strong assumption and may not hold in many practical applications. Discussing the impact of parameter estimation errors on the performance of the AFHMM+SAC would enhance the robustness of the findings.
* **Dataset Specificity:** The experiments are primarily conducted on the HES dataset. While this is a valuable dataset, it would be beneficial to evaluate the approach on additional datasets with different characteristics to assess its generalizability.
* **Impact of δi parameter:** The role of the tuning parameter δi is not fully explored. A more detailed analysis of how different values of δi affect performance would be useful for practitioners.  

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to address a challenging problem in blind source separation.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods.
* The theoretical framework is sound, and the CQP formulation provides a computationally tractable solution.
* The clarity of the presentation and the discussion of computational efficiency contribute to the overall quality of the work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The discussion of related work could be more comprehensive.
* The sensitivity to constraint values (µi0) needs to be addressed more thoroughly.
* The assumption of known model parameters (θ) limits the practical applicability of the approach.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of blind source separation and energy disaggregation. While there are some minor weaknesses, the novelty of the approach, the strong empirical results, and the sound theoretical justification outweigh these concerns. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly expanding the related work discussion and providing more guidance on obtaining reliable constraint values in practice.
