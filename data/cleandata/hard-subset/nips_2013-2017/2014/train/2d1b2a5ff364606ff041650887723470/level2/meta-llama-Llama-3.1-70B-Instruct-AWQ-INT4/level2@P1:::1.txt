This paper introduces a novel approach to blind source separation (BSS) problems by incorporating domain knowledge into the problem, called signal aggregate constraints (SACs). The authors propose an additive factorial hidden Markov model (AFHMM) with SACs, which encourages the total signal for each of the unknown sources to be close to a specified value. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the problem, including a clear explanation of the BSS problem, the AFHMM model, and the introduction of SACs.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The mathematical derivations are sound, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the optimization problem and its solution using a convex quadratic program.

The paper also provides an extensive evaluation of the proposed approach using both simulated and real-world data. The results show that the AFHMM+SAC outperforms the original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art approach in terms of normalized disaggregation error and signal aggregate error.
