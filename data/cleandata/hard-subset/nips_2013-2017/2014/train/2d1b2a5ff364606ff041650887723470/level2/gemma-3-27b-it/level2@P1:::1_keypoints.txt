- Introduces signal aggregate constraints (SACs) to improve identifiability in blind source separation for AFHMM-based energy disaggregation.  
- Formulates SAC-based approximate inference as a convex quadratic program (CQP) and implements it using CVX.  
- Demonstrates substantial performance gains on the real-world HES dataset, outperforming original AFHMM and a recent state-of-the-art.  
- Well-motivated problem statement that highlights identifiability challenges and the practicality of known total appliance energy.  
- Frames SACs within posterior regularization, connecting the method to broader probabilistic modeling.  
- Mathematical derivation of SACs and the CQP is sound; relaxation for efficiency is justified and clearly presented.  
- Includes both simulated and real-world experiments; HES results are particularly compelling.  
- Ablation study on µ0 choices for SACs provides useful insight into setting constraint parameters.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and intuition about why the convex relaxation preserves solution quality.  
- Weakness: does not explore generalizing constraints across multiple time periods, which could be valuable.  
- Weakness: related work omits recent deep learning approaches to energy disaggregation.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies heavily on the HES dataset; more dataset diversity (even synthetic) would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: arbitrary choice of three states per appliance without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Recommendation: paper is a valuable contribution and merits acceptance with minor revisions to address listed weaknesses.
