- Problem: learning the full DPP kernel L is NP-hard and prior methods restrict learning (scalar weights or fixed-kernel ensembles), sacrificing diversity modeling.  
- Contribution: novel EM algorithm that learns the full kernel by reparameterizing with eigenvalues/eigenvectors of the marginal kernel K and lower-bounding the likelihood via Jensen’s inequality.  
- Technical approach: E-step shows the conditional is a k-DPP; M-step yields closed-form eigenvalue updates and efficient eigenvector updates with Stiefel manifold optimization to handle non-convex constraints.  
- Avoids degeneracy: method eliminates the need for projection steps used in gradient ascent (K-Ascent), preventing near-diagonal kernels that lose negative interactions.  
- Runtime: favorable complexity O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) versus K-Ascent’s O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3), assuming k ≪ N.  
- Experiments: on a 29k-example baby registry dataset, EM achieves a 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood for the "safety" category and an average 3.7% gain across 13 categories.  
- Diversity preservation: empirical results show EM maintains meaningful negative interactions while K-Ascent learns redundant, near-diagonal kernels.  
- Originality: fills a gap in the literature by enabling full kernel learning without restrictive parametric assumptions; prior NIPS work focused on restricted or application-specific approaches.  
- Significance: method has clear practical value for recommendation systems and potential applicability to summarization, sensor placement, and image search where diversity matters.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-organized with clear exposition, a clean algorithm presentation, and a detailed supplement.  
- Weakness — conclusion: conclusion understates the contribution and should explicitly emphasize resolving prior full-kernel learning limitations and practical impact.  
- Weakness — comparisons: additional empirical comparison to [8]’s Bayesian approach and [3]’s L-ensemble would better contextualize advantages.  
- Weakness — scalability caveat: runtime analysis depends on k ≪ N and should acknowledge potential limitations for very large sets.  
- Overall assessment: technically rigorous, experimentally validated, and impactful contribution that avoids prior degeneracy issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
