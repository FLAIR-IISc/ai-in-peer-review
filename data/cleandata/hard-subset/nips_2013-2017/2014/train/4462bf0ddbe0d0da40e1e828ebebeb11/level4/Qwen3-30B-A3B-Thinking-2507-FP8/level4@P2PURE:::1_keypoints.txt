- Paper introduces an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices that outperforms standard projected gradient (K-Ascent, KA).  
- Key insight: represent any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" and introduce a variational distribution over subsets.  
- Algorithm alternates between updating the variational distribution (a k-DPP) and single gradient steps in the M-step.  
- Empirical results: EM significantly outperforms KA under naive initialization, and marginally outperforms KA with informed initialization.  
- M-step is technically involved and requires caveats/approximations, though the implementation succeeds.  
- Reviewer requests more experiments on initialization effects, e.g., initializing EM from KA solutions.  
- Criticism of metric choice: using relative log-likelihoods (ratios of log probabilities) complicates interpretation; simple differences in log probabilities are preferred.  
- Minor stylistic criticisms: calling the algorithm "elegant" is unwarranted given caveats, and bolding in the introduction is distracting.  
- Overall: novel and solid contribution to kernel learning in DPPs, with modest improvements over baseline and reasonable experimental validation.
