- Problem: learning the full unconstrained DPP kernel L is NP-hard and prior methods use restrictive parameterizations that sacrifice modeling diversity for quality.

- Main contribution: a novel EM algorithm that learns the full L by optimizing eigenvalues and eigenvectors separately via the eigendecomposition L = VΛVᵀ.

- Key technical insight: reframe the orthonormal eigenvector constraint as an unconstrained optimization on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding problematic projection steps.

- Derivation: EM obtained by applying Jensen’s inequality to a lower bound on the log-likelihood; includes proof that the inverse distribution is also a DPP.

- Novelty/significance: enables learning a full kernel that captures both item quality (eigenvalues) and diversity (eigenvectors), addressing a major bottleneck for practical DPP use.

- Technical soundness: derivations and proofs are clear and well-justified; the Stiefel-manifold update is elegant and effective.

- Clarity/presentation: paper is cohesive, well-written, and makes the complex derivation accessible.

- Experimental validation: synthetic and product-recommendation experiments show the method retains quality while improving diversity, outperforming the KA baseline.

- Practical impact nuance: real-world improvement is modest (e.g., replacing two items in the top-10) but the substitutions increase diversity in a meaningful way.

- Weakness: limited evaluation on real-world, larger-scale tasks where diversity matters (e.g., summarization, search diversification).

- Weakness: conclusion is brief and does not fully summarize the paper’s technical contributions.

- Overall assessment: strong, principled, and technically solid paper that advances learning unconstrained DPP kernels and broadens practical applicability.
