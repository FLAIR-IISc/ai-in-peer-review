- Paper targets learning kernel matrices for DPPs; main contribution is an EM reformulation that outperforms the standard K-Ascent baseline in some settings.  
- EM idea: represent any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs, place a variational distribution over subsets, and alternate E-step (k-DPP update) with an M-step (single gradient steps).  
- Empirically, the EM method yields improvements over projected gradient (K-Ascent), though gains are not large.  
- The M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats and approximations, which is somewhat unsatisfying.  
- Experiments lack analysis of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM from KA results).  
- The paper’s use of "relative log likelihood" is unclear; reviewer asks why ratios of log probabilities are used instead of log-probability differences.  
- Writing/style issues: overly flattering language, unnecessary bold text, and some annoying phrasing.  
- Overall: interesting and promising approach; reviewer leans toward accept pending improved experiments and clearer writing.
