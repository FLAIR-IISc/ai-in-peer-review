- DPPs model diversity over a ground set and learning the kernel L is conjectured NP-hard, so prior work used restrictive parameterizations or partial learning.  
- The paper proposes an EM-based learning method operating on L’s eigenvalues and eigenvectors to avoid those restrictions.  
- Naive gradient ascent requires projection that can harm DPP diversity; the EM approach eliminates the need for such problematic projections.  
- The EM formulation leverages eigendecomposition and Jensen’s inequality to construct a lower-bound objective and effective optimization.  
- Optimization is simplified by exploiting the full-rank eigenvector matrix and mapping constraints to the Stiefel manifold to avoid eigenvector projection.  
- The authors show the resulting inverse distribution is itself a DPP, a noteworthy theoretical result.  
- Detailed derivations and step-by-step reduction to the EM algorithm are clear and well-written.  
- Empirical evaluation on a product recommendation task shows the EM method preserves both quality and diversity and outperforms projected gradient (KA).  
- Differences between EM and KA recommendations are sometimes subtle (e.g., only a couple items swapped in top-10 lists).  
- The paper is clearly written, mathematically rigorous, and the proofs are well-presented.  
- The conclusion could better summarize the paper’s contributions.  
- Experiments are compelling but limited in scope; more diverse real-world datasets would strengthen generality claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of EM approach, technical soundness (Stiefel manifold justification), clarity, and supporting empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: gains over baseline are modest and experimental scope is limited.
