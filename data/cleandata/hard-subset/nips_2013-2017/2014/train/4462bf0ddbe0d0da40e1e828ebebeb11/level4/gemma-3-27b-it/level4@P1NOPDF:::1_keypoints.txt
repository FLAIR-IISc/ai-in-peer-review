- Paper reformulates DPP kernel learning within an EM framework, offering an alternative to direct gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).
- Key idea: represent any DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and instantiate a variational distribution over subsets using a k-DPP.
- Algorithm alternates between updating the k-DPP variational distribution (E-step) and taking a gradient step for the kernel (M-step).
- Empirical results: EM consistently outperforms KA with naive initialization and gives modest gains even with informed KA initialization.
- M-step is computationally complex and relies on caveats/approximations, though the authors still achieve working results.
- Reviewer requests more analysis of initialization effects, specifically trying EM initialized from KA solutions.
- Reviewer questions the choice of reporting “relative log likelihood” instead of plain differences in log probabilities, finding the presented numbers hard to interpret.
- Minor writing/style criticisms: avoid self-descriptive terms like “elegant” for the algorithm and remove unnecessary bolding in the introduction.
- Overall judgment: interesting and solid contribution, but experimental validation could be stronger and performance gains over basic KA are modest.
