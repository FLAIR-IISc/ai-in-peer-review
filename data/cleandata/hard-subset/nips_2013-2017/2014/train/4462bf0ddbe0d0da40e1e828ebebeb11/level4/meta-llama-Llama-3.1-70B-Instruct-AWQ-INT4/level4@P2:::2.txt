The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes. The method combines several nontrivial ingredients: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality. The experiments show favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method is well-founded, and it is believable that the methods should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), so it is not clear how much we benefit from the DPP approach. Additionally, the experiments could be strengthened by providing more detailed analysis of the results, such as error bars or statistical significance tests.

Clarity: The paper is not particularly clearly written. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes the definition comes after using it. The writing is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, mostly because the text is structured to describe a process of how one ends up with the finally proposed approach, with side-remarks of possible alternative approaches and problems interleaved with the main thread. It would be better to present the developed method as clearly as possible and postpone discussion to a later stage.

Originality: The paper looks quite original, and I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: I think the method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset selection literature.

Details:

* On page 2, line 81, the marginal kernel K is not defined before use. What kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel? It would be helpful to provide a clear definition or reference to a previous work that defines it.
* On page 2, line 100, V is not defined. It would be better to define it explicitly or provide a reference to a previous work that defines it.
* On page 3, line 159, "minimizes the change to Frobenius norm" is unclearly stated. It would be better to say "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q".
* On page 4, I found this section hard to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would be nice to outline your (simpler) approach directly so that the reader is not forced to look up the more complex original method.
* On page 4, line 189, what does "weight" refer to? It would be helpful to provide a clear definition or explanation.
* On page 7, line 324, I don't quite get the rationale in (30). Why is m_ij not used here? Why would simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not have worked as initialization? It would be helpful to provide a clear explanation or justification for this choice.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and original method for learning Determinantal Point Processes.
* The method combines several nontrivial ingredients and provides a solid advance in subset selection literature.
* The experiments show favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The evaluation of the method could be improved by providing more detailed analysis of the results and comparing against alternative approaches for subset selection.
* The paper is not particularly clearly written, and the write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow.
* Some notation is not defined, and sometimes the definition comes after using it.
