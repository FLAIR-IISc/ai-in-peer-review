This paper presents a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning the full kernel matrix of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP). The method is technically sophisticated, combining several non-trivial components: it operates on the eigendecomposition of the kernel, treats the selection of eigenvectors as a hidden variable, lower-bounds the log-likelihood using Jensen's inequality, and performs optimization on the Stiefel manifold for the eigenvector updates. The experiments demonstrate that this EM approach outperforms a more direct projected gradient ascent baseline ("K-Ascent"), particularly in terms of robustness and runtime.

**Quality**
The proposed method appears to be technically sound, and the derivation of the EM algorithm is plausible. However, the experimental evaluation could be improved. The experiments are quite limited, comparing only against a simplified DPP learning baseline. The paper does not include comparisons to alternative, non-DPP-based approaches for diverse subset selection (such as those mentioned in the introduction). This makes it difficult to assess the broader benefit of the DPP approach itself for these tasks, beyond the specific algorithmic improvements for learning the kernel.

**Clarity**
The paper is not clearly written and is difficult to follow. The presentation is somewhat disorganized, with key notation used before it is defined. The writing is at times unnecessarily convoluted, largely because the text is structured to describe the process of deriving the proposed method, with side-remarks and discussions of alternative approaches interleaved with the main thread. The paper would be significantly more accessible if the final algorithm were presented clearly and completely first, with the derivation and discussion postponed to a later section.

**Originality**
The approach appears to be highly original. Learning the full, unconstrained kernel for a DPP is a known hard problem, and to my knowledge, this is the first work to propose an EM-style algorithm based on the kernel's eigendecomposition.

**Significance**
The method developed in this paper represents a solid technical advance and a potentially useful contribution to the subset selection literature. By providing a more robust and efficient way to learn a full DPP kernel from data, this work could enable wider practical application of DPPs.

**Detailed Comments for Improvement**

*   On page 2, the marginal kernel `K` is used in Equation (3), but it is not formally defined until the subsequent paragraph. Notation should be defined before its first use.
*   Similarly, on page 2, the matrix `V` is used in Equation (1) without being explicitly defined as the matrix of eigenvectors.
*   On page 3, in the description of the K-Ascent projection step, the explanation is slightly unclear. It would be more precise to state that the projection "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q."
*   On page 4, the M-step for updating eigenvectors refers to a complex algorithm from [26] for optimization on Stiefel manifolds. Since the authors state they use a simplified approach, it would greatly benefit the reader if this simpler update rule were outlined directly in the paper, making the work more self-contained.
*   On page 4, when discussing the mixture representation in Equation (7), the meaning of "mixture weight" could be made more explicit for clarity.
*   On page 7, the rationale for the moments-matching initializer in Equation (23) is not well explained. Why is this specific formula used? For instance, why wouldn't a more straightforward approach, like using the empirical covariance matrix as the initial kernel, be suitable? More intuition here would be helpful.
