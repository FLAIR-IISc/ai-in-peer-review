The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method cleverly combines several non-trivial ingredients: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a lower-bound on the log-likelihood derived using Jensen’s Inequality. Experiments demonstrate favorable performance compared to a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.

**Quality:** The method appears well-founded and the claimed results seem plausible. However, the evaluation could be strengthened. The experiments are somewhat limited and lack comparison to alternative subset selection approaches (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction). Consequently, it’s unclear how much benefit is gained specifically from employing a DPP approach.

**Clarity:** The paper’s presentation could be significantly improved. The writing is occasionally sloppy and difficult to follow. Notation is not always defined before use, and definitions sometimes appear *after* their initial application. The text is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, largely because it describes the development process – including alternative approaches and potential issues – interwoven with the core method. I would have preferred a clearer, more direct presentation of the developed method, with discussion of alternatives and complexities postponed to a later section.

**Originality:** To the best of my knowledge, this paper presents original work. I am not aware of similar approaches in the literature.

**Significance:** I believe the method developed in this paper represents a solid advance and a valuable contribution to the subset selection literature.

**Details:**

*   p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel *K* is used without prior definition. What characteristics qualify a kernel as a ‘marginal kernel’ in this context?
*   p.2, line 100: *V* is not defined.
*   p.3, line 159: “Minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. It would be more precise to state that the algorithm “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite matrix to *Q*.”
*   p.4: This section is difficult to understand. Instead of referring to [26], outlining your (simpler) approach directly would be beneficial, avoiding the need for the reader to consult the more complex original method.
*   p.4, line 189: Please clarify what is meant by “weight” in this context.
*   p.7, line 324: I don't fully understand the rationale behind equation (30). Why isn’t *m<sub>ij</sub>* used here? Furthermore, why wouldn't using the empirical covariance matrix as the initial kernel have been sufficient? 

In summary, this paper proposes a highly non-trivial and, as far as I can tell, original method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. While the write-up lacks clarity and the experiments do not include comparisons to non-DPP methods, the core contribution appears significant.
