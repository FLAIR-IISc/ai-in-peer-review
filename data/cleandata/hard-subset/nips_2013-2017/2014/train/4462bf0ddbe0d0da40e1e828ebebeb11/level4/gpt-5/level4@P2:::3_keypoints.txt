- Paper proposes learning full DPP kernels by directly estimating an unrestricted kernel via an EM-style algorithm in the eigenbasis.  
- Reparameterizes K by its eigenvalues and eigenvectors and uses the subset of eigenvectors (J) as a latent variable via the elementary-DPP mixture.  
- E-step sets q(J|Y) to a conditional k-DPP with a closed-form kernel.  
- M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form using k-DPP marginals and updates eigenvectors by a manifold-constrained gradient (multiplicative) step on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Avoids PSD projection on K, which can truncate eigenvalues, drive K toward diagonal structure, and destroy negative correlations.  
- Runtime: eigenvalue and initial eigenvector gradient updates cost O(n N k^2) per EM iteration; manifold updates and step-size search add O(N^3) costs.  
- Authors compare runtime to projected gradient ascent (KA), showing EM can be substantially faster than KA’s O(n N^3) scaling when k ≪ N.  
- Empirically on a baby registry dataset, EM outperforms KA in test log-likelihood (median gains, up to 16.5% in low-data regimes), is more robust to initialization, and is ~2× faster in wall-clock time.  
- EM better preserves off-diagonal structure encoding negative interactions and yields less redundant recommendations in qualitative examples.  
- Technical development is solid: latent-variable construction and k-DPP-based closed-form eigenvalue updates are correct and well motivated.  
- Eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold is appropriate; multiplicative update is clearly specified.  
- Complexity analysis is careful and practical issues (e.g., keeping λj < 1) are acknowledged.  
- Limitations: objective remains nonconvex and M-step for eigenvectors uses a single gradient step with costly step-size chosen by repeated E-steps.  
- Exact evaluation of the Jensen lower bound is intractable; progress is monitored via likelihood instead.  
- Empirical evaluation is narrow, primarily comparing to KA and using one dataset and likelihood-focused metrics.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized with clear derivations and explanations of EM construction and k-DPP connection.  
- Minor clarity issues: conclusion could better synthesize contributions/limitations and provide practical guidance on step-size and λ near 1.  
- Originality: learning full DPP kernel via eigenbasis EM with k-DPP E-step and Stiefel manifold optimization appears novel relative to prior constrained approaches.  
- Significance: provides a practical method to learn full DPPs, with meaningful runtime and likelihood improvements in realistic k ≪ N regimes.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to more baselines (per-item weights, kernel mixtures, Bayesian parametric), add datasets and diversity metrics, report initialization sensitivity and step-size protocol.  
- Suggested scalability work: discuss and evaluate approximations (low-rank, Nyström) to mitigate O(N^3) manifold-update bottleneck.  
- Overall verdict: clear, technically sound, and original contribution addressing an important gap; broader empirical validation would strengthen the paper.
