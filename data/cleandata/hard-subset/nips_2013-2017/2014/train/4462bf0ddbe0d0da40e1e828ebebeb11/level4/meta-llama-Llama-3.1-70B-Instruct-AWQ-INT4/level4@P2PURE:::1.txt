This paper focuses on learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs), with its primary contribution being a reformulated objective function that surpasses the standard approach of differentiating the log probability with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent, referred to as K-Ascent (KA). By framing the problem within an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework, the authors leverage the fact that any DPP can be represented as a mixture of "elementary DPPs", each corresponding to a subset of elements. A variational distribution is defined over these subsets, and the algorithm iterates between updating this distribution, represented as a k-DPP, and taking single gradient steps in the M-step. Experimental results demonstrate that the EM method significantly outperforms the KA method when the latter is initialized naively, and moderately outperforms it in certain settings when KA is initialized more intelligently.

Overall, I find the proposed approach intriguing, and it appears that the EM method yields some improvement. Although the M-step is complex, and the necessary caveats to make it work are somewhat unsatisfying, the fact that it works is noteworthy.

From an experimental perspective, I would have appreciated a more in-depth analysis of the effect of initialization on the EM algorithm. For instance, it would be interesting to investigate whether initializing the EM algorithm with the result of KA would lead to an improved solution.

I am also perplexed by the use of relative log likelihood as a performance metric. Since log probability differences reflect ratios of probabilities, I question the necessity of taking ratios of log probabilities. Instead, reporting differences in log probabilities would be more interpretable.

On a minor note, I find certain aspects of the writing style distracting. I suggest that the authors avoid describing their own algorithm as "elegant", particularly given the caveats and approximations required in the M-step. Additionally, I find the bold text in the introduction unnecessary.

The paper presents an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs. While the experiments could be improved, and the gains over the basic projected gradient algorithm are not substantial, the paper is overall well-written.
