- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that integrates eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, representing DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s-inequality log-likelihood lower bound.  
- Experimental results indicate the method performs favorably compared to a simpler K-Ascent baseline.  
- Methodology appears well-founded and plausible.  
- Evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods, leaving the practical advantage over existing techniques unclear.  
- Paper is poorly written and disorganized, making the presentation difficult to follow.  
- Notation problems: the marginal kernel K is used before being defined.  
- Notation problems: the symbol V is not defined where first used.  
- Wording issue: the phrase “minimizes the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; should state choosing the closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm.  
- Section on p.4 is hard to follow and overly reliant on reference [26] instead of outlining a simpler approach directly.  
- The term “weight” (p.4, line 189) is not clearly explained.  
- Equation (30) (p.7, line 324) lacks clear rationale—unclear why m_ij is not used and why empirical covariance wouldn’t suffice for initialization.  
- Originality is high—reviewer is not aware of similar prior work.  
- Significance: the proposed method represents a meaningful advancement for subset selection.
