This paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The proposed method is sophisticated, combining several non-trivial components: kernel eigendecomposition, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, the representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a lower-bound on the log-likelihood derived from Jensen's inequality. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms a simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.

**Quality**
The theoretical foundations of the proposed method appear to be sound, and its technical claims are plausible. However, the empirical evaluation could be significantly strengthened. The experiments are limited in scope and, crucially, do not include comparisons against alternative methods for subset selection, such as those mentioned in the introduction. This omission makes it difficult to assess the practical benefits of the DPP-based approach over other established techniques.

**Clarity**
The paper's clarity is a significant weakness. The manuscript is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Several pieces of notation are used before they are formally defined, and at times definitions are provided only after their first use. The writing can also be unnecessarily convoluted. This seems to stem from a narrative structure that describes the process of arriving at the final method, interleaving the main thread with side-remarks on alternative approaches and potential problems. The paper would be far more effective if it were restructured to first present the final, developed method as clearly as possible, postponing discussions of design choices and alternatives to a later section.

**Originality**
To the best of my knowledge, the work presented is original. I am not aware of prior work that has developed a similar learning algorithm for DPPs.

**Significance**
The method developed in this paper represents a solid technical advance and is a potentially useful contribution to the literature on subset selection. If its performance can be validated against a wider range of benchmarks, it could become a valuable tool for practitioners.

**Detailed Comments**
*   **Page 2, Line 81:** The marginal kernel K is used before it is defined. Please provide a clear definition and explain what properties a kernel must have to be considered a marginal kernel.
*   **Page 2, Line 100:** The matrix V is used without a definition.
*   **Page 3, Line 159:** The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is ambiguous. It would be clearer to state that the objective is to find the positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix that is closest to Q in the Frobenius norm.
*   **Page 4:** This section was difficult to follow. The paper would be greatly improved by providing a self-contained outline of the proposed approach, rather than referring the reader to the more complex original method in [26] and forcing them to consult an external source.
*   **Page 4, Line 189:** The term "weight" is used without a clear antecedent. Please clarify what this refers to.
*   **Page 7, Line 324:** The rationale for the initialization scheme in equation (30) is not well explained. Specifically, why is the term m_ij not used in this formula? Furthermore, could the authors comment on why a simpler approach, such as initializing the kernel directly with the empirical covariance matrix, would not be suitable?
