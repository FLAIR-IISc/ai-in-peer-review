- Paper addresses learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs).
- Main contribution: reformulation of the learning objective and optimization within an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework.
- Contrasts EM approach with standard projected gradient ascent on kernel parameters (K-Ascent, KA).
- EM builds on representing any DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and introduces a variational distribution over subsets.
- E-step updates the variational distribution as a k-DPP; M-step performs a single gradient update on kernel parameters.
- Empirical results: EM significantly outperforms naively initialized KA and gives modest improvements over well-initialized KA in some settings.
- M-step is computationally intricate; required approximations and heuristics reduce the method’s elegance.
- Experimental analysis is incomplete—needs more thorough investigation of EM’s sensitivity to initialization.
- Suggested experiment: initialize EM with final parameters from a converged KA run to see if EM can further improve the solution.
- Critique of evaluation metric: using relative log-likelihood is unclear; direct differences in log-likelihood would be more interpretable.
- Presentation notes: calling the algorithm “elegant” is premature given approximations, and bold text in the introduction is unnecessary.
- Overall: novel and solid contribution, but empirical gains vs well-initialized baselines are limited and experiments could be strengthened.
