The paper presents an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), combining several non-trivial components, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood using Jensen's Inequality. The experiments demonstrate favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and its effectiveness is believable. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are limited and do not compare the proposed approach to alternative subset selection methods (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), making it unclear how much benefit is derived from using DPPs.

Clarity: The paper's writing is not particularly clear, with a somewhat sloppy and hard-to-follow presentation. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes definitions appear after their use. The text's structure, which describes the process of arriving at the proposed approach, including side remarks on alternative approaches and problems, can be convoluted. A clearer presentation of the developed method, with discussion postponed to a later stage, would be preferable.

Originality: The paper appears to be highly original, as I am not aware of similar work in this area.

Significance: The method presented in the paper represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:

- On page 2, line 81, the marginal kernel K is not defined before its use. It would be helpful to clarify what type of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.
- On page 2, line 100, the variable V is not defined.
- On page 3, line 159, the phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. A more precise statement would be "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) Positive Semi-Definite (PSD) matrix to Q".
- I found the section on page 4 difficult to understand. Instead of referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline the authors' approach directly, allowing the reader to understand the method without needing to look up the original, more complex method.
- On page 4, line 189, the term "weight" is unclear. Please clarify what this term refers to.
- On page 7, line 324, the rationale behind equation (30) is not entirely clear. It would be helpful to explain why m_{ij} is not used here and why simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel would not have worked as an initialization.
