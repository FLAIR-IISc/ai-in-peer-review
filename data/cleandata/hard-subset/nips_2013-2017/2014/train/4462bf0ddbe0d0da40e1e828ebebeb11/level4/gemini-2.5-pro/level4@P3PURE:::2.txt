This paper presents an EM algorithm for learning the parameters of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP). The proposed method is sophisticated, combining several non-trivial ingredients including the eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, the representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and the use of Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the log-likelihood. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms a simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.

**Quality**
The proposed method appears to be technically sound and well-founded. However, the experimental evaluation could be significantly strengthened. The experiments are limited, comparing only against another DPP-based method ("K-Ascent") rather than broader state-of-the-art subset selection techniques, some of which are mentioned in the introduction. Consequently, it is difficult to gauge the practical benefits of using a DPP-based approach over these alternatives.

**Clarity**
The paper's clarity could be significantly improved, as the exposition is often difficult to follow. Some notation is used before it is formally defined. The writing is at times unnecessarily convoluted, largely because the paper is structured as a narrative describing the derivation process, complete with side-remarks on alternative approaches and potential issues. This structure interrupts the main logical flow of the paper. A more direct presentation of the final method, with discussions of alternatives deferred to a separate section, would have greatly improved readability.

**Originality**
The paper appears to be highly original. To my knowledge, this is the first work to propose such an approach for learning DPPs.

**Significance**
The method derived in this paper represents a solid advance and is a useful contribution to the literature on subset selection.

**Detailed Comments**
*   **p. 2, l. 81:** The marginal kernel K is used before it is defined. The authors should clarify what constitutes a marginal kernel.
*   **p. 2, l. 100:** The matrix V is not defined.
*   **p. 3, l. 159:** The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. It would be better to state that the goal is to find the closest positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to Q in terms of Frobenius norm.
*   **p. 4:** This section is difficult to understand. Instead of simply referring to the more complex method in [26], the authors should provide a self-contained outline of their proposed approach. This would prevent readers from having to consult an external reference to understand the paper's core contribution.
*   **p. 4, l. 189:** The term "weight" is used without a clear definition in this context.
*   **p. 7, l. 324:** The rationale for the initialization in equation (30) is unclear. Specifically, why is the term m_{ij} omitted? Furthermore, the authors should explain why a simpler initialization, such as using the empirical covariance matrix directly as the kernel, would not be sufficient.
