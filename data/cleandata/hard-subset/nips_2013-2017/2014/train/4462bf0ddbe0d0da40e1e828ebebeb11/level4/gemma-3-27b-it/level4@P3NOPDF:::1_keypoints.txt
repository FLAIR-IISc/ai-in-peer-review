- Novel reformulation: proposes an EM framework to learn DPP kernels as an alternative to standard gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).  
- Key idea: decomposes any DPP into a mixture of elementary DPPs (one per subset) and uses this in the EM formulation.  
- Algorithm: iteratively updates a variational distribution over subsets (modeled as a k-DPP) and performs a single gradient step in the M-step.  
- Empirical result: EM consistently outperforms KA with naive initialization and gives modest improvements with informed KA initialization.  
- Technical note: the M-step is mathematically complex and relies on approximations/caveats, but it is notable that it works in practice.  
- Experimental weakness: evaluation could be strengthened—more analysis of initialization effects on EM is needed.  
- Suggested experiment: try initializing EM with the solution from KA to check for further gains.  
- Metric concern: using relative log-likelihood is confusing; reporting differences in log-probabilities would be clearer and more interpretable.  
- Presentation issues: avoid subjective descriptors (e.g., “elegant”) and remove distracting bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: the approach is interesting and methodologically sound; improvements are promising but not large, and clearer experiments and more conservative writing would strengthen the paper.
