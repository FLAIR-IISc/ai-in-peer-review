- Paper proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices instead of direct gradient-based KA.
- Key insight: any DPP is a mixture of elementary DPPs (one per subset), and a variational distribution over subsets is used.
- Algorithm alternates updating a k-DPP variational distribution in the E-step and single gradient updates in the M-step.
- Experiments show EM significantly outperforms KA with naive KA initialization and gives modest gains with stronger KA initialization.
- M-step is complex and relies on approximations and caveats that are somewhat unsatisfying.
- It is commendable the authors made the complex method work effectively despite the approximations.
- Experimental evaluation should analyze the impact of initialization (e.g., initializing EM with KA outputs).
- Reporting metric choice (relative log-likelihood ratios) is confusing; log-likelihood differences would be more interpretable.
- Minor writing issues: avoid self-descriptive terms like "elegant" and unnecessary bold text.
- Overall: interesting, novel, modest empirical gains, paper is a solid contribution but could strengthen experiments and clarify M-step approximations.
