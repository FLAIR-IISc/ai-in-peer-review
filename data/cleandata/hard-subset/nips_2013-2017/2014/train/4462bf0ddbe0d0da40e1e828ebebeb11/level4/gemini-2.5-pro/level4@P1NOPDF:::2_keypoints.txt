- Presents a sophisticated EM algorithm for learning DPPs, combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, DPP-as-mixtures representation, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood bound.  
- Experimental results show the proposed method outperforms a simpler K-Ascent baseline.  
- Theoretical foundations and technical claims appear sound and plausible.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods, undermining practical assessment.  
- The manuscript’s clarity is weak: notation is often used before definition and the narrative is convoluted.  
- The paper interleaves development and side remarks; it should present the final method first and defer design discussions.  
- The work appears original; no prior similar learning algorithm for DPPs is known to the reviewer.  
- The method is a solid technical advance with potential significance if validated on broader benchmarks.  
- Page 2, Line 81: Marginal kernel K is used before being defined; define it and its required properties.  
- Page 2, Line 100: Matrix V is referenced without definition.  
- Page 3, Line 159: Phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is ambiguous; clarify as finding the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.  
- Page 4: Section is hard to follow; provide a self-contained outline rather than referring readers to [26].  
- Page 4, Line 189: The term “weight” lacks a clear antecedent and should be clarified.  
- Page 7, Line 324: Initialization rationale for equation (30) is unexplained—why omit m_ij and why not initialize with empirical covariance?
