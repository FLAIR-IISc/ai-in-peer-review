This paper addresses learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The main contribution is a reformulation of the learning objective within an EM framework that outperforms the standard approach of differentiating the log probability with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent (referred to as K-Ascent, or KA). The key idea is to exploit the fact that any DPP can be represented as a mixture over “elementary DPPs,” with one elementary DPP per subset of elements. A variational distribution is introduced over these subsets, and the algorithm alternates between (i) updating this distribution—represented as a k-DPP—in the E-step, and (ii) taking single gradient steps in the M-step. Empirically, the EM method substantially outperforms KA when KA is naively initialized, and yields modest improvements in some settings even when KA is initialized more carefully.

Overall, the approach is interesting, and the EM method appears to provide meaningful gains. That said, the M-step is fairly involved, and the caveats and approximations required to make it work are somewhat unsatisfying—though it is still valuable to see that the method can be made to work in practice.

On the experimental side, I would have liked more analysis of how initialization affects EM. For example, if EM were initialized with the solution found by KA, would it further improve upon that solution?

I am also confused by the use of “relative log likelihood” as the reported metric. Differences in log probabilities correspond to ratios of probabilities, so it is unclear why one would take ratios of log probabilities. Why not simply report differences in log probability? As presented, the measure is hard to interpret.

Minor writing/style comments: I would prefer that the authors not describe their own algorithm as “elegant,” especially given the caveats and approximations in the M-step that make this characterization debatable. I also find the bolded text in the introduction unnecessary.

In sum: an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs. The experiments could be strengthened, and the improvements over basic projected gradient are not dramatic in all cases, but overall this is a solid paper.
