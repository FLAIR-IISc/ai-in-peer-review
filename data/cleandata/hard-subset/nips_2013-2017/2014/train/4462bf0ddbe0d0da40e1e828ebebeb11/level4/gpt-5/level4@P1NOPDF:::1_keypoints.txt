- Main contribution: reformulation of the DPP kernel learning objective into an EM framework that outperforms the standard projected-gradient baseline (KA).
- Method: view a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs, introduce a variational distribution over subsets represented as a k-DPP, alternate E-step (update distribution) and M-step (single gradient steps).
- Empirical finding: EM substantially outperforms KA with naive initialization and gives modest improvements when KA uses a stronger initialization.
- Strength: interesting, practical EM-based formulation that yields consistent gains.
- Weakness: the M-step is complex and relies on caveats/approximations, making the method feel less principled than presented.
- Missing analysis: what happens if EM is initialized from a KA solution—does EM further improve objective or generalization?
- Missing analysis: sensitivity of EM to different random or heuristic initializations compared to KA.
- Reporting concern: “relative log likelihood” is unclear—taking ratios of log probabilities is hard to interpret; authors should report raw log-likelihoods or differences and standard deviations across runs.
- Minor style notes: don’t call your own algorithm “elegant”; avoid excessive bolding in the introduction.
- Overall: experimental section should be strengthened; gains are meaningful but not always dramatic; overall a solid paper.
