- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn full DPP kernels by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition and introducing a latent index set over eigenvectors (mixture of elementary DPPs).
- Derives an EM lower bound (Jensen); E-step computes a conditional k-DPP over latent eigenvector subsets; M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and updates eigenvectors via a Stiefel-manifold gradient step preserving orthogonality.
- Avoids PSD projection used in projected gradient ascent on K (KA), which the authors argue can trap learning in near-diagonal degenerate solutions.
- Claims asymptotic speed advantage over KA and reports up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood and ~2× speedup on a baby registry dataset, with larger gains when off-diagonal (negative interaction) structure is important.
- Advances prior work by tackling full-kernel learning without restrictive parameterizations, combining DPP mixture/k-DPP properties with EM and Stiefel optimization.
- Methodology is technically sound: E-step as conditional k-DPP and eigenvalue updates are well motivated; Stiefel update is a reasonable choice to maintain orthogonality.
- Experimental evaluation is narrow: only compares to KA on a single dataset and focuses primarily on test log-likelihood.
- Important baselines are missing (per-item convex weight learning, mixtures of fixed kernels, non-DPP subset selection like facility location/submodular methods).
- Needs additional analyses: ablation on EM components, sensitivity to initialization, robustness across domains, and alternative evaluation metrics (MAP, diversity/coverage).
- Clarity issues: definitions (e.g., marginal kernel K, matrix V) and notation are sometimes introduced late or used before definition; exposition interleaves derivation and remarks, obscuring the main thread.
- Recommend clearer structure: DPP basics and mixture view; EM lower bound; E-step as k-DPP; M-step and Stiefel update; complexity, with inline derivations/algorithmic steps.
- Originality: combining an EM lower bound over latent eigenvector subsets with eigenvalue/eigenvector updates for full DPP learning appears novel.
- Significance: enabling full-kernel DPP learning is valuable for modeling negative interactions and diversity; empirical gains are promising but need broader validation.
- Pros: elegant, technically solid EM formulation; avoids projection-induced degeneracy; empirically faster and more robust; clear practical motivation.
- Cons: limited experiments and baselines; presentation and notation problems; evaluation limited to log-likelihood; some implementation details (step-size, handling λ near 1) under-specified.
- Detailed actionable comments: define K and V earlier; rephrase “closest PSD in Frobenius norm”; provide a concise self-contained description of the Stiefel multiplicative update; clarify “weight” in the mixture; justify moment-matching initialization and compare alternatives.
