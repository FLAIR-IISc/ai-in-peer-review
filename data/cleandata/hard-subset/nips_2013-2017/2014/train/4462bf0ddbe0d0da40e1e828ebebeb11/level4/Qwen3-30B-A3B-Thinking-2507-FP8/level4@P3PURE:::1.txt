

This paper addresses the problem of learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The primary contribution is a reformulation of the learning objective within an expectation-maximization (EM) framework, which demonstrates significant improvements over the standard baseline approach of differentiating the log-probability with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent (termed K-Ascent or KA in the paper). The authors observe that any DPP can be represented as a mixture over "elementary DPPs," one for each subset of elements. They introduce a variational distribution over these subsets, represented as a k-DPP, and alternate between updating this distribution and taking single gradient steps in the M-step. Results demonstrate that the EM method significantly outperforms KA under naive initialization, and yields modest improvements over KA when KA is initialized more intelligently.

The approach is interesting and the EM framework does yield tangible improvements. However, the M-step is computationally involved, and the required caveats to ensure convergence are somewhat unsatisfying. Despite this, the authors successfully demonstrate the viability of the method.

Experimentally, the paper would benefit from deeper analysis of initialization effects on the EM algorithm. Specifically, it is unclear whether initializing EM with the solution from KA would further improve upon KA's results. Additionally, the use of relative log likelihood as the primary evaluation metric is problematic. Since log-probability differences reflect ratios of probabilities, reporting ratios of log probabilities (e.g., relative log likelihood) complicates interpretation. Reporting differences in log probabilities would be more straightforward and meaningful for assessing performance.

The writing contains some distracting elements: the authors' claim that their algorithm is "elegant" is debatable given the caveats and approximations required in the M-step, and bolding text in the introduction is unnecessary. Overall, the paper presents a novel approach to kernel learning for DPPs. While the improvements over the baseline projected gradient method are not substantial in all settings, the experimental results are generally sound, and the contribution represents a solid advance in the area.
