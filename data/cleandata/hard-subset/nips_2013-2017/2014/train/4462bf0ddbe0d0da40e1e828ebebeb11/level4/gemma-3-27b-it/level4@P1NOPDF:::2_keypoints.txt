- Presents an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture representation of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood lower bound.  
- Experiments show better performance than a simple “K-Ascent” baseline but are limited in scope.  
- Lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods, so the specific benefit of using DPPs is unclear.  
- Writing is sloppy and sometimes hard to follow; notation is often used before being defined.  
- Presentation is convoluted by mixing development process and alternatives with the core method; suggests postponing alternatives/complexities.  
- Claims originality: reviewer is not aware of similar prior work.  
- Claims significance: method is a solid advance and valuable to subset-selection literature.  
- p.2, line 81: “marginal kernel K” is used without definition—needs clarification.  
- p.2, line 100: Symbol V is not defined before use.  
- p.3, line 159: Phrase “Minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; should state it chooses the closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm.  
- p.4: The section is hard to understand; the authors should outline their simpler approach rather than referring readers to [26].  
- p.4, line 189: “weight” is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- p.7, line 324 / eq. (30): Rationale behind the equation is unclear—why isn’t m_ij used, and why not initialize with the empirical covariance matrix?
