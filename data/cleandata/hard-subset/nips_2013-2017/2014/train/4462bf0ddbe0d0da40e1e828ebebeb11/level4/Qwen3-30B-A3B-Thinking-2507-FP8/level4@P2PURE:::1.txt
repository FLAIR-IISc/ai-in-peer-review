

This paper addresses the problem of learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The primary contribution is a reformulation of the learning objective within an expectation-maximization (EM) framework, which outperforms the standard approach of differentiating the log probability with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent (denoted K-Ascent or KA in the paper). The key insight leverages the representation of any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" (one per subset of elements), introducing a variational distribution over these subsets. The algorithm alternates between updating this distribution (represented as a k-DPP) and taking single gradient steps in the M-step. Results demonstrate that the EM method significantly outperforms KA under naive initialization, and marginally outperforms KA with more informed initialization.

Overall, the approach is interesting and yields tangible improvements. While the M-step is technically involved and requires certain caveats to function, the successful implementation is noteworthy. However, I would have preferred more thorough experimental analysis regarding the impact of initialization on EM. Specifically, it would be valuable to assess whether initializing EM with the solution from KA leads to further improvements.

I am also confused by the use of relative log-likelihood values as the primary metric. Since log-probability differences reflect ratios of probabilities, reporting relative log-likelihoods (ratios of log probabilities) complicates interpretation. Reporting simple differences in log probabilities would be more straightforward and interpretable.

Minor stylistic notes: The authors' self-description of their algorithm as "elegant" is unwarranted given the necessary caveats and approximations in the M-step. Additionally, bolding text in the introduction is distracting and unnecessary. Despite these points, the paper presents a novel approach to kernel learning in DPPs. While the improvements over the baseline projected gradient method are modest, the experimental validation is reasonable, and the paper overall constitutes a solid contribution.
