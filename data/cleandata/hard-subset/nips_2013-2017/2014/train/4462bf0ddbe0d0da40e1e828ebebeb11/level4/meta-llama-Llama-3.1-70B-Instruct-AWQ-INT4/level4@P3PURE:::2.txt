The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), combining several non-trivial ingredients, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimisation on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality. The experiments demonstrate favourable behaviour of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and it is believable that the methods should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), making it unclear how much benefit is derived from the DPP approach.

Clarity: The paper's writing is not particularly clear. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow, with undefined notation and occasionally delayed definitions. The text's structure, which describes the process of arriving at the proposed approach, including side remarks on alternative approaches and problems, can be convoluted. A clearer presentation of the developed method, followed by a discussion of the approach, would be preferable.

Originality: The paper appears to be highly original, as I am not aware of similar work in this area.

Significance: I believe the method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is not defined before use. It would be helpful to clarify what kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.
- p.2, line 100: V is not defined.
- p.3, line 159: The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. It would be better to say "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) Positive Semi-Definite (PSD) matrix to Q".
- p.4: I found this section hard to understand. Instead of referring to [26], it would be nice to outline the approach directly, allowing the reader to understand the method without needing to look up the original, more complex method.
- p.4, line 189: The term "weight" is unclear; please provide a definition or explanation.
- p.7, line 324: The rationale behind equation (30) is not entirely clear. Why is m_{ij} not used here? Furthermore, why would simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not have worked as an initialisation?
