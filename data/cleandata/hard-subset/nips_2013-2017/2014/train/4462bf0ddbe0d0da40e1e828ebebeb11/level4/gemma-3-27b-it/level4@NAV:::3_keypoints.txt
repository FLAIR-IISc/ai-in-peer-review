- DPPs model subset diversity via a PSD matrix L, but fully learning L is NP-hard.  
- Prior work restricts L or learns it partially, often reducing diversity.  
- The paper proposes a novel method to learn L without parametric restrictions.  
- Gradient-ascent approaches require projections that can degrade DPP diversity.  
- The method uses an EM framework operating on eigenvalues and eigenvectors to avoid those projections.  
- Exploiting the full-rank property of the eigenvector matrix V sidesteps problematic projection of eigenvectors.  
- A Jensen’s-inequality-derived lower bound on the objective enables construction of the EM algorithm.  
- The approach preserves diversity while balancing quality, addressing a gap in prior work.  
- Strong theoretical contributions include formulating constraints as optimization on the Stiefel manifold and showing the inverse distribution is also a DPP.  
- The detailed EM derivation and clear, rigorous proofs are valuable contributions.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and a product recommendation task shows improved diversity versus Kernel Alignment, with modest but meaningful recommendation changes.  
- Manuscript is well-written and cohesive, though the conclusion could better summarize contributions; broader real-world experiments would strengthen the empirical case.
