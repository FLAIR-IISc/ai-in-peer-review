- Introduces an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture representation of DPPs, and Jensen’s lower bound.  
- Experiments show the proposed EM approach outperforms a projected gradient ascent baseline (K-Ascent).  
- Method appears technically sound and improvements over K-Ascent are plausible.  
- Evaluation is limited in scope and lacks comparisons to other subset selection baselines, weakening claims about DPP advantages.  
- Writing and exposition are convoluted; notation is sometimes undefined or introduced after use, disrupting clarity.  
- The narrative interweaves method development and tangential discussion; a clearer, more linear presentation and a separate discussion section are needed.  
- The combination of techniques for DPP learning appears novel and not previously published.  
- The approach is potentially significant for applications needing diversity-aware subset selection (recommendation, summarization, etc.).  
- p.2, line 81: “marginal kernel K” is used without definition—clarify what this denotes.  
- p.2, line 100: Variable ‘V’ is used before being defined—define it earlier.  
- p.3, line 159: Rephrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” to “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”  
- p.4: The Stiefel manifold discussion relies too heavily on [26]; provide a brief, self-contained explanation of the simplified approach.  
- p.4, line 189: The term “weight” is ambiguous—clarify its meaning in context.  
- p.7, line 324: Equation (30) rationale is unclear—explain why m_{ij} is omitted and why empirical covariance initialization failed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound method; empirical improvement over K-Ascent; potential impact; tackles learning full DPP kernels.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental comparison to standard methods, clarity issues, and unclear advantage of DPPs over alternatives.  
- Overall: Paper is a worthwhile contribution but needs clearer writing and stronger experimental validation before strong acceptance.
