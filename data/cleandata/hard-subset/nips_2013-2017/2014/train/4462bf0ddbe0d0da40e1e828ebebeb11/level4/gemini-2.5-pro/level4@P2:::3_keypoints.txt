- Tackles the hard problem of learning a full DPP kernel matrix L from data, overcoming prior restricted/convex parameterizations.  
- Prior work limited model expressiveness by learning only per-item weights or mixtures of fixed DPPs.  
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes L via eigenvalues and eigenvectors and introduces a hidden variable for eigenvector selection.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive an iterative EM procedure with a closed-form update for eigenvalues.  
- Updates eigenvectors by optimization on the Stiefel manifold, enforcing orthogonality without projection.  
- Avoids the projection issues of naive gradient ascent, which can yield near-diagonal, non-diverse solutions.  
- Technical strengths include identifying the E-step conditional as a k-DPP for efficient marginals and elegant Stiefel M-step optimization.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides an easy-to-follow eigendecomposition-based derivation.  
- Empirical validation on a product recommendation (baby registry) shows EM is more robust than K-Ascent, faster, and yields qualitatively better diverse recommendations.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to one domain; broader tests (e.g., summarization, image search) would strengthen claims of generality.  
- Weakness: conclusion is brief and could better summarize technical innovations and broader implications.  
- Overall: technically sound, highly original, significant contribution providing the first practical algorithm for learning an unconstrained DPP kernel; represents a clear advance.
