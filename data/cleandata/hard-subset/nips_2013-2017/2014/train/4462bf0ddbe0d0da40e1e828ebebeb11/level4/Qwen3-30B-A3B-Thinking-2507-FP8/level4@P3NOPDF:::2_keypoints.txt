- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, DPP mixture representation, and Jensen’s lower bound.  
- Technical approach is well-founded and likely effective as described.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: compares only to K-Ascent and omits non-DPP baselines (greedy selection, random sampling, other cited methods).  
- Without broader baselines, practical benefits of the DPP framework over simpler methods are unclear.  
- Presentation is disorganized: notation is often introduced after use (e.g., marginal kernel K, matrix V).  
- Method development is interleaved with side discussions, obscuring the core contribution; recommend presenting the finalized method first.  
- Some phrasing is imprecise (e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” should explicitly state closest PSD matrix to Q in Frobenius norm).  
- Section 4 relies on [26] without summarizing the adapted technique, reducing accessibility.  
- Ambiguous terms remain (e.g., “weight” unclear; omission of m_{ij} in Eq.30 unexplained).  
- Originality: the specific combination of techniques is novel and constitutes a distinct contribution.  
- Significance: theoretically strong, but impact is weakened by limited experimental validation.  
- Specific required fixes: define marginal kernel K and V before use; rephrase the Frobenius statement; summarize the technique from [26]; clarify “weight”; justify omitting m_{ij} and why empirical covariance is not a valid initial kernel.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept pending major revisions—add non-DPP baselines, restructure for clarity, and resolve notation/phrasing ambiguities.
