- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, mixture representation, and a Jensen’s-based lower bound.  
- Method appears technically sound and theoretically justified.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited, only comparing to K-Ascent, lacking broader benchmark against other subset-selection methods.  
- Hard to tell how much performance gain is due to the EM learning versus the DPP model itself.  
- Writing is convoluted and difficult to follow; presentation often shows the derivation process instead of clearly presenting the final method first.  
- Notation and definitions are sometimes introduced out of order, hindering comprehension.  
- Stiefel manifold optimization section is terse; authors should summarize their simplified approach rather than relying on external references.  
- Several specific clarifications requested: definition of “marginal kernel K,” meaning of variable V, meaning of “weight,” rationale for equation (30) and initialization choices, and rephrase the Frobenius-norm sentence.  
- Initialization sensitivity appears significant; authors should analyze and provide guidance on starting points.  
- Work is highly original in combining these techniques for DPP learning.  
- The contribution is potentially significant for subset selection and modeling diversity without restrictive parameterizations.  
- Pros: novel, technically solid, creative combination of advanced techniques, and improved results over a baseline.  
- Cons: limited experiments, clarity issues, and insufficient justification for some design choices.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental comparisons and initialization analysis.
