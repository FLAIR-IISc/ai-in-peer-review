- DPPs model diverse subsets and are parameterized by a PSD matrix L, but exact learning of L is NP-hard.  
- Prior work often learns only partial/parametric forms of L or scalar weightings, weakening diversity.  
- The paper proposes a learning method that does not constrain L’s parameterization.  
- Instead of projected gradient ascent (which can harm similarity/diversity), the authors use an EM algorithm over eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- EM avoids eigenvector projection by leveraging the full-rank property of V, removing the need to project eigenvectors.  
- Jensen’s inequality is used to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM procedure.  
- The paper maps the eigenvector constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold to eliminate eigenvector projection.  
- The authors show the inverse distribution is itself a DPP — a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support the claim that the method preserves both quality and diversity.  
- Comparison with KA shows modest improvements (e.g., KA replaces two of top-10 products), supporting the paper’s contribution.  
- Proofs are clearly presented and the staged reduction to EM is insightful and well explained.  
- The conclusion is somewhat rushed, and the paper would benefit from additional experiments on real-world datasets to better demonstrate practical diversity gains.
