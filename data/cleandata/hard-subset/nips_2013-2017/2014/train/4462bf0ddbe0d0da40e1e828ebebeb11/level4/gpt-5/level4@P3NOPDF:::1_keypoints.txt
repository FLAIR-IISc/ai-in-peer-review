- Paper proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernels using a k-DPP variational posterior over subset mixtures.  
- Empirically, EM outperforms projected gradient ascent (KA), especially from naive initialization, with modest gains from strong initialization.  
- Conceptual strength: connects DPP mixture representation with tractable variational EM (k-DPPs).  
- Empirical strength: consistent performance improvements over KA across evaluated settings.  
- Practical strength: method is implementable with standard DPP primitives and offers more stable learning than vanilla projected gradients.  
- M-step complexity: M-step relies on caveats/approximations that complicate elegance and theoretical guarantees.  
- Monotonicity unclear: not established when each EM step provably increases a lower bound on the log-likelihood.  
- Initialization sensitivity: EM’s sensitivity to initialization is not fully explored.  
- Metric choice unclear: “relative log-likelihood” is not well-defined or justified; ratios of log-likelihoods are unconventional.  
- Limited empirical scope: robustness across datasets, hyperparameters, and computational budgets is not demonstrated.  
- Runtime analysis missing: per-iteration and wall-clock comparisons (including eigendecompositions/sampling) versus KA are absent.  
- Clarify EM objective and guarantees: state surrogate objectives for E/M steps and conditions for monotonic improvement.  
- Define relative log-likelihood precisely and, if applicable, use log-likelihood differences or per-datapoint averages.  
- Report computational cost: include per-iteration and total runtimes, and whether gains stem from better updates or more computation.  
- Suggested ablations: study k-DPP variational family effects, M-step approximations, step sizes, and sensitivity curves.  
- Suggested initialization study: try EM initialized from KA and vice versa, with multiple restarts to assess robustness and variance.  
- Convergence diagnostics: plot objective/EM lower bound across iterations, report stopping criteria and iterations to converge.  
- Presentation fixes: avoid subjective descriptors, reduce distracting bolding, recap mixture representation, and provide a concise EM schematic.  
- Be explicit about limitations: state known failure modes and approximation-induced limitations for practitioners.  
- Overall: interesting, practically relevant contribution with promising empirical gains but needs clearer theoretical justification, metric definitions, runtime analysis, and more robust experiments; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
