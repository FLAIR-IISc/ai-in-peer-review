This paper introduces an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The approach effectively integrates several complex components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representing DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and leveraging Jensen’s Inequality to lower-bound the log-likelihood. Experimental results demonstrate performance superior to a basic “K-Ascent” scheme.

**Quality:** The method appears theoretically sound and the claims made are plausible. However, the evaluation could be significantly strengthened. While the experiments demonstrate an improvement over K-Ascent, they are limited in scope and lack comparisons to other established subset selection methods (as acknowledged in the introduction). This makes it difficult to assess the true benefit of employing a DPP-based approach.

**Clarity:** The paper's presentation hinders comprehension. The writing is somewhat disorganized and occasionally imprecise. Notation is not always clearly defined, and definitions sometimes follow their first use. The narrative structure, which interleaves the derivation of the method with discussions of alternative approaches and potential issues, contributes to unnecessary complexity. A more direct presentation of the finalized method, followed by a dedicated discussion of alternatives, would substantially improve clarity.

**Originality:** The work appears highly original; I am not currently aware of similar research in this area.

**Significance:** This paper presents a valuable and substantive advancement to the field of subset selection. The derived method constitutes a useful contribution to the literature.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **p.2, line 81:** The term “marginal kernel K” is used without prior definition. Please clarify what constitutes a valid marginal kernel.
*   **p.2, line 100:** The variable V is undefined at this point.
*   **p.3, line 159:** The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. A more precise phrasing would be "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to Q."
*   **p.4:** This section is difficult to follow. Instead of relying on a reference to [26], providing a concise outline of your simplified approach would greatly benefit the reader and reduce the need to consult the original, more complex method.
*   **p.4, line 189:** Please clarify the meaning of “weight” in this context.
*   **p.7, line 324:** The rationale behind equation (30) is unclear. Specifically, why is *m<sub>ij</sub>* not utilized? Furthermore, why would using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel for initialization have been unsuitable? 

In summary, this paper introduces a novel and potentially impactful method for learning DPPs for subset selection. While the technical details are intricate and appear sound, improvements to the clarity of the presentation and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation – including comparisons with non-DPP based subset selection techniques – would significantly strengthen the paper.
