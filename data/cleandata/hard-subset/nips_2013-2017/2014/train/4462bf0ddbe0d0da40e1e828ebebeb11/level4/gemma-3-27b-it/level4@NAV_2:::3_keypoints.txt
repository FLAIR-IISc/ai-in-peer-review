- Problem: Learning the full DPP kernel L is NP-hard; prior work used restrictive parameterizations (scalar row weights or mixtures of fixed DPPs).

- Main contribution: Novel EM algorithm that learns the full kernel L by reparameterizing L in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors.

- Technical approach: Uses EM and optimization on the Stiefel manifold to avoid eigenvector projection and preserve the DPP structure.

- Motivation: Bypasses problematic projection steps in naive gradient ascent that lead to degenerate, near-diagonal kernels that lose diversity.

- Theoretical claims: Shows the resulting distribution remains a DPP and provides asymptotic runtime advantages over gradient ascent.

- Empirical results: Demonstrates improved performance and maintained diversity on synthetic data and a real-world product recommendation task; includes a concrete illustrative example.

- Strengths: Clear writing, rigorous proofs, detailed EM derivation, and solid theoretical contributions.

- Weaknesses: Experimental evaluation is limited in dataset diversity and baseline comparisons; lacks empirical runtime benchmarks; conclusion could better emphasize contributions.

- Overall recommendation: High-quality paper with significant contributions to DPP learning and suitable for acceptance.
