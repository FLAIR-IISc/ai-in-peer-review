Review- The paper introduces an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes. The approach combines several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representing DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood via Jensen’s inequality. The experiments show favorable performance compared to a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme. 
Quality: To the best of my judgment, the method is well-founded and it is plausible that it operates as claimed. However, the evaluation could be strengthened. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative subset selection methods (e.g., those cited in the introduction), making it unclear how much benefit the DPP approach provides.
Clarity: The paper is not particularly clear. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and difficult to follow. Notation is sometimes undefined when first used, with definitions appearing later. The presentation is also unnecessarily convoluted, largely because the text narrates the development process leading to the final method, with side remarks about alternatives and issues interleaved with the main argument. I would prefer a direct, clear presentation of the final approach, with discussion postponed.
Originality: The paper appears quite original; I am not aware of similar prior work.
Significance: I believe the method represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.
Details:
- p.2, l.81: the marginal kernel K is used before being defined. What exactly qualifies as a marginal kernel?
- p.2, l.100: V is not defined.
- p.3, l.159: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. It would be better to say “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
- p.4: I found this section hard to follow. Rather than referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline your (simpler) approach directly so the reader is not forced to consult the more complex original method. 
- p.4, l.189: what does “weight” refer to?
- p.7, l.324: I do not fully understand the rationale in (30). Why is m_{ij} not used here? Why wouldn’t using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel work as an initialization? The paper puts forward a new method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. The method is highly nontrivial and, as far as I can tell, original. The write-up is unclear, and the experiments consider only DPP-based methods.
