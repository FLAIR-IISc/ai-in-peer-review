- Problem: learning the full DPP kernel matrix is NP-hard; prior work used restrictive parameterizations that sacrifice diversity or efficiency.  
- Contribution: proposes a novel EM algorithm that learns the full kernel without restrictive parameterizations by reparameterizing via eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Key idea: eigendecomposition-based reparameterization avoids problematic projection steps of naive gradient ascent that lead to near-diagonal, non-diverse kernels.  
- Technical methods: derivation of EM using Jensen’s inequality and efficient update rules for eigenvalues and eigenvectors; Stiefel manifold optimization avoids eigenvector projection.  
- Insight: the E-step yields a k-DPP, clarifying the probabilistic interpretation and making inference tractable.  
- Empirical results: experiments (baby registry dataset) demonstrate the EM algorithm outperforms projected gradient ascent (KA), especially from poor initializations.  
- Analysis: compares EM to KA, showing KA’s tendency to learn degenerate kernels and analyzing the relation between data marginals (d metric) and EM gains.  
- Strengths: technically sound, novel, clearly presented, and significant for applications needing diversity (recommendation, summarization).  
- Clarity: well-written, good related work coverage, and clear explanation of algorithms and concepts.  
- Significance: enables full DPP learning without sacrificing diversity, with broad application impact.  
- Weakness: runtime analysis is limited to asymptotic discussion; lacks detailed empirical runtime comparisons across dataset sizes.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to initialization and hyperparameters (e.g., step size) not thoroughly explored; initialization strategies need deeper ablation.  
- Weakness: conclusion is understated and could more strongly emphasize the contribution’s importance.  
- Overall: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
