- Proposes a novel learning method for DPPs that does not restrict the parameterization of the PSD matrix L, preserving DPP diversity.
- Avoids projection steps that can harm similarity/diversity by using an EM algorithm on eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- Exploits full-rank property of matrix V to eliminate eigen-vector projection.
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM procedure.
- Maps the eigen-vector constraint to optimization over the Stiefel manifold, removing the need for projection.
- Demonstrates that the inverse distribution of a DPP is itself a DPP.
- Provides clear, step-by-step derivation from the optimization problem to the EM algorithm.
- Proofs are well-written and the paper is cohesively presented.
- Evaluated on synthetic data and a product recommendation task, showing retention of quality and diversity and outperforming the Kulesza & Taskar (KA) algorithm.
- Empirical result noted: KA replaces only two of the top 10 products with less likely ones, highlighting this method’s advantage.
- Suggests adding more real-world experiments to better illustrate the practical importance of produced diversity.
- Conclusion could better emphasize all contributions, but overall the work is a significant contribution and appropriate for publication.
