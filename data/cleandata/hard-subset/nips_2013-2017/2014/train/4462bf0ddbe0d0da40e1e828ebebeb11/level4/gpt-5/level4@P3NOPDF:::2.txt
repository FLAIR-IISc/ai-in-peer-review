Summary
The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The approach combines several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s inequality–based lower bound on the log-likelihood. Empirically, the proposed method outperforms a simpler K-ascent baseline on the reported tasks.

Strengths
- Technically solid: The algorithmic development is well-motivated and grounded in established tools from matrix analysis and manifold optimization.
- Novelty: To the best of my knowledge, applying EM with the stated decomposition and constraints for DPP learning is original.
- Potential impact: A more reliable learning procedure for DPPs would be a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Weaknesses
- Limited empirical evaluation: Experiments focus primarily on comparisons within the DPP family (vs. K-ascent) and do not benchmark against standard subset selection baselines mentioned in the introduction (e.g., greedy submodular maximization methods, facility-location surrogates, or other probabilistic subset models). This makes it difficult to assess the practical value of DPP learning versus simpler, widely used alternatives.
- Clarity and organization: The exposition is often hard to follow. Key notation is introduced after use, and the narrative interleaves side remarks and derivation pathways with the main algorithm, obscuring the core ideas. This could be streamlined substantially.
- Initialization and design choices: Some choices (e.g., the rationale in Eq. (30), the role of m_{ij}, and the decision not to use the empirical covariance as an initializer) are not sufficiently justified.

Quality
Overall, the technical approach appears correct and the components are plausibly integrated. The derivations and use of EM, eigendecomposition, and Stiefel manifold optimization are sensible. However, the paper would benefit from:
- clearer, self-contained derivations of the key steps (minimization, constraints, and updates);
- ablation studies or sensitivity analyses (e.g., effect of initialization, role of each component in the EM updates);
- stronger baselines and clearer reporting of computational cost (runtime, convergence behavior, and scalability to larger ground sets).

Clarity
The paper is hard to read in places due to late or missing definitions, nonstandard phrasing, and frequent cross-references that force the reader to reconstruct the method. I recommend:
- presenting the full algorithm clearly and self-contained first (definitions, assumptions, objective, parameterization, and EM updates), possibly with pseudocode;
- moving historical derivations, alternatives, and related design discussions to a subsequent section;
- tightening notation and defining all symbols upon first use.

Originality
The paper appears original in its particular EM formulation and combination of spectral decomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, and DPP mixture representation. I am not aware of prior work that develops the same learning procedure.

Significance
A more robust and effective method for learning DPP kernels would be valuable for applications in subset selection and diversity modeling. The contribution is promising, but its empirical significance would be clearer with broader comparisons and analysis of computational trade-offs.

Detailed comments and questions
- Page 2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is used before it is defined. Please define K explicitly (what “marginal kernel” means in the DPP context, its relation to the L-ensemble, and constraints on K such as 0 ⪯ K ⪯ I).
- Page 2, line 100: V is introduced without definition. If V is the eigenvector matrix from the eigendecomposition, define it and state dimensions and orthogonality constraints.
- Page 3, line 159: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. Prefer “chooses the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.”
- Page 4: This section is difficult to follow. Rather than relying on reference [26] for a more complex method, please outline your simplified approach directly and self-contained. A brief derivation and clear statement of assumptions would help.
- Page 4, line 189: Clarify what “weight” refers to (mixture weight over elementary DPPs? a function of eigenvalues? normalization constants?). Provide the exact definition and how it enters the E-step.
- Page 7, line 324 and Eq. (30): The rationale is unclear. Why is m_{ij} not used here? What property motivates this particular initialization or update rule? Please justify why an empirical covariance (or similarity) matrix would or would not be appropriate as an initializer, and discuss any constraints (e.g., ensuring 0 ⪯ K ⪯ I, conditioning) that might make naive covariance initialization problematic.
- Experiments:
  - Include baselines beyond DPP learning, such as greedy submodular diversity functions (e.g., facility location, dispersion), or other probabilistic subset models, to contextualize gains.
  - Report sensitivity to initialization and hyperparameters; show convergence curves and variability across runs.
  - Provide runtime and scaling analysis relative to K-ascent and other baselines; discuss the cost of eigendecompositions and Stiefel optimization in practice.
  - If possible, evaluate on at least one larger-scale dataset to demonstrate scalability.
- Presentation:
  - Provide a concise algorithm box for the EM procedure with all variables defined.
  - Clarify the parameterization (e.g., K vs. L, eigenvalues vs. factors), constraints (PSD; spectral constraints), and how the Stiefel manifold constraint is enforced in each step.
  - Ensure all notation is defined on first use and used consistently throughout.

Minor issues
- Fix spacing and punctuation around “Jensen’s Inequality.”
- Ensure consistent capitalization of matrix norms (e.g., “Frobenius norm”).
- Proofread for grammar and flow; several sentences are unnecessarily convoluted.

Overall assessment
This is a technically interesting and likely original contribution to DPP learning. With clearer exposition and a stronger experimental evaluation against non-DPP baselines, the paper would make a solid and useful addition to the subset selection literature.
