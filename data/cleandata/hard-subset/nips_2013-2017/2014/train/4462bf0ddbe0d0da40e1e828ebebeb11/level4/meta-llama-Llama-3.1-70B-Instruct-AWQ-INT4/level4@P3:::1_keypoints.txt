- Proposes a novel EM reformulation for learning kernel matrices in DPPs that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (KA) in some cases.  
- EM represents any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" and uses a variational distribution over subsets.  
- Algorithm alternates between an E-step (updating a k-DPP) and an M-step (single gradient steps).  
- Paper gives a clear explanation of the EM algorithm and its components.  
- The M-step is fairly involved and relies on caveats/approximations that feel unsatisfying.  
- Experiments show EM significantly outperforms KA when KA is naively initialized.  
- With smarter KA initialization, EM only yields small improvements, suggesting limited practical advantage.  
- EM appears more robust to initialization than KA.  
- Paper lacks analysis of initializing EM with KA results, which could clarify whether EM improves KA solutions.  
- Use of “relative log-likelihood” (ratios of log-probabilities) is unclear; reporting log-probability differences would be more conventional.  
- Minor writing issues: self-praise (“elegant”) and unnecessary bolding distract from presentation.  
- Strengths: novel approach, clear exposition, and empirical gains in some settings.  
- Weaknesses: complex M-step, limited initialization analysis, unclear metric choice, and occasional annoying writing.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the concerns above.
