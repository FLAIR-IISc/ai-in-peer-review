Summary: The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The approach combines several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s inequality-based lower bound on the log-likelihood. Experiments show favorable performance compared to a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.

Quality: As far as I can tell, the method is well-founded, and it is believable that the method works as stated. However, the evaluation could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), so it is not clear how much we benefit from the DPP approach.

Clarity: The paper is not particularly clearly written. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Not all notation is defined, and in several places definitions appear only after the notation is used. The exposition is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, largely because the text is structured around the process by which the authors arrived at the final approach, with side remarks about alternative methods and potential issues interleaved with the main thread. I would have preferred a presentation that first lays out the proposed method as clearly as possible, postponing discussion and alternatives to later sections.

Originality: The paper appears original; I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: The method provides a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details and requested clarifications:
- p. 2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is not defined before use. What precisely qualifies as a “marginal kernel” in this context?
- p. 2, line 100: V is not defined.
- p. 3, line 159: The phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. It would be better to say “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
- p. 4: I found this section hard to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline your (simpler) approach directly so that the reader is not forced to look up the more complex original method.
- p. 4, line 189: What does “weight” refer to here?
- p. 7, line 324: I do not fully understand the rationale in (30). Why is m_{ij} not used? Why would simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not work as an initialization?

Overall assessment: The paper puts forward a new method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. The method is highly nontrivial and appears to be original. However, the write-up is not very clear, and the experiments do not consider methods beyond DPP-based baselines.
