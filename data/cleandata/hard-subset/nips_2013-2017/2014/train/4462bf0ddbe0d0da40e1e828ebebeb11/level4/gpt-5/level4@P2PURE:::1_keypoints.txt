- Proposes EM reformulation to learn DPP kernel matrices that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent, KA).  
- Core idea: represent any DPP as a mixture over “elementary DPPs” and use a variational k-DPP for the E-step and gradient updates in the M-step.  
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms KA with naive initialization and gives modest gains with stronger KA initialization.  
- The M-step is fairly complex and requires caveats/approximations, which the reviewer finds somewhat unsatisfying.  
- Requests more analysis of initialization effects, specifically whether initializing EM with the KA solution yields further improvement.  
- Questions the choice of reporting "relative log-likelihood" (ratios of log probabilities) and suggests reporting differences in log probabilities for interpretability.  
- Minor stylistic critiques: reviewer objects to authors calling the method “elegant” and finds boldface in the introduction unnecessary.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and solid contribution, but experiments could be strengthened and improvements over basic KA are not large.
