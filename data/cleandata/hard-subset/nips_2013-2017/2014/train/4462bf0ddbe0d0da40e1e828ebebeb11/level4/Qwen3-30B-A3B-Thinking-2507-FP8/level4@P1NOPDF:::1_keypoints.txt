- Topic: Proposes an EM-based reformulation for kernel matrix learning in determinantal point processes (DPPs).  
- Contribution: Introduces a variational k-DPP over subsets (mixture of elementary DPPs) and alternates E-step updates with single-gradient M-step updates.  
- Empirical claim: Method outperforms standard K-Ascent (KA) baseline, especially when KA is randomly initialized.  
- Initialization sensitivity: Gains are large versus random KA initialization but only modest when KA uses better initialization.  
- Method caveat: The M-step requires nontrivial approximations and caveats, weakening the claim that the algorithm is “elegant.”  
- Experimental suggestion: Authors should test seeding EM with KA’s solution to evaluate whether EM improves over a KA-initialized start.  
- Metric critique: Using “relative log-likelihood” is confusing and nonstandard; authors should clarify or report standard log-probability differences.  
- Stylistic issues: Self-description as “elegant” is misleading given M-step complexity, and bolding key terms in the introduction is distracting.  
- Overall assessment: A novel, useful, and well-motivated contribution with sound implementation and consistent improvements, but not transformative.  
- Revision needed: Strengthen experiments on initialization effects and justify/report clearer performance metrics; temper elegance claims.
