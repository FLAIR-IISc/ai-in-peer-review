- Paper tackles learning the full DPP kernel via a novel EM-style algorithm using eigenvalue/eigenvector reparameterization.  
- Key insight: view a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs with latent subset J of eigenvectors.  
- EM lower-bounds the log-likelihood with an auxiliary q(J|Y); E-step yields a conditional k-DPP, M-step updates eigenvalues/eigenvectors.  
- Eigenvalue updates have a closed-form; eigenvector updates use a single gradient step on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Empirical results on product recommendations show EM outperforms projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent), especially with poor initialization or limited data.  
- EM is reported to be asymptotically and empirically faster than the K-Ascent baseline.  
- Originality/significance: unconstrained full-kernel learning is a substantial advance over prior restricted convex settings.  
- Technical quality: elegant derivation, solid connection between latent J and elementary DPPs, and avoidance of problematic eigenvalue projection.  
- Clarity and validation: well-written, clear algorithm description, illustrative qualitative example (baby registry), and runtime analysis.  
- M-step approximations: eigenvector update is only a single gradient step and uses true likelihood for line search instead of the lower bound, deviating from standard EM.  
- Evaluation metric concern: reporting "relative test log-likelihood differences" is unconventional and needs justification or replacement with absolute differences.  
- Experimental suggestion: run EM initialized from K-Ascent’s converged solution to stronger demonstrate EM’s ability to find better local optima.  
- Minor presentation note: adopt a more neutral scientific tone in writing.
