- Proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices, using mixtures of elementary DPPs and alternating variational (k-DPP) updates with an M-step gradient update.  
- Main contribution is the novel objective reformulation that outperforms standard differentiated log-likelihood with projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- Empirical results show EM consistently beats KA with simple initialization and gives modest gains even with informed KA initialization.  
- The M-step is complex and depends on practical conditions/approximations; achieving a workable M-step is a notable result.  
- Experiments should more thoroughly analyze initialization effects, especially EM initialized from KA solutions.  
- Question the reported metric: relative log-likelihood is unclear; differences in log-probabilities would be more interpretable than ratios of log-likelihoods.  
- Stylistic issues: avoid self-praise (calling the algorithm “elegant”) and excessive bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and novel approach with demonstrable but not large gains; experiments could be strengthened, yet the paper is solid.
