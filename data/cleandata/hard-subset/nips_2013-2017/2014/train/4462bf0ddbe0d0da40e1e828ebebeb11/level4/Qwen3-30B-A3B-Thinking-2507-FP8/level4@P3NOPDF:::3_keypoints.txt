- Problem: Estimating the full PSD matrix L is NP-hard; prior work used restrictive parametric forms that sacrifice DPP diversity.  
- Proposed method: Freely parameterize L without projection steps to preserve both quality and diversity.  
- Key insight: Leverage full-rank eigenvectors to remove need for eigenvector projection and use Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable EM over eigenvalues/eigenvectors.  
- Theoretical contribution: Map constrained optimization to the Stiefel manifold to avoid projections while maintaining similarity constraints.  
- Proof: Show the inverse distribution is a valid DPP, strengthening the theoretical foundation.  
- Derivation quality: EM derivation is rigorous, clear, and enhances reproducibility via step-by-step reduction.  
- Experimental results: Synthetic and one real-world product recommendation task show maintained diversity and competitive quality; outperforms baseline KA on top-10 recommendations.  
- Limitation: Experimental validation is limited to one real-world dataset, reducing evidence of broad applicability.  
- Writing: Paper is cohesive with concise, accessible proofs; EM derivation is the strongest section.  
- Conclusion gap: Conclusion fails to explicitly highlight the main innovations (free parameterization without projection and the inverse DPP proof).  
- Minor issues: Imprecise phrasing ("compromises on the diversity dimension"), incorrect term ("scalar vector" for L), and unclear baseline reference ("KA").  
- Overall verdict: Strong, novel, and technically sound contribution that merits acceptance; recommend strengthening the conclusion to emphasize key innovations.
