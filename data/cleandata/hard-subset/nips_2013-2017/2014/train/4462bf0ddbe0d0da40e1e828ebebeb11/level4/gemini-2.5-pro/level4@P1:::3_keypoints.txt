- Tackles the hard problem of learning the full DPP kernel matrix L (non-convex, conjectured NP-hard) rather than restricted convex variants.  
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes L by its eigendecomposition (eigenvalues and eigenvectors) and treats eigenvector selection as a latent variable.  
- Derives a Jensen-based lower bound on the log-likelihood and alternates E-step and M-step updates.  
- Shows the E-step posterior is itself a k-DPP, enabling efficient posterior computation.  
- Frames the eigenvector update as optimization on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding unstable projection steps and providing a robust multiplicative update.  
- Technical derivations are sound, insightful, and well-executed.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with effective motivation for avoiding naive projected gradient ascent.  
- Empirical results on a real-world baby registry task show higher test log-likelihoods, faster convergence, and better qualitative diversity than projected gradient ascent, especially in data-poor or poorly initialized settings.  
- Comparison baseline is limited to projected gradient ascent on the full kernel and omits restricted/convex prior methods, reducing comprehensiveness.  
- Qualitative evaluation is limited (single top-ten example with only a two-item swap); more examples or a user study would strengthen practical claims.  
- Conclusion is brief and could better summarize key technical insights (k-DPP connection in E-step, Stiefel optimization in M-step).
