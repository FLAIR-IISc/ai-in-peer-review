- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP parameters combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, DPP-as-mixtures representation, and a Jensen lower bound; outperforms a K-Ascent baseline.
- Method appears technically sound but experimental evaluation is limited and should include broader state-of-the-art subset-selection baselines.
- Clarity suffers: notation is sometimes used before definition and the exposition is often convoluted.
- Paper structure interrupts logical flow by narrating derivations and side remarks instead of presenting the final method up front.
- Recommendation: present the final algorithm directly and move alternative approaches/derivations to a separate section.
- Originality: highly original—apparently the first work to propose this learning approach for DPPs.
- Significance: represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset-selection literature.
- p.2 l.81: marginal kernel K is used before being defined; clarify what constitutes a marginal kernel.
- p.2 l.100: matrix V is not defined; introduce and define V where first used.
- p.3 l.159: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear—rephrase as finding the closest PSD matrix to Q in Frobenius norm.
- p.4: section is hard to follow; provide a self-contained outline of the proposed approach instead of deferring to [26].
- p.4 l.189: the term “weight” is used without a clear definition in context.
- p.7 l.324: rationale for initialization in eq.(30) is unclear (why omit m_{ij}); justify initialization and compare to simpler options like using empirical covariance.
