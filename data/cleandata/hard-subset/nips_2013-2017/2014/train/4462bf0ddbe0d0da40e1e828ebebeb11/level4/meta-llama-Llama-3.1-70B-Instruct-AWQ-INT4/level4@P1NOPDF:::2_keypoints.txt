- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, DPP-as-mixture representation, and a Jensen lower bound; experiments show improvements over a simple K-Ascent baseline.

- Method appears well-founded and likely works as claimed, but the empirical evaluation is limited.

- Experiments fail to compare against alternative subset-selection methods (mentioned in the introduction), so the practical benefit of the DPP approach is unclear.

- Writing and presentation are unclear and sometimes sloppy: undefined notation, definitions appearing after use, and a scattered structure that mixes derivation with side remarks.

- Specific clarity/notation issues: marginal kernel K undefined (p.2, l.81); V undefined (p.2, l.100); ambiguous phrasing about Frobenius-norm minimization (p.3, l.159); unclear “weight” term (p.4, l.189).

- Section on the referenced method (p.4) is hard to follow and should summarize the approach rather than simply referring to [26].

- Equation (30) rationale is unclear (p.7, l.324): why is m_{ij} omitted and why not initialize with the empirical covariance matrix?

- Originality is high—the reviewer is not aware of similar work.

- Significance: the method is a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset-selection literature.

- Overall recommendation: promising and non-trivial work but needs clearer exposition and stronger, broader experimental comparisons.
