The paper presents an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method effectively combines several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a lower-bound on the log-likelihood derived using Jensen’s Inequality. Experiments demonstrate favorable performance of the proposed approach compared to a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.

**Quality:** The method appears well-founded and the claims made are believable. However, the evaluation could be significantly improved. The experiments are limited in scope and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), making it unclear how substantial the benefits of the DPP approach are.

**Clarity:** The paper’s presentation lacks clarity. The writing is somewhat sloppy and difficult to follow, with notation occasionally used before definition. The text is often unnecessarily convoluted, structured as a procedural description of the method’s development, interweaving alternative approaches and potential issues with the main line of reasoning. A more direct presentation of the developed method, followed by a separate discussion of alternatives and challenges, would be preferable.

**Originality:** The paper appears to be quite original; I am not aware of similar work in this area.

**Significance:** I believe the method presented represents a solid advance and a valuable contribution to the literature on subset selection.

**Details:**

*   p.2, line 81: The “marginal kernel K” is used before being defined. Clarification is needed regarding what properties qualify a kernel as a marginal kernel.
*   p.2, line 100: The variable V is not defined prior to its use.
*   p.3, line 159: “Minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclearly stated. A more precise description would be “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to Q.”
*   p.4: This section is difficult to understand. Rather than relying on a reference to [26], outlining the proposed (and presumably simpler) approach directly would greatly benefit the reader and avoid forcing them to consult the more complex original method.
*   p.4, line 189: The meaning of “weight” is unclear in this context and requires clarification.
*   p.7, line 324: The rationale behind equation (30) is unclear. Why is m_{ij} not utilized? Furthermore, why would simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not have been a suitable initialization strategy? 

In summary, the paper proposes a highly nontrivial and likely original method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. However, the writing requires improvement, and the experimental evaluation would be strengthened by comparisons to a broader range of subset selection techniques.
