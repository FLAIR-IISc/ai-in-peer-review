- Introduces an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that integrates eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood lower bound.  
- Experimental results show promising gains over a simpler K-Ascent baseline.  
- Methodology appears sound and results plausible, but empirical evaluation is limited in scope.  
- Lacks comparisons to established subset-selection techniques, making the practical advantages of DPPs hard to judge.  
- Presentation and writing are imprecise and sometimes hard to follow due to mixed methodological development and discussion of alternatives.  
- Paper structure should present the proposed method directly and defer alternative approaches to improve readability.  
- Notation is inconsistent, with some symbols used before being defined.  
- The work is highly original with no obvious similar prior research.  
- The contribution is significant and technically strong for subset selection methodology.  
- p.2, line 81: The “marginal kernel” K is used without prior definition; clarify qualifying properties of K.  
- p.2, line 100: The variable V is undefined at first use.  
- p.3, line 159: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is ambiguous; state precisely (e.g., closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm).  
- p.4: The section is hard to understand; directly outline the simpler approach instead of relying on reference [26].  
- p.4, line 189: Clarify what is meant by “weight” in this context.  
- p.7, line 324: Explain rationale behind equation (30), why m_ij was not used, and why the empirical covariance matrix was unsuitable as an initial kernel.  
- Overall recommendation: improve clarity/notation and broaden empirical comparisons to strengthen the paper.
