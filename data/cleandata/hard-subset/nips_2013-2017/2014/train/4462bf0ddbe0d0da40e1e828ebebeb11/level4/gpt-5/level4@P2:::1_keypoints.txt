- Proposes an EM-style optimization that parameterizes a DPP by eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introduces a latent variable over eigen-index sets J.
- Uses the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view to define an auxiliary distribution q(J|Y) and sets q to the true posterior pK(J|Y) in the E-step (a k-DPP).
- Eigenvalue updates are closed-form and computed efficiently via k-DPP marginals.
- Eigenvector updates are done by a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold with line search, making the method a generalized EM.
- Avoids problematic PSD projection used in projected-gradient (KA), which can force kernels toward near-diagonality.
- Empirically improves test log-likelihood over KA on Amazon baby registries, substantially with naive initialization and modestly with moment-matching initialization.
- Typically faster in wall-clock time and reduces expensive matrix inversions, benefiting when observed sets are small (k ≪ N).
- Derivation of the EM lower bound and E-step is sound and leverages known DPP identities.
- The eigenvector single-step update is practical but lacks discussion of convergence guarantees and safeguards near eigenvalues λ ≈ 1.
- Runtime analysis is clear and empirical runtimes corroborate theoretical benefits on the presented dataset.
- Paper is generally well organized and readable; the E-step and QYi construction are well explained.
- Needs a concise intuition for why KA’s PSD projection induces near-diagonality and how eigenspace updates avoid it.
- The term “relative log-likelihood” is unclear; absolute test log-likelihoods and normalized per-set metrics with error bars should be reported.
- Novelty comes from casting full-kernel DPP learning as EM over the eigenspace; combination of known components for full K learning is new.
- Significance: clear empirical and runtime benefits, especially in data-poor or poorly initialized regimes.
- Evaluation is limited to a single application; additional domains or synthetic ground-truth experiments would strengthen claims.
- Suggest analyzing sensitivity to different initializations, including initializing EM from KA and variations in Wishart scaling/moment-matching.
- Recommend demonstrating monotonic likelihood increases across EM iterations, clarifying stopping criteria, and reporting std. deviations across runs.
- Suggest adding downstream diversity or MAP subset quality metrics to complement likelihood-based evaluation.
- Request discussion of scaling to larger N and whether approximations (e.g., Nyström) could be integrated.
- Ask for practical details on how λ is kept below 1 and whether this constraint limits performance.
- Pros: addresses learning full DPP kernels without restrictive parameterizations, technically sound EM leveraging DPP structure, empirical and runtime improvements.
- Cons: M-step’s single-step updates lack strong convergence guarantees and may be costly; gains over well-initialized KA are modest; presentation choices (metrics, tone) hinder interpretability.
- Overall assessment: a solid, well-motivated contribution that advances practical DPP learning via eigendecomposition-based EM, with room for broader evaluation and convergence/metric clarifications.
