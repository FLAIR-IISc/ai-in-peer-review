This paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning the parameters of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP). The approach integrates several non-trivial components, including eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and the use of Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the log-likelihood. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm outperforms a simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.

**Quality**

The proposed method appears to be technically sound and well-founded. The derivation of the EM algorithm is plausible, and I believe the approach should perform as described.

However, the empirical evaluation could be significantly strengthened. The experiments presented are quite limited in scope. Crucially, the paper does not include comparisons to alternative methods for subset selection beyond the DPP literature (e.g., some of the methods mentioned in the introduction). Without this broader comparison, it is difficult to assess the practical benefit of using this specific DPP learning approach over other established techniques in the field.

**Clarity**

The paper is not written with sufficient clarity, which makes it difficult to follow. The overall presentation feels somewhat unstructured, with undefined notation and definitions that appear only after a term has already been used.

The writing is also unnecessarily convoluted at times. The narrative seems to follow the author's discovery process, interleaving the main technical development with side-remarks about potential alternatives and problems. The paper would be substantially improved if it were restructured to first present the final proposed method as clearly and directly as possible, relegating discussions of alternatives and justifications for design choices to a separate discussion section.

**Originality**

To the best of my knowledge, the work is original. I am not aware of other papers that have proposed a similar EM-based framework for learning DPPs.

**Significance**

Despite the issues with clarity and evaluation, the method developed in this paper represents a solid technical advance. If presented more clearly and validated more thoroughly, it would be a useful and significant contribution to the literature on DPPs and subset selection.

**Detailed Comments**

*   **p.2, line 81:** The marginal kernel K is used before it is formally defined. The authors should clarify what properties a kernel must have to be considered a marginal kernel and define it before its first use.
*   **p.2, line 100:** The matrix V is used without a prior definition.
*   **p.3, line 159:** The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is imprecise. It would be clearer to state that the algorithm "chooses the closest positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to Q in the Frobenius norm sense."
*   **p.4:** This section is difficult to understand. Rather than simply referring to the more complex method in [26], the paper would be more self-contained and accessible if the authors provided a brief outline of their simplified approach directly. This would prevent readers from needing to consult an external reference to understand a core part of the methodology.
*   **p.4, line 189:** The term "weight" is used without a clear definition in this context. To what does this refer?
*   **p.7, line 324:** The rationale for the initialization in equation (30) is unclear. Could the authors elaborate on why the term m_ij is omitted from this calculation? Additionally, it would be helpful to understand why a simpler approach, such as using the empirical covariance matrix as the initial kernel, would not be a sufficient or suitable initialization strategy.
