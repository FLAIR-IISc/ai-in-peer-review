This paper addresses the problem of learning the kernel matrix for a Determinantal Point Process (DPP), a model valued for its ability to balance quality and diversity in subset selection. While learning the full kernel is NP-hard, prior work has focused on restricted learning settings, such as learning scalar weights or fixed kernel combinations, often sacrificing the ability to model diversity effectively. This paper proposes a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm that learns the full kernel matrix by reparameterizing it in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and leveraging a lower bound on the likelihood. This approach avoids the problematic projection steps inherent in direct gradient ascent, which can lead to degenerate solutions and a loss of diversity. 

The core idea – exploiting the eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix – is well-motivated and clearly presented. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of related work in DPPs and subset selection, properly situating their contribution within the existing literature. They convincingly argue that previous approaches fall short in fully capturing the trade-off between quality and diversity, and position their work as a significant step toward addressing this limitation. The mathematical derivations, particularly those surrounding the EM update steps, are detailed and appear sound. The use of Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable lower bound is a standard technique, but its application here is well-justified and executed. 

The strengths of the paper lie in its technical soundness and the clarity of its presentation. The algorithm is described in a step-by-step manner, making it accessible to readers familiar with DPPs and EM. The avoidance of the projection step, and the resulting preservation of diversity, is a key contribution. The connection to optimization on the Stiefel manifold further demonstrates the authors’ technical depth. Experimental results on a product recommendation task support the claim that the proposed EM algorithm outperforms projected gradient ascent, particularly in scenarios where diversity is crucial. The analysis of the runtime, showing the asymptotic speedup of the EM approach, is also valuable.

However, the paper could be strengthened by a more extensive experimental evaluation. While the product recommendation task provides a useful case study, exploring additional datasets—perhaps those with varying degrees of inherent diversity or different characteristics—would enhance the generalizability of the findings. The impact of different initialization strategies could also be investigated more thoroughly. Though the authors acknowledge the importance of initialization, a more systematic comparison of different initialization methods would be beneficial. Additionally, while the authors briefly mention the potential for applying the algorithm to other domains (document summarization, sensor placement), elaborating on these potential applications with preliminary results or concrete examples could further highlight the significance of their work. The conclusion feels somewhat abbreviated given the technical contributions presented, and could benefit from a more emphatic restatement of the key advancements.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed EM algorithm offers a novel approach to learning DPP kernels, addressing a known NP-hard problem without restrictive parameterizations.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The algorithm is mathematically well-founded, with clear derivations and justifications for each step.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Experiments demonstrate that the algorithm outperforms a baseline approach on a relevant task.
*   **Significance:** The ability to learn DPP kernels without sacrificing diversity is a valuable contribution with potential applications in various fields.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the proposed method.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, with a wider range of datasets and initialization strategies.
*   **Abbreviated Conclusion:** The conclusion could more fully articulate the key contributions of the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a technically sound and novel solution to an important problem. The proposed EM algorithm offers a significant improvement over existing methods for learning DPP kernels, and the experimental results support the authors’ claims. While a more extensive experimental evaluation would further strengthen the paper, it is currently of sufficient quality for acceptance to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand the discussion in the conclusion and potentially add additional experimental results.
