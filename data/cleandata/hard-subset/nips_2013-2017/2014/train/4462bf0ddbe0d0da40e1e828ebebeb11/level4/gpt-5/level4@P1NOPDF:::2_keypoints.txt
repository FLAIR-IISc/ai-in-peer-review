- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture representation of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound.  
- Experiments show the EM method outperforms a simpler “K-Ascent” baseline but evaluation is limited.  
- Method appears well-founded and believable in its behavior.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: too few experiments and no comparison to alternative subset-selection methods, so practical benefit over other approaches is unclear.  
- Writing and exposition are unclear and sometimes hard to follow; notation and definitions are introduced out of order.  
- Narrative is convoluted by tracing derivation choices and alternatives instead of presenting the final method clearly.  
- Reviewers believe the work is original and not aware of similar prior work.  
- The method is a meaningful and useful contribution to subset-selection literature.  
- Specific clarity/notation issues: marginal kernel K used before definition (p.2, l.81), V undefined (p.2, l.100).  
- Specific wording/math fixes: clarify “minimizes the change to Frobenius norm” (p.3, l.159), explain “weight” (p.4, l.189).  
- Request to outline the simpler approach directly instead of referring to [26] (p.4).  
- Question rationale in equation (30): why m_{ij} not used and why not initialize with empirical covariance (p.7, l.324).
