The paper introduces an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). This method effectively integrates several complex components, including kernel eigendecomposition, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a DPP mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen’s Inequality-based log-likelihood lower bound. Experimental results demonstrate promising performance gains over a simpler K-Ascent approach.

**Quality:** The proposed methodology appears sound and the claimed results are plausible. However, the empirical evaluation would benefit from expansion. The current experiments are limited in scope and lack comparison to established subset selection techniques (as acknowledged in the introduction), making it difficult to assess the distinct advantages offered by the DPP framework.

**Clarity:** The paper’s presentation could be significantly improved. The writing is somewhat imprecise and can be challenging to follow due to an interweaving of methodological development, alternative approaches, and potential issues. A clearer structure, presenting the developed method directly and deferring detailed discussions of alternatives, would enhance readability. Notation is also inconsistent, with some symbols used before definition. 

**Originality:** The work appears highly original; I am not currently aware of similar research in this area.

**Significance:** This paper presents a valuable and technically strong contribution to the field of subset selection, representing a genuine advancement in the methodology.

**Details:**
- p.2, line 81: The “marginal kernel” *K* is used without prior definition. Clarification regarding the characteristics of a qualifying marginal kernel is needed.
- p.2, line 100: The variable *V* is undefined at its first use.
- p.3, line 159: The phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is ambiguous. A more precise statement, such as “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite matrix to Q,” would be preferable.
- p.4: This section is difficult to understand. Directly outlining the proposed, simpler approach, rather than relying on a reference to [26], would improve clarity and reduce the burden on the reader.
- p.4, line 189: Clarify the meaning of “weight” in this context.
- p.7, line 324: The rationale behind equation (30) is unclear. Explain why *m<sub>ij</sub>* was not utilized, and why the empirical covariance matrix proved unsuitable as an initial kernel.

In summary, this paper introduces a novel and potentially impactful method for learning DPPs for subset selection. While technically strong and original, improvements to the clarity of the writing and the breadth of the experimental evaluation are recommended.
