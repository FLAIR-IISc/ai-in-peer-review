- Problem: Learning a full PSD kernel L for DPPs is NP-hard; prior work restricts L or uses projected gradients that can harm DPP diversity.  
- Main contribution: A novel EM-based learning algorithm that optimizes eigenvalues and eigenvectors of L without restricting its parameterization.  
- Key technique: Use Jensen’s inequality to derive an EM lower bound and optimize eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold to avoid projection steps.  
- Theoretical insight: Proof that the inverse distribution is also a DPP.  
- Strength — preservation: Method preserves both quality and diversity properties of DPPs better than prior approaches.  
- Strength — technical: Clear, step-by-step EM derivation and an elegant manifold-based treatment of orthonormality constraints.  
- Strength — presentation: Coherent writing and well-executed proofs.  
- Strength — experiments: Validation on synthetic and real product-recommendation data; comparison to KA baseline shows improved maintenance of diverse, high-quality recommendations.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Needs evaluation on a broader range of real-world datasets to strengthen practical claims.  
- Weakness — conclusion: Conclusion is brief and does not fully recap the paper’s novel optimization and theoretical contributions.
