The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method combines several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representing DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood via Jensen’s inequality. The experiments show favorable behavior compared with a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.

Quality: As far as I can tell, the method is well-founded, and it is believable that it works as stated. However, the evaluation could be strengthened. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), so it remains unclear how much practical benefit the DPP approach provides over other methods.

Clarity: The paper is not particularly clearly written. The write-up is somewhat hard to follow: not all notation is defined before use, and some definitions appear only after the symbols are introduced. The exposition is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, in part because the narrative traces the process by which the final approach was derived, with side remarks about alternative paths and pitfalls interleaved with the main line of argument. I would prefer to present the final method as clearly and directly as possible, postponing discussion and alternatives to a later section.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge, the paper is original; I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: The method appears to be a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset-selection literature.

Details:
- p.2, l. 81: The marginal kernel K is used before being defined. What exactly qualifies as a “marginal kernel” in this context?
- p.2, l. 100: V is not defined.
- p.3, l. 159: “Minimizes the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. Better: “chooses the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.”
- p.4: I found this section hard to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would help to outline your simpler approach directly so the reader is not forced to look up the more complex original method.
- p.4, l. 189: What does “weight” refer to?
- p.7, l. 324: I do not follow the rationale in (30). Why is m_{ij} not used here? Why wouldn’t simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel work as an initialization?

In summary, the paper proposes a new, nontrivial method for learning DPPs for subset selection that I assume is original. The exposition is unclear in places, and the experiments consider only DPP-based methods, limiting the assessment of practical impact.
