- Contribution: Proposes an EM-based procedure to learn full DPP kernels via eigen-decomposition, avoiding costly eigen-projections and optimizing eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold; shows an “inverse” distribution also defines a DPP.

- Strength — Technical novelty: The Jensen-based EM derivation on eigenvalues/eigenvectors is well-motivated, carefully developed, and clearly presented.

- Strength — Avoiding projections: Formulating eigenvector updates on the Stiefel manifold removes separate eigenvalue/eigenvector projections that can distort kernels.

- Strength — Diversity preservation: Learning the full kernel (not just scalar/diagonal quality parameters) better preserves DPPs’ diversity-inducing properties.

- Strength — Additional theory: The Stiefel-manifold mapping and the inverse-distribution-is-a-DPP result are interesting theoretical contributions.

- Strength — Presentation: Paper is coherent, proofs are well written, and derivations are accessible.

- Weakness — Experimental scope: Empirical evaluation is limited to synthetic data and a single product recommendation task, hindering assessment of generality and robustness.

- Weakness — Baselines and metrics: Unclear what “KA” is and whether it’s a strong baseline; the paper lacks standard ranking and explicit diversity metrics (e.g., NDCG, intra-list similarity, coverage).

- Weakness — Practicality/scalability: Per-iteration complexity, memory footprint, and scalability to large ground sets are not sufficiently analyzed or compared to alternatives.

- Weakness — Sensitivity and initialization: EM’s sensitivity to initialization, convergence diagnostics, and eigenvalue regularization are not studied or discussed.

- Weakness — Assumptions on rank: The handling of rank-deficient L and the assumption that V is full rank need clarification and formal treatment.

- Weakness — Ambiguous interpretation: Qualitative claims about recommendation changes (e.g., “two of the top-10 items are replaced”) lack quantitative evidence that diversity improves while quality is maintained.

- Suggestion — Expand evaluation: Test on more real-world tasks (image summarization, document/video selection, other recommendation datasets) and report standard ranking/diversity/coverage metrics.

- Suggestion — Stronger baselines and ablations: Compare to diagonal-only, low-rank factorizations, k-DPPs, and projected-gradient methods; include ablations (eigenvalues-only vs. full updates).

- Suggestion — Computational analysis: Provide per-iteration complexity, memory scaling with ground-set size/effective rank, runtime/convergence plots, and sensitivity to initialization.

- Suggestion — Clarify technical points: Define “KA”, justify baseline choice, explain the inverse-DPP implication, and specify handling/regularization of zero or near-zero eigenvalues.

- Suggestion — Strengthen conclusion: Conclude by explicitly summarizing EM derivation, Stiefel optimization, theoretical results, and empirical takeaways.

- Overall assessment: Technically solid and principled approach that respects eigenvector constraints and advances DPP learning; main limitation is narrow empirical validation and unclear baseline framing, which if addressed would make the work a strong contribution.
