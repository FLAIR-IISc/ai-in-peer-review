This paper addresses the problem of learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The core contribution is a novel objective reformulation that surpasses the performance of standard kernel parameter optimization via log-probability differentiation and projected gradient ascent – the method termed K-Ascent (KA) in the paper. The authors achieve this by framing the problem within an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework. This is motivated by the fact that a DPP can be expressed as a mixture of “elementary DPPs,” one for each possible subset of elements. The proposed algorithm iteratively updates a variational distribution over these subsets, modeled as a k-DPP, and then performs a gradient-based update in the M-step. Empirical results demonstrate that the EM approach consistently outperforms KA when initialized with standard parameters, and provides modest gains even with more informed KA initialization.

I find this approach to be a valuable contribution, and the EM method appears to offer a genuine improvement. While the M-step is computationally complex and relies on certain approximations and caveats that feel somewhat ad-hoc, the fact that it functions effectively is noteworthy. 

Regarding the experimental evaluation, I would appreciate a more thorough investigation into the impact of initialization on the EM algorithm. Specifically, exploring whether initializing EM with the solution obtained from KA leads to further performance gains would be insightful. 

I also have a question regarding the reporting of results using relative log likelihood. Given that log probabilities reflect probability ratios, the rationale for taking ratios *of* log probabilities is unclear. Reporting differences in log probabilities would likely be more interpretable.

Finally, as a minor point, I found some aspects of the writing style distracting. Self-referential descriptions of the algorithm as “elegant” seem unwarranted, particularly given the approximations involved. Additionally, the use of bold text in the introduction is unnecessary. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting an interesting new approach to kernel learning for DPPs. While the performance improvements over the baseline gradient method are not substantial, the work is well-executed and represents a worthwhile contribution.
