The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), combining several non-trivial ingredients: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimisation on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality. The experiments demonstrate favourable behaviour of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and it is believable that it should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), making it unclear how much benefit the DPP approach provides.

Clarity: The paper's writing is not particularly clear. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow, with undefined notation and occasional definitions appearing after their use. The text is structured to describe the process of arriving at the proposed approach, with side remarks on possible alternative approaches and problems interleaved with the main thread. A clearer presentation of the developed method, with discussion postponed to a later stage, would be preferable.

Originality: The paper appears to be quite original, as I am not aware of similar work in this area.

Significance: I believe the method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:

- p. 2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is not defined before use. What kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel?
- p. 2, line 100: V is not defined.
- p. 3, line 159: Instead of saying "minimises the change to Frobenius norm," it would be clearer to say "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to Q."
- p. 4: I found this section challenging to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline the approach directly, allowing the reader to understand the method without needing to look up the more complex original method.
- p. 4, line 189: The term "weight" is unclear; please specify what it refers to.
- p. 7, line 324: The rationale behind equation (30) is not entirely clear. Why is m_{ij} not used here? Why would simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not have worked as an initialisation?

Overall, the paper proposes a new method for learning DPPs for subset selection, which is highly non-trivial and likely original. However, the write-up could be improved for clarity, and the experiments should consider alternative methods beyond DPPs.
