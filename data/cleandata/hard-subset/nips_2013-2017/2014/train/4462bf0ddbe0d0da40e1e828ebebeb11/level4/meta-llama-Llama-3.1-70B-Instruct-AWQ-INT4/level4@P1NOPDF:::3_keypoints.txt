- DPPs model diversity via a PSD matrix L, but learning L is NP-hard and prior work only partially learns L or restricts its form.
- Previous approaches learned scalar/vector forms of L or constrained parameterizations, compromising DPP's diversity dimension.
- The authors propose a learning method that does not restrict the parameterization of L.
- Their method avoids projection steps that can harm similarity/diversity by using an EM approach on eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- They use Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the objective and derive a step-by-step EM procedure.
- They exploit that eigenvector matrix V is full-rank and map the constraint to optimization over the Stiefel manifold, eliminating eigenvector projection.
- They show the inverse distribution is itself a DPP.
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support that the method preserves both quality and diversity.
- Comparison to KL-divergence approximation (KA) shows improved top-10 product selections (KA replaced two of the top-10 with less likely items).
- Proofs are well-written and the EM reduction is clearly explained; overall writing is cohesive.
- The conclusion is somewhat hasty and under-emphasizes contributions.
- Recommend additional experiments on real-world datasets to better illustrate the produced diversity.
