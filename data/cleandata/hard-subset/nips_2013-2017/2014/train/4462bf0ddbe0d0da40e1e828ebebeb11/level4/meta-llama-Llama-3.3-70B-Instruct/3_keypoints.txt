- Proposes a novel, unrestricted method for learning DPP kernels without imposing parametric restrictions on L.  
- Addresses NP-hardness of learning L by avoiding previous partial-learning or restrictive approaches.  
- Uses an EM algorithm over eigenvalues and eigenvectors to learn the DPP.  
- Avoids eigenvector projection by exploiting that V is full-rank, preserving DPP diversity.  
- Employs Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM procedure.  
- Maps the constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold to eliminate eigenvector projection.  
- Step-by-step EM derivation is clear and insightful, with well-written proofs.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic data and a product recommendation task, retaining both quality and diversity.  
- Reduction of the optimization problem to an EM framework is interesting and well-explained.  
- Writing is cohesive, but the conclusion should better summarize the paper’s contributions.  
- Recommends additional experiments on real-world datasets to further validate the algorithm’s diversity benefits.
