- Problem: Learns the full DPP kernel matrix without restrictive assumptions, addressing a key limitation of prior work.  
- Main contribution: Proposes an EM algorithm using eigendecomposition and a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation to avoid projection steps.  
- Method: Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a lower bound, alternating E-step (k-DPP distribution) and M-step (eigenvalue/eigenvector updates) with efficient k-DPP marginalization.  
- Computational complexity: Presented complexity O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) versus K-Ascent’s O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3).  
- Experiments: Tested on Amazon baby registry; EM outperforms K-Ascent by up to 16.5% with poor initialization and by ~0.4% with moments-matching init.  
- Analysis: Explains performance gap via negative interaction strength d = (1/N)||M||F/||diag(M)||2, matching theoretical concerns about K-Ascent’s projection.  
- Strength: Fills an important gap by enabling full kernel learning for DPPs without restrictive assumptions.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-motivated theoretical development with attention to computational costs.  
- Strength: Appropriate real-world experimental evaluation demonstrating practical value.  
- Strength: Authors acknowledge and analyze initialization sensitivity and other limitations.  
- Weakness: Does not investigate initializing EM from K-Ascent solutions, missing a useful refinement comparison.  
- Weakness: Uses relative log-likelihood percentages (e.g., “16.5% gain”), which is nonstandard and confusing; absolute log-likelihood differences should be clarified.  
- Weakness: Writing/style issues (unnecessary claims of “elegance,” bold text, caveats in eigenvector updates) reduce professionalism.  
- Weakness: Practical significance of the 16.5% gain is limited—average improvement is only 3.7% despite added algorithmic complexity.  
- Quality: Technically sound with solid theoretical and empirical support, though M-step caveats slightly undermine elegance.  
- Clarity: Generally well-organized and clear, but would benefit from improved writing and clearer metric interpretation.  
- Originality: Novel application of EM to full DPP kernel learning; addresses a previously conjectured-hard problem.  
- Significance: Useful in challenging scenarios with poor initialization and strong negative interactions; modest average gains limit broad transformative impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid contribution with addressable issues, especially metric reporting.
