- Paper proposes reformulating DPP kernel learning as an EM problem, representing a DPP as a mixture of "elementary DPPs" and using a variational k-DPP over subsets.  
- Algorithm alternates between updating the subset distribution (E-step) and taking single gradient steps in a somewhat involved M-step.  
- Empirical results show the EM method outperforms standard projected gradient (K-Ascent), especially with naive initialization and less so with smarter initialization.  
- The M-step requires caveats and approximations that are not entirely satisfactory, though the authors managed to implement them.  
- Reviewer requests deeper experimental analysis of initialization effects, e.g., initializing EM with KA results.  
- Critique of the performance metric: using ratios of log likelihoods is confusing—differences in log probabilities would be clearer.  
- Minor writing issues: avoid calling the algorithm "elegant" given the caveats, and avoid unnecessary bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: interesting approach with modest empirical gains and room for improved experiments; generally a reasonably written paper.
