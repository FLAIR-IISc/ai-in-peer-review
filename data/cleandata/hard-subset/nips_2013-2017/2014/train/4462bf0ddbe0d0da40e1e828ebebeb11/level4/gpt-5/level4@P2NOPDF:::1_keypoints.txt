- Paper proposes an EM-based reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices, improving over standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent, KA).
- Core idea: express a DPP as a mixture over “elementary DPPs” (one per subset) and introduce a variational k-DPP over subsets.
- Algorithm alternates an E-step updating the k-DPP variational distribution and an M-step taking single gradient updates of kernel parameters.
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms KA under naive initialization and gives smaller but sometimes meaningful gains with careful KA initialization.
- The M-step is involved and relies on approximations/caveats, which temper claims of conceptual elegance.
- Reviewer requests more analysis of initialization effects, particularly whether EM improves further when initialized from KA.
- Reviewer objects to the use of “relative log-likelihood” (ratios of log-likelihoods) and recommends reporting log-likelihood differences or absolute log-likelihoods for interpretability.
- Minor writing/style suggestions: avoid self-describing the method as “elegant” and remove unnecessary boldface in the introduction.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel EM perspective with real (though not uniformly large) empirical gains; clarifying metrics and expanding initialization analysis would strengthen the paper.
