- Addresses learning DPP kernels; main contribution is a novel reformulation and EM-based learning method that outperforms K-Ascent (KA).
- EM formulation views a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs, introduces a variational k-DPP over subsets, and alternates E-step (update distribution) and M-step (gradient update of kernel).
- Empirically, EM significantly outperforms KA with naive initialization and yields modest improvements over well-initialized KA in some settings.
- The M-step depends on specific approximations and caveats, which weakens the method’s elegance and is a notable drawback.
- Experiments lack a thorough sensitivity analysis to initialization; should test whether EM can further refine KA-pretrained solutions.
- Evaluation metric issue: using a "relative log-likelihood" (ratio of log-likelihoods) is confusing; recommend reporting standard log-likelihood differences.
- Presentation suggestions: adopt a more objective tone (avoid subjective descriptors like "elegant") and remove unconventional boldface emphasis in the introduction.
- Overall: novel and workable contribution with tangible empirical gains, though improvements over well-initialized baselines are sometimes limited and experimental analysis could be strengthened.
