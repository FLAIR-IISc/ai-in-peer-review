The paper presents an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method combines several non-trivial ingredients: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood using Jensen’s Inequality. The experiments demonstrate favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.

**Quality:** As far as I can tell, the method is well-founded, and it is believable that the methods should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are somewhat limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), so it is not immediately clear how much benefit is derived specifically from the DPP approach.

**Clarity:** The paper could be more clearly written. The write-up is somewhat dense and can be hard to follow. Not all notation is defined before use, and sometimes the definition appears after its initial application. The writing is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, largely because the text is structured to describe the process by which the proposed approach is developed, with side remarks about possible alternative approaches and potential problems interleaved with the main narrative. I would have preferred a presentation that first clearly outlines the developed method, postponing detailed discussion to a later stage.

**Originality:** To the best of my knowledge, the paper presents quite original work. I am not aware of similar approaches in the literature.

**Significance:** I believe the method derived in the paper represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

**Details:**

- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel *K* is used before it is defined. The paper should clarify what kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.
- p.2, line 100: *V* is not defined at this point.
- p.3, line 159: “minimizes the change to Frobenius norm” is unclearly stated. It would be clearer to say “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to *Q*”.
- p.4: I found this section hard to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline your (simplified) approach directly, so the reader isn't forced to consult the more complex original method.
- p.4, line 189: What does “weight” refer to in this context?
- p.7, line 324: I don't quite understand the rationale behind (30). Why is *m<sub>ij</sub>* not used here? Why wouldn’t simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel have worked for initialization?

In summary, the paper presents a novel method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. The method is highly non-trivial and appears to be original. While the write-up could be improved for clarity and the experiments would benefit from comparison with a wider range of alternative methods, the work represents a valuable contribution to the field.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and potentially significant contribution to DPP learning.
*   Technically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Demonstrated improvement over a baseline method (K-Ascent).
*   Addresses a challenging problem (NP-hardness of full kernel learning).

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Clarity of presentation could be improved.
*   Experimental evaluation is limited; lacks comparison to non-DPP approaches.
*   The significance could be better highlighted with a broader empirical evaluation.
