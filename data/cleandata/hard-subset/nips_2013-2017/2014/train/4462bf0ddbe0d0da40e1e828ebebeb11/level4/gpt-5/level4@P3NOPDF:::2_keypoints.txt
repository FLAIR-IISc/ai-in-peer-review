- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, DPP-as-mixtures representation, and a Jensen-based lower bound, outperforming K-ascent empirically.

- Strength: Algorithmic development is technically solid and grounded in matrix analysis and manifold optimization.

- Strength: The particular EM formulation and decomposition for DPP learning appears novel.

- Strength: A more reliable DPP learning procedure could have useful impact on subset selection and diversity modeling.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to comparisons within the DPP family (vs. K-ascent) and lacks standard subset-selection baselines.

- Weakness: Paper exposition is hard to follow, with notation introduced late and derivations interleaved with the main algorithm.

- Weakness: Several design and initialization choices (e.g., Eq. (30), role of m_{ij}, not using empirical covariance) are insufficiently justified.

- Quality: Technical approach appears correct and components are plausibly integrated, but key steps need clearer, self-contained derivations.

- Quality: Paper lacks ablation studies and sensitivity analyses (initialization, component roles) to support claims.

- Quality: Needs stronger baselines and clearer reporting of computational cost, runtime, convergence, and scalability.

- Clarity: Recommend presenting the full algorithm first (definitions, assumptions, objective, parameterization, EM updates) with pseudocode.

- Clarity: Move historical derivations and alternative discussions to a later section and tighten notation, defining symbols on first use.

- Originality: The EM formulation combining spectral decomposition, Stiefel optimization, and DPP mixtures appears original.

- Significance: Contribution is promising but empirical significance requires broader comparisons and analysis of computational trade-offs.

- Request: Define the marginal kernel K explicitly early (relation to L-ensemble and constraint 0 ⪯ K ⪯ I).

- Request: Define V when introduced (eigenvector matrix, dimensions, orthogonality).

- Request: Rephrase unclear statements (e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm”) to precise mathematical wording.

- Request: Outline the simplified method directly instead of deferring to reference [26]; provide a brief derivation and assumptions.

- Request: Clarify what “weight” means in the mixture/D-step and how it enters the E-step.

- Request: Explain rationale for Eq. (30), role of m_{ij}, and justify initialization choices including why empirical covariance was not used and constraints that complicate naive initialization.

- Experiments: Add non-DPP baselines (greedy submodular methods, facility-location, other probabilistic subset models) to contextualize gains.

- Experiments: Report sensitivity to initialization/hyperparameters, convergence curves, variability across runs.

- Experiments: Provide runtime and scaling analysis versus K-ascent and others, and evaluate on at least one larger-scale dataset.

- Presentation: Provide a concise algorithm box for the EM procedure with all variables defined and clarify parameterization (K vs. L), constraints, and enforcement of the Stiefel constraint.

- Minor issues: Fix spacing/punctuation around “Jensen’s Inequality,” ensure consistent capitalization of matrix norms, and proofread for grammar and flow.

- Overall: Technically interesting and likely original; would be much stronger with clearer exposition and a broader, more thorough experimental evaluation.
