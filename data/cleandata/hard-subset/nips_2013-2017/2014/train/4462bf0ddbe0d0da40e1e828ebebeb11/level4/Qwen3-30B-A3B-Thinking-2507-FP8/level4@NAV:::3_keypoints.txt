- DPPs favor diverse subsets and are parameterized by a PSD matrix L; learning L is NP-hard.  
- Prior work restricts L or learns partially; using scalar vectors for L sacrifices diversity.  
- Existing methods often use projection steps (e.g., projected gradient ascent) that harm similarity and diversity.  
- This paper proposes an unrestricted method to learn L that avoids eigenvector projection.  
- Authors leverage full-rank eigenvector properties and Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective.  
- They derive an EM procedure that optimizes eigenvalues and eigenvectors without projecting eigenvectors.  
- The approach preserves DPP diversity, unlike prior methods that prioritized quality alone.  
- Mapping constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold eliminates eigenvector projection.  
- The paper proves the inverse distribution remains a DPP.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task show maintained quality and diversity, outperforming KA.  
- The paper is coherently written with rigorous proofs and a clear EM derivation.  
- The conclusion is somewhat premature and omits key contributions; more real-world experiments would strengthen practical impact.
