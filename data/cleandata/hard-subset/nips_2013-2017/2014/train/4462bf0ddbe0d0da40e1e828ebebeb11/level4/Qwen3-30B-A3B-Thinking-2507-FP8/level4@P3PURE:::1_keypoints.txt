- Addresses learning kernel matrices for DPPs via an EM reformulation of the objective.  
- Main contribution: EM framework that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent/KA).  
- Uses the representation of any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs (one per subset).  
- Introduces a variational distribution (k-DPP) over subsets and alternates E-step updates with single-gradient M-steps.  
- Empirical finding: EM significantly outperforms KA under naive initialization.  
- With smarter initialization, EM yields only modest improvements over KA.  
- M-step is computationally involved and requires caveats/approximations that make convergence guarantees unsatisfying.  
- Experiments need deeper analysis of initialization effects (e.g., whether initializing EM from KA’s solution helps).  
- Evaluation metric concern: relative log likelihood is confusing; differences in log probabilities would be more interpretable.  
- Writing issues: calling the algorithm “elegant” is debatable and bolding in the introduction is unnecessary.  
- Overall assessment: novel and viable approach; experimental results are sound but improvements are not uniformly substantial.
