- DPPs assign higher probability to diverse sets but learning the full PSD matrix L is NP-hard, so prior work used restricted parameterizations.  
- Learning only a scalar vector for L sacrifices the diversity dimension of DPPs.  
- Previous methods restrict the parametric form of L; this paper proposes learning without such restrictions.  
- The authors develop an EM-based learning method on eigenvalues and eigenvectors that avoids projection steps that can break diversity.  
- By exploiting full-rank V, the method removes the need to project eigenvectors, reducing projection-induced problems.  
- Jensen’s inequality is used to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM procedure.  
- Mapping constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold and showing the inverse distribution is a DPP are solid theoretical contributions.  
- The method preserves the diversity property of DPPs, unlike many prior approaches that focus only on quality.  
- The EM derivation and proofs are clear and well-explained, offering insightful step-by-step reductions.  
- Experiments on a product recommendation task show the EM algorithm outperforms K-Ascent in some cases.  
- The experimental evaluation is limited and would benefit from more real-world datasets to demonstrate practical diversity gains.  
- The conclusion should better summarize and highlight all contributions of the paper.  
- Overall: novel, well-motivated, and potentially useful, but needs broader empirical validation.
