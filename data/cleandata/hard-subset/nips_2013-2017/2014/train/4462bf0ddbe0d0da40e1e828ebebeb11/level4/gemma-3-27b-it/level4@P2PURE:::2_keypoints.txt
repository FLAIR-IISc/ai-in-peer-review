- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood lower bound.
- Experimental results favorably compare the EM method to a simpler K-Ascent baseline.
- Method appears well-founded and claims are plausible.
- Evaluation is limited in scope and needs strengthening.
- Lacks comparisons to alternative subset selection methods cited in the introduction, making DPP advantages unclear.
- Presentation is disorganized and difficult to follow; notation and definitions are sometimes introduced after first use.
- Writing is overly process-oriented, mixing final method description with alternative approaches and encountered problems.
- The work is highly original; no similar research known to the reviewer.
- The method is a significant and valuable contribution to subset selection literature.
- p.2,line 81: marginal kernel K is used before being defined—clarification needed.
- p.2,line 100: V is not defined.
- p.3,line 159: wording “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is imprecise; should state “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite matrix to Q.”
- p.4: the section is hard to understand; provide a direct outline of the simpler approach rather than deferring to [26].
- p.4,line 189: clarify what is meant by “weight.”
- p.7,line 324: rationale for equation (30) is unclear—why isn’t m_{ij} used, and why not initialize with the empirical covariance as the kernel?
