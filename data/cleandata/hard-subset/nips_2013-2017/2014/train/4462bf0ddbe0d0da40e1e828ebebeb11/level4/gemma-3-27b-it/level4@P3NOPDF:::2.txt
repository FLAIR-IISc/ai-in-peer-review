## Review for NIPS 2013-2017/2014

This paper presents an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method skillfully combines several non-trivial components – eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen’s Inequality-based lower bound on the log-likelihood. Experimental results demonstrate favorable performance compared to a simpler K-Ascent scheme.

**Quality:** The proposed method appears theoretically sound and the claims made are plausible. However, the experimental evaluation would benefit from significant expansion. While the results demonstrate improvement over the K-Ascent baseline, the experiments are limited in scope and lack comparison to alternative subset selection techniques (including those referenced in the introduction). This makes it difficult to assess the true benefit of adopting a DPP-based approach. A more comprehensive evaluation against established methods would substantially strengthen the paper.

**Clarity:** The paper suffers from clarity issues. The writing style is somewhat disorganized and challenging to follow. Notation is inconsistently defined; definitions frequently appear *after* their initial use. The text is often unnecessarily convoluted, structured as a historical account of the method’s development rather than a direct presentation of the final approach. Interleaving alternative approaches and encountered challenges with the main line of reasoning hinders comprehension. A more streamlined presentation, focusing on clearly articulating the developed method upfront, followed by a dedicated discussion of alternative approaches and their limitations, would greatly improve readability.

**Originality:** The paper appears to present original work. I am not currently aware of similar methodologies for learning DPPs in this manner.

**Significance:** The derived method represents a solid advancement and a potentially useful contribution to the subset selection literature. The ability to efficiently learn DPPs could have broad implications for various applications.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **p.2, line 81:** The “marginal kernel” *K* is used without prior definition. Clarification is needed regarding which types of kernels qualify as marginal kernels in this context.
*   **p.2, line 100:** The variable *V* is not defined at this point.
*   **p.3, line 159:** The phrasing “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. A more precise statement would be “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to *Q*.”
*   **p.4:** This section is difficult to understand. Instead of solely referencing [26], the authors should briefly outline their simplified approach directly within the paper. This would reduce the burden on the reader and improve comprehension without significantly increasing the paper’s length.
*   **p.4, line 189:** The meaning of “weight” in this context is unclear. Please provide a definition or clarify its role.
*   **p.7, line 324:** The rationale behind equation (30) is not immediately apparent. Why isn't *m<sub>ij</sub>* utilized in this step? Furthermore, the paper should address why using the empirical covariance matrix as the initial kernel was not a viable option.

In summary, this paper introduces a novel and potentially valuable method for learning DPPs for subset selection. While the technical contribution is significant, the lack of thorough experimental validation and the clarity of the presentation detract from the overall impact. Addressing these issues would substantially strengthen the paper and enhance its contribution to the field.
