This paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The approach integrates several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a mixture representation of DPPs via elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s inequality lower bound on the log-likelihood. Experiments indicate favorable performance relative to a simpler K-Ascent baseline.

Quality: The methodology appears sound and the derivations are plausible. However, the empirical evaluation is limited. The experiments do not compare against alternative subset selection methods (e.g., those cited in the introduction), making it hard to assess the practical benefit of the DPP-based approach.

Clarity: The paper is not particularly clear. The exposition is often difficult to follow, with undefined notation and definitions sometimes appearing only after use. The narrative is also unnecessarily convoluted, as it traces the path to the final method with frequent digressions about alternatives and pitfalls, which obscures the main thread. A clearer structure would present the final method first, with derivation details and alternatives deferred to later sections.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge, the contribution is original; I am not aware of closely related work proposing a similar EM approach for learning DPPs.

Significance: The method appears to be a meaningful advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Detailed comments:
- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is used before being defined. Please define it at first mention. Also clarify what is meant by a “marginal kernel” in this context.
- p.2, line 100: The matrix V is not defined before use. Please introduce it and its role.
- p.3, line 159: The phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. Prefer “chooses the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.”
- p.4: This section is difficult to follow. Rather than relying on [26], please outline your simpler approach directly so the reader can understand it without consulting the more complex original method.
- p.4, line 189: Clarify what “weight” refers to (e.g., mixture weights, eigenvalue-related weights, or something else).
- p.7, line 324: The rationale behind (30) is unclear. Why is m_{ij} not used here? Also, why wouldn’t initializing with the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel be effective?

Overall, the paper introduces a nontrivial and likely original method for learning DPPs for subset selection. While the technical foundation seems solid, the presentation needs substantial clarification, and the experimental evaluation should include comparisons with non-DPP baselines to establish the practical advantages of the proposed approach.
