The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The approach brings together several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a lower bound on the log-likelihood via Jensen’s inequality. The experiments indicate favorable performance relative to a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.

Quality: The method appears well-founded, and it is plausible that it works as described. However, the empirical evaluation could be stronger. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative subset selection methods (e.g., those cited in the introduction), making it unclear how much the DPP-based approach improves over non-DPP baselines.

Clarity: The paper is not especially clear. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Some notation is undefined when first used, and in places definitions appear only after the fact. The exposition is also needlessly convoluted at times, largely because it traces the process that led to the final method, with side remarks about alternative paths and issues interleaved with the main thread. I would prefer a presentation that first states the final method as clearly as possible, deferring discussion and alternatives to later.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge, the work is quite original; I am not aware of closely related approaches.

Significance: The method represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is used before it is defined. What exactly qualifies as a “marginal kernel” here?
- p.2, line 100: V is not defined.
- p.3, line 159: The phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. It would be better to say “chooses the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.”
- p.4: I found this section difficult to follow. Rather than referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline your (simpler) approach directly so the reader need not consult the more complex original method.
- p.4, line 189: What does “weight” refer to?
- p.7, line 324: I do not understand the rationale in (30). Why is m_{ij} not used here? Why wouldn’t simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel serve as an initialization?

In summary, the paper introduces a new, nontrivial method for learning DPPs for subset selection that appears to be original. The writing is not very clear, and the experiments do not compare against non-DPP methods.
