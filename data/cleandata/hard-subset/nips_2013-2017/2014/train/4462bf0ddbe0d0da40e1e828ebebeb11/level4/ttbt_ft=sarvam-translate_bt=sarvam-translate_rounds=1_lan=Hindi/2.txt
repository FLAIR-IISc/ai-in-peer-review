This paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method combines several non-trivial elements: the eigenvalue decomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representing DPPs as a mixture of primary DPPs, and minimizing log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality. Experiments demonstrate the proposed approach's favorable behavior compared to a simple "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: As far as I can tell, the method is well-established, and it is reliable that the methods should work as described. However, the method's evaluation could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare with alternative approaches for subset selection (such as those mentioned in the introduction), so it is unclear how much benefit we get from the DPP approach.

Clarity: The paper is not written particularly clearly. The writing is somewhat careless and difficult to understand. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes the definition comes after the use. The writing is also sometimes unnecessarily complex, mostly because the text is structured to describe a process of how something ultimately ends up with the proposed approach, mixing potential alternative approaches and side-notes on problems with the main formula. I would prefer to present the developed method as clearly as possible and postpone the discussion to a later stage.

Originality: The paper seems quite original to me; I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: I think the method obtained in the paper represents a solid progress and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- Page 2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is not defined before use. What type of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel?
- Page 2, line 100: V is not defined.
- Page 3, line 159: "reduces the change in the Frobenius norm" is stated vaguely. It would be better to say "chooses the closest (in the Frobenius norm) PSD matrix for Q".
- Page 4. I found this section difficult to understand. Instead of mentioning [26], it would be good to directly outline your (simple) approach so that the reader is not forced to look at the more complex original method.
- Page 4, line 189: What is meant by "weight"?
- Page 7, line 324. I did not fully understand the argument in (30). Why is m_{ij} not used here? Why does using only the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not work as an initialisation? The paper presents a new method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. The method is highly non-trivial, and I believe it is original. The writing is not clear enough. The experiments do not consider other DPP methods.
