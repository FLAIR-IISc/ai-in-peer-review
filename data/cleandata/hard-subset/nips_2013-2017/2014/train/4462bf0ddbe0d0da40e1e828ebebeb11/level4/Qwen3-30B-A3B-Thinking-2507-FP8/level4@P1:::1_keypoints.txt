- Addresses learning the full kernel matrix for DPPs to model subset selection balancing quality and diversity.  
- Proposes an EM algorithm leveraging the eigendecomposition and representing any DPP as a mixture of "elementary DPPs" (subsets of eigenvectors).  
- EM alternates between updating a variational distribution over eigenvector subsets (a k-DPP) and gradient updates in the M-step.  
- Method avoids the problematic projection steps required by the baseline K-Ascent (KA) projected gradient method.  
- Theoretical development is sound and well connected to prior DPP literature.  
- Empirically, EM significantly outperforms KA with naive (Wishart) initialization—up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood.  
- With KA initialized by moment-matching, EM still shows modest but consistent improvements, particularly in data-poor settings.  
- Runtime analysis indicates better asymptotic complexity for EM; empirically EM is about 2.1× faster on average.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized, with a motivating introduction and adequate mathematical background.  
- Primary performance metric (relative log-likelihood differences) is confusing and needs clearer justification and interpretation.  
- The M-step relies on several non-trivial approximations and caveats that weaken the claimed elegance of the approach.  
- Experimental analysis of initialization effects is limited; additional strategies (e.g., initializing EM from KA) should be explored.  
- The approach meaningfully advances unconstrained kernel learning for DPPs and has potential applications (summarization, sensor placement, image search).  
- Overall, a technically strong and practically relevant contribution with consistent empirical gains, suitable for NIPS.
