- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and Jensen lower bounds.  
- Experiments show favorable behavior versus a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme but are limited in scope.  
- Method appears well-founded and plausibly works as claimed.  
- Evaluation is weak: lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection approaches referenced in the paper.  
- Paper clarity is poor: write-up is sloppy, sometimes hard to follow, with undefined notation and delayed definitions.  
- Writing is convoluted; the method should be presented clearly first and discussion/alternatives postponed.  
- Originality: the approach seems novel and not duplicated in prior work known to the reviewer.  
- Significance: presents a solid advance and useful contribution to subset-selection literature.  
- p.2 l.81: marginal kernel K is used before being defined—needs clarification what qualifies as a marginal kernel.  
- p.2 l.100: symbol V is not defined and should be clearly introduced.  
- p.3 l.159: phrase “minimizes the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; recommend “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”  
- Section 4 is hard to understand; the simpler approach should be outlined directly rather than deferring to reference [26].  
- p.4 l.189: the term “weight” is ambiguous and should be defined in context.  
- p.7 l.324: rationale for Equation (30) is unclear—explain why m_ij is omitted and why empirical covariance would not work for initialization.  
- For acceptance: novel EM method, nontrivial combination of techniques, and promising experimental results.  
- Against acceptance: inadequate evaluation against alternatives, unclear/sloppy presentation, and need for clearer definitions and organization.
