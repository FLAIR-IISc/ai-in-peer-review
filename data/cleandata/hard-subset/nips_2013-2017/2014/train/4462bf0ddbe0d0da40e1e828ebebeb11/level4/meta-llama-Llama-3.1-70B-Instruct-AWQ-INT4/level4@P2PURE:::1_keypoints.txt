- Primary contribution: reformulates DPP kernel learning as an EM procedure that outperforms standard K-Ascent (KA).
- Methodology: represents any DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and defines a variational distribution over subsets (implemented as a k‑DPP).
- Algorithm: alternates updating the k‑DPP variational distribution and taking single gradient steps in a complex M-step.
- Empirical results: EM significantly outperforms KA with naive initialization and moderately outperforms KA with better initialization.
- Reviewer view: approach is intriguing and practically effective despite a complex, caveated M-step.
- Request for experiments: more analysis of initialization effects, e.g., initializing EM from KA results.
- Metric critique: questions use of relative log likelihood; recommends reporting log-probability differences rather than ratios of logs.
- Stylistic minor points: avoid self-descriptive language like “elegant” and unnecessary bold text in the introduction.
- Overall assessment: interesting new approach with modest gains and room for improved experiments; overall well-written.
