- DPPs favor diverse subsets and are parameterized by a PSD matrix L, but learning L is NP-hard.  
- Prior work used restrictive parametric forms (e.g., scalar/vector) that limit the model's ability to capture diversity.  
- The paper proposes a novel learning method that imposes no constraints on L’s parameterization.  
- Key advantage: the method avoids projection steps used by projected gradient ascent that can degrade similarity and diversity.  
- The authors formulate an EM algorithm over L’s eigenvalues and eigenvectors to learn the model.  
- They avoid projecting eigenvectors by leveraging the full-rank property of the eigenvector matrix V.  
- The EM derivation is obtained by constructing a lower bound on the objective using Jensen’s inequality.  
- Technical contributions include mapping the eigenvector orthonormality constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold.  
- They prove the inverse distribution is also a DPP, a notable theoretical result.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a real-world product recommendation task validate the approach.  
- Results show the method preserves both quality and diversity, outperforming the KA baseline in diversity.  
- The recommendation improvement is modest (replacing two of the top-10 items with more diverse items) but illustrative.  
- Manuscript is coherent with clear proofs and a well-explained EM derivation, though the conclusion is somewhat rushed.  
- Suggested improvement: include more extensive experiments across diverse real-world datasets to better demonstrate practical benefits.
