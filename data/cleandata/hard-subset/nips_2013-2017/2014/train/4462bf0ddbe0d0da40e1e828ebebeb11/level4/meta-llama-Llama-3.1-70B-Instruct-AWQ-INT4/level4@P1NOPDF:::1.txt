This paper focuses on learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The primary contribution is a reformulated objective that outperforms the standard approach of differentiating the log probability with respect to kernel parameters and applying projected gradient ascent, referred to as K-Ascent (KA) in the paper. By reframing the problem within an EM framework, the authors achieve improved performance. This approach works by recognizing that any DPP can be represented as a mixture over "elementary DPPs," each corresponding to a subset of elements. A variational distribution is established over these subsets, and the algorithm alternates between updating the distribution over the subsets, represented as a k-DPP, and taking single gradient steps in the M step. The results demonstrate that the EM method significantly outperforms the KA method when the latter is initialized naively, and by a smaller margin in certain settings when KA is initialized more intelligently.

Overall, I find the approach intriguing, and it appears that the EM method yields some improvement. Although the M-step is complex, and the necessary caveats to make it work are somewhat unsatisfying, the fact that it works is noteworthy.

From an experimental perspective, I would have appreciated a more in-depth analysis of the effect of initialization on the EM algorithm. For instance, if the EM algorithm were initialized with the result of KA, would it improve upon the solution? This would have provided valuable insight into the method's performance.

I am also perplexed by the use of relative log likelihood as a measure. Log probability differences reflect ratios of probabilities, so it is unclear why the authors take ratios of log probabilities. Why not simply report differences in log probabilities? I find it challenging to interpret the meaning of these numbers.

On a minor note, I find some aspects of the writing style distracting. I would prefer it if the authors avoided describing their algorithm as "elegant," especially given the caveats and approximations required in the M-step. Additionally, bolding text in the introduction is unnecessary. The paper presents an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs. While the experiments could be improved, and the improvements over the basic projected gradient algorithm are not substantial, overall it is a good paper.
