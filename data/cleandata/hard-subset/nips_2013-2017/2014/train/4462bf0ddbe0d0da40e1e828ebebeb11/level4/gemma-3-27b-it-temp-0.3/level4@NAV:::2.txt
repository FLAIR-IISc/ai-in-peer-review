The paper introduces an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). This method effectively integrates several complex components: kernel eigendecomposition, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a DPP mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen’s Inequality-based log-likelihood lower bound. Experimental results demonstrate promising performance relative to a K-Ascent baseline.

**Quality:** The proposed method appears theoretically sound and the reported results are plausible. However, the experimental evaluation would benefit from expansion. The current experiments are limited in scope and lack comparison to alternative subset selection techniques (including those referenced in the introduction), making it difficult to assess the specific advantages offered by the DPP approach.

**Clarity:** The paper’s presentation could be significantly improved. The writing is somewhat disorganized and challenging to follow, with notation occasionally introduced after its initial use and a tendency towards unnecessarily complex phrasing. The current structure, which interleaves the derivation of the method with discussions of alternative approaches and potential issues, hinders readability. A clearer presentation of the core method, followed by a dedicated discussion of related work and challenges, would be preferable.

**Originality:** The work appears to be original; I am not currently aware of similar research.

**Significance:** This paper presents a valuable and substantive advancement in the field of subset selection, offering a useful contribution to the existing literature.

**Details:**

*   p.2, line 81: The “marginal kernel” *K* is used without prior definition. Clarification regarding the characteristics of a qualifying marginal kernel is needed.
*   p.2, line 100: The variable *V* is undefined at this point.
*   p.3, line 159: The phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is imprecise. A clearer statement, such as “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite matrix to *Q*”, would be more effective.
*   p.4: This section is difficult to understand. Rather than relying on a reference to [26], outlining the proposed, simplified approach directly would improve accessibility for the reader.
*   p.4, line 189: The meaning of “weight” in this context is unclear.
*   p.7, line 324: The rationale behind equation (30) is not fully explained. Specifically, the exclusion of *m<sub>ij</sub>* and the reasons why using the empirical covariance matrix directly as the kernel would be unsuitable as an initialization are unclear. 

In summary, the paper introduces a novel and potentially significant method for learning DPPs for subset selection. However, improvements to the clarity of the writing and the scope of the experimental evaluation are recommended.
