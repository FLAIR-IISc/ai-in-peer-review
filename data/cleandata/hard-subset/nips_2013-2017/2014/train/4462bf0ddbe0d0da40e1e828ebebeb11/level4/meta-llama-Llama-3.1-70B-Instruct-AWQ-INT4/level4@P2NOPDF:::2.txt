The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) and presents a novel method that combines several non-trivial components, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood using Jensen's Inequality. The experimental results demonstrate favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and its effectiveness is believable. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are limited and do not provide comparisons with alternative approaches for subset selection, making it unclear how much the DPP approach contributes to the results.

Clarity: The paper's writing is not particularly clear, with a somewhat sloppy and hard-to-follow presentation. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes definitions appear after the notation has been used. The text is structured to describe the process of arriving at the proposed approach, with side remarks on alternative approaches and problems interspersed with the main thread. A clearer presentation of the developed method, followed by a discussion, would be preferable.

Originality: The paper appears to be original, as I am not aware of similar work in this area.

Significance: The method presented in the paper represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.
