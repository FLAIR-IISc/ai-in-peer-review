- Addresses learning the full DPP kernel matrix via an EM framework that operates on the eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel K.  
- Avoids restrictive parametric kernel assumptions and learns the full kernel without per-row scalar or fixed-DPP linear constraints.  
- Technically sound EM derivation: E-step identifies a k-DPP variational distribution; M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates and gradient-based eigenvector updates.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains over the K-Ascent baseline on a real-world product recommendation task (up to 16.5% relative test log-likelihood gain).  
- Method is asymptotically faster than K-Ascent and avoids problematic projection steps that cause issues with negative item interactions.  
- M-step uses approximations/caveats (must avoid λj = 1, uses a single gradient step rather than full optimization) that weaken theoretical elegance.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with precise algorithmic descriptions and helpful supplementary material.  
- Lacks sufficient analysis of initialization effects, especially when initializing EM from K-Ascent solutions.  
- Using relative log-likelihood differences as the primary metric is confusing; absolute log-probability differences would be preferable.  
- Strong novelty in applying EM to learn the full kernel matrix for DPPs where prior work imposed structural constraints or used Bayesian methods.  
- Practical significance for diversity-sensitive applications (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement) due to robustness and computational efficiency.  
- Strengths: novel framework, comprehensive derivation, empirical improvements, clear relation to prior work, detailed analysis of kernel-structure effects.  
- Weaknesses: M-step approximations, insufficient initialization study, metric presentation issues, and some minor writing/style choices that detract from professionalism.  
- Overall: solid, original contribution with practical and theoretical value, suitable for publication despite some caveats.
