- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPP parameters using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and Jensen’s inequality, outperforming a K-Ascent baseline.  
- Method appears technically sound and addresses the important problem of learning DPPs from data.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and narrow in scope.  
- Missing comparisons to non-DPP subset-selection baselines, making practical advantage unclear.  
- Main clarity issue: writing is convoluted and structured around authors’ discovery process rather than a clear final presentation.  
- Notation and definitions are imprecise or introduced after first use, forcing re-reading.  
- Originality is high—the EM framework combination appears novel.  
- p.2 l.81: “marginal kernel K” used without definition.  
- p.2 l.100: matrix V is used without definition.  
- p.3 l.159: phrase about “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; should state choosing the closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm.  
- p.4: section is hard to follow; authors should outline their simpler approach instead of deferring to [26].  
- p.4 l.189: the term “weight” is unclear and needs clarification.  
- p.7 l.324: initialization in Eq.(30) is inadequately justified—why not use co-occurrence counts or empirical covariance?  
- Overall: promising and novel work but needs much clearer exposition and broader experimental validation to realize its impact.
