This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), which combines several non-trivial techniques, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood using Jensen's Inequality. The experimental results demonstrate the favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and it is believable that it should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are limited and do not compare the proposed approach to alternative methods for subset selection, such as those mentioned in the introduction. This makes it unclear how much benefit is gained from using the DPP approach.

Clarity: The paper is not clearly written. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow, with undefined notation and occasionally delayed definitions. The text is structured to describe the process of arriving at the proposed approach, including side remarks on alternative approaches and problems, which can make it difficult to follow. A clearer presentation of the developed method, with discussion postponed to a later stage, would be preferable.

Originality: The paper appears to be original, and I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: The method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:

* On page 2, line 81, the marginal kernel K is not defined before use. It would be helpful to specify what kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.
* On page 2, line 100, V is not defined.
* On page 3, line 159, the phrase "minimizes the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. It would be better to say "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) Positive Semi-Definite (PSD) matrix to Q".
* I found the section on page 4 hard to understand. Instead of referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline the approach directly, so that the reader is not forced to look up the more complex original method.
* On page 4, line 189, the term "weight" is unclear. It would be helpful to specify what is meant by this term.
* On page 7, line 324, the rationale behind equation (30) is unclear. It would be helpful to explain why m_{ij} is not used here and why simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel would not have worked as initialization.

Overall, the paper proposes a new method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection, which is highly non-trivial and likely original. However, the write-up could be improved for clarity, and the experiments should consider alternative methods to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
