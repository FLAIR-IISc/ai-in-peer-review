- Paper introduces an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernels that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (KA).
- Key idea: represent a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs with a hidden subset variable and alternate E-step (k-DPP variational distribution) and M-step (gradient updates).
- Experiments show EM consistently outperforms KA, especially with naive KA initialization, with modest improvements even with informed initialization.
- The approach is interesting and yields demonstrable improvements, representing a significant implementation effort given M-step complexity.
- The M-step requires approximations and caveats that limit the method’s theoretical neatness and practical simplicity.
- The reviewer requests experiments showing EM initialized from KA’s output to assess initialization impact.
- Concern about the evaluation metric: reporting relative log-likelihood is unclear; absolute log-likelihood differences would be more interpretable.
- Experimental gains are consistent but not large enough to be considered overwhelming.
- Theoretical justification is clear and well-explained.
- Experiments are well-designed but could be strengthened by more initialization analyses.
- Minor stylistic notes: avoid self-aggrandizing terms like “elegant” and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.
- Overall assessment: promising and solid contribution, worthy of acceptance despite limited gains and M-step approximations.
