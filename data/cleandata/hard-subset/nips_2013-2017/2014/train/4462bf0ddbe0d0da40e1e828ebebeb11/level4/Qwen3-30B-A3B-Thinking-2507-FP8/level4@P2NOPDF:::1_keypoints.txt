- Proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernels by expressing any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and using a variational k-DPP for mixture weights.  
- Core contribution: an EM-based algorithm that substantially outperforms K-Ascent (KA) when KA is poorly initialized and gives modest improvements when KA is well-initialized.  
- Algorithmic procedure: alternates an E-step updating the variational distribution (k-DPP) and an M-step performing single gradient steps on the kernel.  
- The approach is theoretically sound and provides a compelling alternative to direct gradient-based kernel optimization.  
- The M-step relies on non-trivial approximations to handle the intractable subset mixture, which undermines the method’s claimed elegance.  
- These approximations are acknowledged but read as pragmatic concessions rather than a clean, exact solution.  
- Experiments lack investigation of initializing EM with KA’s solution, leaving a crucial comparison gap and weakening the baseline evaluation.  
- The use of “relative log likelihood” (ratios of log probabilities) is problematic and counterintuitive; absolute log-probability differences should be reported.  
- Empirical results: clear gains for EM under naive initialization; modest but consistent gains when KA is initialized effectively.  
- Minor writing/stylistic issues (overstating “elegance,” unnecessary bolding) distract from the technical contribution and should be revised.  
- Concrete suggestions: replace the metric with standard log-probability differences, test EM initialized from KA, soften “elegant” claims, and remove bolded text.  
- Overall verdict: a solid, practically useful contribution with valid technical work and well-executed experiments, suitable for acceptance with minor revisions.
