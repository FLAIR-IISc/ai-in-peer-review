- DPPs favor diversity but learning the full PSD kernel L is NP-hard, so prior work restricts L’s parameterization.
- Paper proposes learning an unconstrained L by reparameterizing via eigenvalues and eigenvectors and using an EM scheme.
- Method avoids harmful projection steps of naive gradient ascent by optimizing on the Stiefel manifold and exploiting V being full-rank, eliminating eigenvector projection.
- Jensen’s inequality is used to lower-bound the log-likelihood and derive the EM procedure.
- The conditional distribution over latent indices in the algorithm is itself a DPP, simplifying analysis and updates.
- The approach better preserves both diversity and item quality compared to projected-gradient baselines (e.g., KA).
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support the claim, with qualitative examples showing KA replacing plausible items.
- EM derivation and proofs are clear, well organized, and instructive.
- Mapping the orthogonality constraint to Stiefel-manifold optimization and showing the latent DPP structure are solid technical contributions.
- Conclusion is somewhat brief and the paper would benefit from additional real-world experiments to further demonstrate practical significance.
