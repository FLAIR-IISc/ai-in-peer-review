- Proposes a novel EM-based objective for learning DPP kernel matrices that reformulates the standard approach.  
- Represents any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" with a variational distribution over subsets, alternating E-step (k-DPP update) and M-step (single gradient steps).  
- Empirical results: EM significantly outperforms KA under naive initialization and gives moderate gains when KA is better initialized.  
- M-step is relatively complex and relies on caveats/approximations, though the authors successfully make it work.  
- Reviewer requests additional analysis of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM from KA).  
- Questions the use of "relative log likelihood" (ratios of log probabilities) instead of straightforward log-probability differences, which hampers interpretability.  
- Minor style concerns: avoid self-descriptive terms like "elegant" for the algorithm and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: interesting contribution with modest empirical gains; experiments could be strengthened but paper is generally well written.
