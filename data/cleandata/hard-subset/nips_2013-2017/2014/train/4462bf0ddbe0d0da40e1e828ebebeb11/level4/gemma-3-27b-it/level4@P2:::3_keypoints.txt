- Addresses learning the full kernel matrix for DPPs via a novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes by eigenvalues/eigenvectors.
- Avoids NP-hard projection steps of direct gradient ascent, preventing degenerate solutions and loss of diversity.
- Exploits eigendecomposition and a Jensen-derived lower bound on likelihood to make learning tractable.
- Mathematical derivations and EM update steps are detailed and appear sound.
- Connection to optimization on the Stiefel manifold demonstrates technical depth.
- Presentation is clear and accessible to readers familiar with DPPs and EM.
- Empirical results on a product recommendation task show EM outperforms projected gradient ascent, especially when diversity matters.
- Runtime analysis indicates asymptotic speedup for the EM approach.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to one main task and could use more datasets with varying diversity characteristics.
- Impact of different initialization strategies is acknowledged but not systematically explored.
- Potential applications (e.g., document summarization, sensor placement) are mentioned but not demonstrated with examples or preliminary results.
- Conclusion is somewhat abbreviated and could better restate key contributions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, empirical validation, significance, and clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental breadth and an abbreviated conclusion.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and strengthen the conclusion.
