- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP parameters combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and Jensen’s inequality; outperforms a K-Ascent baseline.

- Method appears technically sound with a plausible EM derivation and expected behavior.

- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and needs strengthening.

- Missing comparisons to non-DPP subset-selection methods, making practical benefits unclear.

- Paper lacks sufficient clarity and is unstructured, making it hard to follow.

- Notation and definitions are introduced too late or left undefined, causing confusion.

- Writing is convoluted and interleaves main development with side remarks; should present the final method first and alternatives later.

- Originality is good; no known prior work on a similar EM-based DPP learning framework.

- Significance is promising—a solid technical advance that would be more impactful with clearer presentation and stronger validation.

- K (marginal kernel) is used before being defined; clarify the definition and required properties up front.

- Matrix V is used without prior definition; introduce it before use.

- “Minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is imprecise—state that the closest PSD matrix to Q in Frobenius norm is chosen.

- Section relying on [26] is hard to understand; provide a brief, self-contained outline of the simplified approach.

- The term “weight” is used without a clear definition—specify what it refers to.

- Initialization in Eq. (30) is not justified—explain omission of m_ij and why empirical covariance wouldn’t suffice as initialization.
