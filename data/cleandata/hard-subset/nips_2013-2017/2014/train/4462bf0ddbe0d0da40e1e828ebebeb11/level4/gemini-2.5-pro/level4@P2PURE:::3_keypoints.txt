- DPPs model subset diversity via a PSD kernel L, but learning the full L is NP-hard.  
- Prior methods restrict L or use scalar weights, which limits diversity modeling.  
- Paper proposes learning the full, unrestricted L using an EM algorithm on L’s eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- EM avoids the projection steps of standard gradient ascent that can degrade the learned kernel.  
- Orthonormality of eigenvectors is handled by reformulating as optimization on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding eigenvector projection.  
- Jensen’s inequality is used to derive a tractable lower bound and construct the EM procedure.  
- Main focus and strength: preserving DPP diversity rather than emphasizing only item quality.  
- Key contributions: detailed EM derivation, elegant Stiefel-manifold formulation, and proof that the inverse distribution is also a DPP.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task show improved balance of quality and diversity versus KA.  
- Empirical gains in recommendation are modest (e.g., replacing two of top ten products) but still demonstrate benefit.  
- Paper is cohesive with well-written proofs; weaknesses include a brief conclusion and limited diversity of real-world datasets.
