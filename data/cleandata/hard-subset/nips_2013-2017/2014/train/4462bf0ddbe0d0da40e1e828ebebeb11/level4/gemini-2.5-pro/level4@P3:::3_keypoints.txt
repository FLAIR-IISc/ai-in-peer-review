- Addresses learning the full DPP kernel L via a novel EM-style algorithm re-parameterized by eigendecomposition to handle a conjecturedly NP-hard problem.
- Introduces a latent variable J (selection of eigenvectors) to view the DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and derive a tractable lower bound on log-likelihood.
- E-step computes posterior q(J|Y_i) which the authors identify and prove to be a k-DPP, enabling efficient inference.
- M-step updates eigenvalues λ and eigenvectors V, handling orthogonality by optimizing on the Stiefel manifold rather than via projection.
- Stiefel-manifold updates avoid the projection-induced near-diagonal kernels that break diversity modeling in baseline methods.
- Compared to projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent), the EM approach is more robust, yields up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood, especially with poor initialization or limited data.
- The EM algorithm is shown to be asymptotically and empirically faster than the K-Ascent baseline.
- Technical contributions include the eigendecomposition re-parameterization, identifying the posterior as a k-DPP, and applying manifold optimization for eigenvectors.
- The paper is well-written, clearly motivating the problem and systematically deriving the algorithm and runtime analysis.
- Empirical validation on Amazon baby registry data is convincing and includes qualitative examples illustrating improved diversity in recommendations.
- Limitation: experiments are confined to a single application domain (product recommendation); broader-domain evaluation (e.g., summarization, sensor placement) would strengthen generality claims.
- Minor weakness: the conclusion is brief and could better restate key technical insights and broader impact.
- Supplementary proof of Lemma 1 (q(J|Y_i) is a k-DPP) is an important and elegant theoretical underpinning.
- Analysis linking EM performance gains to the off-diagonal "heaviness" of true marginals (d value) reinforces the paper's core motivation.
