- Addresses learning DPPs by estimating the PSD kernel L, avoiding intractability of direct learning.
- Notes prior work restricts L or partially learns it, often compromising diversity.
- Main contribution: an EM algorithm to learn L without projection steps required by gradient methods.
- Operates on eigenvalues and eigenvectors of L to circumvent eigenvector projections.
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable EM procedure.
- Maps the constrained optimization to the Stiefel manifold, removing eigenvector projection needs.
- Shows the resulting inverse distribution remains a DPP.
- Emphasizes preservation of diversity (not just subset quality) as a key strength.
- Clear, step-by-step derivation and well-structured proofs; writing is cohesive and technically sound.
- Experiments (synthetic and a product recommendation task) support claims and outperform a Kernel Alignment baseline in preserving quality and diversity.
- Suggests expanding experimental evaluation with more real-world datasets; conclusion feels somewhat brief.
