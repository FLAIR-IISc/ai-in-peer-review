- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition and treating eigenvector selection as a latent variable.  
- Uses Jensen's inequality to derive a likelihood lower bound, alternating E-step (posterior over eigenvector subsets) and M-step (update eigenvalues/eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold).  
- Eigenvector updates are handled via optimization on the Stiefel manifold with a simplified update rule referenced from prior work.  
- Empirical results on a product recommendation dataset show up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood over projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- EM is more robust to poor initialization and scarce data, and is asymptotically faster than K-Ascent.  
- Novel and significant contribution: tackles the hard problem of learning the full DPP kernel with a coherent combination of non-trivial techniques.  
- Technically sound derivation: eigendecomposition latent-variable formulation and avoidance of projection steps are well-motivated and justified.  
- Empirical validation is convincing for the compared baseline, demonstrating robustness and runtime advantages.  
- Presentation and clarity are lacking: narrative-style derivation, interwoven alternatives, and notational issues make the paper hard to follow.  
- Limited experimental scope: comparison only to K-Ascent; should include non-DPP subset-selection baselines to assess broader gains.  
- Notation and definitions need fixing: key terms (e.g., marginal kernel K, eigenvector matrix V) are introduced before being defined.  
- Explanations could be clearer: specify projection as nearest PSD in Frobenius norm, briefly outline the Stiefel update used, and clarify the mixture-weight product in Eq. (7).  
- Initialization rationale unclear: moment-matching initialization (Eq. 23) needs justification and alternative explanations (e.g., why not empirical covariance)
