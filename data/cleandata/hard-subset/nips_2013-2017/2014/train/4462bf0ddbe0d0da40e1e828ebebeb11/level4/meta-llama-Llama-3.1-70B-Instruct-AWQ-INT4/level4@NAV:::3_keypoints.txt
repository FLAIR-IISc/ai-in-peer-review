- Presents a novel learning method for DPPs that preserves diversity by allowing unrestricted parameterization of the PSD matrix L.  
- Addresses NP-hardness of learning L and limitations of prior partial or restrictive parameterizations.  
- Avoids projections that can harm similarity and diversity properties.  
- Uses an EM algorithm on eigenvalues and eigenvectors to reduce the learning problem.  
- Explores multiple optimization strategies, including projected gradient ascent.  
- Exploits full-rank property of V to eliminate the need to project eigenvectors.  
- Maps the constraint to optimization over the Stiefel manifold, a solid theoretical contribution.  
- Provides detailed, step-by-step derivation of the EM procedure and clear proofs.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic datasets and a product recommendation task, retaining quality and diversity.  
- Empirical comparisons (notably to the KA algorithm) support the paper’s claims.  
- Writing is cohesive, but the conclusion could better highlight key contributions.  
- Recommends additional real-world experiments to further illustrate the algorithm’s diversity benefits.
