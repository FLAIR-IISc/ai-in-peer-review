- Experimental results show improvement over a simple "K-Ascent" baseline.  
- Quality concern: method seems plausible but evaluation is weak and needs strengthening.  
- Missing comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods cited in the introduction, so advantage of DPP approach is unclear.  
- Clarity concern: paper is poorly written, sloppy, and hard to follow.  
- Notation issues: definitions appear after usage and several symbols are undefined.  
- Structural problem: side remarks about alternatives/problems are interwoven into main narrative, harming readability.  
- Originality: method appears highly original with no known similar prior work.  
- Significance: represents a solid advance and valuable contribution to subset-selection literature.
