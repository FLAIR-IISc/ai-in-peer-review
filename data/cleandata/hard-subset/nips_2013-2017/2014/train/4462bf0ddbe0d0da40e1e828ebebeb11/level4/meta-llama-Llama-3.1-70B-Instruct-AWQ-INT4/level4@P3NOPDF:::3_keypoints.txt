- Proposes a novel learning method for DPPs that does not restrict the parameterization of L, preserving DPP diversity.  
- Addresses NP-hardness of learning L by avoiding restrictive parametrizations used in prior work.  
- Introduces an EM algorithm on eigenvalues and eigenvectors that eliminates the need for projection steps.  
- Exploits full-rankness of matrix V to avoid eigenvector projection and preserve similarity/diversity.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and construct the EM procedure.  
- Maps the constraint to optimization over the Stiefel manifold to eliminate eigenvector projection.  
- Proves the inverse distribution is a DPP, a valuable theoretical result.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and a product-recommendation task shows retention of quality and diversity versus the KL algorithm (KA).  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise proofs and an insightful step-by-step EM derivation.  
- Suggests adding more real-world experiments to better demonstrate the practical importance of preserved diversity.  
- Recommends improving the conclusion to explicitly highlight all contributions.
