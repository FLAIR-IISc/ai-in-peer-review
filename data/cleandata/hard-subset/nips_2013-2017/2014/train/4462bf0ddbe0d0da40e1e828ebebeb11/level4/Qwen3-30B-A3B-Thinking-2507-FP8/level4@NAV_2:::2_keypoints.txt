- Introduces an EM algorithm that learns the full DPP kernel by parameterizing it via eigenvalues and eigenvectors, avoiding projection steps in prior gradient methods.
- Novel contribution: first method (to reviewer’s knowledge) to learn the full kernel matrix without restrictive parameterizations or fixed kernels.
- Rigorous derivation: reformulates likelihood via eigendecomposition and Jensen’s inequality to obtain a tractable lower bound.
- Theoretical/runtime benefit: asymptotic analysis shows EM is superior to projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).
- Empirical gains: on product recommendation tasks EM yields up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood and is ~2.1× faster on average.
- Robustness: EM is robust to initialization and better captures negative item interactions critical for diversity.
- Evaluation limitation: experiments are limited to a single application and lack comparisons to non-DPP subset-selection baselines (e.g., submodular maximization).
- Clarity issues: organization and notation are sometimes poor (definitions appear after use; method interleaves alternatives), and the Stiefel manifold update should be more directly described.
- Wording fix suggested: replace “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” with “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
