- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn full DPP kernels via eigendecomposition, representing a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and optimizing eigenvalues/eigenvectors (Stiefel manifold).
- Contrasts with prior work that used restricted parameterizations; experiments compare mainly to K-Ascent and show favorable behavior.
- Technical soundness: derivation and EM formulation appear plausible; E-step, closed-form eigenvalue updates, and orthogonality-respecting eigenvector updates are credible.
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only compares to K-Ascent and omits other subset-selection baselines, leaving practical advantage unclear.
- Clarity problems: notation often undefined or introduced late, and the presentation interleaves detours that obscure the main algorithm.
- Originality: novel in applying EM to learn full DPP kernels via eigenvalues/eigenvectors rather than restricted convex approaches.
- Significance: meaningful advance for diversity-aware subset selection if supported by broader comparisons.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually interesting, technically well-grounded, moves beyond restricted settings, and provides useful algorithmic insights (e.g., avoiding PSD projections).
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments and exposition/clarity issues.
- Suggestion: define the marginal kernel K (K = L(L+I)^{-1} or equivalent) and eigenvalue constraints before first use.
- Suggestion: introduce eigendecomposition notation (V, Λ) before referencing V.
- Suggestion: clarify phrasing like “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” to indicate projecting to the closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm.
- Suggestion: give a concise, self-contained derivation/description of the simplified Stiefel-manifold update instead of mainly citing [26].
- Suggestion: clarify what “weight” refers to (mixture weight over eigenvector subsets) and connect to Eq. (7) and λ_j.
- Suggestion: justify the initialization K̃_{ij} = sqrt(max(K̃_{ii} K̃_{jj} − m_{ij}, 0)) and discuss alternatives such as projecting the empirical moment matrix.
- Overall assessment: technically solid and potentially useful, but needs clearer exposition and broader experimental baselines to fully demonstrate impact.
