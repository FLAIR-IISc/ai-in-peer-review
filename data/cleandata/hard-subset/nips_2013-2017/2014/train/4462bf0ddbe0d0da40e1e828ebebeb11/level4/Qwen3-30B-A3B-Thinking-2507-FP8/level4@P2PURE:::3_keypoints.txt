- DPPs model diversity via a PSD matrix L, but learning L is NP-hard, so prior work focused on partial or restricted parameterizations.
- Learning a scalar/vector for L compromises DPP diversity; previous methods often restrict L’s parametric form.
- Gradient ascent requires projection steps that can harm similarity and diversity properties.
- The paper proposes an EM-based learning method that does not restrict L and avoids projection steps.
- EM is applied over eigenvalues and eigenvectors, using Jensen’s inequality to derive a lower bound for the objective.
- By exploiting full-rank V, the method eliminates the need to project eigenvectors and maps constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold.
- The authors prove the inverse distribution remains a DPP and provide clear, well-structured proofs and derivations.
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task show the method preserves both quality and diversity compared to baselines like KA.
- Comparison with KA shows KA changes only two of the top-10 products, supporting the new method’s practical improvement.
- The paper is well-written and the step-by-step reduction to EM is insightful and engaging.
- The conclusion is somewhat premature and does not fully articulate all contributions.
- More experiments on real-world datasets are needed to better demonstrate the practical importance of the algorithm’s diversity advantages.
