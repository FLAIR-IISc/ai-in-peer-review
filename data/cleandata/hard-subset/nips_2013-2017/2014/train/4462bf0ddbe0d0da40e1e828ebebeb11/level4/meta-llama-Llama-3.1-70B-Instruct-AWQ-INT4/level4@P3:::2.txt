This paper proposes a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), a probabilistic model for subset selection tasks. The method combines several non-trivial ingredients, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality.

Quality: The paper presents a well-founded method, and it is believable that the approach should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection, making it unclear how much we benefit from the DPP approach. Furthermore, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including error bars and statistical significance tests.

Clarity: The paper's writing is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes definitions come after the notation is used. The text is structured to describe the process of developing the proposed approach, with side remarks about possible alternative approaches and problems interleaved with the main thread. A clearer presentation of the developed method, followed by a discussion of its advantages and limitations, would be more effective.

Originality: The paper appears to be original, and the author is not aware of similar work.

Significance: The method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature. DPPs have attractive computational properties, including exact and efficient normalization, marginalization, conditioning, and sampling.

To improve the paper, the author could consider the following:

* Provide a clearer definition of the marginal kernel K and its properties.
* Define V before using it on page 2, line 100.
* Clarify the statement on page 3, line 159, to indicate that the projection step chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.
* Outline the approach to optimization on the Stiefel manifold directly, rather than referring to [26].
* Define the term "weight" on page 4, line 189.
* Explain the rationale behind the initialization method on page 7, line 324, and why using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel would not have worked.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and original method for learning DPPs.
* The method has attractive computational properties and presents a solid advance in the subset selection literature.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The evaluation of the method is limited and does not compare against alternative approaches.
* The writing is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the subset selection literature, but could benefit from improvements in clarity, evaluation, and analysis.
