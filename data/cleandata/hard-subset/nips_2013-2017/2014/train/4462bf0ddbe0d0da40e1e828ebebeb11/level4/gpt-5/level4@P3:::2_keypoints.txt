- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn a full DPP kernel by reparameterizing K via its eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introducing latent J indexing elementary DPPs.  
- E-step computes a k-DPP over J conditioned on each observed set; M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and eigenvectors via a Stiefel-manifold gradient step.  
- Method avoids problematic PSD projection required by naïve projected gradient ascent on K.  
- Experiments on a baby registry recommendation task show consistent improvements in test log-likelihood (up to 16.5%) and ~2× speedups versus a projected-gradient baseline (KA).  
- Builds on existing DPP literature (per-item scalings, mixtures, k-DPPs, sampling/marginalization) but appears novel in the EM/eigendecomposition formulation for full-kernel learning.  
- Technical derivation is principled; using a mixture over elementary DPPs and identifying q(J|Y) as a k-DPP is elegant and useful.  
- Complexity comparison: EM O(n N k^2) per iteration plus O(N^3) manifold updates during line search vs KA’s O(n N^3) inverses/projections.  
- Empirical validation is limited to one application and one family of baselines, restricting generality of claims.  
- M-step is inexact (single gradient step with line search), raising questions about monotonic lower-bound improvement and EM convergence guarantees.  
- Paper is harder to follow than necessary due to organization and several delayed or missing definitions (e.g., K, V, “weight”, matrix-exponential update).  
- Specific clarity issues: define marginal kernel K and eigendecomposition earlier; correct phrasing about Frobenius-norm projection; explain Stiefel update used and its cost.  
- Initialization rationale (Eq. (30) for K̃ off-diagonals) is unclear and should be justified or derived.  
- Recommends restructuring Section 3 to present the final EM algorithm first, then implementation choices and alternatives.  
- Contribution is original in combining mixture view, k-DPP conditioning, eigenvalue closed-form updates, and Stiefel updates for eigenvectors.  
- Significance: addresses practical difficulty of non-convexity and PSD constraints in learning full DPP kernels and shows promising gains, but broader experiments and baselines are needed.  
- Pros: principled EM, avoids PSD clipping, clear eigenvalue update, practical Stiefel update, runtime advantages, improved likelihood and qualitative examples.  
- Cons: limited experiments (single dataset), clarity/organization problems, inexact M-step with uncertain monotonicity, unclear scalability due to repeated eigendecompositions, and unclear initialization rationale.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to more baselines (mixture DPPs, Bayesian DPPs, submodular models), report task metrics (MAP, precision/recall), clarify convergence behavior, provide ablations (gradient steps, initialization sensitivity, large-k behavior), and report wall-clock breakdowns and larger-N experiments.  
- Also suggest improving notation, adding a self-contained derivation/description of the matrix-exponential Stiefel update, and justifying the initialization formula.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and useful method but needs better exposition and broader empirical evaluation.
