- DPPs favor diverse sets but learning the PSD matrix L is NP-hard; prior work only partially learns L.  
- Previous methods either learn scalar/vector forms (compromising diversity) or restrict L’s parametric form.  
- This paper proposes a learning method that does not restrict L’s parameterization.  
- The method avoids projection steps that can harm similarity/diversity by using an EM algorithm on eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- By exploiting full-rank V, they avoid projecting eigenvectors and use Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective for EM.  
- They map the constraint to optimization over the Stiefel manifold to eliminate eigenvector projection.  
- They show the inverse distribution is a DPP.  
- The step-by-step EM derivation and proofs are well-written and insightful.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support that the method preserves DPP quality and diversity compared to KA.  
- Empirical result: only two of the top-10 products are replaced by KA with less likely products, illustrating the method’s benefit.  
- Recommendation: the conclusion should better highlight all contributions.  
- Recommendation: include more real-world dataset comparisons with other methods to further validate the diversity benefit.
