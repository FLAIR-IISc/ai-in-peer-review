- Proposes an EM-based method to learn DPP kernel matrices that reformulates the objective and outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) in many cases.
- EM represents any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" (subsets) and introduces a variational distribution over these subsets.
- Algorithm alternates between updating the subset distribution (a k-DPP) and taking single gradient steps in the M-step.
- Empirical results show EM significantly outperforms naively initialized K-Ascent and slightly improves over better-initialized K-Ascent in some settings.
- The M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats and approximations that reduce the appeal of the approach.
- The paper should analyze the effect of initialization on EM (e.g., initialize EM with KA solution) to see if EM improves such solutions.
- Reporting relative log-likelihood is questioned; differences in log-probabilities would be a clearer metric.
- Improvements over basic projected gradient are modest overall, despite the novel approach.
- Writing issues: calling the algorithm "elegant" is subjective given the M-step caveats.
- Minor presentation gripe: unnecessary bold text in the introduction.
- Overall: an interesting, well-written contribution with room for improved experiments and clearer metric justification.
