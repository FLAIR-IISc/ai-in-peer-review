The paper proposes an EM-based algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes. The approach combines several substantive components: reparameterization via eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization of eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold, the view of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s-inequality lower bound on the log-likelihood. Empirically, the method shows favorable performance relative to a simpler “K-Ascent” projected-gradient baseline.

Quality: The methodology appears technically sound, and it is credible that the algorithm behaves as described. However, the empirical evaluation could be stronger. The experimental scope is limited and does not include comparisons against alternative subset selection methods (e.g., those cited in the introduction), leaving uncertain the extent to which the DPP approach confers practical advantages over competing techniques.

Clarity: The exposition is not especially clear. The write-up is somewhat loose and occasionally difficult to follow; some notation is used before being defined, and certain definitions are introduced only later. The presentation is also more convoluted than necessary because it narrates the development process, interleaving side remarks about alternatives and issues with the main line of argument. A clearer structure would present the final method succinctly first, with additional discussion deferred.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge the contribution is original; I am not aware of closely related prior work.

Significance: The resulting algorithm represents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is referenced before being defined. Please clarify what qualifies as a marginal kernel at first use.
- p.2, line 100: V is used without prior definition.
- p.3, line 159: The phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. Prefer “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
- p.4: This section was difficult to parse. Rather than pointing to [26], it would help to outline your simplified update directly so the reader need not consult the more complex original method.
- p.4, line 189: Please clarify what “weight” refers to in this context.
- p.7, line 324: The rationale in (30) is unclear. Why is m_{ij} not used here? Would initializing with the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not be a reasonable alternative?

Overall, the paper introduces a new method for learning DPPs for subset selection. The technical development is substantial and, as far as I can tell, original. The writing could be clearer, and the experiments would benefit from comparisons to non-DPP baselines.
