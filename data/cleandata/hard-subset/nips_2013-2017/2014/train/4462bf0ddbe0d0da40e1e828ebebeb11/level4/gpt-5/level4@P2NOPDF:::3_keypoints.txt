- DPPs model diverse subsets via a PSD matrix L; learning L is NP-hard so prior work restricts parameterization, often sacrificing diversity.  
- Prior gradient-based methods require projection steps that can distort similarity structure and harm diversity.  
- The paper proposes an EM-based learning method that does not restrict L’s parameterization and avoids projection steps.  
- The method operates directly on L’s eigenvalues and eigenvectors, using Jensen’s inequality to derive an EM lower bound.  
- The authors recast the constrained eigenvector optimization as optimization on the Stiefel manifold, eliminating eigenvector projections.  
- The manifold-based treatment is elegant and practically useful, avoiding costly or distorting projections.  
- The paper observes the theoretical insight that the inverse distribution is itself a DPP.  
- Main contribution: a learning algorithm that preserves diversity (not just quality) in learned DPPs.  
- The step-by-step EM derivation is clear, technically sound, and instructive.  
- Proofs are well organized and the reduction to an EM procedure is well explained.  
- Experiments include synthetic data and a product recommendation task supporting claims of better quality and diversity than the KA baseline.  
- In the recommendation task, KA replaces top-10 products with lower-likelihood items, while the proposed method retains higher-quality, more diverse selections.  
- The paper is cohesive and clearly written overall.  
- The conclusion is brief and does not fully highlight the paper’s key contributions.  
- More experiments on real-world datasets are needed to better demonstrate practical importance and quantify advantages over competitors.
