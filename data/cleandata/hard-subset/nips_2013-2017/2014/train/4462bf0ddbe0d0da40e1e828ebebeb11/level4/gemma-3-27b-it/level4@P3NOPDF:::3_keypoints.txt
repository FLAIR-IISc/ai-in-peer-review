- Problem: Learning DPP kernel L is NP-hard; prior work uses partial learning or restrictive parameterizations that compromise diversity.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel EM algorithm that learns L without restrictive parameterizations.
- Method: Directly optimizes eigenvalues and eigenvectors of L, avoiding PSD projection required by gradient methods.
- Theory: Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a lower bound and construct the EM procedure.
- Technical insight: Exploits full-rank eigenvector matrix and maps constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold, eliminating eigenvector projections; shows the inverse distribution is a DPP.
- Advantage: Preserves both quality and diversity of DPPs, addressing a limitation of prior methods.
- Derivation: Provides a clear, step-by-step derivation and accessible proofs.
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation on synthetic data and a product recommendation task; outperforms Kernel Alignment by maintaining diverse, high-quality recommendations.
- Weakness / future work: Needs broader real-world evaluations (e.g., news recommendation, portfolio optimization) to further demonstrate practical benefits of diversity.
- Presentation & verdict: Well-written and cohesive; minor conclusion weakness; overall strong paper and recommended for acceptance.
