- DPPs model diversity via a PSD matrix L; learning L is NP-hard and prior work restricts L or uses scalar parameterizations that hurt diversity.
- The paper proposes a learning method that keeps L unrestricted and avoids projection steps that can distort similarity structure.
- It uses an EM procedure over eigenvalues and eigenvectors to optimize L, applying Jensen’s inequality to derive EM updates.
- By exploiting full-rank eigenvectors, the method avoids eigenvector projection and maps the constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold.
- The authors prove the inverse distribution is a DPP and provide clear, insightful step-by-step derivations.
- The method explicitly preserves diversity (in addition to quality), addressing a limitation of many prior approaches.
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product-recommendation task show the method retains both quality and diversity compared to KA (noting KA replaced two items in top-10 results).
- The experimental evaluation would benefit from additional real-world datasets to better demonstrate diversity advantages.
- Paper is coherent with well-written proofs and a well-explained reduction to EM, though the conclusion is somewhat hasty.
