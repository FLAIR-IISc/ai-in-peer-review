- Paper proposes an EM-based reformulation for learning DPP kernels, replacing direct differentiation + projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent, KA).  
- Key idea: view any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs (one per subset) and use a variational distribution (a k-DPP) in the E-step.  
- Algorithm alternates between updating the k-DPP variational distribution and taking single gradient steps in a somewhat intricate M-step.  
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms naive-initialized KA and gives modest gains over carefully-initialized KA in some cases.  
- M-step requires practical adjustments and approximations, making its implementation and theoretical cleanliness somewhat unsatisfying.  
- Review requests more analysis of sensitivity to initialization (e.g., starting EM from KA’s solution).  
- Reviewer questions the choice of reporting “relative log-likelihood” (ratio of log probabilities) rather than plain log-likelihood differences, making results hard to interpret.  
- Minor stylistic critique: avoid self-describing the method as “elegant” given M-step caveats, and remove unnecessary boldface.  
- Overall judgment: interesting and solid contribution, but experiments could be strengthened and gains over basic projected gradient are not uniformly large.
