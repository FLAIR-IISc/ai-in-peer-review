This paper presents an innovative approach to learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The authors reformulate the objective function within an expectation-maximization (EM) framework, which outperforms the standard method of differentiating the log-probability with respect to kernel parameters and applying projected gradient ascent. This baseline approach is referred to as K-Ascent (KA) in the paper.

The EM method works by representing any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs," where each elementary DPP corresponds to a subset of elements. A variational distribution is introduced over these subsets, and the algorithm alternates between updating the distribution over these subsets, represented as a k-DPP, and taking single gradient steps in the M-step.

The results demonstrate that the EM method significantly outperforms the KA method when the latter is initialized naively. Even when the KA method is initialized more intelligently, the EM method still achieves a slight improvement in some settings. Overall, the approach is interesting, and the EM method appears to yield some improvement. However, the M-step is fairly involved, and the required caveats to make it work are somewhat unsatisfying.

From an experimental perspective, it would be beneficial to analyze the effect of initialization on the EM algorithm. For instance, if the EM algorithm were initialized with the result of KA, would it improve upon the solution? Additionally, it is unclear why the authors chose to report relative log-likelihood as a measure. Log-probability differences reflect ratios of probabilities, so taking ratios of log-probabilities is not entirely clear. A more straightforward approach would be to report differences in log-probabilities.

Some minor issues with the writing style are also worth noting. The authors' use of the term "elegant" to describe their algorithm is somewhat subjective and debatable, given the caveats and approximations required in the M-step. Furthermore, the bold text in the introduction is unnecessary.

In conclusion, the paper presents an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs. While the experiments could be improved, and the improvements over the basic projected gradient algorithm are not substantial, the paper is well-written and contributes to the field.
