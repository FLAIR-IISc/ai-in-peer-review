- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by treating eigenvector selection as a hidden variable.
- Operates on the kernel eigendecomposition, lower-bounds the log-likelihood via Jensen’s inequality, and updates eigenvectors using Stiefel-manifold optimization.
- Experiments show the EM method outperforms a projected gradient baseline ("K-Ascent") in robustness and runtime.
- Method appears technically sound and derivation of the EM algorithm is plausible.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to non-DPP diverse-subset selection methods, making broader benefits unclear.
- Paper is not clearly written or well organized; notation is sometimes used before definition and the derivation is interleaved with the main presentation.
- Recommend presenting the final algorithm clearly and completely first, with derivation and alternatives moved later.
- Approach is highly original — likely the first EM-style method based on the kernel eigendecomposition for DPP learning.
- Represents a solid technical advance with potential practical significance for subset selection applications.
- Notation issues: define the marginal kernel K before Eq. (3).
- Notation issues: define matrix V (eigenvectors) before Eq. (1).
- Clarify K-Ascent projection step as choosing the closest PSD matrix to Q in Frobenius norm.
- Since a simplified Stiefel update is used, outline that simpler update directly to make the paper self-contained.
- Make the meaning of “mixture weight” in Eq. (7) more explicit.
- Provide better rationale/intuition for the moments-matching initializer in Eq. (23) and justify why simpler initializers (e.g., empirical covariance) aren’t used.
