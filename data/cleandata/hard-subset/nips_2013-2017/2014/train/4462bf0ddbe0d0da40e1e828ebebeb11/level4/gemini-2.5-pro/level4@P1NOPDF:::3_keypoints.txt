- Addresses learning the full DPP kernel matrix L, noting prior work used restrictive parameterizations because full learning is NP-hard.  
- Proposes a novel method that imposes no restrictions on L and avoids problematic projection steps used in standard gradient methods.  
- Key idea: use an EM procedure built on L’s eigendecomposition, optimizing eigenvalues and eigenvectors separately.  
- Avoids projecting eigenvectors by reformulating their constraint as optimization on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Constructs the EM lower bound via Jensen’s inequality to obtain a tractable objective.  
- Theoretical contributions include the Stiefel-manifold eigenvector update and a proof that the inverse distribution is also a DPP.  
- Derivations are clear and cohesive; proofs are well-written.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and product-recommendation data supports the claim that the method preserves quality and diversity.  
- Quantitative gains on the product-recommendation task are modest (two of top-ten items replaced) but still supportive.  
- Minor weaknesses: brief conclusion and limited real-world datasets; more diverse experiments would better demonstrate practical impact.
