- Main contribution: novel EM algorithm to learn DPP kernel by treating a DPP as a mixture of “elementary DPPs” with a latent variable, alternating an E-step (k-DPP variational update) and an M-step (gradient updates to eigenvalues/eigenvectors).
- Baseline comparison: presented as an alternative to projected gradient ascent on kernel entries (K-Ascent, KA).
- Empirical result: EM significantly outperforms KA under naive initialization on a product recommendation task.
- Empirical caveat: EM gives only modest improvement when KA is well-initialized via moment matching.
- Efficiency claim: EM is asymptotically faster and shows notable practical speedups in experiments.
- Novelty: creative reformulation of DPP learning within an EM framework for a difficult non-convex problem.
- Robustness: EM is more robust to naive initialization and avoids near-diagonal convergence issues seen with KA.
- M-step complexity: the M-step is involved and relies on approximations and heuristics (single gradient steps, true-likelihood line search), reducing conceptual simplicity.
- Practical compromises: line search requires repeated E-steps and other expensive operations, detracting from EM elegance.
- Limited empirical analysis: need more investigation of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM from converged KA to see if EM finds better optima).
- Metric concern: use of “relative log-likelihood” is non-standard and hard to interpret; absolute log-likelihood differences would be clearer.
- Presentation note: remove unconventional boldface emphasis in the introduction.
- Overall conclusion: interesting, solid contribution that is more robust and efficient, but experiments and clarity could be improved.
