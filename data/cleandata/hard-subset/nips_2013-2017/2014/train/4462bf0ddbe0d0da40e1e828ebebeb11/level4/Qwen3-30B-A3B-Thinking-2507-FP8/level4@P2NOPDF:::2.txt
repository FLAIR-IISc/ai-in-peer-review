

The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), integrating several sophisticated components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen's inequality-based lower bound on the log-likelihood. The experiments demonstrate superior performance compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.

**Quality:** The proposed method appears theoretically sound and plausible. However, the experimental evaluation is insufficient. The comparison is limited to K-Ascent (a DPP-specific method), omitting benchmark comparisons against established non-DPP approaches for subset selection (e.g., greedy algorithms or random selection methods referenced in the introduction). Consequently, the practical benefit of the DPP framework over simpler alternatives remains unclear.

**Clarity:** The manuscript suffers from poor exposition. The writing is often convoluted, with the main method development interleaved with tangential discussions of alternatives and challenges. Key notation (e.g., marginal kernel *K* on p.2, line 81; matrix *V* on p.2, line 100) is introduced after first use, and definitions are sometimes omitted. The narrative structure impedes understanding; a clearer presentation of the core method followed by a dedicated discussion section would be preferable.

**Originality:** The approach appears novel. To the reviewer's knowledge, no prior work combines these specific technical elements for DPP learning in this manner.

**Significance:** The method represents a substantial contribution to subset selection, offering a more principled and effective learning framework than existing DPP-based techniques. It addresses a meaningful gap in the literature.

**Major Concerns:**
- **Notation & Definitions:** Critical terms are undefined upon first use (e.g., marginal kernel *K* on p.2, l.81; *V* on p.2, l.100). Clarify the properties required for a kernel to qualify as "marginal."
- **Frobenius Norm Clarity (p.3, l.159):** The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is ambiguous. Rewrite as "selects the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to *Q*."
- **Section 4 (p.4) Complexity:** The section relies heavily on reference [26] without outlining the authors' simplified approach. Directly describe the core technique to avoid forcing readers to consult external material.
- **Ambiguous Terminology (p.4, l.189):** The term "weight" lacks definition and context. Specify what "weight" refers to.
- **Method Justification (p.7, l.324):** The rationale for omitting *m*<sub>*ij*</sub> in Equation (30) and the failure of empirical covariance as an initialization requires clearer explanation. Why is this specific initialization necessary? Why would a simpler approach fail?

**Summary:** The paper presents a technically sophisticated and potentially valuable method for DPP learning. However, the current manuscript is poorly written, with significant clarity issues and an inadequate experimental evaluation that fails to establish the method's practical advantage over non-DPP alternatives. Addressing the notation, structural, and experimental shortcomings is essential for publication. The core technical contribution is promising but currently obscured by presentation flaws.
