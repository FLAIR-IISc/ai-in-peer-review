- Proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices by representing a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and using a variational k-DPP over subsets.
- EM alternates updating the variational k-DPP (E-step) and taking gradient steps on kernel parameters (M-step).
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms naive K-Ascent (KA) initialization and gives modest gains over informed KA initialization.
- The M-step is computationally complex and requires caveats/approximations that the reviewer finds somewhat unsatisfying.
- Requests additional experiments on initialization, specifically initializing EM from the KA solution to see if that improves results.
- Questions the use of relative log-likelihood as the primary metric and recommends reporting log-likelihood differences for clearer interpretation.
- Stylistic critiques: avoid self-praising language (e.g., calling the method “elegant”) and remove superfluous bolding in the introduction.
- Overall judgment: interesting and novel approach; improvements over basic projected gradient are limited but the paper is of good quality and a valuable contribution.
