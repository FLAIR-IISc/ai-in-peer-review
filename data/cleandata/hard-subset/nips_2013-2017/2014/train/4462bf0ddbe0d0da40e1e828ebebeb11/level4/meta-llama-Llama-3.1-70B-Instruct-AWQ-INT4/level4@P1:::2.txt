The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), which combines several nontrivial ingredients, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality. The experiments show favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and it is believable that the methods should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction), so it is not clear how much we benefit from the DPP approach.

Clarity: The paper is not particularly clearly written. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes definitions come after their use. The writing is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, mostly because the text is structured to describe a process of how one ends up with the finally proposed approach, with side-remarks of possible alternative approaches and problems interleaved with the main thread. It would be preferable to present the developed method as clearly as possible and postpone discussion to a later stage.

Originality: The paper appears to be quite original, and the reviewer is not aware of similar work.

Significance: The method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset selection literature.

Details:

* On page 2, line 81, the marginal kernel K is not defined before use. It would be helpful to clarify what kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.
* On page 2, line 100, V is not defined. A clear definition of V would improve the readability of the paper.
* On page 3, line 159, the phrase "minimizes the change to Frobenius norm" is unclearly stated. It would be better to say "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q."
* The reviewer found Section 4 hard to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would be nice to outline the simpler approach directly so that the reader is not forced to look up the more complex original method.
* On page 4, line 189, the term "weight" is unclear. It would be helpful to define what "weight" refers to in this context.
* On page 7, line 324, the rationale behind Equation (30) is not entirely clear. It would be helpful to explain why m_ij is not used here and why simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel would not have worked as initialization.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs, which is a significant contribution to subset selection literature.
* The method combines several nontrivial ingredients, including eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality.
* The experiments show favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The evaluation of the method could be improved by comparing against alternative approaches for subset selection.
* The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow, with unclear notation and convoluted writing.
* The paper would benefit from clearer definitions and a more straightforward presentation of the developed method.
