This paper presents a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method cleverly combines several sophisticated techniques: eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s inequality-based lower bound on the log-likelihood. The experimental results demonstrate favorable performance compared to a projected gradient ascent approach (K-Ascent).

**Quality:** The proposed method appears technically sound and well-motivated. The authors convincingly demonstrate that the approach is theoretically justified and likely to function as described. However, the evaluation lacks comprehensive benchmarking. While the comparison to K-Ascent is valuable, the experiments are limited and do not compare against other established subset selection techniques (e.g., those cited in the introduction). This makes it difficult to assess the overall benefit of adopting a DPP-based approach, and how much of the performance gain is attributable specifically to the EM learning method versus the DPP model itself. A more thorough evaluation would significantly strengthen the paper.

**Clarity:** The paper suffers from clarity issues. The writing style is somewhat convoluted and difficult to follow. The presentation often prioritizes detailing the *process* of arriving at the proposed method – including side-remarks on alternative approaches and potential challenges – rather than presenting the final method in a clear and concise manner.  I recommend restructuring the paper to first clearly present the developed method, and then discuss alternative approaches and their limitations in a separate section. Notation is occasionally introduced before being defined, and definitions sometimes appear after their initial use.  These issues hinder understanding.

**Originality:** The work appears highly original. I am not aware of existing literature that combines these specific techniques for DPP learning. The innovative use of eigendecomposition, the Stiefel manifold optimization, and the mixture representation contribute to the novelty of the approach.

**Significance:** The method presented represents a substantial advance and a potentially valuable contribution to the subset selection literature. Addressing the challenging problem of learning DPPs without restrictive parameterizations is significant, and the proposed EM algorithm offers a promising solution. The potential impact of the work is further enhanced by its applicability to various domains where modeling diversity is crucial.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **p.2, line 81:** The “marginal kernel K” is used before being defined. Please clarify what qualifies a kernel as a marginal kernel.
*   **p.2, line 100:** Variable V is used without prior definition.
*   **p.3, line 159:**  “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is imprecise. I suggest rephrasing as “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
*   **p.4:** The discussion of the Stiefel manifold optimization (Section 3.5) is difficult to follow. Rather than solely referencing [26], provide a more detailed outline of your simplified approach directly within the paper to enhance readability and avoid forcing readers to consult external sources.
*   **p.4, line 189:**  Clarify what “weight” refers to in this context.
*   **p.7, line 324:** The rationale behind equation (30) is unclear. Why isn’t `m_{ij}` used? What were the issues with simply using the empirical covariance matrix as an initial kernel?
*   **Initialization:** The impact of different initialization strategies appears significant. It would be valuable to discuss the sensitivity of the EM algorithm to the initialization and to provide some guidance on choosing an appropriate starting point.



**Summary of Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Novel and technically sound approach to DPP learning.
*   Combines several advanced techniques in a creative and effective manner.
*   Demonstrated improvement over a baseline method (K-Ascent).
*   Potentially significant contribution to subset selection.

**Con:**

*   Limited experimental evaluation with a lack of comparison to broader subset selection methods.
*   Clarity issues in the writing and presentation.
*   The motivation behind certain design choices (e.g., initialization) is not fully explained.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable and original contribution to the field of DPP learning. While the clarity of the presentation and the limited scope of the experiments are weaknesses, the technical novelty and potential significance of the work are compelling. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the clarity concerns and expanding the experimental evaluation. Specifically, the authors should include comparisons to state-of-the-art subset selection algorithms and provide a more in-depth analysis of the sensitivity to initialization.
