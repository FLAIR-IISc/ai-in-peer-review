- Proposes an EM-based reformulation for learning DPP kernels by viewing a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and alternating a k-DPP E-step with single-step-gradient M-steps.  
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms the projected-gradient baseline (KA) under naive initialization and gives modest gains with informed initialization.  
- Method is technically sound and motivated by failure modes of projected gradient methods for PSD kernels with eigenvalue constraints.  
- The M-step is fairly involved and depends on practical approximations (single-step updates), which is somewhat unsatisfying though workable.  
- Experiments are informative but lack analysis of EM’s sensitivity to initialization (e.g., initializing EM from KA’s final solution).  
- The use of “relative log-likelihood” as the reported metric is nonstandard and hard to interpret; absolute log-likelihoods or differences should be reported.  
- Paper is generally well organized; the mixture view and k-DPP E-step are clearly explained.  
- M-step derivations are dense; important assumptions and approximations should be isolated and their practical implications summarized.  
- Style suggestions: avoid subjective terms like “elegant” and unnecessary boldface in the introduction.  
- Originality: novel EM recasting of DPP learning using mixture-of-elementary-DPPs and a k-DPP E-step to sidestep problematic projections.  
- Significance: practically useful when KA is poorly initialized; improvements are smaller but present with stronger initialization.  
- Limitations: gains are not universally large and reliance on a careful, approximate M-step tempers enthusiasm.  
- Pros: well-motivated formulation, robust empirical gains under naive initialization, addresses known projected-gradient weaknesses.  
- Cons: M-step approximations, limited initialization-sensitivity analysis, and nonstandard reporting metric.  
- Overall: interesting and solid contribution that would be stronger with clearer reporting and additional analysis of initialization effects.
