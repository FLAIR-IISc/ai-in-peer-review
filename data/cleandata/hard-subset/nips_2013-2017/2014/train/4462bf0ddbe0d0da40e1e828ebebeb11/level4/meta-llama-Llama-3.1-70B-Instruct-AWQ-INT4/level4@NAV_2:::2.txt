The paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes. The method combines several nontrivial ingredients: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood by Jensen's Inequality. The experiments show favorable behavior of the proposed approach compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and it is believable that the methods should work as stated. However, the evaluation of the method could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare against alternative approaches for subset selection, so it is not clear how much we benefit from the DPP approach. To strengthen the evaluation, it would be beneficial to include comparisons with other methods mentioned in the introduction.

Clarity: The paper's writing is not particularly clear. The write-up is somewhat sloppy and hard to follow. Not all notation is defined, and sometimes definitions come after their use. The writing is also unnecessarily convoluted at times, mostly because the text is structured to describe a process of how one ends up with the finally proposed approach, with side-remarks of possible alternative approaches and problems interleaved with the main thread. A clearer presentation of the developed method would be preferred, with discussions postponed to a later stage.

Originality: The paper appears to be original, as I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: I think the method derived in the paper presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset selection literature.

Details:

- On page 2, line 81, the marginal kernel K is not defined before use. What kind of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel? A brief definition or explanation would be helpful.

- On page 2, line 100, V is not defined. Adding a definition or explanation for V would improve clarity.

- On page 3, line 159, the phrase "minimizes the change to Frobenius norm" is unclearly stated. It would be better to say "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q" for clarity.

- On page 4, I found this section hard to understand. Rather than referring to [26], it would be nice to outline the simpler approach directly so that the reader is not forced to look up the more complex original method.

- On page 4, line 189, what does "weight" refer to? A brief explanation or definition would be helpful.

- On page 7, line 324, I don't quite get the rationale in (30). Why is m_ij not used here? Why would simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel not have worked as initialization? A clearer explanation of this choice would be beneficial.

Overall, the paper presents a new method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. While the method appears to be original and non-trivial, the write-up could be improved for clarity, and the experiments could be expanded to include comparisons with other methods.
