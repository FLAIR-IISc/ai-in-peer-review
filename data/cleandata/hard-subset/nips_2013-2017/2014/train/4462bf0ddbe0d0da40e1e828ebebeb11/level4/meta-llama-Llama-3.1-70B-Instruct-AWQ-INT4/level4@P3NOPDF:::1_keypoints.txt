- Proposes an EM-based method to learn DPP kernel matrices, improving over standard projected gradient K-Ascent (KA).
- EM formulates any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs, introduces a variational distribution over subsets, and alternates between updating a k-DPP and M-step gradient updates.
- Experiments show EM outperforms KA, especially with naive KA initialization; gains are smaller with smarter KA initialization.
- The M-step is complex and requires caveats/approximations, which is somewhat unsatisfying.
- Recommend testing EM initialized from KA to see if that yields further improvement.
- Question the use of "relative log likelihood" (ratios of log probabilities); suggest reporting differences in log probabilities for clearer interpretation.
- Minor writing issues: avoid calling the algorithm "elegant" given caveats, and remove unnecessary bolding in the introduction.
- Overall assessment: interesting and well-written contribution with modest empirical improvements; experiments could be strengthened.
