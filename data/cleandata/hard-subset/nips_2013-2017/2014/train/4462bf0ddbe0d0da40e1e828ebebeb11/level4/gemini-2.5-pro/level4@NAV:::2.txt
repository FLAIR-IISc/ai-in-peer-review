This paper introduces an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning the parameters of a Determinantal Point Process. The proposed method integrates several sophisticated components, such as kernel eigendecomposition, optimization over the Stiefel manifold, the characterization of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and the application of Jensen's Inequality to establish a lower bound on the log-likelihood. Experimental results indicate that the proposed algorithm outperforms a simpler baseline, the "K-Ascent" method.

**Quality:** The proposed methodology appears to be theoretically sound, and its described functionality is plausible. However, the primary weakness of the paper lies in its empirical evaluation. The experimental setup is narrow in scope, notably lacking comparisons against other subset selection techniques, including those cited in the introduction. Consequently, the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate the practical advantages of using a DPP-based model over established alternatives.

**Clarity:** The manuscript's clarity is a significant concern. The presentation is often difficult to follow due to issues such as undefined notation and terms being used before their formal definition. The exposition is at times unnecessarily convoluted, seemingly because the text is structured to narrate the derivation of the algorithm, interspersing comments on alternative paths and potential issues along the way. A more direct presentation, where the final method is clearly laid out first with discussions and derivations deferred to a subsequent section, would greatly enhance readability.

**Originality:** To the best of my knowledge, the proposed approach is novel; I am not familiar with prior work that addresses this problem in a similar manner.

**Significance:** The methodology developed in this paper represents a notable advance and stands as a valuable contribution to the literature on subset selection.

**Details:**
*   p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is used before it is formally defined. The text should clarify the properties that qualify a kernel as a marginal kernel.
*   p.2, line 100: The variable V is used without a prior definition.
*   p.3, line 159: The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is ambiguous. It would be clearer to state that the algorithm "chooses the positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix closest to Q in terms of Frobenius norm."
*   p.4: This section is difficult to comprehend. The paper should provide a self-contained outline of its simpler approach rather than solely referencing the more complex method in [26], which requires the reader to consult an external source.
*   p.4, line 189: The term "weight" is used ambiguously. Its specific meaning in this context should be clarified.
*   p.7, line 324: The rationale behind the initialization in equation (30) is unclear. The authors should explain why m_ij is omitted from this step and justify why this specific initialization was chosen over a more straightforward approach, such as using the empirical covariance matrix directly.

This work proposes a novel algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection, which appears to be both original and technically complex. However, its presentation lacks clarity, and the empirical validation is limited as it omits comparisons to non-DPP baseline methods.
