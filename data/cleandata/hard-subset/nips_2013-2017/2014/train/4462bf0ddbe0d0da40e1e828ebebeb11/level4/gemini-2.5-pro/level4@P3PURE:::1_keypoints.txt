- Proposes an EM framework to learn DPP kernels, contrasted with projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- Core idea: represent any DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs and introduce a variational distribution over subsets.  
- Algorithm alternates an E-step (updates variational k-DPP) and an M-step (single gradient steps on kernel parameters).  
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms poorly initialized K-Ascent and gives modest gains over well-initialized K-Ascent in some settings.  
- Strength: creative, novel reformulation that offers a new perspective on the optimization landscape and shows empirical improvements.  
- Weakness: M-step is complex and relies on several approximations and caveats, reducing the conceptual elegance.  
- Weakness: experiments should include initializing EM with the K-Ascent solution to determine if EM refines or simply finds better local optima.  
- Weakness: using "relative log-likelihood" as the primary metric is non-standard and hard to interpret; recommend reporting direct log-likelihood differences or justifying the choice.  
- Minor: revise writing—avoid subjective descriptors like "elegant" for the method and unnecessary bolded emphasis.  
- Conclusion: novel and valuable contribution with clear merit, though gains are not always large and experimental analysis could be strengthened.
