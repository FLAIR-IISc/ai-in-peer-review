- DPPs model diversity via a PSD matrix L, but learning L is NP-hard; prior work restricts L (e.g., scalar-weighted) or imposes strong parametric constraints, reducing diversity.  
- The paper proposes a learning method that does not restrict L’s parameterization, using EM on eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- EM avoids the projection drawbacks of projected gradient ascent by exploiting V’s full rank to eliminate eigenvector projection.  
- The authors derive a Jensen-based lower bound to construct the EM procedure and map eigenvector constraints to the Stiefel manifold.  
- They show the inverse distribution is also a DPP, strengthening theoretical understanding.  
- The method preserves diversity while maintaining quality, unlike prior approaches that prioritized quality over diversity.  
- The step-by-step EM derivation and proofs are clear, well written, and the reduction from the original optimization to EM is insightful.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support the claims (method retains quality and diversity compared to KA), though more real-world experiments are needed.  
- The conclusion is somewhat hasty and does not fully emphasize all contributions.
