

The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The method integrates several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood via Jensen's inequality. Experiments demonstrate favorable performance compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears well-founded and plausible. However, the evaluation requires improvement. The experiments are limited and omit comparisons with alternative subset selection approaches (e.g., those cited in the introduction), making it unclear what specific benefits the DPP approach provides.

Clarity: The paper is not clearly written; the writing is somewhat sloppy and difficult to follow. Notation is often undefined prior to use, and definitions frequently appear after initial reference. The text is unnecessarily convoluted, as it interweaves the development process of the method with side-remarks on alternatives and challenges, rather than presenting the core method concisely followed by discussion.

Originality: The work appears highly original; I am unaware of similar approaches in the literature.

Significance: The derived method represents a solid advancement and a valuable contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- p. 2, line 81: The marginal kernel \(K\) is used without prior definition. What constraints define a marginal kernel?
- p. 2, line 100: The variable \(V\) is undefined.
- p. 3, line 159: The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. It should state: "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semidefinite matrix to \(Q\)."
- p. 4: This section is difficult to follow. Instead of referencing [26], the authors should outline their simplified approach directly to avoid requiring readers to consult the more complex original method.
- p. 4, line 189: The term "weight" is undefined.
- p. 7, line 324: The rationale for equation (30) is unclear. Why is \(m_{ij}\) not utilized? Why would initializing with the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel fail? The paper introduces a highly non-trivial, likely original method for learning DPPs for subset selection. The writing lacks clarity, and the experiments only consider DPP-based methods.
