- Paper proposes a novel reformulation for learning DPP kernels using an EM framework instead of direct gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).  
- EM approach models a variational distribution over subsets as a k-DPP and alternates E-step updates with a single gradient M-step.  
- Empirical results show EM consistently outperforms KA with naive initialization and gives modest gains with informed KA initialization.  
- The approach is conceptually interesting and the EM formulation meaningfully improves performance despite approximations.  
- The M-step is computationally complex and relies on caveats and approximations to be practical.  
- Recommend an experiment initializing EM from the KA solution to test whether EM can refine KA-found solutions.  
- Concern about the reported metric "relative log-likelihood": reporting ratios of log-probabilities is unclear and hard to interpret.  
- Suggest reporting simple differences in log-probabilities for more intuitive evaluation.  
- Minor stylistic critiques: avoid self-congratulatory wording (e.g., calling the algorithm “elegant”) and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: solid, promising work with well-executed ideas but limited experimental gains and room for clearer evaluation and additional experiments.
