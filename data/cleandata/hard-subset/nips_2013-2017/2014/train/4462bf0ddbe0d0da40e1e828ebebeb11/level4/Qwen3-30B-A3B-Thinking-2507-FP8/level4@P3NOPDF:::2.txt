

The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) for subset selection, integrating eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, DPP mixture representations, and Jensen’s inequality for log-likelihood lower-bounding. The method appears theoretically sound, and experiments demonstrate advantages over the simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.  

**Quality.** The technical approach is well-founded and likely effective as described. However, the experimental evaluation is insufficient. The paper compares only against K-Ascent (a DPP-specific method) and omits comparisons to established non-DPP subset selection techniques (e.g., greedy selection, random sampling, or methods cited in the introduction). Without such baselines, it remains unclear whether the DPP framework provides meaningful benefits over simpler, non-probabilistic alternatives. The limited scope undermines the claimed practical significance.  

**Clarity.** The presentation is disorganized and difficult to follow. Key issues include:  
- Notation is introduced *after* use (e.g., marginal kernel $K$ on p.2, l.81; $V$ on p.2, l.100).  
- The narrative interweaves method development with side discussions of alternatives, obscuring the core contribution. A clearer structure would present the finalized method first, deferring design choices and trade-offs to later sections.  
- Critical phrasing is imprecise: "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" (p.3, l.159) should specify "chooses the closest positive semidefinite matrix to $Q$ in Frobenius norm."  
- Section 4 (p.4) references [26] without summarizing the adapted technique, forcing readers to consult external work. A concise self-contained explanation would improve accessibility.  
- Ambiguous terms persist (e.g., "weight" on p.4, l.189; rationale for excluding $m_{ij}$ in Eq. 30 on p.7).  

**Originality.** The approach is novel. To the best of my knowledge, no prior work combines these specific elements (eigendecomposition, Stiefel optimization, mixture representation, and Jensen’s lower bound) for DPP learning. The paper makes a distinct technical contribution.  

**Significance.** The method advances DPP-based subset selection, offering a principled learning framework. While the theoretical contribution is strong, the limited experimental validation weakens the perceived impact. A more comprehensive evaluation would better demonstrate its utility in real-world scenarios.  

**Specific Comments:**  
- **p.2, l.81:** Define "marginal kernel $K$" before use. Clarify its relationship to the full kernel.  
- **p.2, l.100:** Define $V$ (likely a matrix from prior context).  
- **p.3, l.159:** Rewrite to state precisely: "selects the closest positive semidefinite matrix to $Q$ under the Frobenius norm."  
- **p.4:** Replace reliance on [26] with a brief summary of the adapted technique.  
- **p.4, l.189:** Specify what "weight" refers to (e.g., mixture component weights).  
- **p.7, l.324:** Justify why $m_{ij}$ is omitted in Eq. 30. Explain why empirical covariance cannot serve as a valid initial kernel (e.g., due to non-PSD issues or mismatch with DPP structure).  

**Summary.** The paper presents a technically novel EM algorithm for DPP learning with sound theoretical foundations. However, the experimental evaluation lacks comparative context against non-DPP methods, and the writing suffers from poor organization and ambiguous notation. Addressing these issues would significantly strengthen the contribution. The core idea is valuable, but the current presentation limits its accessibility and impact.  

**Recommendation:** **Weak Accept** (with major revisions). The method is original and promising, but the review requires substantial improvements to clarity and experimental rigor before it can be confidently endorsed. Specifically, the authors must: (1) add non-DPP baselines in experiments, (2) restructure the presentation to prioritize clarity over narrative flow, and (3) resolve all notation and phrasing ambiguities. Without these, the paper’s significance remains unconvincing.
