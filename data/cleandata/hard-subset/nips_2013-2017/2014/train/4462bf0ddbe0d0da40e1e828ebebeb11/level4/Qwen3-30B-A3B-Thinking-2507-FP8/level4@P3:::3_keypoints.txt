- Addresses learning the full DPP kernel matrix via a novel EM approach that works in the eigendecomposition space, avoiding restrictive parameterizations.  
- EM formulation derived by lower-bounding the log-likelihood with Jensen’s inequality and using coordinate ascent on eigenvalues/eigenvectors.  
- Eliminates problematic projection steps in gradient ascent that produce degenerate, near-diagonal kernels and destroy negative item interactions.  
- Handles the eigenvector orthogonality constraint as optimization on the Stiefel manifold and uses an efficient multiplicative update to avoid projection.  
- Provides theoretical runtime analysis showing EM is asymptotically faster than gradient ascent (O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs. O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3)).  
- Empirical evaluation on Amazon baby registries shows consistent gains: 3.7% average test log-likelihood improvement across 13 categories and up to 16.5% in one category.  
- Experimental analysis links EM’s improvements to preserving off-diagonal kernel components and modeling negative interactions crucial for diversity.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear derivations, rigorous proofs, and thorough connection to prior work.  
- Supplemental material, code, and data are provided, facilitating reproducibility.  
- Method is technically sound, complete, original, and significant for applications requiring subset diversity.  
- Main weakness: experimental scope is limited to a single application domain (product recommendation).  
- Main weakness: limited exploration of EM’s potential limitations and sensitivity to initialization beyond two tested methods.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel EM approach, theoretical and empirical improvements, clear reproducibility, and practical impact.  
- Rejection arguments: narrow experimental validation and unclear practical significance of some reported improvements in certain contexts.
