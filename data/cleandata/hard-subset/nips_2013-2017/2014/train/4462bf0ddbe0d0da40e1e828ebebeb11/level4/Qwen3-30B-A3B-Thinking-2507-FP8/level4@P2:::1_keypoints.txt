- Addresses learning full kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs), extending beyond prior restricted learning settings.  
- Proposes an EM framework that represents a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs to enable full-kernel learning.  
- EM avoids the projection steps required by standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- Empirical gains over K-Ascent are significant when initialized naively (up to 16.5% relative test log-likelihood improvement).  
- Properly situates the work within prior DPP literature and clearly explains differences from earlier methods.  
- Technical development is rigorous with careful derivations of the EM algorithm.  
- Asymptotic runtime analysis shows EM is more efficient than K-Ascent for moderate-sized problems.  
- Experiments are well-designed on a real-world product recommendation dataset with appropriate statistical analysis.  
- M-step requires non-trivial approximations and eigenvector-update caveats, which slightly weaken theoretical appeal.  
- The method needs non-trivial initialization to avoid degenerate (diagonal) kernels.  
- Absolute improvements are modest in some settings (e.g., 0.4% with moment-matching initialization), limiting practical impact in those cases.  
- Relative log-likelihood metric reporting (ratios of log probabilities) is confusing and should be clarified.  
- Limited analysis of initializing EM with K-Ascent results—more study of initialization effects is needed.  
- Some stylistic issues (distracting bold text, subjective claims like “elegant”) and imprecise language in places.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, technically sound contribution with clear advantages in challenging scenarios; authors should clarify metric interpretation and expand initialization analysis.
