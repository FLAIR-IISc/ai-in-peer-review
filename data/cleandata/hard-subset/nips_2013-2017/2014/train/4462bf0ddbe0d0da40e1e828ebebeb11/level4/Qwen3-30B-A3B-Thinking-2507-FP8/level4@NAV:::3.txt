

Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) define distributions over a fixed ground set that favor diverse subsets, parameterized by a positive semidefinite matrix (L). Learning L is NP-hard, leading prior work to focus on partial learning or restricted parametric forms of L. Using scalar vectors for L sacrifices diversity, while existing methods often require projection steps (e.g., in gradient ascent) that compromise both similarity and diversity properties. This paper proposes an unrestricted learning method for L that avoids such projections. By leveraging the full-rank property of eigenvectors and applying Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective, the authors derive an EM procedure that optimizes over eigenvalues and eigenvectors without projecting eigenvectors—unlike projected gradient ascent, which necessitates projecting both. The approach preserves DPP diversity, a key advantage over prior work that prioritized quality alone. The step-by-step EM derivation is insightful, and contributions such as mapping constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold (eliminating eigenvector projection) and proving the inverse distribution remains a DPP are well-justified. Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task demonstrate that the method maintains both quality and diversity, outperforming KA (where only two of the top 10 recommended products are replaced by less probable items). The paper is coherently written with rigorous proofs, and the EM reduction is clearly explained. However, the conclusion is somewhat premature, omitting key contributions. While the current experiments support the claims, additional testing on real-world datasets would better illustrate the practical impact of the diversity preservation. Overall, the technical contributions are strong, and the method offers a meaningful advancement over existing approaches.
