- Problem & contribution: Proposes learning a full DPP marginal kernel K by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition and deriving an EM algorithm using the DPP-as-mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view.
- E-step: Shows the posterior over eigenvector indices J is a conditional k-DPP with a computable kernel.
- M-step: Updates eigenvalues in closed form via k-DPP marginals and updates eigenvectors with a Riemannian (Stiefel-manifold) gradient step.
- Advantage over KA: Avoids PSD/eigenvalue projection that can collapse eigenvalues to zero and stall off-diagonals.
- Empirical results: On an Amazon baby registry dataset, EM improves test log-likelihood over projected gradient ascent (KA), especially under naive initialization and for categories with strong off-diagonals.
- Runtime: EM is typically ~2× faster in wall-clock time; complexity analysis supports speedups (O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3) for KA).
- Technical soundness: EM lower bound and E-step derivation are sound; closed-form eigenvalue update is efficient and well justified.
- Partial M-step concern: Using only a single Riemannian step per M-step (with line search) breaks EM monotonicity guarantees, though it works in practice.
- Empirical limitations: Evaluation is limited to one real-world domain and lacks comparisons to several relevant baselines (per-item scaling, mixtures of fixed DPPs, Bayesian methods).
- Metric reporting: Reporting “relative test log-likelihood” is hard to interpret; absolute per-example/item log-likelihoods and standard errors are preferred.
- Initialization sensitivity: Not fully characterized—unclear if EM improves already-converged KA or consistently escapes KA’s poor local optima.
- Originality: Novel use of eigendecomposition plus k-DPP marginals and Stiefel steps to learn a full DPP kernel; creative and practically motivated.
- Significance: Modest average gains but meaningful improvements when modeling negative interactions; robustness and speed make it promising for applications.
- Pros: Conceptually neat EM, avoids degeneracy from projections, efficient eigenvalue updates, solid complexity analysis, demonstrated robustness/speed, code release.
- Cons: No monotone EM guarantee, limited empirical breadth and baselines, nonstandard metric reporting, unclear sensitivity to initialization and ability to improve strong KA solutions.
- Suggested experiments: Initialize EM from converged KA, measure how often EM escapes KA optima, try multiple Riemannian steps, and report lower-bound/likelihood trajectories.
- Suggested methodological fixes: Consider logistic reparameterization for eigenvalues (λ = σ(θ)) to avoid λ→1 issues and improve stability.
- Additional baselines & metrics: Compare to per-item scaling, mixtures of fixed kernels, (if feasible) Bayesian parametric baselines, and report absolute likelihoods, standard errors, and task metrics (MAP, coverage/diversity).
- Ablations & robustness checks: Analyze effect of k on runtime/accuracy, vary number of eigenvector update steps, and strengthen KA baselines (momentum, better line search).
- Clarity & presentation: Paper is well organized but should reduce subjective language and boldface; include a brief main-text summary of the conditional k-DPP derivation.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, novel, and practically effective method; recommend acceptance pending improved evaluation (additional baselines, clearer metrics) and clarifications about initialization and the partial M-step.
