- Problem: Learning full DPP kernel L is NP-hard; prior work restricts parameterization (e.g., low-rank) which can hurt captured diversity.
- Contribution: Proposes a learning method that avoids restricting L’s parameterization, preserving full expressiveness.
- Optimization insight: Uses an EM approach on L’s eigenvalues and eigenvectors to avoid projection steps that harm similarity properties.
- Theoretical technique: Applies Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM formulation.
- Manifold formulation: Maps the eigenvector constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold, eliminating eigenvector projection.
- Structural result: Shows the resulting inverse distribution is also a DPP, a noteworthy theoretical contribution.
- Practical benefit: Method preserves DPP diversity (often neglected in prior work focused on quality).
- Empirical validation: Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task show retained quality and improved diversity.
- Baseline comparison: Outperforms Kernel Alignment in recommendations, where KA replaced two of the top ten items with less relevant products.
- Presentation: Step-by-step EM derivation and proofs are clear and well-structured.
- Weakness: Conclusion felt rushed and did not fully reiterate contributions.
- Suggestion: More experiments on varied real-world datasets would strengthen empirical claims about diversity benefits.
