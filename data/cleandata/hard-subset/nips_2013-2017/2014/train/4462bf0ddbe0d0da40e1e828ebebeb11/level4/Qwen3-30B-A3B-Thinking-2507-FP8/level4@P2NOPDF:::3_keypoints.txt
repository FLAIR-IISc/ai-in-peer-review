- Addresses an important gap: learning full-parameter DPP kernels L without sacrificing diversity for tractability.  
- Prior work mostly used partial or restricted parameterizations (e.g., scalar vectors) that compromise diversity.  
- Proposes a novel learning method that avoids restricting L and removes projection steps that hurt diversity and similarity.  
- Key insight: leverage the full-rank property of eigenvector matrix V to avoid projecting eigenvectors during optimization.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a lower bound on the objective for an EM formulation.  
- Maps the constrained optimization to the Stiefel manifold to eliminate eigenvector projection constraints.  
- Solves the resulting problem via eigenvalue and eigenvector updates within an EM procedure.  
- The method preserves DPP diversity properties, unlike prior likelihood-focused approaches.  
- Proves the inverse distribution remains a valid DPP—a non-trivial theoretical contribution.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task validate the approach.  
- Compared to KA baseline, the method maintains both quality and diversity (KA replaced two of the top-10 recommendations with less probable items).  
- Paper is well-structured, technically sound, with clear derivations and reproducible details.  
- Suggests conclusion should better highlight novelty of the Stiefel-manifold mapping and diversity-preserving guarantees.  
- Recommends broader experiments on larger real-world datasets to demonstrate practical, user-facing benefits.  
- Overall: strong contribution with solid theory and empirical support; merits publication at NIPS.
