- Addresses learning kernel matrices for DPPs and proposes a novel EM-based reformulation of the learning objective.
- Shows any DPP can be viewed as a mixture of elementary DPPs and uses this in an EM framework.
- Algorithm alternates between updating a variational distribution over subsets (modeled as a k-DPP) and a gradient-based M-step.
- Demonstrates significant improvement over standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) when KA is naively initialized.
- Shows only modest improvement over KA when KA is given a more informed initialization.
- M-step is complex and requires caveats, but achieving a working solution is noteworthy.
- Recommends further experiments on EM’s sensitivity to initialization, including initializing EM from KA solutions.
- Criticizes use of “relative log-likelihood” reporting; recommends reporting differences in log-probabilities for interpretability.
- Minor writing issues: avoid self-praise (e.g., calling the algorithm “elegant”) and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.
- Overall: interesting and generally well-executed approach, but experiments could be strengthened and performance gains over baseline are not large.
