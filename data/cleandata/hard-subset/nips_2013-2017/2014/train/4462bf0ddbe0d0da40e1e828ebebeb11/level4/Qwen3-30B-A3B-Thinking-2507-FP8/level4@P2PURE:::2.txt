

The paper introduces an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). The approach integrates several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood via Jensen's inequality. Experimental results indicate favorable performance compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.  

**Quality:** The method appears well-founded and plausible, but the evaluation requires strengthening. The experiments are limited and lack comparisons against alternative subset selection methods (e.g., those cited in the introduction), making it unclear what advantage the DPP approach provides.  

**Clarity:** The paper is poorly written. The exposition is sloppy, difficult to follow, and contains undefined notation (e.g., definitions sometimes appear after usage). The structure unnecessarily interweaves side-remarks about alternative approaches and problems into the main narrative, complicating the presentation. A clearer approach would state the developed method concisely and defer discussion to a later section.  

**Originality:** The work appears highly original; I am unaware of similar prior methods.  

**Significance:** The derived method represents a solid advance and a valuable contribution to subset selection literature.  

**Details:**  
- Page 2, line 81: The marginal kernel $K$ is used without prior definition. Clarify what kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.  
- Page 2, line 100: The symbol $V$ is undefined.  
- Page 3, line 159: The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. Revise to: "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semidefinite matrix to $Q$."  
- Page 4: This section is difficult to follow. Instead of referencing [26], outline the simplified approach directly to avoid requiring readers to consult the more complex original method.  
- Page 4, line 189: Clarify the meaning of "weight."  
- Page 7, line 324: The rationale for omitting $m_{ij}$ in equation (30) is unclear. Why not use the empirical covariance matrix as the initial kernel? The paper claims a new method for learning DPPs for subset selection, but the experiments only compare against other DPP methods.
