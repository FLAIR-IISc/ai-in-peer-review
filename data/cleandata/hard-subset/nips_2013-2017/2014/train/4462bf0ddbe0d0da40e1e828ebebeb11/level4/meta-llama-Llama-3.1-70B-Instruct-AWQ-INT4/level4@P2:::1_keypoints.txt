- Proposes a novel EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices that outperforms standard gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).
- Key idea: represent any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" (one per subset) and use a variational distribution over these subsets.
- Algorithm alternates between updating the subset distribution (a k-DPP) and taking single gradient steps in the M-step.
- Empirically, EM yields improvements over the naive KA initialization in the reported experiments.
- The M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats/approximations to make it work, which is a notable weakness.
- The paper is well-written and clearly explains the method and its relation to prior work.
- Experiments are well-designed but could use more analysis, particularly on the effect of initialization on EM (e.g., initializing EM with KA results).
- The use of "relative log likelihood" as the reported metric is confusing; the reviewer suggests using log-probability differences instead.
- Minor writing/style issues: avoid self-praising terms like "elegant" and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.
- Main strength: novel EM formulation that clearly improves over the standard approach.
- Main weakness: complexity and caveats in the M-step and limited analysis in experiments.
- Overall recommendation: positive—paper is a good contribution and deserves acceptance, with suggested clarifications and additional analyses.
