- Problem: learning the DPP kernel L is NP-hard and prior approaches are partial or compromise diversity.  
- Prior limitations: scalar/vector parametrizations and parametric restrictions sacrifice diversity or limit expressiveness.  
- Contribution: proposes a learning method that avoids such restrictions, preserving both diversity and quality.  
- Core idea: eliminate projection steps by leveraging the full-rank property of the eigenvector matrix V.  
- Method: derive an EM procedure via Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and reduce optimization to the Stiefel manifold.  
- Advantage: avoids eigenvector projection while ensuring the learned inverse distribution remains a valid DPP.  
- Derivation quality: step-by-step EM derivation is well-motivated and insightful.  
- Experiments: evaluated on synthetic data and a product recommendation task, compared to baseline KA.  
- Empirical result: retains quality and diversity—only two of the top-10 recommendations are replaced by less probable items under KA.  
- Theory and presentation: proofs are rigorous and well-structured; writing is coherent.  
- Shortcoming: conclusion prematurely summarizes contributions without fully elaborating novelty of eigenvector-free optimization or Stiefel mapping.  
- Shortcoming: additional real-world experiments are needed to better quantify the impact of preserved diversity.  
- Overall assessment: solid technical contribution that addresses a key DPP learning limitation with strong theoretical grounding and empirical validation.
