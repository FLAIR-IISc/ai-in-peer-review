- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture representation, and Jensen lower bounds; outperforms K-Ascent in experiments.

- Quality: method appears well-founded and plausible, but experiments are limited and lack comparisons to other subset-selection methods, so empirical benefits of DPPs are unclear.

- Clarity: paper is disorganized and sometimes hard to follow; notation is inconsistent and some definitions appear after use.

- Originality: approach is novel—leveraging eigendecomposition and EM for DPP learning seems new.

- Significance: solid advance addressing NP-hard DPP kernel learning with a practical EM solution that could impact subset-selection applications.

- Issue: marginal kernel K is used before being defined (p.2, line 81).

- Issue: symbol V is not defined before use (p.2, line 100).

- Issue: phrasing “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; should state choosing the closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm (p.3, line 159).

- Issue: section referencing [26] is hard to follow; authors should outline the simplified approach directly instead of deferring to the original (p.4).

- Issue: clarify what “weight” refers to in the context mentioned (p.4, line 189).

- Issue: rationale behind equation (30) is unclear—why isn’t m_{ij} used and why wouldn’t empirical covariance work for initialization? (p.7, line 324).

- Summary: novel and nontrivial method, but needs clearer presentation and broader experimental validation.

- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel solution to a challenging NP-hard problem, strong theoretical grounding, demonstrated improvement over a baseline, and potential practical impact.

- Anti-acceptance arguments: limited experimental evaluation with missing non-DPP baselines, poor clarity/notation, and insufficient explanation of certain design choices (e.g., initialization).
