This paper presents an EM algorithm for learning the Determinantal Point Process (DPP). This method is a combination of several important elements: eigenvalue decomposition of the kernel, optimization of the Stiefel manifold, representing the DPP as a mixture of the initial DPP, and a lower bound on the log-probability through Jensen's inequality. Experiments show that the proposed method behaves optimally compared to a general "K-accent" method.

Quality: To my knowledge, the method is well-established and credible that it will work as described. However, the method's evaluation could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and have not been compared with subset selection alternative methods (as mentioned in the introduction), so it is not clear how much we will benefit from the DPP method.

Clarity: The paper is not written with particular clarity. The writing is somewhat unstructured and difficult to follow. Not all symbols are defined, and sometimes definitions come after their usage. The writing is sometimes unnecessarily complex, mainly because the text is designed to describe a process that someone would eventually end up with the proposed method, with possible alternative methods and side-comments on problems mixed with the main thread. I would have preferred to present the improved method as clearly as possible and postpone the discussion at a later stage.

Originality: I found the paper quite original; I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: I believe that the method presented in the paper represents a significant advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- Page 2, line 81: The edge kernel K was not defined before being used. What kind of kernel qualifies as an edge kernel?
- Page 2, line 100: V was not defined.
- Page 3, line 159: "reducing the change in the Frobenius norm" is vaguely stated. "Selecting a PSD matrix close to Q (within the Frobenius norm)" would be better.
- Page 4. I found this section difficult to understand. Instead of mentioning [26], it would be better to present your (simple) method directly so that the reader is not forced to look for the more complex main method.
- Page 4, line 189: What does "weight" mean?
- Page 7, line 324. I don't quite understand the logic of (30). Why wasn't m_{ij} used here? Why didn't you start by using only empirical covariance matrices as kernels? The paper presents a new method for learning the Determinantal Point Process for subset selection. The method is highly non-trivial, and I assume it is original. The writing is not very clear. The experiments do not consider anything other than the DPP method.
