- DPP learning is hard (learning L is NP-hard); prior work uses partial/scalar parameterizations that hurt diversity or restrict L's form.
- This paper proposes a learning method that imposes no parametric restrictions on L.
- Instead of gradient ascent with projection (which harms similarity/diversity), the paper uses an EM framework over eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- The method explores optimization algorithms that remove the need for projection and leverages full-rank V to avoid eigenvector projection.
- Using Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM procedure is a key innovation.
- The proposed approach preserves DPP diversity (not just quality), addressing a gap in prior methods.
- The step-by-step EM derivation, mapping constraints to the Stiefel manifold, and showing the inverse distribution is also a DPP are notable technical contributions.
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support the claims that the method retains both quality and diversity (example: only 2 of top-10 products replaced by less likely alternatives).
- The paper is cohesive with well-structured proofs and a clear reduction to an EM framework.
- The conclusion feels rushed and underemphasizes contributions.
- More experiments on real-world datasets would strengthen the empirical case for the algorithm’s diversity benefits.
