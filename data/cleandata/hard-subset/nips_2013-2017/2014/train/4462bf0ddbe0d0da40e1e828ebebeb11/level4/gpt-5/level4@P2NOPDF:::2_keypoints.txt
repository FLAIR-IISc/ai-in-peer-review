- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that integrates eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a mixture representation via elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s inequality lower bound on the log-likelihood.  
- Experiments show favorable performance relative to a simpler K-Ascent baseline.  
- Methodology appears sound and derivations are plausible.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and does not compare against alternative subset-selection methods cited in the paper.  
- Paper lacks clarity: notation is sometimes undefined and definitions often appear after use.  
- Narrative is convoluted with digressions; recommend presenting the final method first and relegating derivations/alternatives to later sections.  
- Contribution appears original; reviewer is not aware of similar EM approaches for DPP learning.  
- Significance: method is a meaningful advance and a useful contribution to subset-selection literature.  
- Specific: marginal kernel K is used before being defined—define it at first mention and clarify the term.  
- Specific: matrix V is not defined before use—introduce V and its role.  
- Wording: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; prefer “chooses the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.”  
- p.4 relies heavily on [26]; outline the simpler approach directly so readers need not consult the original.  
- Clarify what “weight” refers to on p.4, line 189 (mixture weights, eigenvalue weights, etc.).  
- Equation (30) rationale is unclear: explain why m_{ij} is not used and why initializing with the empirical covariance as the kernel would be ineffective.
