- Proposes a novel learning method for DPPs that does not restrict the parameterization of the PSD matrix L, preserving diversity.  
- Addresses NP-hardness of learning L by avoiding restrictive parametric forms used in prior work.  
- Eliminates the need for a projection step that can compromise DPP similarity and diversity.  
- Uses an Expectation-Maximization (EM) procedure on eigenvalues and eigenvectors with Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective.  
- Explores optimization algorithms that avoid projecting values by leveraging the EM formulation.  
- Exploits the full-rank property of matrix V and uses the Stiefel manifold to remove eigenvector projection.  
- Shows the inverse distribution is itself a DPP, supporting the theoretical approach.  
- Provides clear, step-by-step derivation of the EM procedure and well-written proofs.  
- Evaluates on synthetic data and a product recommendation task, comparing to the KA algorithm.  
- Empirical results indicate the method retains both quality and diversity (only 2 of top-10 products replaced by less likely items).  
- Suggests adding more real-world experiments to further demonstrate the algorithm’s diversity benefits.  
- Notes the conclusion could better highlight the paper’s contributions, though overall writing and exposition are cohesive.
