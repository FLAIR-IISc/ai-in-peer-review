- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPP kernel matrices as an alternative to gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).
- Leverages eigendecomposition and a mixture of “elementary DPPs” to make the EM procedure tractable.
- EM alternates between updating a variational distribution over subsets (a k-DPP) and taking gradient steps on kernel parameters.
- Experiments show EM generally outperforms KA, especially when KA is naively initialized.
- Improvements are more modest when KA is given a better initialization.
- The method is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly connected to prior DPP work.
- The M-step uses approximations and has caveats that require careful handling.
- Reviewer requests deeper analysis of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM from KA solutions).
- Criticism of the primary evaluation metric: relative log-likelihood ratios are hard to interpret; differences in log-probabilities may be clearer.
- Minor stylistic critique: claims of the algorithm’s “elegance” should be toned down given its complexity and approximations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid approach; empirical improvements over baseline; clear presentation; creative solution to a hard optimization problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: performance gains are not always large; M-step approximations pose concerns; evaluation metric could be more interpretable.
