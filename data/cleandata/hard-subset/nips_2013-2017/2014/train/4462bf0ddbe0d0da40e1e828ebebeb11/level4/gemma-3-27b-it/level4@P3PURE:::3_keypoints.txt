- DPPs model diversity over a fixed ground set and are parameterized by a PSD matrix L.
- Learning the full L is NP-hard; prior work uses partial learning or restrictive parameterizations.
- Low-rank approximations can harm a DPP’s ability to model diversity.
- The paper proposes a learning method for full L without restrictive parameterization.
- The method avoids projection steps in gradient ascent that can degrade similarity and diversity.
- They develop an EM algorithm operating on L’s eigenvalues and eigenvectors to circumvent projection.
- Jensen’s inequality is used to lower-bound the objective and facilitate the EM construction.
- By optimizing over the Stiefel manifold, eigenvector projection is eliminated via full-rank eigenvector exploitation.
- They show the inverse distribution in their setup is itself a DPP, a notable theoretical result.
- The derivation and proofs are clear, systematic, and well-structured, especially the EM reduction.
- Experiments on synthetic and a product-recommendation dataset support that the learned DPP preserves both quality and diversity.
- Comparisons with Kernel Alignment show the proposed method better maintains diversity in recommendations.
- The paper is cohesive and well-written, though the conclusion is somewhat abrupt and could restate contributions more fully.
- More experiments on additional real-world datasets would strengthen evidence of the algorithm’s diversity benefits.
