This paper studies learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). Its main contribution is an EM-based reformulation of the learning objective that improves on the standard baseline of differentiating the log-likelihood with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent, KA). The key idea is to express a DPP as a mixture over “elementary DPPs,” one for each subset of items. The method introduces a variational distribution over these subsets—represented as a k-DPP—and alternates between updating this distribution (E-step) and taking single gradient updates of the kernel parameters (M-step). Empirically, the EM approach substantially outperforms KA under naive initialization and achieves smaller, sometimes meaningful gains when KA is more carefully initialized.

Overall, the reformulation is interesting and appears to provide improvements. That said, the M-step is quite involved, and the approximations and caveats needed to make it work temper the claimed elegance of the approach.

I would like to see more analysis of initialization effects on EM. In particular, if EM were initialized with the result of KA, would it further improve the solution?

I am also confused by the use of “relative log-likelihood” as the reported metric. Differences in log-likelihood are directly interpretable (they correspond to ratios of probabilities after exponentiation), whereas ratios of log-likelihoods are harder to make sense of. Why use ratios of log probabilities instead of reporting log-likelihood differences or absolute log-likelihoods?

Minor writing/style comments: please avoid calling your own method “elegant,” especially given the approximations in the M-step, and consider removing boldface in the introduction, which feels unnecessary.

In sum, this is a solid paper offering a novel EM perspective for learning DPP kernels. The empirical gains over basic projected gradient ascent are not dramatic across the board, but they are real. Clarifying the evaluation metric and expanding the analysis of initialization would strengthen the work.
