- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, elementary-DPP mixture representation, and a Jensen lower bound; outperforms a K-Ascent baseline.
- Method appears well-founded and credible, but experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to non-DPP subset-selection methods.
- Paper is poorly written and hard to follow, with notation defined late or not at all and a convoluted exposition that mixes development history with the method.
- Notation issue: "marginal kernel K" is used before being defined (p.2, l.81).
- Notation issue: V is not defined (p.2, l.100).
- Wording: replace “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” with “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q” (p.3, l.159).
- The section referring to [26] is hard to understand; the authors should outline their simpler approach directly rather than deferring to the reference (p.4).
- Ambiguity: unclear what “weight” refers to in p.4, l.189.
- Equation (30) is not well motivated: why is m_{ij} not used, and why not initialize with the empirical covariance as the kernel? (p.7, l.324).
- Originality: the approach appears original and not previously seen by the reviewer.
- Significance: the method is a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset-selection literature.
- Overall: novel and nontrivial method but hampered by unclear writing and insufficient experimental comparison.
