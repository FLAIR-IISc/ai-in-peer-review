This paper introduces an EM algorithm for learning the parameters of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP). The proposed method combines several non-trivial components, including the eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, the representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and the use of Jensen's Inequality to lower-bound the log-likelihood. The experimental results demonstrate that this approach outperforms a simpler baseline, the "K-Ascent" scheme.

**Quality**
The technical approach appears to be sound and the proposed algorithm is well-founded. However, the empirical evaluation could be significantly strengthened. The current experiments are limited in scope and, crucially, do not include comparisons against alternative, non-DPP methods for subset selection, some of which are mentioned in the introduction. This omission makes it difficult to assess the practical benefits of using a DPP-based approach over existing techniques.

**Clarity**
The clarity of the paper is a major concern. The writing is often difficult to follow, with a presentation that feels disorganized. Key issues include undefined notation and terms being used before they are formally introduced. The exposition is unnecessarily convoluted, largely because it is structured as a narrative detailing the derivation process. This narrative style, which interleaves the main algorithm with side-remarks on alternative paths and potential problems, obscures the final proposed method. The paper would be much easier to understand if it first presented the algorithm directly and then dedicated a separate section to the derivation, discussion, and comparison with alternatives.

**Originality**
To my knowledge, the proposed method is novel, and I am not aware of prior work that addresses this problem in the same way.

**Significance**
The method derived in this paper represents a solid technical advance and appears to be a valuable contribution to the literature on subset selection.

**Detailed Comments**
*   **p. 2, l. 81:** The marginal kernel K is used before it is defined. Please clarify what this kernel represents and what properties it must have.
*   **p. 2, l. 100:** The matrix V is not defined.
*   **p. 3, l. 159:** The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is ambiguous. It would be clearer to state that the goal is to find the positive semi-definite matrix that is closest to Q in the Frobenius norm.
*   **p. 4:** This section is difficult to understand. Instead of only referring to the more complex method in [26], it would be highly beneficial for the reader if the authors provided a self-contained outline of their proposed approach directly in the text.
*   **p. 4, l. 189:** The term "weight" is used without a clear definition. Please specify what this refers to.
*   **p. 7, l. 324:** The rationale for the initialization in equation (30) is unclear. Could the authors explain why `m_ij` is not used in this step? Furthermore, it is not obvious why a simpler initialization, such as using the empirical covariance matrix for the kernel, would not be sufficient.
