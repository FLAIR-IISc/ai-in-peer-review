- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn full DPP kernel by reformulating the DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" with a latent eigenvector-subset variable.
- EM alternates between an E-step computing a posterior k-DPP over eigenvector subsets and an M-step updating eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- Creative and interesting reformulation that avoids the projection step of projected gradient ascent (KA).
- EM empirically outperforms the KA baseline, especially with naive initialization or limited data, indicating greater robustness to initialization.
- EM shows significant runtime advantages and favorable asymptotic complexity compared to KA.
- M-step uses approximations (single gradient step for eigenvectors) and requires re-running the E-step for line searches; sensitivity of results to these approximations is unclear.
- Recommend experimenting with initializing EM from KA’s converged solution to test whether EM can fine-tune or only escapes poor local optima.
- Criticizes the use of "relative test log-likelihood differences" as unconventional and recommends reporting absolute log-likelihood differences for interpretability.
- Minor presentation issue: bolded emphasis in the introduction is distracting.
- Overall: solid and novel contribution with demonstrated robustness and efficiency, but would benefit from more thorough experiments and clearer evaluation metrics.
