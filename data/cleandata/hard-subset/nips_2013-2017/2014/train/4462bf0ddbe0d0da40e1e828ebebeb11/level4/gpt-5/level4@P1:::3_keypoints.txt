- Proposes EM-based ML learning for full DPP kernels by reparameterizing in eigenvalues/eigenvectors with a latent eigenvector-subset variable.  
- E-step produces a conditional k-DPP over eigenvector indices via Jensen’s inequality.  
- M-step gives a closed-form eigenvalue update and a gradient eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding ad hoc projection.  
- Addresses a failure mode of projected gradient ascent (KA) that yields near-diagonal, degenerate kernels that lose negative interactions.  
- Avoids problematic projections and has better asymptotic complexity than KA, with practical runtime speedups.  
- Empirically outperforms KA on test log-likelihood (up to 16.5% in sparse regimes), is more robust to initialization, and is ~2.1× faster on average.  
- Analysis links improvements to the magnitude of off-diagonal structure and includes qualitative examples showing preserved diversity.  
- Technical quality: sound derivations, appropriate EM use, and convincing empirical support for claims about KA degeneracy.  
- Clarity: coherent organization, clear proofs, and a well-explained Stiefel-manifold mapping; minor terminology clarification (k-DPP) suggested.  
- Originality: novel eigenbasis recasting with latent subset EM and the use of Stiefel optimization for eigenvectors.  
- Significance: enables learning full DPP kernels where negative interactions matter, improving robustness and runtime for diversity modeling.  
- Strengths for acceptance: principled novel method, addresses known projection failure, strong empirical gains, and clear theoretical connections.  
- Weaknesses for acceptance: limited real-world dataset diversity and reliance on log-likelihood as the primary evaluation metric.  
- Presentation weakness: brief conclusion that should better highlight the k-DPP E-step, Stiefel optimization, and robustness findings.  
- Suggestions: include additional real-world domains and task-oriented diversity metrics, expand the conclusion, and add sensitivity analyses and ablation studies.
