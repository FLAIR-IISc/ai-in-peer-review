- Introduces an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood lower bound; outperforms a simple K-Ascent baseline.

- Method appears theoretically sound and claims are plausible.

- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and needs significant expansion.

- Lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection techniques mentioned in the introduction, making benefits of a DPP-based approach unclear.

- Writing is disorganized; notation is inconsistently defined and definitions often appear after first use.

- Presentation reads like a development history rather than a clear exposition of the final method; alternative approaches and challenges are interleaved with the main line of reasoning.

- Paper appears original; reviewer is not aware of similar methods for learning DPPs.

- Significance: a solid advancement with potentially broad implications if DPPs can be efficiently learned.

- p.2, l.81: “marginal kernel” K is used without a prior definition—clarify which kernels qualify.

- p.2, l.100: variable V is not defined at its first occurrence.

- p.3, l.159: unclear phrasing “minimises the change to Frobenius norm”; suggest precise wording about choosing closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm.

- p.4: section relying on [26] is hard to follow; authors should briefly outline their simplified approach in the paper.

- p.4, l.189: the term “weight” is unclear and needs definition/clarification.

- p.7, l.324: rationale for equation (30) is not apparent—why isn’t m_ij used here, and why wasn’t the empirical covariance matrix a viable initial kernel?

- Overall: technically significant contribution but weakened by insufficient experiments and poor clarity; addressing these would substantially strengthen the paper.
