- Proposes an EM algorithm for DPP learning combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood lower bound; shows improvement over K-Ascent.
- Method appears theoretically sound but experimental evaluation is insufficient and limited to a single DPP baseline.
- Lacks comparisons to standard non-DPP subset-selection baselines (e.g., greedy algorithms, random selection), leaving practical benefit unclear.
- Manuscript exposition is poor: method development is interleaved with tangents and key notation/definitions are introduced after first use.
- Recommend reorganizing to present the core method first, followed by a dedicated discussion of alternatives and challenges.
- Approach is novel—no prior work appears to combine these specific technical elements for DPP learning.
- Work is potentially significant for subset selection and improving DPP-based learning frameworks.
- Major concern: critical terms undefined on first use (e.g., marginal kernel K, matrix V); clarify required kernel properties.
- Ambiguous wording: replace “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” with “selects the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
- Section 4 over-relies on reference [26]; authors should succinctly describe their simplified technique rather than forcing readers to consult external material.
- Ambiguous terminology: define “weight” where first used (p.4, l.189) and give context.
- Method justification unclear: explain omission of m_ij in Eq.(30) and why empirical covariance initialization fails versus the proposed initialization.
