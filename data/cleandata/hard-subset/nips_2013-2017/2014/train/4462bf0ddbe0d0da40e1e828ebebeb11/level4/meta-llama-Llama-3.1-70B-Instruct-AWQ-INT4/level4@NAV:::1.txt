This paper focuses on learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs), with its primary contribution being a reformulated objective that surpasses the conventional approach of differentiating the log probability with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent, referred to as K-Ascent (KA). The proposed method reformulates the problem within an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework by representing any DPP as a mixture of "elementary DPPs", each corresponding to a subset of elements. A variational distribution is established over these subsets, and the algorithm iterates between updating the distribution over the subsets, represented as a k-DPP, and taking single gradient steps in the M-step. The results demonstrate that the EM method outperforms the KA method, particularly when the latter is initialized naively, and to a lesser extent when initialized more intelligently.

I find the proposed approach intriguing, and the EM method appears to yield some improvement. Although the M-step is somewhat involved, and the required caveats to make it work are not entirely satisfactory, it is noteworthy that the authors have successfully implemented it. 

From an experimental perspective, I would have appreciated a more in-depth analysis of the impact of initialization on the EM algorithm. For instance, would initializing the EM algorithm with the result of KA lead to further improvement?

I am also perplexed by the use of relative log likelihood as a performance metric. Log probability differences reflect probability ratios, so it is unclear why the authors opted to take ratios of log probabilities instead of simply reporting differences in log probabilities. I find it challenging to interpret the meaning behind these numbers.

On a minor note, I find certain aspects of the writing style to be somewhat distracting. I would recommend that the authors refrain from describing their own algorithm as elegant, particularly given the caveats and approximations necessary in the M-step. Additionally, bolding the text in the introduction seems unnecessary. Overall, the paper presents an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs, with experiments that could be improved and improvements over the basic projected gradient algorithm that, while not substantial, contribute to a generally well-written paper.
