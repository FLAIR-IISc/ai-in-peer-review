- Problem: Learning full DPP kernels is hard (nonconvex, conjectured NP-hard); prior work used restrictive convex parametrizations or mixtures/Bayesian approaches.
- Contribution: Reparameterize the marginal kernel K by its eigendecomposition (Λ, V) and derive an EM algorithm optimizing a Jensen lower bound to learn the full kernel.
- Latent-variable view: Introduce hidden index set J of eigenvectors (standard DPP sampling view) to obtain a tractable lower bound.
- E-step: Set q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with a computable kernel—elegant and practically useful.
- M-step (Λ): Eigenvalues admit closed-form updates via expected inclusion probabilities computed efficiently using k-DPP marginalization.
- M-step (V): Eigenvectors updated by a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding naive PSD projection artifacts.
- Practical benefits: Avoids near-diagonal degeneracy from clipping eigenvalues and is asymptotically faster than projected gradient (KA) when observed set sizes k ≪ N.
- Complexity: The method is analyzed and shown to be faster in theory and practice for small k, but the manifold update’s line search can incur O(N^3) cost (matrix exponential).
- Empirical results: On Amazon baby registry data, EM improves held-out log-likelihood and runtime versus KA, especially when off-diagonal (negative) interactions are strong.
- Interpretation: Authors connect empirical gains to a simple statistic measuring pairwise interaction strength and provide a qualitative example where KA yields redundant recommendations.
- Technical soundness: Derivations are standard and well executed; identification of E-step posterior as a k-DPP is a key technical insight.
- Limitations: V-update is not optimized to convergence and monotone improvement of the lower bound is not guaranteed; improvement is checked on true likelihood requiring extra E-steps.
- Scalability concerns: O(N^3) matrix exponential in line search is costly; alternative retractions (e.g., Cayley transforms) could reduce expense.
- Baselines and evaluation: Comparisons are primarily against KA; missing baselines include per-item scaling, DPP mixtures, and Bayesian parametric methods; evaluation focuses on likelihood rather than task metrics.
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized with helpful pseudocode, though some notational density in the E-step could be lightened and computational costs summarized.
- Originality: Reparameterizing DPP learning as EM with a k-DPP posterior and using Stiefel-manifold updates is novel relative to prior NIPS work.
- Suggestions: Add broader baselines, discuss convergence behavior and typical E-/line-search counts, explore alternative Stiefel retractions, analyze sensitivity to initialization/λ cap, and report task-level metrics and synthetic recovery experiments.
- Overall assessment: Strong, timely, technically sound and novel contribution that enables practical full-kernel DPP learning; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
