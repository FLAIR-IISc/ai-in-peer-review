- Proposes an EM framework to learn DPP kernel matrices, differing from standard projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- EM represents a DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" (subsets) and uses a variational k-DPP for the E-step.  
- Algorithm alternates updating the k-DPP variational distribution and single-step gradient updates in the M-step.  
- Experiments show EM outperforms KA with naive initialization and yields smaller gains with smarter initialization.  
- M-step is relatively complex and relies on caveats/approximations that are somewhat unsatisfying.  
- Recommends additional analysis of initialization effects, e.g., initializing EM from KA solutions.  
- Questions the use of "relative log likelihood" (ratios of log probabilities) as a metric—differences in log-probabilities would be more interpretable.  
- Minor writing issues: avoid self-descriptive terms like “elegant” and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall: interesting and generally well-written contribution with modest empirical improvements and room for stronger experiments.
