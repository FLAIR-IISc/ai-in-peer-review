This paper addresses the problem of learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs). The core contribution is a novel reformulation of the learning objective, which demonstrates improved performance compared to the standard approach of differentiating the log-likelihood with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent – a method the authors term K-Ascent (KA). The proposed approach casts the problem within an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework, leveraging the fact that any DPP can be expressed as a mixture of “elementary DPPs,” one for each possible subset of elements. The algorithm alternates between updating a variational distribution over these subsets, modeled as a k-DPP, and performing a gradient step in the M-step. The results indicate that the EM method significantly outperforms KA when KA is initialized with naive parameters, and offers a modest improvement even with more informed KA initialization.

I find the approach to be interesting and the EM method appears to provide a tangible benefit. While the M-step is complex and requires certain caveats to function correctly, achieving a working solution is noteworthy. 

Regarding the experiments, further analysis of the EM algorithm’s sensitivity to initialization would be valuable. Specifically, investigating whether initializing EM with the solution obtained from KA leads to further improvements would be insightful. 

I am also puzzled by the reporting of results using relative log-likelihood. Given that log-probability differences correspond to ratios of probabilities, the use of ratios of log-probabilities is unclear; reporting differences in log-probabilities would likely be more interpretable.

As a minor point, I found some aspects of the writing style distracting. The authors should avoid self-praise, particularly characterizing their algorithm as “elegant” given the approximations and caveats inherent in the M-step. Additionally, the bolding of text in the introduction seems unnecessary. Overall, this is an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs. The experiments could be strengthened, and the performance gains over the baseline projected gradient algorithm are not substantial, but the paper is generally well-executed.
