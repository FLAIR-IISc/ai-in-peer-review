- Paper proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (KA).
- Core idea: represent a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs (one per subset) and introduce a variational distribution over subsets.
- Algorithm alternates E-step (update variational distribution as a k-DPP) and M-step (single gradient steps on kernel parameters).
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms KA under naive initialization and gives modest gains under careful KA initialization.
- M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats/approximations, which are somewhat unsatisfying despite practical success.
- Reviewer requests more analysis of initialization effects—e.g., whether EM improves a KA-found solution.
- Reviewer objects to the use of “relative log likelihood” (ratios of log probabilities) and suggests reporting differences in log probability for clarity.
- Minor style notes: authors should avoid self-describing the algorithm as “elegant” and remove unnecessary bolded text.
- Overall assessment: interesting and solid contribution, but experiments could be strengthened and gains are not uniformly dramatic.
