- Proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (KA).  
- Leverages representation of any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs" and introduces a variational distribution over subsets.  
- Algorithm alternates between updating the subset distribution (as a k-DPP) and taking single gradient steps in the M-step.  
- Empirical results: EM significantly outperforms KA with naive initialization and provides marginal gains over KA with intelligent initialization.  
- M-step complexity and required caveats/approximations feel unsatisfying.  
- Experiments should analyze initialization effects more deeply, e.g., initialize EM with KA’s solution and compare.  
- Questions the use of "relative log likelihood" as a metric; prefers differences in log probabilities for interpretability.  
- Minor stylistic critiques: avoid self-descriptions of elegance and unnecessary bolding.  
- Overall assessment: an interesting, solid contribution with tangible but modest experimental improvements.
