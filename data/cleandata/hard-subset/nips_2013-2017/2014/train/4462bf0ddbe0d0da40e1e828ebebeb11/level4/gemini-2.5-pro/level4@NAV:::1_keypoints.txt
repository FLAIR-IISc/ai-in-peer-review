- Proposes a novel EM-based reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (KA).  
- Frames a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs, introducing a variational distribution over subsets and alternating E-step (k-DPP update) and M-step (gradient updates).  
- Empirically, EM greatly outperforms a naively initialized KA; with stronger KA initialization, EM yields only modest improvements in some cases.  
- The M-step derivation is non-trivial and requires approximations/constraints, which detract from elegance though its implementation is a notable achievement.  
- Experiments should analyze EM sensitivity to initialization, especially by initializing EM from the converged KA solution to test further refinement.  
- The use of "relative log-likelihood" as an evaluation metric is unclear and nonstandard; authors should justify it or report standard log-likelihood differences.  
- Presentation should adopt a more objective tone (avoid self-praise like "elegant" and unnecessary bolding).  
- Overall assessment: a novel, sound EM-based contribution with tangible but sometimes limited empirical gains; paper quality is good.
