- Problem: Learning the full DPP kernel matrix is NP-hard and has been largely overlooked due to MLE non-convexity.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel EM-based algorithm that directly optimizes the full kernel matrix without restrictive parameterizations.
- Key idea: Reparameterize the kernel via eigendecomposition (eigenvalues/eigenvectors) and use Jensen’s inequality to form an EM lower bound.
- Technical advantage: EM avoids projection steps required by gradient ascent and leverages Stiefel manifold optimization for eigenvectors.
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous derivations and clear reduction of the optimization problem to an EM procedure.
- Empirical result: Up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood on a real-world product recommendation task.
- Empirical insight: K-Ascent often yields near-diagonal kernels that lose negative interactions; EM preserves diversity and captures negative item interactions.
- Experimental rigor: Comprehensive comparisons against a strong baseline across 13 Amazon baby registry categories.
- Analysis: Demonstrates relationship between off-diagonal kernel components (d metric) and when EM provides larger gains.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-organized step-by-step derivation, clear E/M steps, and implementation details (Stiefel optimization, k-DPPs) provided.
- Originality: Novel application of eigendecomposition to formulate an EM procedure for full kernel DPP learning, filling a gap in prior work.
- Significance: Meaningful improvement for diversity-preserving applications; results are valuable to practitioners.
- Strengths summary: Novel EM approach, theoretical insight into gradient failure, strong experiments, clear exposition, improved metrics.
- Weaknesses summary: Conclusion could better highlight contributions, runtime complexity analysis is thin, and more diverse application experiments would strengthen impact.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
