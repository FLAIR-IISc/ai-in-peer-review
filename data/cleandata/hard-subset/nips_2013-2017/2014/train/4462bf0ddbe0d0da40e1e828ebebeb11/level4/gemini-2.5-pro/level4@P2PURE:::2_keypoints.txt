- Proposes an EM algorithm for DPP parameter learning combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen’s-inequality log-likelihood lower bound.
- Empirically outperforms the simpler K-Ascent baseline but experiments are limited in scope.
- Lacks comparisons to alternative non-DPP subset-selection methods mentioned in the introduction, weakening claims of practical benefit.
- Technical approach is sound and the algorithm is well-founded.
- Method appears novel; no prior work known to address the problem in the same way.
- Represents a meaningful and valuable contribution to subset-selection literature.
- Paper clarity is poor: disorganized presentation, notation undefined, and terms used before introduction.
- Exposition is overly narrative and interleaves derivation with the final algorithm, obscuring the proposed method.
- Recommend presenting the algorithm up front and moving derivations, discussions, and alternative paths to a separate section.
- Specific issues: marginal kernel K is used before definition and its required properties are unclear.
- Specific issue: matrix V is not defined where used.
- Specific issue: phrase about “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is ambiguous—should state finding the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.
- Section on the complex method [26] is hard to follow; include a self-contained outline of the proposed approach.
- Term “weight” is used without a clear definition and should be specified.
- Initialization rationale (eq. 30) is unclear—why omit m_ij and why not use a simple empirical covariance-based initialization.
