- Proposes a novel EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices as an alternative to projected gradient K-Ascent.
- Method models a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs, introducing a variational distribution and alternating E-step (k-DPP) and M-step (single gradient update).
- Empirically, EM significantly outperforms naively initialized K-Ascent and gives modest improvements over well-initialized K-Ascent in some settings.
- Novel and creative formulation that offers an interesting alternative to direct gradient optimization.
- Demonstrates that the method can find better solutions than standard baselines when those baselines suffer from poor initialization.
- Technical M-step derivation is non-trivial and the authors present a workable, though complex, algorithm.
- M-step complexity and required approximations reduce practical elegance and may not justify the modest gains over strong baselines.
- Experimental analysis lacks thorough investigation of initialization effects, e.g., initializing EM with K-Ascent solutions to test refinement vs. escaping poor optima.
- The primary evaluation metric ("relative log likelihood") is confusing and should be justified or replaced with direct log-likelihood differences for clearer interpretation.
- Presentation issues: avoid subjective self-praise and excessive bolding; adopt a more objective tone and standard formatting.
- Overall: a promising, novel contribution that would be strengthened by expanded experiments, clearer metrics, and improved presentation.
