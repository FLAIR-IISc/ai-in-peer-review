- Introduces a novel EM algorithm for learning the full DPP kernel matrix, addressing a key limitation of prior work.  
- Prior methods restricted to row weights or fixed-kernel combinations; naive K-Ascent requires projections that produce degenerate, near-diagonal kernels.  
- Leverages eigendecomposition and a mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, using Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable lower bound that avoids projections.  
- Eigenvalue update is self-normalizing (no projection needed) and optimization uses Stiefel manifold updates for robustness.  
- Empirically achieves up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood on a product recommendation task, especially in high-diversity categories where K-Ascent fails.  
- Provides a thorough runtime analysis (O(T₁nNk² + T₁T₂N³)) that matches empirical behavior.  
- Evaluation is limited: compares only to K-Ascent and omits non-DPP subset selection baselines (e.g., greedy submodular maximization) and other non-DPP methods.  
- Writing and organization suffer from inconsistent notation and definitions introduced after use (e.g., K, V, "weight").  
- Exposition interleaves method and side discussions, reducing readability; Stiefel update is cited rather than clearly presented (Eq.21 should be emphasized).  
- Some imprecise phrasing (e.g., "minimises the change to Frobenius norm")—should state "closest PSD matrix in Frobenius norm."  
- Highly original contribution: first EM-based unconstrained DPP kernel learning and novel mixture-of-elementary-DPPs perspective.  
- Significant for enabling full-kernel DPP learning and modeling negative item interactions, but significance is partly undermined by limited baselines proving DPP necessity.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—add non-DPP baselines, clarify notation, simplify Stiefel manifold description, and justify moments-matching initialization.  
- Overall: technically sound and impactful core contribution (overcomes projection barrier); clarity and broader evaluation need improvement.
