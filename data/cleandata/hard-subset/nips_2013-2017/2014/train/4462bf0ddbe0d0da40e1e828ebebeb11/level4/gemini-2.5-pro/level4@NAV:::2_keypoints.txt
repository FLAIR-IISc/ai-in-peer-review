- Introduces an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that integrates kernel eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, mixture-of-elementary-DPPs characterization, and Jensen’s Inequality; outperforms the K-Ascent baseline.  
- Method appears theoretically sound and plausible, but empirical evaluation is narrow and lacks comparisons to other subset-selection methods.  
- Presentation clarity is poor: notation is often undefined and terms are used before formal definition, making the exposition hard to follow.  
- Exposition is convoluted by interleaving derivation, alternatives, and issues; recommend presenting the final method first and derivations later.  
- The approach appears novel; reviewer is not aware of similar prior work.  
- The methodology is significant and a valuable contribution to subset-selection literature.  
- p.2, l.81: Marginal kernel K is used before being defined; clarify qualifying properties.  
- p.2, l.100: Variable V is used without prior definition.  
- p.3, l.159: Phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is ambiguous; state it chooses the PSD matrix closest to Q in Frobenius norm.  
- p.4: Section is difficult to comprehend; provide a self-contained outline of the simpler approach rather than relying on [26].  
- p.4, l.189: The term “weight” is ambiguous in context and should be clarified.  
- p.7, l.324: Initialization in Eq.(30) is unexplained—justify omission of m_ij and why this is preferred over using the empirical covariance.  
- Overall: novel and technically complex work but undermined by unclear presentation and insufficient empirical validation against non-DPP baselines.
