The paper introduces an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). This method skillfully integrates several complex components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, a representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a lower bound on the log-likelihood derived using Jensen’s Inequality. The experimental results demonstrate favorable performance compared to a simpler K-Ascent scheme.

Quality: The method appears well-founded and the claims made are plausible. However, the evaluation could be strengthened. The experiments are limited in scope and lack comparison to alternative subset selection approaches (e.g., those referenced in the introduction), making it difficult to assess the advantages offered by the DPP framework. 

Clarity: The paper’s presentation could be significantly improved. The writing is somewhat disorganized and difficult to follow, with notation occasionally introduced after its first use, and definitions sometimes lacking. The text is also unnecessarily complex due to its process-oriented structure, interweaving descriptions of the final method with discussions of alternative approaches and encountered problems. A clearer presentation of the developed method, followed by a separate discussion of alternatives, would be preferable. 

Originality: The work appears highly original; I am not currently aware of similar research.

Significance: The proposed method represents a solid advancement and a valuable contribution to the literature on subset selection.

Details:
- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is used before being defined. Please clarify what constitutes a marginal kernel.
- p.2, line 100: V is not defined.
- p.3, line 159: "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is imprecise. It would be more accurate to state "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) positive semi-definite matrix to Q".
- p.4: This section is difficult to understand. Instead of referencing [26], a direct outline of your simpler approach would benefit the reader and avoid requiring them to consult the more complex original method.
- p.4, line 189: Please clarify what is meant by “weight”.
- p.7, line 324: The rationale behind equation (30) is unclear. Why is m_{ij} not utilized here? Furthermore, why would using the empirical covariance matrix directly as the kernel not have served as a viable initialization? 

In summary, this paper presents a novel and technically sophisticated method for learning DPPs for subset selection. While the approach appears original and promising, the clarity of the writing and the limited scope of the experimental evaluation should be addressed.
