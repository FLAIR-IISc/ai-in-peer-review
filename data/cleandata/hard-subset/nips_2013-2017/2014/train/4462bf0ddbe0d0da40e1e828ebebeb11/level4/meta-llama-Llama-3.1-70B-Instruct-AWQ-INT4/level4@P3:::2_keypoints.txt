- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, mixture representation, and Jensen bounds.  
- Method is well-founded and believable to work as claimed.  
- Evaluation is limited: experiments are few and do not compare against alternative subset-selection methods.  
- Results lack error bars and statistical significance analyses.  
- Writing and presentation are sloppy and sometimes hard to follow; notation is occasionally undefined or introduced after use.  
- Paper structure interleaves development narrative with side remarks, reducing clarity.  
- Paper appears original; reviewer is unaware of similar prior work.  
- Method is significant: advances subset selection literature and leverages DPPs’ computational benefits (normalization, marginalization, conditioning, sampling).  
- Suggested: provide a clearer definition and properties of the marginal kernel K.  
- Suggested: define V before it is first used (p.2, line 100).  
- Suggested: clarify that the projection step finds the closest PSD matrix to Q in Frobenius norm (p.3, line 159).  
- Suggested: explicitly outline the Stiefel manifold optimization approach rather than only citing [26].  
- Suggested: define the term “weight” where used (p.4, line 189).  
- Suggested: explain and justify the initialization method and why empirical covariance as kernel would fail (p.7, line 324).  
- Overall: valuable and solid contribution but needs improved clarity, broader and comparative evaluation, and more detailed result analysis.
