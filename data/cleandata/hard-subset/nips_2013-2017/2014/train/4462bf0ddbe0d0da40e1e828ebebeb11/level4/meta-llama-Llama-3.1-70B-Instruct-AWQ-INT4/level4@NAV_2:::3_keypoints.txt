- Problem: Learning the full DPP kernel L is conjectured NP-hard, so prior work restricted parameterizations or learned L partially.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel learning method that does not restrict L, preserving both quality and diversity of the DPP.
- Method: Derives an EM algorithm by exploiting the eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel, avoiding harmful projection steps.
- Technical novelty: Uses Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective and maps constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold.
- Theoretical result: Shows the inverse distribution is a DPP, noted as a solid theoretical contribution.
- Experiments: Evaluated on a product recommendation task and compared to a naive projected gradient ascent baseline (KA).
- Empirical findings: EM retains quality and diversity and outperforms KA in test log-likelihood.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear, well-explained proofs and step-by-step EM derivation.
- Weakness: Conclusion is not comprehensive enough and should better summarize all contributions.
- Weakness: Limited experimental validation—needs more real-world datasets to demonstrate the practical importance of diversity.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel effective method, well-derived EM, empirical effectiveness, and strong exposition.
- Arguments for rejection: Insufficiently comprehensive conclusion and limited experimental breadth.
- Overall: Solid contribution to DPP learning; acceptance justified if conclusion expanded and experiments strengthened.
