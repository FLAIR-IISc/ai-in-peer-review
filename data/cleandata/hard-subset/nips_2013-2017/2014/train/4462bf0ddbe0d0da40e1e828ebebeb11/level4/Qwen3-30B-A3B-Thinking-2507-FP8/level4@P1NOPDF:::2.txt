

The paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), combining several nontrivial components: eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood via Jensen's inequality. Experiments demonstrate favorable performance compared to a simpler "K-Ascent" baseline.

**Quality**: The method appears well-founded and plausible, but the experimental evaluation is limited. The authors do not compare against alternative subset selection approaches (e.g., those referenced in the introduction), making it unclear whether the DPP framework provides meaningful advantages over existing methods.

**Clarity**: The writing is often unclear and difficult to follow. Some notation is introduced without definition (e.g., marginal kernel \(K\) on p.2, line 81; matrix \(V\) on p.2, line 100), and definitions sometimes appear after usage. The narrative is unnecessarily convoluted, interleaving side discussions of alternatives and challenges with the main methodological development. A clearer presentation would state the final method concisely first, deferring discussion to later sections.

**Originality**: The approach appears novel; I am unaware of similar work addressing DPP learning via this combination of techniques.

**Significance**: The method represents a solid advance for subset selection, offering a useful contribution to the literature.

**Specific Comments**:
- p.2, line 81: Define "marginal kernel \(K\)" before use. Clarify which kernel types qualify as marginal.
- p.2, line 100: Define matrix \(V\).
- p.3, line 159: Rephrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" to "selects the closest positive semi-definite (PSD) matrix to \(Q\) in Frobenius norm."
- p.4: The section is hard to follow. Instead of referencing [26], briefly outline the simplified approach to avoid forcing readers to consult the original complex method.
- p.4, line 189: Clarify the meaning of "weight."
- p.7, line 324: The rationale for omitting \(m_{ij}\) in Equation (30) is unclear. Why not use the empirical covariance matrix as initialization? The paper claims a new learning method for DPPs, but the experimental scope is restricted to comparisons with other DPP methods only.
