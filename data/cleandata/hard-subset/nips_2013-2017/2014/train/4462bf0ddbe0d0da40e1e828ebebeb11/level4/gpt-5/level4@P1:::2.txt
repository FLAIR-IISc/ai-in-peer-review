Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) by optimizing over the eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel. The method combines several nontrivial components: representing a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs, introducing a latent variable over eigenvector subsets, lower-bounding the log-likelihood via Jensen’s inequality, and performing optimization on the Stiefel manifold for the eigenvectors. The approach contrasts with earlier work that learns only restricted parameterizations (e.g., per-row scalings or mixtures of fixed kernels), and instead aims to learn the full kernel. Experiments compare the method to a simpler projected gradient scheme on K (“K-Ascent”) and show favorable behavior.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The derivation appears well-founded. The EM formulation is plausible, the E-step is clean (q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y)), the eigenvalue updates are closed-form and self-normalizing, and the eigenvector updates respect the orthogonality constraint via a Stiefel-manifold step. Overall, the method is believable and likely to work as stated.
- Empirical evaluation: The experimental evaluation is limited. It primarily compares against K-Ascent and does not include alternative methods for subset selection (including those cited in the introduction). As a result, it is difficult to assess the practical advantage of the proposed DPP-based learning over other established approaches.

Clarity:
The paper is often difficult to follow. The write-up is somewhat sloppy, with notation not always defined before use and with some definitions appearing after the fact. The narrative is also unnecessarily convoluted: the presentation interleaves the final algorithm with detours about alternative directions and potential pitfalls, which obscures the main thread. A clearer structure would present the final method succinctly, then discuss alternatives and caveats afterward.

Originality:
To the best of my knowledge, the paper is original in proposing an EM algorithm that learns the full DPP kernel via eigenvalues and eigenvectors, rather than relying on restricted convex formulations.

Significance:
The contribution is meaningful. Learning full DPP kernels is an important problem for diversity-aware subset selection, and the proposed EM framework seems like a solid advance. If supported by broader comparisons, the method could be useful to practitioners.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Conceptually interesting and technically well-grounded EM approach for full-kernel DPP learning.
- Moves beyond restricted convex settings (e.g., scalar weights or fixed-kernel mixtures).
- Practical algorithmic insights (e.g., avoiding problematic PSD projections, Stiefel-manifold updates).

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited experimental evaluation and lack of comparisons to non-DPP subset selection baselines.
- Clarity issues in the exposition, with missing or delayed definitions and a meandering presentation.

Detailed comments and suggestions:
- p.2, line 81: The marginal kernel K is used before it is defined. Please define K as K = L(L + I)^{-1} (or your preferred equivalent) before first use, and clarify what “marginal kernel” means in this context (including the eigenvalue constraints).
- p.2, line 100: V is not defined before use. Introduce the eigendecomposition K = VΛV^> (or L = V diag(λ/(1−λ)) V^>) when first referencing V.
- p.3, line 159: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear. Prefer: “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”
- p.4: This section is hard to follow. Since you use a simplified Stiefel-manifold update, please outline your actual update directly (as you ultimately do), rather than primarily referring to [26]. A concise, self-contained derivation would help readers avoid chasing the general method in the reference.
- p.4, line 189: Clarify what “weight” refers to (mixture weight over eigenvector subsets J? If so, state this explicitly and connect to Equation (7) and the role of λj).
- p.7, line 324 and Equation (30): The rationale for the initialization is unclear. Why is m_{ij} not directly used? Why would using the empirical covariance (or a PSD-adjusted version thereof) as the kernel not suffice as an initialization? Please justify the specific choice K̃_{ij} = sqrt(max(K̃_{ii} K̃_{jj} − m_{ij}, 0)) and discuss alternatives, including directly projecting the empirical moment matrix to satisfy the DPP constraints.

Overall assessment:
The paper presents a technically solid and seemingly effective EM algorithm for learning full DPP kernels. However, the clarity of exposition needs improvement, and the experimental section should include broader baselines beyond K-Ascent to demonstrate the practical benefits of the approach. If these issues are addressed, the contribution would be a valuable addition to the subset selection and DPP literature.
