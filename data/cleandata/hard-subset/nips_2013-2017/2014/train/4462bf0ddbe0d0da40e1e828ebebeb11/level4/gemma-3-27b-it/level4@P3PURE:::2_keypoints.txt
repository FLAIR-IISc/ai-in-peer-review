- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, a mixture representation of DPPs, and a Jensen’s-inequality log-likelihood lower bound.  
- Experiments show favorable performance versus a simple “K-Ascent” baseline.  
- Evaluation is limited in scope and fails to compare against other subset-selection methods, leaving the practical benefit unclear.  
- Method appears well-founded and claims are believable.  
- Presentation is unclear and sloppy: notation is sometimes used before it is defined.  
- The exposition is convoluted and procedural; a clearer, direct presentation of the method followed by alternatives/challenges is recommended.  
- The paper appears original; reviewer is unaware of similar prior work.  
- The contribution is significant and a valuable advance for subset selection.  
- p.2, line 81: “marginal kernel K” is used before definition—clarify what qualifies a kernel as marginal.  
- p.2, line 100: Variable V is used before being defined.  
- p.3, line 159: “Minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is imprecise—should say “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q.”  
- p.4: Section is hard to understand; the authors should outline their simpler proposed approach instead of deferring to [26].  
- p.4, line 189: The meaning of “weight” is unclear and needs clarification.  
- p.7, line 324: Rationale for equation (30) is unclear—why is m_{ij} not used, and why not initialize using the empirical covariance matrix?
