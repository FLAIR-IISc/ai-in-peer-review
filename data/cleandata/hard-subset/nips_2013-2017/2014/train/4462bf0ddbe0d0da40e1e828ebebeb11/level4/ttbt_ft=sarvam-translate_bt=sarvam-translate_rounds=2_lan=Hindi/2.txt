This paper presents an EM algorithm for learning the Determinantal Point Process (DPP). The method combines several non-trivial elements: the eigenvalue decomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stephenie manifold, presenting the DPP as a mixture of primary DPPs, and reducing the log-probability by Jensen's inequality. Experiments demonstrate the proposed approach's favorable behavior compared to a simple "K-climb" scheme.

Quality: As far as I can tell, the method is well-established, and it is credible that the methods should work as described. However, the method's evaluation could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and do not compare alternative approaches for subset selection (such as those mentioned in the introduction), so it is unclear how much benefit we get from the DPP approach.

Clarity: The paper is not written particularly clearly. The writing is somewhat careless and difficult to understand. Not all notations are defined, and sometimes the definition comes after the use. The writing is also sometimes unnecessarily complex, mostly because the text is structured to describe a process that ends up with the proposed approach, mixing side-notes on potential alternative approaches and problems with the main formula. I would prefer to present the developed method as clearly as possible and postpone the discussion to a later stage.

Originality: The paper seems quite original to me; I am not aware of similar work.

Significance: I think the method obtained in the paper represents a solid progress and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- Page 2, line 81: The boundary kernel K is not defined before use. What kind of kernel qualifies as a boundary kernel?
- Page 2, line 100: V is not defined.
- Page 3, line 159: "reduces the change in the Frobenius norm" is vaguely stated. It would be better to say "chooses the nearest (to Q) PSD matrix in the Frobenius norm".
- Page 4. I found this section difficult to understand. Instead of mentioning [26], it would be good to directly outline your (simple) approach so that the reader is not forced to look at the more complex original method.
- Page 4, line 189: What is meant by "weight"?
- Page 7, line 324. I did not fully understand the argument in (30). Why is m_{ij} not used here? Why does using only empirical covariance matrices as kernels not work as an initialization? The paper presents a new method for learning the Determinantal Point Process for subset selection. The method is highly non-trivial, and I believe it is original. The writing is not clear enough. The experiments do not consider other DPP methods.
