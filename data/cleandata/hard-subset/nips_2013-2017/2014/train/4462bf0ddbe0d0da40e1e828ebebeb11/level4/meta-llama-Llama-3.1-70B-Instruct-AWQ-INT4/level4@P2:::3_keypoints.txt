- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel without restrictive parameterization, preserving quality and diversity.  
- Tackles non-convex log-likelihood by exploiting the kernel's eigendecomposition and introducing a hidden variable.  
- Derives an EM lower-bound via Jensen's inequality and optimizes it with a coordinate-ascent E/M procedure.  
- Expectation step sets the auxiliary distribution to the conditional DPP; maximization updates eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Introduces moment-matching initialization to provide a strong starting point for EM.  
- Maps the parameter constraint to an optimization over the Stiefel manifold.  
- Shows that the inverse distribution is itself a DPP (theoretical contribution).  
- Evaluated on a product recommendation task against K-Ascent (KA).  
- Empirical results: EM outperforms KA with an average test log-likelihood gain of 3.7% and a maximum of 16.5% in one category.  
- Experiments are well-designed and support the claims that the method retains DPP quality and diversity.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise proofs and a well-explained step-by-step EM reduction.  
- Strengths: novel algorithm, solid theoretical derivation, manifold mapping, and supportive experiments.  
- Weaknesses: conclusion lacks comprehensiveness and the paper would benefit from more real-world dataset experiments.  
- Significance: addresses an important ML problem with potential for wide adoption due to preserving DPP properties.
