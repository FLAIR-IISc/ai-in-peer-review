- Proposes an EM-based method for learning DPP kernels as an alternative to K-Ascent (KA).  
- Key idea: decompose any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and use an EM framework (E-step via k-DPP, M-step a gradient update on kernel).  
- Empirical gains: substantial improvement over naive KA initialization (5–10% in log-likelihood) and small gains over well-initialized KA (0.5–2%).  
- Conceptual contribution is interesting and well-motivated—reformulating DPP kernel learning within EM is valuable.  
- Technical execution depends on non-trivial approximations (e.g., k-DPP representation) and caveats for convergence that weaken the claimed elegance.  
- The M-step’s complexity and reliance on approximations lack sufficient justification and analysis of their impact on solution quality.  
- Experiments omit testing EM initialized from KA’s output; it’s unclear if EM is a distinct optimizer or just a better initialization strategy.  
- Evaluation metric is problematic: reporting “relative log likelihood” is misleading; authors should report absolute log-likelihoods or clearly interpreted differences.  
- Writing issues: subjective claims (e.g., “elegant”) and stylistic bolding are unprofessional and should be removed.  
- Overall assessment: promising and robust approach that handles naive initializations well, merits publication after revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: clarify M-step approximations and impacts, report absolute log-likelihoods, add experiments with EM initialized by KA, and remove subjective/stylistic flourishes.
