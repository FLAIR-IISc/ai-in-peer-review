- Tackles the hard, non-convex problem of learning the full unconstrained DPP kernel matrix rather than restricted convex settings.  
- Main contribution: a novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes the kernel via eigendecomposition and treats subsets of eigenvectors as latent variables.  
- E-step computes a posterior over latent eigenvector subsets, which is shown to be a k-DPP.  
- M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and updates eigenvectors via a single gradient step on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Avoids the projection-induced collapse to near-diagonal kernels that plagues naive gradient methods.  
- Empirically outperforms a projected gradient (K-Ascent) baseline: more robust to initialization, asymptotically faster, and higher test log-likelihoods, especially with naive initialization or limited data.  
- Strength: original and significant advance enabling non-parametric DPP learning applicable to new domains.  
- Strength: technically strong and insightful EM formulation that improves robustness and runtime.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and the algorithm derivation is clear and logical.  
- Weakness: M-step uses pragmatic approximations (single gradient step; line search requires E-step) that make the algorithm feel less like conventional EM and lack deeper justification.  
- Weakness: experiments could be deepened—e.g., initialize EM from K-Ascent’s solution to test whether EM escapes poor local optima or simply follows a better path.  
- Weakness: evaluation metric (“relative test log-likelihood differences”) is unclear and should be clarified or replaced by standard log-likelihood differences.  
- Minor issues: stylistic presentation choices (bold text, subjective descriptors) detract slightly from objectivity.  
- Overall: strong, practical contribution that advances DPP learning despite some M-step and evaluation clarifications needed.
