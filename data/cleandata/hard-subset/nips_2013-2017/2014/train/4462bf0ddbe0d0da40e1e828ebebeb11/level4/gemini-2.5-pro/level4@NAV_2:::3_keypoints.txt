- Problem: learning the full DPP kernel from data is non-convex and conjectured NP-hard; prior work focused on restricted, convex parameterizations.
- Core contribution: a novel EM algorithm that learns the unconstrained kernel by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition and treating eigenvector selection as a latent variable.
- Method: use Jensen’s inequality to derive a likelihood lower bound and an iterative EM procedure that avoids projection steps.
- Motivation vs baseline: avoids projection-induced failure modes (e.g., near-diagonal kernels that lose diversity) of naive projected gradient ascent.
- Technical strength: closed-form M-step updates for eigenvalues and optimization on the Stiefel manifold for eigenvectors.
- Technical result: the E-step posterior is itself a k-DPP, a useful and nontrivial property.
- Empirical claim: EM is more robust and asymptotically faster than the projected gradient ascent baseline (KA) on the tested tasks.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, coherent, and derivations are easy to follow.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons omit other relevant methods (e.g., L-ensemble) discussed in the introduction.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single real-world application (product recommendation) and should cover broader DPP uses (document summarization, image search).
- Minor presentation: conclusion is brief and should better summarize the paper’s contributions.
- Overall: strong, elegant, and well-justified technical contribution with solid empirical support.
