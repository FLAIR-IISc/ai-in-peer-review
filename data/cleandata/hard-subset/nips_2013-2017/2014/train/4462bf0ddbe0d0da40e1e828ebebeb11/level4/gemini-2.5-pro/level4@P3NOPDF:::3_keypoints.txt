- Proposes a novel, tractable EM method for learning the full, unrestricted DPP kernel L, overcoming prior structural constraints.  
- Enables modeling of both item quality and diversity directly from data, increasing expressive power over diagonal or constrained parameterizations.  
- Derives a clear lower bound on the log-likelihood using Jensen’s inequality and decouples eigenvalue and eigenvector learning.  
- Recasts eigenvector updates as optimization on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding costly/problematical projection steps.  
- Provides a theoretical proof that the inverse distribution in their formulation is also a DPP.  
- Paper is well-written with clear proofs and an accessible step-by-step development of the EM algorithm.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and a product recommendation task demonstrates improved handling of redundancy and diversity over a baseline.  
- Experiments are somewhat limited in scope; broader real-world evaluations (e.g., summarization, video retrieval, experimental design) would strengthen claims.  
- Conclusion is brief and should better summarize contributions and outline future research directions, including advantages of Stiefel-manifold optimization.
