- Introduces an EM-based reformulation for learning DPP kernels as an alternative to differentiating log probability and projected gradient ascent (KA).
- Leverages the representation of any DPP as a mixture of "elementary DPPs" and defines a variational distribution over subsets (implemented as a k-DPP) for the E-step.
- M-step uses single gradient steps to update kernel parameters; making this practical is a notable achievement but relies on complex caveats and approximations.
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms KA under naive initialization and yields modest gains with more informed initialization.
- Calls for a more thorough analysis of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM with KA's output) to better understand performance differences.
- Criticizes the use of "relative log likelihood" and taking ratios of log probabilities—argues differences in log probabilities would be more interpretable.
- Minor writing/style issues: describing the algorithm as "elegant" is debatable given the M-step caveats, and unnecessary bolding in the introduction is distracting.
- Overall assessment: an interesting novel approach with noteworthy practical contributions, but experiments could be stronger and improvements over basic projected gradient are limited.
