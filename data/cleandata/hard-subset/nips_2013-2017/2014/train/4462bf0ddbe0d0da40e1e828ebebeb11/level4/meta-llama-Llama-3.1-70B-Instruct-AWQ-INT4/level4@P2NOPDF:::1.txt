This paper proposes a novel approach to learning kernel matrices for determinantal point processes (DPPs) by reformulating the objective within an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework. The main contribution lies in deviating from the standard method of differentiating the log-probability with respect to kernel parameters and performing projected gradient ascent, referred to as K-Ascent (KA). The EM approach represents any DPP as a mixture over "elementary DPPs," each corresponding to a subset of elements. A variational distribution is introduced over these subsets, and the algorithm iterates between updating this distribution, represented as a k-DPP, and taking single gradient steps in the M-step.

The experimental results demonstrate that the EM method outperforms the KA method significantly when the latter is initialized naively, and to a lesser extent when the KA method is initialized more intelligently. Overall, the approach is interesting, and the EM method appears to yield some improvement. However, the M-step is relatively complex, and the necessary caveats to make it work are somewhat unsatisfying.

To further improve the paper, additional analysis on the effect of initialization on the EM algorithm would be beneficial. For instance, it would be interesting to investigate whether initializing the EM algorithm with the result of KA would lead to improved solutions.

Furthermore, the use of relative log likelihood as a performance metric is unclear. Log-probability differences reflect ratios of probabilities, so it is not evident why the authors choose to take ratios of log probabilities instead of simply using differences in log probabilities. This makes it challenging to interpret the reported numbers.

On a minor note, some of the writing style choices are distracting. The authors should avoid describing their own algorithm as "elegant," especially given the caveats and approximations required in the M-step. Additionally, bolding text in the introduction is unnecessary.

Despite these minor issues, the paper presents an interesting new approach for learning kernels in DPPs. While the experiments could be improved, and the improvements over the basic projected gradient algorithm are not substantial, the paper is generally well-written and contributes to the field.
