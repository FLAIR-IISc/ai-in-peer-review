- Paper proposes an EM reformulation for learning DPP kernel matrices as an alternative to projected gradient ascent (KA).
- Core idea: represent a DPP as a mixture of "elementary DPPs" (one per subset) and use a variational posterior over subsets in the E-step.
- E-step: update variational distribution to the posterior, which is itself a k-DPP; M-step: perform a single gradient step to update kernel parameters.
- Novelty: the EM formulation and the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs perspective are interesting and conceptually fresh.
- Empirical strength: EM significantly outperforms naive KA initialization and gives modest improvements even versus better-initialized KA in some cases.
- Missing experiment: no analysis of EM sensitivity to initialization or whether EM can further refine a solution after KA convergence.
- Metric clarity: use of "relative log-likelihood" (ratio of log-likelihoods) is non-standard and hard to interpret; recommend reporting log-likelihood differences instead.
- Practicality concern: the M-step requires non-trivial approximations and caveats, reducing the method’s practical elegance.
- Presentation nitpick: avoid subjective self-praise (e.g., "elegant") and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.
- Conclusion: interesting, promising contribution that could be strengthened with more thorough experiments and standard metrics; paper merits acceptance.
