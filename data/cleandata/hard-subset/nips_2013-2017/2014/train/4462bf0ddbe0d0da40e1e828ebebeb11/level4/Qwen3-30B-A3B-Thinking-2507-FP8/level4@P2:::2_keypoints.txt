- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel matrix, addressing a key limitation of prior work.  
- Prior methods only learned restricted parameterizations (scalar row weights or weighted combinations of fixed kernels).  
- Learning the full kernel is conjectured NP-hard; authors reparameterize via eigendecomposition and apply Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the likelihood.  
- EM framework avoids problematic projection steps required by naive gradient ascent methods.  
- Uses Stiefel manifold updates for optimization but provides insufficient explanation of the simplified approach.  
- Experiments on a product recommendation task show up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood over projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) and asymptotically faster runtime.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope, comparing only to K-Ascent and omitting other subset selection methods (e.g., submodular approaches).  
- Paper lacks analysis/experiments explaining why EM outperforms K-Ascent, particularly how learned kernels differ.  
- Presentation is convoluted, mixing the main method with alternatives and pitfalls, reducing readability.  
- Notational issues: marginal kernel K and V are used before definition; phrasing like “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear.  
- Stiefel manifold section cites [26] without a clear explanation of the implementation used.  
- Originality: the combination of eigendecomposition, EM, and Stiefel updates for full-kernel DPP learning is novel and technically significant.  
- Significance: method shows practical benefits for DPP learning, but broader impact on general subset selection is not demonstrated.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel contribution, but limited by unclear presentation and incomplete experimental comparisons.
