- Main contribution: reparameterizes DPP kernel learning and frames it in an EM framework that outperforms standard projected gradient ascent (KA).
- Key idea: represent any DPP as a mixture over “elementary DPPs” (one per subset) and place a variational distribution over subsets.
- Algorithm: alternate E-step (update subset distribution modeled as a k-DPP) and M-step (single gradient update).
- Empirical result: EM substantially outperforms KA with naive initialization and gives modest gains with careful KA initialization.
- Concern: M-step is intricate and requires caveats/approximations, making the claimed elegance debatable.
- Experimental suggestion: investigate initialization effects more thoroughly (e.g., start EM from the KA solution).
- Metric issue: questions about reporting relative log likelihoods — author should clarify why ratios of log probabilities are used instead of log-probability differences.
- Writing/style note: avoid self-descriptive terms like “elegant” and unnecessary boldface; some phrasing is distracting.
- Overall assessment: interesting and notable method, but experiments could be strengthened and gains over basic projected gradient are not dramatic.
