- Paper tackles learning kernel matrices for DPPs, proposing a reformulated objective that outperforms standard gradient-based kernel learning (K-Ascent, KA).  
- Main contribution: reframing the problem within an EM framework that yields improved performance.  
- Key idea: any DPP is a mixture over “elementary DPPs” (subsets); authors set a variational distribution over subsets and alternate E-step (update k-DPP) and M-step (single gradient steps).  
- Empirically, EM significantly outperforms KA when KA is naively initialized.  
- When KA is initialized more intelligently, EM’s advantage shrinks but remains present in some settings.  
- Reviewer finds the approach intriguing and the empirical success noteworthy despite M-step complexity.  
- Criticism: the M-step is complex and requires caveats/approximations that are somewhat unsatisfying.  
- Experimental suggestion: analyze initialization effects more deeply—e.g., initialize EM from KA’s solution to see if EM improves it.  
- Methodological concern: unclear rationale for reporting “relative log likelihood” as ratios of log probabilities rather than differences, making results hard to interpret.  
- Minor writing/style issues: avoid calling the algorithm “elegant” given caveats, and remove unnecessary bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall judgment: interesting new approach with room for improved experiments; gains over basic projected gradient are not huge but the paper is good.
