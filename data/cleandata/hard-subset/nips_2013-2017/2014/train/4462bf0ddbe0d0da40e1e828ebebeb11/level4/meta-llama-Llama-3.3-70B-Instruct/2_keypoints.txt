- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs combining eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen lower bound on the log-likelihood.
- Experimental results show favorable performance compared to the simpler K-Ascent baseline.
- Method appears well-founded and believable, but evaluation needs strengthening.
- Experiments are limited and do not compare to non-DPP subset-selection baselines, leaving DPP benefits unclear.
- Writing is unclear and sometimes sloppy; presentation is process-oriented with side remarks that impair readability.
- Notation issues: symbols are often undefined or introduced after use.
- Paper seems original—no related work known to the reviewer.
- Significance: presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to subset-selection literature.
- Page 2, line 81: marginal kernel K used without definition—specify what qualifies as a marginal kernel.
- Page 2, line 100: symbol V is undefined.
- Page 3, line 159: phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear; suggest rephrasing to "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q."
- Page 4: exposition is hard to follow; instead of citing [26], outline the simpler approach directly for reader clarity.
