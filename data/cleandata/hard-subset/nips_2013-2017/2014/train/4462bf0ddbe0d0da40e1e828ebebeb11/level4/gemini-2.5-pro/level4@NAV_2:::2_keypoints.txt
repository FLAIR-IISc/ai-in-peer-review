- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition and treating eigenvector selection as a hidden variable.  
- Avoids projection steps required by naive gradient ascent, using closed-form eigenvalue updates and an eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Empirical results on a product recommendation task show the EM method is more robust and computationally faster than projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- Highly original and technically sophisticated application of EM combined with Stiefel-manifold optimization.  
- Significant contribution: enables learning an unconstrained DPP kernel, broadening practical applications for modeling complex diversity.  
- Derivation appears technically sound and well-founded, coherently combining advanced mathematical concepts.  
- Clarity issues: presentation is sometimes convoluted, algorithm description is interleaved with alternatives, and some notation lacks upfront definition.  
- Limited experimental comparison: evaluation only contrasts against projected gradient ascent, not against other non-DPP diverse subset selection methods.  
- Suggest formalizing the projection baseline (state it as the nearest PSD matrix minimizing Frobenius norm).  
- Recommend outlining the Stiefel manifold update and explicitly stating simplifications from the referenced algorithm for self-containment.  
- Ask for more intuition behind the moments-matching initialization, particularly the off-diagonal entry form versus empirical covariance.  
- Minor clarifications requested: better motivation/definition of the “marginal kernel” K and explicit labeling of p_K(J) as a “mixture weight” in Equation (9).
