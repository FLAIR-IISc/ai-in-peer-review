- Addresses learning full-parameter DPPs (L matrix) where prior work restricted parameterizations and compromised diversity.
- Proposes a learning method that avoids parameter restrictions and the projection steps of projected gradient ascent that can distort similarity.
- Formulates an EM procedure over eigenvalues and eigenvectors to overcome projection-induced issues.
- Exploits full-rank eigenvector matrix V to avoid projecting eigenvectors, reducing constraint handling.
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to obtain a lower bound on the objective and derive EM updates.
- Maps the constraint to optimization over the Stiefel manifold and shows the inverse distribution is itself a DPP—both noted as solid contributions.
- Method is compelling because it preserves DPP diversity rather than focusing solely on quality.
- Step-by-step EM derivation and proofs are insightful and well written.
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product-recommendation task support claims that the method retains both quality and diversity, outperforming KA in aspects.
- Example result: in top-10 recommendations, KA replaces two items with less likely products, illustrating the benefit (modest but consistent).
- Paper is coherent and clearly written, but the conclusion is somewhat hasty and doesn’t fully foreground all contributions.
- More experiments on real-world datasets are needed to better quantify the practical importance of the achieved diversity versus competing methods.
