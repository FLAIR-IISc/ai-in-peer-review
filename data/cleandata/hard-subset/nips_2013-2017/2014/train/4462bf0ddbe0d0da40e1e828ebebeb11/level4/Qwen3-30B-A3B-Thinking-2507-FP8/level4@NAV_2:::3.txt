

The paper addresses the challenge of learning the full marginal kernel matrix $K$ for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), a critical gap in prior work where learning was restricted to scalar row weights [2] or linear combinations of fixed kernels [3]. While learning $K$ via log-likelihood maximization is conjectured NP-hard [1], previous approaches like projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent, KA) suffer from degenerate solutions due to projection steps that force $K$ toward near-diagonal matrices, undermining DPPs’ diversity modeling. The authors propose an EM-based method leveraging $K$’s eigendecomposition to avoid projections, using Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the likelihood and deriving closed-form updates for eigenvalues and eigenvectors via Stiefel manifold optimization. This approach preserves negative item interactions (critical for diversity) and achieves up to 16.5% higher test log-likelihood than KA on a real-world product recommendation task (13 baby registry categories with $N=71$ items, $n=8,585$ sub-registries), while being asymptotically faster ($O(nNk^2)$ vs. $O(nN^3)$).

**Strengths:**  
- **Quality & Originality:** The EM framework is technically sound, resolving KA’s projection-induced degeneracy (Section 3.1) and enabling full-kernel learning without restrictive assumptions (unlike [2, 3]). The derivation of the k-DPP E-step (Lemma 1) and Stiefel manifold optimization for eigenvectors (Section 3.5) are novel contributions that significantly advance DPP learning beyond NIPS 2011 [2] and 2013 [8] work.  
- **Significance:** Demonstrates practical impact with 16.5% relative gain in log-likelihood on a large-scale recommendation task, where KA fails to model diversity (e.g., selecting multiple redundant video monitors instead of diverse items, Figure 2a). The runtime advantage (2.1× faster on average, Figure 2b) further enhances applicability.  
- **Clarity:** The algorithmic development (Sections 3.2–3.5) is exceptionally well-structured, with clear transitions from hidden-variable modeling to EM steps. The connection between DPP mixture properties and the k-DPP E-step is elegantly explained.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- **Scope of Validation:** The experiments are limited to product recommendation; broader validation (e.g., document summarization or sensor placement) would strengthen claims about general significance.  
- **Initialization Sensitivity:** EM’s advantage over KA diminishes (0.4% gain) when using moment-matching initialization (Section 4.1), suggesting performance depends on initialization—this nuance is underemphasized.  
- **Conclusion Overstatement:** The conclusion claims EM “will similarly have a significant advantage” in all applications, but the paper lacks analysis of settings where KA with moment matching suffices (e.g., data-rich regimes, Figure 1b).  

**Pro/Con Acceptance Summary:**  
- *Pro:* First method for full-kernel DPP learning without projections, proven to preserve diversity, with strong theoretical grounding and empirical gains. Fits NIPS’ focus on probabilistic models for structured prediction (cf. NIPS 2011–2013 DPP papers).  
- *Con:* Limited experimental scope and initialization-dependent gains slightly weaken the generalizability claim.  

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to DPP learning with clear technical innovation and compelling results. Minor improvements to the conclusion and experimental scope would elevate it further. The work is of high quality, originality, and significance for the NIPS community.
