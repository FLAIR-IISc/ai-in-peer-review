- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by treating eigenvector selection as a latent variable via eigendecomposition.
- Uses Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the log-likelihood and derive an EM-style optimization that avoids problematic PSD projection.
- Claims asymptotic speedups over naive gradient/projection methods and better practical performance.
- Evaluated on a real-world product recommendation (baby registry) task and outperforms a projected gradient ascent baseline, especially with scarce data or poor initialization.
- Strength: Tackles a central, challenging problem (full DPP kernel learning) with an original and elegant latent-variable formulation.
- Strength: Derivation is technically sound, combining DPP-as-mixture, Jensen bounding, and optimization on the Stiefel manifold to enforce orthonormality.
- Strength: Empirical results demonstrate robustness, improved diversity modeling, and faster convergence compared to the baseline.
- Weakness: Paper presentation is unclear and disorganized; derivation and final objective are hard to follow due to a “journey” narrative and notation issues.
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited—compares only to a projected gradient (K-Ascent) baseline and omits non-DPP subset-selection baselines (e.g., submodular methods).
- Suggestion: Restructure to present the DPP objective and final EM algorithm first, then detail the derivation (E- and M-steps).
- Suggestion: Fix notation order (define L and K, and the full eigenvector matrix V before using them) to improve readability.
- Suggestion: Include a brief, self-contained description and intuition for the Stiefel-manifold optimization update rather than only citing [26].
- Suggestion: Provide more rationale for the moments-matching initialization (Eq. 23) and why it’s preferred over simpler choices like empirical covariance.
- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel contribution likely worthy of acceptance if clarity/organization and broader experimental comparisons are addressed.
