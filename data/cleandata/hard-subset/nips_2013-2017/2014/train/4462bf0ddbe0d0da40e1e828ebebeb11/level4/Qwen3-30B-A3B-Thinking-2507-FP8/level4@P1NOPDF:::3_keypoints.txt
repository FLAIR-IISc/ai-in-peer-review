- Introduces a novel DPP learning method that avoids restrictive parameterizations and projection steps.  
- Uses an EM algorithm over eigenvalues and eigenvectors to learn the full L matrix.  
- Eliminates eigenvector projection by leveraging the full-rank eigenvector matrix and mapping to the Stiefel manifold.  
- Employs Jensen’s inequality to derive a lower bound on the objective, enabling a clean EM procedure.  
- Theoretical proof shows the inverse mapping preserves DPP structure.  
- Method preserves diversity in learned DPPs, unlike prior scalar-parameter approaches that favor quality only.  
- Step-by-step EM derivation and the Stiefel manifold formulation are clear and insightful.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and a product recommendation task shows improved balance of quality and diversity and outperforms baseline KA.  
- KA baseline replaces only two top-10 recommendations, underscoring practical diversity gains of the proposed method.  
- Paper is well-written with clear proofs, but the conclusion omits highlighting key contributions like the Stiefel formulation and diversity guarantees.  
- Recommendation to perform additional experiments on broader real-world datasets to further validate practical significance.
