This paper presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs). This method is a combination of several important elements: the eigenvalue decomposition of the kernel, optimization on the Stiefel manifold, representing DPP as a mixture of primary DPPs, and the lower-bound on log-likelihood via Jensen's Inequality. Experiments show the proposed method has favorable behavior compared to a simple "K-Ascent" method.

Quality: To my knowledge, the method is well-established and credible that it will work as described. However, the method's evaluation could be improved. The experiments are quite limited and not compared with alternative methods for subset selection (as mentioned in the introduction), so it is unclear how much we will benefit from the DPP method.

Clarity: The paper is not written with particular clarity. The writing is somewhat disorganized and difficult to follow. Not all symbols are defined, and sometimes definitions come after the use of them. The writing is sometimes unnecessarily complex, mainly because the text is designed to describe a process of how someone eventually ends up with the proposed method, with potential alternative methods and side-comments on problems mixed with the main thread. I would have preferred to present the improved method as clearly as possible and postpone the discussion to a later stage.

Originality: I found the paper quite original; I am not aware of similar work.

Importance: I believe the method presented in the paper represents a significant advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Details:
- Page 2, line 81: The edge kernel K was not defined before being used. What kind of kernel qualifies as an edge kernel?
- Page 2, line 100: V was not defined.
- Page 3, line 159: "Minimizing the change of the Frobenius norm" is vaguely stated. "Selecting a PSD matrix close to Q (within the Frobenius norm)" would be better.
- Page 4. I found this section difficult to understand. Instead of mentioning [26], it would be better to directly present your (simple) method so that the reader is not forced to look for the more complex main method.
- Page 4, line 189: What does "weight" mean?
- Page 7, line 324. I don't quite understand the logic of (30). Why wasn't m_{ij} used here? Why didn't using only the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel work to start with? The paper presents a new method for learning Determinantal Point Processes for subset selection. The method is highly non-trivial, and I assume it is original. The writing is not very clear. The experiments do not consider anything other than the DPP method.
