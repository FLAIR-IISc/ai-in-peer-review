- Introduces an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, elementary DPP mixtures, and a Jensen’s Inequality log-likelihood lower bound.  
- Experiments show improvement over a basic K-Ascent baseline but lack breadth and comparisons to other subset selection methods.  
- Method appears theoretically sound and claims are plausible, but empirical evaluation is insufficient to fully assess benefits of DPP-based approach.  
- Presentation is disorganized and sometimes imprecise, hindering comprehension.  
- Notation issues: some symbols and definitions (e.g., marginal kernel K, variable V) are introduced without prior definition.  
- Narrative interleaves method derivation with alternatives/issues; a clearer, linear exposition of the final method followed by alternatives would improve readability.  
- Several phrases and equations are unclear or imprecise (e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm,” the meaning of “weight,” and rationale for equation (30)).  
- Reviewer requests a concise outline of the simplified approach referenced to [26] rather than forcing readers to consult the original complex method.  
- Questions why m_ij is not used in equation (30) and why empirical covariance initialization would be unsuitable.  
- Work is judged highly original with no known similar research.  
- Paper is considered a significant and useful contribution to subset selection literature if clarity and experimental evaluation are improved.
