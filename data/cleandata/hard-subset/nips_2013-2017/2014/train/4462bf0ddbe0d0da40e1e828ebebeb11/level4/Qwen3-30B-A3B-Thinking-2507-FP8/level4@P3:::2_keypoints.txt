- Introduces a novel EM algorithm that learns the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel K.
- EM formulation avoids problematic projection steps of prior projected gradient (K-Ascent) methods that produce degenerate near-diagonal kernels.
- Derives the algorithm by lower-bounding the log-likelihood (Jensen) and optimizing eigenvalues and eigenvectors; derivation is mathematically rigorous.
- Theoretical analysis shows the EM algorithm is asymptotically faster than gradient ascent.
- Empirical results on product recommendation show up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent and greater robustness to initialization/dataset changes.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: comparison only to K-Ascent and not to other subset selection or non-DPP baselines ([4–9]).
- Clarity problems: key notation (marginal kernel K, eigenvector matrix V) is introduced after first use, hindering readability.
- Presentation interleaves alternatives with the main method; recommend presenting the method first, then alternatives/limitations.
- The Stiefel manifold eigenvector update (Eq. 21) is cited but insufficiently explained—readers must consult [26]; a brief outline is needed.
- Some phrasing is unclear (e.g., "minimises the change to Frobenius norm"); suggest explicitly stating closest PSD in Frobenius norm.
- Initialization rationale (moment-matching, Eq. 23) is underexplained; question why not use empirical covariance as the kernel.
- Originality is high: addresses a fundamental gap in DPP learning by enabling full kernel learning without restrictive parameterizations.
- Significance is substantial for applications modeling negative interactions (product recommendation, summarization), but strengthened significance needs broader baseline comparisons.
- Pros: novel, technically sound, clear theoretical and empirical advantages. Cons: limited experiments, unclear writing/notation, and lack of non-DPP comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental comparisons to non-DPP subset selection methods.
