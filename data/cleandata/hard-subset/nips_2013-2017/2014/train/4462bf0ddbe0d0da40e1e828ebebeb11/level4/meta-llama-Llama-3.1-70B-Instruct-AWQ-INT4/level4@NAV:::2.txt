This paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), combining eigendecomposition of the kernel, optimisation on the Stiefel manifold, representation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and lower-bounding the log-likelihood using Jensen's Inequality. The experimental results demonstrate the proposed approach's favourable performance compared to the simpler "K-Ascent" scheme.

Quality: The method appears to be well-founded, and its effectiveness is believable. However, the evaluation could be improved. The experiments are limited and do not compare the proposed approach to alternative subset selection methods, making it unclear what benefits the DPP approach provides.

Clarity: The writing is not particularly clear, with a somewhat sloppy and hard-to-follow presentation. Notation is not consistently defined, and definitions sometimes appear after their use. The text's structure, which describes the process of developing the proposed approach, including side remarks on alternative approaches and problems, can be convoluted. A clearer presentation of the developed method, with discussion postponed to a later stage, would be preferable.

Originality: The paper appears to be original, as I am not aware of similar work in this area.

Significance: The proposed method presents a solid advance and a useful contribution to the subset selection literature.

Some specific details require attention:

- On page 2, line 81, the marginal kernel K is not defined before use. It would be helpful to clarify what type of kernel qualifies as a marginal kernel.
- On page 2, line 100, V is not defined.
- On page 3, line 159, the phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" is unclear. It would be better to state that the approach "chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q".
- The section on page 4 is difficult to understand. Instead of referring to [26], it would be helpful to outline the simpler approach directly, allowing the reader to understand the method without looking up the original, more complex method.
- On page 4, line 189, the term "weight" is not clearly defined.
- On page 7, line 324, the rationale behind equation (30) is not entirely clear. It would be helpful to explain why m_{ij} is not used and why simply using the empirical covariance matrix as the kernel would not have worked as an initialisation.
