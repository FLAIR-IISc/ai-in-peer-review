- Introduces an EM algorithm for learning DPPs that combines eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation, and a Jensen lower bound on the log-likelihood.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms a simpler “K-Ascent” scheme.  
- Method appears well-founded and plausibly works as claimed.  
- Evaluation is weak: experiments are limited and lack comparisons to other subset-selection baselines cited in the introduction.  
- Paper clarity is poor: write-up is sloppy, hard to follow, and notation is sometimes undefined until later.  
- Presentation is convoluted, narrating development and alternatives rather than clearly stating the final method first.  
- Originality is high; reviewer is not aware of similar prior work.  
- Significance is solid; represents a useful advance for subset selection.  
- Specific issues: marginal kernel K used before definition (p.2, l.81).  
- Specific issues: V is not defined when first used (p.2, l.100).  
- Specific issues: phrase “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear; suggest “chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q” (p.3, l.159).  
- Specific issues: Section p.4 is hard to follow; should outline the simpler approach rather than defer to [26].  
- Specific issues: unclear what “weight” refers to (p.4, l.189).  
- Specific issues: rationale for equation (30) is unclear—why is m_{ij} not used and why not initialize with empirical covariance (p.7, l.324).  
- Overall: highly nontrivial and original method, but unclear write-up and insufficient experimental comparisons beyond DPP-based methods.
