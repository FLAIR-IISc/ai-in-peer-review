- Core contribution: Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPP kernels by leveraging eigendecomposition and representing DPPs as mixtures of “elementary DPPs” with a hidden subset variable.
- Method detail: E-step uses a variational k-DPP distribution and M-step updates kernel parameters, with approximations to keep the M-step tractable.
- Experimental result: EM consistently outperforms projected gradient (K-Ascent), especially under poor initialization, though gains are not always large.
- Convergence: Algorithm converges and improves over existing methods despite M-step complexity and approximations.
- Relation to prior work: Clearly connects to and improves on prior DPP learning approaches (single-row weights, ensembles of fixed kernels) and cites relevant optimization literature.
- Suggested analysis: More comprehensive study of initialization strategies, including initializing EM from KA outputs, is needed.
- Reporting critique: Relative log-likelihood gains are unclear; absolute log-likelihood differences would be more interpretable.
- Writing critique: Remove subjective praise (e.g., “elegance”) and unnecessary bold formatting to improve objectivity and clarity.
- Strength — novelty: Provides a new, well-motivated perspective on DPP kernel learning via EM.
- Strength — empirical: Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over K-Ascent.
- Strength — theory: Method is theoretically grounded by DPP properties and eigendecomposition.
- Weakness — complexity: M-step relies on approximations and caveats, reducing theoretical cleanliness and possibly applicability.
- Weakness — modest gains: Performance improvements, while consistent, are often modest.
- Weakness — presentation: Reporting metric choice and some stylistic issues detract from clarity.
- Recommendation: Overall positive — recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address reporting and stylistic concerns.
