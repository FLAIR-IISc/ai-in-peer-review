- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn full DPP kernel K via spectral reparameterization (eigenvalues/eigenvectors) with a latent active-eigenvector set J.  
- E-step sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel enabling efficient marginals.  
- M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and a gradient update on eigenvectors constrained to the Stiefel manifold with line-search on true likelihood.  
- Method avoids problematic PSD/eigenvalue projection used in naive projected gradient ascent (KA).  
- Experiments on Amazon baby registry data show up to 16.5% relative test log-likelihood gain and ~2× runtime speedup, especially where negative interactions are strong.  
- Situates work relative to prior restrictive convex/parametric DPP learning and leverages spectral and mixture-of-elementary-DPP interpretations.  
- Eigen-based EM appears novel for full-kernel DPP learning and distinct from prior quality-only or low-rank methods.  
- Technical derivations are generally sound; E-step k-DPP result underpins efficient computation if supplement proofs hold.  
- Eigenvector update uses first-order gradient valid at q=p and Stiefel manifold updates; authors verify likelihood improvement despite deviating from textbook EM monotonicity.  
- Complexity: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs KA’s O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3), favorable when k ≪ N.  
- Experimental evaluation is careful: two initializations, multiple trials, correlation analysis, data-poor tests, runtime comparisons, and illustrative MAP example.  
- Paper is well written with clear background and EM derivation; some steps rely on supplemental proofs and minor notation clarifications are needed.  
- Contribution is original: combining k-DPP E-step and Stiefel manifold M-step for unconstrained full-kernel learning is elegant and novel.  
- Significance: advances practical scalable full DPP kernel learning without restrictive assumptions, relevant to recommendations, summarization, sensor placement.  
- Pros: conceptually clean EM, robustness to initialization, efficient eigenvalue updates, principled eigenvector updates, empirical likelihood/runtime gains, reproducible code/data.  
- Cons: no guaranteed monotonicity due to approximate M-step and re-evaluated q; theory could be strengthened.  
- Cons: evaluation limited to one real dataset and only KA baseline; lacks comparisons to other learning methods on same task.  
- Cons: no regularization/structural constraints—risk of overfitting in low-data regimes and limited study of generalization beyond likelihood.  
- Cons: scalability concerns for larger N due to repeated N×N matrix exponentials; low-rank/sparse extensions not discussed.  
- Cons: some numerical issues (λ near 1) are deferred; stability safeguards need elaboration.  
- Suggestions: provide theoretical discussion of convergence/monotonicity or generalized EM justification for the approximate M-step.  
- Suggestions: add additional baselines (e.g., per-item convex [2], mixture [3]) to contextualize gains.  
- Suggestions: explore simple regularizers or low-rank constraints to mitigate overfitting and improve scalability.  
- Suggestions: report sensitivity to initialization, step-size, λ cap, and clarify numerical stability measures.  
- Suggestions: clarify Eq. (3) derivation/reference, expand runtime details for matrix exponentials and possible approximations, and add synthetic recovery experiments.  
- Minor: fix typo “negelecting” → “neglecting” and clarify notation/definitions (HYi, BYi, V dimensions).  
- Recommendation: Accept—novel, well-executed approach with clear empirical benefits and likely impact.
