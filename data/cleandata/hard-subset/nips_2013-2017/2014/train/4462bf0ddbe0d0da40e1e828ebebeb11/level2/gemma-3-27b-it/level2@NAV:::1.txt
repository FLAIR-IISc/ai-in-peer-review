## Review of "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of learning the full kernel matrix for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), which is conjectured to be NP-hard. Previous work has focused on restricted learning settings due to this difficulty. The authors propose a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm by reparameterizing the kernel in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors and lower-bounding the likelihood. This is a significant contribution as it circumvents the need for problematic projection steps required by naive gradient ascent.

The paper is well-structured, clearly explaining the background of DPPs and the limitations of existing approaches. The core idea of utilizing the eigendecomposition and formulating an EM algorithm is well-motivated and technically sound. The derivation of the eigenvalue and eigenvector updates appears correct, and the explanation of the computational complexities is appreciated. The authors appropriately acknowledge the challenges of the M-step and describe reasonable approximations. 

The experimental evaluation on a product recommendation task demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method. The reported gains (up to 16.5% in test log-likelihood) compared to projected gradient ascent are compelling. The analysis relating the performance gains to the data characteristics (through the 'd' metric) is insightful. Including a comparison to other DPP learning methods (e.g., Bayesian approaches mentioned in the introduction) would further strengthen the evaluation.

One potential weakness is the limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity within the EM algorithm. The paper doesn't delve into the impact of different step sizes or initialization strategies beyond the two tested. Additionally, while the authors mention the possibility of using the Stiefel manifold update for eigenvectors, they simplify it significantly, and the implications of this simplification aren’t fully discussed.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a fundamental challenge in DPP learning.
*   Presents a novel and theoretically sound EM algorithm.
*   Demonstrates improved performance over a strong baseline.
*   Clear writing and thorough explanation of the methodology.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited hyperparameter analysis.
*   Simplification of eigenvector update without detailed justification.
*   Comparison to a broader range of existing DPP learning methods would be beneficial.



**Overall:** Despite these minor shortcomings, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of DPP learning. The proposed EM algorithm offers a practical and effective approach for learning the full kernel matrix, opening up new possibilities for applying DPPs to a wider range of problems. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
