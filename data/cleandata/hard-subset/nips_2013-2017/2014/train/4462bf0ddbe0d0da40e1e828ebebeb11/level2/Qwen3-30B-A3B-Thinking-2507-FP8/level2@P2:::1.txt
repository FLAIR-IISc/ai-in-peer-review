

This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning the full kernel matrix of Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing a key limitation in previous work that only considered restricted learning settings (e.g., learning single weights per row or linear combinations of fixed DPPs). The authors change the parameterization from matrix entries to eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and develop an EM-style optimization procedure that avoids the problematic projection steps required in gradient ascent approaches.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound algorithm with clear theoretical derivations. The EM approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the optimization procedure, including computational complexity. The empirical results on a product recommendation task demonstrate consistent improvements over the projected gradient ascent baseline (KA), with up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood. However, the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with existing DPP learning methods. Specifically, it fails to adequately address Affandi et al.'s (2013) work on learning DPP kernels, which is directly relevant to this problem. The claim that full kernel learning is NP-hard (citing [1], Conjecture 4.1) is not sufficiently justified or contextualized within the literature. Additionally, the paper does not provide sufficient evidence that the EM algorithm avoids the local optima issues that plague gradient ascent, beyond the single example in Figure 2a.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The background on DPPs and their properties is excellent, and the EM algorithm is presented in a logical sequence. The experimental setup is well-described, though some details about the data preprocessing (e.g., how categories were filtered) could be clarified. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-explained. The runtime analysis is particularly clear and well-motivated.

**Originality:** The EM-based approach to learning the full DPP kernel matrix represents a meaningful contribution to the literature. The authors successfully exploit the eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix to develop a novel optimization procedure. However, the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from prior work, particularly Affandi et al. (2013), who also proposed learning methods for DPPs. The authors cite [3] (Kulesza and Taskar, 2012) but fail to adequately contrast their method with this more relevant prior work. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a more thorough literature review that positions their contribution within the context of existing DPP learning approaches.

**Significance:** The results demonstrate that the EM approach is both more robust and asymptotically faster than the gradient ascent baseline, which is significant for practical applications of DPPs. The product recommendation task is a compelling application domain, and the improvements in log-likelihood suggest the method could be valuable for real-world applications. The paper makes a clear contribution to the DPP learning literature, but the significance would be enhanced by demonstrating the approach's effectiveness in other application domains beyond product recommendation.

**Overall:** This is a solid contribution to the DPP learning literature with a well-motivated algorithm and promising empirical results. The paper's main weakness is insufficient differentiation from prior work, particularly the Affandi et al. (2013) paper which addresses a very similar problem. The authors should revise the paper to more thoroughly compare with this and other relevant work.

**Strengths:**
- Well-motivated EM approach that avoids problematic projection steps
- Clear theoretical derivations and computational complexity analysis
- Consistent empirical improvements over baseline (up to 16.5% gain)
- Demonstrated runtime advantage (2.1x faster on average)

**Weaknesses:**
- Insufficient differentiation from prior work on DPP learning (particularly Affandi et al., 2013)
- Claims about NP-hardness of full kernel learning are not adequately justified
- Limited comparison with other DPP learning methods beyond the KA baseline
- Some details about the experimental setup could be clarified

**Recommendation:** Accept. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the DPP learning literature with a technically sound approach and compelling empirical results. With minor revisions to address the literature comparison and clarify the NP-hardness claim, this paper would be an excellent addition to NIPS. The authors should explicitly compare their approach with Affandi et al. (2013) and provide a more nuanced discussion of the NP-hardness claim.
