- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by re-parameterizing via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and applying Jensen’s inequality to form a tractable lower bound.
- Addresses the NP-hardness of full-kernel DPP learning and extends beyond prior restricted-learning settings.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and situates the work effectively within existing literature and applications.
- The EM derivation appears sound and accessible; computational complexity analysis is included.
- Experiments on a real-world product recommendation task report up to 16.5% relative gain in log-likelihood versus projected gradient ascent.
- Empirical analysis examines initialization effects (Wishart vs. moment-matching), dataset characteristic 'd' influence, and provides runtime comparisons.
- Evaluation is primarily log-likelihood-focused; broader metrics (e.g., subset diversity, qualitative recommendation differences) are lacking.
- A deeper analysis of the learned kernels is needed to clarify what types of diversity the EM algorithm captures.
- Explanation of the Stiefel manifold update (Equation 21) is somewhat hand-wavy; neglecting the Hessian needs stronger justification.
- Reproducibility could be challenging due to implementation complexity and compute requirements; authors’ provision of code is important.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of approach, significance for DPP applications, theoretical soundness, and strong empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparison metrics, potential implementation difficulty, and minor clarity issues.
- Overall: Strong paper recommended for acceptance conditional on addressing minor points (clarify Stiefel update, broaden evaluation, provide well-documented code).
