- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs by reparameterizing the kernel via eigenvalues and eigenvectors to handle non-convex likelihood.
- Claims EM outperforms naive projected-gradient on kernel entries.
- Claims EM is more robust to initialization and dataset changes.
- Claims EM is asymptotically faster than the naive approach.
- Experiments (product recommendation) show up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood for EM.
- Empirical evidence that the naive method often gets stuck in poor local optima while EM is more robust.
- Provides theoretical/computational complexity analysis arguing EM is asymptotically faster.
- Method is practically useful for diversity modeling (recommendation, summarization) and is easy to implement as a drop-in.
- Authors show solid field knowledge and cite relevant literature.
- EM algorithm is a novel contribution addressing non-convex optimization in DPP learning.
- Paper includes pseudocode and experimental details sufficient for reproducibility.
- Limitation: EM may not reach the global optimum; performance depends on initialization and step size.
- Criticism: Eigendecomposition step may not scale to very large datasets; approximations or alternative methods should be explored.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, effective algorithm with strong empirical results and good reproducibility.
- Cons for acceptance: scalability concerns and need for more detailed complexity analysis and alternative optimization strategies.
