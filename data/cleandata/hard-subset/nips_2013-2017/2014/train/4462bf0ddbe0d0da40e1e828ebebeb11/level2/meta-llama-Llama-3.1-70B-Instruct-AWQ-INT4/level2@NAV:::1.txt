**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs) from data. DPPs are probabilistic models that can capture the diversity of items in a set, making them useful for applications such as product recommendation and document summarization. The learning problem is challenging because the log-likelihood function is non-convex in the kernel matrix. The authors propose an expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm that changes the kernel parameterization from matrix entries to eigenvalues and eigenvectors, allowing for a more effective optimization procedure.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed EM algorithm is more effective than the naive approach of maximizing likelihood by projected gradient ascent on the entries of the kernel matrix.
2. The EM algorithm is more robust to initialization and dataset changes.
3. The EM algorithm is asymptotically faster than the naive approach.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide several lines of evidence to support their claims:

1. Experimental results on a product recommendation task show that the EM algorithm achieves higher test log-likelihoods than the naive approach, with relative gains of up to 16.5%.
2. The EM algorithm is shown to be more robust to initialization and dataset changes, with the naive approach often getting stuck in poor local optima.
3. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the computational complexity of the EM algorithm, showing that it is asymptotically faster than the naive approach.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful for several reasons:

1. DPPs are a useful model for capturing the diversity of items in a set, making them applicable to a wide range of applications.
2. The EM algorithm provides a more effective and robust way of learning DPPs from data, which can lead to better performance in these applications.
3. The algorithm is relatively easy to implement and can be used as a drop-in replacement for other kernel-based methods.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field of machine learning and probabilistic modeling. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature and provide proper citations.

**Novelty**

The paper presents a significant improvement over existing approaches to learning DPPs. The EM algorithm is a novel contribution that addresses the challenges of non-convex optimization in the kernel matrix.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including pseudocode for the EM algorithm and a description of the experimental setup.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge that the EM algorithm may not always converge to the global optimum, and that the choice of initialization and step size can affect the performance of the algorithm.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential criticism of the paper is that the EM algorithm may not be suitable for very large datasets, due to the computational complexity of the eigendecomposition step. The authors could consider exploring alternative optimization methods or approximations to address this limitation.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and effective algorithm for learning DPPs.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the EM algorithm over the naive approach.
* The paper is well-written and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The EM algorithm may not be suitable for very large datasets.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm.
* The authors could consider exploring alternative optimization methods or approximations to address the limitations of the EM algorithm.
