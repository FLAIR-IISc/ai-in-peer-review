- Proposes an EM algorithm that learns the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing in eigenvalues and eigenvectors, overcoming prior restricted parameterizations.  
- Key insight: eigendecomposition-based EM avoids the projection step and degenerate PSD solutions that plague naive gradient ascent.  
- Strong technical contribution with clear derivation of E-step and M-step updates and methods to handle exponential-size sums.  
- Runtime analysis: EM has complexity O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), improving on gradient ascent’s O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3).  
- Empirical evaluation on Amazon baby registry data shows average +3.7% test log-likelihood over K-Ascent (up to +16.5% in safety).  
- Reported speedup: EM is on average 2.1× faster than K-Ascent across categories.  
- Improvement magnitude correlates with negative-interaction metric d = (1/N)||M||F/||diag(M)||^2, matching intuition about when full-kernel learning helps.  
- Strengths: technical rigor, clear mathematical presentation, careful derivations, and well-designed experiments with appropriate baselines.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to related work, notably Bayesian DPP learning [8] and recent kernel-parameter DPP methods [11,12].  
- Weakness: limited analysis of method limitations and sensitivity to initializations beyond the noted diagonal-kernel failure.  
- Weakness: lacking practical guidance on choosing the critical step size η for the EM algorithm.  
- Clarity is generally good; mathematical details are accessible to experts and experiments are clearly presented.  
- Significance: addresses an important open problem in DPP learning and enables modeling of negative interactions in real applications.  
- Overall: novel, technically sound, and practically useful contribution that meets criteria for a strong conference submission.
