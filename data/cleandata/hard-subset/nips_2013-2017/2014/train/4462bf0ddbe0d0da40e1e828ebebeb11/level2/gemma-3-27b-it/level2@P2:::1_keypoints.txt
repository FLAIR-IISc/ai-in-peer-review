- Addresses learning full DPP kernels via an EM algorithm using eigenvalue/eigenvector reparameterization to handle non-convex likelihoods.
- Novel EM formulation leverages DPP eigendecomposition and lower-bounding of the likelihood with Jensen’s inequality.
- Demonstrates improved performance on a product recommendation task, e.g., ~16.5% gain over projected gradient ascent in some settings.
- Correctly situates work in prior literature, noting NP-hardness of full kernel learning and limits of prior restricted approaches.
- Technical derivations appear sound and the EM justification is clear; practical simplifications for the non-convex eigenvector M-step are provided.
- Experiments are well-designed and show consistent improvement, although per-category gains vary.
- Insightful analysis of projection issues in K-Ascent and limitations of the projection step.
- Clarity is high: well-written, organized, with clear algorithms, though some equations (e.g., role of R in Eq.17) need more intuition.
- Original contribution: applying EM with eigendecomposition to full DPP kernel learning is novel and not just a recombination of existing techniques.
- Significance: method could enable wider, more robust use of DPPs in applications like recommendation, summarization, and image search.
- Weakness: complexity/runtime analysis is insufficiently detailed, especially w.r.t. dataset size and large N.
- Weakness: performance gains are not uniformly large across all cases.
- Weakness: discussion of M-step eigenvector update limitations could be expanded and improved.
- Suggestions: provide detailed complexity analysis, elaborate on M-step limitations and fixes, explore alternative initializations, include error bars, and discuss scalability/extensions.
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, technically sound, practically useful work with some minor areas for improvement.
