- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigenvalues and eigenvectors, avoiding projection steps in gradient ascent.  
- EM algorithm is technically sound with clear theoretical derivations and detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Empirical results on product recommendation show consistent improvements over projected gradient ascent (KA), up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood and 2.1x speedup on average.  
- The paper fails to adequately compare or differentiate from prior DPP learning work, especially Affandi et al. (2013) and related methods (Kulesza & Taskar 2012).  
- The NP-hardness claim for full kernel learning (citing Conjecture 4.1) is not sufficiently justified or contextualized in the literature.  
- Limited evidence that the EM algorithm reliably avoids local optima beyond a single illustrative example (Figure 2a).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; background on DPPs and notation are excellent.  
- Runtime analysis and presentation of the optimization procedure are particularly clear and well-motivated.  
- Experimental setup is generally well-described but some data preprocessing details (e.g., category filtering) need clarification.  
- Originality: EM-based eigendecomposition approach is a meaningful contribution but not clearly distinguished from closely related prior work.  
- Significance: Demonstrates robustness and practical speedup, but broader evaluation across other application domains would strengthen impact.  
- Strengths: avoids projection steps, solid theory, consistent empirical gains, and demonstrated runtime advantage.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient literature differentiation, weak NP-hardness justification, limited baselines beyond KA, and some experimental detail gaps.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand literature comparison (especially Affandi et al. 2013) and clarify the NP-hardness claim.
