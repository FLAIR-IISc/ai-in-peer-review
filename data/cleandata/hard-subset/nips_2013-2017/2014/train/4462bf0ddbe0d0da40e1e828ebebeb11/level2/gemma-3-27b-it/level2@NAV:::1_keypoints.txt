- Tackles the challenging problem of learning the full DPP kernel matrix, a task conjectured to be NP-hard.  
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm by reparameterizing the kernel via eigendecomposition (eigenvalues and eigenvectors).  
- EM approach lower-bounds the likelihood and circumvents problematic projection steps required by naive gradient ascent.  
- Core idea of using eigendecomposition and formulating EM is well-motivated and technically sound.  
- Derivations of eigenvalue and eigenvector updates appear correct.  
- Computational complexity of the method is explained and appreciated.  
- Authors acknowledge M-step difficulties and propose reasonable approximations.  
- Experimental evaluation on a product recommendation task shows up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood over projected gradient ascent.  
- Analysis linking performance gains to data characteristics via the 'd' metric is insightful.  
- Would benefit from comparison to a broader range of DPP learning methods (e.g., Bayesian approaches).  
- Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity (step sizes, initializations) beyond two tested strategies.  
- Eigenvector update is significantly simplified (Stiefel manifold update mentioned but not used) and implications aren’t fully discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental challenge, novel/theoretically sound method, improved empirical performance, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited hyperparameter analysis, unjustified simplification of eigenvector update, limited baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address raised points.
