## Review of “Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes”

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning the full kernel matrix of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP), a task conjectured to be NP-hard. Previous work has largely focused on restricted learning settings. The authors propose a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm, cleverly re-parameterizing the kernel in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors and leveraging a lower bound on the likelihood. 

The core idea of switching to an eigendecomposition and using an EM approach is a significant contribution. The authors convincingly argue why naive gradient ascent fails (due to projection issues leading to degenerate solutions) and demonstrate how their method avoids this pitfall. The theoretical justification, including the derivation of the EM update rules, appears sound and is well-presented. The experimental evaluation on a product recommendation task (baby registries) provides compelling evidence of the algorithm’s effectiveness, showing gains of up to 16.5% in test log-likelihood compared to projected gradient ascent. The analysis of the runtime also supports the claim of asymptotic speedup.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison to existing DPP learning methods beyond the basic gradient ascent. While the authors acknowledge and address limitations of prior work (e.g., [2, 3, 8]), a direct comparison to the best-performing methods in those categories would strengthen the claims. The discussion of the dataset could also be expanded, providing more details about potential biases or limitations. While the authors mention the importance of the 'd' parameter, a more in-depth analysis of its relationship to performance across different categories would be valuable. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The EM approach for full kernel learning is a significant advance over existing methods.
*   **Soundness:** The theoretical derivations and algorithmic details are well-explained.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experiments demonstrate clear improvements over a baseline method.
*   **Significance:** The ability to learn full DPP kernels has broad implications for diverse subset selection tasks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison to state-of-the-art DPP learning methods beyond basic gradient ascent.
*   Dataset description could be more comprehensive.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective solution to a challenging problem. The technical contributions are significant, and the experimental results are promising. Despite minor areas for improvement, I recommend acceptance to the conference. The authors have presented a well-written and insightful work that is likely to be of interest to the NIPS community.
