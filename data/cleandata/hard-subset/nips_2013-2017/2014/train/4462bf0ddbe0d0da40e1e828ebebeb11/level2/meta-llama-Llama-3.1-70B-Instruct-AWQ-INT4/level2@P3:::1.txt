**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning the kernel matrix of a determinantal point process (DPP) using expectation-maximization (EM). DPPs are probabilistic models that can capture the diversity of sets, making them useful for tasks such as product recommendation. The learning problem is challenging because the log-likelihood is non-convex in the kernel matrix entries. The proposed EM algorithm changes the parameterization of the kernel matrix from entries to eigenvalues and eigenvectors, which allows for a more efficient optimization procedure. The algorithm is tested on a real-world product recommendation task and achieves better performance than a naive approach using projected gradient ascent.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel algorithm**: The paper proposes a new algorithm for learning the kernel matrix of a DPP, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Theoretical analysis**: The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including the derivation of the EM updates and the analysis of the computational complexity.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation of the algorithm on a real-world product recommendation task, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited scope**: The paper focuses on a specific application of DPPs, namely product recommendation. It would be interesting to see the algorithm applied to other tasks.
2. **Comparison to other methods**: While the paper compares the proposed algorithm to a naive approach using projected gradient ascent, it would be useful to compare it to other existing methods for learning DPPs.
3. **Sensitivity to initialization**: The paper mentions that the algorithm is sensitive to the initialization of the kernel matrix. It would be interesting to explore ways to improve the robustness of the algorithm to initialization.

**Originality and Significance**

1. **Originality**: The paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning the kernel matrix of a DPP, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Significance**: The algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable in various tasks that involve modeling the diversity of sets.

**Quality of the Writing**

1. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its theoretical analysis.
2. **Organization**: The paper is well-organized, with a logical structure that makes it easy to follow.
