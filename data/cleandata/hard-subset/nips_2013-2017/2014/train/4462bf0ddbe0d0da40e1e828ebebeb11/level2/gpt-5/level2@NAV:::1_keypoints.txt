- Main claim: Reparameterize DPP kernel by eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introduce latent active-eigenvector subsets to enable an EM-style optimization that avoids PSD projections, is more robust to initialization, and can be faster when k ≪ N.
- Method: Uses mixture view of DPPs; E-step computes a conditional k-DPP over eigen-index sets, M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and a Stiefel-manifold eigenvector update.
- Empirical result: On an Amazon baby registry dataset, the method improves test log-likelihood up to 16.5% over projected gradient ascent and runs ≈2.1× faster.
- Quality: Derivations are careful and technically sound; complexity analysis is plausible and explains when EM has advantages.
- Limitation: Empirical evaluation is limited to one moderate-N domain and a single baseline (projected gradient on K).
- Missing analyses: Statistical significance of gains is not reported and several ablations (e.g., eigenvector steps per M-step, sensitivity to eigenvalue clipping near 1) are absent.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well organized; EM lower bound and k-DPP E-step are well motivated.
- Clarity gap: Presentation of the manifold/Stiefel update could use a brief, self-contained explanation of the matrix exponential step and its cost.
- Originality: Framing full-kernel DPP learning via an EM lower bound in eigen-space and operationalizing the E-step as a k-DPP is novel relative to prior work.
- Significance: If scalable and robust, the approach could materially impact diversity-modeling applications, but broader comparisons and larger-scale tests are needed.
- Pros summary: Clear principled EM formulation, avoids PSD-projection degeneracies, robust to initialization, solid runtime analysis, and provided code/practical initializations.
- Cons summary: Narrow empirical evaluation, no comparisons to alternative optimizers (Riemannian/mirror descent, quasi-Newton), no synthetic recovery or larger-N benchmarks, limited statistical analysis.
- Theoretical gap: Convergence properties of the partial M-step and monotonicity guarantees are not characterized.
- Suggestions: Add synthetic experiments with known ground truth, compare against stronger baselines, report statistical significance, analyze sensitivity to k/initialization/eigenvalue clipping, and discuss convergence diagnostics or guarantees.
