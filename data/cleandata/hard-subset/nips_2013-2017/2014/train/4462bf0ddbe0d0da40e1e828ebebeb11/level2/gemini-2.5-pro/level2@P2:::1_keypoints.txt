- Addresses learning the full, unconstrained DPP kernel from data, a challenging non-convex problem previously avoided by restrictive parameterizations.  
- Key idea: reparameterize the kernel by eigenvalues and eigenvectors and introduce latent eigenvector sets in an EM framework.  
- E-step tractability: identifies the posterior over latent variables as a k-DPP (Lemma 1), enabling efficient E-step computation.  
- M-step: eigenvalues update in closed form; eigenvectors updated via a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold to avoid costly projections.  
- Algorithm: coordinate-ascent on a likelihood lower bound (EM/GEM), with a single gradient step for eigenvectors per M-step.  
- Empirical results: significantly better solution quality and runtime than projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) across tasks.  
- Robustness: more robust to initialization and performs better in data-poor settings than the baseline.  
- Technical quality: derivation is sound, well-motivated, and supported by thorough experiments on real-world recommendation data.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible, with helpful algorithm boxes.  
- Originality: proposes a novel, non-obvious EM approach leveraging eigendecomposition and latent variables, distinct from prior restrictive methods.  
- Significance: high impact—makes learning expressive DPPs practical and likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Strength: elegant combination of eigendecomposition and Stiefel-manifold updates that avoids naive projection pitfalls.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation showing speed, robustness, and effectiveness under varied conditions.  
- Weakness: advantage over baseline shrinks with a strong data-dependent ("moments-matching") initializer in data-rich settings.  
- Weakness: eigenvector M-step is a single gradient step (GEM), not a full maximization—should be stated explicitly for precision.  
- Suggestion: report typical number of line-search iterations (T2) needed for eigenvector step to clarify practical cost.  
- Suggestion: explicitly label the method a Generalized EM (GEM) to reflect that the M-step is not fully optimized.  
- Suggestion: discuss whether the one-time projection in the moment-matching initializer can cause issues or why it is less problematic than iterative projections.
