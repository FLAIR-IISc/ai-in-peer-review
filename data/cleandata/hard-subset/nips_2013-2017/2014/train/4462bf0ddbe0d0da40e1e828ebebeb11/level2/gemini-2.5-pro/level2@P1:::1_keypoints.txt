- Tackles the important problem of learning the full kernel matrix of a DPP from observed subsets, where ML is non-convex and conjectured NP-hard.
- Key contribution: an EM-style algorithm that learns the full, unconstrained kernel via eigendecomposition into eigenvalues Λ and eigenvectors V.
- Introduces a latent variable J (subset of eigenvectors) by viewing a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs, enabling an EM formulation.
- E-step: computes posterior over J given an observed set Yi, and shows this posterior is itself a k-DPP with an explicit kernel.
- M-step: maximizes expected complete-data log-likelihood; eigenvalue updates have a closed-form solution.
- Eigenvector update: performed via a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold to respect orthogonality without naive projection.
- Avoids problematic projection step that causes baseline methods to produce near-diagonal, low-diversity solutions.
- Empirical comparison on Amazon baby registry data shows EM is more robust and achieves significantly better test log-likelihoods, especially from random initialization or sparse data.
- Demonstrated that the EM algorithm is computationally faster in practice than projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).
- Derivation and use of Stiefel manifold optimization are technically sound and well-presented.
- Experimental evaluation is strong: real-world dataset, sensible baseline, and analyses across initializations and data regimes.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with helpful background and useful pseudocode.
- Highly original: first practical, principled algorithm for learning a full DPP kernel via maximum likelihood using this EM re-parameterization.
- Significant contribution: makes DPP learning more accessible and useful for diverse subset selection applications.
- Strengths summarized: novel effective algorithm, elegant EM formulation, robustness and speed advantages, clear failure-mode analysis of baseline, convincing experiments.
- Weaknesses summarized: eigenvector M-step uses a single gradient step rather than full maximization (generalized EM), and runtime includes an O(N^3) term which may limit scalability.
- Overall recommendation: technically strong, significant, and well-executed work; strongly recommend acceptance.
