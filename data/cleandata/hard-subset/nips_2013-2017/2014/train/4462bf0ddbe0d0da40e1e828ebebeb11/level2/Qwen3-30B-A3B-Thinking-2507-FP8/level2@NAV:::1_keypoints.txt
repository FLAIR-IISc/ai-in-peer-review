- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel without restrictive parameterizations, addressing a key limitation of prior methods.  
- Claims EM avoids degenerate solutions produced by projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) caused by the projection step, yielding better log-likelihood and faster runtime.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and clearly presented using the eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix.  
- Strong experimental validation on Amazon baby registry data, showing up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent.  
- Improvements are most pronounced in categories with strong negative interactions (d > 0.025).  
- Runtime analysis shows EM is asymptotically faster than K-Ascent, with an empirical average speedup of 2.1x.  
- Highly original: first EM-based approach for full kernel learning without restrictive parameterizations, properly citing prior row-weight and ensemble methods.  
- Significant practical impact for subset-selection tasks (e.g., recommendations, summarization), producing more natural diverse recommendations.  
- Limitation: both EM and K-Ascent require careful initialization (Wishart vs. moment matching); experiments could include more baselines (e.g., Bayesian methods from Affandi et al., ICML 2013).  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with clear theory and experiments; recommendation to accept.
