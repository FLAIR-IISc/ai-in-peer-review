- Problem: Learn the full (unconstrained) DPP kernel from observed subsets; maximum-likelihood is non-convex and prior work used restricted convex settings.  
- Contribution: Proposes a novel EM algorithm that re-parameterizes the kernel via eigendecomposition and treats eigenvector selection J as a latent variable.  
- Key technical insight: The E-step posterior is a k-DPP, enabling efficient computation; optimizing eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold avoids harmful projection steps.  
- Empirical result: EM is more robust, faster, and yields better solutions than a projected gradient (K-Ascent) baseline on a real-world product recommendation task.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation including initialization strategies, data-poor scenarios, qualitative examples, and runtime analysis supporting theoretical advantages.  
- Significance: Broadens DPP applicability by enabling learning of full kernels without strong parametric assumptions—a substantial advance over prior methods.  
- Originality and quality: The EM formulation is creative, well-executed, and technically sound.  
- Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation and effective use of the baseline to illustrate challenges.  
- Weakness — M-step: Uses a single gradient step (making it a Generalized EM); should acknowledge GEM status and comment on convergence properties.  
- Weakness — scalability: Algorithm is still O(N^3), which may limit large-scale use; discuss future scaling (e.g., low-rank K approximations).  
- Overall summary: High-quality, significant, and likely influential work with only minor, addressable issues.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a fundamental problem, presents a novel theoretically justified method, demonstrates strong empirical gains, and is clearly written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: None substantial; only minor points that can be fixed in revision.
