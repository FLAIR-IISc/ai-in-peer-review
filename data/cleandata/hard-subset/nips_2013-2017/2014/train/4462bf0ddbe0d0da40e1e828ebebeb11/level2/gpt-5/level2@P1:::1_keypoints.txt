- Paper proposes EM-style maximum likelihood learning for full DPP kernels by reparameterizing K via eigendecomposition and using a latent J indexing elementary DPP mixtures.  
- E-step uses the exact posterior pK(J|Y), which is a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel; M-step updates eigenvalues λ in closed form and takes a Stiefel-manifold gradient step for eigenvectors V.  
- Method avoids PSD-projection/eigenvalue-clipping required by naive projected gradient on K (KA), which can cause near-diagonal degeneracy.  
- Experiments on Amazon baby registry data show EM improves held-out log-likelihood over KA, especially with weak/random initialization or limited data, and is faster in practice.  
- Builds on DPP fundamentals and sampling via eigendecomposition; novelty lies in learning a full unconstrained kernel via the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs EM view.  
- Strength: elegant use of mixture representation to derive an EM whose E-step yields an explicit k-DPP posterior.  
- Strength: avoids projection-induced degeneracy of KA, supported theoretically and empirically.  
- Strength: efficient eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and clear Stiefel-manifold eigenvector update with complexity analysis.  
- Strength: empirical gains (up to 16.5% test LL improvement with limited data) and practical speedups (~2.1×).  
- Strength: clear exposition, pseudocode, complexity breakdown, and initialization discussion.  
- Weakness: no convergence/monotonicity guarantees since M-step only takes a single gradient step and line search uses L rather than the bound F.  
- Weakness: comparisons are only against KA; lack of side-by-side baselines (e.g., row-weight or mixture methods) limits contextualization.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—per-step eigendecompositions/matrix exponentials imply O(N^3) cost for line-searches; practical limits for larger N not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: no structured regularization on λ or V; sensitivity to initialization and λ→1 numerical issues not systematically handled.  
- Weakness: eigenvector gradient is valid only at q = p, making the single-step heuristic insufficiently justified without empirical ablations.  
- Quality: technically sound derivations for E-step and λ update; eigenvector gradient derivation is plausible with principled Stiefel update.  
- Quality: repeated E-steps during line search are a practical but cost-increasing workaround; trade-offs are transparently discussed.  
- Quality: experiments are solid for the task but would benefit from additional datasets or synthetic stress tests.  
- Clarity: well-organized, consistent notation, supplement for proofs, and clear statement of k’s role in complexity.  
- Clarity suggestion: summarize numerical stabilization for λ near 1 and clarify whether line-search acceptance guarantees non-decreasing likelihood.  
- Originality: novel EM in the eigenbasis exploiting tractable k-DPP E-step for full-kernel learning.  
- Significance: important advance for applications needing full-kernel DPPs; likely to be adopted due to simplicity and empirical advantages.  
- Questions for authors: provide monotonicity guarantees or L-vs-iteration plots, explore multiple manifold steps/approximate retractions, compare to at least one prior learning strategy, evaluate low-rank/spectral regularization, and discuss exploiting sparsity or block structure for large N.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled novel approach, avoids projected-gradient pitfalls, and shows empirical and runtime improvements.  
- Cons for acceptance: lack of convergence guarantees, limited baselines, and unclear scalability to much larger N.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept; meaningful advance but would be stronger with convergence discussion and expanded evaluation.
