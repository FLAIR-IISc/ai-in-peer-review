- Addresses full-kernel maximum-likelihood learning for DPPs by reparameterizing K via eigenvalues and eigenvectors and deriving an EM-style lower bound.
- Introduces the DPP mixture latent variable J, with an E-step yielding a k-DPP posterior over J that enables efficient expected sufficient statistic computation.
- M-step provides closed-form eigenvalue updates and a manifold-constrained gradient step for eigenvectors using a Stiefel update via a matrix exponential.
- The EM procedure empirically avoids degeneracies seen with naive projected gradient ascent on K’s entries.
- Experiments on Amazon baby registry show up to 16.5% test log-likelihood improvement in data-poor settings and reduced runtime versus the projected-gradient baseline.
- Connects to and extends prior DPP literature (foundational DPP theory, k-DPPs, MAP inference, scalable sampling), while prior learning work mostly used restricted kernel parameterizations.
- Novelty: first principled use of an EM lower bound with spectral DPP mixture latent variables for full-kernel learning.
- Technical soundness: latent-variable formulation and EM derivation are appropriate; E-step as a k-DPP with kernel QY is plausible and consistent with known identities.
- Computational complexity: eigenvalue updates are efficiently computable via k-DPP marginals with complexity O(n N k^2).
- Eigenvector update uses Stiefel-manifold multiplicative steps and verifies true likelihood improvement to mitigate inexact optimization.
- Theoretical guarantees: monotonic likelihood increase is not formally guaranteed due to inexact M-steps, though acceptance checks provide a practical safeguard.
- Convergence and stationarity guarantees are under-discussed and merit deeper analysis.
- Baselines and experiments: projected gradient baseline is natural and well-implemented, and failure modes from projection-induced near-diagonality are convincingly analyzed.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single domain and primarily one baseline, reducing breadth of evidence for generality.
- Clarity: paper is clear, well organized, and includes helpful pseudocode, but has minor issues (citation mapping inconsistencies, handling of eigenvalues near 1, and notation L vs F in Algorithm 2).
- Strengths: principled EM formulation, efficient E-step and closed-form eigenvalue updates, appropriate manifold optimization for eigenvectors, solid runtime analysis, real-world dataset and public code.
- Weaknesses: limited empirical breadth and baselines, no synthetic recovery experiments or sensitivity analyses, convergence properties are empirical-only, and some minor citation/notation inconsistencies.
- Essential suggestions: add at least one additional baseline (e.g., direct spectral-space optimization or Cholesky/softplus PSD parameterization), include a small synthetic experiment with known K, and fix citation/reference inconsistencies.
- Optional suggestions: ablations on number of V-updates per M-step, report eigenvalue clipping frequency and strategy, add a second application domain, and consider simple regularization or eigenvalue priors.
- Pros for acceptance: novel and well-motivated method addressing a central DPP learning challenge, technically sound with efficient computation, and shows practical empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical validation and baselines, lack of formal convergence guarantees, and minor clarity/citation issues.
