- Presents a novel EM-based algorithm using eigendecomposition to learn the full unconstrained DPP kernel, avoiding problematic projection steps.  
- Addresses non-convex DPP log-likelihood optimization and enables learning of the complete kernel while preserving diversity properties.  
- Validated on a real-world Amazon baby registry product recommendation task, showing up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood over projected gradient ascent.  
- Empirical experiments are thorough across multiple product categories and show robustness, efficiency, and superior performance of EM versus the baseline.  
- Provides clear derivations for EM updates, efficient eigenvalue/eigenvector computations, and an asymptotic runtime analysis favoring EM.  
- Paper is technically rigorous but dense; explanations may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with DPPs or matrix optimization.  
- Experimental scope is limited to a single application domain (product recommendation), reducing evidence of generalizability.  
- Both EM and baseline methods show sensitivity to initialization; moments-matching initializer helps but initialization impacts need more discussion.  
- Lacks explicit discussion of limitations, including potential scalability issues and computational cost of eigendecomposition for very large matrices.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand discussion of limitations and initialization strategies.  
- Pro: novel and effective unconstrained DPP learning algorithm with strong empirical and theoretical support.  
- Con: dense presentation and experiments confined to one domain may hinder accessibility and generalizability.
