- Proposes an EM approach for learning the full DPP kernel, addressing limitations of prior work that used restricted parameterizations.  
- Parameterizes the marginal kernel K via eigenvalues and eigenvectors, enabling an EM algorithm that avoids projection steps required by naive gradient ascent.  
- Provides a rigorous derivation using eigendecomposition and the hidden-variable interpretation of DPPs, with clear E-step and M-step updates.  
- Shows eigenvalue updates are self-normalizing, removing the need for explicit projection.  
- Supplies a thorough complexity analysis demonstrating EM is asymptotically faster than gradient ascent.  
- Experimental validation on a real-world product recommendation task shows up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood and stronger handling of negative item interactions.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with effective connections between theory and implementation considerations.  
- Needs more intuitive explanation of why eigendecomposition is necessary and how it overcomes previous method limitations.  
- The link between the hidden-variable interpretation and practical EM advantages would be clearer with a concrete example.  
- Initialization strategies are not compared thoroughly and warrant deeper exploration.  
- Analysis of conditions where the method performs best (e.g., high-d categories) is preliminary and could be strengthened.  
- Original and significant contribution: tackles full-kernel learning previously conjectured to be NP-hard and distinguishes itself from related work.  
- Method avoids degeneracy issues seen in gradient ascent by leveraging the eigendecomposition+EM framework.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-motivated, practically validated, and relevant to the community.
