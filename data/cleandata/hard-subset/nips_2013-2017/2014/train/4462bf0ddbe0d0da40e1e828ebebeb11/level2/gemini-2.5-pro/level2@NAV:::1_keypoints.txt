- Problem & approach: Tackles non-convex learning of a full DPP kernel by a novel EM algorithm on the eigendecomposition, treating active eigenvectors as latent to avoid projection steps.  
- Main contribution: Introduces an EM procedure operating on eigenvalues/eigenvectors of the kernel.  
- Originality/significance: Novel and important — enables learning unconstrained full kernels to capture complex diversity patterns.  
- Technical quality: Sound derivation; correctly identifies failure mode of projected gradient (collapse to near-diagonal) and offers a well-motivated remedy.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates robustness and up to 16.5% test log-likelihood improvement on a real baby-registry dataset, and is computationally faster than the baseline.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, logical derivations, and transparent experiments.  
- Weakness — M-step approximation: Uses a single gradient step on the Stiefel manifold instead of full optimization; impact of this approximation should be briefly discussed.  
- Weakness — scalability: Algorithm has an O(N^3) term; discussion of this limitation and potential fixes (e.g., low-rank approximations) would strengthen the paper.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically solid, empirically validated, and clearly communicated.
