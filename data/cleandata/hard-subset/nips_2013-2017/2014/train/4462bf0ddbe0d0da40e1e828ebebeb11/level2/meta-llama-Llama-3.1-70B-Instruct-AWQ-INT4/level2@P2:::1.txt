This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs) by exploiting their eigendecomposition and using an expectation-maximization (EM) style optimization procedure. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a real-world product recommendation task, achieving relative gains of up to 16.5% in test log-likelihood compared to a naive approach of maximizing likelihood by projected gradient ascent.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to DPPs and their applications. The technical contributions of the paper are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the EM algorithm's convergence and computational complexity. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed comparison of their approach with a baseline method.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel and effective algorithm for learning DPPs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the EM algorithm's convergence and computational complexity, which is essential for understanding the algorithm's behavior.
* The experimental results are well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed comparison of their approach with a baseline method.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:
