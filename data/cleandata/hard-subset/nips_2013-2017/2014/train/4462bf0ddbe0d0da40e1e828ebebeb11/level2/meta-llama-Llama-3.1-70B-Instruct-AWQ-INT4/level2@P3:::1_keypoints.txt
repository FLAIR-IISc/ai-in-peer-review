- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPP kernel matrices by reparameterizing in eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Addresses non-convex log-likelihood by optimizing over eigen-parameters for more efficient learning.  
- Evaluated on a real-world product recommendation task and outperforms a naive projected gradient ascent baseline.  
- Strength: introduces a new, significant algorithmic contribution for DPP learning.  
- Strength: provides thorough theoretical analysis, including EM update derivations and computational complexity.  
- Strength: presents comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating empirical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: evaluation scope is limited to product recommendation and lacks broader task validation.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited—missing evaluation against other established DPP learning methods.  
- Weakness: algorithm shows sensitivity to kernel matrix initialization and lacks robustness improvements.  
- Originality: method is novel and makes a notable contribution to DPP learning.  
- Significance: approach has potential applicability to diverse set-diversity modeling tasks.  
- Writing quality: paper is clear, well-organized, and uses consistent, understandable notation.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends accepting the paper for NIPS 2014.
