- Addresses learning full DPP kernel via an EM algorithm, tackling a conjectured NP-hard problem.  
- Re-parameterizes the kernel by eigenvalues and eigenvectors to optimize a likelihood lower bound.  
- Avoids projection onto PSD matrices, sidestepping issues of projection-based gradient methods.  
- Evaluated on a product recommendation task and shows improvement over projected gradient ascent baseline.  
- Connects diversity gains to data characteristics via the introduced 'd' metric.  
- Includes runtime comparisons demonstrating empirical speed advantages.  
- Well-motivated and clearly articulates limitations of prior methods.  
- EM algorithm is a novel, potentially more robust and efficient alternative to gradient methods.  
- Strong theoretical justification presented for the proposed approach.  
- Complexity analysis is brief; claim of asymptotic speedup needs a more detailed breakdown and clearer assumptions.  
- Stiefel manifold update and practical implementation details (step size selection, regularization) are under-explained.  
- Sensitivity to initialization is a potential weakness; only two initializers tested and robustness to others is unclear.  
- Moment-matching initializer is claimed effective but lacks explanation of why it outperforms projection-based initializers.  
- Some mathematical derivations are abrupt (notably between equations 13 and 20) and need more intermediate exposition.  
- Limited experimental evaluation (single application); broader experiments would strengthen claims.  
- Recommendation: promising and likely acceptable with revisions—expand complexity analysis, explore initialization robustness, and clarify derivations.
