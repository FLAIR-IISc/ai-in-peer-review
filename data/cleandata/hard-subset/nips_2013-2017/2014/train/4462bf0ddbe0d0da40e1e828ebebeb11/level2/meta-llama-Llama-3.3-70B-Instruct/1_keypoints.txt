- Proposes a novel EM-style algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by exploiting its eigendecomposition.  
- EM algorithm removes the need for projection to maintain positive semi-definiteness used in naive gradient methods.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to DPPs and their applications.  
- Main claim: EM is more robust and efficient than projected gradient ascent for DPP learning.  
- Empirical support: experiments on a product recommendation task show up to 16.5% relative test log-likelihood improvement.  
- Strength: thorough review of prior DPP work and clear motivation for a new approach.  
- Strength: EM derivation is well-presented and easy to follow (clear E and M steps).  
- Strength: experiments are well-designed and provide convincing evidence of effectiveness.  
- Limitation: assumes the kernel matrix can be eigendecomposed, which may not always hold in practice.  
- Limitation: requires computing eigendecomposition, potentially expensive for large matrices.  
- Limitation: lacks a thorough computational complexity analysis, making scalability unclear.  
- Novelty: presents a significant, innovative improvement over prior DPP learning methods.  
- Significance: method could impact many domains (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement, image search).  
- Overall recommendation: accept, given strong presentation, derivation, experiments, and potential impact.
