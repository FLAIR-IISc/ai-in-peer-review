- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs by exploiting eigendecomposition.  
- EM is more robust and efficient than projected gradient (K-Ascent) for DPP learning.  
- Demonstrates up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood on a real-world product recommendation task.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful background on DPPs.  
- Provides detailed derivation of E-step and M-step updates for the EM algorithm.  
- Experimental comparison against K-Ascent on a real dataset shows EM outperforms baseline, especially when off-diagonal kernel components matter.  
- Runtime analysis indicates EM is asymptotically faster than K-Ascent.  
- Strengths: novelty of method, empirical effectiveness, and clarity of presentation.  
- Weakness: related work section misses some recent DPP learning papers (e.g., [8], [3]).  
- Weakness: lacks deeper theoretical analysis (convergence guarantees and approximation bounds).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could include more datasets and scenarios to show robustness.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss recent related work, add in-depth theoretical analysis, and broaden experimental evaluation.
