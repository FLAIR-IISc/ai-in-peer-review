- DPPs assign higher probability to diverse sets and are parameterized by a PSD matrix L; learning L is NP-hard and prior work only partially learns L.  
- Learning only scalar/vector parameters for L sacrifices the diversity dimension; prior methods restrict L’s parametric form.  
- This paper proposes a learning method that does not restrict L’s parameterization.  
- The method avoids projection steps in gradient ascent (which can harm similarity/diversity) by using an EM approach on eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- They explore optimization algorithms that remove the need to project values and exploit full-rank V to avoid projecting eigenvectors.  
- Jensen’s inequality is used to lower-bound the objective and derive the EM procedure.  
- The proposed method preserves DPP diversity better than prior work that focused mainly on quality.  
- The step-by-step EM derivation is insightful and well explained.  
- Mapping the constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold and showing the inverse distribution is a DPP are solid technical contributions.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task support the claim that the method retains both quality and diversity, compared to KA.  
- The KA baseline replaces only two of the top-10 products with less likely ones, which still highlights the proposed method’s advantage.  
- Paper writing and proofs are coherent and well-written, though the conclusion is somewhat hasty and omits some contributions.  
- More experiments on real-world datasets are recommended to better illustrate the practical importance of the produced diversity.
