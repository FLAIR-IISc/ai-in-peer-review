- Main contribution: Reformulates kernel learning for DPPs using an EM framework as an alternative to direct gradient-based kernel parameter learning (KA).
- Method: Notes any DPP can be represented as a mixture over elementary DPPs; uses a variational distribution over subsets, E-step updates this as a k-DPP and M-step performs single gradient steps.
- Empirical result: EM outperforms KA substantially with naive KA initialization, and modestly in some settings with smarter KA initialization.
- M-step critique: The M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats/approximations, which weakens the claimed elegance of the method.
- Missing experiment: Lacks analysis of initialization effects — e.g., whether initializing EM from KA solutions yields improvements.
- Metric concern: Questions the use of "relative log likelihood" (ratios of log probabilities) and suggests reporting log-probability differences for easier interpretation.
- Writing/style issues: Dislikes authors calling the algorithm “elegant” and finds bolding in the introduction unnecessary.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and promising approach with modest empirical gains; experiments could be improved, but overall a pretty good paper.
