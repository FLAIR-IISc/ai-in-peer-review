- Presents an EM algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound.
- Method appears well-founded, original, and a meaningful contribution to subset selection literature.
- Experiments show favorable behavior compared to a simple K-Ascent baseline but are limited in scope.
- Lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods mentioned in the introduction, so practical benefits of the DPP approach are unclear.
- Write-up is sloppy and hard to follow; notation is sometimes undefined or introduced after use.
- Paper structure interleaves method derivation with side remarks and alternatives, making the main thread convoluted; recommend presenting the method clearly first.
- Specific notation issues: marginal kernel K and variable V are used before definition.
- Language/phrasing issues: e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” is unclear and should state “closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q”.
- Section referencing external work ([26]) is hard to follow; the authors should outline the simpler approach directly rather than forcing readers to look up the original.
- Ambiguous terms need clarification, e.g., what “weight” refers to in p.4 line 189.
- Questionable rationale in equation (30): why is m_{ij} not used, and why not initialize with the empirical covariance matrix?
- Overall: technically solid and original but needs clearer exposition and substantially stronger experimental comparisons.
