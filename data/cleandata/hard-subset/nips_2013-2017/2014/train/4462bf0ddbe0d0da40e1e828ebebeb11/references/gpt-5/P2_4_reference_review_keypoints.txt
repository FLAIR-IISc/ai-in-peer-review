- Paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning unconstrained DPP kernels by treating eigenvector-index sets J as latent variables in the eigenspace of K.
- E-step sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with a computable kernel; M-step gives closed-form λ updates (posterior inclusion probabilities) and a Stiefel-manifold gradient update for eigenvectors V.
- Method avoids PSD/eigenvalue projection pitfalls that can trap projected gradient ascent (KA) in near-diagonal, degenerate solutions.
- Complexity improves when k ≪ N: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) versus KA’s O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3).
- Experiments on Amazon baby registries show EM consistently improves test log-likelihood over KA (median ~3.7% with Wishart init, ~0.4% with moments init) and is ~2.1× faster.
- EM yields qualitatively better, less redundant selections than KA, with larger gains in data-poor settings and categories with strong off-diagonal structure.
- Technical soundness: EM formulation and use of k-DPP marginals are well-motivated; λ-update and V-update derivations are plausible (some details in supplement).
- Justification: Derivations plus experiments support accuracy and runtime claims; explanation of KA degeneracy is insightful.
- Caveat: V-update is a single gradient step per M-step with step-size search (generalized EM); acceptance uses true L but reported δ uses bound F, so monotonicity/acceptance needs clarification.
- Caveat: No formal convergence guarantees beyond generalized EM heuristics.
- Caveat: Numerical stability issues when λ→1 are noted; more principled regularization (cap, prior, barrier) would help.
- Caveat: Per step-size trial still requires N^3 cost; practical speedups rely on replacing many N^3 inverses with O(N k^2) ops and need broader profiling.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and organized, but key derivations (Lemma 1, Eqns. 19–20) and complexity analyses are deferred to supplement and should be sketched in the main text.
- Originality: Novel EM using latent eigenvector-index sets and k-DPP structure; Stiefel-manifold eigenvector update in this context appears new.
- Significance: Addresses practical projection degeneracy, shows measurable gains, and is likely useful for full non-parametric DPP learning.
- Limitations: Experiments compare only to projected gradient on K; missing baselines (Bayesian/parametric, quality-only/similarity-only, pseudo-likelihood) and ablations.
- Suggested experiments: Ablation of λ-only vs. full EM, synthetic recovery experiments with known K, sensitivity analyses to k, N, and initialization.
- Suggested clarifications: State Algorithm 2 acceptance criterion (L vs. F), conditions for monotone L improvement, practical λ regularization, and scalability/approximation strategies (low-rank, Nystrom).
- Pros: Clean EM formulation, closed-form λ-update, principled V-update, robustness to initialization, demonstrated empirical gains and speedups.
- Cons: Limited baselines and ablations, some derivations deferred, monotonicity/convergence not formalized, scalability still limited and no discussion of large-N approximations.
- Overall recommendation: Novel, practical approach with solid motivation and empirical gains; leaning to weak accept pending clarifications on generalized EM monotonicity and strengthened experimental baselines.
