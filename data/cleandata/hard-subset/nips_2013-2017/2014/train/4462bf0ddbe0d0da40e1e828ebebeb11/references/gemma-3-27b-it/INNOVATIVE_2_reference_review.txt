This paper tackles the challenging problem of learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), specifically addressing the difficulty of optimizing the likelihood function for the kernel matrix L. The authors propose an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm that cleverly exploits the eigendecomposition of L, circumventing the problematic projection step inherent in naive gradient ascent approaches. This is a significant contribution, as it provides a viable learning method for DPPs without restrictive parameterizations or assumptions about L’s form. The connection to hidden variable J, representing the eigenvectors of K, is insightful and well-motivated.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The EM approach, leveraging eigendecomposition and a hidden variable formulation, is a genuinely novel method for DPP learning. It directly addresses a core bottleneck in the field.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the EM updates appears rigorous, and the justification for avoiding the problematic projection step is convincing. The use of Jensen’s inequality is appropriate and well-executed.
* **Potential Significance:**  A robust and scalable learning algorithm for DPPs has broad implications for various applications – recommendation systems, document summarization, and beyond. The empirical results, demonstrating both improved performance and runtime, suggest practical impact.
* **Clear Writing:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized. The background section provides a good overview of DPPs, and the explanation of the EM algorithm is relatively easy to follow, given the mathematical complexity.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Experimental Scope:** While the results on the baby registry dataset are promising, the evaluation is somewhat limited. Testing on additional datasets, particularly those with different characteristics (e.g., higher dimensionality, different data distributions), would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** The EM algorithm, while theoretically elegant, appears complex to implement correctly. The details of the Stiefel manifold update, though referenced, could benefit from a more detailed explanation or pointer to relevant resources.
* **Sensitivity to Initialization:** The authors acknowledge the importance of initialization, and present two methods. It’s not entirely clear how sensitive the algorithm is to different initialization schemes beyond those tested. A more thorough investigation of initialization robustness would be valuable.
* **Runtime Analysis Caveats:** The asymptotic runtime analysis relies on the assumption that k << N. While this is often the case in practice, it's important to acknowledge this assumption and its potential impact on performance in scenarios where k is closer to N.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound solution to a fundamental problem in DPP learning.
* The EM algorithm avoids the pitfalls of gradient ascent and offers advantages in both robustness and asymptotic runtime.
* The experimental results, while not exhaustive, provide compelling evidence of the algorithm’s effectiveness.
* The potential impact of a scalable and robust DPP learning method is significant.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The experimental validation could be more comprehensive, including a wider range of datasets.
* The complexity of the implementation may pose a barrier to adoption.
* The runtime analysis relies on an assumption that may not hold in all scenarios.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that introduces a genuinely novel and promising approach to DPP learning. While there are some minor weaknesses in terms of experimental scope and implementation complexity, the strengths of the paper – particularly its novelty and potential significance – outweigh these concerns.  I am willing to overlook the incomplete validation and potential implementation challenges given the boldness of the approach and its clear potential to move the field forward. The authors have identified a significant bottleneck and proposed a clever solution.  I recommend **acceptance**.
