- Proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn full nonparametric DPP kernels by reparameterizing K = VΛV^T and introducing latent subset J of eigenvectors.  
- Uses the mixture interpretation of DPPs and Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable lower bound and coordinate-ascent updates.  
- E-step: sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with closed-form kernel QY, enabling O(Nk^2) marginal computations.  
- M-step: eigenvalues updated in closed form λj = (1/n) Σi q(j∈J|Yi); eigenvectors updated via a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step with likelihood-increasing line search.  
- Computational complexity stated as O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), with N^3 from matrix exponentials in Stiefel retractions; empirical runtime ~2× faster than baseline for moderate N.  
- Empirical evaluation on a baby-registry dataset (13 categories) shows improved robustness to initialization, better performance with scarce data, and faster runtime versus projected-gradient on K.  
- Strength: elegant exploitation of DPP eigenstructure and k-DPP posterior for computational benefit.  
- Strength: avoids degeneracy to near-diagonal kernels and models diversity more robustly than direct K projection.  
- Strength: clean, self-normalizing eigenvalue updates and practical manifold update engineering.  
- Weakness: eigenvector M-step uses a gradient valid only at q=p, making the algorithm a generalized EM without exact EM gradients.  
- Weakness: theoretical convergence guarantees (to stationary points) are not provided; only practical monotonicity via line search is discussed.  
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—single domain, no comparisons to stronger baselines (feature-based L-learning, Bayesian, structured/low-rank DPPs), and no large-scale (N≫) experiments.  
- Weakness: some notation and derivations are terse or deferred; Lemma 1, QY dimensions, and conditions for Eq.(20) need clearer exposition.  
- Weakness: small average log-likelihood gains (≈0.4%) over good moment-matching initialization in data-rich settings; main gains in low-data/poor-init regimes.  
- Weakness: computational analysis may underestimate costs from repeated E-steps during line search and expensive N^3 retractions.  
- Quality: E-step and eigenvalue updates appear technically correct; eigenvector update is a practical approximation that would benefit from a convergence discussion.  
- Clarity: generally well written but should explicitly state generalized EM status, monotonicity checks, and more detail on k-DPP marginal computation and O(Nk^2) cost.  
- Originality: novel hidden-variable EM formulation using elementary DPP mixtures and k-DPP posteriors for nonparametric kernel learning.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem—learning full DPP kernels—and offers practical gains for practitioners reliant on restrictive parameterizations.  
- Suggestions: add convergence statements (e.g., Zangwill-type), consider cheaper retractions (QR/Cayley), test sensitivity to eigenvector update steps, and compare to parametric/Bayesian baselines.  
- Pros (summary): novel EM formulation, robustness and speed over projected gradient, principled eigenvalue updates, and real-data analysis.  
- Cons (summary): generalized EM without theoretical guarantees, limited baselines/scales, potential scalability bottleneck from N^3 operations.  
- Recommendation: lean accept — promising and practical contribution that would be strengthened by convergence discussion and broader empirical validation.
