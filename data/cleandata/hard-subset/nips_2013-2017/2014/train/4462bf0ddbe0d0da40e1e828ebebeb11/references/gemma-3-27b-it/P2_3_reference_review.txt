## Review of "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning the kernel matrix *L* for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) from data. Existing methods either learn only partial information about *L*, rely on restrictive parameterizations, or suffer from optimization issues (specifically, gradient ascent requiring problematic projections). The authors propose a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm that leverages the eigendecomposition of *L* to avoid these pitfalls. The core idea is to introduce a hidden variable representing the eigenvectors of *L* and then iteratively refine estimates of both the eigenvalues and eigenvectors using an EM framework. The paper demonstrates both theoretical advantages (asymptotic runtime) and empirical improvements over gradient ascent on a product recommendation task.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Learning DPPs is crucial for applications requiring diverse subset selection, and the full kernel learning problem is known to be difficult.
* **Novel Approach:** The use of EM with eigendecomposition is a clever and well-motivated technique, circumventing the issues of direct gradient ascent. The connection to hidden variables related to the DPP sampling process is insightful.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides arguments for the asymptotic speedup of the EM algorithm compared to gradient ascent, which is a significant theoretical contribution.
* **Empirical Validation:**  Experiments on baby registry data demonstrate the effectiveness of the EM algorithm, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between items are important and when data is limited. The comparison to K-Ascent provides a clear benchmark.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The background section provides sufficient context, and the algorithm descriptions are reasonably clear.
* **Robustness:** The EM approach shows robustness against initialization, and avoids the degenerate solutions observed with gradient ascent.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Derivation:** While the authors provide equations for the M-step updates, the derivations are relatively dense and could benefit from further clarification. The connection between equations (19) and (20) and their computational complexities isn’t immediately obvious without referring to the supplement.
* **Practicality of the Stiefel Manifold Update:** While acknowledging the need for a more sophisticated eigenvector update, the authors simplify to a basic multiplicative update.  The impact of this simplification on performance isn’t fully explored.
* **Dataset Specificity:** The experiments focus solely on product recommendation within the baby registry domain. While insightful, this limits the generalizability of the results. Testing on other, diverse datasets would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Limited Ablation Study:**  The paper largely compares EM to K-Ascent. An ablation study investigating the impact of individual components of the EM algorithm (e.g., the choice of initialization, the approximation in the M-step) would provide deeper insights.




**Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical arguments are convincing, and the experimental results are well-supported. The analysis of the projection step in K-Ascent is particularly insightful. It feels like a complete piece of work, although further experimentation would be beneficial.
* **Clarity:** The paper is largely clear, but the mathematical derivations could be improved for readability. The organization is good, but some sections (like the M-step derivation) feel a bit rushed.
* **Originality:**  The EM approach for learning DPPs is a novel contribution. While eigendecomposition is a standard technique for DPPs, its application within an EM framework to address the kernel learning problem is original and well-justified. The paper appropriately references related work.
* **Significance:** The results are significant. A more robust and efficient method for learning DPPs has the potential to impact a wide range of applications beyond product recommendation, including document summarization, sensor placement, and image search.  The demonstrated improvement over existing methods suggests a real advance in the field.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a fundamentally important and challenging problem.
* Proposes a novel and well-motivated solution.
* Provides theoretical justification for the approach.
* Demonstrates empirical improvements over existing methods.
* Clear and well-organized presentation.

**Con:**

* Derivations could be more accessible.
* Dataset specificity limits generalizability.
* Limited ablation study.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The work represents a significant contribution to the field of DPP learning. While there are minor areas for improvement (clarity in derivations, broader experimentation), the novelty, theoretical soundness, and empirical results strongly support its inclusion in NIPS. The authors successfully address a difficult problem with a clever and well-executed solution, and the potential impact of this work is substantial.
