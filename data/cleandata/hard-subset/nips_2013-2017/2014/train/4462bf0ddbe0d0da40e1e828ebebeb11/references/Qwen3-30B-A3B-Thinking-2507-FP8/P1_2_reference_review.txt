

# Peer Review: "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"

## Summary
This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning the kernel matrix for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) from example subsets. While DPPs are powerful models for subset selection that balance quality and diversity, learning their parameters via maximum likelihood is known to be NP-hard. Previous approaches either restricted the parameter space (e.g., learning scalar weights [2]) or used ensemble methods that lose DPP properties [3]. The authors propose an EM algorithm that exploits the eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel K, avoiding the problematic projection steps required in gradient ascent methods. The paper develops theoretical foundations, presents a novel EM algorithm, and validates it on a baby registry dataset with strong empirical results.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-justified mathematical derivations. The authors correctly identify a key limitation of previous gradient-based approaches (the need for projection steps that often produce degenerate results) and provide a compelling solution. The theoretical development in Sections 3.2-3.5 is rigorous, with clear connections between the DPP properties and the EM framework. The experimental results (Section 4) provide strong empirical support for the claims, showing that the proposed EM algorithm consistently outperforms the gradient ascent baseline (K-Ascent) across multiple metrics. The authors appropriately discuss limitations, such as the need for a good initialization, and demonstrate that the EM algorithm is more robust to initialization than gradient ascent. The paper presents a complete work rather than a position paper, with clear theoretical development, algorithmic implementation, and experimental validation.

## Clarity
The paper is well-written and organized. The introduction clearly establishes the problem and motivation, while the background section provides sufficient mathematical foundation for readers unfamiliar with DPPs. The EM algorithm is presented in a logical sequence, with each step properly motivated. The experimental section is clear and directly addresses the claims made in the theoretical section. Minor improvements could be made to clarify the Stiefel manifold update in Equation (21) and the connection between the k-DPP in Lemma 1 and standard DPP algorithms. The authors provide sufficient detail for experts to reproduce their results, including algorithm pseudocode and implementation details.

## Originality
The paper makes a significant contribution to the literature on DPP learning. While previous work has explored restricted learning scenarios for DPPs (e.g., [2, 3, 8]), the authors present the first method that learns an unconstrained, non-parametric kernel matrix without requiring problematic projection steps. The key insight—leveraging the eigendecomposition to develop an EM algorithm—represents a novel approach to this long-standing problem. The paper clearly differentiates its contribution from prior work in the introduction and related work section, demonstrating a thorough understanding of the field. The approach is not merely a combination of existing techniques but a fundamentally new perspective on DPP learning.

## Significance
The significance of this work is substantial. DPPs are widely used in applications requiring subset selection with diversity (document summarization, product recommendation, sensor placement, etc.), and the ability to efficiently learn their parameters from data is crucial for practical adoption. The proposed EM algorithm provides both better performance (as shown in the experiments) and faster convergence than gradient-based methods, making it a valuable tool for practitioners. The paper's results on the baby registry dataset demonstrate clear practical advantages, with EM outperforming gradient ascent by 3.7% on average in test log-likelihood and being 2.1 times faster on average. This represents a meaningful advancement in the state of the art for DPP learning.

## Strengths
- Novel EM approach that avoids the problematic projection steps of gradient ascent
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear connections to DPP properties
- Comprehensive experimental validation on a real-world dataset
- Demonstrated advantages in both performance and computational efficiency
- Clear distinction from prior work with proper citations

## Weaknesses
- Limited to a single dataset for experimental validation (though the dataset is substantial with 13 categories and 8,585 sub-registries)
- Some technical details (e.g., the Stiefel manifold update) could be more thoroughly explained
- The paper doesn't explore the behavior of the algorithm on smaller datasets where the "data-poor" setting (n = 2N) was mentioned but not fully investigated

## Recommendation
This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of probabilistic modeling, particularly for DPPs. The proposed EM algorithm addresses a long-standing limitation in DPP learning with both theoretical novelty and practical significance. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides strong empirical evidence for its claims. The work advances the state of the art in DPP learning in a demonstrable way, making it highly relevant to the NIPS community.

I recommend acceptance with confidence. The paper would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings, providing a useful tool for researchers and practitioners working with DPPs. The authors have made a substantial contribution to the field that will likely be widely adopted.

## Final Score: 8 (Strong Accept)

The paper is of high quality, makes a significant contribution, and would be of great interest to the NIPS community. The experimental results are convincing, the theoretical development is sound, and the practical significance is clear.
