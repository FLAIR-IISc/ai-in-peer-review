Summary
The paper tackles maximum-likelihood learning of Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) without restrictive parameterizations. It proposes an EM-style algorithm that optimizes in the eigenspace of the marginal kernel K, introducing a hidden variable J corresponding to a subset of eigenvectors (the “elementary DPP” mixture view). The E-step sets q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y) and shows q is a k-DPP with a derived kernel. The M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form via k-DPP marginals and takes a Stiefel-manifold gradient step for eigenvectors. The approach aims to avoid the degeneracy associated with projection steps in naive projected gradient ascent (KA). Empirically, on a baby registry dataset, the EM approach improves test log-likelihood and runtime versus KA, especially when off-diagonal structure is important.

Relation to prior work
- Builds on the DPP mixture view via eigen-decomposition (Macchi; Hough et al.; Kulesza & Taskar). Uses k-DPP machinery.
- Contrasts with: (i) diagonal reweighting/convex scaling of L, (ii) linear mixtures of fixed DPPs, (iii) Bayesian parametric inference for L. The proposed algorithm seeks nonparametric K directly.
- Optimization on Stiefel manifolds follows Edelman et al.

Quality
Strengths
- Sensible use of the DPP eigen-mixture latent variable J to derive an EM lower bound.
- Avoids fragile PSD/eigenvalue clipping in KA, a real issue when gradients become diagonal.
- The Stiefel manifold update for V is appropriate; using eigenspace improves interpretability.
- Complexity O(n N k^2) for eigenvalue updates/first-order eigenvector gradients is attractive when k ≪ N.
- Empirical evidence (albeit limited) that KA can collapse to near-diagonal solutions and that EM is more robust and faster on the given dataset.

Major technical issues and ambiguities
- Equation (19) appears incorrect as stated: q(j ∈ J | Yi) = ∑r v̂r(j)^2 over r = 1..N yields 1 for all j (since V̂ is orthonormal), which cannot be a valid marginal. For a k-DPP, marginals equal the diagonal of the k-rank projection (sum over the k selected eigenvectors), not the sum over all N. The paper needs the exact, computable expression (typically involving the eigenvalues of QYi and a DP to form the marginal kernel). As written, the key update for λ (Eq. 18) is not operationally correct.
- E vs M objective inconsistency: the update rule for V uses G = ∂F/∂V (Algorithm 2) but the Stiefel update (Eq. 21) is written with ∂L/∂V. The acceptance criterion in the line search checks L, while δ uses F for convergence. This mixture of objectives breaks the standard EM monotonicity guarantee and is confusing; a clear statement of what is guaranteed to increase (if anything) is missing.
- Algorithmic line search steps are likely incorrect: in both Algorithm 1 and Algorithm 2 the inner loop uses η ← η^2 to reduce the step size. Starting from η = 1 this does nothing; for η < 1 it collapses to 0 extremely fast. Standard backtracking uses η ← βη with β ∈ (0,1). Moreover, Algorithm 2 sets η ← 2η after acceptance, which can destabilize. These details materially affect reproducibility and outcomes.
- Jensen bound derivation: Eq. (11) says concavity of F in q “follows from the concavity of KL divergence”; KL is convex, hence −KL is concave. Please correct.
- Handling λj → 1: the paper admits QYi becomes ill-conditioned/infinite (Eq. 17) and uses ad hoc “tightening” to keep λ < 1. No principled bound or update safeguard is provided; this is critical for stability.
- Use of |det(K − IY)| (Eq. 3): introducing absolute value makes the log-likelihood non-differentiable where the determinant changes sign. The implications for gradient-based KA and for the EM acceptance test are not discussed. The standard formula P(Y) via det(KY) and det(I − K) or det(I − K) times Schur complement is numerically cleaner.
- Complexity claims: the O(n N k^2) derivations rely on the correctness of Eq. (19). The eigenvector gradient (Eq. 20) is only valid for the first gradient step per M-step, by the authors’ admission, and repeated E-steps during line search add overhead. The final cost O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) may be optimistic without careful accounting.
- Proof and derivation gaps: Lemma 1, Eq. (19), Eq. (20) are deferred to the supplement; the main text needs enough detail for verification (at least correct statements and conditions). As Eq. (19) is likely wrong, the core M-step is compromised.

Clarity
- Notational overload: L denotes both the L-ensemble kernel and the log-likelihood function L(K); switch between L and K is occasionally confusing.
- Inconsistent symbols: use of F vs L in gradients and updates (see above).
- Several typos and formatting issues: “param-eterized”, “diversityseeking”, “negelecting”, “Justies” (ref title), “unitary” for real orthogonal, stray “Tuesday, August 5, 14,” spacing and sub/superscript inconsistencies (IY i, H Yi), inconsistent bold/italics. References are largely incomplete (missing years/venues/pages), duplicates, and inconsistent formatting.
- Algorithm boxes: missing hyperparameters and stopping criteria details; c is not defined precisely (absolute vs relative tolerance?).

Originality
- The EM idea via eigen-mixture J is interesting and, to my knowledge, not standard in DPP learning literature at the time. However, many components (k-DPP conditioning, Stiefel optimization) are known. Novelty is moderate and rests on a correct and practical realization of the EM scheme.

Significance
- Potentially useful if it robustly avoids projection degeneracy and scales better when k ≪ N. Current empirical evaluation is narrow (one real-world domain), with no synthetic experiments to validate parameter recovery, no ablations, and no comparison to other learning approaches (Bayesian parametric, mixtures, diagonal scaling). Figures are referenced but not present; absolute log-likelihoods and variability are not reported.

Reproducibility
- Code link given, but the paper lacks sufficient algorithmic detail (correct λ update, line search, ε for λ ceiling, stopping criteria). Without the supplement, core steps are unverifiable.

Questions for authors
- Please correct Eq. (19) and provide the exact, computable expression for q(j ∈ J | Y) for the k-DPP along with complexity.
- Clarify which objective is used for line search acceptance and convergence, and whether any monotonicity is guaranteed.
- Fix the line search update rules (η schedule).
- How do you set the λ < 1 margin? Sensitivity?
- Provide more baselines (e.g., Affandi et al. Bayesian parametric learning, diagonal scaling), synthetic recovery tests, and additional datasets.
- Discuss numerical stability of using log |det(K − IY)| and alternatives.

Pros
- Interesting application of EM via the eigen-mixture variable J.
- Avoids problematic PSD projection; appropriate use of Stiefel updates.
- Promising empirical robustness and speed in the presented setting.

Cons
- Apparent error in a central equation (Eq. 19) and several algorithmic inconsistencies.
- Mixing objectives (F vs L) and non-standard EM that lacks guarantees.
- Limited experiments and missing baselines; incomplete/inconsistent references and multiple typos.

Recommendation
Reject. While the core idea is promising, the paper contains substantive technical and algorithmic issues, unclear or incorrect derivations, and insufficient experimental validation. With corrected mathematics, clearer and consistent optimization procedures, and stronger empirical evaluation, this work could be valuable in a future submission.
