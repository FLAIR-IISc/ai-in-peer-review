- Addresses ML learning of full DPP kernels via an EM-style algorithm operating in the eigenspace of marginal kernel K.  
- Introduces latent subset J of eigenvectors; E-step shows q(J|Y) is a k-DPP, M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates and a manifold gradient step for eigenvectors.  
- Novel contribution: using the mixture/eigendecomposition view to derive a k-DPP E-step and closed-form eigenvalue updates.  
- Computational complexity: data-dependent cost O(n N k^2) per iteration when subset sizes k ≪ N.  
- Fills a gap between partial (item-quality) learning and parametric/Bayesian DPP treatments; situates relative to Kulesza & Taskar and Affandi et al.  
- Theory: latent-variable/Jensen bound is standard; key claims plausible but many proofs are deferred to the supplement.  
- Eigenvalue update: elegant and efficient via low-rank structure (O(N k^2) per example).  
- Eigenvector update: derived only for initial gradient; algorithm uses a single Stiefel manifold gradient step—effectively an inexact M-step (GEM).  
- Convergence: monotonic increase of the variational bound is not guaranteed; method should be presented as GEM with a short convergence discussion.  
- Numerical details: λ≈1 clamping handled but sensitivity not analyzed; matrix-exponential retraction and step-size search incur O(N^3) costs—cheaper retractions (Cayley/QR) suggested.  
- Baselines: projected gradient (KA) is reasonable but could be strengthened with quasi-Newton or PSD-preserving parameterizations and low-rank baselines.  
- Clarity: well written overall but needs explicit GEM framing, a sketch of Lemma 1, clearer derivation of Eq. (19), consistent matrix notation, and reported stopping/line-search details.  
- Originality: combination of EM and manifold optimization for unconstrained DPP MLE is a meaningful methodological contribution.  
- Significance: promising for small-k regimes, but empirical gains are modest and experimental scope is limited.  
- Experiments: solid engineering and insightful analyses, but limited to one domain, lacking ablations (k, initialization, seeds), synthetic recovery, and comparisons to stronger optimizers or low-rank/Bayesian methods.  
- Pros: conceptually clean EM/GEM formulation, avoids projection degeneracies, practical when k small, code/data provided.  
- Cons: inexact M-step weakens theoretical guarantees, limited empirical breadth, modest improvements over baseline, some proofs and implementation details missing.  
- Suggestions: explicitly recast as GEM and add convergence remarks; strengthen baselines and add synthetic/scale experiments; evaluate downstream metrics; try cheaper Stiefel retractions and analyze λ-clamp sensitivity; add implementation details.  
- Overall: technically interesting and relevant with a strong core idea; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying GEM status and broadening empirical validation.
