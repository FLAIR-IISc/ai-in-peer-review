This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing the challenge of efficiently learning the kernel matrix *L* from example subsets. DPPs are well-suited for subset selection problems requiring both quality and diversity, and have seen increasing use in ML. Previous approaches have either partially learned *L* (e.g., learning scalar weights) or relied on restrictive parameterizations or ensemble methods, often sacrificing computational benefits. The authors argue that directly optimizing the likelihood of *L* via gradient ascent requires a projection step that can lead to degenerate solutions (near-diagonal *L*), and propose an EM algorithm leveraging the eigendecomposition of *L* to avoid this issue.

The core idea of exploiting the eigendecomposition of *L* and framing the learning problem in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors is a strong contribution. The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing methods and motivates the need for a more robust learning algorithm. The derivation of the EM update rules, particularly the closed-form update for the eigenvalues, is well-presented, and the computational analysis (asymptotic runtime) is a valuable addition. The experimental validation on baby registry data is relevant and demonstrates the practical benefits of the proposed approach, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between items are important and/or data is limited.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The EM approach for learning DPPs without restrictive assumptions on *L* is a significant contribution. Leveraging eigendecomposition is a clever idea.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivations appear correct, and the analysis of the asymptotic runtime is well-justified. The inclusion of Lemma 1 (k-DPPs) further supports the technical depth.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The motivation and background are clearly explained.
* **Significance:** The ability to learn unconstrained DPPs has the potential to improve performance in a variety of applications, especially those requiring diverse subset selection. Addressing the limitations of gradient ascent is important for practical usability.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the advantages of the EM algorithm over gradient ascent, especially in challenging settings. The analysis of runtime improvements is also valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Initialization Sensitivity:** While the paper addresses the shortcomings of gradient ascent’s sensitivity to initialization, the reliance on a “good” initial kernel (Wishart or moment-matching) is still present. A more in-depth investigation of different initialization strategies and their impact on convergence would be beneficial.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** Implementing the EM algorithm, particularly the Stiefel manifold update for the eigenvectors, can be complex. While the authors mention using a simplification, this might still pose a barrier to adoption. A more detailed discussion of implementation considerations would be helpful.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments focus solely on the baby registry dataset. While the results are convincing within this context, it would be helpful to evaluate the algorithm on other datasets with different characteristics to assess its generalizability.
* **Discussion of Hyperparameters:** The paper does not delve into the sensitivity of the algorithm to hyperparameters like the step size (η) in the Stiefel manifold update or the convergence criterion. An analysis of how these parameters affect performance would enhance the paper.
* **Connection to Related Work:** While the paper cites relevant work, the discussion could be strengthened by explicitly acknowledging and contrasting the proposed approach with other recent advances in DPP learning, such as methods based on kernel alignment or sparse representations.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for learning DPPs.
* It addresses a significant challenge in the field and offers a practical solution.
* The experimental results demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key ideas.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The algorithm's reliance on good initialization could limit its applicability in some scenarios.
* The limited scope of the experiments makes it difficult to assess generalizability.
* The implementation complexity might hinder adoption.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of DPP learning. The EM algorithm is a well-motivated and technically sound approach, and the experimental results are encouraging. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* Investigate alternative initialization strategies and their impact on convergence.
* Provide a more detailed discussion of implementation considerations, including the Stiefel manifold update.
* Evaluate the algorithm on additional datasets to assess generalizability.
* Analyze the sensitivity of the algorithm to hyperparameters.
* Expand the discussion of related work, explicitly contrasting the proposed approach with recent advances in DPP learning.
