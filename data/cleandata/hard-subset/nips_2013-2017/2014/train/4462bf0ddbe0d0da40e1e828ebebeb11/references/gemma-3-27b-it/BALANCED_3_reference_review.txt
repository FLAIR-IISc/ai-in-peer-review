This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing the challenge of learning the kernel matrix L from example subsets, a problem conjectured to be NP-hard. The authors motivate the work by highlighting limitations of previous approaches – either partial learning of L or restrictive parameterizations – and demonstrate that naive gradient ascent suffers from issues related to maintaining the positive semi-definiteness of L. The core contribution is an EM algorithm leveraging the eigendecomposition of L, avoiding the problematic projection step of gradient ascent and offering potential asymptotic speedups.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively establishes the context, motivates the problem, and summarizes the contributions. The background section provides a concise yet sufficient overview of DPPs and relevant mathematical notation. The learning algorithms section is detailed and well-explained, with clear derivations of the EM update equations. The experiments section is comprehensive, comparing the proposed EM method to projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) on a baby registry dataset. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The EM approach to learning DPPs without restrictive parameterizations is a significant contribution. The use of eigendecomposition to avoid the projection step is a clever and impactful idea.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear correct and well-justified. The algorithm is clearly presented and the complexity analysis is reasonable.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the EM algorithm compared to K-Ascent, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between items are important and when dealing with limited training data. The ablation study with different initialization strategies is valuable.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow, even for readers unfamiliar with DPPs. The notation is consistent and the explanations are clear.
* **Significance:** Learning DPPs is a relevant problem with applications in various domains, and a more robust and efficient learning algorithm (like the proposed EM method) is a valuable advancement. 

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While the authors claim asymptotic speedup, the stated complexity, O(T1nNk2 + T1T2N3), still has a significant dependence on N3, and k can be relatively large. A more detailed discussion of the practical implications of this complexity, especially for very large datasets, would be helpful. It would also be useful to compare the empirical runtime with other DPP learning methods in a more comprehensive benchmark.
* **Initialization Sensitivity:** The performance of both EM and K-Ascent appears to be sensitive to the initialization strategy. While the authors explore two options, a more thorough investigation of different initialization techniques and their impact on convergence and performance could be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are focused solely on the baby registry dataset. While this allows for a controlled comparison, it would be beneficial to evaluate the algorithm on other datasets with different characteristics to assess its generalizability.
* **Lack of Comparison to State-of-the-Art:**  The paper primarily compares against K-Ascent, which isn’t necessarily the strongest baseline.  A comparison to recent Bayesian methods like [8] (mentioned in the introduction) or more sophisticated gradient-based methods with adaptive step size selection would provide a stronger assessment of the EM algorithm's advantages.
* **Stiefel Manifold Update:** The simplification of the Stiefel manifold update (neglecting the Hessian) might compromise accuracy. A discussion on the potential impact of this simplification on performance would be valuable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for learning DPPs.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
* The problem addressed is relevant and the solution has the potential for significant impact.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational complexity remains a concern for very large datasets.
* The experiments are limited in scope and lack comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
* The simplification of the Stiefel manifold update might affect performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of DPP learning. The proposed EM algorithm is a significant improvement over existing methods, offering a more robust and efficient solution for learning DPPs without restrictive parameterizations. While some weaknesses remain, such as the computational complexity and limited scope of experiments, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.** The authors should address the concerns regarding computational complexity, provide a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods, and discuss the potential impact of the Stiefel manifold update simplification. Additionally, exploring a wider range of initialization strategies would be beneficial.  Including runtime comparisons in a table format would also strengthen the analysis.
