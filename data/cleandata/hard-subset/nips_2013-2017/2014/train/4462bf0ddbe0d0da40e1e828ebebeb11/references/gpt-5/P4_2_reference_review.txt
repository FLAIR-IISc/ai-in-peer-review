Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses maximum likelihood learning of determinantal point processes (DPPs) without restrictive parameterizations of the kernel. It proposes an EM-style algorithm that optimizes the marginal kernel K via its eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T. The key idea is to exploit the standard mixture representation of a DPP as a mixture over “elementary” DPPs with eigenvalues 1 (equation 7), introducing the hidden variable J denoting the subset of eigenvectors used to generate a sample. The E-step sets q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y). The authors show this posterior is a k-DPP supported on sets of size k = |Y| with kernel QY = R ZY R, where ZY = VY^T VY and R = diag(sqrt(λ/(1−λ))). In the M-step, eigenvalues admit a closed-form update: λj = (1/n) ∑i q(j ∈ J | Yi), and these marginals are computed efficiently using k-DPP marginalization, yielding O(n N k^2) cost where k = max |Yi|. For eigenvectors, the paper derives a tractable expression for the gradient at q=p (equation 20), and performs a single Stiefel-manifold update step using a matrix exponential (equation 21) with line search monitored on the true log-likelihood L.

As a baseline, the paper considers projected gradient ascent on K under PSD and eigenvalue ≤1 constraints (KA). The authors argue that projection can zero many eigenvalues, causing near-diagonal K from which off-diagonal structure (diversity) is hard to recover because the gradient remains diagonal.

Empirically, on a real Amazon baby registries dataset (13 categories, average N≈71, n≈8585), EM improves median test log-likelihood over KA with Wishart initialization by ~3.7% on average and more on categories with stronger off-diagonal structure. With a moments-matching initialization, EM and KA are similar in log-likelihood on full data, but EM is notably better in a data-poor regime (n≈2N). EM is also about 2× faster in practice. The authors argue the method is more robust and asymptotically faster than KA.

Relative to prior work: earlier methods include learning only item-quality weights (diagonal scaling) [2], mixtures of fixed L-ensembles [3], and Bayesian learning under parametric kernel forms [8]. This paper targets unconstrained learning of a full kernel, preserving DPP structure and its computational benefits. The EM viewpoint leverages known DPP identities [21] and k-DPPs [3].

Quality
- Technical soundness: The EM construction via Jensen’s inequality is standard, and the E-step identification of q(J|Y) as a k-DPP with the provided kernel is consistent with known DPP/k-DPP results. The eigenvalue update is correct and efficiently computable via k-DPP marginals. The eigenvector update uses a gradient valid at q=p and a Stiefel-manifold step; although not a full maximization of the M-step objective, the authors use line search on the true likelihood and re-run the E-step for each trial, which provides a pragmatic safeguard. Formal convergence guarantees (e.g., monotonic increase of the lower bound F across full EM iterations) are not established given the approximate V-step; however, the algorithm checks improvements in L instead of F.
- Experimental evaluation: The dataset is substantial and practical. Comparisons consider two reasonable initializations. The analysis connecting EM gains to off-diagonal importance is informative. However, the baseline set is limited to KA; stronger gradient-based baselines (e.g., parameterizing K via VΛV^T with a logistic transform for λ and manifold optimization for V, mirror descent, or factorization-based PSD parameterizations) are not evaluated. Additional datasets beyond a single application domain would strengthen evidence.
- Reproducibility: Core algorithmic details are provided, with proofs relegated to the supplement. Complexity claims are explicit. Code is mentioned as available.

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well organized, with intuitive explanations of DPP structure and the EM construction. Suggestions to improve clarity:
- Be explicit about shapes when defining U = V^T and UY, and briefly justify the k-DPP posterior derivation in the main text.
- Clarify why equation (20) is valid only at q=p and discuss implications for convergence.
- Summarize the k-DPP marginalization computation yielding O(N k^2) per Yi in the main text.
- Specify safeguards for λ near 1 and how the “tightened” constraint is implemented.

Originality
Viewing DPP MLE through an EM lens and optimizing over eigenvalues/eigenvectors without restrictive parameterizations is, to the best of my knowledge, novel. Using the k-DPP posterior as the E-step and combining closed-form λ updates with Stiefel-manifold updates for V is an original and coherent algorithmic design.

Significance
Unconstrained learning of DPP kernels is an important problem, as DPPs are widely used for subset selection with diversity. The proposed method addresses a known difficulty (projection-induced degeneracy) and provides computational advantages. If broadly validated, it is likely to be adopted and built upon. The current empirical scope is promising but narrow; extension to additional domains and stronger baselines would further establish impact.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually clean EM formulation leveraging DPP structure; avoids problematic projections.
- Efficient and scalable updates for λ and a principled update for V on the Stiefel manifold.
- Empirical gains in likelihood and runtime; robustness in data-poor regimes.
- Clear exposition and connection to prior DPP results.

Cons for acceptance
- Approximate M-step for V without convergence guarantees for the lower bound; reliance on line search on L.
- Limited baselines; no comparisons to alternative parameterizations or advanced constrained optimizers.
- Single application domain; no synthetic recovery experiments or downstream task evaluations beyond likelihood/MAP illustration.
- Some technical details are deferred to the supplement; minor notational ambiguities.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Can the V-step gradient be extended beyond q=p, or can a stochastic approximation to the expectation in equation (13) be used to yield an unbiased gradient?
- What empirical behavior is observed for larger N and k close to N? How does the N^3 cost from manifold updates scale in practice?
- Would reparameterizing λ via a logistic transform and optimizing unconstrained variables improve stability when λ approaches 1?
- Provide additional baselines (e.g., optimizing over V, λ directly with manifold methods and logistic λ, or low-rank factorizations) and results on other datasets (e.g., summarization, image search).

Overall, the paper is technically solid, clearly written, and proposes a novel and practically useful method for DPP learning, with evidence of robustness and efficiency.
