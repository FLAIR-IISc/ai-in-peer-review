- Proposal: EM algorithm learns DPP kernel via eigendecomposition, avoiding projection steps and improving modeling of negative interactions and runtime; validated on Amazon baby registry data.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous EM derivation that handles non-convex eigenvector optimization and avoids degeneracy seen in gradient ascent.  
- Experiments: Thorough experimental validation with multiple initializations, showing consistent improvements in test log-likelihood and runtime.  
- Runtime analysis: EM has better asymptotic complexity than projected gradient ascent, especially when subset size k << ground set size N.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with clear background, logical flow, pseudocode, and effective figures.  
- Originality: Novel use of eigendecomposition in an EM framework for non-parametric DPP kernel learning, distinct from prior parametric or Bayesian methods.  
- Significance: Substantial practical impact for subset selection tasks; improves model quality and efficiency and enables future extensions.  
- Strength 1: Avoids degeneracy issues of gradient-based methods via a well-motivated EM approach.  
- Strength 2: Strong theoretical analysis supporting algorithmic choices.  
- Strength 3: Careful and comprehensive experimental design and analysis.  
- Strength 4: Clear exposition and excellent presentation.  
- Strength 5: Demonstrated practical gains on a real-world application.  
- Weakness 1: Relies on a reasonable initialization strategy and lacks exploration of automatic initialization methods.  
- Weakness 2: Runtime claims assume k << N and lack empirical evaluation for cases where k approaches N.  
- Weakness 3: Experiments are concentrated on one application domain (product recommendation).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept for NIPS — technically sound, original, clear, and significant.  
- Suggested improvements: Add discussion on extending to structured DPPs, run synthetic-data experiments with known ground-truth kernels, and clarify the relationship between k and complexity.
