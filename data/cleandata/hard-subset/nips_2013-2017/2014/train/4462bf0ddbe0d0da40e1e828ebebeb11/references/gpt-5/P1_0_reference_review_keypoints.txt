- Paper proposes an EM-style method for maximum-likelihood learning of full unconstrained DPP kernels by treating eigenvector subsets J as latent and optimizing in K’s eigendecomposition.  
- Baseline is projected gradient ascent on K with 0 ≤ K ≤ I; proposed EM optimizes eigenvalues in closed form and eigenvectors via a Stiefel-manifold gradient step.  
- E-step identifies q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), a k-DPP with compact representation enabling efficient marginals for eigenvalue updates.  
- M-step: closed-form eigenvalue updates computable in O(n N k^2) and a single gradient step for eigenvectors with backtracking line search to ensure likelihood improvement.  
- Method avoids projection-induced degeneracies (near-diagonal K) seen in the baseline and can be asymptotically faster when k ≪ N.  
- Empirical results on a large Amazon baby registry dataset (N≈71, n≈8.6k) show EM improves held-out log-likelihood and is about 2× faster in wall-clock time versus KA.  
- EM is especially beneficial in categories with significant off-diagonal structure and remains more robust in data-poor regimes (n ≈ 2N).  
- Stronger moment-matching initialization narrows the gap, but EM retains robustness advantages.  
- Technical derivations (EM lower bound, k-DPP identification) are sound and lead to efficient computations; eigenvector update uses a principled Stiefel step.  
- Runtime analysis is plausible: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), with N^3 costs from repeated manifold eigendecompositions.  
- Experiments are thorough against the main unconstrained baseline (KA) under multiple initializations and regimes; dataset and code promised.  
- Caveat: V-update uses only a single gradient step per M-step and recomputes E-step during line search; no formal convergence guarantee to a stationary point is provided.  
- Scalability concern: repeated O(N^3) manifold updates may not scale to larger N (hundreds–thousands); approximate updates (low-rank, Nyström) are not explored.  
- Baselines limited: lacks comparisons to diagonal reweighting and parametric/Bayesian approaches, which would contextualize practical trade-offs.  
- Clarity is generally good and well organized, but some derivations are deferred to the supplement and would benefit from brief sketches.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos in pseudocode (e.g., line-search notation), unclear numerical safeguards (λ cap, conditioning of HYi), and missing practical constants in complexity terms.  
- Originality: EM over latent eigenvector-set J for full DPP kernel learning appears novel and well-motivated.  
- Significance: advances unconstrained DPP learning by avoiding PSD projection issues, improving robustness and runtime for moderate N and small k, with a compelling application.  
- Recommended additions: convergence statement/analysis under one-step M and backtracking, scalability experiments or approximations for large N, more baselines, and application-oriented evaluation metrics.  
- Verdict: Solid, novel, and practically useful contribution; acceptance recommended despite scalability and baseline-comparison gaps.
