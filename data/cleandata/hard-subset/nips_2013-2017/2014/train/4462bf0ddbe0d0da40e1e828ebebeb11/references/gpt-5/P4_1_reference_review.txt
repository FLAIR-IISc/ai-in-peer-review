Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses maximum-likelihood learning of determinantal point processes (DPPs) over a ground set of size N, parameterized by an N×N PSD kernel. It focuses on learning an unconstrained, nonparametric kernel without restrictive parameterizations. The authors propose an expectation–maximization (EM) algorithm that operates in the spectral domain of the marginal kernel K, updating eigenvalues and eigenvectors while avoiding the problematic PSD-projection encountered in naive projected gradient ascent on K.

Key ideas:
- Baseline: Projected gradient ascent (KA) on K with constraints 0 ≼ K ≼ I using the gradient ∑i (K − IYi)−1 and repeated eigenvalue clipping. The paper argues projection can induce many zero eigenvalues and near-diagonal K, after which the gradient remains diagonal, trapping the optimizer in poor local optima.
- EM formulation: Uses the well-known representation of a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs (eigenvalues of 1 on a subset J of eigenvectors). Introduces J as a latent variable and applies Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the log-likelihood.
- E-step: Sets q(J|Yi)=pK(J|Yi). The conditional distribution over J is shown to be a k-DPP (k=|Yi|) with a specific non-marginal kernel QYi, enabling compact representation and efficient computations via k-DPP marginalization results.
- M-step eigenvalues: Closed-form update λj = (1/n)∑i q(j∈J|Yi); the marginal probabilities are computed efficiently from the eigendecomposition of QYi, yielding an O(nNk^2) cost where k=maxi|Yi|.
- M-step eigenvectors: A single gradient step on V per EM iteration using a Stiefel manifold update (orthogonality-preserving multiplicative update with matrix exponential). The first-gradient formula is derived to be computable in O(nNk^2).
- Runtime: Per-iteration complexity O(nNk^2 + T2N^3) versus KA’s O(nN^3 + T2N^3), giving an asymptotic advantage when k ≪ N.

Experiments on an Amazon baby registries dataset (13 categories; average N≈71, n≈8.6k) compare KA and EM under two initializations: a Wishart-based random kernel and a moment-matching kernel that fits item and pairwise marginals. EM improves median test log-likelihood over KA by 3.7% with Wishart initialization and 0.4% with moment-matching, with clearer gains in categories exhibiting stronger off-diagonal structure (as quantified by a Frobenius/diagonal ratio). In a data-poor regime (n≈2N), EM shows larger improvements (average 4.5%). EM is also observed to be ≈2.1× faster than KA.

This work builds on foundational DPP literature (e.g., Kulesza & Taskar; Hough et al.) and k-DPPs, and contrasts with prior learning approaches that either learn only item-quality scalings (convex), mixtures of fixed kernels (losing DPP structure), or Bayesian parametric forms (restrictive). The spectral EM with k-DPP marginals appears novel in enabling full, unconstrained learning.

Strengths
- Technical contribution: A principled EM formulation leveraging the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view and k-DPP marginalization to produce efficient eigenvalue updates and a viable eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold.
- Sound diagnostics of KA: Clear explanation of projection-induced degeneracy (diagonal kernels leading to diagonal gradients and poor diversity modeling).
- Efficiency: Per-iteration complexity improvement when k≪N and empirical speedups over KA.
- Empirical evidence: Improvements in held-out log-likelihood and qualitative examples showing better modeling of negative interactions; robustness to initialization and data scarcity.
- Clarity of core algorithmic ideas: The decomposition, E-step characterization, and update rules are stated cleanly, with supplementary proofs and code availability.

Weaknesses
- Limited empirical scope: Evaluation is on a single application domain, with baselines restricted to KA. Comparisons to alternative learning paradigms (e.g., Bayesian parametric DPPs or mixture-of-kernels approaches) are absent.
- Convergence guarantees: The M-step for V is an inexact, single gradient step with step-size search. While the inner loop accepts steps that improve likelihood, there is no formal monotonicity or convergence rate analysis for the full EM procedure. The algorithm monitors F for convergence but uses L for line search; the mismatch could be clarified, and monotonicity in L across outer iterations is not proved.
- Computational details deferred: Key steps (e.g., derivation and cost of Eq. 19; numerical handling near λ≈1) are deferred to the supplement. Reproducibility is aided by code but a self-contained complexity analysis would strengthen the paper.
- Scalability limits: Although asymptotics favor EM for small k, the per-iteration cost O(nNk^2) may be large for high n and moderate k; the approach’s behavior for large N (hundreds to thousands) is not demonstrated.
- Sensitivity and ablations: The effect of multiple V updates per M-step, alternative line-search strategies, or different initialization strategies beyond the two considered is not explored.

Evaluation by criteria
- Quality: The methodology is mathematically grounded; the gradient and EM derivations are consistent with DPP theory. The KA failure mode is correctly identified. The lack of a convergence proof and limited baselines are the main quality gaps.
- Clarity: Generally well written and organized. Some notation (e.g., toggling between U=V^T, VYi definitions) could be streamlined. Explicitly stating assumptions and constraints (e.g., λ<1) in the main text would aid clarity.
- Originality: The spectral EM with k-DPP-based E-step and efficient eigenvalue updates appears novel. Using Stiefel manifold optimization for DPP eigenvectors in this context is a meaningful contribution.
- Significance: Learning full DPP kernels is important for applications requiring diversity. The approach has potential impact given improved robustness and efficiency, though broader benchmarks would better substantiate significance.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add baselines to contextualize performance (e.g., Bayesian parametric DPP learning, mixtures of fixed kernels, low-rank parameterizations).
- Provide theoretical analysis of convergence properties for the inexact M-step and the acceptance criterion (linking increases in L and F).
- Expand empirical evaluation to other domains (e.g., summarization, image search) and larger N; report wall-clock per-iteration and total time, memory, and sensitivity to k and n.
- Include ablations: number of V updates per M-step, line-search strategies, and different initialization schemes; examine robustness across random seeds.
- Detail numerical stabilization (handling λ→1), and summarize key computational steps from the supplement in the main text for reproducibility.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and principled EM approach to unconstrained DPP learning with clear technical insights.
- Addresses a real shortcoming of projected gradient methods; improves robustness and efficiency.
- Empirical gains in likelihood and runtime; method likely useful to practitioners working with DPPs.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited experimental breadth and baselines; unclear generality beyond the presented dataset.
- No formal convergence guarantees; some reliance on supplemental material for core computational details.
- Scalability to much larger settings remains to be demonstrated.

Overall, the paper makes a technically sound and original contribution to DPP learning with promising empirical results, though it would benefit from broader evaluation and strengthened theoretical analysis.
