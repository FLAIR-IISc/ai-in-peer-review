- Proposes an EM algorithm using eigendecomposition to learn full DPP kernels, avoiding projection issues of gradient ascent.
- Demonstrates theoretical (asymptotic runtime) and empirical (baby registry) benefits over projected gradient ascent.
- Correctly situates work relative to prior DPP learning methods and highlights their limitations.
- Contribution: full L learning without restrictive parameterizations or ensembles by leveraging eigenstructure.
- Novelty: EM approach that avoids PSD projection and reduces tendency toward degenerate, diagonal kernels.
- Theoretical justification: connects EM to DPP hidden-variable formulation and derives clear M-step updates.
- Empirical validation: experiments show EM outperforms projected gradient ascent and runtime improvements.
- Paper is generally well-written and explanations are accessible to non-experts.
- Addresses a core, broadly applicable challenge in DPP learning.
- Complexity analysis lacks discussion of constant factors and practical impact of dependence on k (largest subset size).
- Initialization sensitivity remains; moment-matching helps but robustness across datasets is not fully explored.
- Experiments are limited to a product recommendation task and should include other domains for broader validation.
- Step-size selection for the EM algorithm is under-explained and needs more practical detail.
- Minor clarity issue: Equation (21) and the “neglecting second-order information” phrase need clarification.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem addressed, novel grounded approach, strong empirical results, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete complexity analysis, limited experimental scope, remaining initialization dependence.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution; weaknesses are minor.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand computational complexity discussion regarding k, explore more initialization strategies, extend experiments to other domains, clarify step-size selection, and detail the Stiefel manifold update.
