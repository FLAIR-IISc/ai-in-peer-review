- Proposes an EM algorithm that learns DPP kernel L by leveraging its eigendecomposition and a hidden eigenvector variable to avoid problematic PSD projection steps.  
- EM introduces a latent variable for eigenvectors, enabling stable optimization and bypassing degenerate near-diagonal solutions from naive gradient ascent.  
- Provides a clear derivation of EM updates for eigenvalues and eigenvectors and analyzes computational complexity.  
- Empirically shows EM outperforms K-Ascent in log-likelihood and runtime, especially in data-poor regimes and when negative interactions matter.  
- Demonstrates practical gains linked to a dataset-specific parameter d, explaining when EM helps most.  
- Well-written, well-organized paper with publicly available code and thoughtful discussion of initialization and step-size choices.  
- Eigenvalue M-step is closed-form, but the eigenvector update is approximated via a single gradient step and step-size search, which may limit optimality.  
- Reliance on Stiefel-manifold-related updates is acknowledged but not deeply analyzed, leaving practical performance implications underexplored.  
- Scalability concerns: runtime O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) can be prohibitive for large N or large subset sizes k.  
- Experiments use a single baby-registry dataset, limiting evidence of generalizability across diverse domains.  
- Comparison baseline is mainly K-Ascent, with limited evaluation against other modern or specialized DPP learning methods.  
- The application of EM to DPP learning via eigendecomposition is novel and constitutes the main technical contribution.  
- Work is potentially significant for recommendation, summarization, and other tasks where capturing negative interactions is important.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and well-justified work that merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: address scalability discussion, broaden datasets, and provide more detail/analysis on the eigenvector-update approximation.  
- Recommendation: Accept (minor revisions).
