## Review of "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning the kernel matrix *L* for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) from example subsets. Existing methods either partially learn *L* (e.g., learning scalar weights) or rely on restrictive parameterizations, sacrificing the attractive computational properties of full DPPs. The authors propose an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm leveraging the eigendecomposition of *L* to bypass the problematic projection step that plagues naive gradient ascent methods for maximizing the likelihood objective.  The core idea is to introduce a hidden variable representing the eigenvectors contributing to observed subsets, enabling a more stable and potentially faster learning process.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Learning DPPs is crucial for applications requiring diverse subset selection, and the difficulty of full kernel learning is well-established.
* **Novel Approach:** The EM formulation, based on eigendecomposition and a hidden variable representing eigenvector contributions, is a genuinely novel way to tackle the learning problem.  It sidesteps the pitfalls of gradient ascent with projection.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the EM algorithm, including derivations of the M-step updates and computational complexity analysis.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on baby registry data demonstrate the EM algorithm's superior performance and robustness compared to projected gradient ascent, particularly in settings with strong item interactions and limited data.  The runtime analysis further solidifies its practical advantages.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and organized, with a logical flow from background to algorithm description to experiments.  Mathematical notation is clear and consistently used.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:**  While the algorithm is theoretically sound, its implementation appears complex, relying on efficient marginalization of k-DPPs and careful handling of the Stiefel manifold constraint.  The authors acknowledge this, but a more detailed discussion of implementation challenges could be beneficial.
* **Data Dependency of Gains:** The benefit of the EM algorithm over projected gradient ascent seems highly dependent on the dataset's characteristics (specifically, the strength of item interactions, quantified by *d*). The authors acknowledge this but a deeper understanding of *why* this dependency exists would be valuable.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are confined to a single domain (baby registries).  While this provides a good initial evaluation, extending the experiments to other application areas would strengthen the paper’s claims of generality.
* **Initialization Sensitivity:** While EM is presented as more robust than gradient ascent, the choice of initialization (Wishart or moment matching) still influences performance.  Further investigation of initialization strategies could be insightful.



**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with a well-motivated algorithm and rigorous analysis. The empirical results support the claims, although the dependency on data characteristics is a potential concern.  It's a complete piece of work, presenting a novel solution to a challenging problem.  The authors are transparent about limitations. **(Strong)**
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The authors explain complex concepts in a digestible manner. **(Excellent)**
* **Originality:** The EM formulation for DPP learning is a novel contribution. The authors adequately reference related work, and the approach clearly differs from previous attempts. **(Strong)**
* **Significance:** The results are significant. A robust and efficient algorithm for learning DPPs has the potential to impact a wide range of applications, from recommendation systems to document summarization. It advances the state of the art by overcoming the limitations of existing methods. **(Strong)**

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Solves an important and challenging problem in DPP learning.
* Presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm.
* Demonstrates superior performance and robustness through empirical evaluation.
* Well-written and clearly explained.
* Addresses a niche but impactful area within machine learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Implementation complexity might be a barrier to adoption.
* Performance gains are data-dependent.
* Limited scope of experimental evaluation.



**Overall Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of DPP learning by developing a novel and effective algorithm that overcomes the limitations of existing methods. While the implementation complexity and data dependency are potential concerns, the strengths of the paper outweigh these weaknesses. This work is likely to be influential and has the potential to drive further research in this area.  I believe it meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.
