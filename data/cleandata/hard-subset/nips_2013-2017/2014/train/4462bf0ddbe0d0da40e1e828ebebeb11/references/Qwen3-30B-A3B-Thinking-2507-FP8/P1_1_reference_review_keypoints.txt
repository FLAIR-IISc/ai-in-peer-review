- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs by optimizing eigenvalues and eigenvectors via eigendecomposition, viewing DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs, and showing better likelihood and runtime than projected gradient on a baby-registry task.  
- Technical quality: Theoretical derivations are sound, eigenvalue/eigenvector updates and complexity (O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3)) are analyzed, and experiments use sensible inits and evaluations; authors note initialization and eigenvalue=1 caveats.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured with clear algorithm descriptions and experimental details, though some technical derivations are deferred to the supplement which may hinder reproducibility.  
- Originality: Novel EM formulation leveraging eigendecomposition and the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view; first EM-based method that avoids projection steps.  
- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem (recommendation, summarization), showing up to 16.5% likelihood gain and ~2.1x runtime speedup, with guidance on when the method helps (datasets where diversity matters).  
- Strengths: Avoids projection-induced degeneracy; strong theoretical justification; comprehensive experiments and runtime gains; insightful analysis of applicability; clear writing and comparisons to prior work.  
- Weaknesses: Depends on reasonable initialization; edge case when eigenvalues approach 1 is not fully explored; limited empirical scalability analysis for very large N.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-justified contribution that meets NIPS criteria and is valuable to researchers and practitioners.
