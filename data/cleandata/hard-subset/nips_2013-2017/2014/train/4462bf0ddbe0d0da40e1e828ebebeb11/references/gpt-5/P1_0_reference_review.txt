Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles maximum-likelihood learning of full, unconstrained determinantal point process (DPP) kernels. DPPs are attractive for subset selection because they trade off item quality and diversity, with efficient normalization, marginalization, and sampling. Prior learning work largely avoided full unconstrained learning of L/K: (a) convex diagonal reweighting of a fixed similarity matrix [2], (b) mixtures of fixed L-ensembles [3], and (c) Bayesian learning with strong parametric assumptions [8]. The authors first present a baseline of projected gradient ascent on the marginal kernel K under 0 ≤ K ≤ I, then propose a new EM-style method that optimizes in the eigendecomposition of K. The key idea is to view a DPP as a mixture over “elementary” DPPs corresponding to subsets J of eigenvectors (projection DPPs), treating J as latent. Using Jensen’s inequality yields an EM lower bound. The E-step sets q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y), which is a k-DPP and can be represented compactly. The M-step splits into: (i) closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals, computable in O(nNk^2), and (ii) a single gradient step for the eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold using a simplified multiplicative update, with step-size chosen via backtracking and checking improvement in likelihood. This avoids the degeneracies seen with projection in the baseline and is asymptotically faster when k ≪ N.

Empirically, on a large Amazon baby registry dataset split into 13 categories (N ≈ 71 on average; n ≈ 8.6k sub-registries), EM improves held-out log-likelihood over projected gradient (KA), especially in categories where off-diagonal structure is important (as measured by a data-derived off-diagonality score). With a stronger, moment-matching initialization, the gap narrows, but EM remains more robust in data-poor settings (n ≈ 2N). EM is also about 2× faster in wall-clock time.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The derivation of the EM lower bound over the latent eigenvector subset J follows standard mixture-model EM. The identification of q(J|Y) as a k-DPP is elegant and leads to efficient marginals for the eigenvalue M-step. The eigenvector update uses a principled Stiefel manifold step (first-order version), with backtracking to ensure monotone likelihood increase. Runtime analysis is plausible: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), with the N^3 term from repeated matrix exponentials/eigendecompositions in the manifold step. The projected gradient baseline and its pitfalls (eigenvalue clipping causing near-diagonal K and locking the gradient) are clearly explained.
- Experimental support: Comparisons are thorough against the most relevant unconstrained baseline (KA) under two initializations and two data regimes; improvements in test likelihood and runtime are shown. The baby registry dataset is realistic and sufficiently large; code/data are promised.
- Caveats: (i) The M-step for V is not fully optimized; instead, a single gradient step is taken, and the E-step is recomputed during line search. While monotonic improvement is enforced by checking L, a formal convergence guarantee to a stationary point is not provided. (ii) The method’s scalability to larger N (thousands) is unclear due to repeated O(N^3) manifold updates; discussion of approximate updates (e.g., low-rank V, Nyström) would help. (iii) Baselines are limited to KA; comparisons to diagonal reweighting [2] or parametric/Bayesian approaches would contextualize practical trade-offs even if not directly comparable in modeling power.

Clarity
- The paper is generally clear and well organized. The use of the eigen-decomposition perspective to derive the EM updates is well motivated.
- Some derivations and proofs (Lemma 1, Eq. 19/20) are deferred to the supplement; including brief derivation sketches would help readers reproduce results.
- Minor issues to fix: typos in pseudocode (e.g., “η ← η2” likely means halving or scaling; please clarify the line search schedule), and clarify numerical safeguards (how close λ is allowed to approach 1; conditioning of HYi inverses). A bit more detail on practical constants in the O(nNk^2) computations would also help.

Originality
- The EM formulation over the latent eigenvector-set variable J appears novel for learning full DPP kernels. Prior work either restricted parameterization, used mixtures with fixed bases, or Bayesian priors. The combination of k-DPP conditionals in the E-step with closed-form eigenvalue updates and a Stiefel manifold step for eigenvectors is a fresh and well-motivated approach.

Significance
- Learning unconstrained DPP kernels is an important and challenging problem. The proposed method removes the need for problematic PSD projections, improves robustness to initialization, and is faster in practice for moderate N and small k. The baby registry application is compelling; the improvements in held-out likelihood and qualitative recommendations (e.g., avoiding multiple redundant monitors) underscore practical benefits. The approach could influence subsequent work on scalable and structured DPP learning.

Pros
- Conceptually clean EM derivation leveraging the DPP’s eigenstructure.
- Avoids PSD/eigenvalue projection degeneracies; more robust across settings.
- Efficient eigenvalue M-step and principled eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold.
- Empirical gains in likelihood and runtime on a realistic dataset; code/data provided.

Cons and questions
- Convergence: provide a statement (or pointer) about monotonicity and convergence properties under the “one-step M” and backtracking strategy; can you show convergence to a stationary point of the likelihood?
- Scalability: discuss and/or demonstrate behavior for larger N (hundreds to low thousands). Can the V-update be approximated (e.g., limited-memory manifolds, low-rank K) to reduce O(N^3)?
- Baselines: include diagonal reweighting [2] and perhaps an L-ensemble mixture [3] as reference points, noting their trade-offs. Even if they target different modeling power, they are common practice and help contextualize gains.
- Evaluation breadth: add application-oriented metrics (e.g., top-k recommendation accuracy/diversity) alongside likelihood, and report iteration counts and sensitivity to initialization and λ cap.
- Clarity: fix pseudocode typos, detail line search schedule, and specify numerical stabilizations (e.g., jitter for HYi, lower/upper bounds on λ).

Verdict
This is a solid, well-motivated contribution that advances learning for DPPs with a principled and practically useful EM method. While additional baselines and scalability discussion would strengthen the paper, the technical ideas and empirical evidence warrant acceptance.
