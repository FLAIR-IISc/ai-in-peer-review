- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm for full, unconstrained DPP kernel ML by treating active-eigenvector subset J as latent, using k-DPPs in E-step and closed-form eigenvalue + manifold-gradient eigenvector updates in M-step, avoiding PSD projections and improving likelihood/runtime vs projected gradient (KA).

- Theory: EM derivation is sound, uses mixture representation to define a tight bound, and enforces likelihood ascent by checking L after each inexact M-step.

- Key lemmas: Characterization of p(J|Y) as a k-DPP and efficient marginal computations underpin practical eigenvalue updates (proofs in supplement).

- Optimization details: Stiefel manifold updates for eigenvectors avoid problematic projections; eigenvector gradient is exact only for first step, mitigated by repeated E-steps and likelihood checks.

- Complexity claims: Per-iteration cost O(n N k^2) + O(T2 N^3) for line search is plausible and advantageous when k ≪ N compared to KA’s O(n N^3).

- Empirics: Shows consistent test log-likelihood and runtime improvements on Amazon baby registry data, with interpretable diagnostic for when EM helps; however baselines are limited to KA.

- Clarity: Well-written and organized; eigen-parameterization and k-DPP links are clear but heavy derivations and some implementation details are deferred to the supplement.

- Originality: Novel EM formulation for full DPP learning combining k-DPP E-steps with Stiefel manifold updates, distinct from prior quality-only or parametric/Bayesian approaches.

- Significance: Addresses practical obstacle of projection-induced degeneracy, yields better likelihood/diversity and is likely to be extended for applications needing negative interactions.

- Empirical strengths: Real-world dataset, multiple initializations, low-data regime analysis, runtime study, and qualitative diversity examples.

- Empirical limitations: No synthetic recovery tests, modest N (~70) so scalability unclear, missing comparisons to Bayesian/structured DPP methods, and limited discussion of regularization/overfitting.

- Pros: Clean EM derivation, avoids projections via manifold optimization, efficient eigenvalue updates, empirical gains in likelihood/runtime, and clear guidance on when EM helps.

- Cons: Limited baselines, scalability not demonstrated for large N, single-gradient eigenvector M-step with empirical convergence, and reliance on supplement for key derivations/implementation details.

- Questions/suggestions: Compare to Bayesian kernel learning and diagonal reweighting; report scalability to N≥1k and possible low-rank V; clarify regularization and sensitivity to λ cap; provide efficient computation details for Eq.(19) and numerical stabilization for Eq.(21); include synthetic recovery experiments.

- Overall: Strong, timely contribution that improves practical DPP learning with principled EM and empirical gains; would be stronger with broader baselines and scaling/implementation details—recommend accept.
