- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm that learns DPP kernel L via eigendecomposition, avoiding projection issues and showing theoretical and empirical benefits on a product-recommendation task.

- Strength: Addresses an important, challenging problem (full L learning) with practical relevance.

- Strength: Novel EM framework using eigenvalues/eigenvectors to circumvent direct gradient-ascent pitfalls.

- Strength: Theoretical justification is sound, leveraging Jensen’s inequality and connections to k-DPPs.

- Strength: Empirical validation on realistic data shows improved performance and runtime versus projected gradient ascent; links performance to dataset structure.

- Strength: Well-written and organized, with useful background material.

- Weakness: Limited baselines; should compare to more existing DPP learning methods for a fuller assessment.

- Weakness: Stiefel manifold update implementation is described briefly; step-size and practical details need clarification for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Scalability to very large N (e.g., >1000) and practical bottlenecks are not fully explored.

- Weakness: Moment-matching initialization works sometimes but lacks analysis explaining when and why it helps.

- Quality: 9/10 — technically sound, convincing theory and experiments.

- Clarity: 8/10 — generally clear but some sections need more detail.

- Originality: 8/10 — novel use of EM/eigendecomposition for DPP learning.

- Significance: 8/10 — practical, impactful advance for applications like recommendation and summarization.

- Pro: Novel, technically solid method with demonstrable empirical improvement and clear presentation.

- Con: Needs broader baseline comparisons, more implementation detail, and deeper scalability discussion.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.
