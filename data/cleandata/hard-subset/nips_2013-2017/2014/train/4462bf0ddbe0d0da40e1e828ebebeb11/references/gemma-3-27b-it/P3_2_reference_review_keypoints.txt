- Overall assessment: Novel, well-motivated EM approach for learning DPPs; technically sound and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem summary: Proposes EM on eigendecomposition of L to avoid projection issues in direct gradient methods by introducing eigenvector hidden variables.  
- Related work: Clearly positions contribution against scalar weight, L-ensemble, Bayesian, and k-DPP approaches and cites foundational/applicational DPP literature.  
- Novelty: EM-based eigendecomposition method is a significant and principled contribution to DPP learning.  
- Theoretical soundness: Derivations (closed-form eigenvalue update, eigenvector gradient) are correct and connect well to k-DPP marginalization.  
- Practical advantages: Avoids projection steps, yields more robust learning and potentially faster convergence than gradient ascent.  
- Clarity of presentation: Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear notation and helpful algorithms.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on baby registry data show EM outperforms gradient ascent, especially with strong interactions and limited data; runtime analysis supports claims.  
- Implementation complexity: Stiefel manifold eigenvector updates may be non-trivial to implement; authors should discuss challenges more deeply.  
- Step-size tuning: Paper lacks practical guidance on selecting step sizes for eigenvector updates.  
- Initialization sensitivity: Performance still depends on initialization; more exploration of schemes would be useful.  
- Scalability concerns: Experiments are limited to N≈71; scalability to much larger N (e.g., >1000) is not addressed.  
- Acceptance pros: Addresses fundamental problem, offers novel/theoretically sound solution, demonstrates empirical gains, clear writing, and high potential impact.  
- Acceptance cons: Potential implementation difficulty and unexplored large-scale scalability.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address step-size guidance, implementation details, and scalability discussion.
