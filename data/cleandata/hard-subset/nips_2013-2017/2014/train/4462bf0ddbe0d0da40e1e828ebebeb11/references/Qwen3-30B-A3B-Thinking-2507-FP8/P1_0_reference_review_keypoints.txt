- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm using K's eigendecomposition and hidden subset J to maximize DPP likelihood without projection, with detailed E- and M-step derivations.

- Strength 1: Novel technical contribution: eigendecomposition-based EM avoids projection steps and addresses a known difficulty in DPP learning.

- Strength 2: Strong experiments on the Amazon baby registry dataset showing EM outperforms gradient-ascent (K-Ascent), especially when off-diagonal kernel mass is significant.

- Strength 3: Clear theoretical analysis explaining why gradient ascent with projection fails (producing near-diagonal, non-diverse K).

- Strength 4: Practical runtime advantage: EM has a theoretically and empirically faster runtime (reported 2.1x speedup) under stated complexity assumptions.

- Strength 5: Well-organized and clearly written with helpful derivations and explanations (e.g., k-DPP in Lemma 1).

- Weakness 1: Limited experimental scope: evaluation only on the baby registry dataset, lacking other DPP application domains.

- Weakness 2: Incomplete comparison to prior methods: missing empirical comparisons to other recent DPP approaches (e.g., Bayesian [8], mixture/L-ensemble [3]).

- Weakness 3: Vague complexity analysis: insufficient justification of the Nk² vs N³ claim and unclear typical relationship between k and N in practice.

- Weakness 4: Lack of theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed EM algorithm.

- Weakness 5: Insufficient analysis of sensitivity to initialization and how different initializations affect EM vs KA.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—significant technical contribution with convincing experiments, despite relatively minor limitations.

- Suggestion 1: Add experiments on at least one additional DPP domain (e.g., document summarization).

- Suggestion 2: Include comparisons to Bayesian [8] and L-ensemble/mixture [3] methods.

- Suggestion 3: Provide a more detailed explanation of computational complexity and typical k vs N relationships.

- Suggestion 4: Perform a thorough initialization-sensitivity study comparing EM and KA under different starts.

- Suggestion 5: Add a brief discussion of EM convergence guarantees or limits, even if standard.
