This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing a known NP-hard problem. The authors motivate the work by highlighting the limitations of existing approaches, such as partial learning of the kernel matrix L or restrictive parameterizations. The core contribution lies in leveraging the eigendecomposition of L to formulate an EM algorithm that avoids the problematic projection step encountered in naive gradient ascent, which can lead to degenerate solutions (near-diagonal L). The paper provides a detailed theoretical development of the algorithm, including derivations for the E-step and M-step updates, and demonstrates its effectiveness through experiments on baby registry data, comparing it to a projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) method.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a challenging problem:** Learning DPPs is a fundamental yet difficult task, and this paper tackles the full learning problem without restrictive assumptions.
* **Novel approach:** The EM algorithm based on eigendecomposition is a creative and well-motivated solution. Avoiding the projection step is a significant advantage over gradient ascent.
* **Theoretical soundness:** The paper provides a comprehensive mathematical treatment of the algorithm, including clear derivations of the update rules.
* **Empirical validation:** The experiments demonstrate that the proposed EM algorithm outperforms K-Ascent, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between items are strong or data is limited.
* **Clear writing and organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the technical details.
* **Runtime analysis:** The inclusion of runtime analysis and comparison with K-Ascent provides a valuable practical perspective.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of implementation:** While the theory is sound, implementing the EM algorithm, particularly the Stiefel manifold update, appears complex and requires careful attention to detail.  The paper does not explicitly discuss implementation challenges.
* **Sensitivity to initialization:** Although the EM algorithm is shown to be more robust than K-Ascent, the initialization strategy still plays a role.  The comparison between Wishart and moment-matching initialization shows that performance can vary.  Further investigation into robust initialization methods could be beneficial.
* **Limited scope of experiments:** The experiments are primarily focused on a single dataset (baby registries) and a single application (product recommendation). While informative, this limits the generalizability of the findings.  Exploring other datasets and applications would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Discussion of the Stiefel manifold update is brief:** While mentioned, the simplification of the Stiefel manifold update (neglecting the Hessian) feels somewhat hand-wavy. Justification for this simplification and a discussion of its potential impact on convergence would be helpful.
* **Equation (21) and notation**: The use of "exp" to denote the matrix exponential might be confusing to some readers; clarifying this notation would be beneficial.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm for learning DPPs, a challenging and important problem.
* The EM algorithm addresses a significant limitation of existing approaches (the projection step in gradient ascent).
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm compared to a reasonable baseline.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the technical details.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The implementation complexity of the algorithm might be a barrier to adoption.
* The experiments are limited in scope, potentially limiting the generalizability of the results.
* Some aspects of the theoretical treatment (e.g., the Stiefel manifold update) could benefit from further elaboration.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of DPP learning. The proposed EM algorithm is a clever and well-motivated solution to a challenging problem. While the implementation complexity and limited scope of experiments are potential weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.  I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NeurIPS, with a request for the authors to address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly by providing more detail on implementation challenges and considering additional experiments.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Implementation details:** Provide a more detailed discussion of implementation challenges and potential optimizations.
* **Robust initialization:** Investigate more robust initialization strategies beyond Wishart and moment-matching.
* **Expanded experiments:** Conduct experiments on additional datasets and applications to demonstrate the generalizability of the results.
* **Stiefel manifold update:** Provide further justification for the simplification of the Stiefel manifold update and discuss its potential impact on convergence.
* **Notation clarification**: Clarify the use of "exp" to denote the matrix exponential.
