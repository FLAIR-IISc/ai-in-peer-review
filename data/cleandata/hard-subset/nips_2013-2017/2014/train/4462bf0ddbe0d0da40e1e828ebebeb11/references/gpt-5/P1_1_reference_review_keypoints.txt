- Proposes EM on the marginal kernel K using eigendecomposition K=VΛV^T and a latent subset J from the elementary-DPP mixture to learn full DPPs without restrictive parameterizations.  
- E-step: q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y) is a k-DPP with a computable kernel; M-step: closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and eigenvector updates by gradient steps on the Stiefel manifold with a line search on the true likelihood.  
- Novelty: using the elementary-DPP mixture to derive an EM that updates eigenvalues in closed form and eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold appears new relative to prior DPP learning work.  
- Empirical benefit: avoids projection-induced degeneracy of projected gradient, shows runtime gains (≈2.1×) and larger relative improvements when negative interactions are strong or data are scarce.  
- Technical soundness: E-step and eigenvalue update are plausible and tractable; V-update as a Stiefel-manifold GEM step with line search is reasonable but approximate.  
- Convergence caveat: V-update is derived assuming q=p but q changes after updating V; the paper should discuss monotonicity/convergence and how the line search ensures non-decreasing likelihood.  
- Complexity caveat: claimed O(nNk^2) cost for eigenvector derivatives needs clarification regarding |Y|×|Y| matrix inversions and whether per-example eigendecompositions are required.  
- k-DPP computation caveat: details on computing k-DPP marginals via eigen-decomposition per QY and any precomputation/assumptions should be clarified.  
- Numerical stability caveat: handling λ→1 by capping needs discussion of bias/consistency implications.  
- Clarity issues: clarify definition of IY and the P(Y)=|det(K−IY)| formula, fix formatting/definition issues for metric d and symbols in Eq. (20), and correct minor typos.  
- Presentation suggestion: include a short proof sketch in the main text (e.g., why q(J|Y) is a k-DPP) instead of deferring all proofs to the appendix.  
- Suggested experiments: add baselines (row-scaling, quality-only/diagonal, low-rank DPP), synthetic experiments with known ground truth to test recovery and initialization sensitivity, and broader domains beyond the single registry dataset.  
- Scalability suggestion: discuss adaptations for larger N (low-rank V, sparsity, Nyström approximations) and how EM would scale in those regimes.  
- Pros: novel EM formulation, closed-form eigenvalue updates, avoids projection degeneracy, computationally attractive when k≪N, real-world dataset and released code/data.  
- Cons: V-update is an approximate M-step with unclear convergence guarantees, complexity claims need justification, limited experimental scope and baselines, scalability beyond N≈100 not demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid methodological contribution and convincing within scope, but needs clearer complexity/convergence discussion and expanded empirical validation.
