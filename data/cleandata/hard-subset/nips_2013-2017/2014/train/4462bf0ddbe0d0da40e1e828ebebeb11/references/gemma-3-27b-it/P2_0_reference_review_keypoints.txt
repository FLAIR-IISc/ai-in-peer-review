- Paper proposes an EM algorithm using eigendecomposition to learn full DPP kernels L, avoiding projection-induced degeneracy of gradient methods.  
- Strength: Tackles a fundamental DPP learning problem with practical importance.  
- Strength: Novel EM formulation leveraging hidden index set J and eigendecomposition.  
- Strength: Rigorous derivation of EM updates and clear computational complexity analysis showing asymptotic runtime benefits.  
- Strength: Empirical results on a product recommendation (baby registry) dataset show improved performance and efficiency over gradient ascent.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing and helpful use of equations.  
- Weakness: Baselines are limited—missing comparisons to recent methods like low-rank L learning.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to initialization is noted but not thoroughly explored beyond two strategies.  
- Weakness: Implementation (e.g., Stiefel manifold update) appears complex, with little practical guidance.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a single dataset, reducing claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis explaining when and why EM outperforms gradient ascent and the role of projection failures.  
- Qualitative: Technically sound with theoretical and empirical support for claims.  
- Qualitative: Generally clear exposition with consistent notation.  
- Qualitative: Original contribution in applying EM and eigendecomposition to DPP learning.  
- Qualitative: Significant potential impact for recommendation, summarization, and feature selection.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound method with demonstrable improvement and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: Limited comparative baselines, single-dataset evaluation, and potential adoption hurdles due to implementation complexity.  
- Overall: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address baselines, dataset diversity, and implementation/analysis details.
