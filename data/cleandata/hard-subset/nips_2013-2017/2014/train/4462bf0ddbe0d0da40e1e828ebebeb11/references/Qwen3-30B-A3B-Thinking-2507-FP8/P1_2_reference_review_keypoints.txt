- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm that leverages eigendecomposition to learn unconstrained DPP kernels, avoiding projection issues in gradient methods, and validates it on a baby-registry dataset.

- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous derivations; EM consistently outperforms gradient-ascent (K-Ascent) and is more robust to initialization.

- Clarity: Well-written and organized with reproducible details; minor clarifications needed for the Stiefel manifold update and k-DPP connections.

- Originality: Novel use of eigendecomposition within an EM framework to learn non-parametric DPP kernels without projections, distinct from prior restricted approaches.

- Significance: Substantial practical impact—better performance and faster convergence for DPP learning, relevant to many subset-selection applications.

- Strengths: Avoids problematic projection steps; strong theoretical foundations; comprehensive real-world experiments; improved accuracy and efficiency; clear differentiation from prior work.

- Weaknesses: Experiments limited to one dataset; some technical details (e.g., Stiefel update) need clearer explanation; behavior in small-data regimes not fully explored.

- Recommendation: Strong accept—paper is a significant, well-supported contribution to DPP learning.

- Final Score: 8 (Strong Accept).
