This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing the challenges of directly maximizing the likelihood function for the kernel matrix *L*. The core idea is to leverage the eigendecomposition of *L* and introduce a hidden variable representing the eigenvectors, allowing for an iterative optimization process that avoids the problematic projection step inherent in gradient ascent methods. The paper provides a detailed theoretical justification, including derivations of the EM updates and a runtime analysis, and supports its claims with experiments on a baby registry dataset.

The paper’s strength lies in its technical depth and thoroughness. The authors rigorously derive the EM algorithm, carefully addressing the potential pitfalls of gradient ascent and outlining a clear procedure for optimization. The inclusion of runtime analysis and a comparison with a projected gradient ascent baseline (KA) is commendable. The empirical evaluation, while focused on a single application, provides evidence supporting the proposed method’s robustness, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between items are significant. The detailed explanation of initialization strategies and their impact on performance is also valuable. The connection to existing DPP literature is well established, and the authors appropriately position their work within the broader context of subset selection.

However, the paper suffers from a number of weaknesses, many of which are surprisingly minor but accumulate to a frustrating degree. First, the writing style, while generally clear, is often verbose and repetitive. For instance, the explanation of the advantages of the EM algorithm over gradient ascent is reiterated multiple times throughout the introduction and subsequent sections. Second, several equations lack sufficient explanation. Equation (17), introducing the *R* matrix, is presented without clarifying the role of the square root, leading to potential confusion. Similarly, the connection between Equation (21) and the Stiefel manifold update could be more explicitly stated. Third, the experimental evaluation is limited. While the baby registry dataset is a reasonable choice, the results presented are not overwhelmingly convincing. The gains of EM over KA, while statistically significant in some cases, are often modest. A more comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets and with varying parameter settings would strengthen the paper’s claims. Fourth, there are several minor notational inconsistencies. The use of both *K* and *L* to represent kernel matrices (and their relationship via Equation 1) is initially confusing. Consistent use of subscripts and superscripts within equations would also improve readability. Fifth, the handling of the non-convexity in the M-step for the eigenvectors is somewhat hand-wavy. While the authors acknowledge the issue and resort to a single gradient step, a more detailed discussion of alternative approaches or potential limitations would be beneficial. Sixth, the supplemental material is referenced frequently in the main text, but its accessibility during review is uncertain, and critical details should be included directly in the paper. Seventh, Algorithm 2 has a missing ')' after the 'q' variable. Eighth, the reference formatting is inconsistent, with some references missing journal or conference names. Ninth, the phrase "baby registries" appears to be missing an apostrophe throughout the paper. Tenth, the "toy" category is oddly spelled "toys" in one instance. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs that addresses limitations of existing methods.
*   Rigorous theoretical analysis and derivation of EM updates.
*   Demonstrated robustness in a product recommendation task.
*   Potential for broader impact in applications requiring modeling of diverse and distinct subsets.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Modest experimental gains, limited dataset diversity.
*   Verbose writing style and minor notational inconsistencies.
*   Lack of detailed discussion regarding the non-convexity of the eigenvector optimization.
*   Overabundance of minor flaws detract from the overall presentation.
*   Reliance on supplemental material for crucial details. 

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the novelty of the approach and the technical soundness of the derivations, the accumulation of minor flaws and the limited impact of the experimental results lead me to recommend **rejection**. While the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of DPP learning, the lack of polish and the modest gains observed in the experiments do not meet the high standards expected for a NIPS publication. Addressing the issues outlined above – particularly improving clarity, expanding the experimental evaluation, and meticulously reviewing the manuscript for inconsistencies – could significantly strengthen the paper and make it more competitive in future submissions.
