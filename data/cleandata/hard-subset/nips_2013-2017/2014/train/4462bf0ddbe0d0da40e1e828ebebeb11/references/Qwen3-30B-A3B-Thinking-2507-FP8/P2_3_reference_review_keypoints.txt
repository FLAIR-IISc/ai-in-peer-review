- Addresses NP-hard MLE for DPPs by proposing an EM algorithm that leverages eigendecomposition to avoid projection steps.  
- EM alternates an E-step computing posterior over hidden eigenvector indices and an M-step updating eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Theoretically and empirically more robust to initialization, yields better likelihoods, and has better asymptotic runtime than gradient ascent.  
- Novel contribution: first to learn unconstrained, non-parametric L without projection steps, building on Kulesza & Taskar.  

Strengths:
- Quality: Rigorous mathematical derivations (E-step Lemma 1, M-step Equations 18–20) and clear analysis of gradient-ascent failures (projection-induced nearly diagonal matrices).  
- Empirical: Strong results on Amazon baby registry—up to 16.5% likelihood gain and ~2.1× faster runtime on average.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, clear derivations, precise algorithm descriptions, and reproducible experimental details.  
- Originality: Novel application of EM to DPP learning via eigendecomposition and extension of k-DPP theory for the E-step.  
- Significance: Addresses a key limitation in DPP learning (learning kernel without restrictive assumptions) with practical implications for summarization, sensor placement, and recommendations.  

Weaknesses:
- Quality: No convergence guarantees for the EM algorithm and no analysis or empirical evidence of convergence rates for the non-convex eigenvector update.  
- Clarity/Generality: Experiments focus on a single domain (baby registries, N≈71), limiting claims about generality and scalability to much larger N (>10k).  
- Originality/Related Work: Needs better comparison to recent DPP learning approaches (e.g., [13], [18]) in the related work and experiments.  
- Significance/Practicality: Practical adoption questions remain—limited domain tests and insufficient discussion of computational barriers for very large datasets.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong, technically rigorous, and empirically effective paper that meaningfully advances DPP learning by eliminating projection-induced degeneracy.  
- Identified weaknesses are minor relative to the contribution and do not undermine practical results.  

Recommendation:
- Accept.
