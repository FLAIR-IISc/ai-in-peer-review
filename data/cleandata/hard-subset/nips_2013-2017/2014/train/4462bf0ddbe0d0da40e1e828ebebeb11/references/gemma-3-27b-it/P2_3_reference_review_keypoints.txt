- Problem addressed: Learning the full DPP kernel L is hard; existing methods are partial, restrictive, or suffer from projection-based optimization issues.
- Main contribution: Proposes an EM algorithm using eigendecomposition and a hidden-variable formulation to learn both eigenvalues and eigenvectors of L.
- Theoretical advantage: Argues asymptotic runtime speedup of EM over gradient-ascent (K-Ascent).
- Empirical results: EM outperforms K-Ascent on a baby-registry product recommendation task, especially with limited data and negative item interactions.
- Robustness: EM is less sensitive to initialization and avoids degenerate solutions seen with gradient ascent.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and organized, with useful background and clear algorithm descriptions.
- Derivation clarity: M-step derivations are dense and could be clarified; link between key equations and complexity requires supplement lookup.
- Eigenvector update: Practical simplification of the Stiefel manifold update to a multiplicative update is used; its impact on performance is not fully explored.
- Dataset scope: Experiments are limited to one domain (baby registries), reducing generalizability.
- Ablation: Lacks a thorough ablation study to isolate effects of initialization choices and M-step approximations.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with convincing theory and supported experiments; projection analysis of K-Ascent is insightful.
- Originality: Novel application of EM with eigendecomposition for DPP kernel learning; well-situated in related work.
- Significance: Method could impact many applications (summarization, sensor placement, image search) by improving DPP learning.
- Acceptance arguments (pro): Addresses important problem, novel method, theoretical and empirical support, clear presentation.
- Acceptance arguments (con): Dense derivations, limited dataset diversity, and limited ablation study.
- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance — significant, novel, and well-executed work with minor areas for improvement.
