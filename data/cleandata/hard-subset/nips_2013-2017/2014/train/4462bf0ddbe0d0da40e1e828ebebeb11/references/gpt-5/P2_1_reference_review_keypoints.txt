- Tackles ML learning of DPPs with unconstrained kernels (only PSD and spectral bounds), addressing limitations of prior work that fixed similarities or used parametric/Bayesian forms.  
- PGA requires repeated PSD/spectral projections that bias kernels toward diagonal structure and harm diversity modeling.  
- Main contribution: an eigenspace EM algorithm that treats the DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs indexed by a hidden eigenvector set J.  
- E-step: q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with a computable kernel.  
- M-step: closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals plus a single eigenvector gradient step on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding projection.  
- Eigenvector update is approximate (single-step gradient) but the algorithm checks actual likelihood improvements, so practical stability is maintained even if classical EM monotonicity isn't guaranteed each substep.  
- Complexity: EM claims O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs PGA's O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3), giving large gains when k << N.  
- Experiments (Amazon baby registry, N≈71, n≈8.6k) show EM yields higher test likelihoods and ~2× speedup vs PGA, with larger gains in data-poor regimes (n≈2N).  
- Technical soundness: E-step and eigenvalue updates appear correct and efficient; eigenvector (V) update is pragmatic but approximate.  
- Concern: limited theoretical convergence guarantees for the V-update and reliance on a single-step M-step update.  
- Complexity caveat: need clearer accounting of the cost to repeatedly evaluate the likelihood L(K) and the cost of multiple E-steps during line search.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and motivated, but some derivations and proofs are deferred to the supplement, requiring readers to consult it for full verification.  
- Minor clarity suggestions: explain cost and numerical stabilization for L(K), clarify invertibility/near-singularity handling for (K−IY), and note efficient det(K−IY) computation for small |Y|.  
- Originality: combining the elementary-DPP mixture view with k-DPP marginals and Stiefel-manifold updates to learn unconstrained K is a novel, well-motivated combination.  
- Significance: important for learning full DPP kernels driven by negative interactions; method is more robust and faster when k << N and code is provided.  
- Experimental limitations: only one real dataset and one main baseline; lacks synthetic recovery studies, other baselines (e.g., Riemannian or factored PSD methods), and larger-scale tests.  
- Pros: principled EM framework, avoids projection-induced degeneracy, better asymptotic/practical runtime when k<<N, and solid empirical improvements with code release.  
- Cons: approximate eigenvector M-step without formal convergence guarantees, limited experimental breadth, and some critical details/proofs in the supplement; minor implementation/numerical details could be clearer.  
- Questions for authors: report per-iteration cost of evaluating L(K); sensitivity to doing more than one V-update per M-step and any monotonicity guarantees; comparisons to Riemannian or factored PSD baselines.  
- Minor comments: fix typos (“negelecting”→“neglecting”, “justies”→“justifies”); note V is orthogonal (real case), not “unitary.”  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, clear, and practically relevant paper that would benefit from broader experimental comparisons to be a definitive reference for unconstrained DPP learning.
