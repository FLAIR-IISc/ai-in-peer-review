Summary
This paper tackles the long-standing problem of learning full, unconstrained DPP kernels from observed subsets by proposing an EM-style algorithm that optimizes over the eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel K. The key idea is to introduce as hidden variables the index sets J of selected eigenvectors (elementary DPP components), leveraging the standard mixture view of DPPs. The E-step identifies q(J|Y) as a conditional k-DPP with a computable kernel; the M-step yields a closed-form, self-normalizing update for eigenvalues λ and a Stiefel-manifold gradient step for eigenvectors V. The approach avoids the problematic PSD projection used by naive projected gradient ascent on K, which the authors argue can drive the solution toward near-diagonal (degenerate) kernels. The authors provide complexity analysis showing potential asymptotic gains (O(n N k^2) terms instead of O(n N^3) per iteration), and empirical evaluation on a real-world baby registry recommendation dataset demonstrating improved test likelihoods, robustness to initialization and data scarcity, and reduced runtime compared to projected gradient.

Relation to prior work
The paper is well-situated within the DPP literature (Kulesza & Taskar; Affandi et al.; related k-DPPs). Prior learning approaches have either learned diagonal/item-quality scalings, mixtures of fixed kernels (sacrificing the DPP form), or Bayesian learning of parametric kernels. This work differs by learning a full, nonparametric K without restricting structure and by exploiting the eigendecomposition and mixture representation to derive an EM algorithm. The use of Stiefel manifold updates for V is also a well-founded choice in constrained optimization. To my knowledge, an EM formulation over DPP eigenspaces with k-DPP conditionals is a novel and elegant contribution.

Strengths
- Novelty and conceptual clarity: Casting DPP learning as EM over elementary DPP components (via eigenvector index sets) is an appealing and nontrivial idea. The closed-form λ updates and the conditional k-DPP E-step are particularly elegant.
- Practical impact: Avoids PSD projection pitfalls that can damage off-diagonal structure and diversity modeling. Addresses a real pain point for practitioners using gradient-based MLE for DPPs.
- Computational advantages: Clear asymptotic analysis and an algorithm that scales with subset size k rather than N in the dominant terms; likely important in many applications where sets are small.
- Empirical evidence: On a sizable real dataset, EM is more robust to initialization, outperforms projected gradient particularly when off-diagonal correlations matter or data is scarce, and is faster in practice. Example recommendations illustrate the qualitative benefit (avoiding redundant items).
- Relevance: DPPs remain widely used for diversity-aware selection; a better learning algorithm is impactful.

Weaknesses and questions
- Limited baselines: The main comparison is to projected gradient with projection (KA). It would strengthen the case to compare against (i) Bayesian parametric learning of L, (ii) diagonal scaling (convex per-row weights), and (iii) mixture-of-DPPs training, even if those methods have different trade-offs.
- Convergence guarantees: The eigenvector update uses only a single gradient step on V per M-step, and Equation (20) is valid for the first gradient step with q = p. This is a generalized EM setup with line search based on likelihood. The paper should clarify convergence properties (e.g., monotone improvement guarantees) and any conditions under which the algorithm might stall.
- Numerical stability near λ ≈ 1: The E-step kernel uses R = diag(sqrt(λ/(1−λ))). The authors note tightening constraints, but it would help to detail how λ is constrained in practice and to report numerical behavior when eigenvalues approach 1.
- Scalability evidence: Although the complexity is favorable, experiments are limited to moderate N (~71). A controlled scaling study (varying N and k) and ablations on n, k would substantiate the asymptotic claims.
- Synthetic validation: A synthetic recovery study (where ground-truth K has strong off-diagonal structure) could illustrate the failure mode of KA (near-diagonal solution) and the success of EM more transparently, and quantify estimation error versus n.
- Some derivations deferred: Lemma 1 and the derivations of Equations (19) and (20) are in the supplement. For reproducibility, including a concise derivation sketch in the main text would help, though the current level of detail is acceptable for space.

Quality
Overall technically sound, with clear derivations where provided, grounded in known properties of DPPs and manifold optimization. The generalized EM approximation for V is reasonable but would benefit from a brief convergence discussion and additional empirical validation. Claims about KA degeneracy are plausible and supported by both analysis and experiments.

Clarity
Well organized and mostly clear. The algorithm summaries are helpful. A brief intuitive explanation of why the EM view preserves off-diagonals (versus projection) would aid readers. Minor suggestions: explicitly state whether the line search guarantees non-decreasing likelihood; summarize the computational steps for computing the k-DPP marginals in O(N k^2).

Originality
High. The EM over eigenspaces with conditional k-DPPs, the closed-form λ update, and the Stiefel-manifold treatment for V combine into a novel, cohesive learning algorithm for nonparametric DPPs.

Significance
Moderate to high. Improving DPP learning is valuable, and the approach is likely to be adopted by practitioners. The conceptual framing may inspire further advances (e.g., structured/low-rank variants, stochastic EM, large-scale approximations).

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually novel and elegant EM formulation leveraging DPP structure
- Addresses a real and important bottleneck in DPP learning
- Demonstrated robustness and speed advantages; practical impact
- Clear potential for broader use and extensions

Cons against acceptance
- Limited experimental breadth and baselines
- Lack of formal convergence guarantees for the generalized EM update on V
- Scaling evidence limited to moderate N

Recommendation
Accept. Despite some experimental limitations, the paper introduces a significant new approach to a core problem in DPP learning with a compelling algorithmic insight and practical benefits. I believe the novelty and potential impact justify acceptance.
