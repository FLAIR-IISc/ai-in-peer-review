- Tackles maximum-likelihood learning of unconstrained DPP kernels via an EM-style algorithm using eigendecomposition and latent eigenvector subsets J.
- E-step computes exact posterior q(J|Y) which is shown to be a k-DPP with kernel QY = R ZY R (Lemma 1).
- M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form λ′j = (1/n) Σ_i p(j ∈ J | Yi) and updates eigenvectors V with a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step.
- Empirically outperforms projected gradient on K: higher test log-likelihood, more robust to initialization, and ~2× faster asymptotically and in practice on Amazon baby-registry data.
- Novel use of DPP mixture/k-DPP machinery to decouple eigenvalue and eigenvector learning appears original and principled.
- Solid algorithmic design: closed-form eigenvalue updates, principled manifold update for V, and explanation why K-projection can lead to near-diagonal degeneracy.
- Complexity claim O(n N k^2) per iteration but omits O(n k^3) for inverting k×k matrices per example; also N^3 costs arise from eigendecompositions and line-search matrix exponentials.
- Method scales better than naive projected gradient but still faces limits when N is large (thousands); low-rank/Nyström variants not explored.
- Baselines limited to naive projected gradient; missing comparisons to strong parametric or Bayesian DPP learning and kernel-mixture methods.
- Experiments limited to a single domain (baby registries); additional domains or downstream metrics (MAP, diversity) would strengthen generality claims.
- EM algorithm presentation has clarity issues: F(q,V,Λ) claimed intractable while ΔF is reported—need to clarify what is computed and whether monotone L is guaranteed.
- V-gradient derivation assumes q = p at M-step start; using a single update per iteration is reasonable but its effect on convergence needs discussion.
- Numerical stability concerns: R = diag(√λ/(1−λ)) ill-conditioned as λ→1; need to quantify λ capping (ε) and its impact.
- Initialization sensitivity not fully characterized; report variance across runs and evaluate moment-matching initialization for EM.
- Complexity discussion should explicitly include O(n k^3) and clarify regimes where k^3 vs other terms dominate.
- Recommend discussing adaptation to low-rank/Nyström approaches to improve scalability and practical applicability for large N.
- Suggest adding at least one stronger baseline (parametric DPP or kernel mixtures) or an ablation (update V vs only Λ).
- Minor clarity fixes: clarify IY definition, introduce BYi earlier, and provide intuition for Lemma 1 and Eq. (19) rather than deferring entirely to supplement.
- Reproducibility is good: code and data provided.
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled method, practical speedups, robustness, and real-world dataset relevance.
- Cons for acceptance: limited baselines/domains, unclear convergence/objective tracking, and scalability not demonstrated experimentally.
- Suggested revisions: clarify generalized EM nature and monitored objective, include O(n k^3) in complexity, add baselines/dataset or additional metrics, and quantify numerical safeguards.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid methodological contribution but needs clearer convergence/monitoring, broader empirical scope, and scalability discussion.
