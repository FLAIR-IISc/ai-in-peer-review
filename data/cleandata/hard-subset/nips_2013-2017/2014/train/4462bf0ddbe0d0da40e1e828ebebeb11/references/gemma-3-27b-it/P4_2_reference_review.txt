The paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing the challenge of learning the kernel matrix L from example subsets. Existing methods either learn only partial information about L, require restrictive parameterizations, or suffer from optimization issues like degenerate results due to projection steps in gradient ascent. The authors leverage the eigendecomposition of L to formulate an EM algorithm that avoids the problematic projection, leading to improved robustness and asymptotic speed.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the problem, related work, and proposed solution. The mathematical derivations are generally clear, though some steps (especially in the M-step involving the eigenvector update) require careful examination of the provided supplementary material. The connection to existing DPP literature is well-established, and the motivation for avoiding the projection step in gradient ascent is convincingly argued. The experimental setup using baby registry data is relevant, and the comparison against a projected gradient ascent method (KA) demonstrates the benefits of the proposed EM approach, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between items are strong or when data is limited.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The EM approach for learning DPPs without restrictive assumptions on L or the need for problematic projections is a significant contribution. While eigendecomposition is commonly used with DPPs, applying it within an EM framework to address the learning problem is novel.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation and derivations appear sound, though some require a deeper dive into the supplement. The explanation of the algorithm's advantages over gradient ascent (avoiding degeneracy) is convincing.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The explanation of DPP concepts is concise and accessible.
*   **Significance:** Learning DPPs effectively has applications in several domains (recommendation, summarization, etc.). A more robust and efficient learning algorithm like the proposed EM method is valuable to the community. The empirical results demonstrate practical improvements.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity of M-Step:** The M-step involving the eigenvector update relies on approximations and simplifications (neglecting the Hessian, using a single gradient step). While acknowledged, a more thorough discussion of the potential impact of these simplifications on the convergence and accuracy of the algorithm would be beneficial.
*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** While EM is shown to be more robust than KA, the choice of initialization (Wishart or moment-matching) still seems important.  A more detailed analysis of the impact of different initializations and potentially methods for automatic initialization selection would be valuable.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are focused solely on the baby registry dataset. While the results are positive, extending the evaluation to other datasets with different characteristics would strengthen the conclusions.
*   **Practical Implementation Details:** The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of practical considerations for implementing the algorithm, such as step size selection and handling potential numerical instability.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in DPP learning.
*   The EM algorithm offers advantages over existing methods in terms of robustness and asymptotic speed.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a relevant dataset.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key concepts and contributions.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The M-step relies on approximations that could impact convergence and accuracy.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset.
*   The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of practical implementation considerations.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of DPP learning. While there are some minor weaknesses, the novelty, technical soundness, and empirical results justify acceptance. The proposed EM algorithm represents a significant improvement over existing methods and has the potential to be widely used by researchers and practitioners. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above regarding the M-step approximations, initialization sensitivity, and scope of experiments.
