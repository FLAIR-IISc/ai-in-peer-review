- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the DPP marginal kernel K without restrictive form assumptions, avoiding projection-induced degeneracy of gradient methods.
- Key insight: treat a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs with hidden eigenvector set J, enabling an EM with posterior E-step and eigenvalue/eigenvector M-step.
- Theoretical derivations are sound, providing E-step (Lemma 1), M-step updates (Eqns 18–21), and complexity analysis.
- Complexity advantage: EM runs in O(nN k²) versus O(nN³) for gradient ascent, attractive for small k.
- Experiments on a baby registry dataset compare EM to K-Ascent under two initializations and show EM yields more diverse, realistic recommendations.
- Analysis using the d statistic shows EM’s relative gains when negative item interactions are strong.
- Evaluation limitations: small dataset size (N≈71), missing comparisons to recent DPP methods ([13,19]), and no large-N scalability study.
- Clarity is generally good and algorithms are reproducible, but the eigenvector update (Eqn 21) needs clearer Stiefel-manifold intuition.
- Originality: novel and non-obvious EM formulation that cleanly avoids degeneracy and learns unconstrained kernels.
- Significance: addresses practical diversity-modeling limits and shows both empirical benefit and asymptotic runtime improvement.
- Strengths: novel EM framework, clear theoretical justification, real-world experiments, runtime advantage, and insightful analysis of when method excels.
- Weaknesses: limited dataset scale, missing recent-method comparisons, insufficient EM convergence analysis, opaque eigenvector update, and limited large-N discussion.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions given solid technical contribution and convincing results.
- Suggested improvements: compare to recent DPP methods, analyze EM convergence, explain eigenvector update intuitively, discuss large-scale limitations, and test on larger datasets.
