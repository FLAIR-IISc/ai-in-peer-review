- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning full DPP kernel L using its eigendecomposition and a hidden eigenvector variable to avoid problematic projection steps.  
- Claims theoretical runtime advantages and empirical improvements over gradient ascent on product recommendation data.  
- Situates work against prior DPP learning approaches that learn only weights or use restrictive parameterizations, and builds on DPP theory and eigendecomposition techniques.  

Strengths:
- Novel EM approach that is theoretically sound and practically effective.  
- Tackles the core challenge of learning the full L matrix for DPPs.  
- Provides theoretical justification and runtime analysis compared to gradient ascent.  
- Empirical validation on a baby-registry dataset shows robust performance and improvement over K-Ascent.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Authors address practical issues like initialization and avoiding eigenvalues hitting 1.  

Weaknesses:
- Implementation is complex (notably the Stiefel manifold update) and may require significant expertise.  
- Experiments are limited to a single product recommendation task, reducing evidence of generality.  
- Performance still depends on initialization; more guidance on choosing initial kernels is needed.  
- E-step computational cost (Nk^2) can be expensive in practice and is not fully explored.  
- No discussion of sensitivity to M-step step sizes (η) or other hyperparameters.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Presents a novel, well-motivated solution to a challenging DPP learning problem.  
- Empirical results support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Clear exposition increases accessibility and impact.  
- Potential to significantly broaden DPP applicability.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- High implementation complexity may limit adoption.  
- Limited experimental scope weakens empirical claims.  
- Lacks thorough discussion of initialization and hyperparameter sensitivity.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong paper with significant contribution to DPP learning; recommended for acceptance (Strong Accept).  

Additional suggestions:
- Provide more implementation details for the Stiefel manifold update.  
- Evaluate on additional DPP applications/datasets.  
- Investigate initialization strategies and their impact.  
- Analyze sensitivity to M-step step sizes (η).  
- Explore approximations to reduce E-step cost for large datasets.
