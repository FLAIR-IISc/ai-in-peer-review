- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP kernel L in eigendecomposition space to avoid PSD projection and degenerate near-diagonal solutions.
- Introduces a hidden variable for eigenvectors and performs EM updates on eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- Provides theoretical justification and leverages k-DPP connections for efficient computation.
- Empirical results on a baby-registry product recommendation task show EM outperforms gradient ascent, especially when negative item interactions matter; includes an initialization ablation.
- Positions itself against prior work by learning a fully non-parametric L rather than scalar or parametric weightings.
- Novelty: EM on eigendecomposition and hidden-eigenvector formulation are significant and well-motivated.
- Theoretical soundness: derivations are rigorous and the advantages over gradient ascent are convincing.
- Presentation is clear with readable notation, a digestible algorithm, and included runtime analysis.
- Practical significance: method could improve applications like recommendation, summarization, and sensor placement.
- Weakness — computational complexity: runtime claims are idealized; EM overhead and step-size search may not scale for very large N and need more practical benchmarks.
- Weakness — sensitivity to initialization: provided Wishart and moment-matching inits may not be optimal universally; more strategies should be explored.
- Weakness — limited experimentation: evaluation focuses on one dataset/application, limiting generalizability claims.
- Weakness — convergence handling: stopping criterion (δ < c) is vague and needs clarification on choice of c and stopping behavior.
- Weakness — step-size selection: description lacks detail, unclear how “average of T2 iterations” informs best step size.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem with a novel, theoretically sound algorithm and strong empirical evidence; well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: runtime analysis, limited experiments, and initialization sensitivity remain unresolved concerns.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address runtime, step-size explanation, and initialization strategies.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
