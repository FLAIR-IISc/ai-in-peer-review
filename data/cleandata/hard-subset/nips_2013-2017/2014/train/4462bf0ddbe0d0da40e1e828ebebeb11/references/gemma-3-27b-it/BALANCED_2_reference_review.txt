This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing the difficulty of directly maximizing the likelihood function due to the positive semi-definiteness constraint on the kernel matrix L. The core idea is to work with the eigendecomposition of L, which avoids the problematic projection step required by naive gradient ascent and enables more robust learning of both quality and diversity aspects of DPPs. The paper thoroughly motivates the problem, provides a clear theoretical foundation, and presents experimental results demonstrating the benefits of the proposed EM approach over a gradient ascent baseline.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a well-defined algorithm with a clear theoretical justification. The use of eigendecomposition is a clever way to circumvent the projection issue in gradient ascent. The derivations appear correct and are supported by relevant citations.
* **Novelty:** While DPPs have been extensively studied, learning L without restrictive parameterizations remains a challenging problem. The proposed EM algorithm offers a novel solution that avoids the limitations of existing methods like scalar weight learning or Bayesian approaches with fixed Ls.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized. The background section provides sufficient context for understanding the DPP formalism, and the algorithm description is clear and concise. Mathematical notation is consistently used and explained.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments on baby registry data demonstrate the effectiveness of the EM algorithm compared to gradient ascent. The ablation study with different initializations and the comparison under data-poor conditions strengthen the conclusions. The runtime analysis is also valuable.
* **Addressing Limitations:** The authors acknowledge and discuss the limitations of the gradient ascent method and the potential issues with initialization, demonstrating a careful and honest evaluation of their work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While the paper claims asymptotic speedup, the practical runtime benefits might be sensitive to the value of ‘k’ (the size of the largest subset). A more detailed discussion of the computational cost in different scenarios would be helpful. The scaling with N could also be elaborated.
* **Initialization Sensitivity:** While two initialization strategies are explored, a more comprehensive investigation of different initialization methods and their impact on convergence and performance would be beneficial.  Is there a way to automatically select a good initialization scheme?
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are confined to a single dataset (baby registries). Evaluating the algorithm on diverse datasets and tasks would further validate its generalizability.  For example, evaluating performance on document summarization, as mentioned in the introduction, would be valuable.
* **Step Size Selection:** The algorithm relies on a step-size selection mechanism, but the details of this process aren’t fully explained. More detail on how the step size is determined and how the E-step is used in the process would improve reproducibility.
* **Stiefel Manifold Update:** The simplification of the Stiefel manifold update (neglecting the Hessian) might affect performance in some cases. A justification for this simplification or a comparison with a full Stiefel update would be useful.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in DPP learning.
* The proposed EM algorithm is a novel and theoretically sound solution.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly motivated.
* It represents a significant advance in the field of DPPs and has potential applications in various domains.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The runtime complexity analysis could be more detailed, and the practical benefits might be limited in certain scenarios.
* The experimental validation is currently limited to a single dataset.
* The step size selection and the simplification of the Stiefel manifold update lack sufficient explanation.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field of DPPs. The proposed EM algorithm is a valuable addition to the existing toolbox for learning DPPs, and the experimental results are convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points raised in the weaknesses section.  Focusing on providing more details about the step size selection, a more thorough complexity analysis, and potentially adding experiments with a second dataset would significantly strengthen the paper.
