- Proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn a full, unconstrained DPP kernel by introducing hidden variable J (subset of eigenvectors) and exploiting eigendecomposition of K.  
- E-step sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), showing q is a k-DPP with a computable kernel, enabling efficient marginalization.  
- M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form via expected inclusion probs q(j∈J|Y) and updates eigenvectors by a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold using a matrix-exponential update.  
- EM avoids projection pathologies of projected-gradient-on-K (KA), which can drive K toward near-diagonality and lose negative interactions.  
- Runtime: asymptotic O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) for k ≪ N; reported 2.1× speedup over KA in experiments.  
- Experiments on Amazon baby-registry show improved test log-likelihood, especially when off-diagonal structure matters or data are scarce.  
- Distinguishes from prior work that uses diagonal/item-quality scaling, fixed mixtures, or parametric Bayesian kernels; aims for nonparametric full-kernel learning.  
- Novelty: EM over elementary-DPP mixture with efficient k-DPP E-step and closed-form eigenvalue updates appears new.  
- Technical soundness: E-step derivation and eigenvalue updates are well-motivated and computationally efficient; Stiefel updates are principled for orthogonality.  
- Caveat: eigenvector gradient formula (Eq.20) is valid only at initial q=p and M-step takes a single gradient step, so bound is not fully optimized w.r.t. V.  
- Caveat: M-step is approximate; monotonicity/convergence guarantees of EM are weakened and theoretical convergence to stationary points is not established.  
- Caveat: numerical issues when λ→1 are handled by clipping but lack deeper analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is fair versus KA but limited to one domain and primarily uses log-likelihood as metric; no canonical benchmarks or task-level MAP metrics.  
- Strengths: elegant EM formulation, efficient eigenvalue updates, principled Stiefel handling, clear diagnosis of KA pathology, runtime benefits, public code.  
- Weaknesses: approximate M-step with limited convergence theory, single-domain evaluation, limited baselines (no Bayesian or low-rank comparisons), scalability concerns for large N, and focus on likelihood over task metrics.  
- Suggestions: provide convergence diagnostics, compare to Bayesian parametric and low-rank baselines, evaluate on additional datasets (summarization, image search), report MAP set quality, and explore low-rank EM variants.  
- Request clarification: computational cost details for computing Q_Yi eigen-decompositions and constants in the O(n N k^2) term.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept — novel, useful, and practically faster/more robust than baseline despite limited theoretical guarantees and narrower evaluation.
