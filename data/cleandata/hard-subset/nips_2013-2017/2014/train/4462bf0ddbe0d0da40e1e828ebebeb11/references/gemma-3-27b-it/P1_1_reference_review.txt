## Review of "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning the kernel matrix *L* for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) from data. Existing approaches either partially learn *L* (e.g., learning item qualities only) or rely on restrictive parameterizations, sacrificing the attractive properties of full DPPs. The authors propose an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm that leverages the eigendecomposition of *L* to overcome the difficulties of directly maximizing the likelihood, particularly the need for problematic projection steps inherent in gradient ascent. The core idea is to introduce a hidden variable representing the eigenvectors used to construct a sample, allowing for a more tractable optimization.  They demonstrate both theoretical advantages (asymptotic runtime) and empirical improvements over gradient ascent on a product recommendation task.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper effectively situates itself within the existing literature on DPP learning. It acknowledges the limitations of prior works like Kulesza & Taskar (2012) [2], which focuses on learning item weights, and others employing parametric forms [8] or ensembles [3].  The authors correctly highlight that these approaches compromise either expressiveness or computational efficiency. This work builds upon the foundation of DPP theory [10, 21] and leverages eigendecomposition, a common technique for DPP manipulation. It also acknowledges and aims to overcome the issues with gradient ascent presented in the introduction.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:** The EM algorithm is a genuinely novel contribution, offering a theoretically sound and practically effective alternative to existing methods. The use of the eigendecomposition and introduction of a hidden variable are insightful.
* **Addresses a Core Challenge:** Learning *L* fully remains a major bottleneck for applying DPPs. This paper provides a substantial step forward in tackling that challenge.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a clear justification for the EM algorithm, including runtime analysis and comparison to gradient ascent, demonstrating its potential efficiency.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on the baby registry dataset are well-designed and show convincing results, demonstrating the robustness of the EM algorithm, especially in challenging scenarios. The comparison against K-Ascent is well-executed.
* **Clear and Well-Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The mathematical derivations are clear and easy to follow.
* **Practical Considerations:** The authors acknowledge and address practical challenges, such as initialization strategies and the need to avoid eigenvalues becoming exactly 1.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the theory is clear, implementing the EM algorithm, particularly the Stiefel manifold update, likely requires considerable effort and expertise. The paper could benefit from more details on implementation nuances.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are focused solely on a product recommendation task. While compelling, it would strengthen the paper to demonstrate the algorithm’s effectiveness on a wider range of DPP applications.
* **Dependence on Initialization:** While the paper demonstrates the EM algorithm is *more* robust to initialization than gradient ascent, the choice of initialization still significantly impacts performance. The discussion could be expanded on strategies for selecting a good initial kernel.
* **Computational Cost of E-Step:** The complexity analysis mentions Nk^2 for the E-step. Although better than gradient ascent asymptotically, for moderate values of N and k, this could still be computationally expensive. This trade-off isn’t fully explored.
* **Lack of discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity:** The paper does not discuss the sensitivity of performance to the step sizes (η) in the M-step updates.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically well-motivated algorithm for a challenging problem in DPP learning.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key ideas.
* The work has the potential to significantly impact the applicability of DPPs in various domains.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The implementation complexity may limit widespread adoption.
* The experimental validation is limited to a single application.
* The paper could benefit from more thorough discussion of initialization strategies and hyperparameter sensitivity.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of DPPs.  The proposed EM algorithm is a clever and effective solution to a difficult problem. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the work far outweigh them.  I believe this paper is well-suited for NIPS and would recommend acceptance.

**Rating:**  Strong Accept

**Confidence:** 8/10

**Further Suggestions for the Authors:**

* Provide more details on the implementation of the Stiefel manifold update.
* Consider conducting experiments on additional DPP applications to demonstrate broader applicability.
* Investigate the sensitivity of the algorithm to different initialization strategies.
* Analyze the impact of different step sizes (η) on the performance of the M-step.
* Discuss potential approximations to reduce the computational cost of the E-step for large datasets.
