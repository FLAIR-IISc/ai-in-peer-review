- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning the marginal kernel K of DPPs, avoiding projection issues in gradient ascent.
- Exploits eigendecomposition of K to derive E-step and M-step updates and avoid degenerate solutions.
- Provides theoretical derivations, explicit update formulas (Lemma 1; Equations 18–21), and runtime analysis.
- Shows asymptotic runtime improvement over gradient ascent (O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3)).
- Empirical evaluation on Amazon baby registry data demonstrates better log-likelihoods and faster runtime than gradient ascent.
- Careful experimental design including initialization strategies (Wishart vs. moments-matching) and per-category comparisons across 13 categories.
- Well-written, organized, and reproducible presentation with clear background, algorithm descriptions, and supplementary proofs.
- Novel approach: applying EM via eigendecomposition to DPP learning, avoiding projection-based optimization.
- Significant practical impact for applications requiring diverse subset selection; up to 16.5% log-likelihood gains and ~2.1x speedup reported.
- Acknowledges limitation: sensitivity to initialization, especially from a diagonal kernel.
- Minor clarity issue: explanation of why projection yields degenerate solutions could use a small matrix example.
- Minor omission: brief discussion of why K was chosen over L (common parameterization in prior work) would strengthen connections to literature.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant; recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
