- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP kernels using eigendecomposition to avoid projection steps that cause degenerate, near-diagonal kernels in gradient ascent methods.  
- Technical soundness: Provides rigorous derivation of E- and M-steps, analyzes computational complexity, and compares theoretically to gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- Empirical results: On a baby registry dataset, EM achieves ~3.7% better log-likelihood and ~2.1x faster runtime than K-Ascent.  
- Originality: Novel application of eigendecomposition-based EM to learning the full DPP kernel, distinguishing it from prior scalar/parametric or Bayesian approaches.  
- Significance: Improves practical DPP learning for diversity-focused applications (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement) by better capturing negative item interactions.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, with clear background, derivations, and readable pseudocode.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: Evaluation is confined to a single baby registry dataset, limiting evidence of broad applicability.  
- Weakness — limited comparisons: Only compared to gradient ascent (K-Ascent); missing comparisons to other DPP learning methods (e.g., Bayesian or alternative optimizers).  
- Weakness — scalability concerns: While asymptotic runtime favors EM, the O(N^3) cost of eigendecomposition may be prohibitive for very large N and isn't fully addressed.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-founded contribution that addresses a real limitation in DPP learning; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper advances DPP learning with a theoretically grounded, practically advantageous method.  
- Pro arguments: Novel EM approach avoiding projection issues, rigorous theory, demonstrated empirical gains, and clear presentation.  
- Con arguments: Single-dataset evaluation, lack of broader method comparisons, and incomplete treatment of extreme-scale numerical issues.
