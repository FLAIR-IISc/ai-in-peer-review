- Problem & approach: Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP marginal kernel K via eigendecomposition to avoid projection steps and degeneracy.
- Theoretical contribution: Principled EM derivation with elegant M-step updates using k-DPP marginalization, yielding efficient eigenvalue updates (O(n N k²)).
- Methodological differentiation: Does not require restrictive L-parameterizations or sacrifice DPP properties; provides a clear analysis of why projection-based methods fail.
- Experimental validation: Amazon baby registry experiments (13 categories, N≈71) show EM outperforms K-Ascent and relate performance gains to the d metric.
- Practical impact: Demonstrates ~2.1× runtime advantage and qualitatively better recommendations from EM versus K-Ascent.
- Limited experiments: Evaluation is confined to one dataset and 13 categories, limiting claims about generalizability to other DPP applications.
- Theoretical limitation: Claimed asymptotic speedup depends on k ≪ N and may vanish when k is comparable to N (e.g., document summarization).
- Initialization sensitivity: EM appears more robust than KA but experiments rely on specific initializations; sensitivity to other inits (especially many small eigenvalues) is not explored.
- Missing comparisons: Lacks evaluation against more recent/large-scale DPP methods (e.g., Nyström approximations, recent MAP inference work).
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, novel, and practically useful EM approach that advances DPP learning by avoiding projection pitfalls.
- Recommendation: Accept.
