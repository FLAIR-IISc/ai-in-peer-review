- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm using L's eigendecomposition to learn full DPP kernels, avoiding PSD projections and validated on baby-registry recommendation data.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on DPP sampling/marginalization and k-DPP ideas to tackle full L learning without strong parametric assumptions.  
- Novelty: Introducing an EM framework over eigenvectors/eigenvalues for DPP learning is a significant and original contribution.  
- Theoretical soundness: Derivation of the EM lower bound and updates appears mathematically justified and well-explained.  
- Practical advantages: EM avoids destabilizing PSD projections of gradient ascent and may have favorable asymptotic runtime.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments show improved log-likelihood and more sensible recommendations versus K-Ascent, with analysis linking gains to dataset characteristics.  
- Clarity of presentation: Paper is well-written, with clear derivations and helpful algorithm pseudocode.  
- Implementation complexity: Stiefel manifold updates and other implementation details are non-trivial and could hinder adoption.  
- Scalability concerns: Experiments on N=71 leave practical scalability to N > 1000 unclear; O(nNk²) terms may still be a bottleneck.  
- Sensitivity to initialization: Performance depends on initialization (Wishart vs moments-matching), but robustness across different inits is not thoroughly studied.  
- Limited baselines: Comparison is mainly to K-Ascent; broader benchmarking against other DPP learning methods is lacking.  
- Hyperparameter discussion: Paper omits detailed discussion of hyperparameters (step size, iterations) and their effects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound method with empirical gains, clear writing, and valuable insights into dataset-algorithm interactions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Implementation difficulty, unproven large-scale scalability, reliance on initialization, and limited baselines.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, impactful work that outweighs minor weaknesses and is suitable for acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address Stiefel update implementation/details, scalability analysis, initialization sensitivity, additional baselines, and hyperparameter reporting.
