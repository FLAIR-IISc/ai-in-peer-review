- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning the marginal kernel K of DPPs, avoiding problematic projection steps in gradient methods.  
- Identifies eigendecomposition of K as the key to deriving a robust EM procedure for DPPs.  
- Derives a Jensen-based lower bound and provides closed-form updates for eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Theoretical analysis shows sound optimization landscape and justifies the EM approach.  
- Demonstrates experimentally on a baby registry dataset that EM outperforms K-Ascent across initializations and data regimes.  
- Reports 3.7% average log-likelihood improvement over K-Ascent with Wishart initialization and 4.5% gain with limited data (n = 2N).  
- Shows asymptotic runtime advantage: EM O(n N k^2) vs. K-Ascent O(n N^3), with ~2.1x average speedup in practice.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, uses meaningful metrics and visualizations, and includes supplemental reproducibility details.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible to ML/NIPS readers familiar with DPPs.  
- Original contribution: novel use of eigendecomposition to avoid projection-induced degeneracy, distinguishing it from prior constrained/parametric DPP work.  
- Significance: addresses a practical barrier to DPP adoption for diversity-aware subset selection tasks (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement).  
- Main limitations: sensitivity to initialization (requires non-diagonal starts) and evaluation limited to a single dataset domain.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically strong, novel, well-validated, and of substantial practical significance.
