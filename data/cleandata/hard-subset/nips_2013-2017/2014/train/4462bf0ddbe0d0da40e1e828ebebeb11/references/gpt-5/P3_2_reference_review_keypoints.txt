- Proposes EM-like MLE for unconstrained DPPs by optimizing eigen-decomposition K = VΛV^T and introducing latent eigenvector subset J via elementary DPP mixture.  
- E-step sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with a compact, computable kernel; enables efficient marginals for λ-updates.  
- M-step yields closed-form eigenvalue updates and a gradient-based eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Claims better empirical behavior than projected gradient on K (avoids projection-induced near-diagonal degeneration).  
- Runtime argued to be asymptotically faster for moderate k: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs. naive O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3).  
- Novelty: first general-purpose MLE algorithm operating on full eigendecomposition without constraining L.  
- Strength: elegant use of elementary DPP mixture to derive an EM lower bound and tractable posterior.  
- Strength: compact expression for k-DPP posterior kernel QY leading to efficient λ marginal computations.  
- Strength: closed-form, self-normalizing eigenvalue updates and principled Stiefel manifold eigenvector step.  
- Strength: runtime analysis favorable for moderate k and empirical gains on a real catalog dataset, with a diagnostic correlating gains to off-diagonal strength.  
- Concern: Convergence guarantees are unclear because only a single V gradient step is taken and step size is chosen by checking true likelihood, breaking standard EM monotonicity proofs.  
- Concern: Complexity claim for λ-updates needs detailed derivation and routine description (k-DPP marginals computation, costs of forming QY/HY).  
- Concern: V-update involves O(N^3) matrix exponentials per line search—number of steps and practicality for large N (500–5000) not addressed.  
- Concern: Baselines are weak; should compare to direct (V,λ) optimization via manifold methods without EM to isolate eigenspace vs EM effects.  
- Concern: Numerical fixes (tightening λ ≤ 1) are ad hoc; recommend explicit barrier/penalty regularization and discussion of conditioning/damping for HY inverses.  
- Concern: Experiments limited to one domain (N≈71); need synthetic recovery experiments, sensitivity to n/k/initialization, and variance reporting.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written but central derivations (Lemma 1, marginals, V-gradient) are deferred to supplement and should have main-text sketches; notation for IY needs illustration.  
- Originality: EM formulation leveraging eigenvector-subset latent variable and k-DPP posteriors is novel and nontrivial; Stiefel update is a useful contribution.  
- Significance: Advances DPP learning beyond partial/parametric methods and could become standard with clearer convergence and scalability extensions.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant, novel EM, closed-form λ-update, avoids projection issues, empirical improvements and faster than baseline.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited convergence guarantees, scalability concerns for large N, weak baseline comparisons, ad hoc numerical fixes, limited theoretical consistency analysis.  
- Suggestions: provide convergence discussion/empirical monotonicity plots, add direct (V,λ) baseline, include synthetic recovery and sensitivity experiments, add explicit regularization for λ→1 and discuss damping, and explore low-rank/feature-parameterized variants for scalability.
