- Method summary: EM over K=VΛV^T treats active-eigenvector subset J as latent; E-step computes k-DPP posteriors, M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates and a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step for eigenvectors, avoiding PSD/eigenvalue clipping.  
- Novelty: EM over the full spectral parameterization using k-DPP E-steps and manifold-constrained V updates is a novel contribution relative to prior DPP learning work.  
- Correctness: Jensen bound and choice q = pK(J | Yi) are sound; identifying the posterior as a k-DPP enabling tractable marginals is elegant and correct.  
- Eigenvalue update: λ′j = (1/n) Σi p(j ∈ J | Yi) is intuitive, removes projection, and is plausibly computable in O(n N k^2) via low-rank structure.  
- Eigenvector update caveat: gradient formula is valid only when q = p and only for the first step, so the single-step Stiefel update is heuristic and monotonicity of the bound is not guaranteed.  
- Monotonicity suggestion: report likelihood vs iterations or provide guarantees/empirical evidence that L increases across EM iterations.  
- Complexity claims: EM cost stated as O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3); clarify cost of likelihood evaluations during line search (determinants) and whether they add O(n k^3) or O(n N^3) in practice.  
- Numerical stability: λ near 1 requires clipping; current pragmatic clipping may bias updates—recommend safer parameterization (e.g., logit).  
- Clarity fixes: clarify UYi as submatrix of U=V^T, explicitly show O(N k^2) computation of p(j ∈ J | Yi), and standardize whether line search checks L or the lower bound F.  
- Empirical results: on Amazon baby registry EM yields higher test likelihoods and ~2× speedups vs projected gradient with Wishart init, and performs better in data-poor regimes; comparable to stronger moments-matching init.  
- Practical significance: addresses degeneracy of projected gradient and is useful for practitioners modeling repulsion; promising when k ≪ N.  
- Strengths: elegant EM formulation, closed-form eigenvalue updates, efficient k-DPP posterior use, principled Stiefel treatment, solid empirical evaluation, and released code/data.  
- Weaknesses: heuristic V-update, limited domains and N scale in experiments, lack of comparisons to Bayesian parametric methods and stronger non-projection optimizers, and incomplete details on P(Y) computation during line search.  
- V-update suggestions: explore retraction-based Riemannian optimizers or trust-region methods that allow multiple feasible steps without repeated matrix exponentials.  
- Scalability suggestion: validate on larger N and investigate low-rank parameterizations to mitigate O(N^3) costs.  
- Minor comments: include statistics of learned set-size distributions, systematically use the off-diagonality metric d in experiments, and justify the Wishart rescaling with statistics.  
- Recommendation: Strong, well-motivated paper; lean toward acceptance subject to clarifications and the suggested minor additions.
