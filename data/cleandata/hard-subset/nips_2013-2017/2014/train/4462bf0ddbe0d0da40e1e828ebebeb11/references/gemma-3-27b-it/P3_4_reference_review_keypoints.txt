- Paper proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPP kernels by leveraging eigendecomposition and a hidden-variable formulation for eigenvectors.  
- EM approach avoids problematic projections and offers asymptotic runtime advantages over direct kernel gradient ascent.  
- Authors position the work well relative to prior DPP methods (partial learning, ensemble, Bayesian) and build on eigendecomposition insights.  
- Comparison to projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) effectively highlights stability and PSD-maintenance benefits of EM.  
- Theoretical derivation of EM updates appears sound and clarifies computational complexity.  
- Empirical results on a baby registry product recommendation task show statistically significant improvements over K-Ascent, especially for complex kernels.  
- Paper is clearly written with effective background and algorithm descriptions.  
- Practical implementation concerns such as initialization and avoiding singular kernels are addressed.  
- EM algorithm implementation is complex, notably requiring Stiefel manifold optimization despite some simplifications.  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset and task, limiting assessment of generalizability.  
- Step-size selection is treated heuristically with insufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Introduction and treatment of k-DPPs is abrupt and lacks discussion of how k affects learning and sensitivity.  
- Runtime claims depend on 'k' (largest training set size), which can be large in practice and reduce the claimed advantage.  
- Pro arguments: novel, theoretically justified, empirically better, and well-presented.  
- Con arguments: implementation burden, limited experiments, unclear step-size methodology, and k-dependent complexity.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to DPP learning with a recommendation to accept despite noted concerns.  
- Minor suggestions: provide more implementation details (Stiefel optimization, step-size search), broaden experiments, and elaborate on k-DPP choice and k-dependence in runtime.
