Summary
The paper proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn a full, unconstrained DPP kernel by working in the eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel K = VΛV^T. It introduces a latent variable J (a subset of eigenvectors) via the known mixture-of-elementary-DPP representation, derives a lower bound using Jensen, shows that the E-step reduces to a k-DPP over eigenvector indices, and gives updates: a closed-form update for eigenvalues λj via posterior inclusion probabilities p(j ∈ J | Yi), and a gradient step for V on the Stiefel manifold. The authors contrast this with projected gradient ascent on K, arguing that projection encourages near-diagonal solutions and poor local optima. On a baby registry recommendation dataset, EM improves test log-likelihood over the KA baseline (especially when off-diagonal structure matters) and is faster in practice.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on standard DPP theory (mixture over elementary projection DPPs, sampling) and connects to earlier partial-parameter learning (e.g., quality weights, mixtures of fixed Ls, parametric Bayesian approaches). To my knowledge, an EM view with a latent subset of eigenvectors to learn a nonparametric K is novel and interesting. The references include core DPP literature, though the formatting and completeness are problematic (see below).

Strengths
- Conceptually clean: exploiting the known mixture representation for an EM scheme is elegant.
- Addresses a real pain point: projected gradient on K often collapses off-diagonals; the proposed approach avoids PSD/upper-bound projections on Λ and uses manifold updates for V.
- Computationally motivated: per-iteration cost scales as O(n N k^2) for data-dependent parts (k = max |Yi|), better than O(n N^3) when k ≪ N, with the caveat of N^3 Stiefel updates during line search.
- Empirical evidence on a sizable, practical dataset, showing robustness to initialization and runtime benefits; the qualitative MAP example is persuasive.

Weaknesses and issues (many minor but cumulatively significant)
Technical clarity/rigor:
- Lemma 1 (E-step kernel): there is a serious ambiguity/inconsistency in dimensions. You define U = V^T (N×N). If UYi denotes selecting rows, then ZYi = UYi(UYi)^T is |Yi|×|Yi| and cannot be pre- and post-multiplied by R (N×N). If UYi selects columns (U_:,Yi, N×|Yi|), then ZYi = U_:,Yi U_:,Yi^T is N×N and QYi = R ZYi R has the right shape for a k-DPP over eigenvector indices. The manuscript must resolve this unambiguously with explicit shapes; as written it is incorrect.
- Eq. (19): The stated marginal p(j ∈ J | Yi) = ∑r v̂r(j)^2 requires that the k-DPP kernel be a projection onto span{v̂r}. That is true if you diagonalize QYi to a rank-k projection; but this crucial low-rank structure and how to compute v̂ without an O(N^3) eigendecomposition is relegated to the supplement. In the main text, the complexity claim O(n N k^2) is not justified. Please either include a clear derivation sketch (leveraging that QYi has rank ≤ k via R U_:,Yi) or correct the complexity.
- Eq. (20) gradient: You state it is valid only for the first gradient step (q = p), then proceed to do only a single step per M-step. This is not standard EM; at best it is a generalized EM with a highly approximate M-step. The monotonicity argument is muddled: you say you check L for improvement in the line search, but Algorithm 2 uses both L and F inconsistently (see below). Please clarify that this is generalized EM, clearly state the objective monitored (L vs F), and provide a monotonicity guarantee (or an empirical check).
- Algorithm 2 inconsistencies:
  - Inner loop condition uses L(V′, λ′) > L(V, λ′) while δ uses F(V′, λ′) − F(V, λ). This mixes objectives and different λ’s.
  - Step-size updates are written “η ← η2” (appears to mean η ← η/2 but is typeset ambiguously here and in Algorithm 1). Also, after acceptance you set “η ← 2η,” which is unusual and unexplained.
  - The gradient symbol switches between ∂F/∂V and ∂L/∂V in Eq. (21).
- Complexity: The stated O(n N k^2) ignores the O(k^3) per-instance inversion of HYi (k×k); please include it. Also, the Stiefel update requires computing a matrix exponential exp[···] of an N×N matrix; discussing its cost and numerical stabilization is necessary. As written, the asymptotic comparison to KA is somewhat optimistic without these details.
- Constraints: You propose “tightening” the λ ≤ 1 constraint to avoid R blowing up. This ad hoc fix needs to be spelled out (e.g., λmax = 1−ε with what ε?) and its effect on optimality discussed.

Methodological concerns:
- Identifiability: Learning V and Λ is nonidentifiable under rotations within degenerate eigenspaces and sign flips; there is no discussion of how EM navigates this or whether multiple equivalent solutions are obtained.
- Regularization: No regularization on Λ (e.g., trace) or on V is used. Overfitting risk and any mitigation (early stopping, priors, shrinkage) are not discussed.
- Baselines: Only KA on K is compared. A stronger baseline would include (i) projected gradient on L; (ii) structurally parameterized L (quality-similarity) trained by gradient; (iii) Bayesian parametric approaches (even if restricted). This would contextualize the advantage.

Clarity/formatting:
- Numerous typos and formatting issues: “negelecting”→“neglecting”; “diversityseeking” missing space; word breaks “param- eterized”; stray “Tuesday, August 5, 14”; footnote marker “1” is misplaced; “Justies”→“Justifies” in ref title; inconsistent use of bold/italics for matrices; inconsistent notation between UYi and VYi; ambiguous norm definition for d in Sec. 4.1 (“d = 1N ||M||F ||diag(M)||2” needs parentheses and a clearer definition—do you mean off-diagonal energy?).
- Figures are not present; quantitative results are summarized only as medians/percentages without CIs or exact values; please include full tables/plots with scales.
- The reference list is severely incomplete and noncompliant: many entries lack years, venues, pages; some are duplicates; punctuation is inconsistent. This is below NeurIPS standards.

Experimental evaluation:
- Only a single application/dataset; consider adding synthetic experiments to validate recovery of off-diagonal structure and sensitivity to k, N, and initialization.
- Hyperparameters and stopping: threshold c, step-size schedule, numerical stabilizers (ε), and implementation details for matrix exponentials are not specified, which impedes reproducibility.
- The “moment matching” initialization (Eq. 23) is heuristic; a brief justification or ablation measuring its effect on both methods would help.

Pros and cons for acceptance
Pros:
- Novel and elegant EM perspective on DPP learning with practical benefits.
- Addresses a real failure mode of projected gradient.
- Promising empirical gains and speedups.

Cons:
- Multiple technical ambiguities (dimension errors, objective mixing) that must be corrected.
- Incomplete theoretical and computational details in the main text; key claims pushed to supplement.
- Weak baselines and limited experimental breadth.
- Significant presentation and reference-quality issues.

Recommendation
I lean to reject in the current form due to the accumulation of technical ambiguities and presentation/formatting problems, despite the interesting idea and promising results. With careful correction of the derivations, a clear generalized-EM framing with monotonicity guarantees or empirical evidence, stronger baselines, and a substantial cleanup of writing and references, this work could be impactful.

Confidence
High on the assessment of technical clarity and DPP background; medium on empirical conclusions given missing figures/details.
