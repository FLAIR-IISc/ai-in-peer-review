Summary
The paper proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn a full, unconstrained determinantal point process (DPP) kernel K by exploiting its eigendecomposition. The key idea is to reinterpret a DPP as a mixture over “elementary DPPs” indexed by subsets J of eigenvectors. The E-step sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), which the authors claim is a k-DPP; the M-step has a closed-form update for eigenvalues (averaging posterior inclusion probabilities) and a constrained gradient step for eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold. The approach is contrasted with projected gradient ascent on K (KA), which can become near-diagonal after projection and fail to model diversity. On a baby registry dataset, the proposed EM is reported to yield modest but consistent test log-likelihood gains and 2.1× speedups.

Strengths
- Addresses an important and challenging problem: learning full DPP kernels without restrictive parameterizations.
- Sound and appealing high-level formulation: latent subset J over eigenvectors enables an EM decomposition; eigenvalues admit a simple probabilistic update.
- Sensible geometric treatment of the orthogonality constraint via Stiefel manifold updates.
- Useful diagnosis of KA’s failure mode (projection-induced near-diagonality) and empirical robustness to initialization.
- Practical dataset and evidence of runtime advantages when k ≪ N.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Technical correctness and derivations
1) Marginals in Eq. (19) appear incorrect or at least ambiguously stated. As written, q(j∈J|Yi)=Σr v̂r(j)^2 where v̂r are eigenvectors of QYi. If the sum is over all N orthonormal eigenvectors, this equals 1 for every j, which is clearly wrong. If the sum is over only the k nonzero eigenvectors (rank-k case), it still does not match general k-DPP inclusion marginals for an L-ensemble; these typically involve eigenvalues (e.g., elementary symmetric polynomials) and are not pure sums of squared eigenvector components except for projection DPPs. Since the λ update (Eq. 18) depends critically on Eq. (19), this is a major concern. The supplement is referenced but not provided; without a correct, unambiguous derivation (including which subset of eigenvectors is summed and why), reproducibility and correctness are in question.

2) Lemma 1 and Eq. (16–17) have dimension/notation ambiguities. U=Vᵀ (N×N). If UYi denotes a row-selected submatrix with |Yi| rows, then ZYi=UYi(UYi)ᵀ is |Yi|×|Yi|, yet QYi=R ZYi R suggests N×N. If UYi denotes column selection, ZYi is N×N. The paper needs to specify clearly the selection operator and shapes; as written, the formula is hard to implement.

3) Gradient for eigenvectors (Eq. 20) is said to be valid “for the particular case of the initial gradient, where we have q=p.” At the beginning of the M-step, q is computed from the current parameters (so q=p at that point), but the paper then performs only a single gradient step on V and evaluates likelihood with updated q for line search. This is a generalized EM approximation; the paper should state the precise conditions under which likelihood is guaranteed to be non-decreasing and clarify which objective (F or L) is being optimized in each step. As written, Algorithm 2 mixes F (for the outer stopping criterion) and L (for step-size acceptance), which is non-standard and potentially breaks the monotonicity of the bound.

4) Likelihood and gradients: the K-based log-likelihood uses log|det(K−IY)| and gradients require inverting (K−IY). For valid DPPs, some sets can have exactly zero probability, making determinants zero; what regularization or numerical safeguards are used to avoid singularities (and exploding gradients)? This is not discussed.

Algorithmic/pseudocode issues
5) Severe line-search typos: Algorithm 1 and 2 both set η←η2 in the inner loop (with η initialized to 1). This does not reduce the step size and appears to be a bug (likely intended η←η/2). The outer loop in Algorithm 2 then sets η←2η after acceptance. As written, the line search cannot function as intended.

6) Complexity claims O(n N k^2). Constructing QYi and computing the statistics needed for Eq. (19) for each Yi at scale n requires careful use of low-rank structure; the paper does not make explicit how to avoid O(N^3) eigen-decompositions per Yi. Without a concrete algorithmic description, the asymptotics are hard to verify.

Experimental evaluation
7) Limited baselines. The only baseline is KA, which is known to suffer from projection issues in this setting. Missing comparisons to alternatives such as parameterized/Bayesian DPP learning (e.g., Affandi et al.), low-rank parametrizations, mirror descent/natural gradient with PSD-preserving reparameterizations, or structured DPP learning. A synthetic recovery experiment would strengthen the technical claims (e.g., recovery of K under varying k, N, n, and noise).

8) Evaluation details. Figures are referenced but not present here; claims of 3.7% average improvement and 2.1× speedup are not accompanied by variance/error bars, number of iterations, or sensitivity to k and N. The anecdotal MAP example is not sufficient to assess practical impact. Also, the code link points to Google Code, which is now defunct.

Clarity and presentation
9) Notational inconsistencies and typos: “negelecting,” “non-parametric,” inconsistent parentheses, ambiguous definitions of IY and selection matrices, stray “Tuesday, August 5, 14.” Algorithms mix L and F in acceptance/stopping criteria.

10) References are incomplete (missing years/venues/pages) and inconsistent in formatting.

Quality: Promising idea, but the current manuscript has significant technical ambiguities (notably Eq. 19 and Lemma 1), pseudocode errors, and insufficient experimental validation.

Clarity: Generally readable, but critical derivations are unclear or deferred; notation needs tightening; algorithms contain obvious bugs.

Originality: The EM formulation over eigen-index subsets is interesting and, to my knowledge, novel for learning full DPP kernels.

Significance: Potentially useful if made correct and reproducible; current evaluation is not strong enough to establish practice-changing impact.

Pros for acceptance
- Interesting EM formulation leveraging the DPP eigenstructure.
- Closed-form eigenvalue updates and principled handling of orthogonality constraints.
- Addresses a real pain point (projection degeneracy) and shows some empirical gains.

Cons for acceptance
- Potentially incorrect or at least underspecified core update (Eq. 19).
- Algorithmic bugs in pseudocode; unclear convergence/monotonicity.
- Ambiguous derivations and shapes; key steps hidden in supplement.
- Limited baselines and analysis; incomplete references and formatting issues.

Recommendation
Reject in current form. The paper could become strong with corrected/complete derivations (esp. Eq. 19 and Lemma 1), fixed algorithms, clearer complexity justifications, stronger baselines (including parametric/Bayesian and low-rank alternatives), and tightened presentation.
