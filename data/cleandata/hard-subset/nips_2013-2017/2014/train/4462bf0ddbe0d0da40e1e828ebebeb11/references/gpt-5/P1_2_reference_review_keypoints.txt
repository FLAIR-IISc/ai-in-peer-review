- Proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn full unconstrained DPP kernels by optimizing over eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T, treating subsets of eigenvectors J as latent variables.  
- E-step: shows q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y) is a k-DPP with an explicit kernel, making the E-step tractable.  
- M-step: gives closed-form eigenvalue (λ) updates via posterior marginals and a Stiefel-manifold gradient update for eigenvectors V using a multiplicative update.  
- Argues EM avoids projection-induced eigenvalue collapse seen in projected gradient ascent on K (KA), preserving diversity modeling.  
- Empirical result: on a large baby-registry dataset, EM improves test log-likelihood over KA and is about 2× faster.  
- Includes analyses of initialization sensitivity (Wishart vs moment-matching), data-poor regimes, and when off-diagonals matter more EM provides larger gains.  
- Situates work relative to prior DPP methods, noting novelty in using elementary-DPP mixtures and k-DPP conditionals to derive an EM for nonparametric kernel learning.  
- Strength: technical novelty and principled tractable EM derivation with closed-form λ updates and Stiefel update for V.  
- Strength: clear diagnosis and insight into failure modes of KA due to projection degeneracy.  
- Strength: computational analysis showing regimes (k ≪ N) where EM is asymptotically and practically faster.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation includes real-world data, initialization/data-size studies, correlation analysis, qualitative MAP example, and released code/data.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to one domain; lacks comparisons to Bayesian parametric, low-rank/structured DPPs, mixtures, and strong submodular baselines.  
- Weakness: eigenvector M-step uses a single gradient step and line search that re-runs E-step checking L rather than bound F; convergence/monotonicity guarantees are unclear.  
- Weakness: complexity claim O(n N k^2) for ∂F/∂V is questionable because naive HYi inversion is O(k^3); needs clarification of algebra/fast solves.  
- Weakness: notational/dimensional inconsistencies in Lemma 1 and definitions around U, VYi, BYi, HYi create confusion.  
- Weakness: numerical stability concerns require clipping λ near 1; practical caps and sensitivity/conditioning of HYi should be discussed.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns for large N due to repeated O(N^3) eigendecompositions during line search; low-rank EM variants should be discussed.  
- Weakness: limited statistical regularization and no analysis of overfitting or uncertainty (no priors, penalties, or error bars reported).  
- Quality: technically sound derivation overall, but some derivations deferred and complexity claim and notation need fixes.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with intuitive explanations, but specific sections (Lemma 1, matrix dimensions, line-search mechanics) need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: EM leveraging elementary-DPP mixtures and k-DPP conditionals for full-kernel learning appears novel; Stiefel update application is neat.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem; provides a practical, more robust alternative to projected gradient that is valuable for several applications.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled method, addresses real KA weakness, solid computational analysis, faster on real dataset, code/data released.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical scope and baselines, derivational/notation issues, unclear convergence properties, and scalability not fully demonstrated.  
- Suggestions: clarify Lemma 1 dimensions and QYi construction; justify or correct the O(n N k^2) cost and describe linear-algebra tricks used.  
- Suggestions: provide convergence discussion for generalized EM, add comparisons to alternative learning methods and synthetic recovery studies.  
- Suggestions: explore low-rank/regularized variants and report variance/error bars, plus more details on line-search and stopping criteria.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on broader empirical validation and clarifications on complexity, notation, and convergence.
