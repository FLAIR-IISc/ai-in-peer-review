- Contribution: Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the marginal kernel K of DPPs by leveraging eigendecomposition, avoiding problematic projection steps of gradient methods.
- Motivation/problem: Gradient-ascent approaches require projections that often yield degenerate (nearly diagonal) K matrices, undermining DPP diversity modeling.
- Method: EM is derived with clear E- and M-step updates using eigendecomposition to sidestep projection-induced degeneracy.
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous mathematical derivations and a strong theoretical analysis of why gradient ascent degenerates (Section 3.1).
- Computational complexity: Presents asymptotic runtime advantages over gradient ascent, with detailed complexity analysis (some details in supplement).
- Experiments: Evaluated on a baby-registry dataset across initializations, showing consistent improvements over K-Ascent.
- Empirical results: Up to 16.5% log-likelihood gain in some categories and average runtime improvement of 2.1x versus K-Ascent.
- Clarity: Well-organized, consistent notation, precise algorithm descriptions; supplement contains reproducibility details.
- Needed clarity: Could provide more intuitive explanation of why projection steps cause degeneracy and move some complexity arguments into main text.
- Originality: Novel and well-justified application of EM to DPP learning distinct from prior gradient or structure-restricted approaches.
- Significance: Practically important for subset selection tasks; method is generalizable and likely impactful for recommendation and summarization.
- Limitations: Limited experimental validation (single dataset) and slightly limited discussion of extreme-case limitations and initialization sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution with sound theory, empirical gains, and clear writing; recommended for publication.
- Recommendation: Accept.
