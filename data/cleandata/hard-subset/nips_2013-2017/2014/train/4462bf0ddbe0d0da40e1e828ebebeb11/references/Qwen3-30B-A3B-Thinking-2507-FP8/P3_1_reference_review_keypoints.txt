- Proposes an EM algorithm for unconstrained DPP learning that uses the eigendecomposition and a hidden variable J to avoid problematic projection steps.  
- Derivation is technically sound, using Jensen’s inequality and Stiefel manifold optimization, with runtime O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3).  
- Runtime is asymptotically better than gradient ascent for k << N, but experiments (N≈70) provide limited evidence for this claim.  
- Theoretical analysis shows projection in gradient ascent leads to degenerate (nearly diagonal) kernels that fail to model negative interactions.  
- Novel contribution: applying EM to DPP learning via eigen-decomposition is a significant conceptual advance over prior approaches.  
- Paper situates itself against earlier work but omits deeper comparison with more recent DPP learning methods (e.g., [14,15,20]).  
- Demonstrates practical impact on a baby registry dataset, showing improved diversity modeling and robustness to initialization/dataset changes.  
- Scalability evidence is limited—no large-N experiments (e.g., N=10,000+) to validate real-world applicability.  
- Initialization analysis is incomplete; effects of Wishart vs. moment-matching on convergence/performance are underexplored.  
- The claim of being “asymptotically faster” is somewhat overstated without showing the O(N k^2) term dominates in relevant regimes.  
- Experimental results show clear advantages and a 2.1x runtime improvement on average, but sample sizes and N are moderate.  
- Writing and organization are clear and rigorous, though some derivations (e.g., Eq. 20) are deferred to the supplement and initialization choices could be better explained.  
- Strengths include clear identification of the projection problem, an elegant EM formulation, thorough theoretical work, solid empirical diversity gains, and a real-world application.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, meaningful contribution with minor weaknesses (mainly scalability evidence) that do not outweigh the paper’s merits.
