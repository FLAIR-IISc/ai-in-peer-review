- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPP kernels by treating eigenvector selection as latent variables, avoiding projection steps.  
- Derives closed-form E-step and eigenvalue M-step updates and an efficient eigenvector update procedure.  
- Provides runtime analysis showing asymptotic advantage of EM over projected gradient ascent.  
- Empirically outperforms K-Ascent on Amazon baby registry data (up to 16.5% log-likelihood improvement) and is ~2.1× faster on average.  
- Original contribution: first EM-based approach exploiting eigendecomposition for unconstrained DPP learning.  
- Technical quality: rigorous derivations (Lemma 1, Eq.18) and detailed complexity analysis.  
- Significance: advances practical DPP parameter learning for diversity-based subset selection tasks.  
- Clarity: well-organized paper with helpful background, figures, and supplemental derivations.  
- Initialization sensitivity: EM’s advantage depends on good initialization (Wishart), with minimal gain using moments-matching.  
- Eigenvector updates incur high computational overhead due to repeated objective evaluations (O(T1 T2 N^3)).  
- Limited theoretical guarantees: no discussion of convergence or local-optima properties for this EM variant.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—technically sound, empirically validated, and practically useful despite noted limitations.
