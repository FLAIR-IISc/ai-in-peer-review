- Tackles learning the DPP kernel L from observed subsets using an EM algorithm that leverages eigendecomposition to avoid projection issues.  
- Introduces a novel EM formulation with a hidden variable for eigenvector selection, substantially different from prior work.  
- Theoretical claims: EM is more robust (avoids degenerate solutions) and offers an asymptotic runtime speedup vs. gradient ascent.  
- Empirical results on baby-registry product recommendation show EM outperforms K-Ascent, especially with strong negative interactions or limited data.  
- Paper is generally well-written, with clear background, logical organization, and precise algorithmic details.  
- Good positioning within related work, highlighting limitations of previous DPP learning approaches.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant, computationally hard problem in DPP learning.  
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated algorithmic approach using eigendecomposition.  
- Strength: Theoretical justification and asymptotic runtime analysis strengthen the contribution.  
- Strength: Empirical validation demonstrates practical benefits on a real task.  
- Weakness: M-step complexity — eigenvector updates rely on approximations and step-size search, adding implementation burden.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to initialization remains; moments-matching helps but is not a complete solution.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited to one domain/dataset; lacks evaluation across diverse settings (e.g., varying sparsity).  
- Weakness: Practical runtime gains are modest (≈2.1×); paper needs a more detailed runtime breakdown/profiling.  
- Weakness: Assumes DPP structure without analyzing model fit or methods for detecting misspecification.  
- Qualitative assessment: Technically sound derivation and convincing analysis of gradient ascent issues, but M-step practicalities need more emphasis.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 8.5/10; Originality 9/10; Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses core challenge, novel EM, empirical improvements, and theoretical insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: M-step complexity/scalability concerns, limited experiments, and unexamined DPP assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: clarify M-step computational cost, expand experiments to other datasets/domains, and discuss DPP model assumptions and validation.
