- Proposes an EM-style algorithm for nonparametric DPP learning using a latent eigencomponent subset J and the spectral mixture view.  
- E-step: posterior q(J|Y) is a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel enabling efficient computation.  
- M-step (eigenvalues): closed-form update λ_j = (1/n) Σ_i q(j∈J|Y_i) computable via k-DPP marginals, with numerical clipping near λ→1.  
- M-step (eigenvectors): single constrained gradient step on the Stiefel manifold per EM iteration (generalized EM), with line search against true likelihood.  
- Avoids PSD/eigenvalue projection issues that hinder projected gradient ascent on K.  
- Experiments on a large baby-registry dataset show improved test log-likelihood and faster runtime, especially when negative off-diagonal interactions are strong.  
- Differs from prior work by learning full K nonparametrically in eigenspace rather than adjusting only quality weights or using parametric/Bayesian forms.  
- The EM construction leverages known DPP spectral and k-DPP identities in a novel application to learning.  
- Decomposition p(Y)=Σ_J p(Y|J)p(J) and Jensen-based variational EM formulation are technically sound.  
- E-step derivation is elegant and computationally enabling, though its proof is deferred to the supplement.  
- Eigenvalue updates are a strong contribution, with practical caveats and clipping handled reasonably.  
- Eigenvector update as a single gradient step yields a generalized EM; monotonicity is enforced empirically but lacks formal guarantees.  
- Complexity estimate O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) is plausible, but cost of likelihood evaluation during line search needs clarification.  
- Dataset/setup and dual initializations (Wishart, moment-matching) are appropriate for sensitivity analysis.  
- Baseline (projected gradient on K) is reasonable, but comparisons to parametric/Bayesian and low-rank DPP methods are missing.  
- Would benefit from additional metrics beyond likelihood (diversity, coverage, relevance) to validate practical gains.  
- Runtime comparisons favor the EM method; scaling studies and Nyström/low-rank experiments for larger N are suggested.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with helpful algorithm boxes and complexity discussion.  
- Clarify Eq. (3) vs standard P(Y)=det(K_Y)det(I−K), definition of I_Y, and computational shortcuts for L during line search.  
- State explicitly that the method is generalized EM and discuss monotonicity/convergence conditions.  
- Request more implementation details: V-update method, matrix exponential approximation, step-size schedule, stopping criteria, and λ clipping handling.  
- Contribution is original: EM over spectral mixture with k-DPP E-step and closed-form eigenvalue updates is novel and practical.  
- Significant for applications needing pairwise repulsion; likely adoptable and extensible with regularization/structure.  
- Pros: elegant EM derivation, closed-form eigenvalue updates, avoids projection pitfalls, robust initialization behavior, good runtime and likelihood gains tied to off-diagonal signal.  
- Cons/questions: limited baselines, no formal monotonicity guarantees, unclear likelihood computation cost, sensitivity near λ→1 and to k, and no regularization to prevent overfitting.  
- Suggestions: add regularization (spectral shrinkage/sparsity), include Lemma 1 sketch, add datasets and baselines, report recommendation metrics, and explore low-rank/Nyström scaling.  
- Recommendation: strong accept leaning, contingent on addressing clarity points and expanding empirical breadth.
