Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes an EM-style algorithm for maximum likelihood learning of determinantal point processes (DPPs) without restrictive parameterizations of the kernel. It parameterizes the marginal kernel K via its eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T, introduces a latent index set J selecting a subset of eigenvectors (mixture-of-elementary DPP view), and derives an EM lower bound. The E-step sets q(J|Y) to the exact posterior p(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP. The M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form via expected inclusion probabilities, and updates eigenvectors by a Stiefel-manifold gradient step using a simplified derivative valid when q = p. The method avoids PSD/eigenvalue projection that can cause degeneracy for projected gradient ascent (KA). Empirical results on an Amazon baby registry dataset show moderate test log-likelihood gains and reduced runtime over KA.

This builds on classic DPP machinery and learning (Kulesza & Taskar; Affandi et al.), uses k-DPPs, and the mixture-of-elementary DPP sampling viewpoint. Compared to learning only item-quality scalings or mixtures of fixed L-ensembles, this aims to learn a full, unconstrained kernel.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: Using the mixture over elementary DPPs as a latent-variable model to drive an EM-like optimization is elegant and addresses PSD constraints without projection.
- Eigenvalue update is simple, self-normalizing, and exploits k-DPP marginals; complexity claims O(nNk^2) are attractive for small k.
- Clear identification of a real failure mode of projected gradient with projection (eigenvalue truncation leading to near-diagonal K).
- Some practical robustness and speedup demonstrated; code release is valuable.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
- Algorithmic correctness/clarity:
  - Eq. (3): P(Y) = |det(K − IY)|. The definition of IY (“identity with entries (i,i) zeroed for i ∈ Y”) is unusual and seems at odds with standard identities (e.g., expressions involving I − K or masking the complement). Please verify the exact masking and sign; relying on absolute value bars obscures potential sign issues. A precise derivation or citation (with consistent notation) is necessary.
  - Algorithm 1 and 2 contain likely step-size bugs: “η ← η2” appears repeatedly in inner backtracking loops; with η initialized to 1 this does not decrease and risks non-termination. I suspect “η ← η/2” was intended. Also in Alg. 2, after acceptance, η is doubled; this is inconsistent with the inner loop. The acceptance criterion uses L, but δ is defined using F with mixed arguments (δ ← F(V′,λ′) − F(V,λ)) while acceptance tests L(V′,λ′) > L(V,λ′). Please unify acceptance and stopping on the same objective and correct step-size schedules.
  - V-update: Eq. (20) is stated to be valid only for the “first gradient step” (q=p). The algorithm then takes only one step per M-step and recomputes the E-step for line search. This is a nonstandard “generalized EM” and monotonicity is not proved. Please provide a clear monotonicity argument (e.g., accept only if L increases guarantees global non-decrease) and fix the δ/acceptance inconsistency noted above.
  - λ near the boundary: R = diag(sqrt(λ/(1−λ))) diverges as λ→1. The ad-hoc “tighten constraint” fix is brittle. A more principled reparameterization (e.g., λj = σ(θj) with a margin or barrier) would avoid infinities and clipping artifacts.
- Complexity claims rely on supplement:
  - The O(nNk^2) cost of Eq. (19) hinges on efficient k-DPP marginal computation for QYi with the specific low-rank/diagonal structure. The main text should provide at least a high-level derivation and algorithmic steps; otherwise, the argument is incomplete. Similarly for Eq. (20) and its cost.
- Baselines and evaluation:
  - Comparisons are only to KA. Missing baselines include: (i) convex quality-scaling [2], (ii) mixtures of fixed L-ensembles [3], (iii) Bayesian parametric approaches [8]. Even if not directly comparable in representation, test likelihood comparisons on the same data (or runtime–quality tradeoffs) would better contextualize significance.
  - Experiments are confined to one application; additional datasets (including synthetic with known ground truth) would help validate parameter recovery and robustness, especially in data-poor regimes.
  - No statistical significance tests or variability (beyond reporting medians) are provided. Report means/variances or confidence intervals across random initializations.
  - The “d” diagnostic correlating EM’s gains with off-diagonal strength is vaguely defined: d = 1N ||M||F ||diag(M)||2 has unclear normalization and units; please define precisely and justify.
- Theoretical guarantees: No convergence theorem or local optimality characterization is provided. While acceptable for a heuristic, the EM framing suggests monotonic improvement—this should be formalized or the claims softened.
- KA critique: While the failure mode is plausible, the paper attributes KA’s issues largely to projection. Stronger step-size or momentum methods, different projections (e.g., projected onto spectrahedron with log-det barrier), or reparameterizations could mitigate degeneracy; a more exhaustive study would strengthen the claim.

Clarity and presentation issues
- Numerous typographical/formatting errors that hinder readability:
  - Spacing and notation: “ofL”, “diversityseeking”, inconsistent V >V vs V^T V, “if L if PSD”.
  - Algorithm pseudocode: step-size update as noted; ambiguous acceptance/δ; missing definitions of constants (c).
  - Mixed use of > to denote transpose alongside inequalities (I − K 0) is confusing.
  - The sentence “This follows from the concavity of KL divergence” is incorrect; KL is convex in q. Here −KL gives concavity.
  - Figure/caption artifact: “Tuesday, August 5, 14” appears in the text.
  - References are poorly formatted (missing years, venues, pages) and inconsistent; several entries have empty fields.
  - Heavy reliance on the supplement for key proofs and algorithmic details; the main paper should include essential derivations to be reproducible.

Pros
- Clever latent-variable formulation for DPP learning; removes projection hassles.
- Simple, interpretable eigenvalue updates; principled use of Stiefel manifold for V.
- Empirical evidence of robustness and speed on a realistic task; code released.

Cons
- Multiple methodological ambiguities/bugs (objective/acceptance mismatch; step-size updates; boundary handling).
- Insufficient theoretical guarantees and reliance on supplement for core complexity/derivations.
- Limited baselines/datasets; evaluation breadth and statistical rigor lacking.
- Numerous presentation and citation issues.

Questions for the authors
1. Please clarify Eq. (3) and the exact definition of IY; provide a short derivation consistent with standard DPP identities.
2. Correct the step-size update rules and unify acceptance/stopping criteria (L vs F). Can you guarantee monotone increase of L per outer iteration?
3. Can you adopt a reparameterization for λ to avoid clipping/divergence and report its effect?
4. Provide a brief derivation or algorithm outline in the main text for computing Eq. (19) in O(Nk^2).
5. Add comparisons to at least one additional learning baseline and another dataset, or a synthetic recovery study.

Recommendation
Reject. The core idea is promising and likely impactful, but the paper in its current form has too many methodological and presentation flaws (some potentially serious, e.g., step-size bugs, objective inconsistencies, boundary behavior), insufficient baselines, and overreliance on the supplement. With corrections, stronger empirical validation, and improved clarity, this work could merit acceptance in a future submission.
