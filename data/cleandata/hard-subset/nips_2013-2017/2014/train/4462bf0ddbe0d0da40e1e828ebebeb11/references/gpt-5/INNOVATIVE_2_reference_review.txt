Summary
The paper tackles the core problem of learning unconstrained determinantal point process (DPP) kernels from observed subsets. Instead of optimizing entries of the marginal kernel K directly (which requires projections to maintain PSD and eigenvalue constraints and can get stuck in near-diagonal solutions), the authors propose an EM-style algorithm that operates in the eigendecomposition of K. They introduce a hidden variable J that indexes the subset of eigenvectors used by an “elementary” DPP; leveraging the known mixture representation of DPPs, they derive a lower bound via Jensen’s inequality and alternate between computing the exact posterior q(J|Y) and updating eigenvalues and eigenvectors.

Key technical ideas:
- Reparameterization to K = VΛV^T and using the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view yields a natural hidden variable J.
- The E-step posterior q(J|Y) is itself a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel (via a transformation involving R = diag(sqrt(λ/(1−λ))) and Z_Y = U_Y U_Y^T); hence it can be represented and manipulated efficiently.
- The M-step gives a closed-form, self-normalizing update for eigenvalues using k-DPP marginals. The eigenvector update uses a single Riemannian gradient step on the Stiefel manifold, with line search chosen to increase the true likelihood L.
- Complexity improves from O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3) for projected gradient on K to O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) for EM, where k = max |Y_i| and typically k ≪ N.

Experiments on a large-scale baby registry recommendation dataset (13 categories, average N ≈ 71, n ≈ 8,585) show that EM improves test log-likelihood over projected gradient (especially where off-diagonals matter), is more robust to initialization, and is ~2× faster. The qualitative example illustrates how EM better captures diversity than the baseline.

Relation to prior work
The paper connects to the DPP literature (Kulesza & Taskar, Hough et al.) and to learning approaches that either only scale item quality (convex reweighting), learn mixtures of fixed L-ensembles, or use Bayesian parameterizations. The novelty here is an unconstrained, nonparametric learning scheme for K that exploits eigenstructure via EM, preserving DPP tractability. The use of k-DPPs for the posterior over J and the resulting closed-form eigenvalue updates appear to be new and elegant. The comparison baseline (projected gradient on K) is appropriate given existing methods; however, comparisons to Bayesian approaches [Affandi et al.] or to other structured/low-rank parameterizations would further situate the contribution.

Strengths
- Conceptually novel: reframing DPP learning as EM over eigenvectors/eigenvalues with a hidden index set J is neat and non-obvious. The k-DPP posterior insight is particularly elegant.
- Practical algorithmic gains: avoids problematic projections, improves robustness to initialization, and has better asymptotic dependence on n and k, which matters in typical subset-selection regimes.
- Sound derivations: the likelihood lower bound, E-step posterior characterization, and eigenvalue update are clean. The Stiefel-manifold update for V is a principled choice.
- Empirical evidence: consistent test likelihood gains and runtime improvements; a concrete qualitative example shows the diversity advantage.

Weaknesses and questions
- Scope of evaluation: all results are on one application domain. Additional datasets (e.g., document summarization, image search) would bolster generality.
- Baselines: only projected gradient on K is compared. Including (i) Bayesian/parametric DPP learning, (ii) mixtures of L-ensembles, or (iii) well-tuned first-order methods with better step-size and alternative projections would strengthen the empirical case.
- Convergence guarantees: this is a generalized EM with only one gradient step on V and q recomputed for line search. While likelihood increases are enforced empirically, a brief discussion of convergence properties and any conditions under which monotonicity holds would improve the theory section.
- Numerical stability: λ close to 1 leads to large R; the “tightening” fix is ad hoc. Please detail a practical bound and its impact. Also discuss stability/conditioning for computing H_Y and (H_Y)^{-1}.
- Scalability: the N^3 term remains in the manifold update and line search. Results with larger N (e.g., hundreds to low thousands) and analysis of wall-clock vs. n, N, k would be helpful. Mini-batch E-steps or low-rank parameterizations might be promising.
- Metrics: only likelihood is reported. For recommendation, showing precision/diversity metrics (e.g., coverage, redundancy) would make the practical significance more tangible.

Quality
Technically solid, with clear derivations for the key steps. Some details are deferred (proof of Lemma 1, derivations of Eqs. 19/20); ensure the supplement is complete and reproducible. The empirical section, while convincing for the chosen domain, could be broader and include stronger baselines.

Clarity
Well written and organized. The mixture view and the role of the hidden variable are explained clearly. Consider adding a brief algorithmic summary of the k-DPP posterior computation and numerical safeguards for λ near 1. A short convergence discussion for generalized EM would help.

Originality
High. The EM formulation over K’s eigendecomposition and the k-DPP posterior for J are, to my knowledge, novel and insightful.

Significance
High for the DPP community and for subset selection applications where diversity matters. The proposed approach could become a default for unconstrained DPP learning and may inspire further scalable/low-rank variants.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel EM formulation leveraging k-DPP posterior; elegant and broadly useful.
- Practical robustness and speed improvements over a widely used baseline.
- Preserves DPP structure and tractability; unconstrained learning of K.
- Clear exposition and publicly available code/data.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited variety of datasets and baselines.
- Lack of convergence guarantees beyond empirical monotonicity checks.
- Incomplete exploration of large-N regimes and numerical stability details.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper introduces a compelling and original direction for learning DPPs that is likely to influence subsequent work, and the empirical gains, while not exhaustive, are meaningful.

Confidence
High.
