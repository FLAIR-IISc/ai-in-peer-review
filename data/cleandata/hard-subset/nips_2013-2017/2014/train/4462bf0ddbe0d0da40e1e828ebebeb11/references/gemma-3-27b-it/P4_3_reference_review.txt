This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), addressing the challenge of learning the kernel matrix *L* from example subsets, a problem conjectured to be NP-hard. Existing approaches either partially learn *L* or rely on restrictive parameterizations. The authors leverage the eigendecomposition of *L* to formulate an EM algorithm that avoids the problematic projection step required by naive gradient ascent, which can lead to degenerate solutions. The paper provides a thorough theoretical justification and experimental validation on a product recommendation task.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the motivation, background, and proposed method. The connection to previous work is comprehensive, citing relevant literature in DPP learning and optimization techniques. The mathematical derivations are generally well-presented, and the authors acknowledge potential computational bottlenecks and propose solutions (e.g., simplifying the eigenvector update). The experimental setup is sound, comparing the proposed EM algorithm against a projected gradient ascent baseline (K-Ascent) on a real-world dataset. The analysis of the results, including the discussion of the impact of initialization and the runtime comparison, is insightful.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The EM algorithm, leveraging eigendecomposition and avoiding problematic projection steps, represents a novel approach to learning DPPs.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivations appear mathematically sound, and the algorithm is well-defined. The authors address potential issues (e.g., computational complexity, eigenvalue constraints) and offer practical solutions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is written in a clear and concise manner, making it easy to follow the technical details.
*   **Significance:** The problem of efficiently learning DPPs is important for various applications, and the proposed solution has the potential to be widely used. The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm.
*   **Thoroughness:** The paper includes a detailed experimental evaluation with comparisons to a reasonable baseline and analysis of runtime.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational Complexity:** While the authors discuss the complexity and claim asymptotic improvements, the explanation regarding the specific impact of *k* (size of the largest subset) on runtime could be more detailed. A more precise breakdown of the computational cost for different steps would be helpful.
*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** Although the paper acknowledges the importance of initialization, the choice of initialization methods seems limited. Investigating other initialization strategies could further improve the robustness of the algorithm.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are focused solely on a baby registry dataset. Expanding the evaluation to other domains and datasets would strengthen the generalizability of the findings.
*   **Discussion of Limitations:** A more explicit discussion of the limitations of the EM algorithm, such as its sensitivity to hyperparameters (step size, number of iterations), would be beneficial.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for learning DPPs, addressing a challenging problem in the field.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to a relevant baseline.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
*   The work has the potential to advance the state of the art in DPP learning and find applications in various domains.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The computational complexity analysis could be more detailed.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset.
*   A more thorough discussion of the algorithm’s limitations would be valuable.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of DPP learning. The proposed EM algorithm is a novel and effective solution to a challenging problem. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above. Specifically, the authors should consider providing a more detailed computational complexity analysis, exploring additional initialization strategies, and expanding the experimental evaluation to other datasets.
