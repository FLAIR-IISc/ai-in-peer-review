## Review of "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning the kernel matrix *L* for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) from observed subsets. Existing approaches either learn only partial information about *L*, rely on restrictive parameterizations, or suffer from optimization issues (specifically, gradient ascent requiring problematic projections). The authors propose an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm that leverages the eigendecomposition of *L* to avoid the aforementioned pitfalls. By working with eigenvalues and eigenvectors, they derive a more stable and efficient learning procedure. The paper includes theoretical justification for the method, alongside experimental validation on a product recommendation task.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Learning DPPs is crucial for a wide range of applications where diversity and quality need to be balanced. The NP-hardness of full *L* learning motivates the need for effective approximation algorithms.
* **Novel approach:** The EM framework utilizing eigendecomposition is a clever way to circumvent the issues associated with direct gradient ascent on *L*.  It’s a genuinely new perspective on the learning problem.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the EM algorithm appears well-motivated and utilizes established techniques (Jensen's inequality).  The connection to k-DPPs for efficient computation is also well-explained.
* **Empirical Validation:** Experiments on a realistic dataset (baby registries) demonstrate the effectiveness of the EM algorithm compared to a projected gradient ascent baseline.  The analysis of runtime improvements is valuable. The insight connecting performance to the data’s underlying structure (the ‘d’ metric) is particularly strong.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a logical flow of ideas. The background section provides sufficient context for understanding the DPP framework.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Baselines:** While comparing against projected gradient ascent (KA) is valuable, the paper would benefit from a comparison to other relevant DPP learning methods, even if they have limitations (e.g., the L-ensemble method from [3]). This would provide a more complete assessment of the EM algorithm's performance.
* **Implementation Details:** The description of the Stiefel manifold update is somewhat brief.  More details on the specific implementation (e.g., the step size selection procedure) would be helpful for reproducibility.
* **Scalability Discussion:** The authors mention the asymptotic runtime advantage of the EM algorithm, but the practical implications for very large datasets (N > 1000) are not fully explored.  A discussion of potential bottlenecks and strategies for scaling the algorithm would be beneficial.
* **Moment Matching Initialization:** While helpful, the moment-matching initialization is not always superior and the analysis doesn’t explain *why* it works when it does. A better understanding of the initialization’s impact would be valuable.



**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality (9/10):** The paper presents a technically sound and well-justified algorithm. The theoretical analysis is convincing, and the experimental results provide strong evidence of its effectiveness. The work feels complete, addressing a clear problem with a novel and well-supported solution.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is generally clearly written, although some sections (e.g., the Stiefel manifold update) could benefit from more detailed explanations. The notation is mostly consistent, and the overall organization is logical.
* **Originality (8/10):** The use of an EM algorithm based on eigendecomposition to learn DPP kernels is a novel contribution.  While DPPs themselves are not new, this particular learning approach offers a significant advance over existing methods.
* **Significance (8/10):** The results are important because they provide a practical and efficient way to learn DPPs, enabling their application to a wider range of real-world problems. The potential for impact in areas such as recommendation systems and document summarization is significant.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem.
* Demonstrable improvement over existing methods in experiments.
* Clear and well-written presentation.
* Potential for significant impact in various applications.

**Con:**

* Limited comparison to a broader range of baselines.
* Implementation details could be more comprehensive.
* Scalability considerations are not fully addressed.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of DPP learning. The proposed EM algorithm is a significant advance over existing methods, and the experimental results are compelling. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above (expanding the baseline comparisons, providing more implementation details, and discussing scalability) would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact. I am confident that this work would be well-received by the NIPS community.
