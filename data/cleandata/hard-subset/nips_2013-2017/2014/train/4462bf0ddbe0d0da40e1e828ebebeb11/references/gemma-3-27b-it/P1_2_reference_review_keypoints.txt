- Tackles learning the full DPP kernel L from data using a novel EM algorithm that leverages eigendecomposition and avoids projection issues in gradient ascent.  
- Positions work relative to prior DPP learning (partial L, kernel ensembles, Bayesian approaches) and clearly states how it differs.  
- Novelty: EM formulation on eigenstructure is a genuine contribution that sidesteps PSD optimization challenges.  
- Theoretical justification: provides asymptotic runtime analysis showing EM is more efficient than gradient ascent.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on baby registry data show EM outperforms gradient-ascent (KA), especially with strong negative item interactions.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-written, derivations are clear, appendix and code availability are strengths.  
- Addresses a core challenge: enables non-parametric learning of L, advancing DPP learning.  
- Implementation complexity: EM requires complex components (k-DPP marginalization, Stiefel manifold updates), increasing implementation burden.  
- Sensitivity to initialization: performance depends on initialization (Wishart used), and more analysis of initialization strategies is needed.  
- Limited empirical scope: evaluation is focused on a single application, reducing generality of claims.  
- Comparative baselines: lacks comparison to broader or more recent DPP learning methods (e.g., neural approaches).  
- k-DPP discussion is brief and needs more explanation for readers unfamiliar with its role in EM.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel/theoretically sound method, strong empirical results, clear writing, reproducible code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical complexity, limited experiments, and initialization sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand practical implementation discussion, analyze initialization sensitivity, broaden experimental discussion and comparisons, and elaborate on k-DPPs.
