- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the marginal kernel K of DPPs without restrictive parameterizations, avoiding projection-induced degeneracy of prior methods.  
- Identifies that K-Ascent’s projection step yields near-diagonal kernels that fail to model diversity.  
- Leverages eigendecomposition and a Stiefel-manifold M-step to maintain orthogonality and avoid problematic projections.  
- Provides a rigorous derivation handling the hidden variable J in the DPP mixture representation.  
- Shows computational complexity advantage: EM O(n N k^2) vs K-Ascent O(n N^3), and demonstrates a 2.1x runtime improvement in practice.  
- Empirically validates EM on a baby registry dataset, showing clear gains especially for domains with strong negative item interactions.  
- Correlates empirical improvements with the metric d = ||M||_F / ||diag(M)||_2 to support theoretical claims.  
- Strength: Clear problem formulation and well-explained connection between projection and degeneracy.  
- Strength: Theoretical soundness in derivation and complexity analysis.  
- Strength: Empirical evidence of improved performance and practical runtime benefits.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited to a single domain (baby registry), reducing generalizability.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art DPP learning methods (e.g., L-ensemble, Bayesian approaches).  
- Weakness: No theoretical convergence analysis for the proposed EM, especially since the M-step is only partially optimized.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of sensitivity to initialization despite testing two initializers.  
- Recommendation: Accept—novel, theoretically and empirically sound contribution with significant practical impact.  
- Suggested enhancements: add comparisons with other DPP methods, broaden experimental domains, and provide detailed convergence analysis.
