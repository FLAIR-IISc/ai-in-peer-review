- Tackles maximum-likelihood learning of unconstrained DPP kernels by directly learning the marginal kernel K via its eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T.  
- Introduces hidden variable J indexing eigenvector subsets and derives a generalized EM using the DPP-as-mixture representation.  
- E-step: sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), showing q is a k-DPP with kernel QY = R ZY R, enabling efficient marginals via k-DPP identities.  
- M-step: closed-form eigenvalue updates λj = (1/n) Σi q(j∈J|Yi) computable in O(n N k^2); eigenvectors updated with a Stiefel-manifold gradient step using V ← V exp[η(V^T G − G^T V)].  
- Compared to naïve projected gradient (KA), EM improves median test log-likelihoods (3.7% on Wishart init), is more robust to initialization, and is ~2.1× faster empirically.  
- Empirical gains are larger in categories with stronger negative interactions and in low-data regimes (n=2N).  
- Strength: principled exploitation of DPP spectral structure to derive efficient generalized EM with closed-form eigenvalue updates.  
- Strength: clear diagnosis of projection pathology in KA leading to near-diagonal degeneracy.  
- Strength: solid computational analysis showing per-iteration cost scales with average set size k rather than N for much of the M-step.  
- Strength: empirical evidence on real data across categories, with code and data provided.  
- Weakness: limited baselines—only compared to KA; missing comparisons to per-item quality learning, kernel mixtures/ensembles, and parametric/Bayesian DPP methods.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single domain (baby registry); lacks demonstrations on typical DPP tasks (summarization, image diversification, sensor placement).  
- Weakness: algorithmic description inconsistencies—Algorithm 2 mixes checks on L vs definition of δ from F, unclear monotonicity guarantees for L.  
- Weakness: line-search typos (η ← η^2 seems wrong; likely should reduce η), needs correction.  
- Weakness: Stiefel update uses matrix exponential (O(N^3)), which may be heavy; cheaper retractions or Cayley/QR approaches should be considered.  
- Weakness: numerical stability when λj → 1 due to R = sqrt(λ/(1−λ)) blowing up; needs principled handling (barrier/penalty or soft clipping) and reporting.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—requires full N×N eigendecompositions and matrix exponentials; consider low-rank (Nyström) or structured kernel variants.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees limited—need discussion of convergence for generalized EM with single V-step.  
- Clarity: generally well organized and readable, but pseudocode needs corrections and more detail on computing q(j∈J|Y) and efficient L(K) evaluation.  
- Originality: novel and elegant use of eigenvector-mixture hidden variables to derive EM for unconstrained DPP learning.  
- Significance: advances robust learning of full nonparametric DPP kernels with practical efficiency gains and broad applicability.  
- Suggestions: fix line-search and F vs L consistency, add broader baselines and domains, consider cheaper manifold retractions, detail λ→1 handling, and expand supplement with derivations and complexity analysis.  
- Acceptance arguments: principled novel EM, practical algorithmic contributions, empirical robustness and runtime gains, and provided code/data.  
- Rejection arguments: narrow experiments and baselines, algorithmic typos/inconsistencies, and unresolved scalability to large N.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (high reviewer confidence).
