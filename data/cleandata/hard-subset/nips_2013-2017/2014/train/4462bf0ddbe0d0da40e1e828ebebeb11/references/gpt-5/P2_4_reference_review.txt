Summary
This paper tackles maximum likelihood learning of unconstrained DPP kernels by working in the eigenspace of the marginal kernel K. The key idea is to view a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs indexed by a subset J of eigenvectors, and to introduce J as a latent variable for an EM-style procedure. The E-step sets q(J|Y) to the true posterior pK(J|Y), which the authors show is a k-DPP with a computable kernel. The M-step decouples: (i) eigenvalues λj admit a closed-form update equal to the average posterior inclusion probability of component j, efficiently computed by k-DPP marginalization; (ii) eigenvectors V are updated by a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold using a derived gradient expression valid at q=p. The method avoids the PSD/eigenvalue projection that can trap naive projected gradient ascent (KA) in near-diagonal solutions, and has favorable asymptotic cost O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) versus KA’s O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3) when k ≪ N.

Experiments on a large real dataset (Amazon baby registries split by category) compare EM to KA under two initializations (Wishart, moments matching). EM consistently improves test log-likelihood over KA (median gains ~3.7% under Wishart; ~0.4% with moments matching; larger gains in data-poor settings and categories with stronger off-diagonal structure), and is about 2.1× faster in wall-clock time. Qualitative examples show EM avoids degenerate over-redundant selections that KA can produce.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The EM formulation via the elementary-DPP mixture is well-motivated and exploits known DPP structure. The E-step characterization of q(J|Y) as a k-DPP and the λ-update as posterior inclusion probabilities are standard consequences of DPP/k-DPP properties; pushing the exponential sums into k-DPP marginal computations is appropriate. The eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold is standard for orthogonality constraints, and the derived initial gradient (Eq. 20) is plausible (though details are in the supplement).
- Justification: Claims are supported by derivations (partly in the supplement) and by experiments showing both accuracy and runtime advantages. The discussion of why KA projections can cause near-diagonal degeneracy is insightful and consistent with the derivative structure.
- Caveats: (i) The V-update is a single gradient step per M-step with step-size search and re-running the E-step—a generalized EM. The paper uses the true likelihood L to accept/reject step sizes but reports δ using the bound F; the acceptance and monotonicity guarantees would benefit from clarification. (ii) No convergence guarantees beyond generalized EM heuristics are provided. (iii) Numerical stability when λ→1 is acknowledged; a more principled regularization (e.g., explicit cap, prior, or barrier) would be useful. (iv) Complexity still involves N^3 per step-size trial via the manifold update; the practical speedup comes from replacing many N^3 inverses with O(N k^2) operations, which is credible but should be profiled in more settings.

Clarity
- The paper is generally clear, well-organized, and provides helpful intuition about quality vs. diversity in DPPs, the problems with projection, and the role of eigen-decomposition.
- Algorithms are stated explicitly. However, several crucial derivations (Lemma 1, Eq. 19, Eq. 20, complexity analyses) are deferred to the supplement; brief sketches in the main text would help. Notation switches (L vs. K) are mostly consistent after Sec. 2 but could be flagged more prominently. Clarify the acceptance criterion in Algorithm 2 (L vs. F) and the exact conditions for monotonic improvement in L.

Originality
- The core contribution—an EM algorithm for learning an unconstrained DPP kernel via latent eigenvector-index sets—appears novel relative to prior works that learn only item weights, mixtures with fixed L, or Bayesian parametric forms. Using k-DPP structure in the E-step and a closed-form λ-update is an elegant idea. The use of Stiefel manifold updates for eigenvectors in this context is appropriate and, to my knowledge, new for DPP learning.

Significance
- Learning full, non-parametric DPP kernels is important for applications where negative interactions matter. The method addresses a long-standing practical bottleneck (projection degeneracy) and shows measurable gains and speedups on a real dataset. The approach is likely to be adopted and extended (e.g., to structured DPPs, priors/regularization, or large-scale approximations).
- Limitations: Experiments compare only to projected gradient on K. It would strengthen significance to include baselines from (a) Bayesian/parametric DPP learning (e.g., Affandi et al.), (b) restricted but common parameterizations (quality-only or similarity-only), (c) pseudo-likelihood or other approximations. An ablation showing the impact of the V-update (e.g., λ-only EM vs. full EM) would clarify where the gains come from. Synthetic experiments with known ground-truth K could quantify recovery.

Pros
- Conceptually clean EM formulation leveraging known DPP structure.
- Avoids problematic PSD/eigenvalue projections; more robust to initialization.
- Closed-form λ-update; principled Stiefel update for V.
- Demonstrated accuracy and runtime gains on a substantial real dataset.
- Clear exposition and practical insights (e.g., when EM helps most).

Cons
- Limited baselines; no synthetic recovery or ablation studies.
- Some key derivations and complexity details deferred to supplement.
- Convergence/monotonicity details for generalized EM and step-size search are not fully formalized.
- Scalability still constrained by N^3; discussion of scaling strategies (e.g., low-rank structure, Nystrom) is absent.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Clarify the acceptance criterion in Algorithm 2 and whether each outer iteration guarantees monotone increase of L. If not, state conditions under which it typically does.
- Include an ablation: λ-only updates vs. full V updates; and sensitivity to k, N, and initialization.
- Add at least one additional baseline (e.g., Bayesian parametric learning or pseudo-likelihood).
- Provide a small synthetic study with known K to assess recovery and the effect of projection in KA.
- Discuss practical regularization of λ near 1 and whether priors on λ or V help.
- Comment on scalability to larger N and potential approximations (e.g., low-rank K, Nystrom).

Overall recommendation
The paper presents a novel and practical EM approach for learning DPPs, with solid motivation, reasonable technical development, and meaningful empirical gains. Despite limited baselines and some deferred details, the contribution is clear and likely to impact practice. I lean to accept (weak accept) contingent on clarification of the generalized EM monotonicity and strengthened experimental baselines.
