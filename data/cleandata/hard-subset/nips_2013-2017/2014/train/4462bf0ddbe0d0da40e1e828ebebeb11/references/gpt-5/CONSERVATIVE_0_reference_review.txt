Summary
The paper proposes an EM-style algorithm for maximum-likelihood learning of determinantal point processes (DPPs) without restrictive parameterizations of the kernel. Using the eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T and the known mixture view of DPPs as mixtures over elementary DPPs (indexed by subsets of eigenvectors J), the authors introduce J as a latent variable. The E-step sets q(J|Y) to the posterior pK(J|Y), which they show is a k-DPP with a computable kernel. The M-step yields a closed-form, self-normalizing update for the eigenvalues λ via k-DPP marginals and a single gradient step for V on the Stiefel manifold using a matrix-exponential update. They argue this avoids problematic eigenvalue/eigenvector projections required by projected gradient ascent (KA) and leads to better robustness and asymptotically faster runtime when observed subsets are small (k ≪ N). Experiments on an Amazon baby registries dataset (13 categories) show modest average test log-likelihood gains over KA (3.7% from Wishart init; 0.4% from moment-matching init; larger gains in low-data settings) and about 2.1× speedups. A qualitative example suggests the EM-trained model better avoids redundant selections.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on DPP fundamentals and learning attempts that either (i) learn only item quality scalings, (ii) learn mixtures or parametric forms that sacrifice DPP structure or impose strong priors, or (iii) Bayesian inference over parametric kernels. The paper distinguishes itself by attempting unconstrained, nonparametric kernel learning via an EM formulation that exploits the eigenstructure.

Strengths
- Conceptual elegance: The EM formulation leverages the elementary DPP mixture and yields a clean E-step and a closed-form eigenvalue update.
- Practicality: Avoids hard PSD/eigenvalue projections that can induce degeneracy in KA; Stiefel manifold update is a reasonable way to respect V^TV = I.
- Complexity: For small set sizes k, the per-iteration complexity O(nN k^2) for λ and gradient computation offers a plausible speed advantage over O(nN^3) KA; the paper provides a thoughtful discussion of runtime contributors.
- Empirical indications: On a real dataset, EM shows robustness to initialization and some runtime and likelihood benefits; anecdotal qualitative example is sensible.

Weaknesses
- Limited baselines: The only quantitative baseline is KA with a simple projection and line search. Missing comparisons to (a) stronger spectral/parameterized projected methods (e.g., learning in eigen-space with soft box constraints, mirror descent on the PSD cone, proximal/Frank-Wolfe variants), (b) established parametric DPP learning (e.g., quality-similarity factorizations common in the DPP literature), and (c) non-DPP models for set selection (independent Bernoulli, simple submodular objectives) to contextualize gains.
- Empirical scope: One application domain with 13 categories; improvements are modest on average and sometimes negligible. No synthetic recovery experiments to validate identifiability/robustness, and no additional domains (summarization, image search) where DPPs are standard.
- Convergence/guarantees: This is effectively a generalized EM that takes a single gradient step on V and relies on repeated E-steps during line-search to check likelihood improvement. While monotonic increase of the observed likelihood is enforced in practice, there is no formal convergence analysis or characterization of stationary points.
- Reliance on supplement: Key derivations (k-DPP posterior, Eqns. 19 and 20) and computational details are deferred; the main text is not self-contained for reproducibility.
- Numerical stability: The method requires clamping λ near 1 to avoid blow-ups in R = diag(sqrt(λ/(1−λ))). The sensitivity of performance to this clamp and to line-search settings is not analyzed.
- Asymptotic claims depend critically on k. The N^3 cost from manifold updates in line search can dominate; empirical scaling with N/k is not extensively studied.

Quality
Technically sound at a high level, with reasonable algorithmic choices. However, theoretical guarantees are limited, and several derivations and practical details are deferred. Empirical validation is narrow and lacks strong baselines and diagnostic ablations (e.g., number of gradient steps on V, effect of initialization, sensitivity to λ clamping).

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized. The high-level intuition and algorithm boxes help. Still, the reader is forced to the supplement for central elements. The Stiefel update and line search could be described more concretely (e.g., how the matrix exponential is implemented, step size policy, stopping).

Originality
Moderate. Using EM on the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs representation is natural but, to my knowledge, has not been previously developed for unconstrained kernel learning. The λ update via k-DPP marginals is neat. The V update is a standard manifold step.

Significance
Potentially useful for practitioners who need unconstrained DPP learning and suffer from projection-induced degeneracy in KA. However, given the modest empirical gains, limited baselines, and lack of broader validation, the impact is uncertain.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add stronger baselines: spectral parameterizations with box constraints on eigenvalues, mirror/proximal descent on the PSD cone, and standard parametric DPP kernels.
- Include non-DPP and submodular baselines to contextualize likelihood and downstream performance (e.g., set completion accuracy).
- Provide synthetic recovery experiments and additional real domains.
- Ablate and analyze: number of V-updates per M-step, effects of λ clamping, initialization sensitivity, and k/N scaling.
- Offer a convergence discussion for the generalized EM scheme and report monotonicity empirically.

Pros
- Elegant EM formulation exploiting DPP structure
- Avoids projection degeneracies; more robust in some regimes
- Potential runtime benefits for small k
- Clear exposition; code/data promised

Cons
- Weak baseline comparisons and narrow empirical scope
- Limited theoretical guarantees for convergence
- Some key derivations deferred; numerical stability considerations underexplored
- Modest average gains

Recommendation
Weak reject. The idea is promising, but stronger empirical validation against established baselines and broader evidence of advantage are needed to justify acceptance.
