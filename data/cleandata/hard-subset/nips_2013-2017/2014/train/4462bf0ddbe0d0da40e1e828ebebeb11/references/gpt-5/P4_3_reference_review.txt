Summary
The paper addresses maximum-likelihood learning of determinantal point processes (DPPs) without restricting the kernel to a parametric form. The authors argue that projected gradient ascent on the marginal kernel K can lead to degenerate, near-diagonal solutions due to eigenvalue clipping, and is asymptotically slower. They propose an EM-style algorithm that exploits the eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T and the well-known mixture representation of a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs indexed by a subset J of eigenvectors. Introducing J as a latent variable yields a Jensen lower bound on the log-likelihood. The E-step sets q(J|Yi) to the true posterior pK(J|Yi), which the authors show is a k-DPP with an explicit kernel QYi, enabling efficient computation of sufficient statistics via k-DPP marginalization. The M-step has a closed-form update for eigenvalues λj using posterior inclusion probabilities q(j ∈ J | Yi) and a single manifold-constrained gradient step for eigenvectors V, using a gradient formula valid when q=pK(J|Yi). The eigenvector update employs a Stiefel manifold update via a matrix exponential. The resulting generalized EM increases the data likelihood, avoids PSD/eigenvalue projection, and has complexity O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), which is asymptotically better than projected gradient ascent (O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3)) when the observed set sizes k are small.

Experiments on a baby registry dataset (13 categories, average N≈71, n≈8,585) compare the proposed EM with projected gradient ascent (KA). With Wishart initialization, EM improves median test log-likelihood by ≈3.7% on average and more for categories with stronger off-diagonal structure; with moments-matching initialization, EM and KA are similar on full data but EM is clearly better in data-poor settings (n≈2N). EM is ≈2.1× faster than KA. A qualitative example shows EM avoids duplicative recommendations.

Relation to prior work
The paper contrasts with prior approaches that learn only diagonal reweights of a fixed kernel (convex, learns “quality” but not similarity) or mixtures/parametric forms that sacrifice DPP structure or restrict expressiveness. It builds on standard DPP properties (marginal kernel K, mixture over elementary DPPs) and k-DPPs, and uses manifold optimization for orthogonality constraints.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The formulation of a latent J via the mixture representation is standard; the EM lower bound and E-step are appropriate. The claim that q(J|Yi) is a k-DPP with kernel QYi is plausible and consistent with DPP/k-DPP algebra; details are deferred to the supplement. The eigenvalue M-step derived from marginal inclusion probabilities is standard for mixture models and computable efficiently via k-DPP marginalization. The eigenvector update uses a gradient valid at q=p and a single step per M-step, making the algorithm a generalized EM. The Stiefel manifold update is appropriate for preserving V^T V=I. The authors check improvements in the true likelihood L when choosing step sizes, which mitigates approximation error in the bound.
- Completeness: Some derivations (Lemma 1; eqs. 19, 20) and computational details are in the supplement. Convergence theory is that of GEM (monotone improvement if each step increases L), but no formal guarantee is provided beyond the step-size acceptance check.
- Runtime analysis: The asymptotic claims are reasonable given that k≪N in many applications; however, the practicality hinges on implementing the O(n N k^2) computations (e.g., exploiting low-rank structure of QYi) efficiently.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The motivation for avoiding projection is well explained. Algorithms are summarized. Some points would benefit from clarification:
- Detail how the k-DPP marginals in Eq. (19) are computed in O(N k^2) per Yi, including the specific eigendecomposition or low-rank operations used on QYi.
- Discuss numerical stability near λj≈1 and the exact tightening used.
- Elaborate on the cost and implementation of the matrix exponential in Eq. (21), and whether alternative retractions (e.g., QR-based) were considered.
- Provide more details on step-size selection overhead (T2) and the number of E-steps per iteration.

Originality
The EM formulation for learning a full, nonparametric DPP kernel via the latent index set J and k-DPP posteriors appears novel. Prior work either learned restricted parameterizations or used Bayesian inference with parametric forms. The eigenvector update on the Stiefel manifold within a GEM scheme for DPPs is also a new combination.

Significance
Learning full DPP kernels is an important open problem with practical impact in subset selection tasks. The proposed method addresses a known difficulty of projected gradient methods and offers both robustness and speed advantages. The experimental evidence is limited to a single application domain but uses a sizable real dataset and shows consistent improvements, particularly in regimes where diversity (off-diagonals) matters. Broader empirical validation would strengthen the case.

Strengths
- Conceptually clean EM/GEM framework leveraging known DPP structure.
- Closed-form eigenvalue updates; constrained eigenvector updates without projection.
- Addresses a real failure mode of projected gradient ascent; robust to initialization.
- Favorable asymptotic complexity; empirical speedups observed.
- Practical implementation details (e.g., step-size selection via likelihood checks).

Weaknesses
- Experimental scope is narrow (one domain, primarily compared to KA). Missing comparisons to other learning strategies (e.g., parametric Bayesian [8], mixtures [3], or diagonal reweighting [2]) under comparable settings.
- Reliance on supplement for key derivations and algorithmic details; some numerical aspects (matrix exponential, λ near 1) are only briefly discussed.
- No synthetic recovery study to demonstrate learning of off-diagonal structure or sensitivity to k and N.
- Evaluation focuses on likelihood; limited downstream task metrics or statistical significance analysis.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add baselines beyond KA, including restricted/parametric approaches, to contextualize gains.
- Include synthetic experiments to test recovery of known kernels and sensitivity analyses.
- Report additional application domains (e.g., summarization, image search) or downstream MAP quality.
- Provide implementation details enabling reproduction of the O(n N k^2) routines; consider retraction-based manifold updates that avoid matrix exponentials.
- Clarify convergence behavior (monotonicity, tolerance criteria) and quantify step-size search overhead.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and technically sound EM formulation for full DPP learning with practical, efficient updates.
- Clear advantage over a widely used baseline in both robustness and speed.
- Addresses a central obstacle in adopting DPPs more broadly (learning nonparametric kernels).

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited empirical validation and baselines; improvements shown in a single application domain.
- Some derivations and algorithmic details are deferred, leaving implementation ambiguity.
- Potential scalability and numerical concerns not fully explored.

Overall assessment
The paper presents a novel and well-motivated learning algorithm for DPPs with solid technical underpinnings and promising empirical results. Despite the limited experimental breadth, the contribution is likely to be useful to the community working on diverse subset selection and probabilistic modeling.
