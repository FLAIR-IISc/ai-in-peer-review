- Proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn an unconstrained DPP marginal kernel K (or L) by treating eigenvector subset J as latent.  
- E-step is tractable: p(J|Y) is a k-DPP with an efficiently constructible kernel.  
- M-step splits into closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals (O(Nk^2) per example) and a gradient Stiefel-manifold update for eigenvectors with likelihood-checked line search.  
- EM avoids PSD/eigenvalue-capping projections that can cause degenerate near-diagonal solutions in projected gradient (KA).  
- EM is asymptotically faster when typical set size k ≪ N and empirically more robust to initialization.  
- Experiments on a large Amazon baby registry dataset show consistent test log-likelihood gains over KA, ~2x speedups, and advantages in data-poor regimes.  
- Novelty: directly learning an unconstrained kernel via eigenstructure and DPP mixture view appears new.  
- Leverages k-DPP marginalization and Stiefel-manifold optimization in a novel combination for DPP learning.  
- Technical soundness: EM lower bound and identification of E-step as k-DPP are correct; eigenvalue M-step derivation is clean.  
- Eigenvector updates are a principled generalized-EM step with monotonicity enforced by likelihood checks, a reasonable tradeoff for intractable full M-step.  
- Complexity claims (EM vs KA) are plausible: EM reduces dependence on N when k small.  
- Empirical support is realistic and fairly large-scale, substantiating claims especially when off-diagonal structure matters.  
- Experiments are limited to one domain and lack comparisons to Bayesian/parametric DPP learning or quality-only baselines.  
- Limitations acknowledged: V-updates approximate, λ must be kept below 1 for stability, sensitivity to degenerate initializations, and deferred proofs/derivations.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized; background and algorithms are helpful, though some derivations should be summarized in main text.  
- Notation mostly consistent; a small glossary for symbols like U, R, BY would help readability.  
- Significance: addresses a practical bottleneck in learning full DPP kernels and is broadly useful across applications.  
- Pros: elegant EM framework, avoids projection degeneracy, closed-form eigenvalue updates, principled manifold updates, realistic evaluation, code availability.  
- Cons: narrow experimental breadth, lack of formal convergence guarantees for generalized EM, reliance on E-step recomputation for line search, gains dependent on small k, some deferred proofs.  
- Suggested experiments: report line-search trials (T2) and variance, ablation of eigenvalues-only vs full EM, compare to Bayesian/parametric and quality-only baselines, evaluate behavior for large |Y|, and test a second dataset.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel, practically relevant algorithm with solid foundations and convincing single-dataset evidence; broader experiments and clarifications would strengthen it.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on DPP/optimization aspects; moderate on empirical generality pending broader evaluations.
