- Paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP kernel L by operating in eigenspace and introducing hidden eigenvector variables.  
- EM avoids the problematic projection step of naive gradient methods that lead to degenerate near-diagonal solutions.  
- Theoretical justification uses eigendecomposition properties of DPPs and Jensen’s inequality to bound the likelihood.  
- Experiments on baby registry data show EM is more robust and asymptotically faster than projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent).  
- Analysis links data interaction strength (d) to scenarios where EM has a clear advantage.  
- Authors provide computational complexity analysis highlighting EM’s asymptotic advantage with respect to k.  
- Paper is generally well written and explains mathematical connections clearly.  
- Good experimental setup: real-world data, reasonable baseline comparison, and multiple initialization strategies.  
- Some technical derivations (M-step gradients) are deferred to the supplement, reducing accessibility of details in the main text.  
- M-step relies on a line-search step-size heuristic evaluated via the E-step, which lacks rigorous justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Scalability experiments are limited (N=71), so practical performance on larger datasets is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (single application domain) and lacks comparison to other DPP learning methods (e.g., Bayesian).  
- Results show strong dependence on initialization (moments-matching helps), indicating sensitivity and need for more robust initialization methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability and step-size details.  
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
