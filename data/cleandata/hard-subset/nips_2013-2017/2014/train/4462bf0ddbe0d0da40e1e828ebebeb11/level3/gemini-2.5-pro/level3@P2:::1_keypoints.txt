- Tackles non-convex learning of the full DPP kernel via a novel EM that reparameterizes the kernel by eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introduces a latent subset of eigenvectors.  
- E-step computes a tractable k-DPP posterior; M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and eigenvectors by a single gradient step on the Stiefel manifold.  

Strengths:
- Original and significant: creative EM over eigenspace addresses a hard, practically important problem beyond prior convex settings.  
- Strong technical motivation: avoids projection-induced spectrum collapse and yields a clear, tractable E-step.  
- Empirical gains: EM outperforms projected gradient baseline (K-Ascent), is more robust to initialization, faster, and shows up to 16.5% improvement in low-data settings.  

Weaknesses:
- M-step complexity: uses a Generalized EM with a single gradient step, requires Stiefel-manifold optimization and line search, reducing simplicity.  
- Incomplete experiments: missing evaluation of EM initialized from K-Ascent solution to probe whether EM escapes local optima.  
- Unclear metric: reporting relative log-likelihood differences is non-standard and hard to interpret; per-sample absolute log-likelihood differences would be clearer.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Presents a novel, technically interesting solution to full-kernel DPP learning.  
- Effectively avoids problematic projection of naive gradient methods, improving robustness.  
- Empirical results show clear advantages in quality and computation in key settings.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Gains are modest with good initialization and sufficient data.  
- M-step is not a true maximization and is relatively complex to implement.  
- Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive to fully characterize differences.  

Overall:
- Solid, creative contribution with convincing benefits and some implementation/evaluation caveats; recommendation: accept.
