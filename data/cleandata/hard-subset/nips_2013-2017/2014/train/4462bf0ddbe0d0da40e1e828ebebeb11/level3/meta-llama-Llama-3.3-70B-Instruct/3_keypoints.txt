- Proposes a novel framework to learn distributed attribute representations jointly with word embeddings.  
- Introduces a third-order multiplicative model where word context and attribute vectors interact to predict the next word.  
- Develops the notion of conditional word similarity via a word embedding tensor.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation of the model and learning procedure.  
- Experiments on sentiment classification, cross-lingual document classification, and blog authorship attribution show effectiveness.  
- Results outperform several baselines and are competitive with state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Novel contribution: combining word embeddings and attribute representations through multiplicative interactions.  
- Strong empirical evaluation with both qualitative and quantitative analyses.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art models on some tasks (e.g., sentiment classification).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the properties and interpretability of learned attribute representations.  
- Meets most conference guidelines but could benefit from broader comparisons and deeper representation analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to include more SOTA comparisons and more thorough analysis of attribute representations.
