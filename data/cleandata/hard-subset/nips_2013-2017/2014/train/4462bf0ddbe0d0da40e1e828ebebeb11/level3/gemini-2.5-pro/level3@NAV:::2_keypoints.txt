- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigendecomposition and optimizing a Jensen lower bound with coordinate ascent (eigenvalues) and Stiefel manifold optimization (eigenvectors).  
- Tackles an important, difficult non-convex problem; prior work mostly addressed restricted/convex variants (e.g., fixed-kernel weights or per-row learning).  
- Main strength: original, technically strong contribution with a sound theoretical derivation offering a new approach for full DPP kernel learning.  
- Major weakness (experiments): limited empirical validation—comparison only to a simple K-Ascent baseline and no evaluations against other subset-selection methods, so practical benefits are unclear.  
- Major weakness (clarity): exposition is poor—sloppy writing, notation introduced after use, and a convoluted structure that mixes core derivation with side remarks, making the method hard to follow.  
- Specific clarity/reproducibility issues: imprecise phrasing, over-reliance on external references instead of explanations (e.g., [26]), and unexplained modeling choices such as the initialization in eq. (23).  
- Recommendation: substantially revise the paper—present the final method clearly and linearly first, justify modeling/initialization choices, and strengthen experiments with a wider set of competitive baselines.  
- Overall verdict: technically valuable and original work but not yet ready for publication due to weak empirical evaluation and significant clarity problems.
