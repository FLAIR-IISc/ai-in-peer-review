- Reparameterizes marginal kernel K via eigendecomposition and introduces latent eigenvector-selection J to derive an EM algorithm for full DPP kernel learning.
- E-step yields a conditional k-DPP enabling efficient marginals; M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and optimizes eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold.
- Builds on prior DPP work (Kulesza & Taskar, k-DPPs, MAP inference, prior learning methods); novelty is using the eigenvector-selection latent variable for EM.
- Conceptual novelty: elegant EM over eigenvalues/eigenvectors leveraging the elementary DPP mixture view.
- Technical soundness: clear derivations, closed-form eigenvalue updates, and a principled Stiefel manifold step for eigenvectors.
- Practical relevance: avoids projection-induced degeneracy of KA, shows up to 16.5% test LL gains in data-poor settings and ~2x faster training on real task.
- Clarity: well explained EM derivation and constraints mapping; writing and proofs are coherent.
- Empirical scope is limited to a single real dataset (baby registries); broader domains would strengthen generality claims.
- Baselines are weak (only KA); comparisons to per-item scaling, mixture-of-DPPs, Bayesian DPPs, and stronger KA optimizers are missing.
- Evaluation focuses on log-likelihood; lacks downstream metrics like MAP set quality, expected-utility, diversity/coverage, and qualitative assessment.
- Scalability concerns: method still requires repeated E-steps and N×N operations for V updates; larger-N experiments or low-rank approximations would help.
- Implementation details are partly ad hoc (λ clipping, step-size search); principled regularizers or stability analysis are needed.
- Algorithm quality: technically sound EM lower bound with expected local convergence; some design choices (λ clipping, repeated E-steps) need more justification.
- Clarity suggestions: move some supplement derivations into main text, clarify BYi’s role in the V gradient, and state convergence criteria and typical iteration counts.
- Originality: likely the first EM algorithm learning a full unconstrained DPP kernel via eigenbasis and elementary-DPP latent variable.
- Significance: important for diversity modeling and preserves DPP structure; robustness and gains shown but broader validation required.
- Key questions for authors: compare to per-item scaling and mixture baselines; report MAP/diversity metrics; sensitivity to initialization and λ bounds; use of regularization; amortizing or mini-batching E-step and caching QYi eigendecompositions; applicability to feature-based or low-rank kernels.
- Pro acceptance points: novel, technically solid EM; preserves diversity modeling and addresses KA failure modes; faster and robust with clear derivations.
- Con acceptance points: limited baselines/datasets, unclear scalability, and some heuristic implementation choices.
- Overall recommendation: leaning toward accept conditional on stronger empirical validation, additional baselines, and clarification of implementation details.
- Reviewer confidence: high familiarity with DPPs; moderate uncertainty about empirical breadth beyond presented tasks.
