- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP kernels as an alternative to direct gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).  
- Reformulates kernel learning by representing any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and using a variational distribution over subsets.  
- E-step updates the variational distribution as a k-DPP; M-step performs single gradient steps on kernel parameters.  
- Provides clear, concise background on DPPs, applications, and learning challenges.  
- Empirically outperforms KA substantially with naive KA initialization and modestly with smarter KA; up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood on a product recommendation task.  
- M-step is fairly involved and relies on caveats/approximations, reducing the claimed elegance of the method.  
- Lacks analysis of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM from KA solutions) and exploration of other initialization strategies.  
- Does not sufficiently analyze trade-offs with KA in computational complexity and convergence rates.  
- Use of "relative log likelihood" as the primary metric is concerning; reporting log-probability differences would be more interpretable.  
- Writing is clear overall but has minor issues (overenthusiastic phrasing, unnecessary bolding).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address initialization analysis, metric choice, and clarity.
