- Summary: Reformulates full-kernel DPP learning via eigen decomposition and derives a generalized EM with a k-DPP E-step and closed-form eigenvalue updates plus a Stiefel-manifold eigenvector update.

- Key technical insight: Any DPP is a mixture of elementary DPPs, letting q(J|Y)=p(J|Y) be computed as a k-DPP for a tractable E-step.

- Eigenvalue updates: Closed-form from the lower bound using k-DPP marginals to avoid exponential sums.

- Eigenvector updates: Performed via a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step with line search, but only the initial gradient is tractable, making the M-step approximate.

- Complexity: Per-iteration cost O(n N k^2) attractive when k ≪ N; N^3 costs arise in manifold eigen computations.

- Empirical results: On baby-registry data EM outperforms projected gradient (KA) with naive Wishart init, modestly outperforms with moment-matching init, and is ~2× faster for reported sizes.

- Initialization sensitivity: EM is robust to naive initializations and regains advantage in data-poor settings; effects of cross-initializing methods are not studied.

- Numerical/stability concerns: Requires capping eigenvalues near 1 to avoid infinities; repeated E-step recomputations during line search add complexity and possible instability.

- Missing experiments: No study of initializing EM from KA or KA from EM, and no analysis of multiple manifold steps per M-step.

- Metrics: Relative test log-likelihood ratios are hard to interpret; reviewer requests per-example log-likelihoods (nats/bits) and perplexity-like measures.

- Dataset and scale limits: Evaluation limited to one domain and medium scale; broader datasets (summarization, image search) and larger-scale tests recommended.

- Ablations requested: Impact of multiple M-step gradient steps, cost/benefit of line search, and sensitivity to the λ cap.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-organized, but should explicitly label the method as generalized EM and separate exact versus approximate steps.

- Style nitpicks: Avoid self-praise (“elegant”) and unnecessary bolding; provide more implementation details (tolerances, stopping criteria, step-size/line-search schedule).

- Originality: Latent-variable mixture view is known but using it as a practical EM for full unconstrained kernels is novel and differentiated from prior work.

- Significance: Useful and practical alternative to naive gradient ascent for full-kernel DPPs, especially when off-diagonals matter and initializations are poor.

- Pros for acceptance: Principled EM framework, computational insights, demonstrated robustness and speed advantages.

- Cons for acceptance: Approximate and intricate M-step, modest gains under good initialization, limited empirical analysis and interpretability of metrics.

- Overall: Technically solid and promising with modest-to-substantial empirical gains; recommend acceptance if empirical gaps and presentation/tone issues are addressed.
