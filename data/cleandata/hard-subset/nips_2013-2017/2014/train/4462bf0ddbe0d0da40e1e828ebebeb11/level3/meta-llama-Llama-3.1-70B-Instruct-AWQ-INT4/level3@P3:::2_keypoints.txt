Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPPs using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound — a well-founded, original contribution.

Clear introduction to DPPs, applications, and learning challenges, plus a thorough review of prior work and its limitations.

Provides a detailed derivation of the EM algorithm, including explicit E-step and M-step updates.

Experiments show the EM algorithm outperforms a K-Ascent baseline in test log-likelihood, is more robust to initialization and dataset changes, and is asymptotically faster.

Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and omits comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods, leaving practical benefits unclear.

Write-up is sometimes sloppy and hard to follow; notation is occasionally undefined or introduced after use.

Paper structure interleaves method derivation with side remarks and alternatives, making the main thread convoluted.

Recommendation to first present the method clearly, then discuss alternatives and side remarks.

Specific notation issues: marginal kernel K and variable V are used before being defined.

Language and phrasing problems (e.g., unclear Frobenius-norm statement) need rewriting for precision.

Section referencing external work ([26]) is hard to follow; authors should outline the simpler approach directly.

Ambiguous terminology (e.g., what "weight" refers to on p.4 line 189) requires clarification.

Questionable rationale around equation (30); authors should explain why m_{ij} is not used and why not initialize with empirical covariance.

Overall assessment: technically solid and original but needs clearer exposition and substantially stronger experimental comparisons.

Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.

Arguments for acceptance: original EM method, technically sound, addresses a difficult problem better than prior work.

Arguments for rejection: sloppy notation/write-up, limited experiments and comparisons, and convoluted structure.
