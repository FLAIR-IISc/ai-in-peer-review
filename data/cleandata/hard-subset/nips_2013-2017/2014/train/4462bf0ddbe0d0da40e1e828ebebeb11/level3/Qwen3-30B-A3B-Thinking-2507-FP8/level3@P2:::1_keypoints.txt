- Summary: Proposes an EM algorithm that learns an unconstrained DPP kernel by exploiting eigendecomposition and viewing a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs, avoiding projection steps in naive gradient methods and showing improved log-likelihood on a product recommendation task.

- Originality: The EM reformulation for DPP learning is novel and clearly differentiated from prior work (Kulesza & Taskar; Affandi et al.).

- Technical soundness: Rigorous derivations for E- and M-steps; EM avoids degenerate solutions seen in gradient-based methods and is supported by experiments.

- Significance: Method applies to real-world diversity modeling tasks and shows up to ~16.5% relative log-likelihood gain on the baby registry dataset.

- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with thorough mathematical derivations and a well-motivated connection between the DPP mixture view and EM.

- M-step complexity: The M-step is involved, requiring multiple approximations, orthogonality constraints, and single-step updates rather than full optimization, weakening the claimed elegance.

- Experimental design gap: Lacks analysis of initialization effects (e.g., initializing EM from KA solutions), which is important for non-convex optimization.

- Metric interpretation issue: Use of "relative log likelihood" (ratios of log probabilities) is misleading; absolute log-probability differences should be reported.

- Writing style critique: Contains unnecessary stylistic elements (e.g., bolding) and overstates the method's elegance given M-step complexity.

- For acceptance: Addresses a significant gap in DPP learning literature with a theoretically sound method that yields meaningful empirical improvements and clear applicability.

- For acceptance: Clearly differentiates itself from prior NIPS work and is generally well-written and organized.

- Against acceptance: M-step approximations and complexity undercut the elegance and may limit practicality.

- Against acceptance: Missing experiments on initialization effects leave an important empirical question unanswered.

- Against acceptance: Misleading performance metric presentation harms interpretability.

- Against acceptance: Some stylistic overstatements detract from professionalism.

- Overall assessment: Interesting, promising, and valuable contribution; novel and technically sound with convincing empirical gains despite M-step and experimental shortcomings.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix metric reporting and tone down claims about elegance.
