- Tackles learning the full DPP kernel L from subsets, a challenging problem previously considered conjectured NP-hard.  
- Key idea: reparameterize L via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introduce hidden variable J (eigenvector selection) to apply EM.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive an EM lower bound and optimizes eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold to avoid projection issues.  
- Provides full theoretical derivation of E-step and M-step, with an elegant proof that the E-step corresponds to a k-DPP.  
- Complexity analysis shows the EM method is asymptotically faster than the gradient-ascent baseline (KA).  
- Empirical results on a product recommendation (baby registry) dataset show up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood and better diversity preservation.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; background and Algorithm 2 make the method accessible.  
- Novelty: first EM approach that learns full kernel without restrictive parameterizations, advancing prior DPP learning work.  
- Practical significance for diverse-set applications (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement) is demonstrated.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—results shown on a single real-world dataset; more domains would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: conclusion is brief and could better articulate specific contributions, limitations, and when EM is most beneficial.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated, preserves DPP diversity, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments and insufficient discussion of practical implementation challenges.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance; contribution is significant and well-executed despite scope limitations.
