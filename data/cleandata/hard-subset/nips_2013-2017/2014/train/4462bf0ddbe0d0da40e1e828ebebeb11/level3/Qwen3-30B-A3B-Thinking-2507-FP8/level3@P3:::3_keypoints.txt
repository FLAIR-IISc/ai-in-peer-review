- Addresses NP-hard problem of learning full DPP kernel L without restrictive parameterizations used in prior work.  
- Prior methods limited to scalar per-row weights, linear combinations of fixed DPPs, or fixed parametric forms, losing diversity or DPP properties.  
- Proposes an EM-based method parameterizing L via its eigenvalues and eigenvectors to avoid projection steps in gradient ascent.  
- Key insight: use eigendecomposition and Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound log-likelihood and derive an EM framework.  
- E-step: introduces hidden variable J (selected eigenvectors) and computes a k-DPP distribution.  
- M-step: updates eigenvalues in closed form and optimizes eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold, avoiding harmful projection.  
- Proves the inverse transformation from K to L preserves DPP properties and provides a complete EM derivation.  
- Technical contributions include mapping optimization to the Stiefel manifold and showing the inverse distribution remains a DPP.  
- Experiments on 13 Amazon baby registry categories show EM achieves 16.5% higher log-likelihood than K-Ascent in data-poor settings.  
- Empirical results demonstrate better diversity preservation (e.g., avoiding repeated product recommendations) and 2.1x average speedup.  
- Paper is technically sound, with rigorous derivation, asymptotic runtime advantages, and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and useful supplementary material with precise algorithms and technical details.  
- Original and significant: removes restrictive parameterizations, representing a fundamental methodological shift with broader applicability.  
- Weaknesses: conclusion omits emphasis on Stiefel manifold contribution, experiments limited to one real-world dataset family, and scalability beyond N=71 not explored.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-supported contribution to DPP learning despite limited broader-dataset evaluation.
