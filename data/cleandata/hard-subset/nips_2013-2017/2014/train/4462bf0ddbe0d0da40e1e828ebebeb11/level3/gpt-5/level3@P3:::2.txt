Summary
This paper proposes an EM algorithm for maximum-likelihood learning of determinantal point processes (DPPs) that parameterizes the marginal kernel K via its eigendecomposition. The key idea is to view a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs (eigenvalues set to 1 for a chosen index set J), treat J as a latent variable, and apply a Jensen lower bound to derive an EM procedure. The E-step sets q(J|Y) to the posterior pK(J|Y), which the authors show is a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel, enabling efficient marginal computations. The M-step has a closed-form update for eigenvalues via k-DPP marginals and performs a gradient step for eigenvectors with orthogonality constraints using a Stiefel-manifold update. The method avoids the degeneracies observed in naive projected gradient ascent on K (“K-Ascent”), where eigenvalue clipping can push the solution toward near-diagonal kernels. On a product recommendation dataset (baby registries), the method improves test log-likelihood over K-Ascent and is typically faster asymptotically and in practice.

Relative to prior DPP learning work that either learns only per-item quality weights or mixtures of fixed DPPs, this paper tackles full-kernel learning without restrictive parameterizations. The approach connects to standard EM for mixture models, Riemannian optimization on the Stiefel manifold, and k-DPP marginalization, and is original in combining these ideas for full DPP learning.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The EM derivation via the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view is well-motivated. The E-step characterization of q(J|Y) as a k-DPP and the eigenvalue update using k-DPP marginals are convincing. The Stiefel-manifold update for V is appropriate for maintaining V^T V = I. The complexity analysis clarifies the practical gains over K-Ascent when typical set sizes k are small.
- Empirical support: The paper demonstrates favorable behavior vs K-Ascent in both accuracy (test log-likelihood) and runtime, and qualitatively shows that EM avoids pathologies induced by hard projections. However, experiments are limited to one application domain and one simple baseline.
- Methodological gaps: The eigenvector M-step uses only a single gradient step per outer iteration, guided by likelihood checks that require re-running the E-step for each step size; while practical, a clearer discussion of convergence behavior would help.

Clarity
The write-up is often hard to follow. Specific issues and suggestions:
- Organization: The main derivation is interleaved with side remarks and alternatives, making the narrative hard to track. Recommend presenting the EM objective and both updates cleanly first (with all notation in place), then discussing alternatives, computational tricks, and runtime.
- Notation introduced after use: The marginal kernel K and the eigenvector matrix V appear before being clearly defined in places. Ensure that the L–K relationship and the eigendecomposition K = VΛV^T are stated before use.
- Language/phrasing: “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” (projection step) is unclear. Please state precisely: “the projection chooses the closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q, followed by capping the eigenvalues at 1.”
- Referencing external work: The Stiefel-manifold update defers to [26]; for readability, outline the specific retraction/exp map used, justify neglecting Hessian terms, and state the update you implement without requiring readers to consult [26].
- Ambiguities: On p.4 line 189, clarify what “weight” refers to (mixture weight over J? per-item quality weight?).
- Initialization rationale: The moment-matching initializer (authors’ Eq. 30) needs justification. Why is m_{ij} ignored or clipped in certain cases (via sqrt(max(...,0)))? Why not initialize with the empirical covariance/co-occurrence matrix and project to the DPP-feasible set? Please discuss the trade-offs and any empirical sensitivity.
- Minor: Some symbols (e.g., V, U, B_Y) are introduced with minimal explanation; define each when first used. Provide a short, self-contained summary of k-DPP marginalization used in Eq. (19).

Originality
The combination of (i) latent J via mixture-of-elementary DPPs, (ii) a Jensen lower bound to derive EM, and (iii) Stiefel-manifold optimization for eigenvectors is original for full-kernel DPP learning. Prior work largely learned per-item scalings, mixtures of fixed kernels, or Bayesian parameterizations; learning an unconstrained full kernel with a principled EM is a meaningful advance.

Significance
The contribution is important for DPP-based subset selection because it provides a practical route to learning full kernels while avoiding projection-induced degeneracies and often improving runtime. That said, the empirical section does not yet demonstrate practical superiority over strong non-DPP subset-selection methods. The impact would be clearer with broader comparisons and tasks.

Experimental evaluation
- Strengths: Careful runtime analysis; demonstrates robustness to initialization and projection pitfalls; shows consistent gains over a natural baseline.
- Limitations: Only one application (product recommendation) and one baseline (K-Ascent). The introduction mentions broader subset-selection contexts (document summarization, image search, sensor placement), but the paper does not compare with standard alternatives such as submodular facility location/diversity models with learned weights, mixture-of-submodular shells, or Bayesian DPP parameterizations. Also consider comparing to learning with fixed eigenvectors and only eigenvalue updates to show the value of the full EM.

Pros
- Well-founded EM formulation leveraging eigendecomposition and k-DPP structure.
- Addresses a long-standing challenge of full DPP learning without restrictive parameterizations.
- Avoids known pitfalls of projected gradient ascent and shows runtime benefits.
- Clear potential for broader use in applications where diversity/repulsion matters.

Cons
- Sloppy exposition and notation, with some definitions and motivations arriving late.
- Experimental scope is narrow; lacks comparisons to strong alternative subset-selection methods.
- Limited discussion of convergence properties of the approximate M-step for V.
- Initialization rationale (moment matching) is underspecified.

Recommendation and suggestions
I find the paper technically solid and original, but the clarity and experimental scope need improvement. If accepted, the final version should:
- Reorganize the method section to present the EM framework cleanly before alternatives; define all notation upfront.
- Provide a self-contained description of the Stiefel-manifold update used.
- Clarify ambiguous language (“weight”), fix phrasing around projections, and expand on the moment-matching initialization (including why m_{ij} may be clipped and why alternatives like empirical covariance + projection were not used).
- Substantially strengthen experiments: add at least one more domain (e.g., summarization or image search) and compare against competitive submodular baselines and prior DPP learning approaches (mixtures of fixed kernels, Bayesian parameterizations). Include ablations (eigenvalues-only vs full EM) and metrics beyond log-likelihood (e.g., MAP quality proxies).

Overall, this is a meaningful contribution to DPP learning; with clearer exposition and stronger empirical comparisons, it would be a strong NIPS paper.
