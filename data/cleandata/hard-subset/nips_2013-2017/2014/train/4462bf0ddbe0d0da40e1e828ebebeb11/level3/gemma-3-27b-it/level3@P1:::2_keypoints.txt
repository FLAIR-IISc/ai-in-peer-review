- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning the full kernel matrix of a DPP using eigendecomposition and a Jensen’s-inequality lower bound.
- Core contribution: reparameterizing the kernel in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors instead of kernel entries.
- Uses Stiefel manifold optimization for eigenvector updates and a rigorous EM derivation.
- Positions work well within DPP literature and cites relevant prior work.
- Technical approach is sound and original.
- Jensen’s-inequality lower bound application is appropriate and standard for EM.
- Experiments show the EM method outperforms a K-Ascent baseline, especially with moments-matching initialization.
- Finds EM performs particularly well when negative interactions (measured by the 'd' metric) are strong.
- Major weakness: no comparison to alternative subset-selection methods (greedy, submodular maximization, other diversity models).
- Unclear whether performance gains justify added algorithmic complexity.
- Paper clarity is poor: convoluted writing, imprecise phrasing, and notation introduced after use.
- Method presentation is disorganized; tangential discussions interrupt the main derivation.
- Specific terminology is ambiguous (e.g., “weight”) and some external techniques are referenced without explanation.
- Initialization strategy is insufficiently justified; omission of m_{ij} and empirical covariance is unexplained.
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and dataset variety.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel technically sound solution to a hard problem, potential practical applications, improvement over baseline.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks broader comparisons, poor clarity/organization, limited experiments, ambiguous notation.
- Overall recommendation: technically interesting but diminished impact; recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing clarity and evaluation gaps.
