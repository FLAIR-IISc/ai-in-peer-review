- Proposes a generalized EM to learn full DPP kernels by reparameterizing K via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and using a latent subset J over elementary DPPs.
- Introduces latent J, expresses likelihood as a mixture, and derives a Jensen lower bound yielding an E-step where q(J|Y) is a k-DPP.
- M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and updates eigenvectors via a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step, avoiding PSD projection.
- Avoids projection-induced degeneracy that plagues naive projected gradient ascent (KA) on K entries.
- Empirically, EM outperforms KA (with Wishart init) in test log-likelihood, gives modest gains with moment-matching init, and is faster.
- Novelty: first to leverage the elementary DPP mixture with latent J to perform EM for a full, unconstrained kernel.
- Technical soundness: derivations, k-DPP E-step characterization, marginal computations, and manifold updates are correct and principled.
- Caveat: M-step is approximate (single gradient step); equation (20) holds only for first step, weakening monotonicity/convergence guarantees.
- Practical tweaks (e.g., keeping eigenvalues λ<1−ε) are ad hoc; sensitivity to ε should be investigated.
- Baseline fairness: KA could be strengthened via projection-free parameterizations (e.g., V σ(Θ) V^T or BB^T) or better step-size/trust-region methods.
- Experiments: large dataset and insightful diversity analysis, but need dispersion (mean±std or IQR) over trials and statistical tests.
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized; introducing a small running example for q(J|Y) and clarifying which objective is guaranteed to improve would help.
- Reporting: prefer absolute per-example log-likelihood differences and clearly define “relative test log-likelihood”; ratios of log-likelihoods are hard to interpret.
- Significance: important for applications modeling negative interactions; gains are modest on average but notable in diverse/data-poor regimes and method scales well.
- Pros: clear reparameterization, efficient k-DPP E-step, closed-form eigenvalue updates, principled manifold eigenvector updates, robust and faster than KA.
- Cons: lack of clean monotonicity/convergence guarantee, need for ad hoc practical fixes, weaker baselines, limited initialization exploration, single-domain evaluation, and incomplete uncertainty reporting.
- Suggested author actions: analyze/empirically report convergence behavior under line search, test EM initialized from converged KA and moment-matching, add projection-free baseline parameterizations, report absolute log-likelihoods with CIs and runtime breakdowns.
- Suggested extensions: evaluate on at least one additional public domain (e.g., summarization or image search) and compare to fixed-L mixtures and Bayesian parametric methods.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original, practically useful contribution with room for stronger baselines and broader experiments; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
