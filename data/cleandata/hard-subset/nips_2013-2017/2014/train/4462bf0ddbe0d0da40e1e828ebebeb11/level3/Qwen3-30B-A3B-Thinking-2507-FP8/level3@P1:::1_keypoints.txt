- Proposes an EM framework to learn the full DPP kernel matrix, overcoming prior work that only learned restricted kernel forms.  
- Reparameterizes the kernel via eigenvalues and eigenvectors and views a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs with a hidden subset J.  
- Derives an EM algorithm with an E-step that computes a k-DPP distribution over eigenvector subsets and an M-step that updates eigenvalues in closed form and eigenvectors by gradient steps.  
- Avoids problematic projection steps of gradient-ascent (K-Ascent) that often yield degenerate diagonal kernels.  
- Provides clear theoretical derivation, including Lemma 1 linking the E-step to k-DPPs, and extends prior eigendecomposition insights.  
- Empirical evaluation on 13 Amazon baby registry categories shows up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent with naive Wishart initialization.  
- Shows EM’s advantage correlates with stronger negative item interactions (d > 0.025) and demonstrates average 2.1x runtime speedup over K-Ascent.  
- M-step is complex and relies on approximations/caveats (handling eigenvalues near 1, single gradient step, Stiefel manifold updates), weakening the claimed elegance.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of initialization effects, e.g., initializing EM from K-Ascent solutions is noted but not explored.  
- Use of "relative log likelihood" as the primary metric is questionable; absolute log-probability differences would be more interpretable.  
- Writing is clear and reproducible, though some stylistic choices (repeated “elegant”) are unnecessary.  
- Contributions are significant: enables unconstrained kernel learning without losing computational properties and produces consistent empirical improvements.  
- Technically sound with well-supported claims, but would benefit from addressing M-step limitations, initialization analysis, and metric reporting.
