- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning full DPP kernel matrices, addressing the non-convex log-likelihood challenge.  
- Uses a change of variables to eigenvalues and eigenvectors to avoid projection issues of naive gradient ascent.  
- Derives the EM procedure clearly, employing Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective.  
- Maps eigenvector constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold as a solid technical contribution.  
- Claims asymptotic speed and greater robustness to initialization and dataset changes compared to gradient ascent.  
- Experimental comparison against K-Ascent (KA) on a product recommendation task shows EM preserves quality and diversity better.  
- Reports significant gains in test log-likelihood and faster runtime for EM versus KA.  
- Paper is well-written with coherent, easy-to-follow proofs.  
- Minor clarity issues: some sections need more detailed explanations.  
- Conclusion is hasty and omits discussion of additional contributions and applications.  
- Recommends broader discussion of potential applications (e.g., document summarization, sensor placement).  
- Suggests more experiments on real-world datasets to demonstrate practical impact of produced diversity.  
- Overall assessments: high quality, originality, and significance (scores: Q 9/10, C 8.5/10, O 9/10, S 9/10).  
- Recommendation: Accept, with revisions to the conclusion and additional real-world experiments.
