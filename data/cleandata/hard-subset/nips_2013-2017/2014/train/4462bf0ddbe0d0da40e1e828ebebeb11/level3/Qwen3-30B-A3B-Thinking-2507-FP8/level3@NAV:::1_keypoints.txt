- Proposes an EM framework to learn the full DPP kernel matrix, addressing prior work's restricted settings.  
- Leverages eigendecomposition of the marginal kernel K and represents DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs.  
- EM alternates an E-step (computing a k-DPP variational distribution) and an M-step (closed-form eigenvalue updates, single-step gradient for eigenvectors).  
- Avoids problematic projection steps required by naive kernel-as-parameter (KA) gradient ascent.  
- Provides a principled alternative to direct kernel parameter learning, reducing degenerate solutions.  
- Empirically, EM outperforms KA with naive Wishart initialization by ~3.7% on average and up to 16.5% in data-poor regimes.  
- Identifies that KA's projection often yields nearly diagonal kernels that fail to capture item diversity.  
- M-step is complex in practice, requiring approximations and special-case handling (e.g., λj = 1, Stiefel manifold updates).  
- Analysis of initialization effects is incomplete—unclear whether initializing EM from KA solutions improves results.  
- Metric choice is questionable: reporting relative log-likelihood ratios obscures interpretation; absolute log-probability differences would be clearer.  
- Writing/stylistic issues: overstated claims of “elegance” and unnecessary bolding detract from clarity.  
- Strengths: technically sound approach, clear DPP background exposition, and robust empirical demonstration.  
- Originality: novel EM reformulation of DPP kernel learning, though grounded in established DPP theory.  
- Impact/significance: modest but meaningful for applications needing high-quality diverse subsets; acceptable for NIPS with minor revisions on metric and initialization analysis.
