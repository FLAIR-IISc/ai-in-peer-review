- Proposes a novel DPP learning algorithm that parameterizes the kernel via eigendecomposition and uses an EM optimization.  
- Avoids projection steps in gradient ascent (which can harm similarity/diversity) and is asymptotically faster than naive gradient ascent.  
- Provides a clear, concise background on DPPs, their properties, and limitations of prior learning methods.  
- EM algorithm is well-motivated and derived step-by-step with an easy-to-follow explanation.  
- Technical contributions include mapping the constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold and deriving the inverse distribution as a DPP.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task show the method retains both quality and diversity versus K-Ascent.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes clear figures and tables.  
- Conclusion is somewhat hasty and omits some contributions.  
- Strength: enables learning a full kernel without restrictive parameterizations.  
- Strength: solid and well-motivated mathematical/technical contributions.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness in preserving quality and diversity.  
- Weakness: needs more experiments on real-world datasets to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed time-complexity analysis and direct comparisons of runtime to existing methods.  
- Argument pro acceptance: novel, effective algorithm with strong technical and empirical support.  
- Argument con acceptance: hasty conclusion and insufficient real-world experimental validation.
