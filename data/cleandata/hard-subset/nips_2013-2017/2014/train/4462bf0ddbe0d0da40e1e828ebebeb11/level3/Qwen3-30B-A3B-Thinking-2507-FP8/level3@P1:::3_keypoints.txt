- Addresses a key limitation in learning full DPP kernel L versus prior restricted approaches due to NP-hardness.
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm that parameterizes the kernel by eigenvalues/eigenvectors to avoid projection steps.
- EM avoids projection-induced nearly-diagonal (degenerate) solutions, preserving off-diagonal diversity modeling.
- Leverages eigendecomposition of marginal K and Jensen's inequality to derive principled E- and M-steps.
- Maps constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold and uses multiplicative updates to preserve orthogonality.
- Theoretical derivation is rigorous, mathematically careful, and well-motivated.
- Experiments on Amazon baby registries show up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent.
- EM is especially superior when negative interactions are strong (d > 0.025) and runs ~2.1x faster on average.
- Analysis demonstrates K-Ascent’s poor performance stems from its projection step forcing diagonalization.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with supplementary material supporting reproducibility.
- Appropriately cites prior DPP literature and clearly differentiates the contribution.
- Limitation: experimental validation is limited to a single application domain; broader datasets needed.
- Limitation: does not evaluate how likelihood gains translate to downstream task performance (e.g., engagement).
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution to DPP learning; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments.
