**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound. The authors aim to address the non-convex optimization problem of learning the kernel matrix of a DPP, which is a challenging task in subset selection literature. The proposed Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a more robust and efficient method for learning DPPs compared to existing approaches.

The paper relates to previous work on DPPs, including the use of eigendecomposition for efficient sampling and marginalization. The authors also mention alternative subset-selection methods, such as those based on submodular functions, but do not provide a comprehensive comparison. The paper's contribution is distinct from previous work, as it focuses on developing an EM algorithm for learning DPPs, which is a novel approach in the field.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The proposed EM algorithm is well-founded and original, providing a meaningful contribution to subset selection literature.
* The experiments show favorable behavior compared to a simple K-Ascent baseline, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper provides a thorough derivation of the EM algorithm, including the use of eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, and a Jensen lower bound.

Weaknesses:

* The experiments are limited in scope, and the paper lacks comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods mentioned in the introduction.
* The write-up is sloppy and hard to follow, with notation sometimes undefined or introduced after use.
* The paper structure interleaves method derivation with side remarks and alternatives, making the main thread convoluted.
* Specific notation issues, such as the use of marginal kernel K and variable V before definition, need to be addressed.
* Language and phrasing issues, such as unclear statements and ambiguous terms, need to be clarified.
