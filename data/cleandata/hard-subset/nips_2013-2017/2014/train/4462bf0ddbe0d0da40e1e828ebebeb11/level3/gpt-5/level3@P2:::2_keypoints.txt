- Proposes learning a full DPP kernel by reparameterizing K = V diag(λ) V^T and introducing a latent subset J over eigenvectors to derive a generalized EM.  
- E-step: q(J|Y) = pK(J|Y), a closed-form k-DPP obtained via k-DPP marginalization from V and λ.  
- M-step: closed-form eigenvalue updates via posterior marginals and a Stiefel-manifold gradient step (matrix exponential retraction) for eigenvectors.  
- Claims runtime improvement over naïve projected gradient on K: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) for EM versus O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3) for K-ascent, with k = max |Yi|.  
- Empirical results on Amazon baby registry data show better held-out log-likelihood and faster convergence versus K-ascent, especially when off-diagonal structure matters or data is scarce.  
- Technical soundness: EM derivation, E-step identification as k-DPP, closed-form eigenvalue update, and Stiefel step are well-motivated and appropriate.  
- Method is a generalized EM (single gradient step for V) with monotonicity enforced via step-size search on the true likelihood.  
- Empirical evaluation is narrow: one real dataset, one baseline (K-ascent), and no task-level metrics like recommendation relevance or diversity.  
- Initialization Eq. (23) (Kij = sqrt(max(Kii Kjj − mij, 0))) is nonstandard and insufficiently justified; PSD effects unclear.  
- Baseline strength concern: K-ascent might be improved with alternative parameterizations (bounded-spectrum or low-rank factorizations) or log-det barriers.  
- Runtime advantage disappears when k ≈ N; need to report performance in large-k regimes or clarify applicability.  
- Missing convergence details: stopping criteria, handling λ near 1 (epsilon), and numerical safeguards for Eq. (21) are not stated.  
- Evaluation metrics should include task-level measures (MAP, greedy MAP, hit/diversity) or user-meaningful proxies in addition to likelihood.  
- Exposition issues: notation not always defined before use, narrative interleaves side remarks, and terms like “weight” are ambiguous.  
- Manuscript should provide a self-contained description of the manifold update and justify why a single first-order step suffices.  
- Originality: high — latent subset over eigenvectors and k-DPP E-step for full-kernel DPP learning appears novel.  
- Significance: potentially high for full-kernel DPP learning but limited by narrow experimental validation and weak baselines.  
- Pros: elegant eigendecomposition EM, closed-form eigenvalue updates, addresses projection pitfalls, plausible runtime gains, robust to initialization, code provided.  
- Cons: limited experiments and baselines, sloppy exposition and notation, some ad hoc design choices (initialization) lacking justification.  
- Requested comparisons/ablations: compare to per-row weight learning, mixtures of fixed kernels, simple submodular baselines; ablate eigenvalues-only vs full EM, multiple V-updates, and different initializations.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to one V-update per M-step, behavior when |Yi| large, and effects of step-size search.  
- Minor fixes: tighten phrasing (e.g., projection definition), ensure consistent equation numbering and symbol definitions, and explicitly state generalized EM and monotonicity guarantees.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — technically solid and original but needs clearer exposition and stronger, broader experiments.
