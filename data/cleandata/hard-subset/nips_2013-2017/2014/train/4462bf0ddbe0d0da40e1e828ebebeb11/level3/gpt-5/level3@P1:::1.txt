This paper proposes a generalized EM algorithm for learning full determinantal point process (DPP) kernels by reparameterizing the marginal kernel K in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors and exploiting the mixture view of DPPs over elementary DPPs. The authors introduce a latent subset J of eigenvector indices, write the likelihood as a mixture over J, and derive a Jensen lower bound that yields an E-step in which q(J|Y) becomes a k-DPP, and an M-step that updates eigenvalues in closed form and takes a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step for the eigenvectors. The approach avoids the problematic PSD projection that plagues naive projected gradient ascent (KA) on K’s entries. Empirically, on a large-scale baby registry recommendation dataset, EM achieves consistent improvements in test log-likelihood over KA with Wishart initialization and modest but sometimes notable gains with a moment-matching initialization, while also being faster.

Relation to prior work: Earlier work learned only diagonal (per-item) weights in a convex setting or weights for mixtures of fixed kernels, and Bayesian approaches learned parametric forms rather than an unconstrained kernel. This paper is the first, to my knowledge, that leverages the elementary DPP mixture representation with a latent J to perform (generalized) EM updates for the full kernel, thereby retaining DPP structure and tractable inference.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The derivation of the latent-variable formulation and EM lower bound is standard and appropriate. The E-step characterization as a k-DPP and the efficient computation of marginals are correct and useful. The eigenvalue update is simple and self-normalizing. For the eigenvectors, the use of Stiefel-manifold updates is principled.
- Caveats: The M-step is not solved exactly; Equation (20) is only valid for the first gradient step, and the algorithm therefore performs a single-step update with a line search, recomputing the E-step to assess improvement. This “generalized EM” is reasonable but weakens monotonicity guarantees; the paper should discuss convergence properties (e.g., monotone increase of the likelihood under the proposed line search) more explicitly. Practical details like keeping λ below 1 by a small margin are necessary but ad hoc; sensitivity to this ε would be useful.
- Baseline fairness: The KA baseline is indeed susceptible to projection-induced degeneracy. However, KA could also be strengthened by reparameterizing K as Vσ(Θ)V^T or BB^T with soft constraints on eigenvalues, or by using better step-size/trust-region strategies to reduce projection truncation. A brief experiment with such parameterizations would further clarify the advantage due to EM versus projection-free parametrization.
- Experiments: The dataset is substantial and the analysis of the “diversity” proxy d and its correlation with EM gains is insightful. It would help to report dispersion (e.g., mean±std or IQR) over the 25 Wishart trials and to include statistical tests.

Clarity:
- The paper is generally well written and well organized, with helpful algorithms and complexity discussions. The introduction of the latent J via the elementary DPP mixture is a clear conceptual anchor.
- Suggestions: Avoid subjective language like “elegant,” and remove typographic emphasis (e.g., bolding) in the introduction. Provide a small running example to build intuition about q(J|Y) as a k-DPP. Clarify precisely which objective (bound vs. likelihood) is guaranteed to improve per iteration. Define “relative test log-likelihood” carefully and prefer reporting absolute per-example log-likelihood differences; ratios of log-likelihoods are harder to interpret.

Originality:
- The use of EM over the latent eigenvector-selection process to learn a full, unconstrained DPP kernel is novel relative to prior convex partial-learning and fixed-kernel mixture approaches. The combination of k-DPP E-step, closed-form eigenvalue update, and Stiefel-manifold eigenvector update is a noteworthy contribution.

Significance:
- Learning full DPP kernels is important for applications where modeling negative interactions matters (summarization, recommendation, sensor placement). The reported gains are modest on average but can be substantial in data-poor or strongly diverse regimes, and the method is faster than KA in practice and asymptotically. The approach should stimulate follow-on work in full-kernel DPP learning.

Pros:
- Conceptually clear reparameterization and latent-variable formulation for DPP learning.
- Efficient E-step via k-DPPs; closed-form eigenvalue updates; principled manifold update for eigenvectors.
- Robustness to initialization and avoidance of degenerate projections; faster than KA.
- Solid empirical study with multiple initializations and correlation analysis with diversity proxy.

Cons:
- Generalized EM with single-step eigenvector updates lacks a clean monotonicity/convergence guarantee; practical caveats (λ<1) are needed.
- Baselines could be stronger (e.g., projection-free parameterizations for KA).
- Limited exploration of initialization effects for EM (e.g., seeding EM from a converged KA solution).
- Reporting “relative” log-likelihood is nonstandard; variance/uncertainty not fully reported.
- Single-domain evaluation; broader datasets would increase generality.

Questions/suggestions for the authors:
- Analyze convergence: is the likelihood guaranteed to increase per outer iteration with the proposed line search? If not, report empirical monotonicity and stopping criteria.
- Add an experiment initializing EM from a converged KA model and from the moment-matching initializer to quantify robustness and any additional gains.
- Strengthen baselines with a parameterization that avoids PSD projection (e.g., K = V diag(σ(θ)) V^T with orthonormal V) and/or proximal/trust-region methods.
- Report absolute per-example log-likelihood differences and confidence intervals, and include runtime breakdowns for E/M steps.
- Evaluate on at least one additional public domain (e.g., document summarization or image search) and compare with fixed-L mixtures and Bayesian parametric learning.

Overall assessment:
This is an interesting and well-motivated contribution that advances full-kernel DPP learning with a principled EM approach and demonstrates clear practical benefits over a widely used baseline. While the M-step approximations and experimental scope leave room for improvement, the paper is technically solid, original, and likely to be used by practitioners. I lean toward acceptance.
