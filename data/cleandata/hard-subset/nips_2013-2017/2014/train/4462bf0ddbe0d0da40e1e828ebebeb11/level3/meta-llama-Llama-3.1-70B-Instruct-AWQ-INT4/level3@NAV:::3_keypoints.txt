- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs that does not restrict kernel parameterization.  
- Reparameterizes the kernel via eigendecomposition (eigenvalues and eigenvectors) to enable EM-style optimization.  
- EM avoids projection steps required by naive projected gradient descent, improving robustness and efficiency.  
- Empirically outperforms naive projected gradient ascent on a product recommendation task in test log-likelihood and runtime.  
- Algorithm captures negative interactions between items and is robust to initialization and dataset changes.  
- Paper provides clear exposition of DPPs and the NP-hardness of log-likelihood maximization.  
- Reviews limitations of prior work focused on restricted convex learning settings.  
- Strength: novel, efficient algorithm enabling more flexible and robust DPP learning.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of learning challenges and limitations of previous approaches.  
- Strength: clear writing and well-presented experiments and results.  
- Weakness: conclusion is hasty and omits some contributions.  
- Weakness: needs more experiments on diverse real-world datasets to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand the conclusion and add real-world experiments.  
- Rating: 8/10.
