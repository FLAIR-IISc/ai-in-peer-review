- Addresses learning full DPP kernel L (NP-hard conjectured) with an EM algorithm avoiding restrictive parameterizations.
- Reparameterizes L via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable lower bound on log-likelihood.
- EM avoids problematic projection steps of naive gradient ascent that lead to nearly diagonal L and lost diversity.
- Derives gradient updates for eigenvectors using optimization on the Stiefel manifold.
- Recognizes the latent variable structure inducing a conditional DPP, linking to existing DPP sampling/marginalization algorithms.
- Technical derivations are clear, rigorous, and well-motivated.
- Experiments on a baby-registry dataset show EM outperforms projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) in test log-likelihood and preserves diversity.
- Provides a qualitative example where EM yields more sensible product recommendations than K-Ascent.
- Runtime analysis shows asymptotic and empirical speed advantages for EM.
- Strength: strong technical soundness and clear mathematical development.
- Strength: original contribution enabling learning of full kernel matrices without restrictive assumptions.
- Strength: significant potential applications (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement) by balancing quality and diversity.
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—only one real-world dataset; needs more diverse datasets and settings.
- Weakness: discussion of limitations (e.g., scalability to very high dimensions) is brief and could be expanded.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to initialization; more initialization strategies or robustness analysis needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, effective empirically, and clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently comprehensive experiments and a somewhat brief limitations discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong recommendation to accept—significant contribution with rigorous methods and promising results.
