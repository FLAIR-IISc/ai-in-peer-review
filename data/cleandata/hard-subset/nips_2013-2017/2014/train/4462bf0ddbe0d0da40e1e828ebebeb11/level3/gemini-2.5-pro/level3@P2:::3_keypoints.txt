- Problem: Learning the full PSD kernel matrix L for DPPs via maximum likelihood is hard/non-convex and prior work used restrictive parameterizations.  
- Contribution: Novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes L by its eigendecomposition and introduces eigenvector-selection latent variables.  
- Key idea: Posterior over latent eigenvector selections is a k-DPP, enabling efficient E-step computation.  
- Technical: Derives EM lower bound, closed-form eigenvalue updates, and efficient eigenvector gradients with Stiefel manifold optimization.  
- Advantage vs baseline: Avoids projection steps of projected gradient ascent that can yield poor local optima (e.g., near-diagonal kernels).  
- Originality: Tackles a fundamental open problem and moves beyond restricted convex parameterizations to learn a richer full kernel.  
- Significance: Makes full-kernel DPP learning practical, benefiting recommendation, summarization, and search applications.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and provides intuitive motivation and step-by-step derivations.  
- Experimental: EM outperforms projected gradient ascent on baby registry data in test log-likelihood, diversity quality, and runtime.  
- Qualitative result: EM yields more diverse recommendations (e.g., diverse baby registry items) than the baseline.  
- Limitation 1: Experimental comparison limited—does not compare against restricted-parameterization methods to quantify gains from full-kernel learning.  
- Limitation 2: Only one real-world dataset (product recommendation); broader domains (summarization, image search) would strengthen generality claims.  
- Limitation 3: Conclusion is brief and could better highlight technical insights and future work.  
- Evaluation summary: High quality, excellent clarity, high originality, and high significance.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—technically sound, novel, and practically valuable with minor experimental-scope weaknesses.
