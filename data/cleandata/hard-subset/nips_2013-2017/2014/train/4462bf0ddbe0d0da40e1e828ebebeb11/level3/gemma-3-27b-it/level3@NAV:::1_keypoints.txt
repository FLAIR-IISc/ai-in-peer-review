- Tackles learning the DPP kernel matrix and proposes an EM framework to avoid direct gradient-based KA optimization.  
- Leverages eigendecomposition of the kernel to enable a more stable and potentially efficient learning procedure.  
- Builds on prior DPP work and addresses limitations like restrictive parameterizations and costly kernel ensembles.  
- Reformulates learning via decomposition into elementary DPPs; E-step uses a variational k-DPP and is technically well-motivated.  
- Empirical evaluation on a product-recommendation task shows clear advantages over a naive KA initialization.  
- Modest gains remain even with improved KA initialization, suggesting a fundamental benefit to the EM formulation.  
- M-step is complex, relies on approximations, and the use of a single gradient step may be a bottleneck.  
- Missing analysis of initialization effects—unclear whether EM initialized from KA solutions would improve results.  
- Primary metric “relative log likelihood” is questionable; reporting log-probability differences would be more intuitive.  
- Minor writing issues: repetitive claims of “elegance” and distracting bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing initialization analysis and clarifying metric choice.  
- Pros summarized: novel approach, technically sound, empirical gains, addresses key DPP learning limitations.  
- Cons summarized: modest empirical gains, complex M-step, missing initialization study, questionable metric, and stylistic issues.
