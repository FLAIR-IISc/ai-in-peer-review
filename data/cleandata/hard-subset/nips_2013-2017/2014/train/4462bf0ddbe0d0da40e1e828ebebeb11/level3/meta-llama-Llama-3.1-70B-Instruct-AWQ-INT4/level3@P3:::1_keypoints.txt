- Proposes a novel EM-based algorithm for learning DPP kernels as an alternative to direct gradient-based learning.  
- Key idea: represent any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and use a variational k-DPP in the E-step.  
- M-step updates eigenvalues and eigenvectors with single gradient steps, avoiding PSD projection required by naive gradient ascent.  
- Empirical results: EM substantially outperforms naive kernel ascent (KA) with naive initialization, and modestly with smarter initialization.  
- Runtime: EM is asymptotically faster than KA (EM: O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs KA: O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3)).  
- M-step is complex and relies on caveats and approximations, reducing the method’s claimed elegance.  
- Authors must tighten eigenvalue constraints (keep them slightly below 1) to avoid infinite values in QYi.  
- Eigenvectors are not fully optimized each M-step—only a single gradient step on V is taken, which may be suboptimal.  
- Suggestion: consider more sophisticated updates for V (e.g., Stiefel manifold optimization).  
- Initialization analysis is limited: only Wishart and moments-matching inits are tested, no broader study (e.g., initializing from KA).  
- Evaluation metric concern: using relative log likelihood (ratio) can be hard to interpret; log-probability differences may be more informative.  
- Writing/style notes: avoid unnecessary bolding in the introduction and subjective descriptors like “elegant.”  
- Overall assessment: well-written, interesting, promising approach demonstrated on a real-world recommendation task.  
- Decision rationale: strengths warrant acceptance, but M-step issues, limited initialization analysis, and metric choice justify minor revisions (rating 8/10).
