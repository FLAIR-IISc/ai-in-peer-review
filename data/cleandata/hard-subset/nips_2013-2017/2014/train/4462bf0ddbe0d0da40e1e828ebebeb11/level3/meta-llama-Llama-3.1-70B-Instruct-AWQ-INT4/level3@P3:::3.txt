This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs), a probabilistic model for set diversity. The key insight is to parameterize the DPP kernel matrix using its eigendecomposition, which allows for an effective expectation-maximization (EM) optimization procedure. The proposed method avoids the need for projection steps in gradient ascent, which can harm similarity/diversity, and is asymptotically faster than naive gradient ascent.

The authors provide a clear and concise background on DPPs, including their definition, properties, and previous learning methods. They highlight the limitations of existing approaches, which either restrict the parameterization of the kernel matrix or require a large number of kernel matrices. The proposed EM algorithm is well-motivated and clearly explained, with a step-by-step derivation that is easy to follow.

The paper makes several solid technical contributions. The mapping of the constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold and the derivation of the inverse distribution as a DPP are particularly noteworthy. The experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in retaining both quality and diversity, compared to the baseline K-Ascent (KA) algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide sufficient details for the reader to understand the proposed method and its implementation. The organization is logical, and the figures and tables are clear and informative. The conclusion is somewhat hasty, omitting some contributions, but overall, the paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

Strengths:

* The proposed EM algorithm is a significant improvement over existing methods, allowing for the learning of a full kernel matrix without restrictive parameterizations.
* The paper provides a clear and concise background on DPPs and their properties.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in retaining both quality and diversity.
* The technical contributions, such as the mapping of the constraint to optimization on the Stiefel manifold, are solid and well-motivated.

Weaknesses:

* The conclusion is somewhat hasty and omits some contributions.
* More experiments on real-world datasets would be beneficial to better illustrate the practical importance of the produced diversity.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of the proposed algorithm and its comparison to existing methods.
