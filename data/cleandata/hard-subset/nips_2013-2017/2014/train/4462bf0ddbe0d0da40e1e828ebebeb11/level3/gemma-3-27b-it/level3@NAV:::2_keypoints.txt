- Introduces a novel EM algorithm for learning the full kernel of a DPP, tackling a known NP-hard problem.  
- Reparameterizes the kernel by eigenvalues/eigenvectors, employs a Jensen lower bound, and uses Stiefel-manifold optimization.  
- Extends prior DPP work by moving beyond restricted learning scenarios (scalar weights or mixtures of fixed DPPs).  
- Core idea is original and represents a meaningful technical contribution with a sound theoretical foundation.  
- Appropriately identifies and avoids projection issues that plague naive gradient-ascent approaches.  
- Claims potential advantages in speed and robustness over simpler methods.  
- Paper suffers from poor clarity and organization, often mixing method derivation with tangential discussion.  
- Notation is introduced before formal definitions, phrasing is imprecise, and some terms (e.g., “weight”) are ambiguous.  
- Key methodological steps rely on external references (e.g., Stiefel-manifold optimization [26]), forcing readers to consult cited sources.  
- Several modeling/implementation choices (such as omitting m_ij in eq. 30 and the initialization strategy) lack adequate justification.  
- Experiments show improved test log-likelihood vs a K-Ascent baseline but are limited in scope.  
- Missing comparisons to other relevant subset-selection or alternative-kernel methods, making practical benefits unclear.  
- Recommendation: technically strong but requires substantial revisions to clarity and experimental validation; current verdict is rejection with encouragement to resubmit.
