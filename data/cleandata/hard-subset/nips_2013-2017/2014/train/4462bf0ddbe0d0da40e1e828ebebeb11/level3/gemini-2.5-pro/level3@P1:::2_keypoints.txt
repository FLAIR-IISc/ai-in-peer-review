- Problem & contribution: Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full unconstrained DPP kernel by reparameterizing in eigenvalues/eigenvectors and treating DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs with a latent variable.

- Key idea: Use Jensen’s inequality to derive an EM lower bound; E-step computes posteriors over latent elementary DPPs, M-step updates eigenvalues and eigenvectors (Stiefel manifold optimization).

- Empirical result: EM outperforms a projected gradient-ascent baseline (K-Ascent) on a baby-registry product recommendation task in test log-likelihood and runtime.

- Strength — originality/significance: Tackles a hard, previously intractable problem (full kernel learning) with a non-trivial, potentially widely useful method.

- Strength — technical soundness: Derivation and latent-variable formulation are elegant and correct; using Stiefel manifold optimization for eigenvectors is appropriate.

- Weakness — clarity/exposition: Writing is dense and Section 3 mixes algorithm, derivations, runtime analysis, and alternatives, making the main algorithmic thread hard to follow.

- Weakness — notation issues: Important symbols (e.g., relationship between K and L, definition of V) are introduced late or after first use, harming readability.

- Weakness — baseline description: Description of the projected gradient baseline is vague; phrases like “closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q” should be precise.

- Weakness — M-step exposition: The eigenvector update refers to external algorithm [26] then describes a simplified version without clearly outlining the steps, forcing readers to consult external sources.

- Weakness — limited experiments: Evaluation compares only to a single, flawed baseline (K-Ascent) and uses one dataset, so practical impact vs. other subset-selection/recommendation methods is not demonstrated.

- Suggestion — restructure: Present the final EM algorithm top-down first, then give detailed derivations of E- and M-steps and runtime/alternatives afterward.

- Question — initialization: Clarify rationale for the moment-matching initialization (Eq. 23), specifically the subtraction of empirical pairwise frequency and the square-root term; consider alternative initializations (e.g., empirical covariance).

- Question — step-size policy: Explain the unusual line-search strategy (η initialized to 1, halved until improvement, then doubled after success), since it conflicts with typical schemes.

- Minor edit: Rephrase projection description to explicitly state that V diag(max(λ,0)) V^T finds the closest PSD matrix to Q in Frobenius norm.

- Overall assessment: Technically solid and original with high potential, but poor exposition and insufficient experimental validation require a major revision before acceptance.
