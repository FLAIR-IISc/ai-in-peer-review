This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs) using an Expectation-Maximization (EM) framework. DPPs are probabilistic models that can capture the diversity and quality of sets, making them useful for various applications such as product recommendation, document summarization, and sensor placement.

The authors' main contribution is reformulating kernel learning for DPPs using an EM framework as an alternative to direct gradient-based kernel parameter learning, known as K-Ascent (KA). The EM algorithm is based on the observation that any DPP can be represented as a mixture over elementary DPPs. The algorithm uses a variational distribution over subsets, updates this distribution as a k-DPP in the E-step, and performs single gradient steps in the M-step.

The empirical results show that the EM algorithm outperforms KA substantially with naive KA initialization and modestly in some settings with smarter KA initialization. The authors also provide an analysis of the runtime complexity of the EM algorithm, which is asymptotically faster than KA.

However, the M-step of the EM algorithm is fairly involved and requires caveats and approximations, which weakens the claimed elegance of the method. Additionally, the paper lacks an analysis of initialization effects, such as whether initializing the EM algorithm from KA solutions yields improvements.

Furthermore, the use of "relative log likelihood" as a metric is questionable, and it would be more informative to report log-probability differences for easier interpretation. Some writing and style issues, such as the use of the word "elegant" to describe the algorithm and unnecessary bolding in the introduction, detract from the overall quality of the paper.

Overall, this is an interesting and promising approach with modest empirical gains. While the experiments could be improved, the paper is well-written and provides a good contribution to the field of DPPs.
