- Proposes a novel third-order model that multiplicatively combines word context and attribute vectors to predict next words, enabling conditional word similarity.  
- Jointly learns distributed attribute representations together with word embeddings.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on sentiment classification, cross-lingual document classification, and blog authorship attribution.  
- Strengths: original idea, technically sound, significant potential impact, and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Provides impressive experimental results with thorough performance analysis.  
- Theoretical analysis supports the model’s claims.  
- Writing issues: occasional overly dramatic language (e.g., “elegant” algorithm) and unnecessary bold text in the introduction.  
- Needs more analysis of the effect of initialization on the proposed EM method.  
- The relative log likelihood measure used in experiments requires clearer explanation.  
- Some notation and terminology could be more clearly explained for readers.  
- Reproducibility: sufficient information for expert readers to reproduce results, though clarity could improve.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address writing, EM initialization analysis, and clarity on evaluation metrics.
