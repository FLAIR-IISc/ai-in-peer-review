- Addresses maximum-likelihood learning of full, unconstrained DPP kernels via an EM-style algorithm in the eigenbasis.  
- Reparameterizes K = V diag(λ) V^T, introduces hidden eigen-index set J, and derives a Jensen lower bound for tractable optimization.  
- E-step yields q(J|Y) that is a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel; M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and eigenvectors via Stiefel-manifold gradient ascent with multiplicative retraction.  
- Avoids problematic PSD/eigenvalue projection that causes projected gradient ascent (KA) to degenerate to near-diagonal kernels.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and Amazon baby registries show improved test log-likelihood, better diversity preservation, and up to 2.1× speedups and 16.5% gains in low-data settings.  
- Qualitative examples show KA overemphasizes item quality and reduces diversity (e.g., recommending multiple similar items) while EM maintains variety.  
- Technical novelty: clean EM formulation in the eigenbasis and recognizing the conditional posterior as a k-DPP.  
- Algorithmic contribution: closed-form eigenvalue updates and Stiefel-manifold eigenvector updates using multiplicative (matrix-exponential) retraction.  
- Insightful derivation with complexity analysis O(nN k^2) per outer iteration and attention to practicalities (λ < 1, step-size selection).  
- Clear writing and coherent presentation, with accessible connections to sampling, conditional DPPs, and manifold optimization.  
- Main empirical shortcoming: comparisons primarily against KA; omits convex per-item-weight and mixture-of-kernels baselines.  
- Convergence concern: practical EM uses step-size trials and reruns E-steps, so monotonicity and convergence guarantees are not analyzed.  
- Limited real-world breadth: experiments focused on one application; additional datasets (summarization, sensor placement) and diversity metrics would help.  
- Conclusion is terse and omits highlighting of some contributions (conditional k-DPP q(J|Y) and Stiefel perspective).  
- Overall quality: technically sound with careful derivations and convincing diagnosis of KA’s failure mode.  
- Originality: novel approach to learning unrestricted DPP kernels via eigen-EM and Stiefel updates.  
- Significance: practically impactful for diversity-sensitive applications and likely reusable algorithmic ideas.  
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on broader baselines and empirical scope to strengthen significance.  
- Suggestions: add baselines (diagonal scaling, mixtures, Bayesian methods), broaden datasets and diversity metrics, and include ablations and a brief convergence discussion.
