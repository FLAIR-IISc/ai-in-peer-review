- Tackles the NP-hard problem of learning the DPP kernel matrix L.  
- Proposes a novel EM method operating on eigenvalues and eigenvectors of L using Jensen’s inequality.  
- Method removes restrictive parameterizations of L and avoids problematic projection steps in gradient methods.  
- Maps constrained learning to optimization on the Stiefel manifold and proves the resulting distribution is a DPP.  
- Step-by-step EM derivation is clear, insightful, and theoretically sound.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and a product recommendation task show improved preservation of quality and diversity over K-Ascent.  
- Comparison to K-Ascent is informative but uses a simplified baseline (top-10 replacements), limiting strength of claims.  
- Limited scope of real-world datasets weakens evidence of broad applicability.  
- Conclusion is rushed and should better emphasize practical implications and contributions.  
- Suggest evaluating on additional DPP application domains (e.g., document summarization, image search).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-grounded theory, clear derivation, empirical gains, solid technical contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited real-world experiments, incomplete conclusion, and somewhat restrictive baseline; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
