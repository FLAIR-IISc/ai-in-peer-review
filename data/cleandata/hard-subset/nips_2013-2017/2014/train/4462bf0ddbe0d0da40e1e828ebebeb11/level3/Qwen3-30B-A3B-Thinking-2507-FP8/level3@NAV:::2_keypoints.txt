- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel matrix, removing restrictive assumptions from prior work.
- Exploits eigendecomposition of marginal kernel K and derives a Jensen lower bound to enable an EM-style optimization.
- Avoids problematic projection steps of naive gradient ascent (K-Ascent) through its EM formulation.
- Introduces novel technical elements, including Stiefel-manifold optimization for eigenvector updates and mixtures of elementary DPPs.
- The approach is theoretically well-founded and builds correctly on DPP theory.
- Demonstrates empirical gains up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent on a product recommendation task.
- Paper is poorly organized: method derivation is interleaved with side remarks, disrupting the main narrative.
- Notation and terms are introduced unclearly (e.g., K, V used before definition; ambiguous use of "weight"; unclear phrasing about Frobenius norm).
- Relies on external reference [26] for key details instead of providing a self-contained explanation.
- Experimental evaluation is limited, comparing only to K-Ascent and omitting other relevant subset-selection baselines (k-DPPs, DPP ensembles).
- Key methodological choices lack justification (e.g., excluding m_{ij} in Eq. 30 and the chosen initialization not using empirical covariance).
- Recommends substantial revisions: reorganize to present method clearly first, define all notation upfront, and include comprehensive comparisons to relevant approaches.
