- Addresses learning the DPP kernel L and proposes an EM algorithm that avoids restrictive parameterizations and projection steps.
- Reparameterizes L via eigenvalues and eigenvectors, preserving DPP diversity properties and preventing degenerate solutions.
- Uses Jensen's inequality to derive a log-likelihood lower bound and formulates constraints as optimization on the Stiefel manifold.
- Empirically outperforms projected gradient ascent on synthetic and product-recommendation tasks in log-likelihood and runtime.
- Paper is well-organized with rigorous derivations, making technical content accessible to experts.
- Limited real-world experiments; more diverse datasets needed to validate generalizability and diversity benefits.
- Conclusion is brief and omits broader implications, limitations, and future directions.
- Method is sensitive to non-trivial initializations (e.g., Wishart or moment-matching), which may limit applicability.
- Pros: advances DPP learning, theoretically sound EM method, strong empirical improvements, multiple application domains.
- Cons: insufficient experimental breadth and reliance on specific initializations.
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested expansion of experiments and a more thorough conclusion in revisions.
