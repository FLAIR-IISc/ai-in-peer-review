This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs), which are probabilistic models that can capture the diversity of sets. The key contribution of this work is a method that does not restrict the parameterization of the DPP kernel, allowing for more flexible and robust learning.

The paper starts by introducing the concept of DPPs and their applications in various machine learning tasks. It then discusses the challenges of learning DPPs, particularly the NP-hardness of maximizing the log-likelihood of the kernel matrix. The authors review previous work on learning DPPs, which has focused on restricted convex learning settings.

The proposed algorithm, Expectation-Maximization (EM) for DPPs, is based on the eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix. By changing the parameterization from matrix entries to eigenvalues and eigenvectors, the authors develop an EM-style optimization algorithm that avoids the need for projection steps. The EM algorithm is shown to be more robust and efficient than the naive approach of maximizing likelihood by projected gradient ascent.

The paper presents experimental results on a product recommendation task, where the proposed EM algorithm outperforms the naive approach in terms of test log-likelihood and runtime. The authors also provide insights into the advantages of the EM algorithm, including its ability to capture negative interactions between items and its robustness to initialization and dataset changes.

The strengths of this paper include:

* A novel and efficient algorithm for learning DPPs that does not restrict the parameterization of the kernel matrix.
* A thorough analysis of the challenges of learning DPPs and the limitations of previous work.
* Experimental results that demonstrate the advantages of the proposed EM algorithm.
* A clear and well-written presentation of the ideas and results.

The weaknesses of this paper include:

* The conclusion is somewhat hasty and omits some contributions.
* More experiments on real-world datasets are recommended to better illustrate the practical importance of the produced diversity.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and provides a new approach to learning DPPs that can be applied to various applications.
