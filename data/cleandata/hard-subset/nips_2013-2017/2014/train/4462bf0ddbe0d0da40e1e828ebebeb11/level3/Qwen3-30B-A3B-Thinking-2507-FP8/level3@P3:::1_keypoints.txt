- Proposes an EM framework for learning the full DPP kernel matrix, addressing prior limitations in kernel learning.  
- Key insight: any DPP can be represented as a mixture over elementary DPPs, enabling an EM formulation that avoids projection steps required by naive gradient ascent.  
- Method reformulates learning using eigendecomposition of K with a hidden variable for eigenvector subsets; E-step computes a k-DPP, M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and takes a gradient step on eigenvectors.  
- EM avoids the problematic projection issues of K-Ascent and provides a principled alternative to previous restricted approaches.  
- Demonstrated on real-world baby registry product recommendation data; EM substantially outperforms KA with naive initialization and modestly with improved initialization.  
- Empirical gains are meaningful (up to 16.5% relative improvement) and the method is faster on average (about 2.1× speedup).  
- Quality: technically sound with a clear theoretical foundation and sufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Concern: M-step relies on approximations (single gradient steps) and requires careful handling of Stiefel manifold constraints, which weakens the claimed elegance.  
- Missing experiment: no analysis of initializing EM from KA solutions to see if combined approaches yield further improvements.  
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized, but the “elegance” claim is overstated and unnecessary bolding distracts; reporting relative log-likelihood ratios is confusing.  
- Presentation issue: derivation and computational complexity of Equation (20) need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: novel application of EM and the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs connection to unconstrained kernel learning, distinguishing it from prior DPP work.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical limitation for diverse subset selection and provides a robust alternative when KA fails due to poor initialization.  
- Strengths: novel, theoretically grounded approach with thorough empirical analysis and useful runtime/practical demonstrations.  
- Weaknesses: M-step complexity undermines elegance, confusing metric choice, and lack of the specific ablation/initialization experiment; the “elegant” self-description is unsupported.
