Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles the long-standing challenge of learning full determinantal point process (DPP) kernels from data. DPPs model diversity in subset selection via a PSD kernel L (or marginal kernel K), but maximizing likelihood over L is conjectured to be NP-hard; prior work therefore learned only restricted parameterizations (e.g., per-item scalings or mixtures of fixed kernels), sacrificing expressivity and often the ability to capture negative interactions crucial for diversity. This paper reparameterizes K via its eigendecomposition (eigenvalues and eigenvectors), introduces a latent variable J indexing eigenvector selections from the elementary DPP view, and derives an EM algorithm by lower-bounding the log-likelihood with Jensen’s inequality. The E-step yields a conditional k-DPP, enabling efficient marginal computations; the M-step updates eigenvalues in closed form and takes a gradient step on eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold, thereby avoiding projections that can induce degeneracy in naive projected gradient ascent (KA). Experiments on synthetic data and a real product recommendation task (baby registries) show improved test likelihood and robustness over KA, with asymptotic and empirical speedups.

This builds directly on the DPP literature at NIPS and elsewhere: Kulesza & Taskar (Foundations and Trends; NIPS Structured DPPs), k-DPPs (ICML), MAP inference (NIPS), and recent parameter-learning approaches (UAI, ICML, NIPS). The key novelty is using the eigenvector-selection latent variable to construct an EM procedure for unconstrained kernel learning while preserving the DPP structure and avoiding PSD/eigenvalue projection pitfalls.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: An elegant EM formulation over eigenvalues/eigenvectors that leverages the mixture-of-elementary-DPPs view. Showing that the E-step posterior is a k-DPP is a neat and useful result.
- Technical soundness: Derivations are clear; the eigenvalue update is closed-form and self-normalizing; the eigenvector update respects V^T V=I via a Stiefel manifold step. The runtime analysis is careful, highlighting an O(n N k^2) dependence for the main per-iteration cost.
- Practical relevance: Avoids projection-induced degeneracy in KA, which can collapse off-diagonals and undermine diversity modeling. Demonstrated gains (up to 16.5% test LL in data-poor settings) and faster training (∼2x) on a real recommendation task.
- Clarity: The EM derivation is well explained; mapping constraints to manifold optimization and surfacing the latent J are pedagogically valuable. Writing and proofs are coherent.

Weaknesses
- Empirical scope: Real-data evaluation is limited to a single domain (baby registries). Additional heterogeneous datasets (e.g., image search, summarization, sensor placement) would strengthen claims of generality.
- Baselines: KA is a relatively weak comparator. It would be informative to compare against: (i) convex diagonal-scaling (per-item weights), (ii) mixtures/ensembles with fixed L’s, and (iii) Bayesian parametric DPP learning. Also try stronger optimizers for KA (e.g., adaptive step sizes, quasi-Newton).
- Metrics: The focus is on log-likelihood. Including downstream utility (MAP or expected-utility set quality, diversity/coverage measures, and qualitative diversity of recommendations) would better demonstrate practical impact.
- Scalability: Although asymptotically better than KA, the method still requires repeated E-steps and N×N operations for V updates. Discussion or experiments on larger N (via low-rank structure, Nyström, or stochastic/mini-batch EM) would improve the significance.
- Implementation details: The handling of λj near 1 is ad hoc (“tighten the constraint”); a principled barrier/regularizer or explicit bound would be preferable. Clarify the cost and stability of repeatedly computing k-DPP eigendecompositions per Yi and any caching strategies.

Quality
- The algorithm is technically sound, with careful use of k-DPP marginals for efficient eigenvalue updates and a justified Stiefel manifold step for V. The lower bound is standard EM; convergence to local optima is expected. Claims are supported by both synthetic checks and a substantive real task. Some design choices (λ clipping, step-size search with repeated E-steps) could be better justified and explored.

Clarity
- Generally well written and organized. The use of equations (16)–(21) and the algorithm summaries is clear. Minor suggestions: move some derivation details from the supplement into the main text (e.g., the proof that q is a k-DPP), clarify the role of BYi in the V gradient, and explicitly state the convergence criterion and typical T1, T2.

Originality
- To my knowledge, this is the first EM algorithm that learns a full unconstrained DPP kernel by reparameterizing in the eigenbasis and using the elementary DPP mixture latent variable. This goes beyond prior convex or parametric constraints and beyond mixtures that leave the DPP family.

Significance
- Learning full DPP kernels is important for applications demanding diversity. The method preserves DPP structure and seems robust to initialization, with notable gains where negative interactions matter. Broader empirical validation and stronger baselines would further elevate impact.

Questions for the authors
- Can you provide comparisons to per-item scaling (convex) and mixture-of-DPPs baselines? How does EM fare on MAP set quality and diversity metrics?
- How sensitive are results to initialization and to the λ upper bound? Any regularization (e.g., trace or Frobenius norm, or eigenvalue entropy) to control overfitting?
- Can the E-step be amortized or mini-batched to improve scalability? Any caching of QYi eigendecompositions?
- Does the method handle feature-based kernels (e.g., L=Q^TQ with learned features), or can the EM be adapted to low-rank K?

Pro acceptance
- Novel and technically solid EM formulation; preserves diversity modeling.
- Addresses a known failure mode of projected gradient; faster and more robust.
- Clear derivations; useful insight that the E-step posterior is a k-DPP.

Con acceptance
- Limited baselines and datasets; practical significance not fully demonstrated across domains.
- Scalability to very large N not yet shown; some implementation choices are heuristic.

Overall recommendation
- Leaning toward accept, contingent on strengthening the empirical section with additional datasets and baselines, and clarifying implementation details. The methodological contribution is novel and relevant to the NIPS community.

Reviewer confidence
- High: familiar with DPPs and probabilistic learning; moderate uncertainty regarding breadth of empirical performance beyond the presented tasks.
