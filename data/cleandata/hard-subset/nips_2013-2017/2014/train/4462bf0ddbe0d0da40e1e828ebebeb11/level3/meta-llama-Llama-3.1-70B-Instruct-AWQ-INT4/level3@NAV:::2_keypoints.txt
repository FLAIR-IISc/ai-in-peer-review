- Proposes a novel, well-founded algorithm for learning DPPs using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound.  
- Clear and concise introduction to DPPs, applications, and learning challenges.  
- Technically sound: thorough derivation of the EM algorithm with clear E-step and M-step updates.  
- Good explanation of the Stiefel-manifold optimization for updating eigenvectors.  
- Experimental results show the method is effective and outperforms a simple K-Ascent baseline.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and lack comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods from the introduction.  
- Practical benefits of the DPP approach remain unclear without stronger empirical comparisons.  
- Write-up is sometimes sloppy, hard to follow, and interleaves method derivation with side remarks, making the main thread convoluted.  
- Notation problems: key symbols (e.g., marginal kernel K, variable V) are used before being defined.  
- Language and phrasing issues (e.g., ambiguous wording like "minimises the change to Frobenius norm") need clarification.  
- Section citing external work ([26]) is hard to follow; the authors should outline simpler approaches directly.  
- Ambiguous terminology (e.g., "weight" on p.4 line 189) requires precise definition.  
- Questionable rationale for equation (30); authors should explain choices like not using m_{ij} and not initializing from empirical covariance.  
- Overall verdict: technically solid and original but requires clearer exposition and substantially stronger experimental comparisons.
