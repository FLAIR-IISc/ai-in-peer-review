- Proposes an EM framework for learning the full kernel matrix of a DPP as an alternative to direct gradient-based kernel learning (KA).  
- Leverages eigendecomposition of the kernel and decomposes into elementary DPPs, using a k-DPP variational distribution in the E-step.  
- Well-motivated and correctly highlights limitations of prior work (convexity restrictions and degenerate solutions with gradient ascent).  
- Theoretical justification for the M-step is provided and connected to Stiefel manifold optimization.  
- The M-step is complex in practice, relies on approximations (e.g., a single gradient step for eigenvectors) and is nontrivial to implement.  
- Empirical evaluation on the baby registry dataset shows up to 16.5% improvement over naive KA initialization, with modest gains over smarter initializations.  
- Experiments omit a key initialization analysis: whether initializing EM from the KA solution improves performance.  
- It is unclear if EM genuinely finds a better optimization trajectory or simply escapes poor KA initializations.  
- The use of “relative log likelihood” as the primary metric is questionable; absolute log-probability differences would be more interpretable.  
- Writing is generally clear but the repeated use of “elegant” and unnecessary bolding are stylistic weaknesses.  
- Overall assessment: promising and theoretically solid, but impact weakened by M-step complexity and lack of initialization analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address initialization experiments, report absolute log-probability differences, clarify M-step approximations, and tone down subjective wording.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach to a challenging problem, solid theoretical foundation, demonstrated empirical gains, and addresses limitations of existing methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical complexity and approximations in the M-step, missing initialization analysis, questionable evaluation metric, and minor stylistic issues.
