- Tackles ML learning of full DPP kernels by reparameterizing K = VΛV^T and introducing hidden index J in an EM framework.
- E-step: q(J|Y) shown to be a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel using U = V^T and R = diag(√(λ/(1−λ))).
- M-step: eigenvalues updated in closed form via k-DPP marginals; eigenvectors updated by a single gradient step on the Stiefel manifold using a multiplicative matrix-exponential.
- Avoids projection-based gradient methods (KA) that can zero eigenvalues and collapse diversity modeling.
- Experiments on baby registry recommendations (N≈71, n≈8.6k) show EM improves test log-likelihood and is ~2.1× faster than KA, with larger gains in low-data regimes.
- Technical soundness: derivation leverages known DPP identities; generalized EM with V-step preserves monotonic likelihood increases.
- Complexity analyzed as O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), favorable when k ≪ N; practical safeguard λ < 1 noted.
- Empirical gains: up to 16.5% likelihood improvement over KA with Wishart init; retains advantage with moment-matching init; qualitative reduction in recommendation redundancy.
- Clarity: paper is well-written with concise preliminaries and reproducible algorithmic details; proofs in supplement.
- Originality: novel eigenbasis EM with k-DPP E-step and manifold-aware V-update within DPP learning literature.
- Significance: approach is broadly applicable to diversity-critical tasks (summarization, search, sensor placement, recommendations).
- Strength: principled EM, closed-form eigenvalue updates, manifold optimization avoids harmful projections, robust to initialization, and runtime advantage.
- Weakness: experiments limited to a single real-world domain; needs additional datasets (e.g., summarization/image) for generality claims.
- Baseline limitations: KA is a reasonable strawman but comparisons to other parameterizations (low-rank, Cholesky, manifold quasi-Newton, Bayesian) are missing.
- Optimization details lacking: no ablation on number of V-updates or step-size sensitivity; single-step V-update choice unexplored.
- Numerical concerns: cost and stability of matrix exponential for larger N not analyzed; line-search involves O(N^3) eigendecompositions.
- Scalability suggestion: discuss/experiment with approximations (Nyström, randomized SVD, low-rank DPPs) for large N.
- Evaluation suggestion: supplement log-likelihood with task metrics reflecting diversity, coverage, and user utility in recommendation.
- Theory gap: convergence guarantees are empirical; brief discussion of generalized EM convergence would help.
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the λ < 1 cap and to initialization; any heuristics for setting the cap?
- Question: can the V-update use Cayley transform or first-order retraction to avoid matrix exponentials?
- Question: have the authors explored low-rank or Nyström approximations within the EM framework?
- Question: how does the method behave when observed sets have widely varying or large sizes k?
- Overall: strong, novel, and practical contribution to full DPP learning with solid empirical/runtime gains; would be stronger with broader validation and more baselines.
