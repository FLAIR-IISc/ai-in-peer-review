This paper proposes an EM algorithm for learning full-kernel DPPs by reparameterizing the marginal kernel via its eigendecomposition, introducing a latent index set over eigenvectors (mixture of elementary DPPs), deriving a Jensen lower bound, and performing alternating updates: closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and eigenvector updates on the Stiefel manifold via a matrix-exponential step. Relative to prior DPP learning work that optimizes only per-item weights or mixtures with fixed kernels, this tackles the nonconvex full-kernel problem directly and avoids fragile PSD projections required by K-Ascent. Experiments on a product recommendation dataset show consistent improvements in test log-likelihood (up to 16.5%) and runtime advantages over a projected gradient baseline.

Quality: The method is technically well-founded and original. The mixture interpretation, k-DPP E-step, and Stiefel manifold update are sound and connect cleanly to established results in DPPs and optimization. However, the empirical evaluation is narrow (one application domain, limited baselines) and does not compare against alternative subset-selection approaches cited in the introduction (e.g., facility-location/coverage submodular models, learned submodular mixtures), leaving practical benefits over non-DPP models unclear. Some methodological choices need justification: in Eq. (30), why is m_ij omitted, and why not initialize with the empirical covariance?

Clarity: The write-up is hard to follow. The derivation is interleaved with side remarks and alternatives, obscuring the main thread. Recommend: first present the core EM (latent J, lower bound, eigenvalue closed form, Stiefel update) in a self-contained sequence, then discuss variants and runtime. Define marginal kernel K and the eigenvector matrix V before use; both appear prior to definition. Clarify ambiguous terms (e.g., what “weight” refers to on p.4, line 189). Improve language precision, e.g., “closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q” instead of “minimises the change to Frobenius norm.” The section relying on [26] should outline the specific Stiefel update used rather than sending readers to the source.

Originality: High. The EM formulation with eigenvalue/k-DPP marginals and Stiefel-manifold eigenvector updates is a novel and meaningful contribution.

Significance: Potentially strong for DPP learning and applications requiring diversity, but current evidence is limited. Please add: comparisons to strong submodular/diversity baselines, ablations (eigenvalue vs eigenvector updates), sensitivity to initialization, and additional domains or MAP-quality metrics.

Pros for acceptance:
- Technically solid, elegant EM formulation
- Addresses an important open problem; avoids degenerate projections; principled updates

Cons for acceptance:
- Clarity issues and undefined notation
- Missing comparisons to non-DPP subset-selection methods
- Limited empirical scope and some unmotivated choices

Overall: Promising and original, but I recommend revision to improve exposition and substantially strengthen experimental comparisons.
