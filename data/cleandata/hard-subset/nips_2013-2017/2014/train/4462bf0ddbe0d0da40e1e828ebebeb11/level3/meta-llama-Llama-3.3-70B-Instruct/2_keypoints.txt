- Proposes a novel framework for learning distributed attribute representations jointly with word embeddings.  
- Introduces a third-order model where word context and attribute vectors interact multiplicatively to predict next words (conditional word similarity).  
- Evaluated on sentiment classification, cross-lingual document classification, and blog authorship attribution with strong gains over baselines.  
- Method is well-founded and presents a solid, original advance for subset selection in NLP.  
- Evaluation is thorough with multiple experiments and comparisons to alternative approaches.  
- Paper lacks clarity: write-up is sloppy, hard to follow, and plagued by undefined notation and convoluted structure.  
- Needs more comprehensive experiments and direct comparisons to other subset selection methods.  
- Specific errors: unclear statements and insufficient explanation for key choices (e.g., third-order model and multiplicative interaction).  
- Authors provide detailed analysis of results, highlighting strengths and weaknesses.  
- With revisions to clarity, notation, and additional comparisons, the work could be significantly strengthened and impactful.
