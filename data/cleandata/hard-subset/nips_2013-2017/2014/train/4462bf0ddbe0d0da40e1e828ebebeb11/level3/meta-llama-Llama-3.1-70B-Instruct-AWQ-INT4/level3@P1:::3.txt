This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs), a probabilistic model that assigns higher probability to diverse sets. The key contribution of this work is the development of an expectation-maximization (EM) style optimization algorithm that avoids the need for projection steps in gradient ascent, which can harm the modeling of similarity and diversity.

The paper begins by discussing the limitations of previous work on learning DPPs, which either restrict the parameterization of the kernel matrix L or only partially learn its entries. The authors argue that these approaches sacrifice the diversity dimension of the model, which is a key aspect of DPPs.

The proposed EM algorithm is based on the eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix L, which allows for a more efficient optimization procedure. The authors show that the EM algorithm preserves the diversity of the model better than previous work, which focused mainly on quality.

The paper also provides a thorough derivation of the EM procedure, including the use of Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the objective. The authors also explore optimization algorithms that remove the need to project values and exploit full-rank V to avoid projecting eigenvectors.

The experimental results on a product recommendation task demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in retaining both quality and diversity. The authors also compare their method to a baseline approach, K-Ascent (KA), and show that their method outperforms KA in terms of test log-likelihood.

Weaknesses:
