- Proposes a novel EM-style algorithm for learning DPPs that avoids projection steps in gradient ascent.  
- Algorithm leverages eigendecomposition of kernel L for more efficient optimization.  
- EM approach preserves diversity better than prior methods focused mainly on quality.  
- Provides thorough derivation of the EM procedure, using Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the objective.  
- Explores projection-free optimization techniques, including mapping constraints to the Stiefel manifold and exploiting full-rank V to avoid projecting eigenvectors.  
- Notes technical contribution that the inverse distribution is a DPP (omitted in the conclusion).  
- Experiments on a product recommendation task show improved retention of both quality and diversity and better test log-likelihood than K-Ascent.  
- Strength: novel algorithm that empirically and theoretically preserves diversity without projections.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with detailed derivations and optimization discussion.  
- Weakness: needs more experiments on diverse real-world datasets to demonstrate practical impact of diversity.  
- Weakness: conclusion is somewhat hasty and omits some technical contributions.  
- Originality: introduces a new projection-free EM algorithm for DPP learning that better preserves diversity.  
- Significance: method has potential applications across recommendation, summarization, sensor placement, etc.  
- Quality: technically sound and experimentally validated, with exploration of alternative optimization strategies.
