- Proposes an EM-style algorithm for learning full DPP kernels as an alternative to direct gradient-based learning on K (KA).
- Key idea: use the mixture representation of a DPP over elementary DPPs indexed by eigenvector subsets J and introduce q(J|Y) as a variational distribution to lower-bound the likelihood.
- E-step sets q(J|Y)=p(J|Y;K), shown to be a k-DPP, enabling efficient computation.
- M-step updates eigenvalues via closed-form marginals and updates eigenvectors by a single gradient step on the Stiefel manifold.
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms KA with naive initialization, is modestly better with stronger initializers, and is asymptotically faster.
- Relates naturally to prior work on learning item weights, mixtures of fixed DPPs, k-DPPs, and eigendecomposition-based DPP inference (e.g., Kulesza & Taskar; Affandi et al.).
- E-step derivation and eigenvalue updates are sound and efficiently implemented using k-DPP marginals.
- M-step is only partially optimized (single gradient step), needs repeated E-steps for step-size selection, and uses approximations (e.g., λ lower bound), breaking monotone EM guarantees.
- Runtime comparison is plausible but should report number of expensive eigendecompositions per iteration and total wall-clock time with variance.
- Clarity is generally good, but the intro’s self-praise/bolding is unnecessary.
- Request to clarify whether reported improvements use the true log-likelihood or the variational lower bound and to define “relative log-likelihood” precisely.
- Recommend reporting absolute log-likelihood differences (per example or per item) instead of ratios of log-probabilities for interpretability.
- Novelty: reformulating kernel learning for DPPs via EM with a k-DPP E-step and eigenvalue/eigenvector separation is a novel contribution.
- Significance: addresses an important problem, shows robustness to poor initialization, and offers practical gains for practitioners.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually novel EM reformulation, principled E-step and efficient eigenvalue updates, consistent empirical gains (especially with naive initialization), and relevant to core NIPS topics.
- Cons for acceptance: M-step complexity and approximations weaken theoretical guarantees and elegance.
- Additional cons: missing analysis of initialization effects (e.g., EM initialized from KA or vice versa) and limited ablations/sensitivity analyses (effect of |Y| distribution, k).
- Metric choice (relative log-likelihood) and limited sensitivity experiments hinder interpretability and assessment of generality.
