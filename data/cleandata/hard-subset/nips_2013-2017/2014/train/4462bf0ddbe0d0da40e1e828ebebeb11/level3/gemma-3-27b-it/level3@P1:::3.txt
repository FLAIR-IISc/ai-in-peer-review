This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), probabilistic models valuable for subset selection tasks requiring both quality and diversity. While DPPs are compactly parameterized by a positive semi-definite kernel matrix *L*, learning *L* from data is conjectured to be NP-hard. Previous approaches have focused on partially learning *L* – either by learning scalar weights for each row or by learning weights for a fixed set of DPPs – thereby sacrificing either diversity or computational efficiency. This work presents a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning the full kernel matrix *L* without restrictive parameterizations, offering a significant step forward in DPP learning.

The core contribution lies in reparameterizing *L* in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and then leveraging Jensen’s inequality to derive a lower bound on the log-likelihood. This transformation allows for an effective optimization procedure that avoids the problematic projection steps inherent in naive gradient ascent methods, which can lead to degenerate solutions and a loss of diversity.  The authors convincingly argue that these projection steps often result in learning a nearly diagonal *L*, failing to capture the crucial negative interactions between items that drive diversity.  

The technical details are well-developed. The paper clearly outlines the derivation of the EM algorithm, providing insightful explanations at each step.  Formulating the learning problem in terms of the eigendecomposition of *L* is a key innovation, connecting the problem to existing algorithms for DPP sampling and marginalization. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate a strong understanding of related work, including efficient algorithms for k-DPPs and optimization on Stiefel manifolds, incorporating these insights into their method.  The explicit derivation of the gradient update for the eigenvectors, utilizing the Stiefel manifold formulation, and the recognition of the hidden variable as inducing a conditional DPP are particularly strong technical contributions.

The experiments conducted on a baby registry dataset provide compelling evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness. The comparison to projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) demonstrates that the EM approach achieves better test log-likelihoods, particularly in scenarios where negative interactions between products are prevalent. The qualitative example provided, showing how the EM-learned DPP leads to more sensible product recommendations compared to K-Ascent, further underscores the benefit of preserving diversity.  The runtime analysis also confirms the asymptotic and empirical speed advantages of the EM method.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper is rigorously developed, with clear mathematical derivations and a well-justified algorithmic approach.
*   **Originality:**  The EM-based learning algorithm for full kernel matrix *L* without restrictive parameterizations is a novel contribution.
*   **Significance:** The ability to learn DPPs that effectively balance quality and diversity has significant potential applications in various fields, including recommendation systems, document summarization, and sensor placement.
*   **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the technical details accessible to readers familiar with DPPs.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the results on the baby registry dataset are promising, expanding the experimental evaluation to more diverse real-world datasets would strengthen the claims.  Specifically, exploring datasets with different characteristics – such as varying levels of diversity and sparsity – could provide a more comprehensive assessment of the algorithm's robustness.
*   **Discussion of Limitations:** The conclusion feels somewhat rushed and could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed method. For example, the scalability to very high-dimensional datasets could be further addressed.
*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** While the paper compares two initialization strategies, exploring the sensitivity of the EM algorithm to different initialization schemes, and potentially developing a more robust initialization procedure, could be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   This paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in probabilistic modeling.
*   The EM algorithm effectively addresses the limitations of previous methods for learning DPPs, enabling the learning of full kernel matrices without restrictive assumptions.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm in preserving both quality and diversity.
*   The clear writing and detailed derivations make the paper accessible and valuable to the research community.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, with a broader range of datasets.
*   The discussion of limitations is somewhat brief.




**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper.  The contribution is significant, the technical details are rigorous, and the experimental results are encouraging. While expanding the experimental evaluation and providing a more thorough discussion of limitations would further strengthen the paper, the current version represents a valuable addition to the literature on DPPs and machine learning.  The proposed EM algorithm offers a promising approach to learning DPPs that effectively balances quality and diversity, and has the potential to impact a wide range of applications.
