- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn full DPP kernels using eigendecomposition, Stiefel manifold optimization, and a Jensen-based log-likelihood lower bound.  
- Tackles non-convexity of DPP log-likelihood and avoids degeneracies seen in projected gradient/K-Ascent.  
- Reparameterizes the kernel via eigenvalues and eigenvectors to preserve positive semi-definiteness during optimization.  
- Use of eigendecomposition and Stiefel manifold optimization is innovative and technically well-justified.  
- Empirical results show the EM method outperforms K-Ascent in test log-likelihood, robustness to initialization, and runtime.  
- Demonstrates up to 16.5% relative gains in data-scarce scenarios, highlighting practical value for applications like product recommendation.  
- Paper has major clarity issues: key terms (e.g., "marginal kernel," "V," "weight") are undefined and notation is dense/inconsistent.  
- Structure is disorganized with critical derivations and details buried in supplementary material.  
- Heavy reliance on external references for key derivations reduces the paper’s self-contained clarity.  
- Evaluation is limited: only compared to K-Ascent and omits alternative parametric, Bayesian, and other subset selection baselines.  
- Pro-accept: novel, addresses a hard problem with clear empirical gains and practical significance.  
- Con-accept: poor writing, unclear notation, and insufficient comparative evaluation weaken the submission.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally pending major revisions to improve writing, define terms, and broaden experimental comparisons.
