This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound. The method appears well-founded, original, and a meaningful contribution to subset selection literature.

The authors provide a clear introduction to DPPs, their applications, and the challenges associated with learning the kernel matrix. They also present a thorough review of previous work on DPPs and subset selection, highlighting the limitations of existing methods. The proposed EM algorithm is well-motivated, and the authors provide a detailed derivation of the algorithm, including the E-step and M-step updates.

The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed EM algorithm compared to a simple K-Ascent baseline. The results show that the EM algorithm achieves higher test log-likelihoods and is more robust to initialization and dataset changes. The authors also provide an analysis of the runtime complexity of the EM algorithm, which is asymptotically faster than the K-Ascent method.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The experiments are limited in scope, and the authors do not provide comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods mentioned in the introduction. This makes it unclear what the practical benefits of the DPP approach are. Additionally, the write-up is sometimes sloppy and hard to follow, with notation that is occasionally undefined or introduced after use.

The paper structure also interleaves method derivation with side remarks and alternatives, making the main thread convoluted. It would be beneficial to present the method clearly first and then discuss the alternatives and side remarks. Specific notation issues, such as the marginal kernel K and variable V being used before definition, should be addressed.

There are also some language and phrasing issues, such as "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" being unclear and should state "closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q". The section referencing external work ([26]) is hard to follow, and the authors should outline the simpler approach directly rather than forcing readers to look up the original.

Ambiguous terms, such as what "weight" refers to in p.4 line 189, need clarification. The rationale in equation (30) is also questionable, and the authors should explain why m_{ij} is not used and why not initialize with the empirical covariance matrix.

Overall, the paper is technically solid and original, but it needs clearer exposition and substantially stronger experimental comparisons.
