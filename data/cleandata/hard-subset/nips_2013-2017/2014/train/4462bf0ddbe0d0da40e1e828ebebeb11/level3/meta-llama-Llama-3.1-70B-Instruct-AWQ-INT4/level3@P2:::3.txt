This paper proposes a novel Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), a probabilistic model that assigns higher probability to diverse sets. The EM algorithm is designed to learn the full kernel matrix of a DPP, which is a challenging task due to the non-convexity of the log-likelihood objective.

The paper starts by introducing the background on DPPs, including their definition, properties, and applications. It then reviews previous work on learning DPPs, which has focused on partial learning of the kernel matrix or restrictive parametric forms. The authors argue that these approaches sacrifice the diversity dimension of DPPs, which is a key aspect of their modeling power.

The proposed EM algorithm is based on a change of variables from kernel entries to eigenvalues and eigenvectors. This allows the authors to develop an optimization procedure that avoids the problematic projection step required by naive gradient ascent. The EM algorithm is shown to be asymptotically faster than gradient ascent and more robust to initialization and dataset changes.

The paper provides a clear and insightful derivation of the EM procedure, including the use of Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the objective. The authors also provide a solid technical contribution by mapping the constraint on the eigenvectors to optimization on the Stiefel manifold.

The experimental evaluation of the EM algorithm is thorough and well-designed. The authors compare their method to a baseline approach, K-Ascent (KA), on a product recommendation task. The results show that the EM algorithm retains both quality and diversity better than KA, with significant gains in test log-likelihood. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the runtime of the two algorithms, showing that the EM algorithm is faster than KA.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs are coherent and easy to follow. However, the conclusion is somewhat hasty and omits some contributions. For example, the authors could have discussed the potential applications of their method in other domains, such as document summarization or sensor placement.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. The proposed EM algorithm is a valuable addition to the toolkit of researchers and practitioners working with DPPs. However, more experiments on real-world datasets are recommended to better illustrate the practical importance of the produced diversity.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the EM algorithm is well-derived. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are significant. (9/10)
