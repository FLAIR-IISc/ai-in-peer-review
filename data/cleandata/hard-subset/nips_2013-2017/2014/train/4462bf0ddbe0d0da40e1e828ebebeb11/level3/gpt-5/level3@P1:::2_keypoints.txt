- Proposes an EM algorithm for maximum-likelihood learning of full DPP kernels via eigendecomposition, using latent active-eigenvector set J and a Jensen lower bound.
- E-step sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), a k-DPP conditioned on |J|=|Y|, enabling polynomial-time marginal computations.
- M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and a gradient-like multiplicative update for eigenvectors on the Stiefel manifold.
- Method avoids projection pathologies of naive projected-gradient on K and has asymptotic per-iteration complexity O(n N k^2).
- Experiments on Amazon baby registries show up to 16.5% test log-likelihood gains over the KA baseline and ~2.1× speedups, with larger gains where off-diagonal structure matters.
- EM construction is principled and leverages known DPP mixture/eigendecomposition properties.
- Convergence guarantees are incomplete: single-gradient M-steps, step-size selection by re-running E-step, and potential loss monotonicity are not fully established.
- Numerical stability concerns when eigenvalues approach 1 (R = diag(√λ/(1−λ))) need discussion and safeguards.
- KA baseline is weak; stronger baselines (manifold optimization, factorized K=BB⊤, Riemannian trust-region/proximal methods) should be compared.
- Experimental evaluation is narrow (single domain) and relies only on test log-likelihood, limiting practical conclusions.
- Additional evaluation metrics (precision/recall/NDCG@k, intra-list similarity, coverage/diversity) and MAP/set-quality measures are needed.
- Recommend synthetic recovery experiments to test identifiability, robustness to initialization, and sensitivity to k and N.
- Compare against alternative subset-selection methods (submodular facility-location/diversity, mixture-of-DPPs, quality-only learning) to contextualize benefits.
- Paper organization is cluttered; derivation interleaved with side remarks obscures the main thread.
- Notation issues: several symbols (e.g., K, V, U, R, ZYi) used before definition and inconsistent subscripts/superscripts; state k = max|Yi|.
- Language needs tightening and clearer phrases (e.g., specify “closest PSD in Frobenius norm” instead of vague wording).
- Stiefel manifold update references are terse; provide a self-contained derivation, define matrix exponential, and detail computation/cost of skew-symmetric direction V⊤G−G⊤V.
- Initialization by moment matching (Eq. 23) needs rationale, discussion of sign ambiguity for Kij, and why empirical covariance isn’t used.
- Contribution is novel in applying EM over elementary DPPs for full-kernel learning and using Stiefel optimization, but incremental relative to known DPP properties.
- Potentially significant across tasks if robust/scalable, but current empirical scope limits demonstrated impact.
- Pros: principled EM, avoids PSD projections, asymptotically faster for small k, empirical evidence of robustness, and practical eigenvalue-update details.
- Cons: unclear convergence/monotonicity under inexact M-steps, narrow experiments and weak baselines, sloppy exposition/notation, and unclear initialization rationale.
- Suggestions: provide a convergence proposition or state limitations, add stronger baselines and broader evaluations, include ablations and sensitivity analyses, and improve notation/structure and Stiefel-update description.
- Recommendation: borderline accept — original and interesting but needs clearer exposition and substantially stronger experiments to establish significance.
