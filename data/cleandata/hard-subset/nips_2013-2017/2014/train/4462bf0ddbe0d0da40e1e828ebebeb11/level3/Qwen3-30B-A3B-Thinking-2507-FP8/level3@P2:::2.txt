

**Review for "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes"**

**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

This paper addresses the challenge of learning the full kernel matrix for Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) from example subsets. While DPPs are well-suited for modeling subset diversity (e.g., in product recommendation, document summarization), learning their kernel matrix K has been limited by the non-convexity of the log-likelihood objective, which is conjectured to be NP-hard. Previous work has focused on restricted learning settings: learning scalar weights for each row of L [2], or learning weights for linear combinations of DPPs with fixed kernels [3]. The authors propose a novel EM-based algorithm that reparameterizes K using its eigendecomposition, enabling a lower-bound optimization that avoids the problematic projection steps required by gradient ascent methods. This approach differs from Bayesian methods [8] by not assuming parametric forms for the kernel.

**Quality**

The paper presents a technically sound method with a clear theoretical foundation. The derivation of the EM algorithm using Jensen's inequality and the lower-bound on the likelihood is well-executed, and the authors provide a thorough computational complexity analysis showing the EM method's asymptotic advantage over K-Ascent. The proof that the EM method avoids degenerate solutions (near-diagonal kernels) is convincing and addresses a key limitation of prior approaches. However, the paper lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence properties of the EM algorithm, which would strengthen the technical contribution.

**Clarity**

The paper suffers from significant clarity issues that hinder understanding of the method. Key notations (e.g., marginal kernel K, eigenvector matrix V) are introduced after their first use, creating confusion. The writing is convoluted, with method derivation interleaved with side remarks and alternative approaches. For example, the Stiefel manifold optimization in Section 3.5 references [26] but fails to outline the simplified approach directly, forcing readers to consult external material. The phrase "minimises the change to Frobenius norm" (Algorithm 1) is unclear and should specify "closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q." The ambiguous term "weight" in line 189 of the paper also requires clarification. The paper would benefit from a more structured presentation: first introducing the method clearly, then detailing the derivation.

**Originality**

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to DPP learning by proposing a novel algorithm for full kernel learning without restrictive assumptions. While prior work has explored convex learning of row weights [2] or ensemble approaches [3], this method uniquely leverages eigendecomposition and EM optimization. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from previous work, though they could strengthen this by more thoroughly reviewing related DPP learning techniques in recent NIPS proceedings (e.g., [11, 13, 14, 16, 19, 20]). The combination of eigendecomposition, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and Jensen's inequality to develop the EM algorithm represents a novel contribution to the subset selection literature.

**Significance**

The significance is limited by the scope of the experiments. The paper demonstrates up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent on a product recommendation task, but fails to compare to alternative subset selection methods (e.g., greedy approaches, other probabilistic models) mentioned in the introduction. Without these comparisons, it's unclear whether the improved DPP model translates to better practical outcomes. The authors also questionably initialize with empirical covariance (equation 30) without justification, as using the empirical covariance matrix would be a more natural starting point. The paper would be more significant if it demonstrated how the improved DPP model leads to better recommendation quality metrics rather than just log-likelihood.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Technically sound method that avoids degenerate solutions in DPP learning
- Clear theoretical justification and computational complexity analysis
- Original approach to full kernel learning without restrictive assumptions
- Demonstrated asymptotic runtime advantage over baseline

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparison to alternative subset selection methods
- Significant clarity issues hinder understanding of the method
- Ambiguous notations and unclear phrasing throughout the paper
- Limited demonstration of practical significance (log-likelihood gains ≠ better recommendations)
- Questionable initialization rationale in equation 30

**Overall Recommendation**

This paper presents a technically solid and original contribution to DPP learning that addresses a known limitation in the field. The EM-based approach is well-motivated and shows promise in the experiments. However, the paper's clarity issues significantly undermine its impact, and the experimental evaluation is insufficient to demonstrate the practical significance of the method. The authors should substantially improve the exposition (defining key notations before use, clarifying ambiguous terms), provide more comprehensive experimental comparisons to alternative subset selection methods, and justify their initialization choices. With these revisions, the paper would be a strong contribution to NIPS. As it stands, the clarity issues and limited experimental scope make it difficult to fully assess the significance of the work.

**Recommendation:** Weak Accept (with major revisions)
