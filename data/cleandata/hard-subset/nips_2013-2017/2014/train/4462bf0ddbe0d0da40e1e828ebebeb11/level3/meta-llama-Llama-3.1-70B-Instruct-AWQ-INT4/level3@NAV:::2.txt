This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound. The method appears well-founded, original, and a meaningful contribution to subset selection literature. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to DPPs, their applications, and the challenges associated with learning them.

The technical soundness of the paper is evident in the thorough derivation of the EM algorithm and its components, including the E-step and M-step updates. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the Stiefel-manifold optimization technique used to update the eigenvectors. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing favorable behavior compared to a simple K-Ascent baseline.

However, the paper has some limitations. The experiments are limited in scope, and the authors do not provide comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods mentioned in the introduction. This makes it unclear what the practical benefits of the DPP approach are. Additionally, the write-up is sometimes sloppy and hard to follow, with notation that is occasionally undefined or introduced after use. The paper structure also interleaves method derivation with side remarks and alternatives, making the main thread convoluted.

Specifically, the notation issues include the use of marginal kernel K and variable V before their definition. Language and phrasing issues, such as "minimises the change to Frobenius norm," are also present and should be clarified. The section referencing external work ([26]) is hard to follow, and the authors should outline the simpler approach directly rather than forcing readers to look up the original. Ambiguous terms, such as "weight" in p.4 line 189, need clarification. The rationale behind equation (30) is also questionable, and the authors should explain why m_{ij} is not used and why the empirical covariance matrix is not used for initialization.

In conclusion, while the paper makes a technically solid and original contribution to the field, it needs clearer exposition and substantially stronger experimental comparisons to demonstrate its practical benefits.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning DPPs, which is a meaningful contribution to subset selection literature.
* The technical soundness of the paper is evident in the thorough derivation of the EM algorithm and its components.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The experiments are limited in scope, and the authors do not provide comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods.
* The write-up is sometimes sloppy and hard to follow, with notation that is occasionally undefined or introduced after use.
* The paper structure is convoluted, and the authors should present the method clearly first.
* Specific notation issues, language and phrasing issues, and ambiguous terms need clarification.
* The rationale behind equation (30) is questionable, and the authors should explain their choices.
