- Introduces a novel EM algorithm to learn full DPP kernel L without restrictive parameterizations, tackling the NP-hard log-likelihood maximization.  
- Uses eigendecomposition and Jensen’s inequality to formulate an EM procedure that avoids projection steps of gradient ascent.  
- Builds on established DPP literature (Kulesza & Taskar) and relates to applications like sensor placement and document summarization.  
- Technical derivation is sound and clearly explained, including mapping constraints to the Stiefel manifold.  
- Novel contribution: eigenvalue/eigenvector parameterization enabling full kernel learning.  
- Empirical results (synthetic and product recommendation) show improved quality and diversity over K-Ascent (KA).  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear mathematical notation.  
- Demonstrates solid technical achievements (inverse distribution is a DPP, manifold optimization).  
- Conclusion is brief and underemphasizes the significance of avoiding projection steps.  
- Evaluation uses a limited set of real-world datasets, weakening generalizability claims.  
- KA baseline implementation is somewhat constrained (replacement strategy limited), potentially biasing comparisons.  
- Details on the step size search during eigenvector updates are sparse and need clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important challenge, offers clear empirical and theoretical advantages, and is well-explained.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited datasets and constrained baseline raise generalizability concerns; conclusion could be stronger.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and technically solid work; recommendation to accept (NIPS 2014).
