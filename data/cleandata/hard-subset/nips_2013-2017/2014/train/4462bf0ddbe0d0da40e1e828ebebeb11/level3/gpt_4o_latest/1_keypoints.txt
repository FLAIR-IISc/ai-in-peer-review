- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning full kernel matrices in DPPs by alternating a k-DPP variational E-step with gradient updates on eigenvalues/eigenvectors in the M-step.
- Method outperforms standard K-Ascent (KA), especially with naive initialization, and is robust in data-poor settings.
- Validated on a real-world product recommendation task with up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood.
- Novelty: advances beyond prior restrictive or parametric DPP learning methods by learning full kernels via EM.
- Empirical performance: shows substantial gains over KA and practical runtime improvements; asymptotically faster.
- Theoretical insights: provides detailed EM derivation, efficient eigenvalue/eigenvector updates, and complexity discussion.
- Practical relevance: useful for recommendation tasks where diversity and negative interactions matter.
- M-step complexity: requires Stiefel manifold updates and careful eigenvalue constraint handling, making implementation complex.
- Initialization analysis is limited; impact of initializing EM with KA or other strategies is not thoroughly explored.
- Metric interpretability: reliance on relative log-likelihood may be hard to interpret; other metrics (precision/recall) could strengthen evaluation.
- Writing/style issues: occasional self-praise and unnecessary formatting detract from professionalism.
- Acceptance pros: addresses a hard problem with a well-motivated, novel method and strong empirical/theoretical support.
- Acceptance cons: M-step complexity and insufficient analysis of initialization and evaluation metrics need refinement.
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted concerns.
