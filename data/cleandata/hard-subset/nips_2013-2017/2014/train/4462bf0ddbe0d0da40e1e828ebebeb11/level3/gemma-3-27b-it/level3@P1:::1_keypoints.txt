- Problem: Learning the kernel matrix for DPPs, a hard non-convex task critical for diverse subset selection.
- Core contribution: Novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes the kernel via eigenvalues/eigenvectors to avoid direct non-convex optimization of L.
- Key insight: Uses the connection between DPPs and mixtures of elementary DPPs to derive an effective optimization procedure.
- Empirical result: EM outperforms projected gradient baseline (KA) on a product recommendation (baby registry) dataset, especially with naive initialization.
- Literature context: Builds on extensive DPP work and clearly identifies prior limitations (restricted learning or loss of full DPP structure).
- Strength — Technical soundness: EM derivation is well-motivated, theoretically reasonable, and uses appropriate variational/Jensen techniques.
- Strength — Novelty: Reformulating learning via eigendecomposition and applying EM offers a new perspective and research direction.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Demonstrates practical benefit over KA, though gains are modest.
- Strength — Transparency: Authors acknowledge M-step limitations and sensitivity to initialization.
- Weakness — M-step complexity: M-step requires approximations and Stiefel manifold updates, adding implementation and conceptual complexity.
- Weakness — Limited experimentation: Evaluation is narrow and does not test initializing EM from KA solutions.
- Weakness — Evaluation metric: Using "relative log-likelihood" is awkward; absolute log-probability differences would be clearer.
- Weakness — Writing/style: Some stylistic choices (boldface, subjective claims like “elegant”) detract from professionalism.
- Acceptance arguments: Tackles an important problem, offers a significant technical contribution, shows empirical gains, and is honest about limitations.
- Rejection arguments: Practical applicability may be limited by M-step complexity, experiments are insufficiently comprehensive, and writing is uneven.
- Overall: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve writing, expand experiments (try EM initialized from KA), and report log-probability differences.
