- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing in eigenvalues/eigenvectors, enabling more robust optimization than projected gradient (K-Ascent).  
- Eigen-based parameterization avoids projection-induced degenerate near-diagonal kernels that fail to model diversity.  
- Technical approach is sound and the asymptotic runtime advantage over K-Ascent is plausible.  
- Experimental evaluation is severely limited: only one dataset (baby registry) and only compared to K-Ascent.  
- Omits comparisons to other subset-selection methods (greedy submodular maximization, other probabilistic diversity models), so practical benefits are unclear.  
- Paper has significant clarity issues: notation often introduced after use, imprecise phrasing, and a disorganized presentation that mixes derivation with side remarks.  
- Section 3.5 forces readers to consult external work [26] instead of explaining the simpler approach; terms like “weight” are ambiguous.  
- Rationale for equation (30) is questionable — e.g., why not initialize with the empirical covariance?  
- Original contribution: novel use of eigendecomposition, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and Stiefel-manifold optimization within an EM framework.  
- Needs to better distinguish itself from Affandi et al. [8], which also learns unconstrained DPP kernels via a different (Bayesian) approach.  
- Significance is limited by narrow experiments and lack of comparisons to established alternatives, weakening claims about modeling negative interactions.  
- Overall: technically solid and original but requires clearer exposition and substantially stronger experiments; recommendation: Weak Accept.
