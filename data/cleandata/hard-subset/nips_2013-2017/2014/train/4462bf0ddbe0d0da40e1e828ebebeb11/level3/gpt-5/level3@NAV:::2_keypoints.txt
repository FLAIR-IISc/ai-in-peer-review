- Proposal: EM algorithm for learning full-kernel DPPs via eigendecomposition, latent index set (mixture of elementary DPPs), Jensen lower bound, alternating closed-form eigenvalue updates (k-DPP marginals) and Stiefel-manifold eigenvector updates (matrix-exponential step).

- Novelty: High — EM formulation with k-DPP E-step and Stiefel updates is a novel contribution.

- Technical soundness: Method is well-founded; mixture interpretation, k-DPP E-step, and manifold update connect cleanly to existing DPP and optimization theory.

- Advantage over prior work: Directly tackles nonconvex full-kernel learning and avoids fragile PSD projections used by K-Ascent.

- Empirical results: Shows up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood on a product recommendation dataset and runtime advantages over a projected gradient baseline.

- Empirical limitations: Evaluation is narrow (single application domain) with limited baselines and no comparison to non-DPP subset-selection methods.

- Missing comparisons: Does not compare against facility-location/coverage submodular models or learned submodular mixtures cited in the paper.

- Methodological questions: Some choices lack justification (e.g., omission of m_ij in Eq. (30); why not initialize with empirical covariance).

- Clarity issues: Derivation is hard to follow due to interleaved remarks and alternatives; key symbols/terms (marginal kernel K, eigenvector matrix V, “weight”) are defined late or ambiguously.

- Writing improvements recommended: Present core EM derivation first, then variants/runtime; define K and V upfront; state the specific Stiefel update rather than deferring to [26]; use more precise language (e.g., “closest (in Frobenius norm) PSD matrix to Q”).

- Suggested additional experiments/analyses: Add comparisons to strong submodular/diversity baselines, ablations (eigenvalue vs eigenvector updates), sensitivity to initialization, more domains, and MAP-quality metrics.

- Pros for acceptance: Technically solid and elegant formulation; addresses an important open problem with principled updates.

- Cons for acceptance: Clarity and notation problems, missing non-DPP comparisons, limited empirical scope, and some unmotivated methodological choices.

- Overall: Promising and original paper that should be revised to improve exposition and substantially strengthen experimental comparisons.
