- Proposes an EM algorithm for learning DPP kernel matrices by reparameterizing via eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Addresses non-convexity of DPP maximum likelihood by constructing a lower bound and applying EM.  
- Builds on extensive prior DPP literature and correctly positions contribution against restrictive prior approaches.  
- Clever use of eigendecomposition and connection to elementary DPPs underpins the method.  
- The EM formulation is well-motivated and theoretical derivations appear sound.  
- Empirical results on a baby registry dataset show EM consistently outperforms a projected gradient ascent baseline (KA).  
- Runtime analysis indicates EM can be significantly faster than KA in practice.  
- Code and data are provided, improving reproducibility.  
- M-step is complex and relies on approximations, single gradient steps, and Stiefel manifold updates, which weakens claimed elegance.  
- Discussion of M-step approximations is brief and feels cursory.  
- Paper lacks analysis of initialization strategies, particularly initializing EM from KA solutions.  
- Use of "relative log-likelihood" as the main metric reduces interpretability; absolute log-probability differences are preferred.  
- Minor stylistic issues (overly enthusiastic wording and unnecessary bolding) reduce professionalism.  
- Overall assessment: solid, promising contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
