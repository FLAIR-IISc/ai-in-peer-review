- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning the full kernel matrix of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP).  
- Re-parameterizes the kernel by eigenvalues and eigenvectors to enable tractable optimization.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a likelihood lower bound and formulate the EM updates.  
- Avoids problematic projection steps of naive gradient methods, preventing degenerate diversity failures.  
- Shows the E-step conditional distribution is itself a DPP, simplifying inference.  
- Exploits the Stiefel manifold for principled eigenvector updates.  
- Provides detailed, rigorous derivations and clear theoretical exposition (Section 3).  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible with consistent notation.  
- Experimental results on a product recommendation (baby registry) task show improved recommendation quality and runtime versus projected gradient ascent.  
- Limited experimental evaluation: only one dataset studied, reducing evidence of generalizability.  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies and sensitivity analysis (e.g., initialization effects).  
- Conclusion is rushed and should better emphasize key technical insights (conditional distribution structure, Stiefel manifold usage).  
- Needs more thorough analysis of computational scaling with number of items and dataset size.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, practically relevant, recommend acceptance with minor revisions; reviewer rates as Strong Accept.
