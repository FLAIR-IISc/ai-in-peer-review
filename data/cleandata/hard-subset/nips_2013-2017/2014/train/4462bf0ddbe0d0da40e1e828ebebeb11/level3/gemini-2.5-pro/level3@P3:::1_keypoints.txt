- Reformulates full DPP kernel learning as an EM problem by treating the kernel eigendecomposition’s subset of eigenvectors as a latent variable.  
- E-step yields a tractable k-DPP posterior over latent subsets; M-step updates eigenvalues and eigenvectors to maximize expected log-likelihood.  
- Presents a novel, principled alternative to direct gradient-based kernel learning for DPPs.  
- Theoretical development is sound and leverages the representation of a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs.  
- Empirical results on a baby-registry dataset show EM often outperforms projected gradient ascent (KA), especially with poor (Wishart) initialization and in data-poor settings.  
- M-step is practically complex and somewhat ad-hoc: full eigenvector optimization is intractable, authors use a single Stiefel-manifold gradient step, re-run E-step for step-size, and handle eigenvalues near 1 specially.  
- Missing critical experiment: initialize EM from KA’s converged solution to test whether EM improves upon KA beyond better robustness to poor starts.  
- Evaluation metric “relative test log-likelihood” is non-standard and hard to interpret; reporting differences in average log-likelihood would be clearer (e.g., log(2) interpretation).  
- Minor writing/style issues: self-descriptive “elegant” claims and jarring bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, novel, empirically promising work recommended for acceptance despite M-step and evaluation shortcomings.
