- Tackles learning the full, unconstrained DPP kernel by re-parameterizing into eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introducing a latent eigenvector-selection variable for an EM algorithm.  
- Original and significant: first practical algorithm to learn the full kernel, enabling joint, data-driven modeling of item quality and item-to-item similarity.  
- Re-parameterization + EM is an innovative way to circumvent the non-convexity of maximum-likelihood DPP learning.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable EM lower bound; derivation is clear and principled.  
- Eigenvector updates are handled via optimization on the Stiefel manifold rather than naive projection, preserving learned structure.  
- Proves the posterior q(J|Yi) is a conditional DPP (k-DPP), enabling efficient E-step computations.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, background, and a step-by-step EM derivation.  
- Empirically, EM outperforms projected gradient ascent (“K-Ascent”) in robustness to initialization and data-poor settings and achieves better test log-likelihoods.  
- Qualitative baby-registry example shows EM yields more diverse, practical item sets compared to the baseline.  
- EM is shown to be asymptotically and empirically faster than K-Ascent.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is narrow (primarily product recommendation); lacks validation on other DPP applications like summarization or image search.  
- Weakness: conclusion is brief and could better summarize technical innovations and future directions.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, and empirically validated contribution that meaningfully advances DPP learning and is suitable for publication.
