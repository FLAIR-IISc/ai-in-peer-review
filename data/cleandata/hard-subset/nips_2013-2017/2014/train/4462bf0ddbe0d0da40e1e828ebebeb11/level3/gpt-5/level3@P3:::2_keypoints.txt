- Proposes an EM algorithm for ML learning of DPPs by parameterizing K via its eigendecomposition and treating eigen-index set J as a latent variable (mixture of elementary DPPs).  
- E-step: sets q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y), shown to be a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel enabling efficient marginals.  
- M-step: closed-form updates for eigenvalues via k-DPP marginals and a gradient update for eigenvectors with orthogonality enforced via a Stiefel-manifold step.  
- Avoids degeneracies of naive projected gradient ascent on K (“K-Ascent”), which can push solutions toward near-diagonal kernels.  
- Empirically improves test log-likelihood over K-Ascent on a product recommendation dataset and is typically faster asymptotically and in practice.  
- Tackles full-kernel learning without restrictive parameterizations, unlike prior work that learned only per-item weights or mixtures of fixed DPPs.  
- EM derivation, E-step k-DPP characterization, eigenvalue updates, and Stiefel update are technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Complexity analysis clarifies practical gains over K-Ascent when typical set sizes k are small.  
- Experiments show favorable accuracy, runtime, and qualitative avoidance of projection pathologies but are limited to one domain and one baseline.  
- Methodological gap: eigenvector M-step uses only a single gradient step per outer iteration and requires re-running the E-step for likelihood checks; convergence behavior needs clearer discussion.  
- Paper is hard to follow: main derivation is interleaved with side remarks and alternatives, hurting narrative clarity.  
- Notation issues: key variables (K, V, eigendecomposition) sometimes introduced after use and some symbols (V, U, B_Y) lack initial definitions.  
- Phrasing unclear in places (e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm”); projection step should be stated precisely.  
- Stiefel-manifold update defers to a citation; the specific retraction/exp map, neglect of Hessian terms, and the actual implemented update should be described in-text.  
- Ambiguity: the term “weight” is used unclearly (mixture weight vs per-item quality weight) and should be clarified.  
- Initialization rationale (moment-matching Eq.30) is underspecified and needs justification and discussion of alternatives like empirical covariance + projection.  
- Originality: the combination of latent J, Jensen lower bound EM, and Stiefel optimization for full DPP learning is novel and meaningful.  
- Significance: provides a practical route to learning full kernels and avoids projection-induced degeneracies, but broader empirical comparisons are needed to demonstrate practical superiority.  
- Experimental strengths: careful runtime analysis, robustness to initialization, and consistent gains over the natural baseline.  
- Experimental limitations: single application and baseline; missing comparisons to submodular/diversity baselines, prior DPP learning approaches, and ablations (eigenvalues-only vs full EM).  
- Pros: well-founded EM formulation, addresses full-kernel learning, avoids K-Ascent pitfalls, and shows runtime benefits.  
- Cons: sloppy exposition/notation, narrow experimental scope, limited discussion of convergence for approximate M-step, and underspecified initialization.  
- Recommendation: reorganize method section and notation, provide self-contained Stiefel update, clarify ambiguous language and initialization, and substantially strengthen experiments (additional domains, stronger baselines, ablations, and extra metrics).  
- Overall: technically solid and original contribution that requires clearer exposition and broader empirical validation to be a strong conference paper.
