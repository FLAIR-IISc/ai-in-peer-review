This paper proposes a novel expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm for learning the kernel matrix of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP). DPPs are attractive models for subset selection, balancing quality and diversity, but learning their kernel parameters is known to be computationally challenging. Previous work has focused on restricted learning settings, such as learning scalar weights or fixed kernel combinations. This work tackles the full kernel matrix learning problem without restrictive parameterizations, offering a significant contribution to the field.

The core idea is to re-parameterize the kernel in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors, allowing for a more tractable optimization procedure. By leveraging Jensen’s inequality, the authors derive a lower bound on the likelihood and formulate an EM algorithm that avoids the problematic projection steps present in naive gradient ascent approaches, which can lead to degenerate solutions that fail to model item diversity. The paper provides a detailed derivation of the EM updates, including a clever exploitation of the Stiefel manifold for eigenvector updates and demonstrating that the conditional distribution in the E-step is itself a DPP. 

The strengths of this paper are numerous. First, the technical contribution is substantial. The re-parameterization and resulting EM algorithm represent a significant advancement in DPP learning. The detailed derivations and insightful explanations in Section 3 demonstrate a deep understanding of the underlying theory. The connections to the Stiefel manifold and the recognition of the conditional distribution as a DPP are particularly strong technical merits. Second, the paper is well-written and clearly organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and situates the work within the existing literature. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are generally accessible. Third, the experimental evaluation, while focused on a single product recommendation task, provides convincing evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness. The comparison to projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) highlights the benefits of avoiding the projection step, and the analysis of the runtime shows a clear performance advantage for the EM method. The example in Figure 2a provides a concrete illustration of the improved quality of the recommendations.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. First, the experimental evaluation, while informative, is limited. While the authors demonstrate gains on the baby registry data, exploring additional real-world datasets would strengthen the conclusions and demonstrate the generalizability of the approach. More detailed ablation studies exploring the sensitivity to initialization would also be valuable. Second, the conclusion feels somewhat rushed and does not fully emphasize the key contributions. Specifically, the insights into the structure of the conditional distribution and the use of the Stiefel manifold deserve more attention in the concluding remarks. Third, while the paper acknowledges the computational complexity, a more thorough analysis of the scaling behavior with respect to the number of items and the size of the training dataset would be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed EM algorithm offers a novel solution to the challenging problem of full kernel learning for DPPs.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivations are rigorous and well-justified, and the algorithm builds upon a strong theoretical foundation.
*   **Practical Impact:** The experimental results demonstrate that the method outperforms existing approaches in a relevant application domain.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the technical contributions accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experimental Evaluation:** The lack of extensive experimentation on diverse datasets limits the generalizability of the findings.
*   **Rushed Conclusion:** The conclusion does not adequately highlight the significant technical contributions of the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of DPP learning. The proposed EM algorithm is technically sound, well-motivated, and experimentally validated. While the experimental evaluation could be more extensive, the existing results are convincing and demonstrate the practical benefits of the approach. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more thorough discussion of the experimental results and a more comprehensive conclusion that fully acknowledges the paper’s contributions. I rate this paper as **Strong Accept**.
