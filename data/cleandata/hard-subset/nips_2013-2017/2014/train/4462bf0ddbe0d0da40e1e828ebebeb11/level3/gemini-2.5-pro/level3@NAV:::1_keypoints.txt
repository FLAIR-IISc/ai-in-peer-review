- Addresses the hard non-convex problem of learning the full DPP kernel from data, beyond prior convex settings.  
- Main contribution: reformulates kernel learning as an EM problem by parameterizing the kernel via eigendecomposition and viewing a DPP as a mixture of elementary DPPs.  
- Algorithm: introduces a latent mixture variable, with an E-step updating a variational k-DPP posterior and an M-step doing gradient updates on eigenvalues/eigenvectors.  
- Primary strength is originality and a principled, creative approach to a long-standing DPP problem.  
- Empirical results: EM substantially outperforms projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) with naive initialization and is asymptotically faster.  
- With stronger moment-matching initialization, gains are more modest but EM remains competitive.  
- Concern: M-step complexity and required approximations (e.g., taking only a single gradient step for eigenvectors) reduce the claimed elegance.  
- Missing experiment: effect of initialization—e.g., initializing EM with the well-initialized KA solution to assess further improvement.  
- Metric issue: using "relative log likelihood" (ratios) is unconventional; recommend reporting log-probability differences for interpretability.  
- Minor stylistic issues: self-description of the algorithm as "elegant" and unnecessary bolding in the introduction.  
- Overall judgment: strong, valuable contribution likely to inspire follow-up work; recommend acceptance.
