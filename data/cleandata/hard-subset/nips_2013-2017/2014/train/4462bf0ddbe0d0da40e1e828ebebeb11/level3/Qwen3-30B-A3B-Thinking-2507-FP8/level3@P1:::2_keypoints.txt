- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel matrix, addressing prior restrictions due to NP-hardness of kernel learning.  
- Avoids problematic projection steps of gradient ascent by leveraging eigendecomposition of K, a Jensen lower bound, and Stiefel manifold optimization.  
- Directly learns an unconstrained, non-parametric kernel, differing from prior works that learn restricted or parametric forms.  
- Technically sound: introduces hidden variable J, derives closed-form eigenvalue updates and multiplicative eigenvector updates.  
- Solid theoretical foundation with appropriate use of Jensen’s inequality and careful handling of the Stiefel manifold constraint.  
- Empirically outperforms K-Ascent on baby registry recommendations (up to 16.5% test log-likelihood gain) and is asymptotically faster.  
- Provides a convincing analysis (d metric) explaining EM’s advantage in settings with strong negative item interactions.  
- Major clarity issues: convoluted writing, interleaved derivations and remarks, and key notation (K, V, J) introduced unclearly or out of order.  
- Problematic phrasing and omissions (e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm” should be clearer), and reliance on [26] forces readers to consult external material.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: compares only to K-Ascent and omits other subset-selection baselines mentioned in the paper.  
- Questionable initialization ("moments-matching") rationale and unexplained choices (why not use m_ij directly, sqrt usage).  
- Dataset limitation: experiments only on baby registry recommendations, no tests on other DPP applications like summarization or sensor placement.  
- Significance tempered by limited comparative experiments and lack of demonstration of practical gains beyond log-likelihood.  
- Strengths summarised: novel EM approach, rigorous derivations, empirical advantage over baseline, and runtime benefit.  
- Weaknesses summarised: poor exposition, narrow experimental scope, missing baseline comparisons, and unjustified design choices.  
- Overall: technically solid and original, but impact is limited by presentation and insufficient broader validation.
