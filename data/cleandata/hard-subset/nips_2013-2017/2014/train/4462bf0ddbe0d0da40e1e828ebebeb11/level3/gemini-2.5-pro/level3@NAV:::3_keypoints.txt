- Addresses learning the full DPP kernel matrix L, a known NP-hard problem, by parameterizing it via eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Proposes a novel EM-style algorithm on the eigendecomposition that avoids projection steps that can collapse eigenvalues.  
- Method allows jointly learning item quality and similarity, moving beyond restricted parameterizations used previously.  
- Derivation is technically sound, using Jensen’s inequality to lower-bound the likelihood and framing eigenvector updates on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Shows the intermediate conditional distribution is itself a DPP, an important theoretical property.  
- Empirical results on a product recommendation task demonstrate better preservation of diversity than projected gradient ascent, especially with limited data or poor initialization.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and organized with sufficient technical detail.  
- Experimental evaluation is somewhat narrow and would be stronger with more, larger-scale datasets from other DPP applications (e.g., summarization, image search).  
- Conclusion is brief and undersells contributions; it should reiterate key technical insights like the auxiliary distribution properties.
