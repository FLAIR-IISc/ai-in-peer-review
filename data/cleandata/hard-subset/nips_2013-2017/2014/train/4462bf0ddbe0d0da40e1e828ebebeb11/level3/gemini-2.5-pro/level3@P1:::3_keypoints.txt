- Problem: Learning the full, unconstrained DPP kernel matrix from data is hard due to non-convex, conjectured NP-hard log-likelihood optimization.
- Main contribution: Proposes an EM algorithm that parameterizes the kernel by eigendecomposition and treats eigenvector selection as a hidden variable.
- Key insight: The posterior over active eigenvectors q(J | Yi) is a k-DPP, making the E-step tractable.
- Optimization advantage: EM avoids harmful projection steps of projected gradient ascent that can truncate eigenvalues and lose diversity.
- Eigenvalue update: Closed-form M-step for eigenvalues that satisfies constraints without projection.
- Eigenvector update: Formulated as optimization on the Stiefel manifold with a multiplicative update, respecting geometry.
- Originality/significance: First practical algorithm for learning the full non-parametric DPP kernel, enabling richer similarity learning.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear motivation, background, and derivation linking projected gradient failure to the EM solution.
- Experiments: On Amazon baby registry, EM is faster and more robust than K-Ascent, yielding higher test log-likelihood and qualitatively more diverse selections.
- Experimental limitation: Evaluation is limited to a single domain; additional real-world datasets (e.g., summarization or image search) would strengthen the claims.
- Conclusion: Conclusion is brief and should better highlight technical innovations and future directions (scaling, alternative optimizations).
- Baseline comparison: Would benefit from comparing to restricted/convex baselines (e.g., single-weight or mixture methods) to quantify gains from learning the full kernel.
