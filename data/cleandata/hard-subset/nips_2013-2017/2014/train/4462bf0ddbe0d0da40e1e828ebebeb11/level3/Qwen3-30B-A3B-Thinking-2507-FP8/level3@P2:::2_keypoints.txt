- Proposes an EM algorithm that reparameterizes the DPP kernel K via eigendecomposition to learn the full kernel from subset data, avoiding projection steps of gradient methods.
- Situates contribution against prior limited DPP learning approaches (row-weight learning, fixed-kernel mixtures) and contrasts with Bayesian methods that assume parametric kernels.
- Derivation uses Jensen’s inequality to obtain a likelihood lower bound; the EM formulation and complexity analysis are technically sound.
- Shows asymptotic computational advantage over K-Ascent and provides a proof that the EM method avoids degenerate near-diagonal kernels.
- Lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for the EM algorithm, weakening the theoretical contribution.
- Significant clarity issues: notations (e.g., K, V) introduced after use and derivation interleaved with side remarks, making the method hard to follow.
- Specific unclear phrases and omissions (e.g., “minimises the change to Frobenius norm,” ambiguous “weight” at line 189, insufficient outline of Stiefel manifold approach) need correction.
- Originality is strong: novel combination of eigendecomposition, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and Jensen’s inequality for full-kernel learning.
- Could strengthen novelty claim by more thoroughly reviewing recent related DPP learning work in NIPS proceedings.
- Experimental results show up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent on product recommendation.
- Experimental scope is limited: no comparisons to alternative subset selection methods (greedy or other probabilistic models) and no demonstration of improved recommendation metrics beyond log-likelihood.
- Initialization choice (empirical covariance in equation 30) is questionable and not justified.
- Recommends major revisions: improve exposition (define notation early, clarify ambiguous terms), expand experimental comparisons, and justify initialization.
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept (with major revisions).
