- Paper proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing K via its eigendecomposition and treating eigenvector selection as latent variables.  
- Uses Jensen’s inequality to derive a tractable lower bound and performs coordinate ascent: E-step for posterior over eigenvector selections, M-step with closed-form eigenvalue updates and Stiefel-manifold eigenvector optimization.  
- The derivation and algorithmic approach (latent representation + Stiefel optimization) are technically sound and well-founded.  
- The work is highly original — likely the first practical method for learning a full, unconstrained DPP kernel.  
- Potentially significant: enabling full-kernel DPP learning could impact recommendation, summarization, and other diversity-aware tasks.  
- Experimental validation is weak: comparison is only to a projected gradient baseline (K-Ascent) on one dataset, showing optimization advantages but not practical superiority over other methods.  
- Lacks comparisons to non-DPP subset-selection or recommendation baselines, making practical benefits unclear.  
- The moment-matching initialization (Eq. 23) is unjustified, especially the off-diagonal formula using sqrt(max(...,0)) instead of direct empirical pairwise frequencies.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor: organization and writing are confusing and disrupt the logical flow of the derivation.  
- Several variables and notations are used before definition (e.g., K and V), harming readability and self-containment.  
- Explanation of the eigenvector update is insufficient; referencing Stiefel-manifold methods without an intuitive derivation reduces accessibility.  
- Vague phrasing (e.g., about projection steps) should be made precise and explicit in the text.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a fundamental hard problem, proposes a novel technically sophisticated EM algorithm, and conceptually advances DPP learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor exposition, insufficient experiments, and undeclared methodological choices mean the paper is not ready for publication.  
- Recommended revision: substantially improve clarity and expand experimental comparisons to demonstrate practical utility.
