- Proposes a novel EM framework for learning DPP kernels as an alternative to direct gradient-based K-Ascent (KA).
- EM is based on representing any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and uses a variational k-DPP in the E-step with gradient updates in the M-step.
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms KA with naive KA initialization and shows modest gains with smarter KA initialization.
- The authors analyze runtime complexity and show EM is asymptotically faster than KA.
- The M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats and approximations, weakening the claimed elegance.
- The paper lacks analysis of initialization effects, e.g., starting EM from KA solutions.
- Use of "relative log likelihood" as a metric is questionable; reporting log-probability differences would be clearer.
- Some writing and style issues (overuse of "elegant", unnecessary bolding) detract from presentation.
- The approach is novel and provides a new perspective on kernel learning for DPPs.
- The method is promising and potentially useful for real applications like recommendation and summarization.
- Experiments show modest empirical gains overall and could be improved.
- The paper is generally well-written, organized, and balances theoretical and empirical results.
- Strengths: novel algorithm, thorough runtime and empirical analysis, clear explanation.
- Weaknesses: complex M-step, missing initialization study, questionable metric choice, some style problems.
