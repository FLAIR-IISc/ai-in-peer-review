- Problem: Learning the full DPP kernel L is NP-hard and prior work either learns only scalar/vector parameters or restricts L’s parametric form, hurting modeled diversity.  
- Method: Proposes a novel EM-based learning algorithm that parameterizes L via its eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Advantage: EM formulation avoids projection steps in gradient ascent that lead to degenerate, nearly-diagonal L matrices and loss of diversity.  
- Theory: EM derivation is principled (via Jensen’s inequality) and maps constraints to optimization on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Technical result: Shows the inverse distribution remains a DPP and provides clear, step-by-step optimization details and proofs.  
- Experiments: Synthetic and product-recommendation experiments show up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood over K-Ascent while better preserving diversity.  
- Efficiency: Method is asymptotically faster than gradient ascent.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear proofs and coherent technical exposition.  
- Strengths: Novel learning approach, solid theoretical contributions, and strong empirical validation with practical impact on diverse subset selection.  
- Weakness/omission: Conclusion is somewhat hasty and fails to highlight some contributions and distinctions from related Bayesian DPP learning work [8].  
- Overall: High-quality contribution that meets NIPS criteria—technically sound, well-presented, and significant for practical applications.
