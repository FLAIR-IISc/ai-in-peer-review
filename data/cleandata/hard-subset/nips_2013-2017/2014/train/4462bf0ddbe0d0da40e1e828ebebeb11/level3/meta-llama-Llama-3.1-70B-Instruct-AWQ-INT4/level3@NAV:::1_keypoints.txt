- Reformulates kernel learning for DPPs as an EM problem, offering an alternative to direct gradient-based learning.
- Represents any DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs and uses a variational distribution over subsets in EM-style optimization.
- E-step: updates the variational distribution as a k-DPP; M-step: uses single gradient steps to update kernel parameters.
- Empirically, EM substantially outperforms naive kernel ascent with naive initialization, and gives modest gains with smarter initialization.
- EM is more robust to initialization and dataset changes and is asymptotically faster than the KA method.
- Paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the EM algorithm and its advantages.
- Experimental results are convincing and include a thorough analysis of results.
- M-step is fairly involved and requires caveats/approximations, which weakens the claimed elegance.
- Paper lacks analysis of initializing EM from KA solutions and other initialization effects.
- Use of “relative log likelihood” as the main metric is questionable; reporting log-probability differences would be clearer.
- Minor writing issues: avoid bolding in the introduction and refrain from labeling the algorithm as “elegant.”
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, empirical superiority, robustness, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: complex/approximate M-step, missing initialization analysis, and metric interpretation concerns.
