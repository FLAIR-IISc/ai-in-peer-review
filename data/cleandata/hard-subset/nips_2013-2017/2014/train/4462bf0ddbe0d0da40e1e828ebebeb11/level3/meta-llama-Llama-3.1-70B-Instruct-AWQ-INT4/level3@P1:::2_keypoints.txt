- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs using eigendecomposition, Stiefel-manifold optimization, mixtures of elementary DPPs, and a Jensen lower bound.  
- Targets the hard non-convex problem of learning DPP kernel matrices and claims improved robustness/efficiency over existing methods.  
- Builds on prior DPP work (eigendecomposition for sampling/marginalization) but does not comprehensively compare to other subset-selection methods (e.g., submodular approaches).  
- Strength: EM algorithm is original, well-founded, and thoroughly derived.  
- Strength: Experiments show favorable behavior versus a simple K-Ascent baseline.  
- Strength: Detailed mathematical treatment of eigendecomposition, Stiefel optimization, and Jensen bound.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited in scope and lack comparisons to alternative subset-selection baselines.  
- Weakness: Write-up is sloppy and hard to follow; notation is sometimes undefined or introduced after use.  
- Weakness: Paper structure interleaves derivation with side remarks and alternatives, making the main thread convoluted.  
- Weakness: Specific notation issues (e.g., marginal kernel K and variable V used before being defined) and unclear language/phrasing.  
- Recommendation: Provide a clearer, more concise write-up with well-defined notation and clearer phrasing.  
- Recommendation: Reorganize the presentation to separate main derivation from side remarks and alternatives.  
- Recommendation: Include more comprehensive experimental comparisons to alternative subset-selection methods.  
- Overall: Technically solid and significant contribution but needs substantially clearer exposition and stronger experimental validation.
