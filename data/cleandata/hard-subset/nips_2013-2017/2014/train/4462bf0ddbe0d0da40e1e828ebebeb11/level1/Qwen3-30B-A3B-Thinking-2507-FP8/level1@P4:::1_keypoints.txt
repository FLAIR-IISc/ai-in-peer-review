- Problem: Learning full DPP kernel by maximizing log-likelihood is difficult; prior work used restrictive parameterizations due to conjectured NP-hardness.  
- Method: Proposes an EM algorithm that reparameterizes the kernel in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors, avoiding the projection step of gradient ascent.  
- Theory: Derives EM via Jensen’s inequality, provides strong theoretical justification, and explains why naive gradient ascent (with projection) fails to model negative interactions.  
- Baseline analysis: Shows K-Ascent baseline’s projection step limits modeling of negative item interactions; moments-matching initializer partially helps but is insufficient.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation on Amazon baby registry data with multiple initializations and medians from 25 trials; reports up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood.  
- Runtime: Demonstrates an asymptotic runtime advantage for EM (O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) vs. O(T1 n N^3 + T1 T2 N^3)).  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Exceptionally well-written, clear derivations, precise algorithms (reproducible by experts), and detailed experimental setup.  
- Originality: Presents a novel, nontrivial parameterization and optimization approach for DPP learning distinct from prior NIPS work.  
- Significance: Important advance for applications needing negative interaction modeling (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement) and likely practical adoption.  
- Strengths: Strong technical contribution, clear writing, comprehensive baseline comparison, and compelling empirical gains.  
- Weaknesses: Evaluation limited to a single real-world dataset and some initialization aspects warrant deeper exploration; limited comparison to other recent DPP methods.  
- Recommendation: Paper meets criteria for acceptance — technically sound, original, clear, significant, and recommended for NIPS.
