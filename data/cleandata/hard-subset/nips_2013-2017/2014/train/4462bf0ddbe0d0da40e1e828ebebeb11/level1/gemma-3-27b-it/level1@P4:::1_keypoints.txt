- Core contribution: Proposes an EM algorithm that learns a DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and optimizing a Jensen-based lower bound on the log-likelihood.
- Motivation/results: Addresses NP-hard direct likelihood maximization and shows improved likelihood and runtime versus projected gradient ascent, especially with strong item interactions.
- Presentation: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, with helpful DPP background and consistent notation.
- Technical approach: Eigendecomposition-based EM, gradient derivations, and Stiefel manifold updates are logically presented and mathematically sound.
- Novelty: Learning the full kernel matrix for DPPs (beyond restricted settings) via this EM approach is a novel and practical contribution.
- Significance: Problem is relevant to many applications (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement) and results suggest broad practical value.
- Strength — clarity: Explanations and notation are clear, making the method accessible to a wide audience.
- Strength — technical soundness: Use of Jensen’s inequality and derivations appear correct; implementation details are well-explained.
- Weakness — initialization: Sensitivity to initialization is noted; discussion and experiments on alternative strategies are insufficient.
- Weakness — computational analysis: Asymptotic runtime is given, but more detailed empirical runtime evaluation (esp. for large datasets and dependence on k) is lacking.
- Weakness — experimental scope: Experiments are limited to baby registry data, raising concerns about generalizability across domains.
- Weakness — Stiefel simplification: Simplified manifold updates ignore second-order information; potential impact on convergence/accuracy is not fully explored.
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important problem, introduces a novel and sound algorithm with compelling empirical gains, and is very well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: Concerns about initialization sensitivity, limited experimental diversity, and possible inaccuracies from manifold-update simplifications.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper recommending acceptance with minor revisions to address initialization, expanded runtime analysis, and discussion of Stiefel update effects.
