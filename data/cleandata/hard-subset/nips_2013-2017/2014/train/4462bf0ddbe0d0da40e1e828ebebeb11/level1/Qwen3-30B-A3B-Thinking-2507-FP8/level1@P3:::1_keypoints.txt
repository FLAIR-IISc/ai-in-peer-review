- Proposal: Introduces an EM algorithm that parameterizes the DPP kernel via eigenvalues/eigenvectors to learn the full kernel matrix and avoid projection degeneracy of gradient ascent.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous derivations; E-step uses the k-DPP conditional distribution, M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates and gradient updates for eigenvectors; experiments show up to 16.5% test log-likelihood gain.  
- Complexity: Provides computational complexity analysis showing asymptotic speed advantage (O(nNk²) vs O(nN³)) and empirical runtime improvements.  
- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written with helpful supplementary material, though the Stiefel manifold update (Eq.21) is under-explained for non-experts.  
- Originality: Novel insight to reparameterize the kernel for EM to enable full-kernel learning, distinct from previous restricted or Bayesian approaches.  
- Significance: Enables modeling of negative item interactions in applications (e.g., recommendations, summarization) and lays groundwork for future DPP learning research.  
- Strengths: New EM approach, solid theoretical justification, strong empirical gains over gradient ascent, clear conditions where method excels (high negative interactions), and demonstrated runtime benefits.  
- Weaknesses: Limited comparison to other recent DPP learning methods (e.g., Bayesian [8]); scalability to very large N (N > 10,000) not addressed; limited theoretical analysis of EM convergence; need clearer explanation of robustness over gradient ascent; numerical stability of eigendecomposition not discussed.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant contribution with theoretical and empirical support despite some analysis gaps.  
- Pro/con summary: For acceptance — solves a key limitation, novel, empirically and theoretically strong, well-written; Against — missing broader comparisons and deeper scalability/convergence analyses.
