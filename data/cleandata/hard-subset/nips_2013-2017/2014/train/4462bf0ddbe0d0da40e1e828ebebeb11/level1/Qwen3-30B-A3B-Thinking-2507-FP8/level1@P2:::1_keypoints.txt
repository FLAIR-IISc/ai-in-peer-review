- Addresses the fundamental problem of efficiently learning the full DPP kernel matrix from data, overcoming prior restrictions and NP-hard perceptions.  
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes the kernel via eigendecomposition to avoid degenerate projection steps in gradient ascent.  
- Derives rigorous E-step and M-step updates with detailed theoretical justification for avoiding degeneracy.  
- Experimental evaluation on Amazon baby registry data shows EM outperforms K-Ascent in test log-likelihood and robustness.  
- Provides comprehensive comparisons across multiple initialization strategies and analyzes when EM is most beneficial (e.g., negative item interactions).  
- Paper is technically sound with clear derivations, pseudocode (Algorithms 1 and 2), and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Writing and organization are exceptional; background and mathematical exposition are accessible to a broad audience.  
- Method is original: first efficient approach for learning the full kernel without restrictive parameterizations, distinguishing it from prior DPP work.  
- Significant practical impact: up to 16.5% relative gains in test log-likelihood and better recommendations in examples.  
- Opens new research directions for DPP learning and broadens applicability to tasks like summarization and recommendation.  
- Strengths: novel EM approach, strong theory, solid real-world experiments, clear presentation, practical significance.  
- Weaknesses: limited exploration of very large N scalability (N > 10k), somewhat limited initialization strategies, and unclear performance on very sparse subset data.  
- Recommendation: strongly pro-accept—meets NIPS criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance; minor limitations do not undermine the contribution.
