- Proposes a novel EM-style algorithm that learns full DPP kernels by reparameterizing K via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introducing latent eigencomponent subsets J.
- E-step: shows q(J|Y) is a k-DPP with a closed-form kernel (QY) and is computationally tractable via eigen-decomposition.
- M-step: gives closed-form eigenvalue updates (posterior inclusion probabilities) and a Stiefel-manifold gradient update for eigenvectors.
- Alternating E/M steps form a generalized EM; a line search enforces increases in the true likelihood.
- Empirically outperforms naïve projected gradient ascent on K (KA) in test log-likelihood, especially in low-data regimes and when off-diagonal interactions matter.
- Algorithm is roughly 2× faster than KA in reported experiments and benefits when k ≪ N.
- Technical soundness: derivations rely on known DPP identities and appear correct with supplement proofs; complexity for eigenvalue updates is O(n N k^2).
- Caveats: only a single gradient step per M-step (approximate M-step), gradient exactness assumes q = p, and λ must be clamped <1 for stability (breaks boundary feasibility).
- Empirical evaluation is careful about initialization and runtime but is limited to one dataset (Amazon baby registries).
- Missing comparisons to Bayesian DPP learning and scalable approximations (e.g., Nyström), and lacks predictive/task-based metrics beyond log-likelihood.
- Reproducibility is good: algorithms, complexity analysis, and code link provided; some derivations in supplement.
- Paper is well-written and organized; motivation and failure modes of projected gradient are clearly explained.
- Suggest clarifying generalized EM status, E-step computational costs, and intuition for Eq. (20)/initial gradient restriction.
- Originality: applying k-DPP mixture interpretation to derive a practical EM with closed-form eigenvalue updates and Stiefel updates is novel despite related prior machinery.
- Significance: meaningful advance for learning expressive DPPs with practical benefits, though scalability to very large N remains a limitation.
- Strengths: elegant eigen-space EM, avoids projection degeneracy, robust initialization, clear complexity advantage when k ≪ N, code provided.
- Weaknesses: limited baselines/datasets, partial convergence guarantees, no regularization/prior on K (overfitting risk), and ad hoc stability handling near λ≈1.
- Acceptance arguments: novel principled solution to an open problem with empirical robustness and speedups; clear potential impact.
- Rejection arguments: narrow empirical scope, incomplete theoretical guarantees, and unproven scalability to large N.
- Suggestions: add another domain dataset and predictive evaluations; compare to Bayesian/approximate methods; test light regularization or featureized kernels; expand convergence/ablation analyses.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept—promising and well-motivated contribution that would be stronger with broader evaluation and more convergence discussion.
