- Proposes an EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing in eigenvalues/eigenvectors and lower-bounding the likelihood.  
- Motivation is strong: learning the full kernel is conjectured NP-hard and prior work is more restrictive.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematical derivations appear sound.  
- Builds appropriately on prior DPP literature and correctly identifies limitations of earlier methods (row-weight learning, L-ensemble).  
- Novelty: EM reparameterization is a significant and clever contribution toward full-kernel learning.  
- Theoretical soundness: likelihood lower-bound and update derivations are well-justified.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on baby registry dataset show EM outperforms projected gradient ascent (K-Ascent) in test log-likelihood.  
- Clarity and organization: notation and explanations are consistent and accessible.  
- Authors acknowledge and offer practical solutions for initialization sensitivity and computational complexity.  
- Computational complexity: practical runtime analysis is limited to one dataset; scaling with N,n needs more study.  
- Initialization sensitivity: performance heavily depends on initialization; moments-matching helps but systematic study is lacking.  
- Practical implementation details: Stiefel manifold update and handling lambda=1 are treated briefly and need more elaboration.  
- Limited experiments: evaluation confined to a single domain (baby registries), reducing generality of conclusions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant open problem, is novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated, and well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited runtime analysis, sensitivity to initialization, and narrow experimental scope.  
- Overall: strong contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to expand runtime analysis, detail Stiefel updates, study initialization sensitivity, and evaluate more datasets.
