The paper "Expectation-Maximization for Learning Determinantal Point Processes" proposes a novel algorithm for learning the full kernel matrix of a determinantal point process (DPP) without assuming fixed values or restrictive parameterizations. The authors change the kernel parameterization from matrix entries to eigenvalues and eigenvectors and then lower-bound the likelihood in the manner of expectation-maximization (EM) algorithms. The proposed EM algorithm overcomes the need for the problematic projection step required for naive gradient ascent to maintain positive semi-definiteness of the kernel matrix.

The authors relate their work to previous research on DPPs, including the use of gradient ascent and convex optimization techniques. They also discuss the limitations of these approaches, such as the need for projection steps and the assumption of fixed kernel matrices. The proposed EM algorithm is shown to be asymptotically faster than gradient ascent and more robust to initialization and dataset changes.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough background on DPPs and their properties, making it easy to follow the development of the EM algorithm. The use of mathematical notation is clear and consistent throughout the paper.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel EM algorithm for learning DPPs without restrictive parameterizations.
* The demonstration of the algorithm's asymptotic speedup over gradient ascent.
* The robustness of the algorithm to initialization and dataset changes.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The assumption that the kernel matrix can be eigendecomposed, which may not always be the case.
* The need for a good initialization of the kernel matrix, which can affect the performance of the algorithm.
* The lack of theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the EM algorithm.

The paper is original and significant, as it proposes a new approach to learning DPPs without restrictive parameterizations. The EM algorithm is shown to be effective in practice, and the paper provides a thorough analysis of its properties and limitations.
