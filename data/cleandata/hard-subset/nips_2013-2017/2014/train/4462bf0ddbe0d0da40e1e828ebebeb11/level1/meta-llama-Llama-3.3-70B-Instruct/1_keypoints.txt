- Proposes a novel algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and using an EM-style lower bound.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a real-world product recommendation task with up to 16.5% relative gain in test log-likelihood vs. projected gradient on kernel entries.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to DPPs and applications.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated and accompanied by detailed computational complexity and runtime analysis.  
- Experimental results are strong and include analysis of advantages and limitations.  
- Strength: addresses limitations of prior methods that fix or restrict kernel parameterization by learning the full kernel.  
- Strength: empirical gains on a practical recommendation task support real-world applicability.  
- Strength: shows asymptotic runtime improvements over the naive approach.  
- Weakness: assumes the kernel matrix is positive semi-definite, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: uses a simplified initialization method that may provide poor starting points.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art DPP learning methods for a fuller evaluation.  
- Pro-accept: novel and effective algorithm with significant empirical gains and solid complexity analysis.  
- Con-accept: PSD assumption, simplistic initialization, and missing comparisons weaken the paper.  
- Overall: solid, novel contribution with strong results but could be strengthened by addressing PSD assumption, initialization strategy, and benchmarking against more baselines.
