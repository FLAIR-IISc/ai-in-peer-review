- Proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn full DPP kernels by reparameterizing in eigenvalues/eigenvectors and using the elementary DPP mixture as latent variables.  
- E-step yields q(J|Y)=p(J|Y), a k-DPP, enabling efficient marginals for closed-form eigenvalue updates.  
- Eigenvector updates are done via gradient steps on the Stiefel manifold with line search (generalized EM), losing standard EM monotonicity guarantees.  
- Contrasts with naive projected gradient ascent (KA), which tends to collapse to near-diagonal kernels due to projection pathology.  
- Empirically improves held-out log-likelihood (up to 16.5% in data-poor settings) and often runs faster than KA, with largest gains where negative interactions are strong.  
- Novelty: using the elementary DPP mixture for EM-based full-kernel learning appears original and nontrivial.  
- Methodology strength: leverages DPP structure, simple closed-form eigenvalue updates, and a reasonable manifold approach for eigenvectors.  
- Convergence concern: single-step generalized EM with line search is heuristic; paper should clarify convergence properties and stationary-point guarantees.  
- Complexity: claimed cost O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) is plausible and favorable when k≪N, but repeated N^3 operations remain costly.  
- Implementation note: QR-based retractions or Cayley transforms could reduce constants vs. matrix exponentials and should be discussed.  
- Numerical stability issues: need reporting on sensitivity to clamping eigenvalues away from 1 and to eigenvalue degeneracies.  
- Experiments are relevant but narrow: single real-world domain, no synthetic recovery experiments, and few baselines beyond KA.  
- Suggest adding comparisons to per-item weight learning, Bayesian parametric DPPs, mixtures/submodular shells, and task-facing metrics (e.g., MAP).  
- Clarity issues: clean up submatrix notation (U/V), explicitly state generalized EM acceptance criterion, and briefly derive Eq. 19 in the main text.  
- Scalability limitation: storing and updating a full dense V limits applicability to large N; low-rank parameterizations should be discussed.  
- Pros summarized: novel EM formulation, closed-form eigenvalue updates, principled manifold updates, and empirical/logical diagnostics for KA failure.  
- Cons summarized: weak convergence guarantees, limited experimental scope, scalability concerns, and some notational/derivational reliance on supplement.  
- Recommended fixes: add synthetic recovery, sensitivity analyses (λ clamping, initializations), convergence plots, and more efficient retractions/parameterizations.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept — solid, original contribution that would benefit from expanded experiments and clearer convergence discussion.
