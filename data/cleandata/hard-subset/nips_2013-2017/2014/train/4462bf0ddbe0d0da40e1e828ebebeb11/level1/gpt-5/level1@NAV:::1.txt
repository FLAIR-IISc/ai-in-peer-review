This paper tackles full-parameter learning for determinantal point processes (DPPs) by reparameterizing the kernel in the eigenbasis and deriving an EM-style procedure that optimizes a Jensen lower bound over a latent index set J (the subset of eigenvectors used by an elementary DPP). Unlike prior work that learns only per-item quality weights [2] or mixtures over fixed kernels [3], the method optimizes both eigenvalues and eigenvectors: the E-step computes q(J|Y) which is shown to be a k-DPP; the M-step gives closed-form updates for eigenvalues via k-DPP marginals and a Riemannian (Stiefel manifold) gradient step for eigenvectors. This addresses known issues with projected gradient ascent on K, whose PSD projection can collapse off-diagonals and stall diversity modeling. Empirically, on an Amazon baby registry recommendation task, the proposed EM improves test log-likelihood over projected gradient (KA), with larger gains when negative interactions matter, and shows favorable runtime scaling O(nNk^2) for the dominant terms versus O(nN^3) for KA. The work is well connected to prior NIPS/DPP literature on sampling, MAP, and k-DPPs [e.g., Kulesza & Taskar; Gillenwater et al.].

Quality: Technically sound and carefully derived. The identification of q(J|Y) as a k-DPP and the efficient marginal computations are convincing. However, theoretical guarantees are limited: the V-update is a single gradient step on the bound, and the line search checks likelihood rather than monotone ascent of the EM bound, so convergence properties are heuristic. Numerical stability near λ≈1 is acknowledged but not systematically addressed.

Clarity: Generally clear and well organized. Algorithmic steps, complexities, and intuitions are explicit. Minor suggestions: report both the bound F and likelihood L during training; provide pseudocode or references for k-DPP marginal computation; discuss initialization sensitivity systematically.

Originality: Recasting DPP learning as EM over elementary DPP mixtures and coupling it with k-DPP machinery is novel relative to convex weight-learning and mixture-of-fixed-DPP approaches.

Significance: Learning unconstrained kernels is important for applications where diversity interactions drive performance. The empirical study is suggestive but limited to one domain and a single baseline; broader benchmarks and comparisons to Bayesian/approximate methods (e.g., parametric DPPs, Nyström-based learning) would strengthen impact.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually neat EM formulation leveraging k-DPP structure
- Practical algorithm that mitigates projection degeneracy and improves runtime
- Clear derivations and useful implementation details (Stiefel update)

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited theoretical convergence guarantees
- Narrow empirical evaluation and baseline coverage
- Potential sensitivity to initialization and λ-regularization not fully explored

Overall, a solid and promising contribution to DPP learning with clear relevance to NIPS.
