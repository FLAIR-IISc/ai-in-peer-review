Paper proposes an EM-style algorithm to learn full DPP kernel via eigendecomposition, latent eigenvector subset J, Jensen lower bound, E-step q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y) (a k-DPP), M-step closed-form λ update and gradient V update on the Stiefel manifold.

E-step is exact; eigenvalue M-step is efficient via k-DPP marginals (O(n N k^2) cost); eigenvector update is a single first-order Stiefel step with line search and likelihood check (partial M-step).

Algorithm is a generalized EM; no formal monotonicity/convergence guarantees are provided, though line search on likelihood mitigates instability in practice.

Equation (20) applies only to the first gradient step (q=p); clarity needed on whether later V-steps ascend the bound or rely solely on likelihood checks.

Experiments on an Amazon baby registry dataset show up to 16.5% test log-likelihood gain in low-data settings, greater robustness to initialization, and better runtime behavior for moderate N.

Baselines are limited to projected gradient (KA); missing comparisons to per-item weight models, mixtures of fixed kernels, and Bayesian parametric approaches.

Runtime for V-update includes O(N^3) components, raising scalability concerns for large N; no low-rank or Nyström variants are explored.

Derivations are plausible and referenced to the supplement, but some central technical details required for implementation are deferred there.

Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and presents concise algorithms.

Reviewer suggestions: explicitly state the generalized-EM nature and discuss monotonicity; summarize the E-step lemma and k-DPP marginalization in the main text.

Numerical guidance is lacking: need advice on enforcing λ<1, conditioning of H_Y, stability of matrix exponential, and cost of computing V̂ for Q_Y.

Originality: novel use of elementary DPP mixture and k-DPP conditionals for learning full eigenspectrum; Stiefel manifold updates for eigenvectors are new in this context.

Significance: important for diversity-sensitive applications; offers a practical alternative to projected gradient when k ≪ N.

Main limitation: empirical scope limited to one domain and one baseline; scalability to large N remains an open question.

Pros for acceptance: elegant and original EM formulation, practical algorithm that avoids degenerate projections, solid empirical gains and runtime improvements, clear exposition, code/data available.

Cons for acceptance: lack of formal convergence/monotonicity guarantees, approximate/partial V-step, limited empirical comparisons, and scalability/implementation details deferred to supplement.

Reviewer questions: can you provide generalized-EM convergence guarantees under your line-search scheme? how sensitive is performance to step-size and λ tolerance? can you add baselines (per-item weights, mixtures)? can you show a low-rank/Nyström variant?

Recommendation: Weak accept.
