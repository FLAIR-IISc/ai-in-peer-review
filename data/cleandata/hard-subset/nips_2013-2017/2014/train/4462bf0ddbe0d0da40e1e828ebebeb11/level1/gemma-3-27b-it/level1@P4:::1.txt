This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for learning the kernel matrix of a Determinantal Point Process (DPP). The core idea is to reparameterize the kernel in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and then leverage this decomposition to derive a lower bound on the log-likelihood that can be optimized efficiently. The authors address a known NP-hardness of directly maximizing the likelihood of a DPP and demonstrate improvements over a projected gradient ascent approach, particularly in scenarios with strong item interactions.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively motivates the problem of DPP learning, highlights the limitations of previous approaches (scalar weighting and L-ensembles), and positions the proposed EM algorithm as a significant advance. The background section provides a concise and useful review of DPPs, including relevant mathematical notation and properties. The technical details of the EM algorithm are presented logically, with clear explanations of each step. The experimental setup and results are well-documented and support the authors’ claims of improved performance and efficiency.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Addressing the full kernel matrix learning problem for DPPs, instead of restricted settings, is a valuable contribution. The eigendecomposition-based approach and the resulting EM algorithm are novel and offer a practical solution to a difficult optimization problem.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear to be correct, and the use of Jensen's inequality to lower-bound the likelihood is a standard and appropriate technique for developing EM algorithms. The details regarding gradient computations and Stiefel manifold updates are well-explained.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are clear and concise.
*   **Significance:** The problem addressed is relevant to a range of applications, including recommendation systems, document summarization, and sensor placement. The demonstrated improvements in log-likelihood and runtime are significant, suggesting that the proposed algorithm has the potential to be widely adopted. The analysis relating performance gains to the strength of item interactions is insightful.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** While the paper acknowledges the importance of initialization, the discussion on the limitations of naive initializations (e.g., diagonal matrices) and the benefits of the moment-matching approach could be expanded. A more thorough investigation of different initialization strategies and their impact on convergence and performance would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Complexity Analysis:** The asymptotic runtime analysis is provided, but a more detailed empirical analysis of the actual runtime performance, particularly for larger datasets and higher dimensions, would be valuable. The authors mention the scaling with *k* (size of the largest subset), but a more explicit discussion of how *k* influences practical performance would be helpful.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are focused on a single type of dataset (baby registries). While the results are compelling within this context, it would be beneficial to evaluate the algorithm on a more diverse set of datasets and application domains to assess its generalizability.
*   **Stiefel Manifold Update Simplification:** While the simplification of the Stiefel manifold update is noted, the potential impact of neglecting second-order information on the convergence and accuracy of the algorithm is not fully discussed.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a challenging and important problem in the field of probabilistic modeling and machine learning.
*   The proposed EM algorithm is novel, technically sound, and offers significant improvements over existing approaches.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, making it accessible to a broad audience.
*   The experimental results are compelling and support the authors’ claims.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The initialization sensitivity of the algorithm could be a potential concern.
*   The limited scope of the experiments raises questions about the generalizability of the results.
*   The simplification of the Stiefel manifold update may introduce inaccuracies.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed EM algorithm for learning DPPs is a significant advance over existing methods, and the paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, particularly regarding initialization sensitivity and a more detailed analysis of computational performance.
