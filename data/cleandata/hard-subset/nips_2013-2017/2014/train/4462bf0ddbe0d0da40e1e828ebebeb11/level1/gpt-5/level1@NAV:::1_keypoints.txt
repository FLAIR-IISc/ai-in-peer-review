- Proposes EM-style learning for full DPP kernels by reparameterizing in the eigenbasis and optimizing a Jensen lower bound over latent index set J.  
- Unlike prior work, jointly optimizes eigenvalues and eigenvectors rather than only per-item quality weights or mixtures over fixed kernels.  
- E-step identifies q(J|Y) as a k-DPP, enabling efficient marginal computations.  
- M-step gives closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and a Riemannian (Stiefel manifold) gradient step for eigenvectors.  
- Addresses issues of projected gradient ascent on K, whose PSD projection can collapse off-diagonals and stall diversity modeling.  
- Empirical results on an Amazon baby registry task show improved test log-likelihood over projected gradient (KA), especially when negative interactions matter.  
- Runtime scaling favorable: dominant cost O(n N k^2) vs O(n N^3) for KA.  
- Technically sound and carefully derived; k-DPP identification and marginal computations are convincing.  
- Theoretical guarantees are limited: V-update is only a single gradient step on the bound and line search checks likelihood rather than guaranteeing monotone EM-bound ascent.  
- Numerical stability issues near λ ≈ 1 are acknowledged but not systematically addressed.  
- Clarity is generally good; algorithmic steps, complexities, and intuitions are explicit.  
- Minor clarity suggestions: report both bound F and likelihood L during training, provide pseudocode/references for k-DPP marginals, and analyze initialization sensitivity.  
- Originality: novel recasting of DPP learning as EM over elementary DPP mixtures leveraging k-DPP machinery.  
- Significance: important direction for learning unconstrained kernels, but empirical evaluation is narrow (one domain, single baseline) and lacks comparisons to Bayesian/approximate methods (e.g., parametric DPPs, Nyström).  
- Pros: neat EM formulation, practical algorithm mitigating projection degeneracy with improved runtime, clear derivations and Stiefel update implementation details.  
- Cons: limited theoretical convergence guarantees, narrow empirical evaluation and baseline coverage, potential sensitivity to initialization and λ-regularization not fully explored.  
- Overall: solid and promising contribution to DPP learning with clear relevance to the conference.
