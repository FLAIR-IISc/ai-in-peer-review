- Proposes a novel EM algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing into eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- EM lower-bounds the likelihood and avoids the problematic PSD-projection step required by naive gradient ascent.  
- Demonstrates asymptotic speedup over gradient ascent and greater robustness to initialization and dataset changes.  
- Provides clear, well-organized exposition and thorough background on DPPs with consistent mathematical notation.  
- Relates work to prior DPP methods (gradient ascent, convex optimization) and highlights their limitations.  
- Strength: learns DPPs without restrictive kernel parameterizations.  
- Strength: empirically faster and more robust than baseline optimization methods.  
- Weakness: assumes the kernel matrix can be eigendecomposed, which may not always hold.  
- Weakness: performance depends on good initialization of the kernel matrix.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for the EM algorithm.  
- Overall assessment: original, significant contribution and well-suited for NIPS.  
- Recommendation: accept, with pro arguments (novelty, speed, robustness) and con arguments (eigendecomposition assumption, initialization sensitivity, no convergence proof).
