- Paper proposes learning the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and using an EM algorithm with a likelihood lower bound, showing empirical gains vs K-Ascent on product recommendation.
- Writing and organization are strong, with a clear introduction and concise background.

Strengths:
- Novelty: EM reparameterization tackles full-kernel DPP learning previously seen as intractable.
- Technical soundness: Derivations and complexity analysis for EM updates appear correct and justified.
- Empirical validation: Improved test log-likelihood and useful runtime comparisons strengthen claims.
- Clarity: Explanations and notation are consistent and accessible.
- Significance: Better DPP learning can improve modeling of diversity/quality tradeoffs in real applications.

Weaknesses:
- Implementation complexity: Stiefel manifold eigenvector updates and overall implementation seem non-trivial.
- Limited experiments: Evaluation is restricted to one application and a specific dataset.
- Initialization sensitivity: Performance depends noticeably on initialization choice.
- Simplification justification: Using a single gradient step for eigenvector updates lacks thorough justification.
- Related-work connections: More discussion needed linking to other nonconvex optimization methods (e.g., ADMM).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, technically sound solution to a challenging problem.
- Demonstrated empirical effectiveness.
- Clear presentation of technical material.
- Potential for real-world impact.

Arguments against acceptance:
- High implementation barrier may limit adoption.
- Narrow experimental scope weakens generality.
- Sensitivity to initialization raises robustness concerns.

Overall assessment:
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address practical implementation challenges and broaden experimental evaluation.
