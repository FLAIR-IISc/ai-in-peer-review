- Proposes a novel EM algorithm that learns the full DPP kernel matrix via eigendecomposition.  
- EM avoids projected-gradient (KA) degeneracies and poor local optima caused by projection steps.  
- Demonstrates up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood on an Amazon baby registry dataset.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing initializations and data regimes.  
- Provides detailed derivations and efficient updates for eigenvalues/eigenvectors.  
- Runtime analysis shows EM is asymptotically faster than KA, supported by empirical runtimes.  
- Addresses an NP-hard DPP learning problem with practical implications for recommendations, summarization, and sensor placement.  
- Clarity is an issue: dense math may be hard for readers unfamiliar with DPPs or eigendecomposition.  
- Limited experimental scope: evaluations confined mainly to product recommendation, reducing demonstrated generality.  
- Initialization sensitivity: algorithm relies on non-trivial initializations (Wishart, moment matching), raising robustness concerns.  
- For acceptance: makes a significant, validated technical contribution that outperforms KA and is practically relevant.  
- Against acceptance: presentation clarity and limited domain evaluation may undermine accessibility and perceived generality.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements to exposition and broader experimental validation.
