- Proposes an EM algorithm for full-kernel DPP maximum likelihood by reparameterizing the kernel via eigenvalues/eigenvectors and introducing a hidden subset J of eigenvectors.

- E-step: shows q(J|Y)=pK(J|Y) is a k-DPP with an explicit, efficiently constructible kernel, enabling tractable computation of required marginals.

- M-step: provides closed-form eigenvalue updates via k-DPP marginals and an eigenvector update via a gradient step constrained on the Stiefel manifold.

- Avoids projection-induced failure modes of naïve projected gradient on K (KA), which can drive K toward degenerate near-diagonal solutions.

- Complexity advantage when average set size k ≪ N: main EM computations O(n N k^2) per iteration versus KA’s expensive matrix inverses (O(n N^3)), though N^3 costs remain for decompositions/updates.

- Empirical results on an Amazon baby registry dataset: EM yields higher test log-likelihoods (median improvements up to 16.5% in low-data regimes) and runs ≈2.1× faster than KA.

- Builds on established DPP foundations (elementary-DPP mixtures, k-DPPs) and leverages known manifold optimization techniques for orthogonality constraints.

- Strength: principled, natural EM reformulation grounded in DPP structure.

- Strength: tractable E-step characterization as a k-DPP enabling efficient marginal computations.

- Strength: efficient M-step with closed-form eigenvalue updates and Stiefel-respecting eigenvector updates.

- Strength: empirically mitigates a real failure mode of projected-gradient methods and shows runtime and likelihood gains.

- Strength: reproducibility via public code and data; robustness to different initializations.

- Weakness: limited baselines—missing comparisons to convex per-row weight learning, Bayesian/parametric DPP methods, and fixed-kernel mixtures.

- Weakness: narrow evaluation scope—single application domain and likelihood-only metrics; lacks task-oriented measures (e.g., recommendation/diversity) and other datasets.

- Weakness: no formal convergence/monotonicity guarantees for the generalized EM with a single gradient step and step-size checks.

- Weakness: scalability concerns—manifold updates/exp still incur N^3 costs, limiting validation for very large N; no low-rank/structured approximations tested.

- Weakness: key technical derivations and numerical-stability handling (e.g., λ→1) are deferred to the supplement rather than included in the main text.

- Quality: technically sound overall, but some complexity claims depend on specialized linear algebra; more explicit algorithmic detail would aid verification.

- Empirical methodology: reasonable (careful init and splits) but would benefit from more hyperparameter/line-search details and fairness exposition.

- Clarity: generally well-written and motivated; suggest moving key derivation sketches and numerical-stability notes into main text.

- Originality: novel EM formulation and computational tricks for practical full-kernel DPP MLE.

- Significance: addresses an important open problem in DPP learning with a practical algorithm likely to influence future work.

- Pros for acceptance: novel principled approach, efficient k-DPP-based updates, demonstrated empirical accuracy and speed gains, and reproducible artifacts.

- Cons for acceptance: limited baselines/datasets, lack of formal convergence guarantees, unclear extreme-scale scalability, and dependence on supplementary material for core technical details.

- Suggestions for improvement: add comparisons to convex per-row and Bayesian methods, include another dataset/task metric, provide monotonicity or empirical non-decrease evidence, explore low-rank/structured scaling, and move key derivations/stability discussion into the main paper.
