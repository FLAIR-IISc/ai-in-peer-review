- Addresses learning the full kernel matrix of a DPP via maximum likelihood, a non-convex problem conjectured NP-hard.  
- Notes prior work focused on restricted convex settings or Bayesian parametric kernels.  
- Main contribution: a novel EM algorithm that directly learns kernel eigenvalues and eigenvectors.  
- Introduces a latent variable (selection of eigenvectors) to derive a tractable lower bound on the log-likelihood.  
- EM procedure avoids pitfalls of naive projected gradient ascent.  
- M-step yields closed-form updates for eigenvalues and eigenvector updates via optimization on the Stiefel manifold.  
- Technical derivation is sound, leveraging the generative interpretation of DPPs as mixtures of elementary DPPs.  
- Experiments on a product recommendation task show EM is more robust, faster, and models diversity better than the baseline.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, making complex derivations easy to follow.  
- Weakness: experimental comparison limited to a single baseline (projected gradient ascent).  
- Weakness: would benefit from comparisons to restricted models (e.g., diagonal-only) to quantify practical gains.  
- Weakness: eigenvector update has O(N^3) complexity, which may be a bottleneck for very large ground sets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled solution to a fundamental problem, strong theory, clear empirical gains, high significance.  
- Argument against acceptance: evaluation could be broader, but overall strengths outweigh minor limitations.
