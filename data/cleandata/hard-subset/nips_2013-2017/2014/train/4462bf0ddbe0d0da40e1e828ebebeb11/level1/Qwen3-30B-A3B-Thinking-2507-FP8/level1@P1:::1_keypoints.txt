- Addresses learning the full DPP kernel matrix, overcoming prior restricted learning settings.  
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm that works with the kernel eigendecomposition to avoid projection steps and degenerate kernels.  
- EM algorithm is carefully derived with detailed E-step (Lemma 1) and M-step updates (Eqs. 18–20).  
- Method is more robust and asymptotically faster than naive gradient ascent; runtime advantages are analyzed and reported.  
- Demonstrates up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood on an Amazon baby registry recommendation task.  
- Experiments are thorough, using multiple initialization strategies (Wishart vs. moment matching) and analyzing when EM helps most.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear background, reproducible derivations, and precise algorithm descriptions.  
- Original contribution: first practical method for learning full unconstrained DPP kernels without restrictive assumptions.  
- Significance: advances both theory and practice, especially for modeling strong negative item interactions in applications.  
- Strengths: solves a meaningful problem, provides a technically sound EM solution, shows strong empirical gains, and includes runtime/convergence analysis.  
- Weaknesses: scalability to very large N is not demonstrated (experiments use N=71).  
- Weaknesses: initialization choices are somewhat ad-hoc with no principled selection method.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical guarantees on global convergence or convergence rates for the EM algorithm.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparisons to methods beyond the K-Ascent baseline.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, novel, and well-validated work despite noted limitations.
