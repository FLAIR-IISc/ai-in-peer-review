- Proposes an EM-based method to learn the full DPP kernel matrix, overcoming prior restrictions to limited parameterizations.  
- Recognizes DPP log-likelihood maximization is non-convex and NP-hard in kernel entries, and situates work relative to prior restricted methods.  
- Key insight: parameterize marginal kernel K via eigendecomposition to enable a novel EM algorithm.  
- EM avoids problematic projection steps required by gradient-ascent (KA) approaches.  
- Strong technical development: EM framework properly derived (Jensen's inequality) and presented in Section 3.  
- Algorithm effectively handles non-convex constraints using eigendecomposition and Stiefel manifold updates.  
- Empirical results on 13 Amazon baby registry categories show up to 16.5% relative improvement in test log-likelihood over KA.  
- EM is asymptotically faster than KA, with an average 2.1x speedup.  
- Analysis links performance gains to importance of off-diagonal kernel entries (d > 0.025).  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Related work is appropriately cited, though broader applications beyond product recommendation could be discussed.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical analysis of convergence properties despite strong practical results.  
- Overall significance: meaningful advance in DPP learning, theoretically sound and practically effective, suitable for NIPS.
