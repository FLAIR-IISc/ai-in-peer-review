- Problem: Learning the full, unconstrained DPP kernel from observed subsets, a non-convex (conjectured NP-hard) problem.  
- Prior work: Previous methods either learned restricted convex forms (quality-only), mixtures with fixed kernels, or Bayesian methods with parametric kernels.  
- Main contribution: Novel EM algorithm that reparameterizes the kernel via eigendecomposition and treats eigenvector selection as a latent variable.  
- EM formulation: Frames learning in an EM framework to avoid problematic projection steps of naive methods.  
- Eigenvalue updates: Provides closed-form updates for eigenvalues in the EM procedure.  
- Eigenvector updates: Uses principled optimization on the Stiefel manifold to enforce orthogonality for eigenvectors.  
- E-step insight: Identifies the posterior over latent variables as a k-DPP, enabling efficient computation.  
- Practical M-step detail: In practice the M-step uses a single gradient step for eigenvectors (an approximation rather than full optimization).  
- Baseline comparison: Outperforms projected gradient ascent, being more robust and faster in experiments.  
- Baseline failure mode: Projected gradient often degenerates to a near-diagonal kernel after projection, which EM avoids.  
- Experiments: Real-world product recommendation task with well-designed comparisons and varied initializations/data regimes.  
- Technical quality: Sound derivations, sophisticated techniques, and a complete, usable algorithm with reproducible detail promised.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, organized, and easy to follow, with clear figures and tables.  
- Originality: Novel application of EM with eigendecomposition latent variables and manifold optimization for DPP learning.  
- Significance: Makes learning full non-parametric DPP kernels practical, likely to broaden DPP applicability and impact.  
- Weakness/minor: Single-step M-step for eigenvectors is a slight deviation from classic EM coordinate ascent and should be clarified.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — strengths substantially outweigh the minor theoretical limitation.
