- Tackles learning the full DPP kernel matrix, a problem conjectured to be NP-hard.  
- Proposes a novel EM algorithm re-parameterizing the kernel by eigenvalues/eigenvectors and deriving a likelihood lower bound.  
- Addresses limitations of prior DPP learning methods that were restricted to convex or fixed-kernel settings.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound with clear update rules and justification for using eigendecompositions.  
- Shows practical gains: up to 16.5% improvement in test log-likelihood over a projected gradient baseline on a product recommendation task.  
- Runtime complexity analysis indicates potential asymptotic speedup versus gradient ascent.  
- Includes comparison to a naive K-Ascent baseline as a concrete benchmark.  
- Experiments are limited in scope (single “baby registry” dataset), raising questions about generality.  
- Sensitivity to initialization is acknowledged but lacks a systematic study of initialization strategies.  
- Eigenvector update is simplified to a single gradient step, which seems ad-hoc; more sophisticated Stiefel manifold methods are suggested.  
- The role of dataset characteristic ‘d’ is noted but underexplored; ablation studies could clarify its impact.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution and recommend acceptance with minor revisions to broaden experiments and refine eigenvector updates.
