- Proposes a novel EM algorithm for learning the full DPP kernel by reparameterizing into eigenvalues/eigenvectors and lower-bounding the likelihood via Jensen’s inequality.  
- Develops an efficient alternating optimization (E-step concave in auxiliary distribution; M-step concave in eigenvalues) with runtime O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3), claimed asymptotically faster than naive methods.  
- Empirical results: EM outperforms naive projected gradient ascent and is more robust to initialization and dataset changes.  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset (Amazon baby registries), raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Comparisons are narrow: only evaluated against naive projected gradient ascent, not against other existing DPP learning methods.  
- Originality: first EM approach for learning the full DPP kernel with detailed efficiency and robustness analysis.  
- Significance: method is promising for applications needing negative-interaction modeling (recommendation, summarization, sensor placement).  
- Quality and clarity: paper is well-written, clearly explained, with consistent notation and well-presented experiments.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel, efficient, and robust algorithm with clear benefits over naive baseline.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: limited experimental scope and insufficient comparison to alternative methods.
