- Problem: Learns the full, unconstrained DPP kernel from observed subsets, a challenging non-convex (conjectured NP-hard) maximum likelihood problem.  
- Literature context: Prior work avoided this by learning restricted parameterizations (e.g., scalar quality weights or k‑DPP ensembles) rather than the full kernel.  
- Key idea: Reparameterize K by eigendecomposition (V, Λ) and apply an EM framework with latent variable J representing selected eigenvectors.  
- E-step insight: The posterior q(J | Yi) is itself a tractable k‑DPP, enabling efficient inference in the E-step.  
- M-step updates: Eigenvalues Λ have a closed-form update; eigenvectors V are updated via gradient steps on the Stiefel manifold to maintain orthogonality.  
- Avoids baseline pitfall: EM sidesteps projection-induced eigenvalue collapse that causes projected gradient ascent (K‑Ascent) to fail.  
- Technical quality: Derivation is sound, the connection between DPP generative process and J is elegant, and manifold optimization is appropriate.  
- Experiments: On the baby registry dataset EM outperforms projected gradient baseline, showing robustness to initialization and computational efficiency.  
- Empirical gains: Reported test log-likelihood improvements up to 16.5% over the naive approach.  
- Originality: Novel synthesis of EM, DPP theory, and manifold optimization; first practical algorithm to learn the full DPP kernel.  
- Significance: Makes DPPs more broadly applicable by enabling learning of both item quality and pairwise similarity/diversity, impacting recommender systems, summarization, search.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, logically organized, and provides helpful algorithm blocks and intuition.  
- Weaknesses: Baseline comparison is somewhat simplistic; would benefit from comparisons to more advanced non-convex constrained optimizers.  
- Evaluation limitation: Experiments are limited to a single domain (product recommendation); additional domains would strengthen empirical claims.
