Summary
The paper tackles learning the full kernel of a determinantal point process (DPP) by maximum likelihood. While prior work largely restricted learning to per-item quality weights (convex) or mixtures/linear combinations of fixed DPPs (sacrificing the pure DPP form or requiring many base kernels), this work proposes an EM-style algorithm that reparametrizes the DPP kernel in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Using the known mixture representation of a DPP as a mixture over elementary DPPs indexed by subsets of eigenvectors, the authors introduce a latent variable J (the selected eigenvector set), form a Jensen lower bound, and derive coordinate updates: a closed-form M-step for eigenvalues based on k-DPP marginals, and a gradient step on the Stiefel manifold for eigenvectors. They contrast with a naive projected gradient ascent (KA) on K that tends to get trapped near diagonal matrices due to projection. Empirically, on a product recommendation dataset derived from Amazon baby registries, the EM approach improves test log-likelihood relative to KA (up to 16.5% in data-poor settings) and is faster in practice, with the largest gains in categories with strong negative interactions.

Relation to prior work: The paper builds on the DPP foundation (Macchi; Kulesza & Taskar) and the eigendecomposition-based algorithms for sampling/inference. It contrasts with learning per-item quality weights (Kulesza & Taskar, UAI’12), learning mixtures of submodular shells (Lin & Bilmes), and Bayesian parametric DPPs (Affandi et al.). The use of the elementary DPP mixture for EM-based learning of a full kernel appears novel.

Quality
- Methodology: The EM construction is thoughtful and leverages DPP structure well. The E-step solution q(J|Y)=p(J|Y) is natural and the observation that it is a k-DPP is powerful, enabling efficient marginals for eigenvalue updates. The eigenvalue update is simple and avoids projection. The eigenvector update via a Stiefel manifold step is reasonable; however, the paper only derives the gradient expression (Eq. 20) for the initial q=p case and then adopts a single-step generalized EM with line search based on likelihood. This loses standard EM monotonicity guarantees; the paper should more clearly acknowledge this and discuss convergence properties (e.g., to stationary points under mild conditions).
- Complexity: The claimed complexity O(T1 n N k^2 + T1 T2 N^3) is plausible given k-DPP marginal computations and repeated orthogonal updates. The asymptotic advantage over KA (which pays O(nN^3)) is convincing when k≪N, a typical case in recommendation/summarization. Still, the cubic factor in N for repeated matrix exponentials is nontrivial; QR-based retractions or Cayley transforms could reduce constants and should be discussed.
- Numerical stability: The method requires clamping λ away from 1 to avoid singularities in R; sensitivity to this clamp and to eigenvalue degeneracies (repeated λ) should be reported. The projection pathology of KA is well illustrated; correspondingly, it would help to show that EM avoids near-diagonal degeneracy even from poor initializations, e.g., via spectra/off-diagonal mass traces across iterations.
- Experiments: The registry study is relevant; demonstrating larger gains in categories with stronger pairwise interactions supports the motivation. However, the experimental scope is narrow: a single domain, no synthetic recovery study with known ground truth, and limited baselines. It would be informative to compare against (a) convex per-item-weight learning (to quantify the value of learning similarities), (b) Bayesian parametric DPPs (Affandi et al.), and (c) mixture-of-DPPs or submodular shells where feasible. Reporting held-out likelihood is appropriate, but task-facing metrics (e.g., diversity-aware recommendation quality, MAP for k-MAP inference) would strengthen the story. Include convergence curves and variance across runs.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. Some notational issues should be cleaned:
- Be consistent with submatrix notation for U/V (rows vs columns) in Eq. 16–17; dimensions of UYi and ZYi are easy to confuse as written.
- Explicitly state that the EM used is generalized EM and clarify the acceptance criterion (checking L after recomputing q) and the implications for monotonicity.
- Provide a brief derivation or intuition for Eq. 19 in the main text (currently deferred).
- Algorithm 2: state the cost of the matrix exponential and whether a retraction is used in code (e.g., polar decomposition/QR) rather than exact exp.

Originality
The core idea—introducing the latent eigenvector set and using the elementary DPP mixture to derive an EM scheme for full-kernel learning—is, to my knowledge, new and nontrivial. Prior works that parameterize only item qualities or mixtures do not offer a route to full unconstrained kernels while keeping DPP structure. The combination with Stiefel manifold updates is appropriate and not standard in the DPP literature.

Significance
Learning full DPP kernels is an important open problem with practical impact in diversity-aware selection. The method is likely to be useful in moderate-N regimes and in domains where negative correlations matter. The main limitations are scalability to very large N (full eigenbasis stored and updated) and the lack of stronger convergence guarantees. Nevertheless, this is a meaningful step beyond previous restricted-learning formulations.

Pros
- Novel EM formulation exploiting DPP eigendecomposition and k-DPP structure.
- Closed-form eigenvalue updates and principled eigenvector updates on a manifold.
- Empirical gains in likelihood and runtime over a strong naive baseline; diagnostic explanation for KA failure.
- Clear connection between dataset structure (strength of interactions) and benefits.

Cons
- Convergence guarantees are weak; generalized EM with approximate gradient and line search on L is heuristic.
- Experimental scope limited: single domain, no synthetic recovery, few baselines beyond KA.
- Scalability concerns remain for large N due to repeated N^3 operations and full dense V.
- Some notational ambiguities and reliance on supplement for key derivations.

Suggestions and questions
- Add comparisons to convex per-item weight learning and to Bayesian parametric DPPs; include synthetic experiments recovering a planted kernel.
- Report sensitivity to λ clamping, initialization, and step-size parameters; include convergence plots of L and off-diagonal mass.
- Discuss/implement more efficient Stiefel retractions (QR/polar) and low-rank parameterizations to scale to larger N.
- Can one extend Eq. 20 beyond the first gradient step, e.g., via Fisher scoring or variational approximations to maintain a tractable surrogate? Any theoretical monotonicity result for the proposed acceptance rule?

Recommendation
Lean accept. The paper presents a clear and original approach to an important problem in DPP learning, with solid derivations and promising empirical results. Strengthening the experimental section and clarifying convergence would further improve it, but the contribution merits presentation at NIPS.
