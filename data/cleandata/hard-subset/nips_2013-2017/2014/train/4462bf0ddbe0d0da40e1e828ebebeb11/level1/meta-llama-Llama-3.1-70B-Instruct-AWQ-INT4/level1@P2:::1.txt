This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning determinantal point processes (DPPs) by exploiting their eigendecomposition and applying an expectation-maximization (EM) style optimization procedure. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on a real-world product recommendation task, achieving relative gains of up to 16.5% in test log-likelihood compared to a naive approach of maximizing likelihood by projected gradient ascent on the entries of the kernel matrix.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the background, motivation, and technical details of the proposed algorithm. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the related work and position their contribution within the existing literature.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A novel and effective algorithm for learning DPPs, which addresses the limitations of previous methods.
* A thorough analysis of the algorithm's properties, including its computational complexity and convergence.
* An extensive evaluation of the algorithm on a real-world dataset, demonstrating its advantages over a baseline method.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The algorithm's dependence on a good initialization, which may not always be available in practice.
* The lack of a clear explanation of how to choose the hyperparameters of the algorithm, such as the step size and the number of iterations.
* The limited scope of the evaluation, which only considers a single dataset and a single baseline method.
