- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Main contributions: unified parallel block update framework with global convergence and O(1/T) rate; shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases; handles overlapping blocks (group lasso).  
- Well-motivated, addressing a gap in multi-block ADMM extensions.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound with reasonable assumptions and clear connections to existing methods.  
- Experimental results (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) show PDMM outperforms sADMM, RBSUMM, GSADMM in convergence speed and runtime.  
- Correctly cites key prior work and establishes novelty via unified framework and overlap handling.  
- Strength: first randomized parallel block-coordinate multi-block ADMM with constant step-size convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation with appropriate baselines and multiple runs.  
- Clarifies role of dual backward steps and relation to existing methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of sensitivity to parameter choices (τi, νi) beyond theory.  
- Weakness: lacking explanation why PDMM1 (single-block updates) sometimes outperforms parallel variants and deeper analysis of block-count vs. computational-cost trade-off.  
- Overall: technically sound, practically useful, significant contribution with a recommendation for acceptance.
