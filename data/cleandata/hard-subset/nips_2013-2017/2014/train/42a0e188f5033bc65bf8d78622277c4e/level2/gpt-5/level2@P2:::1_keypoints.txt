- Problem & contribution: Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel multi-block ADMM variant with a “dual backward step” and sparsity-aware step sizes for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Key algorithmic ideas: (i) evaluate primal updates at a backstepped dual ŷt to stabilize Jacobi-style multi-block updates; (ii) set τi and νi as functions of per-row sparsity di and number of updated blocks K; (iii) allow inexact updates via Bregman proximalization, unifying exact PDMM and proximal-Jacobian ADMM.  
- Theoretical results: Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity with constant steps using a Lyapunov potential and monotone expected decrease.  
- Parameterization insight: τi = K/(K̃i(2J−K)) and νi = 1 − 1/K̃i incorporate both sparsity and parallelism and recover sensible limits (e.g., νi→0 when K=1).  
- Relation to prior work: Extends multi-block ADMM literature by providing a Jacobi-style randomized method with constant steps and O(1/T) rate, bridging sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.  
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show faster convergence than sADMM and RBSUMM and competitive runtime with GSADMM, with PDMM benefiting from sparsity-aware stepsizes.  
- Practical parallelism: PDMM offers inherent parallel updates (tunable K) and leverages problem sparsity to improve performance in structured problems.  
- Technical clarity issue: Construction and roles of matrices Pt and Q in the potential are deferred to the supplement and should be summarized in the main text.  
- Implicit assumptions: Paper should explicitly state any requirements on A (e.g., rank conditions, di≥1) used for bounded dual sequences and defining di.  
- Stochastic guarantees: Analysis is in expectation; high-probability or almost-sure convergence statements would strengthen the theoretical claims.  
- Readability suggestions: Include pseudocode for PDMM, a table mapping parameter choices to sADMM/PJADMM, and a short proof sketch of the main descent inequality.  
- Notation consolidation: Consolidate and briefly interpret symbols Pt, Q, γi, βi in the main text for clarity.  
- Originality: Novel combination of a sparsity-calibrated dual backward step with randomized multi-block updates and a unified inexact framework.  
- Significance: Addresses practical instability of naive Jacobi multi-block ADMM and provides principled, rate-guaranteed parallel updates useful for structured problems.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong theory (global convergence, O(1/T)), novel algorithmic design (dual backward step + sparsity/K-dependent steps), unifying view, and representative empirical evidence.  
- Major cons / experiment fixes: sADMM baseline may be unfair (public implementation omits dual update); reimplement sADMM with proper dual updates and include PJADMM with tuned parameters; report larger-scale and parallel wall-clock results.  
- Ablations & sensitivity: Add sensitivity analyses for ρ, τi, νi, and K; ablation that disables the backward step (νi=0) to isolate its effect; discuss nonuniform sampling and τi, νi adaptation.  
- Overlapping-blocks detail: Provide more details on splitting for overlapping group lasso, memory overhead, effect on di, and proximal choices for efficient updates.  
- Minor comments: Fix typos (e.g., “seperable”→“separable”), clarify inconsistent inequality signs in a remark, and add pseudocode and a parameter-choice table.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — substantive theoretical and algorithmic contribution, but needs fixes to baseline fairness and stronger/clearer experiments and exposition.
