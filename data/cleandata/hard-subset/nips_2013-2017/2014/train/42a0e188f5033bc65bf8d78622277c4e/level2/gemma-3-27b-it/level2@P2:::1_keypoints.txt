- Proposes PDMM: randomized block-coordinate ADMM with a dual backward step for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Well-motivated problem relevant to machine learning and optimization.  
- Thorough connection and positioning relative to ADMM variants (GSADMM, sADMM, PJADMM).  
- Provides substantial theoretical contributions: convergence analysis and iteration complexity.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases, clarifying method relationships.  
- Empirical evaluation on RPCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrates practical performance.  
- Notation and technical flow are dense and unclear; indices and set meanings are hard to track.  
- Explanation of the dual backward step and step-size choices lacks accessibility for broader readers.  
- The link between matrix sparsity and optimal step-size selection is under-explained and needs expansion.  
- Lacks clear comparison to highly optimized, state-of-the-art solvers (e.g., PA-APG, S-APG).  
- Experimental section feels fragmented with poorly justified parameter choices.  
- Limited sensitivity analysis of method performance to parameter settings.  
- RPCA results presented only as figures without quantitative tables, hindering detailed comparison.  
- Claim about handling overlapping blocks is overstated beyond the single demonstrated application.  
- Novelty: combining randomized block coordinate descent with ADMM and a dual backward step is a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: method could impact RPCA, sparse recovery, and structured ML if robust and scalable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel technique combination, solid theory, practical demonstrations, useful connections to existing methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, limited solver comparisons, weak quantitative experiments, overstated overlapping-block claims.  
- Recommendations: improve notation and explanations, expand empirical comparisons and quantitative reporting, analyze parameter sensitivity, clarify advantages for overlapping blocks, and fix minor typos.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper deserving acceptance pending minor revisions.
