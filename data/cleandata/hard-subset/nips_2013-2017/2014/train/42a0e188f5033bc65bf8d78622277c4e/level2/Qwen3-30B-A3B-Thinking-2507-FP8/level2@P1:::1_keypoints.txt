- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM for multi-block linear-constrained optimization.  
- Addresses the challenging problem of generalizing two-block ADMM to multi-block settings.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step to prevent overly aggressive dual updates and enable convergence.  
- Provides a principled way to choose step sizes based on constraint-matrix sparsity and number of randomized blocks.  
- Proves global convergence (Theorem 1) and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size (Theorem 2).  
- Shows existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) are special cases of PDMM and connects to RBSUMM.  
- Theoretical results are supported by rigorous analysis under reasonable assumptions.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art in convergence speed and runtime.  
- Well-written and organized paper with clear notation; Section 2.2 provides insightful connections to prior work.  
- Practical demonstration of how sparsity informs step-size selection for large-scale problems.  
- Suggestion: include a more detailed per-iteration computational-complexity comparison, especially the cost of the dual backward step.  
- Suggestion: make the connection to overlapping group lasso more explicit in the main text (currently in Section 2.3).  
- Suggestion: better articulate trade-offs between block size and convergence speed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong technical quality, significant contribution, and suitable for NIPS; recommend acceptance.
