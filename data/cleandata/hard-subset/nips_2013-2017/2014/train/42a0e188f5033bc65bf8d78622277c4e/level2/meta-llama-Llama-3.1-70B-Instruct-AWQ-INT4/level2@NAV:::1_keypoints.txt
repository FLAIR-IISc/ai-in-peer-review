- Proposes PDMM: a novel parallel randomized block-coordinate variant of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Claims PDMM achieves global O(1/T) convergence with a constant step size.  
- Claims PDMM outperforms ADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM in experiments (RPCA and overlapping group lasso).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical derivation, convergence proofs, and iteration complexity analysis.  
- Experimental results are convincing, showing faster convergence and runtime vs. compared methods.  
- Algorithm can parallelize computation and is potentially useful for large-scale convex optimization.  
- Authors demonstrate strong field knowledge and situate PDMM relative to ADMM variants.  
- PDMM is novel in combining parallelization and randomization for the target problem class.  
- Paper is complete: derivation, proofs, complexity, experiments, and method connections are presented.  
- Limitation: lacks comprehensive comparisons to other classes of methods (e.g., proximal gradient, stochastic gradient).  
- Limitation: does not discuss broader potential applications beyond the two presented examples.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication (significant contribution to convex optimization).  
- Pro: novel algorithm, thorough analysis, and demonstrated effectiveness on two applications.  
- Con: insufficient comparative evaluation and limited discussion of wider applicability.
