- Proposes PDMM, a novel parallel randomized block coordinate method for optimization with multi-block linear constraints.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity results for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM can perform randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods.  
- Paper is well-written with clear introduction; technical analysis and proofs provided in the supplement.  
- Detailed comparison provided with ADMM and its variants.  
- Strength: method handles multi-block linear constraints in parallel randomized fashion.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees (global convergence and iteration complexity).  
- Strength: empirical effectiveness demonstrated in two applications.  
- Weakness: assumes objective is sum of block-separable convex functions, limiting applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of PDMM’s computational complexity.  
- Weakness: no clear comparison with other parallel optimization methods beyond ADMM variants.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, theoretical guarantees, strong experimental results.  
- Arguments for rejection: restrictive separability assumption, missing complexity analysis, insufficient comparison to other parallel methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revisions requested: add detailed computational complexity analysis, compare clearly with other parallel methods, and clarify assumptions (block-separable convexity).
