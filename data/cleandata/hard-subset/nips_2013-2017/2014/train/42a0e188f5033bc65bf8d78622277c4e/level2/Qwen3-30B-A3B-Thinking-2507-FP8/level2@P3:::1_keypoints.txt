- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for multi-block linear-constrained optimization that updates multiple blocks in parallel.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size under reasonable assumptions.  
- Demonstrates PDMM handles overlapping blocks, extending applicability beyond standard randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Shows empirical superiority over state-of-the-art methods (ADMM, GSADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM) on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso in runtime and accuracy.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis linking sparsity and block count to step-size selection, a novel and useful contribution.  
- Clarifies connections to existing methods (sADMM and PJADMM), showing them as special cases and highlighting the role of dual backward steps.  
- Technical results are sound and valuable for large-scale parallel optimization where multi-block ADMM variants lacked guarantees.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation and a strong, contextualized introduction.  
- Experimental design is thorough with appropriate baselines and meaningful applications demonstrating practical utility.  
- Originality: advances over RBSUMM by updating multiple blocks simultaneously and enabling overlapping-block handling.  
- Significance: offers both theoretical advances and practical performance gains for scalable machine-learning optimization.  
- Minor suggestions: strengthen Section 2.2 with a concrete example of sparsity’s effect on step-size and performance.  
- Minor suggestions: add discussion of computational complexity for overlapping vs non-overlapping cases.  
- Minor suggestions: more explicitly address the trade-off between parallelism (blocks updated) and per-iteration cost in distributed environments.
