- Introduces PDMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints common in ML (e.g., RPCA, overlapping group lasso).  
- Employs parallel randomized block coordinate descent with a backward dual update to stabilize multi-block ADMM.  
- Targets the known issue that naive multi-block ADMM extensions lack convergence guarantees.  
- Frames PDMM as a unifying generalization of existing methods like sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Provides global convergence proof and O(1/T) iteration complexity.  
- Experiments show competitive performance vs ADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Ablation on number of randomized blocks K reveals a non-monotonic impact on runtime, highlighting the need for tuning.  
- Paper clearly motivates the problem and relates to limitations of prior multi-block ADMM variants.  
- Insufficient exploration of sensitivity to step-size parameters (τi, νi).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in dataset scale and structural variety.  
- Lacks comparisons to more recent or sophisticated solvers for the studied applications.  
- Notation is dense and reduces readability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify practical implications and parameter-tuning guidance.
