- Addresses minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints and the instability of direct multi-block ADMM extensions.  
- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM that updates a random subset of K blocks each iteration.  
- Introduces a novel "dual backward step" to stabilize parallel updates.  
- Presents an adaptive step-size rule that depends on constraint matrix sparsity and the parallelism level K.  
- Establishes global convergence and an O(1/T) iteration complexity under standard assumptions.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying framework.  
- Extends applicability to problems with overlapping variable groups, addressing a known challenge.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and assumptions are standard; claims are well-supported by theory and experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and situates contributions clearly in related work.  
- Suggests adding high-level intuition in the main text for the specific step-size formulas τ_i and ν_i.  
- The algorithmic combination (randomized blocks, parallelism, dual backward step, adaptive steps) is an original and meaningful contribution.  
- Results are significant for scalable, parallel optimization in machine learning and likely to impact practice and research.  
- Minor concern: the step-size formula is complex and experiments indicate additional tuning can further improve performance.
