- Summary: Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant with a "dual backward step" and step sizes depending on number of updated blocks K and per-row sparsity di, proving global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on multi-block ADMM literature and unifies sADMM and PJADMM as special cases, making damped/over-relaxed dual updates explicit and sparsity-aware.

- Conceptual clarity: Identifies implicit dual ascent in the primal quadratic term and stabilizes it via an explicit backward dual step.

- Generality: Supports randomized parallel updates for arbitrary K and handles overlapping groups via variable duplication, with favorable step-size rules (e.g., τi=0.5).

- Theoretical contribution: Provides global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity with constant step sizes and reductions to known ADMM variants.

- Practical guidance: Presents step-size formulas τi and νi that incorporate K and di, with intuitive discussion of their behavior.

- Empirical results: Shows favorable performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso versus sADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM, and studies how K affects runtime.

- Clarity issue: Key auxiliary quantities (Pt, Q, βi) and Lyapunov/residual specifics are deferred to the supplement, reducing readability.

- Parameter tuning: Bregman terms (ηjt, φjt) for inexact PDMM are problem-dependent with limited guidance; sensitivity analyses for ρ, τi, νi are limited.

- Experimental scope/fairness: All experiments are sequential MATLAB; lacks multi-threaded/distributed runs or simulated parallel speedup and broader baselines (e.g., proximal/nuclear-norm solvers for RPCA).

- Novelty: Moderate—combines known ingredients (splitting, proximal regularization) but contributes a sparsity- and K-aware calibrated dual backward step with proofs.

- Overlapping blocks nuance: Uses standard duplication/equality formulation; claim about open randomized overlapping BCD should cite proximal-gradient alternatives and be nuanced.

- Technical soundness: Theoretical results appear correct and align with recent ADMM analyses, though some proofs/details should be more accessible in the main text.

- Experimental robustness: Results support claims but would benefit from larger-scale and true parallel experiments and more standardized baseline tuning.

- Pros for acceptance: Clear conceptual contribution, unified view of sADMM/PJADMM, constant-step convergence guarantees, and promising empirical improvements.

- Cons for acceptance: Limited parallel experiments, deferred theoretical details in supplement, insufficient practical guidance for inexact PDMM, and narrower comparisons.

- Suggested improvements: Provide concrete defaults/heuristics for ηjt and φjt, add sensitivity studies for ρ/τi/νi, move explicit forms of Pt/Q/βi into main text, and include at least one parallel/multi-core experiment.

- Suggested experimental additions: Expand RPCA baselines (proximal/nuclear solvers), report wall-clock comparisons and robust tuning protocols, and clarify best settings across methods.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — useful and principled variant with solid theory and promising experiments, but clarity and experimental breadth should be improved.
