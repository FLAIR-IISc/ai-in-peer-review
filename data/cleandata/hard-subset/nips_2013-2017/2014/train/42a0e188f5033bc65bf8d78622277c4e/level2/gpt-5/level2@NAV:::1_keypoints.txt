- Proposes PDMM: randomized Jacobi-style primal-dual method updating K primal blocks in parallel with a dual “backward step” and sparsity/K-aware step sizes.  
- Theoretical results: global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity under standard convex assumptions with constant step sizes.  
- Unifies prior multi-block ADMM variants, showing sADMM and PJADMM as limiting cases.  
- Empirical demonstrations on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show promising improvements over sADMM and RBSUMM and competitive performance vs GSADMM.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated algorithmic innovation addressing known instability of Jacobi multi-block ADMM.  
- Strength: solid, technically sound theory with insightful role of dual step parameters τi, νi.  
- Strength: unifying perspective clarifies when small/large dual steps are appropriate and handles overlapping blocks randomly.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to Matlab sequential implementation and mostly synthetic data, lacking true parallel/distributed validation.  
- Weakness: no parallel/multi-core wall-clock or communication/synchronization analysis to validate primary appeal of PDMM.  
- Weakness: key baselines missing empirically (PJADMM, linearized ADMM, Chambolle–Pock variants).  
- Weakness: practical guidance for choosing K and mapping sparsity to τi, νi is insufficient and constants/tuning are opaque.  
- Weakness: presentation issues (typos, dense notation) and missing algorithmic pseudocode impede reproducibility despite a supplement.  
- Suggestion: add parallel/distributed evaluations with scalability and communication analysis vs K and sparsity.  
- Suggestion: include PJADMM and additional primal-dual baselines with tuning.  
- Suggestion: provide pseudocode, a practitioner’s guide for τi/νi/K, and consider adaptive schemes.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on real datasets and release code.  
- Overall: high originality and potentially strong significance, but empirical scope needs strengthening for full impact; promising for acceptance with stronger experiments.
