- Problem: Studies convex block-separable optimization with linear constraints where multi-block ADMM can diverge.  
- Proposal: Introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate method that updates K primal blocks and all dual blocks with a "dual backward step."  
- Step-size design: Chooses τi and νi based on number of updated blocks K and constraint sparsity di, enabling larger safe dual steps for sparse A.  
- Special cases: Shows sADMM and PJADMM are extreme instances of PDMM (small vs large proximal/Bregman regularization).  
- Theory: Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate for exact and Bregman-linearized updates with constant step sizes.  
- Insight: Identifies implicit dual ascent from the quadratic penalty as the instability source in parallel Jacobi ADMM and counters it with an explicit dual backward step.  
- Practicality: Randomized parallel updates can exploit parallel hardware; paper gives guidance on choosing K and group sampling.  
- Empirics: On RPCA and overlapping group lasso, PDMM variants are competitive or superior in iterations and runtime, and varying K is studied.  
- Strength: Provides a unifying view clarifying coupling between primal progress and dual step sizes.  
- Strength: Step-size dependence on sparsity is principled and particularly useful for overlapping group models (di = 2).  
- Strength: Potential function and residuals are explicit and proofs appear careful and aligned with prior ADMM analyses.  
- Weakness (essential): Experiments are sequential Matlab runs; no multi-core/parallel experiments to substantiate parallel scalability claims.  
- Weakness (essential): sADMM baseline is implemented without a dual update (τi = 0), making comparisons potentially unfair; PJADMM with tuned proximal weights is missing.  
- Weakness (essential): Sampling scheme in analysis is unclear—need to specify with/without replacement and whether grouped/cyclic sampling is covered theoretically.  
- Suggestion (optional): Provide practical guidance and sensitivity analysis for tuning ρ and Bregman parameters (ηjt).  
- Suggestion (optional): Include per-iteration complexity breakdown (proximal calls, matvecs, synchronization) to interpret time vs iteration.  
- Suggestion (optional): Report last-iterate (non-ergodic) convergence behavior empirically.  
- Suggestion (optional): Relate the dual backward step to primal-dual hybrid gradient/Chambolle–Pock and extragradient methods.  
- Quality: Algorithmic design and assumptions are sound; inexact/Bregman framework is standard.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear but would benefit from a concise algorithm box and a table mapping PDMM to special cases.  
- Originality: The combination of randomized parallel updates, analyzable sparsity-dependent dual backward step, and unification of sADMM/PJADMM is novel.  
- Significance: Addresses an important issue in multi-block ADMM and offers design principles likely useful in practice, especially for overlapping group problems.  
- Acceptance arguments: Clear fix for parallel Jacobi ADMM instability, principled step-size scheme, solid theory, unification of variants, and strong empirical performance.  
- Rejection arguments: Lack of parallel scalability experiments, incomplete/fair baseline comparisons, and insufficient practical tuning details.  
- Minor fixes: Correct typos (e.g., “seperable” → “separable”), complete bibliographic entries, and clarify definition/dimensions of U in Section 2.3.
