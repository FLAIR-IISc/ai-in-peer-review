- Problem: addresses multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints where naive ADMM extensions fail to converge.  
- Proposal: introduces PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant.  
- Core idea 1: dual backward step uses a conservative intermediate dual variable to stabilize parallel updates.  
- Core idea 2: adaptive step-size rules (τi, νi) incorporate constraint sparsity and number of parallel blocks K.  
- Core idea 3: flexible randomized selection of any K (1..J) blocks for parallel updates.  
- Theory: proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size under standard assumptions.  
- Unification: shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying the role of the backward step.  
- Experiments: PDMM outperforms GSADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso; includes K ablation.  
- Significance: novel, principled way to parallelize ADMM for multi-block problems—important to optimization/ML communities.  
- Theoretical quality: rigorous, clean analysis with practical constant step sizes and rate matching first-order methods.  
- Clarity: well-written, motivates algorithm via failure modes and explains key parameters and connections to prior work.  
- Empirical validation strength: convincing baselines, challenging problems, and practical guidance from ablations.  
- Weakness 1: paper lacks intuitive explanation in main text for the specific τi and νi choices.  
- Weakness 2: experiments were sequential; lacks multi-core wall-clock speedup demonstrations for PDMM’s parallelism.  
- Weakness 3: unclear if theoretical guarantees extend to the cyclic permutation (without-replacement) sampling used in experiments.  
- Weakness 4: minor confusion about sADMM parameter correspondence in the RPCA experiment—needs clarification.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, theoretically and empirically solid paper with minor clarifications needed; strongly recommend acceptance.
