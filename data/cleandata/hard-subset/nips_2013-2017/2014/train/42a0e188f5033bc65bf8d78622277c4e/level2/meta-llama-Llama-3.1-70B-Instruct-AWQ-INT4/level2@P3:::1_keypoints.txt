- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate extension of ADMM for multi-block linear-constrained problems using parallel block updates and a backward dual step.
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with a constant step size.
- Shows PDMM can handle overlapping block problems and demonstrates it on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Novelty: PDMM is an original multi-block extension of ADMM.
- Theoretical strength: rigorous convergence and complexity results.
- Experimental strength: empirical effectiveness shown on two representative applications.
- Good literature connections: situates PDMM relative to ADMM and its variants.
- Paper is highly technical and may be difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Relies on assumptions (e.g., existence of a KKT point) that may not always hold in practice.
- Limited experimental scope: only two applications, so general effectiveness is not fully demonstrated.
- Significance: method has potential broad impact in machine learning and signal processing for multi-block optimization.
- Clarity: well-written and organized but needs more intuitive explanations and examples for accessibility.
- Quality: high-quality analysis and well-presented experiments.
- For acceptance: introduces a new method with solid theoretical guarantees and supporting experiments.
- Against acceptance: steep technical barrier for readers, strong assumptions, and limited empirical validation.
- Recommendations: provide more intuition/examples, expand experimental evaluation, and explicitly discuss method limitations and assumptions.
