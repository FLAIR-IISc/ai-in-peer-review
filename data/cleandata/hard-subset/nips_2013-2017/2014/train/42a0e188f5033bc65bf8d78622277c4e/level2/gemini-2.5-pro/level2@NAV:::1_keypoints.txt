- Problem: Minimizes block-separable convex functions with multi-block linear constraints, relevant for large-scale ML.  
- Proposal: Introduces PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM that updates a random subset of K blocks in parallel with a dual backward step.  
- Theoretical result: Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with a constant step size under standard assumptions.  
- Novelty: Handles overlapping blocks in a randomized coordinate setting, addressing an open problem for standard randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Unification: Shows existing methods (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) are special cases of PDMM.  
- Technical strength: Derives step sizes that exploit matrix sparsity and the number of blocks updated (K).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written; motivation for the dual backward step and connections to prior work are clearly explained.  
- Empirical evidence: Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show practical advantages over baselines.  
- Weakness: Lacks guidance on selecting the crucial hyperparameter K and its trade-offs between per-iteration cost and convergence.  
- Weakness: Experiments were run sequentially despite PDMM’s parallel design; missing discussion of expected parallel speedup, communication costs, and synchronization.
