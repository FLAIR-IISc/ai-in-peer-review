- Proposes PDMM, a novel parallel randomized block ADMM extension for multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a dual backward step to ensure convergence and tunes step sizes based on constraint sparsity and number of randomly selected blocks.  
- Proves global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity for constant step sizes.  
- Provides a unified framework showing sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM.  
- Demonstrates empirical ability to handle overlapping groups in a randomized block coordinate fashion.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with careful convergence proofs and step-size selection.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show consistent runtime improvements over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and well-situated in prior ADMM literature.  
- Lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for the overlapping groups case despite empirical evidence.  
- Does not adequately discuss or distinguish PDMM from the recent RBCADMM work.  
- Experimental comparisons should include more recent state-of-the-art benchmarks.  
- The necessity and intuition behind the dual backward step (νi) are not clearly explained.  
- Recommended improvements: provide theory for overlapping groups, compare to recent methods, clarify dual-step intuition, and detail differences from RBCADMM.  
- Overall significance is high; the method is practically useful and the paper merits consideration for acceptance.
