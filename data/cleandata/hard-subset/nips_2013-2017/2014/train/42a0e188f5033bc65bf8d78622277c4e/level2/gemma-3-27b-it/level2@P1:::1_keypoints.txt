- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses the challenge of extending ADMM to multiple blocks by randomly updating blocks in parallel for potential speedup.  
- Introduces a backward step on the dual update to mitigate convergence issues of parallel Jacobi-type updates.  
- Provides convergence analysis and iteration complexity guarantees, and draws connections to sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Empirically evaluated on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, showing promising performance versus state-of-the-art.  
- Introduction is well-motivated and effectively positions PDMM within existing literature.  
- Notation is dense and sometimes difficult to follow, hindering accessibility.  
- Explanation for step size choices (τi, νi) feels hand-wavy and lacks intuitive justification.  
- Reliance on existing implementations and a referenced pre-print raises reproducibility concerns and omits key details.  
- Experimental comparisons are not fully comprehensive; GSADMM tuning details are unclear.  
- Missing explanation for the observed runtime decrease after K=61 in the overlapping group lasso experiment.  
- Paper lacks a detailed discussion of PDMM’s limitations and conditions where it may perform poorly.  
- Strengths: technically sound with theoretical guarantees, relevant problem focus, novel method with motivating connections, and encouraging experiments.  
- Weaknesses: presentation clarity, incomplete experimental evaluation, and reliance on external pre-print for important details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — improve notation, expand experimental comparisons, and include key details from the pre-print.
