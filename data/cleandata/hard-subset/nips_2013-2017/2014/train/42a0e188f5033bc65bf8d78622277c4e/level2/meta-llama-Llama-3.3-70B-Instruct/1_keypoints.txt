- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for multi-block linear-constrained optimization.
- Main claim: PDMM achieves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with a constant step size.
- Theoretical support: convergence proofs under simple assumptions (existence of KKT point, convex objective).
- Experimental support: faster convergence than existing methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Handles overlapping blocks and is parallelizable, making it suitable for large-scale problems.
- Detailed analysis includes connections to ADMM and its variants.
- Novelty: parallel handling of multi-block linear constraints and improved empirical efficiency.
- Completeness: thorough theoretical analysis, experiments, and discussion of limitations.
- Limitations: may not suit all problem types; requires careful tuning of step size and block-update count.
- Missing comparison: lacks evaluation against other state-of-the-art methods for linear-constrained problems.
- Future work suggested: extend PDMM to non-convex objectives and develop more efficient large-scale solvers.
- Overall assessment: significant, well-supported contribution that is clearly written and implementable.
