- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel block-update generalization of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Core idea: randomly update multiple blocks in parallel, inspired by randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Introduces a backward step on the dual update and leverages sparsity in updates.  
- Provides global convergence guarantees and iteration complexity analysis.  
- Connects PDMM to existing multi-block ADMM variants (sADMM, PJADMM), clarifying relationships.  
- Demonstrates improved empirical performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Addresses the important challenge that naive multi-block ADMM (e.g., Gauss–Seidel) can fail to converge.  
- Theoretical analysis is a strong contribution, but intuition for why randomization improves convergence is insufficient.  
- Assumes existence of a KKT point; implications for ill-conditioned or practical problems are not discussed.  
- Parameter tuning (e.g., ρ, τi, νi) is under-specified, harming reproducibility.  
- Relationship between step-size parameters and problem properties (sparsity, block size) needs deeper exploration.  
- No computational cost/per-iteration complexity analysis is provided to compare practical efficiency.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and datasets; broader comparisons to recent optimized solvers are needed.  
- The claim that PDMM is “faster than existing methods” is too strong given the limited experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method with theory, clear links to prior work, and promising results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, missing tuning and cost details, and weak intuition for randomized updates; overall borderline pending revisions.
