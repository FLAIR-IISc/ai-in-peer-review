This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and the iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size. They also show that PDMM can do randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks. Experimental results demonstrate that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed method. The technical analysis and proofs are provided in the supplement, which makes the paper easy to follow. The authors also provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, including Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and its variants.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel parallel randomized block coordinate method that can handle multi-block linear constraints.
* They establish the global convergence and the iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in two applications.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes that the objective function is a sum of block-separable convex functions, which may not be the case in all applications.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of PDMM.
* The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other parallel optimization methods.
