**Paper Review: Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a new algorithm, Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), for solving block-separable convex optimization problems subject to linear constraints. PDMM is a parallel randomized block coordinate variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM). The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with a constant step size. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in solving robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso problems.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. PDMM is a novel parallel randomized block coordinate method that can solve block-separable convex optimization problems subject to linear constraints.
2. PDMM has a global convergence rate of O(1/T) with a constant step size.
3. PDMM is faster than existing methods, such as ADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM, in solving robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso problems.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis of PDMM, including the derivation of the algorithm, convergence proofs, and iteration complexity analysis. They also provide experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in solving two applications.

The theoretical analysis is sound, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the algorithm and its properties. The convergence proofs are based on standard techniques in convex optimization, and the iteration complexity analysis is thorough.

The experimental results are also convincing, showing that PDMM outperforms existing methods in terms of convergence rate and runtime.

**Usefulness**

PDMM has the potential to be a useful tool for solving large-scale convex optimization problems subject to linear constraints. The algorithm's ability to parallelize the computation and its fast convergence rate make it an attractive option for many applications.

**Completeness**
