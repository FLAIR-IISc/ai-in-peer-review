- Summary: Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual backward step enabling arbitrary-block updates, O(1/T) convergence, and includes sADMM/PJADMM as special cases; applied to RPCA and overlapping group lasso.

- Theoretical contribution: Establishes convergence for a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM with constant step sizes and links PDMM to other ADMM variants.

- Novel algorithmic insight: Introduces a dual backward step to control overly aggressive dual updates from implicit dual ascent in primal Jacobi updates.

- Practical relevance: Handles overlapping group lasso where standard block methods fail; empirical results show advantages on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.

- Clear exposition: Paper is well-organized with clear connections to prior work.

- Weakness — limited comparisons: Does not thoroughly compare with recent ADMM/BSUMM variants (e.g., parallel multi-block ADMM [10], BSUMM [14]) in experiments.

- Weakness — experimental detail: Lacks actual runtime numbers and concrete speedup comparisons (e.g., versus ADMM/GSADMM).

- Weakness — dense theory presentation: Theoretical analysis is dense and would benefit from higher-level intuition before detailed proofs.

- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: Unclear how robust PDMM is to suboptimal choices of τ_i and ν_i beyond the theoretically derived settings.

- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous convergence proofs and empirical support.

- Clarity: Generally clear notation and flow, though theoretical parts need more accessibility.

- Originality: Presents a novel randomized parallel ADMM variant with a unifying view of related methods.

- Significance: Addresses practical large-scale optimization challenges and shows potential for broad adoption.

- Recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical and practical contributions despite some empirical and exposition gaps.

- Requested revisions: Add detailed runtime comparisons, discuss parameter sensitivity, and provide high-level intuition for key theoretical results.
