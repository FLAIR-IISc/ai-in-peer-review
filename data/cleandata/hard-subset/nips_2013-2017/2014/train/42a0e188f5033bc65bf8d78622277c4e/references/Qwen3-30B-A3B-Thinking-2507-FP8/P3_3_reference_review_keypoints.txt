- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant allowing parallel updates, dual backward step, and sparsity-aware step sizes for linearly-constrained block-separable convex problems.

- Theoretical contribution: unifies sADMM and PJADMM, provides rigorous global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.

- Problem relevance: well-motivated application to overlapping group lasso and structured sparsity, highlighting handling of overlapping blocks.

- Empirical results: shows runtime advantages on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, especially with appropriate number of blocks and parallelization.

- Novelty overstatement: claim that RBCD for overlapping blocks is still open is inaccurate; recent work (e.g., Zhao et al. 2018) addresses this and needs clarification.

- Incomplete experimental comparison: missing baselines such as Deng et al.'s Parallel ADMM (2017) and Richtarik & Takac (2016); comparisons to PA-APG and S-APG lack context.

- Lack of practical guidance: step-size formulas are given but implementation guidance and sensitivity analysis are insufficient; claim of matching ADMM rates needs clearer lower-bound context.

- Limited scalability demonstration: experiments use relatively small problems (m=1000, n=5000), not convincingly showing large-scale parallel benefits.

- Overall assessment: technically sound, meaningful unified perspective on Jacobian ADMM variants with promising empirical support.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify relation to existing work, expand experimental comparisons, and improve practical implementation guidance.
