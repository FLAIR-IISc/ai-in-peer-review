- PDMM: randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual backward step and sparsity/K-dependent dual step sizes τi, supporting inexact Bregman-updates and achieving O(1/T) ergodic convergence.  
- Situates among multi-block ADMMs, recovers sADMM and PJADMM as special cases; misses explicit discussion of connections to extragradient/primal–dual methods.  
- Conceptual novelty: identifies implicit dual ascent from Jacobi primal updates and proposes a tunable dual backward step to stabilize and accelerate convergence.  
- Unifying framework: clarifies when dual steps should be conservative vs. aggressive and supports inexact updates with guidance on ψ/ϕ choices.  
- Theory: proves global convergence with explicit τi, νi tuned to K and row-sparsity di and provides an O(1/T) ergodic rate with concrete constants.  
- Practical insight: sparsity- and K-aware step sizes are intuitive and useful, yielding large gains for overlapping structures (e.g., di=2).  
- Empirics: synthetic RPCA and overlapping-group lasso show faster convergence than sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM, with informative K-sweep studies.  
- No parallel validation: experiments are sequential MATLAB runs with no multicore/distributed implementation, scaling, or communication cost analysis.  
- Baseline/tuning concerns: some baselines appear underpowered or unfairly tuned (e.g., sADMM τ=0, RBSUMM schedule); ρ and other hyperparameters may not be consistently tuned.  
- Missing related-work connections: fails to cite/discuss extragradient/primal–dual hybrid methods and ADMM over-relaxation, weakening positioning.  
- Clarity gaps: key technical quantities (Pt, Q) are not defined in the main text and notation is heavy/inconsistent; intuition for τi formula and inexact conditions is limited.  
- Limited experimental scope: only synthetic data, no real-world tests, no ablation isolating the backward step or sparsity-aware τi, and no sensitivity analysis for ρ.  
- Rate limitations: only ergodic O(1/T) shown; no non-ergodic or linear rates under stronger assumptions and constants may scale conservatively with J/K.  
- Quality assessment: algorithm and proofs appear sound but important definitions are deferred to the supplement; empirical evaluation is promising but narrow.  
- Readability: generally readable with helpful intuition but marred by heavy notation, missing definitions, and typos; would benefit from clearer derivations and a diagram.  
- Originality verdict: novel combination of randomized parallel updates with a dual backward step and sparsity/K-dependent steps, but should make relation to primal–dual/extra-gradient methods explicit.  
- Significance: broad and important problem class; PDMM could be practically valuable if validated in parallel settings and on real workloads.  
- Suggested improvements: add parallel implementation and scaling/communication results, ensure fair tuning and stronger baselines (e.g., IALM/SVT), include ablations (ν, τ, ρ), formalize inexact guarantees in main text, and add missing citations/fix notation.  
- Pros summary: principled stabilization of Jacobi ADMM, sparsity-/K-aware step sizes, unifies sADMM/PJADMM, supports inexact updates, and shows explicit convergence with promising results.  
- Cons summary: lacks parallel validation, limited synthetic experiments, questionable baseline tuning, missing related-work links, clarity/definition gaps, and only ergodic rates with possibly conservative constants.  
- Overall recommendation: solid and promising but needs better empirical validation and clarity; reviewer leans borderline accept if addressed, otherwise reject-to-revise.
