- PDMM: randomized block-coordinate method that can update any number of blocks in parallel for linearly-constrained, block-separable convex problems.  
- Key innovations: backward step on dual updates and sparsity-aware step-size selection to improve stability and scalability.  
- Theoretical result: convergence analysis showing O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso show favorable performance; includes ablation on K (number of blocks updated).  
- Builds on ADMM literature, addressing scaling and non-convergence issues in many-block ADMM variants.  
- Positions PDMM as a unifying generalization that recovers sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.  
- Relates PDMM to Gaussian-Seidel and Jacobian ADMM variants and randomized block coordinate descent work.  
- Novelty strength: flexible parallel updates combined with backward step and sparsity-aware steps is a significant contribution.  
- Theoretical strength: rigorous convergence proofs and a useful O(1/T) rate result.  
- Generalization strength: provides a unifying framework clarifying impacts of design choices in prior methods.  
- Empirical strength: practical effectiveness demonstrated and ablation study on K is insightful.  
- Motivation strength: clearly explains limitations of existing methods and rationale for PDMM.  
- Parameter-tuning weakness: interplay of τ_i, ν_i, K, and problem structure may require substantial tuning; sensitivity analysis is limited.  
- Comparison weakness: RPCA experiments lack detailed tuning info for baselines and no statistical significance testing.  
- Notation weakness: dense and occasionally confusing block indexing and matrix partitioning.  
- Bregman divergence section weakness: explanation is brief and needs more detail and examples for choices of φ_j.  
- Redundancy weakness: some explanations and method connections are repeated across the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, generalizes prior work, and shows promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical tuning complexity, insufficiently thorough/rigorous experimental comparisons, and clarity issues.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Revision suggestion: expand parameter-tuning discussion and propose automation strategies.  
- Revision suggestion: provide detailed tuning procedures for baselines and include statistical significance tests.  
- Revision suggestion: improve notation clarity, possibly with visual aids.  
- Revision suggestion: expand Bregman divergence section with concrete examples and justification of φ_j choices.  
- Revision suggestion: reduce repetitive content to streamline presentation.
