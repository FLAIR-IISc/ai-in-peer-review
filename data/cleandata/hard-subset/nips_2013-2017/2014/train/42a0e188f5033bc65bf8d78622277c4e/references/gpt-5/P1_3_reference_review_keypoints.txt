- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant allowing any K of J primal blocks to update in parallel with convergence guarantees.  
- Introduces a “dual backward step” ŷt = yt − νiρ(Aixt − ai) to damp implicit dual ascent from the quadratic penalty.  
- Sets sparsity- and K-aware dual step sizes τi = K/(K̃i(2J − K)) and νi = 1 − 1/K̃i (K̃i = min{di, K}) to exploit row-block sparsity and allow larger dual steps.  
- Provides an inexact PDMM using Bregman linearization to obtain closed-form primal updates and permit full dual steps with strong proximal weights.  
- Shows sADMM (all blocks, τi = 1/J, νi = 1 − 1/J) and PJADMM (strong proximal, τi = 1, νi = 0) are special cases of PDMM.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity and KKT existence assumptions.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM is competitive or faster than GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM, with benefits for larger K and sparse constraints.  
- Theoretical analysis is careful and matches state-of-the-art rates; sparsity dependence via di is a useful refinement.  
- The potential function uses matrices Pt and Q declared PSD but their concrete definitions or sufficient conditions are missing from the main text.  
- The derivation and intuition for the step-size formulas lack detailed explanation and explicit allowable ranges for ρ and ηjt, reducing reproducibility.  
- Inexact PDMM assumptions (Lipschitz gradients, matching strong convexity) are reasonable but offer limited practical guidance for choosing ηjt and φjt.  
- Experiments are sequential MATLAB runs despite parallelization being a main selling point; no parallel/wall-clock validation is provided.  
- Some baseline comparisons are questionable (sADMM implementation reportedly omits dual update; RBSUMM uses decreasing steps), raising fairness concerns.  
- Per-iteration cost and wall-clock times for all methods are not consistently reported; optimality gap and feasibility plots are lacking.  
- Readability suffers from heavy notation, typos, and reliance on appendices for key equivalence claims; Pt and Q should be defined in the main text.  
- Originality is moderate: builds on known randomized Jacobi ADMM and over-relaxation ideas but contributes a novel backward dual step with sparsity- and K-aware sizing and a unifying PDMM view.  
- Significance is high for large-scale structured ML problems, but broader adoption requires stronger empirical and parallel validation.  
- Pros: clear motivation, principled dual backward step, supports exact/inexact updates, unifies sADMM/PJADMM, solid convergence guarantees, demonstrates K effect.  
- Cons: sequential experiments, underspecified theoretical components (Pt/Q, parameter guidance), limited relation to related relaxation/back-substitution methods, presentation issues.  
- Ask authors to provide explicit Pt and Q constructions and discuss tightness of constants.  
- Ask about PDMM sensitivity to ρ, τi, νi and whether adaptive strategies exist.  
- Request quantitative relation between the backward step and standard ADMM over-relaxation parameter α.  
- Suggest including parallel/multi-core or distributed implementations to validate wall-clock speedups.  
- Recommend re-running RBSUMM and sADMM with proper dual updates and comparable tuning for fair comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: add a clear algorithm box with parameter defaults, specify ηjt and φjt choices, include parallel results and broader baselines, and fix typos and heavy notation.
