- Introduces PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant that adds a dual backward step, leverages constraint sparsity, and allows parallel updates of randomly selected blocks.
- Targets multi-block convex problems with linear constraints and addresses divergence issues of direct Jacobi ADMM updates.
- Provides rigorous theoretical results: global convergence and an iteration complexity bound.
- Empirically outperforms ADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso benchmarks.
- Establishes novel connections between PDMM, sADMM, and PJADMM, offering a unified view of these methods.
- Builds appropriately on prior ADMM literature (Boyd et al., Chen et al., Wang et al.) and motivates PDMM by highlighting limits of BSUMM and RBSUMM.
- Novelty: combines backward dual step, sparsity-aware step sizes, and randomized block selection in a new way.
- Practical relevance: demonstrates tangible performance gains on relevant large-scale problems.
- Well-motivated design choices and a thorough study of the effect of the number of selected blocks K.
- Parameter tuning is sensitive; suggested τi and νi formulas need more guidance and discussion.
- Computational overhead from randomized block selection is not fully analyzed, raising concerns for very large-scale problems.
- Comparisons are limited; lacks evaluation against specialized state-of-the-art solvers for RPCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Notation (block/matrix indexing) is sometimes inconsistent and can hinder readability.
- Important proof details are deferred to the supplement, making full verification harder within the main paper.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically strong, and clarifies connections in the ADMM family.
- Arguments against acceptance: parameter sensitivity, unexamined randomization cost, incomplete solver comparisons, and minor notational issues.
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve notation, parameter-selection guidance, computational-cost analysis, and broader comparisons.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
