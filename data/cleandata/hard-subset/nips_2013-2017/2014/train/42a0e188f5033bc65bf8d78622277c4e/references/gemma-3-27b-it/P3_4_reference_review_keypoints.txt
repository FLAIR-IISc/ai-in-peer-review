- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept — PDMM is a valuable, well-analyzed contribution to ADMM-based optimization.  
- Problem addressed: Minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints, common in RPCA and group lasso.  
- Method: Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual “backward step” to ensure convergence.  
- Novelty/positioning: Generalizes and connects to sADMM and PJADMM, filling gaps between Gauss-Seidel and Jacobian ADMM variants.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides global convergence guarantees and O(1/T) iteration complexity with rigorous analysis.  
- Practicality: Designed for parallelization and leverages sparsity in constraint matrix A for step-size tuning.  
- Empirical validation: Competitive experimental results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, plus informative ablation studies on K.  
- Open problem: Offers a promising solution for applying randomized block coordinate descent to overlapping block problems.  
- Clarity issues: Paper is dense with heavy notation; some sections (e.g., Bregman divergences) need more intuitive explanation and examples.  
- Parameter tuning: Sensitivity to τi and νi not thoroughly investigated; practical tuning guidance lacking.  
- Experimental scope: Experiments limited to two applications; broader empirical evaluation would strengthen claims.  
- Relation to other methods: Needs clearer comparison to other randomized coordinate/stochastic-gradient methods in theory and practice.  
- Specific suggestions: Better motivate Bregman divergences, discuss choice of K for overlapping group lasso, state theorem assumptions explicitly.  
- Experimental suggestions: Report runtime, per-iteration computational cost, and include confidence intervals or standard deviations.
