- Introduces PDMM, a randomized block coordinate descent algorithm for linearly constrained, block-separable convex problems.  
- Tackles multi-block ADMM non-convergence by adding a dual "backward step" and sparsity-informed adaptive step sizes.  
- Enables parallel updates of an arbitrary number of primal blocks with convergence guarantees.  
- Proves O(1/T) iteration complexity under standard ADMM-like assumptions.  
- Shows empirical competitiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso against ADMM variants.  
- Unifies PDMM with sADMM and PJADMM, showing they are special cases and offering deeper theoretical connections.  
- Strength: directly addresses a key limitation of multi-block ADMM with a practical, theoretically grounded fix.  
- Strength: novel algorithmic components — dual backward step, adaptive sparsity-based step sizes, and parallel randomized updates.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis and reasonable assumptions.  
- Strength: valuable unified framework linking multiple ADMM variants.  
- Strength: encouraging empirical validation across two application domains.  
- Strength: clear motivation, connection to prior work, and well-structured writing.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to K (number of selected blocks) is implied but not thoroughly explored; ablation study needed.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons — should include more recent accelerated ADMM variants and scalable libraries.  
- Weakness: some experimental details (e.g., competing algorithms’ parameters) are missing, harming reproducibility; sADMM code claim needs verification.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational cost per iteration and parallelization overhead (message gathering).  
- Weakness: the role and intuition behind using Bregman divergences is not fully explained.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, addresses an important open problem, strong convergence guarantees, and supportive experiments.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient exploration of K sensitivity, limited baseline comparisons, missing experimental details, and incomplete computational cost analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution deserving acceptance pending minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting K ablation, more experimental details, broader comparisons, and clarification on sADMM code.
