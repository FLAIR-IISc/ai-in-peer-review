- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM with a dual backward step, sparsity-aware step sizes, and support for updating any number of blocks in parallel.  
- Targets limitations of existing ADMM variants, addressing Gauss-Seidel convergence issues and restrictive Jacobian step sizes.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity under standard KKT existence assumptions.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous with proofs in the supplement and match best-known ADMM rates.  
- Connects PDMM to prior methods, showing sADMM and PJADMM as special cases and providing principled step-size choices based on constraint sparsity and block count.  
- Algorithm design is sound; the dual backward step is justified via analysis of implicit dual ascent in Jacobi updates.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: PDMM outperforms ADMM, GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, especially for moderate block counts.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; algorithm description and connections to prior work are helpful, though theoretical sections are dense.  
- Step-size parameters (τi, νi) could use more intuitive explanation; parameter tuning appears somewhat ad hoc despite principled guidance.  
- Original contribution: novel unified framework that generalizes existing parallel ADMMs and introduces the dual backward step; novel application to overlapping group lasso.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental class of ML optimization problems with practical speedups and useful implementation guidance (sparsity-aware, randomized blocks).  
- Strengths: strong theory, novel method, practical step-size strategy, empirical superiority, clear links to existing work.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient analysis of parallel speedup and computational complexity; some reliance on a companion paper for details.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — substantial and well-motivated contribution to distributed convex optimization.
