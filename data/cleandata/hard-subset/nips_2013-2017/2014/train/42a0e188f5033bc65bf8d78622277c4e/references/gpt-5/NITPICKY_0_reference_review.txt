Summary
The paper proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate variant of ADMM for convex block-separable objectives under linear constraints. Key ideas: (i) a “dual backward step” (using an intermediate ŷ) to counter the implicit dual ascent induced by the quadratic penalty in Jacobi-style primal updates; (ii) step-sizes τi and νi that depend on the number of updated blocks K and the block-sparsity di of A; (iii) allowance for exact or inexact primal updates via a Bregman divergence term. The authors show PDMM recovers sADMM and PJADMM as extreme cases, provide global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate results, and give experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.

Relation to prior work
The work sits among multi-block ADMM variants (Gauss–Seidel and Jacobi), including BSUMM, sADMM (two-block with splitting), and proximal Jacobian ADMM. The “correction/back substitution” idea echoes stabilization in multi-block ADMM (e.g., He–Tao–Yuan Gaussian back substitution). The randomized “update K blocks” design is reminiscent of randomized block coordinate descent and randomized primal-dual methods, though the paper does not fully discuss connections to broader randomized primal–dual literature.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity in identifying implicit dual ascent in Jacobi updates and introducing a backward step to temper it.
- Step-size schedule tied to graph sparsity (di) and randomization level (K) is appealing and practically motivated; recovers sensible limits (e.g., τi=1/di, νi=1−1/di when K=J).
- Unification: sADMM and PJADMM as extreme cases of PDMM clarifies when larger dual steps are safe (when primal steps are more proximal).
- Convergence guarantees (global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate) under standard convex assumptions.
- Empirical studies on two representative applications; exploration of K’s effect.

Weaknesses and detailed issues
Technical correctness and completeness
- Equation (8) appears incorrect as written: νi = 1 − K̃i (missing denominator). Later text implies νi = 1 − 1/K̃i (so that νi = 0 for K=1 and νi=1−1/di for K=J). This must be fixed.
- Inconsistent τi,νi values reported: for PDMM3 the text lists (1/3, 1/3) in RPCA, but the formula suggests νi=2/3 when di=3 and K=3.
- Lagrangian (2) alternates between ρ‖⋯‖22 and (ρ/2)‖⋯‖22. Later derivations (e.g., (9)) mix these conventions. Please standardize and verify all constants.
- The Bregman linearization section: defining φjt = ϕjt − (1/ηjt)ψjt and using Bφjt = Bϕjt − (1/ηjt)Bψjt is problematic unless φjt is convex. The text asserts nonnegativity of Bφjt if “ϕjt is more convex than ψjt,” but the precise sufficient conditions (and associated norms) are not clearly stated nor proved. As presented, Bφjt need not be a Bregman divergence unless φjt is convex and differentiable.
- Several quantities in the analysis are undefined or opaque: Pt and Q are “some positive semidefinite matrices” but not specified; βi is variously written (e.g., βi = KJ K̃i, which is ambiguous). γi is given with tangled fractions and missing parentheses. Theorem 1 begins with an orphan “≤” and does not state the full inequality properly.
- The KKT and “optimality conditions” discussion (17)–(18) is confusing: Acj(xt+1j −xtj)=0 is not an optimality condition in general; it is a consequence only if specific residual terms vanish. The logic mixing sufficient conditions and consequences is imprecise.
- The connection proofs to sADMM and PJADMM are deferred to an external appendix [25]; without those derivations, the unification claims are not verifiable from this submission.

Clarity and notation
- Notational inconsistencies: Ai vs Ari; Acj vs Ajt; Aj vs Acj; x ∈ Rb×J (dimensionally incorrect); z definitions (zr i ∈ RmiJ) and z ∈ RJm unclear; use of “Arixt+1” vs “Aixt+1.” The notations for blocks, row/column partitions, and residual vectors should be carefully unified.
- Overlapping group lasso reformulation has multiple dimensional inconsistencies (U ∈ Rn×(bL), x ∈ Rb×J); needs correction.
- Many grammatical issues (“block-seperable,” “an noisy,” “addded,” “defiend,” “diver-gence,” “convegence,” “public available,” etc.). These hinder readability.

Experimental methodology
- All implementations are sequential, yet the main selling point is parallelism. Claims that PDMM would be faster in parallel are speculative without actual parallel experiments or at least simulated parallel timing (e.g., synchronized matrix-vector products cost, communication).
- Comparisons to sADMM appear unfair: authors state the public code lacks a dual update (τi=0), but then draw conclusions about sADMM’s slowness. Please implement sADMM properly as per the method described.
- “Best” results and tuning: sometimes τi,νi are set per (8), sometimes tuned to {(1/2,0), (1/3,1/2), (1/2,1/2)}; the tuning protocol and grids are not specified, raising fairness concerns.
- RPCA objective (24) is written with “min 1 ‖X1‖2F + γ2‖X2‖1 + γ3‖X3‖∗,” which seems malformed (missing 1/2 factor, and constants γ2,γ3 inconsistently formatted). Please correct and specify γ’s and ρ across methods.
- RBSUMM results are omitted because of non-convergence within 5000 iterations under certain schedules; broader tuning or citations of known working settings would make this comparison more informative. Also, report both primal and dual residuals and clarify the stopping rule (“residual < 1e−4” which residual?).
- No statistical variability (std/CI) reported despite randomization; some experiments “choose blocks cyclically instead of randomly,” deviating from claimed algorithm.

References and formatting
- References are often incomplete or malformed (missing years/venues, corrupted author lists, “ArXiv” without identifiers), and the style is inconsistent.
- Numerous typos, broken equations, and hyphenation errors throughout; footnote for dataset link appears mid-sentence.

Pros
- Addresses a practical and important gap (stable multi-block Jacobi ADMM with theory).
- Sparsity-aware step-size design is insightful.
- Unifying view of sADMM and PJADMM is valuable (if properly demonstrated).
- Convergence and iteration complexity results align with the literature.

Cons
- Multiple technical inconsistencies and undefined quantities in the core theory.
- Heavy reliance on external appendices; insufficient in-paper proofs.
- Clarity/notation/formatting issues pervasive.
- Experimental evaluation lacks true parallel results and has fairness concerns.

Questions for authors
1. Provide corrected, self-contained definitions of τi, νi (fix (8)), βi, Pt, Q, γi, and the full statements/proofs of Theorems 1–2 (or at least precise lemmas sufficient to verify them).
2. Clarify the Bregman linearization: precise conditions ensuring φjt is convex and Bφjt ≥ 0, and how ηjt is chosen.
3. Implement and compare to a correct sADMM baseline; explain tuning protocols for all methods, including ρ.
4. Provide actual parallel experiments or robust simulated timing accounting for communication.
5. Fix all dimensional inconsistencies in Section 2.3 and the RPCA objective.

Recommendation
Reject. While the core idea is interesting and potentially impactful, the submission has too many technical, clarity, and experimental issues to be accepted in its current form. With a thorough revision correcting formulas, completing proofs in the main text, cleaning notation/typos, and strengthening fair, parallel experiments, this work could be much stronger.

Confidence: High.
