- Proposes PDMM: a randomized Jacobi-style multi-block ADMM with a “dual backward step” to stabilize primal updates for linearly constrained convex problems with block-separable objectives.
- Dual step size and backward correction are chosen per dual row block as functions of K (number of primal blocks updated) and di (sparsity: nonzero block neighbors).
- Inexact PDMM uses Bregman divergences to linearize difficult terms and recover proximal/Jacobian-style updates.
- Provides global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates under standard convex assumptions, with explicit parameter choices τi = K/(K̃i(2J − K)) and νi = 1 − 1/K̃i (K̃i = min{di, K}).
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM, offering a unifying view of multi-block ADMM variants.
- Applications and experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM variants competitive or faster than ADMM/GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM in iterations/runtime (sequential runs).
- Strength: Conceptual novelty — dual backward step and sparsity-dependent per-row step sizes are insightful and practically useful.
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees with explicit parameter choices and accommodation of inexact updates.
- Strength: Unification of existing Jacobian ADMM variants clarifies when large dual steps are safe.
- Strength: Practical scope includes handling overlapping groups via standard splitting and explains larger permissible dual steps for structured problems.
- Weakness: Manuscript contains numerous typos and inconsistencies (e.g., “seperable,” inconsistent use of ρ vs ρ/2, typo in νi in Eq.(8)).
- Weakness: Some crucial objects (Pt, Q, γi) are introduced vaguely or without clear definitions; main text lacks derivation sketch for τi and νi.
- Weakness: Experiments run only sequentially — core parallelism/scalability claims lack parallel/distributed wall-clock results.
- Weakness: Baseline fairness issues — sADMM reported with an implementation lacking proper dual update; RBSUMM tuning seems limited and non-convergence claims need broader sweeps and diagnostics.
- Weakness: Limited experimental breadth/scale; missing ablations (backward step on/off) and systematic validation of sparsity (di) vs performance.
- Weakness: Guarantees are ergodic/in-expectation only; no non-ergodic, high-probability, or almost-sure rates and hidden constants in O(1/T) not discussed.
- Weakness: Related-work positioning could better connect the backward step to over-relaxation/damping/extragradient and PDHG/Chambolle–Pock literature.
- Quality: Technically sound with clear theorems in supplement, but main paper omits some definitions and proof sketches.
- Clarity: Organization reasonable but needs significant copyediting, corrected equations, clearer intuition for parameter choices, and complete bibliography entries.
- Originality: Randomized multi-block Jacobi with principled dual backward step and sparsity-dependent step sizes appears novel and useful.
- Significance: Potentially significant for large-scale structured optimization and ML, especially where overlapping structures and sparse A arise; parallel speedups would increase impact.
- Suggested fixes: correct Eq.(8) and ρ notation, define Pt/Q in main text, add derivation/intuition for τi/νi, and relate backward step to existing damping/extragradient methods.
- Suggested experiments: reimplement/tune baselines fairly, add parallel/distributed scaling experiments, ablations on νi, and systematic sparsity studies.
- Suggested reproducibility: clarify choices of ϕjt/ψjt and ηjt, provide guidance on Bregman kernels, and release code.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid idea and theory but needs polishing, stronger/parallel experiments, and fairer baseline comparisons to be a strong paper.
