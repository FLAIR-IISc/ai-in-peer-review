- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant with a backward dual step to stabilize multi-block parallel updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates under standard assumptions and analyzes step sizes leveraging constraint sparsity.  
- Demonstrates empirical performance on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso, comparing favorably to baselines.  
- Connects PDMM to existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM), showing they are special cases of the framework.  
- Positions the work well in the ADMM literature and includes comprehensive related references.  
- Novelty: introduces a genuinely new stabilization technique (backward dual step) for parallel ADMM.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides formal convergence proofs and iteration-complexity results.  
- Practical awareness: accounts for constraint sparsity when selecting step sizes.  
- Clear presentation: paper is dense but well-written with mostly consistent notation.  
- Empirical validation: experiments are promising and comparisons are well-conducted.  
- Parameter tuning: τi and νi formulas are given but their interaction with K and A’s sparsity is complex and hard to tune.  
- Inexact PDMM: Section 2.1 is underdeveloped; practical choices and benefits of Bregman divergences are not fully explored.  
- Experimental depth: experiments could be broader (sensitivity to sparsity, comparisons to more recent solvers).  
- Overlapping group lasso: discussion feels loosely connected to PDMM and not fully integrated into the main analysis.  
- Supplement reliance: several important proofs and parameter details are deferred to the supplement, hindering standalone evaluation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses instability in parallel ADMM, offers rigorous theory, practical benefits, and unifying connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical tuning complexity, incomplete inexactness discussion, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall: strong, theoretically sound contribution with promising experiments; minor weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting clearer parameter guidance, expanded inexact PDMM discussion, more comprehensive experiments, and tighter integration of overlapping group lasso.
