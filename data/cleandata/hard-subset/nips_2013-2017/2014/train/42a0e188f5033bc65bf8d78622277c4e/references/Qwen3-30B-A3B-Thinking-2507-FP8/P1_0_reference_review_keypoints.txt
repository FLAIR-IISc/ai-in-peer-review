- Introduces PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM with a dual backward step for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.
- Addresses convergence issues of Jacobi updates by adding a dual backward step and designing step sizes based on matrix sparsity and number of blocks updated.
- PDMM allows parallel updates of multiple blocks and achieves global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity using constant step sizes.
- Shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying the role of dual backward steps.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show consistent improvements in convergence speed and runtime versus baselines.
- Theoretical contribution: establishes convergence guarantees with constant step sizes, improving over methods needing decreasing step sizes or strict conditions.
- Algorithmic innovation: principled dual backward step and step-size choices are theoretically sound and practically useful.
- Empirical validation: well-designed experiments comparing multiple baselines; PDMM variants outperform competitors in runtime and convergence.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, logical flow, and reproducible detail.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed theoretical comparison of O(1/T) rate against sADMM, PJADMM, and related methods.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of parallel speedup and scalability with increasing processors.
- Weakness: experiments do not study sensitivity to deviations from the proposed step-size parameters (τi, νi).
- Weakness: could better position itself within the broader literature on randomized block coordinate methods.
- Overall: technically sound, original extension of ADMM variants with significant practical relevance for large-scale parallel optimization.
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses; makes meaningful theoretical and practical contributions.
