- The paper introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM that generalizes sADMM and PJADMM and handles overlapping blocks.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity and connects PDMM to existing methods, clarifying the role of the dual backward step.  
- Addresses a meaningful practical gap by enabling randomized parallel updates for problems with overlapping blocks (e.g., overlapping group lasso).  
- Well-structured and clearly written, with logical progression from formulation to analysis and experiments.  
- Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms competitors, especially under parallel execution, and analyzes the effect of block selection parameter K.  
- Lacks a clear comparison of PDMM’s convergence rate to competing ADMM variants despite proving O(1/T), which is also standard for ADMM.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: GSADMM is sequential while PDMM is parallel, so parallel vs. parallel comparisons are needed.  
- Insufficient practical guidance on choosing parameters (ηjt, τi, νi) for real-world use.  
- Theoretical assumptions about Bregman divergences and convexity conditions need clearer explanation and discussion of violations in practice.  
- Originality/significance: unifies existing ADMM variants and substantially advances randomized parallel BCD for overlapping-block problems.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with solid theory, motivation, and convincing experiments; minor issues are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical guarantees, practical relevance for large-scale ML with overlapping structure, unifying algorithmic framework, and strong experimental validation.  
- Key revision considerations: clarify rate comparisons, ensure fair parallel experimental baselines, provide parameter-selection guidance, and elaborate on practical implications of theoretical assumptions.
