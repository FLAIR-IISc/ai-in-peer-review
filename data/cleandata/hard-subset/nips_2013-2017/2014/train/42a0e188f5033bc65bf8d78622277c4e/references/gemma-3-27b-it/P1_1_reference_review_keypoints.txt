- Introduces PDMM: a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant that updates K blocks in parallel with a backward dual step and sparsity-aware step sizes.  
- Connects PDMM to existing methods, showing sADMM and PJADMM as special cases and situating work within multi-block ADMM literature.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM.  
- Novelty: randomized parallel updates and adaptive step sizes based on constraint sparsity.  
- Theoretical guarantees: rigorous convergence analysis for a randomized multi-block method.  
- Unifying framework: clarifies relationships among PDMM, sADMM, and PJADMM.  
- Practical consideration: leverages sparsity information to choose step sizes for large-scale problems.  
- Empirical validation: competitive results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Presentation: generally well-written with consistent (though dense) notation.  
- Weakness — parameter tuning: lacks detailed guidance and sensitivity analysis for τi, νi, and choice of K.  
- Weakness — complexity analysis: O(1/T) ignores per-iteration computational cost, especially for parallel updates.  
- Weakness — empirical scope: experiments limited to two applications and comparisons to some state-of-the-art methods are sparse.  
- Weakness — implementation details: missing specifics on parallelization approach (threads, MPI, etc.) harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness — notation: block/ index notation is cumbersome and could be simplified.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound algorithm with meaningful unification and promising experiments.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient parameter-tuning guidance, limited empirical evaluation, and lacking implementation/complexity details.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to address tuning, complexity analysis, implementation details, and broader experiments.
