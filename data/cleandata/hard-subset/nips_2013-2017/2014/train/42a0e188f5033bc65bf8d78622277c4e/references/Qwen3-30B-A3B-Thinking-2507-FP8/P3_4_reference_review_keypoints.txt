- Proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant enabling parallel block updates for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a dual backward step to enable parallel updates while maintaining convergence and explains why direct Jacobi updates fail.  
- Shows PDMM generalizes sADMM and PJADMM, providing new theoretical connections between existing methods.  
- Establishes global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity under standard assumptions.  
- Algorithm design accounts for practical issues like constraint matrix sparsity and number of blocks updated, improving real-world applicability.  
- Strong experimental validation on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, showing consistent improvements over baselines with careful parameter tuning.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear notation and logical flow from formulation to theory and experiments.  
- Lacks comparisons with some recent multi-block ADMM variants (e.g., Deng et al., 2016) and asynchronous ADMM methods.  
- No detailed computational complexity analysis showing scaling with number of blocks or problem size.  
- Limited analysis of sensitivity to parameter K (number of blocks updated per iteration) beyond empirical tuning.  
- Convergence relies on existence of a KKT point (Assumption 1), which may limit extensions to non-convex settings.  
- Overall: strong, novel theoretical and practical contribution that generalizes prior work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
