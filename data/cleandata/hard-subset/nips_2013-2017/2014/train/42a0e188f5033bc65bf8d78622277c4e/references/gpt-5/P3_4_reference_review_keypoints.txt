- Proposal: PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate method of multipliers that updates K random blocks with optional Bregman proximal terms and a “dual backward step.”
- Key design: Block-wise dual step sizes τ_i and backward params ν_i are explicit functions of K and row-sparsity d_i, allowing larger steps for sparse A.
- Theory: PDMM recovers sADMM and PJADMM as limiting cases and the authors prove global convergence with O(1/T) ergodic rates under standard convex assumptions.
- Empirics: Tested on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, showing favorable iteration/time performance versus GSADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM, and standard ADMM in the considered settings.
- Relation to prior work: Sits among multi-block ADMM variants and randomized block-coordinate methods; the dual backward step is similar to existing damping/back-substitution stability devices.
- Novelty: Main contributions are combining the dual backward step with step-size rules that depend on row degrees and K, and unifying sADMM/PJADMM within one framework.
- Quality (theory): Clear potential-function analysis and Lyapunov decrease, but key technical details (explicit P^t and Q) are missing from the main text.
- Quality (theory): The justification/derivation for the τ_i and ν_i formulas is only sketched; a tighter derivation or inequality is needed.
- Quality (theory): Inexact PDMM assumptions (Lipschitz/strong-convex constants) and practical guidance for tuning η_j are not made explicit.
- Quality (theory): Results are ergodic; non-ergodic rates or primal-dual gap guarantees are not discussed.
- Quality (experiments): Experiments explore effect of K and sparsity benefits, but all runs are sequential, undermining the parallelization claim.
- Quality (experiments): sADMM baseline appears unfairly implemented (τ_i=0); authors should run the proper sADMM parameters or remove the comparison.
- Quality (experiments): RBSUMM tuning seems insufficient; more systematic parameter tuning and reporting passes-through-data for randomized methods are needed.
- Clarity: Paper is well organized with good high-level intuition, but notationally heavy and contains several typos; P^t and Q should be defined in the main text.
- Reproducibility: Include at least one worked-out proximal choice for a common loss and practical sensitivity plots to aid users.
- Significance: PDMM is a useful flexible alternative for linearly constrained problems with sparse A and allows K>1 updates, but theoretical rates match standard O(1/T) and empirical parallel benefits are not demonstrated.
- Requests for authors: Define P^t and Q in main text and justify τ_i, ν_i; provide guidance for η_j and Bregman choices; fix sADMM baseline; add parallel/distributed experiments; discuss non-ergodic/primal-dual gap; clarify grouping/partitioning effects.
- Pros: Unifies sADMM and PJADMM; insightful dual backward step; sparsity-aware step-size design; provable global convergence without diminishing steps; applicable to overlapping group problems.
- Cons: Missing key technical definitions in main text; no true parallel experiments; some baselines unfairly implemented; limited parameter-selection guidance for inexact PDMM; writing needs polishing.
- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution with practical potential; lean weak accept contingent on fairer comparisons, at least one parallel experiment, and clearer presentation of theoretical elements.
