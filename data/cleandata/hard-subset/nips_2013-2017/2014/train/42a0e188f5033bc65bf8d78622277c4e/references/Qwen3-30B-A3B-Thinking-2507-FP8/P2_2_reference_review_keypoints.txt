- Proposes PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant that updates multiple blocks in parallel, adds a dual backward step, and uses adaptive step sizes.
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step sizes.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying theoretical framework.
- Derives step sizes (τi, νi) based on constraint-matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks; derivation is well-motivated and sound.
- Experimental evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrates superior performance vs. ADMM, GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM.
- Strong technical quality: rigorous analysis, complete proofs, and theory matched by experiments.
- Clear presentation of key innovations (dual backward step, adaptive step sizes) though some technical derivations (Section 3) need more explanation for non-experts.
- Experiments are thorough and figures clearly show convergence behavior, but they are sequential and do not fully demonstrate distributed parallelism benefits.
- The paper appropriately acknowledges trade-offs between subproblem complexity and parallelism.
- Original contribution: meaningful advance by combining randomized block updates with dual backward step and adaptive steps, resolving convergence issues of direct Jacobi updates.
- Notable application: solves an open problem for randomized block coordinate methods on overlapping group lasso.
- Significance: broadly applicable to distributed ML problems with linear constraints and competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
- Weaknesses: lack of distributed experiments to showcase parallelism, limited discussion of RBSUMM relation, and unexplored theoretical limits of adaptive step-size choices.
- Recommendation: strong accept for NIPS; minor improvements in discussing parallelism benefits would strengthen the paper.
