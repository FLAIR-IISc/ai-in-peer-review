- The paper proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant, and proves global convergence with O(1/T) complexity and explains the dual backward step's role.  
- PDMM unifies existing Jacobian ADMM methods (sADMM and PJADMM), clarifying their relationship in a single framework.  
- PDMM is shown applicable to overlapping-group problems via randomized block updates, addressing an open problem for RBCD with overlapping blocks.  
- The authors provide practical step-size guidance based on problem sparsity and number of randomized blocks.  
- Experiments are limited (only RPCA and overlapping group lasso) and comparisons are sparse; the sADMM baseline may be unfair due to buggy public code.  
- The paper lacks clear, practical guidance on choosing the number of blocks K and how to trade off subproblem count versus solve efficiency.  
- Communication overhead in distributed/parallel settings is not analyzed, leaving practical parallel benefits unclear.  
- The distinction from recent randomized ADMM work (e.g., RBSUMM) is underdeveloped and the novelty claim for overlapping groups needs stronger justification.  
- Overall, the paper makes a significant theoretical contribution and is well-written, but experimental validation is insufficient to support practical speed claims.  
- Recommended improvements: more comprehensive experiments, concrete guidance on K selection, discussion of communication complexity, and deeper comparison with recent randomized ADMM methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on experimental expansion and parameter-selection guidance.
