- Proposed PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM that can update any number of blocks in parallel and introduces a dual backward step, sparsity-aware step sizes, and arbitrary block selection.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity and rigorous proofs relating PDMM to existing ADMM variants.
- Empirical results: comprehensive experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso showing PDMM outperforms RBSUMM and often matches or exceeds sADMM/GSADMM.
- Reproducibility: algorithmic details, theoretical conditions, and experimental parameters are provided, with supplemental proofs.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, with consistent notation, clear algorithm descriptions, and a lucid explanation of the dual backward step.
- Originality: novel ability to randomly select any number of blocks and unify sADMM/PJADMM/RBSUMM, offering new insights into the role of the dual backward step.
- Significance: practically important for large-scale distributed optimization, offering parallel speedups without sacrificing theoretical rates and strong performance on overlapping group lasso.
- Strengths: generalizes Jacobian ADMM methods, rigorous analysis, comprehensive validation, clear relation to prior work, and practical step-size guidance.
- Weaknesses: needs more discussion on why RBCD fails for overlapping groups, further parameter exploration for comparisons (e.g., RBSUMM), and deeper computational complexity analysis beyond runtime.
- Recommendation: strong accept — meets NIPS criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
