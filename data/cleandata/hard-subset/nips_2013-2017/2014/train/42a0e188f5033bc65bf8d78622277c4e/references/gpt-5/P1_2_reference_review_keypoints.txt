- Proposal: PDMM — a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual “backward” correction and step sizes that depend on number of updated blocks K and matrix sparsity di.  
- Variants/unification: Supports inexact Bregman updates and unifies PJADMM (large proximal terms, τ=1, ν=0) and sADMM (small τ, large ν).  
- Theory: Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate with constant step sizes; τi and νi calibrated to K and di.  
- Empirics: Synthetic RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM competitive or faster than sADMM, RBSUMM, and sometimes GSADMM/ADMM; analysis of K’s effect included.  
- Conceptual strength: Identifies implicit dual ascent in block updates and stabilizes parallel updates via the backward dual step.  
- Generality strength: Handles nonsmooth separable objectives, arbitrary K, and overlapping groups via splitting.  
- Theoretical strength: Sparsity-aware step sizes (τi, νi) and unified exact/inexact framework are meaningful contributions.  
- Connection strength: Clarifies when larger dual steps are safe (with proximal terms) versus when conservative steps are needed.  
- Empirical weakness: Experiments use only synthetic data and sequential Matlab; no true multicore/distributed runs to validate parallel advantages.  
- Baseline fairness: sADMM was run with τ=0 due to public code limits and RBSUMM tuning may be incomplete, possibly understating baselines.  
- Clarity issue: Several technical objects (Pt, Q, βi, ζi) and their roles are briefly described and deferred to the supplement.  
- Theory scope limitation: Only ergodic O(1/T) rates provided; no nonergodic or linear convergence under stronger assumptions.  
- Practical guidance gap: Little advice on choosing ηjt, Bregman generators, or sensitivity to ρ; guidance on linearizing choices is limited.  
- Quality assessment: Algorithmic design is well-motivated and step-size rules are plausible; proofs are in the supplement.  
- Clarity assessment: Paper is readable but notation-heavy, some objects introduced abruptly, and contains typographical errors.  
- Originality: Moderately high — novel combination of randomized parallel updates with a calibrated dual backward step and sparsity-aware stepsizes.  
- Significance: Potentially significant for large-scale, sparse, parallel optimization; theoretical contribution is incremental but useful.  
- Suggested experiments: Re-run sADMM with correct dual update, better-tuned RBSUMM, and add multicore/distributed wall-clock scalability tests.  
- Suggested theory/clarity fixes: Add a brief proof sketch for Theorem 1, define Pt/Q concretely, discuss nonuniform/cyclic sampling, and consider stronger rates if possible.  
- Minor fixes: Provide heuristics for ρ and ηjt, add a table of special cases (K, di, τ, ν), fix typos and unify notation.  
- Pros summary: Solid, well-motivated algorithm; sparsity/K-aware steps; unifies existing methods; supports inexact updates; empirical gains shown.  
- Cons summary: Limited and possibly unfair experiments; heavy reliance on supplement; only ergodic rates; limited practical parameter guidance.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
