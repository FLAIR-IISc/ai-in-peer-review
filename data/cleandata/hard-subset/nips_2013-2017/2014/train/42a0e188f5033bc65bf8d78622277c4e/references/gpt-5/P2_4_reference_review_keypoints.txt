- Proposes PDMM: a randomized, parallel Jacobian-style ADMM with a dual “backward step” ŷ = y − νρ(Ax − a) to prevent multi-block divergence.  
- Updates K randomly chosen primal blocks in parallel and sets blockwise τi, νi based on A’s sparsity (degree di) and K.  
- Provides an inexact variant using Bregman divergences, unifying exact, proximal, and linearized updates.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are extreme cases of PDMM.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates under standard convexity assumptions with constant stepsizes.  
- Degree- and K-dependent stepsize design is an interesting, nontrivial contribution; “implicit dual ascent” intuition is insightful.  
- Some technical derivations (matrices Pt, Q and equation (8)) are sketched or deferred to the supplement and appear abrupt in the main text.  
- Notational inconsistencies and typos reduce confidence in meticulousness, though overall structure aligns with literature.  
- Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso include ablations (K, τ/ν) and comparisons to GSADMM/ADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM.  
- Empirical weaknesses: all runs are sequential Matlab (no real parallel implementation), limited scale, and lack of reported statistical variability.  
- Baselines are potentially unfair or poorly tuned (e.g., sADMM implemented without proper dual update; limited tuning for PJADMM/RBSUMM).  
- Paper is generally readable and provides useful intuition, but is notation-heavy with incomplete references and abrupt definitions that hinder Section 3.  
- Novelty: principled, structure-aware τi/νi and backward step fix multi-block Jacobi instability and allow randomized multi-block updates with constant steps.  
- Limitations: convergence rate itself matches existing results; main novelty lies in stepsize/backward-step design and unification.  
- Significance: meaningful theoretical advance likely to be adopted, but practical impact tempered by weak empirical validation.  
- Pros summarized: clear instability motivation; sparsity- and K-aware stepsizes; unifies sADMM/PJADMM; supports inexact updates; theoretical guarantees; empirical ablations.  
- Cons summarized: clarity/notation issues; no true parallel experiments; limited scale; potentially unfair/tuned baselines; no comparison to modern primal–dual or asynchronous methods.  
- Key suggestions: give intuitive derivation of (8); define Pt, Q, βi in main text; provide practical rules for ηjt/Bregman choices; re-run fair/tuned baselines; add at least one genuine parallel experiment; discuss asynchrony and communication costs.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on improved clarity and stronger empirical validation.
