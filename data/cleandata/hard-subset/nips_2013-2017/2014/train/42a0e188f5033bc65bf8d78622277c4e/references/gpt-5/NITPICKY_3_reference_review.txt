Summary
The paper studies linearly constrained convex optimization with block-separable objectives. It proposes PDMM, a randomized Jacobi-type ADMM variant that updates K randomly selected primal blocks in parallel while using a “dual backward step.” Specifically: (i) the augmented Lagrangian is minimized w.r.t. selected blocks using either exact or inexact (via Bregman linearization) subproblems; (ii) the dual is updated with step sizes τi that depend on the per-row sparsity di of A and the number K of updated blocks; (iii) a “backward” correction ŷ = y − νρ(Ax − a) tempers the implicit dual ascent induced by the quadratic term. The paper claims global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates with constant stepsizes, and shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases (two extremes) of PDMM. Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM is competitive or faster than baselines.

Relation to prior work
The paper situates PDMM among multi-block ADMM variants: (a) GS-ADMM/BSUMM (cyclic Gauss–Seidel), (b) Jacobian ADMM with proximal terms (PJADMM), and (c) randomized block updates (RBSUMM). The idea of randomized block-coordinate primal updates with dual damping is reminiscent of earlier randomized coordinate descent, stochastic ADMM, and primal–dual methods, but this paper’s novelty is its block-dependent dual steps using sparsity di and K, plus the dual backward step connecting sADMM and PJADMM. However, the related-work coverage omits several closely related randomized primal–dual methods and stochastic ADMM variants; please add and compare (e.g., Chambolle–Pock/PDHG and its coordinate/stochastic variants; stochastic ADMM; randomized primal–dual coordinate methods).

Strengths
- Clear high-level idea: stabilize Jacobi ADMM via a dual backward step; scale steps with per-row sparsity and number of updated blocks.
- Unifies sADMM and PJADMM as extremes, offering insight into when dual steps should be conservative vs. aggressive.
- General inexact framework via Bregman linearization; plausible practical benefit for hard subproblems.
- Global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate under constant stepsizes.
- Empirical evidence on RPCA and overlapping group lasso; PDMM can exploit overlapping structures (di=2) to use larger τ.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness/completeness:
- The augmented Lagrangian (2) lacks the usual factor 1/2 in the quadratic term; please clarify convention and consistency across analysis.
- Distribution of random block selection Jt (uniform over K-subsets, with/without replacement) is not specified; the expectations in Theorems 1–2 depend on this.
- The stepsize formulas (8) are stated but not derived in the main text; provide intuition/derivation sketch and precise conditions under which they hold (dense/sparse A, uniform sampling, etc.).
- The convergence statements rely on matrices Pt and Q described only as “some positive semidefinite matrix.” Without explicit definitions, the residual R and Lyapunov function h are not reproducible or interpretable.
- The inexact update: you define φjt = ϕjt − (1/ηjt)ψjt and use Bφ = Bϕ − (1/η)Bψ. For Bφ to be a valid Bregman divergence, φ must be convex; the sufficient conditions (ϕ σ/η-strongly convex, ψ σ-smooth) should be explicitly stated in the main paper and tied to practical choices (how σ is obtained/tuned).
- The condition Bφ ≥ 0 is used, but the role of ηjt in the global convergence rate and allowable τi, νi is not made explicit; please state the allowable ranges and trade-offs.
- Initialization details for ŷt (used in (5)) are missing; specify ŷ0 and its relation to y0.
- Edge cases/assumptions: di ≥ 1 is needed (τi and νi use di); clarify handling of zero rows. Clarify whether A has full row rank or if additional qualifications are required.
- The claimed equivalence to sADMM when τi=1/J and νi=1−1/J conflicts with (8) when K=J, which yields τi=1/di and νi=1−1/di. This suggests sADMM corresponds to the dense case di=J; please state this explicitly and reconcile the two parameterizations.

Clarity/presentation:
- Numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout: e.g., “block-seperable,” “an noisy,” “defiend,” “addded,” “convegence,” “contast,” “permutate,” “negligable,” “precesion,” etc. Please carefully proofread.
- Notation inconsistencies: Acj vs Aij vs Ai vs Ari; “row block”/“column block” switching; z, zri definitions are hard to follow and dimensions unclear. Ensure consistent symbols and shapes.
- Important definitions relegated to supplement (e.g., precise PDMM variants, proofs, Pt, Q). The main text should include algorithm pseudocode and a concise but self-contained statement of assumptions and main theorems.
- Some equations are hard to parse (e.g., missing parentheses, spacing, inconsistent subscripts/superscripts).
- Figures/tables are referenced but not shown; experimental setup lacks sufficient detail to reproduce (hardware, wallclock times, solver tolerances, parameter search ranges).

Empirical evaluation:
- All experiments are sequential; claims about “parallel” performance are speculative. Provide parallel implementations and scaling results or clearly caveat claims.
- Comparisons may be unfair: sADMM code used has τi=0 (no dual update), which is not standard; RBSUMM steps appear ad-hoc; parameter choices for baselines should be systematically tuned and reported.
- Synthetic datasets only; add at least one real dataset for RPCA and group lasso. Report objective, primal/dual residuals, and feasibility over time; include error bars over 10 trials.
- Provide guidance on choosing K and the effect of di-sparsity on practical performance (communication/synchronization costs for Ax).

Originality and significance
- The particular combination of randomized Jacobi updates, dual backward step, and sparsity-aware stepsizes is interesting and potentially useful. However, the novelty relative to a broader body of primal–dual and stochastic ADMM literature is not thoroughly established. The theoretical rate is standard O(1/T). Significance hinges on the practicality of the stepsize rule and robust performance; the current experiments and presentation are insufficient to convincingly demonstrate this.

Questions for authors
- Precisely specify the sampling distribution for Jt and whether choices are independent across iterations.
- Define Pt and Q and give intuition for these terms in R and h. Provide a short derivation or sketch for (8).
- Clarify initialization for ŷ and present pseudocode for PDMM (exact and inexact variants).
- Reconcile the sADMM/PJADMM connections with (8) and di. Under what conditions do you recover τi=1/J vs 1/di?
- Provide parallel results and ablations over K and di on real data; justify fairness of baselines.

Pros
- Conceptually simple idea with a plausible stabilization mechanism.
- Takes sparsity into account in a principled way.
- Unifies existing methods; constant-stepsize convergence with O(1/T).

Cons
- Numerous presentation/notation errors; key definitions missing in main text.
- Unclear derivation/justification of stepsize rules; reliance on unspecified matrices.
- Experimental comparisons incomplete/potentially unfair; no actual parallel results.
- Related work coverage incomplete.

Recommendation
At present, I lean to reject due to the accumulation of presentation and methodological clarity issues, and insufficiently rigorous/convincing empirical validation, despite an interesting idea that could be impactful with a more careful and complete treatment. I encourage the authors to revise thoroughly, strengthen the theoretical exposition, and provide fair, parallel empirical evaluations.
