- Introduces PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant with dual backward step and sparsity-aware step sizes for linearly constrained, block-separable convex problems.  
- Addresses difficulty of parallelizing ADMM for multi-block problems by random block selection and conservative dual updates.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with theoretical justification for design choices.  
- Demonstrates empirical performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, exploring effect of number of updated blocks K.  
- Shows connections to existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM), positioning PDMM as a unifying framework.  
- Tackles a relevant problem: parallelizing ADMM for large-scale optimization in ML.  
- Novel combination: randomized block updates + dual backward step + sparsity-aware step sizes is a meaningful contribution.  
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees that support practical application.  
- Empirical results are promising and sometimes outperform existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Core idea (randomized coordinate descent) has limited novelty relative to prior work and needs clearer differentiation.  
- Experiments are limited to two applications; broader empirical validation is needed.  
- Performance appears sensitive to parameter tuning (τi, νi) with insufficient guidance on selection or sensitivity analysis.  
- Calculating/applying τi and νi may add implementation complexity and practical overhead.  
- Lacks broader comparison to other parallel ADMM strategies beyond sADMM, PJADMM, and related methods.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution with rigorous analysis and encouraging experiments.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, proposes a well-motivated method with theory, shows competitive empirical results, and clarifies links to prior work.  
- Against acceptance: limited novelty in the core randomized idea, narrow experimental scope, and potential implementation/parameter-sensitivity concerns.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (7/10).
