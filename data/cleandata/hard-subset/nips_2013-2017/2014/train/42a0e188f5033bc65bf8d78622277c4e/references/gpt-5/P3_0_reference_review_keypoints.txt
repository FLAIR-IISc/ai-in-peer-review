- Paper proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM with optional Bregman proximal terms and a dual “backward” correction; step-sizes depend on matrix sparsity (di) and sampled block count K.
- Authors prove global convergence and an O(1/T) ergodic rate with constant step-sizes, and show sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM.
- Positioned among multi-block ADMM variants and linearized/inexact ADMM; principal novelties are the explicit dual backward step and adaptive sparsity-/K-aware dual step-sizing.
- Strength: clear conceptual explanation (implicit dual ascent) and principled stabilization via the backward step.
- Strength: technical contribution of convergence and rate results in a truly parallel randomized multi-block setting, including an inexact variant.
- Strength: practical intuition—di- and K-dependent steps likely help sparse problems; supports arbitrary K and overlapping groups.
- Strength: unifies sADMM and PJADMM, clarifying when aggressive dual steps are safe.
- Weakness: notation and augmented Lagrangian scaling inconsistent across the paper; key matrices (Pt, Q) and constants are only specified in the supplement.
- Weakness: central step-size formulas (τi = K/(K̃i(2J−K)), νi = 1−1/K̃i) are heuristically motivated but lack a main-text proof sketch showing they follow from potential descent.
- Weakness: limited discussion/positioning relative to broader randomized primal–dual methods (PDHG/Chambolle–Pock variants, diagonal preconditioning, back-substitution corrections).
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is sequential/small-scale—no multicore/GPU/distributed experiments; baseline tuning appears uneven and standard ADMM diagnostics (primal/dual residuals) are omitted.
- Weakness: ablation studies missing (effect of di-aware vs uniform steps, sensitivity to K, ηjt, νi); fairness to RBSUMM unclear given its theoretical requirements.
- Weakness: conditions for inexact updates (dependence on ψjt, ϕjt, ηjt, Lipschitz/strong convexity) are only briefly stated; a compact theorem summarizing them is absent.
- Quality: main theoretical claims are plausible and supported by a Lyapunov argument, but key constants and matrices need clearer exposition in the main text.
- Clarity: overall readable and well-motivated, but notation/scaling inconsistencies and under-specified proof constants hinder comprehension.
- Originality: moderately novel—dual backward correction plus sparsity-/K-aware steps in randomized Jacobi ADMM is a useful addition, though related correction/proximal ideas exist.
- Significance: potentially valuable for large sparse constrained problems, but demonstrated practical impact requires real parallel implementations and broader benchmarks.
- Pros summary: clear motivation, convergence/O(1/T) rate for randomized parallel updates with constant stepsizes, sparsity- and K-aware dual steps, handles overlapping groups.
- Cons summary: notation/scaling issues, limited empirical validation, incomplete positioning vs modern randomized primal–dual methods, missing ablations.
- Suggestions: fix ρ-scaling and notation, state explicit Pt/Q/βi and sketch derivation of τi, νi in main text; add ablations, report primal/dual residuals, ensure fair baseline tuning, include parallel/multicore experiments and a real dataset, expand related work to PDHG/coordinate primal–dual methods.
- Overall rating: 6/10 (weak accept); Reviewer confidence: 4/5.
