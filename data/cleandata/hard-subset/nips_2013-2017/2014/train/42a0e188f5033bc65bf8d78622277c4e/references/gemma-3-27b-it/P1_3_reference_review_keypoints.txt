- Introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant with a backward dual step for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses divergence issues of multi-block ADMM by enabling parallel randomized updates plus a dual backward step.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM, unifying related methods.  
- Provides convergence analysis establishing O(1/T) iteration complexity.  
- Empirically validates PDMM on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso, showing competitive performance.  
- Analyzes effect of number of blocks K on performance.  
- Tackles an important practical problem: scalable, convergent parallel ADMM for large multi-block problems.  
- Novel contributions: backward dual update and randomized block selection increase generality and robustness.  
- Strong theoretical foundation through convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental results support practical effectiveness and competitiveness.  
- Clearly positions PDMM within the ADMM literature and clarifies relationships to existing variants.  
- Introduces parameters τi and νi tied to A’s sparsity and block count, which may complicate tuning.  
- Experimental comparison with GSADMM is limited and parameter-tuning details are insufficient.  
- Claims about sADMM lacking a dual update appear incorrect and need verification.  
- Scalability analysis is shallow—more detail on runtime, memory, and behavior as K and problem size grow is needed.  
- The motivation for using Bregman divergence for inexact updates could be clearer and should cite Bregman ADMM work.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a practical problem, novel and justified method, competitive experiments, clarifies relations among ADMM variants.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complex parameter tuning, incomplete experimental comparisons, insufficient scalability analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: provide practical guidance for τi and νi, clarify GSADMM experimental setup and sADMM statements, expand scalability analysis (cost and memory vs. K and problem size), and cite/connect to Bregman ADMM [Wang & Banerjee, 2015].
