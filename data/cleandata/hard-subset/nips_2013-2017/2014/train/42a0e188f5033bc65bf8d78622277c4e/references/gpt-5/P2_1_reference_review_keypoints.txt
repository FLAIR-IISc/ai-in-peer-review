- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel block-coordinate method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints, using augmented Lagrangian and K randomly selected primal block updates plus a dual backward step.  
- Introduces per-row-block dual step sizes τi and backward corrections νi that depend on constraint sparsity (di) and number of updated blocks K to temper implicit dual ascent.  
- Presents an inexact PDMM variant using Bregman divergences to linearize difficult terms, enabling closed-form updates while preserving convergence.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM as limiting cases of PDMM: exact primal updates force conservative dual steps; strong proximalization permits τi = 1, νi = 0.  
- Proves global convergence and an O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity and KKT existence assumptions via a Lyapunov function.  
- Empirically compares PDMM to GSADMM/ADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso problems.  
- Algorithmic design is well-motivated: identifying implicit dual ascent in Jacobi ADMM and correcting it with a backward step is insightful.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically solid, showing monotone expected decrease and explicit ergodic bounds for objective and feasibility.  
- The inexact/Bregman framework is general and subsumes common linearization strategies with clear validity conditions.  
- Key derivations and proofs (e.g., sADMM/PJADMM equivalence) are deferred to an external technical report rather than the main paper or official supplement.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and sequential (Matlab), so claimed parallel scalability is not demonstrated with wall-clock speedups.  
- Comparisons may be unfair or inconsistent: baselines tuned differently, PDMM benefits from specific τi/νi choices, and some experiments use cyclic selection contrary to the random-selection theory.  
- Discussion omits related ideas like over-relaxed or corrected ADMM and their relation to the backward step.  
- Writing and notation need tightening (typos, symbol introductions); a diagram or pseudocode would improve clarity.  
- The main-text mapping to sADMM and PJADMM should be summarized instead of relegated to external reports.  
- Practical guidance on selecting K and intuition for the formulas for τi and νi are lacking.  
- Reference formatting and citation completeness are inconsistent.  
- Novelty: randomized updates for arbitrary K with sparsity-aware dual corrections and unifying sADMM/PJADMM is a novel, conceptually unifying contribution.  
- Significance: strong theoretical contribution with constant steps for randomized multi-block updates; practical impact limited by insufficient parallel/distributed experiments.  
- Pros: well-motivated dual backward step, unified exact/inexact framework, solid convergence guarantees, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Cons: limited/ sequential experiments, essential technical details offloaded to another report, sparse discussion of related over-relaxed ADMM work, and little practical tuning guidance.  
- Recommendation: lean weak accept conditional on improving clarity (self-contained proofs and exposition) and ideally stronger empirical evidence of parallel gains.
