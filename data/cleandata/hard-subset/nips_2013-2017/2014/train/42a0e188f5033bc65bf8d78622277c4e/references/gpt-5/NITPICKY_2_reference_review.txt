Summary
The paper proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate variant of ADMM for minimizing block-separable convex functions under linear constraints. At each iteration, PDMM updates K chosen primal blocks in parallel using an augmented Lagrangian with an optional Bregman proximal term, and performs a dual ascent step followed by a “backward step” (a shrinkage of the implicit dual ascent induced by the augmented term). The step sizes τi, νi depend on the row-block sparsity di and K. The authors establish global convergence and an O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity assumptions, and position PDMM as unifying/extending sADMM and PJADMM. Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso suggest improved or competitive performance vs. several ADMM variants.

Strengths
- Addresses a well-motivated gap: multi-block, parallel, randomized ADMM with constant step sizes, incorporating block sparsity in stepsize selection.
- Clear conceptual insight: the “dual backward step” moderates the implicit dual ascent from the quadratic penalty; the dependence of τi, νi on di and K is intuitive.
- Unifying view: shows sADMM and PJADMM appear as special cases.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(1/T) rate (ergodic).
- Empirical evidence on two representative applications; exploration of the effect of K.

Weaknesses (technical/methodological)
1) Theory presentation gaps and ambiguities
- Several key quantities are left underspecified: Pt and Q are introduced as “some positive semidefinite matrices” without explicit definitions. βi is defined as “KJ K̃i” in multiple places; the intended formula (K/(J·K̃i)?) must be stated unambiguously. Without these, Lemma 1 and Theorems 1–2 cannot be independently verified.
- In Theorem 2, x̄T is defined as ∑T t=1 xt but then used in a 1/T rate inequality. Typically x̄T := (1/T)∑T t=1 xt. Please clarify; as written it is dimensionally inconsistent.
- The chain inequality in Theorem 1 is malformed (“≤ Eξt h(v*, vt+1) ≤ Eξt−1 h(v*, vt)”), seemingly missing a leading term (e.g., 0 ≤ …). Similarly, several constants (γi, ζi) are intricate; tighter conditions or intuition on when they are positive would help. As written, positivity is asserted but not transparent.
- The Bregman-prox inexact update: φjt = ϕjt − (1/ηjt) ψjt and “linearity of Bregman divergence” are invoked to write Bφ = Bϕ − (1/η)Bψ. This requires φ to be convex; the condition “ϕjt is σ/η-strongly convex and ψjt L-smooth with L=σ” is cited to ensure Bϕ ≥ (1/η)Bψ, but the argument is compressed and nonstandard; please provide a precise lemma with constants and assumptions (e.g., Bψ ≤ (L/2)||·||^2 and Bϕ ≥ (μ/2)||·||^2 with μ≥L/η).
- Assumption 1 relies on existence of a KKT point for the unaugmented Lagrangian. State minimal qualification/Slater-type conditions ensuring existence under linear constraints and closed proper convex fj, Xj.

2) Comparison baselines and fairness
- All implementations are sequential; yet PDMM’s selling point is parallelism. A parallel runtime comparison (or at least complexity analysis per-iteration per-core) is essential.
- RPCA: Using a public sADMM code “without dual update (τi=0)” is not a meaningful baseline; the algorithmic equivalence you claim (“sADMM should be the same as PDMM3 if τi=1/3”) is not tested. This undermines the comparison.
- RBSUMM: parameterization appears limited; the claim that it does not converge within 5000 iters under multiple settings needs a more systematic search or a sensitivity plot. Otherwise the negative result is unconvincing.
- You sometimes tune τi, νi beyond the theoretically suggested (8) and report “best” settings; this blurs the line between provable and heuristic regimes. Provide both: performance with theoretical τi, νi and with tuned values, clearly separated.

3) Clarity and notation
- Notation shifts: Ari vs Ai in (6)-(7); Acj vs A c j; yi vs ŷi; z variables (zij, zri) are introduced abruptly. Define and use consistently.
- K̃i = min{di, K} is central; discuss edge cases (e.g., di=1 ⇒ τi=1, νi=0; di large; K=1 ⇒ νi=0). Also, what happens if some di=0 (row block with no neighbors)?
- The connection proofs to sADMM and PJADMM are deferred to “Appendix B/C in [25]” (an external arXiv). For a NeurIPS submission, proofs should be in the paper/supplement; relying on another paper’s appendix is not acceptable for review.

4) Empirical details
- RPCA experiment details (data generation, γ2, γ3 selection, stopping criteria for each method including primal/dual residual balancing, over-relaxation, scaling, warm-starts) are too sparse for reproducibility.
- Overlapping group lasso: state precisely how A, b are generated, noise variance, and how λ, dg are chosen; explain how proximal steps for low-rank and group norms are implemented.
- Report primal/dual residuals and feasibility separately, not only objective, and use common ADMM diagnostics.

Formatting, typos, and references (numerous)
- Typos and grammar: “block-seperable” (separable), “an noisy”, “addded”, “defiend”, “convegence”, “contast”, “neglegable”, “permutate” (permute), “o(1/T )” spacing inconsistency, missing parentheses and subscripts in several equations.
- Inconsistent bold/spacing in symbols (Acj vs A c j; x t k 6=j; sometimes spaces before subscripts).
- References are incomplete and inconsistently formatted: many missing years/venues, author names garbled (e.g., “E, S. Boyd, Chu N. Parikh, B. Peleato”), capitalization inconsistent, “ArXiv” without IDs, “Preprint” placeholders. For a NeurIPS paper, full and accurate bibliographic entries are required.
- Footnote format in RPCA section is broken (“1” superscript without proper footnote body; URL placed awkwardly).
- Equations: (21) defines L̃ρ with γi but later in Theorem 2 the same term appears with different indices (x1,y1 vs v0); unify.

Originality and significance
The “dual backward step” interpretation and the step-size selection based on di and K are insightful and provide a useful bridge between sADMM and PJADMM. The ability to update arbitrary K blocks in parallel with convergence guarantees is practically relevant. However, the iteration-complexity result remains standard O(1/T) ergodic, and the empirical evaluation, while suggestive, is not yet compelling due to fairness and implementation concerns. The paper’s impact could be solid if clarity, proofs, and experiments are strengthened.

Pros
- Conceptually appealing algorithm with intuitive parameterization by sparsity and parallelism.
- Global convergence and O(1/T) rate under standard assumptions.
- Unifying perspective connecting sADMM and PJADMM.
- Applications to overlapping groups are natural; K dependence explored.

Cons
- Numerous presentation/notation/reference issues; some equations ambiguous or incorrect.
- Key theoretical objects (Pt, Q, βi) undefined/ambiguous; averaging mismatch in Theorem 2.
- Experimental comparisons incomplete and potentially unfair; no parallel results.
- Reliance on external appendices for core connections.

Recommendation: Reject (weak). The idea is promising, but the paper needs substantial revision for rigor, clarity, and experimental fairness.

Confidence: High.
