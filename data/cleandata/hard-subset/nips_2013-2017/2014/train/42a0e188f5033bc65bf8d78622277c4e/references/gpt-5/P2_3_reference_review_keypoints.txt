- Proposes PDMM: randomized K-block parallel primal updates with a novel “dual backward step” and sparsity-aware dual step sizes (τi, νi) depending on di and K.  
- Unifies exact and inexact (proximal) updates via Bregman linearizations and recovers sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.  
- Proves global convergence in expectation with O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity and KKT existence assumptions.  
- Derivation of τi and νi as explicit functions of A’s sparsity (di) and K is a substantive technical contribution.  
- The intuition about implicit dual ascent and connection to ADMM relaxation is well motivated.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso are encouraging but limited in scope.  
- All experiments run sequentially in MATLAB, so no demonstrated parallel wall-clock speedup despite the algorithm’s parallel design.  
- Some baselines/implementations raise fairness concerns (e.g., sADMM baseline possibly handicapped), limiting empirical conclusiveness.  
- Notation is heavy but mostly consistent; the paper needs polish (typos, formatting, incomplete citations).  
- Key technical definitions (e.g., matrices Pt and Q) are deferred and lack brief intuition in the main text.  
- Practical guidance for choosing ηj and specific ψ/ϕ choices for common losses/regularizers is insufficient.  
- Originality is moderate–high: novel combination of random K-block Jacobi updates, explicit dual backward step, and sparsity-aware step sizes.  
- Significance is moderate: broadly applicable but theoretical rate matches standard ADMM and empirical validation lacks parallel/large-scale demonstrations.  
- Strength: principled design and clear intuition for the dual backward step linking existing methods.  
- Strength: convergence guarantees with constant step sizes and explicit dependence on sparsity.  
- Strength: unified exact/inexact update framework via Bregman divergence.  
- Strength: practical step-size advantages for some structures (e.g., overlapping groups with small di).  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks true parallel experiments, broader baselines (linearized ADMM, PDHG/SPDHG), and careful baseline reimplementation.  
- Weakness: theoretical rate is standard (O(1/T)); no non-ergodic or accelerated results.  
- Weakness: limited advantage when A is dense (di ≈ J) and this tradeoff is not fully discussed.  
- Questions for authors: provide concrete recipes for ψ, ϕ, ηj for common models and sensitivity analyses.  
- Questions for authors: clarify construction and practical impact of Pt and Q in analysis.  
- Questions for authors: report PDMM performance when A is dense and compare with sADMM/PJADMM in that regime.  
- Questions for authors: compare to randomized primal-dual methods (e.g., SPDHG) and include a corrected sADMM baseline with full dual update.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution and unifying perspective; recommend weak accept contingent on clearer baselines, stronger empirical validation, and improved clarity.
