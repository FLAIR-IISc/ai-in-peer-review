Summary
The paper proposes PDMM, a randomized, parallel, multi-block variant of ADMM for solving block-separable convex problems with linear constraints. Key ideas: (i) a “dual backward step” that tempers the implicit dual ascent introduced by the augmented term, (ii) dual step sizes τi and backward parameters νi that depend on the number of randomly updated blocks K and the row-block sparsity di, and (iii) an inexact update framework using Bregman divergences that unifies exact and proximal-Jacobi updates. The authors claim global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate with constant stepsizes, and show that sADMM and PJADMM arise as special cases. Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso suggest PDMM can be competitive/faster than several ADMM baselines (run sequentially in Matlab).

Relation to previous work
The work situates itself among multi-block ADMM variants: Gauss–Seidel ADMM (including versions with back substitution), proximal Jacobi ADMM, and randomized block methods (BSUMM/RBSUMM). The idea of stabilizing Jacobi ADMM with proximal terms is known; here the “dual backward step” is a distinct corrective mechanism, and the step-size schedule reflecting di and K is novel to my knowledge. The convergence rate matches standard O(1/T) results.

Strengths
- Addresses known divergence issues of Jacobi multi-block ADMM with a clear mechanism (dual backward step).
- Derives block-dependent stepsizes that exploit sparsity (di) and number of updated blocks (K), potentially allowing larger steps than sADMM.
- Unifies exact, proximal-Jacobi, and split-variable ADMM within one framework; clarifies relationships among sADMM/PJADMM/PDMM.
- Provides global convergence and ergodic rate guarantees under standard convexity assumptions.
- Includes applications to RPCA and overlapping group lasso (mapping of overlapping groups into equality constraints).

Weaknesses (technical/methodological)
- Equation inconsistencies and likely typos in critical formulas:
  - Eq. (2) uses Lρ with penalty ρ‖Ax−a‖^2, while many later formulas use (ρ/2)‖·‖^2. The factor of 1/2 must be consistent throughout; otherwise x-updates and proofs are ambiguous.
  - Eq. (8) states νi = 1 − K̃i, which is dimensionally and numerically incorrect; later sections imply νi = 1 − 1/K̃i. Please fix and ensure all subsequent derivations reflect the correct expression.
  - Multiple places mix Acj, Ajt, Aij, Ari/Ai without consistent notation.
- Convergence analysis in the main text is too opaque to verify:
  - P_t and Q are referenced as positive semidefinite but are not defined; γi and ζi are given but their role/derivation is unclear without the supplement.
  - Lemma 1 and Theorem 1 statements omit important conditions, and the inequalities are terse; monotonicity is stated “in expectation” without specifying the sampling distribution over subsets Jt (with/without replacement? uniform over K-subsets? grouped sampling?).
- Dependence on external document: Several key claims (e.g., “sADMM and PJADMM are special cases” with exact parameter mapping) are deferred to appendices in [25] (an arXiv paper by the same authors) rather than the current supplement. This is inappropriate for a self-contained, anonymized submission.
- Practical guidance is lacking:
  - How to select ηj (Bregman proximal strength) in practice? The text says “sufficiently large” with Lipschitz/strong convexity assumptions but offers no computable rules or line-search/heuristics.
  - The trade-off between τi and νi is qualitatively discussed but no ablation or sensitivity study is provided beyond isolated tunings; yet experiments heavily rely on tuned τi, νi.
  - Randomization scheme is underspecified (uniform K-without-replacement vs cyclic group partition), yet the theory uses expectation over ξt; it’s unclear which scheme the proofs cover.
- Empirical evaluation is limited:
  - All runs are sequential in Matlab; no actual parallel runtime is reported, undercutting the central claim (“parallel” method).
  - Comparisons use fixed ρ=1 across methods; ADMM-type methods are sensitive to ρ, so equalizing ρ may be unfair. Hyperparameter selection protocols should be identical across baselines.
  - The sADMM implementation used reportedly “does not have a dual update,” which makes the comparison questionable. If the true sADMM differs, implement it faithfully.
  - Datasets are synthetic; no real-world RPCA/group-lasso datasets are tested. Figures/tables (referenced) are not seen; stopping criteria (“residual”) unclear (is it R(·) from Eq. (19) or standard primal/dual residuals?).
- Numerous dimensional/notation slips in Section 2.3 (Uj dimensions, A construction) that make the equivalence mapping hard to verify.

Clarity and presentation issues
- Many typos and grammatical errors: “seperable,” “addded,” “defiend,” “convegence,” “precesion,” etc.; inconsistent spacing and punctuation around equations; malformed expressions (e.g., “τi = 1/J 0.5”).
- Inconsistent and undefined symbols (Ai vs Ari, Ajt vs Acjt, z, zri, Pt, Q).
- References are incomplete and inconsistently formatted; many lack years, venues, author order is wrong, and titles/venues contain typos. 
- Anonymity: acknowledgments and self-referential arXiv citations (with author names) violate double-blind policy.

Originality
- The dual backward step and sparsity-aware stepsizes are interesting. However, beyond unification and parameterization of known variants, the conceptual novelty is incremental relative to proximal Jacobi ADMM and correction/back-substitution variants. The theoretical guarantees match the standard rate.

Significance
- If cleaned up and validated, the sparsity-aware stepsize rules could be useful to practitioners. But the lack of real parallel experiments and the limited empirical scope currently limit impact.

Pros
- Conceptually simple corrective mechanism for Jacobi multi-block ADMM.
- Exploits sparsity to enlarge dual steps.
- Unifies sADMM/PJADMM under one scheme with proofs of O(1/T).

Cons
- Numerous technical/notation errors; key equations incorrect/ambiguous.
- Analysis not self-contained; reliance on external appendices; undefined quantities.
- Inadequate experimental rigor and no parallel benchmarks.
- Anonymity and reference formatting issues.

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix Eq. (2) vs (ρ/2) consistency; correct Eq. (8) to νi = 1 − 1/K̃i; unify notation (A blocks, row/column indices).
- Fully specify the sampling scheme for Jt and align theory and practice.
- Define P_t, Q, γi, ζi precisely in the main paper or the official supplement; include complete proofs and mappings to sADMM/PJADMM in the supplement, not external sources.
- Provide practical rules for ηj (e.g., based on Lipschitz constants) and a sensitivity study for τi, νi, K.
- Add real parallel experiments (multi-core/GPU/cluster) with wall-clock comparisons; include real datasets; adopt fair hyperparameter tuning across baselines.
- Correct references and enforce NeurIPS style; remove identifying info for anonymity.

Recommendation: Reject (significant promise but too many technical/clarity/anonymity issues at present). Confidence: High.
