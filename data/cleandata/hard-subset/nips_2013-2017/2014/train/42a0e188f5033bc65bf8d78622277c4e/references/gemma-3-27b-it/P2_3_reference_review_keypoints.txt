- Proposes PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant with a backward dual step that exploits constraint sparsity and allows parallel updates of randomized block subsets.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (O(1/T) iteration complexity) and connects PDMM to sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso, including analysis of the effect of K (number of randomized blocks).  
- Addresses a practical problem: improves multi-block ADMM applicability and convergence for large-scale problems.  
- Novelty: combines backward dual step, randomized block selection, and sparsity-aware updates in a new way.  
- Theoretical rigor: solid convergence analysis and complexity bounds.  
- Empirical validation: outperforms ADMM variants and RBSUMM on tested problems.  
- Generally clear presentation and mostly consistent notation.  
- Weakness: parameter tuning (τi, νi) is involved and may be problem-dependent; sensitivity analysis is limited.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is narrow; lacks comparisons to modern non-ADMM optimizers and other parallel solvers.  
- Weakness: Section 2.3 on overlapping blocks is abrupt and some block-index notation is unintuitive.  
- Weakness: dense mathematical exposition—would benefit from higher-level explanations alongside derivations.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with strong theoretical support.  
- Clarity assessment: generally good but certain sections and derivations need improvement.  
- Originality assessment: strong—meaningful contribution beyond existing ADMM variants.  
- Significance assessment: good to strong—relevant for scalable block-separable optimization.  
- Pro arguments: strong theory, empirical improvements, practical relevance, novel technique combination, insightful links to related work.  
- Con arguments: parameter tuning difficulty, limited experimental breadth, some unclear sections.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggestions to improve clarity and broaden experimental evaluation in the final version.
