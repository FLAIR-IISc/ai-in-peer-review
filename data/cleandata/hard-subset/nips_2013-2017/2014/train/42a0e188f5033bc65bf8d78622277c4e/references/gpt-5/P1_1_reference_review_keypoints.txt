- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant that updates K random primal blocks in parallel and all dual blocks with sparsity- and K-dependent steplengths.  
- Key innovation is a "dual backward step" using a damped dual iterate ŷt = yt − νiρ(Axt − a) to separate implicit and explicit dual ascent.  
- Offers an inexact variant with Bregman proximal terms to enable closed-form updates, unifying exact and proximal-Jacobian methods.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM arise as extreme parameter choices, clarifying the role of the backward step and dual steplength.  
- Proves global convergence and an O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity and feasibility assumptions with constant stepsizes.  
- Analysis accounts for row-block degrees di and number of updated blocks K, allowing larger τi for sparser A and larger K.  
- Empirical tests on RPCA and overlapping group lasso indicate PDMM can outperform sADMM and RBSUMM in some settings.  
- Experiments are limited: all runs are sequential (no true parallel timings), datasets are modest, and baseline tuning appears asymmetric.  
- Technical presentation issues: key quantities (Pt, Q, γi) are under-specified or introduced abruptly and important constants are inconsistent (ρ vs ρ/2).  
- Paper lacks concise pseudocode and pushes important connections/derivations to the appendix, hurting readability.  
- Novelty: the explicit dual backward step combined with sparsity- and K-aware stepsize analysis is a new and interesting contribution.  
- Significance: relevant for large-scale structured problems where parallel block updates are attractive; theory is valuable to optimization-for-ML community.  
- Strength: provides clear algorithmic insight explaining stabilization of Jacobi updates via the backward step.  
- Strength: interpretable stepsize rules tying sparsity and number of blocks to stability, with extremes recovering known methods.  
- Strength: covers both exact and inexact (Bregman) variants with matched O(1/T) guarantees.  
- Weakness: clarity problems including inconsistent augmented Lagrangian scaling, missing definitions, and many typos/notation heaviness.  
- Weakness: experimental shortcomings—no parallel runs, small problems, possible unfair baseline implementations, and incomplete tuning.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on supplemental material for theoretical details; main text should better explain sufficient conditions and intuition.  
- Key questions: reconcile ρ vs ρ/2 convention; define Pt and Q in main text; sensitivity of PDMM to di/K misspecification and potential adaptive τi, νi; provide true parallel wall-clock results; ensure fair baseline tuning.  
- Suggestions: add compact pseudocode, ablation on τi and νi, larger-scale and parallel experiments, unify constants and define matrices in main text, and clean up typos/references.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—core idea and theory are solid and relevant but require improved clarity and stronger empirical validation.
