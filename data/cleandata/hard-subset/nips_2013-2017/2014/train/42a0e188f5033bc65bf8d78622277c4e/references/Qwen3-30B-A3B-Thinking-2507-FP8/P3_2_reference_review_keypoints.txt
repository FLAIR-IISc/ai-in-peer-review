- Proposal: PDMM — a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant with a dual backward step, sparsity-aware step sizes, arbitrary-number parallel block updates, global convergence, and O(1/T) complexity.
- Theoretical contribution: unifies sADMM and PJADMM as special cases and provides rigorous insight into the role of dual updates in Jacobi-type ADMM.
- Practical innovation: step-size parameters using K̃i = min{di,K} and random K-block updates allow adaptation to problem sparsity and improved parallelism.
- Convergence analysis: thorough, KKT-linked proofs and an O(1/T) iteration complexity result with constant step size.
- Experiments: strong empirical gains over sADMM, PJADMM, and RBSUMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, including analysis of different K values.
- Limited experimental scope: needs broader evaluation (e.g., distributed optimization, large-scale ML) to strengthen significance claims.
- Nonsmoothness handling: unclear — convergence analysis does not explicitly address nonsmooth objective terms beyond convexity assumptions.
- Implementation details: lacks concrete discussion of parallel implementation challenges, scalability, and communication costs.
- Literature comparison: insufficient comparison with more recent related work (e.g., [9]) that may have better convergence rates.
- Critical assessment: substantial and novel theoretical advance (dual backward step) with convincing practical benefits and unification of prior methods.
- Recommendation: accept — solid theoretical and experimental contribution with modest, addressable weaknesses.
