- Summary: Proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM with a dual "backward step" that allows parallel block updates, proves O(1/T) convergence, unifies sADMM/PJADMM, and shows competitive results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.

- Strength: Addresses an important problem of multi-block, linearly-constrained convex optimization relevant to many ML applications.

- Strength: Novel combination of randomized block coordinate descent with a dual backward step and sparsity-tuned step sizes.

- Strength: Strong theoretical guarantees—global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.

- Strength: Provides a unifying framework showing sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM.

- Strength: Empirical validation on RPCA and overlapping group lasso with useful ablation on number of blocks.

- Strength: Mostly clear writing and logical structure.

- Weakness: Heavy, dense notation (block indices and sparsity patterns) that hampers readability and would benefit from visual aids.

- Weakness: Insufficient implementation details (Matlab implementation, parameter tuning) to easily reproduce experiments.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons—some relevant methods (PA-APG, S-APG) not deeply compared and the RBSUMM tuning/convergence claim is underexplained.

- Weakness: Lacks discussion of practical parallelism concerns (communication overhead, synchronization).

- Weakness: Overlapping group lasso experiment is rushed and lacks detailed analysis beyond runtime.

- Qualitative scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.

- Arguments for acceptance: Rigorous theory, novel and practical method, competitive empirical performance, unifying insight, and potential broad impact.

- Arguments against acceptance: Readability issues due to notation, incomplete implementation/practical-parallelism details, and incomplete empirical comparisons.

- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address notation, implementation details, and comparative analysis.
