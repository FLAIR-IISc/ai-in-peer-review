- Presents PDMM, a novel parallel extension of ADMM that randomly updates K blocks in parallel and adds a backward step on the dual update for stability.
- Situates PDMM within ADMM literature and shows sADMM and PJADMM are extreme cases of the PDMM framework.
- Targets linearly constrained, block-separable convex optimization and addresses overlapping block structures (e.g., overlapping group lasso).
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence guarantees and iteration complexity results under reasonable assumptions.
- Introduces adaptive dual step sizes that depend on matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks, improving robustness.
- Offers clear, well-organized presentation and consistent mathematical notation.
- Novel insight into the interplay between primal and dual updates and the connection to existing ADMM variants.
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates promise on RPCA and overlapping group lasso but is limited in scope and variety.
- Comparison with other methods is not exhaustive and parameter tuning procedures are insufficiently described.
- Guidance on selecting the number of blocks K is limited, despite its clear impact on performance.
- Explanations of the connections to sADMM/PJADMM and the rationale for the backward step can be dense and require careful reading.
- Some claims (e.g., PDMM being “faster” than GSADMM) are stronger than justified and should be softened given differences in parallelization.
- Pros: solid theory, potential speedups, novel algorithm, insightful connections, and relevance to important problem classes.
- Cons: limited empirical validation, unclear parameter-selection guidance, and some sections needing clearer exposition.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experiments, provide practical guidance on K, simplify key explanations, and moderate overstated claims.
