- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant for linearly constrained, block-separable convex problems, supporting K-of-J block updates and optional Bregman inexactness.
- Introduces a “dual backward step” plus explicit dual ascent with τi and νi tuned to row-block degree di and number of updated blocks K.
- Unifies exact and inexact updates via Bregman linearization and recovers sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.
- Proves global convergence in expectation and an O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convex assumptions with constant stepsizes for arbitrary K.
- Empirically evaluates PDMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, showing qualitative performance gains.
- Situates PDMM among multi-block ADMM variants and highlights novelty: K- and di-aware step sizes and randomized multi-block updates beyond single-block RBSUMM.
- Key technical insight: instability of naive Jacobi ADMM arises from implicit dual ascent, countered by the backward dual step.
- Main theoretical ingredient: Lyapunov decrease via constructed h(v*,v^t) and residuals using PSD matrices P^t and Q.
- Important theoretical details (forms of P^t, Q; derivation/tightness of τi and νi; nonnegativity conditions; Bregman term assumptions and ηj choices) are deferred to the supplement and under-specified in the main paper.
- Mapping and explicit parameter settings that reduce PDMM to sADMM and PJADMM are not clearly presented in the main text.
- Experiments are sequential Matlab runs; no parallel wall-clock speedups, communication, or runtime profiles are reported.
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair or improperly implemented (sADMM without dual update; limited RBSUMM tuning).
- Empirical scope is limited: only two convex problem classes, moderate sizes, and no ablation on sparsity di or broad sensitivity analyses for K, ρ, τ, ν.
- Presentation issues include typos, inconsistent notation, missing definitions (P^t, Q, βi, γi) in the main text, and scattered intuition for step sizes.
- Novelty: dual backward step and explicit dependence of step sizes on di and K are new and practically compelling.
- Significance: method is broadly applicable and potentially impactful, but lack of parallel experiments and limited empirical validation reduce immediate practical impact.
- Strengths: principled fix for Jacobi instability, di-/K-aware step rules, unifying view with existing ADMM variants, and convergence guarantees with constant steps.
- Weaknesses: insufficient theoretical exposition in main paper, experimental shortcomings (no parallel results, questionable baselines), limited problem diversity, and clarity/notation burdens.
- Suggested improvements: add an algorithm box with admissible parameter ranges and defaults, specify P^t and Q, include proof sketch, implement correct baselines and PJADMM tuning, report parallel wall-clock and communication costs, and add sensitivity/ablation studies.
- Overall: solid and interesting contribution with sound theory and novel ideas, but needs clearer presentation and stronger, fairer empirical validation to maximize impact.
- Pros for acceptance: principled algorithmic idea, theory-backed di- and K-dependent step rules, unifying connections, and O(1/T) convergence with constant steps.
- Cons for acceptance: opaque theoretical details in the main text, experimental limitations (no parallel results; questionable baselines), and presentation/clarity issues.
