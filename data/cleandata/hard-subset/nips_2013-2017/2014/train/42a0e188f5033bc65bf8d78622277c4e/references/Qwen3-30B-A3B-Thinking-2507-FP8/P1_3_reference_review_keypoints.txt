- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual backward step for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Unifies and shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM.  
- Novel algorithmic contribution: can randomly update multiple blocks in parallel and adapts step sizes to constraint sparsity and number of blocks.  
- Practical impact: empirical improvements on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso in convergence speed and runtime.  
- Addresses the open problem of randomized block coordinate descent for overlapping groups.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with consistent notation and reproducible detail.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with complete proofs provided in the supplement.  
- Limited analysis of inexact updates—main guarantees focus on exact updates (η_j = 0).  
- Insufficient exploration of parameter sensitivity for τ_i and ν_i in experiments.  
- Scalability not fully demonstrated on very large datasets where parallelism matters most.  
- Comparison with RBSUMM is limited; RBSUMM did not converge in experiments and needs a more thorough comparison.  
- Overall quality: technically sound and well-executed work.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear and easy for experts to reproduce.  
- Originality: significant advance as a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM with new theoretical insights.  
- Significance: important for large-scale ML optimization; combines O(1/T) rate with parallelism benefits.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel approach, rigorous convergence, unifying framework, strong experiments, solves open problem on overlapping groups.  
- Con-acceptance: limited inexact-update theory, limited large-scale scalability results, parameter sensitivity not fully studied, and incomplete RBSUMM comparison.
