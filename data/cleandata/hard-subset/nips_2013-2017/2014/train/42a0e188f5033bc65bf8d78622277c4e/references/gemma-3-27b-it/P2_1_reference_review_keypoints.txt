- Introduces PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant for linearly constrained, block-separable convex problems.  
- PDMM updates a random subset of blocks in parallel, uses a backward step on the dual for stability, and adapts step sizes to constraint matrix sparsity.  
- Provides global convergence guarantees and iteration complexity under standard assumptions.  
- Empirically evaluated on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso, showing competitive performance versus other ADMM variants.  

Strengths:
- Tackles an important problem: scalable, parallel ADMM for large-scale constrained optimization.  
- Novel combination: randomized block selection + dual backward step + sparsity-aware step sizes.  
- Theoretical rigor: establishes global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Clarifies relations: shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM.  
- Empirical results are encouraging on the tested applications.  
- Well-written and clearly presented with standard notation and clear algorithm description.  

Weaknesses:
- Experiments are limited to only two applications, restricting claims of generality.  
- Lacks a detailed parameter-sensitivity analysis (e.g., impact of K and other hyperparameters).  
- Overlapping Group Lasso experiment descriptions and data-generation/parameter settings are under-specified.  
- Missing implementation details (language, libraries, sparse ops, parallelization strategy).  
- No comparison to algorithms designed specifically for massively parallel/distributed computation.  

Qualitative evaluation:
- Quality: technically sound with plausible convergence analysis (supplement needs detailed checking).  
- Clarity: motivation and method are clearly explained and easy to follow.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of existing techniques with useful theoretical connections.  
- Significance: potential to accelerate ADMM-based methods if empirical findings generalize.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses a challenging, relevant problem.  
- Presents a well-motivated, novel algorithm.  
- Offers theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Shows promising empirical performance.  
- Properly situates the work within existing literature.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Empirical evaluation is too limited.  
- No thorough parameter sensitivity study.  
- Lacks comparisons to truly parallel/distributed algorithms.  

Overall assessment:
- Valuable and promising contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions.  

Requested minor revisions:
- Expand empirical evaluation to more problem types and datasets.  
- Add a detailed sensitivity analysis of PDMM’s key parameters (e.g., K).  
- Provide implementation details (programming language, libraries, parallelization strategy).  
- Consider adding comparisons to methods designed for massively parallel/distributed computation.
