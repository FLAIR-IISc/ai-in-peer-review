- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant using a "dual backward step" ŷ = y − νρ(Ax − a) to stabilize Jacobi updates.
- Step sizes τi and νi are chosen based on constraint sparsity di and number of updated blocks K, with τi = K/(K̃i(2J − K)) and νi = 1 − 1/K̃i.
- Allows inexact updates via a Bregman divergence term to linearize difficult subproblems.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM under specific τ, ν and proximal settings.
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity assumptions, constants depend on di and K.
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM can outperform sADMM and RBSUMM and be competitive with GSADMM in runtime.
- Positions PDMM among Gauss–Seidel, Jacobian/proximal Jacobian, and randomized multi-block ADMM methods.
- Argues the dual backward step explains and mitigates divergence in naive Jacobi ADMM.
- Strength: dual backward step is a clear, practically motivated stabilization insight tied to sparsity and update parallelism.
- Strength: unifying framework that interprets sADMM and PJADMM as PDMM extremes.
- Strength: handles nonsmooth and overlapping-group problems, with inexact updates enabling closed-form steps in common cases.
- Weakness: rate matches known O(1/T) results with no nonergodic or improved rates; constants/matrices (P^t, Q, βi) are only sketched, limiting interpretability.
- Weakness: backward step resembles prior relaxation/extragradient methods; relation to relaxed ADMM and Chambolle–Pock-type algorithms needs clearer comparison.
- Weakness: experiments are sequential MATLAB only—no real parallel/distributed scaling, communication/latency analysis, or fair multi-core benchmarks.
- Weakness: baseline comparisons may be unfair (sADMM lacking dual update, limited RBSUMM tuning); PJADMM and properly tuned sADMM missing.
- Weakness: paper is notation-heavy with unclear definitions of key objects and several typos/grammatical errors; algorithm box and default parameters are needed.
- Originality: tempering dual step tied to di, K seems novel, but must better delineate novelty vs reparameterization of existing stabilized ADMM variants.
- Significance: promising for sparse-A and tunable-K settings, but underdemonstrated without stronger empirical and theoretical detail.
- Questions for authors: are τi, νi choices optimal or sensitive; explicit P^t and Q expressions; precise relation to over-relaxed/extragradient ADMM; adaptive K selection; derivation for overlapping-group special case; extensions to strong convexity or nonconvex settings.
- Pros for acceptance: useful stabilization idea, unifying view, solid convergence under standard assumptions, practical step formulas exploiting sparsity.
- Cons for acceptance: no real parallel experiments, missing explicit constants/definitions in main text, incremental rate guarantees, limited discussion relative to related stabilized/relaxed ADMM literature.
- Recommendation: weak reject—good idea and analysis but needs clearer exposition, explicit constants, and fair/parallel empirical validation.
