- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Key innovations: (1) dual backward step, (2) step sizes that exploit constraint sparsity, (3) ability to randomly update any number of primal blocks in parallel.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step sizes; shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases.  
- Experimental results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show significant speedups over sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong and comprehensive (Assumption 1, Theorem 1, Theorem 2, Lemma 1).  
- Dual backward step is a novel, well-justified contribution addressing convergence issues of Jacobi updates.  
- Resolves an open problem on randomized block coordinate descent for overlapping blocks.  
- Paper is very well written and organized; motivation and technical explanations are clear.  
- Problem is significant for large-scale distributed optimization and overlapping-structure ML applications.  
- Limitation: analysis assumes existence of a KKT point; behavior when no KKT point exists is not discussed.  
- Limitation: experiments cover only two applications; broader benchmarks would better demonstrate generality.  
- Limitation: practical parallel/distributed implementation details and scaling behavior are not provided (runtimes reported sequentially).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept; rates quality, clarity, originality, and significance as high.
