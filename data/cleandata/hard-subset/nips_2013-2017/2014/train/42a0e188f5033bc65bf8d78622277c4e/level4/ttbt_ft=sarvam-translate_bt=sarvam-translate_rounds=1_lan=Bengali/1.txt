This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block- separable functions under linear constraints. The paper proposes a variant of ADMM (known as PDMM) for use in cases with multiple blocks: it randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for updating and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable; the process of updating the primal variable can be implemented in parallel. Sublinear convergence rates are proven for the proposed method. This method generalizes several variants of ADMM.

This paper is well-written and easy to follow. I am positive about accepting this paper. My questions and comments are given below:

1) The description of the algorithm in paragraphs (5) and (6) is not clear enough. Do you update all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} or only one block "i"? I think you only update one block "y" of the dual variable, but I could not find the rule for selecting "i". This should be clarified through correction.

2) In Theorem 2, I think the "\sum_{i=1}^I" was missing behind your "{" in the proof.

3) More discussion about the theorem should be included. Theorem 2 (the main result of this paper) provides the convergence rate of the proposed method PDMM. Firstly, PDMM generalizes several variants of ADMM. A natural question is whether Theorem 2 (correctly selecting parameters like K, J) is consistent with the convergence rates of those variants. Secondly, since you have divided the transformation matrix A into multiple blocks, what is the best way to divide it from your theorem?

4) I am interested in the comparison to the Tensor Completion problem ("Tensor Completion for Estimating Missing Values in Visual Data, 2012"), where there are multiple block structures. Do you have any idea which variant of ADMM is the best?
