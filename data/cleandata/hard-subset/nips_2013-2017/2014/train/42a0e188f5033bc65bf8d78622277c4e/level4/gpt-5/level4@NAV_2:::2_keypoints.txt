- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobi-style) multi-block method for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Contrasts with standard ADMM which uses Gauss–Seidel updates and is mainly for two-block problems.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward correction that moderates dual ascent to compensate for limited information flow in Jacobi updates.  
- Proves global convergence with O(1/T) ergodic complexity, matching ADMM-type rates.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM in the supplementary material, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian methods.  
- Empirical results indicate PDMM is competitive with or outperforms ADMM and several variants.  
- The superior empirical performance is somewhat counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobi methods; additional discussion on when PDMM beats Gauss–Seidel would be helpful.  
- The dual backward step appears novel and crucial for broad-condition convergence.  
- Expands practical options for parallel and distributed optimization.
