- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobian) update method for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints, accommodating multiple blocks.  
- Main contribution is a dual backward step that compensates limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Proves O(1/T)-type convergence results, analogous to ADMM.  
- Shows PDMM generalizes sADMM and PJADMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants on tested problems.  
- Empirical results seem counterintuitive since Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel; the reviewer asks for further explanation.  
- Dual backward step appears novel and crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions.  
- Method provides additional alternatives and opportunities for distributed optimization.
