

This paper addresses the minimization of block-separable functions under linear constraints. It proposes a variant of ADMM termed PDMM for the multi-block case: PDMM randomly selects a subset of K blocks of the primal variable for update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable, with primal updates implementable in parallel. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for PDMM, demonstrating its generalization of several ADMM variants. The paper is well-written and accessible. I recommend acceptance pending revisions addressing the following points:

1) Algorithm descriptions in (5) and (6) require clarification. Does the update apply to all coordinates of \(y^{t+1}\) and \(\hat{y}^{t+1}\) or only a single block \(i\)? The text implies only a single block of the dual variable \(y\) is updated, but the selection mechanism for block \(i\) is unspecified. This must be explicitly stated in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, the summation index \(\sum_{i=1}^I\) appears missing in the proof.

3) Theorem 2 (the main result) warrants deeper discussion. First, does Theorem 2 recover known convergence rates for established ADMM variants when parameters (e.g., \(K\), \(J\)) are appropriately chosen? Second, given the block splitting of transformation matrix \(A\), what are the optimal block-splitting strategies implied by Theorem 2?

4) The authors might consider comparing PDMM with ADMM variants on the tensor completion problem (e.g., "Tensor Completion for Estimating Missing Values in Visual Data," 2012), which also exhibits multi-block structure. A brief discussion on which ADMM variant performs optimally for this application would be valuable.

Minors:
1) Add space after "min" in (9).
2) Remove space before "Section" on line 217.

I recommend acceptance pending revisions addressing the points above, particularly the expanded discussion of Theorem 2.
