

This paper addresses the minimization of block-separable functions subject to linear constraints by proposing a variant of ADMM termed PDMM. The method randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable, enabling parallel implementation of primal updates. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for PDMM, which generalizes several existing ADMM variants. The paper is well-written and clearly presented, and I recommend acceptance. Below are my major and minor comments for revision:

**Major Comments:**  
1. The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) requires clarification. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of \(y^{t+1}\) and \(\hat{y}^{t+1}\) are updated or only a single block \(i\) (corresponding to the randomly selected primal block). The paper states that a single block of the dual variable is updated, but the mechanism for selecting the block index \(i\) is not specified. This must be explicitly stated in the revision.  
2. In Theorem 2, the summation index \(\sum_{i=1}^I\) appears to be missing in the proof (e.g., after the opening brace in the displayed equation). This should be corrected.  
3. Theorem 2 (the main result) warrants deeper discussion. First, since PDMM generalizes multiple ADMM variants, it is essential to verify whether Theorem 2 recovers known convergence rates for those variants by appropriately choosing parameters (e.g., \(K\), \(J\)). Second, given the block splitting of the transformation matrix \(A\), the optimal splitting strategy (e.g., based on problem structure) should be addressed in light of the convergence rate derived in Theorem 2.  
4. The authors reference tensor completion (e.g., "tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012") as a problem with multiple-block structure. It would be valuable to discuss whether the proposed PDMM method offers advantages over existing ADMM variants for this application, and if so, which variant (e.g., standard ADMM, block ADMM) is optimal in this context.  

**Minor Comments:**  
1. Equation (9): A space is missing after "min".  
2. Line 217: Remove the space preceding "Section".  

I recommend acceptance pending revisions addressing the above points, particularly the clarification of the block selection mechanism and expanded discussion of Theorem 2.
