- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant that picks K random blocks per iteration to update primal variables and uses a backward step for dual updates.
- The backward dual step makes updates more conservative and is crucial for global convergence.
- PDMM allows adding a proximal term in the primal update to simplify subproblems.
- The paper proves global convergence and establishes iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J total blocks.
- Main advantages: full parallelization of the primal step and ability to use larger stepsizes than competing methods.
- PDMM can be faster than sADMM, is more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso are promising: PDMM achieves target accuracy with less computation time.
- The authors did not report PDMM’s performance with an actual parallel implementation.
- PDMM requires tuning three parameters, which is less convenient than traditional ADMM.
- Overall assessment: novel and interesting contribution with solid theory and experiments, matching ADMM’s convergence rate while enabling parallelization.
