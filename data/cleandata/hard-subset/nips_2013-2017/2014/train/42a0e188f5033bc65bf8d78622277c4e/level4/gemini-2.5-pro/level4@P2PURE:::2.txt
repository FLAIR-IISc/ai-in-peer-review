This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. In contrast to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which updates primal variables sequentially in a Gauss-Seidel manner and is typically limited to two blocks, the proposed method updates multiple primal blocks in parallel using a Jacobian-style procedure.

The paper's main theoretical contribution is the introduction of a "dual backward step." This step is designed to compensate for the limited information propagation inherent in parallel Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the step size of the dual update. The authors establish an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, which is comparable to that of ADMM. Furthermore, the supplementary materials show that two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM. This result helps to unify and clarify the relationship between different decomposition methods based on the augmented Lagrangian.

Experimental results demonstrate that PDMM is effective when compared with ADMM and its variants. These empirical results are somewhat counter-intuitive, as Jacobian-type methods are generally known to converge more slowly than their Gauss-Seidel counterparts. An elaboration on this point, explaining why PDMM overcomes this tendency, would significantly strengthen the paper.

The novel dual backward step appears to be the key mechanism that enables the method to achieve convergence under general conditions similar to those for ADMM. Overall, the proposed method offers a valuable alternative and opens up new possibilities for distributed optimization.
