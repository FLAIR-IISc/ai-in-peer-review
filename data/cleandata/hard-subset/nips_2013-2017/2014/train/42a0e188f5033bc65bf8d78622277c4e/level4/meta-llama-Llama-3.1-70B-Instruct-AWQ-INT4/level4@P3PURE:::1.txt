This paper considers the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. A variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), is proposed to deal with the multiple blocks case. The PDMM method randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable. This process allows for parallel implementation of the updates to the primal variable. The authors also establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method, demonstrating its generality by showing that it encompasses several variants of ADMM.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept this paper, pending revisions that address the following questions and comments:

1. The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) could be clarified. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated or only a single block "i". Based on my understanding, only a block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but the method for selecting "i" is not explicitly stated. This should be clarified in the revision.

2. In the proof of Theorem 2, it appears that "\sum_{i=1}^I" is missing from the expression.

3. The discussion following Theorem 2 could be expanded. As the main result of the paper, Theorem 2 provides the convergence rate of the proposed PDMM method. Two natural questions arise: Firstly, can Theorem 2 be shown to be consistent with the convergence rates of other ADMM variants by appropriately choosing parameters such as K and J? Secondly, given that the transformation matrix A is split into multiple blocks, what is the optimal way to split it, according to the theorem?

4. I am curious to see a comparison with the tensor completion problem ("tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. Do the authors have any insight into which variant of ADMM is optimal for this problem?

Minor corrections:

1. In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".

2. In line 217, the space before "Section" should be removed.

Overall, I am positive about accepting this paper. However, I expect more discussion to follow Theorem 2 in the revised version.
