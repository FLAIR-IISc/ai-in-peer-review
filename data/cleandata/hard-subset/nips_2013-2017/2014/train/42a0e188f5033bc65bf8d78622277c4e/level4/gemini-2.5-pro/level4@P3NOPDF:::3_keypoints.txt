- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks in parallel, then applies a backward step to update duals.  
- Backward step is crucial: makes dual updates more conservative and enables global convergence.  
- Framework supports a proximal term in primal updates, simplifying subproblems.  
- Theoretical results: global convergence with iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J total blocks.  
- Parallelization advantage: K selected block updates can run simultaneously, fitting multi-core and distributed systems.  
- Improved convergence properties: allows relatively large step-sizes and can be faster than sADMM, more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Empirical validation: promising results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, achieving target accuracy faster than competing methods.  
- Weakness: experiments lack a true parallel implementation—results appear to be serial.  
- Weakness: introduces extra tuning parameters versus traditional ADMM, with limited guidance on setting them.  
- Overall: well-written paper with solid theory and experiments; a promising contribution despite missing parallel-system evaluations.
