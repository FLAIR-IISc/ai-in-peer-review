- Proposes PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant that updates K randomly selected blocks per iteration.  
- Each iteration performs a primal block update followed by a backward dual step that makes the dual update more conservative.  
- The backward dual step is crucial for guaranteeing global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term in the primal update to handle certain problems.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis establishing global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Shows convergence rate O(J/(T K)) for J blocks when K blocks are selected per iteration.  
- Main practical advantages: full parallelization of the primal step and support for relatively large step sizes.  
- Can use larger step sizes than sADMM, offers more flexibility than PJADMM, and greater parallelizability than GSADMM.  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PADMM reaches target accuracy with reduced computational time.  
- Experiments did not report results for a parallel implementation.  
- Downsides: PADMM has three tuning parameters that can be less convenient than traditional ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written contribution that matches ADMM’s convergence rate while enabling parallelization and showing promising practical performance.
