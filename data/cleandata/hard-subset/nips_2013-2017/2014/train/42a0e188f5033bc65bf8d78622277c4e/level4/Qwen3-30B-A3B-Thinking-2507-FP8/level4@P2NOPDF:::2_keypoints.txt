- Introduces PDMM, a parallel direction method of multipliers for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints, using Jacobian-style multi-block updates.  
- Key innovation: a dual backward step that adaptively reduces the dual step size to compensate for reduced information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes convergence guarantees under general conditions with O(1/T) iteration complexity comparable to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Empirical results indicate PDMM outperforms ADMM and its variants in the tested scenarios.  
- The faster convergence is counterintuitive given usual slower behavior of Jacobian methods; the paper needs to explain why PDMM achieves speedups.  
- Reviewer asks for deeper analysis on whether the dual backward step’s adaptive step size fundamentally alters convergence dynamics versus standard Jacobian methods.  
- Reviewer requests clarification on whether observed speedups are consistent across problem classes or depend on specific problem structure.  
- The dual backward step is identified as the novel core contribution that enables convergence where standard Jacobian updates fail.  
- Writing is generally clear but needs minor grammatical fixes (examples provided).  
- Supplementary material effectively contextualizes prior work and strengthens the theoretical narrative.  
- Overall assessment: novel, rigorous, and valuable for distributed optimization, but would be strengthened by theoretical justification of the counterintuitive empirical performance.
