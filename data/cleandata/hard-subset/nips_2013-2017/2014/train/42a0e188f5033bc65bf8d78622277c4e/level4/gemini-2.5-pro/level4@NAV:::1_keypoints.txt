- Addresses block-separable minimization with linear constraints and introduces PDMM, a multi-block ADMM variant.  
- PDMM randomly selects K primal blocks to update in parallel and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable; proves a sublinear convergence rate and generalizes several ADMM variants.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and reviewer supports acceptance.  
- Major issue: equations (5)–(6) are ambiguous about whether y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} updates apply to full vectors or single blocks, and the selection rule for index i is unspecified.  
- Theorem 2 likely has a missing "\sum_{i=1}^I" term after the opening brace; this appears to be an omission.  
- The paper needs a much stronger discussion of Theorem 2’s implications, including whether its rate recovers known rates for special-case ADMM variants (appropriate choices of K, J).  
- Need guidance from the analysis on how to partition the matrix A to achieve faster convergence.  
- Ask for intuition or comparison regarding application to tensor completion and which ADMM variant might be optimal there.  
- Minor typos: missing space after "min" in equation (9); remove space before "Section" on line 217.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and requests a revised version with clarifications and expanded discussion after Theorem 2.
