This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. The authors propose a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), called Proximal Dual Method of Multipliers (PDMM), designed to efficiently handle problems with multiple blocks. PDMM randomly selects *K* blocks of the primal variable for updating and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal variable update can be parallelized. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrate that it generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

This paper is well-written and easy to understand. I recommend acceptance. My specific questions and comments are detailed below:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Do these equations update all coordinates of *y<sup>t+1</sup>* and *ŷ<sup>t+1</sup>*, or only the coordinates corresponding to a single block “*i*”? My understanding is that only a block of the dual variable *y* is updated, but the selection process for *i* is not explicitly stated. This should be clarified in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, I believe a summation symbol “\sum<sub>i=1</sub><sup>I</sup>” is missing from the proof, following the opening brace.

3) The discussion following Theorem 2 should be expanded. Theorem 2 presents the main result of this paper – the convergence rate of PDMM. Given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, a natural question arises: is Theorem 2, with appropriate parameter choices for *K* and *J*, consistent with the established convergence rates of these existing variants? Furthermore, since the transformation matrix *A* is split into multiple blocks, what is the optimal block decomposition strategy according to the theorem?

4) I am interested in a comparison with the tensor completion problem (as described in “tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012”), which also exhibits a multi-block structure. Do you have any insights into which ADMM variant might perform best in that context?

Minor points:

1) In equation (9), a space is missing after “min”.
2) On line 217, please remove the space before “Section”. 

I reiterate my positive assessment and recommend acceptance. Expanding the discussion after Theorem 2, as suggested above, would further strengthen the paper.
