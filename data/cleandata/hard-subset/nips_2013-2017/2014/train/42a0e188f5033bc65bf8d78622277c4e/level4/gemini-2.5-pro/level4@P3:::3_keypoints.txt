- Proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate algorithm that updates a random subset of K blocks in parallel to address multi-block ADMM issues.  
- Introduces a dual “backward step” to temper dual ascent and prevent divergence in parallel ADMM variants.  
- Uses an adaptive dual step-size rule based on constraint sparsity and the number of blocks K.  
- Provides global convergence proof and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Unifies existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) within the PDMM framework, linking primal conservativeness to dual aggressiveness.  
- Demonstrates applicability on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, showing algorithmic flexibility.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches baselines (GSADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM) on non-trivial problems.  
- Includes an ablation study on the effect of the number of parallel blocks K, offering practical insights.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and assumptions align with standard ADMM literature.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides strong motivation and intuitive explanations.  
- Experiments were run sequentially; no parallel implementation provided to validate claimed parallel efficiency or scalability.  
- Practical parameter tuning deviates from the theoretical rule (Equation 8); tuned τi and νi differ from theory, raising usability concerns.  
- Missing discussion/analysis on sensitivity and performance gap between theoretical and empirically optimal step sizes.  
- Overall assessment: significant, novel, and well-supported contribution with strong theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance despite noted implementation and tuning gaps.
