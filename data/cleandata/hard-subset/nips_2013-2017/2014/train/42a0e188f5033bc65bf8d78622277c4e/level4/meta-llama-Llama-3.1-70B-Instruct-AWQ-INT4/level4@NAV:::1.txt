This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints by introducing a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), referred to as PDMM. This method involves randomly selecting K blocks of the primal variable for updates and utilizing a backward step to refine the dual variable, allowing for parallel implementation of the primal variable update process. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method, which generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it suitable for publication. However, I have some questions and comments to ensure clarity and completeness:

1. The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) requires clarification. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated or only a single block "i". My interpretation is that only a block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but the selection process for "i" is not explicitly stated. This should be addressed in the revision.

2. In the proof of Theorem 2, it appears that "\sum_{i=1}^I" is missing after the opening brace.

3. Further discussion on Theorem 2, the primary result of this paper, would be beneficial. The theorem provides the convergence rate of the proposed PDMM method, which generalizes several ADMM variants. It would be interesting to explore whether Theorem 2 is consistent with the convergence rates of these variants when parameters like K and J are properly chosen. Additionally, given that the transformation matrix A is split into multiple blocks, it would be useful to discuss the optimal way to split it based on the theorem.

4. I am curious to know whether the authors have any insights into the comparison with the tensor completion problem ("tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. Specifically, it would be interesting to know which ADMM variant is optimal for this problem.

Minor corrections:

1. In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".

2. On line 217, the space before "Section" should be removed.

Overall, I am positive about accepting this paper, pending the inclusion of additional discussion following Theorem 2.
