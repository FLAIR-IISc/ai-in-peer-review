- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that samples K blocks per iteration, updates those primal blocks in parallel, then applies a conservative backward dual step.
- Backward dual step is motivated as tempering the dual update to stabilize the method and ensure global convergence.
- Allows adding proximal terms to primal subproblems to simplify and speed up their solutions.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(K T)) after T iterations, matching classical ADMM’s asymptotic rate while enabling parallelism.
- Key advantage: full parallelization of primal updates across selected blocks.
- Key advantage: permits relatively large step sizes (can be faster than sADMM), is more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso are promising—reaches target accuracy with reduced computation time.
- Missing evaluation: no reported wall-clock speedups from actual parallel/multi-core or distributed implementations.
- Practical drawback: requires tuning three parameters, less convenient than single-parameter tuning in traditional ADMM.
- Overall judgment: solid, well-written contribution with sound theory and encouraging experiments; would benefit from parallel runtime reporting and parameter-tuning guidance.
