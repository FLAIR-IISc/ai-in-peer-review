- Paper proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM updating K primal blocks in parallel with a backward dual step and proves sublinear convergence.  
- Work generalizes several ADMM variants, is clearly written, technically sound, and makes a solid contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance pending revisions.  
- Major concern: Equations (5)–(6) are ambiguous about whether y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated for all coordinates or only the selected block; block selection mechanism must be specified.  
- Major concern: Theorem 2 proof (line 217) appears to omit a summation index “\sum_{i=1}^I”; this typographical/math error must be corrected.  
- Major concern: Theorem 2 needs deeper discussion—explicitly show recovery of known ADMM convergence rates for special parameter choices (e.g., K=1, J=1).  
- Major concern: Discuss how to choose an optimal block partitioning of A (e.g., based on sparsity, condition number, problem structure) since convergence depends on the block structure.  
- Minor: Typo in Equation (9) — add a space after “min”; and remove the extra space before “Section” at line 217.  
- Additional note: Briefly discuss applicability to tensor completion and whether PDMM offers advantages over existing methods for that problem class.  
- Conclusion: Strong paper with rigorous analysis; addressing the algorithmic clarity, proof correction, and deeper theoretical/practical discussion will substantially improve the manuscript.
