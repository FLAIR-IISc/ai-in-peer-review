- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate ADMM variant that updates K random blocks per iteration for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.
- Unifies and generalizes existing Jacobian ADMM variants, showing sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.
- Provides theoretical insight into the dual backward step parameter introduced by PDMM.
- Develops a randomized block coordinate approach for overlapping groups, addressing an open problem for such methods.
- Proves convergence rates comparable to existing methods while allowing more flexible step size selection based on matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible to readers familiar with ADMM.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound with well-motivated assumptions and coherent proof strategy.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing against relevant baselines on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms existing methods in runtime and matches or improves convergence behavior.
- Authors acknowledge trade-offs between number of blocks updated and subproblem complexity.
- Originality: PDMM’s randomized parallel updates and step-size strategy provide a distinct, meaningful contribution and clarify relations between sADMM and PJADMM.
- Significance: Practical, theoretically grounded method with strong potential for large-scale machine learning and structured sparsity applications.
- Minor concerns to address: clarify dual variable updates in equations (5)–(7), fix missing summation index in Theorem 2, and more explicitly compare convergence rates when specializing PDMM to sADMM/PJADMM.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution with solid theory and experiments; minor fixes are easily addressed.
