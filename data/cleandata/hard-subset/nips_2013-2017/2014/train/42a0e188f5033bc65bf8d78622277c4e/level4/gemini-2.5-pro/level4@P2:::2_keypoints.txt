- Addresses minimizing a sum of block-separable convex functions under linear equality constraints, focusing on multi-block ADMM extensions.  
- Proposes PDMM: a parallel, randomized block-coordinate primal update method.  
- Introduces a novel "dual backward step" to stabilize dual updates and ensure convergence with parallel primal updates.  
- Presents an adaptive dual step-size that leverages constraint-matrix sparsity and the number of updated blocks.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity under standard assumptions.  
- Shows PDMM unifies prior methods (sADMM and PJADMM) as special cases.  
- Empirically validates PDMM on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, showing competitive or superior performance.  
- Strength: novel and significant technical contribution addressing Jacobian-style ADMM convergence.  
- Strength: inherently parallel and practical for distributed/multi-core environments; handles overlapping regularizers.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and clear presentation.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments with convincing baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: needs deeper intuition on why PDMM (Jacobian-type) can outperform Gauss-Seidel in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks practical guidance/heuristics for choosing the per-iteration block count K.  
- Weakness: experiments run sequentially; requires discussion of real parallel implementation overheads (communication/synchronization).  
- Recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, theoretically sound, and practically relevant contribution.
