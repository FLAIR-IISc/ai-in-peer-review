This paper introduces the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex objectives under linear constraints. In contrast to standard ADMM—which typically employs Gauss–Seidel updates and is primarily established for two-block settings—this work performs multi-block, Jacobi-style primal updates. The central technical idea is a backward correction on the dual variables that moderates the dual ascent, compensating for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobi updates by effectively shrinking the dual step. The authors establish global convergence with O(1/T) ergodic complexity, in line with ADMM-type results.

The supplementary material shows that sADMM and PJADMM emerge as special cases within the PDMM framework, which helps clarify how these augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods relate to one another. Empirical results demonstrate that PDMM is competitive with or outperforms ADMM and several variants. These findings are somewhat counterintuitive, since Jacobi-type methods often converge more slowly than Gauss–Seidel schemes; further discussion explaining when and why PDMM can surpass Gauss–Seidel variants would be valuable.

The dual backward step appears to be a novel and crucial mechanism for obtaining convergence under broad conditions comparable to those of ADMM. Overall, the method expands the range of practical options for parallel and distributed optimization.
