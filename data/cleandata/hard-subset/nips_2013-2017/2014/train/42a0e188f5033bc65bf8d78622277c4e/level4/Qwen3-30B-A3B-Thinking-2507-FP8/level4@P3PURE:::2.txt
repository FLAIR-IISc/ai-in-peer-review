

This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which employs Gauss-Seidel updates restricted to two blocks, PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian manner across multiple blocks. The primary theoretical contribution is the dual backward step, which compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size. The paper establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, analogous to ADMM.  

Supplementary materials demonstrate that two prior methods—sADMM and PJADMM—are special cases of PDMM, providing a clearer understanding of their relationship within the landscape of augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Experimental results confirm PDMM's effectiveness compared to ADMM and other variants. However, the results appear somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel-type methods. The authors should elaborate on why PDMM overcomes this general trend. The dual backward step is novel and essential for ensuring convergence under general conditions, matching ADMM's robustness. Overall, PDMM offers more flexible alternatives for distributed optimization.
