- Proposal: Introduces PDMM to minimize block-separable convex functions with linear constraints using parallel (Jacobian) primal updates.  
- Contrast with ADMM: Unlike ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel two-block updates, PDMM updates multiple blocks in parallel.  
- Main innovation: Introduces a dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing dual step size.  
- Convergence: Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, comparable to ADMM.  
- Relation to prior work: Thoroughly connects PDMM to existing methods; shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-organized and clear, with technical proofs in the supplement for accessibility.  
- Experiments: Empirical results demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and variants.  
- Counterintuitive finding: PDMM can converge faster than Gauss–Seidel methods despite using Jacobian updates, attributed to the dual backward step.  
- Strengths: Novel dual backward step, proven convergence, strong empirical performance, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: Limited discussion of broader implications/applications of the dual backward step.  
- Weaknesses: Insufficient analysis of trade-offs between number of blocks and subproblem efficiency.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel contribution with theoretical guarantees and supportive experiments, presented clearly.  
- Rejection arguments: Needs deeper analysis of the dual backward step’s implications and block-efficiency trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: Significant and well-presented contribution to augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods, with some areas for further analysis.
