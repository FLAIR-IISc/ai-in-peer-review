- Proposes PDMM for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints, using Jacobian (parallel) primal updates to handle multiple blocks vs ADMM’s two-block Gauss–Seidel updates.  
- Main technical contribution is the dual backward step, which compensates limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, analogous to ADMM’s results.  
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments indicate PDMM outperforms ADMM and other variants in the evaluated tasks.  
- Results are somewhat counterintuitive since Jacobian-type methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel; the paper should elaborate on this behavior.  
- The dual backward step appears novel and is crucial for achieving convergence under general conditions.  
- PDMM offers additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.
