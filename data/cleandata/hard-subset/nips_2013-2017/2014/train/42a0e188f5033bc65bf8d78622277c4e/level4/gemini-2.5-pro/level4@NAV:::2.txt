This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for the minimization of block-separable convex functions under linear constraints. Unlike the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which utilizes Gauss-Seidel updates and is generally limited to two-block problems, this work presents a method that updates primal blocks in a parallel, Jacobian fashion suitable for multiple blocks. The central contribution of the paper is the development of a "dual backward step," which compensates for the reduced information propagation of Jacobian updates by adaptively decreasing the dual update step size. The authors establish an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, consistent with the theoretical guarantees for ADMM.

The authors demonstrate (in the supplementary materials) that two existing algorithms, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of their proposed framework. This provides a valuable unification and a clearer understanding of the relationships between various augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. The experimental section validates the efficacy of PDMM in comparison to ADMM and its derivatives. These results are somewhat counter-intuitive, given that Jacobian-style methods typically exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel approaches; the paper would be strengthened if the authors could provide more intuition on this point. The novel dual backward step appears to be the key mechanism enabling PDMM to achieve convergence under conditions as general as those for ADMM. Overall, this method offers a valuable new alternative and expands the possibilities for designing distributed optimization algorithms.
