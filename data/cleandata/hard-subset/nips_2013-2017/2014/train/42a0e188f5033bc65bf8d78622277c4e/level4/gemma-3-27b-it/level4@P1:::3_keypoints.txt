- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that selects K of J blocks per iteration and updates primal then dual variables.
- Uses a backward step for dual updates to produce more conservative updates and enable global convergence.
- Allows adding a proximal term in the primal update to simplify solving subproblems.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity.
- Shows convergence rate O(1/T) independent of J and K, comparable to standard ADMM.
- Main advantages: fully parallelizable primal updates and potential for larger step sizes than competing methods.
- Can outperform sADMM by permitting larger update stepsizes.
- Offers more flexibility than PJADMM and better parallelizability than GSADMM.
- Empirical validation on RPCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising accuracy and competitive runtime.
- Does not report results from a parallel implementation, missing evidence of actual speedup.
- Requires tuning three parameters (τi, νi, ηjt), which may be more complex than standard ADMM tuning.
- Convergence rate matches ADMM but does not provide acceleration beyond O(1/T).
- Overall assessment: novel and valuable contribution with solid theory and experiments, but would benefit from parallel implementation results and simpler parameter tuning.
