- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM for multi-block problems that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks, then performs a backward dual update.  
- Backward dual step is crucial to make the dual update conservative and enable global convergence.  
- Supports adding a proximal term to the primal update to simplify subproblems.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity; convergence rate O(J/(T K)) after T iterations when selecting K of J blocks.  
- Enables full parallelization of the primal step.  
- Permits relatively large step sizes, potentially yielding faster convergence than stochastic ADMM.  
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising results and reduced computation time to reach target accuracy.  
- Does not report performance with an actual parallel implementation (no wall-clock parallel results).  
- Harder to tune than standard ADMM due to three algorithm parameters.  
- Overall a valuable contribution: matches ADMM convergence rate while enabling parallelization, with solid theory and experiments.
