- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K random blocks per iteration for multi-block problems.  
- Selected primal blocks are updated in parallel; dual variable updated via a backward (conservative) step.  
- The backward dual step is key to making the dual update conservative and enabling global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term to primal updates to simplify subproblems.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.  
- Main advantages: full parallelization of primal updates and tolerance for relatively large step sizes.  
- Empirical/relative claims: can be faster than sADMM (larger step sizes), more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show promising results and faster attainment of target accuracy.  
- Limitation: paper does not report results from a truly parallel implementation.  
- Practical drawback: tuning three PADMM parameters may be less convenient than standard ADMM.  
- Overall: well-written paper with solid theory and experiments; matches ADMM convergence rate while adding parallelization benefits and strong practical performance.
