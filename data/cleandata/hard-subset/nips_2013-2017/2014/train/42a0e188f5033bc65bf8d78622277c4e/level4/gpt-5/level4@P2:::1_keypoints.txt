- Proposes PDMM: a randomized, parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual “backward” step, handling overlapping blocks and setting dual stepsizes from A’s sparsity and number of updated blocks; proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rate, recovering sADMM and PJADMM as limits.

- Situates PDMM against prior work: addresses divergence of naive multi-block ADMM, contrasts with BSUMM, PJADMM, and RBSUMM, and offers a principled parallel randomized multi-block scheme with a clean O(1/T) guarantee.

- Strength: Clear motivation and strong connections to applications like RPCA and overlapping group lasso.

- Strength: Simple, implementable algorithm with parallel primal updates and a principled dual backward step.

- Strength: Convergence analysis with constant stepsizes and explicit dependence on sparsity and number of updated blocks K.

- Strength: Insightful unification showing sADMM and PJADMM as special cases, clarifying step-size interpretation.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance versus relevant baselines.

- Weakness/question 1: Algorithmic clarity—unclear whether all dual blocks yi and ŷi are updated each iteration or only a subset; need explicit statement of dual update schedule and its interaction with randomized primal selection.

- Weakness/question 2: Theorem 2 notation error—missing ∑_{i=1}^I in the first displayed inequality; needs correction.

- Weakness/question 3a: Theorem 2 — request explicit specialization showing the theorem reduces to known convergence rates for sADMM (K=J, exact solves) and PJADMM (large proximal terms, τi=1, νi=0).

- Weakness/question 3b: Theorem 2 — ask whether bounds depending on di and K̃i can guide principled partitioning of A (e.g., balancing di, exploiting sparsity) and request practical heuristics.

- Weakness/question 4: Additional application—ask for guidance on which variant/parameters (PDMM with specific K, τi, νi; sADMM; PJADMM) are preferable for tensor completion problems and qualitative recommendations.

- Pro-acceptance points: timely, practical impact; conceptually clean and parallelizable algorithm; solid theory with constant stepsizes; strong empirical validation.

- Con-acceptance points: minor ambiguities in algorithm/theorem presentation; limited guidance on block partitioning and parameter selection; missing tensor completion experiment.

- Minor comments: formatting fixes—add space after “min.” in Eq. (9) and remove extra space before “Section” on line 217.

- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested fixes to clarify dual updates, correct Theorem 2 summation, and expand discussion on special-case consistency and block partitioning.
