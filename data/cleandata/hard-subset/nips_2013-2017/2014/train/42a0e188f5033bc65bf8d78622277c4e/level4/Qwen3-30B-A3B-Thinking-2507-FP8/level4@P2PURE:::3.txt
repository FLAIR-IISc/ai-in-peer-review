

This paper introduces a randomized parallel ADMM variant (PADMM) designed for problems with multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM selects K random blocks, updates the corresponding primal variables, and then performs a dual update via a backward step. The authors demonstrate that the backward step ensures a more conservative dual update, facilitating global convergence. Additionally, PADMM permits the inclusion of a proximal term in the primal update, simplifying the solution of certain subproblems. Theoretical analysis establishes global convergence and an iteration complexity of $O(J/(TK))$ after T iterations, where J denotes the total number of blocks and K is the number of blocks updated per iteration.  

The primary advantages of PADMM are: (1) full parallelization of the primal step, and (2) the ability to employ larger step sizes compared to competing methods. Specifically, PADMM outperforms sADMM due to its larger allowable step sizes, offers greater flexibility than PJADMM, and achieves higher parallelism than GSADMM. The authors evaluate PADMM on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, yielding promising results where the algorithm attains target accuracy with reduced computation time. However, the paper does not report performance metrics for a parallel implementation, and tuning the three PADMM parameters is less convenient than for standard ADMM.  

Overall, this work presents a valuable contribution to solving multi-constraint ADMM-type problems. While matching ADMM's convergence rate, PADMM enables parallelization and demonstrates strong empirical performance. The paper is well-written, with both theoretical and empirical components being robust and well-supported.
