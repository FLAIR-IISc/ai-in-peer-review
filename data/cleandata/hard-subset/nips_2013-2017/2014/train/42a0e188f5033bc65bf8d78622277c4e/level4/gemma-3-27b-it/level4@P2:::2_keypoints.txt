- Introduces PDMM: a Jacobian-style parallel update for multi-block convex minimization with a novel "dual backward step."  
- Addresses limitations of extending ADMM beyond two blocks by enabling parallel primal updates.  
- Dual backward step is the key novelty that compensates for reduced information flow in parallel updates.  
- Proves global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity, matching ADMM rates.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases, offering a unifying framework for existing methods.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrate practical effectiveness versus ADMM variants.  
- Highlights potential applicability to distributed optimization due to its parallel nature.  
- The superiority of Jacobian over Gauss-Seidel is counterintuitive and needs deeper explanation.  
- Sensitivity to step-size parameters (τi and νi) is a concern; practical tuning guidance or sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Notation could be streamlined, particularly around block and variable indexing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important multi-block ADMM challenge with novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient explanation for the Jacobian method's strong performance and assumes reader familiarity with ADMM variants.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
