- Proposes PDMM, a parallel (Jacobian) update algorithm with a novel "dual backward step" for multi-block convex minimization with linear constraints.
- Dual backward step reduces dual step size to compensate for slower information propagation in parallel updates.
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, comparable to standard ADMM.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying several augmented-Lagrangian methods.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso are convincing and show competitive or superior performance.
- Algorithm is well-suited to parallel and randomized block updates, making it practical for large-scale problems.
- Novelty: dual backward step is an effective and theoretically-grounded mechanism for multi-block parallel ADMM convergence.
- Theoretical unification provides deeper insight into the role of dual updates across methods.
- Practical relevance: design matches modern computing architectures and distributed settings.
- Weakness: paper lacks detailed explanation for why PDMM is not only convergent but also empirically fast compared to Gauss-Seidel methods.
- Suggestion: add discussion on whether empirical speedups stem from parallelism, problem structure, or other specific properties of test problems.
