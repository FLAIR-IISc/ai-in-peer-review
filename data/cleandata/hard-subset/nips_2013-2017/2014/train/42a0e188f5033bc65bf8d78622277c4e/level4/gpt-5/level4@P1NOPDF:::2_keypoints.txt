- Proposes PDMM for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints, enabling parallel block updates.  
- Contrasts PDMM with ADMM: PDMM uses Jacobi (parallel) updates while ADMM uses Gauss–Seidel (sequential) updates and is effectively limited to two blocks.  
- Main technical contribution is a novel dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobi updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T)-type convergence guarantees for PDMM, analogous to ADMM.  
- Shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying several augmented Lagrangian–based decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and its variants.  
- Notes empirical results are somewhat counterintuitive because Jacobi methods usually converge slower than Gauss–Seidel ones.  
- Recommends the authors explain why PDMM can be competitive or superior, e.g., how the dual backward step offsets parallel-update drawbacks in terms of problem structure, conditioning, or communication/synchronization.  
- Concludes the dual backward step is novel and crucial for convergence under conditions similar to ADMM, broadening tools for distributed optimization with rigorous guarantees.
