- Proposes PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant using a palatalization approach for multi-block problems.  
- Each iteration randomly selects K blocks, updates selected primal blocks, then updates the dual via a backward step.  
- The backward dual update is crucially more conservative, enabling global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term to the primal update to simplify subproblem solutions.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving global convergence and iteration complexity; rate O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J blocks.  
- Enables full parallelization of the primal step.  
- Permits relatively large step sizes and can outperform sequential ADMM (sADMM).  
- Offers greater flexibility than PJADMM and better parallelizability than GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show reduced computational time to reach desired accuracy.  
- Authors did not report results from a parallel implementation.  
- PADMM requires tuning three parameters, which can be inconvenient.  
- Overall a notable contribution: matches ADMM convergence rate, is well-written, and has solid theoretical and empirical support.
