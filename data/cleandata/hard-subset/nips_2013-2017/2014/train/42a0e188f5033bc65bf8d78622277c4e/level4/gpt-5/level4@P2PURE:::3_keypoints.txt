- Proposes PADMM: a randomized, parallel ADMM variant that picks K of J blocks each iteration, updates primal blocks in parallel, then does a backward dual update.
- Backward dual step makes the dual update more conservative and is key to the global convergence guarantee.
- Allows adding a proximal term to primal updates to simplify certain subproblems.
- Theoretical global convergence and iteration complexity: rate O(J/(K T)) after T iterations.
- Main advantages: full parallelization of the primal step and allowance for relatively large step sizes.
- Can be faster than sADMM because it admits larger step sizes.
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PADMM reaches target accuracy with less computation time.
- Paper does not report results from an actual parallel implementation.
- Tuning PADMM’s three parameters is less convenient than tuning standard ADMM.
- Overall: solid theoretical and empirical contribution achieving ADMM-like convergence while enabling parallelization; paper is well written.
