- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K random blocks per iteration for primal updates and uses a backward dual update.  
- Backward dual step yields a more conservative dual update, enabling global convergence.  
- Allows an optional proximal term in the primal update, simplifying certain subproblems.  
- Theoretically proves global convergence and an iteration complexity of O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.  
- Key advantage: full parallelization of the primal step.  
- Allows larger step sizes than sADMM, improving performance.  
- Offers greater flexibility than PJADMM.  
- Achieves higher parallelism than GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show faster attainment of target accuracy and reduced computation time.  
- Missing: no reported performance metrics from a parallel implementation.  
- Practical drawback: tuning the three PADMM parameters is less convenient than standard ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution that matches ADMM convergence rate, enables parallelization, and is supported by solid theory and experiments.
