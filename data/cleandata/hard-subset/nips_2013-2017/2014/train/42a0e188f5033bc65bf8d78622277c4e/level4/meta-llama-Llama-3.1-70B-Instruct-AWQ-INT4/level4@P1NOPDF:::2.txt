This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel manner but is limited to two blocks, this work proposes updating the primal blocks in a Jacobian manner, allowing for multiple blocks. The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of a dual backward step, which compensates for the limited information propagation in the Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the step size for the dual updates. Similar to ADMM, the authors establish O(1/T) type convergence results for PDMM.

The authors demonstrate in the supplementary materials that two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed method, providing a clearer understanding of the relationship between different methods in the landscape of decomposition methods based on augmented Lagrangian. Experimental results show the effectiveness of the PDMM method compared to ADMM and other variants. However, the results are somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence compared to Gauss-Seidel-type methods. It would be beneficial to elaborate on this point, as it may provide valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed method.

The dual backward step in PDMM, which ensures convergence under general conditions similar to ADMM, appears to be a novel and crucial component. This step is key to obtaining convergence guarantees, and its introduction expands the possibilities for distributed optimization by offering an alternative to existing methods. Overall, the proposed method provides more alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
