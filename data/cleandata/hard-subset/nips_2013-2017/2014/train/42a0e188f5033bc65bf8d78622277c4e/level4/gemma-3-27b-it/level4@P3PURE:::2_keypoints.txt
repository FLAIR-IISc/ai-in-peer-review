- Proposes PDMM: parallel (Jacobian) updates for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints, unlike ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel and two-block limitation.
- Introduces a novel dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing dual step size.
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, similar to ADMM.
- Demonstrates sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM (in supplement), clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.
- Empirical results show PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants, which is counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods.
- Reviewer suggests further elaboration on why PDMM can beat Gauss–Seidel methods would be beneficial.
- The dual backward step appears novel and crucial for achieving convergence under general conditions comparable to ADMM.
- Method offers increased flexibility and new possibilities for distributed optimization.
