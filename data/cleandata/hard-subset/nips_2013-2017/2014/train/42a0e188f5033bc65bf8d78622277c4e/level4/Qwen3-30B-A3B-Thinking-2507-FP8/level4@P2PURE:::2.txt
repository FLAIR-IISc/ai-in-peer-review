

This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel fashion limited to two blocks, PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian manner for multiple blocks. The primary contribution is the dual backward step, which compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size. As with ADMM, the paper establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM. Supplementary materials demonstrate that two prior methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM, clarifying their relationship within the landscape of augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Experimental results show PDMM's effectiveness compared to ADMM and other variants. However, the results seem counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically yield slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel methods. The authors should elaborate on this apparent contradiction. The dual backward step, which ensures convergence under general conditions comparable to ADMM, appears novel and is central to the method's theoretical validity. Overall, PDMM provides additional alternatives and opportunities for distributed optimization.
