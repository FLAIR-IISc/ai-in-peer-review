- Proposes PDMM to minimize block-separable convex functions under linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM, PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian (parallel) fashion supporting multiple blocks.  
- Main contribution is the dual backward step, which compensates limited information propagation by effectively reducing dual step size.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence for PDMM, analogous to ADMM.  
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms ADMM variants in the tested settings.  
- Notes results are somewhat counterintuitive since Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel methods, and further explanation is requested.  
- Dual backward step appears novel and crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions.  
- Overall, PDMM provides an additional alternative for distributed optimization.
