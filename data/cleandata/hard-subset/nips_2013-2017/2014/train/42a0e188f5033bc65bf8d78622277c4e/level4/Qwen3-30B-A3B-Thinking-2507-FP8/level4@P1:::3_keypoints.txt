- Introduces PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.
- Updates K randomly selected blocks in parallel per iteration, with adaptive step sizes exploiting sparsity and block selection.
- Introduces a dual backward step to prevent overly aggressive implicit dual ascent and ensure conservative dual progress.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying their parameter choices.
- Provides global convergence guarantees and O(1/T) iteration complexity under constant step sizes (Theorem 2).
- Allows exact or inexact primal updates via Bregman divergence terms and handles overlapping blocks.
- Demonstrates PDMM permits larger step sizes than sADMM by adapting to sparsity (e.g., τ_i scaling with K and K̃_i).
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show significant speedups over sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM.
- PDMM1 (single-block updates) achieves the fastest runtime in RPCA despite simplicity.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and based on minimal assumptions, with clear connections to prior work.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility.
- Weakness: experiments are sequential (Matlab), so parallel-runtime benefits are not empirically demonstrated.
- Weakness: parameter tuning (τ_i, ν_i, K) requires careful selection, though guidelines are provided.
- Weakness: could better contrast PDMM’s constant-step-size convergence with RBSUMM’s decreasing-step approach and clarify the “greater step sizes” claim relative to sADMM.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, significant for scalable multi-block optimization, and recommended for acceptance.
