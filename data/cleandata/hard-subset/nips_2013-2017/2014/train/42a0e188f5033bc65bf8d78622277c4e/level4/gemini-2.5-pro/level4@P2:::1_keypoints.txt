1. Addresses multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints and proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant.  
2. PDMM randomly selects K primal blocks for parallel updates and adds a dual "backward step" to ensure convergence.  
3. Dual step sizes are adaptively chosen based on constraint-matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks.  
4. Positions PDMM within prior work, unifying methods like sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.  
5. Provides rigorous theory: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with a constant step size.  
6. Empirical results (Robust PCA, Overlapping Group Lasso) show PDMM outperforms several state-of-the-art methods.  
7. Strength: novel, flexible algorithm suitable for large-scale distributed optimization with strong theoretical guarantees.  
8. Strength: clear motivation for the dual backward step and generally well-written presentation.  
9. Weakness: dual-update description (eqs. 6–7) is ambiguous—clarify that updates apply for all i = 1,...,I.  
10. Weakness: Theorem 2 needs deeper discussion—explicitly show specialization to sADMM/PJADMM and implications for K and partitioning choices.  
11. Suggestion: expand experiments to additional applications (e.g., tensor completion) to demonstrate broader effectiveness.  
12. Minor corrections: check a missing summation/definition in Theorem 2, add space in Eq. (9) between "min" and "xj", and fix formatting (extra space at Line 217).  
13. Overall recommendation: high-quality, theoretically sound paper; reviewer recommends acceptance pending suggested clarifications and expansions.
