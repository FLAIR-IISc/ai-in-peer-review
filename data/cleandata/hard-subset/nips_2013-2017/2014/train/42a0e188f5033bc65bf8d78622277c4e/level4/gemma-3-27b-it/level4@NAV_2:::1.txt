This paper considers the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. A novel variant of ADMM, termed PDMM, is proposed to address the multi-block case, employing a randomized block selection strategy for primal variable updates and a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal update process is designed for parallel implementation. The paper establishes a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrates its generalization of several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to follow the proposed methodology and theoretical contributions. I recommend acceptance of this paper. Specific questions and comments are detailed below:

1) The algorithmic description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity regarding the scope of the updates. Specifically, is the update performed on all coordinates of  `y^{t+1}` and `\hat{y}^{t+1}`, or only a single block “i”? My understanding is that only a block of the dual variable “y” is updated, but the method for selecting “i” isn't explicitly stated. This point requires clarification in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, a summation symbol (“∑_{i=1}^I”) appears to be missing after the opening brace “{“ in the expression.

3) Further discussion regarding the implications of Theorem 2 is warranted. As the main result of the paper, Theorem 2 establishes the convergence rate of PDMM. First, given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, it would be insightful to explore whether the convergence rate achieved by PDMM, with appropriately chosen parameters (e.g., K, J), aligns with the known convergence rates of those specific variants. Second, considering the decomposition of the transformation matrix A into multiple blocks, it would be valuable to discuss the optimal strategy for this decomposition based on the theoretical findings presented in the theorem.

4) I am curious about the potential applicability of PDMM, or its variants, to the tensor completion problem (e.g., "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also exhibits a multi-block structure. Do you have any preliminary insights regarding which ADMM variant might be most effective in this context?

Minor Comments:

1) In equation (9), a space is missing immediately after the “min” keyword.
2) Line 217: Remove the extraneous space before “Section.” 

Overall, this is a strong paper with significant contributions. Addressing the points raised above, particularly expanding the discussion surrounding Theorem 2, will further enhance its impact and clarity.
