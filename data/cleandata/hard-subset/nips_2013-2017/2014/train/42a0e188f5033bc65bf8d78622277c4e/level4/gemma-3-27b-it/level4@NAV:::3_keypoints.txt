- Introduces PADMM: a randomized, parallel ADMM that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal variables, then uses a backward dual update.
- Backward dual step produces a more conservative update that the authors show is crucial for global convergence.
- Includes a proximal term in the primal update, simplifying solutions for certain problems.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis proving global convergence and O(J/(T K)) iteration complexity after T iterations (J = number of blocks).
- Enables full parallelization of the primal update step.
- Allows larger step sizes than competing methods, improving performance potential.
- Empirically outperforms sADMM due to larger step sizes.
- Offers greater flexibility than PJADMM and better parallelizability than GSADMM.
- Demonstrated on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso with promising results: reduced computation time while meeting accuracy targets.
- Lacks a reported performance evaluation using a parallel implementation.
- Requires tuning of three parameters, making parameter selection slightly more complex than standard ADMM.
- Overall a valuable contribution: comparable convergence to traditional ADMM, enables parallel computation, with strong theoretical and experimental support; paper is well-written.
