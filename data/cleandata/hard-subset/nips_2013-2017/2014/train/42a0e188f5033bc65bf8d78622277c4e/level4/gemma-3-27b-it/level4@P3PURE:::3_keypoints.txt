- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K blocks per iteration, updates selected primal blocks, then updates duals via backward substitution.  
- Backward substitution yields more conservative dual updates, which the authors show is crucial for global convergence.  
- Primal updates can include a proximal term, simplifying solutions for certain problems.  
- Theoretical analysis proves global convergence and gives iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.  
- Allows full parallelization of the primal update step.  
- Supports relatively large step sizes, potentially outperforming sADMM in speed.  
- Offers more flexibility than PJADMM and better parallelizability than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show promising accuracy and reduced computation time.  
- Lacks empirical results from a parallel implementation to directly support parallelization claims.  
- Introduces three tunable parameters compared to traditional ADMM, increasing implementation complexity.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with comparable convergence rate to standard ADMM, solid theory, good empirical performance, and clear writing.
