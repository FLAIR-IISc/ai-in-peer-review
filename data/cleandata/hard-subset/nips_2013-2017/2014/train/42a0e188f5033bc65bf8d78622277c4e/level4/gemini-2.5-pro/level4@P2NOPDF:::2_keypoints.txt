- Proposes PDMM, a parallel (Jacobian-style) algorithm for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints, unlike ADMM’s sequential (Gauss–Seidel) updates.  
- Main technical innovation is a "dual backward step" that effectively reduces the dual update step size to compensate for slower information propagation in parallel updates.  
- The authors prove an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, matching the standard ADMM rate.  
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying these methods under the proposed framework.  
- Experiments show PDMM performs competitively with ADMM and variants, which is somewhat surprising given typical expectations about Jacobian methods converging more slowly.  
- The reviewer requests more intuition or analysis on why PDMM performs so well—whether the dual backward step is the primary reason or if specific problem structures favor parallel updates.  
- The dual backward step is characterized as a novel, key technical contribution that enables fully parallel updates and convergence under conditions as general as ADMM, making PDMM practical for large-scale distributed optimization.
