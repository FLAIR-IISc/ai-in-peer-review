This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. The authors propose a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to handle the multiple blocks case. PDMM randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable, allowing for parallel implementation of the primal variable update process. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method, which generalizes several variants of ADMM.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept this paper, pending revisions that address the following questions and comments.

1. The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated or just a single block "i". Based on my understanding, only a block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but the method for selecting "i" is not explicitly stated. This should be clarified in the revision.

2. In Theorem 2, it appears that the summation symbol "\sum_{i=1}^I" is missing from the proof.

3. The discussion surrounding Theorem 2, which presents the main result of this paper, is somewhat limited. The authors should provide more insight into the implications of this theorem. Firstly, since PDMM generalizes several variants of ADMM, it would be beneficial to discuss whether Theorem 2 is consistent with the convergence rates of these variants when parameters like K and J are properly chosen. Secondly, given that the transformation matrix A is split into multiple blocks, the authors should elaborate on the optimal way to split it based on their theorem.

4. I am interested in seeing a comparison with the tensor completion problem ("tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. The authors may want to investigate which variant of ADMM is optimal for this problem.

Minor corrections:

1. In equation (9), a space is missing after the "min" symbol.
2. In line 217, the space before "Section" should be removed.

Overall, I am positive about accepting this paper. However, I expect more discussion and clarification on the points mentioned above, particularly surrounding Theorem 2.
