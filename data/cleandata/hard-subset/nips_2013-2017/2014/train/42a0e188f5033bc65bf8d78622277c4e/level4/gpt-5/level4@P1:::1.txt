This paper studies convex optimization with block-separable objectives under linear constraints and proposes the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), a randomized, parallel variant of ADMM for the multi-block setting. At each iteration, PDMM selects K primal blocks to update in parallel and applies a “backward step” to temper the dual update. The method leverages sparsity in A and the number of updated blocks to set stepsizes, establishes global convergence with an O(1/T) ergodic rate under constant stepsizes, and shows how to handle overlapping blocks. The authors connect PDMM to existing multi-block ADMM variants, notably sADMM (two-block with splitting) and PJADMM (proximal Jacobian ADMM), as limiting cases. Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso indicate favorable performance.

Relation to prior work: Two-block ADMM is well studied, but its direct extension to multiple blocks can diverge. Prior remedies include Gauss–Seidel variants (e.g., BSUMM), randomized single-block updates (RBSUMM), and proximal Jacobian variants (PJADMM). PDMM contributes a unified perspective: a parallel randomized multi-block scheme with a dual backward step, stepsizes informed by A’s sparsity, and a theoretical O(1/T) rate under constant stepsizes.

Strengths:
- Clear motivation and a simple algorithmic idea (randomized parallel block updates + dual backward step).
- Theoretically grounded: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with explicit stepsize rules depending on K and the block sparsity di.
- Unifies sADMM and PJADMM as special cases, clarifying when conservative vs. full dual steps are appropriate.
- Practical relevance: ability to handle overlapping groups; strong empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.

Weaknesses and questions:
1) Algorithmic clarity for the dual update: Equations (6)–(7) read as full dual-block updates, but the text could be clearer about whether y and ŷ are updated for all i at each iteration or only for selected blocks. If only a subset is updated, how are the dual indices chosen? Please clarify in the algorithm box and text which dual blocks are updated per iteration and how i is selected.

2) Possible typo in Theorem 2: In the first bound, right after the opening brace “{”, I believe a summation over i (i.e., ∑i=1I) is missing. This matches the structure of your proof and the definition of βi.

3) Discussion after Theorem 2:
   - Consistency with known variants: Since PDMM specializes to sADMM and PJADMM for particular parameter choices (e.g., K = J with τi = 1/J, νi = 1 − 1/J for sADMM; sufficiently large proximal terms for PJADMM), it would be helpful to state explicitly that Theorem 2 recovers their O(1/T) ergodic rates (and any improved constants when exploiting sparsity). Similarly, for K = 1, νi = 0 and τi = 1/(2J − 1), can you relate the bound to RBCD-style rates?
   - Guidance on block partitioning: Your bounds depend on di = |N(i)| and K̃i = min{di, K}. From Theorem 2 and the definitions of βi and ζi, what partitioning of A (or grouping of variables) is theoretically preferable? For instance, should one aim to minimize max_i di, balance the di across rows, or align blocks with A’s sparsity pattern to maximize τi and reduce the dual backward step? Any practical heuristics derived from the theory would be valuable.

4) Additional application: Tensor completion (e.g., “Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data,” 2012) naturally yields a multi-block structure (e.g., via multiple low-rank unfoldings). Do you have insights, either theoretical or empirical, on which ADMM variant (PDMM vs. sADMM vs. PJADMM) is preferable in this setting, and how K and the dual steps should be set relative to the tensor’s mode-wise sparsity/structure?

Minor comments:
- Eq. (9): add a space after “min”.
- Line 217: remove the extra space before “Section”.

Overall evaluation: The paper is clearly written, technically solid, and provides a unifying and practically useful perspective on multi-block ADMM with provable guarantees and good empirical performance. I am positive about acceptance. I recommend clarifying the dual-update mechanism and adding discussion after Theorem 2 to connect the rate to known variants and to offer guidance on block partitioning.
