- Introduces PDMM: a parallel direction method of multipliers for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM's Gauss-Seidel two-block updates, PDMM uses Jacobian-style updates for multiple primal blocks.  
- Core contribution: a dual backward step that adaptively reduces the dual step size to address limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, matching ADMM’s rate.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness relative to ADMM and its variants.  
- Notes a counterintuitive result: Jacobian-type methods usually converge slower than Gauss-Seidel, and the authors should elaborate on this discrepancy.  
- The dual backward step is novel and essential for achieving convergence under general conditions comparable to ADMM.  
- PDMM offers greater flexibility for distributed optimization.
