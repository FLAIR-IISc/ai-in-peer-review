- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primals, and uses a backward dual update.  
- Backward dual step enforces conservative dual updates, enabling global convergence guarantees.  
- Adds a proximal term in the primal update to simplify/accelerate certain subproblem solves.  
- Theoretical results prove global convergence and an iteration complexity of O(J/(K T)) after T iterations.  
- Advantages: full parallelization of the primal step and ability to use larger step sizes than related methods.  
- Claims improved performance over sADMM (larger step sizes), more flexibility than PJADMM, and greater parallelism than GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show significant runtime reductions to reach target accuracy.  
- Major omission: no empirical evaluation of a parallel implementation, so claimed parallel speedup is unverified.  
- Practical drawback: requires tuning three hyperparameters (ρ, η, γ), making it less user-friendly than standard ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: technically rigorous, clearly written, strong empirical/theoretical contributions, and a good fit for NIPS despite noted limitations.
