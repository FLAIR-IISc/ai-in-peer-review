- Introduces PDMM, a parallel ADMM variant for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Novel dual backward step compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates and is crucial for convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step sizes under standard assumptions.  
- Unifies previous methods by showing sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.  
- Extends PDMM to overlapping blocks for composite minimization, addressing an open problem for randomized block coordinate methods.  
- Derives step-size rules based on constraint sparsity and number of updated blocks, aiding practical implementation.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound with clear mathematical derivations and connections to prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PDMM outperforms ADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM in convergence and runtime.  
- PDMM with K=1 converges faster than RBSUMM (random one-block updates) in experiments.  
- Performance is robust to parameter choices; authors provide detailed tuning results.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a significant contribution to distributed optimization.  
- Counterintuitive finding: Jacobian-type PDMM outperforms Gauss-Seidel variants like GSADMM, but the paper lacks a full explanation for this.  
- Limited discussion on practical trade-offs and guidelines for choosing the number of blocks K.  
- Some technical details could be made more accessible to a broader audience.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel dual backward step, O(1/T) convergence, unification of methods, strong empirical results, and overall clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete explanation of counterintuitive performance, limited guidance on K selection, and accessibility of some technical parts.
