- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that selects K of J blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks in parallel, then performs a backward dual update.  
- Backward step produces a more conservative dual update, which is key to ensuring global convergence.  
- Optional proximal term in the primal update simplifies certain subproblems.  
- Provides solid theoretical analysis: global convergence and iteration complexity established.  
- Convergence rate proven as O(J/(T K)) after T iterations when selecting K of J blocks.  
- Main advantages: fully parallelizable primal updates and allowance of relatively large step sizes.  
- Empirically can outperform sADMM (due to larger admissible step sizes), is more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PADMM reaches target accuracy with less computation time.  
- Paper does not report results from an actual parallel implementation, leaving practical parallel speedups unverified.  
- PADMM requires tuning three parameters versus standard ADMM, which may be less convenient in practice.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-executed contribution matching classical ADMM rates while enabling parallelism; recommending reporting true parallel speedups and parameter-tuning guidance.
