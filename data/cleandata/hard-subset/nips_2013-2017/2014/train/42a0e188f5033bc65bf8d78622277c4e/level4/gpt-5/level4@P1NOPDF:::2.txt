This paper proposes a Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. In contrast to ADMM, which updates primal variables in a Gauss–Seidel fashion and is effectively limited to two blocks, this work updates multiple primal blocks in a Jacobi manner. The main contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step that compensates for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobi updates by effectively reducing the dual step size. As with ADMM, the paper establishes O(1/T)-type convergence guarantees for PDMM.

The supplementary material shows that two previously proposed methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM. This unifies and clarifies the relationships among several augmented Lagrangian–based decomposition methods. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM compared with ADMM and its variants.

Some of the empirical findings are somewhat counterintuitive, since Jacobi-type methods are generally expected to converge more slowly than Gauss–Seidel methods. It would be helpful if the authors elaborated on why PDMM achieves competitive or superior performance in these settings. In particular, a discussion of when the dual backward step offsets the drawbacks of parallel Jacobi updates—e.g., in terms of problem structure, conditioning, or communication/synchronization patterns—would strengthen the paper.

Overall, the dual backward step appears to be novel and seems crucial for obtaining convergence under general conditions similar to those required by ADMM. The proposed method broadens the toolbox for distributed optimization by enabling parallel block updates with rigorous convergence guarantees.
