- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for convex problems with multi-block linear constraints.  
- Uses Jacobian-style (parallel) primal updates instead of Gauss-Seidel, addressing multi-block ADMM challenges.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step to compensate for limited information propagation in parallel updates.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM subsumes existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM), unifying them under one framework.  
- Handles overlapping blocks, addressing an open problem in randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Theoretical framework accounts for constraint matrix sparsity and number of randomly selected blocks.  
- Rigorous analysis with well-motivated assumptions and clear proofs for convergence and complexity.  
- Experiments (RPCA, overlapping group lasso) show clear performance gains over ADMM variants and RBCD methods.  
- PDMM with K=1 outperforms RBSUMM; PDMM with K=3 converges faster than GSADMM despite parallel updates.  
- Provides insight that conservative dual updates via the backward step can make Jacobian methods faster.  
- Well-organized, clearly written, with useful supplementary materials for implementation.  
- Original contribution differentiating from prior multi-block ADMM work by the dual backward step and broader theoretical coverage.  
- Significant practical impact for distributed optimization and structured sparsity problems.  
- Minor weaknesses: experimental section lacks deeper analysis explaining K=1 vs RBSUMM improvements.  
- Minor weaknesses: limited discussion of communication vs. number-of-blocks (K) trade-offs in distributed settings.
