- Problem & contribution: proposes PDMM to minimize a sum of block-separable convex functions with linear equality constraints, extending ADMM to the multi-block setting.  
- PDMM features: randomly selects a subset of primal blocks for parallel updates, adds a stabilizing dual "backward step", and uses adaptive step sizes based on constraint sparsity and block count.  
- Theoretical results: provides global convergence proof and an O(1/T) iteration complexity rate in expectation with constant step size.  
- Unifying framework: shows PDMM recovers existing parallel ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) as special cases.  
- Practical relevance: designed for parallel implementation and applied successfully to overlapping group lasso and RPCA.  
- Strength — originality: novel and well-motivated solution addressing divergence in direct multi-block ADMM updates.  
- Strength — theory: strong theoretical quality with rigorous proofs and rates achieved under standard assumptions.  
- Strength — unification: contributes a deeper, unified understanding of parallel ADMM methods.  
- Strength — usefulness: practically relevant for large-scale problems and hard coordinate-block tasks like overlapping group lasso.  
- Weakness — clarity: dual update equations (6) and (7) are ambiguously indexed by a single i; explicitly state updates apply for all i = 1,...,I.  
- Weakness — theory discussion: expand on Theorem 2 to show how it recovers known rates for special cases and provide guidance on optimal block partitioning for practical convergence.  
- Weakness — broader applicability: briefly discuss applicability to other multi-block problems (e.g., tensor completion) to underscore generality.  
- Minor corrections: use standard argmin formatting in eq.(9); remove extra space before "Section" on line 217.  
- Recommendation: positive — recommend acceptance, with suggested clarifications to further strengthen the paper.
