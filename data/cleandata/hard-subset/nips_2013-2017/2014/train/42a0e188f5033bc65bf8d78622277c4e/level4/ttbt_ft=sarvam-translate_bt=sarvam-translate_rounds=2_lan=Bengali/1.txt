This paper addresses the problem of reducing block- decoupled functions under linear constraints. It proposes a variant of ADMM (known as PDMM) for use in cases with multiple blocks: it randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for updating and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable; the process of updating the primal variable can be applied in parallel. The proposed method has proven linear convergent rate. This method generalizes several variants of ADMM.

This paper is well-written and easy to follow. I am positive about accepting this paper. My questions and comments are given below:

1) The description of the algorithm in paragraphs (5) and (6) is not clear enough. Do you update all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} or only one block "i"? I think you update only one block "y" of the dual variable, but I could not find the rule for selecting "i". This should be clarified through correction.

2) In Theorem 2, I think you were missing "\sum_{i=1}^I" behind "{".

3) More discussion about the theorem should be included. Theorem 2 (the main result of this paper) provides the convergent rate of the proposed method PDMM. Firstly, PDMM generalizes several variants of ADMM. A natural question is whether Theorem 2 (parameters like K, J are chosen correctly) is consistent with the convergent rate of those variants. Secondly, since you have divided the transformation matrix A into multiple blocks, what is the best way to divide it from your theorem?

4) I am interested in comparing it with the Tensor সমাপ্তি problem ("Tensor সমাপ্তি for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), where there is a multiple block structure. Do you have any idea which variant of ADMM is the best?
