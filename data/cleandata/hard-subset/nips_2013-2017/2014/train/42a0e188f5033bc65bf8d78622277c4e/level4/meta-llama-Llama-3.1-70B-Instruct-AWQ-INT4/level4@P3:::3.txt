This paper proposes a randomized parallel version of ADMM, referred to as Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve linearly-constrained convex optimization problems with multiple blocks. The proposed algorithm updates the primal variables in parallel and uses a backward step on the dual update, making the dual update more conservative and enabling global convergence. PDMM also allows adding a proximal term to the primal update, making some optimization problems easier to solve.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, establishing its global convergence and iteration complexity. They show that the convergence rate of PDMM is O(1/T) with constant step size, which is the same as that of ADMM. The authors also demonstrate that PDMM can be used to solve overlapping group lasso problems in a randomized block coordinate fashion.

The main advantage of PDMM is its ability to allow full parallelization for the primal step and relatively large update step sizes compared to other methods. This makes PDMM more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM. The authors evaluate the algorithm on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, and the experimental results are promising. PDMM achieves the desired accuracy with less computation time.

However, the authors do not report the performance of PDMM with parallel implementation, which would be an important aspect to consider in practice. Additionally, PDMM has three parameters to tune, which may be less convenient than traditional ADMM.

Overall, this is an interesting contribution to solving ADMM-type problems with multiple constraints. The algorithm has the same convergence rate as ADMM but allows parallelization and exhibits promising practical performance. The paper is well-written, and both the theoretical and empirical parts are solid.
