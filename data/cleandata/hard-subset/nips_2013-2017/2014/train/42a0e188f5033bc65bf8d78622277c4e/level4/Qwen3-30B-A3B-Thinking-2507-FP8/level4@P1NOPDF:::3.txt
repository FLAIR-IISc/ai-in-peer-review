

This paper introduces a randomized parallel ADMM variant, PADMM, designed for problems with multiple blocks. At each iteration, PADMM randomly selects K blocks, updates their primal variables, and then performs a dual update via a backward step. The authors demonstrate that this backward step is crucial for global convergence, as it ensures a more conservative dual update. Additionally, PADMM supports a proximal term in the primal update, simplifying certain subproblems. Theoretical analysis establishes global convergence and an iteration complexity of O(J/(TK)) after T iterations when there are J total blocks and K are selected per iteration.

The primary advantages of PADMM are twofold: (1) it enables full parallelization during the primal update phase, and (2) it permits larger step sizes compared to related methods. Specifically, PADMM can outperform stochastic ADMM (sADMM) due to its larger allowable step sizes and offers greater flexibility than parallel Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM) while being more parallelizable than Gauss-Seidel ADMM (GSADMM). The authors evaluate PADMM on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, reporting promising results where the algorithm achieves target accuracy with reduced computational time.

However, a notable limitation is the absence of performance metrics for PADMM under parallel implementation, which is central to its proposed advantage. Additionally, PADMM requires more careful tuning of its three parameters compared to standard ADMM.

Overall, PADMM presents a valuable contribution to the ADMM literature for multi-constraint problems. It achieves the same convergence rate as standard ADMM but provides significant parallelization benefits and demonstrates strong empirical performance. The theoretical analysis is sound, and the experimental results are well-supported. The paper is clearly written, with both theoretical and empirical components presented robustly. This work offers a compelling approach for scalable optimization in distributed settings.
