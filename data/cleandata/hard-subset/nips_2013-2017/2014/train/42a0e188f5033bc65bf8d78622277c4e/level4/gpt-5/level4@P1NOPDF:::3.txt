This paper proposes a randomized parallel variant of ADMM (PADMM) that enables parallelization across multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM selects K blocks at random, updates the corresponding primal block variables in parallel, and then performs a backward step to update the dual variables. The authors convincingly show that the backward step makes the dual update more conservative, which is key to ensuring global convergence. The method also allows an optional proximal term in the primal update, simplifying certain subproblems.

The paper provides a solid theoretical analysis, establishing global convergence and iteration complexity. With J total blocks and K randomly selected per iteration, the convergence rate is O(J/(TK)) after T iterations.

The main advantages of PADMM are: (1) fully parallelizable primal updates, and (2) the ability to use relatively large step sizes compared with competing methods. In particular, PADMM can be faster than sADMM due to its larger admissible step sizes. It is also more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM. The authors evaluate PADMM on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, reporting promising results: PADMM reaches the target accuracy with less computation time.

However, the paper does not report performance with an actual parallel implementation, which is critical to demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed parallelism. In addition, compared with standard ADMM, PADMM requires tuning three parameters, which may be less convenient in practice.

Overall, this is an interesting and well-executed contribution to ADMM-type methods for multi-block/constraint problems. The algorithm matches the convergence rate of classical ADMM while enabling parallelization and showing strong empirical performance. Both the theoretical and experimental components are solid, though reporting true parallel speedups and discussing parameter tuning guidelines would strengthen the paper further.
