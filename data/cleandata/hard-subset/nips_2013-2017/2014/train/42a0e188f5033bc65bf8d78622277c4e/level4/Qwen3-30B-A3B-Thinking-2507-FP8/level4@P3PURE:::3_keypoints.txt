- Proposes randomized parallel ADMM (PADMM) that selects K random blocks per iteration, updates those primal variables, then does a backward dual update.  
- Backward dual step produces a more conservative dual update, which is crucial for establishing global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term in the primal update to simplify or stabilize subproblem solutions.  
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations (J = total blocks, K = blocks per iteration).  
- Main advantages: full parallelization of the primal step and ability to use relatively larger step sizes than competing methods.  
- Empirically can outperform sADMM due to larger allowable step sizes.  
- Offers more flexibility than PJADMM and higher parallelism than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PADMM reaches target accuracy with reduced computational time.  
- Paper does not report performance metrics for a true parallel implementation.  
- Tuning PADMM’s three parameters is less straightforward than tuning traditional ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with same convergence rate as standard ADMM, strong theory, clear writing, and compelling experimental validation.
