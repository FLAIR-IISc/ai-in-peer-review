This paper proposes a Parallel Direction Method of Multiplier (PDMM) for reducing block- separable convex functions under linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates the primal variable in the Gauss-Seidel method but is limited to two blocks, this work proposes updating the primal blocks in the Jacobian method with multiple blocks. The main result of this paper is the introduction of the dual backward step, which effectively reduces the step size for dual updates, compensating for the loss of limited information expansion in the Jacobian update. Similar to ADMM, this paper establishes an O(1/T) type convergence result for PDMM.

(In the supplementary material) it is shown that two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, special cases of the proposed method, help to better understand the relationship between different methods in the context of decomposition methods based on the augmented Lagrangian. The results of testing the effectiveness of the PDMM method compared to ADMM and other types demonstrate that the results are somewhat counterintuitive to me, as generally Jacobian-type methods will give slower convergence compared to Gauss-Seidel-type methods. It would be good to discuss this in detail. The dual backward step in PDMM seems new and important for achieving convergence in general situations like ADMM to ensure convergence. The proposed method gives more options and possibilities for distributed optimization.
