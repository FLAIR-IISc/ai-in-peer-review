This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors propose a new variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), named PDMM, specifically designed for the multi-block setting. The core idea of PDMM involves randomly selecting K blocks of the primal variable for parallel updates, followed by a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed algorithm and demonstrate that it serves as a generalization of several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written, and the proposed method is clearly presented. I find the contributions significant and lean towards accepting the paper, provided the authors can address the following points.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Algorithmic Clarity:** The description of the dual variable updates in equations (5) and (6) is unclear. It is not specified whether the updates to `y^{t+1}` and `ŷ^{t+1}` apply to the entire dual vector or only to a specific block `i`. If only a single block is updated, the mechanism for selecting this block `i` is not described. Please clarify this aspect of the algorithm in the revised manuscript.

2.  **Correction in Theorem 2:** There appears to be a typo in the statement of Theorem 2. Based on the proof, it seems a summation term, `\sum_{i=1}^I`, is missing after the opening brace `{`. Please verify and correct this.

3.  **Discussion of Theoretical Results:** The discussion surrounding Theorem 2, the paper's main theoretical result, could be substantially strengthened. This would significantly improve the impact of the paper. Specifically:
    a) **Consistency with Prior Work:** The paper claims that PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants. A crucial validation of this claim would be to show that the convergence rate in Theorem 2 recovers the known rates of these specialized methods when the parameters (e.g., K, J) are set appropriately. Such a discussion would better situate the contribution within the existing literature.
    b) **Practical Implications for Block Partitioning:** The convergence rate depends on the partitioning of the constraint matrix A. Does the theorem provide any guidance or insight into how to optimally partition A into blocks to achieve the fastest convergence? A discussion on this topic would significantly enhance the practical value of the theoretical results.

4.  **Broader Context and Applications:** It would be interesting to see a discussion on the potential application of PDMM to problems like tensor completion (e.g., Liu et al., "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data," 2012), which naturally exhibit a multi-block structure. Do the authors have any intuition as to whether PDMM, or one of the specialized variants it generalizes, would be particularly well-suited for such problems?

**Minor Points:**

1.  In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".
2.  On line 217, there is an extra space before "Section" that should be removed.

In summary, this is a strong paper with valuable contributions. I am supportive of its acceptance, but I strongly encourage the authors to expand the discussion surrounding their main theoretical result as suggested above to fully articulate its implications.
