- Introduces PDMM for minimizing block-separable convex functions under linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM’s Gauss-Seidel two-block updates, PDMM updates primal blocks in parallel (Jacobian), enabling multi-block problems.  
- Main technical contribution is a "dual backward step" that adaptively reduces the dual step size to offset reduced information flow from Jacobian updates.  
- Proves an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, matching theoretical guarantees of ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying framework for augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and its variants.  
- Results are somewhat counterintuitive because Jacobian methods usually converge slower than Gauss-Seidel; more intuition on this would strengthen the paper.  
- The dual backward step appears to be the critical mechanism enabling convergence under conditions as general as ADMM’s.  
- Overall, PDMM offers a valuable new alternative that expands options for designing distributed optimization algorithms.
