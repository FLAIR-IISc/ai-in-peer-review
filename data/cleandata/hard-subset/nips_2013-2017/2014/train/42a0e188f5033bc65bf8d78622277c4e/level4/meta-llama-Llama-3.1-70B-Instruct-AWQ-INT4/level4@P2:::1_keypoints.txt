- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method that generalizes ADMM and handles nonsmooth multi-block linear-constrained problems.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size, supported by rigorous proofs.  
- PDMM advantages: supports multiple blocks, enables parallel primal updates, and handles nonsmooth objective terms.  
- Experimental validation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PDMM outperforms ADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM in convergence and efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and explains connections to existing ADMM variants.  
- Theoretical results (convergence rate and iteration complexity) are well-presented and convincing.  
- Algorithm description in equations (5)–(6) is unclear: ambiguous whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} or only a single block i are updated.  
- Theorem 2 contains a missing “\sum_{i=1}^I”.  
- Formatting/typos: missing space after “min” in (9) and an extra space before “Section” in Line 217.  
- Minor request: clarify whether y updates apply to all coordinates or a single block.  
- Minor request: include more discussion of Theorem 2, consistency with existing ADMM variants, and optimal splitting of transformation matrix A.  
- Minor request: consider additional comparisons with other ADMM variants on the tensor completion problem.
