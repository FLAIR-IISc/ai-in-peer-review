- Proposes PADMM: randomized, parallel ADMM variant that selects K random blocks per iteration, updates primals in parallel, then performs a conservative backward dual step.  
- Backward (conservative) dual step is key to guaranteeing global convergence.  
- Algorithm supports adding proximal terms to simplify primal subproblems.  
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) for J blocks, K updated per iteration, after T iterations.  
- Advantages: fully parallelizable primal step and ability to use relatively large step sizes compared with alternatives.  
- Claimed faster than sADMM (due to larger step sizes), more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PADMM reaches target accuracy with less computation time.  
- Missing evaluation of a truly parallel implementation to substantiate claimed parallel speedups.  
- Practical drawback: requires tuning three parameters, which is less convenient than traditional ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and solid contribution, matches ADMM convergence rate while enabling parallelization; paper is well written with sound theory and experiments.
