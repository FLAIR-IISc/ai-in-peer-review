This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. The authors propose a novel variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), termed Proximal Dual Method of Multipliers (PDMM), designed to efficiently handle problems with multiple blocks. PDMM updates a randomly selected subset of *K* primal variable blocks in each iteration and employs a backward step for dual variable refinement, enabling parallel implementation of the primal update. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrate that PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand. I recommend acceptance. However, I have several questions and suggestions for improvement, detailed below:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of *y*<sup>*t*+1</sup> and *ŷ*<sup>*t*+1</sup> are updated, or only those corresponding to the selected block *i*. My understanding is that only a single block of the dual variable is updated per iteration, but the method for selecting block *i* is not explicitly stated. This selection process requires clarification in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, the summation symbol appears incomplete. I believe “∑<sub>*i*=1</sub><sup>*I*</sup>” is missing a closing brace “}” immediately following the opening brace.

3) The discussion surrounding Theorem 2, the paper’s primary result, should be expanded. Theorem 2 provides the convergence rate for PDMM. Given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, a natural question arises: are the convergence rates of these variants consistent with Theorem 2 when appropriate parameters (such as *K* and *J*) are chosen? Furthermore, considering the decomposition of the transformation matrix *A* into multiple blocks, what is the optimal block structure, guided by the theoretical framework of Theorem 2? 

4) I am interested in a comparative analysis of PDMM and other ADMM variants in the context of the tensor completion problem (as described in “Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012”), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. Do the authors have any insights regarding which ADMM variant performs best on this type of problem?

Minor Comments:

1) Equation (9) is missing a space after the “min” operator.
2) Line 217 contains an extraneous space before “Section”.

I reiterate my positive assessment and recommendation for acceptance. Expanding the discussion following Theorem 2, as suggested above, would significantly strengthen the paper.
