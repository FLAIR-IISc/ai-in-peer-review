- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobian) update scheme for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints, extending ADMM beyond two blocks.
- Main technical novelty is the dual backward step, which effectively reduces dual update step size to offset limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, comparable to ADMM.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, with derivations in the supplementary material linking augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.
- Experiments indicate PDMM is competitive with ADMM and variants, though this is counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods.
- Recommends authors elaborate on why PDMM achieves competitive empirical performance despite using Jacobian updates.
- Dual backward step is novel and crucial for convergence under general conditions, expanding options for distributed optimization.
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, thoroughly researched ADMM extension with clear potential impact.
