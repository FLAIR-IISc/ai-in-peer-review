- Proposes PDMM for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian (parallel) manner, unlike ADMM's two-block Gauss–Seidel updates.  
- Main theoretical innovation is the dual backward step, which compensates for limited information propagation by effectively reducing dual step size.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, comparable to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relations among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments indicate PDMM outperforms or is competitive with ADMM and other variants.  
- Results are somewhat counterintuitive because Jacobian methods usually converge slower; the paper should better explain why PDMM avoids this.  
- Dual backward step is novel and crucial for convergence under general conditions, giving PDMM robustness similar to ADMM.  
- PDMM provides a more flexible option for distributed optimization.
