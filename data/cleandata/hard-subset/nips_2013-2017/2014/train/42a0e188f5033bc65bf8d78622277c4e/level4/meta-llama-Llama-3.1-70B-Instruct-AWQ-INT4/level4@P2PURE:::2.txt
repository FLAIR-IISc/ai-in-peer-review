This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel manner but is limited to two blocks, this work updates primal blocks in a Jacobian manner, accommodating multiple blocks. The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of a dual backward step, which compensates for the limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the step size for dual updates. Similar to ADMM, the paper establishes O(1/T) type convergence results for PDMM.

As shown in the supplementary materials, the proposed method generalizes two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, providing a better understanding of the relationship between different methods in the landscape of decomposition methods based on augmented Lagrangian. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the PDMM method compared to ADMM and its variants. However, the results seem counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence compared to Gauss-Seidel-type methods. Elaboration on this point would be beneficial. The dual backward step in PDMM, crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions, appears to be a novel contribution, similar to ADMM. The proposed method offers more alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.
