- Proposes PDMM, a parallel-direction method of multipliers for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel (sequential) updates and two-block limitation, PDMM updates multiple primal blocks in a Jacobian (parallel) manner.  
- Main technical contribution is a dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T)-type convergence guarantees, similar to ADMM.  
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants, which is somewhat counterintuitive given expected slower convergence of Jacobian updates.  
- Reviewer requests more explanation on why PDMM performs well; notes the dual backward step appears new and crucial for achieving convergence under conditions comparable to ADMM.  
- Overall, the method broadens available tools and design choices for distributed optimization.
