- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal variables, then applies a novel backward dual update.  
- Backward dual step motivated to prevent aggressive dual updates and encourage more conservative convergence.  
- Incorporates a Bregman divergence term to allow proximal regularization and handle a broader class of problems.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity, with rate influenced by J and K (≈ J/(T K)).  
- Highlights strong parallelization potential and ability to use larger step sizes versus sADMM.  
- Shows greater flexibility than PJADMM and better parallelizability than GSADMM.  
- Demonstrates connections to existing methods: sADMM and PJADMM are special cases, clarifying parameter/step roles.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show competitive runtimes and accuracy.  
- Major weakness: no experiments demonstrating actual speedup on parallel hardware despite claiming parallel efficiency.  
- Practical concern: introduces three parameters (τi, νi, ηjt) that may need extra tuning and lacks concrete selection guidelines.  
- Experimental scope is limited; missing analyses such as performance under varying block overlap for overlapping group lasso.  
- Presentation issue: some notation (e.g., 'di') should be defined earlier or clarified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, rigorous theory, empirical validation, connections to prior work, and potential large-scale impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absent parallel implementation results, parameter tuning complexity, and limited experimental breadth.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written, and promising; recommend acceptance if authors address demonstration of actual parallel performance.
