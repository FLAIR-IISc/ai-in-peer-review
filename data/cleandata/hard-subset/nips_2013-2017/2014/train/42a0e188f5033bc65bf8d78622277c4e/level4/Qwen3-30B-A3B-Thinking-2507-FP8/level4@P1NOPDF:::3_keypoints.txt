- Introduces PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K of J blocks per iteration, updates their primals, then applies a backward dual update.  
- The backward dual step is crucial for global convergence because it yields a more conservative dual update.  
- PADMM supports a proximal term in the primal update, simplifying certain subproblems.  
- Theoretical results: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.  
- Main advantages: enables full parallelization of primal updates and permits larger step sizes than related methods.  
- Outperforms stochastic ADMM (sADMM) due to larger allowable step sizes; more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising speed to target accuracy.  
- Major limitation: no measured performance metrics for a parallel implementation, despite parallelization being a central claim.  
- Practical drawback: requires careful tuning of three parameters compared to standard ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical and empirical contribution to multi-block ADMM literature; clearly written and compelling for scalable distributed optimization.
