- Proposes PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal variables, then applies a backward dual step.  
- Backward dual step produces a more conservative dual update, which is crucial for establishing global convergence.  
- Adds a proximal term to the primal update to simplify solving certain subproblems.  
- Proves global convergence and an iteration complexity of O(J/(T K)) after T iterations (J total blocks, K selected per iteration).  
- Enables full parallelization of the primal step.  
- Supports larger step sizes than competing methods, yielding potential speedups over sADMM and more flexibility than PJADMM.  
- Offers superior parallelism compared to GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show faster time-to-target accuracy.  
- Does not report parallel implementation performance metrics (limitation).  
- Requires tuning three parameters, making it less convenient than traditional ADMM (limitation).  
- Paper is well-written with strong theoretical and experimental contributions and is a valuable addition for multi-constraint ADMM problems.
