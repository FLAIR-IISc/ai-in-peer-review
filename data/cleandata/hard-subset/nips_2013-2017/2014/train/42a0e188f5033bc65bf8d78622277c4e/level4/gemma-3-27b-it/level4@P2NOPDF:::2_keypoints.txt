- Introduces PDMM, a parallel direction method of multipliers for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Contrasts with ADMM’s usual Gauss–Seidel, two-block updates by proposing Jacobian updates that handle multiple blocks concurrently.  
- Main contribution: a “dual backward step” that adaptively reduces dual update step size to mitigate limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes convergence guarantees for PDMM comparable to ADMM, with an O(1/T) rate.  
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian methods.  
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM’s empirical performance gains over ADMM and related algorithms.  
- Reviewer surprised because Jacobian methods usually converge slower than Gauss–Seidel; requests deeper explanation of why PDMM overcomes this.  
- Recommends elaborating on how the dual backward step accelerates convergence despite the Jacobian update structure.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, flexible alternative for large-scale block-separable optimization with potential performance improvements.
