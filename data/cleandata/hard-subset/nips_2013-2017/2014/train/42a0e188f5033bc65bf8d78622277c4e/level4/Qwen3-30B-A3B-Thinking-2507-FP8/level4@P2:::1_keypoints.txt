- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block coordinate method with a dual backward step for convex block-separable problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses a notable gap in multi-block ADMM theory by providing a convergent generalization beyond two blocks.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM subsumes several existing ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM), illuminating the role of the dual backward step.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with comprehensive convergence analysis.  
- Practical algorithm that scales to large problems and handles overlapping blocks.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM in convergence speed and runtime.  
- Well-written exposition that makes complex concepts accessible.  
- Algorithm description (Eqs. 5–7) needs clarification on whether dual updates apply to all blocks or a single block and how blocks are selected.  
- Lacks guidance on how to optimally partition variables into blocks for different problem structures.  
- Comparison to tensor completion (a natural multi-block application) is limited and should be expanded.  
- Theorem 2 contains incomplete summation notation and the paper should verify mathematical expressions for consistency.  
- Suggests verifying notation, adding block-partitioning guidance, expanding tensor-completion comparison, and comparing PDMM’s specialized convergence rates to known ADMM variants.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with minor issues to fix; strong recommendation for acceptance.
