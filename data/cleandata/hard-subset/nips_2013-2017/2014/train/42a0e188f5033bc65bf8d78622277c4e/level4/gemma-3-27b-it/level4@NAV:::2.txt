This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) designed for minimizing block-separable convex functions under linear constraints. Differing from the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), which employs a Gauss-Seidel update for primal variables and is typically limited to two blocks, this work utilizes a Jacobian update scheme capable of handling multiple blocks concurrently. A key contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step, which addresses the potential for limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by adaptively controlling the dual update step size. The authors establish convergence results for PDMM, achieving an O(1/T) rate, analogous to that of ADMM.

The supplementary materials demonstrate that existing methods, specifically sADMM and PJADMM, are encompassed as special cases within the PDMM framework, providing valuable insights into the connections between various augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition techniques. Experimental results confirm the efficacy of PDMM, demonstrating performance improvements over ADMM and its variants. While the observed results are promising, the faster convergence of the Jacobian-based PDMM compared to traditional Gauss-Seidel methods like ADMM is somewhat unexpected, and further discussion of this point would be beneficial. The novel dual backward step appears crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions, mirroring the role of similar mechanisms in ADMM. Ultimately, this method broadens the toolkit for distributed optimization, offering increased flexibility and potential for application.
