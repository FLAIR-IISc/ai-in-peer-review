- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobian) variant of ADMM for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM’s Gauss-Seidel updates limited to two blocks, PDMM updates primal blocks in parallel (Jacobian) to handle multiple blocks.  
- Core contribution is a novel dual backward step that mitigates limited information propagation of Jacobian updates by effectively reducing dual update step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, analogous to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants, a somewhat counterintuitive result given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods.  
- Reviewer requests the authors to elaborate on why PDMM’s Jacobian approach yields competitive or better empirical performance than Gauss-Seidel.  
- PDMM offers greater flexibility and potential for distributed optimization; the dual backward step appears novel and crucial for general-condition convergence.
