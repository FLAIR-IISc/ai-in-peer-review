- Paper addresses minimizing block-separable functions under linear constraints and proposes PDMM, a multi-block ADMM variant.  
- PDMM randomly selects K primal blocks per iteration, uses a backward step to refine the dual, allows parallel primal updates, and attains sublinear convergence while generalizing several ADMM variants.  
- Paper is well-written and the reviewer is inclined to recommend acceptance.  
- Clarify algorithm equations (5)–(6): specify whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and ĥy^{t+1} are updated or only a single block i, and state how index i is selected.  
- Theorem 2 appears to be missing a summation term: add “∑_{i=1}^I” after the opening brace “{” per the proof.  
- Provide additional discussion after Theorem 2: show that Theorem 2 recovers known convergence rates for special choices of K and J, and comment on how to optimally split the transformation matrix A into blocks.  
- Discuss comparison with tensor completion (2012) and give insight on which ADMM variant might be optimal for such multi-block problems.  
- Minor edits: add a space after “min.” in equation (9), and remove the space before “Section” on line 217.  
- Reviewer expects more discussion following Theorem 2 in the revised version despite recommending acceptance.
