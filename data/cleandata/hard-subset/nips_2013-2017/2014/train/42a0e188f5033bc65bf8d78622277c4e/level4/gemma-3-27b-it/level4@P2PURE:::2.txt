This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates primal variables via a Gauss-Seidel approach limited to two blocks, this work proposes updating primal blocks using a Jacobian method, extending the approach to multiple blocks. The core contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step, designed to compensate for limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by effectively controlling the step size for dual variable updates. The paper establishes convergence results of the order O(1/T) for PDMM, mirroring those achieved by ADMM. 

The authors demonstrate (in the supplementary materials) that existing methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM, providing valuable insight into the relationships between various augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Experimental results confirm the effectiveness of PDMM when compared to ADMM and its variants. These results are somewhat surprising, as Jacobian-based methods typically exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel methods; further elaboration on this point would be beneficial. The dual backward step in PDMM appears novel and crucial for achieving convergence under general conditions comparable to those of ADMM. Overall, the proposed method expands the toolkit for distributed optimization, offering increased flexibility and possibilities.
