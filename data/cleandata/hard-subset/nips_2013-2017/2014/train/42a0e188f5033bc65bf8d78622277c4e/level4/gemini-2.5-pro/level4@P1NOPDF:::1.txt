This paper considers the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. To handle the multi-block case, the authors propose a variant of ADMM, which they call PDMM (Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers). The algorithm randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update in parallel and then uses a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and show that it generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

This paper is well-written and easy to follow. My overall assessment is positive, and I recommend that this paper be accepted. My questions and comments for the authors are listed below.

**Major Comments:**

1.  The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. It is ambiguous whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated in each step, or only those corresponding to a single block "i". My understanding is that only a single block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but the method for selecting the index "i" is not specified. This should be clarified in the revision.

2.  In the proof of Theorem 2, I believe the term `\sum_{i=1}^I` is missing after the opening brace `{`. Please double-check this.

3.  The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of Theorem 2. As the main result, this theorem provides the convergence rate for PDMM.
    *   First, since PDMM generalizes several variants of ADMM, a natural question arises: by choosing the parameters K and J appropriately, does the rate in Theorem 2 recover the known convergence rates for these specialized variants? A discussion on this point would help contextualize the result.
    *   Second, the method relies on splitting the matrix A into multiple blocks. Does the theorem provide any insight into the optimal way to perform this partitioning to achieve the best convergence?

4.  I am curious about a potential comparison to methods for the tensor completion problem (e.g., "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data," 2012), which also features a multi-block structure. Can the authors comment on how their method might perform on this problem, or which existing ADMM variant is considered most effective for such applications?

**Minor Points:**

1.  In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".
2.  On line 217, please remove the extra space before "Section".

In summary, this is a strong contribution that I am happy to recommend for acceptance. I would strongly encourage the authors to expand the discussion following Theorem 2 in their revision, as this would significantly strengthen the paper.
