- Introduces PADMM: a randomized, parallel ADMM variant that selects K of J blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks, then updates duals via a backward step.
- Backward dual step makes updates more conservative, enabling global convergence.
- Supports adding a proximal term to primal updates to simplify subproblems.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.
- Enables fully parallel primal updates.
- Allows relatively larger step sizes than competing methods, potentially faster than sADMM.
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso: reaches target accuracy with less computation time.
- Does not report performance from an actual parallel implementation.
- Tuning three PADMM parameters is less convenient than tuning standard ADMM.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written contribution matching ADMM convergence rates while enabling parallelization.
