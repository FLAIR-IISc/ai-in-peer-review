- Paper studies minimizing block-separable functions with linear constraints and proposes PDMM, a randomized K-block parallel ADMM variant with a dual backward step and sublinear convergence guarantees.  
- Well-written; reviewer supports acceptance contingent on addressing listed issues.  
- Major issue: Dual update (eqs. (5)-(6)) is ambiguous—unclear whether all coordinates or only a single block i of y^{t+1}, \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated and how block i is chosen.  
- Major issue: Theorem 2 appears to be missing a summation \sum_{i=1}^I before the curly-braced term; reviewer asks authors to verify against the proof.  
- Major issue: Need a deeper discussion of Theorem 2’s implications, especially whether it recovers known convergence rates of existing ADMM variants when parameters (K, J) are specialized.  
- Major issue: Need guidance on block partitioning—does Theorem 2 indicate how partitioning A (or properties of submatrices) affects convergence or suggest optimal splitting strategies?  
- Major issue: Reviewer requests insight on PDMM’s and other ADMM variants’ suitability for tensor completion problems with similar multi-block structure.  
- Minor: space missing after "min" in equation (9).  
- Minor: remove extra space before "Section" on line 217.  
- Overall: strong contribution; acceptance recommended if authors expand discussion around Theorem 2 and fix clarifications/typos.
