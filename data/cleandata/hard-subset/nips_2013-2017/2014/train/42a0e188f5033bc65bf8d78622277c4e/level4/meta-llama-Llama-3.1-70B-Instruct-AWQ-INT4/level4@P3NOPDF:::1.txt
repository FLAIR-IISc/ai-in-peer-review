This paper proposes a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to minimize block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. PDMM addresses the multiple block case by randomly selecting K blocks of the primal variable to update and utilizing a backward step to refine the dual variable. This process enables parallel implementation of the primal variable update. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method, demonstrating its generality by encompassing several ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept it, pending revisions that address the following questions and comments.

Firstly, the algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) requires clarification. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated or only a single block "i". My understanding is that only a block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but the method for selecting "i" is not specified. This ambiguity should be resolved in the revision.

Secondly, in Theorem 2, it appears that "\sum_{i=1}^I" is missing from the proof.

Furthermore, I believe that Theorem 2 warrants additional discussion. As the main result of the paper, it provides the convergence rate of PDMM. Two natural questions arise: Firstly, can Theorem 2 be made consistent with the convergence rates of existing ADMM variants by appropriately choosing parameters like K and J? Secondly, given that the transformation matrix A is split into multiple blocks, what is the optimal way to split it according to the theorem? Addressing these questions would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, I am curious about the comparison with the tensor completion problem ("tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. Do the authors have any insights into which ADMM variant is optimal for this problem?

Minor corrections include adding a space behind "min" in equation (9) and removing the space before "Section" in line 217.

Overall, I am positive about accepting this paper, but I expect the authors to provide more discussion after Theorem 2 to address the aforementioned questions and clarify the algorithm description.
