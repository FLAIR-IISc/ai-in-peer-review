

This paper proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The method updates K randomly selected primal blocks in parallel and refines the dual variable via a backward step, achieving a sublinear convergence rate. The work generalizes several existing ADMM variants, and the technical contribution is well-motivated. The paper is clearly written and technically sound, making a solid contribution to the literature. I recommend acceptance pending revisions addressing the following points.

**Major Concerns**  
1. **Algorithm Clarity (Equations 5–6)**: The description of the primal and dual updates lacks precision. Specifically, it is unclear whether Equations (5) and (6) update *all* coordinates of \(y^{t+1}\) and \(\hat{y}^{t+1}\) or *only a single block* (indexed by \(i\)). The text states the method "randomly selects K blocks," but the algorithmic steps do not specify how the block index \(i\) is chosen or whether the dual update applies to all blocks or just the selected one. This ambiguity must be resolved—e.g., by clarifying that only the dual coordinate corresponding to the selected primal block is updated, or by explicitly defining the block selection mechanism.  

2. **Theorem 2 Proof (Line 217)**: The proof of Theorem 2 appears to omit a summation index. Specifically, the expression "\(\sum_{i=1}^I\)" is missing from the term following the opening brace in the proof (likely a typesetting error). This must be corrected to ensure mathematical rigor.  

3. **Theoretical Interpretation (Theorem 2)**: The main result (Theorem 2) requires deeper discussion to contextualize its significance. Two key questions must be addressed:  
   - *Consistency with existing ADMM variants*: The paper claims PDMM generalizes prior methods. The authors should explicitly verify that Theorem 2 recovers known convergence rates (e.g., for standard ADMM or alternating minimization) by appropriately setting parameters (e.g., \(K=1\), \(J=1\)).  
   - *Optimal block partitioning*: Since the convergence rate depends on the block structure of \(A\), the authors should discuss how to optimally partition \(A\) into blocks (e.g., based on sparsity, condition number, or problem structure) to minimize the convergence rate constant. This would greatly enhance the practical utility of the method.  

**Minor Points**  
- Equation (9): Add a space after "min" (e.g., "min \(\{ \cdots \}\)").  
- Line 217: Remove the space before "Section" (e.g., "Section 3" instead of "Section 3").  

**Additional Note**  
The paper mentions tensor completion as an application with a similar block structure (citing "tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"). While not critical for acceptance, the authors should briefly discuss whether PDMM (or specific ADMM variants) would be advantageous for such problems, particularly in comparison to existing approaches. This would strengthen the practical motivation.  

**Conclusion**  
This is a strong paper with clear technical contributions and a well-structured presentation. The proposed PDMM method is a meaningful extension of ADMM for multiple blocks, and the convergence analysis is rigorous. Addressing the points above—particularly the algorithmic clarity, proof correction, and deeper theoretical discussion—will significantly improve the paper. I am confident these revisions are straightforward and will elevate the work to a high standard for NIPS. I recommend acceptance.
