- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant that updates K randomly selected blocks in parallel per iteration with a primal update and a dual backward step.  
- Dual backward step enables conservative dual updates, ensuring global convergence.  
- Supports proximal terms in primal updates to simplify certain subproblems.  
- Theoretical results prove global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Convergence rate scales as O(J/(T K)), depending on total blocks J and blocks updated K.  
- Strengths: full parallelization of primal step, larger allowable step sizes, and greater flexibility than PJADMM and better parallel potential than GSADMM.  
- Empirical validation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows significant computational gains and faster attainment of target accuracy, especially for K > 1.  
- Establishes connections to sADMM and PJADMM and provides theoretical justification for the dual backward step.  
- Addresses overlapping block optimization, a challenging case for randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Weaknesses: no reported performance metrics for parallel implementation.  
- Practical concern: requires tuning three parameters (τi, νi, ηjt), which may be more burdensome than traditional ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution that extends ADMM to multiple-block parallelism with solid theory and promising empirical performance.
