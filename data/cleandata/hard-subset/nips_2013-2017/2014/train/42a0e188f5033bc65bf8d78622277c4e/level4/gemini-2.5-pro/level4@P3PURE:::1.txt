This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. The authors propose a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), named PDMM, to handle the multi-block case. The method operates by randomly selecting K blocks of the primal variable for parallel updates, followed by a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and show that it generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

This is a well-written and clear paper. I am supportive of this paper's acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the points below. My questions and comments are as follows:

1.  The description of the dual variable update in equations (5) and (6) is ambiguous. It is unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated, or only those corresponding to a single block "i". My interpretation is that only a single block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but the mechanism for selecting this block "i" is not specified. The authors should clarify this in the revision.

2.  In the statement of Theorem 2, I believe a summation term, `\sum_{i=1}^I`, is missing before the curly braces `{...}`. Please verify this based on the provided proof.

3.  The paper would be significantly strengthened by a more thorough discussion of the implications of Theorem 2, which is the main theoretical result. Specifically:
    a) **Consistency with Prior Work:** PDMM is presented as a generalization of several ADMM variants. A natural and important question is whether Theorem 2 recovers the known convergence rates of these specific variants when the parameters (e.g., K, J) are set appropriately. This would validate the generality of the proposed analysis.
    b) **Guidance on Block Partitioning:** The method requires partitioning the constraint matrix A into blocks. Does the convergence rate provided in Theorem 2 offer any insights into the optimal way to perform this partitioning? For instance, does the rate depend on properties of the sub-matrices, suggesting a preferred splitting strategy?

4.  I am also curious about the method's performance on the tensor completion problem, as explored in "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data" (2012), which exhibits a similar multi-block structure. Do the authors have any insights on which ADMM variant, including their own, might be best suited for this application?

**Minor Points:**
1.  In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".
2.  On line 217, the space before "Section" should be removed.

In summary, this is a strong paper with a valuable contribution. I recommend its acceptance, but I strongly encourage the authors to incorporate a more detailed discussion following Theorem 2, as this would significantly enhance the paper's impact.
