This paper introduces the parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM), a novel approach for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel manner but is limited to two blocks, PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian manner, accommodating multiple blocks. The core contribution of this work lies in the introduction of a dual backward step, which mitigates the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the step size for dual updates. Consequently, the paper establishes O(1/T) type convergence results for PDMM, mirroring those of ADMM.

The authors demonstrate that two existing methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed PDMM, providing a deeper understanding of the relationships between various decomposition methods based on the augmented Lagrangian. Experimental results showcase the effectiveness of PDMM compared to ADMM and its variants. Notably, these results seem counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel-type methods. Further elaboration on this point would be beneficial. The dual backward step in PDMM, which ensures convergence under general conditions akin to ADMM, appears to be a novel and crucial component. The proposed method offers additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization, expanding the toolbox for tackling complex problems.

Overall, the paper presents a well-structured and coherent argument, supported by rigorous analysis and empirical evidence. The introduction of PDMM and its convergence guarantees contribute to the existing body of work on decomposition methods, providing a valuable addition to the field.
