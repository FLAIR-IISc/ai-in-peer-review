- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that updates primal blocks in parallel, uses a conservative backward dual step, and supports proximal terms.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis proving global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.
- PDMM enables full parallelization of the primal step and permits relatively large update step sizes versus alternatives.
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Demonstrates applicability to overlapping group lasso via randomized block coordinate updates.
- Experimental evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising results and faster attainment of desired accuracy.
- Does not report actual runtime performance with a parallel implementation, a key practical omission.
- Requires tuning of three parameters, potentially less convenient than traditional ADMM.
- Novel contributions include extending ADMM to multiple blocks in a randomized parallel fashion and introducing the backward dual step.
- Significant potential for efficiently solving large-scale linearly-constrained convex problems and handling overlapping group lasso.
- Theoretical and empirical parts are high quality: analysis is thorough, experiments are well-evaluated, and the paper is well-written.
- Paper is clear and well-organized with well-defined notation and helpful figures/tables.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, parallelization capability, matching ADMM convergence rate, and promising experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of reported parallel implementation results and extra parameter tuning burden.
