- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobian) update method for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints, enabling multi-block updates beyond ADMM’s two-block Gauss–Seidel scheme.  
- Main technical contribution is a dual backward step that compensates limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, analogous to ADMM’s guarantees.  
- Shows in supplementary material that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results indicate PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants, though this is counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods versus Gauss–Seidel.  
- The dual backward step is novel and crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions, widening options for distributed optimization.  
- Overall, PDMM provides useful new alternatives for distributed optimization and constitutes a valuable contribution.
