This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions under linear constraints, and proposes a novel ADMM variant, termed PDMM, specifically designed for multi-block scenarios. PDMM achieves this through random selection of *K* primal variable blocks for updating, coupled with a backward step for dual variable refinement, enabling parallel implementation of the primal update. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrate its generalization of existing ADMM variants. The paper is clearly written and readily understandable, and I recommend acceptance. 

However, I have the following questions and suggestions for improvement:

1) The algorithm description presented in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of *y<sup>t+1</sup>* and *ŷ<sup>t+1</sup>* are updated, or only those belonging to the selected block *i*. My understanding is that only a block of the dual variable *y* is updated, but the selection process for *i* is not explicitly stated. Clarification on this point is needed during revision.

2) In Theorem 2, the summation symbol appears to be incomplete in the proof; I believe a “\sum<sub>i=1</sub><sup>I</sup>” term is missing.

3) The discussion following Theorem 2 would benefit from further elaboration. Given that Theorem 2 provides the core convergence result for PDMM, and that PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants, it would be valuable to discuss whether the convergence rate established in Theorem 2 (with appropriate parameter choices for *K* and *J*) aligns with the known convergence rates of those generalized variants. Additionally, considering that the transformation matrix *A* is decomposed into multiple blocks, insights into the optimal block decomposition strategy based on the theorem would be appreciated.

4) I am interested in a comparison, or at least discussion of potential performance, when applying PDMM to the tensor completion problem (as described in “tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012”), which also exhibits a multi-block structure. Do you have any preliminary insights regarding which ADMM variant might be most effective in this context?

Minor comments:

1) Equation (9) is missing a space after the “min” operator.
2) On line 217, please remove the space preceding “Section”.

I reiterate my positive assessment of this paper and encourage its acceptance, contingent on addressing the points raised, particularly a more in-depth discussion following Theorem 2.
