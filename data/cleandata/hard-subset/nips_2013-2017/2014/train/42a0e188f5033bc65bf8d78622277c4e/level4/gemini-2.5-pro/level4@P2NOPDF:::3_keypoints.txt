- Introduces PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM for multi-block optimization that samples K blocks per iteration and updates primal variables in parallel.  
- Uses a crucial backward step for dual updates, producing a more conservative dual update that ensures global convergence.  
- Supports adding a proximal term in the primal update to simplify subproblems for certain tasks.  
- Provides formal global convergence proof and derives iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J total blocks.  
- Main advantages: enables full parallelization of primal updates and allows a larger stepsize than related methods.  
- Claimed improvements over prior work: faster than sADMM (larger stepsize), more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PADMM reaches target accuracy with less computational time than competitors.  
- Major weakness: no experiments on a true parallel implementation—results are from serial simulations of parallelism.  
- Additional practical concern: introduces more tuning parameters than two-block ADMM, potentially complicating parameter selection.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with solid theory and empirical validation, offering a valuable, practically promising contribution to large-scale ADMM methods.
