- Problem: minimizing a sum of block-separable convex functions under linear equality constraints, a core formulation in ML.  
- Method: proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate (Jacobian) ADMM-style algorithm updating a random subset of primal blocks.  
- Key technical idea: introduces a "dual backward step" to moderate dual updates and stabilize parallel primal updates.  
- Step-size design: forward and backward dual step sizes adapt to constraint matrix block-sparsity and number of concurrently updated blocks.  
- Theory: proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size, matching standard ADMM rates.  
- Unification: shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, offering a unified framework for parallel ADMM variants.  
- Experiments: demonstrates effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, outperforming several baselines.  
- Originality/significance: novel stabilization of multi-block parallel ADMM via adaptive dual backward step addressing known divergence issues.  
- Quality: technically rigorous analysis and convincing empirical comparisons against GSADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM.  
- Clarity: situates PDMM within literature and clarifies trade-offs between primal exactness and dual aggressiveness.  
- Practicality: handles overlapping blocks and enables massive parallelization suitable for modern computing environments.  
- Weakness: needs more intuition on why PDMM (Jacobian) outperforms Gauss–Seidel variants despite GS often having faster per-iteration progress.  
- Experimental nuance: public sADMM used zero dual step size causing slow convergence; a properly configured sADMM would match PDMM3—this should be emphasized.
