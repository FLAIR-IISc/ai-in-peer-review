- Introduces PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses gap in multi-block ADMM generalizations by enabling parallel (Jacobian) primal updates.  
- Proposes a novel dual backward step that ensures convergence for parallel updates.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of the PDMM framework, connecting existing methods.  
- Demonstrates PDMM can handle overlapping blocks, a scenario where standard methods fail.  
- Key contribution: dual backward step compensates for limited information propagation in parallel updates.  
- Practical consideration: step sizes account for constraint matrix sparsity and number of randomly updated blocks.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and requires relatively mild assumptions.  
- Experiments (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) show practical advantages over ADMM, sADMM, GSADMM, RBSUMM.  
- Experimental setup is thorough and reproducible with detailed parameter settings and metrics.  
- Originality is strong; dual backward step and overlap extension are notable advances.  
- Counterintuitive finding: PDMM often outperforms sequential GSADMM in runtime, but authors do not sufficiently explain why.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, with useful supplementary materials.  
- Overall significance: a practical, theoretically grounded approach for distributed optimization and structured sparsity; main weakness is lack of explanation for PDMM’s empirical superiority over sequential methods.
