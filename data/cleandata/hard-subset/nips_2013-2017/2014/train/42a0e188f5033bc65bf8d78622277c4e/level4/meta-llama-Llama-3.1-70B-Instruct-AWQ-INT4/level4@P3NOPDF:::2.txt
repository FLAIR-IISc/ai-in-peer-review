This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) to minimize block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel manner but is limited to two blocks, this work introduces a Jacobian update scheme for multiple blocks. The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the dual backward step, which effectively reduces the step size for dual updates, thereby compensating for the limited information propagation in the Jacobian updates.

The authors establish O(1/T) type convergence results for PDMM, similar to those of ADMM. Furthermore, they demonstrate that two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed method. This relationship is presented in the supplementary materials and provides a deeper understanding of the connections between different decomposition methods based on augmented Lagrangian.

The experimental results show that PDMM is effective compared to ADMM and its variants. However, the results seem counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence compared to Gauss-Seidel-type methods. It would be beneficial for the authors to elaborate on this point, providing insight into why PDMM achieves competitive performance despite its Jacobian update scheme.

The dual backward step in PDMM appears to be a novel and crucial component in ensuring convergence under general conditions, similar to ADMM. This innovation offers more alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization, making the proposed method a valuable contribution to the field. Overall, the paper presents a well-motivated and thoroughly researched extension of ADMM, with a clear potential for impact in distributed optimization applications.
