- Proposal: PADMM — a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K of J blocks per iteration, updates those primal blocks in parallel, then updates the dual via a backward step.
- Backward dual update is crucial: it makes the dual update more conservative and ensures global convergence.
- Framework allows adding a proximal term in the primal update to make subproblems easier to solve.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) for T iterations.
- Advantages: enables full parallelization of primal updates and permits a larger step size than competing methods.
- Compared to alternatives: can be faster than sADMM (larger step size), more flexible than PJADMM, and more amenable to parallelization than GSADMM.
- Experiments: promising results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, achieving target accuracy in less computation time than baselines.
- Limitation: no true parallel implementation reported, so practical speedup on parallel hardware is unverified.
- Practical drawback: requires tuning three algorithm parameters, which may be less convenient than traditional ADMM.
- Overall: well-written, solid theoretical and experimental contribution that matches ADMM convergence rate while offering parallelization benefits.
