- Introduces PDMM, a novel method for minimizing block-separable convex functions under linear constraints.  
- PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian (parallel) manner, enabling multi-block updates unlike two-block ADMM.  
- Core contribution is a dual backward step that reduces dual step size to mitigate limited information propagation from Jacobian updates.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, comparable to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms ADMM variants, which is counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods and warrants further explanation.  
- The dual backward step appears novel and crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions similar to ADMM.  
- Expands options for distributed optimization, adding useful alternatives to the existing toolbox.  
- Paper is well-structured with rigorous analysis and supporting empirical evidence, making a valuable contribution.
