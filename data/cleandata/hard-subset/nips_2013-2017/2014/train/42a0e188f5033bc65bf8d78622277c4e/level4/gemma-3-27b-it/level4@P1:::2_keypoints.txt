- Proposes PDMM: a parallel Jacobian-style update method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step to regulate dual updates and improve information propagation.  
- Extends ADMM to multiple blocks, overcoming the typical two-block limitation.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates similar to ADMM.  
- Unifies existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) as special cases within the PDMM framework.  
- Experimental results show PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Results are counterintuitive since Jacobian methods usually converge slower than Gauss-Seidel; this needs more explanation.  
- Dual backward step appears crucial and novel for achieving convergence under general conditions.  
- Motivation and background are clear and situate the work within ADMM literature.  
- Technical presentation is generally sound with mostly consistent notation.  
- Theoretical analysis seems rigorous but relies on supplementary material for full details.  
- Paper is dense and could use more intuitive explanations of key concepts, especially the dual backward step.  
- Limited practical guidance on parameter tuning (τi, νi) for different problems.  
- Strengths: novelty, generality, theoretical guarantees, and empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses: counterintuitive experimental findings, presentation complexity, and limited parameter-tuning guidance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify experiments, provide intuition, and offer parameter selection guidance.
