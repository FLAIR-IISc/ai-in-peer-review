- Paper proposes PDMM, an ADMM variant for block-separable functions with linear constraints that randomly updates K primal blocks in parallel and uses a backward step on the dual, with a proved sublinear convergence rate and claims to generalize several ADMM variants.
- Overall well-written and easy to follow; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
- Clarify dual updates in eqs. (5) and (6): unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} or only a single block i are updated, and how block i is selected.
- Theorem 2 appears to have a typo: a missing summation term \sum_{i=1}^I after the opening brace, which is present in the proof.
- Expand discussion of Theorem 2 implications, particularly whether its convergence rate recovers known rates for specific ADMM variants when parameters (e.g., K, J) are set accordingly.
- Discuss whether Theorem 2 gives guidance on how to partition matrix A to achieve faster convergence.
- Provide insight on applicability to multi-block problems like tensor completion and which ADMM variant within the PDMM framework would be best suited.
- Minor typos: missing space after "min" in eq. (9), and an extra space before "Section" on line 217.
- Supportive of acceptance conditional on addressing the above points, especially expanding the discussion after Theorem 2.
