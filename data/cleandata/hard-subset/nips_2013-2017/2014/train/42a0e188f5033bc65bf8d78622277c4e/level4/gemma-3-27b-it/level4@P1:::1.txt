This paper considers the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. A variant of ADMM, called PDMM, is proposed to address the multiple-block case: it randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable; the process of updating the primal variable can be implemented in parallel. A sublinear convergence rate is proven for the proposed method, and it is shown to generalize several variants of ADMM.

This paper is well-written and easy to follow. I recommend acceptance. My questions and comments are detailed below:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) is not entirely clear. Do you update all coordinates of  `y^{t+1}` and `\hat{y}^{t+1}`, or just a single block "i"? My understanding is that you only update a block of the dual variable "y", but the method for selecting "i" isn’t explicitly stated. This should be clarified in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, I believe a summation symbol “∑_{i=1}^I” is missing from within the curly braces.

3) More discussion relating to Theorem 2 would be beneficial. Theorem 2 presents the convergence rate of the proposed PDMM method. First, given that PDMM generalizes several variants of ADMM, a natural question is whether Theorem 2, with properly chosen parameters like K and J, is consistent with the convergence rates of those variants. Second, since you split the transformation matrix A into multiple blocks, what is the optimal way to perform this split based on your theorem’s findings?

4) I am curious about potential comparisons on the tensor completion problem (e.g., "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also has a multiple-block structure. Do you have any insights into which variant of ADMM might be optimal for this problem?

Minor points:

1) In equation (9), a space is missing after "min."

2) Line 217, remove the space before "Section." 

Overall, this is a strong paper, and I believe addressing the questions regarding Theorem 2 and clarifying the algorithm description would further strengthen the contribution. I am positive to accept this paper pending revisions addressing these points.
