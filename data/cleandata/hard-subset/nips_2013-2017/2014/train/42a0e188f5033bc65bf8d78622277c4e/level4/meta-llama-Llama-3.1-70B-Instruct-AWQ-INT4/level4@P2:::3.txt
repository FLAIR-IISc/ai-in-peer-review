This paper proposes a randomized parallel version of ADMM, called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors' main contribution is the development of a parallel randomized block coordinate method that can handle multiple blocks, allowing for full parallelization of the primal step and relatively large update step sizes.

The paper relates PDMM to previous work, including ADMM and its variants, such as sADMM and PJADMM. The authors show that PDMM is a generalization of these methods and can be viewed as a unification of sADMM and PJADMM. They also highlight the advantages of PDMM, including its ability to handle multiple blocks, its flexibility in choosing the number of blocks to update, and its potential for parallelization.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of PDMM, establishing its global convergence and iteration complexity. They show that PDMM converges at a rate of O(1/T) with a constant step size, which is the same as the convergence rate of ADMM. They also provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm's behavior, including the importance of the backward step in the dual update and the role of the proximal term in the primal update.

The paper includes experimental results on two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso. The results show that PDMM performs better than existing methods, including sADMM and GSADMM, in terms of convergence speed and accuracy. However, the authors do not report the performance of PDMM with parallel implementation, which is an important aspect of the algorithm's practicality.
