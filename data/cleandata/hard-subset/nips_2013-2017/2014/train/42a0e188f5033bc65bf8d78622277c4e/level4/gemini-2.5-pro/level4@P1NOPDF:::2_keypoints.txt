- Proposes Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints using parallel (Jacobian-style) primal updates.  
- Introduces a novel "dual backward step" that reduces the dual step size to compensate for limited information propagation in parallel updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, comparable to standard ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results indicate PDMM outperforms ADMM variants in practice, which is counter-intuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods.  
- Requests further explanation of why PDMM overcomes the usual disadvantage of Jacobian-type methods.  
- Presents PDMM as a valuable new alternative for distributed optimization with promising directions for future work.
