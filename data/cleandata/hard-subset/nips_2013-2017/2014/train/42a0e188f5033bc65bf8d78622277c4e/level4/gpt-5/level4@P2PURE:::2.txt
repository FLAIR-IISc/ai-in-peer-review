This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. In contrast to ADMM, which updates the primal variables in a Gauss–Seidel fashion and is typically limited to two blocks, this work updates multiple primal blocks in a Jacobian manner. The main contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step, which compensates for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size. Similar to ADMM, the paper establishes O(1/T)-type convergence guarantees for PDMM.

The supplementary material shows that two prior methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed framework, thereby clarifying the relationships among augmented-Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM relative to ADMM and its variants. These findings are somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type updates are generally expected to converge more slowly than Gauss–Seidel updates; it would be helpful for the authors to elaborate on why PDMM performs well in this setting. The dual backward step appears to be new and crucial for ensuring convergence under conditions comparable to those of ADMM. Overall, the method broadens the set of tools and design choices available for distributed optimization.
