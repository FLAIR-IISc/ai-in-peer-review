- Paper proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM updating K primal blocks per iteration with a dual backward step; proves sublinear convergence and unifies ADMM variants.
- Overall assessment: well written, technically sound, parallelizable and general method; recommend acceptance with clarifications and minor revisions.
- Algorithm specification unclear: need precise description of which dual blocks are updated, update order relative to primal, and whether dual updates are full or blockwise.
- Sampling scheme: clarify how dual/primal block indices are selected (same subset, independent, or deterministic) and provide explicit pseudocode for S_t with |S_t|=K.
- Dual backward-step mechanics: explain synchronization across K primal updates, how the backward step aggregates effects, and exact formulas for y^{t+1} and ĥy^{t+1}.
- If dual updates are full, explain how step sizes scale with K to maintain stability.
- Theorem 2: fix typographical error—missing ∑_{i=1}^I after a left brace—and check consistency of related expressions.
- Specialize Theorem 2: show reductions to 2-block ADMM and other variants by specifying parameters (K, J, step sizes) and verify matching known rates/constants.
- Clarify whether convergence rate is ergodic or non-ergodic, how sampling expectations enter, and give practical parameter selection guidance (penalty, backward-step).
- Block partitioning and K choice: discuss how A’s partition affects constants (spectral norms/Lipschitz), how to choose partitions to optimize rates, and trade-offs between larger K and step-size scaling for wall-clock gains.
- Tensor completion: comment which ADMM/PDMM variants and K choices are theoretically preferable and offer empirical/guidance on block partitioning and parameters.
- Precisely define the dual “backward step” (extragradient/proximal/over-relaxation relation) and collate all assumptions for Theorem 2 (convexity, smoothness, rank conditions) in one place.
- Efficiency: describe how residuals Ax−b are maintained incrementally when only K blocks update, especially for parallel/distributed implementations.
- Minor copyedits: add space after “min” in (9), remove space before “Section” at line 217, and check equation references and hat/superscript notation consistency.
