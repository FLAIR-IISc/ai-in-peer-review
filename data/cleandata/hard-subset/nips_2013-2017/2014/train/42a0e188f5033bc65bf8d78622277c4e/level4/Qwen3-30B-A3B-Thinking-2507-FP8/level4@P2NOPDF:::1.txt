

This paper addresses the minimization of block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints, proposing a parallelizable variant of ADMM termed PDMM. PDMM randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for parallel update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for PDMM, which generalizes several existing ADMM variants. The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and makes a technically sound contribution to the literature. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify key aspects of the algorithm and theorem.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Algorithm Clarity (Equations 5–6):** The description of the dual update (Equations 5 and 6) requires clarification. It is unclear whether $y^{t+1}$ and $\hat{y}^{t+1}$ are updated for *all* dual blocks or *a single randomly selected block* $i$. The text suggests only one block is updated (as PDMM selects K blocks for primal update), but the selection mechanism for block $i$ is not specified. Please explicitly state that a single block $i$ is selected uniformly at random from the $I$ blocks, and clarify how this selection is implemented in the algorithm description.

2.  **Theorem 2 Proof:** In the proof of Theorem 2, the expression "$\sum_{i=1}^I$" appears to be missing immediately after the opening brace "{" (e.g., in the derivation of the bound). This omission should be corrected to ensure the proof's mathematical rigor.

3.  **Theorem 2 Discussion:** Theorem 2 is the paper's central result, providing the convergence rate for PDMM. Two critical points require expansion:
    *   **Consistency with ADMM Variants:** The paper states PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants. The authors should explicitly verify that Theorem 2 recovers known convergence rates for these variants (e.g., when $K=1$ or $J=1$) by choosing appropriate parameters. This would strengthen the generality claim.
    *   **Optimal Block Splitting:** Theorem 2's rate depends on the block structure of $A$. The authors should discuss how the block splitting strategy (i.e., how $A$ is partitioned) influences the convergence rate. Specifically, what criteria (e.g., balancing block sizes, conditioning) lead to the optimal splitting for practical implementation?

4.  **Relevance to Tensor Completion:** The paper mentions tensor completion as a problem with multiple block structure (citing "tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"). The authors should briefly address whether PDMM is applicable to such problems and, if so, which ADMM variant (or parameter choice for PDMM) would be optimal. This contextualizes the method's practical utility.

**Minor Issues:**

*   Equation (9): Add a space after "min" (i.e., "min " → "min ").
*   Line 217: Remove the space before "Section" (e.g., " Section" → "Section").

The paper is strong and makes a valuable contribution. Addressing the above points—particularly the algorithmic clarity, the proof correction, and the deeper discussion of Theorem 2—will significantly enhance its impact and readability. The minor typographical errors are easily fixed. I believe the paper is suitable for publication in NIPS after these revisions.
