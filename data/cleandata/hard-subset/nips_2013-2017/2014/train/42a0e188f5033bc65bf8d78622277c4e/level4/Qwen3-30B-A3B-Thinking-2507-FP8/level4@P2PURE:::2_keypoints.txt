- Introduces PDMM: a parallel direction method of multipliers for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Main algorithmic difference from ADMM: PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian (parallel) manner rather than ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel (sequential) two-block updates.  
- Primary technical contribution: a novel dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Theoretical guarantee: establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, analogous to ADMM.  
- Unifies prior work: shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Empirical claim: experiments demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and other variants.  
- Concern/limitation: results seem counterintuitive because Jacobian-type methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel; authors should explain this contradiction.  
- Significance: dual backward step appears novel and central to PDMM’s theoretical validity, offering additional alternatives for distributed optimization.
