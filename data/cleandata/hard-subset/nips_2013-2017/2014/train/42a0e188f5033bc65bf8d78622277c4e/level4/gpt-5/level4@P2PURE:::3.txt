This paper proposes a randomized, parallel variant of ADMM (PADMM) that supports parallelization across multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM selects K blocks at random, updates the corresponding primal variables (in parallel), and then performs a backward step to update the dual variable. The authors emphasize that this backward step makes the dual update more conservative, which underpins the global convergence guarantee. The method also permits adding a proximal term to the primal update, making certain subproblems easier to solve. Theoretical analysis establishes global convergence and iteration complexity: with J total blocks and K blocks updated per iteration, the convergence rate after T iterations is O(J/(K T)).

The main advantages of PADMM are: (1) full parallelization of the primal step, and (2) allowance for relatively large step sizes compared to other methods. In particular, PADMM can be faster than sADMM because it admits larger step sizes; it is also more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM. The authors evaluate PADMM on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, showing promising results: PADMM reaches the target accuracy with less computation time. However, the paper does not report performance from an actual parallel implementation, and, compared to standard ADMM, tuning PADMM’s three parameters is less convenient.

Overall, this is an interesting contribution to ADMM-type problems with multiple constraints. The algorithm achieves the same convergence rate as ADMM while enabling parallelization and demonstrating strong practical performance. The paper is well written, and both the theoretical and empirical results are solid.
