- Introduces PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant for multi-block problems.  
- Each iteration randomly selects K blocks for parallel primal updates and applies a conservative backward dual update.  
- The backward dual step is crucial to ensure global convergence and prevent divergence.  
- PADMM supports proximal terms in primal updates, enabling efficient subproblem solutions.  
- Theoretical results prove global convergence with iteration complexity O(J/(T K)).  
- The convergence rate matches standard ADMM while allowing parallelism.  
- PADMM enables full parallelization of the primal step and permits larger step sizes than some ADMM variants.  
- Empirically outperforms sADMM (which needs smaller step sizes) and offers more flexibility than PJADMM and greater parallelism than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show significant speedups to reach target accuracy.  
- Missing parallel-runtime experiments: no results showing runtime scaling with increasing processors.  
- Practical adoption may be harder due to three tuning parameters (ρ, θ, τ) that are less straightforward than standard ADMM.  
- Paper is well-written, with rigorous convergence analysis and a strong link between the backward step and theoretical guarantees.  
- Comparisons to sADMM, PJADMM, and GSADMM are thorough and highlight PADMM’s niche.  
- Minor fixes: typo “palatalization” → “multiple blocks”; standardize O(J/(T K)) notation; clarify “larger update step size” is relative to standard ADMM.  
- Overall: meaningful contribution to scalable multi-block ADMM; suitable for acceptance pending minor clarifications and additional empirical parallelization evidence.
