This paper proposes a parallel direction method of Multiplier-Division Modular Functions (PDMM) for reducing block-divisible convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates the primal variable using the Gauss-Seidel method but is limited to two blocks, this method proposes updating the primal variable in a Jacobian manner with multiple blocks. The main result of this paper is the introduction of a double backward step, which compensates for the spread of limited information in the Jacobian update by effectively reducing the step size for double updates. Similar to ADMM, this paper establishes an O(1/t) type of convergence result for PDMM.

It is shown (in the supplementary material) that two previous methods, SDMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed method, which better explains the relationship between different methods in the context of decomposition methods based on advanced Lagrangian. The results of the experiments demonstrated the effectiveness of the PDMM method compared to ADMM and other types. The results are a bit counter-intuitive to me, as generally Jacobian-type methods will give slower convergence compared to Gauss-Seidel-type methods. It would be good to elaborate on this point. In general circumstances, the double backward step in PDMM seems new and important to ensure convergence like ADMM. The proposed method gives more options and possibilities for distributed optimization.
