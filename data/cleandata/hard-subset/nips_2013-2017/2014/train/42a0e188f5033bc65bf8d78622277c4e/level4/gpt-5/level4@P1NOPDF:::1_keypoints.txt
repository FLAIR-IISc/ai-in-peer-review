- Paper proposes PDMM, a randomized K-block parallelizable ADMM variant with a backward dual step, proves sublinear convergence, and subsumes several ADMM variants.  
- Paper is clearly written and reviewer is inclined to accept.  
- Q1: Algorithm clarity — unclear whether all dual coordinates or only selected blocks are updated each iteration, how index i is chosen (random/cyclic/other), and how dual updates align with K-block primal updates.  
- Q2: Theorem 2 typographical error — a missing “∑_{i=1}^I” appears in the proof and should be corrected.  
- Q3a: Request to show that Theorem 2 recovers known convergence rates for special-case ADMM variants by mapping PDMM parameters (e.g., K, J) to those variants and stating resulting rates.  
- Q3b: Ask for discussion on how partitioning A (conditioning, sparsity, block sizes) affects constants/rates and whether there is guidance for optimal block splitting.  
- Q4: For tensor completion, request guidance (theoretical or empirical) on which ADMM variant or PDMM parameter choices (K, J) perform best given the multi-block structure.  
- Minor comments: add space after “min” in Eq. (9); remove the extra space before “Section” on line 217.  
- Overall: positive recommendation; strengthen by clarifying algorithm description and expanding discussion after Theorem 2, particularly connections to existing variants and block partitioning guidance.
