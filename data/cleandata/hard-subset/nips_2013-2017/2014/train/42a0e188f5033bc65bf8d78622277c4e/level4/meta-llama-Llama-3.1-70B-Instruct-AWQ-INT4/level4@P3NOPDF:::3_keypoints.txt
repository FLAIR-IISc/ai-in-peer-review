- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K of J blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks, then updates dual via a backward step.
- Backward dual step is emphasized as critical for making dual updates conservative and enabling global convergence.
- Allows adding a proximal term to the primal update to simplify certain subproblems.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.
- Enables full parallelization of the primal step, potentially reducing computation time.
- Permits relatively large update step sizes, often faster than stochastic ADMM (sADMM).
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than Gauss–Seidel ADMM (GSADMM).
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show promising accuracy with less computation time.
- Does not report results from a parallel implementation, missing evidence of practical parallel speedup.
- Requires tuning three parameters, a minor convenience drawback.
- Overall: solid theoretical and empirical contribution—same convergence rate as ADMM but with parallelization benefits; paper is well-written.
