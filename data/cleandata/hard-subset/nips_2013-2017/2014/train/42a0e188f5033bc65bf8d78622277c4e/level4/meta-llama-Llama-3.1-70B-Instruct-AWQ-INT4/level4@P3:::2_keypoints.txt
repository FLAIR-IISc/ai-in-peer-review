- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Targets the open problem of extending ADMM to multiple blocks by randomly updating blocks in parallel, similar to parallel RBCD.  
- Introduces a dual backward step that reduces effective dual step size and compensates limited information propagation, enabling convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with a constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM can perform randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks, an open extension for RBCD.  
- Demonstrates that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results indicate PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods in robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a rigorous theoretical foundation.  
- Suggests a more detailed analysis of the counterintuitive speed advantage of Jacobian-type methods (PDMM) over Gauss–Seidel-type methods (ADMM).  
- Recommends more comprehensive experiments, including comparisons with other optimization methods.  
- Views the introduction of the dual backward step as a key original contribution with significant potential for large-scale problems.  
- Recommends acceptance for publication, advising the authors to address the noted weaknesses in future work.
