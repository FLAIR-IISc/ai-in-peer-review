- Proposes PDMM to minimize block-separable convex functions under linear constraints, closely related to ADMM and its variants.  
- Thorough literature review highlighting limitations of existing multi-block methods.  
- Shows PDMM generalizes sADMM and PJADMM (these are special cases of PDMM).  
- Main contribution: introduces the dual backward step as a key PDMM component.  
- Dual backward step enables Jacobian-style primal updates and reduces dual step size to ensure convergence.  
- Provides detailed convergence analysis, establishing O(1/T)-type rates.  
- Experiments indicate PDMM can converge faster and outperform ADMM variants, especially for overlapping blocks.  
- Results are counterintuitive because Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel methods.  
- Paper is well-written, technically thorough, and notation is consistent.  
- Lacks sufficient intuition/explanation for why the dual backward step ensures convergence.  
- Originality: PDMM is a significant new method with meaningful connections to prior work.  
- Significance: offers a useful new tool for complex convex optimization problems.  
- Weakness: needs deeper insight into underlying mechanisms and analysis of computational complexity.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with requests to elaborate on intuition behind the dual backward step and provide more complexity analysis.
