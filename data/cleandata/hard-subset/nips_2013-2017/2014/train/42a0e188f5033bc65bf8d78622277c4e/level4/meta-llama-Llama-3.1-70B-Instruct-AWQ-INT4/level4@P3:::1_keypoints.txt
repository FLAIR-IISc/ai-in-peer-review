- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- PDMM randomly selects K primal blocks per iteration and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable.  
- Primal updates are parallelizable, making PDMM suitable for large-scale problems.  
- Authors prove global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Empirical results show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Strength: generalizes several ADMM variants.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis establishing convergence and complexity.  
- Strength: strong experimental performance on the two applications.  
- Weakness: algorithm description around equations (5) and (6) is unclear about whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} or a single block are updated.  
- Weakness: paper needs more discussion of Theorem 2, its convergence-rate implications, and relation to other ADMM variants.  
- Minor: typographical issues—missing space after "min" in (9) and an extra space before "Section" at line 217.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel method generalizing ADMM, solid theory, and superior empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: clarity issues in algorithm description and insufficient discussion of Theorem 2.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying (5)/(6), expanding Theorem 2 discussion, and (optionally) adding experiments (e.g., compare with other ADMM variants on tensor completion).
