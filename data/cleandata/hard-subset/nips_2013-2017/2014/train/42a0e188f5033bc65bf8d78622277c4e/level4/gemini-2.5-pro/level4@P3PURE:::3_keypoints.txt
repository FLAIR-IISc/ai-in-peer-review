- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that samples K out of J blocks per iteration, updates those primal blocks in parallel, then performs a conservative "backward step" dual update.
- The backward (conservative) dual update is highlighted as crucial for ensuring global convergence.
- Includes an optional proximal term in the primal update to simplify subproblems.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving global convergence and iteration complexity.
- Establishes a convergence rate of O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J total blocks and K sampled per iteration.
- Claims advantages: full parallelization of primal updates and tolerance for a relatively large stepsize.
- Argues PADMM can outperform sADMM (larger stepsize), is more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Empirically evaluated on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso with promising results (faster time-to-accuracy).
- Major empirical weakness: no results from a true parallel implementation, despite parallelization being a core motivation.
- Minor drawback: requires tuning three parameters versus one for standard ADMM.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with solid theoretical and empirical contributions; matches standard ADMM convergence while enabling parallelism.
