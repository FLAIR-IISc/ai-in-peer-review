This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) to minimize block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The work is closely related to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and its variants, which have been extensively studied in the context of convex optimization problems. The authors provide a thorough review of the existing literature, highlighting the limitations of current methods, particularly when dealing with multiple blocks. They also establish connections between PDMM and other related methods, such as sADMM and PJADMM, demonstrating that these methods are special cases of PDMM.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the dual backward step, which is a key component of PDMM. This step allows for the update of primal blocks in a Jacobian manner, effectively reducing the step size for the dual updates and ensuring convergence under general conditions. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence properties of PDMM, establishing O(1/T) type convergence results.

The experimental results presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in comparison to ADMM and other variants. The results show that PDMM can achieve faster convergence and better performance in certain scenarios, particularly when dealing with overlapping blocks. However, it is worth noting that the results are somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence compared to Gauss-Seidel type methods.
