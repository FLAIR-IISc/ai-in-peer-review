- Addresses multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints and proposes PDMM for this setting.
- PDMM uses randomized block coordinate updates and a backward dual-step to enable parallel updates and improve stability.
- Theoretical results include global convergence and an iteration complexity bound.
- PDMM is presented as a generalization that unifies several existing ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM).
- Empirical validation on RPCA and overlapping group lasso shows competitive practical performance.
- The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and the motivation is strong.
- Novelty: the randomized block-update strategy is a valuable contribution for multi-block ADMM challenges.
- Theoretical rigor: proofs and convergence analysis strengthen the method’s reliability.
- Generality: framing PDMM as a unifying framework enhances understanding of ADMM variants.
- Weakness: algorithm description (eqs. 5–6) is ambiguous about which dual blocks are updated and how indices are selected.
- Weakness: limited discussion of Theorem 2’s implications, especially dependence on parameters K and J and comparisons to known ADMM rates.
- Weakness: no guidance on optimal block-splitting of matrix A or how block structure affects convergence.
- Weakness: experiments could be broadened (e.g., tensor completion) to show wider applicability and discuss best ADMM choices for such problems.
- Minor issues: typographical/notation errors (Line 109 spacing, Line 217 spacing, missing summation in Theorem 2).
- Recommendation: strong paper with promising impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, theoretical discussion, and block-splitting guidance.
