- Proposes randomized Parallel ADMM (PADMM) that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal variables, then applies a backward dual update.
- Backward dual step yields more conservative dual updates, which the authors show are crucial for global convergence.
- Option to include a proximal term in primal updates to simplify subproblem solutions.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T*K)) after T iterations with J blocks and block size K.
- Key benefits: fully parallelizable primal updates and allowance of relatively large step sizes versus existing methods.
- Claims PADMM can outperform sADMM (due to larger step sizes), offers more flexibility than PJADMM, and better parallelizability than GSADMM.
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PADMM reaches target accuracy with reduced computational cost.
- No experimental results from an actual parallel implementation are provided.
- PADMM introduces three tuning parameters, potentially requiring more careful tuning than traditional ADMM.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with sound theory and experiments; comparable convergence rate to ADMM while enabling parallelization.
