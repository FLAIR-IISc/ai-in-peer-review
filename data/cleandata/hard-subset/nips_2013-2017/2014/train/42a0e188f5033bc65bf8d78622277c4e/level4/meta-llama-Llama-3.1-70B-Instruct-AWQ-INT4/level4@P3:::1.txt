This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method, called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size and demonstrate its effectiveness in two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear description of the problem and the proposed method, and the theoretical analysis is thorough. The experimental results show that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in the two applications.

Here is a summary of the main ideas in the paper:

* The authors consider the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints, which can be cast into a linearly-constrained optimization problem.
* They propose a parallel randomized block coordinate method, called PDMM, which randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and uses a backward step to refine the dual variable.
* The process to update the primal variable can be implemented in parallel, making PDMM suitable for large-scale problems.
* The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size.
* They demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.
