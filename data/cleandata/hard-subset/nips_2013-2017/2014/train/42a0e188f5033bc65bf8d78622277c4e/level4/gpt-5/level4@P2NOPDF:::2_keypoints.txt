- Introduces PDMM for minimizing block-separable convex objectives with linear constraints.
- PDMM performs parallel (Jacobi-style) updates of multiple primal blocks, unlike ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel two-block updates.
- Main technical novelty is a dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation by effectively shrinking the dual step size.
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence guarantees for PDMM, analogous to classical ADMM results.
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, offering a unifying perspective on augmented Lagrangian–based methods.
- Empirical results indicate PDMM performs favorably compared to ADMM and several variants.
- Reviewer notes a counterintuitive experimental finding (Jacobi often slower than Gauss–Seidel) and suggests deeper analysis of why PDMM avoids slowdown—e.g., role of the dual backward step, problem regimes (conditioning, block structure, sparsity), and parallelism vs. iteration-complexity trade-offs.
- Overall assessment: dual backward step is a novel, important ingredient enabling ADMM-comparable convergence with parallel multi-block updates, broadening tools for distributed/parallel convex optimization and systematizing prior variants.
