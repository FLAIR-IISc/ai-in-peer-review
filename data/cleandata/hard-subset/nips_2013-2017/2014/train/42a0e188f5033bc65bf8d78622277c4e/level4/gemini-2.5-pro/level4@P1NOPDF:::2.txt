This paper introduces the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), an algorithm for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. In contrast to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which updates primal variables sequentially in a Gauss-Seidel fashion and is typically limited to two blocks, the proposed PDMM updates all primal blocks in parallel, akin to a Jacobian method.

The central innovation of the paper is the introduction of a "dual backward step," which compensates for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian-style updates by effectively reducing the step size of the dual update. This mechanism appears to be the key to achieving convergence. Similar to standard ADMM, the authors establish an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM.

It is shown in the supplementary materials that two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed framework. This analysis provides a better understanding of the relationships between different augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the PDMM method in comparison to ADMM and its variants. I found these results somewhat counter-intuitive, as Jacobian-type methods generally converge more slowly than their Gauss-Seidel counterparts. It would strengthen the paper if the authors could elaborate on why PDMM overcomes this typical limitation.

The novel "dual backward step" appears to be the key mechanism that enables PDMM to achieve convergence under conditions as general as those required for ADMM. Overall, the proposed method provides a valuable new alternative for distributed optimization and opens up promising possibilities for future work.
