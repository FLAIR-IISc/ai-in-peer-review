- Proposes PDMM: a Primal–Dual Fixed Point Method (Majorized ADMM variant) that updates K randomly selected primal blocks in parallel, with a backward dual update, and proves sublinear convergence while generalizing several ADMM variants.
- Paper is well-written, contributions are clear, and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Major concern: Algorithmic clarity — dual updates in eqs. (5) and (6) are ambiguous about whether they apply to all coordinates or a specific block i, and how i is chosen; clarify for reproducibility.
- Major concern: Theorem 2 needs a deeper discussion of its implications and practical guidance.
- Suggestion: Show that Theorem 2 recovers known convergence rates of special-case ADMM variants when parameters (e.g., K, J) are set accordingly.
- Suggestion: Provide guidance on block partitioning of A — whether certain block structures (equal size, low inter-block coupling) improve convergence.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential applications and comparisons, e.g., performance on natural multi-block problems like tensor completion and which ADMM variant may be preferable.
- Minor issues: check Theorem 2 for a missing summation term \sum_{i=1}^I, add a space after "min" in eq. (9), and remove extraneous space before "Section" on Line 217.
