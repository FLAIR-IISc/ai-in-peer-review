- Introduces PDMM, a parallel algorithm for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints that updates multiple primal blocks concurrently.  
- Central contribution: a "dual backward step" that reduces dual step size to compensate for limited information propagation in parallel Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rate, matching theoretical guarantees of standard ADMM.  
- Novelty/significance: addresses ADMM's sequential bottleneck, enabling scalable parallel and distributed optimization.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides solid convergence analysis giving confidence in stability and reliability.  
- Unification: shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Empirical validation: experiments demonstrate practical effectiveness and strong performance compared to ADMM variants.  
- Suggestion: paper should better explain why PDMM empirically outperforms typical Jacobian methods—clarify the role of the dual backward step versus problem/algorithm structure.  
- Conclusion: strong, practical contribution that enables parallel ADMM-like updates while maintaining rigorous convergence guarantees.
