This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) to minimize block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Addressing limitations of existing Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) approaches – particularly the challenges of extending ADMM’s effectiveness beyond two blocks – this work introduces a Jacobian-style update for primal variables with multiple blocks. A key contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step, designed to mitigate potential issues with information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates and effectively control the step size for dual variable updates. The authors establish global convergence and achieve an O(1/T) iteration complexity, similar to ADMM.

The paper effectively positions PDMM within the broader landscape of decomposition methods based on augmented Lagrangian formulations. Notably, the supplementary materials demonstrate that existing methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM, providing valuable insights into the relationships between these approaches. Experimental results demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and its variants. The observed performance – where PDMM outperforms methods typically associated with faster convergence like Gauss-Seidel – is somewhat counterintuitive and warrants further discussion. The dual backward step appears to be a novel and crucial component, enabling convergence under relatively general conditions similar to those required by ADMM. Overall, the proposed method offers a valuable addition to the toolkit for distributed optimization.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results. The presentation is complete, and the authors demonstrate careful consideration of the strengths and weaknesses of their work. 

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The notation is consistent, and the explanation of the dual backward step is particularly helpful. Providing more intuition behind the observed performance advantage over Gauss-Seidel methods would further enhance clarity.

**Originality:** The PDMM algorithm and the inclusion of the dual backward step represent a novel contribution. The unification of sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM also highlights the originality of the work. The related work section is comprehensive.

**Significance:** The proposed method addresses a significant challenge in the application of ADMM to multi-block problems. The potential for improved performance and the increased flexibility offered by PDMM suggest that it could be adopted by researchers and practitioners working on large-scale optimization problems. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound algorithm for multi-block optimization.
*   Strong experimental results demonstrating performance improvements.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Unification of existing methods (sADMM and PJADMM) provides valuable insights.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The counterintuitive performance advantage over Gauss-Seidel methods could benefit from more detailed explanation and discussion.
*   The experimental evaluation could be expanded to include a wider range of problems and datasets.
