- Proposes PADMM: randomized block selection updating K of J primal blocks in parallel with a conservative backward dual update to stabilize multi-block ADMM.
- Theoretical results: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)), matching ADMM’s O(1/T) rate while revealing a J/K parallel speedup factor.
- Strength: clear algorithmic idea combining randomized parallel primal updates with a stabilizing dual backward step.
- Strength: solid theory with explicit complexity bound capturing dependence on J and K.
- Strength: practical flexibility via optional proximal terms to simplify and stabilize primal subproblems.
- Strength: empirical promise shown on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso where PADMM reached targets faster in reported experiments.
- Weakness: parallel performance not demonstrated—experiments appear single-threaded, so claimed practical speedups are unverified.
- Weakness: additional tuning burden (K, primal/dual step sizes, proximal coefficients) with limited guidance provided.
- Weakness: comparisons and experimental settings need strengthening—sensitivity analyses, per-iteration cost, wall-clock times, and fair hyperparameter tuning are missing.
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions and scope are not crisply stated (convexity, saddle-point existence, Lipschitz/strong-convexity, variance conditions, role of proximal terms).
- Weakness: unclear randomization details—sampling scheme (with/without replacement or importance sampling) and K’s effect on variance beyond the J/K factor need clarification.
- Suggestion: provide multi-core/distributed experiments showing speedups vs single-threaded PADMM, standard ADMM, and other multi-block ADMMs, with scaling plots over threads and K.
- Suggestion: offer practical tuning guidelines and ablation studies for K, dual step sizes, and proximal parameters.
- Suggestion: expand comparisons to sADMM, PJADMM, GSADMM with matched stopping criteria and best-tuned hyperparameters, reporting both iterations and wall-clock times.
- Suggestion: clarify theory and assumptions up front, state whether O(J/(T K)) is in expectation or ergodic, and discuss conditions for faster rates and proximal-term effects.
- Suggestion: discuss implementation details like residual computation costs, preconditioning needs, and potential effects of asynchrony on convergence.
- Minor: fix typos (“palatalization” → “parallelization”), use “step size,” define acronyms at first mention, and cite canonical references.
- Minor: explicitly state what measure the O(J/(T K)) rate characterizes (ergodic primal-dual gap or other) and whether it’s an expected bound.
- Overall: well-written and meaningful contribution with sound theory and promising experiments, but the lack of true parallel evaluation and clearer tuning/theoretical details must be addressed; reviewer leans positive contingent on fixes.
