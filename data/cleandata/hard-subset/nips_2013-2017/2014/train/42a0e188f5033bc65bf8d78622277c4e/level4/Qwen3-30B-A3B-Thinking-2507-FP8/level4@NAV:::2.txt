

This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which employs Gauss-Seidel updates limited to two blocks, PDMM utilizes Jacobian-style updates for multiple primal blocks. The core contribution is the dual backward step, which addresses limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by adaptively reducing the dual step size. The paper establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, analogous to ADMM. Supplementary materials demonstrate that two prior methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM, thereby clarifying their relationships within the landscape of augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Experimental results confirm PDMM's effectiveness relative to ADMM and its variants. The results are somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods typically exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel methods; the authors should elaborate on this discrepancy. The dual backward step is novel and essential for achieving convergence under general conditions comparable to ADMM. Overall, the proposed method provides greater flexibility for distributed optimization.
