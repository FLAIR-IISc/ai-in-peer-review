- Proposes PDMM: a parallel Jacobian-style method for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.
- Contrasts with ADMM: PDMM updates multiple primal blocks in parallel rather than sequential Gauss–Seidel updates, and is not limited to two blocks.
- Introduces a novel "dual backward step" to compensate for limited information propagation in parallel updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.
- Establishes an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, comparable to ADMM.
- Shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying several augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM's empirical effectiveness versus ADMM and variants, despite Jacobian methods typically converging slower.
- Notes that explaining why PDMM overcomes the usual slow convergence of Jacobian methods would strengthen the paper.
- Concludes the dual backward step is the key mechanism enabling convergence under ADMM-like conditions.
- Overall, PDMM offers a valuable alternative and expands options for distributed optimization.
