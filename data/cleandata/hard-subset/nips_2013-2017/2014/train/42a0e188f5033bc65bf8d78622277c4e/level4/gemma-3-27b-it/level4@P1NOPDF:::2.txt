This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel manner and is typically limited to two blocks, this work updates the primal blocks using a Jacobian approach, extending the method to handle multiple blocks. The core contribution of the paper is the introduction of a dual backward step, which addresses the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the step size for dual variable updates. Similar to ADMM, the authors establish O(1/T) convergence results for PDMM.

The supplementary materials demonstrate that existing methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed PDMM, providing a clearer understanding of the relationships between different augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM compared to ADMM and other variants. These results are somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-based methods generally exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel methods. It would be beneficial for the authors to elaborate on this observation. The dual backward step in PDMM appears to be novel and crucial for achieving convergence under general conditions comparable to those of ADMM. Overall, the proposed method provides more flexibility and potential for distributed optimization.
