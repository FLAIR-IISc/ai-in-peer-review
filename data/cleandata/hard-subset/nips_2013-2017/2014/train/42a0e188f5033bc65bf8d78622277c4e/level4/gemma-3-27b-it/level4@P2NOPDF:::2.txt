This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. While existing Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) implementations typically update primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel fashion – and are generally limited to two blocks – this work proposes a Jacobian-based update scheme capable of handling multiple blocks concurrently. The core contribution is the introduction of a “dual backward step,” designed to address the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by adaptively reducing the step size for dual variable updates. The authors establish convergence results for PDMM comparable to ADMM, achieving an O(1/T) rate.

The supplementary material demonstrates that existing methods, sequential ADMM (sADMM) and parallel Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM), are specific instances of the proposed PDMM framework. This provides valuable insight into the relationship between various decomposition methods rooted in augmented Lagrangian approaches.  Experimental results showcase PDMM’s effectiveness, demonstrating performance gains over ADMM and other related algorithms. 

I found some of these results somewhat surprising. Generally, Jacobian-based methods tend to exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel approaches. A more detailed discussion of *why* PDMM overcomes this expectation would strengthen the paper. Specifically, elaborating on the role of the dual backward step in accelerating convergence despite the Jacobian update structure would be beneficial. This dual backward step appears to be a novel and crucial element, enabling convergence under conditions comparable to those of ADMM.

Overall, this work provides a valuable addition to the toolkit for distributed optimization, offering increased flexibility and potentially improved performance compared to existing methods. The proposed method introduces a valuable alternative for tackling large-scale, block-separable optimization problems.
