- Introduces PDMM: a randomized parallel algorithm that updates K blocks per iteration with a dual “backward step” to ensure conservative progress and convergence.  
- Core benefit: enables full parallelization of primal updates and allows potentially larger step sizes than existing methods.  
- Provides global convergence guarantees and iteration complexity bounds.  
- Empirical validation: promising results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso tasks.  
- Thorough contextualization: clearly positions PDMM among ADMM variants and explains limitations of naive multi-block ADMM.  
- Shows relations to prior methods, identifying sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PDMM.  
- Highlights flexibility: adapts to different problem structures and tunable parameters to trade off speed and computation.  
- Experiments are presented sequentially; no parallel implementation or parallel performance measurements are reported.  
- Practical drawback: PDMM has three tunable parameters and the paper lacks detailed guidance or sensitivity analysis for parameter selection.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid theoretical analysis, empirical support, clear presentation, and strong connection to related work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing parallel-performance evidence and increased parameter-tuning complexity for users.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—accept with minor revisions to include discussion of parallel implementation considerations and more detailed parameter-tuning guidance.
