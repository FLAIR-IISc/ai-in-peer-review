- Problem: Extends ADMM to multi-block problems with linear constraints, addressing convergence failures of naive multi-block ADMM.  
- Proposed method: PDMM — a randomized block-coordinate algorithm that updates K of J blocks in parallel each iteration.  
- Key innovation 1: Introduces a "dual backward step" to temper dual updates and prevent divergence in parallel updates.  
- Key innovation 2: Provides principled step-size rules (τ_i, ν_i) that depend on K, J, and block-wise constraint sparsity.  
- Key innovation 3: Unifies prior Jacobian-style ADMM methods (sADMM, PJADMM) as special cases within the PDMM framework.  
- Theory: Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size, matching standard ADMM rates.  
- Applicability: Framework handles overlapping groups, a challenging case for traditional block-coordinate methods.  
- Experiments: Shows competitive or superior performance on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso versus GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM.  
- Strength — originality/significance: Novel, well-motivated solution to multi-block parallel ADMM with valuable unification of prior work.  
- Strength — technical quality: Solid theoretical analysis with correct and well-explained derivations.  
- Strength — clarity: Clear, well-organized exposition and intuitive explanation of parameters and components.  
- Strength — practical relevance: Potentially useful for scalable ML optimization by exploiting parallelism and sparsity.  
- Weakness — parallel evaluation: Experiments were sequential; no empirical multi-core/distributed runtime or scalability results provided.  
- Weakness — choice of K: Little theoretical or practical guidance on selecting the number of blocks K, despite its strong impact.  
- Weakness — step-size tuning: Theoretical step-size formula appears conservative; manual tuning yields substantially better practical performance.  
- Overall: Strong, original, and theoretically sound paper with minor empirical limitations; well-suited for acceptance.
