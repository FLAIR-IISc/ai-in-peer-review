- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that picks K random blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks, then performs a backward dual update.
- Backward dual step makes dual updates more conservative, which the authors show is key for global convergence.
- Algorithm permits adding a proximal term in the primal update to simplify subproblems.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity when J blocks exist and K are chosen per iteration.
- Main advantage: full parallelization of the primal step and allowance of relatively large step sizes compared to sADMM.
- Claimed to be more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PDMM reaches desired accuracy with less computation time.
- Did not report empirical results for a parallel implementation, limiting evidence of practical parallel speedup.
- PDMM requires tuning three parameters, making it less convenient than traditional ADMM for practitioners.
- Suggested improvements: include parallel implementation performance and provide parameter-tuning guidance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient, parallelizable algorithm with matching ADMM convergence rate and solid theoretical/empirical support.
- Arguments for rejection: lack of reported parallel-performance experiments and extra parameter-tuning burden.
