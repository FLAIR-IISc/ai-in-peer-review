- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses gap in literature on multi-block ADMM generalizations beyond the well-studied two-block case.  
- Updates multiple blocks in parallel using randomized block selection and includes a dual backward step to ensure convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, offering a unifying perspective.  
- Validated empirically on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in speed and runtime.  
- Provides a theoretically sound method for parallelizing ADMM with constant step size, filling a key limitation of existing approaches.  
- The dual backward step (Eqs. 6–7) is a significant insight that fixes convergence issues of direct Jacobi updates.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with well-constructed proofs for global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Handles overlapping blocks effectively, an important and challenging case for block-coordinate methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation and strong related-work connections (Section 2.2).  
- Experimental results are convincing, especially the clear advantage on overlapping group lasso.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify that all dual blocks are updated simultaneously in the dual update (Eqs. 5–7).  
- Minor correction: Theorem 2 should include the summation over all dual blocks (\sum_{i=1}^I) as noted.  
- Suggest expanding discussion of Theorem 2 to explicitly relate PDMM convergence rates to known ADMM variants (e.g., K=J → method of multipliers, K=1 → randomized variant).  
- Highly relevant to NIPS due to scalability and theoretical contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance.
