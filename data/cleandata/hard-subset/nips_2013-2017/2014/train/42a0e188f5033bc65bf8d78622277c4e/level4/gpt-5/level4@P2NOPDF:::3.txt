This paper introduces a randomized parallel variant of ADMM (PADMM) for multi-block problems. At each iteration, PADMM selects K blocks uniformly at random, updates the corresponding primal variables (in parallel), and then performs a conservative dual update via a backward step. The authors clearly motivate the backward step: by tempering the dual update, it stabilizes the method and ensures global convergence. The algorithm also permits adding proximal terms to the primal subproblems, which can simplify and accelerate their solution.

Theoretical results establish global convergence and iteration complexity bounds. With J total blocks and K blocks sampled per iteration, the convergence rate is O(J/(K T)) after T iterations, matching the asymptotic rate of standard ADMM while enabling parallelization.

Key advantages of PADMM include:
- Full parallelization of the primal updates across the selected blocks.
- The ability to use relatively large step sizes compared to related methods. In particular, PADMM can be faster than sADMM due to its allowance for larger steps, is more flexible than PJADMM, and is more parallelizable than GSADMM.

The empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso is promising: PADMM reaches target accuracy with reduced computation time. However, the paper does not report performance under an actual parallel implementation, leaving open the question of wall-clock speedups in distributed or multi-core settings. In addition, PADMM requires tuning three parameters, which is less convenient than the single-parameter tuning in traditional ADMM.

Overall, this is a solid and interesting contribution to ADMM-type methods for multi-constraint problems. The paper is well written, the theoretical analysis is sound, and the experiments are encouraging. The method achieves the same convergence rate as classical ADMM while offering parallelization and practical gains, though reporting parallel runtime results and providing guidance on parameter tuning would further strengthen the work.
