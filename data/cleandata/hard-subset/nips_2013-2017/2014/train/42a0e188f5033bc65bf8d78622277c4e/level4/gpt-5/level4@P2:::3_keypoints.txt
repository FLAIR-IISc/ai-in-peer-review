- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel multi-block ADMM variant with K blocks updated per iteration and a dual "backward" correction.  
- Updates include optional proximal/Bregman terms; dual step sizes depend on number of updated blocks and constraint sparsity.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic iteration complexity with constant step sizes; rate scales like O(J/(K T)).  
- PDMM subsumes sADMM (small dual step, exact primal updates) and PJADMM (large proximal terms) as special cases.  
- Handles overlapping groups by introducing equality constraints to enable randomized block updates.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show competitive or improved wall-clock times and iterations versus sADMM, RBSUMM, GSADMM.  
- Novelty: dual backward step plus sparsity-aware step sizes for randomized parallel multi-block updates is conceptually new.  
- Unifying framework clarifies when small vs large dual steps are appropriate and generalizes prior randomized one-block methods.  
- Strength: clear conceptual innovation stabilizing parallel Jacobi-style updates.  
- Strength: supports nonsmooth objectives and linear constraints with clean reformulation for overlaps.  
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees and parameter rules that account for sparsity and K.  
- Strength: practical performance demonstrations on representative applications.  
- Weakness: no parallel implementation experiments to validate claimed scalability.  
- Weakness: several parameters (τi, νi, ρ, proximal/Bregman) require potentially sensitive tuning and lack practitioner guidance.  
- Weakness: GSADMM sometimes needs fewer iterations; fairness of step-size tuning across methods could be clarified.  
- Weakness: notation and parameter rules (dependence on di and K) are dense; a cheat sheet would aid readability.  
- Weakness: focus limited to convex problems; little discussion of nonconvex or asynchronous extensions.  
- Quality: technically sound analysis with clear assumptions and proofs; backward step rationale well motivated.  
- Clarity: generally well written but heavy notation; would benefit from a concise algorithm box with defaults.  
- Originality: provides a novel combination of backward step and sparsity-aware sizing, offering conceptual value.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale constrained learning with overlapping structure and parallel resources.  
- Pro-accept: solid theory, practical relevance, unification of ADMM variants, and competitive experiments.  
- Con-accept: lacks empirical parallel speedups, parameter tuning burden, and some transparency in baseline configurations.  
- Suggestion: add multicore/GPU/distributed experiments to validate scalability and K trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: provide default parameter choices, sensitivity analyses, and ablations on backward/proximal terms.  
- Suggestion: clarify practical estimation/use of sparsity (di) and include a practitioner-oriented step-size summary.  
- Suggestion: discuss asynchronous/partially synchronous variants and possible nonconvex extensions.  
- Overall: strong, timely contribution that would be more compelling with better parameter guidance and parallel validation; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
