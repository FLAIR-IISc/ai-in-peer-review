This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions under linear constraints. The authors propose PDMM, a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) designed for problems with multiple blocks. The method randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for parallel updates and then employs a backward step to refine the dual variable. A sublinear convergence rate is established for the proposed method. PDMM is shown to generalize several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written and the presentation is easy to follow. I lean towards acceptance, and my comments and questions for the authors are listed below.

1) The description of the dual variable updates in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. It is unclear whether all coordinates of `y^{t+1}` and `\hat{y}^{t+1}` are updated, or only those corresponding to a single block `i`. While my interpretation is that only a single block of the dual variable `y` is updated per iteration, the paper does not specify how this block `i` is selected. This aspect requires clarification in a revised version.

2) In the statement of Theorem 2, it appears a summation term, `\sum_{i=1}^I`, is missing after the opening brace `{`. This seems to be a typo, as the term is present in the corresponding proof.

3) The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of Theorem 2, which presents the main convergence result. Specifically:
    a) Since PDMM is presented as a generalization of several ADMM variants, it is natural to ask whether the convergence rate in Theorem 2 recovers the known rates for these specific methods when the parameters (e.g., K, J) are set appropriately. A discussion of this would strengthen the paper's claims.
    b) The theorem's convergence rate depends on the partitioning of the matrix A. Does the result provide any guidance on how to optimally partition A to achieve the fastest convergence?

4) Regarding applications, the tensor completion problem, such as the one described in "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data" (2012), also exhibits a multi-block structure amenable to ADMM. Do the authors have any insights into which ADMM variant—perhaps one subsumed by the proposed PDMM framework—would be best suited or optimal for this particular problem?

Minor points:
1. In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".
2. On line 217, there is an extra space before the word "Section".

In summary, I am supportive of this paper's acceptance, provided the authors address the points above, particularly by expanding the discussion following Theorem 2.
