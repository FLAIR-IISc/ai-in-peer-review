- Introduces PDMM: a parallel (Jacobi) multi-block update method using a “dual backward step” to stabilize reduced coupling, with O(1/T) convergence under convexity.
- Unification: shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, situating several augmented Lagrangian methods in one framework.
- Empirical finding: PDMM performs favorably vs ADMM variants despite using Jacobi updates, with the dual backward step likely key to practical performance.
- Strength — problem relevance: addresses block-separable convex optimization with linear constraints, important for large-scale/distributed learning.
- Strength — conceptual novelty: the dual backward step is a simple, principled mechanism to stabilize and accelerate parallel multi-block updates.
- Strength — theory: provides O(1/T) convergence rates comparable to ADMM under general convex assumptions.
- Strength — unification: clarifies relationships among prior multi-block ADMM variants.
- Strength — practical promise: parallelizable algorithm that empirically competes well and can reduce wall-clock time.
- Weakness — intuition: needs clearer explanation for why Jacobi PDMM can match/surpass Gauss–Seidel methods (stability, reduced wall-clock, oscillation mitigation; illustrative examples or spectral analysis requested).
- Weakness — parameter selection: lacks practical guidance and sensitivity analysis for choosing dual backward step size and penalty parameters.
- Weakness — assumptions/scope: convergence theorem assumptions (convexity/closedness, constraint matrix properties, saddle-point existence) should be stated explicitly and compared to ADMM/sADMM/PJADMM.
- Weakness — complexity: missing per-iteration computational and communication cost analysis and tradeoffs in distributed settings versus ADMM and special cases.
- Weakness — empirical details: needs wall-clock time on multi-core/distributed hardware, problem scales, datasets, stopping criteria, accuracy metrics, and larger-scale experiments for scalability.
- Weakness — randomness/asynchrony: discuss or test combining PDMM with randomized block updates or asynchronous execution for practical deployment.
- Minor — terminology: use “Jacobi” not “Jacobian” for parallel block updates.
- Minor — clarity: move concise summary of how sADMM and PJADMM map to PDMM into the main text, not just the supplement.
- Minor — readability: include a short high-level proof sketch explaining why the dual backward step ensures descent/stability.
- Overall assessment: solid and timely contribution; dual backward step is simple and powerful; recommend acceptance after addressing intuition, parameterization, complexity, and empirical reporting.
