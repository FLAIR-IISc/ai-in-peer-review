This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method, named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The method generalizes the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and can handle nonsmooth functions. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size and demonstrate its effectiveness in two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its connections to existing ADMM variants. The theoretical results, including the convergence rate and iteration complexity, are well-presented and supported by rigorous proofs.

The proposed method has several advantages over existing ADMM variants. First, PDMM can handle multiple blocks, which is a common scenario in many machine learning problems. Second, PDMM can update the primal variable in parallel, which can significantly improve the computational efficiency. Third, PDMM can handle nonsmooth functions, which is a common challenge in many optimization problems.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in two applications. The results show that PDMM outperforms existing ADMM variants, including ADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM, in terms of convergence rate and computational efficiency.
