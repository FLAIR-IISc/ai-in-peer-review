This paper studies minimizing block-separable functions under linear constraints. It proposes a variant of ADMM (PDMM) for the multi-block setting: at each iteration it randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and applies a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal updates can be executed in parallel. The method is shown to have a sublinear convergence rate and it generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept it. My questions and comments are:

1) The algorithm description in (5) and (6) is not sufficiently clear. Do you update all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1}, or only a single block i? My understanding is that you update only one block of the dual variable y, but I could not find how the block i is selected. Please clarify this in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, I believe “\sum_{i=1}^I” is missing after “{” in your proof.

3) More discussion of Theorem 2 should be included. Theorem 2 (the main result) provides the convergence rate of PDMM. First, because PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, a natural question is whether Theorem 2 (with appropriate choices of parameters such as K and J) is consistent with the known convergence rates of those variants. Second, since you split the transformation matrix A into multiple blocks, what does your theorem suggest as the optimal way to split A?

4) I am curious about a comparison on the tensor completion problem (“Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data,” 2012), which also has a multi-block structure. Do you have any indication of which ADMM variant is optimal?

Minors:
1) In (9), a space is missing after “min”.
2) Line 217: remove the space before “Section”.

I am positive about accepting this paper. More discussion following Theorem 2 is expected.
