- Proposes Parallel ADMM (PADMM): randomly selects K of J blocks each iteration, updates chosen primal blocks, then updates duals via backward substitution.  
- Backward substitution yields a more conservative dual update, which is crucial for global convergence.  
- Adds a proximal term to the primal update to simplify solving many optimization subproblems.  
- Provides theoretical global convergence and iteration complexity: O(J / (T·K)) after T iterations.  
- Main advantages: fully parallelizable primal updates and allowance for relatively larger step sizes.  
- Can outperform sADMM due to larger allowable step sizes.  
- Offers greater flexibility than PJADMM and better parallelizability than GSADMM.  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising accuracy and reduced computation time.  
- Experiments did not report results from a parallel implementation (only serial timing reported).  
- Introduces three tuning parameters compared to standard ADMM, making configuration less convenient.  
- Maintains the same convergence rate as standard ADMM while enabling parallelization.  
- Paper is well-written with solid theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.
