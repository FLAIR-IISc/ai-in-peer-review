- Proposes PDMM to minimize block-separable convex functions with linear constraints using parallel (Jacobi-style) primal updates.  
- Contrasts with ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel updates and typical two-block limitation.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobi updates.  
- Dual backward step effectively reduces the dual step size and is key to achieving convergence under general conditions.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, similar to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms ADMM and variants, which is counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobi methods — requires more elaboration.  
- Offers a new alternative for distributed optimization with promising practical and theoretical properties.
