- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method with a dual backward step for multi-block convex optimization under linear constraints.  
- Establishes sublinear convergence and shows PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants.  
- Well-written, clearly presented, with solid motivation and appropriate related-work discussion.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness.  
- Recommends acceptance as a valuable contribution to optimization.  
- Major clarification needed: ambiguous description of dual updates in equations (5)–(6) — specify whether all coordinates or only the selected block i are updated and how i is chosen.  
- Typo in Theorem 2: missing summation “∑_{i=1}^I” before the brace.  
- Theorem 2 needs deeper discussion: compare PDMM’s convergence rate (for choices of K and J) to known rates of specialized ADMM variants.  
- Provide guidance on optimal strategies for splitting matrix A into blocks based on theoretical results.  
- Suggest discussing broader applicability, e.g., potential use and best ADMM variant for tensor completion under PDMM’s framework.
