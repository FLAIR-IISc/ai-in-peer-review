This paper proposes the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike the standard Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), which is typically limited to two blocks and updates primal variables sequentially (in a Gauss-Seidel fashion), PDMM updates multiple primal variable blocks in parallel (in a Jacobian fashion).

The key innovation is a "dual backward step," which compensates for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian-style updates by effectively reducing the dual update step size. The authors establish an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, which is comparable to that of standard ADMM. Furthermore, the supplementary material shows that two existing methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM. This provides a clearer understanding of the relationship between different decomposition methods based on the augmented Lagrangian.

Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM when compared with ADMM and its variants. This strong empirical performance is somewhat counter-intuitive, as Jacobian-type methods generally converge more slowly than their Gauss-Seidel counterparts. It would strengthen the paper if the authors could provide more intuition or analysis as to why PDMM does not suffer from this potential drawback.

The proposed dual backward step appears to be novel and is the key component that enables PDMM to achieve convergence guarantees under conditions as general as those for ADMM. Overall, the proposed method is a valuable contribution that provides a new and effective alternative for distributed optimization.
