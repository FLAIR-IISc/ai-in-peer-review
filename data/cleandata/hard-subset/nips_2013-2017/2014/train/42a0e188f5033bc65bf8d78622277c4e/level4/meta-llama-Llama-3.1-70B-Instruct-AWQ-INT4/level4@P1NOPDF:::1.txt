This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. It proposes a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to handle the multiple blocks case. Specifically, PDMM randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable. This process allows for parallel implementation of the primal variable updates. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method, which generalizes several variants of ADMM.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept it, pending the resolution of a few issues. My questions and comments are provided below:

1. The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) could be clarified. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated or only a single block "i". Based on my understanding, only a block of the dual variable "y" is updated, but I could not find any information on how to select "i". This should be clarified in the revision.

2. In the proof of Theorem 2, it appears that "\sum_{i=1}^I" is missing from the expression following the left curly bracket.

3. The authors should provide more discussion on Theorem 2, which presents the convergence rate of the proposed PDMM method. Firstly, since PDMM generalizes several variants of ADMM, it would be interesting to know whether Theorem 2 is consistent with the convergence rates of these variants when parameters like K and J are properly chosen. Secondly, given that the transformation matrix A is split into multiple blocks, it would be helpful to know the optimal way to split it according to the theorem.

4. I am curious to see a comparison with the tensor completion problem ("tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also has a multiple block structure. Do the authors have any insight into which variant of ADMM is optimal for this problem?

Minor issues:

1. In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".

2. In line 217, there is an unnecessary space before "Section".

Overall, I am positive about accepting this paper. However, I expect more discussion after Theorem 2 to provide further insights into the proposed method.
