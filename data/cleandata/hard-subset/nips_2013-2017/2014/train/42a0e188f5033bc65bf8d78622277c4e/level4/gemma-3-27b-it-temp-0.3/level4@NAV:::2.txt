This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions under linear constraints. Departing from the traditional alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), which employs a Gauss-Seidel update of primal variables and is typically restricted to two blocks, this work utilizes a Jacobian update scheme capable of handling multiple blocks concurrently. A key contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step, designed to address the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by adaptively controlling the dual variable step size. The authors establish convergence rates of O(1/T) for PDMM, mirroring those achieved by ADMM.

The supplementary materials demonstrate that existing methods, specifically sADMM and PJADMM, can be viewed as special cases of the proposed PDMM framework, providing valuable insight into the connections between various augmented Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. Empirical results confirm the effectiveness of PDMM, showing performance gains over ADMM and related algorithms. However, the observed results are somewhat unexpected, as Jacobian-based methods often exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel approaches; further discussion clarifying this point would be beneficial. The novel dual backward step appears crucial for ensuring convergence under broad conditions, comparable to ADMM, and represents a significant advancement. Ultimately, this work expands the toolkit for distributed optimization by offering a flexible and potentially advantageous alternative to existing methods.
