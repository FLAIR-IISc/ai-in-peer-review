- Proposes PDMM: a parallel direction method of multipliers for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel (sequential) updates, PDMM updates primal blocks in a Jacobian (parallel) manner supporting multiple blocks.  
- Introduces a dual backward step that adaptively reduces dual step size to mitigate limited information propagation from Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, matching ADMM’s rate.  
- Supplementary material shows prior methods (sADMM, PJADMM) are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results indicate PDMM outperforms ADMM and its variants in the tested settings.  
- Results appear counterintuitive because Jacobian-type methods are typically slower than Gauss–Seidel; further clarification is needed.  
- The dual backward step is a novel, ADMM-analogous contribution crucial for convergence under general conditions.  
- PDMM expands the set of alternatives for distributed optimization and decomposition methods.
