This paper addresses the minimization of block-separable functions under linear constraints. It proposes a multi-block ADMM variant, termed PDMM, which randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable to update and applies a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal updates can be executed in parallel. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the method, and PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is clearly written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept it. My questions and comments are as follows:
1) The algorithmic steps in (5) and (6) are not sufficiently clear. Are all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} updated, or only a single block i? My interpretation is that only one block of the dual variable y is updated, but I could not find how the index i is chosen. Please clarify this in the revision.
2) In Theorem 2, I believe “\sum_{i=1}^I” is missing immediately after the “{”, consistent with your proof.
3) Additional discussion around the main theorem would be helpful. Theorem 2 provides the convergence rate of PDMM. First, since PDMM subsumes several ADMM variants, can Theorem 2 (with appropriate choices of parameters such as K and J) recover the known convergence rates of those variants? Second, given that the transformation matrix A is partitioned into multiple blocks, does your theorem suggest an optimal or recommended way to split A?
4) I am interested in a comparison on the tensor completion task (“Tensor Completion for Estimating Missing Values in Visual Data,” 2012), which also has a multi-block structure. Do you have any insight into which ADMM variant is optimal for that setting?

Minors:
1) In (9), a space is missing after “min”.
2) Line 217: remove the space before “Section”.

I remain positive about accepting this paper. Further discussion following Theorem 2 would strengthen the work.
