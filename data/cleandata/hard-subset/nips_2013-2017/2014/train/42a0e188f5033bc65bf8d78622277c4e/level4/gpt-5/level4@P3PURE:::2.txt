This paper proposes the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) to minimize block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. In contrast to ADMM, which updates primal variables in a Gauss–Seidel manner and is typically limited to two blocks, this work updates multiple primal blocks in a Jacobi-style manner. The main contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step that compensates for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobi updates by effectively reducing the dual step size. Similar to ADMM, the paper establishes O(1/T) convergence results for PDMM.

The supplementary material shows that two previous methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed method, which clarifies the relationships among different augmented-Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM compared with ADMM and other variants. These results are somewhat counterintuitive, since Jacobi-type methods generally converge more slowly than Gauss–Seidel methods; additional elaboration on this point would strengthen the paper. The dual backward step in PDMM appears to be new and is key to obtaining convergence under general conditions, similar to ADMM. Overall, the proposed method offers additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.
