

This paper addresses the minimization of block-separable functions under linear constraints, proposing a novel ADMM variant termed PDMM for the multiple-block setting. PDMM randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for parallel updates and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable, establishing a sublinear convergence rate. The method generalizes several existing ADMM variants. The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and makes a solid contribution. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.

Specific comments follow:

1.  **Algorithm Clarity (Equations 5–6):** The description of the dual update (equations 5 and 6) requires clarification. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of $y^{t+1}$ and $\hat{y}^{t+1}$ are updated or only a single block $i$. The text suggests updating a single block of the dual variable, but the selection mechanism for block $i$ is not specified. This should be explicitly defined in the revision.

2.  **Theorem 2 Proof Typo:** In the proof of Theorem 2, the expression $\sum_{i=1}^I$ appears to be missing after the opening brace $\{$. This requires correction.

3.  **Theorem 2 Discussion:** Theorem 2 (the paper's main convergence result) warrants deeper discussion. First, since PDMM generalizes multiple ADMM variants, it is crucial to demonstrate that Theorem 2’s convergence rate (with appropriate parameter choices like $K$ and $J$) aligns with the known rates of those specific variants. Second, the paper partitions the constraint matrix $A$ into blocks; the theoretical implications for the optimal block-splitting strategy (e.g., minimizing the convergence rate constant) should be addressed based on Theorem 2.

4.  **Relevance to Tensor Completion:** The authors reference tensor completion (e.g., "tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012") as a problem with multiple-block structure. It would be valuable to discuss whether PDMM or any specific ADMM variant is particularly well-suited for this application, and if so, why.

**Minor Corrections:**
- Line 9: Add a space after "min".
- Line 217: Remove the space before "Section".

I strongly support acceptance of this paper. The core contribution is sound, the convergence analysis is rigorous, and the proposed method is well-motivated. The requested revisions are straightforward and will significantly enhance clarity, particularly regarding the algorithmic details (point 1) and the theoretical implications of Theorem 2 (point 3). The minor corrections (point 4) are easily addressed.
