- Proposes randomized Parallel ADMM (PADMM) for multi-block problems that picks K random blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks, then updates the dual via a backward step.  
- Backward dual step is crucial: makes dual update more conservative and enables global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term to primal updates to simplify subproblems.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis establishing global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Convergence rate shown as O(J/(T K)) when selecting K out of J blocks over T iterations.  
- Main advantages: enables full parallelization of primal step and permits relatively large step sizes.  
- Empirically can be faster than stochastic ADMM (due to larger step sizes), more flexible than PJADMM, and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show promising results and lower computational time to reach desired accuracy.  
- Missing evaluation of PADMM with an actual parallel implementation.  
- Practical drawback: tuning three PADMM parameters is less convenient than traditional ADMM.  
- Overall: interesting, well-written contribution with solid theoretical and empirical support; matches ADMM convergence rate while enabling parallelization.
