This paper studies minimizing block-separable objectives under linear constraints and proposes PDMM, a multi-block variant of ADMM. At each iteration, PDMM randomly selects K primal blocks to update in parallel and applies a backward (correction) step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate and show that PDMM encompasses several existing ADMM variants as special cases. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept. Below are my questions and comments.

1) Algorithm clarity (Eqs. (5) and (6)): The update rules for y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are ambiguous. Do you update all coordinates of the dual variables or only a single block i at each iteration? If only one dual block is updated, how is the block index i chosen (e.g., uniform random, importance sampling, tied to the selected primal blocks)? Please clarify the selection rule and whether multiple dual blocks can be updated per iteration.

2) Possible typo in Theorem 2 proof: It appears that “∑_{i=1}^I” is missing after a left brace “{” in the proof of Theorem 2. Please check and correct.

3) Discussion around Theorem 2:
   - Consistency with known variants: Since PDMM recovers several ADMM variants for particular parameter choices (e.g., K, J, step sizes), does Theorem 2 specialize to the known convergence rates of those methods? It would help to explicitly state how to set K, J, and other parameters to match prior algorithms and show that the rate bounds coincide (or explain any gaps).
   - Block partitioning of A: The theorem assumes a block partition of A. Does your analysis suggest a preferred or optimal way to split A (e.g., by sparsity pattern, conditioning, or balancing Lipschitz constants across blocks)? Any guidance or corollaries on how the block structure affects constants in the rate would be valuable.

4) Relation to tensor completion: For tensor completion problems (e.g., “tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data,” 2012), which naturally have a multi-block structure, do you have insights into which ADMM variant (including PDMM with specific K, J) is empirically or theoretically preferable? Any preliminary comparison or discussion would be useful.

Minor issues:
- In Eq. (9), add a space after “min”.
- Line 217: Remove the space before “Section”.

Overall, the contribution is solid and the exposition is clear. Strengthening the discussion after Theorem 2—especially regarding specialization to existing variants and guidance on block partitioning—would further improve the paper. I am positive about accepting this work.
