- Introduces PDMM to minimize block-separable convex objectives subject to linear constraints.  
- Unlike ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel two-block updates, PDMM updates multiple primal blocks in a Jacobi fashion.  
- Main contribution is a dual backward step that compensates limited information propagation by effectively shrinking the dual step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T)-type convergence guarantees similar to ADMM.  
- Supplementary material shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and its variants.  
- Findings are somewhat counterintuitive because Jacobi schemes are typically expected to converge more slowly than Gauss–Seidel; elaboration is needed.  
- The dual backward step appears novel and is central to ensuring convergence under general conditions.  
- Overall, PDMM expands the set of tools and options for distributed optimization.
