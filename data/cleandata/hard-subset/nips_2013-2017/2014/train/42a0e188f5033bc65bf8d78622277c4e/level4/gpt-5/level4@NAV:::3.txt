This paper introduces a randomized, parallel variant of ADMM that can handle multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM selects K blocks at random, updates the corresponding primal block variables, and then updates the dual variables via a backward step. The authors highlight that the backward step makes the dual update more conservative, which enables global convergence. The algorithm also permits adding a proximal term to the primal update, making certain subproblems easier to solve. The authors provide theoretical guarantees, establishing global convergence and iteration complexity. With J total blocks and K blocks chosen per iteration, the convergence rate is O(J/(TK)) after T iterations.

The main advantages of PADMM are: (1) it enables fully parallel primal updates and (2) it allows relatively large step sizes compared to other methods. In particular, PADMM can be faster than sADMM because it supports larger step sizes. It is more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM. The authors evaluate the method on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, with promising results: PADMM attains the target accuracy with less computation time. However, the paper does not report performance under an actual parallel implementation. Compared with standard ADMM, tuning the three PADMM parameters is less convenient.

Overall, this is an interesting contribution to ADMM-type problems with multiple constraints. The algorithm attains the same convergence rate as ADMM while enabling parallelization and showing promising empirical performance. Overall, this is an interesting contribution to ADMM-type problems. The paper is well written, and both the theoretical and empirical components are solid.
