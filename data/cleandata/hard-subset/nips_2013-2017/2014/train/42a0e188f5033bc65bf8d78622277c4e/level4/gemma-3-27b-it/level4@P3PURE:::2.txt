This paper proposes a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. Unlike ADMM, which updates primal variables in a Gauss-Seidel manner and is limited to two blocks, this work updates the primal blocks using a Jacobian approach with multiple blocks. The core contribution of the paper is the introduction of a dual backward step, which compensates for the limited information propagation inherent in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the step size for the dual variables. Similar to ADMM, the authors establish O(1/T) type convergence results for PDMM.

The paper demonstrates (in the supplementary materials) that existing methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed PDMM, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the relationships between different decomposition methods based on augmented Lagrangian techniques. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM when compared to ADMM and its variants. These results are somewhat counterintuitive, as Jacobian-type methods generally exhibit slower convergence than Gauss-Seidel methods. Further elaboration on this point would be beneficial. The dual backward step in PDMM appears novel and crucial for achieving convergence under general conditions comparable to ADMM. Ultimately, the proposed method offers increased flexibility and new possibilities for distributed optimization.
