- Introduces PDMM, a parallel direction method of multipliers for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Uses a Jacobian (parallel) update for primal variables, enabling simultaneous updates for multiple blocks (unlike ADMM’s two-block Gauss–Seidel).  
- Proposes a novel dual backward step to mitigate limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by adaptively controlling the dual step size.  
- Proves convergence of PDMM with an O(1/T) rate, matching ADMM’s theoretical rate.  
- Shows that existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Empirical results demonstrate PDMM outperforms ADMM and its variants in the reported experiments.  
- Notes the somewhat surprising faster convergence of Jacobian-based PDMM versus Gauss–Seidel ADMM and calls for further discussion on this point.  
- Emphasizes the dual backward step’s crucial role for convergence under general conditions, analogous to mechanisms in ADMM.  
- Concludes PDMM broadens the distributed optimization toolkit by providing increased flexibility and applicability.
