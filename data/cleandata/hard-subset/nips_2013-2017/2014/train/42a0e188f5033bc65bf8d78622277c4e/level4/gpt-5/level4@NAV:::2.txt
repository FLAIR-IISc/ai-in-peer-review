This paper introduces a parallel direction method of multipliers (PDMM) to minimize block-separable convex objectives subject to linear constraints. In contrast to ADMM, which updates the primal variables in a Gauss–Seidel manner and is typically limited to two blocks, this work updates multiple primal blocks in a Jacobi fashion. The main contribution is the introduction of a dual backward step, which compensates for the limited information propagation in Jacobi updates by effectively shrinking the dual step size. Similar to ADMM, the paper establishes O(1/T)-type convergence guarantees for PDMM.

The supplementary material shows that two prior methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of the proposed framework, thereby clarifying the relationships among augmented-Lagrangian-based decomposition methods. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM compared with ADMM and its variants. These findings are somewhat counterintuitive to me, since Jacobi-type schemes are generally expected to converge more slowly than Gauss–Seidel approaches; it would be helpful to elaborate on this point. The dual backward step in PDMM, which ensures convergence under general conditions akin to ADMM, appears to be novel and central. Overall, the proposed method expands the set of tools and options for distributed optimization.
