- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM for multi-block problems that updates K randomly selected blocks per iteration.  
- Each iteration performs block-wise primal updates followed by a backward (conservative) dual update; the backward step is crucial for global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term to primal updates to handle a wider class of optimization problems.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis establishing global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Convergence rate shown: O(J/(T K)) after T iterations when selecting K blocks out of J.  
- Key advantages: enables full parallelization of the primal step and permits relatively large stepsizes.  
- Empirically can outperform stochastic ADMM (sADMM) due to larger allowable stepsizes.  
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show promising accuracy with reduced computation time.  
- Did not report performance with an actual parallel implementation.  
- Downsides: same asymptotic convergence rate as ADMM and tuning is less convenient due to three algorithm parameters.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and well-written contribution with solid theoretical and empirical support for multi-constraint ADMM problems.
