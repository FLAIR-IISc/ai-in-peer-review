Paper tackles multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints and proposes PDMM: parallel updates of random block subsets plus a "dual backward step" and adaptive step-sizes based on constraint sparsity and block count.  
PDMM has rigorous O(1/T) convergence proofs and subsumes sADMM and PJADMM as special cases.  
Empirical validation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PDMM outperforms relevant baselines.

Strength — originality: introduces a novel, effective dual backward step to stabilize parallel ADMM-style updates.  
Strength — theory: provides global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity, covering exact and inexact updates via a Bregman framework.  
Strength — unification: clarifies that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases, offering a unifying perspective on Jacobian-style methods.  
Strength — experiments: strong empirical performance and practical reformulation for overlapping group lasso.

Weakness — intuition: paper should better explain why parallel PDMM can outperform sequential Gauss-Seidel methods in runtime.  
Weakness — parallel implementation: lacks discussion or experiments on real parallel speedups, communication costs, and bottlenecks.  
Weakness — choosing K: needs practical guidance on selecting the number of blocks K to update in parallel given problem/hardware trade-offs.

Evaluation — quality: high; technically sound with rigorous proofs.  
Evaluation — clarity: high; well-written and organized with clear motivation for the dual backward step.  
Evaluation — originality: high; PDMM and adaptive step-sizing are novel contributions.  
Evaluation — significance: high; a practical, parallelizable method likely useful to the community.

Pro-acceptance: addresses an important problem with a novel, convergent parallel method; strong theoretical guarantees; unifies prior Jacobian ADMM variants; demonstrates superior empirical performance.  
Con-acceptance: needs more intuition comparing Jacobian vs Gauss-Seidel and lacks experiments showing real parallel speedups.  
Overall recommendation: accept.
