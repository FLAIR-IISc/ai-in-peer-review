

This paper introduces a randomized parallel ADMM variant, PADMM, designed for problems with multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM randomly selects K blocks, updates the corresponding primal vectors, and then performs a dual update via a backward step. The authors demonstrate that this backward step ensures a more conservative dual update, which is crucial for establishing global convergence. The algorithm also incorporates a proximal term in the primal update, simplifying the solution of certain subproblems. Theoretical analysis confirms global convergence and establishes an iteration complexity of O(J/(TK)) after T iterations when there are J total blocks and K blocks are selected per iteration. The primary advantages of PADMM are (1) full parallelization of the primal step and (2) the ability to use larger step sizes compared to other methods, leading to potential speedups over sADMM and greater flexibility than PJADMM, while offering superior parallelism relative to GSADMM. Experimental evaluation on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso shows promising results, with PADMM achieving target accuracy more efficiently in terms of computation time. However, the authors do not report performance metrics for the parallel implementation, and PADMM requires tuning three parameters, which may be less convenient than traditional ADMM. Overall, this is a valuable contribution to solving multi-constraint ADMM-type problems. The algorithm maintains ADMM's convergence rate while enabling parallelization and demonstrating strong empirical performance. The paper is well-written, with robust theoretical and experimental components.
