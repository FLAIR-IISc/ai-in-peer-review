- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that updates K randomly chosen primal blocks per iteration and then updates the dual with a backward step.  
- Backward dual step motivated as necessary for conservative dual updates and to guarantee global convergence.  
- Incorporates Bregman divergence to allow flexible and potentially more efficient primal updates.  
- Theoretical contributions include global convergence and an O(1/T) iteration complexity (T iterations, K selected blocks).  
- Strength: enables parallelization of the primal step for potential large-scale speedups.  
- Strength: permits larger step sizes than some alternatives, potentially outperforming sADMM.  
- Strength: offers more flexibility than PJADMM and avoids GSADMM’s sequential updates.  
- Empirical validation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising computational efficiency.  
- Weakness: experiments were run sequentially, so actual parallel speedup is not demonstrated.  
- Weakness: introduces three tunable parameters (step size/backward step related), increasing tuning burden and needing practical selection guidance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel multi-block ADMM extension, rigorous theory, supportive experiments, potential large-scale impact, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of genuine parallel implementation results and added parameter tuning complexity.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, minor weaknesses outweighed by strengths—recommend acceptance.
