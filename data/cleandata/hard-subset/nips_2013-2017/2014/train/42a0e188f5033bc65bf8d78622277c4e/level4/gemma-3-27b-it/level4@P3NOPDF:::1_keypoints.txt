- Proposes PDMM, a randomized-block variant of ADMM that updates a subset K of primal blocks per iteration and refines the dual with a backward step.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and logically organized.  
- PDMM is novel and promising for scaling ADMM to many-block problems via random block selection.  
- Theoretical analysis establishes sublinear convergence.  
- PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants, broadening applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Clarify algorithmic details in Equations (5)–(6): whether dual updates y^{t+1} and ŷ^{t+1} act on all dual coordinates or only those of the selected block, and explicitly state how block i is selected.  
- Typo in Theorem 2 proof: missing summation symbol (should be ∑_{i=1}^I {…}).  
- Expand discussion of Theorem 2: show that PDMM’s convergence rate reduces to known rates for existing ADMM variants under specific parameter choices (e.g., K, J).  
- Provide guidance on optimal partitioning of the transformation matrix A into blocks and whether certain block structures improve convergence.  
- Suggest empirical comparison on tensor completion (a natural multi-block problem) and discussion of which ADMM variant (including PDMM) is expected to perform best.  
- Minor formatting issues: space missing after "min" in Eq. (9); remove extra space before “Section” on line 217.
