- Introduces PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant for multi-block optimization.  
- Each iteration randomly selects K blocks, updates the primal variables for those blocks, then performs a backward dual update.  
- The backward dual step yields a more conservative dual update and is key to guaranteeing global convergence.  
- Allows an optional proximal term in the primal update to simplify certain subproblem solutions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis establishing global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Shows convergence rate O(J/(T K)) for J total blocks after T iterations.  
- Main strengths: enables full parallelization of the primal update and permits a larger stepsize than competing methods.  
- Positions PADMM favorably versus sADMM (larger stepsize), PJADMM (more flexible), and GSADMM (more parallelizable).  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrate faster attainment of target accuracy.  
- Limitation: lacks results from an actual parallel implementation.  
- Minor drawback: tuning three PADMM hyperparameters is more involved than tuning ADMM's single parameter.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, well-written contribution with solid theory and promising practical performance, matching ADMM convergence while adding parallelism.
