This paper tackles minimization of block-separable (possibly nonsmooth) convex objectives under linear constraints. It introduces the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), a multi-block ADMM-style method that at each iteration randomly selects K primal blocks to update in parallel and applies a backward (conservative) step to the dual update. The authors establish global convergence with an O(1/T) ergodic rate under constant stepsizes, show how PDMM can handle overlapping blocks, and position it as a unifying framework encompassing several ADMM variants.

The paper is clearly written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept. My questions and comments are below:

1) Algorithmic clarity (Eqs. (5)–(7)): Please clarify whether all coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated, or only a single dual block i. My reading is that only one dual block is updated, but I do not see how i is selected. This point should be made explicit in the revision.

2) Theorem 2: It appears that “\sum_{i=1}^I” is missing immediately after the opening “{” in the bound, consistent with what is used in the proof.

3) Discussion around Theorem 2 (the central result) would benefit from expansion:
   - Consistency with known variants: Since PDMM recovers several ADMM variants via parameter choices (e.g., K, J, τ_i, ν_i), does Theorem 2 specialize to the standard convergence rates for those methods when instantiated accordingly?
   - Block partitioning: Given that A is split into multiple blocks, does your analysis suggest guidance on how to partition A to optimize convergence (e.g., via di, sparsity patterns), and if so, what is the recommended strategy?

4) Application domain: For tensor completion (e.g., “tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data,” 2012), which also has a natural multi-block structure, do you have insight into which ADMM variant (within the PDMM framework) would be preferable in practice?

Minor comments:
1) In (9), there is a missing space after “min”.
2) Line 217: remove the space before “Section”.

Overall, I recommend acceptance. Additional discussion following Theorem 2 would further strengthen the paper.
