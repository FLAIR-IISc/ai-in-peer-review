- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobian-style) update method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step to control dual updates and mitigate Jacobian information propagation issues.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity, comparable to ADMM.  
- Positions PDMM within augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods and unifies existing methods.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM (supplementary material).  
- Experimental results demonstrate PDMM outperforms ADMM and some Gauss–Seidel–type methods.  
- The observed performance advantage over Gauss–Seidel is counterintuitive and needs more explanation.  
- Paper is technically sound; theory and experiments support the claims.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized; notation is consistent and the dual backward step is well explained.  
- Original contribution: PDMM algorithm and dual backward step; unification of prior methods increases novelty.  
- Significance: addresses a key multi-block ADMM limitation and could impact large-scale distributed optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/theoretically sound algorithm, strong experiments, clear writing, and unification of prior methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient explanation for counterintuitive empirical results and limited experimental diversity.
