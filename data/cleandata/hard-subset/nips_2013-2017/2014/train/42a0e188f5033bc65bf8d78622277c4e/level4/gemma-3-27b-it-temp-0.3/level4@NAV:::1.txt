This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions under linear constraints. The authors propose a novel variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), termed PDMM, specifically designed for problems with multiple blocks. PDMM introduces a randomized block selection strategy – updating *K* blocks of the primal variable in each iteration – coupled with a backward step for dual variable refinement, enabling parallel implementation of the primal update. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrate its generalization of several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized. I recommend acceptance. However, I have the following questions and suggestions for improvement:

1) The algorithm description presented in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of *y<sup>t+1</sup>* and *ŷ<sup>t+1</sup>* are updated, or only those corresponding to the selected block *i*. While my understanding is that only a block of the dual variable *y* is updated, the method for selecting *i* is not explicitly stated and should be clarified in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, the summation symbol appears to be incomplete; I believe a “\sum<sub>i=1</sub><sup>I</sup>” term is missing within the curly braces of the equation.

3) The discussion following Theorem 2 should be expanded. As the main result of this paper, Theorem 2 establishes the convergence rate of PDMM. Given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, it would be valuable to discuss whether the convergence rate achieved by PDMM (with appropriate parameter selection for *K* and *J*) is consistent with the known convergence rates of those existing variants. Furthermore, considering the block structure arises from a decomposition of the transformation matrix *A*, exploring the optimal strategy for this decomposition based on the theoretical results would be beneficial.

4) I am interested in a comparison of PDMM with existing ADMM variants on the tensor completion problem (as described in “tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012”), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. Do the authors have any insights into which ADMM variant might perform optimally in this context?

Minor comments:

1) Equation (9) is missing a space after the “min” operator.
2) On line 217, please remove the space preceding “Section”. 

I reiterate my positive assessment and recommend acceptance, contingent upon addressing the points raised, particularly expanding the discussion following Theorem 2.
