- Proposes PDMM, a Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step that enables convergence of Jacobian-style updates for multiple blocks.  
- Updates multiple blocks in parallel via randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying framework.  
- Theoretical analysis accounts for constraint sparsity and number of updated blocks and is technically sound.  
- Extends applicability to overlapping block problems where prior methods struggled.  
- Experiments (robust PCA and overlapping group lasso) show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art in runtime, with PDMM3 and PDMM1 particularly strong.  
- Strengths: comprehensive theory, clear connections to existing methods, thorough experiments, and good reproducibility.  
- Weaknesses: counterintuitive experimental result that Jacobian PDMM outperforms Gauss–Seidel GSADMM needs more explanation.  
- Suggests stronger intuition and discussion around the dual backward step would improve the paper.  
- Overall: technically solid, well-written, addresses an important gap, and has strong potential impact in distributed optimization.
