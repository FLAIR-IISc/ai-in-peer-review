- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Main contribution: a parallel randomized block-coordinate method that handles multiple blocks and allows full parallelization of the primal step with relatively large step sizes.  
- Relates PDMM to prior work, showing it generalizes ADMM and unifies sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Highlights flexibility in choosing how many blocks to update per iteration, enabling parallelization.  
- Theoretical results establish global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size, matching ADMM.  
- Provides analysis of algorithm behavior, emphasizing the backward dual step and proximal term in primal updates.  
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show faster and more accurate convergence than sADMM and GSADMM.  
- Does not report performance of a parallel implementation, omitting an important practical evaluation.  
- Algorithm requires tuning three parameters, which may be inconvenient for users.  
- Novelty: combines ADMM and randomized block-coordinate descent, offering a new perspective on parallelization for ADMM-type problems.  
- Significance: has broad potential applicability in machine learning and optimization due to multi-block handling and update flexibility.  
- Clarity and quality: well-written paper with thorough theoretical and convincing empirical results.  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS; suggest reporting parallel implementation performance in future work.
