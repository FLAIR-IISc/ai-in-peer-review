This paper proposes a randomized, parallel variant of ADMM (PADMM) that can handle optimization problems with multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM selects K random blocks, updates the corresponding primal variables in parallel, and then performs a backward step to update the dual variable. The backward step makes the dual update more conservative, which is key to ensuring global convergence. The algorithm also supports adding a proximal term to the primal updates, simplifying certain subproblems.

The authors provide theoretical guarantees, establishing global convergence and iteration complexity. With J total blocks and K blocks updated per iteration, the convergence rate is O(J/(T K)) after T iterations.

The main advantages of PADMM are: (1) full parallelization of the primal step and (2) the ability to use relatively large step sizes compared with other methods. In particular, PADMM can be faster than sADMM because it permits larger step sizes; it is more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM. The experimental evaluation on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso is promising: PADMM reaches the desired accuracy with less computation time.

That said, the paper does not report performance results for a truly parallel implementation of PADMM, which would be important to substantiate the claimed parallel speedups. In addition, compared with traditional ADMM, PADMM requires tuning three parameters, which is less convenient in practice.

Overall, this is an interesting and solid contribution to ADMM-type problems with multiple constraints/blocks. The algorithm achieves the same convergence rate as ADMM while enabling parallelization and demonstrating strong practical performance. The paper is well written, and both the theoretical and empirical components are sound.
