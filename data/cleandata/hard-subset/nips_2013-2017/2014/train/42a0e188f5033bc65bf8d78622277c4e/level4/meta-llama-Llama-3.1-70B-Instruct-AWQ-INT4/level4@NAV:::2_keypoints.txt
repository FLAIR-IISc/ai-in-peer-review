- Proposes PDMM: a parallel (Jacobian) update method for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Differs from ADMM by updating multiple primal blocks in parallel instead of Gauss–Seidel two-block updates.  
- Main contribution is a dual backward step that reduces effective dual step size to mitigate limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Dual backward step enables O(1/T)-type convergence results comparable to ADMM.  
- Supplementary material shows PDMM generalizes sADMM and PJADMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms ADMM variants, though results seem counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods.  
- The dual backward step is novel and appears crucial for ensuring convergence under general conditions.  
- Overall, PDMM provides a useful alternative for distributed optimization.
