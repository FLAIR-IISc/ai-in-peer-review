

This paper addresses the minimization of block-separable functions under linear constraints. It proposes a variant of ADMM (PDMM) for the multiple-block case, which randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal update process is parallelizable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for PDMM, which generalizes several existing ADMM variants. The paper is well-written and accessible. I recommend acceptance, with the following questions and comments requiring clarification:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of $y^{t+1}$ and $\hat{y}^{t+1}$ are updated or only a single block "i". The text suggests updating a block of the dual variable "y" but does not specify how "i" is selected. This selection mechanism must be explicitly defined in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, the proof appears to be missing the summation symbol $\sum_{i=1}^I$ after the opening brace "{".

3) Theorem 2 (the paper's main result) warrants deeper discussion. First, since PDMM generalizes multiple ADMM variants, it is essential to verify whether Theorem 2 (with appropriate parameter choices like K, J) recovers the known convergence rates of those variants. Second, as the method splits the transformation matrix A into multiple blocks, the optimal block-splitting strategy derived from Theorem 2 should be addressed.

4) The authors reference tensor completion for visual data (2012), which also exhibits multiple-block structure. It would be valuable to discuss whether the proposed PDMM or any specific ADMM variant is optimal for this problem.

Minors:
1) Equation (9): Add a space after "min".
2) Line 217: Remove the space before "Section". 

I recommend acceptance, with the expectation that the discussion following Theorem 2 is expanded as outlined.
