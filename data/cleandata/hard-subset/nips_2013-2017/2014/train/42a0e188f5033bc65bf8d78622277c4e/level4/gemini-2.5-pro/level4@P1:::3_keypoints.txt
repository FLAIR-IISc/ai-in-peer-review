- Introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel variant of ADMM for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Iteratively selects a random subset of K blocks, updates those primal variables in parallel, then updates dual variables.  
- Incorporates a "backward step" in the dual update that makes it conservative and is crucial for global convergence.  
- Allows inexact primal updates using Bregman divergence terms to simplify subproblems.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(J/(T K)) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Designed for parallel computation: primal updates are fully parallelizable and supports larger dual step sizes than some competitors.  
- Positions PDMM favorably versus related work: more parallelizable than GSADMM and more flexible than PJADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods in computation time.  
- Major weakness: experiments were run sequentially; no validation on true parallel hardware to demonstrate real-world speedups.  
- Practical concern: additional step-size parameters (τi, νi) may complicate tuning for new problems.  
- Overall assessment: a valuable, well-written contribution with solid theory and promising empirical performance.
