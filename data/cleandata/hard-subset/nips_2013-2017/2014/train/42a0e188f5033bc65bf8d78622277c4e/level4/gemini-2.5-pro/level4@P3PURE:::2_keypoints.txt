- Proposes PDMM to minimize block-separable convex functions with linear constraints via parallel (Jacobian) primal updates.  
- Contrasts PDMM with standard ADMM, which typically uses two blocks and sequential (Gauss-Seidel) updates.  
- Introduces a novel "dual backward step" that compensates limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Proves an O(1/T) convergence rate for PDMM, comparable to standard ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Empirical results demonstrate PDMM outperforms ADMM variants, despite the expectation that Jacobian methods converge slower.  
- Requests more intuition or analysis explaining why PDMM avoids the typical slowdown of Jacobian-type methods.  
- Asserts the dual backward step is the key novel component enabling convergence under conditions as general as ADMM’s.  
- Concludes PDMM is a valuable, effective alternative for distributed optimization.
