

This paper proposes a randomized parallel ADMM (PADMM) algorithm designed for problems with multiple blocks. In each iteration, PADMM selects K random blocks, updates the corresponding primal variables, and then updates the dual variables via a backward step. The authors demonstrate that the backward step ensures a more conservative dual update, which is crucial for establishing global convergence. The algorithm also permits incorporating a proximal term into the primal update, facilitating the solution of certain subproblems. Theoretical analysis establishes global convergence and an iteration complexity of $O(J/(TK))$ after T iterations, where J denotes the total number of blocks and K is the number of blocks selected per iteration.  

The primary advantages of PADMM are (1) full parallelization of the primal step and (2) the ability to use relatively larger step sizes compared to other methods. Specifically, PADMM can outperform sADMM due to its larger permissible step sizes, offers greater flexibility than PJADMM, and achieves higher parallelism than GSADMM. The authors evaluate PADMM on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, demonstrating promising empirical results where PADMM attains target accuracy with reduced computational time. However, the paper does not report performance metrics for PADMM under parallel implementation. Additionally, tuning the three parameters of PADMM is less straightforward than for traditional ADMM.  

Overall, this work presents a valuable contribution to solving multi-constraint ADMM-type problems. The algorithm achieves the same convergence rate as standard ADMM while enabling parallelization and exhibiting strong empirical performance. The paper is well-written, with a solid theoretical foundation and compelling experimental validation.
