Summary and contributions:
The paper studies minimizing block-separable functions under linear constraints and proposes a multi-block ADMM variant, PDMM. At each iteration, PDMM randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for parallel updates and applies a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate and show that PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

I am positive about accepting this paper.

Major comments and questions:
1) Algorithm clarity (eqs. (5) and (6)): The update rules for y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are not sufficiently clear. Do you update all coordinates of the dual variables, or only a single block i? My understanding is that only one block of y is updated, but the selection rule for i is not specified. Please clarify the intended update (full vs. block, and if block, how i is chosen).

2) Potential typo in Theorem 2: In the proof of Theorem 2, it appears that “\sum_{i=1}^I” is missing immediately after “{”. Please check and correct this.

3) Discussion of Theorem 2:
   - Consistency with known variants: Since PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, does Theorem 2 recover the known convergence rates of those variants under appropriate parameter choices (e.g., K, J)? Please make the correspondence explicit.
   - Block partition of A: Given that A is split into multiple blocks, does your analysis suggest an optimal or recommended way to partition A to improve the constants or practical convergence (e.g., via conditioning, sparsity structure, or block coupling)? Any guidance derived from the theorem would be valuable.

4) Application to tensor completion: I am curious about comparisons on the tensor completion problem (“Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data,” 2012), which also has a multi-block structure. Do you have insight into which ADMM variant (including PDMM) is likely to be optimal for this task, in theory or practice?

Minor issues:
- In (9), a space is missing after “min”.
- Line 217: remove the space before “Section”.

Overall assessment:
The paper is well written, proposes a parallelizable multi-block ADMM method with a sublinear rate, and unifies several variants. I am positive to accept this paper. I would especially appreciate expanded discussion following Theorem 2 to address the consistency with known variants and guidance on block partitioning.
