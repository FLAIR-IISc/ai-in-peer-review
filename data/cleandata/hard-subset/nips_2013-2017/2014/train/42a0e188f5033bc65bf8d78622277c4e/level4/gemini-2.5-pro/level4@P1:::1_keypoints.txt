- Proposes PDMM, a new ADMM variant for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Core idea: randomly select K primal blocks for parallel update, then apply a backward step to refine the dual variable.  
- Authors prove a sublinear convergence rate and show PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and recommended for acceptance.  
- Clarify dual update notation in (6)–(7): it’s ambiguous whether index i means a single dual block is updated or all dual blocks are updated in parallel, and how block i is selected if single-block.  
- Check Theorem 2: a summation term (∑_{i=1}^I) appears to be missing in the first inequality—verify against the appendix derivation.  
- Expand discussion of Theorem 2’s implications: show how its convergence rate reduces to known rates for special-case ADMM settings (e.g., specific K, J).  
- Discuss practical guidance from the theorem on choosing/partitioning matrix A into blocks to speed convergence.  
- Evaluate PDMM on other natural multi-block problems (e.g., tensor completion) to empirically compare variants and demonstrate strengths.  
- Minor fixes: add space after "argmin" in (9); remove unnecessary space before "Section" on line 217.  
- Overall: strong contribution; addressing the above, especially a deeper discussion of Theorem 2, would strengthen the manuscript.
