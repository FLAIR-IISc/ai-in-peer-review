- Introduces PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant that updates K randomly selected blocks in parallel per iteration.  
- Integrates a dual backward step to make dual updates more conservative, which is crucial for convergence.  
- Allows proximal terms in primal updates for flexibility with different problem structures.  
- Proves global convergence and O(J/(T K)) iteration complexity with constant step size (J = total blocks, T = iterations).  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying theoretical perspective.  
- Derives parameter choices (τi, νi) that can be optimized based on constraint-matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, outperforming sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM.  
- Achieves faster convergence and reduced runtime in experiments, with parallelism benefits when K is chosen appropriately.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-structured, addressing a gap in multi-block ADMM literature.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear presentation of theory and experimental metrics.  
- Novelty lies in combining randomized parallel block updates with the dual backward step to enable larger step sizes and convergence.  
- Significance: addresses scalability and convergence for multi-block constrained optimization relevant to large-scale ML.  
- Strengths: theoretical guarantees, unifying view of related methods, practical performance, and parallelization potential.  
- Weaknesses: lacks reported parallel implementation results to quantify speedup.  
- Weaknesses: parameter tuning guidance (τi, νi) is insufficiently detailed for practitioners.  
- Weaknesses: theoretical trade-offs between convergence rate and computational cost when varying K are not fully explored.  
- Weaknesses: experiments could include more diagnostics (e.g., convergence plots for overlapping group lasso) and guidance on when PDMM is most advantageous.
