- Proposes PDMM: a parallel direction method of multipliers for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.
- PDMM uses Jacobian-style parallel updates across multiple blocks, unlike ADMM’s Gauss-Seidel (two-block) updates.
- Introduces a novel dual backward step that adaptively adjusts the dual step size to address restricted information flow in Jacobian updates.
- The dual backward step is essential for establishing convergence without restrictive assumptions and distinguishes PDMM from existing parallel methods.
- Proves convergence guarantees for PDMM with O(1/T) rates, analogous to ADMM.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying their relationships within augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods.
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM outperforms ADMM and other variants on tested problems.
- Notes a counterintuitive result: PDMM’s Jacobian approach is empirically efficient despite typical slower convergence of Jacobian vs Gauss-Seidel methods.
- Requests that the authors explain why this discrepancy arises, specifically the role of the dual backward step in overcoming standard Jacobian limitations.
- Recommends adding discussion of practical implementation issues, e.g., communication overhead in distributed settings.
- Minor writing/phrasing improvements suggested; overall the theory and experiments are sound and the paper makes a meaningful contribution.
