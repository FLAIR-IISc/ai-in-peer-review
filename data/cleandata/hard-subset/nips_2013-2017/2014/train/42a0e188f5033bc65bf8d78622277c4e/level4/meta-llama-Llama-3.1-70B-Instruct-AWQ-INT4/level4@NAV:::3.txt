This paper introduces a randomized parallel variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), denoted as PADMM, which effectively handles multiple block constraints. In each iteration, PADMM randomly selects K blocks, updates the block primal vector, and then refines the dual vector through a backward step. The incorporation of a backward step is crucial, as it renders the dual update more conservative, thereby ensuring global convergence. Moreover, the algorithm allows for the addition of a proximal term to the primal update, facilitating the solution of certain optimization problems. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis, establishing the global convergence and iteration complexity of PADMM. Specifically, when dealing with J blocks and randomly selecting K blocks per iteration, the algorithm's convergence rate is O(J/(TK)) after T iterations.

The primary advantages of PADMM lie in its ability to (1) facilitate full parallelization of the primal step and (2) accommodate relatively large update step sizes compared to other methods. Notably, PADMM can outperform sADMM by allowing larger step sizes, while offering greater flexibility than PJADMM and higher parallelizability than GSADMM. The authors evaluate the algorithm on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, yielding promising experimental results. PADMM achieves the desired accuracy with reduced computational time. However, the authors have not reported the performance of PADMM with parallel implementation. Compared to traditional ADMM, PADMM's three parameters can be less convenient to tune.

Overall, this contribution offers an interesting solution to ADMM-type problems with multiple constraints, achieving the same convergence rate as ADMM while enabling parallelization and exhibiting promising practical performance. The paper is well-written, with both theoretical and empirical components being solid.
