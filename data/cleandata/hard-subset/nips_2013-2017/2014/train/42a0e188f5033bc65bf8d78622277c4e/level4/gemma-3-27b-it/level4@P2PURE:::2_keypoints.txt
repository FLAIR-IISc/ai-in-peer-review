- Proposes PDMM: a parallel direction method of multipliers for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Replaces ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel (two-block) primal update with a Jacobian (parallel/multi-block) primal update.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step to compensate for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by controlling dual step sizes.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence for PDMM, matching ADMM’s convergence rate.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM (demonstrated in supplementary material).  
- Empirical results indicate PDMM outperforms or is competitive with ADMM and its variants.  
- Performance is somewhat surprising since Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel; further explanation would help.  
- Dual backward step appears crucial and novel for attaining ADMM-like convergence under general conditions.  
- Overall, PDMM broadens the distributed optimization toolkit by enabling flexible multi-block parallel updates.
