This paper proposes a randomized parallel version of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), designed to handle problems with multiple block variables. In each iteration, PADMM randomly selects K blocks, updates the corresponding primal variables in parallel, and subsequently updates the dual variable via a backward step. The authors demonstrate the importance of this backward step, which makes the dual update more conservative and thereby enables global convergence. The algorithm also allows for the inclusion of a proximal term in the primal update, which can simplify the resulting subproblems.

The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, establishing its global convergence and iteration complexity. For a problem with J total blocks where K blocks are updated per iteration, the convergence rate is shown to be O(J/(TK)) after T iterations.

The main advantages of PADMM are (1) its capacity for full parallelization of the primal update step and (2) its tolerance for relatively large step sizes compared to competing methods. Specifically, PADMM can be faster than sADMM due to this larger step size, is more flexible than PJADMM, and is more amenable to parallelization than GSADMM. The authors evaluate the algorithm on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, and the experimental results are promising, showing that PADMM achieves the desired accuracy in less computation time.

Nevertheless, the authors have not reported the performance of PADMM with a truly parallel implementation. Additionally, compared to traditional ADMM, tuning the three parameters of PADMM may be less convenient.

Overall, this paper presents an interesting contribution for solving ADMM-type problems with multiple constraints. The algorithm has the same convergence rate as standard ADMM but introduces the key benefits of parallelization and exhibits strong practical performance. The paper is well-written, with solid theoretical and empirical components.
