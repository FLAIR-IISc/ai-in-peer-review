This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints. The authors propose a new variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), specifically designed for problems with multiple blocks. The core idea of PDMM is to randomly select K blocks of the primal variable for a parallel update, followed by a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and show that it generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. I recommend this paper for acceptance, though I have a few questions and suggestions for improvement that I believe would strengthen the manuscript.

My questions and comments are listed below:

1.  The description of the dual variable update in equations (6) and (7) could be clarified. The equations are indexed by 'i', which suggests that only a single block of the dual variable 'y' is updated per iteration. If this is the case, it is not specified how this block 'i' is selected. If, however, all blocks of y are updated in parallel (as a later sentence suggests), the notation could be made more explicit to avoid this ambiguity.

2.  In the statement of Theorem 2, it appears that a summation term, `\sum_{i=1}^I`, might be missing after the opening brace `{` in the first inequality. Please double-check this against the derivation in the appendix.

3.  The paper would be significantly strengthened by an expanded discussion of the implications of Theorem 2, which is a main result. Two points in particular would be valuable to address:
    a) Since PDMM is presented as a generalization of several ADMM variants, it would be insightful to show how the convergence rate in Theorem 2 recovers or relates to the known rates of these specialized methods when the parameters (e.g., K, J) are set accordingly.
    b) The theorem's dependence on the block structure of matrix A raises a practical question: does the theoretical result provide any guidance on how to optimally partition A into blocks to achieve the fastest convergence?

4.  Have the authors considered evaluating PDMM on other problems with a natural multi-block structure, such as tensor completion (e.g., "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data," 2012)? An empirical comparison on such a problem could further highlight the strengths of the proposed method and provide insight into which ADMM variant might be best suited for that setting.

**Minor Comments:**

*   In equation (9), a space is needed after `argmin`.
*   On line 217, there is an unnecessary space before "Section".

In summary, this is a strong paper introducing a promising new algorithm. Addressing the points above, particularly the request for a more detailed discussion surrounding Theorem 2, would make the contribution even more impactful.
