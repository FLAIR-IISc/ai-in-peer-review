

This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints, proposing a parallel randomized block coordinate method called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) for multi-block linear constraints. PDMM randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for parallel updates and employs a backward step for dual variable refinement, enabling efficient parallel implementation. The authors establish global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size, demonstrating that it generalizes several existing ADMM variants. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a compelling theoretical contribution that extends the applicability of ADMM to multiple blocks.

I am positive about accepting this paper. The following questions and comments should be addressed in the revision:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) requires clarification. It is unclear whether all dual blocks (y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1}) are updated or only a single block "i" is updated. The selection mechanism for "i" should be explicitly defined.

2) In Theorem 2, the expression "{\sum_{i=1}^I" appears to be missing the summation index, which should be added for mathematical correctness.

3) A more comprehensive discussion of Theorem 2 is needed. Specifically, the relationship between PDMM's convergence rate and those of existing ADMM variants should be explicitly established through parameter selection (e.g., K, J). Additionally, the optimal block partitioning strategy for the constraint matrix A should be discussed in the context of the theoretical results.

4) The paper would benefit from a comparison with ADMM variants applied to tensor completion problems, which also exhibit multiple-block structures. This would provide valuable context for understanding the relative advantages of PDMM in different problem settings.
