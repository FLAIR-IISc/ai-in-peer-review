- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that selects K random blocks per iteration, updates primal blocks in parallel, then performs a backward (conservative) dual update.  
- Backward dual step is crucial for global convergence by making the dual update more conservative.  
- Allows adding a proximal term in the primal update to simplify solving subproblems.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis showing global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Convergence rate shown as O(J/(T K)) after T iterations when J total blocks and K selected per iteration.  
- Key advantages: enables full parallelization of the primal step and permits relatively large step sizes.  
- Can be faster than stochastic ADMM (sADMM) due to accommodating larger step sizes.  
- More flexible than parallel Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM) and more parallelizable than Gauss–Seidel ADMM (GSADMM).  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising results and reduced computation time to reach desired accuracy.  
- Did not report actual performance with a parallel implementation.  
- PADMM has more tuning complexity than traditional ADMM due to three parameters.  
- Overall assessment: interesting contribution, matches ADMM convergence rate while enabling parallelization; paper is well-written with solid theory and experiments.
