This work addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions that are subject to linear constraints. To this end, the paper introduces a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM), termed PDMM, which is designed for the multi-block setting. The proposed algorithm randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for updating, which can be performed in parallel, and then employs a backward step to refine the dual variable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate for the method and show that it generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The manuscript is clearly written and the presentation is easy to follow. I am supportive of this paper's acceptance. My questions and suggestions for improvement are detailed below:

1) The algorithmic description provided in equations (5) and (6) lacks sufficient clarity. It is ambiguous whether the updates to y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are applied to the full vectors or only to a single block "i". My interpretation is that only a single block of the dual variable "y" is modified, but the paper does not specify how the index "i" is selected. This point requires clarification in the revised manuscript.

2) In the statement of Theorem 2, I believe the summation term "\sum_{i=1}^I" is missing following the opening brace "{". This seems to be an omission based on the provided proof.

3) The paper would be significantly strengthened by an expanded discussion of the implications of Theorem 2, the main theoretical result.
    *   First, since PDMM is presented as a generalization of other ADMM variants, a natural question arises: does the convergence rate in Theorem 2 recover the known rates of these specialized methods when the parameters (such as K and J) are chosen appropriately? Demonstrating this consistency would be valuable.
    *   Second, the analysis relies on the partitioning of the transformation matrix A into blocks. Does the theorem offer any guidance on how to optimally partition A to achieve the fastest convergence?

4) I am curious about a potential comparison with the tensor completion problem (e.g., "Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data," 2012), which also features a multi-block structure. Do the authors have any intuition as to which ADMM variant might be optimal for such an application?

**Minor Points:**
1) In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".
2) On line 217, the space before "Section" should be removed.

I recommend this paper for acceptance, and I look forward to seeing a revised version that includes a more thorough discussion following Theorem 2.
