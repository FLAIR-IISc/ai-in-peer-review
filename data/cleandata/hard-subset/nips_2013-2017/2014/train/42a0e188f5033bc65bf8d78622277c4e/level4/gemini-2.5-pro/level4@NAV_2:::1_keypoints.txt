- Addresses minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints and the challenge of extending ADMM to multi-block settings.
- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a dual "backward step" whose step sizes adapt to constraint sparsity and number of updated blocks.
- Establishes global convergence and an O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.
- Shows PDMM generalizes and unifies existing methods like sADMM and PJADMM.
- Significance: tackles a difficult, important problem with a practical, provably convergent solution.
- Originality: novel combination of parallel randomized updates and adaptive dual backward step; insightful unification of prior methods.
- Quality: theoretical results and proofs appear technically sound; experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show clear benefits.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written, with clear motivation and contextualization.
- Weakness — algorithm clarity: ambiguous dual update in eqs. (6)-(7); unclear if dual blocks are all updated or selected; selection mechanism needs specification.
- Weakness — theory discussion: Theorem 2’s implications need expansion, including relation to known rates of special cases (sADMM) for specific K and J.
- Weakness — theory/practice guidance: request for discussion on how to partition A and trade-offs between number of blocks (parallelism) and per-iteration progress.
- Experimental suggestion: briefly comment on applicability to other multi-block problems (e.g., tensor completion) and potential advantages there.
- Minor issues: probable missing summation in Theorem 2, missing space after "argmin" in eq. (9), and an extra space before "Section" on Line 217.
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper, recommend acceptance with suggested clarifications and discussions.
