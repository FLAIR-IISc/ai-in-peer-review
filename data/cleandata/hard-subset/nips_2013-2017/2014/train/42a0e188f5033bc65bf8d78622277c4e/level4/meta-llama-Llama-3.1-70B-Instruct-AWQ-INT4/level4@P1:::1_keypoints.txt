- Problem: Minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.
- Method: Proposes Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), a multi-block ADMM variant.
- Update scheme: PDMM randomly selects K primal blocks per iteration and applies a backward step to refine the dual variable.
- Parallelism: Primal updates can be implemented in parallel.
- Theory: Proves sublinear convergence rate and shows PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants.
- Experiments: Demonstrates effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso problems.
- Writing and reproducibility: Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Technical quality: Notation and analysis are sound; theoretical results are well-presented.
- Originality: High—method is novel and distinct from prior ADMM contributions.
- Significance: High—advances state of the art and is likely useful to researchers/practitioners.
- Strengths summary: Novel parallel multi-block ADMM, clear presentation, solid theory, and convincing experiments.
- Key weakness: Algorithm description around updates (5) and (6) is ambiguous whether all coordinates or a single block of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are updated.
- Theoretical clarification needed: More discussion on Theorem 2, comparison of convergence rates with other ADMM variants, and guidance on optimally splitting matrix A.
- Minor issues: Typo/formatting in (9) (missing space after "min") and extra space before "Section" at line 217.
- Recommendation: Accept pending minor revisions to address the issues above; no arguments against acceptance.
