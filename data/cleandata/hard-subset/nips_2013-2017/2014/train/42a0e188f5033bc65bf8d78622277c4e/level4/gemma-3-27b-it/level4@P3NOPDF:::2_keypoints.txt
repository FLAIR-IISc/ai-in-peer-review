- Introduces PDMM: a parallel Jacobian-based update method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Proposes a novel “dual backward step” to regulate dual updates and compensate for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates comparable to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of the PDMM framework.  
- Empirical results indicate PDMM performs well versus ADMM and variants, surprisingly overcoming typical Jacobian-method slowdowns.  
- Paper is novel, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated.  
- Method offers flexibility and potential impact for distributed optimization.  
- Missing detailed explanation of why PDMM achieves strong convergence despite using Jacobian updates.  
- Needs deeper analysis of conditions/problem structures where PDMM outperforms ADMM.  
- Requires clarification of the computational overhead introduced by the dual backward step.  
- Unclear how PDMM scales with increasing number of blocks and potential scalability limits.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance contingent on addressing convergence explanation and comparative analysis.
