- Proposes PDMM (parallel direction method of multipliers) for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Updates primal variables in a Jacobian (parallel) manner across multiple blocks, unlike ADMM’s Gauss-Seidel two-block update.  
- Main contribution: introduces a dual backward step that effectively reduces dual step size to compensate for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence results for PDMM, analogous to ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experimental results indicate PDMM outperforms ADMM and other variants on tested problems.  
- Empirical results are counterintuitive given usual slower convergence of Jacobian vs. Gauss-Seidel methods and need further explanation.  
- Dual backward step is the key innovation ensuring convergence under general conditions.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso applications.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Originality: extends ADMM to multiple blocks via the dual backward step and positions work relative to prior methods.  
- Significance: offers a new method for distributed optimization with potential broad impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, theoretical guarantees, strong experimental validation, and applicability to other problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: counterintuitive findings require more elaboration and the paper needs more detailed comparisons (e.g., proximal average method).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
