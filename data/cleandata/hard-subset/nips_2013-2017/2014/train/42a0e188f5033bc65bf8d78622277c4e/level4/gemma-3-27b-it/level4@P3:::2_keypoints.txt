- Proposes PDMM: a Jacobian-style parallel update for multi-block convex optimization with a novel “dual backward step” to ensure convergence.
- Establishes global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity, matching ADMM rates.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying framework.
- Dual backward step is the key innovation enabling compensation for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM is competitive or superior to ADMM and related methods.
- Observation that PDMM outperforms Gauss–Seidel-based methods is counterintuitive and noteworthy.
- Paper is generally well-organized and motivates PDMM clearly.
- Lack of detailed explanation for why Jacobian-style PDMM outperforms Gauss–Seidel in tested settings.
- Insufficient detail and analysis on parameter tuning and sensitivity (choice of τi and νi).
- Comparisons are somewhat limited; broader evaluation against more recent/optimized solvers is needed.
- Notation is dense in places and could be clarified (e.g., earlier definition of variables like di).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experimental explanations and provide practical parameter guidance.
