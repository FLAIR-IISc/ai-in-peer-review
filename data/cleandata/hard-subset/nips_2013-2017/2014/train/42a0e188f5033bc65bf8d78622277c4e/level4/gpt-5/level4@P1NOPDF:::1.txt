This paper studies minimization of block-separable functions under linear constraints. It proposes PDMM, a multi-block variant of ADMM that, at each iteration, randomly selects K blocks of the primal variable for update and applies a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal updates are parallelizable. The authors establish a sublinear convergence rate and show that PDMM subsumes several ADMM variants.

The paper is clearly written and easy to follow. I am inclined to accept it. My questions and comments are below:

1) Algorithm clarity (Eqs. (5) and (6)): The update rules for y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} are not sufficiently clear. Are all coordinates of the dual variables updated each iteration, or only a single block i? My current understanding is that only one block of y is updated, but I do not see how the index i is chosen. Please clarify the selection mechanism (random, cyclic, or other), whether multiple dual blocks are updated per iteration, and how this aligns with the K-block primal updates.

2) Theorem 2 notation: In the proof of Theorem 2, it appears that a “\sum_{i=1}^I” is missing immediately after a “{”. Please check and correct this typographical error.

3) Discussion of Theorem 2: As the main result, Theorem 2 provides the convergence rate for PDMM. Given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, it would be very helpful to:
   - Explain whether Theorem 2, with appropriate choices of parameters such as K and J, recovers the known convergence rates for those special cases. If so, please state the mapping between PDMM parameters and each variant, and specify the resulting rates.
   - Since the transformation matrix A is partitioned into multiple blocks, discuss whether Theorem 2 suggests any guidance on how to optimally split A (e.g., by conditioning, sparsity pattern, or block sizes). Are there choices of blockings that improve the constants or rates in the bound?

4) Applications and variants for tensor completion: For tensor completion (e.g., “Tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data,” 2012), which naturally exhibits a multi-block structure, do your results indicate which ADMM variant (including PDMM with particular K, J) is likely to be most effective? Any theoretical guidance or empirical evidence comparing variants in this setting would be valuable.

Minor comments:
- In Eq. (9), there should be a space after “min”.
- Line 217: remove the space before “Section”.

Overall, I am positive toward acceptance. Adding clarifications to the algorithm description and expanding the discussion after Theorem 2—especially connections to existing variants and guidance on block partitioning—would further strengthen the paper.
