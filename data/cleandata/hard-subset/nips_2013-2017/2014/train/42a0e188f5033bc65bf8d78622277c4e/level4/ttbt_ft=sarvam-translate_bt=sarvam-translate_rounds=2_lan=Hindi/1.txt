This paper explores the problem of reducing block-divisible tasks that are subject to linear constraints. The paper proposes a type of ADMM (called PDMM) for handling multi-block cases: it randomly selects K blocks of variable A and uses a subsequent step to update and refine the dual variable; the process of updating variable A can be applied in parallel. A sub-linear convergence rate has been proven for the proposed method. This method generalized several types of ADMM.

The paper is well-written and easy to understand. I am positive about accepting this paper. My questions and comments are given below:
1) The description of the algorithm in (5) and (6) is not clear enough. Do you update all the coordinates of y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} or only one block "i"? My understanding is that you only update one block of the dual variable "y", but I did not find how to select "i". This should be clarified in the revision.
2) In Theorem 2, I believe that in your proof, "\sum_{i=1}^I" was missing behind the "{" in your proof.
3) More discussion about the theorem should be included. Theorem 2 (the main result in this paper) provides the convergence rate of the proposed method PDMM. First, PDMM generalizes several types of ADMM. A natural question is whether Theorem 2 (by correctly choosing the parameter K, J) corresponds to the convergence rates of those types. Second, since you divide the transformation matrix A into several blocks, what is the best way to divide it from your theorem?
4) I am eager to compare it with the Tensor Completion problem ("Tensor Completion for Estimating Missing Values in Visual Data, 2012"), which also has a multi-block structure. Do you have any clues as to what type of ADMM is optimal?
