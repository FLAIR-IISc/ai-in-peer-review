- Introduces Parallel ADMM (PADMM): randomized block selection of K blocks per iteration with primal updates and a backward dual update.
- Backward dual update is crucial for conservative dual steps and establishing global convergence.
- Adds a proximal term in the primal update to handle a wider class of optimization problems.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving global convergence and deriving iteration complexity.
- Convergence rate shown as O(J / (T K)) after T iterations for J total blocks and K sampled per iteration.
- Enables fully parallel primal updates, improving parallelizability relative to GSADMM.
- Accepts relatively larger step sizes than existing methods, potentially outperforming sequential ADMM (sADMM).
- More flexible than Parallel Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM) and more amenable to parallelization than Gauss–Seidel ADMM (GSADMM).
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show promising accuracy and reduced computation time.
- Lacks reported results demonstrating actual speedups from a parallel implementation.
- Introduces three tunable parameters compared to standard ADMM, increasing practical tuning complexity.
- Overall contribution is valuable: well-written paper with sound theoretical and experimental validation.
