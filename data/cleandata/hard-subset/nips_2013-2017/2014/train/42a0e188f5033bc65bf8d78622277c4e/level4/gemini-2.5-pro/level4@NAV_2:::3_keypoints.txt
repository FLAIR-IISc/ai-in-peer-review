- Problem: minimize a sum of block-separable convex functions under linear equality constraints.  
- Proposal: PDMM—Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers performing parallel randomized block coordinate updates (select K blocks per iteration).  
- Novelty: introduces a dual "backward step" to temper dual updates and ensure convergence for Jacobi-style parallel ADMM.  
- Step-size rule: adaptive, sparsity-aware and randomization-aware step sizes that incorporate constraint-matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks.  
- Generalization: unified framework covering exact and inexact primal updates via Bregman divergence; sADMM and PJADMM are special cases.  
- Theory: proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with a constant step size.  
- Application: novel application to overlapping group regularization, addressing cases standard RBCD cannot handle.  
- Strength — originality/significance: well-motivated, flexible parallelization framework resolving known convergence issues and unifying related methods.  
- Strength — technical quality: rigorous proofs and convergence rate on par with standard ADMM for a parallel randomized method.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written; motivation for backward step and step-size choices clearly explained.  
- Strength — empirical: sequential PDMM experiments (RPCA, overlapping group lasso) are competitive and often faster than GSADMM, sADMM, RBSUMM.  
- Weakness — parallel results: lacks true parallel implementation and empirical speedup, weakening claims about practical parallel benefits.  
- Weakness — parameter tuning: sensitivity to penalty parameter ρ not addressed; experiments fix ρ=1 without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall: valuable, theoretically sound extension enabling parallel randomized multi-block ADMM updates, but practical parallel benefits remain empirically unverified.
