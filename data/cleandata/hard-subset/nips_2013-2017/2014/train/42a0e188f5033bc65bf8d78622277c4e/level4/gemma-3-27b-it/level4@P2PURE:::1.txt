This paper addresses the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. The authors propose a variant of the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), termed PDMM, designed for problems with multiple blocks. PDMM randomly selects *K* blocks of the primal variable for updating and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable; the primal variable update can be parallelized. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrate that PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants. 

The paper is well-written and easy to understand. I recommend acceptance. My questions and comments are as follows:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Specifically, it is unclear whether all coordinates of *y*<sup>*t*+1</sup> and *ŷ*<sup>*t*+1</sup> are updated, or only those corresponding to a single block “*i*”. My understanding is that only a block of the dual variable “*y*” is updated in each iteration, but the method for selecting block *i* is not explicitly stated. This should be clarified during revision.

2) In Theorem 2, I believe the summation symbol “∑<sub>*i*=1</sub><sup>*I*</sup>” is missing from the proof within the curly braces.

3) Further discussion of Theorem 2 is needed. As the main result of this paper, Theorem 2 establishes the convergence rate of PDMM. First, given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, it would be valuable to discuss whether the convergence rate established in Theorem 2 (with appropriate parameter choices for *K* and *J*) is consistent with the known convergence rates of those variants. Second, considering the block structure imposed on the transformation matrix *A*, the theorem does not offer guidance on the optimal block decomposition strategy. Exploring this would strengthen the results.

4) I am interested in a comparison to the tensor completion problem ("tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012"), which also exhibits a multiple block structure. Do you have any insights into which ADMM variant performs best in that context?
