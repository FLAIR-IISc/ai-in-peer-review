- Introduces PADMM: randomized parallel ADMM that selects K blocks per iteration, updates primal variables, then applies a backward dual update.  
- Backward dual update is key for conservative updates and ensuring global convergence.  
- Primal update supports an added proximal term, simplifying optimization for many problems.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Achieves convergence rate O(J / (T K)) for J blocks after T iterations selecting K blocks.  
- Enables full parallelization of primal updates and permits larger step sizes than prior methods.  
- Experiments show PADMM can outperform sequential ADMM by leveraging larger step sizes.  
- Claimed to be more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Validated empirically on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, showing reduced computational cost to reach desired accuracy.  
- Major limitation: no experimental results from a parallel implementation to confirm actual speedups.  
- PADMM introduces three additional parameters compared to standard ADMM, increasing tuning complexity.  
- Reviewer verdict: well-written, solid theory and experiments, and a valuable contribution to multi-block ADMM methods.  
- Reviewer questions: guidance for tuning the three parameters; overhead and practical impact of the backward dual step; plans to release code; behavior as K varies.
