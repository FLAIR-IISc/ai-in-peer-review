- Introduces PDMM: a randomized, parallel ADMM variant that samples K of J blocks per iteration, updates chosen primal blocks in parallel, then applies a conservative backward dual step.  
- Backward dual step is motivated to temper implicit dual ascent from the augmented Lagrangian and enable global convergence.  
- Method supports adding proximal/Bregman terms to primal subproblems to simplify computations.  
- Theoretical results prove global convergence and give an iteration complexity of O(J/(K T)) after T iterations.  
- Key benefits: fully parallelizable primal updates and larger effective dual step sizes than several alternatives.  
- By tailoring step sizes to constraint-matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks, PDMM can outperform sADMM.  
- PDMM is more flexible than PJADMM and allows more parallelism than GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM reaches target accuracies in less wall-clock time.  
- Limitations: no results from an actual parallel implementation reported.  
- Limitation: introduces additional parameters compared with standard ADMM, which may complicate tuning.  
- Overall assessment: compelling contribution with O(1/T) rate, clear writing, and solid theoretical and experimental support.
