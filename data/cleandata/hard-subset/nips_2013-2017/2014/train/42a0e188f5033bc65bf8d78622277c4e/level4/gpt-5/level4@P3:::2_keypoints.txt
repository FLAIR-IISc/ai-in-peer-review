- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel block-coordinate augmented Lagrangian with a dual “backward step” to stabilize Jacobi primal updates.
- Sets per-row dual step sizes τi and backward-step factors νi based on A’s sparsity (row degrees di) and number of updated blocks K.
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity with constant step sizes.
- Incorporates Bregman proximal terms to allow efficient inexact primal updates.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying these ADMM variants conceptually.
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM is competitive or superior to several ADMM variants.
- Novelty: combines randomized parallel multi-block updates with a principled dual backward step and sparsity-tied step-size rules.
- Strength: clear algorithmic idea—backward step counteracts implicit dual ascent and stabilizes parallel Jacobi updates.
- Strength: theoretical guarantees under standard convexity assumptions, covering exact and inexact updates.
- Strength: practical step-size rules exploiting A’s sparsity and block count, useful for overlapping-group problems.
- Weakness: derivation and intuition for τi and νi (eq. 8) need clearer explanation and ablation studies showing the backward step’s effect.
- Weakness: complexity results are only ergodic; discussion of non-ergodic rates or residual decay is missing.
- Weakness: inexact PDMM relies on curvature/Lipschitz conditions that should be stated explicitly with concrete examples.
- Clarity issue: heavy notation—recommend a concise algorithm box with default τi and νi and all steps shown.
- Clarity issue: key definitions (row neighborhood N(i), degree di, K̃i) should appear earlier with a small illustrative example.
- Presentation: several typos and inconsistent equation formatting should be corrected.
- Experimental concern: implementations are sequential Matlab; need distributed/multi-threaded experiments to validate parallelism claims.
- Experimental concern: clarify tuning procedures for ρ, τi, νi, proximal parameters across baselines to ensure fair comparison; report primal/dual residuals and feasibility.
- Experimental suggestion: provide per-iteration cost breakdown, compare random vs cyclic block selection, and perform sensitivity analyses over K, sparsity patterns, and problem sizes.
- Pros summary: principled backward step, constant-step convergence, sparsity-aware rules, unifies prior ADMM variants, solid empirical performance.
- Cons summary: heavy notation and exposition issues, lack of parallel experiments and communication analysis, ergodic-only rates, and potential fairness/tuning gaps in baselines.
- Overall: solid contribution that makes randomized parallel Jacobi multi-block updates practical and principled; exposition, parallel evaluation, and parameter-clarity could be strengthened.
