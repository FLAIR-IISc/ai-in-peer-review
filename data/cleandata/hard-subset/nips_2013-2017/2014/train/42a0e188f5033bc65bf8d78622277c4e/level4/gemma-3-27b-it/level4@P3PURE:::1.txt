This paper considers the problem of minimizing block-separable functions subject to linear constraints. A variant of ADMM, called PDMM, is proposed to address the multiple-block case; it randomly selects *K* blocks of the primal variable to update and employs a backward step to refine the dual variable. The process of updating the primal variable can be implemented in parallel. The authors prove a sublinear convergence rate for the proposed method and demonstrate that PDMM generalizes several existing ADMM variants.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and I recommend acceptance. My questions and comments are detailed below:

1) The algorithm description in equations (5) and (6) lacks clarity. Do these equations update all coordinates of *y*<sup>*t*+1</sup> and *ŷ*<sup>*t*+1</sup>, or only those corresponding to a single block *i*? My understanding is that only a block of the dual variable *y* is updated, but the method for selecting *i* is not explicitly stated. This should be clarified in the revision.

2) In Theorem 2, I believe a summation symbol, “\sum<sub>i=1</sub><sup>I</sup>”, is missing from the proof following the opening brace "{".

3) The discussion following Theorem 2 should be expanded. Theorem 2 presents the main result of this paper – the convergence rate of PDMM. Given that PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants, a natural question arises: is Theorem 2, with appropriately chosen parameters *K* and *J*, consistent with the known convergence rates of those variants? Furthermore, since the transformation matrix A is partitioned into multiple blocks, what is the optimal block structure according to the theorem?

4) I am curious about a comparison to the tensor completion problem (e.g., “tensor completion for estimating missing values in visual data, 2012”), which also exhibits a multiple-block structure. Do the authors have any insights into which ADMM variant might perform best in that context?

Minor comments:

1) In equation (9), a space is missing after "min".
2) Line 217, remove the space before "Section". 

I recommend acceptance of this paper, but I encourage the authors to expand the discussion following Theorem 2 to address the points raised above.
