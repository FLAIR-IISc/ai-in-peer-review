- Introduces PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant for multi-block problems.  
- Each iteration selects K random blocks, updates primal blocks, then updates the dual via a backward step.  
- Backward dual step makes updates more conservative and is key to ensuring global convergence.  
- Optionally adds a proximal term in the primal update to simplify certain subproblems.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis proving global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Shows convergence rate O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J total blocks with K selected per iteration.  
- Enables full parallelization of the primal step.  
- Supports relatively larger update step sizes and can outperform sADMM.  
- More flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM.  
- Empirical validation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows promising results and reduced computation time to reach accuracy.  
- No experiments shown for an actual parallel implementation of PADMM.  
- Parameter tuning (three PADMM parameters) is less straightforward compared to traditional ADMM.  
- Overall: solid contribution that matches ADMM convergence rate while enabling parallelization; paper is well-written with strong theory and experiments.
