- Proposes PDMM for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Replaces ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel (sequential, two-block) primal updates with Jacobian (parallel, multi-block) updates.  
- Introduces a dual backward step that compensates limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, similar to ADMM.  
- Shows (in supplement) that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments demonstrate PDMM’s empirical effectiveness compared to ADMM and other variants.  
- Reviewer finds results counterintuitive because Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel, and requests elaboration.  
- Notes the dual backward step is novel and key for achieving convergence under general conditions.  
- PDMM provides additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.
