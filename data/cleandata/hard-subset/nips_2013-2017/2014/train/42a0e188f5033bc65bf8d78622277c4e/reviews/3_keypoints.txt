- Proposes PADMM: a randomized parallel ADMM that picks K random blocks per iteration, updates selected primal blocks, then updates the dual via a backward step.  
- Backward dual step makes the dual update more conservative and is crucial for global convergence.  
- Allows adding a proximal term to the primal update, simplifying some subproblems.  
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)) after T iterations for J blocks picking K each time.  
- Main advantages: full parallelization of primal step and ability to use larger stepsizes than competing methods.  
- Claims faster performance than sADMM, more flexibility than PJADMM, and greater parallelizability than GSADMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso are promising: achieves target accuracy with less computation time.  
- Shortcoming: no reported results for a parallel implementation of PADMM.  
- Shortcoming: tuning three PADMM parameters is less convenient compared to traditional ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and well-written contribution with solid theoretical and empirical support, matching ADMM’s convergence rate while enabling parallelization.
