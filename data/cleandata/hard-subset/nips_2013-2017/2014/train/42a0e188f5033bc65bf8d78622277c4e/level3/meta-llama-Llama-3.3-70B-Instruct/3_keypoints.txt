- Proposes a novel structured spike-and-slab prior that incorporates prior knowledge about sparsity patterns in sparse signal recovery.
- Models spike-and-slab inclusion probabilities with a Gaussian process, allowing flexible encoding of prior information via covariance functions.
- Provides a Bayesian inference scheme using expectation propagation for the proposed model.
- Paper is well-written and ideas are clearly and logically presented.
- Key innovation: using a GP to model structured sparsity probabilities, enabling flexible and efficient prior encoding.
- Strong experimental evaluation demonstrating effective recovery of structured sparse signals and comparisons with LARS, BG-AMP, and oracle least squares.
- Strengths: novelty of the prior, clear presentation, and thorough numerical validation.
- Weakness: computational complexity scales as O(D^3) in the worst case, though approximations are suggested to mitigate cost.
- Weakness: limited discussion of relations to existing methods (e.g., group spike-and-slab, MRF-based approaches).
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and technical soundness; claims supported by theory and experiments.
- High significance and potential impact across signal processing and ML applications (image/video, audio, biomedical imaging).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution, clear writing, strong experiments, and high potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational cost and limited comparison/discussion with related prior models.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
