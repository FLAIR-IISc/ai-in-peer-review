- Proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant that updates K primal blocks in parallel and uses a backward dual step.  
- PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) providing a unified framework for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Authors establish global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with a constant step size.  
- Empirical results show PDMM is effective on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Algorithm steps (5) and (6) are unclear: ambiguous whether y^{t+1} and ŷ^{t+1} update all coordinates or a single block and how index i is chosen.  
- Theorem 2's proof is incorrect/missing a “∑_{i=1}^I” term and requires correction.  
- Paper needs more discussion on how PDMM’s convergence rates compare to known ADMM variants.  
- Lack of guidance on optimal ways to split the transformation matrix A for best performance.  
- Experiments could be strengthened by evaluating tensor completion (multi-block) and recommending which ADMM variant is optimal there.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written but contains minor typos (e.g., spacing issues).  
- Recommendation: accept subject to clarifying algorithm steps, fixing Theorem 2, and adding discussions on convergence consistency and matrix-splitting strategies.  
- Pro: novel, parallelizable algorithm with theoretical guarantees and demonstrated application effectiveness.  
- Con: unclear algorithm details, a proof error, and insufficient comparative/discussion material on convergence and splitting choices.
