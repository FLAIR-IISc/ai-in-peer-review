- Proposes PDMM, a randomized block coordinate method updating K random blocks in parallel with a conservative dual backward step.  
- Addresses the gap of effective ADMM generalizations to multi-block linear-constrained problems.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step sizes.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.  
- Unifies existing Jacobian ADMM variants under the PDMM framework.  
- Provides principled step-size selection based on constraint sparsity and number of blocks K.  
- Enables practical parallelization of the primal update step.  
- Extends to handle overlapping blocks, addressing an open problem for randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, with clear assumptions and structured proofs matching ADMM requirements.  
- Connects the role of the dual backward step to existing methods, giving theoretical insight.  
- Empirical results (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) show runtime improvements and maintained accuracy.  
- PDMM converges faster than GSADMM and sADMM in experiments, best when updating multiple blocks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible for experts.  
- Main weaknesses: no reported parallel implementation results, requiring careful tuning of τi and νi, and no guidance on choosing K.  
- Quality: technically sound with solid theory and experiments.  
- Originality and significance: novel randomized parallel ADMM variant that balances theory and practical parallelization, valuable for large-scale optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, unification with existing methods, compelling empirical speedups, practical parallel advantages.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of parallel implementation results and limited discussion on parameter/K selection.
