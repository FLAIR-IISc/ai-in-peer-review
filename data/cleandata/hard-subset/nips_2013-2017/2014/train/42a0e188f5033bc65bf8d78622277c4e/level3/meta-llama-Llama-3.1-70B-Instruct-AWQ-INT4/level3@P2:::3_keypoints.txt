- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Provides thorough review of ADMM and variants; positions contribution as a parallel randomized block-coordinate approach.  
- PDMM fully parallelizes the primal step, offering potential computational savings and supports larger step sizes.  
- No experimental results for a parallel implementation, so claimed parallel benefits are not empirically validated.  
- PDMM requires tuning three parameters, which may be less convenient than traditional ADMM in practice.  
- Lacks detailed comparison with other parallel optimization methods to better establish novelty and significance.  
- Paper is well-written and clear; reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall: interesting, solid theoretical and empirical support, with promising but not fully demonstrated practical advantages.
