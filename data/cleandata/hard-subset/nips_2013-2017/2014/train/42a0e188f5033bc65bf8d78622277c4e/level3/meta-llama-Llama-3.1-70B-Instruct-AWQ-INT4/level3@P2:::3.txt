This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method, called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size. The paper also presents experimental results showing that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The problem considered in this paper is a classic problem in optimization, and the authors provide a thorough review of previous work on the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and its variants. The authors' contribution is to propose a parallel randomized block coordinate method that can solve the problem more efficiently than existing methods.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical guarantees. The experimental results are also promising, showing that PDMM can achieve faster performance than competing methods.

One of the main advantages of PDMM is its ability to fully parallelize the primal step, which can lead to significant computational savings. The authors also show that PDMM can use larger step sizes than competing methods, which can further improve its performance.

However, there are some shortcomings to the paper. First, the authors do not report any results for a parallel implementation of PDMM, which would be necessary to fully demonstrate its benefits. Second, the authors note that tuning the three parameters of PDMM can be less convenient compared to traditional ADMM, which may limit its practical applicability.

Overall, this is an interesting and well-written contribution with solid theoretical and empirical support. The authors' proposal of a parallel randomized block coordinate method is a significant improvement over existing methods, and their experimental results demonstrate its potential benefits.
