- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant for multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints.
- Introduces a "dual backward step" to stabilize dual updates and ensure convergence in Jacobi-style parallel updates.
- Uses adaptive dual step sizes based on constraint-matrix sparsity and number of updated blocks.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity under standard assumptions.
- Unifies existing parallel ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) within a single framework.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, showing competitive or superior performance.
- High technical quality with sound motivation, complete theoretical analysis, and supportive experiments.
- Main clarity issue: ambiguity in dual updates (equations (6) and (7)) — unclear if all I dual blocks are updated or only a selected i.
- Minor proof issue: a missing summation ∑_{i=1}^I in the second line of Theorem 2's proof in the supplement.
- Suggests adding a discussion showing how Theorem 2 recovers special cases (e.g., sADMM with K=J, PJADMM) to illustrate unification.
- Requests practical guidance on choosing number of blocks J and strategies for partitioning A to balance subproblem cost and convergence.
- Asks for commentary on applying PDMM to tensor decomposition/completion and which ADMM variant may work best.
- Notes minor typographical fixes (space after "min" in eq. (9); extra space before Sec. 2.1).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated algorithm with strong guarantees, conceptual unification, and solid empirical results likely to impact research and practice.
- Arguments against acceptance: algorithm description ambiguity harms reproducibility and paper needs deeper discussion of theoretical implications and practical guidance.
- Overall recommendation: positive — strengths outweigh weaknesses and paper should be accepted pending clarifications.
