This paper proposes a novel parallel randomized block coordinate method, called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The proposed method is a variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and is designed to handle block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints.

The paper provides a clear and concise summary of the proposed method, including its key components, such as the parallel randomized block coordinate updates, the dual backward step, and the sparsity of the matrix A. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the method's convergence properties, including the global convergence and iteration complexity.

The experimental results presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in solving robust principal component analysis (RPCA) and overlapping group lasso problems. The results show that PDMM outperforms existing methods, including ADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM, in terms of convergence speed and accuracy.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method. The experimental results are convincing, and the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization.
