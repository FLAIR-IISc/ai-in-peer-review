- Addresses extending ADMM to multi-block convex problems by proposing PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate method with a corrective dual backward step.
- PDMM randomly updates K primal blocks in parallel each iteration and introduces a corrective dual update.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving global sublinear O(1/T) convergence under standard assumptions.
- Unifies several ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) under a single framework, clarifying their connections.
- Significant and original: elegant unification and a flexible, parallelizable algorithm applicable to overlapping blocks.
- Strong technical quality: sound theoretical proofs and convergence rate matching state of the art for first-order methods.
- Empirical validation: experiments on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso show PDMM is competitive or better than existing methods.
- Clarity issue: algorithm steps (5) and (6) ambiguous about whether dual updates y^{t+1} and ŷ^{t+1} apply to all coordinates or a single block i and how i is chosen.
- Discussion gap: Theorem 2 implications should be made explicit by recovering known rates for specific ADMM variants via parameter choices and providing practical guidance for splitting matrix A.
- Experimental scope gap: no empirical comparison on tensor completion problems, which naturally fit the multi-block setting and would strengthen the paper.
- Minor corrections needed: missing summation in Theorem 2 proof, missing space after "min" in eq. (9), and an extra space before "Section" at line 217.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, recommend acceptance pending requested clarifications and expanded discussion.
