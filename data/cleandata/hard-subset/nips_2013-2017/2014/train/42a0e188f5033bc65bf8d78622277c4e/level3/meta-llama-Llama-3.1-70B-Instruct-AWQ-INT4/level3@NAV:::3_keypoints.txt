- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for multi-block linear-constrained optimization.
- PDMM randomly updates blocks in parallel and adds a backward step on the dual update for conservative dual progress.
- Dual step size is chosen based on A's sparsity and the number of randomized blocks.
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art in convergence rate and runtime.
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, explanations, and organized figures.
- Strength: novel parallel randomized block coordinate approach applicable to multi-block constrained problems.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical guarantees (global convergence and complexity).
- Strength: strong empirical comparison demonstrating practical benefits.
- Weakness: no results for a parallel implementation, despite being a parallel method.
- Weakness: requires tuning three PDMM parameters, less convenient than traditional ADMM.
- Quality score 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and superior empirical performance.
- Counterarguments: lack of parallel implementation results and more complex parameter tuning.
