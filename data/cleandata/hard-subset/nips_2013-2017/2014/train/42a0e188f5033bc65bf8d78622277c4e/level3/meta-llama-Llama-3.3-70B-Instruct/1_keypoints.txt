- Proposes a novel structured spike-and-slab prior to incorporate prior knowledge about sparsity patterns using generic covariance functions.  
- Develops a Bayesian inference scheme based on expectation propagation (EP) for the proposed model.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making methods and results easy to follow.  
- Prior formulation is a significant contribution, enabling encoding of structural sparsity information.  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic data show improved phase transition and better reconstructions versus baseline methods.  
- The EP-based algorithm is flexible, efficient, and clearly explained for replication.  
- Strength: clear introduction, organization, and well-designed experiments demonstrating benefits.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other approaches that encode sparsity structure (e.g., Markov random fields).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of broader implications and potential applications across domains.  
- Weakness: missing detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability of the algorithm.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality; claims are theoretically and experimentally supported.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
