- Proposes PDMM to perform parallel (Jacobian) updates for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Replaces ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel updates with parallel updates to address multi-block extension issues.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step to compensate limited information propagation and control dual step size.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates, matching ADMM, with rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Demonstrates that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying perspective.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM’s strong practical performance.  
- Highlights the dual backward step as crucial for achieving convergence under broader conditions.  
- Positions PDMM as promising for distributed optimization and enabling alternative algorithms.  
- Notes counterintuitive empirical finding: Jacobian (parallel) PDMM outperforms Gauss–Seidel ADMM, which is unexpected.  
- Criticizes lack of detailed explanation for why PDMM outperforms ADMM on tested problems.  
- Points out limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and insufficient practical guidance for choosing K.  
- Concludes PDMM is a strong, novel, and theoretically sound contribution deserving acceptance, with suggested deeper empirical and tuning analysis.
