- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel (Jacobian) block updates plus a novel dual backward step, supports exact/inexact Bregman updates, and achieves O(1/T) ergodic rates for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Unifies previous Jacobian-style ADMM variants: sADMM and PJADMM are recoverable as special cases, clarifying trade-offs between primal progress and dual step conservatism.  
- Designs per-row dual steps τi and backward factors νi that depend on updated-block count K and row sparsity di (τi = K/(K̃i(2J−K)), νi = 1−1/K̃i with K̃i = min{di,K}), enabling larger steps when A is sparse.  
- Shows PDMM naturally handles overlapping-group problems by equality reformulation (induced di = 2) allowing more aggressive dual steps.  
- Theory: careful Lyapunov-based convergence analysis proving O(1/T) on objective suboptimality and constraint residuals under standard ADMM assumptions; backward step justified to prevent Jacobi divergence.  
- Algorithms: exact and inexact variants unified via a Bregman framework; linearization choices yield closed-form subupdates when needed.  
- Experiments: RPCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrate practical benefits and sparsity exploitation, but all runs are sequential MATLAB without wall-clock parallel/distributed speedups.  
- Clarity: paper is readable with strong intuition for the backward step; suggests adding pseudocode, earlier emphasis on τi/νi dependence and a compact definitions box, and fixing minor typos.  
- Originality: dual backward step appears novel and crucial for stable Jacobian multi-block updates; unification and sparsity-dependent step rules are valuable contributions.  
- Significance: offers a theoretically grounded Jacobian alternative to multi-block ADMM usable in distributed settings and beneficial for structured-sparsity problems.  
- Explains Jacobian vs Gauss–Seidel: augmented term induces implicit dual ascent that Gauss–Seidel dampens via sequential info flow, while PDMM’s calibrated backward step offsets accumulated ascent in Jacobi updates.  
- Analysis formalizes “calibrated damping” via constants in the Lyapunov function showing how sparsity permits more aggressive dual steps.  
- Requests for authors: provide practical heuristics or auto-tuning for K, sensitivity/ablation studies for τi/νi, potential linear rates under strong convexity, asynchronous/stale-update adaptations, and communication/computation trade-off analysis.  
- Experimental suggestions: include parallel/distributed wall-clock comparisons, compare to PJADMM with tuned proximal terms, and test varying di on synthetic sparse A.  
- Pros: novel, well-justified backward step; unifies prior methods; solid O(1/T) theory with sparsity-aware steps; empirical gains on relevant problems.  
- Cons: lacks true parallel/distributed experiments; needs more practical guidance for parameters; theory section dense with some notation/typo issues.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on stronger empirical validation in parallel settings and added implementation guidance for K, τi, and νi.
