- Problem: Extends ADMM to multi-block, block-separable convex optimization, focusing on enabling parallel (Jacobian) updates.  
- Proposal: Introduces the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) to allow parallel primal-block updates.  
- Key innovation: A novel dual backward step that compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Theoretical result: Proves O(1/T) convergence rate matching classic two-block ADMM.  
- Originality/quality: Dual backward step is novel and theoretically sound, controlling dual ascent to prevent divergence.  
- Significance: Provides a principled, convergent parallel algorithm useful for distributed optimization.  
- Unification: Shows existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among methods.  
- Empirical validation: Demonstrated practical effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Clarity concern: The O(1/T) rate is counterintuitive for Jacobian updates; the paper should give more intuitive explanation of how the dual backward step compensates for lack of sequential updates.
