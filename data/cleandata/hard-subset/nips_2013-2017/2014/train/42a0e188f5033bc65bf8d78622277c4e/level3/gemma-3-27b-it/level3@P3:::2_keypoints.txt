- Proposes PDMM: parallel randomized Jacobian updates for block-separable convex problems with a novel “dual backward step.”  
- Replaces Gauss–Seidel ADMM updates with parallel updates to improve multi-block scalability.  
- Introduces the dual backward step to compensate for limited information propagation in parallel updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM.  
- Demonstrates that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying framework.  
- Empirically validates PDMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso problems.  
- Strength — Novelty: Jacobian parallel updates plus dual backward step is a new multi-block ADMM approach.  
- Strength — Theoretical guarantees: proves O(1/T) convergence rates.  
- Strength — Unifying framework: clarifies relationships among PDMM, sADMM, and PJADMM.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: experiments show competitive performance on two applications.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-written with consistent notation.  
- Weakness — Counterintuitive convergence: lacks sufficient intuition why PDMM can outperform Gauss–Seidel methods.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: optimal step sizes (τi, νi) are complex and problem-dependent with no practical selection guidance.  
- Weakness — Limited experiments: only two applications tested, reducing generality of empirical claims.  
- Weakness — Missing baselines: no comparison to standard first-order methods (e.g., FISTA) without block decomposition.  
- For acceptance — Addresses important multi-block ADMM scaling issue with a novel, theoretically sound method and unifying perspective.  
- Against acceptance — Needs deeper explanation of convergence behavior, sensitivity analysis, broader experiments, and baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment — Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focused on intuition for convergence, parameter sensitivity analysis, and expanded experiments.
