- Introduces PADMM: randomized parallel ADMM that updates K blocks per iteration with a backward dual step to ensure convergence.  
- Builds on extensive prior ADMM and randomized coordinate descent work and clearly positions PADMM within that literature.  
- Technically sound with rigorous convergence proof and iteration complexity O(J/(T*K)).  
- Backward (conservative) dual update is key to addressing multi-block ADMM convergence issues.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show improved performance over sADMM, PJADMM, and GSADMM.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: no parallel implementation and the need to tune three parameters (K, ρ, Bregman-related).  
- Notation and derivations are clear and well organized.  
- Explanation of intuition behind the backward dual step could be more accessible.  
- Connection between step-size parameters (τi, νi) and A’s sparsity is clearly explained.  
- Originality: novel combination of randomized block selection and backward dual step; extension to overlapping blocks.  
- Significance: enables full parallelization of the primal step and allows larger step sizes than competing methods.  
- Flexibility: supports inexact primal updates via Bregman divergences.  
- Strength: shows sADMM and PJADMM as special cases of PADMM, strengthening theoretical ties.  
- Weakness: absence of a parallel implementation limits empirical demonstration of scalability.  
- Weakness: parameter tuning may be less convenient than traditional ADMM.  
- Recommendation: overall strong paper with theoretical and empirical support; reviewer recommends acceptance despite limitations.
