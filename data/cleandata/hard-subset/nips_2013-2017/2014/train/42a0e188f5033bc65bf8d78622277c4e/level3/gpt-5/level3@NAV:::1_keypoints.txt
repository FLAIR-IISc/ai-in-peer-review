- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM that updates K randomly selected primal blocks per iteration with a “backward” dual step.  
- Step sizes are chosen based on row-block sparsity di of A and the number K of updated blocks.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates with constant step sizes.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM arise as limiting cases of PDMM.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show favorable runtime versus GSADMM/RBSUMM and sADMM.  
- Positions PDMM relative to two-block ADMM, Gauss–Seidel ADMM with back substitution, PJADMM, randomized BSUMM, and randomized coordinate descent.  
- Conceptual value: backward dual step and sparsity-aware stepsizes help explain why Jacobi-style updates often diverge.  
- Analysis appears technically sound and uses standard ADMM assumptions.  
- Algorithm clarity issue: Steps (5)–(7) should state whether y^{t+1} and ŷ^{t+1} update all dual coordinates or a single block and how index i is selected; add a short pseudocode box.  
- Theorem 2 typo: missing “∑_{i=1}^I” that should be added.  
- Need expanded discussion after Theorem 2 to show consistency with known rates by choosing parameters (e.g., K, J) and to give guidance on splitting A to optimize β_i and step-size choices.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized but notation-dense; fix minor typos (missing space after “min” in (9), extra space before “Section” at line 217).  
- Originality: novel combination of backward dual step with sparsity- and K-aware step sizes and a unifying view including sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Significance: practically important for multi-block ADMM and relevant to theory and systems; would benefit from tensor completion experiments and guidance on which ADMM variant to use.  
- Pros: clear theoretical contributions with O(1/T) rate and unifying framework; practical, parallelizable algorithm with promising empirical results; insightful explanation of Jacobi instability.  
- Cons: ambiguities in algorithm description; minor proof/typo issues; more guidance on parameter choices and missing discussion on tensor completion.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
