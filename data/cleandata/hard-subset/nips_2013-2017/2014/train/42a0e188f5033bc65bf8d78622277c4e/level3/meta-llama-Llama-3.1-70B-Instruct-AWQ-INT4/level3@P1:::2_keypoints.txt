- Proposes PDMM, a novel parallel randomized block-coordinate algorithm extending ADMM to multi-block problems.
- Replaces Gauss–Seidel (sequential) primal updates with Jacobian (parallel) updates and adds a dual backward step to mitigate limited information propagation.
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.
- Demonstrates sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian methods.
- Empirical results show PDMM performs well compared to ADMM and other variants.
- Results are counterintuitive because Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel; the dual backward step is identified as key to convergence.
- Provides additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.
- Reviewer requests more intuition/explanation of why PDMM converges compared to Gauss–Seidel methods.
- Strength: introduces a novel multi-block extension of ADMM.
- Strength: establishes solid theoretical guarantees (global convergence, O(1/T)).
- Strength: demonstrates empirical effectiveness in experiments.
- Weakness: counterintuitive findings that need clearer intuitive justification.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Quality: technically sound and well-executed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical results, and empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: counterintuitive behavior and insufficient intuition comparing to Gauss–Seidel.
