- Proposes PDMM, a novel algorithm replacing sequential Gauss-Seidel primal updates in ADMM with parallel Jacobian updates and a dual backward step.  
- Introduces a dual backward step that reduces the effective dual step size to compensate for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size, matching ADMM guarantees.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Empirical results demonstrate PDMM’s effectiveness compared to ADMM and other variants.  
- Reviewer finds empirical results counterintuitive because Jacobian methods are typically slower than Gauss-Seidel.  
- Highlights novelty of the dual backward step as key to achieving convergence under general conditions.  
- Strength: provides a new distributed optimization alternative (PDMM) with theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: establishes rigorous convergence and complexity results.  
- Strength: thorough and well-presented experimental validation.  
- Weakness: counterintuitive performance relative to established Jacobian vs. Gauss-Seidel behavior.  
- Weakness: paper needs a more detailed explanation of the dual backward step and its role.  
- Quality/clarity: well-written, well-organized, and accessible to non-experts with clear algorithm and theory exposition.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and strong experimental support.  
- Rejection arguments: surprising empirical claims and insufficient detail on the dual backward step.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
