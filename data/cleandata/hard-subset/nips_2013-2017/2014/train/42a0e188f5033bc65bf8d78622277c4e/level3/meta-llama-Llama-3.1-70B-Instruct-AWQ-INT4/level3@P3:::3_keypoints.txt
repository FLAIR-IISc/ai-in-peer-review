- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for optimization with multi-block linear constraints.  
- PDMM is a parallel randomized block-coordinate variant of ADMM that can handle nonsmooth functions.  
- Method features three distinct aspects: a backward dual update, exploitation of A's sparsity, and step-size chosen based on number of randomized blocks.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with a constant step size.  
- PDMM converges at the same rate as ADMM and its variants.  
- Experiments show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis supporting convergence and complexity claims.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate practical effectiveness in two applications.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and provides enough detail for reproduction.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques with clear relation to prior work.  
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and is likely useful to practitioners and researchers.  
- Weakness: no reported results for a parallel implementation of PDMM.  
- Weakness: tuning three PDMM parameters is less convenient than traditional ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: strengths outweigh weaknesses; paper is a valuable, well-supported contribution.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and superior empirical performance.  
- Rejection arguments: missing parallel-implementation results and parameter-tuning burden.
