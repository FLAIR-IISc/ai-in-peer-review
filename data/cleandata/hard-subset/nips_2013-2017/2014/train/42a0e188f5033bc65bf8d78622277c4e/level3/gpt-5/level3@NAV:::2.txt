This paper proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate variant of ADMM for minimizing block-separable convex objectives with linear constraints. The key idea is to replace ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel (two-block, sequential) primal updates by Jacobian (parallel, multi-block) updates while introducing a novel dual backward step that shrinks the effective dual step size to compensate for limited information propagation in parallel updates. The authors establish global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates, with step sizes explicitly calibrated by the number of updated blocks and the row-block sparsity of A. The supplement shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases, which clarifies relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods. Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrate favorable performance relative to ADMM variants.

Quality: The analysis appears technically sound under standard convex assumptions, with clear statements of global convergence and complexity. The dependence on K and di in the step-size rules is thoughtful. However, the counterintuitive success of Jacobian updates over Gauss–Seidel deserves more discussion: why does PDMM not suffer from the usual slower convergence of parallel updates? The dual backward step seems crucial; an ablation isolating its effect (vary νi and τi, include νi=0) would strengthen the case. Guidance on tuning (τi, νi, ηjt) beyond formula (8), and sensitivity to these choices, is needed. Experimental comparisons are primarily sequential; claims about parallelism would be more convincing with multi-core/distributed wall-clock results and speedups on sparse A where di is small.

Clarity: The paper is generally well organized, but notation is heavy. A concise pseudocode box with all parameters, and more intuition for the y-hat “backward step” (e.g., viewing implicit dual ascent in the primal subproblems) would improve readability. Clarify constants in the Lyapunov function and offer intuition for the Pt, Q choices.

Originality: The dual backward step for Jacobian ADMM is novel and insightful; unifying sADMM and PJADMM as extremes is valuable. While the overall template fits within primal-dual/ADMM literature, the step-size coupling to sparsity and randomization is a meaningful advance.

Significance: The work offers a practically useful and theoretically justified alternative for distributed optimization with overlapping structures, likely to be adopted and extended.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel dual backward step enabling parallel multi-block ADMM with O(1/T) rate
- Unifying view connecting sADMM and PJADMM
- Clear dependence of convergence on sparsity and K
- Solid empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso

Cons against acceptance:
- Limited intuition for why Jacobian + backward step beats/seems comparable to GS updates
- Missing ablations and sensitivity analyses for τi, νi, ηjt
- No true parallel/distributed runtime evaluations
- Heavy notation; some proofs deferred with limited guidance in main text
