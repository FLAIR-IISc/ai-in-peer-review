- Proposal of PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for block-separable convex minimization with linear equality constraints.  
- Addresses multi-block ADMM convergence failures by randomly selecting and updating K primal blocks per iteration.  
- Introduces a dual "backward step" to make dual updates more conservative and aid convergence when many blocks are updated.  
- Adapts dual step sizes (τi, νi) based on total blocks J, selected blocks K, and constraint-matrix sparsity.  
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step sizes.  
- Unifies existing methods: shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.  
- Extends to overlapping-block problems (e.g., overlapping group lasso).  
- Strength — originality/significance: novel, significant advance enabling any number of parallel block updates within a single convergent framework.  
- Strength — technical quality: rigorous analysis handling randomized block selection and inexact primal updates.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, motivated, and organized.  
- Strength — empirical: sequential experiments (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) show superior wall-clock performance versus baselines.  
- Weakness — lack of parallel experiments: no multi-core/distributed results to confirm real parallel speedups.  
- Weakness — parameter tuning: τi and νi often require manual tuning beyond theoretical suggestions, raising practical sensitivity concerns.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel provably convergent algorithm, strong theory, unifying perspective, good sequential empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: parallel performance not empirically validated and extra tuning burden may hinder practical adoption.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance.
