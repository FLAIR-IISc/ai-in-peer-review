This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), which is a randomized parallel variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM). The main contribution of PDMM is its ability to update multiple blocks in parallel, making it more efficient than traditional ADMM methods. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of PDMM, including its global convergence and iteration complexity. The paper also presents empirical results on two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, demonstrating the effectiveness of PDMM.
