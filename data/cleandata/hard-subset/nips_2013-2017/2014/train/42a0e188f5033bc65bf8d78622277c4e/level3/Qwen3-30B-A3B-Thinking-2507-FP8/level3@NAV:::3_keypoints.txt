- Introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant that updates K randomly selected blocks per iteration.  
- Key innovation is a backward dual step that produces conservative dual updates crucial for global convergence.  
- Supports proximal terms to simplify subproblems and ease implementation.  
- Proves global convergence and O(J/(T K)) iteration complexity, matching ADMM convergence rates while enabling parallelization.  
- Positions PDMM as a unifying framework that subsumes sADMM and PJADMM, clarifying the role of dual backward steps.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM reaches target accuracy with less computation time than competitors, especially with larger step sizes.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound with comprehensive convergence proofs.  
- Lacks reported parallel implementation results, weakening the claimed parallelizability advantage.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Practical drawback: parameter tuning complexity (three parameters) may hinder adoption.  
- Original and significant: novel randomized parallel approach that handles overlapping blocks and advances multi-block ADMM theory.  
- Could improve by comparing PDMM to a broader set of parallel ADMM variants beyond those discussed.
