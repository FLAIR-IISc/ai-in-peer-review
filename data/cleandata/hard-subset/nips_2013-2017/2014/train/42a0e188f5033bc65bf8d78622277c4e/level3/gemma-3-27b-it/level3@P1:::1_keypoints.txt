- Introduces PDMM for block-separable convex problems, extending ADMM to multiple blocks with parallel randomized updates and a backward dual step.  
- Addresses convergence issues in multi-block ADMM and provides a unifying view connecting PDMM to existing ADMM variants.  
- Presents convergence analysis with proven sublinear iteration complexity.  
- Demonstrates empirical performance on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous convergence proof and sublinear rate.  
- Novelty: randomized block updates plus backward dual step enable parallel multi-block updates.  
- Generalization: shows existing ADMM variants as special cases of PDMM.  
- Empirical validation: promising results on relevant applications.  
- Clear motivation: well-articulated limitations of prior multi-block ADMM approaches.  

Weaknesses:
- Algorithmic clarity: ambiguity in steps (5)–(6) about which dual coordinates are updated and how index i is chosen.  
- Proof issue: missing “∑_{i=1}^I” in Theorem 2 that must be corrected.  
- Limited parameter guidance: lacks practical advice on choosing parameters (e.g., K, J) and linking them to known ADMM rates.  
- Block decomposition: insufficient discussion on how to optimally split matrix A into blocks and sensitivity to block structure.  
- Application scope: experiments limited to two tasks; broader evaluation (e.g., tensor completion) would strengthen claims.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important open problem with a novel, theoretically grounded method and promising empirical results.  
- Provides valuable unifying insights into ADMM variants.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Practical applicability limited by unclear parameter choices and block-splitting guidance.  
- Algorithm description clarity and limited experimental scope raise generalizability concerns.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify algorithm, fix the proof, and expand parameter/block guidance.
