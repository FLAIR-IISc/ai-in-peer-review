- Proposal: Introduces PDMM for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints using randomized parallel updates and a dual backward step.  
- Key mechanisms: Randomly update K primal blocks in parallel, apply a dual backward step to temper implicit dual ascent, and set dual step sizes using K and row-block sparsity di.  
- Variants and connections: Supports exact/inexact (Bregman linearization) variants and shows sADMM and PJADMM are limiting cases.  
- Convergence: Proves global convergence with O(1/T) ergodic rate under standard convexity assumptions.  
- Empirical results: Shows favorable performance on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso against GSADMM/ADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM.  
- Relation to prior work: Addresses multi-block ADMM challenges by offering a principled randomized parallel Jacobian framework with constant steps and a unifying perspective.  
- Overlapping groups: Reformulates overlapping-group problems with auxiliary variables to exploit di = 2 for larger dual steps.  
- Theory quality: Analysis is largely sound; dual backward step and τi, νi rules sensibly incorporate K and sparsity.  
- Inexact PDMM: Bregman linearization framework is technically clean and practical.  
- Proof correction: Theorem 2 proof is missing an outer summation “∑_{i=1}^I” in a displayed expression.  
- Suggested theory addition: Add corollaries recovering known ADMM rates for specific parameter choices (e.g., K = J, dense A; νi = 0, etc.).  
- Clarity issues: Algorithm steps (5)–(7) ambiguous about whether all dual blocks or only coupled ones are updated; make dual-update indexing explicit.  
- Notation: Mixes Acj, Ajt, and Ari — recommend a small symbol table for row/column blocks and indices.  
- Minor edits: Fix typos and spacing (e.g., space after “argmin”, remove extra space before “Section”, correct “addded”, “convegence”).  
- Originality: Dual backward step plus sparsity-/K-aware dual steps is novel and stabilizes Jacobian multi-block ADMM; unifying sADMM and PJADMM is conceptually valuable.  
- Significance: Offers a parallelizable, scalable algorithm with convergence guarantees for common ML multi-block problems; impact would be stronger with wall-clock parallel speedups.  
- Practical guidance: Request heuristics for splitting A to balance per-iteration cost and convergence (minimize max_i di, merge tightly coupled variables, balance block sizes).  
- Tensor completion: Ask for discussion on forming A and blocks for CP/Tucker, typical di, and recommended PDMM specializations (exact/inexact; K; τi, νi).  
- Experimental suggestions: Report simulated or actual parallel runtimes and include an ablation comparing PDMM with νi = 0 vs. νi per (8) to show stabilizing effect.  
- Pros summary: Well-motivated, unifying algorithm; solid convergence analysis with sparsity/K dependence; practical inexact updates; strong empirical performance.  
- Cons summary: Ambiguous algorithm description, missing summation and typos, limited guidance on block design/tensor completion, and experiments run sequentially without demonstrated parallel speedups.  
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to clarity fixes, the minor proof correction, and added guidance/case discussions (especially tensor problems).
