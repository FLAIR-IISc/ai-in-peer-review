- Proposes PDMM, a parallel Jacobian-style extension of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a "dual backward step" to stabilize parallel primal updates and enable convergence.  
- Provides rigorous O(1/T) global convergence rate matching standard ADMM under standard assumptions.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, offering a unifying perspective.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM is competitive or superior in convergence speed.  
- Novel and significant: addresses the challenging problem of provably convergent parallel multi-block ADMM with a simple effective idea.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees: rigorous proofs for global convergence and iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Unifying framework: clarifies relationships and trade-offs between exactness of primal updates and dual step sizes across methods.  
- Practical effectiveness: experiments convincingly demonstrate competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Lacks intuitive explanation of how the dual backward step compensates for information lag in Jacobian updates.  
- Provides little practical guidance on choosing hyperparameter K (number of blocks updated in parallel).  
- Experiments run sequentially; lacks distributed/parallel wall-clock time evaluation and discussion of communication costs.  
- Quality: technically strong and well-supported by theory and experiments.  
- Clarity: clearly written and well-organized presentation.  
- Originality: highly original contribution with impactful stabilization technique and unification insight.  
- Significance: highly significant for large-scale and distributed optimization; likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Overall: strong paper with minor weaknesses mainly in exposition and experimental validation.
