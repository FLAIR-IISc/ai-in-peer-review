- Proposal: PDMM — a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a backward dual step, sparsity-aware per-row-block step sizes, and support for inexact primal updates; sADMM and PJADMM are special cases.  
- Theoretical results: Global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic iteration complexity with constant step sizes.  
- Empirics: PDMM outperforms several ADMM variants on RPCA and overlapping group lasso and includes insightful ablations on K and (τi, νi).  
- Recommendation: Accept (overall score 7/10).  
- Strength — technical: Clean parallel randomized framework with principled backward dual step and di/K-dependent step sizes.  
- Strength — theory: Clear global convergence and explicit O(1/T) rates; clarifies connections to sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Strength — scope: Handles nonsmooth objectives and overlapping blocks via constrained reformulation.  
- Strength — experiments: Competitive/superior empirical performance and useful ablation studies.  
- Weakness — algorithm clarity: Steps (5)–(7) ambiguous; need to state primal updates only for sampled j ∈ J^t, dual updates for all i = 1…I (unless randomized dual variant), make index ranges explicit, and provide compact pseudocode.  
- Weakness — sampling: Clarify how J^t is sampled (uniform w/o replacement? partitioned? independent across iterations?).  
- Weakness — proof bug: Theorem 2 missing an outer summation over i — add ∑_{i=1}^I where appropriate.  
- Weakness — Theorem 2 exposition: Expand consequences and show explicit recovery of known cases (K=J, η=0 → sADMM; large η → PJADMM; K=1 → randomized one-block ADMM), and relate constants to prior refinements.  
- Weakness — practical guidance: Give guidance on splitting A and choosing K (trade-off between di, τi, νi, per-iteration cost) and heuristics for K given compute resources.  
- Weakness — experiments (parallelism): Results are sequential; add parallel/multicore experiments or discussion of expected speedups and communication costs (aggregation Aix).  
- Weakness — experiments (applications): Discuss tensor completion/robust tensor PCA use cases and which PDMM variant to pick when subproblems require SVDs; map formulations to di for expected stepsizes.  
- Weakness — parameter tuning: Provide practical defaults for (τi, νi) when ηjt>0 and a rule-of-thumb for choosing ηjt tied to Lipschitz/strong-convexity.  
- Quality: Analysis is careful and appears sound aside from the minor summation fix; backward dual step and sparsity dependence are insightful.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but needs explicit index ranges, clearer sampling description, compact pseudocode, and a small table mapping parameter choices to known methods.  
- Originality: Novel unifying PDMM framework despite related prior work; overlapping-block randomized treatment is a useful contribution.  
- Significance: Likely useful to practitioners for multi-block/overlapping constraints; clarifies safe parallelization and stepsize setting.  
- Pros for acceptance: Solid theory, unifying perspective, handles overlapping blocks, good empirical performance.  
- Cons against acceptance: Minor correctness/clarity issues, lack of parallel runtime experiments, limited application breadth (no tensor experiments), and weaker parameter-selection guidance.  
- Minor edits: Fix spacing in Eq. (9), remove extra space before “Section” on line 217, and explicitly state that (6) and (7) update all dual blocks i = 1,…,I.
