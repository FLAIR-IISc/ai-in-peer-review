- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that updates K randomly chosen primal blocks in parallel, uses a conservative backward dual step, and allows Bregman proximal terms for inexact updates.
- Backward dual step mitigates implicit aggressive dual ascent in Jacobi ADMM; step sizes τi and νi are chosen per dual row block using sparsity di and the number K of updated blocks.
- PDMM recovers sADMM and PJADMM as extreme parameter settings (ν ≈ 1 and ν = 0).
- The paper proves global convergence and an ergodic O(J/(K T)) complexity, matching ADMM’s O(1/T) rate while showing benefits of larger K.
- Supports randomized updates for overlapping blocks, demonstrated on overlapping group lasso.
- Empirically faster (in iterations/time) than sADMM and RBSUMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso; more flexible than PJADMM and more parallelizable than GSADMM, but experiments are sequential so no measured parallel speedup.
- Algorithm specification, Lyapunov function, and descent lemmas are clear; analysis covers exact and inexact (proximal) updates with convincing intuition for the backward step.
- Convergence and complexity claims are well supported by proofs; empirical results align with claims but scalability claims remain qualitative without parallel implementation.
- Paper is complete (theory, algorithmic connections, experiments) and generally well written, with only minor typos.
- Clarity could be improved by adding a compact algorithm box with defaults, a practical guide for selecting K/τi/νi/ηjt, and a small glossary for block notation.
- Originality: novel combination of randomized parallel block updates, explicit backward dual step, and block-dependent step sizes that exploit sparsity; unifies prior methods and handles overlapping blocks.
- Significance: important for ML and signal processing; promising due to theoretical guarantees and simple default stepsizes despite missing parallel experiments.
- Strengths: clear fix for Jacobi instability, fully parallelizable primal updates, sparsity-exploiting step sizes, flexible proximalization, strong theory, and robust empirical performance.
- Weaknesses: no actual parallel implementation reported, three parameters to tune with limited practical guidance, omission of an explicit PJADMM baseline, and opaque theoretical constants.
- Suggested experiments/analyses: add parallel multi-threaded/distributed experiments, ablations/sensitivity studies and heuristics for τi/νi/ηjt and K, include PJADMM baseline, and clarify grouping when K does not divide J.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled and novel solution to a challenging problem, strong theory, and promising empirical evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of demonstrated parallel implementation and limited tuning guidance could hinder practical impact.
- Overall assessment: a well-written, theoretically solid, and promising contribution recommended for acceptance if supplemented with parallel experiments and more practical tuning guidance.
