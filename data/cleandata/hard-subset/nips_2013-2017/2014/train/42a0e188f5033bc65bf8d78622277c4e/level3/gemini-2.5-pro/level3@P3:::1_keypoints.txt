- Introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM that updates a random subset K of primal blocks and uses a backward step on the dual to stabilize multi-block updates.  
- Proves global convergence with O(1/T) sublinear rate under standard assumptions and constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM unifies several ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) as special cases, providing conceptual insights into dual update parameters.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Novelty/significance: well-motivated for large-scale ML; parallel randomized primal updates plus controlled dual update is a clever solution to multi-block instability.  
- Theoretical quality: rigorous analysis and iteration complexity are major strengths; unification of methods is a strong conceptual contribution.  
- Clarity/organization: generally well-written and logically structured.  
- Algorithm description needs clearer notation: specify whether dual updates (eqs. 6–7) apply to all dual blocks or only those tied to selected primal blocks, and clarify index relationships between primal blocks jt ∈ Jt and dual block i.  
- Proof correction: in Theorem 2 a summation term (∑_{i=1}^I) appears missing in the numerator of the first RHS term and should be checked.  
- Need deeper discussion: explicitly show how PDMM’s rate recovers known rates for sADMM/PJADMM by substituting parameters; provide practical guidance on partitioning A into row/column blocks.  
- Experiments: consider extending or discussing applicability to tensor completion/decomposition and advise which PDMM variants suit such problems.  
- Minor typos: missing space after "min" in eq. (9); extra space before "Section" on line 217.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, address clarity and discussion points in revision; recommend acceptance.
