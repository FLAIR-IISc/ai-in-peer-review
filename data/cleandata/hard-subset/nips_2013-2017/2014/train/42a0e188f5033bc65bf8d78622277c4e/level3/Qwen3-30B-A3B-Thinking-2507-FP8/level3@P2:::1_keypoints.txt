- Introduces PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant that updates K blocks in parallel with a dual backward step to ensure convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Incorporates constraint matrix sparsity into step-size selection and handles overlapping blocks in randomized updates.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing theoretical connections to prior methods.  
- Builds on ADMM, randomized block coordinate descent, and recent multi-block ADMM literature.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Proof techniques are solid and assumptions are reasonable, supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Minor proof issue: Theorem 2 is missing a summation term (add ∑_{i=1}^I).  
- Algorithm description (equations 5–7) is ambiguous about which dual blocks are updated and index selection; needs clarification.  
- Typographical issues: missing space after "min" in Eq. (9) and extra space before "Section" at line 217.  
- Novelty: randomized parallel updates with dual backward step and handling of overlapping blocks is significant and novel.  
- Practical significance: constant step size convergence and parallel overlapping-block handling are valuable for large-scale ML.  
- Weakness: limited guidance on choosing parameters (K, J) for different problems.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of applications beyond the two evaluated (e.g., tensor completion).  
- Recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix clarity and the proof error.
