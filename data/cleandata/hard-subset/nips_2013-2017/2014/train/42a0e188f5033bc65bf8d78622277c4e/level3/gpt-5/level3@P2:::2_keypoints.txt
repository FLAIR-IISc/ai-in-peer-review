- Proposes PDMM: a randomized, parallel multi-block augmented Lagrangian method with a dual backward step and sparsity-aware dual step sizes.
- PDMM updates K primal blocks in parallel (Jacobian-style) and adjusts dual ascent via τi and backward step νi depending on K and per-row block sparsity di.
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates with constant step sizes for randomized parallel multi-block updates under standard convex assumptions.
- Shows PDMM subsumes two Jacobian ADMM variants (sADMM and PJADMM) and supports overlapping blocks via an equality-constrained reformulation.
- Conceptual novelty: dual backward step plus sparsity-aware step-size selection explains and fixes instability of naive Jacobian ADMM.
- Theoretical guarantees: rigorous convergence proofs and ergodic rate for the randomized parallel multi-block setting.
- Unification: clarifies trade-offs between exact vs proximal primal progress and appropriate dual step sizes by connecting sADMM and PJADMM.
- Practical scope: handles nonsmooth objectives, overlapping blocks, and inexact primal updates via Bregman linearization.
- Empirical results: competitive-to-superior performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso in reported single-threaded experiments.
- Counterintuitive empirical behavior: PDMM sometimes outperforms Gauss–Seidel despite Jacobian updates typically being slower; more explanation needed on when/why.
- Parameter guidance lacking: need explicit or adaptive rules for τ, ν, η, and K, and advice on estimating di and sensitivity to misspecification.
- Proof exposition issues: key matrices (Pt, Q) and parameters (βi, γi) are referenced but not defined in the main text; more intuition and definitions should be surfaced.
- Inexact PDMM conditions: requirements for ψjt and ϕjt are brief; authors should spell out common choices, closed forms, and uniform O(1/T) validity for inexact updates.
- Experimental scope limited: all results are single-threaded MATLAB; missing parallel/distributed experiments or communication/computation complexity analysis.
- Reproducibility gaps: exact parameter settings, stopping criteria, and code/data availability are not provided.
- Quality: technically careful analysis with intuitive dependence of τi and νi on K and di, but main text needs more self-contained definitions and a proof sketch.
- Clarity: generally well written with heavy notation; recommend an algorithm box, parameter table, and typo corrections.
- Originality: dual backward step and sparsity-aware step sizes are novel; unifying sADMM and PJADMM is insightful.
- Significance: important for large-scale/distributed optimization; practical recipes and constant-step guarantees are likely impactful.
- Key questions for authors: can τi and νi be adapted online; sensitivity to inaccurate di; explicit dependence of O(1/T) constants on di; simple rules for ηjt; communication complexity per iteration?
- Pros for acceptance: novel idea with theory, unification of ADMM variants, broad applicability, and solid convergence guarantees.
- Cons for acceptance: limited parallel empirical evaluation, deferred theoretical details in supplement, and insufficient parameter selection guidance.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable contribution but needs stronger exposition, parameter guidance, and empirical parallelism evaluation.
