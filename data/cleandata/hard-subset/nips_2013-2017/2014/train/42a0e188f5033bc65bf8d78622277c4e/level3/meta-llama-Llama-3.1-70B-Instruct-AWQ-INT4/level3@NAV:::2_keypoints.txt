- Proposes PDMM, a novel Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers for block-separable convex minimization with linear constraints.  
- Replaces Gauss–Seidel with Jacobian updates to enable efficient randomized parallel block-coordinate optimization.  
- Introduces a dual backward step to compensate limited information propagation and reduce effective dual step size.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Empirically outperforms existing methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Strength: novel algorithm addressing limitations of ADMM and its variants.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation with convergence and complexity analysis.  
- Strength: empirical validation on real-world problems.  
- Weakness: assumes block separability, which may not hold in many practical problems.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to just two problem types, reducing empirical generality.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, original, and potentially widely applicable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: separability assumption, missing complexity analysis, and limited experimental breadth.
