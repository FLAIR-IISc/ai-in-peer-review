**Paper Review**

The paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) for solving block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. PDMM is a randomized block-coordinate variant of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) that allows parallel updates of primal blocks and uses a backward dual step. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size and demonstrate its effectiveness in two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper starts by introducing the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints, which is a common formulation in many machine learning applications. The authors then propose PDMM, which randomly updates K primal blocks in parallel and uses a backward dual step to make conservative progress on the dual variable. The algorithm is shown to generalize several ADMM variants, including two-block ADMM with splitting variables (sADMM) and proximal Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM).

**Strengths**

* PDMM is a novel and efficient optimization algorithm that allows parallel updates of primal blocks, making it suitable for large-scale optimization problems.
* The algorithm is shown to generalize several ADMM variants, providing a unified framework for solving block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints.
* The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size, providing theoretical guarantees for the algorithm's performance.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

**Weaknesses**

* The algorithm steps (5) and (6) are unclear and require clarification. Specifically, it is unclear whether y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} update all coordinates or a single block, and how the index i is selected.
* The proof of Theorem 2 requires correction, as a missing "\sum_{i=1}^I" should be added.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion on the consistency of PDMM's convergence rates with known ADMM variants and guidance on optimal ways to split the transformation matrix A.
* The experimental results could be strengthened by providing comparison or insight for tensor completion applications (multi-block structure) and indicating which ADMM variant may be optimal there.

**Clarity and Organization**

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with each section providing a logical progression of ideas. However, the algorithm steps (5) and (6) require clarification, as mentioned earlier. Additionally, there are a few minor typos, such as a missing space after "min" in (9) and an extra space before "Section" at line 217.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm that generalizes several ADMM variants and provides a unified framework for solving block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The algorithm's ability to allow parallel updates of primal blocks and use a backward dual step makes it a significant contribution to the field of optimization.

**Recommendation**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I am inclined to accept the paper. However, I recommend that the authors address the clarity issues, correct the proof of Theorem 2, and provide more discussion on the consistency of PDMM's convergence rates with known ADMM variants and guidance on optimal ways to split the transformation matrix A.
