- Proposes PADMM/PDMM: a randomized parallel ADMM variant that samples K primal blocks per iteration, updates them in parallel (optionally with proximal/Bregman terms), then applies a conservative backward dual step.
- Introduces blockwise dual stepsizes set as a function of K and A’s sparsity pattern, enabling larger stable steps than Jacobi ADMM.
- Proves global convergence and an ergodic iteration complexity O(J/(T K)), matching ADMM’s O(1/T) rate while allowing parallelism.
- Positions the method relative to prior work (GSADMM, sADMM, PJADMM, RBSUMM), showing sADMM/PJADMM as limiting cases and extending to overlapping blocks.
- Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show improved time-to-accuracy over sADMM and RBSUMM and competitive performance with GSADMM, though all tests are sequential.
- Analysis is careful and technically sound; the backward dual step is well motivated and stepsize rules are explicit with sensible scaling in K.
- Paper is well written and organized; reviewer suggests adding pseudocode, practical guidance for choosing K, τ, ν and proximal parameters, and a brief discussion of distributed communication/synchronization.
- Novelty: the combination of randomized parallel block updates, a dual backward step, and sparsity-aware stepsizes is new and distinct from related methods.
- Significance: enables parallel ADMM-like updates with theoretical guarantees for multi-block problems, relevant for large-scale structured applications.
- Cons: no parallel/multicore/GPU/distributed implementation results; requires tuning three parameters with limited guidance or sensitivity analysis.
