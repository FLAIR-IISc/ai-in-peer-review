- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel extension of ADMM that updates K randomly chosen blocks in parallel for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses the gap that two-block ADMM is well-understood but multi-block generalizations lack reliable convergence.  
- Introduces a dual backward step (eqs. 6–7) as a key innovation enabling convergence, justified by implicit dual ascent analysis (eq. 9).  
- Chooses step sizes based on block sparsity (di) and number of updated blocks (K) (eq. 8), allowing larger steps than competing methods.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relations to prior methods.  
- Enables randomized block coordinate descent for overlapping groups, solving a previously open problem.  
- Empirically outperforms sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso in accuracy, iterations, and runtime.  
- Provides clear experimental comparisons and parameter-tuning studies.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and advances understanding of parallel randomized multi-block methods.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and presents valuable theoretical insights.  
- Major weakness: lacks any true parallel implementation results, so claimed parallel speedups are unverified.  
- Practical downside: PDMM requires tuning three parameters (τi, νi, ηjt), which is less convenient than standard ADMM’s single ρ.  
- Limited comparison to other parallel/distributed ADMM variants and sparse implementation details for overlapping group lasso.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor shortcomings (parameter tuning and missing parallel benchmarks), recommended for acceptance.
