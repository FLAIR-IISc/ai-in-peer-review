- Proposes PDMM, a novel algorithm replacing Gauss-Seidel with Jacobian primal updates to enable parallelization and improve convergence.  
- Introduces a dual backward step to compensate for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates and effectively reduce the dual step size.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size, matching ADMM.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness of PDMM versus ADMM and variants on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Reviewer finds results counterintuitive since Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss–Seidel and requests more elaboration.  
- Authors emphasize the critical role of the dual backward step in achieving convergence under general conditions.  
- PDMM offers new alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.  
- Quality: technically sound, claims well-supported by theory and experiments; careful and complete work.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized, reproducible for experts, but notation could be clearer and some proofs moved into main text.  
- Originality: presents a novel combination of techniques and a significant connection between existing methods.  
- Significance: important advance likely to be adopted and built upon by researchers and practitioners.  
- Pro: novel effective algorithm with solid theoretical guarantees and clarified method relationships.  
- Con: counterintuitive results need more explanation about the dual backward step; minor notation and organization improvements suggested.  
- Overall: a significant, well-supported contribution to optimization and machine learning.
