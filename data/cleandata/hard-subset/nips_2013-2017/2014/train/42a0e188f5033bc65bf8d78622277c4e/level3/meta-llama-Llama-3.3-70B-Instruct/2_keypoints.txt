- Proposes a structured spike-and-slab prior using a Gaussian process on spike probabilities to encode sparsity structure via covariance functions.  
- Introduces a Bayesian inference scheme based on expectation propagation for the proposed model.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with a strong introduction and motivation.  
- Method is clearly explained and appears straightforward to implement.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms LARS and BG-AMP on phase transition and F-measure.  
- Provides thorough experimental evaluation and a detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Computational complexity is high, potentially limiting applicability to large-scale problems.  
- Lacks clear comparison with other structured-sparsity approaches (e.g., graph-based methods).  
- Strengths: novel prior enabling a priori sparsity structure, clear exposition, and strong empirical results.  
- Weaknesses: high computational cost and insufficient benchmarking against alternative structured methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity and comparative evaluation.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
