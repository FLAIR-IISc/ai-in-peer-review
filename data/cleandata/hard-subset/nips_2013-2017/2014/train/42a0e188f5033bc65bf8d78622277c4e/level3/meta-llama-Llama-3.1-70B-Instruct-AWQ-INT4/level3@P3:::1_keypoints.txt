- Proposes PDMM, a novel parallel randomized block coordinate extension of ADMM for multi-block linear-constrained problems.
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, outperforming existing methods.
- Well-written paper with thorough technical analysis and detailed convergence proofs.
- Generalizes several ADMM variants, including sADMM and PJADMM.
- Clearly explains the role of the dual backward step and how A’s sparsity affects the dual step size.
- Ambiguity in Algorithm steps (5)–(6): unclear whether y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} update all coordinates or a single block and how index i is chosen.
- Proof of Theorem 2 missing a “\sum_{i=1}^I”; needs correction.
- Theorem 2 requires more discussion: relation to known ADMM convergence rates and advice on optimal splitting of matrix A.
- More insight requested on experiments with tensor completion and multi-block structure, specifically which ADMM variant is likely optimal and why.
- Minor typos: missing space after "min" in (9) and extra space before "Section" at line 217.
- Overall assessment positive and inclined to accept, contingent on addressing the above clarifications.
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments.
- Originality and significance: novel method addressing an important optimization problem with potential impact.
