The paper "Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers" proposes a novel optimization algorithm for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The algorithm, called Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), is a parallel randomized block coordinate method that extends the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) to multiple blocks.

The main idea of PDMM is to replace the Gauss-Seidel (sequential, two-block) primal updates of ADMM with Jacobian (parallel, multi-block) updates. This allows for more efficient parallelization and scalability. Additionally, PDMM introduces a dual backward step that compensates for the limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.

The authors establish the global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size, which is similar to ADMM. They also show that two existing Jacobian ADMM methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM, clarifying the relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.

The experimental results demonstrate the empirical effectiveness of PDMM compared to ADMM and other variants. The reviewer finds the results counterintuitive because Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss-Seidel methods. However, the authors' introduction of the dual backward step is novel and key for achieving convergence under general conditions.

The paper provides additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization, making it a valuable contribution to the field. The reviewer suggests that the authors elaborate on the intuition behind the convergence of PDMM, particularly in comparison to Gauss-Seidel methods.
