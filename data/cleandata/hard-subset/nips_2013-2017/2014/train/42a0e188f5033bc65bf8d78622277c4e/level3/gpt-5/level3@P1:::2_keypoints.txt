- Proposal: Introduces PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate augmented-Lagrangian method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Novel mechanisms: Uses Jacobi (parallel) primal updates on random block subsets, a “dual backward step,” and sparsity-aware adaptive dual step sizes.  
- Primal updates: Allows exact and inexact (proximal/Bregman-linearized) primal solves within one unified analysis.  
- Dual backward step: Conservatively corrects the dual variable inside primal subproblems to stabilize naive Jacobi ADMM.  
- Adaptive dual steps: Sets τi and νi based on number of updated blocks K and row-sparsity di to permit larger steps when constraints are sparse.  
- Algorithmic formulas: Recommends τi = K/(K̃i(2J−K)) and νi = 1 − 1/K̃i to stabilize Jacobi updates.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity under standard convexity and KKT existence with constant stepsizes.  
- Unification: Recovers sADMM and PJADMM as limiting cases, clarifying their relation via dual step shrinkage and proximalization.  
- Interpretation: Shows augmented term implies implicit dual ascent and the backward step reduces excessive implicit dual progress.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on multi-block ADMM literature and randomized block-coordinate (RBCD) theory, making per-row degrees di explicit.  
- Empirical performance: PDMM outperforms RBSUMM and sADMM on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso in sequential MATLAB experiments.  
- Sparsity advantage: For problems with small di (e.g., overlapping groups with di=2) PDMM can use much larger τi explaining strong empirical gains.  
- Quality: Algorithm design and proofs are sound and align with prior ADMM assumptions; main proofs are in the supplement.  
- Clarity issues: Paper is dense; derivation and intuition for τi and νi and some notation (z, Pt, Q, residual R) need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: The dual backward step plus sparsity-aware randomized updates is novel and conceptually useful.  
- Significance: Provides a practical, provably stable parallel option for multi-block ADMM where Jacobi schemes can fail.  
- Suggested experiments: Add synthetic di ablations, backward-step isolation studies, and true parallel/distributed timing and communication experiments.  
- Suggested analysis: Include short derivation sketches for τi/νi, sensitivity to ρ and Bregman parameters, and discussion of adaptive K and step strategies.  
- Comparison gaps: Broaden baselines to include other stabilized multi-block and primal–dual methods for clearer scope.  
- Limitations: Current experiments are sequential MATLAB runs and heavy reliance on supplementary proofs; step-size intuition is thin in the main text.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — novel, well-analyzed, empirically promising method with minor clarifications and broader experiments suggested.
