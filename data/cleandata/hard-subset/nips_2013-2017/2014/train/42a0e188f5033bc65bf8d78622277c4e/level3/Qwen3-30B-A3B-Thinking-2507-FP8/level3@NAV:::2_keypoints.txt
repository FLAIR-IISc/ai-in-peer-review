- Introduces PDMM, a novel algorithm that generalizes ADMM by using parallel Jacobian updates plus a dual backward step for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for PDMM, comparable to ADMM.
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.
- Provides empirical evidence that PDMM outperforms alternatives on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso tasks.
- Dual backward step is a significant technical contribution that addresses convergence challenges in parallel updates.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, establishing global convergence and iteration complexity under reasonable assumptions.
- Clarifies the relationship among augmented Lagrangian decomposition methods and related work.
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and demonstrates both faster convergence and runtime efficiency.
- The claim that Jacobian (parallel) updates can outperform Gauss–Seidel needs deeper justification.
- Paper lacks sufficient theoretical and empirical explanation for why PDMM overcomes the typical slowdown of Jacobian methods.
- Well-written, clear, and properly contextualized within existing literature.
- High significance: provides a theoretically grounded alternative for distributed, parallel optimization without losing convergence guarantees.
- Recommendation: accept with the authors required to better explain the counterintuitive Jacobian convergence advantage.
