- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate variant of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with multi-block linear constraints.  
- Clearly describes key components: parallel randomized block updates, the dual backward step, and use of A's sparsity.  
- Provides detailed convergence analysis, including global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms ADMM, GSADMM, and RBSUMM in speed and accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written and presents a thorough analysis; makes a significant contribution.  
- Assumes matrix A is sparse, which may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with other methods such as proximal Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM).  
- Insufficient analysis of computational complexity and memory requirements.  
- Recommendation: include a detailed comparison with PJADMM and similar methods.  
- Recommendation: provide a more detailed analysis of computational complexity and memory usage.  
- Recommendation: add more experimental results on varied problem instances.  
- Minor typos: missing space after "min" in eq. (9); extra space before "Section" at line 217.  
- Proof correction: add missing "∑_{i=1}^I" in the proof of Theorem 2.  
- Need more discussion on parameter choices, e.g., step size and number of blocks to update.  
- Need discussion on extending PDMM to handle non-linear constraints.  
- Experimental request: evaluate PDMM on additional instances like tensor completion with multi-block structure.  
- Experimental request: provide insights on which ADMM variant may be optimal for tensor completion applications.
