- Proposes PADMM: a randomized Jacobian-style block-coordinate ADMM that updates K randomly selected blocks in parallel and then updates the dual.
- Core innovation is a conservative "dual backward step" that stabilizes updates and is essential for global convergence.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis proving global convergence with an O(1/T) rate matching standard ADMM.
- Shows PADMM generalizes and unifies existing methods (e.g., sADMM and PJADMM) as special cases.
- Algorithm is flexible: supports any number of parallel block updates (1 to all) and incorporates proximal terms for difficult subproblems.
- Dual step-sizes adapt to block sparsity (di) and number of chosen blocks (K), often permitting larger stepsizes than competitors.
- Strong empirical performance on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso; faster convergence in serial Matlab experiments compared to GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM.
- Main weakness: lacks any true parallel (multi-core or distributed) implementation experiments, leaving parallel benefits unverified in practice.
- Practical drawback: introduces extra tuning parameters (τi, νi) in addition to ADMM’s ρ, and manual tuning outperforms theoretical settings in experiments.
- High technical quality: rigorous and complete theoretical development supporting the claims.
- Clear and well-written presentation; the dual backward step and connections to prior work are well explained.
- Highly original contribution combining randomized parallel block selection with a provably convergent dual modification.
- Significant potential impact for scalable optimization and distributed ML, though real-world parallel performance remains to be demonstrated.
- Overall verdict: excellent, theoretically sound and practically promising work, tempered by the absence of parallel implementation results.
