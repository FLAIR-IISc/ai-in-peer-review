- Proposes PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate descent method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints that generalizes ADMM to multiple blocks with parallel updates and sublinear convergence guarantees.

- Framework encompasses existing methods (sADMM and PJADMM) as special cases.

- Solid, potentially valuable contribution for large-scale optimization; reviewer inclined to accept.

- Paper is well-motivated and clearly outlines limitations of existing multi-block ADMM methods.

- PDMM is presented logically with a strong theoretical foundation including convergence analysis and iteration complexity bounds.

- Insightful connection to sADMM and PJADMM, providing a unified perspective and clarifying the dual backward step.

- Experimental results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso are promising, showing PDMM’s efficacy against state-of-the-art methods.

- Algorithm clarity issues: steps (5) and (6) are unclear—uncertain whether y^{t+1} and ŷ^{t+1} update all coordinates or a single block; selection method for index i in dual update (6) is unspecified.

- Recommend expanding discussion of Theorem 2 to relate sublinear convergence rates to known ADMM variants (e.g., via choices of K and J).

- Suggest providing guidance on practical strategies for splitting the transformation matrix A.

- Recommend adding discussion of applications like tensor completion and which ADMM variant may be optimal there.

- Minor typos to fix: missing space after "min" in equation (9) and extra space before "Section" on line 217.

- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, strong theoretical guarantees, clear links to existing methods, and promising experiments.

- Rejection concerns: lack of clarity in algorithm steps, limited discussion of convergence-rate relationships and A-splitting strategies, and missing application-specific insights.
