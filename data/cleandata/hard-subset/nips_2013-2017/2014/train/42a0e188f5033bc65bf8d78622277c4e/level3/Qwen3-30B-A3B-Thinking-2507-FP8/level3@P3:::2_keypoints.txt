- Introduces PDMM, a novel algorithm for block-separable convex optimization with linear constraints that generalizes ADMM to multiple blocks.  
- Replaces ADMM's sequential Gauss–Seidel updates with parallel Jacobian updates to enable true parallelism.  
- Proposes a novel dual backward step that reduces the dual step size and compensates for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates with constant step size, matching ADMM’s theoretical guarantees.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unified framework for augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Extends PDMM to handle overlapping groups via randomized block coordinate updates, useful for overlapping group lasso.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity under mild assumptions, with step-size conditions that account for constraint sparsity and number of blocks updated.  
- Empirical results show PDMM outperforms ADMM, sADMM, PJADMM, and RBSUMM on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Analyzes the effect of K (number of parallel blocks), showing a trade-off between per-iteration cost and convergence rate and an optimal K in practice.  
- Reviewer praises the dual backward step as an elegant, well-motivated theoretical contribution addressing the implicit dual-ascent issue.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, reproducible, with sound experimental design and proper baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: needs a more intuitive explanation of why the dual backward step enables convergence and reduces “aggressive” dual progress.  
- Weakness: the counterintuitive claim that parallel updates can match ADMM’s O(1/T) rate deserves more explicit discussion.  
- Weakness: some technical details in the convergence analysis could be made more accessible.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to distributed optimization; well-suited for NIPS and recommended for acceptance.
