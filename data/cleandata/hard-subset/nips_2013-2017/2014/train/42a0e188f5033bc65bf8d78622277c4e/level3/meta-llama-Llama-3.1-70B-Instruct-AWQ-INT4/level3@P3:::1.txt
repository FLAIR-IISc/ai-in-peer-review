This paper proposes a novel parallel randomized block coordinate method, named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. PDMM is an extension of the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) to multiple blocks, allowing for parallel updates of the primal blocks. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size, and demonstrate its effectiveness in two applications: robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed method. The technical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed proof of the convergence results. The experimental results are also convincing, showing that PDMM outperforms existing methods in the two applications.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to generalize several ADMM variants, including the two-block ADMM with splitting variables (sADMM) and the proximal Jacobian ADMM (PJADMM). The authors also provide a clear explanation of the role of the dual backward step and the sparsity of the matrix A in determining the step size of the dual update.

However, there are a few areas that require clarification. In Algorithm steps (5) and (6), it is unclear whether y^{t+1} and \hat{y}^{t+1} update all coordinates or a single block, and how the index i is selected. The authors should provide a clear explanation of these steps in the revision.

Additionally, in the proof of Theorem 2, a missing "\sum_{i=1}^I" should be added. The authors should also provide more discussion on Theorem 2, including its consistency with the convergence rates of known ADMM variants and guidance on optimal ways to split the transformation matrix A.

Furthermore, the authors should provide more insight into the experimental results, particularly for the tensor completion application with a multi-block structure. Which ADMM variant may be optimal in this case, and why?

Minor typos include a missing space after "min" in (9) and an extra space before "Section" at line 217.

Overall, I am positive and inclined to accept the paper. However, I recommend that the authors address the above-mentioned issues in the revision.
