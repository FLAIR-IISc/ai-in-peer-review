- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a novel dual backward step using an intermediate variable ŷ^t to enable conservative dual progress and ensure convergence for Jacobian (parallel) updates.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying theoretical framework for multi-block ADMM variants.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Derives step sizes that account for constraint matrix sparsity and number of randomized blocks, making PDMM adaptable to problem structure.  
- Empirically demonstrates faster convergence and runtime than ADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Analyzes the effect of the number of blocks K, showing parallel updates can yield speedups without losing convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: Dual backward step is a clever, novel solution to parallel ADMM convergence challenges.  
- Strength: Theoretical unification of existing methods clarifies the role of dual backward steps.  
- Strength: Matches ADMM convergence rates while enabling parallelism.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments showing clear empirical advantages and effective handling of overlapping blocks.  
- Weakness: Empirical advantages appear counterintuitive given typical slower convergence of Jacobian methods and need more explanation.  
- Weakness: Lacks explicit discussion of computational trade-offs between K and per-iteration cost, particularly for distributed implementations.  
- Weakness: Comparison with other parallel multi-block ADMM work (e.g., Deng et al.) is limited.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, technically sound, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Recommendation: High significance and originality; reviewer recommends acceptance but asks for more empirical explanation and broader method comparisons.
