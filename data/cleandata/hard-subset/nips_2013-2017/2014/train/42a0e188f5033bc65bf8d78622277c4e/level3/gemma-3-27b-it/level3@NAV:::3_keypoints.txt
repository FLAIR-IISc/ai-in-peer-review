- Introduces PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM updating K blocks per iteration with a “backward” dual update for multi-block linearly constrained convex problems.
- Random block selection and backward dual update together address convergence issues of fully parallel multi-block ADMM.
- Backward dual step is crucial for ensuring global convergence, often missing in other parallel ADMM implementations.
- Inclusion of a proximal term in primal updates increases flexibility and can simplify subproblems.
- Provides rigorous global convergence analysis and iteration complexity O(J/(T*K)) after T iterations.
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show faster convergence than sADMM, PJADMM, and GSADMM.
- Clarifies the relationship between PADMM, sADMM, and PJADMM and the role of the backward dual step.
- Major omission: no reported results for an actual parallel implementation demonstrating multi-core speedup.
- Practical drawback: PADMM requires tuning three parameters (K, ρ, proximal parameters), which may be less convenient than standard ADMM.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel parallelization approach, strong theoretical foundation, empirical improvements, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical parallel speedup evidence and increased parameter tuning complexity.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution worthy of acceptance, conditional on addressing the parallel implementation aspect in future work.
