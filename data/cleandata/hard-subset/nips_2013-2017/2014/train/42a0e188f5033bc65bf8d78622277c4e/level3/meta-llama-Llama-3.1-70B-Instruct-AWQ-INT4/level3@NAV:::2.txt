This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors introduce a parallel randomized block coordinate method that replaces the traditional Gauss-Seidel updates with Jacobian updates, which allows for more efficient parallelization. They also propose a dual backward step that compensates for the limited information propagation in Jacobian updates, effectively reducing the dual step size.

The paper establishes the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM, showing that it converges at a rate of O(1/T) with a constant step size. The authors also demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM through experiments on robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso, showing that it outperforms existing methods.

The strengths of this paper include:

* The proposal of a novel algorithm that addresses the limitations of existing methods, such as ADMM and its variants.
* The establishment of global convergence and iteration complexity results, which provide a solid theoretical foundation for the algorithm.
* The demonstration of the algorithm's effectiveness through experiments on real-world problems.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper assumes that the blocks are separable, which may not always be the case in practice. It would be interesting to see how the algorithm performs on non-separable problems.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm, which would be useful for understanding its scalability.
* The experiments are limited to two specific problems, and it would be beneficial to see how the algorithm performs on a wider range of problems.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning, and the proposed algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable.
