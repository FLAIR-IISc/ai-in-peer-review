- Proposes PDMM, a novel parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM for multi-block linearly constrained convex problems.  
- PDMM randomly updates K primal blocks in parallel and introduces a dual backward step to ensure convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity under constant step size.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unified framework.  
- Demonstrates PDMM can handle overlapping group lasso, resolving an open problem for randomized block updates with overlapping blocks.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with step-size selection accounting for matrix sparsity and number of blocks.  
- Improves over prior randomized methods (e.g., RBSUMM) by using constant rather than decreasing step sizes.  
- Empirical results (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) show faster convergence and better scalability than state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Strengths: strong technical contribution, unifying framework, careful convergence analysis, and thorough experiments.  
- Clarity issues: Algorithm (5)–(7) is ambiguous about whether dual updates apply to all dual blocks or a single block and how index i is chosen.  
- Technical correction needed: missing “∑_{i=1}^I” in the proof of Theorem 2.  
- Recommend expanding discussion of Theorem 2’s implications, showing reductions to known ADMM variants (e.g., K=1, J=2) and advising how to split matrix A in practice.  
- Suggest adding tensor completion experiments to compare PDMM, sADMM, and PJADMM in naturally multi-block settings.  
- Fits NIPS scope and motivates important ML applications; meets evaluation criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) at a high level.  
- Main weaknesses (dual-update ambiguity and proof typo) are fixable and do not undermine the paper’s contributions; recommend acceptance.
