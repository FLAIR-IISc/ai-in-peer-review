- Problem: Minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints — parallelizing multi-block ADMM is challenging.  
- Main contribution: PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with a "dual backward step" to enable convergence.  
- Convergence result: Provable global convergence and an O(1/T) iteration complexity matching standard ADMM.  
- Unification: PDMM subsumes existing parallel ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) and clarifies the role of primal/dual step sizes.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM is competitive or superior in runtime to GSADMM and RBSUMM.  
- Strength — significance/originality: Novel, elegant solution addressing Jacobian-style instability via the dual backward step.  
- Strength — theoretical quality: Rigorous analysis with strong convergence and rate results under standard assumptions.  
- Strength — clarity/unification: Good positioning within augmented Lagrangian literature and explanation of related methods.  
- Strength — empirical execution: Well-designed experiments that convincingly demonstrate practical performance.  
- Weakness — counterintuitive results: Single-block randomized PDMM1 sometimes needs fewer iterations than sequential GSADMM; requires deeper explanation.  
- Weakness — parameter intuition: Need clearer, more intuitive explanation for step-size choices (τi, νi) and their dependence on number of parallel blocks K.  
- Weakness — parallel claims: Experiments run sequentially; practical speedup claims should be supported by true parallel/multi-core implementations.  
- Overall: Strong, novel, and theoretically sound paper with valuable unification and empirical support; recommend acceptance but ask for deeper discussion of surprising iteration-count behavior.
