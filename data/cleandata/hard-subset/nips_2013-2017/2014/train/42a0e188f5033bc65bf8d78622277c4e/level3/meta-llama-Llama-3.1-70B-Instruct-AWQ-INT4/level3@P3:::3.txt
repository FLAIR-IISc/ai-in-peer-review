This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size. The experimental results show that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The main contribution of this paper is the proposal of PDMM, which is a parallel randomized block coordinate variant of ADMM. The authors show that PDMM can solve a fairly large class of problems, including nonsmooth functions. The method has three distinct aspects: a backward step on the dual update, the sparsity of A, and the number of randomized blocks are taken into consideration to determine the step size of the dual update.

The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM under fairly simple assumptions. The theoretical results show that PDMM converges at the same rate as ADMM and its variants. The experimental results demonstrate that PDMM performs better than existing methods in two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The strengths of this paper are:

* The proposal of PDMM, which is a parallel randomized block coordinate variant of ADMM.
* The establishment of global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size.
* The experimental results show that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in two applications.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

* The lack of reported results for a parallel implementation of PDMM.
* The tuning of three PDMM parameters is less convenient compared to traditional ADMM.

Overall, this paper is an interesting and well-written contribution with solid theoretical and empirical support. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its connections to related work. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in solving optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints.
