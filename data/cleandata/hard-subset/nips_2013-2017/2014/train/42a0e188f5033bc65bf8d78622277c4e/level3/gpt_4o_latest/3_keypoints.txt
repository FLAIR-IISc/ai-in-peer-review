- PDMM: a novel randomized parallel algorithm generalizing ADMM for multi-block linear constraints with a backward dual step, proximal primal option, and randomized block updates.
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and iteration complexity with rate O(J/(T K)) when K of J blocks are updated per iteration.
- Backward dual update: novel mechanism that stabilizes updates and ensures global convergence versus direct Jacobi updates.
- Proximal primal term: allows inexact primal updates and broadens applicability.
- Parallelization: supports full parallelization of the primal step and permits larger step sizes than sADMM, PJADMM, and GSADMM.
- Empirical results: RPCA and overlapping group lasso experiments show reduced computation time and competitive performance.
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear algorithm description, theory, experiments, and connections to existing methods.
- No parallel implementation results reported, limiting evaluation of real-world scalability and efficiency.
- Parameter tuning: introduces three tunable parameters (τ_i, ν_i, η_j), increasing user burden compared to traditional ADMM.
- Limited experiments: scope restricted to two applications; broader benchmarks would strengthen empirical claims.
- GSADMM comparison: PDMM has better runtime but GSADMM converges in fewer iterations; the runtime-vs-iteration trade-off needs deeper discussion.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical and practical contribution, robust convergence, and solid empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of parallel implementation results and need for extensive parameter tuning reduce practical usability.
- Recommendation: accept the paper, with a suggestion to include parallel implementation results in future work.
