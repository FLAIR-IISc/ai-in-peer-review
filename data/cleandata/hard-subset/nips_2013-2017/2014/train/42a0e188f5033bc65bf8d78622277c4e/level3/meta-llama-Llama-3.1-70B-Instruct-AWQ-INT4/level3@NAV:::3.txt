This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size. The paper also presents empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso, demonstrating that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in terms of convergence rate and runtime.

The main idea of PDMM is to randomly update some blocks in parallel, behaving like parallel randomized block coordinate descent. The authors introduce a backward step on the dual update to make the dual variable make conservative progress. They also consider the sparsity of A and the number of randomized blocks to determine the step size of the dual update.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical guarantees. The empirical results are promising, and the authors provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

Weaknesses:
