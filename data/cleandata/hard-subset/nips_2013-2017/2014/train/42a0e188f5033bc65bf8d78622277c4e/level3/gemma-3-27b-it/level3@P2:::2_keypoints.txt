- Proposes PDMM: a parallel, randomized multi-block extension of ADMM with a novel "dual backward step" to improve convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority on Robust PCA and overlapping group lasso against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Builds on ADMM and multi-block ADMM literature, correctly identifying divergence issues with purely parallel (Jacobian) updates.  
- Dual backward step is a clever mechanism to control dual updates and prevent overly aggressive progress.  
- Shows PDMM encompasses sADMM and PJADMM as special cases, clarifying relationships among methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and motivates the algorithm design.  
- Extension to overlapping block problems addresses scenarios where standard block coordinate descent fails.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, though some notation (e.g., K̃i) lacks explicit definition.  
- Experiments are limited in scope (only two applications), reducing generality of empirical claims.  
- Lacks detailed comparison of per-iteration computational cost and runtime, important given the parallel design.  
- Insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity (τi, νi) and how step sizes affect performance across scenarios.  
- Choice of parameters for baseline methods (e.g., RBSUMM) is not fully detailed, raising fairness concerns.  
- Occasional heavy technical jargon may hinder accessibility for readers less familiar with ADMM literature.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with novel contribution and solid theory; accept with minor revisions to address computational cost analysis and parameter sensitivity.
