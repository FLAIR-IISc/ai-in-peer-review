- Proposes PDMM, a novel algorithm that randomly updates K primal blocks in parallel and uses a backward dual step for convergence.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with a constant step size.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithm description and theoretical guarantees.  
- Algorithm steps (5) and (6) are unclear; authors should clarify how indices i and j are selected.  
- Minor typo in Theorem 2 proof: a missing summation symbol should be added.  
- Require more discussion on the implications of Theorem 2 and how PDMM’s convergence rate compares to known ADMM variants.  
- Need guidance on how to optimally split the transformation matrix A, which is crucial for the method.
