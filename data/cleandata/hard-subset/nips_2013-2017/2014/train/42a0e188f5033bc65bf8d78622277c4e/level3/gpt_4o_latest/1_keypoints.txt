- The paper proposes PDMM, a novel randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant with a backward dual step and proves global convergence and sublinear iteration complexity.  
- PDMM connects to and generalizes existing ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM), providing theoretical insight into the broader method family.  
- The method is practically relevant for large-scale, multi-block optimization by enabling parallel primal updates.  
- Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods in runtime and scalability.  
- Novelty: the backward dual-step is a well-motivated innovation that tempers aggressive dual updates in traditional ADMM.  
- Algorithm description (eqs. 5–6) is unclear about which dual blocks are updated and how blocks are selected, harming reproducibility.  
- Notation error in Theorem 2 (missing summation “∑_{i=1}^I”) reduces clarity and polish.  
- Theorem 2 lacks discussion on consistency with convergence rates of other ADMM variants and guidance on optimal splitting of A for implementation.  
- Comparative analysis could be strengthened by evaluating tensor completion problems to further validate multi-block applicability.  
- Minor typographical issues: missing space in eq. (9) and extra space before “Section” on line 217.  
- Recommendation: positive accept, with a strong request to clarify algorithm steps and expand discussion of Theorem 2 and practical implementation choices.
