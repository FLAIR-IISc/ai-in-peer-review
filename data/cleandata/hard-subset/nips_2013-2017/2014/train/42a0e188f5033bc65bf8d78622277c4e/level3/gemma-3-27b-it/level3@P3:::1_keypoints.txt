- Proposes PDMM: a randomized block-coordinate ADMM extension with a backward dual step for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under mild assumptions and a sublinear O(1/T) rate.  
- Establishes PDMM as a unifying framework connecting to sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Technically sound with detailed theoretical analysis and full proofs in the supplement.  
- Experimental results are promising but limited in scope.  
- Well-motivated for large-scale optimization and builds on established ADMM literature.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with a clear introduction and related work.  
- Steps (5) and (6) of the algorithm are unclear: whether y^{t+1} and hat{y}^{t+1} update all coordinates or only selected blocks.  
- The selection rule for index i in step (6) is not specified (all rows or subset?).  
- Missing “\sum_{i=1}^I” in the proof of Theorem 2 — a correction is needed.  
- Theorem 2 needs more discussion: show recovering rates of known ADMM variants via K and J choices.  
- Provide guidance on optimally splitting matrix A to leverage PDMM given A’s sparsity structure.  
- Experimental evaluation should be expanded, e.g., include tensor completion comparisons due to natural multi-block structure.  
- Clarify which ADMM variant is preferable for tensor completion and why.  
- PDMM’s originality stems from combining randomized coordinate descent, a backward dual step, and unifying ADMM variants.  
- Generalizability to overlapping blocks and potential for parallelization increase practical significance.  
- Recommendations to fix minor typos (space after “min” in eq. (9), extra space before “Section” on line 217).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theoretical guarantees, promising experiments, and clear connections to prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear algorithm steps, limited experiments, and need for deeper theoretical discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: inclined to accept pending clarifications and expanded experiments.
