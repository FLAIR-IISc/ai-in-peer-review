- Introduces PDMM, an algorithm for minimizing block-separable convex functions with linear constraints using Jacobian (parallel) updates.  
- Extends ADMM from two blocks to multiple blocks by replacing Gauss-Seidel updates with Jacobian updates.  
- Key innovation: a dual backward step that reduces dual step size to compensate for limited information propagation in Jacobian updates.  
- The authors prove global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM, matching ADMM’s rate.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, providing a unifying framework for these methods.  
- Experiments on RPCA and overlapping group lasso validate PDMM’s applicability to real problems.  
- Notes that Jacobian (parallel) methods generally converge slower per iteration than Gauss-Seidel methods like GSADMM.  
- Strength: Strong technical contribution with rigorous proofs and clear presentation of the dual backward step.  
- Strength: Unifies existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) under PDMM, offering theoretical insight.  
- Strength: Practical relevance demonstrated through experiments on representative problems.  
- Weakness: Empirical performance is mixed—slower convergence per iteration compared to Gauss-Seidel methods.  
- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity (step sizes τi, νi) may require careful tuning, limiting ease of use.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental scope—only two applications tested, reducing evidence of broad applicability.  
- Weakness: Parallelism not demonstrated—experiments were sequential, so parallel advantages are not empirically shown.  
- Pro: Significant theoretical advance and useful unification; robust convergence guarantees support acceptance.  
- Con: Empirical shortcomings, parameter tuning needs, and lack of parallel implementation weaken the practical case.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking authors to address parameter sensitivity and provide additional (ideally parallel) experimental results.
