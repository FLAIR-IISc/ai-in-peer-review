- Introduces PDMM: a randomized, parallel extension of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with a dual backward step to ensure convergence.  
- Randomly selects K blocks per iteration for simultaneous updates, addressing instability in multi-block ADMM.  
- Positions PDMM within multi-block ADMM literature, unifying Gauss-Seidel and Jacobian variants and showing sADMM/PJADMM as special cases.  
- Extends applicability to overlapping block coordinate descent problems.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical results: global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)).  
- Novelty: randomized block selection plus dual backward step tackles a known instability and enables parallelism.  
- Strong theoretical analysis gives a solid foundation for algorithm behavior.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with insightful connections to existing methods.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show improved performance over some existing methods.  
- Missing experimental demonstration of actual parallel speedups to validate claimed parallelizability.  
- Introduces additional tuning parameters (K, τi, νi) and reliance on Bregman divergence choices, increasing practical complexity.  
- Lacks comparison to highly optimized, problem-specific solvers for the evaluated applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically promising, unifying framework, handles overlapping blocks.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no parallel speedup validation, increased parameter-tuning burden, limited solver comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting parallel speedup experiments and more discussion on practical parameter tuning.
