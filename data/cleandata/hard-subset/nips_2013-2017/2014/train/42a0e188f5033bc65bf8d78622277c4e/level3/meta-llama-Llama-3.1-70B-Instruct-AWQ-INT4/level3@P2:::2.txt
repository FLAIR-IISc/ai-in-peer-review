This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), for minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors replace the traditional Gauss-Seidel (sequential, two-block) primal updates in Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) with Jacobian (parallel, multi-block) updates. They also introduce a dual backward step that compensates for the limited information propagation in Jacobian updates by effectively reducing the dual step size.

The authors establish the global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size, similar to ADMM. They also show that two existing Jacobian ADMM methods, sADMM and PJADMM, are special cases of PDMM, clarifying the relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.

The experimental results demonstrate the empirical effectiveness of PDMM compared to ADMM and other variants. However, the reviewer finds the results counterintuitive because Jacobian methods typically converge slower than Gauss-Seidel methods. The authors' introduction of the dual backward step is novel and key for achieving convergence under general conditions.

* The paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, PDMM, which provides additional alternatives and possibilities for distributed optimization.
