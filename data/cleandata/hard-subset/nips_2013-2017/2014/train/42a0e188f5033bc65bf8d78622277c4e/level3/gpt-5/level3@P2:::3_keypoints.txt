- Proposes PDMM: a randomized, parallel ADMM variant that updates K blocks per iteration and applies a backward-corrected dual ascent to prevent Jacobi-style divergence.  
- PDMM primal updates use augmented Lagrangian plus optional Bregman/proximal terms; dual step sizes τi and backward factors νi depend on K and row-block sparsity di.  
- Authors prove global convergence and an ergodic O(1/T) rate with constants scaling as J/K, so more parallelism improves convergence constants.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are extreme cases of PDMM, unifying existing ADMM variants conceptually.  
- Extends to overlapping composite problems (e.g., overlapping group lasso) where classical randomized BCD struggles.  

- Strength: clear diagnosis of implicit dual ascent and motivation for the backward dual step.  
- Strength: flexible framework supporting exact/inexact updates, arbitrary K, and overlapping groups via equality constraints.  
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees with explicit dependence on K and sparsity di.  
- Strength: practical step-size rules that exploit row sparsity to allow larger steps on sparse problems.  
- Strength: empirical results (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) show faster attainment of target accuracy than sADMM and RBSUMM, and competitive wall-clock time versus GSADMM.  

- Weakness: no parallel/distributed implementation reported, so claimed scalability and communication costs are unvalidated.  
- Weakness: introduces parameters τi, νi, ηj that may require practitioner tuning; default rules exist but sensitivity/adaptivity guidance is limited.  
- Weakness: empirical comparisons omit a tuned PJADMM baseline for overlapping problems and lack larger-scale/distributed experiments.  
- Weakness: heavy notation and presentation; needs a concise algorithm box and clearer practical guidance for setting τi, νi when di varies.  

- Request: clarify how di and K̃i are computed in practice for large sparse A and how they change under reformulations (e.g., overlapping groups).  
- Request: provide a short table of recommended ψjt/ϕjt choices for common losses/regularizers in the inexact variant.  
- Suggestion: add a corollary making the O(J/(K T)) dependence explicit for uniform-di cases to improve readability.  
- Suggestion: include an ablation for overlapping group lasso comparing proposed step sizes to sADMM step sizes.  
- Question: consider adaptive scaling of the backward step (e.g., residual balancing) to reduce tuning.  
- Engineering note: discuss cost and strategy for forming/maintaining Ax and partial sums across blocks, since PDMM relies on reusing sums.  

- Quality assessment: technically sound with clear derivations and proofs; experiments are careful though sequential.  
- Clarity assessment: generally well-written with good intuition, but notation density calls for a concise algorithmic summary.  
- Originality assessment: novel backward dual step in randomized Jacobi ADMM and explicit exploitation of sparsity for dual steps; insightful unification of variants.  
- Significance assessment: addresses a known limitation of multi-block ADMM and offers a practical, parallelizable alternative likely useful for structured large-scale convex problems.  

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a timely problem with a principled fix, solid theory, practical implications, and unifying insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks parallel experiments, has parameter tuning complexity, and needs broader experimental baselines.  

- Overall evaluation: an interesting, well-executed contribution leaning toward acceptance, with recommended minor clarifications and ideally added parallel experiments.
