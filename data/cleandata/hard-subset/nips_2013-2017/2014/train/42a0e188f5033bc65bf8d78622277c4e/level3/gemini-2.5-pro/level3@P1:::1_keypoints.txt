- Addresses multi-block convex optimization with linear constraints and proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate extension of ADMM.  
- PDMM introduces a dual "backward step" and sparsity-/K-aware step sizes to ensure convergence of parallel updates.  
- Provides global convergence proof and O(1/T) iteration complexity for constant step sizes (covers exact and inexact primal updates).  
- Unifies existing parallel ADMM variants by showing sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.  
- Empirical validation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows strong performance versus baselines.  
- Strength: novel and significant contribution enabling principled parallelism for multi-block ADMM with convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis that appears technically sound and comprehensive.  
- Strength: conceptual unification of prior methods clarifies the role of dual updates.  
- Strength: practical relevance for scalable, distributed machine-learning solvers.  
- Weakness: algorithm description (eqs. 5–7) is ambiguous about whether dual blocks y_i are all updated each iteration or only corresponding ones.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 needs more discussion—explicit reductions to known rates for sADMM/PJADMM would strengthen the unification claim.  
- Weakness: dependence on block count J suggests partitioning affects performance; need practical guidance on how to split A.  
- Weakness: broader application scope (e.g., tensor completion/decomposition) not discussed; guidance on preferred PDMM variants for large-block problems would help.  
- Minor technical issue: missing summation ∑_{i=1}^I in the supplementary proof of Theorem 2.  
- Minor typos: missing space after "argmin" (Line 115) and extra space before "Section" (Line 217).  
- Overall recommendation: positive, lean towards acceptance; clarifications and expanded discussion would further strengthen the paper.
