- Introduces PDMM, a method for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints using a novel "dual backward step" to enable parallel (Jacobian) updates.  
- Addresses the known multi-block ADMM divergence issue by reducing dual step size via the dual backward step.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity theoretically.  
- Shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying related methods.  
- Provides empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso where PDMM outperforms other ADMM variants.  
- Highlights PDMM’s strong parallelism potential for large-scale optimization.  
- Places the contribution well within existing ADMM literature and cites key prior work.  
- Strength: the dual backward step is a novel, effective idea addressing parallel-update convergence.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis comparable to ADMM’s complexity guarantees.  
- Strength: clarifying connections to prior algorithms aids understanding of the field.  
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrates practical effectiveness on tested problems.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to parameter choices (τi, νi) — parameter tuning needs deeper study.  
- Weakness: notation and some theoretical explanations are dense and could be clearer.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to two applications; broader evaluation is needed.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with state-of-the-art first-order methods (e.g., proximal gradient).  
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically sound and practically promising paper; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
