- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for multi-block linearly constrained convex problems, addressing gaps in multi-block ADMM generalizations.  
- PDMM updates K primal blocks in parallel (randomly selected), uses a backward dual step, and proves sublinear convergence with constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM generalizes several ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) and can handle overlapping blocks unlike RBCD.  
- The backward dual step (eq. 7) is a key insight explaining why direct Jacobi updates often fail and how to control dual updates.  
- Theorem 1 proves global convergence; Theorem 2 establishes O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.  
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods in runtime when parallel updates are available.  
- Paper is generally well-written, technically sound, and makes a significant contribution to distributed/parallel optimization.  
- Clarification needed: whether updates (steps 5 and 6) apply to all dual coordinates or a single block, and how index i is selected—critical for reproducibility.  
- Proof issue: missing “∑_{i=1}^I” in Theorem 2’s proof needs correction for proper indexing.  
- Suggest expanding Theorem 2 to explicitly show reductions to known ADMM rates for specific parameter choices (e.g., K=J for sADMM, η_j>0 for PJADMM).  
- Practical guidance lacking: how to optimally split matrix A and choose block sizes, and which ADMM variant is best for tensor completion applications.  
- Minor typos: missing space after “min” in eq. (9) and extra space before “Section” at line 217.  
- Overall recommendation: accept after clarifying algorithm description and expanding theoretical/practical guidance.
