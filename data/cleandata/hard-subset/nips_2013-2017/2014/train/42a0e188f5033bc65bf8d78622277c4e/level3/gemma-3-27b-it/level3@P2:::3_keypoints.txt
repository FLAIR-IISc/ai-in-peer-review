- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel extension of ADMM that updates a random subset of blocks in parallel and uses a backward dual step for stability.
- Provides theoretical guarantees including global convergence and iteration complexity O(J/(T K)).
- Empirical results show promising performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, outperforming sADMM and RBSUMM in experiments.
- Situates PDMM within ADMM literature and differentiates it from GSADMM, Jacobian variants (sADMM, PJADMM), and RBSUMM.
- Shows that sADMM and PJADMM can be viewed as special cases of the broader PDMM framework.
- Randomized block selection and backward dual step are key innovations addressing multi-block convergence issues.
- Strong theoretical analysis gives a solid foundation for algorithm behavior.
- Clear, concise writing makes technical details accessible.
- Assumptions required for theoretical guarantees (Assumption 1) need more discussion and guidance for practical verification.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to two applications and lacks comparisons with a broader set of competing methods.
- No true parallel implementation is presented; experiments are sequential, limiting scalability assessment.
- Tuning three PDMM parameters may be inconvenient and performance sensitivity to these parameters is not analyzed.
- Notation could be improved for consistency in subscripts and superscripts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound parallelization of ADMM with strong guarantees and promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing parallel implementation, limited empirical breadth, potential parameter sensitivity, and practical validity of assumptions.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address parallel implementation, broader evaluation, and discussion of theoretical assumptions.
