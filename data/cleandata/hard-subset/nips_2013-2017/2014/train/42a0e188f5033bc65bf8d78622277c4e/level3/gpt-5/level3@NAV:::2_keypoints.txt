- Proposal: PDMM replaces ADMM’s Gauss–Seidel primal updates with parallel Jacobian updates and introduces a dual backward step to shrink the effective dual step size.  
- Theory: Authors prove global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic rates with step sizes explicitly calibrated by the number of updated blocks K and row-block sparsity di.  
- Connections: Supplement shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases, clarifying relationships among augmented-Lagrangian decomposition methods.  
- Experiments: RPCA and overlapping group lasso experiments demonstrate favorable performance relative to ADMM variants.  
- Analysis quality: Technical analysis appears sound under standard convex assumptions; dependence on K and di is thoughtfully handled.  
- Missing intuition: More discussion is needed on why Jacobian (parallel) updates do not suffer the usual slower convergence versus Gauss–Seidel.  
- Ablation need: The dual backward step seems crucial; an ablation study (varying νi and τi, including νi=0) is required to isolate its effect.  
- Tuning guidance: Practical guidance and sensitivity analysis for parameters (τi, νi, ηjt) beyond formula (8) are missing.  
- Parallelism claims: Experiments are largely sequential; true multi-core/distributed wall-clock speedups (especially for sparse A where di is small) are needed to support parallelism claims.  
- Clarity: Notation is heavy; a concise pseudocode box with all parameters would improve readability.  
- y-hat intuition: More intuition is needed for the y-hat “backward step” (e.g., viewing implicit dual ascent in primal subproblems).  
- Lyapunov details: Clarify constants in the Lyapunov function and provide intuition for the choices of Pt and Q.  
- Originality: The dual backward step is novel and insightful; unifying sADMM and PJADMM as extremes is valuable.  
- Significance: The method is a practically useful, theoretically justified alternative for distributed optimization with overlapping structures.  
- Pros summarized: Novel dual backward step enabling parallel multi-block ADMM with O(1/T); unifying view connecting sADMM and PJADMM; clear dependence on sparsity and K; solid empirical results.  
- Cons summarized: Limited intuition for Jacobian+backward step performance; missing ablations and sensitivity analyses; no true parallel/distributed runtime evaluations; heavy notation and some deferred proofs.
