- Core idea: Proposes PDMM, combining randomized block coordinate descent with ADMM, updating K of J blocks in parallel and adding a "dual backward step" to ensure convergence.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proves global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size; provides rules for τi and νi based on block sparsity and K.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions PDMM to subsume methods like sADMM and PJADMM as special cases and handles overlapping blocks via reformulation.  
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show PDMM often faster in wall-clock time than GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM, even when run sequentially.  
- Clarity and exposition: Paper is well-written and organized; Section 2 gives clear intuition for the dual backward step, sparsity effects, and randomization.  
- Weakness — lack of parallel experiments: No multi-core or distributed experiments provided to validate the algorithm’s central parallel scalability claim.  
- Weakness — parameter tuning: Introduces K (number of blocks to update) as a critical hyperparameter with limited guidance; K strongly affects performance.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel contribution with solid theory and convincing sequential results; reviewer recommends acceptance despite missing parallel validation.
