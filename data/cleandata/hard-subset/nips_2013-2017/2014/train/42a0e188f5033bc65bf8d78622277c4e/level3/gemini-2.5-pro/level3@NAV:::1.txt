This paper addresses the important and challenging problem of extending the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) to multi-block convex optimization problems, where direct generalizations are known to be problematic. The authors propose the Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), a novel randomized block coordinate method. The key ideas are to randomly update K primal blocks in parallel at each iteration and to introduce a corrective backward step in the dual update. The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation, proving sublinear O(1/T) convergence. A significant contribution is showing that PDMM provides a unified framework that generalizes several existing ADMM variants, such as sADMM and PJADMM, offering a deeper understanding of their connections.

**Strengths:**
*   **Significance and Originality:** The unification of Jacobian ADMM variants under a single framework is an elegant and important contribution. PDMM provides a flexible and parallelizable algorithm for a wide class of problems, including those with overlapping blocks, which is a significant advance over prior work.
*   **Technical Quality:** The theoretical analysis appears sound, establishing global convergence with a constant step size under standard assumptions. The convergence rate is consistent with the state of the art for first-order methods in this class.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso demonstrate that PDMM is competitive with and often outperforms existing methods, validating the practical utility of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**
While this is a strong paper, several points should be addressed to improve its clarity and impact:
1.  **Clarity of Algorithm:** The description of the algorithm updates in steps (5) and (6) is ambiguous. It is unclear whether the dual variables `y^{t+1}` and `ŷ^{t+1}` update all coordinates or just a single block `i`. The paper should explicitly clarify how index `i` is selected or if the update applies to all `i=1,...,I`.
2.  **Expanded Discussion:** The implications of Theorem 2 could be better highlighted. The authors should explicitly show how their general rate recovers the known convergence rates of specific ADMM variants by choosing parameters (e.g., K, J). Furthermore, some practical guidance on optimal ways to split the transformation matrix A would be valuable for practitioners.
3.  **Experimental Scope:** The paper could be strengthened by discussing or providing an empirical comparison for tensor completion applications, which naturally fit the multi-block structure. Insights into which ADMM variant might be optimal in that setting would be beneficial.
4.  **Minor Corrections:** The proof of Theorem 2 appears to be missing a summation sign ("\sum_{i=1}^I"). Additionally, there are minor typos: a missing space after "min" in equation (9) and an extra space before "Section" at line 217.

Overall, this is a high-quality submission with significant theoretical and practical contributions. I am positive about this paper and recommend acceptance, provided the authors address the requested clarifications and expand the discussion in a revised version.
