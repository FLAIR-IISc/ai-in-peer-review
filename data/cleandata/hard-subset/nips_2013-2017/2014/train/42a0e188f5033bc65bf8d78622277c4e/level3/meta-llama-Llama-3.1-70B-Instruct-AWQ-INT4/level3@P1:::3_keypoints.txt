- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel variant of ADMM that updates multiple blocks in parallel.  
- Main contribution: parallel block updates improve computational efficiency over traditional ADMM.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including global convergence and iteration complexity.  
- Empirical evaluations on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso demonstrate PDMM’s practical effectiveness.  
- Shows PDMM can achieve similar convergence rates to ADMM while enabling parallelism.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent mathematical notation and clear explanations.  
- Includes a detailed comparison with existing ADMM methods, highlighting advantages and disadvantages.  
- Lack of reported results for a parallel implementation — no empirical evidence of actual parallel speedups.  
- Tuning three PDMM parameters is less convenient than traditional ADMM, posing usability concerns.  
- Original and significant contribution to optimization, especially for large-scale problems.  
- Potential to impact the field and motivate further research into parallel ADMM variants.  
- May be difficult for readers unfamiliar with ADMM and its variants to follow.
