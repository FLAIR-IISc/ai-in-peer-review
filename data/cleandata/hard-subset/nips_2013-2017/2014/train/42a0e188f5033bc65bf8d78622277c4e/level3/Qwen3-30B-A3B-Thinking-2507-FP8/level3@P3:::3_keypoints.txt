- Proposes PADMM, a randomized parallel ADMM variant for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a backward dual step that makes the dual update more conservative to enable convergence when K blocks are updated in parallel.  
- Proves global convergence and O(J/(T K)) iteration complexity with constant step size (J = number of blocks, T = iterations, K = blocks per iteration).  
- Shows PADMM generalizes existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM), clarifying their relationships.  
- Addresses a key gap in multi-block ADMM by resolving divergence of direct Jacobi updates while enabling parallel primal updates.  
- Leverages constraint matrix sparsity to choose larger step sizes than competing methods.  
- Handles overlapping group structures in a randomized fashion, addressing an open problem for standard randomized BCD.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with rigorous analysis and comprehensive convergence proofs.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show faster attainment of target accuracy versus sADMM, RBSUMM, GSADMM.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Main weaknesses: no demonstrated parallel implementation or measured parallel speedup.  
- Main weaknesses: requires tuning three parameters (τi, νi, ηjt), with limited guidance on practical selection.  
- Novelty: backward dual step is a significant technical innovation resolving fundamental convergence issues.  
- Significance: enables full parallelization of the primal step while matching ADMM convergence rates, offering practical benefits for large-scale optimization.
