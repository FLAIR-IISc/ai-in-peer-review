- Introduces PDMM: randomly updates K primal blocks in parallel with a novel backward dual update for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Establishes global convergence and sublinear iteration complexity, and shows many ADMM variants are special cases of PDMM.  
- Empirical results on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso show competitive or superior performance to state-of-the-art.  
- Addresses a significant, practical problem in large-scale optimization and multi-block ADMM extensions.  
- Theoretical analysis is a major strength, providing rigorous convergence guarantees and connections to existing methods.  
- Presentation of algorithm steps (5)–(6) is unclear: ambiguous whether y^{t+1}_i and \hat{y}^{t+1}_i update all dual coordinates or a single block, and how index i relates to randomly chosen blocks Jt.  
- Minor technical error in Theorem 2 proof: a missing “∑_{i=1}^I” term in the summation.  
- Recommend expanding discussion around Theorem 2 to show how its convergence rate recovers known ADMM variants (e.g., sADMM, PJADMM) via parameter choices (K, J).  
- Suggest adding practical guidance on how to split the transformation matrix A (e.g., based on sparsity or multiplication cost).  
- Empirical evaluation could be broadened—propose experiments on tensor completion to demonstrate multi-block versatility and compare ADMM variants for that task.  
- Minor stylistic issues: missing space after "min" in Eq. (9) and an extra space before "Section" on line 217.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of PDMM, strong theoretical soundness, empirical validation, and generality unifying ADMM variants.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues in algorithm description, insufficient theoretical discussion for practitioners, and limited empirical scope.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing clarity, theoretical discussion, empirical breadth, and the minor proof correction.
