- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel block-coordinate primal updates with a novel “dual backward step” for block-separable convex problems under linear constraints.
- Dual-backward step tempers implicit dual ascent from the augmented Lagrangian, with step sizes τi, νi set using matrix sparsity (di) and number of updated blocks K.
- Proves global convergence and an O(1/T) ergodic convergence rate with constant step sizes.
- Shows PJADMM and sADMM are extreme cases of PDMM, unifying multi-block ADMM variants in one framework.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso problems.
- Analysis is careful and well-motivated; step sizes are derived and proofs align with ADMM-class results.
- Sensitivity to τi, νi and to K is only partially characterized; practical guidance for choosing them is limited.
- Rate is the standard O(1/T) in expectation; nonergodic or accelerated rates are not explored.
- Baseline comparisons may be unfairly tuned (e.g., RBSUMM) and lack broader recent stochastic/variance-reduced methods.
- Notation is heavy and some terms (e.g., Q, Pt) lack intuition; there are minor typos (“seperable”, “defiend”).
- Original contributions include the sparsity-aware dual-backward step and randomized handling of overlapping blocks.
- PDMM addresses an important gap for provable multi-block parallel ADMM and is likely useful for large sparse problems.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to sequential Matlab runs; demonstrating multi-threaded or distributed speedups would strengthen impact.
- Overall positive acceptance arguments: principled unification, convergence guarantees, and solid empirical performance; negatives: limited runtime scaling evidence, standard convergence rate, and practical tuning/benchmarking gaps.
