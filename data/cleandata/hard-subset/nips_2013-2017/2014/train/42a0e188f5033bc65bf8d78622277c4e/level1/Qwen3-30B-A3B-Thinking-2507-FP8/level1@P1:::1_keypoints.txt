- Proposes PDMM: a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity under constant step size.  
- Connects PDMM to existing methods (sADMM, PJADMM) showing they are special cases.  
- Introduces a dual backward step whose scaling depends on sparsity of A.  
- Addresses overlapping group problems and randomized block updates for overlapping blocks.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and builds on established ADMM theory.  
- Proof techniques and step-size choices are sound but could use more intuition.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible.  
- PDMM is conceptually novel and generalizes prior multi-block ADMM approaches.  
- Significant for large-scale ML due to parallelization and handling overlapping structures.  
- Limited explanation of why the step-size scaling (τi, νi) tied to A’s sparsity is optimal.  
- Experimental comparison with RBSUMM is narrow (only ρ=1) and lacks broader tuning studies.  
- Missing comparisons to some more recent ADMM variants (e.g., cited [9, 27]).  
- Contribution may be viewed as incremental by some given active prior work on multi-block ADMM.  
- Does not thoroughly address PDMM limitations when A’s sparsity structure is unfavorable.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — solid theory, clear presentation, and convincing empirical gains.
