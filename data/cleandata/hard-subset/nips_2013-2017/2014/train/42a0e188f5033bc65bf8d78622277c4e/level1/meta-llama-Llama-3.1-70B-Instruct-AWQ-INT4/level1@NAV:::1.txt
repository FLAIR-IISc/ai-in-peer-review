This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve the problem of minimizing block-separable convex functions subject to linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of PDMM with constant step size.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, related work, and their proposed method. The technical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide detailed proofs of their results in the supplement.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors propose a novel algorithm that can solve a wide range of problems, including nonsmooth functions.
* The algorithm is parallelizable, which makes it suitable for large-scale problems.
* The authors establish the global convergence and iteration complexity of the algorithm, which provides a theoretical guarantee of its performance.
* The experimental results show that PDMM performs better than existing methods in two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The algorithm requires careful tuning of parameters, such as the step size and the number of blocks to be updated.
* The authors do not provide a clear comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as the proximal average algorithm.
* The experimental results are limited to two applications, and it is unclear how the algorithm will perform in other applications.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their proposed algorithm. However, the algorithm requires careful tuning of parameters, and the experimental results are limited. Therefore, I would recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions.
