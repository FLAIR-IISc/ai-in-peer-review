- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM for block-separable convex minimization with multi-block linear constraints.  
- Key innovations: a "dual backward step" to stabilize updates and an adaptive step-size that uses constraint-matrix sparsity and number of parallel blocks.  
- Unifies prior parallel methods by showing sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.  
- Theoretical guarantees: global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with a constant step size.  
- Empirical results: competitive or superior performance on RPCA and overlapping group lasso tasks.  
- Novelty/significance: addresses instability of naive parallel ADMM and extends applicability (e.g., overlapping group lasso) beyond standard RBCD.  
- Quality and clarity: technically sound, well-written, with clear motivation and insightful connection to related work.  
- Weakness: lacks practical guidance or heuristics for selecting K (number of blocks to update in parallel).  
- Weakness: experimental reproducibility could improve with more explicit baseline parameter-tuning details.  
- Overall: high-quality, novel, theoretically well-founded, and empirically effective contribution to distributed and parallel optimization.
