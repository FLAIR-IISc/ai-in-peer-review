- Proposes PDMM to minimize block-separable convex functions with linear constraints and proves global convergence and iteration complexity with constant step size.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and thorough technical analysis plus detailed proofs.
- Strength: Novel algorithm that handles a wide range of problems, including nonsmooth functions.
- Strength: Algorithm is parallelizable, making it suitable for large-scale problems.
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees provided (global convergence and iteration complexity).
- Strength: Experiments show PDMM outperforms existing methods on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Weakness: Algorithm requires careful tuning of parameters (step size, number of blocks).
- Weakness: Lacks clear comparison with other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., proximal average algorithm).
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to two applications, leaving generality unclear.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested fix: Provide clear comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- Suggested fix: Extend experimental results to additional applications.
- Suggested fix: Provide more guidance on how to tune algorithm parameters.
