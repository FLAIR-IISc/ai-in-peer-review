- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with optional Bregman proximal terms and a dual “backward step” to dampen implicit dual ascent.  
- Step sizes τi and νi are explicit functions of updated blocks K and per-row block degree di, allowing larger steps for sparse A.  
- Theoretical results: global convergence in expectation and O(1/T) ergodic convergence under standard convexity and KKT existence.  
- PDMM unifies prior methods: sADMM and PJADMM appear as extreme parameter choices of PDMM.  
- Shows PDMM can handle overlapping-group problems via equality constraints and analyzes sparsity’s effect on step sizes (e.g., τi = 0.5 when di = 2).  
- Empirical evaluation on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso shows PDMM variants outperform RBSUMM and sADMM and are competitive with GSADMM in runtime.  
- Technical soundness: algorithm is well-motivated; implicit dual ascent insight justifies the backward step; convergence analysis matches state-of-the-art for multi-block ADMM.  
- Assumptions are standard; step-size formulas are conservative but interpretable; many proofs are in the supplement.  
- Paper is dense and notation-heavy but largely coherent; clearer pseudo-code and a brief main-text derivation of sADMM/PJADMM equivalences would help.  
- Suggests adding interpretive discussion linking νi to ADMM under-/over-relaxation and extragradient ideas.  
- Originality: novel randomized parallel multi-block ADMM with sparsity-adaptive constant step sizes and the backward-step insight; pedagogically unifies prior methods.  
- Significance: practically relevant for parallelizing block updates on sparse A; standard O(1/T) rate is sufficient; di=2 case highlights tangible advantage over sADMM.  
- Strengths: clear conceptual innovation, principled step-size design, unifying view, solid theory including inexact updates via Bregman terms, and useful applications.  
- Major empirical weaknesses: no parallel/distributed implementation, modest dataset sizes, missing PJADMM baseline, and allegedly incorrect sADMM implementation used for comparison.  
- Recommends reporting sensitivity to ρ, τi, νi; providing principled defaults and robustness analysis; scaling up problems and adding wall-clock multi-threaded results.  
- Clarity/completeness suggestions: include pseudocode, self-contained proof sketch for equivalences, complexity per iteration vs K, and how to compute/choose K and di in practice.  
- Theory suggestions: discuss prospects for non-ergodic or linear rates under stronger assumptions and whether τi can be tightened or adapted (e.g., line-search).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses challenging multi-block ADMM setting, offers new insights and unification, solid guarantees with sparsity/parallelism dependence, and demonstrated benefits.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation lacking true parallel experiments and some baselines, many proofs deferred, and theoretical rate is standard.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on strengthening experiments (parallel runs, proper PJADMM and sADMM comparisons) and clarifying key derivations.
