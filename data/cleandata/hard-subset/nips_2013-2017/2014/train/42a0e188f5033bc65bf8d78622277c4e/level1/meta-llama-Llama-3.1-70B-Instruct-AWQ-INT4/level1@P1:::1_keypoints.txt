- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for optimization with multi-block linear constraints.  
- PDMM randomly updates some blocks in parallel, behaving like parallel randomized block coordinate descent.  
- Introduces a backward step on the dual update to make conservative progress on the dual variable.  
- Dual step size calibrated using matrix A sparsity and number of randomized blocks.  
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM can perform randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and connections to ADMM and its variants.  
- Experimental results show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- PDMM is faster in runtime than GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM in reported experiments.  
- Strength: novel parallel randomized block coordinate method for multi-block constrained problems.  
- Strength: solid theoretical guarantees (global convergence and complexity).  
- Strength: clear exposition and relation to existing methods.  
- Weakness: assumes matrix A is sparse, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis for large-scale problems.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to two applications; broader evaluation desirable.  
- Originality: combines familiar techniques in a novel way and adds a new backward dual step.  
- Significance: addresses an important class of problems and shows practical performance gains.  
- Quality/Clarity: well-organized, clear notation, and sound theoretical development.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: provide computational complexity analysis, consider non-sparse A cases, and include additional experimental applications.
