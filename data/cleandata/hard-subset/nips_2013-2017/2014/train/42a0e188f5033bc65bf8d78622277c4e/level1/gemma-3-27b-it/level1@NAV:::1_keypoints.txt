- Introduces PDMM: a randomized parallel extension of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses convergence issues of naive parallel multi-block ADMM by using random block updates, a dual backward step, and sparsity-informed step sizes.  
- Provides global convergence proofs and iteration complexity bounds.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods on Robust PCA and Overlapping Group Lasso.  
- Positions PDMM within ADMM literature and clearly differentiates it from Gauss-Seidel and Jacobian variants.  
- Shows that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, offering a unifying framework.  
- Establishes connections to randomized block coordinate descent and extends to overlapping blocks.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous but relies on assumptions that may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two applications, restricting evidence of broad effectiveness.  
- Parameter tuning procedures are insufficiently detailed, harming reproducibility.  
- Comparison to more recent/advanced sparse optimization methods is lacking.  
- Writing is generally clear, though occasionally redundant and could be more concise.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions emphasizing expanded experimental details and discussion of theoretical assumption robustness.
