- Proposes PDMM, a randomized block-coordinate method with a dual backward step for multi-block convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Addresses extending ADMM to parallel/multi-block settings and leverages constraint sparsity for step-size design.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (Theorem 1) and iteration complexity bounds (Theorem 2) under reasonable assumptions.  
- Shows PDMM generalizes and connects to existing multi-block ADMM variants (sADMM, PJADMM) and randomized block coordinate methods.  
- Empirically evaluates PDMM on RPCA and overlapping group lasso, showing competitive performance and an ablation study on number of blocks K.  
- Novelty: randomized updates with dual backward step and support for overlapping blocks.  
- Theoretical rigor: solid convergence proofs and complexity results.  
- Clear connections to prior work, unifying several ADMM variants.  
- Empirical validation is convincing but limited to selected baselines.  
- Well-written and organized, though some notation could be clearer.  
- Weakness: insufficient practical guidance for parameter tuning and vague experimental tuning discussion.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent, highly optimized solvers for the target applications.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit per-iteration computational complexity analysis and comparison to observed speedups.  
- Weakness: repetitive explanations (dual backward step) across sections; supplement contains crucial details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, novel and well-motivated method, solid theory, encouraging experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical tuning guidance, incomplete baseline comparison, missing complexity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add practical parameter-tuning guidance, compare to more optimized solvers, include computational complexity discussion, and reduce redundancy on the dual backward step.
