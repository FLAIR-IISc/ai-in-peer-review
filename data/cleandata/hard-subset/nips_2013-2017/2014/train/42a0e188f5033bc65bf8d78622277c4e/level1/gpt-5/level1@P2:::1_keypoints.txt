- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM with K blocks updated per iteration, a backward-stepped dual variable ŷ, and step sizes τi, νi tied to A’s row-block sparsity di and K.  
- Proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity for constant step sizes, covering both exact and inexact (proximal/Bregman) primal updates.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM arise as limiting cases of PDMM (specific τ, ν regimes), linking PDMM to existing multi-block ADMM variants.  
- Emphasizes that matrix sparsity (di) permits larger dual steps and that PDMM is designed for parallelizable multi-block updates.  
- Situates contribution within active multi-block ADMM literature, highlighting the novelty of the backward dual step coupled to sparsity- and K-dependent step sizes.  
- Technical soundness: algorithm clearly specified, standard primal-dual analysis, and proofs plausibly propagate τi, νi dependence on di and K.  
- Completeness: main paper states assumptions, lemmas, and theorems clearly, but core proofs are in the supplement.  
- Empirical concerns: experiments run sequentially (no real parallel speedups), sADMM baseline likely misconfigured (τ=0), and RBSUMM tuning appears limited.  
- Positive empirical notes: experiments study effects of K and τi/νi and include an application (overlapping group lasso) where di=2 favors PDMM.  
- Clarity: main ideas and motivations (especially backward dual step) are well explained, but exposition is dense, notation-heavy, and relies on the supplement for key derivations.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos, abrupt constants (Pt, Q, βi), and missing pseudocode reduce readability.  
- Originality: novel combination of randomized parallel updates, dual backward step, and structure-aware step sizes with unified exact/inexact analysis.  
- Practical significance: advances understanding of stable, parallelizable multi-block ADMM with interpretable step-size rules, though empirical parallel benefits aren’t demonstrated.  
- Strengths: interpretable step-size rules leveraging sparsity and K, unified framework for exact/inexact updates, convergence guarantees, and unification of sADMM/PJADMM.  
- Weaknesses: unfair/insufficient baselines, no parallel runtime experiments, heavy reliance on supplement, and limited empirical ablations on sparsity effects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid novel algorithmic idea, rigorous analysis, practical step-size guidance, and promising application results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical validation is weak (fairness and parallelism), clarity could improve, and theoretical rate is incremental (O(1/T)).  
- Key suggestions: fix sADMM/RBSUMM baselines and report per-iteration costs, provide parallel implementation and wall-clock scaling, move concise derivations of special cases to main text, and add pseudocode and typo corrections.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and originality with moderate-to-high significance, but empirical support and clarity need improvement.
