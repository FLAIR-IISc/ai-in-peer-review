This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method, named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM), to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and the iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size. They also show that PDMM can do randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks.

The main idea of PDMM is to randomly update some blocks in parallel, behaving like parallel randomized block coordinate descent. The authors introduce a backward step on the dual update to make conservative progress on the dual variable. They also take into consideration the sparsity of the matrix A and the number of randomized blocks to determine the step size of the dual update.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its connection to existing methods, such as ADMM and its variants. The theoretical results, including the global convergence and the iteration complexity, are well-established and provide a solid foundation for the proposed method.

The experimental results show that PDMM performs better than state-of-the-art methods in two applications, robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso. The authors also provide a comparison of PDMM with other ADMM methods, including GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM, and show that PDMM is faster than these methods in terms of runtime.
