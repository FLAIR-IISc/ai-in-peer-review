- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method extending ADMM for multi-block linear constraints.
- Enables updating multiple blocks in parallel.
- Establishes global convergence and iteration complexity with a constant step size.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the method and theoretical guarantees.
- Provides a detailed comparison with ADMM variants showing superior convergence speed and accuracy.
- Strength: New parallel method allowing multiple block updates — significant improvement over existing methods.
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation (global convergence and iteration complexity).
- Strength: Empirical demonstrations show practical value.
- Weakness: Requires careful tuning of parameters (step size, number of blocks to update).
- Weakness: May not scale well to problems with a large number of blocks due to parallel update cost.
- Weakness: Empirical comparison is limited to only two applications.
- Overall assessment: Significant contribution with strong theory and promising practical potential.
- Pro-accept: Novel method, solid theoretical guarantees, and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.
- Con-accept: Parameter tuning burden, scalability concerns for many blocks, and limited breadth of experiments.
