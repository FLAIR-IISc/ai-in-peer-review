- Proposes PDMM, a novel parallel randomized block coordinate method for multi-block linear-constrained optimization.  
- Proves global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with a constant step size.  
- Shows PDMM generalizes ADMM and its variants and can handle overlapping blocks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation of method and theory.  
- Experiments (robust PCA, overlapping group lasso) demonstrate PDMM’s empirical effectiveness.  

- Strength: Introduces a novel method that can solve a wider range of problems than existing methods.  
- Strength: Provides thorough theoretical analysis (global convergence and iteration complexity).  
- Strength: Empirical results validate applicability to real-world problems.  

- Weakness: Assumes block separability, which may not hold in all applications.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed empirical comparison to related methods like PJADMM and RBCD.  
- Weakness: Does not discuss computational complexity, important for large-scale use.  

- Originality: Method and theoretical results are novel relative to existing block-coordinate/ADMM literature.  
- Significance: Addresses an important class of optimization problems and has broad potential applications.  

- Quality: Well-written, clear notation, and thorough theoretical and experimental presentation.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with consistent notation and detailed experimental explanations.  

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel method, solid theory, and convincing experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Questionable block separability assumption, missing comparisons, and absent complexity analysis.  

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: add detailed comparisons to PJADMM/RBCD, discuss computational complexity, and clarify experimental setup/evaluation metrics.
