- Proposes PDMM, a novel optimization algorithm that generalizes ADMM to multi-block problems using randomized block-coordinate updates.  
- Introduces a backward step in the dual update to stabilize multi-block updates.  
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.  
- Demonstrates applications to overlapping group lasso and robust PCA (RPCA).  
- Empirical results show PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art ADMM variants in runtime and convergence.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous and address known challenges in multi-block ADMM.  
- Novelty lies in randomized block updates and the dual backward step, with links to sADMM and PJADMM.  
- Experiments are well-structured with multiple baselines and hyperparameter analyses (e.g., block count K).  
- Presentation is dense; derivation of step sizes and role of A’s sparsity need clearer, more intuitive explanations.  
- Limited application scope—only RPCA and overlapping group lasso are explored.  
- Lacks comparisons to non-ADMM optimization methods (e.g., proximal gradient, stochastic methods).  
- Claims parallelism but provides only sequential experiments; no demonstrated parallel implementation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand discussion of applications and comparisons.
