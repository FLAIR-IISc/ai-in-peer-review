- Proposes PDMM, a randomized parallel block-coordinate ADMM variant with a "dual backward step" for multi-block convex problems.
- Establishes global convergence and O(1/T) iteration complexity with constant step size.
- Dual backward step (eqs. 6–7) resolves non-convergence of direct Jacobi multi-block updates.
- Unifies existing methods: sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM (Section 2.2).
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Addresses an open problem: randomized block coordinate descent for overlapping blocks.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly notated, and connects theory to experiments effectively.
- Lacks rigorous theoretical justification for the specific step-size choices (τi, νi in eq. 8).
- Limited sensitivity analysis on key parameters (number of blocks K and Bregman divergences ηj).
- Incomplete empirical comparison with specialized overlapping-group methods (e.g., [1,28,27]).
- Missing detailed per-iteration computational complexity comparison, especially for the dual backward step.
- Problem of multi-block optimization is important; PDMM fills a significant theoretical and practical gap.
- Provides a unified framework that advances understanding of ADMM variants and impacts large-scale ML.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with convincing theory and experiments; well-written.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear convergence guarantees, novel dual-backward insight, unification of variants, strong experimental results, practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear step-size derivation, incomplete method comparisons, limited parameter-sensitivity analysis.
