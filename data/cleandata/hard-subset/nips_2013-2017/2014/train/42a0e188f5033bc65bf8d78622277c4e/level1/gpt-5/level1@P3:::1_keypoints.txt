- Problem and proposal: addresses convex block-separable objectives with linear constraints and proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate ADMM variant.
- Update mechanism: PDMM updates K primal blocks in parallel by minimizing an augmented Lagrangian plus an optional Bregman prox term.
- Dual backward step: introduces an intermediate dual y-hat and parameters τi, νi to temper implicit dual ascent from the quadratic penalty.
- Step-size formulas: derives τi = K/(K̃i(2J−K)) and νi = 1 − 1/K̃i with K̃i = min{di, K}, tying steps to per-row sparsity di and active-block count K.
- Theoretical guarantees: proves global convergence and O(1/T) ergodic complexity in expectation with constant step sizes for exact and inexact updates.
- Special cases and structure: PDMM recovers sADMM and PJADMM as extremes and handles overlapping group structures via equality constraints (di = 2).
- Empirical results: RPCA and overlapping group lasso experiments show faster iteration/runtime convergence vs sADMM, RBSUMM, and GSADMM (sequential).
- Theoretical strengths: clear derivation of sparsity/K-dependent step rules, well-motivated backward step, and Bregman-framework for inexact updates under mild assumptions.
- Missing theoretical details: constructions of P^t and Q, sharpness/maximal admissible τi, νi, and dependence of constants on A (norms/spectral properties) are not explicit.
- Empirical limitations: experiments are sequential, baselines inconsistent (sADMM run without proper dual update; RBSUMM sensitive to tuning), and lack stronger non-ADMM baselines.
- Needed comparisons/ablations: missing PDHG/primal-dual extragradient baselines, ablation on τi/νi and K, and fair sADMM comparison with correct dual updates.
- Scalability claim weak: no parallel/multithreaded wall-clock or scalability experiments to substantiate parallel speedups.
- Clarity issues: paper is dense with heavy notation; would benefit from an algorithm box, default φj/ηj settings, and intuitive discussion of τi, νi limits (K=1 vs K=J; sparse vs dense di).
- Originality: novel integration of randomized parallel block ADMM with a principled dual backward step and sparsity/K-aware step-size rules.
- Significance: relevant for structured large-scale convex problems; O(1/T) guarantees with constant stepsizes are practically meaningful if empirical validation is strengthened.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually corrects Jacobi ADMM failure mode, provides sound analysis, unifies prior methods, and supports inexact Bregman updates.
- Cons for acceptance: limited/uneven empirical validation, opaque theoretical constructs in main text, missing citations/discussion of related primal-dual extragradient methods, and no adaptive strategies or ablation.
- Suggested improvements: add explicit algorithm box and default parameter guidance, clarify P^t/Q and constants and tightness of τi/νi, compare to PDHG/Mirror-Prox, fix baselines and run parallel scalability tests, and correct typos/streamline notation.
