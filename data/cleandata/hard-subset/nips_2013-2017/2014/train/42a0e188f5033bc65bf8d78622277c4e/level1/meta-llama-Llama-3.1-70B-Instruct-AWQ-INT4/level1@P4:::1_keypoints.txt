- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for optimization with multi-block linear constraints, with global convergence and iteration complexity for constant step size.  
- PDMM randomly updates blocks in parallel and adds a backward step on the dual update; dual step size depends on A’s sparsity and number of randomized blocks.  
- Shows PDMM can perform randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, notation, and connections to related work.  
- Theoretical results are sound and experimentally effective on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Novelty: backward dual step and parallel randomized block-coordinate scheme for multi-block constrained problems.  
- Strength: handles overlapping blocks and provides formal convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness: relies on block-separable convex objective, which may limit applicability.  
- Weakness: experiments lack comparisons to state-of-the-art alternatives.  
- Weakness: paper does not discuss computational complexity or runtime comparisons to other methods.  
- Significance: offers a new practical method with theoretical backing for an important problem class.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add comparisons to other methods and a discussion of computational complexity.
