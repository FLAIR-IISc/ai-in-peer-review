- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method that generalizes ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.
- Core idea: randomly update multiple blocks in parallel and add a backward step on the dual update.
- Provides global convergence analysis and iteration complexity guarantees.
- Empirical results on RPCA and overlapping group lasso show improved performance over existing methods.
- Well-motivated; explicitly addresses limitations of extending ADMM to multiple blocks.
- Clarifies connections to sADMM and PJADMM, offering a unifying perspective on related algorithms.
- Notation is dense and potentially confusing due to many indices (i, j, K̃i).
- Discussion of related work occasionally lacks depth, notably the treatment of RBSUMM.
- Intuition and justification for step-size choices (τi, νi) are insufficiently clear, especially regarding block sparsity and number of randomized blocks.
- Experimental section lacks a detailed ablation study to isolate contributions (e.g., backward step, randomization).
- Experimental setup is basic; comparisons with more sophisticated solvers and additional datasets are needed.
- Claim about efficiently handling overlapping groups needs more elaboration and comparison to specialized methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, rigorous theory, clear impact and useful connections to prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation/notation clarity and limited experimental evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental analysis.
