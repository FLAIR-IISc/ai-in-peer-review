- Proposes PDMM: a parallel randomized block-coordinate method for multi-block linear-constrained optimization.  
- Introduces a dual backward step to resolve convergence issues of direct Jacobi updates.  
- Proves global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity using a constant step size.  
- Demonstrates that sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM.  
- Handles overlapping blocks (important for overlapping group lasso) where RBCD reportedly fails.  
- Empirically outperforms ADMM, GSADMM, sADMM, and RBSUMM on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Builds on and distinguishes itself from prior ADMM, randomized block coordinate descent, and BSUMM work.  
- Strength: rigorous, well-structured theoretical analysis with clear assumptions and proofs.  
- Strength: novel unified framework—randomized parallel updates plus dual backward step.  
- Strength: clear exposition and insightful connections between PDMM and existing methods.  
- Strength: practical significance demonstrated via experiments and parallelism advantage.  
- Weakness: does not fully explain why RBCD cannot handle overlapping blocks while PDMM can.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons omit some recent distributed optimization methods (NIPS 2013–2014).  
- Weakness: limited practical guidance for selecting parameters (τi, νi) beyond studied cases.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of parallel scaling or speedup with more blocks/processors.  
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation—accept.
