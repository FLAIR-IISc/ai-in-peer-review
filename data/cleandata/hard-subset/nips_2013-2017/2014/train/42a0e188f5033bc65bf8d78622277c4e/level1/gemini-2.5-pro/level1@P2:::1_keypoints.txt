- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block-coordinate extension of ADMM for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints.  
- Introduces a "dual backward step" to stabilize dual updates and ensure convergence in parallel multi-block settings.  
- Derives adaptive step sizes (τ_i, ν_i) that depend on constraint-matrix sparsity (d_i) and number of blocks updated in parallel (K).  
- Provides a flexible framework allowing any number of blocks to be updated in parallel (from 1 to all).  
- Proves global convergence and an O(1/T) iteration complexity rate under standard assumptions.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, unifying prior parallel ADMM variants.  
- Demonstrates PDMM on overlapping group lasso and RPCA, addressing problems where standard RBCD is not directly applicable.  
- Empirical results indicate PDMM is competitive with or superior to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experiments were run sequentially, so practical parallel speedups are not directly demonstrated.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-motivated, and careful in analysis and assumptions.  
- Writing and organization are clear; core algorithm and intuition are well explained.  
- Minor clarity issue: need to explicitly state how the intermediate dual variable ŷ^t is computed at iteration start.  
- Highly original: novel combination of randomized parallel updates with stabilizing dual step and sparsity-aware step sizes.  
- Highly significant: addresses a major limitation of ADMM for multi-block large-scale optimization with practical relevance.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance; main counterpoint is lack of true parallel experiments.
