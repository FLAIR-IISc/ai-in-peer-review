- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for optimization with multi-block linear constraints.
- Proves global convergence and iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size.
- Shows PDMM can perform randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks.
- Experimental results: PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.
- Paper is well-written and effectively relates to prior research.
- Technical development is sound with a thorough convergence analysis.
- Strength: novel parallel, efficient method for multi-block constrained optimization.
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical guarantees (global convergence and iteration complexity).
- Strength: empirical improvements demonstrated on two applications.
- Weakness: assumes block-separable objective, which may not hold in many applications.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to two applications, so broader performance is unclear.
- Meets review criteria: high quality, clear, original, and potentially significant.
