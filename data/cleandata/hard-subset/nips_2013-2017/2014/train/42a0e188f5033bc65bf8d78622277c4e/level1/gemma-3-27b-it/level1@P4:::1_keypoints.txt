- Proposes PDMM: randomized parallel block updates for block-separable convex problems with linear constraints, extending ADMM to multi-block cases.  
- Randomly updates a subset of blocks in parallel to improve scalability and convergence over Gauss-Seidel/Jacobian ADMM variants.  
- Introduces a dual backward step and sparsity-aware step-size selection (τi, νi) to stabilize updates and exploit problem structure.  
- Establishes convergence guarantees and iteration complexity; proofs provided in supplement.  
- Demonstrates empirical performance gains on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Correctly positions work within multi-block ADMM literature, discussing Gauss-Seidel and Jacobian families and citing key prior papers.  
- Connects PDMM to sADMM and PJADMM, framing PDMM as a unifying/generalizing approach.  
- Assumptions (Assumption 1) and theoretical derivations appear standard and technically sound.  
- Novelty: combination of randomized parallel updates, dual backward step, sparsity-aware steps, and overlapping-block handling.  
- Significance: addresses an important scalability problem in optimization with practical relevance and theoretical foundation.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational-cost analysis, including synchronization overhead and effect of K (number of blocks updated) on runtime.  
- Weakness: some clarity issues—dense "Connections to Related Work" section and occasional confusing multi-index notation.  
- Weakness: experiments are positive but could be more comprehensive and compare to more recent methods.  
- Overall assessment: Strong accept — novel, significant, and well-supported despite minor analysis and clarity gaps.
