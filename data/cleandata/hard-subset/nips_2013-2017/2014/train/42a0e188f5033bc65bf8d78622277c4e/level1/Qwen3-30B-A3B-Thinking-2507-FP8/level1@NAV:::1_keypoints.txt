- Proposes PDMM, a parallel randomized block coordinate method for multi-block linearly constrained convex optimization.  
- Addresses gap: multi-block ADMM generalizations are poorly understood compared to two-block ADMM.  
- Allows parallel updates of randomly selected blocks, extending ADMM.  
- Establishes global convergence with O(1/T) iteration complexity under constant step size.  
- Shows sADMM and PJADMM are special cases of PDMM, offering new theoretical insights.  
- Handles overlapping blocks, a known challenge for existing methods.  
- Empirically validated on robust PCA and overlapping group lasso.  
- Strength: strong theoretical contributions with rigorous convergence proofs.  
- Strength: clear connections to prior ADMM variants and well-motivated technical exposition.  
- Strength: compelling empirical results where PDMM outperforms state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: experiments run sequentially, so actual parallel speedup is not quantified.  
- Weakness: step size selection (τi, νi) may require careful tuning and be hard in practice.  
- Pro acceptance: significant theoretical advance, strong validation, clear prior-work ties, practical relevance.  
- Con acceptance: limited demonstration of parallel efficiency and incomplete addressing of distributed implementation challenges.
