This paper proposes a parallel randomized block coordinate method named Parallel Direction Method of Multipliers (PDMM) to solve optimization problems with multi-block linear constraints. The authors establish the global convergence and the iteration complexity for PDMM with constant step size. The paper also shows that PDMM can do randomized block coordinate descent on overlapping blocks.

The main idea of PDMM is to randomly update some blocks in parallel, behaving like parallel randomized block coordinate descent. The authors introduce a backward step on the dual update to make conservative progress on the dual variable. The sparsity of A and the number of randomized blocks are taken into consideration to determine the step size of the dual update.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its connections to related work. The theoretical results are sound, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of PDMM in solving robust principal component analysis and overlapping group lasso.
