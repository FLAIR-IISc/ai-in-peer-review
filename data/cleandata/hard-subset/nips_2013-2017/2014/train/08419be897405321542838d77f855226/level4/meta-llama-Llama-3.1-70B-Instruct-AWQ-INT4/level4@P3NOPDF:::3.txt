This paper presents an interesting solution to the problem of finding more efficient algebraic identities, leveraging distributed, recursive neural representations to successfully classify these identities.

However, the problem definition appears to be quite restricted, and it would be beneficial to clarify the scope and limitations of the proposed approach. Furthermore, some aspects of the methodology could be better explained. For instance, the description of the "weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, so that the output is also size l)" is unclear. It is not evident whether the authors are using a neural tensor network instead of a simple linear network. This distinction is important, as Bowman has demonstrated that tensor networks can outperform RNNs in similar tasks.

In Section 7.2, the authors discuss efficient identity discovery, but it is unclear whether they are still using the RNN for classification while evaluating different search strategies. The proposal generation process in the search algorithm is also not well-explained and could benefit from additional clarification.

Additionally, there are several typos throughout the paper that should be corrected. These include: "has contains a single matrix" should be "has a single matrix", "listed in table Table 1" should be "listed in Table 1", "vector is the presented to the next operation" should be "vector is presented to the next operation", "The final vector is pass to a softmax classifier" should be "The final vector is passed to a softmax classifier", "as to how explore space of possible" should be "as to how to explore the space of possibilities", "The scheduler is is fixed" should be "The scheduler is fixed", "The difficulty of the task by be appreciated by looking" should be "The difficulty of the task can be appreciated by looking", and "which can easily captured with" should be "which can be easily captured with". Moreover, the label in Figure 3 should be corrected to RNN, not TNN.

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach to finding efficient algebraic identities, but could benefit from further clarification and correction of errors to enhance its overall quality and impact.
