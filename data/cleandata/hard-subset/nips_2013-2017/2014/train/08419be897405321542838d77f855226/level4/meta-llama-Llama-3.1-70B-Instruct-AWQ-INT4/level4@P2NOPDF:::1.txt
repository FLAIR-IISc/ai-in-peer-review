This paper explores the application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to a novel area, namely discovering mathematical identities. While the ideas presented are innovative, I believe that this work is preliminary and may not meet the standards for publication at NIPS. Specifically, the method proposed does not demonstrate impressive performance in identifying mathematical identities, even with a limited input set.

The expressions used to test the method are drawn from a narrow range, which raises concerns about the approach's generality. It is likely that a simple mathematical rule could have been derived to discover these identities, making the application of DNNs seem unnecessary. The analogy of using a sledgehammer to crack a nut is apt in this context.

The authors may argue that their approach can be extended to a broader range of expressions. However, it is unclear whether the existing method can be easily scaled up or if doing so would be meaningful, given the modest initial results.

While I find this paper interesting, I believe it falls slightly short of the publication threshold.
