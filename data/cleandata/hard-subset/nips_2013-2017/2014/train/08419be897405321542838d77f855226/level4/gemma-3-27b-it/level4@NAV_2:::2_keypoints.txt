- Proposes using attribute grammars plus ML to discover lower-complexity mathematical identities.  
- Introduces two learning-guided search methods: an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN).  
- Trains models on simpler expressions to guide search for more complex identities.  
- Well-motivated idea: ML success in pattern recognition suggests promise for symbolic manipulation.  
- Attribute grammar provides a structured representation and search space for expressions.  
- Learning-based search addresses exponential growth of exhaustive search.  
- Empirical results show the method can find non-trivial identities reducing complexity (e.g., O(n^3) to O(n^2)).  
- Integer-based descriptors ensure accurate comparison and avoid numerical issues.  
- n-gram model performs particularly well in experiments.  
- Code and evaluation data are provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Paper lacks clarity in parts (notably Section 7.2) and needs clearer experimental details.  
- Practical significance and broader applicability of discovered identities are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- RNN consistently underperforms the n-gram model and needs deeper analysis and potential fixes.  
- Scope is limited by assumptions (homogeneous polynomials, single-variable targets), reducing generality.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, discuss limitations/impact, and address RNN underperformance.
