- Proposes two methods to search for simpler symbolic expression trees to improve computational efficiency: an n-gram over sub-expression sequences and an RNN-based continuous representation.  
- The RNN is used to create continuous-space embeddings of expression trees to predict likely combination rules for subtrees.  
- N-gram method outperforms the RNN in two of the three presented tasks (Figure 3).  
- Paper quality is high with a thorough investigation of the methods.  
- Clarity is generally good but Section 7.2 needs clearer exposition.  
- Originality is high, offering novel approaches to simplifying symbolic expression trees.  
- Significance is rated low because the practical relevance and impact for users needing efficient computations are unclear.
