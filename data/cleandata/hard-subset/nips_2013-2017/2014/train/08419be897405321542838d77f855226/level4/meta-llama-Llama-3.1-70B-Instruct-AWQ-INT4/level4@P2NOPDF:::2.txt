This paper proposes two novel methods for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees, with the goal of improving computational efficiency. The first method leverages n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network (RNN) to create continuous space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations are then employed to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees. 

As demonstrated in Figure 3, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the RNN method in two out of the three tasks presented. 

Regarding specific review criteria:

Quality: The paper is of high quality, showcasing a thorough investigation into the proposed methods.

Clarity: While the paper is generally well-written, certain sections, particularly Section 7.2, could benefit from clarification to enhance readability.

Originality: The paper demonstrates high originality, presenting innovative approaches to simplifying symbolic expression trees.

Significance: The significance of the proposed methods is low, as the problem addressed may not be of practical concern for individuals seeking to perform efficient computations. The paper focuses on developing methods for finding more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees; however, the relevance and impact of these methods are unclear.
