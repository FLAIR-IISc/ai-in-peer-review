- Novel and interesting application: learning representations for symbolic expressions and discovering mathematical identities with potential impact on symbolic software libraries.  
- Major concern: paper aims to find lower-complexity expressions but the algorithm has no mechanism to enforce or prefer computationally cheaper solutions.  
- Request for solutions: reviewer asks whether authors considered ways to restrict the search space to expressions that exhibit computational improvements.  
- Question about Section 2 restrictions: is the scheduler truly the only limiting factor, and why are these restrictions necessary? reviewer requests formal justification or empirical evidence.  
- Clarity issue: paper structure and exposition hinder understanding; needs a more explicit, stepwise explanation of the methodology.  
- Missing practical constraints: no discussion of vector/matrix size constraints—example RBM-1 expression appears invalid for k=1, n=1.  
- Minor typos to fix: Line 332 “show for” → “show”/“shown for”; Line 148 “has contains” → “contains”; Line 289 “is is” → “is”; Line 338 “by be” → “be”.  
- Overall: promising and novel approach but requires clearer exposition and handling of practical/scaling constraints.
