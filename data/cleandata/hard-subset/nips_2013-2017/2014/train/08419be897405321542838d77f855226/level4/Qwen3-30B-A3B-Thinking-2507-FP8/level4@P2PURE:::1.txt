

First, apologies for the brevity of this review—I drafted a more detailed version with specific comments but misplaced it after correcting a few typos.  

This paper applies DNNs to a novel domain: discovering mathematical identities. While the idea is novel, I believe the work represents preliminary research that does not sufficiently meet NIPS publication standards. Specifically, the method demonstrates only modest performance in identifying mathematical identities, despite being tested on an extremely limited input set. The expressions considered were drawn from such a restricted domain that deriving the identities manually would likely have been simpler than deploying DNNs—effectively using a sledgehammer to crack a nut. Although you might argue the approach could generalize to broader expression classes, it is unclear whether the current methodology would scale effectively in this direction, nor is it evident that such scaling would be meaningful given the modest initial results.  

I find the paper interesting, but I believe it falls slightly below the threshold for publication.
