This paper presents a novel application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the discovery of mathematical identities, and while the ideas are innovative, I believe that this work is preliminary and may not meet the standards for publication at NIPS. Specifically, the method's performance in identifying mathematical identities is not particularly impressive, even with a very limited input set. The expressions used to test the method are drawn from a restricted set, making it seem that the approach may be an overly complex solution to a problem that could be more easily addressed through mathematical rules. The use of DNNs in this context appears to be disproportionate to the task at hand.

While the authors may argue that their approach can be extended to a broader range of expressions, it is unclear whether the existing method can be easily scaled up or if doing so would be justified given the modest initial results. Although I find the paper interesting, I believe that it falls slightly short of the publication threshold.
