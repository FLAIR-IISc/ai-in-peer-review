- Applies machine learning to discover computationally efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar and learning-guided search (n-gram and RNN) to find lower-complexity equivalent expressions.  
- Introduces an attribute grammar framework to represent symbolic expressions for systematic exploration.  
- Proposes two learning-based search strategies—n-gram model and recursive neural network—to navigate the exponentially large space of expression trees.  
- The RNN provides a continuous representation of symbolic expressions, potentially bridging symbolic and learning-based methods.  
- Builds on prior symbolic computation and program induction work but distinguishes itself by using learning to guide search; related theorem-proving and probabilistic programming work are acknowledged.  
- Novelty: combining attribute grammars with ML and applying RNNs to symbolic expression representation is an interesting, original contribution.  
- Technical soundness: framework is well-defined with a clear problem statement and grammar.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates notable complexity reductions (e.g., O(n^3) to O(n^2)) and discovery of previously unknown identities.  
- Code and evaluation data are publicly released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Clarity issues: some sections (notably 7.2 and RNN implementation in 4.2) are dense, with inconsistent notation and need clearer explanations/examples.  
- Practical significance is limited: focus on homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree may not generalize to broad real-world problems.  
- Limited scope: experiments cover a small set of expression families, leaving generalization to more complex domains unclear.  
- RNN underperformance: n-gram models outperform RNNs consistently; causes and potential improvements are not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid approach with encouraging results and released code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable practical impact, clarity and scope limitations, and underwhelming RNN performance.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: interesting and technically sound work; accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, analyze RNN performance, and broaden experimental evaluation.
