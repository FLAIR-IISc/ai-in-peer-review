This paper describes two methods for searching simple symbolic expression trees. One relies on the use of an engram, which is a computation based on the order of a subexpression. The other uses a recurrent neural network to create a continuous space representation of the symbolic expression tree. These representations are then used to predict which rules can be applied to combine subtrees. The use of engrams in Figure 3 seems to be better than RNN in two of the three tasks.
