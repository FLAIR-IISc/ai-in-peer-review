

First, apologies for the brevity of this review—I had drafted a more detailed version but lost it due to typos.  

This paper applies deep neural networks (DNNs) to the novel task of discovering mathematical identities. While the idea is novel, the work appears preliminary and does not fully meet NIPS publication standards. The method demonstrates only modest performance in identifying identities, particularly given the extremely limited input domain tested. The expressions considered are drawn from such a narrow set that deriving identities would likely have been more efficient through direct mathematical analysis rather than DNNs—effectively using a sledgehammer to crack a nut. Although one might argue the approach could generalize to broader expression sets, the current results provide little evidence that the method scales effectively or meaningfully to such a generalization. Given these modest initial results, extending the approach to more complex domains does not appear justified.  

I find the topic intriguing, but the contribution falls below the threshold for publication at NIPS.
