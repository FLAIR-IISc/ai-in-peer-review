This paper introduces two novel approaches for simplifying symbolic expression trees: an ngram-based method and a recursive neural network (RNN)-based method. The ngram approach leverages statistical relationships between sub-expression sequences to guide the search for simpler trees, while the RNN learns continuous representations of these trees to predict likely subtree combination rules. Based on the results presented in Figure 3, the ngram method appears to outperform the RNN on two out of three evaluated tasks.

**Quality:** High

**Clarity:** The paper’s presentation could be improved. While the core ideas are interesting, the explanations are occasionally difficult to follow, particularly in Section 7.2. More detailed explanations and potentially illustrative examples would enhance understanding.

**Originality:** High. The combination of ngram techniques and recursive neural networks applied to symbolic expression tree simplification represents a unique contribution. 

**Significance:** Low. The practical impact of this work is questionable. The problem of finding computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, while theoretically interesting, feels somewhat contrived and lacks immediate relevance to common computational challenges. It’s unclear whether the techniques developed here would find application in real-world scenarios where efficient computation is paramount. The paper focuses on optimizing for computational efficiency *within* the domain of symbolic expression trees, but doesn’t demonstrate a compelling reason why this optimization is valuable beyond the academic exercise itself. 

Further elaboration on the potential benefits or applications of these simplified expression trees would strengthen the argument for significance. Currently, the paper presents effective *methods* for simplification, but lacks a strong justification for *why* simplification is a worthwhile goal in a broader context. The paper would benefit from discussing potential downstream tasks or problems where the resulting simplified trees could provide a measurable advantage.
