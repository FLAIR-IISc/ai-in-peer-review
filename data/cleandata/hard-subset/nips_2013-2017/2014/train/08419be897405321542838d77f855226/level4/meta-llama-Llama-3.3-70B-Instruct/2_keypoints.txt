- Proposes two methods for simplifying symbolic expression trees: an n-gram subexpression analyzer and an RNN that learns continuous tree representations to predict subtree combination rules.
- N-gram approach outperforms the RNN in two of three tasks (Figure 3).
- Technical quality is high.
- Overall clarity is good, but Section 7.2 is convoluted and hard to follow.
- Methods are highly original and innovative.
- Practical significance is limited; the addressed problem may be rare in real-world computational efficiency optimization, making impact uncertain.
