This article describes two methods that search for simple symbolic expressions trees. One relies on the use of an engram, which is a computation based on a sequence of sub-expressions. The other uses a recurrent neural network to create a continuous space representation of the symbolic expression tree. These representations are then used to predict which rules can be applied to combine the sub-trees. The use of engrams in Figure 3 seems to be better than RNNs in two of the three tasks.
