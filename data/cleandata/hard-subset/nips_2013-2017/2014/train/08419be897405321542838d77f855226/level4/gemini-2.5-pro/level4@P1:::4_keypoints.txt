- Presents a novel framework that reframes discovering computationally efficient symbolic identities as a learning-guided search using an attribute-grammar tree representation.  
- Proposes two ML models—an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN)—to guide search in an exponentially large expression space.  
- Trains models on solutions to simpler problems (curriculum learning) so they generalize to more complex target expressions.  
- Introduces a novel application of an RNN to learn continuous vector representations of symbolic expressions for comparison and classification.  
- Successfully discovers new, non-obvious efficient identities for several families of polynomial expressions relevant to machine learning.  
- Original and significant application of modern ML (including deep learning) to symbolic mathematics, moving beyond traditional rule-based systems.  
- Demonstrates practical potential for optimizing numerical libraries and as a component in future compilers.  
- Curriculum-based learning is an effective strategy for managing combinatorial search complexity.  
- The search does not explicitly enforce lower computational complexity; in some experiments authors remove expensive operations from the grammar instead.  
- Scope is limited to homogeneous polynomials over a single matrix or vector; paper lacks discussion on extending to trigonometric functions, recursion, or multiple variables.  
- Paper would benefit from clearer, more sequential presentation of the full workflow from target definition to learned search results.  
- Requests clarification of the (RBM-1)k identity in the supplement (case k=1, n=1 appears invalid) and lists minor typos to fix.
