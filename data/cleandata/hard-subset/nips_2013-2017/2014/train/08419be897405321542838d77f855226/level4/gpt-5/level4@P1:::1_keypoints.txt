- Paper proposes using deep neural networks to guide grammar-based symbolic search for computationally efficient mathematical identities, learning from simpler expressions to handle harder ones.  
- Novel and underexplored problem setting: automated discovery of efficient symbolic identities.  
- Learning-guided search is appealing compared to hand-crafted heuristics or brute-force enumeration.  
- Framing: training on simpler instances to generalize to more complex expressions is conceptually sound.  
- Empirical performance is modest; results are mixed even within the narrow, constrained setting.  
- Expression set is very restricted, raising the possibility that targeted mathematical rules could achieve similar results more simply.  
- Unclear scalability: current methodology may not extend sensibly to richer grammars or realistic domains based on reported results.  
- Quality: reasonable high-level idea but limited empirical evidence and lack of clear advantage over simpler alternatives.  
- Clarity: core idea and motivation are clear, but the paper needs sharper discussion of limitations and failure cases.  
- Originality: application domain and learning-to-guide-search angle are novel, though the implementation feels preliminary.  
- Significance: impact appears modest given constrained scope and unimpressive gains; unlikely to be adopted as-is by practitioners.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel problem, clear motivation for learned guidance, potential to inspire ML–symbolic reasoning work.  
- Cons for acceptance: underwhelming empirical results, narrow expression set where heuristics might suffice, unclear scalability.  
- Recommendation: interesting but slightly below conference threshold; needs stronger empirical advantages or evidence of scalability to merit acceptance.
