- Novel contribution: introduces learning representations for symbolic expressions and discovering mathematical identities; interesting and potentially impactful for symbolic libraries.
- Limited scope: experiments cover only restricted expressions and there is no formal proof of extensibility to more complex cases.
- Objective mismatch: problem statement emphasizes finding lower-complexity expressions but the algorithm does not explicitly enforce or constrain computational complexity.
- Request for solutions: reviewer asks whether authors considered methods to limit the search space to expressions with desirable complexity properties.
- Section 2 restrictions: attributing limitations mainly to the scheduler requires more rigorous justification (formal analysis or empirical demonstration).
- Clarity/structure: paper organization hinders readability and should present the different stages more explicitly and coherently.
- Missing discussion on sizes: no constraints discussed for vector/matrix sizes; RBM-1 expression in the supplement (k=1, n=1) appears invalid.
- Minor typos to fix: Line 332 “show for”; Line 148 “has contains”; Line 289 “is is”; Line 338 “by be”.
- Overall assessment: despite flaws, the approach is novel and promising though currently limited in scope.
