

First, apologies for the brevity—I had prepared a more detailed review but regrettably misplaced it.  

This paper applies DNNs to a novel domain (discovering mathematical identities). While the core idea is novel, the work appears preliminary and does not meet the standard for a NIPS publication. The method achieves only modest performance in identifying identities, despite operating on a highly constrained input space. Given the limited scope of expressions examined, deriving identities via straightforward mathematical analysis would likely be more efficient than deploying DNNs—a disproportionately complex approach for the task. Although scalability to broader expression sets might be envisioned, the current results provide little evidence that such an extension would be feasible or meaningful.  

I find the topic intriguing, but the contribution falls slightly below the publication threshold.
