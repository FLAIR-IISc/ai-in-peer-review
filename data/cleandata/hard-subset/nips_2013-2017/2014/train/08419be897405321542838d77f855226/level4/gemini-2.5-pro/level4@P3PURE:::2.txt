This paper proposes two methods for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees. The first method is based on n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions. The second employs a recursive neural network to generate continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees; these representations are then used to predict the most probable rule for combining subtrees. Based on the results presented in Figure 3, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the recursive neural network on two of the three evaluated tasks.

**Quality:** The technical quality of the work is high.

**Clarity:** The paper is somewhat difficult to follow, and its clarity could be improved. Section 7.2, in particular, is challenging to understand.

**Originality:** The proposed methods are novel, and the originality of the work is high.

**Significance:** The significance of this work appears to be low. While the paper describes ways of searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, the problem itself seems contrived. It is unlikely that these techniques would be adopted in practice by those seeking to perform efficient computations. Consequently, the overall significance of the methods is unclear.
