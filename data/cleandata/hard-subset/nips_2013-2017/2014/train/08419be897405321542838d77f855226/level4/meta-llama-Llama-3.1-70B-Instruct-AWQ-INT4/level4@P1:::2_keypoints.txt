- Proposes a novel ML-based approach to discover efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar representation.  
- Introduces two learning-based search methods: n-gram models for pattern capture and an RNN for continuous symbolic-expression representations.  
- Well-motivated problem with a clear explanation of goals and proposed solutions.  
- Unique and original combination of attribute grammars with learning-based search, distinguishing it from related theorem proving/program induction work.  
- High-quality paper with thorough descriptions of methods and experimental setup.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness and include detailed analysis and comparison of n-gram vs. RNN methods.  
- Authors acknowledge and discuss limitations of their approach.  
- Clarity issues: Section 7.2 is dense and the figures are not clearly explained.  
- Notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to some readers, making parts hard to follow.  
- Originality rated high due to novel method combination and solid related-work discussion.  
- Significance rated moderate; unclear whether the methods will be widely adopted in practice.  
- Problem setting appears somewhat contrived, raising doubts about applicability to more general mathematical expressions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, thorough methods and experiments, strong motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, moderate practical significance, and uncertain general applicability.
