- Paper applies machine learning to discover efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar to represent symbolic expressions.  
- Authors propose two learning approaches to guide search: an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN).  
- Work focuses on a restricted setting where target expressions are homogeneous polynomials of degree k.  
- Experiments include several expression families, including the RBM partition function from machine learning.  
- Novelty: applying ML (especially an RNN) to learn continuous representations of mathematical structures is interesting.  
- Potential: approach could have broad applicability in symbolic computation and program induction.  
- Technical quality: method is sound and clearly explained overall.  
- Evaluation is limited: experiments cover a restricted set of expressions and lack depth.  
- Performance is unimpressive: method struggles to discover identities even with limited inputs.  
- Scalability is unclear: unclear how approach generalizes to more complex or general expressions.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, but the RNN description needs more detail and clarification.  
- Significance is limited by restricted setting and weak results, so impact is uncertain for a NIPS-level contribution.
