- Novel contribution: Introduces an interesting new use case—learning representations for symbolic expressions and discovering identities—though studied on a limited set of expressions.
- Limitation on objective vs algorithm: The algorithm does not enforce the stated goal of finding lower-complexity expressions; authors should consider biasing/constraining search (cost-based regularization, pruning, constrained decoding).
- Restrictions in Section 2: Unclear why restrictions are imposed—ask if the scheduler is the sole limiting factor and request formal justification or ablation experiments showing necessity and impact.
- Paper organization: Method stages and pipeline are not explained clearly or sequentially; restructuring and clearer stage descriptions are needed.
- Dimensionality constraints: Unclear constraints on vector/matrix sizes (e.g., RBM-1 expression in supplement appears invalid for k=1, n=1); needs clarification.
- Minor typos: Fix wording errors at Line 148 (“has contains”), Line 289 (“is is”), Line 332 (“show for”), and Line 338 (“by be”).
- Overall assessment: Interesting and potentially impactful for symbolic computation libraries, but limited scope and lacking evidence of scalability to more complex expressions.
