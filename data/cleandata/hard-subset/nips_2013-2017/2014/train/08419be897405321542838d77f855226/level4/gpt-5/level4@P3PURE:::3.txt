This paper presents an interesting approach to discovering more efficient algebraic identities, and it is compelling to see that distributed, recursive neural representations can successfully classify these identities. However, several aspects of the problem setup and methodology are unclear or appear overly restricted.

Strengths
- The core idea—using recursive neural representations to classify algebraic identities—is interesting and well-motivated.
- The results suggest that these distributed representations can capture structural properties relevant to identity classification.

Concerns and clarity issues
- The problem definition seems quite restricted. It would be helpful to better justify the scope and discuss how broadly the approach may generalize beyond the studied cases.
- The description “weight tensor W3 (of size l × l × l, so that the output is also size l)” is not well explained. Does this mean you are using a neural tensor network rather than a simple linear composition function? Please clarify the exact architecture.
- Related to the above, Bowman showed that a tensor network works a lot better than an RNN on related tasks. How does your choice of composition function (tensor vs. linear vs. standard RNN) affect performance here, and do your experiments reflect this comparison?
- Section 7.2 (Efficient Identity Discovery): It is unclear whether you are still using the RNN for classification in this section while you evaluate different search strategies. Please make explicit which model is used for classification during the search.
- The procedure for generating proposals in the search is difficult to follow. A clearer, step-by-step explanation (with examples) of how proposals are constructed, scored, and selected would substantially improve readability.

Overall assessment
The paper tackles an appealing problem and provides intriguing evidence that recursive neural representations can classify algebraic identities. To strengthen the contribution, please (1) clarify the model architecture, especially the role of the l × l × l weight tensor and whether a neural tensor network is used; (2) explain the relation to prior findings (e.g., Bowman) on tensor vs. RNN performance; (3) make Section 7.2 explicit about the classifier used during search; and (4) provide a clearer description of the proposal-generation mechanism. Addressing these points, along with the listed typos, would significantly improve the clarity and impact of the paper.
