- Paper proposes two learned-guidance approaches for symbolic expression rewriting: n-gram models over linearized subexpressions and recursive neural network (RNN) tree embeddings.
- Experiments on three tasks show the n-gram model outperforms the RNN on two tasks (Figure 3).
- Strength: Novel idea of applying data-driven models to guide symbolic rewriting and simplification.
- Strength: Methods are clearly defined at a high level and the evaluation is systematic; comparison between simple probabilistic and learned compositional models is informative.
- Strength: Insightful empirical finding that a simple n-gram model can outperform an RNN, informing model choice and data requirements.
- Weakness: Practical significance and motivation are unclear; tasks seem contrived and real-world impact is not demonstrated.
- Weakness: Narrow set of baselines; missing comparisons to established rule-based simplifiers, compiler heuristics, and handcrafted search costs.
- Weakness: Clarity issues and missing details (e.g., tree linearization, n-gram order/smoothing, exact RNN architecture/training, rule generation/constraints, search mechanics).
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis explaining why the RNN underperforms—no error analysis or ablations on linearization, rule set size, or beam width.
- Weakness: Evaluation focuses on rewrite success rates rather than end-to-end efficiency; missing runtime, number of rule applications, and cost reduction metrics.
- Question: How are training labels obtained—gold rewrite sequences or only end states, and how is credit assigned to intermediate decisions?
- Question: What linearization strategy is used for n-grams and how sensitive are results to this choice?
- Question: What is the exact grammar/rule set, are rules semantics-preserving, and how is equivalence enforced during search?
- Question: What are beam width, scoring function, stopping criteria, and how is likelihood traded off with cost reduction?
- Question: How large are training sets relative to RNN parameters, and were pretraining, regularization, or alternative architectures (e.g., TreeLSTM) tried?
- Question: Can authors compare to existing symbolic simplifiers or compiler optimizations on shared benchmarks and report wall-clock improvements?
- Suggestion: Strengthen motivation with concrete use cases and demonstrate benefits on realistic workloads.
- Suggestion: Expand baseline suite to include rule-based systems and compiler/CAS heuristics.
- Suggestion: Clarify Section 7.2 and provide concise pseudocode and hyperparameters for the search algorithm.
- Suggestion: Provide ablations and error analyses to isolate effects of model, rule set, linearization, and search parameters.
- Suggestion: Report efficiency metrics (runtime, rewrite counts, complexity reductions, end-to-end speedups).
- Suggestion: Discuss reasons the n-gram model outperforms the RNN and implications for data/inductive biases.
- Rating: Quality—High.
- Rating: Clarity—Moderate.
- Rating: Originality—High.
- Rating: Significance—Low to Moderate.
- Overall: Technically solid and original with useful empirical observations, but needs stronger motivation, broader baselines, efficiency metrics, and clearer exposition (especially Section 7.2).
