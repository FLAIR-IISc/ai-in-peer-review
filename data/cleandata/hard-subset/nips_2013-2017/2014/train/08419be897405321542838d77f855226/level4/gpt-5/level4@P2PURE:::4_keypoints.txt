- Novel contribution: proposes learning representations for symbolic expressions and discovering mathematical identities using neural networks.
- Limited scope: the class of expressions studied is quite restricted and there's no proof the method generalizes to more complex expressions.
- Potential impact: could positively affect symbolic computation libraries despite limited scope.
- Concern: paper emphasizes finding lower-complexity expressions but the algorithm does not enforce this constraint.
- Question: authors should consider mechanisms to restrict or bias the search space toward lower-complexity solutions.
- Restriction source: unclear if the scheduler in Section 2 is the sole factor limiting applicability to more complex expressions.
- Evidence needed: request for formal justification or empirical evidence supporting the restrictions in Section 2.
- Clarity: paper structure is hard to follow and would benefit from a clearer, step-by-step explanation of stages.
- Dimensionality issue: is there a constraint on vector/matrix dimensions? RBM-1 example in supplement appears invalid for k=1, n=1.
- Typos: Line 332 "show for"; Line 148 "has contains"; Line 289 "is is"; Line 338 "by be".
- Overall: interesting and novel contribution despite limited scope.
