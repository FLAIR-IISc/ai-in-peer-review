First of all, apologies for the brevity of this review – I wrote a version with more detailed comments and found some typing errors, but it's not available now.

This paper is an application to a new area of DNA (mathematical identification), and I think the idea is new, but it seems like preliminary work that probably won't reach the level of an NIPS publication. In particular, your method doesn't show particularly impressive performance in identification, even though you only gave it very limited input. The expressions you were looking for identification in were from such a limited set that it would probably be easier to create some mathematical rules to find them instead of applying DNA; it seems like trying to do a big job with a small hammer. Of course, you could argue that your approach could be extended to a much broader range of expressions, but I'm not sure your current approach will be extended that way very easily, or even if it means doing so given your so-called initial results.

I think this is an interesting paper, but I think it's probably a little below the threshold for publication.
