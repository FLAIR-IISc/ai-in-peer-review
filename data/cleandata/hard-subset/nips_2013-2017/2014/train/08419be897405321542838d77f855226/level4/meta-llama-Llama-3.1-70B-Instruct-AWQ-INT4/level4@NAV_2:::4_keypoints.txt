- Introduces a novel use case: using machine learning to discover efficient mathematical identities.  
- Proposes a grammar framework to represent symbolic expressions.  
- Develops two learning approaches: an n-gram model learned from simple expressions and a recursive neural network to learn continuous representations.  
- Demonstrates the methods can discover complex identities unreachable by brute-force search or manual derivation.  
- Learning approaches significantly outperform random search, especially for complex target expressions.  
- Paper is generally well-written and ideas are clearly explained.  
- Minor issues with structure and clarity; the problem statement could be clearer.  
- Restrictions on the class of expressions considered need more formal justification.  
- Needs a more detailed discussion of limitations and future directions (e.g., generalizing to broader expressions, enforcing computational-complexity constraints).  
- Strengths: novel representation/search approach, effective ML-guided search, shows potential to discover complex identities.  
- Weaknesses: limited expression coverage, insufficient discussion of limitations/future work, minor structural/clarity problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and interesting approach, demonstrates ML potential, well-explained.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope, needs deeper limitations/future-work discussion, minor clarity issues.

- Potential impact: could significantly influence symbolic computation/software libraries.
