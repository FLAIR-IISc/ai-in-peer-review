- Introduces an interesting solution using distributed, recursive neural representations to classify algebraic identities.
- Problem definition is quite restricted, which may limit applicability and generalizability of the approach.
- Explanation of weight tensor W3 (l×l×l) is unclear; unclear whether a neural tensor network or a simple linear network is used—Bowman’s work suggests tensor networks can outperform RNNs.
- In Section 7.2 it’s unclear if the RNN is still used for classification during evaluation of different search strategies; the proposal-generation process in the search is poorly explained and needs clarification.
- Several typos and wording errors throughout the paper need correction (e.g., multiple phrasing fixes and Figure 3’s abbreviation should be RNN, not TNN).
- The task is nontrivial and the complexity of algebraic identities can be effectively captured by the proposed approach.
