- Introduces two methods for discovering simplified symbolic expression trees: an n-gram heuristic and an RNN-based continuous representation.
- N-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of three evaluated tasks (Fig. 3), suggesting practical advantages in some cases.
- Technical execution is sound with rigorous empirical evaluation across multiple benchmarks.
- Experimental design adequately supports the paper’s claims.
- Clarity issues: Section 7.2 is poorly structured and obscures key RNN implementation details; overall exposition needs tighter organization and clearer terminology.
- Originality: presents two distinct, non-trivial methodological contributions that depart from conventional symbolic optimization techniques.
- Significance is limited: practical relevance is unclear, the problem formulation appears contrived, and comparisons to standard techniques (algebraic simplification, CSE, numerical approximation) are lacking.
- Recommendation: potentially accept only if authors substantially revise motivation to clarify real-world relevance and improve exposition of the RNN approach; otherwise the contribution may not resonate with the community.
