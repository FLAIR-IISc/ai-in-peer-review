- Paper proposes two methods for searching simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network for continuous representations.  
- N-gram method computes statistics over sequences of sub-expressions to guide search.  
- RNN method builds recursive neural embeddings of expression trees to predict rules for combining subtrees.  
- Experimental result (Figure 3): the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.  
- Quality is rated high.  
- Clarity is lacking, particularly Section 7.2 which is hard to follow.  
- Originality is high.  
- Significance is low: practical utility is questionable, adoption seems unlikely, the problem feels somewhat artificial, and broader impact is unclear.
