This paper introduces two approaches to identify more concise symbolic expression trees. The first method leverages n-grams calculated from sequences of sub-expressions, while the second employs a recursive neural network to generate continuous vector representations of these trees. These representations are then utilized to predict likely rule applications for combining subtrees. Based on the results presented in Figure 3, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the recursive neural network on two out of three tested tasks.

Regarding specific review criteria:

Quality – High.
Clarity – The paper’s presentation is challenging in places, particularly Section 7.2, hindering full comprehension.
Originality – High.
Significance – Low. The practical impact of this work is questionable. The problem addressed feels artificial, and it’s unclear how these methods for finding computationally efficient symbolic expression trees would translate to real-world applications or benefit those seeking to optimize computations. The overall significance of the proposed techniques remains limited.
