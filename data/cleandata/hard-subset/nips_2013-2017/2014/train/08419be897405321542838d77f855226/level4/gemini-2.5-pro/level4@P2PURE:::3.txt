This paper presents an interesting approach to discovering efficient algebraic identities, and the demonstration that distributed, recursive neural representations can successfully classify these identities is a notable contribution.

However, several aspects of the paper require clarification. The problem definition seems quite restricted, and the description of the model architecture could be improved. For instance, the paper states that it uses a "weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, so that the output is also size l)," but does not elaborate. Does this imply the use of a neural tensor network rather than a standard linear layer? As Bowman et al. have shown, tensor networks can perform significantly better than standard RNNs on similar tasks, so clarifying this architectural detail is important.

The methodology for identity discovery could also be explained more clearly. In Section 7.2, "Efficient Identity Discovery," it is unclear whether the RNN classifier is still being used in the evaluation of the different search strategies. Furthermore, the process for generating proposals during the search is not well-described and would benefit from a more detailed explanation.
