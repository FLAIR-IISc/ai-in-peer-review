

This paper presents an interesting approach to discovering efficient algebraic identities, demonstrating that distributed, recursive neural representations can successfully classify such identities. However, several concerns require clarification and address.

The problem definition is overly restricted, limiting the broader applicability of the work. The technical description of the weight tensor $W_3$ (of size $l \times l \times l$, producing an output of size $l$) is ambiguous. It is unclear whether the authors employ a neural tensor network (NTN) rather than a simple linear network, as implied by the tensor structure. Bowman et al. (2015) demonstrated that tensor networks significantly outperform RNNs for similar classification tasks; this context should be addressed.

Section 7.2 ("Efficient Identity Discovery") lacks clarity. It is uncertain whether the RNN is still used for classification while evaluating different search strategies. The proposal generation mechanism during the search process is not sufficiently explained and requires elaboration.
