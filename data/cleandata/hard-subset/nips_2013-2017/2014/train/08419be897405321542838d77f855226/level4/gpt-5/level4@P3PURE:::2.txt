This paper presents two methods for searching for simpler, more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees. The first relies on n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions. The second uses a recursive neural network to learn continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees; these representations are then used to predict which rule is likely to be applied to combine subtrees. According to Figure 3, the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.

Comments with respect to specific review criteria:
- Quality: High.
- Clarity: The paper is somewhat hard to follow, especially Section 7.2.
- Originality: High.
- Significance: Low. It is unlikely that practitioners aiming for efficient computation would adopt these methods; the problem setting feels contrived. While the paper proposes techniques for searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, the practical significance of these methods is unclear.
