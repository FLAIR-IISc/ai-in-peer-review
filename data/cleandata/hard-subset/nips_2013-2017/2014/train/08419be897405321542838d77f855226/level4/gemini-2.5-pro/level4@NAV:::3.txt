This paper presents a novel approach for discovering more efficient algebraic identities. The authors compellingly demonstrate that distributed, recursive neural representations can be effectively utilized to classify such identities.

However, the problem definition appears to be somewhat narrowly defined.

The following description of the model architecture lacks clarity:
"weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, so that the output is also size l),"
This phrasing is ambiguous and suggests the use of a neural tensor network rather than a standard linear layer. The authors should clarify this point, particularly since prior work by Bowman has shown that tensor networks significantly outperform RNNs on similar tasks.

Regarding Section 7.2, "Efficient Identity Discovery," the role of the RNN is not clearly explained. It is difficult to determine if the RNN is still being used for classification while different search strategies are being evaluated.

Furthermore, the process for generating proposals within the search algorithm is not sufficiently detailed and would benefit from a more thorough explanation.
