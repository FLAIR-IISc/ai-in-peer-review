- Introduces a novel approach for discovering more efficient algebraic identities using distributed, recursive neural representations.  
- Proposes an attribute grammar framework combined with ML (n-grams and RNNs) to guide search for computationally simpler expressions.  
- Demonstrates that learned models trained on simpler expressions can discover identities for more complex cases, achieving reductions like O(n^3)→O(n^2) and exponential→polynomial.  
- Problem scope is limited to homogeneous polynomials of a single matrix, reducing broader applicability.  
- Section 4.2 is unclear about weight tensor W3 (l×l×l) and whether a tensor network rather than a standard linear network is used; Bowman (2013) should be discussed.  
- Section 7.2 lacks a clear description of how the RNN interacts with the different search strategies and how proposals are generated.  
- Several typographical errors and a labeling mistake in Figure 3 (RNN vs TNN) need correction.  
- Strength — novelty: applying ML/RNNs to discover mathematical identities is a promising new direction.  
- Strength — effectiveness: experiments find identities beyond brute-force/manual methods with significant complexity reductions.  
- Strength — clear framework: attribute grammar provides a well-defined symbolic representation and manipulation scheme.  
- Strength — reproducibility: code and data are provided.  
- Strength — strong empirical results: methods substantially improve computational efficiency on tested expressions.  
- Weakness — restricted scope: single-matrix homogeneous polynomial focus limits generality.  
- Weakness — search strategy clarity: insufficient detail on how learned models steer the search.  
- Weakness — RNN vs tensor networks: neglects discussion of tensor network benefits shown in prior work.  
- Weakness — limited generalization: performance issues on the hardest family (RBM-2) indicate generalization limits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful approach with convincing experiments and available code/data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted scope, unclear search mechanism, and limited robustness on RBM-2.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contributions; weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify tensor use, detail the search strategy, address RBM-2 generalization limits, and fix typographical errors.
