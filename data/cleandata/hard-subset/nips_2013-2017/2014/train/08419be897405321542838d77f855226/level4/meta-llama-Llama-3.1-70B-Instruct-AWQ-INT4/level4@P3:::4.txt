This paper introduces a novel approach for representing symbolic expressions and finding their mathematical identities using machine learning techniques. The authors propose a framework based on attribute grammars to represent symbolic expressions and apply learning methods to discover efficient mathematical identities. The paper is well-motivated, and the idea of using machine learning to improve the efficiency of symbolic computations is intriguing.

The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their approach, including the grammar framework, the representation of symbolic expressions, and the search strategy. The use of recursive neural networks to learn a continuous representation of symbolic expressions is particularly interesting. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in discovering efficient identities for various mathematical expressions.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. The problem statement mentions that the goal is to find expressions with lower computational complexity, but it is not clear how this constraint is enforced in the algorithm. The authors should provide more discussion on how to limit the search space to solutions with lower computational complexity.

The restrictions mentioned in Section 2, such as limiting the domain to homogeneous polynomials of degree k, could be better justified. The authors should provide more formal or empirical evidence to support these restrictions. Additionally, the structure of the paper could be improved for better clarity. The different stages of the approach could be explained more clearly, and the relationships between the different components could be better highlighted.

The authors should also provide more discussion on the limitations of their approach and potential future directions. For example, how can the approach be extended to handle more complicated mathematical expressions? What are the potential applications of this approach in symbolic software libraries?
