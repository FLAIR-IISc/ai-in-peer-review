This paper proposes a novel application of deep neural networks to the task of discovering mathematical identities. While the direction is interesting, the work in its current form appears preliminary and does not yet meet the threshold for publication at NIPS.

The primary concern is that the proposed method does not demonstrate compelling performance. The results are not particularly impressive, especially considering the highly constrained set of input expressions used for evaluation. The class of expressions on which identities were sought is so limited that a simpler, symbolic, or rule-based approach might have been more effective. The application of a deep learning model to this specific problem seems unnecessarily complex, akin to using a "sledgehammer to crack a nut."

While the authors might argue that the framework could be extended to a more general class of expressions, the paper does not provide a clear path or convincing evidence for such scalability. Furthermore, the modest performance on the current, simplified task casts doubt on the potential efficacy of this approach for more complex problems.

In summary, while the paper introduces an intriguing idea, its contributions are undermined by the limited experimental scope and questionable justification for the method's complexity given the task. The work is promising but feels too preliminary for this venue.
