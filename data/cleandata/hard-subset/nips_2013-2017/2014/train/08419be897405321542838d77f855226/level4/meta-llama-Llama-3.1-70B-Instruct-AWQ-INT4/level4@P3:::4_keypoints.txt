- Introduces a novel ML-based approach using attribute grammars to represent symbolic expressions and discover mathematical identities.
- Well-motivated idea: using machine learning to improve efficiency of symbolic computations.
- Clear and detailed explanation of the grammar framework, expression representation, and search strategy.
- Interesting use of recursive neural networks to learn continuous representations of symbolic expressions.
- Experiments show effectiveness in discovering efficient identities for various expressions.
- Unclear how the algorithm enforces the goal of finding expressions with lower computational complexity.
- Need more discussion and mechanisms to limit the search space to solutions with lower computational complexity.
- Restrictions (e.g., homogeneous polynomials of degree k) are insufficiently justified; require formal or empirical support.
- Paper structure and presentation could be improved: stages of the approach and component relationships need clearer explanation.
- Lacks discussion of limitations, extensions to more complex expressions, and potential applications in symbolic software.
- Contains several minor typos/errors that should be corrected.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong motivation, and convincing experimental results.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear enforcement of complexity constraint, weak justification of restrictions, structural clarity issues, and minor errors.
