- Paper tackles simplifying symbolic expression trees using learned guidance to find computationally efficient, equivalent expressions, proposing two models: an n-gram over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network (RNN) embedding approach.

- Methods evaluated on three symbolic simplification tasks; empirical results (Fig. 3) show the n-gram model outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.

- The approach is original and technically well-executed, framing symbolic simplification within modern ML techniques.

- Clarity is lacking—exposition is difficult to follow overall, with Section 7.2 (RNN architecture and application) particularly dense.

- Section 7.2 needs a more detailed, step-by-step explanation of the forward pass and prediction, plus clearer diagrams or pseudocode.

- The significance and practical motivation are unclear: the connection between these offline search-based simplifications and real-world performance gains is not well-established.

- The paper should provide compelling use cases or domains (e.g., specific physics/engineering problems) where existing heuristics fail and learned methods offer tangible advantages.

- The observation that the simpler n-gram beats the RNN warrants deeper analysis—possible causes include RNN overfitting, insufficient data, or inadequate structural representation.

- A more thorough discussion of why the RNN underperforms and when each approach is preferable would add significant value.
