- Novel application of DNNs to mathematical identity discovery is interesting but primarily conceptual.
- Work appears preliminary and does not meet NeurIPS standards for impact or technical depth.
- Method shows only modest performance and achieves marginal gains over trivial baselines.
- Problem scope is highly constrained (very small input space), where manual derivation would be simpler and more efficient.
- Complexity of the DNN is disproportionate to the simplicity of the evaluated tasks (“sledgehammer to crack a nut”).
- Identities found are simple (e.g., polynomial relationships) and could be obtained by systematic algebra rather than ML.
- No concrete evidence or theoretical justification is provided for scalability or generalization to broader expression spaces.
- Paper lacks ablation studies, comparisons to non-DNN heuristics, and analysis of failure modes.
- Contribution reads as a proof-of-concept rather than a substantial methodological advance.
- Recommended revisions: demonstrate performance on larger/complex identity sets, provide theoretical reasons DNNs are uniquely suited, or show scalability and meaningful generalization; otherwise reject.
