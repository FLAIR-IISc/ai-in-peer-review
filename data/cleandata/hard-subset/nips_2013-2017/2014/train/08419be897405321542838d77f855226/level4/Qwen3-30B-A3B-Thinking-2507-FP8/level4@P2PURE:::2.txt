

This paper introduces two methods for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees. The first method employs n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network to generate continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations predict the likely rule for combining subtrees. As shown in Figure 3, the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.

Comments with respect to specific review criteria:  
Quality: High.  
Clarity: The paper is somewhat difficult to follow, particularly Section 7.2.  
Originality: High.  
Significance: Low. The problem formulation appears contrived, as it is unlikely that anyone seeking efficient computation would pursue this approach. The significance of the proposed methods remains unclear.
