- Paper proposes two methods for simplifying symbolic expression trees: an n-gram model over sub-expression sequences and an RNN that produces continuous tree representations.  
- Representations are used to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees.  
- Empirical result (Figure 3): n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.  
- Quality of the work assessed as high.  
- Clarity issues: paper is somewhat hard to follow, especially Section 7.2, which needs clearer explanations and streamlined presentation.  
- Originality judged high due to the novel combination of techniques and application.  
- Significance judged low: applicability to real-world, performance-critical computations is questionable and the practical impact is not convincingly demonstrated.
