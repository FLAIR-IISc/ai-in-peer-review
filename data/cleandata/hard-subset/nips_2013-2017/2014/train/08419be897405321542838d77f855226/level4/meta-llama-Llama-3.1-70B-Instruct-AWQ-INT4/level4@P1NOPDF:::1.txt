This paper presents an innovative application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the novel area of discovering mathematical identities. While I appreciate the novelty of the ideas, my overall impression is that this work seems preliminary and may not quite meet the standards expected of a NIPS publication. Specifically, the method's performance in identifying mathematical identities appears unremarkable, particularly given the very limited set of inputs provided. The expressions used to test the method were drawn from a narrow set, making it seem that developing a mathematical rule to discover these identities might have been more straightforward than employing DNNs. This approach may be seen as using excessive force to solve a relatively simple problem.

Although the authors might argue that their method can be extended to a broader range of expressions, it is unclear whether their current approach can be easily scaled up or if doing so would be justified, given the mediocre initial results. I find this paper interesting, but I believe it falls slightly short of the publication threshold.
