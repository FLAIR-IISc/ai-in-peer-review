This research paper describes two methods for finding simple symbolic expression trees. One is based on the use of engrams calculated on sequences of sub-expressions. The other uses a recursive neural network to create a continuous space representation of symbolic expression trees. These representations are then used to predict which rule is likely to be used to combine the subtrees. Figure 3 shows that using engrams seems to be better than an RNN for two out of three tasks.
