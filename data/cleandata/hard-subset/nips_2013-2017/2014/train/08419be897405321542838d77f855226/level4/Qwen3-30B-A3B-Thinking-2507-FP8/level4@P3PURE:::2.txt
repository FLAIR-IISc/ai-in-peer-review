

This paper introduces two methods for searching for simplified symbolic expression trees. The first method employs n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network to generate continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations are subsequently leveraged to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees. As evidenced in Figure 3, the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three evaluated tasks.

Comments regarding specific review criteria:  
**Quality**: High.  
**Clarity**: The paper is difficult to follow in places, particularly Section 7.2.  
**Originality**: High.  
**Significance**: Low. The problem addressed—searching for computationally efficient symbolic expression trees—appears contrived. Practitioners seeking efficient computation would not engage with this specific problem, rendering the significance of the proposed methods unclear.
