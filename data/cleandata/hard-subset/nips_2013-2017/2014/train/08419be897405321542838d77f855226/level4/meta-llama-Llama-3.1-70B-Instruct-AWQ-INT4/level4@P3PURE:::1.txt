This paper explores the application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to a novel area, namely the discovery of mathematical identities. While I find the ideas presented to be innovative, my overall impression is that this work appears to be preliminary and may not meet the standards expected of a NIPS publication. Specifically, the method proposed does not demonstrate impressive performance in identifying mathematical identities, despite being given a very limited set of inputs. 

The expressions used for discovering identities were drawn from a limited set, making it seem more practical to derive a mathematical rule for their discovery rather than employing DNNs. This approach may be seen as using a sledgehammer to crack a nut. Although the authors might argue that their method can be extended to a broader range of expressions, it is unclear whether the existing approach can be easily scaled up or if doing so would be sensible given the modest initial results.

I do find this paper interesting, but I believe it falls slightly below the threshold for publication.
