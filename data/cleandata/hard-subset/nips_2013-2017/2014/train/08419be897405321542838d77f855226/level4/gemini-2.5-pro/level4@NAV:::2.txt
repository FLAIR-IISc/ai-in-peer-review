This work introduces two approaches for identifying more concise symbolic expression trees. The first method utilizes n-grams computed from sequences of sub-expressions. The second employs a recursive neural network to generate continuous vector representations of the expression trees, which in turn are used to predict the likely rule for combining subtrees. Based on the results presented in Figure 3, the n-gram-based method seems to outperform the RNN approach on two of the three evaluated tasks.

Comments with respect to specific review criteria:
Quality - High.
Clarity - The manuscript is difficult to follow in parts; Section 7.2 is particularly challenging to comprehend.
Originality - High.
Significance - Low. The practical application for those aiming to optimize computational efficiency is questionable. The problem formulation appears artificial. Although this paper puts forward methods for discovering more computationally efficient symbolic expressions, the broader significance of these techniques is not well-established.
