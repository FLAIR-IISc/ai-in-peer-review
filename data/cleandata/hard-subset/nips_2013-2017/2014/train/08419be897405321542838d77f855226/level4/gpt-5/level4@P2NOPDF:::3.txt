This paper presents a promising approach to discovering more efficient algebraic identities and demonstrates that distributed, recursive neural representations can accurately classify these identities. The idea is compelling and the empirical success of recursive encodings is noteworthy.

That said, the problem formulation appears quite constrained. Please clarify the scope of the identities considered, the set of allowed operations, and how broadly the approach might generalize beyond the current setting.

Model specification and clarity:
- The description “weight tensor W3 (of size l × l × l, so that the output is also size l)” is unclear. This phrasing suggests a neural tensor network rather than a simple linear layer. If you are using a tensor composition function, please provide the exact equation, parameterization, and how it differs from a standard RNN composition (e.g., affine + nonlinearity). Explicitly state whether the tensor is third-order and how inputs are contracted to yield an l-dimensional output.
- Relatedly, Bowman et al. have reported that tensor networks outperform RNNs on related compositional semantics tasks. Please discuss how your architecture relates to that line of work and whether performance gains here are due to the tensor parameterization versus the search procedure or training data. An ablation comparing tensor vs. linear composition would help isolate the contribution.

Section 7.2 (Efficient Identity Discovery):
- It is unclear whether you still use the RNN-based classifier in this section to evaluate candidate identities, or if the focus shifts solely to comparing search strategies with a fixed evaluator. Please make the pipeline explicit: which model scores candidates, whether its parameters are updated during search, and how its outputs are used.
- The proposal mechanism needs more detail. How are initial candidates seeded? What rewrite rules/operations are allowed? What constraints govern expression size/depth? Are proposals generated stochastically (e.g., random rewrites, mutations) or deterministically (e.g., systematic expansions)? How are candidates scored (classifier confidence, estimated computational cost, validation accuracy)? What pruning and termination criteria are used? A brief pseudocode block or a worked example trace would greatly improve clarity.

Evaluation:
- Please clarify the metrics and datasets used for both classification and discovery. If discovery quality is measured by computational savings, specify the cost model and how correctness is verified.

Overall, this is an intriguing direction with encouraging results. Strengthening the problem definition, clarifying the model (especially the role of the tensor composition), and detailing the search proposal mechanism would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and impact.
