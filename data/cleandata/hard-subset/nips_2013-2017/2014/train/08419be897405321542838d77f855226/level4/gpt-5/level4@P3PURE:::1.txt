This paper applies deep neural networks to a novel task: discovering mathematical identities. While the idea is interesting, the work feels preliminary and does not yet meet the bar for a NIPS publication. Specifically, the method does not demonstrate particularly strong performance in discovering identities, even though it is evaluated on a very limited set of inputs. The expressions considered come from such a narrow class that it would likely be easier to devise a direct mathematical rule to find the identities, rather than employing DNNs; in that sense, the approach feels like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut.

One could argue that the approach might be extended to a much broader range of expressions. However, based on the current formulation and results, it is not clear that the existing method would scale easily, or that doing so would be sensible given the so-so initial performance.

Overall, this is an interesting paper, but at present it seems a little bit below the threshold for publication.
