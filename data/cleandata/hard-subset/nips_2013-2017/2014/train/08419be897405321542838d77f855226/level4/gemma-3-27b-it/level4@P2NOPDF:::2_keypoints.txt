- Introduces two novel methods for simplifying symbolic expression trees: an ngram-based method and an RNN-based method.  
- Ngram method uses statistical relationships between sub-expression sequences to guide search for simpler trees.  
- RNN method learns continuous tree representations to predict likely subtree combination rules.  
- Empirical results (Figure 3) show the ngram method outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.  
- Overall quality of the work is rated high.  
- Clarity needs improvement: explanations are occasionally hard to follow, notably Section 7.2; more detail and examples are needed.  
- Originality is high: novel combination of ngram techniques and recursive neural networks for this problem.  
- Significance is rated low: practical impact is questionable and the problem seems somewhat contrived.  
- Paper lacks a compelling argument or demonstrated applications showing why simplified trees matter beyond an academic exercise.  
- Recommends elaborating potential downstream tasks or measurable benefits to strengthen significance.
