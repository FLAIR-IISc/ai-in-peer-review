- Proposes two search methods for simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram method over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network (RNN) producing continuous representations to predict subtree-combining rules.
- Empirical results (Figure 3) show the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.
- Technical quality of the work is high.
- Paper clarity is lacking; Section 7.2 is particularly hard to follow.
- Methods are novel and originality is high.
- Significance is low: the problem feels contrived and the techniques seem unlikely to be adopted in practice, leaving practical impact unclear.
