- Proposes two methods to search for simpler, more efficient symbolic expression trees: an n-gram approach over sub-expression sequences and an RNN that learns continuous representations to predict rule applications.
- Empirical result (Figure 3): the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.
- Quality of the work is rated high.
- Clarity is limited; the paper is somewhat hard to follow, especially Section 7.2.
- Originality is rated high.
- Significance is rated low — methods are unlikely to be adopted by practitioners, the problem setting feels contrived, and practical impact is unclear.
