- Introduces a novel application: neural-network-based representations of symbolic expressions and discovery of mathematical identities.  
- Scope is limited and generalization to more complex expressions is unproven.  
- Core motivation is finding expressions with reduced computational complexity, but the algorithm does not enforce or favor complexity during search.  
- Request for discussion or strategies to limit search space toward computationally simpler solutions.  
- Restrictions (especially the scheduler) are presented ad-hoc; unclear if scheduler is the main bottleneck or if other factors limit scaling.  
- Asks for formal justification or empirical/ablation studies showing the impact of the imposed restrictions.  
- Paper structure and flow between representation, learning, and identity discovery are unclear; recommends clearer, subdivided methodology.  
- Unclear size limitations of vector/matrix representations; supplementary example (RBM-1 when k=1, n=1) appears invalid—needs clarification on dimensional constraints.  
- Lists specific minor grammatical errors to correct (page 332, 148, 289, 338).  
- Overall: novel and interesting work with potential impact, but needs stronger enforcement of complexity objectives, justification of restrictions, clearer presentation, and clarification of size limits.
