This paper explores the application of machine learning techniques to the discovery of efficient mathematical identities. The authors propose a novel attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and introduce two learning approaches, an n-gram model and a recursive neural network, to guide the search for efficient identities.

The paper relates to previous work in theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming, but focuses on a more restricted domain where the target expressions are homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree. The authors also draw inspiration from natural language processing techniques, such as n-gram models and recursive neural networks, to develop their approach.

The main contributions of the paper are the introduction of a novel grammar framework, the application of machine learning techniques to guide the search for efficient identities, and the discovery of new mathematical identities that offer significant reductions in computational complexity.

The authors evaluate their approach on five families of expressions and demonstrate that their learning-based methods can outperform a random search strategy, especially for larger values of k. However, the performance of the methods is not particularly impressive, and the authors acknowledge that the expressions considered are relatively simple.

The paper has some interesting ideas, but the reviewer questions whether the approach is scalable and whether the results are significant enough to warrant publication in NIPS. The reviewer also notes that the method may be overkill for the simple expressions considered and that a more mathematical approach might be more effective.
