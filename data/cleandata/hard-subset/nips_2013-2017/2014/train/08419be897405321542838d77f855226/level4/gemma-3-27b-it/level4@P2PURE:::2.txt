This paper presents two approaches to discovering simpler symbolic expression trees. The first method leverages n-grams calculated from sequences of sub-expressions, while the second employs a recursive neural network to generate continuous-space representations of these trees. These representations are subsequently used to predict the most probable rule for combining subtrees. Based on the results in Figure 3, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the recursive neural network on two out of three tested tasks.

Comments with respect to specific review criteria:

Quality – High.
Clarity – The paper’s clarity is somewhat limited, particularly in Section 7.2, which is difficult to follow.
Originality – High.
Significance – Low. The practical relevance of this work is questionable; it is unlikely that these methods would be applied in scenarios requiring efficient computation. The problem itself feels somewhat artificial. While the paper details methods for finding computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, the broader significance of these methods remains unclear.
