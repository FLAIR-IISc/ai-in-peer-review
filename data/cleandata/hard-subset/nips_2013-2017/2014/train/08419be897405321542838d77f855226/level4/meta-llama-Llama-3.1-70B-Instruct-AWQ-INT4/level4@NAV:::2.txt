This paper presents two novel approaches for simplifying symbolic expression trees. The first method leverages ngrams extracted from sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network to generate continuous representations of symbolic expression trees, which are then employed to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees. According to Figure 3, the ngram-based approach outperforms the RNN method in two out of three tasks.

Comments regarding specific review criteria:
Quality - The paper is of high quality.
Clarity - While the paper is generally well-written, certain sections, particularly Section 7.2, are somewhat challenging to follow.
Originality - The paper demonstrates high originality.
Significance - The significance of the proposed methods is limited. The problem addressed appears to be somewhat contrived, and it is unlikely that the methods would be adopted for efficient computations. The paper explores techniques for searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, but the practical impact of these methods remains unclear.
