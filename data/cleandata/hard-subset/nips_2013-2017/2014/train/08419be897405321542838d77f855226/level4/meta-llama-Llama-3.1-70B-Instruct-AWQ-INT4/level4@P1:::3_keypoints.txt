- Proposes an interesting method to find efficient algebraic identities using distributed, recursive neural representations that can classify identities.  
- Problem definition appears restrictive; authors should discuss limitations and generalization to broader settings.  
- Explanation of weight tensor W3 is unclear; clarify why size l×l×l is used and how it differs from a linear network.  
- Recommend comparing the approach to Bowman's neural tensor networks, which are relevant prior work.  
- Section 7.2 is unclear on whether the RNN is still used for classification and how the RNN integrates with evaluated search strategies.  
- The proposal-generation process in the search needs clearer explanation.  
- Several typos and wording errors throughout the paper; Figure 3 label should be RNN (not TNN).  
- Paper is technically sound; theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims.  
- Clarity can be improved by providing more context, background, and detailed explanations of key components.  
- Originality: presents a novel recursive neural approach with potential for broad applicability.  
- Significance: has important implications for AI/ML and potential use across domains (vision, NLP, etc.).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, significant contribution, and solid theoretical/experimental support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted problem scope, unclear explanations/limitations, and numerous typos to fix.
