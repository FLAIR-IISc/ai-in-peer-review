This paper applies deep neural networks to the novel task of discovering mathematical identities. While the direction is interesting, the work feels preliminary and, in my view, does not yet meet the bar for a NIPS publication. In particular, the method does not demonstrate especially strong performance at uncovering identities, even when evaluated on a very limited set of inputs. The set of expressions considered is so restricted that a simple, hand-crafted mathematical procedure might plausibly handle the task, making the DNN approach feel disproportionately heavy for the problem.

One could argue that the approach might generalize to a broader range of expressions, but it is not clear that the current method would scale effectively, or that such scaling would be warranted given the modest initial results.

Overall, this is an interesting paper, but I believe it is slightly below the threshold for acceptance.
