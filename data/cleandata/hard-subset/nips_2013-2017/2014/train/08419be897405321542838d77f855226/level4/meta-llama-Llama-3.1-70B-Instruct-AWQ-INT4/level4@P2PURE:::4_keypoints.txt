- Novel and interesting neural approach for representing symbolic expressions and finding identities; potentially impactful for symbolic software libraries, though the class of expressions studied is limited.

- Major concern: the paper emphasizes finding expressions with lower computational complexity but the algorithm does not enforce this; authors should discuss ways to limit the search to low-complexity solutions.

- Unclear restrictions in Section 2: it’s not demonstrated whether the scheduler is the sole limiting factor for more complicated expressions; request formal analysis or empirical experiments to justify restrictions.

- Paper organization is hard to follow; the stages of the approach should be explained more clearly.

- No discussion of constraints on vector/matrix sizes; e.g., the RBM-1 expression in the supplement appears invalid for k=1 and n=1.

- Several minor typos to fix (examples: “show for” p.332, “has contains” p.148, “is is” p.289, “by be” p.338).
