First, apologies for the brevity of this review—I had previously written a longer version with more detailed comments and noted a few typos, but I can’t locate it now.

This paper applies DNNs to a novel domain (discovering mathematical identities), and while the ideas are original, the work feels preliminary and may not quite meet the bar for a NIPS publication. Specifically, the method does not exhibit particularly strong performance at finding identities, even under a very limited set of inputs. The expressions considered come from such a constrained class that a straightforward mathematical procedure might have been simpler and more effective than deploying DNNs; it feels like using an overly powerful tool for a small task. One could argue the approach could be extended to a much broader range of expressions, but it is not clear that the current method would scale easily, or that doing so would be warranted given the middling initial results.

I do think this is an interesting paper, but it seems a bit below the threshold for publication.
