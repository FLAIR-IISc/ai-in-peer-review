- Novel application: Uses machine learning to discover efficient mathematical identities, a novel and potentially impactful direction.
- Framework: Introduces an attribute grammar to represent symbolic expressions and structure the search space.
- Learning-guided search: Employs n-gram models and recursive neural networks to direct search toward computationally cheaper equivalent expressions.
- Empirical gains: Demonstrates derivations that reduce computational complexity (e.g., O(n^3) to O(n^2)) with compelling examples.
- Scope limitation: Method is restricted to homogeneous polynomials of degree k with a single matrix variable, limiting generality.
- Justification weakness: The rationale for the restriction (Section 2) is not fully convincing and needs clearer motivation.
- Scalability concerns: Unclear whether scalability limits stem from the scheduler or other factors; more analysis required.
- Presentation issues: Paper structure is disjointed and would benefit from a clearer, more algorithmic description of the methodology.
- Complexity objective: The search does not explicitly enforce or prioritize reduced computational complexity, a potential shortcoming.
- Integration suggestion: Authors should discuss or demonstrate how complexity constraints could be incorporated into the search.
- Edge-case handling: Impact of vector/matrix sizes is inadequately addressed; at least one supplementary example appears invalid.
- Readability of examples: Examples are well-presented and effectively illustrate the method’s benefits.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem, promising results, and clear presentation of key ideas support acceptance.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited scope, lack of complexity constraints, and structural clarity concerns weaken the paper’s impact.
- Recommendation: Overall contribution is worthwhile; accept with minor revisions to clarify scope, add complexity discussion, and fix edge-case handling.
