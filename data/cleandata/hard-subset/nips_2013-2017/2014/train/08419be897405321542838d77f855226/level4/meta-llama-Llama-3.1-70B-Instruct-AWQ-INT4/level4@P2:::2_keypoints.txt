- Proposes a novel framework using an attribute grammar to represent symbolic expressions for discovering efficient mathematical identities.
- Introduces two learning-guided search approaches: an n-gram model (learns patterns from simpler expressions) and a recursive neural network (learns continuous representations).
- Demonstrates effectiveness by discovering new identities that substantially reduce computational complexity.
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of framework and methods.
- Evaluation is thorough and technically sound, with experiments across a range of tasks.
- Use of n-grams and RNNs is well-motivated and empirically validated.
- Related work coverage is insufficient, especially regarding theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming.
- Need for more discussion of limitations and directions for future work.
- Clarity issues remain in some sections (notably Section 7.2), which need more detailed explanations and examples.
- Originality is high due to the novel combination of ML and symbolic computation.
- Significance is somewhat limited for the broader NIPS community, reducing overall impact.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; overall rating 7/10.
