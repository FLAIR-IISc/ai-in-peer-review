This paper presents an interesting approach to discovering more efficient algebraic identities, and it’s promising to see that distributed, recursive neural representations can successfully classify them.

However, the problem definition appears somewhat limited in scope.

The explanation of the weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, resulting in an output of size l) is unclear. Is this indicative of a neural tensor network rather than a simple linear network? Bowman’s work demonstrated that tensor networks outperform RNNs in similar tasks.

Section 7.2, “Efficient Identity Discovery,” lacks clarity regarding whether the RNN is still employed for classification while evaluating different search strategies. 

I found the proposal generation process within the search to be difficult to follow and suggest a more detailed explanation.
