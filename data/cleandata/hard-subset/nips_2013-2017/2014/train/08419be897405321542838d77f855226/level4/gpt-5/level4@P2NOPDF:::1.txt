This paper applies deep neural networks to the discovery of mathematical identities. While the idea is novel and the direction is interesting, the work feels preliminary and, in its current form, below the bar for a NIPS publication.

My main concern is performance: the method does not demonstrate particularly strong results in discovering identities, even within a very constrained input space. The expressions considered come from a narrow, structured set, where simple rule-based approaches might be more direct and efficient. In this context, deploying a DNN seems like overkill.

The paper suggests that the approach could be extended to broader classes of expressions, but it is not clear how the current method would scale or whether such scaling would be justified given the modest initial results. The connection between the limited experiments and a convincing path to generality needs to be made more explicit.

Overall, this is an interesting direction with a novel application, but at present it appears slightly below the threshold for publication.
