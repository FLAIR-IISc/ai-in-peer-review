- Novel and promising application of neural networks to symbolic identity discovery, but the scope of expressions considered is limited.
- The stated goal of finding lower-complexity expressions is not enforced by the algorithm and the connection to the method is unclear.
- The paper should discuss potential strategies for constraining the search to low-complexity solutions (e.g., search heuristics, cost-aware schedulers, pruning).
- Section 2 restrictions are justified by the scheduler claim but need formal justification or empirical evidence showing how boundaries were chosen.
- Provide experiments or analysis that guided the choice of expression boundaries and demonstrate how they scale to more complex expressions.
- The paper’s organization hampers comprehension; explicitly separate and clearly explain representation learning, identity search, and constraint handling stages.
- Supplementary-material error: the RBM-1 example for k=1, n=1 appears invalid and should be verified or clarified with constraint conditions.
- Fix minor typographical errors: Line 332 "show for" → "shown for"; Line 148 "has contains" → "contains"; Line 289 "is is" → "is"; Line 338 "by be" → "by".
- Overall: core contribution is promising but currently lacks sufficient rigor on limitations and constraints; addressing the above will strengthen technical soundness and applicability.
