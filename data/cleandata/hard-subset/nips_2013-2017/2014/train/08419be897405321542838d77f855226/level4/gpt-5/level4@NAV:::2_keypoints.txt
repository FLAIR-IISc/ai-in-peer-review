- Paper proposes two methods for finding simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram method over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network (RNN) method.
- The RNN learns continuous-space representations of expression trees to predict the rule most likely to combine subtrees.
- Figure 3 shows the n-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.
- Quality: High.
- Clarity: Paper is somewhat difficult to follow, especially Section 7.2.
- Originality: High.
- Significance: Low — the problem seems contrived, adoption by practitioners focused on efficient computation looks unlikely, and the practical impact is unclear.
