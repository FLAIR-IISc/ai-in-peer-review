

The paper presents a novel application of neural networks for learning representations of symbolic expressions and discovering their mathematical identities. While the scope of expressions considered is limited and the authors do not provide proof of scalability to more complex expressions, I find this work intriguing and believe it holds potential for significant impact on symbolic computation libraries.  

- The problem statement emphasizes minimizing computational complexity, yet the algorithm does not enforce this constraint. Although this is challenging, the authors should address whether they considered strategies to restrict the search space to solutions satisfying this criterion.  
- Regarding the restrictions in Section 2, is the scheduler the sole factor limiting applicability to more complex expressions? A more formal justification or empirical evidence for these constraints would strengthen the argument.  
- The paper’s structure is difficult to follow; clearer delineation of the methodological stages would greatly improve readability.  
- A constraint on vector/matrix size appears necessary. For instance, the RBM-1 expression in the supplementary material (k=1, n=1) appears invalid without such a constraint.
