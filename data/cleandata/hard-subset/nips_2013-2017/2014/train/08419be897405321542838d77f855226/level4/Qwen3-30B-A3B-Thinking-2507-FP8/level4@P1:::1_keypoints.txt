- Proposes ML-guided discovery of efficient mathematical identities using attribute grammars and n-gram/RNN models.  
- Demonstrates framework on a limited set of polynomial expressions, finding identities that reduce computational complexity.  
- Contributions: grammar framework, ML-guided search using simpler expressions, RNN-based continuous representations, and discovery of new identities.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides good technical detail on representations and search strategies.  
- Experiments are thorough for the narrow problem scope (results for four families of expressions).  
- Strength: novel application of ML to symbolic computation; attribute grammars plus n-gram/RNN guidance is conceptually interesting.  
- Strength: learning continuous embeddings for symbolic expressions is a promising direction with NLP parallels.  
- Major concern: problem scope is extremely limited to specific homogeneous polynomials and narrow input structures, limiting applicability.  
- Concern: performance gains are modest relative to the approach complexity; search spaces shown are not large (e.g., 9,785 trees for k=6).  
- Concern: approach may be overkill—simpler methods (n-gram) outperform RNNs on these examples.  
- Concern: scalability is not convincingly demonstrated; search space grows exponentially and method fails on harder families for larger k.  
- Concern: significance and real-world impact are questionable; connections to dropout/RBM remain speculative without concrete applications.  
- Overall judgment: interesting preliminary work with clarity and some originality, but poor quality/significance for NIPS acceptance.  
- Recommendation: reject.
