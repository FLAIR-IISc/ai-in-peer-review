- Novel contribution: proposes using neural representations of symbolic expressions to discover mathematical identities.  
- Scope limitation: experiments restricted to limited expression classes and no proof of extensibility to more complex forms.  
- Potential impact: approach could significantly benefit development of symbolic software libraries.  
- Major concern: objective claims to find lower-complexity expressions, but the algorithm does not enforce or bias search toward lower computational complexity.  
- Major concern: unclear whether the scheduler is the sole restriction on expression complexity; paper needs formal justification or empirical evidence for these restrictions.  
- Major concern: paper structure and exposition are hard to follow; stages of the method should be explained more clearly and distinctly.  
- Major concern: unclear constraints on vector/matrix sizes in the representation; RBM-1 example in supplement appears invalid for k=1, n=1 and needs clarification.  
- Minor issues: several typos to fix—L148 "has contains", L289 "is is", L332 "show for", L338 "by be".
