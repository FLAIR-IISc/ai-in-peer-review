- Proposes two novel methods for searching simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram model over sub-expression sequences and an RNN that embeds trees to predict subtree-combining rules.  
- Uses the learned representations to predict the most likely rule to combine subtrees.  
- Experimental results (Fig. 3) show the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.  
- Technical content and presentation quality are high.  
- Clarity is somewhat compromised; Section 7.2 needs clearer explanation.  
- The methods are highly original.  
- Significance is judged low: the problem appears contrived and methods unlikely to be adopted in practice for efficiency.  
- Motivation and practical impact are weakly justified and remain unclear.
