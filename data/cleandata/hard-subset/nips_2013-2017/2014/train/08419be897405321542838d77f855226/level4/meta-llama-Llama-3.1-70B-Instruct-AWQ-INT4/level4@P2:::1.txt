This paper explores the application of machine learning techniques to the discovery of efficient mathematical identities. The authors introduce a novel attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and propose two learning approaches, an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN), to guide the search for efficient identities. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of these approaches in discovering complex identities that are beyond the reach of brute-force search or human derivation.

The authors relate their work to previous research in theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming, highlighting the unique aspects of their approach. They also discuss the potential applications of their framework in discovering novel formulae in broader areas of mathematics and its integration with compilers or optimization tools.

The paper presents experimental results on five families of expressions, showing that the learning approaches outperform a random strategy in discovering efficient identities. The authors also discuss the limitations of their approach and potential future directions.

Strengths:

* Novel application of machine learning techniques to mathematical identity discovery
* Introduction of a new attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions
* Demonstration of the effectiveness of learning approaches in discovering complex identities
* Potential applications in discovering novel formulae and integration with compilers or optimization tools

Weaknesses:

* Limited scope of expressions considered in the experiments
* So-so initial results, with room for improvement in the performance of the learning approaches
* Unclear scalability of the approach to a more general range of expressions

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting application of machine learning techniques to a new area.
* The introduction of a new attribute grammar framework and the demonstration of its effectiveness in discovering complex identities are significant contributions.
* The paper has potential implications for discovering novel formulae and improving the efficiency of mathematical computations.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The limited scope of expressions considered in the experiments may not be representative of the broader range of mathematical identities.
* The so-so initial results may not be sufficient to demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
* The unclear scalability of the approach to a more general range of expressions may limit its practical applications.

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting and novel application of machine learning techniques, its limitations and so-so initial results may make it a borderline case for publication. However, the potential implications and contributions of the paper make it worth considering for acceptance.
