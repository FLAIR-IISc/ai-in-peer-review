- Introduces two methods to discover computationally simpler, equivalent symbolic expression trees: an n-gram model over sub-expression sequences and an RNN-based recursive neural network.

- RNN method produces continuous vector representations of expression trees used to predict rules for combining subtrees.

- Empirical results (Figure 3) indicate the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three evaluation tasks.

- Quality: reviewer rates the work as high quality.

- Clarity: presentation is sometimes difficult to follow; Section 7.2 requires significant clarification.

- Originality: reviewer rates the work as highly original.

- Significance: reviewer rates the practical significance as low—problem feels contrived, adoption in practice is unclear, and overall impact is uncertain.
