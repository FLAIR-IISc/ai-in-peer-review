- Paper applies machine learning to discover efficient mathematical identities using a novel attribute grammar to represent symbolic expressions.
- Proposes two learning-guided search methods: an n-gram model and a recursive neural network.
- Focuses on a restricted domain: homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree.
- Draws inspiration from theorem proving, program induction, probabilistic programming, and NLP techniques.
- Main contributions: the grammar framework, ML-guided search, and discovery of new identities that reduce computational complexity.
- Evaluation on five expression families shows learning methods outperform random search, especially for larger k, but gains are modest.
- Reviewer notes expressions are relatively simple and methods’ performance is not particularly impressive.
- Major concerns: scalability to more complex expressions and potential overkill compared to mathematical approaches.
- Strengths highlighted: novel application of ML, new grammar representation, and discovery of useful identities.
- Weaknesses highlighted: limited performance, simplicity of test expressions, unclear scalability, and questionable fit for NIPS.
- Acceptance arguments: novel domain application, new framework, and interesting identity discoveries.
- Rejection arguments: limited empirical impact, simplicity of problems, and doubtful significance for a top conference (maybe more suitable for a smaller venue).
