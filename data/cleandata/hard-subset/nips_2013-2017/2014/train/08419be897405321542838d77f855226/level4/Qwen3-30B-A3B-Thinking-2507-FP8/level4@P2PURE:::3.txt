

This paper presents an interesting approach to discovering more efficient algebraic identities, demonstrating that distributed, recursive neural representations can successfully classify such identities. However, the problem definition appears overly restricted. The explanation of the weight tensor W3 (of size l×l×l, producing an output of size l) is unclear: does this indicate the use of a neural tensor network rather than a simple linear network? Bowman demonstrated that tensor networks significantly outperform RNNs for this task.  

In Section 7.2 ("Efficient Identity Discovery"), it remains unclear whether the RNN is still used for classification while evaluating different search strategies. The method for generating proposals during the search process is also inadequately explained and requires clarification.
