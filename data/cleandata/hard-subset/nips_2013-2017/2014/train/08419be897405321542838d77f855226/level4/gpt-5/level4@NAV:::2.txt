This paper presents two approaches for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees. One relies on ngrams computed over sequences of sub-expressions. The other employs a recursive neural network to learn continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees, which are then used to predict the rule most likely to combine subtrees. Figure 3 indicates that the ngram method outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.
