- Introduces two methods for searching simplified symbolic expression trees: an n-gram model over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network (RNN) producing continuous representations.  
- The RNN representations are used to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees.  
- Empirical result: the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of three evaluated tasks (Figure 3).  
- Overall quality of the work is rated high.  
- Clarity is problematic in places, particularly Section 7.2.  
- Originality is rated high.  
- Significance is rated low: the problem seems contrived and unlikely to attract practitioners seeking efficient computations.
