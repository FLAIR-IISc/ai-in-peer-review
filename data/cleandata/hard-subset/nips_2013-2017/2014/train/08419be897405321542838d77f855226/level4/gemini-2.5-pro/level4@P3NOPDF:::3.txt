### Review

This paper presents a novel approach to the problem of discovering efficient algebraic identities by framing it as a classification task solvable with recursive neural networks. The core idea of using distributed, recursive neural representations to learn and classify the equivalence of mathematical expressions is both interesting and promising. The demonstration that these models can successfully capture the abstract properties of algebraic identities is a valuable contribution.

While the overall direction is compelling, the paper could be significantly strengthened by addressing several points related to methodological clarity and the scope of the problem definition.

**Strengths:**

*   The application of recursive neural networks to symbolic mathematics, specifically for identifying algebraic identities, is a novel and creative research direction.
*   The paper successfully demonstrates that distributed representations can capture complex, abstract properties of mathematical expressions, paving the way for further research in this intersection of machine learning and symbolic reasoning.

**Major Weaknesses and Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Clarity of the Model Architecture:** The description of the model's architecture is ambiguous in a critical section. The paper states: "weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, so that the output is also size l)". This description strongly suggests the use of a Neural Tensor Network (NTN) rather than a simple linear layer for combining vector representations. This is a crucial detail, as prior work (e.g., Bowman et al.) has shown that tensor-based models can significantly outperform standard RNNs on tasks involving compositional semantics. Could the authors please clarify:
    *   Is the model using a Neural Tensor Network?
    *   If so, this architectural choice should be made explicit, justified, and potentially compared against simpler alternatives. If not, the description of this weight tensor and its operation needs to be rephrased to avoid confusion.

2.  **Clarity of the Search and Discovery Process (Section 7.2):** Section 7.2, "Efficient Identity Discovery," is difficult to follow. It is unclear how the components described earlier in the paper are utilized in this part of the experiment.
    *   Is the RNN-based classifier still being used to evaluate the expressions generated by the search strategies? Or is this section purely an evaluation of different search heuristics against a ground-truth oracle? Clarifying the role of the classifier in the discovery loop is essential.
    *   The mechanism for generating proposals within the search space is not sufficiently detailed. A more thorough explanation of how new expressions are proposed and how the search space is explored would greatly enhance the paper's reproducibility and clarity.

3.  **Restricted Problem Definition:** The problem definition, as presented, appears to be quite restricted. While this is acceptable for a proof-of-concept, the paper would benefit from a discussion on the limitations of the current formulation and the potential challenges in extending this methodology to a broader and more complex class of algebraic identities.
