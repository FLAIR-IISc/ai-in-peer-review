- Novelty: Interesting and unconventional idea to apply DNNs for discovering mathematical identities.

- Empirical performance: Results are modest and evaluated on a very limited, highly structured set where higher accuracy would be expected.

- Necessity of approach: Using a DNN seems excessive for the narrow problem; simpler rule-based methods could be more accurate, efficient, and interpretable.

- Generalizability: Claims that the method will scale to richer mathematical domains are unsubstantiated; unclear how it handles more operators, nesting, or different algebraic structures.

- Overall judgment: Paper is preliminary—a proof-of-concept on a toy problem—and not yet suitable for a top-tier venue.

- Recommendation: Either demonstrate overwhelmingly strong performance on the current task or apply the method to a more complex, meaningful class of expressions where traditional methods fail.
