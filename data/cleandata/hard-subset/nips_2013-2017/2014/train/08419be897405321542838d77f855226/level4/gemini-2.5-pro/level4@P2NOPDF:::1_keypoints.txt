- Novelty: Applying DNNs to discover mathematical identities is an intriguing and creative research direction.  
- Motivation: The paper is well-motivated in exploring machine learning for symbolic mathematics.  
- Limited empirical performance: Experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness or robustness.  
- Constrained problem scope: Evaluation covers a narrow class of expressions, making the complex DNN appear over-engineered compared to simpler rule-based alternatives.  
- Lack of justification for learning-based approach: The paper fails to justify why a DNN is preferable to simpler algorithms for the studied domain.  
- Unclear scalability: No convincing argument or plan is provided for scaling the method to broader, more complex expression spaces.  
- Incremental contribution: Given narrow scope, weak results, and scalability concerns, the work feels premature for a top-tier venue.  
- Minor issues: The manuscript contains typos and grammatical errors and needs careful proofreading.
