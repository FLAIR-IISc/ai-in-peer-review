This paper presents an application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to a novel problem – discovering mathematical identities – and while the ideas are interesting, my overall impression is that this work feels preliminary and may not quite meet the high standards for publication at NeurIPS. Specifically, the method doesn't demonstrate particularly strong performance in identity discovery, even considering the limited input set provided. The mathematical expressions used for finding identities were drawn from such a constrained set that it seems a relatively simple mathematical rule could have been devised to uncover them, rather than requiring the complexity of DNNs; it feels like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut. 

While the authors might argue that their approach could be generalized to a much wider range of expressions, it’s not clear to me that the current framework would scale effectively, or that such scaling would even be justified given the modest initial results. 

I do find this paper interesting, but ultimately believe it falls slightly below the threshold for publication.
