- Novel, interesting application of neural nets to learn representations of symbolic expressions and discover mathematical identities; could impact symbolic software libraries.
- Scope is limited and the paper lacks a proof or argument for extensibility to more complex expression classes.
- The problem statement seeks expressions with lower computational complexity, but the algorithm does not explicitly enforce this; authors should consider methods to constrain the search to lower-cost solutions.
- Clarify whether the scheduler is the sole factor limiting expression complexity (Section 2); provide formal justification or empirical evidence for the chosen restrictions.
- Paper structure and presentation are sometimes hard to follow; explain the different stages of the proposed method more distinctly.
- Clarify constraints on vector/matrix representation sizes; the RBM-1 expression in the supplement appears invalid for k=1, n=1.
- Minor typos to fix: Line 148 "has contains" → "contains"; Line 289 "is is" → "is"; Line 332 "show for" → "shows for"; Line 338 "by be" → (typo).
