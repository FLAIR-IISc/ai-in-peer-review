- Introduces an interesting solution for finding efficient algebraic identities using distributed recursive neural representations.  
- Problem definition is quite restricted (homogeneous polynomials), though authors acknowledge this and suggest generalizations.  
- Well-motivated framing connecting symbolic computation and limitations of theorem provers/symbolic engines.  
- Use of attribute grammars provides a structured framework to explore identity space.  
- Technically sound and novel combination of machine learning with grammar-based search.  
- Numerical and learned representations of symbolic expressions are clearly explained.  
- RNN-based representation demonstrates strong effectiveness for expression classification (Section 7.1).  
- Experimental setup is reasonable with clear comparisons between search strategies and ablations.  
- Authors carefully evaluate strengths and weaknesses; code and data are openly available.  
- Clarification needed on the “weight tensor W3” — is a neural tensor network being used and how does it compare to prior work (Bowman)?  
- Insufficient detail on how proposals are generated and how learning strategies concretely guide tree search beyond scoring grammar rules.  
- Several minor writing and presentation errors and awkward phrases need correction.  
- Figure 3 appears to refer to TNN while the paper emphasizes RNN—this is potentially confusing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel ML application to symbolic identity discovery, strong experimental results, well-defined method, and available code/data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted problem scope, unclear aspects of the search strategy, RNN not consistently superior to simpler baselines (e.g., n-grams), and difficulty on RBM-2 family.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, novel, and promising paper with weaknesses that do not outweigh its contributions; suitable for acceptance after revisions.
