This paper presents two approaches for searching for simplified symbolic expression trees. One uses n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions. The other employs a recursive neural network to learn continuous representations of expression trees, which are then used to predict which rule should be applied to combine subtrees. According to Figure 3, the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.

Comments wrt specific review criteria:
Quality - High.
Clarity - The presentation is somewhat difficult to follow, particularly Section 7.2.
Originality - High.
Significance - Low. It seems unlikely that someone focused on efficient computation would use this approach; the problem formulation feels contrived. The paper introduces search methods for identifying more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, but the practical significance of these methods remains unclear.
