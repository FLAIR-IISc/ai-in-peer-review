This paper proposes two novel methods for searching simpler symbolic expression trees. The first method utilizes n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the second employs a recursive neural network (RNN) to create continuous space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations are then used to predict the most likely rule to combine subtrees. 

The experimental results presented in Figure 3 suggest that the n-gram-based approach outperforms the RNN method on two out of three tasks. 

With regards to specific review criteria, the paper exhibits high quality in terms of its technical content and presentation. However, the clarity of the paper is somewhat compromised, particularly in Section 7.2, where the explanation could be improved for better understanding.

The paper demonstrates high originality by introducing innovative methods for searching simpler symbolic expression trees. Nevertheless, the significance of the proposed methods is relatively low. The problem addressed in the paper appears contrived, and it is unlikely that the methods would be employed in practice for efficient computations. The paper's focus on searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees is not well-justified, and the practical impact of the proposed methods remains unclear.
