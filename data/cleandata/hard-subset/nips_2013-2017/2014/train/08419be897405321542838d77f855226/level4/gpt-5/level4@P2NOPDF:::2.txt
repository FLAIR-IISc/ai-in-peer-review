This paper presents two approaches for discovering simpler symbolic expression trees. The first leverages n-gram statistics computed over sequences of subexpressions to guide the search. The second uses a recursive neural network to embed expression trees in a continuous vector space; these embeddings are then used to predict which production rule is most likely to combine subtrees effectively. According to Figure 3, the n-gram approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three evaluated tasks.

Comments with respect to the review criteria:
- Quality: High.
- Clarity: The exposition is at times difficult to follow, particularly Section 7.2.
- Originality: High.
- Significance: Low. The practical impact is unclear, and the problem setting feels somewhat contrived. Although the paper focuses on finding more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, it seems unlikely that practitioners aiming for efficient computation would adopt these methods. Overall, the significance of the contributions is uncertain.
