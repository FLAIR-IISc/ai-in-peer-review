This paper explores the application of machine learning techniques to discover efficient mathematical identities, specifically finding expressions with lower computational complexity than a given target. The authors introduce an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and address the computational intractability of exhaustive search with two novel approaches: an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN). The models are trained to learn from simpler expressions to guide the search for more complex identities.

The core idea is well-motivated, as machine learning’s success in pattern recognition suggests potential benefits for symbolic manipulation. The attribute grammar framework provides a structured approach to exploring the space of possible expressions. The introduction of learning-based search strategies is a significant contribution, aiming to overcome the exponential growth of the search space. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, discovering identities that are difficult or impossible to find manually or through brute-force search, such as reducing the complexity of certain matrix operations from O(n<sup>3</sup>) to O(n<sup>2</sup>).

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of attribute grammars and machine learning for symbolic identity discovery is a novel approach.
* **Technical Soundness:** The framework appears technically sound, with a clear problem definition and a well-defined search space. The use of integer-based descriptors to ensure accurate comparisons is a clever solution to potential numerical issues.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the n-gram model, in discovering efficient identities. The provided examples showcase substantial computational savings.
* **Clear Presentation of Examples:** The presentation of Example 1 and 2 effectively illustrates the problem and the benefits of the proposed approach.
* **Code Availability:**  Providing code and evaluation data fosters reproducibility and encourages further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity:** The paper could benefit from improved clarity, particularly in Section 7.2, which describes the experimental setup and results. More detailed explanations of the experimental families and the criteria for success would be helpful.
* **Significance:** While the technical achievements are noteworthy, the practical significance of discovering more efficient identities for these specific mathematical expressions remains unclear. The paper doesn’t convincingly demonstrate the broader applicability or impact of these findings.  The problem itself feels somewhat contrived.
* **RNN Performance:** The RNN approach consistently underperforms the n-gram model. A more thorough analysis of why this is the case, and potential avenues for improvement, would strengthen the paper.
* **Limited Scope:** The assumption of homogeneous polynomials and single-variable targets restricts the generality of the approach. While acknowledged by the authors, a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential for extending the framework would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in discovering non-trivial mathematical identities.
* The code and data availability promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The practical significance of the discovered identities is unclear.
* The clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in the experimental section.
* The RNN approach shows limited performance compared to the n-gram model.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and technically competent contribution to the field. While the practical significance of the work remains somewhat uncertain, the novelty of the approach and the positive experimental results warrant consideration. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and provide a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential impact of the research.  Specifically, the authors should expand the discussion of the broader applicability of this approach and address the underperformance of the RNN.
