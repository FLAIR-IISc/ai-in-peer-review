This paper presents an application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the novel problem of discovering mathematical identities. While the core idea is interesting, my assessment is that the work currently represents a preliminary investigation that may not yet meet the standards for publication at NeurIPS. Specifically, the demonstrated performance in identity discovery is not particularly compelling, especially considering the constrained input space. The mathematical expressions used for evaluation were limited in scope, to the extent that deriving identities through direct mathematical reasoning may have been more efficient than employing DNNs – an approach that currently feels disproportionately complex for the task. While the authors may argue for potential scalability to a broader range of expressions, it's unclear whether the existing methodology would readily extend in that manner, or if such an expansion would be justified given the modest initial results. 

I find this paper to be a worthwhile exploration, but believe it currently falls slightly short of the publication threshold.
