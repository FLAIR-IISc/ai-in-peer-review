This paper proposes a novel approach to discovering efficient mathematical identities using machine learning techniques. The authors introduce an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and develop two learning-based methods to search for simpler expression trees. The first method employs n-gram models to capture patterns in successful solutions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network (RNN) to learn a continuous representation of symbolic expressions.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solutions. The use of attribute grammars and learning-based methods is a unique combination that sets this work apart from existing approaches in theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming.

The quality of the paper is high, with a thorough description of the proposed methods and experimental results that demonstrate their effectiveness. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, including a comparison of the n-gram and RNN methods, and discuss the limitations of their approach.

However, the clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in Section 7.2, which describes the experimental results. The text is dense, and the figures are not clearly explained. Additionally, the notation and terminology used in the paper may be unfamiliar to some readers, making it challenging to follow.

The originality of the paper is high, as it combines attribute grammars and learning-based methods in a novel way. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work, highlighting the contributions of their approach.

The significance of the paper is moderate. While the proposed methods are effective in discovering efficient mathematical identities, it is unclear whether they would be widely adopted in practice. The problem addressed in the paper is somewhat contrived, and it is uncertain whether the methods would be applicable to more general mathematical expressions.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to discovering efficient mathematical identities using machine learning techniques.
