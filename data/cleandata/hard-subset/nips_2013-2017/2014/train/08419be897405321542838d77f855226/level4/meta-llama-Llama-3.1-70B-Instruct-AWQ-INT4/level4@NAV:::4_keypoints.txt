- Novel application: proposes neural-network-based representations of symbolic expressions and identification of mathematical identities.  
- Limited scope: experiments cover a narrow class of expressions; generalization to more complex expressions is unclear.  
- Potential impact: approach could benefit symbolic software libraries if extended.  
- Objective mismatch: paper emphasizes finding lower-complexity expressions but does not enforce this constraint in the algorithm.  
- Missing discussion: authors should describe strategies to restrict the search space to solutions meeting computational-complexity goals.  
- Scheduler-driven restrictions: limitations in Section 2 seem dictated by the scheduler; require formal justification or empirical evidence.  
- Ambiguity on constraints: unclear whether vector/matrix size constraints exist (e.g., RBM-1 expression may be invalid for k=1, n=1).  
- Presentation issues: paper structure and stage explanations need clearer, more explicit organization.  
- Minor errors: several typos noted (“show for”, “has contains”, “is is”, “by be”).
