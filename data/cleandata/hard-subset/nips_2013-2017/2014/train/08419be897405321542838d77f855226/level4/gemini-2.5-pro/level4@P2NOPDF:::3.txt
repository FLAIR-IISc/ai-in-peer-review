This paper presents a novel and interesting approach to discovering efficient algebraic identities. The core idea of framing this as a classification task for distributed, recursive neural representations is promising, and it is compelling to see that this method can successfully learn to classify valid identities.

However, the paper has several areas that require significant clarification to allow the reader to fully understand the contribution and assess its validity.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Clarity of the Model Architecture:** A primary point of confusion is the exact architecture of the neural model. The paper states that it uses a "weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, so that the output is also size l)." This description strongly suggests the use of a Neural Tensor Network (NTN), not a standard Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) with a simple linear layer. This is a critical distinction, as prior work (e.g., Bowman et al.) has demonstrated that tensor networks can significantly outperform standard RNNs on related tasks. The authors must explicitly clarify whether the model is an RNN or an NTN. This ambiguity is further compounded by the caption for Figure 3, which refers to a "TNN" when the main text refers to an RNN. A precise and unambiguous description of the model is essential for reproducibility and for properly contextualizing the results.

2.  **Scope of the Problem Definition:** The problem definition, while interesting, seems quite restricted. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of the scope of algebraic identities the proposed method can address. How might this framework generalize to more complex or different types of mathematical structures beyond those presented in the paper?

3.  **Explanation of the Search Process:** The method for generating proposals during the identity search is not explained clearly. A more detailed, perhaps algorithmic, description of how candidate expressions are proposed and explored is needed. The current explanation is difficult to follow and hinders a full understanding of the discovery process.

4.  **Clarity of Experiments in Section 7.2:** In the section on "Efficient Identity Discovery," the experimental setup is unclear. It is not specified whether the previously trained RNN/TNN classifier is being used to guide the search or if this section is only evaluating different search heuristics independently of the classifier. Please clarify the exact role of the trained model during this phase of the experiment.
