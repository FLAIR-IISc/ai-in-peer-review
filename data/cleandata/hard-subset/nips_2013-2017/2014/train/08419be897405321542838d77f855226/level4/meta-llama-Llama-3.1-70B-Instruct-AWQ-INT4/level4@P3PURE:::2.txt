This paper proposes two novel methods for searching simpler symbolic expression trees. The first method leverages n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network (RNN) to create continuous space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations are then employed to predict the rule likely to be used to combine subtrees. Notably, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the RNN on two out of the three tasks, as evident from Figure 3.

Comments regarding specific review criteria:

Quality: High.

Clarity: The paper's clarity is somewhat compromised, particularly in Section 7.2, which may benefit from further elaboration to facilitate easier comprehension.

Originality: High.

Significance: Low. It is unlikely that individuals seeking efficient computations would adopt this approach in practice. The problem addressed in the paper appears contrived, and the significance of the proposed methods remains unclear. While the paper presents innovative ways to search for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, the practical impact and relevance of these methods are not convincingly demonstrated.
