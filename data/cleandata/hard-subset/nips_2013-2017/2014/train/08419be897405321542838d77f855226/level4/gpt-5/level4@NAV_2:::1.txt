This paper applies deep neural networks to a new domain (discovering mathematical identities). While the core idea is original, the work feels preliminary and, in my view, falls a bit short of the NIPS threshold. In particular, the method does not demonstrate especially strong performance at uncovering identities, even under a very constrained input setup. The identity families explored are narrow enough that a targeted mathematical rule system might plausibly be simpler and more effective than deploying DNNs; the approach feels like using a heavy tool for a comparatively small problem. One could argue the framework might extend to richer classes of expressions, but it’s not clear that the current method would scale well in practice, or that such scaling would be justified given the middling initial results.

I find the paper interesting, but overall I judge it slightly below the bar for acceptance.
