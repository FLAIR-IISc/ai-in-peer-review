- Paper proposes two methods: an n-gram approach on sequences of sub-expressions and a recursive neural network that produces continuous vector representations to predict subtree combination rules.
- N-gram method outperforms the recursive neural network on two of three tasks (Figure 3).
- Overall quality judged excellent.
- Clarity needs work, with Section 7.2 particularly difficult to follow.
- Work is highly original.
- Significance is limited: practical relevance of efficiently searching for symbolic expression trees is questionable and the problem feels somewhat artificial.
- Methods focus on finding more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, but broader impact is unclear.
