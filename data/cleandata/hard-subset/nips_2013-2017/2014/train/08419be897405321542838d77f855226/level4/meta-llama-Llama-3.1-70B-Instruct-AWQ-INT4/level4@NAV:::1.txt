This paper presents a novel application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the discovery of mathematical identities. While the ideas are innovative, my overall impression is that this work appears to be preliminary and may not meet the standards for publication at NIPS. Notably, the method's performance in identifying identities is not particularly impressive, even when considering the limited input set provided. The expressions used for identity discovery were drawn from a narrow range, making it seem more practical to establish a mathematical rule for their discovery rather than employing DNNs, which might be an overly complex approach for this specific problem.

Although the authors may argue that their method can be extended to a broader range of expressions, it is unclear whether the existing approach can be easily scaled up or if doing so would be justified given the moderate initial results. Despite finding the paper interesting, I believe it falls slightly short of the publication threshold.
