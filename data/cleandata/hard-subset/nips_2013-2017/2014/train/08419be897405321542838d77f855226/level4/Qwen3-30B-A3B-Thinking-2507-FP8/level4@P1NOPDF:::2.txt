

This paper proposes two methods for finding simpler symbolic expression trees. The first approach leverages n-grams derived from sequences of sub-expressions, while the second employs a recursive neural network (RNN) to embed symbolic expression trees into continuous vector spaces. These embeddings predict the most probable rule for combining subtrees. As shown in Figure 3, the n-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of the three benchmark tasks.  

**Specific Comments on Review Criteria:**  
*Quality:* High. The technical execution is sound, and the experimental design adequately tests the proposed methods.  

*Clarity:* Moderate. The paper is generally well-structured but becomes difficult to follow in Section 7.2, particularly regarding the interpretation of embedding-based predictions. A clearer exposition of the RNN’s training objective and the n-gram sequence construction would improve readability.  

*Originality:* High. The use of n-grams for symbolic expression search and the embedding-based RNN approach represent novel contributions to the literature on symbolic computation.  

*Significance:* Low. The problem addressed—searching for computationally simpler symbolic expression trees—lacks practical relevance. The methods described do not align with real-world computational optimization needs; practitioners seeking efficient computation would not adopt this framework. The problem itself appears contrived, diminishing the paper’s broader impact. The proposed techniques, while technically interesting, fail to demonstrate meaningful applicability to existing challenges in symbolic AI or computational efficiency.
