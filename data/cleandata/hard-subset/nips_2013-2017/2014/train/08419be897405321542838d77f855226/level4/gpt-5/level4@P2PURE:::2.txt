This paper presents two approaches for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees. The first uses n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions. The second employs a recursive neural network to produce continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees, which are then used to predict which rule is likely to be applied to combine subtrees. Based on Figure 3, the n-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks.

Comments on specific review criteria:
- Quality: High.
- Clarity: The paper is somewhat difficult to follow, especially Section 7.2.
- Originality: High.
- Significance: Low. It is unlikely that anyone focused on efficient computation would adopt this approach; the problem seems contrived. While the paper proposes methods for searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, the significance of these methods remains unclear.
