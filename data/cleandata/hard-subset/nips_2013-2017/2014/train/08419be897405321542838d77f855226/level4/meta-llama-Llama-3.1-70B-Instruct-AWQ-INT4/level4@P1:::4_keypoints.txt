- Novel and interesting approach for representing symbolic expressions and discovering mathematical identities, but limited in the classes of expressions it handles.
- Problem statement clearly targets finding expressions with lower computational complexity, yet the algorithm does not explicitly enforce or guarantee this constraint.
- Recommend incorporating complexity metrics or pruning techniques to bias or limit the search to low-complexity solutions.
- Restrictions (homogeneous polynomials of degree k, single matrix/vector A) are reasonable but need formal or empirical justification and discussion of their impact on applicability.
- Paper structure could be improved by breaking the methodology into clearer stages with distinct headings and summaries.
- Implementation details lack clarity on constraints like vector/matrix sizes; examples (e.g., RBM-1) may be invalid for certain k and n, hurting reproducibility.
- Several minor typos are present (pages 332, 148, 289, 338) and should be corrected.
- Overall promising contribution that could be valuable after addressing the above concerns.
