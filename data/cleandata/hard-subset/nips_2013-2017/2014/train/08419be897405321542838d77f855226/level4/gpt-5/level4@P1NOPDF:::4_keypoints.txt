- Novel contribution: proposes learning representations for symbolic expressions and discovering mathematical identities, but studies a very limited class of expressions.  
- Potential impact: could meaningfully affect symbolic software libraries despite limited scope and unclear extensibility.  
- Objective mismatch: paper claims goal is finding lower-complexity expressions, but the algorithm does not enforce or guarantee reduced computational cost.  
- Request methods: reviewers ask whether the authors considered restricting search to lower-cost solutions (e.g., via regularization, cost-aware scoring, or constrained decoding).  
- Section 2 restrictions: question whether the scheduler is the sole limiter on applying the method to more complex expressions; need formal justification or empirical ablations.  
- Sensitivity analysis: ask for experiments showing why imposed restrictions are necessary and how performance changes when relaxing them.  
- Structure/exposition: paper is hard to follow; recommend clearer, sequential presentation of method stages with explicit inputs/outputs and transitions.  
- Dimensionality clarity: clarify constraints on vector/matrix sizes and edge cases (e.g., k=1, n=1 where RBM-1 expression seems invalid).  
- Minor proofreading: fix typos (“has contains”, “is is”, “show for”, “by be”) and perform general copyediting.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and novel but needs stronger connection between stated objective and search procedure, clearer justification of restrictions, and improved exposition to increase impact.
