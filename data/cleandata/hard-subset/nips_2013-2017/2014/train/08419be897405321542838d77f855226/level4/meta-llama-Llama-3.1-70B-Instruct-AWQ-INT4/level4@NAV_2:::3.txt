This paper introduces an interesting solution to finding more efficient algebraic identities. It is very interesting to see that distributed, recursive neural representations can successfully classify these identities.

The paper explores how machine learning techniques can be applied to the discovery of efficient mathematical identities. The authors propose a novel attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions, which allows for the discovery of complex identities beyond the reach of brute-force search or human derivation. The framework uses two learning approaches: a simple n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN). The RNN is trained to learn a continuous representation of symbolic expressions, enabling the application of machine learning techniques to symbolic computation.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their framework by discovering new mathematical identities that offer significant reductions in computational complexity for certain expressions. The paper also discusses the limitations of the current approach and potential avenues for future work, such as applying the framework to more general settings or using more complex grammar rules.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The use of examples and figures helps to illustrate the concepts and make the paper more engaging. However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved.

One potential limitation of the paper is the restricted problem definition, which may limit the applicability of the framework to more general settings. Additionally, some of the technical details, such as the use of weight tensors in the RNN, could be explained more clearly. Furthermore, the evaluation of different search strategies in Section 7.2 could be clarified, and the proposal generation process in the search could be better explained.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and novel solution to finding more efficient algebraic identities, and the use of recursive neural representations is a promising approach for symbolic computation. With some minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
