This paper presents two methods for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees. One method leverages n-grams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the other utilizes a recursive neural network (RNN) to create continuous space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations are then employed to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees. 

According to Figure 3, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the RNN method on two out of the three tasks.

Comments regarding specific review criteria:

Quality - High.
 
Clarity - The paper is somewhat difficult to follow, particularly in Section 7.2, where the text could benefit from further clarification and elaboration.

Originality - High.

Significance - Low. It is unlikely that anyone seeking to perform efficient computations would adopt this approach, as the problem addressed in the paper seems contrived. The paper describes methods for searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, but the significance and practical impact of these methods are unclear.
