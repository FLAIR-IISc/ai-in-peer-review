- Introduces method for discovering efficient algebraic identities using attribute grammars and ML (n-gram models and a recursive neural network).
- Demonstrates that distributed, recursive neural representations can classify algebraic identities.
- Problem definition is overly restricted (homogeneous polynomials, single matrix variable), limiting immediate applicability.
- Explanation of the weight tensor W3 (l × l × l) is unclear—uncertain if a neural tensor network is used instead of a linear map.
- Section 7.2 is ambiguous about whether the RNN is used for classification or if different search strategies are being evaluated.
- Proposal generation within the search process is not clearly explained and needs more detail.
- Several typos and errors throughout the paper (e.g., phrasing mistakes and Figure 3 label should be TNN not RNN).
- Novelty: combining attribute grammars with machine learning and using recursive neural representations is a novel contribution.
- Demonstrated success: the method finds non-trivial identities with large computational gains (e.g., O(n^3) to O(n^2)).
- Clear problem formulation and well-defined constraints create a manageable search space.
- Experiments are comprehensive across multiple expression families and compare several search strategies.
- Code and data are released, supporting reproducibility and further research.
- Weakness: n-gram models consistently outperform the RNN; the cause of RNN underperformance is not analyzed.
- Limited generalization: the RNN’s performance degrades on more complex expression families (e.g., RBM-2).
- Method’s efficiency is heavily dependent on the design of the attribute grammar.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, effective results, reproducibility, and potential impact on symbolic computation and optimization.
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted scope, unclear explanations, and underwhelming RNN results.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify explanations (tensor and search), analyze RNN performance, and discuss scope expansion.
