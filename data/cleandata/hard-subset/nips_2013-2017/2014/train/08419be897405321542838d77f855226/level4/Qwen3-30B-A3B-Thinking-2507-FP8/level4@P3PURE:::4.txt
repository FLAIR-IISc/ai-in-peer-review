

The paper introduces a novel application of neural networks for learning representations of symbolic expressions and identifying their mathematical identities. While the scope of expressions considered is limited and the authors provide no proof of scalability to more complex expressions, the approach is interesting and could have significant impact on symbolic software libraries.

The following concerns require clarification:
- The problem statement emphasizes finding expressions with lower computational complexity, yet the algorithm enforces no such constraint. The authors should address how to limit the search space to satisfy this requirement, as it is non-trivial.
- Section 2 outlines restrictions on expression complexity. Is the scheduler the sole limiting factor for handling more complex mathematical expressions? More formal justification or empirical evidence for these restrictions would strengthen the work.
- The paper's structure is difficult to follow. The sequential stages should be explained more clearly to improve readability.
- For k=1, n=1, the RBM-1 expression in the supplementary material appears invalid. Is there an unstated constraint on vector/matrix size that resolves this?
