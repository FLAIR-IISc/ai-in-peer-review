- Introduces an interesting solution for finding efficient algebraic identities using distributed, recursive neural representations.  
- Shows that recursive neural representations can successfully classify algebraic identities.  
- Problem definition is restricted: homogeneous polynomial of degree k, single matrix/vector A, scalar output O, which may limit applicability.  
- Despite restrictions, the setting still allows a rich family of expressions to explore.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed framework.  
- Introduces an attribute grammar framework to represent symbolic expressions and build expression trees.  
- Proposes two learning approaches—n-gram model and recursive neural network (RNN)—to learn from simpler expressions and guide tree search.  
- Experiments demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness; learning approaches outperform random strategy for large k.  
- 3-gram, 4-gram, and 5-gram models sometimes outperform the RNN for certain expression families.  
- Clarification needed on the weight tensor W3—unclear if a neural tensor network or a simple linear network is used.  
- Proposal generation in the search strategy is not explained clearly and needs better description.  
- Several typos and awkward phrasings throughout the paper that should be corrected (multiple examples provided).  
- Figure 1 underscores task difficulty; one sentence needs rephrasing ("which can easily captured with").  
- Figure 3 caption should be corrected from "TNN" to "RNN".  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel ML-based solution, clear framework, and promising experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted problem scope, unclear technical details, and minor presentation issues.
