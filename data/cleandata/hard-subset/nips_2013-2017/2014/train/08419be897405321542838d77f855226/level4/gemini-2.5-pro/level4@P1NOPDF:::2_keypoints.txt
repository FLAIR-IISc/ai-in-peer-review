- Proposes two methods for finding simpler, more efficient symbolic expression trees: an n-gram model over sequences of sub-expressions and an RNN-based recursive embedding approach.  
- The RNN approach learns continuous vector representations of trees to predict the optimal rule for combining subtrees.  
- Experimental results (Figure 3) show the n-gram model outperforms the RNN on two of three evaluation tasks.  
- Overall quality of the work is rated high.  
- Originality of the methods is rated high.  
- Clarity is an issue: exposition is sometimes hard to follow, with Section 7.2 particularly problematic.  
- Significance is low: the problem seems contrived with limited practical relevance and unclear real-world adoption.
