The paper presents a new application of neural networks – learning representations of symbolic expressions to discover mathematical identities. Despite the current limitation to relatively simple expressions and the lack of demonstrated scalability to more complex cases, I find the work intriguing and believe it holds potential benefits for symbolic computation libraries.

Regarding the stated goal of finding expressions with reduced computational complexity, the algorithm doesn't appear to explicitly enforce this constraint. While I recognize this is a challenging problem, I would be interested to know if the authors have considered strategies for limiting the search space to solutions with lower complexity.

Concerning the restrictions detailed in Section 2, it's unclear if the scheduler is the sole bottleneck preventing application to more complex mathematical expressions. A more formal justification or empirical analysis supporting these restrictions would strengthen the work. 

Furthermore, the paper’s structure could be improved for clarity; a more explicit explanation of the different stages would aid reader comprehension. 

Finally, there’s a question regarding constraints on vector/matrix sizes. Specifically, the expression for RBM-1 in the supplementary material (for k=1, n=1) appears to be invalid.
