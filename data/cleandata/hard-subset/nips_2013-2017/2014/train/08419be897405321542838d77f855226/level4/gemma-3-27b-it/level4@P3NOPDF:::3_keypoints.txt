- Positive contribution: introduces distributed, recursive neural representations to discover efficient algebraic identities and demonstrates successful classification.  
- Limited problem scope: the paper’s problem definition is narrow and its generalizability to broader algebraic structures is unclear.  
- Network architecture ambiguity: description of weight tensor W3 is unclear—need to state whether a Neural Tensor Network (NTN) or a standard/RNN is used and justify the choice, citing Bowman’s related findings.  
- Section 7.2 unclear: unclear if the RNN is still used for classification during evaluation of search strategies; roles of the neural network versus the search algorithm must be delineated.  
- Proposal generation under-specified: the method for generating/sampling candidate identities and the criteria guiding the search are poorly explained; include a concrete example.  
- Experimental evaluation lacking: insufficient comparative results against existing methods and vague claims (e.g., “which can easily captured with”) need clarification and stronger empirical support.  
- Figure labeling error: Figure 3 appears to mislabel the network as “TNN” when it seems to be an RNN and should be corrected.  
- Numerous minor errors/typos: several grammatical and typographical mistakes (examples provided) reduce polish and should be fixed.  
- Overall recommendation: clarifying the network architecture and search procedure and discussing limitations and potential generalization will substantially improve clarity and impact.
