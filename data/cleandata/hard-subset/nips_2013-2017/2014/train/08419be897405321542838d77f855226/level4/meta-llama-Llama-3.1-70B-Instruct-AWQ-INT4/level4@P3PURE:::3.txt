This paper introduces an interesting solution to finding more efficient algebraic identities, leveraging distributed, recursive neural representations to successfully classify these identities.

However, the problem definition appears to be quite restricted, which may limit the applicability and generalizability of the proposed approach.

The explanation of the weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, resulting in an output of the same size l) is unclear. Specifically, it is not evident whether the authors employ a neural tensor network instead of a simple linear network. Notably, Bowman has demonstrated that tensor networks outperform RNNs in similar tasks.

In Section 7.2, Efficient Identity Discovery, it is unclear whether the authors continue to use the RNN for classification while evaluating different search strategies. Furthermore, the proposal generation process in the search is not well-explained and could benefit from additional clarification.

There are several typos throughout the paper that should be corrected, including: "has contains a single matrix" should be "has a single matrix", "listed in table Table 1" should be "listed in Table 1", "vector is the presented to the next operation" should be "vector is presented to the next operation", "The final vector is pass to a softmax classifier" should be "The final vector is passed to a softmax classifier", "as to how explore space of possible" should be "as to how to explore the space of possibilities", and "The scheduler is is fixed" should be "The scheduler is fixed". Additionally, the abbreviation in Figure 3 should be RNN, not TNN.
