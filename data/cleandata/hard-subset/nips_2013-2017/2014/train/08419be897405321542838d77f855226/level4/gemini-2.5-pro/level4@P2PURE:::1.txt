This paper explores the novel application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the problem of discovering mathematical identities. While the direction is interesting, the work in its current form feels preliminary and may not meet the publication standards for NIPS.

My primary concerns are as follows:

1.  **Limited Performance and Scope:** The method's performance in discovering identities is not compelling, particularly given the highly constrained set of input expressions used in the experiments. For such a limited problem space, the results presented are not sufficiently impressive.

2.  **Questionable Necessity of the Approach:** The set of expressions is so restricted that a simpler, symbolic, or rule-based method would likely be more effective and efficient. The use of a DNN for this specific task seems unnecessarily complex—akin to using a sledgehammer to crack a nut.

3.  **Unclear Path to Generalization:** While the authors might argue that the approach could be extended to a more general range of expressions, it is not clear from the paper how the current framework would scale to more complex domains. Furthermore, the modest results on this initial, limited task do not provide strong evidence that scaling the approach would be a fruitful endeavor.

In summary, while the paper introduces an intriguing idea, its contributions are undermined by the limited scope of the experiments, unconvincing results, and significant questions regarding the method's scalability and overall necessity. For these reasons, I believe the paper falls below the threshold for publication at this venue.
