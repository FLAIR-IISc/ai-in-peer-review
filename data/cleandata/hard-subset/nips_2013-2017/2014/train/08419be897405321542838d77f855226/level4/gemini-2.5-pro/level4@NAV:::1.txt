This paper presents a novel application of Deep Neural Networks to the domain of discovering mathematical identities. While the core idea is intriguing, my overall assessment is that the work, in its current form, is still exploratory and may not yet meet the high threshold for publication at this venue.

Specifically, the proposed method demonstrates modest performance in identifying new identities, particularly given the highly constrained set of input expressions used for evaluation. The problem scope is narrow enough that it seems plausible that a more direct, rule-based mathematical approach could have achieved similar or better results. This raises the question of whether a complex DNN-based approach is necessary for the task as presented, potentially representing an over-engineered solution for this specific problem set.

While the authors may contend that this framework is a proof-of-concept that can be generalized to a much broader class of expressions, the paper does not yet provide convincing evidence that the current approach would scale effectively. Furthermore, given the modest initial results, the justification for scaling this particular methodology is not fully established.

In summary, while this is a thought-provoking paper exploring a creative research direction, I believe the contributions, as they stand, fall slightly short of the bar for acceptance.
