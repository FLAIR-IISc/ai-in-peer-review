- Introduces a novel framework combining attribute grammars with learned models (n-grams, RNNs) to discover efficient mathematical identities by representing expressions as grammar-derived trees.  
- Well-motivated work that bridges symbolic computation and machine learning, clearly stating limitations of prior methods.  
- Thorough related work discussion, distinguishing the approach from theorem proving, program induction, probabilistic programming, and symbolic engines like Maple/Mathematica.  
- Novelty: applying RNNs to learn continuous representations of mathematical structures and guiding grammar-based search is a genuine contribution.  
- Empirical results show the method discovers nontrivial identities and complexity reductions (e.g., O(n^3) → O(n^2)) that are hard for brute-force or manual search.  
- Problem statement is clear and the authors appropriately acknowledge current restrictions on expression types.  
- Code and evaluation data are publicly available, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Weakness: the search does not explicitly enforce or prioritize computational complexity reduction, and the paper lacks strategies to incorporate this constraint.  
- Weakness: applicability is limited by restrictions (homogeneous polynomials, single matrix variable); needs deeper analysis of whether the scheduler or other factors prevent generalization.  
- Weakness: paper structure and clarity could be improved—components (grammar, representation, search) should be more cleanly separated and the search description simplified.  
- Minor technical issue: clarify the RBM-1 expression edge case for k=1, n=1 in the supplementary material.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with strong empirical evidence and reproducible code, likely to impact symbolic computation and compiler optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalizability, lack of explicit complexity optimization, and organizational/clarity shortcomings.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution recommended for acceptance conditional on addressing the complexity-constraint discussion, improving clarity, and explaining scalability to broader expressions.
