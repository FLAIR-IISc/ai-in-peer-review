This paper presents an application of deep neural networks (DNNs) to the novel problem of discovering mathematical identities. While the core idea is interesting, my assessment is that this work appears preliminary and may not yet meet the high standards for publication at NeurIPS. Specifically, the method’s performance in discovering identities is not particularly compelling, especially considering the limited scope of the input expressions. The expressions used for identity discovery were drawn from a restricted set, suggesting that a more direct, rule-based approach might have been more efficient than employing DNNs – a situation that feels akin to using a sledgehammer to crack a nut. Although the authors may argue that their approach is extensible to a broader range of expressions, it is unclear whether the current methodology would scale effectively, or whether such scaling would even be justified given the modest initial results. 

I find this paper intriguing, but believe it currently falls slightly below the publication threshold for this conference.
