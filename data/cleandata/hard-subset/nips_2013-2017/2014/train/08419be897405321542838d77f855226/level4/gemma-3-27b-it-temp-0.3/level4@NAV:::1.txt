This paper presents an application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the novel problem of mathematical identity discovery. While the core idea is interesting, my assessment is that the work currently represents a preliminary exploration that may not yet meet the standards for publication at NeurIPS. Specifically, the demonstrated performance in discovering identities is not particularly compelling, particularly given the restricted scope of the input expressions. The limited diversity of expressions used suggests that traditional mathematical reasoning might offer a more efficient solution than the application of DNNs – a situation that feels disproportionately complex for the task at hand. While the authors may argue for the potential scalability of their approach to more general expression sets, it is unclear whether the current methodology would readily extend in a meaningful way, or whether such an extension would be justified given the modest initial results. 

I find this work intriguing, but believe it currently falls slightly short of the conference’s publication threshold.
