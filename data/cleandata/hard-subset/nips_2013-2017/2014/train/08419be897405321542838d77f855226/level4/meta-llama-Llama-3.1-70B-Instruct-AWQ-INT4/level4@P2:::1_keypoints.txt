- Paper applies machine learning to discovery of efficient mathematical identities.  
- Introduces an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions.  
- Proposes two learning-guided search methods: an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN).  
- Demonstrates discovery of complex identities beyond brute-force search or human derivation.  
- Relates work to theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming, highlighting novelty.  
- Shows experimental results on five expression families where learned methods outperform random search.  
- Discusses potential applications in discovering novel formulae and integrating with compilers/optimizers.  
- Strength: novel application of ML to a new problem domain.  
- Strength: new attribute grammar representation and demonstrated effectiveness.  
- Weakness: experiments cover a limited scope of expressions.  
- Weakness: initial results are modest and leave room for performance improvement.  
- Weakness: unclear scalability to a broader range of mathematical expressions.  
- Acceptance argument: significant novelty and potential impact warrant consideration.  
- Rejection argument: limited scope, so-so results, and uncertain scalability make it borderline.
