This paper applies Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to the novel problem of discovering mathematical identities. While the direction is intriguing, the work in its current form feels preliminary and may not yet meet the high threshold for publication at NIPS.

Specifically, the method does not demonstrate particularly impressive performance, even on the highly constrained set of input expressions used in the experiments. The domain of expressions explored is so limited that a simpler, symbolic, or rule-based method would likely be more effective and appropriate. The application of a DNN in this context feels like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut.

The authors may argue that this approach can be generalized to a much broader range of expressions. However, it is not clear how the proposed method would scale to more complex problems. Furthermore, given the modest results on this initial task, the justification for pursuing this specific deep learning approach over other alternatives is not yet convincing.

In conclusion, while I find the paper's direction interesting, I believe it is slightly below the bar for acceptance at this time.
