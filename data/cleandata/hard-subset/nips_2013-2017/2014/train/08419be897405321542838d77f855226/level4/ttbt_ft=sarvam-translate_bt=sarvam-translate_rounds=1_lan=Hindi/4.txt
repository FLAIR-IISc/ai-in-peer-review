This paper introduces a new application of neural nets, which is to learn a representation for symbolic expressions and find their mathematical identities.

Although the expressions studied are very limited, and there is no evidence that they can be extended to more complex expressions, I found this paper interesting. And I believe it could have a good impact for symbolic software libraries.

- The problem statement says we are looking for expressions with low computational complexity. But the algorithm does not impose any such constraint anywhere. I understand that this is not trivial, but have the authors taught about possible solutions to limit the search space to such solutions?
- Regarding the restrictions mentioned in Section 2, is the scheduler the only limiting factor for a more complex mathematical expression? I would prefer to see more formal or empirical experiments to determine these restrictions.
- Furthermore, the structure of the paper is not easy to follow. It would be better if the steps were explained more clearly.
- Is there any constraint for the size of the vector/matrix? For example, the expression for RBM-1 in the supplementary material does not seem valid for k=1, n=1.

The paper presented a new approach to representing symbolic expressions and finding their mathematical identities. Even though it is limited to the expressions it can handle, it is an interesting and new approach.
