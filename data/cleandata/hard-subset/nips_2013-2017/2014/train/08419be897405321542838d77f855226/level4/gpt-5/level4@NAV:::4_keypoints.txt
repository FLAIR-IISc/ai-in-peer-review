- Novel application: proposes learning representations of symbolic expressions and discovering mathematical identities; interesting and potentially impactful for symbolic software libraries.

- Limited scope: considered expression set is quite limited and there is no evidence the approach extends to more complex forms.

- Complexity objective mismatch: paper aims to find expressions with lower computational complexity but the algorithm does not appear to enforce this constraint; authors should consider ways to restrict the search space to such solutions.

- Section 2 restrictions unclear: is the scheduler the only bottleneck for handling more complex expressions? more formal treatment or empirical evidence is needed to motivate these constraints.

- Presentation issues: paper organization and exposition are hard to follow; stages need clearer explanation.

- Dimensionality constraints unclear: possible invalid expressions for certain dimensions (e.g., RBM-1 for k=1 and n=1 in supplementary material).

- Typos to fix: line 332 "show for"; 148 "has contains"; 289 "is is"; 338 "by be".

- Overall: method is novel and original despite limitations.
