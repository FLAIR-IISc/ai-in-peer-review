This paper explores the application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to a novel area, specifically finding mathematical identities. While the ideas presented are innovative, the work appears to be preliminary and may not meet the standards of a NIPS publication.

The primary concern is that the method does not demonstrate impressive performance in discovering identities, even with a limited set of inputs. The expressions used for finding identities are drawn from a narrow set, making it seem more practical to develop a mathematical rule for discovery rather than relying on DNNs. This approach may be seen as using a sledgehammer to crack a nut.

A potential argument is that this approach could be extended to a broader range of expressions. However, it is unclear whether the existing method can be easily scaled up or if doing so would be sensible given the mediocre initial results.

Despite these reservations, the paper is interesting, but it falls short of the publication threshold. To strengthen the work, the authors may need to provide more substantial evidence of the method's effectiveness and its potential for generalization to more complex expressions.
