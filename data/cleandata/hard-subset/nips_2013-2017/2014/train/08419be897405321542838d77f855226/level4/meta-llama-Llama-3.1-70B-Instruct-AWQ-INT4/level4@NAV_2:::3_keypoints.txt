- Introduces a novel method for discovering more efficient algebraic identities using machine learning.
- Proposes an attribute grammar framework to represent symbolic expressions and enable identity discovery.
- Implements two learning approaches: a simple n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN).
- Trains the RNN to learn continuous representations of symbolic expressions for symbolic computation.
- Demonstrates discovery of new identities that significantly reduce computational complexity for certain expressions.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and supported by helpful examples and figures.
- Limited problem definition may restrict the framework’s applicability to more general settings.
- Some technical details (e.g., use of weight tensors in the RNN) need clearer explanation.
- Evaluation of search strategies and the proposal generation process in the search require clarification.
- Suggests future work: broader application domains and more complex grammar rules.
- Overall verdict: promising and novel contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
