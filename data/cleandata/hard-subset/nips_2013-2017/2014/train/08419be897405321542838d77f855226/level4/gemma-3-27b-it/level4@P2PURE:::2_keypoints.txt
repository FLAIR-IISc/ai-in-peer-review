- Proposes two methods for discovering simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram approach over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network producing continuous tree representations.
- Continuous representations are used to predict the most probable rule for combining subtrees.
- Experimental results (Figure 3) show the n-gram approach outperforms the recursive neural network on two of three tasks.
- Overall quality rated high.
- Clarity is limited, especially Section 7.2 which is difficult to follow.
- Originality rated high.
- Significance rated low: practical relevance is questionable, the problem seems somewhat artificial, and methods are unlikely to be applied in computation-constrained scenarios.
- Paper focuses on methods for finding computationally efficient symbolic expression trees but the broader significance remains unclear.
