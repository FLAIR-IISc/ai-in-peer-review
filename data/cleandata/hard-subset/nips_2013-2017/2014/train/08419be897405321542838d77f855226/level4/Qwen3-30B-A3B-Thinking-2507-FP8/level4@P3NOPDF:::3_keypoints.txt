- Proposes using distributed, recursive neural representations to discover efficient algebraic identities; idea is promising and relevant to neural program synthesis.  
- Problem scope is overly narrow—evaluation restricted to fixed-size tensor-expressible identities without justification or scalability analysis.  
- Authors should explicitly state limitations of the restricted problem space and outline a pathway to generalization.  
- Neural architecture description is ambiguous: unclear whether an RNN or a tensor neural network (TNN) is used, and the "W3 (l×l×l)" notation is confusing.  
- Must clarify which architecture is used (RNN vs. TNN) and justify the choice relative to Bowman et al. (2015) showing TNN advantages.  
- Search strategy (Section 7.2) is opaque—missing how candidate proposals are generated (mutation rules, sampling distribution).  
- Unclear how the neural classifier affects proposal generation (e.g., via confidence scores, reinforcement learning) and what objective guides the search.  
- Provide a pseudocode snippet or high-level flowchart to make the search dynamics and proposal mechanism precise.  
- Empirical result strength: shows neural representations can classify algebraic identities, which is non-trivial.  
- Claims are overstated: the connection between classification accuracy and actual improvement in identity discovery/search efficiency is not rigorously established.  
- Missing analyses: how classification errors propagate to search failures and whether the model generalizes to unseen identities during search.  
- Paper contains numerous grammatical and typographical errors and inconsistent terminology (e.g., "TNN" vs. "RNN"); needs careful proofreading.  
- Required revisions for acceptance: clarify architecture, expand search methodology, justify or broaden problem scope, and correct language; current recommendation: minor revisions.
