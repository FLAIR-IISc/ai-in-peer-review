This paper applies DNNs to a novel problem—discovering mathematical identities. While the idea is interesting, the work feels preliminary and, in my view, does not quite rise to the level of a NIPS publication. In particular, the method does not demonstrate especially strong performance at finding identities, even under a very limited set of inputs. The expressions considered are so restricted that it would likely be simpler and more reliable to design explicit mathematical rules to uncover the identities, rather than employing DNNs; it feels like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut.

One might argue that the approach could be extended to a much broader class of expressions. However, it is not clear that the current method would scale effectively in that direction, or that such scaling would be justified given the middling initial results.

Overall, I find the paper interesting, but at present it falls slightly below the threshold for publication.
