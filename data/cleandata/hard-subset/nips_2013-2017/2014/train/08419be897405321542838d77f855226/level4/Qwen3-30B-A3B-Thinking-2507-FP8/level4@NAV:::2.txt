

This paper presents two approaches for identifying simplified symbolic expression trees. The first method employs ngrams derived from sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network to generate continuous-space representations of symbolic expression trees. These representations predict the likely rule for combining subtrees. As shown in Figure 3, the ngram-based approach outperforms the RNN on two of the three evaluation tasks.

Comments regarding specific review criteria:  
Quality - High.  
Clarity - The paper is somewhat difficult to follow, particularly in Section 7.2.  
Originality - High.  
Significance - Low. The problem addressed appears contrived, as it is unlikely that practitioners seeking computationally efficient symbolic expressions would adopt these methods. The practical relevance of the proposed techniques remains unclear.
