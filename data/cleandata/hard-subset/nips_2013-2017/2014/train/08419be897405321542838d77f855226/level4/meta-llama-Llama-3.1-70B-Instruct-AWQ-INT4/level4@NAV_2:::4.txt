The paper introduces a novel use case for machine learning techniques, which is learning to discover efficient mathematical identities. 

The authors propose a grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and develop two learning approaches to guide the search for efficient identities. The first approach uses an n-gram model to learn from simpler expressions, while the second approach employs a recursive neural network (RNN) to learn a continuous representation of symbolic expressions. 

The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of these approaches by discovering complex identities that are beyond the reach of brute-force search or human derivation. The results show that the learning approaches can significantly outperform a random search strategy, especially for complex target expressions.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly explained. However, there are some minor issues with the structure and clarity of the paper. For example, the problem statement could be more clearly defined, and the restrictions on the expressions considered could be more formally justified.

The paper could also benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential future directions. For instance, the authors could explore how to extend the approach to more general mathematical expressions or how to incorporate additional constraints, such as limiting the computational complexity of the expressions.

Overall, the paper is a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning and symbolic computation. The ideas presented are novel and interesting, and the results demonstrate the potential of machine learning techniques in discovering efficient mathematical identities.
