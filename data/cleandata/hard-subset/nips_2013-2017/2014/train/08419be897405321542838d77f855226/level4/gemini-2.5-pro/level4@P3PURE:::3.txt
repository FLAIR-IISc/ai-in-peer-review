This paper presents a novel approach for discovering more efficient algebraic identities. It is compelling to see that distributed, recursive neural representations can be successfully employed to classify such identities. However, the paper could be significantly improved by addressing several points of ambiguity and by providing more detailed explanations.

**Major Comments:**

*   **Problem Definition:** The problem definition appears to be quite restricted. The authors could strengthen the paper by discussing the scope of this definition and its limitations.
*   **Model Architecture Clarity:** The description of the model architecture lacks clarity in some areas. For instance, the paper mentions a "weight tensor W3 (of size lxlxl, so that the output is also size l)." Does this imply the use of a Neural Tensor Network rather than a standard linear layer? If so, this is a significant detail, especially since prior work (e.g., Bowman et al.) has shown that tensor networks can outperform standard RNNs on similar tasks. A more detailed explanation of this component is needed.
*   **Experimental Setup in Section 7.2:** In Section 7.2, "Efficient Identity Discovery," it is unclear whether the RNN is still being used for classification while different search strategies are being evaluated. Please clarify the experimental setup for this section.
*   **Proposal Generation:** The mechanism for generating proposals during the search process is not sufficiently detailed. This section would benefit from a clearer explanation of how the space of possible expressions is explored.
