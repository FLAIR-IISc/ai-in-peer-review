- Presents a novel framework that uses an attribute grammar plus ML-guided search (n‑gram models and an RNN) to find analytically equivalent expressions with lower computational complexity.
- Strength: Creative and significant application of machine learning to symbolic identity discovery, recovering non-trivial, useful identities (e.g., RBM partition-function–related).
- Strength: Introduces a learned continuous representation for symbolic expressions via an RNN, with strong classification accuracy supporting its effectiveness.
- Weakness: Problem scope is narrow—experiments focus on homogeneous polynomials of a single matrix—needs discussion on generalizing to multiple variables, non‑polynomial operators, recursion, and other output structures.
- Weakness: RNN architecture description is unclear about the W3 (l×l×l) tensor—need to state if a neural tensor layer is used and motivate this choice versus simpler alternatives.
- Weakness: Search and proposal-generation procedure is insufficiently detailed—unclear how the RNN classifier is repurposed as a scoring strategy, whether softmax is retrained, and how partial expression trees are evaluated/extended by the scheduler.
- Minor issues/typos: several wording and grammar fixes needed (abstract: "beyond reach" → "beyond the reach of"; multiple incorrect sentences in Sections 2–7 listed by reviewer) and clarify Figure 3 legend ("TNN" vs "RNN").
