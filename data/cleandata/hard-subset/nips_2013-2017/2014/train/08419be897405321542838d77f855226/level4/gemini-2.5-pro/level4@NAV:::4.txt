This paper presents a novel application of neural networks for learning representations of symbolic expressions and discovering their mathematical identities. Although the current work is confined to a limited class of expressions and does not yet demonstrate scalability to more complex forms, the approach is intriguing and holds considerable promise. The proposed method could have a significant impact on the development of symbolic software libraries.

- The problem is framed as a search for expressions with lower computational complexity. However, the proposed algorithm does not seem to explicitly enforce this constraint. While acknowledging the difficulty of this task, have the authors considered potential strategies to guide the search towards computationally simpler solutions?
- Regarding the limitations detailed in Section 2, it is not clear if the scheduler is the only component restricting the method to simpler mathematical expressions. The justification for these limitations would be strengthened by a more formal analysis or empirical results.
- The overall structure of the paper could be improved for clarity. A more distinct and detailed explanation of each stage of the proposed methodology would make the work easier to follow.
- Are there constraints on the dimensionality of the vectors/matrices? For example, the expression for RBM-1 in the supplementary material does not appear to hold for the specific case of k=1, n=1.
