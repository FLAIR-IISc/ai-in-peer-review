This paper presents two approaches to identifying more concise symbolic expression trees. The first method leverages n-grams calculated from sequences of sub-expressions, while the second employs a recursive neural network to generate continuous vector representations of these trees. These representations are then utilized to predict appropriate combination rules for subtrees. Based on the results presented in Figure 3, the n-gram approach appears to outperform the recursive neural network on two out of three evaluated tasks.

Regarding specific review criteria:

Quality – Excellent.
Clarity – The paper's presentation could be improved, particularly the details in Section 7.2, which are difficult to follow.
Originality – Highly original.
Significance – Limited. The practical relevance of efficiently searching for symbolic expression trees is questionable, and the problem itself feels somewhat artificial. While the paper details methods for finding more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees, the broader impact and significance of these methods remain unclear.
