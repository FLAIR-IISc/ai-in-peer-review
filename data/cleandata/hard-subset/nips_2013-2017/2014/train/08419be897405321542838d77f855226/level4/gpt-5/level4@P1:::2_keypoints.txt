- Proposes two methods to guide search for simpler symbolic expression trees: n-gram biasing and an RNN-based continuous representation with a classifier.
- Empirical result: n-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of three evaluated tasks (Figure 3).
- Quality: Technically sound approach and clear methodology; learning from simpler solutions to guide harder searches is well executed.
- Experimental finding: learned strategies can outperform random search, with n-grams particularly effective on reported tasks.
- Clarity: Paper is somewhat hard to follow, especially Section 7.2, which is dense and poorly organized.
- Originality: High — novel combination of n-gram models and RNN representations to guide symbolic expression search.
- Significance: Low — practical impact is unclear, the problem feels contrived, and adoption by practitioners seems unlikely.
- Pro: Technically solid learning-based search strategies.
- Pro: Clear experimental evidence that learned guidance improves over random search.
- Pro: Novel application of RNNs for continuous representations of symbolic expressions.
- Con: Clarity issues (notably Section 7.2) hinder understanding of empirical comparisons.
- Con: Practical utility is questionable and not convincingly demonstrated.
- Con: N-gram outperforming the RNN raises doubts about the added value of the more complex model.
