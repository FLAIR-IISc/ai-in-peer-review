This paper presents an application of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) to a novel problem – the discovery of mathematical identities. While the core ideas are interesting, my overall assessment is that this work feels preliminary and may not yet meet the high standards for publication at NeurIPS. Specifically, the demonstrated performance in discovering identities is not particularly compelling, especially considering the limited scope of the input expressions. The expressions used were drawn from a constrained set, such that deriving the identities through manual mathematical reasoning likely would have been more straightforward than employing DNNs; the approach feels somewhat disproportionate to the task. While the authors may argue that their method could be generalized to a broader range of expressions, it is unclear whether the current approach would scale effectively, or whether such scaling would be justified given the modest initial results. 

I find this paper to be an interesting exploration, but believe it currently falls slightly below the threshold for publication.
