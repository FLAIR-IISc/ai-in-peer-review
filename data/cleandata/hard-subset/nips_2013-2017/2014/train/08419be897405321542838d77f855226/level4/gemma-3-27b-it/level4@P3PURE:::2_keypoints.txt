- Proposes two methods for discovering simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram approach over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network for continuous tree representations.  
- Uses the learned representations to predict the most likely rule for combining subtrees.  
- Results (Fig. 3) show the n-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of three evaluated tasks.  
- Overall quality of the paper is rated high.  
- Clarity is poor, especially Section 7.2, making methodological details hard to follow.  
- Originality is high.  
- Significance is low: the problem feels contrived and practical relevance for efficient symbolic computation is unclear.
