First of all, I apologize for the brevity of this review – I wrote a version with more detailed comments and found some errors, but I can't find it now.

This paper is an application of DNA to a new field (finding mathematical identities), and I think the concepts are new, but it seems like preliminary work that may not be able to reach the level of NIPS publications. In particular, your method doesn't work very impressively for identity discovery, even when you use a very limited number of inputs. The expressions you were trying to find identities for were so limited that it would probably have been easier to create some mathematical rules and discover them than to apply DNA; it feels like trying to crack nuts with a hammer. Of course, you could argue that your method could be extended to more extensive expressions, but it's not clear to me that your existing method will easily extend in that way, or whether it would be meaningful to do so given your very- মোটামুটি (very- মোটামুটি) preliminary results.

I think it's an interesting paper, but I think it's probably a bit below the threshold for publication.
