- Proposes neural representations for symbolic expressions to discover mathematical identities; novel and potentially impactful despite limited scope.
- Strength: novel formulation linking continuous representations with symbolic identity discovery and potential practical gains in symbolic algebra.
- Strength: could reduce runtime costs by simplifying expressions, opening a new research direction at the intersection of neural models and computer algebra.
- Major concern: the method does not explicitly enforce or optimize for computational complexity during search and selection.
- Question: clarify how “lower complexity” is operationalized and whether cost-aware objectives (FLOPs, op counts, tree size, memory) are integrated.
- Suggestion: consider cost-informed search strategies (A*, complexity-aware beam search, MCTS) and operator/hardware-specific cost models or learned predictors.
- Major concern: unclear scope restrictions and role of the scheduler versus grammatical/typing constraints.
- Request: formalize grammar (operators, arities, associativity/commutativity, broadcasting) and typing/dimensionality rules, and justify chosen limits with ablations.
- Major concern: exposition is hard to follow; pipeline needs clear staging (representation learning, candidate generation, equivalence testing, selection) with a running example, diagram, and pseudocode.
- Major concern: dimensionality and validity checks are unclear; specify typing rules and shape inference and correct the RBM‑1 k=1,n=1 example.
- Major concern: limited scope—discuss extension to richer operators (non-commutative, matrix calculus, special functions), larger trees, and noisy/approximate identities, with any preliminary results.
- Suggestions: integrate complexity-aware scoring, cost-regularized/multi-objective training, and ablations over operator sets, depth, beam width, and scheduler budgets.
- Suggestion: add a formal grammar and type system with static shape checks and report the fraction of invalid candidates filtered.
- Minor issues/typos: fix phrasing at lines 148, 289, 332, 338 and clarify/correct Supplement RBM-1 dimensionality.
- Overall assessment: interesting and original but underspecified on complexity handling, constraints, and clarity; recommend acceptance if complexity is made a first-class objective, restrictions are formalized/justified, and clarity/dimensional checks are improved.
