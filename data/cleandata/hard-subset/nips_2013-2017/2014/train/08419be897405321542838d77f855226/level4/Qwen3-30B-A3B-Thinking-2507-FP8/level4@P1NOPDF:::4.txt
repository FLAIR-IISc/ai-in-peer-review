

The paper presents a novel application of neural networks for learning representations of symbolic expressions and identifying their mathematical identities. While the scope of expressions considered is limited, the approach is intriguing and holds potential impact for symbolic computation libraries. However, several concerns require clarification.

The problem statement emphasizes finding expressions with lower computational complexity, yet the algorithm does not enforce this constraint. The authors acknowledge this is non-trivial, but it would strengthen the work to discuss potential strategies for limiting the search space to low-complexity solutions. Without such discussion, the connection between the stated goal and the proposed method remains unclear.

Regarding the restrictions in Section 2, the paper states that the scheduler is the primary factor limiting the complexity of expressions. It would be valuable to see either formal justification for these restrictions or empirical evidence demonstrating how they were determined. For instance, what experiments guided the choice of these boundaries, and how might they scale to more complex expressions?

The paper's structure also hinders comprehension. Key stages are not explained with sufficient clarity, making it difficult to follow the methodology. A more logical organization—such as explicitly separating the representation learning phase, identity search mechanism, and constraint handling—would significantly improve readability.

Additionally, a technical concern arises in the supplementary material. For the RBM-1 case with k=1, n=1, the provided expression appears invalid. The authors should verify this example or clarify the constraints under which it holds.

Minor typographical errors require correction:
- Line 332: "show for" → "shown for"
- Line 148: "has contains" → "contains"
- Line 289: "is is" → "is"
- Line 338: "by be" → "by"

The core contribution—applying neural networks to symbolic identity discovery—is promising, but the current presentation lacks sufficient rigor in addressing the limitations and constraints. Addressing these points would substantially enhance the paper's technical soundness and broader applicability.
