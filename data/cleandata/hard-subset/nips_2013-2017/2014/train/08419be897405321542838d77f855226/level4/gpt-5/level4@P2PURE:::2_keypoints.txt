- Proposes two methods to search for simpler symbolic expression trees: an n-gram model over sub-expression sequences and a recursive neural network producing continuous representations to predict rule applications.  
- Empirical finding: the n-gram method outperforms the RNN on two of the three tasks (Figure 3).  
- Quality of the work is rated high.  
- Clarity issues: the paper is somewhat difficult to follow, with Section 7.2 particularly problematic.  
- Originality is rated high.  
- Significance is rated low: the approach seems unlikely to be adopted by those focused on efficient computation and the problem appears contrived.  
- Overall impact unclear: methods for searching more computationally efficient expression trees lack demonstrated practical significance.
