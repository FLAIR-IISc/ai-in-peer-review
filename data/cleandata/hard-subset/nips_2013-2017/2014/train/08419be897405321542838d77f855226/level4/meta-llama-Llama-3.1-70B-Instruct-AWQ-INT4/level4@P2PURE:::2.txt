This paper presents two methods for searching for simpler symbolic expression trees. The first approach leverages ngrams computed over sequences of sub-expressions, while the second utilizes a recursive neural network (RNN) to create continuous space representations of symbolic expression trees, which are then employed to predict the rule likely to be used to combine subtrees. According to Figure 3, the ngram-based method appears to outperform the RNN on two out of the three tasks.

Comments with respect to specific review criteria:

Quality - High.
Clarity - The paper's clarity is compromised, particularly in Section 7.2, which is somewhat difficult to follow.
Originality - High.
Significance - Low. The problem addressed in this paper seems contrived, and it is unlikely that individuals seeking to perform efficient computations would adopt this approach. This paper explores methods for searching for more computationally efficient symbolic expression trees; however, the significance of these methods remains unclear.
