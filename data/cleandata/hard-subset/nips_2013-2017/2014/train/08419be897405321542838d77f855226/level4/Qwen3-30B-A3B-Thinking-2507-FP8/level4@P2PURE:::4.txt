

The paper introduces a novel approach for learning representations of symbolic expressions and identifying their mathematical identities. While the scope is limited to relatively simple expressions and the authors do not provide proof of extension to more complex cases, the work presents an interesting and potentially impactful contribution for symbolic computation libraries.

Key concerns:
- The problem statement emphasizes finding expressions with lower computational complexity, yet the algorithm does not enforce this constraint. The authors should address whether they have considered techniques to restrict the search space toward such solutions.
- Section 2 discusses restrictions on expression complexity. Is the scheduler the sole limiting factor for handling more complicated mathematical expressions? Additional formal justification or empirical evidence for these restrictions would strengthen the work.
- The paper's structure is difficult to follow. Stages of the approach should be clearly delineated for improved readability.
- A constraint on vector/matrix size appears necessary. For instance, the RBM-1 expression in the supplementary material for k=1, n=1 appears invalid without such a constraint.
