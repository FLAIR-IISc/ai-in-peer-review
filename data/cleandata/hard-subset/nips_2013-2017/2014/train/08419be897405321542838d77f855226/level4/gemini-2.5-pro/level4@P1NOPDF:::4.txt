This paper introduces a novel application of neural networks: learning representations for symbolic expressions to discover their mathematical identities. While the scope of expressions studied is currently limited, and the authors do not yet provide a proof of extensibility to more complex forms, the approach is nonetheless interesting and novel. I believe this work could have a significant impact on the development of symbolic software libraries.

**Major Comments:**

1.  The problem statement specifies the goal of finding expressions with lower computational complexity. However, the proposed algorithm does not appear to enforce this constraint. While I recognize that this is a non-trivial challenge, have the authors considered potential methods for biasing the search towards, or constraining it to, solutions with lower complexity?

2.  Regarding the restrictions on expression complexity mentioned in Section 2, it is unclear if the scheduler is the sole limiting factor. The paper would be strengthened by a more formal justification for these restrictions or by empirical evidence supporting their necessity.

3.  The structure of the paper is somewhat difficult to follow. The exposition could be significantly improved by explaining the different stages of the proposed method more clearly and distinctly.

4.  Is there a constraint on the size of the vectors and matrices used in the representation? For example, the expression for RBM-1 provided in the supplementary material does not seem to be valid for the case where k=1 and n=1.
