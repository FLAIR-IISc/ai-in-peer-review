- Novel contribution: introduces learning representations for symbolic expressions to find mathematical identities.  
- Limited scope: studied expressions are very restricted with no proof of extensibility to more complex expressions.  
- Potential impact: approach could benefit symbolic software libraries.  
- Complexity mismatch: problem statement seeks lower computational complexity but the algorithm does not enforce this constraint; authors should consider ways to limit search to low-complexity solutions.  
- Section 2 restrictions: unclear whether the scheduler is the only limiting factor; more formal justification or empirical studies for these restrictions are needed.  
- Organization: paper structure and stages are hard to follow and should be explained more clearly.  
- Technical concern: unclear constraints on vector/matrix sizes—e.g., RBM-1 expression in supplement seems invalid for k=1, n=1.  
- Minor typos: 332 ("show for"), 148 ("has contains"), 289 ("is is"), 338 ("by be").
