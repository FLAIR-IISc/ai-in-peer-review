- Novelty: Combines attribute grammars with machine learning to discover mathematical identities, bridging symbolic computation and statistical learning.
- Technical soundness: Well-defined framework with practical integer-based descriptors and a clearly explained RNN architecture and training procedure.
- Empirical results: Discovers non-trivial identities (e.g., reducing O(n^3) to O(n^2)) that are hard to find by brute force or manually.
- Clear writing: Paper is generally well-written and well-organized.
- Code release: Authors provide code and evaluation data, aiding reproducibility.
- Limited scope: Restricted to homogeneous single-variable polynomials, limiting applicability.
- Hyperparameter tuning: Lacks systematic hyperparameter search and ablation for n-gram and RNN models; choices appear arbitrary.
- RNN performance: RNN underperforms n-gram models on simpler families and lacks deeper analysis explaining this gap.
- Linear combination dependence: Scalability and computational cost of the linear-system step for many candidate trees are not explored.
- Related work coverage: Related work section omits relevant areas such as symbolic regression and equation discovery.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach with compelling results and released code that opens new research directions.
- Arguments against acceptance: Narrow domain, inconsistent RNN gains over baselines, and weak hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: Add hyperparameter tuning/ablations, analyze linear combination complexity, expand related work (symbolic regression/equation discovery), and discuss extending to multivariate/general expressions.
