- Introduces an attribute-grammar framework and uses n-gram models plus RNNs to guide tree search for discovering efficient symbolic identities.  
- Demonstrates discovery of non-trivial identities that reduce computation (e.g., O(n^3) → O(n^2), exponential → polynomial).  
- Builds on solid prior work (attribute grammars, symbolic engines, theorem proving, program induction) and borrows NLP techniques (n-grams, RNNs).  
- Novelty: learns heuristics for search rather than relying on hand-designed rules, differentiating it from prior efforts.  
- Strength: core idea of using learning to navigate symbolic identity space is novel and promising.  
- Strength: experimental results are compelling and show significant computational improvements.  
- Strength: learned RNN representation for symbolic expressions is interesting and broadly applicable.  
- Strength: open-source code improves reproducibility and impact.  
- Weakness: problem scope is narrow (homogeneous polynomials, single matrix/vector), limiting generality.  
- Weakness: RNNs do not consistently outperform simpler n-gram baselines; dropout choice and training details need clarification.  
- Weakness: experiments focus on a few expression families; broader benchmarks would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of limitations of the learned representation and of why learned strategies succeed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scope, analysis of RNN behavior, and deeper explanation of why the methods work.
