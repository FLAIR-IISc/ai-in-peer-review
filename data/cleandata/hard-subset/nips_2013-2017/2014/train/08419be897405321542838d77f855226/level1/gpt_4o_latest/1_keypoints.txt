- Proposes a novel framework combining an attribute grammar with learning-based search (n-gram and RNN) to discover computationally efficient mathematical identities, generalizing from simpler to more complex expressions and demonstrating large complexity reductions (e.g., O(n^3) → O(n^2)).

Strengths
- Novelty: Introduces a unique integration of symbolic computation and machine learning to discover mathematical identities.
- Technical soundness: Well-constructed framework with clear grammar, models, and search strategies; uses both numerical and learned symbolic representations.
- Significance: Demonstrates substantial computational savings with potential applications in compiler optimization and RBM partition function computation.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-organized exposition, illustrative examples, and available code on GitHub.

Weaknesses
- Scope: Currently limited to homogeneous polynomials and specific grammar rules, leaving broader mathematical domains unexplored.
- Evaluation: Heavy reliance on synthetic benchmarks may limit assessment of real-world impact; more practical evaluations are needed.
- RNN performance: RNN underperforms compared to n-gram models; reasons and potential improvements are not fully analyzed.
- Computational cost: Large-scale enumeration and training raise scalability concerns for very complex or higher-degree problems.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and impactful application of ML to symbolic computation with promising research direction.
- Technical contributions (e.g., RNN for continuous symbolic representations) are innovative and could inspire follow-up work.
- Demonstrated improvements over brute-force and random search justify the approach’s practical utility.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited scope and synthetic evaluations reduce immediate applicability to broader or real-world problems.
- RNN’s weaker performance relative to simpler models questions the maturity of the learning approach.

Recommendation
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand discussion of limitations and provide more insight into the RNN’s performance.
