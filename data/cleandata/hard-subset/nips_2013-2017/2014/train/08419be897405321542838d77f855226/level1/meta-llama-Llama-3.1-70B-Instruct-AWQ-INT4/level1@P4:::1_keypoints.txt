- Proposes a novel framework using attribute grammars to discover efficient mathematical identities with ML-guided search.  
- Introduces two learning approaches—an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN)—to guide expression search.  
- Evaluated on several expression families; learning methods find complex identities beyond brute-force or human derivation.  
- Technically sound with clear descriptions; applying attribute grammars to identity discovery is novel and well-motivated.  
- N-gram and RNN approaches are well-described and effectively integrated into the search process.  
- Evaluation is thorough and well-documented for the chosen expression families.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible, balancing technical detail and high-level overview.  
- Novelty: RNN-based continuous representation of symbolic expressions is original and potentially broadly useful.  
- Significance: Demonstrates ML’s potential to advance symbolic computation with implications across CS, physics, and engineering.  
- For acceptance: novel application of ML, original use of grammars and RNNs, and strong evaluation on several families.  
- Against acceptance: definition of “mathematical identities” is narrow, raising questions about generalizability to more abstract expressions.  
- Against acceptance: unclear how the RNN representation scales to much more complex expressions.  
- Against acceptance: evaluation is limited to a small set of expression families, lacking evidence of broader applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address limitations and provide more evidence of generalizability.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
