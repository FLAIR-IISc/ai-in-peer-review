- Paper applies machine learning to discover mathematically equivalent but computationally cheaper expressions.  
- Core idea: use learning to navigate the exponential search space of symbolic transformations.  
- Method: frame search as exploration in an attribute grammar guided by n-gram models and RNNs.  
- Results: promising — discovers identities beyond manual derivation or brute-force search.  
- Motivation and related work: well-motivated and comprehensively positioned within theorem proving, program induction, probabilistic programming, and NLP.  
- Problem formulation: precise focus on polynomial simplification with tractable constraints.  
- Formalism: attribute grammars provide a clear framework for representing and manipulating expressions.  
- Strength — novelty: novel application of ML, especially RNNs, to identity discovery.  
- Strength — technical soundness: solid framework and engineering detail (integer-based descriptors to avoid numerical issues).  
- Strength — empirical results: n-gram models perform well; quantitative success rates and illustrative examples are clear.  
- Strength — clarity and reproducibility: well-written, informative figures, and released code/data.  
- Weakness — RNN performance: RNNs underperform relative to n-grams and lack in-depth analysis of why.  
- Weakness — limited scope: focus on homogeneous single-variable polynomials restricts broader applicability.  
- Weakness — search strategy details: insufficient description of the scheduler and sensitivity to scoring mechanisms.  
- Weakness — computational reporting: experiments use a 10-minute cutoff but lack precise resource and complexity reporting.  
- Weakness — comparisons: lacking direct, time-controlled comparisons against systems like Maple/Mathematica to confirm improvement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, empirically effective, reproducible, and potentially impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: underexplained RNN results, limited problem scope, and weak comparisons to existing symbolic engines.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: deeper RNN analysis (including representation visualizations), discussion of extending to general expressions, and comparisons with Maple/Mathematica plus clearer resource reporting.
