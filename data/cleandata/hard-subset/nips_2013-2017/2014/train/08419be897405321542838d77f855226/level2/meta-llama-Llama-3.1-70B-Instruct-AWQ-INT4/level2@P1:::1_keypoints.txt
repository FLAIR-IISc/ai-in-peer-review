- Proposes a novel attribute-grammar framework to represent symbolic expressions for discovering efficient mathematical identities.  
- Introduces two learning approaches—an n-gram model and a recursive neural network (RNN)—to guide search for efficient identities.  
- Demonstrates the approach can discover complex identities beyond brute-force search or manual derivation.  
- Experiments are thorough, well-explained, and show the effectiveness of the proposed methods.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clear, with detailed related-work discussion.  
- Method is innovative in applying machine learning to guide symbolic-identity search.  
- Significance: approach has potential to impact mathematics by automating discovery of efficient identities.  
- Strength: tackles a challenging problem in a novel and promising way using ML-guided search.  
- Limitation: scope is limited to a specific type of mathematical identity; generalization is not clearly explained.  
- Limitation: heavy reliance on ML models, with no clear strategy for cases where models fail to guide search.  
- Lack of discussion on how to extend the framework to more general mathematical problems.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with some limitations, recommended for acceptance.
