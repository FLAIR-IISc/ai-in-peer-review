- Proposes discovering efficient mathematical identities by combining attribute grammars with n-gram models and recursive neural networks.  
- Clearly defines the problem: find symbolic expressions equivalent to a target with lower computational complexity.  
- Novel application of machine learning to symbolic computation; RNN-based continuous representations for symbolic expressions are particularly interesting.  
- Uses integer-based descriptors and modulo arithmetic to avoid numerical instability.  
- Demonstrates practical results on challenging polynomial families and finds identities with significant computational benefits.  
- Learning approaches outperform random search on complex expressions (e.g., k=6), showing scalability against combinatorial explosion.  
- Related work is discussed (theorem proving, program induction, NLP) but needs stronger distinction from prior symbolic computation methods.  
- Insufficient explanation of why rule-based systems (Maple/Mathematica) are inadequate for this problem.  
- Lacks comparisons to other symbolic computation methods and state-of-the-art theorem provers—only compared against random search.  
- Claim that identities are “previously undiscovered” is hard to verify without a deeper literature review.  
- Technical and experimental details (RNN training, curriculum learning, initialization) are well-documented and support reproducibility.  
- Missing rigorous theoretical analysis of search space complexity and conditions for method success or failure.  
- Problem formulation is restrictive (homogeneous polynomials, fixed degree, single matrix/vector input), limiting broader applicability.  
- Promising applications (e.g., RBM partition function) remain speculative without concrete implementations or performance comparisons.  
- Could better articulate differences from program synthesis and grammar-based search and expand neural-symbolic reasoning connections.  
- Recommends strengthening related work, adding benchmarks/theoretical analysis, and outlining how to relax current constraints in future work.  
- Overall, an interesting and meaningful contribution whose strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommends acceptance conditional on addressing noted limitations.
