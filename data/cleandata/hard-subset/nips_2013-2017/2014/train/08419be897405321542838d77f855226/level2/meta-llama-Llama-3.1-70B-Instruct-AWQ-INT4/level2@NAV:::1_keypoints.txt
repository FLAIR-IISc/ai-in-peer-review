- Proposes an attribute-grammar framework representing symbolic expressions as sequences of grammar rules to find efficient mathematical identities.  
- Uses two learning strategies—n-gram models and a recursive neural network (RNN)—to guide grammar-tree search toward promising branches.  
- Claims: a novel grammar framework for efficiency, ML enables transfer from simple to complex expressions, RNN learns continuous math-structure representations, and many new complexity-reducing identities were discovered.  
- Evaluation on five expression families shows learned strategies outperform random search for large k.  
- Empirical result: 3-gram, 4-gram, and 5-gram models generally outperform the RNN.  
- Strength: novel application of ML to discover mathematical identities with thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: high potential impact across machine learning, computer science, and mathematics; paper is well-written and organized.  
- Weakness: limited scope—only a specific class of expressions is considered.  
- Weakness: reliance on a specific type of ML model and weaker RNN performance versus n-grams.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation and experimental-setup details provided.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations and directions for future work.  
- Meta-review assessment: high quality, high originality, high significance; recommendation to accept with requested clarifications.
