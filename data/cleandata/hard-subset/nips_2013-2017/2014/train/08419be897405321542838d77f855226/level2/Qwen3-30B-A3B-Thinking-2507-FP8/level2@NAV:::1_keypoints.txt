- Presents ML-guided discovery of computationally efficient mathematical identities using attribute grammars to represent symbolic expressions.  
- Main claims: grammar enables systematic exploration of expression space; n-gram/RNN models learn from simple expressions to guide search; approach finds novel identities that reduce computational complexity.  
- Technical quality: solid experiments showing learned models (especially 3-gram) outperform random search across many expression families.  
- RNN representation learning is well-executed with appropriate training practices (curriculum learning, weight initialization).  
- Demonstrates concrete gains (e.g., reducing O(n^3) to O(n^2)) and discovery of previously unknown identities.  
- Originality: novel application of NLP techniques (n-grams, recursive neural nets) to symbolic computation; attribute grammar use has precedents.  
- Significance: relevant to theoretical CS and practical ML optimization (e.g., RBM partition functions, dropout approximations).  
- Clarity and presentation: clear problem formulation, good examples and figures, thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Limitation: restricted scope to homogeneous polynomials and single matrix/vector reduces immediate broader applicability.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for why n-gram models outperform RNNs on certain patterns.  
- Suggestion: supplementary material listing identities is valuable but should be integrated more directly into the main text.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, meaningful contribution; accept with minor revisions to discuss limitations and practical implications.
