- Novel framework to automatically discover computationally efficient, analytically equivalent mathematical identities using an attribute grammar and ML-guided search.  
- Uses numerical evaluation modulo a prime for equivalence checking and an RNN to learn continuous vector representations of expressions.  
- Trains n-gram and RNN models on simpler problems (curriculum learning) to guide search and manage combinatorial explosion.  
- Demonstrates discovery of non-trivial identities that dramatically reduce computational complexity for several expression families (including RBM-related formulas).  
- Integrates a generative grammar, a verification module, and learned search strategies in a coherent, reproducible system.  
- Learning-based strategies significantly outperform random search, especially as expression complexity increases.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and supported by informative figures and implementation details.  
- Highly original application of modern ML (RNN embeddings, n-grams) to symbolic mathematics and search-heuristic learning.  
- Results are significant: practical path for automating optimizations and bridging symbolic reasoning with statistical ML.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on extending beyond homogeneous single-variable polynomials to broader functions and data structures.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis explaining why n-gram models sometimes outperform the RNN.  
- Suggestion: soften claims that identities are “previously undiscovered” to avoid asserting exhaustive novelty.  
- Pro: creative, novel ML application to symbolic math.  
- Pro: technically sound and well-designed framework that finds complex identities.  
- Pro: convincing experimental evidence for the value of learning-guided search.  
- Pro: high potential influence and applicability in ML and symbolic reasoning.  
- Con: evaluation is limited to a relatively narrow class of expressions.  
- Con: comparative analysis of learning models is insufficient.  
- Overall: strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
