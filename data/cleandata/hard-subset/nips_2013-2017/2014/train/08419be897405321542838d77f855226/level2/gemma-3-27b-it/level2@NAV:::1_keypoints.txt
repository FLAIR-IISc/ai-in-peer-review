- Core idea: Use machine learning (attribute grammars + n-gram and RNN models) to guide search for computationally efficient symbolic mathematical identities.  
- Novelty: Interesting and promising approach at the intersection of symbolic computation and ML, addressing exponential search spaces.  
- Framework strength: Attribute grammar representation provides a solid foundation for expressing and searching symbolic expressions.  
- Learning contribution: Integration of n-gram and RNN models to steer search improves efficiency over brute-force methods.  
- Empirical result: Successfully discovered identities that reduce complexity (e.g., O(n^3) to O(n^2)).  
- Reproducibility: Code and data are publicly available.  
- Representation finding: RNN-learned symbolic representations are a noteworthy contribution with future research potential.  
- Limited evaluation: Experiments focus on a specific set of polynomial expressions, limiting claims about generalizability.  
- RNN issues: RNN models underperform simpler n-gram models, raising concerns about their effectiveness and requiring further analysis.  
- Missing analysis: Discussion of limitations, failure modes, scalability, and hyperparameter choices is brief and needs expansion.  
- For acceptance: Novel application, ability to find nontrivial identities, sound technical framework, and public artifacts.  
- Against acceptance: Narrow experimental scope, RNN underperformance, and insufficient limitations discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experiments, analyze RNN performance, and deepen the limitations/future-work discussion.
