- Introduces a framework that uses an attribute grammar to represent expression space and searches for computationally cheaper, analytically equivalent expressions.  
- Uses ML-guided search (n-gram models and an RNN) trained on simpler solutions to scale discovery to complex identities.  
- Novel application of an RNN to learn continuous vector representations of symbolic expressions.  
- Addresses combinatorial explosion in symbolic search by learning search strategies rather than brute-force enumeration.  
- Numerical descriptor via modular arithmetic and the learned RNN are efficient alternatives to slow symbolic equivalence checking.  
- Empirical results show learning-based strategies significantly outperform random search and discover new non-trivial identities.  
- Well-written and organized paper with clear problem statement and useful introductory examples.  
- Currently limited to homogeneous single-variable polynomials producing scalar outputs, restricting generalizability.  
- Simpler n-gram models often outperform the RNN; more analysis is needed to explain RNN weaknesses and guide architectural improvements.  
- The process for generating candidate trees for linear combination solving is under-specified and may affect results.  
- Potential computational bottleneck in solving the M x N linear systems (Gaussian elimination) is not discussed.  
- Overall assessment: highly original, technically sound, strong empirical evidence, recommendation for acceptance.
