- Paper applies ML (n-grams, RNNs) with an attribute grammar to discover computationally cheaper symbolic mathematical identities.
- Core idea: learn from simpler expressions to guide tree search and overcome exponential search spaces.
- Well-motivated problem with clear introduction and sensible use of attribute grammars.
- Novel combination of attribute grammars and ML for identity discovery.
- Practical results showing real improvements (e.g., O(n^3) to O(n^2)) on example families.
- Thorough experimentation across multiple expression families and ablation (dropout) studies.
- Clear presentation and helpful examples; code and data are publicly available.
- Scope is limited to homogeneous polynomials with a single matrix/vector, restricting generality.
- RNNs underperform relative to simple n-gram models; lack of analysis on architecture choices (e.g., LSTM).
- Numerical descriptors with modulo arithmetic may introduce errors/instability; sensitivity to prime p not discussed.
- Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (n-gram size, RNN hidden size).
- Missing baseline comparisons to existing symbolic tools (e.g., Mathematica, Maple).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scope, RNN analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity, and baseline comparisons.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 7/10.
