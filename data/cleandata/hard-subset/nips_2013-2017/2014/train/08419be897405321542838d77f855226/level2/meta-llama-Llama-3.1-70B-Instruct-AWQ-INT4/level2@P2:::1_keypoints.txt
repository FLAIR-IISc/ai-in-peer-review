- Proposes a novel ML-based framework using attribute grammars to represent symbolic expressions as sequences of grammar rules.  
- Introduces two learning methods (n-gram model and recursive neural network) to guide search for efficient mathematical identities.  
- Demonstrates discovery of complex identities unreachable by brute-force search or manual derivation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion and highlights the approach's novelty and applications.  
- Experimental results are strong and convincingly show method effectiveness across several expressions.  
- Clear and well-organized paper structure enhances readability.  
- Strength: novel and interesting ML approach to identity discovery with impressive empirical validation.  
- Strength: clear exposition of framework and learning approaches.  
- Weakness: assumes reader has substantial background in ML and symbolic computation.  
- Weakness: some technical details (notably RNN implementation) are under-explained.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and possible extensions.  
- Clarity could be improved with more figures/diagrams to illustrate methods and results.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution that merits acceptance despite minor issues.
