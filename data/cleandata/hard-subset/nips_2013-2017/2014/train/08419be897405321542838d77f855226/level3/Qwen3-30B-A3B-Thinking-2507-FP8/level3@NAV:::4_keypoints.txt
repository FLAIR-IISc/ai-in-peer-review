- Proposes a novel framework using attribute grammars and ML (n-gram, RNN) to guide search for computationally efficient symbolic identities.  
- Key insight: training on solutions to simpler expressions enables discovery of more complex identities beyond brute-force reach.  
- Technically sound contribution with clear experimental validation and strong learned representations (≤5% classification error for degree 3–6).  
- Successfully discovers novel identities yielding significant complexity reductions (e.g., O(n³) → O(n²)).  
- Good connection to prior work in recursive neural networks and attribute grammars.  
- Scope is severely restricted to homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree with a single matrix variable, without justification for this limitation.  
- Algorithm does not enforce lower computational complexity, so learned strategies could produce high-complexity solutions.  
- Grammar restrictions (e.g., removal of matrix-multiply rule) lack formal justification.  
- Some numerical examples (e.g., RBM-1 in supplement) appear invalid for small parameter values (k=1, n=1).  
- Paper organization is hard to follow—scheduler and search strategy explanations are unclear.  
- Lack of proof or argument for extensibility to more complex expressions weakens significance claims.  
- Several minor typos further undermine readability.  
- Overall: interesting niche contribution but limited scope and unresolved technical concerns reduce impact; needs scope justification and formalization.
