- Introduces a framework to discover computationally efficient mathematical identities using machine learning over attribute grammars.  
- Represents symbolic expressions as trees and guides search with learned models (n-gram and recursive neural nets).  
- Builds on compiler attribute grammars and borrows NLP techniques (n-gram language models, recursive neural nets for trees).  
- High technical quality: well-structured framework, clear problem setup, and thorough experiments.  
- High originality: novel application of learning to symbolic identity discovery, bridging ML and symbolic computation.  
- Empirical results show the method finds complex identities beyond brute-force search.  
- N-gram models outperform RNNs on two of three tasks (Figure 3).  
- Clarity issues, notably Section 7.2, where experimental setup and result interpretation are hard to follow.  
- Low perceived significance: problem domain (homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree k) is narrow and somewhat contrived.  
- Unclear practical utility: paper does not convincingly show real-world adoption or computational benefit of discovered identities.  
- Limited justification of relevance to broader computational challenges.  
- Suggested improvements: strengthen practical relevance justification and improve clarity in methodology and results.
