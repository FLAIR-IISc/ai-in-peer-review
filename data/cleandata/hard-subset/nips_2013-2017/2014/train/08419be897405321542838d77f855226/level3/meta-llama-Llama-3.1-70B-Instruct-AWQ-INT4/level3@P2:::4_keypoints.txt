- Proposes a novel ML-guided framework (attribute grammar + n-gram and RNN models) to discover efficient mathematical identities.
- Demonstrates the framework on several expression families and finds complex identities that random/brute-force search misses.
- Differs from prior theorem-proving and program-induction work by focusing on polynomials of fixed degree and using learned guidance for search.
- Writing is generally clear and the paper is well-organized, with minor typos and a few unclear sentences.
- Strength: novel contribution of using ML to guide symbolic identity discovery.
- Strength: potential impact for symbolic software libraries and more efficient computations.
- Strength: experimental results show effectiveness across multiple expression families.
- Weakness: studied expressions are very restricted, raising questions about generalization to more complex expressions.
- Weakness: the algorithm does not enforce lower computational complexity, despite that being a central goal.
- Weakness: Section 2 restrictions lack clear formal justification or empirical support.
- Weakness: paper structure and the staged presentation are hard to follow and need clearer explanation.
- Technical concern: unclear constraints on vector/matrix sizes; RBM-1 expression appears invalid for k=1, n=1.
- Suggested fix: provide formal justification or empirical studies for expression restrictions.
- Suggested fix: introduce mechanisms to limit the search to low-complexity solutions.
- Suggested fix: clarify vector/matrix size constraints and improve overall organization and clarity.
- Acceptance argument: novel approach with promising results and practical potential.
- Rejection concerns: limited scope, missing complexity enforcement, and minor clarity/typo issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address noted concerns.
