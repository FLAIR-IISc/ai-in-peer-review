- Novel approach: combines attribute grammars with machine learning (n-gram models and an RNN) to discover efficient mathematical identities.
- Core idea is novel and potentially impactful for compiler optimization and related areas.
- Authors correctly identify brute-force search limits and propose learning-based guidance to find cheaper equivalent expressions.
- Problem setting is overly restrictive (homogeneous polynomials of degree k with a single matrix variable), limiting broader applicability.
- Insufficient justification up front for focusing on this restricted problem family.
- RNN architecture description is unclear—specifically the nature and operation of the “weight tensor W3” is not specified.
- No justification for choosing an RNN over potentially better-suited models (e.g., tensor networks per Bowman 2013).
- Section 7.2 is unclear about whether the RNN is used after removing the matrix-matrix multiply rule or only to guide search within the reduced grammar.
- Evaluation methodology is under-specified: unclear how success rates are compared and whether differences are statistically significant.
- Proposal generation and scheduler mechanics (how rule scores translate to selection) are insufficiently detailed.
- Experimental results show learning-based approaches can outperform random search on some problem families.
- Manuscript has technical issues: typos, grammatical errors, repeated sentences, and a mislabeled figure (Figure 3 shows an RNN, not a TNN).
- Overall recommendation: interesting core idea but requires substantial revisions on clarity, justification, baselines, and experimental detail; reviewer recommends rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
