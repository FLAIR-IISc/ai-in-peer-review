This paper introduces a novel approach to discovering efficient mathematical identities using machine learning techniques. The authors propose a framework based on attribute grammars that can be used to represent symbolic expressions and learn a continuous representation of these expressions using a recursive neural network (RNN). The RNN is trained to classify grammar trees into one of several clusters, allowing the framework to efficiently explore the space of possible expressions.

The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach by discovering efficient identities for several families of expressions, including those related to machine learning. The authors also show that their framework can be used to discover novel formulae in broader areas of mathematics.

However, there are several limitations and areas for improvement in the paper. The problem definition appears overly restricted, and the authors could benefit from exploring more general settings. The explanation of the weight tensor W3 is unclear, and it is not evident whether a neural tensor network is used instead of a linear network. The authors should also provide more detail on the proposal generation in the search procedure.

Additionally, the paper contains several typos and grammatical errors throughout, and Figure 3 incorrectly labels TNN as RNN. There are also duplicate/repeated sentences in the manuscript.

Despite these limitations, the paper presents an interesting solution to the problem of discovering efficient mathematical identities and demonstrates the potential of machine learning techniques in this area.
