- Summary: Introduces an attribute grammar and two learning methods to search for efficient identities for homogeneous polynomials (degree k), demonstrating discovery of new complexity-reducing identities.  
- Strength: Proposes an interesting distributed, recursive neural representation to classify algebraic identities.  
- Strength: Provides a clear explanation of the attribute grammar and the two learning approaches guiding the search.  
- Strength: Experimental results show the approach can discover novel, computationally efficient identities.  
- Weakness: Problem definition is overly restricted and unclear how it generalizes to more complex settings.  
- Weakness: Explanation and justification of the weight tensor W3 are unclear; no rationale given for not using a neural tensor network.  
- Weakness: Proposal generation in the search procedure is insufficiently described and needs more detail.  
- Weakness: Manuscript contains several typos, grammatical errors, duplicate sentences, and a mislabeled figure (Figure 3 labels TNN; should be RNN).  
- Quality: Technically sound with theoretical and experimental support; authors are honest about limitations.  
- Quality: Paper would benefit from clearer exposition of technical details (notably W3 and proposal generation).  
- Clarity: Generally well-written, but specific explanations (W3, proposal generation) are unclear; figures and tables are otherwise clear.  
- Originality: Novel application of ML to discover mathematical identities and interesting use of recursive neural networks.  
- Originality: Lacks sufficient discussion on distinctions from prior work in theorem proving and program induction.  
- Significance: Demonstrates ML potential to find identities that reduce computational complexity and has cross-disciplinary applicability.  
- Significance: Needs more discussion of broader implications and potential impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, effective experimental results, and potential broad applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Restricted scope, unclear technical explanations (W3), and manuscript errors.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to clarify W3 and proposal generation, fix typos/duplicates, and correct figure labeling.
