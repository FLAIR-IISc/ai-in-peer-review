- Introduces a novel framework using machine learning within an attribute grammar to discover efficient mathematical identities.
- Represents symbolic expressions as grammar-built trees and uses n-gram models and an RNN to guide search for equivalent, lower-complexity expressions.
- Potentially valuable contribution given limited prior application of machine learning to symbolic reasoning.
- Builds on symbolic computation, theorem proving, and program induction, but distinguishes itself by integrating learning to mitigate combinatorial search explosion.
- Draws on NLP techniques (n-grams, recursive neural nets) and uses attribute grammars as a formal representation foundation.
- Explicitly contrasts learning-based approach with rule-based systems like Maple and Mathematica.
- Strength: novel method that can automatically find identities difficult or impossible to derive manually or via exhaustive search.
- Strength: experiments show learned strategies outperform random search in some cases, and code/data are provided for reproducibility.
- Strength: application of RNNs to continuous representations of symbolic expressions is interesting and broadly applicable.
- Weakness: problem scope is highly restricted (homogeneous polynomials with a single matrix variable), limiting generalizability.
- Weakness: paper needs stronger justification for restrictions and empirical analysis of what happens when restrictions are relaxed.
- Major concern: algorithm does not guarantee discovered expressions are lower complexity; linear combination of trees may yield equivalents that are not more efficient.
- Suggestion: need a robust mechanism to prioritize or enforce lower-complexity solutions during search.
- Presentation issues: organization and flow are unclear; require a clearer step-by-step explanation from grammar definition to identity discovery.
- Experimental details sometimes lacking (e.g., vector/matrix sizes); some supplementary examples (RBM-1) appear invalid for certain parameter values.
- Minor issues: typos throughout the paper detract from polish.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel ML application to symbolic identity discovery, finds complex identities, interesting RNN use, and provides code/data.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope, no guarantees of lower-complexity results, organizational/clarity problems, and minor technical/typo concerns.
- Overall recommendation: promising and novel work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to justify restrictions, add complexity-prioritization mechanisms, and improve clarity and technical correctness.
