- Proposes a novel ML-based method to discover efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar for symbolic expressions.  
- Introduces two learning approaches: n-gram models over sub-expression sequences and an RNN producing continuous subtree representations to predict combination rules.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear explanation of approach and related work.  
- Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of limitations and potential future directions.  
- Section 7.2 is unclear and needs more detailed explanations and examples.  
- Practical usefulness of the search methods for producing computationally efficient symbolic expressions is unclear.  
- The addressed problem appears contrived and unlikely to be widely adopted, so significance is rated low.  
- Originality is high; using RNNs to learn continuous representations of symbolic expressions is particularly innovative.  
- Overall quality is high but impact may be limited to a specific niche.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear exposition, strong experiments, innovative RNN approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: contrived problem, unclear practical utility, limited clarity in parts, low significance.
