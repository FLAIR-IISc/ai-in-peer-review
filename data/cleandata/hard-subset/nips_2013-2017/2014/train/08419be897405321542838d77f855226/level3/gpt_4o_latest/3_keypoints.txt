- Paper proposes a novel method combining attribute grammars with machine learning (n-grams and RNNs) to discover efficient algebraic identities.  
- Framework represents symbolic expressions as grammar trees and uses learning to guide search for computationally efficient equivalents.  
- Demonstrates discovery of identities that reduce computational complexity (e.g., O(n^3) → O(n^2), polynomial approximations for exponential expressions).  
- Builds on prior work in symbolic computation, theorem proving, and RNNs while applying these techniques to symbolic mathematics.  
- Originality: tackles an underexplored problem and introduces a novel integration of attribute grammars with learning-based search.  
- Significance: results have practical applications (e.g., compiler optimizations, ML algorithms) and include discovery of previously unknown identities.  
- Technical contribution: uses RNNs for continuous representations of symbolic expressions, employs curriculum learning, and integer-based descriptors to avoid numerical issues.  
- Weakness — clarity: explanation of the RNN weight tensor W3 is ambiguous, leaving uncertainty about use of a neural tensor network vs a linear network.  
- Weakness — problem scope: focuses narrowly on homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness — related work: insufficient comparison to Bowman et al. and lack of explanation why RNNs were chosen over tensor networks.  
- Weakness — experimental clarity: Section 7.2 unclear about whether RNNs are used for classification during search or only for proposal generation.  
- Weakness — presentation: multiple typos and errors (e.g., "RNN" mislabeled as "TNN" in Figure 3) detract from quality.  
- Pro: novel, challenging problem with significant results and innovative techniques likely to inspire future research.  
- Con: narrow scope, clarity issues, and weak related-work discussion reduce impact and accessibility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — clarify W3, improve related-work discussion, and correct typographical/presentation errors.
