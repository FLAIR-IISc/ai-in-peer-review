- Introduces SVIHMM: a novel application of stochastic variational inference to HMMs in time-dependent data.  
- Proposes an algorithm that adaptively bounds edge-effect errors in subchain sampling.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithm.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic data and a large genomics dataset where batch inference is infeasible.  
- Achieves comparable performance to batch VB while significantly reducing computation time.  
- Originality and potential high impact for scalable Bayesian inference on large datasets.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation overall, enabling reproducibility for expert readers.  
- Paper structure is sometimes hard to follow; methodological stages are not always clearly explained.  
- Lacks a constraint or guidance for expressions with lower computational complexity, limiting search for efficient solutions.  
- Restrictions in Section 2 are not fully justified; more empirical or theoretical support is needed.  
- Concerns about a constraint on vector/matrix size with potential issues for certain values of k and n.  
- Contains several typos that require correction.  
- Meets conference quality criteria and the reviewer recommends acceptance despite the noted weaknesses.
