- Presents a framework to discover efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar and ML-guided search.  
- Proposes two search-guiding methods: an n-gram model that learns patterns and a recursive neural network (RNN) that learns continuous representations.  
- Demonstrates discovery of computationally efficient versions of complex expressions inaccessible to brute-force or manual derivation.  
- N-gram model is simple, effective, and outperforms the RNN on two of three tasks.  
- Applying RNNs to symbolic grammar trees is novel and shows potential for continuous symbolic representations.  
- Provides open-source code and reproducible results.  
- Paper is technically sound with high originality in combining ML and symbolic computation.  
- Clarity is limited, especially Section 7.2; experimental setup and results are hard to follow.  
- Problem significance is questioned—finding efficient symbolic expressions feels contrived with limited practical applicability.  
- Grammar scope is restrictive and methods struggle on more complex expression families (e.g., RBM-2 with higher degrees).  
- RNN underperforms the simpler n-gram model, raising doubts about its practical utility.  
- Recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance for originality and technical contribution, but needs clearer exposition and a stronger case for practical impact.
