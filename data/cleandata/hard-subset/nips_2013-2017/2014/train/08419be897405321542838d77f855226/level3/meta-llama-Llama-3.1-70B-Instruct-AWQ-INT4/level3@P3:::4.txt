This paper proposes a novel approach to discovering efficient mathematical identities using machine learning techniques. The authors introduce an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and employ two learning approaches, n-gram models and recursive neural networks, to guide the search for efficient identities.

The main idea of the paper is to learn representations for symbolic expressions that can be used to find mathematical identities. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by discovering complex identities that are beyond the reach of brute-force search or human derivation. The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly explained.

The authors' contributions are significant, as they introduce a novel grammar framework for finding efficient versions of symbolic expressions and demonstrate how machine learning techniques can be integrated into this framework. The use of recursive neural networks to learn a continuous representation of mathematical structures is also a novel application.

However, the scope of the paper is limited, as the studied expressions are very restricted, and there is no proof of extensibility to more complex expressions. The authors acknowledge this limitation and discuss potential extensions in the conclusion.

The paper has the potential to benefit symbolic software libraries, as the approach could be used to optimize computations. However, the algorithm does not enforce the constraint of lower computational complexity, which is a key aspect of the problem statement. The authors should consider ways to limit the search to low-complexity solutions.
