- Introduces SVIHMM, an SVI algorithm for HMMs that uses subchains to handle time-dependent observations and edge effects.  
- Extends traditional SVI to dependent data by sampling consecutive observation subchains.  
- Addresses challenges of breaking observation dependencies and mitigating edge-effect induced errors.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with detailed technical exposition.  
- Strong, thorough related work section situating the contribution.  
- Strength: novel algorithm that directly tackles dependent observations and edge effects.  
- Strength: clear, detailed description of algorithm components and procedures.  
- Strength: experimental results show effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of algorithm limitations and potential drawbacks.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a few datasets and need broader evaluation.  
- Weakness: some technical details (batch factor derivation, GrowBuf) need clearer explanation.  
- Weakness: could better discuss broader applications and implications beyond presented examples.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality; technically sound.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing noted weaknesses to strengthen the paper.
