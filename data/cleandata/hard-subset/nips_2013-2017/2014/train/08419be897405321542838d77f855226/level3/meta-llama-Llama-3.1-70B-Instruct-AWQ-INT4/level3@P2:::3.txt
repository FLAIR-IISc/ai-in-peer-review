**Paper Review: Learning to Discover Efficient Mathematical Identities**

**Summary and Context**

This paper explores the application of machine learning techniques to the discovery of efficient mathematical identities. The authors introduce an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and propose two novel learning approaches to guide the search for efficient identities. The paper is related to previous work in theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming, but focuses on a restricted setting where the target expression is a homogeneous polynomial of degree k. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several families of expressions, discovering new mathematical identities that offer significant reductions in computational complexity.

**Strengths**

* The paper introduces an interesting solution using distributed, recursive neural representations to classify algebraic identities.
* The authors provide a clear explanation of their attribute grammar framework and the two learning approaches used to guide the search for efficient identities.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in discovering new mathematical identities.

**Weaknesses**

* The problem definition appears overly restricted, and it is unclear how the approach can be generalized to more complex settings.
* The explanation of the "weight tensor W3" is unclear, and it is not justified why a neural tensor network is not used instead of a linear network.
* The paper does not clearly explain the proposal generation in the search procedure and needs more detail.
* There are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript.
* Figure 3 incorrectly labels TNN; it should be RNN.
* There are duplicate/repeated sentences in the manuscript.

**Quality**

* The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
* The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.
* However, the paper could benefit from more clarity in the explanation of certain technical details, such as the weight tensor W3.

**Clarity**

* The paper is generally well-written, but there are some unclear explanations, such as the weight tensor W3.
* The paper could benefit from more detail in the explanation of the proposal generation in the search procedure.
* The figures and tables are clear and well-organized.

**Originality**

* The paper introduces a novel application of machine learning techniques to the discovery of efficient mathematical identities.
* The use of recursive neural networks to classify algebraic identities is an interesting contribution.
* However, the paper could benefit from more discussion on how the approach differs from previous work in theorem proving and program induction.

**Significance**

* The paper demonstrates the potential of machine learning techniques in discovering new mathematical identities that can significantly reduce computational complexity.
* The approach has potential applications in various fields, such as computer science, mathematics, and physics.
* However, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the broader implications of the approach and its potential impact on the field.

**Arguments For Acceptance**

* The paper introduces a novel application of machine learning techniques to the discovery of efficient mathematical identities.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in discovering new mathematical identities.
* The paper has potential applications in various fields and could contribute to the advancement of the state of the art in machine learning and mathematics.

**Arguments Against Acceptance**

* The problem definition appears overly restricted, and it is unclear how the approach can be generalized to more complex settings.
* The paper could benefit from more clarity in the explanation of certain technical details, such as the weight tensor W3.
* There are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper, but with revisions to address the issues mentioned above. The authors should provide more clarity in the explanation of certain technical details, such as the weight tensor W3, and provide more detail in the explanation of the proposal generation in the search procedure. Additionally, the authors should proofread the manuscript to correct the typos and grammatical errors.
