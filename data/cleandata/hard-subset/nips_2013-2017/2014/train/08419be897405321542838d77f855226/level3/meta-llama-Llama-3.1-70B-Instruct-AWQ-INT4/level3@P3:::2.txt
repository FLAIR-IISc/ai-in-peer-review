This paper proposes a novel approach to discovering efficient mathematical identities using machine learning techniques. The authors introduce an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and explore two learning approaches to guide the search for efficient versions of a given target expression. The first approach uses n-gram models over sub-expression sequences, while the second employs a recursive neural network (RNN) to produce continuous subtree representations and predict combination rules.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed framework and its components. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work, highlighting the differences between their approach and existing methods in theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming. The experimental results are also well-presented, with a clear evaluation of the proposed methods on various tasks.

One of the strengths of the paper is its originality. The authors propose a novel application of machine learning techniques to a challenging problem in symbolic computation. The use of n-gram models and RNNs to guide the search for efficient mathematical identities is a new and interesting approach. The paper also demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods on various tasks, including the discovery of efficient identities for complex expressions.

However, the paper has some limitations. The clarity of the paper is limited, particularly in Section 7.2, which could be improved with more detailed explanations and examples. Additionally, the significance of the paper is rated low, as the problem addressed appears contrived and unlikely to be adopted for efficient computations. The practical usefulness of the proposed search methods for producing computationally efficient symbolic expressions is also unclear.
