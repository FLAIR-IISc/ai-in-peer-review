- Proposes a novel ML-based method to discover efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar to represent symbolic expressions.  
- Introduces two search-guiding learning approaches: n-gram models over sub-expression sequences and an RNN that encodes subtrees and predicts combination rules.  
- Paper is well-written, clear overall, and explains the framework and components effectively.  
- Related work discussion is thorough and highlights differences from theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming.  
- Experimental results are well-presented and evaluate the proposed methods on multiple tasks.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness, including discovery of efficient identities for complex expressions.  
- Originality is a major strength: novel application of n-grams and RNNs to symbolic computation.  
- Quality of the work is high in methodology and presentation.  
- Clarity is limited in parts, especially Section 7.2, which needs more detailed explanations and examples.  
- Significance is rated low: the problem seems contrived and unlikely to be adopted for practical efficient computations.  
- Practical usefulness of the search methods for producing computationally efficient symbolic expressions is unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, demonstrated effectiveness, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: contrived problem, unclear practical impact, and sections needing clearer exposition.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity, provide more examples, and better justify practical significance.
