- Novel application of ML (RNNs) to symbolic computation using an attribute grammar framework.  
- Potential broader impact in compiler optimization, symbolic mathematics, and ML (e.g., partition functions, dropout approximations).  
- Technical contribution: integration of continuous representations for symbolic expressions via RNNs.  
- Results are preliminary and underwhelming; methods fail on more complex expressions (e.g., RBM-2 family for k > 5).  
- Problem scope is highly restricted (e.g., homogeneous polynomials with fixed degrees), limiting generalizability.  
- Many presented examples could be handled by simpler rules or brute-force, undermining the need for DNNs.  
- Performance gains are modest and inconsistent; n-gram models often outperform the more complex RNN.  
- Clarity issues: RNN architecture and training details are dense and hinder reproducibility; examples lack diversity.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining when and why the methods work or fail (e.g., RNN generalization limits).  
- Recommendation: reject for NeurIPS in current form; needs stronger experiments, broader scope, and deeper theory.  
- Pro-accept points: novelty of domain, potential impact, and interesting RNN-based continuous representations.  
- Con-accept points: preliminary results, limited scalability, modest utility over simpler methods, and insufficient clarity/theory.
