- Introduces a method to discover computationally efficient mathematical identities by guided search over an attribute grammar using learned models.  
- Uses n-gram and recursive neural network (RNN) models trained on simple expressions to guide search for more complex identities.  
- Novel intersection of symbolic computation, program induction, and machine learning; applies statistical learning to a traditionally rule-based domain.  
- Strength: originality and creative application of deep learning; RNN-based continuous representations of symbolic expressions are intriguing.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear framework and experimental setup.  
- Main weakness: empirical results are modest — learned strategies beat random search but gains are limited.  
- Simpler n-gram models often outperform the more complex RNN, questioning the benefit of deep models here.  
- Experiments are limited to only five expression families, raising concerns about generality and scalability.  
- Risk of overkill: learning machinery may be unnecessary compared to hand-derived mathematical rules for this restricted domain.  
- Conclusion: promising proof-of-concept but currently falls short of strong acceptance; needs broader benchmarks and stronger non-learning baselines to justify complexity.
