This paper proposes a novel framework for discovering efficient mathematical identities using machine learning techniques. The authors introduce an attribute grammar framework for representing symbolic expressions and explore two learning approaches: n-gram models and recursive neural networks (RNNs). The RNN is used to learn a continuous representation of symbolic expressions, which is then employed in a search strategy to find efficient identities.

The paper's main contributions are the introduction of a grammar framework for finding efficient versions of symbolic expressions, the application of machine learning techniques to this framework, and the discovery of new mathematical identities that offer significant reductions in computational complexity.

The authors evaluate their framework on several families of expressions and demonstrate its ability to discover efficient identities that are beyond the reach of brute-force search or human derivation. The results show that the learning approaches generally outperform the random strategy, with the 3-gram, 4-gram, and 5-gram models performing particularly well.

However, there are several areas where the paper could be improved. The problem definition appears overly restricted, and the authors could benefit from exploring more general settings. The explanation of the weight tensor W3 is unclear, and it is not evident whether a neural tensor network is used instead of a linear network. The authors should also provide more detail on the proposal generation in the search procedure and clarify whether the RNN is still used for classification in Section 7.2.

Additionally, there are several typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript, and Figure 3 incorrectly labels TNN as RNN. The authors should also consider removing duplicate or repeated sentences in the manuscript.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting solution to a challenging problem and demonstrates the potential of machine learning techniques in discovering efficient mathematical identities. With some revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the paper could be even stronger.
