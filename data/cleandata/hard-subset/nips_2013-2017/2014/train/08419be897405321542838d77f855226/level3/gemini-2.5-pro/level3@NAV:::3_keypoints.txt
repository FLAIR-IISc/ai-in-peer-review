- Introduces a novel framework that uses attribute grammars and learned models (n-grams, RNN) to find computationally efficient symbolic identities.
- Connects to prior program induction and recursive-models-on-symbolic-data work (e.g., Socher et al., Bowman et al.).
- Strength: original and potentially significant idea—using learned distributed representations to guide symbolic search.
- Strong proof-of-concept: demonstration of discovering new, non-trivial identities.
- Ambiguity in RNN description—unclear specification of the "weight tensor W3" and whether it is a neural tensor network.
- Missing discussion/citation and justification regarding Bowman’s results that tensor networks can outperform standard RNNs on related tasks.
- Lack of detail on the proposal-generation mechanism within the search procedure, harming reproducibility.
- Section 7.2 unclear about evaluation of search strategies and the precise role of the RNN during search.
- Presentation issues: typos, duplicated opening sentence, and incorrect label in Figure 3 (TNN should be RNN).
- Limited problem definition restricts the immediate significance of discovered identities despite the promising framework.
- Recommendation: thorough revision to clarify architecture choice, justify alternatives, detail methods, and fix textual errors.
