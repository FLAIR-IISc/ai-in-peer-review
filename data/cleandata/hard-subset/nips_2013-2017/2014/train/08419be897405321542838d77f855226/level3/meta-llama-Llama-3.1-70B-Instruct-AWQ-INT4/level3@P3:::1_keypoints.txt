- Introduces a grammar-based framework to represent symbolic expressions and applies ML to search for efficient mathematical identities.
- Idea of using deep neural networks for this task is novel and interesting but the work feels preliminary for NIPS standards.
- Demonstrates discovery of some complex identities that random or brute-force search misses, but overall discovery performance is unimpressive.
- Experiments use a very limited set of inputs (only five expression families), raising doubts about representativeness and generality.
- Unclear whether the approach scales to more general expressions or whether scaling is justified given modest results.
- Use of a recursive neural network to learn continuous representations is not well justified; alternatives are not discussed.
- The curriculum strategy for training the RNN is poorly explained and its necessity is unclear.
- Related work coverage (theorem proving, program induction, probabilistic programming) is good, but the paper lacks clear differentiation from prior approaches.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow, but some technical details (e.g., curriculum and ML specifics) need clearer explanation.
- Overall assessment: interesting idea but preliminary results and limited scope weaken the paper’s impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel ML application, clear framework and exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: preliminary work, limited input domain, weak justification for RNN choice, insufficient discussion of limitations and scalability.
- Scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 5/10.
