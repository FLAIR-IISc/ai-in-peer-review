- Introduces a novel learning-based approach (attribute grammar + n-gram and RNN models) to discover efficient mathematical identities.  
- Uses an attribute grammar to represent symbolic expressions and learns search strategies over grammar trees.  
- Demonstrates discovery of complex identities beyond brute-force search and human derivation.  
- Experiments are thorough with detailed analysis and overall clear explanations.  
- Scope is very restricted; no proof the method extends to more complex expressions.  
- Algorithm does not enforce the paper’s stated low-complexity objective, creating a complexity mismatch.  
- Authors should constrain search to favor low-complexity solutions.  
- Section 2 restrictions lack formal justification or empirical support.  
- Paper organization is sometimes hard to follow; structure and stages need clarity.  
- Technical concerns: unclear constraints on vector/matrix sizes and potential invalidity of the RBM-1 supplement expression for k=1, n=1.  
- Significant potential impact: could benefit symbolic software libraries and be applied to broader mathematical discovery.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Reviewer scores: Novelty 8/10, Technical soundness 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Significance 8/10.
