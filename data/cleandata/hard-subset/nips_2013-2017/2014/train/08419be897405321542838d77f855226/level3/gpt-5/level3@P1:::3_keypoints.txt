- Proposes an attribute-grammar-based framework to discover computationally efficient reformulations of symbolic expressions, constrained to scalar outputs and fixed degree.  
- Searches expression trees with grammar rules; exhaustive search is infeasible so learning guides search.  
- Introduces two learned search policies: an n-gram model over subtrees and an RNN that embeds partial trees and outputs a softmax policy.  
- Checks equivalence numerically via randomized evaluation over Zp and handles complex identities by solving linear systems over descriptor vectors.  
- Experiments on multiple expression families show learned guidance greatly increases solvable targets versus random search; n-grams often outperform the RNN.  
- RNN used as a classifier achieves high multiclass accuracy, indicating it learns useful distributed embeddings.  
- Relation to prior work: connects to theorem proving, program induction, compiler strength-reduction, CAS rewriting, and tree-structured neural models (Socher, Bowman).  
- Strength: clear, concrete formulation of identity discovery with a cost model and curriculum-trained learned strategies.  
- Strength: practical Zp-based equivalence checking enabling scalable evaluation and learning integration.  
- Strength: demonstration that learned models (esp. RNN) capture algebraic structure; open-sourced code and many discovered identities.  
- Weakness: lacks formal correctness guarantees for Zp descriptors; needs collision bounds and symbolic verification on held-out discoveries.  
- Weakness: unclear proposal/scheduler details (greedy policy, beam/backtracking/exploration not specified); needs pseudocode and ablations.  
- Weakness: ambiguous RNN architecture (appears like a neural tensor network); need clarity on composition, unary/binary handling, and capacity control.  
- Weakness: limited comparative baselines—should compare to exhaustive/heuristic enumerative search, CAS simplifiers, and non-learned heuristics (e.g., A*).  
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the RNN used in search is the same as the classifier; need training target and leakage prevention details.  
- Clarity issue: cost model per grammar rule unspecified; implications for admissible solutions (e.g., forbidding matmul) need explicit statement.  
- Clarity issue: linear combinations of trees require detail on generation, constraints on weights (sparsity, integrality), and mapping back to algebraic identities.  
- Clarity issue: scheduler hyperparameters (time budget, restarts, throughput) missing for reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel integration of attribute grammars, randomized equivalence testing, and learned search for algebraic identity discovery.  
- Significance: discovered complexity-reducing identities are potentially impactful, but scope is restricted (homogeneous polynomials, single variable, scalar output).  
- Suggestion: justify RNN vs. NTN/TreeLSTM or provide comparative study showing architecture choice rationale.  
- Suggestion: add beam/limited-width best-first search and report sensitivity to beam width and policy dropout.  
- Suggestion: include a table of discovered identities with complexity reductions and symbolic verification for a subset.  
- Suggestion: analyze failure cases (e.g., RBM-2^k), discuss grammar/representation extensions, and bound descriptor length N needed.  
- Minor: numerous typos/formatting issues, mislabeled figure legend (“TNN”→“RNN”), and inconsistent notation needing proofreading.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, promising learning+symbolic search integration with empirical gains and reusable ideas.  
- Arguments against acceptance: methodological ambiguities, restricted scope, limited baseline comparisons, and RNN underperforming n-gram without deeper analysis.
