- Novel application of ML (n-grams and RNN) to discover efficient mathematical identities; learning-from-simple-to-complex is novel and impactful.
- Attribute grammar framework for symbolic expressions is a sound search-space foundation tied to prior work.
- Technical approach (numerical/learned representations) appears logically sound and improves over random search in experiments.
- Public code and data availability promote reproducibility.
- Restriction to homogeneous polynomials with a single matrix variable is overly limiting and undermines generalizability.
- Justification for the problem restrictions is weak and needs strengthening.
- Description of the RNN architecture is unclear—particularly the role and nature of the W3 tensor.
- No justification for choosing an RNN over alternatives like tensor networks, which may suit structured relational tasks better.
- Experimental protocol (Section 7.2) lacks clarity on evaluation and whether the RNN is used during search.
- Proposal-generation procedure and initial tree construction are insufficiently detailed.
- The paper contains presentation issues (typos, duplicated sentences, mislabeled figure) that detract from quality.
- Strengths: novelty, technical soundness in scope, encouraging experimental results, and potential practical impact.
- Reasons to accept: novel approach, empirical feasibility shown, and public release of code/data.
- Reasons to reject: narrow problem domain, unclear model description and experimental setup, insufficient justification/comparison to related methods, and presentation flaws.
- Overall: promising contribution but requires clearer exposition, broader scope or stronger justification, better comparisons, and careful proofreading.
- Recommendation: reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing clarity, scope, model justification, and presentation issues.
