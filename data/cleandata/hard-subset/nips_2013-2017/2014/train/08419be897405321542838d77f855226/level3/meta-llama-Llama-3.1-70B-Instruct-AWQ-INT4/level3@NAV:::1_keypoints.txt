- Proposes a novel method combining deep neural networks and attribute grammars to discover mathematical identities by representing expressions as grammar rule sequences.  
- Framework uses machine learning to learn a search strategy over grammar trees for symbolic expressions.  
- Paper is clearly written and the framework is well-structured.  
- Experiments show the method can discover efficient identities not easily found by humans or brute-force.  
- Work appears preliminary and may not meet NIPS publication standards.  
- Results are not particularly strong and the identity-discovery performance is not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Experimental evaluation uses a very limited set of input expressions, raising generalizability concerns.  
- No clear justification is given for choosing DNNs over other ML methods or traditional mathematical techniques.  
- Paper lacks analysis of computational complexity and scalability to more general expressions.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of DNNs with attribute grammars for this task.  
- Demonstrates a new application of DNNs on mathematical identity discovery.  
- Potential significance: could enable new mathematical techniques and tools for finding efficient identities.  
- Potential applications across computer science, mathematics, and engineering where efficient identities matter.  
- Overall recommendation: slightly below acceptance threshold—interesting and thought-provoking but needs deeper evaluation.  
- For acceptance: novelty, potential impact, and demonstrated effectiveness on some expressions.  
- Against acceptance: preliminary nature, limited evaluation, unimpressive results, and unclear rationale for DNN use.
