- Introduces an attribute grammar framework to represent symbolic expressions and their computational complexities.  
- Proposes two learning-guided search methods for identities: n-gram models and a recursive neural network (RNN).  
- N-gram model predicts the next grammar rule from local context; the RNN encodes expressions as vectors to predict rules.  
- Demonstrates the methods can discover complex mathematical identities beyond brute-force or human derivation.  
- Positions work relative to theorem proving, program induction, and probabilistic programming but focuses on discovering efficient identities.  
- Strength: novel machine-learning approach to automating discovery of efficient mathematical identities.  
- Strength: development and comparison of two complementary learning methods to guide search.  
- Strength: empirical results showing successful discovery of nontrivial identities.  
- Weakness: paper clarity is limited, especially Section 7.2, making parts hard to follow.  
- Weakness: practical usefulness for producing computationally efficient symbolic expressions is unclear.  
- Weakness: perceived significance is low—problem seems contrived and unlikely to see adoption for efficient computations.  
- Review assessments: originality high, quality high (well-written/organized), significance low; presents both strong acceptance and rejection arguments.
