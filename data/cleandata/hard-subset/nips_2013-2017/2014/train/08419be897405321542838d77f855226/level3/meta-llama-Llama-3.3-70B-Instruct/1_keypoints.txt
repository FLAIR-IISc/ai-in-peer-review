- Proposes a novel stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm for HMMs that exploits chain memory decay to bound edge-effect errors.
- Paper is well-written with clear and detailed explanation of the approach.
- Strength: introduces a significant new approach to applying SVI to HMMs.
- Strength: provides thorough theoretical analysis, including proof of convergence to a local mode.
- Strength: demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic experiments and a large genomics dataset.
- Weakness: may not scale well to very long chains because buffer length τ may need to be large.
- Weakness: relies on irreducibility and aperiodicity assumptions that may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparison with other stochastic inference algorithms for HMMs.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.
- Potentially weak on significance due to limited demonstration of broader impact beyond the genomics example.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address scalability, assumptions, comparisons, and clearer significance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid analysis, empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, restrictive assumptions, missing comparisons, unclear general significance.
