- Paper introduces a framework that uses an attribute grammar and ML-guided search (n-gram and RNN) to discover computationally efficient symbolic identities.
- Strength: Highly original idea to apply learned continuous representations and ML heuristics to symbolic mathematics, with the RNN embedding being particularly innovative.
- Strength: Potentially high impact—automatic discovery of efficient identities could optimize compilers and symbolic libraries; chosen experiments (e.g., RBM-related) are relevant to ML.
- Strength: Technically sound use of attribute grammars and learned search strategies moving beyond hand-designed heuristics.
- Weakness: Experimental domain is highly restricted (homogeneous single-variable polynomials, scalar outputs), raising concerns about generalizability to broader classes of expressions.
- Weakness: Paper is unclear and poorly organized; the multi-stage pipeline and connections between components are difficult to follow.
- Weakness: Search does not explicitly optimize for lower computational complexity; manual removal of expensive grammar rules is ad-hoc.
- Weakness: Restrictions (Section 2) lack sufficient justification or discussion on how to relax them.
- Suggestion: Modify the search scheduler to directly favor trees with lower computational complexity using grammar attributes.
- Suggestion: Clarify dimensionality and constraints in the RBM-1 identity (possible error for k=1, n=1) and correct supplementary material.
- Suggestion: Add a high-level figure or algorithm box that walks through the full workflow from target expression to discovered identity.
- Minor: Manuscript contains several typos (e.g., "has contains", "is is", "show for", "by be") that should be fixed.
