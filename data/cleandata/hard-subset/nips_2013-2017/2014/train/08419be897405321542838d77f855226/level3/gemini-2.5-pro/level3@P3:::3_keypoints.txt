- Proposes a framework to discover computationally efficient mathematical expressions using an attribute grammar to define a symbolic search space.  
- Uses ML-guided search (n-gram model and recursive neural network) trained on simpler expressions to guide search for complex targets.  
- Empirically discovers non-trivial identities that yield substantial speedups (e.g., reducing exponential to polynomial complexity).

Strengths:
- Highly original and significant idea: learning to guide symbolic search could impact compilers, scientific discovery, and automated reasoning.  
- Creative transfer of NLP methods (n-grams, RNNs on trees) to symbolic mathematics and learning continuous embeddings for expressions.  
- Strong empirical validation demonstrating meaningful computational savings.

Weaknesses:
- Problem definition is overly restricted to homogeneous single-variable scalar polynomials, limiting immediate applicability and needing a roadmap for generalization.  
- RNN description is ambiguous (unclear specification of the W3 tensor/trilinear form vs. NTN), hurting reproducibility.  
- No justification for choosing a standard RNN over potentially stronger recursive tensor models (e.g., Bowman-style NTNs); lacks comparison or rationale.  
- Experimental details are unclear: evaluation of search strategies (e.g., removal of matrix-multiply rule) and whether the RNN is used in restricted settings is ambiguous.  
- Proposal generation/scheduler behavior is underspecified—how model "scores" are converted into rule choices (argmax, sampling, etc.) is not stated.  
- Presentation quality is poor: typos, duplicated sentences, grammatical errors, and a mislabelled figure (TNN vs RNN).

Summary:
- Quality: original and promising but undermined by unclear model and experimental methodology.  
- Clarity: key architectural and procedural details are underspecified and writing needs significant proofreading.  
- Originality: high.  
- Significance: potentially high if generalized.  
- Recommendation: promising work but not ready for acceptance without substantial revisions clarifying the RNN architecture, justifying model choice, detailing the search procedure, and fixing writing errors.
