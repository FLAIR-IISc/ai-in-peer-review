- Paper presents a novel approach using attribute grammars + machine learning to discover computationally efficient equivalent mathematical expressions.  
- Problem framed clearly; approach uses n-gram models and an RNN to guide grammar-based search.  
- Demonstrated successes include identities that reduce matrix operation complexity (e.g., O(n^3) → O(n^2)).  
- Related work coverage is adequate, distinguishing this learning-guided approach from rule-based symbolic systems.  
- Novelty is high: combining grammars and ML for identity discovery is a fresh idea.  
- Potential significance is substantial if methods generalize to more complex expressions.  
- Framework appears technically sound; numerical and learned expression representations are reasonable.  
- Code and evaluation data are publicly released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Experiments are limited in scope, evaluated on only a few expression families.  
- The RNN consistently underperforms n-gram models, calling its effectiveness into question.  
- The reason for n-gram superiority over the RNN is not analyzed or explained.  
- RNN implementation description is dense and lacks details (architecture, training, dropout justification).  
- Scalability and exponential search-space bottlenecks are acknowledged but not thoroughly discussed.  
- No direct comparison to existing symbolic tools (Mathematica/Maple); claims about uniqueness are unverified.  
- Reviewer recommends expanding experiments, analyzing RNN performance, improving clarity, benchmarking vs. symbolic tools, and discussing scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing clarity, experimental scope, and benchmarking concerns.
