- Overall assessment: Novel, technically sound approach that effectively discovers efficient mathematical identities; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Uses attribute grammars plus ML (n-grams, RNNs) to learn search strategies over symbolic expression spaces and finds nontrivial, efficient equivalent expressions.  
- Related work: Positions work appropriately within theorem proving, program induction, and symbolic computation, claiming distinct contribution in grammar-guided learning.  
- Novelty: Clever integration of attribute grammars with learning-based search; not just straightforward application of ML.  
- Technical soundness: Well-defined grammar-based search space and practical integer-based descriptors; RNN architecture and training are reasonable.  
- Empirical results: Strong evidence of success (e.g., reducing O(n^3) to O(n^2)); learning-guided search outperforms random search on complex cases.  
- Potential impact: Valuable for compiler optimization, symbolic computation, and ML model simplification; public code/data enhances reproducibility.  
- Clarity/accessibility: Some sections (attribute grammar details, RNN training) need clearer explanations, examples, and diagrams.  
- Generalizability: Experiments limited to homogeneous polynomials of fixed degree; applicability to broader expression classes remains unclear.  
- RNN performance: RNNs underperform compared to n-gram models; need deeper analysis of this discrepancy and remedies.  
- Limited grammar scope: Restricted grammar eases search but curtails exploration of wider mathematical expressions.  
- Review criteria: High originality and significance; quality is solid; clarity is moderate and can be improved.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel effective method, significant empirical findings, open code, and broad potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited generalizability, RNNs not competitive with simpler models, and some clarity issues.  
- Recommendation & required revisions: Accept with minor revisions—improve clarity (grammar/RNN sections), analyze RNN performance, acknowledge limitations, and discuss grammar expansion strategies.
