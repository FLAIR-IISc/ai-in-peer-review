- Paper proposes a grammar-based framework that uses n-gram models and RNNs to learn efficient mathematical identities, discovering reductions from O(n^3) to O(n^2) for several expression families.
- Integrates attribute grammars and machine learning to guide symbolic search, training on simpler expressions to tackle complex targets.

- Originality: Novel combination of machine learning with grammar-based symbolic search and continuous RNN representations for symbolic expressions.
- Significance: Results have practical impact for optimizing ML computations and potential integration into compilers/optimizers.
- Quality: Sound technical approach, effective integer-modulo equivalence checking, and well-chosen RNN implementation/details; learning outperforms random search.
- Clarity: Well-organized paper with clear examples and useful supplementary material for reproducibility.

- Theoretical justification is limited; lacks analysis explaining why learning methods (e.g., n-grams vs RNNs) behave as observed.
- Scope is restricted to homogeneous polynomials and single matrix/vector variables, limiting general applicability.
- Comparison to recent program synthesis and symbolic regression work is insufficiently detailed.
- Evaluation metrics focus on discovery within 10 minutes and treat any non-zero count as success, lacking nuance about identity quality or optimality.

- Suggest expanding theoretical discussion on when n-grams vs RNNs succeed or fail.
- Suggest clarifying relationships to recent neural program synthesis and symbolic regression literature.
- Suggest adding more visualizations of grammar trees, especially for complex discovered identities.
- Suggest discussing scalability to larger expressions and more complex grammars given exponential search growth.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and well-executed contribution bridging ML and symbolic computation with convincing empirical results despite some limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept.
