## Peer Review for "Learning to Discover Efficient Mathematical Identities"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and promising approach to automating the discovery of mathematical identities, leveraging attribute grammars and machine learning. The combination is clever and the results, while limited in scope, are impressive, demonstrating the discovery of identities previously unknown. The paper is generally well-written and makes a clear case for its contributions. However, the experimental evaluation lacks depth, and the reliance on specific parameter settings (e.g., dropout rate) feels somewhat arbitrary. Despite these weaknesses, I believe the paper represents a significant contribution and should be accepted, with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper tackles the challenging problem of automating mathematical identity discovery, aiming to find computationally efficient equivalents of given expressions.  It frames this problem within an attribute grammar, allowing exploration of possible expression transformations. Recognizing the intractability of brute-force search, the authors introduce machine learning (n-gram models and RNNs) to guide the search process, learning from solutions to simpler expressions.  The related work section is well-structured and comprehensive. It correctly positions the work relative to theorem proving, program induction, symbolic computation systems, and relevant NLP techniques like recursive neural networks and n-gram language models.  The authors adequately distinguish their work, highlighting the learning-based search strategy as a key differentiator.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of attribute grammars and machine learning for this purpose is highly novel. Applying learning to *guide* a symbolic search, rather than directly predicting the result, is a significant contribution.
* **Demonstrated Results:** The paper provides concrete examples (e.g., reducing O(n³) to O(n²)) of successfully discovered identities. The claim of these being previously undiscovered adds significant value.
* **Sound Framework:** The use of attribute grammars provides a formal and well-defined framework for exploring the space of possible expressions.
* **Learned Representation:** The application of a recursive neural network to learn a continuous representation of symbolic expressions is a valuable contribution in its own right, potentially opening doors for other ML techniques in this domain.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it relatively easy to follow the core concepts.
* **Code Availability:** Providing code and evaluation data encourages reproducibility and further research.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scalability/Scope:** The experiments are limited to a relatively small set of expression families. While the success in those families is encouraging, it's unclear how well this approach would generalize to more complex or diverse mathematical expressions. The restrictions imposed on the target expressions (homogeneous polynomials, single matrix variable) significantly limit the applicability of the method.
* **Experimental Depth:** The experimental results feel somewhat superficial. The figures mainly show success rates without detailed analysis of *why* certain approaches work better for specific families.  A more detailed analysis of the learned representations (e.g., visualization of the RNN embeddings) would be insightful.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance seems sensitive to hyperparameters, such as the dropout rate in the RNN.  The justification for choosing these values isn’t strong enough.  A more thorough hyperparameter search and analysis would strengthen the results.
* **Evaluation Metric:** Using a simple binary "success/failure" metric doesn't provide much information about the efficiency of the search.  Measuring the time to find a solution or the number of trees explored would provide a more nuanced evaluation.
* **Practical Impact:** While the discovered identities are mathematically interesting, the paper doesn’t clearly articulate the practical implications of these improvements in computational complexity.

**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The described methods are logically consistent, and the experimental setup, while limited, appears valid. The claim of discovering new identities suggests a level of rigor.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is well-written, but the explanation of the attribute grammar could be made more accessible to readers unfamiliar with the concept.  A more illustrative example of the grammar in action (beyond the matrices) would be helpful.
* **Originality:** The approach is highly original, representing a novel combination of techniques.
* **Significance:** The potential impact of automating mathematical identity discovery is significant. While the current results are limited, the framework shows promise for broader applications in areas like optimization and symbolic computation.



**5. Arguments for and against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Novel and promising approach to a challenging problem.
* Demonstrates successful discovery of previously unknown identities.
* Well-defined framework based on attribute grammars.
* Valuable contribution of learned representation for symbolic expressions.

**Con:**

* Limited scalability and scope of experiments.
* Lack of detailed experimental analysis.
* Sensitivity to hyperparameters.
* Unclear practical implications.



**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS, contingent on the following minor revisions:

* **Expand the Experimental Evaluation:** Explore additional expression families and provide a more detailed analysis of the results, including search time, tree exploration counts, and visualization of the learned RNN embeddings.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Perform a more thorough hyperparameter search and justify the chosen settings.
* **Discuss Practical Impact:**  Clearly articulate the potential practical applications of the discovered identities and the framework.
* **Clarify Grammar Explanation:**  Provide a more accessible explanation of the attribute grammar, potentially with a simpler illustrative example.
