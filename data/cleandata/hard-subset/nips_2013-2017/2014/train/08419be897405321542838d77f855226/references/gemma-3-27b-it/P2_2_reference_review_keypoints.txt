- Overall assessment: Novel, technically sound work that finds non-trivial (sometimes new) mathematical identities; recommended accept with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Combine attribute grammars with machine learning (n-gram models and an RNN) to guide search for efficient symbolic identities.  
- Results: Demonstrates complexity reductions (e.g., O(n^3) → O(n^2)) and provides concrete example identities.  
- Related work: Well-positioned among theorem proving, program induction, and symbolic computation; novelty lies in combining these with ML.  
- Novelty strength: The grammar+ML approach is original and not a straightforward application of existing methods.  
- Technical soundness: Methodology is well-founded; integer-based descriptor addresses floating-point issues; RNN architecture is appropriate.  
- Reproducibility: Code and data are provided.  
- Limited scope: Focuses on homogeneous polynomials with a single matrix variable, restricting general applicability.  
- Variable performance: Strong on some families (e.g., Σ AAT) but weak on RBM-based families; gains are not uniform.  
- n-gram dominance: n-gram models consistently outperform the RNN, contrary to expectations and requiring further analysis.  
- RNN issues: RNN does not consistently beat random search; authors should investigate why it struggles to generalize.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Dropout and hidden size choices are mentioned but lack detailed tuning discussion and impact analysis.  
- Scalability concerns: Exponential search-space growth raises questions about applicability to larger or more complex problems.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel approach, solid execution, discovered new identities, clear presentation, reproducible code.  
- Rejection risks: Limited scope, scalability concerns, inconsistent performance, and surprising n-gram superiority.  
- Requested revisions: Expand limitations discussion, analyze RNN failure modes, detail hyperparameter tuning, outline future extensions, and add clarity (e.g., summary table of discovered identities).
