This paper presents a fascinating and ambitious attempt to apply machine learning to the discovery of mathematical identities, framed within an attribute grammar. The core idea – leveraging learning to navigate a complex search space of symbolic expressions – is highly novel and represents a genuinely bold approach. The demonstrated ability to rediscover identities with improved computational complexity, *especially* those beyond manual derivation, is impressive and a significant achievement. The application of RNNs to learn a continuous representation of symbolic structures is another strong contribution, potentially opening up new avenues for applying ML techniques to this domain.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, though dense with technical detail. The introduction effectively motivates the problem, and the explanation of the attribute grammar framework is comprehensive. The experimental setup is well-defined, and the results are presented convincingly, though perhaps a bit dryly. The supplementary material is crucial for fully appreciating the discovered identities.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of attribute grammars and machine learning for identity discovery is a genuinely new approach. The application of RNNs to symbolic representation is also novel.
*   **Significance:** The discovery of previously unknown identities with improved computational complexity is a significant contribution. The potential applications to areas like RBM optimization are exciting.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The framework appears technically sound, although more extensive validation would be beneficial (see weaknesses).
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and the concepts are explained clearly, despite the technical complexity.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope:** The current framework is restricted to homogeneous polynomials and single matrices (though extensions to two matrices are mentioned). While the authors acknowledge these limitations, expanding the scope would greatly increase the impact of the work.
*   **Validation Depth:** While the paper demonstrates success on several families of expressions, the evaluation seems somewhat limited. More diverse examples and a more rigorous comparison to existing symbolic computation engines (beyond mentioning their limitations) would strengthen the claims.
*   **RNN Performance:** The RNN's performance isn’t consistently superior to the simpler n-gram models, and is sometimes worse. The analysis of *why* the RNN struggles on certain problem families would be valuable. Dropping activations seemingly helps, but more detail on ablation studies would strengthen the discussion.
*   **Scalability:** While improvements are made with learning, the exponential nature of the search space remains a concern. The paper doesn't fully address how the approach would scale to even more complex expressions, particularly those without repetitive structures.
*   **Numerical Stability:** The use of modular arithmetic to represent expressions is clever but the paper does not address the possibility of collisions.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a truly novel and exciting approach to a challenging problem.
*   The demonstrated ability to rediscover complex identities with improved efficiency is compelling.
*   The application of RNNs to symbolic representation is a significant contribution in its own right.
*   The code and data are publicly available, promoting reproducibility.
*   The authors acknowledge limitations and suggest avenues for future work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The current scope of the framework is limited.
*   The validation could be more thorough and comparative.
*   The RNN's performance isn’t consistently strong and requires further analysis.
*   Scalability remains a potential concern.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a very strong paper with a highly novel and potentially impactful contribution. While some aspects of the validation and scalability could be improved, the innovative nature of the approach and the impressive results outweigh these concerns. The paper pushes the boundaries of applying machine learning to symbolic reasoning and is well-suited for NIPS. I strongly recommend acceptance, even with the noted minor flaws. The novelty is *significant* enough to warrant acceptance despite not being a perfectly polished and thoroughly validated piece of work. I believe this work opens a promising new direction for research.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
