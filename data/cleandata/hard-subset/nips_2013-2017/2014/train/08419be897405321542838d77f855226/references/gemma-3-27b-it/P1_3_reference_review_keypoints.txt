- Overall assessment: Novel, promising approach combining attribute grammars and ML; impressive limited results; accept with minor revisions.
- Problem & approach: Automates discovery of efficient mathematical identities using attribute grammars guided by n-gram models and RNNs.
- Related work: Well-positioned relative to theorem proving, program induction, symbolic systems, and NLP techniques.
- Novelty: Learning-guided symbolic search is a significant and original contribution.
- Demonstrated results: Successfully discovered nontrivial identities (e.g., O(n^3)→O(n^2)), some reportedly previously unknown.
- Framework soundness: Attribute grammars provide a formal, well-defined exploration framework.
- Learned representation: RNN-based continuous embeddings for symbolic expressions are a valuable contribution.
- Clarity & reproducibility: Paper is well-written and code/data are provided for reproducibility.
- Limited scope: Experiments cover a small set of expression families with restrictive assumptions (homogeneous polynomials, single matrix variable).
- Experimental depth lacking: Analysis is superficial—needs deeper insight into why methods succeed on some families.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results appear sensitive to settings (e.g., dropout) without sufficient justification or tuning.
- Evaluation metric weak: Binary success/failure hides search efficiency; should report time or number of trees explored.
- Practical impact unclear: Paper does not convincingly articulate real-world implications of the discovered identities.
- Clarity improvement needed: Attribute grammar explanation should be more accessible with an illustrative example.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: Expand experiments/families, analyze search time and embeddings, perform hyperparameter search, discuss practical impact, and clarify grammar explanation.
