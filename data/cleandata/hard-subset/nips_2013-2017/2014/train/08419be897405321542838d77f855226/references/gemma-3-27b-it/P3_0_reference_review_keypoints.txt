- Overall assessment: Novel, well-written approach combining attribute grammars and ML; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Learn search strategies within an attribute-grammar-defined space to discover efficient mathematical identities.  
- Related work: Thoroughly positioned relative to theorem proving, program induction, symbolic computation, and NLP techniques.  
- Novelty: First integration of attribute grammars with learning for symbolic identity discovery.  
- Results: Discovered previously unknown identities that yield significant complexity reductions (e.g., O(n^3)→O(n^2)).  
- Methodology: Sound attribute-grammar framework with numerical and learned representations for expression comparison.  
- Presentation: Clear exposition and illustrative examples.  
- Reproducibility: Code and evaluation data provided.  
- Experimental scope: Evaluation limited to a small set of expression families; generalization is unclear.  
- RNN performance: RNNs underperform n-gram models consistently without thorough analysis of causes.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results appear sensitive to dropout and weight initialization; more systematic tuning needed.  
- Scalability: Exponential search-space growth remains a core limitation; 10-minute cutoffs may hide solvability.  
- Grammar limitations: Current grammar restricted to homogeneous polynomials; broader expression classes not addressed.  
- Originality: Highly original integration of established techniques and novel learned representations for symbolic expressions.  
- Significance: Potentially high impact for scientific computing and symbolic reasoning by automating efficient identity discovery.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel approach, compelling discoveries, sound methodology, and reproducible artifacts.  
- Cons against acceptance: Limited experiments, RNN underperformance, and unresolved scalability issues.  
- Requested revisions: Expand experimental families, analyze RNN weaknesses, discuss scalability limits and remedies, and outline grammar extensions.
