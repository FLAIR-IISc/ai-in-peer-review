- Overall: Novel and promising approach to discovering computationally efficient mathematical identities; reviewer recommends weak accept pending significant revisions.  
- Core idea: Uses attribute grammars plus learned search strategies (n-grams, RNNs) to find equivalent, cheaper expressions.  
- Related work: Good coverage and positioning vs theorem proving/program induction/symbolic systems, but superficial connection to [7].  
- Novelty: Combining grammars and ML-guided search for identity discovery is genuinely novel.  
- Results: Demonstrated nontrivial complexity reductions (e.g., O(n^3)→O(n^2)) and some reportedly new identities.  
- Framework: Attribute grammar gives a clear, structured search space for expressions.  
- Learned representation: RNN to embed symbolic expressions is an interesting direction.  
- Reproducibility: Code and data are publicly available.  
- Clarity: Paper is hard to follow in parts; notation (e.g., k) and experiment descriptions are unclear.  
- Experimental rigor: Setup feels ad-hoc (choice of expression families, arbitrary 10-minute cutoff), lacking robust statistical comparisons.  
- Success criteria: Unclear definition of “success” and sensitivity to random initialization not addressed.  
- Significance: Paper fails to convincingly show practical importance or real-world impact of discovered identities.  
- RNN vs n-gram: RNN is consistently outperformed by n-grams; deeper analysis of RNN failure/overfitting is needed.  
- Grammar limitations: Fixed grammar restricts search space and identity scope; need discussion of extensions.  
- Table 3 ambiguity: “Search Space Size” is not clearly defined (total trees vs valid rule combos).  
- RNN details missing: Hyperparameters and training procedure (learning rate, dropout, hidden size) are under-specified.  
- High variance: Large error bars in Fig. 3 indicate high result variance that must be addressed.  
- Generalization: Unclear if learned strategies generalize across expression families (e.g., (∑AAT)k → RBM-2k).  
- For acceptance: Innovative idea, tangible discoveries, reproducible code.  
- Against acceptance: Lacks clarity, experimental rigor, demonstrated significance, and convincing RNN results.  
- Required revisions: Improve clarity, add statistical analyses, demonstrate practical significance, analyze RNN limitations, and address specific reviewer questions.
