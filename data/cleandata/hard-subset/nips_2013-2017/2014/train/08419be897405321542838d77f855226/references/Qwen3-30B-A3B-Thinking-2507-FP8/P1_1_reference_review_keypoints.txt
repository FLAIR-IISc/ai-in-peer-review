- Framework proposes discovering efficient mathematical identities using an attribute grammar that attaches computational-complexity attributes to expression trees.  
- Machine learning (n-gram models and recursive neural networks) is used to guide search for equivalent lower-complexity expressions.  
- Expressions are represented as trees and RNNs learn continuous representations of symbolic structures.  
- Empirical results show learning-based search significantly outperforms random search, finding identities that reduce O(n³) to O(n²) and some exponential to polynomial.  
- Main contributions: grammar framework, ML-guided search, novel use of RNNs for symbolic expressions, and discovery of new computationally beneficial identities.  
- Work sits at the intersection of machine learning, symbolic computation, and compiler design, connected to attribute grammars and program synthesis.  
- Paper is technically sound, with a clear framework, well-defined components, and thorough evaluation across multiple expression families.  
- Observation that n-grams work well for repetitive patterns while RNNs handle more complex patterns, but lacking deeper theoretical justification.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with effective examples and clear, informative figures.  
- Insufficient detail on numerical representation using integer modulo p and how choice of prime p affects results and complexity.  
- Novelty: applying ML to a traditionally rule-based problem and learning continuous symbolic representations is innovative.  
- Significance: potential practical applications in ML (e.g., efficient computations) and broader implications for ML-guided combinatorial search.  
- Strengths: clear technical contributions, novel integration of grammars and ML, strong experiments, new identities, and good writing.  
- Weaknesses: restrictive problem formulation (homogeneous fixed-degree polynomials), limited discussion of practical applications and integration, lack of detail on modulo p, short 10-minute run limit concerns, and limited scalability discussion.  
- Recommendation: accept for NeurIPS; valuable technical contribution despite limitations.  
- Suggested author actions: clarify choice/impact of prime p, give examples of other symbolic tasks for RNN use, discuss grammar scaling, explain difficulty of RBM-2^k family, and discuss integration into ML frameworks/compilers.
