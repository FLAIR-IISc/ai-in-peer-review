- Overall: Novel, compelling approach combining attribute grammars and ML; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Framework uses attribute grammars plus learning (n-grams, RNNs) to search for computationally cheaper equivalent expressions.  
- Related work: Properly situated among theorem proving, program induction, probabilistic programming; highlights attribute grammars.  
- Strength — Novelty: Combining attribute grammars with ML and using RNNs for symbolic expression representations is genuinely novel.  
- Strength — Results: Finds identities with large speedups (e.g., O(n^3)→O(n^2), exponential→polynomial); examples convincing.  
- Strength — Framework clarity: Attribute grammar gives a clear, well-defined search space and search process is well explained.  
- Strength — Practicality: Code and data released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength — Presentation: Well-written and accessible despite technical complexity.  
- Weakness — Generalization: Lacks robust quantitative analysis of how the method scales to different expression types (trig, integrals, complex algebra).  
- Weakness — RNN vs n-grams: RNNs underperform n-grams consistently; causes (data, architecture, representation limits) not investigated.  
- Weakness — Modularity/transfer: Unclear whether learned strategies transfer across related expression families or require retraining.  
- Weakness — Runtime cutoff: Fixed 10-minute cutoff may hide long-run benefits; no runtime-success curves provided.  
- Weakness — Descriptor choice: Integer modulo-p descriptor used, but choice of p and collision risks not analyzed.  
- Originality: Highly original integration of techniques and novel application of RNNs to symbolic identity discovery.  
- Significance: Potentially large impact across scientific computing and algorithm design by automating discovery of complex identities.  
- Pro (accept): Novel approach, strong empirical improvements, clear framework, released artifacts, good writing.  
- Con (concerns): Limited generalization analysis, unexplained RNN underperformance, unclear strategy modularity, fixed runtime cutoff.  
- Recommendation (revision requests): Expand experiments on generalization, analyze RNN performance, test transferability between families, provide runtime curves, and discuss choice/impact of modulus p.
