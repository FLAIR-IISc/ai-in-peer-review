- Proposes a novel framework that represents mathematical expressions as attribute-grammar trees and uses machine learning to discover computationally efficient equivalent identities.
- Trains n-gram and recursive neural network (RNN) models on simpler problems to guide search for efficient expressions in harder problems.
- Demonstrates empirical success finding identities that significantly reduce computational complexity (e.g., O(n^3)→O(n^2), exponential→polynomial) and claims many discoveries are novel.
- Positions the work at the intersection of theorem proving, program induction, symbolic computation, and ML, distinct from rule-based symbolic engines by learning search strategies.
- Introduces an attribute-grammar application to identity discovery, an innovation compared to traditional compiler usages.
- Strength: genuinely novel approach to automating discovery of mathematical identities.
- Strength: compelling empirical results with clear illustrative examples showing computational benefits.
- Strength: learned continuous representation of symbolic expressions via RNNs is a valuable contribution.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments comparing n-gram and RNN approaches across problem families.
- Strength: code and evaluation data released, aiding reproducibility.
- Strength: overall clear and well-written presentation.
- Weakness: limited grammar (homogeneous polynomials, single matrix/vector variable) restricts generality and applicability.
- Weakness: scalability to higher-degree polynomials and multiple variables is unproven; some families (RBM-2)k defeat the learned methods.
- Weakness: surprising superiority of n-gram models over RNNs in many cases lacks deeper investigation.
- Weakness: reliance on numerical checks with modular arithmetic raises numerical-stability and false-positive concerns.
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of search space, convergence, and computational complexity.
- For acceptance: novel, impactful idea with strong empirical gains, useful learned representations, and released code.
- Against acceptance: restricted grammar scope, uncertain scalability, and unexplained n-gram vs RNN performance gap.
- Overall: strong, promising paper worthy of acceptance despite noted limitations.
- Rating: Accept (Strongly Consider); Confidence: 8/10.
