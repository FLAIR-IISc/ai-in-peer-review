## Peer Review for "Learning to Discover Efficient Mathematical Identities"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and promising approach to automating the discovery of mathematical identities, leveraging attribute grammars and machine learning. The core idea of using learned search strategies within a grammar-based framework is insightful and the results demonstrate significant progress in finding efficient expressions for polynomial computations. While the scope is currently limited, the potential impact is substantial, and the work is of high quality and well-suited for NIPS. I recommend acceptance, contingent on minor revisions to clarify some experimental details and discussion points.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper tackles the challenging problem of automatically discovering mathematically equivalent but computationally cheaper expressions. It proposes a framework built upon attribute grammars, which define a space of possible expressions.  Naive exploration of this space is intractable for even moderately complex expressions. The key contribution is integrating machine learning – n-gram models and recursive neural networks (RNNs) – to guide the search process, learning from simpler cases to tackle more complex ones. The paper demonstrates the discovery of identities that offer performance improvements, often exceeding those achievable manually. 

The related work section is well-structured and comprehensive. It accurately positions the work within the contexts of theorem proving, program induction, probabilistic programming, and symbolic computation.  The authors appropriately highlight the novelty of applying learning techniques to this specific problem and the unique application of RNNs to symbolic expression representation. The comparison to Bridge et al. [4] and the distinction from rule-based simplification engines like Maple/Mathematica are particularly relevant.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of attribute grammars and machine learning for this specific task is genuinely novel and represents a significant departure from traditional approaches.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed framework is well-defined and logically sound. The use of integer-based descriptors to address floating-point precision issues is a clever practical solution.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the ability to discover identities with significant computational advantages (e.g., reducing complexity from O(n<sup>3</sup>) to O(n<sup>2</sup>)). The examples provided (e.g., Example 1 & 2) are illustrative.
* **Learned Representation:** The application of an RNN to learn continuous embeddings for symbolic expressions is an important contribution in itself, opening doors to leveraging other machine learning techniques in this domain.
* **Code Release:**  The public availability of code and data (https://github.com/kkurach/math_learning) promotes reproducibility and further research.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the complex concepts accessible.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope:** The current framework is restricted to homogeneous polynomials with a single matrix/vector variable. While the authors acknowledge this limitation, expanding the scope (e.g., to multivariate polynomials, or expressions involving more complex functions) would greatly enhance the impact.
* **RNN Performance:** While the RNN shows promise, its performance (as indicated in Fig. 3) is not consistently superior to the n-gram models, especially for simpler families.  A more detailed analysis of the RNN’s limitations and potential improvements would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions cross-validation for selecting the RNN hidden layer size (l=30), but details regarding hyperparameter tuning for other components (e.g., n-gram order, learning rates) are lacking.
* **Solution Presentation:** The discovered identities are primarily presented in the supplementary material.  Integrating a few more key examples directly into the main text could improve clarity.
* **Discussion of Failure Cases:** While the authors acknowledge the difficulty of the (RBM-2)k family, a more in-depth discussion of *why* the framework struggles with these complex cases would be valuable.



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by the experimental results. The use of a linear system to verify solutions is a clever approach. The limitation to homogeneous polynomials and single variables is explicitly stated, making this a complete, although focused, piece of work.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear, but could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the training process for the RNN and the rationale behind the chosen hyperparameters.
* **Originality:**  The core idea of combining attribute grammars and machine learning for automated identity discovery is highly original.
* **Significance:**  The work has the potential to be highly significant. Automated discovery of mathematical identities could have applications in diverse fields, including computer algebra systems, optimization, and potentially even physics and engineering.  The learned representation of symbolic expressions is also a valuable contribution.



**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem.
* Compelling empirical results demonstrating performance improvements.
* Valuable contribution in learned symbolic representations.
* Publicly available code and data.

**Against:**

* Limited scope of the current framework.
* RNN performance not consistently superior to simpler baselines.
* Lack of detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning.




**6. Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses. Addressing the minor concerns outlined above (clarifying RNN training details, providing a more thorough discussion of failure cases, and perhaps integrating a few more key identities into the main text) would further strengthen the paper. This work represents a significant step forward in the field of automated mathematical discovery and has the potential to inspire further research in this exciting area.
