- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by introducing m-graphs and graphical conditions for recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Provides sufficient conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions using "general ordered factorization" (Theorem 1).  
- Shows causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic relations are not (Theorem 4; Example 3).  
- Introduces new graphical non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3).  
- Applies framework to attrition in longitudinal studies, correcting prior misconceptions (Theorems 5–6; critique of Garcia 2013).  
- Theoretical results are technically sound with rigorous proofs and reproducible appendix.  
- Novel contribution: distinguishes probabilistic vs. causal recoverability and fills gaps in prior literature.  
- Clear organization, precise definitions, helpful examples and figures that illustrate concepts.  
- Significant practical implications across disciplines dealing with missing data, especially causal inference.  
- Some technical concepts (e.g., d*-separation, Theorem 4) may be hard for readers unfamiliar with do-calculus.  
- Lacks empirical validation or real-world case studies to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Could better articulate how practitioners would apply the graphical criteria in applied studies.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong, original theoretical contribution suitable for NIPS.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel insights, rigorous extension of prior work, clear graphical criteria, corrects literature errors.  
- Con-acceptance: limited empirical validation and some technical accessibility issues for non-experts.
