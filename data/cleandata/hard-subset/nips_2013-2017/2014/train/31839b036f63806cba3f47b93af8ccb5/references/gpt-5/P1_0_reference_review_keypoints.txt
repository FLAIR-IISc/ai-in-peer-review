- Paper develops graphical criteria and constructive procedures for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from data with missingness using m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013).
- Extends ordered/sequential factorization to include missingness indicators R and allows recovery when missingness mechanisms interact (Theorem 1).
- Provides new recovery formulas using partitions of R and Markov blankets Mb(R(i)) to handle collider paths and latent confounding when no admissible sequence exists (Theorem 2).
- Introduces explicit graphical non-recoverability criteria for P(V) and P(Y|X), giving necessary conditions that flag when recovery assumptions are insufficient (Theorem 3).
- Shows some causal effects P(y|do(x)) are recoverable even when associated probabilistic targets are not and when Y is inseparable from its missingness mechanism, via do-calculus and d*-separation (Theorem 4).
- Gives necessary and sufficient conditions for joint distribution and causal effect recovery in attrition settings with a single missing variable (Theorems 5 and 6).
- Technical soundness: main claims appear well-grounded and theorems conceptually extend prior work; proofs are in the appendix.
- Completeness concern: proofs deferred and “general algorithms” are not fully specified; no algorithmic procedures or complexity guarantees for constructing R partitions/Mb sets.
- Empirics: no simulations, synthetic examples, or case studies to demonstrate finite-sample behavior, robustness, or positivity/sensitivity issues.
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation (e.g., GX/GX ambiguity, asymmetric R′x/Ry convention) and several statements (e.g., condition (2) in Theorem 1) are hard to parse.
- Presentation recommendation: emphasize Definition 2 (minimal set), contrast with chain-rule factorization, and provide a worked example applying Theorems 2–3 on a single graph.
- Suggestion: provide an explicit algorithmic pipeline to (a) search for admissible sequences, (b) construct R partitions and Mb sets, and (c) output estimand or certify non-recoverability, with complexity/completeness discussion.
- Suggestion: for Theorem 4 give a checklist for verifying d*-separation (roles of Z, W, W1) and include an example with latent confounding and multiple W.
- Suggestion: discuss positivity requirements and concrete examples of non-strict positivity extensions; comment on generalizing beyond binary R and to coarsening mechanisms.
- Originality: novel extension of ordered factorizations to include R, novel non-recoverability criteria, and insightful causal recovery under inseparability.
- Significance: advances theory for MNAR with interacting mechanisms and provides practically useful non-recoverability diagnostics, though immediate impact limited by lack of tooling/experiments.
- Pros: strong theoretical advances; first explicit non-recoverability criteria for m-graphs; novel result that causal effects can be recoverable despite Y–Ry inseparability; clear attrition conditions.
- Cons: notation and presentation hinder accessibility; no algorithmic pipeline or complexity analysis; lack of empirical illustrations; some conditions hard to verify without tooling.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful theoretical contribution that would benefit from improved clarity, operational algorithms, and empirical validation.
