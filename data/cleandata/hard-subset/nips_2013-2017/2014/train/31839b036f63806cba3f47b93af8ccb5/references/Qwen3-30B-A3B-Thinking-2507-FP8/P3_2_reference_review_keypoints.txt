- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by developing graphical conditions (m-graphs) for recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Proves sufficient recoverability results for joint/conditional distributions (Theorems 1–2).  
- Provides non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).  
- Shows a key theoretical result: causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness mechanism Ry are inseparable (Theorem 4).  
- Applies results to longitudinal attrition problems and corrects prior misconceptions (e.g., Garcia 2013).  
- Theoretical framework is rigorous with sound proofs and well-structured presentation.  
- Distinguishes clearly between probabilistic and causal recoverability, challenging common assumptions.  
- Lacks empirical validation—no simulations or real-data examples to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Notation is dense and potentially confusing (e.g., Rpart, Mb(R(i)), Rw vs. Wr) and needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Omits comparison or integration with standard missing-data methods (e.g., multiple imputation, IPW).  
- Does not address computational complexity or provide implementation guidance for applying the conditions.  
- Makes a novel contribution by formalizing non-recoverability criteria and extending recoverability theory to causal queries.  
- Recommendation: accept (strong theoretical contribution) but request clearer exposition and empirical/computational discussion in future revisions.
