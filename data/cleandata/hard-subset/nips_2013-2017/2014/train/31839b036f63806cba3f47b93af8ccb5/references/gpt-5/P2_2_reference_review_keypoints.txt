- Core contribution: Extends m-graphs with R-nodes via a stronger sequential factorization allowing interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Core contribution: Provides a constructive recovery formula for joint distributions in semi-Markovian models using R-partitions and collider-path Markov blankets.  
- Core contribution: Introduces new graphical non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions.  
- Core contribution: Identifies conditions where causal effects P(y|do(x)) are recoverable even when related probabilistic queries are not, with necessary/sufficient results for |Vm|=1.  
- Technical soundness: Theorems are careful and mostly well-justified; Theorems 1–4 and related constructions are precise and convincing.  
- Completeness: Theoretical results cover stated settings but stop short of algorithmic completeness (decidability/complexity) for general m-graphs.  
- Empirical validation: No experiments; small synthetic studies would strengthen claims and demonstrate finite-sample behavior.  
- Organization: Logical progression (probabilistic → non-recoverability → causal → attrition) with helpful examples.  
- Notation/definitions: Notation is dense and occasionally inconsistent/typo-prone; key assumptions (e.g., no R→V) should be highlighted earlier.  
- Readability: More self-contained figures and worked recovery derivations would improve accessibility.  
- Originality: Novel extension to include interacting R-nodes, new R-partition recovery formula, explicit non-recoverability results, and insight on causal vs probabilistic recoverability.  
- Practical impact: Provides graphical, checkable conditions and explicit estimands for MNAR queries valuable to practitioners.  
- Research impact: Advances causal/missing data theory and will likely spur follow-up work; negative results prevent wasted effort.  
- Strength: Clear, general recovery and non-recoverability criteria derivable from the graph.  
- Strength: Novel extension allowing interacting R-nodes in ordered factorizations.  
- Strength: Constructive recovery via R-partitions and Markov blankets in semi-Markovian models.  
- Strength: Shows causal recoverability can exceed probabilistic recoverability using dormant independences.  
- Strength: Clean necessary and sufficient conditions for simple attrition and correction of prior literature.  
- Weakness: Notation and typographical issues—standardize symbols and fix typos.  
- Weakness: Lacks concrete algorithms/pseudocode for searching admissible sequences, computing R-partitions/Mb, and outputting estimands; no complexity discussion.  
- Weakness: No robustness discussion on m-graph misspecification or links to testing procedures.  
- Weakness: No empirical simulations illustrating success/failure, bias of MAR methods, or estimator variance.  
- Weakness: Scope limitations (positivity, semi-Markovian assumption, single partially observed variable in some results) need clearer discussion.  
- References: Formatting and completeness of references need cleanup.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Substantial theoretical advances, constructive estimands, and important practical and research impact for MNAR and attrition problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Presentation/notation issues, missing algorithmic/complexity treatment, lack of empirical illustrations, and some restricted settings.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—significant contributions but revisions needed.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
