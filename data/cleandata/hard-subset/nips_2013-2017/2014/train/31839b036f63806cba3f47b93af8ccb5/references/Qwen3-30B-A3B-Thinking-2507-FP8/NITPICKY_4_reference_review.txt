

# Review of "Graphical Models for Recovering Probabilistic and Causal Queries from Missing Data"

This paper extends the work of Mohan et al. (2013) on Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to develop graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data. The authors present novel theoretical results showing that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when the associated probabilistic relations are not recoverable, which is a significant contribution to the literature on missing data. The paper demonstrates applications to attrition problems and provides necessary and sufficient conditions for recoverability.

## Quality

The paper presents technically sound theoretical results that build upon prior work. However, several minor but critical technical inconsistencies undermine the precision of the paper:

1. Notational inconsistencies: The paper uses inconsistent notation for missingness mechanisms (Ry, Ry, Ry) throughout the paper, which creates confusion. For example, in Definition 1, Ry is used, but in the text, it's sometimes written as Ry and sometimes as Ry. This inconsistency appears repeatedly throughout the paper.

2. Mathematical errors: In Example 1, the paper states "P (X,Y, Z) = ∑Ry P (Y,X,Z,Ry) = P (Y |X,Z)P (Z) ∑Ry P (X|Ry)P (Ry)" - the summation should be over Ry, not just Ry, which is a minor but important mathematical error.

3. Misapplication of do-calculus: In Example 3, the paper states "P (y|do(z), w,R′y) = P (y|z, w,R′y) (by Rule-2 of do-calculus)" - this misrepresents Rule-2 of do-calculus, which concerns interventions, not conditioning. Rule-2 specifically states P(y|do(z), w) = P(y|z, w) when w d-separates y from z in the graph with z removed, not the expression provided.

4. Definition inconsistencies: In Theorem 1, the condition "Yi⊥⊥(Ryi, Rxi)|Xi \{Ryi, Rxi}" is redundant since removing Ryi and Rxi from Xi makes the condition trivial. This should be clarified.

5. Missing figures: The paper references Figures 1-4 but provides none in the text, making it difficult to verify the examples and theorems.

## Clarity

The paper is generally well-organized, but the notational inconsistencies and mathematical errors significantly reduce clarity. The paper would benefit from:
- Standardizing all notations (e.g., consistently using Ry for missingness mechanisms)
- Including the referenced figures to verify the graphical examples
- Clarifying the redundant condition in Theorem 1

## Originality

The paper builds upon Mohan et al. (2013) with meaningful extensions, particularly regarding the recoverability of causal queries when probabilistic queries are not recoverable. The distinction between recoverability of probabilistic versus causal queries is novel and significant. However, the paper could better articulate how these results differ from prior work, particularly Garcia (2013) and Mohan and Pearl (2014).

## Significance

The problem of missing data is important in statistics and machine learning, and the paper's contribution to causal inference with missing data has potential significance. The results on attrition problems (Section 7) could be useful for longitudinal studies. However, the significance is somewhat diminished by the technical inconsistencies that make it difficult to assess the full contribution.

## Recommendation

While the paper presents a theoretically interesting extension to prior work on missing data, the numerous minor technical inconsistencies and mathematical errors are significant enough to warrant rejection. As a perfectionist reviewer, I believe these issues fundamentally undermine the paper's precision and readability, which are essential for a scientific contribution. The paper's potential significance is overshadowed by these technical flaws.

The paper would benefit from:
1. Standardizing all notational conventions
2. Correcting the mathematical errors
3. Including the referenced figures
4. Clarifying the redundant condition in Theorem 1
5. Providing a more precise comparison to prior work

These corrections would significantly improve the paper's quality, but as presented, the technical inconsistencies prevent me from recommending acceptance. The paper's contribution is important, but the execution is marred by these minor but critical flaws that make it difficult to evaluate the theoretical results with confidence.
