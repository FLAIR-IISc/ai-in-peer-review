

# Review of "Graphical Models for Recovering Probabilistic and Causal Queries from Missing Data"

This paper introduces an extension of Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) for handling missing data, with a focus on recovering probabilistic and causal queries. The authors present theoretical conditions for recoverability, extending Mohan et al. [2013] by developing new graphical criteria and demonstrating that causal queries can sometimes be recovered even when probabilistic queries cannot.

**Strengths:** The paper makes a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of missing data mechanisms. The distinction between recoverability of probabilistic versus causal queries is insightful, particularly the demonstration that P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and Ry are not d-separable. The extension of the sequential factorization theorem to handle cases where missingness mechanisms interact with one another is a significant technical contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Notational Inconsistency:** The paper suffers from pervasive notational inconsistencies. For example, the paper uses "Ry" (with superscript y), "Ry" (with subscript y), and "R_y" interchangeably. In Definition 1, "R = 0" appears without a comma as "P (Vo, Vm,R = 0) > 0" which should be "P(Vo, Vm, R=0) > 0". The notation "R(i)" is used both for partitions of R variables and for specific missingness variables (e.g., "R(1) = {Rw, Rz}"), creating confusion.

2. **Definition Ambiguity:** The paper introduces "Mb(R(i))" without defining "Mb" (presumably Markov blanket), which is critical for understanding Theorem 2. Similarly, "⊥w" in Theorem 4 is used without definition. The minimality condition in Definition 2 is stated but not thoroughly justified, and the paper fails to explain why the minimality requirement is necessary beyond the example.

3. **Technical Errors:** In Example 1, the derivation of P(X,Y,Z) contains an inconsistency: the paper claims P(X,Y,Z) = P(Y|X,Z,R'x,R'y,R'z)P(Z|R'z)∑Ry P(X|R'x,Ry)P(Ry), but the right-hand side does not match the left-hand side in terms of conditioning variables. Theorem 1 states "Yi⊥⊥(Ryi , Rxi)|Xi \{Ryi , Rxi}" which is confusing because the conditioning set is being removed from itself.

4. **Lack of Concrete Examples:** The paper claims Theorem 2 handles cases where Theorem 1 fails but provides no concrete example where this occurs. The only examples (Figures 2a, 2b, 2c) are referenced but not fully explained in the text, making it difficult to verify the claims.

5. **Missing Details:** The paper claims to provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recoverability but only proves sufficiency in most cases. Theorem 5 states "P(V) is recoverable in m-graph G if and only if Y and Ry are not neighbors and Y and Ry are not connected by a path in which all intermediate nodes are colliders" but provides no proof of necessity. The computational complexity of the proposed recovery algorithms is completely omitted.

6. **Related Work:** The related work section is superficial. The paper cites Garcia [2013] but doesn't clearly explain how this work differs from Garcia's approach. The paper also fails to discuss recent causal inference work on missing data (e.g., Tchetgen Tchetgen et al. [2015] on causal inference with missing data).

7. **Formatting Issues:** The bibliography has inconsistent citation formatting (e.g., "Mohan et al., 2013" vs. "Mohan et al. [2013]"). Some references have incomplete information (e.g., "Allison.,? \Q2002\E, Allison.,"). The paper uses "R" for both the set of missingness mechanisms and specific missingness variables, creating confusion.

**Conclusion:** While the paper presents theoretically interesting extensions to the missing data literature, the numerous notational inconsistencies, technical ambiguities, and lack of concrete examples significantly undermine the clarity and credibility of the results. The failure to provide clear definitions for key concepts (like "Mb" and "⊥w"), the presence of technical errors in derivations, and the omission of necessary proofs of necessity for the claimed "if and only if" conditions are serious shortcomings. A paper of this theoretical nature must be precise in its definitions and derivations, which this paper fails to achieve.

**Recommendation:** Reject. The paper's technical contributions are overshadowed by its numerous methodological and notational flaws that would make it difficult for readers to understand, verify, or build upon the results. The authors should substantially revise the paper to address these issues before reconsideration.
