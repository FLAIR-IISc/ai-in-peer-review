- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to a comprehensive framework for recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Establishes general conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions under missingness.  
- Shows causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness Ry are not d-separated, unlike probabilistic queries.  
- Theorem 4 and Example 3 demonstrate how do-calculus and dormant independencies enable causal recovery.  
- Provides necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for causal effect recovery in attrition settings, correcting misconceptions from Garcia (2013).  
- Theorems 5 and 6 give precise conditions for simple longitudinal/attrition scenarios relevant to longitudinal studies.  
- Introduces general ordered factorization (Theorem 1) to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Provides non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3) that formally identify when recovery is impossible.  
- Lacks empirical validation or simulation studies to demonstrate practical utility on real-world or varied missingness scenarios.  
- Does not address computational complexity or scalability of the proposed recovery algorithms for high-dimensional data.  
- Underdeveloped connection to contemporary ML approaches (e.g., deep-learning imputation) and how the graphical approach could integrate with them.  
- Notation is sometimes confusing (e.g., R, R(i), Rpart, Rw and reuse of "R" for mechanisms and values) and needs clarification.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with significant implications for causal inference; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computation, notation, and include a simple simulation.
