

# Review

This paper presents an extension of the Missingness Graph (m-graph) framework for handling missing data, with a focus on recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries. The authors build upon Mohan et al. [2013] to establish conditions for recovering joint/conditional distributions and demonstrate that causal queries may be recoverable even when probabilistic queries are not. While the paper addresses an important problem in statistics and causal inference, it contains numerous technical inconsistencies, formatting errors, and unclear explanations that undermine its scientific contribution.

## Major Issues

**1. Notation and Terminology Inconsistencies:** The paper suffers from severe notational inconsistencies. For example, in Definition 2, "f(O) = ∏i P(Yi|Xi)" is used, but the subscript i is undefined. The paper uses inconsistent capitalization (e.g., "Ry" vs. "Ry" in Theorem 1), and critical terms like "Ry" (missingness mechanism for Y) are used without clear definition. In Example 1, "P (X,Y, Z) = ∑ Ry P (Y,X,Z,Ry)" contains a typographical error where "Ry" should be "Ry" (the summation variable).

**2. Logical Gaps in Key Arguments:** The paper claims "P (y|do(z)) is recoverable even when Y and Ry are not separable" (Example 3), but fails to explain why the causal query can be recovered when the probabilistic query cannot. The derivation in Example 3 skips critical steps: "P (y|do(z), w,R′y) = P (y|z, w,R′y)" relies on Rule 2 of do-calculus, but the paper doesn't establish the conditions for this rule to apply. Similarly, Theorem 4 lacks clarity about the relationship between the conditions and the causal recovery.

**3. Incomplete Definitions and Missing Explanations:** The paper defines "m-graph" in passing but provides no formal definition. The term "partially observed variable" is used without clear distinction from "substantive variable." The definition of "manifest distribution" (Section 2) contains unexplained notation like "R′x" and "Ry" without proper contextualization. The claim that "the missingness mechanism is only a function of the observed data" (Section 8) is inconsistent with the paper's own framework.

**4. Citation and Formatting Errors:** The references contain numerous formatting inconsistencies (e.g., "Mohan et al\.,? \Q2013\E" with backslashes and strange punctuation). The paper cites Mohan et al. [2013] but doesn't clearly articulate how this work extends the previous results, which is critical for assessing novelty.

**5. Theoretical Claims Without Verification:** The paper states "P (V) is recoverable if no Mb(R(i)) contains a partially observed variable X such that Rx ∈ R(i)" (Theorem 2), but provides no concrete example demonstrating how this condition is verified. The claim that "theorems in Mohan et al. [2013] do not apply" to Figure 2 (referenced in Theorem 2) is unverified as Figure 2 is not included in the text.

## Minor but Significant Flaws

- In Theorem 1, the condition "Yi⊥⊥(Ryi , Rxi)|Xi \{Ryi , Rxi}" is logically problematic because "Xi \{Ryi, Rxi}" implies that Ryi and Rxi are elements of Xi, but the notation is inconsistent with the rest of the paper.
- The paper claims "P (y|do(x)) is recoverable if and only if Y and Ry are neither neighbors nor connected by an inducing path" (Theorem 6), but this contradicts Example 3 where Y and Ry are connected by a collider path (which is different from an inducing path).
- The term "m-graph" is introduced but never consistently used (sometimes written as "m-graphs," sometimes "m-graph").
- Section 3 states "the following example illustrates the use of theorem 1" but provides only a description of the process without a concrete example.
- The paper references "Figure 1 (a)" but does not include the figure in the text.
- In Corollary 1, "R(i) ∩RPasub(R(i)) = ∅" should be "R(i) ∩ RPasub(R(i)) = ∅" (with a space).

## Strengths

The paper correctly identifies an important gap in the literature regarding causal queries under missing data. The extension to causal queries (Section 6) is potentially valuable, and the formalization of recoverability via m-graphs is a well-motivated contribution to the field of causal inference.

## Weaknesses

The paper's weaknesses substantially outweigh its strengths. The numerous technical errors, inconsistent notation, and logical gaps make it difficult to verify the claims. The paper fails to provide sufficient detail for reproducibility, which is a critical requirement for NIPS submissions. The claims about the recoverability of causal queries are not adequately supported by the theoretical development.

## Conclusion

While the paper addresses an important problem and builds on existing work, the numerous technical inconsistencies and unverified claims undermine its scientific contribution. The paper should be rejected due to these significant issues with technical execution, despite its potentially interesting theoretical contributions. The authors should thoroughly revise the paper to address the notational inconsistencies, clarify the logical steps, and provide concrete examples to verify their theoretical claims before resubmission.
