- Extends missingness-graph (m-graph) methods by including missingness indicators R in ordered factorizations, giving a sufficient condition (Theorem 1) for recoverability via admissible sequences that may include R.  
- Provides a general recovery result (Theorem 2) for P(V) in semi-Markovian models using partitions of R by collider connectivity and Markov blankets Mb(R(i)).  
- Gives a corollary (Corollary 1) simplifying recovery when there is no latent confounding between R and substantive variables.  
- Introduces new graphical non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3, Corollary 2) showing adjacency or specific collider-paths between a variable and its missingness mechanism preclude recovery of P(V) or P(Y|X).  
- Shows causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic quantities are not, using do-calculus and dormant independences (Example 3, Theorem 4).  
- Derives necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for simple attrition (|Vm|=1) for joint distribution and causal effect recoverability (Theorems 5 and 6), correcting prior literature.  
- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) by generalizing sequential factorization to interacting missingness mechanisms and grounding results in Pearl’s causal framework and ADMG theory.  
- Treats MNAR explicitly via graphical modeling and constructive estimands, offering an alternative to MAR/MCAR methods (EM/IPW/MI).  
- Technical contributions are well-motivated and consistent with causal graph theory; minimality condition for factorizations is justified.  
- Proofs are in the appendix and main text gives constructive estimands, but no systematic algorithm or complexity analysis is provided for finding admissible sequences or checking theorems.  
- No empirical demonstrations or simulations are included to illustrate practical benefits or robustness.  
- The restriction that R cannot be a parent of V or U is standard but limits applicability to some longitudinal settings where missingness may affect future measurements.  
- Positivity over complete cases is assumed.  
- Notation issues: ambiguous symbols (e.g., GX used inconsistently), undefined variants like GX,W1, and unclear intervention/edge-removal notation.  
- Ambiguity between “collider path” and the variant used in Theorem 3 should be clarified and used consistently.  
- Definitions of R partitions and Mb(R(i)) need brief constructive descriptions or pseudocode in the main text.  
- References contain formatting errors and missing details.  
- Recommends adding a short “recipe” or algorithmic procedure to check recoverability under Theorems 1–3 and 5–6 for reproducibility.  
- Suggests providing algorithms/pseudocode for searching admissible sequences, computing R partitions and Mb(R(i)), and verifying Theorem 4 conditions (including d*-separation checks).  
- Advises clarifying collider vs inducing paths, adding illustrative figures, and discussing sensitivity to m-graph misspecification and integration with available tests.  
- Recommends adding empirical comparisons with MAR-based methods (EM/IPW/MI) on synthetic m-graphs to show bias reduction under MNAR.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — strong theoretical advances and practical significance, but held back by notation/algorithmic clarity issues and lack of empirical validation.
