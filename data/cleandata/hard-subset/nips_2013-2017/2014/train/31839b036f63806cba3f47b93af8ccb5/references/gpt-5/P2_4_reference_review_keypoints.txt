- Paper extends missingness graphs (m-graphs) by allowing missingness indicators R to appear in ordered factorizations, enabling recovery when mechanisms influence each other (Theorem 1).
- Introduces a new sufficient condition for joint distribution recovery via partitioning R and using Markov blankets along collider paths (Theorem 2, Corollary 1).
- Provides necessary graphical conditions for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).
- Shows that some causal effects P(y|do(x)) are recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic queries are not, including cases with Y inseparable from RY (Example 3, Theorem 4).
- Gives complete necessary and sufficient conditions for recovery in simple attrition (|Vm|=1), correcting prior literature (Theorems 5–6).
- Builds nontrivially on Mohan et al. (2013) and leverages do-calculus, dormant independence/d*-separation, and ADMG concepts.
- Technical statements are precise, causally grounded, and consistent with known results; minimal separating sets and examples are well-motivated.
- Non-recoverability criteria align with intuition about inseparability via neighbor or collider paths.
- Causal recovery results correctly use do-calculus and dormant independences.
- Completeness is partial: general case lacks a full decision procedure; Section 4 gives high-level "general algorithms" without full implementation details.
- Proofs are in the appendix; main text relies on deferred formal details.
- No empirical validation; acceptable for a theory paper but small synthetic experiments would strengthen the work.
- Paper is dense but mostly well-structured; key definitions and motivations are clear.
- Some terms (semi-Markovian, dormant independence) are assumed without brief reminders; main text lacks succinct algorithmic description for Sec. 4.
- Figures are crucial; captions/references and some formatting/references contain glitches and typos.
- Novel contributions include R in admissible factorizations, R-partition/Markov blanket sufficiency, explicit non-recoverability criteria, and recoverable causal effects despite probabilistic non-recoverability.
- Simple-attrition completeness and correction to Garcia (2013) are valuable and original.
- Results are significant for causal inference under MNAR and likely to be built upon, though practical impact depends on correct m-graphs.
- Strengths: clear theoretical advances, new non-recoverability criteria, elegant do-calculus use, complete simple-attrition characterization, illustrative examples.
- Weaknesses: no fully specified general-purpose algorithm in main text, no empirical studies, completeness limited to |Vm|=1, sensitivity to m-graph misspecification not addressed, expositional/formatting issues.
- Suggested revisions: add concise pseudo-code/algorithm with complexity/termination conditions for Sec. 4.
- Suggested revisions: discuss robustness/sensitivity to m-graph misspecification and possible diagnostics.
- Suggested revisions: clarify relationship/tightness between Theorems 2 and 3 with boundary examples.
- Suggested revisions: include one or two synthetic experiments comparing against IPW/MI under MAR/MNAR.
- Acceptance arguments: strong novel theoretical contributions, sufficiency and necessity results, important insight that causal recoverability can exceed probabilistic recoverability.
- Rejection arguments: limited general-case completeness, absence of a main-text implementable algorithm, and lack of empirical illustrations.
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline strong); theoretical impact outweighs presentation and empirical limitations.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
