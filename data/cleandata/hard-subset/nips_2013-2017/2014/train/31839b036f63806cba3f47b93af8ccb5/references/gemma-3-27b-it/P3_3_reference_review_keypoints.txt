- Overall: Strong theoretical extension of m-graphs that recovers probabilistic and causal queries; technically sound but needs clearer presentation—recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Contribution: Refines sequential factorization and introduces new non-recoverability criteria for missing data within m-graphs.  
- Novel result: Shows P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y is not d-separable from its missingness mechanism.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and Mohan & Pearl (2014), advancing identifiability results for missing data.  
- Theoretical rigor: Clear theorems and proofs (appendix) and well-motivated extensions to existing theory.  
- Novelty & significance: Offers important advances for causal inference under missingness, with practical relevance to fields like epidemiology and social sciences.  
- Coverage: Addresses both recoverability and non-recoverability, adding new graphical criteria for non-recoverability.  
- Application: Demonstrates relevance through an attrition example, illuminating when causal effects remain estimable.  
- Framework: Use of m-graphs provides a formal, well-defined representation of missingness mechanisms.  
- Clarity issues: Paper is dense, heavy in notation, and can be hard to follow even for specialists.  
- Example complexity: Provided examples are often complex and do not clearly convey intuition; simpler examples are needed.  
- Practical implementation: Lacks discussion of real-world challenges (estimating probabilities, scaling to large m-graphs).  
- Related work: Could better discuss practical limitations of existing methods (e.g., multiple imputation) when assumptions fail.  
- Definition 2 comment: "Minimality" is crucial but not intuitive—add an illustrative example showing failure of non-minimal factorizations.  
- Theorem 4 comment: Conditions for recovering P(y|do(x)) when Y and Ry are inseparable need more explanation and a detailed example walkthrough.  
- Attrition section: Strong connection to theory but needs guidance for practitioners on verifying recoverability conditions in real scenarios.  
- Figure 2(a) comment: Explanation for introducing Ry into ordered factorization is too brief and needs elaboration.  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical contribution, rigorous, addresses key limitations, and has practical applications.  
- Against acceptance: Dense presentation and insufficient discussion of practical implementation may limit impact.  
- Recommendation details: Revise for clarity—simplify key theorems/definitions, add simpler illustrative examples, discuss practical implementation challenges, and expand attrition guidance.
