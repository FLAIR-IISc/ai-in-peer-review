- Overall: Recommends acceptance with minor revisions; extends m-graph framework to better handle missing data and causal queries.  
- Main contribution: Extends the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting R variables and provides graphical criteria for recoverability and non-recoverability.  
- Key novel finding: Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic queries are not.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and Mohan & Pearl (2014), and connects to classical missing-data and causal inference literature.  
- Theoretical rigor: Strong formal foundations with clear definitions, theorems, and proofs (appendix provided).  
- Graphical criteria: Introduces practical graphical tests for non-recoverability to assess feasibility before estimation.  
- Examples and applications: Clear illustrative examples (including attrition) that clarify complex concepts and demonstrate applicability.  
- Notation and clarity: Paper is notationally dense and has a steep learning curve for readers unfamiliar with m-graphs.  
- Empirical validation: Lacks substantial empirical evaluation on real or simulated datasets.  
- Theorem complexity: Some theorems (e.g., Theorem 2, Corollary 1) are complex and need more intuitive exposition.  
- Assumption sensitivity: Needs more discussion on challenges and sensitivity when specifying the m-graph and potential misspecification.  
- Quality/clarity/originality/significance scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Acceptance pros: Strong theory, novel causal recoverability result, useful graphical criteria, relevant attrition application, comprehensive related work.  
- Acceptance cons: Dense notation, limited empirical results, and complex theorem presentation.  
- Recommended minor revisions: Simplify/introduce notation gradually (add glossary), add more worked examples, discuss m-graph misspecification sensitivity, and consider a small empirical evaluation.
