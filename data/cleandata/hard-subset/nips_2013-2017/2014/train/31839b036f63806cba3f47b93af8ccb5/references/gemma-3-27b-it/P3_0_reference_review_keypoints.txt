- Extends missingness graphs (m-graphs) to broaden recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Generalizes the sequential factorization theorem (Theorem 1) and introduces Theorem 2 for interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Demonstrates causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic relations are not.  
- Applies the framework to attrition problems and gives conditions for recoverability in such settings.  
- Provides a clear formal definition of recoverability and introduces graphical non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3 & Corollary 2).  
- Theoretical development is rigorous and mathematically substantial.  
- Offers practical relevance for experimental science and practitioners dealing with missing data.  
- Presentation is dense and could benefit from more intuitive explanations and additional illustrative examples.  
- Omits discussion of computational complexity and practical implementation challenges (e.g., finding admissible sequences).  
- Relies on strong assumptions about correct m-graph specification; sensitivity to model misspecification is not addressed.  
- Lacks empirical validation or experiments on real-world datasets.  
- Needs clearer exposition for Definition 2, the jump to Theorem 4 in Section 6 (d*-separation), and the distinction between Theorems 5 and 6 in Section 7.  
- Should discuss connections to sensitivity analysis and related methods (double machine learning, TMLE).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, address computational/sensitivity issues, and add examples.  
- Pros: significant theoretical advances and novel insights into causal recoverability; Cons: accessibility, no empirical validation, limited practical discussion.
