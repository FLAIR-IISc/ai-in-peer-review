- Paper extends Mohan et al. (2013) to give formal graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Shows causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic relations (e.g., P(y,x)) are not.  
- Introduces new graphical criteria for recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.  
- Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for causal effect recovery under attrition and corrects prior misconceptions.  
- Extends sequential factorization (Theorem 1) to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Formalizes "admissible sequences" with minimality constraints to address limitations of prior work.  
- Applies framework to attrition problems with practical theorems (Theorems 5 and 6) that are testable on graphs.  
- Thoroughly situates contributions within missing data literature and highlights unexplored interactions among missingness mechanisms.  
- Strong theoretical contribution distinguishing probabilistic vs. causal recoverability with important implications for practitioners.  
- Precise formalism and illustrative examples effectively communicate key concepts.  
- Lacks empirical validation such as simulations or real-world case studies to demonstrate practical applicability.  
- Assumes the m-graph (missingness mechanism) is known and does not address how to learn it from data.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental over Mohan et al. (2013); paper should better clarify precise theoretical advances.  
- Provides theoretical conditions but lacks concrete algorithms and implementation details for recovery procedures.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality theoretical work with important insights but limited practical demonstration and assumptions that constrain applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting (1) brief empirical validation, (2) discussion on learning the m-graph, and (3) clearer statement of advances over Mohan et al. (2013).
