- Extends m-graphs to derive general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness Ry are not d-separated (Theorem 4, Example 3).  
- Provides necessary and sufficient recoverability conditions for attrition problems and corrects an error in Garcia (2013).  
- Unifies probabilistic and causal inference under a single graphical framework handling interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and challenges prevailing assumptions about separability and recoverability.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with precise definitions and illustrative examples.  
- Corrects a prior misconception (Garcia 2013) via counterexample, improving the literature’s accuracy.  
- Introduces "d*-separation" but lacks sufficient intuitive motivation for its role in causal (vs probabilistic) recovery.  
- No empirical validation or experiments with real/simulated data, limiting assessment of practical impact.  
- Limited discussion of connections to mainstream machine learning applications dealing with missing data.  
- Does not address computational complexity or scalability of the proposed recovery procedures.  
- Technical development is rigorous with proofs, but high background knowledge is required.  
- Original and novel insight with clear significance for fields where missing data is ubiquitous (e.g., healthcare, social sciences).  
- Recommendation: accept for strong theoretical contribution, but authors should add empirical validation and stronger ML connections in future work.
