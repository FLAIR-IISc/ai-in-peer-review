- Overall assessment: Solid, significant extension of m-graph missing-data theory; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Core contribution: Generalizes sequential factorization to handle missingness indicators that influence each other.  
- Novel result: Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when probabilistic queries are not.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and contrasts with standard ML/imputation/IPW approaches.  
- Theoretical rigor: Mathematically sound with clear definitions, theorems, and proofs in the appendix.  
- Practical relevance: Applicable to real problems like attrition; offers actionable insights for such cases.  
- Coverage: Provides broad, detailed analysis of sufficient and necessary recoverability conditions.  
- Use of do-calculus: Well-justified connection strengthening causal recoverability results.  
- Accessibility weakness: Paper is dense, heavy notation, and assumes strong prior knowledge—needs clearer exposition.  
- Examples weakness: Limited number and complexity of examples; m-graph visualizations should be more prominent.  
- Computational weakness: Does not address practical computational complexity or implementation challenges.  
- Empirical weakness: Largely theoretical; lacks substantive empirical or simulation validation.  
- NIPS criteria: Quality—excellent; Clarity—fair; Originality—good; Significance—good.  
- Pro acceptance points: Strong theory, novel causal recoverability, important problem, ties to established frameworks.  
- Con acceptance points: Dense writing, limited examples/empirics, and no discussion of computational cost.  
- Requested revisions: Improve clarity and notation, add more illustrative examples/visualizations, discuss computational issues, and include a limited empirical evaluation.
