- Summary: Extends m-graphs framework to give refined conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries when standard recoverability assumptions fail.  
- Summary: Shows causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic queries are not.  
- Summary: Introduces sequential factorization theorems, algorithms, non-recoverability criteria, and applies results to attrition.

- Strength: Addresses an important, pervasive problem in handling missing data.  
- Strength: Theoretical rigor with clearly stated theorems and proofs.  
- Strength: Novel result that causal queries can be recovered where probabilistic queries cannot.  
- Strength: Generalizes and extends Mohan et al. (2013) to more complex missingness scenarios.  
- Strength: Practical relevance demonstrated via attrition application.  
- Strength: Clear organization and helpful examples for a theoretical paper.

- Weakness: Highly theoretical and notation-dense, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: No empirical validation or experiments on real/simulated data to show practical performance.  
- Weakness: Theorems and corollaries are complex, which may hinder adoption.  
- Weakness: Relies on specific m-graph structures and identifiability; robustness to model misspecification is not addressed.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion/comparison to practical imputation or weighting methods (e.g., multiple imputation, IPW).

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel theoretical contribution with potential impact, but limited immediate practical applicability due to lack of empirical validation and high complexity.

- Quality: Excellent (technically sound).  
- Clarity: Good for experts but dense and challenging for broader audiences.  
- Originality: Excellent.  
- Significance: High.

- Arguments for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, potential to unlock new insights, rigorous and well-written for target audience.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks empirical validation, high theoretical complexity, and limited connection to practical techniques.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Required minor revisions: expand discussion of practical implementation and challenges; include a small illustrative simulation; add a high-level overview and clearer notation for accessibility; strengthen comparison to existing imputation/weighting methods.
