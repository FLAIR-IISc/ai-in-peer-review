- Generalization of ordered factorizations to include R variables (Theorem 1), yielding new admissible sequences and requiring a “minimality” condition.  
- Recovery without admissible sequences via R partitions and collider-path-based Markov blankets (Theorem 2), with Corollary 1 for models without latent confounding.  
- Graphical necessary non-recoverability criteria for P(V) and P(Y|X) (Theorem 3, Corollary 2), filling a gap in prior work.  
- Causal recoverability can hold even when associated probabilistic quantities do not; Theorem 4 gives conditions (|Vm|=1 with inducing path) using do-calculus and dormant independence.  
- Attrition results: Theorem 5 (necessary and sufficient for P(V) recoverability) and Theorem 6 (for P(y|do(x))) for simple attrition, correcting prior claims.  
- Theorems are sound with clear graphical preconditions and plausible derivations; full proofs in the appendix are appropriate.  
- Paper lacks algorithmic detail for constructing R partitions and Mb(R(i)) and for checking conditions automatically; complexity analysis is missing.  
- Positivity over complete cases is assumed; the paper should discuss sensitivity to violations and monotone missingness, as some preconditions are strong.  
- No empirical validation or simulations; a small synthetic study is recommended to demonstrate finite-sample behavior and robustness to m-graph misspecification.  
- Presentation is notation-dense; recommend a notation table, step-by-step checklists for Theorems 1 and 2, and clearer formalization/examples of minimality.  
- Theorem 4 and related constructs (Y ⟂w Ry|Z, role of W1) need more intuition and a simple worked example.  
- Several reference formatting and citation-style issues need correction.  
- Contributions appear original relative to Mohan et al. (2013): R-inclusive factorizations, collider-path-based recovery, and formal non-recoverability criteria.  
- Significance: advances practical guidance for missing-data recoverability, offers guardrails via non-recoverability criteria, and a notable insight that P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable despite non-recoverable probabilistic relations.  
- Questions for authors: provide pseudocode for finding admissible sequences and computing Rpart/Mb with complexity; can Theorem 4 extend beyond |Vm|=1; how robust are estimands to m-graph misspecification; add a synthetic study.  
- Pros: clear, nontrivial theoretical advances; bridges probabilistic and causal recoverability; provides constructive estimands and corrects prior attrition literature.  
- Cons: heavy notation and some opaque definitions; no empirical illustrations or automated condition-checking guidance; reference issues.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — theoretical contributions and impact outweigh clarity and empirical shortcomings, but revision should address clarity and algorithmic guidance.
