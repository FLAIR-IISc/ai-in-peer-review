- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to a formal framework for recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.
- Introduces new theoretical conditions for recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates that causal effects can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not.
- Introduces d*-separation to handle cases where Y and Ry are connected by an inducing path.
- Provides necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for recovery, including for attrition problems.
- Technically rigorous with thorough proofs and clear logical development.
- Example 3 effectively illustrates the distinction between probabilistic and causal recoverability.
- Carefully distinguishes missingness mechanisms (MCAR, MAR, MNAR) and states assumptions (e.g., positivity of complete-case distributions).
- Corrects misconceptions in prior work (e.g., Garcia 2013) regarding causal recoverability under simple attrition.
- Significant practical relevance due to the ubiquity of missing data in many scientific fields.
- Application to attrition is particularly relevant for longitudinal studies.
- Strengths: strong theoretical foundation, clear definitions, novel results, and thorough comparison to prior work.
- Weaknesses: highly theoretical with limited practical implementation guidance and no empirical evaluation.
- Additional weaknesses: notation for missingness may be non-intuitive for non-specialists and computational complexity is not well discussed.
- Recommendation: accept to NeurIPS as a substantial theoretical advance, with a suggested addition of computational/practical discussion.
