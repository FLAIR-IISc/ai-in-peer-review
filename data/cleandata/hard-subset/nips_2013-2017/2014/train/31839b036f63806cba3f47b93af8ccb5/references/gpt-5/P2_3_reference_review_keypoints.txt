- Extends m-graphs to give new sufficient and necessary conditions for recovering joint/conditional distributions and shows some causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recovered even when probabilistic quantities are not.
- Theorem 1: generalized ordered factorization allowing missingness indicators R in separating sets to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Theorem 2: a constructive sufficient condition for recovering P(V) using partitions of R (based on collider-path connectivity) and Markov blankets Mb(R(i)); Corollary 1 simplifies this without latent confounding.
- Theorem 3 / Corollary 2: necessary graphical conditions for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions—novel formal non-recoverability criteria.
- Theorem 4 and examples: causal effects can be recoverable via do-calculus/dormant independences even when Y and Ry are inseparable and probabilistic recovery fails.
- Attrition case (|Vm|=1): Theorems 5–6 provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering P(V) and causal effects, refining prior work.
- Situates results within Rubin’s MCAR/MAR, ML/EM, IPW/MI, CAR literature and causal identifiability/ADMG/dormant independence frameworks.
- Technical soundness: theorem-driven with proofs in appendix; Theorem 2 gives internally consistent constructive formula; non-recoverability and causal examples are convincing.
- Completeness limitation: no full characterization or decision procedure for multiple partially observed variables; conditions are not jointly sufficient in general.
- Empirical validation missing: purely theoretical paper; a small computational demonstration or tool would strengthen impact.
- Assumptions: positivity stated; semi-Markovian models used but should be defined in main text for clarity.
- Clarity issues: dense notation, unclear usage of “GX” variants, and need for more intuition after theorems and moved algorithmic steps from appendix to main text.
- Minor editorial issues: reference formatting and consistency need fixing.
- Originality: strong—novel extension of ordered factorization, new partition-and-Markov-blanket recoverability approach, and first formal non-recoverability criteria in MNAR graphs.
- Significance: high theoretical impact on MNAR inference and attrition analysis; practical adoption depends on tooling to detect conditions and emit estimands.
- Pros: advances graphical missing data, clarifies causal recoverability, gives tight attrition results, and uses careful do-calculus/collider-path reasoning.
- Cons: lacks end-to-end algorithm/complexity analysis for general case, dense notation/clarity issues, and no empirical or automated demonstration.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful theoretical contributions warrant publication with modest clarity/tooling improvements.
