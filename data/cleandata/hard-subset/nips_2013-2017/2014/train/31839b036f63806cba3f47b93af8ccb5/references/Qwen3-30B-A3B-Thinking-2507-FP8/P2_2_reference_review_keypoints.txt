- Introduces "Missingness Graphs" (m-graphs) to formally represent missing data processes, extending Mohan et al. (2013).
- Establishes conditions for recovering probabilistic queries (joint and conditional distributions) from m-graphs.
- Shows causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not.
- Provides necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for recoverability, including non-recoverability criteria.
- Presents the sequential factorization theorem (Theorem 1) and a more general recovery result (Theorem 2) to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Distinguishes probabilistic recoverability (Theorem 3) from causal recoverability (Theorem 4), illustrated by a compelling example (Example 3).
- Applies the framework to attrition in longitudinal studies and corrects misconceptions in prior work (e.g., Garcia 2013).
- Demonstrates strong technical quality with formal definitions, theorems, and complete proofs (appendix).
- Limits acknowledged: focuses on semi-Markovian models and does not address computational/algorithmic aspects.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with effective figures and illustrative examples.
- Notation can be dense and some technical terms (e.g., Rpart, Mb(R(i))) need clearer explanation for non-experts.
- Original contributions include necessary-and-sufficient recoverability conditions and handling interactions among R variables.
- Work is significant for practice: clarifies when causal effects can be recovered from missing data, impacting longitudinal and experimental studies.
- Overall assessment: high-quality theoretical contribution with important implications — recommendation: Accept.
