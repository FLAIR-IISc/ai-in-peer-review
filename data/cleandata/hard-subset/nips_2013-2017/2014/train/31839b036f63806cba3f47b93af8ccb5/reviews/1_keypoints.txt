- Tackles identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework; improves some existing results and gives useful examples (e.g., causal identifiability when conditional distributions are unavailable).
- Technical quality is high; results are involved and appear correct; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.
- Some results are incremental and do not yet reveal a larger unifying picture.
- Section 3 feels orthogonal to the rest and Theorem 1 is hard to comprehend; relates closely to Richardson (2009).
- Paper quality improves in Sections 5 (clear answers) and 6 (interesting causal-query results).
- Main weakness is clarity: too much material for a conference paper, dense text, poor flow, and a lengthy 13-page appendix; better suited to a journal.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (overuse of capital V, Vi as a vertex vs Vo as a set, illegible subscripts); Corollary 1 exemplifies poor presentation.
- Sections 5 and 6 are potentially significant and address an important problem with useful results.
- The paper should aim for a general identifiability criterion (analogous to causal-effect identifiability) rather than piecemeal results like Theorem 1.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse-probability weighting; likely related to conditioning on Markov blankets for R(i) in the mixed graphs—see Richardson (2003, 2009).
- Specific correction: R^part is a partition of R, so Rx and Ry should be stated to "belong to distinct elements of the partition."
- Language and notation fixes needed (avoid casual terms like "oftentimes"; use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->).
- Clarify whether Garcia (2013) is actually wrong or simply uses a different framework.
- Missing definition: notation for graphs formed by severing edges (used in Definition 4 and Theorem 4) is not defined.
- Vo and Vm are referenced before being defined, apparently unnecessarily.
- Overall verdict: important problem and useful approach, but presentation and notation issues significantly mar the paper.
