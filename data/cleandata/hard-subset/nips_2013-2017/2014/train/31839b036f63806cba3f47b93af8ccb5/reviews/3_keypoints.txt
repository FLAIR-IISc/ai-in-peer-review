- Paper studies when a distribution can be recovered given the data-generating process and a more general missingness mechanism than prior work, producing a novel identification formula and showing some causal queries are identifiable even when the joint is not.

- Results require knowledge of the censoring/missingness process; unclear if this is available in typical ML applications—recommend moving more Figure 1 detail into the main text.

- Section 2 implicitly assumes the full population is known (even entirely unobserved individuals are assumed to exist), excluding problems like population size estimation.

- The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently (sometimes including V* variables, sometimes not); the authors should clarify whether V* variables are being omitted and why.

- The paper forbids R variables as parents of V or U; given R are treated as real quantities, the authors should justify this restriction or state it is for convenience.

- The term "neighbor" is used without a definition and should be defined.

- On p.8: the relation to Garcia et al.'s "rectification" needs clarification regarding positivity assumptions—do Garcia et al. also assume positivity or a stronger condition?

- On p.8: the claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; explain why MLE could or could not be applied to the missingness processes considered here.

- Minor errors and corrections: wording ("can bias outcomes"), Figure 1 qualifications, Example 1 RHS should be P(Y|X,Z), replace Y || R with Y || R_y, fix R^{(N)} and R^{(j)} notation, Corollary 1 lacks a partially observed variable, and provide a reference for the variable-level definition in Footnote 2.

- Overall assessment: interesting, technically sound paper that builds on earlier work, but unclear how broadly applicable it is and it needs several clarifications and fixes.
