- Technical quality is high; results are intricate and appear correct, with Theorem 3 being clear and interpretable.
- Several contributions feel incremental and do not yet form a coherent, overarching explanation of the phenomena.
- Section 3 seems orthogonal to the rest, with Theorem 1 hard to parse and closely related to Richardson (2009).
- Presentation improves in later sections: Section 5 gives clear concrete answers and Section 6 has interesting findings about causal queries.
- Primary weakness is clarity: too much material for a conference paper, poor flow, and an overly dense 13-page technical appendix—better suited to a journal.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (reusing V, unclear subscripts, Vi vs Vo confusion), with examples like Corollary 1 highlighting poor presentation.
- Sections 5 and 6 are original, interesting, and potentially significant for the field.
- The paper would benefit from stepping back to produce a complete identifiability criterion for the general case.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse-probability-weighting and likely rely on conditioning sets that are Markov blankets; connections to Richardson (2003, 2009) are relevant.
- Specific fixes needed: correct wording for R^part as a partition (p4, l214) and ensure Rx, Ry are in distinct partition elements.
- Language style issues (e.g., casual phrasing like “oftentimes”) should be tightened.
- Notational suggestions: use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->, define the graph-severing notation used in Definition 4/Theorem 4, and define Vo and Vm before use.
- Question whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or just uses a different framework.
- Overall: addresses an important problem with useful approaches but is hampered by weak presentation and notation.
