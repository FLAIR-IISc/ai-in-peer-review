This paper proposes novel necessary and sufficient conditions to guarantee whether a causal query can be inferred from data in the presence of missing observations. It extends previous works that explicitly model the missing data generating mechanism into the causal diagram. The paper is clearly written and while it builds up on previous contributions using this approach, the additional insights provided constitute, in the opinion of this reviewer, a valuable contribution to the state of the art in the analysis of causal mechanisms in the presence of missing data.

The authors present a comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data. They introduce the concept of Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) and provide conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions, as well as causal queries. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical concepts.

One of the key contributions of the paper is the extension of the results of Mohan et al. [2013] to more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries. The authors also provide new graphical conditions for recovering causal queries, which is a significant advancement in the field.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The technical results are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the conditions under which the proposed methods are applicable.

The paper is also well-written, with clear and concise language. The authors provide a good balance between technical details and high-level explanations, making the paper accessible to a wide range of readers.
