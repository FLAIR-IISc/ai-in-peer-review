

This paper establishes novel necessary and sufficient conditions for identifying causal queries from data with missing values. It advances prior work by embedding the missing data mechanism directly into causal diagrams, thereby explicitly modeling the data generation process. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, building upon existing frameworks while offering significant new insights. These contributions represent a meaningful advancement in causal inference under missing data, addressing a critical gap in the literature. The technical development is sound, with rigorous proofs and clear exposition of the conditions' applicability. The authors effectively demonstrate how their approach extends beyond prior methods by unifying missing data mechanisms within causal graphical models, enabling more robust inference in practical settings where data incompleteness is prevalent. The clarity of presentation and precision of the theoretical results make this work accessible and impactful for both causal inference and missing data communities. This is a focused, well-executed contribution that substantially strengthens the theoretical foundation for causal analysis in the presence of missing observations.
