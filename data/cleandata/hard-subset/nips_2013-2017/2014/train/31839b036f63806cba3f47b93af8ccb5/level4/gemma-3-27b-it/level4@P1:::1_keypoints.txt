- Addresses identifiability with missing data, building on Mohan et al., and shows identifiability of causal distributions even when conditional distributions are unavailable.  
- Technical quality is high; results are complex but appear correct and interpretable (e.g., Theorem 3).  
- Some contributions feel incremental and do not clearly integrate into a broader framework for the field.  
- Section 3 is disconnected and Theorem 1 is hard to grasp; it seems related to Markov blanket concepts in Richardson (2003).  
- Later sections improve: Section 5 gives clear criteria and Section 6 contains interesting, significant causal-query results.  
- Main weakness is clarity: dense presentation, very long technical appendix, and poor narrative flow — might suit a journal better.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of “V”, illegible subscripts, Vi used in two senses), harming readability.  
- Specific presentation issues: set equation in Corollary 1 is unclear; Vo and Vm used before being defined.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting results, likely relying on Markov blanket conditioning; related Richardson (2003, 2009) work should be acknowledged.  
- Desires a more holistic, comprehensive identifiability criterion within the framework, analogous to non-missingness causal-identification results.  
- Minor corrections and clarifications needed: fix partition language on p4 l214, use “↔” not “<-->” on p4 l213, remove informal phrasing like “oftentimes.”  
- Clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or operates in a different framework.  
- Define notation for graphs formed by severing edges (used in Definition 4 and Theorem 4).  
- Overall: valuable and technically strong work with original and significant results in parts, but impact limited by presentation and clarity issues.
