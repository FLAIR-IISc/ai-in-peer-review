- Proposes novel necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for when causal and probabilistic queries are recoverable from data with missing observations.
- Extends prior work (Mohan et al. 2013) to more general recovery conditions for P(y|x), P(y,x), and causal effects P(y|do(x)).
- Introduces Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to explicitly model the missingness-generating process and dependencies among observed variables.
- Derives graphical criteria for recovering causal effects even when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.
- Provides comprehensive overview and formalization of the estimability problem for queries under missing data.
- Applies theoretical results to attrition problems and characterizes causal-effect recovery under attrition.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with good notation and illustrative examples.
- Presents a thorough related-work discussion and extensive references.
- Strength: delivers significant theoretical advances and practical graphical tools for missing-data causality.
- Weakness: assumes high technical expertise; notation and concepts may be challenging for some readers.
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations and future research directions.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance for publication.
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
