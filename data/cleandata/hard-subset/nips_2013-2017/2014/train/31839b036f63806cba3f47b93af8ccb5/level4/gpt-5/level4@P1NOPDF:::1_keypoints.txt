- Studies identifiability under missing-data generative mechanisms using Mohan et al.’s framework, strengthening some prior results and giving instructive examples.
- Technical quality is high; results are intricate and appear correct, with clear statements like Theorem 3.
- Some contributions are incremental extensions of prior work and do not cohere into a unifying theory.
- Section 3 feels orthogonal to the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to digest and seems related to Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.
- Exposition improves in later sections: Section 5 gives clear answers and Section 6 has interesting causal-query results.
- Main weakness is clarity: too much material for a conference paper, dense text, and a lengthy technical appendix; better suited for a journal.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (overuse of V, Vi vs Vo, illegible subscripts); Corollary 1’s presentation is especially poor.
- Sections 5 and 6 are potentially significant and contain genuinely useful results for an important problem.
- The paper would benefit from a general/complete identifiability criterion; current scattered results make it hard for practitioners to apply.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to instantiate inverse-probability-weighting and likely rely on Markov-blanket conditions; Richardson (2003, 2009) may be relevant.
- Several technical/notation issues: R^part wording is wrong (it’s a partition), use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->, and avoid casual language like “oftentimes.”
- Question raised whether Garcia (2013) is actually incorrect or simply uses a different framework.
- Notation for graphs after severing edges is used but never defined; Vo and Vm appear before being defined.
- Overall: important problem and useful approach, but presentation and notation significantly hinder readability and impact.
