- Addresses identifiability under Mohan-style missing-data mechanisms, refining results and giving examples including causal identifiability when certain conditionals are unavailable.
- Technical quality is high; results are intricate but appear correct and some (e.g., Theorem 3) are clear and interpretable.
- Several contributions feel incremental and do not advance a unifying perspective on core identifiability issues.
- Section 3 seems largely orthogonal to the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to parse and appears related to Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.
- Exposition strengthens in Sections 5 and 6, which provide clear, concrete answers and interesting causal-query results.
- Main weakness is clarity: too much material for a conference paper, poor narrative flow, and dense 13-page technical appendix — better suited to a journal.
- Notation problems (reusing V, confusing subscripts, Vi vs Vo) make the paper hard to read; Corollary 1 exemplifies presentation issues.
- Would be more impactful to aim for a general/complete identifiability criterion rather than a sequence of narrow results.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting; likely tied to conditioning sets acting as Markov blankets, with Richardson (2003, 2009) relevant.
- Specific editorial/technical issues: misstatement about R^part, casual language, incorrect arrow notation, question about Garcia (2013), undefined notation for severed-edge graphs, and Vo/Vm referenced before definition.
- Overall: tackles an important problem and contains useful results but is marred by presentation and notation shortcomings.
