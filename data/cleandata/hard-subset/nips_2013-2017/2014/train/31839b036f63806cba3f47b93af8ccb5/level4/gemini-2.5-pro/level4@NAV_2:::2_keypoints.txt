- Presents a formal graphical analysis for deciding recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing values, extending the m-graph framework.  
- Introduces a generalized sequential factorization theorem that handles dependencies between missingness mechanisms.  
- Proposes novel graphical criteria that are sufficient for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.  
- Shows that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when the associated joint distribution is not, particularly when a variable and its missingness indicator are not d-separable.  
- Applies the theoretical results to the practical problem of attrition.  
- Significance: provides practical tools for a widespread problem and reveals that causal recoverability has weaker requirements than probabilistic recoverability.  
- Non-recoverability criteria are valuable for identifying when estimation is impossible, preventing futile analyses.  
- Originality and quality: several novel, non-trivial theorems (including Theorems 1–4), technically sound and supported by proofs.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition with precise writing, motivating examples, and effective graphical illustrations.  
- Critique: work is primarily theoretical; no major weaknesses identified.  
- Minor recommendation: include a discussion on the algorithmic complexity of checking the proposed graphical conditions.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically solid, original, and significant contribution to causal inference and missing-data research.
