- Strong contribution: generalizes Mohan et al. to broader missingness processes, gives a new identification formula, and shows some causal effects are identifiable even when the observational joint is not.  
- Assumed knowledge of the censoring/missingness process: need discussion of realism in ML settings and concrete examples where this process is known; move more Figure 1 detail into main text.  
- Population-level assumption: explicitly state that the full population/existence is assumed known and clarify exclusion of population size estimation problems (e.g., capture–recapture).  
- Ambiguity in “mgraph”: standardize whether V* nodes are included, explicitly note when V* are omitted for brevity.  
- Restriction that R variables cannot be parents of V or U: justify whether this is conceptual or technical and discuss implications of relaxing it.  
- Undefined term “neighbor”: provide a precise definition (e.g., graphical adjacency or other relation).  
- Clarify “rectification” (Garcia et al.): define or reference it and state whether their results assume positivity and how their conditions compare to the paper’s.  
- “Maximum Likelihood under MAR”: specify precise assumptions needed for ML consistency (model correctness, positivity, MAR definition) and explain why ML methods don’t extend to the more general missingness processes studied.  
- Minor wording/math corrections: p.1 l.33 specify what “can bias outcomes” means; p.2 Figure 1 caption “Qualifications” unclear.  
- Minor equation/notation fixes: p.3 Ex.1 should read P(Y | X, Z, R_y) = P(Y | X, Z); p.3 l.168 replace Y ⟂ R with Y ⟂ R_y; p.3 l.214 use R^{(N)} and p.3 l.215 use R^{(j)}.  
- Minor grammar/refs: p.4 Corollary 1 phrase should be “any partially observed variables”; p.6 Footnote 2 should include a reference for the variable-level definition.  
- Overall: technically strong and novel, but needs clearer assumptions, consistent terminology/notation, and sharper comparisons to existing methods (including ML under MAR) to strengthen practical relevance.
