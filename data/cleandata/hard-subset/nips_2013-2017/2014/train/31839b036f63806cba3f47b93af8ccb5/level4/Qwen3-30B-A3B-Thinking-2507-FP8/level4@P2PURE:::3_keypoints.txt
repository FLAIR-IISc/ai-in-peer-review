(1) The results rely on knowledge of the censoring/missingness process — question whether this is available in ML applications and request for more detail/clarification of Figure 1.  
(2) Section 2 should state the model assumes the full population is known (individual existence is assumed even when no variables are observed), excluding population-size estimation problems.  
(3) The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently with respect to inclusion of V* variables; the paper should explicitly state when V* are omitted.  
(4) The paper forbids R variables from being parents of V or U but treats R as real quantities — authors should justify or clarify this restriction.  
(5) The term "neighbor" is undefined and should be defined where used.  
(6) Clarify whether Garcia et al.’s "rectification" approach assumes positivity like the authors’ recoverability definition, since differing assumptions affect comparability.  
(7) The statement that Maximum Likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; explain why ML cannot be applied to the broader missingness processes considered here.

Overall: An interesting, technically sound paper building on earlier work but with unclear scope of application and several minor issues to fix.
