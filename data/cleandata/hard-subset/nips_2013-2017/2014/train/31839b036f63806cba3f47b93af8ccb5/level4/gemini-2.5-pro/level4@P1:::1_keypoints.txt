- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing values using missingness graphs; extends prior results and gives new graphical conditions.  
- Key contribution: shows causal queries can sometimes be recovered even when corresponding probabilistic queries are not, with illustrative examples.  
- Technical quality is high; theoretical results appear correct and intricate.  
- Some contributions feel incremental and lack a unified, high-level framework.  
- Theorem 3 provides clear, interpretable non-recoverability criteria.  
- Theorem 1 (Section 3) is hard to parse and seems disconnected from the rest of the paper.  
- Sections 5 and 6 significantly improve clarity and present useful non-recoverability conditions and interesting causal recoverability findings.  
- Clarity is a major weakness: the paper is dense, overly ambitious for a conference format, and relies on a large appendix.  
- Notation is confusing and a barrier to understanding (overuse of 'V', confusing Vi vs Vo, small subscripts).  
- Complex expressions (e.g., Corollary 1) exemplify notational and presentation problems.  
- The work is important and potentially significant, but impact would increase with a general recoverability algorithm or completeness result.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear related to inverse probability weighting; may rely on Markov blankets for missingness indicators; relevant prior work includes Richardson (2003, 2009).  
- Minor issues: Vo and Vm used before definition; GX (graphs with severed edges) not defined; suggest using \leftrightarrow for bi-directed edges; clarify partition language for R_part.  
- Clarify the relationship to Garcia (2013): whether it is incorrect or simply uses different assumptions/framework.
