- Problem: deciding if causal or probabilistic queries are estimable from data with missing entries given a model of the missingness process.  
- Extension of Mohan et al. (2013): provides more general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).  
- Main contribution: development of graphical conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions and causal queries.  
- Introduces "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) to formally represent the missingness process and variable dependencies.  
- Theoretical results: several theorems offering sufficient conditions for recoverability of different query types.  
- Handles more general missingness mechanisms, including interactions between mechanisms, with novel identification formulas.  
- Shows causal queries can sometimes be identified even when the observational joint distribution is not.  
- Presentation: paper is well-organized, easy to follow, with a clear and concise introduction.  
- Limitation: approach requires knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may be unavailable in many ML applications.  
- Notation/terminology issues: "m-graph" is used ambiguously (unclear if V* variables are included).  
- Exposition issue: Figure 1 needs more detail as it’s an important illustration.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss applicability to real-world problems and limitations of the approach.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
