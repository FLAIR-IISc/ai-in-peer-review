Main comments:
1. Results rely on knowing the exact missingness (censoring) mechanism—authors should discuss feasibility in real ML settings and expand Figure 1 description.  
2. Framework assumes knowledge of the full population (including individuals with no observed variables); clarify this limitation and its implications (e.g., cannot handle population-size estimation).  
3. "mgraph" notation is inconsistent (V* appears in Fig.1 but not Fig.2); explicitly state when V* is omitted and ensure notation consistency.  
4. Prohibition of R variables as parents of V or U must be justified—clarify whether this is theoretically necessary or for notational convenience.  
5. The term "neighbor" is used without definition on page 4 and must be precisely defined.  
6. Comparison to Garcia et al.: clarify whether Garcia assumes the same positivity condition for recoverability or requires stronger assumptions; adjust claims accordingly.  
7. The Maximum Likelihood claim is imprecise—explain why ML cannot be applied to the missingness processes studied (e.g., non-identifiability or structural constraints).
