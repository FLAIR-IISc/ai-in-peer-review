- Addresses identifiability under various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.’s framework and improves some existing results.  
- Provides illustrative examples, including causal distribution identifiability without conditional distributions.  
- Technical quality is high; results are intricate and appear correct, with Theorem 3 clear and interpretable.  
- Several contributions are incremental and do not advance broader conceptual understanding.  
- Section 3 is disconnected from the main narrative and Theorem 1 is hard to grasp (likely related to Richardson’s Markov-blanket results).  
- Sections 5 and 6 are stronger, offering clear insights and interesting causal-query results.  
- Primary weakness is clarity: paper is overly dense for a conference (13-page appendix, heavy text).  
- Notation is problematic: overuse of capital V, illegible subscripts, and inconsistent Vi (vertex) vs Vo (vertex set).  
- Corollary 1 is poorly presented and exemplifies the notation/readability issues.  
- Paper would benefit from a synthesized general identifiability criterion within the framework to avoid forcing users to navigate multiple complex theorems.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 likely correspond to inverse-probability-weighting and may relate to Markov blankets for R(i); Richardson (2003, 2009) should be considered.  
- Specific technical/notation fixes: R^part is a partition of R (Rx and Ry should be in distinct partition elements); use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->.  
- Queries about whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply uses a different framework.  
- Definition 4 and Theorem 4 use an undefined graph-severing notation; Vo and Vm are referenced before being defined.  
- Overall: tackles an important problem with useful approaches, but marred by poor presentation and notation.
