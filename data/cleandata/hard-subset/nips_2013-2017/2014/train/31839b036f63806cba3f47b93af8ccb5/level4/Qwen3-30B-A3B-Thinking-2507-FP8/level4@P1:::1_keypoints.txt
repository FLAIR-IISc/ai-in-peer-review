- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) / Mohan & Pearl (2014) m-graph framework to derive graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Three main contributions: (1) general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries, (2) showing causal queries can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not, (3) application to attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical development with sufficient conditions (Theorems 1,2,4,5,6) and necessary conditions (Theorem 3, Corollary 2), with proofs in the appendix.  
- Demonstrates that P(y|do(x)) can be recovered even when Y and its missingness mechanism Ry are connected by an inducing path (Example 3; Theorem 4).  
- Offers clear practical implications for handling attrition in randomized trials and corrects prior misconceptions (e.g., Garcia 2013).  
- Technical results are well-supported by illustrative examples showing both utility and limitations.  
- Main strength is the novel insight that causal recoverability can diverge from probabilistic recoverability (Section 6).  
- Significant clarity issues: inconsistent and overloaded notation (e.g., multiple uses of "V", confusion between Vo and Vi, and dual use of "R").  
- Presentation problems: Theorem 1 is dense; the minimality requirement in Definition 2 is crucial but not sufficiently emphasized.  
- Organizational weaknesses: related results should be grouped and more intuitive explanations provided before formal theorems.  
- Weakness in contribution framing: paper feels incremental, presenting specialized results rather than a unified theory of recoverability.  
- Recommended revisions: standardize notation (e.g., V vs V_obs), add intuitive overviews of key concepts, and include a high-level summary linking theorems.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, notation, and organization.
