

This paper presents a valuable extension to the theory of missing data recovery by generalizing the missingness process beyond prior work (e.g., Mohan et al.). The authors establish a novel identification formula for distributions under this broader framework and demonstrate that causal queries can be identified even when the observational joint distribution is not recoverable. The technical contributions are sound, and the paper is generally well-structured. However, several clarifications and minor corrections are needed to strengthen the presentation and ensure precise technical communication. Below, I detail the key points requiring attention.

**Main Comments:**

1.  **Practical Assumption of Censoring Process Knowledge:** The results crucially depend on knowing the exact mechanism generating missingness. The authors should explicitly discuss whether this assumption is feasible in typical machine learning applications (e.g., clinical trials, surveys, observational datasets). Without justification or examples, the practical relevance of the framework remains unclear. Additionally, Figure 1, which is central to the exposition, requires more detail in the main text (e.g., a brief description of the variables and relationships depicted). Reducing the figure size to accommodate this text would be beneficial.

2.  **Assumption of Known Full Population:** The framework implicitly assumes the full population (including individuals with *no* observed variables) is known. Specifically, even when *V*<sub>o</sub> = ∅ and *R*<sub>v</sub> = 1 for all *v* ∈ *V*<sub>m</sub> (i.e., no variables observed for an individual), the model assumes we know that individual exists. This excludes problems like estimating population sizes from incomplete samples (e.g., wildlife counts). The authors should clarify this limitation and discuss its implications for applicability.

3.  **Ambiguity in mgraph Notation:** The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently. Figure 1 includes *V*<sup>*</sup> variables (representing missingness indicators), while Figure 2 omits them. If *V*<sup>*</sup> is omitted in some graphs for simplicity, this must be explicitly stated and justified to avoid confusion. The notation must be consistent throughout.

4.  **Restriction on R Variables as Parents:** The mgraphs prohibit *R* variables from being parents of variables in *V* or *U*. Given the authors treat *R* as real-valued quantities (not just indicators), they must justify this restriction. Is it theoretically necessary, or is it primarily for notational convenience? A clear explanation is essential for the validity of the framework.

5.  **Undefined Term "Neighbor":** The term "neighbor" appears without definition at the bottom of page 4. It is critical to define this term precisely within the context of the mgraph structure.

6.  **Comparison to Garcia et al.:** The paper references Garcia's "rectification" method on page 8. The authors' definition of recoverability assumes positivity (i.e., *P(R = r | Y, X, U) > 0* whenever *P(Y, X, U) > 0*). It is unclear whether Garcia et al. make the same assumption. If not, Garcia's approach might require stronger conditions, which should be acknowledged to accurately position the contribution.

7.  **Clarity on Maximum Likelihood:** The claim on page 8 ("Maximum Likelihood method is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR assumption") is imprecise. The authors should clarify *why* ML cannot be applied to the missingness processes described here (e.g., due to non-identifiability or specific structural constraints), rather than making a vague generalization about MAR.

**Minor Comments:**

*   Page 1, line 33: "can bias outcomes" → "can bias outcomes" (typo).
*   Page 2, Figure 1: Add a brief caption explaining the roles of *R*, *V*, *U*, and *V*<sup>*</sup>.
*   Page 3, Example 1, line 158: "P(Y |X,Z,R_y) = P(X,Z)" → "P(Y |X,Z,R_y) = P(Y|X,Z)".
*   Page 3, line 168: "Y || R | X,Z" → "Y || R<sub>y</sub> | X,Z".
*   Page 3, line 214: "R^{part} = {R^{(1)},R^{(2)}, \ldots , R^N}" → "R^{part} = {R^{(1)}, R^{(2)}, \ldots, R^{(N)}}".
*   Page 3, line 215: "R^j respectively" → "R^{(j)} respectively".
*   Page 4, Corollary 1: "does not contain a partially observed variables" → "does not contain partially observed variables".
*   Page 6, Footnote 2: The reference to "variable-level definition" is unclear. Please provide a specific citation or clarify the reference.

**Recommendation:** The paper presents a technically sound and interesting extension to missing data theory. The core contributions are valid and significant. The required clarifications (particularly regarding the practicality of the censoring process assumption, the full population assumption, mgraph notation consistency, the *R* parent restriction, and the Garcia comparison) are essential for a polished and impactful submission. Addressing these points will significantly enhance the paper's clarity, rigor, and perceived applicability. The minor corrections listed above are straightforward to implement. **I recommend acceptance pending these revisions.**
