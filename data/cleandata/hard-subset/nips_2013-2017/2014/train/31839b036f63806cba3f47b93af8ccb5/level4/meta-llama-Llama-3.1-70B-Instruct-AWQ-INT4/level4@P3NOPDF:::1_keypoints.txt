- Paper tackles identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework and gives insightful examples (e.g., identifying causal distributions without conditional distributions).

- Technical quality is high; results are complex and appear correct, with some clear and interpretable theorems (e.g., Theorem 3).

- Some results are incremental and do not substantially advance broader understanding.

- Section 3 feels disconnected from the rest, with Theorem 1 difficult to follow and seemingly related to Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.

- Later sections improve in quality: Section 5 provides clear answers and Section 6 contains interesting results on causal queries.

- Primary weakness is clarity: the paper is overly dense for a conference, has poor flow, and would be better as a longer journal article.

- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., reuse of capital V, illegible subscripts, Vi vs Vo), and some set expressions (Corollary 1) are poorly presented.

- Sections 5 and 6 are original and potentially significant; the paper addresses an important problem and contains useful results.

- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting; their validity likely ties to Markov blanket conditioning in mixed graphs.

- The work should aim toward a complete identifiability criterion analogous to causal-effect identification without missingness.

- Several presentation and technical corrections are needed: fix partition wording (p4, l214), avoid casual language, use proper symbols (e.g., \leftrightarrow), and clarify Garcia (2013)’s relation.

- Define notation used for graphs obtained by severing edges and ensure Vo and Vm are introduced before use.

- Overall: useful approach on an important problem but undermined by poor presentation and notation.
