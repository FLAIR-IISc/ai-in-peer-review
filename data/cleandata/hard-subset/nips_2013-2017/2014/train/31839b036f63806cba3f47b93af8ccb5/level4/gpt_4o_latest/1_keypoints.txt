- Addresses identifiability under missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework and extends existing results with illustrative examples (e.g., causal identifiability when conditionals unavailable).  
- Technical quality is high; results are intricate and appear correct, with Theorem 3 clear and interpretable.  
- Some contributions are incremental and do not substantially advance the broader understanding of the problem.  
- Section 3 feels disconnected; Theorem 1 is hard to interpret and seems closely related to Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are well-written, provide clear answers, and contain potentially impactful findings on causal queries.  
- Main shortcoming is clarity: dense writing, too much content for a conference paper, and an unwieldy 13-page appendix—better suited to a journal.  
- Notation is confusing (repeated use of capital V for different objects, hard-to-read subscripts, ambiguous Vi vs Vo), harming readability and presentation (e.g., Corollary 1).  
- The paper would benefit from a comprehensive identifiability criterion for the framework rather than piecemeal results scattered across papers.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting; conditioning sets likely act as Markov blankets for R(i), connecting to Richardson (2003, 2009).  
- Specific technical/notation fixes needed: correctly describe R^part as a partition, use ↔ instead of "<-->", and avoid informal language like "oftentimes."  
- Clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply uses a different framework.  
- Define notation for graphs with severed edges (Definition 4/Theorem 4) and avoid referencing Vo and Vm before defining them.  
- Overall: important problem and promising approach, but impact is reduced by unclear presentation and suboptimal notation.
