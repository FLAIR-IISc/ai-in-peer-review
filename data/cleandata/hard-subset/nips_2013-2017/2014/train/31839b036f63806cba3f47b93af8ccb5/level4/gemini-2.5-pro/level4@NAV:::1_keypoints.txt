- Technical quality is high; results are intricate and appear sound, with Theorem 3 being particularly clear and interpretable.  
- Some results are incremental over existing work and do not clearly advance broader conceptual understanding.  
- Section 3 feels disconnected from the main thrust and Theorem 1 is difficult to parse.  
- Theorem 1 seems closely related to Richardson’s work on Markov blankets (2009).  
- The paper strengthens in later parts: Section 5 provides clear insights and Section 6 offers interesting findings on causal queries.  
- Main weakness is clarity: the paper is dense, likely too much material for a conference paper, and would suit a journal submission better.  
- Notation is a major barrier (reuse of capital V for distinct objects, hard-to-read subscripts, inconsistent Vi vs Vo conventions).  
- Presentational issues—e.g., the set equation in Corollary 1—hinder comprehension.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are novel, interesting, and potentially significant for the field.  
- The work would be more impactful if it aimed for a general identifiability criterion rather than many specific results.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear related to inverse probability weighting; their validity may rely on conditioning sets acting as Markov blankets in mixed graphs.  
- Prior work by Richardson (2003, 2009) is likely relevant to several technical claims.  
- Minor fixes: R^part should be defined as “a partition of R” and Rx/Ry belong to distinct partition elements.  
- Other presentation issues: occasional informal prose, use \leftrightarrow instead of `<-->`, clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or uses a different framework, define notation for graphs with severed edges, and introduce Vo and Vm before use.
