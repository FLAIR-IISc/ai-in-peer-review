- Overall: Compelling manuscript that extends Mohan et al. to a broader class of missingness mechanisms, provides a new identification formula, and shows causal queries can be identified even when the observational joint cannot; generally well written with some issues.

Main comments
- (1) Results rely on knowing the censoring mechanism; question realism in ML settings and suggest moving more detail from Figure 1 into the main text.  
- (2) Section 2 assumes the entire population is known (individuals with all variables missing are still known), which excludes problems like estimating population size—this should be stated.  
- (3) The term "mgraph" is used ambiguously: sometimes includes V* nodes (Figure 1) and sometimes omits them (Figure 2); clarify if V* nodes are omitted for readability.  
- (4) mgraphs disallow R variables as parents of V or U; since R are treated substantively, justify this restriction or clarify it's for convenience.  
- (5) The term "neighbor" (bottom of p.4) is undefined and should be defined.  
- (6) On p.8: authors assume positivity for recoverability; clarify whether Garcia et al.'s "rectification" also assumes positivity, since their condition may be stronger.  
- (7) On p.8: statement that "Maximum Likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR" is too vague—explain why ML cannot be applied to the other missingness processes discussed.

Minor comments / errata
- p.1 line 33: wording issue — "can bias outcomes" needs review.  
- p.2 Figure 1: add qualifications (unspecified) to caption or figure.  
- p.2 / Example 1 (p.3 line 158): equation P(Y | X, Z, R_y) = P(X, Z) is wrong; RHS should be P(Y | X, Z).  
- p.3 line 168: independence statement should be Y ⟂⟂ R_y | X, Z (not Y ⟂⟂ R | X, Z).  
- p.3 line 214: notation R^{part} = {R^{(1)}, R^{(2)}, …, R^N} should use R^{(N)} (not R^N).  
- p.3 line 215: "R^j respectively" should be "R^{(j)}".  
- p.4 Corollary 1: does not contain a partially observed variables — clarify or correct.  
- p.6 Footnote 2: provide a reference for the variable-level definition.

- Final note: Interesting extension of earlier work; technical contributions are sound but breadth of applicability is not entirely clear.
