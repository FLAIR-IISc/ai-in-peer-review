- Addresses identifiability of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries given a model of the missingness process.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).  
- Derives graphical conditions for recovering P(y|do(x)) when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.  
- Presents a novel identification formula that can recover causal queries even when the observational joint distribution is not identified.  
- Handles a more general class of missingness processes than previous work.  
- Paper is well-organized, with a clear introduction and helpful examples.  
- Technical details are well-explained and results are theoretically and experimentally supported.  
- Thorough discussion of related work and clear differentiation from prior methods.  
- Main limitation: assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may be unavailable in typical ML settings.  
- Authors should discuss practical limitations and provide guidance for applying the method in practice.  
- Some notation and terminology need clarification (e.g., ambiguous use of "m-graph").  
- Authors should justify the restriction that R variables cannot be parents of variables in V or U.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant contribution to graphical models for missing data.  
- Recommendation split: strong arguments for acceptance (novel formula, solid analysis) but also reasons for rejection if practical assumptions and notation issues are not addressed.
