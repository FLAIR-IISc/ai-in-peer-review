- Paper studies identifiability under various missing-data mechanisms within Mohan et al.’s framework, strengthening some existing results and giving useful examples (e.g., identifiable causal distributions despite unavailable conditionals).  
- Technical quality is high; arguments are intricate but appear correct and results like Theorem 3 are clear and interpretable.  
- Some contributions are incremental extensions of prior work and lack a unifying, broader perspective.  
- Section 3 feels largely orthogonal to the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to digest and resembles Markov blanket results from Richardson (2009).  
- Exposition improves in later parts: Section 5 gives clear, concrete answers and Section 6 has interesting causal-query results.  
- Main weakness is clarity: too much material for a conference paper, dense prose, and a 13-page technical appendix suggest a journal format would be more appropriate.  
- Notation is overloaded and inconsistent (e.g., capital V used for multiple objects; Vi vs Vo confusing; illegible subscripts), harming readability.  
- Presentation issues exemplified by a confusing set equation in Corollary 1; clearer, more consistent notation is needed.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are potentially significant; authors should aim for a general/complete identifiability criterion analogous to completeness results without missingness.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse-probability-weighting and likely rely on conditioning sets being Markov blankets for R(i); related work by Richardson (2003, 2009) could help unify the results.  
- Specific fixes requested: correct phrasing for R^part as a partition, avoid informal prose, use ↔ notation, clarify Garcia (2013) relation, define notation for graphs with severed edges, and introduce Vo and Vm before use.  
- Overall: important problem and promising approach with valuable results, but presentation and notation issues undermine impact; stronger exposition and unification would increase significance.
