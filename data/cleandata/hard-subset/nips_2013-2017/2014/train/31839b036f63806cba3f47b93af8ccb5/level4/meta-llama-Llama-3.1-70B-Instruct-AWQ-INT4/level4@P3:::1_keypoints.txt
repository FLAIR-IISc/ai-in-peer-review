- Tackles whether causal or probabilistic queries are estimable from data with missing entries given a missingness model.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general recovery conditions for probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Applies results to attrition and characterizes recovery of causal effects under attrition.  
- Paper is well-motivated and addresses an important problem.  
- Technical quality is high and results appear correct.  
- Main weakness is clarity: text is dense and hard to follow.  
- Notation problems (reuse of capital V, illegible subscripts) hinder understanding.  
- Organization issues: Section 3 feels orthogonal and Theorem 1 is hard to comprehend.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear and contain interesting, potentially significant results.  
- Suggestion to step back and develop a complete identifiability criterion for the general case.  
- Minor issues: R^part described incorrectly; wording sometimes casual; use ←→ instead of <-->.  
- Minor issues: unclear whether Garcia (2013) is wrong or just different framework.  
- Minor issues: notation for graphs with severed edges is used but not defined; Vo and Vm used before definition.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on revisions to improve clarity and presentation.  
- Overall rating 7/10, reviewer confidence 8/10.
