- Paper studies identifiability of causal effects with missing data, extending Mohan et al. and showing identifiability even without full conditional distributions.  
- Technical quality is strong and results (e.g., Theorem 3) appear correct and interpretable.  
- Many results feel incremental and lack a unifying principle, reducing cumulative impact.  
- Section 3/Theorem 1 is disconnected, hard to understand, and seems closely related to existing Markov blanket results (Richardson 2009).  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clearer and present genuinely interesting, useful results on identifiability and causal queries.  
- Primary weakness is clarity: overly broad scope, dense text, and a long technical appendix—might be better as a journal paper.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., repeated use of ‘V’, Vi vs Vo as vertex vs set), which obscures key concepts and equations like Corollary 1.  
- Recommend aiming for a more general identifiability criterion (analogous to the complete criterion without missing data) rather than many specialized results.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 likely leverage inverse probability weighting and Markov-blanket-like conditioning; connection to Richardson (2003,2009) should be made explicit.  
- Specific editorial/technical fixes needed: clarify R^part wording, formalize arrow notation, resolve Garcia (2013) status, define graph-severing operations before use, and define Vo/Vm before referencing.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but invite revision and resubmission with improved clarity, unified narrative, and corrected notation.
