- Main contributions: generalizes sequential factorization for m-graphs, gives a recoverability theorem when factorization fails, presents graphical non-recoverability criteria, and shows causal queries can be recoverable even when joints/conditionals are not; applies results to attrition.

- Overall assessment: significant, technically sound extension of m-graph work with a novel and insightful result on causal query recoverability.

- Practical applicability concern: methods rely on detailed, practitioner-specified m-graphs—need discussion/examples on how such graphs would be specified in typical ML settings and more explanation of Figure 1.

- Scope limitation: paper seems to assume known population existence for all records (even if all variables missing); must state this explicitly and note it does not handle unknown population-size problems (e.g., capture–recapture).

- m-graph definition clarity: inconsistent use of proxy variables V* across figures—clarify whether V* nodes are omitted for readability and state the canonical m-graph definition used throughout.

- Model structural restriction: authors forbid R variables as parents of V or U; provide justification for this constraint or discuss consequences of relaxing it.

- Undefined term: “neighbor” is used (e.g., Theorem 2) but not defined—provide a precise definition (e.g., adjacency).

- Comparison to prior work: claim of rectifying Garcia (2013) may depend on a positivity assumption—compare underlying assumptions explicitly to validate the claim.

- Maximum Likelihood discussion: statement that ML yields consistent estimates under MAR is too general—explain why standard ML is insufficient for the MNAR models studied to motivate the new graphical criteria.

- Minor issues (typos/notation): wording “can bias outcomes” vague; Fig.1 caption typo “Qualifications”; Example 1 equation likely P(Y|X,Z,Ry)=P(Y|X,Z); clarify d-separation as Y ⟂⟂ Ry | X,Z; use R^{(N)} and R^{(j)} notation; fix “partially observed variable” phrasing; perform final proofreading.
