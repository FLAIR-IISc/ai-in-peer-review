- Extends m-graphs to provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries under missing data.  
- Demonstrates the counterintuitive result that P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when P(y,x) and P(y|x) are not.  
- Introduces general conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions and causal queries, extending sequential factorization to interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Develops new graphical criteria for non-recoverability and formalizes Theorem 4, showing causal effects can be recovered even when Y and Ry are not d-separable.  
- Provides rigorous proofs and clear algorithmic procedures for query recovery, with full proofs in the appendix.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, consistent notation, and effective figures and examples (e.g., Example 3, Figures 1–4).  
- Treats attrition as a special case, resolving prior confusions and demonstrating applicability to longitudinal studies and randomized trials.  
- Original contribution: first formal non-recoverability criteria for joint/conditional distributions and substantial generalization of Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Significant practical implications for causal inference with missing data across fields like clinical trials and social sciences.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical insights, comprehensive missingness treatment, rigorous proofs, and practical examples.  
- Weaknesses: limited empirical validation, sparse discussion of implementation/computational complexity, and limited comparison to methods like multiple imputation or IPW.  
- Recommendation: strong accept for theoretical advance, with suggested improvements via empirical validation and implementation discussion.
