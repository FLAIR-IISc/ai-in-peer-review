This paper addresses the problem of recovering a distribution from knowledge of the generating process and the process leading to missing data, presenting a novel identification formula and considering the identification of causal queries. The authors expand on earlier work by Mohan et al. by examining a more general class of missingness processes.

The paper is generally well-written, although there are some areas that require improvement, as outlined below.

MAIN COMMENTS:

(1) The results presented in this paper rely on knowledge of the process by which observations are censored. However, it is unclear whether this knowledge is readily available in typical machine learning applications. Furthermore, incorporating more detail regarding Figure 1 into the main paper would be beneficial, potentially by reducing the figure's size.

(2) Section 2 assumes that the full population is known, even in cases where no variables are observed for a particular individual (i.e., Vo = emptyset, and Rv =1 for all V in V_m). This assumption excludes certain missing data problems, such as estimating the number of animals in a population. It would be helpful to acknowledge this limitation explicitly.

(3) The term "mgraph" is used ambiguously throughout the paper. Specifically, the graph sometimes includes V* variables (Figure 1) and sometimes does not (Figure 2). If the V* variables are being omitted from the graphs, this should be explained to avoid confusion.

(4) The paper restricts mgraphs from having R variables as parents of variables in V or U. Given that the authors treat R variables as real quantities rather than simply indicators, they should provide justification for this restriction or acknowledge that it is primarily a matter of convenience.

(5) The term "neighbor" is introduced at the bottom of page 4 without a clear definition. Providing a definition or explanation for this term would improve the paper's clarity.

(6) On page 8, the first paragraph mentions the "rectification" of the observation by Garcia. However, the authors' definition of recoverability assumes positivity. It is unclear whether Garcia et al. also assume positivity, and if not, whether they require a stronger condition.

(7) The second paragraph on page 8 states that the "Maximum Likelihood method is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR assumption." This statement is overly vague, as it does not explain why maximum likelihood cannot be applied to the missingness processes described in the paper.

MINOR COMMENTS:

- Page 1, line 33: "can bias outcomes" should be rephrased for clarity.
- Page 2, Figure 1: The figure caption could be improved with additional qualifications.
- Page 3, Example 1, line 158: The equation P(Y |X,Z,R_y) = P(X,Z) should be corrected to P(Y|X,Z).
- Page 3, line 168: The statement "Since Y || R | X,Z" should be revised to "Y || R_y | X,Z" for consistency.
- Page 3, line 214: The notation "R^{part} ={R^{(1)},R^{(2)}, \ldots , R^N}" should be corrected to "R^{(N)}".
- Page 3, line 215: The notation "R^j" should be revised to "R^{(j)}" for consistency.
- Page 4, Corollary 1: The statement does not contain partially observed variables.
- Page 6, Footnote 2: A reference for the variable-level definition would be appreciated.

Overall, this is an interesting paper that builds upon earlier work. While the breadth of its application is unclear, the paper is technically sound.
