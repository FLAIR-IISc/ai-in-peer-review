1. Extends Mohan et al. [2013] graphical framework using m-graphs to analyze recoverability with missing values.  
2. Presents a generalized sequential factorization theorem handling dependencies between missingness mechanisms.  
3. Introduces novel graphical conditions for recovering joint distributions when factorization fails.  
4. Provides new graphical criteria for proving non-recoverability.  
5. Shows causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when corresponding joint/conditional distributions are not.  
6. Applies the theory to attrition in longitudinal studies as a practical case study.  
7. Paper is a solid, original, and non-trivial extension of prior work, technically sound with proofs in the appendix.  
8. Key insight: causal and probabilistic queries differ under missingness, with do-calculus/d*-separation enabling causal recoverability beyond probabilistic recoverability.  
9. Formal graphical criteria for non-recoverability are a useful practical contribution.  
10. Generally well-written with effective illustrative examples, though clarity can be improved in some areas.  
11. Strength: significantly generalizes recoverability conditions to allow interactions between missingness mechanisms.  
12. Strength: demonstration that P(y|do(x)) may be recoverable even when P(y,x) is not.  
13. Strength: first formal graphical criteria for non-recoverability (Theorem 3).  
14. Strength: useful application to the practical problem of attrition.  
15. Concern: strong assumption that full m-graph is known—question about applicability and sensitivity to mis-specification in typical ML settings.  
16. Concern: framework seems to assume known existence of all individuals even if all variables are missing; clarify it does not handle population size estimation.  
17. Concern: model constrains R variables from being parents of V or U—request justification or discussion of relaxing this constraint.  
18. Concern: comparison with Garcia [2013] needs clarification about differing positivity assumptions and whether prior non-recoverability claims assume non-positivity.  
19. Concern: statement about Maximum Likelihood under MAR is vague—clarify why ML is not straightforward for the MNAR models considered even with m-graph specified.  
20. Minor: define the term "neighbor" formally (e.g., two nodes connected by an edge).  
21. Minor: clarify inconsistent graph depictions—whether V* variables are omitted in later figures for simplicity.  
22. Minor typo: p.1, l.33 "can bias outcomes" → "can bias results" or "can introduce bias".  
23. Minor typo: p.2, Figure 1 caption "Qualifications" → "Qualification".
