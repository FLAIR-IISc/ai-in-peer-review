- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missingness using a model of the missingness process.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) to provide more general conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions and P(y|do(x)).  
- Shows causal queries can be recoverable even when factors in their identifying estimands are not.  
- Derives graphical conditions for recovering causal effects when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.  
- Applies results to attrition, characterizing recovery of causal effects under common attrition mechanisms.  
- Builds logically on prior work (Mohan et al., Pearl & Mohan) and situates itself well in the literature.  
- Introduction effectively motivates the problem and emphasizes importance of diagnosing missing data mechanisms.  
- Uses Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to provide a formal and intuitive framework for representing missingness.  
- Theorems 1–6 and corollaries are well-defined and provide significant theoretical insights.  
- Examples (Sections 3 and 6) help illustrate practical implications of the theory.  
- Rigorous mathematical treatment with precise definitions and (appendix) proofs that appear sound.  
- Clearly distinguishes recoverability of probabilistic versus causal queries and extends beyond MAR/MCAR scenarios.  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and practical challenges of applying proposed algorithms to large-scale data.  
- Would benefit from adding comparisons to alternative approaches (e.g., multiple imputation) and sensitivity analysis when m-graph assumptions are violated.  
- Recommendation: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational, comparative, and robustness concerns.
