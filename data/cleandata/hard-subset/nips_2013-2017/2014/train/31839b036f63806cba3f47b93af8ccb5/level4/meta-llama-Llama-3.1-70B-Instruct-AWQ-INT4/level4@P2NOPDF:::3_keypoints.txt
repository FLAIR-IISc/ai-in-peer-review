- Paper studies recovering distributions given the data-generating and missingness processes, generalizes missingness models, proposes a novel identification formula, and shows some causal queries are identifiable even when the observational joint is not.

Main comments:
- Results rely on knowing the censoring/missingness process; unclear how available this is in ML practice—suggest adding more detail on Figure 1 (e.g., reduce figure size and move into main text).
- The missingness models assume the full population is known, excluding tasks like estimating population size when individuals have no observed variables.
- The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently (sometimes includes V* variables as in Fig.1, sometimes not as in Fig.2); authors should explicitly state when V* are omitted.
- The paper disallows R variables as parents of V or U despite treating R as real-valued; authors should justify this restriction or admit it's for convenience.
- The term "neighbor" is introduced without a clear definition and needs a concise definition/explanation.
- The discussion of Garcia’s "rectification" is unclear regarding positivity: authors assume positivity for recoverability but should clarify whether Garcia et al. require the same or a stronger condition.
- The claim that "Maximum Likelihood is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR" is too vague; authors should explain precisely why ML cannot be applied to the missingness processes studied.

Minor comments:
- Page 1, line 33: rephrase "can bias outcomes" for clarity.
- Page 2, Figure 1: move figure qualifications into the main text.
- Page 2, Example 1, line 158: correct equation P(Y | X, Z, R_y) = P(X, Z) to P(Y | X, Z).
- Page 3, line 168: correct to Y || R_y | X, Z.
- Page 3, line 214: correct Condition (ii) to R^(N).
- Page 3, line 215: correct R^j to R^(j).
- Page 4, Corollary 1: statement should account for partially observed variables.
- Page 6, Footnote 2: provide a reference for the variable-level definition.

- Overall: Technically sound and builds on prior work, but applicability/breadth of the results is not clearly demonstrated.
