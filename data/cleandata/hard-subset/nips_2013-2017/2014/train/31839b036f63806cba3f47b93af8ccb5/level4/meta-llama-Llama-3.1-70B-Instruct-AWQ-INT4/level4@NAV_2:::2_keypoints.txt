- Presents a comprehensive framework for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries given a model of the missingness process.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recoverability of probabilistic queries and causal queries of the form P(y | do(x)).  
- Introduces new graphical conditions that identify when joint and conditional distributions are not recoverable.  
- Demonstrates applicability to real-world problems such as attrition.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear overview of missingness graphs and the notion of recoverability.  
- Technical content is sound with sufficient details and references to support claims.  
- Strengths: novel graphical conditions, broader generalization of prior work, practical application, and clear related-work coverage.  
- Weakness: technical details may be challenging for non-experts to follow.  
- Originality: builds on prior work but provides significant new insights and extensions.  
- Significance: results have important implications for social sciences, epidemiology, economics, and other fields with missing data.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add more explanations or examples to aid non-expert readers.  
- Overall rating: 9/10.
