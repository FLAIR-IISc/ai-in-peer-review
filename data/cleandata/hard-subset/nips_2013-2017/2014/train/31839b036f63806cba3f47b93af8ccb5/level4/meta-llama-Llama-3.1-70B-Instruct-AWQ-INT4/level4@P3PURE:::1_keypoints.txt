- Paper studies identifiability with missing data using Mohan et al.'s framework, improving some existing results and giving useful examples.  
- Technical quality is high with involved but seemingly correct results; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Several results are incremental and do not substantially advance broader understanding.  
- Section 3 feels orthogonal to the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to comprehend and may duplicate Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.  
- Quality and clarity improve in later sections; Section 5 gives clear answers and Section 6 has interesting causal-query results.  
- Main weakness is clarity: dense text, unclear flow, and an excessive 13-page appendix for a conference paper.  
- Notation is poor and confusing (reuse of capital V, illegible subscripts, unclear Vi vs Vo), harming readability.  
- Presentation issues include a poorly presented set equation in Corollary 1 and undefined notation for graphs formed by severing edges.  
- The paper addresses an important problem and contains potentially significant results, especially in Sections 5–6.  
- The authors should aim for a complete identifiability criterion rather than incremental theorems spread across papers.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting and likely use Markov blankets; Richardson (2003, 2009) are relevant.  
- Specific fixes: clarify R^part as a partition (rephrase condition), avoid casual language, use proper symbols (↔), and define Vo/Vm before use.  
- Unclear whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply uses a different framework.  
- Overall: important and useful approach, but poor presentation and notation substantially detract from the work.
