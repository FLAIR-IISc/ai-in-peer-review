- Problem and contribution: Extends Mohan et al. (2013) to give more general graphical conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and causal P(y|do(x)) with missing data.  
- Main technical novelty: Derives conditions for identifying causal effects when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.  
- Technical quality: Results appear correct and technically strong.  
- Examples and application: Several illustrative examples provided, including an attrition application.  
- Clarity issue: Paper is overloaded for a conference format and difficult to follow in places.  
- Notation problems: Reuses the same capital 'V' for different objects and other unclear notation choices.  
- Presentation issues: Corollary 1’s set equation is poorly presented and hard to parse.  
- Language/style: Some casual language; would benefit from more formal wording.  
- Literature/reference: The reference to Garcia [2013] needs clarification on whether it contradicts or uses a different framework.  
- Missing definitions: Notation for graphs formed by severing edges (Def. 4, Thm. 4) is used but not defined.  
- Ordering/definitions: Vo and Vm appear in text before being defined, creating confusion.  
- Significance/originality: Results (notably Secs. 5–6) are interesting and address an important problem in graphical models and missing data.  
- Suggested extension: Recommend stepping back to derive a complete, general identifiability criterion for the framework.  
- Acceptance pros: Important problem, clear/interesting results, high technical quality.  
- Acceptance cons: Poor clarity and notation, some results hard to comprehend, lacking a full general criterion.
