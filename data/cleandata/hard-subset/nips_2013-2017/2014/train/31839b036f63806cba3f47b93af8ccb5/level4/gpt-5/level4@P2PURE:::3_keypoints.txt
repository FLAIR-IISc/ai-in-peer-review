- Paper studies recoverability given known data-generating and missingness mechanisms, generalizing prior work and providing a new identification formula.
- Main concern: results assume the censoring/missingness mechanism is known — unclear if this is realistic in typical ML applications; suggest expanding Figure 1 in main text for clarity.
- The missing-data setup implicitly assumes knowledge of the full population (individuals with all variables missing are still assumed known), excluding tasks like population size estimation.
- The term “mgraph” is used inconsistently (sometimes including V* nodes, sometimes not); authors should state when V* nodes are included.
- Paper forbids R variables as parents of V or U in mgraphs despite treating R as real quantities; this restriction needs justification or clarification.
- The term “neighbor” is undefined where used; provide a formal definition.
- Relation to Garcia et al.: the paper’s recoverability notion assumes positivity — clarify whether Garcia et al. also assume positivity or rely on a stronger condition.
- Statement that “Maximum Likelihood is consistent under MAR” is too vague; explain why ML cannot be straightforwardly applied to the broader missingness processes considered and specify limitations/assumptions.
- Minor corrections: several typos and phrasing issues (p.1 line 33; p.2 Figure 1 caption; p.2/p.3 Example 1 P(Y|X,Z,R_y) RHS should be P(Y|X,Z); p.3 line 168 change to R_y; p.3 line 214/215 notation fixes; p.4 Corollary 1 phrasing; p.6 Footnote 2 needs a reference for variable-level definition).
- Overall: interesting and technically sound extension of prior work, but applicability breadth is unclear and several clarifications/corrections are needed.
