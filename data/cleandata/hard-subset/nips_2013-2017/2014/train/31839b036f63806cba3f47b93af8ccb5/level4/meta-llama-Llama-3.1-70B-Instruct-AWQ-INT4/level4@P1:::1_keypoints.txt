- Addresses identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework, improving some existing results and giving useful examples.  
- Technical quality is high and results appear correct; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Some contributions are incremental and it’s unclear how they fit into a broader unifying picture.  
- Section 3 feels orthogonal to the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to understand and seems related to Richardson (2003) Markov blanket results.  
- Quality improves in later sections; Sections 5 and 6 offer clear, interesting, and potentially significant results about causal queries.  
- Main weakness is clarity: too much material for a conference paper and the text is dense with a long technical appendix.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., reuse of capital V, Vi a vertex vs Vo a set), making some expressions illegible.  
- Presentation issues: Corollary 1 and some set equations are poorly presented and hard to follow.  
- Paper would benefit from a higher-level, general criterion for identifiability rather than a sequence of incremental results.  
- Specific language and formal issues: R^part is a partition (not a set of partitions), and phrasing about Rx and Ry should reflect distinct partition elements.  
- Casual language and symbol choices (use of <--> instead of ↔, words like “oftentimes”) should be tightened.  
- Unclear whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply uses a different framework.  
- Some notation is used without definition (graphs after severing edges; Vo and Vm introduced before definition).  
- Overall: important and potentially significant work but marred by poor presentation and notation; revision could make it a strong contribution.
