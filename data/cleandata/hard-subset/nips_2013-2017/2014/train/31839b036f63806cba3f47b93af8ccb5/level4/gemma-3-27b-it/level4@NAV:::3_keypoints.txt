- Paper makes a valuable contribution: generalizes prior missingness work (e.g., Mohan et al.), introduces a novel identification formula, and shows causal queries can be identifiable even when the observational joint is not.

- Manuscript is generally well-written but needs clarifications and minor revisions to improve impact.

- Main concern: the results require knowledge of the censoring/missingness process; discuss practicality in typical ML settings and make Figure 1 more prominent.

- Clarify assumption of a known full population in Section 2 (framework excludes problems estimating population size when individuals are entirely unobserved).

- Explain inconsistent use of “mgraph” and presence/absence of V* across figures; if omissions are intentional, state why.

- Justify the restriction that R variables cannot be parents of V or U in mgraphs, especially given R variables are treated as continuous rather than simple indicators.

- Define the term “neighbor” where it first appears (bottom of page 4).

- On recoverability and “rectification” (Garcia et al.): clarify whether Garcia et al. rely on positivity like this paper or use a stronger condition.

- The claim that “Maximum Likelihood method is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR” is too broad; specify why ML cannot be applied directly to the missingness processes studied here.

- Minor wording: rephrase “can bias outcomes” (P.1 line 33).

- Figure caption: add “Qualifications” to Figure 1 caption (P.2).

- Fix equation in Example 1 (P.2/p.3 line 158): P(Y|X,Z,R_y)=P(X,Z) should be P(Y|X,Z).

- Correct independence notation (P.3 line 168): “Y || R | X,Z” should be “Y || R_y | X,Z”.

- Fix indexing typos (P.3 lines 214–215): R^{part} = {R^{(1)},...,R^{(N)}}, and “R^j respectively” → “R^{(j)}”.

- Corollary 1 should explicitly mention partially observed variables.

- Footnote 2 (P.6): provide a reference for the variable-level definition.
