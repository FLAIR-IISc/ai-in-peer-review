- Positive: Paper is interesting, technically sound, generalizes prior work with a novel identification formula and can identify some causal queries even when the full joint is not identifiable.

- Main (1) Practicality of assumptions: Authors must justify the assumption that the missingness graph is known and discuss realistic settings where such causal knowledge is available.

- Main (2) Scope of problem: Clarify assumption that every individual’s existence is known even if all variables are missing, and explicitly state exclusion of problems where population size is unknown (e.g., capture–recapture).

- Main (3) Ambiguity of "m-graph" definition: Resolve inconsistent use of m-graphs (whether V* surrogate variables are formally part of the m-graph) and state the definition consistently.

- Main (4) Structural restriction on R variables: Explain why R variables are not allowed to be parents of V or U — whether this is necessary for results or a simplifying assumption and its implications.

- Main (5) Undefined terminology: Define the term “neighbor” used on page 4 formally in the graph context.

- Main (6) Comparison to prior work: Clarify whether Garcia et al.’s “rectification” approach assumes positivity like the current recoverability definition, and discuss differences in required conditions.

- Main (7) Specificity on MLE: Explain precisely why standard MLE (consistent under MAR) fails for the broader MNAR processes considered and what specific problems the proposed method overcomes.
