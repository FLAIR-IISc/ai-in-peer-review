- Extends m-graph framework to derive necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries under non-ignorable missingness.  
- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) to handle missingness mechanisms that interact (longitudinal R-variable dependencies).  
- Main theoretical advance: causal queries can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic relations are not (Theorem 4).  
- Generalizes the sequential factorization theorem (Theorem 1) for interacting missingness processes.  
- Introduces formal non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3, Corollary 2) identifying when consistent estimation is impossible.  
- Distinguishes rigorously between probabilistic and causal recoverability throughout; proofs are rigorous.  
- Applies results to attrition, correcting misconceptions in prior work (e.g., Garcia 2013) with precise graphical conditions (Theorems 5–6).  
- Shows causal effects in attrition are recoverable only when Y and R_y are neither neighbors nor connected by an inducing path.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured with effective examples illustrating minimality and limitations.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: expand definition of R_part to clarify the collider path condition.  
- Original and significant: fills a gap in the literature by showing causal recoverability can bypass Y–R_y d-separation and providing first formal non-recoverability criteria.  
- Results have practical importance for longitudinal studies and attrition, enabling pre-data collection diagnosis of recoverability.  
- Primary weakness: lack of empirical validation or case study to illustrate practical impact.  
- Scope limitation: attrition results mainly treat the special case |V_m| = 1, limiting immediate generality.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — theoretical contributions, correctness, and clarity justify acceptance despite limited empirical work.
