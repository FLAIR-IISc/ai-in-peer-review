- Addresses identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms and builds on Mohan et al.’s framework.  
- Technically strong results that are complex but appear correct; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Some contributions seem incremental and don’t advance broader understanding of underlying principles.  
- Section 3 is disconnected from the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to follow and appears related to Richardson (2009).  
- Later sections improve: Section 5 is clear and insightful; Section 6 gives interesting results on causal queries.  
- Overall clarity is poor: material is dense, flow is unclear, and the paper feels more suited to a journal than a conference.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., multiple uses of capital V, illegible subscripts, V_i as vertex vs V_o as set).  
- Presentation issues include a poorly written set equation in Corollary 1 and excessive technical appendix length.  
- Sections 5–6 are potentially significant and useful, but a holistic identifiability criterion for the general case is lacking.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to use inverse probability weighting; effectiveness likely due to conditioning sets acting as Markov blankets.  
- The reviewer suggests relevant prior work by Richardson (2003, 2009) may clarify some results.  
- Specific editorial/notation fixes needed: correct description of R^part (p4 l214), use ↔ instead of <-->, and avoid informal language like “oftentimes.”  
- Clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply in a different framework.  
- Define the notation for graphs formed by severing edges (used in Definition 4 and Theorem 4).  
- Do not use V_o and V_m before defining them; several symbols are introduced prematurely.
