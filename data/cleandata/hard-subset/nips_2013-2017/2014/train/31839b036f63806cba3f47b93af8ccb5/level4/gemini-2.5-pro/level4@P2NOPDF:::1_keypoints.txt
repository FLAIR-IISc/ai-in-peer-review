- Addresses an important, challenging problem with high technical quality and seemingly correct results.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear, interesting, and potentially significant; Theorem 3 is interpretable and insightful.  
- Valuable contribution on identifiability of causal queries even when some conditional distributions are unavailable.  
- Overall impact is reduced because some results appear incremental relative to prior work.  
- Paper lacks a broader conceptual framework situating contributions into a general theory of identifiability.  
- Section 3 is disconnected from the main narrative and Theorem 1 is overly complex and hard to understand.  
- Clarity and dense writing are major weaknesses, making the argument difficult to follow.  
- The amount of technical material (13-page appendix) suggests suitability for a longer journal article.  
- Notation is overloaded and confusing (e.g., capital V used for multiple objects; Vi vs Vo distinction unclear).  
- Dense subscripts and complex set expressions (e.g., Corollary 1) hinder readability.  
- Recommend developing a unified, general identifiability criterion (analogous to do-calculus) for greater practical impact.  
- Suggest discussing connections to inverse probability weighting and Markov blankets, and citing relevant ADMG literature (e.g., Richardson 2003, 2009).  
- Minor fixes: clarify R^part description, use \leftrightarrow for bi-directed edges, define notation for severed-edge graphs, and avoid using Vo/Vm before definition.  
- Ask for clarification on the Garcia (2013) reference and recommend more formal language throughout (avoid informal terms like “oftentimes”).
