- Tackles identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms, building on Mohan et al., and shows causal distributions can be identified even without full conditional distributions.  
- Technical results are intricate and appear correct; theorems (e.g., Theorem 3) are clearly stated and interpretable.  
- Some contributions are incremental and lack a broader unifying perspective.  
- Section 3 feels disconnected; Theorem 1 is hard to grasp and resembles Markov blanket results from Richardson (2009).  
- Paper quality improves in later sections; Section 5 is clear and Section 6 gives interesting causal-query results.  
- Clarity is a major weakness: dense text, overly long technical appendix, and poor flow—better suited to a journal.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of capital “V”, illegible subscripts, Vi vs Vo ambiguity); Corollary 1’s presentation is especially poor.  
- The field would benefit from a holistic identifiability criterion rather than piecemeal results scattered across papers.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to follow from inverse probability weighting; conditioning sets likely act as Markov blankets in the authors’ mixed graphs.  
- Missing or incorrect wording and notation: p4 l214 partition phrasing, informal language like “oftentimes,” and use <--> instead of \leftrightarrow.  
- Unclear whether Garcia (2013) is wrong or simply uses a different framework—needs clarification.  
- Some graph/notation constructions (severing edges) are used before being defined; Vo and Vm are introduced before definition.  
- Overall: addresses an important problem with novel and useful results but is significantly hampered by presentation and notation issues.
