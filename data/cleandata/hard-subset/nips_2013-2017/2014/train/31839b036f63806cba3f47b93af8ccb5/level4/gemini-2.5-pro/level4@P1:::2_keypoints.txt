- Extends m-graphs (Mohan et al. 2013) to determine when probabilistic and causal queries are recoverable from data with missingness.  
- Provides general graphical conditions for recovering P(y|x) and P(y,x), and causal queries P(y|do(x)).  
- Demonstrates causal queries can be recoverable under weaker conditions than probabilistic queries.  
- Derives conditions allowing estimation of P(y|do(x)) even when Y and its missingness indicator Ry are not d-separable.  
- Introduces a generalized sequential factorization theorem (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes a recovery method for cases where standard factorization fails.  
- Presents novel graphical criteria sufficient for non-recoverability (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).  
- Applies theoretical tools to the common practical problem of attrition in experiments, correcting prior heuristics.  
- Paper is high-quality, technically sound, and includes proofs in the appendix.  
- Extensions beyond prior work are substantial and non-trivial.  
- Very clear and well-organized exposition with helpful examples.  
- Theorem 2’s conditions are technical; more intuition in the main text would aid understanding.  
- Lacks discussion of practical estimation issues (statistical efficiency, computational complexity) for the proposed estimators.  
- Section 7.2’s critique of prior work (Garcia 2013) should more clearly explain why the older condition was thought necessary and how the new condition improves on it.
