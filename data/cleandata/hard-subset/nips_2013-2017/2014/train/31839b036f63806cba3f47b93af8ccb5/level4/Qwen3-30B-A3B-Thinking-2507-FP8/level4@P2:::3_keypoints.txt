- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graph framework to provide more general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).
- Key theoretical advance: causal queries can be recoverable even when corresponding probabilistic relations are not.
- Introduces new graphical criteria for recoverability and algorithms to recover joint distributions when sequential factorization fails.
- Applies results to attrition in longitudinal studies, giving necessary and sufficient conditions.
- Technically sound with rigorous proofs and effective illustrative examples (e.g., Example 3).
- Successfully handles interacting missingness mechanisms (R variables influencing each other), filling a gap in prior work.
- Major limitation: assumes the missingness process is known, which may be unrealistic in many ML applications and needs more discussion.
- Clarity issues: inconsistent use of "m-graph" (sometimes includes V* variables, sometimes not) causing confusion.
- Missing definition of "neighbor" (p.4) and several minor typographical errors that reduce precision.
- Corrects prior misconceptions about attrition (e.g., Garcia 2013) by identifying collider paths as causes of non-recoverability.
- Results are highly original and represent a substantial theoretical advance for causal inference under missing data.
- Significant practical implications for researchers dealing with missing data in longitudinal studies.
- Strengths: rigorous theory, novel insight about causal vs. probabilistic recoverability, comprehensive treatment of recoverability, clear attrition application.
- Weaknesses: ambiguous m-graph notation, missing definitions, insufficient discussion of known-missingness assumption, minor errors, and an overly general statement about ML/MLE under MAR.
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to clarifying m-graph definition, discussing the missingness-assumption realism, and fixing minor errors.
