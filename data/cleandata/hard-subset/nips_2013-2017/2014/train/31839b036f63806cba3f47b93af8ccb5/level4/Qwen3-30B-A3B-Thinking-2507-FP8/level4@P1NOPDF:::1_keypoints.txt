- Technical results are strong and sophisticated, with Theorem 3 giving clear, interpretable insights.  
- Several contributions feel incremental and do not advance a broader conceptual framework.  
- Section 3 (Theorem 1) is disconnected from the core focus, hard to parse, and resembles Richardson (2009) on Markov blankets.  
- Sections 5–6 are the paper’s strongest parts, delivering crisp, compelling results on causal queries.  
- Main weakness is clarity: overly dense presentation and a 13-page appendix suggest journal rather than conference scope.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (overuse of capital V, illegible subscripts, Vi vs Vo ambiguity), harming readability.  
- Corollary 1 and other expressions suffer from poor presentation and should be reformulated for clarity.  
- Paper lacks a unifying identifiability criterion; a synthesis analogous to completeness results for causal effects is needed.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to rely on inverse probability weighting/Markov-blanket-like conditioning; relevant prior work (Richardson 2003, 2009) should be acknowledged.  
- Several formal issues and missing definitions: R^part wording, undefined “severing edges” graph notation, Vo and Vm used before definition.  
- Minor fixes: use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->, clarify Garcia (2013) citation framing, and tighten imprecise language (e.g., “oftentimes” → “often”).  
- Overall: technically sound and valuable, but impact is undermined by poor notation, excessive density, and lack of conceptual synthesis; a journal-length revision with clearer notation and organization is recommended.
