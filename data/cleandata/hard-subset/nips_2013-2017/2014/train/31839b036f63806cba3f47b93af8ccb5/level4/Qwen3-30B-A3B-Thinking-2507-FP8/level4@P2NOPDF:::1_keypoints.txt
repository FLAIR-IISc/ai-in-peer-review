- Addresses identifiability under Mohan et al.’s missing-data framework with technically sophisticated, largely correct analysis.  
- Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable; Sections 5–6 deliver the most compelling, well-motivated results on causal identifiability.  
- Several contributions feel incremental and fail to coalesce into a unified theoretical understanding.  
- Section 3 (Theorem 1) is poorly integrated with the paper’s main narrative and suffers from weak exposition.  
- The paper is overly dense (13-page appendix), reads more like a journal submission, and lacks conference-appropriate concision.  
- Logical flow is poor, making the main ideas difficult to follow and the results fragmented.  
- Notation is inconsistent and confusing (e.g., overuse of “V”, illegible subscripts, undefined/use-before-definition symbols like Vo and Vm).  
- Presentation errors (e.g., Corollary 1’s set equation) and undefined graph-severing notation undermine clarity.  
- The paper would benefit greatly from a consistent, reader-friendly notation system and tighter organization.  
- Sections 5–6 contain genuinely interesting, potentially significant advances for causal identifiability.  
- The work lacks a unifying identifiability criterion analogous to completeness results in causal-effect estimation.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to rely on inverse-probability-weighting/Markov-blanket ideas connected to Richardson’s prior work.  
- Specific minor fixes: correct R^part to R^π and rephrase partition condition; use ↔ instead of `<-->`; clarify Garcia (2013)’s relevance; avoid casual language.  
- Overall: technically strong but hampered by presentation; with substantial revision (notation, structure, scope) it could be a significant contribution, otherwise it risks being overlooked.
