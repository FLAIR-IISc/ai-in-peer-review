- Paper tackles identifiability in missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework, providing improved results and illustrative examples; technical results appear correct and complex.  
- Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable; Sections 5 and 6 contain the strongest, clearest contributions (notably causal query analysis).  
- Some contributions are incremental and do not advance a broader conceptual understanding; Section 3 feels orthogonal to the rest.  
- Theorem 1 is particularly difficult to understand and closely related to Richardson (2009) Markov-blanket results.  
- Primary weakness is clarity: overly dense presentation, 13-page technical appendix, and more suitable for a journal than a conference.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., reuse of capital V for different objects, illegible subscripts); presentation errors such as a poorly formatted set equation in Corollary 1.  
- The paper would benefit from developing a unified identifiability criterion for the general case (analogous to non-missingness causal-effect literature).  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse-probability-weighting and likely depend on conditioning sets being Markov blankets, linking to Richardson (2003, 2009).  
- Specific corrections: p4 l214 — R^part is a partition; state R_x and R_y belong to distinct partition elements.  
- Specific corrections: p4 l213 — use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->.  
- Clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply uses a different framework.  
- Definition 4 and Theorem 4 use a “sever edges” graph notation that is not defined.  
- Vo and Vm are used before being defined.  
- Overall: important problem with useful contributions, but impact diminished by poor presentation and notation.
