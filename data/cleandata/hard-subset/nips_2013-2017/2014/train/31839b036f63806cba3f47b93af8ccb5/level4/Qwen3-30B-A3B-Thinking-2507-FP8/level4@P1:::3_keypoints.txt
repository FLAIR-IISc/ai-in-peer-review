- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to allow interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Provides novel graphical conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions.  
- Shows causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations (e.g., P(y,x)) are not.  
- Applies framework to attrition problems in longitudinal studies.  
- Technically sound, correctly builds on prior work with theorems, examples, and proofs.  
- Highlights a novel and important distinction between recoverability of probabilistic versus causal queries.  
- Demonstrates recoverability of causal effects despite lack of d-separation between Y and its missingness indicator Ry (e.g., Fig. 3).  
- Assumes knowledge of the missingness process; needs more discussion on when this is realistic and how to handle unknown processes.  
- Restricts R variables from being parents of V or U variables without sufficient justification, limiting applicability.  
- Uses the term "m-graph" inconsistently (sometimes includes V* variables, sometimes not); needs clarification.  
- Uses the term "neighbor" without definition; add a brief definition for clarity.  
- Assumes positivity (P(Vo, Vm, R=0) > 0) whereas Garcia (2013) does not; reconcile this discrepancy.  
- Statement that "Maximum Likelihood method is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR" is too vague—clarify why ML cannot be applied to the paper's missingness processes.  
- Minor typos and corrections needed (three formula/text errors and wording in Corollary 1).  
- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity and justify technical assumptions.
