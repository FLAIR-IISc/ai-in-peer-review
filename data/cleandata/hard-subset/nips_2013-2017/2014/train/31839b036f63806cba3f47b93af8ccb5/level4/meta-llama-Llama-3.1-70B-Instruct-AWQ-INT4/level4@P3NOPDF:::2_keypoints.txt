- Proposes novel necessary and sufficient conditions for inferrability of causal queries with missing data.  
- Extends prior work that explicitly models the missing-data generating mechanism in causal diagrams.  
- Technical contributions are sound and supported by proofs and examples.  
- Use of causal diagrams to model missingness is a key strength, offering an intuitive framework.  
- Clear, concise, and accessible writing that makes complex material easy to follow.  
- Logical organization with sections that build coherently on each other.  
- Well-motivated by real-world problems and illustrated with practical examples.  
- Overall a valuable contribution to the state of the art and a worthwhile read.
