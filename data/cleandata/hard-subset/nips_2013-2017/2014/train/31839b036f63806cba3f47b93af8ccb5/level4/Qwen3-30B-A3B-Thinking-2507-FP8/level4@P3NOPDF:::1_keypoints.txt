- Addresses identifiability in missing data using Mohan et al.’s framework, providing incremental improvements and illustrative examples (e.g., causal distribution identifiability without conditional distributions).  
- Technical quality is high and proofs appear correct; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Overall contributions feel incremental rather than transformative; Section 3 (Theorem 1) is complex, disconnected, and hard to follow.  
- The paper does not leverage or connect sufficiently to Richardson’s Markov blanket/factorization results, missing a unifying theoretical perspective.  
- Sections 5 and 6 provide the most significant, potentially impactful results on causal queries under missingness.  
- The work stops short of delivering a general identifiability criterion and risks becoming a reference of specific cases rather than a guiding framework.  
- Clarity is a major weakness: overly dense content, a long technical appendix, and presentation better suited to a journal than a conference.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., repeated use of capital V for different concepts, inconsistent subscripts, late definitions of Vo and Vm), which hinders comprehension.  
- Specific fixes needed: correct R^part wording, use \leftrightarrow, clarify relation to Garcia (2013), define “severing edges” in Definition/Theorem 4, and avoid casual phrasing.  
- Recommendation: weak accept only if authors substantially revise presentation, resolve notation issues, and articulate a cohesive theoretical vision tying Sections 3, 5, and 6 together.
