- Novel contribution: extends Mohan et al. with a new identification formula showing causal queries can be identified even when the full observational joint is not identifiable; technically sound.  
- Practical relevance: assumes knowledge of the censoring process—paper must clarify feasibility of obtaining this knowledge (e.g., domain expertise, auxiliary data) and move key Fig.1 details into the main text.  
- Population assumption: implicitly assumes the full population is known; must state limitation for settings with unknown population size (e.g., wildlife counts).  
- m-graph ambiguity: inconsistent inclusion of V* variables across figures (Fig.1 vs Fig.2); clarify notation and when V* are omitted.  
- Restriction on R variables: prohibits R variables from being parents of V or U despite treating R as real-valued—justify whether this is theoretical necessity or convenience.  
- Undefined term: the term “neighbor” is used without definition and should be defined in the context of m-graphs.  
- Positivity assumption: recoverability definition assumes positivity; clarify whether Garcia et al.’s rectification framework also assumes positivity or imposes stronger conditions.  
- MLE claim: statement that “MLE is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR” is misleading—explain precisely why MLE cannot be applied to the proposed missingness (e.g., MNAR) mechanisms.  
- Minor errors/notation fixes: multiple typos and notation issues to correct (p.1 l.33; Fig.1 caption; p.3 Example 1 and lines 158/168/214/215; p.4 Corollary 1 wording; p.6 Footnote 2 reference).  
- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical advance for non-MAR identification in causal inference, but practical utility and clarity depend on addressing the above conceptual, notational, and applicability concerns.
