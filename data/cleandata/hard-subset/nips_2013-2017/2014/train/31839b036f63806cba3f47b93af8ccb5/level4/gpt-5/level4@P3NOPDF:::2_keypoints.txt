- Summary: Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for identifiability with missing data by augmenting causal graphs to represent missingness, unifying MCAR/MAR/MNAR; paper is focused and clear.
- Theoretical significance: Delivers a complete characterization (necessary and sufficient) of identifiability for the considered class of problems.
- Conceptual clarity: Explicit modeling of data-generating and missingness mechanisms gives clear intuition about recoverability, especially under MNAR.
- Practical relevance: Results can guide whether causal questions are answerable, what extra measurements are needed, and when imputation/weighting is futile.
- Presentation: Exposition is clear, well-motivated, and stays focused on its main message.
- Dependence on the causal graph: Conditions assume a correctly specified causal/missingness graph; sensitivity to misspecification and partial-knowledge scenarios is not discussed.
- Scope of queries: Unclear whether results cover interventional effects and counterfactuals or are limited to associational queries; scope should be clarified.
- Constructiveness and computation: Lacks an explicit constructive procedure/algorithm for deriving estimands when conditions hold and systematic certification when they fail, plus complexity discussion.
- Empirical illustration: Needs more substantive examples or simulations to demonstrate gains over MAR-based methods and practical relevance.
- Relation to prior work: Should crisply contrast with earlier graphical approaches, showing cases where prior sufficient conditions fail but the new conditions succeed and where they agree.
- Guidance for practice: Practitioners need suggestions for minimal assumptions, diagnostics, or sensitivity-analysis templates when the missingness mechanism is hard to elicit.
- Suggestion—worked example: Add a step-by-step applied example that builds the augmented graph and derives an estimand.
- Suggestion—robustness: Discuss partial-identification bounds and sensitivity-analysis approaches for ambiguous missingness edges.
- Suggestion—notation: Clarify and consistently use notation/definitions (e.g., missingness indicators, proxies) early on.
- Suggestion—extensions: Comment on extensions to longitudinal/time-varying settings and interactions with selection bias or measurement error.
- Suggestion—software: Provide or reference software or a prototype implementing the graphical checks.
- Overall assessment: Meaningful, well-written theoretical contribution that extends prior work; reviewer leans toward acceptance but recommends addressing scope, comparisons, and illustrative/algorithmic additions.
