- Main contribution: studies identifiability under multiple generative missingness mechanisms, building on Mohan et al., sharpening known results and giving useful illustrative cases (e.g., identifiability of causal distributions despite missing conditionals).  
- Technical quality: arguments are intricate but appear sound; theorem statements (e.g., Theorem 3) are well posed and interpretable.  
- Incrementality: some results feel like incremental extensions of prior work and do not yet form a cohesive broader understanding.  
- Section 3/Theorem 1: Section 3 seems tangential, Theorem 1 is hard to parse and appears related to Markov blanket ideas from Richardson (2009).  
- Best parts: Sections 5 and 6 provide clear takeaways and interesting, potentially impactful findings for causal queries.  
- Clarity issues: principal weakness is presentation — too much material for a conference, dense prose, weak narrative flow, and an overly long technical appendix.  
- Notation problems: overloaded use of capital “V”, confusing Vi vs Vo, nearly unreadable subscripts, and especially problematic set expression in Corollary 1.  
- Recommendation on scope: should aim for a complete identifiability criterion in this framework rather than expect readers to extract specific results across multiple papers.  
- Methodological note: Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting because conditioning sets act like Markov blankets for R(i); Richardson (2003, 2009) are relevant.  
- Specific corrections: R^part should be a partition (Rx and Ry in distinct blocks), use \leftrightarrow not <-->, avoid informal language (e.g., “oftentimes”), clarify Garcia (2013) relation, define notation for severing edges, and introduce Vo/Vm before use.  
- Overall assessment: important and promising work but undermined by weak presentation and notation.
