- Paper studies identifiability under specified missingness mechanisms, extending the m‑graph framework to recover P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).  
- Key contributions: generalized sequential factorization theorem, new non‑recoverability criteria, and examples where causal queries are recoverable though probabilistic ones are not.  
- Applied results to attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Technical quality is high; proofs appear rigorous and theorems sound.  
- Theorem 3’s non‑recoverability criteria are clear, interpretable, and practically useful.  
- Section 6’s result that causal queries can be recoverable despite inseparable variables/missingness is insightful and potentially surprising.  
- Some contributions feel incremental; Section 3 (sequential factorization) is complex, disconnected, and difficult to parse.  
- Clarity is a major weakness: dense presentation, many theorems, and a lengthy appendix reduce accessibility.  
- Notation is confusing and overloaded (e.g., ‘V’ used for multiple meanings; Vi vs Vo ambiguity).  
- Subscripts/superscripts are dense and some notation (graph surgery Gx/Gx) is used without definition.  
- Paper is significant and original in parts (Sections 5–6), but stops short of a unifying or complete recoverability theory or algorithm.  
- Theorem 2/Corollary 1 resemble inverse‑probability weighting; authors should discuss connections to Markov blankets and mixed‑graph factorizations (e.g., Richardson 2003/2009).  
- Suggested improvements: overhaul notation, clarify relation to Garcia [2013], define graph surgery notation, fix minor wording/definition order, and correct LaTeX for bidirected edges.  
- Overall: important and valuable results hindered by poor presentation; a major revision focused on clarity (or a longer journal exposition) is recommended.
