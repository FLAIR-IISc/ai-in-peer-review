- Extends Mohan et al. (2013, 2014) to provide graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic (P(y|x), P(y,x)) and causal (P(y|do(x))) queries from missing data.  
- Focuses on cases where Y and its missingness mechanism Ry are not d-separable, allowing more general missingness interactions.  
- Key contributions: shows causal queries can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not.  
- Key contributions: establishes new graphical criteria for non-recoverability.  
- Key contributions: applies results to attrition problems in longitudinal studies.  
- Technical quality is high; theorems and proofs appear sound and extend prior work meaningfully.  
- Theorems 3–6 are clear and interpretable, offering useful insights into recoverability and graph structure.  
- Some results (notably Theorem 1) seem incremental and are presented in a complex, hard-to-follow way.  
- Presentation and notation are inconsistent and confusing (e.g., reuse of V, ambiguous Vi/Vo), hurting clarity.  
- Section 3 is dense and appears disconnected from the rest of the paper; Corollary 1’s set equation is particularly problematic.  
- Paper is long and dense (≈13 pages plus lengthy appendix), suggesting it may be better suited for a journal.  
- Sections 5–6 contain genuinely novel results, but the work could be better situated within the broader theoretical landscape.  
- The problem addressed is significant for empirical researchers dealing with missing data; the attrition application is practically relevant.  
- Lack of a unified identifiability framework limits immediate practical utility and overall impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: important, technically sound theoretical contributions with relevant applications.  
- Cons for rejection: poor clarity/notation, overly dense presentation, some incremental results, and limited immediate usability.  
- Recommended improvements: substantially improve notation and organization, simplify presentation, and provide a more unified identifiability perspective.
