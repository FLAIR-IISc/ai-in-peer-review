- Problem and approach: Extends m-graph framework to recover probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing values.  
- Main contributions: (1) generalized sequential factorization for dependent missingness mechanisms.  
- Main contributions: (2) criteria to recover joint distributions when factorization fails.  
- Main contributions: (3) new graphical criteria to establish non-recoverability.  
- Main contributions: (4) shows causal effects can be recoverable even when corresponding joint/conditional distributions are not.  
- Quality: High technical quality with detailed proofs; Sections 5 and 6 are particularly strong.  
- Notable result: Theorem 3 offers a simple graphical check to rule out recoverability.  
- Concern: Some results feel incremental; Theorem 1 is a complex generalization whose role is unclear.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense, theorem-heavy, and reads more like a journal article than a focused conference paper.  
- Notation problems: Overloading of V, complex subscripts, and undefined symbols (e.g., G_X, G^X) reduce readability.  
- Originality: Work is original—non-recoverability criteria and decoupling of causal/probabilistic recoverability are novel.  
- Significance: Important practical and theoretical contributions, but impact limited by fragmented “cookbook” presentation and lack of a unified algorithm.  
- Suggested revisions: Focus the narrative on Sections 5 and 6 and move incremental results (e.g., Theorem 1) to the appendix.  
- Suggested revisions: Revise notation, define graphical symbols clearly, and clarify connections to inverse-probability weighting and Markov blankets.  
- Minor corrections: Fix bi-directed edge symbol, clarify R_part definition, clarify Garcia [2013] relation, and define Vo/Vm earlier.  
- Conclusion: Technically strong with important findings, but needs substantial revision for clarity to maximize impact.
