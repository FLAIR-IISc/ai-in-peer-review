- Technical quality is high; results are intricate and appear correct, though some are incremental.  
- Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable, but Theorem 1 (Section 3) is difficult to understand and feels disconnected.  
- Sections 5 and 6 contain clear, novel, and potentially significant results on identifiability and causal queries.  
- The paper lacks coherent flow and is overly dense for a conference paper, with a 13-page appendix indicating too much material.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (e.g., overloading of symbol V, illegible complex subscripts, V_i vs V_o inconsistency).  
- Presentation problems (dense text, unclear relative importance of contributions) obscure the paper’s value.  
- The work would benefit more from a general, complete identifiability criterion rather than a series of specific results.  
- There are likely connections to inverse probability weighting and Markov-blanket arguments; prior work by Richardson (2003, 2009) is relevant.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 may derive validity from conditioning sets acting as Markov blankets for missingness indicators.  
- Specific technical/notation fixes needed: define R^part as a partition, clarify Garcia (2013) relation, and define graph-severing notation used in Definition 4/Theorem 4.  
- Minor issues: informal language (e.g., “oftentimes”) and incorrect LaTeX for bidirected edge (`\leftrightarrow` vs `<-->`).  
- Variables V_o and V_m are referenced before being defined in the text.  
- Overall: important and technically sound contribution but marred by poor presentation and unclear notation.
