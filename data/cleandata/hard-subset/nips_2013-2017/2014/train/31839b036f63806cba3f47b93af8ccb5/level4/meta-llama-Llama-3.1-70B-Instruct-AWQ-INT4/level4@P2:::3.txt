This paper addresses the problem of deciding whether a causal or probabilistic query is estimable from data corrupted by missing entries, given a model of the missingness process. The authors extend the results of Mohan et al. [2013] by presenting more general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries of the form P(y|x) and P(y, x) as well as causal queries of the form P(y|do(x)). They also derive graphical conditions for recovering causal effects of the form P(y|do(x)) when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed solution. The technical details are well-explained, and the examples are helpful in illustrating the concepts.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to handle a more general class of missingness processes than previous work. The authors provide a novel identification formula that can be used to recover causal queries even when the observational joint distribution is not identified.

The paper is technically sound, and the results are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and clearly explain how their approach differs from previous methods.

However, there are some areas for improvement. The paper assumes knowledge of the process by which observations are censored, which may not be available in typical machine learning applications. The authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and provide guidance on how to apply their method in practice.

Additionally, some of the notation and terminology used in the paper could be clarified. For example, the term "m-graph" is used ambiguously, and the authors should explain why they restrict the R variables from being parents of variables in V or U.

Overall, this is a well-written and technically sound paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of graphical models for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.
