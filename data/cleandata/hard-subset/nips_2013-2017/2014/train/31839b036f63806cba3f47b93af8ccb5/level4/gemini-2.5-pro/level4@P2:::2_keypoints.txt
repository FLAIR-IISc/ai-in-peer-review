- Tackles recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries with missing data using m-graphs.  
- Extends m-graph framework to more general conditions for recovering P(y,x) and P(y|x).  
- Major contribution: shows causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when joint/conditional distributions are not.  
- Demonstrates recoverability can hold when a variable is not separable from its own missingness mechanism.  
- Introduces novel graphical criteria for both recoverability and non-recoverability (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).  
- Generalizes sequential factorization to handle dependencies among missingness mechanisms (Theorem 1).  
- Develops results for cases with no admissible sequence, broadening m-graph applicability (Theorem 2).  
- Technical work is rigorous with proofs in the appendix and helpful illustrative examples.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized, guiding reader from probabilistic to causal results and attrition.  
- Applies theory to attrition in longitudinal studies and provides targeted theorems (Theorems 5 and 6).  
- Weakness: entirely theoretical—lacks empirical/simulation validation to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 is dense; needs more intuition and a figure to illustrate R-variable partitioning and Markov blankets.  
- Weakness: connection to dormant independence and d*-separation needs a brief intuitive explanation for non-specialists.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and practically relevant theoretical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
