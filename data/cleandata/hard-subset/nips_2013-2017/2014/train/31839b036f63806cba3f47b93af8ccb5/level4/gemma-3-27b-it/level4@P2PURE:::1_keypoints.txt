- Paper studies identifiability under missing data, building on Mohan et al., and shows identifiability of causal distributions even without complete conditionals.  
- Technical quality is high; results are complex but appear correct, with Theorem 3 clearly stated and interpretable.  
- Some results are incremental and do not substantially advance broader theoretical understanding.  
- Section 3 is disconnected from the rest, and Theorem 1 is difficult to follow; it seems related to Markov blanket results in Richardson (2009).  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear, valuable, and present interesting results on causal queries.  
- Main weakness is clarity: overly ambitious scope for a conference, dense presentation, and an extensive 13-page technical appendix; better suited to a journal.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of ‘V’, illegible subscripts, Vi as vertex vs Vo as set), harming readability; Corollary 1 exemplifies poor presentation.  
- A more holistic, general criterion for identifiability in this framework is needed to make results practically usable.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to rely on inverse probability weighting, with conditioning sets acting as Markov blankets for Ri in the authors’ mixed graphs.  
- Specific technical/notation fixes needed: clarify R^part as a partition, rephrase the partition condition, use ↔ instead of <-->, clarify Garcia (2013)’s relation, define edge-severing graph notation, and define Vo and Vm before use (or remove).  
- Relevant prior work to cite/consider: Richardson (2003) and Richardson (2009).
