- Addresses causal inference with missing data by deriving graphical criteria for identifiability.  
- Core contribution: necessary and sufficient conditions for computing causal effects from incomplete data in a model that includes the missingness mechanism.  
- Significant theoretical advance: provides a complete characterization of identifiability beyond prior sufficient-only results.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation that situates the work within existing literature and makes technical formalisms accessible.  
- Elegant methodological framework: augments causal diagrams to explicitly model missingness as part of the causal system.  
- Needs a practical, running example or case study to illustrate step-by-step application of the graphical criteria.  
- Should discuss sensitivity to causal graph and missingness-model misspecification, and robustness to uncertainty in graph structure.  
- Would benefit from discussion of algorithmic/ computational aspects for checking the criteria and their complexity.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, theoretically sound contribution of broad interest; recommended for acceptance.
