- Requires knowledge of the censoring process; question whether that is typically available in ML applications and request more detail (and smaller rendering) of Figure 1.  
- Assumes the full population is known even when no variables are observed for an individual, which excludes settings like animal population counts.  
- "m-graph" is used ambiguously—Figure 1 includes V* variables while Figure 2 does not; omission of V* should be explicitly stated.  
- Paper forbids R-variables as parents of V or U despite treating R as real quantities; authors should justify or clarify this restriction.  
- The term "neighbor" (bottom of p.4) is undefined and needs definition.  
- Recoverability definition assumes positivity; ask whether Garcia et al. also assume positivity, otherwise their rectification approach may require stronger conditions.  
- Statement that "Maximum Likelihood method is known to yield consistent estimates under MAR" is overly vague; clarify why ML cannot be applied to the more general missingness processes considered.
