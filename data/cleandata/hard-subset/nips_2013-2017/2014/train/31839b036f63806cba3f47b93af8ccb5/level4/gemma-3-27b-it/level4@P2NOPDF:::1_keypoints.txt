- Tackles identifiability with missing data, extending Mohan et al.’s framework and showing identifiability even without complete conditional distributions.
- Technical quality is high and complex results appear correct; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.
- Some contributions feel incremental and lack a cohesive narrative or broader theoretical advance.
- Section 3 is disconnected from the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to grasp and resembles Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.
- Sections 5 and 6 are the paper’s strongest, offering clear, valuable answers and interesting causal-query findings.
- Paper suffers from poor clarity and excessive scope for a conference; dense text and a long appendix suggest journal suitability.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of “V”, illegible subscripts, Vi vs Vo confusion); Corollary 1 exemplifies unclear presentation.
- Lacks an overarching identifiability criterion analogous to non-missingness causal-effect identification, limiting practical usability.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to use inverse-probability weighting; success likely due to conditioning sets acting as Markov blankets in mixed graphs.
- Suggests connections to Richardson (2003, 2009) and potential clarification of Garcia (2013)’s relation or correctness.
- Specific corrections: fix wording about R^part being a partition, clarify Rx and Ry membership, avoid casual language, and correct the odd binary-data notation.
- Notation/definitions missing or out of order: graphs by edge severing not defined; Vo and Vm introduced before definition.
- Overall: important and potentially valuable work, but presentation, notation, and a unifying perspective need substantial improvement.
