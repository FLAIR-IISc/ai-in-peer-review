- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from datasets with missing entries using and extending the m-graph framework.  
- Provides more general graphical conditions for recoverability of P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).  
- Key contribution: shows causal queries can be recoverable even when joint/conditional distributions are not, illustrated with examples and applied to attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Technical quality is high; theoretical results appear sound and involved.  
- Theorem 3’s non-recoverability criteria are clear, interpretable, and valuable as necessary conditions.  
- Sections 5–6 contain novel, interesting results on recoverability of causal queries despite non-recoverable probabilistic terms.  
- Some parts feel incremental; Section 3’s “General Ordered Factorization” is disconnected and dense, limiting practical applicability.  
- Clarity is weak: presentation is dense, hard to follow, and may be better as a longer journal article (13-page appendix signals overload).  
- Notation is confusing (e.g., symbol V used for multiple objects; Vi vs Vo unclear; subscripts hard to read), hindering understanding.  
- Corollary 1’s set equation and other derivations suffer from notational complexity.  
- Paper’s significance would increase with a unified, complete recoverability criterion analogous to causal identification completeness.  
- Should make connections to existing statistical concepts (e.g., inverse probability weighting) and cite relevant work such as Richardson [2003].  
- Suggested manuscript improvements: better integrate or streamline Section 3, clarify critique of Garcia [2013], formally define graph-manipulation notation (G_X, G^X), and reorder definitions (Vo, Vm).  
- Additional fixes: clarify R(part) as a partition of R and distinctness condition for Rx and Ry, adopt a consistently formal tone, and use standard LaTeX commands for bi-directed edges.
