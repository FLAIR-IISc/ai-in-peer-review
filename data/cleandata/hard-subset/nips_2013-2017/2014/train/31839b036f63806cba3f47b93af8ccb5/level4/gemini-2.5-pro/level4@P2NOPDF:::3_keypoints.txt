1. Addresses recovering distributions when the data-generating process and missingness mechanism are known, generalizing prior work to broader missingness processes with a new identification formula.

2. Key contribution: shows certain causal queries are identifiable even when the full observational joint distribution is not recoverable.

3. Paper is technically sound and generally well written but needs stronger treatment of practical assumptions, definition clarity, and modeling justifications.

4. Practicality concern: the method assumes the censoring-process graphical model is known—authors should discuss where this is realistic and give concrete ML examples.

5. Scope issue: framework seems to assume known total population size (even for individuals with no observed variables), excluding problems like capture–recapture; this assumption and its implications should be stated in Section 2.

6. Model restriction question: authors disallow R variables as parents of V or U without clear justification—need to explain whether this is technical convenience or a substantive assumption.

7. Clarity problems: inconsistent use of “m-graph” (sometimes includes V*), missing formal definition of “neighbor,” and Figure 1 needs more exposition or resizing for space.

8. Comparison to prior work: clarify whether Garcia et al.’s rectification assumes positivity like the authors do, since differences affect the strength of identification conditions.

9. MLE claim too general: the statement that MLE is consistent under MAR should be qualified and the paper should explain why standard MLE fails for the MNAR settings considered.

10. Minor wording/typo fixes needed: p.1 line 33 ("can bias outcomes"), p.2 Figure 1 caption ("Qualifications" typo), and p.4 Corollary 1 grammar.

11. Specific equation and notation corrections: p.3 line 158 likely P(Y|X,Z) not P(X,Z); p.3 line 168 specify Y ⟂ R_y | X,Z; p.3 lines 214–215 fix R^{(N)} and R^{(j)} notation.

12. Minor reference request: p.6 Footnote 2 should cite the variable-level definition of MAR.
