- Proposes necessary and sufficient conditions for estimating causal queries from data with missing observations, extending Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Explicitly models the missingness process via missingness graphs (m-graphs).  
- Refines the sequential factorization theorem and provides algorithms for cases where it fails.  
- Presents graphical criteria to identify non-recoverability.  
- Shows P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when constituent factors or Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.  
- Applies results to attrition, characterizing recovery of causal effects under attrition.  
- Novel extension of recoverability conditions to causal queries.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous with clear definitions and logical derivations.  
- Paper is well-written and concepts are explained accessibly for the topic.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance through the attrition application.  
- Provides both sufficient conditions for recoverability and necessary conditions for non-recoverability.  
- Mathematical notation and theory are complex, posing a steep learning curve for non-experts.  
- Limited empirical evaluation; more simulations or experiments would strengthen the work.  
- Relies on correct specification of the m-graph; lacks discussion of sensitivity or robustness to misspecification.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers novel theoretical contributions, and is clearly presented.  
- Against acceptance: primarily theoretical with limited empirical validation and strong assumptions about the m-graph.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution recommended for acceptance, with suggestions for future empirical validation and robustness analyses.
