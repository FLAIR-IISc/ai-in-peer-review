- Presents novel necessary and sufficient conditions for identifying causal effects with missing data by integrating the missingness mechanism into the causal diagram.  
- Extends prior work by moving beyond treating missingness as an external process.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-written; makes complex theoretical results accessible.  
- Provides theoretical completeness by giving both necessary and sufficient conditions, improving on prior sufficient-only results.  
- Precisely characterizes when causal effects cannot be identified, offering valuable negative results.  
- Builds the framework on d-separation and graphical causal representations.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance given pervasive missing data and includes illustrative examples.  
- Would benefit from more discussion of practical challenges applying the conditions in complex real-world causal diagrams.  
- Recommends elaboration on using sensitivity analysis to assess robustness to model misspecification.  
- Suggests a more thorough comparison to MNAR and parametric missingness approaches to clarify differing assumptions and implications.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical contribution likely to influence future research.
