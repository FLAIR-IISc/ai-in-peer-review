- Introduces novel, comprehensive criteria for identifiability of causal effects with missing data.  
- Extends causal-diagram approaches to handle non-ignorable missingness.  
- Provides both necessary and sufficient conditions—advances beyond prior work that gave only necessary conditions.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and fills an important gap in causal inference.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, concise, and clearly presents complex results.  
- Builds logically on prior literature while offering valuable new insights.  
- Completeness of results is a key strength, offering definitive identifiability tests for practitioners.  
- Practical implications are under-discussed and would benefit from expansion.  
- Lacks illustrative examples, especially failure cases demonstrating when conditions do not hold.  
- Mathematical notation may be a barrier for non-experts; a glossary or intuitive explanations are recommended.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically relevant contribution; recommend acceptance.
