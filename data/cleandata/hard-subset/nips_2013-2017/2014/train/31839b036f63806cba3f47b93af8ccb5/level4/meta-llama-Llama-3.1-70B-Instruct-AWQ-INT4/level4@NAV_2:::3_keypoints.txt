- Presents a novel identification formula for recovering distributions from missing data.  
- Considers a more general class of missingness processes than prior work.  
- Shows causal queries can be identified even when the observational joint distribution is not identified.  
- Requires knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may not be available in typical ML applications.  
- Assumes the full population is known even when individuals have no observed variables, excluding problems like population size estimation.  
- Uses the term "m-graph" ambiguously (unclear whether V* variables are included); needs clarification.  
- Restriction that R variables be parents of V or U should be justified given R variables are treated as real quantities.  
- Minor terminology issues: define "neighbor" clearly.  
- Clarify the positivity assumption used in the definition of recoverability.  
- The claim about Maximum Likelihood consistency under MAR is too vague and should be expanded.  
- Paper is technically sound and well written but broad applicability is not fully clear.  
- Extends results of Mohan et al., representing a novel contribution to the field.  
- Quality: 8/10; Clarity: 8/10; Originality: 9/10; Significance: 8/10.
