- Results depend on knowing the censoring/missingness process; unclear if this is available in typical ML applications — add more detail about Figure 1.  
- The framework assumes the full population is known, excluding problems like population-size estimation (e.g., animal capture-recapture).  
- The term “mgraph” is used inconsistently (sometimes includes V* variables, sometimes not); clarify whether V* nodes are omitted.  
- Explain why mgraphs forbid R variables from being parents of V or U, especially since R variables are treated as real-valued rather than purely indicators.  
- Define the term “neighbor” (mentioned at bottom of p.4).  
- Clarify whether Garcia et al.’s “rectification” assumes positivity, since positivity is required for recoverability here.  
- The claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; explain why ML cannot be applied to the missingness processes considered here.
