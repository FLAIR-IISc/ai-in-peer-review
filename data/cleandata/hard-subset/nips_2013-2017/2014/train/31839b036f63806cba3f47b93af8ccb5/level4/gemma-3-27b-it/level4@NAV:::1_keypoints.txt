- Studies identifiability under missing-data mechanisms in the Mohan et al. framework and shows identifiability of causal distributions even when full conditionals are unavailable.  
- Technical quality is high; results are complex but appear correct and Theorem 3 is clearly stated and interpretable.  
- Some contributions are incremental and do not substantially advance broader theoretical understanding.  
- Section 3 is disconnected from the paper; Theorem 1 is hard to follow and resembles Markov blanket results in Richardson (2009).  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear, insightful, and contain interesting, potentially significant results about causal queries.  
- Main weakness is clarity: overly dense material, long (13-page) technical appendix, and lack of cohesive flow—perhaps better suited to a journal.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of “V” for different objects, illegible subscripts, Vi vs Vo inconsistency), harming readability.  
- Corollary 1 and some set equations are poorly presented and hard to parse.  
- Recommend a more holistic criterion for identifiability (analogous to work on causal-effect identification) to avoid fragmented, case-by-case results.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear to rely on inverse-probability-weighting; conditioning sets likely act as Markov blankets in the authors’ mixed graphs.  
- Prior work (Richardson 2003, 2009) is relevant and should be more clearly connected to several results.  
- Several technical fixes requested: correct wording for partition at p4 l214, use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->, avoid informal language, clarify Garcia (2013) relation.  
- Definition/Theorem issues: notation for graphs with severed edges is used without definition; Vo and Vm appear before being defined.  
- Overall: addresses an important problem with useful results but requires substantial improvement in presentation and notation.
