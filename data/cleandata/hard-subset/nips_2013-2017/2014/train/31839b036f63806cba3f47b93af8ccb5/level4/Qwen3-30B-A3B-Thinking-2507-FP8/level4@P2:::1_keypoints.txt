- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to give more general graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic (P(y|x), P(y,x)) and causal (P(y|do(x))) queries under missing data.  
- Demonstrates causal queries can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic relations are not, e.g., P(y|do(x)) recoverable when Y and Ry are not d-separable.  
- Provides necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for recovering causal effects in attrition/longitudinal problems (Theorem 6).  
- Technical quality is strong: rigorous theory and sound proofs; results like Theorem 3 are interpretable.  
- Key contributions are Sections 5–6, which give clear non-recoverability criteria and the surprising causal recoverability result (Example 3).  
- Some results are incremental (notably Theorem 1) and rely on complex notation, reducing accessibility.  
- Major weakness is clarity: dense writing, inconsistent and confusing notation (e.g., reuse of V, messy R subscripts), and poor organization—Section 3/Theorem 1 feels disconnected.  
- The paper would benefit from focusing on main contributions and moving technical details to the appendix to improve readability.  
- Originality: meaningful advance by showing causal recoverability beyond prior separability requirements and useful attrition results, but lacks a unified recoverability framework.  
- Should better connect to related work (e.g., Shpitser & Pearl 2006 on identifiability, Richardson’s Markov blanket work).  
- Practical significance is high for experimental sciences and longitudinal studies, though complex conditions may limit practitioner uptake.  
- Several minor editorial/notation fixes needed (define R^part properly, use ↔, avoid “oftentimes,” define severed-edge graph notation earlier).
