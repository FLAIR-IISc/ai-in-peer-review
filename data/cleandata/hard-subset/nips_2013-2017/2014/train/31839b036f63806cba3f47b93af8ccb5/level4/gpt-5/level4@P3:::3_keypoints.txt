- Paper studies recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries under MNAR using m-graphs, generalizing Mohan et al. (2013).
- Extends sequential factorization to allow interactions among missingness indicators (R variables).
- Provides new algorithms and constructive formulas for joint recovery when ordered factorizations fail.
- Introduces explicit graphical non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions.
- Shows causal effects can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not, using do-calculus and dormant independences.
- Applies results to attrition and gives necessary and sufficient conditions for joint distributions and causal effects under “simple attrition.”
- Advances principled, graph-based methods for MNAR beyond MAR/MCAR, IPW, and imputation literature.
- Assumes a correctly specified m-graph, which may be unrealistic in many ML applications.
- Assumes positivity (strict positivity over complete cases); extension beyond this is under-discussed.
- Notational and expository inconsistencies (e.g., inclusion of V*, undefined terms) reduce readability.
- Discussion of maximum likelihood under MAR is too brief and may mislead regarding limits under MNAR.
- Technical contributions appear sound with proofs; non-recoverability and causal-recoverability claims are convincing.
- Paper is generally well written but needs clearer definitions, consistent notation, and key examples in the main text.
- Originality is high: interacting R mechanisms, explicit non-recoverability, and causal recoverability results are novel.
- Significance is moderate-to-high but hinges on the ability to credibly specify m-graphs; guidance on elicitation would increase impact.
- Major requested clarification: explain how practitioners obtain and validate m-graphs, and robustness to partial misspecification.
- Major requested clarification: state explicitly whether the sampling frame (population units) is assumed known and contrast with capture–recapture settings.
- Major requested clarification: standardize m-graph depiction regarding inclusion/omission of proxy variables V* and map Equation (1) accordingly.
- Major requested clarification: justify the prohibition of edges from R to V or U and whether allowing R→V would affect results.
- Major requested clarification: define “neighbor” and ensure consistency with other graph-theoretic terms.
- Major requested clarification: clarify positivity assumptions and contrast explicitly with Garcia (2013) regarding their assumptions.
- Major requested clarification: temper the ML/MAR claim—state conditions for ML consistency and explain why standard ML/EM fails under the MNAR classes here.
- Minor issues: multiple typos and wording fixes throughout (examples and line-specific corrections provided).
- Arguments for acceptance: solid, novel theory for MNAR, clear positive/negative conditions, and important insight about causal vs probabilistic recoverability.
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on correctly specified m-graphs, expository issues, and limited empirical illustration.
- Overall: technically sound and interesting contribution that requires clarifications on assumptions, notation, and practical guidance before acceptance.
