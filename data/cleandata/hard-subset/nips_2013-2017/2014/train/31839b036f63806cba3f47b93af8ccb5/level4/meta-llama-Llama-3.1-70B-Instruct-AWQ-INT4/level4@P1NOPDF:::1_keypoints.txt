- Tackles identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework and gives useful examples (e.g., causal distributions identifiable without conditional distributions).  
- Technical quality is high; results are involved but appear correct, and Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Some results are incremental and do not significantly advance broader understanding.  
- Section 3 seems unrelated to the rest of the paper and its main result (Theorem 1) is difficult to comprehend.  
- Paper contains too much material for a conference paper—dense text and a 13-page technical appendix hinder readability.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of 'V', Vi vs Vo, illegible subscripts), making the paper hard to follow.  
- Presentation issues exemplified by a poorly written set equation in Corollary 1.  
- Recommend expanding into a journal article to improve exposition and flow.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear, interesting, and potentially significant, particularly for causal queries.  
- The paper should aim for a general identifiability criterion comparable to existing criteria for causal effects without missingness.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting; their validity likely ties to conditioning on Markov blankets in the mixed graphs.  
- Prior work by Richardson (2003, 2009) is relevant and should be connected more explicitly.  
- Specific correction: R^part is a partition of R; rephrase line 214 to state Rx and Ry “belong to distinct elements of the partition.”  
- Minor language/notation fixes needed (avoid casual phrasing like “oftentimes”; use \leftrightarrow instead of “<--->”).  
- Clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply works in a different framework.  
- Define the notation for graphs formed by severing edges (used in Definition 4 and Theorem 4).  
- Avoid using 'Vo' and 'Vm' before they are defined.  
- Overall: addresses an important problem with useful results but is marred by poor presentation and notation.
