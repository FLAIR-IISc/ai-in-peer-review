- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by giving more general graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries.  
- Introduces "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) to model the missingness process and shows causal queries can be recoverable even when component probabilistic queries are not.  
- Derives conditions for recovering causal effects when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable, with application to longitudinal attrition.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: demonstrates novel cases where causal recoverability exceeds probabilistic recoverability.  
- Results are technically sound and supported by rigorous proofs in the appendix.  
- Well-organized exposition that moves clearly from foundations to applications.  
- Originality: handles more general missingness processes including interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Practical significance: provides necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for simple attrition, correcting prior misunderstandings.  
- Ambiguity in m-graph definition and inconsistent inclusion/omission of V* variables across figures.  
- Some terms (e.g., "neighbor") are used without definition, hurting clarity.  
- Insufficient discussion of whether knowledge of the missingness process is available in real-world ML applications.  
- Assumes positivity (P(Vo, Vm, R=0) > 0) without discussing practical restrictiveness.  
- Vague claim about ML/maximum likelihood consistency under MAR needs clarification.  
- Unstated assumption that the full population is known (even when Vo is empty), excluding some problems (e.g., animal population estimation).
