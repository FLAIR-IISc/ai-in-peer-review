- Addresses identifiability with missing data within Mohan et al.'s framework, extending existing results and giving insightful examples (e.g., causal identifiability when conditionals are unavailable).
- Technical quality is high with seemingly correct, involved results; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.
- Some contributions are incremental and do not broaden understanding; Section 3 feels disconnected and Theorem 1 is hard to follow and related to Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.
- Main weakness is clarity: overly dense material, disrupted flow, and a 13-page technical appendix suggest the work suits a longer journal paper.
- Notation problems: reused capital V for different objects, illegible subscripts, confusion between Vi (vertex) and Vo (set), and a poorly presented set equation in Corollary 1.
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear, interesting, and potentially significant; the paper should aim toward a complete identifiability criterion for the framework.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 appear related to inverse probability weighting and Markov blankets; Richardson (2003, 2009) are relevant.
- Specific fixes: correct description of R^part as a partition, clarify Rx/Ry belonging to distinct partition elements, avoid casual language, use ↔ instead of <-->, define notation for severed-edge graphs, and define Vo/Vm before use.
- Unclear whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or uses a different framework.
- Overall: important problem and useful approach, but poor presentation and notation detract from the paper's quality.
