- Presents novel necessary and sufficient conditions for identifiability of causal queries in the presence of missing data by integrating the missingness mechanism into causal diagrams.  
- Formally models missingness as part of the causal graph, extending prior approaches that treated missingness as external.  
- Provides rigorous, graph-theoretic proofs that generalize and sharpen previous results, especially for non-ignorable missingness.  
- Key insight: identifiability depends on the interplay between causal structure and the mechanism generating missingness (including dependence on unobserved variables).  
- Offers a unified framework that subsumes earlier methods as special cases.  
- Focuses on identifiability (when a causal query can be answered) rather than estimation or imputation, giving practitioners a checklist before estimation.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear with well-motivated definitions, illustrative examples, and immediate interpretations of theorems.  
- Resolves ambiguities in the "missingness graph" literature and explicitly compares/contrasts with prior work (e.g., Hernán and Robins).  
- Minor suggestion: discuss computational implications and algorithmic complexity for verifying the proposed conditions.  
- Minor suggestion: include an empirical illustration on a real-world dataset to further ground the theory.  
- Writing is precise, non-overclaiming, well-organized, and the paper is a substantial theoretical advance with practical relevance.  
- Recommendation: accept; contribution is novel, well-executed, and likely to influence future work.
