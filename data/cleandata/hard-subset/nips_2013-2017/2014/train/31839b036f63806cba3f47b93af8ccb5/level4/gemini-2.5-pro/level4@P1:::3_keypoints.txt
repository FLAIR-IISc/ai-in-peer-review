- Paper tackles recovery of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using m-graphs; key contributions: generalized recoverability conditions, graphical non-recoverability criteria, and showing causal effects can be recoverable when observational joints are not, applied to longitudinal attrition.

- Technically sound and significant extensions to prior work; writing is generally clear but needs clarifications.

- Main concern: strong assumption that the missingness-graph is known a priori — the paper should discuss practical applicability and give examples where this is realistic.

- Scope clarification: the framework seems to assume known population membership (no missing records entirely); this exclusion (e.g., capture–recapture) should be stated explicitly in Section 2.

- Notational/visual consistency: Figure 1 shows proxy variables V* but later figures omit them — explicitly state if V* are omitted for clarity.

- Model restriction needing justification: R variables are not allowed to be parents of substantive variables V or U — authors should justify whether this reflects real problems or is for analytical convenience.

- Terminology: the term "neighbor" (used in Theorem 3 and elsewhere) is not formally defined and should be precisely defined.

- Comparison to prior work: the claim of "rectifying" Garcia [2013] must account for different assumptions (notably positivity P(V,R=0)>0); clarify whether Garcia made the same assumptions before declaring a rectification.

- Clarify maximum-likelihood statement: saying "Maximum Likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR" is too general — state that likelihood-based inference is consistent for parametric models under MAR and explain why standard ML fails for MNAR without structural m-graph assumptions.

- Minor errors and typos to fix: abstract wording ("the missingness process"), Figure 1 caption misspelling, Equation/independence typos in Example 1 and related lines, Rpart and R^j notation, wording in Corollary 1, and add a reference for variable-level d*-separation.
