- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Provides general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)); shows causal queries can be recoverable even when identifying factors are not.  
- Key theoretical contribution: Theorem 6 gives necessary and sufficient conditions for causal recovery under simple attrition and corrects Garcia (2013).  
- Results are technically rigorous with clear proofs and illustrative examples (e.g., Example 3 demonstrating causal recoverability despite inseparability).  
- Some results (e.g., Theorem 1) are incremental but represent important technical extensions to prior work.  
- Main weakness is poor clarity and organization with confusing, inconsistent notation (e.g., reuse of V, Vi vs Vo, illegible subscripts).  
- Presentation issues: Theorem 1 and admissible sequences are hard to parse, Section 3 appears disconnected, and the long appendix suggests some material may suit a journal.  
- Originality: novel insight that causal identifiability can hold when probabilistic factors are not recoverable; offers a deeper graphical framework beyond IPW-like approaches.  
- Significance: important practical implications for longitudinal studies with attrition and for empirical fields (medicine, social sciences, economics).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and significant but needs substantial revisions for clarity, notation, and accessibility to maximize impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to improve exposition, unify notation, and provide a more intuitive decision procedure or graphical criterion for practitioners.
