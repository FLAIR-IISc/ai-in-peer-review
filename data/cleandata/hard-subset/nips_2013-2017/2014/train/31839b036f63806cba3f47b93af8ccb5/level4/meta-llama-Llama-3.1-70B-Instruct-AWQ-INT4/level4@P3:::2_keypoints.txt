- Proposes novel necessary and sufficient conditions for when a causal query is recoverable from data with missing observations.
- Extends prior work by explicitly modeling the missing-data generating mechanism within the causal diagram.
- Introduces "Missingness Graphs" (m-graphs) as a new graphical representation to model missing data mechanisms.
- Uses d-separation in causal Bayesian networks to derive recoverability conditions.
- Provides a thorough literature review covering deletion methods, MLE, IPW, imputation, and graphical approaches.
- Includes several illustrative examples, notably addressing attrition in longitudinal studies.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written for readers familiar with causal Bayesian networks and graphical models.
- Main strength: provides a clear, concise framework (m-graphs + d-separation) that is practical and easy to apply.
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in causal Bayesian networks, making notation and concepts potentially hard for some readers.
- Suggestion: add a brief introduction to necessary concepts/notation and more diverse examples beyond attrition.
- Rating: 8/10; Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
