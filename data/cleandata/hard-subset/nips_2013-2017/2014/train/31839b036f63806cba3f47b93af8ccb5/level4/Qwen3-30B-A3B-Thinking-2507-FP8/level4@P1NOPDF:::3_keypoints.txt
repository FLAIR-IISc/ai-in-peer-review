- Results rely on knowing the censoring mechanism; authors should discuss how often this is available in practice and clarify Figure 1 (e.g., reduce its size).
- Section 2 assumes the full population is known (individual existence known even with no observed variables); this excludes population-estimation problems and should be acknowledged.
- The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently (includes V* in Fig.1 but omits in Fig.2); the paper must state a consistent convention for V*.
- mgraphs forbid R variables from being parents of V or U despite treating R as real-valued; the authors should justify or clarify this restriction.
- The term "neighbor" (bottom of p.4) is undefined and needs a definition.
- The reference to "rectification" by Garcia et al. (p.8) needs context; clarify whether their approach assumes positivity as the current recoverability definition does.
- The claim that "Maximum Likelihood ... yields consistent estimates under MAR" (p.8) is vague; specify why ML cannot be applied to the more general missingness processes studied here.
- p.1, line 33: change "can bias outcomes" to "can bias results".
- p.2, Figure 1: add label "Qualification:" to the caption.
- p.3, Example 1, line 158: correct P(Y | X,Z,R_y) to P(Y | X,Z).

- p.6, Footnote 2: clarify the "variable-level definition" and provide a citation.
- Overall: paper is technically sound but its applicability to broader problems remains unclear.
