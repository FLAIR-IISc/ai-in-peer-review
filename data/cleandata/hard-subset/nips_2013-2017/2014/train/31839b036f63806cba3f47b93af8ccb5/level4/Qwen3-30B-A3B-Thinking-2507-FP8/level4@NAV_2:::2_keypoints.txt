- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) to provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal and probabilistic queries from missing data.  
- Derives graphical conditions where causal effects are recoverable even if Y and R_y are not d-separable, while probabilistic queries may remain non-recoverable.  
- Introduces a refined sequential factorization theorem for interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Presents algorithms that enable recovery in cases lacking admissible sequences.  
- Establishes non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions.  
- Applies the theoretical results to attrition in longitudinal studies, giving necessary and sufficient conditions for causal recovery under simple attrition.  
- All theoretical claims are rigorously proven, with complete proofs in the appendix.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured, with precise definitions and illustrative examples.  
- Original contribution: shows causal queries can bypass separability requirements for probabilistic recovery, correcting prior work (e.g., Garcia 2013).  
- High significance and practical relevance for applications like clinical trials with attrition.  
- Pros highlighted: technical soundness, clarity, originality, and practical relevance.  
- Cons: none identified; reviewer recommends acceptance.
