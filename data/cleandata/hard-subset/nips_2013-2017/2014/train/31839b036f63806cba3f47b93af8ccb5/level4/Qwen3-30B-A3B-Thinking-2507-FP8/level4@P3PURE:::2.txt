

This paper establishes novel necessary and sufficient conditions for determining whether a causal query can be identified from data when missing observations are present. It extends prior work by formally incorporating the missing data generating mechanism into causal diagrams, thereby generalizing existing frameworks for causal inference under missingness. The paper is clearly written and makes a significant contribution by providing additional theoretical insights that advance the state of the art in causal analysis with missing data. The focused approach and rigorous presentation further enhance the paper's value to the field.
