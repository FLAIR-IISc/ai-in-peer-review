1. Paper requires knowledge of the censoring process — question whether this is available in ML applications and request to expand/clarify Figure 1 in the main text.  
2. Section 2 should state that missing-data processes assume the full population is known, excluding problems like estimating animal population sizes.  
3. The term "mgraph" is used ambiguously with respect to including V* variables; the authors must explicitly state when V* variables are omitted.  
4. The restriction forbidding R variables as parents of V or U needs justification, since R variables are treated as real quantities.  
5. The term "neighbor" (bottom of p.4) is undefined and needs a definition.  
6. Unclear whether Garcia et al.'s rectification result assumes positivity; the paper’s recoverability definition assumes positivity and this should be clarified.  
7. The claim that Maximum Likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is overly vague; explain why ML cannot be applied to the missingness processes considered here.  

Minor points / typos:  
- p.1 l.33: typo ("can bias outcomes" correction).  
- p.2 Figure 1: add "Qualifications".  
- p.3 Example 1 l.158: correct P(Y | X,Z,R_y) = P(X,Z) → P(Y | X,Z).  
- p.3 l.168: correct "Y || R | X,Z" → "Y || R_y | X,Z".  
- p.3 l.214–215: notation fixes for R^{(N)} and R^{(j)}.  
- p.4 Corollary 1: fix "partially observed variables" wording.  
- p.6 Footnote 2: add reference for variable-level definition.  

Overall: Interesting, technically sound paper building on prior work but applicability is unclear and several clarifications/typos are needed.
