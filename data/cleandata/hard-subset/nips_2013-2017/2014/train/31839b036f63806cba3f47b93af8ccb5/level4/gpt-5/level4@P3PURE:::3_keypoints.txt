1) Assumed knowledge of the censoring process: The results require the missingness/censoring mechanism to be known — request discussion of realistic scenarios where this holds and limits of the assumption, and move some Figure 1 detail into the main text.

2) Clarify population-level assumptions in Section 2: The setup assumes knowledge of the full population (that unobserved individuals are known to exist); this should be stated explicitly and contrasted with capture–recapture settings.

3) Ambiguity in the use of “mgraph”: The paper inconsistently includes or omits V* variables in mgraphs (e.g., Figures 1 vs 2); clarify whether omissions are for readability and fix a consistent convention.

4) Restrictions on parents of R variables: The model forbids R variables from being parents of V or U despite treating R as substantive; justify this restriction (identifiability vs convenience).

5) Undefined terminology: Define “neighbor” precisely when first used.

6) p.8, first paragraph: “rectification” by García et al.: Clarify whether García et al. assume positivity like the authors do, or require a stronger condition, and explain the relationship.

7) p.8, second paragraph: maximum likelihood under MAR: The claim that ML is consistent under MAR is too vague — specify when ML applies or fails relative to the generalized missingness processes studied.

8) Minor comments / typos: Multiple wording, notation, and grammatical errors need fixing (examples: p.1 line 33 phrasing, Fig.1 caption, Example 1 RHS P(Y|X,Z), Y || R_y notation, indexing/superscript errors, Corollary 1 grammar, Footnote 2 reference).

9) Overall strengths: Extends Mohan et al. to a broader class of missingness processes, provides a novel identification formula, and shows causal query identification even without observed joint identification.

10) Overall weaknesses/clarifications needed: Practical availability of the censoring mechanism, consistency of the mgraph formalism (V* treatment), justification for structural R restrictions, and clearer comparisons to prior work on positivity and ML under MAR.
