- Topic: Studies identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework, improving some prior results and giving interesting examples (e.g., causal identifiability without conditional distributions).  
- Technical quality: Results appear technically sound and involved; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Incrementality: Some contributions are incremental and do not advance broader understanding.  
- Section 3/Theorem 1: Section 3 seems unrelated to the rest, Theorem 1 is hard to follow and appears closely related to Richardson (2009) Markov blanket results.  
- Strong parts: Sections 5 and 6 are clear, interesting, and potentially significant for causal queries.  
- Clarity/length: Paper is dense with too much material and a large technical appendix; better suited as a longer journal paper.  
- Notation problems: Confusing reuse of capital V for different objects (Vi vs Vo), poor presentation of set equations (e.g., Corollary 1).  
- Suggestion for direction: Recommend aiming for a complete identifiability criterion in this framework, analogous to work on causal effects without missingness.  
- Relation to prior work: Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting and likely rely on Markov blanket conditioning; Richardson (2003, 2009) are relevant.  
- Specific corrections: R^part is a partition of R (not “a set containing partitions”); Rx and Ry should be said to “belong to distinct elements of the partition.”  
- Language/notation fixes: Avoid informal wording ('oftentimes'); use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->; clarify whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or uses a different framework.  
- Undefined notation: Notation for graphs with severed edges (Definition 4, Theorem 4) is not defined; Vo and Vm are used before being defined.  
- Overall: Addresses an important problem with useful results, but poor presentation and notation reduce overall impact.
