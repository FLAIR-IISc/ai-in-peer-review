- Addresses identifiability under missingness, building on Mohan et al., with technically involved results and useful examples.
- Strong technical quality overall; derivations are intricate and appear correct where understandable.
- Sections 5 and 6 are the paper's strongest, offering clear, original, and potentially impactful identifiability results for causal queries.
- Paper's impact is undermined by severe clarity, notation, and presentation problems that make it hard to follow.
- The manuscript is overly dense for a conference paper and may be better suited for a longer journal version (13-page appendix supports this).
- Some contributions feel incremental and lack a unifying theory; practitioners would benefit more from a general completeness criterion.
- Section 3 seems disconnected from the rest; Theorem 1 is exceptionally complex, hard to comprehend, and of limited practical utility.
- Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable, but Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting and need theoretical contextualization.
- The paper should explicitly connect results to Markov blanket/ mixed-graph literature (e.g., Richardson 2003, 2009) to clarify underlying reasons.
- Notation is a major obstacle: symbol 'V' is overloaded, subscripts are illegible, and distinctions like Vi vs Vo are confusing and error-prone.
- Specific presentation issues (e.g., the set equation in Corollary 1) hinder comprehension rather than aid it.
- Several undefined or ill-placed terms/notations (e.g., G_V' for severed-edge graphs, Vo and Vm used before definition).
- Minor issues: clarify meaning of R^part, state relationship to Garcia (2013), use proper LaTeX for bidirected edge, and adopt a more formal tone.
