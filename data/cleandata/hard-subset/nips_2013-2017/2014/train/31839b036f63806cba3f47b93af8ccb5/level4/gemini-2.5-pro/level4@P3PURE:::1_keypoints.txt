- Addresses identifiability under various missing-data mechanisms using Mohan et al.'s framework, with improvements and examples (e.g., causal identifiability without conditional distributions).
- Technical quality is high and results appear correct; some results (e.g., Theorem 3) are clear and interpretable.
- Several results seem incremental and do not substantially advance broader understanding.
- Section 3 is disconnected from the main narrative and Theorem 1 is particularly hard to comprehend, overlapping with Richardson (2009) Markov blanket ideas.
- Presentation improves in later parts: Section 5 gives clear answers and Section 6 yields interesting causal-query results.
- Main weakness is clarity: the paper covers too much for a conference, has dense text, and relies on a long technical appendix, leading to poor narrative flow.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (repeated use of 'V', illegible subscripts, Vi vs Vo ambiguity), which burdens the reader.
- The work would benefit from a general, complete criterion for identifiability analogous to results in missing-data-free causal identification.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse-probability-weighting and likely rely on conditioning on Markov blankets; Richardson (2003, 2009) may be relevant.
- Specific corrections: R^part should be a partition (distinct elements), avoid informal language, use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->, and clarify Garcia (2013)'s status.
- Notation for graphs obtained by severing edges (Definition 4, Theorem 4) is undefined and needs clarification.
- The variables Vo and Vm are introduced unclearly and perhaps unnecessarily before their definitions.
- Overall: important and potentially significant contributions but undermined by poor presentation and confusing notation.
