- Addresses recoverability of causal and probabilistic queries from data with missing entries, extending Mohan et al. (2013).
- Provides more general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and causal P(y|do(x)).
- Shows causal queries can be recoverable even when factors in their identifying estimands are not.
- Introduces a novel identification formula and a general framework for recovery.
- Paper is novel, technically sound, and generally well-written with a clear introduction and thorough related work.
- Includes several illustrative examples demonstrating the identification formula.
- Main limitation: assumes known missingness/censoring process, which may not be available in typical ML settings and needs discussion.
- Figures need more detail and explanation, particularly Figure 1.
- Minor errors/inconsistencies: ambiguous use of "m-graph" (unclear V* inclusion), undefined term "neighbor," and some notational issues.
- Overall strong contribution; recommendation: accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
