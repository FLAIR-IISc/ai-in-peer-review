- Addresses identifiability with various missing-data mechanisms using the Mohan et al. framework.  
- Builds on prior work and gives insightful examples, e.g., causal distributions identifiable when conditional distributions are not.  
- Technical quality is high: complex results appear correct and Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Some contributions are incremental and do not substantially broaden overall understanding.  
- Section 3 seems unrelated to the rest; Theorem 1 is hard to follow and tied to Markov blanket results in Richardson (2009).  
- Quality improves in later sections; Section 5 gives clear answers and Section 6 contains interesting causal-query results.  
- Major clarity issues: dense text, large appendix, and disrupted flow—paper may be more suitable for a journal.  
- Notation problems: repeated use of capital V for different objects, illegible subscripts, and confusing use of Vi (vertex) vs Vo (set).  
- Presentation errors exemplified by the set equation in Corollary 1.  
- Needs a more comprehensive general-case treatment and a complete identifiability criterion for practical usability.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 seem related to inverse probability weighting and Markov blankets; Richardson (2003, 2009) likely relevant.  
- Specific corrections: R^part is a partition (not “a set containing partitions”); revise the condition on p.4 l.214 accordingly.  
- Language/formalism issues: informal wording like “oftentimes” and use \leftrightarrow instead of <-->.  
- Unclear whether Garcia (2013) is incorrect or simply uses a different framework.  
- Notation for graphs after severing edges (Definition 4, Theorem 4) is not defined; Vo and Vm are introduced before definition.  
- Overall: tackles an important problem with a useful approach, but presentation and notation require significant improvement.
