- Presents novel necessary and sufficient conditions for identifiability of causal queries from observational data with missing values.  
- Extends prior work by explicitly modeling the missing data mechanism within causal diagrams.  
- Paper is clearly written, focused, and represents a valuable theoretical contribution to causal inference under missing data.  
- Main strength: rigorous derivation showing how including the missing-data mechanism yields a more complete identifiability assessment.  
- Clearly explains how results relate to and improve upon existing literature.  
- Lacks discussion of practical applicability: unclear how easily conditions can be checked on complex, real-world graphs.  
- Suggests adding examples, guidance, or automated tools to assist practitioners in applying the conditions.  
- Assumes the missing-data mechanism is known; needs discussion of robustness to misspecification or ways to handle uncertainty in that mechanism.  
- Minor: include a glossary of symbols for less-expert readers and shorten some long sentences for readability.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical work with recommended acceptance, pending addressing practical applicability and sensitivity issues.
