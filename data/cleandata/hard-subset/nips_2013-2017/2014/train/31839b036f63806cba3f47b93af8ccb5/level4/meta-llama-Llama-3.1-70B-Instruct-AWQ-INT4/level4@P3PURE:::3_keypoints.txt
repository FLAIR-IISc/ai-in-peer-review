1. Results depend on knowing the censoring/missingness process, which may not be available in typical ML applications; suggest moving more Figure 1 detail into the main text.  
2. Section 2 assumes the full population is known even when individuals have no observed variables, excluding problems like population size estimation; this limitation should be acknowledged.  
3. The term "mgraph" is used ambiguously (sometimes including V* variables, sometimes not); clarify whether V* nodes are omitted and why.  
4. The paper forbids R variables as parents of V or U despite treating R as real quantities; justify this restriction or state it is for convenience.  
5. The term "neighbor" is introduced without definition; provide a clear definition.  
6. The paper's recoverability assumes positivity; clarify whether Garcia et al.'s "rectification" also assumes positivity or a stronger condition.  
7. The claim that "Maximum Likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR" is vague; explain why ML cannot be applied to the missingness processes considered.

- Provide a reference for the variable-level definition in Footnote 2 (p.6).  

Overall: interesting extension of prior work, technically sound, but applicability/breadth is unclear and several clarifications and corrections are needed.
