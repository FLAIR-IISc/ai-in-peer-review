- Addresses recovery of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries given a model of the missingness process; extends Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Provides more general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and causal queries P(y|do(x)).  
- Demonstrates causal queries can be recoverable even when factors in their identifying estimands are not recoverable.  
- Technical quality is high; results are involved and appear correct.  
- Includes useful examples, e.g., identifiability of causal distributions when conditional distributions are unavailable.  
- Some results are incremental and do not clarify the broader underlying structure.  
- Major weakness is clarity: paper is overly dense with a lengthy (13-page) technical appendix.  
- Notation is confusing (e.g., reuse of capital 'V' for different objects), hindering readability.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are clear and potentially significant contributions.  
- Relevance and significance to the ML/NIPS community are high, with implications for fields like epidemiology.  
- Recommendation: accept but require improvements to clarity and notation.  
- Suggest expanding into a longer journal paper to develop a complete identifiability criterion and better explain the general case.
