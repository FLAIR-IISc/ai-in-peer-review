- Paper studies recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries with missing data using m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013).
- Main technical contributions: generalized sequential factorization including R, algorithms for joint recovery without admissible sequences, graphical non-recoverability criteria, and cases where causal effects are recoverable despite non-recoverable probabilistic quantities.
- Provides necessary and sufficient graphical conditions for “simple attrition” for joint and causal recoverability.
- Technical quality is high overall; Theorem 3 and Sections 5–6 are particularly convincing with informative examples.
- Some results are incremental relative to Mohan et al. (2013); paper lacks a unifying/complete recoverability criterion.
- Section 3 feels orthogonal and Theorem 1 is hard to parse and may overlap with ADMG factorization/Markov blanket results.
- Main weakness is clarity: dense material, awkward narrative flow, and an overloaded 13-page appendix.
- Notation is confusing (reuse of V, hard-to-read subscripts, Vi vs Vo), and some symbols/definitions are introduced late.
- Recommend clearer symbols for vertices vs sets, avoid undefined terms (Vo, Vm), and define “severed”-edge graph notation earlier.
- Sections 5–6 are novel and important, especially the non-recoverability criteria and recoverable causal effects despite inseparability.
- Need to clarify relation to Richardson (2003, 2009) and precisely state novelty over ADMG literature.
- The problem is important and results could impact practice, but lack of a unified characterization limits usability.
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 resemble inverse probability weighting; authors should make IPW and Markov blanket connections explicit.
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, new non-recoverability criteria, insights on causal recoverability, useful attrition analysis.
- Cons for acceptance: poor presentation and notation, unclear novelty of Section 3, and the work feels like extensions rather than a unified theory.
- Specific fixes: correctly state R^part as a partition, use ↔ for bidirected edges, avoid casual language, and clarify Garcia (2013) discrepancy.
- Define severed-edge graph notation before use and avoid introducing Vo/Vm before definition.
- Overall: promising and important work with valuable results (esp. Sections 5–6) but needs clearer notation, tighter narrative, and explicit positioning relative to ADMG/IPW to be a strong contribution.
