Paper studies recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from missing data using m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013).  
Main contribution: proves causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when joint/conditional distributions are not, with new graphical criteria.  
Introduces Theorems 1–3: generalizes sequential factorization, provides recovery methods when no admissible sequence exists, and gives non-recoverability conditions.  
Connects results formally to do-calculus and d*-separation, with rigorous proofs in the appendix.  
Provides clear, well-organized exposition with illustrative examples that highlight counterintuitive findings.  
Applies theoretical results to the practical issue of attrition in experimental studies.  
Weakness: entirely theoretical—lacks empirical validation (simulations or real data) to demonstrate practical performance and biases of naive methods.  
Weakness: assumes a correctly specified m-graph and offers little discussion of practical challenges in constructing or testing the graph.  
Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with clear writing; recommended for acceptance despite missing empirical experiments.
