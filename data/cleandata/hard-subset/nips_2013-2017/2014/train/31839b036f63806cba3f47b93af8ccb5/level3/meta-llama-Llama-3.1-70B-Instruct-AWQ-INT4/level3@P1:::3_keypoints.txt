- Proposes a novel approach to recover probabilistic and causal queries from missing data, extending Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Introduces "Missingness Graphs" (m-graphs) to model the missingness process and gives conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions.  
- Shows some causal queries can be recovered even when the full joint distribution is not recoverable.  
- Paper is technically sound and builds on prior work.  
- Assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may not be available in typical ML applications — needs discussion or relaxation.  
- Implicitly assumes the full population is known; this assumption should be stated and its limitations discussed.  
- Uses "m-graph" inconsistently (sometimes includes V* variables and sometimes not); clarify whether V* are omitted and why.  
- Forbids R variables as parents of V or U without justification; authors should justify or state this is for convenience.  
- Uses the term "neighbor" without definition; provide a clear definition for readers.  
- Relation to Garcia et al.'s "rectification" is unclear regarding positivity assumptions; clarify whether Garcia et al. assume positivity or a stronger condition.  
- Claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; explain applicability of MLE to the missingness processes studied.  
- Several minor errors in wording, figures, and notation need correction to improve readability.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and valuable contribution but requires clarifications and minor revisions (rating 7/10, accept with minor revisions).
