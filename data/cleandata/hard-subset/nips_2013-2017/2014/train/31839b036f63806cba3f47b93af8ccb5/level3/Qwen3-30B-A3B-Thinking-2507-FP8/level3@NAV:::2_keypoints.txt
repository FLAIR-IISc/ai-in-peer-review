- Extends Mohan et al. [2013] by providing novel necessary and sufficient conditions for identifiability with missing data.  
- Introduces "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) that embed the missing-data generating mechanism into causal diagrams.  
- Establishes precise graphical conditions for recoverability of both probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Key insight: causal effects can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic relations are not.  
- Challenges the need for separability between variables and their missingness mechanisms.  
- Well-organized with clear definitions, motivated examples, and comprehensive proofs in the appendix.  
- Theoretical claims are rigorously proven and authors appropriately limit the scope of their conclusions.  
- Clear and reproducible presentation, accessible to causal-inference researchers.  
- Original contribution: handles interacting missingness mechanisms beyond prior work.  
- Significant practical value: offers a foundation for determining recoverability without restrictive assumptions.  
- Suggestion: adding empirical validation would strengthen practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, original, significant; recommend acceptance.
