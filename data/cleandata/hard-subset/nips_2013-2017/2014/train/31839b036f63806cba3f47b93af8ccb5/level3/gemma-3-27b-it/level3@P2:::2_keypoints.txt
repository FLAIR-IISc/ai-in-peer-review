- Addresses recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries from datasets with missing data using m-graphs.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) to determine identifiability and recoverability under missingness graphs.  
- Key contribution: causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic queries are not.  
- Well-motivated, rigorous theoretical development with clear definitions and theorems.  
- Introduces Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 to handle more complex missing-data scenarios.  
- Novel result challenging the assumption that causal effect identifiability requires probabilistic identifiability.  
- Clear organization and notation; illustrative examples (Sections 3, 6) aid understanding.  
- Practical relevance shown via application to attrition problems (Section 7), improving on prior approaches.  
- Thorough related-work discussion, situating the paper among ML, causal inference, and missing-data literature.  
- Framework complexity may limit accessibility to readers without strong causal inference/graphical-models background.  
- Lacks empirical validation and simulations to show practical performance on real-world datasets.  
- Relies heavily on correctly specified m-graphs; more discussion needed on modeling challenges.  
- Does not address computational complexity or scalability of the proposed algorithms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, novel finding, strong presentation, practical application.  
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical complexity, absent empirical validation, unclear computational feasibility.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept with minor revisions (add computational discussion and small simulation).  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10 (Strong Accept).
