- Extends Mohan et al.'s missingness-graph framework to identifiability of probabilistic and causal queries under various missing-data mechanisms.  
- Provides graphical conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions.  
- Demonstrates that causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when Y and its missingness mechanism Ry are not d-separable, challenging conventional wisdom (Section 6 key contribution).  
- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and Richardson (2003, 2009), with Section 6 establishing conditions for causal recoverability beyond probabilistic recoverability.  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous proofs and correct results; Theorem 3 is clear and interpretable.  
- Sections 5–6 are the strongest parts and include useful examples and applications (e.g., attrition problems).  
- Major clarity issues: inconsistent and confusing notation (V, Vi vs. Vo, illegible subscripts) and poor overall flow.  
- Section 3 feels disconnected from the rest of the paper.  
- Theorem 1 and Corollary 1 use confusing or undefined notation (e.g., R^part) and lack good motivation.  
- Paper attempts to cover too much for a conference format (13-page appendix, dense text) and would suit a journal better.  
- Some results (e.g., Theorem 1) seem incremental and there is insufficient relation/clarification regarding Garcia (2013).  
- Overall strengths: important problem, technical soundness, novel causal recoverability result; weaknesses: presentation, organization, and length—needs substantial revision for conference.
