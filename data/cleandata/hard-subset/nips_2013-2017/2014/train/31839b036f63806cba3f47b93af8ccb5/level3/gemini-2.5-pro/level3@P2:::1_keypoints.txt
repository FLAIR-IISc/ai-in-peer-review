- Problem: studies recoverability (identifiability) of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing values using m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Contribution: extends sequential factorization theorem to handle dependencies between missingness mechanisms.  
- Contribution: gives graphical criteria for recovering joint distributions when factorization fails.  
- Contribution: provides novel graphical conditions that preclude recoverability (non-recoverability criteria).  
- Contribution: shows causal queries can be recoverable even when the corresponding probabilistic queries are not; applied to attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Strength: significant and original—advances MNAR analysis with a formal graphical calculus beyond MCAR/MAR.  
- Strength: technically solid—non-trivial theoretical results; Theorem 3 yields interpretable non-recoverability conditions.  
- Strength: high potential impact—useful for empirical fields with missing data; practical relevance shown via attrition example.  
- Weakness: clarity and presentation—paper is dense, covers too much, and is hard to parse.  
- Weakness: notation problematic—many similar symbols (V, Vi, Vo, Vm, V*) and poorly introduced subscripts; some notation (e.g., Corollary 1) is opaque.  
- Weakness: structure and flow—reads like a collection of results; sections (e.g., Section 3) feel disconnected.  
- Weakness: missing definitions—key terms (e.g., G_X, G_W) used in Definition 4/Theorem 4 are undefined.  
- Weakness: incremental elements—some results are extensions of prior work; lacks a unifying recoverability algorithm analogous to causal-identification algorithms.  
- Weakness: limited connections to related work—should explicitly relate to inverse-probability weighting and selection-bias/mixed-graph literature (Richardson).  
- Suggestion: overhaul notation, add a notation table, and define all terms before use.  
- Suggestion: focus conference paper on the most novel results (non-recoverability criteria and causal recovery), moving other material to appendices.  
- Suggestion: clarify claims about prior work (e.g., Garcia 2013) to avoid misrepresentation.  
- Minor fixes: state that Rx and Ry belong to distinct partition elements in R_part; avoid casual language; use standard LaTeX for bidirected edges.  
- Summary pro: rigorous graphical approach with novel and practically useful results.  
- Summary con / conclusion: promising and technically interesting but presentation and clarity issues are severe; major revision needed and better suited for a journal submission in current form.
