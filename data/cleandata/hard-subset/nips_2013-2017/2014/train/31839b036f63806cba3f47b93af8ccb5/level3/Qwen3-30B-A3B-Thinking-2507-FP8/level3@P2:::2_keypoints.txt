- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by introducing novel conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data.  
- Builds on the m-graph framework to give general recoverability conditions for P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)), noting causal queries can be recoverable even when probabilistic components are not.  
- Refines the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Develops new graphical criteria for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.  
- Demonstrates that P(y|do(x)) can be recovered despite Y and Ry being inseparable (Theorem 4), challenging prior assumptions.  
- Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects in attrition problems in longitudinal studies.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous, well-motivated, and precisely proved; Theorem 4’s do-calculus proof is elegant.  
- Authors transparently acknowledge limitations (e.g., |Vm|=1 restriction in Theorems 5 and 6).  
- Experimental validation uses illustrative examples (Figures 2–4), appropriate for a theoretical paper but lacking real-world dataset tests.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with a helpful running example and comprehensive appendix proofs.  
- Originality: meaningfully extends prior work to causal queries and overturns misconceptions (e.g., Garcia 2013).  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental, ubiquitous problem in causal inference with missing data and generalizes beyond MAR/MCAR assumptions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel necessary/sufficient conditions, corrects literature, theoretically sound, clearly written, and impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: m-graph focus may limit audience, lacks empirical validation on real datasets, and graphical conditions may be complex to apply.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
