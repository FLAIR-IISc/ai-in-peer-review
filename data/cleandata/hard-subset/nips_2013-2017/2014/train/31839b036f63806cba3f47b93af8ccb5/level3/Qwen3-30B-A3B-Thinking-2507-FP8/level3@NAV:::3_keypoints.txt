- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by deriving graphical conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries under a more general missingness mechanism.  
- Presents novel identification formulas showing P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when joint P(y,x) is not.  
- Applies results to attrition in longitudinal studies, giving necessary and sufficient conditions for causal effect recovery.  
- Technically rigorous with meaningful advances, notably that causal effects can be recovered without d-separability between Y and Ry.  
- Extends the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Practical applicability is questionable because results require knowledge of the missingness process, which is rarely available in ML settings.  
- Authors should move more Figure 1 details into the main text to improve accessibility.  
- Section 2 implicitly assumes knowledge of the full population, excluding problems like population size estimation.  
- Inconsistent use of "mgraph" terminology (sometimes includes V* variables, sometimes not) needs resolution.  
- The restriction forbidding R variables as parents of V or U requires justification given R variables are treated as real quantities.  
- The term "neighbor" is undefined and the relation to Garcia et al.'s "rectification" needs clarification, including positivity assumptions.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation (R^{(N)} vs R^{(j)}), incorrect RHS in Example 1 (should be P(Y|X,Z)), and missing reference for the variable-level definition in Footnote 2.  
- The claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague and requires elaboration.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but practical relevance and several clarifications must be addressed before publication.
