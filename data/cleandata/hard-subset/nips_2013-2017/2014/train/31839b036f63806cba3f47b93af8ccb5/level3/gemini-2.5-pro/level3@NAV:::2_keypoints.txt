- Problem: determines when causal or probabilistic queries are recoverable from data with missing observations using graphical m-graphs.
- Builds on Mohan et al. (2013) and extends the m-graph framework to handle more complex missingness dependencies.
- Generalizes the sequential factorization theorem to allow dependencies between missingness mechanisms.
- Presents a novel algorithm to recover queries when sequential factorization is not possible.
- Introduces graphical, necessary-and-sufficient criteria for non-recoverability.
- Key insight: a causal query P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even if the observational conditional P(y|x) is not, via do-calculus.
- Applies the theoretical results to the practical problem of attrition in longitudinal studies.
- Strength: significant and original theoretical advancement with new recoverability conditions.
- Strength: technically sound presentation with clear definitions, theorems, and supporting graphical examples.
- Strength: manuscript is well-written, well-organized, and examples effectively aid comprehension.
- Weakness: purely theoretical work; would be strengthened by discussion of algorithmic complexity or a simulation/finite-sample study.
- Overall: high-quality, impactful contribution likely to influence future research.
