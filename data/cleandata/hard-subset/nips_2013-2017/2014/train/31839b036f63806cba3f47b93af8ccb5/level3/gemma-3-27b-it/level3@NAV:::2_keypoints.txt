- Addresses causal and probabilistic query estimation under missing data using Missingness Graphs (m-graphs).
- Main contribution: provides more general and refined conditions for query recoverability by explicitly incorporating the missingness mechanism into the causal diagram.
- Shows causal queries (P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic factors are not.
- Extends the sequential factorization theorem from Mohan et al. [2013] to handle more complex missingness patterns.
- Introduces new graphical criteria to identify non-recoverable queries.
- Demonstrates recoverability when a variable and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.
- Applies results to the practical problem of attrition as an illustrative example.
- Strength: technically sound with clear theoretical derivations, theorems, corollaries, and illustrative examples.
- Strength: coherently builds on and extends existing literature, advancing the state-of-the-art in causal inference with missing data.
- Weakness: dense notation and heavy mathematical formalism may limit accessibility for non-expert readers.
- Weakness: limited exploration of real-world applications beyond the attrition example.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of the computational complexity or tractability of determining recoverability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and significant theoretical contributions, clear extension of prior work, rigorous proofs, and practical application.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility issues due to dense notation, narrow range of empirical examples, and missing complexity analysis.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable theoretical advancements; recommended for acceptance.
