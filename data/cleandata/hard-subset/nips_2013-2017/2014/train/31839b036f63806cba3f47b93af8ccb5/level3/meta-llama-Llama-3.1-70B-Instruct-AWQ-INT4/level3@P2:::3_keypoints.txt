- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by giving more general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries.  
- Introduces "Missingness Graphs" (m-graphs) to explicitly represent the missingness process and variable dependencies.  
- Provides sufficient conditions for recovering joint and conditional distributions and for recovering causal queries; applies results to attrition.  
- Builds on prior work (Mohan & Pearl 2013) and relates to classical missing-data methods (listwise deletion, ML, EM, IPW) and recent graphical approaches.  
- Strength: technically sound with clear, well-organized presentation and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Strength: extends earlier work and offers a more general framework for recovery problems.  
- Weakness: assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process, which may not be available in typical ML settings.  
- Terminology issue: "m-graph" is used inconsistently; unclear whether V* variables are omitted and why.  
- Modeling restriction: paper forbids R variables as parents of V or U — this needs justification or should be stated as convenience.  
- Undefined term: "neighbor" is used without definition and should be clarified.  
- Needs clarification of relation to Garcia et al.'s "rectification," especially regarding positivity assumptions.  
- Claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; authors should explain applicability of MLE to the considered missingness processes.  
- Contains several minor errors (wording, figure qualifications, notation) that need correction.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and technically solid, but broad applicability is unclear and multiple clarifications/fixes are required.  
- Acceptance arguments: clear presentation, thorough related work, meaningful extension of prior results.  
- Rejection arguments: reliance on assumed missingness knowledge, inconsistent terminology and modeling restrictions, and minor errors.  
- Recommendations: clarify m-graph usage and V* omission, justify R-parent restriction, define "neighbor," detail Garcia/MLE issues, and fix minor errors.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10, confidence 8/10.
