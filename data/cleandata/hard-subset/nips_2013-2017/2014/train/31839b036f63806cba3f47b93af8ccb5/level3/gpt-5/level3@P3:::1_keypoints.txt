- Summary: Studies recoverability of probabilistic and causal queries under m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013) and providing new recovery/non-recoverability criteria and formulas.
- Summary: Key contributions include extending ordered factorization to include R-nodes (Thm 1), partition/Markov-blanket-based recovery (Thm 2, Cor 1), graphical non-recoverability conditions (Thm 3, Cor 2), causal recovery despite non-recoverable conditionals (Thm 4), and attrition analyses (Thms 5–6).
- Quality (technical soundness): Results are technically solid and proofs are convincing; Thm 3 and the do-calculus causal recovery are especially clear and interpretable.
- Quality (scope): Sections 5–6 are the most impactful; Section 3 (ordered factorization with R) is interesting but feels orthogonal to the main contributions.
- Quality (validation): Formal proofs in the appendix and examples are provided, though more step-by-step examples would improve accessibility.
- Clarity (overall): Paper is dense, uneven in flow, and hard to follow within conference length; major clarity and organization issues need addressing.
- Clarity (notation): Notation is confusing/inconsistent (overuse of V, unclear R-partition membership, inconsistent edge symbols, missing definitions like Gw/GX,W1, Vo/Vm introduced late).
- Clarity (presentation suggestions): Move or shorten Section 3, lead with concise contributions and a running example, and provide diagrammatic aids and proof sketches for key theorems.
- Originality: Extends prior work and gives the first crisp non-recoverability criteria; showing causal recoverability when probabilistic conditionals are not is novel.
- Originality (limitations): Some results (e.g., Thm 1) feel incremental and the paper lacks a unifying identifiability criterion akin to ID.
- Significance: High potential impact for practitioners dealing with MNAR; Sections 5–6 and attrition results are particularly actionable.
- Relation to prior work: Needs clearer positioning relative to IPW/selection-model literature and ADMG/latent projection results; clarify discrepancies with Garcia (2013).
- Specific comments: Theorem 1 should include a schematic example and motivation for minimality; chain-rule factorization issues need clarifying.
- Specific comments: Theorem 2/Corollary 1 need the partition statement fixed, earlier definitions of Rpart/Mb/Pa_sub, and a diagrammatic intuition.
- Specific comments: Definition 4/Theorem 4 must explicitly define Gw and GX,W1 and restate dormant independence assumptions for self-containment.
- Language/notation fixes: Avoid casual language, standardize bidirected edge notation, and ensure all symbols are defined before use.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with strong, usable non-recoverability criteria and valuable causal-identifiability insights; attrition results are useful.
- Cons for acceptance: Major clarity, organization, and notation problems; some incremental contributions and an unclear unifying framework.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — technically strong and promising but requires substantial revision for clarity, organization, and notation before acceptance.
