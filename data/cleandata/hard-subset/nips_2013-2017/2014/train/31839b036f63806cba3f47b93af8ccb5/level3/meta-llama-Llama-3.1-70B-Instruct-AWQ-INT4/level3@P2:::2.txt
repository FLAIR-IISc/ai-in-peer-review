The paper "Graphical Models for Recovering Probabilistic and Causal Queries from Missing Data" proposes novel necessary and sufficient conditions for determining when a causal query is identifiable from data with missing observations. The authors extend prior work by explicitly incorporating the missing-data generating mechanism into the causal diagram, providing a more comprehensive framework for causal analysis with missing data.

The paper builds upon the concept of "Missingness Graphs" (m-graphs) introduced by Mohan et al. [2013], which provides a formal representation of the missingness process. The authors refine the sequential factorization theorem presented in Mohan et al. [2013] and extend its applicability to a wider range of problems in which missingness mechanisms may influence each other. They also introduce new graphical criteria that preclude recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.

The paper's main contributions are the derivation of general conditions for recovering probabilistic queries of the form P(y|x) and P(y, x) as well as causal queries of the form P(y|do(x)). The authors show that causal queries may be recoverable even when the factors in their identifying estimands are not recoverable. Specifically, they derive graphical conditions for recovering causal effects of the form P(y|do(x)) when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.

The paper also applies the results to problems of attrition and characterizes the recovery of causal effects from data corrupted by attrition. The authors demonstrate that their approach can rectify prevailing opinions in the literature, such as the necessary condition for non-recoverability of causal effects under simple attrition.
