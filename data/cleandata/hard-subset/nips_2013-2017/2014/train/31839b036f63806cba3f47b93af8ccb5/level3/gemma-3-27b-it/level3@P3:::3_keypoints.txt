- Paper studies identifiability of probabilistic and causal queries under missing data, extending Mohan et al. [2013] and showing causal effects can be recoverable even when joint distributions are not.  
- Introduces "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) as a formal representation of the missing data process and derives novel graphical recoverability criteria.  
- Technically sound with logical theorem progression and correct mathematical derivations.  
- Core contribution (conditions for recovering causal queries despite non-recoverable joints) is interesting and potentially impactful.  
- Application to attrition problems provides a concrete use case and numerous examples aid understanding.  
- Major concern: relies on knowing the censoring/missingness process (R variables), which is often unrealistic in practice.  
- Paper should expand on limitations of assuming known R and discuss possible relaxations or scenarios where it holds.  
- Figure 1’s detailed m-graph scenarios and recoverability results should be integrated into main text for clarity.  
- Section 2 assumes knowledge of the full population including entirely unobserved individuals (U), which is a strong assumption excluding problems like population size estimation.  
- The term "mgraph" is used inconsistently with respect to inclusion of V* (proxy) variables and needs clarification.  
- Restriction that R variables cannot be parents of V or U is problematic and requires strong justification or acknowledgement as a simplifying assumption.  
- The term "neighbor" is used without definition and needs a precise definition.  
- Discussion of Garcia et al. [2013] needs clarification on what positivity assumption they make and how it compares to this work’s conditions.  
- Claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; applicability depends on correct specification of the missingness mechanism and should be explained.  
- Authors should detail why MLE could or could not be applied here and how their graphical criteria provide an advantage.  
- Several minor errors/corrections: wording ("can introduce bias"), integrate Figure 1 qualifications into text, fix Example 1 RHS to P(Y|X,Z), replace Y || R with Y || R_y, fix R^{(N)}/R^{(j)} notation, add partially observed variable to Corollary 1, and cite variable-level definition in Footnote 2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical results, technically sound, extends existing work meaningfully, and provides a formal m-graph framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong/possibly unrealistic assumptions about knowing missingness process, limited real-world applicability, clarity and notation issues, and restricted scope from population assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions addressing missingness-process knowledge, notation/clarity issues, and listed technical corrections.
