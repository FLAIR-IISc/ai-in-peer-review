- Tackles identification of causal and probabilistic queries from data with missing observations, extending Mohan et al. [2013].
- Explicitly models the missingness mechanism within causal diagrams and derives general recoverability conditions.
- Key novel finding: causal queries can be identifiable even when underlying probabilistic relationships are not.
- Well-motivated problem framing that connects to common missing-data approaches (deletion, MLE, IPW, imputation).
- Positions work within graphical models literature and extends the sequential factorization theorem to interacting missingness.
- Theoretical framework is rigorous, using d-separation and do-calculus with clearly stated theorems and derivations.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses illustrative figures effectively.
- Maintains a focused scope on recoverability without venturing into tangential topics.
- Limited empirical evaluation; lacks simulations or real-world examples to validate theoretical criteria.
- Dense notation and complex theorems may limit accessibility for readers less familiar with causal graphical models.
- Application to attrition is illustrative but feels somewhat underdeveloped and reliant on examples.
- Technically sound and complete in theoretical development, with careful reasoning and acknowledged limitations.
- Highly original contribution with significant potential impact for causal inference in fields with missing data.
- Recommendation for acceptance contingent on adding empirical validation in future work; concerns about accessibility and lack of experiments.
