- Addresses recovery of probabilistic and causal queries from data with missing entries using and extending the m-graph framework.  
- Key contribution: shows causal queries can be recoverable even when underlying probabilistic relations are not, broadening m-graph applicability.  
- Technically rigorous: solid mathematical derivations and clearly stated theorems (notably Theorem 1 sequential factorization and Theorem 2 generalization).  
- Provides useful non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3 and Corollary 2) and a concrete application to attrition problems.  
- Presentation problems: manuscript is overly long, dense, and sometimes disjointed, making it hard to follow.  
- Notation is inconsistent and confusing (excessive use of V with subscripts; Corollary 1 poorly presented).  
- Some results feel incremental and the paper lacks a broader unifying identifiability perspective.  
- Connections to existing techniques (e.g., inverse probability weighting, Richardson 2003) are hinted at but not explicitly developed.  
- Specific technical fixes needed: correct phrasing of R^part, clarify edge-severing notation, and simplify/explain Theorem 1’s relation to prior work.  
- Suggested revisions: shorten and streamline, adopt consistent intuitive notation, improve section flow, and expand links to related work.  
- Significance: work is important for many fields (epidemiology, social science) because it enables causal recovery under challenging missingness mechanisms.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after substantial revision to improve clarity and unify contributions.
