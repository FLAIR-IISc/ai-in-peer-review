- Summary: Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by generalizing sequential factorization to include R-variables, providing a new joint-distribution recovery strategy via R-partitions/Markov blankets, graphical non-recoverability criteria, causal recoverability results using do-calculus, and necessary/sufficient conditions for simple-attrition recoverability.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on graphical missing-data literature (Mohan et al., Rubin, EM) and recent ML MNAR work, connecting dormant independences from Shpitser & Pearl to missingness recovery.

- Strength: Clear technical contributions—generalized sequential factorization (Thm.1) and joint recovery without admissible sequences (Thm.2) applicable to semi-Markovian m-graphs.

- Strength: Useful negative results—graphical non-recoverability criteria for P(V) and P(Y|X) (Thm.3, Cor.2).

- Strength: Conceptual insight—causal effects P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when related probabilistic quantities are not, with precise conditions (Thm.4) and illustrative example.

- Strength: Practical relevance—accurate, actionable conditions for attrition (Thms.5–6) that correct common misconceptions in applied literature.

- Weakness: Assumes known m-graph; needs discussion of elicitation, testing, partial specification, and robustness to misspecification, and moving Fig.1 exposition into main text for practitioners.

- Weakness: Scope unclear—appears to assume fixed cohort/item missingness and known population, excluding unit nonresponse or unknown population size; authors should state and discuss extensions.

- Weakness: Inconsistent definition/usage of m-graph and scope (V*, R, V, U); needs a single clear definition and consistent notation, plus clarification of “substantive subgraph.”

- Weakness: Structural restriction forbidding R→V or R→U edges needs justification or discussion of implications if R→V were allowed.

- Weakness: Missing definitions (e.g., “neighbor”); define early since several theorems rely on it.

- Weakness: Relation to Garcia and positivity—clarify role of positivity/overlap assumptions and whether corrections depend on different/stronger support conditions.

- Weakness: Overly broad MLE-under-MAR claim—state precise ignorability conditions, discuss ML for MNAR requiring explicit R models, and when graphical recoverability pairs with likelihood estimation.

- Weakness: Notational and presentation issues in Example 1 and Corollary 1—fix conditioning, ambiguous R notation, clarify Corollary wording, provide illustrative example and citation for Footnote 2.

- Suggestion: Add practical checklist/algorithmic summary for users (test admissible sequence, apply Thm.2 partitioning, non-recoverability checks, causal recovery) and a small synthetic example contrasting recoverable vs non-recoverable cases.

- Quality: Technically sound with correct use of do-calculus and d-/d*-separation; proofs in appendix appear careful; negative criteria valuable.

- Clarity: Generally readable for experts but hampered by notation inconsistencies and imprecise MLE/MAR statements; addressing listed issues would improve readability.

- Originality: Novel extension of sequential factorization and R-partition/Markov-blanket recovery; important tailoring of dormant-independence ideas to missing-data settings.

- Significance: High potential impact for causal inference and missing-data work, especially longitudinal attrition, but broader ML adoption depends on guidance for specifying/testing m-graphs.

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theoretical advances beyond Mohan et al., important causal recoverability insight, and practical corrections for attrition.

- Arguments against acceptance: Heavy reliance on a correctly specified m-graph, notational inconsistencies and unclear statements, and limited empirical/synthetic illustrations.

- Recommendation: Weak accept—technically strong and relevant, but needs revisions for clarity, notation, and practical guidance.

- Minor comments/typos: Reword “can bias outcomes,” clarify Figure 1 captions and distinguish V*/R/substantive variables, replace Y || R with Y ⟂⟂ Ry, fix R(i) indexing, ensure Example 1 expressions match intended conditioning, and discuss/checkable strict positivity over complete cases.
