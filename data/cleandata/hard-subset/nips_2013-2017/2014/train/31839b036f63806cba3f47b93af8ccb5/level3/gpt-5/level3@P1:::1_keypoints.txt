- Paper studies recoverability of P(y, x), P(y|x), and P(y|do(x)) under missing data using m-graphs, extending Mohan et al. (2013).
- Contribution (i): Generalizes ordered/sequential factorization to allow R-nodes in separating sets (Theorem 1), addressing interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Contribution (ii): Provides joint-distribution recovery conditions without admissible sequences by partitioning R and conditioning on Markov blankets Mb(R(i)) (Theorem 2, Corollary 1).
- Contribution (iii): Presents clear graphical non-recoverability criteria for joint and conditional distributions (Theorem 3, Corollary 2).
- Contribution (iv): Shows some causal effects are recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not, using do-calculus and dormant independences (Theorem 4).
- Contribution (v): Applies results to attrition, giving necessary and sufficient joint and causal recoverability conditions when |Vm| = 1 (Theorems 5–6).
- Technical development is substantial and careful; non-recoverability criteria and attrition results are especially strong and practical.
- Central conceptual contribution — causal effects recoverable despite inseparability of Y and Ry — is novel and well-illustrated.
- Theorem 1 is difficult to parse and Section 3’s general factorization feels disconnected from later results.
- Claims about Garcia (2013) need clearer reconciliation of differing assumptions.
- Paper is dense with heavy notation, late/inconsistent definitions, and flow problems; Sections 5–6 are clearer.
- Notational issues (overloaded symbols, illegible subscripts, node vs. set confusion) add friction and harm readability.
- Originality: extending to interactive missingness and causal recoverability in MNAR is novel; Theorem 2 resembles IPW/Markov blanket ideas and should be positioned relative to ADMG theory.
- Significance: provides valuable graphical (non-)recoverability criteria for MNAR settings and useful attrition examples.
- Lacks a unified decision procedure; unifying Theorems 1–3 and 5–6 into a single algorithmic criterion would increase impact.
- Recommend clarifying Theorem 1’s role, giving a compact definition and test for “admissible sequence,” and adding a running example.
- Suggest explicit connections to ADMG factorization/Markov blanket literature (Richardson 2003 et al.) and contrast with IPW.
- Non-recoverability strengths should include a brief intuition and a figure showing collider-path failures.
- For Theorem 4, define graph-surgery notation in main text and provide a checklist for application.
- Clarify differences with Garcia (2013) via a table of assumptions rather than categorical claims.
- Improve notation consistency: define Vo/Vm early, use ↔ for bidirected edges, fix R-subscript legibility, and simplify Corollary 1 presentation.
- Add 1–2 end-to-end numeric examples and discuss computational aspects (testing conditions, search complexity) and robustness to positivity violations.
- Pros for acceptance: tackles under-explored MNAR recoverability, strong non-recoverability results, novel causal recoverability, practical attrition conditions.
- Cons for acceptance: presentation and notation hinder readability, fragmented narrative, some incremental parts, and unresolved alignment with prior work.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising contributions but needs reorganization, clearer presentation, and stronger unification to be acceptable.
