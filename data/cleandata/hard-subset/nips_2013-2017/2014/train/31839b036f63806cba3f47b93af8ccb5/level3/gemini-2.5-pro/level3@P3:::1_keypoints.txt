- Addresses identifiability of probabilistic and causal queries with missing data using the m-graph framework, including MNAR settings.  
- Derives graphical conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and P(y|do(x)).  
- Extends the sequential factorization theorem to handle dependencies among missingness indicators (R-variables).  
- Provides a new theorem to recover joint distributions when sequential factorization fails by partitioning R-variables and using their Markov blankets.  
- Introduces novel graphical criteria for non-recoverability (Theorem 3).  
- Shows causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even if corresponding probabilistic queries are not, with application to attrition in longitudinal studies.  
- Technical quality is high; proofs appear rigorous and the results are well-supported.  
- Theorem 3 is especially clear, interpretable, and practically useful.  
- Paper clarity is poor: overly dense, disjointed flow, and tries to cover too much for a conference.  
- Section 3 and Theorem 1 are hard to follow and feel orthogonal to the main narrative.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (overuse of 'V', ambiguous Vi/Vo, illegible subscripts).  
- Corollary 1 and several formulas are presented densely with insufficient context.  
- Some results are incremental extensions of Mohan et al. (2013), not fundamental leaps.  
- Sections 5 and 6 contain highly original and significant contributions that distinguish the work.  
- The topic and results are significant and likely useful to researchers, but poor presentation may limit impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, contains novel correct technical contributions, and offers practical graphical tools.  
- Arguments against acceptance: extremely poor clarity and organization, overstuffed for a conference, and some incremental material dilutes impact.  
- Recommend focusing the conference version on non-recoverability criteria and causal recovery results, moving other material to the appendix or a journal.  
- Urge a complete notation overhaul and earlier definition of terms (e.g., Vo, Vm).  
- Suggest adding intuitive explanations and simpler examples for Theorem 1 and connecting it to Richardson (2009).  
- Recommend discussing links between Theorem 2/Corollary 1 and IPW/Markov-blanket weighting, citing related work.  
- Specific fixes requested: correct R^part wording, define graph manipulations (e.g., G_X), clarify the claim about Garcia (2013), formalize language, and use standard LaTeX symbols.
