- Introduces a novel identification formula for recovering distributions and causal queries from data with missingness, extending Mohan et al. (2013) to interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Shows causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when the observational joint distribution is not, with application to attrition problems.
- Strength — Novelty: Provides new graphical conditions and handles interacting missingness mechanisms, filling gaps in prior work.
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: Results supported by formal proofs and both recoverability and non-recoverability criteria.
- Strength — Practical relevance: Methods applicable to attrition in longitudinal studies.
- Strength — Clarity in examples: Examples (e.g., recovery of P(y|do(z)) when Y and R_y are inseparable) effectively illustrate results.
- Weakness — Censoring assumption: Requires knowledge of the censoring process, which may be unavailable in practice.
- Weakness — Full population assumption: Assumes full population known, excluding problems like estimating population sizes.
- Weakness — Terminology issues: Inconsistent use of "mgraph" and unclear restriction disallowing R variables as parents of V or U.
- Weakness — Undefined terms: Important terms (e.g., "neighbor") are not defined, risking reader confusion.
- Weakness — Clarity problems: Vague statements (e.g., on MLE under MAR), typos, and ambiguous notation reduce readability.
- Weakness — Relation to prior work unclear: Alignment with Garcia et al. and contextualization in related work need improvement.
- Argument for acceptance: Significant, novel theoretical contribution with practical implications and solid proofs.
- Argument against acceptance: Strong/possibly unrealistic assumptions and clarity/terminology issues limit impact and accessibility.
- Recommendation: Accept pending revisions to clarify notation and terminology, justify assumptions, and improve presentation.
