- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) by providing graphical conditions using m-graphs for recovering probabilistic and causal queries from missing data, showing causal effects can be recoverable even when underlying distributions are not.  
- Generalizes sequential factorization (Section 3), introduces non-recoverability criteria (Theorem 3, Corollary 2), and proves causal recovery results (Theorem 4, Example 3); applied to attrition problems (Section 7).  
- Technical quality: rigorous theorems and sound proofs; key causal recovery result is significant with practical implications (e.g., clinical trials).  
- Significance: addresses causal identifiability under MNAR where probabilistic recovery fails, and corrects prior work (Garcia 2013) with actionable graphical criteria.  
- Originality: introduces novel non-recoverability conditions and causal recovery under inseparability; provides a new graphical framing of IPW-like results extending prior work.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent and late-introduced notation (Vo, Vm, V*, R^part), dense/disjoint Section 3, and an overly long appendix that obscures impact.  
- Structural issues: Section 3 feels like an add-on rather than integrated motivation; Theorem 1’s claimed extension of Mohan et al. is insufficiently justified.  
- Incrementalism: lacks a unifying identifiability criterion analogous to causal-effect identification; some results are technical extensions without broader framework.  
- Critical omissions: definition of G_X is missing and Garcia (2013)’s error is not clearly contextualized.  
- Pros for acceptance: groundbreaking causal recoverability result, first formal non-recoverability criteria, and correction of prior literature.  
- Cons for acceptance: presentation, notation, and structure problems hinder reproducibility and conference suitability; appendix too lengthy.  
- Recommendation: Borderline (weak reject) — strong technical contributions but requires substantial restructuring and clarity improvements.  
- Specific suggestions: define Vo/Vm/V*/G_X early; clarify R^part and R(i) notation; merge Theorem 2 and Corollary 1; remove or integrate Section 3 into main narrative; fix notation (Ry→R_y) and use “d-separated.”
