- Problem: Inferring causal and probabilistic queries from datasets with missing observations; provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recoverability.  
- Methodological contribution: Extend Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) by incorporating the missing data generating mechanism into the causal diagram.  
- Novelty: Enables analysis of scenarios where missingness mechanisms interact or influence one another.  
- New results: Introduce graphical criteria for identifying non-recoverability.  
- Theoretical development: Systematically refine prior results (e.g., sequential factorization) and generalize them to broader problem classes.  
- Algorithms: Present new algorithms and graphical conditions for recoverability, supported by rigorous proofs.  
- Key insight: Causal queries (e.g., P(y|do(x))) can be recoverable even when related probabilistic queries (e.g., P(y,x)) are not.  
- Originality: Addresses previously unexplored scenarios such as recovery when variables and their missingness mechanisms are not d-separable.  
- Practical application: Apply results to attrition problems in longitudinal studies, demonstrating real-world relevance.  
- Contextualization: Situates contributions relative to imputation, inverse probability weighting, and coarsened data frameworks while clarifying novelty.  
- Significance: Advances state of the art in causal inference with missing data and is relevant to social sciences, healthcare, and longitudinal research.  
- Strength: Introduces novel, generalizable recoverability conditions that extend prior work.  
- Strength: Rigorously treats both recoverability and non-recoverability, filling a gap in the literature.
