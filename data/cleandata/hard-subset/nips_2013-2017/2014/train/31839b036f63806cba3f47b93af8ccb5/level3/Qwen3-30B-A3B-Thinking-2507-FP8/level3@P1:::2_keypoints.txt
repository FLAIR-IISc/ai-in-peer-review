- Extends Mohan et al.'s m-graphs to provide necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries with missing data.  
- Core innovation: shows P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when P(y,x) and P(y|x) are not, especially when Y and Ry are not d-separable.  
- Introduces two main theorems (Theorems 1 and 2) generalizing sequential factorization to interacting missingness mechanisms.  
- Theorem 4 is notable for establishing recoverability when Y and Ry are connected by an inducing path.  
- Provides necessary conditions for non-recoverability (Theorem 3 and Corollary 2), helping identify impossible estimation cases.  
- Offers compelling applied examples (gender wage-gap, attrition) that demonstrate practical use of the conditions.  
- Theoretical arguments and proofs are rigorous, complete, and included in the appendix for reproducibility.  
- Clearly distinguishes recoverability of probabilistic versus causal queries, filling a gap in prior literature.  
- Corrects misconceptions from prior work (e.g., Garcia 2013) about which conditions imply non-recoverability.  
- Introduces elegant use of d*-separation and dormant independence to establish causal recoverability.  
- Significance: provides a theoretically sound framework with broad impact for handling missing data in causal inference.  
- Writing, notation, and organization are exceptionally clear and well-motivated.  
- Minor weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity for recovery procedures on large graphs.  
- Minor weakness: could include more on practical implementation challenges in real-world applications.  
- Minor weakness: could more explicitly contrast with related missing-data work in machine learning (e.g., Marlin et al. 2011).  
- Overall recommendation: strong NIPS submission—novel, rigorous, well-presented, and recommended for acceptance.
