- Generalizes recoverability conditions for missing data using m-graphs, building on Mohan et al. (2013).  
- Main contributions: new identification formula for the joint under a more complex missingness mechanism; shows some causal queries are identifiable even when the full joint is not.  
- Strengths: strong technical quality, originality, and a non-obvious insight about causal vs. probabilistic recoverability.  
- Major concern: framework assumes knowledge of the true causal graph for missingness, making practical applicability unclear; authors should discuss this and move Figure 1 details into the main text to motivate the model.  
- Assumes the full population is known, which excludes problems like population size estimation and needs justification.  
- The definition/usage of “m-graph” is inconsistent regarding inclusion of V* variables and must be clarified.  
- The restriction that missingness indicators R cannot be parents of substantive variables (V, U) should be justified or framed as a simplifying assumption.  
- The term “neighbor” is used without a formal definition and should be defined precisely.  
- Comparison to Garcia et al. needs clarification about the role of positivity assumptions.  
- Claim that maximum likelihood yields consistent estimates under MAR is too vague; discuss whether MLE applies to the missingness processes considered.  
- Minor corrections needed: rephrase “can bias outcomes”; RHS of Example 1 should be P(Y|X,Z); Y || R should be Y || R_y; make R^{(N)} vs R^{(j)} notation consistent; include the missing partially observed variable in Corollary 1; add a reference for Footnote 2’s definition.  
- Overall: interesting and technically sound with novel contributions, but impact is limited until practical applicability and clarity issues are addressed.
