This paper studies recoverability of probabilistic (P(y, x), P(y|x)) and causal (P(y|do(x))) queries from data with missing entries using missingness graphs, extending Mohan et al. (NIPS’13). The main technical contributions are: (i) a generalized sequential factorization that admits R-variables in admissible orders, enabling recovery when missingness mechanisms interact; (ii) graphical non-recoverability criteria for joints and conditionals; (iii) conditions under which causal effects are recoverable even when the corresponding joint is not (via do-calculus, d*-separation/dormant independences); and (iv) necessary and sufficient results for “simple attrition,” clarifying and correcting aspects of Garcia (2013). The work is well-situated within Pearl/Shpitser identification theory and the MAR/ML literature.

Quality: The theoretical development is careful and technically sound, with clear links to identification, Markov blankets, and collider paths. The result that P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable despite non-recoverability of P(V) is novel and important. However, several assumptions/definitions need clarification to assess scope and correctness in practice: the paper assumes knowledge of the censoring/missingness process; please discuss how such knowledge is obtained in typical ML workflows and move more of the Figure 1 specification into the main text. Section 2 appears to assume the full population exists (including entirely unobserved individuals); clarify implications and whether population size estimation problems are excluded.

Clarity: The term “m-graph” is used inconsistently (sometimes with V* included, sometimes not); please standardize and state explicitly when V* variables are omitted and why. You forbid R → V or R → U; since R are treated as substantive quantities, justify this restriction (causal implausibility vs. technical convenience). Define “neighbor” when first used (it is pivotal in Thm. 2, Thm. 3). On p.8, the relation to Garcia’s “rectification” should specify positivity/overlap assumptions; do Garcia et al. assume the same positivity over complete cases or stronger overlap? Also on p.8, the statement that MLE is consistent under MAR is too general; explain whether/why standard ML/EM could or could not be applied to the MNAR mechanisms considered here.

Minor issues: “can bias outcomes” → “can bias estimates/conclusions”; qualify Figure 1 in text; Example 1 RHS should be P(Y|X,Z); replace Y ⫫ R with Y ⫫ RY; fix R^(N) vs. R^(j) notation; Corollary 1 seems to omit an explicit partially observed variable; add a citation for the variable-level definition in Footnote 2.

Originality and Significance: The extensions beyond Mohan et al. and the non-recoverability criteria are novel and likely to influence causal inference with missing data. Applicability may be limited by the need to know the missingness graph; discussing elicitation and diagnostics would broaden impact.

Pros: strong theory; novel causal-recovery insight; clear graphical conditions; useful attrition results.
Cons: reliance on known missingness process; clarity/notation issues; ambiguous assumptions about positivity and MLE; limited guidance for typical ML applications.
