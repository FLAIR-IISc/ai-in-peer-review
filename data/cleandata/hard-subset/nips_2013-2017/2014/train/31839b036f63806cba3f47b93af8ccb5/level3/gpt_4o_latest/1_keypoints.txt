- Addresses identifiability with missing data by extending Mohan et al.'s m-graphs to give graphical conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and causal P(y|do(x)).  
- Key contribution: shows causal queries can be recoverable even when their identifying estimands are not, relevant for attrition and corrupted data.  
- Technically rigorous with clear theoretical contributions; Theorem 3 gives non-recoverability criteria and Sections 5–6 offer strong insights into causal query recovery.  
- Original extension of Mohan’s framework to handle interactions between missingness mechanisms.  
- Novel result: recovery of P(y|do(x)) even when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.  
- Significant potential impact for epidemiology, social science, and ML; provides practical graphical conditions for recoverability and non-recoverability.  
- Presentation is dense and hard to follow; notation is overly complex and inconsistently introduced (e.g., premature use of Vo and Vm).  
- Theorem 1 is difficult to understand and appears tangential to the main narrative.  
- Some results (e.g., Theorem 2 and Corollary 1) resemble existing techniques like inverse probability weighting and need clearer contextualization.  
- Key terms and notations (e.g., severed-edge graphs) are undefined; casual language and specific errors reduce professionalism.  
- Specific errors noted: misuse of phrasing on p4 l214 and inconsistent symbols (e.g., "<-->" vs. ↔).  
- For acceptance: important problem with significant theoretical contributions and novel causal-recovery results.  
- Against acceptance: dense presentation limits accessibility, some incremental results, and lack of a general identifiability criterion reduces practical utility.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to improve clarity, streamline notation, fix presentation errors, and consider journal-format expansion if needed.
