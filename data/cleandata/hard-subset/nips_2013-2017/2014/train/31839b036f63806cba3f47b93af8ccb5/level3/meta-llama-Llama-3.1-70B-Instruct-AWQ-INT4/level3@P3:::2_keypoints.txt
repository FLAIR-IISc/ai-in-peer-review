- Presents novel necessary and sufficient conditions for identifiability of causal queries with missing data using an explicit model of the missing-data mechanism.
- Extends prior work by incorporating the missing-data generating mechanism into causal diagrams, offering new theoretical insights.
- Introduces "missingness graphs" (m-graphs) to explicitly represent the missingness process and dependencies among observed variables.
- Presents a refined sequential factorization theorem that handles settings where missingness mechanisms influence each other.
- Proposes new graphical criteria that rule out recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates causal queries can be recoverable even when factors in their identifying estimands are not recoverable.
- Derives graphical conditions for recovering P(y | do(x)) when Y and its missingness mechanism are not d-separable.
- Applies results to attrition problems and characterizes recovery of causal effects from data corrupted by attrition.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with numerous examples and illustrations aiding comprehension.
- Includes a thorough related-work discussion that situates the contributions within the literature.
- Assumes a high level of familiarity with graphical models and causal analysis, limiting accessibility for non-experts.
- Some proofs and technical details are relegated to the appendix, which may obscure full appreciation of technical contributions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, important results; clear organization; helpful examples and illustrations.
- Arguments against acceptance: steep background required and technical details not fully in main text.
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and worthy of acceptance, with a suggestion to add more background/explanations for broader accessibility.
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 10/10, Significance 10/10.
