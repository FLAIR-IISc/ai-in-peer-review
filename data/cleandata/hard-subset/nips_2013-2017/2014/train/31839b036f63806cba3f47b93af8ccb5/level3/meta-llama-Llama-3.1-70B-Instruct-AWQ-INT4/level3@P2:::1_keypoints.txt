- Addresses whether causal or probabilistic queries are estimable from data with missing entries given a missingness model.  
- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) with more general conditions for recovering probabilistic and causal queries.  
- Paper is technically sound and results appear correct.  
- Introduces Missingness Graphs (m-graphs) to represent missingness processes and dependencies.  
- Defines recoverability and derives conditions for recoverability in MNAR datasets.  
- Well-organized with clear explanations of main technical results.  
- Notation and presentation suffer clarity issues (confusing use of V, Vi, Vo; illegible subscripts).  
- Long, dense 13-page appendix makes the paper harder to follow.  
- Main technical strength: high-quality results, with Theorem 3 notably clear and interpretable.  
- Some results feel incremental and lack a unifying conceptual picture.  
- Section 3 seems orthogonal to the rest and Theorem 1 is difficult to comprehend.  
- Paper would benefit from a more general identifiability criterion analogous to causal-effect identifiability.  
- Comparison with prior work (Mohan et al. 2013, Garcia 2013) is unclear; relationship to Garcia [2013] is not resolved.  
- Significant potential impact—Sections 5 and 6 contain particularly useful results.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to fix clarity issues and provide a more general identifiability criterion.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Revision suggestions: revise notation/presentation, develop a general identifiability criterion, clarify relation to prior work, and shorten/clarify appendix.
