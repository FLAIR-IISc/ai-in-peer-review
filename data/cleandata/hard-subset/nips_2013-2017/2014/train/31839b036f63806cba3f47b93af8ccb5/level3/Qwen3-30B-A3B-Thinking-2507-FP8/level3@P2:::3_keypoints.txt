- Extends Mohan et al. (2013) m-graphs to give more general conditions for recovering P(y|x), P(y,x), and causal queries from missing data.
- Shows causal queries P(y|do(x)) can be recoverable even when associated probabilistic relations are not.
- Introduces novel graphical criteria for recoverability and non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.
- Generalizes the sequential factorization theorem to handle interacting missingness mechanisms.
- Proves new graphical criteria for non-recoverability of joint and conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates causal recoverability despite lack of d-separation between Y and its missingness mechanism.
- Provides necessary and sufficient conditions for recovering causal effects under simple attrition.
- Technical development is rigorous with formal definitions, theorems, and proofs.
- Requires knowledge of the missingness process, limiting applicability in many ML settings.
- Positivity assumptions are insufficiently addressed and the relation to Garcia et al.'s rectification of attrition is unclear.
- Inconsistent use of the term "mgraph" (sometimes includes V* variables, sometimes not) needs clarification.
- Paper implicitly assumes knowledge of the full population, excluding problems like population size estimation.
- The term "neighbor" is used without definition and should be clarified.
- More detail from Figure 1 should be moved into the main text for clarity.
- Several minor notational errors (e.g., R^{(N)} vs R^{(j)}, Example 1 RHS, Y || R vs Y || R_y) need correction.
- Original contribution: shows causal recoverability can exist without probabilistic recoverability, challenging prior intuition.
- Advances the literature on missing data by combining graphical models and causal inference for broader recoverability results.
- Significant implications for experimental science and longitudinal studies with attrition.
- Strong strengths: clear extension of prior work, novel causal insights, comprehensive attrition analysis, rigorous proofs, and well-motivated examples.
- Key weaknesses: reliance on known missingness processes, implicit full-population assumption, terminology/notation inconsistencies, unclear positivity discussion, vague MLE claim under MAR, and minor errors.
- Recommendation: interesting, technically sound paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing clarifications and corrections.
- Pro: first general conditions for recovering causal queries when joint is not recoverable, resolves misconceptions, provides intuitive graphical conditions, and has sound theorems.
- Con: limited applicability due to missingness-process knowledge requirement, full-population assumption excludes some applications, notational/definition issues reduce accessibility, and practical implementation connections are underdeveloped.
